'''Greet Death''' is an American [[rock music|rock]] band from [[Flint, Michigan]]. Greet Death has released two full-length albums.

== History ==
Greet Death released their first full-length album in 2017 titled ''Dixieland''.<ref name="detroitmetrotimes">{{cite web |last1=Gallippo |first1=Eric |title=Greet Death revises the '90s with ear-bleeding pop |url=https://www.metrotimes.com/detroit/greet-death-revises-the-90s-with-ear-bleeding-pop/Content?oid=8508150 |publisher=[[Detroit Metro Times]] |accessdate=November 22, 2020}}</ref><ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web |last1=Cohen |first1=Ian |title=Greet Death: Dixieland |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/greet-death-dixieland/ |publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |accessdate=November 22, 2020}}</ref> The album was released through Flesh and Bone Records. The band was featured as one of [[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]'s "10 up-and-coming artists from Detroit you need to know".<ref name="alternativepress">{{cite web |title=10 up-and-coming artists from Detroit you need to know |date=February 22, 2018 |url=https://www.altpress.com/features/new_artists_detroit_music/ |publisher=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |accessdate=November 22, 2020}}</ref> In 2019, Greet Death released their second full-length album titled ''New Hell'', also through Deathwish Inc.<ref name="stereogum">{{cite web |last1=DeVille |first1=Chris |title=Stream Greet Death's New Album New Hell |date=November 8, 2019 |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2064264/greet-death-new-hell/music/ |publisher=[[Stereogum]] |accessdate=November 22, 2020}}</ref><ref name="pitchfork1">{{cite web |last1=Cohen |first1=Ian |title=Greet Death: New Hell |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/greet-death-new-hell/ |publisher=Pitchfork |accessdate=November 22, 2020}}</ref>
