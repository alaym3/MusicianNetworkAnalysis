'''The Emotions''' are an American [[soul music|soul]]/[[Rhythm and blues|R&B]] vocal group from [[Chicago]]. The group started out in gospel music but transitioned into [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]] and [[disco]] music. The Emotions were named by [[VH1]] as one of the 18 most influential girl groups of all time.<ref name=Allmusic /><ref name=Grammy /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/5003/the-most-influential-girl-groups/|title=From The Andrews Sisters To Pussy Riot, The Most Influential Girl Groups Of All Time|date=March 13, 2015|publisher=[[VH1]]|website=vh1.com}}</ref>

==History==
===Early career===
The group was originally a gospel outfit known as the Hutchinson Sunbeams who toured the gospel circuit with their father Joe Hutchinson. The Sunbeams sang on [[Jerry Van Dyke]]’s “Children’s Gospel” television show and also occasionally performed in the concert with [[Mahalia Jackson]]. They eventually became an R&B/Soul act with a popular following in their hometown of Chicago, Illinois. Soon being renamed as The Emotions, they signed with the Memphis-based Volt imprint of [[Stax Records]] in the late 1960s.<ref name=NYT>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1977/08/21/archives/an-emotional-success-story.html|title=An "Emotional" Success Story|author=Rockwell, John|author-link=John Rockwell|date=August 21, 1977|website=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://staxrecords.com/artist/the-emotions/|title=The Emotions|publisher=[[Stax Records]]|website=stax.com}}</ref><ref name=Bio>{{cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1995-01-26/news/cb-24499_1_grammy-winner|title=Grammy Winners Reunite to Prove They're 'Bigger Than Bubblegum'|author=Osorio Colon, Marisa|date=January 26, 1995|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]}}</ref><ref name=Bio2>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1991-02-14-ca-1877-story.html|title=Musical Emotions Return With Melodic Sound : Singers: The Emotions, a trio of sisters, revives its career as musical tastes return to tuneful songs|author=D’Agostino, John|date=February 14, 1991|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]}}</ref> Under the production of [[Isaac Hayes]] and [[David Porter (musician)|David Porter]] the group issued their 1969 debut album entitled ''So I Can Love You'' on Stax.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.45worlds.com/vinyl/album/vos6008|title=The Emotions: So I Can Love You|website=45worlds.com}}</ref>

"So I Can Love You" rose to no. 43 upon the [[Billboard Magazine|Billboard]] [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|Top R&B Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/r-b-hip-hop-albums/song/890428|title=The Emotions: So I Can Love You (Top R&B Albums)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> The album's title track got to No. 3 on the Billboard Hot R&B Songs chart and No. 39 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/HSI/song/462476|title=The Emotions: So I Can Love You (Hot 100)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/462476|title=The Emotions: So I Can Love You (Hot R&B Songs)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref> Another single entitled "The Best Part Of A Love Affair" rose to no. 27 upon the Billboard Hot R&B Songs chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/897087|title=The Emotions: The Best Part Of A Love Affair (Hot R&B Songs)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref>

During 1970, The Emotions released a single entitled "[[Heart Association]]." That song reached No. 29 on the Billboard Hot Soul Songs chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/895755|title=The Emotions: Heart Association (Hot Soul Songs)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref>
The girl group went on to release their sophomore LP entitled ''[[Untouched (The Emotions album)|Untouched]]'' in 1972 upon Stax.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.45worlds.com/vinyl/album/vos6015|title=The Emotions: Untouched|website=45worlds.com}}</ref> A song from the album called "[[Show Me How (The Emotions song)|Show Me How]]" rose to No. 13 upon the Billboard [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|Hot Soul Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/579694|title=The Emotions: Show Me How (Hot R&B Songs)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref>

During 1972, the girl group also released another single called "[[My Honey and Me]]." That song reached No. 18 on the Billboard Hot Soul Songs chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/896554|title=The Emotions: My Honey and Me (Hot Soul Songs)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref>

The Emotions then started to work on their third studio album entitled ''Songs of Innocence and Experience''. This LP was due to be issued in 1973, but was eventually shelved. The girl group went on to appear in the 1973 feature film ''[[Wattstax]]'', performing the song "Peace Be Still." The tune went on to be added to the movie's soundtrack. ''Wattstax'' was also nominated for a [[Golden Globe]] in the category of [[Golden Globe Award for Best Documentary Film|Best Documentary]]. With Stax becoming defunct in 1975, the group then left the record label altogether.<ref name=SIE>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/songs-of-innocence-and-experience-mw0000340007|title=The Emotions: Songs of Innocence and Experience|author=Unterberger, Richie|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|website=allmusic.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2003-jun-05-wk-movies5-story.html%3f_amp=true|title=The Woodstock of soul|author=Holmes II, Emory|date=June 5, 2003|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.baltimoresun.com/news/bs-xpm-2000-05-28-0006140332-story,amp.html|title=Wattstax: a peaceful explosion of soul|author=Hornaday, Ann|date=May 28, 2000|publisher=[[Baltimore Sun]]|website=baltimoresun.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goldenglobes.com/winners-nominees/1974?page=6|title=1974 Golden Globes|publisher=[[The Golden Globes]]|website=goldenglobes.com}}</ref>

===Above and beyond===
The group then joined up with [[Columbia Records]] where an association with [[Maurice White]] of [[Earth, Wind & Fire]] brought them their greatest level of success.<ref name=Allmusic>{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p4190|label=The Emotions Biography}}</ref> With White and [[Charles Stepney]] on production, The Emotions issued in 1976 their third studio album ''[[Flowers (The Emotions album)|Flowers]]'' on [[Columbia Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.45worlds.com/vinyl/album/pc34163|title=The Emotions: Flowers|website=45worlds.com}}</ref> ''Flowers'' rose to No.5 upon the [[Billboard Magazine|Billboard]] [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart and No. 45 on the [[Billboard 200]]. The album was also certified [[Music recording certification|Gold]] in the US by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BLP/song/378286|title=The Emotions: Flowers (Top R&B Albums)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/TLP/song/378286|title=The Emotions: Flowers (Billboard 200)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref>
<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=The+Emotions+Flowers#search_section|title=The Emotions: Flowers|website=RIAA.com}}</ref>  The LP's [[Flowers (The Emotions song)|title track]] got to No. 16 on the Billboard [[Hot R&B Songs]] chart. Another single called "[[I Don't Wanna Lose Your Love (The Emotions song)|I Don't Wanna Lose Your Love]]" rose to No. 4 on the Billboard [[Dance Club Songs]] chart and No. 13 on the Billboard Hot R&B Songs chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/r-b-hip-hop-songs/song/673723|title=The Emotions: Flowers (Hot R&B Songs)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/374889|title=The Emotions: Flowers (Hot R&B Songs)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/673723|title=The Emotions: I Don't Wanna Lose Your Love (Hot R&B Songs)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/DSI/song/374889|title=The Emotions: I Don't Wanna Lose Your Love (Dance Club Songs)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref>

Following Charles Stepney's death in 1976, White took over producing the Emotions.<ref name=NYT /> During 1977 the group issued their follow up album entitled ''[[Rejoice (The Emotions album)|Rejoice]]'' The album reached No. 1 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|Top R&B Albums]] chart and No. 7 on the [[Billboard 200]] chart. ''Rejoice'' has also been certified [[Music recording certification|Platinum]] in the US by the [[RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Emotions&ti=Rejoice&lab=&genre=&format=&date_option=release&from=&to=&award=&type=&category=&adv=SEARCH#search_section|title=The Emotions: Rejoice|publisher=[[RIAA]]|website=riaa.com}}</ref>

With the LP came the single “[[Don't Ask My Neighbors]]" which got to the top ten on the Billboard R&B singles charts.
Another song called "[[Best of My Love (The Emotions song)|Best of My Love]]" reached No. 1 on the Billboard Pop and R&B charts<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=song|id=t825476|pure_url=yes}}|title=Best of My Love – The Emotions|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=April 13, 2008}}</ref> and won a [[Grammy]] for [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals|Best R&B Performance By a Duo or Group with Vocals]],<ref name="Grammy"/> as well as an American Music Award for Favorite Soul/R&B Single.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theamas.com/winners-database/?winnerKeyword=Emotions&winnerYear=|title=The Emotions|publisher=[[American Music Awards]]|website=theamas.com}}</ref> "Best of My Love" has also been certified [[Music recording certification|Platinum]] in the US by the [[RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Emotions&ti=Best+of+My+Love#search_section|title=The Emotions: Best of My Love|publisher=[[RIAA]]|website=riaa.com}}</ref>

During summer 1978 The Emotions went on to issue their third Columbia album called ''[[Sunbeam (The Emotions album)|Sunbeam]]''. The album rose to No. 12 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|Top Soul Albums]] chart and No. 40 on the [[Billboard 200]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/r-b-hip-hop-albums/song/315512|title=The Emotions: Sunbeam (Top Soul Albums)|website=billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/billboard-200/song/315512|title=The Emotions: Sunbeam (Billboard 200)|website=billboard.com}}</ref> John Storm Roberts of [[High Fidelity (magazine)|High Fidelity]] stated ""Sunbeam" sparkles with good tracks."<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.americanradiohistory.com/hd2/IDX-Audio/Archive-High-Fidelity-IDX/IDX/70s/High-Fidelity-1978-11-OCR-Page-0147.pdf#search=%22emotions%201978%22|title=The Emotions: Sunbeam|author=Storm Roberts, John|volume=28|issue=11|page=159|date=November 1978|publisher=[[High Fidelity Magazine]]|website=americanradiohistory.com}}</ref>
Glenn Clark of ''[[The Morning Call]]'' wrote "The best thing I can say about this LP is that I like it. The Emotions' brand of snappy and upbeat soul is good for my soul. Maurice White, guru of Earth, Wind &amp; Fire, produced this album, and I think he has blended well the talent he has brought together."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/280309086/?terms=%22Emotions%2BColumbia%22&match=2|title=The Emotions: Sunbeam|author=Clark, Glenn|page=52|date=September 30, 1978|publisher=[[The Morning Call|Morning Call]]|website=newspapers.com}}</ref>

''Sunbeam'' has been certified [[Music recording certification|Gold]] in the US by the [[RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Emotions&ti=&lab=&genre=&format=&date_option=certification&from=1978-01-01&to=1978-12-31&award=&type=&category=&adv=SEARCH#search_section|title=The Emotions: Sunbeam|publisher=[[RIAA]]|website=riaa.com}}</ref> An album cut called Smile reached No. 6 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|Hot Soul Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/373531|title=The Emotions: Smile Chart History (Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref>

During 1979, Earth, Wind & Fire collaborated with The Emotions on the single "[[Boogie Wonderland]]". The song reached No. 6 and No. 2 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Hot 100]] and [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|Hot Soul Songs]] charts. "Boogie Wonderland" has also been certified [[Music recording certification|Gold]] in the US by the [[RIAA]]. 
Within October of that year the Emotions issued their follow up studio album again produced by White entitled ''[[Come into Our World]]'' upon Columbia, which rose to no. 35 upon the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] Top R&B Albums chart. Jon Wall of [[Melody Maker]] wrote "throughout ''Come into Our World'' The Emotions' superb vocal control, range and harmonic sense are displayed to maximum effect". Wall also added "''Come into Our World'' is one of the most appealing albums I've heard since ''[[Off the Wall]]''. I can't get the album off the turntable and I don't want to". Bill Rhedon of [[The Baltimore Sun]] noted that the album has "excellent material" with "simply steady, unvarying Coming at You, Soul."
A song called "[[What's the Name of Your Love?]]" also got to no. 30 upon the [[Billboard Magazine|Billboard]] [[Hot R&B Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.45worlds.com/vinyl/album/jc36149|title=The Emotions: Come into Our World|website=45worlds.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|title=The Emotions: Come into Our World|page=20|date=January 19, 1980|author=Wall, Jon|publisher=[[Melody Maker]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/377644197/|title=The Emotions: Come into Our World|page=15|date=December 8, 1979|author=Rhedon, Bill|publisher=[[Baltimore Sun]]|website=newspapers.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/r-b-hip-hop-songs/song/897355|title=The Emotions: What's the Name of Your Love?|website=Billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/r-b-hip-hop-albums/song/314675|title=The Emotions: Come into Our World (Top R&B Albums)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> As well Maurice White went on to be [[Grammy]] nominated in the category of [[Grammy Award for Producer of the Year, Non-Classical|Producer of the Year Non-Classical]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/maurice-white|title=Maurice White|publisher=[[The Recording Academy]]|website=grammy.com|date=November 23, 2020}}</ref>

Wanda and Jeanette went on to appear on [[Jennifer Holliday]]'s Grammy nominated 1983 LP ''[[Feel My Soul]]''. Pamela and Wanda also featured on Earth, Wind & Fire's 1983 album [[Electric Universe]].<ref>{{cite work|title=Jennifer Holliday: Feel My Soul|date=1983|publisher=[[Geffen Records]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/jennifer-holliday|title=Jennifer Holliday|publisher=[[The Recording Academy]]|website=grammy.com|date=November 23, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite work|title=Earth, Wind & Fire: Electric Universe|date=1983|publisher=[[Columbia Records]]}}</ref>

The Emotions' eighth studio album entitled ''[[Sincerely (The Emotions album)|Sincerely]]'' was issued in 1984 on Red Label Records.<ref>{{cite work|title=The Emotions: Sincerely|date=1984|publisher=Red Label Records}}</ref>  The album rose to No. 3 on the [[United Kingdom|UK]] [[Blues & Soul]] Hiplist chart and No. 33 on the [[United States|US]] [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|Top Soul Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite work|title=Blues & Soul Hiplist|date=April 24, 1984|publisher=[[Blues & Soul]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BLP/song/887392|title=The Emotions: Sincerely (Top Soul Albums)|website=billboard.com}}</ref> Dave Hillson of [[Blues & Soul]] said The Emotions "caught the mood of eighties Soul perfectly".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bluesandsoul.com/review/444/the_emotions__sincerely/|title=The Emotions: Sincerely|author=Hillson, Dave|publisher=[[Blues & Soul]]|website=bluesandsoul.com}}</ref> Hugh Wyatt of the [[New York Daily News]] declared "this album is top shelf".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/488377306/?terms=%22The%2BEmotions%2BSincerely%22&match=1|title=The Emotions: Sincerely|author=Wyatt, Hugh|page=470|date=June 1, 1984|publisher=[[New York Daily News]]|website=newspapers.com}}</ref> As a single, "[[You're the One (The Emotions song)|You're the One]]" reached  No. 19 on the UK [[Blues & Soul]] Hiplist chart and No. 34 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|Hot R&B Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite work|title=Blues & Soul Hiplist|date=27 March 1984|publisher=[[Blues & Soul]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/BSI/song/888085|title=The Emotions: You're The One (Hot R&B Songs)|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|website=billboard.com}}</ref>
Another single called "[[You're the Best (The Emotions song)|You're the Best]]" rose to No. 33 on the US [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Dance Club Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-emotions/chart-history/dance-club-play-songs/song/353028|title=The Emotions: You're The Best (Dance Club Songs)|website=billboard.com}}</ref> During 1985 the group also issued their subsequent studio LP entitled ''If I Only Knew'' on [[Motown Records]]. The album got to No. 11 on the UK [[Blues & Soul]] Hiplist chart.<ref>{{cite work|title=The Emotions: If I Only Knew|date=1985|publisher=[[Motown Records]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite work|title=Blues & Soul Hiplist|date=April 16, 1985|publisher=[[Blues & Soul]]}}</ref>

The Emotions went on to feature on [[Tyler Collins (singer)|Tyler Collins]] 1989 album ''[[Girls Nite Out (Tyler Collins album)|Girls Nite Out]]''. A song off the LP that the girl group sang on called "Watcha Gonna Do?" rose to No. 8 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/amp/Tyler-collins-whatcha-gonna-do-lyrics|title=Tyler Collins: Girls Nite Out|website=genius.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/tyler-collins/chart-history/r-b-hip-hop-songs/song/368263|title=Tyler Collins: Whatcha Gonna Do?|website=billboard.com}}</ref>

Jeanette and Wanda then appeared upon EWF's 1990 album ''[[Heritage (Earth, Wind & Fire album)|Heritage]]''. The Emotions also guested upon [[Nancy Wilson (jazz singer)|Nancy Wilson]]'s 1990 album ''[[A Lady with a Song]]'' and jazz group [[Urban Knights]]'s 1995 LP ''[[Urban Knights I]]''. They then featured on  [[LL Cool J]]'s 1995 LP ''[[Mr. Smith (album)|Mr. Smith]]'' which has been certified [[Music recording certification|Double Platinum]] in the US by the [[RIAA]].<ref name=Allmusic /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=Ll+Cool+J+Mr+Smith+Polygram#search_section|title=LL Cool J: Mr. Smith|website=RIAA.com}}</ref> The Emotions also appeared on [[George Duke]]'s 1995 album ''Illusions'' and the [[Gospel Gangstaz]]'s 1996 album [[Do or Die (Gospel Gangstaz album)|Do or Die]]. During 1998 they featured on an episode of [[Motown Live]] with fellow girl group [[Divine (group)|Divine]] and made a guest appearance on [[Smokey Robinson]]'s 1999 [[Grammy]] nominated album ''[[Intimate (Smokey Robinson album)|Intimate]]''.<ref name=Allmusic /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/smokey-robinson|title=Smokey Robinson|website=grammy.com|date=December 15, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/intimate-mw0000245975|title=Smokey Robinson: Intimate|website=Allmusic.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/502775/rb-trios-success-matter-of-divineb-intervention/|title=R&B Trio's Success a Matter of Divine Intervention|author=Reiss, Randy|date=October 27, 1998|publisher=[[MTV]]|website=mtv.com}}</ref>

During 2000, Pamela eventually rejoined the group.<ref name=Allmusic /> A year later they were bestowed with the Pioneer Award from the [[Rhythm and Blues Foundation]].<ref name=Award /> The Emotions went on to guest upon Earth, Wind & Fire's 2003 album ''[[The Promise (Earth, Wind & Fire album)|The Promise]]'', which was produced by Maurice White. A song from the album featuring The Emotions called "[[All in the Way]]" rose to No. 13 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Adult R&B Songs]] chart and No. 25 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Adult Contemporary Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20140120,00.html|title=Picks and Pans Main: Song|date=May 26, 2003|publisher=[[People (magazine)|People]]|website=people.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/earth-wind-fire/chart-history/hot-adult-r-and-b-airplay/song/433444|title=Earth, Wind & Fire: All In The Way (Adult R&B Songs)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/earth-wind-fire/chart-history/adult-contemporary/song/433444|title=Earth, Wind & Fire: All In The Way (Adult Contemporary)|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> As well the group appeared on a 2004 [[PBS]] soul music special hosted by [[Patti LaBelle]] where they performed "Best Of My Love".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pbshawaii.org/70s-soul-superstars/|title=70s Soul Superstars|website=PBS Hawaii}}</ref> Within September of that year, ''Songs of Innocence'' and ''Experience'' were finally released by Stax.<ref name=SIE />

The Emotions went on to collaborate with rapper [[Snoop Dogg]] on a track called "Life" upon his 2006 album ''[[Tha Blue Carpet Treatment]]''.<ref>{{cite work|title=Snoop Dogg: Tha Blue Carpet Treatment (Bonus Tracks)|date=2006|publisher=Doggy style/Geffen Records}}</ref> The album has been certified [[Music recording certification|Gold]] in the US by the RIAA.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=Snoop+Dogg+Tha+Blue+Carpet+Treatment#search_section|title=Snoop Dogg: Tha Blue Carpet Treatment|website=riaa.com}}</ref> The Emotions also appeared as a guest artist upon [[Terrace Martin]]'s 2016 Grammy nominated album ''[[Velvet Portraits]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/velvet-portraits-mw0002933114|title=Terrace Martin: Velvet Portraits|website=Allmusic.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/terrace-martin|title=Terrace Martin|website=grammy.com}}</ref>

===Pamela's death===
On September 18, 2020, Pamela Hutchinson died at the age of 61.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tmz.com/2020/09/20/the-emotions-singer-pamela-hutchinson-dead-dies/|title='Best of My Love' Emotions Singer Pamela Hutchinson Dead at 61|website=Tmz.com|access-date=20 September 2020}}</ref>

==Legacy==
The Emotions have been sampled by rappers such as [[Big Daddy Kane]], [[Tupac Shakur]], [[LL Cool J]], [[Wu Tang Clan]], [[50 Cent]], [[Ice Cube]], [[Salt n Pepa]], [[De La Soul]], [[Kanye West]], [[A Tribe Called Quest]] and [[Notorious BIG]].<ref name=S>{{cite web|url=https://secondhandsongs.com/artist/2712/covers#nav-entity|title=The Emotions|website=secondhandsongs.com}}</ref><ref>Kelley, Frannie (November 4, 2011). Retrieved October 28, 2012.</ref>

Artists such as [[Toni Braxton]], [[112 (band)|112]], [[Mariah Carey]], [[Lisa Lisa and Cult Jam]], [[Mary J Blige]], [[Ginuwine]], [[Keyshia Cole]], [[Tamia]] and [[Janet Jackson]] have also sampled the girl group.<ref name=S /><ref name=Genius>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/artists/The-emotions|title=The Emotions|website=genius.com}}</ref><ref>Frere-Jones, Sasha (April 3, 2006).
