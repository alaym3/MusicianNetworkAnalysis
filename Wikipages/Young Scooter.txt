'''Kenneth Edward Bailey''' (born March 28, 1986), better known by his stage name '''Young Scooter''', is an American rapper<ref name="complex2">{{cite web|title=Young Scooter Jailed for Probation Violation|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/04/young-scooter-jailed-for-probation-violation|publisher=Complex|access-date=2013-04-12|first=David|last=Drake|date=2013-04-12}}</ref><ref name="ybf">{{cite web|title=Young Scooter|url=http://www.yourbestfeature.com/artist/scooter/|publisher=Yourbestfeature.com|access-date=2013-02-27}}</ref> who is affiliated with [[Freebandz]] and [[1017 Brick Squad Records]]<ref name=gg>{{cite web|title=Young Scooter Clears Up Rumors Of Being Signed To Gucci Mane & Talks "Colombia Remix" (VIDEO)|url=http://globalgrind.com/music/young-scooter-clears-rumors-being-signed-gucci-mane-talks-colombia-remix-video|publisher=Global Grind|access-date=2013-03-11}}</ref><ref name="got">{{cite web|title=Young Scooter's "Colombia" Is Apparently Blasphemous To REAL Colombian Drug Cartels|url=http://gossiponthis.com/2013/02/01/young-scooter-colombia-blasphemous-real-colombian-drug-cartels/|publisher=Gossiponthis.com|access-date=2013-02-27}}</ref> and CEO of his own label Black Migo Gang. Scooter grew to prominence in 2012 after releasing a number of successful mixtapes and collaborating with notable rappers such as [[Gucci Mane]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Waka Flocka Flame]] and [[Chief Keef]].<ref name="complex">{{cite web|title=Who Is Young Scooter?|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/01/who-is-young-scooter|publisher=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex Music]]|access-date=2013-02-27|first=David|last=Drake|date=2013-01-25}}</ref> His mixtape ''Street Lottery'' received positive reviews and was certified gold on popular mixtape site [[DatPiff]].<ref name="dp_sl">{{cite web|title=Young Scooter Street Lottery|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Young-Scooter-Street-Lottery-mixtape.436249.html|publisher=[[DatPiff]]|access-date=2013-02-27}}</ref> It was placed at number 24 on ''[[Spin (magazine)|SPIN]]''{{'}}s 40 Best Hip-Hop Albums of 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=SPIN's 40 Best Hip-Hop Albums of 2013|url=https://www.spin.com/2013/12/best-rap-albums-2013/131122-young-scooter-street-lottery/|publisher=SPIN|access-date=27 December 2013}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Scooter was born in [[Walterboro, South Carolina]]. When he was nine, his family moved to the [[Kirkwood, Atlanta|Kirkwood]] Community (a.k.a. Lil Mexico) of [[Atlanta, GA]].  In 2008, after he was charged with drug trafficking, he decided to start pursuing a career in music. Scooter is a childhood friend with fellow Atlanta rapper [[Future (rapper)|Future]].<ref name="ybf" />

While his first mixtape ''Plug Talkin'' didn't receive much attention, with his second release ''Finessin and Flexin{{'}}'' he made a name for himself, already collaborating with Future.<ref name="complex" /> Scooter put out 2 more mixtapes in 2012 – ''Married to the Streets'' and ''Voice of the Streetz'', featuring production from [[Zaytoven]], DJ Spinz, [[Nard & B]], Da Honorable C.N.O.T.E. and guest verses by [[Gucci Mane]] and Alley Boy.<ref name=datpiff>{{cite web|title=Free Young Scooter Mixtapes|url=http://www.datpiff.com/mixtapes-search.php?criteria=young%20scooter&sort=official|publisher=DatPiff|access-date=2013-02-27}}</ref>

Scooter's real breakthrough came in January 2013, when he released his mixtape ''Street Lottery''. ''[[Spin (magazine)|SPIN]]'' named it "Rap release of the week"<ref name="spin">{{cite web|title=Rap Release of the Week: Young Scooter's 'Street Lottery'|url=http://www.spin.com/blogs/rap-release-of-the-week-young-scooters-street-lottery|publisher=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|access-date=2013-02-27|first=Brandon|last=Soderberg|date=2013-09-01}}</ref> and [[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] featured it on its "Best mixtapes of January" list.<ref name="xxl">{{cite web|title=The 12 Best Mixtapes of January|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/01/the-12-best-mixtapes-of-january/10/|publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|access-date=2013-02-27|first=Eric|last=Diep|date=2013-01-31}}</ref> Eric Diep of XXL called Scooter "one of the hottest street rappers coming out of Atlanta" and wrote that tracks like the single "Colombia" and "Street Lottery" (featuring [[Bun B]]) are "proof of his undeniable talent".<ref name="xxl" /> The tape was also a viral success gaining over 100,000 downloads on [[DatPiff]].<ref name="dp_sl" /> The official video for "Colombia", directed by Decatur Dan, premiered on [[MTV]] on January 31. The song became a hit in the south, especially in Scooter's hometown Atlanta.<ref name="got" /> Brandon Soderberg of ''SPIN'' classified it as a mix of [[Rick Ross]]' "[[Hustlin']]" and Future's "[[Tony Montana (song)|Tony Montana]]".<ref name="spin" /> By the end of the month, an official remix with rappers Rick Ross, [[Birdman (rapper)|Birdman]] and [[Gucci Mane]] was released.<ref name="xxl_cr">{{cite web|title=Young Scooter Enlists an All-Star Cast for "Colombia (Remix)"|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/bangers/2013/02/young-scooter-enlists-an-all-star-cast-for-colombia-remix/|publisher=XXL|access-date=2013-02-27|first=Eric|last=Diep|date=2013-02-20}}</ref> [[Lil Wayne]] also covered the song for his mixtape ''[[Dedication 5]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dedication 5|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.25275/title.lil-wayne-dedication-5-cover-art-tracklisting-download-mixtape-stream-|publisher=HipHopDX|access-date=2013-09-01}}</ref> The rapper's recent success prompted [[OnSmash]] to state "the buzz for Scooter is at an all-time high."<ref name=onsmash>{{cite web|title=Young Scooter – Pass Around (feat. Gucci Mane & Wale)|url=http://www.freeonsmash.com/news/2013/02/23/young-scooter-pass-around-feat-gucci-mane-wale/|publisher=[[OnSmash]]|access-date=2013-02-27}}</ref> On April 8, Scooter was arrested for violation of parole.<ref>{{cite web|last=Paine |first=Jake |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23561/title.young-scooter-arrested-for-parole-violation- |title=Young Scooter Arrested For Parole Violation |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-04-12 |access-date=2013-05-14}}</ref>

Scooter's collaboration mixtape with [[Gucci Mane]] ''Free Bricks 2'' was released on February 28, 2013.<ref name="dp_fb2">{{cite web|title=Gucci Mane & Young Scooter - Free Bricks 2|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Gucci-Mane-Young-Scooter-Free-Bricks-2-mixtape.460428.html|publisher=DatPiff|access-date=2013-02-27}}</ref> On August 29, 2013, Young Scooter released his mixtape ''From The Cell Block To Your Block'', featuring guest appearances from [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Gucci Mane]], [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], [[YG (rapper)|YG]], [[Lil Boosie]], [[Webbie]] and [[Lil Phat]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|title=Young Scooter - From The Cell Block To Your Block|url=http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/22877/young-scooter-from-the-cell-block-to-your-block.html|publisher=LiveMixtapes|access-date=2013-09-01}}</ref>

==Musical style==
Scooter is known for his freestyle type of rapping, without writing down his lyrics, similarly to [[Gucci Mane]]. He has defined his style as "count music," and explained it in an interview with [[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]: "I don't really care what I say on a beat as long as it's about some money. When you try to think hard and write it out, that's when it's gonna be fucked up."<ref name="complex" />

Scooter's lyrical themes are largely about money and drugs. David Drake of Complex compared his "populist, kingpin rapping" to that of [[Young Jeezy]], although he noted that Scooter doesn't have the "all-encompassing grandiosity" of his fellow Atlanta rapper.<ref name="complex" />

Scooter is influenced by famous hip-hop artists such as [[Jay-Z]], [[50 Cent]] and [[Diddy (rapper)|Diddy]].<ref name="ybf" />

==Legal issues==
Young Scooter was arrested for probation violation during a traffic stop in DeKalb County, Georgia, on April 8, 2013. According to HipHopDX, Scooter shared a jail cell with frequent collaborator [[Gucci Mane]], who was arrested for probation violation on April 12, 2013.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20130424014807/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23662/title.gucci-mane-young-scooter-reportedly-sharing-cell-at-dekalb-county-jail/]</ref> Scooter spent six months behind bars before being released in mid-October 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complexmag.ca/music/2013/10/young-scooter-released-from-jail |title=Complex CA &#124; Latest News in Canadian Pop Culture |website=Complexmag.ca |access-date=2020-04-08}}</ref> On March 2, 2015, Young Scooter was released off probation.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=youngscooter|author=Black Migo Scooter|number=572421287374688256|date=2 March 2015|title=FINALLY OFF PROBATION }}</ref>
