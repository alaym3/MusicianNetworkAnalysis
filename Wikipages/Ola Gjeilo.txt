'''Ola Gjeilo''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|j|eɪ|l|oʊ}} {{respell|YAY|loh}},<ref>[http://olagjeilo.com/admin/wp-content/uploads/Ola-Gjeilo-Sinfini-Interview.pdf Ola describes it as "YAY-lo"] ''olagjeilo.com'', accessed 2 September 2020</ref> {{IPA-no|ˈûːlɑ ˈjæ̀ɪlʊ|lang}}; born May 5, 1978) is a [[Norwegians|Norwegian]] [[composer]] and [[pianist]], living in the [[United States]].<ref name="about">[http://olagjeilo.com/about/ Ola Gjeilo's official biography, from his website] Accessed March 26, 2016</ref>

He writes [[choir|choral]] music, and has written for [[piano]] and [[concert band|wind symphony]], publishing through Walton Music, [[Edition Peters]], and [[Boosey & Hawkes|Boosey and Hawkes]].<ref name="about"/><ref>Cheo, Patrick, “Wind Symphony,” Ola Gjeilo: Composer, Pianist, 2014, accessed October 2, 2014, http://olagjeilo.com/sheet-music-wind-band/ {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141124232234/http://olagjeilo.com/sheet-music-wind-band/ |date=2014-11-24 }}.</ref>

==Biography==

Ola Gjeilo was born to Inge and Anne-May Gjeilo, and grew up in [[Skui, Norway]]. He began playing piano and composing when he was five years old and learned to read music when he was seven years old.<ref name="musicroom">{{cite web|url=http://www.musicroom.com/blog/an-exclusive-interview-with-ola-gjeilo-19828|title=An exclusive interview with Ola Gjeilo - Musicroom Blog - Everything for the Musician|date=5 February 2013|work=musicroom.com|access-date=22 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201033000/http://www.musicroom.com/blog/an-exclusive-interview-with-ola-gjeilo-19828|archive-date=1 December 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Gjeilo studied classical composition with [[Wolfgang Plagge]].<ref name="musicroom"/> In his undergraduate career, Gjeilo studied at the Norwegian Academy of Music (1999–2001), transferred to the [[Juilliard School]] (2001), and studied at the [[Royal College of Music]], London (2002–2004) to receive a bachelor's degree in composition.<ref>Singers.com, “Ola Gjeilo,” United Singers International: CGI Copyright, 2014, accessed September 30, 2014, http://www.singers.com/composers/Ola-Gjeilo/.</ref>  He continued his education at Juilliard (2004–06) where he received his master's degree in 2006, also in composition. From 2009 to 2010, Gjeilo was composer-in-residence for [[Phoenix Chorale]].<ref name="musicroom"/>

He currently resides in Manhattan, working as a freelance composer. He is currently composer-in-residence with [[Distinguished Concerts International New York|DCINY]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dciny.org/ola-gjeilo/|title=Ola Gjeilo|access-date=Mar 17, 2019}}</ref> and Albany Pro Musica.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.timesunion.com/entertainment/article/Albany-Pro-Musica-concert-to-mark-debut-of-12305174.php|title=Albany Pro Musica concert to mark debut of partnership with composer|first=Amy|last=Biancolli|date=Oct 25, 2017|website=Times Union|access-date=Mar 17, 2019}}</ref>
