'''WRLD''' (95.3 [[FM broadcasting|FM]], "95.3 Smooth R&B") is a [[radio station]] broadcasting an [[urban adult contemporary]] music format.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.arbitron.com |title=Station Information Profile |work=Arbitron}}</ref> Licensed to [[Valley, Alabama]], United States, the station serves the [[Columbus, Georgia]], area.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fcc.gov/fcc-bin/fmq?call=WRLD |title=WRLD-FM Facility Record |work=United States [[Federal Communications Commission]], audio division}}</ref> The station is currently owned by PMB Broadcasting, LLC. The station features programming from [[Westwood One (1976–2011)|Westwood One]].  Its studios are co-located with four other sister stations on Wynnton Road in Columbus east of downtown, and its transmitter is located south of Valley.

==History==
This station's original [[construction permit]] was granted by the [[Federal Communications Commission]] on March 4, 1992.<ref name="fccp">{{cite web |work=FCC Media Bureau |title=Application Search Details (BPH-19900918MA) |url=http://fjallfoss.fcc.gov/cgi-bin/ws.exe/prod/cdbs/pubacc/prod/app_det.pl?Application_id=152448 |date=1992-03-04}}</ref> The new station was assigned the call letters WUAF on April 23, 1992.<ref name="fcc1">{{cite web |title=Call Sign History |url=http://fjallfoss.fcc.gov/cgi-bin/ws.exe/prod/cdbs/pubacc/prod/call_hist.pl?Facility_id=52040&Callsign=WRLD-FM |work=FCC Media Bureau CDBS Public Access Database}}</ref> The station's callsign was changed to WRLD-FM on February 22, 1994.<ref name="fcc1"/> After several delays, WRLD-FM received its [[broadcast license|license to cover]] on April 5, 1996.<ref name="fcc5">{{cite web |work=FCC Media Bureau |title=Application Search Details (BLH-19950921KE) |url=http://fjallfoss.fcc.gov/cgi-bin/ws.exe/prod/cdbs/pubacc/prod/app_det.pl?Application_id=213964 |date=1996-04-05}}</ref>

In May 1999, Pearce Broadcasting Company, Inc., reached an agreement to sell WRLD-FM to McClure Broadcasting, Inc.<ref name="fcc9">{{cite web |work=FCC Media Bureau |title=Application Search Details (BALH-19990527GI) |url=http://fjallfoss.fcc.gov/cgi-bin/ws.exe/prod/cdbs/pubacc/prod/app_det.pl?Application_id=285613 |date=1999-09-21}}</ref> The FCC approved the deal on July 22, 1999, and the transaction was consummated on September 21, 1999.<ref name="fcc9"/>

In December 2002, McClure Broadcasting, Inc., (Chuck McClure Jr., president) reached an agreement to sell WRLD-FM to Archway Broadcasting Group (Al Vicente, president/CEO) through their affiliate ABG Georgia Licenses, LLC.<ref name="fccx">{{cite web |work=FCC Media Bureau |title=Application Search Details (BALH-20021220AAL) |url=http://fjallfoss.fcc.gov/cgi-bin/ws.exe/prod/cdbs/pubacc/prod/app_det.pl?Application_id=627393 |date=2003-04-25}}</ref> This was part of a four-station deal, along with [[WRCG]], [[WCGQ]], and [[WKCN]], for a reported combined sale price of $15 million.<ref>{{cite news |date=2003-01-20 |url=http://www.broadcastingcable.com/article/CA271324.html?q=WRLD |work=Broadcasting & Cable |title=Changing Hands - 1/20/2003}}</ref> The deal was approved by the FCC on February 21, 2003, and the transaction was consummated on April 25, 2003.<ref name="fccx"/> At the time of the sale, WRLD-FM carried an [[oldies]] music format.

On July 31, 2008, local investment group PMB Broadcasting LLC (headed up by Jim Martin) purchased this station along with Columbus-area sister stations WRCG, WCGQ, and WKCN from Archway Broadcasting Group LLC for a reported sale price of $7.2 million.<ref>{{cite news |date=2008-08-18 |url=http://www.broadcastingcable.com/article/CA6588011.html?q=WRLD |work=Broadcasting & Cable |title=Deals - 8/18/2008}}</ref>

On September 6, 2010, the station shortened its call sign from WRLD-FM to simply WRLD.

On June 6, 2014, WRLD shifted their format from oldies to classic hits.

On August 12, 2016, WRLD changed their format from classic hits to classic country, branded as "95.3 Kissin' Country" (swapping formats with [[WBOJ]] 1270 AM Columbus, GA.<ref>[https://radioinsight.com/blog/headlines/107702/boomer-and-country-legends-swap-spots-in-columbus-ga/ Boomer and Country Legends Swap Spots in Columbus, GA] Radioinsight - August 12, 2016</ref>

On September 1, 2019, WRLD changed their format from classic country to urban adult contemporary, branded as "95.3 Smooth R&B".<ref>[https://radioinsight.com/headlines/180221/wrld-flips-to-smooth-rb/ WRLD Flips to Smooth R&B] Radioinsight - September 1, 2019</ref>
