'''Trevor Powers''' (born March 18, 1989) is an American musician from [[Boise, Idaho]]. Powers was active as '''Youth Lagoon''' from 2010 up until he formally retired the project in 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2016/02/trevor-powers-announces-end-of-youth-lagoon/|title=Trevor Powers announces end of Youth Lagoon|date=2016-02-01|work=Consequence of Sound|access-date=2018-06-15|language=en-US}}</ref> Powers announced his return with his new self-titled project in May of 2018.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/05/trevor-powers-fka-youth-lagoon-returns-with-solo-single-playwright-stream/|title=Trevor Powers (fka Youth Lagoon) returns with solo single, “Playwright”: Stream|date=2018-05-02|work=Consequence of Sound|access-date=2018-06-15|language=en-US}}</ref> Powers' music has been described as [[Electronic music|electronic]] and [[Experimental music|experimental]] with elements of [[Pop music|pop]].

==As Youth Lagoon==
Powers was active as Youth Lagoon from 2010 to 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/festivals/chicago/2012/lineup/45-youth-lagoon/|title=The Pitchfork Guide to Festivals|date=2014-04-10|publisher=Pitchfork|accessdate=2015-11-29}}</ref> Youth Lagoon's music was described as [[neo-psychedelia]],<ref name="axs">{{cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/youth-lagoon-announce-dates-for-an-upcoming-north-american-and-europea-63431|title=Youth Lagoon Announces Dates|last1=Gabel|first1=Sue|date=August 11, 2015}}</ref> and included elements of [[Americana music|Americana]] and [[Experimental music|experimental]].<ref name="autogenerated1" />

Youth Lagoon's debut album, ''[[The Year of Hibernation]]'', was released on [[Fat Possum Records]] on September 27, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/15873-the-year-of-hibernation/ |title=Youth Lagoon: The Year of Hibernation &#124; Album Reviews |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2011-09-29 |accessdate=2015-11-29}}</ref> Based on minimalism and hypnotic ambience melded with atmospheric and electronic elements, the debut explored themes such as psychological dysphoria and mental distress.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Youth+Lagoon |title=Youth Lagoon — Free listening, videos, concerts, stats and pictures at |publisher=Last.fm |date= |accessdate=2015-11-29}}</ref>

Powers' second album, ''[[Wondrous Bughouse]],'' was released on March 5, 2013 by Fat Possum.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.spin.com/2013/03/youth-lagoon-wondrous-bughouse-fat-possum/ |title=Youth Lagoon, ‘Wondrous Bughouse’ (Fat Possum) |publisher=SPIN |date= |accessdate=2015-11-29}}</ref> It was spawned from what he described as "becoming more fascinated with the human psyche and where the spiritual meets the physical world."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://indiemusicfilter.com/tag/wondrous-bughouse |title=wondrous bughouse Archives |publisher=Indie Music Filter |date= |accessdate=2015-11-29}}</ref> During the time he composed the album, Powers became intrigued with the metaphysical universe and blending those ideas with pop music.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aux.tv/2013/01/track-of-the-week-dropla-by-youth-lagoon/ |title=Track Of The Week: "Dropla" by Youth Lagoon |publisher=AUX.TV |date=2013-01-18 |accessdate=2015-11-29 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208142818/http://www.aux.tv/2013/01/track-of-the-week-dropla-by-youth-lagoon/ |archivedate=2015-12-08 }}</ref><ref>Carrie Battan, [http://pitchfork.com/news/49041-youth-lagoon-announces-new-album/ "Youth Lagoon Announces New Album,"] [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]], January 4, 2013.</ref>

Powers' tour in support of ''Wondrous Bughouse'' was cut short due to the death of a close friend.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Marketti|first1=Anna|title=Interview: Trevor Powers of Youth Lagoon|url=http://soundofboston.com/interview-trevor-powers-youth-lagoon/|website=Sound of Boston|accessdate=22 November 2015}}</ref>

On November 12, 2014, Powers announced, via [[Twitter]], that writing for his third album had been finished. Recording started in January 2015. The July 10, 2015 release of "The Knower", a free single-sided 7" single, marked the announcement of his third album, ''[[Savage Hills Ballroom]]'', released on September 25, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/60330-youth-lagoon-announces-new-album-savage-hills-ballroom/ |title=Youth Lagoon Announces New Album Savage Hills Ballroom &#124; News |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2015-07-10 |accessdate=2015-11-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Henry |first=Dusty |url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2015/09/album-review-youth-lagoon-savage-hills-ballroom/ |title=Youth Lagoon – Savage Hills Ballroom &#124; Album Reviews |publisher=Consequence of Sound |date=2015-09-21 |accessdate=2015-11-29}}</ref>

In July 2015, Youth Lagoon announced a US tour in support of ''Savage Hills Ballroom''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Geslani|first1=Michelle|title=Youth Lagoon announces US tour|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2015/07/youth-lagoon-announces-us-tour/|website=Consequence of Sound|accessdate=22 November 2015}}</ref>

On February 1, 2016, Powers announced on Twitter that his Youth Lagoon project was concluding.<ref>Alex Young, [http://consequenceofsound.net/2016/02/trevor-powers-announces-end-of-youth-lagoon/ "Trevor Powers announces end of Youth Lagoon,"] [[Consequence of Sound]], February 1, 2016.</ref>  

==Under his own name==
On May 2, 2018, Powers announced his return to music by issuing a personal letter<ref name=":0" /> along with the single "Playwright", his first song release since 2015. The letter elaborated on Power's self-titled project, highlighting the project as a new work.

On May 20, 2018, he announced that he would release his first album under his own name, ''[[Mulberry Violence]]'', in late 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/05/trevor-powers-announces-new-album-mulberry-violence-and-tour-dates/|title=Trevor Powers (fka Youth Lagoon) Announces New Album 'Mulberry Violence'|date=2018-05-30|work=Spin|access-date=2018-06-15}}</ref> Powers released two more singles from the forthcoming album, "Ache" and "Plaster Saint" with a newly penned letter providing more insight into the project:<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/trevor-powers-youth-lagoon-announces-debut-solo-lp-tour-shares-two-songs/|title=Trevor Powers (Youth Lagoon) announces debut solo LP & tour, shares two songs|website=BrooklynVegan|language=en-US|access-date=2018-06-15}}</ref>

On July 29, 2020, Powers surprise-released his second album as Trevor Powers, titled ''[[Capricorn (Trevor Powers album)|Capricorn]],'' along with limited-edition cassettes and booklet designed by Los Angeles-based designer Collin Fletcher, who also designed the artwork for the album. <ref>{{Cite web|last=Yoo|first=Noah|title=Trevor Powers Surprise-Releases New Album Capricorn|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/trevor-powers-surprise-releases-new-album-capricorn-listen/|access-date=2020-08-19|website=Pitchfork|language=en-us}}</ref>
