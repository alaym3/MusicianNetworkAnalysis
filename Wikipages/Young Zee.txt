'''Dewayne Battle''' (born December 24, 1970),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/_r7pm_EWaJ/|title=It's My Born Day|website=Instagram|access-date=December 24, 2015}}</ref>  known professionally as '''Young Zee''', is an American underground hip hop [[emcee]]. He was a former member of the [[Outsidaz]], alongside [[Pacewon]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/83097-Young-Zee|title=Young Zee|website=Discogs|access-date=December 23, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==

===Outsidaz===
The Outsidaz, a large hip hop crew that became the first act signed to [[Ruffhouse Records|Ruff Life Records]], produced several singles and released a record in the mid-'90s. The group including Young Zee had guest vocals on the [[Fugees]]' song "Cowboys" in 1996. Zee made an appearance on [[Rah Digga]]'s first solo album in 1999. The group released ''[[Night Life (Outsidaz album)|Night Life]]'' (2000) and ''[[The Bricks]]'' (2001). In 2002 his solo track, "[[8 Mile soundtrack|That's My Nigga fo' Real]]", was included on the hit soundtrack to [[Eminem]]'s film [[8 Mile (film)|8 Mile]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/young-zee-mn0000688232/biography|title=Young Zee {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=December 23, 2016}}</ref>

===Solo artist===
Due to a history of collaboration, Zee and fans expected him to be signed to Eminem's [[Shady Records]] label under [[Aftermath Records|Aftermath]]/[[Interscope records]], but instead he signed as a solo artist to [[D12]] members Kuniva and [[Kon Artis]]' label Runyon Ave Records, but he made the only related appearance in "Commercial Break" on ''[[D12 World]].''{{cn|date=May 2021}}

In 2005, Young Zee appeared on [[Casual (rapper)|Casual]]'s ''[[Smash Rockwell]]'' album. In 2009, he was featuring on Chicago rap duo [[Saurus and Bones]]'s debut album ''[[Mind Like Mine]]''. Zee collaborated with [[D12|The Dirty Dozen]] on their 2011 mixtape [[D12 discography|''Return Of The Dozen Vol. 2'']]. Also in 2011, he performed on [[Jedi Mind Tricks]]'s ''[[Violence Begets Violence]]'' album. In 2012, Young Zee and [[Mr. Green (record producer)|Mr. Green]] released ''[[One Crazy Weekend]]'' featuring former Outsidaz members.

== Personal life ==
He had a relationship with fellow rapper [[Rah Digga]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,41902,00.html|title=Rah Digga Ready To Blow Up|last=Farley|first=Christopher John|date=March 26, 2000|newspaper=Time|issn=0040-781X|access-date=December 27, 2016}}</ref> The couple has a daughter named Sativa, born in 1997.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0226497/bio|title=Rah Digga|website=IMDb|access-date=December 27, 2016}}</ref> Young Zee has a son, Krash Battle (born on September 9, 1992),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/krashbattle|title=Karma (@KrashBattle) {{!}} Twitter|website=twitter.com|access-date=December 27, 2016}}</ref> who is also a rapper.
