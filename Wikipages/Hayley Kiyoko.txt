'''Hayley Kiyoko Alcroft''' (born April 3, 1991) is an American singer, songwriter, dancer and actress. <!--occupation(s) integral to artist's notability per reliable sources--> Beginning dance lessons as a child, Kiyoko's interest in entertaining led to her working as a child model and actress. She appeared in a variety of films including ''[[Scooby-Doo (film series)|Scooby-Doo!]]'' film series (2009–2010), ''[[Lemonade Mouth (film)|Lemonade Mouth]]'' (2011), ''[[Blue Lagoon: The Awakening]]'' (2012), ''[[Jem and the Holograms (film)|Jem and the Holograms]]'' (2015), ''[[Insidious: Chapter 3]]'' (2015), and ''[[XOXO (2016 film)|XOXO]]'' (2016). Alongside her film roles, she also held a recurring role in the TV series ''[[The Fosters (2013 TV series)#Webisodes|The Fosters]]'' (2014) and a lead role on ''[[CSI: Cyber]]'' (2015–2016) and ''[[Five Points (TV series)|Five Points]]'' (2018–2019).

Kiyoko was a founding member of [[The Stunners]] in 2007. The group supported [[Justin Bieber]]’s [[My World Tour]], before disbanding in 2011. After this, Kiyoko issued three solo extended plays: ''[[A Belle to Remember (EP)|A Belle to Remember]]'' (2013), ''[[This Side of Paradise (EP)|This Side of Paradise]]'' (2015, which includes the single "[[Girls Like Girls]]"), and ''[[Citrine (EP)|Citrine]]'' (2016).

Following the singles "Sleepover", "[[Feelings (Hayley Kiyoko song)|Feelings]]", and "Curious" she released her debut studio album ''[[Expectations (Hayley Kiyoko album)|Expectations]]'' on March 30, 2018. The album reached the top 20 of the charts in the [[United States]], [[Canada]], and [[Australia]]. She has also created a comic book, with the help of Naomi Franquiz and Marla Vazquez, based on her song "[[Gravel to Tempo]]".

Following the release of ''Expectations'', Hayley released the single "I Wish"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kBQB3H64HYA |title=Hayley Kiyoko - I Wish (Official Video) |website=YouTube |date=July 18, 2019}}</ref> in July 2019, the first track from her fourth EP, ''[[I'm Too Sensitive for This Shit]]'', which was released on January 14, 2020.

==Personal life==
===Sexuality and musical influence===
Kiyoko identifies as a [[lesbian]]. She knew she was attracted to girls when she was six years old, [[coming out]] to her parents in the sixth grade.<ref>Tan, Sara. Bustle, October 18, 2018, "[https://www.bustle.com/p/why-hayley-kiyokos-brows-mean-so-much-to-her-12597262 Why Hayley Kiyoko's Brows Mean So Much To Her]".</ref><ref name=RollingStone>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/how-hayley-kiyoko-became-lesbian-jesus-695667/|title=#20gayteen: The Year of Hayley Kiyoko|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|website=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=July 20, 2018|access-date=July 20, 2018}}</ref> She had a crush on a female swim teacher, and upon realizing she liked girls, she grew up struggling with those feelings, fearing rejection and judgment if she came out. Kiyoko wanted to inspire confidence in young people dealing with the same struggles.<ref name="Paper">{{cite web |url= http://www.papermag.com/hailey-kiyoko-outspoken-2143268093.html |title=Hayley Kiyoko On Her Sexuality and Finding a Voice |work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]] |author= Hayley Kiyoko |date= December 12, 2016}}</ref> Kiyoko's music is focused on her story and emotions she's experienced while coming to terms with her identity. The "This Side of Paradise" music video focused on her struggles with expressing her true self<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BaPyMNsDuKv|title=If any of you have been to my shows , you know the true story behind this song.|author=hayleykiyoko|website=Instagram|date=October 14, 2017|access-date=August 20, 2018}}</ref> and the "Gravel to Tempo" music video draws on her experiences with having crushes on girls while growing up.<ref name="Refinery29"/> In a 2016 interview, she expressed her frustration about not connecting to people the way she wanted. When Lily May-Young, one of the co-writers for "Girls Like Girls", asked Kiyoko about something about herself that no one knew and she was afraid to sing about, Kiyoko wanted to sing about the fact that she likes girls, but was struggling to be out about that.<ref name=PAPER2018/> [[Tegan and Sara]] and [[Katy Perry]]'s "[[I Kissed a Girl]]" were Kiyoko's turning point and inspiration to turn to pop music.<ref name=PAPER2018>{{cite web|url=http://www.papermag.com/hayley-kiyoko-pride-2577280716.html|title=Hayley Kiyoko Knows What She Needs|last=Love Michael|first=Michael|website=PAPER Magazine|date=June 12, 2018|access-date=August 17, 2018}}</ref><ref name=RollingStone/> Through her music, Kiyoko works to normalize lesbian relationships in a society and music industry that she sees as being very [[heteronormative]]:
{{cquote|"If you see two girls falling in love and normalizing that, then [people] can go, 'I can fall in love, too. I can be that person. I can look like that. I can get a girl that looks like that.' If they see that, then they can believe it. It's just how we are."<ref name=b55/>}}
After seeing the impact her music has on her fans, Kiyoko regretted not being open about her sexuality sooner.<ref name=BBImpact>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7832880/hayley-kiyoko-new-album-impact-lgbt-community|title=Hayley Kiyoko on Being Open With Her Fans, Her Impact on the LGBT Community|last=Kaplan|first=Ilana|date=June 14, 2017|access-date=August 18, 2018|website=Billboard}}</ref>

Her song, "Mercy/Gatekeeper" talks about the depression she dealt with after suffering from her concussion.<ref name=Harper/>

===LGBT advocacy===
Since publicly coming out, Kiyoko has been an advocate for LGBT rights and is widely considered to be a [[gay icon]] by her fans.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/feb/22/hayley-kiyoko-on-her-lesbian-pop-this-is-bigger-than-i-thought-it-was|title=How Hayley Kiyoko became pop's 'lesbian Jesus'|last=Pollard|first=Alexandra|date=February 22, 2018|access-date=August 18, 2018|website=The Guardian}}</ref> Her music videos highlight her own experiences and various LGBT-related issues. The music video for [[Citrine (EP)|"One Bad Night"]] raised awareness of violence against [[Trans woman|transgender women.]]<ref name=BBImpact/> For Pride Month 2017, Kiyoko partnered with MeUndies to promote their "Celebrate" campaign. Each pair of underwear sold had a portion of the profit donated to the [[Los Angeles LGBT Center]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/lifestyle/7809338/hayley-kiyoko-meundies-pride-collection-exclusive|title=Hayley Kiyoko Fronts MeUndies 'Pride' Campaign in Support of the Los Angeles LGBT Center: Exclusive|last=Saunders|first=Nicole|website=Billboard|date=June 1, 2017|access-date=August 18, 2018}}</ref> In 2018, Kiyoko critiqued [[Rita Ora]]'s song, "[[Girls (Rita Ora song)|Girls]]", for its depiction of same-sex attraction and harmful implications for the LGBT community. Ora later apologized for the song's implications.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8460704/rita-ora-girls-controversy-interview|title=Rita Ora Reflects on 'Girls' Controversy: 'There's Not One Way to Come Out'|last=Real|first=Evan|website=Billboard|date=June 12, 2018|access-date=August 18, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/HayleyKiyoko/status/995026145484689408|title=Real talk|website=Twitter|date=May 11, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/hayley-kiyoko-rita-ora-charli-xcxs-girls-does-more-harm-than-good-for-the-lgbtq-community/|title=Hayley Kiyoko: Rita Ora, Charli XCX's "Girls" "Does More Harm Than Good for the LGBTQ+ Community"|last=Kim|first=Michelle|website=Pitchfork|date=May 11, 2018|access-date=August 18, 2018}}</ref> After 78 bras were thrown at Kiyoko during the Expectations Tour, she and Neara Russell donated them to I Support The Girls, an organization that supports homeless women, to support homeless LGBT youth.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bkl2603F0bN/|title=If you're 'curious', @hayleykiyoko exceeded our 'expectations'. . .|website=Instagram|author=isupportthegirls|date=June 28, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BklyUAADnKU/|title=HEY BRA THROWERS. Remember all of those bras you threw at me on tour?!?!|website=Instagram|author=hayleykiyoko|date=June 28, 2018}}</ref> In her acceptance speech for Push Artist of the Year at the [[2018 MTV Video Music Awards]], Kiyoko dedicated her win to queer women of color.<ref name=Push/> She marched in a Pride parade for the first time, attending the 2019 World Pride Parade in New York City as the float ambassador for W Hotels Worldwide.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.papermag.com/hayley-kiyoko-w-hotels-pride-2639063349.html?rebelltitem=17#rebelltitem17 |title = Hayley Kiyoko Rides for Pride|date = July 3, 2019}}</ref>
