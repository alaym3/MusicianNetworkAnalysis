'''Dropout Kings''' (formerly '''Phoenix Down''') is an American [[nu metal]] band from [[Phoenix, Arizona]], United States, that formed in 2016. They are currently signed to [[Suburban Noize Records]]<ref>https://undergroundhiphopblog.com/news/dropout-kings-sign-to-suburban-noize-records/</ref> and have released two albums: ''Audiodope and GlitchGang,'' which were released August 10th, 2018 and April 6th, 2021.

==History==
Dropout Kings was founded by Adam Ramey after his previous band [[The Bad Chapter]] broke up in 2015.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dropout Kings |url=http://www.arteryglobal.com/artists/portfolio/dropout-kings/ |website=Artery Global |accessdate=August 6, 2018}}</ref> As a way to stay active musically, he approached rapper [[Eddie Wellz]] to do a cover of [[Linkin Park|Linkin Park's]] “Lying From You” after seeing a video of Wellz performing on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Dyroff |first1=Denny |title=The Coatesville Times The Downingtown Times Contact Us Home D: Arts & Entertainment On Stage: Want to go seriously retro? Check out Blackmore's Night |url=http://chescotimes.com/?p=26241 |website=The Times of Chester County |accessdate=August 6, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180719215644/http://chescotimes.com/?p=26241 |archive-date=July 19, 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> Due to a positive reception, Ramey approached previous members of The Bad Chapter; Trevor Norgren and later Staig Flynn and Rob Sebastian<ref>{{cite web |last1=Crane |first1=Matt |title=Ex-Attack Attack! vocalist Phil Druyor starts new band, releases new song |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/ex_attack_attack_vocalist_phil_druyor_starts_new_band_releases_new_song/ |website=Alternative Press |accessdate=August 6, 2018 |date=2014-07-11 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191009110218/https://www.altpress.com/news/ex_attack_attack_vocalist_phil_druyor_starts_new_band_releases_new_song/ |archive-date=October 9, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> and previous bandmate Chucky Guzman from We The Collectors<ref>{{cite web |title=We The Collectors |url=https://wethecollectors.bandcamp.com/ |website=bandcamp |accessdate=August 6, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191009110220/https://wethecollectors.bandcamp.com/ |archive-date=October 9, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> to form a new band called Phoenix Down, which by 2017 had changed its name to Dropout Kings.

On April 6, 2018, it was announced that Dropout Kings signed with [[Napalm Records]], & the band released the music video for '"NVM", their first single from their debut album ''AudioDope'' the same day.<ref>{{cite web |title=News Blog :: DROPOUT KINGS - Sign with Napalm Records! |url=http://label.napalmrecords.com/blog/?entryID=1345 |website=Napalm Records |accessdate=April 6, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180608194743/http://label.napalmrecords.com/blog/?entryID=1345 |archive-date=June 8, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On June 8, 2018, the band released the music video for "Scratch & Claw", the second single off of ''AudioDope''.<ref>{{cite web|title=DROPOUT KINGS Release Official Music Video For "Scratch & Claw" Album Cover Artwork & Track Listing!|url=https://heavymag.com.au/dropout-kings-release-official-music-video-scratch-claw-album-cover-artwork-track-listing/|website=HeavyMag|accessdate=June 8, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190117100543/https://heavymag.com.au/dropout-kings-release-official-music-video-scratch-claw-album-cover-artwork-track-listing/|archive-date=January 17, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> ''AudioDope'' was released on August 10, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=ALBUM REVIEW: Dropout Kings – Audiodope|url=http://www.deadpress.co.uk/album-review-dropout-kings-audiodope/|website=Dead Press|accessdate=August 19, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181215023229/http://www.deadpress.co.uk/album-review-dropout-kings-audiodope/|archive-date=December 15, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> ''AudioDope'' reached 2 US Charts, #52-Top New Artist Albums & #112-Record Label Independent Current Albums.<ref name="insta"/>

In July–August 2018, the band toured the United States and Canada with [[OTEP]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Dropout Kings announced as direct support for OTEP Summer Tour|url=https://www.cbent1.com/single-post/2018/05/15/Dropout-Kings-announced-as-direct-support-for-OTEP-Summer-Tour|website=CB Entertainment|accessdate=May 15, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191009110220/https://www.cbent1.com/single-post/2018/05/15/Dropout-Kings-announced-as-direct-support-for-OTEP-Summer-Tour|archive-date=October 9, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> During the [[Otep]] tour, the band announced that they were managed by [[Dez Fafara]], the lead vocalist for [[DevilDriver]] & [[Coal Chamber]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theoraclemanagement.com/dropout-kings/|title=Dropout Kings|date=2018-07-30|website=The Oracle Management|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190623165631/http://theoraclemanagement.com/dropout-kings/|archive-date=2019-06-23|url-status=live}}</ref>

In February–March 2019, the band toured with [[Outline in Color]], [[Deadships]] and [[Dead Crown]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Outline in Color Tour Dates|url=http://outlineincolor.com/tour|website=OutlineinColor.com|accessdate=January 28, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190115213757/http://outlineincolor.com/tour|archive-date=January 15, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

On March 19, 2019, [[Dayshell]] featured the band on their single “KOMBAT."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/kombat-feat-dropout-kings-single/1455331524|title=Kombat (feat. Dropout Kings) - Single by Dayshell|accessdate=Apr 18, 2020|via=music.apple.com}}</ref>

In May–June 2019, the band joined [[Crazy Town]] as direct support on their 20th Anniversary Tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freemanpromotions.com/dropout-kings-kick-off-us-tour-dates-w-crazy-town/|title=DROPOUT KINGS Kick Off US Tour Dates w/ Crazy Town {{!}} Freeman Promotions|website=www.freemanpromotions.com|access-date=2019-06-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190623164134/http://www.freemanpromotions.com/dropout-kings-kick-off-us-tour-dates-w-crazy-town/|archive-date=2019-06-23|url-status=live}}</ref>

On May 26, 2019, it was announced that the band would be playing the Main Stage at the 20th Annual Soopa [[Gathering of the Juggalos]] on August 3, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.juggalogathering.com/dropout-kings/|title=dropout-kings|date=2019-05-27|website=Gathering of the Juggalos|language=en|access-date=2019-06-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190623164130/https://www.juggalogathering.com/dropout-kings/|archive-date=2019-06-23|url-status=live}}</ref>

On April 6, 2021, it was announced that Dropout Kings had signed to [[Suburban Noize Records]].<ref>https://undergroundhiphopblog.com/news/dropout-kings-sign-to-suburban-noize-records/</ref>

On June 15, 2021, Dropout Kings embarked on their first headlining tour.
