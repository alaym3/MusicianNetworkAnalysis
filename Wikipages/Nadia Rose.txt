'''Nadia Rose''' (born 11 June 1993) is a British rapper and songwriter from [[Croydon]], London.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/jun/25/nadia-rose-british-rapper-boom-station-squad|title=Nadia Rose: 'There's no point doing this if you're not confident'|first=Alex|last=Macpherson|date=25 June 2016|publisher=|via=The Guardian}}</ref> 

Rose studied Music and Music Management at university.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-38371449|title=Nadia Rose: BBC Sound of 2017 interview|last=Savage|first=Mark|date=4 January 2017|work=BBC News|access-date=7 July 2017}}</ref> She is a cousin of [[Stormzy|Michael "Stormzy" Omari]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.m-magazine.co.uk/features/interviews/interview-nadia-rose-3/|title=Interview: Nadia Rose - M Magazine|date=2019-10-08|website=M magazine: PRS for Music online magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-10}}</ref> 

Rose released her debut EP ''Highly Flammable'' on 13 January 2017 following the successful singles "Station", "BOOM", and "D.F.W.T".<ref name="auto" />  Rose is represented by Sony Music Group under the subsidiary [[Relentless Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sonymusic.co.uk/artists/nadia-rose|title=Nadia Rose {{!}} Sony Music UK – Artists|website=Sony Music UK|language=en|access-date=2019-12-10}}</ref> She came fifth in the BBC's 'Sound of 2017' shortlist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/2xpPCC0j9nsX0yQHkQ0YlHX/the-longlist|title=2017, BBC Music Sound Of - The Longlist - BBC Music|website=BBC|access-date=7 July 2017}}</ref> In 2018 she released the track WUT2 and appeared in a Stella McCartney catwalk show in Milan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/go/london/music/nadia-rose-interview-a-collaboration-with-my-cousin-stormzy-will-happen-a3908356.html|title=Nadia Rose's dream collaboration is Jamiroquai|date=2018-08-10|website=Evening Standard|language=en|access-date=2019-12-10}}</ref>

In 2019, Rose took part in an [[Operation Black Vote]] campaign to encourage people from ethnic minority communities to register to vote in the 2019 UK General Election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2019/nov/25/black-asian-change-election-register-to-vote|title=Black and Asian people can change this election: it’s urgent that we register now {{!}} Simon Woolley|last=Woolley|first=Simon|date=2019-11-25|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-12-10|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Rose also took part in the 2019 Grime4Corbyn campaign in support of the Labour Party and its leader Jeremy Corbyn.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://metro.co.uk/2019/11/24/grime4corbyn-relaunch-unexpected-boost-labour-11211588/|title=#Grime4Corbyn relaunch in unexpected boost for Labour|date=2019-11-24|website=Metro|language=en|access-date=2019-12-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/politics/article/47044/1/grime4corbyn-reviews-jeremy-corbyn-labour-party-transformative-manifesto|title=Grime4Corbyn reviews the Labour party’s transformative manifesto|last=Dazed|date=2019-12-03|website=Dazed|language=en|access-date=2019-12-10}}</ref> Rose wrote an article for Metro encouraging people to vote Labour in the election.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://metro.co.uk/2019/11/26/i-know-what-its-like-to-feel-your-vote-doesnt-count-but-heres-why-it-does-11219946/|title=I know what it's like to feel your vote doesn't count but here's why it does|last=Rose|first=Nadia|date=2019-11-26|website=Metro|language=en|access-date=2019-12-10}}</ref>

In 2020, Rose revealed that she had been unable to release new music because of her label.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Savage|first=Mark|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-51704533|title=When record contracts go wrong|date=2020-03-02|work=BBC News|access-date=2020-03-02|language=en-GB}}</ref> Rose collaborated with [[Melanie C]] on the track "Fearless" for Melanie C's self-titled album, [[Melanie C (album)|Melanie C]].<ref name="spice" /> The music video had a world premiere on 16 September 2020. Of the collaboration, Rose stated: "“It’s no secret that I’m a super [[Spice Girls]] fan so this whole experience has been very surreal."<ref name="spice">{{cite web |last1=Spanos |first1=Brittany |title=Melanie C Recruits Nadia Rose for New Single 'Fearless' |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/melanie-c-nadia-rose-fearless-1060909/ |website=Rolling Stone |accessdate=16 September 2020 |date=16 September 2020}}</ref>
