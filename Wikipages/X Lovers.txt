'''X Lovers''' is a [[Los Angeles]]-based pop music duo consisting of London Jackson and Jacob Ames, who go professionally by their first names, London and Jake.

London (guitar, vocals, songwriting)<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.outnowmagazine.com/x-lovers-release-sweatshirt-single | title = X lovers release new track & music video for "Sweatshirt" | publisher = Out Now | access-date = 18 December 2019 }}</ref> and Jacob (production) met as fourth-grade classmates in Nevada City, California. They began playing music together as children, citing [[Green Day]] and [[Kanye West]] as major inspirations, and formed a punk band called Opposition.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/electronic-pop-duo-x-lovers-yearns-for-love-in | title = Electronic Pop Duo X Lovers Yearns For Love in "Colder When It Rains" Video | publisher = Ones to Watch | last = Yung | first = Alyssa | date = 7 May 2019 | access-date = 12 December 2019 }}</ref>

They continued to write and perform through their school years, organizing and playing their own house and street shows.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/x-lovers-is-a-shared-childhood-dream-for-world | title = X Lovers Is a Shared Childhood Dream for World Domination | publisher = Ones to Watch | last = Polo | first = Maxamillion | date = 26 June 2019 | access-date = 12 December 2019 }}</ref> Max Mann was in the band too, but he left because the band was going in directions he didn't love. He had been a part of their tours back in high school.

By the time they graduated from high school they had played over 150 shows and saved enough money to move to [[Los Angeles]]<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/x-lovers-sweatshirt-is-a-sweet-somber-song-to | title = X Lovers' "Sweatshirt" Is a Sweet, Somber Song to Curl Up in | publisher = Ones to Watch | last = Kato | first = Precious | date = 6 November 2019 | access-date = 18 December 2019 }}</ref> and launch their professional career.

Settling in [[Hollywood]], they continued writing and recording. They gained notoriety in 2018 for producing two tracks, "Human" and "Would I," for [[Maggie Lindemann]]<ref>{{cite web | url = https://genius.com/Maggie-lindemann-human-lyrics | title = Human: Maggie Lindemann | publisher = Genius | access-date = 12 December 2019 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.chordzone.org/2018/12/maggie-lindemann-would-i-chords-and.html | title = Maggie Lindemann: Would I Chords and Lyrics | publisher = ChordZone | access-date = 12 December 2019 }}</ref> and posting their own acoustic originals and covers on [[SoundCloud]].

In late 2018, [[Logic (rapper)|Logic]] manager Chris Zarou's Visionary Records<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/logic-manager-chris-zarou-launches-sony-music-imprint-visionary-records/ | title = LOGIC MANAGER CHRIS ZAROU LAUNCHES SONY MUSIC IMPRINT, VISIONARY RECORDS | publisher = Music Business Worldwide | last = Stassen | first = Murray | date = 24 January 2019 | access-date = 19 December 2019 }}</ref> signed them to a record deal in conjunction with [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.musicconnection.com/x-lovers-signs-with-visionary-records-rca-records/ | title = X LOVERS SIGNS WITH VISIONARY RECORDS/RCA RECORDS | publisher = Ones to Watch | last = Yung | first = Alyssa | date = 2 May 2019 | access-date = 12 December 2019 }}</ref> They were Visionary's first signing after Visionary partnered with Sony Music/RCA.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://hitsdailydouble.com/news&id=316262 | title = RCA'S IN LOVE WITH VISIONARY'S X LOVERS | publisher = Hits Daily Double | date = 2 May 2019 | access-date = 12 December 2019 }}</ref>

Through 2019 the duo released a series of singles. "Colder When It Rains" netted more than half a million streams in less than a week upon release<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.idolator.com/7769050/new-find-x-lovers-impress-with-colder-when-it-rains | title = New Find: X Lovers Impress With "Colder When It Rains" | publisher = Idolator | last = Wass | first = Mike | date = 8 May 2019 | access-date = 18 December 2019 }}</ref> and two million total on [[Spotify]]. "King of Capulet" had nearly as many. The video for "Sweatshirt" premiered on The Fader on 5 November.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.thefader.com/2019/11/05/watch-x-lovers-video-for-sweatshirt | title = Watch X Lovers’ Video for "Sweatshirt" | publisher = The Fader | last = Denton | first = Jack | date = 5 November 2019 | access-date = 18 December 2019 }}</ref>

They released their first EP, the eight-song ''virgin,''<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/x-lovers-virgin-is-an-avalanche-of-emotional | title = X Lovers' 'virgin' Is an Avalanche of Emotional Depth | publisher = Ones to Watch | last = Bianca | first = Brown | date = 25 November 2019 | access-date = 13 December 2019 }}</ref> on Visionary/RCA on November 22, 2019.
