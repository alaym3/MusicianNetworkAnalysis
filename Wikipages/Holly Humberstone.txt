'''Holly Ffion Humberstone''' is a British musician from [[Grantham]], England. In 2021, she signed a record deal with [[Interscope Records|Interscope]] and [[Polydor Records]]. Her first EP following the signings, ''The Walls Are Way Too Thin'', was released on 12 November 2021.

==Personal life==
Humberstone is from [[Grantham, Lincolnshire|Grantham]] and is one of four sisters.<ref name="lincolnshirereporter">{{cite web |last1=Creaghan |first1=Connor |title=Grantham singer, 19, gets first gig at Glastonbury |url=https://lincolnshirereporter.co.uk/2019/04/grantham-singer-19-gets-first-gig-at-glastonbury/ |work = Lincolnshire Reporter | accessdate=18 August 2020}}</ref><ref name="dorkmagazine">{{cite web |title=Getting to know... Holly Humberstone |url=https://www.readdork.com/features/getting-to-know-holly-humberstone |work = [[Dork (magazine)|Dork]] | accessdate=18 August 2020}}</ref> She was previously studying at the [[Liverpool Institute for Performing Arts]].<ref name="lincolnshirereporter"/>

==Musical style and influences==
Humberstone cites [[Damien Rice]], [[Ben Howard]], [[Phoebe Bridgers]], and [[Haim (band)|HAIM]] as musical inspirations.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.indieisnotagenre.com/holly-humberstone-overkill/ |title=Holly Humberstone releases video for 'Overkill', announces debut EP |work=IINAG |accessdate=18 August 2020 |author=Taylor, Daniel}}</ref> Rice's debut album ''[[O (Damien Rice album)|O]]'' is considered by Humberstone to be her 'first favourite album'.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.noisetrend.com/a-conversation-with-holly-humberstone/ |title=A Conversation with Holly Humberstone |work=Noisetrend |accessdate=18 August 2020 |author=Devine, Anna Miles}}</ref>

==Career==
{{expand section|date=August 2020}}
Humberstone performed at [[Glastonbury Festival 2019]] on the BBC Introducing stage.<ref name="totalntertainment">{{cite web |title=Holly Humberstone Reveals Debut New Single |url=https://www.totalntertainment.com/music/holly-humberstone-reveals-debut-new-single/ |work =Total Ntertainment |accessdate=18 August 2020}}</ref>

Her debut single "Deep End", was released on 30 January 2020.<ref name="nmemagazine">{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Thomas |title=What's Your Band Called, Mate? Get to know Holly Humberstone |url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/whats-your-band-called-mate-get-to-know-holly-humberstone-2603629 |work=[[NME]] |accessdate=6 June 2020}}</ref> Her second single, "Falling Asleep at the Wheel", was released on 19 March, while her third single, "Overkill", was released on 26 June.<ref name="newyorktimes">{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/20/arts/music/playlist-dirty-projectors-perfume-genius.html |title=Dirty Projectors Also Covered John Lennon, Thankfully, and 10 More New Songs |work=[[The New York Times]] |accessdate=6 June 2020 |author=Caramanica, Joe}}</ref><ref name="americansongwriter">{{cite web |url=https://americansongwriter.com/holly-humberstone-wakes-up-on-new-single-falling-asleep-at-the-wheel/ |title=Holly Humberstone wakes up on new single 'Falling Asleep At The Wheel' |work=[[American Songwriter]] |accessdate=6 June 2020 |author=Vitagliano, Joe}}</ref><ref name="atwoodmagazine">{{cite web |url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/hhfw-holly-humberstone-falling-asleep-at-the-wheel-song-review/ |title=Artist to Watch: Holly Humberstone's second single "Falling Asleep at the Wheel" is a hauntingly intimate upheaval |work=[[Atwood Magazine]] |accessdate=6 June 2020 |author=Mosk, Mitch}}</ref><ref name="eonline">{{cite web |url=https://www.eonline.com/uk/news/1132952/the-mixtape-presents-the-weeknd-kelsea-ballerini-halsey-and-more-new-music-musts/ |title=The MixtapE! presents The Weeknd, Kelsea Ballerini, Halsey and more new music musts |work=[[E!]] |accessdate=6 June 2020 |author=Nilles, Billy}}</ref><ref name="billboard">{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9342956/quarantine-playlist-new-pop-songs-to-discover/ |title=20 Great Songs To Discover While You're Quarantined |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=6 June 2020 |author1=Ginsberg, Gab |author2=Lipshutz, Jason}}</ref><ref name="dorkmagazine2">{{cite web |url=https://www.readdork.com/news/holly-humberstone-has-released-another-absolute-gem-with-new-track-overkill/ |title=Holly Humberstone has released another absolute gem with new track 'Overkill' |work=[[Dork (magazine)|Dork]] |accessdate=29 June 2020 |author=Muir, Jaime}}</ref> On 30 July 2020, she released a cover of "[[Fake Plastic Trees]]" by the English rock band [[Radiohead]]. Her debut EP, also titled ''Falling Asleep at the Wheel'', was released on 14 August, which contained her three previous singles, alongside the tracks "Vanilla", "Drop Dead" and "Livewire".<ref name="nmemagazine2">{{cite web |last1=Moore |first1=Sam |title=Listen to Holly Humberstone's cover of Radiohead's 'Fake Plastic Trees' |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/holly-humberstone-covers-radiohead-fake-plastic-trees-radar-2718306 |work=[[NME]] |accessdate=1 August 2020}}</ref><ref name="newyorktimes2">{{cite web |last1=Caramanica |first1=Jon |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/10/arts/music/holly-humberstone-falling-asleep-at-the-wheel.html |title=Holly Humberstone wants her songs to last a lifetime |work=[[The New York Times]] |accessdate=18 August 2020}}</ref>

On December 9, 2020 she was included in [[Vevo|VEVO DSCVR]]'s Artists To Watch 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Vigil|first=Dom|title=Holly Humberstone Shares "DSCVR Artists To Watch 2021" Performance Video {{!}} Prelude Press|url=https://preludepress.com/news/2020/12/09/holly-humberstone-shares-dscvr-artists-to-watch-2021-performance-video/|access-date=2021-01-12|language=en-US}}</ref> She performed her song '''[[Vanilla (Holly Humberstone song)|Vanilla]]''' on the channel.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-14|title=Vevo Artists To Watch 2021: Olivia Dean, Holly Humberstone, Alfie Templeman, Kylie Morgan, Dylan Fuentes|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/vevo-artists-to-watch-2021-olivia-dean-holly-humberstone-alfie-templeman-kylie-morgan-dylan-fuentes/|access-date=2021-01-12|website=Music Business Worldwide|language=en-US}}</ref>

In March 2021, ahead of the release of her single "Haunted House" and her second EP, Humberstone signed with [[Polydor Records]] in the United Kingdom and Darkroom/[[Interscope Records]] in the United States. She also landed a publishing deal with Universal Music Publishing Group.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Earl|first=William|date=2021-03-17|title=UK Newcomer Holly Humberstone Signs With Darkroom/Interscope/Polydor Records|url=https://variety.com/2021/music/news/holly-humberstone-signs-with-darkroom-interscope-records-1234932515/|access-date=2021-04-07|website=Variety|language=en-US}}</ref>

On 13 October 2021, Holly performed "Scarlett" from her forthcoming EP, ''The Walls Are Way Too Thin'' on The Tonight Show with Jimmy Fallon.
