'''''Choir Boy''''' is a [[List of coming-of-age stories|coming-of-age]] [[Play (theatre)|play]] by American [[playwright]] [[Tarell Alvin McCraney]]. The play premiered in September 2012 at the [[Royal Court Theatre]], [[London]], before going on to play productions at [[New York City Center]], [[Alliance Theatre]], [[Geffen Playhouse]], and many more [[Regional theater in the United States|regional theatres]] across the [[United States]]. The show opened on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] at the [[Samuel J. Friedman Theatre]] on January 8, 2019, after entering previews on December 12, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/watch-highlights-from-tarell-alvin-mccraneys-choir-boy-on-broadway|title=Watch Highlights From Tarell Alvin McCraney’s Choir Boy on Broadway|last=Clement|first=Olivia|date=January 4, 2019|website=Playbill|language=en|access-date=2019-06-15}}</ref>

== Production history ==
=== Royal Court Theatre, London ===
''Choir Boy'' opened at the Royal Court Theatre on September 4, 2012 and it played until October 6. [[Dominic Cooke]] directed and the cast featured: Dominic Smith (Pharus), [[David Burke (British actor)|David Burke]] (Mr Pendleton), [[Gary McDonald (actor)|Gary McDonald]] (Headmaster Marrow), Eric Kofi-Abrefa (Bobby), Kwayedza Kureya (Junior Davis), Khali Best (Anthony Justin), and Aron Julius (David Heard).<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2012/sep/11/choir-boy-review|title=Choir Boy - Review|last=Gardner|first=Lyn|date=2012-11-09|work=The Guardian|access-date=June 10, 2019|language=en-UK|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

=== Manhattan Theater Club at City Center, Stage I ===
''Choir Boy'' was commissioned by Manhattan Theater Club. The production began previews on June 18, 2013, with an official opening on July 2, 2013.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/07/03/theater/reviews/in-tarell-mccraneys-choir-boy-spirituals-are-solace.html|title=Hoping the Songs Lead Him to Freedom|last=Isherwood|first=Charles|date=2013-08-11|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-02-17|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> The production starred [[Jeremy Pope (actor)|Jeremy Pope]] as Pharus Jonathan Young, Nicholas L. Ashe as Junior Davis, Kyle Beltran as David Heard, Grantham Coleman as Anthony Justin 'AJ' James, [[Chuck Cooper (actor)|Chuck Cooper]] as Headmaster Marrow, [[Austin Pendleton]] as Mr. Pendleton, and Wallace Smith as Bobby Marrow.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.manhattantheatreclub.com/2012-2013-season/choir-boy/|title=Manhattan Theatre Club – Choir Boy|website=Manhattan Theatre Club|language=en|access-date=2019-02-17}}</ref> The show closed on August 11, 2013.<ref name=":0" />

=== Alliance Theatre ===
''Choir Boy'' premiered on the Hertz Stage at Alliance Theatre on September 20, 2013, and closed on October 13, 2013. 

The show featured Jeremy Pope as Pharus Jonathan Young and Nicholas L. Ashe as Junior Davis, both reprising their performances from the City Center run of the show. New cast members included Caleb Eberhardt as David Heard, Joshua Boone as Bobby Marrow, Scott Robertson as Mr. Pendleton, John Stewart as Anthony Justin 'AJ' James, and Charles E. Wallace as Headmaster Marrow. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://alliancetheatre.org/sites/default/files/FY14%20Choir%20Boy%20Study%20Guide.pdf|title=Choir Boy at Alliance Theatre Study Guide|last=Alliance Theatre Institute for Educators and Teaching Artists Staff|date=2013|website=Alliance Theatre}}</ref> Serving as the production understudies were Victor Jackson for Pharus Jonathan Young and Junior Davis; Patrick McColery for Mr. Pendleton; Kevin O'Hara for Headmaster Marrow; and Alex B. West for Anthony Justin 'AJ' James, David Heard, and Bobby Marrow.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://issuu.com/encoreatlanta/docs/alliance_theatre_choir_boy|title=Alliance Theatre "Choir Boy"|website=Issuu|language=en|access-date=2019-02-17}}</ref>

The production was directed by Trip Cullman, and featured set and costume design by [[David Zinn]], lighting design by [[Peter Kaczorowski]], sound design by Fitz Patton, music direction and vocal arrangements by Jason Michael Webb, and casting by Nancy Piccione, Kelly Gillespie, and Jody Feldman.<ref name=":1" />

=== Geffen Playhouse ===
[[Geffen Playhouse|The Geffen Playhouse]] production in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]] began previews on September 16, 2014, before the official opening night on September 26, 2014, and a final performance on October 26, 2014. This production marked the show's [[West Coast of the United States|West Coast]] premiere. 

The cast included Jeremy Pope as Pharus Jonathan Young, Nicholas L. Ashe as Junior Davis, and Grantham Coleman as Anthony Justin 'AJ' James, all reprising their performances from New York City Center Stage I, and Caleb Eberhardt as David Heard, reprising his performance from the Alliance Theatre production.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://geffenplayhouse.org/show_cast.php?show_id=200|title=Show Cast {{!}} Geffen Playhouse {{!}} Best Live Entertainment In Los Angeles|website=geffenplayhouse.org|access-date=2019-02-17}}</ref> New to the company was Michael A. Shepperd as Headmaster Marrow, Donovan Mitchell as Bobby Marrow, and Leonard Kelly-Young as Mr. Pendleton. <ref name=":2" />

The production was directed by Trip Cullman and featured set design by [[David Zinn]], costume design by E.B. Brooks, lighting design by [[Peter Kaczorowski]], sound design by Fitz Patton, music direction and vocal arrangements by Jason Michael Webb, and casting by Phyllis Schuringa.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://geffenplayhouse.org/more_info.php?show_id=200|title=Choir Boy at Geffen Playhouse {{!}} Best Live Shows and Theatrical Performances in Los Angeles|last=Playhouse|first=Geffen|website=geffenplayhouse.org|language=en-US|access-date=2019-02-17}}</ref>

=== Broadway ===
''Choir Boy'' began previews on Broadway at the [[Samuel J. Friedman Theatre]] on December 12, 2018, officially opening on January 8, 2019 and closing on March 10. Trip Cullman directed the production and Jeremy Pope reprised his role as Pharus Jonathan Young.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/tarell-alvin-mccraneys-choir-boy-extends-again-on-broadway|title=Tarell Alvin McCraney’s Choir Boy Extends Again on Broadway|last=Clement|first=Olivia|date=January 25, 2019|website=Playbill|language=en|access-date=March 19, 2019}}</ref>
