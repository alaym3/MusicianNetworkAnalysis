'''Marylou Mayniel''' (born 23 April 1993), better known by her stage name '''Oklou''' (ˈəʊˈkeɪ luː), is a French musician, singer, music producer, DJ, composer, and actress.

==Early life==
Marylou Maniel was born on 23 April 1993, in Poitiers, France.<ref name="le7">{{Cite web|title=Oklou, la nouvelle vague|url=https://www.le7.info/article/12327-oklou-la-nouvelle-vague|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Le7.info|language=fr}}</ref> Mayniel grew up in the countryside of western France with her two parents and an older brother.{{fact|date=October 2021}}

She was classically trained in piano and cello and sung in choirs as a child.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dazed|date=2018-02-02|title=Meet the French producer turning childhood memories into future-facing pop|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/38882/1/oklou-friendless-video-interview|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Dazed|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==

=== 2014–2019: Early beginnings ===
Mayniel self-released her debut EP, ''Avril'', in June 2014, under the moniker Loumar.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-07-03|title=We Love Loumar|url=https://happymag.tv/loumar-avril/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Happy Mag|language=en-US}}</ref>

She moved to Paris in 2015,<ref name="le7"/> and self-released her first Oklou EP, ''First Tape'', alongside a limited run of cassettes.<ref>{{Cite web|title=oklou first tape - splash tape limited edition release, by Oklou|url=https://oklou.bandcamp.com/album/oklou-first-tape-splash-tape-limited-edition-release|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Oklou}}</ref> After meeting in late 2015, Mayniel co-founded the female DJ collective TGAF (These Girls Are on Fiyah), alongside DJ Ouai, Miley Serious, and Carin Kelly. They would play weekly radio shows on French station PIIAF and live shows around Paris.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-05-17|title=Rencontre : These Girls Are On Fiyah (TGAF), et nous aussi|url=https://www.beyeah.net/longues/these-girls-are-on-fiyah-interview/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=BEYEAH|language=fr-FR}}</ref> The group gained a notable following in its formative years, appearing in many online publication features.<ref>{{Cite web|title=A Tour of Paris’ Next-Generation Club Scene, Where Inclusivity Reigns Supreme|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/kbjmzx/a-tour-of-paris-next-generation-club-scene-where-inclusivity-reigns-supreme|access-date=2021-03-12|website=www.vice.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=9 All-Female DJ Collectives You Need To Know Right Now|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/02/17/female-dj-crews-discwoman-sister-mahoyo-tgaf|access-date=2021-03-12|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-03-29|title=Internet Wave : nouveau genre, nouvelle scène|url=https://www.traxmag.com/internet-wave-nouveau-genre-nouvelle-scene/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Trax Magazine|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Morin|first=Marie-Lou|title=Internet Wave, airs numériques|url=https://www.liberation.fr/musique/2016/03/25/internet-wave-airs-numeriques_1442068/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Libération|language=fr}}</ref> TGAF disbanded in July 2018.<ref>{{Citation|title=TGAF on Facebook Watch|url=https://www.facebook.com/thesegyalsareonfiyah/videos/hi-everyone-after-2-and-a-half-years-of-fun-together-sharing-our-favorite-tunes-/1885887334805249/|language=en|access-date=2021-03-12}}[[Wikipedia:SPS|{{sup|[''self-published'']}}]]</ref>

Mayniel acted in the short film ''After School Knife Fight'' in 2017. The film received a festival release from 2017 to 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=After school knife fight - ecce films|url=http://eccefilms.fr/After-school-knife-fight|access-date=2021-03-12|website=eccefilms.fr|language=en}}</ref> In December 2017, Mayniel released her collaborative EP with Canadian musician and close collaborator Casey MQ, titled ''For the Beasts''. This marked their first collaboration. The EP was released via the Permalnk label.<ref>{{Cite web|title=FOR THE BEASTS, by Oklou & Casey MQ|url=https://permalnk.bandcamp.com/album/for-the-beasts|access-date=2021-03-12|website=PERMALNK}}</ref> In early 2018, Mayniel became associated with record label and collective, NUXXE, and its co-founders, Scottish music producer [[Sega Bodega]], British rapper [[Shygirl]] and French producer and performer [[Coucou Chloe]]. She released her third EP, ''The Rite of May'', via the label in March of that year.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dazed|date=2018-05-24|title=Club collective Nuxxe is bringing new sound to dance floors|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/40130/1/nuxxe-shygirl-new-ep|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Dazed|language=en}}</ref> It featured collaborations with Sega Bodega, [[Rodaidh McDonald]], Krampf and [[Bok Bok]]. Later that year, Mayniel and Krampf co-created the video game, ''Zone W/O People''. It was made as a part of [[Red Bull Music Academy]] France's 2018 Diggin’ in the Carts event.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Red Bull Music Festival Paris: Diggin' in the Carts at Gaite Lyrique, La, Paris (2018) ⟋ RA|url=https://ra.co/events/1133628|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Resident Advisor|language=en}}</ref> Mayniel also produced a soundtrack for the game, which she later released on a limited cassette.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Zone W/O People OST, by Oklou|url=https://oklou.bandcamp.com/album/zone-w-o-people-ost|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Oklou}}</ref> Mayniel also appeared on the late American rapper [[Chynna Rogers|Chynna]]’s October 2018 single "Xternal Locus".<ref>{{Citation|title=Listen to "Xternal Locus" by Chynna / Oklou|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/xternal-locus-ft-oklou/|language=en|access-date=2021-03-12}}</ref>

Throughout 2019, Mayniel played at various festivals, such as Loom Festival<ref>{{Cite web|title=Loom Festival 2019 at Utopia 126, Barcelona (2019) ⟋ RA|url=https://ra.co/events/1228723|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Resident Advisor|language=en}}</ref> and [[Pitchfork Music Festival|Pitchfork's Paris Music Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pitchfork|title=Pitchfork Music Festival Paris 2019 Reveals Full Lineup|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/pitchfork-music-festival-paris-2019-reveals-full-lineup/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Pitchfork|language=en-us}}</ref> She also released a single, titled "Forever", via TaP Records in October. It was co-produced with Sega Bodega.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hear Oklou’s radiant new single "Forever"|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/10/29/oklou-forever-premiere-marylou-maynie|access-date=2021-03-12|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''Galore'' and collaborations ===
In January 2020, Mayniel released a collaborative single with French experimentalist Flavien Berger, titled "Toyota", via [[Because Music]]. It is currently Mayniel's only French-language release.<ref>{{Cite web|title=France Rocks {{!}} New single: OKLOU x FLAVIEN BERGER ‘TOYOTA’|url=http://www.francerocks.com/new-releases/new-single-oklou-x-flavien-berger-toyota/|access-date=2021-03-12|language=en-US}}</ref> This was followed by another single in February, titled "entertnmnt", which was co-produced with British producer [[Mura Masa]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Oklou Links With mura masa On Hypnotic Single 'entertnmnt'|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/oklou-links-with-mura-masa-on-hypnotic-single-entertnmnt|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> and another single in April, titled "SGSY" (standing for She's Gonna Slaughter You).<ref>{{Cite web|last=Torres|first=Matt|date=2020-04-24|title=Oklou Shares New Single, "SGSY" – Stream|url=https://soundazed.com/oklou-shares-new-single-sgsy-stream/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Soundazed|language=en-US}}</ref> The singles were released via TaP Records and [[True Panther Sounds]].{{fact|date=October 2021}}

Mayniel announced her debut mixtape ''Galore'' in July 2020, sharing three tracks, one of which ("unearth me") was accompanied with a music video.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-07-30 |title=Rising French producer Oklou announces debut mixtape Galore, shares three new songs: Stream|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2020/07/oklou-galore-debut-mixtape-unearth-me-video/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Consequence of Sound|language=en-US}}</ref> Another three tracks from the mixtape were released in August 2020, alongside a music video for the "god’s chariots" track.<ref>{{Cite web|title=French electro-pop mastermind Oklou unveils her mesmerising new single "god’s chariots" |url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/song-of-the-day/oklou-gods-chariot|access-date=2021-03-12|website=The Line of Best Fit|language=en}}</ref> The full mixtape was released 24 September 2020, via Because Music, TaP Records and True Panther Sounds.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-24|title=Oklou unveils new debut mixtape Galore: Stream|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2020/09/stream-oklou-galore-mixtape/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Consequence of Sound|language=en-US}}</ref> The tape was made primarily in collaboration with Casey MQ, but also featured collaborations with Sega Bodega, Shygirl, [[PC Music]]’s [[A. G. Cook]] and [[Easyfun|EASYFUN]], and [[Grades (record producer)|GRADES]]. It also appeared on [[Gorilla vs. Bear]], [[Rough Trade (shops)|Rough Trade]], and Dummy Mag’s 2020 year-end lists.<ref name="gorillavsbear">{{cite web|url=https://www.gorillavsbear.net/gorilla-vs-bears-albums-of-2020/|website=gorillavsbear.net|title=gorilla vs. bear's albums of 2020 |date=2020-12-01|author=Chris|accessdate=2021-03-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-10|title=Albums of the Year 2020|url=https://blog.roughtrade.com/albums-of-the-year-2020/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Rough Trade Blog|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Dummy|title=The 25 Best Albums of 2020|url=https://www.dummymag.com/features/the-25-best-albums-of-2020/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=DummyMag|language=en-US}}</ref> Additionally in 2020, Mayniel released remixes of [[Caroline Polachek]]’s "Door",<ref>{{Cite web|title=Caroline Polachek unveils four Pang track remixes by Toro Y Moi, Oklou and more|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/caroline-polachek-unveils-four-pang-remixes-by-toro-y-moi-oklou-and-more|access-date=2021-03-12|website=The Line of Best Fit|language=en}}</ref> and [[Dua Lipa]]’s "[[Fever (Dua Lipa and Angèle song)|Fever]]".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dummy|title=Oklou delivers an entrancing remix of Dua Lipa's and Angèle's 'Fever'|url=https://www.dummymag.com/new-music/oklou-delivers-an-entrancing-remix-of-dua-lipas-and-angeles-fever/|access-date=2021-03-12|website=DummyMag|language=en-US}}</ref>

Mayniel produced a remix of French singer [[Pomme (singer)|Pomme]]’s "les cours d’eau" track in January 2021, followed by a remix of Swedish rapper [[Bladee]] and producer Mechatok's "Rainbow" track in March 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hear Oklou’s shimmering remix of Bladee and Mechatok’s “Rainbow”|url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/03/19/oklou-bladee-mechatok-rainbow-remix-good-luck|access-date=2021-03-20|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref> Mayniel appeared on A. G. Cook's ''[[Apple vs. 7G]]'' remix album in May 2020, covering "Being Harsh" from Cook's 2020 ''[[7G (album)|7G]]'' album. She also released remixes of her tracks "fall" and "galore", by Cook. Mayniel will embark on a tour across the US with Caroline Polachek throughout November and December 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2021-07-26|title=Caroline Polachek Adds 2021 Tour Dates, Shares New “Bunny Is a Rider” Video|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/caroline-polachek-adds-2021-tour-dates-shares-new-bunny-is-a-rider-video/|access-date=2021-08-02|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref> A remix EP celebrating ''Galore''<nowiki/>'s anniversary was released on 13 October, featuring an extended mix of "asturias", and two remixes from Casey MQ, and Pomme & [[Danny L Harle]].{{fact|date=October 2021}}
