'''Ndivhudzannyi Ralivhona''', known professionally as '''Makhadzi''', is a South African singer. Born and raised in Ha-Mashamba, [[Limpopo]], she is best known for her songs "Tshanda Vhuya" and "Matorokisi".

Makhadzi's 2020 album ''Kokovha'' ("Crawl") became one of her biggest successes and debuted at number one on the South African [[iTunes]] albums charts. It contained the chart-topping singles "Amadoda", "Mphemphe", "Kokovha" and "Tshikwama".<ref>{{cite web|title=Kokovha – Makhadzi {{!}} Top Charts|url=https://www.top-charts.com/r/kokovha-makhadzi|work=Top Charts}}</ref>

== Early life ==

Makhadzi was born in a small village called Ha-Mashamba Tshivhangani, just outside Elim in [[Limpopo]] province. After her parents separated, Ndivhudzannyi and her two siblings had to stay with their mother. She completed her grade 12 at Mukula Integrated School. Ndivhudzannyi obtained her qualification in Public Relations and she is currently studying drama.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/entertainment/2019-08-13-songbird-makhadzi-promises-fireworks-at-royal-heritage-festival-in-venda/|title=Songbird Makhadzi promises fireworks at Royal Heritage Festival in Venda|website=SowetanLIVE}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://briefly.co.za/38723-viral-matorokisichallenge-sends-sa-a-frenzy-dance-videos.html|title=Viral #MatorokisiChallenge sends SA into a frenzy with dance videos|first=Claudio|last=Leng|date=7 October 2019|website=Briefly}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=4 February 2020|title=Makhadzi Biography, Songs, Albums, Awards, Education, Net Worth, Age & Relationships »|url=https://www.ubetoo.com/artists/makhadzi-bio-top-songs-albums-music-downloads-stream-bio-age-relationships/|access-date=5 February 2021|website=Ubetoo|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last1=Nkosi|first1=Joseph|last2=MA|title=Makhadzi biography, age, profile, awards & background {{!}} The Nation|url=https://thenation.co.za/bio/makhadzi-biography/|access-date=5 February 2021|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Mueni|first=Priscillah|date=9 January 2020|title=Makhadzi alleges not being paid by her manager: full story|url=https://briefly.co.za/49043-makhadzi-bio-age-songs-albums-latest-news.html|access-date=5 February 2021|website=Briefly|language=en}}</ref>

===2021–present: ''African Queen'' ===
On 5 April 2021, Makhadzi released a single "Mjolo" featuring [[Mlindo the Vocalist]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Makhadzi collaborates with Mlindo The Vocalist on Mjolo|date=8 April 2021|url=https://justnje.com/makhadzi-collaborates-with-mlindo-the-vocalist-on-mjolo/|work=Just Nje}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Ooh Lala: Makhadzi Drops 'Mjolo' with Mlindo the Vocalist|url=https://briefly.co.za/97678-ooh-lala-makhadzi-drops-mjolo-mlindo-vocalist.html|work=Briefly|first= Sibusisiwe |last=Lwandle|date=5 April 2021}}</ref> The song peaked number 28 on South Africa iTunes charts.<ref>{{cite web|title=iTunes South Africa Top 100 Songs|url=http://www.popvortex.com/amp/music/top-songs-south-africa.php|work=Popvortex}}</ref> On 7 July 2021, she released "Ghanama" featuring Prince Benza.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://zaplaylist.com/2021/07/08/makhadzi-ganama-ft-prince-benza/|title=Makhadzi ft Prince Benza Ghanama|work=ZAPlaylist|last=Mike|first=Steven|date=8 July 2021|access-date=8 July 2021}}</ref> On 16 August 2021, she announced her album
''African Queen'' on Twitter. The album was scheduled to be released on 3 September 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title=Makhadzi Reveals Release Date Of Her Upcoming Album|url=https://samusicmag.co.za/2021/08/makhadzi-reveals-release-date-of-her-upcoming-album/amp/|work=South Africa Magazine|date=16 August 2021}}</ref> On 27 August 2021, she released a single "Tchukutsha" featuring Lady Du.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thesouthafrican.com/lifestyle/entertainment/makhadzi-lady-du-release-tchukutsha-amapiano-ghanama-listen/amp/|title=Girl power! Makhadzi and Lady Du release 'Tchukutsha' [listen]|first=Naledi |last=de Wee|date= 27 August 2021|work=The South African}}</ref>

On 6 August 2021, [[Kabza De Small]] teased a collaboration single with Makhadzi on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kabza De Small and Makhadzi tease collaboration with Salungano and Connections – JustNje|url=https://justnje.com/kabza-de-small-and-makhadzi-tease-collaboration-with-salungano-and-connections/|date=10 August 2021|work=JustNje}}</ref> The song "Salungano" was released on 2 September 2021. On 3 September 2021, her album was released.<ref>{{cite web|title=Makhadzi drops much-awaited album 'African Queen'|url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/s-mag/2021-09-03-makhadzi-drops-much-awaited-album-african-queen/|date=3 September 2021}}</ref> The album features [[Cassper Nyovest]], [[Kabza De Small]] and Lady Du.<ref>{{cite web|title=New music Friday: Makhadzi is indeed an 'African Queen' {{!}} The Citizen|url=https://www.citizen.co.za/entertainment/music/2604115/new-music-friday-makhadzi-is-indeed-an-african-queen/|first=Sandisiwe |last=Mbhele|date=3 September 2021|work=The Citizen}}</ref>

At the 6th ceremony of [[All Africa Music Awards]], she received a nomination for Best Female Artist in South Africa.<ref>{{cite web|title=All Africa Music Awards 2021: All the nominees {{!}} Music In Africa|url= https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/all-africa-music-awards-2021-all-nominees|work=Music In Africa|first= Gabriel Myers |last=Hansen|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref>

== Television ==

In 2018, Makhadzi was hosted on Yo TV for her first television performance.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/entertainment/2019-08-28-makhadzi-grabs-most-nominations-for-tshima-awards/|title=Makhadzi grabs most nominations for Tshima awards|website=SowetanLIVE}}</ref> She has since conducted interviews with various radio stations, including [[Munghana Lonene FM]], [[Phalaphala FM]], Nzhelele FM, [[Makhado FM 107.3|Makhado FM]], Sloot FM, and [[Capricorn FM|Capricon FM]], where she spoke about her music, life and her hardships.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.zoutnet.co.za/articles/entertainment/50832/2019-08-30/tshima-2019-all-the-nominees|title=Zoutnet &#124; Entertainment &#124; Tshima 2019 – All the nominees|website=www.zoutnet.co.za}}</ref> She also appeared on The Morning Show on [[E.tv|ETV]]. Makhadzi was one of the performers at the 2020 DSTV Mzansi Viewers Choice Awards. Her performance was hailed as one of the best performances of the night on social media.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ebnewsdaily.co.za/2020/03/makhadzis-performance-at-dstvmvca-best-for-the-night.html|title=Makhadzi's performance at #DSTVMVCA 'best for the night'|publisher=ebnewsdaily.co.za|date=1 March 2020|access-date=2 June 2020}}</ref>
