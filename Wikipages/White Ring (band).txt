'''White Ring''' is an American [[Witch house (genre)|witch house]] band formed in 2009 in New York City by Bryan Kurkimilis and Kendra Malia.

==History==
Founding White Ring members Bryan Kurkimilis and Kendra Malia met each other on the social networking website [[MySpace]] in 2006. After Malia suggested they should work together on songs, they began working together for three years. In 2009, Kurkimilis moved from [[New Orleans]] to New York to pursue White Ring full-time with Malia.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wheelscene.co.uk/2011/11/white-ring-interview/ |title=White Ring Interview |work=Wheel Scene |date=November 21, 2011 |accessdate=July 27, 2018}}</ref> The two were later joined by Adina Viarengo in 2016 who serves as a second vocalist.<ref>{{cite web |last=Simpson |first=Paul |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/white-ring-mn0002597003/biography |title=White Ring {{!}} Biography & History |work=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=July 28, 2018}}</ref> The current touring line-up consists of Kurimilis and Viarengo.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://skopemag.com/2018/04/11/white-ring-announces-debut-lp-gate-of-grief-their-first-release-in-8-years |title=White Ring announces debut LP 'Gate of Grief' – their first release in 8 years |work=Skope Magazine |date=April 11, 2018 |accessdate=June 18, 2018}}</ref>

The band's debut single was the split [[7-inch single]] "Roses / Seaww" with [[oOoOO]], released in March 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://boomkat.com/products/roses-seaww-emotion-single |title=White Ring / OOOOO - Roses / Seawww (Emotion Single) |work=Boomkat |accessdate=July 27, 2018}}</ref> In 2011 they released their debut EP ''Black Earth That Made Me'' (Disaro Records) and the DJ mix 	
''Chaind Vol. 1'' ([[Pendu Sound Recordings]]); after that they went on seven year-long hiatus.<ref>{{cite web |first=Rob |last=Hakimian |url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/white-ring-make-an-unmistakable-return-with-leprosy-and-announce-album-152 | title=White Ring make an unmistakable return with 'Leprosy' and announce album |work=The 405 |date=April 19, 2018 |accessdate=June 18, 2018}}</ref> In July 2018, the band made a comeback and released their debut studio album, ''Gate of Grief''. The album was originally scheduled to be released on June 22, but was eventually delayed and released on July 27.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.tinymixtapes.com/news/witch-house-coven-white-ring-announces-gate-grief-their-first-new-album-eight-years | title= Witch House coven White Ring announces Gate of Grief, their first new album in eight years | first=Tristan | last=Kneschke | work=[[Tiny Mix Tapes]] | date=April 12, 2018 | accessdate=June 18, 2018}}</ref> The album was in the works for eight years, but was put off due to Malia's health issues.<ref>{{cite web |last=Simpson |first=Paul |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/gate-of-grief-mw0003177474 |title=Gate of Grief - White Ring |work=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=July 27, 2018}}</ref>

On November 6, 2019 it was reported by the official White Ring Facebook page that on October 25, 2019, Kendra Malia had died.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.facebook.com/52614102360/posts/10156730448737361?d=n&sfns=mo |title=On October 25th 2019 Kendra Malia left us, I really don’t know what else to say at the moment except I love you Kendra. KENDRA FOREVER ❤️ |accessdate=November 6, 2019}}</ref>
