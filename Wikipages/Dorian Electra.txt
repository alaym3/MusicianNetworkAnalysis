'''Dorian Electra Fridkin Gomberg''' is an American singer, songwriter, and performance artist. Their debut studio album, ''[[Flamboyant (album)|Flamboyant]]'', was released in 2019, followed by their second  studio album, ''[[My Agenda]],'' in 2020.

==Early life==
Electra's father is Paul Gomberg, known as "the Rockstar Realtor" in Houston.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://abc13.com/1220442/|title=Realtor selling 'filthiest home in Houston' offers limo rides to showings|date=February 27, 2016|website=ABC13 Houston|language=en|access-date=October 25, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191025172519/https://abc13.com/1220442/|archive-date=October 25, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> Their mother is artist and jewelry designer Paula Fridkin.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.houstonchronicle.com/life/style/fashion/article/Paula-Fridkin-A-woman-of-curiosities-6827230.php|title=Style Profile: A woman of curiosities|first=Joy|last=Sewing|date=February 12, 2016|website=HoustonChronicle.com|access-date=July 27, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190727055912/https://www.houstonchronicle.com/life/style/fashion/article/Paula-Fridkin-A-woman-of-curiosities-6827230.php|archive-date=July 27, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> Electra graduated from [[School of the Woods - Montessori|School of the Woods]], a [[Montessori method|Montessori]] high school in [[Houston]], [[Texas]].<ref name="blog.shimer"/> They attended [[Shimer College]], a [[Great Books]] school in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]], from 2010 to 2014.<ref name="firstday">{{cite video | people=Electra, Dorian | date=September 8, 2010 | title=My First Day at Shimer College | medium=YouTube | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ppesEM3uHqo | location=Chicago, Illinois | publisher=Shimer College | access-date=October 18, 2012 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140511092737/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ppesEM3uHqo | archive-date=May 11, 2014 | url-status=live }}</ref> Electra is [[Jews|Jewish]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8520161/dorian-electra-interview-flamboyant-debut-album|title=How Dorian Electra Channels Camp & Queer Culture On Their 'Whimsically Self-Aware' Debut Album|last=Russell|first=Erica|date=July 7, 2019|access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
Electra first drew national attention in 2010 with the music video "I'm in Love with Friedrich Hayek",<ref name="hayek">{{Cite video | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psosLpDALuA | title = I'm in Love with Friedrich Hayek | people = Electra, Dorian | date = December 19, 2010 | access-date = October 18, 2012 }}</ref> which lauded the philosophy of the Austrian economist [[Friedrich Hayek]] and garnered commentary from the modern Austrian theory professor [[Steven Horwitz]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://studentsforliberty.org/blog/this-is-the-best-they-can-do/|title=This is the Best They Can Do?|last=Horwitz|first=Steve|date=July 10, 2012|access-date=October 18, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121220083524/http://studentsforliberty.org/blog/this-is-the-best-they-can-do/|archive-date=December 20, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2011, they released two more videos, "Roll with the Flow" and "We Got it 4 Cheap". Both were covered by mainstream political media.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nationalreview.com/corner/280301/more-hayek-vs-keynes-veronique-de-rugy|author=Veronique de Rugy|work=[[National Review]]|title=More Hayek vs. Keynes|date=October 17, 2011|access-date=October 18, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111221031203/http://www.nationalreview.com/corner/280301/more-hayek-vs-keynes-veronique-de-rugy|archive-date=December 21, 2011|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://reason.com/blog/2011/10/17/new-hayekian-musicecon-video-r|author=Matt Welch|author-link=Matt Welch|work=[[Reason.com]]|title=New Hayekian Music/Econ Video: "Roll With the Flow (My Date With Keynes)"|date=October 17, 2011|access-date=October 18, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120707092352/http://reason.com/blog/2011/10/17/new-hayekian-musicecon-video-r|archive-date=July 7, 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> "We Got it 4 Cheap" came in second in the [[Lloyd V. Hackley]] Endowment's "Supply and Demand Video Contest".<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.uncfsu.edu/sbe/HackleyChair/index.htm | title = Supply and Demand Video Contest Winners | date = February 1, 2012 | access-date = October 19, 2012 | author = Lloyd V. Hackley Endowment | publisher = [[Fayetteville State University]] | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20120806090752/http://www.uncfsu.edu/sbe/HackleyChair/index.htm | archive-date = August 6, 2012 | df = mdy-all }}</ref>

In 2012, they interned at production company Emergent Order.<ref name="blog.shimer">{{Cite web|url=http://blog.shimer.edu/shimer/2012/05/dorian-electras-in-texas.html|title=Dorian Electra's in Texas|work=Blog.shimer|access-date=October 18, 2012|date=May 30, 2012|last=Electra|first=Dorian|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120711062242/http://blog.shimer.edu/shimer/2012/05/dorian-electras-in-texas.html|archive-date=July 11, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="2012SIM">{{Cite web|url=http://alumni.shimer.edu/s/1028/index.aspx?sid=1028&gid=1&pgid=252&cid=1538&ecid=1538&ciid=4590&crid=0|archive-url=https://archive.today/20121215010328/http://alumni.shimer.edu/s/1028/index.aspx?sid=1028&gid=1&pgid=252&cid=1538&ecid=1538&ciid=4590&crid=0|url-status=dead|archive-date=December 15, 2012|title=2012 SIM Interns|last=College|first=Shimer|date=April 18, 2012|access-date=October 19, 2012}}</ref>  Emergent Order had previously published "[[Fear the Boom and Bust]]", a similar Hayek-oriented rap video.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.npr.org/blogs/money/2010/01/watch_fear_the_boom_and_bust.html | title = Watch: 'Fear The Boom And Bust' | publisher = [[National Public Radio|NPR]] | last = Kenney | first = Caitlin | access-date = October 18, 2012 | date = January 25, 2010 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20121019205050/http://www.npr.org/blogs/money/2010/01/watch_fear_the_boom_and_bust.html | archive-date = October 19, 2012 | url-status = live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.cnbc.com/id/35370923/Fear_the_Boom_and_Bust_A_Rap_Anthem_for_the_Economy | publisher = [[CNBC]] | title = 'Fear the Boom and Bust': A Rap Anthem for the Economy | date = February 12, 2010 | access-date = October 18, 2012 | last = Perman | first = Cindy | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20121016172419/http://www.cnbc.com/id/35370923/Fear_the_Boom_and_Bust_A_Rap_Anthem_for_the_Economy | archive-date = October 16, 2012 | url-status = live }}</ref>

Electra then produced a new, similarly economics-oriented pop video, "FA$T CA$H", with the support of an award from the [[Moving Picture Institute]].<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://convergenceculture.org/futuresofentertainment/2012/speakers/#dorian-electra | title = Speakers at the Futures of Entertainment program | publisher = Futures of Entertainment | access-date = October 18, 2012 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20121202220028/http://convergenceculture.org/futuresofentertainment/2012/speakers/#dorian-electra | archive-date = December 2, 2012 | url-status = live }}</ref>

In September 2012, Electra released the music video "Party Milk", which they describe as an attempt to merge common party scene symbolism with something one would never associate with a party, but that everyone is familiar with in another context.<ref name="Party Milk">{{Cite video | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T57hZy7qIPI | title = Party Milk | medium = YouTube | date = September 8, 2012 | access-date = October 18, 2012 | people = Electra, Dorian; Bush, Wolf; Hong, Lynn (Director) }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Party Milk|url = http://www.aweh.tv/posts/party-milk/full|website = Aweh {{!}} Casual Creative Culture|access-date = February 9, 2016|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160215021544/http://www.aweh.tv/posts/party-milk/full|archive-date = February 15, 2016|url-status = live}}</ref>

In 2014, Electra (as Dorian Electra & The Electrodes) released a music video called "What Mary Didn't Know",<ref name="What Mary Didn't Know"/> based on [[Frank Cameron Jackson|Frank Jackson]]'s philosophical thought experiment of the same name (1986).<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Jackson|first1=Frank|title=What Mary Didn't Know|journal=The Journal of Philosophy|date=May 1986|volume=83|issue=5|pages=291–295|doi=10.2307/2026143|jstor=2026143|url=http://home.sandiego.edu/~baber/analytic/Jackson.pdf|access-date=February 11, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160406005337/http://home.sandiego.edu/~baber/analytic/Jackson.pdf|archive-date=April 6, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

2015 saw the release of Electra's video "Forever Young: A Love Song to Ray Kurzweil", a tribute to the futurist [[Ray Kurzweil]].<ref name="Forever Young"/><ref name="Inverse">{{cite web|last1=Toobin|first1=Adam|title=Singularity Futurist Ray Kurzweil Gets 'Forever Young' Cover He Deserves|url=https://www.inverse.com/article/9342-singularity-futurist-ray-kurzweil-gets-forever-young-cover-he-deserves|website=Inverse|access-date=February 11, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160215185716/https://www.inverse.com/article/9342-singularity-futurist-ray-kurzweil-gets-forever-young-cover-he-deserves|archive-date=February 15, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2019, Electra released their debut album, ''[[Flamboyant (album)|Flamboyant]]''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Flamboyant|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/4svcCm5yRZtKE0tXn4n1cn?si=4k12lWLiRjy7vQVOYM7zaQ|access-date=July 17, 2019}}</ref> In August 2019, Electra embarked on the Flamboyant: Chapter I tour, which lasted until November 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://bandstours.net/artists/dorian-electra |title=Dorian Electra concert schedule 2019 |access-date=February 26, 2021}}</ref> They began the second leg, Flamboyant: Chapter II, in early 2020. However, in March of the same year, the rest of the tour dates were postponed due to [[COVID-19]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://soundazed.com/dorian-electra-announces-2020-flamboyant-tour-dates/ |title=Dorian Electra Announces 2020 'Flamboyant' Tour Dates |last=Torres |first=Matt |date=January 9, 2020 |website=Soundazed |access-date=March 28, 2021}}</ref>
In 2020, Electra released the single "Thirsty (For Love)", a collaboration with fans.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.papermag.com/dorian-electra-thirsty-for-love-2644999427.html|title=Dorian Electra and Friends Made a New Bop with Fans|last=Ting|first=Jasmine|date=1 February 2020|work=Papermag|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200203150316/https://www.papermag.com/dorian-electra-thirsty-for-love-2644999427.html|archive-date=February 3, 2020}}</ref> They also released a deluxe version of "Flamboyant" later that year. Following this, they released the singles "[[Sorry Bro (I Love You)]]", "Give Great Thanks", "Gentleman", and "M'Lady". On September 21, 2020, Electra announced their project ''[[My Agenda]]'', featuring appearances from [[Rebecca Black]], [[Sega Bodega]], [[Lil Mariko]], [[Mood Killer]], [[Faris Badwan]], [[Pussy Riot]], [[Village People]], and [[Dylan Brady]] among others. The project was released on October 16, 2020, and is described as exploring "[[toxic masculinity|crisis in masculinity]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2020/09/22/dorian-electra-new-project-my-agenda|title=Dorian Electra Announces New Proect 'My Agenda'|website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]|date=September 22, 2020|access-date=September 22, 2020}}</ref>
