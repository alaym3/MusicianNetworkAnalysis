'''Valee Taylor''', known mononymously as '''Valee''', is an American rapper known for his [[mixtape]] ''1988'' which got him signed to [[Kanye West]]'s record label [[GOOD Music]]. He released ''GOOD Job, You Found Me'' on March 2, 2018. The first song he released was with Ty Money ("Cash Don't Bend").<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/03/07/valee-kanye-west-ep-executive-producer/amp|title=Here's how Kanye West put his touch on Valee's new EP|work=The Fader|last=Dandridge-Lemco|first=Ben|date=March 7, 2018|access-date=July 15, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://amp.www.complex.com/music/2018/03/7-things-you-need-to-know-about-good-musics-newest-signee-valee/|title=7 Things You Need to Know About G.O.O.D. == Early life ==
Valee Taylor grew up on Chicago's South Side, living in the [[Robert Taylor Homes]] and moving repeatedly once they were demolished.<ref name=":13">{{Cite web|url=http://www.valeemusic.com/valee|title=Biography of Valee|website=Valee Music|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref> Valee has said his mother exposed him to musicians such as [[Erykah Badu]], [[Sade (singer)|Sade]], [[Jill Scott]] all at an early age.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3058122/valee-chicago-rookie-kanye-chance-the-rapper/|title=10 Things To Know About Valee, The Chicago Rap Rookie Backed By Kanye And Chance|last=Holmes|first=Charles|website=MTV News|language=en|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref> To this day, he still references the artists showed to him at a young age as heavily influential on his current music.<ref name=":2" /> Taylor was well trained as an auto mechanic and made money by working on people's automobiles from his mothers garage.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/valee-good-music-interview-747478/|title=Valee Is a Quiet Rapper Making a Lot of Noise|last1=Klinkenberg|first1=Brendan|date=2018-11-01|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-11}}</ref>

Throughout his schooling, he had repeated issues with anger and his outbursts led to him being kicked out of numerous schools.<ref name=":13"/> The rapper cites [[Cash Money Records|Cash Money]], and [[Project Pat]] as early hip hop influences.<ref name=":13"/>

Taylor says he decided to make music on a whim while living in the South Side of Chicago. He says he was on his way to purchase a video game system, but on the way decided to go to [[Guitar Center]] instead. He purchased the necessary equipment to start making beats and shortly after he began rapping over them<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/nzg84d/welcome-to-the-warped-world-of-valee|title=Welcome to the Warped World of Valee'|last=Terry|first=Bryan Allen Lamb,Josh|date=2017-04-21|website=Vice|language=en|access-date=2020-02-12}}</ref>

== Music ==
Valee's early work includes collaborations with producers such as Rio Mac and Chase the Money.<ref name=":13"/>

Between 2015 and 2017, Valee would go on to release five independent projects; ''12:12'' (2015), ''1:11'' (2016), ''2:22'' (2016), ''12:12'' ''Again'' (2016) and ''1988'' (2017).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/artists/Valee|title=Valee|website=Genius|language=en|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref>

Valee's first official commercial project was, ''GOOD Job, You Found Me'' EP, which was released in March 2018, and was executive produced by [[Kanye West]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/valee-good-job-you-found-me-ep/|title=Valee: GOOD Job, You Found Me EP|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref> This EP received minimal attention from the mainstream, however [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] reviewed the project and gave it a score of 7.4/10.<ref name=":3" />

In 2019, he released a surprise EP, ''Runnin Rich,'' which included features from [[G Herbo]], [[King Louie (rapper)|King Louie]], and [[Vic Mensa]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/06/04/valee-runnin-rich|title=Valee drops surprise EP Runnin' Rich|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=2020-01-31}}</ref> The cover art is a picture of the rapper's pet dog, whom he was publicly condemned for dying red. Valee responded to the critics explaining he used "edible vegan dye" in order to color the pet's fur.<ref name=":0" /> He also released tracks with [[Big Baby DRAM]] (''About U''), [[Matt Ox]] (''Awesome''), and [[Lil Yachty]] (''Wombo''). More recently he appeared on new [[YG (rapper)|YG]] album ''[[4Real 4Real|4REAL 4 REAL]]''.<ref name=":0" />

Valee is currently managed by Andrew Barber, founder of notable Chicago Hip-Hop Blog: Fake Shore Drive.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.redroll.com/2020/01/28/valee-a-new-wave-of-music/|title=Valee: A New Wave of Music|last=two|first=Eric HendrixWriter at Red RollJust a native south side Chicago kid with an opinion or|date=2020-01-28|website=Red Roll|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-11}}</ref>
