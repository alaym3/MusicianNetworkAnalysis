'''Terror Jr''' is an American pop duo formed in 2016 founded by former [[The Cataracs]] band member David "Campa" Benjamin Singer-Vine, Lisa Vitale, and Felix Snow, who left the group in late 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Electropop Group Terror Jr Reveal Lisa & Release Concluding Bop City EP|url=http://celebmix.com/terror-jr-reveal-lisa-release-last-bop-city-installment/|website=CelebMix|access-date=2 October 2017|date=30 September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/04/01/kylie-jenner-lip-gloss-commercial-song|title=No, That's Not Kylie Jenner Singing In Her Lip Gloss Commercial}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2016/04/kylie-jenner-new-video-glosses|title=Here's the Mysterious Group Behind the Song In Kylie Jenner's New Lip Gloss Music Video|website=[[Complex Networks]]}}</ref> Their single "Come First" peaked at number 10 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Spotify Velocity chart, number 6 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot Dance/Electronic songs chart, number 54 in the [[Czech Republic]], and number 59 in [[Slovakia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-blogs/who-are-terror-jr-the-mysterious-pop-trio-rumoured-to-be-fronted-by-kylie-jenner|title=Who Are Terror Jr, The Mysterious Pop Trio Rumoured To Be Fronted By Kylie Jenner? - NME.COM|last=NME.COM|website=[[NME]]}}</ref>

==Background==
Members Felix Snow and David "Campa" Benjamin Singer-Vine met through [[Twitter]] in 2014 and first worked together on a project called ''Momma'', releasing a 4 track EP.<ref>https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/6ejqnr/pop-music-for-the-average-schmuck-campa-of-the-cataracs-is-back</ref> They reconnected the following year working with singer [[Kiiara]] on her single "[[Gold (Kiiara song)|Gold]]". In 2016, they started Terror Jr as a "social experiment", with Singer-Vine adding "It's a puzzle, and we're gonna give out pieces with each song."<ref name="LA weekly" /<ref name="LA weekly">{{cite web|last1=Mahadevan|first1=Tara|title=New Label EFFESS Is Something Special — Just Ask Gallant, SZA and Kylie Jenner|url=http://www.laweekly.com/music/new-label-effess-is-something-special-just-ask-gallant-sza-and-kylie-jenner-6888123|website=L.A. Weekly|access-date=10 November 2016|date=9 May 2016}}</ref> The group released their debut [[extended play]] ''Bop City'' on October 21, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sonis|first1=Rachel|title=Kylie Jenner's Rumored Band Terror Jr To Release New Project 'Bop City' {{!}} Idolator|url=http://www.idolator.com/7648205/terror-jr-kylie-jenner-bop-city-announcement|website=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|publisher=Hive Media|access-date=October 20, 2016|date=12 October 2016}}</ref>

In November 2016, Kylie Jenner was rumored to be the lead singer of Terror Jr.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/3am/celebrity-news/kylie-jenner-follows-kim-kardashians-9211782|title=Kylie Jenner NOT CONFIRMED as lead singer of Terror Jr|last=Haigh|first=Joshua|date=2016-11-07|newspaper=mirror|access-date=2016-11-07}}</ref> Jenner denied being a part of the group in a [[Snapchat]] post on November 9, 2016.<ref name="Us Weekly">{{cite web|last1=Marquina|first1=Sierra|title=Kylie Jenner Denies She's Terror Jr Singer|url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/kylie-jenner-denies-shes-terror-jr-singer-w449637|website=Us Weekly|access-date=10 November 2016}}</ref>

Terror Jr released the song "Heartbreaks" through their YouTube channel on November 24, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|title=Watch a Schoolgirl's Solo Parking Lot Dance-Off in Terror Jr's "heartbreaks" Video {{!}} Thump|url=https://thump.vice.com/en_us/video/terror-jr-heartbreak-music-video|website=Thump|access-date=20 December 2016}}</ref> On February 14, 2017, the group released a remix package of "Come First".<ref name="Pitchkfork">{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/71595-listen-to-terror-jrs-new-song-with-lil-b-and-father/|title=Listen to Terror Jr's New Song With Lil B and Father {{!}} Pitchfork|last1=Strauss|first1=Matthew|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=15 March 2017}}</ref>

In May 2017, the group released "Caramel" and "Death Wish" as the first two songs from ''Bop City 2: TerroRising'', released on June 16, 2017.<ref name="Project U">{{cite web|title=Terror Jr's 'Caramel' contains 7 seconds of a fly buzzing at the start|url=https://www.projectu.tv/terror-jrs-caramel-contains-7-seconds-of-a-fly-buzzing-at-the-start/|website=Project U|access-date=5 May 2017|date=5 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Terror Jr "Death Wishes" (Single Premiere)|url=http://www.josepvinaixa.com/blog/terror-jr-death-wishes/|website=Ultimate Music|access-date=19 May 2017|date=18 May 2017}}</ref>

On September 8, 2017, Terror Jr released a new single "Holding Your Tongue". The single was quickly followed by the announcement of the group's third LP, ''Bop City 3: The Girl Who Cried Purple'', released September 29, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gore|first1=Sydney|title=Terror Jr Bites Back On "Holding Your Tongue"|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/09/10/terror-jr-holding-your-tongue|website=The FADER|access-date=15 September 2017|language=en}}</ref> They released the song "Sad Sad Girl" in December 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gore|first1=Sydney|title=Terror Jr's "Sad Sad Girl" Is A Dark, Twisted Christmas Carol|url=http://trl.mtv.com/news/3052302/terror-jr-sad-sad-girl/?xrs=_s.tw_news|website=MTV News|access-date=12 December 2017|language=en}}</ref>

In 2018, they released the singles "Heaven Wasn't Made For Me" and "A-OK (Everything's Perfect)".<ref name="Highsnobsiety">{{cite web |last1=Gore |first1=Sydney |title=Terror Jr's "Heaven Wasn't Made For Me": Listen |url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/terror-jr-heaven-wasnt-made-for-me/ |website=Highsnobiety |access-date=2 January 2019 |date=2 January 2019}}</ref><ref name="Out - A-OK">{{cite web |last1=Dommu |first1=Rose |title=Bops Only: Terror Jr Channels Political Exhaustion On New Track 'A-OK' |url=https://www.out.com/music/2018/9/30/bops-only-terror-jr-channels-political-exhaustion-new-track-ok |website=www.out.com |access-date=2 January 2019 |language=en |date=30 September 2018}}</ref>

Their debut album ''[[Unfortunately, Terror Jr]]'' was released on January 25, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Jr |first1=Terror |title=We really did it . . . Our debut album is available for pre-order now . . . Unfortunately, Terror Jr will be yours on 1.25.19 |url=https://twitter.com/terrorjrmusic/status/1084873061642383361/video/1 |website=@terrorjrmusic |access-date=14 January 2019 |language=en |date=14 January 2019}}</ref> That same year, the duo departed [[Atlantic Records]]. On October 4, they released the single "9 2 5". On November 8, 2019, the group released the EP ''Come Outside and Break Your Heart'', their first release under their own label, Grape Music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/terror-jr-925-premiere-2640844873.html?rebelltitem=5#rebelltitem5?rebelltitem=5|title=Terror Jr Talk Leaving Atlantic and Their New Horny Bop|date=2019-10-04|website=PAPER|language=en|access-date=2019-11-08}}</ref> On November 13, 2020, they released the EP ''Bloody Waters.''<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-13|title=Terror Jr are in "Bloody Waters" in Pensive New Release|url=https://celebmix.com/terror-jr-bloody-waters/|access-date=2020-11-16|website=CelebMix|language=en-GB}}</ref> Three days later, they announced on their social media that their upcoming album, ''Rancho Catastrophe'', would be released later that week on November 20.<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/terrorjrmusic/status/1328374881147252742|url=https://twitter.com/terrorjrmusic/status/1328374881147252742|access-date=2020-11-16|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>
