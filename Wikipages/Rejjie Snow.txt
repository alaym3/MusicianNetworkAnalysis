'''Alexander Anyaegbunam'''<ref name=fader/> better known by his stage name '''Rejjie Snow''', is an Irish [[Rapping|rapper]] and [[songwriter]] from [[Drumcondra, Dublin|Drumcondra]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Corr |first=Alan |url=https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/music-reviews/2018/0215/941077-rejjie-snow-puts-ireland-on-the-hip-hop-map/ |title=Dear Annie review : Rejjie Snow puts Ireland on the hip hop map |website=Rte.ie |date=2018-02-15 |accessdate=2020-04-11}}</ref> [[Dublin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/new-originals/id-mobile-presents-new-originals-rejjie-snow|publisher=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|date=25 April 2016|title=Traveling Back To Ireland With Rejjie Snow|accessdate=19 May 2016}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Rejjie Snow was born Alexander Anyaegbunam in [[Dublin]], [[Ireland]],<ref name=fader>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/05/02/rejjie-snow-dublin-300-dear-annie-interview |title=Rejjie Snow Is Leaving Ireland To Be An American Rap Success Story |publisher=The Fader |date=2018-02-16 |accessdate=2020-04-11}}</ref> to a Nigerian father and an Irish-Jamaican mother.<ref name=fader/> Dublin is his hometown.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/rejjie-snow-rejovich#_|publisher=[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]]|title=Interview with Rejjie Snow|date=24 June 2013|accessdate=10 October 2013}}</ref> He attended [[Belvedere College SJ]]. Later in 2011, he relocated to the [[United States]] and began attending school at [[Montverde Academy]] in [[Florida]] to play soccer on an athletic scholarship.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maxpreps.com/athlete/alex-anyaegbunam/x5SI7vTqEeKZ5AAmVebBJg/default.htm|publisher=[[MaxPreps]]|title=Alex Anyaegbunam's (Montverde, FL) High School Timeline|accessdate=10 October 2013}}</ref> After graduating high school in 2012,{{citation needed|date=April 2019}} he attended [[Savannah College of Art and Design]] in [[Savannah, Georgia]] on an athletic scholarship to study film and design.{{citation needed|date=April 2019}} After a semester, he dropped out and moved back to [[Ireland]] and focused on his music career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/blogs/poplife/2012/05/17/lecs-luther-aka-rejjie-snow-in-i-d-magazine/|publisher=[[The Irish Times]]|date=17 May 2012|title=Lecs Luther aka Rejjie Snow|accessdate=10 October 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://i-donline.com/2012/05/i-ntroducing-lecs-luther/ |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120622222021/http://i-donline.com/2012/05/i-ntroducing-lecs-luther/ |archivedate=2012-06-22 |title=i-Ntroducing: Lecs Luther &#124; i-D Online |date= |accessdate=2020-04-11}}</ref>{{Failed verification|date=April 2019}} As of May 2021, Snow has a daughter.

==Career==
Snow released his debut EP, ''Rejovich'', in June 2013. It immediately topped the [[iTunes]] Hip-Hop chart, ahead of releases from [[Kanye West]] and [[J Cole|J. Cole]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.factmag.com/2016/04/29/320498rejjie-snow-new-originals/|publisher=Fact Magazine|title=Watch Rejjie Snow tell his story in New Originals short documentary|date=29 April 2016}}</ref> Its track, "1992", went on to surpass one million [[YouTube]] views, along with the previously released "Lost in Empathy".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o-3q0e-ZRsg|publisher=[[YouTube]]|date=9 January 2013|title=Rejjie Snow - "Lost in Empathy" (Official Video) - YouTube|accessdate=10 October 2013}}</ref> In June 2015, Snow released his official debut single with the Cam O’bi-produced "All Around the World".<ref>{{cite web|last=Golden |first=Zara |url=https://www.thefader.com/2015/06/23/rejjie-snow-all-around-the-world |title=Rejjie Snow Premieres New Single "All Around The World" |publisher=The Fader |date=2015-06-23 |accessdate=2020-04-11}}</ref> The video, featuring [[Lily-Rose Depp]], was played 500,000 times in its first week.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6627205/johnny-depp-daughter-lily-rose-depp-music-video-rejjie-snow |title=Johnny Depp's Daughter Lily-Rose Depp Makes Her Music Video Debut: Watch |website=Billboard.com |date=2015-10-07 |accessdate=2020-04-11}}</ref> In December 2015, Snow released his follow-up "Blakkst Skn", produced by [[Kaytranada]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Cliff |first=Aimee |url=https://www.thefader.com/2015/12/07/rejjie-snow-blakkst-skn-video |title=Rejjie Snow Finds Gold In The Gutter In His Weirdest Video Yet |publisher=The Fader |date=2015-12-07 |accessdate=2020-04-11}}</ref>

In 2016, Snow signed with [[300 Entertainment]] (with marketing from [[BMG Rights Management]]), with whom he released the track, "D.R.U.G.S", in September. Snow subsequently confirmed that the long-awaited ''Dear Annie'' was near completion. In May 2017, Snow released a free mixtape, ''The Moon & You''. Snow's debut album ''Dear Annie'' was released on 16 February 2018.
