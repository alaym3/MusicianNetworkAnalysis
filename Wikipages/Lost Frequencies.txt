'''Felix De Laet''' (born 30 November 1993),<ref>[http://www.top40.nl/top40-artiesten/lost-frequencies Dutch Top 40 Profile]</ref> known by his [[stage name]] '''Lost Frequencies''', is a Belgian [[Disc jockey|DJ]] and [[record producer]]. He is best known for his [[Single (music)|singles]] "[[Are You with Me#Lost Frequencies version|Are You with Me]]" in 2014, "[[Reality (Lost Frequencies song)|Reality]]" in 2015, and "[[Where Are You Now (Lost Frequencies song)|Where Are You Now]]" with [[Calum Scott]] in 2021.

== Background ==

=== Name origin ===
The name "Lost Frequencies" was first used when De Laet had used old songs, which he calls 'lost frequencies' to make remixes when he started the project.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ministryofsound.com.au/latest/get-know-lost-frequencies/#tD5km6WbcDGMeWl4.97|title=Get to Know Belgian Hitmaker Lost Frequencies|date=8 September 2015|website=Ministry of Sound|access-date=2016-12-12|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160314215220/http://www.ministryofsound.com.au/latest/get-know-lost-frequencies/#tD5km6WbcDGMeWl4.97|archive-date=14 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==

===2014–2015: "Are You with Me"===
In 2014, Lost Frequencies made a [[remix]] of [[Country music|country]] artist [[Easton Corbin]]'s "[[Are You with Me#Lost Frequencies version|Are You with Me]]". The song had originally appeared in Corbin's second studio album, ''[[All Over the Road]]'', but had not been released as a single. Lost Frequencies remixed the song and uploaded it to [[SoundCloud]]. [[Armada Music]] A&R, and founder of The Bearded Man label, Marwen Tlili<ref>http://djmag.nl/comin/05202015-1355/vital-label-bearded-man</ref> discovered the record and subsequently signed it. The official release was on 27 October 2014. The track topped Belgium's [[Ultratop]] chart on 15 November 2014, reaching number two in the Walloon chart as well. In 2015, the single peaked the top of the charts of Australia and Austria. It was also charted in several countries in Europe, reaching the top 10 in Finland, France, Netherlands, Norway, Spain, Germany, Poland, Ireland, Switzerland, United Kingdom, Sweden and Turkey.

=== 2016–2017: ''Less Is More'' ===
On 3 June 2016, Lost Frequencies released a single, "[[Beautiful Life (Lost Frequencies song)|Beautiful Life]]", featuring Sandro Cavazza, which is the lead single for his debut studio album. He announced on [[Twitter]] that he planned to release the album in September 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/LFrequencies/status/747720713927286785|title=Lost Frequencies on Twitter|access-date=2016-07-06}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/LFrequencies/status/747728255910817792|title=Lost Frequencies on Twitter|access-date=2016-07-06}}</ref> On 8 September, he remixed a [[Major Lazer]] song "[[Cold Water (song)|Cold Water]]" featuring [[Justin Bieber]]. He released his debut studio album titled ''[[Less Is More (Lost Frequencies album)|Less Is More]]'' on 21 October 2016. It includes his massive hit "[[Are You with Me]]" as well as follow-up singles "[[Reality (Lost Frequencies song)|Reality]]", "Beautiful Life", "[[What Is Love 2016]]" and "All Or Nothing" ft. [[Axel Ehnström]]. They are soon joining [[The Chainsmokers]] on tour in May and June 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://instagram.com/p/BP8BlzxgRBt|title=Lost Frequencies on Instagram|access-date=2017-01-31}}</ref> ''[[Less Is More (Lost Frequencies album)|Less Is More]]'' was shortlisted by [[IMPALA]] (The Independent Music Companies Association) for the Album of the Year Award 2016, which rewards on a yearly basis the best album released on an independent European label.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://impalamusic.org/content/25-artists-best-independent-album-year-europe/ | title=25 artists up for best independent album of the year in Europe| publisher=IMPALA | access-date=14 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title =  All Lost Frequencies Lyrics |url = https://www.lyricsgetit.com/lost-frequencies-lyrics-hnbhem.html|website = LyricsGetit.com|access-date = 10 August 2016}}</ref>

===2018–present: Found Frequencies===
In partnership with [[Armada Music]], De Laet launched his own record label named Found Frequencies, stating "it will serve as a home" for his music and as the "go-to label for up-and-coming talents".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.ravejungle.com/2017/11/24/lost-frequencies-found-frequencies-crazy/ |title=Lost Frequencies announces his own label 'Found Frequencies' with release 'Crazy' |date=2017-11-24 |website=Rave Jungle |language=en-US |access-date=2018-05-01}}</ref> His second studio album titled ''[[Alive and Feeling Fine]]'' was released on 4 October 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/lisakocay/2019/10/04/lost-frequencies-alive-and-feeling-fine-album/|title=Lost Frequencies Releases 'Alive And Feeling Fine' Album|first=Lisa|last=Kocay|date=4 October 2019|website=[[Forbes]]|access-date=20 October 2019}}</ref>
