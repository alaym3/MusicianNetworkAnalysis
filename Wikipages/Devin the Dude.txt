'''Devin Copeland''' (born June 4, 1970), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Devin the Dude''', is an American rapper from [[Houston, Texas]]. He is known for his unique rapping style and his 2002 songs "Lacville '79" and "Doobie Ashtray".

==Early life==
Devin Copeland was born in [[Pontiac, Michigan]] on June 4, 1970. He spent most of his early childhood in [[St. Petersburg, Florida]] and moved to [[Texas]] while in the fourth grade.<ref name="Allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://allmusic.com/artist/devin-the-dude-p545921/biography|title=Devin the Dude Biography|last=Salazar-Moreno|first=Quibian|work=Allmusic|access-date=2011-01-01}}</ref> He spent the rest of his youth moving back and forth between [[New Boston, Texas|New Boston]] and [[Houston]], finally settling in Houston after graduating from high school. He smoked [[marijuana]] for the first time at a [[Roller rink|skating rink]] in seventh grade; marijuana would later become a major influence on his music.<ref name="Dubcnn">{{cite web|url=http://www.dubcnn.com/interviews/devinthedude07/|title=Devin the Dude Interview|work=Dubcnn.com|date=February 2007|access-date=2011-01-01}}</ref> As a teenager, Copeland became interested in [[breakdancing]], joining several dance crews until he began rapping, which soon became his main interest.<ref name="Dubcnn"/> After graduating from high school, he met Rob Quest, a [[Blindness|blind]] rapper and record producer, and Jugg Mugg. The trio formed the group the [[Odd Squad (hip-hop group)|Odd Squad]].<ref name="Allmusic"/>

==Musical career==
After forming the Odd Squad (later known as the Coughee Brothaz), the group signed to [[Rap-A-Lot Records]]. The label is notable for being the home of [[Hip hop music|hip-hop]] artists such as [[Geto Boys]], [[Scarface (rapper)|Scarface]], and [[Too Much Trouble]]. Copeland moved on to become part of Scarface's [[Facemob]] before going solo in 1998.<ref name="Shapiro">Shapiro, Peter (2005) ''The Rough Guide to Hip-Hop'', Rough Guides, {{ISBN|1-84353-263-8}}, p.86-87</ref> Copeland has released ten solo albums: ''[[The Dude (Devin the Dude album)|The Dude]]'' ([[1998 in music|1998]]), ''[[Just Tryin' ta Live]]'' ([[2002 in music|2002]]), ''[[To tha X-Treme]]'' ([[2004 in music|2004]]), ''[[Waitin' to Inhale]]'' ([[2007 in music|2007]]), ''[[Landing Gear]]'' ([[2008 in music|2008]]), ''[[Suite 420]]'' ([[2010 in music|2010]]), ''[[Gotta Be Me]]'' (2010), ''[[One for the Road (Devin the Dude album)|One for the Road]]'' (2013), Acoustic Levitation (2017) and Still Rollin' Up: Somethin' To Ride With (2019). He also made a number of guest appearances, including on [[Dr. Dre]]'s "Fuck You" in 1999, [[De La Soul]]'s "Baby Phat" in 2001, [[Slim Thug]]'s "I'm Back" off of Boss of All Bosses in 2009, [[Gucci Mane]]'s "Kush Is My Cologne" of off The State vs Radric Davis in 2009 alongside [[Bun B]] & [[E-40]], [[Tech N9ne]]'s "After Party" in 2010 off of The Gates Mixed Plate, and Young Jeezy's "Higher Learning" off of the late 2011 album Thug Motivation 103: Hustlerz Ambition.

In 2007, he appeared in a documentary titled ''Screwed In Houston'' produced by ''VBS/Vice Magazine'' that details the history of the Houston rap scene.

In 2008, he ended his 15-year relationship with Houston-based [[Rap-A-Lot Records]] after he decided not to renew his contract.<ref>Richburg, Chris (2008) "[http://allhiphop.com/stories/news/archive/2008/02/05/19242606.aspx Devin The Dude Leaves Rap-A-Lot]", ''Allhiphop.com'', 5 February 2008</ref> Later that year, he signed with indie label [[Razor & Tie]].<ref>Crosley, Hillary (2008) "[http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1044781/devin-the-dude-inks-with-razor-tie Devin The Dude Inks with Razor and Tie]", ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'',  15 July 2008</ref> Devin is currently being distributed by [[E1 Entertainment]], formerly Koch. In 2013 Devin starred in the [[stoner comedy]] ''Highway 420'', in which two pot smoking buddies go on a quest to find the best bud in the country on the legendary Highway 420. The soundtrack featured songs by [[2 Chainz]], [[UGK]], [[Smoke DZA]], [[Tha Dogg Pound]], [[Slim Thug]], [[Curren$y]], [[David Banner]], [[Asher Roth]] and more.<ref name="www.hiphopdx.com">{{cite web | title = Devin The Dude Announces "Highway" Film & Soundtrack | url = http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.21826/title.devin-the-dude-announces-highway-film-soundtrack | access-date = 10 November 2012 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20121111130902/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.21826/title.devin-the-dude-announces-highway-film-soundtrack/ | archive-date = 11 November 2012 | url-status = dead }}</ref> On June 24, 2013 Devin announced that his eighth studio album would be titled ''[[One for the Road (Devin the Dude album)|One for the Road]]'' and be released in September 2013. It would later be confirmed for an October 8, 2013 release.<ref>{{cite web |last=Paine |first=Jake |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24430/title.devin-the-dude-reveals-eighth-solo-album-title |title=Devin The Dude Reveals Eighth Solo Album Title {{pipe}} Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-06-24 |access-date=2013-09-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130630112458/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24430/title.devin-the-dude-reveals-eighth-solo-album-title |archive-date=2013-06-30 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Reception==

Despite being a critical success, Devin the Dude has not achieved mainstream success. ''[[The New York Times]]'' has called him "A brilliant oddball with a spaced-out flow."<ref>Sanneh, Kalefa (2005) "[https://www.nytimes.com/2005/04/17/arts/music/17BSann.html A Visitor's Guide to the Houston Sound]", ''The New York Times'', 17 April 2005</ref> In addition, he has been called "Rap's best-kept secret" and "Your favorite rapper's favorite rapper".<ref>Martinez, Rafael (2000) "[http://www.prefixmag.com/features/devin-the-dude/your-favorite-rappers-favorite-rapper/12400 Devin the Dude: Every Rapper's Favourite Rapper]", ''Prefix'', 1 January 2000</ref>
