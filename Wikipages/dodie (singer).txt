'''Dorothy Miranda Clark'''<ref>{{cite video|url=https://youtube.com/watch?v=2dDTUCV5Tr0?t=4m31s|publisher=[[YouTube]]|last=Clark|first=Dodie|title=You got questions, kid? I got answers|date=31 August 2015|access-date=8 December 2016}}</ref> (born 12 April 1995), known mononymously as '''Dodie''' (stylised '''dodie'''), is an English singer-songwriter, author, and [[YouTuber]] from [[Epping, Essex]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Clark|first1=Dodie|title=doddleoddle|url=https://twitter.com/doddleoddle/status/666736548029833216?lang=en|website=Twitter|publisher=Twitter|access-date=4 January 2017|date=17 November 2015}}</ref> Dodie began her career uploading original songs and covers to Youtube. She<!-- Dodie uses she or they, so there is no need to change this --> has over 160 videos, over 1.9M subscribers, and over 340 million views on her main channel (as of February 2020).<ref name="youtube.com1"/> On her side channel, she has 350+ videos, over 900k subscribers, and over 94 million views.<ref name="youtube.com"/>
On 9 December 2016, she released the first video on her [[Vevo]] channel, dodieVEVO, where she has over 47&nbsp;million views. She has collaborated with many other musicians, including [[Tessa Violet]], [[Julia Nunes]], [[Lauren Aquilina]], [[Orla Gartland]], [[Thomas Sanders (entertainer)|Thomas Sanders]], [[Jacob Collier]], [[Emma Blackery]], [[Pomplamoose]], and Flashback.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCayDH74OUbNnLgmZ1uy136g|title=dodieVEVO|website=YouTube|access-date=2 October 2017}}</ref> In 2018, Dodie collaborated with singer [[Tom Walker (singer)|Tom Walker]] on "Human", the lead single from her third EP, also titled ''Human''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/66CvE1ZO3bSW5OODC6C0HK?si=otCiQVvLSMWKOwLdYGxBxA|title=dodie, Tom Walker - Human|website=Spotify|access-date=24 January 2019}}</ref>

Dodie has released three independent EPs—''[[Intertwined]]'' (2016), ''[[You (EP)|You]]'' (2017), and ''[[Human (Dodie EP)|Human]]'' (2019)—all of which charted within the top 40 of the official [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/50423/dodie/|title=dodie|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]}}</ref> Her debut studio album ''[[Build a Problem]]'' was released on 7 May 2021, and peaked at number 3 on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1318162668751081473|user=doddleoddle|title=my debut album BUILD A PROBLEM is out March 5th|author=dodie}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Dodie was born Dorothy Miranda Clark in [[Enfield, London, England]], to mother Astrid Clark and father Ralph Clark.{{cn|date=May 2021}} She is the second child of three, including older brother Iain Clark, and younger sister Heather "Hedy" Clark. Hedy appeared in several of Dodie's earlier YouTube videos, and later began a channel of her own.<ref>{{Citation|last=doddleoddle|title=Stuck The Way We Are - Original Song|date=4 June 2012|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uSnIwrcaAPQ|access-date=15 April 2019}}</ref>

Dodie attended [[The Leventhorpe School]].<ref>{{cite journal |publisher=[[The Leventhorpe School]] |url=http://www.leventhorpe.net/news-and-events/newsletter/2012/03/44.aspx |journal=The Leventhorpe Newsletter|issue=44|date=15 March 2012|title=Dodie Clark - Youtube Star}}</ref> After leaving school she moved to [[Bath, Somerset|Bath]], England, with Jamie Jo, who is also a YouTuber and a painter.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/Bananajamana|title=BananaJamana|website=YouTube|language=en|access-date=15 April 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
===2007–2016: YouTube beginnings and ''Intertwined''===
Dodie's first channel was called "Dodders5," now known as the "Alice and Dodie show!" which was created on 1 August 2007 and shared with her friend Alice Webb.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/dodders5|title=dodders5|website=YouTube|access-date=6 August 2017}}</ref>

Dodie's main channel "doddleoddle" was created on 7 February 2011; her first video on that channel, an original song called "Rain," was uploaded on 14 April that year. In addition to her main channel, Dodie has a [[VEVO]] channel, "dodieVEVO",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCayDH74OUbNnLgmZ1uy136g|title=dodieVEVO|website=YouTube}}</ref> and a side channel, "doddlevloggle".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/doddlevloggle|title=doddlevloggle|last=Clark|first=Dodie|publisher=YouTube|access-date=3 January 2017}}</ref>

On 18 November 2016, Dodie self-released her first EP, ''Intertwined''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teneightymagazine.com/2016/10/15/dodie-announces-ep-and-headline-show|title=Dodie Releases EP and Sells Out Headline Show|last=Riley|first=Conor|date=25 November 2016|publisher=TenEighty Magazine|access-date=8 December 2016}}</ref> Despite its unsigned status, the EP reached number 35 on the official UK album charts during its first week of release.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/search/albums/intertwined|title=Official Albums Chart Results|date=1 December 2016|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=8 December 2016}}</ref>

Dodie also co-hosted [[Coca-Cola]]'s CokeTV alongside fellow YouTuber Manny Brown, where they took part in various activities.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.coca-cola.co.uk/stories/coketv-and-chill-tune-in-to-coca-colas-new-youtube-channel-with-hosts-manny-and-dodie|title=CokeTV and chill? Tune in to Coca-Cola's new YouTube channel with hosts Manny and Dodie|publisher=[[Coca-Cola]]|last=Hepburn|first=Matthew|date=23 June 2016|access-date=8 December 2016}}</ref>

===2017–2019: ''You'', ''Secrets For the Mad'' and ''Human''===
{{Main|You (EP)|Human (Dodie EP)}}
Dodie's second EP, ''You'', was released on 11 August 2017. In its opening week of release, the EP debuted at number 6 on the official UK Albums Chart—29 places higher than the peak of ''Intertwined'', thereby marking a new personal best for Dodie.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/ed-sheeran-pips-elvis-presley-to-albums-number-1-in-one-of-the-years-closest-chart-races__19986/|title=Ed Sheeran pips Elvis Presley to Albums Number 1 in one of the year's closest chart races|date=18 August 2017|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|last=White|first=Jack|access-date=18 August 2017}}</ref> It also debuted at number 55 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7596042/dodie/chart?f=305|title=Dodie – Chart history (Billboard 200)|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=22 August 2017}}</ref>

In June 2017, Dodie announced that she was writing a book.<ref>{{Citation|last=Clark|first=Dodie|title=party tattoos – original song {{!}}{{!}} dodie|date=15 June 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0JtISvgf2Aw|access-date=14 July 2018}}</ref> On 2 November 2017, Dodie published her autobiographical book titled ''Secrets for the Mad: Obsessions, Confessions and Life Lessons.''

In 2018, she was featured in a collaboration with [[Faultline (musician)|Faultline]], a cover of the song "[[All I Do Is Dream of You]]" which was featured in an advertising campaign by [[Audi]] in the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tvadmusic.co.uk/2018/07/audi-approved-dream/ |title=Audi Approved – Dream |date=2 July 2018 |access-date=8 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b5T81OvRPu4 |title=Audi Approved 'Dream' |author=Audi |date=12 June 2018 |access-date=8 July 2018}}</ref>

On 18 September 2018, Dodie announced her third EP, entitled ''[[Human (Dodie EP)|Human]]'', which came out on 18 January 2019, with the title track being the lead single released on 21 September.  The EP rose to number 5 on the official UK Albums Chart.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vicarstreet.ie/component/thelist/show/1328-dodie-vicar-sreet-live-in-vicar-street-dublin-8-Ireland-on-18-Mar-2019.html?Itemid=1328 |title=Dodie Live at Vicar Street |website=vicarstreet.ie |access-date=24 September 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180924185535/http://www.vicarstreet.ie/component/thelist/show/1328-dodie-vicar-sreet-live-in-vicar-street-dublin-8-Ireland-on-18-Mar-2019.html?Itemid=1328 |archive-date=24 September 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Build a Problem''===
{{Main|Build a Problem}}
In 2019, she also was a part of the ''[[Moominvalley (TV series)|Moominvalley]]'' soundtrack with a song titled Ready Now. Later that year they released a single called Guiltless, as well as a collaboration with [[Jacob Collier]], with a cover of [[Here Comes the Sun]]. Following the release of her collaboration with [[Jacob Collier]], she announced the release of a new single "[[Boys Like You (Dodie Clark song)|Boys Like You]]" on 6 September. It was released on 27 September.

On 19 October 2020, Dodie announced she would be releasing her first full-length studio album ''[[Build a Problem]]'' on 5 March 2021. The first single, "Cool Girl" was premiered on BBC Radio and released on multiple streaming platforms.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CGhjpvwB0Wx/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link|title = Login • Instagram}}</ref> On 8 February 2021, it was announced that the album was to be delayed until 26 March 2021 due to complications from the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] and [[Brexit]].<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U2lJ5wWcsgg |title=some news : l |author=Dodie |date=8 February 2021 |access-date=24 February 2021}}</ref> She later made an announcement on 11 March that it would be further delayed until 7 May 2021.<ref>{{Cite tweet|title=due to delays of vinyl production bc of The Pandemic and Brexshit album has now been pushed back until May 7th|author=Dodie|user=doddleoddle|number=1370087658144239617|access-date=2021-03-11|language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In May 2016, Dodie [[came out]] as [[bisexual]] in a YouTube video,<ref>{{cite news |last=Lanning |first=Carly |date=November 20, 2016 |title=For Dodie Clark, opening up about domestic abuse meant educating thousands |work=[[Daily Dot]] |url=https://www.dailydot.com/upstream/dodie-clark-youtube-abuse-interwined/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161121115231/https://www.dailydot.com/upstream/dodie-clark-youtube-abuse-interwined/ |archive-date=November 21, 2016 |url-status=live}}</ref> and later, in 2017, she released a song entitled "''I'm bisexual—a coming out song!''".<ref>{{cite news |last=Azzopardi |first=Chris |date=April 8, 2021 |title=Dodie is building an understanding of herself |work=Out in Jersey |url=https://outinjersey.net/dodie-is-building-an-understanding-of-herself/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210917121340/https://outinjersey.net/dodie-is-building-an-understanding-of-herself/ |archive-date=September 17, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref>

Dodie uses both she/her and they/them pronouns, according to her Twitter<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dodie's Twitter account|url=https://twitter.com/doddleoddle|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-21|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> and Instagram<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dodie's Instagram Account|url=https://www.instagram.com/doddleoddle/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-21|website=www.instagram.com}}</ref> bios.
