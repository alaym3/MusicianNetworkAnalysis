'''QUIN''', born '''Bianca Leonor Quiñones''',<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://vmagazine.com/article/next-wave-quin/|title=The Next Wave: Quiñ {{!}} V Magazine|website=vmagazine.com|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://desphosphenes.com/2017/12/18/quin/|title=QUIÑ|date=2017-12-18|website=PHOSPHENES|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref> is an [[United States|American]] Afro-[[Hispanic and Latino Americans|Latina]] singer/songwriter/producer born in [[Los Angeles]], Calif.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://officemagazine.net/qui%C3%B1-sxsw|title=QUIÑ at SXSW|date=2017-03-31|website=Office Magazine|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://galoremag.com/meet-quin-the-la-based-singer-whose-music-spreads-love/|title=Meet QuiÃ±: The LA-Based Singer Whose Music Spreads Love|date=2015-10-09|website=Galore|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref>

She is currently signed to Interscope Records/Fantasy Soul.

== Early life ==
QUIN’s passion for music and performing began as a child as she grew up around her musician father play the conga drums and trumpet,<ref name=":3" /> her mother was a dancer and school teacher,<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2016/10/meet-quin-interview|title=Interview: Singer QUIN Talks Shedding Her Fears & New Album|date=2016-10-28|website=Vibe|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref> and she sang in her church and high school choirs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nylon.com/articles/quin-interview|title=Meet QUIÃ', Your New Favorite Pop Star From Planet 'Galactica'|date=2016-10-28|website=NYLON|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.baystatebanner.com/2017/06/01/5-questions-quin/|title=5 questions: QUIÃ'|date=2017-06-01|website=The Bay State Banner|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref name=":0" /><ref name=":4" /> According to QUIN’s mother, the singer wrote her first song at a very young age after the birth of her sister, Bailey Rose.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=http://popcrush.com/quin-dream-girl-popcrush-presents-interview/|title=QUIN Is Ready to Be Everyone's 'Dream Girl'|last=Tan|first=Emily|website=PopCrush|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref>

She attended school in Pasadena, California.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":0" /> After graduating from [[Pasadena High School (California)|Pasadena High School]], she moved on to college at [[California State University, Northridge|Cal State Northridge]] in [[California]]. After a year and a half of studying Psychology, QUIN moved to West Oakland<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":0" /> and began focusing on music full time at age 19.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":1" />

== Career ==
QUIN dropped her debut EP ''Galactica''<ref name=":1" /> in October 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/09/28/quin-galacticat-video-premiere|title=QUIÑ Teases Her Upcoming EP With A Cosmic Visual For "Galacticat"|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref> which includes a feature from [[G-Eazy]]<ref name=":1" /> on the song “OVER AGAIN.” She describes the EP as “the first chapter to my Fantasy Soul Universe.”<ref name=":4"/> Her song “LIGHTSPEED” was involved in the Visions of Harmony project with [[Apple Music]] and [[NASA]], which aspired to educate the public on the [[Juno (spacecraft)|Juno spacecraft]] by combining science and art.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/tech/2016/06/30/apple-making-music-space/86562950/|title=Apple links with NASA to make music from space|website=USA TODAY|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://gizmodo.com/nasa-and-apple-collaborate-on-short-film-inspired-by-ju-1782919664|title=NASA and Apple Collaborate on Short Film Inspired by Juno Mission|last=Ouellette|first=Jennifer|website=Gizmodo|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.macrumors.com/2016/06/30/apple-music-nasa-juno-jupiter-mission/|title=Apple Music and NASA Team Up on Short Film and Exclusive Songs Celebrating Juno Mission|last=Clover|first=Juli|website=www.macrumors.com|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref name=":1" />

A year later, in September 2017,<ref name=":5" /><ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7949232/quin-dream-girl-new-ep-interview|title=Rising Star QUIÑ Talks New EP and Redefining Soul Music|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref> QUIN released her ''DREAMGIRL'' EP,<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestranger.com/events/24587022/gnash-goody-grace-quin-triangle-park|title=Gnash, Goody Grace, Quin, Triangle Park|website=The Stranger|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://missbish.com/committoyou-missbish-apparel-quin/|title=#CommitToYou in MISSBISH Apparel {{!}} QUIÑ|date=2018-01-29|website=MISSBISH - She describes her sound as “fantasy soul” and music that “has the power of versatility” and in 2017, QUIN introduced her label, Fantasy Soul, named after the genre she coined.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicinsf.com/qa-quin/|title=Q&A: QUIÑ {{!}} Music in SF|date=2017-08-31|website=Music in SF® {{!}} The authority on the San Francisco Music Scene|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.laweekly.com/music/fantasy-soul-singer-quin-will-demonstrate-her-unique-genre-at-smokin-grooves-9540255|title=Fantasy Soul Singer QUIÃ' Will Demonstrate Her Unique Genre at Smokin' Grooves|last=Ju|first=Shirley|date=2018-06-11|website=L.A. Weekly|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/quin-galactica-interview|title=Meet the "Fantasy-Soul" Singer You Need to Put On Your Playlists|last=Nast|first=Condé|website=Teen Vogue|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref name=":4"/><ref name=":8" />

In August 2018, she released her song “Remind Me”,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2018/08/208823/new-music-august-31-2018|title=New Music To Know This Week: HAERTS Return, Vivian Green Gets Slutry & More|last=Smith|first=Courtney E.|website=www.refinery29.com|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref><ref name=":9">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/08/30/quin-remind-me-lucid|title=QUIÑ's "REMIND ME" is a sweet antidote for all your life's worries|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref> described by [[The Fader|The FADER]] as “daydream-inducing antidote for all life's worries.<ref name=":9" />” Later that year, QUIN teamed up with [[Vans]] for a campaign highlighting the importance of creativity and personal style.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kicksonfire.com/vans-partners-with-artists-athletes-musicians-to-celebrate-creativity-through-personal-style/|title=Vans Partners With Artists, Athletes & Musicians To Celebrate Creativity Through Personal Style • KicksOnFire.com|last=JuanofaKind|date=2018-09-11|website=KicksOnFire.com|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref>

In June 2019,she signed to Interscope Records and also has her own label Fantasy Soul under Universal Music then released her fourth EP "LUCID" in November and the EP features two collabs with Atlanta rapper 6lack including their latest single Mushroom Chocolate.

She is currently working on her debut album for Interscope and Fantasy Soul Records.

QUIN has opened for [[Erykah Badu]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.theroguemag.com/features/quin|title=QUIÑ|website=Rogue Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref> [[Gnash (musician)|Gnash]]<ref name=":7" /> [[JMSN]], and performed at THE FADER Fort at [[South by Southwest|SXSW]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ticketfly.com/event/1426651-jmsn-brooklyn/|title=JMSN - Tickets - Warsaw - Brooklyn, NY - May 26th, 2017|last1=Ave.Brooklyn|first1=Warsaw 261 Driggs|last2=NY|website=Ticketfly|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-30|last3=ages|first3=11222 Doors 7:30 PM / Show 8:30 PM This event is all}}</ref> [[Afropunk Festival|AFROPUNK]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://afropunk.com/tag/quin/|title=QUIÑ|website=AFROPUNK|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-30}}</ref> and Galore’s Girl Cult Festival.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.laweekly.com/promotions/free-stuff/win-a-pair-of-tickets-to-girl-cult-festival-8-20-8527849|title=Win a pair of tickets to Girl Cult Festival 8/20! {{!}} Los Angeles Free Stuff|website=L.A.
