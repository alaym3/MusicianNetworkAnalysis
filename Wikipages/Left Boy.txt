'''Ferdinand Sarnitz''' (born 17 December 1988),<ref name="standard">{{cite web |url=http://derstandard.at/1323916665733/Andre-Hellers-Patchwork-Familie-Familie-ist-doch-eigentlich-jeder-den-man-einlaedt-Familie-zu-sein |title=Familie ist doch eigentlich jeder, den man einlädt, Familie zu sein |date=December 16, 2011 |work=[[Der Standard]] |publisher=Standard Verlagsgesellschaft m. b. H |language=German |accessdate=September 23, 2012}}</ref> known by his stage name '''Left Boy''', is an Austrian singer and producer from [[Vienna]], Austria.

== Biography ==
[[File:Left Boy - Rock am Ring 2019-3033.jpg|thumb|Left Boy live at [[Rock am Ring]] 2019.]]
Sarnitz was born on 17 December 1988 in Vienna, Austria to Austrian musician [[André Heller]] and Sabina Sarnitz. He attended the [[American International School of Vienna]] and graduated in 2007. Sarnitz spent much of his free time at school rapping. At the age of 18, Sarnitz went to New York City to study [[audio engineering]] at the [[Institute of Audio Research]] for a year.<ref name="standard"/> After living in his hometown Vienna for a short while, he decided to move to [[Brooklyn]] to live in a shared apartment with two directors, a producer and a photographer.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.juice.de/news/left-boy-guns-bitches-weed |title=Left Boy – Guns, Bitches, Weed |date=July 27, 2012 |work=Juice Magazine |publisher=piranha media GmbH |language=German |accessdate=September 23, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mica.at/magazin/left-boy/mica-interview-mit-left-boy |title=mica-Interview mit Left Boy |date=February 27, 2012 |work=mica – music austria |publisher=Music Information Center Austria |language=German |accessdate=September 23, 2012}}</ref><ref name="thegap"/>

In December 2010, Sarnitz released his first mixtape ''The Second Coming'' for free download.  In mid-2011, he started making music videos for all of the songs.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.seaoflove.de/lineup/left-boy/ |title=Sea of Love: Left Boy |work=Sea of Love |language=German |accessdate=September 23, 2012}}</ref> Sarnitz often uses [[Sampling (music)|samples]] for his English songs which haven't been released for usability which is why an official sound carrier couldn't be released.<ref name="thegap">{{cite web |url=http://www.thegap.at/musikstories/artikel/15-minutes-to-fame/seite-2/ |title=15 Minutes To Fame |date=March 2, 2012 |work=The Gap |publisher=Monopol Medien GmbH |language=German |accessdate=September 23, 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121101144107/http://www.thegap.at/musikstories/artikel/15-minutes-to-fame/seite-2/ |archivedate=November 1, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> Even though he hadn't been signed to a record label, he was able to perform at festivals in 2012, including "Sea of Love" and "HipHop Open". Live, he is accompanied by the dance group "Urban Movement". Sarnitz has a son called Yves-Louis.<ref name="standard"/> He cites [[Wu-Tang Clan]], [[Oxmo Puccino]], [[De La Soul]], [[Atmosphere (music group)|Atmosphere]], [[Ugly Duckling (hip hop group)|Ugly Duckling]], [[Daft Punk]], [[Édith Piaf]], [[Nina Simone]], [[Oumou Sangaré]], and [[Gipsy Kings]] as his inspirations.<ref name="thegap"/>

In 2015, he rapped in the Official Theme Tune for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2015]] "Building Bridges".
