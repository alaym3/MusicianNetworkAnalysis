'''Isabelle Geffroy'''<ref name="Raphaël3">{{cite web |url=http://www.purepeople.com/article/zaz-de-saltimbanque-chanteuse-de-rue-au-sommet-des-charts_a56802/1 |title=Zaz : de saltimbanque chanteuse de rue... au sommet des charts !|publisher=Purepeople |language=fr |access-date=28 May 2010}}</ref> (born 1 May 1980), known professionally as '''Zaz''', is a French singer-songwriter who mixes [[jazz]]y styles, [[Music of France|French variety]], [[Soul music|soul]] and [[Acoustic music|acoustic]]. She is known for her single "[[Je veux]]", taken from her [[Zaz (album)|self-titled debut album]], released on 10 May 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.2424actu.fr/actualite-culturelle/interview-et-live-de-zaz-chanteuse-857255/#read-857255 |title=interview et live de Zaz, chanteuse aux actualités A2 |publisher=2424actu |language=fr |access-date=28 May 2010}}</ref>

==Early life==
Zaz was born in [[Tours]], France. Her mother was a Spanish teacher, and her father worked for an electric company. In 1985, she entered the Conservatoire de Tours with her sister and her brother, attending courses from the ages of 6 to 11. She studied music theory, specifically the violin, piano, guitar, and choral singing. In 1994, she moved to [[Bordeaux]]. In 1995, she took singing lessons and played sports for a year in Bordeaux. In 2000, she won a scholarship from the regional council, which allowed her to join a school of modern music, the CIAM (Centre for Musical Activities and Information) of Bordeaux. Her musical influences included 'Four Seasons' by Vivaldi, jazz singers such as [[Ella Fitzgerald]], and other singers such as [[Enrico Macias]], [[Bobby McFerrin]], and [[Richard Bona]], as well as African, Latin, and Cuban rhythms. In 2006, she moved to Paris.

==Career==
[[File:Zaz Benoit Simon.JPG|thumb|left|Zaz performing with Benoit Simon at the [[TFF Rudolstadt]] festival in 2011]]
In 2001, she started her singing career in the blues band "Fifty Fingers". She sang in musical groups in [[Angoulême]], especially in a jazz quintet.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.respectmag.com/2010/09/20/zaz-na-pas-la-voix-dans-sa-poche-4237 |title=Zaz n'a pas la voix dans sa poche |publisher=Respectmag |language=fr |access-date=5 January 2011}}</ref> She became one of the four singers of Izar-Adatz (Basque for "Shooting Star"), a variety band which consisted of sixteen people with whom she toured for two years, especially in the [[Midi-Pyrenees]] and the [[French Basque Country|Basque Country]]. She worked in the studio as a backing singer in Toulouse and performed with many singers, including Maeso, [[Art Mengo]], Vladimir Max, [[Jean-Pierre Mader]], and Serge Guerao.

In 2011, Zaz won an [[EBBA Awards|EBBA]] Award. Every year the European Border Breakers Awards (EBBA) recognize the success of ten emerging artists or groups who reached audiences outside their own countries with their first internationally released album in the past year.

In May 2010, French magazine ''[[Télérama]]'' announced: "Rumor has swelled in recent weeks: Zaz is an extraordinary voice, and she will be the revelation of the summer!".<ref name="Zaz - Zaz - Chanson - Télérama.fr">{{cite journal |url=http://www.telerama.fr/musiques/zaz,55742.php |title=Zaz – chanson |journal=Telerama |language=fr |access-date=4 January 2011}}</ref> On 10 May 2010, Zaz released her first album. It contains songs she wrote ("Trop sensible") and co-composed ("Les passants", "Le long de la route", "Prends garde à ta langue", "J'aime à nouveau", "Ni oui ni non"). Kerredine Soltani produced the album on the label "Play On" and wrote and composed the hit single "[[Je veux (Zaz song)|Je veux]]". The pop singer [[Raphaël Haroche]] wrote her songs "[[Éblouie par la nuit]]", "Port Coton" and "La fée". In 2010, she signed a contract for her tours with Caramba and publisher Sony ATV. She was invited to make several television appearances (such as ''[[Taratata]]'' or ''[[w:fr:Chabada|Chabada]]'') and was featured in several programs on the radio. On Sunday 6 October 2013, Zaz appeared on [[BBC One]]'s ''[[The Andrew Marr Show]]'' in London and sang "Je veux" live.

Zaz then toured [[France]] ([[Paris]], [[La Rochelle]], [[Montauban]], [[Saint-Ouen, Seine-Saint-Denis|Saint-Ouen]], Chateauroux, [[Landerneau]], [[Fécamp]]...), performed at the [[Les FrancoFolies de Montréal|Francofolies]] of [[Montreal]] ([[Canada]]), and sang in [[Monthey]] ([[Switzerland]]), [[Brussels]], [[Berlin]], and [[Milan]]. In autumn Zaz topped the charts in [[Belgium]], Switzerland, and [[Austria]]. Matthieu Baligand, her manager and producer at Caramba Entertainment, explained to ''[[Libération]]'': ''"There's a lot about her right now and people want to see her tour... But despite the demand, it seems preferable for her to begin touring in fifty small places, which will make her more credible. Zaz is a popular, intuitive artist, who is familiar with music, who can sing, but doing a quality show is something else (...)."'' In November 2010, the debut album ''Zaz'' became double platinum and she was awarded "Revelation Song" by the [[Académie Charles Cros]]. Zaz also won the [[European Border Breakers Award]]: she was named the French artist most played abroad in 2010. According to a survey published by ''L'Internaute'', Zaz was the most popular French singer in the 2010 ranking.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.linternaute.com/actualite/personnalites/francais-annee-2010/zaz-personnalite-de-l-annee.shtml |title=Musique: Zaz personnalité préférée de l'année |publisher=Linternaute |language=fr |access-date=4 January 2011}}</ref>

She is also featured on the song "Cœur Volant" for the soundtrack of the 2011 film, ''[[Hugo (film)|Hugo]]''. Her live CD and DVD ''Zaz live tour Sans Tsu Tsou'' was revealed.

Her song "[[Eblouie Par La Nuit]]" was featured in the 2013 American neo-noir crime thriller, "Dead Man Down".

In 2012, Zaz went on tour and held concerts in various countries around the world including [[Japan]], Canada, [[Germany]], [[Poland]], Switzerland, [[Slovenia]], [[Czech Republic]] ([[Colours of Ostrava]]), [[Croatia]], [[Bulgaria]], [[Serbia]], and [[Turkey]], among other countries.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/zazofficiel?sk=app_123966167614127|title=ZAZ – Bandsintown – Facebook}}</ref>
[[File:ZAZ Brest Arena 11-2016 13.jpg|thumb|Zaz (''Paris'' Tour) in [[Brest, France|Brest]] in 2016.]]
With her album ''Paris'' she won the 2015 [[Echo (music award)|Echo award]] for best international female rock/pop artist.<ref>{{cite web|title='Die Helene-Fischer-Festspiele haben begonnen' |trans-title=The Helene Fischer festival has begun |language=de |publisher=[[Rundfunk Berlin-Brandenburg]] |work=RBB online |first=Ula |last=Brunner |date=27 March 2015 |access-date=27 March 2015 |url=http://www.rbb-online.de/kultur/beitrag/2015/03/musikpreis-echo-2015.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20150330121504/http://www.rbb-online.de/kultur/beitrag/2015/03/musikpreis-echo-2015.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=30 March 2015 }}</ref>

In 2016, Zaz was invited to perform in [[Zacatecas City]], Mexico as part of the Festival Cultural Zacatecas.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.mexicoescultura.com/actividad/146485/zaz.html|title=Zaz|website=Mexico Es Cultura |access-date=2 January 2018}}</ref>

In September 2018, Zaz released "Qué vendrá", the first track from an upcoming album titled ''Effet miroir'' ("Mirror Effect"). The track's title and chorus are in Spanish.<ref name="Qué vendrá on YouTube">{{Cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yLelhd6F2w8 |title=Qué vendrá |date=7 September 2018}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
In 2011, she joined the [[Les Enfoirés]] charity ensemble. 
	
Zaz created the project Zazimut to "develop and promote projects for a society more respectful of life in all its forms".
