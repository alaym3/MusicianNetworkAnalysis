'''Hyperium Records''' was a [[Germany|German]] [[independent record label]] specializing in [[darkwave]], [[Neoclassical (Dark Wave)|neoclassical]], [[ethereal wave|ethereal]], [[gothic rock]], and [[dark ambient|ambient]] music, founded by Oliver Roesch<ref>[http://www.musicaldiscoveries.com/reviews/chandeen2004.htm Reviews]</ref> (also known as "Oli" Roesch) and Oliver van Essenberg in [[1991 in music|1991]].<ref>[http://www.lictd.com/pgs/history.html History] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070317054643/http://www.lictd.com/pgs/history.html |date=2007-03-17 }}</ref> Roesch died on August 1, 2002 in a motorcycle accident.<ref>[http://www.hertz-lion.com/archive/17august2002.html Hertz-Lion] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928001248/http://www.hertz-lion.com/archive/17august2002.html |date=2007-09-28 }} (August 17, 2002)</ref>

==Releases==

===''Heavenly Voices'' compilations===
Hyperium are best known for their ''Heavenly Voices'' compilation series, which featured prominent use of [[female vocals]] in a [[Neoclassical Darkwave|neo-classical]], [[neofolk]], [[world music]], [[trip hop]] or [[Ethereal wave|ethereal]] style. "Heavenly Voices" has often been used as a genre term, particularly during the mid to late 1990s.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.projekt.com/projekt/product.asp?dept_id=20&sku=HYY00086 |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2007-03-05 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061118060942/https://www.projekt.com/projekt/product.asp?dept_id=20&sku=HYY00086 |archivedate=2006-11-18 }}</ref> [[Love Is Colder Than Death (band)|Love Is Colder Than Death]] claim that the series was inspired by their music.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lictd.com/pgs/history.html |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2007-03-05 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070317054643/http://www.lictd.com/pgs/history.html |archivedate=2007-03-17 }}</ref>

The first few compilations featured music by many of the label's own artists, as well as guest appearance by other notable bands of the ethereal/gothic/darkwave scene including [[Bel Canto (band)|Bel Canto]], [[Faith & the Muse]], [[Gitane Demone]], [[In The Nursery]], [[Miranda Sex Garden]], [[Ordo Equitum Solis]] and The Moon Seven Times. In 1997, American independent label [[Cleopatra Records]] released a Hyperium licensed compilation called ''Heavenly Voices'' subtitled, "A collection of the finest female vocals in ethereal, darkwave & gothic".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.projekt.com/projekt/product.asp?sku=CLE01982&dept_id=20 |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2007-03-05 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061118030313/http://www.projekt.com/projekt/product.asp?sku=CLE01982&dept_id=20 |archivedate=2006-11-18 }}</ref>
