'''Imee Ooi''' [[FRSA]] ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|iː|m|iː|_|ˈ|w|uː|i}} {{respell|EE|mee|_|WOO|ee}}; {{zh|c=黃慧音|p=Huáng Huìyīn}}) is a Chinese-Malaysian record producer, composer, and singer who composes and [[Arrangement|arranges]] music for classic [[Buddhist chant]], [[mantra]], and [[dharani]]. She performs her compositions in [[Sanskrit]], [[Pali]], [[Standard Tibetan|Tibetan]], and [[Mandarin Chinese|Mandarin]]. In 1997 she founded a [[record label]], I.M.M. Musicworks, to publish her music. To date, she has released more than 50 albums (55 between 1998 and 2020<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.immmusic.com/imee-ooi |title='Imee Ooi’s Musical Works & Dharma Concert Production', 2020 |language=English|website=www.immmusic.com |date= |accessdate=5 March 2021}}</ref>).

Ooi also composed and [[Musical director|directed]] three highly acclaimed [[Musical theatre|stage musicals]]: ''Siddhartha'', ''Above Full Moon'', and ''Princess Wen Cheng'' (aka ''Jewel of Tibet'').

==Biography==
Ooi was born in [[Taiping, Perak|Taiping]], [[Malaysia]] to a musically-inclined [[Hoklo people|Hoklo]] family. Her grandfather, who was a writer and dramatist, emigrated to Malaysia from [[Guangdong]], China. Ooi's mother was a piano teacher; Imee and her four siblings all learned piano. Ooi continued her study of piano and trained as a [[classical pianist]].
