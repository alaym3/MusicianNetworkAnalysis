'''Gracie Madigan Abrams''' (born September 7, 1999) is an American singer-songwriter. Her father is American film director [[J. J. Abrams]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Gracie Abrams |url=https://www.interscope.com/artists/gracie-abrams |website=Interscope Records |access-date=14 April 2021 |language=en |date=2 July 2020}}</ref> Her debut EP ''Minor'' was released on July 14, 2020, by [[Interscope Records]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=genius|url=https://genius.com/albums/Gracie-abrams/Minor|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Born and raised in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]], Abrams is the daughter of [[J. J. Abrams]], a film director, and Katie McGrath, a film and television producer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thecut.com/2018/04/gracie-abrams-is-your-new-favorite-instagram-songwriter.html|title=Lorde-Approved Gracie Abrams Is Instagram's Favorite Songwriter|website=The Cut|last=Sundberg|first=Emily|date=April 6, 2018|access-date=March 31, 2021}}</ref> She also has two brothers, Henry and August.<ref>{{Cite web|title=IMDb|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0009190/bio|url-status=live}}</ref> She became interested in music at a young age. She attended [[The Archer School for Girls]].<ref name=":8" /> She is studying international relations at [[Barnard College]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Gracie Abrams {{!}} Biography & History |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/gracie-abrams-mn0003885012/biography |website=AllMusic |access-date=28 March 2021 |language=en}}</ref> Her father's family is [[Jewish]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://jewishjournal.com/uncategorized/70238/|title=J.J. Abrams talks about 'Star Trek,' being Jewish and interfaith marriage|website=Jewish Journal|last=Greenberg|first=Brad|date=May 7, 2009|access-date=June 11, 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
In October 2019, Abrams released her debut single "Mean It" under [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/gracie-abrams-mean-it-is-a-heartbreaking-debut|title=Gracie Abrams' "Mean It" Is a Heartbreaking Debut|website=Onestowatch.com|last=Kato|first=Precious|date=October 24, 2019|access-date=March 31, 2021}}</ref>

On July 14, 2020, Abrams released her debut EP ''Minor''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/gracie-abrams/16757|title=Gracie Abrams to release 'Minor' on July 14.|website=Coupdemainmagazine.com|last=Graves|first=Shahlin|date=July 1, 2020|access-date=March 31, 2021}}</ref> The EP was supported by several singles, including "I miss you, I'm sorry" and "Friend". Abrams promoted the release with performances on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' and ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]]''.

On March 24, 2021, Abrams released a new single with [[Benny Blanco]] titled, "Unlearn". The single is part of Blanco's album ''[[Friends Keep Secrets 2]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/gracie-abrams/16804|title=Gracie Abrams and Benny Blanco Unite For New Single 'Unlearn'|website=Udiscovermusic.com|last=Schube|first=Will|date=March 24, 2021|access-date=March 31, 2021}}</ref>

On May 7, 2021, Abrams released the single 'Mess It Up' along with its [[music video]].<ref>{{cite web | last=Peters | first=Jo | title=Songwriter Gracie Abrams has shared her poignant new single ‘Mess It Up’  • WithGuitars | website=WithGuitars | date=May 12, 2021 | url=https://www.withguitars.com/286914-2/ | access-date=November 13, 2021}}</ref>
Later, Abrams released her single ‘Feels Like’ which followed with the music video. A few weeks after, she announced her new song ‘Rockland’ which was created with [[Aaron Dessner]].

On November 1, 2021, Abrams announced her new project  ''[[This Is What It Feels Like (Gracie Abrams album)|This Is What It Feels Like]].'' She then surprised-released her song 'Alright,' on November 10, 2021, claiming she was releasing it 'just for fun' on her [[Instagram]]. The project was released on November 12, 2021. The project includes the preceding singles 'Feels Like,' 'Alright' and 'Rockland.'

== Artistry and reception ==
Abrams has cited [[Joni Mitchell]], [[Simon & Garfunkel]], [[Elvis Costello]], [[Bon Iver]], [[Elliott Smith]], [[Kate Bush]], [[The 1975]], [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]], [[ Taylor Swift]], [[Lorde]], and [[Phoebe Bridgers]] as her musical influences.<ref name="losangelestimes">{{cite web|url=https://www.interscope.com/artists/gracie-abrams|title=Gracie Abrams|website=Interscope.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2021/02/kyle-meredith-with-gracie-abrams/|title=Gracie Abrams on Being Inspired by Lorde's Pure Heroine|website=Consequence of Sound|date=February 4, 2021|access-date=March 31, 2021}}</ref><ref name=":8">{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2020-07-29/gracie-abrams-jj-abrams-phobe-bridgers-minor|title=Her famous dad's a fan. So is Phoebe Bridgers. But Gracie Abrams has a voice all her own|website=Los Angeles Times|last=Wood|first=Mikael|date=July 29, 2020|access-date=March 31, 2021}}</ref>

Bridgers, Lorde, [[Post Malone]], [[Billie Eilish]] and [[Olivia Rodrigo]] have each expressed their admiration for Abrams,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gq.com/story/live-from-gracie-abrams-bedroom|website=GQ|title=Live From Gracie Abrams's Bedroom|last=Bennett|first=Willa|date=October 13, 2020|access-date=March 31, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lofficielusa.com/pop-culture/gracie-abrams-music-interview-billie-eilish-lorde-jj-abrams|website=L'Officiel|title=Musician Gracie Abrams on Why Music Should be Opinionated|last=Lecoq|first=Noémie|date=September 12, 2020|access-date=March 31, 2021}}</ref> with Rodrigo crediting ''Minor'' for inspiring her hit single "[[Drivers License (song)|Drivers License]]".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Darmon|first=Aynslee|date=2021-01-14|title=Olivia Rodrigo On The Massive Success Of 'Drivers License': 'I Can't Believe Any Of It'|url=https://etcanada.com/news/735039/olivia-rodrigo-on-the-massive-success-of-drivers-license-i-cant-believe-any-of-it/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=ET Canada|archive-date=January 20, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210120003459/https://etcanada.com/news/735039/olivia-rodrigo-on-the-massive-success-of-drivers-license-i-cant-believe-any-of-it/|url-status=live}}</ref>
