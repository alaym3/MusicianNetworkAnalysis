'''Rodriquez Jacquees Broadnax'''<ref name="Billboard">{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0003306954|label=Jacquees}}</ref> (born April 15, 1994), better known as '''Jacquees''', is an American singer and songwriter from [[Decatur, Georgia]]. In 2015, he signed a recording and management contract with [[Cash Money Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lilwaynehq.com/cash-money-records/|title=Cash Money Records - Information On The Label & Artists|publisher=LilWayneHQ|access-date=October 19, 2015}}</ref> His 2016 single, "B.E.D.", was on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart for seven weeks, peaking at number 69.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/song/jacquees/7958094/bed|title=B.E.D. {{!}} Billboard|publisher=Billboard|access-date=May 30, 2017}}</ref> His debut album, ''[[4275]]'', was released on June 15, 2018.

==Career==
In 2007, Jacquees met his first manager, Corey Battle. He performed in a variety of talent shows before he met and switched management to Russell “Block" Spencer, who was CEO and founder of [[Block Entertainment]]. Jacquees worked in various recording studios as part of a production team. Block's focus was only rap music, not R&B. Jacquees later began working closely at Block's recording studio, gaining exposure with the track, "Krazy", featuring Atlanta rapper [[T.I.]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thisisrnb.com/2010/02/musicemerging-artist-jacquees-feat-ti-u-must-be-crazy/|title=Music/Emerging Artist: Jacquees feat. T.I. – U Must Be Crazy|work=ThisisRnB|date=February 25, 2010|access-date=October 9, 2016}}</ref>

On March 25, 2014, Jacquees independently released his second [[Extended play|EP]], ''19'', which debuted at number 15 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top R&B Albums]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/01/the-come-up-presents-jacquees/|title=The Come Up: Jacquees|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=C.M.|first=Emmanuel|date=January 29, 2016|access-date=October 9, 2016}}</ref> The 11-track EP features [[guest appearance]]s from [[Chris Brown]], [[Rich Homie Quan]], [[Lloyd (singer)|Lloyd]],and [[Trinidad James]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/19/id847966653|title=19 by Jacquees |publisher=[[iTunes Store]] (US)|access-date=October 9, 2016}}</ref> On October 1, 2014, Jacquees announced that he had signed a recording deal with [[Cash Money Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fashionably-early.com/2014/10/01/jacquees-signs-to-cash-money-recordsrich-gang/|title=Jacquees Signs to Cash Money Records/Rich Gang|work=Fashionably-Early|author=Malbin|date=October 1, 2014|access-date=October 9, 2016}}</ref> In May 2016, he collaborated with label-mate [[Birdman (rapper)|Birdman]] on an EP titled ''Lost at Sea''.<ref name="LAS">{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/birdman-and-jacquees-lost-at-sea-new-mixtape.116418.html|title=Lost At Sea|publisher=HotNewHipHop|access-date=January 25, 2017}}</ref> He found mainstream success with his 2016 single "B.E.D.", charting at number 69 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name=":0" />

In November 2019, he released his second studio album ''King of R&B''.<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Shaffer |first1=Claire |title=The First Time: Jacquees |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/jacquees-king-rb-first-time-video-916199/ |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=November 22, 2019|access-date=February 11, 2021}}</ref> The album debuted at number 20 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=billboardcharts|number=1196458818089172992|title=Debuts on this week's #Billboard200 (1/2)|date=November 18, 2019|access-date=November 19, 2019}}</ref> ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' stated that Jacquees appeared "well-rounded" on the album, and his vocals paired well with his collaborators.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pearce |first1=Sheldon |title=Jacuqees: King of R&B Album Review |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/jacquees-king-of-randb/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |date=November 15, 2019|access-date=February 11, 2021}}</ref> Jacquees embarked on the King of R&B Tour in early 2020 in support of the album.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Brissett |first1=Danielle |title=Jacquees Announces 'King of R&B' Tour |url=https://ratedrnb.com/2020/01/jacquees-announces-king-of-rb-tour/ |website=Rated R&B |date=January 16, 2020|access-date=February 11, 2021}}</ref>
