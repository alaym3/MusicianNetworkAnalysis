'''Turnpike Troubadours''' is an American country music group from [[Oklahoma]] founded in 2005.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0002038565|pure_url=yes}}|title=Turnpike Troubadours Biography|last=Leggett|first=Steve|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=July 12, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.turnpiketroubadours.com/pg/about|title=About – TURNPIKE TROUBADOURS – ''Goodbye Normal Street''|publisher=Turnpike Troubadours|work=Turnpike Troubadours|accessdate=25 October 2012 }}</ref> They started their own imprint, Bossier City Records, in 2007 and have released five studio albums. Their self-titled 2015 album peaked at number 17 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

==Career==
The band was formed in 2007, with original members including [[Evan Felker]] and R.C. Edwards. ''Bossier City'' was recorded a month after the formation of the band, in order to have a recording to sell at live shows.<ref name=wofford>{{cite news|last1=Writer|first1=JERRY WOFFORD World Scene|title=Evan Felker of Turnpike Troubadours talks on the band's success ahead of its Cain's show|url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/blogs/scene/offbeat/evan-felker-of-turnpike-troubadours-talks-on-the-band-s/article_7e39eb72-2257-5186-8c9e-1fa7fc3dbe3b.html|accessdate=3 May 2018|work=Tulsa World|language=en}}</ref> Felker later said the musical arrangements were "not a good representation" of what the band later became.<ref name=wofford/> The songs "Easton and Main" and "Bossier City" were re-recorded for their 2015 self-titled album.<ref name=wofford/> Turnpike Troubadours’ most recent album, ''[[A Long Way from Your Heart]]'', was released in October 2017 and peaked at #3 on the [[Top Country Albums|Billboard Top Country Albums]] chart.

As of May 31, 2019, the band is on indefinite hiatus. 

On November 24, 2021, the band's Instagram was cleaned of all previous posts and their website, www.turnpiketroubadours.com, read simply "Coming Soon" with what appears to be a black band logo on an orange background.<ref>https://turnpiketroubadours.com/</ref>

In November 2021, the band announced its reunion and return to touring, with a show at [[Red Rocks Amphitheatre]] announced for May 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Crutchmer|first=Josh|last2=Crutchmer|first2=Josh|date=2021-11-30|title=Exclusive: Turnpike Troubadours Talk Unexpected Hiatus, Newfound Sobriety, and Surprise Reunion|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/turnpike-troubadours-reunion-evan-felker-interview-1264002/|access-date=2021-12-01|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref>
