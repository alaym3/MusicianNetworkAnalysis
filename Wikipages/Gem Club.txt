'''Gem Club''' is an American [[chamber pop]] band formed in [[Somerville, Massachusetts]] by singer-songwriter Christopher Barnes.

==History==

Gem Club was formed by Christopher Barnes, with cellist Kristen Drymala, at the end of 2009.
<ref>{{Cite web
|url= http://blogs.bostonmagazine.com/boston_daily/2011/07/28/a-gem-club/
|title= Q & A: Gem Club’s Christopher Barnes
|publisher= [[Boston Magazine]]
|date = 2012-07-29
|accessdate=2012-10-24}}
</ref>
Their first EP, ''Acid and Everything'', was recorded in Barnes' [[Somerville, MA]] home and self-released on June 2010.<ref>Barnes, C. (2010). [Liner notes]. Acid and Everything [CD]. Somerville, MA: Mount Desert Isle Music.</ref> The EP was written as part of the [[RPM Challenge]], a challenge for musicians to write and record an album during the month of February.<ref>{{Cite web
|url= http://www.cambridgeday.com/2010/05/20/gem-clubs-music-a-prism-making-peace-out-pain/
|title= Gem Club’s music a prism making peace out pain
|publisher= Cambridge Day
|date = May 20, 2010
|accessdate=October 24, 2012}}
</ref>

Gem Club was soon after signed to [[Hardly Art]], a [[Sub Pop]] sub-label, and began working with vocalist Ieva Berberian.<ref>{{Cite web
|url= http://www.cambridgeday.com/2011/06/28/gem-club-signs-with-label-preps-full-length-album/
|title= Gem Club signs with label, preps full-length album
|publisher= Cambridge Day
|date = June 28, 2011
|accessdate=October 24, 2012}}
</ref>
The group's debut full length, ''Breakers'', was released September 27, 2011<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/breakers-mw0002202474 |title=Breakers - Gem Club |work=AllMusic}}</ref> and was met with critical success. The album was well reviewed by music sites and magazines [[NME]], [[AllMusic]], [[All Songs Considered|NPR]], [[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]], Dusted, among others.

Their sophomore album, ''In Roses'', was recorded entirely in analog to tape at [[John Vanderslice]]'s Tiny Telephone studio in San Francisco, CA, with orchestral work from Magik*Magik Orchestra featured on several songs.<ref>{{Cite web
|url= http://www.hardlyart.com/gemclub.html
|title= Hardly Art - Gem Club
|publisher= [[Hardly Art]]
|date= July 16, 2014
|accessdate= July 16, 2014}}
</ref> After a pre-release stream on [[NPR]]'s First Listen, the album was released January 28, 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/in-roses-mw0002586720 |title=In Roses - Gem Club |work=AllMusic}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Boilen |first1=Bob |title=First Listen: Gem Club, 'In Roses' |url=https://www.npr.org/2014/01/12/261379769/first-listen-gem-club-in-roses |website=NPR |accessdate=8 July 2020 |language=en}}</ref> A music video for the album's lead single ''Polly'' was directed by Matthew Salton with photographer Charlie Engman and featuring Engman's mother Kathleen McCain Engman.<ref>{{Cite web
|url= http://www.thefader.com/2014/02/13/watch-gem-clubs-jarring-polly-video/
|title= Watch Gem Club’s Jarring "Polly" Video
|publisher= [[The Fader]]
|date= February 13, 2014
|accessdate= July 16, 2014}}
</ref>

A limited run of postcards, each with a photograph taken by Boston-based [http://larsonmcgrath.tumblr.com/ Larson McGrath] that was inspired by a song from ''In Roses'' album, were sold with the album. At a private performance in the South End in Boston, McGrath's photographs were displayed in the studio where the show was held.<ref name="An Intimate Evening With Gem Club">{{cite web|last1=Bedian|first1=Knar|title=An Intimate Evening With Gem Club|url=http://soundofboston.com/intimate-evening-gem-club/|website=Sound of Boston}}</ref> During this show, Barnes explained that "Marathon" is a song about the abusive relationship of an acquaintance of Barnes.<ref name="An Intimate Evening With Gem Club"/>

Their songs have been featured on the American television shows [[Parenthood (2010 TV series)|Parenthood]], [[Arrow (TV series)|Arrow]], [[Private Practice (TV series)|Private Practice]] and [[Revenge (TV series)|Revenge]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://abc.go.com/music-lounge/artist/gem-club/914097 |title=ABC Music Lounge |publisher=ABC |accessdate=October 24, 2012 }}</ref> as well as [[Skins (TV series)|Skins]] in the UK.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.skinsmusic.co.uk/php/Season_6/skins_Season6_episode9.php |title=Skins Music |publisher=E4 |accessdate=October 24, 2012 }}</ref>
