'''José Martín Cuevas Cobos''' (born September 28, 1969), known by his stage name '''Pedro Fernández''',  is a [[Mexicans|Mexican]] singer, songwriter, actor, and television host. Fernández began his international career as ''Pedrito'' Fernández at the age of seven.

Fernández has recorded thirty-nine albums, acted in seven soap operas, and twenty five films. He has won 4 Latin Grammy Awards.<ref>{{cite web|title=Past Winners Search|url=https://www.latingrammy.com/en/nominees/search?artist=%22Pedro+Fernández%22&field_nominee_work_value=&year=All&genre=All|website=Latin Grammy|publisher=[[Latin Recording Academy]]|access-date=7 December 2016}}</ref> His stage name is composed of the names of two of his favorite singers, (Pedro) Infante and Vicente (Fernandez).

==Early life==
His first album, ''[[La de la Mochila Azul]]'', named after the single, was released in 1978.  His starring role in the film ''[[La niña de la mochila azul]]'' (''The Girl with the Blue Backpack'') was released in 1979 and led to a lifetime career of recording and film contracts.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pedro Fernández es el tercer coach de 'La Voz Kids'|url=http://www.telemundo.com/shows/2015/01/07/pedro-fernandez-es-el-tercer-coach-de-la-voz-kids|date=January 7, 2015|language=es|website=Telemundo.com|publisher=Talpa Media|access-date= December 8, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Pedro Fernandez walmart.jpg|thumb|250px|right|Pedro Fernandez.]]

In 2001, his album [[Yo No Fui]] won the [[Latin Grammy Award]] for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Ranchero Album|Best Ranchero Album]].

His album ''De Corazón'' was nominated for the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Ranchero/Mariachi Album]] at the [[2003 Latin Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Fernández, Pedro|url=http://www.encyclopedia.com/education/news-wires-white-papers-and-books/fernandez-pedro|website=Encyclopedia .com|publisher=Cengage Learning|access-date=6 March 2017}}</ref>

In 2009, Fernández starred in [[Televisa]]'s telenovela ''[[Hasta que el dinero nos separe]]'' with actress and singer [[Itatí Cantoral]]. He won the [[TVyNovelas Award for Best Lead Actor]] of 2010 for his portrayal of "Rafael Medina Nuñez" in the telenovela. The couple took home a triumph as Best Couple of the Year and Best Telenovela.

In 2010, Fernández won the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Regional Mexican Song]] (2010) for "[[Amarte a la Antigua]]",<ref name="auto">{{cite news|author=<!--Staff writer(s); No by-line-->|url=http://www.dailybreeze.com/article/ZZ/20101112/NEWS/101118450|title=Mexican pop-rock group Camila, singer Guerra big winners at Latin Grammys|date=November 12, 2010|newspaper=[[Daily Breeze]]|agency=[[Associated Press]]|quote=Pedro Fernandez wore an elegant white charro suit to sing 'Celosa' and 'Amarte a la antigua,' which won best regional Mexican song.|access-date=December 22, 2016}}</ref>

In 2012, Fernández starred as the protagonist in the telenovela [[Cachito de cielo]]. Fernández wrote the theme song "Cachito de Cielo" for the telenovela and as the theme song's songwriter and performer, he won the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Regional Mexican Song]] in 2013.

In July 2014, Fernández starred in the Mexican telenovela [[Hasta el fin del mundo]] produced by [[Televisa]]. Fernández also wrote and performed the theme song for the series and produced an album by the name [[Hasta el fin del mundo (album)|Hasta El Fin Del Mundo]].

In 2015, his album [[Acaríciame El Corazón]] won the Latin Grammy Award for [[Latin Grammy Award|Best Ranchero/Mariachi Album]] at the [[16th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/latin-awards/6770030/latin-grammys-2015-winners-list|title=Latin Grammys 2015: See the Full Winners List|website=Billboard}}</ref> Fernández appeared on TV as a team coach for ''[[La Voz Kids]]'' for seasons three (2015) and four (2016),<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Fernandez|first=Suzette|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/6851836/la-voz-kids-daddy-yankee-natalia-jimenez-pedro-fernandez|title=Daddy Yankee, Natalia Jimenez & Pedro Fernandez Return as Coaches on 'La Voz Kids'|date=January 25, 2016|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=December 9, 2016}}</ref> and as an official presenter/host for the [[Billboard Latin Music Awards|''Billboard'' Latin Music Awards]] for 2015 and 2016.<ref>{{cite news|last=Tinoco|first=Armando|url=http://www.latintimes.com/premios-billboard-2016-9-things-we-know-about-telemundo-latin-music-award-show-380834|title=Premios Billboard 2016: 9 Things We Know About Telemundo Latin Music Award Show|date= April 21, 2016|newspaper=[[Latin Times]]|access-date=December 9, 2016}}</ref>

In September 2016, Fernández collaborated with [[Grupo Cañaveral]] for their 20-year anniversary album, recording a [[Cumbia]] version of Fernández's song "[[Si Te Vas (Pedro Fernández song)|Si Te Vas]]".<ref>{{cite web|last=Huitrón|first=Alejandro|url=http://www.actualmx.com/grupo-canaveral-nuevo-material/|title=Grupo Cañaveral, prepara su nuevo material|date=8 September 2016|language=es|publisher=ActualMX|access-date=December 9, 2016}}</ref>

The Honduran paper ''[[La Prensa (Honduras)|La Prensa]]'' has called Fernández "one of the most recognized artists not only in Mexico but throughout Latin America for his music and acting which continues today".<ref>{{cite news|last=Ramos|first=Daniela Michelle|title='En la musica hay que dar lo mejor siempre': Pedro Fernández|trans-title=''In music you must always give your best.'': Fernández|date=September 4, 2016|language=es|newspaper=[[La Prensa (Honduras)|La Prensa]]}}</ref>

In November 2017, Fernández recorded “El Mudo” with [[Sonora Santanera|La Sonora Santanera]] on their [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Traditional Tropical Album|Latin Grammy]] nominated album “La Fiesta  Continúa”.<ref>{{cite web|title=La fiesta sigue para la Sonora Santanera|url=https://www.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/noticia/1412494.la-fiesta-sigue-para-la-sonora-santanera.html/|website=elsiglodetorreon.com.mx|<!-- access-date=August 2019| -->language=es}}</ref>

2018 was an eventful year for Fernández. This year he began celebrating 40 years of his artistic career with a year of concerts and television appearances. In February he was named “Mr. Amigo”.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mramigo.com/about.html|title=Mr Amigo Association|website=mramigo.com}}</ref> Each year, the Mr. Amigo Association honors a Mexican citizen to promote international friendship and goodwill between the U.S. and Mexico.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mr. Amigo Pedro Fernández wows Charreada audience with warmth, charm|url=https://www.utrgv.edu/en-us/about-utrgv/news/press-releases/2018/february-22-mr-amigo-pedro-fernandez-wows-charreada-audience-with-warmth-charm/index.htm/|website=www.utrgv.edu/<!-- |access-date=August 2019 -->}}</ref>

In March, Fernández released the first single titled from his upcoming new album [[Arránquense Muchachos]]. March also saw the release of “Toquen Mariachis Canten” (En Vivo), a duet Fernández recorded with [[Leo Dan]] for his new album “Celebrando a una Leyenda” (En Vivo).<ref>{{cite web|title=Leo Dan lanza su nuevo album Celebrando una a Leyenda/|url=https://www.sonymusic.com.mx/leo-dan-lanza-su-nuevo-album-celebrando-una-leyenda/|website=www.sonymusic.com.mx<!-- |accessdate= August 2019| -->language=Spanish}}</ref>

In May, Fernández recorded another duet “Un Millón De Rosas” with the Grammy winning Tejano Group, [[La Mafia]] on their new album “Vozes”.

July brought the release of “Como Ella”, the second single from his new album Arránquense Muchachos. In August the complete album was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.saps.com.mx/noticias/ahi-viene-pedro-fernandez.html|title="¡ARRÁNQUENSE MUCHACHOS!" que ahí viene Pedro Fernández|date=17 August 2018|website=Saps.com.mx|access-date=24 October 2018}}</ref>

In September, the title cut from the album [[Arránquense Muchachos|¡Arránquense Muchachos¡]] was nominated by the [[19th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latingrammy.com/en/nominees|title=19a Entrega Anual del Latin GRAMMY|website=Latingrammy.com|access-date=24 October 2018}} 
</ref> for Best Regional Song.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.saps.com.mx/noticias/latin-grammy-2018-al-regional-mexicano.html|title=Latin Grammy 2018 con nominados al Regional Mexicano|date=20 September 2018|website=Saps.com.mx|access-date=24 October 2018}}</ref>

In 2019, along with the continuation of his multi-country “40th Anniversary Tour”, Fernández was selected to perform the opening theme “Como Te Extraño Mi Amor” for the Telemundo new political drama [[Preso No. 1]] an American political thriller television series produced by [[Keshet International]] and [[Telemundo Global Studios]]  which premiered on 30 July 2019.<ref name="cast">{{cite web |title=TELEMUNDO INTRODUCES STAR-STUDDED CAST FOR UPCOMING POLITICAL-THRILLER "PRESO NO. 1" |url=https://www.nbcumv.com/news/telemundo-introduces-star-studded-cast-upcoming-political-thriller-%E2%80%9Cpreso-no-1%E2%80%9D?network=33144 |website=nbcumv.com |access-date=16 May 2019}}</ref>  On August 30, 2019 Spanish singer [[Natalia Jiménez]] released her album “México De Mi Corazón” (Mexico of my heart), that includes a duet with Fernández, “Ya Lo Sé Que Tú Te Vas” (I know that you are leaving).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8528773/mexico-de-mi-corazon-natalia-jimenez-interview|title=Why Natalia Jiménez Was Afraid to Release a Tribute Album in Honor Of Mexico|website=Billboard.com|access-date=31 August 2019}}</ref>

In March 2020, Fernández released the cover for his upcoming 40th album with a single titled “Como Te Extraño Mi Amor” (How I miss you my love), written by [[Leo Dan]] and encouraged by the popularity of his shorter version performed by Fernández for the theme of the Telemundo political drama [[Preso No. 1]] last year.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.saps.com.mx/noticias/pedro-fernandez-presenta-su-version-de-como-te-extrano-mi-amor.html|title= Pedro Fernández presenta su versión de "Como Te Extraño Mi Amor"|website=Saps.com|access-date=16 April 2020}}</ref>

In 2021 Fernández takes the lead role as the title character in [[Malverde: El Santo Patrón]], an American biographical-drama television series based on the life of the Mexican bandit Jesús Malverde. Produced by Telemundo Global Studios.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://peopleenespanol.com/telenovelas/malverde-el-santo-patron-conoce-al-elenco-de-la-serie-de-telemundo/ |title=Conoce al elenco de Malverde: el santo patrón, la primera superserie de época de Telemundo|publisher=[[People en Español]] |accessdate=18 February 2021 |language=Spanish |date=18 February 2021}}</ref>

==Awards==
Among his many national and international awards over his long career Pedro Fernández has been awarded the [[TVyNovelas Award for Best Actor]] (2010) for ''[[Hasta Que el Dinero Nos Separe]]'', [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Ranchero/Mariachi Album]] (2001) for ''[[Yo No Fui]]'',<ref>{{cite news|author=<!--Staff writer(s); No by-line-->|url=http://laprensa-sandiego.org/archieve/november02/4grammy.htm|title=Latin Recording Academy Announces 2nd Annual Latin Grammy Award Winners|date=October 30, 2001|newspaper=La Prensa San Diego|access-date=December 22, 2016}}</ref> [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Regional Mexican Song]] (2010) for "[[Amarte a la Antigua]]",<ref name="auto"/> [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Regional Mexican Song]] (2013) (writer and performer) for "[[Cachito de Cielo (song)|Cachito de Cielo]]",<ref>{{cite web|title=Latin Grammys 2013: The complete list of winners and nominees|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-latin-grammys-2013-nominees-winners-list-story.html|website=latimes.com|access-date=23 December 2016}}</ref> and the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Ranchero/Mariachi Album]] (2015) for ''[[Acaríciame El Corazón]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Latin Grammys 2015: See the Full Winners List|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/latin-awards/6770030/latin-grammys-2015-winners-list|website=Billboard.com|access-date=23 December 2016}}</ref>
