'''Christian Friedrich Johannes Büttner''' (born 1 June 1979), known professionally as '''TheFatRat''', is a German DJ, record producer and musician. His genre is often described as "[[glitch-hop]]".<ref name="glitch hop">{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7431360/thefatrat-releasing-music-without-record-label | title=Go Your Own Way: How German Producer TheFatRat Finds Creative Freedom in Label Independence | work=Billboard.com | date=12 July 2016 | access-date=17 December 2016 | author=Bein, Kat}}</ref> He is best known for his 2016 extended play ''Jackpot'' which reached 23 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Dance/Electronic Albums]] chart.<ref name="chart">{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/music/thefatrat/chart-history|title=TheFatRat Chart History {{pipe}} Billboard|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|language=en|access-date=8 August 2017|archive-date=6 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406183222/https://www.billboard.com/music/thefatrat/chart-history|url-status=dead}}</ref> He peaked at 15 on Billboard's Next Big Sound chart in February 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/next-big-sound-25/2015-02-21 |title=Next Big Sound |website=Billboard |access-date=4 June 2019}}</ref>

==Music career==
Büttner began producing music in 2001 under his real name. He began by making background music for television, radio and advertisements and produced music for DJs.<ref name="pmlabelgroup">{{Cite web|url=https://www.pmlabelgroup.com/thefatrat/|title=TheFatRat {{!}} Artist info|website=PM Label Group|language=en-US|access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref>

In 2010, Büttner produced the song [[Mignon Mignon]] for [[René la Taupe]], which became a number-one single in France.<ref>{{cite web |title=René la Taupe - "Mignon Mignon", French Singles Chart |url=http://lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Ren%E9+La+Taupe&titel=Mignon+mignon&cat=s |publisher=Lescharts |language=fr |access-date=28 December 2010}}</ref> Around that time, he also produced the song "Audubon Ballroom" on [[Lupe Fiasco]]'s album ''[[Food & Liquor II: The Great American Rap Album Pt. 1]]'', which charted at #5 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/474861/green-day-no-doubt-lupe-fiasco-headed-for-big-billboard-200-debuts|title=Green Day, No Doubt & Lupe Fiasco Headed for Big Billboard 200 Debuts|date=16 March 2011|first=Keith|last=Caulfield|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|location=Los Angeles|access-date=4 October 2012}}</ref>

In July 2011, he began making solo music under the pseudonym TheFatRat, a nickname given to him in his youth. His career began with the release of his first [[Extended play|EP]], ''Do Be Do Be Do''.<ref name="pmlabelgroup"/>

By 2016, Büttner's music was used in over 1.5 million YouTube videos.<ref name="Jackpot">{{cite web |url = http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/TheFatRat-Releases-Jackpot-EP-20161122 | title = TheFatRat Releases 'Jackpot' EP | work=BroadwayWorld.com | date=22 November 2016 | access-date=17 December 2016}}</ref> His single "Unity"  was included on the album ''Sounds of Syndication Vol. 1''<ref>{{cite web |url = http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/twitch-streamer-curated-album |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170211081932/http://thecreatorsproject.vice.com/blog/twitch-streamer-curated-album |url-status = dead |archive-date = 11 February 2017 | title=Twitch Streamer Curates Album for Millions | work=[[Vice Media]] |date = 6 May 2016 | access-date=1 January 2019}}</ref> curated by [[Tom Cassell]], one of the most subscribed streamers on [[Twitch (service)|Twitch]].

In November 2016, Büttner released his second EP, ''Jackpot'' on the [[Universal Music]] label.<ref name="Jackpot" /> The EP reached #23 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Dance/Electronic Albums]] chart.<ref name="chart" /> In December 2016, Büttner started a record label, The Arcadium, in collaboration with [[Universal Music]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://m.goettinger-tageblatt.de/Die-Region/Goettingen/TheFatRat-auf-YouTube-von-Musikproduzent-Christian-Buettner-aus-Niedernjesa | title=Zwei Millionen YouTube-Abonnenten | work=Göttinger Tageblatt |date = 14 November 2018| access-date=31 December 2018}}</ref>

In June 2017, he released the single "Fly Away" featuring vocalist [[Anjulie]]. In July, he performed at the [[Electric Forest Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.electricforestfestival.com/artist/thefatrat/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=18 October 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171019060015/https://www.electricforestfestival.com/artist/thefatrat/ |archive-date=19 October 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In October 2017, he released the single "Oblivion" featuring vocalist [[Lola Blanc]].<ref>{{Citation|title=TheFatRat - Oblivion (feat. Lola Blanc) - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Gc3tqnhmf5U|language=en|access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref>

In March 2018, he released the single "MAYDAY".<ref>{{cite web | url=http://ventsmagazine.com/2018/03/26/thefatrat-finally-releases-his-mayday/ | title=TheFatRat Finally Releases His "MAYDAY" | work=Vents Magazine | date=26 March 2018 | access-date=31 December 2018}}</ref> In July 2018, he released "Warrior Songs", a music pack for the video game ''[[Dota 2]]'', which has been made available in the game as of 13 September 2018, according to Matthew "Cyborgmatt" Bailey, director of operations of [[Team Secret]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/Cyborgmatt/status/1040333987187748864|title=The @ThisIsTheFatRat music pack is now available to purchase on the @DOTA2 store.|last=Bailey|first=Matthew|date=13 September 2018|website=Twitter|language=en|access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref>

In December 2018, he posted a video about how YouTube's content ID system is "[[Copyfraud|broken]]", as one anonymous user known as Ramjets claimed all the earnings for his song "The Calling" and YouTube refused to settle the dispute.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.dailydot.com/upstream/thefatrat-youtube-copyright-claim-system/ | title=YouTube star TheFatRat is highly 'frustrated' with the platform's copyright claim system | work=[[The Daily Dot]] | date=20 December 2018 | access-date=1 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.digitalmusicnews.com/2018/12/27/christian-buettner-thefatrat-youtube-content-id-petition/ | title=A Fed Up Musician Demands That YouTube Fix Its Broken Content ID System. More Than 100,000 People Have Signed His Petition. | work=Digital Music News | date=27 December 2018 | access-date=1 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://boingboing.net/2018/12/26/youtube-let-a-contentid-scamme.html | title=YouTube let a contentID scammer steal a popular video | work=[[Boing Boing]] | date=26 December 2018 | access-date=1 January 2019}}</ref> The claim was removed by YouTube later.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://in.mashable.com/tech/9892/youtube-gives-content-creators-more-control-over-copyright-claim-disputes-with-new-update|title=YouTube Gives Content Creators More Control Over Copyright Claim Disputes With New Update|date=27 December 2019|website=Mashable India|language=en-in|access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref>

In February 2020, ''[[Redbull.com]]'' made a 10-minute documentary about Büttner and his career titled, ''TheFatRat: Everything You Ever Wanted To Know About Him''. They released this within their article, "TheFatRat: Meet the EDM DJ who's elevated video game music to high art."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.redbull.com/us-en/thefatrat-gaming-producer-portrait|title=TheFatRat: Meet the EDM DJ who's elevated video game music to high art|date=5 February 2020|work=[[Redbull.com]]|access-date=27 April 2020}}</ref>

On 7 February 2020, Büttner released a [[science fiction|sci-fi]] music video on YouTube starring himself and Maisy Kay for Büttner's song, "The Storm," which he performed with Kay.<ref>{{cite AV media|people=Büttner, Christian (TheFatRat) & Kay, Maisy|date=7 February 2020|title=The Storm (Official Music Video)|language=en|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L4bZRIwI-QA|access-date=27 April 2020|publisher=The Arcadium}}</ref> The music video was filmed in [[Iceland]], with [[Surtshellir|Surtshellir Cave]] on the first day, [[Hekla|Hekla Mountain]] on the second day, and [[Hjörleifshöfði]] on the third and final day. Accompanying the music video is a game for mobile dubbed ''The Storm - Interactive'', where one can play through the story of the video.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.redbull.com/de-de/thefatrat-the-storm-musik-video-hintergrund|title=Expedition Island: Das steckt hinter dem TheFatRat-Video zu "The Storm"|date=7 February 2020|work=[[Redbull.com]]|language=de-de|access-date=27 April 2020}}</ref>
