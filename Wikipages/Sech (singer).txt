'''Carlos Isaías Morales Williams''' (born December 3, 1993), better known as '''Sech''', is a Panamanian singer. He is from the [[Río Abajo]] township of [[Panama City]] and is best known for his single "[[Otro Trago]]", which was nominated for Best Urban Song and Best Urban Fusion/Performance at the [[20th Annual Latin Grammy Awards|2019 Latin Grammy Awards]]. He has collaborated with numerous Latin artists including [[Maluma (singer)|Maluma]], [[Nicky Jam]], [[Daddy Yankee]], and [[Bad Bunny]]. He released his debut studio album ''[[Sueños (Sech album)|Sueños]]'' in April 2019 and his second album ''1 of 1'' was released on May 21, 2020.

==Biography==
===1993–2014: Early life and career beginnings===
Carlos Isaías Morales Williams was born the youngest of four children in the [[Río Abajo]] township of Panama City to parents who were both pastors.<ref name=rsteddybear>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/sech-suenos-otro-trago-latin-grammys-2019-891137/|title= Meet Sech: First-Time Latin Grammy Nominee, Absolute Teddy Bear|work=Rolling Stone|last=Exposito|first=Suzy|date=November 13, 2019|accessdate=May 8, 2020}}</ref> He developed his singing voice while singing in his church's choir. He additionally enjoyed writing poetry and singing along to the music of [[Boyz II Men]].<ref name=rsteddybear/> After high school, he worked in the food service and construction industries while producing music on the side with his friend Focking Rafita.<ref name=rsteddybear/> He began his musical career in Panama as a street vendor who also sang to customers.<ref name=smooth>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8509845/latin-artist-on-the-rise-meet-sech|title=Latin Artist on the Rise: Meet Sech, Bringing Smooth Vocals to Urban Music|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Roiz|first=Jessica|date=2 May 2019|accessdate=18 June 2019}}</ref> Morales started his career in the group Los Principiantes. His first song with the group, titled "Te Llamaba", was released in June 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y4MCfTkXI-o|title=Los Principiantes - Te Llamaba|work=[[YouTube]]|accessdate=22 June 2020}}</ref>

===2014–2018: Solo career and national success ===
In 2014, Sech released his debut single "Yo Sin Ti". Between 2014 and 2017, Sech began releasing new songs until the release of "Miss Lonely", which became his first national hit and gave him his national recognition.<ref name="Duality">{{Cite web|url=https://www.pollstar.com/article/hotstar-the-duality-of-sech-latin-americas-rising-urbano-star-142653|title=Hotstar: The Duality Of Sech, Latin America's Rising Urbano Star|website=www.pollstar.com}}</ref> The song went viral in Colombia and attracted the attention of producer and DJ [[Dimelo Flow]].<ref name=smooth/> Following this, Sech was signed to [[Rich Music]] in 2017, and in December of that year, he released his first mixtape album, titled ''The Sensation Mixtape''.

Sech's debut [[extended play]] ''The Sensation'', was released on December 6, 2018. Consisting of 8 songs, the album includes the singles "Me Gustaría", "La Vida" and the original version of "Que Más Pues", which its remix would be included on his following release. The album also features guest appearances from International artists, including [[Justin Quiles]], [[Jowell & Randy]] and [[Dalex]].

===2019–present: Breakthrough international success, ''Sueños'' and ''1 of 1''===
In April 2019, Sech experienced worldwide success with "[[Otro Trago]]". The song reached number one in his native Panama, as well in Spain, Argentina, Colombia, Peru, Paraguay, Honduras and Mexico. It also became Sech's first entry on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] peaking at number 34.<ref name="Forbes">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/garysuarez/2019/06/09/with-two-concurrent-billboard-latin-hits-sech-could-be-urbanos-next-big-thing/#382ee00f68b4|title=With Two Concurrent Billboard Latin Hits, Sech Could Be Urbano's Next Big Thing|work=[[Forbes]]|last=Suarez|first=Gary|date=9 June 2019|accessdate=18 June 2019}}</ref> Sech describes "Otro Trago" as "the song that truly changed [his] life".<ref name=rsteddybear/> The track was nominated for Best Urban Song and Best Urban Fusion/Performance at the [[20th Annual Latin Grammy Awards|2019 Latin Grammy Awards]].<ref name=rsteddybear/> That same month, Sech released his debut studio album ''Sueños'', which reached number three on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Latin Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/latin-albums|title=Top Latin Albums|date=10 August 2019}}</ref> The album includes the Panamanian top 10 single "Solita" featuring [[Farruko]] and [[Zion & Lennox]]. It also includes the remix of the song "Qué Más Pues" featuring [[Maluma (singer)|Maluma]], [[Nicky Jam]], Lenny Tavarez, [[Justin Quiles]] and [[Dalex]].<ref name=smooth/> ''Sueños'' was ranked number three on the ''Rolling Stone'' list of the "25 Best Latin Albums of 2019".<ref name=25best>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin-lists/latin-albums-best-2019-924381/sech-suenos-929125/|title=The 25 Best Latin Albums of 2019|work=Rolling Stone|date=December 29, 2019|accessdate=May 8, 2020}}</ref> In August 2019, Suzy Exposito of ''Rolling Stone'' noted that Sech is "rapidly becoming urbano's most wanted collaborator".<ref name=needtoknow>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/sech-otro-trago-feat-darell-ozuna-nicky-jam-anuel-aa-868525/|title=Song You Need to Know: Sech, ‘Otro Trago (Remix)’|work=Rolling Stone|last=Exposito|first=Suzy|date=August 7, 2019|accessdate=May 9, 2020}}</ref>

He featured on Nicky Jam's 2019 ''[[Íntimo]]'' album on the song "Atrévete", released in November 2019. The song peaked at number four in Sech's native Panama.<ref name=intimobillboardreview>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8542056/viva-friday-playlist-nicky-jam-intimo|title= Viva Friday Playlist: Nicky Jam's 'Intimo' Album & More|work=Billboard|date=November 1, 2019|accessdate=April 17, 2020}}</ref> Sech also appeared on the song "[[Ignorantes (Bad Bunny and Sech song)|Ignorantes]]" with [[Bad Bunny]], included on Bad Bunny's album ''[[YHLQMDLG]]''. The song reached the top ten in Panama, Spain and the US ''Billboard'' Hot Latin Songs chart, and peaked at number 49 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name=ignorantesrs>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/9324797/bad-bunny-sech-ignorantes-lyrics-translated-english|title= Bad Bunny & Sech Get Real About Failed Relationships in 'Ignorantes' Lyric Translation|work=Billboard|last=Roiz|first=Jessica|date=February 27, 2020|accessdate=May 9, 2020}}</ref> Other songs Sech was featured includes "[[Definitivamente (Daddy Yankee and Sech song)|Definitivamente]]" with [[Daddy Yankee]], and "Ganas De Ti" with [[Wisin & Yandel]], with both songs peaking at number two in Panama.

During a [[Zoom Video Communications|Zoom]] listening party on May 7, 2020, Sech announced that his second studio album, ''1 of 1'' would be released on May 21, 2020. The album's lead single, "Si Te Vas" with [[Ozuna (singer)|Ozuna]], reached the top ten in Panama and Spain. The album also includes the Panamanian top 10 single "Relación" and features appearances from [[Daddy Yankee]], [[Arcángel]], Nando Boom, and Lenny Tavarez.<ref name=1of1rs>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/sech-new-album-1-of-1-995834/|title=Sech Announces Upcoming Sophomore Album '1 of 1'|date=May 8, 2020|accessdate=May 9, 2020|work=Rolling Stone|last=Exposito|first=Suzy}}</ref> Sech explained that the album also includes "throwbacks to Panama's classic reggae en español sound".<ref name=1of1rs/> "Relación" became Sech's fourth top 10 on the [[Hot Latin Songs]] chart and his first solo song to do so. The song also garnered popularity on the video-sharing app [[TikTok]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9422461/sech-relacion-top-10-hot-latin-songs-chart|title=Sech's 'Relación' Hits Top 10 on Hot Latin Songs Chart|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Bustios|first=Pamela|date=July 22, 2020|accessdate=July 24, 2020}}</ref>

==Musical style==
Sech possesses a [[tenor]] vocal range.<ref name=needtoknow/> A ''Billboard'' staff member wrote that Sech's music contains "soothing vocals, sensual urban fusion melodies, and lyrics that focus on romance, dreams and positive vibes".<ref name=smooth/> In an interview with ''Billboard'', Sech mentioned [[El General]] as being an influence on his music.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Roiz |first1=Jessica |title=Sech Reveals His Favorite Things About Panamanian Culture, Big Dreams in New 'Growing Up Latino' Episode |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8528580/sech-growing-up-latino-video |website=Billboard |accessdate=21 September 2019 |date=28 August 2019}}</ref> "Otro Trago" was described by ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' as a "piano-reggaeton ballad about drinking and dancing the heartbreak away".<ref name=rsteddybear/> Sech is a fan of jazz music and expressed a desire to incorporate jazz music into his compositions.<ref name=rsteddybear/> He described his musical experimentation by saying, "Everything changes so fast. Today, we're singing reggaeton. Tomorrow, we'll be singing in Arabic! You never know where things are gonna go, and that's an exciting challenge".<ref name=rsteddybear/>
