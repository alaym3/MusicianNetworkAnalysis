'''The Revivalists''' are an American [[Rock music|rock]] band formed in [[New Orleans]], [[Louisiana]], in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-revivalists-mn0002026581/biography|title=The Revivalists - Biography & History - AllMusic|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref> The eight-piece band consists of Ed Williams (pedal steel guitar, guitar), David Shaw (lead vocals), Zack Feinberg (guitar), Rob Ingraham (saxophone), George Gekas (bass guitar), Andrew Campanelli (drums and percussion), Michael Girardot (keyboards and trumpet), and Paulet "PJ" Howard (drums and percussion).

After The Revivalists released their album, ''Men Amongst Mountains'', on July 17, 2015, they were named by ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine as one of "10 Bands You Need to Know".<ref name="Rolling Stone 1">{{cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/pictures/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-march-2016-20160324/the-revivalists-20160321|title=10 New Artists You Need to Know: March 2016|publisher=Rolling Stone|access-date=August 2, 2016}}</ref> In September 2016, their song "[[Wish I Knew You]]" reached No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' [[Adult Alternative Songs]] chart, and in May 2017, the song reached No. 1 on the [[Alternative Songs]] chart. On September 11, the single was certified platinum by the [[RIAA]].<ref name="RIAA">{{cite web |title=Award |url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=The+Revivalists#search_section |website=riaa.com |access-date=21 September 2018}}</ref>

A 2018 single, "All My Friends," from their album, ''Take Good Care'', went to No.1 at Adult Alternative radio.

== History ==
=== Early history ===

The Revivalists began when guitarist Zack Feinberg met vocalist/lyricist David Shaw as a young man riding his bike on a different route from usual, and rode past Shaw, recently transplanted from [[Hamilton, Ohio]]. Feinberg grew up outside [[New York City]] and has immersed himself in the [[jam band]] and [[funk]] scenes.<ref>Greenhaus, Mike https://relix.com/articles/detail/the-revivalists-hope-heritage/ Relix</ref> The two struck up a conversation and got together to play music.<ref name="Times Picayune 1">{{cite news |last1=Spera |first1=Keith |title=The Revivalists, born of a chance encounter, hit their stride |url=https://www.nola.com/music/index.ssf/2011/11/the_revivalists_born_of_a_chan.html |access-date=19 September 2018 |work=Times Picayune |date=November 10, 2011}}</ref> The duo began playing with Andrew Campanelli, a drummer Feinberg had met at [[Tipitina's]]. Within a week, the earliest version of the band, now named The Revivalists, played their first show at Checkpoint Charlie's Laundromat/Bar on Esplanade Avenue.<ref name="Huffington Post">{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/michael-bialas/going-along-for-the-ride_b_3538423.html|title=Going Along for The Ride, Part 1: The Revivalists Step Up, Up and Away|first1=Michael Bialas|last1=Entertainment|first2=Sports|last2=Journalist|date=3 July 2013|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref><ref name="AXS">{{cite news |last1=Todd |first1=Nathan |title=Watch: The Revivalists share video for 'Wish I Knew You,' continue western tour |url=https://www.axs.com/watch-the-revivalists-share-video-for-wish-i-knew-you-continue-western-77750 |access-date=19 September 2018 |work=AXS |date=March 4, 2016}}</ref> They began regular jam sessions at Tipitina's.<ref name="Times Picayune 2">{{cite news |last1=Brasted |first1=Chelsea |title=The Revivalists return home to play two-night stand at Tipitina's |url=https://www.nola.com/music/index.ssf/2015/11/revivalists_new_orleans_tipiti.html#incart_river_home |access-date=19 September 2018 |work=Times Picayune |date=November 17, 2015}}</ref> The Revivalists, now including Feinberg, Shaw, Campanelli, Ed Williams (pedal steel guitarist), Rob Ingraham (saxophonist) and George Gekas (bassist), and occasionally Michael Girardot (keyboardist/trumpeter),<ref name="Times Picayune 1" /> went on to play an average of 150 dates a year.<ref name="NPR">{{cite news |title=STUDIO SESSIONS The Revivalists On World Cafe |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2015/01/12/376752718/the-revivalists-on-world-cafe |access-date=19 September 2018 |work=NPR |date=January 12, 2015}}</ref>

===''The Revivalists'' EP, ''Vital Signs''===
The Revivalists released their first few records independently. This included the self-titled EP on May 15, 2008, which was named one of the best albums of 2008 by [[WWOZ]] New Orleans DJ Sherwood Collins.<ref name="wwoz.org">{{cite web |title=The Best CDs of 2008: Show Host Picks |url=https://www.wwoz.org/367-best-cds-2008-show-host-picks |website=wwoz.org |access-date=19 September 2018}}</ref>

Their debut album, ''Vital Signs'', came out on March 3, 2010,<ref name="Augusta Chronicle">{{cite news |last1=Williams |first1=Diana Renee |title=Revivalists ready to 'let the good times roll' |url=http://www.augustachronicle.com/news/20180606/revivalists-ready-to-let-good-times-roll |access-date=19 September 2018 |work=Augusta Chronicle |date=June 6, 2018}}</ref> and was produced by engineer Chris Finney.<ref name="allmusic 2">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/chris-finney-mn0000114311/credits|title=Chris Finney - Credits - AllMusic|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref> The New Orleans ''Times Picayune'' said the music had "irresistible hooks, melodies and arrangements."<ref name="Times Picayune 3">{{cite news |last1=Spera |first1=Keith |title=The Revivalists' new CD, slate of bookings make them band on the verge |url=https://www.nola.com/music/index.ssf/2012/03/the_revivalists_new_cd_slate_o.html |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Times Picayune |date=March 9, 2012}}</ref>

The band spent the time between the release of the EP and after the release of ''Vital Signs'' touring the US.<ref name="Offbeat">{{cite news |last1=Lafont |first1=Aaron |title=The Revivalists, Vital Signs (Independent) |url=http://www.offbeat.com/music/the-revivalists-vital-signs-independent/ |access-date=19 September 2018 |work=Offbeat |date=May 1, 2010}}</ref>

===''City of Sound''===
The Revivalists' second album, ''City of Sound'' was self-released in 2012 and re-released on March 4, 2014, by [[Wind-up Records]], with a second disc containing over an hour of live tracks.<ref name="NPR" /> On ''City of Sound'', the band expanded their sound toward folk, pop, and soul.

Singles from this album included "Criminal",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.glidemagazine.com/hiddentrack/hidden-track-interview-the-revivalists/|title=Hidden Track Interview: The Revivalists - Glide Magazine|date=14 January 2013|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2014/02/25/album-stream-the-revivalists-city-of-sound-david-shaw/5780041/|title=Album stream: The Revivalists reissue 'City of Sound'|access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref> "Upright",<ref name="Times Picayune 3" /> and  "Navigate Below",<ref name="Relix">{{cite news |title=The Revivalists "Navigate Below" |url=https://relix.com/video/the_revivalists_navigate_below/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Relix |date=July 17, 2014}}</ref> and it was produced by [[Galactic]] saxophonist Ben Ellman.<ref name="Times Picayune 1" />

In June 2013, The Revivalists appeared at New York City's Governors Ball, among other festivals.<ref name="Spin">{{cite news |last1=McGovern |first1=Kyle |title=Governors Ball 2013 Lineup: Kanye West, Dinosaur Jr., Nas, Kendrick Lamar, Grizzly Bear |url=https://www.spin.com/2013/01/governors-ball-2013-lineup-kanye-west-dinosaur-jr-nas-kendrick-lamar-grizzly-bear/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Spin |date=January 22, 2013}}</ref>

===''Men Amongst Mountains''===
The Revivalists' third album, ''Men Amongst Mountains'', was released on July 17, 2015.<ref name="Paste Magazine">{{cite news |last1=Orr |first1=Dacey |title=Album Stream: The Revivalists - Men Amongst Mountains |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2015/07/album-stream-the-revivalists---men-amongst-mountai.html |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Paste Magazine |date=July 13, 2015}}</ref> In March 2016, the band was picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'' show hosted by [[Kathie Lee Gifford]] and [[Hoda Kotb]] and broadcast nationally performing their single "[[Wish I Knew You]]".<ref name="Times Picayune 4">{{cite news |last1=Brasted |first1=Chelsea |title=The Revivalists picked as Artist of the Month on Today Show |url=https://www.nola.com/music/index.ssf/2016/04/revivalists_today_show_music_n.html |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Times Picayune |date=April 12, 2016}}</ref>

The single, "Wish I Knew You" went to #1 on ''Billboard'''s Adult Alternative and Alternative radio charts,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7518685/revivalists-first-adult-alternative-songs-number-1|title=The Revivalists Achieve First Adult Alternative Songs No. 1|last1=Rutherford|first1=Kevin|date=22 September 2016|publisher=Billboard|access-date=24 September 2016}}</ref> and remained on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 for nine weeks.<ref name="Augusta Chronicle" /> The song was certified platinum by the [[RIAA]], and ''[[Forbes]]'' magazine called it: "one of the best and catchiest radio singles of the year".<ref name="Forbes">{{cite news |last1=Baltin |first1=Steve |title=The Revivalists Enjoying Their 'Overnight Success' Ten Years In With Hit Song |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/stevebaltin/2017/06/27/the-revivalists-enjoying-their-overnight-success-ten-years-in-with-hit-song/#53e64fd3d47b |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Forbes |date=June 27, 2017}}</ref>

The Revivalists made performances on ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]]'', ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show|Ellen]]'', and [[Conan (talk show)|''Conan'']].<ref name="Gotham">{{cite news |last1=Kelly |first1=Cait Rohan |title=David Shaw of The Revivalists on Playing Summerstage & Splitting His Head Open While Singing Live |url=https://gotham-magazine.com/david-shaw-of-the-revivalists-on-playing-summerstage-and-splitting-his-head-open-on-stage |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Gotham Magazine |date=August 7, 2017}}</ref> ''Rolling Stone'' touted them among “10 Artists You Need To Know,”  and they received a [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Award]] nomination for Top Rock Song<ref name="People">{{cite news |last1=Sands |first1=Nicole |title=Billboard Music Awards 2018: Check Out the Complete List of Winners! |url=https://people.com/music/billboard-music-awards-2018-winners-list/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=People magazine |date=May 20, 2018}}</ref> and as well as nominations for Alternative Rock Song of the Year and Best New Rock/Alternative Rock Artist at the [[iHeartRadio]] Music Awards.<ref name="Us Magazine">{{cite news |title=2018 iHeartRadio Music Awards: Complete Winners List |url=https://www.usmagazine.com/page/423/?p=0&KSID=0c7a08e30532ecf185aa88c6c5c5f9d3&jwsource=cl |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Us Magazine |date=September 1, 2018}}</ref>

''Men Amongst Mountains'' received more than 2 million streams on [[Spotify]],<ref name="Rolling Stone 1" /> and ended up in the Top 5 of the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Heatseekers chart.<ref name="EW">{{cite news |last1=Vain |first1=Madison |title=Breakout rockers The Revivalists share a soulful, jam-tastic playlist of their influences |url=https://ew.com/music/2017/06/26/revivalists-playlist-influences/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Entertainment Weekly |date=June 26, 2017}}</ref>

During this period, The Revivalists played New York City's Central Park SummerStage<ref name="Salon">{{cite news |last1=Marcotte |first1=Amanda |title=Salon Stage: The Revivalists perform an exclusive acoustic set |url=https://www.salon.com/2017/08/12/salon-stage-the-revivalists-perform-an-exclusive-acoustic-set/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Salon |date=August 12, 2017}}</ref> as well as [[Austin City Limits]],<ref name="Billboard">{{cite news |last1=Weatherby |first1=Taylor |title=Front Porch Jams to Top 40 Radio: The Revivalists Discuss How 'Wish I Knew You' Propelled Their Career, 10 Years in the Making |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8023103/the-revivalists-wish-i-knew-you-interview |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Billboard |date=November 3, 2017}}</ref> New Orleans Jazz Fest (opening for [[Dave Matthews|Dave Mathews]]),<ref name="Times Picayune 5">{{cite news |last1=Brasted |first1=Chelsea |title=The Revivalists rock New Orleans Jazz Fest 2017 |url=https://www.nola.com/jazzfest/index.ssf/2017/05/jazz_fest_the_revivalists_new.html |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Times Picayune |date=May 5, 2017}}</ref> Outside Lands,<ref name="Stereogum">{{cite news |last1=Rettig |first1=James |title=Outside Lands 2015 Lineup |url=https://www.stereogum.com/1789323/outside-lands-2015-lineup/news/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Stereogum |date=March 24, 2015}}</ref> KROQ Weenie Roast & Fiesta,<ref name="Forbes" /> and more.

Two months after the death of [[David Bowie]], The Revivalists premiered their tribute to the late artist in the song they wrote called "Bowie" featuring a verse sung by saxophonist Ingraham.<ref name="Live for Live Music">{{cite news |title=The Revivalists Debut New Original Song Dedicated To David Bowie [Watch] |url=http://liveforlivemusic.com/news/the-revivalists-debut-new-original-song-dedicated-to-david-bowie-watch/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Live for Live Music |date=February 7, 2016}}</ref>

In late 2017, Paulet "PJ" Howard was added to the line-up on drums and percussion.

The band has a partnership with NOLA Brewing Company for The Revivalists American Pale Ale. The artisanal beer debuted during the band's annual New Year's Eve residency at The Orpheum Theater in New Orleans and followed through their distribution center shortly after. A portion of the proceeds benefits The Roots of Music Foundation.<ref name="Baton Rouge Advocate">{{cite news |last1=Day |first1=Brenton |title=On Tap: Dogfish Head launching in Louisiana and more craft beer news |url=https://www.theadvocate.com/baton_rouge/entertainment_life/article_dc11e3cc-ef1f-11e7-a6fe-0f4f341f01ca.html |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Baton Rouge Advocate |date=January 3, 2018}}</ref>

===''Take Good Care''===
In August 2018, "All My Friends" was released as the first single from The Revivalists' fourth studio album, ''Take Good Care'',<ref name="Alt Press">{{cite news |last1=Hannan |first1=Devon |title=10 New Songs You Need to Hear This Week |url=https://www.altpress.com/features/new-songs-august-4/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Alternative Press |date=August 4, 2018}}</ref> and later went to No. 1 on the Adult Alternative radio chart at Billboard.<ref name="Billboard 3">{{cite news |last1=Rutherford |first1=Kevin |title=The Revivalists' 'All My Friends' Rises to No. 1 on Adult Alternative Songs Chart |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8475863/the-revivalists-all-my-friends-adult-alternative-songs-chart |access-date=23 September 2018 |work=Billboard |date=September 18, 2018}}</ref> The album, released November 9, 2018, is the band's first release with the indie label Loma Vista Recordings. For the first time, The Revivalists recorded and co-wrote with multiple producers and writers, enlisting the talents of Dave Cobb ([[Sturgill Simpson]], [[Chris Stapleton]]), Andrew Dawson ([[Kanye West]], [[Fun.]], [[Sleigh Bells (band)|Sleigh Bells]]), and Dave Bassett ([[Elle King]], [[Vance Joy]]) for sessions Nashville's [[RCA]] Studio B. Additionally, it would be the first record with drummer/percussionist PJ Howard joining the band in the studio.<ref name="Billboard 2">{{cite news |last1=Kress |first1=Bryan |title=The Revivalists Find Themselves in Good Company On 'All My Friends': Premiere |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8468242/the-revivalists-all-my-friends |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Billboard |date=August 2, 2018}}</ref><ref name="AXS 2">{{cite news |last1=Dominguez |first1=Erica |title=The Revivalists to release new studio album in November |url=https://www.axs.com/the-revivalists-to-release-new-studio-album-in-november-133406 |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=AXS |date=September 14, 2018}}</ref>

The Revivalists continue to tour, with shows sometimes four hours at a time<ref name="ABC News">{{cite news |last1=Scott |first1=Rachel |title=The Revivalists Keep New Orleans Rock Alive |url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/revivalists-orleans-rock-alive/story?id=39092224 |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=ABC News |date=May 13, 2016}}</ref> with a tour schedule that includes [[Bonnaroo]],<ref name="Tennessean">{{cite news |title=Bonnaroo: What happened while you were sleeping on Day 1 |url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/bonnaroo/2018/06/08/bonnaroo-what-happened-manchester-line-up/685143002/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Tennessean |date=June 8, 2018}}</ref> Voodoo Music & Arts Experience, and Music Midtown.<ref name="AXS 2" />

==Philanthropy==
Over the years, The Revivalists have made efforts to raise money to help various causes. Feinberg, Gekas and Ingraham joined members of [[Naughty Professor]] and Cardinal Sons as well as RumpelSTEELskin at The Howlin' Wolf in New Orleans for a show that contributed financial support to [[Emily's Entourage]], a non-profit dedicated to finding a cure for [[cystic fibrosis]].<ref name="Live for Live Music" />

The group also worked with the Roots of Music, an after-school music and tutoring program for over 150 local children. One of the fundraisers included The Roots of Music presents Band Together, a concert with many of the kids and local bands including The Revivalists.<ref name="WGNO ABC">{{cite news |title=Revivalists and Roots 'Band Together' for weekend concert |url=https://wgno.com/2016/10/19/revivalists-and-roots-band-together-for-weekend-concert/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=WGNO ABC |date=October 19, 2016}}</ref> The band also donated money from some of their own shows to the program.<ref name="Jambands">{{cite news |title=The Revivalists Set New Year's Eve Show in New Orleans |url=https://jambands.com/news/2016/09/26/the-revivalists-set-new-year-s-eve-show-in-new-orleans/ |access-date=20 September 2018 |work=Jambands |date=September 26, 2016}}</ref>

In the fall of 2019, The Revivalists established Rev Causes. Rev Causes is a fund that supports the essential work of organizations dedicated to reviving and investing in their communities, health, and our environment. By donating a portion of ticket sales, and through fan donations and a variety of fundraising efforts, Rev Causes aims to assist these organizations in building a better future. Organizations include The Center for Disaster Philanthropy (CDP), Everytown for Gun Safety, National Alliance on Mental Safety (NAMI), Songs for Kids Foundation, and Upturn Arts.
