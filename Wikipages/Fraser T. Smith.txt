'''Fraser Thorneycroft-Smith''' (born 8 February 1971), known professionally as '''Fraser T. Smith''', is an English record producer, songwriter and musician. Some of the singles he collaborated on include [[Adele]]'s "[[Set Fire to the Rain]]", [[James Morrison (singer)|James Morrison]]'s "[[Broken Strings (song)|Broken Strings]]", [[Tinchy Stryder]]'s "[[Number 1 (Tinchy Stryder song)|Number 1]]" and [[Taio Cruz]]'s "[[Break Your Heart]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://themanifesto.co/feature/2014/03/fraser-t-smith |title=— The Manifesto |publisher=Themanifesto.co |date=2014-03-26 |access-date=2014-05-30 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140531123947/http://themanifesto.co/feature/2014/03/fraser-t-smith |archive-date=31 May 2014}}</ref> In 2016, Smith teamed up with Stormzy to produce his debut album ''[[Gang Signs & Prayer]]'', which won best album at the 2018 Brit Awards. Earlier that year he produced seven tracks on Kano's ''[[Made in the Manor]]'' album and co produced the debut EP from South London rapper Dave. Smith has also worked with [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]].

Smith released his debut album, 12 Questions, on 30 October 2020. The record is based on a series of 12 questions all born of Smith’s own anxieties about the world, discussing issues such as faith, freedom, race, gender, wealth, equality and ecology. Smith collaborated with [[Dave (rapper)|Dave]], [[Kano (British musician)|Kano]], [[Ghetts]], [[Bastille (band)|Bastille]], [[Idris Elba]] and [[Stormzy]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Stewart, Rev. Hugh Fraser, (1863–23 Jan. 1948), Emeritus Reader in French; Fellow of Trinity College, Cambridge; Fellow of Eton College; Chevalier de la Légion d’Honneur|date=2007-12-01|url=http://dx.doi.org/10.1093/ww/9780199540884.013.u232220|work=Who Was Who|publisher=Oxford University Press|access-date=2021-10-26}}</ref>

On 18 March 2021, Smith was presented with a RSL (Rockschool Ltd) Fellowship.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-18|title=RSL Honorary Fellowship: Fraser T Smith|url=https://www.rslawards.com/rsl-fellow-fraser-t-smith/|access-date=2021-10-26|website=RSL Awards|language=en-GB}}</ref>

In August 2021, Dave and Smith were nominated for the Ivors with Apple Music 2021 in the Best Contemporary Song category for "Children of the Internet" (Reference:<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-08-10|title=Nominations announced for The Ivors with Apple Music 2021|url=https://ivorsacademy.com/news/nominations-announced-for-the-ivors-with-apple-music-2021/|access-date=2021-10-26|website=The Ivors Academy|language=en-GB}}</ref> Dave and Smith subsequently won. This was their third Ivor Novello win in four years. The pair were awarded best contemporary song for Children of the Internet. The song is performed by Future Utopia. This was producer Smith’s new project and features Dave and [[Es Devlin]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-09-21|title=Fraser T Smith, Dave & Es Devlin's "Children Of The Internet" Wins 2021 Ivor Novello For Best Contemporary Song|url=https://grmdaily.com/ivor-novello-awards-2021-winners/|access-date=2021-10-26|website=GRM Daily|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Celeste, Obongjayar, Dave and Fraser T Smith win at Ivor Novello awards 2021|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/celeste-obongjayar-dave-fraser-t-smith-win-at-ivor-novello-awards-2021|access-date=2021-10-26|website=The Line of Best Fit|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Edmonds|first=Lizzie|date=2021-09-21|title=Harry Styles among first-time winners at Ivor Novello Awards|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/uk/ivor-novello-awards-harry-styles-firsttime-winners-b956576.html|access-date=2021-10-26|website=www.standard.co.uk|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The Ivors 2021: Full Winners List|url=https://headlinermagazine.net/blog/the-ivors-2021-full-winners-list.html|access-date=2021-10-26|website=headlinermagazine.net}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Smith started his career playing guitar in pubs and clubs both as a solo musician and in bands.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundonsound.com/sos/nov09/articles/it_1109.htm |title=Secrets of the Mix Engineers: Fraser T Smith |publisher=Soundonsound.com |access-date=2014-05-30}}</ref> In 1992, Smith met [[Rick Wakeman]] with whom he toured and recorded.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/2010/March22_4_20_43.html |title=A&R, Record Label / Company, Music Publishing, Artist Manager and Music Industry Directory |publisher=Hitquarters.com |access-date=2014-05-30}}</ref> Smith then went on to work with [[Tony Hadley]] and [[Adam Wakeman]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.explore-multimedia.co.uk/ExM001.htm |title=Explore Multimedia |publisher=Explore Multimedia |access-date=2014-05-30}}</ref> In the mid-1990s, they formed Jeronimo Road; a progressive rock band. Smith then became involved in playing guitar in studio sessions, performing on over 200 records, including [[Rui da Silva (DJ)|Rui da Silva]]'s number one "[[Touch Me (Rui Da Silva song)|Touch Me]]" and Tim Deluxe's "It Just Won’t Do".<ref>{{cite web|author=Cassandra |url=http://musicbrainz.org/release/d7afcd00-aa8a-4fbe-8d37-902384348270 |title=Release "Touch Me" by Rui da Silva feat. Cassandra |publisher=MusicBrainz |date=2001-04-02 |access-date=2014-05-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Tim-Deluxe-Featuring-Sam-Obernik-It-Just-Wont-Do/release/591329 |title=Tim Deluxe Featuring Sam Obernik - It Just Won't Do (CD) at Discogs |publisher=Discogs.com |access-date=2014-05-30}}</ref>

==Career==
In 1999, Smith was introduced to the then-unknown [[Craig David]] and spent five years working as his guitarist performing on television and radio, as well as at concerts including the [[John Lennon]] Tribute Concert at [[Radio City Music Hall|Radio City Hall]] in New York, the [[Tsunami Relief Cardiff]] at the [[Millennium Stadium]] in [[Cardiff]], and [[Live 8 concert, London|Live 8, London]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gforcesoftware.com/artists/interviews/fraser-smith-craig-david-plan-b |title=Fraser Smith - From Craig David to Plan B |publisher=Gforce Software |access-date=2014-05-30}}</ref> They remixed songs together under the pseudonym of Treats and have written the songs "World Filled With Love", "6 of 1 Thing", and "Hot Stuff".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Songs written by Fraser T. Smith {{!}} SecondHandSongs|url=https://secondhandsongs.com/artist/53484|access-date=2021-10-26|website=secondhandsongs.com}}</ref>

In 2011, Smith won the Grammy Award for his work with Adele.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-23|title=Fraser T. Smith|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/fraser-t-smith/10109|access-date=2021-10-26|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref>

In 2012, Smith was nominated for an [[Ivor Novello Awards|Ivor Novello Award]] for his writing on "[[Broken Strings (song)|Broken Strings]]".<ref>{{cite web |last1=CMU Editorial |title=Ivor Novello noms out |url=https://www.completemusicupdate.com/article/ivor-novello-noms-out/ |website=Complete Music Update |publisher=3CM UnLimited |date=21 April 2010}}</ref> In 2015, Smith was nominated for Album of the Year in the [[57th Grammy Awards]] for his contribution to Sam Smith's album, ''[[In the Lonely Hour]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/grammy-awards-2015-the-complete-winners-list-20150208|title=Grammy Awards 2015: The Complete Winners List|work=Rolling Stone}}</ref>

Between 2013 and 2016, Smith worked with long-time collaborator [[Kano (British musician)|Kano]] on his album ''[[Made in the Manor]]'', which was subsequently shortlisted for the 2016 [[Mercury Prize]]<ref name=nominees2016>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-36962513|title=Mercury Prize 2016: David Bowie gets posthumous nomination|publisher=BBC|access-date=5 August 2016}}</ref> and nominated for Album of the Year at the [[2017 Brit Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-38624740|title= Brit Awards 2017: The nominations|date=14 January 2017|work=BBC News|access-date=3 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.nme.com/news/music/brit-awards-2017-full-winners-list-1986346|title= BRIT Awards 2017 full winners list|date=22 February 2017|work=NME|access-date=23 February 2017}}</ref> It went on to win Best Album at the [[MOBO Awards]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Kano named Best Album at 2016 Mobo Awards |url=https://www.itv.com/news/update/2016-11-04/kano-named-best-album-at-2016-mobo-awards/ |website=ITV News |language=en |date=4 November 2016}}</ref>

Smith also collaborated with Mexican duo [[Jesse & Joy]] on their album ''[[Un Besito Más]]'', which was released in December 2015. At the 17th Annual Latin Grammy Awards, the album received the nomination for [[Latin Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] and won for Best Contemporary Pop Vocal Album.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Here Is the Complete List of Nominees for the 2017 Grammys|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7597556/grammys-nominees-complete-list-2017|access-date=2021-10-26|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

In 2016, Smith was approached by grime and hip hop artist [[Stormzy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-43224339|title=Meet the man Stormzy hugged at the Brits|first=Mark|last=Savage|date=1 March 2018|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> They spent ten months writing and producing his debut album, ''[[Gang Signs & Prayer]]'', released independently through #Merky Records on 24 February 2017. The album was supported by the lead single "[[Big for Your Boots]]".

During the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Smith produced the [[Times Like These (song)#Live Lounge Allstars charity single|Live Lounge Allstars' charity version]] of the [[Foo Fighters]]' "[[Times Like These (song)|Times Like These]]", featuring dozens of musicians recorded from their homes.<ref name="Times Like These">{{Cite web |title=Fraser T Smith: How I brought 24 musicians together during lockdown for Radio 1's first Number One |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/articles/35RvGLnlk74hX6YbhxhFDHs/fraser-t-smith-how-i-brought-24-musicians-together-during-lockdown-for-radio-1-s-first-number-one |website=BBC |access-date=2020-05-03}}</ref>

In 2018, Dave and Smith were nominated for and won an Ivor Novello Award for the track "Question Time".<ref>{{Cite news|date=2018-05-31|title=Ivor Novello Awards: Rapper Dave wins for political anthem Question Time|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-44318307|access-date=2021-10-26}}</ref>

In 2020, Dave and Smith were nominated for and won an Ivor Novello Award for the track "Black".<ref>{{Cite news|date=2018-05-31|title=Ivor Novello Awards: Rapper Dave wins for political anthem Question Time|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-44318307|access-date=2021-10-26}}</ref>
