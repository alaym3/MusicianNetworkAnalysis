'''Adam John Grandmaison''' (born November 24, 1983), more commonly known as '''Adam22''', is an American [[podcast]]er, [[Blog|journalist]], [[Internet celebrity|internet personality]], [[Music executive|former record executive]], and [[adult film star]].<ref name=afs>Hollins, Evan. [https://www.vladtv.com/article/268978/adam22-explains-his-involvement-in-the-porn-business EXCLUSIVE: Adam22 Explains His Involvement in the Porn Business]. VladTV. January 23, 2021.</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/AVNMediaNetwork/status/1460713807781765128|title=AVN Media Network on Twitter ''"Adam22, Lena the Plug Debut ‘Plug Talk’ Podcast Series'' |work=Twitter|access-date=November 16, 2021|language=en}}</ref> He is best known for being the creator and host of [[pop culture]]-oriented [[podcast]] '''No Jumper'''.<ref name=law>Grierson, Tim.  .
[http://www.laweekly.com/music/adam22s-no-jumper-podcast-has-become-essential-listening-for-rap-fans-8050618 How Adam22 Turned His BMX Podcast Into Essential Listening for Rap Fans]. LA Weekly. March 22, 2017.</ref><ref>Roberts, Molly.

[http://www.miaminewtimes.com/music/no-jumpers-adam22-on-trap-circus-and-miamis-exploding-rap-scene-9847029 Meet Adam22, the Podcast Mogul Who's Hosting Trap Circus]. Miami New Times. November 22, 2017.</ref>

==Early life==
Grandmaison became a [[BMX]] rider at a young age and enjoyed [[Hip-Hop music]], including [[Gucci Mane]]'s hit "[[The State vs. Radric Davis|Bricks]]" that includes the line, "I'm ballin' like an athlete but got no Jumper." This inspired the name ''No Jumper'' for his future blog. Grandmaison has mentioned this on his podcast and in interviews many times.<ref>Roberts, Molly. [https://www.inputmag.com/features/hip-hop-no-jumper-adam22-youtube-livestream-pay-for-play Are you the next Lil Pump? These YouTubers will tell you — for a price.]. Input Magazine. March 17, 2020.</ref>

Former [[United States|U.S.]] [[President of the United States|President]] [[Bill Clinton]] was close friends with Grandmaison's father, Philip J. Grandmaison, whose brother was one of Clinton's earliest supporters in [[1992 United States presidential election|1992]]. In December of 2000, Clinton pardoned Grandmaison's father after formerly being convicted of mail fraud in 1996.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://caselaw.findlaw.com/us-1st-circuit/1140274.html|title= ''UNITED STATES v. GRANDMAISON''|work=findlaw.com|access-date=November 16, 2021|language=en}}</ref><ref>Miller, Bill.
[https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/politics/2000/12/23/a-former-giant-of-house-is-pardoned/40db3f5d-b527-4ef2-8ff0-e2e85b1487ce/]. Washington Post. December 12, 2000.</ref>

==Career==
===2006-2011'': The Come Up and ONSOMESHIT''===
In 2006, Grandmaison started the website "The Come Up," which has become one of the most popular BMX websites of all time.<ref name=lawone>Libman, Kristen. [http://www.laweekly.com/arts/how-adam-grandmaison-is-changing-the-way-you-look-at-bmx-2371186 How Adam Grandmaison Is Changing the Way You Look at BMX]. LA Weekly. November 31, 2012.</ref>

Later, he created ONSOMESHIT, a BMX biking team and BMX-based clothing line which at one point had a retail store on [[Melrose Avenue]] in Los Angeles.<ref name=law /><ref name=pp>Gardner, Alex. [http://pigeonsandplanes.com/in-depth/2017/09/adam-22-no-jumper-video-interview How No Jumper's Adam22 Became an Unlikely Rap Tastemaker]. Pigeons & Planes. February,32 2017.</ref>

Grandmaison hosted the inaugural Trap Circus music festival in Miami, Florida in 2017.<ref name=mnt /><ref>Coleman, Vernon C.
[http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/11/cardi-b-previews-more-new-music-ahead-of-debut-album/ Cardi B Previews More New Music Ahead of Debut Albums]. XXL. November 18, 2017.</ref>

===2011-present'': No Jumper''===
''No Jumper'' was originally a blog on [[Tumblr]], started by George Potter in 2011 and financed by Grandmaison.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/thefader/status/978724794899185664|title=The FADER on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=August 3, 2018|language=en}}</ref> The blog reviewed early underground artists such as [[SpaceGhostPurrp]] and [[Clams Casino (musician)|Clams Casino]], and reviewed obscure [[Gucci Mane]] mixtapes, treating the underground with "critical attention."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/03/27/no-jumper-adam22-tumblr-origin-story|title=The true origin story of No Jumper|work=The FADER|access-date=August 3, 2018|language=en}}</ref>

Revived in 2015, Grandmaison's ''No Jumper'' podcast, hosted on YouTube, focuses on interviews with underground rap artists and tastemakers.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/23/arts/music/popcast-soundcloud-rap.html Hip-Hop's Punk Moment Has Arrived]. New York Times. June 23, 2017.</ref> As of March 2017, the interviews had received approximately 500,000 to 1 million views each.<ref name="law" /> It originally started as a BMX podcast with vlogs dedicated to Grandmaison's life running ONSOMESHIT's retail store. His interview of [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]] rapper and well-known underground rap figure Xavier Wulf soon turned viral within hip-hop communities, sparking Grandmaison's newfound interest in interviewing known figures within both underground and mainstream hip-hop.<ref name="Study Breaks">{{Cite news|url=https://studybreaks.com/culture/music/no-jumper-podcast/|title=Adam Grandmaison's 'No Jumper' Podcast Is a Drama and Hip Hop Extravaganza|date=August 1, 2018|work=Study Breaks|access-date=August 3, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

Grandmaison, through his ''No Jumper'' podcast, has been noted for interviewing up-and-coming talent as well as established rappers. He has interviewed artists including [[Lil Yachty]], [[Lil Peep]], [[Pouya (rapper)|Pouya]], [[Juice WRLD]], [[Suicideboys]], [[Young Thug]], [[6ix9ine|Tekashi69]] and most famously [[XXXTentacion]] in April 2016; the latter interview received over 13 million views.<ref name=pp /><ref name=rs /> This was the Florida rapper's first official professional interview, and has been credited with giving him exposure to a wide audience.<ref>Salisbury, Drew. [https://www.spin.com/2017/12/xxxtentacion-accusations-timeline/ XXXTentacion Case: A Timeline of the Accusations]. Spin. December 22, 2017.</ref><ref>Buford, Corinna. [http://www.vulture.com/2017/12/a-complete-timeline-xxxtentacions-controversial-career.html The Complete History (So Far) of XXXTentacion's Controversial Career]. Vulture. December 20, 2017.</ref>

[[WQHT|Hot 97]] DJ [[Peter Rosenberg]] has said he views Grandmaison "as an advance scout searching for combustible new talent," while ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' described him as "underground hip-hop's major tastemaker."<ref name=rs />

The ''No Jumper'' YouTube channel had over 518 million total video views and 2.7&nbsp;million subscribers as of March 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://socialblade.com/youtube/user/thecomeuphowtos|title=thecomeuphowtos YouTube Stats, Channel Statistics - Socialblade.com|website=socialblade.com|access-date=November 1, 2018}}</ref> [[Jon Caramanica]] of the ''[[New York Times]]'' described ''No Jumper'' as "''[[The Paris Review]]'' for the [[face tattoo|face-tattoo]] set."<ref>Caramanica, Jon. [https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/22/arts/music/soundcloud-rap-lil-pump-smokepurrp-xxxtentacion.html?_r=0 The Rowdy World of Rap's New Underground]. New York Times. June 22, 2017.</ref>

On June 20, 2018, Grandmaison held a memorial event for [[XXXTentacion]], [[Murder of XXXTentacion|who had been murdered]] two days prior, in front of his ONSOMESHIT store, with a crowd of 300 people.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://twitter.com/ktla/status/1009281086268952576?s=21 |title=KTLA on Twitter |work=Twitter|access-date=June 20, 2018 |language=en}}</ref> The crowd grew to over 1,000, and police in [[Riot control|riot gear]] eventually appeared.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://variety.com/2018/music/news/los-angeles-xxxtentacion-memorial-1202851814/ |title=Hundreds Gather in Los Angeles to Remember XXXTentacion |last=Nyren |first=Erin |date=June 20, 2018 |work=Variety|access-date=June 20, 2018 |language=en-US}}</ref> According to reports, [[rubber bullet]]s were shot and [[tear gas]] was used to disperse the crowd.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://ktla.com/2018/06/19/crowd-of-300-takes-to-the-street-in-fairfax-district-amid-memorial-for-slain-rapper-xxxtentacion/ |title=Throng of Hundreds Shuts Down Melrose Avenue Amid Memorial for Slain Rapper XXXTentacion |date=June 20, 2018 |work=KTLA|access-date=June 20, 2018 |language=en-US}}</ref>

Grandmaison was one of the promoters for YouTube personality [[Yousef Erakat|FouseyTube]]'s event, "Hate Dies, Love Arrives", which was shut down via a bomb threat which left 1,500 people evacuated.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.newsweek.com/fouseytube-drake-fouseycon-adam22-keemstar-1029155|title=YouTuber FouseyTube had promised Drake would show up at his show, he never did|date=July 17, 2018|work=Newsweek|access-date=August 3, 2018|language=en}}</ref> Shortly after the event, while Grandmaison interviewed [[Shane Dawson]], Mid-interview, [[Yousef Erakat|FouseyTube]], [[Keemstar]], and numerous other YouTube personalities interrupted, causing the interview to go viral.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.polygon.com/2018/7/18/17581452/fouseytube-hate-dies-love-arrives-bomb-threat-swatting-tanacon|title=FouseyTube's disastrous festival echoes TanaCon, reopens debate over YouTuber events|work=Polygon|access-date=August 3, 2018}}</ref>

In addition to underground and established [[Hip hop music|rappers]], Grandmaison through his ''No Jumper'' channel has interviewed a variety of podcast guests of different careers and backgrounds including [[Pornographic film actor|Porn Actresses]], [[Music executive|Record Label Executives]], [[Livestreaming|Twitch Streamers]], Other [[YouTuber]]s, [[Music journalism| Music Critics]], and [[Fashion design| Fashion Designers]]. He has interviewed people such as [[Anthony Fantano]], [[Adin Ross]], [[Riley Reid]], [[Sean O'Malley (fighter)|"Sugar" Sean O'Malley]], [[Asspizza]], and [[Nelk| The Nelk Boys]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/adam22/status/986154552524095489|title=Adam22 on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=April 17, 2018|language=en}}</ref>
  
===2017-present: ''Pornography''===
Grandmaison began debuting in personalized [[Pornography| adult films]] alongside his partner Lena Nersesian (known professionally as Lena The Plug) for the website [[Onlyfans]] in 2017; uploading a two-minute and sixteen second video to Youtube announcing a [[Amateur pornography| Sex Tape]] starring the two of them to be released once his girlfriend was to reach 1 million subscribers.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pSWURIYUkRE|title=Lena The Plug on Youtube - ''$EXTAPE AT 1 MILLION''|work=Youtube|access-date=January 17, 2017|language=en}}</ref><ref>Walker, Angus.

In December 2019, the film was announced to be a professional [[Pornography|film]] and was released for free in collaboration with [[Pornhub]] titled "Podcast Smash." The title is a reference to Adam22's ''No Jumper'' podcast and stars him and his partner Lena The Plug.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l98GrV72j9E|title=Lena The Plug on Youtube - ''THE 1 MILLION SUBS TAPE IS FINALLY HERE!''|work=Youtube|access-date=December 19, 2019|language=en}}</ref>

Grandmaison, along with his fiancé Lena, announced a new podcast in November 2021 entitled ''Plug Talk'', which focuses on interviewing (and having intercouse with) [[adult film star]]s on [[Onlyfans]]. It premiered November 16, 2021 with guest [[Adriana Chechik]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/plugtalkshow/status/1460666712034975745|title=PlugTalkShow on Twitter ''"The first episode of Plug Talk is now live! Grandmaison is engaged to vlogger, internet personality, and [[Pornographic film|adult film]] actress Lena Nersesian (Lena the Plug).<ref>[https://www.maxim.com/women/youtube-vlogger-lena-nersesian-threesome-hottie-2017-8 Watch Behind-the-Scenes Footage of the Threesome This YouTube Vlogger Had With a Hottie She Met on Twitter]. August 3, 2017.</ref> On Valentine's Day of 2020, the two announced that they are expecting their first child together.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/accounts/login/|title=Login • Instagram|website=www.instagram.com}}</ref> His daughter Parker Ann Grandmaison was born on November 14 at 7:40&nbsp;pm as Grandmaison announced on Twitter.<ref>{{Cite web|title=adam22 on Twitter:The baby is here, healthy and adorable|url=https://twitter.com/adam22/status/1327810416089579520|access-date=November 15, 2020|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>

Grandmaison identifies as an [[Atheism|atheist]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Quadeca Interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w2-JQpsRUGM?t=15m47s|date=March 26, 2019|access-date=March 27, 2019|publisher=[[No Jumper]]|website=YouTube}}</ref>

In 2018, Grandmaison was accused of [[Sexual assault|sexual]] and [[Assault|physical assault]] by two women.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/sexual-assault-allegations-against-adam22-of-no-jumper-detailed-by-two-women/|title=Sexual Assault Allegations Against Adam22 of No Jumper Detailed by Two Women|first=Marc|last=Hogan|website=Pitchfork}}</ref> Though he denied these accusations,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/no-jumper-podcastlabel-founder-adam-grandmaison-accused-of-rape-responds/|title=No Jumper Podcast/Label Founder Adam22 Accused of Rape, Responds|first=Braudie|last=Blais-Billie|website=Pitchfork}}</ref> [[Atlantic Records]] eventually severed its relationship with Grandmaison.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8490057/atlantic-records-drops-adam22s-no-jumper-label-deal-sexual-assault|title=Atlantic Records Drops Adam22's No Jumper Label Deal Following Sexual Assault Allegations|website=Billboard}}</ref>
