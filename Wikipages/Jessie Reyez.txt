'''Jessica Reyez''' (born June 13, 1991) is a Canadian singer and songwriter. Her 2016 single "[[Figures (song)|Figures]]" peaked at number 58 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] in 2017 and was certified triple [[Music Canada#Certification awards|Platinum]] by [[Music Canada]] and Platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref name="Chart history">{{cite web|title=Jessie Reyez – Chart history (Canadian Hot 100)|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7796643/jessie-reyez/chart?f=793|website=www.billboard.com|access-date=August 29, 2017}}</ref> Her 2017 [[Extended play|EP]], ''[[Kiddo (EP)|Kiddo]]'', led to four nominations at the [[Juno Awards of 2018|2018 Juno Awards]], winning [[Juno Award for Breakthrough Artist of the Year|Breakthrough Artist]]. Her follow up EP, ''[[Being Human in Public]],'' was released in 2018. It won [[Juno Award for R&B/Soul Recording of the Year|R&B/Soul Recording of The Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2019|2019 Juno Awards]] and was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album|Best Urban Contemporary Album]] at the [[2020 Grammy Awards]]. Reyez again won the [[Juno Award for R&B/Soul Recording of the Year|R&B/Soul Recording of the Year]], for "Feel it Too" with [[Tory Lanez]] and [[Tainy]], at the [[Juno Awards of 2020|2020 Juno Awards]], where she was also nominated for [[Juno Award for Artist of the Year|Artist of the Year]]. Reyez has written songs for [[Calvin Harris]] , [[Kehlani]] , [[Dua Lipa]], and [[Normani]], most notably penning the hit "[[One Kiss (song)|One Kiss]]", and has collaborated with [[Eminem]] on multiple occasions.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9344610/jessie-reyez-eminem-coffin|title=Jessie Reyez And Eminem Have Obsessive Love on "Coffin"|date=2020-03-27|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-04-17}}</ref> Her debut album, ''[[Before Love Came to Kill Us]]'', was released on March 27, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/hear_jessie_reyez_cover_patsy_clines_crazy|title=Hear Jessie Reyez Cover Patsy Cline's "Crazy"|website=exclaim.ca|language=en-ca|access-date=2019-11-23}}</ref>

==Early life==
Jessica Reyez was born in the [[Jane and Finch]] area of [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]] to [[Colombians|Colombian]] parents.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/how_jessie_reyez_became_the_2018_juno_awards_most_nominated_artist|title=How Jessie Reyez Became the 2018 Juno Awards' Most Nominated Artist|website=exclaim.ca|language=en-ca|access-date=2019-06-17}}</ref><ref name="NOW-2018">{{cite news |last1=Edwards |first1=Samantha |title=A day in the life of Jessie Reyez, Toronto's next big pop star |url=https://nowtoronto.com/music/features/jessie-reyez-interview-cover-story/ |access-date=30 June 2020 |work=NOW Toronto |date=10 October 2018 |location=Toronto ON |quote=Born in Toronto, Reyez grew up near Jane and Driftwood and then moved to Brampton .}}</ref>  She was introduced to the guitar by her father, leading to a career in art and music. She played guitar as a child and began writing her own music in high school.<ref name=":0" /> Jessie and her family then moved to [[Brampton]].<ref name="NOW-2018" /> Her roots brought her back to Toronto during her sophomore year. After graduating, she opted out of college and instead chose to pursue her music while doing odd jobs on the side. In 2012, Reyez and her family moved to [[Florida]], where she was primarily bartending and busking on the beach,<ref name=":0" /> but she returned to Toronto in 2014 after being accepted to The Remix Project's Academy of Recording Arts.<ref name="Noisey">{{cite web|last1=Patsuk|first1=Slava|title=Toronto's Jessie Reyez Connects with King Louie on Unlikely Terms|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/jessie-reyez-was-saved-by-the-remix-project|website=Noisey|access-date=May 3, 2017|language=en-us}}</ref> At the Remix Project, Reyez was mentored by Daniel Daley, from [[Dvsn]], and her music was heard by [[King Louie (rapper)|King Louie]].<ref name=":0" />

==Career==
=== Early career and ''Kiddo'' (2014–2017)  ===
In 2014, Reyez and [[King Louie (rapper)|King Louie]] released a joint single titled "Living in the Sky", followed by a second single, "It Hurts (Selena)", in 2015.<ref name=":0" /> In early 2017, she released the single "Shutter Island"<ref name="Popdust">{{cite web|last=Twardzik|first=Tom|title=Listen to the Newest Single By Upcoming R&B Hitmaker Jessie Reyez|url=https://www.popdust.com/jessie-reyez-shutter-island-2210511020.html|website=[[Popdust]]|access-date=May 3, 2017|date=January 24, 2017}}</ref> and toured Europe opening for [[PartyNextDoor]]. Her follow-up single, "[[Figures (song)|Figures]]", was premiered by [[Zane Lowe]] on [[Beats 1|Beats 1 radio]], alongside its music video. "Figures" peaked at number 58 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] in 2017<ref name="Chart history" /> and was certified [[Music Canada#Certification awards|triple platinum]] by Music Canada.

In April 2017, Reyez released her debut EP, [[Kiddo (EP)|''Kiddo'']]<ref name="Exclaim">{{cite web|last=Patrick|first=Ryan B.|title=Jessie Reyez Kiddo|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/jessie_reyez-kiddo|website=Exclaim|access-date=3 May 2017}}</ref> as well as her short film ''Gatekeeper'', which addressed sexism and exploitation in the music industry. It specifically was based on her experience with [[music producer]] [[Detail (record producer)|Noel "Detail" Fisher]], who was accused of [[sexual misconduct]] by multiple women, including artist [[Bebe Rexha]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Leight |first1=Elias |title=Jessie Reyez Accuses Beyonce Producer Detail of Sexual Misconduct |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/jessie-reyez-accuses-beyonce-producer-detail-of-sexual-misconduct-628308/ |access-date=9 August 2018 |work=Rolling Stone |date=10 May 2018}}</ref> Kiddo peaked at #83 on the [[Canadian Albums Chart|Canadian Album Chart]].

''Kiddo'' was placed on the Long List of the [[2017 Polaris Music Prize]] and lead to four nominations at the 2018 Juno Awards (R&B/Soul Recording of the Year, Breakthrough Artist, Video of the Year, and Juno Fan Choice Award),<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://nationalpost.com/pmn/entertainment-pmn/who-is-jessie-reyez-a-look-at-torontos-soulful-voice-leading-the-juno-nominees |title=Who is Jessie Reyez? A look at Toronto's soulful voice leading the Juno nominees |work= National Post|last=Friend |first=David |date=2018-02-07 |agency=[[The Canadian Press]] |language=en-CA|access-date=2019-06-17}}</ref> where she won Breakthrough Artist. She also received two [[2017 iHeartRadio Much Music Video Awards]] nominations for Best New Canadian Artist and Fan Fave Video for "Shutter Island".<ref name="MMVA">{{cite web|title=Fan Fave Video Nominee Jessie Reyez Drops Personal Short Film 'Gatekeeper'|url=http://mmva.muchmusic.com/jessie-reyez-short-film-gatekeeper/|website=MMVAs|access-date=May 18, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170811225824/http://mmva.muchmusic.com/jessie-reyez-short-film-gatekeeper/|archive-date=August 11, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Watch the iHeartRadio MMVAs Sunday June 18 @ 9E/6P|url=http://mmva.muchmusic.com/nominees/|website=MMVAs|access-date=May 24, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110520231740/http://mmva.muchmusic.com/nominees/|archive-date=May 20, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> She was also nominated for two 2018 [[MTV Music Video Awards]], including Push Artist of the Year and Video With A Message (for "Gatekeeper"). Reyez performed "Figures" at the [[BET Awards 2017|2017 BET Awards]] on June 25, 2017<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.canyon-news.com/beyonce-reigns-supreme-2017-bet-awards/69557|title=Beyoncé Reigns Supreme At 2017 BET Awards - Canyon News|date=June 26, 2017|website=Canyon News|access-date=June 26, 2017}}</ref> and on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' on August 8, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2017/08/08/first-look-jessie-reyez-performs-tonight-show-starring-jimmy-fallon/|title=Jessie Reyez Delivers Excellent Performance On Jimmy Fallon's "Tonight Show"|last1=Cantor|first1=Brian|date=8 August 2017|website=Headline Planet|access-date=21 August 2017}}</ref> Later in 2017, she signed a record and publishing deal with [[Island Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7950275/jessie-reyez-gatekeeper-film-island-records-interview|title=Jessie Reyez Interview: On 'Gatekeeper' Film & Island Records Deal|date=September 5, 2017|website=Billboard}}</ref>

=== ''Being Human in Public'' (2018–2019) ===
[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] named Reyez one of the "10 Hip-Hop and R&B Artists to Watch in 2018".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8092669/hip-hop-rb-artists-to-watch-in-2018|title=10 Hip-Hop and R&B Artists to Watch in 2018|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-06-17}}</ref> She also received a [[SOCAN Songwriting Prize]] nomination for the song "Cotton Candy".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/2018_socan_songwriting_prize_nominees_revealed|title=2018 SOCAN Songwriting Prize Nominees Revealed|magazine=[[Exclaim!]]|date=May 24, 2018}}</ref> Reyez released her second EP, ''[[Being Human in Public]]'', and toured across North America. The EP won 2019's [[Juno Award for R&B/Soul Recording of the Year|R&B/Soul Recording of The Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2019|2019 Juno Awards]] and was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album|Best Urban Contemporary Album]] at the [[2020 Grammy Awards]]. It also appeared on the short list for the [[2019 Polaris Music Prize]].

In August, Reyez collaborated with [[Eminem]] on "Nice Guy" and "Good Guy", from the album ''[[Kamikaze (Eminem album)|Kamikaze]],'' both charting on the [[Billboard Hot 100]]<ref name="rolling stone - kamikaze">{{cite web |last1=Leight |first1=Elias |title=Eminem Producer Explains Why 'Kamikaze' Is a Throwback to Slim Shady |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/eminem-kamikaze-slim-shady-producer-718242/ |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] |publisher=Wenner Media LLC|location=New York City|access-date=September 19, 2018 |date=August 31, 2018}}</ref> and appeared on the soundtrack to ''[[Fifty Shades Freed (film)|Fifty Shades Freed]].'' On April 9, 2019, Reyez released the single "[[Imported (song)|Imported]]" featuring [[6lack]], a re-working of a song off her last EP. The single was later certified Gold by both the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] and [[Music Canada]]. She also had a cameo in the [[Netflix]] film, ''[[Someone Great (film)|Someone Great]]'' and appeared on ''[[The Lion King: The Gift]],'' performing (with [[070 Shake]]) and co-writing the song "Scar"''.''

=== ''Before Love Came to Kill Us'' (2020–present) ===
Reyez received her first Grammy nomination, when ''Being Human in Public'' was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album|Best Urban Contemporary Album]] at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|2020 Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ctvnews.ca/entertainment/jessie-reyez-grabs-first-grammy-nod-alongside-fellow-canadians-shawn-mendes-drake-1.4693872|title=Jessie Reyez grabs first Grammy nod alongside fellow Canadians Shawn Mendes, Drake {{!}} Entertainment & Showbiz from CTV News|website=www.ctvnews.ca|access-date=2019-11-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/alessia-cara-and-tory-lanez-lead-the-2020-juno-nominations-1.5436386|title=Alessia Cara and Tory Lanez lead the 2020 Juno nominations|website=[[CBC Music]]}}</ref> Reyez opened for [[Billie Eilish]] on her [[Where Do We Go? World Tour|Where Do We Go? world tour]], with some of the dates postponed due to [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variancemagazine.com/sounds/11034-jessie-reyez-before-love-came-to-kill-us|title=Jessie Reyez shares towering debut album 'Before Love Came to Kill Us'|last=Robles|first=Jonathan|website=Variance Magazine|language=en-gb|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref> She was also booked to perform for the first time at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] festival,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/live/8551230/jessie-reyes-before-love-came-to-kill-us-tour-dates|title=Jessie Reyez Reveals Before Love Came to Kill Us Tour|date=2020-02-18|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref> which was also postponed due to the pandemic.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/festivals/9356038/watch-festivals-online-coachella|title=Seven Festivals & Concerts to Stream This Weekend: Coachella, Farm Aid & More|date=2020-04-10|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref>

Reyez released her debut studio album, ''[[Before Love Came to Kill Us]]'', on March 27, 2020. The album includes collaborations with [[Eminem]] and [[6lack]], with the deluxe edition including additional features from [[Rico Nasty]], Melii, [[JID]], and [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]]. The album was preceded by the single "Love in the Dark" and also included previous singles "Figures" and "Imported". ''Before Love Came to Kill Us'' debuted at #13 on the [[Billboard 200]] and was short-listed for the [[2020 Polaris Music Prize]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/jessie-reyez|title=Jessie Reyez|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Here's the Polaris Music Prize 2020 Short List|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/heres_the_polaris_music_prize_2020_short_list|access-date=2020-07-15|website=exclaim.ca|language=en-ca}}</ref>

Reyez was nominated for two awards at the [[Juno Awards of 2020|2020 Juno Awards]]: [[Juno Award for Artist of the Year|Artist of the Year]] and [[Juno Award for R&B/Soul Recording of the Year|R&B/Soul Recording of the Year]] (for "Feel It Too" with [[Tory Lanez]] and [[Tainy]]), winning the latter award.<ref>{{Cite web|title=PUP, Half Moon Run, Jessie Reyez, more among 2020 Juno Award winners {{!}} Indie88|url=https://indie88.com/pup-half-moon-run-jessie-reyez-more-among-2020-juno-award-winners/|access-date=2020-07-01|language=en-US}}</ref> The music video for Reyez's song "Far Away" won the 2020 [[Prism Prize]], with director [[Peter Huang (director)|Peter Huang]] winning the $20,000 award.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Trapunski|first=Richard|date=2020-07-24|title=Prism Prize 2020: Jessie Reyez wins best music video|url=https://nowtoronto.com/music/jessie-reyez-wins-2020-prism-prize|access-date=2020-08-05|website=NOW Magazine}}</ref> Reyez was featured in [[Beyoncé]]'s musical film ''[[Black Is King]]'', which premiered on [[Disney+]] in July 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/beyonce-black-is-king-disney-1036650/|title=5 Takeaways From Beyonce's Elaborate New Visual Album 'Black Is King'|last=Shaffer|first=Claire|work=Rolling Stone|date=July 31, 2020|access-date=July 31, 2020}}</ref>

Reyez is nominated for five awards at the [[2021 Juno Awards]]: [[Juno Award for Artist of the Year|Artist of the Year]], [[Juno Award for Songwriter of the Year|Songwriter of the Year]], and [[Juno Award for R&B/Soul Recording of the Year|R&B/Soul Recording of the Year]] (for ''Before Love Came to Kill Us''), with two of her music videos nominated [[Juno Award for Video of the Year|Video of the Year]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Weaver|first=Jackson|title=Junos 2021: The Weeknd gets 6 nominations; JP Saxe, Justin Bieber, Jessie Reyez score 5 each|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/junos-2021-nominations-1.5941815|url-status=live|website=[[CBC News]]}}</ref> She performed at the ceremony and won Video of the Year for "No One's in the Room", which was directed by Emma Higgins.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Junos announce full performer lineup — including JP Saxe, Ali Gatie, Tate McRae and Jessie Reyez|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/junos-performers-1.6041938|url-status=live|website=[[CBC News]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Juno Awards: Congratulations to B.C.'s winners|url=https://vancouversun.com/entertainment/juno-awards-congratulations-to-b-c-s-winners|access-date=2021-06-08|website=vancouversun|language=en-CA}}</ref>
