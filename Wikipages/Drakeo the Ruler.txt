'''Darrell Caldwell''' (born December 1, 1993), known professionally as '''Drakeo the Ruler''', is an American rapper and songwriter based in Los Angeles, California. He is known for his unique [[Rap#Flow|flow]], as well as his "oddly expressive, poetic word-choices",<ref name="stereo">{{cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1977873/drakeo-the-ruler-is-different-and-different-is-good/franchises/status-aint-hood/|title=Drakeo The Ruler Is Different, And Different Is Good|first=Tom|last=Breihan|date=10 January 2018|publisher=[[Stereogum]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref> leading the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' to call him "the most original West Coast stylist in decades."<ref name="LAT">{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-drakeo-the-ruler-20180309-story.html|title=L.A. rapper Drakeo the Ruler is a man in demand|first=Jeff|last=Weiss|date=9 March 2018|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|access-date=September 3, 2021}}</ref>

His fourth mixtape, ''Cold Devil'', has been streamed over 10 million times.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/05/drakeo-the-ruler-interview-the-break/|title=THE BREAK PRESENTS: DRAKEO THE RULER|first=Peter A.|last=Berry|date=18 May 2018|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Caldwell was raised in [[South Los Angeles]] by a single mother. He attended [[Washington Preparatory High School|Washington High School]] in nearby [[Westmont, California|Westmont]].<ref name=LAT/> Also, his blood brother is other local Los Angeles rapper, Ralfy the Plug.

==Career==
After recording a handful of mixtapes with much more of a [[trap music|trap]] influence than the [[West Coast hip hop]] he became known for, he was discovered by [[DJ Mustard]], who remixed his song "Mr. Get Dough". The song, which premiered on [[WorldStarHipHop|WorldStar]] in April 2015, became his breakout song,<ref name=LAT/> and has amassed over 6.2 million [[YouTube]] views (as of August 2019). Six months later, in October, Drakeo release his official debut mixtape, ''I Am Mr. Mosely'', as his first project under Mustard's 10 Summers label.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/drakeo-the-ruler-i-am-mr-mosley-new-mixtape.116078.html|title=I Am Mr. Mosley|first=Rose|last=Lilah|date=16 October 2015|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref>

He released his second project, ''I Am Mr. Mosely 2'', on 21 July 2016, with features including [[Mozzy]], [[Skeme]], and [[Philthy Rich]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/07/22/drakeo-the-ruler-i-am-mr-mosley-2-mixtape|title=Listen To Drakeo The Ruler's I Am Mr. Mosely 2 Mixtape|first=Ben|last=Dandridge-Lemco|date=22 July 2016|work=[[The Fader]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref> He followed this up with the release of ''So Cold I Do Em'' in December, which included one of his most popular songs, "Impatient Freestyle".

After his jail release in November 2017, he recorded a 16-track mixtape, ''Cold Devil'', in 10 days, and released it the following month.<ref name=stereo/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/12/22/drakeo-the-ruler-cold-devil-album-premiere|title=Drakeo The Ruler returns with his Cold Devil album|first=Ben|last=Dandridge-Lemco|date=22 December 2017|work=[[The Fader]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref> Paul A. Thompson of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' described it as "the most compelling album of the Los Angeles emcee's career", adding that his "avant deadpan and impressionistic relationship to the beat" is "icy and unforgettable".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/drakeo-the-ruler-cold-devil/|title=Cold Devil review|first=Paul A.|last=Thompson|date=5 January 2018|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref> Similarly, Grant Rindner of ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' called it "one of the most impressive California rap projects in years".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2018/06/drakeo-the-ruler-interview|title=Time to Take Over the World: An Interview With Incarcerated Rapper Drakeo the Ruler|first=Grant|last=Rindner|date=14 June 2018|department=Pigeons and Planes|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref> He released music videos for "Flu Flamming", "Big Banc Uchies" and "Out the Slums" within a month, amassing 4.4 million, 3.6 million and 4.0 million [[YouTube]] views, respectively. "Flu Flamming" was also notably remixed by [[Lil Yachty]], and "Big Banc Uchies" by [[Shy Glizzy]]. The music video for "Roll Bounce" was released in September, with Drakeo in jail awaiting trial.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/09/11/drakeo-the-ruler-roll-bounce-video-premiere|title=While he awaits trial, Drakeo The Ruler shares a new video for "Roll Bounce"|first=Ben|last=Dandridge-Lemco|date=11 September 2018|work=[[The Fader]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref>

He released the project, ''[[Thank You for Using GTL]]'', in June 2020, while incarcerated.<ref name="PF" />
His ninth mixtape, ''The Truth Hurts'', was released on February 24, 2021. The mixtape contains notable guest appearances from [[Don Toliver]], and [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] on the song "Talk to Me".<ref name="Truth">{{cite tweet|number=1363287391943806977|title=THE TRUTH HURTS OFFICIAL TRACKLIST STAY MAD FEB 23 2021 ...|user=IamMrMosely|date=February 20, 2021|accessdate=February 22, 2021}}</ref>

==Legal issues==
In January 2017, Caldwell was arrested by the [[Los Angeles Police Department|LAPD]] when they raided a condo where he regularly shot music videos. He was subsequently held at [[Men's Central Jail]] after being charged with unlawful possession of a firearm by a felon.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.laweekly.com/music/drakeo-could-be-las-gucci-mane-and-not-just-because-hes-currently-doing-time-7931454|title=Drakeo Could Be L.A.'s Gucci Mane â€” and Not Just Because He's Currently Doing Time|first=Jeff|last=Weiss|date=15 February 2017|work=[[LA Weekly]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref> He was released in November.<ref name=stereo/>

In March 2018, he was arrested again, this time charged with first-degree murder, attempted murder and conspiracy to commit murder. The charges stemmed from a December 2016 shooting in [[Carson, California]] where one person was killed and two were injured.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/04/drakeo-the-ruler-proclaims-innocence-murder-charges/|title=DRAKEO THE RULER PROCLAIMS HIS INNOCENCE AS HE SITS IN JAIL FACING MURDER CHARGES|first=C. Vernon|last=Coleman II|date=8 April 2018|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL Mag]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref> He was facing life in prison.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/42056/1/drakeo-the-ruler-rapper-cold-devil-interview|title=An interview with LA rapper Drakeo the Ruler from behind bars|first=Charlie Robin|last=Jones|date=5 November 2018|work=[[Dazed]]|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref> Simultaneously, members of his Stinc Team collective, including his brother Ralfy the Plug, were arrested in [[San Francisco]] on a variety of charges.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.passionweiss.com/2018/04/06/a-jail-house-interview-with-drakeo-the-ruler/|title=A Jail House Interview With Drakeo the Ruler|first=Jeff|last=Weiss|date=6 April 2018|work=Passion of the Weiss|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref>

On July 25, 2019, he was acquitted of his murder and attempted murder charges in a [[Compton, California|Compton]] courthouse.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/07/25/drakeo-the-ruler-murder-verdict-news-july-2019|title=Drakeo The Ruler acquitted of murder and attempted murder charges|first1=Jeff|last1=Weiss|first2=Alex Robert|last2=Ross|work=[[The Fader]]|date=25 July 2019|access-date=8 August 2019}}</ref> However, the district attorney decided to refile charges of criminal gang conspiracy and shooting from a motor vehicle in August, two counts that resulted in a hung jury during his initial trial.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/09/03/drakeo-the-ruler-charges-refiled|title=Drakeo The Ruler faces possibility of life in prison as DA refiles charges|work=[[The Fader]]|first1=Jeff|last1=Weiss|first2=Alex Robert|last2=Ross|date=3 September 2019|access-date=5 March 2020}}</ref> His trial date was set for August 3, 2020. While incarcerated, he recorded ''[[Thank You for Using GTL]]'', which ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' called "likely the greatest rap album ever recorded from jail".<ref name="PF">{{cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/drakeo-the-ruler-thank-you-for-using-gtl/ |title= Album review: Drakeo the Ruler: Thank You for Using GTL |first= Matthew |last= Ismael Ruiz |work= [[Pitchfork Media]] |date= June 10, 2020 |access-date= June 19, 2020 }}</ref> He was released from jail in November 2020 after the district attorney's office offered him a plea deal following three years in jail.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/11/05/931836700/drakeo-the-ruler-released-from-jail-after-accepting-plea-deal|title=Drakeo The Ruler Released From Jail After Accepting Plea Deal|work=[[NPR]]|last=Limbong|first=Andrew|date=November 5, 2020|accessdate=February 22, 2021}}</ref>
