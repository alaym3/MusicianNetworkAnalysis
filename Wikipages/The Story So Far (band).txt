'''The Story So Far''' is an American [[pop punk]] band from [[Walnut Creek, California]], formed in 2007. They are currently signed to [[Pure Noise Records]] and have released 4 studio albums.

==History==
[[File:The Story So Far Logo.png|thumb|The Story So Far's logo from ''[[Under Soil and Dirt]]'' (2011).]]
===Formation and early releases (2007–2010)===
The Story So Far formed in Walnut Creek, California in 2007.<ref name=AMbio>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-story-so-far-mn0002342677/biography|title=The Story So Far - Biography|author=Thomas, Fred |work=AllMusic|access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> Their name is taken from the [[New Found Glory]] song "The Story So Far".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/features/entry/11_bands_named_after_songs|title=11 Bands Named After Songs|work=Alternative Press|author=Major, Nick|date=February 28, 2014|access-date=July 26, 2016}}</ref> The band consisted of Parker Cannon on vocals, Kevin Geyer on lead guitar, Kevin Ambrose on rhythm guitar, Ryan Torf on drums, and Kelen Capener on bass.<ref name=AMbio/> On December 22, the band released the ''5 Songs'' EP.<ref name=5Songs>{{cite web|url=https://thestorysofarca.bandcamp.com/album/5-songs|title=5 Songs {{!}} The Story So Far|work=thestorysofarca.bandcamp.com|access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> Ambrose parted ways with the band<ref name=AMbio/> when he went to college in 2010 and was replaced by William Levy.<ref name=punknewsinterview>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/article/46424/interviews-the-story-so-far|title=Interviews: The Story So Far|work=punknews.org|access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> In March 2010 it was announced the band had signed to [[Pure Noise Records|Pure Noise]].<ref name=AP.netPNsigning>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=1614682|title=Pure Noise Tells The Story So Far|work=AbsolutePunk.net|author=DiVincenzo, Alex |date=March 29, 2010|access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> Two months later the band released an EP, ''While You Were Sleeping'' (2010).<ref name=AP.netPNsigning/> In November, the band released a split with Maker.<ref name=TSSF-Maker>{{cite web|url=https://thestorysofarca.bandcamp.com/album/the-story-so-far-maker-7-split|title=The Story So Far/Maker 7'' Split {{!}} The Story So Far|work=thestorysofarca.bandcamp.com|access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref>

===''Under Soil and Dirt'', ''What You Don't See'' and ''Songs Of'' EP (2011–2014)===
{{Main|Under Soil and Dirt|What You Don't See}}
Barrett Records released a split of The Story So Far and Morgan Foster in May 2011.<ref name=TSSF-MF>{{cite web|url=http://music.barrettrecords.net/album/the-story-so-far-morgan-foster-split|title=The Story So Far / Morgan Foster - Split {{!}} Barrett Records|work=music.barrettrecords.net|access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> Pure Noise released the band's debut album ''[[Under Soil and Dirt]]'' in June.<ref name=altpressUSAD>{{cite web|last=Pearlman |first=Mischa |url=http://www.altpress.com/reviews/entry/the_story_so_far_-_under_soil_and_dirt/ |title=The Story So Far – Under Soil And Dirt – Alternative Press |publisher=Altpress.com |date=June 16, 2011 |access-date=February 16, 2014}}</ref> On March 26, 2013, they released their second studio album, ''[[What You Don't See]]'',<ref name=altpressWYSD>{{cite web|last=Moseley |first=Brittany |url=http://www.altpress.com/reviews/entry/the_story_so_far_what_you_dont_see/ |title=The Story So Far – What You Don't See – Alternative Press |publisher=Altpress.com |date=March 26, 2013 |access-date=February 16, 2014}}</ref> which debuted at number 46 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart in the U.S.<ref name=Bill>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/1561918/the-story-so-far/chart?f=305 |title=The Story So Far - Chart history (Billboard 200) |work=Billboard |access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> They have also released a split with [[Stick to Your Guns (band)|Stick to Your Guns]] on June 18.<ref name=TSSF-STYG>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3145951|title=The Story So Far / Stick To Your Guns Releasing Split|work=AbsolutePunk.net|author=Nassiff, Thomas |date=April 25, 2013|access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> This split contains one new original song, Clairvoyant, and a cover of [[Pinback]]'s song "Loro". The band was featured on the cover of [[Alternative Press (music magazine)|Alternative Press]]' March 2013 issue "100 Bands You Need to Know."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/magazine/issue/297_100_bands_you_need_to_know |title=100 Bands You Need to Know |publisher=Alternative Press |access-date= March 9, 2013}}</ref> The band played the entire [[Warped Tour 2014]] on the main stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://officialwarped.tumblr.com/post/76497415632/can-now-say-get-pumped-for-warpedtour-2014-with |title=Vans Warped Tour • Can now say, get pumped for #warpedtour 2014 with... |publisher=Warped Tour |date= February 12, 2014 |access-date=February 14, 2014}}</ref>

On April 24, 2014, The Story So Far announced they would be releasing ''Songs Of'', an acoustic EP on June 17 through Pure Noise.<ref name=SongsOf>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3697885|title=The Story So Far Announce Acoustic EP, 'Songs Of,' Out June 17|work=AbsolutePunk.net|author=Nassiff, Thomas |date=April 24, 2014|access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> The record contains some acoustic versions of previous songs, as well as one new original song ("Navy Blue") and a [[Bob Marley]] cover.

===''The Story So Far'' (2015–2016)===
{{Main|The Story So Far (The Story So Far album)}}
On January 5, 2015, a video titled "The Story So Far Album Teaser #1" was uploaded on the band's official YouTube channel. The video shows multiple clips of the band members with friends goofing around and having fun. At the end of the video a text roll appears, saying "We are writing a new record stay tuned..."<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pSC3p5VnzB8 The Story So Far New Album Teaser #1] The Story So Far CA. January 5, 2015</ref> On February 23, 2015 a video titled "The Story So Far Album teaser #2" was uploaded on the same channel, showing behind the scenes-footage of the making of the album.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9TuBd8NqDk0 The Story So Far New Album Teaser #2]  The Story So Far CA. February 23, 2015</ref> On March 15, the band announced their self-titled album would be released on May 18, 2015. "Solo" and "Heavy Gloom" were uploaded to YouTube soon after. On May 11, the band released their full album to stream on their website ahead of the release date of May 19.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kerrang.com/31260/story-far-stream-self-titled-album-full/|title=The Story So Far Streams Self-Titled Album in Full|date=May 12, 2015|website=Kerrang!|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518233344/http://www.kerrang.com/31260/story-far-stream-self-titled-album-full/|archive-date=May 18, 2015|url-status=dead|access-date=March 21, 2018}}</ref>

===''Proper Dose'' (2017–present) ===
The band reportedly entered the studio to record their fourth studio album in April 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/the_story_so_far_begin_recording_new_album|title=The Story So Far begin recording new album|publisher=Alternative Press|access-date=August 20, 2017}}</ref> On September 13, 2017, the band released the song "Out of It". The track is included on a charity 7" vinyl, which was released on November 3.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/the_story_so_far_out_of_it_tour_dates|title=The Story So Far debut new song, "Out Of It," announce fall tour with Turnstile, Drug Church|publisher=Alternative Press|access-date=September 13, 2017}}</ref>

The album, titled ''[[Proper Dose]]'', was officially announced on July 12, 2018, and was released on September 21, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/the-story-so-far-proper-dose-details/|title=The Story So Far reveal details of new album 'Proper Dose'|date=2018-07-12|work=Alternative Press|access-date=2018-07-12|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/thestorysofarca/status/1017438937608929280|title=The Story So Far on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=2018-07-12|language=en}}</ref> ''Proper Dose'' debuted at number 19 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, but quickly declined, falling off the chart the following week.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-story-so-far|title=The Story So Far Proper Dose Chart History|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref> In efforts to promote the album, the band embarked on a European tour with supporting acts from [[Citizen (band)|Citizen]] and All Get Out.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/the-story-so-far-have-confirmed-a-uk-and-european-tour/|title=The Story So Far Have Confirmed A UK And European Tour|website=Kerrang!|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref> At the conclusion of their European tour, the band immediately started a U.S. tour with [[Movements (band)|Movements]], Citizen, and [[Turnover (band)|Turnover]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.axs.com/the-story-so-far-announce-2018-fall-u-s-tour-132733|title=The Story So Far announce 2018 fall U.S. tour|work=AXS|access-date=2019-03-21|language=en-US}}</ref> On September 21, 2018 the band released their first music video from ''Proper Dose'' for their song "Upside Down",<ref>{{Citation|last=Pure Noise Records|title=The Story So Far "Upside Down" Official Music Video|date=2018-09-21|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jgc8a7ikd2w|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref> followed by music videos for "Take Me As You Please" and "Proper Dose" on November 8, 2018 and January 22, 2019, respectively.<ref>{{Citation|last=Pure Noise Records|title=The Story So Far "Take Me As You Please" Official Music Video|date=2018-11-08|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8AnjMkLgxKo|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=Pure Noise Records|title=The Story So Far "Proper Dose" Official Music Video|date=2019-01-22|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3c1nuOBSr_Q|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref>

==Style==
''[[Under Soil and Dirt]]'' (2011),<ref>{{cite web|author=Nassiff, Thomas |url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2334372 |title=The Story So Far - Under Soil And Dirt - Album Review |work=absolutepunk.net |access-date=October 24, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2011/07/album-revew-story-so-far-under-soil-and.html |title=Album Review: The Story So Far|work=alterthepress.com|date=July 28, 2011 |access-date=October 24, 2015}}</ref> ''[[What You Don't See]]'' (2013),<ref>{{cite web|last=Solomon |first=Blake |url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3086171 |title=Story So Far, The - What You Don't See - Album Review |work=AbsolutePunk.net |date=March 18, 2013 |access-date=October 24, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Moseley |first=Brittany |url=http://www.altpress.com/reviews/entry/the_story_so_far_what_you_dont_see/ |title=The Story So Far - What You Don't See |work=Alternative Press |date=March 26, 2013 |access-date=October 24, 2015}}</ref> and ''[[The Story So Far (The Story So Far album)|The Story So Far]]'' (2015)<ref>{{cite web|author=Ham, Robert |url=http://diymag.com/2015/05/16/the-story-so-far-the-story-so-far-album-review|title=The Story So Far - The Story So Far album review|work=DIY|access-date=October 24, 2015}}</ref><ref name=Idobi>{{cite web|url=http://idobi.com/review/album-review-the-story-so-far-st/|title=Album Review: The Story So Far – S-T|work=idobi.com|author=Hines, Hannah|date=May 19, 2015|access-date=October 24, 2015}}</ref> have all been described as [[pop punk]]. With their fourth album, ''[[Proper Dose]]'', marking a departure from pop punk and saw the band incorporating indie rock into their sound. The Story So Far has also been described as having an "edgier" take on pop punk, due to their frequent incorporation of [[hardcore punk]] and [[punk rock]] elements in their music.<ref>{{cite web|author=renaldo69 |url=https://www.punknews.org/review/11912/the-story-so-far-what-you-dont-see |title=The Story So Far - What You Don't See |work=punknews.org |access-date=March 18, 2013}}</ref>
