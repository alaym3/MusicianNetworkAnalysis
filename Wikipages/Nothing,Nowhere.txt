'''Joseph Edward Mulherin''' (born June 4, 1992), better known by his stage name '''Nothing,Nowhere''' (stylized as '''nothing,nowhere.'''), is an American singer, rapper, and songwriter.<ref name="jcaramanica">[[Jon Caramanica]], [https://www.nytimes.com/2017/10/20/arts/music/nothing-nowhere-hip-hop-emo-reaper.html?_r=0 "nothing,nowhere. Blends Hip-Hop and Emo to Make Tomorrow's Pop,"] ''[[New York Times]]'', October 20, 2017.</ref>

While the music composed by Mulherin is entirely written and produced by him, he maintains a group of musicians which consist of associates that perform with him for live shows also under the ''nothing,nowhere.'' name. The band has toured as a supporting act for [[Grandson (musician)|Grandson]], [[Real Friends (band)|Real Friends]], [[Tiny Moving Parts]], [[Thrice]] and [[La Dispute (band)|La Dispute]] on multiple tours.

== Early life ==
[[File:NothingNowhere.png|thumb|left|190px|Mulherin in 2018]]
Mulherin was raised in [[Foxborough, Massachusetts]], and spent summers in [[Hyde Park (town), Vermont|Hyde Park, Vermont]].<ref name="jcaramanica" /> He attended [[Foxborough Public Schools#Schools|Igo Elementary School and Ahern Middle School]].<ref name="sun">{{cite web|url=http://www.thesunchronicle.com/news/local_news/on-the-straight-and-narrow/article_62526239-3aae-5c73-b880-47d524f83883.html|title=On the straight and narrow|last=Mortimer|first=Frank|website=[[The Sun Chronicle]]|location=Foxboro, United States|archive-url=https://archive.today/20171223235645/http://www.thesunchronicle.com/news/local_news/on-the-straight-and-narrow/article_62526239-3aae-5c73-b880-47d524f83883.html|archive-date=December 23, 2017|url-status=live|access-date=December 29, 2017|publication-date=May 30, 2013}}</ref> Afterwards, Mulherin spent his freshman year of high school at [[Xaverian Brothers High School]], before transferring to Foxborough High School his sophomore year, where he studied computer-based design.<ref name="boston">{{cite web|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/metro/regionals/south/2013/06/26/from-home-movies-cannes-foxborough-film-maker-makes-big-time/6df6TSeUGZFopHomcWDmiK/story.html|title=From home movies to Cannes, film-maker makes it to the big time|last=Bolton|first=Michele Morgan|website=[[The Boston Globe]]|archive-url=https://archive.today/20171229143150/https://www.bostonglobe.com/metro/regionals/south/2013/06/26/from-home-movies-cannes-foxborough-film-maker-makes-big-time/6df6TSeUGZFopHomcWDmiK/story.html|archive-date=December 29, 2017|url-status=dead|access-date=December 29, 2017|publication-date=June 27, 2013}}</ref>

==History==
In 2015, Mulherin began uploading songs on [[SoundCloud]] under the name nothing,nowhere.<ref name="jcaramanica"/> In June of that year, he released his debut album titled ''the nothing,nowhere. LP'' onto [[Bandcamp]]. After releasing two EPs, ''Bummer'', and ''Who Are You'', featuring Austrian producer Oilcolor,<ref name="cpayne"/> on October 20, 2017, he released his commercial debut, ''Reaper'', an album of guitar-based emo rap that the ''[[New York Times]]'' called "one of the most promising pop albums of the year."<ref name=jcaramanica/> He produced the album with Erik Ron and Jay Vee.<ref name=jcaramanica/> The track "Hopes Up" features vocals from [[Chris Carrabba]] of [[Dashboard Confessional]],<ref name="cpayne">Chris Payne, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7889878/nothing-nowhere-hopes-up-video-dashboard-confessional-chris-carrabba "Emo-Meets-Hip-Hop Project nothing,nowhere. Premieres 'Hopes Up' Video With Dashboard Confessional's Chris Carrabba,"] ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', August 7, 2017.</ref><ref>Mosi Reeves, [https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/review-nothingnowheres-reaper-w510654 "Review: Nothing,Nowhere.'s Tormented Emo-Rap Shows Hip-Hop's Post-Modern Evolution,"] ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', October 31, 2017.</ref> and the track "REM" features Delaware rapper Lil West.<ref>Mitch Findlay, [https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/nothing-nowhere-and-lil-west-collide-for-some-emo-rap-new-song.1976034.html "nothing, nowhere & Lil West Collide For Some Emo Rap,"] HotNewHipHop, October 23, 2017.</ref><ref name="jbalfour">Jay Balfour, [https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/nothing-nowhere-rem-ft-lil-west/ "Tracks,"] ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'', October 23, 2017.</ref> ''New York Times'' music critic [[Jon Caramanica]] listed ''Reaper'' as his #1 album of 2017.<ref>Jon Caramanica, [https://www.nytimes.com/2017/12/06/arts/music/best-albums-2017.html?_r=0 "The Best Albums of 2017,"] ''New York Times'', December 6, 2017.</ref>

Mulherin has had several different aliases outside of "nothing,nowhere.", notably: never,forever, TRAU CHOI, & Lil Tofu. never,forever was a little side project Mulherin released on [[SoundCloud]]. TRAU CHOI was a band between nothing,nowhere. and other associated artists, only releasing a demo song in 2014 and a self-titled ep of 5 tracks on [[Bandcamp]] in 2016. Lil Tofu was a parody of stereotypical Soundcloud rapper and emo culture.

On February 16, 2018, it was confirmed that Mulherin had signed to [[Fueled by Ramen]] as he released a new single titled "Ruiner", the first of several that would appear on his upcoming album of the same name, which was released on April 13, 2018.<ref name="staylor">Sam Taylor, [https://www.upsetmagazine.com/news/nothing-nowhere-has-released-his-new-album-ruiner-check-it-out-now/ "Nothing, Nowhere Has Released His New Album 'Ruiner',"] ''Upset'', April 13, 2018.</ref> In March 2018, Mulherin cancelled his tour due to chronic laryngitis and a hemorrhaged vocal fold, this included his first European show in London.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bringthenoise.com/blog/nothingnowhere-cancels-remaining-headlining|title=nothing,nowhere. Cancels Remaining Headlining Dates Due To Vocal Cord Injury / the NOISE|work=the NOISE|access-date=2018-11-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/nothingnowhere.-has-cancelled-the-remainder-of-his-current-tour|title=nothing,nowhere. Has Cancelled The Remainder Of His Current Tour - News - Rock Sound Magazine|work=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=2018-11-03|language=en-GB}}</ref>

From October 19 to November 9, 2018, Mulherin was on tour in Europe.<ref name="staylor"/>

On January 28, 2020, Mulherin released a new single titled "Nightmare",<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "nightmare - out now" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1222173929646829568?s=20 |access-date=28 January 2020}}</ref> subsequently announcing dates for a world tour.<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "uk/eu: tickets are officially on sale cop one and come to a show excited to see you again" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1223205153442009088?s=20 |access-date=31 January 2020}}</ref> However, these dates were soon cancelled in March of the same year due to complications involving the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "i will be back stay healthy🔨" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1240370161514942465?s=20 |access-date=18 March 2020}}</ref> On April 18, 2020, Mulherin released a single titled "Death".<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "DEATH" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1251360969269358592?s=20 |access-date=18 April 2020}}</ref> On July 10th, 2020, Mulherin released an album titled "One Takes: Vol. 1".<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "one takes: vol 1. out now" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1281666328756133898?s=20 |access-date=10 July 2020}}</ref> The album features versions of songs released prior that were all recorded in one take. Music publication [[The Line of Best Fit]] rated it a 7.5/10, calling the album an exposé of "the raw nature of human hurt and understanding".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Loftin |first1=Steven |title=Burgeoning emo rapper nothing.nowhere exposes the rawness of life |url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/reviews/albums/nothing-nowhere-one-takes-album-review |access-date=10 July 2020 |work=The Line of Best Fit}}</ref> On July 24, 2020, Mulherin released a single titled "Lights (4444)".<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "lights (4444) out now" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1286519896764043264?s=20 |access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref> On September 18, 2020, Mulherin released a single titled "Pretend".<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "PRETEND // OUT NOW" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1306809237323087874?s=20 |access-date=18 September 2020}}</ref> On October 5, 2020, [[Neck Deep]] announced new dates for their ''All Distortions Are Intentional US Tour'', with Mulherin as a supporting act.<ref>{{cite web |title=Neck Deep on Twitter: "Unfortunately, due to the ongoing COVID19 pandemic, we have had to postpone our US headline tour until 2021. We've tried to preserve as many of the dates as poss &amp; are super buzzed to confirm that @nothingnowhere &amp; @HeartAttackMane will join us on ALL SHOWS" |url=https://twitter.com/NeckDeepUK/status/1313160117567844353?s=20 |access-date=5 October 2020}}</ref> On October 27, 2020, Mulherin released a single titled "Blood" featuring indie rock singer [[KennyHoopla]] and producer Judge.<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "🩸BLOOD🩸 (FT. @KENNYHOOPLA &amp; @JUDGEBEATS)" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1320938350702927875?s=20 |access-date=27 October 2020}}</ref> On December 7, 2020, Mulherin announced his upcoming fourth album, ''Trauma Factory'', which would be released on February 19, 2021.<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "TRAUMA FACTORY FEBRUARY 19" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1335967858250166273?s=20 |access-date=7 December 2020}}</ref> He subsequently released a new single for the album, titled "Fake Friend".<ref>{{cite web |title=NOTHING,NOWHERE. on Twitter: "FAKE FRIEND OUT NOW" |url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1335968404579233792?s=20 |access-date=7 December 2020}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Mulherin has almost never used alcohol, cigarettes or other recreational drugs, and in his freshman year of college,<ref>{{cite AV media |date=14 July 2020 |title=Ep 10: nothing,nowhere. on REVERB's Quarantine Kitchen|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YR0xCDmTuQo&ab_channel=REVERB |access-date=20 February 2021 |time=4:41 |quote="I've been vegan about 9 years"}}</ref> he became [[Straight edge#Veganism|vegan straight edge]].<ref name="jcaramanica"/><ref name=sun/>

In August 2018, Mulherin cancelled a string of shows, including an appearance at [[Reading and Leeds Festivals|Reading & Leeds Festival]] due to severe anxiety.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/nothingnowhere-cancels-this-weekends-appearance-at-reading-leeds/|title=nothing,nowhere. cancels this weekend's appearance at Reading & Leeds|date=2018-08-23|work=Alternative Press|access-date=2018-11-03|language=en-US}}</ref> He had previously canceled shows due to depression and anxiety in July 2018,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/nothingnowhere/status/1024051650036727808?lang=en|title=nothing,nowhere. on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=2018-11-03|language=en}}</ref> further admitting he was pursuing treatment.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/nothingnowhere.-has-dropped-a-personal-new-track1|title=nothing,nowhere. Has Dropped A New Track - News - Rock Sound Magazine|work=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=2018-11-03|language=en-GB}}</ref> In an interview with The Fader in November 2018, Mulherin disclosed that he had felt anxiety and panic attacks regularly as a child and the effects it had on his life led to depression.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/10/30/nothing-nowhere-reaper-ruiner-interview|title=How mindfulness practice helped experimental rapper nothing,nowhere|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-11-03|language=en}}</ref>

Before creating music, Mulherin had an interest in [[filmmaking]]. He attended film school at [[Burlington College]] in Vermont and while in college, Mulherin co-created the short film ''Watcher'' which won a prize at the Vermont International Film Festival.<ref name=boston/> In 2013, he participated in a contest by the organization Creative Mind Group at the [[66th Cannes Film Festival]] in [[France]], shooting, directing and co-editing the film ''One Day'' which received three awards.<ref name=sun/>
