'''Willem Bijkerk''' ({{IPA-nl|ˈʋɪləm ˈbɛikɛr(ə)k}}; born 20 April 1980), known professionally as '''Waylon''', is a Dutch singer. His stage name came from the name of his idol [[Waylon Jennings]]. He represented the Netherlands with [[Ilse DeLange]] as [[The Common Linnets]] at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2014]], finishing in second place in the final.<ref name=euro2014>{{cite web|title=Eurovision 2014 Results: Voting & Points|url=http://eurovisionworld.com/?eurovision=2014|website=Eurovision World|access-date=26 January 2017}}</ref> He performed in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2018]] and finished in 18th place.

==Career==
Waylon started his career in 1995 singing in the television program ''[[Telekids]]'' where he sang the chorus of the song "Harry Stallion."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Roggeveen|first1=Herman|title=Waylon was Harrie de Hengst uit Telekids|url=http://nlpop.blog.nl/pop/2009/10/19/waylon-was-harrie-de-hengst-uit-telekids|website=NLpop|access-date=26 January 2017|language=nl|date=19 October 2009}}</ref> Since childhood he was inspired by country music and he played the drums with the band West Virginian Railroad.<ref name="npo">{{cite web|title=Waylon Biografie|url=http://www.nposoulenjazz.nl/artiest/432/waylon/biografie|website=NPO Radio 2 Soul & Jazz|access-date=26 January 2017|language=nl}}</ref> He was signed in 1997 by [[EMI Records|EMI]] and went to [[Nashville]] to record an album. The album was never completed.

In 2001, he lived and worked for a year in the United States where he also performed with his idol, [[Waylon Jennings]]. After Jennings died in 2002, Waylon returned to the Netherlands.  Waylon's talent was further developed with Lisa Boray. After returning from the United States, he joined the [[cover band]] Santa Rosa. He also sang in the band Millstreet.<ref name="npo" /> In 2005, Waylon and [[Rachel Kramer]] performed as the duo Rachel & Waylon in the preliminary rounds of the National Song Contest with the song "Leven als een beest."

===Breakthrough===
Waylon took part in the first season of ''[[Holland's Got Talent]]'' on [[SBS 6]] in 2008. He auditioned with the song "[[It's a Man's Man's Man's World]]" by [[James Brown]]. He received positive feedback from jurors [[Henkjan Smits]] and [[Patricia Paay]].<ref name=youtube>{{cite web|title=Waylon - It's A Man's World (James Brown)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Zd_39SBasa4|publisher=YouTube|access-date=2 August 2014}}</ref> Waylon reached the final and finished second.

A year later, [[Universal Music]] signed Waylon as the first Dutchman to the [[Motown Records|Motown label]].<ref name=nu>{{cite web|title=Nederlandse zanger tekent bij Motown|url=http://www.nu.nl/muzieknieuws/2047164/nederlandse-zanger-tekent-bij-motown.html|website=NU.nl|access-date=2 August 2014|language=nl|date=23 July 2009}}</ref> His first single, "Wicked Way", was released on 7 August 2009 as a preview of the album ''Wicked Ways'', released later that month.<ref name=nu /> "Wicked Way" reached the top 10 in the Dutch Top 40 charts.

In April 2010, Waylon won his first major music award, a [[3FM]] Award for "Best Newcomer". A month later he received in a gold record for his debut album on the television program ''De Wereld Draait Door''. In June 2010, Waylon was one of the winners of the [[TMF Awards]], winning the Borsato Award for new talent. The prize was awarded to him by the namesake, [[Marco Borsato]], at the TMF Awards Festival in Enschede.

Waylon released "The Escapist" in October 2011 as the first single from his second album, ''After All'', which was released on 3 November 2011. This was followed by the singles "Lose It" and "Lucky Night". Waylon was voted best singer in the April 2012 3FM Awards.

===Eurovision Song Contest 2014===
Together with singer [[Ilse Delange]], Waylon was the Dutch entry to the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2014]] in [[Copenhagen]]. The two formed the duo [[The Common Linnets]] and sang the [[Country music|country]]/[[Bluegrass music|bluegrass]] song "[[Calm After the Storm]]".<ref name=ad>{{cite web|title=Ilse DeLange en Waylon naar Eurovisie Songfestival|url=http://www.ad.nl/ad/nl/13344/Dossier-Eurovisie-Songfestival/article/detail/3550975/2013/11/25/Ilse-DeLange-en-Waylon-naar-Eurovisie-Songfestival.dhtml|website=AD.nl|access-date=2 August 2014|date=25 November 2013}}</ref> They won second place with a total of 238 points behind [[Conchita Wurst]] from Austria, who scored 290 points.<ref name=euro2014 />

Waylon last performed with Delange at a concert on 21 June 2014 in [[Enschede]], following which he left The Common Linnets.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Granger|first1=Anthony|title=The Netherlands: Waylon Leaves The Common Linnets|url=https://eurovoix.com/2014/05/28/the-netherlands-waylon-leaves-the-common-linnets/|website=Eurovoix|access-date=26 January 2017|date=28 May 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Knoops|first1=Roy|title=ESC Today|url=http://esctoday.com/84130/netherlands-waylon-decides-fly-free|website=The Netherlands: Waylon Decides To Fly Free|access-date=26 January 2017|date=29 May 2014}}</ref>

===''Heaven After Midnight''===
Waylon's third studio album, ''Heaven After Midnight'', was released in September 2014 and reached #1 in the Dutch iTunes charts as well as in the Dutch Album Top 100 in the week of its release.<ref>{{cite web|title=Waylon - Heaven After Midnight|url=http://www.dutchcharts.nl/showitem.asp?interpret=Waylon&titel=Heaven+After+Midnight&cat=a|website=Dutch Charts|access-date=26 January 2017}}</ref> He worked in [[Nashville]] and [[Hollywood]] on the album with, among others, [[Bruce Gaitsch]], who previously wrote for [[Chicago (band)|Chicago]] and [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]].<ref name=nu2>{{cite web|title=Waylon schreef nieuw album in tien dagen|url=http://www.nu.nl/muziek/3676420/waylon-schreef-nieuw-album-in-tien-dagen.html|website=NU.nl|access-date=2 August 2014|date=15 January 2014}}</ref>

=== ''Seeds'' ===
In 2016, Waylon made a few appearances on talk show [[RTL Late Night]]; he lectured about the origins of pop-music and previewed tracks from his forthcoming album which came as a surprise. ''Seeds'' covered all styles and was led by the 70s-disco-tribute ''Our Song''. He continued to explore his versatility by playing an annual concert-series in at the Rotterdam Ahoy in a joint venture with Radio Veronica's Top 1000 All Time Classics.

===Eurovision Song Contest 2018===
On 9 November 2017, it was announced that Waylon would participate in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2018]] for the [[Netherlands in the Eurovision Song Contest|Netherlands]]. The choice of the song "[[Outlaw in 'Em]]" was announced on 2 March 2018. The Eurovision Song Contest fans gave him the pet name 'Walton'.
He performed in the second semi-final on 10 May 2018 and qualified for the final on 12 May 2018. Waylon finished 18th place with 121 points. In June 2018, Waylon returned to RTL Late Night to perform ''Thanks But No Thanks'', one of the other shortlisted songs, for the departing host [[Humberto Tan]].

In 2019 Waylon returned as a professional in ''[[It Takes Two (game show)|It Takes Two]]''. He also hosted the second episode of ''[[De Wereld Draait Door|DWDD Summerschool]]'' exploring the key-artists of [[Outlaw country]].  He continued to do so in a theatre-tour titled ''My Heroes Have Always Been Cowboys''.

===''Human''===
Late 2019, Waylon released his sixth album ''Human''; early 2020, he announced that the fifth edition of his Ahoy concert-series will be devoted to Dutch(-language) pop classics.

==Personal life==
He married in 2002, fathered a son, Dylan, and divorced after three years of marriage. He has a daughter with [[YouTuber]] Bibi Breijman.<ref name=telegraaf>{{cite news|last1=Cortie|first1=Patricia|title=Waylon verliet vrouw voor droom|url=http://www.telegraaf.nl/prive/article20440842.ece|access-date=2 August 2014|work=De Telegraaf|date=8 May 2010|language=nl}}</ref>
