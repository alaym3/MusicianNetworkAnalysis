'''Joshua Karpeh'''<ref name="Erika Ramirez">{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2018/12/cautious-clay-interview-2018|title=Cautious Clay is Rising Fast and Keeping His Balance|last=Ramirez|first=Erika|date=December 6, 2018|website=Complex|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=2019-09-28}}</ref> (born January 30, 1993), known professionally as '''Cautious Clay''', is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer.<ref name="Michael Marottaon">[https://vanyaland.com/2019/06/18/cautious-clay-takes-us-on-a-dream-sequence-in-sidewinder/ Cautious Clay takes us on a dream sequence in ''SIDEWINDER''] Michael Marottaon, ''Vanyaland'', June 18, 2019</ref><ref name="www.thefader.com">{{cite web |last1=WANG |first1=STEFFANEE |title=Meet Cautious Clay, the flute-playing Brooklyn soul singer who's become a semi-big deal |url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/03/01/cautious-clay-interview |website=thefader.com |publisher=The Fader inc |accessdate=March 1, 2018}}</ref><ref name="billboard">{{cite news |last1=Nattress |first1=Katrina |title=Cautious Clay Talks New EP 'Blood Type,' Working With Petit Biscuit & Shaping His Identity |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8232039/cautious-clay-interview-blood-type-petit-biscuit |accessdate=March 5, 2018 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=March 5, 2018}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Karpeh was born on January 30, 1993,{{Citation needed|date=September 2019}} in Cleveland, [[Ohio]].<ref name="pilerats.com">{{cite web |last1=Davies |first1=Hayden |title=Australia, it's time to get across Brooklyn's Cautious Clay |url=http://pilerats.com/written/get-to-know/australia-its-time-to-get-across-brooklyns-cautious-clay/ |website=pilerats.com |accessdate=July 16, 2019}}</ref> He graduated with a degree in International Affairs<ref name="okayplayer.com">{{cite web |last1=Herwees |first1=Tasbeeh |title=First Look Friday: Cautious Clay Wants to Find the Purpose in All Things and Nothing |url=https://www.okayplayer.com/music/cautious-clay-interview-table-of-context.html/ |website=‘’okayplayer.com’’|accessdate=September 30, 2019}}</ref><ref name="GW Hatchet">{{cite web |last1=Hillware |first1=Amanda |title=Alumnus, R&B artist Cautious Clay takes on All Things Go Fall Classic |url=https://www.gwhatchet.com/2018/10/01/alumnus-rb-artist-cautious-clay-takes-on-all-things-go-fall-classic/ |website=‘’gwhatchet.com’’ |accessdate=September 30, 2019}}</ref> from the [[George Washington University]] in 2015.<ref name="Erika Ramirez" /> Karpeh worked as a [[real estate agent]] in New York City for two years and in advertising for a year before pursuing music full-time.<ref name="Erika Ramirez" />

==Career==
Karpeh learned music production at GW University by remixing [[Beat (music)|beats]] for friends.<ref name="billboard" /> His music style is influenced by [[hip-hop]], organic and electronic sounds.<ref name="www.thefader.com" /> He plays multiple instruments including the saxophone, flute, guitar, bass, keyboard and drums.
 
Known by his stage name Cautious Clay, Karpeh, on September 22, 2017 released "Cold War", the first single from his debut EP ''Blood Type'', which was first released on February 21, 2018,<ref name="www.thefader.com" /> and then re-released on April 13, 2018 with the additional single "Stolen Moments".<ref name="www.npr.org">{{cite web |last1=Bote |first1=Joshua |title=In Cautious Clay's 'Stolen Moments,' A Lament Crooned Too Late |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/allsongs/2018/04/11/600807612/in-cautious-clays-stolen-moments-a-lament-crooned-too-late |publisher=NPR |accessdate=April 11, 2018}}</ref>
 
Karpeh's second EP ''Resonance'', was first released on May 30, 2018 and then re-released on August 22, 2018 with the additional song "Crowned".<ref name="kcrw.com">{{cite news |last1=Kramer |first1=Jason |title=EP Premiere: Cautious Clay "RESONANCE" |url=https://www.kcrw.com/music/articles/ep-premiere-cautious-clay-resonance |accessdate=July 16, 2019 |publisher=KCRW |date=May 30, 2018}}</ref><ref name="www.bongminesentertainment.com">{{cite web |last1=Thomson |first1=Zangba |title=Cautious Clay releases a delicious soul tune, entitled, "Crowned" |url=https://www.bongminesentertainment.com/cautious-clay-crowned/ |website=bongminesentertainment.com |accessdate=August 22, 2018}}</ref> His third EP ''Table of Context'', was released on March 27, 2019.<ref name="hypebeast">{{cite news |last1=Ingvaldsen |first1=Torsten |title=Cautious Clay Shares First Project of 2019, 'Table of Context' |url=https://hypebeast.com/2019/3/cautious-clay-table-of-context-ep-stream |accessdate=July 16, 2019 |agency=HypeBeast |date=May 27, 2019}}</ref>

Karpeh played at the 2019 [[Billy Reid (fashion designer)|Billy Reid Shindig]] in [[Florence, Alabama|Florence]], Alabama.<ref>[https://www.billyreid.com/shindig11 Billy Reid Shindig 11]</ref> He also featured on the soundtrack of ''13 Reasons Why: Season 3'' with the song "Swim Home".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/13-Reasons-Why-Explicit-Season/dp/B07VX54BPB/|title=13 Reasons Why [Explicit] (Season 3) by 5 Seconds of Summer & YUNGBLUD & Alexander 23 on Amazon Music|website=Amazon.com|accessdate=August 20, 2019}}</ref> Swim Home is one of two collaborations with [[John Mayer]], the other is "Carry Me Away", featured on ''Table of Context''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8529709/cautious-clay-interview|title=How Cautious Clay Linked Up With John Mayer and Scored a Credit on Taylor Swift's 'Lover'|last=LeDonne|first=Rob|date=11 September 2019|website=Billboard|url-status=live|archive-url=|archivedate=|accessdate=November 1, 2019}}</ref>

"Cold War" was sampled on the song "London Boy" for [[Taylor Swift]]'s seventh studio album ''[[Lover (album)|Lover]]'' (2019).<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/how-cautious-clay-ended-up-on-taylor-swifts-lover-881709/|title=How Cautious Clay Ended Up on Taylor Swift's 'Lover'|last=Bernstein|first=Jonathan|magazine=Rolling Stone|date=September 10, 2019|accessdate=September 11, 2019}}</ref>
