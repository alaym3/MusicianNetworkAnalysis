{{Nihongo|'''Hiroyuki Sawano'''|澤野 弘之|Sawano Hiroyuki|born September 12, 1980}} is a Japanese [[composer]], [[arranger]], [[musician]], [[lyricist]] and [[pianist]] best known for his work on many [[anime]] series,  television dramas, and films. His works include the musical scores for ''[[Attack on Titan (TV series)|Attack on Titan]]'', ''[[Blue Exorcist]]'', ''[[Kill la Kill]]'', ''[[Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn]], [[Aldnoah.Zero]]'' and ''[[The Seven Deadly Sins (2014 TV series)|The Seven Deadly Sins]]''. He was represented by Legendoor between 2006 and 2017, and is currently represented by VV-ALKLINE. In 2014, he started a new vocal project under the name <nowiki>''</nowiki>'''SawanoHiroyuki[nZk]'''<nowiki>''</nowiki>.<ref name="WowJapan">{{cite news|author=Rico|first=Aura|date=July 13, 2016|title=4 Composers Behind That Awesome Anime Soundtrack Music|website=Wow Japan|publisher=[[Anime Festival Asia]]|url=https://wowjapan.asia/2016/07/music-4-composers-behind-awesome-anime-soundtrack-music/|url-status=dead|access-date=April 7, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200808045242/https://wowjapan.asia/4-composers-behind-that-awesome-anime-soundtrack-music/|archive-date=2020-08-08}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/pp/sawanohiroyuki03|last=Narimatsu|first=Satomi|title=SawanoHiroyuki[nZk]「o1」インタビュー|trans-title=SawanoHiroyuki[nZk] "o1" Interview|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|language=ja|date=September 9, 2015|access-date=April 7, 2018}}</ref> His pieces tend to have exceedingly strange titles, featuring symbols and letters from various different alphabets.<ref name="Anitrendzinterview">{{cite news|url=https://anitrendz.net/news/2021/02/10/interview-music-composer-hiroyuki-sawano/|title=Interview: Music Composer Hiroyuki Sawano|website=Anime Trending|date=February 10, 2021|access-date=April 2, 2021}}</ref> Sawano cited [[Joe Hisaishi]], [[Yoko Kanno]], [[Danny Elfman]], and [[Hans Zimmer]] as major inspirations.<ref name="Inspiration">{{cite news|url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/culture/2021/02/12/music/hiroyuki-sawano-soundtrack/|last=Schley|first=Matt|title=Anime composer Hiroyuki Sawano's soundtracks stream worldwide for the first time|website=[[The Japan Times]]|date=February 12, 2021|access-date=March 15, 2021}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Sawano was born in [[Tokyo]], Japan. He began playing piano in elementary school. From the age of 17 he studied composition, arrangement, orchestration, and piano under Nobuchika Tsuboi.<ref>{{cite web|title=Legendoor website {{!}} 澤野弘之 {{!}} PROFILE|url=http://www.legendoor.com/a_01/|publisher=Legendoor.com|access-date=2 May 2012|year=2006|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141016125401/http://www.legendoor.com/a_01/|archive-date=16 October 2014}}</ref> He has been active as a composer since 2004.<ref name="WowJapan"/><ref name="Otherworks">{{cite web|url=http://www.sawanohiroyuki.com/works-artist_etc.html|title=HIROYUKI SAWANO OFFICIAL WEBSITE: Works|website=sawanohiroyuki.com|access-date=July 2, 2017|language=ja}}</ref>

In 2014, he launched a new vocal song project under the name "SawanoHiroyuki[nZk]", producing the first album ''[[UnChild]]'' with [[Aimer]] as the vocalist under the name SawanoHiroyuki[nZk]:Aimer.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/177196|title=澤野弘之、Aimerやななみを迎えてTV版「ガンダムUC」OP＆ED発表|trans-title=Hiroyuki Sawano, Aimer, and Nanami "Gundam UC" OP & ED TV version announced|date=February 23, 2016|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|language=ja|access-date=2018-04-07}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.sh-nzk.net/artist/sawanohiroyukinzk/info/440457|title=UnChild (Info) – SawanoHiroyuki[nZk] {{!}} Sony Music|website=www.sh-nzk.net|language=ja|access-date=2016-07-17}}</ref> Since then his songs under this vocal song project have also been used as the opening and ending themes for various anime, including ''[[Aldnoah.Zero]]'', ''[[Seraph of the End]]'', ''[[Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn|Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn RE:0096]]'', ''[[Legend of the Galactic Heroes|The Legend of the Galactic Heroes: Die Neue These Kaikō]]'', and ''[[Re:Creators]]''. Sawano's vocal song project was moved to the [[Sacra Music]] record label under [[Sony Music Entertainment Japan]] in April 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2084189/full/|title=ソニー、新レーベル『SACRA MUSIC』4月発足 Kalafina、ClariSら擁し海外展開|trans-title=Sony new label, "SACRA MUSIC", launched in April; Kalafina, ClariS, etc. expand overseas|publisher=[[Oricon]]|access-date=January 6, 2017|date=January 6, 2017}}</ref>

On July 2, 2017, Sawano's contract with Legendoor was terminated after approximately 12 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legendoor.com/news/217|script-title=ja:【澤野弘之】マネジメント契約終了に関するお知らせ|publisher=Legendoor|access-date=July 2, 2017|language=ja}}</ref> He is now represented by VV-ALKLINE.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sawanohiroyuki.com/vvalkline.html|title=HIROYUKI SAWANO OFFICIAL WEBSITE: Link|website=sawanohiroyuki.com|access-date=July 2, 2017|language=ja}}</ref>
