'''Brittnee Moore''', known professionally by the stage name '''bbymutha''', is an American rapper. She was born in [[Chattanooga, Tennessee]] and began her career through [[SoundCloud]]. In 2017, she rose to prominence after the video for her song "Rules" went viral. Her debut studio album, ''Muthaland'', was released in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-31|title=Bbymutha bows out from the rap game with ‘Muthaland’|url=https://lab.fm/genres/hip-hop/bbymutha-bows-out-from-the-rap-game-with-muthaland/|access-date=2020-09-09|website=lab.fm|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Moore was born and raised in [[Chattanooga, Tennessee]]. Both of her parents were in high school at the time of her birth.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/03/01/bbymutha-interview|title=In bbymutha we trust|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref> Moore was raised primarily by her mother, a devout [[Christians|Christian]]. She has stated that her father, a barber,<ref name="auto">{{Cite news|url=http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/37733/1/bbymutha-dazed-interview-rap-rules|title=Meet BbyMutha: the ‘black Marge Simpson’ who can really rap|last=Dazed|date=2017-10-11|work=Dazed|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref> was physically abusive.<ref name="auto1"/> She has a younger brother.<ref name="auto2">{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/10/17/bbymutha-rapper-interview-rules|title=BbyMutha is the anti-parental advisory rapper who’s putting us all on game|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref>

Moore had a turbulent adolescence. At school, Moore was an outcast and was bullied.<ref name="auto3">{{Cite news|url=http://www.maskmagazine.com/the-fling-issue/work/brittnee-moore-bbymutha|title=Interview with BBYMUTHA|work=Mask Magazine|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref> At the age of 12, she moved to Pensacola with her mother.<ref name="auto3"/> After physically assaulting a teacher at her school, Moore was committed to a psychiatric ward, where she was diagnosed with [[Major depressive disorder|depression]] and [[Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder|ADHD]].<ref name="auto"/> She was expelled from the school and moved back to Tennessee.<ref name="auto3"/> By the age of 13, Moore had begun selling drugs and having sex with older boys.<ref name="auto1"/>  At 17, she became pregnant with her first pair of twins, Mekel and Mekeila, who she has raised alone, describing the children's father as a '[[Pedophilia|pedophile]]'.<ref name="auto1"/>

Moore would listen to rap music at her father's house during her summer break from school.<ref name="auto"/> Beginning in early childhood, she enjoyed writing.<ref name="auto3"/> She began writing poetry in fourth grade,<ref name="auto4">{{Cite news|url=http://thehoodhippie.com/featured-artist-brittnee-moore-a-k-a-bbymutha/|title=Featured Artist: Brittnee Moore a.k.a Bbymutha - TheHoodHippie|date=2014-05-15|work=TheHoodHippie|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en-US}}</ref> and became interested in music and performance after participating and winning a dance competition with her cousins in elementary school.<ref name="auto1"/> She began rapping in tenth grade.<ref name="auto4"/> During that year, Moore and her cousins formed a group called Money over Niggas.<ref name="auto3"/>

==Career==
Moore's "first official song" as a solo artist was "Slut".<ref name="auto5">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/first-person-bbymutha-on-creativity-and-parenting/|title=Rapper bbymutha on Creativity and Parenting {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2018-09-02}}</ref> Moore released the song on [[SoundCloud]]. 
She performed under the stage name Cindyy Kushh from the age of "17 or 18",<ref name="auto4"/> before changing her stage name to bbymutha. The name was inspired by her experience of being called a "baby mama" by the other female partner of her unfaithful boyfriend.<ref name="auto2"/>

After working in retail and in call centres for several years,<ref name="auto1"/> she became pregnant with her second pair of twins, Khloe and Tyler, after moving to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] to study fashion design.<ref name="auto"/> The father of the twins was abusive, and he disapproved of Moore's rapping, leading Moore to take a hiatus from her rap career.<ref name="auto3"/>
After giving birth to her second pair of twins, Moore moved back to Chattanooga<ref name="auto3"/> and returned to rapping.<ref name="auto5"/> In December 2015, she released a three-song EP entitled ''Weave''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/bbymutha/sets/weave-ep|title=WEAVE EP|website=SoundCloud|language=en|access-date=2018-09-02}}</ref>

=== Breakthrough ===
In December 2016, Moore released the EP ''Glow Kit''. The EP was included in "20 best Bandcamp releases of 2017". 
In 2017, the track "Rules", which was produced by Luna God, was released as a single from the EP. The music video for the song was later released in August on YouTube.<ref name="auto1"/> The video went viral, and led to Moore to receive attention from prominent [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]] musicians [[SZA (singer)|SZA]] and [[Kehlani]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.dazeddigital.com/projects/article/39785/1/bbymutha-musician-biography-dazed-100-2018-profile|title=Vote for bbymutha on the #Dazed100|last=Dazed|date=2018-04-24|work=Dazed|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref> The track was also played by [[Björk]] as part of a DJ set,<ref name="auto1"/> and was featured in [[The Fader|FADER]]'s "101 best songs of 2017".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/12/03/best-songs-2017-apple-music-spotify-playlist|title=The 101 best songs of 2017|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref> In 2019, "Rules" was included in the soundtrack of the [[Hulu]] original series [[Ramy (TV series)|Ramy]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Carlin|first=Shannon|title=Hulu's "Ramy" Has A Seriously Good Soundtrack (You're Welcome)|url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2019/04/230075/ramy-hulu-soundtrack-songs-season-1|access-date=2021-02-12|website=www.refinery29.com|language=en}}</ref>

In 2018, Moore has released three EPs so far. In January 2018, ''Muthaz Day 2'' was released.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/bbymutha-shares-new-song-the-come-up-new-song.1977741.html|title=BbyMutha Shares New Song "The Come Up"|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=2018-09-02}}</ref> The EP featured production from Rock Floyd and [[Crystal Caines]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://indiecurrent.com/bbymutha-raps-over-grimes-genesis-on-muthaz-day-2/|title=Bbymutha Raps Over Grimes’ "Genesis" On Muthaz Day 2 {{!}} IndieCurrent|website=indiecurrent.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-09-02}}</ref> This was followed by ''BbyShoe,'' which was released in February 2018 to positive reviews.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mefeater.com/done-bbymutha-has-plenty-of-truth-to-tell-on-the-smooth-bbyshoe-ep/|title=Bbymutha Has Plenty of Truth To Tell on the Smooth BbyShoe EP - MEFeater|website=mefeater.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-09-02}}</ref> The EP also received praise from ''Pitchfork''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/great-records-you-may-have-missed-winter-2018/|title=Great Records You May Have Missed: Winter 2018 {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2018-09-02}}</ref> In May 2018, Moore released a surprise EP entitled ''Free Brittanee.''<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/05/13/bbymutha-free-brittnee|title=BbyMutha shares surprise EP Free Brittnee|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref> Moore performed at [[Pride Toronto|Toronto Pride]] in June 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://nowtoronto.com/music/features/best-music-pride-toronto-2018/|title=Nine can’t-miss artists playing Toronto Pride 2018|last=Ritchie|first=Kevin|date=2018-06-21|work=NOW Magazine|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en-us}}</ref>

"Indian Hair", a track from Moore's 2015 EP, ''Weave'', featured in the soundtrack to the second season of [[Netflix]] series ''[[Dear White People (TV series)|Dear White People]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.indiewire.com/2018/05/dear-white-people-soundtrack-songs-season-2-netflix-1201959431/|title=‘Dear White People’ Soundtrack: All the Songs from Season 2 at Winchester|last=Greene|first=Steve|date=2018-05-05|work=IndieWire|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Retirement, ''Mutha Magick Apothecary'', and comeback ===
On August 28, 2020, after years of delays, her debut studio album, ''Muthaland'', was finally released on music distribution platforms. Declaring it her last official release, she expressed her frustrations via Twitter with the process behind making the album, adding how much stress it caused her.<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/bbymutha/status/1299247159938813952?ref_url=https%3a%2f%2flab.fm%2fgenres%2fhip-hop%2fbbymutha-bows-out-from-the-rap-game-with-muthaland%2f|url=https://twitter.com/bbymutha/status/1299247159938813952?ref_url=https%3a%2f%2flab.fm%2fgenres%2fhip-hop%2fbbymutha-bows-out-from-the-rap-game-with-muthaland%2f|access-date=2020-09-09|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> The album received positive reviews from music publications, with critics praising the lyrics and creativity. Pitchfork scored the album an 8.0 rating, with contributor Dylan Green writing "It’s a deliriously entertaining, ambitious project from an artist operating at her peak".<ref>{{Cite web|title=BbyMutha: Muthaland|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/bbymutha-muthaland/|access-date=2020-09-09|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref> She recently started an apothecary, named Mutha Magick Apothecary, where she sells soaps, candles, and essential oils among other products. In addition, she also plans to open a physical brick-and-mortar store for the company, while also focusing on possibly releasing a mini-EP to be made exclusively available on [[OnlyFans]], and a documentary.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Honeycutt|first=Shante|title=Mutha Magick: A profile of Bbymutha’s homegrown apothecary – NBGA MAG – No Basic Girls Allowed|url=https://nobasicgirlsallowed.com/mutha-magick-a-profile-of-bbymuthas-homegrown-apothecary/|access-date=2020-09-09|language=en-US}}</ref> She soon recanted her statements on retirement and appeared to make a comeback with the release of her EP ''Muthaleficient 2'' on March 2, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-02|title=Bbymutha Releases Tough, Adventurous New EP 'Muthaleficent 2': Stream|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2117943/stream-bbymuthas-tough-adventurous-new-ep-muthaleficent-2/music/|access-date=2021-05-19|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref> She later released the third volume of her Bastard Tapes EPs, ''The Bastard Tapes, Vol. 3'' on May 10, 2021. Tom Breihan for Stereogum describes it as "[not] as musically far-out as ''Muthaleficent 2''. Instead, it’s mostly hard, fundamental Southern rap. [...] Mostly, though, the appeal is just Bbytalking fly, charismatic, nasty shit. She is extremely good at doing that."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-05-10|title=Bbymutha Releases New EP 'Bastard Tapes Vol. 3': Stream|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2147121/stream-bbymuthas-new-ep-bastard-tapes-vol-3/music/|access-date=2021-05-19|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref>

=== Influences ===
Moore has credited [[Gucci Mane]] as her biggest musical influence.<ref name="auto3"/> She has also listed [[Southern hip hop]] artists [[Trina]], [[La Chat]], and [[Gangsta Boo]] as influences.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://capitolsounddc.com/capitol-sound/2017/10/10/interview-bbymutha|title=Interview: Bbymutha|work=Capitol Sound DC|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en-US}}</ref> She has described her music as being influenced by living in the ghetto in Chattanooga.<ref name="auto4"/> Her music is also influenced by her religious upbringing in Tennessee, in the [[Bible Belt]].<ref name="auto6">{{Cite news|url=https://nylon.com/articles/bbymutha-rapper-interview|title=BbyMutha Is Unfiltered, Unashamed, And Unfazed By Your Hate|date=2018-03-30|work=NYLON|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref>

She is known for having a colourful fashion sense and frequently wearing different wigs.<ref name="auto1"/> Moore has named [[Jimi Hendrix]], [[Lil Kim]], and [[Kelis]] as style influences.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/bbymutha-interview|title=Meet the Tennessee Rapper Stanned by Björk and Earl Sweatshirt|last=White|first=Brooklyn|work=Teen Vogue|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Moore lives in Tennessee with her four children. Her father lives next door to her, and helps her with childcare when she travels to perform.<ref name="auto6"/> In a 2015 interview, Moore stated that she is not at a point in her life where she is ready for a relationship, having previously had unhealthy relationships.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://themuthaboard.wordpress.com/2015/04/29/faq-with-bbymutha/|title=FAQ WITH BBYMUTHA|date=2015-04-29|work=themuthaboard|access-date=2018-09-02|language=en-US}}</ref> Moore identifies as [[Bisexuality|bisexual]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://officemagazine.net/life-tips-bbymutha|title=Life Tips with BbyMutha|date=2018-02-28|work=Office Magazine|access-date=2018-11-20|language=en}}</ref>
