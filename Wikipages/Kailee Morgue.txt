'''Kailee Moore''' (born July 15, 1998<ref name="Moore 2018">{{Cite news|last=Moore|first=Jacob|date=October 28, 2017|title=Kailee Morgue's "Medusa" Went From Viral Tweet to Major Label Pop Hit|work=PigeonsandPlanes|url=https://pigeonsandplanes.com/music/2017/10/kailee-morgue-medusa|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref>), professionally known as '''Kailee Morgue''', is an American singer-songwriter.

== Career ==
Kailee first began posting on her YouTube channel in late 2015, and started with [[Cover version|covers]] of songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/Kaileemoore/videos?view=0&sort=dd&flow=grid|title=Kailee Morgue|via=YouTube|access-date=1 March 2019}}</ref> Her most popular cover being "Spirit Desire" by [[The Tigers (Japanese band)|Tigers Jaw]].<ref>{{Citation|last=Kailee Morgue|title=Spirit Desire – Tigers Jaw (acoustic cover)|date=November 7, 2015|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q8oJ0NS8w4M|access-date=1 March 2019}}</ref> In January 2017, Morgue tweeted a preview of "Medusa" that went [[Viral video|viral]]. Later that year, Morgue signed a deal with [[Republic Records]].<ref name="Moore 2018" /> A music video for "Medusa" was released in October 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/10/25/kailee-morgue-medusa-video|title=Watch Kailee Morgue's creepy-crawly video for "Medusa"|last=Tanzer|first=Myles|date=October 25, 2017|work=[[The FADER]]|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref> She worked with producer [[CJ Baran]] to develop a final version of the song.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/10/12/kailee-morgue-medusa-tweet|title=This haunting song went viral before it was even finished. Now Kailee Morgue's "Medusa" is finally ready.|last=Tanzer|first=Myles|date=October 12, 2017|work=The FADER|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref> Morgue performed at her first music festival, [[Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival]] in August 2018.<ref name="Tuozzolo 2018">{{Cite news|url=http://www.dailycal.org/2018/08/14/kailee-morgue-outside-lands/|title=Kailee Morgue hesitates, eventually finds her groove at Outside Lands|last=Tuozzolo|first=Ryan|date=August 14, 2018|work=[[The Daily Californian]]|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref> In 2018, Morgue released the single "Siren" continuing the Greek mythology theme to her songs such as "Medusa."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.completemusicupdate.com/article/approved-kailee-morgue/|title=Approved: Kailee Morgue|last=Malt|first=Andy|date=September 20, 2018|website=[[Complete Music Update]]|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
''[[Highsnobiety]]'' described Morgue as a mix of goth and punk. She cites [[Gwen Stefani]] and [[Avril Lavigne]] as an early influence.<ref name="Gore 2018">{{Cite news|url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/kailee-morgue-siren-premiere/|title=Kailee Morgue Takes the Edge off Just Enough With "Siren"|last=Gore|first=Sydney|date=November 16, 2018|work=[[Highsnobiety]]|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=. @morguemami 's unique sound was inspired by @AvrilLavigne , and I simply cannot get enough of her!! |url=https://twitter.com/MTV/status/1301565624741625856 |accessdate=5 September 2020 |agency=MTV (Twitter)}}</ref> ''[[Atwood Magazine]]'' described Morgue's singing as a mixture of light and dark "like a dream within a nightmare, or vice versa."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/medusa-kailee-morgue-interview-2018/|title=Dark Spells & Light Dreams: An Interview with Kailee Morgue – Atwood Magazine|last=Mosk|first=Mitch|date=January 19, 2018|work=Atwood Magazine|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref>

== Reception ==
Sydney Gore of [[MTV]] called Morgue an "emerging pop star" after the release of her EP, ''Medusa''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://trl.mtv.com/news/3058198/the-drop-january-19-2018/|title=The Drop: Fall Out Boy, Porches, Kailee Morgue|last=Gore|first=Sydney|date=January 19, 2018|publisher=MTV News|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Morgue is based in Los Angeles.<ref name="Moore 2018" /> She is openly queer and [[Pansexuality|pansexual]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8503873/kailee-morgue-interview-hayley-kiyoko-headcase-song|title=Kailee Morgue Talks Working With Hayley Kiyoko, Queer Artistry & the Importance of Authenticity|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-07-16}}</ref> She practices [[witchcraft]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/7xejzb/10-things-you-need-to-know-about-kailee-morgue|title=10 things you need to know about kailee morgue|last=Dunn|first=Frankie|date=January 18, 2018|work=[[i-D]]|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref> Her favorite fictional character is [[Sailor Moon (character)|Sailor Moon]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_au/article/7xejzb/10-things-you-need-to-know-about-kailee-morgue|title=10 things you need to know about kailee morgue|last1=Hanra|first1=Hanna|last2=Dunn|first2=Frankie|date=2018-01-19|website=I-D|language=en-UK|access-date=2019-07-16}}</ref>
