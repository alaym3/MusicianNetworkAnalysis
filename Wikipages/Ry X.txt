==Early life==
RY X was born on Woodford Island in the Northern Rivers of New South Wales, where he became a surfer.<ref name=trying>{{cite news | url=https://theculturetrip.com/pacific/australia/articles/ry-x-im-just-trying-to-love-everyone-i-meet-a-lot/ | title=Ry X: ‘I’m Just Trying To Love Everyone I Meet A Lot’ | first=Ann | last=Lee | work=The Culture Trip | date=13 March 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
In an interview with ''[[Entertainment Tonight]]'', RY X mentioned [[Pearl Jam]] and [[Jeff Buckley]] as his greatest influences.<ref name=trying/> He started writing music at 16, after he heard Buckley's album ''[[Grace (Jeff Buckley album)|Grace]]''.<ref name=trying/> His song "Let Your Spirit Fly" appeared on the soundtrack of the 2006 film ''[[Hoot (film)|Hoot]]''. He moved to Los Angeles, California, where he was signed by [[Jive Records]], releasing his self-titled debut album on 20 July 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url={{Allmusic|class=album|id=r1826788|pure_url=yes}}|title=Ry Cuming (album)|date=20 July 2010|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=16 September 2010}}</ref> He briefly toured with [[Maroon 5]], becoming their opening act in select venues.<ref name="Cantiello" />

RY X won Australia's Dolphin Awards for "Best Pop Song" and "Best New Artist".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.y93.fm/new2/featured/f/2515|title=KYYY: RY Cuming|work=92.9 FM – [[Y-93]]|accessdate=16 September 2010}}</ref> In 2012, he collaborated with Frank Wiedemann to release "Howling" on the Berlin record label Innervisions, including a remix by [[Âme]].<ref name=melt /> His EP ''[[Berlin (EP)|Berlin]]'', released on 28 August 2013,<ref>[http://nordicwaves.net/ry-x-berlin Nordic Waves: RY X – ''Berlin''] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131211054649/http://nordicwaves.net/ry-x-berlin |date=11 December 2013 }}</ref> was made available as 12" vinyl and digital. The title track charted in France, Germany, and the United Kingdom.<ref name="FRA" /><ref name="GER" /><ref name="UK" /> The track also got the attention of [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]] who created their cover version.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2014/01/listen-sam-smith-covers-ry-xs-berlin/|title=Listen: Sam Smith covers RY X's "Berlin"|website=Consequence of Sound|language=en-US| access-date = 2016-02-29}}</ref> In 2014, RY X became the lead singer of the band The Acid, which he formed with Los Angeles producer Freeland and Steve Nalepa. They released an album titled ''Liminal''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.residentadvisor.net/news.aspx?id=24829|title=Adam Freeland, RY X and Steve Nalepa are The Acid|website=Resident Advisor|access-date=2016-06-30}}</ref> Together with Frank Wiedemann he formed the band Howling and released a full length album Sacred Ground on [[Ninja Tune]] and Monkeytown Records.

RY X's hit single "Howling" was featured in the movie ''Everything, Everything''. The remix by Âme was featured in the movie ''[[Taken 3]]''.

On May 6, 2016, RY X released his second album ''<nowiki/>'Dawn'''  through [[Infectious Records]] and [[Loma Vista Recordings|Loma Vista Records]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/05/05/arts/music/ry-x-dawn.html|title=Review: RY X Stretches Pop Structures on 'Dawn'|last=Pareles|first=Jon|date=2016-05-04|newspaper=The New York Times|issn=0362-4331|access-date=2016-06-30}}</ref> The album charted at #34 in the [[UK Albums Chart|UK Album Charts]] as well as charting in [[Germany]], [[France]], [[Canada]] and [[Australia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/uk/artists/music/ry-x/albums/dawn/|title=iTunesCharts.net: 'Dawn' by RY X (British Albums iTunes Chart)|website=www.itunescharts.net|access-date=2016-06-30}}</ref> It gained favourable reception from the media, with a [[The New York Times|New York Times]] review saying "His voice is a pearly, androgynous tenor, a vessel for liquid melancholy that blurs words at the edges. He stretches pop structures with repetition that grows devotional, obsessive, hypnotic".<ref name=":0" /> In support of the album, RY X began an extensive tour across Europe and the US as well as appearing at many festival throughout 2016 such as Barn on the Farm and [[Montreux Jazz Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.songkick.com/artists/6888059-ry-x|title=Ry X|website=Songkick|access-date=2016-06-30}}</ref>

In October 2018, RY X confirmed that his third studio album, ''Unfurl'', would be released on 15 February 2019 via Infectious Music. The album announcement coincided with the release of a new single, "Untold", and the announcement of a global tour, beginning in Europe, that commenced in February 2019.
