'''Fit for an Autopsy''' is an American [[deathcore]] band from [[Jersey City, New Jersey]], formed in 2008. The band consists of guitarists Will Putney, Pat Sheridan and Tim Howley, drummer Josean Orta, lead vocalist Joe Badolato, and bassist Peter "Blue" Spinazola. Fit for an Autopsy are currently signed to [[Nuclear Blast Records]]<ref name=autopsysigns>{{cite web |url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/fit-for-an-autopsy-signs-with-eone-music-good-fight-music/ |title=Fit for an Autopsy Signs With eOne Music/Good Fight Music |publisher=Blabbermouth |date=July 25, 2013}}</ref> and have released five studio albums since formation.

== History ==
The band released their first demo in 2008, followed a year later by the group's first EP, ''Hell on Earth''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2010/12/03/black-market-activities-is-fit-for-an-autopsy/ |title=BLACK MARKET ACTIVITIES IS FIT FOR AN AUTOPSY |publisher=MetalSucks |date=December 3, 2010}}</ref> In 2011, Fit for an Autopsy released their first full-length LP, ''The Process of Human Extermination.''<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/the-process-of-human-extermination-mw0002148279 |title=The Process of Human Extermination: Review by Phil Freeman |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=July 2, 2014}}</ref><ref>Gregory Heaney. [http://www.allmusic.com/artist/fit-for-an-autopsy-mn0002696900/biography Fit for an Autopsy Biography]. [[AllMusic]]</ref>

In September 2013, the band released their second album, ''Hellbound''.<ref name=autopsysigns /> Less than a year later, in April 2014, the band announced that vocalist Nate Johnson would be leaving the band. Greg Wilburn of the Devastated was immediately named as Johnson's temporary replacement.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.metalinjection.net/latest-news/breakups/fit-for-an-autopsy-has-been-nate-johnsoned-announce-new-vocalist |title=FIT FOR AN AUTOPSY Has Been "Nate Johnsoned," Announce New Vocalist |publisher=Metal Injection |date=April 16, 2014}}</ref>

As of early 2015, the band have announced the departure of Greg Wilburn and their new vocalist, Joe Badolato. Along with this, they have announced that they are in the writing process for a new album. This third album, named ''Absolute Hope Absolute Hell'', was released on October 2, 2015.<ref>[http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/fit-for-an-autopsy-to-release-absolute-hope-absolute-hell-album-in-october/ Fit for an Autopsy: To Release 'Absolute Hope, Absolute Hell' Album In October]. [[blabbermouth.net]]. July 9, 2015.</ref>

In July 2016, the band have announced a split-album, named ''The Depression Sessions'' with fellow deathcore bands, [[Thy Art Is Murder]] and [[The Acacia Strain]]. The EP was released on August 12, 2016.<ref>[http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/thy-art-is-murder-the-acacia-strain-fit-for-an-autopsy-to-release-the-depression-sessions/ THY ART IS MURDER, THE ACACIA STRAIN, FIT FOR AN AUTOPSY To Release 'The Depression Sessions'] [[blabbermouth.net]]. July 9, 2015.</ref>

The band released a new song, "Heads Will Hang," from their upcoming fourth studio album ''The Great Collapse'' on January 31, 2017. The album was released on March 17, 2017.<ref>[http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/fit-for-an-autopsy-releases-heads-will-hang-video/ FIT FOR AN AUTOPSY Releases 'Heads Will Hang' Video'] [[blabbermouth.net]]. January 31, 2017.</ref> On May 8, 2018 Fit For An Autopsy announced they signed with Nuclear Blast

On October 25, 2019 the band released their fifth studio album ''The Sea of Tragic Beasts'' through Nuclear Blast Records. <ref>[https://www.nuclearblast.com/en/music/band/discography/details/6059711.4105086.the-sea-of-tragic-beasts.html/ ‘The Sea of Tragic Beasts, Fit for an Autopsy’][[nuclearblast.com]]. October 25, 2019.</ref>

On April 6, 2020, the band released a standalone single "Fear Tomorrow" through Nuclear Blast Records. <ref>[https://www.nuclearblast.de/en/label/music/band/news/details/6576476.4105086.fit-for-an-autopsy-share-new-single.html][[nuclearblast.com]]. April 6, 2020.</ref>

On September 22, 2021, the band announced their new album ''Oh What the Future Holds'' set to release on January 14, 2022. Two days later, they released the album's first single "Far from Heaven."<ref>[https://loudwire.com/fit-for-an-autopsy-far-from-heaven-lyrics-video-oh-what-the-future-holds-album/ ‘Fit for An Autopsy Debut ‘Far From Heaven’ Off 2022 Album ‘Oh What the Future Holds’][[loudwire.com]]. September 24, 2021.</ref>
