'''Amel Bent Bachir''' ({{lang-ar|Ø¢Ù…Ø§Ù„ Ø¨Ù†Øª Ø¨Ø´ÙŠØ±}}; born 21 June 1985) is a French [[contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[pop music|pop]] singer who gained fame after reaching the semi-finals of season 2 of French TV singing competition ''[[Nouvelle Star]]''. She is best-selling artist to come from that competition.

She is currently a "coach" on the similar French talent show ''[[The Voice: la plus belle voix]]''.

== Biography ==

Amel Bent grew up in the Paris suburb of [[La Courneuve]]<ref name="purepeople">{{cite news |url=http://www.purepeople.com/people/amel-bent_p58 |title=Biographie de Amel Bent|work=purepeople.com |access-date=2010-08-29 }}</ref> with her [[Algeria]]n father and [[Morocco|Moroccan]] mother. She has a brother and a sister.<ref name="purepeople"/><ref name="algeriades">{{cite news|url=http://www.algeriades.com/news/previews/article1850.htm |title=Biographie de Amel Bent |work=algeriades.com |access-date=2011-06-14 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110504142101/http://www.algeriades.com/news/previews/article1850.htm |archive-date=2011-05-04 }}</ref> Her career jump started after making it to the semi-finals of the reality TV show ''[[Nouvelle Star|Nouvelle Star 2]]'', France's version of [[Pop Idol]]. Although her performance did not make it to the finals, she was still noticed by some of the show's producers, and would end up making her dÃ©but album later that year, titled: ''Un Jour d'Ã©tÃ©'', released in late 2004.
