'''Kodaline''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|k|oʊ|d|əl|aɪ|n}})<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/Kodaline/status/241084563735052288 |title=Twitter / Kodaline: @LiveDrive ah cheers thanks |publisher=Twitter.com |date=30 August 2012 |access-date=2 May 2014}}</ref> are an Irish [[rock music|rock]] band. Originally known as '''21 Demands''', the band adopted their current name in 2012 to coincide with the changing of their music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/2.681/true-bloods-1.1250800|title=True bloods|website=The Irish Times|language=en|access-date=2020-04-05}}</ref> The group comprises Steve Garrigan, Mark Prendergast, Vincent May and Jason Boland.

Garrigan and Prendergast grew up in [[Rivervalley, Swords, Dublin]], and have known each other since childhood. May and Garrigan attended Colaiste Choilm CBS, [[Swords, Dublin|Swords]], where they participated in [[battles of the bands]]. Boland joined the group in 2012. They have released four albums: ''[[In a Perfect World (Kodaline album)|In a Perfect World]]'' (2013), ''[[Coming Up for Air (Kodaline album)|Coming Up for Air]]'' (2015), and ''[[Politics of Living]]'' (2018). Their fourth studio album, ''One Day at a Time'', was released on 12 June 2020.<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/2020/0501/1136025-kodaline-take-it-one-day-at-a-time-with-new-album/|title=Kodaline take it One Day at a Time with new album|date=1 May 2020|access-date=6 September 2020|website=Rte.ie}}</ref>

==Career==
===2005–11: 21 Demands===
As 21 Demands,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://popspoken.com/music/2020/05/kodaline-drummer-vincent-may-reflects-on-growing-up-with-the-band-and-adapting-to-the-new-normal|title=Vincent May Reflects on Growing with the Band and Adapting to the New Normal|publisher=Popspoken|date=13 May 2020|access-date=13 May 2020}}</ref> the band first came to attention in November 2006, when they participated in the fifth series of [[Raidió Teilifís Éireann|RTÉ]] talent show ''[[You're a Star (series 5)|You're a Star]]''; finishing as the series' runners-up. On 3 March 2007, 21 Demands released their own single called "Give Me a Minute" through [[Pieta House]] downloads on the [[Raidió Teilifís Éireann|RTÉ]] website. They made history when their single topped the [[Irish Singles Chart]], becoming the first independently released track to achieve this milestone.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/2007/0323/409459-youreastar/|title=21 Demands make Irish chart history|publisher=RTÉ|date=23 March 2007|access-date=6 October 2007}}</ref><ref name=IRE>Peak positions in Ireland:
* For "Give Me a Minute": {{cite web|url=http://irish-charts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=21+Demands|title=Discography 21 Demands|publisher=irish-charts.com|access-date=28 December 2012}}
</ref>
21 Demands also wrote the single "One of Those Days" which they played on the Irish [[SuperValu (Ireland)|SuperValu]] channel Balcony TV.

===2012–14: ''In a Perfect World''===
The quartet released their debut [[extended play]]—''The Kodaline EP''—on Friday 7 September 2012, produced by [[Stephen Harris (producer)|Stephen Harris]].<ref name="thekodalineep">{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/The-Kodaline-EP/dp/B008YE12JC/ref=ntt_mus_dp_dpt_1|title=Kodaline – 'The Kodaline EP' – Digital Download|publisher=[[Amazon.com|Amazon]]|access-date=28 December 2012}}</ref><ref name="air-million">{{cite web|url=http://air-million.com/philip-magee-speaks-about-producing-kodaline-the-script-more/ref=ntt_mus_dp_dpt_1|title=Philip Magee speaks about producing Kodaline, The Script & More|publisher=[Air // Million]|access-date=8 August 2015}}{{Dead link|date=February 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> "All I Want" was selected as [[BBC Radio 1]] DJ [[Fearne Cotton]]'s ''Record of the Week'', featured in the season 9 episode of ''[[Grey's Anatomy (season 9)|Grey's Anatomy]]'', "[[Remember the Time (Grey's Anatomy)|Remember the Time]]",<ref name="guardian">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/oct/04/kodaline-new-band|title=New Band of the Day: Kodaline|publisher=The Guardian|date=4 October 2012|access-date=28 December 2012}}</ref> and was used in the background music for [[Google]]'s ''2012: Year in Review'' video. On 9 December 2012, the [[BBC]] announced that Kodaline had been nominated for the [[Sound of...|Sound of 2013]] poll.<ref name="so13">{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p010q6bc/profiles/kodaline|title=Sound of 2013 Profiles: Kodaline|publisher=BBC|date=9 December 2012|access-date=10 December 2012}}</ref> On 17 June 2013, Kodaline released their first studio album, ''[[In a Perfect World (Kodaline album)|In a Perfect World]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/kodaline-busking-their-way-back-from-reality-8638432.html|title=Kodaline: Busking their way back from reality|publisher=[[The Independent]]|date=31 May 2013|access-date=18 June 2013}}</ref> ''In a Perfect World'' includes the singles "[[High Hopes (Kodaline song)|High Hopes]]", "[[All I Want (Kodaline song)|All I Want]]", "[[Love Like This (Kodaline song)|Love Like This]]", and "Pray", as well as seven new songs. The [[iTunes]] deluxe edition of the album contains "The Answer", "Perfect World", "Lose Your Mind", and "[[Latch (song)|Latch]]", with music videos for "All I Want" and "High Hopes". The CD deluxe edition, instead, contains a CD of live performances of "All I Want", "High Hopes", "Love Like This", "Pray", "All Comes Down", and "The Answer" at The Button Factory in Dublin. Their song "All I Want" was featured on the [[MTV]] show ''[[Catfish: The TV Show]]''. "All I Want" was also in Season 5, episode 10 of ''[[The Vampire Diaries]]''. The song "Brand New Day" from this album, is currently used as the theme tune for the hit [[Channel 4]] show ''[[Gogglebox]]''. In April 2014, Kodaline released a [[cover version]] of [[Paul McCartney]]'s 1980 single "[[Coming Up (song)|Coming Up]]" in celebration of [[Record Store Day]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indieshuffle.com/paul-mccartney-coming-up-kodaline-cover/ |title=Paul McCartney – Coming Up (Kodaline Cover) |publisher=Indieshuffle.com|access-date=14 April 2014}}</ref> In April 2014, the song "All I Want" was released as part of the soundtrack for ''[[The Fault in Our Stars (film)|The Fault in Our Stars]]'', as well as used in the film. In July 2014, the songs "Pray" and "High Hopes" were featured in trailers for the films ''[[Horns (film)|Horns]]'' and ''[[Love, Rosie (film)|Love, Rosie]]'', respectively.<ref>{{cite news|title=Osheaga 2014: CBC's top picks for acts you don't want to miss|url=http://www.cbc.ca/m/touch/canada/montreal/story/1.2724834|website=CBC|access-date=5 August 2014|date=1 August 2014}}</ref> The song "All I Want" was used in ''TCM Remembers'', [[Turner Classic Movies]]' annual remembrance of film industry contributors who died in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tcm.com/mediaroom/video/1054520/TCM-Remembers-2014-TCM-Original-.html |title=Thank You For Signing Up! |website=Tcm.com |access-date=2015-12-07}}</ref>

===2014–17: ''Coming Up for Air''===
In December 2014, the band announced their second LP ''[[Coming Up for Air (Kodaline album)|Coming Up for Air]]'', and released a song called "Honest" on YouTube. A few days later they also published the song "Unclear". Following its release on 9 February 2015, the album entered the [[UK Albums Chart]] at number 4. "The One" and "Honest" both reached Top 40 in the [[UK Singles Chart]].{{citation needed|date=December 2015}} Lead singer Steve Garrigan appeared on episode 18 of ''The Midnight Hour''.

===2017: ''Politics of Living''===
The lead single "[[Brother (Kodaline song)|Brother]]" was released on 23 June 2017. Garrigan once again appeared on The Midnight Hour alongside Eldeniro90 where he premiered new music. The group also released a single, 'Follow Your Fire', on 23 March 2018 which was a massive hit in Romania reaching number 7 on the charts. "Shed a Tear" was released as the third single from the album on 15 June 2018. "Worth It" was released as the fourth single from the album on 20 July 2018. "[[Head Held High]]" was released as the fifth single from the album on 24 August 2018. The song peaked at number sixty-eight on the [[Irish Singles Chart]]. The album titled ''[[Politics of Living]]'' was released on 28 September 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/Kodaline/status/1022438033554120705|title=Kodaline on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=2018-07-31|language=en}}</ref>

===2020: ''One Day at a Time''===
On 10 January 2020, the band released "[[Wherever You Are (Kodaline song)|Wherever You Are]]" as the lead single from their fourth studio album. The song peaked at number sixty-six on the [[Irish Singles Chart]]. "[[Sometimes (Kodaline song)|Sometimes]]" was released as the bands second single from their fourth studio album on 6 March 2020. The song peaked at number ninety-four on the [[Irish Singles Chart]]. The album titled ''[[One Day at a Time (Kodaline album)|One Day at a Time]]'' was released on 12 June 2020. The album peaked at number two on the [[Irish Albums Chart]]. In November 2020, the band released the single "This Must Be Christmas".
