'''Billy Straus''' is an American music producer and songwriter. He is known for his work in children's television including the [[Disney]] series ''[[Little Einsteins]],'' ''[[Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego? (album)|Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego?]],'' the two [[PBS]] children's [[computer-animated]] television series ''[[Bob the Builder]]'' and ''[[WordWorld]].'' He produced and mixed original Broadway cast albums for ''[[The Full Monty (musical)|The Full Monty]]'' and ''[[Dirty Rotten Scoundrels: Original Broadway Cast Recording|Dirty Rotten Scoundrels]]''. He won an [[Daytime Emmy Award|Emmy Award]] for his work on ''[[Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego? (album)|Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego?]]'' He founded Rock River Communications to introduce the concept of non-traditional music distribution into the retail marketplace.<ref name="Duhigg">{{Cite news |url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2005-nov-22-fi-rockriver22-story.html |title=With Custom CDs, Retailers Hear Cash Registers Jingle |date=22 November 2005 |first=Charles |last=Duhigg |work=Los Angeles Times |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210306045550/https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2005-nov-22-fi-rockriver22-story.html |archive-date=6 March 2021}}</ref>

==Career==

Straus was raised in New York City.<ref name="Kahn">{{Cite news |url=http://hostcolony.com/websound/globe_article.html |title=Mastering the moods |date=September 14, 2002 |publisher=The Boston Globe |first=Joseph P. |last=Kahn }}{{dead link|date=July 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> While enrolled at Brown University in 1978, Straus started working as a recording engineer, recording live albums and broadcasts for artists such as [[George Jones]], [[Miles Davis]], [[Grateful Dead]] and [[Joni Mitchell]].<ref name="Kahn"/> He was a member of the band Redline during the early 1980s and toured with [[U2]] in 1981.<ref name="Kahn"/><ref name="Adler">{{Cite web |url=https://money.cnn.com/magazines/fsb/fsb_archive/2001/10/01/311236/ |title=Billy Straus Rock River Communications Founder |first=Carlye |last=Adler |date=1 October 2001 |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref>

Straus worked as an engineer at [[The Hit Factory]] recording studio in Manhattan, working with artists including [[Bruce Springsteen]] and [[Julian Lennon]].<ref name="Kahn"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://albumlinernotes.com/Valotte__1984_.html |title=Valotte (1984) |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref> In 1985 he started ''The Manhattan Recording Company'',<ref name="Kahn"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.belafontetracks.ca/albums_A38-A44%20(1977-01).htm |title=Albums 1977-2001 |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.discogs.com/label/281020-Manhattan-Recording-Company |title=Manhattan Recording Company |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref> where he created The Manhattan Jazz Hour radio series, hosted by ''[[The New York Times]]'' jazz critic, [[John S. Wilson (music critic)|John S. Wilson]], and syndicated nationally by [[American Public Radio]]. Straus also composed and produced jingles for [[Miller Brewing Company]] and [[Mars, Incorporated]].<ref name="Kahn"/><ref name="Eckel">{{Cite news |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1876&dat=20010810&id=ziUfAAAAIBAJ&pg=3496,3226209 |title=Shopping gets a soundtrack |first=Mike |last=Eckel |date=10 August 2001 |publisher=Herald-Journal |access-date=4 June 2014 |page=A13}}</ref> He produced for the [[a cappella]] musical group [[Rockapella]] in the late 1980s and 1990s <ref name="Change">{{Cite web |url=http://mudcat.org/thread.cfm?threadid=16851 |title=Lyr/Chords Req: Change in My Life (Billy Straus) |access-date=4 June 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140607044234/http://mudcat.org/thread.cfm?threadid=16851 |archive-date=7 June 2014 }}</ref> including two albums spun off from the PBS television series, ''Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego?'' for which he won an Emmy Award.<ref name="Kahn"/>

Straus founded [[Rock River Music|Rock River Communications]] in 1995 in Manhattan.<ref name="Duhigg"/><ref name="Harvey">{{Cite web |url=http://www.brownalumnimagazine.com/content/view/1261/40/ |title=Music to Spend By |first=Charlotte Bruce |last=Harvey |access-date=4 June 2014 |publisher=Brown Alumni Magazine}}</ref> The company later moved to Brattleboro, Vermont and also has an office in California.<ref name="Kahn"/><ref name="Adler"/> Rock River was an innovator in non-traditional music distribution, creating music collections for sale through non-music retailers.<ref name="Harvey"/> Rock River's first such music collection, ''Pottery Barn: A Cool Christmas'', was released in 1995.<ref name="Kahn"/> Straus subsequently produced 44 additional collections for Pottery Barn and, through the Rock River label, released over 124 compilations.<ref name="Kahn"/> Straus produced ''Street Mix'' for [[Volkswagen]] as well as music for other retailers such as [[Gap Inc.]], [[W Hotels]], [[Jaguar Cars]] and [[Jose Cuervo]].<ref name="Duhigg"/><ref name="Kahn"/>

Straus has composed and produced music for children's television series on [[Nickelodeon]], PBS and [[Walt Disney Television]],<ref name="Kahn"/> including ''[[Dora the Explorer]]'', ''[[Out of the Box (TV series)|Out of the Box]]'' and [[Gullah Gullah Island]].<ref name="Biography"/><ref name="NYT"/><ref name="Sendra">{{Cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/dora-the-explorer-mw0000404718 |title=Review by Tim Sendra: Dora the Explorer |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref><ref name="Gullah1">{{Cite web |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1839001/fullcredits?ref_=tt_cl_sm#cast |title=Gullah, Gullah Island (TV Series) |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref><ref name="Gullah2">{{Cite web |url=http://www.paleycenter.org/collection/item/?q=see&p=197&item=T:57009 |title=Gullah Gullah Island (TV) |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref> Straus wrote the songs for the animated television series, ''[[Little Einsteins]]'', in 2005,<ref name="Biography">{{Cite web |url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0833759/bio?ref_=nm_ov_bio_sm |title=Billy Straus Biography |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref><ref name="Disney">{{Cite book |title=The Disney Song Encyclopedia |first1=Thomas S. |last1=Hischak |first2=Mark A. |last2=Robinson |publisher=Scarecrow Press |date=29 July 2009 |page=121 |isbn=9780810869387}}</ref> ''[[Bob the Builder]]'' and ''[[WordWorld]]''.<ref name="Putney">{{Cite news |url=http://www.reformer.com/headlines/ci_7425051 |title=Putney kids sing theme for new PBS series |first=Howard |last=Weiss-Tisman |publisher=Battleboro Informer |date=10 November 2007}}</ref>

In 2003, Straus wrote music and lyrics for ''Rock Odyssey'', a musical adaptation of [[Homer]]'s ''[[Odyssey]]''.<ref name="Kahn"/><ref name="Odyssey">{{Cite web |url=http://www.stephencolewriter.com/#!rock-odyssey/cdje |title=Rock Odyssey |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref> His song, ''Change in My Life'', originally recorded by Rockapella, appeared in the Steve Martin film, ''[[Leap of Faith (film)|Leap of Faith]]'', in 1992.<ref name="Kahn"/><ref name="Change"/> In 2007, Straus served as executive producer of ''Roots, Rock, Remixed'', a collection of remixes of reggae legend [[Bob Marley]]'s earliest recordings,<ref name="Marley">{{Cite web |url=http://music.msn.com/music/album-credits/bob-marley/roots-rock-remixed.1/ |title=Roots, Rock, Remixed:Credits |access-date=4 June 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140606205505/http://music.msn.com/music/album-credits/bob-marley/roots-rock-remixed.1/ |archive-date=6 June 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and two volumes of ''Christmas Remixed: Holiday Classics Re-Grooved''.<ref name="Remixed">{{Cite web |url=http://www.artistdirect.com/nad/store/artist/album/0,,3405524,00.html |title=Christmas Remixed, Vol. 2 |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/release/six-degrees-collection-christmas-remixed-holiday-mr0000317911/credits |title=Six Degrees Collection: Christmas Remixed-Holiday |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref>

In 1999 Straus launched Websound to provide internet radio for websites.<ref name="Kahn"/><ref name="Harvey"/>

He is a co-founder of the non-profit, Next Stage Arts Project.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.rutlandherald.com/article/20131209/NEWS/312099999/1004 |title=Putney film series offers popcorn and psychology |first=Kevin |last=O'Connor |date=9 December 2013 |publisher=Rutland Herald |access-date=2014-06-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140606234758/http://www.rutlandherald.com/article/20131209/NEWS/312099999/1004 |archive-date=2014-06-06 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Awards and recognition==
In 2001 Straus and collaborator [[David Yazbek]] were nominated for a [[Grammy Award]] for producing the original Broadway cast album, ''The Full Monty'', in the category of Best Musical Show Album.<ref name="Harvey"/><ref name="Billboard">{{Cite news |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=wA8EAAAAMBAJ&q=Billy+Straus&pg=PA21 |publisher=Billboard |date=26 January 2002 |title=Congratulation |volume=114 |number=4 |page=21}}</ref> ''Dirty Rotten Scoundrels'', which Straus also produced with Yazbek, was also nominated for a Grammy Award in the same category in 2005.<ref name="Gans">{{Cite web |url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/97809-Grammy-Awards-Presented-Feb-8-on-CBS-TV |title=Grammy Awards Presented Feb. 8 on CBS-TV |first=Andrew |last=Gans |date=8 February 2006 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140606215601/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/97809-Grammy-Awards-Presented-Feb-8-on-CBS-TV |archive-date=6 June 2014 }}</ref>

Straus won an [[Daytime Emmy Award|Emmy Award]] for Outstanding Achievement in Live and Direct To Tape Sound Mixing for ''Where in the World Is Carmen Sandiego?'' in 1996.<ref name="Duhigg"/><ref name="Winner">{{Cite web |url=http://awardsandwinners.com/winner/?name=billy-straus&mid=/m/0n43917 |title=Billy Straus |access-date=4 June 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Straus currently lives with his family in [[Vermont]]. He is a volunteer emergency medical technician.<ref name="billystrausmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.billystrausmusic.com/bio.php|publisher=billystrausmusic.com|title=Billy Straus Music: Billy|access-date=4 December 2016}}</ref> He is the brother-in-law of economist and policy maker [[Paul Weinstein (economist)|Paul Weinstein]].
