'''Charlton Kenneth Jeffrey Howard''' (born 17 August 2003), professionally known as <!--please do NOT capitalise "the" in accordance with [[MOS:NICKNAMETHE]]-->'''the Kid Laroi''' (stylised '''The Kid LAROI.'''), is an Australian singer, rapper and songwriter. Born in [[Waterloo, New South Wales]], he originally gained recognition from his association and friendship with [[Juice Wrld]] while he was on tour in Australia.

He gained a local following before joining a partnership agreement with [[Lil Bibby]]'s [[Grade A Productions]] and [[Columbia Records]].<ref name="billboard-glicksman" /> and attained further popularity with his song "[[Go (The Kid Laroi and Juice Wrld song)|Go]]" featuring Juice Wrld, which peaked at number 52 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His debut mixtape ''[[F*ck Love]]'' (2020) peaked at number one on the Australian [[ARIA Charts]], making him the youngest Australian solo artist ever to reach the top, and also reached number one on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://texxandthecity.com/2020/12/meet-the-kid-laroi-the-17-year-old-australian-hip-hop-prodigy-poised-to-take-the-world-by-storm-with-his-debut-album/|title=Meet The Kid LAROI: the 17-year-old Australian hip hop prodigy poised to take the world by storm with his debut album|date=14 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nit.com.au/rap-prodigy-the-kid-laroi-continues-rise-to-fame-with-debut-mixtape/|title=Rap prodigy The Kid LAROI continues rise to fame with debut mixtape|first=Grace|last=Crivellaro|date=20 August 2020}}</ref> Additionally, Laroi's songs "[[Without You (The Kid Laroi song)|Without You]]", its remix with [[Miley Cyrus]], and "[[Stay (The Kid Laroi and Justin Bieber song)|Stay]]" (with [[Justin Bieber]]) reached the top ten of the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, with "Stay" peaking at number one for seven non-consecutive weeks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9612247/kid-laroi-justin-bieber-stay-number-one-hot-100|title = The Kid LAROI & Justin Bieber's 'Stay' Hits No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100}}</ref>

==Career==
=== Early years ===
Howard started out recording raps over beats on his mother's phone and uploading them to SoundCloud.<ref name=":17">{{Cite web|title=The Kid LAROI Talks 'Go' Collab With 'Big Brother' Juice WRLD & Relationship-Fueled 'F--k Love' Project|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9403374/the-kid-laroi-go-love-big-brother/|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=14 April 2020|title=At Just 16 Years Old, The Kid Laroi Is Shaking Up SoundCloud Rap|url=https://studybreaks.com/culture/music/kid-laroi-2/|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Study Breaks|language=en-us}}</ref> In an interview with [[Triple J]], Howard stated that the first rap name he gave himself was "FC6".<ref name=":21">{{Citation|title=Meet The Kid LAROI, 14 with a dream|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cpEViD7CR5c|language=en|access-date=16 August 2021}}</ref> In 2015, Howard formed the duo "Dream$Team" with [[Adelaide]] rapper DJ Marcus Jr. (Aka LadyKiller) who became his mentor and support.<ref name=":11" /> The two recorded songs together and performed to local audiences as DJ Marcus Jr. guided him through promoting, recording and developing buzz.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Jrr|first=Marcus|date=2021|title=Instagram - Laroi performing from Marcus Jn archives|url=https://www.inshttps://www.instagram.com/p/B_xj8FuJiKm/tagram.com/accounts/login/|access-date=11 August 2021|website=www.instagram.com}}</ref><ref name=":11" /> Howard's mother, Sloane, created "Mama Capone Management" to promote the duo and her son's career and he was often referred to as "Charlton Laroi" in her promotions.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Management|first=Mama Capone|date=2021|title=Mama Capone Management|url=https://www.facebook.com/mamacaponemanagement/|access-date=11 August 2021|website=www.facebook.com|language=en}}</ref>

Howard met his now collaborator, Grammy-nominated producer [[Khaled Rohaim]] at a recording studio in Sydney.<ref name=":8" /> Moved by Howard's talent and difficult living situation, Rohaim would pick Howard up from various houses that he would live in around Sydney so they could eat together and record at his rented studio in North Strathfield.<ref name=":8" /> Rohaim gave Howard some work writing songs for other artists.<ref name=":16" /> In one instance, he wrote a song that featured [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie|A Boogie]] and Howard snuck into the studio so he could meet him, eventually recording a song together.<ref name=":16" />

In 2017, Howard was signed to a development deal with [[Sony Music Australia]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Moran|first=Robert|date=13 August 2020|title=The Kid Laroi, the pride of Waterloo, takes on the world|url=https://www.smh.com.au/culture/music/the-kid-laroi-the-pride-of-waterloo-takes-on-the-world-20200813-p55lff.html|access-date=10 August 2021|website=The Sydney Morning Herald|language=en}}</ref> In the same year he was a co-host at the Fernside Festival hosted by Weave Youth and Community Services.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Weave Youth & Community Services|url=https://www.facebook.com/WeaveYouthFamily/photos/a.169210533129379/1447195535330866/?type=3|access-date=11 August 2021|website=www.facebook.com|language=en}}</ref>

Howard's accent and heavy use of American colloquial terms has been questioned<ref name=":18">{{Cite web|date=17 February 2021|title=Kid Laroi explains why American fans are confused by his unique accent|url=https://oltnews.com/kid-laroi-explains-why-american-fans-are-confused-by-his-unique-accent|access-date=13 August 2021|website=OLTNEWS|language=en-US}}</ref> since he has only been living there since 2019.<ref name=":4" /><ref name=":17" /> In an interview with [[Zach Sang]], he explained that he has many friends from Chicago and to make himself understood he often tweaks phrases and adapts as they do not understand many Australian words.<ref name=":16" /><ref name=":18" /> In interviews as far back as 2018, it can be easily seen by Australian native speakers that he was mimicking American rapper terms using phrases not used in Australian colloquialism such as "We don't got no money".<ref name=":19" /><ref name=":20" />

On 9 August 2021, Howard noted in an Instagram post that he was recovering from [[COVID-19]] and had been in isolation for a week.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=The Kid LAROI Celebrates 'Stay' Topping Hot 100 & Being 'COVID Free'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9612425/the-kid-laroi-justin-bieber-react-stay-number-1-billboard-hot-100|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> The post was live for a short period before it was edited to remove that information and Howard tweeted a video in which he stated he was quarantining with his girlfriend as they both had the virus. During the video he received an update to say he was now COVID-19 free.<ref name=":0" />

Fashion has been a big part of Howard's transformation from [[Redfern, New South Wales|Redfern]] resident to international star. Howard explained to Spout Podcast that in Australia he did not have a lot of money and was wearing a lot of sport tracksuits.<ref name="Hickman">{{Cite web|last=Hickman|first=Jamontae|title=Tie-Dye It up Like The Kid Laroi|url=https://www.askmen.com/style/fashion_advice/style-defined-celebrity-edition-the-kid-laroi.html|access-date=10 August 2021|website=AskMen|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":9" /> Since moving to California, Howard's style has switched to high end designers such as [[Celine (brand)|Celine]],<ref name="Hickman"/> [[Louis Vuitton]], [[Comme des Garçons]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Kid LAROI, Lil Mosey 'WRONG' Music Video|url=https://incorporatedstyle.com/video/the-kid-laroi-lil-mosey-wrong-music-video/|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Incorporated Style|language=en-US}}</ref> and is known for his love of knitted jumpers.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2 August 2021|title=Rosalía Takes The Lead In This Week's Looks Of The Week|url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2021/08/02/looks-of-the-week-rosalia-the-kid-laroi-zayn-malik/|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Wonderland|language=en-US}}</ref> Howard has featured in style editorials for magazines such as ''[[Flaunt]]''<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Kid LAROI {{!}} The Next Chapter Reads Lift Off|url=https://flaunt.com/content/the-kid-laroi-the-future-experience|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Flaunt Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> and was on the cover of ''[[Wonderland (magazine)|Wonderland]]'' magazine in Autumn 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=23 July 2021|title=Wearing Fendi, The Kid Laroi Covers Our Autumn 21 Issue|url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2021/07/23/the-kid-laroi-autumn-21/|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Wonderland|language=en-US}}</ref>
