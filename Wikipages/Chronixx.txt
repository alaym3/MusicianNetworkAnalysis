'''Jamar Rolando McNaughton''' (born 10 October 1992),<ref name="Chronixx Biography">{{cite web|url=http://chronixx.com#about|accessdate=2020-05-18|title=Chronixx Biography}}</ref> popularly known as '''Chronixx''', is a Jamaican [[reggae]] artist. His stage name replaced the name "Little Chronicle" which he was given because of his father, the singer "Chronicle".<ref name="Jackson1404">Jackson, Kevin (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Chronixx-tops-Billboard-s-reggae-chart_16457582 Chronixx tops Billboard’s reggae chart]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 12 April 2014. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref><ref name="Jeffries">Jeffries, David "[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/chronixx-mn0002996745/biography Chronixx Biography]", [[Allmusic]]. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref> Chronixx and his music has been branded as a "Reggae Revival", alongside other reggae musicians including [[Alborosie]], Dre Island, Jah Bouks, [[Jah9]], [[Protoje]], Kelissa, [[Jesse Royal (musician)|Jesse Royal]], Keznamdi and Kabaka Pyramid. His lyrical content revolves around themes of anti-war, romantic declarations and resiliency.<ref name="Meschino">Meschino, Patricia (2013) "[http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/global/5733093/is-chronixx-jamaican-reggaes-next-big-thing-chris-blackwell-diplo Is Chronixx Jamaican Reggae's Next Big Thing? Chris Blackwell, Diplo Think So]", ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', 25 September 2013. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref><ref name="Bakare">Bakare, Lanre (2013) "[https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/oct/11/chronixx-roots-reggae Chronixx puts Rastafarianism back into Jamaican reggae]", ''[[The Guardian]]'', 11 October 2013. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref>

==Early life==
Jamar began songwriting at an early age and his father Selvin McNaughton, known professionally as "Chronicle", began to nurture his musical aspirations.<ref name="Jackson1404" />
McNaughton wrote his first song, "Rice Grain", at the age of 5.<ref name="Henry">Henry, Krista (2012) "[http://jamaica-gleaner.com/gleaner/20120115/ent/ent5.html Chronixx Begins Doing His Part]", ''[[Jamaica Gleaner]]'', 15 January 2012. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref> During an interview with Okay Player Chronixx spoke on his early life:
<blockquote>
"My music come from early beginnings, from childhood days. I used to sing at school, in church, and then my whole family sings. My daddy, Chronicle, caused me to be very exposed to music from a very tender age. That’s where the music started for me. Professionally now, that’s when I was in high school. I started producing. Making riddims. But the music go from then until now. When I was 15, 16 I started producing and it was a great vibe for me."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.okayplayer.com/interviews/new-reggae-artist-chronixx-interview.html|title=LargeUp Exclusive: Chronixx Interview|work=Okayplayer}}</ref>
</blockquote>
He attended St Catherine High School in [[Spanish Town]].<ref name="Jackson1404" /> He started his recording career at the age of 11, recording a Gospel track with producer Danny Browne (which was not released), and went on to provide harmony vocals for artists such as [[Lutan Fyah]] as well as beginning production work, composing rhythms used by artists such as [[Konshens]], [[Popcaan]], and [[Munga Hornourebel]] for Mavrick Records.<ref name="Jackson1404" /><ref name="Henry" /> At the age of 14, he began producing and building riddims, including the Freezer riddim for Ice Box Records.<ref name="Jackson1404" /> In 2009 Chronixx's brother died which led to him writing with Romaine 'Teflon' Arnett of Zincfence Records.<ref name="Jackson1404" />

==Career==
Chronixx popularity grew throughout 2012, with significant airplay in Jamaica, and performances at festivals such as [[Reggae Sumfest]] and a show in December at the Tracks and Records nightclub/restaurant in Kingston, which was attended by [[Usain Bolt]].<ref name="Meschino"/> He was featured on the [[Major Lazer]]-curated mixtape ''Start a Fire''.<ref name="ok">"[http://www.okayplayer.com/news/major-lazer-presents-chronixx-walshy-fire-mixtape-download.html LargeUp Premiere: Major Lazer Presents Chronixx & Walshy Fire &#91;Mixtape&#93;]", [[okayplayer]]. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref>
In 2013, he had hits with "Smile Jamaica" and "Here Comes Trouble", and he toured the [[United Kingdom]] (including a [[BBC 1Xtra]] concert in [[Leeds]]) and the United States with his Zincfence Redemption Band.<ref>Henry, Davina (2013) "[http://jamaica-gleaner.com/gleaner/20131106/ent/ent2.html US, UK Tours Open Chronixx's Eyes]", ''[[Jamaica Gleaner]]'', 6 November 2013. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref><ref name="Johnson">Johnson, Richard (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Chronixx-carries-the-torch_15991109 Chronixx carries the torch]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 10 February 2014. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref><ref name="JamO1403">"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Chronixx-makes-outdoor-debut-in-New-York-s--Groovin- Chronixx makes outdoor debut in New York's 'Groovin']", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 7 March 2014. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref> In March 2013, he travelled to [[Kenya]], where he has a large fanbase, as a Peace Ambassador during the country's general election, and performed at the Tuko Rada Peace Concert in [[Nairobi]].<ref>"[http://96mixx.com/en/2013/03/22/jamaican-reggae-artiste-chronixx-named-peace-ambassador-to-kenya/?cbg_tz=-60 Chronixx Named Peace Ambassador to Kenya] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140512225122/http://96mixx.com/en/2013/03/22/jamaican-reggae-artiste-chronixx-named-peace-ambassador-to-kenya/?cbg_tz=-60 |date=12 May 2014 }}", 96mixx.com, 22 March 2013. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref> He again performed at Sumfest in 2013, in front of an audience of more than 10,000.<ref name="Dreisinger">Dreisinger, Baz (2013) "[https://www.npr.org/2013/12/29/256116848/jamaica-s-hottest-new-school-reggae-artists-return-to-roots Jamaica's Hottest New-School Reggae Artists Return To Roots]", [[NPR]], 29 December 2013. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref>

His [[Extended play|EP]], ''Dread & Terrible'', was released on 1 April 2014, and topped the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard charts|Top Reggae Albums]] charts on the week of 18 April.<ref name="Jackson1404" /><ref name="Meschino1407">Meschino, Patricia (2014) "[http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6203717/chronixx-tops-reggae-album-chart-tonight-show-central-park Chronixx Tops Reggae Album Chart Following 'Tonight Show' & Central Park Performances]", ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', 31 July 2014. Retrieved 1 August 2014</ref> Following his appearance on the Jimmy Fallon show and his Central Park concert, the EP saw significant gains, according to Nielsen SoundScan, earning a number two position on the Digital Reggae Singles chart.<ref name="Meschino" /> The EP returned to the number one spot on the [[Billboard charts|Top Reggae Albums]] chart, according to SoundScan.<ref name="Meschino" /> By March 2015, the EP had spent 42 consecutive weeks in the top 10 of the Top Reggae Albums chart.<ref>"[http://jamaica-gleaner.com/article/entertainment/20150323/chronixx-ep-enjoys-42nd-week-reggae-billboard-top-ten Chronixx EP Enjoys 42nd Week On Reggae Billboard Top Ten]", ''[[Jamaica Gleaner]]'', 23 March 2015. Retrieved 25 March 2015</ref>

In May 2014, he won Culture Artist of the Year, Best New Artist, and Entertainer of the Year at the Linkage Awards in New York.<ref name="Linkage">"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Chronixx-tops-Linkage-Awards_16614014 Chronixx, I-Octane top Linkage Awards]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 7 May 2014. Retrieved 10 May 2014</ref> In July 2014, he performed on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' on US network [[NBC]].<ref>Jackson, Kevin (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Here-comes-CHRONIXX_17228621 Here Comes Chronixx]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 24 July 2014. Retrieved 24 July 2014</ref> He followed this with a concert in [[Central Park]] attended by over 5,000 people.<ref>Campbell, Howard (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Jagger-comes-out-for-Chronixx_17258516 Jagger comes out for Chronixx]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 29 July 2014. Retrieved 30 July 2014</ref> In September 2014, he was nominated for a [[MOBO Award]] in the Best Reggae Act category.<ref name="MOBO">Jackson, Kevin (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/5-for-MOBO-REGGAE 5 For MOBO Reggae]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 24 September 2014. Retrieved 27 September 2014</ref> The following month he won awards for Best Song (for "Smile Jamaica") and Best Music Video (for "Here Comes Trouble") at the 33rd International Reggae & World Music Awards.<ref>"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Chronixx-takes-two-IRAWMA_17706701 Chronixx takes two IRAWMA]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 8 October 2014. Retrieved 9 October 2014</ref> He founded his own ZincFence Recordz production house along with producer Romain "Teflon" Arnett and co-producer/engineer Ricardo "Shadyz" Lynch.<ref name="Meschino" /> As well as Chronixx, ZincFence has produced hits by [[Jah Cure]], Kabaka Pyramid, and [[Protoje]], and had worked with [[Maverick Sabre]], [[Mavado (singer)|Mavado]], and Nomaddz.<ref>Jackson, Kevin (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Behind-the-zinc-fence_16677311 Behind the Zinc Fence]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 16 May 2014. Retrieved 19 May 2014</ref>

In January 2015, Chronixx appeared on "Belly of the Beast" a song which featured on American rapper [[Joey Badass]] debut album [[B4.Da.$$]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00MHS5AVM/ | title=Amazon.com: Joey Bada$$: B4.DA.$$ (LP): Music | publisher=[[Amazon Music]] | accessdate=9 January 2015}}</ref>
In June 2015, Chronixx performed at the Glastonbury Festival.{{citation needed|date=May 2020}}

His 2017 debut album, [[Chronology (Chronixx album)|''Chronology'']], received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination, and in the same year he received a Prime Minister's National Youth Award for Excellence.<ref>"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/splash/chronixx-cops_119354?profile=1116 Chronixx cops PM youth award]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 8 December 2017. Retrieved 9 December 2017</ref>

On 13 March 2020, Chronixx released the first single "Dela Move" from his upcoming second album, "Dela Splash". The title of the track and album were inspired by his hometown – De La Vega City, a district in [[Spanish Town]], Jamaica.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2020/03/13/music/chronixx-debuts-new-song-dela-move-off-his-upcoming-album-dela-splash.html|title=Chronixx Debuts New Song 'Dela Move' Off His Upcoming Album 'Dela Splash'|date=13 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/style/2020/03/chronixx-dela-splash-interview|title=Exclusive Preview: Chronixx’s Second Album, Dela Splash|date=13 March 2020}}</ref>
