'''Hermannus Jantinus van Veen''' (born 14 March 1945) is a Dutch stage performer, actor, author, singer-songwriter and musician. He worked with [[Accompaniment|accompanists]] [[Laurens van Rooyen]] and [[Erik van der Wurff]], both were pianist annex composer.

In addition to performing internationally, van Veen is famous as the creator of ''[[Alfred J. Kwak]]'' (1976). The original one-man theatre show was adapted as a Dutch-German-Japanese cartoon (1989), which was broadcast internationally. In addition to creating the show's story, van Veen also wrote, composed and performed most of the show's music.<ref>[http://www.hermanvanveen.com/Herman-van-Veen/en-US/biography/biography.aspx Official English profile] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081231152309/http://www.hermanvanveen.com/Herman-van-Veen/en-US/biography/biography.aspx |date=31 December 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title= Herman Van Who?|work= Chicago Tribune|date=11 November 1990|url= http://articles.chicagotribune.com/1990-11-11/entertainment/9004030396_1_herman-dario-fo-resists|access-date=2010-10-19|first=Matt|last=Wolf}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title= THE STAGE: VAN VEEN'S ONE-MAN SHOW|work= The New York Times|date=9 December 1982|url= https://www.nytimes.com/1982/12/09/theater/the-stage-van-veen-s-one-man-show.html|access-date=2010-10-19|first=Frank|last=Rich}}</ref>

He also performed the voice-overs for the characters Johan Sebastian Kwak, Krabnagel de Kater and Professor Paljas (Professor Buffon) in the Dutch and German versions of ''Alfred J. Kwak''.
