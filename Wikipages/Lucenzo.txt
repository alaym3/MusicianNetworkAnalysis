'''Luís Filipe Fraga Oliveira''' ({{IPA-pt|luˈiʃ fɨˈlip(ɨ) ˈfɾaɣɐ ɔliˈvɐjɾɐ|eu}}; born 27 May 1983),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmjornal.xl.pt/detalhe/noticias/lazer/musica/kuduro-conquista--palcos-e-youtube-com-video |title=Kuduro conquista  palcos e YouTube (COM VÍDEO) |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100929182329/http://www.cmjornal.xl.pt/detalhe/noticias/lazer/musica/kuduro-conquista--palcos-e-youtube-com-video |archivedate=29 September 2010 }}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Lucenzo''', is a Portuguese–French [[reggaeton]] recording artist and record producer. His parents emigrated to [[France]], from [[Portugal]]. Lucenzo signed with the [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music]] record label. He is best known for his dance hit with rapper [[Big Ali]], "[[Vem Dançar Kuduro|Vem dançar Kuduro]]". [[Puerto Rico|Puerto Rican]] [[reggaeton]] artist [[Don Omar]] released a Spanish/Portuguese version of the song under the title "[[Danza Kuduro]]" with Lucenzo.

==Career==
Lucenzo released his debut single in 2008, a Portuguese/[[Multilingualism|multilingual]] [[reggae]]/[[reggaeton]] song entitled "Emigrante del mundo".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XvnpyxmEq2I |title=Audio of "Emigrante del mundo" the debut single by Lucenzo |work=Youtube |date=6 September 2007 |accessdate=1 May 2011}}</ref> Signed to Scopio Music, the song became a Portuguese radio hit.  It was followed in 2009 by "Dame reggaeton".<ref>[http://www.jukebo.fr/lucenzo/artistbio.html JukeBo: Lucenzo biography] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100503133257/http://www.jukebo.fr/lucenzo/artistbio.html |date=3 May 2010 }} (in Portuguese)</ref> But it was with his bilingual English/[[Portuguese language|Portuguese]] dance hit "Vem Dançar Kuduro"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=a-MdGLodXUw |title=Music video for "Vem Dançar Kuduro" by Lucenzo featuring Big Ali |work=Youtube |date=7 February 2010 |accessdate=1 May 2011}}</ref> in collaboration with [[Big Ali]] produced by Lucenzo. It was a Top 40 hit in [[Switzerland]], reaching #31. An alternative Spanish version by [[Don Omar]] with Lucenzo was released on 15 August 2010, entitled "[[Danza Kuduro]]". The accompanying music video was released on 17 August 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7zp1TbLFPp8 |title=Music video for "Danza Kuduro" by Don Omar featuring Lucenzo |work=Youtube |date=11 August 2010 |accessdate=1 May 2011}}</ref> It is also featured on Don Omar's compilation album ''[[Meet the Orphans]]''.<ref>{{cite web|author=Amar Toor |url=http://www.aolradioblog.com/2010/08/19/don-omar-danza-kuduro-lucenzo/ |title=Don Omar, 'Danza Kuduro' feat. Lucenzo – New Song |publisher=AOL Radio Blog |date=19 August 2010 |accessdate=1 May 2011}}</ref>

On July 1, 2015, Lucenzo released a new song "Vida Louca" in Portuguese and reserved to the Lusophone marker. It was part of a series of compilations VIDISCO and available on iTunes Portugal. At the end of July 2015, Lucenzo started promoting his single in Portugal and was invited to several televisions including [[Sociedade Independente de Comunicação|SIC]], [[Rádio e Televisão de Portugal|RTP]], TVi giving interviews where he explained that he wanted to book the premiere of his new work in Portugal before presenting it internationally in Spanish.
