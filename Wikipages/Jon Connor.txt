'''Jon Kevin Freeman Jr.''' (born April 13, 1985), better known by his stage name '''Jon Connor''', is an American [[Rapping|rapper]] and record producer. Connor has released various mixtapes and an album effort during his career including the [[Jay-Z]] inspired ''The Blue Album'', and the [[Eminem]] inspired ''The People's Rapper LP''. His debut studio album, ''[[Unconscious State]]'' was released in 2013. Shortly thereafter, he was signed to [[Dr. Dre]]'s [[Aftermath Entertainment]] from 2013 to 2019, where he did not release an album. In 2015, he garnered notoriety for being featured on Dr. Dre's album ''[[Compton (album)|Compton]]'' (2015) as well as [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]]'s album ''[[The Documentary 2.5]]'' (2015).

== Early life ==
Jon Freeman Jr. was born on April 13, 1985 in [[Flint, Michigan|Flint]], Michigan, the son of Mahley and Jon Freeman Sr. His father, a musician turned [[Christians|Christian]] minister, served as his earliest musical influences. Jon Connor has stated that he grew up listening to [[Genesee County, Michigan|Genesee County]] acts such as [[Top Authority]], [[The Dayton Family]] and [[MC Breed]].<ref name="mlive1">{{cite web |first= William E. III| last= Ketchum |url=http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/flint/index.ssf/2013/10/jon_connor_announces_deal_with.html |title=Jon Connor announces deal with Dr. Dre's Aftermath Entertainment at BET Hip Hop Awards | work= MLive.com |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref>

He graduated from Flint Powers Catholic High School.<ref name="abc2">{{cite web |last=Daunt |first=Josh |url=http://www.abc12.com/story/23702569/flint-rapper-jon-connor-signs-with-aftermath-entertainment |title=Flint rapper Jon Connor signs with Aftermath Entertainment | work= ABC12.com | publisher= ABC 12 – WJRT | location= Flint, Michigan |access-date=3 November 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20131104043118/http://www.abc12.com/story/23702569/flint-rapper-jon-connor-signs-with-aftermath-entertainment |archive-date=November 4, 2013 }}</ref>

== Musical career ==
===As a youth===
Jon began songwriting at age ten. In [[middle school]], he began selling his own mixtapes out of his backpack and from the trunk of his mother's car.  At age 14, with the support of his mother, he founded his own independent record label, All Varsity Music. By 16, he had financed and created an in-house studio with money he earned through odd jobs in the neighborhood.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.hiphopweekly.com/2013/02/11/hhw-artist-of-the-month-jon-connor-to-drop-exclusive-t-shirt-all-star-weekend/ |title=HHW Artist of the Month Jon Connor To Drop Exclusive T-Shirt All Star Weekend!!| work= Hip Hop Weekly Magazine | via= hiphopweekly.com |date= February 11, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130215191431/http://www.hiphopweekly.com/2013/02/11/hhw-artist-of-the-month-jon-connor-to-drop-exclusive-t-shirt-all-star-weekend/ |archive-date=February 15, 2013 }}</ref>

=== 2005–2010: Beginnings and early mixtapes ===
On January 1, 2005, Jon Connor released his first official mixtape, ''The Calling, Pt. 1: The Prequel''.<ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.mtv.com/artists/jon-connor/discography/1238152/ |title=The Calling, Pt. 1: The Prequel [Platinum Edition&#93; by Jon Connor |publisher=MTV |date=January 1, 2005 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> He followed that with another mixtape ''Everybody Hates Connor'' in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/everybody-hates-connor-mw0001090280 |title=Everybody Hates Connor – Jon Connor  | publisher= AllMusic |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref><ref name="hiphopdx1">{{cite web|last=Arnold |first=Paul W |url= http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.13652/title.jon-connor-talks-nas-co-sign-possibly-signing-to-a-major-label |title=Jon Connor Talks Nas' Co-Sign, Possibly Signing to a Major Label  |publisher= HipHop DX |date=January 12, 2011 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> Then he released his third mixtape, ''The Calling Pt. 2: The Second Coming'' in 2008.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/><ref name="uproxx1">{{cite web |author=Beware |url=http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2010/09/download-jon-connor-%E2%80%93-jon-connor-as-vinnie-chase-season-1 |title=Jon Connor Starring As Vinnie Chase| work= The Smoking Section |via= smokingsection.uproxx.com |date=September 29, 2010 |access-date=3 November 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131104144310/http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2010/09/download-jon-connor-%E2%80%93-jon-connor-as-vinnie-chase-season-1 |archive-date=November 4, 2013 }}</ref> In 2009 he would link up with basketball player [[Mateen Cleaves]], then later basketball player [[Jason Richardson]], and Kendall "Young Sav" Freeman, vice president of rapper [[Rick Ross]]' [[Maybach Music Group]] record label to official start a record label. All who currently either run or co-own it.<ref name="mlive2">{{cite web| last= Ketchum| first= William E. III |url= http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/flint/index.ssf/2012/02/jon_connor_talks_concept_favor.html |title=Flint rapper Jon Connor talks concept, favorites of latest mixtape "The Blue Album" (Video) | work= MLive.com |date=February 14, 2012 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| first= Patrick |last= Hayes |url=http://www.mlive.com/news/flint/index.ssf/2009/11/basketball_star_mateen_cleaves.html |title=Basketball star Mateen Cleaves gets into the music business, managing Flint hip-hop artist Jon Connor |work= MLive.com |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> On September 29, 2010, Connor released his fourth mixtape, the [[Entourage (U.S. TV series)|Entourage]]-inspired, ''Jon Connor as Vinnie Chase: Season One''.<ref name="uproxx1"/> ''Jon Connor as Vinnie Chase: Season One'' was produced primarily by Connor and All Varsity Music producer Optiks. Rappers [[Saigon (rapper)|Saigon]] and [[Consequence (rapper)|Consequence]] would be featured on the remix to his song "The Message".<ref name="hiphopdx1"/>

=== 2011: ''Salvation'' and ''Season 2'' ===
On July 12, 2011, Jon Connor released ''Salvation'', a collaboration album with producer Rob "Reef" Tewlow, who has produced for [[50 Cent]] and [[Bad Meets Evil]] in the past.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/salvation/id454667625 |title= Salvation by Jon Connor |publisher= [[iTunes Store]]|date=July 12, 2011 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref><ref name="uproxx2">{{cite web |first=Greg |last=Whitt |url=http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2011/07/review-of-jon-connors-salvation |title="F.L.I. 69" – Review Of Jon Connor's Salvation |work=The Smoking Section |via=smokingsection.uproxx.com |date=July 28, 2011 |access-date=3 November 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131104143951/http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2011/07/review-of-jon-connors-salvation |archive-date=November 4, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The album was met with positive reviews from music critics.<ref name="uproxx2"/><ref name= "hiphopdx2">{{cite web|last=Ketchum |first=William E. |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/album-reviews/id.1716/title.jon-connor-salvation |title=Jon Connor – Salvation &#124; Read Hip Hop Reviews, Rap Reviews & Hip Hop Album Reviews |publisher=HipHop DX |date= July 14, 2011 |access-date= 3 November 2013}}</ref> William E. Ketchum III of HipHopDX stated, "''Salvation'' undeniably showcases a sincerity and hunger that are tough to find in much of Hip Hop today. If Jon Connor continues to build on the skill set he already has, living up to his fictional namesake shouldn't be so far off."<ref name="hiphopdx2"/> Later in 2011, Connor was in talks to sign with [[Def Jam Recordings]], however that deal fell through.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> On December 12, 2011, Connor released his fifth mixtape, a sequel to ''Jon Connor as Vinnie Chase: Season One'', titled ''Season 2''. The mixtape featured guest appearances from [[GLC (rapper)|GLC]] and [[Freeway (rapper)|Freeway]].<ref>{{cite web|author= damaya919 |url=http://rapradar.com/2011/12/12/new-mixtape-jon-connor-season-2/ |title=New Mixtape: Jon Connor Season 2 |publisher=Rap Radar |date=December 12, 2011 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> The mixtape garnered 130,000 downloads on [[Bandcamp]] within three months.<ref name="mlive2"/>

=== 2012: The "Best in the World" series and ''While You Were Sleeping'' ===
On February 14, 2012, Jon Connor released ''The Blue Album'', a mixtape featuring him rapping over various [[Jay-Z]] instrumentals from the three Blueprint albums, ''[[The Blueprint]]'', ''[[The Blueprint 2: The Gift & The Curse]]'' and ''[[The Blueprint 3]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2012/02/jon-connor-drops-new-mixtape-the-blue-album-download-now/ |title=Jon Connor Drops New Mixtape, The Blue Album| work= [[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |date=February 14, 2012 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref><ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web |date= March 15, 2012 |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1681182/jon-conner-the-blue-album-jay-z.jhtml |title=Jay-Z's Blueprint Inspires Jon Connor To Be 'The Best'| publisher=MTV |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> The mixtape title is also a play on words, referencing Blue City Club, a group he leads. The collective consists of Mickey Wallace, Caas Swift, Brandon Bars, Lia Mack and D. Wayne, who are all prominently featured on the mixtape.<ref name="mlive2"/> He told [[MTV]] that he wrote the whole mixtape in four days.<ref name="autogenerated1"/> Adam Fleischer of ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' gave the mixtape a positive review: "Lyrically, Connor is a clever artist that is able to weave words together and astonish without simply relying on punchlines.... === 2013–present: ''Unconscious State'' and signing to Aftermath Entertainment ===
{{main|Unconscious State}}
In early 2013, Jon Connor revealed that he was working on various projects including, first, his debut studio album ''[[Unconscious State]]'', then ''Unsung Heroes LP,'' a collaboration album with rapper [[Chris Webby]], his second studio album and a collaboration album with [[Ski Beatz]].<ref name="autogenerated2">{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/photo-gallery/jon-connor-speaks-unconscious-state-working-chris-webby-and-genius-cyndi-lauper |title=Jon Connor Speaks On 'Unconscious State' LP, Working With Chris Webby, And The Genius Of Cyndi Lauper |work= [[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]] |date=April 24, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> On July 2, 2013, Jon Connor released ''Unconscious State'' under All Varsity Music.<ref name="mlive4">{{cite web|first= William E. III| last= Ketchum |url= http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/flint/index.ssf/2013/07/jon_connor_makes_billboard_cha.html |title=Jon Connor makes Billboard charts debut with album 'Unconscious State' | date= July 13, 2013 |publisher= MLive.com |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> The album featured guest appearances by [[Danny Brown (rapper)|Danny Brown]], Chris Webby, [[Freddie Gibbs]], [[Royce da 5'9"]], [[Talib Kweli]] and [[Smoke DZA]] among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1709968/jon-connor-unconscious-state-mixtape.jhtml |title=Jon Connor's Unconscious State Is All About 'Having Fun' |publisher= MTV |date=July 2, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> Production was handled by Connor himself, [[Mr. Porter]], Brix and Optiks among others.<ref name="autogenerated2"/><ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/jon-connor-unconscious-state |title=Jon Connor – Unconscious State |publisher =DJbooth.net |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> The album was supported by the singles, "Michigan Shit" featuring Royce da 5'9" and "Rise Up" featuring [[Talib Kweli]].<ref name="mlive4"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopwired.com/2013/05/06/jon-connor-ft-royce-da-59-michigan-sht-listendownload/ |title=Jon Connor ft. Royce Da 5'9 – 'Michigan Sh*t' |work= Hip-Hop Wired |date=May 6, 2013 |access-date= 3 November 2013}}</ref> Upon its release the album peaked at number 35 on the US ''Billboard'' [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] and number seven on the ''Billboard'' [[Heatseekers Albums]] charts.<ref name="mlive4"/>

On July 20, 2013, it was revealed that Jon Connor had recently been in the studio with Dr. Dre.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fashionably-early.com/2013/07/20/jon-connor-in-the-studio-with-dr-dre/ |title=Jon Connor in the Studio With Dr. Dre |work= Fashionably-Early.com |date= July 20, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapradar.com/2013/09/29/dr-dre-signs-jon-conner-to-aftermath/ |title= Dr. Dre Signs Jon Connor To Aftermath |publisher= RapRadar.com |date=September 29, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> Connor told MTV that the two immediately had chemistry in the studio, and after working with each other over the following months, Dre signed Connor to his record label [[Aftermath Entertainment]], which is distributed by [[Interscope Records]].<ref name="autogenerated3">{{cite web|url= http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1715655/jon-connor-dr-dre-aftermath-label.jhtml |title=Jon Connor's Record Deal With Dr. Dre Isn't About The Money | publisher= [[MTV]]|date=October 16, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> At the end of his freestyle during a cypher at the taping of the 2013 BET Hip Hop Awards on September 29, Jon Connor revealed that he had signed to Dre's Aftermath Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2013/10/16/jon-connor-talks-signing-to-aftermath-working-with-dr-dre-more/ |title=Jon Connor Talks Signing To Aftermath, Working with Dr. Dre & More | work= [[The Source]] |date=October 16, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1714769/jon-connor-bet-awards.jhtml |title=Meet Jon Connor, The Rapper Who Set The 2013 BET Hip-Hop Awards On Fire |publisher= MTV |date=September 30, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref> He said he chose to announce then because, "I was thinking, 'How can I end this? How can I make this a moment?' This was my first time doing the cypher and I wanted to make it a moment. I ran it by Dre and he was like, 'Yo, that's dope.'"<ref name="autogenerated3"/> His freestyle during the 2013 BET Hip Hop Awards was well-praised, including being named the third best of the night, by ''XXL''.<ref name="abc2"/><ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/10/ranking-the-2013-bet-hip-hop-awards-cypher-verses/23/ |title=Ranking The 2013 BET Hip-Hop Awards Cypher Verses| work= XXLmag.com| date=October 16, 2013 |access-date=3 November 2013}}</ref>
