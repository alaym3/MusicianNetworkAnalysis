'''Maija Johanna Vilkkumaa''' (born 9&nbsp;November&nbsp;1973) is a Finnish [[pop rock]] singer-songwriter. Beginning her musical hobbies playing piano at an age before school, Vilkkumaa studied in [[lukio|high school]] where she and her friends set up the band Tarharyhmä in 1990, which broke up in 1995. While studying Finnish language at the [[University of Helsinki]], she started to take singing lessons and went on to start her solo career in 1999.

During her solo career from 1999 to 2011, Vilkkumaa has released eight full-length solo albums and sold over 300,000&nbsp;[[List of music recording certifications|certified]] records, becoming the tenth-best-selling female soloist in Finland.<ref name="maijavilkkumaafinlandsales">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/artistit/maija+vilkkumaa|title=Maija Vilkkumaa|publisher=[[Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland]]|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> Along with meeting commercial success, the singer&mdash;who writes and composes the songs by herself<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fono.fi/TekijaHakutulos.aspx?esittaja=vilkkumaa+maija|title=Tekijähaku " 'vilkkumaa maija'|publisher=[[YLE|Finnish Broadcasting Company]]|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref>&mdash;has also been credited for her songwriting skills by receiving the [[Juha Vainio#Legacy|Juha Vainio Award]].<ref name="juhavainioaward">{{Cite web|url=http://yle.fi/alueet/kymenlaakso/2010/07/juha_vainio_-palkinto_maija_vilkkumaalle_1868464.html|title=Juha Vainio -palkinto Maija Vilkkumaalle|date=3 July 2010|publisher=Finnish Broadcasting Company|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100804155851/http://www.yle.fi/alueet/kymenlaakso/2010/07/juha_vainio_-palkinto_maija_vilkkumaalle_1868464.html|archivedate=4 August 2010}}</ref> She has also received five [[Emma-gaala|Emma Awards]], accolades for outstanding achievements in music, awarded by the Finnish music industry federation, [[Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|Musiikkituottajat]], and five Emma nominations.

==Life and career==
===1973&ndash;1993: Early life and education===
Maija Vilkkumaa was born in Helsinki on 9&nbsp;November&nbsp;1973.<ref name="gargano">{{cite book|first1=Luca|last1=Gargano|first2=Maija|last2=Vilkkumaa|title=Maija|publisher=[[Sanoma|WSOY]]|year=2004|language=Finnish|isbn=951-0-29082-3}}</ref> She has two little sisters and she lived her childhood in the neighborhood of [[Töölö]].<ref name="maija-vilkkumaa-net-biografia">{{Cite web|url=http://www.maijavilkkumaa.net/biografia/|title=Biografia|publisher=Maija Vilkkumaa|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120205133813/http://www.maijavilkkumaa.net/biografia/|archivedate=5 February 2012}}</ref> She played piano beginning younger than at the age of seven and finishing when she was 16. In 1992, Vilkkumaa completed her [[Lukio|matriculation examination]] at [[Kallio]] [[lukio|High School]] in Helsinki and studied [[Finnish language]] at the [[University of Helsinki]] since 1993.<ref name="gargano"/>

===1990s: Tarharyhmä, solo career beginnings and ''Pitkä ihana leikki''===
In 1990, Maija Vilkkumaa set up the band Tarharyhmä with her friends, gaining national radio play with its songs. Vilkkumaa wrote and composed and most of the songs. With Tarharyhmä breaking up in 1995, Vilkkumaa started to attend singing lessons. She has also played keyboard in the band Hunajamelonit (''Honey Melons'').<ref name="gargano"/>

In 1999, Vilkkumaa started a solo career setting up a band around her. The band consisted of keyboard player Tero Pennanen, bassist Niko Kokko and guitarist Mikko Kosonen. In August of the same year, Vilkkumaa released her debut album ''[[Pitkä ihana leikki]]'' (''Long Sweet Game''), after the arrival of its debut single "[[Satumaa-tango]]".<ref name="gargano"/> With the debut album peaking at number three on the [[Finnish Albums Chart]]<ref name="leikkichart">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/maija+vilkkumaa/pitkä+ihana+leikki|title=Maija Vilkkumaa: Pitkä ihana leikki|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> and selling [[List of music recording certifications#Albums|gold]] with over 38,000&nbsp;copies,<ref name="maijavilkkumaafinlandsales"/> critics welcomed Vilkkumaa as an inventive new artist in the then-sparse scene of Finnish female pop rockers<ref name="citymagazinereview">{{Cite web|url=http://www.city.fi/lehti/pop/arvostelut/63_maija+vilkkumaa-pitk%C3%A4+ihana+leikki|title=Maija Vilkkumaa &ndash; Pitkä ihana leikki|first=Roope|last=Lehtinen|work=[[City (magazine)|City]]|publisher=Janton|date=1 September 1999|accessdate=2 February 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110928054146/http://www.city.fi/lehti/pop/arvostelut/63_maija+vilkkumaa-pitk%C3%A4+ihana+leikki|archive-date=28 September 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="anttiravelin">{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/pitk-ihana-leikki-r579483/review|title=Pitkä Ihana Leikki &mdash; Maija Vilkkumaa|publisher=[[Rovi Corporation]]|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref><ref name="soundireview">{{Cite web|url=http://www.soundi.fi/arvostelut?nid=6005|title=MAIJA VILKKUMAA &ndash; Pitkä ihana leikki|work=Soundi|publisher=A-lehdet|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120311003335/http://www.soundi.fi/arvostelut?nid=6005|archivedate=11 March 2012}}</ref> and she received the 2000 [[Emma-gaala|Emma Award]] for New Female Soloist of the Year.<ref name="all-emma-winners">{{Cite web|url=http://ifpi.fi/palkinnot/emmat/emma-palkinnon-voittajat|title=Emma palkinnon voittajat|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> At the same event, ''Pitkä ihana leikki'' was nominated for Debut Album of the Year.<ref name="emma-2000-nominees">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/uutiset/arkisto/emma-gaala%202000|title=15.2.2000 Emma-gaala 2000|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref>

===2000s: ''Meikit, ketjut ja vyöt'', ''Ei'', ''Se ei olekaan niin'' and ''Superpallo''===

In 2001, Vilkkumaa released her second studio album ''[[Meikit, ketjut ja vyöt]]'' (''Make-ups, Necklaces and Belts'') which was her first number-one release on the Albums Chart<ref name="meikitchart">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/maija+vilkkumaa/meikit%2C+ketjut+ja+vy%C3%B6t|title=Maija Vilkkumaa: Meikit, ketjut ja vyöt|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> and sold nearly 37,000&nbsp;copies and earning her her second gold certification. By the 2003 release of her third studio album ''[[Ei (album)|Ei]]'' (''No''), Vilkkumaa established her status as one of the prominent contemporary Finnish musicians&mdash;the album debuted at number one on the Albums Chart,<ref name="eialbumchart">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/maija+vilkkumaa/ei|title=Maija Vilkkumaa: Ei|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> while charting for over a year (56&nbsp;weeks), and sold over 120,000&nbsp;copies<ref name="maijavilkkumaafinlandsales"/> (double-platinum) in Finland. ''Ei'' earned her three Emma Awards; for Female Soloist of the Year, Album of the Year and [[pop rock|Pop/Rock]] Album of the Year.<ref name="all-emma-winners"/>

''Ei'' was followed by Vilkkumaa's fourth studio album ''[[Se ei olekaan niin]]'' (''But It's Not Like That'') in 2005, which sold over 47,000&nbsp;copies and earned the artist her second platinum certification<ref name="maijavilkkumaafinlandsales"/> and the 2005 Emma Award for Female Soloist of the Year.<ref name="all-emma-winners"/> Preceding Vilkkumaa's first compilation, ''[[Totuutta ja tehtävää]]'' (''Truth and Dare''), its lead single "[[Hei tie]]" (''Hey There, Road'') was Vilkkumaa's first number-one release on the [[Finnish Singles Chart]], in September&nbsp;2006. Released in November 2006, ''Totuutta ja tehtävää'' peaked at number four on the Albums Chart<ref name="totuuttaalbumchart">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/maija+vilkkumaa/totuutta+ja+tehtävää|title=Maija Vilkkumaa: Totuutta ja tehtävää|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> and sold gold with over 25,000&nbsp;copies.<ref name="maijavilkkumaafinlandsales"/> In early 2007, Maija Vilkkumaa embarked on her first major concert tour, Totuutta ja tehtävää.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.huviopas.net/tampere/a_tamperetalo_9.php|title=Maija Vilkkumaa konserttisalikiertueellaan Tampere-taloon|publisher=HuviOpas|date=18 January 2007|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120325133602/http://www.huviopas.net/tampere/a_tamperetalo_9.php|archivedate=25 March 2012}}</ref> The performances were recorded for her first live album, ''[[Ilta Savoyssa]]'' (''Evening at Savoy'').

Vilkkumaa's fifth studio album ''[[Superpallo]]'' (''Bouncy Ball'') was released in October&nbsp;2008. Selling gold with 19,000&nbsp;copies<ref name="maijavilkkumaafinlandsales"/> and spawning three singles, "[[Suojatiellä]]" (''On the Pedestrian Crossing''), "Luokkakokous" (''Class Reunion'') and "Mä haluun naimisiin" (''I Wanna Get Married''), the album did not meet with as much success as the previous studio albums, though still debuting at number one on the Albums Chart.<ref name="superpallochart">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/maija+vilkkumaa/superpallo|title=Maija Vilkkumaa: Superpallo|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> However, Vilkkumaa received three nominations at the 2009 Emma Awards: Female Soloist of the Year,<ref name="emma-2009-femsolo-nominees">{{Cite web|url=http://www.emmagaala.fi/2009/ehdokkaat/naissolisti|title=VUODEN NAISSOLISTI|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110929112518/http://www.emmagaala.fi/2009/ehdokkaat/naissolisti|archivedate=29 September 2011}}</ref> Album of the Year (''Superpallo'')<ref name="emma-2009-album-nominees">{{Cite web|url=http://www.emmagaala.fi/2009/ehdokkaat/albumi|title=VUODEN ALBUMI|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110929112544/http://www.emmagaala.fi/2009/ehdokkaat/albumi|archivedate=29 September 2011}}</ref> and Rock Album of the Year.<ref name="emma-2009-rock-album-nominees">{{Cite web|url=http://www.emmagaala.fi/2009/ehdokkaat/rock-albumi|title=VUODEN ROCK-ALBUMI|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110929112554/http://www.emmagaala.fi/2009/ehdokkaat/rock-albumi|archivedate=29 September 2011}}</ref> In 2009, Vilkkumaa embarked on her second major tour, 10 + 10, to celebrate the ten years that had passed since the release of ''Pitkä ihana leikki''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.voice.fi/index.php?mw=Maija%20Vilkkumaa&option=com_sbsarticle&tmpl=blog&cid=13369&cat=1|title=Maija Vilkkumaan Lottovoitto ei ollut täysosuma|date=3 March 2010|first=Sini|last=Virtanen|language=Finnish|publisher=[[ProSiebenSat.1 Media#European Free-to-air, Pay-TV- and Radio channels|The Voice]]|accessdate=2 February 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111001030032/http://www.voice.fi/index.php?mw=Maija%20Vilkkumaa&option=com_sbsarticle&tmpl=blog&cid=13369&cat=1|archive-date=1 October 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===2010s: ''Kunnes joet muuttaa suuntaa'' and departure from the band===

In April&nbsp;2010, Vilkkumaa released her sixth studio album ''[[Kunnes joet muuttaa suuntaa]]'' (''Until Rivers Change Direction''). Though continuing the trend of declining record sales, the album still debuted at number one on the Albums Chart<ref name="kunneschart">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/maija+vilkkumaa/kunnes+joet+muuttaa+suuntaa|title=Maija Vilkkumaa: Kunnes joet muuttaa suuntaa|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> and sold gold with 16,000&nbsp;copies.<ref name="maijavilkkumaafinlandsales"/> In July&nbsp;2010, Vilkkumaa was awarded the prestigious [[Juha Vainio#Legacy|Juha Vainio Award]] worth €6,000 for her merits as the "pioneer songwriter of her generation". The Board of Juha Vainio Fund justified its decision saying "Maija Vilkkumaa's texts present good [[Colloquialism|colloquial language]]" and that "the lyrics are a classical example of how modern language works in songs".<ref name="juhavainioaward"/> In February&nbsp;2011, Vilkkumaa received her fifth Emma nomination for Female Soloist of the Year.<ref name="emma-2011-femsolo-nominees">{{Cite web|url=http://www.emmagaala.fi/2011/ehdokkaat/naissolisti|title=2010 ehdokkaat &ndash; Vuoden naissolisti|publisher=Musiikkituottajat &ndash; IFPI Finland|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110929112612/http://www.emmagaala.fi/2011/ehdokkaat/naissolisti|archivedate=29 September 2011}}</ref> On 5 April 2011, Maija Vilkkumaa announced in her press release that she would leave her band after the 2011 summer tour. Not sure when she would start doing music again and whether with a band or not, she stated she wanted to have a break and "take a breath".<ref name="vilkkumaaleavesherband-hs">{{Cite news|url=http://www.hs.fi/kulttuuri/artikkeli/Maija+Vilkkumaa+j%C3%A4tt%C3%A4%C3%A4+b%C3%A4ndins%C3%A4/1135265167787|title=Maija Vilkkumaa jättää bändinsä|date=5 April 2011|work=[[Helsingin Sanomat]]|publisher=[[Sanoma]] News|agency=[[Suomen Tietotoimisto]]|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> In January&nbsp;2012, Vilkkumaa performed at a fundraising concert for the [[2012 Finnish presidential election|presidential campaign]] of the [[Green League]]'s candidate [[Pekka Haavisto]].<ref name="haavisto-concert">{{Cite news|url=http://www.iltasanomat.fi/presidentinvaalit/tuhannet-saapuivat-haaviston-tukikonserttiin/art-1288445755154.html|agency=Suomen Tietotoimisto|title=Ultra Bra villitsi Haaviston tukikonsertissa|work=Iltasanomat|publisher=Sanoma News|date=3 January 2012|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref>

==Personal life==
On 28 April 2007, Maija Vilkkumaa married Mikko Kosonen (the guitarist of her band), after having been in a relationship since 2002.<ref name="maijagotmarriediltalehti">{{Cite web|url=http://www.iltalehti.fi/viihde/200704286040794_vi.shtml|title=Maija Vilkkumaa naimisiin tänään|first=Hely|last=Korppi|date=2 April 2007|work=[[Iltalehti]]|publisher=[[Alma Media]]|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref> The couple has two children, daughter Saara who was born in 2007, and another daughter, who was born in January&nbsp;2011.<ref name="iltalehtimaijavauva">{{Cite web|url=http://www.iltalehti.fi/viihde/2011012613078218_vi.shtml|title=Maija Vilkkumaa sai tyttölapsen|work=Iltalehti|publisher=Alma Media|date=2 January 2011|language=Finnish|accessdate=2 February 2012}}</ref>
