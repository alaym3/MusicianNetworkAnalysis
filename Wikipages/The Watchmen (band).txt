'''The Watchmen''' are a [[Canadians|Canadian]] rock band. They were most commercially successful in the mid to late 1990s. During their peak years, the band had one platinum record (''[[In the Trees]]'') and three more [[Music recording sales certification|gold record]]s (''[[McLaren Furnace Room]]'', ''[[Silent Radar]]'',<ref name="Inc.1999">{{cite book|author=Bettsy Powell|title=There's no place like home|work=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=TQ0EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA58|date=16 January 1999|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|pages= and 58|issn=0006-2510}}</ref> and ''[[Slomotion]]''). The band has toured Canada a number of times, were the opening act for [[The Tragically Hip]], and co-headlined a national tour with [[Big Wreck]].

==History==
The Watchmen first came together in Winnipeg, Manitoba in 1988. Founding members were [[Daniel Greaves (musician)|Daniel Greaves]], Joey Serlin, Sammy Kohn and Pete Loewen.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/19990908201209/http://www.chartattack.com:80/features/98/wm/index.html "The Watchmen: Hi-tech, Lo-hype"]. ''Chart Attack'', May 31, 1999. by Sarah Chauncey</ref>  After performing together for several years, they released an album, ''McLaren Furnace Room'', in 1992.<ref>[http://www.calgarysun.com/2016/04/01/go-canada-my-list-of-the-greatest-canadian-bands-to-never-win-a-juno "Go Canada: My list of the greatest Canadian bands to never win a Juno"]. By Dean Pilling, ''Calgary Sun'', April 01, 2016.</ref>

In 1994, Loewen left the band and was replaced by Ken Tizzard. That same year, the band released their second album, ''[[In the Trees]]''. The album was certified Platinum in Canada and is the band's most successful album to date.

The band released the albums ''[[Brand New Day (The Watchmen album)|Brand New Day]]'' and ''[[Silent Radar]]'' in 1996 and 1998, respectively.

In 1999 the band performed as part of Humble & Fred Fest at [[Fort York]] in Toronto.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20000203072814/http://chartattack.com:80/road/reviews/1999/19990917-humblefred.html "Live Reviews: Humble & Fred Fest August 28, 1999 Historic Fort York, Toronto"]. ''Chart Attack'', Howard Druckman, August 28, 1999</ref> Kohn had left the band when they recorded their 2001 album ''[[Slomotion]]''; the percussion tracks were created electronically.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20011122023818/http://chartattack.com:80/damn/2001/09/2501.cfm "CD REVIEWS: The Charlatans UK, Spiritualized, The Watchmen and many more"]. ''Chart Attack'', September 25, 2001</ref><ref>[http://www.usc.uwo.ca/gazette/2001/October/17/Arts_and_Entertainment3.htm "Watchmen release innovative collection"]. ''UWO Gazette'', Volume 95, Issue 25 October 17, 2001</ref>  A single from the album, "Absolutely Anytime" was in the Top Ten on the Canadian charts for several weeks.<ref>[https://www.newspapers.com/newspage/81628186/ "The Watchmen Cometh"]. ''Chilliwack Progress'',  December 7, 2001. Page 34</ref>  While touring to support the album, the band took on drummer Ryan Ahoff.<ref>[http://jam.canoe.com/Music/Artists/W/Watchmen/ConcertReviews/2004/09/07/619343.html "Butt-rocking show"]. IAN NATHANSON -- ''Ottawa Sun'', Sep 7, 2004</ref>

In 2003, the Watchmen announced their farewell tour and their intention to disband.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20031126212708/http://www.chartattack.com/damn/2003/11/1705.cfm "The Watchmen Pack It In, Look Towards New Projects"]. ''Chart Attack'', Nov 17, 2003</ref> They returned to Winnipeg to perform at a fundraiser for the [[Israel national bobsleigh team]].<ref>[http://www.winnipegfreepress.com/historic/31377119.html "Watchmen rock for Israeli bobsled team"]. ''Winnipeg Free Press'', 09/25/2003</ref>

In 2010, the Watchmen came together for a reunion show at the Horseshoe Tavern in Toronto.<ref>[http://www.panicmanual.com/2010/09/27/concert-review-the-watchmen-with-birds-of-wales-horseshoe-tavern-september-25-2010/ "Concert Review:The Watchmen with Birds of Wales"]. ''Panic Manual'', September 25, 2010</ref> They have since continued to perform occasional shows across Canada.

The Watchmen are members of the Canadian charity Artists Against Racism.<ref>http://artistsagainstracism.org/artists/</ref>

On June 25, 2019, ''[[The New York Times Magazine]]'' listed The Watchmen among hundreds of artists whose material was reportedly destroyed in the [[2008 Universal fire]].<ref name="Rosen2">{{cite web |last1=Rosen |first1=Jody |title=Here Are Hundreds More Artists Whose Tapes Were Destroyed in the UMG Fire |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/25/magazine/universal-music-fire-bands-list-umg.html |website=The New York Times |accessdate=June 28, 2019 |date=June 25, 2019}}</ref>
