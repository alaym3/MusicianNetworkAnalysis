'''Emily Burns''' (born 22 September 1994) is a British singer-songwriter. Her music style is influenced by artists such as [[Tove Lo]], [[Kehlani]], [[Sigrid (singer)|Sigrid]] and [[Banks (singer)|Banks]].

==Background==
Burns was born in [[Livingston, West Lothian]], Scotland,<ref name="Gtimes"/> but raised at [[Rugby, Warwickshire]], England,<ref name="wom"/> where she attended [[Rugby High School For Girls]]<ref>{{cite web |title=CENTENARY FESTIVAL TICKETS |url=https://www.rugbyhighschool.co.uk/centenary-festival-tickets |publisher=Rugby High School |accessdate=21 July 2020 |archive-date=21 July 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200721181017/https://www.rugbyhighschool.co.uk/centenary-festival-tickets |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Career ==
Burns entered the [[Live and Unsigned]] competition in 2010, reaching the final to perform at the [[indig02]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://breakingmorewaves.blogspot.co.uk/2013/06/emily-burns.html|title=Emily Burns – New Waves|website=breakingmorewaves.blogspot.co.uk|language=en-GB|access-date=2 May 2018|archive-date=20 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180320043804/http://breakingmorewaves.blogspot.co.uk/2013/06/emily-burns.html|url-status=live}}</ref> She was subsequently invited to attended a [[masterclass]] at [[Abbey Road Studios]], where inhouse producer Rob Cass signed Burns to Cave Productions, leading to the release of the single "Plasters, Glitter and Glue".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.entertainment-focus.com/music-section/underdogs/emily-burns-is-one-to-watch/|title=Emily Burns is one to watch|date=6 August 2013|first=Pip|last=Ellwood-Hughes|work=Entertainment Focus|access-date=18 June 2019|archive-date=8 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131208102444/http://www.entertainment-focus.com/music-section/underdogs/emily-burns-is-one-to-watch/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-coventry-warwickshire-18190523|title=Burns on track for music career|date=24 May 2012|work=BBC News|access-date=2 May 2018|language=en-GB|archive-date=4 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190404024234/https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-coventry-warwickshire-18190523|url-status=live}}</ref>

She performed at numerous festivals, including a tour of the [[Czech Republic]] and appearances on two different stages at the Secret Garden Party.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://glasswerk.co.uk/magazine/article/18902/Introducing+Emily+Burns/|title=Introducing Emily Burns|first=Gordon|last=Johnston|website=Glasswerk Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180502212744/http://glasswerk.co.uk/magazine/article/18902/Introducing+Emily+Burns/|archive-date=2 May 2018}}</ref> Emily also had a slot on the [[BBC introducing]] stage at [[T in the Park]] in July 2013, which led to "Plasters, Glitter and Glue" being play listed on [[BBC Radio 1]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/introducing/entries/941ed49e-61b2-3e75-a933-e1c88f4e7bd6|title=On the playlist: Emily Burns – Plasters, Glitter and Glue|date=6 August 2013|website=BBC Music Introducing|language=en-GB|access-date=2 May 2018|archive-date=26 October 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181026153126/http://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/introducing/entries/941ed49e-61b2-3e75-a933-e1c88f4e7bd6|url-status=live}}</ref> Burns also performed on [[SB.TV]] and [[Mahogany Sessions]] in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.recordoftheday.com/on-the-move/record/emily-burns/take-it-or-leave-it|title=Record of the Day – In tune. Informed.
