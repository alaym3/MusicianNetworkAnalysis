'''Wolfgang "Wolle" Petry''' (born 22 September 1951<ref>[[BBC]] "[https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/567c98c2-d2f6-4b7d-a6d7-af7c032ab2df/releases Wolfgang Petry Releases]"</ref> as ''Franz Hubert Wolfgang Remling'') is a German [[schlager music]]ian and [[songwriter]] from [[Cologne]], Germany. In 1997, he was named the leading [[German language]] musician in terms of chart figures for the year, with his most successful album ''Alles''.<ref>''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' 17 January 1998 [https://books.google.com/books?id=rg0EAAAAMBAJ&pg=RA1-PA47&dq=%22The+leading+German-language+artist+in+the+year-end+charts+was+BMG's+Wolfgang+Petry+with+his+album+%22Alles%22%22]</ref>

He won the award for "Best Folk/Pop Artist" National/International" during the [[Echo (music award)|Echo awards]] in 1997, 1998, 1999, 2000 and 2001. He also won the [[Goldene Stimmgabel]] award in 1996, 1997, 1999, 2000, 2001, 2002, 2003 and 2005 for ''Most successful German solo pop act''. In 1998 and 2006 he also won the "Platinum Life Award" during the same Goldene Stimmgabel awards. 

In late 2017, he announced a name change to '''Pete Wolf'''. He released the album ''Happy Man'' under this moniker on 27 October, with his [[Music genre|musical genre]] shifted towards [[country music|country]] and lyrics in [[English language]].<ref>http://www.mdr.de/meine-schlagerwelt/wolfgang-petry-128.html</ref>

== Personal life ==
Petry was born in the [[Cologne]] neighborhood of Raderthal and grew up there. After his father's death at age 16, he assumed responsibility for his 11-year-old brother. He obtained a vocational degree in [[precision mechanics]] while touring with his band in Cologne and surroundings. He married Rosemarie Remling in 1972 and had a son, [[Achim Petry]], two years later. Achim has re-recorded or made new editions of some of his father's songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/wolfgang-petry-mn0000716816/biography|title=Wolfgang Petry {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=14 April 2020}}</ref>
