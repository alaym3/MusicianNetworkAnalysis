'''Muhammad Shahid Nazir''' ([[Urdu]], {{lang-pa|{{Nastaliq|محمد شاہد نذیر}}}}; born 1981), also known as the '''One Pound Fish Man''' (sometimes stylised as '''£1 Fish Man'''), is a [[Pakistani people|Pakistani]] trader and singer-songwriter who found Internet fame with his [[viral video]], "[[One Pound Fish]]". His subsequent appearance on ''[[The X Factor (UK series 9)|The X Factor]]'' led to a record deal with [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/london-life/the-one-pound-fish-man-everyones-hooked-including-warners-8335130.html |title=The One Pound Fish man: everyone's hooked, including Warners |newspaper=[[London Evening Standard]] |date=2012-11-20 |access-date=2020-04-28}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Nazir was born in the town of [[Pattoki]], near [[Lahore]] in [[Punjab, Pakistan|Punjab]], Pakistan.<ref name="telegraph1"/> He grew up listening to [[Music of Bollywood|Bollywood]] and [[Punjabi music|Punjabi]] music, and would sing religious songs during assembly at school. Prior to emigrating to the United Kingdom for a better life, Nazir worked for the family-owned transport company.<ref name="dawn">{{cite news|title=Family of "One Pound Fish Man" is praying for his video hits on Christmas|url=http://dawn.com/2012/12/16/one-pound-fish-family-prays-for-christmas-hit/|access-date=16 December 2012|newspaper=[[Dawn (newspaper)|Dawn]]|date=16 December 2012}}</ref> He is married to Kashifa. The couple has four children. His mother, wife and children are still living in [[Pattoki]].<ref name="dawn"/>

==Career==
=== "One Pound Fish" ===
Following brief employment at a [[variety store|pound shop]], Nazir, who had since settled in [[East London]], began work at a fish stall at [[Queen's Market]], [[Upton Park, London|Upton Park]], where his employer instructed him to use a trader's call to attract customers. He soon composed the song "One Pound Fish":

{{quote|<poem>“Come on ladies, come on ladies
One pound fish 
Have-a, have-a look
One pound fish
Very, very good, very, very cheap
One pound fish
Six for five pound 
One pound each”</poem>}}

After  passing customers uploaded videos of him performing his song onto [[YouTube]], it became an internet smash and a [[viral video]], and he was invited to perform the song on the television show ''The X Factor''. The song was later covered by [[Alesha Dixon]], [[Timbaland]] and [[Mindless Behavior]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/london-life/the-one-pound-fish-man-everyones-hooked-including-warners-8335130.html|title=The One Pound Fish man: everyone's hooked, including Warners|author=Susannah Butter|date=20 November 2012|work=Evening Standard}}</ref>

=== Post ''X Factor'' ===
In November 2012, Nazir was signed by [[Warner Music]] as a recording artist, and released a dance version of his market sales pitch. A video was also filmed which featured Bollywood-style dancers and an appearance from former weatherman [[Michael Fish]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Sarah Shaffi |url=https://www.newhamrecorder.co.uk/entertainment/1-fish-man-releases-offishial-video-for-one-pound-fish-1-1736884 |title=£1 Fish man releases offishial video for One Pound Fish |newspaper=[[Newham Recorder]] |access-date=2020-04-28}}</ref> The song was in the running for securing the [[Christmas Number One]] for 2012 alongside ''X Factor'' winner [[James Arthur (singer)|James Arthur]]'s release of his winning song "[[Impossible (James Arthur song)|Impossible]]".<ref name="telegraph1">{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/topics/christmas/christmas-videos/9736559/One-Pound-Fish-song-makes-bid-for-Christmas-No1.html|title=Video: One Pound Fish song makes bid for Christmas No1 - Telegraph|date=11 December 2012|work=Telegraph.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/showbiz/celebrity-news/james-arthur-smashes-x-factor-sales-record-8406458.html|title=James Arthur smashes X Factor sales record|date=11 December 2012|work=Evening Standard}}</ref>

===Return to Pakistan===
Nazir's fame alerted the UK's immigration services. Nazir had arrived in the United Kingdom{{when|date=May 2016}} on a [[student visa]], but left his studies{{where|date=May 2016}} to work  as a [[fishmonger]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-18075428|title=BBC News - One pound fish song an internet smash|work=BBC News}}</ref> In December 2012, under the government policy he was ordered to leave the country as his visa had expired.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thenewstribe.com/2012/12/24/one-pound-fish-man-shahid-nazir-faces-deportation-from-uk-over-visa-issue/ |title=One Pound Fish man, Shahid Nazir, faces deportation from UK over visa issue |date=24 December 2012 |website=The News Tribe |first=Ubaid |last=Mughal |access-date=2016-05-09}}</ref> He returned to Pakistan in December 2012. His management said he would return to the UK to promote his musical career with a new entry visa. His agent, Samir Ahmed, said: "He will be returning to Pakistan and will be celebrating New Year with his family. His return to the UK is pending approval of a working visa. We fully expect him to return in the coming weeks".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/uk-news/one-pound-fish-man-told-1504472|title=One Pound Fish man told to return to Pakistan because of visa issues - Mirror Online|author=Laura Martin|date=24 December 2012|work=mirror}}</ref>
According to a 2016 article in the ''[[New Statesman]]'', Nazir was declined a new visa to enter the UK because he violated the terms of his last visa by singing as a professional entertainer. A year after that he applied for another visa to try and appear in a film about his life, however it was also denied.<ref>{{cite web|last=Kerley |first=Mike |url=https://www.newstatesman.com/science-tech/internet/2016/10/living-meme-what-happened-one-pound-fish-man |title=Living the Meme: What happened to the One Pound Fish man? |website=Newstatesman.com |date=2016-10-24 |access-date=2020-04-28}}</ref>
