'''Kamau Mbonisi Kwame Agyeman''', known professionally by his stage name, '''KAMAUU''' (formerly '''KAMAU''' and '''brother KAMAU'''), is an American singer and rapper based in [[Brooklyn]], New York City. He released his debut EP, ''A Gorgeous Fortune'', in 2016. His most recent project, an EP called ''TheKAMAUU-CASSETTE: MíXD GRēēNS'', came out in July 2019 on [[Atlantic Records]].

==Early life and education==

Kamau Mbonisi Kwame Agyeman was born in Washington, D.C.<ref name="Ozy">{{cite news | last=Coleman| first=Libby| title =Hello, Hello, Hello, Hello, Hello – It's Kamau| work =OZY| date =April 5, 2017 | url =https://www.ozy.com/rising-stars/hello-hello-hello-hello-hello-its-kamau/76310| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> and grew up in [[Prince George's County, Maryland|Prince George's County]], Maryland.<ref name="VibeNext">{{cite news | last=Thompson| first=Desire| title =NEXT: KAMAU's Presence Is A Present To Creative Expression| work =Vibe| date =March 23, 2017 | url =https://www.vibe.com/2017/03/next-kamau-interview| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> His parents introduced him and his two siblings to music at a young age.<ref name="Ozy"/> His older brother is a fellow musician who goes by the stage name, Nkō Khélí.<ref name="ComplexGra">{{cite news | last=Skelton| first=Eric| title =KAMAU Previews New EP With Powerful Video for "GRā" Featuring Nkō Khélí| work =Pigeons & Planes| date =September 5, 2017 | url =https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2017/09/kamau-gra-kko-kheli| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> As a child, Agyeman attended school at the Ujamaa Shule in Washington which is described as the "oldest completely independent Afrikan-centered school in the United States". While at the school, he was exposed to a variety of traditional African instruments like the [[djembe]], and he studied African music, history, and culture.<ref name="Ozy"/><ref name="Essence">{{cite news | last=Scott| first=Sydney| title =New & Next: KAMAU Is An Artist Creating A Lane Of His Own| work =Essence| date =April 11, 2017 | url =https://www.essence.com/entertainment/new-next-kamau/| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> In 2009, he moved to Brooklyn, New York where he began attending the [[Pratt Institute]] to study film. While at Pratt, he met other musicians and artists and was introduced to the artist collective, BiGCiTYBiGCiTY. Members of the collective helped him hone his musical skills.<ref name="VibeNext"/> He later graduated from Pratt in 2014.<ref name="Essence"/>

==Career==

In February 2014, KAMAUU released a mixtape called ''TheKAMAU-CASSETTE''.<ref name="VibeNext"/> In 2015,<ref name="Soul">{{cite news | last=Hamm| first=Ken| title =KAMAU, Lion Babe & No Wyld Are Doing This Refix ‘Jusfayu’| work =SoulBounce| date =July 8, 2016 | url =http://www.soulbounce.com/2016/07/kamau-lion-babe-no-wyld-are-doing-this-refix-jusfayu/| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> Agyeman received recognition for his song covers including those for [[Outkast]]'s "[[Hey Ya!]]" and [[Adele]]'s "[[Hometown Glory]]".<ref name="Atwood">{{cite news | last=Payano| first=Brandon| title =Our Take: KAMAU’S Personal and Electrifying Fortune| work =Atwood Magazine| date =September 20, 2016 | url =http://atwoodmagazine.com/gorgeous-fortune-kamau-review/| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> In May 2016, he released a video for his song, "Jambo". The video was the first in a six-part series of films that would be made for each song on his ''A Gorgeous Fortune'' EP.<ref name="ID">{{cite news | title =video premiere: kamau, 'jambo'| work =i-D| date =May 24, 2016 | url =https://i-d.vice.com/en_us/article/bjz483/video-premiere-kamau-jambo| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> Another single (and its accompanying video) from that collection called "Jusfayu" (featuring [[No Wyld]]) was released in June 2016.<ref name="NPR2016">{{cite news | last=Ramirez| first=Erika| title =Songs We Love: KAMAU, 'Jusfayu (Feat. No Wyld)'| publisher =NPR| date =June 8, 2016 | url =https://www.npr.org/2016/06/08/481123153/songs-we-love-kamau-jusfayu-feat-no-wyld| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> It would go on to be featured on an episode of the [[HBO]] series, ''[[Insecure (TV series)|Insecure]]''<ref name="ComplexInsecure">{{cite news | title =All of the Music Played During 'Insecure' Season 1| work =Complex| date =July 31, 2018 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2018/07/insecure-season-one-songs/| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> and on the soundtrack for ''[[FIFA 17]]''.<ref name="VibeMint">{{cite news | last=Thompson| first=Desire| title =KAMAU Explores The Paradigms Of Blissful Love With Talibah Safiya On "MĭNT"| work =Vibe| date =January 24, 2017 | url =https://www.vibe.com/2017/01/kamau-talibah-safiya-mint| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> It has also accumulated over 10 million streams on various platforms as of August 2019.<ref name="Vibe2019">{{cite news | last=Thompson| first=Desire| title =Premiere: Discover KAMAUU's Lyrical Gems In "bamboo" Visual| work =Vibe| date =August 23, 2019 | url =https://www.vibe.com/2019/08/premiere-kamauu-bamboo-video| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> A remix of the song by [[Lion Babe]] was released later in June 2016.<ref name="Bill2016">{{cite news | last=Ramirez| first=Erika| title =KAMAU Recruits Lion Babe For 'Jusfayu' Remix: Exclusive Song Premiere| work =Billboard| date =June 27, 2016 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7416643/kamau-lion-babe-jusfayu-remix-exclusive-song-premiere| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> ''A Gorgeous Fortune'' itself came out in July 2016 on Invisible Firm and was followed by a KAMAUU summer tour with Lion Babe.<ref name="NPR2016"/><ref name="Complex2016">{{cite news | last=Black| first=Adrienne| title =Stream KAMAU's 'A Gorgeous Fortune' EP| work =Pigeons & Planes| date =July 1, 2016 | url =https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2016/07/stream-kamaus-a-gorgeous-fortune-ep| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref>

In 2016, KAMAUU helped co-found "DoloBhana", a multi-disciplinary artist collective that was based in an apartment in [[Crown Heights, Brooklyn]].<ref name="VibeNext"/> In October 2016, KAMAUU was featured on the soundtrack for the film, ''[[The Birth of a Nation (2016 film)|The Birth of a Nation]]'', with the song, "The Icarus".<ref name="Ozy"/> The following month, he released the single, "MĭNT" featuring Talibah Safiya.<ref name="FaderMint">{{cite news | last=Akinlana| first=Sola| title =N.Y.C. Artist KAMAU Sings About Rock Bottom's Silver Lining On "MINT"| work =The Fader| date =November 28, 2016 | url =https://www.thefader.com/2016/11/28/kamau-mint-premiere| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> The music video for that song came out in January 2017.<ref name="VibeMint"/> It would serve as the lead single for his second mixtape entitled ''TheKAMAU-CASSETTE: ŭRTH GōLD'' which was released in September 2017.<ref name="VibeUrth">{{cite news | last=Ellis| first=Stacy-Ann| title =Close Your Eyes And Vibe To KAMAU's New Project, 'TheKAMAU-CASSETTE: ŭRTH GōLD'| work =Vibe| date =September 18, 2017 | url =https://www.vibe.com/2017/09/kamau-cassette-urth-gold| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> In February 2018, he was featured on the Kota the Friend song, "Black Sheep".<ref name="GeniusKota">{{cite news | title =Black Sheep – Kota the Friend| work =Genius| date =February 20, 2018 | url =https://genius.com/Kota-the-friend-black-sheep-lyrics| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> In March of that year, KAMAUU was featured on the Topaz Jones track, "Pleasure Pain Passion".<ref name="PitchTopaz">{{cite news | last=Buerger| first=Megan| title = Topaz Jones – "Pleasure Pain Passion" (ft. KAMAU)| work =Pitchfork| date =March 6, 2018 | url =https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/topaz-jones-pleasure-pain-passion-ft-kamau/| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> Later that month, he played a show in [[Nairobi]], [[Kenya]] at the Thrift Social installation.<ref name="Star">{{cite news | last=Ndolo| first=Davies| title =Brother KAMAU set to rock Nairobians at Thrift Social| newspaper =The Star| date =March 13, 2018 | url =https://www.the-star.co.ke/sasa/word-is/2018-03-13-brother-kamau-set-to-rock-nairobians-at-thrift-social/| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref>

After a year, he returned with the track "Patiently" as part of the collaborative project, Splash Brothers, which also features [[Allan Kingdom]], Topaz Jones, Haile Supreme, and SIIMBA SELASSIIE.<ref name="Complex11">{{cite news | last1=Moore| first1=Jacob| last2=Gardner| first2=Alex| title =11 Rising Artists Who Defy Genre| work =Pigeons & Planes| date =March 25, 2019 | url =https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2019/03/new-rising-artists-who-defy-genre| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> In May 2019 he began using his current stage name, KAMAUU, and was featured on the Marques Martin song, "Dinner Date".<ref name="ComplexDinner">{{cite news | last=Bishop| first=Aaron| title =Premiere: Marques Martin Brings His Laid-Back Flow To "Dinner Date" With KAMAUU| work =Complex| date =May 9, 2019 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2019/05/premiere-marques-martin-dinner-date-feat-kamauu| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> The following month he was featured on the Stella Santana song, "Tell The Truth".<ref name="NylonStella">{{cite news | last=Stubblebine| first=Allison| title =Stella Santana Wants You To 'Tell The Truth'| work =Nylon| date =June 13, 2019 | url =https://nylon.com/stella-santana-tell-the-truth| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> In July of that year, he released a four-track EP entitled ''TheKAMAUU-CASSETTE: MíXD GRēēNS''.<ref name="Lemonade">{{cite news | last=Garrison| first=Lucas| title =TheKAMAUU-CASSETTE: MíXD GRēēNS – (KAMAUU)| work =Lyrical Lemonade| date =July 31, 2019 | url =https://www.lyricallemonade.com/p/thekamauu-cassette-mixd-greens-kamauu| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref> Music videos for two of the EP's songs ("clover" and "bamboo") were released the following month.<ref name="Vibe2019"/><ref name="AP2019">{{cite news | last=Gueye| first=Awa| title =KAMAUU IS BACK AND IN LOVE| work =AfroPunk| date =August 9, 2019 | url =https://afropunk.com/2019/08/kamauu-is-back-and-in-love/| accessdate =September 24, 2019}}</ref>
