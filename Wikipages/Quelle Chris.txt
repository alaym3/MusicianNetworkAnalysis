'''Gavin Christopher Tennille''' (born October 19, 1984), known as '''Quelle Chris''', is an [[Americans|American]] [[record producer]], [[rapper]] and [[songwriter]] from [[Detroit]], [[Michigan]].<ref name="AllMusic">{{Cite web |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/quelle-chris-mn0002817183/biography |title=Quelle Chris: Biography & History |last=Simpson |first=Paul |website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=February 6, 2018}}</ref> He has released over 15 albums as a solo act and with his collaborative projects.<ref name="TheFader">{{Cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/02/06/quelle-chris-interview |title=How Quelle Chris Turned His Self-Doubt Into A Stellar Album |last=Fintoni |first=Laurent |date=February 6, 2017 |website=[[The Fader]] |language=en |access-date=February 6, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Tennille was born in [[Johnson City, New York]] to a shoe designer.

Tennille's career began expanding as he joined Wasted Youth and attracted the attention of hip hop recording artists in Detroit such as [[J Dilla]] and [[Proof (rapper)|Proof]]. He later formed a musical group with Denmark Vessey called Crown Nation and soon began using the pseudonyms Q-Life and Quelle, the latter of which means "source" in [[German language|German]].<ref name="TheFader"/> With credit as a songwriter on several songs on [[Danny Brown]]'s early albums, he began working on a solo career.<ref name="AllMusic"/>

His debut studio album, ''Shotgun & Sleek Rifle'', was released in 2011, featuring guest appearances from artists such as Brown, Roc Marciano and Big Tone. He signed with [[Mello Music Group]] after releasing two [[extended plays]] with the music group Racehorses are Resources, which also consisted of poet [[John Sinclair (poet)|John Sinclair]]. With two studio albums ''[[Niggas Is Men]]'' and ''[[Ghost at the Finish Line]]'' released on the label in 2013, Tennille has produced for musicians such as [[Pharoahe Monch]], [[Diamond District]] and [[Open Mike Eagle]].<ref name="AllMusic"/>

His fourth studio album ''[[Innocent Country]]'' was released in 2015, followed by the 2016 instrumental album ''Lullabies for the Broken Brain''. His fifth studio album ''[[Being You Is Great, I Wish I Could Be You More Often]]'' was released in 2017.<ref name="AllMusic"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/migos-jay-z-kendrick-lamar-40-best-rap-albums-of-2017-w514061/quelle-chris-being-you-is-great-i-wish-i-could-be-you-more-often-w514072 |title=40 Best Rap Albums of 2017 |last=Weingarten |first=Christopher |date=December 22, 2017 |website=[[Rolling Stone]] |access-date=February 6, 2018}}</ref> He described the album as "about being human. Being great, being flawed, being you."<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.npr.org/2017/02/02/512833152/first-listen-quelle-chris-being-you-is-great-i-wish-i-could-be-you-more-often |title=Review: Quelle Chris, 'Being You Is Great, I Wish I Could Be You More Often' |last=Aku |first=Timmhotep |date=February 2, 2017 |website=[[National Public Radio]] |language=en |access-date=February 6, 2018}}</ref> His debut collaborative album titled ''Everything's Fine'' with partner [[Jean Grae]] was released on March 30, 2018, via Mello.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2018/03/jean-grae-quelle-chris-everythings-fine/|title=Stream Jean Grae and Quelle Chris' 'Everything's Fine' Album - XXL|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=2018-04-08}}</ref> On March 29, 2019, Tennille released his acclaimed sixth solo album [[Guns (Quelle Chris album)|Guns]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Guns by Quelle Chris|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/guns/quelle-chris|language=en|access-date=2020-03-17}}</ref> On March 16, 2020, Tennille his seventh solo album ''Innocent Country 2'' and released it on April 24. It features guest appearances from [[Earl Sweatshirt]], [[Billy Woods (rapper)|billy woods]], Tune-Yardsâ€™ [[Merrill Garbus]], [[Denmark Vessey]], [[Homeboy Sandman]], Pink Siifu, and [[Josh Gondelman]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/quelle-chris-announces-new-album-innocent-country-2-shares-new-song-listen/|title=Quelle Chris Announces New Album Innocent Country 2, Shares New Song|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=2020-03-17}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In December 2017, he proposed to marry his longtime partner who is also his frequent collaborator, [[Jean Grae]].<ref name="XXLMag">{{Cite web |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/01/jean-grae-quelle-chris-everythings-fine-joint-album/ |title=Jean Grae and Quelle Chris Prep 'Everything's Fine' Joint Album - XXL |last=India |first=Lindsey |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=February 6, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.45378/title.jean-grae-quelle-chris-say-theyre-engaged# |title=Jean Grae & Quelle Chris Say They're Engaged |last=Eustice |first=Kyle |date=December 3, 2017 |website=[[HipHopDX]] |language=en-US |access-date=February 6, 2018}}</ref> The two married on August 5, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/08/jean-grae-quelle-chris-get-married/|title=Jean Grae and Quelle Chris Get Married|last=Burks|first=Tosten|date=August 9, 2018|website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|access-date=April 16, 2019}}</ref>
