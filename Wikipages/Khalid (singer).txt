'''Khalid Donnel Robinson''' (born February 11, 1998)<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Ju|first=Shirley|date=February 12, 2019|title=Khalid Celebrates 21st Birthday With Bash at Beverly Hills Mansion|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/khalid-21st-birthday-party-1203137065/|access-date=November 10, 2020|magazine=Variety}}</ref> is an American singer signed to Right Hand Music Group and [[RCA Records]]. He rose to fame after the release of his debut studio album, [[American Teen (album)|''American Teen'']] (2017). The album spawned the US top-20 singles "[[Location (Khalid song)|Location]]" and "[[Young Dumb & Broke]]", and was later certified [[RIAA certification|double platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).

Later that year he was featured on Logic's US top-ten single "[[1-800-273-8255 (song)|1-800-273-8255]]" with [[Alessia Cara]], which earned him a [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year]] nomination. The following year, Khalid released the number-one pop songs "[[Love Lies (song)|Love Lies]]" with [[Normani]] and "[[Eastside (song)|Eastside]]" with [[Benny Blanco]] and [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]], both of which tied the record for longest charting songs on the [[Mainstream Top 40]] chart and reached the top-10 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. In September 2018, he released the number-one [[Rhythmic (chart)|Rhythmic]] song "[[Better (Khalid song)|Better]]" as the lead single from his top-10 EP ''[[Suncity]]''. In April 2019, he released his second studio album, [[Free Spirit (Khalid album)|''Free Spirit'']], which debuted atop the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200 chart]]. The lead single "[[Talk (Khalid song)|Talk]]" peaked at No. 3 in the US and earned him a [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year]] nomination.

Khalid has received multiple accolades including six [[Grammy Award]] nominations, six [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], three [[American Music Awards]] and a [[MTV Video Music Award]]. In 2019, Khalid was named one of [[Time (magazine)|''Time'']] magazine's ''[[100 Most Influential People]]''.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Khalid: The 100 Most Influential People of 2019|url=https://time.com/collection/100-most-influential-people-2019/5567874/khalid/|access-date=September 22, 2020|magazine=TIME|language=en-us}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Robinson was born in [[Fort Stewart]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Shifferaw|first1=Abel|title=Kel Mitchell, Rachael Leigh Cook Star in Khalid's High School-Themed Video for "Young Dumb & Broke"|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2017/08/khalid-new-video-young-dumb-and-broke|website=Complex|access-date=September 29, 2017}}</ref> He spent his childhood in various locations due to his mother, Linda Wolfe, and father's military careers, including [[Fort Campbell]] in [[Kentucky]], [[Fort Drum]] in [[Watertown (city), New York|Watertown, New York]], and six years in [[Heidelberg]], Germany.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Acosta|first1=Dave|title=Khalid finds his 'Location' in El Paso|url=http://www.elpasotimes.com/story/entertainment/2017/02/07/khalid-finds-his-location-el-paso/97422304/|website=El Paso Times|access-date=September 29, 2017}}</ref> Wolfe worked as a supply technician and became a member of the [[United States Army|Army]] corps. In high school, Robinson studied singing and musical theater. He attended [[Carthage Senior High School (Carthage, New York)|Carthage Central High School]] in [[Carthage, New York]] for his first three years of high school before he and his mother moved to [[El Paso, Texas]], where he attended [[Americas High School]] for his senior year.<ref name="Saved documentary">Scott Perry, [http://www.thefader.com/2017/02/02/khalid-documentary-location-american-teen "Saved" documentary], The Fader, February 2, 2017, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2016–2017: ''American Teen'' ===
Khalid began writing and creating music in high school; he posted his early works to [[SoundCloud]]. Shortly after graduating from Americas High School, Khalid reached number two on the [[Billboard Twitter Real-Time|''Billboard'' Twitter Emerging Artists chart]] in July 2016. His single "Location" began charting, which led to his being featured in many publications, including [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']],<ref name="billboard.com">Elias Leight, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7633323/rb-hip-hop-artists-to-watch-in-2017, "10 Hip-Hop and R&B Artists to Watch In 2017"], Billboard, December 27, 2016, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref> [[Yahoo!]],<ref>Jim Farber, [https://www.yahoo.com/music/10-artists-to-watch-in-2017-200907285.html, "10 Artists to Watch in 2017"], Yahoo! Music, December 28, 2016, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref> [[BuzzFeed]],<ref>Emma Cooke, [https://www.buzzfeed.com/emmacooke24/brilliant-songs-you-need-in-your-january-playlist "26 Brilliant Songs You Need In Your January Playlist"], Buzzfeed, January 11, 2017, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref> and ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref>[https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-february-2017-w462565, "10 New Artists You Need to Know: February 2017"], Rolling Stone, February 1, 2017, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref> With production from Syk Sense, Tunji Ige, and Smash David, "Location" finished the year 2016 at number 20 on the US Mainstream R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay chart<ref name="billboard.com" /> and reached the Top 10 on the US Hot R&B Songs chart on January 21, 2017.<ref>Amaya Mendizabal, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7654991/khalid-location-hot-rb-songs-top-10, "The 'Location' of Khalid's Breakout Hit? The Top 10 on Hot R&B Songs Chart"], Billboard, January 12, 2017, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref> The music video for "Location" premiered on ''[[The Fader]]'''s website.<ref>Myles Tanzer, [http://www.thefader.com/2016/09/26/khalid-location-video "Khalid Finds A Digital Love In 'Location' Video"], The Fader, September 26, 2016, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref> The album's second single "[[Young, Dumb & Broke]]" became a sleeper hit, becoming a hit at rhythmic formats and crossing over to pop radio into early 2018.

In January 2017, [[Alina Baraz]]'s single "Electric", featuring Khalid, was released.<ref>{{cite web|title=Alina Baraz and Khalid Link Up on Seductive New Track, 'Electric'|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/alina-baraz-and-khalid-link-up-on-seductive-new|website=[[Ones to Watch]]|access-date=October 10, 2017}}</ref> His collaboration with [[Brasstracks]], called "Whirlwind", was part of the Yours Truly & [[Adidas]] Originals Songs from Scratch series, and received over 700,000 SoundCloud plays.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Black|first1=Adrienne|title=Listen to Khalid and Brasstracks Team Up for "Whirlwind"|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/music/2016/12/khalid-brasstracks-whirlwind|website=PigeonsandPlanes|access-date=October 10, 2017}}</ref> Khalid contributed uncredited vocals to Kendrick Lamar's song "[[The Heart Part 4]]", released on March 24, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/03/24/kendrick-lamar-the-heart-part-4-khalid|title=Kendrick Lamar's "The Heart Part 4" Features An Uncredited Vocal From Khalid|publisher=[[The Fader]]|access-date=March 24, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/03/khalid-featured-kendrick-lamar-the-heart-part-4/|title=Khalid Is Featured on Kendrick Lamar's New "The Heart Part 4"|publisher=XXL|access-date=March 24, 2017}}</ref> On April 28, 2017, [[Logic (musician)|Logic]] released the single "[[1-800-273-8255 (song)|1-800-273-8255]]", which featured Khalid and Alessia Cara; the single peaked at number three on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, making it Khalid's highest-charting single.<ref>{{cite web|title=Khalid on '1-800-273-8255' Hitting Top 5: 'A Song Like This Was Necessary'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7957961/khalid-on-1-800-273-8255-hitting-top-5-a-song-like-this-was-necessary}}</ref>

He sold out every venue on his 21-city January–February 2017 Location Tour, including the 1,500-capacity Tricky Falls in El Paso.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.elpasotimes.com/story/entertainment/blogs/the-beat/2017/07/25/el-pasos-khalid-nominated-best-new-artist-vma/510957001/|title=El Paso's Khalid nominated for MTV VMA for Best New Artist|work=El Paso Times|access-date=December 24, 2017}}</ref> Following the completion of the tour, his debut [[studio album]] ''[[American Teen (album)|American Teen]]'' was released on March 3, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=American Teen by Khalid on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/american-teen/id1200868601|website=iTunes|access-date=October 10, 2017|date=March 3, 2017}}</ref> The album received critical acclaim and [[Grammy Award]] nominations for [[Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album|Best Urban Contemporary Album]] and [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Song|Best R&B Song]] (for "Location"), at the [[2018 Grammys|2018 ceremony]].<ref name=":0" /> On October 24, 2017, the album was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA) for selling over 1,000,000 in combined pure sales and [[album-equivalent units]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/10/24/khalids-american-teen-platinum|title=Khalid's debut Album American Teen is certified Platinum|publisher=The Fader|access-date=October 25, 2017}}</ref>

In 2017, Khalid won a [[MTV Video Music Award|VMA Award]] for [[MTV Video Music Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://ew.com/awards/2017/08/27/vmas-khalid-best-new-artist-2017/|title=Khalid wins Best New Artist at the MTV VMAs|work=EW.com|access-date=December 24, 2017}}</ref> Khalid made his television debut performing "Location" on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' March 15, 2017, backed by [[The Roots]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/03/16/khalid-fallon-performance-location-american-teen|title=Watch Khalid's Charismatic Performance Of "Location" On Fallon|last1=Myers|first1=Owen|website=The FADER|access-date=October 10, 2017}}</ref> His song "Angels" was featured on the ABC drama series ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]'' in the episode entitled "Don't Stop Me Now", which aired April 27, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/thegreatkhalid/videos/707324652807711/|title=Khalid|website=www.facebook.com|access-date=October 10, 2017}}</ref> September 2018, El Paso Mayor Dee Margo Friday presented Khalid with the key to the City of El Paso.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.kvia.com/news/el-paso/mayor-presents-khalid-with-key-to-the-city-of-el-paso/794515915 |title=Mayor presents Khalid with Key to the City of El Paso }}</ref>

Khalid's next single was a collaboration with American DJ [[Marshmello]] titled "[[Silence (Marshmello song)|Silence]]", which was released on August 11, 2017, via [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/hear-marshmello-khalid-unite-on-stirring-new-song-silence-253069/|title=Hear Marshmello, Khalid Unite on Stirring New Song 'Silence'|date=August 11, 2017|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=September 22, 2019}}</ref> The song appeared on the Top 200 in over 28 countries. It topped the Dance charts in Australia, the United Kingdom, and the United States, and charted in the Top 10 of more than fifteen countries such as Germany, Sweden and Norway. It also appeared on the year-end charts of Hungary, Denmark, Austria, Belgium, and the Netherlands. Additionally, it was certified multi-platinum in many countries. Among them were platinum by [[British Phonographic Industry|BPI]] (United Kingdom) and [[Bundesverband Musikindustrie|BM]] (Germany), double platinum by [[Belgian Entertainment Association|BEA]] (Belgium), [[Recorded Music NZ|RMNZ]] (New Zealand) and RIAA (United States), triple platinum by [[Music Canada|MC]] (Canada), a quadruple platinum by [[Swedish Recording Industry Association|SRIA]] (Sweden) and a quintuple platinum by [[Australian Recording Industry Association|ARIA]] (Australia).

=== 2018–2019: ''Suncity'' and ''Free Spirit'' ===
Khalid's song "The Ways" with rapper [[Swae Lee]] was featured in the Marvel film ''[[Black Panther (film)|Black Panther]]''. As of February 23, 2018, the song peaked at number 63 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100|title=Music: Top 100 Songs {{!}} Billboard Hot 100 Chart|work=Billboard|access-date=February 23, 2018}}</ref> In 2017, he collaborated with [[Lorde]], [[SZA (singer)|SZA]], and [[Post Malone]] on Lorde's "[[Homemade Dynamite#Remixes|Homemade Dynamite]]" remix. He released a [[duet]] with singer [[Normani]], called "[[Love Lies (song)|Love Lies]]", recorded for the [[Love, Simon (soundtrack)|''Love, Simon'' film soundtrack]], which reached the top ten of the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/love-simon-original-motion-picture-soundtrack/1334779517|title=Love, Simon (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack) by Various Artists on Apple Music|work=iTunes Store (US)|access-date=January 16, 2018}}</ref> "Love Lies" was each artist's first number one on the US Pop Songs chart, and finished in the top 20 of the 2018 ''Billboard'' Hot 100 Year-End chart.

In May 2018, he wrote [[Youth (Shawn Mendes song)|"Youth"]] in collaboration with Canadian artist [[Shawn Mendes]]. The song was featured on Mendes' self titled album and contains references to the social and political climate at that time. Mendes and Khalid performed "Youth" at the [[2018 Billboard Music Awards|2018 ''Billboard'' Music Awards]] on May 20, 2018, as a tribute to the victims of gun violence. The performance featured the show choir from [[Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Kaufman|first=Gil|date=May 20, 2018|title=Shawn Mendes & Khalid Put on Powerful Performance of 'Youth' at the Billboard Music Awards 2018: Watch|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/bbma/8456921/shawn-mendes-khalid-perform-youth-billboard-music-awards-2018-video|work=Billboard|access-date=May 21, 2018}}</ref>

Khalid wrote and provided vocals for DJ/producer [[Martin Garrix]]'s song "[[Ocean (Martin Garrix song)|Ocean]]", released through [[Stmpd Rcrds]] on June 15, 2018. It peaked at number 78 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, number 5 on the [[Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart, and finished at number 16 on its 2018 year end chart, then number 81 the next year.

He collaborated with [[Billie Eilish]] on the song "[[Lovely (Billie Eilish and Khalid song)|Lovely]]", which was sampled multiple times on ''[[Goodbye & Good Riddance]]'', the debut album of rapper [[Juice Wrld]]. In July 2018, he was featured alongside [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]] on [[Benny Blanco]]'s debut single "[[Eastside (song)|Eastside]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/benny-blanco-halsey-khalid-eastside-new-song-698891/|title=Hear Benny Blanco, Halsey, Khalid Team for Bittersweet 'Eastside'|last=Blistein|first=Jon|date=July 12, 2018|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=August 30, 2018}}</ref> The song charted in the ''Billboard'' Hot 100's top ten and reached number one on the US Pop Songs chart, after a record 30-week climb. He also released a song called "[[Better (Khalid song)|Better]]" on September 14, the first day of two hometown shows.<ref>{{cite tweet |user=thegreatkhalid|number=1039636695963127809|date=September 11, 2018|title=With love, to the 915. On 9/14 a song for y'all}}</ref> The song topped the Rhythmic Songs airplay ranking and charted in the top five at pop radio.

In October 2018, Khalid released an EP, ''[[Suncity]]'', on October 19, 2018, through [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Suncity by Khalid|date=October 19, 2018|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/suncity/1438904068|access-date=October 21, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/09/khalid-better-new-music/|title=Khalid shares sexy new jam 'Better': Stream|date=September 14, 2018|work=Consequence of Sound|access-date=October 21, 2018}}</ref> The EP debuted at number 8 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="BB200">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8482014/a-star-is-born-soundtrack-third-week-no-1-album|title='A Star Is Born' Soundtrack Earns Third Week at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Chart, Future & Juice WRLD Debut at No. 2|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=October 28, 2018|work=Billboard|access-date=October 29, 2018}}</ref> The album also spawned the song "[[Saturday Nights (Khalid song)|Saturday Nights]]", later remixed to feature country artist [[Kane Brown]].

In February 2019, Khalid released the single "[[Talk (Khalid song)|Talk]]", and he made his debut on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' on March 9, 2019, performing "Talk" and "Better".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/hiphop/khalid-talk-snl-performance-falsetto/|title=Khalid's Powerful Falsetto On 'Talk' Shines In His Debut 'SNL' Performance|first=Aaron|last=Williams|publisher=UpRoxx|date=March 10, 2019|access-date=March 10, 2019}}</ref> His second studio album ''[[Free Spirit (Khalid album)|Free Spirit]]'' was released on April 5, 2019, along with an accompanying short film. It debuted atop the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with 202,000 album-equivalent units (including 85,000 pure album sales) in its first week, becoming Khalid's first US number-one album.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8507048/khalid-free-spirit-debuts-at-no-1-on-billboard-200-albums-chart|title=Khalid's 'Free Spirit' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Albums Chart|magazine=Billboard|access-date=April 29, 2019}}</ref>

On April 20, 2019, Khalid made history by becoming the first and only artist to occupy the entire top 5 of the [[R&B Songs|''Billboard'' R&B Songs]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/hiphop/khalid-top-5-rb-songs-chart/|title=Khalid Is The First Artist To Simultaneously Have The Top 5 R&B Songs|date=April 16, 2019|website=UPROXX|access-date=April 29, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/khalid-becomes-first-artist-in-history-to-secure-top-5-spots-on-randb-songs-chart-news.77446.html|title=Khalid Becomes First Artist In History To Secure Top 5 Spots On R&B Songs Chart|website=HotNewHipHop|date=April 16, 2019|access-date=April 29, 2019}}</ref>

On April 25, 2019, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine reported that since March 2018, Khalid had landed a 59-week straight streak of hits (and counting) on the [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] since his [[Normani]] collaboration [[Love Lies (song)|"Love Lies]]" debuted on the chart. He charted 12 tracks (both solo hits and features) on the Hot 100 throughout the last year.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/photos/8508713/khalid-hot-100-heat-index|title=How Khalid Spent the Whole Past Year on the Hot 100|magazine=Billboard|access-date=April 29, 2019}}</ref>

== Musical style and influences ==
Khalid's music is primarily [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]],<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/khalid-mn0000095880/biography|title=Khalid Biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|first=Andy|last=Kellman|access-date=August 15, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://time.com/5574497/khalid-cant-sing-time-100/|title=Khalid Says People Used to Chant 'Khalid Can't Sing' at Him in High School|magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|first1=Alana|first2=Ashley|last1=Abramson|last2=Hoffman|date=April 23, 2019|access-date=August 15, 2019}}</ref> [[hip hop music|hip hop]],<ref>{{cite magazine|url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8550073/sony-earnings-report-khalid-new-music|title=Is a New Khalid Album Coming Soon?|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=February 4, 2020|access-date=July 19, 2020}}</ref> [[pop music|pop]]<ref name="allmusic" /> and [[pop-soul]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/11/28/arts/music/khalid-grammy-nominations-american-teen.html|title=Khalid, the Teenager With 5 Grammy Nominations: 'They Got It Right This Year'|website=[[The New York Times]]|first=Joe|last=Coscarelli|date=November 28, 2017|access-date=August 15, 2019}}</ref> He sings in both the [[baritone]] and [[tenor]] ranges, effectively rendering him a [[baritenor]]. Khalid possesses a two octave vocal range, ranging from the low G2 to the middle B♭4.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://therangeplace.boards.net/thread/2769|title=Khalid {{!}} The Range Place|website=therangeplace.boards.net|access-date=October 23, 2018}}</ref> Khalid cites his mother as his leading musical inspiration<ref name="Saved documentary" /> and has cited [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[ASAP Rocky|A$AP Rocky]], [[Father John Misty]], [[Frank Ocean]], [[Grizzly Bear (band)|Grizzly Bear]], [[Chance the Rapper]], [[Lorde]], [[India Arie|India.Arie]], and [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]] as other influences.<ref>Kevito, [http://www.okayplayer.com/news/first-look-friday-khalid-interview.html/2, "First Look Friday: Chucotown's Own Khalid Is The Future of Millennial R&B"], Okayplayer, December 2016, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref><ref>Eddie Cepeda, [https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/irl-paso-texas-singer-khalid-is-making-music-to-connect-to "IRL Paso: Texas Singer Khalid Is Making Music to Connect To"], Noisey, January 17, 2017, retrieved February 7, 2017</ref>
