'''Horsey''' are an English [[Band (rock and pop)|rock band]] from London, formed in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/horsey-everyones-tongue|title=Say hello to Horsey, the tantalisingly sinister South London newcomers set to cause a huge stir|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-16}}</ref> Their sound has been described as [[art rock]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://beatsperminute.com/album-review-horsey-debonair/|title=Album Review: Horsey – Debonair|website=[[Beats Per Minute (website)|Beats Per Minute]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-16}}</ref> [[punk jazz| jazz]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2019/01/22/horsey-new-song-bread-and-butter-listen|title=London Oddballs Horsey Share Surreal, Jazz Apocalypse 'Bread & Butter'|website=[[DIY_(magazine)|DIY]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-16}}</ref> and [[post punk]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/reviews/albums/horsey-debonair-album-review|title=Experience the ludicrous and the profound on Horsey's Debonair|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-16}}</ref>

They have a strong association with [[King Krule]], who produced their debut LP and featured on the lead single  of their debut album, ''Seahorse''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2147806/horsey-seahorse-feat-king-krule/music/|title=Horsey – "Seahorse" (Feat. King Krule)|website=[[Stereogum]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-23}}</ref>  Their members include his brother Jack Marshall on bass,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gigwise.com/reviews/3400667/live-review--horsey-at-moth-club--london--06-07-21|title=Live Review: Horsey at Mothclub, London, 06/07/21|website=[[Gigwise]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-22}}</ref> his drummer George Bass on drums,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ourculturemag.com/2021/05/15/horsey-announce-debut-album-team-up-with-king-krule-for-new-song-seahorse/|title=Horsey Announce Debut Album, Team Up With King Krule for New Song 'Seahorse'|website=[[Our Culture Mag]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-22}}</ref> and his friend and collaborator Jacob Read on guitar, who is known in his own right for releasing music as Jerkcurb.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/45043/1/jerkcurb-air-con-eden-album-interview|title=Jerkcurb's songwriting evokes a surreal Americana that never existed|website=[[Dazed]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.loudandquiet.com/reviews/jerkcurb-air-con-eden|title=Jerkcurb Air Con Eden|website=[[Loud and Quiet]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/reviews/jerkcurb-air-con-eden|title=Jerkcurb - Air Con Eden|website=[[Clash (magazine)]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-23}}</ref>

They released their debut EP ''Everyone's Tongue'' in 2017, and in 2021 released their debut LP ''Debonair'' to positive critical reception.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/horsey-debonair|title=Horsey, Debonair|website=[[Pitchfork (website)| Pitchfork]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Wirth |first=Jim |url=https://archive.org/details/uncut-august-2021/page/n26/mode/1up|title=Horsey Debonair Album Review|website=[[Uncut_(magazine)|Uncut]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://archive.org/details/uncut-august-2021/page/n26/mode/1up|title=Horsey Debonair Album Review|magazine=[[Mojo_(magazine)|Mojo]]|last=McNair|first=James|language=en|access-date=2021-08-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2021/07/29/horsey-debonair-album-review|title=Horsey - Debonair|website=[[DIY (magazine) | DIY]]|language=en|access-date=2021-08-23}}</ref>
