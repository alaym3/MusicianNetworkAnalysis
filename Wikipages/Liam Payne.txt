'''Liam James Payne''' (born 29 August 1993) is an English singer and songwriter. He rose to fame as a member of the boy band [[One Direction]]. Payne made his debut as a singer in 2008 when he auditioned for the British television series ''[[The X Factor]]''. After being eliminated in the competition, he auditioned again in 2010 and was put into a group with four other contestants to form One Direction, which went on to become one of the [[Boy band#Best-selling boy bands|best-selling boy bands of all time]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Oliver|first1=Sarah|title=Zayn Malik and Liam Payne – The Biography|date=2014|publisher=John Blake Publishing|location=New York|isbn=978-1782199496}}</ref><ref name="auto">{{cite web|last1=Nash|first1=Victoria|title=Liam Payne now sprinting to X Factor success|url=https://www.expressandstar.com/entertainment/2010/09/11/liam-payne-now-sprinting-to-x-factor-success/|website=Express & Star|access-date=17 July 2015}}</ref> Payne worked with other producers under the monikers "Big Payno" and "Payno" creating remixes for songs by his group and [[Cheryl (entertainer)|Cheryl]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/one-direction/news/listen-to-1ds-liam-payno-payne-remix-of-cheryls-i-dont-care | title=Listen to 1D's Liam Payne's Remix of Cheryl's new single 'I Don't Care'. | work=MTV UK | date=14 October 2014 | access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref>

After One Direction's hiatus, Payne signed a recording deal with [[Republic Records]] in North America.<ref name="republic">{{cite magazine |last1=Kaufman |first1=Gil |title=Liam Payne Joins Ariana Grande & the Weeknd on Republic Records Roster |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7542073/liam-payne-signs-to-republic-records-joins-ariana-grande-the-weeknd- |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=16 August 2018 |date=17 October 2016}}</ref> In May 2017, Payne released "[[Strip That Down]]" as the lead single from his debut album. It peaked at number three on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and number ten on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], being certified platinum in both countries. His debut album, ''[[LP1 (Liam Payne album)|LP1]]'', was released in December 2019. He has sold over 18 million singles in just two years since One Direction and over 3.9 billion career streams in that span.<ref>{{Cite web|title=LIAM PAYNE'S DEBUT ALBUM 'LP1' IS OUT NOW|url=https://www.pressparty.com/pg/newsdesk/RepublicRecords/view/200007/|access-date=2020-10-26|website=Pressparty|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-07-18|title=Liam Payne|url=https://www.republicrecords.com/artists/liam-payne|access-date=2020-10-26|website=Republic Records|language=en}}</ref>

==Early life==
Liam James Payne was born at New Cross Hospital, in the [[Heath Town]] district of [[Wolverhampton]], [[West Midlands (region)|West Midlands]], England. He is the son of Karen Payne, an infant nurse, and Geoff Payne, a fitter. He has two older sisters, Nicola and Ruth Payne.<ref>{{cite news|title=Mums of One Direction stars reveal how the boys have grown into music sensations|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/entertainment/celebrity/mums-of-one-direction-stars-reveal-1119384|access-date=17 July 2015|work=Daily Record|date=16 April 2012}}</ref> Payne was born three weeks early, getting ill frequently. Until the age of four, Payne had regular tests done in hospital as doctors noticed one of his kidneys was scarred and dysfunctional. To help cope with the pain, he had sixteen injections in his arm every morning and another sixteen every evening as a child.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Davies|first1=Rebecca|title=One Direction's Liam Payne: 'I effectively died as a baby' |work=Digital Spy|date=18 September 2011}}</ref> As a student, Payne was heavily involved in sports, particularly cross country running. Payne originally joined the Wolverhampton and Bilston Athletics Club to pursue his running career. Payne claimed in an interview that he was '3rd best in the country at one point'.<ref>{{cite tweet |user=LaureusSport |author=Laureus |number=1229459812737527808 |title='I was the third best in the country at one point.' @LiamPayne the Olympian? #Laureus20 |date=17 February 2020 |access-date=21 March 2020}}{{Primary source inline|date=April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thepowerof10.info/athletes/profile.aspx?athleteid=59036|title=Athlete Profile|website=Thepowerof10.info}}</ref>

As he was dealing with bullying from some older students in secondary school, he took up boxing lessons at the age of twelve.<ref>{{cite news|title=One Direction's Liam Payne: "I Was Devastated After Breaking Up With Danielle Peazer|url=http://www.capitalfm.com/artists/one-direction/news/liam-payne-danielle-peazer-break-up/|agency=Capital FM}}</ref> Payne completed 11 [[GCSE]]s at [[St Peter's Collegiate School]] before moving on to study music technology at Wolverhampton College's Paget Road campus.<ref name="auto"/>

Payne was first introduced to the world of show business at age twelve as a member of the Pink Productions Theatre Company, even appearing as Tony Manero from ''[[Saturday Night Fever]]''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Liam Payne theatre company holds auditions to find their 'next star'|url=http://nativemonster.com/lifestyle/liam-payne-theatre-company-on-look-out-for-next-star|publisher=Nativemonster.com|access-date=20 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222120410/http://nativemonster.com/lifestyle/liam-payne-theatre-company-on-look-out-for-next-star|archive-date=22 December 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> Payne had previously performed in front of a crowd of 26,000 during a [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]] football match.<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{cite news|title=Liam Payne's journey from Bushbury to Big Apple|url=https://www.expressandstar.com/entertainment/music/2012/03/17/liam-paynes-journey-from-bushbury-to-big-apple/|work=Express & Star|access-date=17 July 2015}}</ref>

==Career==

===2008–2015: ''The X-Factor'' and One Direction===
Payne first auditioned for the [[The X Factor (UK series 5)|fifth series]] of ''[[The X Factor (UK)|The X Factor]]'' in 2008, at just 14 years old, in front of judges [[Simon Cowell]], [[Cheryl Cole]], [[Dannii Minogue]] and [[Louis Walsh]]. Payne moved past the first round after performing [[Frank Sinatra]]'s "Fly Me to the Moon". He was subsequently cut at the Boot Camp stage, but Cowell then changed his mind and asked Payne to return for the judges' houses stage.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.expressandstar.com/entertainment/2010/09/06/liam-payne-shows-he-has-the-x-factor/|title=Liam Payne shows he has the X Factor|newspaper=Wolverhampton Express And Star|date=6 September 2010|access-date=26 November 2015}}</ref> Payne was cut again during judges' houses, but was encouraged by Cowell to "come back in two years".<ref>{{cite episode

Payne returned to the show in 2010 for the [[The X Factor (UK series 7)|seventh series]], auditioning in front of Cowell, Cole, Walsh and guest judge [[Natalie Imbruglia]]. He sang the [[Michael Bublé]] version of "[[Cry Me a River (1953 song)|Cry Me a River]]", which earned him four yeses and a standing ovation from Cowell. Payne was considered the second favourite to win the competition following his solo audition.<ref>{{cite episode

For their qualifying song at Judges' houses, and their first song as a group, they performed an acoustic version of "[[Torn (Ednaswap song)|Torn]]". Cowell later commented that the performance convinced him that the group "were confident, fun, like a gang of friends, and kind of fearless as well." The group quickly gained popularity in the UK, ultimately coming in third place on the show.<ref>{{cite web|title=Exclusive Q&A: Simon Cowell on One Direction's Rise to Stardom|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/exclusive-q-a-simon-cowell-on-one-directions-rise-to-stardom-20120409|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=20 October 2015|date=9 April 2012}}</ref>

[[File:Liam Payne Sydney Australia.jpg|thumb|upright=0.9|Payne in 2012]]
Following ''The X Factor'', [[One Direction]] were signed to Simon Cowell's Syco Entertainment.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Nicholson|first1=Rebecca|title=Also-rans to world's biggest boy band: the rise of One Direction|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/nov/17/one-direction-biggest-band-world|work=The Guardian|access-date=20 October 2015}}</ref> A book licensed by One Direction, ''One Direction: Forever Young (Our Official X Factor Story)'', was released in February 2011 and topped ''[[The Sunday Times]]''{{'}} Best Seller list. Their debut single, "[[What Makes You Beautiful]]", was released in September 2011, and was a commercial and international success, reaching number one in several countries.<ref>{{cite magazine | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7408939/ask-billboard-one-directions-career-sales |title = Ask Billboard: One Direction's Career Sales|magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)]]|date = 19 June 2016}}</ref> Their debut album, ''[[Up All Night (One Direction album)|Up All Night]]'', was released in Ireland and the UK of November of that same year, charting at number one and number two, respectively. The album was released worldwide in March 2012, and One Direction became the first UK group to have their debut album reach number one in the United States.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.idolator.com/6226811/one-direction-debut-chart-history?safari=1&Exc_D_LessThanPoint002_p1=1 | title=One Direction Debut at No. 1, Make Album Chart History| date=21 March 2012}}</ref> Following the release of the album, the group headlined the [[Up All Night Tour]]. Originally intended with shows taking place only in the UK and Ireland,<ref>{{cite web | archive-url=http://www.freezepage.com/1333935670URKQOKXYQQ?url=http://www.stereoboard.com/content/view/167954/9 | archive-date=9 April 2012 |url=http://www.stereoboard.com/content/view/167954/9 | title=One Direction Announce UK Tour Dates & Ticket Details – Stereoboard}}</ref> Australian and North American legs of the tour were added due to demand.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.idolator.com/6226892/one-direction-north-american-tour-dates?safari=1 |title = One Direction Reveal North American Tour Dates|date = 21 March 2012}}</ref> A commercial success, tickets for the tour sold out in minutes and garnered positive reviews from critics who applauded the band's singing abilities and stage presence.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.heraldsun.com.au/subscribe/news/1/?sourceCode=HSWEB_WRE170_a&dest=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.heraldsun.com.au%2Fentertainment%2Fone-direction-infection-sweeps-melbourne%2Fnews-story%2Fd24bd97352b8eb096d0a8ddf59405a03&memtype=anonymous&mode=premium&nk=114e1396fff7ed6a2acbb008e50de654-1575638693|title=Heraldsun.com.au {{pipe}} Subscribe to the Herald Sun for exclusive stories|website=Herald Sun}}</ref> The band released ''[[Up All Night: The Live Tour]]'', a video album documenting the tour, in May 2012. That same month, One Direction's first book to be licensed in America, ''Dare to Dream: Life as One Direction'', was published and topped [[The New York Times Best Seller list|''The New York Times'' Best Seller list]].<ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.nytimes.com/best-sellers-books/2012-06-10/paperback-books/list.html |work=The New York Times | title=Best Sellers | date=10 June 2012 | access-date = 24 August 2012 | first=Gregory | last=Cowles}}</ref>

In September 2012, "[[Live While We're Young]]", the lead single from the group's second album, was released, and was a global success. Another single, "[[Little Things (One Direction song)|Little Things]]", spawned the band's second [[List of UK Singles Chart number ones of the 2010s|number one single]] in the UK.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/one-direction-score-number-1-single-and-album-__2564/ | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180830142315/http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/one-direction-score-number-1-single-and-album-__2564/ | archive-date=30 August 2018 |title = One Direction score Number 1 single AND album!| website=[[OfficialCharts.com]] }}</ref> In November 2012, the group released their second album, ''[[Take Me Home (One Direction album)|Take Me Home]]'', and it reached number one in over 35 countries. Reaching number one on the ''[[Billboard 200]]'', the group became the first boy band in US chart history to record two number-one albums in the same calendar year alongside becoming the first group since 2008 to record two number-one albums in the same year.<ref>{{cite magazine | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/474059/one-directions-take-me-home-debuts-at-no-1-with-years-third-biggest-opening |title = One Direction's 'Take Me Home' Debuts at No. 1 with Year's Third-Biggest Opening|magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)]]|date = 21 November 2012}}</ref> Following the release of the album, the group embarked on their second headlining concert tour, the [[Take Me Home Tour (One Direction)|Take Me Home Tour]]. Performing 123 shows in North America, Asia, Oceania, and Europe, grossing $114&nbsp;million. During the tour, One Direction covered [[Wheatus]]'s "Teenage Dirtbag". In the original song lead vocalist [[Brendan Brown]] performs a segment in a "female voice" which, in One Direction's rendition of the song Payne covered in falsetto. This garnered praise from Brown, who commented, "You can do my girl part anytime."<ref>{{cite news|title=One Direction Get 'Teenage Dirtbag' Love|url=http://www.mstarz.com/articles/15598/20130702/one-direction-teenage-dirtbag-love-photo-video-wheatus-praises-louis.htm|publisher=Mstarz.com|access-date=20 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208073827/http://www.mstarz.com/articles/15598/20130702/one-direction-teenage-dirtbag-love-photo-video-wheatus-praises-louis.htm|archive-date=8 December 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> In August 2013, ''[[One Direction: This Is Us]]'', a [[3D film|3-D]] documentary [[concert film]] was released, accumalting a box office gross of $68.5&nbsp;million.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/aug/20/one-direction-fans-film-premiere|title=One Direction fans queue for prime view of a global success story|first=Caroline|last=Sullivan|date=20 August 2013|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> One Direction's third book, ''One Direction: Where We Are: Our Band, Our Story: 100% Official'', was released that same month.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/one-direction-announces-we-are-556785 |title = One Direction Announces 'Where We Are' Book|website=The Hollywood Reporter|date = 23 May 2013}}</ref>

The group's third album, ''[[Midnight Memories]]'', was released in November of that same year. It was the best-selling album worldwide in 2013 with 4 million copies sold globally.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-28599903 | title=One Direction top global album chart| work=BBC News| date=August 2014}}</ref> The album's lead single, [[Best Song Ever]], is their highest charting single in the US to date.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/one-direction/chart-history/hsi/ |title = One Direction Chart History|website=Billboard.com|date = 12 September 2008}}</ref> The group embarked on their third headling tour, the [[Where We Are Tour (One Direction)|Where We Are Tour]]. Tickets sold out in minutes,<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/one-direction/news/one-direction-tour-tickets-sell-out-in-minutes |title = One Direction Tour Tickets Sell Out in Minutes |publisher=MTV}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://metro.co.uk/2013/05/25/fans-left-devastated-as-one-direction-where-we-are-tour-tickets-sell-out-in-minutes-3808314/ | title=1D add tour dates after 'instant sell-out'|website=Metro.co.uk|location=UK| date=25 May 2013}}</ref> and more shows were added due to "overwhelming demand."<ref>{{cite web | url=https://chicago.suntimes.com/news/2013/12/4/18541298/one-direction-adds-show-to-chicago-stop-on-tour |title = One Direction adds show to Chicago stop on tour|date = 4 December 2013}}</ref> The tour was their first all-stadium tour, averaging 49,848 fans per show.<ref name="billboard1">{{cite web | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6281518/one-direction-where-we-are-tour-three-million | title=One Direction's Where We Are Tour Attended by 3.4 Million Fans|website=Billboard.com| date=10 October 2014}}</ref> In total, the tour grossed over $290&nbsp;million and was the highest-grossing tour of 2014, the 15th [[List of highest-grossing concert tours|highest-grossing concert tour]] of all time, and is still the highest-grossing tour of all time by a vocal group.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2015/03/24/these-were-the-10-highest-grossing-tours-of-2014/ |title = These Were the 10 Highest-Grossing Tours of 2014|website=Forbes.com}}</ref> The tour was attended by 3.4&nbsp;million fans.<ref name="billboard1"/> In September 2014, One Direction's fourth book, ''One Direction: Who We Are: Our Official Autobiography'' was released. The group's second [[concert film]], ''[[One Direction: Where We Are – The Concert Film]]'', was released in October 2014, showing footage of the band's shows at [[San Siro Stadium]] in Milan, Italy. In November 2014, the group's fourth album, ''[[Four (One Direction album)|Four]]'', was released, making it the last album to include [[Zayn Malik]]. Singles "[[Steal My Girl]]" and "[[Night Changes]]" were released, both achieving platinum status in the US among other countries.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=One+Direction&ti=Steal+My+Girl#search_section |title = Gold & Platinum|website=Riaa.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=One+Direction&ti=Night+Changes#search_section |title = Gold & Platinum|website=Riaa.com}}</ref> The album debuted number one in 18 countries, selling 3.2&nbsp;million copies.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.lthqofficial.com/news/2018/11/17/fourth-anniversary-of-one-directions-album-four |title = Fourth Anniversary of One Direction's Album, Four – LTHQ Official}}</ref> One Direction became the only group in the 58-year history of the [[Billboard Hot 200|''Billboard'' 200]] albums chart to have their first four albums debut at number one.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Caufield|first1=Keith|title=One Direction's 'Four' Makes Historic No. 1 Debut on Billboard 200 Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6327789/one-direction-four-no-1-debut-billboard-200|website=Billboard.com|access-date=20 October 2015}}</ref> The group embarked on their fourth headlining world tour, the [[On the Road Again Tour]], grossing $208&nbsp;million and selling 2.3&nbsp;million tickets. In November 2015, One Direction's fifth album, ''[[Made in the A.M.]]'', was released. Singles "[[Drag Me Down]]" and "[[Perfect (One Direction song)|Perfect]]" both debuted at number one in various countries, and the album reached number one in the UK and number two on the US ''Billboard 200''. Following the release of the album, the group went on an indefinite hiatus.

Following the departure of former member [[Zayn Malik]], Payne was tasked with taking over the bulk of Malik's vocals.<ref>{{cite news|last1=McCorimck|first1=Neil|title=One Direction, Millennium Stadium Cardiff, review: 'business as usual'|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/live-music-reviews/11655547/One-Direction-Millenium-Stadium-Cardiff-review-In-an-ear-battering-competition-between-a-giant-PA-and-female-fandom-Id-say-the-girls-just-about-edged-it.html|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|access-date=20 October 2015}}</ref> Bandmate [[Harry Styles]] admitted, "Liam [Payne] stepped up to do the high notes. Payne was known as one of the principal songwriters in One Direction, credited for co-writing more than half of the songs on the band's third and fourth album.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Corner|first1=Lewis|title=One Direction's Liam Payne, Louis Tomlinson named band's top earners|url=http://www.digitalspy.com/music/news/a555301/one-directions-liam-payne-louis-tomlinson-named-bands-top-earners.html#~piJh0d0x9CXVuD|work=Digital Spy|access-date=20 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Liam Payne Now Writing Post-Zayn 1D Songs|url=http://www.inquisitr.com/2008232/liam-payne-now-writing-post-zayn-1d-songs-guess-next-album-title/|publisher=Inquisitr.com|access-date=20 October 2015}}</ref>

===2016–present: Solo projects and ''LP1''===
