'''Wet''' is an American [[indie pop]] group from [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. The band's first two albums—the 2013 self-titled EP and 2016 [[Don't You (Wet album)|''Don't You'']]—are credited to Kelly Zutrau, Joe Valle, and Marty Sulkow.<ref>{{cite web |last=Lanham |first=Tom |url=http://archives.sfexaminer.com/sanfrancisco/kelly-zutrau-of-wet-paints-a-song/Content?oid=2921115 |title=Kelly Zutrau of Wet paints a song |work=[[The San Francisco Examiner]] |date=February 25, 2015 |accessdate=December 4, 2015}}</ref> In March 2018, Wet released a single, "There's a Reason", as a duo of Zutrau and Valle. Signed to [[Columbia Records]], Wet was called the most promising group in music by ''[[The Fader]]'' in 2015.<ref name=FaderPromising>{{cite web |last=Cooper |first=Duncan |url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/03/04/kanye-wet |title=Wet Has What It Takes To Make Everyone Care About An Indie Band |work=[[The Fader]] |date=March 4, 2015 |accessdate=December 4, 2015}}</ref>

==History==
Wet is currently made up of members Kelly Zutrau and Joe Valle, and formerly Marty Sulkow.<ref name="Wet are back, triumphantly"/> The three met in New York through mutual friends while Sulkow and Valle were attending [[New York University]] and Zutrau was a student at [[Cooper Union]].<ref name=BillboardWet>{{cite web |last=Lipshutz |first=Jason |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5901288/wet-why-you-need-to-know-this-brooklyn-trio |title=Wet: Why You Need to Know This Brooklyn Trio |work=Billboard |date=February 13, 2014 |accessdate=December 4, 2015}}</ref> Sulkow and Zutrau formed a band called Beauty Feast in 2007.<ref name=FaderPromising/> It grew to seven members but eventually dissolved.<ref name=FaderPromising/> Zutrau went on to the [[Rhode Island School of Design]] but continued to write music.<ref name="FaderPromising" /> Sulkow and Valle became involved with her work and in 2012 the group moved in together in Sulkow's apartment in [[Bedford–Stuyvesant, Brooklyn|Bedford–Stuyvesant]].<ref name="FaderPromising" />

=== Neon Gold and ''Wet'' ===
The group began booking gigs around Brooklyn and posting music online, gaining attention around 2013. Wet signed with the boutique record label [[Neon Gold Records|Neon Gold]] during that same year. Neon Gold had a partnership with [[Columbia Records]] at the time Wet signed with them.<ref name="FaderPromising" /> The label switched its partnership to [[Atlantic Records]] in 2014 and Wet subsequently signed with Columbia after fielding offers from numerous major labels.<ref name="FaderPromising" /> On September 24, Neon Gold announced that Wet would be releasing a self-titled extended play.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://neon.gold/gold047-wet/|title=GOLD047: WET – Neon Gold Records|website=neon.gold|language=en-US|access-date=2017-03-13}}</ref> It was released on October 3 and October 15 on their Bandcamp.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://allwet.bandcamp.com/album/wet|title=Wet, by Wet|website=Wet|access-date=2017-03-13}}</ref>

=== ''Don't You'' ===
The group moved to [[Western Massachusetts]] in 2014 where they began working on their debut album under Columbia.<ref name="FaderPromising" /> The album, titled ''[[Don't You (Wet album)|Don't You]]'', was released on January 29, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|title=Don't You by Wet on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/dont-you/id1069079628|website=iTunes|date=29 January 2016|accessdate=19 January 2017}}</ref> The album peaked at number 55 on the [[Canadian Albums Chart]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6766153/Wet/chart?f=309|title=Wet - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=www.billboard.com|access-date=2017-03-13}}</ref>

In October 2016, the band released a pair of singles, "The Middle" and "Turn Away".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1906427/wet-the-middle-turn-away/music/|title=Wet – "The Middle" & "Turn Away"|last=Robinson|first=Collin|date=2016-10-20|work=Stereogum|access-date=2018-03-10}}</ref>

=== ''Still Run'' ===
In March 2018, the band released a single "There's a Reason" as a duo consisting of Zutrau and Valle;<ref>{{cite news|last=Cooper|first=Duncan|title=Wet are back, triumphantly|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/03/09/wet-theres-a-reason|accessdate=10 March 2018|work=The Fader|date=March 9, 2018}}</ref> Andrew Sarlo is also credited as a producer.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Israelsohn|first1=Sophie|title=Wet – "There's A Reason"|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1985977/wet-theres-a-reason/music/|accessdate=10 March 2018|work=Stereogum|date=9 March 2018}}</ref> On April 13, 2018, the band released "Softens", the second song from their upcoming album.<ref>{{cite news|last=Colburn|first=Randall|title=Wet shares tender new single, "Softens": Stream|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/04/wet-shares-tender-new-single-softens-stream/|accessdate=13 April 2018|work=COS|date=April 13, 2018}}</ref>
