'''Tyree Lamar Pittman'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/work/885069955 |title=ACE Repertory |website=Ascap.com |access-date=2020-04-29}}</ref> (born November 14, 1993), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Young Chop''', is an American [[record producer]], rapper, and songwriter. In 2012, he gained widespread recognition within the American hip hop community for producing [[Chief Keef]]'s hits "[[I Don't Like]]", "[[Love Sosa]]", and "3Hunna". He released his first studio album ''Precious'' in 2013, established his own label, Chop Squad Records, and released the album ''Still'' the following year. Young Chop, with [[Chief Keef]], the late [[Fredo Santana]], and others, belong to the [[Chicago]] collective called 3hunna, making [[drill music]] in which they generally rap about drug dealing, weapons, and their lifestyles.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TybFtK6VTVo Welcome to Chiraq - Chiraq - Ep 1] YouTube.com</ref>{{Unreliable source?|date=June 2020}}

==Life and career==
Pittman grew up in the [[South Side, Chicago|South Side]] of Chicago, Illinois. Pittman started making beats when he was 11 years old with the help of his cousin.<ref name="ref3">[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=whvS70vlARM Off the Record: Young Chop] YouTube.com</ref>{{Unreliable source?|date=June 2020}} He met [[Chief Keef]] on Facebook and went on to produce many of his songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.reddit.com/r/hiphopheads/comments/2ksjug/my_name_is_young_chop_on_the_beat_ama/clodu7w?context=3|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150101012034/http://www.reddit.com/r/hiphopheads/comments/2ksjug/my_name_is_young_chop_on_the_beat_ama/clodu7w?context=3|archive-date=2015-01-01|title=YoungChop comments on My name is Young Chop on the Beat AMA|date = 30 October 2014}}</ref> Pittman is part of a production team collective called BandKamp which also includes producer and CEO BandKamp, Paris Beuller and intern WaldooBeatz, Chief Keef's ''[[Back from the Dead (mixtape)|Back From The Dead]]'' was officially the first mixtape he produced.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globalgrind.com/music/young-chop-behind-chief-keefs-i-dont-like-remix-interview |title=The Man Behind Chief Keef's "I Don't Like:" Meet Young Chop |website=Globalgrind.com |date=2012-08-20 |access-date=2020-04-29}}</ref> Pittman recently started his own independent label called Chop Squad.<ref name="Sherron, Shabazz">{{cite web | url=http://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.2491/title.young-chop-says-i-don-t-even-know-what-drill-music-is- | title=Young Chop Says, "I Don't Even Know What Drill Music Is" | publisher=HipHopDX | date=July 23, 2014 | access-date=17 October 2015 | author=Sherron, Shabazz}}</ref> He has also started a website called SoundKitWiz.com, an online retail music production website where producers and engineers can purchase audio engineering and music production related products.<ref name="Sherron, Shabazz"/> His producer tag is "Young Chop On the Beat", spoken by his 4 year old nephew.

===Controversy===
In April 2020, Pittman uploaded a video on [[Instagram]] of him firing shots outside his house after people outside were calling his name.<ref>{{cite web |title=Young Chop Fires Gun From His Porch at People in Car |url=https://www.vladtv.com/article/261673/young-chop-fires-gun-from-his-porch-at-people-in-car?page=2 |website=[[VladTV]] |access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> On April 5, 2020, Pittman was streaming on [[Instagram Live]] whilst looking for [[Atlanta]]-based rapper [[21 Savage]].<ref name="ubershot">{{cite web |title=Young Chop Goes Looking for 21 Savage, Claims He Was Shot At |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2020/04/young-chop-shot-at-looking-for-21-savage/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |publisher=XXL |access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> Pittman had already called out 21 Savage publicly a few days before the incident.<ref>{{cite web |title=Young Chop Disses 21 Savage |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2020/03/young-chop-disses-21-savage/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> After returning to the [[Uber]] car transporting him, Pittman claimed that the vehicle was shot at, showing footage of shattered glass as proof.<ref name="ubershot"/>

On April 16, 2020, Pittman was arrested in [[Gwinnett County, Georgia]] for violating his probation.<ref name="violatingprobation">{{cite magazine |last1=Peters |first1=Mitchell |title=Young Chop Arrested for Violating Probation in Georgia |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9361155/young-chop-arrested-violating-probation-georgia |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> He was also charged with aggravated cruelty to animals-death after allegedly starving his dog to death in February 2020.<ref name="dogstarve">{{cite web |title=Update: Young Chop Arrested for Allegedly Starving a Dog to Death |url=https://www.vladtv.com/article/261933/update-young-chop-arrested-for-allegedly-starving-a-dog-to-death |website=[[VladTV]] |access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref>
