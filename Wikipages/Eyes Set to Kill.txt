'''Eyes Set to Kill''' is an American [[post-hardcore]]/[[metalcore]] band from [[Tempe, Arizona]].<ref name="Allmusic"/> The Rodriguez sisters, lead guitarist Alexia (born November 23, 1987) and bassist Anissa (born January 27, 1989),<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.newstimes.com/news/article/Eyes-Set-To-Kill-make-huge-impact-in-Brazil-220707.php |title=Eyes Set To Kill make huge impact in Brazil |date=May 13, 2009 |website=Newstimes.com |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref> along with vocalist Lindsey Vogt started the band in 2003. Lindsey Vogt departed the band in mid-2007 due to issues with management. Lindsey would go on to form a solo project known as "The Taro Sound", and eventually form the band "The Attraction." After Lindsey's departure, Alexia assumed her position as lead vocalist as well as guitarist. Anissa left in late 2016.<ref name="Allmusic">Heaney, Gregory. "[{{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p1020563/biography|pure_url=yes}} Biography]". [[AllMusic]]</ref>

''[[The World Outside (Eyes Set to Kill album)|The World Outside]]'' received widespread critical acclaim and the group was featured on the cover of ''USA Today'', as one of Alternative Press Magazine's "100 Bands You Need To Know", and hosted an episode of MTV's Headbangers Ball.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.myspace.com/eyessettokill |title=Eyes Set to KILL &#124; Listen and Stream Free Music, Albums, New Releases, Photos, Videos |website=Myspace.com |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref> ''[[Broken Frames]]'' was ranked fifth on the list of "Locals Only: The Best Albums and EPs in 2010".<ref name="PerhoenixNewtimes">{{Cite news |url=http://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/2010-12-30/music/phoenix-only-the-best-albums-and-eps-from-valley-bands-in-2010/ |title=Locals Only: The Best Albums and EPs From Valley Bands in 2010 |last=Cizmar |first=Martin |date=December 30, 2010 |work=[[Phoenix New Times]] |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref>

== History ==
Eyes Set to Kill began in 2003 as a three-piece band from [[Tempe, Arizona]]. Alexia Rodriguez (lead guitar, backing vocals), Anissa Rodriguez (bass), and Lindsey Vogt (lead vocals); their name derived from a line out of a poem Rodriguez{{which|date=August 2020}} wrote in secondary school. Over the next several months, the band continued to undergo changes in their lineup. In early 2006 Alex Torres (rhythm guitar), Corey Pattakos (backing vocals), and Caleb Clifton (drums) joined recorded a nine-song EP, ''When Silence Is Broken, the Night Is Torn'', at Larry Elyea's Glendale, Arizona home studio: Mind's Eye Digital Recording Studios. The EP featured guitarist Alex Torres and original vocalist, Lindsey Vogt and sold 11,000 units. Their debut full-length album, ''Reach'', was released February 19, 2008, peaked at No.&nbsp;29 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] Heatseekers chart and No.&nbsp;77 on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Independent Music charts in 2008 selling 1,900 copies in its first week.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/eyes-set-to-kill-mn0000669742/awards |title=Eyes Set to Kill &#124; Awards |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref> The first single from the album was "Reach", with their following single as "Darling".

Their second album, ''[[The World Outside (Eyes Set to Kill album)|The World Outside]]'', was released June 2, 2009. It sold 2,400 copies in the U.S. in its first week of release, according to Nielsen SoundScan.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.idiomag.com/artist/eyes%20set%20to%20kill#/93681/1/ |title=Eyes Set To Kill Magazine – News, Reviews, Albums and Videos |date=July 24, 2009 |website=Idiomag.com |access-date=July 2, 2010}}</ref>  and peaked at No.&nbsp;9 on the ''Billboard'' Heatseekers chart<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2009-06-20/heatseekers-albums |title=Heatseekers Albums: Up and Coming Musicians Chart |date=June 20, 2009 |website=Billboard.com |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref> and No.&nbsp;26 on the Independent Albums chart<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2009-06-20/independent-albums |title=Independent Albums |date=June 20, 2009 |website=Billboard.com |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref>

Their third studio album, ''[[Broken Frames]]'', was released on June 8, 2010. Its first single, "All You Ever Knew", was released on April 1, 2010 on revolvermag.com and on their MySpace page. Brandon Anderson resigned from the band in early July.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.revolvermag.com/music/detail/exclusive-mp3-eyes-set-to-kill-premiere-all-you-ever-knew/ |title=Exclusive MP3: Eyes Set to Kill Premiere "All You Ever Knew," the First Single From Their New Album – Revolver Music |website=Revolvermag.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100610091257/http://www.revolvermag.com/music/detail/exclusive-mp3-eyes-set-to-kill-premiere-all-you-ever-knew/ |archive-date=June 10, 2010 |access-date=July 2, 2010}}</ref>

On October 12, 2010, Rodriguez{{which|date=August 2020}} released her debut solo album, ''[[Underground Sounds]]'' as Lexia. On October 26, 2010, "Let Me In", which as released as a bonus track on ''Broken Frames'', was released as its first single with a video.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=148374 |title=Eyes Set To Kill Frontwoman Releases 'Let Me In' Video - Blabbermouth.net |date=October 26, 2010 |website=Roadrunnerrecords.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101030153303/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=148374 |archive-date=October 30, 2010 |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref> Greg Kerwin left the band in early 2011.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/eyes_set_to_kill_part_ways_with_guitarist_vocalist_greg_kerwin/ |title=Eyes Set To Kill part ways with guitarist/vocalist Greg Kerwin - News - Alternative Press |website=Altpress.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110201053834/http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/eyes_set_to_kill_part_ways_with_guitarist_vocalist_greg_kerwin/ |archive-date=February 1, 2011 |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref>

On June 7, 2011, the band announced they started their own record label, Foresee Records,<ref name="grafwall.indiestar.tv" /> and would no longer be with Breaksilence Records. They released ''[[White Lotus (album)|White Lotus]]'' through a joint venture between their own label Foresee Records and Maphia Entertainment on August 9,2011.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/eyessettokill?sk=info |title=Eyes Set To Kill |website=Facebook.com |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref> They embarked on a tour with [[Falling in Reverse]] and [[For All Those Sleeping]] throughout September and October 2011.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/falling_in_reverse_announce_early_fall_tour |title=Falling In Reverse announce early fall tour - News - Alternative Press |website=Altpress.com |access-date=April 22, 2016}}</ref>

On July 19, 2012, they announced signing to [[Century Media Records]].<ref name="Eyes Set to Kill" /> On December 20, 2012, they reached 1,000,000 likes on [[Facebook]] prompting them to release a free download of a new demo for their upcoming album.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/eyes_set_to_kill_release_true_colors_demo_for_free_download |title=Eyes Set To Kill release "True Colors" demo for free download - News - Alternative Press |date=December 20, 2012 |website=Altpress.com |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref> To promote their upcoming album the band released a video for the song "Infected" on September 5, 2013. ''Masks'' was released September 17, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://loudwire.com/eyes-set-to-kill-alexia-anissa-rodriguez-masks-musical-influences/ |title=Eyes Set to Kill Talk 'Masks,' Musical Influences + More |last=Raman |first=Liz |website=Loudwire}}</ref>

On July 10, they announced that they were forced to cancel an Oklahoma City show due a health issue with Anissa Rodriguez.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://loudwire.com/eyes-set-to-kill-anissa-rodriguez-released-from-hospital-blood-clot-scare/ |title=Eyes Set to Kill's Anissa Rodriguez Released From Hospital |last=Hartmann |first=Graham |website=Loudwire}}</ref> In September 2015, they were support for [[New Years Day (band)|New Years Day]] on their "Other Side Tour" North American Tour.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://screamermagazine.com/press-releases/eyes-set-to-kill-to-join-new-years-day-the-other-side-tour/ |title=Eyes Set To Kill To Join New Year's Day "The Other Side" Tour |date=September 24, 2015 |website=Screamer Magazine}}</ref>

In March 2016 the band announced a co-headliner tour named "The Visions Tour" with Texas-based band [[One-Eyed Doll]] and Open Your Eyes as support. To promote the tour, Eyes Set to Kill and One-Eyed Doll recorded a cover of Depeche Mode's "Enjoy the Silence".<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://piercingmetal.com/one-eyed-doll-eyes-set-to-kill-announce-co-headlining-the-visions-tour/ |title=One-Eyed Doll & Eyes Set To Kill Announce Co-Headlining "The Visions" Tour |date=March 9, 2016 |website=PiercingMetal.com}}</ref>

The band released, "Break", in advance of their sixth album on March 24, 2017 via [[Spotify]] and on February 1, 2018, "Not Sorry". On February 16, 2018, they released their self-titled album via Century Media Records.<ref name=":0" />

In 2017, Alexia Rodriguez formed band The Secret Destroyers with former vocalist Lindsey Vogt and Teresa Brenneman.<ref>{{cite web | title=The Secret Destroyers | website=Tempe Marketplace | url=https://tempemarketplace.com/event/the-secret-destroyers/ | access-date=2020-06-16}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title=The Secret Destroyers | website=Facebook | url=https://www.facebook.com/TheSecretDestroyers/photos/a.345245885878337/345246325878293/?type=3 | access-date=2020-06-16}}</ref>

On February 18, 2021 Eyes Set To Kill premiered new single "Find Our Way" via SiriusXM Octane, their first with new label Revival Recordings. The band's track "Face The Rain (Feat. Howard Jones)" also premiered on Octane, and was released on September 23, 2021 alongside the announcement that their new EP DAMNA would be released on December 3, 2021.
