'''Tenille Townes''' (born '''Tenille Nicole Nadkrynechny'''; January 20, 1994) is a Canadian [[country music]] singer from [[Grande Prairie]], [[Alberta]]. In 2011, at the age of 17, she was nominated for a [[Canadian Country Music Association|Canadian Country Music Award]] for Female Artist of the Year.<ref>{{cite news|last=MacLeod|first=Reinisa|date=August 2011|title=Tenille takes on the country|url=http://www.dailyheraldtribune.com/ArticleDisplay.aspx?e=3273711|newspaper=[[Media in Grande Prairie|Daily Herald-Tribune]]|accessdate=12 September 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Griwkowsky|first=Fish|date=27 July 2011|title=Two Edmonton country stars nominated for music awards|url=http://www.canada.com/story_print.html?id=5169029&sponsor=|newspaper=[[Edmonton Journal]]|accessdate=20 September 2011}}{{Dead link|date=June 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=no }}</ref>

== Biography ==
Townes was raised in Grande Prairie, Alberta, and attended Peace Wapiti Academy high school.<ref name=national>{{cite news|last=Wood|first=Damien|date=2010|title=Tenille's song hits national radio|url=http://www.dailyheraldtribune.com/ArticleDisplay.aspx?archive=true&e=2157444|newspaper=Daily Herald-Tribune|accessdate=20 September 2011}}</ref> In 2009, she released the single "Home Now", a song she wrote from the perspective of a daughter whose father is posted in the [[War in Afghanistan (2001–present)|war in Afghanistan]], a topic she learned about in school.<ref name=national /><ref name=Sperounes>{{cite news|last=Sperounes|first=Sandra|date=11 November 2009|title=Alberta teen pays tribute to war heroes: Home Now captures national sentiment|newspaper=[[Edmonton Journal]]|page=D1}}</ref> The track was produced by country musician [[Duane Steele]].<ref name=Sperounes />

She released her first album, ''Real'', in June 2011.<ref>{{cite news|last=Ilika|first=Dan|date=June 2011|title=Dream becomes 'Real' for Tenille|url=http://www.dailyheraldtribune.com/ArticleDisplay.aspx?e=3175771|newspaper=Daily Herald-Tribune|accessdate=20 September 2011}}</ref> She has raised over $1.9 million for Sunrise House, a shelter for homeless youth in Alberta, through her annual fundraiser, Big Hearts For Big Kids. She appeared on the national television show ''[[Canada AM]]'' in August 2011, performing her single "Real Me,"<ref>{{cite news|last=Rinne|first=Diana|date=August 2011|title=Tenille nominated for first Canadian Country Music Award|url=http://www.dailyheraldtribune.com/ArticleDisplay.aspx?e=3235477|newspaper=Daily Herald-Tribune|accessdate=20 September 2011}}</ref> and has released multiple songs to radio. She released her second album, ''Light'', in March 2013.

Although she started her career under her birthname, she later changed her surname to "Townes." In a 2018 interview on Everything GP, she explained, "Townes is actually from Township Road 722, which is the road that I grew up on and the house that built me essentially, so that’s where that all comes from.”<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://everythinggp.com/2018/03/12/local-musician-makes-it-big/|title=Local musician makes it big}}</ref>

On April 13, 2018, she announced, through her official [[Facebook]] page that she had signed a record deal with [[Sony Music Nashville|Columbia Nashville]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Townes|first1=Tenille|title=Tenille Townes signs with Columbia Nashville|url=https://www.facebook.com/tenilletownes/|accessdate=13 April 2018|agency=Facebook|publisher=Tenille Townes}}</ref> She released her first single with the label, "[[Somebody's Daughter (Tenille Townes song)|Somebody's Daughter]]," in September 2018, and it became her first to chart, where it reached number 92 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] and was a Number One hit on the [[Canada Country|Canadian Country]] chart dated February 2, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/2019-02-02/canada-country|title=Canada Country chart for February 2, 2019|work=Billboard|accessdate=January 31, 2019}}</ref>

In 2018, she served as the opening act for all dates for [[Miranda Lambert]] and [[Little Big Town]] on their joint ''The Bandwagon Tour''. In 2019 she appeared on [[Donovan Woods (musician)|Donovan Woods]]'s album ''The Other Way'', as a duet vocalist on the song "I Ain't Ever Loved No One".<ref name=otherway>[https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/donovan-woods-truck-full-of-money-acoustic-the-other-way-822900/ "Hear Donovan Woods’ New Acoustic Version of ‘Truck Full of Money’"]. ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', April 16, 2019.</ref>

Townes won four awards at the 2019 [[Canadian Country Music Association]] (CCMA) Awards, which included one for Female Artist of the year, and three for "Somebody's Daughter" which won Single, Song, and Video of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailyheraldtribune.com/entertainment/local-arts/it-was-a-crazy-feeling-tenille-townes-reflects-on-four-wins-at-country-music-awards|title='It was a crazy feeling': Tenille Townes reflects on four wins at Country Music Awards|author=Peter Shokeir|date=September 10, 2019|publisher=Daily Herald Tribune|accessdate=September 10, 2019}}</ref>
