'''Maher Zain''' ({{lang-ar|ماهر زين}}; born 16 July 1981)<ref>{{cite tweet|user=MaherZain|author=Maher Zain|number=312996761113346049|date=16 March 2013|title=It is not my birthday today! :) it is July 16... :))))}}</ref> is a [[Lebanon|Lebanese]]-[[Sweden|Swedish]] [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] singer, songwriter and music producer.<ref>{{cite news|title=Muslim pop megastar Maher Zain sings for Syria aid|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-24920208 |publisher=BBC News |date=13 November 2013 |access-date=24 August 2014}}</ref> He released his debut album ''[[Thank You Allah]]'', an internationally successful album produced by [[Awakening Records]], in 2009. He released his follow-up album ''[[Forgive Me (Maher Zain album)|Forgive Me]]'' in April 2012 under the same production company, and a third album [[One (Maher Zain album)]] in 2016.

==Career==

===Beginnings===
Maher Zain's Lebanese family emigrated to [[Sweden]] when he was eight.<ref>{{cite web |title=Maher zain's story|url= https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJbnHVRCMqo |website=YouTube |access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref> He completed his schooling there, gaining a bachelor's degree in [[Aerospace engineering|Aeronautical Engineering]]. After university, he entered the music industry in Sweden, and in 2005 linked up with the [[Morocco|Moroccan]]-born Swedish producer [[RedOne]].<ref name="Atlantic">Entertainment (2011-08-10). [https://www.theatlantic.com/entertainment/archive/2011/08/maher-zains-hip-but-pious-soundtrack-to-the-arab-spring/243191/ "Maher Zain's Hip but Pious Soundtrack to the Arab Spring"]. ''The Atlantic''. Retrieved 10 October 2011.</ref> When RedOne moved to New York in 2006, Zain soon followed to continue his music industry career in the United States, producing for artists such as [[Kat DeLuna]].<ref>Eena Houzyama (2010-10-11). [http://stage2.mmail.com.my/node/51854 "Thank God for Maher Zain"]. ''The Malay Mail'', retrieved 10 October 2011  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120910030702/http://stage2.mmail.com.my/node/51854 |date=10 September 2012 }}</ref>

On returning home to Sweden, he became engaged once more with his Islamic faith<ref>{{cite news| last1=Shahid |first1=Omar |title=Maher Zain: 'My music is a message of Islam' |url= https://www.theguardian.com/music/2011/dec/15/maher-zain-music-message-of-islam |newspaper=The Guardian |date=15 December 2011 |access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref> and decided to move away from a career as a music producer to become a singer/songwriter of contemporary R&B music with a strong [[Muslim]] religious influence.<ref>[http://www.islamiclyrics.net/maher-zain/ "Maher Zain"]. IslamicLyrics, retrieved 10 October 2011</ref>

===Breakthrough and success===
In January 2009, Maher Zain began working on an album with [[Awakening Records]]. His debut album, ''[[Thank You Allah]]'', with 13 songs and two bonus tracks, was released on 1 November 2009 with percussion versions and [//www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kvger_C4LN8 French] versions of some tracks released shortly afterwards.<ref>Music & Festivals [http://www.timeoutbeirut.com/music-festivals/article/4875/maher-zain.html "Maher Zain Interview"]. ''Time Out Beirut'', retrieved 14 October 2011  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120326133002/http://www.timeoutbeirut.com/music-festivals/article/4875/maher-zain.html |date=26 March 2012 }}</ref>

Zain and Awakening Records successfully used social media such as [[Facebook]], [[YouTube]]<ref>Admin (2010-11-16). [http://www.nxtnews.com/muslim-singer-maher-zain/ "Muslim Singer Maher Zain"]. ''NXT News'', retrieved 14 October 2011 {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120313214942/http://www.nxtnews.com/muslim-singer-maher-zain/ |date=13 March 2012 }}</ref> and [[iTunes]] to promote tracks from the album.<ref name="JKGlobe">Lisa Siregar (2011-05-02). [http://www.thejakartaglobe.com/lifeandtimes/a-portrait-of-the-artist-as-a-young-muslim/438684 "A Portrait of  the Artist As a Young Muslim"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111001163807/http://www.thejakartaglobe.com/lifeandtimes/a-portrait-of-the-artist-as-a-young-muslim/438684 |date=1 October 2011 }}. ''Jakarta Globe'', retrieved 14 October 2011</ref><ref name="Straits">2011-08-26 [http://www.nst.com.my/nst/articles/16folas/Article/index_html "Being pious in the heart of modernity"]. ''New Straits Time'', retrieved 14 October 2011  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110826151347/http://www.nst.com.my/nst/articles/16folas/Article/index_html#ixzz1XEqpfxMq |date=26 August 2011 }}</ref> In early 2010 his music very quickly gathered a huge online following in Arabic-speaking and Islamic countries as well as among young Muslims in western countries.<ref>Sundus Awan (Transcribed) 2010-11-29. [http://muslimyouthvoice.ca/node/345 "Maher Zain Interview"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110904011718/http://muslimyouthvoice.ca/node/345# |date=4 September 2011 }}. ''The Muslim Youth Voice Website'', retrieved 14 October 2011</ref> By the end of 2010, he was the most Googled celebrity in Malaysia for that year.<ref name="Straits" /> Malaysia and Indonesia have been the countries where he has had the most commercial success. The album ''[[Thank You Allah]]'' has been certified multiple platinum by Warner Music Malaysia and Sony Music Indonesia.<ref name="Atlantic" /> It became the highest selling album of 2010 in Malaysia.<ref name="Maher Zain receives 8 platinums in Malaysia" />

Zain sings mainly in English but has released some of his most popular songs in other languages.  The song  "Insha Allah", for example, is now available in English, French, Arabic, Turkish, Malay and Indonesian versions. Another song, "Allahi Allah Kiya Karo" ("Continuously Saying Allah"), is sung in [[Urdu]] and features the [[Pakistan]] born Canadian singer [[Irfan Makki]].<ref name="JKGlobe"/> Zain has performed concerts around the world, including in the [[UK]], the [[United States]], [[Malaysia]], [[Indonesia]], [[Saudi Arabia]] and [[Egypt]]. He has fan clubs in several countries including Malaysia, Egypt, and Morocco.<ref>{{cite web|title=Maher Zain Indonesia Fanclub|url=https://www.facebook.com/MaherZain/posts/286663124679608|access-date=24 August 2014}}</ref> He took part in the judging committee of the Awakening Talent Contest to choose Awakening Records' new star in 2013.<ref>{{cite news|title=Awakening Talent Contest – Talents Matter Most|url=http://www.emmagem.com/v1/article/awakening-talent-contest-talents-matter-most/|publisher=EmmaGem|date=11 July 2013|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref>

==Collaborations, appearances and awards==
<!-- Deleted image removed: [[File:Maher Zain Press Conference.jpeg|(160 × 240 pixels)px|thumbnail|right|Maher Zain in a press conference at Tangier, Morocco (February 2014)]] -->
In January 2010, Maher Zain won Best Religious Song for 'Ya Nabi Salam Alayka', on Nogoum FM, a major Middle East mainstream music station, beating other prominent singers including Hussein Al-Jismi, [[Mohammed Mounir]] and [[Sami Yusuf]].<ref>News Summary (January 2010). [http://www.awakeningworldwide.com/05_news/index_2010_01.htm "Maher Zain wins Nugoom FM's Best Song Award"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140322070423/http://www.awakeningworldwide.com/05_news/index_2010_01.htm# |date=22 March 2014 }}. Awakening Worldwide. Retrieved 2 September 2011.</ref>

In March 2011, Maher Zain released "Freedom",<ref name="JKGlobe"/> a song inspired by the events and the actions of the people taking part in the [[Arab Spring]].

Maher Zain was chosen as a Muslim Star of 2011 in a competition organized by Onislam.net.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.onislam.net/english/culture-and-entertainment/music/456364-insha-allah-.html|title=Insha Allah|access-date=1 April 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120401223429/http://www.onislam.net/english/culture-and-entertainment/music/456364-insha-allah-.html|archive-date=1 April 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> In July 2011 he featured on the cover of the UK Muslim lifestyle magazine ''[[Emel (magazine)|Emel]]''.<ref>Issue 82 (July 2011). [http://www.emel.com/article?id=89&a_id=2421 Maher Zain digital issue – OUT NOW!]. Emel. Retrieved 18 October 2011.</ref>

Zain was featured on [[Irfan Makki]]'s track "I Believe" from his debut studio album of the same name.<ref>[http://www.islamiclyrics.net/irfan-makki/i-believe/ "I Believe Lyrics"]. IslamicLyrics, retrieved 18 October 2011.</ref>

Maher Zain has appeared in the 40-episode Indonesian TV drama ''Insya-Allah''. The show was aired on Malaysian satellite TV channel, [[Astro Oasis]] and [[Mustika HD]], starting 17 July 2012, concurrent with the broadcasting of the show on Indonesia's [[SCTV (Indonesia)|SCTV]].<ref>[http://www.hmetro.com.my/articles/MaherZainbintangisinetron/Article Maher Zain bintangi sinetron] ''Harian Metro'' retrieved 26 June 2012 {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120628002732/http://www.hmetro.com.my/articles/MaherZainbintangisinetron/Article |date=28 June 2012 }}</ref>

In 2013, he took part in the ''Colors of Peace'' project constituting songs based on works by [[Fethullah Gülen]] on the album ''Rise Up'' where Maher Zain performs the track "This Worldly Life".<ref>{{cite news|date=9 March 2013|url=http://www.radikal.com.tr/Radikal.aspx?aType=RadikalDetayV3&ArticleID=1124369&CategoryID=82|title=Barış için müzikal birlik|newspaper=[[Radikal]]|language=tr|trans-title=Musical unity for peace}}</ref><ref>[http://zaman-online.de/18573/savas%CC%A7in-kars%CC%A7isina-du%CC%88nyayi-kucaklayan-bir-albu%CC%88mle-c%CC%A7iktilar ''Zaman'' Online: Savaşın karşısına Hocaefendi’nin şiirlerinden oluşan albümle çıktılar] (In Turkish)  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130311122615/http://zaman-online.de/18573/savas%CC%A7in-kars%CC%A7isina-du%CC%88nyayi-kucaklayan-bir-albu%CC%88mle-c%CC%A7iktilar |date=11 March 2013 }}</ref>

==Philanthropic activities==
*In 2013, Zain performed in [[Canada]] on a tour organised by [[Islamic Relief]] to raise donations<ref>{{cite news|last1=S. QAZI|title=Islamic Relief Canada Declared War On Hunger to Support Poor People|url=https://push.pk/380|publisher=push.pk|access-date=20 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140821145955/https://push.pk/380|archive-date=21 August 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> for [[typhoon]] victims in the [[Philippines]].<!-- <ref>{{cite news|title=Islamic Relief Canada seeking donations for typhoon victims in Philippines|url=https://www.insidehalton.com/news-story/4226584-islamic-relief-canada-seeking-donations-for-typhoon-victims-in-philippines/|website=InsideHalton.com|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref>  unsafe website! --> Zain took part in the [[UK]] "Sound of Light" event<ref>{{cite web|title=Sound Of Light Official Website|url=http://soundsoflight.org/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20010715091026/http://soundsoflight.org/|archive-date=15 July 2001|access-date=20 August 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> organized by [[Human Appeal International]] in support of the [[Syrian]] people.<ref>{{cite news|title=Muslim pop megastar Maher Zain sings for Syria aid|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-middle-east-24920208|publisher=BBC News|date=13 November 2013|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref> Zain also dedicated his song "Love will prevail" (sung in Arabic and English) to the Syrians.{{citation needed|date=June 2016}}
* Zain celebrated his birthday with his 3.9 million Facebook fans by asking them to donate money<ref name="BirthdayCharity">{{cite web|title=My Birthday For Clean Water|url=https://my.charitywater.org/maher-zain-s-birthday-charity-water/maherzain|publisher=Charity: Water|author=Maher Zain|access-date=7 December 2017}}</ref> to a United States–based charity organization that builds water wells in Africa.<ref>{{cite web|title=Koba Mender Community|url=https://my.charitywater.org/p/myprojectsview?project_id=ET.GOH.Q3.12.124.102&campaign_id=28311|access-date=7 December 2017}}</ref> His fans paid more than $15,000 within a few weeks.<ref name="BirthdayCharity"/>
*Zain took part in a [[London]] pro-Palestinian rally, joined by thousands of people, to call for the end to [[Israel]]i military action in [[Gaza Strip|Gaza]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Maher Zain – Freedom at the London demonstration GAZA protest 19/07/2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kyKxO6AZm4Q|website=[[YouTube]]|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref> In August 2014, Zain took part in "The Great Wall of China Trek 2014" a 10-day humanitarian mission in association with Human Appeal to raise donations for clean water to children in Gaza.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://humanappeal.org.uk/events/great-wall-of-china-trek-2014/|title=Great Wall of China Trek 2014 with Maher Zain & Omar Regan|access-date=7 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Maher Zain treks through China for Gaza|url=http://www.vocfm.co.za/maher-zain-treks-through-china-for-gaza/|publisher=[[Voice of the Cape]]|location=Cape Town, South Africa|date=18 August 2014|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Maher Zain Instagram|url=http://instagram.com/p/r3zOjjSoOK/?modal=true|website=[[Instagram]]|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref>
