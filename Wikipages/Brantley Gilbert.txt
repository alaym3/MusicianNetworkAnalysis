'''Brantley Keith Gilbert'''  (born January 20, 1985) is an American [[country music]] singer, songwriter and record producer from [[Jefferson, Georgia|Jefferson]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]]. He was originally signed to [[Colt Ford]]'s label, [[Average Joes Entertainment]], where he released ''[[Modern Day Prodigal Son]]'' and ''[[Halfway to Heaven (album)|Halfway to Heaven]]''. He is now signed to the Valory division of [[Big Machine Records]] where he has released four studio albums—a deluxe edition of ''Halfway to Heaven'', ''[[Just as I Am (Brantley Gilbert album)|Just as I Am]]'', ''[[The Devil Don't Sleep]]'', ''[[Fire & Brimstone (album)|Fire & Brimstone]]'', and 11 country chart entries, four of which have gone to number one. He also co-wrote (with Colt Ford) and originally recorded [[Jason Aldean]]'s singles "[[My Kinda Party (song)|My Kinda Party]]" and "[[Dirt Road Anthem]]."

==Career==
===2007-2013: ''Modern Day Prodigal Son'' and ''Halfway to Heaven''===
Brantley Gilbert went to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] as a songwriter, signed to Warner Chappell Publishing.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/brantley-gilbert/biography/ |title=Brantley Gilbert Bio &#124; Brantley Gilbert Career |publisher=CMT |access-date=2014-07-27}}</ref> He continued performing at local venues. In 2009, he released his debut album, ''[[Modern Day Prodigal Son]]'', under independent label [[Average Joes Entertainment]]. He followed with ''[[Halfway to Heaven (album)|Halfway to Heaven]]'' in 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.brantleygilbert.com/discography.html |title=Albums and Songs |publisher=Brantley Gilbert |access-date=2014-07-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160601073334/http://brantleygilbert.com/discography.html |archive-date=2016-06-01 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2011, he signed with Valory Music Co., a division of [[Big Machine Records]], who released a deluxe edition of ''Halfway to Heaven''. The album was produced by [[Dann Huff]]. Its first two singles, "[[Country Must Be Country Wide]]" and "[[You Don't Know Her Like I Do]]", both went to number one on the [[Hot Country Songs]] chart. After them, "[[Kick It in the Sticks]]" peaked at number 34, and "[[More Than Miles]]" at number 7 on [[Country Airplay]]. He won the [[Academy of Country Music|ACM]] ''New Male Artist'' award in 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.countryweekly.com/news/2013-acm-awards-nominees-winners |title=2013 ACM Awards: Nominees & Winners |publisher=Country Weekly |date=2013-04-07 |access-date=2014-07-27 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150606020825/http://www.countryweekly.com/news/2013-acm-awards-nominees-winners |archive-date=2015-06-06 }}</ref>

===2014-2017: ''Just as I Am'' and ''The Devil Don't Sleep''===
Gilbert's second Valory album (third overall), ''[[Just as I Am (Brantley Gilbert album)|Just as I Am]]'', was released in May 2014. Its lead single, "[[Bottoms Up (Brantley Gilbert song)|Bottoms Up]]," also reached number one. The second single, "[[Small Town Throwdown]]," featured guest vocals from labelmates [[Thomas Rhett]] and [[Justin Moore]]. The third single, "[[One Hell of an Amen]]," became Brantley's fourth number one hit in 2015. The album's fourth single was "[[Stone Cold Sober (Brantley Gilbert song)|Stone Cold Sober]]," released with the album's platinum edition.

Ahead of his fourth album, ''The Devil Don't Sleep'', Gilbert released the single "[[The Weekend (Brantley Gilbert song)|The Weekend]]" as the album's leadoff single. Also included on the album is "[[The Ones That Like Me]]."<ref name="boot">{{cite web |url=http://theboot.com/brantley-gilbert-single-the-weekend/ |title=Brantley Gilbert Reveals New Single, 'The Weekend' [LISTEN]|first= Christina|last= Vinson |date=July 22, 2016|work=The Boot }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboot.com/brantley-gilbert-the-ones-that-like-me/|title=Brantley Gilert Shares New Single, 'The Ones That Like Me' [Listen]|last=Stecker|first=Liv|work=The Boot|access-date=March 14, 2018}}</ref>

A deluxe edition of ''The Devil Don't Sleep'' includes ten bonus tracks: five demos, and five tracks cut from a live performance at the [[Red Rocks Amphitheatre]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Brantley Gilbert - The Devil Don't Sleep [2 CD][Deluxe Edition] - Amazon.com Music|url=https://www.amazon.com/Devil-Dont-Sleep-CD-Deluxe/dp/B01MTX8WZW|website=Amazon.com|publisher=Amazon|accessdate=13 June 2021}}</ref>

===2018-present: ''Fire & Brimstone''===
In December 2018, Gilbert released a duet with [[Lindsay Ell]], "[[What Happens in a Small Town]]", as the leadoff single to his upcoming fifth studio album, ''[[Fire & Brimstone (album)|Fire & Brimstone]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://popculture.com/country-music/2018/12/17/brantley-gilbert-what-happens-in-a-small-town-with-lindsay-ell/|title=Brantley Gilbert Releases 'What Happens In A Small Town' With Lindsay Ell|last=Thompson|first=Gayle|work=Pop Culture|date=December 17, 2018|access-date=April 13, 2018}}</ref> The album was released in October 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://big955chicago.iheart.com/featured/brooke-taylor/content/2019-03-05-brantley-gilbert-reveals-new-album-details/|title=Brantley Gilbert Reveals New Album Details!|last=Taylor|first=Brooke|work=iHeart Radio|date=March 5, 2019|access-date=June 10, 2019}}</ref> "Fire’t Up" was released as the second single off the album.<ref name="Fire’t Up">{{cite web|first=Angela|last=Stefano|title= Brantley Gilbert Is Ready to 'Fire't Up' in New Single [LISTEN]|url=https://theboot.com/brantley-gilbert-firet-up/|date=18 November 2019|work=The Boot}}</ref>

In June 2020, Gilbert released the single "[[Hard Days]]."<ref>{{cite web|first=Angela|last=Stefano|title= Brantley Gilbert's 'Hard Days' 'Is About Hope and Healing' [Listen]|url= https://tasteofcountry.com/brantley-gilbert-hard-days/|date=5 June 2020|work=Taste of Country}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://countryaircheck.com/pdfs/current06012020.pdf|title=Country Aircheck Weekly - June 1, 2020|work=Country Aircheck}}</ref> The song was later included on a deluxe edition of ''Fire & Brimstone''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.country965.com/2020/09/29/brantley-gilbert-to-release-deluxe-version-fire-brimstone/|title= Brantley Gilbert to Release Deluxe Version "Fire & Brimstone"|date=September 29, 2020|work=Wild Country 96.5}}</ref>

In June 2021, Gilbert released the single "The Worst Country Song of All Time", featuring [[Hardy (singer)|Hardy]] and [[Toby Keith]],<ref>{{cite web|first=Chris|last=Parton|title=BRANTLEY GILBERT, TOBY KEITH AND HARDY TEAM FOR 'THE WORST COUNTRY SONG'|url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/music/brantley-gilbert-toby-keith-hardy-worst-country-song/|date=18 June 2021|work=Sounds Like Nashville}}</ref> and followed it up with the single "Gone But Not Forgotten" in September 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wjvl.com/2021/09/17/new-at-noon-brantley-gilbert-gone-but-not-forgotten/|title=New at Noon – Brantley Gilbert "Gone But Not Forgotten"|first=Chet|last=Daniels|work=WJVL|date=September 17, 2021}}</ref>

==Songwriting==
In addition to his original work, Gilbert has written songs that [[Colt Ford]] and [[Jason Aldean]] have recorded. The songs "[[Dirt Road Anthem]]" (co-written and originally recorded by him and Colt Ford) and "[[My Kinda Party (song)|My Kinda Party]]", were released on Aldean's 2010 album ''[[My Kinda Party]]''. "My Kinda Party" was originally recorded by Gilbert on ''Modern Day Prodigal Son'', while "Dirt Road Anthem" was on ''Halfway to Heaven''. Aldean has also covered Gilbert's "The Best of Me", available on the iTunes release of his 2009 album ''[[Wide Open (Jason Aldean album)|Wide Open]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://todayscountrymusicvideos.com/2010/08/25/brantly-gilbert-jason-aldean/ |title=Is Brantley Gilbert Jason Aldean's Favorite Songwriter? |publisher=Today's Country Music Videos |date=2010-08-25 |access-date=2011-07-17 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110831064628/http://todayscountrymusicvideos.com/2010/08/25/brantly-gilbert-jason-aldean/ |archive-date=2011-08-31 }}</ref> Gilbert also wrote the track "The Same Way" on Aldean's album ''[[9 (Jason Aldean album)|9]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://theboot.com/jason-aldean-album-9-information-details/ |first=Bobby|last=Moore|title= Everything We Know About Jason Aldean's New Album, '9'|work=The Boot|date=18 November 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
It was announced in September 2012 that Brantley Gilbert was dating country music singer and actress [[Jana Kramer]]. They met at the CMT Music Awards in June 2012. They got engaged on January 20, 2013, his 28th birthday.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/brantley-gilbert-jana-kramer-dating/|title=Brantley Gilbert Confirms That He and Jana Kramer Are Dating|last=Dukes|first=Billy|date=September 21, 2012|work=Taste of Country|access-date=September 23, 2012}}</ref> They split in August 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last=Takeda |first=Allison |url=http://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/jana-kramer-fiance-brantley-gilbert-split-2013158 |title=Jana Kramer Split: Singer, Fiance Brantley Gilbert Call Off Engagement |work=Us Weekly |date=August 15, 2013 |access-date=December 18, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://thenew1037.cbslocal.com/2014/11/07/brantley-gilbert-reveals-who-hes-engaged-to/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-11-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20141110044256/http://thenew1037.cbslocal.com/2014/11/07/brantley-gilbert-reveals-who-hes-engaged-to/ |archive-date=2014-11-10 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.people.com/article/brantley-gilbert-engaged |title=Brantley Gilbert Is Engaged! |publisher=People Magazine |date=October 21, 2014 |access-date=October 22, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141023141037/http://www.people.com/article/brantley-gilbert-engaged |archive-date=October 23, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In June 2015, Gilbert married Georgia schoolteacher Amber Cochran in a small ceremony at his home.<ref>{{cite web |last=Caldwell |first=Lindsay |date=June 30, 2015 |url=http://www.eonline.com/news/671967/brantley-gilbert-marries-amber-cochran |title=Brantley Gilbert Marries Amber Cochran |publisher=Eonline.com |access-date=July 26, 2016}}</ref> He admitted that she was the girl who inspired his singles "You Don't Know Her Like I Do" and "More Than Miles", among several others.<ref>{{cite web |last=Olya |first=Gabrielle |date=November 13, 2014 |url=http://www.people.com/article/brantley-gilbert-engaged-amber-cochran |title=Brantley Gilbert Engaged To Amber Cochran |publisher=People |access-date=July 26, 2016}}</ref> In 2017, on his "The Devil Don't Sleep" tour, he announced he would be a father in November. On November 11, 2017, Gilbert and his wife welcomed son Barrett Hardy-Clay Gilbert. On September 9, 2019, they welcomed their second child, a daughter named Braylen Hendrix Gilbert.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/brantley-gilbert-welcomes-daughter-braylen-hendrix-first-photo/|title=Brantley Gilbert and Wife Amber Welcome Daughter Braylen Hendrix — See Her First Photos}}</ref>

In 2013, Gilbert embarked on an eight-day USO tour to entertain American service members stationed in Italy and Kuwait.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.uso.org/stories/1779-singer-brantley-gilbert-makes-lifelong-friends-on-his-first-uso-tour|title=Singer Brantley Gilbert Makes Lifelong Friends on His First USO Tour|last=Lee|first=Joseph Andrew|date=June 17, 2013 |publisher=United Service Organizations |access-date=October 26, 2016}}</ref>

Brantley Gilbert was in a near-fatal truck accident when he was 19.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://1023blakefm.com/near-fatal-truck-crash-leads-brantley-gilbert-to-music-2/ |title=Near-Fatal Truck Crash Leads Brantley Gilbert To Music |publisher=1023blakefm.com |access-date=2015-05-19}}</ref> Gilbert is an active Christian, and his song "My Faith In You", from his album ''Just as I Am'', speaks of his faith. In May 2015, Gilbert got a tattoo showing his support of the [[Second Amendment to the United States Constitution]].<ref name=tattoo>{{cite news |last1=Dukes |first1=Billy |title=Brantley Gilbert shows off Second Amendment tattoo |url=http://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/2015/05/06/brantley-gilbert-shows-support-second-amendment-with-new-tattoo/ |publisher=[[Fox News]] |access-date=7 May 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150507064856/https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/2015/05/06/brantley-gilbert-shows-support-second-amendment-with-new-tattoo/ |archive-date=May 7, 2015}}</ref>
