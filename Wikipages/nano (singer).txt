'''Hana Ishiyama Ohara''', known professionally as {{Nihongo|'''Nano'''|ナノ||stylized as '''nano'''; born July 12, 1988}}, is a [[Japanese Americans|Japanese-American]] singer and lyricist managed by HoriPro and signed to the [[Victor Entertainment|Flying Dog]] label. Nano can sing in both Japanese and English, as she was born in [[New York City]].<ref name="nano Official Site">''nano Official Site'' (in English). [http://en.nanonano.me/ nano Official]</ref> Nano posted music on [[YouTube]] and [[Niconico]] before being signed by a record label.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.barks.jp/news/?id=1000075187 |title=正体不明の"ナノ"、2012年デビュー|website=Barks Japan|language=Japanese}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Nano was born on July 12, 1988, in [[New York City]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://profile.ameba.jp/niconiconano/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20131016000916/http://profile.ameba.jp/niconiconano/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=October 16, 2013 |title=Blog profile |website=Ameba |accessdate=August 2, 2017 }}</ref> She was raised in the [[United States]] as a child but then moved to [[Japan]] when she was fourteen.

Nano posted covers of [[Vocaloid]] and [[anime]] songs online, first on [[YouTube]] and later on the [[Japanese language|Japanese]] site [[Niconico]]. She is [[Multilingualism|bilingual]], and sometimes wrote English lyrics to the cover songs, giving them a Western touch without losing the original style. Eventually, this led to a record deal with Japanese major label [[Victor Entertainment|Flying Dog]]. Her debut album ''Nanoir'' was released on March 14, 2012. Nano's first live performance was on March 16, 2013.

Nano has recorded several original theme songs for various [[anime]] series, such as, "Bull's Eye" for ''[[Hidan no Aria]]'', "Now or Never" for ''[[Phi Brain: Puzzle of God]]'', "No Pain, No Game" and "Exist" for ''[[Btooom!]]'', "Savior of Song" (feat. [[My First Story]]) for ''[[Arpeggio of Blue Steel]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/99085|title=日本初のイージス艦上撮影実現！ナノ新曲PV制作に密着|website=Natalie.mu|language=Japanese}}</ref> "Born To Be" for ''[[Magical Warfare]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.en.barks.jp/news/?id=1000004031| title= nano Restarts Activities, Forth Single to be Ending Theme for "Magical Warfare" Anime|website=Barks}}</ref> "Sable" for ''[[M3 the dark metal]]'', "Dreamcatcher" for ''[[Magical Girl Raising Project]]'', "Rock On." for the first film of ''[[Arpeggio of Blue Steel]]'',  ''Arpeggio of Blue Steel: Ars Nova DC'', “star light, star bright” for ''[[Conception (video game)|Conception]]'', and "Kemurikusa" for ''[[Kemurikusa]]''.

Some of Nano's other commercially featured works include:
*"Destiny: 12 Kaime no Kiseki" as the opening of the [[PlayStation Portable]] game ''[[Conception: Ore no Kodomo o Undekure!]]''.
*"Silence" as the theme song of the Japanese horror channel ''Den Of Horror: Horror no Sokutsu!''
*"Happy Ending Simulator" as the theme song of the arcade game ''[[Gunslinger Stratos|Gunslinger Stratos 2]]''.
*"Scarlet Story" as the theme song for the Japanese TV puppet show ''[[Sherlock Holmes (puppetry)|Sherlock Holmes]]''.
*"Infinity≠Zero" as the theme song for the Japanese live action film ''Bakumatsu Kokosei''.
*"Paralyze:D" as the theme song of the [[PlayStation Vita]] game ''Re:Vice[D]''.
*"Identity Crisis" and "Restart" for the video game ''[[Warriors Orochi]]''.
*"Mirror, Mirror" for the [[PlayStation Vita]] game ''Bad Apple Wars''.
*"Dare Devil" for the mobile phone game "Houkago Girls Tribe."
