'''Arctic Lake''' are an alt pop duo<ref>{{cite web|title= Interview: Arctic Lake |url=https://www.tonguetiedmag.com/interviews/interview-arctic-lake |website=tonguetiedmag.com |date=15 September 2021 |accessdate=24 September 2021}}</ref> from [[London]], England composed of vocalist Emma Foster and multi-instrumentalist producer Paul Holliman.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gary Crowley Presents…Arctic Lake |url=https://amazingradio.com/uncategorized/gary-crowley-presents-arctic-lake |website=amazingradio.com |date=29 July 2014 |accessdate=10 May 2020}}</ref>

==Biography==
Emma Foster and Paul Holliman met in 2012 while studying in London and formed Arctic Lake in 2014<ref>{{cite web|title= Interview: Arctic Lake |url=https://slantedpress.com/articles/spotlight-arctic-lake |website=slantedpress.com |author=Ben Standring |date=17 April 2019 |accessdate=24 September 2021}}</ref> through a shared desire to create music.<ref name="Bio">{{cite web|title=Fresh: "Holding On" - Arctic Lake |url=https://talkaboutpopmusic.com/2019/03/27/fresh-holding-on-arctic-lake/ |website=talkaboutpopmusic.com |date= 27 March 2019|accessdate=10 May 2020}}</ref> They released their first original song online in 2014, titled "How Long Can You Stare".<ref>{{cite web|title=Arctic Lake |url=https://www.musicglue.com/arctic-lake/about |website=musicglue.com |date= |accessdate=10 May 2020}}</ref> Their debut single, "Limits", came out in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Introducing - Arctic Lake |url=https://sodwee.com/blog/2015/01/introducing-arctic-lake/ |website=sodwee.com |date= 22 January 2015|accessdate=10 May 2020}}</ref> After releasing the single "Heal Me" in 2016, they were invited to perform at the [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]] in England that same year.<ref name="Bio" />

Arctic Lake released their debut EP, titled ''Closer'', in 2017 and their follow-up, ''What You May Find'', in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=Arctic Lake: Musical Footprints [Interview] |url=https://www.waevlength.com/post/arctic-lake-interview |website=waevlength.com |date=2 September 2019 |accessdate=10 May 2020}}</ref> ''See Inside'', their third EP, came out in February 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=See Inside - EP |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/see-inside-ep/1495336857 |website=music.apple.com |date=7 February 2020 |accessdate=10 May 2020}}</ref> During this period, they had their first UK headline tour (in May 2018),<ref>{{cite web|title= REVIEW: ARCTIC LAKE AT THE LOUISIANA, BRISTOL |url=https://milk-magazine.co.uk/review-arctic-lake/ |author=Rose Parish |website=milk-magazine.co.uk |date=29 July 2018 |accessdate=24 September 2021}}</ref> toured with [[Freya Ridings]] and [[Vancouver Sleep Clinic]], and collaborated with house producer [[Lane 8]] and neo-classical pianist Peter Sandberg.<ref>{{cite web|title= Peter Sandberg Teams With Arctic Lake On Finely Poised Single 'Unhold Me' |url=https://www.clashmusic.com/videos/peter-sandberg-teams-with-arctic-lake-on-finely-poised-single-unhold-me |website=clashmusic.com |author=Robin Murray |date=18 March 2020 |accessdate=24 September 2021}}</ref> In 2021, they teamed up with [[Zhu (musician)|ZHU]] on "Yours", a noirish techno track that hit #16 on Billboard's Dance chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zhu |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/zhu/chart-history/dance-electronic-songs/2 |website=billboard.com |accessdate=24 September 2021}}</ref> Their next single, "Lonely", followed on 10 September 2021.

==Musical style==
Arctic Lake's sound has been described as "delectably dreamy alt-pop",<ref>{{cite web|title= Interview: Arctic Lake |url=https://www.tonguetiedmag.com/interviews/interview-arctic-lake |website=tonguetiedmag.com |date=15 September 2021 |accessdate=24 September 2021}}</ref> and ''Mundane Mag'' noted their "ability to channel tightly-wound emotions and turn them into breathtaking, electromagnetic anthems".<ref>{{cite web|title= ENGLISH ALT-POP DAYDREAM, ARCTIC LAKE! DROPS VIDEO FOR 'LONELY' |url=https://mundanemag.com/english-alt-pop-daydream-arctic-lake-drops-video-for-lonelyphoto/ |website=mundanemag.com |accessdate=24 September 2021}}</ref>
