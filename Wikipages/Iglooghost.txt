'''Seamus Rawles Malliagh''', known by his stage name '''Iglooghost''', is an [[England|English]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/iglooghost_tries_to_explain_the_bonkers_worlds_of_the_clear_tamei_and_steel_mogu_eps|title=Iglooghost Tries to Explain the Bonkers Worlds of the 'Clear Tamei' and 'Steel Mogu' EPs|last=Keating|first=Daryl|website=exclaim.ca|language=en|access-date=2018-10-07}}</ref> [[electronic music]] producer based in [[London]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BqsVQbUn5k4/|title=Lazy Oaf on Instagram: "London based electronic music producer @iglooghost takes a spin at our next curated gift guide (banana not included) Link in bio!"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2018-11-28}}</ref>

==Career==
Malliagh released a cassette in 2014 titled ''Treetunnels'' on Error Broadcast.<ref name="imposemagazine">{{cite web|last1=Gillespie|first1=Blake|title=IGLOOGHOST, "FRENCHOPEN" (FEAT. MILO)|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/iglooghost-frenchopen-feat-milo|publisher=[[Impose (magazine)|Impose]]|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref> In 2015, he released his second EP titled ''[[Chinese Nü Yr]]'' on Brainfeeder.<ref name="laweekly">{{cite web|last1=Weiss|first1=Layne|title=Brainfeeder's Latest Release Is About a Worm in a Witch Hat|url=http://www.laweekly.com/music/brainfeeders-latest-release-is-about-a-worm-in-a-witch-hat-6227150|publisher=[[LA Weekly]]|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref><ref name="exclaim">{{cite web|last1=Hampson|first1=Ashley|title=Iglooghost Chinese Nü Yr|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/iglooghost-chinese_nu_yr|publisher=[[Exclaim!]]|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref> He also released in that same year a collaborative EP with Mr. Yote titled ''Milk Empire'' through Activia Benz.<ref name="clashmagazine">{{cite web|last1=Murray|first1=Robin|title=Premiere: Iglooghost x Mr. Yote - 'Tobaccochewer'|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/premiere-iglooghost-x-mr-yote-tobaccochewer|publisher=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref> In March 2016, he released his third EP titled ''Little Grids'', again on Brainfeeder,<ref name="xlr8r">{{cite web|last1=Holbrook|first1=Cameron|title=Iglooghost Announces New EP on Brainfeeder; Shares New Track|url=https://www.xlr8r.com/news/2016/03/iglooghost-announces-new-ep-on-brainfeeder-shares-new-track/|publisher=[[XLR8R]]|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref><ref name="imposemagazine1">{{cite web|last1=G|first1=Andre|title=IGLOOGHOST, "PEANUT CHOKER"|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/iglooghost-peanut-choker|publisher=Impose|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref> and in September 2017 he released his album ''[[Neō Wax Bloom]]'', also on Brainfeeder.<ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web|last1=Balfour|first1=Jay|title=Iglooghost: Neō Wax Bloom|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/iglooghost-ne-wax-bloom/|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref><ref name="exclaim1">{{cite web|last1=Hampson|first1=Ashley|title=Iglooghost: Neō Wax Bloom|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/iglooghost-ne_wax_bloom|publisher=Exclaim!|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref><ref name="popmatters">{{cite web|last1=Harrison|first1=A Noah|title=Iglooghost: Neō Wax Bloom|url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/iglooghost-ne-wax-bloom/|publisher=[[PopMatters]]|accessdate=4 October 2017}}</ref>

On February 4, 2021 Iglooghost announced the oncoming release of his fourth album, ''Lei Line Eon'', which was set for April 2 alongside the release of the album's lead single, "Sylph Fossil".<ref>{{Cite tweet |number=1357298628038176770 |user=IGLOOGHOST |title=OK FRIENDS...BEYOND EXCITED TO FINALLY ANNOUNCE MY ALBUM. IT'S ENTITLED 'LEI LINE EON' & IS OUT APRIL 2nd... |author=Iglooghost |date=February 4, 2021 |access-date=February 5, 2021}}</ref><ref name="LeiLineEon">{{Cite news |date=February 4, 2021 |title=Iglooghost Announces Solo Sophomore Album Lei Line Eon Out April 2nd |work=Imprint |url=https://imprintent.org/iglooghost-announces-solo-sophomore-album-lei-line-eon-out-april-2nd/ |access-date=6 February 2021}}</ref> As of September of 2021, Iglooghost has over 112,000 active monthly listeners on Spotify, and 23,500 subscribers on YouTube. He is going on his 2021-2022 world tour starting in early October of 2021.
