'''Takudzwa Victoria Rosa''' "'''Tkay'''" '''Maidza''' ({{IPAc-en|t|iː|'|k|eɪ|_|'|m|aɪ|d|z|ə}} {{respell|tee|KAY|_|MY|dzə}})<ref name="SMH" /> (born 17 December 1995) is a [[Zimbabwe]]an-born Australian singer-songwriter and rapper.<!-- She has been compared to [[M.I.A. (rapper)|MIA]], and [[Azealia Banks]]. -->

== Biography ==
Takudzwa Victoria Rosa Maidza was born in  [[Harare]], Zimbabwe. Her parents are [[Metallurgy|metallurgists]].<ref name="SMH">{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/tkay-maidza-moving-in-pops-forward-lane-20150415-1mlh9d.html|title=Tkay Maidza moving in pop's forward lane|date=16 April 2015|work=The Sydney Morning Herald|publisher=Fairfax Media|access-date=28 April 2015|first=Craig|last=Mathieson}}</ref> In 2001, her family moved to Australia when she was five years old. They lived in [[Perth]] and [[Kalgoorlie]] in Western Australia and in [[Whyalla]] in South Australia before relocating to [[Adelaide]] in 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/nov/07/tkay-maidza-on-drugs-drake-and-music-industry-snakes|title=Adelaide's Tkay Maidza on drugs, Drake and music industry snakes|first=Alexander |last=Bisley|date=7 November 2014}}</ref> She graduated from St Michael's College at age 16 and studied architecture at the [[University of South Australia]] before becoming a full-time musician.<ref name="TheMusic">{{cite news|url=http://themusic.com.au/interviews/all/2014/12/17/tkay-maidza-iggy-azalea-has-opened-up-a-path-to-america/|title='Iggy Azalea Has Opened Up A Path To America'|date=17 December 2015|publisher=theMusic.com.au (Street Press Australia Pty Ltd)|access-date=28 April 2015}}</ref>

Maidza took part in Adelaide's Northern Sound System artist development program and released her debut single, "Brontosaurus", at age 17 with producer BadCop. She has since released an EP, ''Switch Tape'', featuring collaborations with [[Night Slugs]]' [[Bok Bok]] and producer [[SBTRKT]].<ref name="Stereogum">{{cite web|last=DeVille |first=Chris |url=http://www.stereogum.com/1713392/stream-tkay-maidza-switch-tape/mp3s/album-stream/ |title=Stream Tkay Maidza Switch Tape |publisher=stereogum.com |date=22 October 2014 |access-date=8 March 2015}}</ref>  In January 2016, Maidza reached #66 in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2015|2015 Triple J Hottest 100]] with her 2015 single "MOB". MOB was Maidza's first song to be played on Australian commercial radio.

Maidza has played [[Splendour in the Grass]] and the [[CMJ Music Marathon]] in 2014 and replaced [[Lykke Li]] at the 2015 [[St Jerome's Laneway Festival]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/laneway-festival-2015-lykke-li-loss-is-tkay-maidzas-gain-20150106-12ii9e.html|title=Laneway Festival 2015: Lykke Li loss is Tkay Maidza's gain|date=6 January 2015|first=Peter |last=Vincent}}</ref> She performed at the [[Triple J]] "Beat the Drum" concert in 2015 to celebrate the 40th anniversary of the radio station, both as a solo artist and with many other Australian guest rappers on an extended version of [[Hilltop Hoods]]' "[[Cosby Sweater]]".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.fasterlouder.com.au/news/41653/Hilltop-Hoods-joined-by-a-whos-who-of-Aussie-hip-hop-at-Beat-The-Drum|title=Hilltop Hoods joined by a who's who of Aussie hip-hop at Beat The Drum|date= 17 January 2015}}</ref> Maidza played most of the Australian major festivals in 2015 including Grooving the Moo, Falls Festival, Meredith Festival and a cameo at Stereosonic with WhatSoNot.

Maidza was featured in Australian ''Vogue'' magazine in July 2015 and the French magazine ''Konbini''. After featuring on [[Troye Sivan]]'s debut album ''[[Blue Neighbourhood]]'', Maidza supported Sivan in London, Paris and Berlin as well as a guest appearance in Los Angeles. Earlier Maidza had supported [[Charli XCX]] on her Australian tour as well as British band [[Years & Years]] in the UK.

In November 2015 Tkay featured in an episode of ''Le Petit Journal'' in her debut French TV appearance. A scheduled appearance at the French festival La Maroquinerie in November 2015 was cancelled. She later appeared on American radio as part of the second episode of [[Skrillex]]'s OWSLA Radio on 13 February 2016.

On 30 August, Tkay premiered her single "Carry On", featuring guest vocals from US rapper [[Killer Mike]]. The track is the first single from her debut album simply titled ''[[Tkay]]'', which was released on 28 October 2016.

In May 2020, Tkay announced she had signed with acclaimed British independent label [[4AD]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://4ad.com/news/12/5/2020/tkaymaidzasignsto4adandreleasessingleshook|title=Tkay Maidza Signs To 4AD And Releases Single 'Shook'|language=en-US|access-date=2020-06-30}}</ref> and released her new single 'Shook' with praise from [[NME]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/music-interviews/tkay-maidza-new-single-shook-4ad-signing-2666483|title=Tkay Maidza on her infectious new single, 'Shook': "I'm starting to feel more confident in my skills"|date=12 May 2020|language=en-US|access-date=2020-06-30}}</ref> and [[Rolling Stone]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://au.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/tkay-maidza-new-single-shook-10991/|title=Tkay Maidza Shares Audio and Video for New Single, 'Shook'|date=12 May 2020|language=en-US|access-date=2020-06-30}}</ref>

In February 2021, Tkay released her new track ‘Kim’, six months on from the release of her Last Year Was Weird, Vol.2 EP working with US artist Yung Baby Tate.<ref>[https://au.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/song-you-need-to-know-tkay-maidza-kim-22753/ Song You Need to Know: Tkay Maidza, ‘Kim’]. Tyler Jenke, ''Rolling Stone '', 04 February 2021. Retrieved 04 February 2021.</ref> 

Alongside the track’s release, Tkay also shared a new Adrian Yu-directed music video, which sees her embodying the imagery of three iconic Kims: [[Kim Possible (character)|Kim Possible]], [[Lil' Kim]], and [[Kim Kardashian]].<ref>[https://au.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/song-you-need-to-know-tkay-maidza-kim-22753/ Song You Need to Know: Tkay Maidza, ‘Kim’]. Tyler Jenke, ''Rolling Stone '', 04 February 2021. Retrieved 04 February 2021.</ref>

In May 2021, Maidza announced that the third mixtape in her ''Last Year Was Weird'' series would be released on July 9 that year.<ref name="Vol 3 Release" />

On 19 November 2021, Maidza released "Real Nice (H.C.T.F.)" with [[Young Franco]] featuring Nerve which served as the theme song for the A-League, the Australian and New Zealand professional soccer league.<ref name="real nice"/>

== Artistry ==
Maidza has said she admires the work of [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Santigold]], [[Major Lazer]] and [[Lorde]].<ref>{{cite news|author=The Guardian|title=Tkay Maidza on bringing a new sound to Australian hip-hop: I didn't really want to fit in|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/oct/24/tkay-maidza-on-bringing-a-new-sound-to-australian-hip-hop-i-didnt-really-want-to-fit-in|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=19 December 2016|date=23 October 2016}}</ref>
