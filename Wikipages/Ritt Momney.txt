'''Jack Rutter''' (born October 12, 1999), better known as '''Ritt Momney''', is an American singer from [[Salt Lake City]], [[Utah]].<ref name="rollingstone">{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/pro/news/ritt-momney-corinne-bailey-rae-put-your-records-on-1061116/|title=An Indie Artist Covered Corinne Bailey Rae – and Scored a Record Deal|first=Samantha|last=Hissong|date=September 17, 2020|website=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref><ref name="billboard">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/9483116/ritt-momney-put-your-records-on-hot-100-interview/|title=Ritt Momney Doesn't Care If His 'Put Your Records On' Cover Is His Only Pop Hit|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=November 13, 2020|first=Josh|last=Glicksman}}</ref> His stage name is a [[spoonerism]] of American politician [[Mitt Romney]], who has been a [[United States Senate|United States senator]] from Utah since 2019.

== Career ==
Ritt Momney was previously the name of an indie band formed by Rutter in high-school, but he became a solo artist after his bandmates departed to become [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints|LDS]] missionaries.<ref name="nme">{{cite web|last1=Buchanan|first1=Rhys|date=October 14, 2020|title=Ritt Momney: Salt Lake City's lo-fi indie hero channels lockdown misery into spirited pop cover|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/ritt-momney-salt-lake-citys-lo-fi-indie-put-your-records-on-review-2782568|access-date=October 26, 2020|website=NME|language=en}}</ref>

Rutter recorded a cover version of the song of "[[Put Your Records On]]", which was released on April 24, 2020, originally independently through [[DistroKid]] and later re-released through [[Disruptor Records]] and [[Columbia Records]]. The song became a [[sleeper hit]] after gaining popularity on the [[Online video platform|video sharing service]] [[TikTok]] in September 2020.<ref name="rollingstone" /> Domestically, it peaked at number 30 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. The song also charted in the top 40 of 15 countries, and peaked in the top 10 in Australia and New Zealand.

When asked about Ritt, U.S. Senator Mitt Romney said, "They say that imitation is the sincerest form of flattery. In all seriousness, I wish Ritt Momney the best of luck in his music career and all his future endeavors."<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Benson|first=Samuel|date=June 22, 2021|title=Meet Ritt Momney, the viral singer who doesn’t know Mitt|url=https://www.deseret.com/entertainment/2021/6/22/22538828/does-mitt-romney-know-about-ritt-momney-singer-put-your-records-on-cover-tiktok-columbia-records|access-date=August 12, 2021|website=Deseret News|language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Rutter was born on October 12, 1999, in [[Dallas]], [[Texas]]. When he was about two years old, his family moved to Salt Lake City, where he resides to this day. He grew up in a heavily [[The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints|LDS]] community, which influenced his early musical career.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ritt Momney |url=https://www.paradigmagency.com/music/ritt-momney/ |website=Paradigm Talent Agency |access-date=April 22, 2021}}</ref> Rutter graduated from [[East High School (Utah)|East High School]] in Salt Lake City.<ref name=":0" />

Rutter considers himself to be on the [[political left]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Ritt Momney Talks "Put Your Records On", Corinne Bailey Rae & The Story Behind His Name |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DSX7cQQl-vU |website=YouTube |publisher=Zach Sang Show |access-date=April 20, 2021}}</ref>
