'''Tilmann Otto''' (born 19 April 1974), better known by his stage name '''Gentleman''', is a German [[reggae]] musician.

== Personal life ==
Gentleman lives{{when|date=May 2019}} in the Neubrück area of [[Cologne]].<ref name="Kriesch">Kriesch, Adrian & Scholz, Jan-Philipp (2014) "[http://www.dw.de/german-reggae-in-africa-courtesy-of-gentleman/a-17457768 German reggae in Africa, courtesy of Gentleman]", ''[[Deutsche Welle]]'', 26 February 2014. Retrieved 1 March 2014</ref> He is the son of a [[Lutheranism|Lutheran]] pastor and is the father of two children, named Samuel and Tamica. He is married to Tamika, a [[backing singer]] of the Far East Band, which has supported him since his first concert tour of Germany in 2002. Although regularly on tour and in the studio, Gentleman generally considers himself a rather family oriented person, which becomes apparent in his music projects such as his long-player ''[[Journey to Jah]]'', which was inspired by the birth of his first son Samuel.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/gentleman-mn0000803479/biography|title=Gentleman {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=2019-05-03}}</ref> His song ''Mama'' from the collaborative album ''Conversations'' that he recorded with [[Ky-Mani Marley]] deals with his deep spiritual relationship to his mother.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gentleman-music.com/bio/bio-english/|title=Gentleman Official Website. Recent album: Gentleman & Ky-Mani Marley - Conversations :: Bio english|website=www.gentleman-music.com|language=en|access-date=2019-05-03}}</ref>

[[File:Gentleman - Ilosaarirock 2011.jpg|230px|thumb|Gentleman at the 2011 [[Ilosaarirock]] festival.]]

== Music ==
Gentleman has traveled to Jamaica regularly since he was 18 years old.<ref name="Kriesch" /> His career began with the collaboration of the band [[Freundeskreis]] which produced the song "Tabula Rasa". After beginning his career as a [[deejay]], he based his style to the classic form of the reggae genre like that of [[Bob Marley]]. He sings mainly in [[English language|English]] or [[Jamaican Patois]]. With songs like "Send a Prayer", Gentleman expresses his deep belief in [[God]]. His album, ''[[Confidence (Gentleman album)|Confidence]]'', climbed to number 1 on the German album charts in 2004. 

Gentleman was featured on the album [[True Love (Toots & the Maytals album)|''True Love'']] by [[Toots and the Maytals]], which won the [[Grammy Award]] in 2004 for Best Reggae Album.<ref>“Toots & the Maytals - True Love." Amazon. www.amazon.com. Web. <https://www.amazon.com/True-Love-Toots-Maytals/dp/B0001LUH56>. Retrieved 9 November 2016.</ref> In 2005, Gentleman performed with [[Mamadee]] with the song "Lass los" representing [[North Rhine-Westphalia]] in the [[Bundesvision Song Contest 2005]], placing 15th with 10 points.<ref name="fan-lexikon">{{cite web|url=http://www.fan-lexikon.de/film-tv/bundesvision-song-contest/|title=Bundesvision Song Contest|work=fan-lexikon.de|language=de|access-date=19 April 2015}}</ref> 

After ten years releasing music under the label [[Four Music]] he moved to [[Universal Music Group|Universal]] in 2010.<ref name="de">{{cite web|url=http://www.wildstylemag.com/index.php/magazine/news/1-news/1898-kitty-kat-gentleman-wechseln-zu-universal.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=March 25, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100104144157/http://wildstylemag.com/index.php/magazine/news/1-news/1898-kitty-kat-gentleman-wechseln-zu-universal.html |archive-date=January 4, 2010 }}</ref>
