'''Kierra Valencia "Kiki" Sheard-Kelly''' (born June 20, 1987) is an American [[gospel music|gospel singer]], songwriter, fashion designer, actress, entrepreneur, author and creative director. She is the daughter of gospel singer [[Karen Clark Sheard]] (member of gospel singing group [[the Clark Sisters]]) and granddaughter of gospel choral director [[Mattie Moss Clark]]. Sheard portrayed her mother in the hit Lifetime movie, [[The Clark Sisters: First Ladies of Gospel]]. After appearing on her mother's (most notably, the seminal ''[[Finally Karen]]'') and aunt's albums, Sheard broke to the music scene with the release of her debut album ''[[I Owe You]]'' in 2004. Her hit single "[[You Don't Know (Kierra Sheard song)|You Don't Know]]" was inspired by her mother almost losing her life to a fatal blood clot.

==Early life==
Born in Detroit, Michigan, and raised in the suburb of West Bloomfield, Sheard spent her formative years surrounded by the influences of her family, their faith, and their music.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p354150|pure_url=yes}} |title=Kierra "KiKi" Sheard Biography |access-date=March 29, 2009 |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |author=James Christopher Monger}}</ref> At age 5, she began singing in the choir at Greater Emmanuel Institutional [[Church of God in Christ]], pastored by her father Bishop J. Drew Sheard.

==Music career==

===1997–2003: Early career===
Sheard made her professional recording debut at the age of 9, on her mother's debut solo album ''[[Finally Karen]]'' (1997). The song "The Will Of God" won a [[Stellar Award]] for Best Children's Performance.<ref name="B&E">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/66402/be-kierra-kiki-sheard|title=B&E: Kierra "Kiki" Sheard|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|via=[[Billboard.com]]|date=October 22, 2004|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref> In the following years, Sheard performed as background vocalist for her mother, aunt [[Dorinda Clark-Cole]] and [[the Clark Sisters]]. In 2002, Sheard was featured on Clark-Cole's [[Dorinda Clark-Cole (album)|self-titled debut album]]. In 2003, she featured on her mother's third album, ''[[The Heavens Are Telling]]'', on "You Loved Me" (a gospel cover of [[Jill Scott]]'s "[[He Loves Me (Lyzel In E Flat)]]") and alongside [[J Moss]]  on "Don't Change".<ref name="Heavens">{{cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Rg8EAAAAMBAJ&q=karen+clark+sheard+kierra+sheard+you+loved+me+billboard&pg=PA17|title=Sheard Spreads Out On Heavens|page=17|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|via=[[Google Books]]|date=November 29, 2003|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gospelflava.com/reviews/karenclarksheardheavens.html|title=GospelFlava.com - Reviews - Karen Clark Sheard (The Heavens Are Telling)|work=GospelFlava.com|first=Melanie|last=Clark|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref>  That same year, [[EMI Gospel]] won a bidding war among several record companies to sign Sheard.<ref name="BMI 2008">{{cite web|url=https://www.bmi.com/news/entry/kierra_sheard_lengthens_a_family_lineage|title=Kierra Sheard Lengthens a Family Lineage|first=Malcom|last=Venable|work=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.|BMI]]|date=December 8, 2008|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Heavens"/>

===2004–2005: ''I Owe You''===
Her debut album ''[[I Owe You (album)|I Owe You]]'' was released on September 7, 2004. The 11-track set included production and songwriting from [[Rodney Jerkins]], [[Warryn Campbell]], Erica and Tina Campbell of [[Mary Mary]], Sheard's cousin [[J Moss]], and [[Tonéx]].<ref name="BMI 2008"/> The album also functioned as the production debut for her brother J Drew. With the release of ''I Owe You'', Sheard became the first new artist in ''Billboard'' history to debut at number one on the [[Top Gospel Albums]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Charts|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=MhMEAAAAMBAJ&q=billboard+kierra+sheard+I+OWE+YOU&pg=PA73|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|page=73|date=September 25, 2004|access-date=April 26, 2020|via=[[Google Books]]}}</ref>

EMI commissioned a collection of remixes entitled ''[[Just Until...]]'' on August 2, 2005. The title of the stopgap release was abbreviated from its working title, "Just Until The Next Record".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gospelcity.com/dynamic/news-articles/gospel_news/166 |title=Kierra "KiKi" Sheard to be Kellogg's Gospel Sing Off Spokesperson & Judge |date=May 5, 2005 |publisher=GospelCity.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070423062826/http://www.gospelcity.com/dynamic/news-articles/gospel_news/166 |archive-date=April 23, 2007 }}</ref> The album had a surprise international hit in the Godson Concept remix of "Let Go". The upbeat gospel single, released only in Japan, raced to the top of the mainstream R&B charts and remained there for several weeks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.christianpost.com/news/-kiki-sheard-tops-japanese-radio-receives-3-stellar-nominations-5823/|title='Kiki' Sheard Tops Japanese Radio, Receives 3 Stellar Nominations|author=Justin Camacho|date=January 5, 2006|work=The Christian Times}}</ref><ref name="BMI 2008"/>

===2006–2007: ''This is Me''===
Shortly after graduating high school, Sheard readied her second album, ''[[This Is Me (Kierra Sheard album)|This Is Me]]''. It debuted at No. 1 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s Top Gospel Albums chart when it was released on June 27, 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gospelcity.com/dynamic/music-articles/new_music/288|publisher=GospelCity.com|date=July 10, 2006|title=Kierra "Kiki" Sheard's 'This Is Me' debuts at #1|access-date=November 27, 2006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060905135928/http://www.gospelcity.com/dynamic/music-articles/new_music/288/|archive-date=September 5, 2006|url-status=dead}}</ref> A midtempo fan favorite, the [[Fred Jerkins III]]-produced "Why Me?" was chosen as the lead single for the album. Sheard shot her first music videos for two subsequent singles from the album, "Yes" and "This Is Me". The album was [[Grammy Award]]-nominated for Best Contemporary R&B Gospel Album in December 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/GRAMMY_Awards/49th_Show/list.aspx |title=49th Annual Grammy Awards Nominee List |publisher=National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences |url-status=dead |archive-url=http://webarchive.loc.gov/all/20061220160454/http://www.grammy.com/GRAMMY_Awards/49th_Show/list.aspx |archive-date=December 20, 2006 }}</ref>

In addition to performing at the 2007 [[Stellar Awards]] ("[[This Is Me (Kierra Sheard album)|Why Me?]]"), Sheard appeared on [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]]'s Celebration of Gospel, standing in for pregnant [[Faith Evans]] in "Endow Me" (as popularized by Sheard's mother and aunts) along with [[Coko]], [[Lil' Mo]], and [[Fantasia Barrino|Fantasia]]. Sheard also sang with her mother for ''A Tribute to [[Aretha Franklin]]''.

===2008–2009: ''Bold Right Life''===
In 2008, Sheard featured on [[Mary Mary]]'s crossover hit, "[[God in Me]]", from ''[[The Sound (Mary Mary album)|The Sound]]'' album. "God in Me" became Sheard's first appearance on the US [[Hot 100]], where it peaked at number 68.<ref name="Billboard Hot 100"/> Furthermore, the single broke the ''Billboard'' record for the longest chart-run by a group and longest chart-run by a gospel song on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart,<ref name="MySpiritDC God in Me"/> where it spent a total of 74 weeks and peaked at number 5.<ref name="Billboard Hot R&B/Hip-Hop - Featured"/> "God in Me" was also a major hit on ''Billboard'''s [[Dance Club Songs|Dance Club]] and [[Hot Gospel Songs]] charts, peaking at number one on both and spending 7 consecutive weeks atop the latter.<ref name="Billboard Dance"/><ref name="Billboard Gospel Songs - Featured"/> Further to this, "God in Me" won a [[Grammy Award]] for "[[Grammy Award for Best Gospel Song|Best Gospel Song]]" at the [[52nd Annual Grammy Awards]] and was also awarded for "Outstanding Song" at the [[41st NAACP Image Awards]].<ref name="MySpiritDC God in Me">{{cite web|url=https://myspiritdc.com/57881/mary-mary-breaks-billboard-rbhip-hop-chart-record/|title=Mary Mary Breaks Billboard R&B/Hip-Hop Chart Record|work=[[WYCB|Spirit 1340 AM]]|publisher=[[Urban One]]|first= Bobby|last= B.|date=February 22, 2010|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref>

In October 2008, Sheard released her third album, ''[[Bold Right Life]]''. Named after Sheard's youth organization in Detroit, the album was described by Billboard as a 'powerful mix of R&B/hip-hop, pop, rock and traditional gospel' which 'shifts from a hot uptempo beat (opener “Won’t Hold Back”) to pop (“My Boyfriend”) then to rock (“Invisible”) and traditional (“Praise Him Now”)'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/1301804/kierra-sheard-makes-bold-debut|title=Kierra Sheard Makes 'Bold' Debut|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|via=[[Billboard.com]]|first= Gail|last= Mitchell|date=November 5, 2008|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref> The album's two lead singles were "Praise Him Now" and "Won't Hold Back".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://praisecleveland.com/1639875/happy-birthday-to-kierra-kiki-sheard-today/|title=HAPPY BIRTHDAY TO KIERRA "KIKI" SHEARD TODAY!!!|work=[[WJMO|Praise 94.5]]|publisher=[[Urban One]]|date=June 20, 2019|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref> "Praise Him Now" peaked at number 7 on the US Hot Gospel Songs chart,<ref name="Billboard Gospel Songs"/> whilst "Love Like Crazy" became Sheard's second Japanese hit, peaking at number 7 on the [[Japan Hot 100]].<ref name="Japan Hot 100"/>

In January 2009, Sheard performed the Clark Sisters' "Jesus Is a Love Song" at the BMI Trailblazers Luncheon in honor of her aunt [[Elbernita "Twinkie" Clark]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bmi.com/press/entry/537669|title=Twinkie Clark, Donald Lawrence and CeCe Winans Honored at 2009 Trailblazers Luncheon|work=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.|BMI]]|date=January 16, 2009|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref>

The same year, Sheard released a compilation, ''[[KiKi's Mixtape]]'', on September 22, 2009.<ref name="Amazon KiKi's Mixtape"/> ''KiKi's Mixtape'' included two new songs - “Sing to the Lord”, co-written by Kierra and described as 'a high-octane amalgam of rock, CCR and power pop' and a 'bold and bracing' cover of [[Donny Hathaway]]'s "[[This Christmas (Donny Hathaway song)|This Christmas]]" with [[Marcus Cole]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://journalofgospelmusic.com/gospel/kierra-sheard-kikis-mixtape/|title=Kierra Sheard – Kiki's Mixtape - The Journal of Gospel Music|first=Bob|last=Marovich|work=The Journal of Gospel Music|date=November 6, 2009|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref>

===2010–2013: ''Free'' and ''The Sheards''===

Sheard had a role in the film ''[[Preacher's Kid (film)|Preacher's Kid]]'' (starring platinum-selling R&B artist [[LeToya Luckett]], [[Tank (American singer)|Durrell Babbs]], [[Clifton Powell]], [[Gregory Alan Williams]], and [[Sharif Atkins]]), which was released in theaters across the U.S. on January 29, 2010 and was released on DVD and [[Blu-ray]] disc on May 4, 2010.

Sheard's fourth album, ''[[Free (Kierra Sheard album)|Free]]'', was released on October 18, 2011. This was her first album on her family's gospel label, Karew Records (pronounced Kuh-Rue). Originally scheduled for a July 2011 release, the album was leaked and subsequently pushed back and reformatted with a different track listing.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://human-mag.com/2011/08/17/kierra-sheard-reveals-new-album-cover-track-listing-for-free/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=September 26, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120402232756/http://human-mag.com/2011/08/17/kierra-sheard-reveals-new-album-cover-track-listing-for-free/ |archive-date=April 2, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The album features Sheard's BRL (Bold Right Life) Choir.

On April 7, 2013, Sheard and her family debuted in a new [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]] reality series, ''The Sheards''. The show is based on the lives of Sheard, her brother and producer J. Drew, and her parents, Grammy Award-winning Karen Clark Sheard and Pastor J. Drew Sheard Sr. Season 1 had a run of eight episodes, and it was announced on the BET Twitter account that Season 2 would debut in the fall of 2014.

===2013&ndash;present: ''Graceland'', Clark Sisters biopic and ''Kierra''===
Sheard released the single "Trumpets Blow" in 2013, which was followed by "2nd Win" in 2014, which peaked within the top 20 of ''Billboard'''s Top Gospel Songs chart.<ref name="Billboard Gospel Songs"/> Shortly after in 2014, her fifth album ''[[Graceland (Kierra Sheard album)|Graceland]]'', was released on June 22, 2014. ''Graceland'' became her second top 40 appearance on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]<ref name="BB200"/> and fourth number one on the Top Gospel Albums chart.<ref name="Gospel Albums"/> ''Graceland'' also earned Sheard a nomination for Contemporary Female Vocalist of the Year at the [[Stellar Awards|30th Annual Stellar Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ugospel.com/30th-annual-stellar-gospel-music-awards-list-of-nominees/|title=30th Annual Stellar Gospel Music Awards List of Nominees|first= Jim|last= Asker|date=January 13, 2015|website=uGospel.com|language=en|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref>

In 2020, a [[Lifetime (TV network)|Lifetime]] biographical film, ''[[The Clark Sisters: First Ladies of Gospel]]'' was released, starring Kierra Sheard as her mother [[Karen Clark-Sheard]], which became the highest rated original movie release by Lifetime in four years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt10098482/|title=The Clark Sisters: First Ladies of Gospel|via=www.imdb.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2020/04/the-clark-sisters-first-ladies-of-gospel-biopic-lifetime-tv-ratings-1202907356/|title='The Clark Sisters: First Ladies Of Gospel' Biopic Marks Highest-Rated Lifetime Movie In Four Years|last=Ramos|first=Dino-Ray|date=April 13, 2020|website=Deadline|language=en|access-date=April 13, 2020}}</ref> The film's soundtrack, also starring Sheard, debuted at number 8 on the ''Billboard'' Top Gospel Albums chart, where it remained in its second week.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/gospel-albums/2020-04-25|title=Top Gospel Albums Chart|date=April 25, 2020|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard)]]|via=[[Billboard.com]]|language=en|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/gospel-albums/2020-05-02|title=Top Gospel Albums Chart|date=May 2, 2020|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard)]]|via=[[Billboard.com]]|language=en|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref>

Following this, Sheard released her sixth album ''Kierra''. Debuting at number one on the ''Billboard'' Top Gospel Albums chart with 6,000 equivalent album units, it became her fifth chart-topping album, replacing [[Kanye West]]'s ''[[Jesus is King]]'' which had held the number one spot for 6 months since November 9, 2019. Sheard co-wrote the entire album, which includes a mix of studio and live tracks recorded in her hometown, [[Detroit]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9368245/kierra-sheard-top-gospel-albums-chart-kanye-west|title=Kierra Sheard Halts Kanye West's 6-Month Reign on Top Gospel Albums Chart|first= Jim|last= Asker|date=April 29, 2020|website=[[Billboard.com]]|language=en|access-date=May 4, 2020}}</ref> The album spawned 3 top 20 singles on the Hot Gospel Songs chart; "Don't Judge Me", "It Keeps Happening" and "Something Has To Break" (featuring [[Tasha Cobbs Leonard]]), the latter peaking at number 5.<ref name="Hot Gospel Songs - May 2, 2020">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/gospel-songs/2020-05-02|title=Hot Gospel Songs Chart|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|via=[[Billboard.com]]|date=May 2, 2020|access-date=April 27, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Billboard Gospel Songs"/>

In April 2021, Sheard was featured on actor and rapper, [[Nick Cannon]]'s rendition of [[Fred Hammond]]'s gospel classic, ''No Weapon''.<ref name="No Weapon - April 2021">{{cite web|url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/nick-cannon-releases-gospel-single-no-weapon-with-kierra-sheard.html|title=Nick Cannon Releases Gospel Single, "No Weapon" {{! }}Christian Post|via=[[christianpost.com]]|date=April 11, 2021|access-date=April 13, 2021}}</ref> In October 2021, Kierra's "Something Has to Break," featuring her mother, Karen Clark-Sheard, peaked at number one on the Hot Gospel Airplay chart, making it her fourth song to top the chart. Billboard explained, 'Kierra ties for the third-most Gospel Airplay No. 1s among women, dating to the chart's 2005 inception, matching [[Tasha Cobbs Leonard]]. [[Tamela Mann]] leads and shares the overall mark with [[Kirk Franklin]] (eight No. 1s), while [[Jekalyn Carr]] has notched five.'<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9649538/kierra-karen-clark-sheard-gospel-airplay-chart/|title=Kierra & Karen Clark-Sheard Team Up Atop Gospel Airplay Chart|first=Xander|last=Zellner|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|via=[[Billboard.com]]|date=October 22, 2021|access-date=October 25, 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Sheard is a graduate of [[Wayne State University]] in Detroit, Michigan, where she received her bachelor's degree in English with a minor in psychology.  Though her touring schedule has been curbed to accommodate her academic goals, Sheard did embark on a Japanese tour with her mother Karen Clark-Sheard in August 2006.{{citation needed|date=February 2012}} In 2013, Sheard began preaching at her father's church and others. In 2017, she was officially licensed in the COGIC as an evangelist.<ref>https://www.instagram.com/p/BXdI9exBW2W/</ref>

In June 2015, Sheard released the Fall/Winter collection of Eleven60,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://myeleven60.com/|title=Eleven60}}</ref> which honors her inspiration, her mother, Karen Clark Sheard.  Eleven60 is named for the month and year that Clark Sheard was born. Sheard has personally designed classic and refreshing looks for the professional curvy woman that is chic, sexy, bold and eclectic. She has crisscrossed the globe and absorbed the fashion trends of New York City, London, Paris, Tokyo and Seoul.

In 2020 after releasing 3 singles “Don’t Judge Me (ft. Missy Elliot)”, “It Keeps Happening”, and “Something has to Break (Ft. Tasha Cobbs Leonard)” she released her live self titled “KIERRA” produced by her brother and producer J. Drew Sheard.

On June 20, 2020, her 33rd birthday, Sheard became engaged to her Fiancé Jordan Kelly and on December 12, 2020, the two married.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.essence.com/love/kierra-sheard-jordan-kelly-engaged/|title = 'The Clark Sisters' Star Kierra Sheard is Engaged}}</ref>

On December 12, 2020, Sheard posted that she married her fiancé Jordan Kelly, in a private ceremony surrounded by close family and friends.
