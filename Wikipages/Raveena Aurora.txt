'''Raveena Aurora''',<!-- middle name needs confirmation, NEED SOURCE (born September 30, 1994) --> also known [[mononym]]ously as '''Raveena''', is an American singer and songwriter.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://nylon.com/raveena-interview-lp-lucid |title=Raveena Moves Into The Light |last=Ridner |first=Grant |date=May 31, 2019 |work=[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]] |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref><ref name="npr room">{{Cite news |url=https://www.npr.org/2019/06/05/728644002/make-room-for-raveena-debut-album-lucid |title=Make Room for Raveena |last=Madden |first=Sidney |date=June 5, 2019 |access-date=August 1, 2019 |publisher=[[NPR]]}}</ref> Her debut [[Extended Play|EP]], ''Shanti'', was released independently in 2017. Her first album, ''Lucid'', was released independently in 2019 and distributed through [[Empire Distribution]].<ref name="billboard stunning">{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8515822/raveena-lucid-debut-interview |title=Rising R&B Artist Raveena On Her Stunning Debut 'Lucid' |last=Blais-Billie |first=Braudie |date=June 14, 2019 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=V EXCLUSIVE: Raveena Aurora is the Goddess of DIY|url=https://vmagazine.com/article/v-exclusive-raveena-aurora-is-the-goddess-of-diy/|access-date=2021-06-04|website=V Magazine}}</ref>

==Early life==
Aurora was born in [[Massachusetts]]<ref name="billboard stunning" /> and grew up in both [[Queens]], New York and [[Stamford, Connecticut]].<ref name="billboard stunning" /><ref name="npr room" /> Her family immigrated to Queens from India after the [[1984 anti-Sikh riots|1984 anti-Sikh pogroms]] in which her maternal uncle was killed, and her family's business was burned down. She was raised in a traditional [[Sikh]] household.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.vogue.com/vogueworld/article/raveena-mama-new-music-video-india-immigrant-mothers-day-style |title=This R&B Singer's Style Is a Colorful Tribute to Her Indian Immigrant Mama |last=Hahn |first=Rachel |date=May 15, 2019 |work=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]] |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref> She became exposed to [[R&B]], [[Soul music|Soul]], [[Jazz]] and [[Folk music|Folk]] music in middle school, which piqued her interest in music and influenced her musical style later in life.<ref name="i-D sweet time">{{Cite news |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_us/article/d3ybnk/premiere-raveena-aurora-celebrates-women-of-color-in-her-self-directed-sweet-time |title=Premiere: Raveena Aurora celebrates women of color in her self-directed "Sweet Time" |last=Blais-Billie |first=Braudie |date=October 12, 2017 |work=[[i-D]] |access-date=August 1, 2019 |publisher=[[Vice Media]]}}</ref> She studied at [[New York University Tisch School of the Arts]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Make Room For Raveena|url=https://www.npr.org/2019/06/05/728644002/make-room-for-raveena-debut-album-lucid|access-date=2021-05-31|website=NPR.org|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
Aurora started working with record producer Everett Orr in 2015. She amassed a sizable online following after the release of her first EP, ''Shanti'' in December 2017. ''Shanti'' explored themes of self-love and healing, and blended R&B, soul, and jazz music.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.spin.com/2018/02/raveena-i-wont-mind-stream-review |title=Listen to R&B Singer Raveena's Warm and Gentle Ballad "I Won't Mind" |last=Jagota |first=Vrinda |date=February 20, 2018 |access-date=August 1, 2019 |publisher=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]}}</ref> Her debut EP drew praise from Sidney Madden of [[NPR]] for her "cool delivery and chill-inducing falsetto runs" and "astute songwriting chops".<ref name="npr slingshot">{{Cite news |url=https://www.npr.org/2018/01/09/570751142/slingshot2018 |title=Meet The 2018 Slingshot Artists |last=Madden |first=Sidney |date=January 9, 2018 |work=[[NPR]] |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref> She directed several of her own music videos where she displayed her Indian heritage and "rich interiority of women of color like herself".<ref name="i-D brown">{{Cite news |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_us/article/pa95qk/raveena-temptations-video-queer-brown-love |title=Singer-songwriter Raveena is normalizing queer, brown love |last=Blais-Billie |first=Braudie |date=October 24, 2019 |work=[[i-D]] |access-date=August 1, 2019 |publisher=[[Vice Media]]}}</ref>

In the summer of 2018, she was included in [[ModCloth]]'s ''Say It Louder'' campaign, which aimed to celebrate individuality and strong female icons in music. She was featured alongside other outspoken female musicians like [[Lizzo]], [[Awkwafina]] and [[Hayley Kiyoko]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://nylon.com/articles/modcloth-say-it-louder-campaign |title=Awkwafina and Mary Lambert Star In ModCloth's New Campaign, Alongside Musician Raveena |last=Intner |first=Katie |date=August 6, 2018 |work=[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]] |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref> In November 2018, she performed at [[Tyler, The Creator]]'s [[Camp Flog Gnaw Carnival]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://earmilk.com/2018/11/14/6-standout-up-and-coming-acts-from-this-years-camp-flog-gnaw-carnival/ |title=6 standout up-and-coming acts from this year's Camp Flog Gnaw Carnival |last=Tulay |first=Rasheed |date=November 14, 2018 |work=[[Earmilk]] |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref>

In March 2019, Aurora co-headlined the [[Java Jazz Festival]] in [[Jakarta, Indonesia]], with [[H.E.R]] and [[Toto (band)|Toto]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.thejakartapost.com/life/2019/03/06/java-jazz-festival-2019-wraps-up-after-weekend-of-diverse-acts.html |title=Java Jazz Festival 2019 wraps up after weekend of diverse acts |last=Gitomartoyo |first=Wening |date=March 6, 2019 |work=[[Jakarta Post]] |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref>

On May 31, 2019, she released her debut album ''Lucid'', distributed through [[Empire Distribution]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://hypebeast.com/2019/6/raveena-lucid-album-stream |title=Raveena Offers Silky Vocals & Intimate Lyricism on Debut Album 'Lucid' |last=Ingvaldsen |first=Torsten |date=June 4, 2019 |website=Hype Beast |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref> Lyrically, she explored sensuality, healing from trauma, and spirituality; through tracks like "Stronger" and "Salt Water", she opened up about her experience as a sexual assault and abuse survivor.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2019/07/asian-women-in-music/ |title=5 Emerging Asian Women in Music |last=Eda |first=Yu |date=July 23, 2019 |access-date=August 1, 2019 |publisher=Complex Magazine}}</ref><ref name="crack">{{Cite news |url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/long-reads/raveena-maps-space-healing/ |title=With heavenly R&B, Raveena maps out a space for healing |last=Daly |first=Rhian |work=[[Crack Magazine]] |access-date=August 1, 2019 |issue=102}}</ref> ''Lucid'' was met with very positive reviews. Sidney Madden of [[NPR]] described ''Lucid'' as "comforting but nuanced, balancing cultures old and new by mashing up contemporary R&B with traditions from the South Asian diaspora".<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.npr.org/2019/06/05/728644002/make-room-for-raveena-debut-album-lucid |title=Make Room For Raveena |last=Madden |first=Sidney |date=June 5, 2019 |access-date=August 1, 2019 |publisher=NPR}}</ref> In a track review for the penultimate track, "Petal", Vrinda Jagota from [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] wrote: "over the course of 12 songs, her sound becomes bolder and clearer, finding strength in everything from sun showers to her mother’s resilience to her own femininity and womanhood".<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/raveena-petal/ |title=Track Review: "Petal" by Raveena |last=Jagota |first=Vrinda |date=June 13, 2019 |work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=August 1, 2019}}</ref> "Lucid" was named one of the "Best Albums of 2019" by [[NPR]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=NPR Staff |title=NPR Music Staff Picks: The Best Albums Of 2019 |url=https://www.npr.org/2019/12/17/788715692/npr-music-staff-picks-the-best-albums-of-2019 |accessdate=21 December 2019 |publisher=NPR |date=December 17, 2019}}</ref> Aurora's 2019 single "Stronger" was named one of the "100 Best Songs of 2019" by [[Noisey]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Vice Staff |title=The 100 Best Songs of 2019 |url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/akwwxz/the-100-best-songs-of-2019 |accessdate=22 December 2019 |agency=Noisey |publisher=Vice Media |date=December 18, 2019}}</ref>
