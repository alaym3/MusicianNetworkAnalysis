'''Joseph Davis'''<ref name=chicagotribune2018>{{cite web|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-ott-joey-purp-quarterthing-thalia-hall-preview-0921-story.html|title=Chicago rapper Joey Purp talks 'QUARTERTHING,' trusting his path and staying grateful|work=[[Chicago Tribune]]|first=Jessi|last=Roti|date=September 19, 2018|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> (born August 3, 1993),<ref name=birthday>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/JoeyPurps/status/496016728439414785|title=365 days ago I turned 20 in jail.|website=[[Twitter]]|author=Joey Purp|date=August 3, 2014|access-date=September 8, 2018}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Joey Purp''', is an American rapper from [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.redeyechicago.com/music/redeye-joey-purp-iiidrops-mixtape-review-20160525-story.html|title=Joey Purp's great mixtape 'iiiDrops' is worth the 3-year wait|work=[[RedEye]]|first=Josh|last=Terry|date=May 27, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> He is one half of [[Leather Corduroys]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2016/06/joey-purp-interview-iiidrops-savemoney|title=Get to Know Joey Purp, the Laid-Back Astrologist in Chicago's SaveMoney Crew|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|first=Chris|last=Mench|date=June 2, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> He is one of the founding members of [[Savemoney]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/joey-purp-iiidrops-release|title=Joey Purp Drops His Proud, Triumphant Mixtape 'iiiDrops'|work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|first=John|last=Hill|date=May 27, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref>

==Early life==
Joey Purp grew up bouncing between various neighborhoods of Chicago, ranging from [[Wrigleyville]] to [[Humboldt Park, Chicago|Humboldt Park]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/features/rising/9897-joey-purp-and-the-responsible-party/|title=Joey Purp and the Responsible Party|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Britt|last=Julious|date=June 3, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> His father ran a restaurant and his mother managed thrift stores.<ref name=chicagotribune2016/> He attended [[Whitney M. Young Magnet High School|Whitney Young High School]], where he realized that he had a talent for freestyle rapping.<ref name=chicagotribune2016/> In his senior year, he dropped out of high school.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/read/joey-purps-moment-of-clarity-iiidrops-noisey-next|title=This Feels Crazy, Yeah This Feels Different: Joey Purp's Moment of Clarity|work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|first=Kyle|last=Kramer|date=June 1, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> He worked and interned at LDRS 1354, a streetwear store at [[Wicker Park, Chicago|Wicker Park]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/joey-purp-save-money-rapper-iiidrops-chance-mensa/Content?oid=22219059|title=How Chicago made Joey Purp|work=[[Chicago Reader]]|first=Leor|last=Galil|date=May 25, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2012, Joey Purp released his first mixtape, ''The Purple Tape''.<ref name=chicagotribune2016>{{cite web|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/kot/ct-joey-purp-ott-0603-20160531-column.html|title=Joey Purp is a man of many musical neighborhoods|work=[[Chicago Tribune]]|first=Greg|last=Kot|date=June 2, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> In 2016, he released a mixtape, ''[[iiiDrops]]''.<ref name=consequenceofsound2016>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2016/05/stream-joey-purps-new-mixtape-iiidrops/|title=Stream: Joey Purp's new mixtape iiiDrops|work=[[Consequence of Sound]]|first=Michelle|last=Geslani|date=May 28, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> Tentatively titled ''Eyedrops'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2016/05/joey-purp-iiidrops/|title=Joey Purp Releases 'iiiDrops' LP|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|first=C. Vernon|last=Coleman II|date=May 30, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> it featured contributions from [[Chance the Rapper]] and [[Vic Mensa]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.38999/title.joey-purp-enlists-chance-the-rapper-vic-mensa-for-iiidrops-mixtape|title=Joey Purp Enlists Chance The Rapper & Vic Mensa For "iiiDrops" Mixtape|work=[[HipHopDX]]|first=Cherise|last=Johnson|date=May 27, 2016|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> It was included on the year-end lists by ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/best-albums-of-2016/joey-purp-iiidrops|title=The 50 Best Albums of 2016: 36. Joey Purp, 'iiiDrops'|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=December 6, 2016|access-date=February 9, 2020}}</ref> ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/9987-the-top-20-rap-albums-of-2016/|title=The 20 Best Rap Albums of 2016 (page 1 of 2)|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=December 5, 2016|access-date=February 9, 2020}}</ref> ''[[Rolling Stone]],''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/40-best-rap-albums-of-2016-105945/joey-purp-iiidrops-114063/|title=40 Best Rap Albums of 2016: 31. Joey Purp, 'iiiDrops'|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=December 22, 2016|access-date=February 9, 2020}}</ref> and Lyrical Lemonade.

In 2018, Joey Purp released his debut studio album, ''[[Quarterthing]]''.<ref name=billboard2018>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8473919/joey-purp-caroline-partner-quarterthing-album-distribution|title=Joey Purp Partners With Caroline for 'QUARTERTHING' Debut Album Release|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Starr|last=Bowenbank|date=September 6, 2018|access-date=September 8, 2018}}</ref> It included guest appearances from [[GZA]] and [[RZA]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.48333/title.joey-purp-returns-with-quarterthing-album|title=Joey Purp Returns With "QUARTERTHING" Album|work=[[HipHopDX]]|first=Justin|last=Ivey|date=September 6, 2018|access-date=September 8, 2018}}</ref>

==Style and influences==
Joey Purp grew up listening to [[Wu-Tang Clan]], [[Sex Pistols]], [[The Casualties]], [[The Velvet Underground]], [[Lou Reed]], [[Dipset]], and [[Lil Wayne]].<ref name=xxlmag>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2014/06/the-break-presents-joey-purp/|title=The Break Presents: Joey Purp|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|first=Eric|last=Diep|date=June 4, 2014|access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref> In a 2014 interview, he stated that his early recordings were heavily inspired by [[Currensy]].<ref name=xxlmag/>
