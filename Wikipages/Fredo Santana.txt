'''Derrick Coleman'''<ref>{{cite web|last=Gale |first=Alex |url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2013/01/24/details-of-chief-keef-s-6-million-record-deal-emerge.html |title=Chief Keef Could Earn Up to $6 Million From Record Deal {{pipe}} News |publisher=BET |access-date=2013-12-27}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://kollegekidd.com/news/fredo-santana-beat-up-a-school-teacher-when-he-was-14/|title=Fredo Santana Beat Up A School Teacher When He Was 14|first=KollegeKidd|last=Staff|date=30 August 2014|access-date=20 May 2019}}</ref> (July 4, 1990 ‚Äì January 19, 2018), known professionally as '''Fredo Santana''', was an American rapper from [[Chicago Illinois|Chicago, Illinois]]. The older cousin of Chicago rapper [[Chief Keef]], Santana began recording music in 2011, releasing a series of mixtapes throughout 2012 and 2013.

His debut studio album, ''[[Trappin Ain't Dead]]'', was released on October 31, 2013, via Savage Squad, and peaked at number 45 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>Ramirez, Erika [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5777986/fredo-santana-debuts-kendrick-lamar-assisted-song-jealous-listen Fredo Santana Debuts Kendrick Lamar-Assisted Song, 'Jealous': Listen] ''Billboard''. May 30, 2015</ref> Santana's second studio album, ''Fredo Krueger 2'', a sequel to his 2013 mixtape ''Fredo Krueger'', was released on September 8, 2017.

Santana died of a seizure on January 19, 2018.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=http://abc7chicago.com/society/chicago-rapper-fredo-santana-cousin-of-chief-keef-dies-at-27/2973726/|title=Chicago rapper Fredo Santana, cousin of Chief Keef, dies at 27|publisher=Sun-Times Media|date=21 January 2018}}</ref><ref name="auto1">{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/fredo-santana-dead-rapper-drake-video-27-friend-maxo-kream-instagram-a8169521.html|title=Fredo Santana dies: Rapper who appeared in Drake video passes away age 27|date=21 January 2018|newspaper=The Independent|author=Loughrey, Clarisse}}</ref><ref name="BB">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8095243/chicago-rapper-fredo-santana-dies-at-27|title=Chicago Rapper Fredo Santana Dies at 27|date=20 January 2018|magazine=Billboard|author=Cook-Wilson, Winston}}</ref>

== Music career ==
{{update section|date=September 2019}}
Fredo's first mixtape, ''It's a Scary Site'', was released on September 20, 2012. It featured production by [[TM88]], [[Young Chop]], 12Hunna, Leek E Leek, J-Hill, C-Sick, and Paris Bueller as well as guest appearances from Chief Keef, [[Lil Reese]], [[King Louie (rapper)|King L]], Gino Marley, Frenchie, [[G Herbo|Lil Herb]], [[Lil Bibby]], and [[Lil Durk]]. Santana's second mixtape, ''Fredo Kruger'', was released on February 28, 2013, and featured production by [[808 Mafia]], [[Young Chop]] and [[Mike Will Made It]], as well as guest appearances from [[Migos]], [[Juelz Santana]], [[Soulja Boy]], [[Young Scooter]], [[Fat Trel]], Alley Boy, Lil Durk and Lil Reese among others. It would later be released for retail sale via iTunes on May 7, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/fredo-kruger/id646030047 |title=iTunes - Music - Fredo Kruger by Fredo Santana |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2013-05-07 |access-date=2013-12-27}}</ref>

On September 24, 2013, Fredo Santana made a cameo appearance in [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s music video for "[[Hold On, We're Going Home]]", in which he portrayed a bad guy kidnapping Drake's "girlfriend."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://wordonroad.net/interview/fredo-santana-speaks-part-drakes-hold-going-home-video/ |title=Fredo Santana Speaks On His Part In Drake's "Hold On, We're Going Home" |publisher=Wordonroad.net |date=2013-09-24 |access-date=2013-12-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131012010957/http://wordonroad.net/interview/fredo-santana-speaks-part-drakes-hold-going-home-video/ |archive-date=2013-10-12 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

His debut album, ''Trappin Ain't Dead'', was released on November 20, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.audiomack.com/album/wegothiphop/fredo-santana-trappin-aint-dead |title=Fredo Santana - Trappin Ain't Dead - Listen |publisher=Audiomack |access-date=2015-09-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5777986/fredo-santana-debuts-kendrick-lamar-assisted-song-jealous-listen |title=Fredo Santana Debuts Kendrick Lamar-Assisted Song, 'Jealous': Listen |magazine=Billboard |date=2013-10-31 |access-date=2015-09-29}}</ref> The album featured guest appearances by [[Kendrick Lamar]], Chief Keef, [[Peewee Longway]] and other members of Glory Boyz Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/10/fredo-santana-instagrams-tracklist-new-project/ |title=Kendrick Lamar, Chief Keef Will Be On Fredo Santana's New Project - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2013-10-29 |access-date=2013-12-27}}</ref> ''It's a Scary Site 2'' was released on December 20, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Fredo-Santana-Its-A-Scary-Site-2-mixtape.562319.html |title=Fredo Santana - Its A Scary Site 2 Mixtape - Stream & Download |publisher=Datpiff.com |date=2013-12-20 |access-date=2015-09-29}}</ref>

On February 27, 2014, Santana announced that he and Keef were going to release a collaboration album ''Blood Thicker Than Water'', which never came to fruition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/02/chief-keef-fredo-santana-coming-album/ |title=Chief Keef And Fredo Santana Are Coming Out With An Album - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |access-date=2015-09-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/fredo-santana-announces-joint-album-with-chief-keef-news.9565.html |title=Fredo Santana Announces Joint Album With Chief Keef |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=2014-02-28 |access-date=2015-09-29}}</ref> On July 9, 2014, he revealed the track list for his upcoming album ''Walking Legend''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/fredo-santana-reveals-tracklist-and-cover-art-for-walking-legend-news.11228.html |title=Fredo Santana Reveals Tracklist And Cover Art For "Walking Legend" |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=2014-07-09 |access-date=2015-09-29}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Santana was the older cousin of [[Chicago]] rapper [[Chief Keef]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xJDixZT4IZA |title=Chief Keef RedEye Interview in Chicago |publisher=YouTube |date=2012-04-28 |access-date=2013-12-27}}</ref>

=== Health issues ===
Santana was a heavy user of [[drug]]s, at one point being addicted to [[Alprazolam|Xanax]] and [[Lean (drug)|lean]]. Santana attributed his heavy drug use to trauma experienced during his childhood, admitting he had [[posttraumatic stress disorder]] and turned to drugs as a coping mechanism.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.twitter.com/FREDOSANTANA300/status/919036045911400450|title=FREDO SANTANA SSR on Twitter|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.twitter.com/FREDOSANTANA300/status/919035867611611136|title=FREDO SANTANA SSR on Twitter|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref>

Santana was hospitalised in March 2017 after suffering a seizure, which he blamed on a heavy workload and his poor sleep schedule.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/03/fredo-santana-hospital-seizure/|title=Fredo Santana Taken to Hospital After Having a Seizure - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref> After the seizures persisted, Santana was diagnosed with [[idiopathic epilepsy]] in May 2017 and prescribed [[Levetiracetam|Keppra]] to treat it. Despite the medicine, Santana continued to suffer from seizures, usually multiple seizures in a row.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/08/fredo-santanas-cause-of-death-cardiovascular-disease-and-epilepsy/|title=Fredo Santana's Cause of Death Includes Cardiovascular Disease - XXL|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref>

Santana was hospitalized once again in October 2017 after friend and fellow rapper Gino Marley found Santana mid seizure on the floor of his house with blood coming from his mouth.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.twitter.com/FREDOSANTANA300/status/919036654735671297|title=FREDO SANTANA SSR on Twitter|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref> Santana was rushed to hospital and diagnosed with both liver and kidney failure, with the main factors being his addiction to Xanax and lean.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/10/fredo-santana-hospital-liver-kidney-failure/|title=Fredo Santana in the Hospital With Liver and Kidney Failure - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref> Santana expressed interest in going to rehab while in hospital.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.twitter.com/FREDOSANTANA300/status/919036165759516674|title=FREDO SANTANA SSR on Twitter|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref>

== Death ==
On the evening of January 19, 2018, at around 11:30&nbsp;p.m. local time, Santana's girlfriend discovered him unresponsive at their home in [[Reseda, Los Angeles]].<ref name="auto"/><ref name="auto1"/><ref name="BB"/> Shortly after, Santana was pronounced dead. He had suffered a fatal seizure, and an autopsy revealed he had developed [[cardiovascular disease]] in addition to the previous conditions he suffered from.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/08/11/the-cause-of-chicago-rapper-fredo-santanas-death-has-been-determined|title=The cause of Chicago rapper Fredo Santana's death has been determined|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref> Santana is one of a number of [[27 Club|celebrities who were 27 years old when they died]].
