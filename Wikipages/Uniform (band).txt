'''Uniform''' is an American [[industrial metal]] band, formed in 2013 in [[New York City|New York]] by vocalist Michael Berdan and guitarist Ben Greenberg. The current line-up of the band features Mike Sharp on drums.

==History==
Greenberg previously played with [[The Men (punk band)|The Men]] and Pygmy Shrews whereas Berdan sang for Drunkdriver and York Factory Complaint.<ref name="allmusic-bio"/> The band came together after Greenberg and Berdan, who played in bands 3 years prior, started living on the same street.<ref name="revolver-interview"/> The duo recorded their eponymous 12" single, which was released in 2014. Their debut studio album, 2015's ''[[Perfect World (Uniform album)|Perfect World]]'', was issued on Alter Records. For the release of 2016's ''Ghosthouse'' EP, the band signed to [[Sacred Bones Records]].<ref name="allmusic-bio"/>

2017 saw the release of the band's second album, ''[[Wake in Fright (album)|Wake in Fright]]''. Following the tour with [[experimental metal]] band [[The Body (band)|The Body]], Berdan contributed to their 2018 album ''I Have Fought Against It, But I Can't Any Longer.'', and the bands released a collaborative record ''Mental Wounds Not Healing''.<ref name="allmusic-bio"/> The band also enlisted [[Liturgy (band)|Liturgy]] and [[Zs (band)|Zs]] drummer Greg Fox for its third studio album, 2018's ''[[The Long Walk (album)|The Long Walk]]''.<ref name="tmt-longwalk-news"/> A second collaborative album with The Body, titled ''Everything That Dies Someday Comes Back'', was issued in 2019.<ref name="stereogum-the-body-news"/> In January 2020, the band released the track "Awakening" as a part of the [[Adult Swim]] single series.<ref name="stereogum-awakening-news"/> In June 2020, the band announced their fourth album, ''[[Shame (Uniform album)|Shame]]'', and released its lead single "Delco".<ref name="revolver-delco"/>  The second single from the album, "Dispatches From the Gutter," was released in July 2020 with an accompanying music video.<ref name="stereogum-dispatch"/> Released on September 11, 2020, the record was mixed by [[Randall Dunn]] and features Mike Sharp on drums.<ref name="stereogum-shame-news"/><ref name="vents-shame-news"/>

==Musical style==
Uniform's style has been described as [[industrial metal]],<ref name="revolver-delco"/><ref name="pitchfork-wake-review"/><ref name="exclaim-shame-review"/> [[noise rock]],<ref name="allmusic-bio"/><ref name="allmusic-wake-review"/><ref name="chicago-reader20"/> [[industrial rock]]<ref name="revolver-delco"/><ref name="allmusic-wake-review"/> and industrial punk.<ref name="exclaim-longwalk-review"/> The band incorporates elements from [[industrial music]], [[thrash metal]] and [[power electronics]];<ref name="allmusic-bio"/> according to Luca Cimarusti of ''[[Chicago Reader]]'', the band "continually streamlined their sound, toying with [[Wax Trax! Records|Wax Trax! industrial]], straightforward [[punk rock|punk]], and electronic synth swaths—sometimes all at once."<ref name="chicago-reader20"/> Their second record,''Wake in Fright'', has brought together elements from [[electronic body music|EBM]], [[hardcore punk|hardcore]] and [[noise music|noise]],<ref name="exclaim-longwalk-review"/> while "leaning more heavily towards their hardcore and metal heritage" than their debut album, ''Perfect''.<ref name="pitchfork-wake-review"/> [[AllMusic]]'s Paul Simpson compared the record to "an overdriven version of [[Ministry (band)|Ministry]]'s most thrash-influenced material, while likening Berdan's vocals to those of [[David Yow]] and "a severely electroshocked [[David Thomas (musician)|David Thomas]]."<ref name="allmusic-wake-review"/> [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'s Andy O'Connor has compared the "mangled, mid-paced guitar riffs" on ''The Long Walk'' to "[[Celtic Frost]] recorded through a series of budget amps and distortion pedals, then played at deafening volume."<ref name="pitchfork-longwalk-review"/>

The band's live setup features heavy use of [[drum machine]]s, [[Synthesizer|bass synthesizer]]s and multiple [[guitar amplifier]]s;<ref name="allmusic-bio"/> Berdan additionally works on [[electronic musical instruments|electronic instruments]] such as synthesizers and [[Music sequencer|sequencers]] while Greenberg handles [[drum programming]], synthesizer parts and [[Record producer|production]].<ref name="revolver-interview"/> ''Perfect World'' was recorded with a single bass synthesizer and a drum machine; for its follow-up ''Wake in Fright'', the band sought to "get a bigger sound" by the use of [[Sampling (music)|sampling]] and sequencing on [[Pro Tools]].<ref name="revolver-interview"/><ref name="quietus-interview"/> Samples of [[gunshot]]s and [[explosion]]s were also used to produce [[rhythm section|rhythm tracks]].<ref name="timeout-interview"/> Introduction of on Greg Fox's live drums on ''The Long Walk'' necessitated pre-production and modifications on Greenberg's guitar rig; his modified rig enabled him to trigger the bass synthesizer while playing the guitar.<ref name="revolver-interview"/><ref name="fact-interview"/> Live drums on the follow-up record,  ''Shame'', were performed by Mike Sharp; the record featured "various shades of heavy, electronically charged rock,"<ref name="allmusic-shame-review"/> while incorporating elements from [[doom metal]], [[black metal]] and thrash metal.<ref name="allmusic-shame-review"/><ref name="music-omh-shame-review"/>
