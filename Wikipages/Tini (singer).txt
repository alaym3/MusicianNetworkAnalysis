'''Martina<!-- Before adding 'Alejandra', see talk page discussion. --> Stoessel-Muzlera''' (born 21 March 1997),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elcomercio.com/tendencias/martinastoessel-entrevista-violetta-disney-television.html|title=Martina Stoessel: Violetta 'ha hecho popular al español en lugares remotos'|last=Orquera|first=Luis Fernando|work=[[El Comercio (Ecuador)|El Comercio]]|language=es|date=6 May 2015|access-date=8 November 2018}}</ref> known professionally as '''Martina Stoessel''' ({{IPA-es|ˈtini estoˈesel|lang}}), or by the stage name '''Tini''' (stylised in [[all caps]]) is an [[Argentine]] actress, singer, songwriter, dancer and model. Stoessel began her career by appearing on the Argentine children's television series [[Patito Feo (telenovela)|''Patito Feo'']] (2007–2008). In her teens, she rose to prominence for her lead role as [[List of Violetta characters#Violetta Castillo|Violetta Castillo]] in the [[Disney Channel (Latin America)|Disney Channel Latin America]] original telenovela, ''[[Violetta (TV series)|Violetta]]'' (2012–2015).

Following her role in the ''Violetta,'' Stoessel has recorded eight successful [[soundtrack album]]s, that includes original musical numbers from diverse musical genres such as [[pop music]] and [[Latin music]]. The albums reached No. 1 and top 5 positions in [[Argentina]] and several [[Europe]]an countries, and were also reached multiple gold and platinum certifications. Alongside her television career, Stoessel has starred her role in the film ''[[Tini: The Movie]]'' (2016), a sequel to the ''Violetta'', where she along with four cast members from series, reprise their roles.

With achieved great worldwide success, Stoessel signed with [[Hollywood Records]], to release her self-titled debut studio album, ''[[Tini (album)|Tini (Martina Stoessel)]]'' (2016). The album contains Spanish-English songs, and also includes music from ''Tini: The Movie''. The album sold over 200,000 copies worldwide in less than two months, and reached No. 1 in Argentina, as well as the top 10 in [[Italy]], [[Germany]], [[Austria]], [[Poland]] and several other countries in Europe. Stoessel's sophomore studio album, ''[[Quiero Volver]]'' (2018) with the sound of [[latin pop]], reached at the top of the album chart in Argentina, becoming her second No. 1 album in the country. The album's lead single, which is AR certified platinum, "Te Quiero Más", and single "Por Que Te Vas", were Stoessel's first songs that charted in the top 20 on the AR [[Argentina Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. The album also spawned her single collaborations "Princesa" with [[Karol G]], with whom she broke through US [[Latin Airplay]] and US [[Latin Pop Airplay|Latin Pop]] charts, and album's title track, a collaboration with [[Sebastián Yatra]], that reached number 21 on the AR Hot 100.

Stoessel maintained her latin pop and [[ballad]] sound, where she also experimented new sounds like [[reggaeton]], [[Cumbia (Colombia)|cumbia]] and [[Trap music|trap]] on her third studio album, ''[[Tini Tini Tini]]'' (2020), which received [[Album certification|double diamond certification]] by [[Argentine Chamber of Phonograms and Videograms Producers|CAPIF]], for selling more than 500,000 digital copies in Argentina. ''Tini Tini Tini'' album, spawned her singles AR double platinum "Fresa", AR platinum "Oye" and AR gold "Ella Dice", that were Stoessel's first singles to debut at top 5 in AR ''Billboard'' Hot 100. The singles made her break the record for most top 5 debuts on the AR Hot 100 chart. In 2021, Stoessel achieved big success with the single "[[Miénteme]]", by which she announced her upcoming fourth studio album. The single debuted at No. 1 on Billboard AR Hot 100, becoming Stoessel's first number-one single on the chart. The single also found its way in high position on the [[Billboard Global 200|''Billboard'' Global 200]] and Global Exl. US, making Stoessel the first Argentine artist to achieve such success.

Stoessel's accolades include two [[Premios Gardel|Gardel Awards]], seven Premios Quiero Awards, one [[Nickelodeon Argentina Kids' Choice Awards|Argentina Kids' Choice Award]], one [[Nickelodeon Colombia Kids' Choice Awards|Colombia Kids' Choice Award]], three [[Bravo Otto|Bravo Otto Awards]], two [[Martín Fierro Awards]], one [[Billboard Argentina|Billboard Award AR]], two [[MTV Millennial Awards]], and nominations for the [[MTV Europe Music Awards]], [[2019 MTV Europe Music Awards|MTV EMA Seville]] and [[Heat Latin Music Awards]]. In 2016, 2019 and 2021 Stoessel was named one of the 10 most influential women from Argentina. She became the only artist that ever completely sold-out nine [[Estadio Luna Park]] shows with her [[Quiero Volver Tour]]. In 2020, Stoessel was named Billboard's Argentina artist of the year and No. 1 Argentine artist on [[Spotify]].

== Life and career ==
=== 1997–2011: Early life and career beginnings ===
Stoessel was born in Buenos Aires, the daughter of Mariana Muzlera and television director and producer Alejandro Stoessel. She has a brother Francisco, who is one year older than her.<ref>[http://www.bestmovie.it/news/martina-stoessel-il-mio-primo-bacio-meglio-di-quello-di-diego/236867/ Martina Stoessel: «Il mio primo bacio? Meglio di quello di Diego» | Best Movie]. Bestmovie.it. Retrieved 7 December 2013.</ref> Stoessel began her artistic training at a young age studying singing, piano, musical comedy, musical theater, and dance in her native Buenos Aires.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lanacion.com.ar/1482067-quien-es-la-nueva-chica-disney-argentina|title=¿Quién es la nueva chica Disney argentina?|last=Ajmat|first=Silvina|work=[[La Nación]]|language=es|date=15 June 2012|access-date=15 September 2018}}</ref> Stoessel was educated at two private bilingual schools: Colegio San Marcos and Colegio Martín y Omar de San Isidro.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clarin.com/espectaculos/fama/Martina-Stoessel-cuarta-temporada-Violetta_0_r14FHiO9PQx.html|title=Martina Stoessel: "No habrá cuarta temporada de Violetta"|work=Clarin.com|language=es|date=15 October 2014|access-date=24 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.infobae.com/teleshow/infoshow/2016/06/05/tini-stoessel-a-solas-con-teleshow-estos-ultimos-dos-anos-senti-un-gran-crecimiento-personal-2/|title=Tini Stoessel, a solas con Teleshow: "Estos últimos dos años sentí un gran crecimiento personal"|work=Infobae.com|language=es|date=5 June 2016|access-date=24 January 2019}}</ref>

In 2007, Stoessel had a role in the first season of the popular Argentine children's telenovela, ''[[Patito Feo]]'', as "Martina",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1217518/?ref_=nm_flmg_act_2|title=Patito feo (TV Series 2007– )|publisher=[[IMDb]]}}</ref> one of Fito Bernardi's assistants.<ref>[http://www.ilmondodipatty.it/2013/06/28/violetta-martina-stoessel-nel-mondo-di-patty-video/ Il Mondo di Patty | Sito Ufficiale]. Ilmondodipatty.it. Retrieved 7 December 2013.</ref> She also played the role of "Anna" in a [[flashback episode]] in the same series.

In 2011, Stoessel recorded the Spanish version of the [[Shannon Saunders]]' song, "The Glow", called "[[Disney Princess|Tu Resplandor]]"; the song was included on the album, ''Disney Princess: Fairy Tale Songs''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://adiccionmusical.com/tu-resplandor-martina-stoessel/|title=Tu resplandor – Martina Stoessel|author=usrtrp12yt|work=Adicción Musical|language=es|date=29 November 2011|access-date=10 March 2020}}</ref> She performed the song at an event for Disney Channel Latin America called ''[[Disney Channel (Latin America)#Celebratón 2011|Celebratón]]'' on 31 December 2011. The song was later released on an album for the series in March 2012.

=== 2012–2014: Breakthrough with ''Violetta'' ===
[[File:Tini Stoessel photoshoot 2013.png|thumb|left|180px| Stoessel at backstage photoshoot for Violetta season 2 in 2013]]
Stoessel's father presented an unrelated project to [[Disney Channel]] producers, who later informed him about auditions for the lead role for the network's upcoming television series, ''[[Violetta (TV series)|Violetta]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clarin.com/espectaculos/chicos/adolescente-suenos-musicales_0_SyYPVN2D7l.html|title=Una adolescente con sueños musicales|work=Clarin.com|language=es|date=12 May 2012|access-date=15 September 2018}}</ref> In late 2011, after an intense casting process, Stoessel obtained the principal role in the series, a tween-oriented musical series launched as a co-production between [[Disney Channel (Latin America)|Disney Channel Latin America]], [[Disney Channel (Central and Eastern Europe)|Europe]], [[Disney Channel (Europe, Middle East and Africa)|Middle East and Africa]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lanacion.com.ar/1472512-hecho-en-argentina-tv-para-el-mundo|title=Hecho en Argentina: TV para el mundo|last=Marín|first=Ricardo|work=[[La Nación]]|language=es|date=12 May 2012|access-date=18 September 2018}}</ref> The show's first season began production in Buenos Aires in 2012. Both songs were used as the theme songs for the series in its international broadcasts in Italy, the United Kingdom and the United States via [[Netflix]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/in-my-own-world-from-violetta/id687155282|title=In My Own World (From "Violetta") – Single|publisher=[[iTunes Store]]}}</ref> For this role Stoessel won an award for "Female Newcomer" in the 2012 edition of the [[Kids' Choice Awards Argentina]]<ref name="premi"/> and was also nominated for the U.S. version, the [[Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Awards]], in the "Favorite Latin Artist" category.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lateengossip.com/2013/02/2013-kids-choice-awards-artista-latino-favorito/|title=2013 Kids Choice Awards Artista Latino Favorito|work=Lateengossip.com|date=14 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141023182334/http://lateengossip.com/2013/02/2013-kids-choice-awards-artista-latino-favorito/|archive-date=23 October 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Stoessel released "Got Me Started", along with "Ya No Hay Nadie Que Nos Pare", as her third solo single to coincide with the release of the deluxe edition of her album. The music video for "Got Me Started" was released on 8 December 2016, while the video for "Ya No Hay Nadie Que Nos Pare" was released on 19 January 2017. Stoessel's fourth solo single, "Si Tú Te Vas", was released on 5 May 2017, alongside the music video for the song. == Artistry ==
=== Musical style ===
While working on ''Violetta'', Stoessel met with [[pop music]] and [[Latin music]]. She later transferred the pop sound to her first solo album ''[[Tini (album)|Tini (Martina Stoessel)]]''. As Stoessel matured on her second album ''[[Quiero Volver]]'', she began to use the more serious sound of Latin music. Later in her third album ''[[Tini Tini Tini]]'', Stoessel experimented with genres that are common to her country and that she has not yet tried, such as [[reggaeton]], [[Cumbia (Colombia)|cumbia]], [[Urban contemporary|urban]] and [[trap music]]. In addition, Stoessel is also recognizable by her romantic [[ballad]]s. She also used in the poem with [[tango music|tango]] and said that "tango and reggaeton is something that represents Argentina and all Argentines. Those genres represent the Argentine heritage.".

=== Influences ===
Even before being released by Disney, and even more because of that, Stoessel was already a fan and was inspired throughout her career by [[Miley Cyrus]],<ref>[https://www.efe.com/efe/espana/cultura/martina-stoessel-la-estrella-disney-dice-adios-a-violetta-y-hola-tini/10005-2909654]</ref> [[Demi Lovato]] and [[Selena Gomez]], who, like her, were successful protagonists in [[Disney Channel]]'s series and later launched themselves in the music industry.<ref>[https://g1.globo.com/musica/noticia/tini-canta-em-sp-neste-sabado-conheca-a-estrelinha-argentina-revelada-em-violetta.ghtml]</ref> She was even questioned by the media if she would follow the same path, sometimes controversial, of some of them and she stated that each one does what they like and follows their own road, she would not follow the same path as the other Disney stars.<ref>[https://f5.folha.uol.com.br/televisao/2013/10/1362904-nao-seguirei-o-caminho-de-miley-cyrus-diz-nova-estrela-da-disney.shtml]</ref>

As Stoessel grew in her profession as a singer, she also had other inspirations. In 2019, in an interview with ''[[Seventeen (American magazine)|Seventeen]]'' magazine in Mexico, when asked which was the biggest of them, Stoessel replied: "[[Beyoncé]]! She's awesome, I love her. I love everything she does, her music, her shows are amazing, how she dances... I like that you can see how she is present in the smallest details.".<ref name="hollywoodrecords.com">[https://www.hollywoodrecords.com/app/uploads/2019/07/tini-seventeen_compressed.pdf]</ref> This, in fact, was already confirmed in previous years by her visual style (she even worked with stylist Paula Selby Avellaneda, who worked with [[Beyoncé]]<ref>[https://arteview.com.br/tini-stoessel-de-violetta-vem-ao-brasil-com-a-turne-got-me-started/]</ref>), her live performances<ref>[https://www.latinpopbrasil.com.br/noticias/exclusivo-tini-stoessel-o-show-do-brasil-sera-surpreendente/]</ref> and choreographies. Stoessel has always made clear her admiration and how much she is a fan of the singer, citing her on her YouTube channel in 2016 as the person who she would most like to collaborate with.<ref name="hollywoodrecords.com"/> While in the Latin world, Stoessel said that she grew up listening to [[Daddy Yankee]] and [[Alejandro Sanz]], with whom she recorded a ballad "Un Beso en Madrid", which she described as "dream come true" .

== Personal life ==
Stoessel resided in [[San Isidro, Buenos Aires]] with her parents and her brother, until she temporarily moved to [[Miami|Miami, Florida]], due to the expansion of her music career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clarin.com/viva/Tini-Stoessel-criticas-angustian_0_1574842641.html|title=Tini Stoessel: "Las criticas ya no me angustian más"|work=Clarin.com|language=es|date=15 May 2016|access-date=16 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gente.com.ar/entretenimiento/celebrities/los-motivos-detras-del-viaje-de-tini-stoessel-a-miami/|title=Los motivos detrás del viaje de Tini Stoessel a Miami|work=[[Gente y la actualidad|Gente]]|language=es|date=30 May 2021|access-date=29 September 2021}}</ref> She speaks Spanish (her native language), Italian, and English.

=== Relationships ===
In 2013, Stoessel started dating fellow Argentine actor [[Peter Lanzani]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.zyri.net/2021/06/15/the-first-photo-of-martina-stoessel-and-peter-lanzani-together/|title=The first photo of Martina Stoessel and Peter Lanzani together
|work=tiry.net|language=en|date=26 November 2016|access-date=13 January 2018}}</ref> After two years of dating, they broke up in April 2015. They got back together in the summer of the same year, but broke up again in December 2015.<ref>[https://www.ciudad.com.ar/espectaculos/peter-lanzani-tini-stoessel-separados-motivo_68218]</ref>

In September 2016, Stoessel announced that she started dating Spanish model Pepe Barroso Silva, who was a guest star in the music video for "Great Escape".<ref>[https://www.purepeople.com/article/martina-stoessel-alias-violetta-so-in-love-de-son-nouveau-petit-ami_a204651/1]</ref> In February 2018, Stoessel announced that she and Silva are no longer dating, but they got back together in May 2018.<ref>[https://www.infobae.com/gente/lo-ultimo/2018/09/04/la-foto-mas-hot-de-tini-stoessel-y-su-novio-cuando-tengo-tu-vitamina/]</ref> On 22 December 2018, Stoessel announced that she and Silva broke up again.<ref>[https://www.diezminutos.es/famosos-corazon/famosos-espanoles/amp25830053/pepe-barroso-tini-stoessel-ruptura/]</ref>

On 10 June 2019, during the ''Martin Fierro'' awards ceremony, Stoessel announced that she started dating Colombian singer and frequent collaborator [[Sebastián Yatra]].<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8515450/sebastian-yatra-tini-dating-see-pics]</ref> In May 2020, it was announced that Stoessel and Yatra broke up.<ref>[https://www.eltiempo.com/cultura/gente/sebastian-yatra-y-la-cantante-tini-stoessel-terminan-su-noviazgo-496220]</ref>

== Other ventures ==
In 2016, Stoessel became the face of the clothing brand ''Cher''.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20170107182825/http://muy.clarin.com/fama/tini-stoessel-sensual-campana-ropa-83103.html]</ref> Later, she launched her clothing line called ''Tini by Martina Stoessel''.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20170401173156/http://www.efeestilo.com/noticia/martina-stoessel-coleccion-moda/]</ref>

=== Activism and philanthropy ===
On 10 August 2013, Stoessel performed, along with the cast of ''Violetta'', at the [[UNICEF]] television charity event ''Un sol para los chicos'', where she sang the songs, "Ser Mejor" and "En Mi Mundo". On 17 November 2016, Stoessel was named an [[Honorary consul|honorary ambassador]] of the world peace by fellow human rights activist [[Adolfo Pérez Esquivel]] and Guatemalan political activist [[Rigoberta Menchú]], at the ''Red voz por la paz'' ceremony in Buenos Aires.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|date=26 November 2016|title=Revista Gente|url=http://www.gente.com.ar/actualidad/tini-stoessel-embajadora-de-paz/24379.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170609150734/http://www.gente.com.ar/actualidad/tini-stoessel-embajadora-de-paz/24379.html|archive-date=9 June 2017|access-date=13 January 2018|work=Gente.com.ar|language=es}}</ref> She is also a philanthropist and uses her fame as a platform to positively influence the lives of people around her.

In August 2018 and 2019, Stoessel participated again in ''Un sol para los chicos'', a solidarity campaign for the benefit of UNICEF. Stoessel became a part of UNICEF's protect #GeneracionUnica for the rights of every child.
