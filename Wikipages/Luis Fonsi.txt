'''Luis Alfonso Rodríguez López-Cepero''' (born April 15, 1978), known by his stage name '''Luis Fonsi''' ({{IPA-es|ˈlwis ˈfonsi}}), is a Puerto Rican singer. He is known for multiple songs, one of them being "[[Despacito]]" featuring rapper [[Daddy Yankee]].

Fonsi received his first [[Latin Grammy Award]] nominations in the ''Record of the Year'' category and won ''Song of the Year'' thanks to the song "[[Aquí Estoy Yo]]". The song also won three [[Billboard Latin Music Awards|''Billboard'' Latin Music Awards]]. "Despacito" became Fonsi's biggest success winning four Latin Grammy Awards, seven ''Billboard'' Latin Music Awards, five [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Awards]] and received three [[Grammy Award]]s nominations. He also won one [[Latin American Music Award]] for "[[Échame la Culpa]]" with [[Demi Lovato]].

As of 2018, Fonsi has broken six [[Guinness World Records]] and sold more than 11 million records with "Despacito",<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/news/2018/10/luis-fonsi-receives-seven-guinness-world-records-titles-for-global-chart-topper-d-544436|title=Luis Fonsi receives seven Guinness World Records titles for global chart-topper Despacito|date=October 17, 2018|website=Guinness World Records|access-date=January 18, 2019}}</ref> whose [[music video]] was the [[List of most-viewed YouTube videos|most-watched]] YouTube video of all time until November 2020, with over 7 billion views.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/nov/02/baby-shark-becomes-most-viewed-youtube-video-ever-beating-despacito |title=Baby Shark becomes most viewed YouTube video ever, beating Despacito|first=Ben |last=Beaumont-Thomas|date=November 2, 2020 |work=[[The Guardian]] }}</ref>

== Early life ==
Fonsi was born on April 15, 1978 in [[San Juan, Puerto Rico]], the oldest child of Alfonso Rodríguez and Delia López-Cepero. He has two younger siblings: [[Jean Rodríguez]], who also is a singer, and Tatiana Rodríguez.

Growing up, Fonsi idolized the stars of the popular group [[Menudo (band)|Menudo]],<ref>Jump up ^ Ross, Karl. "Remember MENUDO?". Retrieved February 22, 2017</ref> and the [[San Juan Children's Choir]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.etonline.com/luis-fonsi-on-his-influential-20-year-career-and-his-mission-to-help-rebuild-puerto-rico-exclusive|title=Luis Fonsi on His Influential 20-Year Career|website=Entertainment Tonight|access-date=September 2, 2019}}</ref>

At the age of 10, his family decided to move from Puerto Rico and relocated to [[Orlando, Florida]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cordeiro|first=Monivette|title=Luis Fonsi talks about going from singing on Orlando's corners to creating a worldwide hit|url=https://www.orlandoweekly.com/orlando/luis-fonsi-talks-about-going-from-singing-on-orlandos-corners-to-creating-a-worldwide-hit/Content?oid=7030053|access-date=2021-10-18|website=Orlando Weekly}}</ref> He attended Westridge Middle School and [[Dr. Phillips High School]], both in Orlando. During his high school years, he participated in an [[a cappella]] group called "The Big Guys".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Luis Fonsi Says He Was in a Doo Wop Group With Joey Fatone, Performs 'Imposible' On 'Tonight' Show': Watch|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8495873/luis-fonsi-tonight-show-performance-interview-videos/|access-date=2021-10-18|website=Billboard}}</ref> They sang at school parties and local festivals. One of the members of the group, [[Joey Fatone]], later joined [[NSYNC|*NSYNC]].

In 1995, Fonsi enrolled at [[Florida State University]] School of Music on a full scholarship,<ref>"Luis Fonsi Biography, Discography, Chart History." Top40-Charts.com. N.p., n.d. Web. March 6, 2017. majoring in Vocal Performance.</ref><ref>"Luis Fonsi – Biography | Billboard". ''Billboard''. Retrieved February 23, 2017</ref> He also joined the school's [[choir]], the Florida State University Singers, and sang with the [[City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Evan |last=Bailyn |url=http://www.musicofpuertorico.com/index.php/artists/luis_fonsi |title=Artists – Fonsi, Luis |publisher=Music of Puerto Rico |date=April 15, 1978 |access-date=November 13, 2015}}</ref> Eventually, he dropped out in order to pursue a career in music.<ref>"Biografia de Luis Fonsi". www.biografiasyvidas.com (in Spanish). Retrieved February 23, 2017.</ref> Soon after, he was offered a recording contract by [[Universal Music Latin Entertainment|Universal Music Latin]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.universalmusic.com/label/universal-music-latin-entertainment/ |title= Universal Latin Music Entertainment |author=<!--Not stated--> |website= universalmusic.com |publisher= Universal Latin Music Entertainment |access-date= 5 August 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 1998–2006: Early albums ===
[[File:Luis Fonsi (cropped).JPG|thumb|upright|Fonsi in 2005]]
In 1998, Fonsi recorded his debut album, ''[[Comenzaré]]'' (''I Will Begin''). The record peaked at number 11 on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Top Latin Albums chart and contained the singles "Si Tú Quisieras", "Perdóname", "Dime Como", and "Me Iré".<ref name="allmusicbio">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/luis-fonsi-mn0000312004|title=Luis Fonsi – Biography|last=Birchmeier|first=Jason|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=August 13, 2012}}</ref> ''Comenzaré'' became a hit in Puerto Rico and across Latin America, with Fonsi doing well in markets like Colombia, the Dominican Republic, El Salvador, Mexico, and Venezuela. His 2000 follow-up, ''[[Eterno]]'', was even more successful. Around this time, Fonsi also recorded a duet with [[Christina Aguilera]] for her 2000 Spanish-language album, ''[[Mi Reflejo]]''. On May 1, 2000, Fonsi performed at the [[Jubilee 2000|Great Jubilee Concert for a Debt-Free World]], an outdoor concert in Rome, attended by [[Pope John Paul II]]. That same year, [[Ednita Nazario]] won a [[Latin Grammy Award]] for a song composed by Fonsi. He also performed, with other artists, at the White House in honor of the victims of the [[September 11 attacks]].

In 2002, Fonsi was the opening act for [[Britney Spears]]' [[Dream Within a Dream Tour]] in the US and Mexico. In the same year he debuted his English album "Fight The Feeling" trying to do a crossover with the first single "Secret" featuring [[Joey Fatone]] in the video. In 2003, Luis Fonsi performed to billions of viewers across the globe at [[Miss World 2003]] in China.

His fifth CD, ''[[Abrazar la vida]]'', sold very well, which opened new markets in Europe. "¿Quién Te Dijo Eso?" reached number one on the ''Billboard'' Latin charts. During his time in the studio for the next album, Fonsi recorded "Amazing", a duet with [[Spice Girls|Spice Girl]] [[Emma Bunton]] for her 2004 album ''[[Free Me (album)|Free Me]]''. His sixth CD, ''[[Paso a Paso]]'', debuted at number one and sent him into international markets. "[[Nada Es Para Siempre]]" also reached number one on the music charts, and was a nominee for the Latin Grammy Awards. In 2006, he contributed to ''El Piruli'', a tribute album honoring [[Víctor Yturbe]], singing a classic [[bolero]], "Historia de un Amor".

=== 2007–2015: ''Palabras del Silencio'', ''Tierra Firme'', and ''8'' ===
In 2007, Luis Fonsi was selected to be part of the jury of the new version of the Latino boy band [[Menudo (band)|Menudo]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Gurza|first1=Agustin|title=Remaking the band: MTV revives Menudo|url=http://www.seattletimes.com/entertainment/remaking-the-band-mtv-revives-menudo/|access-date=January 12, 2016|publisher=The Seattle Times|date=April 15, 2007}}</ref> The band would be a fusion of urban, pop and rock music in English and Spanish to produce several albums with the label of [[Sony BMG]] [[Epic Records]]. Several auditions were held in different cities such as [[Los Angeles]], [[Dallas]], [[Miami]], [[New York City|New York]], among others. Fonsi was a part of the [[Dallas]] competition where along the side of radio announcer [[Daniel Luna]] they chose various contestants and in their pickings, rising star [[JC Gonzalez]] was 1 of the 25 selected.

His seventh CD, ''[[Palabras del Silencio]]'', debuted and stayed in the number one position for many weeks. Luis Fonsi entered the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] for the first time in September 2008 with his song "[[No Me Doy por Vencido]]", debuting at number 98 and peaking at number 92. It reached number one on the ''Billboard'' Hot Latin Tracks chart, one of his biggest hits to date. "No Me Doy por Vencido" became ''Billboard''{{'}}s "Latin Pop Song of the Decade", and spent 21 weeks at number one on the Hot Latin Songs chart.

In November 2009, Luis Fonsi was awarded a [[Latin Grammy Award]] for "Song of the Year" for his composition "[[Aquí Estoy Yo]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latingrammy.com/en/awards|title=20a Entrega Anual del Latin GRAMMY|website=Latin GRAMMYs|access-date=September 2, 2019}}</ref>

On December 11, 2009, Fonsi performed at the [[Nobel Peace Prize Concert]] in [[Oslo]], honoring recipient President [[Barack Obama]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.monstersandcritics.com/music/features/article_1518804.php/In-Pictures-Nobel-Peace-Prize-Concert?page=4|title= In Pictures: 'Nobel Peace Prize Concert'|access-date = February 11, 2018|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20121011073252/http://www.monstersandcritics.com/music/features/article_1518804.php/In-Pictures-Nobel-Peace-Prize-Concert?page=4 |archive-date=October 11, 2012}}</ref>

In 2011, Fonsi released the album ''[[Tierra Firme (album)|Tierra Firme]]'', and went on tour to promote it throughout Latin America.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://adondeirhoy.com/conciertos-en-costa-rica/luis-fonsi-en-costa-rica|title=Luis Fonsi en Costa Rica|publisher=adondeirhoy.com|access-date=October 21, 2011}}</ref>

On July 9, 2011, Billboard named Fonsi "Leader of Latin Music's New Generation".<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/latin-awards/6069833/luis-fonsi-wisin-join-forces-for-billboard-latin-music-awards|title=Luis Fonsi & Wisin Join Forces for Billboard Latin Music Awards (Video)|magazine=Billboard|access-date=September 2, 2019}}</ref>

In 2014, Fonsi released his album ''[[8 (Luis Fonsi album)|8]]'', a reference to his eighth career album. He then did a tour named "Somos Uno" in 2014–15. He also mentioned that acting would be on his career path and that he wouldn't mind acting with Mexican actors and actresses.

===2017–present: "Despacito" and international success===
[[File:Luis Fonsi in concert.jpg|thumb|upright=1.3|Fonsi in concert]]
In January 2017, "[[Despacito]]" featuring [[Daddy Yankee]] was promoted and released. On April 5, 2018, the music video reached five billion views on [[YouTube]], with the song becoming number one in nearly every Latin ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' chart, and the most viewed video in the world. In April 2017, the song was given an English remix featuring Canadian singer [[Justin Bieber]]. The remix featuring Bieber reached number one on the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100 for the week ending May 27, 2017, becoming both Fonsi and Yankee's first number one on the chart, and Bieber's fifth. Bieber promoted the remix at one of his shows in Puerto Rico, inviting Fonsi onto stage to perform the duet with him. The song remained number one for 16 consecutive weeks, tying One Sweet Day, by Mariah Carey and Boyz II Men as the longest reigning top song in the chart's history.

On August 15, 2017, Fonsi was named the tourism ambassador of Puerto Rico<ref>{{Cite magazine|date=August 15, 2017|title=Luis Fonsi Named Puerto Rico's Tourism Ambassador After 'Despacito' Video's Global Smash Success|url=http://www.billboard.com/index.php/articles/columns/latin/7905040/luis-fonsi-puerto-rico-tourism-ambassador-despacito|access-date=September 24, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

In November 2017, he released the song "[[Échame la Culpa]]", featuring [[Demi Lovato]], which debuted at number three on the Hot Latin chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8053915/luis-fonsi-echame-la-culpa-hot-latin-songs-demi-lovato|title=Luis Fonsi Returns to Hot Latin Songs Top 3 With Demi Lovato Collaboration 'Echame La Culpa'}}</ref> The song won ''Song of the Year'' at the [[Latin American Music Awards of 2018]] and received one nomination for ''Best Latin'' at the [[2018 MTV Video Music Awards|MTV Video Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.etonline.com/luis-fonsi-demi-lovato-vmas-message-love-support-exclusive-108228|title=Luis Fonsi Sends Message of Love & Support to Demi Lovato (Exclusive)|website=Entertainment Tonight|access-date=September 2, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://remezcla.com/lists/music/latino-artists-mtv-vmas/|title=Latino Artists Blessed Us With These Memorable Moments at the MTV VMAs|date=August 20, 2018|website=Remezcla|access-date=September 2, 2019}}</ref> As of 2018 the song sold more than 1 million copies worldwide.

In June 2018, Fonsi released the single "[[Calypso (Luis Fonsi and Stefflon Don song)|Calypso]]", with [[Stefflon Don]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Fernandez|first=Suzette|title=Luis Fonsi Announces Release Date for New Song 'Calypso,' Feat. Stefflon Don: Listen to a Preview (Exclusive)|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8459601/luis-fonsi-calypso-tease-stefflon-don-release-date|access-date=June 19, 2018|work=Billboard|date=June 6, 2018}}</ref> The song peaked at number 11 on the Hot Latin chart. On November 2, 2018, he collaborated on the track  "[[Baby (Clean Bandit song)|Baby"]] by [[Clean Bandit]] from their [[What Is Love? (Clean Bandit album)|second studio album.]]<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8482076/clean-bandit-announce-new-single-baby-luis-fonsi-marina|title=Clean Bandit Announce New Single 'Baby' Featuring Luis Fonsi and Marina|magazine=Billboard|access-date=January 20, 2019}}</ref> The song peaked at number 13 of [[Dance/Electronic Singles Sales|Billboard Hot Dance/Electronic Songs]] and at number 15 of [[UK Singles Chart]].

In October 2018, he collaborated with Italian singer [[Eros Ramazzotti]] for the song "[[Per le strade una canzone]]", from the album ''[[Vita ce n'è]]''.<ref name="The 105">{{cite web|url=https://www.105.net/news/music-biz/252935/eros-ramazzotti-ecco-il-video-di-vita-ce-n-e.html|title=Eros Ramazzotti, ecco il video di 'Vita ce n'è'|publisher=[[Radio 105 Network|Radio 105]]|language=it|date=October 19, 2018|access-date=November 4, 2018}}</ref>

On January 13, 2019, the singer joined with [[Wisin]], [[Alejandra Guzmán]] and [[Carlos Vives]] as coaches of the first Spanish season of [[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|La Voz]] by [[Telemundo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/veronicavillafane/2019/01/12/telemundo-debuts-la-voz-with-luis-fonsi-alejandra-guzman-carlos-vives-and-wisin/|title=Telemundo Debuts 'La Voz' With Luis Fonsi, Alejandra Guzmán, Carlos Vives And Wisin|last=Villafañe|first=Veronica|website=Forbes|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref>

After five years since  "[[8 (Luis Fonsi album)|8"]], on February 1, 2019 his 10th studio album "[[Vida (Luis Fonsi album)|Vida"]] was released. The track list contains "Despacito (Remix) ", "[[Échame la Culpa]]" and "Sola".<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8493018/luis-fonsi-new-album-vida-feb-1|title=Luis Fonsi Will Release New Album 'Vida' Feb. 1: Exclusive|magazine=Billboard|access-date=January 25, 2019}}</ref> The album topped the [[Billboard Top Latin Albums]] and [[Productores de Música de España|Spanish]] album chart, and entered at number 18 of the [[Billboard 200]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.promusicae.es/listas/semana/3970-%C3%81lbumes-semana-6-2019|title=Top 100 Albumes – Semana 6: del 1.2.2019 al 7.2.2019|publisher=[[Productores de Música de España]]|language=es|access-date=February 13, 2019}}</ref><ref name="BB200">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/garysuarez/2019/02/12/luis-fonsi-ozuna/#cfd8bd84b518|title=Luis Fonsi Logs Three Simultaneous Billboard Latin Chart Hits|work=[[Forbes]]|last=Suarez|first=Gary|date=February 12, 2019|access-date=February 12, 2019}}</ref>

In 2019, Fonsi performed "Right Where I'm Supposed to Be" as the Official Song of the [[2019 Special Olympics World Summer Games]] in [[Abu Dhabi]], [[United Arab Emirates]] in collaboration with [[Ryan Tedder]], [[Avril Lavigne]] [[Hussain Al Jassmi]], [[Assala Nasri]] and [[Tamer Hosny]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/right-where-im-supposed-to-be-single/1457094508|title=Right Where I'm Supposed to Be - Single by Ryan Tedder|publisher=Apple}}</ref>

On July 26, 2019, Luis Fonsi performed at the opening ceremony of the [[2019 Pan American Games]] in [[Lima]], [[Peru]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1082481/luis-fonsi-lima-2019-opening-ceremony|title=Singer Luis Fonsi "honoured" to be part of Lima 2019 Opening Ceremony|date=July 22, 2019|website=insidethegames.biz|access-date=July 31, 2019}}</ref> He sang his songs including his worldwide hit "[[Despacito]]" at the ceremony.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/ap-explains-a-look-at-the-pan-american-games-in-lima/2019/07/26/c7a361ca-b01d-11e9-9411-a608f9d0c2d3_story.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190731181400/https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/ap-explains-a-look-at-the-pan-american-games-in-lima/2019/07/26/c7a361ca-b01d-11e9-9411-a608f9d0c2d3_story.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=July 31, 2019|title=AP Explains: A look at the Pan American Games in Lima|newspaper=Washington Post|access-date=July 31, 2019}}</ref>

== Film and television appearances ==
In 1992, Fonsi made a brief cameo in the romance film ''[[Like Water for Chocolate (film)|Como Agua Para Chocolate]]'' in which he played the friend of the male lead, Pedro Múzquiz.

In 2004, Fonsi made his second acting appearance on the Mexican [[telenovela]] named ''[[Corazones al límite]]'' in which he played Roy. He also had a special appearance in 2001 on the [[Nickelodeon (Latin America)|Nickelodeon]] television series ''[[Taina (TV series)|Taina]]''. Fonsi joined the cast of Broadway's ''Forever Tango'' for a two-week engagement in August 2013

== Personal life ==
In 2003, Fonsi started to become romantically interested in actress [[Adamari López]], who, as an [[Univision]] artist and fellow Puerto Rican, constantly found herself near Fonsi.<ref name="lub">{{Cite web|url=http://www.netglimse.com/celebs/pages/luis_fonsi/index.shtml|title= Luis Fonsi – Biography|access-date = February 11, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090215123008/http://www.netglimse.com/celebs/pages/luis_fonsi/index.shtml |archive-date=February 15, 2009}}</ref> That same year, Fonsi released the song "Abrazar la vida" ("To Embrace the Life"), off the eponymous album. The song would later become one of López's favorites.<ref name="lub" />

During 2005, Fonsi was in the middle of an international tour that was abruptly cancelled when López was diagnosed with [[cancer]]. Fonsi promised to stay by her side, and traveled with her to Mexico, Miami, and Puerto Rico for various treatment- and work-related trips.<ref name=lub/> She has been in remission since 2006.

On June 3, 2006, Fonsi and Adamari López were married in a religious ceremony in [[Guaynabo, Puerto Rico]], attended by many celebrities, including [[Joey Fatone]], [[Charytín Goyco]], [[Ednita Nazario]], and [[Carlos Ponce]], as well as his brother Jean Rodríguez. On November 8, 2010, they officially divorced.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lovetripper.com/newswire/61174-adamari-lopez-fonsi.html |title=Adamari Lopez, Luis Fonsi's Puerto Rico Wedding |publisher=Lovetripper.com |date=June 3, 2006 |access-date=November 13, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180128182420/http://www.lovetripper.com/newswire/61174-adamari-lopez-fonsi.html |archive-date=January 28, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Fonsi and [[Spaniards|Spanish]] [[Model (person)|model]] Águeda López were married on September 10, 2014, after three years of living together.<ref>{{cite web |author=Carreira |title=Everything About Luis Fonsi: All You Have to Know! |url=https://instageeked.com/everything-about-luis-fonsi-all-you-have-to-know/ |website=Instageeked |date=July 14, 2018|access-date=December 8, 2020}}</ref> They had their first child together, a daughter, in December 2011 in [[Miami]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/Entertainment/Music/Puerto-Rican-singer-Spanish-model-have-their-first-child/Article1-785605.aspx|title=Puerto Rican singer, Spanish model have their first child|access-date = February 11, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131030004515/http://www.hindustantimes.com/Entertainment/Music/Puerto-Rican-singer-Spanish-model-have-their-first-child/Article1-785605.aspx |archive-date=October 30, 2013}}</ref> On December 20, 2016, they welcomed their second child, a son.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.univision.com/entretenimiento/el-bebe-de-luis-fonsi-y-agueda-lopez-nacio-el-mismo-dia-que-mikaela-su-primogenita |title = ''El bebé de Luis Fonsi y Águeda López nació el mismo día que Mikaela, su primogénita| website = univision.com|access-date = February 11, 2018}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==

Since 2008, Luis Fonsi has been an ambassador of [[St. Jude Children's Research Hospital]], which fights against [[childhood cancer]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stjude.org/get-involved/other-ways/st-jude-thanks-and-giving/luis-fonsi-visits-st-jude.html|title=Luis Fonsi brings smiles to our patients|website=stjude.org|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref> In 2017 [[Billboard Latin Music Awards]] honored him with the ''[[Billboard Spirit of Hope Award|Spirit of Hope Award]]'' for his humanitarian work.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7767491/luis-fonsi-spirit-of-hope-award-2017-billboard-latin-music-awards|title=Luis Fonsi to Receive Spirit of Hope Award at the 2017 Billboard Latin Music Awards|magazine=Billboard|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref>

After [[Hurricane Maria]] struck [[Puerto Rico]] and the [[Dominican Republic]] in 2017, the singer became one of the major helpers to raise the islands from the devastation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3037709/luis-fonsi-puerto-rico-hurricane-irma-relief/|title=Luis Fonsi Tearfully Calls For Unity And Help For Puerto Rico|last=Hughes|first=Hilary|website=MTV News|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref> For his contribution he was honored with the ''Global Gift Philanthropy Award.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.surinenglish.com/lifestyle/201807/06/luis-fonsi-receive-global-20180706094041-v.html|title=Luis Fonsi to receive Global Gift Philanthropy award|last=Castro|first=Nieves|date=July 6, 2018|website=surinenglish.com|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref>

He is also a supporter of spreading Latin culture around the world.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/jeffbenjamin/2019/01/13/la-voz-premiere-luis-fonsi-wisin-interview/|title=Luis Fonsi & Wisin Discuss 'La Voz' Premiere & Its Importance To New Latin-Music Talent|last=Benjamin|first=Jeff|website=Forbes|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref>
