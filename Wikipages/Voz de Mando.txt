'''Voz de Mando''' is a [[Regional Mexican]] band based in [[Los Angeles, California]], [[United States]].

Their hit single "Levantando Polvadera" won song of the year in the Regional Music category at the 2016 iHeart Radio awards, the first-ever regional Mexican band to win an iHeart radio award.

In 2011, Voz de Mando appeared in the Oscar-nominated film ''[[A Better Life]]'' starring Demian Bichir. Their hit "Commands M. P. ( 500 Balazos )" was also in the film's soundtrack. In 2016, the band participated in the television series ''Route 35'' starring Danna Garcia and Julio Bracho Castillo. Their single "Compadre No Rajes" was the show's theme song.

== Music career ==
=== Origin ===
From Los Angeles, California, the band members also have roots in the Mexican states of [[Michoacan]] and [[Baja California]]. The band was founded in 2009.

=== Early ===
The band started in 2007 with their first release, "Escoge Tu Camino".<ref>http://www.allmusic.com/album/escoge-tu-camino-mw0000225395</ref> The band made a name for itself with the self-released albums ''Levantando la Voz'' (2009) and ''12 Impactos de Alto Calibre'' (2009).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thefair.com/espanol/concert|title=CONCIERTO FIESTAS PATRIAS CON VOZ DE MANDO, PROYECTO X, MARIACHI DIVAS - Washington State Fair|website=www.thefair.com|access-date=7 September 2016}}</ref> The early success led to a contract with [[Universal Music Group|Universal's]] Latin music imprint, [[Disa Records]]. In 2010, they released their first major-label album with ''Con la Nueva Federacion''.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/431106/voz-de-mando/biography|title=Voz de Mando - Biography |magazine=Billboard |access-date=7 September 2016}}</ref> One of several songs on the album written by Gaxiola brother Miguel, the corrido "Comandos del M.P. (500 Balazos)" was a breakout hit single for the group, reaching the top ten in the ''Billboard'' Regional Mexican Songs chart.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WWxbO-SGG7AC&q=500%2520balazos%2520billboard%2520position&pg=RA5-PA46|title=Billboard |date=3 July – 25 September 2010 |publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.}}</ref>

In 2011, Voz de Mando appeared in the [[Academy Awards|Oscar]] nominated film ''A Better Life'', starring [[Demián Bichir|Demian Bichir]]. Their hit "Commands M. P. ( 500 Balazos )" appeared on the film's soundtrack. Subsequent singles "Y Ahora Resulta" (2012) and "Muchacho de Campo" (2013) were crossover hits winning numerous awards, critical and fan recognition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin-notas/5665526/billboard-mexican-music-awards-2013-complete-list-of-finalists|title=Billboard Mexican Music Awards 2013: Complete List of Finalists|access-date=7 September 2016}}</ref>

=== Contemporary (2014 to present) ===
In 2014, the group left Universal Music Group and formed a partnership with [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony Music]]. Founding band member Jorge Gaxiola formed his independent record label AfinArte Music and, along with Sony, released the band's hit single "Batalla del Golfo". The single formed the basis for Voz de Mando's sixth studio album, ''Levantado Polvadera''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://monitorlatino.com/llega-voz-de-mando-levantando-polvadera-al-top-de-monitorlatino-usa/|title=Llega Voz de mando Levantando Polvadera al Top de monitor |website=Monitor Latino|access-date=7 September 2016}}</ref> The album's hit single "Levantando Polvadera" won song of the year in the Regional Music category at the 2016 [[iHeartRadio Music Awards]], the first ever Mexican regional band to win an iHeartRadio award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.iheart.com/news/2016-iheartradio-music-awards-winners-14561927/|title=2016 iHeartRadio Music Awards Winners|access-date=7 September 2016}}</ref>

In 2015, founding member Miguel Gaxiola launched his own line of handmade guitars, Fortaleza Guitars. Fortalezas quickly became a popular instrument in the Mexican music world used by bands like [[Kinky (band)|Kinky]] and [[Conjunto Primavera]].<ref>{{Citation|last=Univision|title=Conoce las guitarras que ahora fabrican los integrantes de Voz de Mando|date=12 August 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oTOJ1-AoXtU|website=Youtube|access-date=7 September 2016}}</ref>

In 2016, the band appeared in the television series ''[[Ruta 35]]'' starring [[Danna García|Danna Garcia]] and [[:es:Julio Bracho Castillo|Julio Bracho Castillo]]. Their single "Compadre No Rajes" was the show's original theme song.<ref>{{Citation|last=VozDeMandoVEVO|title=Voz de Mando - Compadre No Rajes (Official Video)|date=11 March 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NU2pXP8CYtM|website=Youtube|access-date=7 September 2016}}</ref>

In 2017, Voz de Mando released their seventh studio album featuring two chart-topping singles, including the viral hit "Soldado Latinoamericano", which quickly became a hymn of Latino pride in the US and across the border. "Pa’ que no me anden Contando", a top-ten Billboard hit dominated radio play in the US.
