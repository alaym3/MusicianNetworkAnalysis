'''Sloan Christian Struble''' (born August 10, 1999),<ref name=":1"/> is an American singer, songwriter, and producer from [[Aledo, Texas]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/dayglows-feel-good-indie-music-is-the-antidote|title=Dayglow's Feel-Good Indie Music Is the Antidote for Dark Pop|last=|first=|date=June 29, 2019|work=Ones To Watch|access-date=}}</ref>  He is best known as the indie pop project '''Dayglow'''. Dayglow released its debut album, ''Fuzzybrain'', on September 28, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fuzzybrain, by Dayglow|url=https://dayglowband.bandcamp.com/album/fuzzybrain|access-date=2020-04-30|website=Dayglow}}</ref> Dayglow performing members include bassist Peyton Harrington, drummer Brady Knippa,<ref>{{cite web |title=A Reprieve From Chaos With Dayglow |url=https://www.chicagomaroon.com/article/2020/4/1/reprieve-chaos-dayglow/ |website=www.chicagomaroon.com |language=en}}</ref> keyboardist Norrie Swofford,<ref>{{cite web |title=Dayglow 1.21.20 |url=https://kutx.org/studio1a/dayglow-1-21-20 |website=KUTX}}</ref> guitarist Colin Crawford, and in the past have included guitarist Nate Davis, bassist Reece Myers, keyboardist Nico Fennell, bassist Eric Loop, and saxophonist Marshall Lowry.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://acltv.com/2021/05/26/dayglow-tapes-an-effervescent-acl/ |title=Dayglow Tapes an Effervescent ACL|website=Austin City Limits |access-date=August 29, 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Struble was born in [[Aledo, Texas]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/dayglows-feel-good-indie-music-is-the-antidote|title=Dayglow's Feel-Good Indie Music Is the Antidote for Dark Pop [NEW ARTIST DISCOVERY] / Ones To Watch|website=Ones To Watch|access-date=2020-04-30}}</ref> At 11 years old, he started to work with [[GarageBand|Garageband]] to teach himself how to play guitar, bass, keyboard, synthesizer and drums, as well as produce and mix to create music.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=‘I just feel so lucky to have the opportunity’: Student musician reaches worldwide audiences, handles fame - The Daily Texan|url=http://www.dailytexanonline.com/2019/04/30/‘i-just-feel-so-lucky-to-have-the-opportunity’-student-musician-reaches-worldwide|access-date=2021-02-03|website=www.dailytexanonline.com|language=en}}</ref> He started attending the [[University of Texas at Austin]] in 2018, where he studied advertising.<ref name="auto1" />

He uses [[Logic Pro]] to produce his music.

In December 2020, Struble got engaged to Reagan Liddell. On May 28, 2021, he and Liddell were married.

==Career==

=== KINDRED (2016–2017) ===
On October 1, 2016, under the name KINDRED, Struble released his first album, also titled ''KINDRED''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=KINDRED (LP)|url=https://soundcloud.com/kindredsongs/sets/kindred-lp|access-date=2021-03-31|website=SoundCloud|language=en}}</ref> Throughout 2017, KINDRED released three singles. The first, a re-written version of "That's Just Life" by [[Francis and the Lights]], was released on January 14, 2017.<ref>{{Citation|title=Francis and the Lights - That's Just Life (Kindred Re-Write)|url=https://soundcloud.com/kindredsongs/francis-atl-thats-just-life-kindred-re-write|language=en|access-date=2021-03-31}}</ref> This was followed by "This Love" on March 6, 2017.<ref>{{Citation|title=This Love|url=https://soundcloud.com/kindredsongs/this-love|language=en|access-date=2021-03-31}}</ref> On March 30, 2017, Struble released his last song under the name KINDRED, "Spent my Life".<ref>{{Citation|title=Spent My Life|url=https://soundcloud.com/kindredsongs/spent-my-life|language=en|access-date=2021-03-31}}</ref> "Spent my Life" is the only song released under the name KINDRED that is available on streaming services such as [[Spotify]], [[YouTube]] and [[Apple Music]], while the rest of KINDRED's songs are only available on [[SoundCloud]].

=== Dayglow (2017–present) ===

==== 2017-2020: ''Fuzzybrain'' ====
In late 2017, Struble founded the solo indie pop project Dayglow. Despite being the sole member, Struble has stated that he does not view himself as a solo artist, and describes Dayglow as a band, similar to [[Tame Impala]] or [[Mutemath]]. On September 29, 2017, Dayglow released its first single, "Mindless Creatures".<ref>{{Citation|title=Mindless Creatures|url=https://soundcloud.com/dayglowmusic/mindless-creatures|language=en|access-date=2021-03-31}}</ref> The next day, "Run The World!!!" was released.<ref>{{Citation|title=Run The World!!!|url=https://soundcloud.com/dayglowmusic/run-the-world|language=en|access-date=2021-03-31}}</ref> On January 30, 2018, Dayglow released "Can I Call You Tonight?".<ref>{{Citation|title=Can I Call You Tonight?|url=https://soundcloud.com/dayglowmusic/can-i-call-you-tonight|language=en|access-date=2021-03-31}}</ref> "Can I Call You Tonight?" became their first commercially successful song and was eventually certified Gold by the RIAA, in part due its popularity on [[TikTok]] and [[Spotify]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Young|first1=Brigid|title=Review: Peach Pit dazzles the audience at Foellinger Auditorium : Music : Smile Politely|url=https://www.smilepolitely.com/music/review_peach_pit_dazzles_the_audience_at_foellinger_auditorium/|website=www.smilepolitely.com}}</ref> On May 30, 2018, Dayglow released its fourth single, "False Direction".<ref>{{Citation|title=False Direction|url=https://soundcloud.com/dayglowmusic/false-direction|language=en|access-date=2021-03-31}}</ref>

On September 27, 2018, Dayglow released its first full length album, ''Fuzzybrain'', featuring the previously released singles "Run The World!!!", "Can I Call You Tonight?", and "False Direction".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fuzzybrain|url=https://soundcloud.com/dayglowmusic/sets/fuzzybrain-1|access-date=2021-03-31|website=SoundCloud|language=en}}</ref> Following the album release, Dayglow was invited to perform at [[Austin City Limits Music Festival]] at BMI Stage on October 12, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Quiles|first1=Alyssa|last2=Oct. 13|first2=Sun|title=ACL Live Review: Dayglow|url=https://www.austinchronicle.com/daily/music/2019-10-13/acl-live-review-dayglow/|website=www.austinchronicle.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Blackstock|first=Peter|title=ACL Fest wild card: Surfing the park on Saturday afternoon|url=https://www.austin360.com/entertainmentlife/20191012/acl-fest-wild-card-surfing-park-on-saturday-afternoon|access-date=2021-02-03|website=Austin 360|language=en}}</ref> The album cover features a clay model of Struble's head, which he molded himself.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Webb|first=Eric|title=Dayglow is Austin's Mr. Brightside|url=https://www.austin360.com/entertainmentlife/20200408/dayglow-is-austinrsquos-mr-brightside|access-date=2021-02-03|website=Austin 360|language=en}}</ref> On November 13, 2019, Struble released an expanded version of ''Fuzzybrain'', featuring the new songs "Nicknames" and "Listerine", under Acrophase Records.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dayglow – Fuzzybrain (2019, Yellow, Vinyl)|url=https://www.discogs.com/Dayglow-Fuzzybrain/release/13661601|access-date=2021-02-03|website=Discogs|language=en}}</ref>

==== 2021-present: ''Harmony House'' ====
On January 11, 2021, Dayglow released its first song since 2019, "Close to You", along with an accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Blackstock |first1=Peter |title=Austin360 On The Record: Singles spotlight with Dayglow, Pat Byrne, Balmorhea, more |url=https://www.austin360.com/story/entertainment/music/2021/01/22/austin-360-record-singles-spotlight-dayglow-more/6660245002/ |website=austin360}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Close To You - Single by Dayglow|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/close-to-you-single/1543882901|language=en-us|access-date=2021-02-03}}</ref> On January 13, 2021, the single "Can I Call You Tonight?" was certified Gold.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Dayglow Certifications|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=Dayglow#search_section|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=4 February 2021|website=[[RIAA]]}}</ref>

On February 22, 2021, Struble announced Dayglow's second full-length album, ''[[Harmony House (Dayglow album)|Harmony House]]''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Harmony House by Dayglow|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/harmony-house/1552018965|language=en-us|access-date=2021-02-22}}</ref> On the same day, Dayglow released the album's second single, "Something". According to Struble, "Something" reflects on the way social media is used, saying, "Stuff is never going to fulfill us, yet we are always wanting more and more and it’s just such an unfortunate waste of time".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ackroyd|first=Stephen|date=2021-02-22|title=Dayglow's new album 'Harmony House' is coming this May {{!}} Dork|url=https://readdork.com/news/dayglow-harmony-house-something/|access-date=2021-02-22|website=readdork.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

On April 1, 2021, the third single off of ''Harmony House'', "Woah Man" was released. "Balcony" followed on May 6.

On May 21, 2021 ''Harmony House'' was released, alongside a music video for the second track on the album "Medicine".

==Style==
Struble chose the name Dayglow from the song "Day Glo" by [[Brazos (band)|Brazos]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://orangemag.co/orangeblog/2019/10/18/orange-asks-dayglows-sloan-struble|title=ORANGE Asks: Dayglow’s Sloan Struble|website=ORANGE Magazine}}</ref> Dayglow's music is a blend of [[Alternative rock|alternative]] and [[indie pop]].<ref name=":2">{{cite web |last1=Asilo |first1=Rito P. |title=Everything you’ve always wanted to know about Dayglow |url=https://entertainment.inquirer.net/402903/everything-youve-always-wanted-to-know-about-dayglow |website=INQUIRER.net |language=en |date=3 February 2021}}</ref> ''[[Washington Square News]]'' wrote that Struble's style "draws inspiration from the upbeat, groovy style of other artists such as the [[Doobie Brothers]] and [[Michael McDonald (musician)|Michael McDonald]]."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Zack|first=Ethan|date=|title=Dayglow Uses Music to Express Optimism, Change and Friendship {{!}} Washington Square News|url=https://nyunews.com/arts/music/2019/11/21/dayglow-interview/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-03|website=[[Washington Square News]]|language=en-US}}</ref> Other inspirations for his musical style include [[Michael Jackson]] and [[Tame Impala]].<ref name=":1" />
