'''Kevin Robert Morby''' (born April 2, 1988) is an American musician, singer and songwriter. A former member of [[Woods (band)|Woods]] and [[The Babies]], Morby has released six  solo studio albums: ''[[Harlem River (album)|Harlem River]]'' (2013), ''[[Still Life (Kevin Morby album)|Still Life]]'' (2014), ''[[Singing Saw (album)|Singing Saw]]'' (2016), ''[[City Music]]'' (2017), ''[[Oh My God (album)|Oh My God]]'' (2019), and ''[[Sundowner (album)|Sundowner]]'' (2020).<ref>"[http://www.woodsist.com/news/kevin-morby-announces-debut-solo-lp-stream-first-track/ Kevin Morby announces debut solo lp stream first track] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20131224024521/http://www.woodsist.com/news/kevin-morby-announces-debut-solo-lp-stream-first-track/ |date=December 24, 2013 }}", ''Woodsist''. ==Biography==
===Early life, Woods, and The Babies===
Kevin Morby was born in [[Lubbock, Texas]] on April 2, 1988; his family relocated around the U.S. due to his father's employment with [[General Motors]] before settling in [[Kansas City, Missouri]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/kevin-morby-singing-saw-album-release-interview|title=A Wanderlust Soundtrack of L.A.: Kevin Morby on His New Album, Singing Saw|website=Vogue.com|access-date=April 26, 2019}}</ref> Morby learned to play guitar when he was 10. In his teens he formed the band Creepy Aliens.<ref name=Pitch />

17-year-old Morby dropped out of [[Blue Valley Northwest High School]], got his [[GED]], and moved from his native [[Kansas City]] to [[Brooklyn]] in the mid-2000s, supporting himself by working bike delivery and café jobs.<ref name=Pitch>"[https://web.archive.org/web/20150924093026/http://www.pitch.com/kansascity/the-babies-our-house-on-the-hill-riot-room-november-2012/Content?oid=3069107 With Woods, and now the Babies, JoCo native Kevin Morby is inching toward indie-rock renown ]", ''[[The Pitch (newspaper)|The Pitch]]''. Retrieved on 2013-12-28.</ref> Morby has stated he had "loved New York from the movies" he'd seen, "I just wanted to experience it".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.indieberlin.de/music/kevin-morby-interview.html|title=Exclusive Interview with indie legend Kevin Morby  |date=2016-05-11|website=Indieberlin.de|language=en-GB|access-date=2016-05-11}}</ref> He later joined the noise-folk group [[Woods (band)|Woods]] on bass. While living in Brooklyn, he became close friends and roommates with [[Cassie Ramone]] of the punk trio [[Vivian Girls]], and the two formed a side project together called [[The Babies]], who released albums in 2011 and 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.politico.com/states/new-york/city-hall/story/2012/11/brooklyn-bred-band-the-babies-seek-inspiration-from-way-out-west-073398|title=Brooklyn-bred band the Babies seek inspiration from way out west|first=Tobias|last=Carroll|website=Politico.com|access-date=2020-04-02}}</ref>

===''Harlem River'', ''Still Life'', and ''Singing Saw'' (2013–2016)===
Following his move to Los Angeles, Morby recorded a collection of songs with Babies producer Rob Barbato that were intended to be an homage to New York City. Released in 2013 by Woodsist Records, the eight-song collection was called ''[[Harlem River (album)|Harlem River]]'' and became Morby's debut as a solo artist. The album also features drummer Justin Sullivan (The Babies) as well as contributions from Will Canzoneri, Tim Presley (White Fence), Dan Iead, and [[Cate Le Bon]].

While on tour, Morby wrote songs that were later featured on his second album, ''[[Still Life (Kevin Morby album)|Still Life]]''. The album was once again produced by Barbato and released on October 14, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.woodsist.com/catalog/12/kevin-morby-still-life-cdlp/|title=Kevin Morby - Still Life|publisher=[[Woodsist]]|access-date=2014-10-16|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141014180846/http://www.woodsist.com/catalog/12/kevin-morby-still-life-cdlp/|archive-date=October 14, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Morby worked with [[Sam Cohen (musician)|Sam Cohen]] ([[Apollo Sunshine]], [[Yellowbirds]]) on his third album, titled ''[[Singing Saw (album)|Singing Saw]]'', which was released on April 15, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/62998-kevin-morby-woods-the-babies-announces-solo-album-singing-saw-and-tour/|title=Kevin Morby (Woods, the Babies) Announces Solo Album Singing Saw and Tour|publisher=Pitchfork|access-date=2016-03-28}}</ref>

In 2016, Morby wrote ''Beautiful Strangers'', a protest song in remembrance of [[November 2015 Paris attacks|Paris 2015 attacks]], [[Orlando nightclub shooting|Orlando 2016 shooting]], and [[death of Freddie Gray]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2019-04-24|title=The 10 Best Kevin Morby Songs|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/music/kevin-morby/the-10-best-kevin-morby-songs/|access-date=2020-12-21|website=Pastemagazine.com|language=en}}</ref> After various live performance that year,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Beautiful Strangers b/w No Place To Fall, by Kevin Morby|url=https://kevinmorby.bandcamp.com/album/beautiful-strangers-b-w-no-place-to-fall|access-date=2020-12-21|website=Kevin Morby}}</ref> it was released as a single in October 2016, with the proceeds benefitting [[Everytown for Gun Safety]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kevin Morby & Waxahatchee Perform Acoustic 'Beautiful Strangers'|url=https://www.jambase.com/article/kevin-morby-waxahatchee-beautiful-strangers-acoustic-video|access-date=2020-12-21|website=JamBase|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

===''City Music'' (2017–2018)===
Morby's fourth studio album was recorded with his live band, with guitarist Meg Duffy noting: "We all worked on the next record together, pretty collaboratively in terms of arranging and playing. We spent a week in a beautiful studio, up near [[Stinson Beach]]."<ref name=TheRepriseInterview>{{cite web|last=Lindsay|first=Andrew|title=Vulnerable Creativity: A Hand Habits Interview |url=http://www.thereprise.org/features/2017/2/28/vulnerable-creativity-a-hand-habits-interview|work=The Reprise|access-date=28 February 2017|date=28 February 2017}}</ref> The album, ''[[City Music]]'', was released in June 2017.

Apart from ''[[City Music]]'' he also released in 2017 a cover version of “After Hours,” from the [[Velvet Underground]]’s 1969 self-titled album, in duo with [[Waxahatchee]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/waxahatchee-and-kevin-morby-cover-the-velvet-underground-listen/ |title=Waxahatchee and Kevin Morby Cover the Velvet Underground: Listen |date=2017-10-19 |website=Pitchfork.com |access-date=2019-10-20}}</ref> As a [[Homage (arts)|tribute]] to [[Jason Molina]], the duo covered in January 2018 two Jason Molina tracks for [[MusiCares]], an American charity supporting musicians’ health. The digital single, also available as 7” vinyl through [[Dead Oceans]], contained the two songs, “Farewell Transmission” and “The Dark Don’t Hide It”.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/waxahatchee-and-kevin-morby-cover-jason-molina-for-charity-listen/ |title=Waxahatchee and Kevin Morby Cover Jason Molina for Charity: Listen |date=2018-01-25 |website=Pitchfork.com |access-date=2019-10-20}}</ref> The longtime private and musical relationship later led to touring and shows together, among others [[SXSW]] shows and a successful Australia tour ([[Sydney]], [[Melbourne]]) in November 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2031891/watch-kevin-morby-waxahatchee-cover-it-aint-me-babe-at-sydney-opera-house/video/ |title=Watch Kevin Morby & Waxahatchee Cover "It Ain't Me Babe" At Sydney Opera House |date=2019-02-12 |website=Stereogum.com |access-date=2019-10-20}}</ref>

===''Oh My God'' (2019)===
Morby's fifth studio album, ''[[Oh My God (album)|Oh My God]]'',  was announced on February 27, 2019. The first single off the album, "No Halo" was released on the same day. The album was released April 26, 2019 via [[Dead Oceans]]<ref name="ohmygod">{{cite web |last1=Pierre |first1=Alphonse |title=Kevin Morby Announces New Album Oh My God, Shares New Song "No Halo": Listen |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/kevin-morby-announces-new-album-oh-my-god-shares-new-song-no-halo-listen/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=February 27, 2019 |date=February 27, 2019}}</ref> and was met with wide critical acclaim (19 of 20 published reviews aggregated on [[Metacritic]] were scored positive).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/oh-my-god/kevin-morby|title=Oh My God by Kevin Morby|website=Metacritic.com}}</ref> It was featured in the April 24, 2019 [[The Wall Street Journal]] print edition headlined “Devine Intervention” and called Morby’s best.  The album reached number 2 on [[Billboard charts]] Heatseekers albums.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/kevin-morby|title=Kevin Morby|website=Billboard.com|access-date=2020-04-02}}</ref> In September 2019, Morby reunited with Woods for a special performance at their Woodsist festival.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jambands.com/features/2019/10/07/kevin-morby-explores-songs-for-the-mind/|title=Kevin Morby Explores Songs for the Mind|date=October 7, 2019|website=Jambands.com|access-date=October 4, 2021}}</ref>

===''Sundowner'' and more (2020-present)===
Morby's sixth studio album, ''[[Sundowner (album)|Sundowner]]'', was announced on September 1, 2020. The first single off the album, "Campfire", was released on the same day. The album was released on October 16, 2020 via Dead Oceans.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Ruiz |first=Matthew Ismael |date=September 1, 2020 |title=Kevin Morby Announces New Album Sundowner, Shares New Video With Waxahatchee |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/kevin-morby-announces-new-album-sundowner-shares-new-video-with-waxahatchee-watch/ |access-date=September 1, 2020 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref>

On May 1, 2020, Morby released a live version of ''Beautiful Strangers & Harlem River'' on an album titled ''On Mon Dieu: Live à Paris''. The recording came from Morby's sold-out performance at the Cabaret Sauvage in 2019. The album was released on Morby's [[Bandcamp]] through [[Dead Oceans]].<ref>{{Cite web| title= Kevin Morby: Oh Mon Dieu: Live in Paris| url= https://recordstoreday.com/SpecialRelease/12005| website= Record Store Day|access-date= February 11, 2021}}</ref>

In October 2020, Morby released a standalone single, "US Mail" with an accompanying music video as well as sharing his own P.O. box number as a way to promote the [[USPS]].<ref>{{Cite web| url= https://pitchfork.com/news/kevin-morby-shares-new-song-us-mail-listen/|title= Kevin Morby Shares New Song "US Mail"| website= Pitchfork| date= 27 October 2020| access-date= 23 February 2021}}</ref>

In February 2021, [[Marc Maron]] mentioned several times on [[Instagram Live]] that he doesn't know who Morby is, which Morby responded to on his own Instagram page, eventually leading to Maron posting an interview with Morby on Instagram Live.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Rettig |first1=James |title=Marc Maron Finally Knows Who Kevin Morby Is |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2117736/marc-maron-kevin-morby-interview/news/ |access-date=5 March 2021 |publisher=Stereogum |date=28 February 2021}}</ref>

On October 12, 2021, Morby and [[Hamilton Leithauser]], who are touring together in the fall of 2021, released their new single, "Virginia Beach".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ruiz |first1=Matthew Ismael |title=Listen to Hamilton Leithauser and Kevin Morby’s New Song "Virginia Beach" |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/listen-to-hamilton-leithauser-and-kevin-morby-new-song-virginia-beach/ |access-date=19 October 2021 |publisher=Pitchfork |date=12 October 2021}}</ref> On October 8, 2021, Morby released ''A Night at the Little Los Angeles'', an album of 4-track demo versions of songs that were originally on his 2020 album ''Sundowner''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Greene |first1=Linnie |title=A Night at the Little Los Angeles |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/kevin-morby-a-night-at-the-little-los-angeles/ |access-date=19 October 2021 |publisher=Pitchfork |date=15 October 2021}}</ref>

==Mare Records==
In June 2017, Morby officially launched his new label imprint, Mare Records, with the signing of [[Shannon Lay]] and announcement of her new LP, ''Living Water.'' Mare will operate as an imprint of the Woodsist label, which also released Morby's first two solo albums.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.woodsist.com/introducing-mare-our-new-imprint-with-kevin-morby-and-its-first-release-shannon-lays-living-water/|title=Introducing Mare, our new imprint with Kevin Morby and its first release, Shannon Lay's Living Water – Woodsist|website=Woodsist.com|access-date=April 26, 2019}}</ref>
Anna St Louis' debut album, ''If Only There Was a River,'' was scheduled to be released in December 2018 on Woodsist / Mare.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicnewsnet.com/2018/08/anna-st-louis-announces-debut-album-if-only-there-was-a-river-out-1012-on-woodsist-mare-.html|title=ANNA ST. LOUIS ANNOUNCES DEBUT ALBUM "IF ONLY THERE WAS A RIVER', OUT 10/12 ON WOODSIST / MARE|website=Music News Net|access-date=April 26, 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 2017, Morby moved from Los Angeles to [[Overland Park, Kansas]], near where he grew up.<ref name="vulture">{{cite news |last1=Curto |first1=Justin |title=The House That Rebuilt Them |url=https://www.vulture.com/2021/01/profile-katie-crutchfield-kevin-morby.html |access-date=4 February 2021 |publisher=Vulture |date=8 January 2021}}</ref> He is in a relationship with Katie Crutchfield (of the band [[Waxahatchee]]).<ref name="vulture"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.talkhouse.com/artist-couples-talk-on-the-talkhouse-podcast/|title=Artist Couples Talk on the Talkhouse Podcast|website=Talkhouse.com|access-date=2020-04-02}}</ref> Together they have covered songs by [[Jason Molina]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/waxahatchee-and-kevin-morby-cover-jason-molina-for-charity-listen/|title=Waxahatchee and Kevin Morby Cover Jason Molina for Charity: Listen|website=Pitchfork.com|access-date=2020-04-02}}</ref> [[Bob Dylan]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2031891/watch-kevin-morby-waxahatchee-cover-it-aint-me-babe-at-sydney-opera-house/video/|title=Waxahatchee & Kevin Morby Cover Bob Dylan's "It Ain't Me, Babe" At Sydney Opera House: Watch|website=Stereogum.com|date=February 12, 2019}}</ref> [[The Velvet Underground]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/waxahatchee-and-kevin-morby-cover-the-velvet-underground-listen/|title=Waxahatchee and Kevin Morby Cover the Velvet Underground: Listen|website=Pitchfork.com|access-date=2020-04-02}}</ref> and [[Everclear (band)|Everclear]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/72641-waxahatchee-and-kevin-morby-cover-everclear-listen/|title=Waxahatchee and Kevin Morby Cover Everclear: Listen|website=Pitchfork.com|access-date=2020-04-02}}</ref>
