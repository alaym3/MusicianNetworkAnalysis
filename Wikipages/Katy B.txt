'''Kathleen Anne Brien''' (born 8 May 1989), better known as '''Katy B''', is an English singer and songwriter.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/katy-b/|title=Katy B|work=MTV Artists}}</ref><ref name="CamdenCrawl"/> She released her debut studio album, ''[[On a Mission (Katy B album)|On a Mission]]'', in 2011, which spawned two top five hits, "[[Katy on a Mission]]" and "[[Lights On (Katy B song)|Lights On]]". In 2014 she released her second studio album, ''[[Little Red (album)|Little Red]]'', which produced her third top five hit, "[[Crying for No Reason]]". She was nominated for the 2011 [[Mercury Prize]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/katy-b-roundhouse-london-2319841.html |title=Katy B, Roundhouse, London The Independent 25 July 2011 |publisher=Independent.co.uk |date=25 July 2011 |access-date=26 September 2011}}</ref>

==Early life==
Kathleen Anne Brien was born on 8 May 1989, in [[Peckham]], [[London]],<ref>{{cite video |people= UK Funky Live |date=16 August 2009|title=Katy B Interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nqk7VtUfofg|medium=Webcast|publisher=YouTube|access-date=20 July 2010}}</ref> where she attended at Lyndhurst Primary School, [[Haberdashers' Aske's Hatcham College]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/katy-b-academy-manchesterdeath-from-above-1979-forum-london-2280721.html|title=Katy B, Academy, ManchesterDeath From Above 1979, Forum, London|author=Reviewed Simon Price|date=7 May 2011|work=The Independent}}</ref> and the [[BRIT School]]. Katy also attended at the Glenlyn Academy in South London, where she took dance classes.<ref name="CamdenCrawl">{{cite web|url=http://www.thecamdencrawl.com/artist/katy-b|title=The Gaymers Camden Crawl 2010: Artists: Katy B|publisher=The Camden Crawl|access-date=15 July 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120318202254/http://www.thecamdencrawl.com/artist/katy-b|archive-date=18 March 2012}}</ref> Katy's father (David O'Brien) was a member of the [[Les Humphries Singers]], who represented [[Germany in the Eurovision Song Contest|Germany]] at [[Eurovision 1976]], with the song "[[Sing Sang Song]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://wiwibloggs.com/2013/06/17/katy-b-reveals-her-eurovision-roots-on-graham-norton-show/28339/ |title=Katy B Reveals Her Eurovision Roots on Graham Norton Show|publisher=Wiwi Bloggs|access-date=12 October 2013}}</ref>
Katy B possesses a [[soprano]] vocal range.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.mtvhive.com/2011/09/09/katy-b-from-regular-girl-to-uk-dance-diva/ | title = Katy B: Regular Girl Turned UK Dance Diva | work=MTV Hive | access-date=9 September 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url = https://www.dailypost.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/multi-talented-singer-songwriter-katy-11671713.amp| title = Multi-talented singer-songwriter Katy B is just an ordinary girl next door| work=[[Daily Post (North Wales)|Daily Post]]| date=29 July 2016| access-date=15 March 2018| first=Blake| last=Welton}}</ref>

==Career==

===2007–2012: ''On a Mission''===
[[File:Katy B @ Wellington Square.jpg|thumb|left|180px|Katy B performing in 2011]]
Katy has guested on many tracks, both before and during her album release campaign. DJ NG's song "Tell Me", on which she featured as Baby Katy,<ref name="Footloose">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/1xtra/footloose/20070911.shtml|title=BBC – 1Xtra – Footloose|date=11 September 2007|work=[[BBC 1Xtra]]|access-date=15 July 2010}}</ref> was initially released on a [[white label]] and then later signed to [[Ministry of Sound]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ministryofsound.com/product/na/bundle/bundledeal/addicted_to_bass_2010_2009/|title=Addicted to Bass Collection (6CD)|publisher=[[Ministry of Sound]]|access-date=15 July 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ministryofsound.com/product/na/albums/cd/ministry_of_sound_clubbers_guide_summer_2008//|title=Clubbers Guide Summer 2008|publisher=[[Ministry of Sound]]|access-date=15 July 2010}}</ref> She also provided vocals on the track "Hold Me" for [[The Count & Sinden]]'s debut album ''Mega Mega Mega'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dominorecordco.com/uk/news/11-06-10/the-count--sinden-new-album-mega-mega-mega/|title=The Count & Sinden: New Album: Mega Mega Mega Free Album Mix|date=11 June 2010|publisher=[[Domino Recording Company|Domino Records]]|access-date=15 July 2010}}</ref> and features on [[Magnetic Man]]'s tracks "[[Perfect Stranger (Magnetic Man song)|Perfect Stranger]]" and "Crossover".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2010/jul/09/new-band-magnetic-man|title=New band of the day – No 822: Magnetic Man|last=Lester|first=Paul|date=9 July 2010|work=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=16 July 2010}}</ref>

presented by Rinse and produced by [[Benga (musician)|Benga]], was released on 22 August 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rinse.fm/2010/07/katy-on-a-mission/|title=Katy B – Katy on a Mission|date=8 Katy B performed at the [[London Jazz Festival]] in 2009 with [[Ms Dynamite]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.londonjazzfestival.org.uk/events/2009-11-13/ladies-unplugged-featuring-ms-dynamite-katy-b-yolanda-brown-shola-ama|title=Ladies Unplugged: Ms Dynamite + Katy B + Yolanda Brown + Shola Ama • Events • London Jazz Festival|publisher=[[London Jazz Festival]]|access-date=15 July 2010|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120804165545/http://www.londonjazzfestival.org.uk/events/2009-11-13/ladies-unplugged-featuring-ms-dynamite-katy-b-yolanda-brown-shola-ama|archive-date=4 August 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> who also featured on Katy's second single "[[Lights On (Katy B song)|Lights On]]", which was released in the United Kingdom on 19 December 2010, and debuted at number four on the UK Singles Chart. Her third single "[[Broken Record (Katy B song)|Broken Record]]" was released in March 2011, and peaked at number eight. The fourth single "[[Easy Please Me]]" was released on 3 June 2011. Her fifth single "[[Witches' Brew (song)|Witches' Brew]]" was released on 28 August 2011. Katy's debut album ''[[On a Mission (Katy B album)|On a Mission]]'' was released on 4 April 2011, and peaked at number two on the [[UK Albums Chart]].

Katy B supported [[Tinie Tempah]] on his tour during Spring of 2011, and on 30 April 2011 she embarked on her own debut concert tour throughout Europe until September, then the UK in October.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rinse.fm/katyb/ |title=Rinse FM – Katy B – Lights on feat. ===2013–2014: ''Little Red''===
[[File:Katy B 2013.jpg|thumb|upright|Katy B in 2013]]
On 1 October 2013, Katy announced that her second studio album would be titled ''Little Red''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a520300/katy-b-announces-new-album-little-red-at-london-show.html|title=Katy B announces new album 'Little Red' at London show|work=Digital Spy}}</ref> A single, "[[What Love Is Made Of]]", was released on 7 July 2013, charting at number 21 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]; although 
when the official ''[[Little Red (album)|Little Red]]'' track list was revealed on 13 December, "What Love Is Made Of" was no longer included.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151771324732447&amp;set=a.464199357446.254383.116591157446&amp;type=1|title=Katy B - Timeline Photos - Facebook|work=facebook.com}}</ref> Lead single "[[5 AM (Katy B song)|5 AM]]" was released on 4 November 2013, charting at number 14 on the UK Singles Chart.  "[[Crying for No Reason]]" was released on 10 February 2014, reaching the Top 5 of the singles chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.whatsgoodonline.co.uk/music/katy-b-crying-for-no-reason/|title=Katy B - Crying for No Reason - Music Video|work=What's Good? Online}}</ref> ''Little Red'' topped the UK Albums Chart on 19 February 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-26219111|title=Katy B tops the UK album chart with Little Red|work=BBC News}}</ref> "[[Still (Katy B song)|Still]]" was released as the third and final single from the album.<ref name="iTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/still-remixes-single/id871308572|title=Still (Remixes) - Single|date=2 May 2014}}</ref>

On 18 February 2014, Katy's video for the ongoing 'Rinse Sessions x Metropolis' project was revealed, which included a back-to-back cover of [[Beyoncé]]'s 'Drunk in Love' and [[Tinashe]]'s "Vulnerable".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.whatsgoodonline.co.uk/music/katy-b-drunk-in-love-vulnerable/|title=Amazing Cover of 'Drunk' In Love By Katy B - Katy B - Rinse Sessions x Metropolis|work=What's Good? Online}}</ref> In February, she also appeared on the BBC One show ''[[The Voice UK (series 3)|The Voice]]'' as [[Ricky Wilson (British musician)|Ricky Wilson]]'s team mentor.<ref>{{cite web|first=Catriona |last=Wightman |url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/tv/s154/the-voice-uk/news/a553663/the-voice-katy-b-tinie-tempah-more-join-as-mentors-pictures.html |title=The Voice: Katy B, Tinie Tempah, more join as mentors – pictures |date=25 February 2014|access-date=8 March 2014}}</ref>

In March 2014, Katy took part in the recording of England's 2014 World Cup song, collaborating with fellow pop stars [[Melanie C]], [[Eliza Doolittle (singer)|Eliza Doolittle]], [[Emma Bunton]], [[Conor Maynard]], [[Kimberley Walsh]] and [[Pixie Lott]] on "Greatest Day", a track originally performed by the British band [[Take That]]. The track was produced by [[Gary Barlow]] and recorded at [[Sarm Studios]] in London, and also featured former footballers such as [[Gary Lineker]], [[Michael Owen]], [[Geoff Hurst]], [[David Seaman]], [[Peter Shilton]], [[Glenn Hoddle]] and [[Dion Dublin]] on backing vocals.{{citation needed|date=March 2021}} She performed at [[Birmingham Pride]] in May 2014.

===2015–present: ''Honey''===
In October 2015, Katy provided vocals, along with [[Tinie Tempah]] on "[[Turn the Music Louder (Rumble)]]", a re-worked version of British DJ [[KDA (DJ)|KDA]]'s track "Rumble". It topped the UK charts becoming Katy's first UK number one single.

In December 2015, Katy confirmed in a backstage interview at [[Capital (radio network)|Capital FM]]'s [[Jingle Bell Ball]] that her third studio album would be called ''[[Honey (Katy B album)|Honey]]'' and would be released sometime in 2016. It was also confirmed that a track of the same name would feature. The album was released on 22 April 2016, containing 14 songs.<ref>{{cite av media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5PuSExMDU88|title=EXCLUSIVE: Katy B reveals title of brand new album|date=6 December 2015|via=YouTube}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://beatcog.com/katy-b-honey/|title=Katy B "Honey" [Third Album]|publisher=BeatCog|access-date=7 December 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151223044238/http://beatcog.com/katy-b-honey/|archive-date=23 December 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.josepvinaixa.com/blog/katy-b-honey/|title=Ultimate Music - Katy B "Honey" (Official Album Tracklist)|author=<meta itemprop="name" content="josepvinaixa"/>|work=Ultimate Music}}</ref>

==Political views==
In November 2019, along with 34 other musicians, Katy signed a letter endorsing the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] leader [[Jeremy Corbyn]] in the [[2019 United Kingdom general election|2019 UK general election]] with a call to end austerity.<ref name="theguardian1">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2019/nov/25/musicians-backing-jeremy-corbyns-labour|title=Musicians backing Jeremy Corbyn’s Labour|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=25 November 2019|access-date=1 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="theguardian2">{{cite news |last=Gayle|first=Damien|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2019/nov/25/musicians-backing-jeremy-corbyns-labour|title=Stormzy backs Labour in election with call to end austerity|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=25 November 2019|access-date=1 December 2019}}</ref>

==Awards and nominations==
Katy B has received five nominations at the [[MOBO Awards]]. She was nominated for Best Push Act at the [[MTV Europe Music Awards 2011]]. In 2011 she was also nominated for the [[Mercury Prize]]. In 2012 she won a NME Award for Dancefloor Anthem.
