'''Nana Richard Abiona''' (born 2 December 1988),<ref>{{cite web|author=Scott Kerr |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/fuse-odg-mn0002961009/biography |title=Fuse ODG &#124; Biography & History |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref> better known by his [[stage name]] '''Fuse ODG''', is a [[Ghanaians in the United Kingdom|Ghanaian-English]] [[afrobeats]] recording artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.modernghana.com/music/16894/3/chipmunk-fuse-odg-to-thrill-fans-on-dec-27.html |title=Chipmunk, Fuse Odg To Thrill Fans On Dec. 27 |website=Modernghana.com |date=19 December 2011 |access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref> He is best known for his singles "[[Antenna (song)|Antenna]]" and "[[Dangerous Love (song)|Dangerous Love]]", and for featuring on [[Major Lazer]]'s "[[Light It Up (Major Lazer song)|Light It Up (Remix)]]".

==Early life==
Born in [[London]] and raised in [[Ghana]], Fuse returned to London for his secondary education, where he attended [[the Archbishop Lanfranc School]], Croydon. He grew up in [[Mitcham]], [[South London]].<ref name="hangout.altsounds.com">{{cite web|url=http://hangout.altsounds.com/news/158309-dance-craze-antenna-uks-biggest-afrobeats-export.html |title=AltSounds - The Best New Music Videos In The World |work=AltSounds |access-date=7 July 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141104144008/http://hangout.altsounds.com/news/158309-dance-craze-antenna-uks-biggest-afrobeats-export.html |archive-date=4 November 2014  }}</ref> The stage name "Fuse" comes from his fusion of musical styles, as he is known for his unusual combination of genres such as Afro-pop, hip-hop, R&B, funk and rock. "ODG", meanwhile, stands for 'Off Da Ground'.<ref>{{Cite web|title=How Did Fuse ODG Choose His Name?|url=https://www.capitalxtra.com/artists/fuse-odg/lists/facts/stage-name/|access-date=2021-01-10|website=Capital XTRA|language=en}}</ref>

==Music career==
===2013–14: ''T.I.N.A.''===
{{Main|T.I.N.A. (album)}}
On 2 June 2013, Fuse ODG released his debut single "[[Antenna (song)|Antenna]]", the song peaked at number 7 on the [[UK Singles Chart]], the song also peaked at number 85 on the [[Irish Singles Chart]]. On 29 September 2013 he released the single "[[Azonto (Fuse ODG song)|Azonto]]", the song peaked at number 30 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. On 19 October 2013, he was awarded 'Best African Act' at the MOBO Award's 18th Anniversary.<ref>{{cite news|title=Fuse ODG Wins MOBO at the 18th Anniversary Awards|url=http://whatsgoodonline.co.uk/events/mobo-award-winners-2013-announced/|access-date=19 October 2013|newspaper=What's Good? Online|date=19 October 2013}}</ref> He recently won four awards at the Urban Music Awards 2013; Best Music Video, Best Single, Best Artiste and Best Collaboration.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://infolodge.net/blog/blog/2013/11/25/fuse-odg-claims-top-gongs-urban-music-awards-2013/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2 March 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203014220/http://infolodge.net/blog/blog/2013/11/25/fuse-odg-claims-top-gongs-urban-music-awards-2013/ |archive-date=3 December 2013 }}</ref> On 29 December 2013 he released the single "[[Million Pound Girl (Badder Than Bad)]]", the song peaked at number 5 on the [[UK Singles Chart]], the song also peaked at number 65 on the [[Irish Singles Chart]]. On 18 May 2014 Fuse ODG released "[[Dangerous Love (Fuse ODG song)|Dangerous Love]]", featuring [[Sean Paul]]. It peaked at number 3 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. His next single, "[[T.I.N.A. (song)|T.I.N.A.]]" featuring British R&B artist [[Angel (British musician)|Angel]], was released on 19 October 2014. His debut album, ''[[T.I.N.A. (album)|T.I.N.A.]]'', an acronym for "This Is New Africa", was released on 3 November 2014 and includes all five singles, the album peaked to number 25 on the [[UK Albums Chart]], the album also peaked to number 63 in [[Scotland]]. Fuse ODG turned down an offer to sing on the [[Band Aid 30]] project because he objected to the way the lyrics of the song portrayed the victims of the ongoing [[Ebola virus epidemic in West Africa]], and Africa generally.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2014/nov/19/turn-down-band-aid-bob-geldof-africa-fuse-odg |title=Why I had to turn down Band Aid &#124; Fuse ODG &#124; Opinion |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref>

===2015–present===
In April 2015 he featured on [[Tinchy Stryder]]'s single "Imperfection". In July 2015 he released the single "Only".<ref name="iTunesOnly">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/only-single/id1004991324 |title=Only - Single by Fuse ODG on iTunes |website=Itunes.apple.com |date=10 July 2015 |access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref> He features on [[Angel (British musician)|Angel]]'s single "Leyla". The song was released on 17 July 2015.<ref name="iTunesLeyla">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/leyla-feat.-fuse-odg-single/id1003093719 |title=Leyla (feat. Fuse ODG) - Single by Angel on iTunes |website=Itunes.apple.com |date=17 July 2015 |access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref> On 4 September 2015 he released the single "Top of My Charts".<ref name="iTunesTopofMyCharts">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/top-of-my-charts-single/id1018200416 |title=Top of My Charts - Single by Fuse ODG on iTunes |website=Itunes.apple.com |date=4 September 2015 |access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref>

In 2016, he collaborated with British Asian artist [[Zack Knight]] and Indian rapper [[Badshah (rapper)|Badshah]]. They came together and composed an African/Indian soundtrack, ''Bombae''.
