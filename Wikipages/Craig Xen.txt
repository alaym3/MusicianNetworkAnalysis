'''Craig J. Gordwin''' (born October 24, 1994), known professionally as '''Craig Xen''', is an American rapper and songwriter. He is best known for his collaborations with [[XXXTentacion]] and American hip hop collective [[Members Only (hip hop collective)|Members Only]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.xxlmag.com/craig-xen-xxxtentacion-tribute/ | title=Craig Xen's Tribute to XXXTentacion One Year After His Death: "You've Helped So Many People" | work=XXLmag.com | date=June 18, 2019 | access-date=November 3, 2020 | author=Cline, Georgette}}</ref>

==Early life==
Gordwin was born on October 24, 1994 in [[Houston]], Texas. He was raised [[Catholic Church|Catholic]] and frequented a Catholic school until 6th grade.<ref>{{Cite web|date=July 1, 2019|title=Craig Xen|url=https://notion.online/craig-xen/|access-date=August 1, 2020|website=Notion|language=en-GB}}</ref> In an interview with [[Adam22]] in February 2016, he stated that he was raised by his mother while his father spent time in jail and that he started rapping when he was 6 years old with his cousin.<ref name="youtube.com">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=No Jumper - The Craig Xen Interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XrX9pKa4GCg|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=YouTube}}</ref> He grew up listening to [[Screwed Up Click]], [[Green Day]], [[Linkin Park]], [[Eminem]] and [[Lil B]], the last one being one of his biggest musical inspiration.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Meet Craig Xen: A Houston Native with a Different Agenda|url=https://www.socialstatuspgh.com/blogs/social-status/meet-craig-xen-a-houston-native-with-a-different-agenda|access-date=July 19, 2020|website=Social Status|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://datpizz.com/craig-xen-discography/|title=Craig Xen Discography|date=May 18, 2017|website=DatPizz|language=en-US|access-date=March 28, 2020}}</ref> The last name in his stage name, Xen (formely [[Zen]]) came from the time he was in high school when Xen was interested by [[spirituality]], used [[hallucinogen]]s and practised [[meditation]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hastings|first=Ben|date=January 19, 2020|title=Craig Xen — Fall in love with me|url=https://medium.com/@hastingsb97/craig-xen-fall-in-love-with-me-8ccded5d6157|access-date=July 19, 2020|website=Medium|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
Craig Xen released his first song, "H.D.A.B.", in April 2013 on [[SoundCloud]] featuring his cousin Kay Rillo.<ref name="youtube.com"/><ref>{{Citation|title=Craig Xen & Kay Rillo – H.D.A.B.|url=https://genius.com/Craig-xen-and-kay-rillo-hdab-lyrics|language=en|access-date=May 25, 2020}}</ref>  In 2015, before joining Members Only, he was a member of the Schemaposse collective, which included rappers JGRXXN, [[Lil Peep]] and [[Ghostemane]], until their dissolution.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/artists/Schemaposse|title=SCHEMAPOSSE|website=Genius|language=en|access-date=March 28, 2020}}</ref> In February 2016, he released the song "Good Fellas" featuring [[Pouya (rapper)|Pouya]] and Shakewell.<ref>{{Citation|title=Craig Xen (Ft. Pouya & Shakewell) – Good Fellas|url=https://genius.com/Craig-xen-good-fellas-lyrics|language=en|access-date=August 3, 2020}}</ref>

In 2016, Craig Xen joined the hip hop collective [[Members Only (hip hop collective)|Members Only]], which  was originally a duo between [[XXXTentacion]] and [[Ski Mask the Slump God]]. Xen appeared on 4 songs from the mixtape [[Members Only, Vol. 3]], released on June 26, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/04/xxxtentacion-members-only-vol-3-cover/|title=XXXTentacion Reveals 'Members Only Vol. 3' Cover - XXL|last=Weinstein|first=Max|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=March 28, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2017/6/xxxtentacion-ski-mask-the-slump-god-members-only-vol-3-stream|title=XXXTENTACION & Ski Mask the Slump God Share New Project, 'Members Only Vol. 3'|website=HYPEBEAST|access-date=March 28, 2020}}</ref> The hip hop collective's debut album, [[Members Only, Vol. 4]], was released on January 23, 2019 by [[Empire Distribution|Empire]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8494632/xxxtentacion-members-only-vol-4|title=XXXTentacion's Members Only 'Vol. 4' Project: Listen|date=January 23, 2019|website=Billboard|access-date=March 28, 2020}}</ref> The project debuted at number 18 on the US [[Billboard 200]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Billboard 200 Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2019-02-09|website=Billboard|access-date=May 25, 2020}}</ref> and Craig Xen appeared on 8 songs.<ref>{{Citation|title=XXXTENTACION Presents: Members Only, Vol. 4|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/4EeDFhIVRxOg4dx7ozSNXa|language=en|access-date=May 25, 2020}}</ref>
