'''Ruel Vincent van Dijk''' (born 29 October 2002),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ruel-mn0001646575/biography|title=Ruel Biography|publisher=All Music|author=Stephen Thomas Erlewine|access-date=12 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190112195233/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ruel-mn0001646575/biography|archive-date=12 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> known [[mononym]]ously as '''Ruel''', is a British-Australian singer and songwriter from [[Sydney]], best known for his singles "[[Don't Tell Me (Ruel song)|Don't Tell Me]]", "[[Younger (Ruel song)|Younger]]", "As Long As You Care"  and "[[Painkiller (Ruel song)|Painkiller]]". At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2018]], he won the award for [[ARIA Award for Breakthrough Artist – Release|Breakthrough Artist]] for his single "[[Dazed & Confused (Ruel song)|Dazed & Confused]]".

==Early life==
Ruel Vincent van Dijk was born on 29 October 2002, in [[Isleworth]], [[London]], to English mother, Kate Vincent, and Dutch-born and New Zealand-raised father, Ralph van Dijk.<ref name="SMH" >{{cite news|url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/a-ruel-twist-of-fate-and-a-schoolboy-soul-singer-is-on-his-way-20180702-p4zoy2.html|title=A Ruel twist of fate and a schoolboy soul singer is on his way|first=Andrew|last=Drever|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=2 July 2018|access-date=11 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190111175822/https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/a-ruel-twist-of-fate-and-a-schoolboy-soul-singer-is-on-his-way-20180702-p4zoy2.html|archive-date=11 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> His father is the founder of Australian radio agency, ''Eardrum''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://au.linkedin.com/in/ralph-van-dijk-b947257|title=LinkedIn|publisher=LinkedIn|access-date=2019-12-08}}</ref> His family moved to Sydney, Australia in 2006.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/ruel-it-s-weird-to-think-it-is-normal-even-though-i-know-it-isn-t-20190911-p52q8a.html|title=Quiet rooms and coping mechanisms: how Ruel is handling his rise to fame|first=Rod |last=Yates|publisher=The Sydney Morning Herald|date=14 September 2019|access-date=28 May 2020}}</ref>

==Music career==
===2015–2017: Early years===
In 2015, van Dijk's father sent a demo of him singing [[James Bay (singer)|James Bay]]'s "[[Let It Go (James Bay song)|Let It Go]]" to [[Grammy Awards|Grammy Award-winning]] producer [[M-Phazes]].<ref name="SMH"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://themusic.com.au/news/exclusive-go-behind-the-scenes-with-ruel-and-m-phazes-on-their-new-track-dont-tell-me/3f3O8fDz8vU/14-07-17/|title=Exclusive: Go Behind The Scenes With Ruel & M-Phazes On Their New Track, 'Don't Tell Me'|work=[[The Music (magazine)|The Music]]|date=13 July 2017|access-date=2 May 2018}}</ref>

In April 2017, van Dijk released his debut single "[[Golden Years (Ruel and M-Phazes song)|Golden Years]]" with M-Phazes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/entertainment/confidential/and-ruel-releases-debut-single-golden-years/news-story/5c0625e666734efbb32b02cc19828d81|title= Ruel releases debut single Golden Years|publisher=[[Herald Sun]]|date=17 April 2017 |access-date=20 August 2017}}</ref> In June, van Dijk made his live radio debut performing [[Jack Garratt]]'s "Weathered" on [[Triple J]]'s [[Like a Version]]. At 14, he was the youngest-ever featured performer on the segment.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.livenation.co.nz/artist/ruel-tickets|title=Ruel|publisher=Live Nation NZ|access-date=28 May 2020}}</ref> The video gathered half a million video views in less than 48 hours.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.umusicpub.com/au/Artists/R/Ruel.aspx|title=Ruel (artists)|publisher=Universal Music Publishing Group|access-date=2 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171224213443/http://www.umusicpub.com/au/Artists/R/Ruel.aspx|archive-date=24 December 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

In July 2017, van Dijk released "[[Don't Tell Me (Ruel song)|Don't Tell Me]]", which peaked at number 86 on the [[ARIA Chart|ARIA Singles Chart]]. Later in September, [[Elton John]] played "Don't Tell Me" on [[BBC Radio 1]], giving Ruel high praise.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://themusic.com.au/news/all/2017/11/22/elton-john-continues-to-praise-sydney-artist-ruel-you-have-the-most-amazing-voice/|title=Elton John Continues To Praise Sydney Artist Ruel: 'You Have The Most Amazing Voice'|publisher=The Music|date=22 November 2017|access-date=2 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180816185125/http://themusic.com.au/news/all/2017/11/22/elton-john-continues-to-praise-sydney-artist-ruel-you-have-the-most-amazing-voice/|archive-date=16 August 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>  The track was also featured in CelebMix's 21 songs by under-21s in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://celebmix.com/celebmixs-21-songs-by-under-21s/|title=CelebMix's 21 Songs by Under-21s|publisher=CelebMix.com|date=30 November 2017|access-date=7 February 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190209124442/https://celebmix.com/celebmixs-21-songs-by-under-21s/|archive-date=9 February 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

In November, van Dijk joined [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] on his American Teen Tour of Australia and New Zealand.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/oneruel/status/909600804016857088|title=ive been invited to support Khalid on his australia/nz tour! so grateful for this opportunity! [...]|publisher=Twitter|date=18 September 2017|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> Later that month, van Dijk signed with [[RCA Records]] internationally.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rcarecords.com/news/ruel-signs-rca-records/|title=Ruel Signs With RCA Records|publisher=RCA Records|date=27 November 2017|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> In December, Tom Thum released a cover of [[Rag'n'Bone Man]]'s "[[Human (Rag'n'Bone Man song)|Human]]" featuring van Dijk on vocals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/human-feat-ruel/1319641874?i=1319641884|title=Human (featuring Ruel)|publisher=iTunes Australia|access-date=4 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180505135359/https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/human-feat-ruel/1319641874?i=1319641884|archive-date=5 May 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2018–2020: ''Ready'' and ''Free Time'' EPs===
On 24 March 2018, van Dijk played at Pop Spring Festival, [[Tokyo]], where he did his first meet and greet.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/oneruel/status/960037538994962432|title=im going to be playing at POPSPRING FESTIVAL in japan nxt month!!! tickets r now on sale so ill see u guys sooooon|publisher=Twitter|date=4 February 2018|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> On 4 April, van Dijk performed at the [[2018 Commonwealth Games opening ceremony|Opening Ceremony]] of the [[2018 Commonwealth Games]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.news.com.au/sport/commonwealth-games/live-coverage-of-the-2018-commonwealth-games-opening-ceremony-on-the-gold-coast/news-story/d20d3ed254dbef79ad9abaf2e29e244e|title=Gold Coast dazzles on the world stage as Commonwealth Games are officially opened|publisher=[[news.com.au]]|date=5 October 2018|access-date=27 April 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180428021741/http://www.news.com.au/sport/commonwealth-games/live-coverage-of-the-2018-commonwealth-games-opening-ceremony-on-the-gold-coast/news-story/d20d3ed254dbef79ad9abaf2e29e244e|archive-date=28 April 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> On 19 April, a music video for the single "Don't Tell Me" was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eE48SVjGMUc|title=Ruel - Don't Tell Me (Official Video)|via=YouTube|date=19 April 2018|access-date=8 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180611110306/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eE48SVjGMUc|archive-date=11 June 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 2 June, van Dijk announced the release of his debut [[extended play]] ''[[Ready (Ruel EP)|Ready]]'' on 15 June, as well as a headline tour in June and July.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://amnplify.com.au/ruel-reveals-official-video-for-dazed-and-confused/|title=Ruel reveals official video for 'Dazed & Confused' first ever solo headline shows this June and July, pre-order Ruel's debut EP Ready, set for release June 15|publisher=amnplify|date=2 June 2018|access-date=5 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612140128/https://amnplify.com.au/ruel-reveals-official-video-for-dazed-and-confused/|archive-date=12 June 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> In October, van Dijk performed to a sell-out crowd in Sydney, and received the news that his single "[[Younger (Ruel song)|Younger]]" had recently been certified gold by ARIA.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aria.com.au/pages/httpwww.aria.com.aupagesSinglesAccreds2019.htm|title=ARIA Charts - Accreditations - 2019 Singles|publisher=ARIA|date=31 December 2019|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> On 11 October, van Dijk was nominated for the Breakthrough Artist award for the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2018|ARIA Awards]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ariaawards.com.au/nominees/2019/ARIA-Awards/Breakthrough-Artist|title=NOMINEES|publisher=ARIA Awards|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> which he later won, making him the youngest ever to do so.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://icon.ink/articles/ruel-youngest-solo-artist-aria-awards/|title=Ruel: The youngest solo artist ever to win an ARIA|publisher=Icon|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref>

On 10 January 2019, van Dijk confirmed that he would appear on [[Hilltop Hoods]]' new album ''[[The Great Expanse]]'', on the track "Fire & Grace". The album was then released on 22 February.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/hilltop-hoods-new-album-tracklist-illy-ecca-vandal-timberwolf/10705834/ |title=Hilltop Hoods just dropped the tracklist for their feature-packed new album |work=[[Australian Broadcasting Corporation|ABC]] ([[Triple J]]) |date=10 January 2019 |access-date=4 August 2019 |author=Newstead, Al}}</ref> On 1 May, he released the lead single from his ''[[Free Time (EP)|Free Time]]'' EP, "[[Painkiller (Ruel song)|Painkiller]]", alongside an accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://themusicnetwork.com/sotd-ruel-sets-the-tone-for-next-ep-with-painkiller/ |title=SOTD: Ruel sets the tone for next EP with 'Painkiller' |work=[[The Music Network]] |date=1 May 2019 |access-date=4 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190511094203/https://themusicnetwork.com/sotd-ruel-sets-the-tone-for-next-ep-with-painkiller/ |archive-date=11 May 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTwj7PhpY9M|title=Ruel - Painkiller (Official Video)|author=Ruel|date=1 May 2019|access-date=2 May 2019|via=YouTube|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190501234812/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTwj7PhpY9M|archive-date=1 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> On 9 August, van Dijk released the single "[[Face to Face (Ruel song)|Face to Face]]" alongside a music video that was shot whilst on tour in France.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/ruel/15908|title=Ruel drops new single + video for 'Face To Face'.|first=Rose|last=Riddell|publisher=Coup De Main Magazine|date=9 August 2019|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> On 13 September, Ruel released his second EP, ''Free Time''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/australian-pop-sensation-ruel-gets-real-about-music-industry-mental-health-crisis-on-new-free-time-ep/news-story/e3723ca834ef1ee55705a7a6ff9ca874|title=Australian pop sensation Ruel gets real about music industry mental health crisis on new Free Time EP|first=Kathy |last=McCabe|publisher=Billboard|date=12 September 2019|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> In November 2019, van Dijk was an opening act for two shows of [[Shawn Mendes]]' [[Shawn Mendes: The Tour|self-titled tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8504457/shawn-mendes-dan-and-shay-ruel-australia-new-zealand-tour|title=Shawn Mendes Adds Dan + Shay, Ruel as Australia & New Zealand Tour Openers|first=Tallie|last=Spencer|publisher=Billboard|date=27 March 2019|access-date=28 May 2020}}</ref>

On 31 January 2020, van Dijk collaborated with [[Cosmo's Midnight]] on the track "Down for You". The music video for this song premiered on the 18th of March.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/ruel-and-gracey-team-up-for-new-single-empty-love-2673813|title=Cosmo's Midnight and Ruel share 'Down For You' music video|first=Jasper|last=Bruce|publisher=New Musical Express|date=18 March 2020|access-date=28 May 2020}}</ref>

===2020: ''Bright Lights, Red Eyes''===
On 4 September 2020, van Dijk posted a video on [[Instagram]] with the caption "alayc", hinting at new music.<ref name="ALAYC"/> On 10 September 2020, a new single "As Long As You Care" was premiered on [[Triple J]], before releasing later that day, alongside an accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/ruel-announces-new-ep-and-drops-as-long-as-you-care/12648378/ |title=First Spin: Ruel announces new EP and drops 'As Long As You Care' |publisher=[[Triple J]] |date=10 September 2020 |access-date=10 September 2020 |author=Bracken, Claire}}</ref> van Dijk additionally announced his third EP, ''[[Bright Lights, Red Eyes]]'', which was released on 23 October 2020.<ref name="ALAYC"/>
