'''Planetshakers''' is the [[contemporary worship music]] band, a central part of [[Planetshakers Church]] in Melbourne, Australia.

With over 30 internationally acclaimed albums, the band tours annually to the US, UK, Europe, South Africa, Asia, South America, Australia and New Zealand<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.planetshakers.com/music/|title=Planetshakers Music|author1=Planetshakers|date=5 March 2018|website=Planetshakers|access-date=5 March 2018}}</ref> and has seen much success having been nominated for multiple [[GMA Dove Award|Dove Awards]].<ref name="Hutchison">{{cite web | archive-url = http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/151030/20150304-1423/www.abc.net.au/7.30/content/2006/s1825436.htm | url = http://www.abc.net.au/7.30/content/2006/s1825436.htm | title = Praise pit to faith | last = Hutchison | first = Tracee | author-link = Tracee Hutchison | work = [[The 7.30 Report]] | publisher = [[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]] (ABC) | archive-date = 4 March 2015 | date = 11 January 2007 | access-date = 1 July 2015 }}</ref>

== History ==
Formed in Adelaide for the first Planetshakers Conference in 1997, the band has a goal to spread contemporary worship music worldwide. They are now based in [[Melbourne]] at ''Planetshakers Church'', under the leadership of senior pastors [[Russell Evans (pastor)|Russell]] and [[Samantha Evans (Planetshakers singer)|Samantha Evans]].

[[Guy Sebastian]], member of the Planetshakers band from 2002 to 2005 was a [[worship leader]] and background vocals recording albums for [[Planetshakers Church]] at [[Paradise Community Church]] conferences, won went on to win the first ''[[Australian Idol]]'' and become a massive star in the country.<ref name="Lallo">{{cite news | url = http://www.theage.com.au/articles/2007/04/07/1175366538300.html?page=fullpage | title = Young believers pray and sway to a new beat | last = Lallo | first = Michael | work = [[The Age]]  | date = 8 April 2007 | access-date = 1 July 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Paradise Community Church |url=https://www.newreleasetoday.com/artistdetail.php?artist_id=2609 |publisher=New Release Today |access-date=22 August 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://rhemadev.co.za/september/worship.html |title=Rhema on Planetshakers |website=Rhemadev |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080914042424/http://rhemadev.co.za/september/worship.html |archive-date=14 September 2008 }}</ref>

It was reported in 2008 that Michael Guglielmucci, former bass player in the Planetshakers band, had fraudulently claimed he was dying of cancer.<ref>{{Cite news |last = Smith | first=Sharon |title=Chart-topping pastor's cancer lie |pages=1 |location=Adelaide, Australia |date = 29 August 2008 |url = https://au.news.yahoo.com/a/4972420/charttopping-pastors-cancer-lie/|newspaper=7News}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news | last = Wheatley | first = Kim | title = Go to police, church tells lying pastor; praise to the fraud | pages = 1–2 | newspaper = The Advertiser | location = Adelaide, Australia | date = 22 August 2008 }}</ref> During this time Guglielmucci received money from supporters who believed his illness was real.<ref>{{cite news |last=Wheatley |first=Kim |date=11 September 2008 |title=Porn pastor unlikely to face charges &#124; The Advertiser |newspaper=The Advertiser |url=http://www.adelaidenow.com.au/news/south-australia/porn-pastor-unlikely-to-face-charges/story-e6frea83-1111117460461 |access-date=21 February 2014}}</ref> Guglielmucci wrote the "Healer" song for the album "Saviour of the World" was released in June 2007. "Healer" is a song of encouragement for believers who were suffering from cancer.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.theage.com.au/national/thats-sir-conman-to-you-copper-2008s-bizarre-rap-sheet-20081115-67pd.html | location=Melbourne, Australia | newspaper=The Age | title=That's Sir Conman to you, copper: 2008's bizarre rap sheet | first=Mark | last=Russell | date=16 November 2008}}</ref> Guglielmucci explained his actions as being a result of a long-term [[pornography addiction]].<ref>{{cite news |date=28 August 2008 |publisher=ABC News |title=Fake cancer preacher admits porn addiction |url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2008/08/24/2344811.htm |access-date=21 February 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=26 August 2008 |title=Money back pledge from disgraced pastor |work=ABC News |url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2008/08/25/2345377.htm |access-date=21 February 2014 |publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation}}</ref>

== Awards ==

In 2002, the album ''Open Up the Gates'', was nominated for [[Dove Award]] "Worship Album of the Year".<ref>{{cite web |author1=Russ Hutto |title=Review: Nothing Is Impossible by Planetshakers |url=https://www.theworshipcommunity.com/review-nothing-is-impossible-by-planetshakers/ |publisher=The Worship Community |access-date=7 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=846|title=Open Up The Gates|publisher=New Release Today|access-date=27 January 2015}}</ref>

''[[Endless Praise: Live]]'', released in March 2014, reached No. 3 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Heatseekers]] chart<ref>{{cite web|title=Planetshakers Chart history|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/340642/Planetshakers/chart?f=324|website=Billboard|access-date= 22 September 2016}}</ref> and No. 16 in on the ''Billboard'' [[Christian Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Planetshakers Chart history|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/340642/Planetshakers/chart?f=310|website=Billboard|access-date= 22 September 2016}}</ref>
The video of the song "Endless Praise" was nominated for Dove Award Long Form Video of the Year 2014.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|author1=Alekz Aminadab Mundo|title=Se revelaron los nominados de la 45 edición de los Dove Awards.|date = 16 August 2014|url=http://ondaexclusiva.com/se-revelaron-los-nominados-de-la-45-edicion-de-los-dove-awards/|website=Onda Exclusiva|access-date= 22 September 2016|language=es}}</ref>

The album, ''[[Nada Es Imposible]]'', in Spanish which was released in July 2014, reached position No. 17 on the ''Billboard'' "[[Latin Pop Albums]]" chart<ref>{{cite web|title=Planetshakers Chart history|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/latin-pop-albums/2014-07-19|website=Billboard|access-date= 19 June 2017}}</ref> and was nominated by the [[Arpa Awards]] in three categories: best group or duo album, album rock or hard rock and producer of 2014.<ref>{{cite web|author=Premios Arpa|title=XI EDICIÓN PREMIOS ARPA|website=Premios Arpa|publisher=Academia Nacional de la Música y las Artes Cristianas|url=http://premiosarpa.org/2015/11/13/xi-edicion-premios-arpa/|access-date=23 September 2016|language=es}}</ref>

The video of the song "This is Our Time" was nominated for the Dove Award Long Form Video of the Year 2014.<ref>{{cite web|author1=The Christian Beat|title=46th Annual GMA Dove Awards Full Nominations List|date = 12 August 2015|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/763-46th-annual-gma-dove-awards-full-nominations-list|website=The Christian Beat|access-date=7 January 2018}}</ref>

The video of the song "Let's Go" was nominated for the Dove Award Long Form Video of the Year 2015.<ref>{{cite web|author1=GMA DOVE AWARDS|title=LONG FORM VIDEO OF THE YEAR – CAT 43|date=10 November 2015|url=http://doveawards.com/2016-nominees/|website=GMA DOVE AWARDS|access-date=22 September 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161114211659/http://doveawards.com/2016-nominees/|archive-date=14 November 2016}}</ref>

The video of the song "Overflow" was nominated for Dove Award Long Form Video of the Year 2016.<ref>{{cite web|author1=GMA DOVE AWARDS|title=LONG FORM VIDEO OF THE YEAR – CAT 38|date=12 September 2017|url=http://doveawards.com/awards/2017-nominees/|website=GMA DOVE AWARDS|access-date=12 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171225132324/http://doveawards.com/awards/2017-nominees/|archive-date=25 December 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Planetshakers was nominated for the Dove Award in the category: "Spanish Language Album of the Year" for ''[[Sé Quién Eres Tú]]'' (featuring Su Presencia).<ref>{{cite web|author1=GMA DOVE AWARDS|title=SPANISH LANGUAGE ALBUM OF THE YEAR – CAT 28|date=12 September 2017|url=http://doveawards.com/awards/2017-nominees/|website=GMA DOVE AWARDS|access-date=12 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171225132324/http://doveawards.com/awards/2017-nominees/|archive-date=25 December 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Planetshakers (featuring Su Presencia) have been nominated by the Arpa Awards in 2016 in the category: "Best song in participation" "Sé quién eres tú".<ref>{{cite web |author=Premios Arpa |title=Lista Oficial de Nominados XIII Edición Premios ARPA |url=http://premiosarpa.org/2017/07/11/xiii/ |website=Premios Arpa |publisher=Academia Nacional de la Música y las Artes Cristianas |access-date=29 September 2019 |language=es}}</ref>
