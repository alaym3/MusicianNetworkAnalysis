A '''[[municipality]]''' ({{lang-et|omavalitsus}}, plural {{lang|et|omavalitsused}}) is the smallest administrative subdivision of [[Estonia]]. Each municipality is a unit of self-government with its representative and executive bodies. The municipalities in Estonia cover the entire territory of the country.

Municipalities in Estonia are of two types: 
*'''Urban municipalities''' or '''towns''' ({{lang|et|linnad}}, singular {{lang|et|linn}})
*'''Rural municipalities''' or '''[[Parish (administrative division)|parish]]es''' ({{lang|et|vallad}}, singular {{lang|et|vald}}).

There is no other status distinction between them.

Municipalities may contain one or several [[Populated places in Estonia|settlements]]. All urban municipalities plus 6 rural municipalities contain only one settlement. Of the latter six, 5 are so-called "borough-parishes", consisting of one borough, while [[Ruhnu Parish]] consists of one village.

Some municipalities are divided into districts. The 8 urban districts ({{lang|et|linnaosad}}, singular {{lang|et|linnaosa}}) of Tallinn have limited self-government, while other urban districts are formed for administrative purposes. Some rural districts ({{lang|et|osavallad}}) have limited self-government, while other types of rural districts do not.

Municipalities range in population from [[Tallinn]] with 427,500 inhabitants to [[Ruhnu]] with 68.<ref name=einst.ee>{{cite web |url=http://www.einst.ee/factsheets/factsheets_uus_kuju/local_government_reform.htm |title=Archived copy |access-date=2002-06-19 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20020619141032/http://www.einst.ee/factsheets/factsheets_uus_kuju/local_government_reform.htm |archive-date=2002-06-19 }}</ref> Previously, as over two-thirds of the municipalities had a population of under 3,000, many of them found it advantageous to co-operate in providing services and carrying out administrative functions.

Currently, after the [[Administrative reform of Estonia|administrative reform]] was completed in October 2017, there are total of 79 municipalities, 15 of which are urban and 64 rural. 51 of the present municipalities were the result of mergers, 28 remain unchanged. == Structure of local authorities ==
In each municipality there is a [[local government]] as well as a [[Local Government|council]].

The '''council''' ({{lang|et|volikogu}}) is a representative body elected by the residents of a municipality for a term of three years. The members of the council elect a chairman ({{lang|et|volikogu esimees}}), who organises the council's work and represents the municipality.

The '''government''' ({{lang|et|valitsus}}) is an executive body formed by the council. It is headed by a mayor ({{lang|et|linnapea}} in towns, {{lang|et|vallavanem}} in parishes), who is appointed for a four-year term. The mayor cannot be the chairman of the council. Other members of the government are chosen by the mayor with the approval of the council.
