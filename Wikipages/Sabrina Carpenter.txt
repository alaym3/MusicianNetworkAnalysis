'''Sabrina Annlynn Carpenter'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ohmy.disney.com/music/2014/10/13/sabrina-carpenter-takes-the-playlist-pop-quiz/|title=Sabrina Carpenter Takes the Playlist Pop Quiz|work=Oh My Disney|author=Emily Brandon|date=October 13, 2014|access-date=November 10, 2017}}</ref> (born May 11, 1999<ref name=medianet />) is an American singer and actress.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Naoreen|first1=Nuzhat|date=December 26, 2014|title='Girl Meets World' Star Sabrina Carpenter Talks Working With Meghan Trainor and Beyoncé's Decision to Pass on 'All About That Bass'|url=http://blog.peopleschoice.com/2014/12/26/sabrina-carpenter-working-with-meghan-trainor/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160801110718/http://blog.peopleschoice.com/2014/12/26/sabrina-carpenter-working-with-meghan-trainor/|archive-date=August 1, 2016|access-date=January 17, 2016|website=People's Choice}}</ref> Carpenter made her acting debut with an appearance in the crime series ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'' and first received recognition for her recurring role as the young version of Chloe Goodwin in the television series ''[[The Goodwin Games]]''. She then had her breakthrough starring as Maya Hart in the [[Disney Channel]] series ''[[Girl Meets World]]'' from 2014 to 2017.<ref name="ew-20130131">{{cite news|last=Hibberd|first=James|date=January 31, 2013|title='Boy Meets World' spin-off casts Riley's best friend|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|publisher=[[Meredith Corporation]]|url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2013/01/31/boy-meets-world-spin-off-maya/|url-status=live|access-date=February 1, 2013}}</ref> Carpenter has starred in the feature films [[Horns (film)|''Horns'']] (2013) and [[The Hate U Give (film)|''The Hate U Give'']] (2018) and starred as Jenny in the [[Disney Channel Original Movie]] ''[[Adventures in Babysitting (2016 film)|Adventures in Babysitting]]'' (2016). As a voice actress, she had a recurring role as Princess Vivian in the [[Disney Channel]] animated series ''[[Sofia the First]]'' from 2013 to 2018, and she voiced Melissa Chase on the [[Disney XD]] animated series ''[[Milo Murphy's Law]]'' from 2016 to 2019.

In 2014, Carpenter was signed to [[Hollywood Records]],<ref name="ew-201301312">{{cite news|url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2013/01/31/boy-meets-world-spin-off-maya/|title='Boy Meets World' spin-off casts Riley's best friend|last=Hibberd|first=James|date=January 31, 2013|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|access-date=February 1, 2013|publisher=[[Meredith Corporation]]|location=New York city}}</ref> and her debut EP, ''[[Can't Blame a Girl for Trying]]'', and debut [[Can't Blame a Girl for Trying (song)|single of the same name]] were released in the same year. She released her debut album, ''[[Eyes Wide Open (Sabrina Carpenter album)|Eyes Wide Open]]'', in 2015 and has since released another three studio albums: ''[[Evolution (Sabrina Carpenter album)|Evolution]]'' (2016), ''[[Singular: Act I]]'' (2018), and ''[[Singular: Act II]]'' (2019). After departing from Hollywood Records, Carpenter was signed by [[Island Records]], and she released her first song with the label, "[[Skin (Sabrina Carpenter song)|Skin]]", on January 22, 2021.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Aswad|first=Jem|date=January 21, 2021|title=Sabrina Carpenter Addresses 'Drivers License' Drama in New Song, 'Skin'|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|url=https://variety.com/2021/music/news/sabrina-carpenter-drivers-license-olivia-rodrigo-skin-1234889998/amp/|url-status=live|access-date=January 22, 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2011–2012: Career beginnings ===
Carpenter booked her first acting role in 2011, a guest role on the [[NBC]] drama series ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit (season 12)|Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]''. She played a young rape victim interviewed by Detective [[Elliot Stabler]] ([[Christopher Meloni]]).<ref name=themorningcall-2011-01-05 /><ref name=15minutes>{{cite web|title=Interview with Sabrina Carpenter|url=http://www.blogtalkradio.com/15minsoffame/2010/04/23/interview-with-sabrina-carpenter|work=15 Minutes of Fame|publisher=blogtalkradio|access-date=August 25, 2012}}</ref> Around the same time, she performed live on the Chinese television station [[Hunan Broadcasting System]] for the Gold Mango Audience festival. She performed [[Etta James]]' "Something's Got a Hold on Me", in the style of [[Christina Aguilera]] from the movie ''[[Burlesque (2010 American film)|Burlesque]]''.<ref name=themorningcall-2011-01-05 /><ref name="test">{{cite web|date=January 5, 2011|title=Hollywood Bound|url=https://www.nbcphiladelphia.com/local/hollywood_bound_philadelphia/1919908/|url-status=live|access-date=January 24, 2021|website=NBC10 Philadelphia}}</ref>

Less than two years later, Carpenter booked a series recurring role on Fox's ''[[The Goodwin Games]]'' as Young Chloe, a series regular role on Disney Channel pilot ''Gulliver Quinn'', and the ABC pilot ''The Unprofessional''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Carpenter|first=Sabrina|date=March 19, 2012|title=It's official!!! I booked a new ABC pilot called The Unprofessional...|url=https://www.facebook.com/sabrinacarpenter/posts/271799709567545|url-status=live|access-date=July 17, 2019|via=Facebook}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Sabrina Carpenter has been described as a "teen pop" singer<ref>{{cite web|first=James|last=Monger|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/sabrina-carpenter-mn0002814181|title=Sabrina Carpenter Bio, music|acoustic]], and [[Country music|country]] on ''[[Eyes Wide Open (Sabrina Carpenter album)|Eyes Wide Open]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Wass|first=Mike|date=September 22, 2014|title=Sabrina Carpenter's 'The Middle Of Starting Over' Is Completely Irresistible: Watch The Candy-Colored |work=AND Pop|date=October 22, 2014|access-date=November 14, 2018}}</ref> ''[[Singular: Act I]]'' and ''[[Singular: Act II]]'' dived into "more mature" genres and lyrical content including [[dance-pop]], [[Trap music (hip hop)|trap]], [[Hip hop|hip-hop]], and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]].<ref name="itunes3">{{cite
