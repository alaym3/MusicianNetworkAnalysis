'''She Wants Revenge''' was an American [[Rock music|rock]] band, based in [[San Fernando Valley]], California; they were active 2004–2012, and 2015–2020. Personnel were singer/guitarist [[Justin Warfield]] and multi-instrumentalist [[Adam Bravin]]. Their music was influenced by [[post-punk]], [[darkwave]] and [[gothic rock]]. The band has sold more than 300,000 records in the US. 

The group's debut album ''[[She Wants Revenge (album)|She Wants Revenge]]'' was released in early 2006, with three singles ("[[These Things]]", a video featuring [[Shirley Manson]] from [[Garbage (band)|Garbage]], "Out of Control", and "[[Tear You Apart]]" for which the video was directed by [[Joaquin Phoenix]]). The band's second album, ''[[This Is Forever (She Wants Revenge album)|This Is Forever]]'', was released in 2007. The group's third album, entitled ''[[Valleyheart (She Wants Revenge album)|Valleyheart]]'', was released in 2011.

==History==
[[Image:JustinWarfield(by Scott Dudelson).jpg|thumb|210px|left|[[Justin Warfield]] of She Wants Revenge - Live in Concert]]

The band toured extensively through 2006 with [[Depeche Mode]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thetrainingground.blogspot.com/2006/03/she-wants-revenge-depeche-mode-tour.html|title=Blogger|website=thetrainingground.blogspot.com|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref> and [[Placebo (band)|Placebo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.livedaily.com/news/10595.html|title=Placebo to play US shows with She Wants Revenge|access-date=13 September 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071102141129/http://www.livedaily.com/news/10595.html|archive-date=2 November 2007}}</ref>

In 2007, the group was featured on the track "Time" with [[Timbaland]]. In a recent interview, Adam Bravin told [[Suicide Girls]] that She Wants Revenge has a lot of unreleased potential b-sides, like "Love to Sleep", which they would like to share. Adam added that the band would like to tour with their heroes [[The Cure]] and [[Bauhaus (band)|Bauhaus]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://suicidegirls.com/interviews/Adam+Bravin+of+She+Wants+Revenge/|title=SuicideGirls|website=SuicideGirls|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref> and in 2011 they did tour with [[Peter Murphy (musician)|Peter Murphy]], lead singer of Bauhaus, on his "Ninth" tour in the US.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.slicingupeyeballs.com/2011/09/06/peter-murphy-she-wants-revenge-tour-dates/|title=Peter Murphy bringing ‘Ninth’ tour back to U.S. for fall dates with She Wants Revenge|website=[[Slicing Up Eyeballs]]|access-date=12 March 2021}}</ref> 
They were released{{when|date=December 2018}} from their contract with the label Geffen/Interscope, according to a MySpace bulletin. The group formed their own label, Perfect Kiss, formerly an imprint, in 2008.

Their EP, ''Save Your Soul'', was released to [[iTunes]] on May 13, 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://phobos.apple.com/WebObjects/MZStore.woa/wa/viewAlbum?id=279662065|title=Save Your Soul - EP by She Wants Revenge on Apple Music|date=13 May 2008|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref> The EP contained four tracks: "Save Your Soul", "A Hundred Kisses", "Sugar", and "Sleep". The group has formed a fan club and participated in the 2008 [[Nylon magazine|Nylon]] summer tour. Their song "Tear You Apart" appeared in the first season of the TV show ''[[Fringe (TV series)|Fringe]]'' (episode 18, "Midnight"), in the movie ''[[The Number 23]]'' starring [[Jim Carrey]], and in the season 5 première of ''[[American Horror Story: Hotel|American Horror Story]]'' (episode 1, "Checking In").

An EP titled ''[[Up and Down (She Wants Revenge EP)|Up and Down]]'', was released via iTunes on September 22, 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/WebObjects/MZStore.woa/wa/viewAlbum?i=331049683&id=331049447&s=143441&uo=6|title=Up and Down iTunes Download Link|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref> At the end of their tour to promote the release of ''Up and Down'', She Wants Revenge began working on their third [[LP album|studio album]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.facebook.com/group.php?gid=2209077151&ref=ts|title=Security Check Required|website=www.facebook.com|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref> which would revisit the dark roots of their first album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lostinreviews.com/2009/10/swr-bottleneck/|title=Contact Support|website=lostinreviews.com|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref>

A new video titled "[[Take The World]]" was released February 7, 2011, prior to the release of their first single, "Must Be The One". The video was band member Adam Bravin's directorial debut. Filming for "Must Be The One" began on February 8, and was released in March. ''[[Valleyheart (She Wants Revenge album)|Valleyheart]]'', the band's third studio album, was released on May 23, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.facebook.com/OfficialSheWantsRevenge/posts/159342027457503|title=She Wants Revenge|website=www.facebook.com|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/shewantsrevenge|title=JUSTIN and ADAM (@shewantsrevenge) - Twitter|website=twitter.com|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref>

On August 1, 2012, the band announced that they are taking an indefinite hiatus.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.facebook.com/OfficialSheWantsRevenge/posts/453001394730470|title=She Wants Revenge|website=www.facebook.com|access-date=13 September 2017}}</ref>

In 2015, She Wants Revenge gained popularity following "Tear You Apart" appearing in ''[[American Horror Story: Hotel]]''{{'}}s season première. The band announced 10 year anniversary shows throughout California in 2016 and have teased a full-return.{{citation needed|date=December 2018}}

On 22 February 2019, it was announced that She Wants Revenge would be appearing at Infest Festival,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://infestuk.com/|title=Infest UK|website=Infest UK|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-02-22}}</ref> held at the University of Bradford Student Union, West Yorkshire, UK, over the August Bank Holiday Weekend.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://infestuk.com/2019/02/22/infest-2019-she-wants-revenge-confirmed/|title=Infest 2019: She Wants Revenge CONFIRMED!|last=Mark|date=2019-02-22|website=Infest UK|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-02-22}}</ref>

In August 2020, the band announced that they were disbanding.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CDfLRrvn0Bl/|title=Adam & Justin on Instagram: "Thank you all. And a huge, heartfelt thank you to @thomasfroggatt and @scottellis for being such an integral part of SWR since day one. We…"|website=Instagram.com|access-date=4 October 2020}}</ref>
