'''Nicholas Foster Rattigan''' (born August 6, 1992) is an American singer-songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, vocalist, journalist and [[music video director]], originating from [[Reno, Nevada]]. He currently releases music under the moniker '''Current Joys'''. He also is the drummer and lead singer of the [[Independent music|indie]] [[Surf music|surf rock]] band [[Surf Curse]].

As an artist, Rattigan began releasing music as '''The Nicholas Project''' from 2011 to 2012, thereafter releasing music as '''TELE/VISIONS''', releasing his debut album, ''Wild Heart'', on January 2, 2013, as well as all of his music on [[Bandcamp]]. After 2015, Rattigan began performing as Current Joys, with his second studio album ''Me Oh My Mirror'', released on February 16, 2015, reflecting the significant change in his style of music.

He released his third album, ''A Different Age'', on March 2, 2018. His fourth album, ''Voyager'', was released on May 14, 2021.

== Personal life ==
Rattigan was born in [[Henderson, Nevada]], but moved to Reno for education. Early in his career, he moved to [[Brooklyn]], New York. He now currently resides in Los Angeles, California and Dallas, Texas.

According to Rattigan, he taught himself guitar mostly through online [[Tablature|guitar tabs]] for artists such as [[System of a Down]] and [[Flogging Molly]]. He also took drum lessons when he was eight years old.<ref name="James-Wilson 2018">{{Cite news|url=https://issuu.com/forgeartmag/docs/issuu7_small_/67|title=FORGE. Issue 7: Recovery|last=James-Wilson|first=Matthew|date=April 27, 2015|work=FORGE.|access-date=March 4, 2018|publisher=[[issuu]]}}</ref>

Rattigan has revealed that he suffers from a form of [[generalized anxiety disorder]].<ref name="Israelsohn 2018">{{Cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1977239/current-joys-become-the-warm-jets-fear-videos/video/|title=Current Joys – "Become The Warm Jets" & "Fear" Videos|last=Israelsohn|first=Sophie|date=January 8, 2018|work=[[Stereogum]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.ecollegetimes.com/a-q-a-with-current-joys-nick-rattigan|title=A Q & A with Current Joys’ Nick Rattigan|last=Rutherford|first=Madison|date=March 10, 2018|work=College Times|access-date=March 12, 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2011 – 2015: The Nicholas Project, TELE/VISIONS, Franco Years, Devil Worship, and Surf Curse ===
Rattigan started individually writing and releasing music as The Nicholas Project, after an anonymous [[Myspace]] message inspired him to compose music. He self-released a slew of independently-produced recordings on [[Bandcamp]] from November 2011 to October 2012 under the moniker.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thenicholasproject.bandcamp.com/album/the-basement-tapes|title=The Basement Tapes by The Nicholas Project|date=November 14, 2011|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thenicholasproject.bandcamp.com/track/la-dream|title=LA Dream by The Nicholas Project|date=October 11, 2012|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref>

As TELE/VISIONS, Rattigan self-released his debut album, ''Wild Heart'', on January 2, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://televisionsss.bandcamp.com/album/wild-heart|title=Wild Heart by TELE/VISIONS|date=January 2, 2013|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> Citing the name change as being influenced by ''[[Videodrome]]'', Rattigan said to ''FORGE Magazine'' that "I was writing this song and my roommate had given me a drum machine, so I was like "This is perfect! I can start playing live shows!" and they were like "yeah but no one is going to book you with your name as the Nicholas Project" and they were right.<ref name="James-Wilson 2018" />

In 2013, he and classmates Jacob Rubeck, Ciarán Fitzpatrick, Sinéad Kelly & Stephen Cafferky who self-release music under the moniker Gap Girls (Previously Casino Hearts),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://casinohearts.bandcamp.com/|title=Casino Hearts on Bandcamp|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> formed the band [[Surf Curse]]. This [[Surf punk (music genre)]] band would be their second major project after a small project named Buffalo 66.<ref>{{Cite news|last=James-Wilson|first=Matthew|date=July 31, 2017|title=FORGE. Issue 16: Perspective|work=FORGE.|publisher=[[issuu]]|url=https://issuu.com/forgeartmag/docs/issuu16/92|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> Through Big Joy Records, an independent label in Los Angeles, California, they released their debut album, ''Buds'', in June 2013<ref>{{Cite web|title=Buds, by Surf Curse|url=https://surfcurse.bandcamp.com/album/buds|access-date=2020-09-30|website=Surf Curse}}</ref> and the [[extended play]] (EP) ''Sad Boys EP'' in October.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sad Boys EP, by Surf Curse|url=https://surfcurse.bandcamp.com/album/sad-boys-ep|access-date=2020-09-30|website=Surf Curse}}</ref>

Three EPs were released in 2013: ''Young Luv'' in January,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://televisionsss.bandcamp.com/album/young-luv-ep|title=Young Luv EP by TELE/VISIONS|date=January 19, 2013|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> ''No One Will Dance'' in February<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://televisionsss.bandcamp.com/album/no-one-will-dance|title=No One Will Dance by TELE/VISIONS|date=February 13, 2013|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> and ''Neon Gold'' in June.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://televisionsss.bandcamp.com/album/neon-gold|title=Neon Gold by TELE/VISIONS|date=June 23, 2013|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> Also, from May to July 2013, Rattigan wrote for Noisey, a brand under ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/contributor/nick-rattigan|title=Nick Rattigan – Noisey|website=Noisey|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref>

An extended play, ''2013'', was released in January 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://televisionsss.bandcamp.com/album/2013|title=2013 by TELE/VISIONS|date=January 21, 2014|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> ''2008–2010'', a compilation album, was released as the final work under The Nicholas Project in May.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thenicholasproject.bandcamp.com/album/2008-2010|title=2008–2010 by The Nicholas Project|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> A compilation of b-sides and unreleased tracks, ''B-Sides, Rarities and Demos'', was released a month later.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://televisionsss.bandcamp.com/album/b-sides-rarities-and-demos|title=B-Sides, Rarities and Demos by TELE/VISIONS|date=June 5, 2014|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref>

Rattigan wrote for ''[[Impose (magazine)|Impose]]'' as an intern from September to October 2014.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/profile/nick-rattigan|title=Nick Rattigan|work=[[Impose (magazine)|Impose]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref>

Found on his YouTube channel, Rattigan has performed under additional names, [[Franco Years]] and Devil Worship. Both of these projects can be found on Bandcamp.

=== 2015 – present: Current Joys and Danger Collective ===
In February 2015, Rattigan announced that the follow-up to ''Wild Heart'' was almost complete.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/currentjoys/photos/a.791257194228467.1073741829.628160127204842/902966913057494/|title=Current Joys – New album is almost done. Should be out...|date=February 4, 2015|website=Facebook|access-date=March 4, 2018}}{{Primary source inline|date=January 2019}}</ref> The album, ''Me Oh My Mirror'', was then self-released on February 16.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://currentjoys.bandcamp.com/album/me-oh-my-mirror|title=Me Oh My Mirror by Current Joys|date=February 16, 2015|website=[[Bandcamp]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref>

In January 2016, the limited-edition [[zine]] ''Alabama (2000 Light Years)'' was announced for release in February.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/currentjoys/photos/a.654552841232237.1073741828.628160127204842/1076592839028233/|title=Current Joys – Zine release show Feb 13th at The Smell...|date=January 24, 2016|website=Facebook|access-date=March 4, 2018}}{{Primary source inline|date=January 2019}}</ref>

On March 6, 2016, the song "New Flesh" from ''Wild Heart'' was used in a short video titled "Flesh" on [[Vine (service)|Vine]] by Internet celebrity Emma Greer, under her online alias GIBBERTON. The video was her last before her death from cancer twenty-one days later and caused a rise in interest towards the song.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/alyssa6hogan/status/714978164410228736|title=@emmagreerie I want to know the songg|date=March 29, 2016|website=Twitter|access-date=March 4, 2018}}{{Primary source inline|date=January 2019}}</ref> Two days later, Rattigan released a song, "Kids", and announced the name change to Current Joys instead of TELE/VISIONS, stating that "I no longer feel like it represents the music I want to make and there are too many conflicts with the band Television which present problems for future prospects."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/currentjoys/posts/918462294841289|title=Current Joys – Dear close friends and supporters, I have...|date=March 8, 2015|website=Facebook|access-date=March 4, 2018}}{{Primary source inline|date=January 2019}}</ref> To fit with the change, Rattigan re-released all of his music under the moniker on October.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/currentjoys/photos/a.654552841232237.1073741828.628160127204842/1273336362687212/?type=3|title=Current Joys – My entire discography (previously released...|date=October 26, 2016|website=Facebook|access-date=March 4, 2018}}{{Primary source inline|date=January 2019}}</ref>

Rattigan, debuting as a [[music video director]], produced and directed the music video for "123" by the folk punk band [[Girlpool]] in March 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1928100/girlpool-123-video/video/|title=Girlpool – "123" Video|last=Rettig|first=James|date=March 6, 2017|work=[[Stereogum]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.spin.com/2017/03/girlpool-new-album-powerplant-123/|title=New Music: Girlpool Announce New Album Powerplant, Share "123"|last=Cook-Wilson|first=Winston|date=March 6, 2017|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|access-date=March 5, 2018}}</ref> In July, Rattigan released a second limited-edition zine entitled ''Days'', which came with an unreleased five-song EP.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/currentjoys/photos/a.654552841232237.1073741828.628160127204842/1544960932191419/|title=Current Joys – Hey everyone! Sorry I've been quiet on...|date=July 11, 2017|website=Facebook|access-date=March 4, 2018}}{{Primary source inline|date=January 2019}}</ref> He also collaborated with labelmate BOYO for a 4-track EP, ''Split'', released by Danger Collective and Terrible Records on September 6.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://store.dangercollective.com/products/599294-current-joys-boyo-split|title=Current Joys / BOYO – Split|website=Danger Collective|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref>

On January 8, "Become The Warm Jets" and "Fear" were released as the lead singles for ''A Different Age'', his debut album on Danger Collective.<ref name="Israelsohn 2018" /> The music video for "In a Year of 13 Moons", named after a 1978 film by [[Rainer Werner Fassbinder]], was released on January 30 as the third single.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ozs2pnP0gq8|title=Current Joys – In a Year of 13 Moons|date=January 30, 2018|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> In February, the fourth single from the album, "My Nights Are More Beautiful Than Your Days", titled after [[My Nights Are More Beautiful Than Your Days|the film of the same name]], premiered on [[L.A. Record]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://larecord.com/news/2018/02/14/video-premiere-current-joys-my-nights-are-more-beautiful-than-your-days|title=VIDEO PREMIERE: CURRENT JOYS "MY NIGHTS ARE MORE BEAUTIFUL THAN YOUR DAYS"|date=February 14, 2018|work=[[L.A. Record]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref> The album was released on March 2. Within the same month, [[NPR]] included "Fear" in "The Austin 100", a playlist of songs from artists performing at SXSW.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/03/01/588748343/the-austin-100-current-joys|title=The Austin 100: Current Joys|last=Thompson|first=Steven|date=March 1, 2018|publisher=[[NPR]]|access-date=March 4, 2018}}</ref>
