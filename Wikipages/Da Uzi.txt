'''Davy Ngoma Di Malonda''' (born August 28, 1992), better known by his stage name '''Da Uzi''' (sometimes stylized as '''DA Uzi''' and pronounced Dé-A Uzi), and also nicknamed '''Le Libanais''' ({{literally|The Lebanese}}), is a French rapper of Congolese origin ([[Democratic Republic of Congo|DRC]]).<ref name=genius>[https://genius.com/artists/Da-uzi Genius: Da Uzi biography] {{In lang|fr}}</ref>

==Early years==
Davy Ngoma Di Malonda was born 1992 in [[Angers]], [[Pays de la Loire]].
He passed his early years until 11 years old in [[Villemomble]] and [[Villeparisis]] where he played football (soccer) in a local sports team. Then he moved to live with his aunts in a community in [[Seine-et-Marne]], then a year at [[Poitiers]] where he enrolled in a football school. At fifteen, he moved to Trois Tours, [[Sevran]], [[Île-de-France|Paris Region]].<ref name=genius/>

He wanted a sports career playing football, but after an injury, he was dropped for treatment and spent the remaining of his short sports career in third level competition at his club before quitting altogether at 15.<ref name=sofoot>{{cite news|url=https://www.sofoot.com/da-uzi-a-la-cite-mateta-on-l-appelait-ibrahimovic-482497.html|author=Matias Edwards|title=DA Uzi: "A la cité, Mateta, on l'appelait Ibrahimovic"|publisher=sofoot.com|date=21 April 2020|accessdate=17 November 2020|language=fr}}</ref>After that, he started rapping and taking part in rap competitions specially at Villeparisis.

==Career==
His beginnings in rap are still sketchy as he was imprisoned several times. But in 2016, he was noted for several freestyles efforts collected in the self-release ''La D en personne'' in 2017.<ref name=rfi>{{cite news|url=https://musique.rfi.fr/rap/20200512-da-uzi-confessions-enfant-cite|author=Anne-Laure Lemancel|title=DA Uzi, les confessions d’un enfant de la cité|publisher=RFI|date=12 May 2020|accessdate=17 November 2020|language=fr}}</ref> At the end of that year, he was signed to Vrai2Vrai Industry and the rap label Rec 118, an affiliate of [[Warner Music]]. With the signing, he was featured in releases by of Sevran artists, including [[Maes (rapper)|Maes]] and the 13 Block collective. His single with [[Ninho]] titled "Entre les murs" charted peaking at #62 on the [[SNEP]] French singles chart in 2018. 

in 2019, DA Uzi released his mixtape ''Mexico'' that peaked at number 5 on the French Albums Chart in addition to 4 freestyle releases titled ''WeLaRue''. His debut studio album ''Architecte'' was released on 3 April 2020 peaking at #1 in France,<ref name=rfi/> selling 9,700 copies in its first week of release,<ref>[https://www.booska-p.com/new-da-uzi-les-ventes-de-son-album-architecte-n122459.html Booska-P: DA Uzi: Les ventes de son album «Architecte»] {{In lang|fr}}</ref> and eventually more than 50,000 copies and certified gold.  "Crois-moi" featuring is his highest charting single reaching #3 in France.
