'''Kofi Owusu-Ansah''' (born 1998 [[Ghana]]), known by his stage name '''Genesis Owusu''', is a [[Ghanaian people|Ghanaian]]-Australian singer from [[Canberra]]. He is the younger brother of fellow [[Australian hip hop|hip hop]] rapper, Kojo Owusu-Ansah {{abbr|p.k.a.|performer known as}} [[Citizen Kay]]. Owusu's debut studio album, ''[[Smiling with No Teeth]]'' (March 2021), reached the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Albums Chart]] top&nbsp;30. At the [[2021 ARIA Music Awards]] he won four trophies for [[ARIA Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]],  [[ARIA Award for Best Hip Hop Release|Best Hip Hop Release]], [[ARIA Award for Best Independent Release|Best Independent Release]] and [[ARIA Award for Best Cover Art|Best Cover Art]] (latter shared with Bailey Howard).

== Early years ==

Genesis Owusu was born as Kofi Owusu-Ansah on 17 August 1998 in [[Koforidua, Ghana]].<ref name="Martin">{{Cite news |last=Martin, Josh |date=27 January 2021 |title=Genesis Owusu: 'I'd rather be a legend on my own little island than compete for a throne' |work=[[NME]] |url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/genesis-owusu-cover-interview-2021-smiling-with-no-teeth-2866041 |access-date=14 March 2021}}</ref>) His older brother, Kojo (born ca. 1994), is also a rapper who performs as [[Citizen Kay]].<ref name="Bloul">{{Citation | last = Bloul | first = Scherry | date = 12 August 2015 | title = Rapper Genesis Owusu hits right beat to make Triple j Unearthed High top five | periodical = [[The Canberra Times]] | url = https://www.canberratimes.com.au/story/6063002/rapper-genesis-owusu-hits-right-beat-to-make-triple-j-unearthed-high-top-five/ }}</ref> The family migrated to [[Canberra]], Australia in 2000.<ref name='Buckley">{{Citation | last = Buckley | first = Nick | date = 1 March 2019 | title = Genesis Owusu: Black, Red, White and Rap | periodical = Broadsheet | url = https://www.broadsheet.com.au/national/entertainment/article/genesis-owusu-black-red-white-and-rap }}</ref><ref name="Newstead">{{Citation | last = Newstead | first = Al | date = 15 March 2019 | title = First Spin: Genesis Owusu's 'WUTD' & 'Vultures', a tasty double dip of hip hop fusion | publisher = [[Triple J]] | url = https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/genesis-owusu-wutd-vultrues-double-dip-headline-shows/10901814 }}</ref> He was dubbed "Genesis" by Kojo in primary school, who told his friends that his younger brother is Kofi: "his friends were like 'oh, what's your brother's name?' and he said 'Coffee' and they just didn't believe him, for whatever reason. So he just apparently made Genesis up on the spot, and since then it's just stuck."<ref name="Sharma"/> Owusu completed a [[Bachelor of Journalism]] at the [[University of Canberra]] in 2018.<ref name="Sharma">{{cite web | url=http://www.curieux.com.au/music/a-warm-cup-of-kofi-meeting-genesis-owusu/ | title=A warm cup of Kofi: meeting Genesis Owusu | last=Sharma | first=Geeta | website=Curieux Magazine | date=20 February 2018 | via=[[National Library of Australia]] | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20180315183006/http://www.curieux.com.au/music/a-warm-cup-of-kofi-meeting-genesis-owusu/ | archive-date=16 March 2018 | access-date=25 November 2021 }}</ref><ref name="Streader">{{cite journal | url=http://www.beat.com.au/music/rapper-genesis-owusu-personal-political | title=For rapper Genesis Owusu, the personal is political | last=Streader | first=Kate | journal=Beat Magazine | year=2018 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20190319154325/http://www.beat.com.au/music/rapper-genesis-owusu-personal-political | archive-date=20 March 2019 | access-date=25 November 2021 }}</ref> He designs and makes his own clothes and started a fashion line, Pur.<ref name="Sharma"/>

==Career==

===2015–2019: Career beginnings and ''Cardrive'' ===

Owusu is featured on his older brother, [[Citizen Kay]]'s album track, "Family Ties", from ''With the People'' (October 2015).<ref name="Fuamoli">{{cite journal | last = Fuamoli | first = Sosefina | date = 15 October 2015 | title = Citizen Kay takes us through new album With The People track-by-track! | periodical = The AU Review | url = http://music.theaureview.com/features/citizen-kay-takes-us-through-new-album-with-the-people-track-by-track/}}</ref><ref name="NLA Family Ties">{{Citation | author1=Citizen Kay | author-link1=Citizen Kay | title=With the People | year=2015 | publisher=Asphalt Records | url=https://trove.nla.gov.au/work/205744312 | access-date=25 November 2021}}</ref> Kay explained, "[it] is a song about the appreciation of my family and how they've played parts in who I am, both as a person and as a writer/artist. It seemed fitting to get fellow rapping family members on the verses."<ref name="Fuamoli"/> The pair performed and recorded as the Ansah Brothers and in 2015 released an [[extended play]], ''Polaroid''.<ref name="Parnell 2">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/115061/20210227-0000/bmamag.com/2018/02/28/renaissance-men-the-ansah-brothers-answer-the-call/amp/index.html | title=Renaissance Men: The Ansah Brothers Answer the Call | last=Parnell | first=Matt | website=BMA Magazine | date=28 February 2018 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20210228223253/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/115061/20210227-0000/bmamag.com/2018/02/28/renaissance-men-the-ansah-brothers-answer-the-call/amp/index.html | archive-date=1 March 2021 | access-date=25 November 2021 }}</ref>

Fellow Canberra rapper Kirklandd issued a four-track EP, ''The Visions Minitape'' (September 2016), which featured Owusu on the track,<ref name="Staff Writer The Music">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/10761/20170301-0906/themusic.com.au/music/streams/2016/09/21/premiere-kirklandd-the-visions-minitape/index.html | title=Premiere: Kirklandd – ''The Visions Minitape'' | author=staff writer | website=theMusic.com.au | publication-date=21 September 2016 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20170302023118/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/10761/20170301-0906/themusic.com.au/music/streams/2016/09/21/premiere-kirklandd-the-visions-minitape/index.html | archive-date=2 March 2017 | access-date=25 November 2021 }}</ref> "We On", which was issued by Kirklandd as a single in August of that year.<ref name="AirIt We On">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/163090/20180608-0147/airit.org.au/Kirklandd-We-On-feat.-Genesis-Owusu-Internet-Download.html | title=Hip Hop :: Kirklandd – 'We On (feat. Genesis Owusu)' – Internet Download | website=Amrap's AirIt | year=2018 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20180608153752/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/163090/20180608-0147/airit.org.au/Kirklandd-We-On-feat.-Genesis-Owusu-Internet-Download.html | archive-date=9 June 2018 | access-date=25 November 2021 }}</ref> Owusu's seven-track debut EP, ''Cardrive'', was issued in June 2017.<ref name="Parnell">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/115061/20180427-0000/bmamag.com/2017/07/13/genesis-owusu-cardrive-ep/amp/index.html | title=Genesis Owusu – ''Cardrive'' EP | last=Parnell | first=Matt | website=BMA Magazine | date=13 July 2017 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20180426153301/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/115061/20180427-0000/bmamag.com/2017/07/13/genesis-owusu-cardrive-ep/amp/index.html | archive-date=27 April 2018 | access-date=25 November 2021 }}</ref> It was described by ''BMA Magazine''{{'}}s Matt Parnell, "the throaty, deep voice, the swagger-infused flow and menacing, jazzy beats give each track a distinctive flavour. Additionally, the polished sound and confidence with which each tune is presented gives the sound an experienced feeling that belies the age of the artist."<ref name="Parnell"/> The single, "Sideways", followed in September and includes lyrics in the Ghanaian dialect, [[Twi]].<ref name="Sharma"/> It received national exposure on youth radio, [[Triple J]] and toured Australia's east coast to promote it.<ref name="Hellyer">{{cite journal | last = Hellyer | first = Isabelle | date = 27 October 2017 | title = genesis owusu: rapper, meme admin, aspiring stock photo model | periodical =i-D | url = https://i-d.vice.com/en_au/article/wjgdyw/genesis-owusu-rapper-meme-admin-aspiring-stock-photo-model | access-date=25 November 2021 }}</ref> ''i-D''{{'}}s Isabelle Hellyer felt, "Where [Owusu's] older work would flit between jazz and hip hop, 'Sideways' synthesises both genres into sound of his own."<ref name="Hellyer"/>

The singer rejected the archetypical objectification of women by hip hop artists with his second single, "Awomen Amen" (2018), and explained, "As a rapper, I never used to hear those kinds of messages... it was something that always bothered me."<ref name="Streader"/> He released a double A-sided single, "WUTD"/"Vultures" early in 2019, which Caitlin Medcalf of ''Purple Sneakers'' reviewed, "[WUTD is] a smooth, RnB track I guarantee will never get out of your head and, 'Vultures', a wavy rap journey... [his] vocals match the mood with a drawling vocal melody and a light flow that never once feels sharp, but simply goes with the flow."<ref name="Medcalf">{{cite web | url=http://www.purplesneakers.com.au/2019/03/missed-calls-genesis-owusu-leftprojects/ | title=Missed Calls: Genesis Owusu, Leftprojects & more | last=Medcalf | first=Caitlin | website=Purple Sneakers | date=March 2019 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20190409064215/http://www.purplesneakers.com.au/2019/03/missed-calls-genesis-owusu-leftprojects/ | archive-date=9 April 2019 | access-date=25 November 2021 }}</ref> On 3 July 2019 Owusu performed as an opening act at [[5 Seconds of Summer]]'s sold-out [[benefit concert]] in Sydney.<ref>{{Cite web |title=5 Seconds of Summer to Play Special Benefit Gig in Sydney |url=https://themusic.com.au/news/5-seconds-of-summer-and-amy-shark-to-play-special-benefit-gig-in-sydney-next-week/DS8VAQADAgU/27-06-19/ |access-date=16 July 2020 |website=The Music }}</ref> "WUTD" was featured on an ad for [[Bose headphones|Bose]] noise-cancelling headphones in October 2019.<ref>{{Citation | title = Bose & Alexa - Bose Noise Cancelling Headphones 700 {{!}} Haircut | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wYrnkafl6Es | url-access=registration }}</ref>

===2020–present: ''Smiling with No Teeth''===

{{Main|Smiling with No Teeth}}
Owusu's debut studio album, ''[[Smiling with No Teeth]]'', was released on 5 March 2021. The album peaked at number 27 on the ARIA charts. In July, a deluxe version was released titled ''Missing Molars (SWNT Deluxe)''.<ref name="MM Deluxe">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/genesis-owusu-announces-smiling-with-no-teeth-deluxe-album-2993177|title=Genesis Owusu releases ''Smiling With No Teeth'' deluxe album|website=[[NME Australia]]|date= 14 July 2021|access-date=15 July 2021|author=Triscari, Caleb}}</ref>
