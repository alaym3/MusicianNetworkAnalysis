'''Durand Bernarr''' is an American singer-songwriter and producer. He frequently provides background vocals for [[Neo soul|neo-soul]] artist [[Erykah Badu]] and featured vocals for other artists such as [[Anderson .Paak]], [[Kaytranada]], and [[The Internet (band)|The Internet]]. In 2019, Bernarr was a runner-up on the [[BET]] reality music competition ''The Next Big Thing''. He released his latest album ''DUR&'' in 2020.<ref name="billboard2019-4artists" /><ref name="loverboymag">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2020|title=Durand Bernarr: 'I don't look at music like a competition, I'm doing me.'|url=https://www.loverboymagazine.com/durand-bernarr/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-10-14|website=Loverboy Magazine|language=en-GB}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Bernarr was born and raised in [[Cleveland]], Ohio.<ref name=out2019lenawaith>{{Cite web|date=2019-08-12|title=Lena Waithe and AT&T Debut 4 Artists You Should Know|url=https://www.out.com/music/2019/8/12/lena-waithe-and-att-debut-4-artists-you-should-know|access-date=2020-10-14|website=www.out.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Rodriguez|first=Anthony|date=2016-10-12|title=Durand Bernarr Comes Back 'Around' {{!}} SoulBounce {{!}} SoulBounce|url=http://www.soulbounce.com/2016/10/durand-bernarr-comes-back-around/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-10-14|website=www.soulbounce.com|language=en-US}}</ref> He was raised an only child by his mother, a professional music teacher and vocal coach, and his father, an audio engineer who worked with artists like [[Jay-Z]], [[Beyoncé|Beyonce]], [[Rihanna]], [[Jill Scott]], and [[Whitney Houston]]. At 16, Bernarr accompanied his father on tour for [[Earth, Wind & Fire]] as a production assistant.<ref name="numero2020" /> He attended church where his mother was the music director and his interest in music was further nurtured by the gospel music he heard there.<ref name="out2019">{{cite web|last1=Street|first1=Mikelle|date=2019-10-30|title=This Viral Singer Turns Gospel Into Bops About Fuckboys and Weed|url=https://www.out.com/music/2019/10/30/viral-singer-turns-gospel-bops-about-fuckboys-and-weed|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|accessdate=12 October 2020|website=Out}}</ref> He cites [[Erykah Badu]], [[Rick James]] and [[Little Richard]] as his biggest influences.<ref name="loverboymag" />

== Career ==
In 2008, Bernarr began to grow his digital fanbase by posting [[YouTube]] videos that incorporated singing, dancing, and comedic commentary. He also covered popular songs by artists like [[Kanye West]], [[Amy Winehouse]], and [[Gnarls Barkley]].<ref name=numero2020>{{Cite web|last=Aubry|first=Lucas|date=2020-09-25|title=Durand Bernarr, le phénomène repéré par Erykah Badu sur Twitter|url=https://www.numero.com/en/node/16884|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-11-27|website=Numero.com|language=en}}</ref> In 2009, he released his first mixtape titled ''alcoholharmony: The MixT@pe'' consisting of studio versions of his popular covers from YouTube and original content via [[Bandcamp]].<ref name=":0" />

In 2010, Bernarr released his second EP ''8ight: The Stepson of [[Erykah Badu]]'', a compilation of Badu covers and medleys.<ref name="billboard2019">{{cite web |last1=Francis |first1=Glendon |title=9 LGBTQ Artists to Discover During Black History Month |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8499302/lgbtq-singers-black-history-month |website=Billboard |accessdate=12 October 2020}}</ref><ref name="numero2020" /> Shortly after, Badu messaged him through Twitter and, having heard ''8ight'', she hired him as a background vocalist in her band Nedda Stella in 2011.<ref name="numero2020" /> He has toured with her regularly in the years since, as recently as 2020.<ref name="loverboymag" />

Bernarr collaborates with R&B and soul acts as a background or a featured vocalist. He has collaborated and performed with [[Ari Lennox]], [[Kaytranada]], [[The Foreign Exchange]], [[The Internet (band)|The Internet]], [[Sam Sparro]], [[Thundercat (musician)|Thundercat]], [[Knxwledge]], [[Qveen Herby]], and [[Teedra Moses]].<ref name="out2019" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Durand Bernarr Is Soul Music's Best Kept Secret|url=https://www.soundoflife.com/blogs/people/durand-bernarr-is-soul-music-s-best-kept-secret|access-date=2020-10-14|website=Sound of Life}}</ref>

Bernarr competed on [[BET]]'s 2019 reality music competition series ''The Next Big Thing''.<ref name="billboard2019-4artists">{{cite web |last1=Lynch |first1=Joe |title=4 Rising Artists You Should Know in 2019 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/partner/8526749/4-rising-artists-you-should-know-in-2019 |website=Billboard |accessdate=12 October 2020}}</ref> He both rapped and sang, and eventually finished as one of the top three performers.<ref name="out2019" />

He released the album ''DUR&'' (pronounced Durand) in September 2020.<ref name="essence2020">{{cite web|last1=Clark|first1=Kevin L.|title=Kaash Paige Introduces Essence's Official Cuffing SZN Opener Playlist|url=https://www.essence.com/entertainment/kaash-paige-official-cuffing-season-playlist/|accessdate=12 October 2020|website=Essence}}</ref> In a review, Lucas Aubry of ''[[Numéro]]'' stated that the album "once again shows his incredible ability to go up in the highs like [[D'Angelo|D’Angelo]] and down into the low and groovy frequencies of [[Nate Dogg]] in numerous rapped moments."<ref name="numero2020" />

== Personal life ==
Bernarr resides in [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="loverboymag" /> He identifies as [[queer]].<ref name="billboard2019" />
