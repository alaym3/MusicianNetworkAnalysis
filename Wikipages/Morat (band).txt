'''Morat''' is a Colombian band formed in the country's capital city, [[Bogotá]]. The band is formed by Juan Pablo Isaza (guitars and vocals), Juan Pablo Villamil (banjo and vocals) and the brothers Simón Vargas (bass and choruses) and Martín Vargas (percussion and choirs). Previously, until the end of 2016, drummer Alejandro Posada had been in the band, but the percussionist had to leave the project, being subsequently replaced by Martín Vargas.

== History ==

=== Formation ===
The members of Morat have known each other since they were five years old.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.elconfidencial.com/cultura/2017-11-23/musica-morat-pelotazo-pop_1479418/ |title=Morat, el pelotazo que vu of Los Andes}}</ref> They started playing together several times and, when they finished their formation, they decided to create a band. Some of their musical influences are [[Joaquín Sabina]], [[Eric Clapton]] and [[Bacilos]].

The band's original name was "Malta". The name was inspired by a pet owned by Isaza. They switched to "Morat" because it was the surname of an ancestor of Alejandro Posada, former member of the band. The name was also inspired by the farm where the band made their first rehearsals which was named "La Morat":<ref>{{Cite journal |url=https://us.hola.com/actualidad/2016062386665/morat-detalles/ |title=Morat, así es la banda colombiana que ha puesto 'patas arriba' el verano |date=23 June 2016 |magazine=[[Hola!]] |language=es |access-date=14 April 2019}}</ref>

=== 2014-2017: ''Sobre el amor y sus efectos secundarios'' ===
Morat saw their first turning point when the singer [[Paulina Rubio]] showed interests in their composition "Mi Nuevo Vicio" and wanted them to turn it into a single.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/musica/20161004/41752187712/morat-paulina-rubio-entrevista.html|title="Cedimos ‘Mi nuevo vicio’ a Paulina porque sabíamos que haríamos temas mejores"|date=4 October 2016|publisher=La Vanguardia}}</ref> In 2015, "[[Mi Nuevo Vicio]]" was released under the collaboration of [[Paulina Rubio]] and Morat, achieving great success internationally. The single rose to No.12 on the Billboard Latin Pop Airplay chart, and reached No.1 in Spain.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7378272/morat-band-on-the-rise|title=Meet Morat, the Colombian Pop Band Who Hit the Charts With Paulina Rubio|publisher=Billboard|date=20 May 2016}}</ref> The single was also certified Platinum Record.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telecinco.es/quetiempotanfeliz/actuaciones/Terelu-entrego-Platino-Paulina-Rubio_2_2006580078.html |title=Terelu entregó el disco de Platino a Paulina Rubio por sus ventas de ‘Mi nuevo vicio’ |date=20 June 2015 |access-date=14 April 2019 |work=[[Telecinco]] |publisher=[[Mediaset España]]}}</ref>

At the end of 2015, they received another international success with the new single, "Cómo te atreves", reaching the peak position in [[iTunes]] and bringing them to fame in Spain. In June 2016, Morat released their first studio album ''[[Sobre el amor y sus efectos secundarios]]'' through [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Spain]]. The album debuted No.1 in several Hispanic countries such as Colombia, Mexico or Spain. Within this album, Morat obtained 4x Platinum Record as well as a Latin Grammy award nomination for "Cómo te atreves", and Golden Record for "Cuánto me duele".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.europapress.es/andalucia/noticia-morat-presentara-disco-amor-efectos-secundarios-marzo-granada-20170130125437.html|title=Morat presentará su disco 'Sobre el amor y sus efectos secundarios' el 9 de marzo en Granada|publisher=[[Europa Press]]|date=30 January 2017}}</ref>

In 2017, Morat produced the single "[[Yo contigo, tú conmigo (The Gong Gong Song)|Yo Contigo, Tú Conmigo]]" in collaboration with [[Álvaro Soler]] as the theme music of [[Despicable Me 3]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rlm.es/2017/06/12/morat-y-alvaro-soler-ponen-musica-a-gru-3-mi-villano-favorito-a-la-venta-el-16-de-junio/|title=MORAT Y ÁLVARO SOLER PONEN MÚSICA A ‘GRU 3. MI VILLANO FAVORITO’, A LA VENTA EL 16 DE JUNIO|date=12 June 2017}}</ref> The single peaked at No.9 on Billboard on August 19, 2017<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/morat/chart-history/italy-digital-song-sales/song/1032342|title=Chart History Morat "Yo Contigo, Tú Conmigo"}}</ref> and won the "Song of the Year" award in [[LOS40 Music Awards 2017]].<ref name="Los40">{{cite news|url=https://los40.com/los40/2017/11/14/videos/1510672836_079372.html|title=‘YO CONTIGO, TÚ CONMIGO’ EN DIRECTO EN LOS40 MUSIC AWARDS|publisher=Los40}}</ref> This single was later included in the re-issue of ''Sobre el amor y sus efectos secundarios'', which also contains the acoustic version of "Cómo te atreves".

=== 2017-2019: ''Balas perdidas'' ===

In 2018, Morat released their second studio album the single ''[[Balas perdidas]]''. The single "Cuando nadie ve" peaked at No.1 on Billboard on August 18, 2018,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/morat/chart-history/MPE|title=Chart History Morat|publisher=Billboard|access-date=5 April 2020}}</ref> and the single "No se va", released on March 7, 2019, hit almost 2&nbsp;million visits on YouTube in less than a week.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://los40.com/los40/2019/03/11/videoclips/1552322418_638553.html|title=MORAT PRESENTAN EL VIDEOCLIP DE ‘NO SE VA’|date=11 March 2019}}</ref>

Equipped with these success, Morat kicked off their first-ever US tour titled "Balas perdidas" to promote their album in 2019, with first performance in San Francisco on April 4, 2019. The tour ended in Orlando on May 5, 2019, with performance in other cities such as Los Angeles, Chicago, and New York.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8505377/morat-balas-perdidas-tour-interview|title=Morat Ready to Bring Their Colombian Flavor to the U.S. With Balas Perdidas Tour|publisher=Billboard|date=2 April 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://seitrackus.com/portal/en/morat-inicia-su-tan-esperada-gira-balasperdidasus-tour/|title=Morat kicked off their "Balas Perdidas US Tour"|publisher=Seitrack US|date=5 April 2019}}</ref>

In April, 2019, Morat released a song collaborated with [[Aitana (singer)|Aitana]] named "[[Presiento]]".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/musica/20190414/461639511735/aitana-morat-presiento-proyecto-video-seo-ext.html |title=Aitana colabora con el grupo Morat en ‘Presiento’ |date=14 April 2019 |access-date=14 April 2019 |newspaper=[[La Vanguardia]] |language=es}}</ref> This single received more than 600,000 streams in [[Spotify]] in its first 24 hours. After that period of time, "Presiento" became the song with the highest debut in Spain, with more than 230,000 streams there, beating [[Rosalía (singer)|Rosalía]] and [[J Balvin]]'s 2019 track "[[Con altura]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vinilonegro.com/2019/04/presiento-de-morat-y-aitana-mejor-debut-del-ano-en-spotify-espana/|title=‘Presiento’ de Morat y Aitana, mejor debut del año en Spotify España|date=2019-04-13|website=Vinilo Negro|language=es}}</ref> "Presiento" was later included in the re-issue of Balas perdidas, ''Balas perdidas (Edición especial)'', on May 10, 2019 together with three other songs.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://elrescatemusical.com/morat-balas-perdidas-edicion-especial/|title=Morat presenta ‘Balas perdidas: Edición especial’|publisher=El Rescate Musical|author=Sandra Pérez Abad|date=10 May 2019}}</ref>

On December 15, 2019, Morat ended their Spain tour with the last performance at the [[Palacio de Deportes de la Comunidad de Madrid|WiZink Center]] in Madrid. [[Antonio José Sánchez Mazuecos|Antonio José]], [[Aitana (singer)|Aitana]], [[Cali y El Dandee]], and [[Camila Anastasia Gallardo Montalva|Cami]] were also invited to perform at this concert.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://okdiario.com/happyfm/morat-cierra-gira-balas-perdidas-madrid-anuncia-vuelta-2020-4939655|title=Morat cierra su gira ‘Balas Perdidas’ en Madrid y anuncia su vuelta en 2020|publisher=HappyFM|date=16 December 2019}}</ref> Up to this show, Morat has held more than 80 concerts in 13 different countries.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://los40.com/los40/2019/12/16/musica/1576464137_392759.html|title=MADRID SE RINDE ANTE MORAT EN SU ÚLTIMA "BALA PERDIDA"|date=17 December 2019}}</ref>

=== 2019-present: ¿A Dónde Vamos?===

On December 13, 2019, Morat released a new song "Enamórate de alguien más".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://laverdadnoticias.com/espectaculos/Morat-presenta-su-nueva-musica-para-cerrar-el-ano-Enamorate-de-alguien-mas-20191216-0281.html|title=Morat presenta su nueva música para cerrar el año "Enamórate de alguien más"|date=16 December 2019}}</ref>

On March 6, 2020, Morat released "No Termino", the third preview of their third album and the first of 2020. On April 2, "Nunca te olvide", the fourth single from the third album, was released, the video clip of the song premiered on May 1, which features some of the band’s followers. On May 22, "Bajo la mesa", along with [[Sebastián Yatra]], was released, the fifth single from the third album and the first one that includes collaboration. On June 11, their collaboration with [[Reik]] came out with their song "La Bella y la Bestia." On July 30 they released "Más de lo que aposté", their second collaboration with [[Aitana]], and on August 6, "Labios Rotos", which is a tribute to the Mexican band [[Zoé (band)]].

On November 7, and after more than 5 months without playing on stage, they offered a virtual concert through the Rappi Live Events platform called "Echando un cuento." According to the app, it was the best-selling virtual concert on the continent in 2020.

On November 13, "Al aire" was released, the sixth single from their third album, with its respective video clip, which was recorded in the municipality of Guasca, Cundinamarca.

On March 11, 2021, they released the single "No hay más que hablar". A week later, they released the single "De cero".

On April 15, they collaborate with [[Beret]] on the song, "Porfa no te vayas".

On June 3, 2021, they released a collaboration with [[Danna Paola]].
On July 15, 2021, Morat released their third studio album, "¿A Dónde Vamos?". That same day they released the single "Simplemente Pasan", along with Cami.

== Personal life ==
On March 27, 2020, Morat posted an announcement as well as a three-minute-long video on Instagram, saying that one of its members, Juan Pablo Villamil, had tested positive for coronavirus ([[COVID-19]]) after traveling to Spain and Mexico. In the video, the band explained that they were visiting Spain and Mexico two weeks ago and as soon as they arrived in Bogota, Colombia, they took safety precautions to self-quarantine.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/9346182/morat-juan-pablo-villamil-says-he-tested-positive-coronavirus|title=Morat Bandmember Says He Tested Positive For Coronavirus After Visiting Spain and Mexico|date=30 March 2020}}</ref>
