'''Snail Mail''' is the American [[indie rock]] solo project of guitarist and singer-songwriter '''Lindsey Jordan'''.<ref name="thefader">{{cite web|last1=McDermott|first1=Patrick|title=The Old-School Beauty Of Snail Mail’s Suburban Slowcore|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/06/28/snail-mail-lindsey-jordan-interview-thinning-habit|work=The Fader|date=28 June 2017|accessdate=23 December 2017}}</ref> The band started playing songs live in 2015 and released the EP [[Habit (EP)|''Habit'']] in 2016.<ref name="stereogum">{{cite web|last1=Claymore|first1=Gabriela|title=At SXSW 2017, Everyone’s Talking About Snail Mail|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1930131/at-sxsw-2017-everyones-talking-about-snail-mail/concert/|work=[[Stereogum]]|date=16 March 2017|accessdate=23 December 2017}}</ref><ref name="pitchfork1">{{cite web|last1=Pelly|first1=Jenn|title=Snail Mail’s Lindsey Jordan Is the Wisest Teenage Indie Rocker We Know|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/10027-snail-mails-lindsey-jordan-is-the-wisest-teenage-indie-rocker-we-know/|work=Pitchfork|date=1 March 2017|accessdate=23 December 2017}}</ref> Snail Mail's debut studio album, ''[[Lush (Snail Mail album)|Lush]]'', was released on June 8, 2018, via [[Matador Records]] and was nominated for Breakthrough Artist/Release and Best Rock Album at the 2019 [[Libera Awards]].

==History==
Lindsey Jordan released her self-recorded solo EP ''Sticki'' in 2015 and played several live shows with her new band as Snail Mail.<ref name="baltimorecitypaper3">{{cite web|url=http://www.citypaper.com/music/music-features/bcp-051717-music-snail-mail-20170516-story.html|title=Slow and Steady: Snail Mail is making moves|last1=Callahan|first1=James|date=16 May 2017|work=[[Baltimore City Paper]]|accessdate=23 December 2017}}</ref> She was joined by Ryan Vieira playing bass and Shawn Durham on the drums.<ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web|last1=Moreland|first1=Quinn|title=Snail Mail: Habit EP|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/snail-mail-habit-ep/|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=27 July 2017|accessdate=23 December 2017}}</ref> After completing one short DIY tour in 2016, the band released the EP ''Habit'' on [[Sister Polygon Records]], which gained traction after being featured on several major US music sites, and signed with Ground Control Touring.<ref name="pitchfork" /> Pitchfork added the EP's opening track, "Thinning", to their ''Best New Track'' series.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/18720-snail-mail-thinning/|title="Thinning" by Snail Mail|last=Mashurova|first=Nina|date=|website=Pitchfork|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>

Snail Mail, now with bassist Alex Bass and drummer Ray Brown, toured North America extensively in 2017 supporting [[Priests (band)|Priests]], [[Girlpool]], [[Waxahatchee]] and [[Beach Fossils]].<ref [[Matador Records]] in September 2017 and played her first headlining tour with Snail Mail in the beginning of 2018.<ref name=":0" />  Snail Mail's debut full-length album ''Lush'' was released on June 8, 2018, to generally positive reviews from music critics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2018/02/13/stand-and-deliver-snail-mail-interview-neu-2018|title=Stand and deliver: Snail web|url=http://matablog.matadorrecords.com/2018/03/21/coming-june-8-snail-mail-lush/|title=Coming June 8 : Snail Mail – ‘Lush’|date=2018-03-22}}</ref> In 2019, the band toured New Zealand, Australia, Canada, and the US, playing with artists such as [[Mac DeMarco|Mac Demarco]] and [[Thundercat (musician)|Thundercat]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-10-08|title=Review – Mac DeMarco Sipped On Red Rocks Like A Tasty Domestic Beer|url=https://303magazine.com/2019/10/review-mac-demarco-sipped-on-red-rocks-like-a-tasty-domestic-beer/|access-date=2021-04-02|website=303 On September 15, 2021, Snail Mail announced their second album, ''[[Valentine (Snail Mail album)|Valentine]].'' The album was released on November 5, 2021, receiving positive reviews from critics.

== Critical reception ==
Snail Mail has been well received by critics. Their sound has been described as reminiscent of the '90s sound, "refiguring music from the decade they were born".<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-album-reviews/review-snail-mails-lush-is-the-work-of-an-indie-rock-prodigy-628936/|title=Review: Snail Mail’s ‘Lush’ Is The Work of An Indie-Rock Prodigy|last=Grow|first=Kory|date=2018-06-08|website=Rolling Stone Magazine|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2018-09-05}}</ref> The band’s influences include [[Avril Lavigne]],<ref>{{cite web |last1= Havens |first1= Lindsey |title= Not So 'Complicated': How Avril Lavigne Became an Unlikely Inspiration To Indie Rock's New Wave |url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8301763/avril-lavigne-unlikely-inspiration-indie-rock-soccer-mommy-snail-mail |website= Billboard |accessdate= August 11, 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180812021906/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8301763/avril-lavigne-unlikely-inspiration-indie-rock-soccer-mommy-snail-mail |archive-date= August 12, 2018 |url-status= live }}</ref> [[My Bloody Valentine (band)|My Bloody Valentine]], [[Sonic Youth]], [[Elliott Smith]], [[Liz Phair]], [[Cat Power]], [[The Cranberries]], [[Pavement (band)|Pavement]], [[The Velvet Underground]], [[Paramore]], and  [[Fiona Apple]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/snail-mail-lush/|title=Snail Mail: Lush Album Review {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2018-09-06}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=KEXP|title=Snail Mail - Full Performance (Live on KEXP)|date=2018-09-04|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8DEFQ5hVnpU|access-date=2018-09-06}}</ref> [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] described Snail Mail's lyrics as "emotionally wise, musically clear, and encompasses the once and future sound of indie rock".<ref name=":2" /> ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine gave Snail Mail's debut album ''[[Lush (Snail Mail album)|Lush]]'' three and a half stars and described the potential of Lindsey Jordan and her music as "the work of an indie-rock prodigy".<ref name=":1" />

== Personal life ==
Jordan is openly gay.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/snail-mail-on-heartbreak-queerness-and-her-debut-downunder/MSDDJTAPVHETRM7VRCZKJ5H7KA/ | title=Snail Mail on heartbreak, queerness, and her debut Downunder|last=Fenwick|first=George|date=2019-03-06|newspaper=[[New Zealand Herald]]|access-date=2021-11-11}}</ref> As of September 2021, she lives in [[East Village, Manhattan]], in [[New York City]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/interview/snail-mail-valentine-interview/|title=Snail Mail Can't Help But Confess|last=Moreland|first=Quinn|date=2021-09-21|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|access-date=2021-11-11}}</ref>
