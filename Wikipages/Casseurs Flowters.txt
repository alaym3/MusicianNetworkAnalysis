'''Casseurs Flowters''' ({{IPA-fr|kɑˈsœʁ floˈtœʁ}}) is a French [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] duo established in 2004 by rappers [[Orelsan]] and [[Gringe]]. The duo's name is a reference to the two "Wet Bandits" from the movie ''[[Home Alone]]'', called the ''"Casseurs Flotteurs"'' in the French dialogue.<ref name=musiquemag>{{cite news|url=http://musique.jeuxactu.com/news-orelsan-les-casseurs-flowteurs-vont-sortir-un-album-10860.htm|title=Orelsan va sortir un album des Casseurs Flowteurs|date=9 March 2012|accessdate=29 December 2013|language=French}}</ref>

==History==
The two rappers met in the year 2000 in [[Paris]].<ref name="fnac">[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Wy0XkTMoTFs Orelsan et Gringe, les Casseurs Flowters, passent à table] – Fnac on [[YouTube]]. Retrieved 14 May 2014. {{in lang|fr}}</ref> ''Les Inrocks'' describe their meeting as being "as simple as a [[Franck Dumas]] match analysis."

{{Centered pull quote
|People had told me about him [Orelsan]. I knew that he hosted a roller skating program on the radio, that he was passionate about hip hop and I saw him hanging out in town with his Cambodian military uniforms. [...] He came to see me and asked me if I wanted to start a group with him, and I immediately said yes. We began writing songs without any ambition. We were rapping on the B-sides of [[The Roots]] even when I hate The Roots. We composed songs with the sound card of a comp we stole at my [[University Institutes of Technology|UIT]]. Initially, we wrote for our friends, that's it. And then Orel professionalised his thing. In the meantime, I met a girl and I went back to studying a bit.|author=Gringe |source=in an interview with ''Les Inrocks''.<ref name="LesInrocks">{{cite web|url=http://www.lesinrocks.com/musique/critique-album/orelsan-et-gringe-sont-les-casseurs-flowters/ |title=Casseurs Flowters : bête et beau comme un jour sans fin |date=12 December 2013 |author=Pierre Siankowski |publisher=LesInrocks.com |accessdate=14 May 2014|language=fr}}</ref>}}

Casseurs Flowters released their first [[mixtape]] ''Fantasy: Episode 1'' with 11 tracks, with help from French record producer [[Skread]], in 2004.<ref name=charts1>[http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Orelsan/news-85758.html Charts in France: Orelsan : un nouvel album des Casseurs Flowters avec Gringe!] {{in lang|fr}}</ref> Afterwards, the two rappers decided to focus on building their solo careers, with Orelsan releasing ''[[Perdu d'avance]]'' in 2009 and ''[[Le chant des sirènes]]'' in 2011, his first two [[studio album]]s, and Gringe releasing a mixtape entitled ''Fantasy Mixtape'' in 2009.

Following the success of Orelsan's second studio album ''[[Le chant des sirènes]]'', the duo released their first collaborative single, "[[Bloqué]]", on 3 July 2013,<ref name=figaro>{{cite news|url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/musique/2013/07/03/03006-20130703ARTFIG00476-orelsan-et-gringe-signent-le-retour-de-casseurs-flowters-avec-bloque.php|author=Morgane Giuliani|title=Orelsan et Gringe signent le retour de Casseurs Flowters avec Bloqué|date=3 July 2013|accessdate=13 July 2013|language=French}}</ref> as a pre-release for their upcoming debut studio album, ''[[Orelsan et Gringe sont les Casseurs Flowters]]'', which was released on 15 November 2013 to generally positive reviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/casseurs-flowters-mw0002605419 |title=Orelsan et Gringe sont les Casseurs Flowters |publisher=[[Allmusic]].com |accessdate=22 March 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/fr/album/orelsan-et-gringe-sont-les/id732848459 |title=Orelsan et Gringe sont les Casseurs Flowters - iTunes |language=French |publisher=[[iTunes]] |date=18 November 2013 |accessdate=22 March 2014}}</ref>

===Other works===
Gringe has released a number of independent materials and has collaborated with a number of artists such as El Matador, [[Brasco]], Pit Baccardi, La Province, Jamal and Nubi.

Orelsan has also released a number of other independent materials, notably the ''Zéro EP'' with 14 tracks that included five tracks featuring his work with Gringe as Casseurs Flowters, namely "Arrêtes", "Toc Toc", "Mauvaises ondes", "Venu pour flowter" and "J'vais baiser ta femme".
