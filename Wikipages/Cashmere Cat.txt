'''Magnus August Høiberg''' (born 29 November 1987), known professionally as '''Cashmere Cat''', is a Norwegian DJ, record producer, songwriter and musician. He is best known for producing songs for artists including Kanye West, The Kid Laroi, Selena Gomez, Camila Cabello and The Weeknd, as well as his own critically acclaimed musical output.  

His debut EP, ''[[Mirror Maru]]'' <ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://www.swide.com/luxury-magazine/Faces/Music/cashmere-cat-and-the-mirror-maru-new-ep-introduction-and-review/2012/11/5|title=The Cashmere Cat EP&nbsp;– Perfect for Winter|publisher=Swide Magazine|date=5 November 2012|first=Ben|last=Taylor|access-date=11 November 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121108050934/http://www.swide.com/luxury-magazine/Faces/Music/cashmere-cat-and-the-mirror-maru-new-ep-introduction-and-review/2012/11/5|archive-date=8 November 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/17408-mirror-maru/|title=Cashmere Cat: Mirror Maru|publisher=[[Pitchfork Media]]|date=28 November 2012|first=Jonah|last=Bromwich}}</ref>, was released in October 2012 and followed by the full length albums ''[[9 (Cashmere Cat album)|9]]'', in 2017, and ''Princess Catgirl'', in 2019. 

Høiberg has toured the world over, performing at festivals including Coachella (US), Lollapalooza (US), Hangout (US), Firefly (US), Ultra (US), Clockenflap (HK), Sonar (ES), Roskilde (DK), Lowlands (NL), Pukkelpop (BE), Falls Festival (AUS) and Øya (Nor).

== Career ==

=== 2006&ndash;15: Early years, ''Mirror Maru'' and ''Wedding Bells'' ===
As a teenager, Høiberg began producing music and teaching himself how to DJ. He represented [[Norway]] in the [[DMC World DJ Championships]] from 2006 to 2009 under the pseudonym DJ Final. 

Høiberg's debut [[extended play]] (EP) as Cashmere Cat, ''[[Mirror Maru]],''<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://www.swide.com/luxury-magazine/Faces/Music/cashmere-cat-and-the-mirror-maru-new-ep-introduction-and-review/2012/11/5|title=The Cashmere Cat EP&nbsp;– Perfect for Winter|publisher=Swide Magazine|date=5 November 2012|first=Ben|last=Taylor|access-date=11 November 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121108050934/http://www.swide.com/luxury-magazine/Faces/Music/cashmere-cat-and-the-mirror-maru-new-ep-introduction-and-review/2012/11/5|archive-date=8 November 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/17408-mirror-maru/|title=Cashmere Cat: Mirror Maru|publisher=[[Pitchfork Media]]|date=28 November 2012|first=Jonah|last=Bromwich}}</ref> was released on 22 October 2012 on French label Pelican Fly. The diversity and influence of multiple genres gained support from notables such as [[Hudson Mohawke]], [[Rustie]] and [[Gilles Peterson]]. <ref>{{cite web|url=http://soundcloud.com/rustie/essential-mix-bbc-radio-1|title=Rustie – Essential Mix – BBC Radio 1 – 7th April 2012 |publisher=Soundcloud|date=April 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.liveforthefunk.com/recent/post/2-chainz-drake-no-lie-cashmere-cat-edit/ |title=2 Chainz & Drake- No Lie (Cashmere Cat Edit) |publisher=LiveForTheFunk |date=3 October 2012 |first=Michael |last=Washington |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121025150248/http://www.liveforthefunk.com/recent/post/2-chainz-drake-no-lie-cashmere-cat-edit/ |archive-date=25 October 2012 |df=mdy }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gillespetersonworldwide.com/gpww-presents-cashmere-cat/|title=GPWW presents Cashmere Cat|publisher=Gilles Peterson Worldwide|date=October 2013|access-date=3 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150203192048/http://www.gillespetersonworldwide.com/gpww-presents-cashmere-cat/#|archive-date=3 February 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> 

He continued to gain recognition with a number of remixes, including his edits of [[2 Chainz]]' "No Lie," Lana Del Rey's "National Anthem," Miguel's "Do You..." and Jeremih's "773 Love." In January 2013, producer, songwriter and musician [[Benny Blanco]] invited Cashmere to New York to collaborate on production work and helped book his first. In 2013 Cashmere Cat also played his first concerts in the United States. That summer, he moved from Norway to [[Manhattan]]. <ref>Borba, R. (2018). Hotstar: Cashmere Cat, From Underground Turntable Champ To Producer Of The Stars To Touring Force. Retrieved 30 November 2021, from https://www.pollstar.com/News/hotstar-cashmere-cat-from-underground-turntable-champ-to-producer-of-the-stars-to-touring-force-135623</ref> 

On 11 February 2014, Høiberg's second EP ''Wedding Bells'' was released on UK label LuckyMe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.residentadvisor.net/review-view.aspx?id=14421|title=Cashmere Cat: Wedding Bells|publisher=[[Resident Advisor]]|date=11 February 2014|first=Andrew|last=Ryce}}</ref> In August same year, he produced and featured on "Be My Baby" from [[Ariana Grande]]'s second studio album ''[[My Everything (Ariana Grande album)|My Everything]]''. He was also the opening act during Grande's North American [[The Honeymoon Tour]].

=== 2016–present: ''9'', ''Princess Catgirl'', other contributions ===
On 28 February 2016, Cashmere confirmed that his debut studio album, ''9'', was on the way.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://twitter.com/CASHMERECAT/status/704045922418708480?ref_src=twsrc%255Etfw|title = CASHMERE CAT on Twitter|website = Twitter|access-date = 3 March 2016}}</ref> Ahead of the album, Cashmere Cat release singles "[[Wild Love (Cashmere Cat song)|Wild Love]]" featuring [[The Weeknd]] and [[Francis and the Lights]]; "[[Trust Nobody]]" featuring [[Selena Gomez]] and [[Tory Lanez]]; "[[Love Incredible]]" featuring [[Camila Cabello]] and co-produced by [[Sophie (musician)|Sophie]]; and "9 (After Coachella)" featuring [[MØ]] and Sophie. <ref>{{Cite news|url=http://runthetrap.com/2017/03/31/stream-download-cashmere-cat-9-after-coachella-ft-mo-sophie/|title=Cashmere Cat Officially Releases "9 (After Coachella)" ft. MØ and Sophie|last=Gallagher|first=Peach|date=31 March 2017|work=Run The Trap|access-date=11 April 2017}}</ref>  The album was released on the 28th of April 2017 with further contributions from [[Kehlani]], [[Kacy Hill]], [[Ariana Grande]], [[Ty Dolla Sign]] and [[Jhené Aiko]]. 

Cashmere co-produced "[[Wolves (Kanye West song)|Wolves]]", alongside [[Sinjin Hawke]], for [[Kanye West]]'s 2016 studio album ''[[The Life of Pablo]].''

In 2018, Cashmere Cat co-produced album tracks for [[Kanye West]] (''[[Kids See Ghosts (album)|Kids See Ghosts]]'') and [[Nas]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2018/06/cashmere-cat-helped-produce-nas-new-album-nasir/|title=Cashmere Cat is a featured producer on Nas' new album, 'Nasir'|last=Fleisher|first=Grace|date=16 June 2018|work=[[Dancing Astronaut]]|access-date=30 November 2018}}</ref> He continued to produce for a number of artists, with notable credits on Benny Blanco's debut single, the global hit "Eastside" with Halsey and Khalid<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicnorway.no/2018/09/07/cashmere-cat-hits-1-in-uk-with-benny-blancos-eastside//|title=Cashmere Cat hits #1 in UK with Benny Blanco's Eastside|last=Buflaten|first=Erlend|date=7 September 2018|work=Music Norway|access-date=30 November 2018}}</ref> and [[Shawn Mendes]] and [[Camila Cabello]]'s global hit "[[Señorita (Shawn Mendes and Camila Cabello song)|Señorita]]".

Cashmere Cat's second album, ''[[Princess Catgirl]]'', was released on September 20, 2019 featuring singles "Emotions" and "For Your Eyes Only."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://runthetrap.com/2019/08/09/cashmere-cat-emotions-princess-catgirl/|title=Cashmere Cat Returns With Breathtaking Single 'Emotions' Off Forthcoming Album ''Princess Catgirl''|last=Farley|first=Jordan|date=9 August 2019|work=Run The Trap|access-date=10 August 2019}}</ref>

Cashmere Cat co-wrote and co-produced [[The Kid Laroi]]'s 2021 song "[[Stay (The Kid Laroi and Justin Bieber song)|Stay]]" (with [[Justin Bieber]]), which went on to reach number one on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 in August 2021.<ref>https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9612247/kid-laroi-justin-bieber-stay-number-one-hot-100</ref><ref>https://lucidmonday.com/2021/07/11/spotlighting-the-producers-behind-the-kid-laroi-and-justin-biebers-new-single-stay/</ref>
