'''Violette Wautier''' ({{lang-th|วิโอเลต วอเทียร์}}; born October 10, 1993<ref>{{cite news |author1=Atitaya Phorplook |title="วี"พร้อมพัฒนาตัวเอง วงการมายามีสิ่งให้เรียนรู้เยอะ |url=http://daily.khaosod.co.th/view_news.php?newsid=TURObGJuUXdNVEEwTVRJMU9RPT0 |access-date=August 13, 2018 |work=Khaosod Daily |issue=9503 |date=December 4, 2016 |page=13 |language=th}}</ref>) is a Thai-Belgian singer and actress. She made her singing debut with the audition on [[The Voice Thailand (season 2)|season 2]] of [[The Voice Thailand]] in 2013. Then started off her career in both singing and acting. Her notable milestones are winning the Suphannahong Award for Best Supporting Actress in Thailand (2016) and her first English album ''Glitter and Smoke'' (2020).

== Career ==
Wautier first appeared on [[The Voice Thailand (season 2)|season 2]] of the singing competition ''[[The Voice Thailand]]'' in 2013, and went on to record songs for several films and television series. She appeared in productions with studio [[GMM Tai Hub|GTH]]/[[GDH 559|GDH]], winning the [[Suphannahong Award]] for Best Supporting Actress for her performance in ''[[Heart Attack (2015 film)|Heart Attack]]'' (2015), and co-starring in ''[[A Gift (2016 film)|A Gift]]'' (2016). After several years under Universal Music Thailand, she left to become an independent artist and launched her own record label in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rodsom |first1=Sopida |title=Everybody Loves Violette |url=https://www.timeout.com/bangkok/music/Violette-Wautier |website=Time Out Bangkok |access-date=August 13, 2018 |language=en |date=May 23, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Sumatchaya |first1=Khwankaew |title=My space |url=https://www.bangkokpost.com/learning/learning-entertainment/1299231 |access-date=August 13, 2018 |work=Bangkok Post/Student Weekly |date=August 5, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sharerice.com/index.php?title=Violette_Wautier|title=Violette Wautier Information}}</ref> 

In 2018, Wautier released her first English-language single, "Drive". She followed up on the success of the hit with a second track named "Smoke" in the same year. "Smoke" went on to break the record for most views for an English song performed by a Thai artist on YouTube with over 60 million views. The single also ranked No. 1 in 8 countries, including Thailand, Singapore and Malaysia.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Chua |first1=Christy |title=Asia Spotlight: Beneath the Glitter and Smoke, Violette Wautier shares her journey from The Voice Thailand to the regional stage |url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/asia-spotlight-glitter-and-smoke-violette-wautier-the-voice-thailand-to-the-stage-of-asia-brassac |website=Bandwagon Asia |access-date=June 19, 2020 |language=en |date=June 18, 2020}}</ref> Followed the success of "Smoke" and reunited with [[List of Universal Music Group labels|Universal Music Thailand]] after more than a year away from the recording studio, Wautier released her long-awaited third single in March 2020. Named after a small town in the south of France, "Brassac" is an alternative pop anthem inspired by Violette's own short-lived summer romance which what she called a "[[Call Me by Your Name (film)|Call Me By Your Name]]" vibe.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Thaitrakulpanich |first1=Asaree |title=WHY I SING IN ENGLISH: AN INTERVIEW WITH VIOLETTE WAUTIER |url=https://www.khaosodenglish.com/life/entertainment/2020/03/11/why-i-sing-in-english-an-interview-with-violette-wautier/ |website=Khaosod English |access-date=June 19, 2020 |language=en |date=March 11, 2020}}</ref> Her latest single "I'd Do it Again", an anthemic electro-pop which reminisces about her past love was set to be the lead single for her debut all-English album ''Glitter and Smoke'', dropping on June 19, 2020.
