'''Herminio Jose Lualhati Alcasid Jr.''' (born August 27, 1967), better known as '''Ogie Alcasid''' ({{IPA-tl|ˈɔɡi ɐlˈkasɪd}}), is a Filipino singer-songwriter, television presenter, comedian, parodist, and actor. He is currently the President of OPM (Organisasyon ng Pilipinong Mang-Aawit).

==Early career==
Ogie is a former batch member of the all-male singing group ''[[Kundirana]]'' batch 1984<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lo |first1=Ricky |title=Kundirana alumni reunite for a fund-raising concert |url=https://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2019/07/05/1932038/kundirana-alumni-reunite-fund-raising-concert |access-date=5 August 2021 |work=Philstar.com |date=5 July 2019}}</ref> after [[Gary Valenciano]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Policarpio |first1=Allan |title=The tie that binds Ogie, Dingdong, Randy and Gary V |url=https://entertainment.inquirer.net/337260/the-tie-that-binds-ogie-dingdong-randy-and-gary-v |access-date=5 August 2021 |work=INQUIRER.net |date=6 July 2019 |language=en}}</ref> His first album was released in 1989.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pinoystop.com/bio/celebrity-profile/407/ogie-alcasid-biography|title=Ogie Alcasid Biography|website=PinoyStop|access-date=2020-02-28}}</ref> His debut movie was ''Feel Na Feel''<ref>{{cite book |last1=Maglipon |first1=Jo-Ann Q. |title=Primed: Selected Stories, 1972-1992 |date=1993 |publisher=Published and exclusively distributed by Anvil Pub. |isbn=978-971-27-0327-0 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0bNuAAAAMAAJ&q=%22Feel+Na+Feel%22+ogie |access-date=5 August 2021 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="pepph1">{{cite news |title=Ogie Alcasid still looks good after 20 years in showbiz |url=https://www.pep.ph/lifestyle/17058/ogie-alcasid-still-looks-good-after-20-years-in-showbiz |access-date=5 August 2021 |work=PEP.ph |date=23 September 2008 |language=en}}</ref> released by Regal Entertainment in 1990. In 1991, Ogie made his VIVA films debut movie, ''Pitong Gamol''.<ref name="pepph1"/>

==Television career==
{{BLP unsourced section|date=June 2015}}
Alcasid's television career started as one of the hosts of comedy show ''Small Brothers'' on [[ABS-CBN]] in 1992. He appeared on other comedy programs such as ABS-CBN's ''Mana Mana'' (1991–92), [[Associated Broadcasting Company|ABC]]'s ''[[Tropang Trumpo]]'' (from 1994 to 1995), [[GMA Network]]'s ''[[Bubble Gang]]'' (1995-2013), [[Q (TV network)|QTV]]'s ''Ay, Robot!'' (2005–07), and a sitcom ''[[Show Me Da Manny]]'' (2010–11).{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

He also branched out as a [[game show host]], beginning in ABS-CBN's ''[[Game Na Game Na!]]'' in 1995, IBC 13's ''[[Fastbreak]]'', a former basketball game show in 1999, the Philippine version of ''[[Family Feud: Philippine Edition|Family Feud]]'' on [[TV5 (Philippines)|ABC-5]] in 2001 and ''[[Celebrity Duets: Philippine Edition]]'' on GMA. He was a host on ''[[SOP Rules]]''.<ref name=Music/> He got his big break in ''[[Hanggang Kailan]]'', his first drama show, and also sang its main theme with [[Aiza Seguerra]]. In 1995, he moved to GMA Network, leaving ABS-CBN.{{citation needed|date=May 2017}}

Alcasid is recently the judge in ''[[Pinoy Idol]]''.<ref name="Pinoy">{{cite news|first=Archie|last=Dee Calma|title=Ogie Alcasid wants to have a baby with Regine but willing to wait for the right time|date=January 15, 2008|publisher=Philippine Entertainment Portal|url=http://www.pep.ph/news/16067/Ogie-Alcasid-wants-to-have-a-baby-with-Regine-but-willing-to-wait-for-the-right-time}}</ref> and hosted such game shows as ''[[Da Big Show]]'' and ''[[Hole in the Wall (Philippine game show)|Hole in the Wall]]''. In 2013, he left GMA Network and moved to TV5, where his first project under the channel was ''[[The Mega and the Songwriter]]''.

In 2016, he returned to his home network, ABS-CBN. He joined Tawag Ng Tanghalan on ''[[It's Showtime (Philippine TV program)|It's Showtime]]'' as a judge and he was also a judge of ''[[Your Face Sounds Familiar (Philippine TV series)|Your Face Sounds Familiar: Kids]]'', replacing [[Jed Madela]], the judge of season 1 and 2 of the same show.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

Before he returned to ABS-CBN, his contract on [[TV5 (Philippines)|TV5]] had expired. He later appeared on several [[GMA Network]] shows as a guest. He appeared as a guest on ''[[Yan Ang Morning!]]'', ''[[Sunday PinaSaya]]'', and ''[[Eat Bulaga!]]'' as a guest judge. He was a special guest in ''[[Sarap Diva]]'', hosted by his wife, [[Regine Velasquez|Regine Velasquez-Alcasid]], and the comedy show ''[[Bubble Gang]]'', where he recently a cast member until 2013 before his transfer to TV5.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

==Recording career==
[[File:Singer Ogie.jpg|thumb|Ogie Alcasid in 2010.]]
Alcasid debuted as a singer in 1989 with the release of his self-titled album. ''Ogie Alcasid'' reached [[Music recording sales certification|gold record status]], while his debut single "Nandito Ako" (I Am Here) was awarded "Song of the Year" by a local radio station [[Magic 89.9]]. He has since released 18 albums, including a Christmas album (''Larawan ng Pasko''/Images of Christmas, 1994), a live album (''OA sa Hits (Live)'', 2002), and four greatest hits albums. He has received a total of twelve gold records, three platinum records, and three double platinum records.<ref name=Music>{{Cite web |url=http://www.ogiealcasid.ph/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=January 15, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080115004245/http://www.ogiealcasid.ph/ |archive-date=January 15, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1995, Alcasid married [[Australian]] beauty queen [[Michelle van Eimeren]], who represented Australia in the [[Miss Universe 1994|1994 Miss Universe]] pageant held in the Philippines. The couple had two children, [[Leila Alcasid|Leila]] and Sarah.<ref name="Eimeren">{{cite news|url=http://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/04/03/15/look-ogie-reunites-daughters-michelle|title=LOOK: Ogie reunites with daughters with Michelle|website=News.abs0cbn.com|date=August 5, 2015|access-date=September 5, 2016}}</ref> In 2007, Alcasid announced that he and Eimeren had separated.<ref name="Eimeren"/>

On December 22, 2010, he married multi-plantinum singer [[Regine Velasquez]], at a resort in [[Nasugbu, Batangas]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gmanetwork.com/news/story/208588/showbiz/pep/pep-all-is-set-for-ogie-alcasid-regine-velasquez-wedding|title=PEP: All is set for Ogie Alcasid-Regine Velasquez wedding|website=Gmanetwork.com|date=December 17, 2010|access-date=September 5, 2016}}</ref> He and Velasquez have a son, Nathaniel James ("Nate"), born on November 8, 2011.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.gmanetwork.com/news/story/237887/showbiz/regine-velasquez-gives-birth-to-baby-boy-nathaniel-james|title=Regine Velasquez gives birth to baby boy Nathaniel James|website=Gmanetwork.com|date=November 9, 2011|access-date=September 5, 2016}}</ref>

Alcasid is a Christian and an attendee of [[Victory Christian Fellowship]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.pep.ph/news/42686/ogie-alcasid-on-sharing-his-faith-with-showbiz-friends-if-forgiveness-is-the-name-of-the-game-all-ou|title=Ogie Alcasid on sharing his faith with showbiz friends: "If forgiveness is the name of the game, all our problems in this world are solved."|author=Garcia, Rose|website=PEP.ph|date=March 9, 2014|access-date=September 5, 2016}}</ref>
