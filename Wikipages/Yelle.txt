'''Yelle''' is a French band founded by lead singer and namesake Yelle (Julie Budet) and GrandMarnier (Jean-François Perrier). A third member, Tepr (Tanguy Destable), joined the band before the recording of their debut album. Yelle and GrandMarnier began working on music together in 2000. The band came to prominence when it posted an early version of the song "[[Je veux te voir]]" on [[MySpace]] in September 2005, which later reached the top five in France. The success of the song on MySpace attracted the attention of a record label, which set them to work on their debut album, ''[[Pop Up (album)|Pop Up]]'', which was released in 2007. The group's second album, ''[[Safari Disco Club]]'', was released in 2011, their third, ''[[Complètement fou]]'', in 2014, and their fourth, ''[[L'Ère du Verseau]]'' in 2020.

They have enjoyed a significant following overseas in non-French-speaking countries as well. They toured internationally in support of each of their album releases and have played at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] three times.

==History==
[[File:Yelle @ BSF 2011 (6070940583).jpg|thumb|240x240px|Yelle performing live in 2011]]
Budet met and became good friends with producer GrandMarnier in 2000, and in 2005, they began making music together when both wanted to start an electropop band.<ref name=virgin>{{cite web |url=http://www.virgin.com/news/yelle-interview-french-pop-artiste |title=Yelle: interview with a French pop artiste |publisher=[[Virgin Group|Virgin]] |date=2 March 2009 |access-date=2 December 2009}}</ref><ref name="aural">{{cite web |last=Szeto |first=Greg |url=http://auralstates.com/2008/10/interview-yelle.html |title=Interview: Yelle |publisher=Aural States |date=10 October 2008 |access-date=3 December 2009}}</ref><ref name=metro>{{cite web|url=http://www.metro.us/us/article/2008/10/15/04/2325-72/index.xml|title=The rebel Yelle|publisher=[[Metro International]]|access-date=4 December 2009|date=14 October 2008}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref><ref name=ign>{{cite web|url=http://music.ign.com/articles/874/874417p1.html|title=Yelle 2009|publisher=IGN|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120213234226/http://music.ign.com/articles/874/874417p1.html|archive-date=13 February 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=laptop>{{cite web|url=http://www.laptoprockers.eu/music/p1/interview-yelle-france/|title=YELLE: LOVE AND FRENCH CHANSONS|date=6 November 2008|publisher=Laptop Rockers}}</ref> The name of the band, Yelle, began as an acronym of the phrase "You Enjoy Life", "YEL", but was later feminised to "Yelle" because a Belgian band had the name already.<ref name=virgin/><ref name=metro/> Yelle rose to fame on MySpace when they posted a song titled "Short Dick Cuizi", which later became "Je veux te voir", in late 2005.<ref name=ign/><ref name=laptop/><ref name="format">{{cite web|url=http://www.formatmag.com/features/yelle/|first=Jesse|last=Ship|date=17 August 2008|access-date=2 December 2009|publisher=FORMATMAG.COM|title=Yelle}}</ref> The song's original title refers to Cuizinier of French [[alternative hip-hop]] band [[TTC (band)|TTC]] and was released as a mock [[diss track]]. It received a positive response and soon attracted the attention of record label Source Etc.<ref name="aural"/><ref name=laptop/> The song later charted at No. 4 in France. Yelle then began working on and recording their debut album, ''[[Pop Up (album)|Pop Up]]'', in 2006. While performing at night,<ref name=ign/> producer Tepr had been roped into the band in 2007 after meeting GrandMarnier while working as a journalist.<ref name=virgin/> They were in need of a keyboard player on tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.flashfloodmedia.net/2011/02/21/interview-with-tepr-of-yelle/ |title=Interview with Tepr of YELLE |publisher=Flash Flood Media |date=21 February 2011 |access-date=17 February 2012}}</ref>

''Pop Up'' was released in September 2007. [[AllMusic]] called it "a perfect storm of glossy production values, smart songwriting, and Budet's sparkling delivery."<ref>{{cite web| last= Reges| first= Margaret| url= http://www.allmusic.com/album/pop-up-r1216892/review| title= Pop-Up – Yelle| publisher= AllMusic| date= 31 August 2007| access-date= 17 February 2012}}</ref>
The album produced another single, "[[A cause des garçons#Yelle version|A cause des garçons]]", which reached number eleven on the French Singles Chart. Yelle was featured on [[Fatal Bazooka]]'s French number-one song "[[Parle à ma main]]" in 2007.<ref name="FRA"/>
The band has enjoyed success outside France despite making songs exclusively in French and have toured extensively overseas in non-French-speaking countries.<ref name= virgin /><ref name= aural /><ref name= ign /><ref name= laptop /><ref name= format />
In the US, they were featured as MTV's Artist of the Week at the end of March 2008.<ref>{{cite web| last= Anitai| first= Tamar| url= http://buzzworthy.mtv.com/2008/03/24/artist-of-the-week-yelle/| title= Artist of the Week: Yelle| publisher= [[MTV Buzzworthy]]| date= 24 March 2008| access-date= 3 December 2009}}</ref>
After touring for three years, Yelle took three months off{{dash}}until September 2009, when they began working on their next album.<ref name= austin>{{cite web| last= Apollo| first=Chris| url= http://republicofaustin.com/is-french-electro-pop-group-yelle-moving-to-austin-interview-safari-disco-club-album-review/| title= Is French electro-pop group Yelle moving to Austin? [INTERVIEW + Safari Disco Club ALBUM REVIEW&#93; &#124; Life Music and Culture Blog in Austin Texas – Republic of Austin| date= 22 April 2011| access-date= 17 February 2012}}</ref><ref name= rocket>{{cite web| url= http://www.rocketmagazine.net/?p=4085| title= INTERVIEW: YELLE &#124; Rocket Magazine – contemporary culture magazine| publisher= Rocket Magazine| date= 28 March 2011| access-date= 17 February 2012}}</ref>
By February 2010, they had started their own record label, Recreation Center, overseen by GrandMarnier.<ref name=rocket /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.recreation-center.com/what-is-the-recreation-center |quote=The RECREATION CENTER is YELLE's own microlabel. |title=RECREATION CENTER – RECREATION CENTER? |publisher=Recreation Center |date=27 February 2010 |access-date=28 September 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140408052411/http://www.recreation-center.com/what-is-the-recreation-center |archive-date=8 April 2014 }}</ref>

Yelle's second album, ''[[Safari Disco Club]]'', was released in March 2011. Budet explained that unlike their debut, there was no deadline for the album because they were on their own label, thus allowing them to focus more on the harmonies and melodies and Budet's singing voice.<ref name=tsquat>{{cite web|author=melissa |url=http://t-squat.com/?p=4931 |title=Yelle Interview |publisher=T-Squat.Com |date=1 July 2011 |access-date=17 February 2012}}</ref><ref name=gutfire/> They were also able to focus on writing and recording the album for a year as opposed to having to perform at night like for ''Pop Up''. The album received generally favourable reviews, with the ''[[The Independent|Independent]]'' declaring that the album was "essential for anyone who appreciates dancefloor-friendly European synth pop."<ref>{{cite news|author=Simon Price |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/album-yelle-safari-disco-club-barclay-2269163.html |title=Album: Yelle, Safari Disco Club (Barclay) – Reviews – Music |work=The Independent |location=UK |date=17 April 2011 |access-date=17 February 2012}}</ref> The title track was released for free on their website in January 2011 and was released as a double single with "Que veux-tu". Katy Perry asked them to open for her on her 2011 [[California Dreams Tour]] in the UK.<ref name=tsquat/> They embarked on their own tour afterwards, returning to the US in the fall.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hangout.altsounds.com/news/137041-yelle-announces-safari-disco-club-remixes-11-a.html |title=Yelle Announces 'Safari Disco Club Remixes' for 11/2 Release; &#124; Altsounds.com News |publisher=Hangout.altsounds.com |access-date=17 February 2012}}</ref> On 11 February 2013, the band released the single "L'amour parfait".<ref name="lamour-parfait">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/fr/album/lamour-parfait-remixes-ep/id596357132?l=en |title=L'amour parfait (Remixes) – EP by Yelle |publisher=[[iTunes Store]] (FR). [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] |access-date=6 February 2013}}</ref>

In July 2014, it was announced that Yelle's third studio album, ''[[Complètement fou]]'', was planned for release in late September 2014. The album was co-produced by [[Dr. Luke]] and his team of producers (Kojak, AC, Billboard Mat, Oliver, Cirkut, Jmike, Madmax), who discovered Yelle through their remix of [[Katy Perry]]'s "[[Hot n Cold]]" and a live show in Los Angeles. Dr. Luke eventually signed Yelle to his [[vanity label]], [[Kemosabe Records]].<ref name="drluke">{{cite web |last=Payne |first=Chris |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6106399/yelle-dr-luke-new-album-2014-interview-completement-fou |title=Yelle Announces New Dr. Luke-Produced LP 'Completement Fou,' Shares New Song & Tour Dates |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |date=9 July 2014 |access-date=31 July 2014}}</ref> The album was preceded by the single "Bouquet final", for which a lyric video premiered on 30 June 2014.<ref>{{cite web |last=Hudson |first=Alex |url=http://exclaim.ca/MusicVideo/ClickHear/yelle-bouquet_final_lyric_video |title=Yelle – "Bouquet Final" (lyric video) |work=[[Exclaim!]] |date=30 June 2014 |access-date=31 July 2014}}</ref> ''Complètement fou'' was released on 29 September 2014 through Kemosabe and followed by an extensive international tour that saw the band play at Coachella for the third time in their career as well as all over Europe, the Americas, and China.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://musique.fnac.com/a7650933/Yelle-Completement-fou-CD-album |title=Complètement fou – Yelle – CD album |language=fr |publisher=[[Fnac]] |access-date=14 October 2014}}</ref> The band released a song titled "OMG!!!" on 21 August 2018<ref>{{cite web |title=New song called "OMG!!!" will be out August 21st and it will come with a music video |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bmam3PHF7P_/ |author=Yelle |access-date=14 August 2018 |date=13 August 2018 |via=Instagram}}</ref> and a song titled "Je t'aime encore" on 28 April 2020.

==Artistry==
For their first two albums, GrandMarnier and Tepr produced the music, while Budet co-wrote the lyrics<ref name=Chicago>{{cite web|url=http://chicago.timeout.com/articles/clubs/67961/interview-with-julie-budet-of-yelle|title=Yelle|date=16 October 2008|publisher=[[Time Out (company)|Time Out Chicago]]|first=Josh|last=Klein|access-date=3 December 2009}}</ref> with GrandMarnier.<ref name=bright>{{cite web |author=Patrick |url=http://www.brightshinymusic.com/2011/05/yelle-interview.html |title=Bright & Shiny Music: Exclusive B&S Interview with Yelle |publisher=Brightshinymusic.com |date=16 May 2011 |accessdate=17 February 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120326205547/http://www.brightshinymusic.com/2011/05/yelle-interview.html |archive-date=26 March 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> While working on their second album and meeting with American labels, they were repeatedly queried about singing in English in the future. However, Yelle maintain that they would not be able to express themselves in a language that they were not familiar with.<ref name=gutfire/> Singing French songs to many non-francophone audiences all over the world led Yelle to launch her own Yelle Translator companion app (developed by Pomp & Clout) in 2014 The Yelle translator app. The app works to decode lyric videos from the album ''Complètement fou'' by letting the user choose whether they'd like to view the original French lyrics or the English translation by holding their phone up to the music video screen and selecting a language. The user is also able to toggle between the languages in real time. Their debut album ''Pop Up'', is of the electropop genre with 1980s influences.<ref name=gutfire>{{cite web|url=http://www.gutfiremagazine.com/features/2011/05/ou-les-animaux-dansent-an-interview-with-yelle/ |title=OĂ LES ANIMAUX DANSENT: An Interview with Yelle |publisher=GUTFIRE! Magazine |date=2 May 2011 |access-date=17 February 2012}}</ref> Their lyrics are playful and sarcastic. The debut single "Je veux te voir" was a humorous reply to the misogynist lyrics of some French rappers, especially Cuizinier of TTC.<ref name=spectrum>{{cite web|url=http://spectrumculture.com/2011/06/interview-yelle.html |title=Interview: Yelle |publisher=Spectrum Culture |date=1 June 2011 |access-date=17 February 2012}}</ref> For ''Safari Disco Club'', they retained the sound of ''Pop Up'' while incorporating more rhythmic drums into the production, such as Caribbean influences and new wave influences in the synthesizers.<ref name=gutfire/> Budet described it as a marriage between "hotter rhythms and cooler synthesizer", highlighting the sense of humor and melancholy coexisting in the lyrics as well.<ref name=gutfire/><ref name=spectrum/> The melancholy was attributed to Yelle having toured for three years for their first album and having that experience abruptly end to begin work on their next album.<ref name=austin/>

For their third album, ''Completement fou'', the band collaborated with American music producer, Dr. Luke. Luke and his team of producers brought a new focus on melody rather than the beats-driven approach that Yelle previously had to songwriting.<ref name="drluke"/>

Budet's colorful stage outfits are influenced by designers such as [[Jeremy Scott]], Andrea Crews, Jean-Paul Lespagnard and [[Jean-Charles de Castelbajac]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.speakerboxxx.com/2009/03/yelle-an-interview-with-a-french-pop-artiste/ |title=Yelle, An Interview with a French Pop Artiste |publisher=Speakerboxxx |date=1 March 2009 |access-date=17 February 2012}}</ref>
