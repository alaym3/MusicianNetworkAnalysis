'''Dylan Sinclair''' is a [[Canada|Canadian]] rhythm and blues singer and songwriter from [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]],<ref>Kenisha Alexander, [https://ca.hellomagazine.com/music/02021013158827/seven-canadaian-up-and-coming-musicians-2021 "Seven up-and-coming Canadian musicians to watch in 2021"]. ''[[Hello! Canada]]'', February 1, 2021.</ref> whose full-length debut album ''Proverb'' was a [[Juno Award]] nominee for [[Juno Award for Traditional R&B/Soul Recording of the Year|Traditional R&B/Soul Recording of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2021]].<ref>Holly Gordon, [https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/the-weeknd-jp-saxe-jessie-reyez-and-justin-bieber-lead-2021-juno-award-nominations-1.5940551 "The Weeknd, JP Saxe, Jessie Reyez and Justin Bieber lead 2021 Juno Award nominations"]. [[CBC Music]], March 9, 2021.</ref>

Sinclair released his first EP, ''Red Like Crimson'', in 2018. In January 2021, he was named one of the winners of [[SOCAN]]'s inaugural Black Canadian Music Awards alongside [[Tobi (musician)|Tobi]], [[Naya Ali]], RAAHiiM and Hunnah.<ref>Brock Thiessen, [https://exclaim.ca/music/article/here_are_the_winners_of_the_inaugural_black_canadian_music_awards "Here Are the Winners of the First Black Canadian Music Awards"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', January 11, 2021.</ref>
