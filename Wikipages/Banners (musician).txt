'''Michael Joseph Nelson''',<ref name="Island bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.islandrecords.com/artists/banners/ |title=Banners |publisher=[[Island Records]] |accessdate=25 January 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160131222952/http://www.islandrecords.com/artists/banners/ |archive-date=31 January 2016 |df=dmy }}</ref><ref name="Holy Ground Billboard"/> known under the stage name '''Banners''' (styled as '''BANNΞRS'''), is an English musician from [[Liverpool]], based in [[Toronto, Ontario]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Zyzda |first1=Rachel |title=A Chat With: BANNERS |url=https://www.anchrmagazine.com/interviews/2018/a-chat-with-banners |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=Anchr Magazine |date=6 February 2018}}</ref> He released his debut studio album ''Where the Shadow Ends'' in 2019.

==History==
===2015–2018: Early EPs===
Nelson grew up singing in [[Liverpool Cathedral]] Choir and performed with the choir in various locations throughout Europe before deciding to start a solo career.<ref name="AllMusic Bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/banners-mn0003438435/biography |title=Banners {{!}} Biography & History |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |first=Marcy |last=Donelson |accessdate=25 January 2016}}</ref><ref name="riff">{{cite news |last1=Gokhman |first1=Roman |title=Q&A: Banners on the cusp of making a big splash |url=https://riffmagazine.com/qa/introducing-banners/ |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=Riff Magazine |date=12 February 2018}}</ref> In 2015, he relocated to Toronto, Ontario, Canada, from his native Liverpool,<ref name="ct">{{cite news |last1=Dickinson |first1=Chrissie |title=That ethereal Banners sound is no accident |url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-ott-0217-banners-20170213-story.html |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=Chicago Tribune |date=16 February 2017}}</ref> and contacted Canadian producer [[Stephen Kozmeniuk]] to work on his sound.<ref name="Island bio"/> Nelson released his debut single "Ghosts" in March 2015 under the stage name Raines.<ref name="AllMusic Bio"/> A music video for the song was directed by Christopher Ranson.<ref name="Spin Shine video"/>

His second single, "[[Shine a Light (Banners song)|Shine a Light]]", was released in August 2015 under the new moniker Banners.<ref name="Interview Mag">{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/exclusive-video-premiere-shine-a-light-banners |title=Exclusive Video Premiere: "Shine a Light" Banners |work=[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]] |first=Emily |last=McDermott |date=11 November 2015 |accessdate=25 January 2016}}</ref> A music video for the song was directed by Christopher Ranson and released in October 2015.<ref name="Spin Shine video">{{cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/2015/10/banners-shine-a-light-video/ |title=Banners Battles Darkness in Epic "Shine a Light" Video |work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |first=Nathan |last=Diller |date=9 October 2015 |accessdate=25 January 2016}}</ref> "Shine a Light" peaked at number 5 on the ''[[Billboard charts|Billboard]]'' Canada Rock chart, and number 71 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]].<ref name="standard">{{cite news |last1=Johns |first1=Lauren |title=Underrated Artist Review: Banners |url=http://www.the-standard.org/opinion/underrated-artist-review-banners/article_83fafb62-ba9a-11ea-906a-5b6e00bc1667.html |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=The Standard |date=30 June 2020}}</ref>

In December 2015, [[Spotify]] included Banners on its Spotlight list, which highlights 15 artists to watch in 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/brendanklinkenberg/these-are-the-15-artists-spotify-thinks-will-break-big-next |title=15 Artists Spotify Thinks Will Be Huge Next Year |first=Brendan |last=Klinkenberg |publisher=[[Buzzfeed]] |date=3 December 2015 |accessdate=25 January 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.islandrecords.com/news/banners-named-spotify-spotlight-artist-to-watch-in-2016/ |title=Banners Named Spotify Spotlight Artist to Watch in 2016 |publisher=[[Island Records]] |date=7 December 2015 |accessdate=25 January 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160201001847/http://www.islandrecords.com/news/banners-named-spotify-spotlight-artist-to-watch-in-2016/ |archive-date=1 February 2016 |df=dmy }}</ref>
On 4 December, Banners released his third single "Start a Riot".<ref name="Island Start Riot premiere">{{cite web|url=http://www.islandrecords.com/news/banners-start-a-riot/ |title=Banners Premieres New Single "Start A Riot" |publisher=[[Island Records]] |date=4 December 2015 |accessdate=25 January 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160131211355/http://www.islandrecords.com/news/banners-start-a-riot/ |archive-date=31 January 2016 |df=dmy }}</ref><ref name="iTunes Start Riot"/> The song peaked at number 24 on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Rock & Alternative Songs]] chart.

Banners signed with [[Island Records]] in 2016.<ref name="riff"/><ref name="ct"/> His debut [[Banners (EP)|self-titled EP]], produced by Kozmeniuk, was released on 15 January 2016 through Island Records. In addition to the three previously released singles, it included two new tracks: "Gold Dust" and "Back When We Had Nothing".<ref name="Island EP release">{{cite web|url=http://www.islandrecords.com/news/banners-releases-self-titled-ep/ |title=Banners Releases Self-Titled EP |publisher=[[Island Records]] |date=15 January 2015 |accessdate=25 January 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160131203511/http://www.islandrecords.com/news/banners-releases-self-titled-ep/ |archive-date=31 January 2016 |df=dmy }}</ref> The EP debuted at number 67 on the ''Billboard'' [[Canadian Albums Chart]].
On 22 January 2016, [[Capitol Records]] released a soundtrack album for the TV series ''[[The Royals (TV series)|The Royals]]'', which included Banners' song "Half Light".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/bounce/id1077480458? |title=The Royals (Original Television Soundtrack) by Various Artists |publisher=[[iTunes]] (US) |accessdate=25 January 2016}}</ref>
On 27 January 2016, Banners made his television debut performing "Shine a Light" on [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]'s show ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.islandrecords.com/news/watch-banners-perform-shine-a-light-jimmy-kimmel-live/ |title=Watch Banners Perform "Shine A Light" on Jimmy Kimmel Live |publisher=Island Records |date=28 January 2016 |accessdate=28 January 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160202220010/http://www.islandrecords.com/news/watch-banners-perform-shine-a-light-jimmy-kimmel-live/ |archive-date=2 February 2016 |df=dmy }}</ref> From 29 February to 31 March 2016, he joined American bands [[POP ETC]] and [[The Moth & The Flame]] for his first U.S. tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/BANNERSmusic/photos/a.766186393473134.1073741829.748084465283327/917718774986561/ |title=Tickets are now on sale for my first tour in the USA! ... |publisher=[[Facebook]] |date=29 January 2016 |accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref> 

On 3 June 2016, Banners released the single "Half Light".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.noise11.com/news/music-news-new-music-releases-for-3-june-2016-20160603 |title=New Music Releases For 3 June 2016 |publisher=[[Noise11]] |first=Gavin |last=Ryan |date=3 June 2016 |accessdate=3 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/BANNERSmusic/videos/995039173921187/ |title=Banners - My New Single Is Out...tomorrow. Stay tuned |publisher=[[Facebook]] |date=2 June 2016 |accessdate=3 June 2016}}</ref>

On 24 July 2016, Banners performed at [[WayHome Music & Arts Festival]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Murphy |first1=Sarah |title=WayHome Reveals 2016 Lineup |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/wayhome_reveals_2016_lineup |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=Exclaim |date=9 February 2016}}</ref> On 31 July 2016, he performed at [[Lollapalooza]] in Chicago.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Long |first1=Zach |title=Lollapalooza 2016 lineup |url=https://www.timeout.com/chicago/music/lollapalooza-2016-lineup |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=Timeout |date=23 March 2016}}</ref> 

On 16 September 2016, Banners released "Into the Storm"<ref name="iTunes Into the Storm"/> which peaked at number 16 on the Canada Rock chart. This was followed in November 2016 by "Holy Ground".<ref name="Holy Ground Billboard">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7581180/banners-holy-ground-new-song-premiere |title=Banners Shares Dreamy New Track "Holy Ground": Exclusive |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |first=Lyndsey |last=Havens |date=17 November 2016 |accessdate=19 November 2016}}</ref> The song's music video stars [[Ashley Greene]].<ref name="ct"/> 

On 31 May 2017, Banners began touring with band [[Milky Chance]] in the US. On 3 November 2017, Banners released his second EP, ''Empires on Fire''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Scott |first1=Jason |title=BANNERS ignites ‘Empires on Fire’ with new EP |url=https://bsidesbadlands.com/banners-empires-on-fire-ep-review/ |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=B-Sides and Badlands}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Where the Shadow Ends''===
On 4 October 2019, Banners released his debut album, ''Where the Shadow Ends'', preceded by the singles "Got It In You" and "Where the Shadow Ends"<ref>{{cite news |title=BANNERS New Album Out Now |url=https://www.alternativeaddiction.com/AANewsArticle/banners-new-album-out-now |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=Alternative Addiction |date=7 October 2019}}</ref> The album was supported with a five-week headline tour across North America.<ref>{{cite news |title=BANNERS and Youngbombs Release New Title Track, "Where the Shadow Ends" |url=https://www.alternativeaddiction.com/AANewsArticle/banners-and-youngbombs-release-title-track-where-the-shadow-ends |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=Alternative Addiction |date=24 September 2019}}</ref> 

In 2020, his 2017 single "Someone to You" received a resurgence in streaming following a [[TikTok]] trend.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Scott |first1=Jason |title=BANNERS Enjoys Streaming Hit In Pandemic Times |url=https://americansongwriter.com/banners-someone-to-you-streaming-hit-interview/ |access-date=27 November 2020 |work=American Songwriter}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Banners Grateful for "Someone to You" TikTok Exposure, Shares Favorite B-Side From Coldplay |url=http://b-sides.tv/interviews/20200928-video-interview-banners-grateful-for-someone-to-you-tiktok-exposure-shares-favorite-b-side-from-coldplay |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=B-Sides TV |date=28 September 2020}}</ref> The single was certified Gold in Australia by the [[Australian Recording Industry Association]].<ref name="ARIA gold">{{cite certification|region=Australia|type=single|certyear=2020|access-date=23 April 2020}}</ref> 

On 1 May 2020, Banners released his third EP, ''Always Yours'', on Island Records, with acoustic versions of previously released tracks, along with the new title track.<ref>{{cite news |title=Banners Drops New EP, ''Always Yours'' |url=https://www.alternativeaddiction.com/AANewsArticle/banners-drops-new-ep-always-yours |access-date=27 November 2020 |publisher=Alternative Addiction |date=6 May 2020}}</ref>

==In popular culture==
His song "Ghosts" was featured on season 5 episodes of both ''[[Suits (season 5)|Suits]]'' and ''[[Teen Wolf (season 5)|Teen Wolf]]''. On 29 May 2017, the single "Start a Riot" was featured on the last episode of the second season of the TV series ''[[Lucifer (TV series)|Lucifer]]''.

The single "[[Shine a Light (Banners song)|Shine a Light]]" was featured on the 7th episode of the first season of the TV series ''[[The Good Doctor (TV series)|The Good Doctor]]'', on the video game ''[[FIFA 16]]'', the soundtrack of the 2017 movie ''[[The Space Between Us (film)|The Space Between Us]]'', and the 2018 film ''[[Beautiful Boy (2018 film)|Beautiful Boy]]''.<ref name="riff"/> The single was also featured near the end of the 2018 movie ''[[Love, Simon]]'' but was not included in the soundtrack of the same name.<ref name="standard"/>

In April 2018, Banners appeared on [[American Idol (season 16)|season 16]] of the U.S. TV competition ''[[American Idol]]'' to perform two duets with contestants Ron Bultongez and Alyssa Raghu. He sang his own "[[Someone to You]]" with Bultongez and [[Coldplay]]'s "[[Yellow (Coldplay song)|Yellow]]" with Raghu.

In 2018 the single "Half Light" was featured in the trailer of the TV series ''[[New Amsterdam (2018 TV series)|New Amsterdam]]'', and on an episode of the TV series ''[[The Royals (TV series)|The Royals]]''.<ref name="riff"/> 

In 2019, the single "Got It in You" was featured in the 17th episode of the second season of the US TV series ''[[The Good Doctor (TV series)|The Good Doctor]]'' and in an episode of ''[[America's Got Talent]]''. "Someone to You" is used in the 2019 movie ''[[After (2019 film)|After]]'',<ref>{{Citation|title=After (2019) - IMDb|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt4126476/soundtrack|access-date=2019-12-11}}</ref> in the trailer of the TV series ''[[Love, Victor]]'', in a 2017 episode of ''[[Kevin (Probably) Saves the World]]'',<ref>{{cite web |title=Kevin (Probably) Saves the World Soundtrack |url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/kevin-probably-saves-the-world/season-1/54453 |website=tunefind.com |access-date=27 November 2020}}</ref> and in a commercial for [[T-Mobile]]. It was also used in late 2020, season 17 of ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]'', for the return of Derek Shepherd.

In 2021, the single "If I Didn't Have You" was used in the trailer of the second season of the show ''[[Love, Victor]]'', making it the second time a Banners song was used for the show.
