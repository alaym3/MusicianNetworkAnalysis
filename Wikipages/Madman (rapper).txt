'''Pierfrancesco Botrugno''' (born 25 July 1988), better known by the stage name '''Madman''' sometimes stylized as '''MadMan''' is an [[Italy|Italian]] [[Rapping|rapper]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-01-05|title=Gemitaiz & Madman, dalle parole ai fatti!|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150105113725/http://www.myhiphop.it/2013/01/31/gemitaiz-madman-dalle-parole-ai-fatti/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=web.archive.org}}</ref>

== Biography ==

=== Early years, ''Escape from Heart'' ===
Madman entered the world of [[hip hop]] by participating in the [[Tecniche Perfette#2006|2006 edition]] of [[Tecniche Perfette]] (a well-known [[italian freestyle]] competition), in [[Apulia]], later won in [[Turin]] by July B.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-11-14|title=Tecniche Perfette - Freestyle Rap Italiano - La scheda della battle|url=https://freestylerapitaliano.it/tecniche-perfette/|access-date=2021-05-11|website=Freestyle Rap Italiano|language=it-IT}}</ref>

In 2007 he made his debut with the self-produced [[mixtape]] ''Riscatto mixtape'', made in collaboration with [[TempoXso]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Madman & tempoxso presentano riscatto mixtape -free-|url=https://hotmc.com/madman-tempoxso-presentano-riscatto-mixtape-free/|access-date=2021-05-11|language=it-IT}}</ref> followed in the same year by ''Prequel'' (recorded together with [[Esse-P]]),<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prequel, EP - ep del 2007 Che cos'è. Enciclopedia|url=https://amp.ww.it.freejournal.info/8078296/1/prequel-ep.html|access-date=2021-05-11|website=ww.it.freejournal.info|language=it}}</ref> and in 2008 by ''R.I.P. Street album.''<ref>{{Cite web|title=R.I.P. (MadMan) Che cos'è. Enciclopedia|url=https://amp.it.info-about.ru/6461628/1/r-i-p-madman.html|access-date=2021-05-11|website=it.info-about.ru|language=it}}</ref> In 2010 Madman released the debut album ''Escape from Heart'', released for free through the [[Honiro|Honiro Label]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Madman (18) - R.I.P. Street Album|url=https://www.discogs.com/it/Madman-RIP-Street-Album/release/7134287|access-date=2021-01-15|website=Discogs|language=it}}</ref>

=== Collaborations with Gemitaiz, ''MM Vol. 1'' ===
Since 2011 Madman has embarked on a musical collaboration with rapper [[Gemitaiz]], with whom he has released the mixtape ''[[Haterproof]]'' (2011) and the [[Extended play|EP]] ''[[Detto, Fatto.]]'' (2012), both released through the Honiro Label.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-07-14|title=Escape From Heart: il diamante nero|url=https://rapteratura.home.blog/2019/07/14/escape-from-heart-il-diamante-nero/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=RAPTERATURA|language=it-IT}}</ref>

On June 28, 2013 Madman made the [[mixtape]] ''MM Vol. 1 Mixtape'', consisting of 18 tracks. It was subsequently extracted from it as the only single "Come ti fa Mad", released by the label [[Tanta Roba]] of [[Gué Pequeno]] and [[DJ Harsh]],<ref>{{Cite web|date=2013-11-13|title=Fuori su iTunes "Come ti fa Mad" single pack - Tanta Roba|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131113025125/http://www.tantarobalabel.com/come-ti-fa-mad-single-pack-fuori-su-itunes/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=web.archive.org}}</ref> with whom Madman signed a recording contract on September 25.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2013-11-13|title=MadMan firma per Tanta Roba - Tanta Roba|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131113025145/http://www.tantarobalabel.com/madman-firma-per-tanta-roba/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=web.archive.org}}</ref>

In 2014 the rapper returned to collaborate with Gemitaiz for the realization of the album ''[[Kepler (MadMan album)|Kepler]]'', released on May 24. Anticipated by the videoclips of "Non se ne parla" and "Haterproof 2", the album met with great success in Italy, making its debut at the top of the Italian album chart and being certified [[Gold Disc|gold]] by the [[Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana|Italian Music Industry Federation]] for over 25,000 copies sold in the country.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-09-12|title=L’album "KEPLER" è disco d’oro - Tanta Roba|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140912114953/http://www.tantarobalabel.com/lalbum-kepler-e-disco-doro/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=web.archive.org}}</ref>

=== ''Doppelganger'' and ''MM Vol. 2'' ===
In July 30, 2015, through his [[Facebook]] page, the rapper announced the completion of the second studio album ''Doppelganger'', released on September 11 for Tanta Roba with [[Universal Music Group]] distributio.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-07-30|title=Ecco data e titolo del disco ufficiale di MadMan: Doppelgänger|url=http://www.rapburger.com/2015/07/30/ecco-data-e-titolo-del-disco-ufficiale-di-madman-doppelganger/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=RapBurger|language=it-IT}}</ref>

On 27 October 2016 the mixtape ''MM Vol.2 Mixtape'' was released for [[free download]], consisting of 16 tracks and anticipated by the [[Video clip|videoclip]] of ''Bolla papale freestyle''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-10-27|title=Scarica "MM Vol.2" il nuovo mixtape di Madman|url=http://www.rapburger.com/2016/10/27/madman-mm-vol-2-download/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=RapBurger|language=it-IT}}</ref>

=== ''Back Home'' and ''MM Vol. 3'' ===
On February 2, 2018 Madman releases his third studio album ''Back Home'', anticipated by the singles "Trapano" and "Centro" (with the collaboration of the singer [[Coez]]).<ref>{{Cite web|last=Mattarella|first=Sergio|date=2018-02-23|title=Back Home – Madman ha fatto Centro?|url=https://raphaolic.com/2018/02/23/back-home-madman-ha-fatto-centro/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=Raphaolic|language=it-IT}}</ref> He also collaborates with rapper [[Nitro (rapper)|Nitro]] on the track called "O.K. Corral", from the album ''No Comment'',<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ok Corral (feat. Madman) — Nitro|url=https://box.last.fm/it/music/Nitro/_/Ok+Corral+(feat.+Madman)|access-date=2021-05-11|website=Last.fm|language=it}}</ref> and with rapper [[Enigma (rapper)|Enigma]] in the track "Che roba è?!", contained in the album ''Shardana''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=En?gma Shardana, la recensione del nuovo album|url=https://www.onstageweb.com/recensioni-album/enigma-shardana-2018/|access-date=2021-05-11|website=Onstage|language=it-IT}}</ref>

A year later the mixtape ''MM Vol.3 Mixtape'' was released for [[Digital download (music)|digital download]], promoted by the singles "7/8 Sour" and "Supernova".<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2019-02-01|title=Madman: annunciata sui social l'uscita di "MM Vol. 3" aspettando il tour|url=https://www.allmusicitalia.it/news/madman-mm-vol-3.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-15|website=[[AllMusic|All Music Italia]]|language=it-IT}}</ref>

=== ''Scatola nera'' ===
In 2019 Madman returned to collaborate with Gemitaiz for the creation of a new part of the ''Veleno'' series, materializing on June 7 with the release of ''[[Veleno 7]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-06-07|title=Ascolta "Veleno 7", il nuovo episodio della saga di Gemitaiz & Madman|url=http://www.rapburger.com/2019/06/07/ascolta-veleno-7-il-nuovo-episodio-della-saga-di-gemitaiz-madman/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=RapBurger|language=it-IT}}</ref> The single achieved great success, debuting at the top of the Top Singles Billboard and beating the record of daily audience on [[Spotify|Spotify Italy]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Classifiche - FIMI|url=https://www.fimi.it/top-of-the-music/classifiche.kl|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-15|website=[[FIMI]]|language=it-IT}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=TG24|first=Sky|date=|title=Gemitaiz e Madman: 'Veleno 7' è record Spotify di ascolti|url=https://tg24.sky.it/spettacolo/musica/2019/06/12/gemitaiz-madman-veleno-7-record-spotify|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-15|website=[[Sky TG24]]|language=it}}</ref>

The song also played the role of the lead single on an album by the duo, entitled ''[[Scatola Nera|Scatola nera]]'' and released on September 20 of the same year.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-09-19|title='Scatola Nera', il nuovo disco di Gemitaiz & MadMan: "Insieme ci prendiamo una pausa da noi stessi"|url=https://www.repubblica.it/spettacoli/musica/2019/09/19/news/gemitaiz-236403989/|access-date=2021-01-15|website=la Repubblica|language=it}}</ref>
