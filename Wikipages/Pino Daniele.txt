'''Pino Daniele''' (19 March 1955 – 4 January 2015) was an Italian singer-songwriter, and guitarist, whose influences covered a wide number of genres, including pop, blues, jazz, and Italian and Middle Eastern music.

==Biography==
{{more citations needed section|section|date=January 2016}}
Daniele was born to a working-class family in [[Naples]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/pino-daniele-mn0000765696/biography|title=Biography|publisher=All Music|first=Marisa|last=Brown|access-date=21 August 2019}}</ref> his father being a harbor worker. A self-taught guitarist, he began his career as a musician playing for other successful singers of the 1970s. His debut in the Italian music world was in 1977 with the album ''Terra mia'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/news/pino-daniele-singer-songwriter-and-guitarist-whose-work-fused-blues-jazz-rock-and-the-traditional-9988838.html|title=Pino Daniele: Singer, songwriter and guitarist whose work fused blues, jazz, rock and the traditional music of Naples|publisher=Independent|first=Marisa|last=Brown|page=62|date=19 January 2015|access-date=21 August 2019}}</ref> which proved to be a successful mix of [[Napoli|Neapolitan]] tradition and Blues sounds. Daniele defined his music with the term "tarumbò", which indicated a mix of [[tarantella]], [[blues]] and [[Flamenco Rumba|rumba]]. His lyrics also attracted critical praise: written and sung in an intense [[Neapolitan language|Neapolitan]], they contained strong and bitter accusations against the social injustices of Naples, as well as Italian society in general, and included melancholic personal themes. Several of the later songs are characterized by a free intermingling of English, Italian and [[Neapolitan language|Neapolitan]] passages.

Daniele's talent was confirmed on the following album ''[[Pino Daniele (album)|Pino Daniele]]'' (1979). He scored his greatest success in 1980, with ''[[Nero a metà]]'' ("Half-Black Skinned"), which was noted by some authorities as the hallmark of the rebirth of [[Neapolitan song]]. In that year Daniele opened the [[Bob Marley]] concert at the [[San Siro]] stadium in [[Milan]]. In 1981 ''[[Vai Mo]]'' was released. The presence of some of the most renowned musicians of the Neapolitan musical milieu, including [[James Senese]], [[Enzo Avitabile]], [[Tullio De Piscopo]] and [[Tony_Esposito_(musician)|Tony Esposito]], as session men on his albums has also been widely praised.

In 1982, Daniele shifted to a personal and early version of [[world music]]: in ''[[Bella 'mbriana]]'' musicians such as [[Alphonso Johnson]] and [[Wayne Shorter]] appeared as guest players. In the following year Daniele held a concert in [[Havana]], and later formed a Latin-American band. In 1983 Daniele collaborated with the American singer/guitarist [[Richie Havens]] on ''[[Common Ground (Richie Havens album)|Common Ground]]'', an album written and played together. His interest in [[Arabic music]] is emphasized on ''[[Bonne Soirée]]'' (1987), while the subsequent ''[[Schizzechea With Love]]'' (1988) was more Mediterranean-oriented. In the same year he started a collaboration with the Italian actor and director [[Massimo Troisi]]: Daniele completed the soundtracks for Troisi's films ''[[Le vie del Signore sono finite]]'' and ''[[Pensavo fosse amore invece era un calesse]]''.

''[[Non calpestare i fiori nel deserto]]'', released in Spring 1995, is an attempt to revive inspiration through African and Ethnic influences, and sold more than 800,000 copies.<ref name="napoli"/> The subsequent tour ended with a double date with Jazz guitarist [[Pat Metheny]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://video.repubblica.it/dossier/addio-pino-daniele/pat-metheny-fa-cantare-je-so-pazzo-alla-sua-chitarra/188091/186995?ref=tblv|title=Pat Metheny fa cantare 'Je so pazzo' alla sua chitarra|website=Video.repubblica.it|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref>

In 2010, Daniele played at [[Crossroads Guitar Festival]], called by his friend [[Eric Clapton]] at ''Toyota Park'' in Chicago, playing with [[Joe Bonamassa]] and [[Robert Randolph (guitarist)|Robert Randolph]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://video.repubblica.it/dossier/addio-pino-daniele/pino-daniele-con-joe-bonamassa-al-festival-di-eric-clapton/188090/186994|title=Pino Daniele con Joe Bonamassa al festival di Eric Clapton|website=Video.repubblica.it|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref> In 2011, he performed in concert with Clapton at [[Cava de' Tirreni]] stadium.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.panorama.it/musica/pino-daniele-ed-eric-clapton-quella-notte-cava-dei-tirreni|title=Pino Daniele ed Eric Clapton: quella notte a Cava dei Tirreni|website=Panorama.it|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref>

Daniele died of a heart attack on 4 January 2015, at [[Sant'Eugenio Hospital]] in Rome.<ref name="rainewsil"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundsblog.it/post/326364/pino-daniele-morto-infarto|title=Pino Daniele è morto|website=Soundsblog.it|date=5 January 2015|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ilsecoloxix.it/p/cultura/2015/01/05/ARYTOe5C-morto_pino_daniele.shtml|title=Annuncio di eros ramazzotti - E' morto Pino Daniele|website=Ilsecoloxix.it|date=2 August 2012|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref>

==Covers==
Many artists recorded cover versions of Pino Daniele's songs: among others, [[Sarah Jane Morris (singer)|Sarah Jane Morris]] (''Alleria'' on album [[Cello Song]]), [[Randy Crawford]] ({{lang|it|Quanno chiove}}, in English ''It's Raining'', on album [[Through the Eyes of Love]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Randy-Crawford-Through-The-Eyes-Of-Love/release/517627|title=Randy Crawford - Through The Eyes Of Love (CD, Album)|website=Discogs.com|date=29 December 2015|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref>), [[Patricia Marx]] ({{lang|it|Quanno chiove}}, in Portuguese {{lang|pt|Quando chove}}, on album [[Charme do mundo]]<ref>{{cite web|author=André Arteiro|url=http://fcsimplesmentepaty.blogspot.it/2015/01/morreu-pino-daniele-autor-de-quando.html|title=Fã-Clube Oficial Patricia Marx: MORREU PINO DANIELE, AUTOR DE "QUANDO CHOVE"|website=Fcsimplesmentepaty.blogspot.it|date=30 August 2013|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref>), [[Marisa Monte]] (''E po' che fa'', in Portuguese ''Bem que se quis'', on album [[MM (album)|MM]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://video.repubblica.it/dossier/addio-pino-daniele/pino-daniele-marisa-monte-rifa-e-po-che-fa-in-portoghese/188089/186993|title=Pino Daniele, Marisa Monte rifà 'E po' che fa' in portoghese|website=Video.repubblica.it|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref>), and [[Issac Delgado]] (Quando, on album Malecon<ref>{{cite web|author=Staff|url=https://www.amazon.com/Malec%C3%B3n-Issac-Delgado/dp/B004LP1YZ0|title=Issac Delgado profile|website=Amazon.com|access-date=10 January 2016}}</ref>).
