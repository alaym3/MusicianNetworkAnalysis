'''Parquet Courts''' is an American [[rock music|rock]] band from [[New York City]].<ref name="AMG">{{cite web|last=Thomas |first=Fred |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/parquet-courts-mn0003031551 |title=Parquet Courts - Music Biography, Credits and Discography |website=[[AllMusic]] |date=2013-01-15 |access-date=2013-02-28}}</ref> The band consists of Andrew Savage (vocals, guitar), Austin Brown (vocals, guitar, keyboard), Sean Yeaton (bass, vocals), and Max Savage (drums).

==History==
Band members Andrew Savage and Austin Brown met while both students at the [[University of North Texas]], in a club named Knights of the Round Turntable, where they listened to and shared new records. <ref>{{cite web |title=Alumni come together to form a 'band to watch' |url=https://northtexan.unt.edu/content/band-watch |website=Northtexan.unt.edu |access-date=26 May 2021}}</ref>

The band released their debut album, ''[[American Specialties]]'', as a limited cassette release in 2011.<ref name="AMG"/> The band's second studio album, ''[[Light Up Gold]]'' (2012), was initially released on Savage's Dull Tools label and later reissued on [[What's Your Rupture?]] in 2013.<ref name="AMG"/>  ''Light Up Gold'' received widespread critical acclaim in both the DIY underground and mainstream rock press.<ref>{{cite web|last=Thomas |first=Fred |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/light-up-gold-mw0002461612 |title=Light Up Gold - Parquet Courts : Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |website=[[AllMusic]] |date=2013-01-15 |access-date=2013-02-28}}</ref>

In 2014, the band reached #55 on the ''Billboard'' album chart with its third studio album, ''[[Sunbathing Animal]]''. Later in 2014, the band released "Uncast Shadow of a Southern Myth" as a single under an alternative name, Parkay Quarts. Soon afterward they released their fourth studio album, ''[[Content Nausea]]'', on which Sean and Max were absent due to other commitments.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefourohfive.com/news/article/parkay-quartz-parquet-courts-share-uncast-shadow-of-a-southern-myth-and-announce-new-album-content-nausea-141|title=Parkay Quartz (Parquet Courts) share 'Uncast Shadow Of A Southern Myth' and announce new album, 'Content Nausea'|work=The 405}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://whatsyourrupture.bigcartel.com/product/parquet-courts-content-nausea-preorder-cd-lp-2xcd|title=Whats Your Rupture?|website=Whatsyourrupture.bigcartel.com}}</ref>

The following year the band released a collaboration LP ''Ramsgate'' with PC Worship under the name PCPC. They also released a mostly instrumental, [[experimental (music)|experimental]] EP, entitled ''[[Monastic Living]]''.

On February 4, 2016, the band announced their fifth studio album, entitled ''[[Human Performance (album)|Human Performance]]''. The album was released on April 8 through [[Rough Trade Records|Rough Trade]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/63339-parquet-courts-announce-new-album-human-performance-expand-tour-share-dust-video/|title=Parquet Courts Announce New Album Human Performance, Expand Tour, Share "Dust" Video|website=Pitchfork.com}}</ref>

On October 13, 2017, the band's co-frontman A. Savage released his first solo album, ''[[Thawing Dawn]]'', through Dull Tools.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://asavage.bandcamp.com/album/thawing-dawn|title=Thawing Dawn, by A. Savage|website=Asavage.bandcamp.com|access-date=2017-11-10}}</ref> That same month the band released ''[[Milano (album) |Milano]]'', a collaboration album with [[Daniele Luppi]].

[[File:Parquet Courts at Solid Sound Festival (North Adams MA), 27 June 2015.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Parquet Courts at Solid Sound Festival, 2015]]

On May 18, 2018, the band released their sixth LP, ''[[Wide Awake!]]'' It was named "album of the year"  by Australian radio station [[Double J (radio)|Double J]].<ref name="double-j-best-2018">{{cite web |title=The 50 best albums of 2018 |url=https://www.abc.net.au/doublej/music-reads/features/the-50-best-albums-of-2018/10595676 |website=Abc.net.au|access-date=16 December 2018 |ref=double-j-best-2018}}</ref>

Their song, "Almost Had to Start a Fight/In and Out of Patience", is featured in the video game [[EA Sports]] [[NHL 19]], "One Man No City" is featured in an Episode of [[The Blacklist (TV series)|''The Blacklist'']],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt6769268/soundtrack|title="The Blacklist" The Debt Collector (No. 46) (TV Episode 2017) |website=IMDb.com|access-date=23 October 2021}}</ref> "Wide Awake" appeared as soundtrack in [[Konami]] football game, ''[[eFootball Pro Evolution Soccer 2020]]''.

The band's seventh full-length album, ''[[Sympathy for Life]]'', was released on October 22, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2021-08-18|title=Parquet Courts Announce New Album Sympathy for Life, Share Video for New Song|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/parquet-courts-announce-new-album-sympathy-for-life-share-video-for-new-song-watch/|access-date=2021-08-18|website=Pitchfork.com|language=en-US}}</ref>
