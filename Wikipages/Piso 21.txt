'''Piso 21''' is a [[Colombia]]n Latin pop group.  Their singles "[[Me Llamas]]", "[[Besándote]]" and "[[Déjala Que Vuelva]]" were hits in their home country, Latin America and Spain.

==Career==
Piso 21 is a Colombian group composed of Pablo Mejía (Pablito), Juan David Huertas (El Profe), David Escobar (Dim) and David Lorduy Hernández (Lorduy). With Colombian artist [[Llane|Juan David Castaño]] (El Llane) being one of its founding members. The name Piso 21 derives from an elevator in a building in [[Medellin]], [[Colombia]], the building had 21 floors.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/podcasts/7728624/latin-connection-podcast-piso-21-interview|title=Latin Connection Podcast: Piso 21 Talks Forthcoming Album, Maluma Collaboration & More|date=2017}}</ref>

The group formed in 2007, but it was not until 2012 that the group started receiving widespread recognition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.campuscircle.com/review.cfm?r=16584|title=Piso 21 Can Only Go Up From Here On|via=Campus Circle}}</ref> With songs like: "Vagones Vacíos", "Ángel Mortal" and "Correr El Riesgo", the group's fame in Colombia steadily grew.  Later in 2012, thanks to the success of their debut studio album ''Piso 21''. The group was nominated for Best New Artist in the Latin Grammy Awards that same year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://revistadiners.com.co/articulo/13_930_ellos-son-piso-21-los-colombianos-nominados-al-grammy-latino-2012|title=Piso 21, los nominados al Grammy Latino 2012|via=Revista Diners}}</ref>

In 2014, Piso 21 released the single "[[Suele Suceder]]" featuring Puerto Rican singer [[Nicky Jam]]. The song was a commercial success in Latin America and reached the top 20 in the [[Monitor Latino|Colombian Monitor Latino]] charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://monitorlatino.com/la-union-de-piso-21-ft-nicky-jam-logra-la-fuerza-para-entrar-al-top-20-de-monitorlatino-colombia/|title=La unión de Piso 21 ft Nicky Jam logra la fuerza para entrar al Top 20 de monitorLATINO Colombia|via=Monitor Latino}}</ref> The song also became the group's first charting single in the US charts built for Latin audiences by [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]], where it peaked at number 29 on the [[Tropical Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?f%5b0%5d=ts_chart_artistname%3APiso%2021&f%5b1%5d=itm_field_chart_id%3A374&f%5b2%5d=ss_bb_type%3Achart_item&type=2&artist=Piso%2021|title=Piso 21 _ Tropical Songs History|via=Billboardbiz}}</ref> The following year in 2015, the group released the singles: "Quítate La Pena", "Delicia" and "Hoy" featuring Chilean singer Florencia Arenas.

In 2016, Piso 21 signed with [[Warner Music Latina]], and later on in the year, released the single "[[Me Llamas]]", which ended up reaching the top 40 in several countries throughout Latin America and became their most successful single to date at the time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://boomonline.com/en/espanol-piso-21-firma-con-warner-music/|title=Piso 21 Firma Con Warner Music|via=Boom Online|access-date=2017-09-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180623182740/http://boomonline.com/en/espanol-piso-21-firma-con-warner-music/|archive-date=2018-06-23|url-status=dead}}</ref> On 2 December 2016, an official remix featuring Colombian singer [[Maluma (singer)|Maluma]] was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/me-llamas-feat-maluma-remix/id1170626646?i=1170626959|title=Me Llamas (Maluma Remix)|via=iTunes}}</ref> The remix helped further add to the song's momentum.

In early 2017, they released the single "[[Besándote]]" which like their previous single, ended up becoming a widespread commercial success, reaching the top 40 in several countries in Latin America. On 1 September 2017, the group released an official remix featuring British singer [[Anne-Marie (singer)|Anne-Marie]] to celebrate their [[YouTube]] channel surpassing over a million subscribers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.national-report.com/piso-21-celebran-su-primer-millon-de-suscriptores-incursionando-en-el-mercado-anglo/|title=Piso 21 Celebran Su Orimer Millon De Suscriptores Incursionado En El Mercado Anglo|via=National Report}}</ref> On 19 October 2017, they released the single "[[Déjala Que Vuelva]]" featuring [[Manuel Turizo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kienyke.com/kienyke-fm/dejala-que-vuelva-lo-ultimo-de-piso-21-con-manuel-turizo|title='Déjala que vuelva', lo nuevo de Piso 21 con Manuel Turizo|via=Boom Online|date=2017-10-20}}</ref> The song became an instant success across Latin America, where it topped multiple [[Spotify]] charts in the region and became certified Gold in Colombia just merely two weeks after its release. On 10 November 2017, they released the single "Tu Héroe".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://veromerol.com.mx/piso-21-tu-heroe|title=Piso 21 "Tu Héroe" (Estreno del Sencillo)|website=veromerol.com|date=2017-11-13}}</ref> Which later ended up peaking at number seven on the Paraguayan Monitor Latino charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://charts.monitorlatino.com/top20/Paraguay/General/20180305|publisher=[[Monitor Latino]]|access-date=12 March 2018|title=Top 20 Paraguay – Del 5 al 11 de Marzo, 2018|language=es|date=5 March 2018}}</ref> The song was then followed by the single "Adrenalina" on 1 December 2017, which featured Spanish rapper Maikel Delacalle.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://veromerol.com.mx/piso-21-adrenalina|title=Piso 21 "Adrenalina" ft. Maikel Delacalle (Estreno del Sencillo)|website=veromerol.com|date=2017-12-05}}</ref>

In early 2018, Piso 21 collaborated with Argentinian singer [[Paulo Londra]] on the single "[[Te Amo (Piso 21 and Paulo Londra song)|Te Amo]]", which was released on 15 March 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/es/album/te-amo-single/1356305067|title=Te Amo - Single|via=iTunes}}</ref> On 11 May 2018 the group released the single "La Vida Sin Ti" to coincide with the release of their second studio album ''[[Ubuntu (album)|Ubuntu]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oxigeno.fm/2018/piso-21-presenta-primer-trabajo-discografico-titulado-ubuntu-198927.html|title=Piso 21 presenta su primer trabajo discográfico titulado "Ubuntu"|website=oxigeno.fm|date=2018-05-11}}</ref> Later in the year, on 14 September 2018, they released the song "Punto Suspensivos", as the eighth single from their second studio album ''Ubuntu''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bnmu12rnizN/|title=14/09/18 #PuntosSuspensivos...|publisher=[[Instagram]]|access-date=13 September 2018|language=es}}</ref> The group then released the single "Te Vi" with Venezuelan singer Micro TDH on 14 December 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8490279/viva-friday-playlist-piso-21-maluma|title=Viva Friday: Best Music Picks Of the Week by Piso 21, Maluma and More|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Jessica|last=Roiz|date=14 December 2018|access-date=9 March 2019}}</ref>

In early 2019, on the 3 February, one of the founding members of the group Juan David Castaño (El Llane), announced his departure from the group to pursue a career as a solo artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8496249/llane-leaves-piso-21|title=Llane Announces He's Leaving Piso 21 With Heartfelt Video Message: Watch|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Jessica|last=Roiz|date=4 February 2019|access-date=9 March 2019}}</ref> He was subsequently replaced by the up-and-coming Colombian artist David Lorduy Hernández (Lorduy).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/j-balvin-maluma-more-send-llane-support-after-his-split-with-piso-21-137044|title=J Balvin, Maluma & more send Llane support after his split with Piso 21|work=AXS|first=Lucas|last=Villa|date=25 February 2019|access-date=9 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/JhonJairoSilva/status/1092410706937499648|title=J Balvin, Maluma & more send Llane support after his split with Piso 21|work=Twitter|first=Jhon Jairo|last=Silva|date=4 February 2019|access-date=9 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190310033414/https://twitter.com/JhonJairoSilva/status/1092410706937499648|archive-date=9 March 2019}}</ref>

The group has toured extensively in South America and overseas.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.piso21music.com/index.php/biografia|title=Biografia|date=2012|access-date=2016-12-20|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180309115648/http://www.piso21music.com/index.php/biografia|archive-date=2018-03-09|url-status=dead}}</ref>
