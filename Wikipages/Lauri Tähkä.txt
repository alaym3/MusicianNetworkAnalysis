'''Jarkko Tapani Suo''', professionally known as '''Lauri Tähkä'''<ref>[http://www.iltasanomat.fi/viihde/uutinen.asp?id=1303229 Lauri Tähkä erosi vaimostaan - Viihde - Ilta-Sanomat<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> (born November 9, 1973 in [[Vaasa]], [[Finland]]), is a [[Finland|Finnish]] [[musician]] and recording artist. He was a leading member in the former band [[Lauri Tähkä & Elonkerjuu]].

Growing up he listened to [[Kiss (band)|Kiss]], [[Iron Maiden]], the Finnish band [[Eppu Normaali]], the works of [[Ismo Alanko]] as well as [[Kauko Röyhkä]]. Tähkä found his musical identity in a cross between rock and national ballads. With his cousin Simo Ralli he created "Niin kauan minä tramppaan" (I'll trek as long as), which won the "Lapuan Spelit" competition.

The Elonkerjuu band's home base was [[Teuva]], Finland. Currently Lauri Tähkä is making a solo career. His first album, ''[[Polte]]'', was released in October, 2011.
