'''Trevor Something''' is the alias of an anonymous American musician known for creating [[synthwave]] music. He creates both original music as well as remixed music through the sampling and mixing of music by other artists.<ref name=mcmillan>{{cite news |last=McMillan |first=Aaron |date=7 July 2014 |title=New Album Stream: TREVOR SOMETHING – "Trevor Something Does Not Exist" (Alternative Synth-Pop/New Wave) |url=http://indieunderground.ca/indie-music/new-album-stream-trevor-something-trevor-something-does-not-exist-alternative-synth-popnew-wave.html |publisher=Indie Underground |access-date=1 September 2016 }}</ref>

== Recording career ==
Trevor Something's first single, 'All Night' was released on September 11, 2013. His first [[LP album|LP]], titled 'Synthetic Love' was released on January 28, 2014. It was followed up with his first mixtape, 'Trevor Something Does Not Exist' and two EPs, 'Distorted Reality' and 'Lost Memories' later in 2014. His second and third LPs, 'Death Dream' and 'Soulless Computer Boy and The Eternal Render' debuted in 2015 and 2016 respectively.<ref>{{cite news |last=Galetic |first=Filip |date=26 October 2015 |title=Trevor Something - Death Dream |url=http://www.newretrowave.com/album-reviews/2015/10/26/trevor-something-death-dream |publisher=NewRetroWave |access-date=2 September 2016 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Edsall |first=Joey |date=18 August 2016 |title=Trevor Something - Soulless Computer Boy and The Eternal Render |url=http://www.newretrowave.com/album-reviews/2016/8/18/trevor-something-soulless-computer-boy-and-the-eternal-render |publisher=NewRetroWave |access-date=2 September 2016 }}</ref> On May 19, 2017, his fourth LP, 'Die With You' was released, featuring a 2005 illustration "The Last Embrace" by artist Laurie Lipton as its cover art.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://trevorsomething.bandcamp.com/album/die-with-you|title=Die With You|website=[[Bandcamp]]|author=Trevor Something|access-date=12 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laurielipton.com/2001-2005/|title=2001-2005|author=Laurie Lipton|website=laurielipton.com|access-date=12 May 2017}}</ref> The third EP from Trevor Something, 'Lost Love' was released on June 21, 2018. It was followed by his fifth LP, 'ULTRAPARANOIA' on October 12, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://trevorsomething.bandcamp.com/album/ultraparanoia|title=ULTRAPARANOIA - Trevor Something |website=[[Bandcamp]]|author=Trevor Something|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref> His sixth LP, titled 'Bots Don't Cry' was released February 15, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://trevorsomething.bandcamp.com/album/bots-dont-cry|website=[[Bandcamp]]|author=Trevor Something|title=Bots Don't Cry, by Trevor Something|access-date=8 March 2019}}</ref> On July 19, 2019, Trevor Something debuted his fourth and most recent EP, 'Escape'. His seventh LP, 'microwaves' was released on April 17, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://trevorsomething.bandcamp.com/album/microwaves|title=Microwaves |website=[[Bandcamp]]|author=Trevor Something|access-date=17 April 2020}}</ref> It was quickly followed up with his eighth LP, 'Deep Wave Data Dark Web Daemons' on May 8, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://trevorsomething.bandcamp.com/album/deep-wave-data-dark-web-daemons |title=Deep Wave Data Dark Web Daemons - Trevor Something |website=Bandcamp |author=Trevor Something |access-date=26 May 2020}}</ref> Trevor's ninth and latest LP, 'Love Me and Leave Me' debuted on June 4, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://trevorsomething.bandcamp.com/album/love-me-and-leave-me |title=Love Me and Leave Me - Trevor Something |website=Bandcamp |author=Trevor Something |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref>
