'''Ellinor Miranda Salome Olovsdotter''' (born 8 October 1985), known professionally as '''Elliphant''', is a Swedish singer, songwriter and rapper. Elliphant's sound was initially created together with the Swedish production duo Jungle, which consists of Tim Deneve and Ted Krotkiewski.<ref name="Jungle biography">{{cite web|title=Jungle Biography|url=http://www.ten.se/artist/jungle/|access-date=23 May 2014}}</ref> The music they created together caught the attention of [[TEN Music Group]], to which Elliphant signed in 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ten.se/artist/elliphant/ |title=Elliphant's Biography |access-date=23 May 2014}}</ref> Elliphant also supported [[Major Lazer]], the side project of American producer and DJ [[Diplo]], on their 2015 European tour.

==Life and career==
===Early life and career beginnings===
Elliphant was born Ellinor Miranda Salome Olovsdotter in [[Stockholm]], Sweden, to an [[Icelandic people|Icelandic]] family.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/Catalog.aspx?detail=writerid&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&keyid=1407956&subid=0 |title=Songwriter/Composer: Olovsdotter Ellinor Miranda Salome |website=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.|BMI]] |access-date=10 June 2018}}</ref><ref name="Pigeonsandplanes">{{cite web |url=https://pigeonsandplanes.com/in-depth/2013/11/interview-elliphant |title=Interview: Elliphant on Sweden, Diplo, Skrillex and her Debut album |website=[[Pigeons & Planes]] |date=7 November 2013 |access-date=10 June 2018}}</ref> In 2011, Elliphant was at a party in [[Paris]] when she met producer Tim Deneve, who needed someone that could sing and help him with his demos. Six months later, she signed to [[TEN Music Group]].<ref name="billboard">{{cite web |last=Lipshutz |first=Jason |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6281653/elliphant-five-wild-facts |title=Who Is Elliphant? Five Wild Facts About The Swedish Pop Prospect |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=14 October 2014 |access-date=11 August 2015}}</ref>

===2012–2013: ''Elliphant'' and ''A Good Idea''===
[[File:Elliphant live in 2016.jpg|thumb|Elliphant at Bitterzoet, Amsterdam in 2016]]
Elliphant released her debut single "Tekkno Scene" in 2012. The track, which features rapper Adam Kanyama, was positively received and was featured in the video game ''[[FIFA 13]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fifasoundtrack.com/fifa13soundtrack.htm |title=FIFA 13 Soundtrack |publisher=FIFA Soundtrack |access-date=28 February 2014 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130425224519/http://www.fifasoundtrack.com/fifa13soundtrack.htm |archive-date=25 April 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The song earned comparisons to [[M.I.A. (artist)|M.I.A.]] and [[Diplo]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Snapes |first=Laura |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/13481-tekkno-scene-ft-adam-kanyama/ |title=Elliphant: "Tekkno Scene" [ft. Adam Kanyama] |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |date=18 April 2012 |access-date=28 February 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Skotarczyk |first=Rachel Lynn |url=http://www.indieshuffle.com/elliphant-tekkno-scene-ft-adam-kanyama-2/ |title=[LISTEN] Elliphant – Tekkno Scene (Ft Adam Kanyama) |publisher=Indie Shuffle |date=4 May 2012 |access-date=28 February 2014}}</ref> In October 2012, Elliphant released her eponymous debut [[extended play]] on A Bigger Splash Records, including the singles "Tekkno Scene" and "Down on Life".<ref>{{cite web |last=Snapes |first=Laura |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/17695-elliphant-ep/ |title=Elliphant: Elliphant EP |website=Pitchfork |date=31 January 2013 |access-date=28 February 2014}}</ref>

Following the release of her first EP, Elliphant stated in an interview with [[Idolator (website)|Idolator]] in July 2013 that she had signed to [[Dr. Luke]]'s label [[Kemosabe Records]] and that her Swedish debut studio album would be titled ''A Good Idea''.<ref>{{cite web |last=Pyarali |first=Adam |url=http://www.idolator.com/7468081/elliphant-interview |title=Meet Elliphant: Sweden's Most Unconventional Pop Star |website=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]] |date=2 July 2013 |access-date=28 February 2014}}</ref> The album was released in her native Sweden on 9 October 2013,<ref name="AGoodIdea">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/se/album/a-good-idea/id712022324 |title=A Good Idea av Elliphant |website=[[iTunes Store]] (Sweden) |language=sv |access-date=28 February 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140726234556/https://itunes.apple.com/se/album/a-good-idea/id712022324 |archive-date=26 July 2014}}</ref> and features a number of artists and producers, including Swedish duo [[Niki and the Dove]], who are featured on the track "More Fire".

===2014–present: ''Look Like You Love It'', ''One More'' and ''Living Life Golden''===
[[File:Elliphant 2014.jpg|thumb|Elliphant performing in 2014]]
Elliphant released her second EP, titled ''Look Like You Love It'', in April 2014 via Kemosabe Records and [[Mad Decent]], featuring production from Dr. Luke, Diplo, [[Skrillex]] and [[Dave Sitek]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.idolator.com/7514361/elliphant-look-like-you-love-it-ep-diplo-skrillex |title=Stream Elliphant's Star-Studded 'Look Like You Love It' EP |website=Idolator |date=15 April 2014 |access-date=11 August 2015}}</ref> It was followed by yet another EP, ''One More'', in October, whose [[One More (Elliphant song)|title track]] features Danish singer [[MØ]].<ref name="billboard"/> Elliphant was one of the opening acts on [[Charli XCX]]'s [[Girl Power North America Tour]] in September and October 2014.<ref>{{cite web |last=Zentner |first=Emily |url=http://www.statepress.com/article/2014/10/not-beyonc-but-charli-xcx-brings-girl-power-to-phoenix/ |title=Not Beyoncé, but Charli XCX brings girl power to Phoenix |work=[[State Press]] |date=21 October 2014 |access-date=11 August 2015}}</ref>

In early July 2015, it was announced that Elliphant's second studio album and North American debut album, ''Living Life Golden'', would be released on 25 September by Kemosabe Records.<ref>{{cite web |last=Williott |first=Carl |url=http://www.idolator.com/7599697/elliphant-debut-album-living-life-golden |title=Elliphant Announces Debut Album 'Living Life Golden' |website=Idolator |date=8 July 2015 |access-date=9 August 2015}}</ref> However, it was reported in late August 2015 that the album's release had been pushed back to an unspecified date.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4299846.htm |title=Azealia Banks, Diplo, Big Freedia to feature on Elliphant's (unfortunately delayed) new album |website=[[Triple J]] |date=25 August 2015 |access-date=3 October 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150828033227/http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4299846.htm |archive-date=28 August 2015}}</ref> In December 2015, it was announced that the album would be released on 25 March 2016.<ref name="LivingLifeGolden">{{cite web |last=Gordon |first=Jeremy |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/62617-elliphant-teams-with-twin-shadow-on-new-song-where-is-home/ |title=Elliphant Teams With Twin Shadow on New Song "Where Is Home" |website=Pitchfork |date=18 December 2015 |access-date=19 December 2015}}</ref> At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2017]] the [[ARIA Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] category was won by "[[Stranger (Peking Duk song)|Stranger]]" featuring Elliphant.<ref name="McCabe">{{cite news | url = http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/awards/arias/2017-aria-awards-winners-revealed-as-it-happens/news-story/d9ab3f0270ef1a7dbac5e96a15492d19 | title = 2017 ARIA Awards: All the action on music's big night | first1 = Kathy | last1 = McCabe | first2 = Katrina | last2 = Sichlau | first3 = Zoe | last3 = Smith | work = news.com.au | publisher = [[News Corp Australia]] | date = 28 November 2017 | access-date = 29 November 2017 }}</ref> As one of the track's song writers, Elliphant also won Dance Work of the Year and Most Played Australian Work at the [[APRA Music Awards of 2018]].<ref name="APRAWinners 2018">{{cite web | title = Full List of Winners | url = http://apraamcos.com.au/awards/2018-awards/apra-music-awards/full-list-of-winners/ | publisher = [[Australasian Performing Right Association]] (APRA) &#124; [[Australasian Mechanical Copyright Owners Society]] (AMCOS) | year = 2018 | access-date = 13 April 2018 }}</ref>

In 2018, the song "Everybody" was featured at the end of the film ''[[The Spy Who Dumped Me]]''. Later in 2018, a new Elliphant song "To the End" was featured in the film ''[[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse]]'', serving as the introduction music for [[Spider-Woman (Gwen Stacy)|Spider-Woman]]. The song was released in January 2019.<ref name="PMStudio">{{cite web |url=https://www.pmstudio.com/music/music.html?page=20190129-8774 |title=Elliphant Premieres New Song "To The End" from Computer Animated Film "Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse" |website=PM Studio|date=29 January 2019 |access-date=27 August 2020}}</ref>

She released her third studio album ''Rocking House'' on April 2021, preceded by the singles "Uterus", "Had Enough", "Time Machine", "White Tiger", "Could This Be Love", "Drunk & Angry" and "Notorious".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.mxdwn.com/2021/04/12/news/elliphant-announces-new-album-rocking-horse-for-april-2021-release/|title=Elliphant Announces New Album Rocking Horse for April 2021 Release|access-date=2021-05-02|first=Ariel|last=King|date=2021-04-12|work=Mxdwn}}</ref>
