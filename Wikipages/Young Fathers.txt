'''Young Fathers''' are a Scottish band based in [[Edinburgh]], [[Scotland]]. In 2014, they won the [[Barclaycard Mercury Prize|Mercury Prize]] for their album ''[[Dead (Young Fathers album)|Dead]]''.

==History==
Formed in Edinburgh in 2008 by Alloysious Massaquoi, Kayus Bankole and Graham 'G' Hastings, the group started performing in nightclubs when the band members were all in their teens.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/apr/04/young-fathers-tour-south-africa|title=Young Fathers Tour South Africa |first=Paul |last=MacInnes |date=4 April 2015 |work=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref>

In 2012, they signed to LA-based label [[Anticon]] and released their introductory mixtapes, ''[[Tape One]]'' and ''[[Tape Two]]'', with ''[[Tape Two]]'' winning the [[Scottish Album of the Year Award]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/arts_ents/13166318.SAY_Award_winners_Young_Fathers_talk_friendship__hip_hop_and_civil_war|title=SAY Award winners Young Fathers talk friendship, hip hop and civil war |first=Barry |last=Didcock |date=20 June 2014 |work=[[The Herald (Glasgow)|The Herald]] |access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref> ("The SAY Award").

The trio then signed to [[Big Dada]] and released their debut album, ''[[Dead (Young Fathers album)|Dead]]'', which was released in 2014. The album gained a lot of critical attention and went on to win the prestigious [[Mercury Prize]]. ''[[Dead (Young Fathers album)|Dead]]'' entered the UK albums chart at the 35th position and topped the independent UK album chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/oct/29/mercury-2014-young-fathers-win|title=Mercury prize 2014: Young Fathers defy the odds to win |first=Hannah |last=Ellis-Petersen |date=30 October 2014 |work=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref>

Following an extensive world tour the band decamped to [[Berlin]] to begin work on their second album, ''[[White Men Are Black Men Too]]'', which was released in April 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/dec/08/best-albums-of-2015-no-9-white-men-are-black-men-too-young-fathers|title=Best albums of 2015: No 9 – White Men Are Black Men Too by Young Fathers |first=Paul |last=MacInnes |date=8 December 2015 |work=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref>
 
In June 2017 Young Fathers played in the Royal Festival Hall at the [[Southbank Centre]] as part of [[M.I.A. (rapper)|M.I.A.]]'s [[Meltdown (festival)|Meltdown Festival]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/live/soulwax-young-fathers-to-play-mias-meltdown|title=Soulwax, Young Fathers To Play M.I.A.'s Meltdown |first=Robin |last=Murray |date=19 April 2017 |work=[[Clash Magazine]]|access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref>

Six tracks on the ''[[T2 Trainspotting]]'' soundtrack feature Young Fathers, including "Only God Knows", written specifically for the film. In a statement the director [[Danny Boyle]] described the song as "the heartbeat for the film".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/71103-young-fathers-share-new-track-only-god-knows-from-t2-trainspotting-listen|title=Young Fathers Share New Track "Only God Knows" From T2 Trainspotting |first=Evan |last=Minsker |date=24 January 2017 |work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1919124/trainspotting-2-soundtrack-revealed/news|title=Trainspotting 2 Soundtrack Revealed|first=Collin |last=Robinson |date=10 January 2017 |work=[[Stereogum]]|access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref>

Young Fathers' third studio album, ''[[Cocoa Sugar]],'' was announced with the single "In My View" on 17 January 2018. The album was released on British independent label [[Ninja Tune]] on 9 March 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/young-fathers-announce-new-album-cocoa-sugar-share-new-single-view-2221003|title=Young Fathers announce new album 'Cocoa Sugar' and share new single ‘In My View’|first=Rhian |last=Daly |date=17 January 2017 |work=[[NME]]|access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref> The album entered the UK Albums Chart at number 28, making it the band's highest-charting album.<ref name="UK"/>

While not an overtly political band, Young Fathers have spoken about issues such as racism and the treatment of refugees<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-02-25|title=Young Fathers: ‘Everybody has a dark side. We’re all complicit…’|url=http://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/feb/25/young-fathers-interview-cocoa-sugar|access-date=2021-09-05|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref> Due to their support of the [[BDS movement]], the band was dropped from the 2018 [[Ruhrtriennale]] line-up.<ref>{{cite news|author=Alex Marshall |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/07/01/arts/music/bds-germany-young-fathers.html |title=Unwelcome Sound on Germany’s Stages: Musicians Who Boycott Israel |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |access-date=2020-04-05}}</ref>

The band was featured on the FIFA2019 soundtrack. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bigtop40.com/playlists/fifa-19-soundtrack-playlist/ |title=FIFA 19 Soundtrack: This Year's Complete Playlist |website=BigTop40.com |date=2018-09-11 |access-date=2020-04-05}}</ref>

==Members==
* '''Alloysious Massaquoi''' was born in [[Liberia]] and moved to Edinburgh at the age of four, where he attended [[Boroughmuir High School]].
* '''Kayus Bankole''' was born in Edinburgh to Nigerian parents. He spent several years living in [[Maryland]] and Nigeria before moving back to the city of his birth, in his teens. He went on to attend [[Boroughmuir High School]] in Edinburgh, the same school as Alloysious and the two became close friends.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.edinburghnews.scotsman.com/what-s-on/music/young-fathers-take-on-the-world-after-mercury-win-1-3589930 |title=Young Fathers take on the world after Mercury win |first=Gina |last=Davidson |newspaper=[[Edinburgh Evening News]] |date=31 October 2014 |access-date=4 February 2015}}</ref> Bankole has commented that Scotland's history in relation to slavery<ref>{{Cite web|title=Slavery|url=https://www.nls.uk/collections/topics/slavery/|access-date=2021-09-05|website=National Library of Scotland|language=en}}</ref> should be taught in schools to help overcome systemic racism<ref>{{Cite web|title=Young Fathers star: Scotland's slavery past should be taught in every school|url=https://www.scotsman.com/whats-on/arts-and-entertainment/young-fathers-star-scotlands-slavery-past-should-be-taught-every-school-2887781|access-date=2021-09-05|website=www.scotsman.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Young Fathers star shines a light on Scotland and slavery|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/18128418.message-skies-young-fathers-star-kayus-bankole-shines-light-scotland-slavery/|access-date=2021-09-05|website=HeraldScotland|language=en}}</ref>
* '''Graham "G" Hastings''' was born in Edinburgh and grew up in the North Edinburgh housing scheme of [[Drylaw]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2012/09/28/young-fathers-deadline-mp3/ |title=Young Fathers "Deadline" MP3 |work=[[The Fader]] |first=Amber |last=Bravo |date=28 September 2012 |access-date=4 February 2015}}</ref>
