'''Florist''' is an American [[Folk music|folk]] band from [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]].

==History==
Florist began in 2013 with the release of a six-track EP titled ''We Have Been This Way Forever''.<ref name="thedelimagazine">{{cite web|title=Dream folk from Brooklyn: Florist plays Silent Barn on 02.20|url=http://nyc.thedelimagazine.com/20642/dream-folk-from-brooklyn-florist-plays-silent-barn-on-0220|publisher=[[The Deli Magazine]]|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref> Florist recorded another six-song EP, titled ''6 days of songs'', in May 2014, after lead vocalist Emily Sprague was severely injured in a hit-and-run while riding her bicycle.<ref name="thelesigh">{{cite web|last1=Trewin|first1=Madalyn|title=EP: FLORIST - 6 DAYS OF SONGS|url=http://www.thelesigh.com/2014/07/ep-florist-6-days-of-songs.html|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref><ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web|last1=Jayasuriya|first1=Mehan|title=Florist: The Birds Outside Sang|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/21445-the-birds-outside-sang/|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref> In October 2015, Florist released another EP titled ''Holdly'' on [[Double Double Whammy]].<ref name="spinmagazine">{{cite web|last1=Joyce|first1=Colin|title=Florist's New Single Is as 'Cool and Refreshing' as Its Title Suggests|url=http://www.spin.com/2015/10/florist-cool-and-refreshing-single-stream-holdly/|publisher=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref><ref name="ghettoblaster">{{cite web|last1=Anderl|first1=Timothy|title=Florist ready EP for Double Double Whammy, tour (2015)|url=http://ghettoblastermagazine.com/2015/florist-ready-ep-for-double-double-whammy-tour-2015/|publisher=Ghettoblaster Magazine|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref><ref name="stereogum">{{cite web|last1=Rettig|first1=James|title=Stream Florist Holdly EP|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1840301/stream-florist-holdly-ep/mp3s/|publisher=[[Stereogum]]|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref> Also in 2015, the band was listed on Stereogum's "50 Best New Bands Of 2015" list.<ref name="stereogum1">{{cite web|title=Stereogum's 50 Best New Bands Of 2015|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1838310/stereogums-50-best-new-bands-of-2015/franchises/list/2/|publisher=Stereogum|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref> The band was also featured as one of Stereogum's "Band To Watch" in 2015.<ref name="stereogumdotcom">{{cite web|last1=White|first1=Caitlin|title=Band To Watch: Florist|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1835369/band-to-watch-florist/franchises/interview/|publisher=Stereogum|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref>

In January 2016, Florist released their debut studio album titled ''The Birds Outside Sang'' on Double Double Whammy.<ref name="pitchfork"/><ref name="stereogum2">{{cite web|last1=Rettig|first1=James|title=Stream Florist The Birds Outside Sang|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1854757/stream-florist-the-birds-outside-sang/mp3s/|publisher=Stereogum|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref><ref name="spinmag">{{cite web|last1=Joyce|first1=Colin|title=Florist Make More Indie-Pop Naturalism on 'The Birds Outside Sang'|url=http://www.spin.com/2015/11/florist-the-birds-outside-sang-stream-premiere/|publisher=Spin|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref> The album was listed at number 34 on Noisey's "100 Best Albums of 2016" list.<ref name="noiseyvice">{{cite web|title=The 100 Best Albums of 2016|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/the-100-best-albums-of-2016|publisher=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref><ref name="nytimes">{{cite web|last1=Caramanica|first1=Jon|title=Review: Florist Offers Skeletal Songs That Find Strength in Damage|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/01/28/arts/music/review-florist-offers-skeletal-songs-that-find-strength-in-damage.html?_r=0|work=[[New York Times]]|accessdate=27 February 2017}}</ref>

Sprague is an avid [[modular synthesizer]] collector and has made a number of YouTube videos showcasing her equipment. She self-released her debut solo album ''Water Memory,'' an [[Ambient music|ambient]] album composed with these instruments, in December 2017 with a follow-up album "Mount Vision" released in September 2018.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sherburne|first1=Philip|title=Emily A. Sprague: Water Memory|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/emily-a-sprague-water-memory/|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|accessdate=7 March 2018}}</ref>

In 2019, [[Beyoncé]] used an instrumental portion of Florist track "Thank You" from their 2016 release ''The Birds Outside Sang'' in her Netflix concert movie ''[[Homecoming (2019 film)|Homecoming]].''

Florist's latest album, ''Emily Alone'', was released on July 26th, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dbldblwhmmy.com/products/643797-florist-emily-alone|title=Double Double Whammy|website=www.dbldblwhmmy.com|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref> It was granted "Best New Music" by ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]].''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/florist-emily-alone/|title=Florist: Emily Alone|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref>

==Band members==
*Emily Sprague – lead vocals, guitar
*Rick Spataro – bass guitar, keyboards, synthesizers, backing vocals
*Jonnie Baker – guitar, keyboards, synthesizers
*Felix Walworth – drums
