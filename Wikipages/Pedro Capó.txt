'''Pedro Capó''' (born 14 November 1980) is a Puerto Rican musician, singer, songwriter and actor, who is a grandson of Puerto Rican singer [[Bobby Capó]] and former Miss Puerto Rico [[Irma Nydia Vázquez]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/pedro-cap%C3%B3-mn0001538324/biography|title=Pedro Capó {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=12 October 2017}}</ref> He studied at Colegio Calasanz in Río Piedras, Puerto Rico.

Pedro picked up the guitar at an early age and quickly became proficient in the instrument, later playing guitar in and becoming the main voice of the group Marka Registrada.<ref>{{cite web | title=Marka Registrada Myspace |url=http://www.myspace.com/markaregistrada| work=Myspace | access-date=26 May 2009}}{{Primary source inline|date=April 2019}}</ref>

Pedro resided in New York, where he starred in musical productions including: [[Apollo Theatre (42nd Street)|Apollo Theatre]]'s production of ''The Sweet Spot'' (New York City) and the Off-Broadway hit musical production of ''CELIA: The Life and Music of Celia Cruz''.<ref>{{cite web | title=Azúcar! Celia: The Life and Music of Celia Cruz at the New World Theater |url=http://www.brooklynrail.org/2008/03/music/azcar-celia-the-life-and-music-of-celia-cruz-at-the-new-world-theater| access-date=26 May 2009}}</ref> He has also starred in the films ''Shut Up And Do It''<ref>{{cite web | title=Shut Up And Do It IMdb|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1058564/| publisher = IMDb | access-date=26 May 2009}}</ref> and ''Paraiso Travel'', which is directed by Simón Brand and co-stars John Leguizamo.<ref>{{cite web | title=Pedro Capo Filmography in Fandango |url=http://www.fandango.com/pedrocapo/filmography/p580862 | publisher = Fandango | access-date=26 May 2009}}</ref>

In 2009, he performed a duet with popular Mexican singer [[Thalía]] called "[[Estoy Enamorado (Donato & Estéfano song)|Estoy Enamorado]]" from her new album ''[[Primera Fila (Thalía album)|Primera Fila]]'' and another duet with the Puerto Rican singer [[Kany García]] called "Si Tú Me Lo Pides".

In 2017, Capó joined multiple artists such as [[Jennifer Lopez]], [[Gloria Estefan]], and [[Rita Moreno]] on [[Lin-Manuel Miranda]]'s "[[Almost Like Praying]]". All proceeds from the song benefited the [[Hispanic Federation]] and its efforts to help those in Puerto Rico affected by [[Hurricane Maria]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vox.com/culture/2017/10/6/16435920/almost-like-praying-video-lin-manuel-miranda|title=Lin-Manuel Miranda releases "Almost Like Praying," a star-studded new single for Puerto Rico relief|work=Vox|access-date=12 October 2017}}</ref>

In 2018, after much time in the acting industry, Capó returned to music with a single called "[[Calma (song)|Calma]]". After Farruko joined him for the remix, the music video became a huge success with more than 1.5 billion views on YouTube as of August 2019. The singer was also awarded his first Latin Grammy Award for Best Long Form Music Video for "Pedro Capó: En Letra de Otro".

==Awards==

Capó has won two ASCAP Awards, for "Fiebre de Amor" (2013) and "La Mordidita" (2016).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latintimes.com/ascap-latin-music-awards-2016-gente-de-zona-natalia-lafourcade-more-perform-ceremony-383797|title=Gente De Zona, Natalia Lafourcade, More To Perform at ASCAP Awards|date=5 May 2016|website=Latin Times|access-date=14 January 2019}}</ref> He is also a Latin Grammy Award winner in the category of Best Long Form Music Video for "Pedro Capó: En Letra de Otro".
