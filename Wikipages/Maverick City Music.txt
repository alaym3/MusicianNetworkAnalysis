'''Maverick City Music''' is an American [[contemporary worship music]] collective and record label founded by Tony Brown and Jonathan Jay,<ref name="Relevant The Rise of Maverick">{{cite web |url= https://relevantmagazine.com/magazine/the-rise-of-maverick-city-music/ |title= The Rise of Maverick City Music {{!}} RELEVANT |last= Huckabee |first= Tyler |date= September 1, 2020 |website= [[Relevant (magazine)|Relevant]] |accessdate= October 11, 2020}}</ref> originating from [[Atlanta|Atlanta, Georgia]]. The collective made its debut in 2019 with the release of two extended plays, ''Maverick City, Vol. 1'' and ''Maverick City, Vol. 2''. In 2020, Maverick City Music released two live albums: ''[[Maverick City Vol. 3 Part 1]]'' and ''[[Maverick City, Vol. 3 Pt. 2]]'', with the former featuring the singles, "[[Man of Your Word]]" and "[[Promises (Maverick City Music song)|Promises]]". The collective later released their first collaborative EP with Upperroom, ''You Hold It All Together'' (2020), followed by the holiday-themed EP, ''Maverick City Christmas'' (2020).

In 2021, Maverick City Music released their second collaborative EP with Upperroom, ''[[Move Your Heart]]''. This was followed by another EP titled ''[[Jubilee (EP)|Jubilee]]'' (2021). Maverick City Music released their first Spanish-language album, ''Como En El Cielo'' (2021), which featured the single "Acercame". They also released a collaborative album with [[Elevation Worship]] titled ''[[Old Church Basement]]'', which contained the hit promotional singles "[[Jireh (song)|Jireh]]", "[[Talking to Jesus]]" and "[[Wait on You]]".

==History==
Maverick City Music was launched in 2018, after hosting seventeen songwriting camps which brought together over one hundred Christian artists and songwriters and the result being the creation of over one hundred songs.<ref name="NRT Profile">{{cite web |url= https://www.newreleasetoday.com/artistdetail.php?artist_id=6493 |title= Maverick City Music Artist Profile {{!}} Biography And Discography {{!}} NewReleaseToday |last= Ford |first= Bradden |date= November 11, 2019 |website= NewReleaseToday |accessdate= October 11, 2020}}</ref><ref name="A Step FWD">{{cite web |url= https://astepfwd.com/maverick-city-music-a-new-sound-in-worship-music/ |title= Maverick City Music: A New sound in worship music {{!}} Step FWD UK Christian Chart |last= Williams |first= Savannah |date= May 23, 2020 |website= The Christian Beat |accessdate= October 11, 2020}}</ref> Tony Brown and Jonathan Jay intended to create space for diversity by including often marginalized creatives such as black people and women who identified as [[Contemporary Christian music|CCM]] singers and songwriters, and bringing their voices to fore.<ref name="Relevant The Rise of Maverick" /><ref name="Relevant Maverick Interview">{{cite web |url= https://relevantmagazine.com/culture/music/how-maverick-city-music-is-breaking-the-worship-music-mold/ |title= How Maverick City Music Is Breaking the Worship Music Mold {{!}} RELEVANT |date= May 26, 2020 |website= [[Relevant (magazine)|Relevant]] |accessdate= October 11, 2020}}</ref> Maverick City Music released their first extended play, ''Maverick City, Vol. 1'', on July 29, 2019. The EP debuted on [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']]'s Top Gospel Albums Chart at number ten.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/gospel-albums/2019-08-17 |title= Top Gospel Albums Chart {{!}} Billboard |date= August 14, 2020 |website= Billboard |version= Top Gospel Albums Chart |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= October 11, 2020}}</ref> On November 12, 2019, Maverick City Music released their follow-up EP, ''Maverick City, Vol. 2''. The release debuted at number thirty-five on ''Billboard'''s [[Top Christian Albums Chart]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/christian-albums/2019-11-23 |title= Top Christian Albums Chart {{!}} Billboard |date= November 19, 2020 |website= Billboard |version= [[Top Christian Albums Chart]] |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= October 11, 2020}}</ref>
