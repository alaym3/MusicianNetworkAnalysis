'''Stokeley Clevon Goulbourne'''<ref name="florida.arrests.org">{{Cite web|url=http://florida.arrests.org/Arrests/Stokeley_Goulbourne_28306291/|title=Stokeley Goulbourne Mugshot {{!}} 08/02/16 Florida Arrest|website=florida.arrests.org|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref> (born April 18, 1996), known professionally as '''Ski Mask the Slump God''' (formerly stylized as '''$ki Mask "The Slump God"'''), is an American rapper and songwriter. He initially rose to prominence alongside [[XXXTentacion]] and their collective [[Members Only (hip hop collective)|Members Only]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.45035/title.ski-mask-the-slump-god-reveals-why-he-distanced-himself-from-xxxtentacion|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Reveals Why He Distanced Himself From XXXTENTACION|work=HipHopDX|access-date=September 10, 2018}}</ref> In 2017, he released the singles "Catch Me Outside" and "Babywipe", both of which were featured on his mixtape ''[[You Will Regret]]'' (2017), which was certified Gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref name="hotnewhiphop1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/members-only-the-complete-2017-roster-news.34409.html|title=Members Only: The Complete 2017 Roster|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref>

Goulbourne's mixtape, ''[[Beware the Book of Eli]]'', was released in May 2018 and peaked at No. 50 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/beware-the-book-of-eli/1383001656|title=BEWARE THE BOOK OF ELI by Ski Mask the Slump God|work=Apple Music|date=May 11, 2018|access-date=August 4, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Beware The Book Of Eli Chart History |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/ski-mask-the-slump-god/chart-history/billboard-200/song/1078371 |magazine=Billboard |access-date=June 4, 2018}}</ref> His debut album ''[[Stokeley]]'' (2018) peaked at number 6 on the US ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/stokeley/1444949614|title=STOKELEY by Ski Mask the Slump God on Apple Music|work=Apple Music|date=November 30, 2018|access-date=August 4, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Stokeley Clevon Goulbourne was born on April 18, 1996, in [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida]]. He is of [[Jamaicans|Jamaican]] descent.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/theslumpgod/status/928435751401947137?lang=en|title=$ki Mask on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=August 12, 2018}}</ref>

Goulbourne grew up listening to [[Busta Rhymes]], [[Missy Elliott]], [[Wu-Tang Clan]], and [[Lil Wayne]], among other artists. He has said that his parents often played Jamaican music around the house. Goulbourne's father&mdash;a rapper who used the stage name "Sin City"&mdash;would often force his son to focus on writing his own rap music.<ref>{{cite web|title=Open Space: Ski Mask The Slump God {{!}} Mass Appeal|date=July 27, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ad5e97iGK0|access-date=June 20, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Mojica">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/video/2018/06/ski-mask-the-slump-god-dad-is-a-rapper-from-florida/|access-date=June 20, 2018|author=Nick Mojica|date=June 13, 2018|title=Ski Mask The Slump God's Dad Is A Rapper From Florida}}</ref> In 2013, Goulbourne was sent to a juvenile detention center for possession of "around $10 worth of weed", where he met [[XXXTentacion]].<ref name=":B">{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/04/the-break-presents-ski-mask-slump-god/#photogallery-1=5|title=The Break Presents: Ski Mask The Slump God - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref> The duo became friends and frequently collaborated on songs after being released from detention.<ref>{{cite web|title=X talks about how he met Ski Mask in Jail - No Jumper Highlights|date=December 22, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f7M1gBBnqyU|access-date=August 12, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
===2014–2017: Mainstream debut===
Following Goulbourne's release from detention, he formed the rap group "Very Rare" with XXXTentacion and released his first song "Catch Me" on the streaming service [[SoundCloud]]. Goulbourne eventually co-founded XXXTentacion's collective Members Only<ref name="hotnewhiphop1"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/02/xxxtentacion-interview-case-drake/|title=XXXTentacion Aims to Be the Biggest Artist Ever Despite Legal Case and Drake Controversy - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref> after the release of ''Members Only Vol. 1'', a collaboration mixtape with XXXTentacion, in 2015. The mixtape would eventually be followed up with ''Members Only, Vol. 2'' and then ''Members Only, Vol. 3'' on June 26, 2017''.''<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.43889/title.xxxtentacion-crew-release-members-only-vol-3-mixtape#|title=XXXTENTACION & Crew Release "Members Only Vol. 3" Mixtape|work=HipHopDX|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/members-only-vol-3-xxxtentacion-ski-mask/|title=Listen to 'Members Only Vol. 3' Featuring XXXTentacion and Ski Mask The Slump God|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref>

Goulbourne released multiple singles on the SoundCloud platform including "Catch Me Outside", "Where's the Blow" featuring [[Lil Pump]], "Stunt!" featuring UnoTheActivist, "Life Is Short", "Like a Soccer Mom", "Take a Step Back", and "BabyWipe". "Catch Me Outside" used the instrumental of Missy Elliott's "[[She's a Bitch]]". ===2018–present: ''Stokeley'' and ''Sin City The Mixtape''===
In May 2018, Goulbourne released his third mixtape ''[[Beware the Book of Eli]]'', which featured the single "[[DoIHaveTheSause?]]". In June that same year, he was named one of ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''{{'}}s "2018 Freshman Class".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/06/2018-xxl-freshman-cover/|title=XXL 2018 Freshman Class Revealed|date=June 12, 2018|magazine=XXL|access-date=June 14, 2018}}</ref> On November 30, 2018, Goulbourne released his debut studio album, ''[[Stokeley]]'', which includes the songs "[[Faucet Failure]]" and "Nuketown" featuring [[Juice Wrld]].<ref name="BBStokeley">{{cite magazine | last=Norris| first=John| title =Ski Mask the Slump God on His Eclectic Album 'Stokeley' & Life-Changing Advice Given to Him by XXXTentacion| magazine =Billboard| date =December 5, 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8488724/ski-mask-the-slump-god-interview-stokeley-xxxtentacion| access-date =December 18, 2018}}</ref> The album peaked at number 6 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref name="XXLBillboard">{{cite magazine | last=Centeno| first=Tony M.| title =Ski Mask The Slump God's 'Stokeley' Album Enters Billboard 200 Top 10| magazine =XXL| date =December 11, 2018 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/12/ski-mask-the-slump-god-stokeley-album-billboard-200-top-10/| access-date =December 18, 2018}}</ref>

Following the success of ''Stokeley'', Goulbourne released only one single in 2019, "Carbonated Water". In July 2020, he released the single "Burn the Hoods" alongside a music video on [[Lyrical Lemonade]]. Simultaneously, he announced that his second album would release later that year, however the album was never released. Goulbourne has stated that he plans to release more music to the public.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/accounts/login/|title=Login • Instagram|website=www.instagram.com|accessdate=May 5, 2021}}</ref>

In May 2021, Goulbourne announced a mixtape titled ''Sin City The Mixtape'' to be released in June.,<ref>{{Cite web|last=THESLUMPGOD|date=May 14, 2021|title=Sin City The Mixtape Dropping In June|url=https://twitter.com/theslumpgod/status/1393289296078155781|url-status=live|access-date=June 20, 2021|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> Goulbourne released ''Sin City''on June 25

==Musical style==
Goulbourne has said that his musical production has been most influenced by Busta Rhymes,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/04/ski-mask-the-slump-god-nationwide-video-busta-rhymes/|access-date=June 19, 2018|author=Roger Krastz|date=June 19, 2018|title=Ski Mask The Slump God's "Nationwide" Video Will Pay Homage To Busta Rhymes}}</ref> [[Missy Elliot]], and [[Chief Keef]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/ski-mask-the-slump-god-breaks-down-the-meaning-of-nuketown|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Breaks Down The Meaning Of "Nuketown"|website=Genius}}</ref> Goulbourne has also stated he does not have any main musical influences; "I listen to every genre: rap, rock, classical, heavy metal...I listen to [[Adele]] sometimes too".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://officemagazine.net/ski-mask-slump-god?page=51|title=Ski Mask the Slump God|date=July 13, 2017|work=Office Magazine|access-date=August 12, 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
As of June 2018, Golbourne lives in Atlanta, Georgia.<ref name="Mojica"/> He had a heart condition that required surgery in March 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/03/ski-mask-the-slump-god-heart-surgery/|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Shares Health Battle, Needs Surgery - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=September 10, 2018}}</ref>

In a 2016 interview, Goulbourne signaled support for the legalization of [[gay marriage]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Xxxtentacion Interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?t=1015&v=PV7Q2teRLPc&feature=youtu.be}}</ref> In 2020, Goulbourne expressed distaste for President [[Donald Trump]], comparing him to a [[white supremacist]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ski Mask the Slump God Is Back From Hiatus, and He Has a Lot to Say|url= https://www.complex.com/music/2020/07/ski-mask-the-slump-god-interview}}</ref>

=== Friendships ===

==== XXXTentacion ====
Goulbourne was close friends with [[XXXTentacion]] before they both started rapping. They met in a juvenile center in 2013. Goulbourne said in an interview with [[Adam22]] that he was surprised by X's criminal charges.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Burford|first=Corinna|date=June 19, 2018|title=The Complete History of XXXTentacion's Controversial Career|url=https://www.vulture.com/2018/06/a-complete-timeline-xxxtentacions-controversial-career.html|access-date=June 9, 2020|website=Vulture|language=en-us}}</ref> Whilst in jail, X had taught Goulbourne about different rapping cadences. After the duo were released, they met up with the intention of committing [[home invasion]]s but instead began releasing music together. The rappers founded the collective [[Members Only (hip hop collective)|Members Only]]. In 2017, Goulbourne and XXXTentacion embarked on ''The Revenge Tour'' with [[Craig Xen]]. The tour was postponed following the shooting of XXXTentacion's cousin.<ref>{{Cite web|last=India|first=Lindsey|title=XXXTentacion Announces The Revenge Tour With Ski Mask The Slump God and Craig Xen - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/xxxtentacion-ski-mask-craig-xen-revenge-tour/|access-date=June 9, 2020|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=II|first=C. Vernon Coleman|title=XXXTentacion Postpones Tour After His Cousin Gets Shot - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/xxxtentacion-postpones-tour-cousin-shot/|access-date=June 9, 2020|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> By late 2017, the two had parted ways and begun working on their own projects.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Berry|first=Peter A.|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Distances Himself From XXXTentacion - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/ski-mask-the-slump-god-love-xxxtentacion-distance-friendship/|access-date=June 9, 2020|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> Around this time, it was insinuated that the two were feuding.{{By whom|date=October 2020}}

At the Rolling Loud Miami festival in 2018, Goulbourne and XXXTentacion were reunited, reaffirming their friendship.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cline|first=Georgette|title=Ski Mask The Slump God, XXXTentacion Reunite at 2018 Rolling Loud - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/ski-mask-the-slump-god-xxxtentacion-reunite-2018-rolling-loud-festival/|access-date=June 9, 2020|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> On June 18, 2018, [[Murder of XXXTentacion|XXXTentacion was murdered]]; Goulbourne learned of his best friend's death while on Instagram Live and tearfully informed his fans of the rapper's death.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Reacts To Reported XXXTentacion Shooting|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ski-mask-the-slump-god-reacts-to-reported-xxxtentacion-shooting-news.52980.html|access-date=June 9, 2020|website=HotNewHipHop|date=June 18, 2018|language=en}}</ref>

==== Juice Wrld ====
In early 2018, Goulbourne befriended rising rapper [[Juice Wrld]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tracing Juice WRLD & Ski Mask The Slump God's Friendship|url=https://genius.com/a/tracing-juice-wrld-ski-mask-the-slump-gods-friendship|access-date=June 10, 2020|website=Genius|language=en}}</ref> The two went on to collaborate on the track "Nuketown", which became Goulbourne's highest charting song on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. It was announced that the two were working on a mixtape titled ''Evil Twins'', but the project never released.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Orcutt|first=K. C.|title=Ski Mask The Slump God, Juice Wrld's 'Evil Twins' Drops in 2019 - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/ski-mask-slump-god-juice-wrld-evil-twins-project/|access-date=June 10, 2020|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> In 2019, following the release of Juice Wrld's second album, the two embarked on the ''Death Race for Love Tour'' together along with Cole Bennett of [[Lyrical Lemonade]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=February 20, 2019|title=Juice Wrld announces Death Race for Love Tour|url=https://twitter.com/juiceworlddd/status/1098403641713721344?ref_url=https%3a%2f%2fwww.vividseats.com%2fblog%2fjuice-wrld-tour-dates|access-date=June 10, 2020|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>

On December 8, 2019, Juice Wrld died from an opioid-induced [[seizure]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=January 23, 2020|title=Juice WRLD's death ruled as an accidental overdose|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-51218254|access-date=June 10, 2020}}</ref> Goulbourne reacted emotionally to the news of the rapper's death.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Reacts To News Of Juice WRLD's Passing|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ski-mask-the-slump-god-reacts-to-news-of-juice-wrlds-passing-news.97715.html|access-date=June 10, 2020|website=HotNewHipHop|date=December 8, 2019|language=en}}</ref> Goulbourne was present at Juice Wrld's funeral later that month.

===== Aftermath =====
The death of his two best friends in a span of eighteen months had a profound impact on Goulbourne.<ref>{{Cite web|date=June 8, 2019|title=Ski Mask wishes death upon himself|url=https://twitter.com/theslumpgod/status/1203707428249985027|access-date=June 10, 2020|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=May 5, 2020|title=Ski Mask looks back on the deaths of his friends|url=https://twitter.com/theslumpgod/status/1257682198976761856|access-date=June 10, 2020|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> Following XXXTentacion's death, Goulbourne honored him multiple times during his own concerts; Goulbourne met XXXTentacion's son, Gekyume Onfroy, for the first time in person at a show.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Clarkson|first=Brett|title=XXXTentacion's baby son Gekyume was on stage at Ski Mask The Slump God's concert|url=https://www.sun-sentinel.com/news/florida/fl-ne-xxxtentacion-son-gekyume-slumpgod-orlando-20191212-k2h3dh3p7baxva6ornblbo24mq-story.html|access-date=June 10, 2020|website=sun-sentinel.com}}</ref> 

===Controversies===

Show - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref> The attack was said to be instigated by an ongoing feud between the two rappers, starting when Goulbourne refused to leave the stage during Stone's opening act. [[Rob Stone (rapper)|Rob Stone]] was kicked off the remainder of [[Desiigner]]'s Outlet Tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/04/ski-mask-the-slump-god-escorted-off-stage-rob-stone-san-diego/|title=Ski Mask The Slump God Gets Escorted Off Stage as Rob Stone Performs at San Diego Show|website=XXL Mag|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref>

During a concert on August 10, 2018, in [[Austin, Texas]], Goulbourne asked the crowd for a moment of silence for his murdered friend XXXTentacion. An audience member [[Heckler|heckled]], "Fuck X, that woman-beating piece of shit!" Goulbourne threatened the audience member and told the rest of the audience to "fuck his ass up", leading to a melee fight. While the crowd was beating the man, Goulbourne played one of XXXTentacion's most popular songs, "[[Look at Me (XXXTentacion song)|Look at Me]]" before the venue's security dispersed the disturbance and escorted the man outside of the venue.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.47993/title.fan-attacked-for-yelling-fuck-x-at-ski-mask-the-slump-god-show#|title=Fan Attacked For Yelling "Fuck X" At Ski Mask The Slump God Show|website=HipHopDX|date=August 11, 2018}}</ref>

====Legal issues====
