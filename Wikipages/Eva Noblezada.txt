'''Eva Maria Noblezada''' ({{IPAc-en|iː|11=l|17=d|16='|15=ɒ|14=z|13=.|12=ɛ|10=b|.|9='|8=ɒ|7=n|6=_|5='|4=ɑː|v|18=ɑː}}; born March 18, 1996) is an American actress and singer.{{fact|date=September 2021}}

Noblezada made her [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] debut as Kim in a revival of ''[[Miss Saigon]]'', a performance for which 21-year-old Eva received a nomination for a [[71st Tony Awards|2017]] [[Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical|Tony Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role in a Musical]], becoming one of the youngest nominees in the category.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2017/legit/news/tony-nominations-2017-full-list-nominees-1202406314/|title=Tony Nominations Announced: Full List of 2017 Nominees|last=Kelley|first=Seth|date=May 2, 2017|work=Variety|access-date=June 21, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Broadway.com Q&A">{{Cite web|last=Wolf|first=Matt|date=September 18, 2014|title=London ''Miss Saigon'' Star Eva Noblezada on her Chipotle Ordering Skills, Meeting Lea Salonga & 'B to the S' Rumors|url=https://www.broadway.com/buzz/177524/london-miss-saigon-star-eva-noblezada-on-her-chipotle-ordering-skills-meeting-lea-salonga-b-to-the-s-rumors/|url-status=live|access-date=May 14, 2020|website=[[Broadway.com]]}}</ref> She also originated the lead role of [[Eurydice]] in ''[[Hadestown]]'' on Broadway, a performance for which 23-year-old Noblezada received her second nomination for a [[73rd Tony Awards|2019]] [[Tony Awards|Tony Award]] for Best Actress in a Leading Role in a Musical, as well as winning the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|2020]] [[Grammy Award for Best Musical Theater Album]]. Noblezada also played the title character of Rose in the 2020 film ''[[Yellow Rose (film)|Yellow Rose]]'', starring alongside [[Lea Salonga]], who originated the role of Kim in ''Miss Saigon''.{{fact|date=September 2021}}

== Early life and education ==
Eva Maria Noblezada was born on March 18, 1996, in [[San Diego|San Diego, California]] to a Filipino father, Jon, and a Mexican-American mother, Angie Noblezada.<ref name="Broadway.com Q&A"/> She and her family moved to [[Charlotte, North Carolina]], where she attended the [[Northwest School of the Arts]] until she was 17.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Salonga|first=Lea|date=October 2, 2014|title=The girl who would be Kim|work=Inquirer.net|url=http://entertainment.inquirer.net/153827/the-girl-who-would-be-kim|access-date=November 5, 2017}}</ref> Her aunt is [[Annette Calud]], who played Celina on ''[[Sesame Street]]'' and also played Kim in ''[[Miss Saigon]]'' on Broadway.<ref name="wmag">{{cite web|last=Barnard|first=Christopher|date=June 9, 2017|title=Meet Eva Noblezada, the 21-Year-Old Competing for a Tony Award Against Veterans Bette Midler and Patti LuPone|url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/eva-noblezada-miss-saigon-tony-awards-2017-bette-midler-and-patti-lupone|url-status=live|access-date=July 1, 2019|work=W Magazine}}</ref>

== Acting career ==
On July 1, 2013, Noblezada became one of five finalists for the 2013 [[National High School Musical Theatre Awards]]<ref>{{Cite web|date=April 27, 2017|title=Eva Noblezada's Journey from the Jimmy Awards to Broadway Leading Lady|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/eva-noblezadas-journey-from-the-jimmy-awards-to-broadway-leading-lady|url-status=live|access-date=November 5, 2017|website=Playbill}}</ref> Her performance of the song "With You" from ''[[Ghost (musical)|Ghost]]'' during the awards ceremony at the [[Minskoff Theatre]] in [[New York City]] was noted by casting director Tara Rubin, who arranged an audition for Noblezada, then 17, before producer [[Cameron Mackintosh]] for the forthcoming [[West End theatre|West End]] revival of ''[[Miss Saigon]]''. Noblezada was cast in the lead role of Kim.{{fact|date=September 2021}}

In the spring of 2014, Noblezada left the [[Northwest School of the Arts]] in [[Charlotte, North Carolina|Charlotte]] to star in ''Miss Saigon'' in [[London]]. For her performance as Kim, Noblezada won the [[2015 WhatsOnStage Awards|2015 WhatsOnStage Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role in a Musical]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/feb/15/miss-saigon-dominates-whatsonstage-awards|title=Miss Saigon dominates WhatsOnStage awards|last=Ellis-Petersen|first=Hannah|date=February 15, 2015|work=The Guardian|access-date=November 5, 2017|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> She performed "I'd Give My Life for You" at the [[2015 Laurence Olivier Awards]] ceremony.<ref>{{Citation|last=Lzbth|first=Emma|title=Miss Saigon Olivier Awards 2015 – 'I'd Give My Life For You'|date=April 13, 2015|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B6PoGJ-YKa0|access-date=November 5, 2017}}{{Dead link|date=April 2021}}</ref>

Following the limited run of ''Miss Saigon'' in [[London]], Noblezada assumed the role of [[Éponine]] in the [[West End theatre|West End]] production of ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]'' in April 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|date=April 4, 2016|title=Photo Flash: Eva Noblezada Joins the Barricade as 'Eponine' in West End's LES MISERABLES|work=BroadwayWorld|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Photo-Flash-Eva-Noblezada-Joins-the-Barricade-as-Eponine-in-West-Ends-LES-MISERABLES-20160404|access-date=November 5, 2017}}</ref>

On May 2, 2016, Noblezada made her [[Carnegie Hall]] debut, celebrating [[Alain Boublil]] and [[Claude-Michel Schönberg]], performing "The Movie in My Mind" with [[Lea Salonga]] and [[The New York Pops]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=The New York Pops 33rd Birthday Gala|url=https://www.carnegiehall.org/Calendar/2016/5/2/0700/PM/The-New-York-Pops-33rd-Birthday-Gala/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171107010801/https://www.carnegiehall.org/Calendar/2016/5/2/0700/PM/The-New-York-Pops-33rd-Birthday-Gala/|archive-date=November 7, 2017|access-date=November 5, 2017|website=carnegiehall.org}}</ref>

Noblezada reprised her performance as Kim in the first [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] revival of ''Miss Saigon'', which opened at the [[Broadway Theatre (53rd Street)|Broadway Theatre]] on March 23, 2017, for a limited run through January 14, 2018. For her performance, Noblezada was nominated for the [[71st Tony Awards|2017]] [[Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical|Tony Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role in a Musical]].<ref name="wmag" />

In November 2018 Noblezada opened in the lead role of Eurydice in the [[Royal National Theatre]]'s production of the musical ''[[Hadestown]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hadestown|url=https://www.nationaltheatre.org.uk/shows/hadestown|url-status=live|website=National Theatre|date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> She continued in the role of Eurydice when the production transferred to Broadway's [[Walter Kerr Theatre]] in April 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|date=February 11, 2019|title=Full Cast Announced for HADESTOWN on Broadway; Rehearsals Begin Today!|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Full-Cast-Announced-for-HADESTOWN-on-Broadway-Rehearsals-Begin-Today-20190211|url-status=live|website=BroadwayWorld}}</ref> She received her second Tony Award nomination for her role<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/04/30/theater/tony-nominations.html|title=Full List of the 2019 Tony Award Nominees|date=April 30, 2019|work=The New York Times|access-date=2019-06-01|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> and won the Broadway.com Audience Choice Award for Favorite Leading Actress in a Musical.<ref name="bcomwinners">{{Cite web|date=May 16, 2019|title=''Be More Chill'' Leads Broadway.com Audience Choice Award Winners; ''Pretty Woman'', ''Mockingbird'' Also Take Top Prizes|url=https://www.broadway.com/buzz/195832/be-more-chill-leads-broadwaycom-audience-choice-award-winners-pretty-woman-mockingbird-also-take-top-prizes/|url-status=live|access-date=2019-06-01|website=Broadway.com}}</ref>

Noblezada made her film debut in the [[Diane Paragas]] film ''[[Yellow Rose (film)|Yellow Rose]]'' (2019) as the title character, Rose.  She starred alongside [[Lea Salonga]], who originated the role of Kim in ''Miss Saigon''. The film was released by [[Sony Pictures]] [[Stage 6 Films|Stage 6]] on October 9, 2020.{{fact|date=September 2021}}

Noblezada is represented by [[United Talent Agency]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kroll|first1=Justin|date=October 8, 2020|title=UTA Signs Grammy Winner Eva Noblezada|work=Deadline|url=https://deadline.com/2020/10/uta-eva-noblezada-1234593403/|access-date=2020-10-12}}</ref>

==Personal life==
On November 4, 2017, Noblezada married her longtime boyfriend, actor Leo Roberts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/miss-saigons-eva-noblezada-marries-uk-actor-leo-roberts|title=Miss Saigon's Eva Noblezada Marries U.K. Actor Leo Roberts|last=McPhee|first=Ryan|date=November 5, 2017 |work=Playbill}}</ref> Noblezada separated from Roberts in 2019 and later divorced. Since 2019, she has been dating ''[[Hadestown]]'' co-star [[Reeve Carney]].<ref>{{cite instagram|user=reevecarney|postid=B8khku-lUAV|date=February 15, 2020|title=Hoping you all have someone in your life who...|access-date=2020-12-07}}</ref>
 
Noblezada has been vocal about her personal struggles with [[Anxiety disorder|anxiety]], [[Major depressive disorder|depression]], [[Bulimia nervosa|bulimia]], and [[body dysmorphic disorder]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ferri|first=Josh|date=June 9, 2017|title=Eva Noblezada Opens Up About 10 Things She Learned About Herself, Life, & Showbusiness During Her Years as Kim in ''Miss Saigon''|url=https://www.broadwaybox.com/daily-scoop/10-things-miss-saigon-taught-eva-noblezada-about-herself/|url-status=live|access-date=2020-06-13|website=BroadwayBox.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|author=@livevamaria|date=December 20, 2019|title=having a mid show anxiety attack is never fun. However, in my own journey...|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B6USwo9pACk/|access-date=2020-06-13|via=[[Instagram]]}}</ref><ref name="wmag" />
