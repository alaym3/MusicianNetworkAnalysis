'''Alexandra Ashley Hughes''' (born 31 July 1985), known by her stage name '''Allie X''', is a Canadian singer, songwriter and visual artist. She began her career as an [[indie pop]] artist in [[Toronto]] in the mid-2000s, playing with local bands and writing and recording a handful of self-released albums.<ref name="cfcalumni">{{cite web |url=http://cfccreates.com/alumni/720 |title=Allie X (Canadian Film Centre) |access-date=24 May 2017}}</ref><ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/allie-x-mn0003221710/biography |title=Allie X – Biography & History – AllMusic |access-date=24 May 2017}}</ref>

After relocating to [[Los Angeles]], California, in 2013, Hughes began working with producers [[Cirkut (record producer)|Cirkut]] and [[Billboard (record producer)|Billboard]] and achieved success with her first single released under the name Allie X, "[[Catch (Allie X song)|Catch]]", which peaked at number 55 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] chart and was well received by music critics.<ref name="allmusic" /><ref name="timemagazine">{{cite web |url=http://time.com/4409/allie-x-catch-single/ |title=Allie X's "Catch" Is a Perfect Pop Gem |last=Cox |first=Jamieson |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |access-date=24 May 2017}}</ref><ref name="billboard">{{cite web|last1=Lipshutz|first1=Jason|title=Who Is Allie X? 'Catch' Singer Talks Katy Perry Shout-Out, Her Pop Takeover Plan|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5930347/who-is-allie-x-catch-singer-talks-katy-perry-shout-out-her-pop|website=Billboard|date=10 March 2014|access-date=7 October 2015}}</ref>

The song also rose to prominence after pop singer [[Katy Perry]] referred to it as her "spring jam" on social media.<ref name="billboard" /><ref name="katyperry">{{cite tweet |user=katyperry |author=Katy Perry |number=441690587545088000 |title=Currently I'm obsessed with this song CATCH by Allie X |date=6 March 2014}}</ref> Hughes released her debut extended play in 2015, ''[[CollXtion I]]'', followed by her debut studio album ''[[CollXtion II]]'' in 2017, and ''[[Super Sunset]]'' in 2018. In 2019, she also released an analog version of ''Super Sunset''. Her second studio album, ''[[Cape God]]'', was released on 21 February 2020.

==Early life==
Alexandra Ashley Hughes was born on 31 July 1985 in [[Oakville, Ontario]].<ref name="oakville" /> Her family is of British descent.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://fault-magazine.com/2016/07/allie-x-will-make-your-head-spin-in-this-kaleidoscope-shoot-exclusively-for-fault-online/ |title=Allie X Will Make Your Head Spin in This Kaleidoscopic Shoot Exclusively for Fault Online |work=Fault |date=29 July 2016 |access-date=24 May 2017}}</ref> She attended the [[Etobicoke School of the Arts]], then studied classical piano and voice at the [[Interlochen Center for the Arts|Interlochen Arts Academy]] in [[Michigan]], and graduated from [[Sheridan College]]'s Musical Theatre Performance program, and also studied musical theatre.<ref name="dreammachine">{{cite web |title=Dream Machine – Allie X |url=http://dreammachine.me/team/allie-x/ |access-date=24 May 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170527203950/http://dreammachine.me/team/allie-x/ |archive-date=27 May 2017 |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="CFC">{{cite web |title=CFC announces participants for slaight music residency |url=http://cfccreates.com/news/media/68-cfc-announces-participants-for-slaight-music-residency |access-date=9 April 2015 |website=cfccreates.com |publisher=Canadian Film Centre |date=18 March 2013 |archive-date=13 April 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150413195346/http://cfccreates.com/news/media/68-cfc-announces-participants-for-slaight-music-residency |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="lyriccanada">{{cite web |title=LyricCanada 2010 National Conference |url=http://spartan.ac.brocku.ca/~dvivian/LyricCanada2010/documents/Lyric_Canada_2010_Showcase_FINAL_v4.pdf |access-date=9 April 2015}}</ref><ref name="allmusic" />

==Career==
===2006–2012: Career beginnings===
Hughes first started her career under the stage name Allie Hughes in [[Toronto]] around 2006, performing [[indie pop]], [[indie rock]], [[jazz]] and [[electronic music]].<ref name="allmusic" /> She released the 10-track compilation, ''Ladies & Gentlemen'' on 1 December 2006 through A Tear in a Corner Records.<ref name="L&G">{{cite web|url=https://atearinacorner.bandcamp.com/album/ladies-gentlemen|title=Ladies & Gentlemen by Allie Hughes|website=Bandcamp|accessdate=20 February 2021}}</ref>

In 2008, she took part in [[CBC Television]]'s ''[[How Do You Solve a Problem Like Maria? (Canada)|How Do You Solve a Problem Like Maria?]]'', the Canadian version based on the series [[How Do You Solve a Problem Like Maria?|with the same name]] which aired in the United Kingdom on [[BBC One]] in 2006. She performed "[[My Favorite Things (song)|My Favourite Things]]" from ''[[The Sound of Music]]'' and "[[It's Oh So Quiet]]" by [[Björk]],<ref name="dreammachine" /> but was eliminated from the show after two weeks. Her acting credits from the time also include other Canadian movies and television programmes, including ''[[The Boys in the Photograph]]'', ''[[King (2011 TV series)|King]]'', ''[[Being Erica]]'' and ''[[Instant Star]]'', and her music was featured in ''[[Rookie Blue]]'', ''[[Saving Hope]]'' and ''Love Me''.<ref name="CFC" /><ref name="lyriccanada" />

In June 2009 she set out to record her debut studio album, collaborating with Dave Newfeld of [[Broken Social Scene]], Leon Taheny, and Adam King of Run With Kittens. The album was set to be released in fall 2010, then delayed to 2011 and remains unreleased.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alliehughes.com/info.html|title=Allie Hughes - Info|website=Alliehughes.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100612223350/http://www.alliehughes.com/info.html|archive-date=12 June 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://alliehughes.com/about|title=Allie Hughes - About|website=alliehughes.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100922061931/http://alliehughes.com/about|archive-date=21 July 2010}}</ref>

===2013–2015: ''CollXtion I''===
In July 2013, Hughes decided to make the move to Los Angeles,<ref name="billboardinterview">{{cite web|title=Allie X – Biography|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6033633/allie-x/biography|work=Billboard|access-date=3 April 2014}}</ref> to pursue a full-time career as a songwriter. Despite this, she didn't stop working on her solo material.<ref name="allmusic" /> She hadn't been satisfied with her sound, and spent time learning about sound design and production.<ref name="STAR">{{cite interview|title=Allie X explores her 'shadow self' in her dark radio-ready pop|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2015/04/03/allie-x-explores-her-shadow-self-in-her-dark-radio-ready-pop.html |access-date=24 May 2017}}</ref> She adopted the stage name Allie X and began working with producers [[Cirkut (record producer)|Cirkut]] and [[Billboard (record producer)|Billboard]], whom she had met during her time in [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="allmusic" /><ref name="billboard" />

In early 2014, Hughes released her debut single under the Allie X name, titled "[[Catch (Allie X song)|Catch]]". Upon release, "Catch" received praise from multiple sources: ''[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]'' magazine's Jamie Milton said that "Allie X stepped on the scene like a bottle of wine at a wedding – everyone wanted a piece of her."<ref name="diymagazine">{{cite interview|title=Allie X Shares New Track |work=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |url=http://diymag.com/archive/watch-allie-x-shares-new-prime-track/ |access-date=2 April 2015}}</ref> and Jamieson Cox from ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine said the track "shines with a level of polish and craft that's remarkable", and complemented Hughes' "attention to detail".<ref name="timemagazine" /> Fellow singer-songwriter [[Katy Perry]] also praised the track on Twitter, calling it her "spring jam".<ref name="billboard" /><ref name="katyperry" /> "Catch" also charted on the [[Canadian Hot 100]], peaking at number 55 on the chart.<ref name="canadahot100">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/allie-x/chart-history/CAN|title = Allie X Chart History - Canadian Hot 100|work=Billboard|accessdate=8 February 2021}}</ref> "Catch" went on to be [[Catch (Allie X song)#Track listing|re-released]] as an EP in November 2015, featuring additional tracks.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/catch-ep/id1059769323 |title=Catch – EP by Allie X on iTunes |access-date=24 May 2017}}</ref>

Prior to the release of Hughes' debut extended play, ''[[CollXtion I]]'', two further singles, "Prime" and "Bitch", were put out by the singer on 18 March and 6 April 2014 respectively. ''CollXtion I'' was then released a year later, on 7 April 2015 in Canada,<ref>{{cite web|title=Allie X's Journey To Dimension X: Idolator Interview|date=April 2015 |url=http://www.idolator.com/7585493/allie-x-interview-catch-collxtion |access-date=24 May 2017}}</ref> with the worldwide release being pushed back to 21 April.<ref>{{cite twitter |user=alliex |author=Allie X |number=585512617495625730 |title=Despite their efforts to stop me, CollXTion I has been retrieved, and will be released worldwide officially on April 21st. |date=7 April 2015}}</ref> The EP was also well received, with ''Edmonton Journal''{{'}}s Julia LeConte calling it, "a smartly written seven-song EP that offers up a smorgasboard of indelible hooks," and goes on to say Hughes' song creating ability matches her "spectacular" voice.<ref>{{cite web|title=Julia LeConte of Edmonton Journal reviews CollXtion I|url=http://www.blogs.edmontonjournal.com/2015/04/06/album-review-allie-x-collxtion-i/|access-date=24 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150413052513/http://blogs.edmontonjournal.com/2015/04/06/album-review-allie-x-collxtion-i/|archive-date=13 April 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> She also held a small North American tour in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.axs.com/allie-x-will-embark-on-her-first-ever-u-s-tour-60890|title=Allie X will embark on her first ever U.S. tour|website=AXS|access-date=29 March 2016}}</ref>

Besides working on solo music, in 2014 and 2015, Hughes also contributed to Australian singer [[Troye Sivan]]'s album ''[[Blue Neighbourhood]]'', co-writing a total of seven tracks, including two of the album's three singles, "Talk Me Down" and "Youth".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.idolator.com/7607386/allie-x-interview-collxtion-reception-troye-sivan-bite-part-two |title=Allie X On The Warm Reception Of 'CollXtion I,' Co-Writing Troye Sivan's "Bite" & Part II: Idolator Interview |website=Idolator.com |first=Mike |last=Wass |date=9 September 2015 |access-date=8 February 2016}}</ref> Together with this, she supported Troye Sivan on his [[Blue Neighbourhood Tour|2016 tour]]'s North American leg.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fuse.tv/2015/12/troye-sivan-american-tour-2016-shamir-allie-x-lany|title=Troye Sivan Snags Shamir, Allie X & LANY for 2016 Tour|website=Fuse|access-date=29 March 2016}}</ref>

Hughes was one of four musicians to receive the [[Canadian Film Centre]] Slaight Music Residency Showcase in 2013.<ref name="CFC" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2014/music/global/international-star-you-should-know-allie-x-1201217844/|title=International Star You Should Know: Allie X|last=Friend|first=David|website=Variety|date=13 June 2014|access-date=29 March 2016}}</ref>

===2016–2018: ''CollXtion II'' and ''Super Sunset''===
Despite having released albums and EPs in the past, Hughes had yet to release a studio album under the Allie X name. In February 2016, Hughes released a free-to-download song called "Old Habits Die Hard", which was originally meant to serve as a non-album single.<ref>{{cite web|title=Allie X Offers 'Old Habits Die Hard' As Free Download: Listen To The New Synth-Pop Song |url=http://www.idolator.com/7622291/allie-x-old-habits-die-hard-free-download-listen |last=Daw |first=Robbie |date=3 February 2016 |publisher=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]] |access-date=3 May 2017}}</ref> On 13 May 2016, Hughes released another song called "Too Much to Dream"<ref>{{cite tweet |user=alliex |author=Allie X |number=730809344128733184 |title= DEAR X's: TOO MUCH TO DREAM will be released worldwide tomorrow. |date=12 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet |user=alliex |author=Allie X |number=731237296385732608 |title= X's the first track from #CLXIIɄ – Too Much To Dream is out now! |date=14 May 2016}}</ref> and announced a project called ''CollXtion II: Ʉnsolved'', which saw her release a collection of demos and songs on Spotify over the course of the summer, in order to shortlist the songs her fans wanted to see on ''CollXtion II''.<ref>{{cite tweet |user=alliex |author=Allie X |number=731109139376672769 |title= COLLXTION II is ɄNSOLVED. |date=13 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet |user=alliex |author=Allie X |number=731534653895495680 |title= COLLXTION II is Ʉ so I'm not sure at this point. ''[[CollXtion II]]'' began to be teased by Hughes in February 2017, with it being officially announced for a 9 June release date on 23 February.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/collxtion-ii/id1230185539|title=CollXtion II by Allie X on Apple Music|access-date=3 May 2017}}</ref> On 28 April 2017, Hughes released "Paper Love" as the first single from the upcoming album.<ref>{{cite web|title=Allie X Gets Ripped Apart on 'Paper Love' |url=http://www.out.com/music/2017/4/28/allie-x-gets-ripped-apart-paper-love |last=Moran |first=Justin |work=[[Out (magazine)|Out]] |date=28 April 2017 |access-date=3 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Allie X Is a Sucker for the Sweet Sting on 'Paper Love' |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/allie-x-is-a-sucker-for-the-sweet-sting-on-paper-love |last=Stone |first=Avery |date=27 April 2017 |publisher=[[Noisey]] |access-date=3 May 2017}}</ref> "Need You" was released on 26 May 2017.<ref>{{Citation|title=Allie X (Ft. Valley Girl) – Need You|url=https://genius.com/Allie-x-need-you-lyrics|access-date=12 September 2019}}</ref>

On 1 June 2018, Hughes released "Focus" as the first single from her then-untitled second studio album,<ref name="billboard-july-2018">{{cite web|title=Allie X Talks 'Apocalyptic' New Single, Writing With Troye Sivan & Her Connection to the LGBTQ Community |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8458950/allie-x-interview-pride-troye-sivan |last=Crowley |first=Patrick |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=June 2018 |access-date=18 July 2018}}</ref> followed by the release of the second single "Not so Bad in LA" on 13 July.<ref>{{cite web|title=Allie X's "Not So Bad in LA" Is A Dark Pop Masterpiece |url=http://www.idolator.com/7682675/allie-x-not-so-bad-in-la-listen |last=Nied |first=Mike |date=13 July 2018 |publisher=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]] |access-date=18 July 2018}}</ref> The following Monday, Hughes announced her extended play, ''Super Sunset'', to be released in the upcoming fall, alongside with a four date promotional tour called "The Super Sunset Xperience".<ref>{{cite web|title=ALLIE X Announces New Body Of Work, SUPER SUNSET, For Fall 2018 |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/ALLIE-X-Announces-New-Body-Of-Work-SUPER-SUNSET-For-Fall-2018-20180717 |publisher=Broadway World |access-date=18 July 2018}}</ref> On 25 December 2018, Hughes released ''Super Sunset Analog'', an alternate version of her previously released album ''Super Sunset.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BrTJ75TFJoQ/|title=ALLIE X on Instagram: "MERRY XMAS! 🌲🌟🌃 The first surprise: SUPER SUNSET ANALOG featuring all existing analog versions of Super Sunset + two unheard versions. === 2019–present: ''Cape God'' ===
On 26 September 2019, Hughes released "Fresh Laundry" along with a music video for her next album that was then untitled. This was followed by "Rings a Bell" on 17 October. On 8 November, the third single, "Regulars", was released. The fourth single, a collaboration with singer-songwriter [[Troye Sivan]], "[[Love Me Wrong]]", was released on 6 December. On 16 December, Allie announced the pre-order, album artwork, and album title for her upcoming album, ''[[Cape God]]'', through her social media accounts. A fifth single, "Devil I Know", was released on 31 January 2020. The album was released 21 February 2020. Hughes' vocals were featured on "Shadow" by Dutch electronic music duo [[Vicetone]], released on Canadian label [[Monstercat]] on 30 October 2020. The singer also released a collab with Mitski, "Susie Save Your Love". In 2020, she appeared as a guest host in the seventh episode of ''[[Canada's Drag Race]]''.<ref>Allie Gregory, [https://exclaim.ca/comedy/article/tom_green_allie_x_ralph_to_guest_on_canadas_drag_race "Tom Green, Allie X, Ralph to Guest on 'Canada's Drag Race'"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', 15 June 2020.</ref>

She is slated to perform in the 2021 virtual edition of [[Pride Toronto]].<ref>Kevin Ritchie, [https://nowtoronto.com/news/pride-toronto-2021-lineup-includes-priyanka-allie-x-and-iskwe "Pride Toronto lineup includes Priyanka, Allie X and iskwē"]. ''[[Now (newspaper)|Now]]'', May 3, 2021.</ref>

In July 2021, Hughes released a single with drag queen [[Violet Chachki]], "Mistress Violet", produced by French producer Lecomte De Brégeot and written by Hughes. The song's 1980s influenced music video was shot in analog and features Hughes and Chachki in [[Maison Schiaparelli|Schiaparelli]] haute couture outfits. Chachki had previously appeared with Hughes in the music video for her 2016 single "All the Rage".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Allie-X-Violet-Chachki-Release-Mistress-Violet-20210730|title=Allie X & Violet Chachki Release 'Mistress Violet'|last=Leiber|first=Sarah Jae|date=30 July 2021|website=BroadwayWorld}}</ref>

==Public advocacy==

==Discography==
{{main|Allie X discography}}

Allie X has released two studio albums: ''[[CollXtion II]]'' in 2017 and ''[[Cape God]]'' in 2020. She has released five EPs, including ''[[CollXtion I]]'' (2015) and ''[[Super Sunset]]'' (2018). She co-wrote several songs on each of [[Troye Sivan]]'s first two albums, ''[[Blue Neighbourhood]]'' and ''[[Bloom (Troye Sivan album)|Bloom]]'', and has written songs for other artists including [[BTS]], [[Seohyun]], [[Lea Michele]], and [[Betty Who]].
