'''Jorik Scholten''' ({{IPA-nl|ˈjɔrɪk sxɔltən}}; born 15 October 1994), professionally known as '''Lil' Kleine''' ({{IPA-nl|lɪl ˈklɛi̯nə|pron}}), is a Dutch musician and actor. He collaborated with fellow rapper [[Ronnie Flex]] to produce the [[List of best-selling singles|number one single]] "[[Drank & Drugs]]" and the [[List of best-selling albums|number one album]] ''[[WOP!]]''.

==Career==
===1994–2013: Early life and career beginnings===
Scholten was born on 15 October 1994 in the [[Nieuwmarkt]] district of Amsterdam, Netherlands. At the age of one, he was diagnosed with [[leukemia]] in his back that required surgery.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=http://www.lilkleine.nl|title=Lil' Kleine – Officiële website|website=www.lilkleine.nl|language=nl|access-date=6 November 2017}}</ref> He spent the first seven years of his life in [[De Wallen]], moving to [[Amsterdam-Oost]] with his father after his parents divorced.<ref name=":4" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/3AlRiyjMywTVNzTcHbf9QT|title=Lil' Kleine|website=Spotify|language=en|access-date=3 November 2017}}</ref>

Scholten was signed with talent agencies at an early age and made his acting debut in the 2005 film ''[[Diep]]''. After befriending rapper [[Lange Frans]], Scholten began performing with Lange Frans' band [[D-Men]] on-stage. In 2012, Scholten adopted the stage name "Lil' Kleine" and released his first EP, "Tuig van de Richel".<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://dewerelddraaitdoor.bnnvara.nl/gasten/lil-kleine?npo_cc=126&|title=Lil' Kleine|website=[[BNNVARA]]|language=nl-NL|access-date=3 November 2017}}</ref> The following year, the song "Zo Verdomd Alleen", which he recorded with [[Danny de Munk]], reached the [[Single Top 100]].

===2013–present: "Drank & Drugs", ''WOP!'', ''Alleen'' and other endeavours===
After signing with hip-hop label [[TopNotch]] in 2013, Scholten collaborated with fellow rapper [[Ronnie Flex]] to produce the single "[[Drank & Drugs]]", a song about alcohol, drugs and sex, which reached number one on the Single Top 100 and was [[Music recording sales certification|certified]] double platinum.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> "Drank & Drugs" won the [[NPO 3FM#3FM Awards|3FM Award]] for Best Single in March 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npo3fm.nl/nieuws/3fm/373902-dit-zijn-de-winnaars-van-de-3fm-awards?npo_cc=126&|title=Dit zijn de winnaars van de 3FM Awards|website=NPO 3FM|language=nl-nl|access-date=6 November 2017}}</ref>

In February 2016, Scholten released his debut album ''[[WOP!]]''.<ref name=":1" /> In the album’s first week, all eleven of its tracks ranked within the top 35 of the Single Top 100 and the album reached number 1 on the [[Album Top 100]] – it is the first hip hop/rap album in the Netherlands to accomplish these feats.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://3voor12.vpro.nl/artikelen/overzicht/2016/Februari/lil-kleine-wop-nummer-1-album-single-top-100.html|title=Lil' Kleine breekt records: heel WOP in de Single Top 100 – stories|work=[[3VOOR12]]|access-date=6 November 2017|language=nl-NL}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nu.nl/muziek/4217564/lil-kleine-breekt-meerdere-records-in-nederlandse-hitlijsten.html|title=Lil' Kleine breekt meerdere records in Nederlandse hitlijsten|work=[[NU.nl]]|access-date=6 November 2017|language=nl}}</ref> ''WOP!'' also became the most-streamed new album on [[Spotify]] in Dutch history, being streamed over 1.5 million times during the 24 hours after its release, beating the previous record holder, [[Justin Bieber]]'s ''[[Purpose (Justin Bieber album)|Purpose]]''.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":3">{{Cite news|url=https://www.puna.nl/lil-kleine-breekt-records-met-debuutalbum-wop/|title=Lil Kleine breekt records met debuutalbum WOP|date=15 February 2016|work=www.puna.nl|access-date=6 November 2017|language=nl}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.metronieuws.nl/nieuws/showbizz/2016/02/lil-kleine-breekt-records-met-debuutalbum-wop|title=Lil&#039; Kleine breekt records met debuutalbum WOP|work=Metronieuws|access-date=6 November 2017}}</ref> Also in its first week, all of the album’s tracks ranked within Spotify's Netherlands Top 50, with singles "Niet Omdat Het Moet" and "1, 2, 3" (both featuring Ronnie Flex) at numbers 1 and 2 respectively.<ref name=":3" /> "Niet Omdat Het Moet" received over half a million views on [[YouTube]] during its first few days, being the most viewed YouTube video in the Netherlands at the time.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":3" />

Scholten’s second album, ''Alleen'', was released in May 2017. All eleven of its tracks reached Spotify's Netherlands Top 25, with songs "Krantenwijk" (featuring [[Boef]]) and "Alleen" at numbers 1 and 2 respectively. Collectively, Scholten’s songs were streamed by 1.84 million listeners on Spotify during the month preceding the album’s release – the first time an individual Dutch artist’s music was streamed by that many listeners in the space of a month.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.puna.nl/lil-kleine-breekt-records-met-zijn-nieuwe-album-alleen/|title=Lil Kleine breekt records met zijn nieuwe album Alleen|date=29 May 2017|work=www.puna.nl|access-date=6 November 2017|language=nl}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://top-notch.nl/nieuws/lil-kleine-met-zijn-complete-album-in-de-top-25|title=Lil Kleine met zijn complete album in de top 25|work=TopNotch|access-date=6 November 2017|language=nl-NL}}</ref>

In 2017, Scholten was one of fifteen celebrities who competed on the [[Expeditie Robinson 2017|nineteenth season]] of ''[[Expeditie Robinson]]''. He was the first contestant to leave the competition, having been voted off at his own request after three days.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nhnieuws.nl/nieuws/212077/Lil-Kleine-verlaat-als-eerste-Expeditie-Robinson|title=Lil' Kleine verlaat als eerste Expeditie Robinson|date=14 September 2017|work=NH Nieuws|access-date=9 April 2018|language=nl-NL}}</ref>

In 2018, Scholten became a coach on the [[The Voice of Holland (season 9)|ninth season]] of ''[[The Voice of Holland]]'' and served as a coach until the [[The Voice of Holland (season 10) |tenth season]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/rtlboulevard/lil-kleine-nieuwe-coach-the-voice|title=Lil' Kleine nieuwe coach The Voice|work=RTL Nieuws|date=16 February 2018|access-date=9 April 2018}}</ref>

In 2020, Scholten released his third album, ''Jongen Van De Straat''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.funx.nl/news/music/46022-lil-kleine-breekt-spotify-record-met-album-jongen-van-de-straat?q=&start=&end=|title=Lil Kleine breekt Spotify-record met album 'Jongen van de Straat'|website=FunX.nl|language=nl-nl|access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref>

From 2021, Scholten offers entrepreneurial courses together with [[Boef]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hln.be/showbizz/nu-te-koop-via-een-prijzige-cursus-het-succes-van-boef-en-lil-kleine~a8e388cd/|title = DPG Media Privacy Gate}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Scholten is engaged to Jaimie Vaes, and they have a son born in June 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.destentor.nl/show/rapper-lil-kleine-voor-het-eerst-vader-geworden~ab9b337b/|title=Rapper Lil' Kleine voor het eerst vader geworden|last=Segers|first=Maxime|date=9 June 2019|website=De Stentor|access-date=9 June 2019}}</ref>

On 23 May 2021, Scholten was arrested in Ibiza for beating his partner, Jaimie Vaes, and breaking her phone. After spending the night at the Civil Guard, he was provisionally released and his case was provisionally dismissed by the Violence Against Women Court. The complainant did not appear before the court and had reportedly left the country.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pérez |first1=Paco S. |title=Detenido en Ibiza el rapero 'Lil' Kleine por agredir a su pareja |url=https://www.periodicodeibiza.es/pitiusas/ibiza/2021/05/24/1268001/violencia-machista-detenido-ibiza-lil-kleine-por-agredir-pareja.html |website=Periódico de Ibiza y Formentera |date=24 May 2021 |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Santegoeds |first1=Evert |title=Lil Kleine vast op Ibiza |url=https://www.telegraaf.nl/entertainment/1473605761/lil-kleine-vast-op-ibiza |website=De Telegraaf |date=23 May 2021 |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref> The incident made the theme of domestic violence a much discussed subject in the Dutch media that week.<ref>{{cite web |title=Vertrekken bij gewelddadige partner moeilijk: 'Man belooft vaak beterschap' |url=https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/editienl/artikel/5233168/incident-lil-kleine-jaimie-vaes-roept-discussie-huiselijk-geweld-op |website=RTL Nieuws |date=26 May 2021 |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Koelewijn |first1=Rinskje |title=Partnergeweld is meer dan een privéprobleem |url=https://www.nrc.nl/nieuws/2021/05/31/meer-dan-een-priveprobleem-a4045463 |website=NRC |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=van Leeuwen |first1=Marlies |title=Het blijft stil rondom Lil' Kleine na verdenking huiselijk geweld: 'Niet zo'n lieverdje als fans denken' |url=https://www.ad.nl/show/het-blijft-stil-rondom-lil-kleine-na-verdenking-huiselijk-geweld-niet-zo-n-lieverdje-als-fans-denken~a3028314/ |website=AD |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=van den Heuvel |first1=John |title=Reactie Lil Kleine herkenbaar bij huiselijk geweld |url=https://www.telegraaf.nl/watuzegt/431375614/reactie-lil-kleine-herkenbaar-bij-huiselijk-geweld |website=De Telegraaf |date=26 May 2021 |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref>

On 2 June 2021, the couple released a joint video in which they denied physical violence had taken place.<ref>{{cite web |title=Jaimie Vaes en Lil Kleine delen videoboodschap over incident in Spanje |url=https://www.nu.nl/287474/video/jaimie-vaes-en-lil-kleine-delen-videoboodschap-over-incident-in-spanje.html |website=Nu.nl |access-date=4 June 2021}}</ref>
