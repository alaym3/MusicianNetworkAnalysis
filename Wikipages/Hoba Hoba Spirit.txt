'''Hoba Hoba Spirit''' is a musical fusion band based in [[Casablanca]], [[Morocco]] that was formed in 1998.<ref>{{cite news |title=HIT RADIO fête l'album NEFS & NIYA du groupe HOBA HOBA SPIRIT |url=http://www.emarrakech.info/HIT-RADIO-fete-l-album-NEFS-NIYA-du-groupe-HOBA-HOBA-SPIRIT_a32331.html |newspaper=Marrakech |date=February 8, 2010 |access-date=May 18, 2011}}</ref> It is composed of Adil Hanine ([[drummer]]), Anouar Zehouani ([[guitar]]ist), Saâd Bouidi ([[bassist|Bass guitar]]), Reda Allali - ([[vocals|vocalist]] and guitarist) and Othmane Hmimar([[percussion]]ist). The name of the group is based on a song by [[Bob Marley]].<ref>{{cite news |title=400.000 personnes à la clôture du Festival gnaoua, musiques du monde : la musique célébrée dans une ambiance fraternelle |author=Hassan Alaoui |url=http://www.lematin.ma/Actualite/Express/ArticlePrint.asp?id=42238 |newspaper=[[Le Matin du Sahara et du Maghreb]] |date=June 28, 2004 |access-date=May 18, 2011}}</ref>

Hoba Hoba Spirit's musical style mixes [[Rock music|Rock]], [[Reggae]] and [[Gnawa music|Gnawa]]. They refer to their music as "Hayha". Their lyrics are mostly in [[Darija]] ([[Moroccan Arabic]] dialect), French and sometimes English. Recurring themes in their songs are the disorientation and confusion of young Moroccans.

A notable duo of Hoba Hoba Spirit was with Moroccan rapper Bigg in a single called "Goulou Bazz",<ref>{{Citation|title=Hoba Hoba Spirit (Ft. Don Bigg) – Baz|url=https://genius.com/Hoba-hoba-spirit-baz-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2021-02-22}}</ref> which was a huge success. The single is included in the album Trabando.

Since 2003, the band has emerged as one of the most popular rock acts in Morocco, frequently playing the country's major festivals, such as the [[Mawazine]] Festival in Rabat, L'Boulevard Tremplin in Casablanca, Timitar Festival in Agadir, and the [[Gnaoua World Music Festival|Gnaoua and World Music Festival in Essaouira]].

During the 2007 Mghrib Music Award, Hoba Hoba Spirit won three awards in the categories of "best fusion artist, best album for "Trabando", released in 2007, and best title for the song "Fhamator."<ref>{{cite news |title=Mghrib Music Awards : Hoba hoba spirit rafle trois prix |author=Amine Harmachis |url=http://www.yabiladi.com/article-culture-678.html |newspaper=Yabiladi |date=May 13, 2008 |access-date=May 18, 2011}}</ref>
