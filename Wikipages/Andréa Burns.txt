'''Andréa Burns''' (born February 21, 1971) is an American actress and singer, best known for her portrayal of the hairdresser Daniela in [[Lin-Manuel Miranda]]'s [[Tony Award]]-winning musical ''[[In the Heights]]'', as Carmen alongside [[Nathan Lane]] in [[Douglas Carter Beane]]'s ''[[The Nance]]'' , and as Mrs. Spamboni in ''[[The Electric Company (2009 TV series)|The Electric Company]]''.

==Early life==
Burns was born in [[Miami Beach]], Florida to a [[Jews|Jewish]] father and a [[Venezuelans|Venezuelan]] mother.<ref>[http://www.talkinbroadway.com/cabaret/081106.html "Cabaret"] talkinbroadway.com</ref><ref>[http://www.playbill.com/celebritybuzz/article/122528-ONSTAGE-BACKSTAGE-Hitting-New-Heights "Hitting New Heights"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120107012212/http://www.playbill.com/celebritybuzz/article/122528-ONSTAGE-BACKSTAGE-Hitting-New-Heights |date=January 7, 2012 }} Playbill</ref><ref>[http://www.robertwestfield.com/archive/westcoasttour.htm ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150610215107/http://www.robertwestfield.com/archive/westcoasttour.htm |date=June 10, 2015 }} robertwestfield.com</ref> She has described herself as "…a Venezuelan Jewess from Miami who grew up loving Broadway."<ref>{{cite news| title =Q&A with Andrea Burns | date=April 28, 2009 | url=http://broadwayworld.com/article/QA_with_Andrea_Burns_on_IN_THE_HEIGHTS_Her_Actors_Fund_Concert_at_Feinsteins_and_More_20090428 |work=broadwayworld.com | accessdate=August 5, 2016}}</ref> She spent many summers training at the [[French Woods Festival of the Performing Arts]] and is a graduate of Miami's [[New World School of the Arts]].

==Career==
Burns began her career touring the opera houses of Europe as Maria in ''[[West Side Story]]'' when she was 18 years old. She has appeared on Broadway as "[[Belle (Beauty and the Beast)|Belle]]" in [[Disney]]’s ''[[Beauty and the Beast (musical)|Beauty and the Beast]]'', as "Vicki Nichols" in ''[[The Full Monty (musical)|The Full Monty]]'', as Googie Gomez in ''[[The Ritz (play)|The Ritz]]'' and has sung concerts at [[Carnegie Hall]]. Burns was in the original company of [[Jason Robert Brown]]’s critically acclaimed ''[[Songs for a New World]]'' and since has become one of the foremost interpreters of his work. Burns portrayed Lucille Frank in the national tour of [[Jason Robert Brown]]'s ''[[Parade (musical)|Parade]]'', directed by [[Harold Prince]], for which she received a [[Touring Broadway Awards]] nomination for Best Actress.<ref>{{cite news |year=2001 |url=http://www.nationalbroadwayawards.com/winners-2001.html |website=Nationalbroadwayawards.com |title=Winners – National Broadway Theatre Awards |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20020215112021/http://www.nationalbroadwayawards.com/winners-2001.html |archivedate=February 15, 2002 |accessdate=June 5, 2013}}</ref> She was also selected by [[Stephen Sondheim]] to create the role of Celeste in [[Saturday Night (musical)|''Saturday Night'']] at [[Second Stage Theatre]].

Her début solo album ''A Deeper Shade Of Red'' was described by [[Playbill]] as "superb on all counts!" In 2006, "100 Stories," a single she co-wrote with her brother, music producer Mike Burns, rose to the No. 2 spot on the 2006 [[Dance Club Songs|Billboard Hot Dance Club Play]] charts.

Television credits include [[Marvel Comics]]'s'' [[Jessica Jones (TV series)|Jessica Jones]]'' on [[Netflix]], ''[[Kevin Can Wait]]'', ''[[Sky Dancers]]'', ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit|Law & Order: SVU]]'', '' [[Rescue Me (U.S. TV series)|Rescue Me]]'', ''[[Blue Bloods (TV series)|Blue Bloods]]'', ''[[The Electric Company (2009 TV series)]]'', and ''[[Wonder Pets]]''. She can also be heard on the original [[cast recording]]s of ''[[In The Heights]]'', ''[[Songs for a New World]]'', ''This Ordinary Thursday – The Songs of [[Georgia Stitt]]'', ''[[Saturday Night (musical)|Saturday Night]]'', ''It's Only Life'', ''[[Dear Edwina]]'', ''[[Shine!]]'', ''[[Broadway Bound]]'', and'' Broadway Musicals of 1953''.

Most recently, Andrea starred as "Lenora" in the 2015 film ''[[Akron (2015 film)|Akron]]'', an official selection of the Out on Film Festival 2015.

==Personal life==
She is married to director Peter Flynn.  Their son, Hudson Flynn, is featured on the cover art of her solo album, ''A Deeper Shade of Red''.
