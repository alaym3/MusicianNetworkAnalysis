'''Carla Patricia Morrison Flores''' (born 19 July 1986, [[Tecate]], [[Baja California]]), known professionally as '''Carla Morrison''', is a Mexican [[indie-pop]] singer and composer. She has released three [[studio albums]] and has received various awards and nominations including two [[Grammy Award]] nominations and three [[Latin Grammy Awards]].

== Early life ==
Carla Morrison was born in Tecate, Baja California, to Mexican parents, Porfiria Flores and Hilario Morrison. Her father, previously surnamed Vera, obtained his last name when he was adopted by William Guy Morrison, born in California to an English migrant named Laura Morrison.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.infobae.com/america/mexico/2021/02/15/esta-es-la-razon-por-la-que-carla-morrison-trabajara-con-la-nasa-en-su-llegada-a-marte/|title=Esta es la razón por la que Carla Morrison trabajará con la NASA en su llegada a Marte|website=[[Infobae]]|date=February 15, 2021|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref>

Morrison lived in Tecate during her early years, taking classes of dancing, drawing and dancing. At 17, she moved to [[Phoenix, Arizona]] to study music at the [[Mesa Community College]], later dropping out to perform at the lead singer of the band ''Babaluca'' alongside Nicolas Kizer and Niki Petta.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/music/carla-morrison-if-id-have-followed-the-rules-id-still-not-be-doing-anything-6596781|title=Carla Morrison: "If I'd Have Followed the Rules, I'd Still Not Be Doing Anything"
|website=Phoenix New Times|first=Yezmin|last=Villarreal|date=May 22, 2013|accessdate=August 28, 2021}}</ref> The band started working with Mark Erickson from Colorstone but never released a project, Morrison would later abandon the band in order to pursue a solo career.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.artforprogress.org/afp-live-review-carla-morrison-prospect-park-bandshell/|title=AFP Live Review: Carla Morrison @ Prospect Park Bandshell|website=Artforprogress|accessdate=August 28, 2021}}</ref>

== Musical career  ==
In 2009, Morrison released her first EP, entitled ''Aprendiendo a Aprender'', produced independently in the home studio of Jordan Beriault in [[Tempe, Arizona]], the record contains six songs composed and produced by herself, plus a cover of [[Ramón Ayala]]'s song "Tragos de Amargo Licor".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mehaceruido.com/2009/06/entrevista-carla-morrison/|title=Entrevista / Carla Morrison|website=Mehaceruido|date=June 25, 2009|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> Her second EP ''Mientras tú Dormías...'' was released in 2010 and was produced by Mexican singer and songwriter [[Natalia Lafourcade]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tn.com.ar/musica/noticias/2016/11/08/carla-morrison-sobre-su-ultimo-disco-hablo-del-amor-desde-otro-punto-de-vista/|title=Carla Morrison, sobre su último disco: "Hablo del amor desde otro punto de vista"|website=TN|date=November 8, 2016|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> The latter EP received a nomination for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album|Best Alternative Music Album]] at the [[12th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]].<ref name="LG12th">{{cite web|url=http://www.cubadebate.cu/noticias/2011/09/15/lista-completa-de-los-nominados-al-grammy-latino-2011-omara-y-adalberto-otra-vez/|title=Lista completa de los nominados al Grammy Latino 2011: Omara y Adalberto, otra vez|website=Cubadebate|date=September 15, 2011|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> In 2011, she performed her EPs in several venues including the [[Teatro Metropólitan]] and the Lunario of the [[Auditorio Nacional (Mexico)|Auditorio Nacional]], both in [[Mexico]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mehaceruido.com/2011/11/carla-morrison-teatro-metropolitan/|title=Carla Morrison @Teatro Metropólitan|website=Mehaceruido|date=November 28, 2011|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://rockeros.net/eventos/2340-carla-morrison-en-el-lunario-del-auditorio-nacional-ciudad-de-mexico-df.html|title=Carla Morrison En El Lunario Del Auditorio Nacional Ciudad De Mexico Df|website=Rockeros|date=May 13, 2011|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref>

On March 24, 2012, she released her debut album ''[[Déjenme Llorar]]'' produced by Juan Manuel Torreblanca from the band Torreblanca and Andrés Landon.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reforma.com/aplicacioneslibre/articulo/default.aspx?id=207095&md5=e92b41dd6bd6d57cfe3c89bf27cd479f&ta=0dfdbac11765226904c16cb9ad1b2efe|title=Se va... ¡Sin Despedir!|website=Reforma|date=April 17, 2014|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> The album was certified platinum in [[Mexico]] and entered both the [[Billboard Top Latin Albums|Top Latin Albums]] and [[Latin Pop Albums]] [[Billboard charts]], at number 56 and 15 respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208125933/http://www.amprofon.com.mx/certificaciones-amprofon.php?disquera=&anio=&tipoAlbum=&tipoCertificacion=&aniocer=&key=carla+morrison&action=Buscar|title=AMPOFRON the [[13th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]], Morrison received four nominations including [[Latin Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] and won [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album|Best Alternative Music Album]] for the album and [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Song|Best Alternative Song]] for the title track "Déjemne Llorar".<ref name="LG13">{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/files/pages/57thpresslist12042014-with_aoy.pdf |title=Final Nomination List : 57th Grammy Awards |publisher=Grammy Award |accessdate=21 February 2015}}</ref> The album also received a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Rock, Urban or Alternative Album|Best Latin Rock, Urban or Alternative Album]] at the [[55th Annual Grammy Awards]].<ref name="G55th">{{cite news|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-grammys-2013-complete-list-of-nominees-and-winners-story.html |title=Grammys 2013: Complete list of nominees and winners |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]] |accessdate=3 September 2017}}</ref> After the release of the album, she participated in different music festivals, including the Festival Viva la Canción 2012 in [[Madrid]], [[Estéreo Picnic Festival#2013|Estéreo Picnic Festival]] in [[Bogotá]], [[Pal Norte#Pa’l Norte Rock Festival 2012|Pa’l Norte Rock Festival 2012]] in [[Monterrey]] and [[Lollapalooza Chile#2013|Lollapalooza Chile 2013]] in [[Santiago]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://filtermexico.com/2012/04/23/resena-festival-viva-la-cancion-en-madrid-russian-red-natalia-lafourcade-carla-morrison-y-lido-pimienta/|title=[Reseña] Festival Viva la Canción en Madrid: Russian Red, Natalia Lafourcade, Carla Morrison y Lido Pimienta|website=Filtermexico|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite 2021|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.indierocks.mx/musica/conciertos/pal-norte-rock-festival/|title=PA’L NORTE ROCK FESTIVAL|website=Indierocks|date=November 30, 2012|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://paniko.cl/lollapalooza-chile-anuncia-nuevo-sideshow-de-manuel-garcia-y-carla-morrison/|title=Lollapalooza Chile anuncia nuevo sideshow de Manuel García y Carla Morrison|website=Paniko|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref>

[[File:PressPhoto 2.jpg|thumb|Carla Morrison - 2020]]
In 2013, Morrison was featured in the song "Yo sé que está en tu corazón" from [[Juan Gabriel]]'s album ''[[Los Dúo, Vol. 2]]'', the album was composed of new versions of songs by Gabriel as duets with different artists.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sandiegored.com/es/noticias/49141/Carla-Morrison-canta-con-Juan-Gabriel|title=Carla Morrison canta con Juan Gabriel - SanDiegoRed.com|website=SanDiegoRed|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> Also in 2013, Morrison released her third EP ''Jugando en Serio'', consisting of acoustic versions of the songs "Yo Sigo Aquí", "Compartir", "Lágrimas", "Buena Malicia" and "Pan Dulce" from her previous records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vozpopuli.com/altavoz/cultura/carla-morrison-voz-indie-latinoamericano_0_1040596407.html|title=Carla Morrison, la voz del indie latinoamericano|website=Vozpopuli|date=July 1, 2017|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref>

In 2015, she made her acting debut in the romantic comedy film ''[[Ana Maria in Novela Land]]'' as Laura.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://elsantodelrock.com/2015/02/03/carla-morrison-debuta-como-actriz/|title=Carla Morrison debuta como actriz|website=El Santo del Rock|date=February 3, 2015|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> On November 6, 2015, she released her second studio album ''[[Amor Supremo]]'' through Cosmica.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.indierocks.mx/musica/resenas/carla-morrison-amor-supremo/|title=Carla Morrison - Amor supremo|website=Indierocks|first=Adriana|last=Cartagena|date=November 17, 2015|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> The album was recorded through eight months at [[Playas de Tijuana]] with production from Alejandro Jiménez and Demián Jiménez.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://zetatijuana.com/2016/05/carla-morrison-presume-su-amor-supremo/|title=Carla Morrison presume su “Amor Supremo”|website=Zeta Tijuana|date=May 9, 2016|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> With the album, she earned her second [[Grammy Award]] nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Rock, Urban or Alternative Album|Best Latin Rock, Urban or Alternative Album]] while at the [[17th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]], she was nominated for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album|Best Alternative Music Album]] and won [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Song|Best Alternative Song]] for "Vez Primera".<ref name="G59th">{{cite news|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-grammys-2017-nominations-winners-list-20161205-story.html |title=2017 Grammy Awards: Complete list of nominees |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]] |accessdate=6 December 2016}}</ref><ref name="LG17th">{{cite web|url=https://www.latingrammy.com/en/nominees |title=Nominees 17th Annual Latin Grammy Awards |publisher=Latin Grammy Award |accessdate=17 November 2016}}</ref> To promote the album, Morrison performed at various festivals and venues like the [[Coachella Festival]] in [[United States]] and the [[Teatro de la Ciudad|Teatro Esperanza Iris]] in [[Ciudad de México]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.milenio.com/espectaculos/carla-morrison-imprime-sello-mexicano-coachella|title=Carla Morrison imprime sello mexicano a Coachella|website=Milenio|date=April 15, 2016|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.melomagazine.com.mx/carla-morrison-en-el-teatro-de-la-ciudad-esperanza-iris/|title=Carla Morrison en el Teatro de la Ciudad “Esperanza Iris”|website=MeloMagazine|date=December 19, 2016|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> On December 8, 2016, she released the EP ''La Niña del Tambor'', the project consisted in six [[Christmas carols]] both in english and spanish including "[[Silent Night|Noche de Paz]]", "[[White Christmas (song)|White Christmas]]" and "[[Have Yourself a Merry Little Christmas]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rrnoticias.mx/2016/11/17/carla-morrison-se-adelanta-epoca-decembrina-posada-villancicos/|title=Carla Morrison se adelanta a época decembrina con posada y villancicos|website=RRNoticias|date=November 17, 2016|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref>

On June 9, 2017, she released ''[[Amor Supremo Desnudo]]'', the album was produced alongside Alejandro Jiménez and consists of thirteen acoustic versions of songs from her previous albums plus two new songs, "Te Regalo" and "Dime Mentiras".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://plasmamx.net/2017/06/14/carla-morrison-amor-supremo-desnudo/|title=Carla Morrison – Amor Supremo Desnudo|website=PlasmaMX|date=June 14, 2017|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> The idea for an acoustic version of her albums came during a tour in [[Spain]], where according to Morrison, "many people that I met gave me the idea of doing an acoustic version, so I didn't stop until making it, I realized that it sounded really good".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.publimetro.com.mx/mx/entretenimiento/2017/06/10/carla-morrison-se-desnuda-ante-amor-supremo.html|title=Carla Morrison “se desnuda” ante Amor Supremo|website=Publimetro México|first=Marisol|last=Orta|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> The album was nominated for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album|Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album]] at the [[19th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]].<ref name="LG19th">{{cite news|url=https://www.univision.com/especiales/latin-grammy/lista-completa-de-los-nominados-a-los-premios-latin-grammy-2018|title=Lista completa de los nominados a los premios Latin GRAMMY 2018|publisher=infobae|accessdate=20 September 2018|date=20 September 2018|language=es}}</ref> In 2018, she collaborated in the single "Ser Paloma" by Mexican singer [[Lila Downs]], the music video for the song featured women of different ages, backgrounds and professions to represent the diversity of women in society.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://anton.com.mx/2018/07/lila-downs-lanzara-el-video-de-su-sencillo-ser-palona-con-carla-morrison/|title=Lila Downs lanzará el vídeo de su sencillo ‘Ser Palona’ con Carla Morrison|website=Anton|date=July 6, 2018|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref> The same year, she appeared in the track "Vibras" from [[J Balvin]]'s album ''[[Vibras (album)|Vibras]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rpp.pe/musica/internacional/j-balvin-estrena-su-nuevo-album-vibras-noticia-1125376|title=J Balvin estrena su nuevo álbum "Vibras"|website=RPP Noticias|date=May 27, 2018|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref>

On September 21, 2020, she released the single "Ansiedad" from her upcoming album ''El Renacimiento'', the music video for the song was directed by Colin Solal Cardo and recorded in [[Lithuania]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/2020/09/24/carla-morrison-regresa-a-la-musica-con-ansiedad-el-primer-sencillo-de-su-disco-el-renacimiento/|title=Carla Morrison regresa a la música con "Ansiedad", el primer sencillo de su disco "El Renacimiento"|website=[[CNN en Español]]|first=Marysabel|last=Huston-Crespo|date=September 24, 2020|accessdate=August 28, 2021|language=es}}</ref>
