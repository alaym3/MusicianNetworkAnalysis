'''Shortparis''' is a Russian [[experimental music|experimental]] group formed in [[Saint Petersburg]] in 2012.

== History ==
Nikolai Komyagin, Alexander Ionin, and Pavel Lesnikov met in their hometown, [[Novokuznetsk]], and formed Shortparis in 2012 upon moving to Saint Petersburg. They were later joined by St. Petersburg natives Danila Kholodkov and Alexander Galyanov.<ref name="village" />

In 2013, Shortparis released their debut album ''Docheri'' ({{Lang-ru|«Дочери»|lit=Daughters}}), with songs in English and French.<ref name="docheri"/> In 2017, they released the album ''Paskha'' ({{Lang-ru|«Пасха»|lit=Easter}}). In contrast to their previous album, all of the songs on ''Paskha'' are in Russian.<ref name="paskha"/> In 2019, Shortparis released their third album, ''Tak zakalyalas' stal'' ({{Lang-ru|«Так закалялась сталь»|lit=Thus the Steel Was Tempered}}).<ref name="quietus2019" />

In September 2020, Shortparis released the single "KoKoKo / Struktury ne vykhodyat na ulitsy" ({{Lang-ru|«КоКоКо / Cтруктуры Не Выходят На Улицы»|lit=CluckCluckCluck (Structures Don't Take To The Streets)}}) and an accompanying music video. The title of the track refers to a piece of graffiti from the [[May 68|1968 Paris riots]].<ref name="quietus2020" /><ref name="meduza2020" />

In January 2021, Shortparis contributed a track to the album ''Sokhrani moyu rech' navsegda'' ({{Lang-ru|«Сохрани мою речь навсегда»|lit=Keep My Words Forever}}), a compilation of songs based on [[Osip Mandelstam|Osip Mandelstam's]] work, released in honor of the 130th anniversary of the poet's birth.<ref name="mandelstam" />

In June 2021, Shortparis released their fourth studio album, ''Yablonny sad'' ({{Lang-ru|«Яблонный сад»|links=no|lit=Apple Orchard}}).<ref name="newsmuz"/>

== Live performances ==
Shortparis opened for [[The Kooks]] in 2015 and [[alt-J]] in 2017 at Saint Petersburg's A2.<ref name="nobility" /><ref name="beauty" /> The band started gaining attention in English-language music publications for their performances at [[Haldern Pop]] Festival, MENT Festival,<ref name="louder" /><ref name="ment" /> [[Off Festival|OFF Festival]],<ref name="off" /> Pop-Kultur Berlin,<ref name="gigwise" /><ref name="kultur" /> and Station Narva Festival<ref name="drowned" /><ref name="narva" /> in 2018. In April 2018, Shortparis performed alongside Kazuskoma, Spasibo, [[Glintshake]], and [[Elektroforez]] in [[Minsk]], [[Warsaw]], [[Poznań|Poznan]], [[Berlin]], and [[Kaliningrad]] as part of a tour showcasing Russian bands.<ref name="vice" /> In May 2019, Shortparis undertook their first UK tour, including performances at [[Liverpool Sound City]]<ref name="13best" /> and [[The Great Escape Festival]].<ref name="diymag" /> In August 2019, they played at Brave! Factory Festival in [[Kyiv]].<ref name="brave" />

== Musical style ==
Shortparis performs songs in Russian, French, and English.<ref name="interview" /> According to John Doran of ''[[The Quietus]]'', Shortparis is following in [[Sergey Kuryokhin|Sergey Kuryokhin's]] legacy of provocative performance art; Doran describes the group as "Ambitious, bombastic, incredibly pretentious, erotic, thrilling, impossible to pin down, vaguely deviant, fun to dance to and full of revolutionary potential".<ref name="review" />

== Awards and nominations ==
Shortparis made the shortlist for [[GQ Russia]]'s "Discovery of the Year" award in 2019.<ref name="gq" /> At the 2019 Jager Music Awards, Shortparis won Band of the Year, as well as Single of the Year and Video of the Year for "Strashno" ({{Lang-ru|«Страшно»|lit=Scary}}).<ref name="jager" /><ref name="msktimes" />

== Media appearances ==
Shortparis appeared in the 2018 film ''[[Leto (film)|Leto]]'' ({{Lang-ru|Лето|lit=Summer}}), performing a cover of [[David Bowie|David Bowie's]] "[[All the Young Dudes]]".<ref name="esquire" /> In 2019, their song "Chto-to osoboe vo mne" ({{Lang-ru|Что-то особое во мне}}) featured in the episode "Chapter 4: SYZYGY" of season 2 of ''[[The OA]]''.<ref name="tunefind" />
