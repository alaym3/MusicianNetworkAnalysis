'''Alessandra Amoroso''' ({{IPA-it|alesˈsandra amoˈroːzo; -oːso}}; born 12 August 1986 in [[Galatina]], [[Province of Lecce]]) is an Italian [[Pop music|pop]]/[[Soul music|soul]]<ref name="allmusic"/> singer, songwriter, [[Record producer|producer]] and TV personality. She was the winner of the 2009 edition of the Italian talent show ''[[Amici di Maria De Filippi]]''. Since then all her eight albums have peaked at the top of the ''Italian Albums Chart'' and she has released successful singles and collaborations, including three number one hits, selling over 2.5 millions records in Italy.

In 2014 she became the first female Italian artist to win the [[MTV Europe Music Award for Best European Act]], winning overall three [[MTV Europe Music Award]], seven [[Wind Music Awards]], and received several nominations at the [[Kids' choice awards]] and [[World Music Awards]].

She also acted in [[Io che amo solo te (film)|''Io che amo solo te'']] by [[Marco Ponti]] in 2015 and she co-host with [[Gianni Morandi]] the tv program ''Grazie a tutti'' in 2009.

==Biography and career==

===Early career===
Since her youth, Amoroso participated in numerous local competitions, obtaining good results.<ref>{{Cite web | title = Biography on the official site | url = http://www.alessandraamoroso.it/it/content/biography | access-date = 10 September 2010 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20100903161224/http://www.alessandraamoroso.it/it/content/biography | archive-date = 3 September 2010 | url-status = dead }}</ref>
In June 2007 she participated in a contest, ''Fiori di Pesco'', singing the song ''Amor mio'', and placed first among the competitors, and was rewarded by Mogol.<ref>{{Cite web | title = Minutes of the second edition of 'Fiori di pesco' | url = http://www.summertimeweb.it/pagine/speciale_finale_fdp.html | access-date = 10 September 2010 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110722053858/http://www.summertimeweb.it/pagine/speciale_finale_fdp.html | archive-date = 22 July 2011 | url-status = dead }}</ref> When she was seventeen, she participated in the auditions for ''Amici'' but she failed.<ref>{{Cite web| title = Official myspace of the singer | url = https://www.myspace.com/alessandraamorosoofficial}}</ref>

===''Amici di Maria De Filippi''===
[[File:Alessandra Amoroso Stupida Tour Torino.jpg|thumb|left|175px|Alessandra Amoroso in concerto a [[Torino]].]]
{{Main|Immobile (Alessandra Amoroso song)|Find A Way (Alessandra Amoroso song)}}
On 5 October 2008, Amoroso passed the audition for the eighth season of ''Amici di Maria De Filippi'', singing the single from [[Alicia Keys]], "[[If I Ain't Got You]]". Rudy Zerbi, the chairman of [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony Music Italy]], assigned three unpublished singles to Amoroso. She participated in the recording of the compilation disc of the programme, ''Scialla''. "[[Find a Way (Alessandra Amoroso song)|Find a Way]]" reached the fourth place in the charts, while "[[Immobile (Alessandra Amoroso song)|Immobile]]" reached the top position.<ref>{{Cite web| title = Immobile on the FIMI Chart | url = http://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Alessandra+Amoroso&titel=Immobile&cat=s}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web| title = Find a way on the FIMI Chart | url = http://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Alessandra+Amoroso&titel=Find+A+Way&cat=s}}</ref> During the program, the singer distinguished herself thanks to her voice: all the teachers, especially Luca Jurman, recognized a sandblasted timbre.<ref>{{Cite web| title = Comments on Alessandra Amoroso by Luca Jurman | date = 25 March 2009| url = http://www.tvblog.it/post/13283/alessandra-amoroso-vince-amici-8}}</ref> On 25 March 2009, Amoroso was proclaimed winner of ''Amici di Maria De Filippi'', winning the first prize of 200,000 [[euro]]s. On the same evening, she also received the critics' prize, a scholarship worth 50,000 euros. With this money, Amoroso says she will continue to study with [[voice teacher]] Luca Jurman.<ref>{{Cite web | title = Alessandra Amoroso: this is how I'll spend the money I won | url = http://www.musicroom.it/articolo/alessandra-amoroso-ecco-come-spendero-i-200mila-euro/5511/ | access-date = 10 September 2010 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110107091827/http://www.musicroom.it/articolo/alessandra-amoroso-ecco-come-spendero-i-200mila-euro/5511/ | archive-date = 7 January 2011 | url-status = dead }}</ref>

===''Cinque passi in più''===
Alessandra announced in late September 2011 that she was working about a new project of live about and she confirmed that it was ready just to the end of that year, in Dicembre.
Comes out 4 November 2011 E' vero che vuoi restare, the single who anticipates the release of about a month the live album "Cinque passi in più" containing a cd with 5 new songs and a live cd with the concert that Alessandra kept in Milano on 22 December 2010 at Mediolanum Forum of Assago. A Deluxe Version of the album containing a DVD of the concert and backstage scenes, five postcards, photo of Alessandra and a 36-page-booklet is also available.
