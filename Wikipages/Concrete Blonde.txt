'''Concrete Blonde''' was an American [[alternative rock]] band from [[Hollywood, California]]. They were initially active from 1982 to 1995, and reunited twice: first from 2001 to 2004, and again from 2010 to 2012. They were best known for their album ''[[Bloodletting (Concrete Blonde album)|Bloodletting]]'' (1990), its top 20 single "[[Joey (Concrete Blonde song)|Joey]]", and [[Johnette Napolitano]]'s distinctive vocal style.

==Career==
Singer-songwriter/bassist [[Johnette Napolitano]] formed the band with guitarist [[James Mankey]] in Los Angeles, in 1982. Their first recording was the song "Heart Attack", released under the band name '''Dreamers''' on the compilation album, ''The D.I.Y. Album'' (1982).<ref>[http://www.popsike.com/php/detaildata.php?itemnr=4853356424 Auction entry on ''The D.I.Y. Album''], ''Popsike.com'', Retrieved February 21, 2013.</ref> Joined by drummer [[Michael Murphy (drummer)|Michael Murphy]], they became '''Dream 6''' , releasing an eponymous [[Extended play|EP]] in on the independent label "Happy Hermit" in 1983 (released in France by [[Madrigal (French record label)|Madrigal]]).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Dream-6-Dream-6/master/329062|title = Dream 6 - Dream 6|website = [[Discogs]]}}</ref> When they signed with [[I.R.S. Records]] in 1986, their label-mate [[Michael Stipe]] suggested the name Concrete Blonde,<ref>{{cite news|title=Concrete Blonde's Ambition – Concrete Blond leaves a little blood on the tracks|author= Linden, Amy|date=September 1990|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=b9_qs_-pof8C&q=johnette+napolitano+spin+magazine&pg=PA56|page=56|access-date=August 20, 2010}}</ref> describing the contrast between their hard rock music and introspective lyrics. They were joined by drummer [[Harry Rushakoff]] on their eponymous debut album.<ref name="St. Petersburg">{{cite news|url=http://www.sptimes.com/2002/04/14/Floridian/Among_the_ghosts_and_.shtml|title=Among the ghosts and demons|author=Gina Vivinetto|newspaper=St. Petersburg Times|date=April 14, 2002}}</ref>

Their first release was ''[[Concrete Blonde (album)|Concrete Blonde]]'' (1986), which included "Still in Hollywood".  They added a full time bass guitarist, Alan Bloch, for their album ''[[Free (Concrete Blonde album)|Free]]'' (1989). This allowed Napolitano to focus on her singing without the burden of playing the bass at the same time. This album included the college radio hit "God Is a Bullet".<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/free-mw0000204027|title=Concrete Blonde|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 20, 2013}}</ref>

Their third album, ''[[Bloodletting (Concrete Blonde album)|Bloodletting]]'' (1990), became their most commercially successful, reaching #8 in Australia. [[Roxy Music]] drummer [[Paul Thompson (musician)|Paul Thompson]] replaced Rushakoff on ''Bloodletting'' while Rushakoff was in treatment for drug addiction.<ref name="St. Petersburg"/> The album was certified gold by the RIAA<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?table=SEARCH_RESULTS&artist=Concrete%20Blonde&format=ALBUM&go=Search&perPage=50#|title=RIAA – Gold & Platinum – October 10, 1991: Concrete Blonde certified albums|publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]|access-date=June 18, 2013}}</ref> and included their highest charting single, "[[Joey (Concrete Blonde song)|Joey]]", which spent 21 weeks on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Top 100 Chart, peaking at 19, and #2 in Australia.<ref name="billboard.com">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=concrete blonde|chart=all}}|title=Artist/Concrete Blonde|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=February 20, 2013}}</ref>

''[[Walking in London (album)|Walking in London]]'' (1992) saw the return of original drummer Rushakoff (due to Thompson's immigration problems); its successor ''[[Mexican Moon]]'' (1993) included the ''Bloodletting'' lineup with Thompson back on drums. Neither album was received well commercially or critically, though Walking in London reached #18 in Australia, and Napolitano broke up the band in 1993.<ref name="allmusic" />

The band reunited in 1997, with Napolitano and Mankey teaming up with the band [[Los Illegals]] for the album ''[[Concrete Blonde y Los Illegals]]''. The vocals were primarily in Spanish. During live shows, the band changed the refrain for "Still in Hollywood" to "Still in the Barrio", and featured covers of [[Led Zeppelin]]'s "Immigrant Song" and [[Jimi Hendrix]]'s "Little Wing".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/509597/concrete-blonde-y-los-illegals-rock-l.jhtml|title=Concrete Blonde y Los Illegals Rock L. A.|publisher=[[MTV]]|date=May 8, 1987}}</ref>

The band reunited again in 2001 and released the album ''[[Group Therapy (Concrete Blonde album)|Group Therapy]]'' (2002).  The album was recorded in 10 days and included Rushakoff once again on drums. Rushakoff was eventually kicked out of the band for failing to show up for shows. After initially being replaced on tour by lighting tech Mike Devitt, he was eventually replaced on a long-term basis by Gabriel Ramirez.<ref name="St. Petersburg"/> ''[[Mojave (album)|Mojave]]'' was released in 2004.

== Retirement and post-retirement ==
On June 5, 2006, Napolitano announced that the band had officially retired. From the Concrete Blonde website, there was the following open message: "Thanks to everyone who heard and believed in the music. Music lives on. Keep listening. Keep believing, keep dreaming. Like a ripple, the music moves and travels and finds you. Drive to the music, Make love to the music, cry to the music. That's why we made it. Long after we're gone the music will still be there. Thanks to everyone who helped us bring the music to you & thanks to every face and every heart in every audience all over the world."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebrag.com/2010/09/06/interview-concrete-blonde/|title=Concrete Blonde – Still Bloodletting|author=Mike Gee|publisher=The Brag|date=September 6, 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130411225319/http://www.thebrag.com/2010/09/06/interview-concrete-blonde/|archive-date=April 11, 2013}}</ref>

On July 13, 2010, [[Shout! Factory]] released a remastered 20th anniversary edition of ''Bloodletting''.<ref name="allmusic" /> It features six bonus tracks: "I Want You", "Little Wing", the French extended version of "Bloodletting (The Vampire Song)", and live versions of "Roses Grow", "The Sky Is A Poisonous Garden", and "Tomorrow, Wendy".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/128468-concrete-blondebloodletting-20th-anniversary-edition/|title=Concrete Blonde: Bloodletting (20th Anniversary Edition)|author=Andrew Gilstrap|magazine=[[PopMatters]]|date=July 30, 2010}}</ref> An error in the packaging of the re-release uses early non-album period photos featuring original drummer Harry Rushakoff, who had been replaced the night before the first studio session for "Bloodletting" by Roxy Music drummer Paul Thompson. The band followed the release with the "20 Years of Bloodletting: The Vampires Rise" tour through the rest of that year.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://content.usatoday.com/communities/popcandy/post/2010/06/concrete-blonde-kick-off-a-bloodletting-anniversary-tour/1#.UVG16DtsIdo|title=Concrete Blonde kick off a 'Bloodletting' anniversary tour|author=Whitney Matheson|newspaper=[[USA Today]]|date=June 11, 2010}}</ref>

In 2012, the band released the single "Rosalie" with the B-side "I Know the Ghost". In December 2012, the band engaged in a small tour of nine cities, mostly on the east coast of the U.S.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://popshifter.com/2012-12-06/new-vinyl-concrete-blonde-rosalie-bw-i-know-the-ghost/|title=Rosalie|author=Cait Brennan|publisher=Popshifter|access-date=December 6, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121213094849/http://popshifter.com/2012-12-06/new-vinyl-concrete-blonde-rosalie-bw-i-know-the-ghost/|archive-date=December 13, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Compilation and live albums===
* ''[[Still in Hollywood]]'' (1994) – compilation of live recordings, B-sides, and previously unreleased material
* ''[[Recollection: The Best of Concrete Blonde]]'' (1996) – compilation of 17 tracks from first five albums, plus live cover version of [[Mercedes Benz (song)|Mercedes Benz]].
* ''Classic Masters'' (2002) – 24-bit remastering of 12 tracks from first five albums
* ''[[Live in Brazil 2002]]'' (2003) – double live album
*  ''The Essential'' (2005) – includes 13 remastered tracks from first four albums, and a different version of the song "Sun"
