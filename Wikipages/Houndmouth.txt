'''Houndmouth''' is an American alternative blues band from [[New Albany, Indiana]], formed in 2011, consisting of Matt Myers (guitar, vocals), Zak Appleby (bass, vocals), and Shane Cody (drums, vocals).<ref name="trio">{{cite web |url=http://www.indystar.com/story/entertainment/music/2016/06/07/houndmouth-myers-sedona-garfield-indianapolis-wtts-mokb-borns/85476614/ |title=Reduced to a Trio, Houndmouth Finds Bigger Sound |last=Lindquist |first=David |date=June 7, 2016 |work=[[Indianapolis Star]] |publisher=[[Gannett]]}}</ref> According to lead singer Matt Myers at a concert in Little Egypt on October 1, 2016, the band is recording their live audiobook.

==Background==
Houndmouth formed in the summer of 2011. After playing locally in Louisville and Indiana,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indystar.com/story/entertainment/music/2013/11/15/houndmouth-represents-indiana-with-authentic-sound/3576637/ |title=Houndmouth represents Indiana with authentic sound |website=Indystar.com |accessdate=2020-03-12}}</ref> they performed at the [[South by Southwest|SXSW music festival]] in March 2012 to promote their homemade self-titled EP. [[Geoff Travis]], the head of [[Rough Trade Records|Rough Trade]] was in the audience and offered a contract shortly after.<ref>[http://leoweekly.com/music/music-issue-2012-houndmouth%E2%80%99s-big-break Music Issue 2012: Houndmouth's big break | Louisville's Alt-Weekly | LEO Weekly<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20140515195216/http://leoweekly.com/music/music-issue-2012-houndmouth%E2%80%99s-big-break |date=2014-05-15 }}</ref> In 2012, the band was named "Band Of The Week" by [[The Guardian]].<ref>{{cite news|author=Paul Lester |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/jul/06/new-band-houndmouth |title=New band of the day – No 1,303: Houndmouth &#124; Music |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |accessdate=2020-03-12}}</ref>

===''From the Hills Below the City'' (2013)===
In 2013 Houndmouth's debut album, ''From the Hills Below the City'', was released by Rough Trade. This led to performances on Letterman,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbs.com/shows/late_show/video/ONI7AWxp9cLhfx9YXXYLIYTOpzgsd_Yv/david-letterman-houndmouth-ludlow-/ |title=The Late Show with Stephen Colbert - CBS All Access |website=Cbs.com |accessdate=2020-03-12}}</ref> Conan,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://teamcoco.com/video/houndmouth-06-05-13 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-05-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140529081316/http://teamcoco.com/video/houndmouth-06-05-13 |archive-date=2014-05-29 |url-status=dead }}</ref> World Cafe,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2013/09/03/218534897/houndmouth-on-world-cafe |title=Houndmouth On World Cafe : World Cafe |website=Npr.org |date=2013-09-03 |accessdate=2020-03-12}}</ref> and several major festivals (ACL, [[Americana Music Festival & Conference|Americana Music Festival]], [[Bonnaroo Music Festival|Bonnaroo]], [[Lollapalooza]], and [[Newport Folk Festival]]). [[Spin (magazine)|SPIN]] and [[Esquire (magazine)|Esquire.com]] named Houndmouth a "must-see" band at Lollapalooza, and ''[[Garden & Gun]]'' said, "You'd be hard pressed to find a more effortless, well-crafted mix of roots and rock this year than the debut album from this Louisville quartet."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gardenandgun.com/article/must-hear-music-2013 |title=Must-Hear Music of 2013 – Garden & Gun |website=Gardenandgun.com |date=19 November 2013 |accessdate=2020-03-12}}</ref>

===''Little Neon Limelight'' (2015)===
The band's second LP, ''Little Neon Limelight'', was released on March 17, 2015. It includes the single "[[Sedona (song)|Sedona]]". The band performed "Sedona" on the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]'' on March 31, 2015, and at the [[El Rey Theatre]] in Los Angeles was featured on ''[[Last Call with Carson Daly]]'' on November 18<ref>{{cite web|title=Watch Houndmouth: "Sedona" from Last Call with Carson Daly on NBC.com |url=http://www.nbc.com/last-call-with-carson-daly/video/houndmouth-sedona/2939169 |website=NBC |accessdate=2015-11-28 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151201094943/http://www.nbc.com/last-call-with-carson-daly/video/houndmouth-sedona/2939169 |archive-date=2015-12-01 }}</ref> and 27, 2015.

===Katie Toupin departure (2016)===
On April 8, 2016, the band announced that Katie Toupin left the band to pursue other opportunities.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jambands.com/news/2016/04/08/katie-toupin-leaves-houndmouth|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160411123815/http://www.jambands.com/news/2016/04/08/katie-toupin-leaves-houndmouth|archive-date = 2016-04-11|title = Katie Toupin Leaves Houndmouth|date = 8 April 2016}}</ref> The keyboard and Toupin's harmony parts have been performed by Caleb Hickman following Toupin's departure. Graeme Gardiner and Drew Miller subsequently joined the band on dual saxophones.

===''Golden Age'' (2018)===
On August 3, 2018, Houndmouth released their third album, ''Golden Age'', consisting of 10 songs that clock in at just under 36 minutes.<ref>{{cite web|author=Timothy Monger |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/golden-age-mw0003186626 |title=Golden Age - Houndmouth &#124; Songs, Reviews, Credits |website=[[AllMusic]] |date=2018-08-03 |accessdate=2020-03-12}}</ref>

===''Good For You'' (2021)===
On November 5, 2021, Houndmouth released their fourth album, the pandemic written ''Good For You'', consisting of 10 songs and clocking in at 36min and 35sec.  Written and recorded at The Green House in New Albany, Indiana, an 18th century shotgun house owned by the family of drummer Shane Cody, it is accompanied by several videos shot at the house and released around the time of the album's release.
