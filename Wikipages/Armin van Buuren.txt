'''Armin Jozef Jacobus Daniël van Buuren''' {{post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Orange-Nassau|OON]]}} ({{IPAc-en|v|æ|n|_|ˈ|b|j|ʊər|ə|n}} {{respell|van|_|BEWR|ən}},<ref>Van Buuren's own pronunciation that can be heard on ''A State of Trance''.</ref> {{IPA-nl|ˈɑrmɪˈɲoːzəf jaːˈkoːbʏz ˈdaːnijɛl vɑm ˈbyːrə(n)|lang}}; born 25 December 1976)<ref name="armadaartistpg" /> is a Dutch DJ and record producer from [[Leiden]], [[South Holland]]. Since 2001, he has hosted ''[[A State of Trance]]'' (ASOT), a weekly radio show, which is broadcast to nearly 40&nbsp;million listeners in 84 countries on over 100 FM radio stations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.astateoftrance.com/about/|title=About – A State of Trance|access-date=19 May 2016}}</ref> According to the website ''DJs and Festivals'', "the radio show propelled him to stardom and helped cultivate an interest in trance music around the world."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.djsandfestivals.com/2015/armin-van-buuren-to-make-ims-asia-pacific-debut/|title=Armin van Buuren to make IMS Asia-Pacific debut -|date=2015-12-07|website=www.djsandfestivals.com|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-21|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160812043428/http://www.djsandfestivals.com/2015/armin-van-buuren-to-make-ims-asia-pacific-debut/|archive-date=12 August 2016|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Van Buuren has won a number of accolades. He has been ranked the number one DJ by ''[[DJ Mag]]'' a record of five times, four years in a row.<ref>{{cite web|title=17 Armada DJs in DJ Mag Top 100. Though– Armin is No. 1 again!|url=http://www.armadamusic.com/news/2012/10/17-armada-djs-in-dj-mag-top-100-armin-is-1-again/|access-date=19 October 2012}}</ref> He was ranked fourth on the ''DJ Mag'' Top 100 DJs list in 2015, 2016, 2019 and 2020,<ref>{{cite web |title=top 100 DJs|url=https://djmag.com/top100djs |website=djmag.com |access-date=18 November 2020}}</ref> as well as third in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://djmag.com/top100dj|title=Top 100 DJs {{!}} DJMag.com|website=djmag.com|access-date=2017-06-01}}</ref> In 2014, he was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording]] for his single "[[This Is What It Feels Like]]" featuring [[Trevor Guthrie]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/5819883/grammy-awards-2014-full-nominations-list-complete-nominees|title=Grammy Awards 2014: Full Nominations List|website=Billboard|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> which makes him the fourth trance artist ever to receive a [[Grammy Award]] nomination.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.djmastercourse.com/famous-progressive-trance-djs/|title=The Most Famous Progressive Trance DJs Of All Time|date=2015-03-02|website=DJ Master Course|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-21|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160821024001/https://www.djmastercourse.com/famous-progressive-trance-djs/|archive-date=21 August 2016|df=dmy-all}}</ref> In the United States, he holds the record for most entries, twenty-one, on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Dance/Electronic Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|last=Murray|first=Gordon|title=Armin van Buuren Sets Record on Dance/Electronic Albums Chart|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/code/5869512/armin-van-buuren-sets-record-on-dance-electronic-albums-chart|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|access-date=24 May 2014|date=10 January 2014}}</ref>

==Music career==
===1996–1999: Early career and success===
In 1996, Van Buuren released ''EP'' and ''Push'' on Timeless Records. Van Buuren had his first success with a track called "Blue Fear", which was released under the Cyber Records label in 1997. Another successful track, "Communication", was released under the same label and became popular in [[Ibiza]], Spain in the summer of 1999.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.magneticmag.com/2015/05/armin-van-buuren-bio/|title=Armin van Buuren Bio|date=5 May 2015|website=Magneticmag.com|access-date=21 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160713061945/http://www.magneticmag.com/2015/05/armin-van-buuren-bio/|archive-date=13 July 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> After being signed to [[AM PM Records]],<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/store/music/artist/armin-van-buuren/47780200-0200-11db-89ca-0019b92a3933/bio?display=grid|title=Armin Van Buuren Bio|website=Microsoft.com|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> this track entered the [[UK Singles Chart]] at No.&nbsp;18 in 2000.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/3672/armin/?Day=&Month=&Year=1963|title=ARMIN {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|website=www.officialcharts.com|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref>

In the beginning of 1999, Van Buuren started his label Armind together with United Recordings. The first release, Gig – "One", was well received. The second release, "Touch Me" under the name Rising Star, was signed to [[Ministry of Sound]] in the UK, before the record was released. By the time of his third release, Gimmick – "Free" was signed to [[R&S Records]]. During this year, he remixed the first two singles of [[Gouryella]] (''Gouryella'' and ''Walhalla'') and, under the guise of Rising Star, produced a remix of ''L'Esperanza'', a song by [[Airscape]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ferrycorsten.com/news/ferry-corsten-announces-gouryella-live|title=From the studio to the stage: Ferry Corsten announces Gouryella live!|publisher=[[Flashover Recordings]]|date=2 December 2015|access-date=8 February 2017|author=Flashover Recordings|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160912040028/http://ferrycorsten.com/news/ferry-corsten-announces-gouryella-live|archive-date=12 September 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.arminvanbuuren.com/music/airscape-lesperanza-armin-van-buurens-rising-star-mix/ |title=Airscape - L'Esperanza (Armin Van Buuren's Rising Star Mix) |website=[[Armada Music]] |access-date=8 February 2017}}</ref>

===2000–2004: ''76'' and ''A State of Trance'' series===
In 2000, Van Buuren started his own Armin van Buuren compilation series, a mix of [[progressive house]] and [[vocal trance]] styles of music.<!-- according to who? --> His first compilation album, ''A State of Trance'' (not to be confused with his weekly ''[[A State of Trance]]'' radio shows), sold 10,000 copies<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://gazettereview.com/2016/04/richest-djs-world/|title=The Top 10 Richest DJs in the World - The Gazette Review|last=Deml|first=Jessica|date=2016-04-09|website=The Gazette Review|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> and contains Van Buuren's remix of [[Moogwai]], "Viola". Teaming up with [[Tiësto]], two new projects were born: Major League – "Wonder Where You Are?" was released on [[Black Hole Recordings]] and Alibi – "Eternity" was released on Armind. "Eternity" received club and chart success and was signed to [[Paul van Dyk]]'s imprint [[Vandit Records]]. Another major collaboration followed this. Together with [[Ferry Corsten]], Van Buuren recorded a [[riff]] titled "Exhale<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.armadamusic.com/news/2006/11/907/|title=Armin van Buuren - 10 Years - Order now! - Armada Music|date=2006-11-06|website=Armada Music|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref>" for the System F album, ''[[Out of the Blue (Ferry Corsten album)|Out of the Blue]]''. Released as a single, this track reached Gold status.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ultramusic.com/artist/ferry-corsten/|title=Ferry Corsten / Ultra Music / Ultra Records / Ultra|website=Ultra Music / Ultra Records / Ultra|access-date=2016-06-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160503043754/http://ultramusic.com/artist/ferry-corsten/|archive-date=3 May 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Under the pseudonym [[Gaia (Armin van Buuren alias)|Gaia]] he released "4 Elements" on Captivating Sounds, a sub-label of [[Warner Brothers]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.arminvanbuuren.com/music/gaia-4-elements/|title=Gaia – 4 Elements – Armin van Buuren|website=Arminvanbuuren.com|access-date=23 August 2020}}</ref>

His second album, ''Basic Instinct'' featured a new track: "Perpetuous Dreamer" – "The Sound of Goodbye". This track entered the Dutch charts in June 2001 at number 26. Later in the year, the track hit No.&nbsp;1 on the [[Dance Club Songs|Hot Dance Music/Club Play]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ellesdegraaf.net/elles_bio.html|title=Elles de Graaf {{!}} Biography|last=Krogull|first=Andreas|website=www.ellesdegraaf.net|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> The third album, ''In Motion'' was released 6 August 2001. A fourth album, ''Transparence'', followed in 2002. In 2003, he worked with such artists such as DJ Seth Alan Fannin throughout a global tour on Dance Revolution in Europe, selling out to crowds of 20,000 people in the Netherlands.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://themusicessentials.com/artist-of-the-month-february-2015-armin-van-buuren/|title=King Of Trance: Our Artist Of The Month For February 2015, Armin van Buuren! {{!}} T.H.E - Music Essentials|date=2015-02-02|website=T.H.E - Music Essentials|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160820150227/http://themusicessentials.com/artist-of-the-month-february-2015-armin-van-buuren|archive-date=20 August 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In March 2001, Van Buuren started his own radio show on [[ID&T]] Radio<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.armadamusic.com/news/2006/05/803/|title=A State of Trance celebrates its 250th broadcast! - Armada Music|date=2006-05-10|website=Armada Music|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> (traditionally broadcast in Dutch and later English since ASOT 183). In this weekly two-hour show, entitled ''[[A State of Trance]]'', he plays the latest popular trance music tracks, and publishes the artists and track titles on his website. When ID&T Radio changed genres in 2004, Van Buuren moved ''A State of Trance'' to [[Fresh FM (Netherlands)|Fresh FM]] and later [[SLAM!FM]], two Dutch radio stations. It is now a weekly feature on [[Radio 538]], the Dutch radio station,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.538.nl/programma/37/a-state-of-trance|title=A State Of Trance|website=538.nl|language=nl-NL|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> [[DI.FM]], an online radio station,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.di.fm/shows/a-state-of-trance|title=A State Of Trance with Armin van Buuren|website=Digitally Imported|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> and on [[XM Satellite Radio]], [[Electric Area|channel 52]] in the United States and Canada.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.siriusxm.com/electricarea/weeklyschedule|title=Weekly Schedule - Electric Area|website=SiriusXM|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> A complete list of stations that broadcast ''A State of Trance'' can be found at the ASOT section of Armin's website.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.astateoftrance.com/stations/|title=Radio stations broadcasting A State of Trance|website=A State of Trance|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref>

In 2002, he had a residency at Glow in Washington D.C.,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.clubglow.com/djs/armin-in-dc-a-14-year-old-journey/|title=Armin van Buuren + Glow Washington DC – A 14 Year Old Journey|website=Club Glow Washington DC|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> and played across North America. He regularly appeared at [[Amnesia (nightclub)|Amnesia]] on the island of [[Ibiza]], Balearic Islands, Spain. In October that year, Van Buuren was voted number 5 in the ''[[DJ Magazine|DJ Mag]]''.

In June 2003, Van Buuren celebrated the 100th episode of ''A State of Trance'' at Bloomingdale, [[Bloemendaal aan Zee]], Netherlands and released his debut studio album, ''[[76 (album)|76]]'', a 76-minute album divided into 13 tracks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.arminvanbuuren.com/discography/?release=Arma056|title=Armin van Buuren – 76 – Release|access-date=10 January 2012}}</ref> In the same year he was voted No. 3 in the ''[[DJ Magazine|DJ Mag]]'' and held third place for the next two years (2004, 2005). Also in 2004, Van Buuren remixed the ''[[24 (TV series)|24]]'' theme song into a trance hit.

===2005–2006: ''Shivers''===
[[File:Armin van Buuren 2005 (cropped).jpg|thumb|240px|Armin van Buuren at [[Sensation (event)|Sensation White]] 2005]]
On 2 June 2005, he celebrated the 200th episode of ''[[A State of Trance]]'' at the [[Museumplein]] of [[Amsterdam]]. On 8 August 2005, Van Buuren released his second studio album, ''[[Shivers (album)|Shivers]]''. On the album Van Buuren worked with artists such as Pakistani-American singer [[Nadia Ali (singer)|Nadia Ali]], English singer [[Justine Suissa]] and American trance duo [[Gabriel & Dresden]]. On 25 May 2006, he celebrated with various artists the 250th episode of ''A State of Trance'' at Asta, [[The Hague]], Netherlands. He was voted number two in the 2006 ''[[DJ Magazine|DJ Mag]]''. On 17 May 2007, he celebrated with various artists the 300th episode of ''A State of Trance'' at Pettelaarse Schans, [['s-Hertogenbosch]], Netherlands.

===2007–2009: ''Imagine''===
Van Buuren was elected best DJ in the world in the 2007 ''[[DJ Mag]]'' edition. On 12 January 2008, Van Buuren was given the "Buma Cultuur Pop Award," the most prestigious Dutch music award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.armadamusic.com/news/pop-award-goes-to-armin-van-buuren|title=Pop Award goes to Armin van Buuren|website=Armadamusic.com|language=en-US|access-date=2016-10-06}}</ref> On 17 April 2008, Van Buuren released his third studio album, ''[[Imagine (Armin van Buuren album)|Imagine]]''. It features collaboration with singers such as [[Jacqueline Govaert]] of [[Krezip]]. The album debuted at number one on the [[MegaCharts|Dutch Albums Chart]]. The second single released from the album, [[In and Out of Love (Armin van Buuren song)|"In and Out of Love"]], featuring [[Sharon den Adel]] from the band [[Within Temptation]], has spawned a music video that has been viewed over 242 million times on YouTube.<ref name="YouTube">{{cite web|author=Armada Music|author-link=Armada Music|date=6 August 2008|title=Armin van Buuren ft. Sharon den Adel – In and Out of Love (Official Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TxvpctgU_s8|url-status=live|access-date=11 April 2021|publisher=YouTube}}</ref> Since the release of ''Imagine'', Van Buuren has been working with [[Benno de Goeij]] of [[Rank 1]] on solo productions and remixes.{{Citation needed|date=January 2012}} On 1 May 2008, he celebrated with various artists the 350th episode of ''A State of Trance'' at Noxx, [[Antwerp]], Belgium. He was voted No. 1 in the 2008 ''[[DJ Magazine|DJ Mag]]'' again.

He collaborated with his brother, guitarist Eller van Buuren, at [[Together as One (festival)|Together As One]] in Los Angeles, U.S., on New Year's Eve 2009, as well as on Armin's 2008 studio album ''[[Imagine (Armin van Buuren album)|Imagine]]''. His performance with his brother at Together As One was also the last event of his ''Armin Only: Imagine'' world tour. To celebrate the 400th episode of ''A State of Trance'', he performed at three shows with various artists in April, 2009 at Club Butan, [[Wuppertal]], Germany, [[AIR (nightclub)|AIR]], Birmingham, United Kingdom and Maassilo, [[Rotterdam]], [[Netherlands]].

Also in 2009, Foreign Media Games announced the production of ''[[Armin van Buuren: In the Mix]]'', a [[music video game]] being produced in collaboration with Cloud 9 Music and Van Buuren's Armada Music label. The title was released on 12 November 2010 exclusively for the [[Wii|Wii console]].<ref>[http://worthplaying.com/article/2009/10/30/news/69826/ 'In The Mix: Featuring Armin van Buuren' (Wii) Shows Off Career Mode – Screen], 30 October 2009.</ref> He was voted number one DJ in the 2009 ''[[DJ Mag]]'' for the third consecutive year.

The locations were: [[The Guvernment]], Toronto, Canada on 1 April 2010, [[Roseland Ballroom]], New York City on 2 April and on 3 April again, Expo Arena, [[Bratislava]], Slovakia on 9 April and [[Hala Stulecia (Wrocław)|Centennial Hall]], [[Wroclaw]], Poland on 24 April.

The 2010 edition of ''[[Armin Only]]: Mirage'' kicked off in November 2010, in [[Utrecht, Netherlands]], and was followed by shows in [[Saint Petersburg]], [[Kyiv]], Buenos Aires, [[Melbourne]], [[Beirut]], [[Poznań]], Moscow and [[Bratislava]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.arminonly.com/shows-tickets/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100627145226/http://www.arminonly.com/shows-tickets/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2010-06-27 |title=Shows & Tickets " Armin Only |publisher=Arminonly.com |access-date=2012-08-30 }}</ref>

[[File:Armin on air.jpg|thumb|240px|right|Van Buuren broadcasting the milestone 500th episode of his ''[[A State of Trance]]'' show at the official pre-party, live from Club Trinity in [[Cape Town]] on 17 March 2011.]]
In March and April, 2011, celebrating the 500th episode of his radio show, Van Buuren performed five live shows along with various artists; the first was in [[Johannesburg]], South Africa at MTN Expo Centre, and the second in [[Miami]]'s [[Ultra Music Festival]]. He then performed in [[Argentina]] at [[Estadio G.E.B.A.|GEBA]] in Buenos Aires, followed by a large event at Brabanthallen in [[Den Bosch]], Netherlands which included more than 30 DJs from countries all over the world and was attended by over 30,000 people. The final show was held in Sydney on 16 April at [[Acer Arena]]. In 2011, Van Buuren was appointed Officer of the [[Order of Oranje-Nassau]] for services to Dutch dance and music economy.<ref name="officer">{{Cite web|title=Armin named Officer in the Order of Oranje-Nassau!|url=https://www.armadamusic.com/news/armin-named-officer-in-the-order-of-oranje-nassau|website=Armada Music|date=30 April 2011|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170810170550/https://www.armadamusic.com/news/armin-named-officer-in-the-order-of-oranje-nassau|archive-date=2017-08-10|access-date=10 August 2017}}</ref> That year he also received the [[Key to the City|Key]] to ([[Freedom of the City|Freedom]]) of [[Leiden]] from the City Mayor, as well as being voted No. 2 in the 2011 ''[[DJ Magazine|DJ Mag]]''.

To celebrate the 550th episode of ''A State of Trance'', he performed with various artists on two different continents during fourth weeks. The show was called ''A State of Trance 550: Invasion''. The first event was celebrated at [[Ministry of Sound]] in London on 1 March 2012. On 7 March, Moscow held the second event at [[Expocentre]]. [[Kyiv]]'s show was performed at International Exhibition Centre on 10 March. On 17 March, as part of the [[Beyond Wonderland]] festival in Los Angeles and on 25 March at the [[Ultra Music Festival]] in Miami. On 31 March, the biggest and final edition of ASOT 550 took place in the Brabanthallen of [[Den Bosch]], Netherlands. On 19 October 2012, Van Buuren was announced for a fifth non-consecutive year as the world's number one DJ according to ''[[DJ Magazine|DJ Mag]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.arminvanbuuren.com/news/2012/10/armin-van-buuren-back-to-number-one-in-dj-mag-top-100/ |title=Armin van Buuren back to number no one in DJ Mag Top 100! " Armin van Buuren |website=Arminvanbuuren.com |access-date=20 October 2012}}</ref>

To celebrate the 600th episode of ''A State of Trance'', Van Buuren announced that he would perform with various artists all around the world in early 2013. The show was called ''A State of Trance 600: The Expedition''. It was announced that the show would tour the world, including stops in [[Madrid]], [[Mexico City]], [[São Paulo]], [[Minsk]], [[Sofia]], [[Beirut]], [[Kuala Lumpur]], [[Mumbai]], [[Miami]], [[Guatemala City]], [[New York City]], and [[Den Bosch]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.astateoftrance.com/a-state-of-trance-600 |title=A State of Trance 600 " A State of Trance |publisher=Astateoftrance.com |access-date=2013-07-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2013/10/asot-650-new-horizons-2014-dates-announced-tour-will-be-massive-says-armin-van-buuren/|title=ASOT 650 New Horizons 2014 dates announced, tour will be 'massive,' says Armin van Buuren|date=6 October 2013|website=Dancingastronaut.com|access-date=23 August 2020}}</ref>

===2013–2014: ''Intense''===
The first single of Van Buuren's fifth studio album, ''[[Intense]]'', titled "[[This Is What It Feels Like]]", featured Canadian singer and songwriter [[Trevor Guthrie]], and was released on 5 April 2013. The single nominated for a [[Grammy Award]] for Best Dance Recording.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.newson6.com/story/21641513/1-dj-armin-van-buuren-releases-1st-single-this-is-what-it-feels-like-from-his-5th-artist-album-intense-anaarmada-north-america-april-5th |title=#1 DJ Armin van Buuren Releases 1st Single "This Is What It Feels Like" from His 5th Artist Album 'INTENSE' (ANA/ARMADA NORTH AMERICA) April 5th – NewsOn6.com – Tulsa, OK |publisher=NewsOn6.com |access-date=2013-07-19 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130411130254/http://www.newson6.com/story/21641513/1-dj-armin-van-buuren-releases-1st-single-this-is-what-it-feels-like-from-his-5th-artist-album-intense-anaarmada-north-america-april-5th |archive-date=11 April 2013 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> The tour for the album, ''Armin Only: Intense'', was announced on 17 October 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.arminonly.com/news-item-details-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131101201334/http://www.arminonly.com/news-item-details-19 |url-status=dead |archive-date=1 November 2013 |title=20 Dates and 19 Destinations for Armin Only Intense |publisher=Armin van Buuren |date=17 October 2013 |access-date=2013-10-29 }}</ref>

30 April 2013 saw the abdication of Queen [[Beatrix of the Netherlands]] in favor of her son, King [[Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands]], who was crowned that day, which was [[Koningsdag|Queen's Day]] as well. Van Buuren was the headline act in a performance to a live audience celebrating the King's crowning in Amsterdam. While he was performing live together with the [[Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra]] in the [[Port of Amsterdam|Amsterdam Harbor]] on [[Java-eiland]], King [[Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands|Willem-Alexander]], Queen [[Queen Máxima of the Netherlands|Máxima]] and their three daughters made an unplanned visit on stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GeVvRGkXTLc |title=Armin van Buuren & The Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra perform for new Dutch king Willem-Alexander |publisher=Armada Music |date=19 May 2013 |access-date=2013-12-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.demorgen.be/nieuws/uitstapje-koningspaar-stond-niet-in-draaiboeken~b7a57bcd/ |title='Uitstapje' koningspaar stond niet in draaiboeken ('pleasure-trip' of royal couple was not part of plan of action) |publisher=De Morgen |date=1 May 2013 |access-date=2020-11-28}}</ref>

On 19 October 2013, Armin van Buuren was ranked number two DJ in the world by ''[[DJ Mag]]''. On 26 January 2014, Armin van Buuren attended the [[56th Annual Grammy Awards|56th Grammy Awards]], in relation to his Grammy Award nomination for the single featuring Trevor Guthrie, "This Is What It Feels Like" in the category of [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording|Best Dance Recording]].

===2015–2018: ''Embrace''===
[[File:Armin van Buuren logo.svg|thumb|240px|Van Buuren's DJ logo, used in shows as of 2015]]
On 8 May 2015, Armin van Buuren released the first single from his sixth studio album, ''[[Embrace (Armin van Buuren album)|Embrace]]'', which was released on 29 October 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.astateoftrance.com/news/armin-van-buuren-announces-new-artist-album-embrace/|title=Armin van Buuren announces new artist album|website=Astateoftrance.com}}</ref> The single is titled "[[Another You (Armin van Buuren song)|Another You]]" featuring [[Mr Probz]]. Three weeks after releasing "Another You", Van Buuren also debuted another remix from ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' official theme which is originally made by composer [[Ramin Djawadi]].<ref>{{cite news|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Matt|last=Medved|date=8 June 2015|title='Game of Thrones' Theme Receives Trance Remix By Armin van Buuren|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/6590616/game-of-thrones-theme-armin-van-buuren-remix}}</ref>

On 16 October 2015 Armin van Buuren released the next single from ''Embrace'' called "Strong Ones" featuring Cimo Fränkel. This was followed by the release of his collaboration with [[Cosmic Gate]] "Embargo" on 22 October 2015. To accompany the album's release, Van Buuren announced the return of the ''Armin Only'' series of parties.

This edition of the tour was titled ''Armin Only: Embrace'' and took place throughout both 2015 and 2016. As the title of the tour suggests, it was based around the collection of music that he showcased on his new album.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.arminvanbuuren.com/news/2016/01/first-dates-armin-only-world-tour-may-6th7th-in-the-netherlands/|title = First dates Armin Only world tour: May 6th/7th in the Netherlands|website = Web site of DJ/Producer Armin van Buuren.|access-date = 2016-03-03}}</ref>

On 2 February 2017, Armin van Buuren officially opened a new A State of Trance radio show studio in Amsterdam as part of his celebrations for the 800th episode of the radio show. The studio is located in the Armada headquarters.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7677720/armin-van-buuren-a-state-of-trance-radio-studio-amsterdam |title=Peak Into Armin Van Buuren's New ASOT Radio Studio, Live Video Streams Start Today: Exclusive |last=Bein |first=Kat |date=2 February 2017 |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=9 June 2017}}</ref> With the new accommodations made possible by the studio, he introduced a new segment of the radio show called Service for Dreamers where he invites fans to send in their favorite trance tracks that have a special meaning to them. Every week one track gets chosen to be featured live on the show and introduced by the fans of the radio show live, either by a walk-in, phone call, Skype or other means of contact.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.edmsauce.com/2017/02/07/exclusive-interview-armin-van-buuren-embracing-music/ |title=[Exclusive Interview] Armin van Buuren: Embracing the Music |last=Vargas |first=Ulises |date=7 February 2017 |website=edmsauce |access-date=9 June 2017}}</ref>

In 2017, Armin van Buuren announced he will be releasing an online [[MasterClass]] sharing his expertise in electronic music production.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8055125/armin-van-buuren-masterclass-teach-fans-to-dj|title=Armin van Buuren Teaches Fans to DJ With MasterClass: Exclusive|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-22}}</ref>
On 13 April 2017, Armin van Buuren performed at the Heineken stage in [[Shanghai]], in the first Formula 1 Festival along with [[Angela Zhang]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/headlines/2018/4/shanghai-to-host-heineken-f1-festival.html|title=Shanghai to host Heineken F1 Festival|work=Formula1.com|access-date=2018-04-05}}</ref>

Armin performed a seven-hour-long set at the Romania Untold Festival on 6 August 2018, clocking from 2:00&nbsp;a.m. to after 9:00&nbsp;a.m.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2018/08/armin-van-buuren-throws-seven-hour-trance-set-untold-festival-watch/ |title=Armin van Buuren throws a seven hour trance set at Untold Festival [WATCH] |last=Stack |first=Chris |date=2018-08-06 |website=[[Dancing Astronaut]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180807052117/https://dancingastronaut.com/2018/08/armin-van-buuren-throws-seven-hour-trance-set-untold-festival-watch/ |archive-date=2018-08-07 |access-date=2018-08-07}}</ref> This outlasted his previous 2017 ASOT performance, which had gone on for five hours.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.youredm.com/2017/08/06/armin-van-buuren-pulls-nighter-untold-music-festival-5-hour-set-watch/ |title=Armin Van Buuren Pulls All-nighter for Untold Music Festival With 5 Hour Set |last=Powell |first=Karlie |date=2017-08-06 |website=Your EDM |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180122214757/https://www.youredm.com/2017/08/06/armin-van-buuren-pulls-nighter-untold-music-festival-5-hour-set-watch/ |archive-date=2018-01-22 |access-date=2018-08-07}}</ref>

On 25 October 2019, Armin released his seventh studio album, ''[[Balance (Armin van Buuren album)|Balance]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.edmtunes.com/2019/09/armin-van-buuren-announces-album-title-and-asot-950-event|title=Armin van Buuren announces album title and ASOT 950 Event|first=Scott|last=Lombardo|date=September 5, 2019|website=EDMTunes|access-date=September 6, 2019}}</ref>
