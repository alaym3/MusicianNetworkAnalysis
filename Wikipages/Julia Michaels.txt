'''Julia Carin Cavazos''' (born November 13, 1993),<ref name="BBC">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-40704643|title=Julia Michaels: 'Dare to suck'|first=Mark|last=Savage|date=July 30, 2017|access-date=August 15, 2017|via=www.bbc.com}}</ref><ref name=birthday>{{cite magazine|title=Women in Music: Hitmakers from Behind the Scenes|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/events/women-in-music/6785502/women-in-music-hitmakers-from-behind-the-scenes|magazine=Billboard|access-date=December 5, 2015}}</ref> known professionally as '''Julia Michaels''', is an American singer and songwriter.

Born in Iowa and raised in California, Michaels began her career writing for other artists. Following her success as a writer, she released her debut single with [[Republic Records]] in 2017, "[[Issues (Julia Michaels song)|Issues]]", which peaked at number 11 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in the United States and was certified quintuple-platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). Her major-label debut extended play, ''[[Nervous System (EP)|Nervous System]]'' (2017), peaked at number 48 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart in the United States. She has since toured alongside [[Maroon 5]], [[Keith Urban]], and [[Niall Horan]] and has appeared on the UK top 20 hits "[[If the World Was Ending]]" with [[JP Saxe]] and "[[I Miss You (Clean Bandit song)|I Miss You]]" with [[Clean Bandit]].

As a songwriter, Michaels has written songs for artists including [[Selena Gomez]], [[Demi Lovato]], [[Fifth Harmony]], [[Shawn Mendes]], [[Britney Spears]], [[Justin Bieber]], [[Hailee Steinfeld]], and [[Gwen Stefani]]. She has earned two ''[[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'']]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] number one songs with Bieber's "[[Sorry (Justin Bieber song)|Sorry]]" and Gomez's "[[Lose You to Love Me]]".

Michaels has also received three [[Grammy Award]] nominations, two of which were for [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] and the third being for [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=julia+michaels#search_section |title=Gold & Platinum: Michaels, Julia |publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]] |access-date=August 1, 2017}}</ref> as well as nominations from [[MTV Video Music Awards]], [[Billboard Music Awards|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], and [[American Music Awards]].

== Early life ==
Julia Michaels was born in [[Davenport, Iowa]], but moved to [[Santa Clarita, California]], about 35 miles northwest of [[Los Angeles]], with her family including her older sister [[Jaden Michaels|Jaden]] who is also a songwriter. Her father is Mexican-Puerto Rican and changed his name from Juan Manuel Cavazos to John Michaels to pursue an acting career.<ref name="BBC"/> Michaels began singing at age 12. When she was 14, she met songwriter Joleen Belle, with whom she wrote the theme song to ''[[Austin & Ally]]'' and many other songs for TV and film. At 19, she met [[Lindy Robbins]], with whom she wrote "Fire Starter" for [[Demi Lovato]] and "[[Miss Movin' On]]" for [[Fifth Harmony]].<ref name=mystery>{{cite web|last1=Kimpel|first1=Dan|title=Julia Michaels -- Pop's Mystery Girl|url=http://musicconnection.com/songwriter-julia-michaels-pop-mystery/|website=Music Connection|access-date=March 28, 2015}}</ref>

Michaels has said her inspirations include [[Fiona Apple]], [[Lisa Mitchell]], [[Laura Marling]], [[Missy Higgins]], [[Paramore]], [[Juliet Simms]], [[Sarah Blasko]], and [[The Fray]].<ref name=press>{{cite news|last1=Noonan|first1=Heather|title=Julia Michaels Releases Debut Self-titled EP, Available Everywhere July 20th Through SA TrackWorks|url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/JuliaMichaels/EP/prweb4267964.htm|access-date=March 28, 2015|publisher=PRweb|date=July 20, 2010}}</ref> Michaels had been writing in the Hollywood Pop Circuits since she was 16. When she was 20, she met her songwriting partner [[Justin Tranter]] with whom she frequently collaborates.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1Z9scChpTpc| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211118/1Z9scChpTpc| archive-date=2021-11-18 | url-status=live|title=Semi Precious Weapons' Justin Tranter On Hey Qween with Jonny McGovern|last=Hey Qween|date=May 23, 2016|via=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref> She is credited with writing hits for artists including [[Justin Bieber]], [[Selena Gomez]], and [[Fifth Harmony]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7890227/julia-michaels-songs-you-didnt-know-wrote-justin-bieber-selena-gomez|title=10 Songs You Didn't Know Julia Michaels Co-Wrote|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> Together with the Norwegian musician [[Kygo]] she performed "[[Carry Me (Kygo song)|Carry Me]]" at the closing ceremony of the [[2016 Summer Olympics]] in [[Rio de Janeiro|Rio de Janeiro, Brazil]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Iasimone|first1=Ashley|title=Rio Olympics 2016 Closing Ceremony Highlights: Kygo and Julia Michaels Perform 'Carry Me,' Simone Biles Makes History & More|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7480220/rio-olympics-2016-closing-ceremony-highlights-kygo-julia-michaels-simone-biles|magazine=Billboard|access-date=February 23, 2017}}</ref>

== Career ==
In January 2017, Michaels released her first solo single, "[[Issues (Julia Michaels song)|Issues]]".<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Stutz|first1=Colin|title=Hit Songwriter Julia Michaels Releases Debut Single 'Issues': Listen|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7655247/julia-michaels-debut-single-issues-stream|magazine=Billboard|access-date=February 23, 2017}}</ref> According to Michaels, many big-name artists fought for the song, but she kept it for herself. She said, "It was the first time I'd written a song that sounded so much like myself that I couldn't picture anyone else singing it." In April 2017, her new song "How Do We Get Back to Love" was premiered on the HBO series ''[[Girls (TV series)|Girls]]''.<ref name="Girls">{{cite web|last1=Graves|first1=Shahlin|title=Julia Michaels previews new song 'How Do We Get Back to Love' on 'Girls'.|url=http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/julia-michaels/12503|website=Coup De Main Magazine|access-date=May 19, 2017|language=en|date=April 12, 2017}}</ref> Michaels's EP ''[[Nervous System (EP)|Nervous System]]'' was released on July 28, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7873117/julia-michaels-nervous-systems-album-ep|title=Julia Michaels Announces 'Nervous System,' Her Debut 'Mini-Album'|access-date=July 19, 2017|date= July 19, 2017|first=Liza|last=Shcherbakova|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> Her second single "[[Uh Huh (Julia Michaels song)|Uh Huh]]" was released on June 2, 2017.<ref name="Uh Huh">{{cite web|last1=Wass|first1=Mike|title=Julia Michaels' "Uh Huh" Is Every Bit As Catchy As "Issues"|url=http://www.idolator.com/7664044/julia-michaels-uh-huh-full-song-review|website=idolator|access-date=June 2, 2017|date=June 1, 2017}}</ref> From November 25 to December 6, 2017, Michaels was the opening act for [[Shawn Mendes]]'s [[Illuminate World Tour]] on the Oceania Leg.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/julia-michaels/13075|title=Julia Michaels to open for Shawn Mendes on NZ/AUS 2017 tour.|first=Shahlin|last=Graves|date=July 28, 2017}}</ref>

At the [[2018 Grammy Awards]], she was nominated for two awards, [[Best New Artist]] and [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] for "Issues".<ref>{{cite web|title=The 35 Most Anticipated Albums Of 2017|url=http://ew.com/music/2016/12/22/most-anticipated-albums-2017/|website=EW.com|access-date=February 23, 2017|date=December 22, 2016}}</ref> On February 8, 2018, "Heaven" was released which was included in the soundtrack for the film ''[[Fifty Shades Freed (film)|Fifty Shades Freed]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/hear-julia-michaels-fifty-shades-freed-song-heaven-w515934|title=Hear Julia Michaels' Sleek 'Fifty Shades Freed' Song 'Heaven'|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=May 4, 2018}}</ref> On May 4, 2018, "[[Jump (Julia Michaels song)|Jump]]" was released featuring [[Trippie Redd]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/05/04/julia-michaels-trippie-redd-new-single-jump|title=Julia Michaels shares new single "Jump" featuring Trippie Redd|work=The FADER|access-date=May 4, 2018|language=en}}</ref> From March 12 to May 12, 2018, Julia was the opening act for the European dates of [[Niall Horan]]’s [[Flicker World Tour]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.onthescenemag.co.uk/julia-michaels-supports-niall-horan-flicker-tour/.|title=Julia Michaels supports Niall Horan on his Flicker tour|first=Maximilian|last=Hengst|date=February 14, 2018}}</ref>

From May 30 to October 15, 2018, Michaels was the opening act for [[Maroon 5]]'s [[Red Pill Blues Tour]] on the North American leg.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8014725/maroon-5-red-pill-blues-tour-julia-michaels|title=Maroon 5 Hitting the Road with Julia Michaels for Red Pill Blues Tour|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=October 26, 2017|access-date=October 9, 2018}}</ref> She collaborated with [[Lauv]] on the single "There's No Way", released September 27, 2018.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Weatherby|first=Taylor|date=September 27, 2018|title=Lauv & Julia Michaels Fight Their Feelings in New Collaboration 'There's No Way': Watch|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8477309/lauv-julia-michaels-theres-no-way-video/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180928114820/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8477309/lauv-julia-michaels-theres-no-way-video/|archive-date=September 27, 2018|access-date=2021-01-05|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> In November 2018, she appeared on the soundtrack to the Disney film ''[[Ralph Breaks the Internet]]'', where she performed "In This Place", a pop rendition of the film's musical number "A Place Called Slaughter Race."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3104309/julia-michaels-in-this-place-ralph-breaks-the-internet/|title=Julia Michaels Finds Nirvana in a Video Game with her ''Ralph Breaks the Internet'' Anthem|publisher=[[MTV|MTV.com]]|date=November 15, 2018|access-date=September 26, 2020}}</ref>

On December 21, 2018, Michaels was featured on [[5 Seconds of Summer|5 Seconds of Summer's]] song, "[[Lie to Me (5 Seconds of Summer song)|Lie to Me]]".<ref>{{cite web|last=Bajgrowicz|first=Brooke|date=December 21, 2018|title=5 Seconds Of Summer Debut 'Lie To Me' Remix With Julia Michaels: Listen|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8491225/5-seconds-of-summer-lie-to-me-remix-julia-michaels|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190114224257/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8491225/5-seconds-of-summer-lie-to-me-remix-julia-michaels|archive-date=January 14, 2019|access-date=February 4, 2019|work=Billboard}}</ref> "Lie to Me" charted in multiple countries and was later certified platinum in Australia and Gold in Canada.<ref>{{Cite web|title=5 Seconds of Summer/5SOS|url=http://www.aria.com.au/pages/httpwww.aria.com.aupagesSinglesAccreds2019.htm|access-date=July 24, 2020|website=www.aria.com.au}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Gold/Platinum 5 Seconds of Summer- Lie to Me|url=https://musiccanada.com/gold-platinum/|access-date=July 24, 2020|website=Music Canada}}</ref>

From January 23 to February 5, 2019, Michaels opened for [[Keith Urban]] on the Australian leg of his [[Graffiti U World Tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.keithurban.net/news/453806/just-announced-the-graffiti-u-world-tour-australia|title=Just Announced: The Graffiti U World Tour Australia|work=KeithUrban.net|date=July 26, 2018|access-date=July 29, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180730050456/https://www.keithurban.net/news/453806/just-announced-the-graffiti-u-world-tour-australia|archive-date=July 30, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> On January 24, 2019, Michaels released her EP, ''[[Inner Monologue Part 1]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/julia-michaels-selena-gomez-song-anxiety-784097/|title=Hear Julia Michaels' New EP, Song With Selena Gomez 'Anxiety'|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=January 24, 2019|access-date=January 24, 2019}}</ref> In June 2019, she began teasing songs from ''[[Inner Monologue Part 2]]'', including "17" and "Falling for Boys".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/juliamichaels/|title=Julia Michaels (@juliamichaels)|via=[[Instagram]]|access-date=June 25, 2019}}</ref>

On May 11, 2020, Michaels appeared in the second-season episode of ''[[Songland]]'' and released the song "Give It to You".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://talentrecap.com/songland-julia-michaels-recap-this-new-heartfelt-song-will-make-you-cry/|title='Songland' Julia Michaels Recap: This New Heartfelt Song Will Make You CRY|work=Talent Recap|author=Agate, Samantha|date=May 11, 2020|access-date=May 12, 2020}}</ref>

On October 1, 2020, Michaels released the song "Lie Like This" as the lead single from her forthcoming debut studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2100846/julia-michaels-lie-like-this/music/|title=Julia Michaels – "Lie Like This"|work=Stereogum|author=DeVille, Chris|date=October 1, 2020|access-date=October 1, 2020}}</ref> On March 26, 2021, "All Your Exes" was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2121999/julia-michaels-all-your-exes/music/|title=Julia Michaels – "All Your Exes"|work=Stereogum|author=Rettig, James|date=March 26, 2021|access-date=April 8, 2021}}</ref> On April 14, Michaels announced the title of her album, ''[[Not in Chronological Order]]'', and its release date of April 30, 2021.<ref>{{cite instagram|user=juliamichaels|postid=CNp2U3Tg5yW|title=Not In Chronological Order out April 30|date=April 14, 2021|accessdate=April 14, 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==

In July 2019, Michaels started dating Canadian musician [[JP Saxe]]. This relationship arose from their collaboration on the duet, "[[If the World Was Ending]]".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Aderoju |first1=Darlene |title=Julia Michaels and JP Saxe Are Dating After Collaborating: She 'Has Loved Me Through All of This' |url=https://people.com/music/julia-michaels-dating-jp-saxe-after-collaboration/ |website=People}}</ref>
