'''Labinot Gashi'''<ref name="rawtracks">{{cite web | title = GASHI – "Who Made Me" | work = Raw Tracks | publisher = [[WordPress]] | url = https://rawtracks.wordpress.com/2013/07/23/hip-hop-g4shi-who-made-me/ | date = 2013-07-23 | access-date = 2015-08-25}}</ref><ref name="thehundreds">{{cite web | title = From Albania to Brooklyn :: Rapper GASHI's Steady Grind | work = The Hundreds | publisher = www.thehundreds.com | url = http://thehundreds.com/g4shi-interview/ | date = 2015-05-06 | access-date = 2015-08-25}}</ref><ref name="artsy">{{cite web | title = Breathe Easy Hip Hop, 'I'll Be Right With You': Interview With GASHI | work = Artsy Magazine | publisher = www.artsymagazine.com | url = http://www.artsymagazine.com/interview-with-g4shi/ | date = 2013-02-21 | access-date = 2015-08-25 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20180129141402/http://www.artsymagazine.com/interview-with-g4shi/ | archive-date = 2018-01-29 | url-status = dead }}</ref> ({{IPA-sq|gaˈʃi|pron}}; born 4 October 1989), known mononymously as '''Gashi''' (stylised as '''GASHI)''', is an American rapper, singer and songwriter.<ref>{{Citation | last =Hawgood | first =Alex | title = Gashi, a Son of Kosovo Refugees, Is the 'Trap Phil Collins' | work = The New York Times | date = 9 April 2019 | url =https://www.nytimes.com/2019/04/09/style/gashi-a-son-of-kosovo-refugees-is-the-trap-phil-collins.html | access-date = 25 May 2019}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Labinot Gashi was born in Libya,<ref name="rawtracks"/><ref name="thehundreds"/> on October 4, 1989, to ethnic [[Albania]]n parents from [[Kosovo]].{{ref label|status|a|}} He spent his childhood as a refugee in several African and European countries before settling in Brooklyn, New York at the age of 10.<ref name="thehundreds" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/on-the-come-up-g4shi-news.11394.html?gallery-19277-photo-1|title=On The Come Up: GASHI|date=2014-07-23|work=Hot New HipHop|publisher=www.hotnewhiphop.com|access-date=2015-08-25}}</ref> 

Struggling to pick up the English language, he turned to music to help ease his adjustment into society, and quickly became hooked to rap. Gashi graduated from New Utrecht High School and earned a football scholarship to play at a Division 2 school in Massachusetts ([[American International College]]).<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20180121000019/http://www.aicyellowjackets.com/roster.aspx?rp_id=1127 American International College Yellow Jackets - Larry Gashi - 2009<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Although Gashi earned honors during his first year in college, he decided to drop out of college and concentrate more on music. He prepared his first mixtape and initially adopted the stage name The Kid Gashi<ref name=thehundreds/> to change it later to Gashi and eventually G4SHI and His stage name has officially changed back to GASHI.

Gashi won several music contests and he was the winner of "First Time Performances" of First Look Sessions in both 2010 and 2011.<ref name=pp>[https://web.archive.org/web/20130201020438/http://prishtinapress.info/g4shi-the-rockstar-of-music-and-fashion-industry/ G4shi the rockstar of music and fashion industry | Prishtina Press - Prishtinë - New York - London<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> His debut project, the mixtape ''Last of a Rare Breed'' (2011) was hosted by Erkman, and featured tracks with [[Nipsey Hussle]] and [[French Montana]]. In 2012, G4shi founded his own group called Moxy.<ref name=pp/> 2012 was a breakthrough year with the release of the hit "Rocket". In 2012, he also released "Beautiful World" under his crew Moxy. Future tracks, which included "Who Made Me" and "Laughing", were featured on Hot 97 after Peter Rosenberg co-signed him. G4shi also attracted the attention of fashion and clothing businesses. As of 2012 Gashi is the face and the representative of "Problem Child NYC" clothing line and did modelling for Problem Child line in New York.<ref name=pp/>

In 2013, he released a follow-up mixtape ''I'll Be Right With You''.<ref name=artsy/> A later project, entitled, ''4Play'' featured his single "Room 4".<ref>{{cite web|author=OK-Tho |url=http://www.ok-tho.com/2014/01/top-of-year-deans-list-top-25-artists.html |title=Top Of The Year: The Deans List (Top 25 Artists of 2013) |website=Ok-tho.com |date=2014-01-15 |access-date=2020-04-22}}</ref> In 2015, he released the track "Switch Up" and in 2016 "Day Ones" followed by "Turn Me Down" and "Disrespectful" both in 2017. The song "Disrespectful" was also featured in episode 4 of [[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime]]'s ''[[The Chi]]''.

The self-titled LP is his first studio release for RCA Records and was released in 2019. The album comprises 16 songs, featuring [[G-Eazy]], French Montana, [[DJ Snake]] and Maxx Owa, among others. The project was led by a number of singles, including "Creep on Me" featuring French Montana, "Safety" featuring DJ Snake, and "My Year" with G-Eazy.{{source needed|date=October 2019}}
