'''Zak David Zilesnick''' (born 1 March 1995 in London), better known as '''Zak Abel''', is an English-Moroccan singer, songwriter and musician. He has also been an English Cadet national table tennis champion.

==Early and personal life==
His father, who had been born in [[Morocco]] and [[Migration of Moroccan Jews to Israel|emigrated to Israel]], died when Zilesnick was 12 years old.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=https://www.thejc.com/lifestyle/features/abel-and-willing-to-be-a-success-1.57181|title=Abel and willing to be a success|website=www.thejc.com|access-date=2019-11-30}}</ref>  Zilesnick is [[Jewish]].<ref>[https://www.pressreader.com/uk/the-jewish-chronicle/20160520/282166470413156 ''The Jewish Chronicle'': 2016-05-20 - "ZAK ABEL"<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He grew up in [[Hendon]] with his mother Rachel, and attended the Jewish state school Matilda Marks, and [[University College School]], from which he graduated in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://issuu.com/ucsdevoffice/docs/the_gower_2013/178|title=The Gower 2013|website=issuu.com|date=19 January 2015|access-date=13 March 2018}}</ref><ref name=autogenerated1 />  He has moved to [[London Borough of Hackney|Hackney]].<ref name=autogenerated1 />

Zilesnick is a vegan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/zakabel/p/BpphrkFAIaB/?hl=en |title=Zak Abel on Instagram: "...Happy #WorldVeganDay to everyone in the world. I've been vegan for just over a year now & it's one of the best decisions I ever made..." |website=Instagram.com |date=2018-11-01 |access-date=2020-05-20}}</ref>

==Musician==
Zilesnick won a competition to sing at a ''[[Yom Haatzmaut]]'' celebration in [[Wembley Stadium]] when he was in primary school.<ref name=autogenerated1 />

He is most notable for his featured appearance on the UK top 20 hit "[[Unmissable]]" with [[Gorgon City]]. He has also released two extended plays, entitled ''Joker presents Zak Abel'' and ''One Hand on the Future''.

In 2016, he worked with producers such as [[Kaytranada]] and [[Wookie (musician)|Wookie]] on his debut album, ''Only When We're Naked'', which was released on 6 October 2017,<ref name="iTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/only-when-were-naked/1166625554|title=Only When We're Naked by Zak Abel on Apple Music|work=[[iTunes Store]] (AU)|access-date=3 February 2018}}</ref> and debuted at number 100 on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/48324/zak-abel/|title=Zak Abel Official Chart history|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=3 February 2018}}</ref> He was signed by [[Atlantic Records]].<ref name=autogenerated2>[https://tabletennisengland.co.uk/news/archived/zak-abel-from-table-tennis-star-to-music-sensation/ "Zak Abel: From table tennis star to music sensation" — Table Tennis England<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

==Table tennis==
He was ranked No. 1 in the Cadet age group in England and was 2009 Cadet Boys' Singles national champion at the age of 14; he was also ranked No. 5 for under 18s in England.<ref name="autogenerated3">{{cite web|url=https://www.thejc.com/sport/sport-news/the-maccabi-awards-in-full-1.14108|title=The Maccabi Awards: In full|website=www.thejc.com|access-date=2019-11-30}}</ref><ref name=autogenerated2 /> Between 2008 and 2010 he won the Under 15 national singles and Under 18 doubles titles.<ref name=autogenerated3 />  In 2010 he was ranked number 39 in the senior category.<ref name=autogenerated3 />
