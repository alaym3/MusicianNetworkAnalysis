'''The Griswolds''' are an Australian two-piece [[indie rock]] band from [[Sydney]], [[Australia]]. The band have cited influences such as [[Vampire Weekend]], [[MGMT]], [[Kanye West]], [[Devendra Banhart]], [[Of Montreal]], and [[The Beach Boys]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://myamn.com.au/index.php/en/artists/item/702-the-griswolds |accessdate=4 February 2016 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130509163807/http://www.myamn.com.au/index.php/en/artists/item/702-the-griswolds |archivedate=9 May 2013 }}</ref> The Griswolds signed with [[Wind-up Records]] in May 2013 and began recording their debut album shortly after with producer [[Tony Hoffer]]. They are named after the fictional family from the [[National Lampoon's Vacation (film series)|Vacation film series]].

On 16 March 2017, Tim John, their bass player, left the band.
<ref>http://blog.wearethegriswolds.com</ref>

==Background==
The Griswolds formed in February 2012.<ref>[https://archive.is/20130624225246/http://www.musexpo.net/musexpoLA13/showcaseartists.php?p=thegriswolds ]</ref><ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://whatisthehaps.com/2012/06/11/the-griswolds/ |title=The Griswolds &#124; whatisthehaps &#124; Life enthusiasts talk travel, music, art & culture |publisher=whatisthehaps |date=2012-06-11 |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref> The band's single "Heart of a Lion", reached #12 on the [[We Are Hunted]] emerging artists list.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.winduprecords.com/news/wind-up-records-signs-the-griswolds/ |title=Signs The Griswolds |publisher=Wind-up Records |date=2013-05-02 |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref>

The Griswolds became a [[Triple J]] [[Unearthed (talent contest)|Unearthed]] feature artist after winning a slot on the 2012 Parklife Festival line-up alongside [[Passion Pit]] and [[The Presets]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tonedeaf.com.au/features/qanda/209296/griswolds.htm |accessdate=4 February 2016 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131212193659/http://www.tonedeaf.com.au/features/qanda/209296/griswolds.htm |archivedate=12 December 2013 | title= The Griswolds | first=Al | last=Newstead | date=4 October 2012 | publisher=Tonedeaf }}</ref> Their ''Heart of a Lion'' EP, released in 2012, received positive reviews,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.purplesneakers.com.au/2012/10/review-the-griswolds-heart-of-a-lion-ep/ |title=REVIEW: The Griswolds 'Heart of a Lion' EP |publisher=Purple Sneakers |date=2012-10-14 |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.athousandguitars.com/2013/01/hipsters-meet-your-new-favourite-band-the-griswolds.html |title=Hipsters, meet your new faMvourite band – The Griswolds « A Thousand Guitars |publisher=Athousandguitars.com |date=2013-12-25 |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thegroundmag.com/the-griswolds-heart-of-a-lion-ep/ |title=The Griswolds – Heart Of A Lion EP - The GROUND Magazine |publisher=Thegroundmag.com |date=2012-10-05 |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref> and helped start their touring career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.beat.com.au/music/griswolds-announce-heart-lion-tour |title=The Griswolds Announce Heart Of a Lion Tour &#124; Beat Magazine |publisher=Beat.com.au |date= |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref> After success touring in their native Australia, the band made their first live appearance in [[New York City]] on 11 May 2013 at the [[Brooklyn Bowl]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://reviewfix.com/2013/05/the-griswolds-hurl-a-strike-at-brooklyn-bowl/ |title=The Griswolds Hurl a Strike at Brooklyn Bowl |publisher=Review Fix |date= |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref> The band began recording their debut album with producer [[Tony Hoffer]] in January 2014.<ref name="inveterate1">{{cite web | url=http://www.inveterate.co.uk/music/interview-the-griswolds-2015/145994/ | title=Interview: The Griswolds – "Tony Hoffer Pushed Us To Our Absolute Creative Limits" | publisher=Inveterate | date=21 May 2015 | accessdate=21 May 2015}}</ref>

The official music video for "Red Tuxedo" premiered on the [[Noisey|VICE Noisey]] website on Valentine's Day (14 February), 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/heres-the-griswolds-video-for-red-tuxedo |title=Play The Griswolds' Video for "Red Tuxedo" on Your Valentine's Date Tonight &#124; NOISEY |publisher=Noisey.vice.com |date=2014-02-14 |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref> The band's song "Beware the Dog" is the first song vocalist Chris Whitehall and lead guitarist Daniel Duque-Perez wrote together. Whitehall described it as being "about losing someone we were really close with to heavy drug use. [... It's] about that journey – the good and the bad times."<ref>{{cite web|last=Law |first=Tarynn |url=http://www.thefourohfive.com/news/article/check-out-the-latest-song-from-the-griswolds-beware-the-dog-139 |title=The Griswolds - 'Beware the Dog' &#124; New Music &#124; The 405 |publisher=Thefourohfive.com |date=2014-05-13 |accessdate=2015-06-12}}</ref>

In March 2015, the band confirmed that once they return to Australia, following their tour alongside [[Passion Pit]], they were going to commence work on their second album.<ref name="inveterate1"/>

The first single off their second album, ''Out of My Head'', was released on 12 August 2016. ''High Times for Low Lives'', was released on 11 November 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2016/10/13/griswolds-high-times-low-lives|title=The Griswolds Announce New Album and Premiere Title Track|website=[[Entertainment Weekly]]}}</ref>

In September 2016, the band was picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] in the United States and was featured on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'' show hosted by [[Kathie Lee Gifford]] and [[Hoda Kotb]] and broadcast nationally where they performed live the single "Out of My Head".

On 30 August 2019, the band released ''Nice To Meet Ya!'', the lead single off of their second EP and first release since 2016.
