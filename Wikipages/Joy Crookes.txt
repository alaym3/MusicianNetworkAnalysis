'''Joy Elizabeth Akther Crookes''' (born 9 October 1998)<ref name="Findmypast" /><ref name="Companies House">{{Cite web|url=https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/officers/_kWbJ9f-2nyyMLufnE9KMXoaEzU/appointments|title=Joy Elizabeth AKTHER CROOKES - Personal Appointments|website=[[Companies House]]|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=8 January 2020|postscript=. "Date of birth: October 1998" "Nationality: Irish" "Country of residence: England"}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BotlEd2hCtV/|title="It’s my birthday. Progression is one way of looking at it…"|last=@joycrookes [confirmed account]|date=9 October 2018|website=[[Instagram]]|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191204205730/https://www.instagram.com/p/BotlEd2hCtV/?hl=en|archive-date=4 December 2019|access-date=4 December 2019}}</ref> is a British<ref>{{Cite news|last=Abdul|first=Geneva|date=7 October 2021|title=Joy Crookes’s Introspective Soul Digs Deep Beneath Her ‘Skin’|work=[[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/10/07/arts/music/joy-crookes.html|access-date=13 October 2021|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> singer-songwriter. She incorporates details about relationships, self-reliance, her culture, her [[South London]] roots and her identity in her music. Crookes has released three extended plays since 2017 and was nominated for the [[Rising Star (Brit Award)|Rising Star Award]] at the [[2020 Brit Awards]]. Her debut studio album, ''[[Skin (Joy Crookes album)|Skin]]'', was released in October 2021 to critical acclaim.

== Early life ==
Joy Elizabeth Akther Crookes was born in the [[Lambeth]] district of [[South London]] on 9 October 1998 to a [[Bangladeshis|Bangladeshi]]-[[Bengalis|Bengali]] mother from [[Dhaka]] and an [[Irish people|Irish]] father from [[Dublin]].<ref name="Stylist" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/jan/12/joy-crookes-reminiscence-ep|title=One to watch: Joy Crookes|last=Joshi|first=Tara|date=12 January 2019|work=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=5 December 2019|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref><ref name=":10" /> She grew up in [[Elephant and Castle]],<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/interviews/joy-crookes-on-the-rise|title=On The Rise: Joy Crookes|last=O'Shea|first=Clare|date=22 March 2019|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> where she spent eight years at a Catholic state primary school.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/esmagazine/joy-crookes-the-soulful-singer-whos-taken-over-2019-a4268781.html|title=Joy Crookes: the soulful singer who's taken over 2019|last=Fishwick|first=Samuel|date=23 October 2019|website=[[Evening Standard]]|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="Stylist" /> Crookes gained interest in singing after attending a jazz and blues workshop and, by the age of 13, had started publishing [[Cover version|covers]] of [[Laura Marling]] and [[reggae]] to [[YouTube]].<ref name=":0" /> Whilst a teenager, Crookes taught herself how to play guitar, piano and bass, before writing her own music.<ref name=":9" /> When aged 14, her parents separated, and she moved with her mother to [[Ladbroke Grove]].<ref name=":0" /> In April 2013, Crookes uploaded a cover of "[[Hit the Road Jack]]" by [[Ray Charles]] to YouTube at the age of 14.<ref>{{Citation|title=Hit The Road Jack-Ray Charles (cover by Joy and Paulo)|date=12 April 2013|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xooCiCmkS6U|work=[[YouTube]]|language=en|access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref> The video gained the attention of over 600,000 viewers, notably including her current manager a few months later.<ref name=":10">{{Cite web|url=http://gal-dem.com/joy-crookes-turning-bad-choices-into-summer-jams/|title=Joy Crookes: Turning Bad Choices into Summer Jams|last=Dos Santos|first=Vanessa|date=31 May 2017|website=[[gal-dem]]|language=en-GB|url-status=live|access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2016–2017: ''Influence'' ===
At the age of 17, Crookes released her debut single "New Manhattan" in February 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/joy-crookes-shares-debut-single-new-manhattan-145|title=Joy Crookes shares impressive debut single 'New Manhattan'|last=Buck|first=Courtney|date=6 February 2016|website=[[The 405 (website)|The 405]]|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160209010812/http://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/joy-crookes-shares-debut-single-new-manhattan-145|archive-date=9 February 2016|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> as well as "Sinatra" in August 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/08/10/joy-crookes-sinatra-premiere|title=Joy Crookes Plays With Fire On Trip-Soul Single "Sinatra"|last=Darville|first=Jordan|date=10 August 2016|website=[[The FADER]]|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> and "Bad Feeling" in June 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2017/06/06/premiere-joy-crookes-bad-feeling/|title=Premiere: Joy Crookes - "Bad Feeling"|date=6 June 2017|website=Wonderland Magazine|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> "New Manhattan" is a love song that was named after the area in [[Brussels]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/introducing-joy-crookes|title=Introducing... Joy Crookes|last=Murray|first=Robin|date=4 February 2016|website=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> ''[[M Magazine]]'' wrote about Crookes at the start of her career, stating, "[She] may not be out of school yet, but what she lacks in experience she certainly makes up for in sonic dexterity."<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.m-magazine.co.uk/newmusic/30seconds/30-seconds-interview-joy-crookes/|title=30 seconds interview: Joy Crookes|date=18 November 2016|website=[[M Magazine]]|language=en-US|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> When describing the aesthetic in Crookes' debut music video for "Sinatra", Pip Williams from ''[[The Line of Best Fit|Line of Best Fit]]'' wrote, "much like [her] sound, [the video] blurs the classic with the contemporary, blanketing the listener in nostalgia whilst teasing them with hints of something brand new."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/joy-crookes-sinatra|title=Joy Crookes drops video for nostalgic pop masterpiece "Sinatra"|last=Williams|first=Pip|date=29 September 2016|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref>

Crookes released her debut EP, ''Influence'', with Speakerbox and [[Sony Music#Sony Music UK|Insanity Records]] in July 2017.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://respect-mag.com/2017/07/joy-crookes-unlocks-soul-class-influence/|title=Joy Crookes Unlocks Soul, Class on ‘Influence’|last=Blanchet|first=Benjamin|date=24 July 2017|website=[[Respect (magazine)|Respect]]|language=en-US|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref><ref name=":4" /> The five-track EP lasts less than 20 minutes and features "Sinatra", "Bad Feeling", "New Manhattan", "Mother May I Sleep With Danger?" and "Power".<ref name=":3" /> Crookes performed "Mother May I Sleep With Danger?" along with her guitar player Charles J Monneraud on the global music platform, [[ColorsxStudios|COLORS]], in December 2017.<ref name=":6" /> As of November 2019, the video gained over eight million views on [[YouTube]].<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url=https://colorsxstudios.com/shows/joy-crookes-mother-may-sleep-danger/|title=Joy Crookes - Mother May I Sleep With Danger? {{!}} A COLORS SHOW|date=21 December 2017|website=[[COLORS Studios]]|language=en-US|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> She told ''[[BBC]]'' that she wrote the song by herself on New Year's Day of 2017 and began playing the song on tour, stating "When you tour a song you get to know it - you stay over at its house, you meet its mum, you get to know the sibling it doesn't like. So by the time we did Colors, it was a walk in the park. The video really changed everything. For about six months after that, everywhere I went people would say, 'Are you Joy from Colors?'"<ref name=":9" />

== Personal life and artistry ==
As of August 2021, Crookes has over 211,000 followers on her [[Instagram]] account.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/joycrookes/|title=Joy Crookes (@joycrookes) • Instagram photos and videos|website=[[Instagram]]|publisher=@joycrookes [confirmed account]|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> Crookes tattooed the name of her Irish grandfather, Frankie Crookes, onto her arm before he died in 2018.<ref name=":11" />

Crookes has cited [[Black Uhuru]], [[Marvin Gaye]], [[The Pogues]], [[Sinéad O'Connor]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Gregory Isaacs]] and [[Kate Nash]] as some of the names incorporated with her first experiences with music.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://indie-mag.com/2017/07/joy-crookes/|title=Rising R&B-star Joy Crookes gives it all for her dream|last=Migowski|first=Max|date=11 July 2017|website=INDIE Magazine|language=en-GB|url-status=live|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref><ref name=":1" /><ref name=":5" /> She told [[BBC]] about the music she was exposed to whilst driving with her father to her Irish dancing lessons, "My dad wanted to give me a real education. From [[Nick Cave]] to [[King Tubby]] to all this [[Music of Pakistan|Pakistani music]]. He'd say, 'This is from your ends of the world, you should hear this.'"<ref name=":9" />
