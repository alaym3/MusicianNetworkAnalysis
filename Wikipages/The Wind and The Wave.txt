'''The Wind and The Wave''' (stylized as '''The Wind + The Wave''') is an American [[Musical ensemble|band]] composed of Dwight A. Baker and Patricia Lynn.

==Background==
Dwight Baker and Patricia Lynn played their first show on July 4, 2013, in [[Austin, Texas]], opening for [[Bob Schneider]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JbkRxDzP56g#t=82 |title=YouTube |publisher=YouTube |date= |accessdate=August 10, 2015}}</ref>

Previously, Lynn had been the lead singer of [[the Soldier Thread]]<ref name="nytimes1">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/09/us/as-sxsw-begins-eyes-are-on-the-local-bands.html?_r=0|title=AS SXSW Begins Eyes are on Local Bands|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|accessdate=August 10, 2015}}</ref> and Baker worked both in front of the microphone as well as in the [[recording studio]] for [[Alpha Rev]], [[Brandi Carlile]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dwightabaker.com/brandi-carlile/ |title=Brandi Carlile – Dwight A. Baker |publisher=Dwightabaker.com |date= |accessdate=August 10, 2015}}</ref> [[Blue October]], and Bob Schneider.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/dwight-a-baker-mn0000159836 |title=Dwight A. Baker &#124; Credits |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |date= |accessdate=August 10, 2015}}</ref> In addition, Baker has also written music for [[Kelly Clarkson]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dwightabaker.com/kelly-clarkson-my-december/ |title=Kelly Clarkson – My December – Dwight A. Baker |publisher=Dwightabaker.com |date= |accessdate=August 10, 2015}}</ref> and is the owner/operator of Matchbox Studios in Austin.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.apiaudio.com/nw_950.html |title=API: Press Releases |publisher=Apiaudio.com |date= |accessdate=August 10, 2015}}</ref>

Signed to [[RCA Records]] in 2013,<ref name="nytimes1"/> their debut album, ''From the Wreckage'', was released on August 5, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/thewindandthewave |title=The Wind and The Wave |publisher=Facebook |date= |accessdate=August 10, 2015}}</ref>

The band's cover of "[[Chasing Cars]]" by [[Snow Patrol]] was featured on the episode, "She's Leaving Home", in season eleven of ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]''. The placement propelled the cover song to the number one spot on [[Shazam (service)|Shazam]]'s USA future hits chart.

The band has also had other limited success on television, having various songs featured on [[Nashville (season 4)]], [[Hart of Dixie]], and [[The Vampire Diaries]].<ref name="american1">{{cite news|url=http://americansongwriter.com/2016/06/wind-wave-offer-single-forthcoming-album/|title=Video Premiere: The Wind And The Wave, "Grand Canyon "|magazine=[[American Songwriter]]|accessdate=June 24, 2016}}</ref>

The band signed with [[Island Records]] in March 2016 and released their first single, "Grand Canyon", from their second album, ''Happiness is Not a Place'' on May 13, 2016. The band premiered their official music video for "Grand Canyon" on [[American Songwriter]] on June 24, 2016.

On October 28, 2016, the band released their first album under [[Island Records]] titled ''Happiness is Not a Place''.<ref name="billboard1">{{cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7487256/the-wind-and-the-wave-happiness-is-not-a-place|title=The Wind and The Wave Unveil 'Happiness Is Not a Place' Album Details, Share Title Track: Exclusive First Listen|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=August 25, 2016}}</ref> The album was produced by [[Butch Walker]]<ref name="butchproduce">{{cite news|url=http://thewindandthewave.com/|title=Butch Walker produces second album|website=The Wind and The Wave|accessdate=November 3, 2016}}</ref> and features a more raw, less polished sound than the band's freshman album.

The band's third album ''Human Beings Let You Down'' was released in October 2018,{{citation needed|date=November 2018}} and the band supported the album with a national tour.{{citation needed|date=November 2018}} On March 6, 2020, the band was the opening act for [[Stereophonics]] at [[London]]'s [[The O2 Arena|O<sub>2</sub> Arena]].
