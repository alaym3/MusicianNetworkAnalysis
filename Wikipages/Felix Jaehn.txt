'''Felix Kurt Jähn''' (born 28 August 1994), known professionally as '''Felix Jaehn''' ({{IPAc-en|j|iː|n}}<ref>{{Cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lXlf5_XQ7wY|title=Felix Jaehn {{!}} 17 Favorite Things|date=19 January 2018|work=Cntl+Alt+Delete|publisher=[[YouTube]]|medium=Video|time=0:00|people=Jaehn, Felix|access-date=19 June 2021}}</ref>), is a German DJ and record producer specialising in [[tropical house]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/entertainment/articles/2015-02/19/what-is-tropical-house-a-guide |title=Like it or Not, Kygo's Tropical House is the Sound of the Summer}}</ref> He achieved international success with his [[remix]] of [[Omi (singer)|Omi]]’s song "[[Cheerleader (song)|Cheerleader]]", which topped the charts in multiple countries and reached number-one on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in 2015.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Molanphy|first=Chris|date=2015-07-20|title=Why Is OMI’s “Cheerleader” No. 1?|language=en-US|work=Slate|url=http://www.slate.com/blogs/browbeat/2015/07/20/omi_s_cheerleader_is_no_1_on_the_billboard_hot_100_this_is_the_summer_of.html|access-date=2020-12-15|issn=1091-2339}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Jaehn was born in [[Hamburg]] and raised in [[Schönberg, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern|Schönberg]], near [[Wismar]] in [[Mecklenburg-Vorpommern]], Germany. He took violin lessons at age of 5 and began his DJ career at 16. Jaehn lived in London for a year, where he attended [[Point Blank Music College]] at the age of 17.<ref>[http://www.popsprite.de/hamburgs-neuer-stern-felix-Jähn/#arvlbdata Popsprite.com: Hamburgs neuer Stern Felix Jähn]{{Dead link|date=August 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} {{in lang|de}}</ref><ref name="PB">{{cite web|title=Student Success: March 2015 – Felix Jähn, Anna Kay and R3WIRE|url=http://plus.pointblanklondon.com/student-success-march-2015-felix-jaehn-anna-kay-r3wire/|website=Point Blank London|access-date=28 June 2015|date=16 April 2015}}</ref> Afterwards he briefly studied Business Administration at [[Humboldt-University]] in Berlin.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.spiegel.de/spiegel/kulturspiegel/d-135652765.html |title=Der Glücksbringer |date=2014-06-27 |work=Kulturspiegel |access-date=2015-07-18|last1=Keller |first1=Maren |last2=Leppin |first2=Jonas }}</ref>

==Music career==
===2013–present===
He began his career in the early 2010s. In August 2013, he released his debut single "Sommer am Meer".<ref name="iTunesSommer">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/de/album/sommer-am-meer-ep/id677647389|title="Sommer am Meer – EP" von Felix Jaehn in iTunes|work=iTunes}}</ref> In November 2014, he released the single "Shine".<ref name="iTunesShine">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/de/album/shine-feat.-freddy-verano/id944072563|title="Shine (feat. Freddy Verano & Linying) – Single" von Felix Jaehn in iTunes|work=iTunes}}</ref> In March 2015, he released the single "Dance with Me".<ref name="iTunesDancewithMe">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/de/album/dance-me-feat.-thallie-ann/id978056237|title="Dance With Me (feat. Thallie Ann Seenyen) – Single" von Felix Jaehn in iTunes|work=iTunes}}</ref> In April 2015, he released a remix of [[Rufus (band)|Rufus]] and [[Chaka Khan]] classic "[[Ain't Nobody]]" retitled "Ain't Nobody (Loves Me Better)" featuring the vocals of [[Jasmine Thompson]] (based on an earlier charting version of the song by Thompson). The remix was an international pan-European hit topping the German Singles Chart. He is now signed to [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music]].<ref name="PB"/> In July 2015, he released the single "Eagle Eyes".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/de/album/eagle-eyes-feat.-lost-frequencies/id1016978844|title="Eagle Eyes (feat. Lost Frequencies & Linying) – Single" von Felix Jaehn auf Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=2016-12-25}}</ref> In 2017, he collaborated with [[Mike Williams (DJ)|Mike Williams]] to release the single "Feel Good" through [[Spinnin' Records]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bigfm.de/news/21503/gut-fuehlen-felix-jaehn-mike-williams-helfen|title=Sich einfach mal gut fühlen: Felix Jaehn und Mike Williams helfen Dir dabei!|date=2017-08-04|work=bigFM|access-date=2017-08-07|language=de}}</ref>

He is arguably best known for remixing Jamaican singer [[Omi (singer)|Omi]]’s song "[[Cheerleader (song)|Cheerleader]]". The single was an international hit for him topping the charts in Germany, Austria, Belgium (Flanders and Wallonia), Canada, Mexico, Denmark, France, Ireland, the Netherlands, Slovakia, Sweden, Switzerland, the United Kingdom, and the United States. It was also a very popular wedding dance song in the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|title = DJ Kamayo Entertainment – The Best Wedding DJs in Ventura County|url = http://www.djkamayo.com/#!Best-NEW-Wedding-Dance-Songs-2015/cc56/561f25fd0cf2c3576e5ec5d7|website = DJ Kamayo Entertainment – The Best Wedding DJs in Ventura County|access-date = 2015-10-16}}</ref>

In 2018, [[NOTD]] and Jaehn released the single "[[So Close (NOTD and Felix Jaehn song)|So Close]]", with a music video that starred [[Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue|''Sports Illustrated'' Swimsuit]] cover girl [[Camille Kostek]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8517789/notd-felix-jaehn-so-close-video|title=NOTD & Felix Jaehn Enlist 'Sports Illustrated' Cover Girl Camille Kostek For 'So Close' Video: Premiere|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-06-27}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=Camille Kostek|title=Camille Kostek x So Close Music Video|date=2019-06-26|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TanEY7NHAcA|access-date=2019-06-27}}</ref> That year, he also released his debut studio album, ''[[I (Felix Jaehn album)|I]]'', in February 2018.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|title=I: Felix Jaehn|url=https://itunes.apple.com/de/album/i/1287706713|access-date=July 3, 2018|website=[[iTunes]] (DE)}}</ref> The album features guest vocals by [[Marc E. Bassy]], [[Gucci Mane]], [[Jasmine Thompson]], [[Polina (singer)|Polina]], [[Alma (Finnish singer)|Alma]], and [[Herbert Grönemeyer]].<ref name=":0" />

===Eff===
In 2015, Jaehn launched '''Eff''', a music duo project made up of German singer [[Mark Forster (singer)|Mark Forster]] (real name Mark Cwiertnia, born 11 January 1984) as vocalist and Jaehn as DJ and producer. The two met in an event in Vienna in 2015. Eff is a reference to Felix and Forster. Their first and only single as Eff was "Stimme" that has topped the German Singles Chart for three consecutive weeks, also charting in Austria and Switzerland.

== Personal life ==
