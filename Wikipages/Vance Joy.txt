'''James Gabriel Keogh''' (born 1 December 1987),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tealmagazine.com/articles/vance-joy-interview-at-acl|title=Interview with Vance Joy at ACL Festival: His Next Album, Skate Crew, and Savage Garden|website=Tealmagazine.com|access-date=2020-03-26}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.warnerchappell.com/composer-songs/KEOGH+JAMES+GABRIEL+(CA)|title=James Gabriel Keogh – Search|work=Warner/Chappell Music}}</ref> known professionally as '''Vance Joy''', is an [[Australian]] singer and songwriter. He signed a five-album deal with [[Atlantic Records]] in 2013.<ref name="Adams">{{cite news|last=Adams |first=Cameron |title=Singer-songwriter Vance Joy signs breakthrough US album deal with Atlantic Records |url=http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/music/singer-songwriter-vance-joy-signs-breakthrough-us-album-deal-with-atlantic-records/story-e6frfn09-122663249969 |access-date=19 August 2013 |newspaper=News.com.au |date=1 May 2013 }}{{dead link|date=May 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> He released his debut EP ''[[God Loves You When You're Dancing]]'' in March 2013. His song "[[Riptide (Vance Joy song)|Riptide]]" was voted number 1 on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2013|2013 Triple J Hottest 100]]. Joy released his debut studio album ''[[Dream Your Life Away]]'' on 5 September 2014 in Australia and on 9 September 2014 elsewhere.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/dream-your-life-away/id896107161|title=iTunes – Music – Dream Your Life Away by Vance Joy|work=iTunes|access-date=18 November 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/dream-your-life-away/id898051683 |title=iTunes – Music – Dream Your Life Away by Vance Joy |work=iTunes |access-date=18 November 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150121103324/https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/dream-your-life-away/id898051683 |archive-date=21 January 2015 }}</ref> At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2015]], he won [[ARIA Award for Best Male Artist|Best Male Artist]]. His second studio album, ''[[Nation of Two]]'', was released in 2018.

==Early life==
Joy attended St. Patricks Primary School, Murrumbeena in Melbourne. He was school captain, and graduated from [[St Kevin's College, Melbourne|St Kevin's College, Toorak]] in 2005. Joy later graduated from [[Monash University]] with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] and [[Bachelor of Laws]].{{When|date=November 2010}}<ref name="SMH Top 10"/>

Prior to his music career, Joy was a promising [[Australian rules football]] player. In 2008 and 2009, he played for the [[Coburg Football Club]], then called the Coburg Tigers, as key defender in the [[Victorian Football League]] (VFL),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://websites.sportstg.com/assoc_page.cgi?client=1-118-0-0-0&sID=60264&&news_task=DETAIL&articleID=27889070|title=Former Coburg player claims Hottest 100 - VFL|website=Sportstg.com|access-date=2020-03-26}}</ref> winning best first year player award in 2008.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://websites.sportstg.com/club_info.cgi?c=1-118-10471-0-16771085&sID=56260&&news_task=DETAIL&articleID=7001048|title=2008 Coburg Football Club Best Fairest Results - Coburg|website=Sportstg.com|access-date=2020-03-26}}</ref><ref name="SMH Top 10">{{cite newspaper|url=http://m.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/top-10-things-to-know-about-hottest-100-winner-vance-joy-20140128-31jis.html|title=Top 10 things to know about Hottest 100 winner Vance Joy |newspaper=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=28 January 2014|access-date=24 August 2014|first1=Melissa|last1=Jenkins|first2=Roger|last2=Ma}}</ref>

Joy explains his choice for a music career over a football one by saying that he "[...] wasn't ever really in the mix (for the AFL)", being "good enough to go from local footy to the VFL. Out of three seasons, I had a handful of games. You always think you can do better, but I think I reached my potential as a football player."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thewest.com.au/entertainment/music/joy-moves-from-footy-to-musical-goals-ng-ya-353905|title=Joy moves from footy to musical goals|date=19 July 2013|website=The West Australian}}</ref> In another interview, he stated "for a period of time I was super focused on footy, I played a step below professional level. I had opportunities to play for a couple of teams but my heart wasn't completely in it. I guess I was just doing something in the meantime before writing my first songs."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/vance-joy-interview-riptide-singer-talks-football-brandon-flowers-and-his-debut-album-9730232.html|title=Vance Joy interview: 'Riptide' singer talks football, Brandon Flowers|date=15 September 2014|newspaper=[[The Independent]]}}</ref>

==Music career==
===2013: ''God Loves You When You're Dancing''===
{{Main|God Loves You When You're Dancing}}
On 21 January 2013, Joy released his debut single "[[From Afar (song)|From Afar]]". On 22 March 2013, he released his debut EP ''[[God Loves You When You're Dancing]]''.<ref name="EP1">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/god-loves-you-when-youre-dancing/id596264820|title=iTunes – Music – God Loves You When You're Dancing – EP by Vance Joy|work=iTunes}}</ref> The single "[[Riptide (Vance Joy song)|Riptide]]" became a success on Australian commercial radio,<ref>{{cite news|last=Kuch|first=Jesse|title=Thanks to Riptide, Vance Joy is on his way to being Australia's next big musical superstar|url=http://www.cairns.com.au/article/2013/08/01/246050_entertainment.html|access-date=19 August 2013|newspaper=Cairns.com.au|date=1 August 2013}}</ref> peaking at number 6 on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Singles Chart]] and being certified Nine times platinum by the [[Australian Record Industry Association]] (ARIA).<ref name="AUS2013">{{cite web|title=ARIA Charts – Accreditations – 2013 Singles|url=http://www.aria.com.au/pages/httpwww.aria.com.aupagesaria-charts-accreditations-singles-2013.htm|work=ARIA Charts|publisher=ARIA|access-date=6 January 2014}}</ref> The song featured in a [[GoPro]] TV advertising campaign in the USA.<ref>{{cite news|last=Fitzsimons|first=Scott|title=Vance Joy Features In US GoPro TV Ad|url=http://themusic.com.au/news/all/2013/07/19/vance-joy-lands-us-gopro-tv-ad/|access-date=19 August 2013|newspaper=theMusic.com.au|date=19 July 2013}}</ref> "Riptide" was a worldwide commercial success, helping to launch Joy's career, and became the longest-charting song in [[ARIA Charts]]' history.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/entertainment/music/vance-joys-riptide-breaks-lady-gagas-incredible-record-on-the-australian-charts/story-fni0bvjn-1227349125707|title= Vance Joy's Riptide breaks Lady Gaga's incredible record on the Australian charts|first= Cameron|last= Adams|publisher= www.heraldsun.com.au|date= 10 May 2015|access-date= 10 May 2015}}</ref> The song is also certified 5x platinum in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kawashima|first1=Dale|title=Vance Joy Breaks Through With Hit "Riptide" and Debut Album; Will Open For Taylor Swifts Tour|url=http://www.songwriteruniverse.com/vance-joy-interview-2014.htm|website=Songwriteruniverse.com|access-date=18 March 2015}}</ref>

"Riptide" was inspired by a motel Joy visited as a kid. "As a child, I used to go on family holidays down the coast of Australia", Joy told the Brand Alley blog. "While we were there, we would stay at this motel called Riptide. So that's where that part of the idea came from."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Heggs|first1=Melanie|title=Vance Joy: 5 Things You Didn't Know About The 'Riptide' Singer|url=http://www.fashionnstyle.com/articles/33303/20141130/vance-joy-5-things-you-didnt-know-about-riptide-singer.htm|website=Fashionnstyle.com|access-date=9 March 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150312184527/http://www.fashionnstyle.com/articles/33303/20141130/vance-joy-5-things-you-didnt-know-about-riptide-singer.htm|archive-date=12 March 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> In an interview with ''Soundcheck'' in January 2014, Joy discussed his upcoming studio album. saying "I think there’s always a bit of variety with my songwriting, so probably the EP is a good reflection of what the rest of the album will be like. It’s all different songs, I guess, but all tied together in a way."<ref>{{cite news|last=Law|first=Adrienne|title=INTERVIEW: Vance Joy|url=http://thisissoundcheck.co.uk/interview-vance-joy/|newspaper=thisissoundcheck.co.uk|access-date=13 January 2014}}</ref> Joy said in an interview with Triple J about his stage name, "I was going through a [[Peter Carey (novelist)|Peter Carey]] phase and reading a lot of his books. There's a book called ''[[Bliss (novel)|Bliss]]''. The main character's name is Harry Joy and his grandfather is Vance Joy. He's the storyteller and a crazy old man. Plus, I thought it was a cool name."<ref>{{cite web|title=Vance Joy profile|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/media/s3742548.htm|access-date=18 November 2015|publisher=abc.net.au}}</ref>

===2014–2016: ''Dream Your Life Away''===
[[File:ACL Music Festival 2015 Overview (Austin, Texas, 2015-10) (22278137626).jpg|right|190px|thumb|upright=0.9|Joy at the [[Austin City Limits Music Festival]] in [[Austin, Texas]] in 2015]]
On 15 July 2014, Joy announced the title of his debut album, ''[[Dream Your Life Away]]'', which was released on 5 September 2014 in Australia, 9 September 2014 in the United States, and 15 September 2014 worldwide.<ref>''[http://vancejoy.com/dreamyourlifeaway Dream Your Life Away] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140718043031/http://vancejoy.com/dreamyourlifeaway |date=18 July 2014 }}'', vancejoy.com; accessed 17 July 2014.</ref> In July 2014, he released "[[Mess Is Mine]]" as the third overall single from the album, with the song peaking at number 37 in Australia. He gave his first live concert with songs on the album in September 2014, in [[Melbourne]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vancejoy.com/dreamyourlifeaway |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-07-17 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140718043031/http://vancejoy.com/dreamyourlifeaway |archive-date=18 July 2014 }}</ref> On 7 September 2014, Joy released "First Time" as the fourth single from the album in the [[United Kingdom]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/first-time-single/id897562433 |title=iTunes Store |work=apple.com |access-date=18 November 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150212085558/https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/first-time-single/id897562433 |archive-date=12 February 2015 }}</ref>

"[[Georgia (Vance Joy song)|Georgia]]" was released as a single in February 2015. Speaking to ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', Joy revealed that he began writing "Georgia" in 2006, then sat on it until he figured out the words and melody.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Spanos|first1=Brittany|title=Watch Vance Joy's War-Themed 'Georgia' Video With a Surprising Twist|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/premieres/watch-vance-joys-war-themed-georgia-video-with-a-surprising-twist-20150326|website=Rollingstone.com|access-date=6 April 2015}}</ref> A deluxe edition was released on 4 September 2015, which consisted of two new tracks and five live songs. "[[Fire and the Flood]]" and "[[Straight into Your Arms]]" were also released as singles, with "Fire and the Flood" later being certified platinum by ARIA. He promoted his album on the Dream Your Life Away Tour, which began on 17 October 2013 in [[Dallas, Texas]].<ref>[http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Vance-Joys-Debut-Album-DREAM-YOUR-LIFE-AWAY-to-Be-Released-99-20140715 Vance Joy's Debut Album 'DREAM YOUR LIFE AWAY' to Be Released 9/9] – ''BWWMusicWorld'' (published 15 July 2014)</ref>

Joy was announced as the support act for [[Taylor Swift]]'s [[The 1989 World Tour|1989 World Tour]] on 3 November 2014. Joy appeared on all the tour dates in [[North America]], the [[United Kingdom]] and [[Australia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/taylor-swift-reveals-massive-1989-world-tour-20141103|title=Taylor Swift Reveals Massive 1989 World Tour|work=Rolling Stone}}</ref> Swift performed a rendition of "Riptide" on 9 October 2014 for the [[BBC]]'s 'Live Lounge' segment.<ref>{{cite web|title=Taylor Swift covers Vance Joy's Riptide in the Live Lounge|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2GGRdwfhl-U|website=[[YouTube]]|access-date=2 January 2015|format=Video upload|date=9 October 2014}}</ref> In November 2015, he was picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] and was featured on the American television network [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'', where he performed his hit single "Riptide".

On 13 May 2015, he performed "Riptide" in a duet featuring [[Quentin Alexander]] live to a standing ovation at the [[Dolby Theater]] during the [[American Idol (season 14)|season 14 finale]] of ''[[American Idol]]''. On 16 June 2015, Joy released "Great Summer", a single from the soundtrack for ''[[Paper Towns (film)|Paper Towns]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/albums/Various-artists/Music-from-the-motion-picture-paper-towns|title=Music From The Motion Picture Paper Towns|work=[[Genius (website)|Genius]]|access-date=December 20, 2019|date=July 10, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6642055/paper-towns-author-john-green-soundtrack-perfect-summer-album|title='Paper Towns' Author John Green: Film Soundtrack is the Perfect 'Summer Album|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Jackie|last=Frere|date=June 24, 2015|access-date=December 20, 2019}}</ref> At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2015]] he won the [[ARIA Award for Best Male Artist|Best Male Artist]] category for ''Dream Your Life Away'', with six further nominations.<ref name="ARIA List">ARIA Music Awards for Vance Joy:

===2017–2020: ''Nation of Two'' & ''Live at Red Rocks Amphitheatre''===
{{Main|Nation of Two|Live at Red Rocks Amphitheatre}}
[[File:Vance Joy (40466599182).jpg|left|thumb|225px|Joy performing in 2018 during the [[Nation of Two World Tour]]]]
In 2017, Joy released two singles from his forthcoming second studio album: "[[Lay It on Me (Vance Joy song)|Lay It All on Me]]" in July, which was nominated for [[ARIA Award for Single of the Year|Song of the Year]] at the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2018]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ariaawards.com.au/news/2018/2018-aria-award-nominees-announced|title=2018 ARIA AWARD NOMINEES ANNOUNCED |publisher=ARIA|date=11 October 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref> and "[[Like Gold]]" in November, which peaked at number 14 on the Australian charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/23790/20171114-0001/Issue1446.pdf|title=ARIA Report Issue 1446|publisher=ARIA|date=13 November 2017|access-date=22 September 2018}}</ref> The song was certified Gold by [[ARIA]] in 2019. He released "[[We're Going Home (Vance Joy song)|We're Going Home]]" on 12 January 2018, which was written while Joy was opening for the [[1989 World Tour]] in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/first-spin-vance-joy-were-going-home/9317998|title= First Spin: Vance Joy premieres 'We're Going Home', inspired by touring with Taylor Swift|publisher= ABC|date= 11 January 2018|access-date=25 January 2018}}</ref> "[[Saturday Sun (Vance Joy song)|Saturday Sun]]" was released on 1 February 2018 as the fourth single, which he stated was inspired by the beaches along the [[California State Route 1|Pacific Coast Highway]] in [[Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/vance-joy-nation-of-two-songwriting/9478806|title=Vance Joy's Nation of Two: the stories behind the songs |publisher=ABC|date=23 February 2018 |access-date=23 May 2018}}</ref>

The last single before the album's release, "[[Call If You Need Me]]", was released on 11 February 2018, and  appeared on the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] [[Hot Rock & Alternative Songs]] chart. Joy released his second studio album ''[[Nation of Two]]'' on 23 February 2018. The album debuted at number 1 in Australia, was certified gold and won [[ARIA Award for Best Adult Contemporary Album|Best Adult Contemporary Album]] at the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2018]]. He released an upbeat, remastered version of "[[I'm with You (Vance Joy song)|I'm With You]]" as a single from the album on 21 September 2018, which was certified platinum by the ARIA.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.auspop.com.au/2018/9/aria-chart-watch-492/|title=ARIA Chart Watch #492|publisher=auspOp|date=29 September 2018|access-date=27 October 2018}}</ref> In November 2018, Joy released a live album titled ''[[Live at Red Rocks Amphitheatre]]'', a live concert album of the Nation of Two World Tour, recorded at the [[Red Rocks Amphitheatre]] in [[Colorado]]. Joy was the opening act for [[Pink (singer)|P!nk]] on her [[Beautiful Trauma World Tour]] in 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Vance Joy Unveils Heartfelt Acoustic Cover of Pink's 'What About Us': Watch |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8513666/vance-joy-acoustic-cover-pink-what-about-us |website=Billboard}}</ref>

===2021–present: "You" and "Missing Piece"===
On 29 January 2021, Vance released the single "[[You (Benny Blanco, Marshmello and Vance Joy song)|You]]", a collaboration with [[Benny Blanco]] and [[Marshmello]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9517447/vance-joy-benny-blanco-marshmello-you-video/ |title=Vance Joy teams up with Benny Blanco and Marshmello on joyous 'You': Watch |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=29 January 2021 |access-date=25 May 2021 |author=Brandle, Lars |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210129071148/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9517447/vance-joy-benny-blanco-marshmello-you-video/ |archive-date=29 January 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref>

On 20 May 2021, Joy released the single "[[Missing Piece (Vance Joy song)|Missing Piece]]". In [[scoop (news)|an exclusive]], ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' revealed that the song would be featured in the next episode of ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://variety.com/2021/music/news/vance-joy-greys-anatomy-missing-piece-song-1234976136/ |title=Vance Joy's new single, 'Missing Piece,' scores television debut on 'Grey's Anatomy' (Exclusive) |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=20 May 2021 |access-date=21 May 2021 |author=Shafer, Ellise |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210520132038/https://variety.com/2021/music/news/vance-joy-greys-anatomy-missing-piece-song-1234976136/ |archive-date=20 May 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> The song has reached gold status in Australia.<ref>{{Cite web|title=ARIA Top 50 Singles Chart|url=https://www.aria.com.au/charts/singles-chart|access-date=2021-08-18|website=www.aria.com.au|language=en}}</ref>
