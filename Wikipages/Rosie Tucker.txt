'''Rosie Tucker''' is an American musician from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref name="guitarworld">{{cite web |last1=Doria |first1=Matt |title=Spotlight: Rosie Tucker |url=https://www.guitarworld.com/features/spotlight-rosie-tucker |publisher=[[Guitar World]] |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref> They identify as queer and use they/them pronouns.<ref name="guitardotcom">{{cite web |last1=Baines |first1=Huw |title=Introducing… Rosie Tucker, the underground darling crafting songs about sweetness, grit and queer self-discovery |url=https://guitar.com/features/interviews/introducing-rosie-tucker-the-underground-darling-crafting-songs-about-sweetness-grit-and-queer-self-discovery/ |publisher=[[Guitar.com]] |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref>

==History==
Tucker self-released their first full-length album in 2015 titled ''Lowlight''.<ref name="stereogum">{{cite web |last1=Rettig |first1=James |title=Rosie Tucker – “Gay Bar” |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2028547/rosie-tucker-gay-bar/premiere/ |publisher=[[Stereogum]] |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref> In 2019, Tucker announced their second full-length album, ''Never Not Never Not Never Not'', which was released on New Professor Music.<ref name="stereogum"/> In 2021, Tucker announced they had signed to [[Epitaph Records]].<ref name="brooklynvegan">{{cite web |last1=Hatfield |first1=Amanda |title=Rosie Tucker signs to Epitaph for new LP, ‘Sucker Supreme’ (watch “Habanero” video) |url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/rosie-tucker-signs-to-epitaph-for-new-lp-sucker-supreme-watch-habanero-video/ |publisher=[[BrooklynVegan]] |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref> They also announced their third full-length album titled ''Sucker Supreme''. The album was released on April 30, 2021.<ref name="allsongsconsidered">{{cite web |title=New Music Friday: The Top 7 Albums Out On April 30 |url=https://www.npr.org/2021/04/27/991243810/new-music-friday-the-top-7-albums-out-on-april-30 |publisher=[[All Songs Considered]] |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref> The album was listed on Pitchfork's list of "29 Great Records You May Have Missed: Spring 2021".<ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web |last1=Philo |first1=Katie |title=29 Great Records You May Have Missed: Spring 2021 |url=https://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/29-great-records-you-may-have-missed-spring-2021/ |publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref>
