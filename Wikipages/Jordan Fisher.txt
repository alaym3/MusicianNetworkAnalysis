'''Jordan William Fisher''' (born April 24, 1994) is an American actor, singer, dancer, musician and gamer. He began his career with recurring roles on several television series, including ''[[The Secret Life of the American Teenager]]'' in 2012 and ''[[Liv and Maddie]]'' from 2015 to 2017. He also had supporting roles in the television films ''[[Teen Beach Movie]]'' (2013), ''[[Teen Beach 2]]'' (2015) and ''[[Grease Live]]'' (2016), and starred in ''[[Rent: Live]]'' (2019).

Fisher and his dancing partner [[Lindsay Arnold]] won the [[Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 25)|25th season]] of ''[[Dancing with the Stars (U.S. TV series)|Dancing with the Stars]]'' (2017). He subsequently hosted ''[[Dancing with the Stars: Juniors]]'' in 2018, and commentated the 2019 ''[[Fortnite World Cup|Fortnite World-Cup]]''. As a singer, his self-titled EP was released by [[Hollywood Records]] in 2016.

On [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]], Fisher portrayed [[John Laurens]]/[[Philip Hamilton]] in ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]'' from 2016 to 2017, and took on the lead role of [[Evan Hansen]] in ''[[Dear Evan Hansen]]'' in 2020. He has also starred in the 2020 [[Netflix]] films ''[[To All the Boys: P.S. I Still Love You]]'' and ''[[Work It (film)|Work It]]''.

==Career==
In 2014, Fisher released three [[Pop music|pop]]-[[Soul music|soul]] songs on [[Radio Disney]]: "By Your Side", "Never Dance Alone" and "What I Got".<ref name="mhudelson"/><ref name="13things">[https://ohmy.disney.com/music/2015/07/05/13-things-you-should-know-about-jordan-fisher/ "13 Things You Should Know About Jordan Fisher,"] Oh My Disney, July 5, 2015.</ref> In 2015, he signed a record deal with [[Hollywood Records]].<ref name="aharvey"/> On February 1, 2016, he released "Counterfeit", his first track for the label.<ref>Bianca Gracie, [http://www.idolator.com/7621998/jordan-fisher-grease-live-releases-new-counterfeit-track-listen "Jordan Fisher From 'Grease: Live' Releases New 'Counterfeit' Track: Listen,"] [[Idolator (website)|Idolator]], February 1, 2016.</ref>

Fisher's first single, "All About Us", was released on April 15, 2016, and produced by Warren "Oak" Felder of the production duo [[Pop & Oak]].<ref name="eceron">Ella Ceron, [http://www.teenvogue.com/story/jordan-fisher-single-all-about-us "Why You Need to Put Grease Live!'s Jordan Fisher on Your Playlists ASAP,"] ''[[Teen Vogue]]'', June 21, 2016.</ref><ref name="rdaw">Robbie Daw, [http://www.idolator.com/7633280/jordan-fisher-all-about-us-watch-video "Jordan Fisher Makes Pop Debut With 'All About Us': Watch The Video,"] Idolator, May 11, 2016.</ref> The song's music video, directed by [[TK McKamy]], premiered on [[Vibe.com]] on May 11, 2016.<ref name="rdaw"/><ref>Mikey Fresh, [http://www.vibe.com/2016/05/jordan-fisher-all-about-us-video/ "Premiere: Jordan Fisher's 'All About Us' Video,"] ''[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]'', May 11, 2016.</ref> For the week of June 13, 2016, "All About Us" ranked as the second-most added song on pop radio stations.<ref>Brian Cantor, [http://headlineplanet.com/home/2016/06/14/shawn-mendes-treat-you-better-repeats-as-pop-radios-most-added-jordan-fisher-follows/ "Shawn Mendes' 'Treat You Better' Repeats as Pop Radio's Most Added; Jordan Fisher Follows,"] Headline Planet, June 14, 2016.</ref> It is the first track on Fisher's self-titled EP, which was released on August 19, 2016.<ref>Lauren Craddock, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7476112/jordan-fisher-all-i-wanna-do-premiere "'Grease Live!' Star Jordan Fisher Takes Influence From '90s R&B for New Single 'All I Wanna Do': Exclusive Premiere,"] ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', August 17, 2016.</ref> He has referred to the EP's sound as pop-soul-R&B, influenced by '80s soul music.<ref name="tjensen">Tom Jensen, [http://www.foxsports.com/nascar/shake-and-bake/phoenix-international-raceway-jordan-fisher-sprint-cup-series-good-sam-500-031516 "Actor/musician Jordan Fisher calls NASCAR experience 'unreal',"] [[Fox Sports]], March 15, 2016.</ref> Fisher plays six instruments: piano, guitar, bass, harmonica, French horn and drums.<ref>[http://glittermagrocks.com/connect/2015/07/04/interview-with-jordan-fisher-2/ "Interview with Jordan Fisher,"] ''Glitter'', July 4, 2015.</ref>

In 2015, Fisher joined [[Disney Channel Circle of Stars]] for a remake of the song "[[Do You Want to Build a Snowman?]]" from the film ''[[Frozen (2013 film)|Frozen]]''.<ref name="aharvey"/> For his role on ''[[Liv and Maddie]]'', he sang both a duet and ballad version of the song "True Love", which appeared on the show's soundtrack in 2015.<ref>Chelsea Duff, [http://www.girlslife.com/trending/celebs/25483/exclusive-we-talked-to-jordan-fisher-about-liv-and-maddie-his-upcoming-music-and-ultimate-career-goals "Exclusive: We talked to Jordan Fisher about Liv and Maddie, his upcoming music and ultimate career goals,"] Girls' Life, February 1, 2016.</ref> He contributed two tracks, "Fallin' For Ya" and "Wanna Be With You", along with vocals on three others, to the ''[[Teen Beach 2]]'' soundtrack, released in 2015.<ref name="13things"/> He is also featured on [[Olivia Holt]]'s self-titled debut EP on "Thin Air". On March 13, 2016, he sang the national anthem before the [[NASCAR]] [[Good Sam 500]] stock car race at the [[Phoenix International Raceway]] in [[Avondale, Arizona]].<ref name="tjensen"/> At the 2016 [[Apple Music Festival]] in London, Fisher opened for [[Alicia Keys]].<ref>Becky Lucas, [http://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/article/apple-festival-2016-lineup "Apple Music Festival 2016 Exclusive: Line-Up and Tickets,"] ''[[GQ]]'', August 24, 2016.</ref><ref>Tristan Banning, [http://sidewalkhustle.com/apple-music-festival-10-alicia-keys-jordan-fisher/ "Apple Music Festival 10: Alicia Keys & Jordan Fisher,"] Sidewalk Hustle, September 2016.</ref> Fisher is featured alongside [[Lin-Manuel Miranda]] on "You're Welcome", an end-credits song for the 2016 animated Disney film ''[[Moana (2016 film)|Moana]]''.<ref>Olivia Singh, [http://tigerbeat.com/2016/10/alessia-cara-jordan-fisher-moana-soundtrack/ "Alessia Cara and Jordan Fisher Will Be Featured in This Disney Movie Soundtrack,"] ''Tiger Beat'', October 29, 2016.</ref> In July 2016, he was picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] and was featured on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'' show hosted by [[Kathie Lee Gifford]] and [[Hoda Kotb]] and broadcast nationally where he performed live his single "All About Us". He covered the 1971 [[Ten Years After]] song "[[I'd Love to Change the World]]" for the 2017 [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] miniseries ''[[When We Rise]]''.<ref>[http://ew.com/tv/2017/02/23/when-we-rise-jordan-fisher-music-video/ When We Rise: Jordan Fisher music video released]</ref> Fisher has performed at numerous [[We Day|WE Day]] concerts across the United States, to benefit [[WE Charity]].<ref>[https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/WE-Day-Announces-New-York-Line-Up-Featuring-Jordan-Fisher-Rowan-Blanchard-Darren-Criss-and-More-20170315 "WE Day Announces New York Line-Up Featuring Jordan Fisher, Rowan Blanchard, Darren Criss, and More,"] Broadway World, March 15, 2017.</ref><ref>[http://www.beautifulballad.org/2017/04/21/lily-collins-jordan-fisher-and-yara-shahidi-attend-we-day-seattle/ "Lily Collins, Jordan Fisher and Yara Shahidi Attend We Day Seattle,"] Beautiful Ballad, April 21, 2017.</ref>

Fisher's single "Mess" was released on October 6, 2017.<ref>"Jordan Fisher Announces New Single 'Mess' & Will Compete on DWTS Season 25!" Just Jared Jr, September 6, 2017.</ref>
In 2019, he released his single "Be Okay".
In 2020, he released two singles, "Contact" and "Walking on the Ceiling".

Fisher's first television roles were in 2009 as a guest star on ''The Hustler'' on [[Crackle (company)|Crackle]] and ''[[iCarly]]'' on [[Nickelodeon]]. His first major part was as Grace Bowman's half-brother Jacob on seasons 4 and 5 of [[ABC Family]]'s ''[[The Secret Life of the American Teenager]]''.<ref>Stephanie Osmanski, [http://www.m-magazine.com/posts/6-projects-you-never-realized-jordan-fisher-was-in-56830/photos/6-projects-you-never-realized-jordan-fisher-was-in-89894#photo-anchor "6 Projects You Never Realized Jordan Fisher Was In,"] ''[[M Magazine]]'', April 23, 2015.</ref> He portrayed the recurring character Holden Dippledorf on ''[[Liv and Maddie]]'' on the [[Disney Channel]] starting in 2015, and has also appeared in ''[[The Thundermans]]'' and ''[[Teen Wolf (2011 TV series)|Teen Wolf]]''.<ref name="myagoda"/> He played the surfer gang leader Seacat in the cable TV movies ''[[Teen Beach Movie]]'' (2013) and ''[[Teen Beach 2]]'' (2015).<ref name="aharvey"/><ref>Katie Cannon, [http://www.al.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2013/07/birmingham_native_jordan_fishe.html "Birmingham's Jordan Fisher co-stars in Disney Channel's 'Teen Beach Movie',"] ''The Birmingham News'', July 10, 2013.</ref> On ''[[Grease: Live]]'', a live performance of ''[[Grease (musical)|Grease]]'' televised on [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]], Fisher starred as Doody, opposite [[Carly Rae Jepsen]] as his girlfriend Frenchy.<ref name="jhong"/><ref>{{cite web|title=How 'Grease Live!' kickstarted Jordan Fisher's R&B music career|url=http://www.aol.com/article/2016/06/23/how-grease-live-kickstarted-jordan-fishers-randb-music-career/21400729/|website=Aol|access-date=July 8, 2016|date=June 23, 2016}}</ref> He sings a rendition of "[[Those Magic Changes]]" that was widely praised as a highlight of the show.<ref name="eceron"/><ref>Lindsey Weber, [http://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/tv/q-and-a/a53064/grease-live-doody-jordan-fisher-interview/ "Grease: Live! His professional partner was [[Lindsay Arnold]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/dancing-stars-season-25-celebrity-cast-frankie-muniz/story?id=49603734|title='Dancing With the Stars' season 25 celebrity cast: Frankie Muniz, Barbara Corcoran, Nick Lachey and more|work=ABC News|date=September 6, 2017|access-date=September 6, 2017}}</ref> On November 21, Fisher and Arnold reached the finals, and were declared the winners of the season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/tv/dancing-with-the-stars-season-25-winner-jordan-fisher|title=Jordan Fisher crowned Dancing with the Stars Season 25 Mirrorball Champion|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=November 21, 2017|access-date=November 22, 2017}}</ref>

From 2018 to 2020, Fisher voiced the character of Sea Hawk in ''[[She-Ra and the Princesses of Power]]''.  

In 2019, Fisher appeared in another musical production on Fox, this time ''[[Rent: Live]]'', as Mark Cohen.<ref>{{cite news|last=Turchiano|first=Danielle|title=Tinashe, Kiersey Clemons Among Cast for Fox's Live Version of ''Rent''|url=https://variety.com/2018/tv/news/fox-rent-live-musical-casting-tinashe-kiersey-clemons-brandon-victor-dixon-valentina-1202677983/|work=Variety|date=29 October 2018|access-date=January 28, 2019}}</ref>
