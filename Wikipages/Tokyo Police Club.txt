'''Tokyo Police Club''' is an [[indie rock]] band from [[Newmarket, Ontario|Newmarket]], [[Ontario]], [[Canada]]. Founded in 2005, it consists of [[vocalist]] and [[bassist]] Dave Monks, [[keyboardist]] Graham Wright, [[guitar]]ist Josh Hook, and [[drum kit|drummer]] Greg Alsop.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2008/04/breaking-out-tokyo-police-club/ |title=Tokyo Police Club |publisher=SPIN.com |date=2008-04-23 |access-date=2011-02-23}}</ref> The band found early success with their 2007 debut EP ''[[A Lesson in Crime]],'' which they followed with several popular releases including the albums ''[[Elephant Shell]]'' and ''[[Champ (album)|Champ]]'' in 2008 and 2010, respectively. During the 2010s, they released three other studio albums. Among other nominations, the band has been twice nominated for the [[Juno Award for Alternative Album of the Year]], in 2011 for ''Champ'' and in 2019 for ''[[TPC (album)|TPC]]''.

==History==

=== 2005–2007: Formation and early years ===
David Monks (born January 21, 1987), Graham Wright (born February 16, 1987), Josh Hook (born May 11, 1987), and Greg Alsop (born March 20, 1985) grew up and attended school together in Newmarket, Ontario.<ref name="autogenerated1" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://observer.case.edu/tokyo-police-club-visits-grog/|title=Tokyo Police Club visits Grog|last=Sakthi|first=Yugan|website=The Observer|access-date=2020-01-09}}</ref> In high school, the four played in a band called ''Suburbia'' which eventually disbanded. In 2004, the group informally came together again to form Tokyo Police Club;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-2007-10-05-0710030466-story.html|title=Tokyo Police Club says sayonara to a lark|last=critic|first=Greg Kot, Tribune music|website=chicagotribune.com|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-09}}</ref> the name of the band comes from a 2000s-era online Band Name Generator.<ref name=":0" />

Early on, the band played some small shows in the Toronto area. Tokyo Police Club were asked to play in the [[Pop Montreal]] festival,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2007/05/an_interview_wi_19.html |title=an Interview with Tokyo Police Club |publisher=Brooklynvegan.com |date=2007-05-17 |access-date=2011-02-23}}</ref> and soon after they signed with Toronto label [[Paper Bag Records]]; Monks and Alsop dropped out of college to become professional musicians.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://minnesota.publicradio.org/display/web/2010/08/19/tokyo-police-club-live/ |title=Tokyo Police Club performs in The Current studios &#124; The Current from |publisher=Minnesota Public Radio |access-date=2011-02-23}}</ref>

The band released its debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''[[A Lesson in Crime]]'', in 2006 on [[Paper Bag Records]]. The recording was less than seventeen minutes long, and some of the songs were quite short.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ukula.com/TorontoArticle.aspx?SectionID=2&ObjectID=1715&CityID=3 |title=Article at UKULA|website=Ukula.com}}</ref> That year they appeared at [[Edgefest]] and the inaugural [[Osheaga Festival]].

The ''[[Smith EP]]'' was released in 2007. During a July show in [[Omaha]], [[Nebraska]], the band signed a deal with [[Saddle Creek Records]] which would release their debut LP.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/42620-tokyo-police-club-sign-to-saddle-creek |title=Tokyo Police Club Sign to Saddle Creek |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080103055043/http://pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/42620-tokyo-police-club-sign-to-saddle-creek |archive-date=2008-01-03 |author=Paul Thompson and Amy Phillips |date= July 20, 2007}}</ref> The single "Your English Is Good"  was released in July and later included on their album ''[[Elephant Shell]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/page/news/43141-tokyo-police-clubs-english-is-good-on-new-single |title=Pitchfork Article|website=Pitchforkmedia.com}}</ref> That year the band performed at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]], [[Lollapalooza]], [[Bumbershoot]], the [[Glastonbury Festival]], and the [[Reading and Leeds Festival]].

=== 2008–2012: Debut record and successful follow-up ===
In 2008, Tokyo Police Club released their first album, ''[[Elephant Shell]]'',<ref>[https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/a-completely-biased-ranking-of-the-60-best-canadian-indie-rock-songs-of-the-00s-part-ii "A Completely Biased Ranking of the 60 Best Canadian Indie Rock Songs of the 00s Part II"]. ''Vice'', Cam Lindsay Apr 10 2017</ref> first via the US [[iTunes Store]] on March 25, 2008, and then on their new label Saddle Creek in April in North America, and in May in the U.K. and Continental Europe via Memphis Industries.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/11426-elephant-shell/|title=Tokyo Police Club Elephant Shell|website=Pitchforkmedia.com}}</ref> In the same year, the band played the [[Roskilde Festival]], the [[Rock am Ring]]-Festival, and [[Street Scene (San Diego music festival)|Street Scene]]. In the fall, they played on the last six weeks of [[Weezer]]'s Troublemaker Tour along with [[Angels and Airwaves]]. 

In 2010, the band played the [[Bonnaroo Music Festival]]. They also played at San Francisco's 2010 [[Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival]]. Their second full-length album, ''[[Champ (album)|Champ]]'', was released in June; it was produced by [[Rob Schnapf]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.spinner.ca/2010/04/28/tokyo-police-club-reminisce-about-coachella-prep-for-new-album/|title=Tokyo Police Club Reminisce About Coachella, Prep for New Album|date=April 28, 2010|work=[[Spinner.com|Spinner]]|access-date=28 April 2010}}</ref> That fall the album appeared on the !Earshot National Top 50 Chart.<ref>[http://www.earshot-online.com/charts/index.cfm?intChartTypeID=101&dWeekOfID=2010-10-26 "The National Top 50 For the Week Ending: Tuesday, October 26, 2010"]. ''!Earshot''.</ref>

On March 27, 2011, Tokyo Police Club performed their single "Bambi" at the 40th Juno Awards in Toronto. In June, the video for "Wait Up (Boots of Danger)" was nominated as best indie video of the year by [[MuchMusic]]. The video was directed by Mike Juneau and Kyle McCreight, and produced by [[Jesse Ewles]] and Chris Cunningham. In 2011, the band also released ''[[Ten Songs, Ten Years, Ten Days|10 Days. 10 Covers. 10 Years.]]'', a project in which they recorded and released a new cover song from each of the previous 10 years, from 2001 to 2010.

In December 2011, the band performed an unreleased song "Beaches" at a live show. In 2012, they joined [[Foster the People]] on the North American portion of their [[Torches (album)|Torches]] tour along with [[Kimbra]]. While continuing to work with the band, Wright also hosted programming on [[CBC Radio 3]].{{Citation needed|date=January 2020}}

=== 2013–present: New albums and anniversaries ===
After a prolific period, the band was relatively quiet from late 2012 until December 2013, when the band debuted a nine-minute [[Music Video#Lyric videos|lyric video]] for "Argentina (Pts. I, II, and III)" on YouTube.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://blog.muchmusic.com/tokyo-police-club-return-with-an-epic-lyric-video-for-argentina-parts-i-ii-iii/|title=Tokyo Police Club Return With An Epic Lyric Video for Argentina, Parts I, II, III|date=December 11, 2013|access-date=11 December 2013}}</ref> Both "Beaches" and "Argentina" are on their album ''[[Forcefield (album)|Forcefield]]'' which was released March 24, 2014. In July the band performed at the 2014 Calgary Stampede in Calgary, Alberta. The band also recorded two songs for the ''Daytrotter Sessions'', which were released online.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.daytrotter.com/daytrotterSessions/564/free-songs-tokyo-police-club|title=Daytrotter Sessions|website=Daytrotter.com}}</ref>

In 2016, the band released their ''Melon Collie and the Infinite Radness'' project, consisting of two companions EPs titled [[Melon Collie and the Infinite Radness: Part One|''Part One'']] and ''[[Melon Collie and the Infinite Radness: Part Two|Part Two]]''. The two were later combined and formally released as a full length LP of the same name. The EPs celebrated a more spontaneous recording ethos, similar to their early albums. With this same celebratory nature, the band also released an expanded tenth anniversary edition of ''[[A Lesson in Crime]]'' that year.

Tokyo Police Club continued to record in 2018 in preparation for a fourth album.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8464459/tokyo-police-club-announce-new-album-tpc |title=Tokyo Police Club Announce New Album 'TPC,' Reveal First Single: Exclusive |work=Billboard |date=July 9, 2018 |access-date=July 9, 2018 |author=Kress, Bryan}}</ref> They released ''[[TPC (album)|TPC]]'' on October 5, 2018 and received a Juno nomination for Alternative Album of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://junoawards.ca/nomination/2019-alternative-album-of-the-year-tokyo-police-club/ |title=The Juno Awards 2019 Alternative Album of the Year Nominees}}</ref> A companion EP titled ''TPC DLX'' was released in October 2020, made up of tracks from the ''TPC'' recording sessions and acoustic versions of songs.<ref>{{Cite web|title=TPC DLX EP, by Tokyo Police Club|url=https://tokyo-police-club.bandcamp.com/album/tpc-dlx-ep|access-date=2021-02-11|website=Tokyo Police Club}}</ref>

In December 2020, the band announced that they would be releasing a special tenth anniversary edition of their album ''[[Champ (album)|Champ]]'', slated for release in March 2021. With this announcement, the band released the single "Hundred Dollar Day."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-02|title=Tokyo Police Club Announce Champ 10th Anniversary Edition|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/music/tokyo-police-club/tokyo-police-club-announce-champ-anniversary/|access-date=2021-02-11|website=pastemagazine.com|language=en}}</ref>

==Television==
On April 19, 2007, Tokyo Police Club made their first US TV performance on the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]''. They played their single "Nature of the Experiment", along with a tambourine accompaniment by the [[CBS Orchestra]]. A year later the band made a second appearance on the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]'', performing the lead single "Tessellate" off their debut LP ''Elephant Shell''.

On November 16, 2008 they appeared on the television show "[[Desperate Housewives]]" in the episode "[[City on Fire (Desperate Housewives)|City on Fire]]" as "Cold Splash", a band competing in a battle-of-the-bands contest. They performed "In A Cave" from their album ''Elephant Shell''. A month later they played "Your English is Good" on [[The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson]] on CBS. On June 28, 2010 they appeared on the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]'', performing the single "Wait Up (Boots of Danger)" off of their second album, [[Champ (album)|''Champ'']].
