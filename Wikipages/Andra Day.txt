==Early life and education==
Day was born on December 30, 1984, in [[Edmonds, Washington|Edmonds]], [[Washington (state)|Washington]].<ref name="Spokane"/><ref name="ASCAP">{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/Home/ace-title-search/index.aspx|title=Mistakes Work ID No. 888648708 ISWC No. T9166826068|publisher=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]]|access-date=December 27, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130306032310/https://www.ascap.com/Home/ace-title-search/index.aspx|archive-date=March 6, 2013}} Work ID No. 888648708 {{ISWC|T9166826068}}</ref> She moved to [[Southern California]] at age three, and grew up in [[Southeast San Diego]], California, with her family.<ref name="SDUTMain2"/><ref name="Cuttler, Andrea">{{cite web|url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/features/a35232878/andra-day-billie-holiday-interview/|title=Andra Day Is Ready for a Different Kind of Spotlight|author=Cuttler, Andrea|publisher=Harper's Bazaar|accessdate=2021-03-18|date=2021-02-08}}</ref> She began singing at a young age at the First United Methodist Church in [[Chula Vista]], California. Day also began taking dance lessons at age 5 (a discipline she continued into her 20s). She attended [[Valencia Park, San Diego|Valencia Park Elementary School]] which she credits with fostering her interest in performing arts. At age 12, Day was introduced to the sounds of jazz vocalists such as [[Billie Holiday]], [[Ella Fitzgerald]], and [[Dinah Washington]], all of whom she counts as early influences on her sound. Day attended the [[San Diego School of Creative and Performing Arts]] in [[Paradise Hills, San Diego|Paradise Hills]], where she graduated in 2003.<ref name="SDUTMain2"/> On July 11, 2019, Day was inducted as an honorary member of [[Delta Sigma Theta]] sorority.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.watchtheyard.com/deltas/delta-sigma-theta-andra-day/|title=Delta Sigma Theta Inducts Grammy Nominated Singer/Songwriter Andra Day as an Honorary Member|website=Watchtheyard.com|date=July 13, 2019|access-date=March 13, 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
After high school, Day worked about 20 different jobs, including one as a children's entertainer.<ref name="NYPOST"/> In 2010, Stevie Wonder's then-wife, Kai Millard, noticed Day performing at a strip mall and brought her to the attention of her husband.<ref name="Newsday">{{cite news | last =Gamboa | first =Glenn | title =Andra Day: Rising star opens up about newfound fame | newspaper =[[Newsday]] | date =March 14, 2016 | url =http://www.newsday.com/entertainment/music/andra-day-rising-star-opens-up-about-newfound-fame-1.11573301 | access-date =July 8, 2016 | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20160819045320/http://www.newsday.com/entertainment/music/andra-day-rising-star-opens-up-about-newfound-fame-1.11573301 | archive-date =August 19, 2016 | url-status =live }}</ref> Day received a call from Wonder himself soon after. No immediate partnership was formed, but the two reconnected around a year later. Wonder introduced Day to producer [[Adrian Gurvitz]] at that time. Gurvitz would eventually collaborate on Day's debut album a few years later.<ref name="NYPOST"/><ref name="Paste"/>

Day was signed in 2011 to a development deal with Buskin Records founded by Jeffrey Evans, who Day asked to be her manager in 2013.  Later Day & Buskin earned a record deal with [[Warner Bros. Records]] in part because of the popularity of her numerous unplugged [[Cover version|covers]] and [[Mashup (music)|mashups]] on her [[YouTube]] channel, most of which were filmed in her sister's bedroom in San Diego.<ref name="SDUTMain2"/><ref name="NYPOST"/><ref name="Paste"/> Her covers include [[Jessie J]]'s "[[Mamma Knows Best]]",<ref name="CBSMamma">{{cite news | last =Goodman | first =William | title =Listen to a stunning cover of "Mamma Knows Best" | work =[[CBS News]] | date =October 17, 2012 | url =https://www.cbsnews.com/news/listen-to-a-stunning-cover-of-mamma-knows-best/ | access-date =July 8, 2016 | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20150926131835/http://www.cbsnews.com/news/listen-to-a-stunning-cover-of-mamma-knows-best/ | archive-date =September 26, 2015 | url-status =live }}</ref> [[Eminem]]'s "[[Lose Yourself]]",<ref name="RS">{{cite magazine | title =10 New Artists You Need to Know: July 2015 - Her first studio album, ''Cheers to the Fall'', was released on August 28, 2015.<ref name="SDUTMain2"/> It was met with critical acclaim with [[NPR]]'s Katie Presley saying Day's voice has "[[Eartha Kitt]]'s unflappable confidence, Amy Winehouse's effortless grasp of classic [[jazz]], Billie Holiday's access to raw emotion and [[Adele]]'s range and pop sensibility".<ref name="NPR">{{cite news | last =Presley | first =Katie | title =First Watch: Andra Day, 'Gold' | publisher =[[NPR]] | date =August 11, 2015 | url =https://www.npr.org/event/music/431273137/first-watch-andra-day-gold | access-date =July 9, 2016 | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20160530090228/http://www.npr.org/event/music/431273137/first-watch-andra-day-gold | archive-date =May 30, 2016 | url-status =live }}</ref> The album was nominated for Best R&B Album and "Rise Up" was nominated for Best R&B Performance at the 58th Grammy Awards in 2016 (an event at which she also performed).<ref name="SDUTMain2"/> The album also peaked at number 48 on the ''Billboard'' 200 list.<ref name="US200">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/andra-day/chart-history/TLP|title=Andra Day Chart History (Billboard 200)|website=Billboard|access-date=February 28, 2021}}</ref> In September 2015, "Rise Up" was used in an advertisement for [[Beats by Dre]] that featured tennis player [[Serena Williams]].<ref name="Quartz"/> In November and December 2015, Day appeared alongside Stevie Wonder in an Apple TV commercial in which the two sang Wonder's song "Someday at Christmas".<ref name="Spokane"/>

Her first national tour took place in August and September 2015 when she opened for [[Lenny Kravitz]]. In October 2015, she sang "Rise Up" at [[The White House]] and again performed the song for [[A&E Networks|A&E]]'s live special, ''[[Shining a Light: A Concert for Progress on Race in America]]''.<ref name="SDUTMain2"/>

In May 2016, Day entered into an agreement with [[McDonald's]] and [[Coca-Cola]] that saw her image on around 50 million cups at McDonald's. The partnership was part of the "Share a Cup" lyrics program and featured lyrics from Day's "Rise Up". Purchasers of the cups at McDonald's could enter a sweepstakes that would have allowed them to see Day perform at the Essence Music Festival in June 2016.<ref name="MSN">{{cite news| last=Landrum| first=Jonathan Jr.| title=Andra Day to appear on 50 million Coke cups at McDonald's| publisher=[[MSN]]| date=April 25, 2016| url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/music/news/andra-day-to-appear-on-50-million-coke-cups-at-mcdonalds/ar-BBseMLz| access-date=July 9, 2016| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160819045548/http://www.msn.com/en-us/music/news/andra-day-to-appear-on-50-million-coke-cups-at-mcdonalds/ar-BBseMLz| archive-date=August 19, 2016| url-status=live}}</ref> Her Cheers to the Fall Tour began in November 2016 in her hometown of San Diego.<ref name="SDUTTour">{{cite news|last=Varga|first=George|date=June 21, 2016|title=Andra Day sets new tour, will begin in San Diego|newspaper=[[San Diego Union-Tribune]]|url=http://www.sandiegouniontribune.com/news/2016/jun/21/andra-day-announces-2016-fall-tour/|url-status=live|access-date=July 8, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160622125846/http://www.sandiegouniontribune.com/news/2016/jun/21/andra-day-announces-2016-fall-tour/|archive-date=June 22, 2016}}</ref>

Day performed on the second day of the [[2016 Democratic National Convention]] in Philadelphia to rousing applause. She followed immediately after a moving address by [[Mothers of the Movement]], a group of black mothers who had lost a child through encounters with police and gun violence.

In December 2016, Andra Day received the Powerhouse Award at the [[Billboard Women in Music]] event. When asked what makes a 'powerhouse' powerful to others, Day responded, "I think you just remind them of their value, you remind them of their purpose. You remind them that you are not here just to exist, you're here to impact people in their lives...I think it just reinforces that."<ref name="Billboard">{{cite news| last=Platon| first=Adelle| title=Andra Day Receives Powerhouse Award & Delivers Soul-Stirring 'Rise Up' Performance at Billboard Women in Music 2016| work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]| date=December 9, 2016| url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/women-in-music/7616879/andra-day-powerhouse-award-billboard-women-in-music| access-date=December 12, 2016| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161211230545/http://www.billboard.com/articles/events/women-in-music/7616879/andra-day-powerhouse-award-billboard-women-in-music| archive-date=December 11, 2016| url-status=live}}</ref>

==Awards and nominations==
{{main|List of awards and nominations received by Andra Day}}

Day has received three [[Grammy Award]] nominations for her R&B Album, ''[[Cheers to the Fall]]'' and her performance for "Rise Up" in 2016, and for [[Grammy Award for Best Song Written for Visual Media|Best Song Written for Visual Media]] for "[[Stand Up for Something]]" from ''[[Marshall (film)|Marshall]]''. In 2018 she received a [[Daytime Emmy Award]] nomination for her live performance of "Rise Up" on ''[[The View (talk show)|The View]]''. For the 2020 film ''[[The United States vs. Billie Holiday]]'', at the [[Golden Globe Award|Golden Globes]], she received a nomination for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]] for "Tigress and Tweed," and won [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Drama|Best Actress in a Motion Picture - Drama]] for her title performance as [[Billie Holiday]]. She also received a nomination for the [[Academy Award for Best Actress]] for the role.
