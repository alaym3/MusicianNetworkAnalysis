'''Isles & Glaciers''' was an American [[supergroup (music)|supergroup]] formed in 2008. They recorded material for their debut EP, ''[[The Hearts of Lonely People]]'' December 3–13, 2008 in Seattle, Washington and again in April 2009.{{citation needed|date=May 2015}} It was produced by Casey Bates,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Casey Bates — KMGMT|url=https://www.kmgmt.com/caseybates|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210311094815/https://www.kmgmt.com/caseybates|archive-date=2021-03-11|access-date=2021-03-11|website=KMGMT|language=en-US}}</ref> and was released on March 9, 2010. Vocalist [[Craig Owens (vocalist)|Craig Owens]] stated that the group will not be collaborating or releasing new material again. However [[Vic Fuentes]] stated that the band may release more material in the future.<ref name="Vic Interview">{{cite web|last=Tsai|first=Matthew|title=Isles & Glaciers Not Completely Melted - News Article|url=http://absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2103092|publisher=[[AbsolutePunk]]|access-date=5 February 2011|date=4 January 2011}}</ref>

==History==
Isles & Glaciers was first rumored to exist after [[Craig Owens (musician)|Craig Owens]] ([[Chiodos]], [[Destroy Rebuild Until God Shows]]) and [[Jonny Craig]] ([[Dance Gavin Dance]], [[Emarosa]]) performed a show in Lexington, Kentucky on August 30, 2008.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R_VT1Ge0acc Youtube.com] August 30, 2008.</ref> Owens confirmed the rumors on September 4, 2008.<ref>[http://www.iamcraigowens.com/category/journal/ Iamcraigowens.com] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081015123219/http://www.iamcraigowens.com/category/journal/ |date=2008-10-15 }} September 4, 2008</ref> Alternative Press announced the project officially and released the band's final lineup on December 15, 2008.{{citation needed|date=May 2015}}

On January 18, 2009, both Owens and Brian Southall premiered three rough demos, "Clush" and "Hills Like White Elephants" and "Empty Sighs and Wine", for an upcoming EP, and took part in an interview with [[89X]] Radio in Windsor, Canada.<ref>[http://www.89xradio.com/homeboy.htm 89xradio.com] January 18, 2009</ref>

Isles & Glaciers played their first and only scheduled live show on March 21, 2009, which was presented by [[Alternative Press (music magazine)|Alternative Press]] at Emo's Bar in [[Austin, Texas]] for a free show during [[SXSW]].<ref>[http://www.altpress.com/news/6073.htm Altpress.com] February 23, 2009</ref><ref>[http://vimeo.com/3818923 Vimeo.com-Altpress] March 23, 2009</ref><ref>[http://altpress.com/features/islesandglaciersvid.htm AP: FEATURES - Isles & Glaciers] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090328232738/http://www.altpress.com/features/islesandglaciersvid.htm |date=2009-03-28 }} March 23, 2009</ref> In an interview done by DJ Rossstar on [[Stickam]], Craig Owens stated that members in the band want to perform future shows in random locations, such as Alaska.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.stickam.com/viewMedia.do?mId=183325128 |title=Video by DJ Rossstar |publisher=Stickam.com |date=2011-09-30 |access-date=2011-10-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110719024453/http://www.stickam.com/viewMedia.do?mId=183325128 |archive-date=2011-07-19 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Their debut EP, ''[[The Hearts of Lonely People]]'', was released on March 9, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=1551362 |title=Isles & Glaciers EP Release Date - News Article |publisher=AbsolutePunk.net |date=2010-03-09 |access-date=2011-10-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.equalvision.com/news/show/515 |title=Happy Holidays from Equal Vision Records! : Equal Vision Records |publisher=Equalvision.com |access-date=2011-10-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927184300/http://www.equalvision.com/news/show/515 |archive-date=2011-09-27 |url-status=dead }}</ref> A remix was released on November 11, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/isles_glaciers_stream_the_hearts_of_lonely_people_remixes|title=Listen to Isles & Glaciers' 'The Hearts Of Lonely People (Remixes)' - Alternative Press|work=Alternative Press|access-date=12 May 2015}}</ref>
