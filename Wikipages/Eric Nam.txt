'''Eric Nam''' ({{Korean|hangul=에릭남}}; born November 17, 1988) is an American singer, songwriter, television host and content creator of Korean descent. He was named "2016 Man Of The Year" by ''GQ Korea,''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sbs.com.au/popasia/blog/2016/11/25/eric-nam-named-one-gq-koreas-men-year|title=Eric Nam named one of GQ Korea's 'Men of the Year'|website=SBS PopAsia|language=en|access-date=2019-02-17}}</ref> honored "30 Under 30 Asia 2017" by ''[[Forbes]],''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://kpopherald.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=201704141434011293170_2|title=Eric Nam features in Forbes '30 Under 30 Asia' list|website=kpopherald.koreaherald.com|access-date=2019-02-17}}</ref> and YouTube Music's Global Trending "Artist on the Rise."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/ericnamofficial/status/1210257364261261312?lang=en|title=I am trending right now as YouTube Music's "Artist On The Rise"! This is crazy...see y'all on tour. #CONGRATULATIONSpic.twitter.com/zpQnfJyVnK|last=Nam)|first=에릭남 (Eric|date=2019-12-26|website=@ericnamofficial|language=en|access-date=2020-03-23}}</ref>

==Early life==

Eric Nam was born and raised in [[Atlanta]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], United States. He has two younger brothers, Eddie and Brian Nam, both of whom are soccer players. Eddie played varsity soccer for [[Wofford College]] and [[Northeastern University]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Eddie Nam - Men's Soccer|url=https://nuhuskies.com/sports/mens-soccer/roster/eddie-nam/1990|url-status=live|access-date=August 14, 2020|website=Northeastern University Athletics|language=en}}</ref> whereas Brian played soccer for [[Columbia University]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Brian Nam - Men's Soccer|url=https://gocolumbialions.com/sports/mens-soccer/roster/brian-nam/7663|url-status=live|access-date=August 14, 2020|website=Columbia University Athletics|language=en}}</ref> Nam graduated from [[The Lovett School]] in 2007<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/250954/20151024/eric-nam-finding-genius.htm|title=Korean Singer Eric Nam Discusses His Struggles With Racism In The United States|date=24 October 2015|website=KpopStarz}}</ref> and from [[Boston College]] in 2011 with a major in [[International Studies]], a minor in [[Asian Studies]],<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=January 14, 2017|title=Evening with Eric Nam|url=https://www.bc.edu/bc-web/bcnews/campus-community/alumni/k-pop-star-eric-nam-at-the-heights.html|url-status=live|access-date=June 19, 2021|website=Boston College}}</ref> and spent a year abroad at [[Peking University]] in [[Beijing]], China. {{Citation needed|date=March 2019}} He had accepted a position as a [[business analyst]] at [[Deloitte Consulting]] in [[New York City]], before deferring to participate in microfinance initiatives in India. After this, he decided to pursue a career in entertainment.<ref name=":0" /> Nam is fluent in English, Korean, and proficient in Spanish.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}}

==Career==
===2011–2012: Pre-Debut===
Following a viral [[YouTube]] cover, [[Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation|MBC]] invited Nam to [[Seoul]] to compete on the popular program, "Star Audition:Birth of a Great Star 2", a show similar to [[X Factor]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Aran|first=Isha|date=June 30, 2016|title=Eric Nam could become the first K-pop artist to make it big in America|url=https://splinternews.com/eric-nam-could-become-the-first-k-pop-artist-to-make-it-1793857947|url-status=live|access-date=June 19, 2021|website=Splinter}}</ref> After eight months, he placed within the top five contestants and jump-started his career in [[South Korea]].

On September 25, 2012, Nam signed under [[B2M Entertainment]] as a solo artist.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ho|first=Stewart|date=September 25, 2012|title='The Great Birth 2's Eric Nam Joins Lee Hyori's B2M Entertainment|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/15875/the-great-birth-2s-eric-nam-joins-lee-hyoris-b2m-entertainment|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150620053803/http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/15875/the-great-birth-2s-eric-nam-joins-lee-hyoris-b2m-entertainment|archive-date=June 20, 2015|access-date=February 24, 2015|publisher=CJ E&M enewsWorld}}</ref>

===2013–2015: Debut with ''Cloud 9'' and hosting===
On January 23, 2013, Nam made his debut with the release of his first EP ''Cloud 9'' with lead single "Heaven's Door".{{citation needed|date=January 2019}}

From 2013 to 2016, Nam was the [[MC]] of Arirang TV's ''[[After School Club]]'' and its spin-off, ''The ASC After Show'', an interactive show for K-pop artists and their fans, broadcast in 188 countries. The live shows regularly trend worldwide number one on [[Twitter]]. He left to focus on his music career.

Nam has created his niche as the "go-to" personality for international celebrity interviews in South Korea through his role on [[Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation|MBC]]'s Section TV – Entertainment Relay. Nam's interviews, both international and Korean, have garnered rave reviews due to his wit and ability to connect with interviewees on a personal level; they have also become viral hits, regularly taking over Korea's real-time issue charts. He thus became a way for the American and Korean media to interact with one another and their respective actors and actresses. {{citation needed|date=April 2018}}

In April 2014, Nam released his first digital single, "우우 (Ooh Ooh)", and he sold out stage at the Seoul Jazz Festival alongside names such as [[Chris Botti]], [[Damien Rice]], [[Jamie Cullum]], [[Paolo Nutini]], and [[Craig David]]. In December, he released his second digital single "Melt My Heart".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Jung|first=Hee-seo|date=December 11, 2014|title=에릭남 신곡 '녹여줘', 절친★ 응원 봇물 '훈훈'|url=https://www.xportsnews.com/?ac=article_view&entry_id=531415|url-status=live|access-date=June 19, 2021|website=Xports News|language=ko}}</ref>

In February 2015, Nam was featured on [[Amber Liu (singer)|Amber Liu]]'s EP ''Beautiful'' on the song "I Just Wanna". In March, he released his single "I'm OK" and its accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web|last=Danbi Hong|first=Grace|date=March 5, 2015|title=[Video] Eric Nam Insists He′s Alright in ′I′m OK′ MV|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/88554/video-eric-nam-insists-hes-alright-in-im-ok-mv|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402122529/http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/88554/video-eric-nam-insists-hes-alright-in-im-ok-mv|archive-date=April 2, 2015|access-date=March 6, 2015|work=CJ E&M enewsWorld}}</ref> In May, Nam released his single, "Dream", featuring [[15&]]'s [[Park Ji-min (singer, born 1997)|Park Ji-min]] for Charity Project.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kim|first=Ye-na|date=May 19, 2015|title='작곡가 그룹' 스윗튠, 기부 프로젝트 시작…에릭남 with 박지민|url=https://entertain.naver.com/now/read?oid=213&aid=0000690288|url-status=live|access-date=May 14, 2021|website=TV Report|language=ko}}</ref>

In December 2015, Nam signed an exclusive contract with [[CJ E&M Music Performance Division|CJ E&M]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Jung|first=Ji-won|date=December 23, 2015|title=[단독]'대세' 에릭남, CJ E&M과 전속계약 체결|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=311&aid=0000557852|url-status=live|access-date=April 29, 2021|website=Xports News|language=ko}}</ref>

===2016–2017: ''Interview'' and US release===
Nam released his second EP, ''Interview'' with the lead single "Good For You" on March 24, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2016/03/eric-nam-is-good-for-you-in-his-long-awaited-comeback-mv|title=Eric Nam is 'Good For You' in his long-awaited comeback MV!|date=2016-03-23|website=Allkpop.com|access-date=2016-05-19}}</ref> That same month, he also took part in [[SM Entertainment]]'s digital music channel [[SM Station]] by releasing "[[Spring Love (Eric Nam and Wendy song)|Spring Love]]", a duet track with [[Red Velvet (group)|Red Velvet]]'s [[Wendy (singer)|Wendy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soompi.com/2016/03/02/red-velvets-wendy-and-eric-nam-featured-in-next-sm-station-track/|title=Red Velvet's Wendy and Eric Nam Featured in Next SM STATION Track|date=2016-03-02|publisher=Soompi|access-date=2016-05-19}}</ref>

On April 16, 2016, Nam hosted and performed at ''[[Saturday Night Live Korea|SNL Korea]]'', receiving one of the highest ratings of the season.{{citation needed|date=April 2018}} In late April, Nam joined the virtual marriage show ''[[We Got Married]]'' with [[Solar (singer)|Solar]] from [[Mamamoo]] as his partner.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Jang|first=Ah-reum|date=April 2, 2016|title='우결4' 측 "에릭남♥솔라, 오늘(2일) 첫 촬영 중"(공식입장)|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=421&aid=0001977666|url-status=live|access-date=April 29, 2019|website=News 1|language=ko}}</ref>

On June 10, 2016, Nam released his first U.S. single, "Into You", in collaboration with the electronic band KOLAJ. "Into You" premiered worldwide on Beats 1 Radio and The Fader, subsequently leading to a number one charting on [[Hype Machine]]. Later that month, Nam headlined [[KCON (music festival)|KCON]] NY 2016 and performed five of his songs, including "Into You", to a sold-out [[Prudential Center]]. He then followed up on the success of "Into You" by releasing his digital single "Can't Help Myself" in July 2016, with the lyrics written by [[Epik High]]'s [[Tablo]] and featuring [[AOMG]] rapper Loco. Nam also headlined [[KCON (music festival)|KCON]] LA 2016 and performed "Can't Help Myself" and "Into You" with KOLAJ to a sold-out [[Staples Center]].

Nam became the [[television host|host]] of the new [[Mnet (TV channel)|Mnet]]'s talk show ''Yang and Nam Show'' with comedian [[Yang Se-hyung]] in November 2016. Additionally, he premiered his collaboration with Grammy award-winning producer [[Timbaland]] titled "Body" at the [[2016 Mnet Asian Music Awards]].

In 2017, Nam collaborated with [[Gallant (singer)|Gallant]] and Tablo on the single release of "Cave Me In". "Cave Me In" premiered worldwide on Zayn Lowe's Beats 1 Radio Show and was trending globally on YouTube and Facebook for over 72 hours upon release. The music video was shot in [[Hong Kong]].<ref name="cave">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/7669837/tablo-eric-nam-gallant-collaboration-cave-me-in-interview|title=Tablo & Eric Nam Discuss Gallant Collab "Cave Me In"|last=Herman|first=Tamar|date=January 27, 2017|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=February 4, 2017}}</ref> He also featured on [[Seohyun]]'s WP ''[[Don't Say No (Seohyun EP)|Don't Say No]]'' on the song "Hello", and collaborated with [[Jeon So-mi]] for the release of the single "You, Who?". Furthermore, Nam featured on DJ / dance producer [[Arty (musician)|Arty's]] single, "Idea of You".

Nam performed three sold-out shows in [[New York City]] and [[Atlanta]] in August 2017, featuring [[Alec Benjamin]] as his opener. These shows sold out in one minute during the presale. Due to overwhelming demand, Nam added the date in Atlanta and increased the venue size in New York City.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/eric-nam-plays-a-sold-out-show-at-irving-plaza-121890|title=Eric Nam Plays a Sold Out show at Irving Plaza|website=AXS|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref>

===2018–2019: ''Honestly'' and North American Tour===
At the [[2018 Winter Olympics]] in South Korea, Nam was named as an Honorary Ambassador.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lee|first=Ji-hyun|date=January 26, 2018|title=에릭남 "2018년 지겨울 정도로 많이 활동하려고요" [화보]|url=https://sports.chosun.com/news/ntype.htm?id=201801270100207750014755&servicedate=20180126|url-status=live|access-date=April 29, 2021|website=Sports Chosun|language=ko}}</ref> Additionally, Nam was featured on American broadcasting network [[NBC]] primetime, in which he provided an in-depth look at K-pop with interviewers [[Johnny Weir]] and [[Tara Lipinski]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allkpop.com/article/2018/02/eric-nam-teaches-johnny-weir-tara-lipinski-about-k-pop-at-the-pyeongchang-olympics-on-nbc|title=Eric Nam teaches Johnny Weir & Tara Lipinski about K-Pop at the 'Pyeongchang Olympics' on NBC!|website=Allkpop.com|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref>

On April 11, 2018, Nam released his third EP ''Honestly''. Fronted by singles "Honestly" and "Potion", the latter of which features rapper Woodie GoChild and was released ahead of the album on April 9. ''Honestly'' plays up the artist's synth and electro-pop leanings while bringing in some dancehall and reggaeton influences. Nam worked with numerous songwriters on the album, including [[Brian Lee (songwriter)|Brian Lee]], Tablo, Jake Torrey, and more.<ref>{{cite web|last=Herman|first=Tamar|title=Eric Nam Releases Romantic Electro-Pop 'Honestly' EP|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8305493/eric-nam-electro-pop-honestly-ep|date=April 11, 2018|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=April 11, 2018}}</ref> Additionally, Nam stated that the members of [[BTS (band)|BTS]] helped provide him with numerous feedback as he was creating the album.<ref>{{Cite web|first=|date=April 11, 2018|title=에릭남 "방탄소년단 슈가, 신곡 듣고 '짱'이라고 감탄해줘 확신 생겼다"|url=http://stoo.asiae.co.kr/article.php?aid=45340440329|url-status=live|access-date=April 29, 2021|website=yhnews|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Lee|first=So-dam|date=April 11, 2018|title='컴백' 에릭남 "방탄소년단 등 아이돌 20명에 조언 얻어" [종합②]|url=https://entertain.naver.com/now/read?oid=109&aid=0003758276|url-status=live|access-date=April 29, 2021|website=entertain.naver.com|publisher=Osen|language=ko}}</ref> In the first week, the album debuted at number six on the Billboard World Album charts.<ref>{{cite web|title=World Albums Chart EP|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/world-albums|date=April 29, 2018|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=April 29, 2018}}</ref>

From June 5 to June 29, Nam embarked on his 2018 North American ''Honestly'' Tour, which encompassed 15 shows in 14 cities. The tour featured special guests [[Loote]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8290401/kpop-eric-nam-announces-honestly-north-american-tour|title=K-Pop Star Eric Nam Announces 'Honestly' North American Tour|last=Herman|first=Tamar|date=5 April 2018|website=Billboard}}</ref>

Additionally, Nam released the song "Float", which he co-wrote with the producer [[Kool Kojak]]. The song was picked up by [[Sony Pictures]] and featured in the official soundtrack for the movie ''[[Hotel Transylvania 3: Summer Vacation]]''. Upon release, the movie was number one at the US box office.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8463215/eric-nam-float|title=Eric Nam Wants to 'FLOAT' in New Song for 'Hotel Transylvania 3' Soundtrack: Premiere|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-08-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://deadline.com/2018/07/dwayne-johnson-skyscraper-ant-man-and-the-wasp-hotel-transylvania-3-weekend-box-office-1202426089/|title=Why Dwayne Johnson Pic 'Skyscraper' Went Up In Flames At The B.O. With $25M+|last=D'Alessandro|first=Anthony|date=2018-07-15|work=Deadline|access-date=2018-08-03|language=en-US}}</ref>

Nam also featured on a remix for "Your Side of the Bed" with [[Island Records]] pop duo [[Loote]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8462300/loote-eric-nam-your-side-of-the-bed-remix|title=Loote & Eric Nam Team Up For Epic 'Your Side of the Bed' Remix: Listen|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-08-03}}</ref>

On July 21, 2018, Nam performed at [[We the Fest]] in Jakarta, Indonesia. He was the only Korean act on the lineup.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hellokpop.com/kpop/eric-nam-to-fire-up-indonesia-with-his-performance-on-we-the-fest-2018/|title=Eric Nam To Fire Up Indonesia With His Performance On "We The Fest 2018!"|date=2018-06-14|work=hellokpop|access-date=2018-08-03|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== 2019–2020: ''Before We Begin'', World Tour, and ''The Other Side'' ===
On May 8, 2019, Nam released the digital single, “Runaway.”<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lee|first=Soodam|date=May 8, 2019|title="둘만의 밤으로 '런어웨이'"…에릭남, 국민남친의 달콤한 일탈 [퇴근길 신곡]|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=109&aid=0004005063|url-status=live|access-date=May 19, 2021|website=OSEN|language=ko}}</ref>

On April 17, 2019, he debuted his podcast show titled ''K-Pop Daebak w/ Eric Nam''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Tan|first=Pakkee|date=April 23, 2019|title=Eric Nam Launches New Podcast; Talks Upcoming BTS Collaboration|url=https://www.eonline.com/ap/news/1034817/eric-nam-launches-new-podcast-talks-k-pop-releases-and-stars|url-status=live|access-date=June 19, 2021|website=E! Online}}</ref> With episodes releasing every Monday, it quickly gained a sizable audience. ''K-Pop Daebak'' was named “New & Noteworthy” and “Best Listens of 2019” by Apple Podcasts, as well as “Top 10 Music Podcasts” by Spotify in 2019.

On November 14, 2019, Nam released his first English album ''Before We Begin''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Herman|first=Tamar|date=November 14, 2019|title='Before We Begin' EP Is Just a Taste of What Eric Nam Has to Offer U.S. Music Industry: 'We Still Have So Much to Do'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8543683/eric-nam-interview-before-we-begin-ep|url-status=live|access-date=June 19, 2021|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

On December 10, 2019, Nam released his second podcast, ''I Think You're Dope w/ Eric Nam''. The show reached number one on the Music Podcast Charts in over 25 countries including the US, U.K., and South Korea.

On January 9, 2020, Nam began his first official World Tour spanning 34 cities across Asia, North America, and Latin America. In March 2021, Nam announced the cancellation of the Latin American leg of his World Tour due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. The Los Angeles dates of the North American leg of the tour were postponed to September 10 and 12, 2020, before those shows were cancelled as well.<ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1238533446776934400|user=ericnamofficial|title=Los Angeles - Postponement Update|author=Eric Nam|access-date=June 19, 2021}}</ref>

On July 30, 2020, Nam released his fourth EP, ''The Other Side''. Nam said of the album in an interview "I think this one is a little more playful, a little more upbeat, a little brighter." The tracks "The Other Side" and the lead single "Paradise" were hits in South Korea and other countries around Asia. [[Day6]] vocalist [[Young K]] co-wrote four of the songs with Nam.<ref>{{Cite web|date=July 10, 2020|title=에릭남, 30일 컴백 확정…여름 휴가송|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=052&aid=0001463155|url-status=live|access-date=June 19, 2021|website=YTN|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=K-Pop Phenom Eric Nam Talks New Mini-Album 'The Other Side' And Life As One Of Korea's Biggest Stars|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/k-pop-phenom-eric-nam-talks-new-mini-album-other-side-and-life-one-koreas-biggest-stars|website=Recording Academy|publisher=Recording Academy|access-date=14 August 2020}}</ref> 

=== 2021–present: ''There and Back Again'' ===
On May 21, 2021, Nam released the single "Echo" in collaboration with Indian singer and composer [[Armaan Malik]] and Indian-American composer [[Kshmr]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pawa|first=Anjana|date=May 21, 2021|title=Inside Armaan Malik, Eric Nam, and KSHMR's Imaginative "Echo"|url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/inside-armaan-malik-eric-nam-kshmr-global-collab-echo|url-status=live|access-date=June 19, 2021|website=Teen Vogue|language=en-US}}</ref> On June 4, it was announced that Eric Nam's contract with CJ ENM had expired.<ref>{{Cite web|date=June 4, 2021|title=[단독] 'FA' 에릭남, 스톤뮤직과 지난해 계약만료…뒤늦게 알려져|url=http://m.joynews24.com/v/1373289|url-status=live|access-date=June 4, 2021|website=Joy News|language=ko}}</ref>

On October 15, 2021, Nam released the new digital single, "I Don't Know You Anymore," which marked his first independent release.<ref>{{Cite web|title="I Don’t Know You Anymore" release|url=https://twitter.com/ericnamofficial/status/1448870863483260930|access-date=2021-10-14|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>

On October 19, 2021, Nam announced his There and Back Again World Tour 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=There and Back Again World Tour 2022|url=https://twitter.com/ericnamofficial/status/1450130616415105041|access-date=2021-10-26|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>

On November 12, 2021, Nam released another digital single, "Any Other Way".<ref>{{Cite web|title="Any Other Way" release|url=https://twitter.com/ericnamofficial/status/1456431035042856965|access-date=2021-11-04|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>

On January 7, 2022, Nam will release his second English album ''There and Back Again'', which includes the singles "I Don't Know You Anymore" and "Any Other Way".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/news/music/eric-nam-sophomore-album-there-and-back-again-new-single-any-other-way-3093782?utm_source=hootsuite&utm_medium=&utm_term=&utm_content=&utm_campaign=|title=Eric Nam announces sophomore album with new single ‘Any Other Way’|date=November 12, 2021|access-date=November 12, 2021|website=NME|language=en}}</ref>
