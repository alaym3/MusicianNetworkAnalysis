'''The Zambonis''' are a [[Connecticut]]-based [[indie rock]] band formed in 1991 by musicians [[Dave Schneider (musician)|Dave Schneider]] ([[The LeeVees]]), [[Peter Katis]], Jon Aley, and Tarquin Katis. The Zambonis write songs exclusively about [[ice hockey]].  Schneider says of the band's musical style: “We’re the only band in the world whose two biggest influences are The Beatles and Wayne Gretzky!”<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/si_blogs/scorecard/daily_list/2007/04/top-sports-sounding-rock-bands.html |title=Daily List: Top Sports-Sounding Band Names |last=McAllister |first=Mike |website=[[Sports Illustrated]] |date=25 April 2007 |accessdate=2013-11-01}}</ref>

The NHL has commissioned the group to compose theme songs for the [[Boston Bruins]] and the [[Colorado Avalanche]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2002/04/14/nyregion/for-love-of-the-game-a-hockey-band-yes-a-hockey-band.html?pagewanted=all |title=For Love of the Game, a Hockey Band (Yes, a Hockey Band) |last=Cruice |first=Valerie |website=[[The New York Times]] |date=14 April 2002 |accessdate=2013-11-01}}</ref> The band has toured with the [[Stanley Cup]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hhof.com/htmlSTCjournal/exSCJ06_15.shtml |title=Stanley Cup Journal 2006, July 24th: Aaron Ward, Matt Cullen |last=Shea |first=Kevin |publisher=[[Hockey Hall of Fame]] |accessdate=2014-10-24 |quote=Like last year, Cullen hired The Zambonis to perform. The rock band from Connecticut performs all hockey-themed songs, including their most recognized track, 'I Wanna Drive the Zamboni.'}}</ref> and fundraised for [[Matt Cullen]]'s organization, Cullen Children's Foundation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cullyskids.com/our-sponsors-a-list.php |title=Our Sponsors |website=Cullen Children's Foundation |accessdate=2014-10-23}}</ref>

In deference to the [[ice resurfacer]] and [[Frank Zamboni]], The Zambonis are a licensee of the [[Zamboni Company]].

==History==
The Zambonis debut album, ''100% Hockey...and Other Stuff'',<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1997-03-05/sports/sp-34833_1_true-hockey-fan |title=The Hot Corner |website=[[Los Angeles Times]] |last=Najarian |first=Ara |date=5 March 1997 |accessdate=2013-11-01}}</ref> was recorded at Tarquin Studios in Connecticut, USA and was released in 1996. The 15-song disc cracked the Top 40 on the [[CMJ]] charts,<ref>{{cite journal|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2ywEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PT16 |title=June Top 75 |work=[[CMJ|CMJ New Music Monthly]] |date=June 1996 |number=34 | page=16 | accessdate=2013-11-01}}</ref> and was added to the [[NHL]], [[NCAA]] and minor league hockey arena playlists.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nhldigest.com/best-hockey-songs/ |title=Top 10 Hockey Songs |website=NHL Digest |date=1 September 2009 |accessdate=2013-11-01}}</ref>

In October 1999, the band released its second full-length album, ''More Songs About Hockey...and Buildings and Food''.<ref>{{Allmusic|class=album|id=r540871|label=More Songs About Hockey...and Buildings and Food}}</ref>

In 2002, the Zambonis opened the [[52nd National Hockey League All-Star Game|NHL All-Star Game]] along with [[Jewel (singer)|Jewel]] and [[Five for Fighting]].

In 2003, The Zambonis released a new album, ''Chippy Sessions'', a compilation of rare live cuts, singles and demos.

==In popular media==
In 2000, the Zambonis single "Hockey Monkey" was featured in the [[Mr. Wong (web series)|Mr. Wong]] episode "Meet the Creep, Pt. 2".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.icebox.com/archive/index.php?id=episode&show_id=s10&episode_id=e6 |title=Episode 6:  Meet The Creep, Part II |website=[[icebox.com|Icebox]] |accessdate=2014-10-23}}</ref>

In 2002, four Zambonis songs were featured on the Midway video game [[NHL Hitz 20-03]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.psxextreme.com/ps2-reviews/185.html |title=NHL Hitz 2003 Review |last=Stensrud |first=Matthew |website=PSX Extreme |date=14 October 2002 |accessdate=2013-11-01}}</ref>

In March 2006, The Zambonis single "Hockey Monkey" (co-written by cartoonist [[James Kochalka]]) was chosen by [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] as the theme song to a new sitcom called ''[[The Loop (U.S. TV series)|The Loop]]''.{{Citation needed|date=May 2010}}
