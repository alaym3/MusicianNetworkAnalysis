'''Osinachi Kalu Okoro Egbu''',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/wireStory/dove-awards-king-country-top-artist-73936832|title = Dove Awards name for KING & COUNTRY top artist}}</ref> known professionally as '''Sinach''', is a Nigerian singer, songwriter and senior worship leader at [[Christ Embassy|Loveworld]].<ref name=DailyMedia>{{cite web | url=http://dailymedia.com.ng/biographyprofilehistory-singer-osinachi-kalu-popularly-sinach/ | work=Daily Media| publisher=DailyMedia Nigeria | date=26 May 2016 |title=Who is Sinach | accessdate=26 September 2017 }}</ref><ref name="9999carolsingers bio">{{cite web | url=http://9999carolsingers.com/index.php/sinach | title=Sinach | publisher=9999CarolSingers | accessdate=3 June 2014}}</ref> She Songwriter For Twelve Weeks In A Row|url=https://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/7783-sinach-named-top-christian-songwriter-for-twelve-weeks-in-a-row|access-date=16 December 2020|website=TheChristianBeat.org|date=5 June 2020|language=en-gb}}</ref> Her song "[[Way Maker]]", bagged three nominations and won the Song of the Year at the [[51st GMA Dove Awards]], making her the first [[Nigerians|Nigerian]] to win the Award.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 November 2020|title=2020 Dove Awards: Sinach's Way Maker emerges song of the She has released 9 studio albums with several other hit songs, including "I Know Who I Am", "Great Are You Lord", "Rejoice", "He Did It Again", "Precious Jesus", "The Name of Jesus", "This Is My Season", "Awesome God", "For This", "I Stand Amazed", "Simply Devoted" and "Jesus is Alive".<ref name="infogospelsinger">{{cite web|url=http://infogospelsinger.blogspot.com/|title=Biography of Top Nigerian Gospel Singers|accessdate=3 June 2014}}</ref><ref name="bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.sinach.org/profile|title=Biography|author=Sinach|publisher=sinach.org|accessdate=26 September 2017}}</ref>

"Way Maker" has also piled up many recognition and awards since it was released in 2015. Smith]], [[Darlene Zschech]], [[Leeland (band)|Leeland]], [[Bethel Music]], and [[Mandisa]] and in many languages.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.modernghana.com/nollywood/35690/sinach-set-to-host-1st-edition-of-annual-mega-conc.html|title=Sinach Set To Host 1st Edition Of Annual Mega Concert|website=Modern Ghana}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://parade.com/738213/laurawhitmore/exclusive-premiere-michael-w-smith-shares-waymaker-and-announces-new-album-awaken-the-surrounded-experience/|title=Exclusive Premiere: Michael W. Sinach received a Bethlehem Hall of Faith certificate of commemoration during her visit to Israel in December 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|date=11 December 2017|title=Sinach honoured with "Bethelehem Hall Of Faith" Certificate of Commemoration|url=http://www.sinachmusic.com/news/sinach-honoured-bethelehem-hall-faith-certificate-commemoration/|access-date=24 October 2019|website=SINACH|language=en-US}}</ref> In September 2019, Sinach became the first gospel artist from Africa to tour India, headlining concerts with several thousands in attendance.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://punchng.com/sinach-takes-india/|title=Sinach takes India|website=Punch Newspapers|date=6 October 2019|language=en-US|access-date=8 October 2019}}</ref> In May 2020, she became the first African artist to top the ''[[Billboard charts|Billboard]]'' Christian Songwriters chart.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Nebianet Usaini |title=Sinach Becomes First African To Top Billboard 'Christian Songwriters' Chart |url=https://www.channelstv.com/2020/05/06/sinach-becomes-first-african-to-top-billboard-christian-songwriters-chart/ |website=Channels TV |accessdate=7 May 2020 |date=6 May 2020}}</ref>

== Biography ==
=== Early life ===
Sinach hails from [[Ebonyi State]], [[Eastern Region, Nigeria|Eastern Nigeria]], and is the second daughter of seven children.<ref>Claudia E. Dianou, [https://archives.beninwebtv.com/2019/03/voici-tout-ce-quil-faut-connaitre-de-la-figure-de-proue-du-gospel-africain-sinach/ Voici tout ce qu'il faut connaitre de la figure de proue du Gospel africain Sinach], beninwebtv.com, Benin, 16 March 2019</ref>

Sinach started singing for family and friends in 1989 as a hobby, while working as a staff and choir member in [[Christ Embassy]], Pastor Chris Oyakhilome's Church.<ref>Bonface Nyaga, [https://nairobinews.nation.co.ke/chillax/i-know-who-i-am-sinach 'I know who I am' – Sinach], nairobinews.nation.co.ke, Kenya, 4 December 2016</ref> She studied physics and graduated from the University of Port Harcourt, Rivers State, Nigeria.<ref>Naledi Sande, [https://www.herald.co.zw/nigerian-singer-sinach-to-perform-in-harare/ Nigerian singer Sinach to perform in Harare], herald.co.zw, Zimbabwe, 7 September 2016</ref> She considered relocating from Nigeria after her University education, but was advised against it by Pastor Chris Oyakhilome.<ref name="Punch Newspapers">{{cite web|url=https://punchng.com/i-dont-mind-my-gospel-music-being-played-in-nightclubs-sinach/|title=I don't mind my gospel music being played in nightclubs – Sinach|publisher=Punch Newspapers|author=Olusola Ricketts|date=12 August 2018|accessdate=22 September 2018}}</ref>

=== Personal life ===
In 2014, Sinach married Joseph Egbu in her home Church Christ Embassy. On 17 November 2019, at the LIMA Awards Pastor [[Chris Oyakhilome]], announced the arrival of Sinach's first child.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.google.com/search?q=sinach+baby|title=Sinach welcomes first child five years after|last1=Praise|first1=Olowe|date=17 November 2019|publisher=The nation|last2=Sunday|first2=Akpan}}</ref>

== Musical career ==
As a child, Sinach stated she had dreams, where she saw herself singing to large crowds, but didn't actively pursue anything musically as a career besides joining the choir, and also working as an administrative staff in the Church. Sinach had written many songs before she released her first album, ''Chapter One'' in 2008.<ref>Isaac, [https://www.pulse.ng/gist/good-news-gospel-singer-sinach-set-for-the-altar/wwrttdv Gospel Singer Sinach Set For The Altar], pulse.ng, Nigeria, 13 June 2014</ref> Her song 'This Is Your Season' won the Song of the Year award in 2008.

Speaking on how she came by her stage name ''Sinach'', she said: "I chose that from my name Osinachi because it is easy to pronounce and catchy".<ref name="african voice">{{cite web | url=https://africanvoiceonline.co.uk/sinach-set-rock-london/ | title=Sinach set to rock London | work=African Voice | location=UK | date=5 January 2018 | access-date=23 November 2020 }}</ref>

In 2016, Sinach was the first recipient of the LIMA Songwriter of the Decade Award, recognizing her contribution to gospel music in the previous decade. Her songs were being sung in many countries,
<ref name="KonnectAfrica">{{cite web|url=http://www.konnectafrica.net/osinachi-kalu-sinach/|title=Osinachi Kalu "Sinach", An Extraordinary Nigerian Star.|author=daman Joshua Olayinka Bunmi|date=11 July 2014|publisher=konnectafrica.net|accessdate=26 September 2017}}</ref> translated to many languages, around the world.<ref name="LoveworldMusicTV">{{cite web|url=http://www.loveworldmusictv.com/gospel-artiste-sinach-honored-lima-awards-2016-songwriter-decade/|title=Gospel Artiste Sinach honored with Song Writer of the Decade|author=Loveworld News|date=22 November 2016|publisher=Loveworld Music TV|accessdate=26 September 2017}}</ref> That same year, she received the African Achievers' Award for Global Excellence.<ref name="BuzzNigeria">{{cite web|url=https://buzznigeria.com/winners-2016-african-achievers-awards/|title=Winners 2016 African Achievers Awards|author=Iheoma Hendy|year=2016|publisher=Buzz Nigeria|accessdate=26 September 2017}}</ref> Also that same year, for the second time in a row,<ref name="GrooveAwards">{{cite web|url=http://www.grooveawards.co.ke/groove-2015-winners/|title=Groove 2015 Winners|author=Groove Awards|date=12 December 2015|publisher=Groove Awards|accessdate=26 September 2017}}</ref> she won the Western Africa Artist of the Year by [[Groove Awards]] in Kenya, and was listed by YNaija alongside [[Chris Oyakhilome]], [[Enoch Adeboye]] as one of the Top 100 Influential Christians in Nigeria.<ref name="Ynaija">{{cite web|url=https://ynaija.com/yynaija-com-church-culture-present-100-influential-people-christian-ministry-nigeria/|title=Yemi Osinbajo, Poju Oyemade, Chioma Jesus and more – See list of 100 Most Influential People in Christian Ministry in Nigeria|author=Tolu|date=1 May 2017|publisher=Ynaija|accessdate=20 July 2017}}</ref>

As a songwriter, Sinach has written over 200 songs and won several awards. Her song 'This Is Your Season' won the Song of the Year award in 2008.  One of her most popular songs is 'I Know Who I Am'.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dailymedia.com.ng/biography-of-gospel-award-winning-songwriter-vocalist-singer-sinach/|title=Biography Of Gospel Award Winning Songwriter, Vocalist & Singer Sinach – Daily Mail Nigeria|website=dailymedia.com.ng|accessdate=12 August 2017}}</ref>

=== Performance ===
Sinach has performed and headlined concerts in over 50 countries<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/6hKHFC67DZJNw9tg1l0lIe|title=Sinach|website=Spotify|language=en|access-date=23 November 2019}}</ref> including Kenya, [[Dominica]], South Africa, United States, Canada, [[Antigua & Barbuda]], [[Trinidad and Tobago]], [[Jamaica]], [[Grenada]], Uganda, [[Barbados]], The [[British Virgin Islands]], Zambia, Saint Maarten, the United Kingdom and India<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wazobians.com/sinach-hold-live-concert-uganda/|title=Sinach hold live concert in Uganda – Wazobians|website=wazobians.com|accessdate=12 August 2017}}</ref>
[[File:Pastor Robert and Jessica Kayanja welcome Sinach in Uganda for the easter music performance at Rubaga Miracle Center Cathedral In Uganda.jpg|thumb|Sinach being welcomed in Uganda to perform on 15 April 2014]]

In one of a recent chat with music journalist Motolani Alake of Pulse Nigeria, Sinach reveals how God arrested her into gospel ministration through Pastor [[Chris Oyakhilome]] anointing.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gist |first1=Yinka |title=How God Arrested Me Through Pastor Chris Oyakhilome |url=https://gyonlineng.com/how-god-arrested-me-through-pastor-chris-sinach/ |website=GYOnlineNG.COM |access-date=18 February 2021}}</ref>

On December 22, 2020, at the [[2020 African Entertainment Awards  USA|third ceremony]] of [[African Entertainment Awards USA]] she won  Best Gospel Artist award.<ref>{{cite web|work= Music In Africa |url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/africa-entertainment-awards-usa-2020-all-winners=|title=Africa Entertainment Awards USA 2020: All the winners|first= Lucy |last=Ilado
|date=22 December 2020}}</ref>
