'''King of the Dot Entertainment''', also known as '''KOTD''', is a North American [[Battle rap|rap battle]] league founded in [[Toronto|Toronto, Ontario]], Canada in 2008. The company's HQ is based in Toronto, from which it gets its name. Successful music artists such as [[Shotty Horroh]], [[360 (rapper)|360]], [[Canibus]], and [[Madchild]] have battled in King of the Dot.

==About==
King of the Dot is hosted by its founder [[Organik (musician)|Organik]]. Past co-hosts have included various [[hip hop]] artists, including [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Method Man]], [[Raekwon]], [[The Alchemist (musician)|The Alchemist]], [[MC Hammer]], [[Too $hort]], [[E-40]], [[DJ Skee]], [[Sticky Fingaz]], [[Spider Loc]], [[Reef the Lost Cauze]], [[Slaine (rapper)|Slaine]], [[Sabac Red]], [[Sid Wilson]], [[Jus Allah]], and more. The league began in August 2008 in an alleyway near [[Toronto Eaton Centre]],<ref name="arts.nationalpost">{{cite web|url=http://arts.nationalpost.com/2010/06/29/hip-hop-week-king-of-the-dot-reigns-supreme/|title=Hip Hop Week: King of the Dot battle league reigns supreme &#124; National Post|publisher=Arts.nationalpost.com|date=|accessdate=2015-11-28|archive-url=https://archive.is/20120713160355/http://arts.nationalpost.com/2010/06/29/hip-hop-week-king-of-the-dot-reigns-supreme/|archive-date=2012-07-13|url-status=dead}}</ref> and now features over 150 [[emcee]]s, battling in [[Toronto]], [[California]], United Kingdom, [[Arizona]], [[Michigan]], [[Massachusetts]] and [[Vancouver]].<ref name="aux">{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.aux.tv/2010/08/king-of-the-dot-gets-international/|title=King of the Dot Gets International|publisher=AUX.TV|date=2010-08-11|accessdate=2015-11-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208180608/http://www.aux.tv/2010/08/king-of-the-dot-gets-international/|archive-date=2015-12-08|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Primarily featuring Canadians and Americans, emcees from countries such as England, Scotland, Australia, Sweden, Norway, Philippines, South Africa, and Russia have all battled in King of the Dot's annual ''World Domination'' event. The influence of King of the Dot has spread globally, inspiring the creation of other battle leagues such as [[FlipTop Battle League|FlipTop]] in the Philippines, Dissneeland in Vietnam, and [[w:ru:Versus Battle|Versus Battle]] in Russia.<ref name="arts.nationalpost"/>

King of the Dot has three leagues — PY Battles (Try-Outs); GZ Battles (North American stand alone smaller league); and KOTD (worldwide big league).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/article/1051616--fighting-words-aplenty-at-king-of-the-dot |title=Fighting words aplenty at King of the Dot &#124; Toronto Star |work=Thestar.com |date=2011-09-09 |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref>

King of the Dot events have featured all elements of hip hop culture, including [[a cappella]] [[emcee]] battles, freestyle battles over instrumentals, live performances by hip hop artists, [[beatboxing]], [[graffiti]] exhibits, [[Disc jockey|DJing]], and [[B-boying|breaking]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://medsbc.com/?p=776 |title=Made for Entertainers Designed for Style - M.E.D.S.|publisher=Medsbc.Com |date= |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref>

==Origins==
Travis Fleetwood, a.k.a. [[Organik (musician)|Organik]], built a reputation as one of Canada's elite battle emcees, winning title after title, including 4 wins at the well known [[Toronto]]-based freestyle competition ''Proud 2B Eh Battle MC'',<ref>{{cite AV media |date=2011-05-28 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDfvo5nUHA4 |title=PROLIFIC VS. ORGANIK-"Proud 2B Eh Battle MC: Round 3"(Live In Toronto Jul/15/2006) |publisher=GrizOnTheGrind |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=2021-09-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170204015940/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDfvo5nUHA4 |archive-date=2017-02-04 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite AV media |date=2007-05-22 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XEPFskckrJ4 |title=Proud To Be EH MC #5 Road to the Finals PT 2 |publisher=dirtdick |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=2021-09-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170204014152/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XEPFskckrJ4 |archive-date=2017-02-04 |url-status=live}}</ref> where he met a local producer/DJ by the name of RyanPVP. The two put together the first event which was initially intended to be a [[flash mob]] style event at [[Yonge-Dundas Square]] in downtown Toronto. However, the police presence and private security at the location forced the event to move down the road to an alley beside radio station [[CFXJ-FM|Flow 93.5]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.flow935.com/ |title=Home |publisher=FLOW 93-5 |date= |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref><ref>{{cite AV media |date=2008-11-17 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q7lfDeE7Bro |title=KOTD - Rap Battle - Kid Twist vs Big Mac Part 1 |publisher=King Of The Dot Entertainment |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=2021-09-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210202072825/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q7lfDeE7Bro |archive-date=2021-02-02 |url-status=live}}</ref> After a few events at that location, they were unable to accommodate the growing crowds, so Organik opted to re-locate to [[Alexandra Park, Toronto|Alexandra Park]] for the wide open space and gritty location. The crisp visuals and production value, as well as local celebrity guest judges contributed to King of the Dot's rise.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kingofthedot.com |title=KOTDTV |publisher=Kingofthedot.com |date= |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref>

==Format==
The King Of The Dot battle is usually a set of three rounds. There are varying round times, between 60 seconds and 3 minutes. The rules are not overall that strict and rappers are frequently allowed to stretch beyond the time limits, as was showcased in Dizaster's battle with DNA, co-hosted by Drake.<ref name="youtube1">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c9g2XgrxOk4 |title=KOTD - Rap Battle - Dizaster vs DNA *Co-Hosted by DRAKE* |publisher=YouTube |date=2011-11-15 |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref>

==Magazine==
KOTD magazine is available to be read online on the official King of the Dot Entertainment website. There is also an option to order the magazine in hard copy. There have been six issues released to date. The magazine has articles discussing past events, interviews with various rappers and much more.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kingofthedot.com |title=KOTDTV |publisher=Kingofthedot.com |date= |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref>

==Past events==
In 2010, King of the Dot hosted its first Grand Prix.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2010/06/king-of-the-dot-to-crown-1st-grand-prix-champion-articlenews/ |title=King of the Dot to crown 1st Grand Prix Champion [Article/News&#93; · HipHopCanada.com · Canadian Hip-Hop News, Rap Music |publisher=Hiphopcanada.com |date=2010-06-12 |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref> [[Kid Twist (rapper)|Kid Twist]] was the first King of the Dot Champion.<ref name="arts.nationalpost" />

Several major battles events have been hosted by King of the Dot, including World Domination II in August 2011.<ref name="forbes">{{cite web|author= |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/jasonoberholtzer/2011/09/22/behind-the-scenes-at-world-domination-ii-part-one/ |title=Behind the Scenes at World Domination II, Part One |work=Forbes.com |date= |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref>  American rapper [[Illmaculate]] defeated [[Bender (rapper)|Bender]] during this event,<ref>{{cite AV media |date=2011-09-22 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bEYdhO4N918 |title=KOTD - Rap Battle - Illmaculate vs Bender {{!}} #WD2 |publisher=King Of The Dot Entertainment |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=2021-09-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210203033511/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bEYdhO4N918 |archive-date=2021-02-03 |url-status=live}}</ref> which was featured in [[Forbes]].<ref name="forbes" />

On November 5, 2011, King of the Dot hosted Flatline (2011), which featured a battle between rappers Dizaster and DNA.  This promo event was co-hosted by Canadian [[hip hop]] artist [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], who put up $3000 of his own money for both rappers .<ref name="youtube1"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://chronicle.durhamcollege.ca/story.php?id=6549 |title=Drake Comes To KOTD Event|accessdate=December 27, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120425221452/http://chronicle.durhamcollege.ca/story.php?id=6549 |archivedate=April 25, 2012 }}</ref>

On July 9, 2012, King Of The Dot hosted Vendetta, which was headlined by the mainstream rapper [[Canibus]] battling Dizaster. The battle spurred minor controversy in the Hip-Hop community, when Canibus appeared to forget his lines and pulled out a notepad to read his lyrics from.

Blackout 3 took place on January 26, 2013 and was located at The Guvernment Nightclub in Toronto. Hosted by Organik, Gully T.K., Lush One, Maestro Fresh Wes, Scott Jackson and Drake, KOTD sold out club that night with over 1400 patrons. Dizaster, from Los Angeles, California, was facing Arcane from Hamilton, Ontario, in the title match. This battle was judged by Dax Flow, Yas DCS, Adam Bomb, Knamelis and [[Shotty Horroh]]. Sponsors for this event were: Bke Technology, Metro News, Pound for Pound, 682 Records, Guerilla Printing, Marketing Kings, Urbanology Magazine, Think Don't Shoot, Guvernment Nightclub, and Trevor D Custom Jewellery.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qL9_o_8GExQ |title=KOTD - Rap Battle - Dizaster vs Arcane (Title Match) *Co-Hosted By Drake* |publisher=YouTube |date=2013-02-12 |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref>

Blackout 4 took place on January 25 & 26, 2014. It was the first time that the matches other than the main events, were not released to the public.<ref>{{cite web|last=Tardio |first=Andres |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26478/title.kotd-says-blackout-4-will-be-innovative |title=KOTD Says "Blackout 4" Will Be Innovative |publisher=HipHopDX |date=2013-12-03 |accessdate=2015-11-28}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2014/01/king-of-the-dot-announces-new-twist-for-blackout-4-event-jan-24-25-news/ |title=King of the Dot announces new twist for Blackout 4 event Jan. 24-25 [News&#93; · HipHopCanada.com · Canadian Hip-Hop News, Rap Music |publisher=Hiphopcanada.com |date=2014-01-07 |accessdate=2015-11-28 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170824180746/http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2014/01/king-of-the-dot-announces-new-twist-for-blackout-4-event-jan-24-25-news/ |archive-date=2017-08-24 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Instead just the names of the battlers involved were released to the public. Each battler had a custom made introduction played on a big screen to bring them to the stage with.<ref>{{cite AV media |date=2014-03-04 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jgg0T7UYG84 |title=KOTD - Media - #BO4 Battlers Intros |publisher=KOTD Media |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=2021-09-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160307121725/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jgg0T7UYG84 |archive-date=2016-03-07 |url-status=live}}</ref> It was at this event that Pat Stay made his first successful title defense against Dizaster to remain KOTD Champion.
