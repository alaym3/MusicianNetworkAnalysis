'''YN Jay''' (born 1998/1999) is an American rapper. After releasing his debut mixtape ''MVP'' in 2019, his song "Coochie" went viral on social media in 2020. His first two studio albums, ''Ninja Warrior'' and ''Coochie Chronicles'', were released in 2021 through [[Todd Moscowitz|Alamo Records]].

==Early life==
Jay was raised in [[Beecher, Michigan]],<ref name="pforkmrap">{{cite web |last1=Pierre |first1=Alphonse |title=A Guide to Michigan Rap, 2020's Most Exciting Regional Scene |url=https://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/a-guide-to-michigan-rap-2020s-most-exciting-regional-scene/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=December 11, 2020}}</ref> and had one older brother, Gabe, who inspired Jay to start rapping at age 10. As a teenager, he recorded eight to 10 songs a day, and started rapping as a career after his brother died.<ref name="xxlbreak">{{cite web |last1=III |first1=Robby Seabrook |title=The Break Presents: YN Jay |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/yn-jay-interview-the-break/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=October 7, 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
In November 2019, he released his debut mixtape ''MVP''. He released the single "Coochie" with Louie Ray in April 2020, which went viral on YouTube and other social media platforms. He released the single "Gotta Get Rich" in July 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pierre |first1=Alphonse |title=The Ones: YN Jay's "Gotta Get Rich" |url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/yn-jay-gotta-get-rich-new-song-listen/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=July 31, 2020}}</ref> His mixtapes ''Coochie Land'' and ''Watch This'' were released in 2020, in August and September, respectively.<ref name="xxlbreak" /> His single "Blind 'Em" with [[Lil Yachty]] was released in September 2020, and his single "Doonie Van" was released in October 2020.<ref name="blindem">{{cite web |last1=A. |first1=Aron |title=Lil Yachty & YN Jay Shine On New Collab "Blind 'Em" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-yachty-and-yn-jay-shine-on-new-collab-blind-em-new-song.1988609.html |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=September 16, 2020}}</ref><ref name="pfdoonvan" />

Jay released his debut studio album, ''Ninja Warrior'', in January 2021 through [[Todd Moscowitz|Alamo Records]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bloom |first1=Madison |last2=Hussey |first2=Allison |last3=Minsker |first3=Evan |title=5 Albums Out This Week You Should Listen to Now |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/5-new-albums-you-should-listen-to-now-laura-mvula-g-herbo-bobby-gillespie-and-jehnny-beth-and-more/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=July 2, 2021}}</ref> He released the single "Big Hoes" featuring [[Lil Pump]] in March 2021, and was featured on Lil Yachty's song "Flintana" from his mixtape ''[[Michigan Boy Boat]]'' in April 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wright |first1=Abby |title=YN Jay & Lil Pump Get Nasty On "Big Hoes" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/yn-jay-and-lil-pump-get-nasty-on-big-hoes-new-song.1990525.html |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=March 6, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Pierre |first1=Alphonse |title=Lil Yachty: Michigan Boy Boat |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/lil-yachty-michigan-boy-boat/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=April 28, 2021}}</ref> His song "Triple S" went viral on TikTok, and a remix of the song featuring [[Coi Leray]] was released in June 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rouhani |first1=Neena |last2=Lee |first2=Cydney |title=R&B/Hip-Hop Fresh Picks of the Week: EST Gee, YN Jay, TOBi & More |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9594226/rb-hip-hop-fresh-picks-est-gee-yn-jay-tobi/ |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=June 29, 2021}}</ref> Jay was featured on Mace Supreme's song "Jumanji" that same month.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Jefferson |first1=Devon |title=New Music Friday - New Singles From Roddy Ricch, Ty Dolla $ign, Rich The Kid + More |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.62482/title.new-music-friday-new-singles-from-roddy-ricch-ty-dolla-ign-rich-the-kid-more# |website=[[HipHopDX]] |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=June 3, 2021}}</ref> His second studio album, ''Coochie Chronicles'', was released in July 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Cole |first1=Alexander |title=YN Jay Crafts One Of The Fattest Blunts In "How To Roll" History |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/yn-jay-crafts-one-of-the-fattest-blunts-in-how-to-roll-history-new-video.46656.html |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=July 24, 2021}}</ref> He released the single "Lamar" in November 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Powell |first1=Jon |title=YN Jay channels "BMF" for "Lamar" single |url=https://www.revolt.tv/new-music/2021/11/10/22774249/yn-jay-lamar-song |website=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]] |access-date=November 14, 2021 |language=en |date=November 10, 2021}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Jay's music has been described as [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[Trap music|trap]].<ref name="blindem" /> Alphonse Pierre of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' wrote, "YN Jay endlessly recycles his own lyrics and somehow keeps coming up with something new."<ref name="pfdoonvan">{{cite web |last1=Pierre |first1=Alphonse |title=The Ones: YN Jay's "Doonie Van" |url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/yn-jay-doonie-van-new-song-listen/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=October 20, 2020}}</ref> Neena Rouhani of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' called Jay a "Detroit favorite", while ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]''{{'}}s Eli Enis called Jay one of "the most prolific voices from Michigan's fertile rap scene".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rouhani |first1=Neena |title=Here's What Detroit Rapper Tone Tone is Hoping To Inspire With His Toney Island Restaurant Launch |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9547337/tone-tone-detroit-toney-island-restaurant-interview |website=Billboard |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=March 27, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Enis |first1=Eli |last2=Jones |first2=Marcus |last3=Suskind |first3=Alex |title=Friday Five: BRS Kash's 'Throat Baby' remix, Weezer bring in the orchestra, and more |url=https://ew.com/music/music-reviews/friday-five-brs-kash-throat-baby-weezer/ |website=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en}}</ref> In 2021, ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' included the music video for "Coochie" on their list of the funniest hip-hop music videos of the prior five years.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Seabrook III |first1=Robby |title=These Are the Funniest Hip-Hop Videos of the Last Five Years |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/funniest-hip-hop-videos-last-five-years/ |website=XXL |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=March 2, 2021}}</ref> Jay refers to himself as the "Coochie Man".<ref name="pforkmrap" /><ref name="xxlbreak" />
