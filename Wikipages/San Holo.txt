'''Sander van Dijck''' (born 26 November 1990), better known by his stage name '''San Holo''', is a Dutch DJ, musician and record producer from [[Zoetermeer]]. He gained international recognition for his remix of [[Dr. Dre]]'s "[[The Next Episode]]", which currently has over 232 million views on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{Citation|last=Trap Nation|title=Dr. Dre – The Next Episode (San Holo Remix)|date=20 October 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vZv9-TWdBJM|access-date=9 March 2017}}</ref> He has released music on several record labels including [[Spinnin' Records]], [[OWSLA]], Barong Family, and [[Monstercat]]. He also founded [[Bitbird]], a label which has released several of his singles including "Still Looking" and "[[Light (San Holo song)|Light]]" and his debut album ''[[Album1]]''. San Holo's first EP, ''Cosmos'', was released via Heroic on 18 September 2014 and was in the Top 100 Electronic category of [[iTunes]]. San Holo's first album, ''[[Album1]]'', was released via Bitbird on 21 September 2018 and debuted at #7 on the ''Billboard'' [[Dance/Electronic Albums]] chart.<ref name="San Holo Album1 Chart History">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/san-holo/chart-history/dance-electronic-albums/song/1099341|title=San Holo Album1 Chart History|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-10-06}}</ref>

In 2017, he was nominated at the [[Electronic Music Awards]] for New Artist of the Year.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/lists/2017-electronic-music-awards-winners-1041765/item/radio-show-year-1041754|title=Radio Show of the Year – Bonobo, Rufus du Sol, Eric Prydz & More Win 2017 Electronic Music Awards|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2018-06-20}}</ref> His second studio album, ''[[BB U OK?]]'', was released on 4 June 2021.

==Background==
Van Dijck studied music production and guitar at [[Codarts]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.codarts.nl/alumni-pop-eng/|title=Codarts Pop alumni|newspaper=Codarts|language=en-GB|access-date=20 December 2016}}</ref> He graduated in 2012.<ref name="partyflock.nl">{{cite web|url=http://partyflock.nl/en/artist/83381:San-Holo|title=San Holo|work=Partyflock|access-date=1 November 2015}}</ref> After years of working with bands, Van Dijck began ghost producing for DJs. His production experienced considerable commercial success, which led him to consider going solo and creating the stage name, San Holo.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.youredm.com/2015/11/11/san-holo-talks-life-music-business-with-your-edms-live-stream-team/|title=San Holo Talks Life, Music & Business With Your EDM's Live Stream Team|last=Viz|first=Nicole|date=11 November 2015|website=Your EDM|access-date=7 August 2016}}</ref>

==History==
=== 2013–2014: Early EPs, Heroic Recordings and Walt Disney Pictures ===
In early 2013, van Dijck released his debut EP, ''Corellia''.<ref name="corelliaep">{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/077XJ5IfZ4Gx2PuJZVs89L |title=Corellia – EP by San Holo|website=Spotify|access-date=7 August 2018}}</ref> Later that year in October, he released his second EP, ''Demons''.<ref name="demonsep">{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/1XxPrtCG7fmOmRGTDxXG48 |title=Demons by San Holo|website=Spotify|access-date=7 August 2018}}</ref> These first two EPs have since been removed from streaming services. He began to gain attention in 2014, when he was signed by Heroic Recordings.{{citation needed|date=May 2018}} ''Cosmos EP'' was released on 18 September 2014<ref name="cosmosep">{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/cosmos-feat.-the-nicholas-ep/id1042332034 |title=Cosmos (feat. The Nicholas) – EP by San Holo on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=7 August 2016}}</ref> and was in the Top 100 Electronic category of iTunes.{{citation needed|date=May 2018}}

In September 2014, [[Walt Disney Pictures]] threatened a lawsuit against van Dijck.<ref name="gerardvandenijssel">{{cite web|url=http://denhaagfm.nl/2014/09/22/disney-dreigt-met-rechtszaak-tegen-haagse-producer-san-holo/|title=Den Haag FM » Disney dreigt met rechtszaak tegen Haagse producer San Holo|author=Gerard van den IJssel|date=22 September 2014|work=Den Haag FM|access-date=1 November 2015}}</ref> They thought his stage name San Holo was [[spoonerism|too similar]] to [[Han Solo]], a character in the [[Star Wars]] movies.<ref name="gerardvandenijssel" /> In November, he released a remix of [[Dr. Dre]]'s "[[The Next Episode]]", which has over 205 million views on YouTube.<ref name="thenextepisode">{{Citation|last=Trap Nation|title=Dr. Dre – The Next Episode (San Holo Remix)|date=20 October 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vZv9-TWdBJM|access-date=7 August 2016}}</ref>

=== 2015–2016: OWSLA, Spinnin, Monstercat, bitbird ===
On 4 May 2015, he released a song called "Victory" on [[Monstercat]].<ref name="victoryep">{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/victory-ep/id990455769|title=Victory – EP by San Holo on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=7 August 2016}}</ref> On 25 May 2015, he also released a music video of his song "Hold Fast" in Monstercat; both songs become part of Monstercat's 22nd album titled ''Contact'' and San Holo's [[Victory (San Holo EP)|''Victory'' EP]], also from Monstercat.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://music.monstercat.com/album/monstercat-022-contact|title=Monstercat 022 – Contact – Monstercat|date=1 June 2015|work=Monstercat|access-date=1 November 2015}}</ref> On 4 September 2015, he released a video of his single with Father Dude called "IMISSU" on [[Spinnin' Records]].<ref>{{cite av media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q3gqn_KCZOw|title=San Holo x Father Dude – IMISSU (Official Music Video)|date=4 September 2015|work=YouTube|access-date=1 November 2015}}</ref> On 2 October 2015, he released a remix of [[Porter Robinson]]'s song titled "Natural Light".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/ca/album/worlds-remixed/1037438921 |title=Worlds (Remixed) by Porter Robinson on Apple Music |website=[[iTunes Store]]|access-date=2018-05-05 }}</ref> The remix became a part of Robinson's ''[[Worlds Remixed]]'' album.

On 26 January 2016, he released a single with [[Yellow Claw (DJs)|Yellow Claw]] named "Alright" through [[OWSLA]].<ref name="alrightsingle">{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/alright/id1075925446?i=1075926408|title="Alright" from OWSLA Worldwide Broadcast by Various Artists on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=7 August 2016}}</ref> This was van Dijck's second song on OWSLA, after "Smile On Your Face" in 2015 with the French group Point Point. "Alright" later became a part of a compilation album by OWSLA called ''OWSLA Worldwide Broadcast''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/owsla-worldwide-broadcast/1075925446 |title=OWSLA Worldwide Broadcast by Various Artists on Apple Music |website=[[iTunes Store]] |date=2016-01-09 |access-date=2018-08-06}}</ref>

On 23 February 2016, van Dijck released his single "We Rise" on OWSLA's NEST HQ imprint.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/sanholobeats/san-holo-we-rise |title=San Holo – We Rise |date=2016-02-23 |work=SoundCloud |access-date=2020-11-13}}</ref> This song would go on to become one of his most successful releases, reaching 60 million Spotify streams and over 100 million views on YouTube.

On 11 March 2016, he released the ''New Sky'' EP, with a second single and music video on [[Monstercat]] called "They Just Haven't Seen It", featuring David Chris.<ref name="newskyep">{{Cite web|url=http://music.monstercat.com/album/new-sky-ep |title=New Sky EP, by San Holo|website=Monstercat|access-date=31 May 2017}}</ref> This, as well as the EP title track, became part of Monstercat's ''026 – Resistance''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.monstercat.com/album/monstercat-026-resistance |title=Monstercat 026 – Resistance – Monstercat |date=2016-03-21 |work=Monstercat |access-date=2018-08-06}}</ref> On 5 June 2016, he teased an unreleased song "RAW", which appeared as the background track of the credits in Episode 7 of Season 3 in the American comedy series [[Silicon Valley (TV series)|Silicon Valley]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://nesthq.com/san-holo-raw|title=San Holo's Silicon Valley-Featured "RAW" Gets VIP|last=Beer|first=Nathan|website=NEST HQ|access-date=4 September 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170213191627/http://nesthq.com/san-holo-raw|archive-date=13 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> It was then fully released on 19 July 2016.<ref name="rawsingle">{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/raw/id1133553522?i=1133553632|title="Raw" from Raw – Single by San Holo on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=7 August 2016}}</ref>

On 7 June 2016, he released a single named "Still Looking" on his own label [[bitbird]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://runthetrap.com/2015/10/14/san-holos-label-bitbird-releases-another-dope-single/|title=BeauDamian – No Know {{!}} RTT|date=14 October 2015|website=Trap Music Blog – Run The Trap: The Best Hip Hop, EDM & Club|access-date=7 August 2016}}</ref> On 22 November 2016, he released his single "[[Light (San Holo song)|Light]]" on bitbird which debuted on #38 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot [[Dance/Electronic Songs]] the week of 17 December 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/dance-electronic-songs/2016-12-17|title=EDM Music & Dance Songs Chart|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=16 December 2016}}</ref> The following week it made the highest gains on the chart rising 12 spots to #26 following a strong streaming performance.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/dance-electronic-songs|title=EDM Music & Dance Songs Chart|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=16 December 2016}}</ref> The next week it continued to climb to #19 for the tracking week of 31 December 2016 after continued streaming performance.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/dance-electronic-songs|title=EDM Music & Dance Songs Chart|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=20 December 2016}}</ref> The track reached its peak position at #13 on 11 February 2017, after climbing up from the 27th position the week before. "Light" spent a total of 20 weeks on the Hot Dance/Electronic chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6779031/san-holo/chart?f=1234|title=San Holo – Chart history – Billboard|website=www.billboard.com}}</ref> It has since surpassed 100 million streams on Spotify.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bh2XG27nEQB/|title=San Holo on Instagram: "Light just passed 100 million streams... THANK YOU for listening, this is insane! 💜🌈 where did you hear it for the first time…"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2018-10-06}}</ref>

The 15-track album ''Album1 (A Lot of Remixes)'' featured many up and coming acts as well as friends of van Dijck's.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-12-21|title=San Holo's 'album1' gets 'a lot of remixes'|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2019/12/san-holos-album1-gets-a-lot-of-remixes/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Dancing Astronaut|language=en-US}}</ref> Released on 20 December 2019, the album included remixes such as: "everything matters (when it comes to you)" remixed by Aiobahn and Laxcity, "Lift Me from the Ground"  remixed by Jaron, Manilla Killa,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-12-28|title=Manila Killa offers fresh perspective on San Holo's 'Lift Me from the Ground' [Stream]|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2019/12/san-holo-lift-me-from-the-ground-manila-kila-remix-stream/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Dancing Astronaut|language=en-US}}</ref> and Phuture Noize, "show me" remixed by The Nicholas, "brighter days" remixed by [[Atmozfears]] and Duumu, "always on my mind" remixed by MELVV, "go back in time" remixed by EMBRZ, Rootkit, and Skygate, "love (wip)" remixed by Tails, "voice in my head" remixed by Flaws, "worth" remixed by Prblm Chld, "forever free" remixed by Janee, "surface" remixed by Former Hero, and "vestal avenue" remixed by Golden Vessel.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-12-20|title=San Holo Shares Stacked 'album1 (a lot of remixes)' Ft. Manila Killa, Golden Vessel, Laxcity & More|url=https://thissongissick.com/post/san-holo-album1-remixes/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=This Song Is Sick|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: Stay Vibrant & bb u ok? (Album 2) ===
Following van Dijck’s ''Album1 (A Lot Of Remixes)'' release, on 31 January 2020 he released “Honest” featuring [[Broods]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Stone|first=Katie|title=San Holo Debuts Beautifully "Honest" Single ft. Broods|url=https://edm.com/music-releases/san-holo-honest-broods|access-date=2020-07-01|website=EDM.com – The Latest Electronic Dance Music News, Reviews & Artists|language=en}}</ref> The track entered the Billboard Hot [[Dance/Electronic Songs]] charts on 15 February 2020 and stayed there for eight weeks, making its way to #25. "Honest" was also featured in [[Dance/Mix Show Airplay]], and LyricFind Global. The accompanying music video shows the theme of self-referential irony and van Dijck’s struggle with false expectation and self-preservation.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-31|title=San Holo & Broods' 'Honest' Video: Exclusive|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8549751/san-holo-broods-honest-video|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Billboard}}</ref> On 10 April 2020 he released three remixes for "Honest" by Midnight Kids,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-01|title=Midnight Kids Work Their Magic On San Holo's "Honest" Featuring Broods|url=https://www.djtimes.com/2020/04/san-holo-honest-midnight-kids-remix/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=The Nexus of All Things DJ|language=en-US}}</ref> [[Lunice]],<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-10|title=San Holo Recruits Trap Legend Lunice On Wonky New Remix Of His & Broods' Track "Honest"|url=https://thissongissick.com/post/san-holo-honest-lunice-remix/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=This Song Is Sick|language=en-US}}</ref> and [[220 Kid]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-10|title=San Holo & Broods "Honest" Remix Package with Support from Lunice, 220 KID, and Midnight Kids|url=https://oneedm.com/edm-releases/san-holo-broods-honest-remixes-lunice-220-kid-and-midnight-kids/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=One EDM|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Vigil|first=Dom|title=San Holo Releases "Honest" Remix Pack Featuring Midnight Kids, Lunice and 220 Kid|url=https://preludepress.com/news/2020/4/10/san-holo-releases-honest-remix-pack-featuring-midnight-kids-lunice-and-220-kid/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Prelude Press|language=en-US}}</ref>

On van Dijck’s social media activity fans have noticed he has adopted the phrase "stay vibrant"<ref>{{Cite web|title=Stay Vibrant|url=https://sanholo.com/stay-vibrant/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=SAN HOLO|language=en}}</ref> and the use of the "%" sign to express his mood and emotions (blessed % / the sacred %) with his fans. van Dijck aims to embrace the fact that not all days are equally "vibrant" and emphasizes that life is all about ups and downs. The percentage sign is used as a mood meter, and he encourages his fans to use it to show how blessed they are feeling that day, without having to put it into words.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Graves|first=Ciara|date=2020-03-31|title=San Holo Brings us Together in 'Stay Vibrant' an Ever-Expanding Music Collection|url=http://www.thenocturnaltimes.com/san-holo-brings-us-together-in-stay-vibrant-an-ever-expanding-music-collection/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=The Nocturnal Times|language=en-US}}</ref>

During the [[Coronavirus disease 2019|Coronavirus pandemic]] in Spring of 2020, San Holo started the rollout of his [[Stay Vibrant]] (stylized as stay vibrant) EP.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Wright|first=Raven|date=2020-04-30|title=San Holo is Fully Invested in Helping Us 'stay vibrant'|url=https://edmidentity.com/2020/04/30/san-holo-stay-vibrant/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=EDM Identity|language=en-US}}</ref> Released on his label Bitbird, the EP consists of seven singles: “(if only i could) hold you”,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-30|title=San Holo releases two new tracks: '(if only i could) hold you' and a Midnight Kids remix of 'Honest'|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2020/03/san-holo-releases-two-new-tracks-if-only-i-could-hold-you-and-a-midnight-kids-remix-of-honest/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Dancing Astronaut|language=en-US}}</ref> “don’t forget to breathe today”,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-06|title=San Holo Reminds Us, "Don't Forget To Breathe Today," In Brilliant New Single|url=https://thissongissick.com/post/san-holo-dont-forget-to-breath-today/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=This Song Is Sick|language=en-US}}</ref> “in the end i just want you to be happy”,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Mullins|first=About the Author / Ellie|date=2020-04-15|title=San Holo – in the end i just want you to be happy|url=https://weraveyou.com/2020/04/san-holo-in-the-end-i-just-want-you-to-be-happy/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=We Rave You|language=en-US}}</ref> “idk anything (demo)”,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Staff|first=Conscious Electronic|date=2020-04-22|title=San Holo releases fourth track of his weekly-expanding music collection 'stay vibrant'|url=https://consciouselectronic.com/2020/04/22/san-holo-releases-fourth-track-of-his-weekly-expanding-music-collection-stay-vibrant/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Conscious Electronic|language=en-US}}</ref> “in case i never see you again…”,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-29|title=San Holo shares gliding crescendo and fifth work from his 'stay vibrant' project|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2020/04/san-holo-shares-gliding-crescendo-and-fifth-work-from-his-stay-vibrant-project/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Dancing Astronaut|language=en-US}}</ref> “we’re all just on our way home”,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-05-04|title=San Holo rounds out 'stay vibrant' EP with 'we're all just on our way home' ft. Luwten|url=https://consciouselectronic.com/2020/05/04/san-holo-were-all-just-on-our-way-home-ft-luwten/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Conscious Electronic|language=en-US}}</ref> and “staring at the sea without you next to me”.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-05-14|title=San Holo releases final track of 'stay vibrant'—for now|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2020/05/san-holo-releases-final-track-of-stay-vibrant-for-now/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Dancing Astronaut|language=en-US}}</ref> Artists such as Analogue Dear, ILIVEHERE, and Luwten are featured on the EP. The project was inspired by van Dijck looking for a way to express his emotions and connect with fans while in quarantine.<ref name="Wright">{{Cite web|last=Wright|first=Raven|date=2020-05-08|title=San Holo Talks Secret Sky, 'stay vibrant,' and bitbird|url=https://edmidentity.com/2020/05/08/san-holo-secret-sky-festival-interview/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=EDM Identity|language=en-US}}</ref>

During this time to stay engaged with his audience, van Dijck streamed weekly on [[Twitch (service)]]. He also premiered footage from past live shows such as his performance at [[Red Rocks Amphitheatre]] in June 2019, [[Electric Daisy Carnival]] in 2017, and [[Lollapalooza]] in 2019. San Holo also participated in his independent record label’s first ever livestream, “a day with bitbird”,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-18|title=Tune into San Holo's bitbird virtual showcase event|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2020/03/tune-into-san-holos-bitbird-virtual-showcase-event/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Dancing Astronaut|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-19|title=Watch Bitbird's Virtual Music Festival Live Stream|url=http://runthetrap.com/2020/03/18/bitbird-virtual-music-festival/|access-date=2020-07-01|website=Run The Trap: The Best EDM, Hip Hop & Trap Music|language=en-US}}</ref> featured on [[Diplo's Revolution]] on [[Sirius XM]].

In May 2020, San Holo premiered many of the songs from the Stay Vibrant EP at [[Porter Robinson]]'s Secret Sky online music festival.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Yopko|first=Nick|title=[WATCH] Relive The Magic of Porter Robinson's Secret Sky Music Festival|url=https://edm.com/events/relive-secret-sky|access-date=2020-07-01|website=EDM.com – The Latest Electronic Dance Music News, Reviews & Artists|language=en}}</ref><ref name="Wright"/> Money raised from the festival was donated to the [[MusiCares COVID-19 Relief Fund]].

On 5 November 2020, San Holo announced social media hiatus, "to clear my mind and live in the moment a little more". He also declared: "Let's start a new chapter soon!"<ref>{{Cite web |last=Heffler |first=Jason |title=San Holo Announces Social Media Hiatus in Letter to Fans |url=https://edm.com/news/san-holo-announces-social-media-hiatus |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=EDM.com |language=en}}</ref> 21 days later, he announced the release of the single "BB U OK?" for 1 December,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mullins |first=Ellie |date=2020-11-26 |title=San Holo announces new track 'bb u ok?' |url=https://weraveyou.com/2020/11/san-holo-new-single-bb-u-ok/ |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=We Rave You |language=en-US}}</ref> which started "a new chapter" in his career.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mullins |first=Ellie |date=2020-12-01 |title=San Holo – bb u ok? |url=https://weraveyou.com/2020/12/san-holo-bb-u-ok/ |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=We Rave You |language=en-US}}</ref> The song was San Holo's first release on Counter Records.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Peters |first=Emily |date=2020-12-03 |title=San Holo Signs to Counter Records with New Single |url=https://edmidentity.com/2020/12/03/san-holo-bb-u-ok-counter-records/ |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=EDM Identity |language=en-US}}</ref>

On 27 January 2021, he teased the "biggest announcement in San Holo history", to occur on 3 February.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Yopko |first=Nick |title=San Holo Teases "Biggest Announcement in San Holo History" |url=https://edm.com/news/biggest-announcement-in-san-holo-history |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=EDM.com |language=en}}</ref> The announcement was his second studio album, ''[[BB U OK?]]'', along with a second single, "Find Your Way". The album was initially set to be released on 21 May via [[Bitbird]], his own independent label, and Counter Records.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Wright |first=Raven |date=2021-02-03 |title=San Holo Releases New Single Off Upcoming Album 'bb u ok?' |url=https://edmidentity.com/2021/02/03/san-holo-single-off-upcoming-album-bb-u-ok/ |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=EDM Identity |language=en-US}}</ref> He then released "It Hurts!" on 10 March,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Heffler |first=Jason |title=Listen to San Holo Sing on Soaring New Single, "IT HURTS!" |url=https://edm.com/music-releases/san-holo-it-hurts |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=EDM.com |language=en}}</ref> and "Black and White / My Fault" on 14 April.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-04-15 |title=San Holo expands 'bb u ok?' tracklist with two new singles |url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2021/04/san-holo-expands-the-bb-u-ok-tracklist-with-a-double-single-release/ |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=Dancing Astronaut |language=en-US}}</ref> On 6 May, San Holo announced that the album was delayed to 4 June.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Yopko |first=Nick |title=San Holo Pushes Release Date for "bb u ok? (album 2)" |url=https://edm.com/music-releases/san-holo-bby-u-ok-release-date-delayed |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=EDM.com |language=en}}</ref> On 22 May, he released "You've Changed, I've Changed", with [[Chet Porter]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-05-22 |title=San Holo & Chet Porter Deliver Wistful Indie Electronic Single "you've changed, i've changed" on Bitbird [LISTEN] |url=https://www.youredm.com/2021/05/22/san-holo-chet-porter-deliver-wistful-indie-electronic-single-youve-changed-ive-changed-on-bitbird-listen/ |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=Your EDM |language=en-US}}</ref> On 24 May, he announced a North American tour to promote the album.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Gray |first=Kaelyn |title=San Holo Announces Massive "bb u ok?" North American Tour |url=https://edm.com/events/san-holo-bb-u-ok-north-american-tour-2021 |access-date=2021-06-04 |website=EDM.com |language=en}}</ref>

On 17 September 2021, San Holo played the Fremont Stage at the Life Is Beautiful festival in Las Vegas, NV USA, where he played most of the bb u ok? album.
