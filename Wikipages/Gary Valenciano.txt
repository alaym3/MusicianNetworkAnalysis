'''Edgardo Jose''' "'''Gary'''" '''Santiago Valenciano''' ({{IPA-tl|valɛnˈʃano}}, born August 6, 1964), also known as '''Gary V.''', is a [[Filipino people|Filipino]] singer-songwriter, dancer, musician, actor, music producer and television host. Valenciano has released 39 albums, and won the [[Awit Awards|Awit Award]] for "Best Male Performer" twelve times.

He is currently part of [[ABS-CBN Corporation|ABS-CBN]]'s contract artists, having been a mainstay of the Sunday noontime musical variety show ''[[ASAP (variety show)|ASAP]]'' and the noontime, weekday singing contest segment of ''[[It's Showtime (Philippine TV program)|It's Showtime]]''. Valenciano is frequently tapped to sing theme songs for the network's soap operas and in-house film arm, [[Star Cinema]]. Aside from music, he is [[UNICEF Philippines]]' first [[List of UNICEF Goodwill Ambassadors|National Ambassador]], since 1998.

Valenciano was given the ASAP Elite Platinum Circle Award several times for his achievements in the local music industry. In his 34 years in the music industry, he has been awarded multi-platinum recording awards for most of his full-length studio albums.<ref>{{cite web|author=Created With Citymall |url=http://www.pep.ph/guide/guide/3122/pinoy-singers-join-the-platinum-circle |title=Guide {{pipe}} PEP.ph – Philippine Entertainment Portal |publisher=PEP.ph |date=September 20, 2017 |access-date=October 17, 2017}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Gary Valenciano was born in [[Santa Mesa, Manila|Santa Mesa]] district of City of Manila on August 6, 1964. He is the sixth of seven children of Vicente Calacas Valenciano, a Bicolano from [[Camalig, Albay|Camalig]], Albay and Grimilda Santiago y Olmo (1934–2019) from [[Arecibo]], [[Puerto Rico]].<ref name="birth">{{cite web |url=https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/3:1:939F-VPSH-YS |title=Philippines, Manila, Civil Registration, 1899-1984 Image Philippines, Manila, Civil Registration, 1899-1984; ark:/61903/3:1:939F-VPSH-YS — FamilySearch.org |access-date=October 30, 2015}}</ref> His parents met in [[New York City]], married, and settled in Manila.<ref>[http://garyv.com/content/news_files/archive01/paper/jewel.htm bulletin.gif<!-- Bot generated title -->]{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> His mother is a Puerto Rican of [[Italian people|Italian]] descent<ref name="pep.ph">{{cite web |author=PEP.ph |url=http://www.pep.ph/photos/1090/Gary_V%3A_Live_at_25!/num/10/19 |title=Gary V: Live at 25! {{pipe}} PEP.ph: The Number One Site for Philippine Showbiz |publisher=PEP.ph |access-date=March 13, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131111090726/http://www.pep.ph/photos/1090/Gary_V%3A_Live_at_25%21/num/10/19 |archive-date=November 11, 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbn.com/700club/guests/bios/Gary_Valenciano060308.aspx |title=Gary Valenciano: A Winning Lifestyle |publisher=Cbn.com |access-date=March 13, 2012}}</ref> who sang [[opera]] in Manila during the 1960s. Valenciano attended primary and secondary school in [[La Salle Green Hills]].

==Career==
{{BLP sources section|date=February 2018}}
In 1978, at 14 years old, Valenciano made his first [[Television in the Philippines|television]] appearance in an advertisement for Fress Gusto, a now-discontinued soft drink sold by [[San Miguel Corporation]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zTVmDxJxC90 |title=Fress Gusto Classic Philipine TVC 1975 (with Gary V) |via=YouTube |date=July 13, 2007 |access-date=March 13, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Video 48 |url=http://video48.blogspot.com/2009/07/gary-vs-fress-gusto-circa-1978.html |title=Video 48: GARY V.'S "FRESS GUSTO" Circa 1978 |publisher=Video48.blogspot.com |date=July 5, 2009 |access-date=March 13, 2012}}</ref>

He started as a choir singer, then launched his career in singing and show business on May 13, 1983.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/10/19/08/gary-v-celebrates-his-25th-anniversary-music-industry|title=Gary V unleashing pure energy in Araneta|date=October 19, 2008|publisher=ABS-CBN News|access-date=January 24, 2009}}</ref> He first appeared as a solo artist in 1982 in the television programme ''The Pilita and Jackie Show'', and later in ''Germspesyal'' and ''Penthouse Live''.

He had his first solo concert in April 1984 at the [[Araneta Coliseum]], followed by a number of albums. Three of his albums were released internationally, including the Christian-inspired album ''Out of the Dark''.  He won the [[Awit Awards|Awit Award]] twelve times in the "Best Male Recording" category alone.<ref>{{cite web|author=garyv |url=http://garyv.com/about/ |title=About Us |publisher=Gary V |access-date=October 17, 2017}}</ref> He has been called "Mr. Pure Energy" due to his high energy dance performances in concerts.<ref name="pep.ph"/>

As an actor, he won the "Best Single Performance by an Actor" trophy when he performed in a drama television movie for GMA7 in the 90s. He has appeared in 14 full-length films as the lead actor for some of the top movie companies like Viva Films, Regal Films, Seiko Films, and Star Cinema, with leading ladies like Vilma Santos, Maricel Soriano, Lorna Tolentino, Kris Aquino, Sharon Cuneta, Gretchen Barretto, Princess Punzalana, and Ms. Universe 1993 Dayanara Torres.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

As an inspirational artist, he recorded a number of Christian albums and was chosen to do a music video for the Billy Graham Evangelistic Association (BGEA) for a song he co-wrote, "Could You Be Messiah," in 1994. He was then tapped for Global Mission in Puerto Rico in 1994 where he performed with Michael W. Smith and Steve Green. BGEA tapped him as well as in Amsterdam 2000 where he performed for over 10,500 evangelists. He is a member of the Board of Trustees of Christian Broadcasting Network, producers of ''The 700 Club''. He performed at the 50th anniversary of CBN in Virginia Beach and has guested on ''The 700 Club USA'' many times.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

As a performing artist, he was called the "Michael Jackson of the Philippines" in the 80s and 90s, and continues to hold concerts all over the world. He has sold-out venues and theatres in the Philippines and all over the world, performing in venues like the Shrine Auditorium and Microsoft Theater in California, the Roy Thompson Hall in Canada, the Budokhan Hall in Japan, the Navy Pier in Chicago, and the Sydney Entertainment Center and Hillsong Auditorium in Australia. He has performed all over the world, including in the US, Canada, Hong Kong, Japan, Korea, China, Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, Singapore, Brunei, Taiwan, Papua New Guinea, New Zealand, Australia, Israel, Puerto Rico, Saudi Arabia, Dubai, Bahrain, Abu Dhabi, Al Ain, Oman, Qatar, Paris, London, Finland, Norway, Ireland, Germany, Spain, Switzerland, Italy, and Amsterdam.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

In 1998, he became [[UNICEF Philippines]]' first National Ambassador. In 2006, the Doha Asian Games Organising Committee has named Gary Valenciano the official performer of the [[2006 Asian Games|Asian Games]]' theme song, "Side By Side".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pep.ph/news/local/11435/gary-valenciano-to-sing-asian-games-anthem|title=Gary Valenciano to sing Asian Games anthem|author=Candice Lim|date=30 November 2006}}</ref> In 2008, he marked his tenth year as a UNICEF Ambassador with a visit to Sitio Avocado, a former war zone in [[Negros Oriental]].

Valenciano returned to acting in 2008 by appearing in the drama anthology ''[[Maalaala Mo Kaya]]'', where he portrayed a prisoner who finds religion and becomes a [[Born again (Christianity)|born again Christian]]. That same year, he was nominated for "Best Single Performance by an Actor" in the 22nd PMPC Star Awards for TV.<ref>{{Cite web|title=PMPC bares nominees for "22nd Star Awards for Television"|url=https://www.pep.ph/news/19548/pmpc-bares-nominees-for-22nd-star-awards-for-television|access-date=2020-11-28|website=PEP.ph|language=en}}</ref>

In 2009, Valenciano released a collaborative album with [[Martin Nievera]] called ''As 1'', with the carrier single of the same title.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Martin Nievera and Gary Valenciano's As 1 album certified Gold|url=https://www.pep.ph/guide/music/4695/martin-nievera-and-gary-valenciano39s-as-1-album-certified-gold|access-date=2020-11-28|website=PEP.ph|language=en}}</ref>

In 2010, Valenciano was in a [[Holy Week in the Philippines|Holy Week]] drama special, ''Gulong'' ("Wheel"), a CBN Asia production shown on [[GMA Network|GMA]].{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

For his 25th anniversary in 2008, STAR RECORDS, the music arm of ABSCBN, released ''GV 25'', a compilation of songs he composed and recorded sung by music stars of the Philippines like Sharon Cuneta, Rachel Ann Go, Martin Nievera, Piolo Pascual, Jed Madela, Erik Santos, and Christian Bautista.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

For his 30th anniversary in 2013, his concert ARISE, which was originally staged at the Araneta Coliseum for two nights and restaged at the MOA ARENA to a jam-packed audience, won as PMPC's Best Concert of the Year. His album ''With You'' won the Best Inspirational Album for the same year at the Catholic Mass Media Awards.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

===Philanthropy===
Valenciano formed the Shining Light Foundation in 1989, focusing on supporting diabetics and a scholarship program that has produced many multi-awarded scholars and students.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

==Personal life==
Valenciano married Maria Anna Elizabeth "Angeli" Pangilinan, the sister of senator [[Francis Pangilinan]], on August 6, 1984. Together, they have three children: Paolo Valenciano, a director and the vocalist of the band Salamin; [[Gabriel Valenciano]], a singer, keyboardist, dancer, and director; and [[Kiana Valenciano]], a model and singer-songwriter. The family resides in [[Antipolo]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Gary V biking from Antipolo to Calatagan is the most inspiring thing you'll see today |url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/ancx/drive/cars-and-bikes/02/08/21/this-video-of-gary-v-biking-from-antipolo-to-calatagan-is-the-most-inspiring-thing-youll-see-today |publisher=ABS-CBN News Channel |access-date=February 8, 2021 |date=February 8, 2021}}</ref>

He is currently living with [[Type 1 diabetes|type 1]] [[diabetes]], and has been an endorser for several wellness products related to the condition.<ref>[http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/tv-current-affairs/10/01/09/gary-vs-battle-diabetes-i-survived Gary Valenciano currently living with diabetes.]</ref> In May 2018, Valenciano started feeling ill after a performance with his son Gabriel for the celebration of his 35th anniversary in showbiz in ABS-CBN's top-rating Sunday afternoon show, ASAP, and had to undergo [[Cardiac surgery|open heart surgery]] due to blockage of his [[left anterior descending artery]] caused by his diabetes. The procedure was successfully performed on May 6, 2018,<ref>{{cite news |title=Gary Valenciano undergoes heart surgery |url=https://www.panaynews.net/gary-valenciano-undergoes-heart-surgery/ |access-date=July 6, 2018 |work=Panay News |date=May 8, 2018}}</ref> and he was released from the hospital by May 12.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cepeda |first1=Cody |title=Gary V finally released from hospital after open heart surgery |url=http://entertainment.inquirer.net/274437/p2fb-look-gary-v-finally-released-from-hospital-after-open-heart-surgery |access-date=July 6, 2018 |work=Philippine Daily Inquirer |date=May 14, 2018}}</ref>

On April 17, 2019, Valenciano's mother died in [[Orlando, Florida]], after battling pneumonia, congestive heart failure, and H1N1 flu.<ref>[https://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/04/18/19/gary-valencianos-mother-passes-away]</ref>
