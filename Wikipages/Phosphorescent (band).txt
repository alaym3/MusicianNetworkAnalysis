'''Phosphorescent''' is the stage name of American singer-songwriter '''Matthew Houck''' (born July 27, 1980).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Thomas |first1=Fred |title=Artist Biography |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/phosphorescent-mn0000846092/biography |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=January 15, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fuse.tv/videos/2013/10/austin-city-limits-festival-2013-phosphorescent-robert-plant|title=Phosphorescent Played, Partied With Robert Plant on His Birthday|first=Exclusive|last=Interview|website=Fuse}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2013/06/phosphorescent-announces-u-s-tour-dates-opening-for-robert-plant/|title=Phosphorescent announces U.S. tour dates, opening for Robert Plant|date=June 18, 2013}}</ref> Originally from [[Huntsville, Alabama]], Houck began recording and performing under this nickname in 2001 in [[Athens, Georgia]].  He is currently based in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].

==Overview==
Before recording under the name Phosphorescent, Matthew Houck traveled the world playing under the name Fillup Shack and self-released a limited pressing of the album ''Hipolit'' in 2000.<ref>Fred Thomas, [{{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p562319/biography|pure_url=yes}} "Artist Bio: Phosphorescent"], ''AllMusicGuide'', retrieved October 30, 2008.</ref> Houck later changed his recording name to Phosphorescent and released the full-length [[LP record|LP]] ''[[A Hundred Times or More]]'' in 2003. The album was released through [[Athens, Georgia]]-based independent label [[Warm Records]]. The following year, he released the [[extended play|EP]] ''[[The Weight of Flight]]''. Phosphorescent rose to wider critical acclaim after releasing ''[[Aw Come Aw Wry]]'' in August 2005 and ''[[Pride (Phosphorescent album)|Pride]]'' in October 2007. The latter was named the 12th best album of 2007 by ''[[Stylus Magazine]]''<ref>Stylus Staff, [http://stylusmagazine.com/articles/pop_playground/top-50-albums-of-2007.htm "Top 50 Albums of 2007"], ''Stylus Magazine'', October 31, 2007.</ref> and received an 8.0 rating from the online indie magazine ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]''.<ref>William Bowers, [http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/record_review/46528-pride "Album Reviews: Phosphorescent – ''Pride''"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071024030434/http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/record_review/46528-pride |date=October 24, 2007 }}, ''Pitchfork Media'', October 22, 2007.</ref> In 2009, inspired by [[Willie Nelson]]'s tribute album to [[Lefty Frizzell]] ''[[To Lefty from Willie]]'', Houck crafted a tribute album to Nelson himself entitled ''[[To Willie]]'', which was released through [[Dead Oceans]]. Phosphorescent released ''[[Here's to Taking It Easy]]'' in 2010.

''[[Muchacho (album)|Muchacho]]'', Phosphorescent's sixth studio album, was released in 2013 to critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Nemo |first1=Nemo |title=Phosphorescent: Muchacho De Lujo (Deluxe Edition) |url=http://www.secretlydistribution.com/release.html?catalog=DOC087 |website=www.secretlydistribution.com/ |access-date=January 15, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Caramanica |first1=Jon |last2=Chinen |first2=Nate |title=Albums by Low and Phosphorescent |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/03/19/arts/music/albums-by-low-and-phosphorescent.html |work=The New York Times |access-date=January 15, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Barton |first1=Laura |author-link1=Laura Barton |title=Phosphorescent: 'It's hard to know how far you've slipped, if you're slipping' |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/nov/28/phosphorescent-matthew-houck-interview-muchacho |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |location=London |access-date=2021-05-06 |date=2013-11-28 }}</ref> The full studio album ''C'est La Vie'' followed in 2018.
