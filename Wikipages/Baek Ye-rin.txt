'''Baek Ye-rin''' ([[Korean language|Korean]]:백예린; born June 26, 1997), also credited as '''Yerin Baek''', is a South Korean singer-songwriter. A former member of duo [[15&]], she debuted as a solo artist with the mini album ''Frank'', in 2015. Known for her delicate and sweet vocals,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2020-12-10|title=Yerin Baek returns with her latest album 'tellusboutyourself', sharing a new sound and stories of transformation|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/yerin-baek-shares-a-new-side-and-stories-of-growth-in-her-latest-album-tellusboutyourself-korea-blue-vinyl-kpop-comeback-interview-december-2020|access-date=2021-07-16|website=Bandwagon Asia|language=en}}</ref> since 2015, she has had eight singles peak in the top ten on [[Gaon Digital Chart]]. A singer-songwriter, Baek is credited with composing and writing the lyrics for the majority of her songs, often touching on personal topics and writing from real-life experiences. In addition to her solo career, she is also the front person for Korean rock band The Volunteers and has been performing with them from 2018 onwards.

==Biography==
Baek was born on June 26, 1997, in [[Jung District, Daejeon|Jung District]], Daejeon. While a trainee under [[JYP Entertainment]], she spent two years living in New York where her family now lives.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://15and.jype.com/profile.asp|author=JYPE|title=Baek Yerin Profile|publisher=JYP Entertainment|access-date=April 14, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160403191601/http://15and.jype.com/profile.asp|archive-date=April 3, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> She graduated from [[Hanlim Multi Art School]] in February 2016 alongside 15& member [[Park Ji-min (singer, born 1997)|Park Ji-min]] and [[Yugyeom|Kim Yu-gyeom]] of [[Got7]].

== Career ==

===2007–2012: Career beginnings===
In a 2007 episode of the [[Seoul Broadcasting System|SBS]] variety show ''[[Star King (TV series)|Amazing Contest Star King]]'', Baek performed "[[Listen (Beyoncé Knowles song)|Listen]]" by [[Beyoncé Knowles|Beyoncé]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1ezm1QGZz1c|author=starkingsbs|title=Baek Ye-rin – Listen|work=YouTube|publisher=[[Seoul Broadcasting System|SBS]]|date=October 8, 2012|access-date=July 5, 2013|author-link=Star King (TV series)}}</ref> and was labeled a "[[contemporary R&B]] genius".<ref name="allkpop">{{cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2012/10/15s-baek-ye-rin-revealed-to-be-a-student-of-singer-kim-jo-han|author=allkpop staff|title=15&'s Baek Ye Rin revealed to be a student of singer Kim Jo Han|work=[[allkpop]]|date=October 2, 2012|access-date=July 5, 2013}}</ref>{{unreliable source|date=April 2021|reason=As per [[WP:KO/RS#UR]]}} Baek Ye-rin, who appeared on the stage as a "10-year-old ballad genius", surprised the cast and viewers.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-02-24|title="잘 컸다" 백예린, 스타킹 출연· JYP 오디션 보니... '변화 無'|url=http://www.sporbiz.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=80598|access-date=2021-04-07|website=한스경제_한국스포츠경제|language=ko}}</ref> After her ''Star King'' appearance, Baek decided to audition for [[JYP Entertainment]]. She performed "Listen" on the audition day,{{cn|date=April 2021}} and later won the second place, behind only [[2PM]]'s [[Jang Woo-young]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/article/17958|author=Stewart Ho|title=15&'s Baek Ye Rin Joined JYP Entertainment After Placing 2nd in JYP Auditions Behind 2PM's Wooyoung|work=ENews World|publisher=[[CJ E&M|CJ E&M Corporation]]|date=October 17, 2012|access-date=July 5, 2013}}</ref> She spent five years (2007–2012) training in South Korea and the United States, and was called a "genius girl" for her vocal talent, skills in using the piano and fluency in the English language.<ref name="allkpop" />{{unreliable source|date=August 2019|reason=As per [[WP:KO/RS#UR]]}}

On September 27, 2012, JYP revealed Baek would debut as part of a duo along with [[Park Ji-min (singer, born 1997)|Park Ji-min]] called [[15&]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/article/16146|author=Kim Ji-yeon and Erika Kim|title=JYP Reveals ′K-Pop Star′ Park Ji Min′s Fellow Member for 15&, Baek Ye Rin|work=ENews World|publisher=[[CJ E&M|CJ E&M Corporation]]|date=September 27, 2012|access-date=July 5, 2013}}</ref> On October 7, the duo debuted with their first single, "I Dream", on [[Seoul Broadcasting System|SBS]] ''[[Inkigayo]]''. The duo began a four-year hiatus in February 2015 and disbanded following Park's departure from JYP Entertainment.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.v.daum.net/v/20190806090221880|script-title=ko:박지민, 7년만에 JYP 떠난다.."피프틴앤드 사실상 해체|work=[[Yonhap News Agency|Yonhap News]]|trans-title=Park Ji-min, leaving JYP after 7 years."15& de facto disbanding"|via=[[Daum (web portal)|Daum]]|last=Lee|first=Eun-jung|date=August 6, 2019|access-date=August 6, 2019|language=ko}}</ref>

===2015–2016: Solo debut and EPs releases===
In 2015, she planned a solo album for the music style she pursued and met Cloud, a producer of [[Cheeze (singer)|CHEEZE]]. At the first meeting, Baek Yerin did not know Cloud, but he said that he was a fan of Baek Yerin, and later he was able to meet her with the introduction of his agency.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-19|title=[문화뉴스 RichPOP] 백예린의 프로듀서 '구름', 그는 어떤 음악을 몰고올까|url=http://www.mhns.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=403975|access-date=2021-05-07|website=문화뉴스|language=ko}}</ref> In November 2015, Baek debuted as a solo artist with her first extended play, ''Frank'', with the lead single titled "Across the Universe".<ref>{{cite web | url=https://m.news.naver.com/read.nhn?mode=LSD&mid=sec&sid1=103&oid=009&aid=0003632143 | title=백예린 첫 솔로 Frank 공개, 전곡 작사·작곡 참여}}</ref> The lead song's lyrics were inspired by the film ''[[The Martian (film)|The Martian]]''.

Baek released her first digital single titled ''Bye Bye My Blue'' on June 20, 2016.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://m.entertain.naver.com/read?oid=277&aid=0003770125 | title=백예린 새 앨범 'Bye bye my blue', 작사+작곡으로 감성 아티스트 등극}}</ref> Her follow-up digital single titled ''[[Love You on Christmas]]'' was released on December 7.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://m.ilyoseoul.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=155751#hi | title=백예린 깜짝 크리스마스 송 'Love you on Christmas' 발표| date=December 2, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://m.entertain.naver.com/read?oid=312&aid=0000222879 | title=백예린 7일 'Love you on Christmas' 공개, 백예린표 감성 듬뿍}}</ref>

===2018–2019: Solo success and departure from JYP ===

As Baek continued to feature on songs by popular artists, she also began performing covers and her own unreleased songs at festivals.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.seouljazz.co.kr/artist/208 |title=Archived copy |access-date=October 24, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181024192346/http://www.seouljazz.co.kr/artist/208 |archive-date=October 24, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newstap.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=77206 |title=성시경, 헤이즈, 크러쉬, 백예린··· '2018할리스커피페스티벌' 총출동 – 뉴스탭 |publisher=Newstap.co.kr |date=September 25, 2012 |access-date=December 29, 2018}}</ref> In 2018, she formed The Volunteers with members of the independent rock band Bye Bye Badman, and released their first EP, the post-grunge rock "Vanity & People". The Seoul-based rock band consists of vocalist and guitarist Baek, bassist Hyungseok Koh, guitarist Jonny, and drummer Chiheon Kim. Like the majority of Baek's post-JYP solo work, The Volunteer's lyrics are written entirely in English. 

Baek released her second mini-album, ''Our Love Is Great'', on March 18, 2019. The following day, her title track "Maybe It's Not Our Fault" went to the top of all eight domestic music sites' realtime charts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allkpop.com/article/2019/03/baek-ye-rins-maybe-its-not-our-fault-tops-all-8-domestic-realtime-music-charts|title=Baek Ye Rin's 'Maybe It's Not Our Fault' tops all 8 domestic realtime music charts|website=allkpop|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.soompi.com/article/1311174wpp/baek-yerin-soars-to-top-of-all-major-realtime-charts-with-maybe-its-not-our-fault|title=Baek Yerin Soars To Top Of All Major Realtime Charts With "Maybe It's Not Our Fault"|date=March 18, 2019|website=Soompi|language=en|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref>{{unreliable source|reason=As per [[WP:KO/RS#UR]]|date=September 2021}}  ''Our Love is Great'' and lead track "Maybe It's Not Our Fault" won Album of the Year, Best Pop Album, and Best Pop Song respectively at the 2020 Korean Music Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|date=27 February 2020|title=17th Korean Music Awards Announces Winners; Baek Yerin Snags 3 Awards|url=https://www.soompi.com/article/1385364wpp/17th-korean-music-awards-announces-winners-baek-yerin-snags-3-awards|url-status=live|website=Soompi}}</ref>{{unreliable source|reason=As per [[WP:KO/RS#UR]]|date=September 2021}}

Baek also contributed to the soundtrack of the popular 2019 K-drama Crash Landing on You, with an original ballad, "Here I am Again." The song peaked at #6 on Billboard's K-pop Hot 100 chart, her highest position on the chart yet.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 March 2021|title=Yerin Baek On Running Her Own Label, 'Tellusboutyourself,' And Performing A Song For 'Crash Landing On You'|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/music/a35755652/yerin-baek-tellusboutyourself-interview/|url-status=live|website=Elle.com}}</ref>

On September 13, 2019, Baek announced her contract with [[JYP Entertainment]] had finished, and that she was leaving to create her own independent record label, Blue Vinyl.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.koogle.tv/media/news/baek-ye-rin-announces-her-departure-from-jyp-entertainment/|title=Baek Ye Rin announces her departure from JYP Entertainment|website=www.koogle.tv|language=en|access-date=October 3, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hellokpop.com/news/baek-yerin-departure-jyp-entertainment-after-12-years/|title=Baek Yerin Announces Departure From JYP Entertainment After 12 Years|date=September 13, 2019|website=hellokpop|language=en-US|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.soompi.com/article/1352779wpp/jyp-entertainment-confirms-baek-a-yeons-departure-from-the-agency|title=JYP Entertainment Confirms Baek A Yeon's Departure From The Agency|date=September 16, 2019|website=Soompi|language=en|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref> {{unreliable source|reason=As per [[WP:KO/RS#UR]]|date=September 2021}}

=== 2019–present: Founding of label Blue Vinyl, ''Every letter I sent you'', ''Tellusboutyourself'' and ''Love, Yerin'' ===
Baek officially launched her own label, Blue Vinyl, on November 6,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allkpop.com/article/2019/11/baek-ye-rin-announces-independent-label-blue-vinyl|title=Baek Ye Rin announces independent label 'Blue Vinyl'|website=allkpop|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/290554/20191107/singer-baek-yerin-left-jyp-entertainment-to-start-her-own-independent-label.htm|title=Singer Baek Yerin Left JYP Entertainment To Start Her Own Independent Label|date=November 7, 2019|website=KpopStarz|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref>{{unreliable source|reason=As per [[WP:KO/RS#UR]]|date=September 2021}} and on November 22 she released her first single through the label, "Popo (How Deep Is Your Love)"''.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.soompi.com/article/1367354wpp/baek-yerin-announces-1st-release-since-establishing-independent-label|title=Watch: Baek Yerin Reveals "Popo (How Deep Is Your Love?)" MV For 1st Release With New Independent Label|date=November 22, 2019|website=Soompi|language=en|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref> In the build up to Baek's new album, it was revealed that songs which had previously been released on her [[SoundCloud]] would feature on the new release, as well as new, never-heard-before tracks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hellokpop.com/news/baek-yerin-new-december-album-details/|title=Baek Yerin To Include Previously-Released Songs On Her SoundCloud In Upcoming Album|date=November 27, 2019|website=hellokpop|language=en-US|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref>{{unreliable source|reason=As per [[WP:KO/RS#UR]]|date=September 2021}}

On December 10, 2019, Baek released the first studio album and first double album ''[[Every letter I sent you]].'' with 17 out of the 18 songs being written in English. Her next single "Square (2017)" also made Baek the first South Korean artist to top charts with a song in English.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ajudaily.com/view/20191211154330551|title=Singer Baek Yerin becomes first S. Korean artist to top song charts with English song|date=December 11, 2019|website=www.ajudaily.com|language=en|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref> The track went on to score an 'all kill', where an artist tops the daily and realtime charts of [[Melon (online music service)|Melon]], [[Genie Music|Genie]], [[NHN Entertainment Corporation|Bugs]], and [[Soribada]]; and the realtime charts of Flo and iChart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=108&aid=0002829297|title=백예린, 신곡 'Square' 이틀째 음원차트 1위 '쾌거'|trans-title=Baek Ye-rin, No. 1 'Square' on the second track of the new song 'Square'|date=December 11, 2019|access-date=April 25, 2020|work=Star Today|last=Yoon|first=Seong-yeol|language=ko|via=[[Naver]]}}</ref> As well as "Square (2017)" finding success, the album also topped charts in South Korea.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.koreatimes.co.kr/www/art/2020/01/732_280151.html|title=Baek Ye-rin's English album sweeps music charts|date=December 11, 2019|website=koreatimes|language=en|access-date=January 19, 2020}}</ref> Baek was awarded Top 10 artist and Best R&B/Soul for her track "Square (2017)" at the 12th Melon Music Awards in 2020, as well as Best Pop Album for ''Every letter I sent you.'' at the 2021 Korean Music Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|date=5 December 2020|title=Check out Winners of the 'Melon Music Awards 2020|url=https://www.allkpop.com/article/2020/12/check-out-winners-of-the-melon-music-awards-2020|url-status=live|website=Allkpop}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=28 February 2021|title=Here are the Winners of the 2021 Korean Music Awards|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/bts-baek-yerin-18th-korean-music-awards-winners-list-2021|url-status=live|website=Bandwagon Asia}}</ref>

In February 2020, her first solo concert, "Turn on that Blue Vinyl", was a success. As tickets for about 4,400 seats were sold out in 30 seconds, it is an example of how Baek Yerin's reputation is clearly different from before.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-31|title=백예린, 단독콘서트 1분 매진에 추가 공연 확정..."Turn on that Blue Vinyl"|url=http://www.mhns.co.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=381136|access-date=2021-05-07|website=문화뉴스|language=ko}}</ref>

On December 10, 2020, exactly one year after her previous album, Baek Yerin released her second studio album ''[[tellusboutyourself]]'', consisting of 14 tracks. The first single "0415" also came with a music video on the same day. Overall 5 music videos ("0415", "Hate You", "I'll be your family!", "I'm in love", and "You're so lonely now, so you need me back by your side again") were released along with the album. The album features a both a wider range of musical genres and lyrical themes than her previous work.<ref name=":0" />

On February 16, 2021, Baek released ''tellusboutyourself (remixes)'', a digital EP featuring 6 remixed tracks.

Her band The Volunteers launched their self-titled debut album under Blue Vinyl on May 27, 2021 after previously making their music solely available through SoundCloud and [[YouTube]].<ref>{{cite news |title='백예린 밴드' 더 발룬티어스, 27일 첫 정규 발매 '정식 데뷔' |url=https://www.news1.kr/articles/?4306610 |publisher=뉴스1}}</ref> The album draws influence from rock acts such as [[Oasis (band)|Oasis]], [[Red Hot Chili Peppers]] and [[the Beatles]], while sonically channelling "a sense of rebellion".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/the-volunteers-yerin-baek-hyungseok-koh-jonny-chiheon-kim-interview-debut-album-vanity-and-people-release-new-music-blue-vinyl-rock-band-korea-2021|title=The Volunteers on their debut album and the process behind their first-ever album|website=Bandwagon Asia|last=Basbas|first=Francesca Judine|date=May 27, 2021|access-date=June 4, 2021}}</ref>

On September 10, 2021, Baek released the cover album ''Love, Yerin'', featuring 6 remake tracks.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://entertain.naver.com/now/read?oid=609&aid=0000482137|title=백예린, 9월10일 커버앨범 ‘선물’ 발매 “타 뮤지션 음악 리메이크”|trans-title=Yerin Baek, cover album 'Gift' released on September 10th “Remake of other musicians’ music”|date=August 24, 2021|access-date=August 24, 2021|work=Newsen|last=Park|first=Soo-in|language=ko|via=[[Naver]]}}</ref>

=== Artistry and Reception ===
Baek has cited [[Amy Winehouse]], [[Oasis (band)|Oasis]], [[Avril Lavigne]], [[Rage Against the Machine]], as well as Korean artists [[BoA]], Light & Salt, and [[Yoo Jae-ha|Yoo Jae Ha]] among her many varied influences.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=December 7, 2020|title=Baek Ye Rin cites BoA as someone 'who raised me'|url=https://www.allkpop.com/article/2020/12/baek-ye-rin-cites-boa-as-someone-who-raised-me|url-status=live|website=Allkpop}}</ref>{{unreliable source|reason=As per [[WP:KO/RS#UR]]|date=September 2021}} She named her first album as a solo artist, ''Frank'', as a tribute to [[Amy Winehouse]], whose [[Frank (Amy Winehouse album)|debut album]] shared the same name. 

On her decision to write most of her recent songs in English, Baek explains her intentions as wanting to make her music accessible to international fans, plans for an international tour, and the influence English-language artists have had on her style and music. In a 2021 interview she mentioned that it is her dream to perform in the home countries of the many western artists that inspired her.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Yee|first=Andrew|date=January 27, 2021|title=Interview: Yerin Baek|url=https://lifewithoutandy.com/featured/mad-love/interview-yerin-baek/|url-status=live|website=Life Without Andy}}</ref>

Whether in English or Korean, Baek is known for her candid and straightforward lyrics. On her lyrical inspiration for her most recent album, Baek cites the poetic metaphors of [[Blossom Dearie]] and the straightforward style of Amy Winehouse as influences.<ref name=":1" />
