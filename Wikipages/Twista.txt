'''Carl Terrell Mitchell''' (born November 27, 1973), better known mononymously as '''Twista''' (formerly '''Tung Twista'''), is an American rapper. He is best known for his [[Chopper (rap)|chopper]] style of [[rapping]] and for once holding the title of fastest English rapper in the world according to [[Guinness World Records]] in 1992, being able to pronounce 598 syllables in 55 seconds.<ref>{{cite news|last=Stevens|first=Mary|title=Tung Twista Lands Spot in Guinness Book|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/1992-06-26/entertainment/9202260488_1_world-records-guinness-book-toy-boat|access-date=27 June 2012|newspaper=Chicago Tribune|date=26 June 1992}}</ref>

In 1997, after appearing on [[Do or Die (group)|Do or Die]]'s hit single "[[Po Pimp]]", produced by [[The Legendary Traxster]], Twista signed with Big Beat and Atlantic Records, where he released his third album ''[[Adrenaline Rush (album)|Adrenaline Rush]]'' and formed the group [[Speedknot Mobstaz]], in 1998. His 2004 album ''[[Kamikaze (Twista album)|Kamikaze]]'' reached the top of the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] album chart, after the success of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] #1 single "[[Slow Jamz]]".

==Early life==
Carl Terrell Mitchell was born on November 27, 1973, and grew up in the [[K-Town, Chicago|K-Town]] area of the [[West Garfield Park, Chicago|West Garfield Park]] neighborhood on the [[West Side, Chicago|West Side]] of [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]] and began rapping at the age of 12.<ref name="Atlantic"/>

==Career==
In 1992, Mitchell released his first album called ''[[Runnin' Off at da Mouth]]'' under the name Tung Twista.

His second album, titled ''[[Resurrection (Twista album)|Resurrection]]'', was set to be released in 1994, but due to problems with his record label at the time [[Atlantic Records]] and marketing issues involving fellow Chicago rapper [[Common (rapper)|Common]]'s [[Resurrection (Common album)|album of the same name]], the album was only released in the city of [[Chicago]] and thus got little national attention.

In 1996, he teamed with Chicago producer [[The Legendary Traxster]] and Cwal's [[Do or Die (group)|Do or Die]] on the track "[[Po Pimp]]" produced by The Legendary Traxster, which became a hit single. This led to a Cwal's label deal with Atlantic Records, which released ''[[Adrenaline Rush (album)|Adrenaline Rush]]'' in 1997.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Erlewine|first=Stephen Thomas|title=Twista > Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p215301|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=allmusic|year=2007|access-date=2008-08-18}}</ref> Twista had by then dropped the "Tung" from his stage name.<ref name="Atlantic">{{cite web|title=Twista|url=http://www.atlanticrecords.com/Twista/about/|year=2005|publisher=Atlantic Records|archive-date=2007-02-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070204083513/http://www.atlanticrecords.com/Twista/about/}}</ref>  ''Adrenaline Rush'' became Twista's first charting album, as it peaked at #77 on the [[Billboard Hot 200]]. The album also spawned a [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] hit in "Get It Wet" produced by The Legendary Traxster, which peaked at #96.

In 1998, Twista teamed with other Chicago area rappers to form the [[Speedknot Mobstaz]]. They released their first album, ''Mobstability'' produced entirely by The Legendary Traxster, in 1998. Twista then formed his own Legit Ballin' label, which released two compilation albums: ''Legit Ballin''' in 1999 and ''Legit Ballin' Vol. 2: Street Scriptures'' in 2001.<ref name="Atlantic"/> The label later released ''Respect The Game, Vol. 3'' in 2002 and ''Volume 4: Tha Truth'' in 2006. In 2000, Twista collaborated with [[Ruff Ryders]] and [[Drag-On]] on the ''Ruff Ryders: Ryde or Die Vol. 2'' album, on the track "Twisted Heat." He also collaborated with Memphis Bleek on “Is That Your Chick” on “The Understanding” album.

Beginning in 2002, Twista began recording his album ''[[Kamikaze (Twista album)|Kamikaze]]'' with rappers like [[Kanye West]] and [[Ludacris]]. ''Kamikaze'' came out in 2004 and debuted at the top spot of the American [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] album chart.<ref>{{cite news|last=D'Angelo|first=Joe|title=Twista Blows Straight To Top Of Billboard Albums Chart|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1484808/20040204/Twista.jhtml|publisher=MTV News|date=2004-02-04|access-date=2008-08-18}}</ref> Its first single "[[Slow Jamz]]" (also featured on [[Kanye West]]'s debut album ''[[The College Dropout]]''), featured West and [[Jamie Foxx]] and became a [[Hot 100 number-one hits of 2004 (United States)|number-one hit]] in the US.<ref name="allmusic"/> Other singles included "Overnight Celebrity" and "So Sexy" (featuring [[R. Kelly]]); the album sold around two million copies.<ref name="Atlantic"/> The remix of the song "Jook Gal" by [[Elephant Man (musician)|Elephant Man]] featured Twista and the [[YoungBloodZ]]. The ''Kamikaze'' album and its singles gave Twista a new level of success and rose his profile significantly within the rap community.[[File:Twista College of Charleston 2008.04.23.jpg|thumb|Twista in 2008.]]
Twista's album ''[[The Day After (album)|The Day After]]'' was released in 2005. It featured the hit singles "Girl Tonite" featuring [[Trey Songz]], "Hit the Floor" featuring [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]], and "[[So Lonely (Twista song)|So Lonely]]" featuring [[Mariah Carey]]. His next album, ''[[Adrenaline Rush 2007]]'' came out in 2007. Its singles were "Give it Up" featuring [[Pharrell Williams|Pharrell]] and "Creep Fast" featuring [[T-Pain]]. This album sold poorly compared to Twista's earlier albums.{{citation needed|date=September 2010}} Twista appeared on the single "[[Hell No (Leave Home)]]" from [[Monica (singer)|Monica's]] album fall 2006 ''[[The Makings of Me]]''. In 2008, Twista launched a new record label called "Get Money Gang Entertainment" and the album ''[[Category F5]]'' with the hit, "Wetter".<ref>{{cite web|last=Ford|first=Tracey|title=Twista Launches Get Money Gang Record Label|url=http://www.theboombox.com/2008/01/15/Twista-launches-get-money-gang-record-label/|work=The Boombox|date=2008-01-15|access-date=2008-08-18}}</ref> In 2009, Twista appeared on the single "Legendary" with fellow Chicago rappers AK47 of [[Do or Die (group)|Do or Die]] and [[Saurus and Bones]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.gowherehiphop.com/2009/11/twista-and-ak-of-do-or-die-join-chicago-duo-for-fast-paced-legendary-record/ |title=Twista and AK (Of Do Or Die) join Chicago duo for fast-paced "Legendary" record |publisher=Gowhere Hip Hop |date=2009-11-09 |access-date=2012-04-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120330083952/http://www.gowherehiphop.com/2009/11/twista-and-ak-of-do-or-die-join-chicago-duo-for-fast-paced-legendary-record/ |archive-date=2012-03-30 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Frank says |url=http://www.fakeshoredrive.com/2009/11/saurus-bones-feat-twista-ak-of-do-or-die-legendary.html/ |title=Saurus & Bones feat. The album features artists such as [[Waka Flocka Flame]], [[Raekwon]], Tia London, [[Sean Combs|Diddy]], [[Ray J]], and others. Production on the album is mainly by The Legendary Traxster along with Streetrunner, Twinz, Tight Mike and [[No I.D.]], Twista discussed his new documentary, directed by Vlad Yudin and the making of The Perfect Storm, including his belief that artists need to do more than just record songs and that they need to become more involved with the community around them.<ref>[http://conspiracyworldwide.podomatic.com/entry/index/2010-10-30T10_07_48-07_00/ Twista Oct 31st Uncensored Radio Interview]</ref>

==Personal life==
Twista was married from 1991 to 1999 and had a daughter with his ex-wife.<ref>{{cite web|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040829234809/http://www.suntimes.com/output/mitchell/cst-nws-mitch29.html |archive-date=August 29, 2004 |title=Rap star Twista leaves daughter waiting, wanting |url=http://www.suntimes.com/output/mitchell/cst-nws-mitch29.html |work=Chicago Sun-Times |author=Mitchell, Mary |date=August 29, 2004 |access-date=April 11, 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> 

During the holiday season in 2010, Twista and his GMG team worked together with Chicago Food Bank's Produce mobile program to feed hungry people in the community.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.urblife.com/the-word/giving-back-Twista-helps-the-hungry|title=Giving Back: Twista Helps Feed the Hungry in Chicago!|work=UrbLife.com|date=December 2, 2010}}</ref>
