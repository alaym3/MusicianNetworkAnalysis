'''Wallows''' is an American [[alternative rock]]<ref name="WADA">{{Cite magazine|last=|first=|date=February 1, 2019|title=Wallows Announce Debut Album & World Tour: Watch 'Are You Bored Yet?' Video Starring Noah Centineo, More|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8496151/wallows-debut-album-world-tour-are-you-bored-yet-video|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190215223350/http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8496151/wallows-debut-album-world-tour-are-you-bored-yet-video|archive-date=February 15, 2019|access-date=July 30, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> band based in Los Angeles composed of [[Dylan Minnette]], [[Braeden Lemasters]], and Cole Preston. The band began releasing songs independently in April 2017 starting with "Pleaser", which reached number two on the [[Spotify]] Global [[Viral 50]] chart.

In 2018, Wallows signed a deal with [[Atlantic Records]] and released their major label debut EP, [[Spring (Wallows EP)|''Spring'']]. The band released their debut studio album, ''[[Nothing Happens (album)|Nothing Happens]]'', in 2019, which featured the single "Are You Bored Yet?", followed by their 2020 EP ''[[Remote (EP)|Remote]]''.

==History==
As teenagers in 2011, [[Braeden Lemasters]] (guitar/vocals), Cole Preston (guitar/drums), and [[Dylan Minnette]] (guitar/vocals) formed a group called Join the Band with Zack Mendenhall (bass). They were students enrolled in the GigMasterz program at Keyboard Galleria Music Center in [[Southern California]].<ref name="SubLeaves">{{cite news | last=Hudelson| first=Molly| title =Wallows release new single 'Pulling Leaves Off Trees'| website=Substream Magazine| date =November 21, 2017| url =https://substreammagazine.com/2017/11/wallows-pulling-leaves-off-trees/| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> They changed their name to Feaver and played the 2011 [[Warped Tour]].<ref name="Chron" /> They also used the name The Narwhals before settling on Wallows in 2017, now without Mendenhall.<ref name="Chron">{{cite news | last=Spuhler| first=Robert| title =Breakout band Wallows kicks off headlining tour in SF| newspaper =San Francisco Chronicle| date =January 17, 2018| url =https://www.sfchronicle.com/music/article/Breakout-band-Wallows-kicks-off-headlining-tour-12504935.php| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref>

In April 2017, the band released their first single under the Wallows moniker, "Pleaser".<ref name="TV">{{cite news | last=Elizabeth| first=De| title =13 Reasons Why's Dylan Minnette Announces Band's First Single| work=Teen Vogue| date =April 13, 2017| url =https://www.teenvogue.com/story/13-reasons-why-dylan-minnette-band-first-single| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Nylon">{{cite news | last=Gore| first=Sydney| title ='13 Reasons Why' You're Going To Love Wallows| website=Nylon| date =April 13, 2017| url =https://nylon.com/articles/dylan-minnette-wallows-pleaser| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> The song would eventually reach number two on the Spotify Global Viral 50 chart and number one on the [[KROQ-FM|KROQ]] Locals Only playlist.<ref name="SubLeaves"/> In May 2017, Wallows released a second single, "Sun Tan",<ref name="Euph">{{cite news | last=Ersoy| first=Laura| title =Wallows – Sun Tan| website=Euphoria| date =May 22, 2017| url =http://www.euphoriazine.com/blog/2017/05/new-music/tracks-wallows-sun-tan/| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> and began playing live shows in the Los Angeles area, selling out [[Roxy Theatre (West Hollywood)|The Roxy]] and the [[Troubadour (West Hollywood, California)|Troubadour]].<ref name="Sugar">{{cite news | last=Garcia| first=Kelsey| title =We Need to Talk About Dylan Minnette's Band — Because It's Really Good!| website =PopSugar| date =May 10, 2017| url =https://www.popsugar.com/entertainment/Dylan-Minnette-Band-Wallows-43523191| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> Their third single, "Uncomfortable", was released in September 2017.<ref name="SubComfort">{{cite news | last=Hudelson| first=Molly| title =Prepare to get 'Uncomfortable' with Wallows' new single| website=Substream Magazine| date =September 14, 2017| url =https://substreammagazine.com/2017/09/wallows-uncomfortable/| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref>

[[File:Young Wallows Band.jpg|thumb|The band members in 2011]]
In November 2017, their song "Pulling Leaves off Trees" premiered  on [[Zane Lowe]]'s [[Beats 1]] radio show.<ref name="VarianceLeaves">{{cite news | last=Howard| first=Lindsay| title =Hear Wallows' Breezy New Track 'Pulling Leaves Off Trees'| website=Variance| date =November 16, 2017| url =http://variancemagazine.com/sounds/8634-wallows-pulling-leaves-off-trees| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> That month, the band also announced their first headlining North American tour that would run from January to March 2018.<ref name="OTW">{{cite news | last=Otero| first=Gabriela| title =NEW ARTIST DISCOVERY: Young Alt-Rock Trio Wallows Releases New Single & 2018 Tour Dates| website=Ones to Watch| date =November 29, 2017| url =http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/new-artist-discovery-young-alt-rock-trio-wallows| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> The tour started in San Francisco on January 24.<ref name="Chron"/><ref name="Nexus">{{cite news | last=Jackson| first=Hannah| title =Indie Act Wallows Ignites Velvet Jones| newspaper =Daily Nexus| date =February 3, 2018| url =http://dailynexus.com/2018-02-03/indie-act-wallows-ignites-velvet-jones/| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> In February 2018, the band announced that they had signed to [[Atlantic Records]] and were planning to release their major label debut EP, [[Spring (Wallows EP)|''Spring'']], in April 2018. They also released a new single, "Pictures of Girls", their first on Atlantic.<ref name="SubAtlantic">{{cite news | last=Hudelson| first=Molly| title =Wallows sign to Atlantic Records; release 'Pictures Of Girls'| website=Substream Magazine| date =February 27, 2018| url =https://substreammagazine.com/2018/02/wallows-atlantic-records-pictures-of-girls/| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> The song was chosen as a "Critical Cut" by [[SiriusXM]] [[Alt Nation]].<ref name="Verge">{{cite news|last=Mohaghegh|first=Neelu|date=March 23, 2018|title=Wallows Releases Nostalgic Single "These Days" Worth Not Missing|work=Verge Campus|url=https://vergecampus.com/2018/03/wallows-releases-nostalgic-days/|url-status=dead|access-date=April 9, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190204081239/https://vergecampus.com/2018/03/wallows-releases-nostalgic-days/|archive-date=February 4, 2019}}</ref>

Wallows ended their North American tour at [[South by Southwest]] in March 2018.<ref name="OTW"/><ref name="VarianceDays">{{cite news | last=Howard| first=Lindsay| title =Listen to Wallows' Summery New Track 'These Days'| website=Variance| date =March 26, 2018| url =http://variancemagazine.com/sounds/9037-wallows-these-days| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref> Later in the month, they released a second track from [[Spring (Wallows EP)|''Spring'']] titled "These Days".<ref name="VarianceDays"/> The EP was released on April 6, 2018 by Atlantic Records and was produced by [[John Congleton]].<ref name="MTVSpring">{{cite news| last =Gore| first =Sydney| title =Wallows Lead Us To Believe That Spring Is Really Here| publisher =MTV| date =April 6, 2018| url =http://trl.mtv.com/news/3072656/wallows-spring-ep/| access-date =April 9, 2018| archive-date =April 6, 2018| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20180406201755/http://trl.mtv.com/news/3072656/wallows-spring-ep/| url-status =dead}}</ref><ref name="Stereo">{{cite news | last=Helman| first=Peter| title =Wallows – '1980s Horror Film'| website = Stereogum| date =April 5, 2018| url =https://www.stereogum.com/1989975/wallows-1980s-horror-film/premiere/| access-date =April 9, 2018}}</ref>

On February 1, 2019, Wallows released the single "Are You Bored Yet?" featuring [[Clairo]]. This song is a part of their debut album, [[Nothing Happens (album)|''Nothing Happens'']], released on March 22, 2019. A music video was also released on that day with a cameo of [[Noah Centineo]] and [[Kaitlyn Dever]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wlgmyE5Juzw|title=YouTube|website=www.youtube.com|access-date=February 3, 2019}}</ref>

Wallows embarked on another leg of their Nothing Happens Tour in February 2020, for 15 additional stops.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/live/8531740/wallows-2020-tour-animated-felt-video-treacherous-doctor|title=Wallows Announce 2020 Tour, Release Animated Felt Video for â€˜Treacherous Doctorâ€™|date=September 30, 2019|magazine=Billboard|access-date=March 11, 2020}}</ref> The band partnered with local non-profit charities in each of the cities in this leg of the tour such as Project Lazarus and the LGBT Center of Raleigh.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vCO7ZLuOwaQ|title=Wallows share stories of tour life, a bus FIRE and alternative band names!|author=97X Videos|date=February 21, 2020|website=Youtube}}</ref> Before each show, the band posted a list of donatable items for their attendees to bring (with the incentive of donating being a free Wallows pin) on their social media accounts, and were able to garner a plethora of items to donate after each show.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/wallowsmusic/status/1228055066344673280|title=tour starts today and we're very happy to be partnering with local nonprofits in every city we play. it would mean the world to us if you brought any of the items listed for the charity listed in the city you're coming to see us in. if you do, you'll get a free pin :)pic.twitter.com/tiOTTXx7Sq|last=Wallows|date=February 13, 2020|website=@wallowsmusic|language=en|access-date=March 11, 2020}}</ref> In an interview with [[WSUN (FM)|97X]], a Tampa radio station, [[Dylan Minnette|Minnette]] remarked that the nonprofit organization they partnered with in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, Handy Inc., shared that Wallows fans had brought an equivalent of five months' worth of supplies to the band's show.<ref name=":0" />
