'''Julianna Grace LeBlanc''' ({{IPAc-en|l|ə|ˈ|b|l|ɒ|ŋ|k}}; born December 5, 2004<ref>{{cite web |last1=Adamson |first1=Sydney |title=EXCLUSIVE: Everything you've ever wanted to know about Annie LeBlanc |url=https://www.girlslife.com/trending/celebs/34685/everything-youve-ever-wanted-to-know-about-annie-leblanc |website=Girls' Life |access-date=December 2, 2020 |date=October 30, 2018}}</ref>{{bsn|date=December 2020}}), known professionally as both '''Annie LeBlanc''' and '''Jules LeBlanc''', is an American YouTuber, actress, singer, and former gymnast. In December 2018, ''[[Business Insider]]'' called her one of the "most famous teens in the world."<ref name=bi>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/annie-leblanc-kenzie-ziegler-star-in-brat-holiday-spectacular-analysis-2018-12|title=The 'two biggest female Gen Z stars' are about to be in a holiday YouTube movie together, and an expert says it will be a massive hit|last=McAlone|first=Nathan|website=[[Business Insider]]|date=December 12, 2018|access-date=July 27, 2019}}</ref> She appeared on the YouTube channel Bratayley from the age of four and has since gained an extensive online following of her own.

LeBlanc was on the [[YouTube Premium|YouTube Red]] series ''We Are Savvy'' before going on to work with the [[Brat (digital network)|Brat]] network, starring as Rhyme McAdams in ''[[Chicken Girls]]'' and its [[Chicken Girls: The Movie|spinoff films]] and the titular character of ''A Girl Named Jo''. On television, she plays Lex in the [[Nickelodeon]] [[buddy comedy]] series ''[[Side Hustle]]''.

== Early life ==
Julianna LeBlanc was born to parents Billy and Katie while her father was stationed at [[Fort Gordon]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 31, 2020|title=Annie LeBlanc Wiki, Biography, Age, Career - Influencer Profile|url=https://influencermarketinghub.com/wiki/annie-leblanc/|access-date=October 3, 2020|website=Influencers wiki|language=en-US}}</ref>{{better source needed|reason=Wikis are [[WP:NOTRS]].|date=December 2020}} She has two siblings, a younger sister Hayley Noelle, and an older brother Caleb Logan (July 13, 2002&nbsp;– October 1, 2015). In 2015, Caleb died from a heart condition, [[hypertrophic cardiomyopathy]] at the age of 13.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2015/oct/09/caleb-bratayley-vlogger-death-not-mystery-youtube|title=Caleb Bratayley's death is not a mystery – online sleuths should stand down|last=Parkinson|first=Hannah Jane|date=October 9, 2015|work=The Guardian|access-date=November 2, 2019|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> LeBlanc started taking gymnastics classes when she was 2 years old, and her family started posting videos of her online when she was three.<ref name="Gaurano">{{cite web|url=https://collegecandy.com/2018/01/05/annie-leblanc-net-worth-2018-how-much-salary-income-earnings|title=Annie LeBlanc Net Worth 2018: How Much is Annie Worth Now?|first=Lillie Mae|last=Gaurano|date=January 5, 2018|website=College Candy}}</ref> She trained as a gymnast until August 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nickiswift.com/174835/who-is-annie-leblanc-dating-now|title=Who is Annie LeBlanc dating now?|first=Briana|last=Trusty|date=November 13, 2019|access-date=September 27, 2020|website=Nicki Swift}}</ref>

The LeBlancs previously lived in [[Severna Park, Maryland]] but then moved to [[Los Angeles]], [[California]] so that LeBlanc would be able to start acting and doing music.<ref>{{Cite web|title=EXCLUSIVE: Everything you've ever wanted to know about Annie LeBlanc|url=http://www.girlslife.com/trending/celebs/34685/everything-youve-ever-wanted-to-know-about-annie-leblanc|access-date=October 3, 2020|website=www.girlslife.com}}</ref>

== Career ==
LeBlanc was originally known for her gymnastic videos.<ref name=":3">{{Cite news|url=https://gazettereview.com/2017/08/annie-leblanc-net-worth-wealthy-gymnast-now/|title=Annie LeBlanc Net Worth 2018 – How Rich is the Gymnast – Gazette Review|last=Carter|first=Brooke|date=August 23, 2017|work=Gazette Review|access-date=June 20, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.santafenewmexican.com/life/family/youtube-youth-market-anti--mean-girl-image/article_6aa99f01-5646-563b-9633-24ae679ccc10.html|title=YouTube youth market anti-'mean girl' image|last=Times|first=Hayley KrischerThe New York|work=The Santa Fe New Mexican|access-date=June 20, 2018|language=en}}</ref> Her gymnastic meets and tutorials started being published in 2008 on her YouTube channel, formerly known as Acroanna, when she was 3 years old.<ref name="Gaurano" /> The channel's name was changed in 2017, and now features music videos of Jules Leblanc.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tuko.co.ke/379025-most-famous-sagittarius-youtubers-share-sign-with.html|title=Most famous Sagittarius YouTubers that you share your sign with|first=Gladys Mokeira|last=Obiero|date=September 10, 2020|access-date=September 27, 2020|website=TUKO}}</ref>

LeBlanc is one of the stars of a daily [[vlog]] on the [[YouTube]] channel "Bratayley" (7.3M+ subscribers). The vlog, which was initially just for relatives and started being filmed when LeBlanc was six, follows the lives of the LeBlanc family; no further videos have been posted since November 2019.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://people.com/celebrity/5-things-to-know-about-the-bratayleys/|title=5 Things to Know About the Bratayleys|last1=Heyde|first1=Jennifer|website=People|access-date=May 16, 2018}}</ref> The stars include parents Billy and Katie with their two daughters Annie and Hayley. The name "Bratayley" was initially a nickname for Hayley, combining the word "brat" and the name "Hayley."<ref name=":0" />

LeBlanc had begun to receive attention for her music on her [[TikTok]] handle @julesleblanc.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.capitalfm.com/news/music/ultimate-musically-stars/|title=The 12 musical.ly Stars You Need To Start Following Right Now|work=Capital|access-date=June 19, 2018|language=en}}</ref> In April 2018, she won the [[Shorty Awards|Shorty Award]] for "Muser of the Year".<ref name=":5">{{cite web|title=Annie LeBlanc – The Shorty Awards|url=http://shortyawards.com/10th/annieleblanc|website=shortyawards.com|publisher=Shorty Awards}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thecelebritycafe.com/2018/04/shorty-awards-2018-complete-winners-list/|title=Shorty Awards: 2018 Complete Winners List - TheCelebrityCafe.com|website=thecelebritycafe.com|language=en-US|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.celebritysauce.com/annie-leblanc-wins-big-at-the-shorty-awards/|title=Annie LeBlanc Wins Big at the Shorty Awards – Celebrity Sauce|date=April 17, 2018|work=Celebrity Sauce|access-date=June 19, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

LeBlanc and Hayden Summerall collaborated on a YouTube cover of "Little Do You Know" by [[Alex & Sierra]], which went viral.<ref name=beast>{{cite news|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/how-fans-invented-hannie-the-ultimate-social-media-supercouple|title=How Fans Invented #Hannie, the Ultimate Social Media Supercouple|last=Lorenz|first=Taylor|website=[[The Daily Beast]]|date=December 28, 2017|access-date=July 27, 2019}}</ref> LeBlanc and Summerall's cover appeared at position 48 on the Emerging Artists Billboard Chart.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/annie-leblanc/chart-history/AAF/song/1030763|title=Annie LeBlanc Artist Chart Chart History|work=Billboard|access-date=July 11, 2018}}</ref> LeBlanc and Summerall were cast as leads in ''[[Chicken Girls]]'' despite having no acting experience prior to starring in the series.<ref name=beast /> In August 2017, [[Brat (digital network)|Brat]] released the series on [[YouTube]]. The series features LeBlanc and Summerall's relationship, and focuses on the daily lives of a group of friends and dancers at the faux high school, "Attaway High".<ref>{{cite web|last1=McNary|first1=Dave|title=Digital Creator Brat Unveils Fall Slate of High School-Set Shows|url=https://variety.com/2017/digital/news/digital-creator-brat-fall-slate-set-in-high-school-exclusive-1202532236/|website=Variety|access-date=March 12, 2018|date=August 22, 2017}}</ref> The show's debut episode has received over 10 million YouTube views, and as of May 2018, the second season had between 2 million and 4.5 million views per episode.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/brat-founder-rob-fishman-talks-franchise-building-book-and-movie-deals-2018-4|title=A founder who sold his last startup for ~$50 million is using book and movie deals to boost his new YouTube-centric media company|work=Business Insider|access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref>

In November 2019, Bratayley stopped making family vlogs after 9 years, for the family to focus on other projects.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xOl-WRUwSyY|title=The End of an Era (WK 465)|date=November 29, 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref>
