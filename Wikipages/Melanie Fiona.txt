'''Melanie Fiona Hallim''' (born July 4, 1983) is a Canadian singer and songwriter. She was born and raised in [[Toronto]], Ontario.<ref name="Melanie Fiona: Priceless">[http://www.rapindustry.com/Melanie-Fiona.htm Melanie Fiona: Priceless] {{Webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20120525045251/http://www.rapindustry.com/Melanie-Fiona.htm |date=2012-05-25 }} RapIndustry.com. Retrieved January 2, 2010.</ref> She began her career in 2002 as part of a Canadian girl group trio, who was nominated for a [[Juno Award for R&B/Soul Recording of the Year]] for the group's self-titled album. She went on to be part of The Renaissance (with rapper [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]]), and under the name Syren Hall, recorded some reggae songs.

Hallim's debut solo album ''[[The Bridge (Melanie Fiona album)|The Bridge]]'' was released in 2009, having collaborated with Future Cut, [[Vada Nobles]], [[Stereotypes (producers)|Stereotypes]], J. Phoenix and Peter Wade Keusch. Her debut single, "[[Give It to Me Right]]", was sent to radio stations on February 28, 2009, and peaked at number 20 on the [[Canadian Hot 100]] chart and number 41 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=17797 |title=Give It To Me Right Songfacts |publisher=Songfacts.com |access-date=2011-03-02}}</ref> The second single, "[[It Kills Me]]", became her breakout song on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] where it entered the Top 50, along with topping the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart. The song earned Hallim a [[Grammy Award]] nomination for [[Best Female R&B Vocal Performance]]. The song "[[Monday Morning (Melanie Fiona song)|Monday Morning]]" is her biggest hit in Europe to date. ''The Bridge'' also earned her a [[NAACP Image Award]] nomination for Outstanding New Artist. In 2012, Hallim won two Grammy Awards for [[Grammy Award for Best Traditional R&B Performance|Best Traditional R&B Performance]] and [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Song|Best R&B Song]] for the song "[[Fool for You]]" with [[CeeLo Green]].

==Life and career==

===1983–2006: Early life and career beginnings===

Melanie Fiona Hallim was born on July 4, 1983, in [[Toronto|Toronto, Ontario]], Canada.<ref name="NYDN">{{cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music/2010/03/21/2010-03-21_canadian_soul_singer_melanie_fiona_travels_in_time_between_where_music_has_been_.html |title=Canadian soul singer Melanie Fiona travels in time between where music has been and where it's going |last=Farber |first=Jim |date=March 21, 2010 |work=[[Daily News (New York)|New York Daily News]] |pages=1–2 |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5UefYA?url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music/2010/03/21/2010-03-21_canadian_soul_singer_melanie_fiona_travels_in_time_between_where_music_has_been_.html |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p1114906|pure_url=yes}}|title=Melanie Fiona > Biography|last=Chisling|first=Matthew|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=18 April 2010}}</ref> She is the daughter and second child of [[Guyana|Guyanese]] immigrant parents, who immigrated to Canada in the late 1970s.<ref name="NYDN"/><ref name="MH">{{cite web|url=http://www.miamiherald.com/2010/03/18/1535007/making-music.html|title=Making music in Miami Gardens|last=Burch|first=Audra D.S.|date=March 18, 2010|work=[[The Miami Herald]] |access-date=18 April 2010}}</ref> Her parents are of [[Afro-Guyanese]], [[Indo-Guyanese]], and Portuguese descent.<ref name="Melanie Fiona: Priceless"/>
Her father was a janitor before working in finance, and her mother worked in banking.<ref name="NYDN"/> Hallim began writing songs at age 16.<ref name="FB">{{cite web |url=http://www.fanbolt.com/headline/7212/Interview:_Melanie_Fiona |title=Interview: Melanie Fiona |last=Mahn |first=Jessica |date=July 19, 2009 |publisher=Fanbolt |pages=1–2 |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5V6noO?url=http://www.fanbolt.com/headline/7212/Interview:_Melanie_Fiona |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Living in a music-filled household, Hallim says she always knew music was her passion. Her father was a guitarist in a band and would allow her to sit on the stage when she was younger as he would play. In 2002, Hallim was part of a [[girl group]] called [[X-Quisite]]<ref name="TS">{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/article/756842 |title=Grammy Watch: Melanie Fiona |last=Patch |first=Nick |date=January 28, 2010 |work=[[Toronto Star]] |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5VlMPs?url=http://www.thestar.com/entertainment/article/756842 |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref> alongside [[Nicole Holness]] and [[Nirvana Savoury]]. Signed to [[Warner Music Canada]], the group released their self-titled debut studio album ''X-Quisite'' in 2003. At the [[Juno Awards of 2004|2004 Juno Awards]], the group was nominated as one of five finalists for [[Juno Award for R&B/Soul Recording of the Year|R&B/Soul Recording of the Year]] for the album ''X-Quisite''. The group had a number of singles from the album, notably "Bad Girl" and "No Regrets". The group disbanded in 2005.

She was also in a group called The Renaissance with rapper [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]]. Early in her career, she would perform at [[nightclub]]s in Toronto.<ref name="T-Dot">{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/article/775791 |title=Singer Melanie Fiona saves love for T-Dot |last=Infantry |first=Ashante |date=March 7, 2010 |work=Toronto Star |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5X080z?url=http://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/article/775791 |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref> In 2005, Hallim traveled to [[Los Angeles]], in search of a [[recording contract]]. According to Hallim, record labels "loved" the way she looked and sung, but she "knew that the minute I worked with them or would have signed with them, they would have tried to change me into someone else completely different. That was something that I didn't want to do."<ref name="TS"/> She went on to co-write songs for recording artists [[Rihanna]] and [[Kardinal Offishall]],<ref name="TS"/> working with, amongst others, super producer [[Mike City]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Soul Jones Interview with Melanie Fiona|url=http://souljoneswords.blogspot.co.uk/2011/05/star-quality-melanie-fiona-interview.html|work=Interview|publisher=souljones.com|access-date=2012-12-19}}</ref> Hallim also recorded the [[reggae]] song "Somebody Come Get Me" under the [[stage name]] Syren Hall, which was included in the ''Reggae Gold 2008'' compilation album.<ref name="USAT">{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/printedition/life/20100317/melaniefiona17_st.art.htm |title=The little R& B singer who could—and did |last=Jones |first=Steve |date=March 17, 2010 |work=[[USA Today]] |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5Xt48D?url=http://www.usatoday.com/printedition/life/20100317/melaniefiona17_st.art.htm |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Billboard">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/268223/melanie-fiona |title=Melanie Fiona |last=Varghese |first=Susan |date=July 2, 2009 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=18 April 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140108124255/http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/268223/melanie-fiona |archive-date=January 8, 2014 }}</ref>

===2007–2010: ''The Bridge''===
[[File:Melanie performin in Switzerland.JPG|thumb|right|Fiona performs in [[Bern]], Switzerland at the club Bierhübeli in 2009.]]
Entrepreneur [[Steve Rifkind]] discovered and signed Hallim to [[SRC Records]] and [[Universal Motown]] through Title 9 Productions in 2007.<ref name="NYDN"/><ref name="TS"/> She went on to tour with [[Kanye West]] in his [[Glow in the Dark Tour]]<ref name="USAT"/> Prior to completing her debut album, Hallim met with recording artist [[Jay-Z]] and his friend Tyty. She played them some of her music and was then signed to be managed by [[Roc Nation]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1631223/20100203/fiona__melanie.jhtml |title=Melanie Fiona Sees Her Hard Work Pay Off in 2010 |last=Roberts |first=Steven |date=February 3, 2010 |publisher=[[MTV News]] |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5ZKHxK?url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1631223/20100203/fiona__melanie.jhtml |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref> She released her debut album, ''[[The Bridge (Melanie Fiona album)|The Bridge]]'', in June 2009, which has sold 248,000 copies in the United States.<ref name="USAT"/> ''[[Beyond Race Magazine]]'' ranked Hallim among "50 Emerging Artists" of 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://beyondrace.com/articles/features/2212-melanie-fiona|title=Melanie Fiona|last=W.|first=Damien|work=[[Beyond Race Magazine]]|access-date=18 April 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20100209044349/http://beyondrace.com/articles/features/2212-melanie-fiona|archive-date=9 February 2010}}</ref> Several singles were released from the album, including "[[It Kills Me]]", which topped the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart for 10 weeks and earned her a [[Grammy Award]] nomination at the [[52nd Grammy Awards]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Female R&B Vocal Performance|Best Female R&B Vocal Performance]].<ref name="TS"/><ref name="T-Dot"/> Other singles that were released were "Sad Songs" was released in April 2009 in the UK only as a digital download, with the reggae-tinged songs "Somebody Come Get Me" and "Island Boy" as B-sides. "Sad Songs" did not chart in the UK. "Give It to Me Right" was the first official single from ''The Bridge''. The song peaked at #41 in the UK but failed to chart on the Billboard Hot 100 in the US. It did, however, reach #57 on the U.S. Billboard Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart and at #41.

"Monday Morning" charted in Switzerland and Poland at number 1 and in Austria at number 5. It was released as the third U.S. single, however, "[[Bang Bang (Melanie Fiona song)|Bang Bang]]"<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F6HlYfbaWd0 Melanie Fiona - Bang Bang - YouTube]</ref> was sent to radio as the second Canadian single for Canadian top 40 & hot AC play. Due to "Priceless" not being released in Canada, "Monday Morning" will serve as the fifth single in Canada due to hot adult contemporary radio station CKZZ-FM (Virgin Radio 953) in Vancouver having the song on its playlist. "Ay Yo" was released as the fourth official single from ''The Bridge'', according to Hallim's website and Twitter. The music video premiered on April 12, 2010. "Priceless" will be the fifth U.S. single due to airplay on urban adult contemporary stations. Due to "Priceless" not being released in Canada, "Monday Morning" will be released to Canadian stations as the fifth single, even though "Monday Morning" appeared as the third American single.

Hallim participated in "[[We Are the World 25 for Haiti]]" to benefit after the [[2010 Haiti earthquake]]. She began touring with [[Alicia Keys]] as an [[opening act]] on [[The Freedom Tour]] in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1629361/20100108/fiona__melanie.jhtml |title=Melanie Fiona Looks Forward to 'Empowering' Tour with Alicia Keys |last=Roberts |first=Steven |date=January 8, 2010 |publisher=[[MTV News]] |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5a7Tf7?url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1629361/20100108/fiona__melanie.jhtml |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref>
The album spawned six singles with one being "It Kills Me" which reached the top spot on US R&B chart. Melanie Fiona was also put on the top 100 charts for her song "it kills me" and "4am". [20]

===2011–present: ''The MF Life'' and ''Awake''===
In 2011, Hallim began recording her second studio album. The lead single was titled "Gone and Never Coming Back." The single peaked at 37 on the US R&B chart becoming her second highest peaking song on the R&B chart so far. Later on that year Fiona released her second single, "[[4 AM (Melanie Fiona song)|4 AM]]" was sent to urban stations on August 30, 2011.<ref>[http://www.allaccess.com/urban/future-releases Urban/UAC Future Releases &#124; R&B, Hip Hop, Release Schedule and Street Dates &#124;], Allaccess.com</ref> 4am peaked on the US R&B chart at #8, the song was about a lover who had gone out and was cheating on her.
The album was released on March 20, 2012.<ref name="rap-up.com">[http://www.rap-up.com/2011/09/19/fall-2011-album-release-schedule Rap-Up.com &#124;&#124; Fall 2011 Album Release Schedule], Rap-up.com</ref><ref name="wrongside">{{cite web | url=http://www.rap-up.com/2012/01/13/new-music-melanie-fiona-wrong-side-of-a-love-song/ | title=New Music: Melanie Fiona 'Wrong Side of a Love Song' | publisher=Rap-Up | work=Rap-Up.com | date=January 13, 2012 | access-date=January 14, 2012}}</ref><ref name="mflife">{{cite web | url=http://www.rap-up.com/2012/02/09/melanie-fiona-reveals-release-date-cover-art-for-the-mf-life/#comments | title=Melanie Fiona Reveals Release Date, Cover Art For "The MF Life" | publisher=Rap-Up | work=Rap-Up.com | date=February 9, 2012 | access-date=February 9, 2012}}</ref>

On January 2, 2012, she performed "[[O Canada]]" at the [[2012 NHL Winter Classic|NHL Winter Classic]] between the [[Flyers–Rangers rivalry|New York Rangers and Philadelphia Flyers]] at [[Citizens Bank Park]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nhl.com/ice/news.htm?id=609417|title=Legendary performer LaBelle ready for anthem first|date=January 2, 2012|access-date=January 2, 2012|first=Adam|last=Kimelman|work=NHL.com|publisher=National Hockey League}}</ref>

A newly independent artist, Fiona is currently working on her third album ''Awake''. She released a single "Cold Piece" in 2013. On May 12, 2015, she premiered a new song and lyric video "Bite the Bullet", set to appear on her new album (2016).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/branding/5762980/melanie-fiona-newly-indie-teams-with-pepsi-complex-for-cold-piece|title=Melanie Fiona, Newly Indie, Teams with Pepsi, Complex for 'Cold Piece' Music Video|date=October 17, 2013|work=[[Andrew Hampp]] |access-date=20 October 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/melanie-fiona-discusses-upcoming-album-hot-97 |title=Melanie Fiona Discusses Upcoming Album On Hot 97 |website=Vibe.com |date=2013-07-19 |access-date=2020-03-14}}</ref> "I feel like sometimes people know my songs but haven’t gotten the opportunity to know me" Hallim told the [[Vibe (magazine)|VIBE]]. "I want to make people feel good and I want people to feel that they are not alone, that this album and me as an artist represent a voice for people to feel like 'Yeah, that is the life we’re living and we’re living it together so let’s do our part to move forward positively, and do some really great things while we are alive here" she added. In September, she announced two more tracks for the album: "I Tried" – the first track written for the album, and "Killing Time" – written when she broke up with her boyfriend. Her album ''Awake'' is set for 2016 release.

==Artistry==

===Voice===
Fiona is a [[dramatic soprano]].<ref name=autogenerated1>[http://www.vibe.com/article/vibe-vixen-cover-reveal-melanie-fiona-heartbreaker VIBE Vixen Cover Reveal: Melanie Fiona, The Heartbreaker | Vibe<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Her voice has been referred to as being both "soulful" and "sassy."<ref name=autogenerated1 /> Some have praised her vocals for being "very passionate, but clean and controlled, and not over-the-top singing."<ref>[http://arispeaks.com/2011/11/14/fool-for-you-by-cee-lo-and-melanie-fiona-honoring-the-rbs-greatest-collaborators-past-and-present/ “Fool For You,” By Cee-Lo and Melanie Fiona: Honoring R&B’s Greatest Collaborators Past and Present! | Ari Speaks<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

===Musical style and influences===
Hallim cites [[Mariah Carey]], [[Whitney Houston]], [[Christina Aguilera]], [[Sam Cooke]], [[Sade (band)|Sade]], [[Amy Winehouse]], [[India.Arie]], [[Patsy Cline]] and [[Bob Marley]] as her musical influences.<ref name="MH"/><ref name="FB"/><ref name="Billboard"/> Furthermore, she mentioned [[Brandy Norwood|Brandy]] as major inspiration,<ref>{{YouTube|DMTNJVdu2qY| Melanie Fiona Loves Brandy}}</ref> naming her the only artist that had her starstruck next to [[Prince (musician)|Prince]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y3aNBeFyVFk |title=Melanie Fiona Eyes Drake Collabo, Awed by Brandy and Prince |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2011-04-07 |access-date=2020-03-14}}</ref> She also cited [[Janet Jackson]]'s career as inspirational, who she called an [[cultural icon|icon]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1630659/20100128/blige_mary_j.jhtml |title=Mary J. Blige Honored by Essence for Iconic Career |last=Vena |first=Jocelyn |author2=Marino, Kelly |date=January 28, 2010 |publisher=MTV News |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5dePxu?url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1630659/20100128/blige_mary_j.jhtml |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref> She called [[Lauryn Hill]]'s ''[[The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill]]'' the album that changed her perspective, calling it an "amazing body of work".<ref name="FB"/> She attributes her parents for her vintage sound. Her father was a [[guitarist]] in a band, while her mother would sing around the house. She explained that "[m]y parents were big music lovers and played soul music all the time", who would play [[The Supremes]], [[The Ronettes]] and Cooke, as well as Caribbean music like [[soca music|soca]], [[Calypso music|calypso]] and [[reggae]].<ref name="NYDN"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pbpulse.com/music/music-news/rb/2010/03/15/grammy-nominated-melanie-fiona-mixes-many-cultures-into-her-sound/|title=Grammy-nominated Fiona mixes many cultures into her sound|last=Swan|first=Rhonda|date=March 15, 2010|work=[[The Palm Beach Post]]|access-date=18 April 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120603123619/http://www.pbpulse.com/music/music-news/rb/2010/03/15/grammy-nominated-melanie-fiona-mixes-many-cultures-into-her-sound/|archive-date=3 June 2012}}</ref>

Hallim's occasional songwriting revolves around her personal experiences. She stated that she attempts to "keep [the songs] as universal as possible, unless I am writing about something very personal, even then the themes are universal".<ref name="MH"/> She has received comparisons to [[Amy Winehouse]], [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Macy Gray]], [[Chrisette Michele]], [[Marsha Ambrosius]] and [[Jill Scott]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.shieldsgazette.com/music/Melanie-Fiona-could-be-next.5527010.jp |title=Melanie Fiona could be next big thing |date=August 5, 2009 |work=[[Shields Gazette]] |access-date=18 April 2010 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y5eaNvi?url=http://www.shieldsgazette.com/music/Melanie-Fiona-could-be-next.5527010.jp |archive-date=7 July 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
