==Early life==
Matthew Hauri was born in [[Rochester, Minnesota|Rochester]], [[Minnesota]].<ref name=":0" /> Hauri graduated from [[Mayo High School]] in 2014.<ref>[https://www.medcitybeat.com/news-blog/2019/yung-gravy-hits-the-charts Mayo High grad riding the Gravy train to musical fame<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He attended the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison|University of Wisconsin]] in [[Madison, Wisconsin|Madison]], [[Wisconsin]], and earned his degree in marketing in December 2017.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite news|last=Walters|first=Anna|date=August 7, 2019|title=Former Badger Yung Gravy reveals origin of moniker, career before Lollapalooza|work=The Badger Herald|url=https://badgerherald.com/artsetc/2019/08/07/former-badger-yung-gravy-lollapalooza/|access-date=July 30, 2020}}</ref> While attending college, he began rapping for fun and started his career on [[SoundCloud]] after being inspired by the rise of rappers [[Lil Yachty]] and [[Lil Peep]].<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|last=Mass Appeal|date=May 4, 2018|title=Open Space: Yung Gravy {{!}} Mass Appeal|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?reload=9&v=AYLCrVGCcp0|access-date=July 30, 2020|website=YouTube}}</ref> In 2016, he quit his job and dedicated himself to making music, spending months self-recording songs.<ref name=":0" />

==Style==
Yung Gravy's musical style is a blend of modern [[Trap music (hip hop)|trap music]] with themes inspired by the [[Soul music|soul]] and [[oldies]] movements of the [[1950s]] and [[1960s]],<ref>{{Cite web|work=Power 106 Los Angeles|date=August 29, 2018|title=Yung Gravy On How He Got His Name, Inspiration From Ugly God & Felony Case|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rGuRF8F0ruM|access-date=July 30, 2020|via=YouTube}}</ref><ref name=":7" /> as well as 1970s and 1980s [[Soul music|soul]] and [[funk]] music.<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":8">{{Cite news|last=Lee|first=Ella|date=August 7, 2019|title=Yung Gravy on sampling, disco, M.I.L.F.s and more|work=The DePaulia Online|url=https://depauliaonline.com/42353/artslife/yung-gravy-on-sampling-disco-milfs-and-more/|access-date=July 30, 2020}}</ref> For example, his song "Gravy Train" samples [[Maxine Nightingale]]'s the 1976 song "[[Right Back Where We Started From]]",<ref>{{Cite news|last=Houston|first=Henry|date=October 10, 2019|title=Gravy Train: Trap rapper Yung Gravy talks MILFs, advertising on PornHub and partying in Eugene|work=Eugene Weekly|url=https://www.eugeneweekly.com/2019/10/10/gravy-train/|access-date=July 30, 2020}}</ref> and his breakout hit "Mr. Clean" samples the popular 1954 song "[[Mr. Sandman]]" by [[The Chordettes]].<ref name=":8" />

His musical style has been described as humorous, satirical, and groovy.<ref>[https://observer.case.edu/yung-gravy-talks-about-the-message-he-wants-his-listeners-to-take-away-from-his-music/ Yung Gravy talks about the message he wants his listeners to take away from his music – The Observer<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref><ref name=":3" /><ref name=":4" /><ref name=":5" /><ref name=":7" /><ref>{{Cite news|last=Lundquist|first=William|date=February 19, 2020|title=February hip-hop roundup|work=The Badger Herald|url=https://badgerherald.com/artsetc/2020/02/19/february-hip-hop-roundup/|access-date=July 30, 2020}}</ref> Yung Gravy has cited musicians from multiple genres as inspirations. They include [[Hip hop|hip-hop]] acts such as [[Outkast|OutKast]] and [[Three 6 Mafia]], and [[Soul music|soul]] acts ranging from [[Smokey Robinson]] to [[The Blackbyrds]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=February 3, 2020|title=Yung Gravy Plays At The Signal April 7|work=The Chattanoogan|url=https://www.chattanoogan.com/2020/2/3/403442/Yung-Gravy-Plays-At-The-Signal-April.aspx|access-date=July 30, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|author=Masked Gorilla|date=July 24, 2019|title=Yung Gravy & bbno$ Interview – Masked Gorilla Podcast|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hH3JTPp0L2k|access-date=July 30, 2020|via=YouTube}}</ref>
