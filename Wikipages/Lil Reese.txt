'''Tavares Lamont Taylor''' (born January 6, 1993), known  professionally as '''Lil Reese''', is an American rapper from [[Chicago, Illinois]]. Hailing from Chicago's [[drill music|drill]] scene in the early 2010s, he is known for his collaborations with fellow rappers [[Chief Keef]], [[Fredo Santana]] and [[Lil Durk]]. In 2012, Lil Reese was featured on Chief Keef's single "[[I Don't Like]]", which peaked at number 73 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], peaked at number 20 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart, and peaked at number 15 on the [[Hot Rap Songs]] chart. His debut mixtape, ‘''Don't Like’'' was released later that year. Since the first mixtape release, Lil Reese has gone on to release six mixtapes in total, including the 2013 ‘''[[Supa Savage]]’'' mixtape'','' as well as three EPs, including the 2017 collaborative project ‘''Supa Vultures’'' EP with [[Lil Durk]].

==Career==
Lil Reese gained recognition when he was featured on [[Chief Keef]]'s hit "[[I Don't Like]]", which garnered widespread international attention. He then began to receive popularity through his music videos, including "Us" and "Beef". He then caught the eye of producer [[No I.D.]], who had produced albums or tracks for artists like [[Common (rapper)|Common]], [[Kanye West]], and others, which led to Lil Reese signing with hip hop label [[Def Jam Recordings|Def Jam]].<ref>{{cite web | title=Lil Reese, Chicago Rapper Signs With Def Jam Recordings! | url=http://www.islanddefjam.com/artist/news_single.aspx?nid=11506&artistID=7389 | date=July 2, 2012 | access-date=November 17, 2012 | work=islanddefjam.com }}</ref>

In November 2012, he released a remix for his song "Us" with [[Rick Ross]] and [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]] which later appeared on Rick Ross' mixtape ''[[The Black Bar Mitzvah]]''.<ref name="Rick Ross And Drake remix US">{{cite web | title=Rick Ross And Drake remix US | url=http://globalgrind.com/music/rick-ross-drake-lil-reese-us-remix-new-music | date=October 9, 2012 | access-date=November 17, 2012 | work=Globalgrind.com }}</ref> Lil Reese also created many songs with up-and-coming music producers, such as [[Young Chop]]. He is also featured on [[Juelz Santana]]'s song "Bodies". In January 2013, Lil Reese released a remix to his song "Traffic" featuring [[Young Jeezy]] and [[Twista]]. On September 2, 2013, Lil Reese released his second solo mixtape ''[[Supa Savage]]'', featuring guest appearances from Chief Keef, [[Lil Durk]], [[Fredo Santana]], [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]] and [[Waka Flocka Flame]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/09/lil-reese-releases-supa-savage-mixtape/|title=Lil Reese Releases 'Supa Savage' Mixtape|work=XXL Mag|access-date=November 25, 2015}}</ref>

==Legal issues==
In May 2010, Lil Reese pleaded guilty to burglary charges and was given two years of probation.<ref name="hiphopdxlil">{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23740/title.lil-reese-found-sleeping-in-car-arrested-on-a-warrant|title=Lil Reese Found Sleeping In Car, Arrested On A Warrant|author=HipHopDX|date=April 30, 2013|work=HipHopDX|access-date=November 25, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130702210342/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23740/title.lil-reese-found-sleeping-in-car-arrested-on-a-warrant/|archive-date=July 2, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On October 24, 2012, a video allegedly showing Lil Reese assaulting a woman was posted to the internet.<ref name="suntimes">{{cite news|url=http://www.suntimes.com/news/metro/15966048-418/lil-reese-rapper-involved-in-chief-keef-and-jojo-feud-tied-to-video-beating.html |title=Lil Reese tied to video beating |work=suntimes.com |date=November 2, 2012 |access-date=October 24, 2012 |author=BECKY SCHLIKERMAN |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121027012558/http://www.suntimes.com/news/metro/15966048-418/lil-reese-rapper-involved-in-chief-keef-and-jojo-feud-tied-to-video-beating.html |archive-date=October 27, 2012 }}</ref> On April 28, 2013, Lil Reese was arrested by [[Chicago Police]] on a warrant issued two days earlier, based on criminal trespass to a residence with persons present, battery, and mob action from the video incident from February 2012.<ref name="chicagotribune">{{cite news| url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/news/local/breaking/chi-south-side-rapper-lil-reese-arrested-on-warrant-20130428,0,1948123.story | work=Chicago Tribune | first=Geoff | last=Ziezulewicz | title=South Side rapper Lil Reese arrested on warrant - chicagotribune.com | date=April 29, 2013}}</ref><ref name="hiphopdxlil"/>

On June 23, 2013, Lil Reese was arrested in Chicago and charged with [[motor vehicle theft]] after an incident on April 13, 2013, where he was not able to provide proof of ownership for a BMW 750Li.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24431/title.lil-reese-arrested-charged-with-auto-theft-|title=Lil Reese Arrested, Charged With Auto Theft|author=HipHopDX|date=June 24, 2013|work=HipHopDX|access-date=November 25, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130703170735/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24431/title.lil-reese-arrested-charged-with-auto-theft-|archive-date=July 3, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> However, the charge was later dropped. On July 13, 2013, Lil Reese was arrested again in Chicago for [[marijuana]] possession, a violation of his probation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24676/title.lil-reese-arrested-for-drug-possession|title=Lil Reese Arrested For Drug Possession|author=HipHopDX|date=July 14, 2013|work=HipHopDX|access-date=November 25, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150524193223/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24676/title.lil-reese-arrested-for-drug-possession|archive-date=May 24, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Personal life ==

=== 2019 shooting ===
On November 11, 2019, Lil Reese was shot in the neck and critically wounded<ref name=TMZ>{{cite news |url=https://www.tmz.com/2021/05/15/rapper-lil-reese-shot-again-chicago/ |title=Rapper Lil Reese Grazed in Eye During Shooting&nbsp;... |website=TMZ |date=May 15, 2021 |access-date=May 16, 2021 }}</ref> at a busy intersection in the area of Markham and Country Club Hills. Country Club Hills police responded to 167th Street and Pulaski Road around 2:30&nbsp;p.m. Witnesses told police Reese was pursued by a driver of another car during a chase. Witnesses reported hearing as many as 12 gunshots during the chase. The driver of that vehicle got out of his car and shot the man with what witnesses called a small rifle and then fled the scene.<ref>{{cite web|title=Chicago rapper Lil Reese critically wounded in shooting at Country Club Hills intersection|url=https://wgntv.com/2019/11/11/police-respond-to-reports-of-shooting-in-country-club-hills/|last=Negovan|first=Tom|date=November 11, 2019|work=WGN|access-date=November 11, 2019}}</ref>

On November 18, Reese sent out information about being released from the hospital and that he survived the shooting and is "Alive and Well".<ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Reese Says He Had 'Hella Luck' Discharge From Hospital Following Shooting|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8543926/lil-reese-gives-update-shooting|date=November 18, 2019|work=Billboard|access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref> A day after leaving the hospital, he released a new song "Come Outside".<ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Reese Drops First Song Since Being Shot, 'Come Outside': Listen|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8544132/lil-reese-first-song-since-being-shot-come-outside|date=November 19, 2019|work=Billboard|access-date=November 19, 2019}}</ref>

=== Twitter racism controversy ===
In March 2020, during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Lil Reese posted a controversial message on Twitter: "Chinese people nasty asl man got the whole [world] fuck up".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lil Reese Sends Racist Tweet About Coronavirus|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-reese-sends-racist-tweet-about-coronavirus-news.106107.html|website=HotNewHipHop|language=en|access-date=2020-05-21}}</ref>{{Better source needed|reason=HotNewHipHop is not a reputable or reliable source.|date=April 2021}} While thousands supported his statement, others condemned him for racism. Reese faced immediate backlash for his comment; many asked him to delete the tweet. After his Twitter account was suspended, he went on Instagram to post a screenshot of the official violation notification from Twitter with the captions, "Lol Look how the Chinese people did my Twitter".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lil Reese Twitter Suspended Over Racist Coronavirus Tweet|url=https://urbanislandz.com/2020/03/16/lil-reese-twitter-suspended-over-racist-coronavirus-tweet/|last=Dowlatt|first=Orlando|date=2020-03-16|website=Urban Islandz|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-21}}</ref>{{Better source needed|reason=Urban Islands is not a reputable or reliable source.|date=April 2021}}

=== 2021 shooting ===
On May 15, 2021, Reese and two other men were injured in a shootout at a parking garage in [[Chicago]] and were taken to Northwestern Memorial Hospital.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Callas |first1=Brad |title=Lil Reese Reportedly Shot in Chicago |url=https://www.complex.com/music/lil-reese-reportedly-shot-in-chicagos-river-north-neighborhood |website=Complex |date=May 15, 2021 |access-date=15 May 2021}}</ref> His eye was grazed by a gunshot; he and another of the men, who was shot in the knee, were later listed in fair to good condition, and the third in critical condition with multiple wounds to the torso.<ref name=TMZ/> The shooting was reportedly over a stolen [[Dodge Durango]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/news/2021/5/15/22437859/lil-reese-reportedly-shot-in-chicago|title=Lil Reese and two others reportedly shot in Chicago |last=Harris |first=Christopher |website=Revolt |date=May 15, 2021 |access-date=May 15, 2021}}</ref>
