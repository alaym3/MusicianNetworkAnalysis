'''Hypocrisy''' is a Swedish [[death metal]]<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|last=Harris |first=Chris |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1505778/20050714/index.jhtml?headlines=true |title=Metal File: Motley Crue, Soulfly, Nile & More - News Story &#124; Music, Celebrity, Artist News &#124; MTV News |website=MTV.com |date=14 July 2005 |access-date=18 October 2011 |quote=Swedish death-metal veterans '''Hypocrisy''' will release their 10th studio album, ''Virus'', on September 19.}}</ref> band formed in October 1991<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hypocrisy.cc/home/biography/|title=Biography - Hypocrisy Official Website|website=Hypocrisy.cc|access-date=18 July 2018}}</ref> in [[Ludvika]] by [[Peter Tägtgren]].

==History==
After spending three years in [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.faceculture.nl/pain/index.htm# |title=Peter Tägtgren Video interview |website=FaceCulture.nl |date=22 March 2007 |access-date=16 October 2007 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071010005847/http://www.faceculture.nl/pain/index.htm |archive-date=10 October 2007 }}</ref> in 1990, founder [[Peter Tägtgren]] returned to his homeland of Sweden, to form his own band. Hypocrisy's early releases were noted as being well-executed death metal, but panned for not being particularly innovative. That criticism waned as the band matured, cultivating a more distinctive sound as well as employing an introspective lyrical approach. Later releases have a more atmospheric sound, with lyrical exploration of aliens and abduction experiences.

Tägtgren's experience as a producer may also have led to the band's change in musical direction, as he is more closely involved with many other bands while producing their albums.

On 17 November 2009, Hypocrisy cancelled their U.S. tour. After cancelling earlier in 2009, Hypocrisy confirmed a North American tour for 2010. Joining Hypocrisy on the "Long Time, No Death" tour were [[Scar Symmetry]], [[Hate (band)|Hate]], [[Blackguard (band)|Blackguard]] and [[Swashbuckle (band)|Swashbuckle]]. From January to March 2010, they toured Germany, Eastern Europe and [[Scandinavia]], finishing in [[Moscow]]. Their North American tour took place in May 2010.

On 14 October 2011, Hypocrisy released a DVD called ''Hell Over Sofia - 20 Years of Chaos and Confusion''. It contains a full concert from the "Long Time, No Death" tour. The DVD also contains a one and a half-hour documentary about the band.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalstorm.net/events/news_comments.php?news_id=14343 |title=Hypocrisy - New DVD Announced |website=Metalstorm.net |date=11 August 2011 |access-date=18 October 2011}}</ref> On 10 November 2011, Mikael Hedlund announced writing material for a new studio album during an interview with ''Metal Shock Finland''{{'}}s chief editor, Mohsen Fayyazi. Mikael tells ''Metal Shock Finland'': {{cquote|It's too early to say what it's going to sounds like, but all I can say is that it's not gonna be any change of style. It will be a classic HYPOCRISY album without [any] compromises. We already got some material and we're really looking forward to enter the studio again. We don't know yet when it will [be], but hopefully after the summer 2012. Nothing is confirmed yet. We're doing the Metalfest tour [of Europe] in May/June plus some other festivals. That's the plans at the moment.}}<ref>{{cite web|title=HYPOCRISY Working On New Material|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=165961|website=Blabbermouth.net|date = 10 November 2011|access-date=4 December 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Fayyazi|first=Mohsen|title=Interview With Hypocrisy: Our new DVD is including a documentary and finally we can tell the true story of Hypocrisy.|date=10 November 2011|url=http://metalshockfinland.com/2011/11/10/interview-with-hypocrisy-our-new-dvd-is-including-a-documentary-and-finally-we-can-tell-the-true-story-of-hypocrisy/|publisher=Metal Shock Finland|access-date=4 December 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=HYPOCRISY's Mikael Hedlund - "We're Focusing On Writing A New Album"|url=http://www.bravewords.com/news/172398|website=Bravewords.com|access-date=4 December 2012}}</ref>

In early 2012 the band began to write new material for a follow up to ''[[A Taste of Extreme Divinity]]''. On 21 December 2012 Hypocrisy announced that the title of the upcoming album would be [[End of Disclosure]].<ref>{{cite news|title=HYPOCRISY's Peter Tägtgren Discusses Writing Process For End Of Disclosure Album; Audio Interview Posted|url=http://www.bravewords.com/news/199602|access-date=18 January 2014|newspaper=Bravewords|date=27 February 2013}}</ref> It was released on 22 March 2013.

Hypocrisy's thirteenth studio album, and first in eight years, ''Worship'' was released on November 26, 2021. The album was preceded by a music video for the first single "Chemical Whore".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/hypocrisy-to-release-worship-album-in-november-chemical-whore-music-video-available/|title=HYPOCRISY To Release 'Worship' Album In November; 'Chemical Whore' Music Video Available|website=[[Blabbermouth.net]]|access-date=September 10, 2021|date=September 10, 2021}}</ref>

== Musical style and lyrical themes ==
Musically, the band started off with a traditional death metal sound on their early albums, but soon turned into a [[melodic death metal]] band.<ref name="dailycollegian1">[http://dailycollegian.com/2005/05/10/on-the-road-at-the-new-england-metal-festival-part-2-continued/ "On the road at the New England Metal Festival: part 2 (continued)"]. Dailycollegian.com. 10 May 2005. Retrieved 2012-01-04.</ref><ref name="intelligencewire1">{{cite web|url=http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-12680934_ITM |title=Ohio State U.: CD REVIEW: In Flames scorches with 'Come Clarity'. &#124; The America's Intelligence Wire |website=Accessmylibrary.com |date=10 February 2006 |access-date=18 October 2011}}</ref> Their early lyrics – written by original vocalist [[Emperor Magus Caligula|Masse Broberg]] – dealt with [[anti-Christian]] themes and [[Satanism]]. However, the band later chose to focus on themes such as the [[paranormal]] and [[extraterrestrial life|extraterrestrials]]. Contrary to what some might believe, Magnus did not leave because of a shift in lyrical content, but because of a cracked eardrum during their 1st European tour (he later went on to [[black metal]] band [[Dark Funeral]] as [[Emperor Magus Caligula]]). Their tenth album, ''[[Virus (Hypocrisy album)|Virus]]'', contains themes more typical of death metal such as violence, the horrors of reality, insanity, torture, war, drug addiction, and emotional strife. Their twelfth album ''[[End of Disclosure]]'' deals with conspiracy and anti-Illuminati themes.
