'''Jacob Rabon IV''' (born December 19, 1995), better known by his online pseudonym '''Alpharad''', is an American [[YouTuber]], [[esports]] personality, and musician. He is best known for his gaming videos on the ''[[Super Smash Bros.]]'' series along with his participation in the [[fighting game community]] as a tournament organizer.

== Early life ==
Rabon was born on December 23, 1995, in [[Los Angeles]], California. He gained an interest in filmmaking around the fifth grade, uploading comedic videos for his friends, one of his first videos being ''Major O's''.<ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dTbuEm3Xbs0|title=Major O's|date=December 22, 2008|author=Rabon, Jacob, IV|type=video|publisher=JRabonProductions|access-date=August 4, 2021|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> Later, he would take video editing and cinematography classes in high school.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Groot|first=Justin|date=October 11, 2016|title=How to Play Anything: An Interview with Alpharad|url=https://killscreen.com/themeta/play-anything-interview-alpharad/|url-status=live|access-date=January 22, 2019|website=The Meta}}</ref> Rabon attended the [[University of Oklahoma]] while making [[YouTube]] videos as a full-time job.<ref name=":1"/><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Bonnet|first=Siandhara|date=February 4, 2019|title=Oklahoma City band collaborates with former OU student, YouTuber|url=https://www.oudaily.com/culture/oklahoma-city-band-collaborates-with-former-ou-student-youtuber/article_8a682cde-28c1-11e9-9d11-e7ce63d13c42.html|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=OUDaily}}</ref> His original goal was to be a writer, director, or editor of some kind, but soon learned that in being someone else's editor he couldn't have as much creative freedom as he could making his own content.<ref name=":1" />

== Career ==

=== YouTube ===
Rabon created his main YouTube channel on January 13, 2014. Since its creation, he has made various gaming videos on his channel, Alpharad, including the ''Super Smash Bros.'' series, ''[[Pokémon]]'', ''[[Sonic the Hedgehog]]'', and ''[[Fall Guys]]'', as well as other Nintendo titles.<ref name=":2"/><ref name=":7">{{Cite web|last=Haime|first=Jackson|date=August 14, 2020|title=Fall Guys: Is Yellow Team Really The Worst?|url=https://screenrant.com/fall-guys-yellow-team-worst-bad-why-red-blue/|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=[[Screen Rant]]}}</ref><ref name=":11">{{Cite web|last=Weiss|first=Geoff|date=September 1, 2020|title=CAA Signs Prominent Gamers Alpharad And The Completionist (Exclusive)|url=https://www.tubefilter.com/2020/09/01/caa-signs-alpharad-and-the-completionist/|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=[[Tubefilter]]}}</ref> He has also dabbled in [[speedrunning]], specifically in a video of him intensely playing through a ''[[Super Mario 64]]'' [[first-person shooter]] mod, as well as being a retired ''[[Super Mario Odyssey]]'' speedrunner.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=Hernandez|first=Patricia|date=March 1, 2021|title=The latest Super Mario 64 speedrun trend gives Mario a gun|url=https://www.polygon.com/2021/3/1/22307966/super-mario-64-fps-gun-speedrun-alpharad-youtube|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=[[Polygon (website)|Polygon]]}}</ref> Rabon's main YouTube channel has reached over 2 million subscribers as of August 2020.<ref name=":7"/><ref name=":11"/>

=== Esports ===
Rabon has been involved in esports as a tournament organizer and affiliate with other prominent esports personalities. In January 2020, he participated in a ''Pokémon'' VGC tournament with fellow competitive ''Super Smash Bros.'' players ESAM, [[Mew2King]], and [[Ludwig Ahgren]] amongst other content creators.<ref name=":10">{{Cite web|last=Bartlett|first=Eric|date=January 14, 2020|title=Eight Professional Super Smash Bros. Players and Personalities Give Pokemon VGC a Try in Wolfe Glick's Invitational Tournament|url=https://thegamehaus.com/pokemon/eight-professional-super-smash-bros-players-and-personalities-give-pokemon-vgc-a-try-in-wolfe-glicks-invitational-tournament/2020/01/14/|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=The Game Haus}}</ref>

Two months later, in the midst of the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Rabon co-hosted five online tournaments called the “Quarantine Series” with fellow YouTuber [[Cr1TiKaL]],<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last=Michael|first=Cale|date=March 24, 2020|title=Alpharad, Cr1TiKaL launch Super Smash Bros. Ultimate Quarantine Series|url=https://dotesports.com/fgc/news/alpharad-cr1tikal-launch-super-smash-bros-ultimate-quarantine-series|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=[[Gamurs|Dot Esports]]|publisher=[[Gamurs|GAMURS Group]]}}</ref> which consisted of two events, Minors and Majors, offering $5,000 and $10,000 in prizes, respectively.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|last=Kelly|first=Michael|date=May 3, 2020|title=Sonix defeats MKLeo to win Cr1tikal & Alpharad's Smash Ultimate major|url=https://www.dexerto.com/smash/sonix-wins-cr1tikal-and-alpharads-smash-ultimate-major-1361268/|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=Dexerto}}</ref><ref name=":6">{{Cite web|last=Tate|first=Dylan|date=March 25, 2020|title=Quarantine Series to feature online Smash Bros. tournaments|url=https://daily.upcomer.com/quarantine-series-to-feature-online-smash-bros-tournaments/|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=Upcomer}}</ref> The major invitationals featured top ''Super Smash Bros.'' players such as [[MKLeo]], [[Samsora]], [[Nairo (gamer)|Nairo]] and [[Marss]],<ref name=":6"/> and was streamed on both Cr1TiKaL's [[Twitch.tv|Twitch]] channel and Rabon's YouTube channel.<ref name=":4"/>

In 2018, Rabon purchased a monthly stake for the esports organization, Panda Global, becoming their content director and a minority shareholder.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|last=Observer|first=The Esports|date=June 28, 2018|title=Panda Global Adds YouTuber Alpharad as First Equity Partner|url=https://esportsobserver.com/youtuber-invests-panda-global/|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=The Esports Observer}}</ref><ref name=":9">{{Cite web|last=Khan|first=Imad|date=January 26, 2019|title=Panda Global, the Esports Team That's Saying No to VC's|url=https://esportsobserver.com/panda-global-ceo-interview/|url-status=live|access-date=April 22, 2021|website=The Esports Observer}}</ref> He had previously expressed his desire to hold more weight in the organization.<ref name=":1"/>

In October 2021, Rabon hosted a ''[[Nickelodeon All-Star Brawl]]'' charity tournament with Panda Global member Coney for [[The Breast Cancer Research Foundation]]. The tournament featured content creators such as Cr1TiKaL, Ludwig Ahgren, [[Trihex]] and [[Jaiden Animations]], and ultimately raised over $100,000.<ref name="Nickelodean All-Star Tournament">{{Cite web|last=Michael|first=Cale|date=September 28, 2021|title=Panda to host Nickelodeon All-Star Brawl charity event featuring Ludwig, Cr1TiKaL, and more|url=https://dotesports.com/fgc/news/panda-to-host-nickelodeon-all-star-brawl-charity-event-featuring-ludwig-cr1tikal-and-more|url-status=live|access-date=October 8, 2021|website=[[Gamurs|Dot Esports]]|publisher=[[Gamurs|GAMURS Group]]}}</ref><ref name="Nickelodeon All-Star Tournament 2">{{Cite web|last=Michael|first=Cale|date=October 6, 2021|title=ConnorEatsPants wins Panda All-Star Nickvitational, event raises $100,000 for The Breast Cancer Research Foundation|url=https://dotesports.com/news/connoreatspants-wins-panda-all-star-nickvitational-event-raises-100000-for-the-breast-cancer-research-foundation|url-status=live|access-date=October 8, 2021|website=[[Gamurs|Dot Esports]]|publisher=[[Gamurs|GAMURS Group]]}}</ref>

=== Music ===
Rabon is also a musician who formed a band named Ace of Hearts.<ref name=":12" /><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|author=Rabon, Jacob, IV|date=February 2, 2020|title=Alpharad in 2020 ❤️|url=https://www.twitlonger.com/show/n_1sr4pus|url-status=live|access-date=August 4, 2021|website=TwitLonger}}</ref><ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1224037446196789248|user=Alpharad|title=tl;dr - Making less content in 2020 (going from 5-7 weekly uploads to like 2-3) - Will probably never ever stop making content, I love it too much - Focusing on music / mystery project - My band (Ace of Hearts) is releasing an album (Scorpion Queen) in May of 2020 - #LTP|author=Rabon, Jacob, IV|access-date=August 16, 2021}}</ref> Their first single was "Fool for You", which was released on April 1, 2020. The band's second EP, ''Scorpion Queen'', was released on December 13, 2020.<ref name=":0" /> The band's single "Lights Off" was released on March 31, 2021. Ace of Hearts' most recent single, "Deadbeat Boulevard", released on November 11, 2021. There is an album currently in the works, entitled "Frozen in Time", set to be released on December 10, 2021.

=== Copypasta Theatre ===
On June 27, 2021, Rabon announced a one-time [[pop-up show]] to be held the very same day. He and thirteen other creators dramatically read [[copypasta]]s to a live audience at the [[Black box theater|Black Box theatre]] in [[Los Angeles]]. Stage guests included Daniel Thrasher, Adriana Figueroa, Drumsy, Mari Norwood, Jules Conroy, Phil Visu, Cristina Vee Valenzuela, [[Ross O'Donovan]], Dan Fulling, C. J. Ya, and Rabon’s project manager Deanna Gowland. The viral video of the performance involved a segment where several of gaming streamer [[Ninja (gamer)|Ninja]]’s [[Twitter]] posts were read aloud; Ninja later shared the video on his Twitter page.<ref name=":16">{{Cite web|last=McGuire|first=Keegan|date=July 15, 2021|title=Ninja's Tweets Have Never Seemed So Dramatic|url=https://www.svg.com/462881/ninjas-tweets-have-never-seemed-so-dramatic/|url-status=live|access-date=July 20, 2021|website=[[ZergNet|SVG]]}}</ref><ref name="Copypasta Theatre video">{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GWnoJav4Rs0|title="Copypasta Theatre"|date=July 12, 2021|author=Rabon, Jacob, IV|type=video|publisher=Alpharad|access-date=July 20, 2021|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>

=== Romeo and Juliet ===
On September 12, 2021 Rabon announced and performed a pop up show where he and friends performed 
[[William Shakespeare|Shakespeare]]'s "[[Romeo and Juliet]]", with no rehearsals nor script at the same [[Black box theater|black box theatre]] as the Copypasta Theatre. In lieu of a script, English major and collaborator Josniffy compiled a slideshow, containing basic descriptions of each scene. The cast included Jacob as Romeo, Deanna Gowland as Juliet, Anthony Potero (Anthpo) as Mercutio, [[Jaiden Animations|Jaiden Dittfach]] as Benvolio, Cristina Vee as Tybalt, Jules Conroy (FamilyJules) as Lord Capulet, Adriana Figueroa as Lord Montague, Dashell "Altrive" as Lawrence, Caleb Swindell as Nurse, Eric Morino (Pointcrow) as Paris, C.J Ya (cjya) as Peter, Johnathan Garrett as the Apothecary, and 8illy as Prince Escolus. The show was shot by Thomas Feezel and Josh Fraser, and hair and make up were done by Sophia La Frazia. The show features a reference to [[We the Kings|We The Kings]]' 2007 song "[[Check Yes Juliet|Check Yes Juliet]]".

== Personal life ==
In 2018, Rabon married his long-time girlfriend, Fiorella Zoll. They were living in their home state, Oklahoma, before moving to Los Angeles alongside close friend and frequent collaborator JoSniffy. On June 15, 2020, Rabon [[Coming out|came out]] as [[Bisexuality|bisexual]].<ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BnJTk5JoXxQ|title=Super Sir Mario World|date=June 15, 2020|author=Rabon, Jacob, IV|type=video|publisher=Alpharad Deluxe|access-date=June 18, 2021|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> Rabon announced his divorce to his wife Fiorella on October 20, 2021, stating that they had been separated for some time before the announcement.<ref>{{Cite twitter|last=Rabon|first=Jacob|user=alpharad|number=1450856460649259013|title=💙|access-date=2021-10-26}}</ref>
