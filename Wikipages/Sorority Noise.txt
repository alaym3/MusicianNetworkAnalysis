'''Sorority Noise''' was an American [[rock music|rock]] band from [[Hartford, Connecticut]]. The band consists of members from the bands [[Old Gray]] (Boucher and Singer, and Ackerman), [[Prawn (band)|Prawn]] (McKenna), Small Circle (Boucher, Singer, and Ackerman), and En Route (Singer).

On March 2, 2018, Sorority Noise announced that they would enter hiatus following the conclusion of their Spring 2018 tour.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/03/sorority-noise-hiatus/|title=Sorority Noise Announce Hiatus|date=2018-03-02|work=Spin|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref>

==History==

=== Formation and beginnings (2013–2016) ===
Sorority Noise was formed in 2013 by vocalist Cameron Boucher of the screamo band [[Old Gray]]. Joined by Old Gray drummer Charlie Singer, Prawn bassist Ryan McKenna, and second guitarist Adam Ackerman, the band formed in Hartford, Connecticut.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/sorority-noise-mn0003316230/biography|title=Sorority Noise {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref> They released their first cassette, titled ''Young Luck'', in 2013 via [[Broken World Media]].<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20150218120459/http://funeralsounds.com/post/64229940005/sorority-noise-young-luck-hartford] </ref> In May 2014, the band released their debut album, titled ''Forgettable'', via Dog Knights Productions and [[Broken World Media]].

In March 2014, the band supported three dates of McKenna's other band Prawn's headlining tour alongside Old Gray. In June 2014, the band supported Modern Baseball on their US headlining tour alongside Tiny Moving Parts, The Hotelier, and Old Gray on select dates. In October 2014, the band played a small string of co-headlining dates on the East Coast with the band Pinegrove. In November 2014, the band released a split [[extended play]] with [[Somos (band)|Somos]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Shultz|first1=Brian|title=Somos/Sorority Noise - Split EP|url=http://www.altpress.com/reviews/entry/somos_sorority_noise_split_ep|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=18 February 2015}}</ref>

In February 2015, Sorority Noise opened for [[You Blew It!]] on select shows, and in the spring, they supported the band Hostage Calm at their final show in Wallingford, Connecticut as well as the band [[Fireworks (punk band)|Fireworks]] on their final tour before their hiatus. Their sophomore album ''[[Joy, Departed]]'' was released on June 16, 2015 via [[Topshelf Records]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sharp|first1=Tyler|title=Sorority Noise to release new album through Topshelf Records|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/sorority_noise_to_release_new_album_through_topshelf_records|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=18 February 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Hudelson|first1=Molly|title=Sorority Noise to release sophomore album on Topshelf Records this year|url=http://www.axs.com/sorority-noise-to-release-sophomore-album-on-topshelf-records-this-yea-37861|publisher=AXS|accessdate=18 February 2015}}</ref> In the summer of 2015, Sorority Noise opened for [[Motion City Soundtrack]] during their ''Commit This to Memory'' 10-year anniversary tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/motion_city_soundtrack_tap_the_spill_canvas_sorority_noise_for_commit_this|title=Motion City Soundtrack tap the Spill Canvas, Sorority Noise for ‘Commit This To Memory’ summer tour - News - Alternative Press|website=Alternative Press|language=en|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref> They would also open for several other bands including [[Knuckle Puck]] and [[Modern Baseball]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/seaway_sorority_noise_head_north_announced_for_knuckle_puck_headliner|title=Seaway, Sorority Noise, Head North announced for Knuckle Puck headliner - News - Alternative Press|website=Alternative Press|language=en|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/sororitynoise/photos/a.173563872833951.1073741828.172965089560496/442060159317653/?type=3&theater|title=Sorority Noise|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref> [[Alternative Press (magazine)|''Alternative Press'']] named them as one of the "100 Bands You Need to Know in 2015".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.topshelfrecords.com/roster/sorority-noise|title=Topshelf Records|website=Topshelf Records|language=en|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref>

Sorority Noise released an EP titled ''It Kindly Stopped For Me'' on April 22, 2016. The EP was announced during a [[Nottingham]] show and promoted with the song "Either Way".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2016/02/22/sorority-noise-either-way-premiere|title=Sorority Noise open their hearts on 'Either Way'|website=DIY|language=en|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref> In support of the EP, they opened for [[Citizen (band)|Citizen]] and [[Bayside (band)|Bayside]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/bayside_announce_new_album_tour_with_the_menzingers_sorority_noise|title=Bayside announce new album, tour with the Menzingers, Sorority Noise - News - Alternative Press|website=Alternative Press|language=en|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/citizen_announce_spring_tour_with_turnover_sorority_noise_and_milk_teeth|title=Citizen announce spring tour with Turnover, Sorority Noise and Milk Teeth - News - Alternative Press|website=Alternative Press|language=en|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref> In October 2016, they released a split single with [[The World Is A Beautiful Place & I Am No Longer Afraid To Die]] titled "Leaf Ellis/Smoke & Felt" via [[Triple Crown Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://triplecrownrecords.merchdirect.com/products/58857-leaf-ellis-smoke-felt|title=Triple Crown Records - Leaf Ellis/Smoke & Felt|publisher=Triple Crown Records via Merch Direct|accessdate=13 November 2016}}</ref> Later that month, Boucher announced his own independent record label, Flower Girl.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/thecamboucher/status/790961126624391168|title=@cameronboucher: "I'm announcing out my first record on my new label tomorrow, this has been a long time coming, check out @flowergirlrcrds at 1pm"|publisher=Twitter|accessdate=13 November 2016}}</ref> The label re-released Sorority Noise's debut ''Forgettable''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.flowergirlrecords.com/products/587692-sorority-noise-forgettable|title=Sorority Noise - Forgettable|website=Flower Girl Records|language=en|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref> The band embarked on their first United States headlining tour in the fall of 2016, with support from [[Ratboys]] and [[Free Throw (band)|Free Throw]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2016/08/sorority-noise-announce-fall-tour-and-split-ep/|title=Sorority Noise announce fall tour and split EP|date=2016-08-31|work=Consequence of Sound|access-date=2018-04-11|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== ''You're Not As ____ As You Think'' (2017–2018) ===
The band released their third album, ''[[You're Not As _____ As You Think]],'' on March 17, 2017 via Triple Crown Records. The album's lead single, "No Halo", was released on January 19, 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2017/01/sorority-noise-drop-first-single-no-halo-for-new-a.html|title=Sorority Noise Drop First Single, "No Halo," Off New Album|work=pastemagazine.com|access-date=2018-04-11|language=en}}</ref> Originally slated to support [[Modern Baseball]]'s Spring 2017 tour, Sorority Noise announced a cross-country headlining tour with support from The Obsessives, Forth Wanderers, Sinai Vessel, Shannen Mose, and Citizen frontman Mat Kerekes after Modern Baseball unexpectedly broke up.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/modern-baseball-kevin-devine-sorority-noise-the-obsessives-announce-tour/|title=Modern Baseball, Kevin Devine, Sorority Noise, & The Obsessives announce tour|website=BrooklynVegan|language=en-US|access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref> 

On October 12, 2017, the band announced a 7" single called "Alone" as a followup to ''[[You're Not As _____ As You Think]]'.''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/sororitynoise/photos/a.173563872833951.1073741828.172965089560496/717719381751728/?type=3&theater|title=Sorority Noise|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2017-10-13}}</ref> It was announced that the word "alone" is supposed to fill in the gap of the album's title.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1966813/stream-sorority-noises-surprise-new-alone-7/music/album-stream/|title=Stream Sorority Noise’s Surprise New Alone 7″|date=2017-10-12|work=Stereogum|access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref> In support of this single, the band played a three-date headlining tour in Australia with support from [[Yours and Owls]] Festival, and the band supported [[Citizen (band)|Citizen]] on their U.S. Fall 2017 tour alongside Great Grandpa.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.secretsounds.com/tours/sorority-noise-tour-2017/|title=Sorority Noise announce Australian tour 2017 {{!}} Secret Sounds|work=Secret Sounds|access-date=2018-04-12|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/citizen_as_you_please_tour|title=Citizen announce ‘As You Please’ tour with Sorority Noise and Great Grandpa - News - Alternative Press|website=Alternative Press|language=en|access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref> On December 8, 2017, the band supported [[Foxing (band)|Foxing]] at the 20th anniversary showcase of [[Triple Crown Records]], playing their third album in entirety.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/foxing-sorority-noise-moving-mountains-shortly-celebrated-20-yrs-of-triple-crown-pics/|title=Foxing, Sorority Noise, Moving Mountains & Shortly celebrated 20 yrs of Triple Crown (pics)|website=BrooklynVegan|language=en-US|access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref>  

On February 21, 2018, the band announced a complete re-working of ''You're Not As _____ As You Think'' entitled ''YNAAYT'' on the one-year anniversary of the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sororitynoise.limitedrun.com/products/611760-ynaayt|title=YNAAYT|website=Sorority Noise|language=en|access-date=2018-02-22}}</ref> The record features all songs from their previous LP reworked with strings, except for  "Where Are You?" and "New Room", which were replaced with a cover of [[Leonard Cohen]]'s "Chelsea Hotel #2" and a new song, "Windowwww", respectively.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/02/sorority-noise-ynaayt-rework-album-chelsea-hotel-no-2-cover/|title=Sorority Noise Announce YNAAYT Rework Album, Cover “Chelsea Hotel No. 2”|date=2018-02-21|work=Spin|access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref> 

In March 2018, Sorority Noise announced that they would be going on hiatus following their Spring 2018 tour due to frontman Cam Boucher's mental health.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/03/sorority-noise-hiatus/|title=Sorority Noise Announce Hiatus|date=2018-03-02|work=Spin|access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref> However, the band cancelled some of their tour dates with [[The Wonder Years (band)|The Wonder Years]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/sorority-noise-member-denies-rape-accusation-2289536|title=Sorority Noise cancel shows as member denies rape accusation - NME|date=2018-04-11|work=NME|access-date=2018-04-12|language=en-US}}</ref> after Boucher was accused of sexual assault on social media site [[Reddit]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/sorority-noise-cancel-tour-following-rape-allegation/|title=Sorority Noise Cancel Tour Following Rape Allegation {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2018-04-13}}</ref> Boucher denied the allegations.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1990799/sorority-noises-cameron-boucher-denies-rape-accusation/news/|title=Sorority Noise’s Cameron Boucher Denies Rape Accusation|date=2018-04-10|work=Stereogum|access-date=2018-04-12}}</ref> In December 2018, the band released a statement on their official Facebook page, stating that Boucher had spoken to the person in question, and they had both 'made peace' with a situation that was 'not purposeful or malicious'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/sorority-noise-issue-new-statement-about-sexual-assault-allegation/|title=Sorority Noise Issue New Statement About Sexual Assault Allegation|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=2019-01-16}}</ref>
