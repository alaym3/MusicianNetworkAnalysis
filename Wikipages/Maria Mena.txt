'''Maria Viktoria Mena''' (born 19 February 1986) is a Norwegian award-winning pop artist, best known for her singles such as "[[You're the Only One (Maria Mena song)|You're the Only One]]", "Just Hold Me", "All This Time" which charted in multiple countries.

==Early life==
Maria Mena was born into an artistic family. Her father is a drummer. There was a common misconception that her mother is a playwright, but Mena debunked this in an interview.<ref>{{Cite AV media|author=FaceCulture|title=Maria Mena interview (part 2)|date=May 31, 2009|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GjyX6-QxYkE}}</ref> Both Maria and her brother, Tony, are named after characters from [[Leonard Bernstein]]'s ''[[West Side Story (musical)|West Side Story]]''. Her mother is Norwegian and her father is an American of [[Afro-Nicaraguan]] descent.

Her father played in several bands in [[Oslo]], which influenced Mena to write and record her own music. When Mena was nine years old, her parents divorced. She suffered from depression and developed an [[eating disorder]].<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1666696/bio Maria Mena (I) – Biography<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

When Mena was 13 years old, she moved to live with her father. She sang and wrote lyrics as a form of self-soothing. "My Lullaby", a song from Mena's diary, expresses her pain from her parents' divorce. After pleading with her father to make a demo, he contacted some acquaintances in the music industry to record the demo. Presenting his daughter's demo to several record companies, [[Sony Music]] signed Mena to their label.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://nrk.no/musikk/artistar/3554332.html|title=NRK.no – Artistar|last=NRK|website=nrk.no|language=nb-NO|access-date=28 October 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
===2002–2007: Early beginnings===
In 2002, she released her debut single "Fragile (Free)" in Norway; however, it did not chart there. "My Lullaby" was released as the second single and the song reached number 5 on the Norwegian Singles Chart. It received heavy rotation on [[NRK]] P1, P3, [[P4 Radio Hele Norge|P4]], [[Radio 1 (Norway)|Radio 1]], and Radio Oslo. The young singer quickly gained fans and soon earned her first [[platinum record]]. After the success of "My Lullaby", she released her debut album ''[[Another Phase]]'' in Norway which reached number 6 on the Norwegian Albums Chart.
Mena made an appearance on the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]'' to promote her first international album, ''[[White Turns Blue]]'', which debuted at the top position on the ''[[Billboard charts|Billboard]]'' [[Top Heatseekers]] chart a week later and at number 102 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. She made a breakthrough into the worldwide singles charts that year with "[[You're the Only One (Maria Mena song)|You're the Only One]]", a single successful in multiple countries. The song was her only single to appear on a ''Billboard'' chart, making No. 25 on the U.S. [[Top 40 Mainstream]]. "You're the Only One" peaked at No. 30 on the [[Dutch Top 40]] and No. 19 on the Dutch [[Mega Single Top 100]]. Two months earlier, in March 2004, Mena had released her second album in Norway. ''[[Mellow (Maria Mena album)|Mellow]]''. Less successful than her 2002 debut, it still managed to peak at No. 7 in Norway. The second single from both albums was "Just a Little Bit", which did not chart anywhere.

2005 saw the release of ''[[Apparently Unaffected]]'' in Norway and several other European countries, fronted by singles "Miss You Love" and "Just Hold Me". A significant success in Norway, the album has earned her three [[Spellemannprisen|Spellemann]] nominations: Best Female Artist, Best Hit, and Best Music Video. In the [[Netherlands]], the album was released in June 2006 and charted at No. 82. It slowly climbed the chart to reach its peak position in its 27th week at No. 11, as "Just Hold Me" was getting airplay again. "Just Hold Me" was released in May 2006, but then did not chart in the official [[Dutch Top 40]], but did chart in the country's physical singles chart, the [[Mega Single Top 100]], at No. 27. In October 2006, the song started getting heavy airplay again and this time it reached the official Dutch Top 40 and peaked at No. 26. In the Mega Single Top 100, it peaked at No. 7 in the beginning of November 2006. Mena performed at sold-out concerts in [[Utrecht (city)|Utrecht]] and [[Amsterdam]], following the success of "Just Hold Me". The album was at No. 48 at the Dutch year-end chart, outselling major artists such as [[Pink (singer)|Pink]]'s ''[[I'm Not Dead]]'', [[Nelly Furtado]]'s ''[[Loose (Nelly Furtado album)|Loose]]'', and [[Beyoncé Knowles]]' ''[[B'Day (Beyoncé album)|B'Day]]''. "Miss You Love" was released as the album's second single in the Netherlands and reached No. 61 on the Mega Singles Top 100.

[[File:Maria Mena1.jpg|thumb|Maria Mena 2008]]
On 7 July 2007, she performed at the [[Live Earth concert, Hamburg|German leg]] of [[Live Earth]] in Hamburg.

===2008–2014: ''Cause and Effect'', ''Viktoria'', and ''Weapon in Mind''===
Having worked on her three first studio albums with Norwegian producer [[Arvid Solvang]], Mena started collaborating with [[Martin Sjølie]] in early 2008 on what would become her fourth studio album, ''[[Cause and Effect (Maria Mena album)|Cause and Effect]]''.
The album's first single, "Belly Up" had its radio premiere mid-June 2008 on Norwegian radio. ''Cause and Effect'' was released on 17 September in Norway. The first international single was "All This Time". The album was internationally released on 26 September 2008. This album garnered her a [[Spellemannprisen|Spellemann]] for Best Female Artist.

Mena's song "Sorry" was featured on the third season of US ''[[So You Think You Can Dance (U.S. TV series)|So You Think You Can Dance]]'' and "What's Another Day" was featured during the fourth season. She released "All This Time" in the UK on 11 April 2010.

In 2011 Mena recorded her version of "[[Mitt lille land]]" (My Little Country) by [[Ole Paus]] as part of a project by the Norwegian TV channel TV2. On 23 July, the day after the [[2011 Norway attacks|Norway terrorist attacks]] and after requests from her Norwegian audience, Mena uploaded her version of the song to the streaming site [[SoundCloud]]. It quickly became an anthem in memory of the victims of the attacks. She contributed to the album ''[[Mitt lille land (2011 album)|Mitt lille land]]'', performing one of two versions of the title track (the other was performed by Ole Paus). As of 2011 Mena had sold a total of 700,000 copies of her albums, and half of those copies had been sold in Norway.<ref name="http://dagbladet.no">{{cite web|url=http://www.dagbladet.no/2011/12/28/nyheter/arets_navn/maria_mena/musikk/19476685/ |title=Mega-Mena |publisher=Dagbladet |date=28 November 2011 |access-date=24 May 2016}}</ref>
[[File:Maria Mena Tilburg 013 feb '14.JPG|thumb|Maria Mena performing in 013 Tilburg, 10 February 2014 in The Netherlands during her Weapon in Mind Tour]]

Her fifth studio album ''[[Viktoria (Maria Mena album)|Viktoria]]'' was released on 23 September 2011 and was produced by Martin Sjølie.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Genzel|first1=Christian|title=Viktoria – Maria Mena {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits {{!}} AllMusic|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/viktoria-mw0002239333|website=AllMusic|access-date=2 March 2017}}</ref>

Her sixth album ''[[Weapon in Mind]]'' was released in 2013, becoming her first album to reach number one in Norway. Even though all three singles of the album failed to chart inside top 20 Norway Singles Chart, "Fuck You" and "I Always Liked That" became two of her best selling singles in Norway, both certified 2× Platinum.<ref name="http://ifpi.no/component/rstrophybridge/">{{cite web|url=http://ifpi.no/component/rstrophybridge/ |title=IFPI |publisher=IFPI Norway |date=10 March 2014 |access-date=16 June 2018}}</ref>

===2015–present: ''Growing Pains'' and ''They Never Leave Their Wives''===
Mena released the single "I Don't Wanna See You with Her" on 6 November 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Single Review: Maria Mena – I Don't Wanna See You With Her|url=https://abitofpopmusic.com/2015/11/08/single-review-maria-mena-i-dont-wanna-see-you-with-her/|website=A Bit Of Pop Music|access-date=2 March 2017|date=8 November 2015}}</ref> Her seventh studio album ''Growing Pains'' was released on 4 December 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Growing Pains by Maria Mena on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/growing-pains/id1051288823|website=iTunes|access-date=2 March 2017|language=en}}</ref>

In March 2020, Mena announced a new album (sometimes recognized as an [[Extended play|EP]]), ''They Never Leave Their Wives''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Maria Mena til Hamar|url=https://www.ostlendingen.no/maria-mena-til-hamar-i-november/s/5-69-901152|website=Østlendingen|access-date=5 March 2020|date=5 March 2020|language=no}}</ref> The album was released on 23 October 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=They never leave their wives by Maria Mena on Apple Music |url=https://music.apple.com/no/album/they-never-leave-their-wives/1532299735/|website=Apple.com|access-date=23 October 2020|language=en}}</ref> Mena released three singles from the album, "Not OK", "Lies (They Never Leave Their Wives)", and "You Live and You Learn".<ref>{{cite web|title=Not OK – Single av Maria Mena på Apple Music|url=https://music.apple.com/no/album/not-ok-single/1505337258|website=Apple Music|access-date=24 April 2020|language=no}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-28|title=Nieuwe single Maria Mena – "Lies (They Never Leave Their Wives)"|url=https://www.dansendeberen.be/2020/06/28/nieuwe-single-maria-mena-lies-they-never-leave-their-wives/|access-date=2020-06-29|website=Dansende Beren|language=nl}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-04|title=You Live and You Learn - Single by Maria Mena on Apple Music|url=https://music.apple.com/nz/album/you-live-and-you-learn-single/1528340374|access-date=2020-09-04|website=Apple Music|language=en}}</ref>

In October 2020, Mena announced that she will release a new album in March 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title=Maria Mena zing over affaire met getrouwde man|url=https://www.nu.nl/muziek/6085552/maria-mena-zingt-over-affaire-met-getrouwde-man-ben-meer-vergevingsgezind.html|website=nu.nl|date=23 October 2020|access-date=23 October 2020|language=nl}}</ref> In April 2021 Mena made a new announcement, saying she will postpone the release of her new material until she can play concerts again. The concert venues in Norway have been closed for longer periods during the Covid-19 pandemic.<ref>{{cite web|title=Maria Mena utsetter album: – Sitter på noen skikkelig bra låter |url=https://www.vg.no/rampelys/musikk/i/Ky49QM/maria-mena-utsetter-album-sitter-paa-noen-skikkelig-bra-laater |website=vg.no |access-date=7 May 2021|language=no}}</ref>
She described the upcoming album as a sequel to ''They Never Leave Their Wives'', containing songs about a woman who is on her own and then finds love.<ref>{{cite web|title=Maria Mena: Må avlyse turné - men lykkelig privat |url=https://www.vg.no/rampelys/musikk/i/9vjkop/maria-mena-maa-avlyse-turne-men-lykkelig-privat|website=vg.no|access-date=23 October 2020|language=no}}</ref>
