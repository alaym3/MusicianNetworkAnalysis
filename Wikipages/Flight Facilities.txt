'''Flight Facilities''' is an Australian [[Electronic music|electronic]] [[Record producer|producer]] duo that also performs as '''Hugo & Jimmy'''. In 2009, they began mixing songs by other artists before crafting their own original material. The duo consists of Hugo Gruzman and James Lyell. Their debut album ''[[Down to Earth (Flight Facilities album)|Down to Earth]]'' was released in October 2014 and features prominent names in the industry such as [[Kylie Minogue]], [[Emma Louise]], [[Reggie Watts]], [[Bishop Nehru]], [[Christine Hoberg]], [[Owl Eyes]], and Stee Downes.

==History==
===2009–2020: ''Down to Earth'' and ''Live with the Melbourne Symphony Orchestra''===
{{main|Down to Earth (Flight Facilities album)|Live with the Melbourne Symphony Orchestra}}
Flight Facilities are a duo consisting of Hugo Gruzman and James Lyell, which first began mixing various songs by other artists in 2009 in Sydney.<ref name="Daverh">{{cite news | url = http://www.inthemix.com.au/features/47327/Flight_Facilities_Take_off | title = Flight Facilities: Take Off | last = Daverh | work = In the Mix | publisher = [[Sound Alliance]] | date = 8 July 2010 | access-date = 31 January 2013 }}</ref> They have reworked [[Bag Raiders]], [[Toecutter]] and [[Sneaky Sound System]].<ref name="Daverh"/><ref name=autogenerated1 /> Their first original song, "[[Crave You]]", written by and featuring [[Giselle Rosselli]], received considerable [[Broadcasting#Economic models|airtime]] in Australia on the alternative music radio station [[Triple J]] in 2010.<ref name="Daverh"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/music/hitlist_track.htm |title=Current Triple J Hitlist |publisher=Triple J |access-date=15 June 2010 }}</ref> It was co-written by Gruzman, Lyell and Rosselli after the duo contacted the musician/producer to provide an original track for a compilation album by their label Bang Gang.<ref name="Daverh"/><ref name="APRACrave">{{cite web | publisher = [[Australasian Performing Right Association]] (APRA) | title = 'Crave You' at APRA search engine | url = http://www.apra-amcos.com.au/worksearch.axd?q=Crave%20You | access-date = 31 January 2013 }}</ref> In July 2010 Flight Facilities commenced their first tour, Maiden Voyage.<ref name="Daverh"/> They played at various Australian nightclubs: Elsewhere in the [[Gold Coast, Queensland|Gold Coast]], Empire in [[Brisbane]], Limbo in [[Adelaide]], and Adult Disco in Sydney before going to [[Japan]] to play at Onzieme in [[Osaka]], and Le Baron in [[Tokyo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://flightfacilities.com/blog/ |title=Maiden Voyage Tour |publisher=flightfacilities.com |access-date=15 June 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6E0rHijwn?url=http://flightfacilities.com/blog/ |archive-date=28 January 2013 }}</ref> "[[Crave You]]" was listed at No.&nbsp;19 in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2010]], and was featured as the soundtrack to [[Myer]]'s 2010 [[Spring Racing Carnival]] [[television commercial]] campaign.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/10/countdown/cd_19.htm|title=Hottest 100 – #19 Flight Facilities – Crave You {{!}} triple j|date=26 January 2011|website=abc.net.au|access-date=13 December 2019}}</ref>

In 2011, "Foreign Language" was nominated for a J-Award for its music video,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/jawards/12/video/flightfacilities.htm|title=Flight Facilities – Foreign Language {{!}} J Awards 2012 {{!}} triple j|date=2012-01-11|website=abc.net.au|access-date=2019-12-13}}</ref> and appeared as number 72 in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2011]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/11/countdown/80-61.htm|title=Tools {{!}} Hottest 100 – 2011 {{!}} triple j|date=2011-07-12|website=abc.net.au|access-date=2019-12-13}}</ref> In February that year while performing as Hugo & Jimmy in [[Jakarta]], [[Indonesia]], the duo claimed to be from [[Trinidad and Tobago]].<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/homeandhosed/blog/s2853915.htm |title=Triple J Home and Hosed |publisher=Dom Alessio |access-date=15 June 2010 }}</ref><ref name="inthemixDec2011">{{Cite web|url=https://junkee.com/inthemix-goodbye/179992|title=A Farewell From inthemix: Thanks For All The Memories|date=2018-10-31|website=Junkee|language=en-US|access-date=13 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="jp">[http://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2011/02/20/something-good-%E2%80%98love-garage%E2%80%99.html Something good at ‘love garage’ | The Jakarta Post<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> They have jokingly told journalists the same thing and that they are [[Calvin Harris]].<ref name="Johnson-Ingra">{{cite news | url = http://www.sfcritic.com/2012/04/05/flight-facilities-interview-with-hugo/ | title = Flight Facilities Interview with Hugo | last = Johnson-Ingra | first = David | work = SF Critic | publisher = David Johnson-Igra | date = 5 April 2012 | access-date = 1 February 2013 }}</ref>

In 2012, "[[Clair De Lune (Flight Facilities song)|Clair de Lune]]" appeared as number 17 in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2012]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/12/countdown/17.htm|title=#17: Flight Facilities – Clair De Lune {Ft. Christine Hoberg} {{!}} Hottest 100 – 2012 {{!}} triple j|date=26 January 2012|website=abc.net.au|access-date=2019-12-13}}</ref> In April 2012, they were broadcast on Triple J with four one-hour-long mixes, each featuring a decade spanning from 1972 to 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thedecademixes.com/|title=Flight Facilities – The Decade Mixes|website=thedecademixes.com|access-date=13 December 2019}}</ref>

In 2013, [[KCRW]] invited Flight Facilities to [[SXSW]] and headlined their home countries Falls Festival and Southbound Festivals (alongside [[The Roots]], [[!!!]], [[MGMT]], [[Bonobo]] and [[Grizzly Bear (band)|Grizzly Bear]]). In 2014 they were asked to play one of the world's biggest festivals, Coachella Music and Arts Festival in Palm Springs where they played to 8000 plus people each day.

The C90s track "Shine a Light" (Flight Facilities remix) gained mainstream attention in 2013 when featured in the popular [[video game]] ''[[Grand Theft Auto V]]'', created by [[Rockstar Games]].

In 2014, "[[Two Bodies]]" (featuring [[Emma Louise]]) appeared as number 39 and "Sunshine" (Ft. [[Reggie Watts]]) as number 52 in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2014]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/14/countdown/|title=1-100 List {{!}} Hottest 100 2014 {{!}} triple j|date=2014-01-11|website=abc.net.au|language=en|access-date=13 December 2019}}</ref> On 24 October 2014, they issued their debut album, ''Down to Earth'', which reached No.&nbsp;3 on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Albums Chart]].<ref name="AUS"/> It received a gold certification from ARIA by the end of the following year.<ref name="ARIA Cert15">{{Cite certification|region=Australia|type=album|certyear=2015}}
</ref>

In 2015, ''Down to Earth'' was made into a music video starring [[Sam Rockwell]]. At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2015|ARIA Music Awards]] that year, Flight Facilities were nominated in three categories: [[ARIA Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]], [[ARIA Award for Best Pop Release|Best Pop Release]] and [[ARIA Award for Best Cover Art|Best Cover Art]] (by Timothy Lovett) for ''Down to Earth''.<ref name="ARIA List">ARIA Music Awards for Flight Facilities:
* Search Results 'Flight Facilities': {{cite web | url = http://www.ariaawards.com.au/history/search/?view=list&text=Flight%20Facilities | title = Search Results for 'Flight Facilities' | publisher = [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA) | access-date = 12 December 2015 }}
* 2013 winners and nominees: {{cite web | url = http://www.ariaawards.com.au/history/year/2013 | title = Winners by Year 2013 | publisher = Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) | access-date = 12 December 2013 }}
* 2015 winners: {{cite web | url = http://www.ariaawards.com.au/news/2015/and-the-aria-award-goes-to%E2%80%A6 | title = And the ARIA Awards Goes to... | publisher = Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) | date = 27 November 2015 | access-date = 12 December 2015 }}
</ref>

In December 2015 Flight Facilities released a ''[[Live with the Melbourne Symphony Orchestra|live album]]'' following their performance with the [[Melbourne Symphony Orchestra]]. The album consisted of tracks from the duo's debut album as well as previous releases. It won the [[ARIA Award for Best Classical Album]] at the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2016]].

In 2017, Flight Facilities released the song "Arty Boy", featuring Emma Louise on vocals,<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/flight-facilities-return-first-new-song-three-years-arty-boy/|title=Flight Facilities Return With First New Song in Three Years, 'Arty Boy' – Music Feeds|magazine=Music Feeds|last=Williams|first=Tom|date=21 June 2017|access-date=23 June 2017}}</ref> followed by the single "Stranded", featuring [[Broods]], [[Reggie Watts]] and Saro.<ref>{{Citation|title=Flight Facilities – Stranded feat. Broods, Reggie Watts & Saro (Official Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i-ktB4wQe-M|language=en|access-date=13 December 2019}</ref>

In November 2019, Google began a YouTube campaign for Google Nest products using Flight Facilities "Better Than Ever".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.adnews.com.au/news/google-unveils-local-nest-campaign-with-72andsunny|title=Google unveils local Nest campaign with 72andSunny – AdNews|website=adnews.com.au|language=en|access-date=13 December 2019}}</ref>

===2021: ''Forever''===
{{main|Forever (Flight Facilities album)}}
In September 2021, Flight Facilities announced the release of their second studio album, titled ''[[Forever (Flight Facilities album)|Forever]]''. The album was released on 12 November 2021, with a single called "Heavy" featuring the American artist [[Caroline Smith and the Good Night Sleeps|Your Smith]], released a week before the album was due.<ref>https://www.totalntertainment.com/music/flight-facilities-unveil-new-single-heavy/</ref><ref>https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/flight-facilities-share-new-single-heavy-featuring-your-smith-3084736</ref>
