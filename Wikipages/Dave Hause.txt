'''Dave Hause''' (born March 12, 1978) is an [[United States|American]] singer-songwriter. He currently performs both solo and with his band The Mermaid. His music draws from [[heartland rock]], [[folk rock]] and [[punk rock]]. He has also played in multiple [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania|PA]] area [[punk rock|punk]] and [[hardcore punk|hardcore]] bands, including [[The Loved Ones (American band)|The Loved Ones]] and [[The Falcon (band)|The Falcon]].

==Music and career==

=== 1990 to 2003: Early years and The Curse ===
In the mid-90s, Hause played guitar in a Philadelphia-based hardcore/punk band called Step Ahead, who released one album. After they broke up, Hause and Brendan Hill, the drummer from Step Ahead, formed The Curse.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UNL00NDG42o|title=Music4Autobahns Interview: The Curse (Dave Hause from the Loved Ones)|date=17 January 2009|publisher=YouTube}}</ref> The Curse, which featured Hause on guitar and sharing vocals, released two songs on the four-way split album ''[[The Philadelphia Sound]]'' and released a six-track EP on Hell Bent Records before playing their last show on April 12, 2003 opening for [[Kid Dynamite (band)|Kid Dynamite]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/article/5626 |title=Kid Dynamite Openers: Grey Area Reunion / Curse Breaking Up? |website=Punknews.org |date=2003-03-22 |accessdate=2016-09-24}}</ref> While with The Curse, he also played guitar and sang back-up vocals for [[Paint It Black (band)|Paint It Black]].

=== 2004 to 2008: The Loved Ones ===
In April 2004, he formed [[The Loved Ones (American band)|The Loved Ones]] with former Kid Dynamite member Michael "Spider" Cotterman and former Trial By Fire drummer Mike Sneeringer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/article/8836 |title=Members of Kid Dynamite/Trial By Fire/Paint It Black form The Loved Ones |website=Punknews.org |date=2004-04-04 |accessdate=2016-09-24}}</ref> Shortly after, in July 2004, he quit Paint It Black to focus on The Loved Ones.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/article/9587 |title=Dave Hause leaves Paint It Black |website=Punknews.org |date=2004-07-02 |accessdate=2016-09-24}}</ref>

=== 2009 to 2012: Early solo career & ''Resolutions'' ===
In 2009, Hause performed solo material alongside [[Chuck Ragan]], [[Tim Barry]], and [[Jim Ward (musician)|Jim Ward]] as part of Ragan's Revival Tour, a collaborative folk concert tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/article/34537/tours-chuck-ragan-jim-ward-tim-barry-dave-hause-revival-tour-2009|title=Tours: Chuck Ragan / Jim Ward / Tim Barry / Dave Hause (Revival Tour 2009)|website=Punknews.org}}</ref>

Hause subsequently released his first solo music, a 7-inch single, in May 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/article/38140 |title=Dave Hause (The Loved Ones) to release solo 7 |website=Punknews.org |date=2010-05-03 |accessdate=2016-09-24}}</ref> He released a CD single in December, 2010, followed by the January 24 release of his debut, full-length solo album, ''[[Resolutions (album)|Resolutions]]'' via Paper + Plastick.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://davehause.com/?p%3D1 |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2011-01-26 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110201231338/http://davehause.com/?p=1 |archive-date=2011-02-01 }}</ref> On 12 April 2011 a video was released for the song "Time Will Tell<ref>{{cite web|url=http://filtermagazine.com/index.php/media/entry/watch_dave_hause_tells_time_with_a_shave_filter_premiere |title=Media - WATCH: Dave Hause ‘Tells Time’ With A Shave (FILTER Premiere) |publisher=FILTER Magazine |date=2011-04-12 |accessdate=2016-09-24}}</ref> The video pays homage to the 1967 [[Martin Scorsese]] short film ''[[The Big Shave]]''. He was featured in [[The Gaslight Anthem]]'s music video for their single "Bring It On" from their album [[American Slang]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2011/05/premiere-gaslight-anthems-divorce-video/ |title=PREMIERE: Gaslight Anthem’s Divorce Video |publisher=SPIN |date=2011-05-19 |accessdate=2016-09-24}}</ref> A video for the song "C'mon Kid" was released on Sept 1st, 2011 via Rocksound.

In September, 2011, [[Xtra Mile Recordings]] licensed ''[[Resolutions (album)|Resolutions]]'' and released the record in the UK and Europe and over the next two months, Hause performed across Europe with [[The Revival Tour]], alongside [[Chuck Ragan]], [[Dan Andriano]] of [[Alkaline Trio]], and [[Brian Fallon]] of [[The Gaslight Anthem]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/article/42237/tours-brian-fallon-chuck-ragan-dave-hause-dan-andriano-revival-tour-europe |title=Tours: Brian Fallon / Chuck Ragan / Dave Hause / Dan Andriano (Revival Tour Europe) |website=Punknews.org |accessdate=2016-09-24}}</ref>

In 2012, Hause released a series of five 7" singles, each on a different label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/article/46607 |title=Dave Hause plans 7-inch series for 2012 |website=Punknews.org |date=2012-06-19 |accessdate=2016-09-24}}</ref> Each 7" features two newly recorded versions of songs from ''Resolutions'' and two covers of songs from the catalog of the label of releasing that 7".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://davehause.com/7-series-coming-soon.php |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2012-04-19 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120407130416/http://davehause.com/7-series-coming-soon.php |archive-date=2012-04-07 }}</ref> The first release came on [[Record Store Day]] and the last one in October, and the labels that released them were, in order, [[Side One Dummy Records]], [[Jade Tree Records]], [[Bridge Nine Records]], [[Chunksaah Records]] and Sabot Productions.

=== 2013 to 2016: Second album ''Devour'' ===
In early 2013, Hause was signed to [[Rise Records]], who re-released his solo album ''Resolutions'' in March.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.punknews.org/article/50617/rise-records-signs-dave-hause-re-release-resolutions |title=Rise Records signs Dave Hause, re-release ‘Resolutions’ |date=2013-02-11 |accessdate=2013-07-15 |work=Punknews.org }}</ref> It was later announced that Rise would release his second solo album ''[[Devour (Dave Hause album)|Devour]]'', in October 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.punknews.org/article/52238/dave-hause-to-release-devour-this-october-streams-new-song |title=Dave Hause to release ‘Devour’ this October, streams new song |date=2013-07-12 |accessdate=2013-07-15 |work=Punknews.org }}</ref>

In December 2014 Dave Hause released his song "Seasons Greetings From Ferguson“ as a lyric video.<ref>{{Citation|last=DAVE HAUSE OFFICIAL|title=Seasons Greetings From Ferguson|date=2014-12-05|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XB8AEs-weT8|access-date=2018-08-28}}</ref> The song deals with police violence against African Americans and was written under the impact of the protests that followed the [[shooting of Michael Brown]].

=== 2017 to 2018: ''Bury Me in Philly'' and The Mermaid ===
Hause's third solo album ''[[Bury Me in Philly|Bury Me In Philly]]'' was released on February 3rd 2017. It was recorded and produced in Philadelphia by [[Eric Bazilian]] ([[The Hooters]]) and William Wittman.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cooldadmusic.com/2017/01/interview-matt-chrystal-chats-with-dave.html#.WyUmjiAyWUnA|title=Interview: Matt Chrystal Chats with Dave Hause|website=www.cooldadmusic.com|access-date=2018-08-02}}</ref> A few days before the official release, the album was streamed in full at several websites.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://thekey.xpn.org/2017/01/31/listen-to-dave-hauses-new-album-bury-me-in-philly/|title=Listen to Dave Hause's new album, Bury Me In Philly - The Key|date=2017-01-31|work=The Key|access-date=2018-08-02|language=en-US}}</ref> Described as a unification of punk and Americana<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/how-dave-hause-married-punk-and-americana-on-marvelous-new-album-107150/|title=How Dave Hause Married Punk and Americana on Marvelous New Album|last=Hudak|first=Joseph|date=2017-02-03|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2018-08-02|language=en-US}}</ref> by [[Rolling Stone]], the record embraced a more optimistic outlook, influenced by falling in love again, moving from Philadelphia to California and the new musical collaboration between Dave and his brother Tim Hause.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://openthetrunk.com/hause/|title=Dave Hause Interview|work=TrunkSpace|access-date=2018-08-02|language=en-US}}</ref>

Until then Dave Hause had performed his solo material solo or as a duo but for the ''Bury Me In Philly'' tour he put together a band, The Mermaid,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jadedinchicago.com/2017/06/dave-hause-3/|title=Dave Hause Interview {{!}} Jaded In Chicago|website=www.jadedinchicago.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-08-02}}</ref> composed of Dave on vocals and guitar, Dave´s brother Tim Hause on lead guitar, Miles Bentley ([[Jay Bentley]]´s son) on bass, Kevin Conroy on drums and Kayleigh Goldsworthy on keyboard. They toured American and Europe in this configuration; their first show in Cologne on March 1st 2017 was recorded for the German TV show [[Rockpalast]] ([https://www1.wdr.de/fernsehen/rockpalast/events/dave-hause-and-the-mermaid-siebzehn-uebersicht-100.html video]).

In 2018, Hause continued touring across North America and Europe. He supported [[Brian Fallon]] and [[The Gaslight Anthem]] on their respective European tours with brother Tim.

=== 2019: ''Kick'' ===
In 2019, Hause released his fourth solo record, ''Kick''. He toured the record with The Mermaid, again performing across North America and Europe.

=== Inspiration and influences ===
Hause has cited [[Bruce Springsteen]], [[Tom Petty]], [[The Clash]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/hear-americana-punk-dave-hause-talk-misfits-u2-with-chris-shiflett-114679/|title=Hear Americana Punk Dave Hause Talk Misfits, U2 With Chris Shiflett|last=Crawford|first=Robert|date=2017-05-30|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2018-08-02|language=en-US}}</ref> [[Bob Dylan]], [[The Beatles]], [[The Hooters]], [[Bryan Adams]], [[Aerosmith]], [[Iron Maiden]], [[Metallica]], [[Misfits (band)|The Misfits]], [[Sick of It All|Sick Of It All]], [[Steve Earle]], [[Tori Amos]], [[The Bouncing Souls]], [[Avail]], [[Patty Griffin]], [[Brandi Carlile]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mablesyndrome.libsyn.com/season-2-episode-24-dave-hause|title=Mable Syndrome Punk Rock Podcast: Season 2 Episode 24: Dave Hause|website=mablesyndrome.libsyn.com|language=en|access-date=2018-08-02}}</ref> [[Hot Water Music]], [[Social Distortion]], [[Ramones|the Ramones]] and [[Pearl Jam]] as influences.
