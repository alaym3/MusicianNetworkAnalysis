== History ==
 
Band members Zachary Hannah, Nathan Esquite, and David Labuguen began creating together as a group of friends in [[Boston]] while attending [[Berklee College of Music]] and [[Emerson College]].<ref name="TM"/><ref name="BaebleMain">{{cite news | last=Dudek| first=Mandi| title =Arizona Is a Band You Need To Know About| publisher =Baeble| date =March 2, 2017 | url =http://www.baeblemusic.com/musicblog/3-2-2017/a-r-i-z-o-n-a-is-a-band-you-need-to-know-about.html| accessdate =July 26, 2017}}</ref> During college, the three of them began working as songwriters and producers, before moving to Los Angeles, England, and finally back to their home state of New Jersey. They began making music together in 2015 and were discovered by now-manager, [[Jake Posner]], on [[Reddit]].<ref name="BBFirst"/>
 
Arizona was signed to Atlantic Records by [[Mike Caren]] in 2016.<ref name="IDOL">{{cite news | last=Wass| first=Mike| title =Arizona Get It Very Right With "Cross My Mind": Premiere| publisher =[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]| date =August 11, 2016 | url =http://www.idolator.com/7642778/arizona-cross-my-mind-premiere| accessdate =July 26, 2017}}</ref> The band released several singles that year, including "I Was Wrong", "Oceans Away", and "Cross My Mind".<ref name="IDOL"/><ref name="NYLONSong">{{cite news | last=Manders| first=Hayden| title =Arizona's New Video Will Move You| publisher =[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]]| date =May 9, 2016 | url =https://www.nylon.com/articles/arizona-i-was-wrong| accessdate =July 26, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Earmilk">{{cite news | last=Webster| first=McKenzii| title =Arizona explores the southwest in new "Oceans Away" visual [Premiere + Interview]| publisher =Earmilk| date =December 13, 2016 | url =http://earmilk.com/2016/12/13/a-r-i-z-o-n-a-explores-the-southwest-in-new-oceans-away-visual-premiere-interview/| accessdate =July 26, 2017}}</ref><ref name="BaebleSong">{{cite news | last=Spruch| first=Kirsten| title =BAEBLE FIRST PLAY: Arizona's Heart-Wrenching, Intimate 'I Was Wrong'| publisher =Baeble| date =September 29, 2016 | url =http://www.baeblemusic.com/musicblog/9-29-2016/baeble-first-play-a-r-i-z-o-n-as-heart-wrenching-intimate-i-was-wrong.html| accessdate =July 26, 2017}}</ref> [[Robin Schulz]] produced the official remix of "I Was Wrong",<ref name="BBFirst"/><ref name="DA">{{cite news | title =ARIZONA – I Was Wrong (Robin Schulz Remix)| publisher =[[Dancing Astronaut]]| date =July 14, 2016 | url =http://www.dancingastronaut.com/2016/07/r-z-o-n-wrong-robin-schulz-remix/| accessdate =July 26, 2017}}</ref> and, by August 2016, the original song had accrued 16 million streams online.<ref name="IDOL"/> The Robin Schulz remix also appeared on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Dance/Electronic Songs|Hot Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart for 9 weeks.<ref name="BBGarrett">{{cite news | last=Corpuz| first=Kristin| title=Arizona on Touring With Kevin Garrett, Upcoming Debut Album & Finding an Audience on Spotify| publisher =[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]| date =March 2, 2017 | url =http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7710034/a-r-i-z-o-n-a-kevin-garrett-tour-debut-album-interview| accessdate =July 26, 2017}}</ref>
 
In 2017, the band toured with and opened for [[Kevin Garrett (musician)|Kevin Garrett]] and, later, [[Coin (band)|Coin]].<ref name="TM"/><ref name="BBGarrett"/> In March of that year, their single, "Oceans Away" had garnered 35.8&nbsp;million streams on [[Spotify]].<ref name="BBGarrett"/> On May 19, 2017, the band released its debut studio album, ''Gallery'', on Atlantic Records.<ref name="BBNew"/> The album peaked at No. 143 on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart.<ref name="BB200"/> Arizona has also played several festivals including [[South by Southwest]], the [[Governors Ball Music Festival]], [[Firefly Music Festival]], and [[Lollapalooza]].<ref name="BBNew"/><ref name="Forbes">{{cite news | last=Briones| first=Isis| title=Music Festival Musts: If You Haven't Seen Arizona, You're Missing Out| work =[[Forbes (magazine)|Forbes]]| date =June 16, 2017 | url =https://www.forbes.com/sites/isisbriones/2017/06/16/arizona-band-headlining-tour-governors-ball-interview/#28cd46c86910| accessdate =July 26, 2017}}</ref><ref name=ones2watch>{{cite news | last=Damoui | first=Yasmin| title =Q&A: How Arizona Found Success From The Studio to the Stage| work=Ones to Watch| date =August 23, 2017 | url =http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/qa-how-a-r-i-z-o-n-a-found-success-from-the| accessdate =July 20, 2018}}</ref> The band went on a North American headlining tour in the Fall of 2017.<ref name="Forbes"/><ref name=nj>{{cite news | last=Olivier | first=Bobby| title =A new N.J. band has exploded online; how did they fare on a real stage? (PHOTOS)| work=NJ.com| date =September 8, 2017 | url =https://www.nj.com/entertainment/music/index.ssf/2017/09/a_r_i_z_o_n_a_arizona_concert_review_2017_nyc_bowe.html| accessdate =July 20, 2018}}</ref> Also in 2017, [[Don Diablo]] (a Dutch DJ who is No. 11 in the world according to ''[[DJ Mag]]'')<ref name=edm>{{cite news | last=Stone | first=Katie| title =Don Diablo is Heating Things Up with Zonderling on New Collaboration 'No Good' | work=EDM.com| date =July 6, 2018 | url =https://edm.com/music-releases/dondiablo-zonderling-newsingle-nogood| accessdate =July 20, 2018}}</ref> produced the song, "[[Take Her Place]]" with Arizona. The song appeared on Diablo's album, ''[[Future (Don Diablo album)|Future]]''.<ref name=billboarddiablo>{{cite news | last=Armstrong | first=Megan| title =Don Diablo and Arizona Fuse Dance With Indie on 'Take Her Place': Listen| work=Billboard| date =October 27, 2017 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8014898/don-diablo-a-r-i-z-o-n-a-take-her-place| accessdate =July 20, 2018}}</ref><ref name=dancingastronaut>{{cite news | last=Chrys | first=Kassi| title =Don Diablo announces 'Future' North American tour with Lost Frequencies | work=Dancing Astronaut| date =November 15, 2017 | url =https://dancingastronaut.com/2017/11/don-diablo-announces-future-north-american-tour-lost-frequencies/| accessdate =July 20, 2018}}</ref>

In July 2018, the band released two new songs, "Summer Days" and "Freaking Out". They also started an arena tour that month, opening for [[Panic! at the Disco]].<ref name=billboard2018>{{cite news | last=Rincón | first=Alessandra| title =Arizona Talks Touring With Panic! At the Disco, Addressing Anxiety With New Music: 'We All Have Those Moments'| work=Billboard| date =July 12, 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8465010/arizona-interview-panic-at-the-disco-tour| accessdate =July 20, 2018}}</ref><ref name=variance>{{cite news | last=Schmitt | first=Tyler| title =Get Lost in Arizona's Dazzling New Track 'Summer Days' | work=Variance| date =July 6, 2018 | url =https://variancemagazine.com/sounds/9477-a-r-i-z-o-n-a-summer-days| accessdate =July 20, 2018}}</ref>

The band's sophomore album "Asylum" was released on October 11, 2019 and peaked at No.17 on Billboards Heatseekers Album Chart<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/a-r-i-z-o-n-a/chart-history/TLN/song/1169074|title = A R I Z O N A|magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>
