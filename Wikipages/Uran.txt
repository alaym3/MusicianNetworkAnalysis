'''Uran''' is a coastal town and part of [[Navi Mumbai]], [[Maharashtra|Maharashtra state]] in [[Konkan division]]. It lies in the [[Raigad district]], east of [[Mumbai]] across the [[Dharamtar]] Creek. Uran is primarily a fishing and agriculture village, which has developed into the special economic zone of Uran. The primary languages spoken are [[Agri language|Agri]] and [[Parkari Koli language|Koli]] which is a dialect of [[Maharashtrian Konkani|Marathi-Kokani]].

==History==
The city name is derived from the [[Hindu deities|Hindu]] goddess Uranavati. It was called '''Uruvan''' during the rule of [[Madhavrao I]], the fourth [[Peshwa]] of the [[Maratha Empire]]. Later, the area was named Uran by the Portuguese and Ooran by the British.<ref name=JourneyMart>{{cite web | url = http://www.journeymart.com/de/india/maharashtra/uran-history.aspx | title = History of Uran | publisher = JourneyMart.com}}</ref>

Many Indian dynasties have ruled the area. In early history, these included the [[Maurya Empire]], [[Sātavāhana Empire]], [[Western Kshatrapas]], [[Vakataka dynasty|Vākāṭaka Empire]], [[Chalukya dynasty|Chalukyas]], and [[Seuna (Yadava) dynasty|Yadavas]].<ref name=JourneyMart/><ref>{{Cite book|title=Greater Bombay District Gazetteer|series=Maharashtra State Gazetteers|volume= 27 | number = 1|year=1960|publisher=Gazetteer Department ([[Government of Maharashtra]] | pages=127–150)}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title=Bombay: The Cities Within |last1=Dwivedi |first1=Sharada |author-link1=Sharada Dwivedi |last2=Mehrotra |first2=Rahul |publisher=Eminence Designs |year=2001 |isbn=81-85028-80-X | page = 79}}</ref>

The area around [[Mumbai]], including Uran, was under [[Portuguese India|Portuguese]] and [[British India|British]] rule in the 16th to 19th centuries.<ref name=JourneyMart/>

Uran was subject to the [[List of Maratha dynasties and states|Maratha princely states]] in the [[Maharashtra]]. Uran was once the base of the famous Maratha ''Sarkhel'' (Admiral) [[Kanhoji Angre]],<ref>{{cite book | publisher = Outlook Traveller Getaways | title = Weekend Breaks from Mumbai | page = 413 | year = 2003}}</ref> who battled European naval interests in the 18th century.

==Geography==
Uran is located at {{Coord|18.88|N|72.94|E|}}.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.fallingrain.com/world/IN/16/Uran.html | title = Uran, India Page | publisher = Falling Rain Genomics, Inc. | date = 27 February 2015}}</ref> Uran is at an average elevation of {{convert|21|m|ft}}.

On the tip of a peninsula, Uran district is surrounded by sea on three sides. The town of Uran itself is centrally located on the peninsula, flanked by [[Karanja, Raigad|Karanja village]] to the south and [[Mora, Maharashtra|Mora]] village and port to the north.

Uran is home to extensive [[Salt evaporation pond|salt pans]], part of a long-standing network of salt manufacturing around Mumbai.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://indiatogether.org/saltpan-human-rights | title = KONKAN SALT PANS: Wilful distortion denies salt pan owners justice | date = 3 April 2008 | work = India Together | publisher = Oorvani Media Pvt. Ltd.}}</ref> The land has been under redevelopment pressure.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://infochangeindia.org/agenda/coastal-communities/saltpan-city.html | title = Saltpan city | first = Freny | last = Manecksha | work = Infochange News & Features | date = April 2010}}</ref>

The Uran wetlands were once home to many species of birds and reptiles, some critically endangered. The wetlands were developed in 2009 and 2010, leaving only the [[Panje-Dongri wetlands]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://mangroveactionproject.org/the-map-news-236th-ed-30-april-2010/ | title = The MAP News | work = Mangrove Action Project | publisher = Mangrove Action Project | date = 30 April 2010 | edition = 236}}{{failed verification|date=March 2015}}</ref>

==Economy==
[[File:ONGC Oil Platform.jpg|thumb|240px|ONGC Oil and Gas Processing Platform in the [[Bombay High]] oilfield]]
[[File:Jaskhar Fair.jpg|thumb|240px|alt=Jaskhar village fair at night|Jaskhar Fair at night in the month of April]]
The main occupation in Uran is fishing; 80% of the fish production of Mumbai comes from [[fishermen]] based out of the Uran in [[Navi Mumbai]], especially the villages of Karanja and Mora.

The second major occupation is farming. Uran contributes to the production of [[rice]] in the Raigad district. Raigad is the second largest district in terms of rice production in India.

[[Shipping]], [[shipbuilding]], and port support are major economic factors in Uran district. [[Jawaharlal Nehru Port]] (JNPT) is the largest [[container terminal]] in [[India]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://business.rediff.com/slide-show/2010/oct/07/slide-show-1-the-major-ports-of-india.htm | title = The biggest ports of India | work = Rediff Business | publisher = Rediff.com. | date = 8 October 2010 | access-date = 29 July 2013}}</ref> Other container terminals in the Uran district include [[APM Terminals]] (formerly GTI) and [[DP World]] (formerly the British [[Peninsular and Oriental Steam Navigation Company]]). Salt pan owners revolted against the government decision to build a seaport,{{when|date=March 2015}} but the port has proved to be the main source of income to residents and nearby villages, as major projects and warehouses have opened nearby.

The [[Indian Navy]] maintains a naval base near Mora. Due to Security reasons sea travel restrictions is imposed by the Indian Navy .

The [[Oil and Natural Gas Corporation]] (ONGC) has a plant nearby. GTPS-[[Maharashtra State Electricity Board|MSEB]] is [[Asia]]'s first power plant run by [[natural gas]].

Other industrial and manufacturing employers Grindwell Norton Limited,<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.grindwellnorton.co.in/aboutus.htm | title = About Us | publisher =  Grindwell Norton Ltd. | year = 2007 | access-date = 12 March 2015}}</ref> NAD, and the Skols Brewery (closed in 2005<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.business-standard.com/article/companies/sabmiller-shuts-skol-uran-unit-105103101010_1.html | title = SABMiller shuts Skol Uran unit | location = Mumbai | date = 31 October 2005 | work = Business Standard | publisher = Business Standard Ltd.}}</ref>).

==Demographics==
Uran is growing. In the 2001 India [[census]], Uran had a population of 23,254;<ref name="Census2001">{{cite web|url=http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A&state5=999|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040616075334/http://www.censusindia.net/results/town.php?stad=A&state5=999|archive-date=2004-06-16|title= Census of India 2001: Data from the 2001 Census, including cities, villages and towns  (Provisional)|access-date=2008-11-01|publisher= Census Commission of India}}</ref> this had increased by the 2011 census to 30,439.<ref name="Census2011" /> Males constitute 53% of the population and females 47%. In Uran, 10–11% of the population is 6&nbsp;years of age or younger.<ref name="Census2011" /><ref name="Census2001" />

Uran's average literacy rate is also rising, from 79% in 2001 to 82% in 2011.<ref name="Census2011" /><ref name="Census2001" /> Female literacy lags behind male literacy: male literacy was 83% in 2001 and 85% in 2011, and female literacy was 75% in 2001 and 79% in 2011.<ref name="Census2011" /><ref name="Census2001" /> India's national literacy average is 59.5%.

===Other attractions===

Another major tourist attraction in Uran are the three beaches: Pirwadi Beach, Nagav Beach, and Mankeshwar Beach which is frequented by tourists from nearby areas like [[Panvel]] in [[Navi Mumbai]], other parts of [[Navi Mumbai]], and [[Mumbai]]. A distant view of [[South Mumbai]] is visible from here. A local rural beach Kegav Beach is present, which can also be visited by tourists, and tourists do visit this beach too, but mostly it remains isolated. So, in totality, Navi Mumbai's Uran node has four beaches.

Ransai Dam is a destination in Uran. It is located near Dighode Village.

An old military fort named [[Dronagiri]] is located just beside the [[ONGC]] plant on the top of the Dronagiri mountain. This was a [[Maratha]] fort for a short period before passing into possession of the [[Portuguese language|Portuguese]], according to the locals. Entry has been banned for security reasons.

Khopate Village: Khopate Village is popular for Dashami Festival which is celebrated on behalf of Saint Gopalkaka Maharaj.

Dronagiri Mandir: A view from the top of the mountain where the temple of Dronagiri Devi is situated.

Bird watching at the Panje-Funde wetlands.
