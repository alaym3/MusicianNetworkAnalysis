'''Spyair''' (stylized as '''SPYAIR''') is a Japanese [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Nagoya]], [[Japan]], formed in 2005. The band currently consists of Hidefumi "Ike" Ikeda (vocals), Yuji "UZ" Nakai (guitar, programming), Kenji "Momiken" Momiyama (bass guitar, leader) and Kenta Sasabe (drums).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.jpopasia.com/spyair/|title=SPYAIR Biography|work=JpopAsia|accessdate=November 2, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Overview">{{cite web|url=https://rockinon.com/artist/5890|title=SPYAIRの記事まとめ|trans-title=SPYAIR Overview|website=[[Rockin'On Japan]]|language=ja|accessdate=May 1, 2018}}</ref> The band's name, SPYAIR, was determined in one shot by the members, which came from the word [[spyware]], a type of computer virus.<ref name="Overview"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.generasia.com/wiki/SPYAIR|title=SPYAIR Biography|work=Generasia|accessdate=November 2, 2017}}</ref>

==History==
The band was formed in 2005 and produced and released a number of materials themselves up until 2009 when they signed with the indie label U-Project. While with U-Project, they released the singles "Japanication" and "Kanjō Discord" before signing a major record deal with [[Sony Music Entertainment Japan|Sony Music]] in 2010. Their major debut single, "Liar," was released on August 11 and was used as the theme song for the drama ''[[Hammer Session!]]''.<ref name="Barks">{{cite web|website=Barks|url=http://www.en.barks.jp/artist/?id=2000210128&m=bio|title=SPYAIR : Biography|accessdate=November 2, 2017|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170928005620/http://www.en.barks.jp/artist/?id=2000210128&m=bio|archivedate=September 28, 2017}}</ref> Their second major single, "Last Moment," was released on December 1 and was used as the 25th ending song for the [[anime]] ''[[Bleach (anime)|Bleach]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.barks.jp/news/?id=1000066343|title=SPYAIR、新曲「Last Moment(BLEACH ver.)」配信開始|trans-title=SPYAIR's new song "Last Moment" (''Bleach'' ver.) released|website=Barks Japan|language=ja|date=December 10, 2010|accessdate=May 1, 2018}}</ref>

Spyair's indies debut single "Japanication" was re-released on March 16, 2011, followed by their fourth major single, "Samurai Heart (Some Like It Hot!!)," on June 8, which was used as the 17th ending song to the anime ''[[Gin Tama]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.barks.jp/news/?id=1000069211|title=SPYAIR、新曲「サムライハート(Some Like It Hot!!)」が2週連続1位を獲得|trans-title=SPYAIR's new song "Samurai Heart (Some Like It Hot !!)" won 1st place for 2 consecutive weeks|website=Barks Japan|language=ja|date=April 19, 2011|accessdate=December 20, 2018}}</ref> In early 2012, the single "My World" was used as the second ending song for ''[[Mobile Suit Gundam AGE]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tower.jp/article/news/2011/12/19/n02|title=「機動戦士ガンダムAGE」新OP&EDテーマはねごととSPYAIRが担当|trans-title=Negoto and SPYAIR perform the new OP & ED themes of "Mobile Suit Gundam AGE"|website=[[Tower Records#Japan|Tower Records Japan]]|language=ja|date=December 19, 2011|accessdate=December 20, 2018}}</ref> The single "0 Game (Love Game)" was used as an official soundtrack for the 2012 film ''[[The Amazing Spider-Man (2012 film)|The Amazing Spider-Man]]'' in Japan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/70746|title=SPYAIR新曲は「スパイダーマン」日本版テーマソング|trans-title=SPYAIR's new song was used as ''Spider-Man'' Japan theme song|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|language=ja|date=June 8, 2012|accessdate=May 1, 2018}}</ref> They also provided the theme song for the [[Gintama: The Movie: The Final Chapter: Be Forever Yorozuya|second ''Gin Tama'' movie]], "Genjō Destruction".<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/2013/05/spyair-to-sing-for-upcoming-gintama-movie/ |title=SPYAIR to sing for upcoming 'Gintama' movie |website=Tokyohive.com |date=May 3, 2013 |accessdate=April 11, 2014}}</ref> Their eighth major single "Naked" was released on September 5<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/2012/07/spyair-announces-new-single-naked/ |title=SPYAIR announces new single, "Naked" |website=Tokyohive.com |date=July 21, 2012 |accessdate=April 11, 2014}}</ref> and was followed by the announcement of the band's second album, ''Just Do It'', which features 12 tracks including their last three singles.<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/2012/08/spyair-to-release-second-album-just-do-it/ |title=SPYAIR to release second album, "Just Do It" |website=Tokyohive.com |date=August 8, 2012 |accessdate=April 11, 2014}}</ref>

In October, DJ ENZEL☆ announced that he would be leaving the band after their [[Nippon Budokan]] concert on December 18.<ref>{{cite news|title=SPYAIR's DJ Enzel Leaving Band After Nippon Budokan Concert|work=[[Anime News Network]]|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2012-10-11/spyair-dj-enzel-leaving-band-after-nippon-budokan-concert |accessdate=December 27, 2012|date=October 11, 2012|last=Loveridge|first=Lynzee}}</ref> The single "Wendy: It's You" was used as the theme song for the drama ''Koi Suru Hae Onna'', which started airing in November.<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/2012/10/spyair-to-release-new-single-wendy-its-you/ |title=SPYAIR to release new single, "WENDY ~It's You~" |website=Tokyohive.com |date=October 2, 2012 |accessdate=April 11, 2014}}</ref> On December 18, the band successfully held their first live concert at the Nippon Budokan, performing a total of 21 songs including their latest singles. This concert was Enzel's last performance with the group. After the concert, they announced a new single, "Sakura Mitsutsuki," set for release on March 13, 2013. It was used as the 13th opening song for ''Gin Tama'' for the month of January.<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/2012/12/spyair-holds-their-1st-live-at-nippon-budokan-new-single-announcement/ |title=SPYAIR holds their 1st live at Nippon Budokan + new single announcement |website=Tokyohive.com |date=December 19, 2012 |accessdate=April 11, 2014}}</ref>

Spyair's third studio album, ''Million'', was released on August 7. On September 23, it was released by [[JPU Records]] in Europe, where the CD only version included three bonus tracks: "Last Moment," "Samurai Heart (Some Like It Hot!!)" and "My World." In October, the single "Just One Life" was used as the first opening song of the anime ''[[Samurai Flamenco]]''.<ref>{{cite news|first=Crystalyn|last=Hodgkins|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2013-09-05/galileo-donna-samurai-flamenco-anime-tv-ad-posted|title=''Galilei Donna'', ''Samurai Flamenco'' Anime's TV Ad Posted|work=Anime News Network|date=September 5, 2013|accessdate=May 1, 2018}}</ref> Their next single "Imagination" was released on April 30, 2014, and served as the first opening song of the anime ''[[Haikyū!!]]''.<ref>{{cite news|first=Egan|last=Loo|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2014-02-13/haikyu-theme-songs-performed-by-spyair-nico-touches-the-walls|title=''Haikyuu!'' theme songs performed by Spyair, Nico Touches the Walls|work=Anime News Network|date=February 13, 2014|accessdate=May 1, 2018}}</ref> The single "Firestarter," released on July 22, 2015, was used as the theme song for the drama ''[[The Last Cop (2015 TV series)|The Last Cop]]''.<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.billboard-japan.com/d_news/detail/29276/2|language=ja|title=SPYAIR ドラマ『THE LAST COP/ラストコップ』主題歌のMV公開|trans-title=SPYAIR releases MV of ''The Last Cop'' drama theme song|publisher=[[Billboard Japan]]|date=June 19, 2015|accessdate=May 1, 2018}}</ref> In October, Spyair contributed with the first opening song of the second season of ''Haikyū!!'', "I'm a Believer",<ref>{{cite web|last=Green|first=Scott|url=https://www.crunchyroll.com/anime-news/2015/08/30/haikyu-second-season-anime-cast-additions-and-theme-song-announced|title="Haikyuu!!" Second Season anime cast additions and theme song announced|publisher=[[Crunchyroll]]|date=August 30, 2015|accessdate=May 1, 2018}}</ref> and a year later, their single "Rage of Dust" was used as the opening song of the second season of ''[[Mobile Suit Gundam: Iron-Blooded Orphans]]''.<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=https://www.sbs.com.au/popasia/blog/2016/08/29/spyairs-new-song-be-opening-theme-new-gundam-anime-series|title=SPYAIR's new song to be opening theme for new 'Gundam' anime series|publisher=[[Special Broadcasting Service|SBS]]|date=August 29, 2016|accessdate=May 1, 2018}}</ref>

== Musical style and influences ==
The members of the band have cited their musical influences to be [[Korn|KORN]],<ref name="JPU Records">{{cite news|publisher=[[JPU Records]]|url=https://www.jpurecords.com/spyair-european-festival-appearance/|title=Anime Rock Stars SPYAIR Celebrate Album Launch with European Festival Appearance|date=September 18, 2013|accessdate=March 25, 2017}}</ref> [[Linkin Park]],<ref name="JPU Records"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://jame-world.com/us/articles-97750-interview-with-spyair.html|title=Interview with SPYAIR|work=JaME WORLD|date=September 21, 2013|accessdate=March 25, 2017}}</ref> and [[Michael Jackson]].<ref name="JPU Records"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.spyair.net/profile/|title=Profile {{!}} SPYAIR Official Website|website=Spyair.net|language=ja|accessdate=March 25, 2017}}</ref> SPYAIR combines elements of [[Rock music|rock]], [[Pop music|pop]] and [[electronic music]].
