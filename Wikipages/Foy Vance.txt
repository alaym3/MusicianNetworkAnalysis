'''Foy Vance''' (born 1974)<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/foy-vance-mn0000907816/biography| first=Dave| last=Donnelly| title=Foy Vance: Biography by Dave Donnelly| website=[[AllMusic]]| access-date=20 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/foy-vance-on-how-the-scottish-highlands-saved-him-1.1481818| date=2 August 2013| first=Lauren| last=Murphy| newspaper=The Irish Times| title=Foy Vance on how the Scottish Highlands saved him}}</ref> is a musician and singer-songwriter from Northern Ireland, signed to [[Ed Sheeran]]'s [[Gingerbread Man Records|Gingerbread Man]] label.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/ed-sheeran-13-1188841| first=Larry| last=Bartleet| title=Foy Vance becomes second signing to Ed Sheeran's label Gingerbread Man Records| website=[[NME]]| access-date=12 March 2019}}</ref> Vance has toured as a support act to British singer-songwriter Ed Sheeran and his music has been featured on numerous TV shows. In August 2013 he released his second full-length album, ''[[Joy of Nothing]]'', which was produced by Irish producer and arranger Michael Keeney and was met with critical praise, being called "a gorgeous collection of open, uplifting songs, which showcase [Vance's] magnificent songwriting skills."<ref name="folkradio1">{{cite web|author=Alex |url=http://www.folkradio.co.uk/2013/06/foy-vance-debut-album-joy-of-nothing-documentary/ |title=Foy Vance new album: Joy of Nothing (+ documentary) &#124; Folk Radio UK |publisher=Folkradio.co.uk |date=10 June 2013 |access-date=22 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Coleman|first1=Maureen|title=ALBUM REVIEW: Joy of Nothing|url=http://www.culturenorthernireland.org/article/5951/album-review-joy-of-nothing|website=Culture Ireland|access-date=10 August 2014}}</ref> In May 2016, his third studio album ''The Wild Swan'', produced and mixed by [[Jacquire King]], was released and it was announced that Vance would support [[Elton John]] on selected tour dates in June and [[Josh Groban]] in July and August.

==Music career==

===2006–2011: Early career and ''Hope''===
Vance's first single, "Gabriel and the Vagabond", was released on 18 December 2006 on Wurdamouth Records. The single's [[B-side]] was "Indiscriminate Act of Kindness". The songs "Homebird" and "Gabriel and the Vagabond" were both featured in the second season of the US TV drama ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/showcase/clips/p00g889s/in/justadded/justadded |title=Music Showcase – Just Added – Foy Vance – I Got Love |publisher=BBC |date=4 September 2011 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref>

"Indiscriminate Act of Kindness" is featured in a TV commercial for [[Great Ormond Street Hospital]], which was aired in the UK up until Christmas of 2006.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/articles/music/Foy_Vance_Irish_soul_music_and_Indiscriminate_Acts_Of_Kindness/30263/p1/ |title=Foy Vance: Irish soul music and Indiscriminate Acts Of Kindness |publisher=Cross Rhythms |date=14 January 2008 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> Vance, who often uses alternative tunings for his guitar, had previously released two limited edition EPs on Wurdamouth Records, ''Live Sessions and the Birth Toilet Tour'' and ''Watermelon Oranges''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.contactmusic.com/new/home.nsf/webpages/foyvancex01x08x06 |title=Foy Vance &#124; Watermelon Oranges Ep EP Review |publisher=Contactmusic.com |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=One on One with... FOY VANCE!|url=http://www.oneononemusic.co.uk/one-on-one-with-foy-vance/4576193536|website=One on One Music|access-date=10 August 2014}}</ref>

On 21 July 2007, Vance's debut album, ''Hope'', was self-released via Wurdamouth Records.<ref>{{cite web|author=British Broadcasting Corporation |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/northernireland/atl/review_specific100170.shtml |title=Northern Ireland – Across The Line |publisher=BBC |date=7 February 2007 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> The album obtained some critical acclaim within the UK with the album track "Two Shades of Hope", which contains a hidden recording of Vance's daughter, Ella, singing "[[You Are My Sunshine]]" at the end.<ref>{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://www.foyvance.com/biography/|website=Foy Vance|publisher=Glassnote Records|access-date=10 August 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140812205733/http://www.foyvance.com/biography/|archive-date=12 August 2014}}</ref> His pianist is London based composer [[Jules Maxwell]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Taylor |last=Bruce |url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2008/04/foy-vance-the-homebirds-chorus.html |title=Foy Vance: The Homebird's Chorus :: Music :: Features :: Paste |work=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |date=21 April 2008 |access-date=25 June 2013}}</ref>

From 2007, Vance performed his contemporary songs at Ireland's largest annual Christian youth festival [[Summer Madness (festival)|Summer Madness]] at the [[King's Hall, Belfast]] for several consecutive years.<ref name="Vance at Summer Madness">{{cite web|title=Summer Madness|url=http://www.last.fm/festival/279727+Summer+Madness|website=last.fm|access-date=26 June 2016}}</ref>

In early 2008, Vance was invited to perform at a number of prisons in England, including [[HM Prison New Hall|New Hall]] women's prison near Wakefield and [[HM Prison Full Sutton|Full Sutton]] maximum security prison near York.<ref>{{cite web|first=Erwin |last=James |url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2008/jul/04/filmandmusic1.filmandmusic |title=Erwin James reports on the effect musicians visiting prisons may have on the inmates |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=4 July 2008 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref>

In 2010, Vance, along with [[David Holmes (musician)|David Holmes]], composed the music for a short film by director [[Terry George]], and as a result was offered a part in the director's next feature film, ''[[Whole Lotta Sole]]'', as a busker. The short film, [[The Shore (2011 film)|''The Shore'']], won a 2012 Academy Award.{{citation needed|date=May 2016}}

===2011–2012: ''The Melrose EP''===
On 31 January 2011, Vance went to Los Angeles to finish the recording of ''The Melrose EP'' with Holmes. During his trip to L.A., Vance put on a special show for fans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thepublicqueue.com/2012/introducing-mr-foy-vance/ |title=Introducing Mr. Foy Vance |publisher=Thepublicqueue.com |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref>

As part of Belfast Music Week 2011, Vance hosted a [[hoedown]] branded as "Foy Vance and Friends". It featured a spread of Vance's close friends from Belfast who joined him on the stage. The event sold out [[Ulster Hall]] in the city.{{Citation needed|date=October 2017}}

During 2012's [[South by Southwest]] festival in Austin, Texas, Vance was programmed as part of Brooklyn Vegan's tribute event to [[Daniel Johnston]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2012/03/bv_presents_dan.html |title=BV Presents Daniel Johnston's 'Space Ducks' SXSW Launch Party w/ Daniel, Built to Spill & many more performing |publisher=Brooklynvegan.com |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref>

On 27 August 2012, Vance released ''The Melrose EP'', on [[Communion Music|Communion Records]]. The EP featured four new tracks which were produced by Holmes. The EP makes up the score of [[Terry George|Terry George's]] [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] winning film ''[[The Shore (2011 film)|The Shore]]'' and the lead track from the EP, "Be the Song" has featured on episodes of ''[[Private Practice (TV series)|Private Practice]]'', ''[[House (TV series)|House]]'', ''[[Arrow (TV series)|Arrow]]'', ''[[Haven (TV series)|Haven]]'' and ''[[Constantine (TV series)|Constantine]]'' in addition to the film ''[[Warm Bodies (film)|Warm Bodies]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.communionmusic.co.uk/2012/07/foy-vance-melrose-ep-release/ |title=Foy Vance – Melrose EP release – Communion |publisher=Communionmusic.co.uk |date=19 July 2012 |access-date=17 June 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131006090505/http://www.communionmusic.co.uk/2012/07/foy-vance-melrose-ep-release/ |archive-date=6 October 2013  }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Warm Bodies Movie (2013) Soundtrack |url=http://www.songonlyrics.com/soundtracks/warm-bodies-soundtrack-list.html |title=Warm Bodies Soundtrack List &#124; Warm Bodies Movie (2013) |publisher=Songonlyrics.com |date=1 February 2013 |access-date=22 June 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130525030832/http://www.songonlyrics.com/soundtracks/warm-bodies-soundtrack-list.html |archive-date=25 May 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===2013: ''Joy of Nothing''===

In early 2013, Vance signed with [[Glassnote Records]] and completed his new album, ''[[Joy of Nothing]]'', which was released on 26 August.<ref>{{cite web|author=Glassnote Records |url=http://foyvance.com/ |title=Joy Of Nothing |publisher=Foy Vance |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> The album announcement was accompanied by a music video for the song "Joy of Nothing" which featured Vance and his daughter Ella.<ref>{{cite web|author=LeePL |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uVC_glG6RQ8 |title=Foy Vance – Joy Of Nothing |publisher=YouTube |date=25 February 2013 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> The album includes vocal features from [[Bonnie Raitt]] and Ed Sheeran,<ref name="folkradio1"/> the latter of whom expressed his excitement for the release.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/edsheeran/status/340165508827602944 |title=Twitter / edsheeran: very fortunate to have been given an early copy |publisher=Twitter.com |access-date=22 June 2013}}</ref> On 10 June 2013, Vance released a 22-minute documentary, ''Recording Nothing'', showing the process of writing and recording the album.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.recordingnothing.com/ |title=Foy Vance |publisher=Recording Nothing |access-date=22 June 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130613103544/http://recordingnothing.com/ |archive-date=13 June 2013  }}</ref>

Vance performed at [[Tennent's Vital]] on 15 August 2013 along with headliners [[Snow Patrol]], and also toured with Bonnie Raitt throughout the UK.<ref>{{cite web|author=Across The Line |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/acrosstheline/posts/Snow-Patrol-to-headline-Vital |title=Blogs – Across the line – Snow Patrol headline Vital with guests Foy Vance and Kodaline |publisher=BBC |access-date=22 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Natalie |last=Xenos |url=http://culturefly.co.uk/music-video-of-the-week-joy-of-nothing-foy-vance/ |title=Joy Of Nothing – Foy Vance |publisher=Culturefly |date=13 June 2013 |access-date=22 June 2013}}</ref> He and Sheeran traded cameos, with Sheeran joining Vance on stage at the [[Bowery Ballroom]] on 4 November 2013 and Vance joining Sheeran at [[Madison Square Garden]] on 7 November 2013.{{citation needed|date=December 2020}}

On 12 November 2013, ''Joy of Nothing'' won the inaugural [[Northern Ireland Music Prize]] for best album. Other nominees included [[Tired Pony]], [[Girls Names]], and [[Two Door Cinema Club]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Jones|first1=Rhian|title=Foy Vance wins inaugural Northern Ireland Music Prize|url=http://www.musicweek.com/news/read/foy-vance-wins-inaugural-northern-island-music-prize/056710|work=Music Week|publisher=Intent Media|access-date=10 August 2014|date=13 November 2013}}</ref>

===2015–present: ''The Wild Swan''===

In July 2015, Vance appeared at the Barn on the Farm festival at Over Farm, [[Over, Gloucestershire|Over]], near [[Gloucester]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.summerfestivalguide.co.uk/Festival-News/2015/06/05/Barn-on-the-Farm-2015-lineup-complete|title=Barn on the Farm 2015 lineup complete|publisher=summerfestivalguide.co.uk|access-date=26 June 2016}}</ref> and recorded a number of session for the festival's [[YouTube]] channel.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2RQl9xFe9bE|title=Foy Vance – Upbeat Feel Good – Barn on the Farm Sessions|last=Barnonthefarm|date=30 December 2015|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=26 June 2016}}</ref>

In November 2015, Vance signed with Sheeran's [[Gingerbread Man Records]], and on 13 May 2016, he released his third studio album, ''The Wild Swan''. Reviewing the album for the ''[[London Evening Standard]]'', Andre Paine commented: "[...] after a decade as a jobbing songwriter, Vance deserves a commercial breakthrough."<ref>{{cite web|first=Andre |last=Paine |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/stayingin/music/foy-vance-the-wild-swan-review-deserves-a-commercial-breakthrough-a3247426.html |title=Foy Vance – The Wild Swan, review: 'deserves a commercial breakthrough' |publisher=Standard.co.uk |access-date=16 May 2016}}</ref>

In May 2016, it was announced that Vance would support [[Elton John]] in June on selected dates of his Wonderful Crazy Tour in Europe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://foyvance.com/foy-to-support-elton-john-on-tour/ |title=Foy to Support Elton John on Tour |publisher=Foy Vance |access-date=16 May 2016}}</ref> On 10 September, Vance embarked on his Wild Swan World Tour with a sold-out performance in [[Perth]]. After Australia, the tour was scheduled to include dates in North America, the UK and Europe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://foyvance.com/the-wild-swan-world-tour-starts-today/|title=Foy Vance – The Wild Swan World Tour Starts Today|publisher=foyvance.com|access-date=14 February 2017}}</ref>

Vance co-wrote the song "Pushin' Time" with [[Miranda Lambert]] and [[Natalie Hemby]] that appeared on Lambert's album ''[[The Weight of These Wings]]'', which was released in November 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/news/miranda-lamberts-weight-of-these-wings-track-by-track-w450764|title=Miranda Lambert's 'The Weight of These Wings': A Track-by-Track Guide|last=Leahey|first=Andrew|date=16 November 2016|newspaper=Rolling Stone|access-date=29 December 2016}}</ref>

In December 2016, Vance released the single "Moonshine", which appeared on the soundtrack for the [[Ben Affleck]] film ''[[Live by Night (film)|Live by Night]]''. "Moonshine" features [[Kacey Musgraves]] and is meant to pay tribute to "the films strong female characters", whilst telling "the story of a bootlegger running moonshine".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/news/hear-foy-vance-kacey-musgraves-stomping-moonshine-w460250|title=Hear Foy Vance, Kacey Musgraves' Stomping New Song 'Moonshine'|publisher=rollingstone.com|access-date=14 February 2017}}</ref> In January 2017 a music video was released for "Moonshine" and premiered on Billboard.com.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7662679/foy-vance-kacey-musgraves-moonshine-song-live-by-night-video|title=Foy Vance & Kacey Musgraves Team Up For New Song 'Moonshine': Video Premiere|work=Billboard|access-date=14 February 2017}}</ref>

===2019: ''From Muscle Shoals'' and ''To Memphis''===
In June 2019, Vance released a new album, ''From Muscle Shoals'', a collection of [[Americana]]-inspired songs recorded at FAME Studios in [[Muscle Shoals, Alabama]].

In July 2019, he released the dubstep/future bass song "Blood" in collaboration with the American DJ and producer [[Illenium]]. It represents the sixth single from Illenium's studio album ''[[Ascend (Illenium album)|Ascend]]''.

In September 2019, he released a new album, ''To Memphis''.

On 4 June 1994, Vance appeared as [[Andrew Strong]] of [[The Commitments (film)|''The Commitments'']] on the TV talent show ''[[Stars in Their Eyes]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/title/509862 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131007032318/http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/title/509862 |url-status=dead |archive-date=7 October 2013 |title=Stars in their Eyes (1994) |publisher=Ftvdb.bfi.org.uk |date=16 April 2009 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> In 2012, his music was used in several episodes of The CW series ''[[Beauty & the Beast (2012 TV series)|Beauty and the Beast]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/beauty-and-the-beast/season-2/17467|title=Music from Beauty and the Beast S2E16|website=Tunefind|language=en-US|access-date=28 January 2018}}</ref>{{citation needed|date=May 2016}}

In 2007, Vance appeared on RTE's ''Other Voices'', filmed in [[Dingle]], Ireland.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.othervoices.ie/artists/other-voices-6/foy-vance/ |title=Other Voices &#124; Foy Vance |publisher=Othervoices.ie |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> Vance took part in the [[Channel 4]] series ''4Thought.tv'', where he spoke about how profound he found the hymn, "[[Amazing Grace]]", how it impacted him hearing a vagabond at [[World Trade Center site|Ground Zero]], New York, playing this song.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.4thought.tv/themes/why-is-amazing-grace-so-amazing/foy-vance |title=Foy Vance – Why is Amazing Grace so amazing? |publisher=4thought.tv |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> A Channel 4 documentary about Vance, covered all aspects of his career from him growing up, playing shows in Ireland, playing a show in New York with [[Pete Townshend]], and organically recording his debut album ''Hope''.<ref>{{cite web|author=foyvancemusic |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VCY2V-57tFs |title=Foy Vance – 4Play |publisher=YouTube |date=12 August 2009 |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> In September 2011, Vance joined Snow Patrol on the BBC Two's ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]'', to perform their single, "Fallen Empires".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/foyvance/status/116179407256825857 |title=Twitter / foyvance: Singing with Snow Patrol on Later with Jools |publisher=Twitter.com |access-date=17 June 2013}}</ref> 
In May 2015, Vance appeared with [[Rudimental]] on ''Later with Jools Holland''.
Vance presents ''Notes from the USA'' on [[Euronews]].<ref>[https://www.euronews.com/2019/01/14/getting-to-know-singer-songwriter-foy-vance Getting to know singer-songwriter Foy Vance] www.euronews.com, 15 January 2019</ref>
