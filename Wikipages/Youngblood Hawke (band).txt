'''Youngblood Hawke''' is an American [[indie pop]] band based in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref name=all>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/youngblood-hawke-mn0002982300|title=biography|last=Monger|first=James Christopher|work=[[Allmusic]]|publisher=Rovi Corp|access-date=7 October 2012}}</ref>

==History==
Both Simon Katz and Sam Martin were in the band [[Iglu & Hartly]], but after a 2011 hiatus, the two reformed with three of their long-time friends to form Youngblood Hawke.<ref name=jig>{{cite web|url=http://www.jigsawmagazine.com/2012/08/youngblood-hawke.html|title=Youngblood Hawke|date=22 August 2012|work=Jigsaw Magazine|access-date=7 October 2012}}</ref> Their name is taken from the [[Herman Wouk]] novel of the [[Youngblood Hawke|same name]].<ref name=pop>{{cite web|url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/post/163387-20-questions-youngblood-hawke/|title=20 Questions: Youngblood Hawke|last=Sawdey|first=Evan|date=27 September 2012|work=[[Popmatters]]|publisher=Buzzmedia Music|access-date=7 October 2012}}</ref> In October 2011, before landing a [[recording contract]] with Universal Republic, Youngblood had a residency at The Satellite<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thesatellitela.com/ |title=The Satellite |publisher=Thesatellitela.com |access-date=2013-08-07}}</ref> in Los Angeles. Aside from that, the group has performed at [[South by Southwest]], playing showcases such as  Comedy Central Workaholics showcase,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.comedycentral.com/shows/workaholics |title=Workaholics - Series &#124; Comedy Central Official Site |publisher=ComedyCentral.com |access-date=2013-08-07}}</ref> and Purevolume House.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.purevolume.com/thehouse/getting-to-know-youngblood-hawke/ |title=Getting to Know Youngblood Hawke « The PureVolume House |publisher=Purevolume.com |date=2012-03-02 |access-date=2013-08-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120313085049/http://www.purevolume.com/thehouse/getting-to-know-youngblood-hawke/ |archive-date=2012-03-13 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Sherrick |first=Alex |url=http://www.mxdwn.com/2012/03/08/news/workaholics-sxsw-house-party-presented-by-comedy-central-announced/ |title=Workaholics SXSW House Party presented by Comedy Central Announced |publisher=Mxdwn.com |date=2012-03-08 |access-date=2013-08-07}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.purevolume.com/thehouse/the-2012-purevolume-house-presented-by-iheartradio-is-here/ |title=The 2012 PureVolume House Presented by iHeartRadio is Here! « The PureVolume House |publisher=Purevolume.com |date=2012-02-28 |access-date=2013-08-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120511031035/http://www.purevolume.com/thehouse/the-2012-purevolume-house-presented-by-iheartradio-is-here/ |archive-date=2012-05-11 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The band was based in [[Silverlake, Los Angeles]], where they were working on their debut album and worked odd jobs on the side during the recording process.<ref name=art>{{cite web|url=http://www.artistdirect.com/entertainment-news/article/see-the-music-youngblood-hawke-s-we-come-running/10242185|title=See the Music: Youngblood Hawke's "We Come Running"|last=Pannacione|first=Maggie|date=17 September 2012|work=[[Artistdirect]]|publisher=Rogue Digital|access-date=7 October 2012}}</ref> Youngblood Hawke's first single "[[We Come Running]]", was described by ''[[LA Weekly]]''{{'}}s Kevin Bronson as showing the quintet's "skills at grabbing hold of the pop jugular vein".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://buzzbands.la/2012/07/30/stream-youngblood-hawke-we-come-running|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120801225809/http://buzzbands.la/2012/07/30/stream-youngblood-hawke-we-come-running|archive-date=2012-08-01|title=Stream: Youngblood Hawke, 'We Come Running'|date=1 August 2012}}</ref> Their track "Forever" was the Starbucks Pick of the Week for September 2012.<ref>{{cite web|last=Heringer |first=Mark |url=http://www.mark-heringer.com/2012/09/starbucks-itunes-pick-of-week_25.html |title=Starbucks iTunes Pick of the Week - Youngblood Hawke - Forever |publisher=Mark-heringer.com |date=2012-09-25 |access-date=2013-08-07}}</ref> Before that, they joined the line-up for both the Echo Park Rising Festival<ref>{{cite web|url=http://echoparkrising.com/ |title=August 17 and 18, 2013 |publisher=Echo Park Rising |access-date=2013-08-07}}</ref> as well as Lobsterfest.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lobsterfestival.com/ |title=Home - Redondo Beach Lobster Festival |publisher=Lobsterfestival.com |access-date=2013-08-07}}</ref> "We Come Running" was also featured on the soundtrack for the [[association football]] video game ''[[FIFA 13]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.ea.com/uk/football/news/fifa-13-soundtrack-02 |title=FIFA 13 Soundtrack |date=September 6, 2012 |publisher=EA.com |access-date=January 5, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120909201803/http://www.ea.com/uk/football/news/fifa-13-soundtrack-02 |archive-date=September 9, 2012 }}</ref> "We Come Running" was also used in the first episode of ''[[The 100 (TV series)|The 100]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/the-100/season-1/17925|title=Music from The 100 S1E01|website=Tunefind}}</ref> In 2014, another song from their debut album, "Stars", was featured in a commercial for the streaming service Netflix.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ispot.tv/ad/7fh2/netflix-hold-on-song-by-youngblood-hawke|title=Netflix TV Commercial, 'Hold On' Song by Youngblood Hawke|website=iSpot.tv|language=en|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref>

Youngblood Hawke's video for their debut single "We Come Running" was featured on ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', "where they play music and scuba dive with sharks in time" in hopes to help promote the conservation of sharks.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/youngblood-hawke-dive-below-the-surface-in-we-come-running-premiere-20121011#ixzz29Qmj8i8O |title=Youngblood Hawke Dive Below the Surface in 'We Come Running' Premiere - Video |magazine=Rolling Stone |date=2012-10-11 |access-date=2013-08-07}}</ref>

The band is currently in the process of recording their second studio album<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/YoungbloodHawke/status/579032411217874944|title=Youngblood Hawke on Twitter|work=Twitter}}</ref> and its lead single  "[[Pressure (Youngblood Hawke song)|Pressure]]" was released in early 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/YoungbloodHawke/status/420702541962166274|title=Youngblood Hawke on Twitter|work=Twitter}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/pressure-single/id830872288|title=Pressure - Single|work=iTunes}}</ref> They "plan on releasing a cover song accompanied by a band-made music video every month until the album is released," starting with [[Private Eyes (song)|Private Eyes]] by [[Hall & Oates]] which was released on March 2, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.purevolume.com/news/WATCH-Youngblood-Hawke-Cover-Hall-And-Oates-Private-Eyes|title=WATCH: Youngblood Hawke Cover Hall & Oates' 'Private Eyes'|author=PureVolume|work=PureVolume|access-date=2015-03-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180501081616/http://www.purevolume.com/news/WATCH-Youngblood-Hawke-Cover-Hall-And-Oates-Private-Eyes|archive-date=2018-05-01|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The band's song "Knock Me Down"<ref name="twitter.com">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/YoungbloodHawke/status/585234623799496704|title=Youngblood Hawke on Twitter|work=Twitter}}</ref> was released on July 30, 2015 as the second single from the band's forthcoming second album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/knock-me-down/id1025154784?i=1025154826|title=Knock Me Down - Single|work=iTunes}}</ref> A lyric video for the song was released on May 30, 2017, nearly two years after its initial release.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.facebook.com/story.php?story_fbid=1439346776131684&id=121130994619942&refsrc=http://fanlink.to/kmdlyric&_rdr|title=Youngblood Hawke|website=m.facebook.com}}</ref> Meanwhile, the song was featured in a commercial for [[PlayStation 4]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/YoungbloodHawke/status/576426022708473856|title=Youngblood Hawke on Twitter|work=Twitter}}</ref> and also in the [[Ultimate Fighting Championship|UFC]] documentary ''Road to the Octagon''.<ref>{{cite av media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jU6F7ksP7CQ|title=YouTube|work=youtube.com}}</ref> The song was later used in an [[Applebee's]] commercial<ref>{{cite av media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9bQQAct040k|title=Applebee's NEW Triple Hog Dare Ya|date=18 May 2015|work=YouTube}}</ref> and was also featured in the soundtrack for ''[[Madden NFL 16]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hardcoregamer.com/2015/08/03/madden-nfl-16-full-soundtrack-tracklist-revealed/161261/|title=Madden NFL 16 Full Soundtrack Tracklist Revealed|work=hardcoregamer.com}}</ref>

The band's song "[[Robbers (Youngblood Hawke song)|Robbers]]" was released on June 15, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/youngblood-hawke-offers-dizzying-new-slice-of-pop-gold-robbers-405-premiere-149|title=Youngblood Hawke offers dizzying new slice of pop gold 'Robbers' [405 Premiere]|author=William Tomer|work=The 405|date=June 15, 2017}}</ref> Another single called "Trust", co-written and co-produced by Andrew Taggart of [[The Chainsmokers]],  was released on February 9, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/trust-single/1331615572|title=Trust - Single by Youngblood Hawke|website=Music.apple.com}}</ref>

On July 28, 2020, the band announced their second full-length album ''Edge of the World'', which is set to be released on October 9, 2020.<ref name="EOTW">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/YoungbloodHawke/status/1288140972535185409|title=Youngblood Hawke on Twitter|work=Twitter}}</ref> The album's lead single "Waking Up the World" was released on August 6th via the band's [[SoundCloud]] channel, one day prior to the song's scheduled release date.<ref name="WUTW">{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/youngbloodhawke/03-wakinguptheworld-1644-ref-22jun2020|title=Waking Up The World by Youngblood Hawke|work=[[SoundCloud]]}}</ref> The album's second single called "Criminals" was released on August 28, followed by "Find a Way" as the album's third and final single on September 18.<ref name="FindAWay">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/YoungbloodHawke/status/1303369206927249408|title=Youngblood Hawke on Twitter|work=[[Twitter]]}}</ref>

Buzz Bands LA touched on the band's extended hiatus, writing "[the band] has been in the background, ensconced in things like day jobs, solo projects, musical collaborations and impending parenthood."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://buzzbands.la/2017/06/16/stream-youngblood-hawke-robbers/|title=Stream: Youngblood Hawke, 'Robbers'|author=Kevin Bronson|work=Buzz Bands LA|date=June 16, 2017}}</ref>

In 2021 Sam Martin released a solo single called "Patience" under the name Sunshine Boysclub.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://buzzbands.la/2021/06/18/ears-wide-open-sunshine-boysclub/|title=Ears Wide Open: Sunshine Boysclub|date=June 18, 2021}}</ref>
