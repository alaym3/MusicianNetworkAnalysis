'''Timucin Lam'''<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2017/07/jax-jones-interview/ |title=From the church to the Grammys: Jax Jones on taking his UK house sound global |work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |author=McMahon, Milly |date=27 July 2017 |access-date=6 July 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180303225733/http://www.complex.com/music/2017/07/jax-jones-interview/ |archive-date=3 March 2018 |url-status=live}}</ref> (born '''Timucin Fabian Kwong Wah Aluo'''; 25 July 1987), known professionally as '''Jax Jones''', is an English DJ, [[songwriter]], [[record producer]] and remixer. He rose to fame in 2014 by featuring on [[Duke Dumont]]'s number-one single "[[I Got U]]". He followed this up with his own singles "[[You Don't Know Me (Jax Jones song)|You Don't Know Me]]" featuring [[Raye (singer)|Raye]] and "[[Instruction (song)|Instruction]]" featuring [[Demi Lovato]] and [[Stefflon Don]] in 2016 and 2017, respectively. Jax's debut studio album, ''[[Snacks (Supersize)]]'', was released on 6 September 2019. It was preceded by the single "[[One Touch (Jess Glynne and Jax Jones song)|One Touch]]" featuring [[Jess Glynne]].

==Personal life==
Jax grew up in a [[Multiracial|multi-ethnic]] household and was exposed to a wealth of influences. His father is Turkish and gave him the name Timucin. He is also of Chinese Malaysian descent.<ref name="BBC">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/a2507d4d-3387-4d31-a6e8-f2c9794fb37e |title=Jax Jones |publisher=BBC |access-date= 14 July 2016}}</ref> After his Nigerian stepfather introduced him to [[afrobeat]] via [[Fela Kuti]] and rap with a tape of a [[The Notorious B.I.G.|Notorious B.I.G.]] album, Jax listened to hip hop, R&B, gospel and traditional African music.<ref name="Skiddle">{{cite web|url=http://www.skiddle.com/news/all/Jax-Jones-the-boy-thats-anything-but-blas/24109/ |title=jax jones: the boy that's anything but blasÃ© |publisher=skiddle.com |access-date= 10 December 2014}}</ref> His Malaysian mother was into music ranging from [[Kylie Minogue]] to [[Luther Vandross]] and also influenced him.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.gulf-times.com/story/533747/Talented-Jax-Jones-finds-success-with-You-Don-t-Kn|title=Talented Jax Jones finds success with You Donâ€™t Know Me|date=2017-02-20|work=Gulf-Times|access-date=2017-11-01|language=ar}}</ref> Producers like [[The Neptunes]], [[Timbaland]] and [[Rodney Jerkins]] shaped his musical horizons and his fascination with the anatomy of a rhythm section. Having initially studied classical guitar from a young age, Jax took up beat-making at 15 after discovering [[Steinberg Cubase|Cubase]] on his friend's [[Atari]].<ref name="EarMilk Interview">{{cite web|url=http://earmilk.com/2014/10/16/earmilk-interview-jax-jones/ |title=EarMilk Interview with Jax Jones |publisher=earmilk.com |access-date= 10 December 2014}}</ref>

On 18 September 2020, Jax announced on his Instagram that his wife had given birth to their daughter, Lawana.{{citation needed|date= October 2021}}

==Music career==
===Early beginnings===
Performing at [[open mic|open mic clubs]] around London, Jax eventually came together with Ripperman, a [[Grime (music genre)|grime]] producer from [[Mitcham, London|Mitcham]], at [[Brixton]]'s Raw Materials Studio, and worked on releases for [[Big Narstie]] and Roadside Gs, among others in the scene. His parents encouraged him to go to university at 18. Opting to stay closer to Raw Materials, he declined an interview at the [[University of Oxford]] to attend [[Brunel University London|Brunel University]] in [[Uxbridge]].<ref name="EarMilk Interview" /> Despite a two-hour trip, Jax went from lectures to the studio daily to work on his craft, posting beats regularly on [[Myspace]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-22|title=10 Interesting Facts About Jax Jones You Didn't Know Before|url=https://buzz4fun.net/facts-about-jax-jones/|access-date=2021-08-01|website=Buzz4Fun|language=en-US}}</ref><!-- WP:RS needed; removed link to WP:SPS (Twitter, FB, WP)--> After graduation, Jax rented a studio in [[Sydenham, London|Sydenham]] after not being allowed back in the family home, bought a [[Mac Pro|Mac tower]] from [[Gumtree]] and worked daily to hone his craft.<ref name="EarMilk Interview" />

===2014: Breakthrough===
After meeting [[Duke Dumont]], he developed an interest in [[house music]] and together they co-wrote and produced "[[I Got U]]", "[[Won't Look Back (song)|Won't Look Back]]" and "[[Ocean Drive (Duke Dumont song)|Ocean Drive]]".<ref name="hypetrak">{{cite web|url=http://hypetrak.com/2014/02/duke-dumont-featuring-jax-jones-i-got-u/ |title=Duke Dumont featuring Jax Jones â€“ I Got U |publisher=hypetrak.com |access-date= 10 December 2014}}</ref> Jones features on the single "[[I Got U]]", the song peaked at number one on the [[UK Singles Chart]] in March 2014, leading to his breakthrough as an artist.<ref name="Official Charts">{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/duke-dumont-scores-second-number-1-single-with-i-got-u__3881/ |title=Duke Dumont scores second Number 1 single with I Got U |publisher=Official Charts Company |access-date= 23 March 2014}}</ref>

===2020â€“present: Europa, I Miss U, and Deep Joy===
Jax also collaborated with fellow DJ [[Martin Solveig]] to create the musical duo [[Europa (musical duo)|Europa]]. Their first song together, "[[All Day and Night]]" (featuring [[Madison Beer]]), was included Jones' 2018 EP ''Snacks'' and was released as single in March 2019. They released their next single, "Tequila" (featuring [[RAYE]]), in February 2020.

On 9 October 2020, Jones released the single "[[I Miss U]]" alongside Antiguan-German singer [[Au/Ra]]. The song peaked at #25 on the UK Singles Chart, and a subsequent acoustic version followed. Upon its release, Jones tweeted about the song's message in relation to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], and an inability to see family and friends due to national lockdowns. He said "i miss u is finally out in the world we all miss something right now and i miss u is supposed to highlight that and remind ourselves that it's ok to feel this way sometimes."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jax Jones drops his dancefloor filler 'i miss u' with Au/Ra ðŸŽ¶|url=https://planetradio.co.uk/hits-radio/entertainment/music/jax-jones-aura-i-miss-u/|access-date=2021-09-16|website=Hits Radio|language=en}}</ref>

On 4 June 2021, Jones released the song "Feels", the lead single off his second EP ''Deep Joy'', which was released on 2 July 2021.

13 August 2021 saw the release of "[[Out Out (song)|Out Out]]", a collaborative single with fellow English DJ [[Joel Corry]] and featuring the vocals of singer [[Charli XCX]] and rapper [[Saweetie]]. The song peaked at #6 on the UK Singles Chart.
