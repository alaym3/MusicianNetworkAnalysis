'''Moira Rachelle Bustamante Cruzado Dela Torre-Hernandez''' (born November 4, 1993),<ref name="yes">{{cite magazine |date=April 2018 |title=Spotlight |url=https://www.pressreader.com/philippines/yes-philippines/20180401/283034055132878 |magazine=[[Yes! (Philippine magazine)|Yes!]] |location=Philippines |publisher=[[Summit Media]] |access-date=November 4, 2018 }}</ref><ref name="Inquirer.Net">{{cite news|title=Where Did Moira Dela Torre Get Inspiration for her Hit Song 'Malaya'|url=http://preen.inquirer.net/64195/moira-dela-torre-get-inspiration-hit-song-malaya|work=Philippine Daily Inquirer|date=December 6, 2017}}</ref> better known for her stage name '''Moira Dela Torre''', is a [[Filipinos|Filipina]] singer-songwriter. She rose to fame for her covers of [[Imago (band)|Imago]]'s "Sundo", [[Moonstar88]]'s "Torete", and the [[Himig Handog]]-winning single "Titibo-tibo".

==Career==
Dela Torre's career started as a voice artist working on corporate jingles and theme songs, including [[McDonald's Philippines|McDonald's]] "Hooray for Today", [[Surf (detergent)|Surf]]'s "Pinalaki", and [[Johnson & Johnson]]'s "Signature of Love".<ref name="Inquirer.Net"/>

===2013–2014: ''The Voice of the Philippines''===
Moira Dela Torre joined the [[The Voice of the Philippines (season 1)|first season]] of ''[[The Voice of the Philippines]]'',<ref>{{cite news|title=Meet the voice behind the McDo PH jingle|url=http://news.abs-cbn.com/video/entertainment/06/29/13/meet-voice-behind-mcdo-ph-jingle|work=ABS-CBN News|date=June 30, 2013}}</ref> impressing Coach [[apl.de.ap]] with her rendition of the song "Hallelujah" of [[Bamboo Mañalac]], and became part of the Team Apl. She advanced to the battle rounds of the competition where she was picked to sing "[[One Night Only (song)|One Night Only]]" together with Cara Manglapus and Penelope Matanguihan. She was eliminated as Penelope Matanguihan won the battle round.<ref>{{cite news|title='Little Big Shots': Boy fulfills dream of recording song with Moira dela Torre|url=http://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/12/03/17/little-big-shots-boy-fulfills-dream-of-recording-song-with-moira-dela-torre|work=ABS-CBN News|date=December 3, 2017}}</ref>

===2014: First single and debut EP===
She released her first single titled "Love Me Instead" through her debut EP album titled ''[[Moira (Moira Dela Torre EP)|Moira]]''.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

===2016–present: Breakthrough===
Dela Torre performed singles for the official soundtracks of the romantic films ''[[Camp Sawi]]'' and  ''[[Love You to the Stars and Back]]''.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

In October 2017, she performed in the finals of the songwriting and music video competition [[Himig Handog]]. Dela Torre's interpretation of the Libertine Amistoso song "Titibo-tibo" made her the grand winner.<ref>{{cite news|title='Titibo-Tibo' interpreted by Moira wins Himig Handog 2017|url=http://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/11/26/17/titibo-tibo-interpreted-by-moira-wins-himig-handog-2017|work=ABS-CBN News|date=November 26, 2017}}</ref> In late October, she became a member of the acoustic group [[ASAP (variety show)|ASAP Jambayan]].

In February 2018, her first concert "Tagpuan" sold out in four days on its first night; it was directed by [[John Prats]]. Due to public demand, the concert had a second night.<ref>{{cite web|title=Moira Dela Torre's "Tagpuan" concert opens second night|url=https://www.onemusic.ph/news/moira-s-tagpuan-concert-opens-second-night-3650|work=One Music PH|date=January 11, 2018}}</ref> Her 2018 debut album, ''[[Malaya (Moira Dela Torre album)|Malaya]]'', featured her hit singles "Malaya" and "Tagpuan".<ref>{{cite news|title=AUDIO JUNKIE: Hits from Pinoy movies, series and 'Istorya'|url=https://entertainment.mb.com.ph/2018/01/09/audio-junkie-hits-from-pinoy-movies-series-and-istorya/|work=Manila Bulletin|date=January 8, 2018}}</ref> In December 2018, she became [[Spotify]]'s No. 1 most streamed artist in the Philippines.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://push.abs-cbn.com/2018/12/9/fresh-scoops/moira-dela-torre-is-spotifys-no-1-most-streamed-197720|title=Moira dela Torre is Spotify's No. 1 Most Streamed local artist|last=Cagape|first=Kristhoff|website=push.abs-cbn.com|language=en|access-date=2019-09-03}}</ref> ''Malaya'' album was certified 8× Platinum in the Philippines with more than 120,000 copies sold, making it the best-selling album in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|last=News|first=ABS-CBN|date=2020-10-18|title=Moira dela Torre turns emotional over multiplatinum certifications for her albums|url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/10/18/20/moira-dela-torre-turns-emotional-over-multiplatinum-certifications-for-her-albums|access-date=2021-11-02|website=ABS-CBN News|language=en}}</ref>

In 2019, she also made a wedding song ft. Jason Hernandez "Ikaw at Ako" and have some tune of [[Pachelbel's Canon|Canon]].{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

Also, she became one of the judges in ''[[Idol Philippines]]'', and on September of the said year, staged her second major concert at the Araneta Coliseum titled Braver.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

In March 2021 her second studio album "''Patawad"'' was released, the album spawned the hit singles "Mabagal" featuring Daniel Padilla, "Patawad, Paalam" and "Paalam" featuring Ben&Ben.<ref>{{Cite web|last=C.A.|title=Review: Moira's 'Patawad' Album {{!}} uDOu PH|url=https://udou.ph/music/latest-drop/review-moiras-patawad-album/|access-date=2021-11-02|language=en-US}}</ref> ''Patawad'' album was certified Platinum in the Philippines with more than 15,000 copies sold.<ref>{{Cite web|last=News|first=ABS-CBN|date=2020-10-18|title=Moira dela Torre turns emotional over multiplatinum certifications for her albums|url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/10/18/20/moira-dela-torre-turns-emotional-over-multiplatinum-certifications-for-her-albums|access-date=2021-11-02|website=ABS-CBN News|language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Dela Torre is a devout [[Christians|Christian]], but has said "I don't like imposing my convictions on other people." She considers writing and performing worship songs her passion.<ref>{{cite web |title=Moira dela Torre on writing worship songs: "It's my passion talaga" |url=https://push.abs-cbn.com/2017/12/8/videos/moira-dela-torre-on-writing-worship-songs-its-m-172759 |website=[[ABS-CBN Digital Media|PUSH.com]] |publisher=[[ABS-CBN Digital Media]] |date=August 12, 2017 |access-date=September 12, 2019 |quote=As a Christian, I don't find any other way to live my life than to be guided by his Word. [...] I don't like imposing my convictions on other people.}}</ref> Dela Torre married singer Jason Marvin Hernandez on January 14, 2019.<ref>{{cite news |title=IN PHOTOS: Moira dela Torre and Jason Marvin Hernandez's garden wedding |url=https://www.rappler.com/entertainment/news/220970-photos-moira-dela-torre-jason-marvin-hernandez-wedding-january-2019 |work=Rappler|access-date=January 14, 2019}}</ref>

Of her "ritual" before performing, Dela Torre told the Philippine magazine ''[[Yes! (Philippine magazine)|Yes!]]'': "Before performing, I just go to the ''banyo'' [restroom] and poop. Otherwise, I'll just fart [while performing], which I've done a number of times."<ref name="yes"/> She also revealed that two months after her marriage, she underwent a botched [[Rhinoplasty|nose job]], from which she recovered three months later while confined at home. According to Dela Torre, the non-invasive procedure had resulted in [[necrosis]] that put her "at high risk of being blind, of aneurysm, of heart attack, and stroke". Despite this, she said she had forgiven the surgeon who carried out the procedure and did not file a complaint.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Guno |first1=Niña |title=Moira Dela Torre reveals risk of going blind due to nose job gone wrong |url=https://entertainment.inquirer.net/342203/moira-dela-torre-reveals-risk-of-going-blind-due-to-nose-job-gone-wrong |work=Philippine Daily Inquirer|date=August 15, 2019 |access-date=September 12, 2019}}</ref>
