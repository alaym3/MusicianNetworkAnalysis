'''Freya Ridings''' (born 19 April 1994) is an English singer, songwriter and multi-instrumentalist. Ridings rose to prominence in 2017 with her ballad, "[[Lost Without You (Freya Ridings song)|Lost Without You]]", which became a top ten hit on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. She followed this with the release of her debut extended play, ''[[You Mean the World to Me (EP)|You Mean the World to Me]]'' (2019). [[Freya Ridings (album)|Her self-titled debut album]] was supported by the single "[[Castles (song)|Castles]]", which would become her international breakthrough.

==Early life==
Ridings was raised in [[North London]] and grew up in [[Palmers Green]]. She is the daughter of British actor and musician [[Richard Ridings]], and learned guitar from watching him play. She attended [[St Christopher School, Letchworth|St Christopher School]] in Letchworth, followed by the [[BRIT School]] from the age of 16.<ref name="Smooth">{{cite web|url=https://www.smoothradio.com/features/freya-ridings-album-lost-without-you-age-tour/|title=Freya Ridings: Meet the talented 'Lost Without You' singer-songwriter|publisher=[[Smooth Radio (2014)|Smooth Radio]]|date=30 July 2018|access-date=2 August 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Ridings released her debut single, "Blackout", on 5 May 2017. She released the single "[[Maps (Yeah Yeah Yeahs song)#Freya Ridings version|Maps]]" on 30 June 2017. On 22 September 2017 she released her debut live album ''Live at St Pancras Old Church''. After releasing the album she went on to her first full headline tour in the UK. She spent most of 2017 supporting the likes of [[Tears for Fears]], [[Tash Sultana]] and [[Lewis Capaldi]].<ref name="Smooth"/> She released "[[Lost Without You (Freya Ridings song)|Lost Without You]]" on 3 November 2017. The song peaked at number 9 in the [[UK Singles Chart]], after being featured on the reality show ''[[Love Island (2015 TV series, series 4)|Love Island]]'' in July 2018. It was subsequently chosen by [[Scott Mills]]' as his 'Tune of the Week' on his [[Scott Mills (radio show)|Radio 1 show]] in August 2018. She released her second live album, ''Live at Omeara'', on 30 March 2018. She released the single "Ultraviolet" on 15 June 2018.

In 2019, she released the single "[[You Mean the World to Me (Freya Ridings song)|You Mean the World to Me]]", which was re-produced by [[Greg Kurstin]], followed by an extended play of the same name. She also announced her [[Freya Ridings (album)|self-titled debut album]], with an original release date of 31 May 2019, but it was pushed back to 19 July 2019.
