'''Anna Cooke Kendrick''' (born August 9, 1985)<ref name="annabio"/> is an American actress and singer. She is the recipient of [[List of awards and nominations received by Anna Kendrick|various accolades]], including nominations for an [[Academy Award]], a [[Primetime Emmy Award]], a [[Tony Award]] and a [[Screen Actors Guild Award]]; making her one of the youngest people to be nominated for the "[[Triple Crown of Acting]]".

She began her career performing in theater productions at a young age. Her first starring role was in the 1998 [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] musical ''[[High Society (musical)|High Society]]'', for which she earned a nomination for the [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2016/11/14/501714265/anna-kendrick-says-acting-is-the-way-that-i-learn-about-other-people|title= Anna Kendrick Says Acting Is 'The Way That I Learn About Other People'|website= [[NPR]]|access-date= May 11, 2020}}</ref> She made her film debut in the musical comedy ''[[Camp (2003 film)|Camp]]'' (2003), and rose to prominence for her role in ''[[The Twilight Saga (film series)|The Twilight Saga]]'' (2008–2012). Kendrick achieved further recognition for the comedy-drama film ''[[Up in the Air (2009 film)|Up in the Air]]'' (2009), which earned her a nomination for the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]], and for her starring role in the [[Pitch Perfect (film series)|''Pitch Perfect'' film series]] (2012–17).

Kendrick also had prominent roles in films such as the action comedy ''[[Scott Pilgrim vs. the World]]'' (2010), the comedy drama ''[[50/50 (2011 film)|50/50]]'' (2011), the crime drama ''[[End of Watch]]'' (2012), the musical fantasy ''[[Into the Woods (film)|Into the Woods]]'' (2014), the drama ''[[Cake (2014 film)|Cake]]'' (2014), the comedy ''[[Mike and Dave Need Wedding Dates]]'' (2016), the animated comedy ''[[Trolls (film)|Trolls]]'' (2016), the comic thriller ''[[A Simple Favor (film)|A Simple Favor]]'' (2018), the fantasy comedy ''[[Noelle (2019 film)|Noelle]]'' (2019), and the animated sequel ''[[Trolls World Tour]]'' (2020). She also sang on various soundtracks for her films and published a memoir, ''[[Scrappy Little Nobody]]'', in 2016.

== Career ==
=== 1998–2007: Early career, theater, and film debut ===
[[File:AnnaKendrick09TIFF-cropped.jpg|thumb|upright|Kendrick at the 2009 [[Toronto International Film Festival]]]]
Kendrick started her career as a child, auditioning for theater productions in [[New York City]]. When she was 12 years of age, she played a supporting role in the 1998 Broadway musical ''[[High Society (musical)|High Society]]''. Her performance earned her a [[Theater World Award]], as well as nominations for Featured Actress in a Musical at the [[Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Featured Actress in a Musical|Drama Desk Awards]] and [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical|Tony Awards]]. She went on to play a supporting role in the 2003 [[New York City Opera]] production of [[Stephen Sondheim]]'s musical ''[[A Little Night Music]]''.

Kendrick made her film debut in the musical film ''[[Camp (2003 film)|Camp]]''. Her performance as the nerdy Fritzi Wagner earned her a nomination for the [[19th Independent Spirit Awards|Independent Spirit Award]] for Best Debut Performance. She went on to play an ambitious high school debater in ''[[Rocket Science (film)|Rocket Science]]'' (2007), for which she earned a subsequent [[23rd Independent Spirit Awards|Independent Spirit Award]] nomination for Best Supporting Female.

=== 2008–2011: ''Twilight'' and ''Up in the Air'' ===
Kendrick rose to prominence in 2008 for the fantasy romance ''[[Twilight (2008 film)|Twilight]]'', based on [[Stephenie Meyer]]'s [[Twilight (Meyer novel)|novel of the same name]], which became a major blockbuster at the box office. Kendrick played Jessica Stanley, a friend of protagonist [[Bella Swan]]. In 2009, she appeared in the comedy ''[[The Marc Pease Experience]]'', played her first leading role in the crime thriller ''[[Elsewhere (2009 film)|Elsewhere]]'', and reprised her role as Jessica Stanley in Twilight's sequel, ''[[The Twilight Saga: New Moon|New Moon]]''.

She then starred alongside [[George Clooney]] in director [[Jason Reitman]]'s ''[[Up in the Air (2009 film)|Up in the Air]]'' (2009). Her performance as an ambitious college graduate was praised by critics, who called it "funny and touching"<ref>{{cite web|last=Lumenick|first=Lou|title=Top flight!|url=https://nypost.com/2009/12/04/top-flight/|newspaper=[[New York Post]]|date=December 4, 2009|access-date=February 13, 2016}}</ref> and commented that she "grabs every scene she's in".<ref>{{cite web|last=Dargis|first=Manohla|title=Neither Here Nor There|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/12/04/movies/04upinair.html|work=The New York Times|date=December 3, 2009|access-date=February 13, 2016}}</ref> She was nominated for Best Supporting Actress at several awards ceremonies, including the [[82nd Academy Awards|Academy Awards]], [[67th Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe Awards]], [[16th Screen Actors Guild Awards|Screen Actors Guild Awards]], and [[63rd British Academy Film Awards|BAFTA Awards]].

In 2010, Kendrick again portrayed Jessica Stanley in the Twilight saga's third installment, ''[[The Twilight Saga: Eclipse|Eclipse]]''. Later that year, she appeared in ''[[Scott Pilgrim vs. the World]]'', in which she played the sister of the title character. The film did not fare well at the box office, but has emerged as a [[cult classic]] over time. In 2011, she appeared in the critically acclaimed comedy-drama ''[[50/50 (2011 film)|50/50]]'', playing an inexperienced therapist to a cancer patient played by [[Joseph Gordon-Levitt]]. Later that year, she reprised her role of Jessica Stanley in Twilight's fourth installment, ''[[Breaking Dawn – Part 1]]'' (2011), her final on-screen appearance in ''The Twilight Saga''.

She has been a member of the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] in the Actor's Branch since 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://ew.com/article/2010/06/25/academy-new-members-bono-gabourey-sidibe/ |title=Academy invites 135 new members |last=Karger |first=Dave |work=Entertainment Weekly |date=June 25, 2010 |access-date=July 15, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref>

=== 2012–2015: ''Pitch Perfect'' and ''Into the Woods'' ===
In 2012, Kendrick featured as part of the ensemble cast of ''[[What to Expect When You're Expecting (film)|What to Expect When You're Expecting]]'', loosely based on the [[What to Expect When You're Expecting|pregnancy guide of the same name]]. Also that year, she lent her voice to the stop-motion animated film ''[[ParaNorman]]'', starred in the commercially successful crime drama ''[[End of Watch]]'', and appeared in [[Robert Redford]]'s political thriller ''[[The Company You Keep (film)|The Company You Keep]]''.

[[File: Anna Kendrick March 22, 2014 (cropped).jpg|thumb|upright=0.75|Kendrick at Geffen's Fundraiser in March 2014]]
Kendrick's most successful film of 2012 was the musical comedy ''[[Pitch Perfect]]'' (2012), loosely based on the non-fiction book ''Pitch Perfect: The Quest for Collegiate A Cappella Glory''. Kendrick played Beca Mitchell, a rebellious college freshman who joins an [[a cappella]] group called the Barden Bellas and finds that her more modern approach to music clashes with the traditional approach of the group's leader. The film emerged as a major commercial success and received mostly positive reviews from critics, who called Kendrick's performance "splendid", saying that she "hits just the right note between pithy and chummy."<ref>{{cite web|last=Richards|first=Olly|title=Pitch Perfect Review|url=https://www.empireonline.com/movies/pitch-perfect/review/|magazine=[[Empire (magazine)|Empire]]|date=June 20, 2012|access-date=February 13, 2016}}</ref>

In 2013, Kendrick featured in the romantic comedy-drama ''[[Drinking Buddies]]'', which received mostly positive reviews from critics, as well as the largely panned fantasy comedy ''[[Rapture-Palooza]]''.

At the [[2014 Sundance Film Festival|Sundance Film Festival]] in January 2014, Kendrick was featured in three films. She played leading roles in the comedy-drama ''[[Happy Christmas (film)|Happy Christmas]]'' and the horror comedy ''[[The Voices (film)|The Voices]]'', which both received generally favorable reviews from critics, as well as a supporting role in the [[zombie comedy]] ''[[Life After Beth]]''. Both ''Happy Christmas'' and ''Life After Beth'' received limited releases later in 2014, while ''The Voices'' was given a limited release in early 2015.

At the [[2014 Toronto International Film Festival|Toronto International Film Festival]] in September 2014, Kendrick was featured in two films. She starred in the musical romance ''[[The Last Five Years (film)|The Last Five Years]]'', an adaptation of the [[The Last Five Years|off-Broadway musical of the same name]]. While the film itself received mixed reviews, Kendrick garnered widespread critical acclaim for her performance. She also played a supporting role in the comedy-drama ''[[Cake (2014 film)|Cake]]''. ''Cake'' was eventually given a wide release in January 2015, while ''The Last Five Years'' received a limited release in February 2015.

Kendrick was next seen playing [[Cinderella]] in [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney's]] ''[[Into the Woods (film)|Into the Woods]]'' (2014), director [[Rob Marshall]]'s film adaptation of [[Stephen Sondheim]]'s [[Into the Woods|musical of the same name]]. Kendrick was a part of the large ensemble cast. The film became a major commercial success and received mostly positive reviews from critics.

Kendrick competed in the first season of ''[[Lip Sync Battle]]'' against [[John Krasinski]], one of the show's executive producers, in an episode aired in April 2015. She first performed "[[Steal My Girl]]" by [[One Direction]], in which she jokingly revealed the object of her affection to be Krasinski's newlywed wife, [[Emily Blunt]]. During her performance, Kendrick stares adoringly at images of Blunt on a monitor while sporting a t-shirt imprinted with a heart-shaped photograph of her and Blunt together. Kendrick also performed "[[Booty (Jennifer Lopez song)|Booty]]" by [[Jennifer Lopez]], in which she makes a surprise appearance at the end of the song. Krasinski performed "[[Bye Bye Bye]]" by [[NSYNC]] and "[[Proud Mary]]" by [[Ike & Tina Turner]]. The audience declared Kendrick the winner. The episode garnered over 1.75 million U.S. viewers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2015/04/17/thursday-cable-ratings-lip-sync-battle-wins-night-vikings-wwe-smackdown-impractical-jokers-more/390465/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150418095923/http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/2015/04/17/thursday-cable-ratings-lip-sync-battle-wins-night-vikings-wwe-smackdown-impractical-jokers-more/390465/|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 18, 2015|title=Thursday Cable Ratings: 'Lip Sync Battle' Wins Night, 'Vikings', 'WWE Smackdown', 'Impractical Jokers', 'Ridiculousness' & More|work=TV by the Numbers|date=April 17, 2015|access-date=May 14, 2020|first=Sara|last=Bibel}}</ref>

Kendrick played a supporting role in ''[[Digging for Fire]]'', which premiered at the [[2015 Sundance Film Festival]] and received a limited release in August of that year. In May 2015, she reprised her role of Beca Mitchell in ''[[Pitch Perfect 2]]''. The film, which followed Mitchell in her senior year of college as the co-president of the Barden Bellas, emerged as a major box office blockbuster and surpassed the success of the first film.
