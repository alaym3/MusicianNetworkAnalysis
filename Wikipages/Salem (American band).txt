'''Salem''' (sometimes stylized as '''S4LEM''' or '''SALEM''') is an American band from [[Traverse City, Michigan]]. It was founded by Heather Marlatt, Jack Donoghue and John Holland.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/salem-1|title=Salem|website=[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]]|first=Julianne Escobedo|last=Shepherd|date=September 1, 2011|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref> Salem is considered one of the pioneers of the [[witch house (genre)|witch house]] genre.

In 2020, the group released a mixtape, ''Stay Down'', followed by their second album, ''[[Fires in Heaven]]'', both without Marlatt,<ref name="Pitchfork Red River"/> who is disputing her ouster.<ref name=controversy/>

==Career==
Salem was formed in 2008 by Jack Donoghue, John Holland, and Heather Marlatt. Salem released the debut studio album, ''[[King Night]]'', in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/king-night/salem|title=King Night by Salem|website=[[Metacritic]]|publisher=[[CBS Interactive]]|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref> It was included on the year-end lists by [[AllMusic]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/blog/post/allmusics-favorite-albums-of-2010|title=AllMusic's Favorite Albums of 2010|website=[[AllMusic]]|date=January 4, 2011|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref> ''[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://diymag.com/archive/diy-albums-of-the-year-the-winner-is|title=DIY Albums Of The Year: The Winner Is...|website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]|date=December 17, 2010|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref> ''[[NME]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/list/50-best-albums-of-2010-1405|title=50 Best Albums Of 2010|website=[[NME]]|date=December 1, 2010|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref> ''[[The Quietus]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thequietus.com/articles/05391-quietus-albums-of-the-year-2010|title=Angst Music For Sex People: Quietus Albums Of The Year 2010 & Spotify Playlist|website=[[The Quietus]]|first=Luke|last=Turner|date=December 10, 2010|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref> and ''[[Stereogum]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/593442/stereogums-top-50-albums-of-2010/franchises/list/|title=Stereogum's Top 50 Albums Of 2010|website=[[Stereogum]]|date=December 8, 2010|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref>

Salem had been inactive since 2012, with no new releases since their 2011 EP ''[[I'm Still in the Night]]''. In 2016, a new Salem album was announced via photographer [[Wolfgang Tillmans]]' Instagram account.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2016/08/03/salem-to-bring-back-witch-house-with-first-album-in-six-years/|title=Salem to bring back witch house with first album in six years|work=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]|first=April Clare|last=Welsh|date=August 3, 2016|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref> In that year, Salem officially returned with a remix of Tillmans' song "Make It Up as You Go Along", which was included on his ''Device Control'' EP.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/08/22/wolfgang-tillmans-salem-remix-make-it-up-as-you-go-along-video|title=The Video For Salem's Remix Of Wolfgang Tillmans Will Give You Nightmares|website=[[The Fader]]|first=Owen|last=Myers|date=August 22, 2016|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2016/08/22/wolfgang-tillmans-make-it-up-as-you-go-along-salem-remix/|title=Salem return with remix of Wolfang Tillmans' 'Make It Up As You Go Along'|website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]|first=Claire|last=Lobenfeld|date=August 22, 2016|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/67358-salem-remix-wolfgang-tillmans-make-it-up-as-you-go-along-listen/|title=Salem Remix Wolfgang Tillmans' "Make It Up As You Go Along": Listen|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Kevin|last=Lozano|date=August 22, 2016|accessdate=February 10, 2020}}</ref> 

The band was inactive until May 2020, when they returned with a new mixtape titled ''Stay Down'', which was presented on NTS Radio.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Salem return after a lengthy silence with this remarkable mix, titled STAY DOWN.|url=https://www.nts.live/shows/remote-utopias/episodes/salem-2nd-may-2020|last=|first=|date=|website=NTS|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> The return of the band brings with it a controversy with Heather still in dispute.<ref name=controversy>{{cite web |last1=Heather |first1=Marlatt |title=Instagram post |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B-dXS7PD_zZ/ |website=Controversy |publisher=Heather Marie Marlatt |accessdate=18 September 2020}}</ref>

Salem released their first single in nearly a decade "Starfall" on September 18, 2020, via [[Mad Decent]]'s Decent Distribution. The music video follows the band's trip with veteran storm trackers through Tulsa, Oklahoma and Dallas as well as through Texas during tornado season. It was directed by Donoghue and Holland along Tommy Malekoff.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/salem-return-with-video-for-new-song-starfall-watch/|title=SALEM Return With Video for New Song “Starfall”: Watch|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|last1=Bloom|first1=Madison|date=September 18, 2020}}</ref> In a press release, it was stated that the song was "a signal of additional work to come".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/indie/salem-starfall-video-comeback/|title=Influential Witch House Group Salem Return After Ten Years With A Storm-Chasing ‘Starfall’ Video|work=[[Uproxx]]|last1= Rossignol|first1=Derrick|date=September 18, 2020}}</ref> Their second album ''[[Fires in Heaven]]'', which comes ten years after their debut, was released on October 30, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/nz/album/fires-in-heaven/1535710389|title=Fires In Heaven by Salem|work=iTunes, Apple Music|date=October 30, 2020|access-date=October 16, 2020}}</ref> Along its pre-order on October 16, a new single called "Red River" was unveiled.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/nz/album/red-river-single/1534374422|title=Red River – Single by Salem|work=iTunes, Apple Music|date=October 16, 2020|access-date=October 16, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Pitchfork Red River">{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/salem-announce-new-album-fires-in-heaven-share-new-song-listen/|title=SALEM Announce New Album Fires in Heaven, Share New Song: Listen|work=Pitchfork|date=October 16, 2020|author=Noah Yoo}}</ref>

==Style and influences==
According to Heather Phares of [[AllMusic]], Salem's sound blends ethereal [[electronica]] and slow [[Southern hip hop]] rhythms, and is noted for pioneering the music subgenre known as [[witch house (genre)|witch house]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/salem-mn0002540652/biography|title=SALEM: Biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|first=Heather|last=Phares|accessdate=November 26, 2019}}</ref>
