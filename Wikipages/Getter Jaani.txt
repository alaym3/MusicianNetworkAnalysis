'''Getter Jaani''' (born 3 February 1993)<ref>[http://www.delfi.ee/teemalehed/getter-jaani Delfi – Getter Jaani]</ref> is an [[Estonia]]n singer and actress. She represented [[Estonia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2011|Estonia]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2011]] with the song "[[Rockefeller Street]]".<ref>[http://publik.delfi.ee/news/inimesed/veel-uks-staar-lopetaja-getter-jaani-saab-juunis-kooliga-uhele-poole-hirm-on-kull-suur?id=71432325 Veel üks staar-lõpetaja! Getter Jaani saab juunis kooliga ühele poole: hirm on küll suur] {{in lang|et}}</ref>

==Early life==
Getter was born and raised in [[Tallinn]]. In 2015, she graduated [[night school]] and was awarded a [[high school diploma]].<ref>[http://www.epl.ee/artikkel/593365 Eesti Laul 2011 finaali võitis Getter Jaani looga Rockefeller Street] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110720125820/http://www.epl.ee/artikkel/593365 |date=20 July 2011 }} {{in lang|et}}</ref>

Besides Estonian, she also has Russian ancestry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://limon.postimees.ee/6437283/estonskaya-pevica-getter-yaani-rasskazala-o-lyubvi-russkih-muzhchin-i-rodstvennikah-v-moskve|title=Эстонская певица Геттер Яани рассказала о любви русских мужчин и родственниках в Москве|date=2018-10-24|website=Limon|language=ru|access-date=2020-03-22}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Getter Jaani 20120515 by Ahsoous.jpg|thumb|right|220px|Jaani performing in May 2012.]]
Jaani first became publicly known in Estonia in 2009 as a participant in [[Eesti otsib superstaari (season 3)|the third season]] of ''[[Eesti otsib superstaari]]'', the Estonian version of ''[[Pop Idol]]''. She finished fourth and her performance gained her role of [[Sharpay Evans]] in the Estonian version of ''[[High School Musical]]''.

Jaani represented [[Estonia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2011|Estonia]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2011]], with her entry "[[Rockefeller Street]]" coming in 24th place in the final.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://eurovision.tv/event/dusseldorf-2011/grand-final?sorting=rank&direction=asc|title=Grand Final 2011 Scoreboard|website=eurovision.tv|access-date=March 31, 2020}}</ref> That same year, she participated as a celebrity contestant on ''[[Tantsud tähtedega]]'', the Estonian version of ''[[Dancing with the Stars]]''.

In 2012, Jaani presented the scores [[Estonia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2012|on behalf of Estonia]] for the grand-finals of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2012]] in [[Baku]], Azerbaijan.<ref name="voting">{{cite web|title=Running order for the voting tonight|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=running_order_for_the_voting_tonight|publisher=[[European Broadcasting Union]]|access-date=26 May 2012}}</ref>

In 2013, Jaani landed the role of Sandy in the musical ''[[Grease (musical)|Grease]]''.

Jaani's entry song Rockefeller Street became an [[internet meme]] after a video of two Japanese men playing a "[[Nightcore]]" version of the song using the rhythm game [[osu!]] following them dancing to the song became popular on social media. Their dance moves also became popular, spreading to apps and sites such as [[TikTok]] and [[YouTube]] in 2018. The original song is in E♭ minor while the meme nightcore version is in G minor, having been sped up by 1.25x with the pitch also increased. Jaani spoke out about this in 2019 on "[[Eesti Televisioon|etv]]", "When I heard the new version which is a lot faster, the original Rockefeller Street seems like a great lullaby next to it."{{citation needed|date=October 2021}}
