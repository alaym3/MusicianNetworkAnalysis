'''Zachary Knight Galifianakis''' ({{lang-el|Ζαχαρίας Γαλιφιανάκης}}; {{IPAc-en|ˌ|g|æ|l|ɪ|f|ə|ˈ|n|æ|k|ɪ|s}}, {{IPA-el|ɣalifçaˈnacis}}; born October 1, 1969)<ref name="filmref">{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/51/Zach-Galifianakis.html|title=Zach Galifianakis Biography (1969–)|publisher=filmreference.com|access-date=June 5, 2010}}</ref> is an American actor, comedian, musician and writer. He appeared in ''[[Comedy Central Presents]]'' special and presented his show ''[[Late World with Zach]]'' on [[VH1]]. He has starred in films, such as [[The Hangover (film series)|''The Hangover'' trilogy]] (2009–2013), ''[[Due Date]]'' (2010), ''[[The Campaign (film)|The Campaign]]'' (2012), ''[[Birdman (film)|Birdman or (The Unexpected Virtue of Ignorance)]]'' (2014) and ''[[Masterminds (2016 film)|Masterminds]]'' (2016). He starred in animated films, including ''[[Puss in Boots (2011)|Puss in Boots]]'' (2011), ''[[The Lego Batman Movie]]'' (2017), ''[[Missing Link (2019 film)|Missing Link]]'' (2019), and ''[[Ron's Gone Wrong]]'' (2021). Galifianakis currently hosts the [[Funny or Die]] website talk show ''[[Between Two Ferns with Zach Galifianakis]]''. He starred in the [[FX (TV channel)|FX]] series ''[[Baskets (TV series)|Baskets]]'' and was nominated for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series]] in 2017.

==Early life==
Galifianakis was born in [[Wilkesboro, North Carolina]].<ref name=filmref/> His mother, Mary Frances (''née'' Cashion), was a community arts center owner, and his father, Harry, a heating oil vendor.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grreporter.info/en/after_hangover_iii_zack_galifianakis_will_shoot_war_epic_film_about_crete/9391|title=After|work=grreporter.info}}</ref> His mother is of [[Scottish people|Scottish]] ancestry, and his paternal grandparents, Mike Galifianakis and Sophia Kastrinakis, were emigrants from [[Crete]], [[Greece]].<ref name="NYTimes_20090531">{{cite news|last=Wray|first=John|title=The Making of Zach Galifianakis|work=[[The New York Times]] |date=May 28, 2009 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/31/magazine/31Galifianakis-t.html |access-date=June 6, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.therecordofwilkes.com/newsa.asp?edition_number=302&pg=D |title=Mr. Paul Lindsay Cashion, age 89, died Tuesday, August 16, 2005 |publisher=Therecordofwilkes.com |access-date=August 10, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120407121536/http://www.therecordofwilkes.com/newsa.asp?edition_number=302&pg=D |archive-date=April 7, 2012  }}</ref> Galifianakis was baptized in his father's [[Greek Orthodox Church|Greek Orthodox]] church.<ref name="brianpalm">{{cite web| url=http://www.brianmpalmer.com/galifianakis.htm| title=Brian M. ==Career==
===Early work===
After his television debut ''[[Boston Common (TV series)|Boston Common]]'', Galifianakis joined ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' for two weeks.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gq.com/entertainment/humor/201008/tracy-morgan-paul-rudd-zach-galifianakis-comedy-issue|title=Three of Our Most Serious Minds Confer...|author=Devin Friedman|publisher=GQ}}</ref> He has stated "I worked on ''Saturday Night Live'' for two weeks, and Britney Spears was the host one week when I was doing it. Wrote a sketch, Will Ferrell was going to play a bodyguard to her belly button, and we were going to shrink Will down to fit into a belly button. ...she just stared at me after I explained it to her. And then she finally goes 'Yeah that's funny.'" <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M_PWrgSmTLk| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211030/M_PWrgSmTLk| archive-date=2021-10-30|title=Actually Me: Zach Galifianakis|editor=Jeremy Ray Smolik|publisher=GQ}}{{cbignore}}</ref> Galifianakis co-starred in the film ''[[Out Cold (2001 film)|Out Cold]]'', and had small roles in ''[[Corky Romano]]'', ''[[Below (film)|Below]]'', ''[[Bubble Boy (film)|Bubble Boy]]'', ''[[Heartbreakers (2001 film)|Heartbreakers]]'', ''[[Into the Wild (film)|Into the Wild]]'', ''[[Super High Me]]'', ''Little Fish Strange Pond'' and ''Largo''.

[[File:Zach Galifianakis Mar08 2.jpg|thumb|left|Galifianakis on Inside Joke in [[New York City]] in 2008]]
In September 2001, he appeared in an episode of ''[[Comedy Central Presents]]''. It included a stand-up routine, a segment with a piano, and [[a cappella]] group The Night Owls  (introduced as his "12 ex-girlfriends") singing "[[Eternal Flame (song)|Eternal Flame]]" by [[The Bangles]] while he made jokes. In 2002, he hosted his own VH1 talk show called ''[[Late World with Zach]]''. It featured many of his friends and regular performers from the LA comedy and music venue Largo where he appeared frequently during this time period. One episode featured Largo regulars Jon Brion and Rhett Miller as musical guests. He played [[Davis (Tru Calling character)|Davis]] in the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] drama series ''[[Tru Calling]]''.<ref name="brianpalm"/> He appeared many times on ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]]'' and played Frisbee in ''[[Reno 911!]]''.

Galifianakis played Alan Finger on the [[Comedy Central]] show ''[[Dog Bites Man]]'', a fake news program that caught people during candid moments thinking they were being interviewed by a real news crew. He also guest-starred in the episode of the Comedy Central show ''[[The Sarah Silverman Program]]'' as Fred the Homeless Guy. He also had a recurring guest role as a doctor on the animated [[Adult Swim]] show ''[[Tom Goes to the Mayor]]'' and appeared in several episodes of ''[[Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job!]]'' in a recurring role as Tairy Greene. In 2006, Galifianakis appeared in [[Fiona Apple]]'s music video for the song "[[Not About Love#Video|Not About Love]]", where he is seen [[lip sync|lip-syncing]] the lyrics to the song. A year later, [[Kanye West]] employed Galifianakis and indie rock musician [[Will Oldham]] for similar purposes in the second version of the video for his song "[[Can't Tell Me Nothing]]". In June 2006, Galifianakis released the single "Come On and Get It (Up in 'Dem Guts)",<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UMJdeqi3g4U| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211030/UMJdeqi3g4U| archive-date=2021-10-30|title=Alan Finger talks about his song, Up In Them Guts|access-date=August 11, 2012}}{{cbignore}}</ref> a comedic hip-hop dance song which features Apple's vocals.

Galifianakis, [[Patton Oswalt]], [[Brian Posehn]] and [[Maria Bamford]], are the four [[The Comedians of Comedy|Comedians of Comedy]], a periodic packaged comedy tour in the style of [[The Original Kings of Comedy]] and the [[Blue Collar Comedy Tour]]. They chose to perform at live rock clubs as opposed to comedy clubs to try to reach a different audience. Much of the tour was taped and has been featured in both a short-lived TV series on Comedy Central and a full-length movie that has appeared at [[South by Southwest|SXSW]] and on [[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime]]. On February 22, 2008, he made an appearance on the ''[[Jackassworld.com: 24 Hour Takeover]]''. He interviewed various members of the ''[[Jackass (TV series)|Jackass]]'' cast. Galifianakis starred in first leading role in the independent film ''[[Visioneers]]'' which premiered in 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://visioneersthemovie.com/ |title=Visioneers - Welcome to the Jeffers Corporation|work=visioneersthemovie.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150525070635/http://visioneersthemovie.com/|archive-date=May 25, 2015}}</ref> The film which was released on direct-to-DVD. That same year, Galifianakis appeared in a web video series of advertisements for [[Absolut]] vodka, along with [[Tim Heidecker]] and [[Eric Wareheim]], creating a parody of the ''[[Golden Girls]]'' in which one has a deep anger issue, breaking the [[fourth wall]] in exasperation and outright violence on the set.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5p0QtJMKt1s A Vodka Movie by Zach Galifianakis, Tim and Eric<!-- Bot generated title -->] from YouTube</ref> He also completed the pilot ''Speed Freaks'' for Comedy Central.

Zach's 2006 stand-up concert film ''[[Zach Galifianakis Live at the Purple Onion]]'' was one of the first original programs from [[Netflix]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pro.imdb.com/company/co0144901/?ref_=ttco_co_1|title=Netflix (US) Distributor - Production |publisher=[[IMDb]]|access-date=May 3, 2018 |url-access=subscription}}</ref>

===Mainstream performances===
Galifianakis played Alan Garner in the hit comedy ''[[The Hangover]]'' and earned the [[MTV Movie Awards|MTV Movie Award]] for the Best Comedic Performance.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/37868/the-twilight-saga-new-moon-a-surprise-winner-mtv-movie-awards-new-eclipse-footage|title=The Twilight Saga: New Moon a "Surprise" Winner at MTV Movie Awards / New Eclipse Footage|publisher=DreadCentral}}</ref> He was also prominently advertised in subsequent films that featured him in supporting roles, such as ''[[G-Force (film)|G-Force]]'', ''[[Youth in Revolt]]'' and ''[[Up in the Air (2009 film)|Up in the Air]]''.

Galifianakis starred in the [[HBO]] series ''[[Bored to Death]]'' and hosted ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' on March 6, 2010 [[Saturday Night Live (season 35)|during the show's 35th season]], during which he shaved his beard mid-show for a sketch, and closed the show wearing a fake one.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbc.com/saturday-night-live/video/clips/snl-backstage-zach-shaves-his-beard/1207127/|title=clip from NBC|publisher=[[NBC]]}}</ref> He hosted again on March 12, 2011 and shaved his head this time, in a [[Mr. T]]-like hairstyle, which was allegedly supposed to be used for a sketch that never aired due to time constraints.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/03/15/snl-backstage-zach-galifianakis_n_835908.html|title=SNL Backstage: Zach Galifianakis Shaves Head Like Mr. T (VIDEO)|work=[[The Huffington Post]]|first=Carol|last=Hartsell|date=March 15, 2011}}</ref>

In 2010, he starred in several films, including ''[[Dinner for Schmucks]]'', ''[[It's Kind of a Funny Story (film)|It's Kind of a Funny Story]]'', and ''[[Due Date]]''.<ref>{{cite news |title = Ryan Fleck's It's Kind of a Funny Story Begins Production|url = http://www.slashfilm.com/2009/11/30/ryan-flecks-its-kind-of-a-funny-story-begins-production/|author=Sciretta, Peter |work = [[/Film]] |date = November 30, 2009 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20100306030804/http://www.slashfilm.com/2009/11/30/ryan-flecks-its-kind-of-a-funny-story-begins-production/ |archive-date = March 6, 2010 |url-status=dead |df = mdy-all }}</ref> On October 29, 2010, while debating [[marijuana]] legalization on the show HBO's ''[[Real Time with Bill Maher]]'', Galifianakis appeared to have smoked marijuana on live television;<ref>{{cite news| url=http://politicalticker.blogs.cnn.com/2010/11/01/not-one-toke-over-the-line-2/?hpt=T2 |publisher=CNN | title=Not one toke over the line? | date=November 1, 2010}}</ref> host Bill Maher denied that it was real marijuana in an interview with [[Wolf Blitzer]] during an episode of ''The Situation Room''. In 2011, he reprised his role for ''[[The Hangover Part II]]'', which was set in [[Thailand]]<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.time.com/time/arts/article/0,8599,2073913,00.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110528133544/http://www.time.com/time/arts/article/0,8599,2073913,00.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=May 28, 2011|title=The Hangover Part II: The Wolf Pack Is Back, and This Time They've Brought a Monkey |date=May 25, 2011|work=Time Magazine|access-date=June 9, 2011|first=Mary|last=Pols}}</ref> and voiced Humpty Dumpty in ''[[Puss in Boots (2011 film)|Puss in Boots]]''. Galifianakis starred alongside Will Ferrell in [[Jay Roach]]'s 2012 political comedy ''[[The Campaign (film)|The Campaign]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://collider.com/zach-galifianakis-puss-in-boots-hangover-3-interview/122262/ |title=Zach Galifianakis Talks Puss In Boots, The Hangover 3, Dog Fight, Bored To Death |publisher=Collider |access-date=August 10, 2012}}</ref> He received critical praise for his performance in the 2014 film ''[[Birdman (film)|Birdman or (The Unexpected Virtue of Ignorance)]]'', in which he starred with [[Michael Keaton]], [[Emma Stone]] and [[Edward Norton]]. In 2017, Galifianakis voiced [[Joker (character)|The Joker]] in ''[[The Lego Batman Movie]]''.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/lego-batman-movie-finds-joker-814851 | work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] | title='Lego Batman' Movie Finds Its Joker (Exclusive) | date=August 12, 2015 |first=Borys |last=Kit}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In August 2012, Galifianakis married Quinn Lundberg (from Edmonton, Alberta, Canada), co-founder of the Growing Voices charity, at the [[UBC Farm]] in [[Vancouver|Vancouver, British Columbia]]. On September 7, 2013, Lundberg gave birth to their first child, a boy, and Galifianakis missed the premiere of his film ''[[Are You Here]]'' to attend the birth.<ref name=ZachBaby>{{cite news|title=Galifianakis skips TIFF as wife about to give birth |url=http://www.torontosun.com/2013/09/08/galifianakis-skips-tiff-as-wife-about-to-give-birth|work=[[Toronto Sun]]|date=September 8, 2013}}</ref> He and Quinn had a second son on November 7, 2016.<ref name=ZachBaby2>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/babies/zach-galifianakis-welcomes-son-rufus-emmanuel/|title=Zach Galifianakis Welcomes Son Rufus Emmanuel}}</ref>

In January 2014, Galifianakis and ''A Night of a Thousand Vaginas'' co-star [[Sarah Silverman]] announced their intention to raise $20,000 to help fund the Texas Abortion Fund, part of a nationwide network of funds set up to assist women in obtaining abortions in states whose legislatures had placed restrictions on the practice. The fundraiser was set up in response to the passage of [[Texas H.B. 2]], which established several restrictions that forced a majority of the state's [[abortion clinic]]s to close.<ref name=TexasAbortion>{{cite news|title=Sarah Silverman, Zach Galifianakis Help Raise Money for Texas Women Seeking Abortions|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/sarah-silverman-zach-galifianakis-help-672639|date=January 20, 2014 | work=The Hollywood Reporter |first=Tina |last=Daunt}}</ref>

Galifianakis owns a farm in [[Sparta, North Carolina|Sparta, Alleghany County, North Carolina]],<ref>[http://blog.bad-diet.com/post/107998080426/zach-galifianakis-leads-an-atv-tour-of-his-farm-in Bad Diet HQ — Zach Galifianakis leads an ATV tour of his farm in] Retrieved June 30, 2018.</ref> and splits his time between the farm and his work. He said, "My farm is a place that I get to think clearly and pretend to know what I am doing."<ref name=ZachFarm>{{cite web|title=Zach Galifianakis Has a Farm (Doo-Dah, Doo-Dah)|url=http://www.vanityfair.com/online/oscars/2009/05/zach-galifianakis-has-a-farm-doodah-doodah|website=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]}}</ref>

Galifianakis befriended Marie "Mimi" Haist, a homeless woman in her 80s who was living in a Santa Monica laundromat for 18 years. Galifianakis got her an apartment across the street from the laundromat. The story was revealed in the 2015 documentary ''[[Queen Mimi]]''.<ref name=Zach>{{cite web|title=How This Homeless Woman Became Good Friends With Zach Galifianakis and Renée Zellweger |url=http://www.glamour.com/story/queen-mimi-documentary-homeless-woman-became-friends-zach-galifianakis-renee-zellweger |work=[[Glamour (magazine)|Glamour]]|date=May 13, 2016 |first=Jessica |last=Radloff }}</ref>
