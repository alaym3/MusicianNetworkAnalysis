'''Maye''' is a Venezuelan-born American singer-songwriter.

==Life==
Maye was born in [[Caracas]], and her family moved to [[Miami]] when she was young. Her father is [[Fernando Osorio]], a prolific singer-songwriter in his own right, who won a [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Tropical Song]] in 2004.<ref>{{cite web |title=Fernando Osorio Latin GRAMMY Awards |url=https://www.latingrammy.com/en/nominees/search?artist=%22Fernando+Osorio%22&field_nominee_work_value=&year=All&genre=All |website=The Latin Recording Academy}}</ref>
She first started writing songs when she was eight. Her first songs were in English, but her father encouraged her to write in Spanish as well.<ref>{{cite web |last=Vega |first=Leonardo |title=Conoce a Maye, una promesa de pop |url=https://www.indierocks.mx/musica/buenas-nuevas/conoce-a-maye-una-promesa-del-pop/ |language=Spanish |trans-title=Meet Maye, a promising new pop star |website=Indie Rocks! |access-date=May 22, 2021 |date=November 4, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==

Maye was on the songwriting team of [[Aitana (singer)|Aitana]]'s song "[[Nada Sale Mal]]", and was also part of the songwriting team of Aitana's debut album [[Spoiler (album)|Spoiler]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Credits: Aitana - Nada Sale Mal |url=https://genius.com/Aitana-nada-sale-mal-lyrics#song-info |website=Genius |access-date=May 31, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Spoiler - Aitana. Release Credits|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/release/spoiler-mr0005074463/credits |website=AllMusic |access-date=May 31, 2021}}</ref> She and her father were also part of the songwriting effort behind [[Danny Ocean (singer)|Danny Ocean]]'s song "Dime Tú", and she performed backing vocals as well.<ref>{{cite web |title=Credits (Dime Tú) |url=https://genius.com/Danny-ocean-dime-tu-lyrics#song-info |website=Genius |access-date=May 22, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Dime Tu - Danny Ocean. Credits |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/dime-tu-mw0003331771/credits |website=AllMusic |access-date=May 31, 2021}}</ref>

In 2019, she released her first single, "My Love". The song features a switch in tempo at the chorus. This track helped her get her first deal with [[Warner Chappell Music]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Leight |first1=Elias |title=Meet Maye, a Songwriter who Transfixed YouTube With a Bolero |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/maye-moody-903055/ |website=Rolling Stone |date=25 October 2019 |access-date=May 22, 2021}}</ref>

Soon after, she released her second single, "Tú". The track has over 21 million streams on Spotify, and [[Barack Obama]] listed it as one of his favorite songs of summer 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mamo |first1=Heran |title=See Which Artists Made it Onto Barack Obama's 2020 Summer Playlist |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9435742/barack-obama-2020-summer-playlist |website=Billboard |access-date=May 22, 2021}}</ref>
