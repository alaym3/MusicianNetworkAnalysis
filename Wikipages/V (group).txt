'''V''' were a British five-piece [[boy band|boyband]] made up of Antony Brant, Aaron Buckingham, Mark Harle, [[Kevin McDaid]] and Leon Pisani.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Barr |first1=Gordon |title=Our star sons |url=https://www.chroniclelive.co.uk/news/north-east-news/our-star-sons-1617682 |website=Chronicle Live |access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref> Founded in 2003, it disbanded after two years, in 2005. 

==Career==
V were formed in 2003 after a series of auditions by Prestige, the same management team behind [[Busted (band)|Busted]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Confession time for V|url=https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/whats-on/music/confession-time-for-v-1109431|website=Manchester Evening News |access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref> Group member Antony Brant came up with the band name "V" because V is the [[Roman numeral]] for five and there were five members in the band. V made their debut at the same time as [[McFly]] by supporting Busted's arena tour in the spring of 2004.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ipswichstar.co.uk/news/busted-v-good-1-100383|title=Busted V good|publisher=Ipswich Star|date=9 June 2004|access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref>

Their first [[single (music)|single]], "Blood, Sweat and Tears", was released in May 2004 and went into the [[UK Singles Chart]] at number 6.<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums">{{cite book

The music video showed the group dressed up in day-to-day jobs like an ice cream man and milkman.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.designermagazine.org/VInterview1.html|title=V Interview}}</ref> It charted at number 5 in the UK Singles Chart.

The third single, "You Stood Up" reached number 12 after an appearance on ''[[Top of the Pops]]'', but their debut album of the same name only made number 86 on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. In March 2005, V announced on their website their decision to split up, stating "it just hasn't happened for us as big as we would have liked."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.digitalspy.com/showbiz/a19637/boyband-v-decide-to-split/|title=Boyband 'V' decide to split|author=Neil Wilkes|publisher=Digital Spy|date=3 March 2005|access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref>

==After break-up==
 
*Mark Harle became a [[drummer]] for [[Independent music|indie]] band [[Little Comets]] until 2011, when he left the band.
*Kevin McDaid became a professional photographer, including photographing the covers for the [[Westlife]] albums ''[[Where We Are]]'' and ''[[Gravity (Westlife album)|Gravity]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=McAlpine |first1=Fraser |title=Can you recognise these boyband veterans from how they look now? |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/articles/fb9cde0d-e2fd-4288-8386-f8ddb9b15a33 |website=BBC |access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref>
* Aaron Buckingham moved into [[Artists and repertoire|A&R]] work, including managing the band [[Lawson (band)|Lawson]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Robinson |first1=Peter |title=Have We Witnessed the Death of Boybands? |url=https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/yvwgkk/have-we-witnessed-the-death-of-boybands |website=Vice |access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref>
* Antony Brant remained friends with the members of McFly, and toured with the band alongside James Bourne and [[The Vamps (British band)|The Vamps]] in the spring of 2013 as a compere to their [[Memory Lane: The Best of McFly|Memory Lane]] tour.<ref>{{cite web |title=McFly rock Gloucester Rugby fan Zoe’s world |url=https://www.gloucesterrugby.co.uk/2013/05/20/mcfly-rock-gloucester-rugby-fan-zoes-world/ |website=Gloucester Rugby |access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref>
