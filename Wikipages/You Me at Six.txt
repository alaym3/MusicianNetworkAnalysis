'''You Me at Six''' are an English [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Weybridge]], [[Surrey]]. Formed in 2004, the group achieved success in 2008 with the release of their debut album, ''[[Take Off Your Colours]]'', which included the singles "[[Save It for the Bedroom]]", "[[Finders Keepers (You Me at Six song)|Finders Keepers]]" and "[[Kiss and Tell (You Me at Six song)|Kiss and Tell]]", with the latter two peaking at number 33 and number 42 respectively in the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref name="You Me at Six Chart Position">{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artists/|title=The Official Charts Company—You Me at Six|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=7 October 2008}}</ref> Their second album ''[[Hold Me Down]]'' (2010) debuted at number  5 in the UK, while third album ''[[Sinners Never Sleep]]'' (2011) peaked at number 3, was certified Gold, and spawned the band's fifth top 50  single "[[Loverboy (You Me at Six song)|Loverboy]]".

"[[The Swarm (song)|The Swarm]]" was released as a non-album single for the launch of the [[The Swarm (roller coaster)|roller coaster of the same name]]; it peaked at number 23. ''[[Cavalier Youth]]'' was released as their fourth album to a number 1 debut in 2014 and contained the number 11 single "[[Lived a Lie]]". You Me at Six has toured extensively, with appearances on [[Warped Tour]], [[Soundwave (Australian music festival)|Soundwave]], and has toured with [[Thirty Seconds to Mars]], [[Paramore]], [[Fall Out Boy]] and [[All Time Low]]. You Me at Six won the award for Best British Band at the [[Kerrang! Awards]] in 2011. The band's influences include American rock bands [[Blink-182]], [[Incubus (band)|Incubus]] and [[Thrice]].

==History==
===''We Know What It Means to Be Alone'' EP (2004–2007)===
You Me at Six formed in Weybridge, Surrey in 2004. The band's line-up consisted of vocalist [[Josh Franceschi]], guitarists Max Helyer and Chris Miller, bassist Matt Barnes,<ref name=AMbio>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/you-me-at-six-mn0001982318/biography|title=You Me at Six {{!}} Biography & History  |work=AllMusic|author=Birchmeier, Jason |access-date=12 January 2016}}</ref> and drummer Joe Phillips.<ref name=McMahon20>McMahon, ed., 2014, p. 20</ref> The group released an EP, ''We Know What It Means to Be Alone'' in 2006,<ref name=AMbio/> after which, Phillips left the band.<ref name=McMahon20/> He was replaced by Dan Flint, who the band had gone to college with, following the band's appearance at [[Slam Dunk Festival|Slam Dunk Festival 2007]].<ref name=MD2019>{{cite web|url=http://www.mikedolbear.com/story.asp?StoryID=2109|title=Interview with Dan Flint - You Me At Six - Dec 09|work=Mike Dolbear DRUMS |date=December 2009|access-date=12 January 2016}}</ref> Flint was initially asked to fill in on drums for a tour, before being asked to join the band.<ref name=MD2019/>

===''Take Off Your Colours'' (2007–2008)===
The band released another EP later in 2007.<ref name=MD2019/> During summer 2007, the owner of [[Drive-Thru Records|Drive-Thru]], Richard Reines, had arranged a meeting with the band.<ref name=RSrecorddeal/> The band were ecstatic about the prospect and proceeded to wait in London for three hours, only for Reines to fail to show up.<ref name=RSrecorddeal>{{cite web|url=http://rocksound.tv/articles/1504/Feature-YMA6-Sign-Record-Deal.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080630113557/http://rocksound.tv/articles/1504/Feature-YMA6-Sign-Record-Deal.html|title=Feature: YMA6 Sign Record Deal|work=Rock Sound|author=Kelham, Andrew |archive-date=30 June 2008|access-date=12 January 2016}}</ref>

Their debut album, ''[[Take Off Your Colours]],'' came out in October 2008 on [[Slam Dunk Records]]. Six singles were released from the album: "Save It for the Bedroom", "If I Were in Your Shoes", "Gossip", "Jealous Minds Think Alike", "Finders Keepers" and "Kiss and Tell". ''Take off Your Colours'' was re-released as a "deluxe" version, with added tracks, and the band supported [[Fall Out Boy]] in late 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gigwise.com/news/46802/you-me-at-six-to-support-fall-out-boy-on-uk-tour|title=You Me At Six To Support Fall Out Boy On UK Tour|author=Jason Gregory|work=gigwise.com}}</ref> They were nominated for "Best British Band" at the 2008 and 2009 [[Kerrang! Awards]], but lost both times to [[Bullet for My Valentine]].

===''Hold Me Down'' (2009–2010)===
The band released second album, ''[[Hold Me Down]]'', on [[Virgin Records]] in January 2010.<ref name="AP1">{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=1422111|title=You Me at Six Album Info|author=Joe DeAndrea|publisher=AbsolutePunk.net|date=11 November 2009|access-date=2 December 2009}}</ref> The album was preceded by a free digital download of song "The Consequence". Recorded at Outhouse Studios in Reading, produced and engineered by Matt O'Grady and mixed by [[John Mitchell (musician)|John Mitchell]], ''Hold Me Down'', debuted at No. 5 in the [[UK Albums Chart|UK album charts]]. They have completed their third sell-out headline tour in support of their second album ''Hold Me Down''. They were joined by Sean Smith and Elissa Franceschi on selected dates. In Belfast, Matt told GiggingNI.com how it was "exciting to see how much you've grown from a year and a half ago".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2010-10-26|title=You Me at Six talks to GiggingNI.com - Promoting the local music scene...|url=http://giggingni.com/site/interview.php?id=35|access-date=2020-08-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101026045127/http://giggingni.com/site/interview.php?id=35|archive-date=26 October 2010}}</ref> Follow-up singles, the ''[[Underdog EP]]'' ''and'' "Stay With Me", were also released. You Me At Six again lost out to Bullet For My Valentine in the "Best British Band" category at the 2010 Kerrang! Awards and collaborated with American rap duo [[Chiddy Bang]] on a track titled "Rescue Me" that was released in February 2011.

On 18 May 2010, You Me at Six visited BBC Radio One's [[Live Lounge]] for the second time, presented by [[Fearne Cotton]]. Aside from being interviewed they played a version of "[[Liquid Confidence]]" and a cover of [[Ellie Goulding]]'s "[[Starry Eyed (Ellie Goulding song)|Starry Eyed]]" at Maida Vale, in London live on air.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio1/livelounge/artist/100518_youmeatsix.shtml|title=BBC - Radio 1 - Live Lounge - Stornoway|website=Bbc.co.uk}}</ref> You Me at Six kicked off their year playing a launch show in Kingston – originally intended to be an instore gig at Banquet Records, it was quickly upgraded to the new Hippodrome<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.banquetrecords.com/CDV3071|title=You Me At Six - Hold me Down|website=BanquetRecords.com}}</ref> – in support of their new album ''[[Hold Me Down]]'' on 11 January. They played 3 dates, including, also, an HMV instore and an HMV event headline promoting new British music at the Relentless Garage London. They announced that they would be playing several festivals including Punkspring in Japan, the whole [[Warped Tour 2010|Vans Warped Tour]];<ref>{{Cite web|date=2010-08-17|title=Vans Warped Tour 2010 lineup|url=http://amplified.tv/2010/03/05/vans-warped-tour-2010-lineup/|access-date=2020-08-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100817185211/http://amplified.tv/2010/03/05/vans-warped-tour-2010-lineup/|archive-date=17 August 2010}}</ref> and supported [[Paramore]] on all [[Soundwave (Australian music festival)|Soundwave]] dates, as well as on Paramore's tour.<ref name="myspace.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.myspace.com/youmeatsix|title=You Me at Six|website=Myspace.com}}</ref>

===''Sinners Never Sleep'' and ''Cavalier Youth'' (2011–2014)===
You Me At Six recorded a third album, ''[[Sinners Never Sleep]]'', at [[The Sound Factory]] studio in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]]. The record was released in October 2011, and peaked at No. 3 in the UK Album Charts. A single, "[[Bite My Tongue]]", featuring [[Oliver Sykes]] was released two months later, and the group finally won the "Best British Band" award at the Kerrang! Awards.<ref name="BBC">{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/7521112.stm|title=Welsh band set for Kerrang! trio|publisher=BBC|date=23 July 2008|access-date=19 August 2009}}</ref> A further single,  "[[No One Does It Better (song)|No One Does It Better]]", was released in 2012, as was a song specially recorded for [[The Swarm (roller coaster)|The Swarm]], a ride at [[Thorpe Park]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thorpepark.com/you-me-at-six/ |title=You Me at Six Write Official Single for the Swarm at Thorpe Park |publisher=Thorpepark.com |access-date=2012-03-31}}</ref>  On 16 April 2011, [[Blink-182]] announced that their headlining tour would be delayed till 2012 due to the fact that they have not finished recording for the new album yet. As a result, You Me at Six will not be touring with them. Upcoming tour dates for the UK in September will be released on 15 June, and the band have announced that they will be touring Australia with [[We the Kings]] in late August–early September.

On 29 August 2011, the fourth track of the ''[[Sinners Never Sleep]]'', "This Is the First Thing" was leaked online by one of the inside recording team member back in Los Angeles while the band was tracking their vocals. On 1 September 2011, a 30-second snippet of the track from the record "Bite My Tongue" featuring Oli Sykes was leaked on the internet as well. It was removed 30 minutes later due to infringement and copyrighted issues. The band's next UK tours started in March 2012. The first two tour dates, which were supposed to be played in Belfast and Dublin were postponed due to Josh Franceschi contracting tonsillitis and being unable to perform. You Me at Six played the entire [[Warped Tour 2012]]. In 2013 the band returned to Los Angeles to record with producer [[Neal Avron]]. The resulting album, ''[[Cavalier Youth]]'', was released in January 2014, with single Lived A Lie trailing the full-length in September 2013.

===''Night People'', ''VI'' and ''Suckapunch'' (2016–present)===
Their fifth album, ''[[Night People (You Me at Six album)|Night People]]'' was released on 13 January 2017,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/you_me_at_six_return_with_album_announcement_new_song|title=You Me At Six return with album announcement, new song|work=Alternative Press|author=Ralph, Caitlyn |date=22 August 2016|access-date=22 August 2016}}</ref> and their sixth album, [[VI (You Me at Six album)|''VI'']], was released on 5 October 2018. On 15 January 2021, the band released their seventh studio album [[Suckapunch (album)|''Suckapunch'']].
