'''Mike Lévy''' ({{IPA-fr|majk levi}}; born 13 June 1987<!-- Do not change this to 1985 -->),<ref>{{cite web|url =https://www.chron.com/life/article/Birthdays-June-13-12973215.php|title =Birthdays: June 13|website =Houston Chronicle|date=2018-06-13|quote =DJ/producer Gesaffelstein is 31}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url =https://www.mercurynews.com/2017/06/13/celebrity-birthdays-for-june-13-2017/|title =Celebrity birthdays for June 13, 2017|website =The Mercury News|date=2017-06-13|quote=DJ/producer Gesaffelstein is 30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url =https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2019/03/25/gesaffelsteins-dance-music-punctures-the-coachella-bubble|title =Gesaffelstein's Dance Music Punctures the Coachella Bubble|first =Carrie|last =Battan|date=2019-03-25|quote =Mike Lévy, a thirty-one-year-old Frenchman who records as Gesaffelstein}}</ref> known professionally as '''Gesaffelstein''' ({{IPA-de|ɡəˈzafl̩ʃtaɪ̯n}}), is a French musician, DJ, songwriter and record producer from [[Lyon]]. He has worked alongside artists as varied as [[The Weeknd]], [[Daft Punk]], [[Kanye West]], [[ASAP Rocky|A$AP Rocky]], [[Electric Youth (band)|Electric Youth]], [[Haim (band)|Haim]], [[Miss Kittin]], [[The Hacker]], [[Jean-Michel Jarre]] and [[Pharrell Williams]]. He currently resides in [[Paris]].

==Name==
Gesaffelstein is a [[portmanteau]] of [[Gesamtkunstwerk]] ("total artwork" in German, also title of an album by the American electro group [[Dopplereffekt]]) and [[Albert Einstein]].<ref name="Makarechi">{{cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/12/05/gesaffelstein-live-kanye_n_4391960.html|title=Gesaffelstein Produces For Kanye, Embarks On Live Tour And Keeps A Few Secrets|last=Makarechi|first=Kia|date=2013-12-05|access-date=2013-12-12|work=Huffington Post}}</ref><ref name="Gesaffelstein France 3">{{Cite web|url=https://france3-regions.francetvinfo.fr/hauts-de-france/2014/05/09/main-square-festival-2014-gesaffelstein-qui-es-tu-474043.html|title=Main Square Festival 2014 : Gesaffelstein, qui es-tu ?|access-date=2020-08-10|website=[[France 3]]}}</ref>

==Career==
Mike Lévy was born in [[Lyon]], [[France]], in June 1987. He started producing music at the age of sixteen when he first played an [[analog synthesizer]].<ref name="Inrocks 18072012">{{cite web|title=Gesaffelstein, nouveau prince de la techno française|url=http://www.lesinrocks.com/musique/critique-album/gesaffelstein-nouveau-prince-de-la-techno-francaise|author=Géraldine Sarratia|website=[[Les Inrockuptibles|Les Inrocks]]}}.</ref> He released his first EP in 2008 through the label OD Records. In 2009, he founded with [[The Hacker]], Alex Reynaud and David Rimokh, the independent label Zone.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tsugi.fr/zone-le-noir-label-de-the-hacker-et-gesaffelstein-fete-ses-10-ans/|title=Zone : le noir label de The Hacker et Gesaffelstein fête ses 10 ans - Tsugi|date=2019-03-26|website=Tsugi}}</ref>

In July 2012, Gesaffelstein made the cover of [[DJ Magazine|DJ Mag]] with [[Brodinski]].<ref name="DJ Mag cover 072012">{{cite web|title=French new wave! Brodinski & Gesaffelstein bring the Bromance to SW4|url=http://www.olddjmag.com/news/magazine/54|website=[[DJ Magazine|DJ Mag]]|date=July 2012}}{{Dead link|date=December 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}.</ref> The same year, his track "Viol" was used by [[Citroën]] and [[Givenchy]] advertisings.<ref name="reclame">{{cite web|title=Musique de la pub Citroën DS4 – Just Mat|url=http://lareclame.fr/musique+citroen+ds4+just+mat|website=reclame.fr}}.</ref> He co-produced two tracks on ''[[Yeezus]]'', the sixth album of American rapper [[Kanye West]], including the lead single "[[Black Skinhead]]" and "[[Send It Up]]", both tracks produced with [[Kanye West]], [[Daft Punk]], Brodinski and [[Mike Dean (record producer)|Mike Dean]].<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/06/arts/music/gesaffelstein-aka-mike-levy-at-webster-hall.html?smid=pl-share | work=The New York Times | first=Jon | last=Caramanica | title=Gesaffelstein, a.k.a. Mike Levy, at Webster Hall |date=2013-12-05}}</ref>  On October 28, 2013, Gesaffelstein released his debut album ''[[Aleph (album)|Aleph]]'' under [[Parlophone Records]] and [[Owsla|OWSLA]] (in North America only), which had been recorded since 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gesaffelstein's Debut Album 'Aleph' - Out Now!|url=https://www.edmsauce.com/2013/10/29/gesaffelstein-debut-album-aleph-now/|website=EDM Sauce|date=2019-01-17}}</ref> His remix of "Shockwave" by [[The Hacker]] is featured in the [[2013 in video gaming|2013 video game]] ''[[Grand Theft Auto V]]'', on the [[Soulwax]] FM radio station. 

In 2015, Gesaffelstein released the single "Conquistador" in collaboration with [[Jean-Michel Jarre]], which is present on the album ''[[Electronica 1: The Time Machine]]''.<ref name="Film single"/> He also produced the soundtrack for the French-Belgian 2015 film ''[[Disorder (2015 film)|Maryland]]'', directed by [[Alice Winocour]].  In early 2018, he produced the tracks "I Was Never There" and "Hurt You" from [[The Weeknd]]'s ''[[My Dear Melancholy,]]'' EP.<ref>{{cite news|last=Meadow|first=Matthew|url=https://www.youredm.com/2018/03/29/weeknd-skrillex-gesaffelstein-dear-melancholy/|title=BREAKING: The Weeknd Calls On Skrillex, Daft Punk, Gesaffelstein & More For New EP [MUST LISTEN]|date=March 29, 2018|publisher=Your EDM|access-date=2019-01-17}}</ref>

Gesaffelstein signed to [[Columbia Records]] in November 2018, with whom he released the lead single "Reset" from his second studio album ''[[Hyperion (Gesaffelstein album)|Hyperion]]'' in the same month.<ref>[https://pitchfork.com/news/gesaffelstein-returns-signs-to-columbia/ Gesaffelstein Returns, Signs to Columbia] ''Pitchfork''</ref> In January 2019, he released a collaboration with The Weeknd titled "[[Lost in the Fire]]" as the second single from ''Hyperion'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.capitalfm.com/artists/the-weeknd/tease-new-music-gesaffelstein-lost-in-fire/|title=The Weeknd's Got Everyone Excited Teasing New Music 'Lost In The Fire'|website=Capital|access-date=2019-01-17}}</ref> and later in March 2019 released another collaboration with Pharrell Williams titled "Blast Off".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8500620/gesaffelstein-pharrell-williams-blast-off-video|title=Gesaffelstein & Pharrell Williams Go Pop In The Shadows On 'Blast Off': Watch|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-03-09}}</ref> ''Hyperion'' was released on March 8, 2019 to mixed reviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8501662/gesaffelstein-hyperion-stream|title=Gesaffelstein's 'Hyperion' Is a Moody Mix Of Pop, Goth & Cinematic Scores: Listen|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-03-09}}</ref><ref name = "pitchfork">{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/gesaffelstein-hyperion|title=Gesaffelstein – Hyperion|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|author=Larry Fitzmaurice|date=12 March 2019|access-date=12 March 2019}}</ref> On October 3, 2019, Gesaffelstein announced his surprise EP ''Novo Sonic System'', consisting of 6 tracks, which was released the following day.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2019/10/gesaffelstein-surprises-fans-with-new-ep-novo-sonic-system/|title=Gesaffelstein surprises fans with new EP, 'NOVO SONIC SYSTEM'|date=2019-10-03|website=Dancing Astronaut}}</ref>

For [[List of Apple Inc. media events|Apple's October 2021 event]], his track "Orck" was used as the pre-intro and outro theme music.

==Musical style==  
Mixmag describes Gesaffelstein's style as being a "dark and threatening techno, though enchanting";<ref>{{cite web|title=Brodinski: the family guy|url=http://www.mixmag.net/words/features/brodinski-the-family-guy|author=Bridget Mills-Powell|website=[[Mixmag]]|access-date=2019-01-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121002141028/http://www.mixmag.net/words/features/brodinski-the-family-guy|archive-date=2012-10-02|url-status=dead}}.</ref> The Inrocks see it as "black, ultra-violent music, [which] revives the techno fundamentals, the intransigence of Underground Resistance, the mental and obsessive structures of Drexciya, the contemporary power and more".<ref name="Inrocks 18072012"/> Megan Buerger from the Washington Post describes his style has a mixture of dark and underground music, and notes that a specialty of the artist is his use of silences to create a "tension" before a "raucous explosion of bass and percussion".<ref>{{cite web|title=Gesaffelstein album review: 'Rise of Depravity'|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/gog/music-events/gesaffelstein,1238198/critic-review.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130630142939/http://www.washingtonpost.com/gog/music-events/gesaffelstein,1238198/critic-review.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2013-06-30|last=Buerger|first=Megan|pages=WW.6<!--endif p.totales-->|language=en|date=2012-08-31}}.</ref> 

''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine sees his music as being "a little more aggressive and punk-rock than normal".<ref>{{cite web|title=Who Should Be Big|last=[[Skrillex]]|pages=24<!--endif p.totales-->|language=en|date=31 May 2012}}.</ref>

==Influences==

Gesaffelstein cites as references [[Dopplereffekt]], [[Kraftwerk]], but also new wave formations from the 80s such as [[Deutsch Amerikanische Freundschaft|D.A.F.]] or [[Nitzer Ebb]]. He also appreciates [[Joy Division]] and [[The Hacker]] also influenced him a lot.<ref name="Gesaffelstein France 3"/>
