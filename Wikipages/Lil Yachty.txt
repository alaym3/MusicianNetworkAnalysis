'''Miles Parks McCollum''' (born August 23, 1997), known professionally as '''Lil Yachty''', is an American rapper from [[Atlanta|Atlanta, Georgia]]. McCollum first gained recognition on the internet in 2015 for his singles "[[One Night (Lil Yachty song)|One Night]]" and "[[Minnesota (song)|Minnesota]]" (featuring [[Quavo]], Skippa Da Flippa and [[Young Thug]]) from his debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''Summer Songs''.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Post-Internet Worlds of Atlanta Rappers Lil Yachty and Playboi Carti|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2734247/lil-yachty-playboi-carti-atlanta/|website=MTV|access-date=March 30, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Lil Yachty, Lil Uzi Vert and Playboi Carti, Oddball Rap's Children, at Play|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/02/29/arts/music/lil-yachty-lil-uzi-vert-and-playboi-carti-oddball-raps-children-at-play.html?_r=0|website=New York Times|access-date=March 30, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Yachty Lil Boat|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/21736-lil-boat/|website=Pitchfork|access-date=March 30, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Meet Lil Yachty, The Atlanta Rapper Born To Go Viral|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/02/22/lil-yachty-interview-kanye-west
|website=The Fader|access-date=March 30, 2016}}</ref> He released his debut [[mixtape]] ''[[Lil Boat]]'' in March 2016. On June 10, 2016, Yachty announced that he had signed a [[wikt:joint venture|joint venture]] record deal with [[Motown]], [[Capitol Records]], and [[Quality Control Music]].<ref name="New Deal">{{cite web |url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.39179/title.lil-yachty-announces-signing-to-quality-control |title=Lil Yachty signs deal with Quality Control |last=Hernandez |first=Victoria |date=June 10, 2016 |website=Hiphopdx.com |access-date=June 11, 2016}}</ref>

Yachty has released four studio albums, beginning with ''[[Teenage Emotions]]'' in 2017. His second and third studio albums, ''[[Lil Boat 2]]'' and ''[[Nuthin' 2 Prove]]'', were released in 2018, and his most recent album, ''[[Lil Boat 3]]'', was released on May 29, 2020.<ref name="Johnson">{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2020/05/lil-yachty-to-drop-lil-boat-3-next-week/|title=Lil Yachty to Drop Lil Boat 3 Album Next Week|work=[[XXL Magazine|XXL]]|last=Johnson|first=Zoe|date=May 20, 2020|access-date=May 21, 2020}}</ref> All four of his albums have charted within the top 20 of the [[Billboard 200]], with ''Lil Boat 2'' peaking at #2. Yachty has also released several mixtapes and EPs throughout his career. Lil Yachty is best known for his features on the 2016 multi-platinum songs [[Broccoli (song)|"Broccoli"]] and [[ISpy (Kyle song)|"ISpy"]], his eccentric hairstyle, and his optimistic image.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Ihaza|first=Jeff|date=2021-06-15|title=Rapper and Entrepreneur Lil Yachty -- Future 25|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/pro/features/lil-yachty-future-25-1179452/|access-date=2021-06-17|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> Yachty has been nominated for a [[Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] for his work on "Broccoli".<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Here Is the Complete List of Nominees for the 2017 Grammys|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7597556/grammys-nominees-complete-list-2017|access-date=2021-06-17|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

==Early life==
Miles McCollum was born in [[Mableton, Georgia]].<ref name="Birthday">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/08/happy-birthday-lil-yachty/|title=Happy Birthday, Lil Yachty!|website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=Madden|first=Sidney|date=August 23, 2016|access-date=December 17, 2016}}</ref> He attended [[Alabama State University]] in fall 2015 but soon dropped out to pursue his musical career.<ref>{{cite web | title=9 Rappers Who Went to College But Didn't Graduate | url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/08/rappers-who-went-to-college-but-didnt-graduate/ | last=J. | first=Miranda |website=XXL | date = August 31, 2016 | access-date = February 16, 2018}}</ref> He adopted the name "Yachty" and moved from his hometown of [[Atlanta]] to New York City to launch his career. In New York, he lived with a friend and networked with online [[street fashion]] personalities, while he built up his own [[Instagram]] following.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|last=Weiner|first=Jonah|date=November 9, 2016|title=How Lil Yachty Went From Instagram Whiz to Kanye West Collaborator|newspaper=[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/how-lil-yachty-went-from-instagram-to-kanyes-inner-circle-w449066|access-date=December 6, 2016}}</ref> He worked at [[McDonald's]] to supplement his income early in his career.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/lil-yachty-on-his-favorite-beatles-song-why-hes-never-been-drunk-194794/|title=Lil Yachty on His Favorite Beatles Song, Why He's Never Been Drunk|first1=Andy|last1=Greene|date=June 20, 2017|access-date=December 29, 2019}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
Lil Yachty has called his style "[[Bubblegum pop|bubblegum]] [[Trap music (hip hop)|trap]]."<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> His songs have sampled sounds from ''[[Mario Bros.|Mario Bros]].'', [[Charlie Brown]], the theme from ''[[Rugrats]]'', the startup sound of a [[GameCube]] console, as well as [[J-pop]] singer [[Daoko]].<ref name=":0" /> Other themes in his works include clouds, [[cotton candy]], the [[Super Nintendo Entertainment System|Super Nintendo]], and scenes from [[Pixar]] films. His friend [[TheGoodPerry]] is heavily involved in the production of his songs.<ref name=":1" /> Yachty's style has also been described as [[mumble rap]].<ref name=ringer>{{cite web|url=https://theringer.com/mumble-rap-moratorium-lil-yachty-desiigner-15bc3647dae9|title=Declaring a Moratorium on the Term "Mumble Rap"|author=Charity, Justin|publisher=The Ringer|date=April 18, 2017|access-date=May 14, 2017}}</ref>

''[[Rolling Stone]]'' described his music as "catchy, intentionally dinky-sounding tunes packed with off-color boasts delivered in a proudly amateurish singsong."<ref name=":0"/> ''[[The Guardian]]'' called his music "fun, hook-first pop rap oblivious to songcraft and structure that doesn't take itself too seriously, with very little interest in legacy and even less in rap canon."<ref name=":1" />

==Personal life==
On September 1, 2015, Yachty and one other man were arrested at a mall in [[Palm Beach Gardens, Florida]] for [[credit card fraud]], Yachty was released after posting a [[Bail|bail bond]] of $11,000.<ref name="Arrest">{{cite web|last=Sacasa|first=Adam|date=September 1, 2015|title=Two men accused of having dozens of fake credit cards at mall in Palm Beach Gardens|url=http://www.sun-sentinel.com/local/palm-beach/fl-palm-beach-gardens-mall-fraud-20150901-story.html|website=The Sun-Sentinel}}</ref> According to Yachty, his records were expunged.<ref name=":0" />

In a 2016 interview for [[CNN]], Yachty expressed support for [[Bernie Sanders]] in the [[2016 United States presidential election|2016 presidential election]], and praised Sanders for his work during the [[civil rights movement]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/videos/politics/2016/04/21/lil-yachty-gets-political-bernie-sanders-2016-lil-b-rap-origwx-allee.cnn|title=Lil Yachty on getting into Bernie Sanders via Lil B|publisher=CNN|access-date=August 13, 2018}}</ref> On October 20, 2021 Yachty announced the birth of his first child, a girl. At this time the mother is unknown.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Moorwood|first=Victoria|date=2021-10-20|title=Lil Yachty reportedly welcomes baby girl|url=https://www.revolt.tv/news/2021/10/20/22736809/lil-yachty-father-baby-girl|access-date=2021-10-21|website=REVOLT|language=en}}</ref>
