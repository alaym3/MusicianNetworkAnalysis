'''Emily King''' (born July 10, 1985) is an American singer and songwriter. She started her career in 2004 and her first album ''[[East Side Story (Emily King album)|East Side Story]]'' was released in August 2007. In December 2007, King was listed as a Grammy nominee for [[Best Contemporary R&B Album]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/news/article.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=11602205|title=Emily King, Auckland Arts Festival|work=New Zealand Herald|access-date=20 March 2016}}</ref> In 2019 Emily King was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Song|Best R&B Song]]<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Grammy Awards 2020: Full list of nominees, winners for ‘music’s biggest night’|url=https://globalnews.ca/news/6458865/grammy-awards-2020-winners/|access-date=2020-11-25|website=Global News|language=en-US}}</ref> for the 62nd [[Grammy Award]]s and her album ''Scenery'' was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Engineered Album, Non-Classical|Best Engineered Album, Non-Classical]].<ref name=":0" /> In 2020 Emily King was nominated again, this time for the category of [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance|Best R&B Performance]]<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-11-24|title=Grammy Nominations 2021: See the List|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/11/24/arts/music/grammy-nominees-list.html|access-date=2020-11-25|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> for her song "See Me".

==Biography==
Born in New York City in 1985, King grew up in a small apartment on the Lower East Side.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hyman |first1=Dan |title=Is Emily King irresistible, as she has been called? Well, a lot of people are hooked |url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-ott-ott-emily-king-lincoln-hall-0201-story.html |access-date=2 January 2021 |work=Chicago Tribune |date=30 January 2019}}</ref> Her parents, Marion Cowings and Kim Kalesti, were a singing duo who performed and traveled regularly taking her and her older brother with them. At age 16, King left high school after earning her [[General Educational Development|GED]] to pursue her music career.<ref name="WBUR piece">{{cite news |last1=Ruddell |first1=Charley |title=Grammy-Nominated Singer Emily King Brings New 'Scenery' To Somerville |url=https://www.wbur.org/artery/2020/02/27/grammy-nominated-emily-king-scenery-somerville |access-date=2 January 2021 |work=WBUR |date=27 February 2020 |language=en}}</ref> She began playing shows in restaurants and venues around New York City including [[CBGB]] and [[The Bitter End]].<ref name="NPR Music Bio">{{cite web |title=Emily King |url=https://livesessions.npr.org/artists/emily-king |website=NPR Music Live Sessions |access-date=2 January 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
King signed her first record deal with [[Sony Records|J Records]] in 2004 and appeared on [[Nas]]' 2004 album ''[[Street's Disciple]]'' credited as simply "Emily".<ref name="Exclaim-scenery-review">{{cite news |last1=Patrick |first1=Ryan B. |title=Emily King's Minimalist R&B Album 'Scenery' Puts Feelings First |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/emily_kings_minimalist_r_and_b_album_scenery_puts_feelings_first |access-date=2 January 2021 |work=Exclaim! |date=1 February 2019 |language=en-ca}}</ref> Her first album ''[[East Side Story (Emily King album)|East Side Story]]'' was released in August 2007. It received a [[Grammy]] nomination for Best Contemporary R&B Album of the Year. After leaving the label in 2008, King continued her work independently with producer [[Jeremy Most]]. She self-recorded her follow-up EP ''Seven'' in her home, released in July 2011.<ref name="WBUR piece" /> Throughout this time, King toured domestically and internationally with many artists including Nas, [[John Legend]], [[Floetry]], [[Alicia Keys]], [[Chaka Khan]], [[Erykah Badu]], and [[Maroon 5]].<ref name="NPR Music Bio" />

In 2012, King was awarded the Holly Prize (a tribute to the legacy of [[Buddy Holly]]) from [[The Songwriters Hall of Fame]] for recognition of the "all-in songwriter" whose work exhibits the qualities of Holly’s music: true, great and original.<ref>{{cite web |title=Emily King {{!}} Songwriters Hall of Fame |url=https://www.songhall.org/scholarships/view/emily |website=Songwriters Hall of Fame |access-date=2 January 2021}}</ref> In the fall, King was invited by [[Emeli Sandé]] to open for her UK tour playing sold out shows in five cities including at [[The Royal Albert Hall]] in London.<ref name="NPR Music Bio" /> King collaborated with [[José James]] on his album ''No Beginning No End'' in 2013 and can be heard on the tracks "Heaven on the Ground" and the acoustic version of "Come to My Door".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ratliff |first1=Ben |title=Pursuing Many Paths to Find His Own (Published 2013) |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/01/20/arts/music/jose-jamess-no-beginning-no-end.html |access-date=2 January 2021 |work=The New York Times |date=18 January 2013}}</ref> In 2014, King performed as an opening act for [[Sara Bareilles]]' Little Black Dress tour.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lipshutz |first1=Jason |title=Sara Bareilles Announces Summer Tour |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6077478/sara-bareilles-summer-tour-dates-2014 |access-date=2 January 2021 |work=Billboard |date=5 May 2014 |language=en}}</ref>

King's second studio album, ''[[The Switch (Emily King album)|The Switch]]'', was self-released by her own label, Making Music Records, on 26 June 2015. ''The Wall Street Journal'' remarked that the album is "a tasteful collection of eleven songs that showcase King’s distinctive voice".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2015/06/22/emily-king-listens-to-her-instincts-on-the-switch-exclusive-album/|title=Emily King Listens to Her Instincts on 'The Switch' (Exclusive Album)|newspaper=The Wall Street Journal|author= Eric R. Danton|date=22 June 2015|access-date=20 March 2016}} {{subscription}}</ref>

She signed with the independent label [[ATO Records]] in 2017 and with them released her third studio album, ''[[Scenery (Emily King album)|Scenery]]'', on 1 February 2019.<ref name="WBUR piece" />

In late 2019, King once again performed as an opening act for Sara Bareilles in her Amidst The Chaos Tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://atorecords.com/featured/emily-king-announces-fall-arena-tour-supporting-sara-bareilles/|title=Emily King Announces Fall Arena Tour Supporting Sara Bareilles |publisher=ATO Records|access-date=26 March 2020}}</ref>

In 2019, King helped to compose "Being Human", the ending theme song for the [[Cartoon Network]] animated series ''[[Steven Universe Future]]'', which she sang. Her song "Can't Hold Me" was also used in an episode of the series.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ifeanyi |first1=K. C. |title=‘Steven Universe’ creator Rebecca Sugar explains the series finale, ‘Future’—and her future |url=https://www.fastcompany.com/90480992/steven-universe-creator-rebecca-sugar-explains-the-series-finale-future-and-her-future |access-date=2 January 2021 |work=Fast Company |date=31 March 2020}}</ref>

King released her fourth studio album, ''Sides'', on January 17, 2020. The album features acoustic takes on her previous songs, and includes a guest appearance by [[Sara Bareilles]] on the track "Teach You."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Sun-Times |first1=Selena Fragassi-For the |title=A big year for Emily King brings Grammy nominations, praise from stars and critics |url=https://chicago.suntimes.com/2020/2/12/21134226/emily-king-scenery-sides-intimate-new-tour-music-chicago |access-date=2 January 2021 |work=Chicago Sun-Times |date=12 February 2020 |language=en}}</ref> In response to [[2020–2021 United States racial unrest|racial unrest in the summer of 2020]], King released the song "See Me" in August.<ref>{{cite news |title=Emily King Releases New Song "See Me" |url=https://atorecords.com/news/emily-king-releases-new-song-see-me/ |access-date=2 January 2021 |work=ATO RECORDS |date=26 August 2020}}</ref> The song was nominated for Best R&B Performance in the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]].

King appeared on stream with [[Marc Rebillet]] on March 21, 2021 as his first ever guest performer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/marcrebillet/status/1373703413544808449|url=https://twitter.com/marcrebillet/status/1373703413544808449|access-date=2021-03-23|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>
