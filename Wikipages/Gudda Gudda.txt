'''Carl Eugene Lilly Jr.''' (born March 11, 1983), better known by his stage name '''Gudda Gudda''', is an American [[Rapping|rapper]] from [[New Orleans]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/search/workID/884838805|title = ACE Repertory}}</ref> He was originally a member of [[Sqad Up]] with [[Kidd Kidd]] and [[Lil Wayne]]. After forging a long-time friendship with Wayne, he signed to [[Young Money Entertainment]], and later [[Republic Records]].

== Biography ==
=== 1983–2003: Early life and career ===
Gudda was born Carl Lilly on March 11, 1983, in [[San Jose, California]].<ref name="Gudda &#124; Topics">{{cite web|url=http://www.bet.com/topics/g/gudda-gudda.html |title=Gudda Gudda &#124; Topics |publisher=BET |date=April 30, 2012 |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> When he was still a baby he moved to [[New Orleans, Louisiana]]. He grew up in the [[Ninth Ward of New Orleans|9th Ward]], where he befriended [[Lil Wayne]] at the age of fifteen, who he met at a [[dice game]].<ref name="DefinitiveGuddaGudda">{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/the-definitive-gudda-gudda-interview |title=The Definitive Gudda Gudda Interview &#124; NOISEY |publisher=Noisey.vice.com |date=March 11, 2013 |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> This was much before he had ever thought of rapping as a career.<ref name="allhiphoplil">{{cite web|author=Mikey Thurston (@MTMovieStar) |url=http://allhiphop.com/2012/12/11/gudda-gudda-discusses-cash-moneys-massive-roster-says-lil-wayne-inspired-him-to-rap/ |title=Gudda Gudda Discusses Cash Money's Massive Roster; Says Lil Wayne Inspired Him To Rap |website=AllHipHop.com |date=December 11, 2012 |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> As he began to rap, Wayne would mentor and inspire him, and actually wrote Gudda's first rap.<ref name="MTV">{{cite web|last=Reid |first=Shaheem |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1627708/gudda-gudda-introduces-himself-on-new-mixtape.jhtml |title=Lil Wayne Protege Gudda Gudda Introduces Himself On New Mixtape – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=December 7, 2009 |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> Together they formed the group [[Sqad Up]] with rappers Dizzy and [[Kidd Kidd]]. Throughout 2002 and 2003, Gudda was featured on the seven mixtapes that Sqad Up released over the time period.<ref name="Gudda &#124; Topics" /><ref name="DefinitiveGuddaGudda"/>

=== 2005–12: ''Guddaville'' series and collaborations ===
Gudda Gudda would be one of the first artists signed to Wayne's [[Young Money Entertainment]] upon its officially founding in 2005.<ref name="Gudda &#124; Topics" /> Gudda had been present the day Wayne had come up with the idea for Young Money.<ref name="allhiphoplil"/> A short time later, Gudda signed a deal with [[Cash Money Records]] and [[Republic Records]]. On May 26, 2009, Gudda released ''[[Certified (Lil' Flip & Gudda Gudda album)|Certified]]'' a collaboration album with fellow rapper [[Lil Flip]] on [[Real Talk Entertainment]]. The album was met with generally positive reviews.<ref>{{cite web|author=David Jeffries |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/certified-mw0000816795 |title=Certified – Lil' Flip &#124; Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |website=AllMusic |date=May 26, 2009 |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> On September 28, 2009, Gudda released his first official solo mixtape ''Guddaville'', which featured [[guest appearance]]s from Lil Wayne, [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], [[Tyga]], [[Lil Twist]], [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Jae Millz]], [[Shanell (singer)|Shanell]], [[Mack Maine]], Lil Flip, [[Tity Boi]], [[Bow Wow (rapper)|Bow Wow]], and [[Ya Boy]] among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.2dopeboyz.com/2009/09/28/gudda-gudda-guddaville-mixtape/ |title=Gudda Gudda – Guddaville (Mixtape) |publisher=2dopeboyz |date=September 28, 2009 |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> He described the mixtapes theme to [[MTV]] saying, "I'm taking people on a walk through my eyes. Every time you heard me been a group project. ''Guddaville'' is just me expressing how I feel about a lot of different things, taking you in my world."<ref name="MTV"/>

Gudda was featured on [[Birdman (rapper)|Birdman]]'s album ''[[Pricele$$]]'' on the track "Hustle" and prominently featured on the [[Young Money]] group album ''[[We Are Young Money]]'' being featured on eight tracks, including the singles "[[Every Girl (Young Money song)|Every Girl]]" and "[[BedRock (song)|BedRock]]." Both singles were hits, each peaking in the top ten of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="Gudda &#124; Topics" /> He was also featured on the track "I Don't Like the Look of It" on Lil Wayne's album ''[[I Am Not a Human Being]]''. On June 7, 2010, Gudda released the sequel to his first mixtape titled, ''Back 2 Guddaville''. His second official mixtape featured guest appearances from [[Waka Flocka Flame]], Mack Maine, [[Jay Rock]], Lil Wayne, [[Vado (rapper)|Vado]], Ya Boy and Jae Millz, among others.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.karencivil.com/2010/06/07/gudda-gudda-travels-back-2-guddaville/ |title=Gudda Gudda Travels "Back 2 Guddaville" « |publisher=Karencivil.com |date=June 7, 2010 |access-date=September 15, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100612003341/http://www.karencivil.com/2010/06/07/gudda-gudda-travels-back-2-guddaville/ |archive-date=June 12, 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Reid |first=Shaheem |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1638845/gudda-gudda-recalls-getting-willy-wonka-from-lil-wayne.jhtml |title=Gudda Gudda Recalls Getting 'Willy Wonka' From Lil Wayne – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=May 11, 2010 |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref>

Then for two years Gudda remained quiet recording music without releasing hardly any music.<ref name="DefinitiveGuddaGudda"/> He was featured on the remix to "[[I'm On]]" along with [[Big K.R.I.T.]], [[Jadakiss]], [[J. Cole]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[B.o.B]], Tyga, and [[Bun B]]. On November 5, 2012, Gudda released the tracklist to his third official mixtape ''Guddaville 3''. The tracklist revealed guest appearances from [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[2 Chainz]], Lil Wayne, [[Mystikal]], [[Crooked I]], [[Trae tha Truth]], [[Ace Hood]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]], Jae Millz, Birdman, [[Kevin Gates]], Tyga, and Mack Maine.<ref>{{cite web |last=Civil |first=Karen |url=http://www.karencivil.com/2012/11/05/gudda-gudda-releases-tracklisting-for-guddaville-3-dropping-november-7th/ |title=Gudda Gudda Releases Tracklisting for 'Guddaville 3′, Dropping November 7th « |publisher=Karencivil.com |date=July 31, 2013 |access-date=September 15, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130216012512/http://www.karencivil.com/2012/11/05/gudda-gudda-releases-tracklisting-for-guddaville-3-dropping-november-7th/ |archive-date=February 16, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Two days later on November 7, 2012, Gudda released ''Guddaville 3''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/gudda-gudda-guddaville-3-mixtape.66754.html |title=Gudda Gudda Mixtapes – Guddaville 3 (Hosted by DJ ill Will & DJ Scoob Doo) |date=November 7, 2012 |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> Around the time he formed the group Loyalty Amongst Thieves, featuring [[Kevin Gates]], Flow and T-Streets, which he said planned to release a self-titled mixtape.<ref name="hiphopdxinterview">{{cite web |last=Balfour |first=Jay |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.2056/title.gudda-gudda-says-lil-wayne-inspired-his-formula-for-redrum- |title=Gudda Gudda Says Lil Wayne Inspired His Formula For "Redrum" &#124; Rappers Talk Hip Hop Beef & Old School Hip Hop |publisher=HipHop DX |date=March 19, 2013 |access-date=September 15, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141231133833/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.2056/title.gudda-gudda-says-lil-wayne-inspired-his-formula-for-redrum- |archive-date=December 31, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

=== 2013–present: ''Redrum'' and ''Guddaville: The Album'' ===
On March 11, 2013, Gudda released his fourth official mixtape ''Redrum''. The mixtape featured guest appearances from [[Busta Rhymes]], Jae Millz, [[Cory Gunz]], [[Boo & Gotti|Boo]], and Charlie Rock among others. Production was handled by [[Cardiak]], [[Young Chop]], [[I.N.F.O.]], and [[Jahlil Beats]] among others.<ref>{{cite web |last=Horowitz |first=Steven J. |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23159/title.gudda-gudda-redrum-mixtape-download-stream |title=Gudda Gudda "REDRUM" Mixtape Download & Stream &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=March 11, 2013 |access-date=September 15, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518211021/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23159/title.gudda-gudda-redrum-mixtape-download-stream |archive-date=May 18, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The mixtape was mainly a compilation of songs that did not make the final tracklist of ''Guddaville 3''.<ref name="DefinitiveGuddaGudda"/> Following its release, Gudda announced in an interview that he was working on his debut studio album ''Guddaville: The Album'', later confirmed for release in 2014.<ref name="hiphopdxinterview"/><ref name="yoraps1">{{cite web |url=http://www.yoraps.com/features1.php?subaction=showfull&id=1376667267&archive&start_from&ucat=3 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130915065942/http://www.yoraps.com/features1.php?subaction=showfull&id=1376667267&archive&start_from&ucat=3 |url-status=dead |archive-date=September 15, 2013 |title=EXCLUSIVE INTERVIEW: Gudda Gudda – Family Life |publisher=Yoraps.com |date=August 16, 2013 |access-date=September 15, 2013 }}</ref> He also said that he would release another mixtape ''Guddaville 4'' prior to the album's release.<ref name="hiphopdxinterview"/> Additionally he revealed he was starting his own production company Gudda Music and the ''Loyalty Amongst Thieves'' mixtape had already been completed.<ref name="DefinitiveGuddaGudda"/><ref name="hiphopdxinterview"/>

''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' praised his verse on [[Lil Wayne]]'s ''[[I Am Not a Human Being II]]'' track "Gunwalk" as "show stealing".<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1554297/lil-wayne-i-am-not-a-human-being-ii-track-by-track-review |title=Lil Wayne, 'I Am Not A Human Being II': Track-By-Track Review |magazine=Billboard |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> Gudda and Mack Maine would be the only rappers to be featured on ''The H'' a mixtape by [[Rick Ross]] and Birdman.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1563570/rick-ross-birdman-release-the-h-the-lost-album-vol-1-mixtape |title=Rick Ross, Birdman Release 'The H: The Lost Album Vol. 1' Mixtape |magazine=Billboard |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> He was also featured on two tracks on the 2013 [[YMCMB]] compilation album ''[[Rich Gang (album)|Rich Gang]]'', including "Panties To The Side" with Bow Wow, [[French Montana]] and Tyga.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapradar.com/2013/07/20/new-music-bow-wow-french-montana-tyga-gudda-gudda-panties-to-the-side/ |title=New Music: Bow Wow, French Montana, Tyga & Gudda Gudda "Panties To The Side" |publisher=Rap Radar |date=July 20, 2013 |access-date=September 15, 2013}}</ref> He confirmed guest appearances on ''Guddaville: The Album'' to come from Lil Wayne, Busta Rhymes, and Drake, while also saying his dream collaborations would be with [[Kool G Rap]] or [[Dr. Dre]].<ref name="yoraps1"/> On March 11, 2014, Young Money released their second compilation album ''[[Young Money: Rise of an Empire]]'', where Gudda was featured on two songs, "Fresher Than Ever" and "You Already Know".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/rise-of-an-empire-mw0002620510|title=Rise of an Empire - Young Money &#124; Songs, Reviews, Credits &#124; AllMusic}}</ref> ''L.A.T (Loyalty Amongst Thieves)'' will be released in July 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.youngmoneyhq.com/2014/06/20/gudda-gudda-reveals-artwork-for-his-lat-project-dropping-in-july/|access-date=November 19, 2017|date=June 20, 2014|title=Gudda Gudda Reveals The Artwork For His "L.A.T" Project Dropping In July|first1=Danny|last1=M.|website=youngmoneyhq.com}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
As of early 2013, Gudda Gudda was living in [[Houston]], Texas and Cash Money Records headquarters Miami, Florida. He indicated most of his time was spent in the studio, with the rest of his time going towards being with his children.<ref name="DefinitiveGuddaGudda"/>
