'''Todd Anthony Dulaney''' (born December 20, 1983) is an American [[gospel music]]ian, and former [[baseball|baseball player]]. His music career started in 2011, with the release of the CD version, ''Pulling Me Through''. This would be his breakthrough released upon the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] [[Top Gospel Albums|Gospel Albums]] chart. He would release another album, ''A Worshipper's Heart'', in 2016 with EntertainmentOne Nashville, and this would place even higher on the Gospel Albums chart.

==Early life==
Dulaney was born on December 20, 1983 in [[Maywood, Illinois]] as Todd Anthony Dulaney, to Thomas and Tommye Dulaney.<ref name="WS Bio">{{cite web|title=Bio |url=http://todddulaneyland.com/bio/|publisher=Todd Dulaney|author=Dulaney, Todd|access-date=April 19, 2015}}</ref><ref name="GM Story">{{cite web|title=Pulled Him Through – Meet GoldStreet Gospel's Todd Dulaney|url=http://www.glossmagazineonline.com/Articles/Entertainment/Pulled-Him-Through-%E2%80%93-Meet-GoldStreet-Gospel%E2%80%99s-Todd-Dulaney.html|publisher=Gloss Magazine|first=Cicely V.|last=Teal|access-date=April 19, 2015|date=November 7, 2011}}</ref><ref name="BB Bio">{{cite web|title=Todd Dulaney Minor League Statistics & History |url=https://www.baseball-reference.com/minors/player.cgi?id=dulane001tod|publisher=Baseball-Reference |author=Baseball-Reference|access-date=April 19, 2015}}</ref> He was athletic as a youth playing sports in particular [[baseball]], where he would play at a local community college, [[Wabash Valley College]], getting drafted by the [[New York Mets]] in the 2002 MLB June Amateur Draft as a 32nd round selection.<ref name="WS Bio"/><ref name="BB Bio"/> He never reached the major leagues, but played his short stint as a professional baseball player in the minor leagues.<ref name="BB Bio"/> He decided after the 2005 season to retire from the sport because he felt God's call to play [[gospel music]], which he did under the tutelage of artist, [[Smokie Norful]], who asked him to tour with him.<ref name="WS Bio"/>

==Music career==
His music recording career commenced in 2011, with the physical album, ''Pulling Me Through'', and it was released on May 31, 2011 by Dulaney independently.<ref name="CD">{{cite web|title=Todd Dulaney: Pullin Me Through|url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00545KR18|author=Todd Dulaney|access-date=April 19, 2015|date=May 31, 2011}}</ref> This was his breakthrough release upon the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] [[Top Gospel Albums|Gospel Albums]] chart, placing at No. 43.<ref name="AM Awards">{{cite web|title=Todd Dulaney : Awards|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/todd-dulaney-mn0002771995/awards|author=Billboard|author-link=Billboard (magazine)|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=April 19, 2015}}</ref> His subsequent release, ''Pulling Me Through'', was released by GoldStreet Gospel Records on January 22, 2013.<ref name="Digital">{{cite web|title=Pulling Me Through by Todd Dulaney|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/pulling-me-through/id584258265|author=Todd Dulaney|publisher=[[iTunes]]|access-date=April 19, 2015|date=January 22, 2013}}</ref> This time it placed even higher at No. 23 on the aforementioned chart because it was released digitally along with extended content.<ref name="AM Awards"/> He released ''A Worshipper's Heart'' on April 15, 2016, with [[Entertainment One Music]].<ref name="WL Review">{{cite magazine |first=Brendan |last=Prout |title=Noel Robinson: ''Outrageous Love'' |url=http://media.worshipleader.com/lib/2016mar/html5/index.html?page=73 |magazine=[[Worship Leader (magazine)|Worship Leader]] |publisher=Chuck Fromm, PhD (Worship Leader Partnership) |editor1-first=Jeremy |editor1-last=Armstrong |location=[[San Juan Capistrano, California]] |issn=1066-1247 |access-date=March 1, 2016 |volume=March/April 2016 |page=73}}</ref> The album peaked at No. 1 on the Gospel Albums chart, while it got No. 150 on [[Billboard 200|The ''Billboard'' 200]] and No. 13 on the [[Independent Albums]] chart.

On June 11, 2021, Dulaney released the live album ''Anthems & Glory'' with [[Entertainment One Music]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cluver|first=Ross|title=Todd Dulaney Releases New Album ‘Anthems & Glory’|url=https://www.ccmmagazine.com/news/todd-dulaney-releases-new-album-anthems-glory/#:~:text=Nashville%2C%20TN%20(June%2011%2C,sold%20and%20streamed%20digitally%20now.|date=June 11, 2021|access-date=September 22, 2021|website=Billboard}}</ref> Dulaney earned his fifth Gospel Airplay No. 1 With 'Revelation 4' on the list dated September 25, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Asker |first=Jim |title=Todd Dulaney Scores His Fifth Gospel Airplay No. 1 With 'Revelation 4'| url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9634515/todd-dulaney-fifth-gospel-airplay-number-1-revelation-4/| date=September 22, 2021| access-date=September 22, 2021| website=Billboard}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Dulaney's wife is Kenyetta Stone-Dulaney, and together they have four children, Tenley, Taylor, Tyler, and Todd Jr., who attend church at All Nations Worship Assembly located in Chicago, Illinois.<ref name="WS Bio"/>
