'''Asin Thottumkal''' (born 26 October 1985), known [[Mononymous person|mononymously]] as '''Asin''', is a former Indian actress who appeared in [[Tamil language|Tamil]], [[Hindi]], and [[Telugu language|Telugu]] films. She is a trained [[Bharatanatyam]] dancer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.timesofindia.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/news/Asins-classical-avataar/articleshow/5115585.cms|title=Asin's classical avataar|work=[[The Times of India]]|date=13 October 2009}}</ref> She has received three [[Filmfare Awards]]. She began her acting career in the [[South Indian film industry]], but later shifted her focus to [[Bollywood]].<ref>{{cite Making her acting debut with [[Sathyan Anthikkad]]'s [[Malayalam film]] ''[[Narendran Makan Jayakanthan Vaka]]'' (2001), Asin had her first commercial success with the [[Telugu language|Telugu]] film ''[[Amma Nanna O Tamila Ammayi]]'' in 2003, and won a [[Filmfare Best Actress Award (Telugu)|Filmfare Best Telugu Actress Award]] for the film. ''[[M. Kumaran S/O Mahalakshmi]]'' (2004) was her debut in [[Tamil language|Tamil]] and a huge success. She received her [[Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Tamil|Filmfare Best Tamil Actress Award]] for her most noted critically acclaimed performance in her third Tamil film, ''[[Ghajini (2005 film)|Ghajini]]'' (2005). She then played the lead female roles in many successful films, the most notable being the action films ''[[Sivakasi (film)|Sivakasi]]'' (2005), ''[[Varalaru]]'' (2006), ''[[Pokkiri]]'' (2007), ''[[Vel (film)|Vel]]'' (2008) and ''[[Dasavathaaram]]'' (2008), hence establishing herself as the leading actress of Tamil cinema and was also honoured by the Tamil Nadu government with the [[Kalaimamani]] award for her excellence in the field of art and literature.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.hindu.com/2009/11/29/stories/2009112960210600.htm | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091202084151/http://www.hindu.com/2009/11/29/stories/2009112960210600.htm | url-status=dead | archive-date=2 December 2009 | location=Chennai, India | work=[[The Hindu]] | title=Kalaimamani awards presented to 70 persons | date=29 November 2009}}</ref> In 2013, Asin was conferred with the [[2nd South Indian International Movie Awards#Generation Next Awards|Pride of South Indian Cinema]] award at SIIMA for her contribution to Tamil cinema.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/movies/features/type/view/id/5511|title=Asin bags the Pride of South Indian Cinema Award - Latest Movie Features - Bollywood Hungama|author=Bollywood Hungama|work=bollywoodhungama.com}}</ref>

In late 2008, Asin made her debut in the [[Bollywood]] film ''[[Ghajini (2008 film)|Ghajini]]'' (2008), opposite [[Aamir Khan]], which was the first Bollywood film to have collected more than {{INR}}1&nbsp;billion in the domestic box office, subsequently collecting {{INRConvert|1.9|b}} worldwide. Asin won the [[Filmfare Best Female Debut Award]] and many accolades for Ghajini. 2011 marked the most successful phase of Asin's Bollywood career, as she starred in [[Anees Bazmee]]'s [[romantic comedy]] ''[[Ready (2011 film)|Ready]]'', in which she co-starred alongside [[Salman Khan]]. The film was a major hit at the box office, collecting {{INRConvert|1.84|b}} worldwide.<ref>[http://boxofficeindia.com/arounddetail.php?page=shownews&articleid=3997&nCat== Top Worldwide Grossers All Time: 100 Crore] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130523013047/http://boxofficeindia.com/arounddetail.php?page=shownews&articleid=3997&nCat=%3D |date=23 May 2013 }}. Box Office India (3 February 2012). Retrieved 10 July 2012.</ref> In 2012, Asin first starred in [[Sajid Khan]]'s multistarrer ''[[Housefull 2]]'', which collected more than {{INR}}1&nbsp;billion. She then featured in ''[[Bol Bachchan]]'' and ''[[Khiladi 786]]'', which were also commercially successful with both grossing over {{INR}}1&nbsp;billion.

==Career==

===Early work (2001–04)===
[[File:Asin08.jpg|right|thumb|Asin, with the cast and crew of ''[[Ghajini (2008 film)|Ghajini]]'', celebrating the film's success.]]
Asin's first assignment was an advertisement for [[Loop Mobile|BPL Mobile]]. She debuted in the [[Malayalam]] film ''[[Narendran Makan Jayakanthan Vaka]]'' in 2001, at the age of 15. After taking a year out to pursue her education, Asin returned with her breakthrough film as an actress, ''[[Amma Nanna O Tamila Ammayi]]'' opposite [[Ravi Teja]], portraying a [[Tamil people|Tamil]] girl in her first [[Telugu language]] film, which subsequently fetched her the [[Filmfare Best Actress Award (Telugu)|Telugu Filmfare Award for Best Actress]].<ref name="awards">{{cite web|author=Thottumkal, Asin|publisher=AsinOnline.com|title=Awards|date=24 December 2008|url=http://www.asinonline.com/awards.htm|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref> In the same year she won the [[Santosham Best Actress Award]] for her performance alongside [[Nagarjuna (actor)|Nagarjuna]] in her second Telugu film ''[[Shivamani (film)|Shivamani]]''.<ref name="awards"/> Her following two [[Telugu language|Telugu]] films ''[[Lakshmi Narasimha (film)|Lakshmi Narasimha]]'' and ''[[Gharshana]]'', both portrayed Asin as the love interest for police officers and both were successful ventures.

Asin's first [[Tamil language]] film was ''[[M. Kumaran S/O Mahalakshmi]]'', in which she co-starred with [[Jayam Ravi]]. Asin reprised her role from her film ''Amma Nanna O Tamila Ammayi'' for the remake, which saw her portraying a [[Malayali]] girl instead of the Tamil girl in the original. The movie subsequently went on to super-hit in [[Tamil cinema]] during 2004, introducing Asin to [[Tamil film industry]].<ref name="mkumaran">{{cite web|author=Radhakrishnan, Mathangi |publisher=MusicIndiaOnline |title='M. Kumaran...' rules the Tamil box office |date=15 October 2004 |url=http://www.musicindiaonline.com/n/i/tamil/903/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070224041036/http://www.musicindiaonline.com/n/i/tamil/903/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=24 February 2007 |access-date=24 December 2008 }}</ref> After a brief return to Telugu films, to do ''[[Chakram (2005 film)|Chakram]]'', she appeared in ''[[Ullam Ketkumae]]''.<ref name="chakram">{{cite news|author=Narasimhan, ML|work=The Hindu|title=Dubbed films rule yet again |date=30 December 2005|url=http://www.hindu.com/thehindu/fr/2005/12/30/stories/2005123002160100.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060925022241/http://www.hindu.com/thehindu/fr/2005/12/30/stories/2005123002160100.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=25 September 2006|access-date=24 December 2008|location=Chennai, India}}</ref> The film, initially launched in 2002, was supposed to be her debut as a lead heroine, is a college love story, directed by [[Jeeva (director)|Jeeva]], which was long-delayed but eventually became a successful venture at the box office, creating wider opportunities for her and the rest of the cast.<ref name="uk">{{cite web|author=Shivram, Prasanna|work=The Hindu|title=Jeeva's lyrical frames |date=13 July 2007|url=http://www.thehindubusinessline.com/life/2007/07/13/stories/2007071350040200.htm|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref>

===Tamil film breakthrough and stardom (2005–07)===
After the release of ''Ullam Ketkumae'', Asin was cited as a leading heroine of the Tamil film industry after starring in blockbuster films such as ''[[Ghajini (2005 film)|Ghajini]]'', ''[[Sivakasi (film)|Sivakasi]]'', ''[[Varalaru]]'' and ''[[Pokkiri]]'' respectively.<ref name="fourbig">{{cite news|author=Kamath, Sudhish|work=The Hindu|title=Asin turns out to be the brightest sparkler this year|date=1 November 2005|url=http://www.hindu.com/2005/11/01/stories/2005110117190200.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060525182001/http://www.hindu.com/2005/11/01/stories/2005110117190200.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=25 May 2006|access-date=24 December 2008|location=Chennai, India}}</ref> The film which provided the breakthrough for Asin was ''[[Ghajini (2005 film)|Ghajini]]''. The film, co-starring [[Suriya (actor)|Surya]], directed by [[A.R. Murugadoss]], earned her the [[Filmfare Best Actress Award (Tamil)|Tamil Filmfare Award for Best Actress]]. Her role was that of a vivacious young model named Kalpana. [[Sify]].com praised her portrayal as "magical", describing her character as a "lovable chatterbox", played with "sheer ability in the romantic interludes, the poignant and heartfelt scenes when she rescues minor girls from villains and her gory end are touching".<ref name="sifykalapna">{{cite web|publisher=[[Sify]]|title=Ghajini|date=27 September 2005|url=http://sify.com/movies/tamil/review.php?id=13952885&ctid=5&cid=2429|access-date=23 December 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090805204548/http://sify.com/movies/tamil/review.php?id=13952885&ctid=5&cid=2429|archive-date=5 August 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref> On the following [[Diwali]] in 2005, Asin had two releases, ''[[Sivakasi (film)|Sivakasi]]'' and ''[[Majaa]]''. Despite the latter becoming an average grosser, the former went on to be a successful venture at the box office.<ref name="sivakasi">{{cite news|author=Rangarajan, Malathi|work=The Hindu|title=With the formula intact |date=11 November 2005|url=http://www.hindu.com/thehindu/fr/2005/11/11/stories/2005111100180200.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060919093039/http://www.hindu.com/thehindu/fr/2005/11/11/stories/2005111100180200.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=19 September 2006|access-date=24 December 2008|location=Chennai, India}}</ref><ref name="majaa">{{cite web|author=Ashok Kumar, SR|work=The Hindu|title=Where is the entertainment? |date=4 November 2005|url=http://www.hindujobs.com/thehindu/fr/2005/11/04/stories/2005110402340200.htm|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref>

The following year, her long-delayed venture ''[[Varalaru (film)|Varalaru]]'' opposite [[Ajith Kumar]] also succeeded at the box office, and ''[[The Hindu]]'' said she "looks lustrous and sails through her role smoothly".<ref name="Varalaru">{{cite news|author=Rangarajan, Malathi|work=The Hindu|title=In the race, surely&nbsp;— Varalaaru |date=27 October 2006|url=http://www.hindu.com/fr/2006/10/27/stories/2006102700060200.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061114233324/http://www.hindu.com/fr/2006/10/27/stories/2006102700060200.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=14 November 2006|access-date=24 December 2008|location=Chennai, India}}</ref> Asin appeared in [[Pawan Kalyan]]'s ''[[Annavaram]]'', another successful venture, which was a remake of [[Thirupaachi]].<ref name="Annavaram">{{cite web|author=Vardhan, Adithya|publisher=[[Rediff]]|title=Annavaram is paisa vasool|date=2 January 2007|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2007/jan/02anna.htm|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref> In January 2007, Asin appeared opposite Ajith Kumar and Vijay in two different films, ''[[Aalwar]]'' and ''[[Pokkiri]]'', with the latter becoming a success, whilst ''Aalwar'' became a failure. Though Asin's role in ''Aalwar'' was criticised, her performance in ''Pokkiri'' was lauded by critics.<ref name="LAalwarl">{{cite web|author=Mohan, Piraba|publisher=Behindwoods|title=Aalwar: Ajith is the saving grace|date=14 January 2007|url=http://www.behindwoods.com/tamil-movie-articles/movies-05/aalwar-review.html|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref><ref name="LPokkiri">{{cite web|author=Bhaskar, Shweta|publisher=[[Rediff]]|title=Pokkiri: Watch only for Vijay, Asin|date=15 January 2007|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2007/jan/15sspokkiri.htm|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref> Her final project of the year was opposite Surya, in [[Hari (director)|Hari's]] ''[[Vel (film)|Vel]]'', which had a Diwali 2007 release, subsequently becoming her third successful film which released during the Diwali season in three years. Asin, who portrayed a [[TV anchor]], was appreciated for her role in the film.<ref name="LondonVel">{{cite web|author=Hari, TSV|publisher=[[Rediff]]|title=Vel is slick and neat|date=8 November 2007|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2007/nov/08vel.htm|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref>

===Hindi film debut (2008–10)===
[[File:Asin 'Bol Bachchan' team on the sets of Taarak Mehta Ka Ooltah Chashmah 03.jpg|thumb|left|150px|Asin in 2012]]
Asin appeared in her first dual role in [[K. S. Ravikumar]]'s ''[[Dasavathaaram]]'' opposite [[Kamal Haasan]], who portrayed ten roles. The film had been under production since September 2006. Despite being overshadowed by Haasan's ten roles, Asin's portrayals in the film were praised as her "best-to-date", with one of her roles being that of a [[Vaishnavite]] in 12th century [[Tamil Nadu]]; whilst the other character was a [[Brahmin]] girl from [[Chidambaram]], for which she won her first [[ITFA Best Actress Award]].<ref name="Dasavtharamperf">{{cite web|author=Vijayasarathy, R. G.|publisher=[[Rediff]]|title=Dasavathaaram is spectacular|date=13 June 2008|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2008/jun/13dasa.htm|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref> ''Dasavathaaram'' subsequently went on to become one of the blockbusters.<ref name="Dasavtharamblockbuster">{{cite web|work=[[The Times (South Africa)|The Times]] |location=South Africa |title=Kamalhassan's new blockbuster hit is setting records worldwide|date=29 June 2008|url=http://www.thetimes.co.za/Entertainment/Article.aspx?id=792296|access-date=24 December 2008}}</ref>

Since establishing herself as the leading actress in South India, Asin opted to move into [[Bollywood]]. Her first film ''[[Ghajini (2008 film)|Ghajini]]'', opposite [[Aamir Khan]], was the remake of her breakthrough film of the same name. Upon release, the film garnered positive reactions from critics and masses alike, with Asin being singled out for her "fabulous" portrayal. Noted critic [[Taran Adarsh]] described her Hindi debut as "fabulous" and that "to share the screen space with an actor of the stature of Aamir Khan and yet remain in your memory even after the show has ended is no cakewalk. She looks fresh and photogenic and acts her part brilliantly, giving Asin's performance a positive outlook."<ref name="GhajiniHindi">{{cite web|author=Adarsh, Taran|publisher=[[Sify]]|title=Review: Ghajini has blockbuster written all over it|date=23 December 2008|url=http://sify.com/movies/bollywood/review.php?id=14824058&ctid=5&cid=2425|access-date=23 December 2008|author-link=Taran Adarsh|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081226010942/http://sify.com/movies/bollywood/review.php?id=14824058&ctid=5&cid=2425|archive-date=26 December 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref>

''Ghajini'' became the highest grossing Bollywood film of 2008 and the sixth [[List of highest-grossing Bollywood films|highest-grossing Bollywood film]] of all time according to net collections.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=127&catName=MjAwMC0yMDA5|title=Top Lifetime Grossers 2000–2009 (Figures in Ind Rs)|publisher=Box-Office-India}}</ref> Asin later appeared in [[Vipul Shah]]'s ''[[London Dreams]]'', alongside [[Salman Khan]] and [[Ajay Devgn]], in which she played a south Indian girl, Priya, who is emotional but strong willed and an ambitious dancer at the same time.<ref name="LondonDreamsHindi">{{cite web|author=Jha, Subhash. ===Final work (2011–15)===
[[File:Akshay and Asin on the Ramp.jpg|thumb|right|250px|Asin with [[Akshay Kumar]] on the Ramp during ''[[Housefull 2]]'' promotions, 2012]]
Her next movie with Salman Khan, ''[[Ready (2011 film)|Ready]]'', was released on 3 June 2011 to mixed reviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ibosnetwork.com/newsmanager/templates/template1.aspx?a=22264&z=9 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120710060504/http://ibosnetwork.com/newsmanager/templates/template1.aspx?a=22264&z=9 |url-status=dead |archive-date=10 July 2012 |title=Box Office: Ready opens to good opening with mixed response |publisher=ibosnetwork |access-date=4 June 2011 }}</ref> Critics praised the chemistry between Salman and Asin.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indiaglitz.com/channels/hindi/review/12984.html |title=Ready Movie Review |publisher=Behindwoods |access-date=5 June 2011}}</ref> Upon release, ''Ready'' became the second highest weekend grossing Bollywood film, after ''[[Dabangg]]''<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2011-06-07/box-office/29629146_1_multiplexes-small-budget-releases-single-screen|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121105152529/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2011-06-07/box-office/29629146_1_multiplexes-small-budget-releases-single-screen|url-status=dead|archive-date=5 November 2012|title=Ready is second to Dabangg|access-date=7 June 2011|work=The Times of India|date=7 June 2011}}</ref> and went on to become the fifth film in Bollywood history to cross the coveted {{INRConvert|1|b}} mark in the domestic market.

In 2012, she first appeared alongside [[Akshay Kumar]] in [[Sajid Khan]]'s multistarrer comedy ''[[Housefull 2]]''. Though it received a mixed critical reception,<ref>{{cite web|title=Housefull 2 gets average reviews from film critics|url=http://entertainment.oneindia.in/bollywood/features/2012/housefull-2-rave-reviews-critics-verdict-090412.html|access-date=1 April 2013}}</ref> it was a major commercial success.<ref>{{cite web|title=Top Ten 2012 Lifetime Grossers |url=http://boxofficeindia.com/boxnewsdetail.php?page=shownews&articleid=5038&nCat= |publisher=Box Office India |access-date=1 April 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121102181249/http://www.boxofficeindia.com/boxnewsdetail.php?page=shownews&articleid=5038&nCat= |archive-date=2 November 2012 }}</ref> Asin generally received favourable reviews for her performance, with Adarsh stating that "[a]mongst the female leads, Asin has the meatiest role and gives a decent account of herself."<ref>{{cite web|last=Adarsh|first=Taran|title=Housefull 2 Movie Review (2012)|url=http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/moviemicro/criticreview/id/542290|publisher=Bollywood Hungama|access-date=1 April 2013}}</ref>

On 6 July 2012, [[Rohit Shetty]]'s ''[[Bol Bachchan]]'', alongside [[Ajay Devgn]] and [[Abhishek Bachchan]], released with mixed reviews with the critics praising the performances of Asin, Ajay Devgn, and Abhishek Bachchan. [[Taran Adarsh]] from Bollywood Hungama, who praised Asin's performance stated: "Asin does well, though her screen time is limited. Nonetheless, her amiable looks and stimulating smile add to her charisma!!."<ref>[http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/moviemicro/criticreview/id/545484 Bol Bachchan (2012) | Hindi Movie Critic Review By Taran Adarsh]. Bollywood Hungama. Retrieved 10 July 2012.</ref> While the reviewer from Behindwoods quoted: "There is a small surprise with regards to Asin's role. She is the romantic interest of Ajay and looks serene and absolutely cute."<ref>[http://www.behindwoods.com/tamil-movie-reviews/reviews-2/jul-12-01/bol-bachchan-review.html Bol Bachchan Review]. Behindwoods.com. Retrieved 10 July 2012.</ref> The movie is now declared a super hit.<ref name="articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com">{{cite news|author=Bharti Dubey |url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-07-13/box-office/32662398_1_box-office-bol-bachchan-net-box |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130521085834/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-07-13/box-office/32662398_1_box-office-bol-bachchan-net-box |url-status=dead |archive-date=21 May 2013 |title=Bol Bachchan's business going strong at box office |date=13 July 2012 |work=The Times of India |access-date=26 October 2012}}</ref>

''[[Khiladi 786]]'', in which she reunited with her ''[[Housefull 2]]'' costar, [[Akshay Kumar]], released on 7 December 2012 to mixed reviews, but was strong at the box office and eventually became a commercially successful venture.<ref name="hindustantimes.com">[http://www.hindustantimes.com/Entertainment/Bollywood/BO-Report-Akshay-Kumar-s-Khiladi-786-passes-the-test/Article1-971496.aspx BO Report: Akshay Kumar's Khiladi 786 passes the test] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121220175208/http://www.hindustantimes.com/Entertainment/Bollywood/BO-Report-Akshay-Kumar-s-Khiladi-786-passes-the-test/Article1-971496.aspx |date=20 December 2012 }}. ''Hindustan Times'' (11 December 2012). Retrieved 11 June 2013.</ref>

Asin did not appear in any movie in 2013 or 2014. Her only movie release of 2015 was ''[[All Is Well (2015 film)|All Is Well]]'', directed by [[Umesh Shukla]] and produced by [[Bhushan Kumar]]. In one interview, director Umesh Shukla stated that Asin would be seen in an "important dramatic role".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indianexpress.com/news/asin-to-romance-abhishek-bachchan-in-mere-apne/1152793/?SocialMedia |title=Asin to romance Abhishek Bachchan in Mere Apne |work=The Indian Express |date=8 August 2013 |access-date=19 October 2014}}</ref>
