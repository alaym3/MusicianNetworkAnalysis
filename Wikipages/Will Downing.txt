'''Wilfred''' "'''Will'''" '''Downing''' (born November 29, 1963)<ref name="Soul">{{cite web|title=Will Downing Page |url=http://www.soulwalking.co.uk/Will%20Downing.html|website=Soulwalking.co.uk|access-date=October 27, 2020}}</ref> is an American singer and songwriter.<ref name="AMG">{{cite web|last=Wynn|first=Ron|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/will-downing-mn0000960874|title=Will Downing - Music Biography, Credits and Discography|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=March 28, 2013}}</ref> He is married to singer Audrey Wheeler, who was a member of the R&B group [[Unlimited Touch]].<ref name="Soul"/>

==Biography==
Downing enrolled in [[Erasmus Hall High School]] in Brooklyn, New York. Alumni of the school include [[record label]] executives [[Clive Davis]] (who founded [[Arista Records]]), [[Kedar Massenburg]] (who later became president of [[Motown]]), Stephanie Mills and singer [[Barbra Streisand]].<ref>[http://www.blakeradio.com/html/video_lounge/vl_will.shtml] {{webarchive 
|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070701154909/http://www.blakeradio.com/html/video_lounge/vl_will.shtml |date=July 1, 2007 }}</ref> Downing, who eventually recorded for Motown, graduated with Massenburg in the class of 1981. Downing then attended college at [[Virginia Union University]] in [[Richmond, Virginia]]. In the mid-1980s, he moved back to New York City, and worked on [[album]]s by Mariah Carey, The O'Jay's, Billy Joel, [[Gerald Albright]], [[Billy Ocean]], [[Regina Belle]], [[Rachelle Ferrell]] and [[Mica Paris]]. Later he sang with [[Arthur Baker (musician)|Arthur Baker]]'s group, Wally 'Jump' Junior.<ref name="AMG"/> Downing then used Baker as a producer after he signed with [[Island Records]].

In 1988, Downing signed a [[recording contract]] with [[4th & Broadway]] Records, and his first solo album, ''Will Downing'', was released that same year. His version of the [[John Coltrane]] piece, "[[A Love Supreme]]",<ref name="AMG"/> reached No.&nbsp;4 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Dance Club Songs]] [[record chart|chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/will-downing-mn0000960874/awards|title=Will Downing - Awards |publisher=AllMusic|access-date=March 28, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121119065909/http://www.allmusic.com/artist/will-downing-mn0000960874/awards|archive-date=November 19, 2012}}</ref> Downing stayed with 4th & Broadway for just one album and switched to Island Records for his second release, ''Come Together As One'' (1989).<ref name="AMG"/>  Switching labels to [[Mercury Records|Mercury]] for his fourth outing, 1993's ''[[Love's the Place to Be]]'', Downing scored his best-selling album to date with about 235,000 copies sold in the United States and over 300,000 copies in the [[United Kingdom|UK]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.enotes.com/contemporary-musicians/downing-will-biography |title=Downing, Will Study Guide & Homework Help |publisher=eNotes.com |access-date=2013-03-28}}</ref> He remained with Mercury for two more albums, ''Moods'' in 1995 and ''Invitation Only'' in 1997, both of which sold well.<ref name="AMG"/> ''All the Man You Need'' received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination for Best Traditional R&B Album in 2000. The album included "When You Need Me", a duet single with [[Chante Moore]].<ref>Billboard - 8 Jul 2000 - Page 24 "released first single "When You Need Me," a duet with MCA's Chante Moore"</ref> In 2002, Downing received the International Association of African-American Music Diamond Award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iaaam.com/IAAAMCelebrationPhotoGallery.html|title=International Association of African-American Music|publisher=Iaaam.com|date=2002-06-07|access-date=March 28, 2013}}</ref>

In December 2006, Downing suffered from [[polymyositis]].<ref name="AMG"/> Despite the debilitating aspects of the disease, he worked towards releasing a new album in 2010.<ref name="ABILITY">{{cite web|url=http://abilitymagazine.com/Will_Downing.html |title=Will Downing Will Power |publisher=Abilitymagazine.com |date=2007-01-03 |access-date=2013-03-28}}</ref> ''[[Love, Lust, & Lies: An Audio Novel]]'' was released in September that year.<ref name="AMG"/> Beyond performing, Downing is a spokesperson for the [[American Stroke Association]]. On tour, he conducts the charity bowling event, Strike Against Stroke, to raise funds towards awareness and education in the African American community, which is disproportionately affected. The [[American Heart Association]] recognized Downing for his efforts, honoring him as a Celebrity Ambassador. He is also a keen photographer, and self-published the 2005 coffee table book ''Unveiled Series I''. He is a member of [[Omega Psi Phi]] fraternity. He has three children (Will Jr., Aron Siobhan & Aja)
