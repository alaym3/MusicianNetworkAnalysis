'''Tierra Umi Wilson'''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Yu|first=Eda|date=June 22, 2020|title=Rising R&B star UMI makes soulful songs to help us heal|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/y3zw5v/rising-randb-star-umi-makes-soulful-songs-to-help-us-heal|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200623022848if_/https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/y3zw5v/rising-randb-star-umi-makes-soulful-songs-to-help-us-heal|archive-date=June 23, 2020|access-date=August 17, 2020|website=[[i-D]]|issn=0894-5373}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Sanchez|first=Omar|date=January 12, 2020|title=Musician and artist UMI manifested community through a charity yard sale event|url=https://dailytitan.com/2020/01/musician-artist-umi-manifested-community-charity-yard-sale/|access-date=August 17, 2020|website=[[The Daily Titan]]|language=en-US|oclc=15193278}}</ref> (born February 9, 1999)<ref name="OfficialFB"/> is an American singer and songwriter. Born in [[Seattle, Washington]], she attended the [[University of Southern California]], but dropped out in 2019 in order to pursue music. She is best known for her 2018 song "Remember Me", which has over 90 million streams on [[Spotify]]. She also accompanied [[Conan Gray]] as an opening act on her [[Comfort Crowd Tour]] in 2019.

Wilson was born in Seattle in 1999 to an African-American father and a Japanese mother.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Yu|first=Eda|date=September 12, 2019|title=UMI Is a Neo-Soul and R&B-Inspired world|url=https://happymag.tv/introducing-umi-delivering-neo-soul-magic-thats-out-of-this-world/|access-date=August 17, 2020|website=Happy Mag|language=en-US}}</ref> Her mother taught her how to read, write, and speak Japanese.{{Citation needed|date=August 2020}} Wilson's mother plays piano and her father plays the drums.<ref name=":0" /> UMI started writing music when she was 4 or 5, with a songwriting journal.<ref>{{cite web|date=February 22, 2019|title=Interview with Singer and Songwriter UMI|url=https://brightlitemag.com/2019/02/rb-singer-and-songwriter-umi/|access-date=23 March School]] in [[Maple Valley, Washington|Maple Valley]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Cotterill|first=TJ|date=April 21, 2016|title=Tahoma's 4x100 relay team, Olympic-hopeful Princess Joy Griffey are fast ==Career==

===2017–2018: ''Happy Again'' and debut EP===
In 2017, UMI released five singles before her four-track debut EP, ''Interlude''. This led to making it onto Spotify’s Fresh Finds Best of 2017 playlist.{{Citation needed|date=August 2020}} She opened for rapper ODIE in Los Angeles.{{Citation needed|date=August 2020}}

UMI's most popular release to date is "Remember Me". She has over 21 million views on YouTube and 53 million plays on Spotify.{{Citation needed|date=August 2020}} UMI debuted "Remember Me" with a music video, highlighting universalism by portraying couples of different race, class, and sexual orientation.{{Citation needed|date=August 2020}} She said, "the message in the video, for me, is that no matter who you love or how you love, we all hurt the same in the end."<ref>{{cite web|last=Robinson|first=Kristin|date=October 17, 2018|title=Meet UMI, the Lo-fi R&B Songstress Capturing the Human Experience|url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/meet-umi-the-lo-fi-rb-songstress-capturing-the|access-date=23 May 2019|website=[[Ones to Watch]]}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Balance'', ''Love Language,'' ''Introspection'' EP, and ''Introspection Reimagined''===
She released the ''Balance'' EP on May 17, 2019, which included the songs "Ordinary" and "Down To Earth." On October 30, 2019, she released her EP, ''Love Language''<ref>{{cite web|last1=del Rosario|first1=Michael|date=November 1, 2019|title=Love Language EP- [UMI]|url=https://www.lyricallemonade.com/p/love-language-ep-umi|access-date=July 13, 2020|website=[[Lyrical Lemonade]]}}</ref> On June 21, 2020, she released her EP, ''Introspection''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Folk|first1=Antwane|date=June 22, 2020|title=Stream UMI's New EP ''Introspection''|url=https://ratedrnb.com/2020/06/stream-umi-new-ep-introspection/|access-date=July 13, 2020|publisher=Rated R&B}}</ref> Following the release of her EP, ''Introspection'', UMI released a follow-up EP entitled ''Introspection Reimagined'' on March 26, 2021, featuring new versions of the songs on the ''Introspection'' EP as well as two new interludes.<ref name=":1">{{Cite tweet |number=1374073492346388485 |user=whoisumi |title=''Introspection Reimagined'' • out this Friday<br><br>This project is an art piece channeled from my spirit. Inspired by artists like D'angelo and Erykah Badu - and recorded live at Shangri-La , I hope this project brings you peace.<br><br>Welcome to the next evolution of ''Introspection'' |author=Umi |date=March 22, 2021 |access-date=April 10, 2021}}</ref>

==Artistry==

UMI has cited her biggest musical influences as [[Frank Ocean]], [[D'Angelo]], [[Jhené Aiko]], [[Erykah Badu]] and [[SZA (singer)|SZA]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Heifner|first=Makaila|date=March 7, 2018|title=UMI Talks Activism, Frank Ocean, and Remaining Authentic|url=https://berkeleybside.com/umi-talks-activism-frank-ocean-and-remaining-authentic/|website=Berkeley B-Side}}</ref> She grew up listening to R&B, soul, gospel and Japanese pop/rock. UMI has been described by [[Vogue (magazine)|''Vogue'']] as having a "lo-fi, alternative, laid back, R&B style".<ref name=kim />
