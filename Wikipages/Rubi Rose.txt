'''Rubi Rose''' is an American rapper and model.

== Early life ==
Rose was born Rubi Rose Benton and raised in [[Lexington, Kentucky]] to Nardos Ghebrelul, an [[Eritrea]]n immigrant dentist, and John Benton, a Black American and Japanese lawyer.<ref>{{Cite web|first=Rubi| last= Rose |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=76byl70M814|title=I got a special delivery....|date=February 18, 2021|website=Youtube}}</ref> Her mother was born in [[Ethiopia]] although being an [[Eritreans|Eritrean]]. Her paternal grandmother is of Black American descent and her paternal grandfather is of Japanese descent. She has an older sister named Scarlette and a younger sister.<ref>{{Cite web|first=Rubi| last= Rose |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0g4XFKpMi48|title=rubi rose q&a ... kinda|date=February 18, 2021|website=Youtube}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=June 2021}} She lived for one year in [[Geneva]], [[Switzerland]]. She moved to [[Snellville]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]] in her junior year of high school.<ref name="auto">{{cite web |last1=Rice |first1=Lillyanne |title=Inside Rubi Rose's life, Including her parents and boyfriend |url=https://thenetline.com/inside-rubi-rose-life/ |website=TheNetline |date=July 22, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|first=Juliana| last= Ukiomogbe |url=https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/mulatto-rubi-rose-and-sukihana-meet-the-women-of-wap|title=Mulatto, Rubi Rose, and Sukihana: Meet the Women of "WAP"|date=December 9, 2020|website=Interview Magazine}}</ref> She studied politics at [[Georgia State University]].<ref>{{Cite web|first=Danielle |last=Ransom|url=https://www.bet.com/music/2020/11/06/atlanta-rapper-rubi-rose-mixtape.html?cid=facebook|title=Atlanta Rapper Rubi Rose Has Popstar Ambitions From Madonna And Industry Insight From Kendrick Lamar|website=[[BET.com]]|date=November 6, 2020}}</ref> She is influenced by [[Nicki Minaj]] and [[Foxy Brown (rapper)|Foxy Brown]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/rubi-rose/|title=Rubi Rose - 2021 XXL Freshman}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mefeater.com/the-rise-of-rubi-rose/|title = The Rise of RUBI ROSE Through​ the Power of Music|date = August 20, 2021}}</ref> She grew up listening to [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Biggie]], [[Marvin Gaye]] and [[Chaka Khan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/rubi-rose-interview-the-break/|title = The Break Presents: Rubi Rose}}</ref>

== Career ==
Rose first gained fame as the main model for hip-hop group [[Migos]]' music video for the single "[[Bad and Boujee]]". Around this time, she started regularly featuring on a Twitch stream with Jamaican-born East Coast Hip Hop Commentator DJ Akademiks.<ref>{{Cite web|first=Akademiks| last= Twitch |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cf4w54EELUM|title=Playboi Carti ex girlfriend 'Rubi Rose' clears up if he's actually gay or not.|date=December 2, 2017|website=Youtube}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=June 2021}} Rose started releasing her own music and made traction in 2019 with her single "Big Mouth".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://earmilk.com/?p=695374|title=Rubi Rose is rising to the top while making music 'For The Streets' [Interview]|first=Vicky|last=Inoyo|date=December 29, 2020|website=EARMILK}}</ref> Rose made a [[cameo appearance]] in American rapper [[Cardi B]]'s music video for her single "[[WAP (song)|WAP]]", which was released on August 7, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 11, 2020|title=Meet The Next Wave Of Female Rappers, Courtesy Of Cardi B's 'WAP'|url=https://uproxx.com/music/cardi-b-wap-video-mulatto-rubi-rose-sukihana/|access-date=August 12, 2020|first=Aaron|last=Williams|website=UPROXX|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2019, she was signed by A&R Chris Turner to [[LA Reid]]'s record label Hitco Entertainment.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://flaunt.com/content/rubi-rose|first=Shirley|last=Ju|title=Rubi Rose On Signing To HitCo, "Big Mouth," DMs & More!|website=Flaunt Magazine|date=December 6, 2019}}</ref>

On December 25, 2020, Rose released her first official mixtape, ''For the Streets'', featuring guest appearances from [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and [[PartyNextDoor]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/12/rubi-rose-for-the-streets-stream|title=Listen to Rubi Rose's New Project 'For The Streets' f/ Future & PARTYNEXTDOOR|website=Complex|date=December 24, 2020|first=Jordan|last=Rose}}</ref>
Rose was featured in the 2021 [[XXL (magazine)|''XXL'']] Freshman List.<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.xxlmag.com/rubi-rose-freshman-freestyle/ |title= RUBI ROSE'S 2021 XXL FRESHMAN FREESTYLE |website=XXL|date=July 9, 2021|first=Rubi|last=Rose}}</ref>
