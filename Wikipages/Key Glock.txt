'''Markeyvius LaShun Cathey''' (born August 3, 1997), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Key Glock''', is an American rapper and songwriter from [[Memphis, Tennessee]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/06/01/key-glock-russian-cream-glock-bond-glockoma-interview|title=Key Glock is Memphis's best new lyricist|last=Godfrey|first=Gavin|website=The Fader|access-date=2020-01-18}}</ref> A protégé to [[Young Dolph]], he rose to fame in 2017 with his mixtape ''Glock Season''. He is signed to [[Young Dolph]]’s record label Paper Route Empire.

==Early life==
Markeyvius LaShun Cathey was born in [[South Memphis, Memphis, Tennessee|South Memphis]] on August 3, 1997.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/key-glock-mn0003641789/biography|title=Key Glock {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|access-date=2020-01-18}}</ref> When he was 20 months old, his mother was sent to prison, where she would be for the next 15 years, in both state and federal facilities. His father was in and out of his life, so he was raised by his grandmother and his aunt. According to Cathey, he and his grandmother occasionally visited his mother in prison during his youth.<ref name=":0" /> At 18 years old, he was charged with three counts of [[Aggravated Assault|aggravated assault]] related to his involvement in a shooting.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wmcactionnews5.com/story/31407610/man-arrested-for-ice-bar-shooting-that-injured-3|title=Man arrested for ICE Bar shooting that injured 3|last=Staff|first=WMCActionNews5 com|date=|website=WMC5|language=en-US|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-01-19}}</ref> Music was a distraction from his oftentimes tumultuous life.<ref name=":0" />

Cathey has said he grew up listening to [[Gucci Mane]] and [[Lil Wayne]], and cites [[Three 6 Mafia]] as his largest influence. He was also a big fan of [[Project Pat]], whom he describes as the "[[Drake (musician)|Drake]] of Memphis".<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/03/key-glock-interview-the-break/|title=The Break Presents: Key Glock - XXL|last=III|first=Robby Seabrook|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=2020-01-20}}</ref> According to Glock, Pat's songs "If You Ain't From My Hood" and "Gorilla Pimp" changed his life. The latter's chorus inspired one of his breakout songs, "Dig That". The first track that Glock recorded himself was a freestyle of [[Future (rapper)|Future]]'s "Ain't No Way Around It" in 2011.

==Career==
Young Dolph signed Key Glock to the Paper Route Empire label in 2017.<ref name=":0" /> On January 25 of that year, Glock released his debut single "Racks Today" featuring American rapper Jay Fizzle. On June 16, 2017, Glock released his debut mixtape, ''Glock Season'', under the label.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://damixhub.com/mixtape/key-glock-glock-season|title=Glock Season - Key Glock (Paper Route Empire)|website=damixhub.com|access-date=2020-01-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/key-glock-glock-season-new-mixtape.117286.html|title=Glock Season|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=2020-01-20}}</ref> His collaborative album ''[[Dum and Dummer]]'' with Young Dolph reached number eight on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/young-dolph|title=Young Dolph Chart History|website=Billboard}}</ref> Young Dolph's "Major" featuring Key Glock reached number 47 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart.

Following the release of his collaborative album ''Dum and Dummer'' with Young Dolph, he went on a European tour across [[Manchester]] and [[London]]. The pair brought out UK rapper [[Blade Brown]] as a supporting act. The two proceeded to start the "No Rules" Tour in 2020, beginning on February 5 in [[Seattle]].

On January 31, Key Glock released his fifth solo mixtape titled ''[[Yellow Tape (Key Glock album)|Yellow Tape]]'', which debuted at number 14 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. On May 22, 2020, Key Glock released his sixth mixtape, ''Son of a Gun'', again with no features.<ref name="Son" />

On March 5, 2021, Key Glock and Young Dolph released the single, "Aspen", and announced the release of their second collaborative album, ''Dum and Dummer 2'', due for release on March 26, 2021. It is a sequel to 2019's ''Dum and Dummer''. The project's cartoon artwork references ''[[Beavis & Butthead]]''.<ref name="Aspen">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/young-dolph-key-glock-aspen-dum-and-dummer-2-album|title=Young Dolph and Key Glock Link for New Song "Aspen" and Announce 'Dum and Dummer 2' Album. As of early 2021, Key glock has been working on YT2 which has been rumored to be releasedlate September|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=Mahadevan|first=Tara C.|date=March 5, 2021|accessdate=March 5, 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Key Glock is the cousin by marriage of Young Dolph.<ref name="Dolph">{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/young-dolph-key-glock-interview-1203376855/amp|title=How Rappers Young Dolph and Key Glock Turned Street Smarts Into Business Savvy|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|last=Ju|first=Shirley|date=October 21, 2019|accessdate=May 26, 2020}}</ref>

In May 2019, Key Glock was arrested for possession of a firearm and over 20 grams of marijuana.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Eustice|first=Kyle|date=May 14, 2019|title=Key Glock Arrested On Felony Firearm & Marijuana Possession Charges|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.51421/title.key-glock-arrested-on-felony-firearm-marijuana-possession-charges|url-status=live|website=[[HipHopDX]]}}</ref>
