'''''31 minutos''''' (lit: ''31 minutes'') is a [[Chile|Chilean]] [[comedy]] [[television series]] and a [[children's music]] [[Musical ensemble|band]] created by the production company [[Aplaplac]] (owned by [[Álvaro Díaz González|Álvaro Díaz]], [[Pedro Peirano]] and Juan Manuel Egaña) that began to be transmitted on March 15, 2003 by the signal of [[Televisión Nacional de Chile]] (TVN). The program is a parody to ''[[60 Minutos|60 minutos]]'', a controversial news program of the same channel, transmitted in the 1970s and 1980s. It focuses on the adventures of the team of a news program of little prestige in which something unexpected always happens, whose presenter is [[Tufillo Triviño Tulio|Tulio Triviño]]. The program's notes are educational and leave an explicit or implicit message, while others are quite ridiculous.

In its first period, the series had three seasons, from 2003 to 2005, in addition to a participation for the [[2003 Chilean telethon|2003 Chilean Telethon]] and a Christmas special that same year. On March 27, 2008, the series was taken to the cinema under the title of ''[[31 minutos, la película]]''.

After the third season and for the next nine years the series had no new episodes. In 2012, the production company Aplaplac confirmed that the series would return to television with a fourth season, which was released on October 4, 2014 through TVN, and its last original episode was broadcast on the night of December 27, 2014.

From 2004 to 2007, it was broadcast throughout Latin America by [[Nickelodeon (Latin American TV channel)|Nickelodeon]] and from 2015, it began to be broadcast by [[Cartoon Network (Latin American TV channel)|Cartoon Network]]. It also broadcasts in Mexico on [[Canal Once (Mexico)|Canal Once]] and [[Once Niñas y Niños|Once Niños]], and its most recent season is available in the [[Netflix|Netflix Latin America]] catalog.

''31 minutos'' has performed throughout Chile and [[Mexico]], making the program a musical band. On their tours they perform the songs broadcast on the program and their musical works outside of it.

== History ==

=== Origins and foundation of Aplaplac ===

''31 minutos'' date back to 1990, when its creators Álvaro Díaz and Pedro Peirano entered the [[University of Chile|Universidad de Chile]] to study journalism and met each other for the first time.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.latercera.com/culto/2018/07/07/alvaro-diaz-periodista-guionista-sentido-supervivencia-me-interesara-en-teatro/ |title=Álvaro Díaz: "El sentido de supervivencia hizo que me interesara en el teatro" |author=Pedro Bahamondes |date=July 6, 2018 |website=La Tercera |language=es |trans-title=Álvaro Díaz: "The sense of survival made me interested in the theater" |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> At first, Díaz did not meet with Peirano nor was he interested in talking to him, but they got together to do some academic work, which led both of them to realize that they shared the same sense of humor and to become friends.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.latercera.com/noticia/manifiesto-alvaro-diaz-creador-de-31-minutos/ |title=Manifiesto: Alvaro Díaz, creador de 31 Minutos |author=Tamy Palma |date=April 24, 2016 |website=La Tercera |language=es |trans-title=Manifesto: Alvaro Díaz, creator of 31 minutos |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> The two met again in 1995, when they produced programs such as [[Plan Z (TV series)|''Plan Z'']] and ''[[El Factor Humano]]'' for the Chilean television channel [[Canal 2 Rock & Pop|Rock & Pop]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://edu.fintual.cl/%C3%A1lvaro-d%C3%ADaz-la-plata-es-lo-m%C3%A1s-ordinario-que-hay-951c82cf31c5/ |title=Álvaro Díaz: "La plata es lo más ordinario que hay" |author=Francisca Feuerhake |website=EDU Fintual |language=es |trans-title=Álvaro Díaz: " Money is the most ordinary thing there is" |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> But, contrary to their expectations, neither production prospered.<ref name="ref_1">{{cite web |url=http://www.periodismo.uchile.cl/noticias/2002/peirano.html |title=El factor Peirano |author=Alejandro Morales Vargas |date=September 9, 2002 |website=Instituto de la Comunicación e Imagen de la Universidad de Chile |language=es |trans-title=The Peirano Factor |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> In addition, the channel ceased broadcasting on December 1, 1999,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.emol.com/noticias/Espectaculos/2018/11/25/928476/Coautor-de-libro-sobre-Canal-2-Rock--Pop-Nunca-se-recupero-el-estereotipo-de-medio-desordenado.html |title=Co-autor de libro sobre Pop: " Never recovered the stereotype of a messy environment" |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> leaving both Peirano and Diaz unemployed.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/cultura/pedro-peirano-nadie-se-intereso-por-el-factor-humano/2002-08-20/165700.html |title=Pedro Peirano: "Nadie se interesó por el "Factor Humano"" |date=August 20, 2002 |website=Radio Cooperativa |language=es |trans-title=Pedro Peirano: "Nobody was interested in the " Factor Humano "" |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref>

To get out of this situation, they founded the production company Aplaplac together with Juan Manuel Egaña.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.elciudadano.com/tendencias/revelan-el-origen-de-la-palabra-aplaplac-y-proviene-del-ingenio-de-un-tenor/11/21/ |title=Revelan el origen de la palabra Aplaplac y proviene del ingenio de un tenor |author=Ines Hazbun |date=November 21, 2017 |website=El Ciudadano |language=es |trans-title=They reveal the origin of the word Aplaplac and comes from the ingenuity of a tenor |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> Initially, Aplaplac developed two programs: ''Sangre, sudor y lágrimas'' (Blood, Sweat and Tears), —a human-interest program bought by the sports channel [[Panamerican Sports Network|PSN]]—,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.redlideres.cl/lider/pedro-peirano/ |title=Pedro Peirano |website= Red de Líderes Jóvenes |language=es |access-date= October 29, 2020}}</ref> and ''Mira tú'' (Look at You), a cultural program produced with funds from the [[National Television Council (Chile)|Consejo Nacional de Televisión]] in 2001 and broadcast by Televisión Nacional de Chile in 2002.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/cultura/peirano-y-diaz-realizaran-programa-cultural-con-fondos-del-cntv/2001-06-05/152800.html |title=Peirano y Díaz realizarán programa cultural con fondos del CNTV |date=June 5, 2001 |website=Radio Cooperativa |language=es |trans-title=Peirano and Diaz will carry out a cultural program with funds from the CNTV |access-date= October 29, 2020}}</ref>

''Mira tú'' stood out for its quality and became the production company's letter of introduction,<ref name="ref_1">{{cite web |url=http://www.periodismo.uchile.cl/noticias/2002/peirano.html |title=El factor Peirano |author=Alejandro Morales Vargas |date=September 9, 2002 |website=Instituto de la Comunicación e Imagen de la Universidad de Chile |language=es |trans-title=The Peirano Factor |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> therefore Díaz, Peirano and Egaña applied for a second time to a CNTV competition fund in 2002, this time in the children's television category.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/cultura/consejo-nacional-de-television-financiara-seis-programas-en-2003/2002-07-24/183400.html |title=Consejo Nacional de Televisión financiará seis programas en 2003 |date=July 24, 2002 |website=Radio Cooperativa |language=es |trans-title=Consejo Nacional de Televisión will finance six programs in 2003 |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> The project presented was entitled ''El gabinete del Doctor Mojado'' (The Wet Doctor's Cabinet), and in it a fish was conducting a television program from a fishbowl. In one of its segments, a puppet reported on what was happening with the excrements in the treatment plants.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/321966 |title=Cinco consejos para que emprendas al estilo '31 minutos' |author=Gabriel Orihuela |website=Entrepreneur |language=es |trans-title=Five tips to get you started '31 minutos' style |access-date= October 29, 2020}}</ref> Aplaplac won the contest and obtained funds to produce 21 chapters that would be broadcast on Televisión Nacional de Chile.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.emol.com/especiales/linkodromo/31minutos/index.htm |title=Quiero ser niño |author=Felipe Ossandón |date=May 30, 2003 |website=EMOL |language=es |trans-title=I want to be a kid |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> The idea evolved into a puppet and marionette news show called "31 minutos" -—a name that originated from the contest rules, which mentioned that the projects had to last half an hour—.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://granarenamonticello.cl/novedad/por-que-31-minutos-se-llama-asi/ |title=¿Por qué 31 Minutos se llama así? |website=Gran Arena Monticello |language=es |trans-title=Why is 31 minutos called that? |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref>

At the same time, Peirano worked at the Chilean newspaper [[El Mercurio]], where he met the artist [[Rodrigo Salinas (comedian)|Rodrigo Salinas]], who -together with the artists [[Daniel Castro Steinert|Daniel Castro]] and Matias Iglesis- formed the art collective [[La nueva gráfica chilena]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.d21.cl/rodrigo-salinas/ |title=Rodrigo Salinas |website=Departamento 21 |language=es |access-date= October 29, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= http://biut.latercera.com/vidacotidiana/2013/07/en-los-pantalones-de-rodrigo-salinas/ |title= Rodrigo Salinas… en los pantalones de un dibujante y comediante |author=Constanza Cortés |website=Biut |language=es |trans-title=Rodrigo Salinas... in the pants of a cartoonist and comedian |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> Peirano invited them to be part of the production team for 31 minutos, leaving Salinas and Castro as scriptwriters —along with Díaz and Peirano—, Iglesis as art director —establishing the visual identity of the program and its characters—<ref name="ref_2">{{cite web |url=http://ergocomics.cl/wp/2003/10/11/rodrigo-salinas-y-31-minutos/ |title= Rodrigo Salinas y 31 Minutos |date=October 11, 2003 |website=Ergocomics |language=es |trans-title=Rodrigo Salinas and 31 minutos |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> and Juan Manuel Egaña as executive producer of Aplaplac and ''31 minutos''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/entretencion/television/31-minutos/31-minutos-esta-tras-los-pasos-del-mercado-asiatico/2006-04-18/195901.html |title= "31 minutos" está tras los pasos del mercado asiático |date=April 18, 2006 |website=Radio Cooperativa |language=es |trans-title="31 minutos" is following the steps of the Asian market |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> Due to requirements from TVN, a stable female character had to be included in the episodes. The actress and comedian [[Jani Dueñas|Alejandra Dueñas]] —known from La nueva gráfica chilena— later joined the team, giving life to Patana's character.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.chvnoticias.cl/vina360/patana-jani-duenas-31-minutos-machismo_20190222/ |title=La historia tras Patana, el personaje de Jani Dueñas en "31 Minutos" que fue víctima de machismo |date=22 February 2019 |website=Chilevisión noticias |language=es |trans-title=The story behind Patana, Jani Dueñas' character in "31 Minutos" who was a victim of male chauvinism |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref>

The conviction of their creators at the beginning was to make a type of television program that they would have liked to see as children.<ref name="ref_3">{{cite web |url=https://www.australvaldivia.cl/site/apg/reportajes/pags/20031221043137.html |title=Monos de 31 minutos: la gran novedad |date=December 21, 2003 |website=El Austral de Los Ríos |language=es |trans-title=31 minutos characters: the great novelty |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.milenio.com/cultura/libertad-empatia-claves-exito-31-minutos |title=Libertad y empatía, claves en el éxito de '31 Minutos' |author=Jorge Maldonado Varela |date= November 8, 2018 |website=Milenio |language=es |trans-title=Liberty and empathy, keys to the success of '31 Minutos |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> They were fully aware that minors no longer consumed only programs that were aimed at a child audience and, based on that, came to the idea that a satire of the news had to be made.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.chilango.com/cine-y-tv/historia-de-31-minutos-alvaro-diaz/ |title=¡Tulio, estamos al aire! Conoce la historia de 31 Minutos |author=Daniela Barranco |date=November 9, 2018 |website=Chilango |language=es |trans-title=Tulio, we're on the air! Know the story of 31 Minutos |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> The idea also arose of inventing songs and presenting them in a musical classification. To that end, Peirano —who shared friendship with the members of the Chilean funk rock group [[Chancho en Piedra]]— introduced [[Pablo Ilabaca]] —guitarist of that band—, who brought with him a compact disc of tracks without lyrics, with which he didn't know what to do.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.zancada.com/musica-mi-muneca-me-hablo-la-historia-segun-alvaro-diaz/ |title=Música: "Mi muñeca me habló", la historia según Álvaro Díaz |date=November 6, 2012 |website=Zancada |language=es |trans-title=Music: " Mi muñeca me habló", the story according to Álvaro Díaz |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref> The first instrumental Díaz and Peirano heard was the one that later became the central theme of ''31 minutos'', of which they quickly became fond. The rest of the tracks were used for the soundtrack of the chapters, along with other songs invented by the team. Ilabaca remained as the music producer for ''31 minutos'', giving rise to the Ranking Top segment, of which the character Policarpo Avendaño —interpreted by Daniel Castro— is in charge.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.radiosantotomas.cl/?p=4021 |title=De calcetines a las cuerdas: El proceso creativo de las canciones de 31 Minutos |author=Sebastián Díaz |date=June 9, 2017 |website=Radio Santo Tomás |language=es |trans-title=From Socks to Strings: The Creative Process of 31 Minutos Songs |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.eldinamo.cl/cultpop/2016/02/29/por-que-huachimingo-y-calcetin-con-rombos-man-tambien-ganaron-un-oscar-gracias-a-bear-story/ |title=¿Por qué "Huachimingo" y "Calcetín con Rombos Man" también ganaron un Óscar gracias a Bear Story? |date=February 29, 2016 |website=El Dínamo |language=es |trans-title=Why did "Huachimingo" and " Calcetín con Rombos Man" also win an Oscar thanks to Bear Story? |access-date=October 29, 2020}}</ref>

=== Success and internationalization ===
''31 minutos'' made its debut on March 15, 2003. Its first episodes marked 6 points of screen rating, but as time went on it began to popularize and increase its audience to an average of 14 points.<ref name="ref_4">{{cite web == Spin-offs ==

Aplaplac has produced several mini-series with the characters of ''31 minutos''. As a project for the National Museum of Dentistry of the University of Chile, the production company made a four-episode [[web series]] called ''Las muelas de Guaripolo'',<ref>{{cite web|author=Cecilia Espinosa Cortés|title=Videos "Las muelas de Guaripolo", 31 Minutos|url=http://www.odontologia.uchile.cl/extension/unidad-de-fomento-a-las-actividades-de-extension/76486/videos-las-muelas-de-guaripolo-31-minutos|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=Facultad de Odontologia Universidad de Chile|language=es}}{{Dead link|date=March 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Irving Ávila|date=January 18, 2013|title=Una Boca Limpia con Guaripolo de 31 Minutos|trans-title=A clear mouth with Guaripolo of 31 Minutos|url=http://enteratedf.com/culturama/18-enero-2013-601pm/una-boca-limpia-con-guaripolo-de-31-minutos|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=entératedf.com|language=es}}</ref> in which this character appears teaching the public information about dental hygiene and its history.<ref>{{cite web|date=November 18, 2011|title=Las muelas de Guaripolo, una campaña sobre dientes y más|trans-title=Las muelas de Guaripolo, a campaign about teeth and more|url=https://www.zancada.com/las-muelas-de-guaripolo-una-campana-sobre-dientes-y-mas/|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=Zancada|language=es}}</ref> Together with Las muelas de Guaripolo, in 2017, ''31 minutos'' in collaboration with the [[Ministry of Health (Chile)|Ministry of Health of Chile]] made the web series ''La nube de humo''. This mini-series consists of three chapters of four to five minutes and deals with how Juan Carlos Bodoque —already retired from journalism— decides to fall into Ramona's requests to do a report on air pollution in Chile.<ref>{{cite web|date=August 22, 2017|title=Ministerio del Medioambiente anunció el retorno del "conejo más famoso de Chile"|trans-title=Ministry of the Environment announced the return of "Chile's most famous rabbit"|url=https://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/entretencion/television/31-minutos/ministerio-del-medioambiente-anuncio-el-retorno-del-conejo-mas-famoso/2017-08-22/193849.html|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=Radio Cooperativa|language=es}}</ref>

On July 11, 2009, ''[[Las vacaciones de Tulio, Patana y el pequeño Tim]]'' premiered on TVN.<ref>{{cite web|date=July 3, 2009|title=Las Vacaciones de Tulio, Patana y el Pequeño Tim: desde el 11 de julio|trans-title=Las Vacaciones de Tulio, Patana y el Pequeño Tim: from July 11|url=https://www.zancada.com/las-vacaciones-de-tulio-patana-y-el-pequeno-tim-desde-el-11-de-julio/|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=Zancada|language=es}}</ref> The program —which consists of 12 episodes—<ref>{{cite web|date=July 10, 2009|title=Mañana vuelve a las pantallas 31 minutos|trans-title=Tomorrow returns to the screens 31 minutos|url=https://www.latercera.com/noticia/manana-vuelve-a-las-pantallas-31-minutos/|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=La Tercera|language=es}}</ref> is designed as a spin-off of ''31 minutos'', that is, a production derived from some of its characters;<ref>{{cite web|date=April 15, 2020|title=La vida es un dibujo|trans-title=Life is a drawing|url=https://www.emol.com/noticias/Tendencias/2009/02/10/727890/la-vida-es-un-dibujo.html|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=EMOL|language=es}}</ref> and the one chosen was precisely Tulio Triviño, the self-centered news anchor who now moves to his lavish summer cabin with his niece Patana and Tim, a friend of hers.<ref>{{cite web|date=May 28, 2009|title=Alvaro Díaz: En la nueva serie mantenemos el humor de "31 minutos"|trans-title=Alvaro Diaz: In the new series we keep the humour of "31 minutos"|url=https://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/entretencion/television/31-minutos/alvaro-diaz-en-la-nueva-serie-mantenemos-el-humor-de-31-minutos/2009-05-28/174446.html|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=Radio Cooperativa|language=es}}</ref> The stay is boring, so Tulio entertains his little companions by telling them stories, and so each chapter begins. It is worth mentioning that Juan Carlos Bodoque is also present in Tulio's stories giving ecological advice.<ref>{{cite web|date=July 10, 2009|title=Personajes de "31 minutos" protagonizarán ciclo dedicado a la eficiencia energética|trans-title=Characters of "31 minutos" will star in a cycle dedicated to energy efficiency|url=https://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/entretencion/television/31-minutos/personajes-de-31-minutos-protagonizaran-ciclo-dedicado-a-la-eficiencia/2009-07-10/162243.html|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=Radio Cooperativa|language=es}}</ref>[[File:Pablo Ilabaca 2018.jpg|thumb|243x243px|Pablo Ilabaca is one of the composers of the songs of ''31 minutos'', along with the writers and actors of the series.<ref>{{Cite web|date=December 31, 2019|title=Pablo Ilabaca: Es un orgullo que se tribute la música de "31 minutos" en otros países|trans-title=Pablo Ilabaca: It is an honor that the music of "31 minutos" is being tributed in other countries|url=https://www.cooperativa.cl/noticias/entretencion/television/31-minutos/pablo-ilabaca-es-un-orgullo-que-se-tribute-la-musica-de-31-minutos-en/2009-12-31/113141.html|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=Radio Cooperativa|language=es}}</ref>]]
