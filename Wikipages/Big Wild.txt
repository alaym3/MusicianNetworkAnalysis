'''Jackson Stell''' (born 1990), known by his stage name of '''Big Wild''', is an American electronic producer, composer, vocalist and sound engineer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.smilepolitely.com/music/big_wild_is_on_the_rise/|title=Big Wild is on the rise|last=Porkoney|first=Therese|date=February 15, 2018|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

As a performer, his live shows often consist of switching between multiple instruments (drum pads, synths, keyboards & live vocals).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://bigwildmusic.com/about |title=About |website=bigwildmusic.com |accessdate=3 May 2019}}</ref> Coinciding with the release of his debut album, ''Superdream'', Big Wild kicked off a world tour in the Spring of 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.pollstar.com/artist/big-wild-192765?tab=upcomingDates&page=0 |title=Big Wild |website=pollstar.com |accessdate=13 May 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
Stell began his music career as a hip-hop beat producer, known as '''J Beatz''', in his native Massachusetts at the age of 14.<ref name="Vice">{{cite web |url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/6azm3k/big-wild-interview |title=Eating Ice Cream with Electronica's Latest Wiz Kid, Big Wild |author=Lindner, Emilee |work=Vice |date=May 13, 2016 |accessdate=October 25, 2019}}</ref> Stell graduated from [[Northeastern University]] and worked as a composer for an advertising agency before moving to [[Los Angeles]], where he switched his focus to producing [[electronic music]] and singing under the moniker “Big Wild”.<ref name="Vice"/><ref name="NPR">{{cite web |url=https://www.npr.org/2019/04/07/710402578/big-wild-bottles-his-challenges-and-curiosities-into-debut-album |title=Big Wild Bottles His Challenges And Curiosities Into Debut Album |author=Evstatieva, Monika |work=NPR |date=April 7, 2019 |accessdate=October 25, 2019}}</ref>

In 2015, Big Wild began touring with electronic duo [[Odesza]]; later that year, he collaborated with the duo on a remix of their track "Say My Name".<ref name="allmusic">{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/big-wild-mn0003462805/biography|title=Big Wild {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=2019-11-30}}</ref> Big Wild’s first breakout track, “Aftergold” featuring [[Tove Styrke]], was released in April 2015, topping the [[Spotify]] Global Viral chart upon release.<ref name="NPR"/> He released his debut EP, ''Invincible'', in February 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.foreignfamilycollective.com/music-artists |title=Artist — Foreign Family Collective |website=foreignfamilycollective.com |accessdate=13 May 2019}}</ref>

In February 2019, after signing with Counter Records,<ref name="allmusic"/> Big Wild released his debut album, ''Superdream''. The new album debuted Big Wild as the primary vocalist, lyricist, and songwriter for the first time ever. Though rooted in electronica, the album features elements of energetic pop, indie, disco, with subtle nods to his early influence from DJ Maul-IE.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://edm.com/music-releases/big-wild-debut-album-superdream |title=Big Wild Releases Beautiful Debut Album, Superdream |website=edm.com |accessdate=13 May 2019}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Stell lives with his girlfriend in [[Portland, Oregon]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.riffmagazine.com/features/big-wild-jackson-stell-new-frontier/|title=Interview: Big Wild's Jackson Stell expands into a new frontier|date=2019-07-29|website=RIFF Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-03}}</ref> Many members of Stell's family are also musicians; his father played guitar and participated in a few bands throughout college, his mother was a life long choir singer, and his grandmother was a [[big band]] vocalist in the 1930s and 1940s.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://pilerats.com/written/get-to-know/big-wild-superdream-interview/|title=Big Wild on the risks and challenges of his debut album, Superdream|website=PILERATS|access-date=2019-12-03}}</ref>

In late September 2013, Stell was diagnosed with [[thyroid cancer]].<ref name="cancer">{{Cite web|url=https://riverbeats.life/big-wild-bout-with-cancer-and-album-superdream/|title=Big Wild Opens Up About Former Bout With Cancer & His Debut Album 'Superdream'|last=Schmidt|first=Sean|date=2018-11-08|website=RIVER BEATS|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-03}}</ref> He announced he was “healthy and cured” in an Instagram post, in which he also shared his struggles with depression.<ref name="cancer"/> His experience battling and recovering from thyroid cancer inspired the song on his ''Superdream'' album, “Maker”.<ref name="cancer"/>
