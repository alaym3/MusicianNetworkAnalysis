'''Jonathan Piuze-Roy''' (born 1989) is a Canadian pop singer and songwriter. Son of hockey great [[Patrick Roy]], he initially pursued hockey in junior leagues, but abandoned that path in favour of music while still a teenager. Having previously released three albums, his major-label debut, ''Mr. Optimist Blues'', was released in 2017, and includes several songs written by 1980s pop star [[Corey Hart (singer)|Corey Hart]].

== Music career ==
Roy's album ''What I've Become'' came out in 2009.<ref name=Globe /> His second album, ''Found My Way'', came out in 2010.<ref>{{cite interview|last=Roy|first=Jonathan|last2=Roy|first2=Patrick|interviewer=[[Beverly Thomson]]|title=Patrick Roy's son embarks on music career|work=[[Canada AM]]|publisher=[[CTV Television Network|CTV]]|location=Toronto|date=May 4, 2010}}</ref> He released his French-language debut album, ''La route'', in 2011.<ref>{{cite news|author=Usinger, Mike|date=February 15, 2017|url=https://www.straight.com/music/869176/perfection-about-only-option-optimistic-jonathan-roy|title=Perfection about the only option for optimistic Jonathan Roy|newspaper=The Georgia Straight|accessdate=February 19, 2017}}</ref> The title track, a duet with [[Natasha St-Pier]], received the most attention.

Roy had a critically acclaimed role in the 2012 revival of the musical ''[[Don Juan (musical)|Don Juan]]''.<ref>Williams, John (December 1, 2015). "[http://www.torontosun.com/2015/12/01/jonathan-roy-trades-goalie-stick-for-guitar Jonathan Roy trades goalie stick for guitar]", ''[[Toronto Sun]]''. Retrieved September 5, 2017.</ref>

In 2012, his father helped him set up a meeting with 1980s pop singer [[Corey Hart (singer)|Corey Hart]], who runs Siena Records, an affiliate of [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music]]. Hart offered to sign him to his label after Roy performed a [[Teddy Thompson]] song for him at Hart's home in [[Nassau, Bahamas]]. Roy and Hart had a hit holiday release in 2016 when they duetted on the [[Chris Rea]] song "Driving Home for Christmas".<ref name=Gazette />

In 2017, Roy released the album ''Mr. Optimist Blues'' on Siena Records, Corey Hart's music label. Hart executive-produced the album, and wrote three of the album's songs. The ''[[Montreal Gazette]]'''s music critic characterizes the style as "easygoing 21st-century pop with a little light reggae thrown in for good measure. Think Ed Sheeran meets Paolo Nutini and then slap on Bob Marley’s greatest hits."<ref name=Gazette /> Roy is based in Toronto.
