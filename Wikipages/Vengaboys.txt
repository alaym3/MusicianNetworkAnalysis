'''Vengaboys''' are a Dutch [[Eurodance]] music group based in [[Rotterdam]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Deboick|first=Sophia|date=17 May 2020|title=The Vengaboys, Eurodance and beyond: is Rotterdam the port of pop?|newspaper=The New European|url=https://www.theneweuropean.co.uk/brexit-news/rotterdam-as-the-port-of-pop-79590}}</ref> The group was the creation of Dutch producers [[Wessel van Diepen]] and Dennis van den Driesschen (known as Danski and Delmundo). It consists of lead (female) vocalists Kim Sasabone and Denise Post-Van Rijswijk and male vocalists Robin Pors and Donny Latupeirissa. Casting and selection of the performing act 1997–2000 was done by Wendelien van Diepen.

The group enjoyed commercial success in the late 1990s. They are best known for their hit singles "[[We Like to Party (Vengaboys song)|We Like to Party]]", "[[Boom, Boom, Boom, Boom!!]]" and "[[We're Going to Ibiza]]", the latter two of which topped the [[UK Singles Chart]]. They have sold an estimated 25 million records worldwide.<ref name="eurodance_encyclopaedia">{{cite encyclopedia | url=http://www.eurokdj.com/search/eurodb.php?name=Vengaboys | title=Vengaboys | encyclopedia=The Eurodance Encyclopædia | publisher=KDJ Eurodance Ltd | access-date=3 May 2012}}</ref> On 2 May 2001, the [[World Music Awards]] gave Vengaboys the award for best-selling dance group of the year.<ref name=wma>{{cite encyclopedia | url=http://www.funtrivia.com/en/Music/Music-Industry-Awards-1842.html | title=Music Industry Awards | encyclopedia=Encyclopedia Fun Trivia | access-date=3 May 2012 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120213231915/http://www.funtrivia.com/en/Music/Music-Industry-Awards-1842.html | archive-date=13 February 2012 | url-status=dead | df=dmy-all }}</ref>
In 2019, Dutch online newspaper [[Nu.nl]] called Vengaboys the most successful Dutch pop group in history.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nu.nl/muziek/5915445/profiel-vengaboys-de-succesvolste-nederlandse-popgroep-aller-tijden.html|title=Profiel Vengaboys: De succesvolste Nederlandse popgroep aller tijden|date=30 May 2019|website=Nu.nl|access-date=22 December 2019}}</ref>

==History==
===1997–1998: Formation and ''Up and Down''===
Dutch producers Danski and Delmundo initially found success organising illegal beach parties during the summer.<ref name="eurodance_encyclopaedia" />

===1998–1999: Second line-up and ''The Party Album''===
In 1998, with the addition of the three new members Denise Post-Van Rijswijk, Roy den Burger and Robin Pors and existing member Sasabone, the now four-piece entered the [[UK Singles Chart]] at number 4 with "[[Up and Down (song)|Up and Down]]",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/19981122/7501/|title=Official Singles Chart Top 100|date=22–28 November 1998|website=Official Charts}}</ref> and their follow-up "[[We Like to Party! (Vengaboys song)|We Like to Party! (The Vengabus)]]" peaked at number 1 in Belgium<ref name="UltratopVengabus">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ultratop.be/nl/showitemurl.asp?cat=s&id=ecd&realurl=/nl/song/ecd/vengaboys-we-like-to-party!-(the-vengabus)|title=Vengaboys - We Like To Party! (The Vengabus)|website=Ultratop.be|access-date=22 December 2019}}</ref> and was a Top 10 hit in other European countries,<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://worldradiohistory.com/UK/Music-and-Media/90s/1999/MM-1999-03-20.pdf |title=Eurochart Hot 100 SIngles |magazine=Music & Media |date=20 March 1999 |volume=16 |issue=12 |page=8}}</ref> Canada,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bac-lac.gc.ca/eng/discover/films-videos-sound-recordings/rpm/Pages/image.aspx?Image=nlc008388.8154&URLjpg=http://www.collectionscanada.gc.ca/obj/028020/f4/nlc008388.8154.gif&Ecopy=nlc008388.8154|title=Image: RPM Weekly|website=Library and Archives Canada|date=31 May 1999}}</ref> Australia,<ref name="UltratopVengabus"/> and New Zealand.<ref name="UltratopVengabus"/> It reached number 26 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Vengaboys/chart-history/HSI|title=Chart History: Vengaboys|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> Vengaboys released their second album, ''[[The Party Album (Vengaboys album)|The Party Album]]''. In June 1999, "[[Boom Boom Boom Boom]]" topped the charts in the UK,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/19990620/7501/|title=Official Singles Chart Top 100|date=20–26 June 1999|website=Official Charts}}</ref> [[New Zealand]] and the [[Netherlands]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://charts.nz/showitem.asp?interpret=Vengaboys&titel=Boom,+Boom,+Boom,+Boom!!&cat=s|title=Vengaboys – Boom, Boom, Boom, Boom!! (song)|website=charts.org.nz}}</ref> In November of that year, "Boom, Boom, Boom, Boom!!" was also used in an [[television advertisement|advert]] in [[Japan]] for [[Nissan]] for their [[Nissan AD|Wingroad]] [[station wagon|5-door estate]].<ref>{{cite web|title=NISSAN Wingroad CM(1999)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fsE0X_jZzwM|work=[[YouTube]]|access-date=14 May 2017|date=14 February 2012}}</ref> ''The Party Album!'' reached the top of the charts in over 20 countries including the U.K., Canada, Germany, Spain, South Africa, Thailand, Poland, Australia, and Mexico.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} "[[We're Going to Ibiza]]", a reworking of [[Typically Tropical]]'s 1975 number 1 hit "Barbados", also reached the top slot in the Netherlands in September 1999.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://charts.nz/showitem.asp?interpret=Vengaboys&titel=We%27re+Going+To+Ibiza!&cat=s|title=Vengaboys – We're Going To Ibiza! (song)|website=charts.org.nz}}</ref> The LP spent 30 consecutive weeks on the U.S. [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]],<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Vengaboys/chart-history/TLP/song/174412|title=Chart History: Vengaboys|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> and was certified [[music recording sales certification|gold]] (500,000 units) in November 1999.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Vengaboys&ti=The+Party+Album#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum|website=Recording Industry Association of America}}</ref> ''The Party Album!'' was re-released as ''Greatest Hits - Part 1''. ''[[The Remix Album (Vengaboys album)|The Remix Album]]'', released in late 1999.

===2000–2002: Departure of Pors, ''The Platinum Album'' and Hiatus===
For their fourth single, the group gained a virtual member, the computer-animated Cheekah.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} The single "[[Cheekah Bow Bow (That Computer Song)]]" was not as successful as their previous singles.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} They released their single "[[Forever as One (Vengaboys song)|Forever as One]]" in February 2001 with little promotion.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} The single charted at 28 in the UK, making it their worst-performing single.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} Shortly after this release, Denise Post-Van Rijswijk and Bakker left the group{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} The group eventually disbanded for several years.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} During her departure, Denise Post-van Rijswijk gave birth to her first child.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}}

===2006–2009: Return on stage and line-up changes===
In 2006, they returned to the club scene with new member Donny Latupeirissa a.k.a. Ma'Donny, replacing den Burger as Cowboy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.planetromeo.com/en/blog/queer-careers-donny-latupeirissa-vengaboys/|title=Queer Careers - Donny Latupeirissa, Vengaboys|date=2018-08-19|website=ROMEO|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

===2010–2011: ''The Best of Vengaboys''===
In 2010, the band released a new single, "[[Rocket To Uranus (song)|Rocket To Uranus]]", a collaboration with singer-songwriter [[Pete Burns]] and American celebrity [[Perez Hilton]], which was only released in their home country in June.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} The video premiered on 6 June at the Dutch television station TMF,<ref>{{Cite web | date=1 June 2010 | url=https://twitter.com/vengatweets/status/15233580614 | title=Vengaboys: And for tv addicts: the fu ... | publisher=[[Twitter]] | access-date=3 May 2012}}</ref> and in 2D and 3D formats on their own website and YouTube channel.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} They released a [[Best of]] in [[Australia]] in December 2011.<ref>[http://chaos.com/product/vengaboys-best-of-australian-tour-edition/11569784-5185.html Best Of (Australian Tour Edition): Vengaboys: Music] {{Webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20120707073246/http://chaos.com/product/vengaboys-best-of-australian-tour-edition/11569784-5185.html |date=7 July 2012 }}. Chaos.com (9 December 2011). Retrieved on 2012-04-29.</ref>

===2012–2013: Single releases===
[[File:Vengaboys Gouden Pijl 2013 02.jpg|thumb|Vengaboys (2013)]]
In 2012, Vengaboys began performing a cover of T-Spoon's song "Sex On The Beach" in their live shows. In January 2013, the group first performed "Hot, Hot, Hot", a cover of the 1983 hit originally by 'Arrow' during their National Tour in Australia. The song was released in July 2013 as a single, containing 13 remixes.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} The single was released worldwide except France on iTunes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/Vengaboys/photos/a.10151305112780349/10151727563495349/?type=3|title=Vengaboys|website=Facebook.com}}</ref> Around that time, Kim Sasabone became pregnant. The group continued to maintain a busy tour schedule, with most shows based in Europe and Australia.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}}

===2014–2015: ''X-mas Party Album''===
[[File:2015332235403 2015-11-28 Sunshine Live - Die 90er Live on Stage - Sven - 5DS R - 0482 - 5DSR3599 mod.jpg|thumb|The Vengaboys on stage in 2015]]
On January 2014, Robin Pors said in an interview in Dubai that the group intended to continue touring in Europe and that they were working on future shows in the Middle East and India, along with performing their greatest hits, their latest single, "Hot, Hot, Hot", and reportedly new unreleased tracks. He also stated that releasing new material was planned for 2014. In May 2014 a new remix from their single "To Brazil" re-titled "2 Brazil" was released. In November 2014 a new album was released called ''The X-mas Party Album'' including all the Vengaboys hits with a Christmas sound. Also, the single "Where Did My X-mas Tree Go" was released together with a new music video.{{Citation needed|date=October 2019}} A tour of South Africa was scheduled for December.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vengaboys.com/schedule/|title=Vengaboys – Schedule|website=Vengaboys.com|access-date=22 December 2019}}</ref>

[[File:Vengaboys Oldenburg.jpg|thumb|Vengaboys (2016)]]

===2015-2017: Australia and New Zealand tour===
In late February 2015, Denise Post-Van Rijswijk said that she was pregnant with her second child, a girl.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/vengaboys/photos/a.10151813802445349.1073741825.309650080348/10153087156590349/?type=1&theater|title=vengaboys - Mobile Uploads - Facebook|website=Facebook.com}}</ref>

In 2016, the group toured Australia and New Zealand.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vengaboys.com/|title=VENGABOYS – Official Homepage|website=Vengaboys.com|access-date=12 October 2017}}</ref> In June, the Vengaboys hosted MTV Pride, a pop-up channel in the UK, dedicated to the LGBT community.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pinknews.co.uk/2016/06/22/special-pride-tv-channel-to-be-hosted-by-the-vengaboys/|title=Special Pride TV channel to be hosted by the Vengaboys|website=Pinknews.com|access-date=12 October 2017}}</ref>

In November and December 2017, Vengaboys supported [[Steps (group)|Steps]] at their [[Party on the Dancefloor Tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FVTX8B9XRX4|title=Vengaboys - The Party On The Dancefloor tour with Steps - UK & Ireland|last=Vengaboys|date=21 December 2017|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>

=== 2019: 20th anniversary and ''Unplugged'' EP ===
In 2019, Vengaboys celebrated their 20th anniversary and released the songs "Boom, boom, Boom, Boom!! - Unplugged" and "We're going to Ibiza! - Unplugged" in June on an EP titled ''Unplugged #1's''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-gb/entertainment/music/the-vengaboys-release-unplugged-hits-to-mark-20th-anniversary/ar-AADyNAH?li=BBoPWjQ&item=personalization_enabled:false|title=The Vengaboys release unplugged hits to mark 20th anniversary|website=Msn.com|access-date=2019-08-29}}</ref>

=== 2020–2021: Covers and re-mixes ===
In the summer of 2020, the band released a festival version of their worldwide hit "Up & Down" with Australian DJ and producer [[Timmy Trumpet]], with the new version credited to Timmy Trumpet x Vengaboys .<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://weraveyou.com/2020/08/timmy-trumpet-vengaboys-present-a-new-version-of-up-down/|title=Timmy Trumpet & Vengaboys present a new version of 'Up & Down'|website=We Rave You|date=21 August 2020|first=Lakshay|last=Bhagtani}}</ref>

In September 2021, the band released a cover version of "[[1999 (Charli XCX and Troye Sivan song)|1999]]", Charli XCX's Top 20 UK hit with Troye Sivan, which was retitled "1999 (I Wanna Go Back)" and came with a deep fake-style video which saw the cover stars from various 1990s albums lip-syncing to the song and the band put into the ''Friends'' title sequence with the sofa and fountain.<ref>https://www.nme.com/news/music/vengaboys-throw-it-back-to-the-90s-with-a-cover-of-charli-xcx-and-troye-sivans-1999-3042719</ref><ref>https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/vengaboys-look-back-at-their-biggest-hits-after-boom-boom-boom-boom-we-were-set-for-life__34020/</ref>

September 20, 2021, also marked the Vengaboys' first live performance since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, taking part in 90's Nostalgia: Electric Circus, a Canadian tour featuring [[Eurodance]] music.<ref>{{Cite web|title=A 90's Nostalgia Concert Is Coming To Abbotsford With Aqua + Vengaboys|url=https://604now.com/events/90s-nostalgia-electric-circus-concert-abbotsford-2021-aqua-vengaboys/|access-date=2021-09-21|website=604 Now|language=en-CA}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=90's Nostalgia Canadian Tour - featuring Aqua, Vengaboys, Ace of Base, Eiffel 65, 2 Unlimited & Haddaway|url=https://90snostalgia.ca/|access-date=2021-09-21|website=90's Nostalgia Outdoor Concert|language=en-CA}}</ref>
