'''Bob Moses''' is a Canadian electronic duo based in [[Vancouver]], British Columbia.  Formed in [[New York City]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.straight.com/music/549436/bob-moses-keeps-it-live|title=Bob Moses keeps it live|date=2015-10-07|website=Georgia Straight Vancouver's News & Entertainment Weekly|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref> the band consists of Tom Howie and [[Jim Vallance]]. A remix of their song ''Tearing Me Up'' by [[RAC (DJ)|RAC]] won a Grammy Award for [[Grammy Award for Best Remixed Recording, Non-Classical|best remixed recording, non-classical]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hernandez |first1=Christina |title=RAC wins Grammy for his remix of ‘Tearing Me Up’ by Bob Moses, Feb 2017 |url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2017/02/rac-wins-grammy-re-work-tearing-bob-moses/ |website=dancingastronaut.com |publisher=Dancing Astronaut |access-date=2021-09-02}}</ref>

== History ==
===Formation===
Howie and Vallance attended the same middle school and high school in Vancouver but were merely acquaintances, despite enjoying similar tastes in music. They had both dabbled into the sounds of [[Punk rock in California|California punk]], [[Grunge|Northwest grunge]], and [[Electronic music|minimalist electronic]] but never together.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.redbull.com/us/en/music/stories/1331664034357/an-interview-with-brooklyn-band-bob-moses|title=Bob Moses Is Working On a Building|last=Mejia|first=Paula|website=www.redbull.com|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref>

After high school, Vallance moved to New York City, where he worked for a record engineering company for Dutch artist Matthew Dekay on [[Lee Burridge]]'s All Day I Dream imprint.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://beatroute.ca/2015/10/13/electronic-duo-bob-moses-leaves-deep-house-behind-for-deep-music/|title=Electronic duo Bob Moses leaves deep house behind for deep music|date=2015-10-13|website=BeatRoute Magazine|access-date=2016-06-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160807100415/http://beatroute.ca/2015/10/13/electronic-duo-bob-moses-leaves-deep-house-behind-for-deep-music/|archive-date=2016-08-07|url-status=dead}}</ref> He began his career making [[Trance music|trance]] and [[progressive house]] music, with early success at age 18 with releases on [[Ultra Music|Ultra Records]] and remixes for [[Sia Furler|Sia]] and her record "[[Buttons (Sia song)|Buttons]]" alongside [[Chris Lake]] and [[Markus Schulz]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rhapsody.com/artist/sia/album/buttons-ultra-records-llc|title=Buttons|date=2008-11-18|website=Rhapsody|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref> After working for Dekay, Vallance began writing and engineering for [[Get Physical Music|Get Physical]]'s co-founder and one-half of M.A.N.D.Y., Philipp Jung.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.beyondthedrop.com/2014/08/24/interview-bob-moses/|title=Interview: Get to Know Bob Moses|date=2014-08-24|website=BTD|access-date=2016-06-15|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160424081237/http://www.beyondthedrop.com/2014/08/24/interview-bob-moses/|archive-date=2016-04-24|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Howie continued singing and after a year at [[Berklee College of Music]] in [[Boston]], Massachusetts, he also moved to New York.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/2015/09/bob-moses-all-eyes-on/|title=Bob Moses: One Name, Two Emo-Tronic Spiritual Bros {{!}} SPIN|date=2015-09-24|website=Spin|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref> He was originally in the rock band Coalition, known for their song ''Snowboarding''.

Vallance and Howie reunited in a [[Lowe's]] hardware store parking lot.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://vancouversun.com/entertainment/vancouver-duo-bob-moses-on-going-from-a-parking-lot-to-the-grammys/wcm/818cfa4e-2988-435a-925a-7d5a579737ec|title=Vancouver duo Bob Moses on going from a parking lot to the Grammys|last=Friend|first=David|date=February 8, 2017|newspaper=Vancouver Sun/ The Canadian Press|access-date=February 14, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180711064045/https://vancouversun.com/entertainment/vancouver-duo-bob-moses-on-going-from-a-parking-lot-to-the-grammys/wcm/818cfa4e-2988-435a-925a-7d5a579737ec|archive-date=July 11, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> They started working on music in Howie's apartment,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://milk.xyz/feature/bob-moses-is-way-more-emo-than-your-typical-edm-group/|title=Bob Moses Is Way More Emo Than Your Typical EDM Group {{!}} Milk|date=2016-04-15|website=Milk|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-15}}{{Dead link|date=June 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> then shared a flat and began working on music as a duo. They helped write a vocal hook for one of the songs that Jung was working on,<ref name=":1" /> and experimented with different sounds, using Howie's background in [[rock music]]<ref name=":0" /> and Vallance's in [[trance music]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://houseplanet.dj/2010/03/31/houseplanet-exclusive-interview-with-jimmy-vallance/|title=HousePlanet Exclusive Interview with Jimmy Vallance|last=adminhtml|date=2010-03-31|website=HousePlanet|access-date=2016-06-15|archive-date=2016-08-20|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160820143504/http://houseplanet.dj/2010/03/31/houseplanet-exclusive-interview-with-jimmy-vallance/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

After some studio time, the duo showed the [[Scissor and Thread|Scissor & Thread]] crew, specifically Francis Harris and Anthony Collins, aka Frank & Tony, what they had been working on.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/6677638/bob-moses-days-gone-by-debut-interview|title=Bob Moses Discusses Debut Album 'Days Gone By' & the Intersection of Dance Music and Songwriting|website=Billboard|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref> Harris suggested the name 'Bob Moses' after New York architect [[Robert Moses]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sodwee.com/blog/2015/12/itw-bob-moses/|title=Bob Moses: They Apparently Like Confusing People|website=Sodwee|access-date=2020-12-23}}</ref> and their debut EP, ''Hands to Hold'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/the-bob-moses/sets/bob-moses-hands-to-hold-ep|title=Bob Moses - Hands to Hold EP "Vinyl" Preview|website=SoundCloud|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref> debuted on that label in October 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.residentadvisor.net/reviews/11770|title=Review: Bob Moses - Hands to Hold EP|website=Resident Advisor|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref> Soon after the release of this first EP, they went on to deliver a second, ''Far from the Tree'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.residentadvisor.net/reviews/13693|title=Review: Bob Moses - Far From The Tree EP|website=Resident Advisor|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref> before signing to [[Domino Recording Company|Domino Records]] in March 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.completemusicupdate.com/article/domino-signs-bob-moses/|title=Domino signs Bob Moses {{!}} Complete Music Update|website=www.completemusicupdate.com|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref>

===''Days Gone By'' (2015–2017)===
Bob Moses' debut album, ''Days Gone By'', was released on September 18, 2015. "Tearing Me Up" became the most prominent song on the album, earning [[Grammy Award]] nominations for [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording|best dance recording]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Remixed Recording, Non-Classical|best remixed recording, non-classical]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/nominees?genre=66|title=59th Annual GRAMMY Awards|access-date=10 December 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-grammys-2017-nominations-winners-list-20161205-story.html|title=2017 Grammy Awards: Complete list of nominees|access-date=10 December 2016}}</ref> It went on to win the latter award for remixer RAC. Vallance said the concept for the song was imagined while both of them were "going through something very similar" in their romantic lives.<ref name=":2" /> Howie has pointed out that part of the song's inspiration came from the beat of [[Gary Glitter]]'s "[[Rock and Roll (Gary Glitter song)|Rock and Roll Part 2]]". The song received considerable radio airplay in the United States, having peaked at No. 16 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''<nowiki/>'s [[Alternative Songs]] chart.<ref name="bbalt">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6700846/bob-moses/chart?f=377|title=Bob Moses Album & Song Chart History – Alternative Songs|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 15, 2017|archive-date=January 18, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170118203716/http://www.billboard.com/artist/6700846/bob-moses/chart?f=377|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In January 2016, Bob Moses performed on ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'', after being personally picked by [[Ellen DeGeneres]] to appear on the show. They performed "Tearing Me Up".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ellentube.com/videos/0-lfcowokx/|title=Bob Moses Performs 'Tearing Me Up'|website=ellentube|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref> Tracks from ''Days Gone By'' have also been played on [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]'s TV show, ''[[The Catch (TV series)|The Catch]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://leosigh.com/listen-to-the-cool-bob-moses-track-tearing-me-up-from-abcs-the-catch-its-mellow-and-cool-video/|title=Listen to the Cool Bob Moses Track 'Tearing Me Up' From ABC's 'The Catch' — It's Mellow and Cool (Video) {{!}} Leo Sigh|website=leosigh.com|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tunefind.com/show/the-catch/season-1/31906|title=Music from The Catch Episode S1E03|website=[[TuneFind]]|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tunefind.com/show/the-catch/season-1/31909|title=Music from The Catch Episode S1E06|website=[[TuneFind]]|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-15}}</ref>

On August 19, 2016, Bob Moses released a special Never Enough edition of their album ''Days Gone By''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dancingastronaut.com/2016/06/bob-moses-release-days-gone-never-enough-edition-august/|title=Bob Moses to release extended version of 'Days Gone By' in August - Dancing Astronaut|website=[[Dancing Astronaut]]|access-date=2016-09-01}}</ref> The album featured remixes of "Like It or Not" and "Tearing Me Up" from artists like [[A-Trak]], [[RAC (musician)|RAC]], [[Joris Voorn]], and Tale of Us.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ventsmagazine.com/2016/06/30/bob-moses-release-days-gone-never-enough-edition-announce-tour-dates-debut-documentary|title=Bob Moses Release Days Gone By Never Enough Edition, Announce Tour Dates, Debut Documentary|website=Vents Magazine|access-date=2016-09-01}}</ref> The album also featured two new bonus tracks, "She Don't Mind" and "Here We Are", along with live versions of "Tearing Me Up", "Before I Fall", and "Nothing At All."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dominomusic.com/bob-moses/80/bob-moses-to-release-days-gone-by-never-enough-edition|title=Bob Moses to release Days Gone By (Never Enough Edition)|date=2016-06-30|access-date=2016-09-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160830162520/http://www.dominomusic.com/bob-moses/80/bob-moses-to-release-days-gone-by-never-enough-edition|archive-date=2016-08-30|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2017, Bob Moses was nominated for Live Act of the Year at the [[Electronic Music Awards]].

===''Battle Lines'' (2018)===
On May 30, 2018, Bob Moses announced a new tour in North America, along with a new single titled "Heaven Only Knows".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2018/05/bob-moses-new-heaven-only-knows-tour/|title=Bob Moses returns with incendiary new track 'Heaven Only Knows,' announces tour|date=2018-05-30|website=[[Dancing Astronaut]]|access-date=2018-06-06}}</ref>

On July 26, 2018, Domino announced that ''Battle Lines'' would be released on September 14, 2018, and released the first single from it, "Back Down."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dominomusic.com/bob-moses/421/bob-moses-announces-new-album-battle-lines|title=Bob Moses announces new album, Battle Lines|date=2018-07-26|work=Domino Music|access-date=2018-09-14|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180914203523/https://www.dominomusic.com/bob-moses/421/bob-moses-announces-new-album-battle-lines|archive-date=2018-09-14|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On September 14, 2018, Bob Moses released their second album, ''Battle Lines'', on multiple streaming and music services as well as on CD/LP via their label Domino.<ref name=":3">{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/Bobmosesmusic/status/1040450091902033921?s=19|title=Bob Moses on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=2018-09-14|language=en}}</ref>

On December 12, 2019, the duo released the ''Unplugged'' EP, featuring acoustic versions of three songs from the ''Battle Lines'' album, plus a cover of the song "Save a Prayer" by Duran Duran (from the album ''Rio'', 1982).

===''Desire'' (2020)===
Bob Moses released their third album, ''Desire'', on August 28, 2020, to multiple streaming and music services.<ref name="forbes.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/lisakocay/2020/08/28/bob-moses-album-desire/#72a198d5b215|title=Bob Moses Drop Smoldering New Album ‘Desire’|date=2020-08-28|website=[[Forbes]]|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dominomusic.com/news/us/bob-moses-announce-desire-and-share-title-track-with-zhu-plus-interactive-animated-video|title=Bob Moses announce 'Desire' and share title track with ZHU, plus interactive animated video|date=2020-07-16|website=[[dominomusic.com]]|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref> The duo streamed a performance to a live audience at the Inside Lands festival on August 28.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sfoutsidelands.com/insidelands/|title=Inside Lands 2020 Schedule|website=[[sfoutsidelands.com]]|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://edm.com/music-releases/bob-moses-desire-album|title=BOB MOSES RELEASE HIGHLY ANTICIPATED THEMATIC PROJECT "DESIRE"|date=2020-08-28|website=[[edm.com]]|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref>

Described by the artists as "a love tale for the digital age", the album is presented over six continuously mixed tracks.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dominomusic.com/releases/bob-moses/desire-2/12?_ga=2.178115426.671256553.1601672505-1994385512.1601672505|title=Bob Moses: Desire LP|date=2020-08-28|website=[[dominomusic.com]]|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref> With typical tour schedules disrupted by the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], the duo told [[Forbes]] that they intend to perform live stream sets and begin to collect content for another album.<ref name="forbes.com"/>
