'''Shaeem Santino Wright''' (born 12 July 1996) known as '''Lord Apex''', is a British [[Rapping|MC]] who grew up in White City, London, United Kingdom.<ref name=":0">{{cite web |title=Lord Apex is the cult MC floating to the top |url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/profiles/lord-apex/ |website=Crack Magazine |publisher=Yemi Abiade}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=2020-09-04|title=Lord Apex, sombre Sensei des bas-fonds londoniens|url=https://www.thebackpackerz.com/lord-apex-portrait/|access-date=2021-02-20|website=BACKPACKERZ|language=fr-FR}}</ref> His music crosses multiple styles of rap, including [[British hip hop|UK hip-hop]], [[alternative hip hop]], and [[Lo-fi music|lo-fi hip hop.]]<ref name=":0" />

== Style and projects ==
Apex's stage name was inspired by hip-hop producer [[Madlib]]'s alter ego, Quasimoto aka Lord Quas.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lord Apex is thecult MC floating to the top |url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/profiles/lord-apex/ |website=Crack Magazine}}</ref>

Apex frequently states that he doesn't like to be put into any one genre with his music.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Artist Feature: Lord Apex|url=https://us.carhartt-wip.com/blogs/journal/artist-feature-lord-apex|access-date=2021-02-07|website=Carhartt WIP USA|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-01-17|title=Backstage Pass: Lord Apex {{!}} Wonderland Magazine|url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2018/01/17/backstage-pass-lord-apex/|access-date=2021-02-07|website=Wonderland|language=en-US}}</ref> Journalists, and Apex himself, often reference lo-fi, 90s US [[hip hop]] acts like [[J Dilla]] and [[MF Doom]] as influences.<ref>{{Cite web|title=West London Rapper Lord Apex Shares Meditative New Video 'Up Early'|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/videos/west-london-rapper-lord-apex-shares-meditative-new-video-up-early|access-date=2021-02-07|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> He often also talks about his love for [[anime]] and [[vintage clothing]], both of which have inspired the visuals for his music and his general aesthetic.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-01-25|title=Wise Words of The Sensei: Interview with Lord Apex - @SENSEIAPEX with @MisterrCha - Producers United|url=http://theproducersunited.com/2015/01/25/wise-words-sensei-interview-lord-apex-senseiapex-misterrcha/|access-date=2021-02-07|website=Producers  United|language=en-US}}</ref>

Since releasing his first track in 2014, Apex has seen over 7 million views on his [[YouTube]] channel, with 50,000 subscribers as of February 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=LORD APEX's YouTube Stats (Summary Profile) - Social Blade Stats|url=https://socialblade.com/youtube/user/tinoapex|access-date=2021-02-07|website=socialblade.com}}</ref> Apex also appeared on the popular German music YouTube channel [[ColorsxStudios|COLORS]], where he performed his track Vintage Garms.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-07-01|title=Lord Apex Performs "Vintage Garms" For Colors|url=https://grmdaily.com/lord-apex-colors-vintage-garms/|access-date=2021-02-07|website=GRM Daily|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref name=":1" />

He has also featured in promotions, and released his own radio segment, for the US hip hop affiliated [[workwear]] brand [[Carhartt]].<ref name=":2" />

In 2020 he released a studio album with New York-based producer V-Don called Supply and Demand. Reviewers noted Apex's "swaggering and ominous" vocal delivery and "off-kilter flow" throughout the Supply and Demand project.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lord Apex + V Don - Supply & Demand [Stream]|url=https://www.thepitldn.com/pitnews/supply-and-demand|access-date=2021-02-13|website=The Pit London|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Track Of The Day 24/8 - Lord Apex|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/track-of-the-day-248-lord-apex|access-date=2021-02-13|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> The album featured several other prominent American rappers, such as [[Murs (rapper)|Murs]] of [[Rhymesayers Entertainment]] and [[CJ Fly]] of the [[Pro Era]] collective.<ref name=":1" /> Another feature on the record was from New York's [[Westside Gunn]], formerly of [[Shady Records]], on the deluxe version track London Fog.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lord Apex & V Don ft: Westside Gunn - London Fog|url=https://www.wordplaymagazine.com/blog-1/2020/12/28/lord-apex-amp-v-don-ft-westside-gunn-london-fog|access-date=2021-02-11|website=WORDPLAY|language=en-US}}</ref>

As well as his collaboration with Carhartt, he was also part of a promotional campaign for musical equipment maker [[Novation Digital Music Systems]], featuring a live performance of his track Miyagi Blueprint using their Launchpad X.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Everything Just Synchronises {{!}} Novation|url=https://novationmusic.com/en/news/made-to-express-lord-apex|access-date=2021-02-11|website=novationmusic.com}}</ref>

In 2020 Lord Apex was nominated for a [[UK Music Video Awards|UK Music Video Award]] in the Best Hip Hop/Grime/Rap Video Newcomer category, for his video with Kam-Bu entitled Different.<ref>{{Cite web|title=NEWS: UK MUSIC VIDEO AWARDS 2020 NOMINATIONS|url=https://www.videostatic.com/news/2020/09/30/uk-music-video-awards-2020-nominations|access-date=2021-02-12|website=VideoStatic|language=en}}</ref> In January 2021, he featured on [[Complex Networks|Complex's]] 21 UK Rappers to Watch in 2021 list, in which they said he is "perfectly positioned to rise to the top."<ref>{{Cite web|title=21 UK Rappers To Watch In 2021|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2021/01/21-uk-rappers-to-watch-in-2021/|access-date=2021-02-11|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>
