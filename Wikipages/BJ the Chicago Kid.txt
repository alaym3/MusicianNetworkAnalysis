'''Bryan James Sledge'''<ref name = "allmusic">{{cite web|title=Bryan Sledge|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/bj-the-chicago-kid-mn0002517403/biography|website=AllMusic|access-date=30 December 2013}}</ref> (born November 23, 1984), better known by his stage name '''BJ the Chicago Kid''' (or simply '''BJ'''), is an American singer and songwriter from [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]]. Sledge is best known for frequently collaborating with [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] recording artists [[Schoolboy Q]], [[Ab-Soul]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], and [[Jay Rock]]. He has also worked with several other prominent rappers, such as [[Freddie Gibbs]], [[Warren G]], [[Nekfeu]], [[Big K.R.I.T.]], [[GLC (rapper)|GLC]], [[Kanye West]] and [[Anderson .Paak]], among numerous others.

Sledge's first work in the music industry was on a [[Dave Hollister]] record in 2001, titled "For You", which he co-wrote alongside Gerald Haddon. Sledge's vocals were first featured on a high-profile [[Single (music)|single]] in 2006; a song by Chicago rapper [[Kanye West]] titled "[[Impossible (Kanye West song)|Impossible]]" was released in promotion for the 2006 American film ''[[Mission: Impossible III]]''. In February 2012, he independently released his debut studio album, ''Pineapple Now-Laters''. Sledge subsequently secured a record deal with [[Motown]], in August 2012. The label then serviced the ''Pineapple Now-Laters'' song "Good Luv'n", as Sledge's commercial debut single.

==Life and career==

===1984&ndash;2008: Early life and career beginnings===
Bryan Sledge was born to church choir directors in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]]. He grew up in the [[Washington Heights, Chicago#Brainerd|Brainerd]] neighborhood on [[South Side, Chicago|Chicago's South Side]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chicagomag.com/Chicago-Magazine/February-2016/BJ-the-Chicago-Kid/|title=How BJ the Chicago Kid Broke Out of Brainerd and Onto FM Radio|work=Pollock, Matt|publisher=[[Chicago (magazine)|Chicago]]|date=2016-10-02|access-date=2021-01-16}}</ref> Sledge became inspired to pursue a music career after seeing [[Janet Jackson]] perform on tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/05/who-is-bj-the-chicago-kid/family|title=Family - Who Is BJ the Chicago Kid? - Complex|work=Drake, David|publisher=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=2013-05-05|access-date=2014-04-20}}</ref> At the age of 19, he moved to [[Los Angeles, California]], where he became a [[backup singer]] for American [[urban contemporary gospel|gospel]] duo [[Mary Mary]]. He also performed on [[Stevie Wonder]]'s ''[[A Time to Love (album)|A Time to Love]]'' (2005). He later wrote for [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[gospel music|gospel]] artists such as [[Crystal Aikin]], [[Shirley Caesar]], [[Lalah Hathaway]], [[Joe (singer)|Joe]] and [[Kindred the Family Soul]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Andy|last=Kellman |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/bj-the-chicago-kid-mn0002517403/biography |title=BJ the Chicago Kid &#124; Biography & History |website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=2016-02-19}}</ref> Later, he performed on [[Kanye West]]'s single "Impossible," which also features [[Twista]] and [[Keyshia Cole]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/05/who-is-bj-the-chicago-kid/|title=Who Is BJ the Chicago Kid?|first=David|last=Drake|work=Complex|access-date=8 March 2015}}</ref>

===2009&ndash;2011: Mixtapes===
In 2009, Sledge released his debut [[mixtape]], ''A Taste of Chicago'', under the [[moniker]] BJ the Chicago Kid. Later that year, he followed up with ''The New Beginning''. In 2011, he released his third mixtape, titled ''The Life of Love's Cupid''.<ref name="grammy1">{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/news/exclusive-grammycom-interview-with-bj-the-chicago-kid |title=Exclusive GRAMMY.com Interview With BJ The Chicago Kid |website=GRAMMY.com |date=2013-10-25 |access-date=2016-02-19}}</ref>

===2012&ndash;present: In My Mind and Motown record deal===
In February 2012, Sledge independently released his debut [[studio album]], titled ''Pineapple Now-Laters'', under M.A.F.E MUSIC LLC. In August 2012, it was announced that Sledge signed a [[recording contract]] with American record company, [[Motown]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.20669/title.bj-the-chicago-kid-signs-to-motown|title=BJ The Chicago Kid Signs To Motown|author=HipHopDX|date=3 August 2012|work=HipHopDX|access-date=8 March 2015}}</ref> After inking his new deal with the label, Motown Label Services began promoting the ''Pineapple Now-Laters'' track "Good Luv'n," at Urban and Rhythmic radio formats in North America and released it as a [[Single (music)|single]] via [[digital distribution]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allhiphop.com/2012/08/03/bj-the-chicago-kid-joins-universal-motown-family-with-newly-signed-record-deal/ |title=Hip-Hop News, Rumors, Rap Music & Videos &#124;AllHipHop » BJ The Chicago Kid Joins Universal Motown Family With Newly Signed Record Deal |website=AllHipHop.com |date=2012-08-03 |access-date=2016-02-19}}</ref>

In February 2013, Sledge released the track titled "Honey." Sledge's collaboration with American rappers [[Freddie Gibbs]] and [[Problem (rapper)|Problem]], titled "Smokin' and Ridin'", is featured on [[Radio Los Santos]] in ''[[Grand Theft Auto V]]'', the fifth installment in the critically acclaimed video game series. 
In October 2013, Sledge revealed he began working on his [[major record label|major label]] debut, which was then announced to be released sometime in 2014.<ref name="grammy1"/> Later that year, in December 2013, Sledge released a [[Christmas]]-themed [[extended play]] (EP), titled ''A Soulful Christmas'', for free online.<ref>{{cite web|last=Sargent |first=Jordan |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/12/bj-the-chicago-kid-soulful-christmas |title=Download BJ The Chicago Kid's New Holiday EP "A Soulful Christmas" |website=Complex.com |date=2013-12-24 |access-date=2016-02-19}}</ref>

On April 22, 2014, West Coast rapper [[Schoolboy Q]] released "[[Studio (song)|Studio]]", which features vocals from Sledge, as the fourth single from his album ''[[Oxymoron (album)|Oxymoron]]''. "Studio" acquired Sledge [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] chart placement and went on to chart in the [[Top 40]] of the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. On November 19, 2014, Sledge released his fourth mixtape, ''The M.A.F.E. Project''. The mixtape features 12 songs and guest appearances from Freddie Gibbs, Sasha Go Hard, Schoolboy Q and [[Smoke DZA]].<ref name="mafeproject">{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/bj-chicago-kids-mafe-project-mixtape|title=Listen To BJ The Chicago Kid's 'The M.A.F.E. Project'|work=Vibe|access-date=8 March 2015}}</ref> On December 23, 2014, Motown Records released a song from ''The M.A.F.E. Project'' titled "It's True", which features a verse from Schoolboy Q, as a single. The single was accompanied by a [[music video]] directed by Verluxe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/12/22/bj-the-chicago-kid-schoolboy-q-its-true-video|title=BJ The Chicago Kid And Schoolboy Q Premiere "It's True" Video|first=Naomi|last=Zeichner|work=The FADER|access-date=8 March 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/12/bj-the-chicago-kid-its-true-schoolboy-q|title=Watch BJ The Chicago Kid's "It's True" Video f/ Schoolboy Q|website=Complex}}</ref>

On February 19, 2016, Sledge released his major label debut album, "[[In My Mind (BJ the Chicago Kid album)|In My Mind]]".

Sledge released his third studio album, ''1123'', on July 26, 2019.<ref name="tsa">{{cite web |last1=Pierre |first1=Alphonse |title=BJ the Chicago Kid Announces New Album 1123, Shares New Video With Offset: Watch |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/bj-the-chicago-kid-announces-new-album-1123-shares-new-video-with-offset-watch/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=June 21, 2019 |date=June 21, 2019}}</ref>
