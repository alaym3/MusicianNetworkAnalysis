'''Elephanz''' is a French [[electro (music)|electro]] / [[Pop music|pop]] duo formed in January 2008 by the two brothers Jonathan 'Jon' and Maxime 'Max' Verleysen, originating from [[Nantes]], France. The Verleysen brothers are the sole composers and songwriters of their albums. They are both vocalists, with Jonathan playing guitar and Maxime the keyboards. Their live performances include Clement Plaza-Illand playing the drums.

They are currently signed to Blackbird / Affection London / Musicast and are managed by Yamani 'Momo' Dazi (who previously co-founded the label Big Cheese Records).

The formation released the EP ''Ideal Roommates'' in 2009 and were invited later that year to the prestigious [[Rencontres Trans Musicales|Transmusicales Festival]] in Rennes. In 2010, the band received the first 'Prix Chorus' (Chorus Award) <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.leparisien.fr/hauts-de-seine-92/six-groupes-selectionnes-pour-le-prix-chorus-02-02-2010-800589.php|title=Six groupes sélectionnés pour le prix Chorus|author=|date=2 February 2010|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2017}}</ref> out of 600 possible candidates.

Elephanz's first album, ''Time for a Change'', was released in 2013 with a Deluxe version published in 2014. The first single is ''Stereo'' (which features in the compilation [[Radio Nova (France)|Nova Tunes 2.4]] , and in the movie [[Les Profs 2]]) while the track ''Time for a Change'' was used in several TV adverts, including [[Fnac]], [[Volvo]], [[Numericable]].

The band was nominated for the highly esteemed [[Victoires de la Musique]] award for 'Electronic Music Album of the Year'in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rtl.fr/culture/medias-people/victoires-de-la-musique-2014-les-nominations-devoilees-par-le-parisien-7768817036|title=Victoires de la musique 2014 : les nominations dévoilées par "Le Parisien"|author=|date=|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2017}}</ref> The awards ceremony is the French equivalent of the Brit Awards or Grammys. Their 2014-2015 tour included 50 dates and saw the group play in iconic venues, such as [[La Cigale]] in Paris, Stereolux in Nantes, and famous festivals like [[Les Francofolies de La Rochelle|Francofolies]], Papillons de Nuit and [[Printemps de Bourges]].

Their second album, ''Elephanz'', was released on October 13, 2017<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ouest-france.fr/pays-de-la-loire/nantes-44000/les-nantais-d-elephanz-sont-de-retour-plus-electro-pop-que-jamais-5310573|title=Les Nantais d’Elephanz sont de retour, plus électro-pop que jamais|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2017}}</ref> with their first single ''Blowing like a Storm''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.presseocean.fr/actualite/nantes-elephanz-prepare-sa-tournee-a-stereolux-a-suivre-ce-soir-en-direct-12-10-2017-249063|title=Nantes. Le nouveau tube d'Elephanz ce soir depuis Stereolux [VIDÉO]|author=|date=|publisher=|accessdate=13 November 2017}}</ref> receiving airplay on [[Radio FG|FG Radio]], [[M6 (TV channel)]] and W9. This second opus is remarkably different to the first, with more consistency between singles (often all relating to love, passionate, or feeling lost' as well as a more 'electro' sound, all underpinned by an improved, more slick production level. An ''Intro'' and two other tracks (''Freakshow'' and ''American Lover'') also mix French with English lyrics.

The band started touring for this second album in late October 2017 with feature shows in Paris, Marseille, Lyons, Strasbourg, Rennes and Mulhouse in November, with more dates to follow in 2018.
