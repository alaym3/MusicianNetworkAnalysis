'''Ethan Snoreck''', better known by his stage name '''Whethan''' (formerly Wheathin), is an [[Americans|American]] DJ and music producer from [[Chicago]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.edmsauce.com/2017/01/05/whethan-savage-feat-flux-pavilion-max/|title=Whethan - Savage (feat. Flux Pavilion & MAX)|last=Cooper|first=Dan|date=2017-01-05|website=EDM Sauce|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://pilerats.com/written/get-to-know/meet-whethan-the-17-year-old-producer-working-skrillex/|title=Meet Whethan, The 17-year-old Producer Working With Skrillex|last=Davies|first=Hayden|date=August 2, 2016|newspaper=PILERATS|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref> He gained recognition after his remix of the track "XE3" by Mssingno, which as of December 2018 has garnered over 13 million plays on SoundCloud.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://edm.com/articles/2017/1/22/get-to-know-whethan|title=Watch Out for Mega-Talented Up-and-Coming DJ, Whethan|last=Woods|first=Rachel|date=2017-01-22|newspaper=EDM.com|access-date=2017-02-11|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Mssingno - XE3 (Whethan Turn)|url=https://soundcloud.com/whethan/xe3|language=en|access-date=2018-01-20}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Whethan attended [[Carl Sandburg High School]] from his freshman year until his first semester of senior year, and at his senior homecoming for his entire school previewing his unreleased work to his classmates.<ref name=":3">{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/sandburghs/status/779341350315499520?lang=en|title=Whethan will be performing at homecoming.|date=September 23, 2016|work=Twitter|access-date=2018-02-03}}</ref> In May 2017, Whethan took a short break from touring with The Chainsmokers in order to attend his graduation.

He first began releasing house music under his real name Ethan Snoreck. At the same time, Snoreck had an alternative account for his trap remixes and edits. That account was previously known as Wheathin which came from his real name "Ethan" and few of his friends happened to say it regularly which made the name stay until he was forced to change his name to Whethan in 2016.<ref name=":4" /><ref name=":5" /> He started producing music by 'messing around' on [[GarageBand|Garage Band]] on his [[iPad]].<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=http://edmchicago.com/2015/08/01/chicagos-16-year-old-producer-exclusive-interview-with-wheathin/|title=Chicago's 16-Year-Old Producer: Exclusive Interview with Wheathin|last=Acosta|first=Pedro|date=2015-08-01|website=EDM Chicago|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref><ref name=":3" /> He first became interested in music when he began rapping with his friend Trevor in [[Sixth grade|6th]] and [[Seventh grade|7th grade]]. Since then he listened to well-known musicians such as [[Daft Punk]], [[Skrillex]], and [[Deadmau5]].<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=https://melismamagazine.com/2015/01/23/young-producers-spotlight-wheathin/|title=Young Producers Spotlight: Wheathin|last=Bretherton|first=Ross|date=2015-01-23|website=Melisma Magazine|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref> 

When he was 16 years old, he was recognized as the youngest ever producer to release a record on [[Australians|Australian]] [[Electronic dance music|EDM]] label, [[Future Classic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://eventseeker.com/artist/58893-whethan|title=Artist Profile: Whethan|website=eventseeker.com|language=en|access-date=2020-03-17}}</ref> He later caught the attention of [[dubstep]] pioneer and [[OWSLA]]-founder, [[Skrillex]].<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4" />

== Career ==
Whethan often incorporates a mix of indie future synths with electropop in his songs.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.theuntz.com/news/this-whethan-kid-is-on-a-rocketship-to-superstardom/|title=This Whethan kid is on a rocketship to superstardom.|last=Gimpel|first=Alexandra|date=2016-12-07|newspaper=The Untz|access-date=2017-02-11|language=en}}</ref> His main influence is [[Flume (musician)|Flume]], considered one of the pioneers of the future bass movement. 

He frequently collaborates with Oliver Tree.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.edmsauce.com/2017/01/18/new-whethan-oliver-tree-collab-will-ever-talk/|title=The New Whethan and Oliver Tree Collab Will Be 'All You Ever Talk About'|last=Giorgio|first=Alec|date=2017-01-18|website=EDM Sauce|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref> The duo have released singles titled "All You Ever Talk About", "When I'm Down", "Enemy","Do You Feel Me?" and "Freefall" together.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.edmsauce.com/2017/01/12/virtu-flips-whethan-oliver-trees-im-stellar-results/|title=Virtu Flips Whethan and Oliver Tree's 'When I'm Down' With Stellar Results|last=Erik|date=2017-01-12|website=EDM Sauce|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref> Whethan has also released "Can't Hide" and "Savage", featuring Ashe and [[Flux Pavilion]] and [[Max Schneider|MAX]], respectively.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.youredm.com/2016/08/13/whethan-cant-hide-feat-ashe/|title=Whethan - Can't Hide (feat. Ashe)|last=Yau|first=Jeffrey|date=2016-08-13|website=Your EDM|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://variancemagazine.com/sounds/7221-whethan-savage|title=Listen: Whethan Releases Massive New Single 'Savage'|last=Robles|first=Jonathan|date=6 December 2016|newspaper=Variance Magazine|access-date=2017-02-11|language=en-gb}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://earmilk.com/2017/09/29/oliver-tree-whethan-join-forces-on-new-single-enemy/|title = Oliver Tree & Whethan join forces on new single 'Enemy'|date = 29 September 2017}}</ref> An official music video was released for the song "Can't Hide" which has over 2 million [[SoundCloud]] plays.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.edmsauce.com/2016/11/21/whethan-releases-official-music-video-cant-hide-feat-ashe/|title=Whethan Releases Official Music Video for, "Can't Hide" feat. Ashe|last=Ninivaggi|first=Elizabeth|date=2016-11-21|website=EDM Sauce|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref> The song also debuted at number one on Billboard's Spotify Viral 50 chart.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/spotify-viral-50/2016-09-03|title=Spotify Viral 50|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref>

Whethan performed at [[South by Southwest|SXSW]] music festival in March.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/events/sxsw/7669850/fbi-director-james-comey-sxsw|title=FBI Director James Comey to Speak at SXSW|date=27 January 2017|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref> He will also be performing at [[BUKU Music + Art Project]] festival, which fuses [[electronic music]], [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[indie rock]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7603841/buku-festival-2017-lineup|title=deadmau5, Zeds Dead, Tycho & More to Headline BUKU Festival 2017|date=6 December 2016|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref> Whethan toured with [[Trap music (EDM)|trap]] artists [[Flosstradamus]] in November alongside fellow DJs [[Slushii]], [[Towkio]], and Gent & Jawns.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7476092/flosstradamus-hi-def-youth-fall-us-tour|title=Flosstradamus Announce Hi Def Youth Fall U.S. Tour|last=Havens|first=Lyndsey|date=16 August 2016|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=2017-02-11}}</ref>

In 2018, Whethan collaborated with pop singer [[Dua Lipa]] on the song "High", which was featured in the film ''[[Fifty Shades Freed (film)|Fifty Shades Freed]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.youredm.com/2018/01/08/whethan-reveals-details-new-collab-dua-lipa-coming-soon/|title=EDM's Upcoming 18-Year-Old Star Reveals New Collab with Dua Lipa|date=2018-01-08|work=Your EDM|access-date=2018-01-20}}</ref> In March of the same year, he released the single "Be Like You" featuring [[Broods]].<ref name="Be Like You single">{{Cite web |url=https://music.soundgrail.com/blog/whethan-be-like-you/ |title=Whethan Drops Lush New Single 'Be Like You' |last=Wiedenman |first=Ian |date=2018-03-19 |website=SoundGrail |access-date=2018-03-19}}</ref>

In 2020, he was set to perform at the Ultra Music Festival in Miami, but the festival was cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://djmag.com/news/ultra-announces-official-cancellation-festival-2020|title=Ultra announces official cancellation of festival in 2020 due to coronavirus|last=Fielding|first=Amy|date=2020-03-06|website=DJMag.com|access-date=2020-03-17}}</ref>
