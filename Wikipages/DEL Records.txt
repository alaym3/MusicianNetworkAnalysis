'''DEL Records''' is an American Spanish language [[record label]] founded by Angel del Villar in 2008.  Its headquarters is in the [[Los Angeles]] suburb of [[Bell Gardens, California]] and includes DEL Records, DEL Publishing, DEL Studios and DEL Entertainment, all of which focus on [[Regional Mexican]] music, specifically with genres from Mexico's pacific states such as [[Banda music|Banda]], [[Norteño (music)#Regional variations|Pacific-style Norteño]], [[Norteño (music)#Regional variations|Norteño-Banda]], Sierreño, Sierreño-Banda and [[Mariachi]]. DEL Records has launched the careers of chart-topping artists like [[Gerardo Ortíz]], [[Luis Coronel]], Ulices Chaidez y Sus Plebes, and [[Régulo Caro]].<ref name="Billboard 4">{{cite news|title=Billboard's Latin Power Players List Revealed|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7564703/billboard-latin-music-power-players-list-2016|accessdate=8 May 2017|work=Billboard 4|date=November 3, 2016}}</ref> DEL Records and its artists are frequent winners at the [[Billboard Latin Music Awards]] including five in 2017.<ref name="Billboard 2017">{{cite news|last1=Cobo|first1=Leila|title=Nicky Jam Wins Big at Billboard Latin Music Awards: See the Full Winners List|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/events/latin-awards/7776260/billboard-latin-music-awards-2017-winners-list|accessdate=30 May 2017|work=Billboard 2017|date=April 27, 2017}}</ref>
In seven years, DEL's sales, touring and content development divisions lead and transform the genre. DEL boasts 3 platinum and 9 gold records, and more than 10 #1 singles, as well as #1 albums. DEL Records’ artist tour every week of the year, with over 200 live concert dates in the US and Mexico.

==History==
The label was formed by Ángel del Villar who previously ran a fencing company. The label's first signing was with Gerardo Ortíz in 2010. Originally, DEL Records had a partnership with [[Anaheim, California]] based VIP Music.<ref name="Latin Times">{{cite news|last1=Roiz|first1=Jessica Lucia|title=AB Quintanilla Signed To DEL Records: Selena's Brother Joins Label Owned By Chiquis Boyfriend|url=http://www.latintimes.com/ab-quintanilla-signed-del-records-selenas-brother-joins-label-owned-chiquis-boyfriend-389646|accessdate=9 May 2017|work=Latin Times|date=June 10, 2016}}</ref> The label is currently distributed by Sony Music Latin.<ref name="Discogs">{{cite web|title=DEL Records|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/4857057-DEL-Records|website=Discogs|accessdate=8 May 2017}}</ref>

In April 2015, DEL Records donated $12,200 to the Rowland Unified School District in Rowland Heights, California in order to help with their area student and teacher scholarships.<ref name="Rowland Schools">{{cite web|title=DEL Records Donates More than $12,000 To Nogales High School and Rowland Unified School District Programs|url=http://www.rowlandschools.org/apps/news/article/456088|website=Rowland Schools|accessdate=8 May 2017}}</ref>

In 2016, DEL Records received six [[Billboard Latin Music Awards]] including those for Regional Mexican Airplay Label of the Year, Regional Mexican Airplay Imprint of the Year and Regional Mexican Albums Imprint of the Year.

On April 14, 2016, the label entered into a partnership with [[Combate Americas]], the first-ever U.S. Hispanic [[Mixed Martial Arts]] sports franchise. The premier event was April 18, 2016 for [[NBC Universo]]'s fight series, ''Road to the Championship''. Regulo Caro and King Lil G walked the [[Mixed martial arts|MMA]] fighters to the cage and also performed later in the night.<ref name="Billboard 5">{{cite news|title=DEL Records & Combate Americas Announce Partnership: Regulo Caro, King Lil G Set to Participate|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7333946/del-records-combate-americas-partnership-regulo-caro-king-lil-g|accessdate=8 May 2017|work=Billboard 5|date=April 14, 2016}}</ref> Also in 2016, DEL Records partnered with local and national organizations to launch "Shut Up the Haters and Vote," which encouraged high school students of eligible age to register to vote.<ref name="Billboard 6">{{cite news|last1=Flores|first1=Griselda|title='Shut Up the Haters and Vote': DEL Records Partners with Advocacy Groups In Youth Voting Campaign|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7370297/del-records-shut-up-the-haters-and-vote-campaign|accessdate=9 May 2017|work=Billboard 6|date=May 12, 2016}}</ref>

Two years after the death of their artist [[Ariel Camacho]], DEL Records released on February 24, 2017 a tribute album of duets with other artists on the label such as Gerardo Ortiz, Luis Coronel and Regulo Caro. ''Ariel Camacho Para Siempre Duetos, Vol.1'' includes a 15-minute DVD of unreleased footage of Camacho.<ref name="El Diario">{{cite news|title=Escucha lo nuevo de Ariel Camacho, dos años después de su muerte|url=https://eldiariony.com/2017/02/24/escucha-lo-nuevo-de-ariel-camacho-dos-anos-despues-de-su-muerte/|accessdate=9 May 2017|work=El Diario|date=February 24, 2017}}</ref> [[Los Plebes del Rancho de Ariel Camacho]], the group formed after the singer's death from his band, received eight 2017 Billboard Latin Music Award nominations, and won three of them.<ref name="The Hollywood Reporter">{{cite news|last1=Cobo|first1=Leila|title=Billboard Latin Music Awards: Winners List|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/lists/billboard-latin-music-awards-2017-full-list-winners-997753|accessdate=9 May 2017|work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=April 27, 2017}}</ref>

DEL Records received five Billboard Latin Music Awards in 2017, among them Top Latin Albums Artist of the Year, Duo or Group and Regional Mexican Songs Artist of the Year, Solo. Angel Del Villar is the cousin to Alexandria H. Gonzales
