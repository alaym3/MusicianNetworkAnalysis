'''Svetha Yallapragada Rao''' ([[Telugu language|Telugu]]: శ్వేత యల్లాప్రగడ రావు) (born January 11, 1986), professionally known as '''Raja Kumari''', is an [[Telugu Americans|Indian-American]] rapper, songwriter and singer from [[Claremont, California]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/while-you-were-at-coachel_b_9872266 |author=Vivianne Lapointe  |title=While You Were at Coachella: I Escaped to Bali With the People Who Write the Hits |date=May 11, 2016  |work=[[HuffPost]] }}</ref><ref name="rk-mute1"/> Kumari is best known for her collaboration with notable artists including [[Gwen Stefani]], [[Iggy Azalea]], [[Fifth Harmony]],<ref name="rk-na">{{cite web|url=http://time.com/4257624/gwen-stefani-interview-this-is-what-the-truth-feels-like-blake-shelton/ |title=Gwen Stefani: How Making My New Album Saved My Life |author=Nolan Feeney |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |date= March 18, 2016}}</ref> [[Knife Party]], [[Fall Out Boy]].<ref name="rk-fob">{{cite web|url=http://top40.about.com/od/2014Reviews/fl/Fall-Out-Boy-Centuries.htm |title=Fall Out Boy – "Centuries" |author=Bill Lamb |work=[[About.com]] }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/raja-kumari-mn0003162934|title=Raja Kumari|publisher=AllMusic}}</ref> She is also notable for receiving as a songwriter, the [[BMI Award|BMI Pop Awards]] in 2016,<ref name="rk-bmi1"/> and featuring on [[BBC Asian Network]] programme ''[[Bobby Friction]]'' on July 5, 2016.<ref name="BBC-Kumari">{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p040k0sp  |title=Raja Kumari – Mute!  |work=[[BBC Asian Network]] – [[Bobby Friction]] |date=July 5, 2016}}</ref>

==Life ==
Raja Kumari is born as Svetha Yallapragada Rao to [[Telugu language|Telugu]] parents in [[Claremont, California|Claremont]], [[California]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Sengupta|first=Meenakshi|title=All hail the Raja Kumari!|url=https://telanganatoday.com/all-hail-the-raja-kumari|access-date=2020-10-15|website=Telangana Today|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Adivi|first=Sashidhar|date=2020-06-11|title=‘The Indian Princess’ of rap|url=https://www.deccanchronicle.com/entertainment/music/110620/the-indian-princess-of-rap.html|access-date=2020-10-15|website=Deccan Chronicle|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-08-07|title=My culture is in my art: Rapper Raja Kumari|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/art-and-culture/my-culture-is-in-my-art-rapper-raja-kumari/story-9aMy2Ipv0risYzXLSFGJFL.html|access-date=2020-10-15|website=Hindustan Times|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Indian-American rapper Raja Kumari to WION: One day I will bring a Grammy home|url=https://www.wionews.com/entertainment/indian-american-rapper-raja-kumari-to-wion-one-day-i-will-bring-a-grammy-home-189854|access-date=2020-10-15|website=WION|language=en}}</ref> Kumari holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in [[religious studies]], with an emphasis on South Asian religions.

Her parents played an important role in developing her Indian character and trained her to become an [[Indian classical dance]]r who started performing at the age of 5.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-01-27|title="My parents poured all their Indianness in me!" Raja Kumari talks about what makes her the bridge between the East and the West|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/brunch/my-parents-poured-all-their-indianness-in-me-raja-kumari-makes-a-case-for-being-the-only-woman-rapper-from-mumbai-s-underbelly/story-B3UQ75rtXcCCdq0JGuvb7M.html|access-date=2021-05-06|website=Hindustan Times|language=en}}</ref> In an interview with ''Rolling Stones'',  Raja Kumari said that she found it tough and lonely as a South Indian girl growing up in Los Angeles.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-13|title=COVER STORY: How Raja Kumari Embraced Culture and Controversy -|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/cover-story-how-raja-kumari-embraced-culture-and-controversy/|access-date=2021-05-06|website=My Site|language=en-US}}</ref>

While in [[fifth grade]], Kumari discovered [[hip hop]] through the [[Fugees]]' album ''[[The Score (Fugees album)|The Score]]''. By the age of 14, she was a noticed [[Freestyle music|freestyle]] [[Master of ceremonies|MC]] known as "Indian Princess" (IP) or "Raja Kumari". She is a trained dancer in [[Kuchipudi]], [[Kathak]], and [[Bharatanatyam]] and had given many live performances in [[Ravindra Bharathi]] in [[Hyderabad]].<ref name=":0" />

During her artist career, she has contributed to several philanthropic activities through her performances including the creation of a hospital in [[Bangalore]] and a meditation hall in [[South India]] for the Vegesna Foundation, a school for children with physical [[Disability|disabilities]]. As a result, Kumari has been recognized as a benefactor by the Foundation for [[Indo-Aryan languages|Indic]] Philosophy and Culture (Indic Foundation) and has been honored with the Kohinoor Award for excellence in the Classical Arts by the Governor of [[Tamil Nadu]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://urbanasian.com/featured/2011/05/multi-talented-starlet-raja-kumaris-got-a-heart |title=Multi-talented starlet Raja Kumari's got a heart |work=Urban Asia |author=Nila Choudhury |date=May 26, 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
Kumari<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.freepressjournal.in/entertainment/raja-kumari-on-cross-cultural-music-collaborations-like-to-call-it-a-brown-renaissance|title=Raja Kumari on cross-cultural music collaborations: Like to call it a brown renaissance}}</ref> co-wrote, featured in or performed background vocals in a number of notable songs including Fall Out Boy's double-platinum single "[[Centuries (song)|Centuries]]",  "[[Change Your Life (Iggy Azalea song)|Change Your Life]]" feat. [[T.I.]], [[Vicetone]]'s  "Don't You Run",<ref name="rk-dontyourun">{{cite web|url=http://thebanginbeats.com/2016/03/23/preview-vicetone-ft-raja-kumari-dont-you-run-original-mix/ |title=Preview: Vicetone ft. Raja Kumari – Don’t You Run (Original Mix) |work=Bangin Beats }}</ref> ''[[Brave Enough]]'' by [[Lindsey Stirling]] and "Set Me Free" for [[Baz Luhrmann]]'s original Netflix series ''[[The Get Down]]'' about the birth of Hip Hop. She has also contributed to songs in albums by Gwen Stefani, Fifth Harmony (''[[Reflection (Fifth Harmony album)|Reflection]]''), Twin Shadow, and Dirty South. Recently she released her debut solo single "Mute"  featuring Elvis Brown and produced by Jules Wolfson at [[Epic Records]].<ref name="rk-mute1">{{cite web |url=http://www.papermag.com/raja-kumari-1896696090.html |title=Rapper Raja Kumari Blends Indian Classical Music With Trap Beats on "Mute"|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper Magazine]] |author= Nadya Agrawal |date=July 1, 2016}}</ref><ref name="BBC-Kumari"/><ref name="rk-mute2">{{cite web|url=http://livefastmag.com/2016/07/raja-kumari-mute-ft-elvis-brown-video/ |title=Raja Kumari – MUTE Ft. Elvis Brown  |work=LiveFast magazine }}</ref> which premiered on Paper Magazine's website. In May 2016, Kumari was honored at the 2016 [[BMI Award|BMI Pop Awards]] for co-writing the song "[[Centuries (song)|Centuries]]" with Fall Out Boy.<ref name="rk-bmi1">{{cite web|url=http://www.bmi.com/news/entry/2016_bmi_pop_awards |title=BMI Honors Taylor Swift and Legendary Songwriting Duo Mann & Weil at the 64th Annual BMI Pop Awards |work=BMI |date=May 2011}}</ref><ref name="rk-bmi2">{{cite web |url=https://theknockturnal.com/meet-l-a-reids-latest-protege-raja-kumari-video/ |title=Meet L.A. Reid's Latest Protege Raja Kumari |work=The Knockturnal}}</ref> In addition, she has a recording contract with [[Epic Records]] through [[L.A. Reid]] and collaborated with several players in the music industry, including artists [[Polow Da Don]], [[J.R. Rotem]], [[Timbaland]], [[Rodney Jerkins]], [[Soulshock and Karlin]], Tricky Stewart and The-Dream, [[Justin Tranter]] and [[Fernando Garibay]].{{citation needed|date=February 2020}} She has also been inspired in her music style by the Indian composer and producer [[A. R. Rahman]].<ref name="rk-mute1"/> In 2018, she co-wrote and performed the song titled '''Roots''<nowiki/>' and ''<nowiki/>'City Slums'<nowiki/>'' in collaboration with [[Divine (rapper)|Divine]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thewire.in/caste/art-music-rap-caste-divine-raja-kumari|title=For How Much Longer Can We Ignore Caste and Still Call Ourselves Progressive?|website=The Wire|access-date=August 13, 2018}}</ref> She has starred in ''[[Gully Boy]]'' as a judge, which was a cameo appearance.

She also hosted the American Music Awards Pre-Show telecast, alongside Lauren Jaregui.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.freepressjournal.in/entertainment/hollywood/raja-kumari-becomes-first-indian-to-host-american-music-awards-2019|title=Raja Kumari becomes first Indian to host American Music Awards 2019}}</ref> She has been nominated for an MTV European Music Awards three times for Best India Act.

Kumari has also been announced as the face of MAC Cosmetics' holiday campaign: MAC STARRING YOU, in which she appears in adverts and marketing materials around the globe.

She also was a judge on season one of MTV HUSTLE, a rap battle competition show that was the highest rated premiere in MTV History.
