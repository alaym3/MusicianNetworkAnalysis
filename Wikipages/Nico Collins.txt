'''Nico Collins''' (born March 19, 1999) is an [[American football]] [[wide receiver]] for the [[Houston Texans]] of the [[National Football League]] (NFL). He played [[college football]] at [[Michigan Wolverines football|Michigan]].

==Early years==
Collins was a wide receiver at [[Clay-Chalkville High School]] in [[Pinson, Alabama]]. As a junior, he caught 60 passes for 1,103 yards for 16 touchdowns. This was followed by a senior year with 43 passes for 865 yards and 9 touchdowns.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mgoblue.com/sports/football/roster/nico-collins/19005|title=Nico Collins - Football|website=University of Michigan Athletics|language=en|access-date=2020-01-04}}</ref> In February 2017 Collins committed to play football at Michigan over [[Georgia Bulldogs football|Georgia]] and [[Alabama Crimson Tide football|Alabama]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sbnation.com/college-football-recruiting/2017/2/1/14391150/nico-collins-commitment-national-signing-day-2017-michigan-alabama-georgia|title=4-star WR Collins picks Michigan over Bama, Georgia|last=Kirshner|first=Alex|date=2017-02-01|website=SBNation.com|language=en|access-date=2020-01-04}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.al.com/alabamafootball/2019/12/what-drew-star-recruit-from-birmingham-to-michigan-instead-of-alabama.html|title=Why star Birmingham WR chose Michigan over Alabama|last1=Casagr|first1=Michael|last2=Mcasagr|first2=E. {{!}}|date=2019-12-30|website=al|language=en|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref>

==College career==
On October 12, 2017, Collins made his first collegiate reception on a 12 yard pass against [[Rutgers Scarlet Knights football|Rutgers]]. As a freshman, he appeared in 2 games and had three receptions for 27 yards. He had a breakout season in his sophomore year in 2018. He had a 51-yard catch against [[Maryland Terrapins football|Maryland]], a 47-yard catch against [[Penn State Nittany Lions football|Penn State]] and four catches for a career high 91-yard and two touchdowns against [[Ohio State Buckeyes football|Ohio State]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlive.com/wolverines/2019/02/michigan-wr-nico-collins-can-capitalize-on-breakout-sophomore-season.html|title=Michigan WR Nico Collins can capitalize on breakout sophomore season|date=2019-02-18|website=mlive|language=en|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> He ended the season as the most improved offensive player of the team with 38 receptions for 632 yards and 6 touchdowns. On November 23, 2019, Collins had a career 146 receiving yards and 3 touchdowns against Indiana. Despite playing along [[Donovan Peoples-Jones]], [[Tarik Black (American football)|Tarik Black]] and [[Ronnie Bell (American football)|Ronnie Bell]], Collins was second on the team in receiving yards in 2019, with 729 yards from 37 receptions and had 7 touchdowns.

==Professional career==
Collins was drafted 89th overall by the [[Houston Texans]] in the third round of the [[2021 NFL Draft]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://mgoblue.com/news/2021/4/30/football-michigan-2021-nfl-draft-day-two.aspx |title=Mayfield, Collins, Thomas Selected in Third Round of NFL Draft |website=MGoBlue.com |first1=Dave |last1=Ablauf |first2=Chad |last2=Shepard |date=April 30, 2021 |access-date=April 30, 2021}}</ref> On May 12, 2021, Collins officially signed with the Texans.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.profootballrumors.com/2021/05/nfl-draft-pick-signings-51221#ref=home|title = NFL Draft Pick Signings: 5/12/21}}</ref> He was placed on injured reserve on September 21, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title= Houston Texans Transactions (9-21-2021)|url=https://www.houstontexans.com/news/houston-texans-transactions-9-21-2021|website=HoustonTexans.com|date=September 21, 2021}}</ref> He was activated on October 16.<ref>{{cite web|title=Houston Texans Transactions (10-16-2021)|url=https://www.houstontexans.com/news/houston-texans-transactions-10-16-2021|website=HoustonTexans.com|date=October 16, 2021}}</ref>
