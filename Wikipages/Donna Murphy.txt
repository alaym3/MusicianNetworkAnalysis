'''Donna Murphy''' (born March 7, 1959) is an American [[actor|actress]] and [[singing|singer]], known for her work in [[musical theater]]. A five-time [[Tony Award]] nominee, she has twice won the Tony for [[Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actress in a Musical|Best Actress in a Musical]]: for her role as Fosca in ''[[Passion (musical)|Passion]]'' (1994–95) and as [[Anna Leonowens]]'' in [[The King and I]]'' (1996–97). She was also nominated for her roles as Ruth Sherwood in ''[[Wonderful Town]]'' (2003), [[Lotte Lenya]] in ''[[LoveMusik]]'' (2007) and Bubbie/Raisel in ''[[The People in the Picture]]'' (2011).

Murphy made her [[Broadway (theatre)|Broadway]] debut as a replacement in the 1979 musical ''[[They're Playing Our Song]]''. Her other stage credits include the original [[off-Broadway]] productions of ''Song of Singapore'' (1991) and ''[[Hello Again (musical)|Hello Again]]'' (1993), as well as the alternate to [[Bette Midler]] as the title character in a Broadway revival of ''[[Hello, Dolly! (musical)|Hello, Dolly!]]'' (2017–18). In 1997, she won a [[Daytime Emmy Award]] for Outstanding Performer in a Children's Special for her role in ''Someone Had to be Benny'', an episode of the [[HBO]] series ''[[Lifestories: Families in Crisis]]''. Her film roles include Anij in ''[[Star Trek: Insurrection]]'' (1998), Rosalie Octavius in ''[[Spider-Man 2]]'' (2004), [[Mother Gothel]] in the animated film ''[[Tangled]]'' (2010), and one of the government secretaries in ''[[The Bourne Legacy (film)|The Bourne Legacy]]'' (2012).

==Early life and education==
Murphy, the eldest of seven children, was born in [[Corona, Queens]], [[New York (state)|New York]], the daughter of Jeanne ([[married and maiden names|née]] Fink) and Robert Murphy, an aerospace engineer.<ref>[http://www.rottentomatoes.com/celebrity/donna_murphy/ Donna Murphy] RottenTomatoes.com</ref><ref name=Pogrebin2003>{{cite news |first=Robin |last=Pogrebin |author-link=Robin Pogrebin |title=A Perfectionist Is at Home in a Role Funny and Loose |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2003/12/15/theater/a-perfectionist-is-at-home-in-a-role-funny-and-loose.html |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=December 15, 2003 |access-date=2009-04-08 }}</ref> Murphy is of Irish, French, German, and Czech ancestry.<ref name="murphy091">{{cite news|title=Twentyquestions: Donna Murphy currently leads a screwball conga-line of Brazilian sailors in the Broadway revival of ''Wonderful Town''|publisher=American Theatre|date=2004-04-01|url=https://www.questia.com/read/1G1-115633060|access-date=2009-08-05}}</ref><ref name="ref092">{{cite news|last=Kilian|first=Michael|title=Making the Break|publisher=Chicago Tribune|date=1998-12-17|url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/chicagotribune/access/37240174.xml?dids=37240174:37240174&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Dec+17%2C+1998&author=Michael+Kilian%2C+Tribune+Staff+Writer.&pub=Chicago+Tribune&desc=MAKING+THE+BREAK+DONNA+MURPHY+BEAMS+HERSELF+TO+HOLLYWOOD+BY+WAY+OF+%60STAR+TREK'&pqatl=google|access-date=2009-08-05}}</ref> Her family moved to [[Hauppauge, New York|Hauppauge]], [[Long Island]], New York. At age three, she asked for [[vocal pedagogy|voice lessons]], and she put on shows as a child in Hauppauge.<ref name=Pogrebin2003/> She later moved to [[Topsfield, Massachusetts]] and graduated from [[Masconomet Regional High School]] in 1977.

==Career==
Murphy dropped out of the [[New York University]] drama program in her sophomore year when she was cast to understudy the three backup singers in the 1979 Broadway musical ''[[They're Playing Our Song]]''. In a 2007 interview, Murphy explained, "At the end of my sophomore year, I took a leave of absence. I needed to audition without cutting classes."<ref name=Pogrebin2003/><ref>Nassour, Ellis. [http://www.broadwaystars.com/ellis/2007/05/donna_murphy_singing_in_a.shtml "Donna Murphy: Singing in a Different Key in ''LoveMusik''"], broadwaystars.com, May 7, 2007</ref><ref>IMDB shows that she earned a bachelor of fine arts in 1980.</ref> She also studied at the [[Lee Strasberg Theatre Institute]].<ref>
{{cite web |url=https://movies.nytimes.com/person/531052/Donna-Murphy/biography |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090630094344/http://movies.nytimes.com/person/531052/Donna-Murphy/biography |url-status=dead |archive-date=2009-06-30 |department=Movies & TV Dept. |work=[[The New York Times]] |publisher=[[Baseline (database)|Baseline]] & [[All Movie Guide]] |date=2009 |title=Donna Murphy|access-date=2009-04-08 }}</ref>

She has appeared in many off-Broadway productions, including the musical ''Francis'' in 1981 at the [[York Theatre]] at St. Peter's, ''[[The Mystery of Edwin Drood (musical)|The Mystery of Edwin Drood]]'' in 1985 at the [[Public Theater]]'s [[Delacorte Theatre]], ''[[Birds of Paradise (musical)|Birds of Paradise]]'' in 1987 (Promenade Theatre), ''[[Privates on Parade]]'' ([[Roundabout Theatre]]) in 1989, the musical ''Song of Singapore'' in 1991, the [[Michael John LaChiusa]] musical ''[[Hello Again (musical)|Hello Again]]'' at the [[Lincoln Center]] [[Mitzi Newhouse Theater]] in 1993, ''Twelve Dreams'' at the Mitzi Newhouse Theater in 1995, and ''Helen'' at the Public Theater/New York Shakespeare Festival in 2002. In 2012, she appeared in [[Stephen Sondheim]]'s ''[[Into the Woods]]'' at The Public Theater's Delacorte Theatre as the Witch.<ref>Hetrick, Adam. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/168336-Once-Upon-a-Time-Later-Into-the-Woods-Casts-Spell-in-Central-Park-Starting-July-24 "Once Upon a Time, Later": 'Into the Woods' Casts Spell in Central Park Starting July 24"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120728050930/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/168336-Once-Upon-a-Time-Later-Into-the-Woods-Casts-Spell-in-Central-Park-Starting-July-24 |date=July 28, 2012 }}. Playbill.com, July 24, 2012</ref>
  
On [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]], after ''They're Playing Our Song'' (1979), she was an understudy in the musical/opera ''[[The Human Comedy (musical)|The Human Comedy]]'' in April 1984 and played various roles in ''[[The Mystery of Edwin Drood]]'' from 1985 to 1987. She also played Audrey in [[Howard Ashman]] and [[Alan Menken]]'s ''[[Little Shop of Horrors (musical)|Little Shop of Horrors]].'' In 1994, she played the role of Fosca in [[Stephen Sondheim]]'s and [[James Lapine]]'s ''[[Passion (musical)|Passion]]'', winning the Tony Award for [[Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actress in a Musical|Best Actress in a Musical]] for her performance. A year later she appeared in Lapine's revival, ''[[Twelve Dreams]]''. In 1996, she played [[Anna Leonowens]] in the revival of ''[[The King and I]]'' alongside [[Lou Diamond Phillips]], also recording a cast album. The role earned her a second Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical. She appeared as Ruth Sherwood in a revival of ''[[Wonderful Town]]'' from 2003 to 2005 (having previously performed in the [[New York City Center]] [[Encores!]] 2000 staged concert of that musical), and was nominated for the Tony Award, Best Actress in a Musical and won the [[Drama Desk Award]] for Outstanding Actress in a Musical. In 2007, she appeared in ''[[LoveMusik]]'' as [[Lotte Lenya]], opposite [[Michael Cerveris]] as [[Kurt Weill]], receiving nominations for Tony and Drama Desk Awards. She appeared in the 2007 New York City Center [[Encores!]] staged concert of ''[[Follies]]'' as Phyllis.<ref>Brantley, Ben. [http://theater2.nytimes.com/2007/02/10/theater/reviews/10foll.html?fta=y&pagewanted=all "Oh, Those Sharp Stones in a Dance Down Memory Lane"], ''The New York Times'', February 10, 2007</ref> She appeared in the [[Roundabout Theatre]] production of a new musical, ''[[The People in the Picture]]'', which opened on April 28, 2011 and closed on June 19, 2011.<ref>BWW News Desk.  [http://broadwayworld.com/article/Donna_Murphy_to_Return_to_Broadway_in_THE_PEOPLE_IN_THE_PICTURE_20101031 "Donna Murphy to Return to Broadway in 'The People In The Picture'"] broadwayworld.com, October 31, 2010</ref> She was nominated for a 2011 Tony Award for Leading Actress in a Musical for her role in the production.<ref>[http://broadwayworld.com/article/2011-Tony-Nominations-Announced-THE-BOOK-OF-MORMON-Leads-With-14-20110503 "Tony Nominations Announced"] broadwayworld.com, May 3, 2011</ref>

She appeared in the Broadway revival of ''[[Hello, Dolly! (musical)|Hello, Dolly!]]'' as Dolly Levi, the alternate to Bette Midler on Tuesday evenings and other select performances. She played her last performance on January 7, 2018.<ref>McPhee, Ryan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/donna-murphy-sets-last-night-in-hello-dolly-bernadette-peters-to-play-8-performances-a-week# "Donna Murphy Sets Last Night in 'Hello, Dolly!'; Bernadette Peters to Play 8 Performances a Week"] Playbill, September 7, 2017</ref><ref>McPhee, Ryan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/bette-midler-david-hyde-pierce-taylor-trensch-beanie-feldstein-bid-farewell-to-hello-dolly-january-14# "Bette Midler, David Hyde Pierce, Taylor Trensch, Beanie Feldstein Bid Farewell to 'Hello, Dolly!' January 14"] Playbill, January 14, 2018</ref> Murphy returned to ''Hello, Dolly!'' for six performances (July 22 and 29, August 5, 12, 19, and 20) when Midler rejoined the musical before it closed on August 25, 2018.<ref>McPhee, Ryan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/donna-murphy-to-return-to-broadways-hello-dolly# "Donna Murphy to Return to Broadway's 'Hello, Dolly!'"] Playbill, May 29, 2018</ref>

Murphy's film roles include Anij, Captain [[Jean-Luc Picard]]'s love interest, in ''[[Star Trek: Insurrection]]'' (1998), in the film ''[[Center Stage (2000 film)|Center Stage]]'', as a ballet teacher (2000), as Rosalie Octavius, wife of [[Doctor Octopus|Dr. Otto Octavius]], the film's villain in ''[[Spider-Man 2]]'' (2004), as Betty, a surgical research assistant in [[Darren Aronofsky]]'s film ''[[The Fountain]]'' (2006), and [[Scarlett Johansson]]'s mother in ''[[The Nanny Diaries (film)|The Nanny Diaries]]'' (2007). As [[Mother Gothel]] in the animated [[musical film]] ''[[Tangled]]'' (2010) she sang the song "[[Mother Knows Best (song)|Mother Knows Best]]".<ref>
[http://broadwayworld.com/article/BWW_EXCLUSIVE_Alan_Menken_Talks_TANGLED_SISTER_ACT_LEAP_HUNCHBACK_ALADDIN_More_20101115_page2 "Alan Menken Interview"] broadwayworld.com, November 15, 2010</ref> She played Kathleen, [[Vera Farmiga]]'s mother in ''[[Higher Ground (film)|Higher Ground]]'' (2011) and Marie in ''[[Dark Horse (2011 film)|Dark Horse]]'' (2011). In 2012, she appeared as government secretary Dita Mandy, in ''[[The Bourne Legacy (film)|The Bourne Legacy]]''.

On television, Murphy appeared in the [[NBC]] [[soap opera]] ''[[Another World (TV series)|Another World]]'' from 1989 through 1991 as District Attorney Morgan Graves. She won an [[Emmy]] in 1997, for playing Armando Agrelo in "Someone Had to be Benny" (1996), an episode of the [[HBO]] series ''[[Lifestories: Families in Crisis]]''. Other [[television program|television series]] roles include a recurring role as [[Abigail Adams]] in ''[[Liberty!|Liberty! The American Revolution]]'' (1997), ''[[Murder One (TV series)|Murder One]]'' (1995–1996), ''[[Law & Order (TV series)|Law & Order]]'' as Carla Tyrell in a recurring role (2000), ''[[Hack (TV series)|Hack]]'' (2002–2003), ''[[Trust Me (U.S. TV series)|Trust Me]]'' (2009) and ''[[Quantico (TV series)|Quantico]]'' (2017). Her voice-over work in TV commercials, includes the Le Vian chocolate diamonds series for Jared Jewelry.

==Personal life==
Murphy was married to actor and singer [[Shawn Elliott (actor)|Shawn Elliott]] from 1990 until his death in March 2016.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/article/actor-shawn-elliott-husband-of-donna-murphy-dies# "Actor Shawn Elliott, Husband of Donna Murphy, Dies at 79"] Playbill, March 18, 2016</ref> She is the stepmother of Elliott's two daughters. In 2005, they adopted a daughter from [[Guatemala]], Darmia Hope.<ref>Robertson, Campbell.[https://www.nytimes.com/2007/04/30/theater/30murp.html?_r=1 "No Ill Feelings: Producers Bet on a 'Superwoman'", discussing her adopted daughter]''The New York Times'', April 30, 2007</ref>
