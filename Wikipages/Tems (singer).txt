'''Temilade Openiyi''' (born 11 June 1995), better known by her stage name '''Tems''',<ref>{{Cite web|title=The sweet, arresting harmonies of Nigeria's Tems|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/03/09/tems-mr-rebel-try-me-interview|access-date=4 August 2021|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref> is a Nigerian singer. In 2021, she earned two US top 40 entries on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], with her collaboration with [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] on the song "Fountains" from his album ''[[Certified Lover Boy]]'', and the [[Wizkid]] single "[[Essence (song)|Essence]]"; the latter of which reached the top ten following a remix by [[Justin Bieber]], and earned her a [[Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] nomination.

In the same year, Tems was ranked number one on the Billboard [[Next Big Sound]] chart<ref>{{Cite web|date=19 May 2021|title=Tems Claims Number One Spot on Billboard Next Big Sound Chart|url=https://www.arise.tv/tems-claims-number-one-spot-on-billboard-next-big-sound-chart/|access-date=2 October 2021|website=Arise News|language=en-US}}</ref> and number-five on the Billboard [[Emerging Artists|Emerging Artist]] chart.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Emerging Artists Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/emerging-artists|access-date=2 October 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> In September 2021, Tems was signed to [[RCA Records|RCA records]], and released her second [[Extended play|EP]] ''[[If Orange Was a Place]]'', which reached the top ten on the [[Billboard World Albums|''Billboard'' World Albums]] chart.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Tems Signs to Since '93/RCA, Named Apple Music 'Up Next' Artist|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9630254/tems-signs-since-93-rca-records-apple-music-up-next-artist/|access-date=2 October 2021|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Temilade Openiyi was born on 11 June 1995 in [[Lagos]], [[Nigeria]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=Olatunji|first=Mercy|date=18 February 2021|title=Full biography of Nigerian singer Tems and other facts about her|url=https://dnbstories.com/2021/02/full-biography-of-nigerian-singer-tems.html|url-status=live|access-date=4 August 2021|website=DNB Stories Africa|language=en-US}}</ref> Born to a Nigerian mother and a British-Nigerian father, Tems' family moved to the United Kingdom shortly after birth. Her parents divorced when she was five. Tems relocated to Nigeria with her mother, they resided in ilupeju before moving to [[Lekki]] then [[Ajah, Lagos|Ajah]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=The sweet, arresting harmonies of Nigeria's Tems|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/03/09/tems-mr-rebel-try-me-interview|access-date=15 June 2020|website=[[The Fader]]}}</ref> Tems attended [[Dowen College]] before proceeding to South Africa for her Tertiary education.<ref name=":3" /> At school, she was noticed by her music teacher and she learned to play the piano. She would practice singing with her brother's guitar accompaniment.<ref name=reb>{{Cite web|title=Native Exclusive: How Tems Triumphs after "Mr Rebel"|url=https://thenativemag.com/profile/native-exclusivetems/|date=23 October 2018|website=[[The Native]]|access-date=5 May 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2018–2019: Career beginnings, ''Mr Rebel'' and ''Try Me'' ===
In 2018 Tems quit her job to pursue a career in music, she recorded "Mr Rebel", a song of her own composition.<ref name="reb" /> In August 2019, she released the single "Try Me" which became a hit track.<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 February 2020|title=Interview: Tems Is On a Mission to Take Over|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/tems-try-me-nigerian-music-interview/|access-date=25 December 2020|website=[[OkayAfrica]]}}</ref> In 2020, DJ Edu chose her as one of "ten artists to watch" that year.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rema|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/african-music-artists-to-watch-2020-dj-edu-bbc-radio/?rebelltitem=1#rebelltitem1?rebelltitem=1|date=6 February 2020|website=[[OkayAfrica]]|access-date=4 May 2020}}</ref> She was recruited that year by American singer [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] to join him and fellow Nigerian [[Davido]] on an [[Afrobeats]] remix of "[[Know Your Worth]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Khalid Recruits Davido and Tems on New Single 'Know Your Worth (Remix)'|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/listen-to-khalid-davido-tems-new-music/|date=23 April 2020|website=[[OkayAfrica]]|access-date=4 May 2020}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''For Broken Ears'', ''Essence'' and ''If Orange Was a Place'' ===
On 25 September 2020, Tems released her debut extended play ''[[For Broken Ears]]'', it was produced primarily by Tems and her single "[[Damages (song)|Damages]]" from the EP became another follow up hit to "Try Me", peaking at number one on the Apple Music Nigeria and number six on the then-newly launched [[TurnTable Top 50 chart|TurnTable Top 50]] chart and garnering five million views on [[YouTube]]. In 2020, her collaboration with [[Wizkid]] on their song "[[Essence (song)|Essence]]" earned her a number one spot on [[BBC 1Xtra]] Airplay Chart.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BBC 1Xtra Airplay Chart|url=http://ukairplaychart.com/1239/BBC_1Xtra/|access-date=30 January 2021|website=ukairplaychart.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The Essence of Wizkid and Tems|url=https://mp3chord.com/entertainment/the-essence-of-wizkid-and-tems/|access-date=10 April 2021|website=Mp3chord.com}}</ref> and also earned her first career entry on [[Billboard Hot 100]]<ref>{{cite magazine | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9601362/wizkid-essence-enters-hot-100|title=Wizkid's Essence Enters Hot 100 Shoutout to Good Music Lovers Everywhere|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]| date=15 July 2021|access-date=4 August 2021}}</ref>

On 8 November 2020, she was included in [[The Future Awards Africa]]: Class of 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Future Awards Africa: Class of 2020 |url=https://awards.yafri.ca/the-future-awards-africa-class-of-2020/ |website=The Future Awards Africa |access-date=16 June 2021 |date=8 November 2020}}</ref>

In December 2020, Tems and fellow Nigerian artist [[Omah Lay]] were arrested in Uganda after a live performance at a show for violating [[COVID-19]] lockdown safety laws by the Ugandan Police authority. Two days later, they were released and the charges were dropped.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Bebe Cool make police arrest Tems and Omah Lay for Uganda?|work=BBC News Pidgin|url=https://www.bbc.com/pidgin/tori-55359767|access-date=27 February 2021}}</ref>

On 2 September 2021, it was announced on billboards in Lagos that Tems was set to feature on rapper [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s ''[[Certified Lover Boy]]'' album. her collaboration with Drake on the song "Fountains" earned her her second [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] entry debuting at number twenty six.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Espinoza|first=Joshua|date=2 September 2021|title=Drake Reveals 'Certified Lover Boy' Album Features on Billboards|url=https://www.complex.com/music/drake-teases-certified-lover-boy-album-features-on-billboards/|url-status=live|access-date=2 September 2021|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2 September 2021|title=Tems to feature on Drake's CLB album|url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/tems-to-feature-on-drakes-clb-album/5zq68ry|access-date=16 September 2021|website=Pulse Nigeria|language=en}}</ref>

On 15 September 2021, Tems released her second extended play titled ''[[If Orange Was a Place]]'', primarily produced by [[GuiltyBeatz|GuiltyBeatz]] and supported by the single "Crazy Tings" which peaked at number three on the ''[[UK Afrobeats Singles Chart|UK Afrobeats]]'' [[UK Afrobeats Singles Chart|chart]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tems shares new EP If Orange Was A Place|url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/09/16/tems-new-ep-if-orange-was-a-place|access-date=5 October 2021|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|date=27 September 2021|title=Tems' 'Found' with Brent Faiyaz Hits New Peak on the UK Afrobeats Chart|url=https://notjustok.com/news/tems-found-with-brent-faiyaz-hits-new-peak-on-the-uk-afrobeats-chart/|access-date=5 October 2021|website=NotjustOk|language=en-US}}</ref> it debuted at number seven on the ''[[Billboard World Albums]]'' chart<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=World Albums Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/world-albums/2021-10-02|access-date=1 October 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> and number nine on the ''[[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Album]]'' chart.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Heatseekers Albums Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/heatseekers-albums|access-date=5 October 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> her collaboration with Brent Faiyaz on the song "Found" from the EP debuted at number seventeen on the ''[[R&B Songs|Hot R&B Songs]]'' chart,<ref name=":0" /> number thirty three on the ''[[New Zealand singles chart|New Zealand hot singles chart]]''<ref name=":1" /> and number one on the ''UK Afrobeats Chart''.<ref name=":2" />
