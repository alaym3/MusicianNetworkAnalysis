'''Get Lit â€” Words Ignite''' is an American poetry organization based in [[Los Angeles]].<ref>[https://getlit.org/ Get Lit official website]</ref> Get Lit was founded by Diane Luby Lane in 2006, in order to bring [[poetry|written]] and [[slam poetry]] to inner-city youth.<ref>[https://tunein.com/podcasts/Arts--Culture-Podcasts/Change-Lab-Conversations-on-Transformation-and-Cr-p1026438/?topicId=140448392 Change Lab from ArtCenter College of Design - "Get Lit Words Ignite founder Diane Luby Lane on empowering teens through spoken word poetry"]</ref> The organization hosts a yearly competitive [[poetry slam]] called the Classic Slam.

Get Lit also employs a paid group of young poets called the Get Lit Players who travel to schools and events.<ref>[https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-2009-feb-20-me-outthere20-story.html The Los Angeles Times - "Get Lit Players bring poetry's emotions to other L.A. teenagers"]</ref> The organization has expanded to include a publishing house, releasing the book "''Get Lit Rising''", and the production company '''Literary Riot Media'''.<ref>[https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Get-Lit-Rising/Diane-Luby-Lane/9781582705767 Simon & Schuster - "Get Lit Rising"]</ref> ("''Summertime''", a [[spoken word]] poetry film which debuted at [[Sundance Film Festival|Sundance]], was born out of the work of Get Lit.)<ref>[https://ew.com/film-festivals/2020/01/17/summertime-sundance-poster-carlos-lopez-estrada/ Entertainment Weekly - "See exclusive poster for Sundance spoken-word poetry film Summertime"]</ref><ref>[https://collider.com/summertime-cast-interview-sundance/ Collider - "'Summertime' Stars on Their Unique Journey to Hollywood"]</ref> Poets from Get Lit performed at the [[United Nations]] for the [[International Day of Peace]] celebration in 2018.<ref>[http://www.solacilike.com/get-lit-united-nations Laci. - Get Lit/United Nations]</ref><ref>[https://www.nylon.com/articles/voices-of-peace-get-lit-zine-premiere Nylon - "These Young Artists Are Bringing Their Activism Straight To The UN"]</ref> They have also performed at the [[White House]], the [[Kennedy Center]], and the [[Lincoln Center]].<ref>[https://getlit.org/get-lit-players/ Get Lit - Get Lit Players]</ref>
