'''Anthony''' "'''Romeo'''" '''Santos''' (born July 21, 1981)<ref>{{cite web|last1=Birchmeier|first1=Jason|title=Romeo Santos – Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/romeo-santos-mn0002367865/biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|publisher=Rovi|access-date=January 23, 2015}}</ref> is an American singer and songwriter best known as the lead member and vocalist of the [[Bachata (music)|bachata]] group [[Aventura (band)|Aventura]]. In 2002, the song "[[Obsesión (Aventura song)|Obsesión]]" reached number one in Italy for 16 consecutive weeks. He released several albums with Aventura before the group broke up. Since then, Santos has embarked on a solo career which has spawned seven number one songs on the [[Hot Latin Songs]] chart and sixteen number ones on the [[Tropical Songs]] chart.

== Early life ==
Santos was born in 1981 in [[the Bronx]], New York City to a [[People of the Dominican Republic|Dominican]] father and a [[Puerto Rican people|Puerto Rican]] mother.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Romeo Santos Finds His Own Formula After Aventura Success|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/465564/romeo-santos-finds-his-own-formula-after-aventura-success|magazine=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|access-date=July 22, 2015|date=October 21, 2011}}</ref> He had a humble upbringing, with his father working in construction and his mother staying at home to take care of the family.

Santos began his career by singing in a church choir at a young age.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/anthony-santos-mn0002367865|title=Romeo Santos {{!}} Biography, Albums, Streaming Links|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=March 26, 2020}}</ref> Romeo was exposed to Latin genres of music at a young age due to his parents.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.liveabout.com/romeo-santos-biography-and-artist-profile-2141886|title=Get Ready to Swoon Over Romeo Santos, Bachata Superstar|last=Quintana|first=Carlos|website=LiveAbout|access-date=March 26, 2020}}</ref>

== Music career ==
=== Aventura ===
[[File:Romeo Santos.jpg|thumb|Santos in 2011]]
Romeo was the main singer, songwriter and co-producer of [[Aventura (band)|Aventura]]. Aventura was formed in 1996 by Anthony "Romeo" Santos, his cousin [[Henry Santos]] "Hustle Hard", and friends [[Lenny Santos]] "Len Melody", and [[Max Santos]] "Mikey a.k.a Max Agende".

Aventura released their debut album in 1999 with the hopes of breaking bachata music into the mainstream from its traditional base and fuse it with [[hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[contemporary R&B|R&B]]. In 2002, the song, "[[Obsesión (Aventura song)|Obsesión]]" achieved huge success in many countries, topping many international charts like the France, Germany and Italy. Aventura released five studio albums in a decade, spawning many top 10 hits like internationally famous "Obsesión", "Cuando Volverás", "Un Beso", :Amor de Madre", "Los Infieles", "El Perdedor", "Por Un Segundo", "Dile Al Amor", and many others.

In 2009, Aventura was invited to perform for the [[List of Presidents of the United States|44th President of the United States]] [[Barack Obama]] at the [[White House]].

Aventura separated in 2011. According to Romeo, the group was "on pause to do individual projects".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/music/news/story/2012-01-12/on-the-verge-romeo-santos/52400608/1|title=On the Verge: 'Bachata King' Romeo|work=[[USA Today]]|publisher=Gannett Co. Inc.|author=Mallenbaum, Carly|date=January 8, 2012|access-date=May 16, 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130121022811/http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/music/news/story/2012-01-12/on-the-verge-romeo-santos/52400608/1|archive-date=January 21, 2013}}</ref> Romeo, along Lenny, Mikey and Henry (Aventura) reunited to close out the second night of Romeo Santos' sold-out Yankee Stadium concerts on July 12, 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Feeney|first1=Michael|title=Romeo Santos joined by Marc Anthony, former Aventura members at sold-out Yankee Stadium show|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music/romeo-santos-joined-marc-anthony-aventura-members-article-1.1865408|access-date=July 18, 2014|newspaper=[[New York Daily News]]|date=July 13, 2014}}</ref> It was announced on December 1, 2015, that Aventura would have a set of concerts for the last time as a group for the whole month of February at the United Palace Theater in New York City. Their first concert since their split started with a sold-out crowd on February 4, 2016, with the final concert ending on February 28, 2016. On December 8, 2019, Romeo Santos posted a video saying that Aventura is going on tour around the United States in 2020 called 'Gira Inmortal'.

=== Solo ===

In April 2011, after his success as a songwriter and the lead singer for Aventura, Romeo announced he was leaving the band to pursue a solo career. Romeo Santos signed with Sony records on April 7, 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sony.com/en_us/SCA/company-news/press-releases/sony-music-entertainment/2011/celebrated-latin-superstar-anthony-romeo-santos-signs-to-sony-music.html|title=Celebrated Latin Superstar Anthony "Romeo" Santos Signs to Sony Music|website=www.sony.com|language=en-US|access-date=March 26, 2020}}</ref>

On May 9, he released the first single off his debut album ''[[Formula, Vol. 1]]'', called "You".<ref>{{cite web|title=Billboard Bits: Charlie Sheen Releases 'Winning' Single, Bruno Mars Responds to Tyler Dis|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/471638/billboard-bits-charlie-sheen-releases-winning-single-bruno-mars-responds-to|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|date=May 10, 2011|access-date=May 16, 2011}}</ref> The song went to number one on the [[Hot Latin Songs]] and [[Tropical Songs]] charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/column/chartbeat#/column/chartbeat/chart-highlights-pop-dance-club-play-songs-1005185612.story|title=Chart Highlights: Pop, Dance/Club Play Songs & More|website=Billboard.com|date=May 16, 2011|access-date=May 16, 2011}}</ref> The second single "Promise", featured a collaboration with R&B singer [[Usher (singer)|Usher]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musica.terra.com/generos/urbano/romeo-santos-junto-a-usher-en-promise,2f9cc31c80c12310VgnVCM20000099f154d0RCRD.html|title=Romeo Santos junto a Usher en "Promise"|work=[[Terra Networks]]|language=es|date=August 30, 2011|access-date=September 2, 2011}}</ref> As with the previous single, "Promise" topped the [[Hot Latin Songs]] and [[Tropical Songs]] charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/467382/chart-highlights-rodney-atkins-takes-back-road-to-no-1-on-country|title=Chart Highlights: Rodney Atkins Takes 'Back Road' To No. 1 On Country Songs|work=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|date=September 12, 2011|access-date=September 12, 2011}}</ref>

In 2012, he sold out New York City's famous Madison Square Garden three nights in a row. The performance was then turned into a CD and DVD package and subsequently released as a live album, ''[[The King Stays King: Sold Out at Madison Square Garden]]''. In 2014, he sold out an entire double-header show at [[Yankee Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/from-corner-delis-to-yankee-stadium-romeo-santos-historic-bronx-gig-20140711|title=From Corner Delis to Yankee Stadium: Romeo Santos Historic Bronx Gig|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=July 11, 2014|access-date=October 6, 2014}}</ref> Santos was the first Latin artist to headline at the stadium. The Fania All-Stars last did so, one block south at the [[Yankee Stadium (1923)|old Yankee Stadium]] on August 24, 1973.

His second album, ''[[Formula, Vol. 2]]'', was released on February 25, 2014. Featuring collaborations with [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Marc Anthony]], [[Carlos Santana]], [[Tego Calderón]], and [[Kevin Hart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musica.univision.com/noticias/article/2014-01-17/fornula-vol-2-de-romeo-santos-saldra-a-la-venta-el-25-de-febrero|title='Fórnula Vol. 2' de Romeo Santos saldrá a la venta el 25 de febrero|language=es|work=[[Univision]]|publisher=[[Univision Communications]]|date=January 17, 2014|access-date=January 18, 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140221183752/http://musica.univision.com/noticias/article/2014-01-17/fornula-vol-2-de-romeo-santos-saldra-a-la-venta-el-25-de-febrero|archive-date=February 21, 2014}}</ref> ''Formula, Vol. 2'' became the best-selling Latin album of 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musica.univision.com/noticias/article/2014-07-04/romeo-santos-primer-lugar-en-ventas-con-formula-vol2|title=Romeo Santos, primer lugar en ventas con 'Formula Vol.2'|language=es|work=[[Univision]]|publisher=[[Univision Communications]]|date=April 7, 2014|access-date=October 7, 2014}}</ref>

He released a new single "[[Héroe Favorito]]" in February 2017, the lead single for his upcoming album "Golden". He released his new album Golden on July 21, 2017, featuring collaborations with [[Swizz Beatz]], [[Juan Luis Guerra]], [[Ozuna (singer)|Ozuna]], [[Nicky Jam]], [[Daddy Yankee]], [[Julio Iglesias]] and [[Jessie Reyez]]. Winner of Best Bachata Video in the Videoclip Awards 2018 with the music video of the single "Imitadora" directed by Carlos Pérez, also nominated as Best Latin Album in the [[Billboard Music Awards]] 2018.

In April 2019, he released his fourth studio album "Utopia" where he united with pioneers and legends of traditional modern bachata. El Chaval De La Bachata, Frank Reyes, Raulin Rodriguez, [[Elvis Martínez (singer)|Elvis Martinez]], Kiko Rodriguez, Teodoro Reyes, Joe Veras, Zacarias Ferreira, [[Luis Vargas (musician)|Luis Vargas]], Monchy & Alexandra, and Antony "El Mayimbe" Santos all participated in the album to show that unification within bachata is possible. The band [[Aventura (band)|Aventura]], as a group, released an official song within this album also.

== Concert film and documentary ==
In June 2021, ''Romeo Santos: Utopia Live from MetLife Stadium'' was released as a PPV event, based on the September 2019 concert in New York that featured guests [[Cardi B]], [[Daddy Yankee]], [[Emilio Estefan]], [[Marc Anthony]], [[Thalía]] and more.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Mier|title=Romeo Santos to Star in Documentary and Concert Film|url=https://people.com/music/romeo-santos-to-star-in-documentary-and-concert-film/|website=People.com|access-date=May 13, 2021|date=May 14, 2021}}</ref> Concurrently, HBO Max released a documentary on Santos called ''Romeo Santos: King of Bachata'' which traced the history of Bachata, and showed Santos travelling to the Dominican Republic with [[The Kid Mero]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Gomez|title=King of Bachata will Showcase Rise of Romeo Santos|url=https://aldianews.com/articles/culture/film-television/king-bachata-will-showcase-rise-romeo-santos/64766|website=aldianews.com|access-date=May 20, 2021|date=May 20, 2021}}</ref> To promote the documentary, HBO built an elaborate pop-up promotion in [[Washington Heights, Manhattan|Washington Heights]], NYC in the form of a [[bodega (store)|bodega]], called House of Bachata, with free haircuts, Presidente beer, and karaoke parties honoring Santos. At its center was a [[PORTL]] "hologram" device in which Santos appeared in lifelike, lifesized, volumetric 4K to interact with fans.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kaplan|title=HBO Max Pa'lante's Pop-up Promoting Romeo Santos Shows Is a Love Letter With Holograms|url=https://www.adweek.com/performance-marketing/hbo-max-pop-up-romeo-santos/|website=adweek.com|access-date=July 26, 2021|date=July 26, 2021}}</ref>

== Acting career ==
His first film appearance was on the 2007 Dominican film "Sanky Panky" as himself alongside his fellow band members from Aventura performing live at Altos de Chavón in the Dominican Republic. Romeo Santos made his Hollywood debut in the film ''[[Furious 7]]'', released April 2015, alongside [[Vin Diesel]], [[Dwayne Johnson]], and [[Paul Walker]].<ref name=elcomercio>{{cite web|url=http://elcomercio.pe/luces/cine/rapidos-y-furiosos-7-adelanta-su-estreno-noticia-1740590|title="Rápidos y furiosos 7" adelanta su fecha de estreno|language=es|work=El Comercio|publisher=Empresa Editora El Comercio|date=July 4, 2014|access-date=October 6, 2014}}</ref><ref name=international>{{cite web|url=http://au.ibtimes.com/articles/558748/20140710/fast-furious-7-spoilers-update-paul-walker.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20141008165013/http://au.ibtimes.com/articles/558748/20140710/fast-furious-7-spoilers-update-paul-walker.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 8, 2014|title=Fast and Furious 7 Spoilers, Update: 7 Things Fans Need to Know About Paul Walker and Vin Diesel Starrer Movie|work=International Business Times|publisher=International Business Times AU.|date=July 10, 2014|access-date=October 6, 2014}}</ref> He was nervous about acting in ''Furious 7'' but the cast made him feel welcomed.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Latin Post Staff|title=Furious7 Cast News&Trailer:Romeo Santos|url=http://www.latinpost.com/articles/45261/20150330/fast-furious-furious-7-romeo-santos-vin-diesel-paul-walker-dwayne-johnson-michelle-rodriguez-jordana-brewster-tyrese-gibson-chris-ludacris-bridges-elsa-pataky-mma-fighter-ronda-rousey.htm|website=Latinpost.com|access-date=April 12, 2015|date=March 30, 2015}}</ref>

Santos was also the voice of the cartoon character Early Bird in the 2016 film ''[[The Angry Birds Movie]]''.

He sang "Quiero Ser Tu Amigo", a song about friendship, on an episode of ''[[Sesame Street]]'' in 2013.
