'''Carmelo Domenic Licciardello''' (January 19, 1956 – February 16, 2021), known by his stage name '''Carman''', was an American [[contemporary Christian music]] singer, rapper, dancer, songwriter, television host, [[life coach]],<ref name="LifeCoach">{{Cite news |last=Law |first=Jeannie |date=April 14, 2017 |title=Carman Licciardello Cancer Update: Singer Fulfills Dream of Becoming Life Coach Before Upcoming Tumor Surgery |work=[[The Christian Post]] |url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/carman-licciardello-cancer-update-singer-fulfills-dream-of-becoming-life-coach-before-upcoming-tumor-surgery-180406/ |access-date=September 7, 2018}}</ref> and [[Evangelism|evangelist]].

== Early life ==

An [[Italian Americans|Italian American]], Carman was born in [[Trenton, New Jersey]].<ref name="billboardbio">{{Cite book |last=Alfonso |first=Barry |title=The Billboard guide to Contemporary Christian Music |publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard Books]] |year=2002 |isbn=978-0-8230-7718-2 |location=New York |page=134}}</ref> As a child he performed in his mother's band; as a teen, he found some success performing at casinos in [[Atlantic City, New Jersey]].<ref name="CT obit">{{Cite web |last=Silliman |first=Daniel |title=Died: Carman, Christian Showman Who Believed in Celebrity and Spectacle for Jesus |url=https://www.christianitytoday.com/news/2021/february/died-carman-licciardello-christian-singer-las-vegas-tour.html |access-date=February 17, 2021 |publisher=[[Christianity Today]]}}</ref>

While attending an [[Andraé Crouch]] concert, he became a [[Born again (Christianity)|born again Christian]], and embraced evangelical Christianity.<ref name="CT obit" /><ref>{{Cite news |last=Daniels |first=Lisa |date=October 30, 1992 |title=Carman is Born (Again) to Boogie |publisher=[[Daily Press (California)|Daily Press]]}}</ref>

== Career ==

In 1980, he made a custom album titled ''God's Not Finished with Me''. The following year, he was invited by [[Bill Gaither (gospel singer)|Bill Gaither]] to tour with [[The Bill Gaither Trio]].<ref name="CCM Encyclopedia">{{Cite book |last=Powell |first=Mark Allan |url=https://archive.org/details/encyclopediaofco00mark/page/141 |title=Encyclopedia of Contemporary Christian Music |publisher=Hendrickson Publishers |year=2002 |isbn=1-56563-679-1 |pages=[https://archive.org/details/encyclopediaofco00mark/page/141 141–43] |url-access=registration}}</ref>

After relocating to [[Tulsa, Oklahoma]], he released a moderately successful eponymous debut album (later issued as ''Some-o-Dat'')<ref name="billboardbio" /> in 1982<ref name="allmusic">{{Cite web |last=Ankeny |first=Jason |title=Carman&nbsp;– Biography |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p2229|pure_url=yes}} |access-date=December 9, 2009 |publisher=[[Allmusic]] ([[Macrovision Corporation]])}}</ref>—which contained mostly novelty songs. Then, with the release of the album ''Sunday's on the Way'' in 1983, a string of contemporary Christian music chart successes started, beginning with the title song. As he continued his music career, he established the nonprofit organization Carman Ministries.<ref name="billboardbio" /> With the 1985 release of ''The Champion'' came his first number-one song, of the same name. His first number one album on Christian charts, ''Revival in the Land'', followed in 1989.<ref name="CT obit" />

Between 1987 and 1989, he was named'' Charisma'' magazine's readers' choice for favorite male vocalist.<ref name="allmusic" /> In 1990 and 1992, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' named him the Contemporary Christian Artist of the Year.<ref name="CT obit" /> In 1995, he translated some of his songs into and released his first Spanish-language album, ''Lo Mejor''. ''Heart of a Champion'', a 30-song retrospective was released in 2000.<ref name="allmusic" />

He was nominated for four Grammys and sold over 10 million records.<ref name="CT obit" /> It is believed he holds the [[world record]] for the largest single Christian concert in history.<ref name="CT obit" /> In the first, he was the main act in August 1993 in [[Johannesburg]], South Africa, with more than 50,000 in attendance.<ref>{{Cite web |last=van Jaarsveld |first=Ingrid |title=Christene laat dak lig vir vrede 50 000 op Wanderers vir grootste vredesbyeenkoms nog in SA |trans-title=Christians Raise the Roof for Peace: 50,000 at Wanderers |url=http://argief.beeld.com/argief/berigte/beeld/1993/08/16/6/6.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304054011/http://argief.beeld.com/argief/berigte/beeld/1993/08/16/6/6.html |archive-date=March 4, 2016 |language=af}}</ref> The following year he performed a free concert at [[Texas Stadium]] October 22, 1994, with 71,132 attendees,<ref name="billboardbio" /><ref name="allmusic" /> and 80,000 in [[Chattanooga, Tennessee]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=February 17, 2021 |title=Christian Music Pioneer Carman Dies Due to Complications from Surgery |url=https://www.patheos.com/blogs/reelfaith/2021/02/christian-music-pioneer-carman-dies-due-to-complications-from-surgery.html |access-date=February 17, 2021}}</ref>

Beyond his music career, he participated in various television productions and interview duties as a host for both the [[Trinity Broadcasting Network]] in general and its flagship program ''[[Praise (TV series)|Praise the Lord]]''.<ref name="albuquerque">{{Cite web |last=Dunn |first=Patrick |date=February 25, 2005 |title=Christian Artist Carman Keeps Up With the Times |url=http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-3882365_ITM |access-date=December 9, 2009 |website=[[Albuquerque Journal]]}}</ref> In 2001, he starred in the film ''Carman: The Champion''.<ref name="billboardbio" /><ref>{{Cite web |last=Cling |first=Carol |date=March 2, 2001 |title=Opening This week |url=http://www.reviewjournal.com/lvrj_home/2001/Mar-02-Fri-2001/weekly/15543864.html |access-date=December 9, 2009 |website=[[Las Vegas Review-Journal]]}}</ref>

In late March 2013, he announced a Kickstarter campaign for a new album and music video. A short time later, he announced an upcoming 60-city tour, as well, additionally noting that the online fundraising campaign had raised more than $230,000 within several weeks.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Carman Announces 60-City Tour After Raising More Than $200K on Kickstarter |url=http://www.christianpost.com/news/carman-announces-60-city-tour-after-raising-more-than-200k-on-kickstarter-96154/ |website=Christian Post}}</ref>

== Personal life ==

In November 2011, Carman was the passenger in a car that was struck by a truck from oncoming traffic. The accident took place just outside the church where his appearance was scheduled. He performed the concert, but collapsed afterward and required surgery to repair internal injuries.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Carman in Car Accident |url=http://www.allaccess.com/net-news/archive/story/99280/carman-suffers-injuries-in-car-accident |access-date=November 22, 2011 |publisher=All Access Music Group}}</ref>

He began battling cancer, [[multiple myeloma]], in 2013, and he was given a prognosis of three to four years to live.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Carman Licciardello |url=https://www.facebook.com/CarmanLicciardello?ref=stream#!/photo.php?fbid=10151402347819350 |website=Facebook}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=January 10, 2014 |title=Carman Cancer Update: Singer Enters Second Round of Chemo Treatments, 'I've Never Been as Optimistic in My Life' |url=http://www.breathecast.com/articles/carman-cancer-update-live-across-america-tour-13479/ |website=BREATHEcast}}</ref> By early 2014, he said, medical tests indicated his body was free of cancer. He then continued preparing for his ''Live Across America'' album and tour.<ref>{{Cite web |date=September 8, 2014 |title=After Fears, Carman Tells Fans He's Cancer Free |url=http://www.cbn.com/cbnnews/us/2014/February/After-Fears-Carman-Tells-Fans-Hes-Cancer-Free |website=cbn.com}}</ref> When the cancer went into remission, he attributed his healing to the faith of his fans.<ref name="CT obit" /> He pledged to his Facebook and Kickstarter supporters to begin his "No Plan B Tour" as soon as he regained his health.<ref name="itickets.com">{{Cite web |title=Concerts, events, concert, festival & conference tickets |url=http://www.itickets.com/search/?sw=carman&selectName=99&offset=0&limit=20 |website=itickets.com}}</ref> Later that year he toured the eastern United States.<ref name="itickets.com" />

In December 2017, at the age of 61, he married Dana Morrow. His new family included six stepchildren and nine step-grandchildren.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Law |first=Jeannie |date=December 19, 2017 |title='Proverbial Bachelor' and Christian Star Carman Licciardello Finally Gets Married at 61 |work=[[The Christian Post]] |url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/proverbial-bachelor-and-christian-star-carman-licciardello-finally-gets-married-at-61-210687/ |access-date=September 7, 2018}}</ref> Carman filed for divorce in 2018 and it was settled in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Cooke |first=Bruno |date=February 17, 2021 |title=Carman Licciardello wife: Meet sales executive Dana Licciardello |url=https://www.thefocus.news/music/carman-licciardello-wife/ |accessdate=August 21, 2021}}</ref>

In January 2020, he announced his cancer had returned.<ref>{{Cite web |date=January 17, 2020 |title='I Still Trust God': Christian Singer Carman's Birthday Wish After Cancer Returns |url=https://www1.cbn.com/cbnnews/us/2020/january/i-still-trust-god-christian-singer-carmans-birthday-wish-after-cancer-returns |access-date=June 24, 2020 |website=CBN News |language=en}}</ref> He resumed live concert church tours in June 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Carman Licciardello |url=https://www.facebook.com/CarmanLicciardello/photos/a.10151796119314350/10158452712009350/?type=3 |access-date=June 24, 2020 |website=www.facebook.com}}</ref>

=== Death ===

Carman died on February 16, 2021, at a Las Vegas, Nevada, hospital after a series of complications resulting from surgery to repair a [[hiatal hernia]], 28 days after his 65th birthday.<ref name="CT obit" /><ref>{{Cite web |date=February 16, 2021 |title=GMA Gospel Music Hall of Fame Member, CARMAN, Iconic CCM Trailblazer, Dies at Las Vegas Hospital |url=https://www.conduitmedia.agency/pressreleases/2021/2/16/gma-gospel-music-hall-of-fame-member-carman-iconic-ccm-trailblazer-dies-at-las-vegas-hospital |publisher=Conduit Media Solutions}}</ref> He is survived by his wife, 5 stepchildren, and 10 step-grandchildren.
