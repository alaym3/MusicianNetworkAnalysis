'''Gretta Ray''' (born 1998) is an Australian singer-songwriter from [[Melbourne]], Victoria.

In 2016, she was the winner of the national [[Triple J Unearthed]] radio competition for bands and songwriters, and the 2016 [[Vanda & Young Global Songwriting Competition]], with her song "[[Drive (Gretta Ray song)|Drive]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4564467.htm |title=Gretta Ray wins $50,000 Vanda & Young songwriting competition: "It's unbelievable!" |publisher=[[Triple J]] |date=27 October 2016 |access-date=26 May 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161028020441/http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4564467.htm |archive-date=28 October 2016 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Gretta Ray grew up in Melbourne, Australia, attending [[Princes Hill Secondary College]]. She sang in choirs from the age of five, latterly singing and touring with Young Voices of Melbourne, and as the youngest member of ''If You See Her, Say Hello'', a group of 21 Melbourne-based singer-songwriters.<ref name="mismatch">{{cite web |url=http://www.mismatch.tv/a-chat-with-gretta-ray/ |title=A Chat With...Gretta Ray |publisher=Mismatch |access-date=21 August 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
===2016-2020: Career beginnings, ''Elsewhere'' and ''Here and Now''===
{{main|Here and Now (EP)}}
In February 2016, Ray released her debut EP, ''Elsewhere''.

In August 2016, she won the national competition for emerging new artists called [[Triple J Unearthed|Triple J Unearthed High]], for her song "[[Drive (Gretta Ray song)|Drive]]", which was produced by Nashville-based Australian music producer Josh Barber with Jonathan Dreyfus, recorded by Nick Edin and Fraser Montgomerey and mixed by US producer Ryan Hewit.<ref>{{cite web |title=Gretta Ray - Drive |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5d0rwhERGBw |publisher=Gretta Ray |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=25 March 2017}}</ref> The song received high-rotation airplay on [[Triple J]].<ref name="triplejunearthed">{{cite web |url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/article/gretta-ray-wins-unearthed-high-2016/ |title=Gretta Ray wins Unearthed High 2016! |publisher=[[Triple J Unearthed]] |access-date=21 August 2016}}</ref> The announcement was made by Triple J presenters [[Matt Okine|Matt]] and [[Alex Dyson|Alex]], who snuck into a school concert while she was performing.<ref name="youtube">{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bQnNbN77SOs |title=Matt and Alex surprise Unearthed High winner Gretta Ray |publisher=[[Triple J]] |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=21 August 2016}}</ref> Unearthed music director, Dave Ruby Howe called her a "...bold and exciting new talent who seems to win over everyone that comes into orbit of her music".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2016-08-18/melbourne-musician-gretta-ray-wins-triple-j's-unearthed-high/7762976/ |title=Melbourne musician Gretta Ray wins triple j's Unearthed High |publisher=[[ABC News (Australia)|ABC News]] |date=18 August 2016 |author=Jones, Ruby}}</ref> Ray also a nomination for a [[J Awards|J Award]] for Unearthed Artist of the Year at the [[J Awards of 2016|2016 J Awards]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/jawards/16/nominations/ |title=J Awards 2016: Nominations for 2016 |publisher=[[Triple J]] |date=2 November 2016 |access-date=2 November 2016}}</ref>

On 27 October 2016, Ray won the 2016 edition of the [[Vanda & Young Global Songwriting Competition]], which carries the largest first prize for any songwriting competition in the world.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://apraamcos.com.au/news/2016/october/gretta-rays-drive-wins-2016-vanda-and-young-songwriting-competition/ |title=Gretta Ray's "Drive" wins 2016 Vanda & Young Songwriting Competition! |publisher=[[APRA AMCOS]] |date=27 October 2016}}</ref>

In August 2018, Ray released her second EP titled ''[[Here and Now (EP)|Here and Now]]''.

In September 2019, Ray released a cover of Bon Iver's "re: Stacks" with Dustin Tebbutt and in December 2019, "Heal You in Time".

===2020–present: ''Begin to Look Around''===
{{main|Begin to Look Around}}
On 20 May 2021, Ray announced her debut album ''Begin to Look Around'', which was released on 27 August 2021.<ref name="NME-20210520">{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/gretta-ray-announces-debut-album-begin-to-look-around-2944515/ |title=Gretta Ray announces debut album 'Begin to Look Around' |work=[[NME Australia]] |date=20 May 2021 |access-date=21 May 2021 |author=Langford, Jackson |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210520024507/https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/gretta-ray-announces-debut-album-begin-to-look-around-2944515/ |archive-date=20 May 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref>
