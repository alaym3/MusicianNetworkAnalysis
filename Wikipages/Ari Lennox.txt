'''Courtney Shanade Salter''' (born March 26, 1991), known professionally as '''Ari Lennox''', is an American [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] singer from [[Washington, D.C.]] She is the first female artist to be signed to [[J. Cole]]'s record label, [[Dreamville Records]]. She released her first EP ''[[Pho (EP)|Pho]]'' on the label in 2016, when she began gaining recognition. Her debut studio album ''[[Shea Butter Baby]]'' followed in 2019.

== Early life ==
Courtney Shanade Salter was born on March 26, 1991, in [[Northern Virginia]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newsweek.com/who-ari-lennox-shea-butter-baby-singer-1418207|title=Who Is Ari Lennox? Everything We Know about the ''Shea Butter Baby'' Singer Trending on Twitter|work=[[Newsweek]]|date=May 7, 2019}}</ref> and spent her formative years in the [[Washington metropolitan area]]. Her stage name was influenced by Mary Lennox, a character in the film adaptation of ''[[The Secret Garden (1993 film)|The Secret Garden]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Shultz |first1=Alex |title=Ari Lennox Went from Uber Driving to Headlining Her Own Tour |url=https://www.gq.com/story/ari-lennox-interview |website=GQ |date=June 17, 2019 |access-date=June 24, 2019}}</ref>

== Musical career ==
=== 2012–2017: Career beginnings and ''Pho'' EP ===
Ari Lennox began uploading her music on the internet in 2009. She released her debut mixtape, ''Five Finger Discount'', and debut EP, ''Ariography'', in 2013. In 2014, she independently released the single "Bound".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Jeffries |first1=David |title=Ari Lennox Biography |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ari-lennox-mn0003462621/biography |website=AllMusic}}</ref> On July 21, 2015, she was featured on [[Omen (musician)|Omen]]'s album ''[[Elephant Eyes]]'' on the track "Sweat it Out". Later that year, her music was circulating around the Dreamville team, as [[J. Cole]] wanted her to work with him on some references for [[Rihanna]].<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Maher |first1=Natalie |title=Ari Lennox on Her Love for D.C., Being Signed to J. Cole & Protecting Her Mental Health |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8475991/ari-lennox-whipped-cream-j-cole-dreamville-interview |magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

On December 8, it was announced that she was signed to [[Dreamville Records]]. She made an appearance on the collaborative album ''[[Revenge of the Dreamers II]]'', on the track "Backseat" with [[Cozz]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Ortiz|first=Edwin|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2015/12/j-cole-signs-ari-lennox-and-lute-to-dreamville-records|title=J. Cole Signs Two New Artists to Dreamville Records|website=Complex|date=December 8, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author1=Victoria Hernandez|title=J. Cole x Dreamville "Revenge Of The Dreamers II" Cover Art, Tracklist & Album Stream|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.36551/title.j-cole-x-dreamville-revenge-of-the-dreamers-ii-release-date-cover-art|website=[[HipHopDX]]|access-date=January 28, 2016|date=December 10, 2015}}</ref> On October 21, 2016, she released her debut EP titled ''[[Pho (EP)|Pho]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/dreamvilles-ari-lennox-releasing-new-ep-300342946.html|title=Dreamville's Ari Lennox Releasing New EP|website=prnewswire.com}}</ref> Ari Lennox later appeared on J. Cole's song "[[Change (J. Cole song)|Change]]", and performed as an opening act on the [[4 Your Eyez Only Tour]] during 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7694374/j-cole-4-your-eyez-only-tour-dates|title=J. Cole Reveals 4 Your Eyez Only Tour Dates}}</ref>

=== 2018–present: ''Shea Butter Baby'' ===
In  2018, she went on to guest feature on two songs with her label-mates: [[EarthGang]] on "Nothing but the Best", and [[Bas (rapper)|Bas]] on "Icarus". On July 16, 2018,<ref>{{cite web |title=Ari Lennox Releases Sweet New Single "Whipped Cream" |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/07/ari-lennox-releases-sweet-new-single-whipped-cream |website=Complex}}</ref> Ari Lennox released the first single, "Whipped Cream", from her debut album, ''[[Shea Butter Baby]]'' with an accompanied music video on September 5.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2018/09/ari-lennox-whipped-cream-video|title=Ari Lennox's "Whipped Cream" Music Video: Watch|date=September 5, 2018|website=Vibe}}</ref> On November 9, she released two more singles called "40 Shades of Choke" and "Grampa".<ref>{{cite web |title=Ari Lennox Shares New Songs "40 Shades of Choke" and "Grampa" |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/11/ari-lennox-new-songs-40-shades-of-choke-grampa |website=Complex}}</ref> On November 13, she then released two more singles called "No One" and "Pedigree". She then appeared on the [[Creed II (soundtrack)|Creed II soundtrack]] with Cole on a track entitled "[[Shea Butter Baby (song)|Shea Butter Baby]]" on November 16, and released as a single on February 26, 2019, for her debut album of the same name. The track was accompanied by a music video on February 20, 2019, and surpassed 3 million views via YouTube within the first week. In December 2018, she was a supporting act on the From East Atlanta With Love Tour with [[6lack|6LACK]].

On March 27, 2019, she announced her first headlining tour, with the first leg beginning on May 12 and ending on June 14 in her hometown [[Washington, D.C.]]<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Spencer |first1=Tallie |title=Ari Lennox Announces Shea Butter Baby Tour Dates |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8504416/ari-lennox-tour-dates |magazine=Billboard |access-date=March 30, 2019}}</ref> Her debut album, ''[[Shea Butter Baby]]'' was released on May 7, 2019. Several times on this tour she was opened by [[Baby Rose]] and Mikhala Jené.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2019/06/ari-lennox-shea-butter-baby-nyc-tour-review|title=Ari Lennox's Grown And Sexy Energy Wins NYC Tour Stop|date=June 6, 2019|website=Vibe|language=en|access-date=October 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8512008/ari-lennox-shea-butter-baby-LA-concert-recap|title=Ari Lennox Delivers Intimate & Energetic Performance at LA Shea Butter Baby Tour Stop|magazine=Billboard|access-date=October 27, 2019}}</ref> She also went on the road with [[Lizzo]] on the [[Cuz I Love You Too Tour]] from July to October. The second leg of her headlining tour was in Europe, during December 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ari Lennox has been tapped to join Lizzo on U.S. tour |url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2019/7/16/ari-lennox-has-been-tapped-to-join-lizzo-on-us-tour.html |website=ABC News |access-date=August 6, 2019}}</ref> In February 2020, Ari Lennox was placed on [[BET]]'s "Future 40" list, which is a list of "40 of the most inspiring and innovative vanguards who are redefining what it means to be unapologetically young, gifted & black".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://chicagodefender.com/bet-digital-celebrates-black-excellence-with-new-original-editorial-series/|title=BET DIGITAL CELEBRATES BLACK EXCELLENCE WITH NEW ORIGINAL EDITORIAL SERIES|date=February 7, 2020|website=Chicago Defender|language=en-US|access-date=February 15, 2020}}</ref> She released multiple of singles in 2020 including "Bussit", "Chocolate Pomegranate", and a cover of "[[If You Want Me to Stay]]", as well as guest appearances and collaborations with [[Kiana Ledé]], [[Skip Marley]], 6lack, and [[Spillage Village]], among others.

== Artistry ==
Ari Lennox describes her own voice as "vulnerable but soulful; imperfect but pretty." Her voice has been compared to [[Erykah Badu]]. Referencing her artistry she explains, "Sometimes women are put in this box where we're only supposed to talk about certain things, I want to be braver and riskier. I think people want to hear that kind of honesty and frankness."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dreamville.com/artist/ari/|title=Ari|website=Dreamville|language=en-US|access-date=March 11, 2019}}</ref> Ari Lennox said in multiple interviews that she is inspired by 90s and 2000s R&B singers such as [[Erykah Badu]], [[D'Angelo]], [[Bilal (American singer)|Bilal]], [[Whitney Houston]], [[Aaliyah]], [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Amerie]], [[Ciara]], [[SWV]], [[Mary J Blige]], among many others, and soul singer [[Minnie Riperton]]. In an interview with [[The Fader]], she also mentioned her admiration of [[Kanye West]] and how she found multiple other artists through him.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/10/14/ari-lennox-dreamville-backseat-video-interview|title=Ari Lennox Is Singing Sensual Soul Cuts As Dreamville's New Artist}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://nationofbillions.com/a-scattershot-type-of-gal-like-ari-lennox|title=A Scattershot Type of Gal Like Ari Lennox}}</ref> The Gateway described Ari Lennox's sound as "successfully blends individual slices of classic Motown, modern R&B, and new-age soul into one seamless product."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thegatewayonline.ca/2016/11/album-review-pho/|title=Album Review: ''Pho'' – The Gateway|date=November 14, 2016}}</ref>
