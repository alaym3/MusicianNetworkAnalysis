'''Alina Baraz''' ({{lang-ru|Алина Бараз}}; born September 24, 1993) is an American R&B singer and songwriter from [[Cleveland|Cleveland, Ohio]]. In 2015, Baraz and [[Electronic music|electronic]] producer [[Galimatias]] released a collaborative EP together titled ''Urban Flora'' through [[Ultra Music]] after discovering each other's work online. The EP was met with positive reception and [[streaming media|streaming]] success.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ruder|first=Claire|title=After 2 years Galimatias resurfaces— with a new voice [Interview]|url=https://earmilk.com/2017/06/15/after-2-years-galimatias-resurfaces-with-a-new-voice-interview/|work=Earmilk|date=June 15, 2017|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> Baraz released her first solo EP, ''The Color of You'' in 2018.<ref name="TCOY" /><ref>{{cite web|last=Jasmine Montgomery|first=Sarah|title=Alina Baraz teases new song "Buzzin"|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/09/25/alina-baraz-tease-buzzin|work=[[The Fader]]|date=September 25, 2017|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> In April 2020, Baraz released her debut studio album, ''[[It Was Divine]]''.<ref name="It Was Divine, by Alina Baraz">{{Cite web|title=It Was Divine, by Alina Baraz|url=https://alinabaraz.bandcamp.com/album/it-was-divine|website=Alina Baraz|access-date=2020-06-02}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Alina Baraz was born on September 24, 1993, in [[Cleveland|Cleveland, Ohio]] to [[Russian Americans|Russian]] parents. She was the first child in her family born in United States. Growing up, her parents raised her on classical music, and on her own, she became interested in [[1990s in music|'90s music]] and singers such as [[Adele]], [[Amy Winehouse]] and [[Corinne Bailey Rae]]. She began singing at a young age in her [[choir|gospel choir]]. At age 19, she decided to move to [[Los Angeles|Los Angeles, California]] to pursue singing as a professional career. She developed a passion for songwriting and wrote her first song titled "Roses Dipped in Gold", released on April 15, 2013, in the "Lounge Masters Vibes" collection series.<ref name="Bazaar">{{cite web|last=Rutherford|first=Chrissy|title=Alina Baraz On Creating Her Debut Album Over Facebook|url=http://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/art-books-music/interviews/a12553/alina-baraz-interview-2015/|work=[[Harper's Bazaar]]|date=October 13, 2015|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref><ref name="MomPop">{{cite web|title=Alina Baraz — Mom+Pop|url=http://www.momandpopmusic.com/alina-baraz/|publisher=[[Mom + Pop Music]]|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
===2013–2016: ''Urban Flora''===
After moving to Los Angeles, Baraz discovered Danish electronic producer [[Galimatias]]' music online. She wrote lyrics for one of his instrumentals and released it on SoundCloud as a song titled "Drift" in 2013. Galimatias heard the track and decided to create more music with her. The two created an EP titled ''Urban Flora'' by sending each other music through Facebook and did not meet in person until February 2015, when the project was already finished.<ref name="Huffington">{{cite web|last=Duwe|first=Morena|title=Exclusive Interview And Tea Party With Galimatias & Alina Baraz|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/morena-duwe/exclusive-interview-and-t_b_7843048.html|work=[[HuffPost|Huffington Post]]|date=July 21, 2015|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> Alongside "Drift", the songs "Pretty Thoughts", "Make You Feel" and "Fantasy" were released individually preceding ''Urban Flora''. The EP was originally released on SoundCloud but on May 19, 2015, it received a commercial digital release through [[Ultra Music]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Alina Baraz and Galimatias Team Up for Seductive EP, 'Urban Flora|url=http://edm.com/articles/2015/4/28/alina-baraz-and-galimatias-urban-flora-1|work=EDM.com|access-date=January 1, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180101194704/http://edm.com/articles/2015/4/28/alina-baraz-and-galimatias-urban-flora-1|archive-date=January 1, 2018|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref name="UFiTunes"/> Upon release ''Urban Flora'' was met with positive critical reception and it peaked at number 111 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and number 2 on the US [[Dance/Electronic Albums|''Billboard'' Top Dance/Electronic Albums Chart]]. On September 9, 2015, a remix EP for ''Urban Flora'' was released, and later that month Baraz and Galimatias embarked on a brief co-headlining North American concert tour in support of the EP.<ref>{{cite web|title=Urban Flora (Remixes) by Alina Baraz on Apple Music|date=September 4, 2015|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/urban-flora-remixes/1031366029|publisher=[[Apple Music]]|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref><ref name="UFTour"/> In August 2016, Baraz signed a record deal with independent label [[Mom + Pop Music]] and released a vinyl edition of ''Urban Flora''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Alina Baraz Signs to Mom + Pop and Releases Urban Flora on Vinyl|url=http://www.momandpopmusic.com/blog/2016/08/alina-baraz-signs-to-mom-pop-and-releases-urban-flora-on-vinyl|publisher=[[Mom + Pop Music]]|date=August 16, 2016|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Alina Baraz & Galimatias - Urban Flora - Amazon.com Music|url=https://www.amazon.com/Urban-Flora-Alina-Baraz-Galimatias/dp/B01G036X1U/ref=tmm_vnl_swatch_0?_encoding=UTF8&qid=1514812897&sr=8-1|publisher=[[Amazon (company)|Amazon]]|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref>

===2016–2020: ''The Color of You'' and ''It Was Divine''===
In spring and summer of 2016, Baraz made her first solo live appearances by performing at North American music festivals such as [[Lightning in a Bottle]], [[Sasquatch! Music Festival]], [[Electric Forest Festival]] and [[Lollapalooza]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Minsker|first1=Evan|last2=Monroe|first2=Jazz|title=Lollapalooza 2016 Lineup Announced|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/64249-lollapalooza-2016-lineup-announced/|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=March 23, 2016|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> Also in summer of 2016, Baraz shared that she was working on her debut solo studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/alinabaraz/status/746860065982930944|title=Alina Baraz on Twitter: "Working on my album"|publisher=[[Twitter]]|date=June 25, 2016|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> Baraz's first solo single, "Electric" featuring R&B singer [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]], was released on January 20, 2017 through [[Mom + Pop Music]], the single later appeared on her second EP ''The Color of You'' in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|last=Vensand|first=Rachel|title=Alina Baraz and Khalid Link Up on Seductive New Track, 'Electric'|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/alina-baraz-and-khalid-link-up-on-seductive-new|website=Ones to Watch|date=January 24, 2017|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> Following the release of the single, Baraz announced her first solo concert tour, entitled the 'Let's Get Lost Tour'. The tour took place from March to April 2017 and visited various cities across the [[United States]] and [[Canada]].<ref name="LGLTour"/>

In September and October 2017, Baraz supported British rock band [[Coldplay]] for the final 8 North American shows on their [[A Head Full of Dreams Tour]].<ref name="Coldplay">{{cite web|title=Alina Baraz Announces Upcoming Tour Dates with Coldplay|url=http://www.momandpopmusic.com/blog/2017/2/alina-baraz-announces-upcoming-tour-dates-with-coldplay|publisher=[[Mom + Pop Music]]|date=February 17, 2017|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> On September 29, 2017, Baraz released two singles titled "Lavender and Velvet" and "Buzzin" in anticipation of her upcoming debut studio album.<ref name="Complex">{{cite web|last=Shouneyia|first=Alexa|title=Alina Baraz Shines on Her 2 New Songs "Buzzin" and "Lavender and Velvet"|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2017/09/alina-baraz-new-songs-buzzin-lavender-and-velvet|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=September 29, 2017|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> "Buzzin" peaked at number 10 on the US [[Dance/Electronic Digital Songs|''Billboard'' Dance/Electronic Digital Songs Chart]] and the music video for the song, Baraz's first music video, was released exclusively through [[Apple Music]] on December 22, 2017.<ref name="ElecDig"/><ref>{{cite web|title="Buzzin" on Apple Music|date=December 22, 2017|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/music-video/buzzin/1326906505|publisher=[[Apple Music]]|accessdate=January 1, 2018}}</ref> It was announced that Baraz will be part of the [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] 2018 lineup.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.sandiegouniontribune.com/entertainment/music/sd-me-music-coachella-20180102-story.html|title=Coachella 2018 lineup will include Beyoncé, Eminem, The Weeknd & SZA|last=Varga|first=George|work=sandiegouniontribune.com|access-date=January 4, 2018}}</ref>

On April 6, 2018, Baraz released her first solo EP ''The Color of You'' and released a music video for the track "I Don't Even Know Why Though".<ref name="TCOY">{{cite web|last=Ortiz|first=Edwin|title=Exclusive: Alina Baraz Shares "I Don't Even Know Why Though" Video and Talks 'The Color of You'|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2018/04/alina-baraz-i-dont-even-know-why-though-video-and-the-color-of-you-stream|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=April 5, 2018|accessdate=April 6, 2018}}</ref>

In September 2019 Baraz released the single, "To Me".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ik3HHmVuHio |title=Alina Baraz - To Me |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2019-09-24 |accessdate=2020-03-17}}</ref> In January 2020, Baraz released the standalone single "Trust", which was followed by the singles "Morocco" and "More Than Enough" in February and March respectively.

In March 2020, Baraz announced the release her debut studio album, ''[[It Was Divine]]'', which was released on April 24, 2020.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=alinabaraz|number=1237485167855501314|title=The Album It Was Divine May 5th|date=March 10, 2020|accessdate=March 11, 2020}}</ref> The album was supported by the singles; "To Me", "Morocco" featuring [[6lack]], "More Than Enough" and "Off The Grid" featuring [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]]. ''It Was Divine'' also features guest appearances from [[Nas]] and [[Smino]] and charted at #43 on the [[Billboard 200]].<ref name="It Was Divine, by Alina Baraz"/>

===2021–present: Independent music and touring ===

On July 23, 2021, Alina Baraz released the single "Alone With You".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alina Baraz Drops Intimate "Alone With You"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/alina-baraz-drops-intimate-alone-with-you-new-song.1992088.html|access-date=2021-08-06|website=HotNewHipHop|date=July 23, 2021|language=en}}</ref> The song would mark her first release as an independent artist, following her departure from her previous record label, Mom+Pop. Alongside the song's release, Baraz announced a tour of the same name, set to start in late 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Courtney|first=Ian|date=2021-07-28|title=Alina Baraz Announces 'Alone With You' Tour|url=https://celebrityaccess.com/2021/07/28/alina-baraz-announces-alone-with-you-tour/|access-date=2021-08-06|website=CelebrityAccess|language=en-US}}</ref> On September 24, Baraz released the EP ''Sunbeam''. The EP serves as part one of a two part EP series.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alina Baraz Returns With Her Four-Track EP 'Sunbeam'|url=https://www.complex.com/music/alina-baraz-sunbeam-stream|access-date=2021-09-25|website=Complex}}</ref> The second EP, ''Moongate'', was released on October 29, 2021.
