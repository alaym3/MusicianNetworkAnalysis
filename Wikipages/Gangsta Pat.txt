'''Patrick Hall''' (born November 17, 1973), better known by his stage name '''Gangsta Pat''', is an American [[rapping|rapper]] from [[Memphis, Tennessee]] who established himself in the Memphis [[underground rap|underground]] during the late 1980s and is the son of [[Stax Records]] drummer, [[Willie Hall (drummer)|Willie Hall]].<ref name="Larkin">Larkin, Colin (1999) ''The Virgin Encyclopedia of Dance Music'', Virgin Books, {{ISBN|0-7535-0252-6}}, p. 133</ref> Gangsta Pat is also one of the first rap artists from the city to make the move from an [[Independent record label|indie]] label to a [[major label]] when signing to [[Atlantic records]] during the start of the [[Gangsta rap]] era.<ref name="Malone">Malone, Bill C. & Wilson, Charles Reagan (2009) ''The New Encyclopedia of Southern Culture: Volume 12: Music'', {{ISBN|978-0-8078-5908-7}}, p. 77</ref><ref name="Birchmeier">Birchmeier, Jason "[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/gangsta-pat-p42/biography Gangsta Pat Biography]", [[Allmusic]], retrieved 2011-01-26</ref> He wrote, produced, and played all of the instruments on his early releases.<ref name="Larkin" /><ref name="Hoffman">Hoffman, Frank W. (2005) ''American Popular Music: Rhythm & Blues, Rap, and Hip-hop'', Facts On File Inc, {{ISBN|978-0-8160-5315-5}}, p. 101</ref>
