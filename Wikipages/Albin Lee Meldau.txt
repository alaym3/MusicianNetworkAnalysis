'''Albin Lee Meldau''' (born February 8, 1988) is a Swedish singer, songwriter and musician. He self-released his debut EP, ''Lovers'', in 2016 with help from producer [[Björn Yttling]] (of [[Peter Bjorn and John]]).

Later that year, Albin signed to [[Astralwerks]], who re-released his ''Lovers'' EP, and in 2017 released the follow-up ''Bloodshot'' EP.

In 2017, Albin was named by NPR Music as one of their must-see artists at that year's SXSW,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/allsongs/2017/03/13/519686449/the-all-songs-considered-sxsw-preview-2017|title=The All Songs Considered SXSW Preview, 2017|work=NPR.org|access-date=2018-03-27|language=en}}</ref> and featured as part of their Tiny Desk Concert series. NPR Music described his voice as "breathtaking, soulful, thunderous and impossible to ignore".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/event/music/538090328/albin-lee-meldau-tiny-desk-concert|title=Albin Lee Meldau: Tiny Desk Concert|work=NPR.org|access-date=2018-03-27}}</ref>

He was previously part of a band called the Magnolia.

His debut album, ''About You'', was released on 1 June 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.astralwerks.com/news/albin-lee-meldau-announces-june-1-release-debut-album/|title=ALBIN LEE MELDAU ANNOUNCES JUNE 1 RELEASE OF HIS DEBUT ALBUM "ABOUT YOU"|work=astralwerks|access-date=2018-04-16|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2018, he appeared on ''[[Så mycket bättre]]'', which is broadcast on TV4.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.expressen.se/noje/har-ar-artisterna-i-sa-mycket-battre-2018/|title=Här är artisterna i 'Så mycket bättre' 2018|publisher=Expressen|accessdate=5 December 2018}}</ref> On 8 May 2020, Meldau released the first EP out of two, called ''På svenska (Sida A)''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Albin Lee Meldau släpper första delen av albumet "På svenska"|url=http://www.mynewsdesk.com/se/sonybmg/pressreleases/albin-lee-meldau-slaepper-foersta-delen-av-albumet-paa-svenska-2997384|last=|first=|date=8 May 2020|website=[[MyNewsDesk]]|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>
