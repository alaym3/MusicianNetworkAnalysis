'''54-40''' (often stylized '''54•40''') is a Canadian [[alternative rock]] group from [[Tsawwassen]], [[British Columbia]]. The band take their name from the [[slogan]] "54-40 or Fight!", coined to express the unsuccessful expansionist agenda of [[James K. Polk]]'s presidency, which was intent upon controlling a contested U.S.-Canada border area in the [[Oregon boundary dispute]]. 54-40 has had a successful career, with four of their albums being certified Platinum in Canada. The band has been nominated for eight [[Juno Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://junoawards.ca/awards/past-winners-nominees/?search_year_val1=1970&search_year_val2=2020&search_category_val=&award_name=&search_wins_val=no&tag_search_val=54*40&submit=submit|title=PAST NOMINEES + WINNERS|website=junoawards.ca|access-date=May 7, 2020}}</ref> Between 1996 and 2016, 54-40 were among the top 150 selling Canadian artists in Canada and among the top 50 selling Canadian bands in Canada.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bdsradio.com/pd/docs/Nielsen_Canadian150_2017_vs2.pdf|title=NIELSEN MUSIC & BILLBOARD PRESENT CANADA 150 CHARTS|website=bdsradio.com|page=28|access-date=January 2, 2020}}</ref>

==History==
Neil Osborne and Brad Merritt met in 1978 at [[South Delta Secondary School|South Delta High School]] in [[Tsawwassen, British Columbia|Tsawwassen]], [[British Columbia]]. After studying at the [[Berklee College of Music]] in [[Boston]], Osborne returned home and formed the band 54-40 with Merritt and drummer Ian Franey. The band played their first gig in [[Coquitlam]] on December 8, 1980, the night [[John Lennon]] was killed. That same year, they made their first recordings for the [[Mo-Da-Mu]] label, with four tracks appearing on the independent compilation LP ''Things Are Still Coming Ashore'', which also featured music by Vancouver bands Animal Slaves and Junco Run. In June 1982, the band released the EP ''[[Selection (album)|Selection]]''. Shortly after the release of ''Selection'', Franey left the band. Franey was replaced by [[Darryl Neudorf]] and Phil Comparelli was added on guitar, trumpet and vocals. The band recorded the album ''[[Set the Fire]]'' in 1983, releasing it in 1984. Neudorf, frustrated with the band's prolonged time in the studio and away from the road, left the band in 1985. He was replaced by Matt Johnson. 

The band's [[54-40 (album)|self-titled second album]], released in 1986 through Warner/[[Reprise Records]], began to attract attention from radio and record buyers across Canada, with the singles "Baby Ran" and "[[I Go Blind]]" gaining significant college radio airplay. Dave Osborne (keyboards, harmonica), toured and recorded with the band from 1987 to 1993. The band's third album, ''[[Show Me (54-40 album)|Show Me]]'', became their commercial breakthrough in Canada, with the hits "[[One Day in Your Life (54-40 song)|One Day in Your Life]]" and "One Gun".

Although the band was popular on [[college radio]] in the United States, they never had a commercial breakthrough, and Warner dropped 54-40 in 1990. In 1991, the band released the compilation album, ''[[Sweeter Things: A Compilation|Sweeter Things]]''. Also during that year, the band signed a deal with Sony and released ''[[Dear Dear]]'' in 1992. It was the band's first album to be certified Gold in Canada and was certified Platinum in 1993. The band released ''[[Smilin' Buddha Cabaret]]'' in 1994 and ''[[Trusted by Millions]]'' in 1996, both of which would later be certified Platinum.

The band's song "[[I Go Blind]]" was covered in the mid-1990s by American band [[Hootie & the Blowfish]], and was featured on the first soundtrack to the TV series ''[[Friends]]''. The song also appears on two compilations released by the band: 2000's ''[[Scattered, Smothered and Covered]]'' and 2003's ''[[The Best of Hootie & the Blowfish: 1993–2003|The Best of Hootie & the Blowfish (1993 Thru 2003)]]''. Royalties from the Hootie and the Blowfish cover enabled the band to build their own recording studio in Vancouver.

[[Dave Genn]], formerly of [[Matthew Good Band]], joined 54-40 in 2003. In February 2005, it was announced that Comparelli left the band due to personal reasons.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chartattack.com/damn/2005/02/1702.cfm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060306121832/http://www.chartattack.com/damn/2005/02/1702.cfm|url-status=dead|archive-date=March 6, 2006|title=54-40 Guitarist To Be Replaced By Former Matthew Good Ax-Man|publisher=[[Chart Attack]]|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref> That same year, 54-40 signed with [[True North Records]].

In 2010, to celebrate the band's 30 years together and almost 25 years since their first commercial album ''[[54-40 (album)|The Green Album]]'', the band went on a promotional [[concert tour]] with a two-set act. The first set was "[[54-40 (album)|The Green Album]]" from start to finish. The second set was one song from every album since, with the exception of ''[[Dear Dear]]'' where they played two songs. 54-40 also performed a new song from their upcoming record, "Lost In The City", which was released on June 14, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://5440.com/index.php?view=article&catid=1:latest-news&id=71:5440-celebrates-30th-anniversary-by-going-qgreenq-again&tmpl=component&print=1&layout=default&page= |title=The Official Website of 54•40 – 54•40 Canada's Band|website=5440.com|access-date=January 22, 2020}}</ref>

In January, 2016, the band released a greatest hits album titled ''La Difference: A History Unplugged''. Tracks included "[[One Day in Your Life (54-40 song)|One Day In Your Life]]", "[[I Go Blind]]", and "[[Ocean Pearl]]". The album was released through [[eOne Music]]. The band then set out on a month-long theatre tour across Canada to support the album. In 2017 they played at Peachfest in Penticton.<ref>[https://www.pentictonwesternnews.com/entertainment/54-40-rocks-the-fest/ "54-40 rocks the fest"]. ''Penticton Western News'', Aug. 11, 2017</ref>

In 2018 54-40 performed with fiddler [[Daniel Lapp]], both locally<ref name=dwyer>[https://www.coastreporter.net/entertainment/arts-entertainment/54-40-pull-all-the-right-strings-1.23210136 "54-40 pull all the right strings"]. Paul Dwyer, March 22, 2018</ref> and also as the opening band at Ottawa's [[CityFolk Festival]].<ref>[https://ottawacitizen.com/entertainment/local-arts/cityfolk-review-54-40-puts-a-tasty-new-spin-on-its-old-hits "CityFolk review: 54-40 puts a tasty new spin on its old hits"]. ''Ottawa Citizen'', Lynn Saxberg, September 14, 2018</ref>

Lead singer Neil Osborne began playing shows with his daughter, singer-songwriter [[Kandle (musician)|Kandle]], billing themselves as A Family Curse, self-described as "pure folk-art worked from found objects (something old, something new)."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yvonne Matsell presents an evening with A Family Curse|url=https://www.eventbrite.com/e/yvonnematsell-presents-a-family-curse-neil-osborne5440kandle-earlyshow-tickets-61314467222#|access-date=2020-07-08|website=EventBrite}}</ref> A [[Toronto]] show took place May 8, 2019<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yvonne Matsell presents an evening with A Family Curse|url=https://www.eventbrite.com/e/yvonnematsell-presents-a-family-curse-neil-osborne5440kandle-earlyshow-tickets-61314467222#|access-date=2020-07-08|website=EventBrite}}</ref> at the intimate and tiny 20-capacity Radical Road Brewing Co. The father-daughter duo played later that year on August 7, 2019<ref>{{Cite web|title=A Family Curse - Roy Thompson Hall|url=https://www.roythomsonhall.com/tickets/a-family-curse/|access-date=2020-07-08|website=Roy Thompson Hall}}</ref> as part of [[Roy Thompson Hall]]'s Live on the Patio<ref>{{Cite web|title=Neil (54-40) and Kandle Osborne at Roy Thompson Hall patio|url=https://www.toronto.com/events/9520289--neil-54-40-and-kandle-osborne/|access-date=2020-07-08|website=Toronto.com}}</ref> series, also in Toronto. These shows were bookended by shows in Montréal, where Kandle resides, in February<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kandle & Neil Osborne present A Family Curse|url=https://lepointdevente.com/billets/16022019familycurse|access-date=2020-07-08|website=Le Point de Vente}}</ref> and August,<ref>{{Cite web|title=A Family Curse (Kandle & Neil Osborne) in Montreal|url=https://montreal.eventful.com/events/family-curse-kandle-neil-osborne-/E0-001-129062104-6|access-date=2020-07-08|website=Eventful Montreal}}</ref> 2019.<!-- dummy edit; can be deleted. -->

54-40 played The Legendary [[Horseshoe Tavern]]'s 72nd anniversary party<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Horseshoe Tavern's 72nd Birthday Celebration|url=https://www.horseshoetavern.com/e/54-40-71689799119/|access-date=2020-07-08|website=Legendary Horseshoe Tavern calendar}}</ref> on December 6, 2019. Sam Cash, son of Toronto post-punk musician turned city politician, [[Andrew Cash]], opened the show.<!-- dummy edit; can be deleted. -->
