'''Queen Naija Bulls''' ({{IPAc-en|Àà|n|…ëÀê|d í|…ô}};<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=19tGDN74eME|title=Queen Naija Goes Undercover on YouTube, Twitter and Wikipedia|work=GQ|date=December 1, 2020|access-date=September 1, 2021}}</ref> born October 17, 1995) is an American singer and Social Media Creator. Born in [[Ypsilanti, Michigan]], though she was raised majorly in [[Detroit|Detroit, Michigan]], she started out her career as a [[vlog]]ger on YouTube and then participated in season 13 of ''[[American Idol]]''. Her self-released song "Medicine" (2017) reached the top 50 of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in 2018, and she was signed to [[Capitol Records]].

Queen Naija's self-titled [[Extended play|EP]] was released in 2018 and debuted at number 26 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. Three singles from the album were [[certified platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]]. In 2020, Queen Naija released her debut album, ''[[Missunderstood (Queen Naija album)|Missunderstood]]''. The album debuted and peaked at number 9 on the ''Billboard'' 200, her first top 10 album on the chart.

== Early life ==
Queen Naija was born on October 17, 1995, in [[Ypsilanti, Michigan]]. She has described her background as a mix of "Arab, Black and Italian",<ref>{{Citation|last=Queen Naija|title=Queen Naija Reveals Her Heritage|date=January 10, 2018|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/queennaija/status/951330076477534208|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref> but has also mentioned having Indian{{efn|Due to the ambiguity of the word "Indian" in English, this could mean "Indian" in reference to having partial Native American ancestry, or "Indian" meaning she has ancestry from the Indian subcontinent.}} ancestry, though she was not sure how much.<ref>{{Citation|title=MY FIRST Q&A (GET TO KNOW QUEEN)|date=April 20, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IDvc1bv-3qc&t=7m42s|quote="I'm half Arab, and then the other part is Black and Italian. My mom is Black, Italian and Indian, but I don't know how much so I just say "Arab, Black, and Italian."|access-date=July 26, 2020|via=YouTube}}</ref> Her father is from [[Yemen]].<ref name="truth1">{{cite web|last=Queen Naija|title=THE TRUTH BEHIND MY DAD|date=November 6, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IqiZY2voxHA|access-date=October 4, 2018}}</ref><ref name="pronunciation">{{cite web|last=Queen Naija|title=Queen Naija Reveals Origin Of Her Name and Correct Way To Pronounce It|date=September 10, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T4FasmxtC-U|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref>

Her birth name, Queen Naija, was given to her by her mother. "Queen" was inspired by her grandmother, who had the same first name, and "Naija" represented her father's name.<ref>{{Citation|via=DJ Smallz Eyes 2 on YouTube|title=Queen Naija Reveals Origin Of Her Name and Correct Way To Pronounce It|date=September 10, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T4FasmxtC-U&t=215s|access-date=October 5, 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2014‚Äì2017: ''American Idol'' and YouTube ===
Queen Naija first gained prominence as a contestant on the [[American Idol (season 13)|thirteenth season]] of ''[[American Idol]]''. She had auditioned two years earlier but had failed to advance into the next round.<ref name="idol1">{{cite web|last=American Idol|title=Road to Hollywood: Queen Bulls ‚Äì AMERICAN IDOL SEASON XIII|date=January 22, 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NYkUIQdxb_o|access-date=October 5, 2018}}</ref> In the thirteenth season, she advanced into the Hollywood round, but was cut when the top 30 were chosen.<ref name="idol1" /> Afterwards, Queen Naija went back to working as a security guard. She then continued on building her [[YouTube]] career.<ref>{{Citation|last=106 KMEL|title=Queen Naija talks Detroit Slang, Cardi B, American Idol, and Her Ex!|date=July 16, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zi1jfk5DSho&t=682s|access-date=October 5, 2018}}</ref>

In August 2016, Queen Naija began her YouTube career alongside her then-husband, Christopher Sails, in [[Detroit|Detroit, Michigan]] and then moving base to [[Houston|Houston, Texas]]. In December 2017, Queen Naija filed for divorce from Sails. Their channel, which held over 2.3 million subscribers, was given to Sails by Queen Naija.<ref name="club1">{{cite web|last=Breakfast Club Power 105.1 FM|title=Queen Naija Opens Up About New Relationship, New Music, Karma + More,Medicine and Karma have been certified gold in the United States |date=July 26, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mk5oLgFvdL8|access-date=October 2, 2018}}</ref>

=== 2017‚Äì2019: Musical career beginnings and ''Queen Naija'' EP ===
Queen Naija released her song "Medicine" on December 31, 2017, and "Karma", her second single,  on June 29, 2018.{{citation needed|date=February 2021}}

Queen Naija did a video interview for the song "Medicine" with [[Genius (website)|Genius]] in January 2018.<ref name="medicine genius">{{cite web|last=Genius|title=Queen Naija "Medicine" Official Lyrics & Meaning {{!}} Verified|date=January 23, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wsaq1qsnbG4|access-date=October 5, 2018}}</ref> Queen documented on her YouTube channel a few meetings with record labels that she attended with her team/management.<ref>{{Citation|last=Queen Naija|title=FINALLY TOOK Y'ALL TO MY MEETING!|date=March 21, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bLd-7E6D4lc|access-date=October 5, 2018}}</ref> By March 2018, Queen's initial song post of "Medicine" had more than 10 million views.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bf2G1tUB2RP/|title=Queen Naijaüëë on Instagram: "Came to Get Pizza & Pieces from @cutsandslicesnyc @mclarenkickshop1 & then CLARENCE HANDSOME BEAUTIFUL SELF CAME & POPPED CHAMPAGNE ON A‚Ä¶"|via=Instagram|access-date=October 4, 2018}}</ref> An official music video was released later that month.<ref name="billboard qn1">{{Cite news|title=How YouTube Vlogger Queen Naija's Viral Breakup Inspired Her Hot 100 Breakthrough|work=Billboard|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8344128/queen-naija-medicine-hot-100-youtube-breakup-interview|access-date=October 4, 2018}}</ref> The video acquired 4.5 million views on the day of its release, and has over 150+ million views as of December 2019.<ref>{{Citation|last=Queen Naija|title=MEDICINE ‚Äì QUEEN NAIJA (OFFICIAL VIDEO)|date=March 29, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dc89yyOS0Z8|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> In July 2018, Queen Naija created a new collaborative YouTube channel, "Royal Family", alongside her new boyfriend, Clarence White. The channel has 2.79 million subscribers as of March 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCtj45MepAoKxZoyR_Mnt86Q/videos|title=Royal Family|website=YouTube|language=en|access-date=October 4, 2018}}</ref>

On April 25, 2018, Queen Naija signed to [[Capitol Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BiFshAohSMW/|title=Queen Naijaüëë on Instagram: "First of all, I want to give ALL THE GLORY AND HONOR TO JESUS!!! And I TRULY wouldn't have been able to do this WITHOUT ROYALTY SQUAD!!!‚Ä¶"|via=Instagram|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> On June 28, she released her second single, "Karma". She wrote and recorded the song in January 2018.<ref>{{Citation|last=Brittney Michelle|title=Queen Naija sings new song Karma live|date=January 15, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6ikO-08pvwk|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> On the same day, the song reached number one overall on the [[iTunes]] charts.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/queennaija/status/1012784860438466560|title=Blue Rose üåπ on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> "Karma" debuted at number 13 on the ''Billboard'' [[Digital Songs]] sales chart,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2018/07/10/queen-naijas-karma-debuts-13-digital-song-sales-63-hot-100/|title=Queen Naija's "Karma" Debuts At #13 On Digital Song Sales, #63 On Hot 100|date=July 10, 2018|work=Headline Planet|access-date=October 6, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> and at number 63 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2018-07-14|title=Billboard Hot 100: July 14, 2018|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 10, 2018}}</ref> The audio for the song has over 116 million views as of March 2021.<ref>{{Citation|last=QueenNaijaVEVO|title=Queen Naija ‚Äì Karma (Audio)|date=June 28, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bf71mnxB9kI|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref>

Queen Naija released her self-titled [[extended play]] on July 27, 2018. It consists of five songs: "Medicine", "Karma", "Mama's Hand", "Butterflies", and "Bad Boy".<ref>{{Citation|title=Queen Naija ‚Äì EP by Queen Naija|date=July 27, 2018|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/queen-naija-ep/1413905925|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> The project includes production from [[No I.D.]], El Jefe, Rob Grimaldi, 30 Hertz Beats, and ClickNPress.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/read-all-the-lyrics-to-queen-naijas-self-titled-debut-ep|title=Read All The Lyrics To Queen Naija's Self-Titled Debut EP|website=Genius|language=en|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> Queen described the EP as an emotional journey in which each song describes either an experience or emotion she has gone through.<ref>{{Citation|last=Genius|title=Queen Naija Discusses Her Debut EP & Grammy Goals {{!}} For The Record|date=August 8, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lCbw6Ddu9sA|access-date=October 7, 2018}}</ref> The songs reference topics including heartbreak and betrayal, motherhood, and finding new love again.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://thesource.com/2018/07/28/queen-naija-ep/|title=Queen Naija Goes the Self-Titled Route With Her Debut EP {{!}} The Source|date=July 28, 2018|work=The Source|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> "Medicine" deals with her heartbreak and emotions toward her ex-partner, and "Karma" explains overcoming the heartbreak while sending a message that she will be fine. "Mama's Hand" is dedicated to her son; she promises to give him everything and encourages him to have optimistic thoughts about the future.<ref name="bong review1">{{Cite news|url=https://bongminesentertainment.com/queen-naija-debut-ep/|title=Queen Naija releases a beautiful self-titled debut EP [REVIEW]|date=August 1, 2018|work=Bong Mines Entertainment|access-date=October 6, 2018}}</ref> "Butterflies" reveals that she is beginning to fall in love again with someone new. "Bad Boy" describes her connection with a new lover, and how her goodness may cause him to change for the better.<ref name="bong review1" /> The EP debuted at number 26 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and reached number one on the [[Apple Music]] R&B charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2018-08-11|title=Billboard 200: August 11, 2018|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=August 7, 2018}}</ref>
