'''Li Lykke Timotej Zachrisson''' (born 18 March 1986), known as '''Lykke Li''' ({{IPA-sv|ˈlʏ̂kːɛ ˈliː}}), is a [[Sweden|Swedish]] singer, songwriter and model. Her music often blends elements of [[indie pop]], [[dream pop]] and [[Electronic music|electronic]]. Her debut studio album, ''[[Youth Novels]]'', was released in 2008, and has been followed by ''[[Wounded Rhymes]]'' (2011), ''[[I Never Learn]]'' (2014), and ''[[So Sad So Sexy]]'' (2018).

==Career==

===2007–10: Career beginnings and ''Youth Novels''===
Li released her first [[extended play|EP]], ''Little Bit'', in 2007. [[Stereogum]] named her an artist to watch in October 2007 and described her music as a mix of [[soul music|soul]], [[Electro (music)|electro]] and "powdered-sugar pop".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://stereogum.com/archives/band-to-watch/artist-to-watch-lykke-li_006945.html |title=Artist To Watch: Lykke Li |publisher=Stereogum |date=26 October 2007 |access-date=7 May 2014}}</ref>
Li's debut album, ''[[Youth Novels]]'', was released on LL Recordings in the [[Nordic countries|Nordic]] region on 30{{nbsp}}January 2008 and received a wider European release in June 2008. The album was produced by [[Björn Yttling]] of [[Peter Bjorn and John]] and Lasse Mårtén and was reportedly inspired by a previous relationship of three years.<ref>{{cite news|author=Interview by Charlotte Cripps |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/lykke-li-the-swedish-pop-sensation-shows-wisdom-beyond-her-years-852260.html |title=Lykke Li: The Swedish pop sensation shows wisdom beyond her years – Features – Music |newspaper=The Independent |date=23 June 2008 |access-date=16 September 2012}}</ref> It was released in the United States on 19{{nbsp}}August 2008. The album was released in the United Kingdom in June 2008, promoted by a performance of "Little Bit" on ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]'' on 25{{nbsp}}May 2008.

[[File:Lykke Li 2.JPG|thumb|left|180px|Studio self-portrait (2009)]]
Li performed live with a pared-down drum kit, a necklace made out of percussive instruments, a guitar, a bass and a microphone.{{citation needed|date=June 2014}} ''Youth Novels'' was placed on many of the year's Best Of lists<ref>{{cite web|title=NME's Top 50 Albums of 2008|url=https://www.stereogum.com/41012/nmes_top_50_of_2008/list/|website=Stereogum|access-date=30 November 2014|date=11 December 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Staff Lists: The 50 Best Albums of 2008|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/staff-lists/7573-the-50-best-albums-of-2008/|website=Pitchfork|access-date=30 November 2014|date=19 December 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The Best Albums of 2008|url=https://www.popmatters.com/feature/66813-the-best-albums-of-2008-60-51/P2/|website=PopMatters|access-date=30 November 2014|date=18 December 2008}}</ref> and saw Li sell out tours, including sets at festivals [[Glastonbury Festival|Glastonbury]], [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] and [[Lollapalooza]]. She appeared on ''[[Late Night with Conan O'Brien]]''.<ref>{{cite web|author=Lykke LiInfoTijdlijnInfo |url=https://www.facebook.com/lykkeli/info |title=Lykke Li – Info |publisher=Facebook |date=30 May 2008 |access-date=7 May 2014}}{{Primary source inline|date=June 2020}}</ref>

She appeared on Swedish musician [[Kleerup]]'s [[Kleerup (album)|self-titled album]], contributing vocals to the track "Until We Bleed". She also worked with Norwegian electronic duo [[Röyksopp]] on their 2009 album ''[[Junior (Röyksopp album)|Junior]]'', contributing vocals to "Miss It So Much" and "Were You Ever Wanted". Li appeared on ''[[Last Call with Carson Daly]]'' on 18{{nbsp}}February 2009. She covered "Knocked Up", originally recorded by [[Kings of Leon]] who had approached Lykke to cover a song of her choice, and "Gifted" in which she performs with [[Kanye West]]. Li performed at the 2009 Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival on 19{{nbsp}}April and the 2009 Lollapalooza festival on 8{{nbsp}}August as part of the promotional tour for ''Youth Novels''.

A remixed version of her song "I'm Good, I'm Gone" was featured in the 2009 horror film ''[[Sorority Row]]''. The song "[[Possibility (song)|Possibility]]" was written for the 2009 film ''[[The Twilight Saga: New Moon]]''. Li had been asked to write a song to the film soundtrack but was reluctant to commit to the project. It was after she had seen an early screening of the film that she decided she wanted to contribute to the soundtrack. The soundtrack was released on 16{{nbsp}}October 2009.

===2011–13: ''Wounded Rhymes''===
The song "[[Get Some (Lykke Li song)|Get Some]]" was featured in the 15th episode of the first season of ''[[Hawaii Five-0 (2010 TV series, season 1)|Hawaii Five-0]]'' titled "Kai e'e" which aired 23{{nbsp}}January 2011. The song was also used in [[ABC Family]]'s drama ''[[Pretty Little Liars]]'' in the 18th episode of the second season which was titled "A Kiss Before Lying" which aired 30{{nbsp}}January 2012. The song was also used in the 19th episode of [[List of The Vampire Diaries episodes#Season 2 (2010–2011)|the second season]] of [[The CW]]'s ''[[The Vampire Diaries]]'', titled "Klaus" and originally aired 21{{nbsp}}April 2011,<ref name="The Vampire Diaries">{{cite web|title=Vampire Diaries Music site |url=http://www.cwtv.com/thecw/the-vampire-diaries-music |work=CWTV.com |access-date=10 May 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110427225331/http://cwtv.com/thecw/the-vampire-diaries-music |archive-date=27 April 2011 }}</ref> and in the show ''[[Don't Trust the B---- in Apartment 23]]'' as well as the sixth episode of [[List of Teen Wolf (2011 TV series) episodes|the first season]] of ''[[Teen Wolf (2011 TV series)|Teen Wolf]]'', titled "Heart Monitor" and originally aired 4{{nbsp}}July 2011.<ref name="Teen Wolf">{{cite web|title=Teen Wolf Music Site|website = [[MTV]]|url=http://www.mtv.com/shows/teen_wolf/episode_music.jhtml?episodeID=180615|access-date=11 July 2011}}</ref> The song was also used in the film ''[[Premium Rush]]'' as one of its soundtracks. The song "Unrequited Love" was used in episode 19 ("The Wheels of Justice"), season four of ''[[The Good Wife]]''.
[[File:Lykke Li @ Paradiso 11.jpg|thumb|right|180px|Li performing at [[Paradiso (Amsterdam)|Paradiso]] in Amsterdam, December 2008]]
Li's song "Melodies and Desires" was featured in the 2010 Australian film ''[[Griff the Invisible]]'' and an edited version of "Get Some" was featured in the [[Catwoman]] trailer for the video game, ''[[Batman: Arkham City]]''. She collaborated with singer Kleerup on the song "Until We Bleed", which was featured on an episode of UK TV series ''[[Misfits (TV series)|Misfits]]'' and an episode of the television series ''[[Ringer (TV series)|Ringer]]''.

Her second album ''[[Wounded Rhymes]]'' was released in 2011. The album was featured on several lists of 2011's best albums, including ''[[Q (magazine)|Q]]'', ''[[Mojo (magazine)|Mojo]]'', ''[[The Observer]]'', ''[[The New York Times]]'', ''[[The Huffington Post]]'' and ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Almassi|first=Hannah|title=Lykke Li Is Signed By Viva Model Management – Music Girls Are Taking Over Fashiondom!|url=http://www.graziadaily.co.uk/fashion/archive/2012/01/11/music-girls-are-taking-over-fashiondom-lykke-li-joins-the-model-singer-club.htm|work=[[Grazia]]|access-date=27 January 2012|date=11 January 2012}}</ref> The 19{{nbsp}}April 2011 episode of ''Glee'' (titled "A Night of Neglect") featured student Tina singing a version of Li's "[[I Follow Rivers]]". On 30{{nbsp}}April 2011, she performed on ''Later... with Jools Holland'' in the UK, playing "Get Some", "Sadness Is a Blessing" and "I Follow Rivers". Li played at the 2011 [[Latitude Festival]], held between 14 and 18{{nbsp}}July 2011 at [[Henham Park]] in Suffolk, England. She appeared with her band on US late night talk show ''[[The Tonight Show with Jay Leno]]'' in August 2011 and on the ''[[Late Show with David Letterman]]'' in November 2011.

Belgian DJ/producer The Magician's remix of "I Follow Rivers" is featured in [[Jacques Audiard]]'s 2012 film ''[[Rust and Bone]]'' and the 2013 [[Palme d'Or]]-winning ''[[Blue Is the Warmest Colour]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rust and Bone (2012) Soundtracks|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2053425/soundtrack|publisher=IMDb|access-date=3 May 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Wolff|first=Spencer|title=Buried in the Sand: The Secret Politics of Blue Is the Warmest Color|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/spencer-wolff/buried-in-the-sand_b_4237379.html|work=HuffPost|access-date=3 May 2014}}</ref> Li contributed to the 2012 compilation "Volym 1" with the track "Come Near" released by the Swedish artist collective and record label [[Ingrid (record label)|INGRID]] where she is a founding member. She also contributed a cover of "[[Silver Springs (song)|Silver Springs]]" to a 2012 [[Fleetwood Mac]] tribute album, which included renditions from [[Best Coast]], [[Marianne Faithfull]], and [[MGMT]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/song-of-the-day/lykke-li-silver-springs-100179 |title=Lykke Li – Silver Springs [Best Fit Premiere] |publisher=The Line Of Best Fit |access-date=16 September 2012}}</ref>

In 2013, she was featured as a guest musician on [[David Lynch]]'s second studio album, ''[[The Big Dream]]'', performing vocals on the lead single, "[[I'm Waiting Here]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/50983-david-lynch-announces-new-album-the-big-dream-shares-track-featuring-lykke-li|title=David Lynch Announces New Album, The Big Dream, Shares Track Featuring Lykke Li|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|last=Minkser|first=Evan|date=3 June 2013|access-date= 3 June 2013}}</ref>

===2014–2017: ''I Never Learn'', acting debut and Liv ===
On 27{{nbsp}}February 2014, Li released the video for the title track of her third studio album, ''[[I Never Learn]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5SrEdAeGj6Y |title=Watch the video of Lykke Li's, "I Never Learn" |via=YouTube|access-date=27 February 2014}}</ref> Following the release, Li released the video for the single "Love Me Like I'm Not Made of Stone" on 4{{nbsp}}March 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D7iLERCEznI|title=Lykke Li – Love Me Like I'm Not Made Of Stone|via=YouTube|access-date=4 March 2014}}</ref> On 10{{nbsp}}April 2014, she released a video for the [[lead single]], "[[No Rest for the Wicked (Lykke Li song)|No Rest for the Wicked]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hh-0y8Qe0Sw |title=Watch the video of Lykke Li's new single, "No Rest For The Wicked" |via=YouTube|access-date=10 April 2014}}</ref> On 15 July 2014, she also released a video for the single "Gunshot".

Li officially released ''I Never Learn'' on 2{{nbsp}}May 2014. Once again her album was featured on several lists of 2014's best albums, including ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'', and [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]], among others. In United States, ''I Never Learn'' debuted at number 29 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], making it Li's highest-peaking album on the chart. Prior to the release, Li released a remix of "No Rest for the Wicked", featuring [[ASAP Rocky]], on 21 April 2014. On 30 May 2014, Li released a remix EP of "No Rest for the Wicked".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/no-rest-for-wicked-remixes/id881986379?ign-mpt=uo%3D4|title=iTunes Music – No Rest For the Wicked (Remixes) – EP by Lykke Li|date=30 May 2014|publisher=iTunes Store|access-date=30 May 2014}}</ref> German electronic DJ [[Robin Schulz]] remixed "No Rest for the Wicked" and the edit was featured in Schulz's debut album ''[[Prayer (Robin Schulz album)|Prayer]]''.{{citation needed|date=February 2017}}

Li made her acting debut in the 2014 Swedish crime film ''Tommy'', directed by [[Tarik Saleh]], and recorded a song for the film, "Du är den ende". She wrote "No One Ever Loved" for the [[The Fault in Our Stars (soundtrack)|soundtrack]] to ''[[The Fault in Our Stars (film)|The Fault in Our Stars]]'', which was released on 19{{nbsp}}May 2014. Li was also featured as a guest musician on [[U2]]'s album ''[[Songs of Innocence (U2 album)|Songs of Innocence]]'', performing vocals on the last track "The Troubles".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/lykke-li-surprise-u2-collaboration-the-troubles-songs-of-innocence-20140911|title=How Lykke Li Wound Up on U2's Secret Album|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=11 May 2017}}</ref>

On 14{{nbsp}}November 2014, Li gained considerable attention from Internet music publications regarding her cover of Drake's "[[Hold On, We're Going Home]]", performed the night prior at London's [[Eventim Apollo]]. On 5{{nbsp}}January 2015, Li posted a picture on her [[Instagram]] account, in which she announced her temporary retirement.

In 2015, Li was featured on [[Emile Haynie]]'s debut album ''[[We Fall]]'' on the track "Come Find Me", which also features [[The xx|Romy Madley Croft]] from [[The xx]]. She also teamed up with [[Yoann Lemoine|Woodkid]] to create a song for the ''[[The Divergent Series: Insurgent – Original Motion Picture Soundtrack|Divergent Series: Insurgent – Original Motion Picture Soundtrack]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Beauchemin|first1=Molly|title=Woodkid and Lykke Li Team for 'Never Let You Down' From Insurgent|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/58765-woodkid-and-lykke-li-team-for-never-let-you-down-from-insurgent/|website=Pitchfork|date=9 March 2015|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref> The song, "Never Let You Down", was released as a single on 9{{nbsp}}March 2015.

On 6{{nbsp}}April 2015, she released a video for "Never Gonna Love Again",<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/watch-lykke-li-crash-in-never-gonna-love-again-video-235135/|title=Watch Lykke Li Crash in 'Never Gonna Love Again' Video|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|date=6 April 2015|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=11 February 2019}}</ref> directed by Philippe Tempelman.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/news/lykke-li-releases-intense-video-for-never-gonna-love-again-47822|title=Lykke Li releases intense video for 'Never Gonna Love Again'|website=AXS|access-date=11 February 2019}}</ref> In September 2016, supergroup [[Liv (band)|Liv]], featuring Lykke Li, released their first single "[[Wings of Love (Liv song)|Wings of Love]]".<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/hear-lykke-li-miike-snow-supergroups-shimmering-debut-song-112448/|title=Hear Lykke Li, Miike Snow Supergroup's Shimmering Debut Song|last=Reed|first=Ryan|date=30 September 2016|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=11 February 2019}}</ref> The music video was directed by Lykke Li.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/lykke-li-wings-of-love-liv-supergroup|title=Lykke Li on Directing the Racy, Carefree Video for "Wings of Love"|website=Vogue|date=13 October 2016|access-date=11 February 2019}}</ref>

===2018–2019: ''So Sad So Sexy'' ===
Li released her fourth studio album ''[[So Sad So Sexy]]'' on 8 June 2018 through [[RCA Records|RCA]].<ref name="SSSS">{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/lykke-li-announces-new-album-shares-2-new-songs-listen/|title=Lykke Li Announces New Album, Shares 2 New Songs: Listen|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|last1=Kim|first1=Michelle|last2=Strauss|first2=Matthew|date=19 April 2018|access-date=19 April 2018}}</ref> Along with the announcement, Li released the first two singles, "Deep End" and "Hard Rain".<ref name="SSSS"/>

In April 2019, she sang lead vocals and appeared in the video for [[Mark Ronson]]'s "[[Late Night Feelings]]," the title track from [[Late Night Feelings|the album of the same name]].

In July 2019, Li released the EP ''Still Sad Still Sexy'', featuring 4 remixes from ''[[So Sad So Sexy]]'' and two new songs.<ref>{{Citation|title=still sad still sexy|date=26 July 2019|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/40lqihqmkBdwM0QczKnoME|access-date=5 August 2019}}</ref>

===2020–present: Upcoming fifth studio album ===
In a 2019 interview speaking about her fifth studio album, Li told NME ”I think, maybe to everyone’s disappointment, I’m going to really scale it down and back and slow it down,”(...)“It’ll be more like soul music. It’ll still be sad, and still be sexy.”<ref>{{Citation|title=nme interview fifth album|date=11 July 2019|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/lykke-li-mad-cool-2019-mezcal-mark-ronson-courtney-love-soulful-new-album-2527293|access-date=2 July 2021}}</ref>

Li made her return to music releasing 'BRON', a song sung entirely in Swedish, in October 2020.<ref>{{Citation|title=bron|date=9 October 2020|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/lykke-li-returns-with-new-single-bron-2777290|access-date=2 July 2021}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Li has cited [[Neil Young]], [[the Shangri-Las]], [[This Mortal Coil]], [[the Beatles]], and [[The Rolling Stones]] as influences, stating, "They aren't pop anymore by today's standards, but they were."<ref>{{cite web|title=Lykke Minded – Lykke Li Interview|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/feature/lykke-minded-lykke-li-interview|access-date=22 November 2013|date=14 February 2011}}</ref> Other influences include [[the Velvet Underground]], [[Leonard Cohen]], and [[Beach House]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aux.tv/2011/01/lykke-lis-new-songs-influenced-by-neil-youngs-harvest-moon/|title=Lykke Li's new songs influenced by Neil Young's 'Harvest Moon'  |work=AUX.TV|access-date=9 May 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160313083825/http://www.aux.tv/2011/01/lykke-lis-new-songs-influenced-by-neil-youngs-harvest-moon/|archive-date=13 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ameliasmagazine.com/music/an-interview-with-swedish-musician-lykke-li/2010/06/08/|title=Amelia's Magazine – An interview with Swedish musician Lykke Li|work=ameliasmagazine.com}}</ref>

Li possesses the vocal range of a [[soprano]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2011/apr/17/lykke-li-review-kitty-empire|title=Lykke Li – review|work=The Guardian|date=17 April 2011|access-date=26 July 2012|first=Kitty|last=Empire}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Of her practice of [[transcendental meditation technique|transcendental meditation]], Li said in 2014: "It's really interesting from a creative point of view. Before, I could only write a few sentences and I'd have to take a break for a few days before I could go back to it. All of a sudden, I could write and finish a song in one go – verse, verse, verse, chorus, the lot. And that would happen with song after song. Finally, I unlocked the gate."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carroll|first1=Jim|title=Lykke Li: 'I had the blues and that was a good thing'|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/lykke-li-i-had-the-blues-and-that-was-a-good-thing-1.1812714?page=2|newspaper=The Irish Times|access-date=30 June 2014|quote=One of the reasons Li cites for her heightened state of creativity of late is a growing interest in mindfulness and transcendental meditation}}</ref>

On 30{{nbsp}}October 2015, Li posted a photo on Instagram announcing her pregnancy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6746184/lykke-li-pregnant-photo|title=Lykke Li Expecting Her First Baby, Posts Pregnant Photo|date=30 October 2015|work=Billboard}}</ref> On 12 February 2016, she announced the birth of her son Dion, whose father is musician [[Jeff Bhasker]], on Facebook.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://shopdoen.com/blogs/journal/112794117-doen-doe-welcome-to-motherhood-lykke-li|title=Dôen + Doe: welcome to motherhood Lykke Li|website=DÔEN {{!}} www.shopdoen.com|access-date=15 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BBxm1C3L8iA/|title=Lykke Li on Instagram: "HAPPY VALENTINES DAY MY LOVE AND BABY DADDY !! @jeffbhasker @byruvan"|website=Instagram|access-date=15 March 2016}}</ref> Her mother died three weeks after Dion was born; she says that her album ''so sad so sexy'' was born out of this difficult time in her life.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-10-17|title=We Speak To Lykke Li In The Autumn Issue {{!}} Wonderland Magazine|url=http://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2019/10/17/lykke-li-interview-autumn-19-issue/|access-date=2020-12-23|website=Wonderland|language=en-US}}</ref>
