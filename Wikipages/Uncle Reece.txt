'''Maurice Hicks Jr.''' (born February 14, 1984), who goes by the stage name '''Uncle Reece''', is an American [[Christian hip hop]] musician. He released his first album, 2014's ''Bold'', with Obed Music Group. This was a ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' chart breakthrough release.

==Early life==
Uncle Reece was born Maurice Hicks Jr. on February 14, 1984, in [[Savannah, Georgia]],<ref name="PH">{{cite web|title=Introducing: Uncle Reece|url=http://www.praizhouse.com/2012/06/introducing-uncle-reece.html|first=Kayode|last=Martins-Olowo|publisher=PraizHouse|access-date=March 8, 2015|date=June 19, 2012}}</ref> to a father who served in the military, Maurice Hicks Sr. and his mother Cecilia Anita Hicks. He loves music.<ref name="PH"/> He graduated from [[Florida State University]] with an Associate degree in Mathematics.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Future: Uncle Reece|url=http://www.theshadowleague.com/articles/the-future-uncle-reece|first=Kadeem|last=Lundy|publisher=The Shadow League|access-date=March 8, 2015|date=June 10, 2014}}</ref>

==Music career==
His music career started in 2012, with the release of "Until I Pass Out" single on March 1 of that year,<ref name="NRTS">{{cite web|title=Until I Pass Out – Single by Uncle Reece |url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=11843|author=New Release Tuesday|publisher=New Release Tuesday|access-date=March 8, 2015|date=March 1, 2012}}</ref> and it charted at No. 15 on the Hot Gospel Songs chart by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''.<ref name="AMA">{{cite web|title=Uncle Reece: Awards|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/uncle-reece-mn0002537629/awards|author=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref> His second single, "I Cant' Help Myself", would chart at No. 19 on that chart and at No. 48 on the [[Hot Christian Songs]]. His first album, ''Bold'', came out on June 3, 2014 on his own label Obed Music.<ref name="NRTA">{{cite web|title=Bold by Uncle Reece|url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=14080|author=New Release Tuesday|publisher=New Release Tuesday|access-date=March 8, 2015|date=June 3, 2014}}</ref> This would chart on two ''Billboard'' charts, on the [[Christian Albums]] at No. 30 and on the [[Top Gospel Albums]] at No. 9.<ref name="AMA"/> The album received a three and a half star out of five review by Dwayne Lacy of New Release Tuesday,<ref name="NRTR">{{cite web|title=An |url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/userprofile_reviewssinglepost.php?review_id=22388&user_id=43067|first=Dwayne|last=Lacy|publisher=New Release Tuesday|access-date=March 8, 2015|date=June 11, 2014}}</ref> and it comes "highly recommend[ed]" from Daniel Cody at Wade-O Radio.<ref name="WADEO">{{cite web|title=Album Review: Uncle Reece – 'Bold'|url=http://wadeoradio.com/album-review-uncle-reece-bold/|publisher=Wade-O Radio|first=Daniel|last=Cody|access-date=March 8, 2015 |date=May 29, 2014}}</ref> Rea Melissa Davis, writes for [[AllHipHop]], how "Uncle Reece is quite the musical melting pot. With ''BOLD'' ,Uncle Reece is not confined to the church, and there are no limits or boundaries to the way he can express his praise."<ref name="AHH">{{cite web|title=Uncle Reece Chooses to Be BOLD|url=http://allhiphop.com/2014/05/19/uncle-reece-chooses-to-be-bold/|publisher=[[AllHipHop]]|first=Rea Melissa|last=Davis|access-date=March 8, 2015|date=May 19, 2014}}</ref> He is nominated at the [[Stellar Awards|30th Stellar Awards]] for the Rap Hip Hop Gospel CD of the Year and New Artist of the Year.<ref name="STELLAR">{{cite web|title=2015 N0MINEES: 30th Stellar Awards|url=http://www.thestellarawards.com/nominees.html|author=Stellar Awards|publisher=Central City Productions|access-date=March 8, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121015054243/http://www.thestellarawards.com/nominees.html|archive-date=October 15, 2012}}</ref>
