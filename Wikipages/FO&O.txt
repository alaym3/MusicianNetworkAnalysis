'''FO&O''' is a Swedish pop [[boy band]] made up of [[Oscar Enestad]], [[Omar Rudberg]] and [[Felix Sandman]].<ref name=aftonbladet>{{cite news|url=http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/musik/rockbjornen/justinbieber/article16639983.ab|last=Nilsson |first=Christoffer|title=Okända svenskarna är Justin Biebers förband - Genomför sitt första riktiga spelning i Globen|work=Aftonbladet|date=21 April 2013|language=sv}}</ref> Originally established in October 2013 under the name '''the Fooo''', the group changed its name to '''the Fooo Conspiracy''' in September 2014. Founding member Oscar "Olly" Molander left the group in November 2016, and the group changed its name for a third time in February 2017, to FO&O.

==Career==

===2013–2015: ''Off the Grid''===
{{Main|Off the Grid (The Fooo album)}}
In 2013, the band started out doing street performances in the streets of [[Stockholm]] while uploading the footage onto [[YouTube]] for their fans (called 'Foooers') to see. During 2013, the band made several television performances, at the gala Tillsammans för Världens Barn,<ref name=artipelag>{{cite news|url=http://www.artipelag.se/sv/blogg/svt-s%C3%A4nder-tillsammans-f%C3%B6r-v%C3%A4rlden-barn-fr%C3%A5n-artipelag |title=SVT sănder tillsammans för Världen barn frän Artipelag |publisher=Artipelag |date=25 September 2013 |access-date=10 November 2013 |language=sv |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131109221921/http://www.artipelag.se/sv/blogg/svt-s%C3%A4nder-tillsammans-f%C3%B6r-v%C3%A4rlden-barn-fr%C3%A5n-artipelag |archive-date=9 November 2013 }}</ref> and at the ''[[Idol 2013]]'' finale in Globen.<ref name=expressen>{{cite news|url=http://www.expressen.se/noje/forsta-gastartisten-till-finalen-av-idol-klar/|last=Engman |first=Pascal |title=Första gästartisten till finalen av "Idol" klar|work=Expressen|access-date=10 November 2013|language=sv}}</ref> The band became known after Canadian singer [[Justin Bieber]] and his manager saw a video clip of the band on YouTube.<ref name=aftonbladet/><ref name=svtregionalt>{{cite news|url=http://www.svt.se/nyheter/regionalt/abc/forsta-spelningen-forband-till-justin-bieber |last1=Luik |first1=Colette van |last2=Arborén |first2=Henrik |title=Första spelningen: förband till Justin Bieber |publisher=SVT Regionalt |date=22 April 2013 |access-date=10 November 2013 |language=sv |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131109221447/http://www.svt.se/nyheter/regionalt/abc/forsta-spelningen-forband-till-justin-bieber |archive-date=9 November 2013 }}</ref> Bieber's manager called the Fooo's management and asked if the band would want to become the opening act for Bieber's concert in [[Globen]] in April 2013 which the band accepted.<ref name=alltomstockholm>{{cite news|url=http://www.alltomstockholm.se/klubbkonsert/article4063719.aos |title=Upptäcktes av Bieber - spelar för 48 000 i Globen |last=Carlson |first=Joakim |publisher=AlltomStockholm |date=23 April 2013 |access-date=10 November 2013 |language=sv |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131109222119/http://www.alltomstockholm.se/klubbkonsert/article4063719.aos |archive-date=9 November 2013 }}</ref> The Fooo's first charting single was "[[Build a Girl]]", which reached number 41 on [[Sverigetopplistan]], the official Swedish singles chart. The song also charted on Digilistanchart reaching number 3.<ref name=sverigeradio>{{cite news|url=http://sverigesradio.se/sida/topplista.aspx?programid=2697&ListId=3497|title=Sverigeradio Digilistan - 1 September 2013|publisher=Sverigeradio|date=1 September 2013|access-date=10 November 2013|language=sv}}</ref> The Fooo's first EP, the self-titled ''[[Build a Girl|The Fooo]]'', was released on 16 August 2013 by The Artist House. They won the Swedish [[Grammis]] award "Innovator of the Year". Their debut studio album ''[[Off the Grid (The Fooo album)|Off the Grid]]'' topped the official Swedish Albums Chart in its first week of release on 2 April 2014. On 27 August 2014, they released their second extended play, ''[[Conspiration]]''. The EP peaked at number 19 on the [[Sverigetopplistan|Swedish Albums Chart]]. They released their third extended play, ''Serenade'' in the United States on 30 October 2014. They released ''[[Coordinates (EP)|Coordinates]]'' as their fourth extended play on 7 January 2015. The EP peaked at number 33 on the Swedish Albums Chart. "Wild Hearts" was released as the lead single from the EP on 19 January 2015. They released the single "[[Run with Us (The Fooo Conspiracy song)|Run with Us]]" on 5 June 2015, followed by the single "[[Jimi Hendrix (song)|Jimi Hendrix]]" on 4 December 2015.

===2016–2017: Melodifestivalen and ''FO&O''===
On 29 January 2016, they released the single "[[My Girl (The Fooo Conspiracy song)|My Girl]]". The song peaked at number 58 on the [[Sverigetopplistan|Swedish Singles Chart]]. It was followed by the single "[[Summer Love (The Fooo Conspiracy song)|Summer Love]]", released on 29 April 2016 and peaking at number 54 on the Swedish Singles Chart. They re-released "My Girl" on 17 June 2016, featuring vocals from [[Danny Saucedo]]. On 28 October 2016, they released the single "[[Who Doesn't Love Love]]". In November 2016, Oscar "Olly" Molander left the band. On 30 November 2016, the Fooo Conspiracy was announced as one of the 28 acts to compete in [[Melodifestivalen 2017]] with the song "[[Gotta Thing About You]]", under the new name of FO&O. They qualified to "andra chansen" from the third semi-final and later qualified for the final, winning against [[De Vet Du]]'s "Roadtrip". They placed eleventh in the final.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/robin-bengtsson-med-i-can-t-go-on-ar-vinnaren-av-melodifestivalen-2017|title=Robin Bengtsson med ”I Can't Go On” är vinnaren av Melodifestivalen 2017|last=Petersson|first=Emma|work=svt.se|access-date=2017-05-04|language=sv}}</ref>

FO&O announced the release of their [[FO&O (album)|self-titled album]] on social media, including "[[Gotta Thing About You]]" and new single "[[So So Good]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BTYpq60gjfm/|title=Instagram post by FO&O • Apr 27, 2017 at 10:18am UTC|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2017-05-04}}</ref>

In September 2017, after speculation in the Swedish press about the band's split, the band confirmed that they were on hiatus and were taking time to pursue solo projects.<ref name="Hiatus">{{cite web|url=https://escxtra.com/2017/09/22/foo-go-on-a-hiatus/|title=FO&O go on a hiatus|website=ESCxtra|last=Jumawan|first=Tim|date=22 September 2017|access-date=28 December 2020}}</ref>
