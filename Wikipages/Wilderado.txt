'''Wilderado''' is an American [[indie folk]] band originally from [[Tulsa, Oklahoma]], United States, and based in [[Los Angeles]] [[California]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|title=EP6 Cocktails with Bright Antenna - Wilderado|date=2019-06-10|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dv6GkkB5KNo&t=6s|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=2019-07-22}}</ref> The band was first signed to [[Iamsound Records]], but is now signed to [[Bright Antenna Records]].<ref name="billboard">{{cite web |last1=Olivier |first1=Bobby |title=The Band You Don't Know That You Need to See at Each of the Big Summer Festivals |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/summer-festivals-2017-bands-you-dont-know |publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=7 June 2019}}</ref><ref name="broadwayworld">{{cite web |title=Wilderado Releases New EP Favors + New Tour Dates |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Wilderado-Releases-New-EP-Favors-New-Tour-Dates-20180525 |publisher=[[BroadwayWorld]] |accessdate=7 June 2019}}</ref>

Wilderado's Max Rainer and Tyler Wimpee met through mutual friends in college and started writing songs together in their fifth year after most of their friends had left.<ref name=":0" />

In 2016, the band released their first EP, ''Misty Shrub''.<ref name="onestowatch">{{cite web |title=Ones To Watch Presents: Wilderado |url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/ones-to-watch-presents-wilderado |publisher=[[Ones to Watch]] |accessdate=7 June 2019}}</ref> On July 22, 2016, the band released their second EP titled ''Latigo'' which amassed over 18 million streams on [[Spotify]].<ref name="consequenceofsound">{{cite web |last1=Havens |first1=Lyndsey |title=Wilderado premiere glorious new track "Morning Light" — listen |url=https://citywinery.com/chicago/x-6-26-19.html |publisher=[[Consequence of Sound]] |accessdate=7 June 2019}}</ref> On the back of ''Latigo,'' Wilderado spent the next year touring with acts such as [[Band of Horses]], [[Rainbow Kitten Surprise]], [[Lindsey Buckingham]], and [[Judah & the Lion|Judah & The Lion]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Wilderado-Premieres-New-Single-SUREFIRE-With-Consequence-of-Sound-20190718|title=Wilderado Premieres New Single SUREFIRE With Consequence of Sound|author=BWW News Desk|website=BroadwayWorld.com}}</ref>

In 2018, Wilderado released their third EP titled ''Favors'' to warm critical and fan acclaim.<ref name="broadwayworld" />

In July 2019, Wilderado released the single ''Surefire''. The song was inspired by the poem “A Gradual Canticle for Augustine” by [[Tabitha King]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2019/07/wilderado-surefire-origins-stream/|title=Wilderado take a shot at the Origins of their new song, "Surefire": Stream|website=Consequenceofsound.net|date=July 18, 2019}}</ref> ''Surefire'' was named one of the "5 Songs You Need to Hear Right Now" by [[Sirius XM Satellite Radio|Sirius XM]]'s Spectrum.

In 2020, the band released 3 singles, ''CFS'', ''Revenant'', and ''Take Some Time'', remixed by [[RJD2]], Emancipator, Kyle McEvoy, & by Gus of [[Alt-J]].

In 2021, the band released their newest single, ''Head Right''.

In October, 2021, the band released their debut album self-titled Wilderado.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Major|first=Michael|date=24 September 2021|title=Wilderado Releases 'Mr. Major' Single From Debut Album|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Wilderado-Releases-Mr-Major-Single-From-Debut-Album-20210924|url-status=live|access-date=27 October 2021|website=Broadway World}}</ref>

==Band name==
The current name is an altered version of the name of a town in Texas called [[Wildorado, Texas|Wildorado]], which the band came across while driving late at night.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bedian |first1=Knar Bedian |title=Interview: Wilderado |url=https://soundofboston.com/interview-wilderado/ |website=Sound of Boston |access-date=1 March 2021 |date=1 June 2017}}</ref>
