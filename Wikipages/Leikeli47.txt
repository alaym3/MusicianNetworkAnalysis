'''Leikeli47''', also known as '''Leikeli''', is an American musician signed to [[RCA Records]].<ref name=":1" /> In 2018, she released her second full-length album, ''Acrylic.''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/acrylic/1441710639|title=Acrylic by Leikeli47|author=|date=|publisher=|accessdate=March 5, 2019|via=itunes.apple.com}}</ref> She is known for concealing her face with a [[bandana]] or [[Balaclava (clothing)|balaclava]] at all performances and professional appearances.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.culturedmag.com/leikeli47/|title=Leikeli47 Refashions Anonymity in Her Own Image|date=2019-06-18|website=Cultured Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-28}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Leikeli was raised in both [[Virginia]] and [[New York (state)|New York]], primarily in the [[Brooklyn]] neighborhoods of [[Brownsville, Brooklyn|Brownsville]] and [[Bedford–Stuyvesant, Brooklyn|Bedford–Stuyvesant]].<ref name="vice-April2015">{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/ryzvey/leikeli47-interview-profile-2015|title=Invincible Eyes: What's Beneath Leikeli47's Mask?|last1=Kramer|first1=Kyle|website=Vice|accessdate=February 10, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> She described herself as having been a shy child who was interested in becoming a musician from a young age. She cites [[Michael Jackson]] and [[N.E.R.D.]] as some of her favorite performers.<ref name=":1" />

== Career ==
Prior to being signed to RCA Records, Leikeli released a mixtape in 2010 titled ''LK-47''.<ref name=":4">{{cite web|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2018-11-15-leikeli47-interview-acrylic|title=Leikeli47 is Truth, Strength, and Love Personified|last=Chesman|first=Donna-Claire|date=|website=DJBooth|access-date=March 5, 2019}}</ref> This was followed by a string of releases including the sequel mixtape ''LK-47 Pt. II'' in 2014<ref name="vice-April2015" /> and a self-titled EP in 2015, ''Leikeli47''.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|title=Leikeli47|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/3160394-Leikeli47|access-date=2020-08-02|website=Discogs|language=en}}</ref> In 2015, Leikeli's song "Fuck the Summer Up" was included on [[Jay-Z]]'s inaugural [[Tidal (service)|Tidal]] playlist called ''F*ck The Summer Up''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tidal.com/browse/playlist/48653a01-4968-44c3-9d0c-22d902e3e83c|title=JAY-Z: F**k the Summer Up|author=|date=|website=TIDAL Browse - High Fidelity Music Streaming|accessdate=March 5, 2019}}</ref>

Leikeli's first full-length debut, ''Wash & Set'', was released on September 8, 2017.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/leikeli47-is-ready-to-give-hip-hop-a-new-look/|title=Leikeli47 Is Ready to Give Hip-Hop a New Look|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=February 9, 2019}}</ref> It was received positively by critics. ''[[Afropunk Festival|Afropunk.com]]'' wrote, "the album showcases Leikeli47’s distinctively hard-hitting yet playful lyricism, genre-bending musicianship, and enigmatic character."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://afropunk.com/2017/09/brooklyn-rapper-leikeli47-drops-breathtaking-debut-album-new-video-m-l-k/|title=Brooklyn rapper Leikeli47 drops breathtaking debut album and new video "M I L K"|date=September 11, 2017|website=AFROPUNK|language=en-US|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref> Darryl Robertson wrote for ''[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]'', "the dynamic, and featureless, effort is loaded with creativity, vigor and the power of her own feminism. It's evident that Leikeli is aiming to break rules and push boundaries hip-hop."<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2017/09/leikeli47-wash-set|title=Leikeli47 Brings Creativity And Vigor On Her Debut Album, 'Wash & Set'|date=September 8, 2017|website=Vibe|language=en|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref>

''Acrylic'' was released on November 15, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2018/10/leikeli47-release-date-new-album-and-three-new-songs/|title=Leikeli47 Shares Release Date for New Album and Three New Song|last=Coleman II|first=C. Vernon|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=February 9, 2019}}</ref> The title was inspired by her mixture of southern, Caribbean, and city roots.<ref name=":0" /> The first single on the album, "Girl Blunt", was featured on the third season of ''[[Insecure (TV series)|Insecure]]'' <ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/leikeli47-acrylic/|title=Leikeli47: Acrylic|last=Pelly|first=Jenn|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=February 9, 2019}}</ref> and on [[The_Wilds_(TV_series)]] Season 1 Episode 8 Day Sixteen.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/the-wilds/season-1/103096|title=Tune Find Com The Wilds}}</ref> The album received positive critical reviews. Jenn Pelly wrote at [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork.com]]: "Her flow is always gripping, her details novelistic: An aunt sells candy from a windowpane, an extension cord snakes across a hallway, and the lights flicker in an E train tunnel."<ref name=":2" /> Dhruva Balram wrote for ''[[NME]]'', "At times, and especially on ‘Iron Mike’, Leikeli47 sounds like the best of [[M.I.A. (rapper)|M.I.A]], [[Cardi B]] and [[Kamaiyah]] rolled into one."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/leikeli47-acrylic-review|title=Leikeli47 – 'Acrylic' review|last=Balram|first=Dhruva|website=NME|language=en-US|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref> ''Acrylic'' was named one of [[NPR]]'s 50 Best Albums of 2018, as well as other lists including ''[[Paper Magazine]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.papermag.com/top-25-albums-2018-2623655747.html|title=PAPER's Top 20 Albums of 2018|author=|date=December 17, 2018|website=PAPER|access-date=March 5, 2019}}</ref> ''Esquire'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.esquire.com/entertainment/music/g19100722/best-rap-songs-2018/|title=The Best Hip-Hop Songs of 2018|last=Miller|first=Matt|date=November 28, 2018|website=Esquire|access-date=March 5, 2019}}</ref> and ''Forbes''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/garysuarez/2018/12/22/10-rap-albums-2018-you-missed/|title=10 Great Hip-Hop Albums From 2018 You May Have Missed|last=Suarez|first=Gary|date=|website=Forbes|access-date=March 5, 2019}}</ref> ''Acrylic'' is the second part of a planned music trilogy that Leikeli has developed, called the "Beauty Series"''.<ref name=":4" /><ref name=":0" />''

She stated that the next installment to the "Beauty Series" will be called ''Shape Up''.<ref name=":0" /> About the theme of beauty salons, she told ''[[The Fader]]'', "They're safe havens for all of us — all women, but especially the African-American community. I wanted to create a body of work that spoke to not looking like what you actually are going through."<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/20/leikeli47-acrylic-interview|title=Leikeli47's love letter to black sacred spaces|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref> She has stated that she seeks to normalize Black beauty standards, such as decorated fingernails.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/gy7qz7/leikeli47-new-album-acrylic-interview|title=An Interview in a Nail Salon with Leikeli47 About Her New Album 'Acrylic'|last1=Corry|first1=Kristin|last2=Friedlander|first2=Emilie|date=November 16, 2018|website=Noisey|language=en-US|access-date=February 10, 2019|last3=Ozzi|first3=Dan}}</ref>

Leikeli performed with [[Pharrell]] during his I Am Other set at 2017's Complexcon,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2017/11/pharrell-migos-2-chainz-lil-uzi-vert-complexcon|title=Watch Special Guests 2 Chainz, Lil Uzi Vert, and Migos Surprise the Pigeons & Planes Stage at ComplexCon|author=|date=|website=Complex|accessdate=March 5, 2019}}</ref> as well as Natalie Portman's [[Boston Calling Music Festival|Boston Calling]] set in 2018, held at Portman's alma mater Harvard University.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/st-vincent-zola-jesus-to-join-natalie-portman-at-boston-calling-2018/|title=St. Vincent, Zola Jesus to Join Natalie Portman at Boston Calling 2018|author=|date=|website=Pitchfork|accessdate=March 5, 2019}}</ref>

Her first headlined tour began in March 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Invincible Eyes: What's Beneath Leikeli47's Mask?|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/ryzvey/leikeli47-interview-profile-2015|access-date=2020-08-02|website=www.vice.com|language=en}}</ref>

== Persona ==
Leikeli is known for being private and has not released her legal name or performed without her face covered.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Bacher|first1=Danielle|date=20 September 2016|title=Leikeli47 On Using Music as Survival|url=http://www.papermag.com/leikeli47-beautiful-people-2010557145.html|journal=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|language=en}}</ref> In June 2019, she told ''Cultured Magazine'', “Being black, we gotta learn how to wear a mask early.”<ref name=":0" />

According to an interview with ''[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]'', she conceals her face because "I feel like the ''Dark Knight'', or one of those superheroes, or Superman… the mask, it represents freedom. I'm free with it on."<ref name=":3" />
