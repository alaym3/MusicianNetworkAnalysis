'''Will Varley''' (born 7 March 1987) is an English singer-songwriter, musician and visual artist. He is one of the founding members of the arts collective Smugglers Records. Since self releasing his debut album ''Advert Soundtracks'' in 2011 he has toured extensively across the world as a headline act and opened for the likes of [[Billy Bragg]], [[Valerie June]], [[The Dead South]] and [[The Proclaimers]]. He has headlined venues including [[Union Chapel, Islington|Union Chapel]] and [[Shepherd's Bush Empire]]. Will’s songs have been covered by acts as diverse as [[The Pretty Things]] and [[Frank Turner]]. In recent years Will has co-written songs with numerous artists including Skin of [[Skunk Anansie]] and [[Shae Dupuy]]. His sixth studio album ''The Hole Around My Head'' was released in 2021.

==History==
Will Varley began playing open mic nights in London in the early 2000s, setting up his own nights in the Grey Horse, Kingston with friends Tom Farrer and Nick Marks. He was a regular at numerous venues including [[The Half Moon, Putney]] and the [[12 Bar Club]] and would often play different open mics seven nights a week. During this time he shared stages with acts including [[Jamie T]], [[Bastille (band)|Bastille]] and [[The Vaccines]]. In 2010 he moved to Kent and set up Smugglers Records with the band [[Cocos Lovers]].<ref>{{cite web|title=willvarley.com/history|url=http://www.willvarley.com/history/|work=Official Biography}}</ref> He released his debut album ''Advert Soundtracks'' through his own label in 2011 and toured the album on foot.<ref>{{cite web|title=Radiant: 21st Century Troubadour – Will Varley|url=http://centmagazine.co.uk/radiant-21st-century-troubadour-will-varley/6733|work=Cent Magazine}}</ref>

In September 2013 his second album ''As The Crow Flies'' was released and garnered positive reviews &<ref>{{cite journal|last=Thompson|first=Liam|title=Will Varley As The Crow Flies Review|journal=fRoots Magazine|date=December 2013|issue=366|page=65}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Hirst|first=Gemma|title=Will Varley Review|url=http://www.fatea-records.co.uk/magazine/2013/WillVarley.html|work=Fatea}}</ref> was described as "Excellent" by ''Q'' magazine.<ref>{{cite journal|journal=Q|title=As The Crow Flies|issue=April 2014|pages=106}}</ref> The album received airplay on BBC Radio 3, Radio 6 & XFM.<ref>{{cite web|title=Siba in Session|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b03t0dfc|work=World on 3|publisher=BBC}}</ref>{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}<!-- WP:RS needed; removed link to WP:SPS (Twitter, FB, WP)-->

In an interview on BBC Introducing in March 2014, Varley announced he would be embarking on another walking tour in May, this time travelling the entire south coast of England.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p001d7pg/episodes/player|title=BBC Radio Cambridgeshire - BBC Music Introducing in Cambridgeshire - Available now|website=BBC|accessdate=17 March 2019}}</ref> ''The Rambling Tour'' saw Varley walk almost 500 miles with a guitar on his back, playing 24 shows along the way.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rambling Tour Website|url=http://www.theramblingtour.com|website=Theramblingtour.com}}</ref>

During an interview with [[Steve Lamacq]] on BBC6 Varley said he was working on new material and was aiming to release a third album in early 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Steve Lamacq's Roundtable|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b044zcp8|work=29/05/2014}}</ref>

On 29 March 2015 Varley signed a record deal with [[Xtra Mile Recordings]] while backstage at [[The Royal Albert Hall]] shortly before taking to the stage to open for [[Frank Turner]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://xmrstreetteam.tumblr.com/post/115031919369/right-before-taking-the-stage-at-royal-albert|title=XMR Street Team • Right before taking the stage at Royal Albert...|website=XMR Street Team}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://life.royalalberthall.com/2015/04/01/pictures-and-setlists-frank-turner-concludes-the-2015-teenage-cancer-trust-season/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=10 June 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150610070040/http://life.royalalberthall.com/2015/04/01/pictures-and-setlists-frank-turner-concludes-the-2015-teenage-cancer-trust-season/ |archive-date=10 June 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref> A new digital only EP ''Will Varley Live at The Lighthouse'' was released in May 2015 to coincide with Varley's spring tour of the UK. The EP reached number 19 in the iTunes singer/songwriter chart{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}<!-- WP:RS needed; removed link to WP:SPS (Twitter, FB, WP)--> and national radio play from XFM's John Kennedy.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.xfm.co.uk/playlist/2015/may/7/xposure-with-john-kennedy/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=13 May 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518090014/http://www.xfm.co.uk/playlist/2015/may/7/xposure-with-john-kennedy/ |archive-date=18 May 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Soon after the record was released it was announced that Varley would be supporting [[The Proclaimers]] on thirteen dates across the UK.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.proclaimers.co.uk/cpt_shows/bournemouth-pavilion/|title=Bournemouth Pavilion|website=Proclaimers.co.uk|accessdate=17 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.willvarley.com/uk-support-tour-with-the-proclaimers/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=13 May 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518103444/http://www.willvarley.com/uk-support-tour-with-the-proclaimers/ |archive-date=18 May 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Varley's third album ''Postcards from Ursa Minor'' was released on Xtra Mile Recordings on 30 October 2015. ''The Independent'' described it as a "gem of a record".{{citation needed|date=March 2019}} The album reached number 11 in the Official Independent Album Breakers Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/independent-albums-breakers-chart/20151106/255/|title=Official Independent Album Breakers Chart Top 20 - Official Charts Company|website=Officialcharts.com}}</ref> The album's lead single, "Seize the Night", was played on BBC Radio 1.<ref name="bbc.co.uk">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b08vqdhv|title=BBC Radio 1 - Huw Stephens, Independents' Day Takeover|website=BBC}}</ref> The song has since been streamed almost 6 million times on Spotify. It was later included on NPR's Austin 100 list for the annual SXSW festival.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2016/03/01/468352361/the-austin-100-a-sxsw-2016-mix|title=The Austin 100: A SXSW 2016 Mix|website=Npr.org}}</ref>

Following the album release, Varley toured the UK and Germany with [[Frank Turner]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://platform-online.net/2016/02/i-think-he-was-probably-drunk-will-varley-chats-about-songwriting-his-new-album-and-touring-with-frank-turner/|title=Welcome platform-online.net - Justhost.com|website=Platform-online.net}}</ref> and shortly afterwards began his own headline tour of the UK, finishing with a sell-out show at London's [[Scala (club)|Scala]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.folkandhoney.co.uk/london/gig-review-will-varley-at-scala-n60/|title=Gig Review:k Will Varley at Scala - Folk and Honey|website=Folkandhoney.co.uk}}</ref> A week later he began a tour of North America supporting [[Skinny Lister]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://newnoisemagazine.com/xtra-mile-announces-spring-2016-tour-with-frank-turner-skinny-lister-beans-on-toast-will-varley/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=20 April 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160430030148/http://newnoisemagazine.com/xtra-mile-announces-spring-2016-tour-with-frank-turner-skinny-lister-beans-on-toast-will-varley/ |archive-date=30 April 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The tour began with a show on the Salty Dog Cruise from Miami to The Bahamas curated by [[Flogging Molly]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.common-tales.com/2016/03/rockpalast-zeigt-flogging-mollys-salty-dog-cruise-2016/2714|title=Rockpalast zeigt Flogging Molly's Salty Dog Cruise 2016|date=24 March 2016|website=Common-tales.com}}</ref>

In April 2016, Varley announced he would be doing a further twenty-five shows with [[The Proclaimers]] over the summer,<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.willvarley.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/03/Will-Varley-ProclaimersA5-2.jpg |title=Archived copy |access-date=20 April 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160509025451/http://www.willvarley.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/03/Will-Varley-ProclaimersA5-2.jpg |archive-date=9 May 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and three shows opening for [[Billy Bragg]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.picturedrome.net/tickets/Billy-Bragg/20160601.htm|title=Billy Bragg|website=Picturedrome.net}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nnfestival.org.uk/festival/contemporary_music/billy-bragg|title=We're recruiting! - Norfolk & Norwich Festival|website=Norfolk & Norwich Festival|accessdate=17 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.willvarley.com/shows-with-billy-bragg/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=20 April 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160509025437/http://www.willvarley.com/shows-with-billy-bragg/ |archive-date=9 May 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He also appeared at numerous UK festivals including [[Bestival]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.bestival.net/news/the-future-of-new-music-invaders-of-the-future |title=Archived copy |access-date=20 April 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160427010649/http://www.bestival.net/news/the-future-of-new-music-invaders-of-the-future |archive-date=27 April 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> [[Cambridge Folk Festival]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cambridgelivetrust.co.uk/folk-festival/line-up|title=2019 LINE UP - Cambridge Live|website=Cambridgelivetrust.co.uk}}</ref> and [[Glastonbury Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/LF16-953.png|format=PNG|title=Photographic image of Festival poster|website=Glastonburyfestivals.co.uk|accessdate=17 March 2019}}</ref>

In September it was announced that ''Kingsdown Sundown,'' Varley's 4th studio album, would be released on 4 November 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.1.xtramilerecordings.com/news/2016/9/9/will-varley-new-album-kingsdown-sundown-4-november-2016-watch-to-build-a-wall-video-november-uk-headline-tour|title=WILL VARLEY NEW ALBUM 'KINGSDOWN SUNDOWN' OUT 4 NOVEMBER 2016 // WATCH 'TO BUILD A WALL' VIDEO // NOVEMBER UK HEADLINE TOUR|website=Xtra Mile Recordings}}</ref> The album reached number 26 in the Official Independent Album Charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/independent-albums-chart/20161111/131/|title=Official Independent Albums Chart Top 50 - Official Charts Company|website=Officialcharts.com}}</ref> Over the Autumn Varley embarked on further tours of North America, Ireland, Germany and the UK including a sold-out show at [[Union Chapel, Islington]].

Throughout the Spring of 2017 Varley toured Europe opening for [[Valerie June]] and the US extensively, opening for [[Frank Turner]], [[William Elliott Whitmore]] and [[Lincoln Durham]]. While on stage at [[Glastonbury Festival]] 2017,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/glastonbury-2017-line-adds-even-names-field-avalon-bill-revealed-2056240|title=Glastonbury 2017 line-up adds even more names with Field Of Avalon bill revealed|last=Trendell|first=Andrew|date=21 April 2017|website=Nme.com}}</ref> Varley played two brand new songs and said he was working on new material for an album. On 4 July during an interview with [[Huw Stevens]] on BBC Radio 1 the head of [[Xtra Mile Recordings]], Charlie Caplowe, stated that the label were planning on releasing a new Varley album in Autumn 2017.<ref name="bbc.co.uk"/>

On 9 February 2018 ''The Spirit of Minnie'' was released on Xtra Mile Recordings, reaching number two in the iTunes Singer Songwriter charts in the UK, number three in Germany and making the top 30 in the US. The album was produced by legendary record producer [[Cameron McVey]] and mastered by Frank Arkwright at [[Abbey Road Studios]], London. It differs from previous Varley albums as it was recorded with a full band. The UK leg of the album tour culminated with a show at London's 2000 capacity [[Shepherd's Bush Empire]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereoboard.com/content/view/202866/9|title=Will Varley Confirms November 2017 O2 Shepherd's Bush Empire Show - Stereoboard|last=Stickler|first=Jon|website=Stereoboard.com}}</ref>

The album's lead single "Seven Days" was featured on Channel 4's ''[[Sunday Brunch]]''. The album was reviewed favourably, with ''The Times'' describing Varley as being at his "Lyrical Best" and ''Clash Magazine'' saying the album "Evokes the likes of Bright Eyes, Springsteen and Dylan effortlessly."

A week later Varley began a three-week tour of the US opening for label mates Skinny Lister, before returning to Europe in April for a headline tour, selling out shows across Germany and The Netherlands. May 2018 saw Varley and his band in residence with [[John Kennedy (DJ)|John Kennedy]] at [[Radio X (United Kingdom)|Radio X]], recording live tracks at Radio X Studios, London which were broadcast nationally over the following week.

In July 2018 Varley was invited to perform at [[Fairport Convention]]’s Cropredy festival by members of the original band. This marked his biggest show to date, performing in front of some 10,000 festival attendees. That summer he also made appearances at Bearded Theory Festival, Green Man, and his own Smugglers Festival, amongst others.

In Autumn 2018 Varley self released his first live album ''Live at Shepherds Bush Empire''. The album was well received and has been streamed on Spotify over half a million times since its release. Autumn also saw Varley opening for [[The Proclaimers]] again in the UK and touring the UK and Europe as a headline act, selling out numerous shows across the UK, Germany, Netherlands, Denmark, Switzerland and Austria. During this tour Varley also played his first ever shows in Poland, Italy and France.

In March 2019 during his showcase at Stephen F's Hotel in Austin, TX Varley announced he had parted ways with [[Xtra Mile Recordings]]. In June 2019 Varley embarked on his first headline tour of the US, playing 11 shows across the Midwest and the East Coast, including sold out shows in New York, Boston and Wisconsin.

In September 2019 Varley said on social media that he had been working on new material and would be playing new songs at an intimate show at the 200 capacity London venue, [[The Lexington, Islington|The Lexington]] in November. The show sold out in 90 minutes.

On May 26th 2021 Will Varley put out ''The Hole Around My Head'' his first new single since 2018. An album of the same name was released on October 22nd, reaching number 20 in the UK’s official Folk and Americana charts.
