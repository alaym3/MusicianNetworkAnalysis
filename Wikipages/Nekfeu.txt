'''Ken Samaras''' ({{Lang-el|Κεν Σαμαράς|link=no}}, {{lang|el-Latn|Ken Samarás}}; born 3 April 1990), better known by his stage name '''Nekfeu''' ({{IPA-fr|nɛk.fø}}), is a French rapper, actor and record producer. He is also a member of the bands [[L'entourage]] and [[1995 (band)|1995]]. He started his career as a member of S-Crew, with childhood friends Framal, Mekra, 2Zer Washington and DJ Elite. He joined 1995 in 2007, participating in [[open mic]] duels around [[Paris]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.booska-p.com/biographie-nekfeu.html|title=Biographie de Nekfeu.|last=Booska-p}}</ref>

==Early life and career==
Samaras was born in [[La Trinité, Alpes-Maritimes|La Trinité]], a [[Communes of France|commune]] within the [[Nice metropolitan area]], to a father of [[Greek people|Greek]] descent and a mother of [[Scottish people|Scottish]] descent.<ref name="libération">{{cite web|url=http://next.liberation.fr/musique/2015/06/24/allumez-le-feu_1336307 |title=Nekfeu, allumez le feu |date=24 June 2015 |first=Gurvan |last=Kristanadjaja |work=[[Libération]] }}</ref> At the age of 11, he and his family moved to the [[15th arrondissement of Paris]].<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Les flammes du bien / Nekfeu le 5e élément du rap français |date=July–August 2015 |first=Geoffrey |last=Delamarre |magazine=RAPRNB <!--|access-date=3 January 2020-->}}</ref>

After two [[extended play]]s with 1995 (''Aurélie'' in 2011 and ''Anais'' in 2012), as well as the [[studio album]] ''Paris Sud Minute'' in 2013, Nekfeu released his debut studio album ''[[Feu (album)|Feu]]'' on 8 June 2015, for which he won Best Urban Music Album at the [[Victoires de la musique]] in February 2016, as well as ''Destins Liés'' with S-Crew in June 2016. In 2015, he also wrote and performed a song for the French version of the film ''[[Creed (film)|Creed]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Creed : Nekfeu serre les poings avec Stallone et signe le générique du nouveau Rocky|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Nekfeu/news-100550.html|website=Charts in France|language=fr|date=13 January 2016|access-date=14 August 2017}}</ref> In 2016, he released his second album entitled ''Cyborg''.

In 2017, he made his acting debut in the film ''Tout nous sépare'', opposite [[Catherine Deneuve]].<ref>[http://www.leparisien.fr/culture-loisirs/cinema/nekfeu-et-catherine-deneuve-reunis-au-cinema-le-premier-teaser-devoile-28-07-2017-7162329.php Nekfeu et Catherine Deneuve réunis au cinéma, le premier teaser dévoilé], ''[[Le Parisien]]'', 13 August 2017</ref>

In 2019, he released his third album “les étoiles vagabondes”. The album premiered in cinemas, as a movie accompanied the album. The movie premiered 2h before the album was made available on streaming platforms. The album was extremely well received. It Included a song featuring mainstream Belgian rapper Damso (Tricheur), and many other songs entered the top 20 in the French charts.

==Personal life==
Nekfeu is an follower of [[Paris Saint-Germain F.C.|Paris Saint-Germain]], based in the city where he grew up, and his hometown club [[OGC Nice]].<ref name="libération" />
