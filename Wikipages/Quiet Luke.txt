'''Alexander Luke Bahta''' known professionally as '''Quiet Luke''', is an American singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, record producer and artist based in [[New York City]].<ref name="fader">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/10/20/quiet-luke|title=Enter The Mysterious And Existential World Of Quiet Luke|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=2019-10-13}}</ref><ref name="deli">{{Cite web|url=http://m-nyc.thedelimagazine.com/37593/quiet-luke-breaks-nyc-scene-surreal-pop?external=1|title=Quiet Luke breaks into NYC scene with surreal pop |website=The Deli Magazine|access-date=2019-10-13}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Bahta was born in [[Broward County, Florida|Broward County]], in a suburb north of [[Miami|Miami, Florida]]. His mother enrolled him in piano lessons when he was around five years old.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2019-12-02-meet-quiet-luke-interview-21st-century-blue-new-music|title=Meet Quiet Luke, the New York Artist Producing Musical Epics|last=Chesman|first=Donna-Claire|website=DJBooth|language=en|access-date=2019-12-22}}</ref>

He cites his experiences in choir and his immersion in the craft of beat-making in his teen years as influential to his musical direction.<ref name=":0" /> There are mentions of [[bedroom]] [[romanticism]] in interviews about his work.<ref name=":1" />

He chose the [[nom de plume]] Quiet Luke around the age of 16 as he was gaining interest in the concept of the artist as [[superhero]] and [[Eastern religions]]. Talking to [[The Fader]] in 2016, he said "I liked the idea of sitting in a corner being contemplative, being a better listener than a speaker."<ref name="fader" />

== Career ==
Bahta graduated from [[New York University Tisch School of the Arts|NYU’s Clive Davis Institute of Recorded Music]] in 2017 after releasing his debut single "Where U Were" and debut EP, ''Beholden,'' in 2016.<ref name="deli" /> He released his next single "I Wanna Go" and another EP, ''Your Happy Place,'' in 2017.

He released his debut album, ''21st Century Blue'', on December 4, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2019/08/best-new-artists-august-2019/kenny-mason|title=Best New Artists of the Month (August)|last=|first=|date=|website=Complex|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-10-13}}</ref>

== Musical style and influences ==
In an interview for ''[[Crack Magazine]]'', Quiet Luke cites influences such as [[Michael Jackson]], [[Antonio Vivaldi]], [[Jimi Hendrix]] and [[Sade (singer)|Sade]]. As for style, Bahta said in 2016 that his music "[belongs] in the canon of glam, rock in general, P-funk, Japanese aesthetics, and sci-fi".<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/discover/nmquietluke/|title=Quiet Luke studies and refashions the sounds of his youth|website=Crack Magazine|access-date=2019-10-13}}</ref> 

In other interviews he has paid homage to [[Timbaland]] and the [[Dada]] art movement of the 20th century.<ref name=":0" /> He has noted "architectural attention to form" as a characteristic of his music and highlighted "[understanding] humanity in this time of '[[data]]'" as a central question in his practice, as well as Smucker's Uncrustables. <ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-08|title=Quiet Luke's debut album is a Greek drama for the 21st century|url=https://www.documentjournal.com/2020/01/quiet-lukes-debut-album-is-a-greek-drama-for-the-21st-century/|access-date=2021-01-21|website=Document Journal|language=en-US}}</ref>
