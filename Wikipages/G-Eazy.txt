'''Gerald Earl Gillum''' (born May 24, 1989), known professionally as '''G-Eazy''', is an American rapper. His first major-label album, ''[[These Things Happen (G-Eazy album)|These Things Happen]]'', was released on June 23, 2014, and peaked at number&nbsp;3 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. His second studio album, ''[[When It's Dark Out]]'', was released on December 4, 2015. It featured the single "[[Me, Myself & I (G-Eazy and Bebe Rexha song)|Me, Myself & I]]" (with [[Bebe Rexha]]), which reached the top 10 of the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His third album, ''[[The Beautiful & Damned (album)|The Beautiful & Damned]]'', was released on December 15, 2017,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/11/g-eazy-beautiful-and-damned-release-date/ |title=G-Eazy Shares 'The Beautiful & Damned' Album Release Date - XXL |website=XXL Mag |df=mdy-all |access-date=2017-11-26}}</ref> preceded by the single "[[No Limit (G-Eazy song)|No Limit]]" (featuring [[Cardi B]] and [[ASAP Rocky]]), which reached number 4 of the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100. His EP ''[[B-Sides (G-Eazy EP)|B-Sides]],'' released on June 27, 2019 (updated on August 22 and September 19, 2019), included all the songs that did not make it to his fourth studio album ''[[These Things Happen Too]],'' released on September 24, 2021.<ref name=lwhq>{{cite web|url=https://www.lilwaynehq.com/2020/11/g-eazy-confirms-lil-wayne-feature-these-things-happen-too-album/|title=G-Eazy Confirms Lil Wayne Will Be Featured On His "These Things Happen Too" Album|website=Lil Wayne HQ|last=Mousdell|first=Daniel|date=November 21, 2020|access-date=November 25, 2020}}</ref>

==Early life==
When Gillum was in the first grade, his mother left his father, an associate professor of art at [[California State University, Fresno|CSU Fresno]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Ed Gillum, Professor |url=http://www.fresnostate.edu/artshum/artanddesign/faculty-staff/edgillum.html |website=Fresnostate.edu |df=mdy-all |access-date=2015-12-12}}</ref> Gillum moved in with his grandparents in [[Berkeley, California]]. They later moved to [[North Oakland, Oakland, California|North Oakland]], although Gillum continued to attend school in Berkeley. Gillum attended [[Loyola University New Orleans]] and graduated in 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.biography.com/musician/g-eazy |title=G-Eazy Biography |access-date=September 14, 2020}}</ref>

===2014–16: ''These Things Happen'' and ''When It's Dark Out''===
On June 23, 2014, G-Eazy released his major-label debut album, ''[[These Things Happen (G-Eazy album)|These Things Happen]]''. The album topped the US ''Billboard''{{'s}} Hip-Hop/R&B and Top Rap Albums charts, also earning him the number 3 spot on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and the Top Digital Albums Chart. The album has sold nearly 265,000 copies to date.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aclfestival.com/2015-artist/g-eazy/ |title=G-Eazy &#124; ACL Music Festival |website=Aclfestival.com |df=mdy-all |access-date=2016-01-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160111174925/http://www.aclfestival.com/2015-artist/g-eazy/ |archive-date=January 11, 2016}}</ref> On October 21, 2014, Gillum embarked on his sold-out "From the Bay to the Universe" tour.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.baeblemusic.com/artist/g-eazy |title=G-Eazy {{!}} Videos {{!}} Photos {{!}} Editorial|website=baeblemusic.com |df=mdy-all |access-date=2019-03-27}}</ref> The tour traveled across the globe to countries like Australia and New Zealand. This was his first headlining tour overseas.

During the summer of 2015, G-Eazy played some of the main stages at a series of notable music festivals, including [[Lollapalooza]], [[Electric Forest Festival|Electric Forest]], [[Bonnaroo]], [[Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival|Outside Lands]], [[Budweiser Made in America Festival|Made in America]], and [[Austin City Limits Music Festival|Austin City Limits]]. With the rise of his music career, Gillum has also taken up in interest in fashion by releasing a collaboration with Rare Panther in the fall of 2015 and being named [[GQ Magazine]]'s top 10 most stylish at [[New York Fashion Week]].<ref>{{cite web |title=The 10 Best-Dressed Men at New York Fashion Week|url = https://www.gq.com/gallery/the-10-best-dressed-men-at-new-york-fashion-week |df=mdy-all |access-date=2015-09-30 |first =Matthew |last=Sebra}}</ref> Gillum's second album, ''[[When It's Dark Out]]'', was released on December 4, 2015.<ref>{{cite web |title=G-Eazy Details The Creation Of His Sophomore Album. [New Video] |url= http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/g-eazy-details-the-creation-of-his-sophomore-album-new-video.35471.html |df=mdy-all |access-date=2015-09-30}}</ref> On January 6, 2016, G-Eazy launched his second world tour. The tour saw G-Eazy tour the United States, Europe, and Australia.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.g-eazy.com/tour/ |title=G-Eazy Tour |website=G-Eazy Official Website |access-date=February 27, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160225084512/http://www.g-eazy.com/tour/|archive-date=February 25, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> His single "[[Me, Myself & I (G-Eazy and Bebe Rexha song)|Me, Myself & I]]", in collaboration with [[Bebe Rexha]], peaked at number 7 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7311567/rihanna-work-hot-100-sixth-week-meghan-trainor |work=Billboard |title=Rihanna's 'Work' Leads Hot 100 for Sixth Week, Meghan Trainor's 'No' Hits Top 10 |first=Gary |last=Trust |date=March 28, 2016 |access-date=March 28, 2016}}</ref>

He co-headlined the Endless Summer tour with fellow rapper [[Logic (rapper)|Logic]], along with supporting acts [[YG (rapper)|YG]] and [[Yo Gotti]], from June to August. Before the tour, he announced his new [[mixtape]], Endless Summer II. He cancelled the tape due to problems with sample clearance.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/oVnCZHv9gXs] {{dead link|date=December 2017}}</ref>
In May 2016, G-Eazy confirmed that he would be featured on [[Britney Spears]]' single "[[Make Me... ===2017–18: ''Step Brothers'' and ''The Beautiful & Damned''===
Gillum released an EP with Guatemalan DJ [[Carnage (DJ)|Carnage]] called ''[[Step Brothers (EP)|Step Brothers]]'' on March 27, 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://m.vk.com/wall-31352730_553436 |title=Wall |website=M.vk.com |access-date=December 15, 2017}}</ref>  G-Eazy released his new single, a collaboration with singer [[Kehlani]] called "[[Good Life (G-Eazy and Kehlani song)|Good Life]]", which is a part of the eighth installment of the film franchise ''[[Fast & Furious]]'', titled ''[[The Fate of the Furious]]''. He was also featured in the new [[Dillon Francis]] single titled "[[Say Less (Dillon Francis song)|Say Less]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BEu1TBvk_YV/ |title=Wall|website=Instagram.com |access-date=December 15, 2017}}</ref>

On June 14, 2017, G-Eazy announced through [[Instagram]] and [[Twitter]] that his next studio album, ''[[The Beautiful & Damned (album)|The Beautiful & Damned]]'', would be released in the fall of 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7832983/g-eazy-the-beautiful-damned-new-album-title |title=G-Eazy New Album: Rapper Reveals 'The Beautiful & Damned' Titl |work=Billboard |last=Lamarre |first=Carl |date=June 14, 2017|access-date=August 23, 2017}}</ref> On November 8, 2017, the official release day was announced as December 15, as well as an accompanying short film.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rap-up.com/2017/11/08/g-eazy-reveals-the-beautiful-damned-release-date-film-trailer/ |title=G-Eazy Reveals 'The Beautiful & Damned' Release Date, Film Trailer |work=Rap-Up |date=November 8, 2017 |access-date=November 8, 2017}}</ref>

On December 5, 2017, G-Eazy released his second single from ''The Beautiful & Damned'' titled "[[Him & I]]" with [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]], with whom he was in a relationship. They performed the song together on [[Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve]] of 2017.<ref name=Ghalseyrelationship>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8464279/halsey-g-eazy-relationship-timeline |title=A Timeline of Halsey & G-Eazy's Relationship |work=Billboard}}</ref> On February 27, 2019 G-Eazy released the single, "West Coast", featuring [[Blueface]] and released the video on March 28, 2019, adding ALLBLACK and YG.

== Legal issues ==
