'''Dima Nikolayevich Bilan'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dni.ru/showbiz/2008/6/27/144750.html|title=Певец Дима Билан сменил имя|trans-title=Dima Bilan changed his name|language=ru|work=Dni.ru|date=27 June 2008|access-date=18 September 2020}}</ref> ({{lang-ru|Ди́ма Никола́евич Била́н}}; born '''Viktor Nikolayevich Belan''', {{lang-ru|Ви́ктор Никола́евич Бела́н|links=no}}, on 24 December 1981) is a Russian singer, songwriter, and actor. He represented Russia at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006]] with "[[Never Let You Go (Dima Bilan song)|Never Let You Go]]", finishing second, and he won the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2008|contest in 2008]] in [[Belgrade]], with the song "[[Believe (Dima Bilan song)|Believe]]". He has had several Russian number one hit records. He is sometimes referred to as "Russia's [[Enrique Iglesias|Iglesias]]" due to his similarity to the Spanish singer.

==Early life==
Dima Bilan was born as Viktor Belan in [[Ust-Dzheguta]] in the autonomous republic [[Karachay-Cherkessia]] in a mixed family of [[Tatars|Tatar]], [[Karachays|Karachay]] and [[Russian people|Russian]] heritage.<ref>{{cite web |title=Дима Билан |url=https://24smi.org/celebrity/21-dima-bilan.html |website=24smi.org |access-date=27 April 2021}}</ref> Bilan's father was born in [[Kabardino-Balkaria]] and his mother in [[Tatarstan]]. After having briefly lived in Tatarstan as a child, Bilan's family relocated to [[Maysky, Kabardino-Balkarian Republic|Maysky]] in [[Kabardino-Balkaria]].<ref name="Komsomolskaya Pravda">{{cite web |last1=Netrebko |first1=Ivan |title=Дима Билан в молодости: мама боялась, что "мальчика из Нальчика" перепутали в роддоме |url=https://www.stav.kp.ru/daily/27249/4378688/ |website=Stav.kp.ru |publisher=[[Komsomolskaya Pravda]] |access-date=27 April 2021}}</ref>

Bilan started playing [[Bayan (accordion)|bayan]] in the fifth class of school and participated in different music contests as a child. He became interested in music as he enjoyed singing during school and started to write letters to the [[Gnessin Musical College]], asking them for entrance requirements. Although the musical college did not respond to his writings, he still went to the auditions and managed to enroll.<ref name="Komsomolskaya Pravda"/> Between 2000 and 2003, he specialised in classical singing. Afterwards, he enrolled at the [[Russian Institute of Theatre Arts]], where he studied acting. At the time, Bilan lived at an apartment of an acquainted family. He financed his studies while working as a nighttime stock clerk in a shoe store.<ref>{{cite web |title=Дима Билан |url=https://www.forbes.ru/profile/dima-bilan |website=Forbes.ru |access-date=27 April 2021}}</ref>

Bilan started his music career during school. He regularly performed at school parties and for guests at home. He idolised [[Valeriy Meladze|Valeriy]] and [[Konstantin Meladze]] in his teen years.<ref>{{cite web |title=Дениза Хекилаева. "Вера". Голос.Дети-4. Слепое прослушивание. Фрагмент выпуска от 17.03.2017 |url=https://www.1tv.ru/shows/golos-deti-4/vystupleniya/deniza-hekilaeva-vera-golos-deti-4-slepoe-proslushivanie-fragment-vypuska-ot-17-03-2017 |website=1tv.ru |publisher=[[Channel One Russia]] |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref>  In 1995, he first performed at a televised music contest, singing [[Valeriy Meladze]]'s "Aktrisa".<ref>{{cite web |title=Валерий и Константин Меладзе. Сегодня вечером. Выпуск от 19.09.2015 |url=https://www.1tv.ru/shows/segodnya-vecherom/vypuski/segodnya-vecherom-vypusk-ot-19092015 |website=1tv.ru |publisher=[[Channel One Russia]] |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref> Meladze's 1997 single "Vera" especially became important to Bilan.<ref>{{cite web |title=Дениза Хекилаева. "Вера". Голос.Дети-4. Слепое прослушивание. Фрагмент выпуска от 17.03.2017 |url=https://www.1tv.ru/shows/golos-deti-4/vystupleniya/deniza-hekilaeva-vera-golos-deti-4-slepoe-proslushivanie-fragment-vypuska-ot-17-03-2017 |website=1tv.ru |publisher=[[Channel One Russia]] |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref>

In 1998, he first travelled to Moscow to participate in a children's music contest. He received a diploma from [[Iosif Kobzon]], who was the chairman of the jury.<ref>{{cite web |title=ДИМА БИЛАН |url=https://dni.ru/person/Bilan/ |website=Dni.ru |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref> After moving to Moscow to studying at Gnessin, Bilan started recording his first songs. His first music video "Osen'" was released in late 2000. He performed his first music video under the pseudonym Dima Belan.<ref>{{cite web |title=Дима Билан - Осень / Dima Bilan - Osen' |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2tt-g_MbJgE |website=YouTube.com |publisher=bilanofficial |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref>

Although Bilan studied to become an opera singer, he quickly found himself bored with is coursework and dreamt of becoming a pop singer. Via a classmate, he met [[Yuri Aizenshpis]], who saw potential in Bilan.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gasparyan |first1=Artur |title=Айзеншпис ставил Виктору Цою в пример "Ласковый май" |url=https://www.mk.ru/culture/2020/07/14/ayzenshpis-stavil-viktoru-coyu-v-primer-laskovyy-may.html |website=MK.ru |publisher=[[Moskovskij Komsomolets]] |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref> Bilan then officially started performing under the pseudonym "Dima Bilan", taking both the first name and surname of his grandfather.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mineeva |first1=Alina |title=Нажился на Цое, открыл Билана: махинации Юрия Айзеншписа |url=https://www.gazeta.ru/culture/2019/09/20/a_12663505.shtml |website=Gazeta.ru |publisher=Gazeta.ru |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref>

In August 2002, Bilan participated in the first edition of [[New Wave (competition)|New Wave]]. At the end of the contest, he finished in fourth place.<ref>{{cite web |title=Артисты, ставшие популярными после конкурса "Новая волна". Справка |url=https://ria.ru/20090730/179160935.html |website=ria.ru |publisher=[[RIA Novosti]]}}</ref> On the last night of the competition, he premiered his first commercial single "Boom".

After New Wave, Bilan released a music video for "Boom". In 2003, Bilan released his debut album ''Ya nochnoy huligan'', which became popular in Russia and was received well among critics. As a result, he performed its eponymous single "Ya nochnoy huligan" at the 2003 edition of ''[[Pesnya goda]]''. However, Bilan's big break came with the release of the ballad "Na beregu neba", which peaked at #2 at the [[TopHit]] radio charts.<ref>{{cite web |title=Дима Билан |url=https://tophit.ru/ru/artist/%D0%94%D0%B8%D0%BC%D0%B0_%D0%91%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%B0%D0%BD |website=Tophit.ru |publisher=[[TopHit]] |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref> The subsequent album ''Na beregu neba'' became one of the best-selling albums in Russia of 2004, receiving a gold certification.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://nfpf.org/priz/index.php |title= Список призов / Золотой диск |publisher= [[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry]] |access-date=2020-07-02 |url-status=dead|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20130205005444/http://nfpf.org/priz/index.php|archive-date= 2013-02-05}}</ref>

===2005-2008: Eurovision participations, "Believe" and brief international breakthrough===
Enjoying large success in his native Russia, Bilan was signed up by his producer Aizenshpis to compete at Russia's [[Eurovision Song Contest 2005]]'s national selection. With the English-language track "Not That Simple", Bilan was the favourite to win the selection. However, he eventually finished second behind Natalia Podolskaya despite being on top of the televoting for most of the final.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mineev |first1=Kirill |last2=Minkin |first2=Anatoliy |title=Россиянам предложили выбрать артиста на Евровидение. Но к голосованию уже есть вопросы |url=https://360tv.ru/news/tekst/vybrat-artista-na-evrovidenie/ |website=360tv.ru |publisher=360}}</ref> The Russian version of the song, titled "Ty dolzhna ryadom byt'" became the 21st most-played track on Russian radio in 2005.<ref>{{cite web |title=Top Radio Hits (2005) |url=https://tophit.ru/ru/chart/airplay/yearly/2005-01-01/all/all |website=TopHit.ru |publisher=[[TopHit]] |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref>

In mid-March 2006, Bilan was chosen internally by Russian broadcaster [[Channel One (Russia)|Channel One]] to represent Russia at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006|Eurovision Song Contest]] in [[Athens]], Greece. Out of 37 participating countries, Bilan took second place with the dark pop song "[[Never Let You Go (Dima Bilan song)|Never Let You Go]]", equalling one of Russia's best ever showing at Eurovision — [[Alsou]]'s "Solo" was runner-up back in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2000|2000 contest]].

In September 2006, for second year in a row, Dima Bilan won Artist of the Year and Song of the Year at [[MTV Russian Music Awards]]. On 2 November Bilan represented Russia at [[MTV EMA]] in Copenhagen.

On 15 November 2006 Bilan received award for Best Selling Russian Artist and performed at the [[World Music Awards]] in London along with [[Michael Jackson]], [[Beyoncé Knowles|Beyoncé]], [[Rihanna]], [[Nelly Furtado]] and [[Bob Sinclar|Bob Sinclair]].

In February 2007, Bilan started to record his first international album in English in Los Angeles, [[Miami]] and in [[Philadelphia]] in collaboration with producers [[Jim Beanz]] and [[Ryan Tedder]]. One of the new tracks will be a duet with [[Nelly Furtado]].{{Update after|2014|3}}

Also in 2007 he premiered the first single from his new album — Number One Fan. According to Russian and Eastern European radio airplay chart, Number One Fan topped the charts for eight weeks. Video for this song<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wHAwMHtGw_Q |title=Dima Bilan – Number One Fan |work=Youtube.com |date=24 August 2007 |access-date=24 August 2007 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080112061402/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wHAwMHtGw_Q |archive-date=12 January 2008 }}</ref> was shot in London by famous British director [[Trudy Bellinger]] who has also made videos for acts including [[Girls Aloud]], [[Sugababes]] and [[Sophie Ellis-Bextor]].

On 4 October, Bilan premiered his second single from the forthcoming album, Amnesia, at the [[MTV Russian Music Awards|MTV Russia Music Awards]] 2007. During this show, Bilan won three awards, for [[MTV Russian Music Awards#2007|Best Performer]], [[MTV Russian Music Awards#2007|Best Song]] and — for the third consecutive year — [[MTV Russian Music Awards#2007|Best Artist]].

[[File:Russia in the 2008 Eurovision Song Contest.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Bilan performing at the Eurovision Song Contest final in Belgrade, 2008. He is accompanied by [[Edvin Marton]] (left) and [[Evgeni Plushenko]] (right).]]

In 2008, Bilan released three albums — in Russian, English and Spanish.{{Update after|2010|11}} He also released an English album in 81 countries in co-operation with [[Interscope Records]] ([[Universal Music International|Universal Group]]). He worked on his English album, with some tracks produced by [[Jim Beanz]] and [[Ryan Tedder]] ([[OneRepublic]]).{{Update after|2010|11}} Also his new Spanish album included a duet with [[Nelly Furtado]].{{Citation needed|date=September 2009}}

On 20 February, Bilan went to [[Miami]] and recorded a few tracks for his Spanish album with famous producer Rudy Perez. The Spanish album will include{{Update after|2010|11}} a duet with [[Nelly Furtado]] and a Spanish version of his first international single "Number One Fan."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shoowbiz.ru/news/15723.html |title=Новости шоу-бизнеса, скандалы, светская хроника " Билан исполнит на отборочном этапе "Евровидения-2008" песню на испанском |publisher=Shoowbiz.ru |access-date=6 October 2012}}</ref>

In 2008, Bilan once again represented Russia in the Eurovision Song Contest in Belgrade with the song "Believe", accompanied by Hungarian [[violin]]ist [[Edvin Marton]] and Russian olympic and three-time world champion figure skater [[Evgeni Plushenko]]. On 24 May 2008, Bilan won first place in the contest, receiving 272 points and seven twelves. His victory's integrity was questioned by [[Ukraine]]'s officials later in May.<ref>[http://newsru.com/cinema/29may2008/eurovision.html Украина заявляет о фальсификации результатов "Евровидения-2008"] ''[[NEWSru.com]]'', 29 May 2008.</ref>

=== 2009–2010 ===
During 2009 Bilan has continued with concerts and he also performed during the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2009]], before handing over the title of Eurovision Song Contest winner to [[Alexander Rybak]] of Norway.

In 2010 Dima recorded a demo version of the song "White Nights", which would be competing in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2010]], but he did not enter the contest saying that it would be too soon to return to the contest after just 2 years after his win at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2008]].

In the Summer of 2010, Bilan produced and starred in the short film Театр Абсурда (''Theatre of the Absurd'', written and directed by Maxim Apriatin and based upon Bilan's song Он хотел (''He Wanted To'').  In the film, Bilan performs monologues in which he shares his opinion on the moral values of the people who live in the Russian capital, as well as quotations from [[Ecclesiastes]], and scraps of the diary of [[Jim Morrison]]. The film was released on the Internet in late 2010.<ref name="7d.org.ua">{{cite news|url=http://www.7d.org.ua/?news=films&id=8125|script-title=ru:Первый фильм Димы Билана попал в Интернет (ВИДЕО)|date=1 February 2011|work=7d.org.ua|language=ru|access-date=13 February 2011}}</ref><ref name="Карта Музыки">{{cite news|url=http://mapmusic.ru/newsm/3072-bilan-prezent-teatr-absurda.html |script-title=ru:Дима Билан презентовал фильм "Театр абсурда" ("Он хотел") |date=10 February 2011 |work=Карта Музыки |language=ru |access-date=13 February 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110820145825/http://mapmusic.ru/newsm/3072-bilan-prezent-teatr-absurda.html |archive-date=20 August 2011 }}</ref><ref name="Tophit">{{cite web|url=http://www.tophitru.com/newsshowdetale.shtml?id=11390|title=Dima Bilan plays in ''Absurd Theatre''|work=tophit.ru|publisher=12 November 2010|access-date=13 February 2011}}</ref>

===2012–present===
Bilan has recently competed at [[Russia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2012|Russia's Eurovision 2012 song selection]] for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2012]] in [[Baku]], [[Azerbaijan]]. He entered the competition with [[t.A.T.u.]] member [[Yulia Volkova]] with their song "Back To Her Future". They finished in 2nd place with 29.25 points from the winning entry, "[[Party for Everybody]]" by Russian ethno-pop band [[Buranovskiye Babushki]] who was 38.51 points ahead of them.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lifenews.ru/news/84135 |title=Объявлен список претендентов на "Евровидение-2012" — LIFE &#124; NEWS Шоубиз |publisher=[[LifeNews|Lifenews.ru]] |date=29 March 2012 |access-date=6 October 2012}}</ref>

In December 2012, Bilan signed an open letter attacking the author of a St. Petersburg bill against homosexual propaganda, along with pop stars like [[Philipp Kirkorov]] and [[Valery Syutkin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.rian.ru/russia/20121220/178294940.html |title=Russian Pop Stars Chime in on 'Sodomite Propaganda' |publisher=RIA Novosti |date=20 December 2012 |access-date=20 December 2012}}</ref>

Also, Dima Bilan appeared in the [[Eurosong 2013 - a MAD show]] (the national selection of Greece) as a guest, along with other 5 Eurovision winners, which include [[Ruslana]], [[Marija Šerifović]], [[Alexander Rybak]], [[Elena Paparizou|Helena Paparizou]] and [[Vicky Leandros]], in order to choose the artist and the song that would have represented [[Greece in the Eurovision Song Contest 2013]] in [[Malmö]].{{citation needed|date=May 2016}}

Dima Bilan is one of the four coaches of the Russian reality talent show [[The Voice (Russia TV series)|The Voice]] in seasons 1–3, 5–6.

Dima Bilan is one of the three coaches of the Russian reality talent show [[The Voice Kids (Russia)|The Voice Kids]] in seasons 1–4. His team's artists became the winners of this show in the [[The Voice Kids (Russia, season 3)|third]] (Danil Pluzhnikov) and in the [[The Voice Kids (Russia, season 4)|fourth]] (Elizaveta Kachurak) seasons, respectively.

== Filmography ==
Dima Bilan starred in several [[cinema of Russia|Russian films]] and serials:

* 2005 - ''[[Not Born Beautiful]]'' (cameo)
* 2006 - ''Club'' (cameo)
* 2006 - ''The Adventures of Pinocchio'' (Duremar)
* 2007 - ''Star break'' (Fortiano)
* 2007 - ''Kingdom of Crooked Mirrors'' (Gurd)
* 2008 - ''Goldfish''
* 2009 - ''Pinocchio'' (visiting singer)
* 2011 - ''[[Theatre of the Absurd (short film)|Theatre of the Absurd]]''
* 2013 - ''[[Frozen (2013 film)|Frozen]]'' (Russian dub, voice of [[Hans (Frozen)|Hans]])<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.intermedia.ru/news/248821 |title=Дима Билан, Наталия Быстрова и Сергей Пенкин подарили голоса героям "Холодного сердца"|language=Russian|access-date=18 February 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203095338/http://www.intermedia.ru/news/248821 |archive-date=3 December 2013 }}</ref>
* 2014 - ''Space Pirate Captain Harlock'' live-action (Harlock in youth)
* 2016 - ''[[The Heritage of Love]]'' live-action (His first role in full-length film)
