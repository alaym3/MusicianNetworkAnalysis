'''Wussy''' is an American five-piece [[indie rock]] band formed in [[Cincinnati]], [[Ohio]] in 2001.<ref name=NPR-FreshAir-StrongWork-Pain-2009>{{cite web|last1=Tucker|first1=Ken|title=Wussy: Strong Work, And Not Without Pain|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=105747735|website=[[Fresh Air]]|publisher=[[NPR]]|access-date=19 July 2014|date=22 June 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140512225126/http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=105747735|archive-date=12 May 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> The band consists of Chuck Cleaver (vocals/guitar), Lisa Walker (vocals/guitar), John Erhardt (pedal steel), Joe Klug (drums) and Mark Messerly (bass).<ref name="PortlandMercury-GiveaDamn-2012" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7257015/wussy/biography|title=Wussy - Biography|last=Deming|first=Mark|website=www.billboard.com|publisher=Rovi|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160924092133/http://www.billboard.com/artist/7257015/wussy/biography|archive-date=2016-09-24|url-status=live}}</ref> Cleaver and Walker write most of the songs and either alternate lead vocals or sing them in harmony.<ref name="NPR-FreshAir-StrongWork-Pain-2009" /> Live performances feature the two vocalists having a "combative rapport".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/music/on-the-cheerfully-combative-midwestern-noise-pop-of-wussy-6391242|title=On the Cheerfully Combative Midwestern Noise-Pop of Wussy|last=Harvilla|first=Rob|date=2009-06-24|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160815092535/http://www.villagevoice.com/music/on-the-cheerfully-combative-midwestern-noise-pop-of-wussy-6391242|archive-date=2016-08-15|url-status=live}}</ref> They have released six albums, one live album, two EPs, one mini LP and a number of singles. The group has received critical acclaim from ''[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]],''<ref name="RStone-NewMusicReport-2009">{{cite web|last1=Hoard|first1=Christian|title=New Music Report: ==History==
Wussy formed in Cincinnati, Ohio, in 2001 while [[Chuck Cleaver]] had just released the last album with his previous group, [[Ass Ponys]].<ref name=OutsightRadioHours-Interviews-2001-2012>{{cite web|last1=Chuck Cleaver-Lisa Walker on Outsight Radio Hours|title=Outsight Radio Hours|url=https://archive.org/details/ChuckCleaver-lisaWalkerOnOutsightRadioHours|website=[[Archive.org]]|access-date=19 July 2014|format=Audio interviews|year=2001, 2006, 2009, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140513140124/https://archive.org/details/ChuckCleaver-lisaWalkerOnOutsightRadioHours|archive-date=13 May 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> He met Lisa Walker and asked her to perform with him at a local awards show in Cincinnati in 2001. Following the performance the duo decided to form a band together.<ref name=PortlandMercury-GiveaDamn-2012>{{cite web|last1=Lannamann|first1=Ned|title=If You Ever Gave a Damn: For Wussy, the West Coast's Waiting|url=http://www.portlandmercury.com/portland/if-you-ever-gave-a-damn/Content?oid=6233740|website=[[Portland Mercury]]|access-date=19 July 2014|date=14 June 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141205002434/http://www.portlandmercury.com/portland/if-you-ever-gave-a-damn/Content?oid=6233740|archive-date=5 December 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> Walker, originally from [[Muncie, Indiana|Muncie]], Indiana, attended [[Cedarville University]] and has worked in marketing.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://www.cincinnatimagazine.com/features/the-ballad-of-chuck-and-lisa3/|title=The Ballad of Chuck and Lisa -|last=Cohen|first=Jason|date=December 1, 2009|website=Cincinnati Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160822235942/http://www.cincinnatimagazine.com/features/the-ballad-of-chuck-and-lisa3/|archive-date=August 22, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> They were joined by Messerly on bass and Dawn Burman on drums in 2002.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.damnably.com/wussy/|title=Wussy bio|date=2010-01-17|website=Damnably Records|language=en-US|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160808005345/http://www.damnably.com/wussy/|archive-date=2016-08-08|url-status=live}}</ref> Burman had not yet mastered the instrument but joined as she "loved their sound and wanted to be part of it".<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=054RW1IvwJsC|title=Wussy Rising|last=Bergantino|first=Matt|date=2008-01-01|publisher=Cincinnati Magazine|pages=26–28|language=en}}</ref> Cleaver finalised his previous band with a compilation in 2005 leaving him to concentrate on Wussy.<ref name=":3" /> They made their recording debut in 2005 with the album ''[[Funeral Dress (album)|Funeral Dress]]'' on Shake it Records,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://artsfuse.org/111069/fuse-music-interview-i-cant-admit-defeat-wussy-carries-on/|title=Fuse Music Interview:  "I Can’t Admit Defeat" — Wussy Carries On|last=Melnick|first=Jeff|date=July 23, 2014|website=artsfuse.org|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161019024959/http://artsfuse.org/111069/fuse-music-interview-i-cant-admit-defeat-wussy-carries-on/|archive-date=October 19, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> the label of a local record store of the same name.<ref name="CBS-GoingStrong-2014" /> The record was produced by [[The Afghan Whigs|John Curley]] at his Ultrasuede Studios,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://osaurus.net/|title=Brian Niesz|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160809183906/http://osaurus.net/|archive-date=2016-08-09|url-status=live}}</ref> where the band would continue to record future albums.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/music/qanda-wussys-chuck-cleaver-and-lisa-walker-on-the-cincinnati-scene-playing-music-with-your-ex-and-shaking-the-critics-band-tag-6655261|title=Q&A: Wussy's Chuck Cleaver And Lisa Walker On The Cincinnati Scene, Playing Music With Your Ex, And Shaking The "Critic's Band" Tag|last=Cohan|first=Brad|date=March 2, 2012|website=villagevoice.com|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160815093623/http://www.villagevoice.com/music/qanda-wussys-chuck-cleaver-and-lisa-walker-on-the-cincinnati-scene-playing-music-with-your-ex-and-shaking-the-critics-band-tag-6655261|archive-date=August 15, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> While making the album all four band members suffered some personal hardship that inspired much of the 2007 record ''Left for Dead.''<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.goldminemag.com/article/wussy-is-left-for-dead-as-former-ass-ponys-leader-bandmates-leave-turmoil-behind-2|title=Wussy is 'Left for Dead' as former Ass Ponys leader, bandmates leave turmoil behind|last=Lindblad|first=Peter|date=December 21, 2007|website=goldminemag.com|language=en-US|access-date=2010-04-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101127050658/http://www.goldminemag.com/article/wussy-is-left-for-dead-as-former-ass-ponys-leader-bandmates-leave-turmoil-behind-2|archive-date=November 27, 2010|url-status=live}}</ref> Contrary to other releases it relied heavily on Walker's writing,<ref name=":2" /> and was described by Spin as doing "relationship songs right".<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Db0ytsazMqUC|title=Wussy - Left for Dead|last=Kandell|first=Steve|date=November 2007|publisher=SPIN Media LLC|pages=126|language=en}}</ref> It was followed by a self-titled album in 2009. The same year drummer Burman left and was replaced by Joe Klug.<ref name=":1" /> In late 2010, the band recorded an acoustic version of their debut as ''Funeral Dress II'' live at Ultrasuede that was released for Record Store Day 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wussy.bandcamp.com/album/funeral-dress-ii|title=Funeral Dress II, by Wussy|website=Wussy|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160822150146/https://wussy.bandcamp.com/album/funeral-dress-ii|archive-date=2016-08-22|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Wussy-Funeral-Dress-II/release/3304675|title=Wussy - Funeral Dress II|website=Discogs|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161010032238/https://www.discogs.com/Wussy-Funeral-Dress-II/release/3304675|archive-date=2016-10-10|url-status=live}}</ref>

The album ''Strawberry'' was released in November 2011 and was described as "driven by an unpretentious, rust-flecked honesty and a warped worldview".<ref name="PortlandMercury-GiveaDamn-2012" /> Former Ass Ponys member John Erhardt joined the band before recording started.<ref name=":0" /> The band embarked on a three-week headlining tour in support of the album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://occur.la/blog/226/interview-wussy|title=Interview - Wussy|last=Bobbitt|first=Melissa|date=July 2, 2012|website=The Occurrence|language=en-US|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160819135351/https://occur.la/blog/226/interview-wussy|archive-date=August 19, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2012 they released the Europe-only seventeen track compilation ''Buckeye'' on Damnably Records,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/reviews/albums/wussy-buckeye-100515|title=Wussy – Buckeye|last=Lo|first=Chris|date=July 5, 2012|website=thelinebestfit.com|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160822083409/http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/reviews/albums/wussy-buckeye-100515|archive-date=August 22, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/jul/12/wussy-buckeye-review|title=Wussy: Buckeye – review|last=Jonze|first=Tim|date=2012-07-12|newspaper=The Guardian|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160827105434/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/jul/12/wussy-buckeye-review|archive-date=2016-08-27|url-status=live}}</ref> their first international release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://aftertheshowmusic.com/interview-with-wussy/|title=Interview with Wussy|last=Kulpa|first=Dan|date=September 9, 2013|website=After The Show|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160822234649/http://aftertheshowmusic.com/interview-with-wussy/|archive-date=August 22, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2014 they released the album ''Attica!'', the title inspired by the film ''[[Dog Day Afternoon]],'' while the single and [[The Who]] tribute "Teenage Wasteland",<ref name=":0" /> received some airplay. The album was released in the UK through Damnably Records.<ref name=":1" />

In March 2016 the band released their seventh album, ''Forever Sounds'', which reached number 20 on the Billboard Heatseeker album chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7257015/wussy/chart|title=Wussy - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=www.billboard.com|access-date=2016-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160924092144/http://www.billboard.com/artist/7257015/wussy/chart|archive-date=2016-09-24|url-status=live}}</ref>

The band's eighth album, ''[[What Heaven is Like]]'', was released in May 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=Wussy Announce New LP With 'Fargo'-Inspired Single 'Gloria'|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1984649/wussy-gloria/premiere/|website=Stereogum|access-date=10 March 2018|date=26 February 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180310135910/https://www.stereogum.com/1984649/wussy-gloria/premiere/|archive-date=10 March 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

At the beginning of the band Cleaver and Walker were a couple but they separated in 2007.<ref name="PortlandMercury-GiveaDamn-2012" /> On the band's name, Wussy: Cleaver joked that "I think it looks good on a t-shirt."<ref name="CBS-GoingStrong-2014">{{cite news|last1=Mason|first1=Anthony|last2=Nair|first2=Vinita|title=Critically acclaimed band Wussy going strong|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/videos/critically-acclaimed-band-wussy-going-strong/|access-date=29 November 2014|work=[[CBS This Morning]] - Saturday|date=29 November 2014|format=Video interview/feature|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141129152033/http://www.cbsnews.com/videos/critically-acclaimed-band-wussy-going-strong/|archive-date=29 November 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

Pedal steel guitarist John Erhardt died on May 4, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=R.I.P. John Erhardt of Wussy and Ass Ponys |url=https://www.kexp.org/read/2020/5/7/rip-john-erhardt-wussy-and-ass-ponys/ |website=www.kexp.org |access-date=7 May 2020}}</ref>

The band is a favorite with some rock critics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thesunbreak.com/2014/06/20/tsb-interview-wussys-chuck-cleaver-talks-to-the-sunbreak-about-his-great-word-of-mouth-band/|title=TSB interview: Wussy’s Chuck Cleaver talks to the SunBreak 20, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2012 [[Robert Christgau]] wrote that "Wussy have been the best since they released the first of their five superb albums in 2005",<ref name="BNReview-ReasonstoLoveWussy-2012">{{cite web|last1=Christgau|first1=Robert|title=The Many Reasons to Love Wussy|url=http://bnreview.barnesandnoble.com/t5/Rock-Roll/The-Many-Reasons-to-Love-Wussy/ba-p/7259|work=[[Barnes & Noble]] Review|access-date=19 July 2014|date=16 March 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140712040931/http://bnreview.barnesandnoble.com/t5/Rock-Roll/The-Many-Reasons-to-Love-Wussy/ba-p/7259|archive-date=12 July 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> and he has called them his "favorite band".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/expert-witness-with-robert-christgau-beauty-pill-wussy-animal-collective|title=Guitar Rock Grooves: Expert Witness with Robert songs were radio transmissions from a resistance we hadn't dared to hope existed."<ref name="LARB-SupportWussy-2014">{{cite web|last1=Taylor|first1=Charles|title=Support Your Local Wussy: The sad
