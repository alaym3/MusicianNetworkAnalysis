'''Ultimate Painting''' were an English alternative rock [[musical ensemble|band]], formed in London in 2014. The band was composed of co-frontmen [[Jack Cooper (English musician)|Jack Cooper]] and James Hoare, with a revolving cast of rhythm sections.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.troubleinmindrecs.com/artists/ultimate-painting/ |title=Artists - Ultimate Painting - Trouble In Mind Records |website=Troubleinmindrecs.com |access-date=2016-11-07}}</ref> The band were named after a painting made by the [[Drop City]] art community in [[Colorado]] in the late 1960s.<ref name="Trouble In Mind Records2">{{cite web |title=Ultimate Painting "Ultimate Painting" |website=Trouble In Mind Records |url=http://www.troubleinmindrecs.com/ultimate-painting-ultimate-painting/ |access-date=2019-02-16}}</ref>

==History==
The band were formed in 2014 by [[Blackpool]]-born singer-guitarist Jack Cooper, who previously fronted [[Mazes (band)|Mazes]] and co-fronted [[The Beep Seals|Beep Seals]] with ex-[[Alfie (band)|Alfie]] guitarist Ian Smith,<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=pnC_CAAAQBAJ&q=%22jack+cooper%22+%22beep+seals%22&pg=PT244 |title=The Ballad of Britain: How Music Captured The Soul of a Nation |author=Will Hodgkinson |page=244 |date= May 2012|isbn=9781907554766 |access-date=2016-11-07}}</ref> and [[Torbay]]-born singer-guitarist James Hoare, who co-fronts Proper Ornaments and also co-fronted [[Veronica Falls]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Marc Masters |url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/20842-green-lanes/ |title=Ultimate Painting: Green Lanes Album Review |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2015-08-03 |access-date=2016-11-07}}</ref> 

The band signed to Trouble in Mind and released their self-titled debut album in 2014. Follow-up album ''Green Lanes'' was released in 2015,<ref>{{cite web |last=Lucas |first=Dan |url=http://drownedinsound.com/releases/18947/reviews/4149275 |title=Album Review: Ultimate Painting - Green Lanes / Releases |website=Drownedinsound.com |date=2015-08-13 |access-date=2016-11-07 |archive-date=17 September 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160917194904/http://drownedinsound.com/releases/18947/reviews/4149275 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and the third album ''Dusk'' on 30 September 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aquariumdrunkard.com/2016/07/12/ultimate-painting-dusk/ |title=Ultimate Painting :: Dusk |publisher=Aquarium Drunkard |access-date=2016-11-07}}</ref>

In October 2017, the band signed to UK label [[Bella Union]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://bellaunion.com/2017/10/ultimate-painting-sign-to-bella-union/|title=Ultimate Painting sign to Bella Union {{!}} Bella Union|website=bellaunion.com|language=en-US|access-date=2019-02-16}}</ref> and it was confirmed that the band were working on their next album at James Hoare’s home studio.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ultimatepainting.band/ |title=Ultimate Painting: News |website=Ultimatepainting.band|access-date=November 7, 2017}}</ref> On 29 January 2018, the band released ''Not Gonna Burn Myself Anymore'' as a digital single<ref name="Union 2018">{{cite web |last=Union |first=Bella |title=Not Gonna Burn Myself Anymore - Ultimate Painting |website=Qobuz |url=https://www.qobuz.com/gb-en/album/not-gonna-burn-myself-anymore-ultimate-painting/jq4bvhszxa44a |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180129195727/https://www.qobuz.com/gb-en/album/not-gonna-burn-myself-anymore-ultimate-painting/jq4bvhszxa44a |archive-date=2018-01-29 |url-status=dead}}</ref> and revealed the release date of fourth album ''Up!'' for April.<ref>[https://www.qobuz.com/gb-en/album/up-ultimate-painting/qzvwzeb8eueuc] {{Dead link|date=February 2019|fix-attempted=yes}}</ref>

On 12 February 2018, Jack Cooper announced that the band were splitting up, adding that "the partnership at the core of this band has always been a very fragile thing, but due to an irreconcilable breakdown we will no longer be working with each other". The previously announced album release was cancelled at Cooper's express request and the UK tour to support it was also cancelled.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/UltimatePaintingOfficial/posts/1446266252150122|title=Ultimate Painting|date=2018-02-12|website=Facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2019-02-16}}</ref> Cooper commented on 6 April 2018, the proposed release day of ''Up!'', that he had "had lots of emails, messages and questions about the unreleased UP album ever coming out. I reeeaaally appreciate the curiosity but no, it isn’t coming out. I’m onto to something really fresh and exciting so sit tight!".<ref name="Twitter 2018">{{cite web |title=Jack Cooper on Twitter |website=Twitter |url=https://twitter.com/jackc00per/status/982222255689117696 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180408134548/https://twitter.com/jackc00per/status/982222255689117696 |archive-date=8 April 2018 |date=2018-04-06 |url-status=dead |access-date=8 April 2018 }}</ref>
