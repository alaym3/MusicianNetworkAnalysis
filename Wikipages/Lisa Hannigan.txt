'''Lisa Margaret Hannigan''' (born 12 February 1981) is an [[Irish people|Irish]] musician, singer, composer, and voice actress. She began her musical career as a member of [[Damien Rice]]'s band. Since beginning her solo career in 2007 she has released three albums: ''[[Sea Sew]]'' (2008), ''[[Passenger (Lisa Hannigan album)|Passenger]]'' (2011), and ''[[At Swim (album)|At Swim]]'' (2016). Hannigan's music has received award nominations both in Ireland and the United States. Hannigan also received attention in North America for her role as Blue Diamond in ''[[Steven Universe]]'', an American animated television series created by [[Rebecca Sugar]].

==Early life and education==
Hannigan was born in Dublin but grew up in [[Kilcloon]], County Meath, Ireland.<ref name="Hannigan shenanigans" /> She attended primary school at Scoil Oilibhéir Naofa<ref>[http://www.kilcloonns.com/ News] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120402124002/http://www.kilcloonns.com/ |date= 2 April 2012 }} at Scoil Oilibhéir Naofa web site</ref> in Kilcloon and secondary school at [[The King's Hospital]] in [[Palmerstown]] and enrolled at [[Trinity College, Dublin]] to study French and art history.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/Lisa+Hannigan |title=Lisa Hannigan Discography |publisher=Discogs|access-date=2016-08-18}}</ref>

== Career ==
While still in college, Hannigan met Damien Rice at a concert in Dublin in early 2001.<ref name="RTÉ Other Voices 2007" /><ref name="RTÉ Rice & Hannigan Split" /> Rice enlisted Hannigan to sing on his 2002 album ''[[O (Damien Rice album)|O]]'' and his later album "[[9 (Damien Rice album)|9]]", featured in the hit "[[9 Crimes]]". She toured with Rice as part of his band during that period,<ref name="RTÉ Other Voices 2007">{{cite web|url=http://www.rte.ie/tv/othervoices/hanniganl.html |title=Lisa Hannigan |access-date=4 February 2009 |work=[[Other Voices (Irish TV series)|Other Voices]] |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090110134539/http://www.rte.ie/tv/othervoices/hanniganl.html |archive-date=10 January 2009 }}</ref><ref name="Lisa Hannigan interview for her album 'See Sew'" /> lending vocal support and occasionally playing guitar, bass or drums.<ref name="RTÉ Other Voices 2007" />

In 2007, Hannigan returned to Dublin and began a solo career.<ref name="RTÉ Rice & Hannigan Split">{{cite web|url=http://www.rte.ie/ten/news/2007/0327/409476-riced/|title=Rice & Hannigan no longer working together|date=27 March 2007|access-date=4 February 2009|publisher=[[Raidió Teilifís Éireann]]}}</ref><ref name="Lisa Hannigan interview for her album 'See Sew'" /><ref name="Irish Independent Musters Chutzpah" /><ref name="Hannigan shenanigans" /><ref name="Damien Rice: The world's most reluctant pop star">{{cite news|author=Nick Duerden|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/damien-rice-the-worlds-most-reluctant-pop-star-494677.html|title=Damien Rice: The world's most reluctant pop star|date=19 June 2005|access-date=5 February 2009|work=The Independent  | location=London|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090225125129/http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/damien-rice-the-worlds-most-reluctant-pop-star-494677.html|archive-date=25 February 2009}}</ref> Some of Hannigan's live recordings were made available through trading networks radio shows. These recordings included: "Willy" by [[Joni Mitchell]], "Be My Husband" by [[Nina Simone]] (from the 1965 album ''[[Pastel Blues]]''), "Mercedes Benz" by [[Janis Joplin]] and "Love Hurts" by [[Boudleaux Bryant]]. Hannigan also performed live with her own band, called The Daisy Okell Quartet and contributed guest vocals to the recordings of [[Mic Christopher]], [[The Frames]] and [[Herbie Hancock]].

===Soundtracks and film work===
[[File:HF2014 03-Lisa Hannigan-01.jpg|thumb|upright=1.3| Lisa Hannigan at Halfway Festival 2014 in Białystok, Poland.]]
In 2004, Hannigan credited on soundtrack for ''[[Closer (2004 film)|Closer]]'' (song: "Cold Water", arranged and performed by Damien Rice and Lisa Hannigan, featuring Vyvienne Long).

In 2007, Hannigan credited on soundtrack for ''[[Shrek the Third]]'' (song: "9 Crimes", performed by Damien Rice and Lisa Hannigan).

In 2009, Hannigan credited on soundtrack for ''[[Ondine (film)|Ondine]]'' (songs: "Braille", "Lille", written and performed by Lisa Hannigan).{{R|IMDBOndine}}

In 2013, Hannigan contributed vocals to the soundtrack for Alfonso Cuarón's film ''[[Gravity: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack|Gravity]]'' and performed a cover of [[Richard_Hawley]]'s "You Haunt Me" for the film ''[[Another_Me_(film)|Another Me]]''.

In 2014, Hannigan provided vocals along with John Smith for the Steven Price conducted soundtrack for the Brad Pitt blockbuster ''[[Fury (2014 film)|Fury]]''.

In 2014, she voiced the character of Bronagh, the [[Selkie]] mother of the main characters in the animated film ''[[Song of the Sea (2014 film)|Song of the Sea]]'', and provided several songs to the film's soundtrack.

In 2015, she sang a version of "[[Danny Boy]]" for the seventh episode of the second season of ''[[Fargo (TV series)|Fargo]]''.

In 2017, she started voicing the character Blue Diamond for the television series ''[[Steven Universe]]'' which was made by [[Rebecca Sugar]]. She also performed a cover of [[David Bowie]]'s "[[Oh! You Pretty Things]]" for the closing credits of the sixth episode of the first season of ''[[Legion (TV series)|Legion]]''.

In 2018, she arranged and performed a version of the traditional Irish song "[[Weela Weela Walya|Weile Weile Waile]]" for the soundtrack of the movie ''[[The Hole in the Ground (film)|The Hole in the Ground]]''.

In 2019, she reprised her role as Blue Diamond for the television film ''[[Steven Universe: The Movie]]''. In 2020, returned to the role once more in the follow-up series ''[[Steven Universe Future]]''.

===Other work===
In 2020, Hannigan was part of an [[Irish people|Irish]] collective of female singers and musicians called "[[Irish Women in Harmony]]", that recorded a version of the song "[[Dreams (The Cranberries song)|Dreams]]" in aid of the charity Safe Ireland, which deals with [[domestic abuse]] which had reportedly risen significantly during the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the Republic of Ireland|Covid-19 lockdown]].<ref name="it-1">{{cite news |last=Boland |first=Rosita |date=19 June 2020|title=Irish Women in Harmony record Cranberries song in aid of Safe Ireland |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/irish-women-in-harmony-record-cranberries-song-in-aid-of-safe-ireland-1.4283348 |access-date=28 July 2020 |work=The Irish Times |language=en}}</ref><ref name="mcd-1">{{cite web |title=Irish Women in Harmony |url=https://mcd.ie/irish-women-in-harmony/ |website=MCD.ie |access-date=28 July 2020 |date=n.d.}}</ref>

==Reception==
Her music has received critical praise, airplay and award nominations both in her native Ireland and the United States. In 2008, her debut album ''Sea Sew'' spawned the single "[[Lille (song)|Lille]]", a [[Choice Music Prize]] nomination, two [[Meteor Music Awards]] nominations and a [[Mercury Prize]] nomination.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Choice Music Prize Shortlist |url=https://www.hotpress.com/music/choice-music-prize-shortlist-5171298 |website=Hot Press |date= 14 January 2009 |access-date= 29 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/blogs/ontherecord/2009/01/27/meteor-music-awards-folks-wake-up-and-shut-down-website/|title=Meteor Music Awards folks wake up and shut down website|date=2009-01-27|access-date=29 April 2020|work=[[The Irish Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=BBC - Mercury Prize 2009 - Lisa Hannigan |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/musicevents/mercuryprize/2009/nominations/lisahannigan/ |publisher=[[BBC]] |access-date=29 April 2020}}</ref>

Hannigan performs using "broken-down, wheezy old instruments".<ref name="Lisa Hannigan interview for her album 'See Sew'" /> [[Herbie Hancock]] said of her vocals, "there's so much jazz in the notes and phrases that she picks. She was singing the ninths, the elevenths of the chords...I mean some of the things sound like choices that [[Miles Davis|Miles]] would have made."<ref>Documentary film "Herbie Hancock: Possibilities", ©2006 HDNet Films LLC; ©2006 Magnolia Home Entertainment</ref>

==Personal life==
Hannigan briefly studied English and art history at [[Trinity College, Dublin|Trinity College]] in Dublin. During her first week at university she became friends with Irish singer-songwriter [[Damien Rice]].<ref name="soundcloud.com">{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/irishtimes-lifestyle/lisa-hannigan-music-month |title=Lisa Hannigan MUSIC MONTH by Róisín Meets... &#124; Free Listening on SoundCloud |website=Soundcloud.com |access-date=2016-08-18}}</ref> Hannigan eventually left university to travel with Rice across Europe.{{citation needed|date=February 2021}}

Returning to Ireland, both Hannigan and Rice began to work together recording music, including songs like "Unplayed Piano" and "Once I Loved." Hannigan featured on Rice's first two solo albums and a number of EP's and live recordings. Both Irish artists toured for a number of years together and were in a relationship, but in 2007 Hannigan left Damien Rice and his band<ref>{{cite web|author=John Meagher |url=http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/damien-rice-hits-heartbreak-and-an-eightyear-silence-30267064.html |title=Damien Rice: hits, heartbreak and an eight-year silence |website=The Irish Independent |date=2014-05-13 |access-date=2016-08-18}}</ref> the afternoon before a live show in [[Munich]].<ref name="soundcloud.com"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eskimofriends.com/forum/showthread.php?t=15616 |title=Lisa Hannigan on being dumped by Damien Rice and going solo - The Igloo |website=Eskimofriends.com |date=2010-05-26 |access-date=2016-08-18}}</ref> Rice released a statement saying that their professional relationship had "''run its creative course''" in March 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/4917/lisa_hannigan_and_damien_rice_part_ways/news/ |title=Lisa Hannigan And Damien Rice Part Ways |website=Stereogum.com |date=2007-03-26 |access-date=2016-08-18}}</ref>

Returning to Dublin, from 2007 onward Hannigan began to focus on her solo career which to date has produced three solo albums. In 2015, Hannigan decided to move from Dublin to London to begin a new life, which involved beginning to write new material for her third studio album ''[[At Swim (album)|At Swim]]''. She also started pursuing a part-time degree in English literature.

As of 2016, Hannigan planned to return to Dublin with her London-based husband following her ''At Swim'' tour of Europe and the US.<ref>{{cite web|author=Catherine Conroy |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/lisa-hannigan-i-started-to-really-lose-sight-of-what-the-point-of-me-was-1.2743825 |title=Lisa Hannigan: 'I started to really lose sight of what the point of me was' |website=The Irish Times |date=2016-08-04 |access-date=2016-08-18}}</ref><ref name=":0"/>

===Mercury Prize===
''Sea Sew'' was nominated for the [[Mercury Prize]] on 21 July 2009.<ref name="Hannigan makes Mercury prize shortlist">{{cite web|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/breaking/2009/0721/breaking40.htm|title=Hannigan makes Mercury prize shortlist|date=21 July 2009|access-date=24 July 2009|work=The Irish Times }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/various-artists/46222|title=Barclaycard Mercury Prize 2009: Nominees announced|work=NME|date=21 July 2009|access-date=23 July 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|access-date=20 May 2009|url=http://www.snowpatrol.com/news/default.aspx?nid=6945|title='Eyes Open' shortlisted for Choice Music Prize!!!|publisher=Snow Patrol}}</ref> It was Hannigan's first nomination.

Hannigan was referred to as 2009's "token folk nominee" in the UK,<ref name="Mercury is rising for our sensitive songbird"/> with the ''[[NME]]'' calling her a "token folkie".<ref name="Mercury Nominations – Who's Been Overlooked?">{{cite web|author=Luke Lewis |url=https://www.nme.com/blog/index.php?blog=10&title=mercury_nominations_who_s_been_overlooke&more=1&c=1&tb=1&pb=1 |title=Mercury Nominations – Who's Been Overlooked |date=21 July 2009 |access-date=25 July 2009 |work=NME|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090726120902/http://www.nme.com/blog/index.php?blog=10&title=mercury_nominations_who_s_been_overlooke&more=1&c=1&tb=1&pb=1 |archive-date=26 July 2009 }}</ref> One British journalist even claimed she was "truly obscure" and part of the "moribund sensitive singer-songwriter genre".<ref name="Mercury is rising for our sensitive songbird"/> Ed Power, writing in the ''[[Irish Independent]]'', criticised such claims, wondering if [[Jape (band)|Jape]] (whose album ''[[Ritual (Jape album)|Ritual]]'' beat Hannigan to the Choice Music Prize) had come close to receiving a Mercury nomination—"Or, for that matter, how many of the judges had even heard of him".<ref name="Mercury is rising for our sensitive songbird">{{cite web|author=Ed Power|url=http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/mercury-is-rising-for-our-sensitive-songbird-1840253.html|title=Mercury is rising for our sensitive songbird|date=25 July 2009|access-date=25 July 2009|work=[[Irish Independent]]}}</ref>
