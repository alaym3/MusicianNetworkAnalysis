'''Marvin William Bailey''' (born 6 March 1994), known professionally as '''Fredo''', is a British rapper and singer from [[Queen's Park, London]]. He had a UK number-one single in 2018 with fellow rapper [[Dave (rapper)|Dave]], called "[[Funky Friday]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/51266/fredo/|title=Fredo &#124; full Official Chart History &#124; Official Charts Company|website=Officialcharts.com}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Fredo grew up in [[Queen's Park, London]].<ref name="MEN" /><ref name="Vice" /> His mother is of English and Moroccan descent and his father is from [[Barbados]]. He spent most of his childhood growing up in West London and Hertfordshire, where he also attended school and was expelled before his [[General Certificate of Secondary Education|GCSEs]]. He lived on West London's Mozart Estate where he was surrounded by gang violence and crime.<ref>https://www.umusicpub.com/uk/Digital-Music-Library/song/386231/fredo-young-t-bugsey-ay-caramba {{dead link|date=May 2020}}</ref> During his teenage years he also joined the Mozart Estate Gang (also known as Pattern Gang).

Fredo spent time in prison on remand in 2016 and 2017, both times for stabbing-related charges that were dropped before reaching court. He has also been the victim of a stabbing attack, having been stabbed four times on one occasion on his local high street.<ref>{{Cite news|title=London rapper Fredo on the neighbourhood that shaped his music|url=https://www.ft.com/content/5355f564-288a-11e9-88a4-c32129756dd8|url-status=live|access-date=29 April 2021|newspaper=Financial Times|date=7 February 2019|last1=Hunter-Tilney|first1=Ludovic}}</ref>

During Fredo's youth, he listened to [[Giggs (rapper)|Giggs]] and [[50 Cent]], stating "I remember I used to bang ''[[Get Rich or Die Tryin']]'' every day".<ref>{{Cite web|title=UK Rap Star Fredo Isn't Resting On His he Laurels|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/02/fredo-interview|access-date=29 April 2021|website=Complex}}</ref>

== Career ==
Fredo released his first track "They Ain't 100" in March 2016. Three weeks later he went to prison, on a charge he later beat.<ref name="Vice">{{cite web | url = https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/a3bvnk/fredo-third-avenue-album-interview-2019 | title = Fredo: "I've Spent More Helping My Friends in Prison Than in Harrods" | work = [[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] | last = Thapar | first = Ciaran| date = 1 February 2019 | access-date = 16 December 2019 }}</ref> Whilst he was in prison, the track gained popularity, with radio play and millions of views. Inspired by its success, and despite a second stint in prison, he persisted in his newfound rapping and recording career, releasing two mixtapes, ''Get Rich or Get Recalled'' in 2017, and in 2018 ''Tables Turn'', which reached the Top 10 in the album charts.<ref name="FT">{{Cite web |last=Hunter-Tilney |first=Ludovic |date=7 February 2019 |title=London rapper Fredo on the neighbourhood that shaped his music |url=https://www.ft.com/content/5355f564-288a-11e9-88a4-c32129756dd8 |url-access=subscription |access-date=15 February 2019 |website=[[Financial Times]]}}</ref> He also appeared as a featured artist on tracks by [[Kojo Funds]] and [[Young T & Bugsey]], as well as on [[Dave (rapper)|Dave]]'s 2018 hit single "[[Funky Friday]]".

Fredo's first full-length album, ''[[Third Avenue (album)|Third Avenue]]'', was released on 1 February 2019. Produced mostly by JB,<ref name="Vice" /> it marked Fredo's debut on [[RCA Records|RCA]]'s Since '93 imprint. It included "Love You for That", a track dedicated to his mother and apologising for not being the perfect son.<ref name="MEN">{{cite web | url = https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/whats-on/fredo-manchester-interview-o2-ritz-15860793 | title = UK rapper Fredo: "My mum's a really big part of my life" | work = [[Manchester Evening News]] | last = Sue | first = David| date = 2 February 2019 | access-date = 16 December 2019 }}</ref> The album takes its title from the West London housing estate he grew up on.

In January 2021, Fredo released his second album ''Money Can't Buy Happiness'', which contained features from [[Pop Smoke]], [[Dave (rapper)|Dave]], [[Summer Walker]] and [[D-Block Europe|Young Adz]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=29 January 2021|title=Fredo releases new album 'Money Can't Buy Happiness'|url=https://www.sonymusic.co.uk/fredo-releases-new-album-money-cant-buy-happiness/|access-date=29 April 2021|website=Sony Music UK}}</ref> It peaked at number two on the UK Albums Chart.<ref>{{Cite web|title=money can't buy happiness {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/search/albums/money-can%27t-buy-happiness/|access-date=9 August 2021|website=officialcharts.com}}</ref>

On 24 June 2021, Fredo announced his third album ''Independence Day'' which released on 5 August 2021. The album announced that his music would be released independently.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Stream Fredo's 'Independence Day' Album f/ Headie One, Potter Payper, Clavish & More|url=https://www.complex.com/music/fredo-independence-day-album-stream|access-date=9 August 2021|website=Complex}}</ref>
