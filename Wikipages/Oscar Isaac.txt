'''Óscar Isaac Hernández Estrada''' (born March 9, 1979)<!-- See reference; Isaac states that he is 34, in an interview published on December 5, 2013. --><ref>{{cite web |url=http://abclocal.go.com/kabc/story?id=9350650 |title=Oscar Isaac says he can relate to 'Llewyn Davis' character &#124; abc7.com |publisher=Abclocal.go.com |date=December 5, 2013 |access-date=March 7, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140223222429/http://abclocal.go.com/kabc/story?id=9350650 |archive-date=February 23, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/12/23/oscar-isaac-a-most-violent-year_n_6284630.html |title=Oscar Isaac Had A Most Excellent Year |last=Rosen |first=Christopher |work=HuffPost |access-date=April 5, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.recapo.com/live-with-kelly-ripa/live-with-kelly-interviews/kelly-michael-inside-llewyn-davis-star-oscar-isaac-real-birthday/|title=Kelly + Michael: Inside Llewyn Davis Star Oscar Isaac Real Birthday|first=Pat|last=Howard|work=Recapo|access-date=April 5, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170821125428/http://www.recapo.com/live-with-kelly-ripa/live-with-kelly-interviews/kelly-michael-inside-llewyn-davis-star-oscar-isaac-real-birthday/|archive-date=August 21, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> is a Guatemalan and American actor. He starred in the [[tragicomedy]] ''[[Inside Llewyn Davis]]'' (2013), for which he received a [[Golden Globe Award]] nomination, the crime drama ''[[A Most Violent Year]]'' (2014), and the science fiction thriller ''[[Ex Machina (film)|Ex Machina]]'' (2014).

In 2006, he portrayed [[Saint Joseph]], husband of [[Mary, mother of Jesus|Mary]], in ''[[The Nativity Story]]''. He also portrayed [[José Ramos-Horta]], former president of [[East Timor]] and corecipient of the 1996 Nobel Peace Prize, in the Australian film ''[[Balibo (film)|Balibo]]'', for which he won the [[AACTA Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role]].
He starred in the [[Star Wars sequel trilogy|''Star Wars'' sequel trilogy]] as [[X-wing]] pilot [[Poe Dameron]], and in ''[[X-Men: Apocalypse]]'' (2016) as the titular [[Mutant (Marvel Comics)|mutant]] supervillain [[Apocalypse (comics)|Apocalypse]]. He also headlined the 2015 [[HBO]] miniseries ''[[Show Me a Hero]]'' as politician [[Nick Wasicsko]], which earned him the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actor – Miniseries or Television Film]]. In 2021, he also starred in the [[HBO]] miniseries ''[[Scenes from a Marriage (American miniseries)|Scenes from a Marriage]]'' and the epic science fiction film [[Dune (2021 film)|''Dune'']], directed by [[Denis Villeneuve]]. Isaac will star as [[Moon Knight|Marc Spector / Moon Knight]] in the upcoming [[Disney+]] series ''[[Moon Knight (TV series)|Moon Knight]]'' (2022), which is set in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Vary|first=Adam B.|date=2021-05-27|title=Marvel Finally Confirms Oscar Isaac as 'Moon Knight' Star|url=https://variety.com/2021/tv/news/oscar-isaac-moon-knight-marvel-studios-disney-plus-1234983362/|access-date=2021-05-28|website=Variety|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Breakthrough (2013–present)===

Isaac played [[X-Men]] villain [[Apocalypse (comics)|Apocalypse]] in the film ''[[X-Men: Apocalypse]]'', which was released on May 27, 2016.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://variety.com/2014/film/news/oscar-isaac-x-men-apocalypse-casting-1201342774|title='X-Men: Apocalypse': Oscar Isaac Nabs Villain Role|magazine=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=November 24, 2014|access-date=November 24, 2014}}</ref> Also that year, he played the lead role in the historical drama ''[[The Promise (2016 American film)|The Promise]]'', with costar [[Christian Bale]], in a story about the [[Armenian genocide]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sneider|first1=Jeff|title=Christian Bale, Oscar Isaac to Star in ''The Promise''|url=https://www.thewrap.com/christian-bale-oscar-isaac-to-star-in-terry-georges-epic-love-story-the-promise-exclusive/|website=The Wrap|access-date=January 10, 2016|date=June 15, 2015}}</ref> He narrated the [[Nike, Inc.|Nike]] ad "Unlimited You" created by [[Wieden+Kennedy]].{{citation needed|date=December 2016}} In November 2016, the first episode of the podcast series ''[[Gimlet Media#Homecoming|Homecoming]]'' was released. It is a six-episode psychological thriller from [[Gimlet Media]], costarring [[Catherine Keener]] and [[David Schwimmer]]; Isaac voices a soldier trying to rejoin civilian life.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://gimletmedia.com/show/homecoming/ |title=Homecoming – Gimlet Media |publisher=Gimletmedia.com |access-date=December 21, 2016}}</ref>

In July 2017, Isaac starred as [[Prince Hamlet]] in [[The Public Theater]] production of ''[[Hamlet]]'' alongside [[Keegan-Michael Key]], [[Ritchie Coster]], [[Gayle Rankin]], [[Roberta Colindrez]], and [[Anatol Yusef]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2017/legit/reviews/hamlet-review-oscar-isaac-1202493640/|title=Off Broadway Review: Oscar Isaac in 'Hamlet'|last=Stasio|first=Marilyn|date=July 14, 2017|work=Variety|access-date=August 17, 2017}}</ref> The production, directed by Sam Gold, ran until September 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.publictheater.org/Public-Theater-Season/Hamlet/|title=Hamlet|website=publictheater.org|access-date=August 17, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170818004628/https://www.publictheater.org/Public-Theater-Season/Hamlet/|archive-date=August 18, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> His performance received acclaim from critics and audiences alike.<ref name="vf" /> Also that year, Isaac appeared in the black comedy ''[[Suburbicon]]'', directed by [[George Clooney]] and written by the Coen Brothers.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2016/film/news/paramount-george-clooney-matt-damon-comedy-suburbicon-1201705681/|title=Paramount Buys George Clooney-Matt Damon Comedy ''Suburbicon''|first=Dave |last=McNary|author-link=Dave McNary|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=February 13, 2016|access-date=May 11, 2016}}</ref> It was his second role in a Coens-written film, after ''Inside Llewyn Davis''.

Isaac costarred with [[Natalie Portman]] in the [[Annihilation (film)|film adaptation]] of the novel ''[[Annihilation (VanderMeer novel)|Annihilation]]'', playing her character's husband.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2016/film/news/oscar-isaac-natalie-portman-annihilation-1201742054/|title=Oscar Isaac Joins Natalie Portman's ''Annihilation''|first=Justin |last=Kroll|work=Variety|date=March 30, 2016|access-date=May 11, 2016}}</ref> Directed by [[Alex Garland]], the role is Isaac's second collaboration with the director, after ''Ex Machina''. In late-January 2019, Isaac was cast as [[Leto I Atreides|Duke Leto Atreides]] in the 2021 [[Denis Villeneuve]] film ''[[Dune (2021 film)|Dune]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/dune-movie-casts-oscar-isaac-1180655 | title= Oscar Isaac Joining Denis Villeneuve's ''Dune'' | first1=  Borys | last1 = Kit | first2=  Aaron | last2 = Couch |date = January 29, 2019 | access-date = January 30, 2019 | work = The Hollywood Reporter }}</ref> In 2019, Isaac was cast in the Paul Schrader film ''[[The Card Counter]]'' and starred in the action-adventure film ''[[Triple Frontier (film)|Triple Frontier]]'' directed by [[J. C. Chandor]] as Pope, an ex-[[Delta Force|CAG]] operative.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/film/news/oscar-isaac-to-star-in-paul-schraders-next-film-the-card-counter-1203386770/|title=Oscar Isaac to Star in Paul Schrader's Next Film 'The Card Counter' (EXCLUSIVE)|last=Kroll|first=Justin|date=October 29, 2019|website=Variety|access-date=October 29, 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Isaac is an accomplished singer and [[guitar player]], having played since the age of twelve.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/film/oscar-isaac/#_|title=Oscar Isaac|website=Interview|date=December 17, 2013|access-date=December 18, 2013}}</ref> He has been dubbed a [[sex symbol]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.someecards.com/entertainment/movies/oscar-isaac-bill-murray/|title='Star Wars' sex symbol Oscar Isaac performs Bill Murray's classic parody of the theme song.|website=someecards.com|access-date=May 4, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160918210008/http://www.someecards.com/entertainment/movies/oscar-isaac-bill-murray/|archive-date=September 18, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://decider.com/2015/05/05/oscar-isaac-dont-become-a-heartthrob/|title=Oscar Isaac, Please Don't Become A Heartthrob|website=decider.com|date=May 5, 2015|access-date=May 5, 2015}}</ref> and "the Internet's Boyfriend" by multiple media outlets, most notably ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine, though he has expressed skepticism about the label.<ref name="rolling stone"/>

In December 2007, he became engaged to Maria Miranda.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Oscar Isaac due for Miranda rites|url=https://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/gossip/oscar-isaac-due-miranda-rites-article-1.277088|access-date=2021-06-09|website=nydailynews.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Oscar Isaac's Dating History Is Incredibly Confusing, So Let's Solve This Once & For All|url=https://www.bustle.com/articles/135662-oscar-isaacs-dating-history-is-incredibly-confusing-so-lets-solve-this-once-for-all|access-date=2021-06-09|website=Bustle|language=en}}</ref> In a 2010 interview, he mentioned having a wife.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Lopez|first=John|title=How to Train Your Villain with Robin Hood's Oscar Isaac|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2010/05/how-to-train-your-villain-with-robin-hoods-oscar-isaac|access-date=2021-06-09|magazine=Vanity Fair|date=May 10, 2010|language=en-US}}</ref>

He met Danish film director [[Elvira Lind]] in 2012,<ref>{{Cite news|title=Not My Job: Actor Oscar Isaac On Folk Music Vs. Filk Music|url=https://www.npr.org/2013/12/14/250791919/not-my-job-actor-oscar-isaac-on-folk-music-vs-filk-music|website=NPR.org|language=en|access-date=2020-05-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Elvira N Lind on Instagram: "After 15 fabulous years together this slightly slutty yet also maternal vintage dress (that helped me lure in my now husband 8 years ago)…"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B9VA_LchCzI/|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-05-02}}</ref> and they married in February 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.popsugar.com/latina/Oscar-Isaac-Facts-39682554?stream_view=1#photo-39682565|title=11 Things You Must Know About the Internet's New Boyfriend, Oscar Isaac|website=POPSUGAR.com|access-date=May 4, 2016}}</ref> They have two sons: Eugene, born April 2017,<ref>{{cite web|author=Alexis Soloski |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/07/06/theater/oscar-isaacs-mom-died-now-hes-working-out-his-grief-in-hamlet/ |title=After His Mother's Death, Oscar Isaac Turns to Shakespeare for Solace |website=The New York Times |access-date=July 7, 2017}}</ref> and Mads, born October 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://people.com/parents/oscar-isaac-welcomes-second-child-son-elvira-lind/|title=Oscar Isaac Welcomes a Son with Wife Elvira Lind|last=Mizoguchi|first=Karen|date=October 3, 2019|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=7 Famous Babies with Celebrity Parents |url=https://poweredbyorange.com/7-famous-babies-with-celebrity-parents/ |website=poweredbyorange.com|date=March 14, 2020 }}</ref> The family lives in [[Williamsburg, Brooklyn]].<ref name="Zach baron">{{cite magazine |last1=Baron |first1=Zach |title=Oscar Isaac Talks Annihilation, Star Wars, and the Most Turbulent Year of His Life |url=https://www.gq.com/story/oscar-isaac-gq-style-spring-cover-story |access-date=February 16, 2019 |magazine=GQ |date=February 20, 2018}}</ref>

Isaac is a [[United States nationality law#Naturalization|naturalized]] [[United States citizen]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://issuu.com/spafax/docs/118-in_enero_2014_web/34|title=Oscar Isaac and His Secret Weapon|magazine=In Magazine|first=Gabriel|last=Lerman|date=January 2, 2014|access-date=November 3, 2021}}</ref> He is bilingual in English and Spanish.<ref>{{Citation|title=Oscar Isaac on Being Bilingual|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xiEMAxgBFhM|language=en|access-date=2021-03-05}}</ref>
