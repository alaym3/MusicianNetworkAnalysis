'''Roy Alexander James Blair''' (13 June 1921 &ndash; 31 May 2002) was a [[New Zealand]] [[roller skating|roller skater]], [[speed skating|speed skater]], [[cricket]]er and golfer. He was born and died in [[Dunedin]]. He worked as a teacher, and in the Second World war served with the Air Force.

==Skating==
Blair was New Zealand's best roller skater between 1937 and 1941, winning 9 national titles on the 440 yards, 880 yards, and mile races. He then switched to speed skating, winning the quarter mile race at the national championships in 1947 and 1948.<ref name="Meikie">{{cite news |last1=Meikie |first1=Hayden |title=Greatest moments in Otago sport - number 131 |url=https://www.odt.co.nz/greatest-moments-otago-sport-number-131 |access-date=18 January 2021 |work=Otago Daily Times |date=14 June 2011}}</ref>

==Cricket==
He was a left-handed batsman and right-arm off-break bowler who played for [[Otago cricket team|Otago]]. 

Blair made a single first-class appearance for the side, during the 1953â€“54 season, against [[Canterbury cricket team|Canterbury]]. From the opening order, he scored a duck in the first innings in which he batted, and when positioned further down in the order for the second innings, he scored 2 runs.

Blair's sons, [[Bruce Blair|Bruce]] and [[Wayne Blair (cricketer)|Wayne]], and uncle [[James Blair (cricketer)|James]] also played first-class cricket.
