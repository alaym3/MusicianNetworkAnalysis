'''Cleopatrick''' (stylized in lowercase) is a [[Canada|Canadian]] [[hard rock]] duo based out of [[Cobourg]], [[Ontario]]. They have their own record label, called Nowhere Special Recordings.<ref>{{Cite web|title=cleopatrick have announced their debut album, 'Bummer'|url=https://www.upsetmagazine.com/news/cleopatrick-album-bummer|access-date=2021-05-16|website=Upset|language=en}}</ref>

== Background ==
Childhood friends Ian Fraser and Luke Gruntz grew up in [[Cobourg|Cobourg, Ontario]] had been friends since age 4<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wisniewski |first1=Dominik |title=Cobourg band cleopatrick sees growth in fan base around the world |url=https://www.northumberlandnews.com/whatson-story/8130122-cobourg-band-cleopatrick-sees-growth-in-fan-base-around-the-world/ |website=Northumberland News |accessdate=November 18, 2018 |date=February 13, 2018}}</ref> and began getting interested in music around age 8 when they first listened to [[AC/DC]]. Later in their teens they began recording their own music.

Their debut [[Extended play|EP]], titled '"14"' was released on 14 February 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|title=14, by cleopatrick|url=https://cleopatrick.bandcamp.com/album/14|access-date=2021-04-21|website=cleopatrick}}</ref> though they began to gain popularity with single "Hometown". Released in 2017, it peaked at No. 6 on the US Mainstream Rock Charts.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mainstream Rock Airplay Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-mainstream-rock-tracks/2021-02-06|access-date=2021-04-21|website=Billboard}}</ref> It was taken from their second EP, "[[The Boys (Cleopatrick EP)|The Boys]]", which was released on 29 June 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=the boys, by cleopatrick|url=https://cleopatrick.bandcamp.com/album/the-boys|access-date=2021-04-21|website=cleopatrick}}</ref> The band's first music festival was the 2018 instalment of [[Lollapalooza]] in [[Chicago]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pollock |first1=Matt |title=Lollapalooza Lineup 2018: Bruno Mars, the Weeknd, and a Conspicuous Absence of Women |url=https://www.chicagomag.com/arts-culture/March-2018/Lollapalooza-Lineup-2018-Bruno-Mars-the-Weeknd-and-a-Conspicuous-Absence-of-Women/ |website=[[Chicago (magazine)|Chicago Magazine]] |publisher=[[Tribune Publishing]] |accessdate=November 18, 2018 |date=March 21, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=cleopatrick - Lollapalooza |url=https://www.lollapalooza.com/artist/cleopatrick/ |website=lollapalooza.com |accessdate=November 17, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181118081557/https://www.lollapalooza.com/artist/cleopatrick/ |archive-date=November 18, 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and continuing into 2019 they played many festivals, including [[Two Thousand Trees Festival|2000 Trees Festival]] in [[England]] and [[Shaky Knees Music Festival]] in [[Atlanta]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Korstanje |first1=Emma |title=Tame Impala, Beck, Cage The Elephant, Incubus to Headline Shaky Knees 2019 |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2018/11/shaky-knees-lineup-announced.html |website=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |accessdate=November 18, 2018 |date=November 14, 2018}}</ref>  Non-album single "Sanjake" was released in March 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Exclusive: cleopatrick's New Track Is Driving Hard Rock With A Cheeky Edge|url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/exclusive-cleopatricks-new-track-is-driving-hard-rock-with-a-cheeky-edge/|access-date=2021-04-21|website=Kerrang!}}</ref>

On 11 December 2020, they released the single "Good Grief", the first single of their debut album, titled "Bummer".<ref>{{Cite web|title=cleopatrick have shared a video for their swaggering new single, 'Good Grief'|url=https://www.upsetmagazine.com/news/cleopatrick-have-shared-a-video-for-their-swaggering-new-single-good-grief|access-date=2021-04-21|website=Upset|language=en}}</ref> This was followed on 11 March 2021 with second single "The Drake".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Taylor|first=Sam|date=2021-03-12|title=cleopatrick have shared their new video and single, 'The Drake' {{!}} Dork|url=https://readdork.com/news/cleopatrick-the-drake/|access-date=2021-04-21|website=readdork.com|language=en-US}}</ref> The third single, "Family Van" was released on 20 April 2021 alongside the announcement of their debut album, which was released on 4 June 2021.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Childers, Chad |date=April 21, 2021 |title=Cleopatrick Reveal New Song + Game 'Family Van,' Announce 'Bummer' Album |url=https://loudwire.com/cleopatrick-family-van-song-game-lyrics-bummer-album/ |access-date=April 21, 2021 |website=Loudwire}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
The band has drawn comparisons in their sound and personal reflection of music to bands such as [[Highly Suspect]], [[Royal Blood (band)|Royal Blood]], [[Arctic Monkeys]], and [[Catfish and the Bottlemen]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Your new favourite bands, according to Radio 1's indie expert Jack Saunders |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/articles/4W1mwj404142M5dh1DN9GzV/your-new-favourite-bands-according-to-radio-1s-indie-expert-jack-saunders |website=[[BBC Radio 1]] |publisher=[[BBC]] |accessdate=November 17, 2018 |date=September 6, 2018}}</ref>

The band has also stated [[AC/DC]], [[Highly Suspect]], Zig Mentality, and Ready The Prince as their biggest musical influences, along with the stylings and musical risk taking associated with modern [[Hip hop music|Hip hop]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://bigtakeover.com/interviews/InterviewLukeGruntzandIanFraserofcleopatrick| title=Interview: Luke Gruntz and Ian Fraser of cleopatrick |website= [[The Big Takeover]] | access-date= June 4, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.moshville.co.uk/feature/botd/2018/05/band-of-the-day-cleopatrick/| title=Band of the Day: cleopatrick |website= The Moshville Times| access-date= May 2, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.altpress.com/features/cleopatrick-bummer-album-review/| title=Cleopatrick Are Making Guitar Music That Matters on Debut Album 'Bummer' |website= Alternative Press| access-date = June 4, 2021}}</ref>
