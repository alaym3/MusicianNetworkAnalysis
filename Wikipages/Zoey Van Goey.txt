'''Zoey Van Goey''' were an [[indie pop]] [[musical ensemble|band]] from [[Glasgow]], [[Scotland]], made up of Matt Brennan, Michael John McCarthy, Kim Moore and Adam Scott. Brennan, McCarthy and Moore, hailing from Canada, Ireland and England respectively, formed the initial trio in Glasgow in 2006, with Scott becoming a full-fledged member in 2010. The band stopped performing in 2012.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.chemikal.co.uk/artists/zoey-van-goey/|title=Zoey Van Goey|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref> 

The band's debut [[single (music)|single]], "Foxtrot Vandals," was produced by the [[Belle and Sebastian]] frontman [[Stuart Murdoch (musician)|Stuart Murdoch]], and released on seven-inch vinyl in 2007. They released another single, "Sweethearts in Disguise," on seven-inch vinyl in 2008 which was produced by [[Paul Savage (musician)|Paul Savage]] of [[The Delgados]]. 

Their debut [[album]], ''The Cage Was Unlocked All Along'', also produced by Savage, was first self-released in 2009 and then re-released by [[Chemikal Underground Records]]. Their second album, ''Propeller Versus Wings'', was released in 2011.

==Press coverage==
Zoey Van Goey were received onto the Scottish music scene being described as "like the [[The Postal Service|Postal Service]] with a sense of humour,"<ref>{{Citation|last=Paterson|first=Laura|title=Maps are intent on destruction|date=2007-11-12|url=http://www.skinnymag.co.uk/content/view/6017/|publisher=The Skinny|access-date=2008-04-02}}</ref> being included in national press alongside established acts such as [[The Chemical Brothers]].<ref>{{Citation|last=Suttie|first=Heather|title=On My Nano|date=2007-11-06|url=http://www.redorbit.com/news/science/1132135/on_my_nano/index.html?source=r_science|publisher=The Daily Record|access-date=2008-04-02}}</ref>

==Tours and gigs==
In 2007, Glasgow-based writer [[Alan Bissett]] approached the band and [[Y'all is Fantasy Island]] suggesting they perform together. In May 2007, all three performed together in a short tour of [[Central Belt|Central Scotland]]. The three acts, dubbed as ''The Super Puny Humans'' also performed as a collective during the concerts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/zoey-van-goey-mn0001704574/biography|title=Zoey Van Goey {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref> 

In 2008, Zoey Van Goey played the unsigned stages of Scotland's biggest music festivals, including [[T in the Park]], [[Connect Music Festival]], [[Wickerman Festival]], [[Live at Loch Lomond]] and [[Rock Ness]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://belleandsebastian.com/zoey-van-goey/|title=Zoey Van Goey|website=Belle & Sebastian {{!}} Official Site|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref>

In December 2010, Zoey Van Goey played at Bowlie 2, the [[All Tomorrow's Parties (music festival)|All Tomorrow's Parties Festival]] curated by [[Belle and Sebastian]]. In  2011, Zoey Van Goey launched their new album Propeller Versus Wings and supported Belle & Sebastian on their European tour.<ref name=":0" />
