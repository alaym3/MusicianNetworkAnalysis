'''Claudia Alexandra Madriz Meza'''<ref name="birthname" >{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/columns/hip-hop/9415345/snow-tha-product-on-my-sh-freestyle-video|title = Snow Tha Product Turns Her Living Room into a One-Stop Music Shop for 'On My Sh-- Freestyle' Video|date = July 8, 2020}}</ref> (born June 24, 1987) known professionally as '''Snow Tha Product''', is an American rapper, singer, label owner, entrepreneur, and actress.<ref>{{cite web|title=MC Lyte & Snow Tha Product to Guest Star on USA's 'Queen of the South'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7783912/mc-lyte-snow-tha-product-queen-of-the-south|access-date=May 3, 2017}}</ref> She was signed to [[Atlantic Records]] from 2012 until November 2018. Her most well-known projects are her 2013 mixtape ''Good Nights & Bad Mornings 2: The Hangover''<ref>{{Citation|title=Snow Tha Product – Good Nights & Bad Mornings 2: The Hangover {{!}} Download & Listen [New Mixtape]|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/snow-tha-product-good-nights-and-bad-mornings-2-the-hangover-mixtape.98286.html|access-date=February 22, 2017}}</ref> and the 2018 compilation album VIBEHIGHER.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Vibe Higher Mixtape by Vibe Higher Hosted by DJ Pumba, Sikwitit, EC Martinez, Brown Royal, Chris Banks, Young Taylor, CXDY|url=https://www.datpiff.com/Vibe-Higher-Vibe-Higher-mixtape.931533.html|access-date=2021-06-19|website=www.datpiff.com}}</ref> On April 28, 2021, Snow collaborated with Bizarrap on ''Snow Tha Product: Bzrp Music Sessions, Vol. 39'' (2021).<ref>{{Citation|title=Snow Tha Product {{!}}{{!}} BZRP Music Sessions #39|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t490zXLrQDE|language=en|access-date=2021-06-19}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Claudia Alexandra Madriz Meza was born on June 24, 1987 in [[San Jose, California]]<ref name="AbqJArticle">{{cite news |last1=Gomez |first1=Adrian |title='No resting': Rapper Snow Tha Product pushes her socially conscious music |url=https://www.abqjournal.com/1089997/rapper-snow-tha-product-pushes-her-socially-conscious-music.html |work=Albequerque Journal |date=November 10, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product Is Mexican AF (GGN) – YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q-S7WHNpbiM|publisher=GGN via YouTube|access-date=January 24, 2014}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Snow tha Product {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/snow-tha-product-mn0002640574/biography|access-date=December 17, 2020|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> to Mexican parents. Her father was an undocumented immigrant and her mother legally immigrated to the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Snow Tha Product|date=October 8, 2016|title=Rapper Snow Tha Product: "Immigrants Don't Feel Safe to Have a Voice, but Their Children Can Stand Up For Them"|url=https://www.popsugar.com/node/42236842|website=POPSUGAR Latina|language=en-US}}</ref> During her younger years, Meza became more involved with her music. At the age of 6, she appeared in school talent shows and began performing with her grandfather's [[mariachi]] band in [[Redwood City, California|Redwood City]]. She got her first exposure to [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] when she moved to San Diego. As a teenager, she started [[Freestyle rap|freestyling]] with her friends.<ref name="Latina">{{cite web|author=Samantha Leal|date=June 13, 2012|title=Introducing Music Artist Snow Tha Product|url=http://www.latina.com/entertainment/music/introducing-music-artist-rapper-snow-tha-product|access-date=December 22, 2012|publisher=Latina.com}}</ref>

== Musical career ==
=== 2007–2010: Beginnings and early success ===
After graduating high school, she briefly attended [[San Diego Mesa College]] to begin studying to become a social worker. At the age of 19, she began taking music seriously and dropped out of college to pursue a career as a rapper.<ref name="dfw">{{cite web|author=Cary Darling|date=August 8, 2012|title=Snow Tha Product creates a flurry of buzz|url=http://www.dfw.com/2012/08/07/662601/snow-tha-product-female-rapper.html|access-date=December 22, 2012|publisher=DFW.com}}</ref> She adopted her name from [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]]'s character [[Snow White (Disney character)|Snow White]]. Initially she used the name "Snow White", and then added "the Product". Then she changed it into her new moniker as "Snow White Tha Product",<ref name="hiphopdx">{{cite web|author=Edwin Ortiz|date=December 12, 2012|title=Snow Tha Product Discusses "Good Nights & Bad Mornings," Confirms Tech N9ne Collaboration|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22185/title.snow-tha-product-discusses-good-nights-bad-mornings-confirms-tech-n9ne-collaboration|access-date=December 22, 2012|publisher=HipHopDX}}</ref><ref name="dfw" /><ref name="dope">{{cite web|date=November 12, 2012|title=SNOW Tha Product – DOPE MAG Cover Queen|url=http://dopemag.net/2012/11/snow-tha-product-dope-mag-cover-queen/|access-date=December 22, 2012|publisher=DOPE Magazine}}</ref> and ultimately "Snow Tha Product". After recording a number of songs in Spanish, she caught the attention of Mexican artist Jaime Kohen. They recorded a collaboration song, titled "Alguien", which became popular in Mexico and appeared on Kohen's album ''Fotosíntesis''.<ref name="dfw" /><ref name=":2" />

In 2007, Snow released her first compilation album "Verbal Assault Vol.1" with many local artists under her label Product Entertainment. It was followed in 2008 with her first solo mixtape "Raising Tha Bar: Tha Mixtape", which included many remixes of popular songs of the era. In 2009, "Verbal Assault Vol. 2" was released, with the single "Whatever It Takes." In 2010, Snow released "Wake Ya Game Up, Vol. 1", along with the Wake Ya Game Up and Woke clothing brands and apparel.

=== 2010-2011: ''Unorthodox'' and breakthrough ===
In 2010, Snow released the mixtape "Run Up or Shut Up", which was hosted by DJ Ames from the UK and included the hit single "Drunk Love". On February 16, 2011, the official music video was featured in the popular Latino music television channel Mun2.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Weekly|first=Fort Worth|date=2011-02-16|title=Snow Tha Product: Immigrant Song|url=https://www.fwweekly.com/2011/02/16/snow-tha-product-immigrant-song/|access-date=2021-06-19|website=Fort Worth Weekly|language=en-US}}</ref> Snow then moved to [[Texas]] and performed for two consecutive years at the [[South by Southwest]] festival.<ref name="dfw" />

On October 26, 2011, Snow released her debut independent album on Street Science Ent. ''[[Unorthodox (Snow Tha Product album)|Unorthodox]]''.<ref name="itunes_unorthodox">{{cite web|title=Unorthodox|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/unorthodox/id477320740|publisher=iTunes Store|access-date=December 22, 2012}}</ref> "Drunk Love" was rereleased as a single along with "Woke Wednesday".<ref name="drjays_bio">{{cite web|title=Summer Sensations // Snow Tha Product|url=http://www.drjays.com/content/summersensations/2012/snow-tha-product.html|publisher=Dr. Jays|access-date=December 22, 2012}}</ref> Her real breakthrough came with the song "Holy Shit". The 90-second-long song received acclaim and was featured on [[50 Cent]]'s site ThisIs50.com.<ref name="dfw" /> The success of her songs caught the attention of three major record labels, which got in contact with her – [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony]], [[Universal Music Group|Universal]] and [[Atlantic Records|Atlantic]].<ref name="dfw" />  Adam Bernard of RapReviews.com, impressed by her work, went on to write "[Snow] may very well be one of the leaders of ... a bold new era of female emcees."<ref name="rapreviews">{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product Interview|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/interview/snowthaproduct2011.html|publisher=RapReviews.com|access-date=December 22, 2012|author=Adam Bernard|date=September 27, 2011}}</ref>

=== 2012–2014: Signing with Atlantic Records and ''Good Nights & Bad Mornings'' ===
In 2012, Snow signed to [[Atlantic Records]] following her independent success with Unorthodox. She appeared on songs by major artists, such as [[DJ Paul]]<ref name="2db_raw">{{cite web|title=DJ Paul – I'm Dat Raw (rmx) f. Snow Tha Product|url=http://www.2dopeboyz.com/2012/11/06/dj-paul-im-dat-raw-rmx-f-snow-tha-product-video/|publisher=2DopeBoyz|access-date=December 22, 2012}}</ref> and [[Krizz Kaliko]].<ref name="2db_damage">{{cite web|title=Krizz Kaliko – Damage f. Snow Tha Product (Video)|url=http://www.2dopeboyz.com/2012/12/16/krizz-kaliko-damage-f-snow-tha-product-video/|publisher=2DopeBoyz|access-date=December 22, 2012}}</ref> In November, she performed at the [[ABN (rap duo)|ABN]] Reunion concert in Houston, Texas, among rappers [[Trae]] and [[Z-Ro]].<ref name="songkick">{{cite web|title=Abn Reunion Trae Tha Truth|url=http://www.songkick.com/concerts/14416059-abn-reunion-trae-tha-truth-at-house-of-blues|publisher=Song Kick|access-date=December 22, 2012}}</ref> On December 12, 2012, Snow released her anticipated mixtape ''[[Good Nights & Bad Mornings]]'', which spawned 3 singles: "Cookie Cutter Bitches", "Damn It" and "Lord Be with You".<ref name="djbooth">{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product – Good Nights & Bad Mornings|url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/snow-tha-product-good-nights-bad-mornings|publisher=DJ Booth|access-date=December 22, 2012}}</ref>

The video for the song premiered on [[MTV]] on August 8, 2013.<ref name="mp-mtv">{{cite web|title=Video Premiere: Chuckie Featuring Lupe Fiasco, Too $hort + Snow Tha Product, 'Makin' Papers'|url=http://buzzworthy.mtv.com/2013/08/07/chuckie-lupe-fiasco-too-short-snow-tha-product-makin-papers-video/|publisher=MTV|access-date=August 19, 2013}}</ref> In 2013, Snow went on the Fight to Unite Tour with [[Kottonmouth Kings]] and Dizzy Wright<ref>{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product Discusses Her Favorite Albums|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24130/title.snow-tha-product-discusses-her-favorite-albums|publisher=HipHopDX|access-date=July 21, 2013}}</ref> and performed at the 2013 [[South by Southwest]] (SXSW) and [[Rock the Bells]] festivals.<ref>{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product|url=http://schedule.sxsw.com/2013/events/event_MS21235|publisher=SXSW|access-date=August 19, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Rock the Bells Hip Hop Festival {{!}} Los Angeles {{!}} September 7 & 8|url=http://www.rockthebells.net/line-up/los-angeles/|publisher=RockTheBells.net|access-date=August 21, 2013|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6J2K2HsAV?url=http://www.rockthebells.net/line-up/los-angeles/|archive-date=August 21, 2013}}</ref> In February 2014, Snow went on a ''F#*K YOUR PLANS (COME KICK IT)'' tour. On March 19, 2014, Snow was featured on the song "Not for the Weak Minded" by [[Crooked I|Kxng Crooked]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/crooked-i-not-for-the-weak-minded-feat-snow-tha-product-no-tags-new-song.1851564.html|title=KXNG Crooked – Not for the Weak Minded (No Tags) Feat. === 2017–2019: ''VIBEHIGHER'' and going independent  ===
On October 4, 2017, Snow released the unfinished mixtape of ''VIBEHIGHER'' with artists Castro Escobar, Lex the Great, Jandro, and AJ Hernz on YouTube. She followed this with a tour of the same name in 2017 and 2018. 

On November 2018, Snow left Atlantic Records and went independent. She announced that she was releasing the full VIBEHIGHER mixtape on December 22, 2018, since it was finished and ready to be put out under her independent label ''Product Entertainment LLC''. 

On May 1, 2019, Snow went on the 30-day "Going Off" tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product|url=https://www.snowthaproduct.com/|access-date=March 26, 2019|website=Snowthaproduct.com}}</ref> On September 12, she went on tour again on "Going Off: Part 2."

=== 2020–present: ''VALEMADRE'' ===
Snow announced that her album VALEMADRE would be released in mid-2021. This would be her first solo project in five years and her first studio album in a decade. 

== Acting career ==
Snow Tha Product is featured as ''Lil' Traviesa,'' also known as ''Lil' T'', on ''[[Queen of the South (TV series)|Queen of the South]]''.<ref name=":1" />

== Musical style and influences ==
Snow is known for her [[Chopper (rap)|rapid fire style]] of rapping.<ref name="dope"/> She stated she admires and supports artists who have their own original style. She is influenced by artists such as [[Missy Elliott]], [[Da Brat]],<ref name="Latina" /> [[Big Pun]], [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Aaliyah]], [[Amy Winehouse]], [[André 3000]], [[Ludacris]], [[Johnny Cash]], [[Eminem]], [[Busta Rhymes]], [[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]],<ref name="rapreviews"/> [[Mac Dre]], [[Tech N9ne]], and [[Brotha Lynch Hung]],<ref name="hiphopdx"/> as well as Mexican actress [[María Félix]] and singers [[Selena Quintanilla]], [[Gloria Trevi]] and [[Lupita D'Alessio]].<ref name="hhdx2" />

Snow is bilingual, speaking fluent English and Spanish, and records in both languages.<ref name="drjays_bio"/>

== Personal life ==
In a June 2018 interview, Snow revealed that she was married for a decade and has a child by the name of Andrew Feliciano with her former husband.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kIPBgIEQ1lE |title=Snow Tha Product on Myself, Coming Out and Immigration |publisher=YouTube |date=June 19, 2018 |access-date=April 11, 2020}}</ref> During an interview with Power 106 Los Angeles radio show in July 2018 she spoke about her experience raising her son and said her son was eight years old. He was born April 2010.<ref name="Snow Tha Product On Raising An 8-Year-Old & Representing The Mexican Community">{{cite web|title=Snow Tha Product On Raising An 8-Year-Old & Representing The Mexican Community|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uI6fJQLUsnI|publisher=Youtuber}}</ref> 

Since 2017, Snow has been dating Julissa, also known as JuJu. On July 10, 2019, Julissa and Snow announced that they are engaged.<ref>{{cite web |title=SHE PROPOSED... (STORY TIME) |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3p6Z4oBRbPY |publisher=YouTube}}</ref> They have a [[YouTube]] channel called "everydaydays" and a podcast called "everynightnights".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC62DsCYR_xWzxeH8ou1WKlg |title=everydaydays |publisher=YouTube |date=March 7, 2020 |access-date=April 11, 2020}}</ref> The YouTube channel was created on January 23, 2017, but was not officially used until November 28, 2018, for its first video.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/OXiSphrxTBs]{{dead link|date=April 2020}}</ref>
