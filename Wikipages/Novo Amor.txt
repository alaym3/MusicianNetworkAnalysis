}}'''Ali John Meredith-Lacey''' (born 11 August 1991), better known under the moniker '''Novo Amor''', is a Welsh multi-instrumentalist, singer, songwriter, sound designer and producer. Lacey rose to prominence after the release of his debut EP ''Woodgate, NY'' in March 2014.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.undertheradarmag.com/news/premiere_novo_amour_woodgate_ny_ep_stream/|title=Premiere: Novo Amor – "Woodgate, NY" EP Stream|website=undertheradarmag.com|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref>

== Career ==
Lacey was born on 11 August 1991 and raised in [[Llanidloes]], Wales<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/wales/entries/bb5deafc-2ca6-3343-a5e3-9e17336a842a?sortBy=HighestRating&sortOrder=Descending&filter=none|title=Less waffle, more splendid noise|date=7 January 2013|website=Wales|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref> and now resides in [[Cardiff]]. ''Drift,'' a two-track single self-released in 2012, was Lacey's first release as Novo Amor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.folkradio.co.uk/2012/10/introducing-novo-amor-plus-free-ep/|title=Introducing Novo Amor (plus free EP) {{!}} Folk Radio UK|date=9 October 2012|website=Folk Radio UK|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref>

Lacey's first Novo Amor EP, ''Woodgate, NY'', was released 31 March 2014 on Norwegian label Brilliance Records.<ref name=":0" /> Building on the success of the EP, Lacey released the single "Faux", a collaboration with [[Ed Tullett]], on Dumont Dumont and Brilliance Records on 23 June 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://earmilk.com/2014/06/19/novo-amor-ed-tullett-resonate-withfaux-premiere/|title=Novo Amor & Ed Tullett resonate with"Faux" [Premiere]|last=Thomas|first=Aaron|date=19 June 2014|website=EARMILK|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref> In the same month, Lacey also signed to UK publisher BDi Music, part of [[Bucks Music Group]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bdimusic.com/roster/27-novo-amor|title=BDi Music Ltd|website=bdimusic.com|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref>

"Callow", a further single as Novo Amor, was released 17 November 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thefourohfive.com/tv/novo-amor-callow-405-premiere-141|title=Novo Amor shares video for 'Callow' [405 Premiere]|last=http://thefourohfive.com|website=The 405|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref> This was followed by "[[Welcome to the Jungle]]", a [[Guns N' Roses]] cover for an [[Axe (brand)|AXE]]/[[Lynx (disambiguation)|Lynx]] advertising campaign on 6 January 2015,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.com/media/news/a619000/is-this-lynx-cover-the-least-guns-n-roses-version-of-a-gnr-song/|title=Is this the least GN'R cover ever?|date=6 January 2015|website=Digital Spy|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref> its video directed by [[Nabil Elderkin|Nabil]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://skopemag.com/2015/01/07/novo-amor-announces-new-single-welcome-to-the-jungle|title=NOVO AMOR ANNOUNCES NEW SINGLE 'WELCOME TO THE JUNGLE' – Skope Entertainment Inc|last=Skope|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref> Lacey toured Germany, Switzerland and Austria across April and May 2015,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://novoamor.co.uk|title=Novo Amor|website=novoamor.co.uk|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref> and was nominated for 2 awards at the [[Music Week]] Sync Awards for the use of his music commercially.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bdimusic.com/news/517-novo-amor-gets-2-nominations-for-the-music-week-sync-awards-2015|title=BDi Music Ltd|website=bdimusic.com|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref> "Anchor", another single as Novo Amor, was released 23 October 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2015/10/novo-amor-reinterprets-the-selkie-legend-with-music-video-for-anchor-watch/|title=Novo Amor reinterprets the Selkie legend with music video for "Anchor" – watch|date=2 October 2015|website=Consequence of Sound|access-date=30 April 2016}}</ref>

A second collaborative single with [[Ed Tullett]], "Alps", was released on 15 April 2016.{{fact|date=January 2020}}

On 26 May 2017, ''Bathing Beach,'' the second of Novo Amor's EPs, was released via AllPoints, the in-house label of Believe Recordings.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-04-25|title=Review: The Heartache & Beauty of Novo Amor's Intimate "Carry You"|url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/carry-you-novo-amor-review/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=Atwood Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allpointsmusic.com/about/|title=About|website=ALL POINTS|access-date=22 September 2017}}</ref>

On 10 November 2017, Lacey and Ed Tullett released ''Heiress,'' their full collaborative album on All Points.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/novo-amor-ed-tullett-cavalry-heiress|title=Novo Amor & Ed Tullett air "Cavalry", the first single from collaborative record Heiress|work=The Line of Best Fit|access-date=17 August 2017}}</ref>

On 14 June 2018, Lacey announced his debut solo record ''Birthplace'', to be released on 19 October 2018 via All Points.<ref>{{cite web |title=Q&A: Novo Amor on his musical 'Birthplace,' an ocean away from home |url=https://riffmagazine.com/qa/novo-amor-birthplace/ |website=RIFF Magazine |date=26 November 2018}}</ref>Promoting the releasing of the album, Lacey collaborated with Sil van der Woerd and Jorik Dozy (Studio Birthplace), the producer by Sean Lin and New Fronteir Pictures, cinematograph Nihal Friedel to released the music video for the single as the same name 'Birthplace'. The music videos tells the human abuse of the oceans, around the view of free diver Michael Board. For the purpose of the video, according to Independent, "the 13-meter long model of the whale, which was made from plastic collected by school children, who received books in return for their donations."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-06-14|title=Watch this stunning music video from Novo Amor that sends a powerful message about our effect on the natural world|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/novo-amor-birthplace-video-watch-ocean-plastics-pollution-album-release-date-a8398191.html|access-date=2021-01-29|website=The Independent|language=en}}</ref> The music video was nominated on august 8 to the Association Of Independent Music (AIM) Awards<ref>{{Cite web|title=Novo Amor nominated for best Independent Video at AIM Awards 2018|url=https://smv.de/640-novo-amor-s-birthplace-video-nominated-for-best-independent-video-aim-awards-2018|access-date=2021-01-29|website=smv.de}}</ref> winning the category 'Independent Video of the Year'.<ref>{{Cite web|title=AIM Awards 2018 winners crowned|url=https://www.prsformusic.com/m-magazine/news/aim-awards-2018-winners-crowned/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=www.prsformusic.com|language=en}}</ref>

Lacey toured with [[Gia Margaret]] in 2019. They recorded and released two songs together, "Lucky For You" and "No Fun" on 28 February 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=New Release: Novo Amor And Gia Margaret - No Fun / Lucky For You |url=https://thankfolkforthat.com/new-release-novo-amor-and-gia-margaret-no-fun-lucky-for-you/ |website=Thank Folk For That |accessdate=7 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Kenneally |first1=Cerys |title=Gia Margaret and Novo Amor Team up for Collaborative Tracks 'No Fun' and 'Lucky For You" |url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/gia-margaret-novo-amor-collaborate-on-two-tracks-no-fun-and-lucky-for-you |website=The Line of Best Fit |accessdate=7 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Sadler |first1=Adam |title=Novo Amor & Gia Margaret: No Fun / Lucky For You |url=http://streamingdept.com/song-reviews/novo-amor-gia-margaret-no-fun-lucky-for-you/ |website=Streaming Dept. |publisher=Streaming Dept. |access-date=7 March 2020}}</ref> 

On August 2019, Lacey started working on his second full-length album at a home-made studio at Wales, UK.<ref name=":1">{{cite web |title=Novo Amor Talks About The Indecision of His Latest Album, ‘Cannot Be, Whatsover’ « American Songwriter |url=https://americansongwriter.com/novo-amor-talks-about-the-indecision-of-his-latest-album-cannot-be-whatsover/ |website=American Songwriter |date=22 January 2021}}</ref> The album recording ended around February 2020. On 30 July 2020, he announced the release of his second solo album, ''Cannot Be, Whatsoever'', through AllPoints on 6 November 2020. The album features a embroidered cover by Dutch artist Tilleke Schwarz, titled 'Birdcage', later becoming one of the songs titles in the album. On 2 December, Lacey released the album documentary titled 'Please Don’t Stand Up When Room Is In Motion' directed by Josh Bennett.<ref name=":1" /> 

=== Etymology ===
Novo Amor is Portuguese for New Love. The artist confirmed in a tweet that the name had a "Portuguese/Latin" ''[sic]'' origin, without providing any further explanation.<ref name="twitter_reply">{{cite web  |title=Twitter Reply in 2014 |url=https://twitter.com/iamnovoamor/status/541000942297288704 |website=twitter.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210405154248/https://twitter.com/iamnovoamor/status/541000942297288704 |archive-date=5 April 2021 |date=5 December 2014 |quote=yes it is Portuguese/Latin. The reason is a secret ;) |url-status=live}}</ref> He stated in an interview that he went through a break-up in 2012 before he started producing and that led to this name as he found his "New Love" in music.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Curry |first1=Thomas |title=Novo Amor |url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/introducing/novo-amor-interview |website=thelineofbestfit.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210405155501/https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/introducing/novo-amor-interview |archive-date=5 April 2021 |date=6 May 2014 |url-status=live}}</ref>
