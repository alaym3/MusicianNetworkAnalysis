'''Gaetano "Nino" D'Angelo''' (born 21 June 1957) is an Italian singer, songwriter, actor, television personality, film director, screenwriter and author.

He was born to a poor family in [[San Pietro a Patierno]], a suburb of [[Naples]], and dropped out of school, taking jobs like ice cream vendor and wedding singer.<ref>Allmusic profile</ref>

His first album, ''A storia mia'' ([[Neapolitan language|Neapolitan]]: "My Story") was received very well, especially in [[Sicily]]. In 1979 he married Annamaria with whom he had two sons. He started a career as an actor in ''[[sceneggiata|sceneggiate]]'' musical dramas, a genre native to Naples, and later as a cinema actor. His first film was ''Celebrità'', released in 1981, a star vehicle written around his singing and the first of five films he acted in that was directed or produced by Ninì Grassia.

In 1982 he released the album ''Un jeans e una maglietta'' ("Jeans and a T-shirt") and a film with the same title.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.repubblica.it/spettacoli-e-cultura/2012/02/01/news/nino_d_angelo-teatro_jeans-29147557/|title=La doppia vita di Nino D'Angelo "Ma ora basta con il caschetto"|date=1 February 2012|language=it|work=la Repubblica|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> The album sold over one million copies and the film surpassed the Hollywood movie ''[[Flashdance]]'' at the box office.{{where|date=December 2014}} He participated in the [[Sanremo Music Festival]] in 1986 with the song "Vai" ("Go"). His album ''Cantautore'' was the best seller at the time, although it was not listed in the official ''[[Hit Parade]]''.{{citation needed|date=December 2014}} He performed in Australia, Germany, Switzerland, United States, and France—where he debuted 15 December 1987 at the Olympia in Paris.

In 1990, following the deaths of both his parents, he released the album "Tiempo" (Neapolitan for "Time"), which was championed by Italian music critic Goffredo Fofi.

In 1997 he wrote his first musical, ''Core pazzo'' ("Mad Heart"), and co-hosted the Dopofestival show that aired immediately after each night of the [[Sanremo Music Festival]] with [[Piero Chiambretti]]. He also won the David Di Donatello Award for Best Music for his soundtrack to the film ''[[To Die for Tano]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://artdaily.com/news/22519/Italian-Artist-Roberta-Torre-at-Festival-for-Expanded-Media|title=Italian Artist Roberta Torre at Festival for Expanded Media|publisher=artdaily.com|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> In 2000, he also starred, directed, wrote and performed the music for  ''Aitanic'', a ''[[Titanic (1997 film)|Titanic]]'' parody and starring fellow Italian actors [[Sabina Began]] and [[Giacomo Rizzo]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://variety.com/2000/film/reviews/aitanic-1200465668/|title=Aitanic|date=5 November 2000|work=Variety|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref>

The following year he competed in the Sanremo Festival, singing "Senza giacca e cravatta" ("Without Suit and Tie") and reaching 8th place. He also took part in Sanremo in 2002 and 2003 with the songs "Mari'" and "'A Storia 'E Nisciuno" (Neapolitan for "Nobody's Story"). In 2003 he sang in [[Brașov]], [[Romania]], and appeared in the film [[Incantato]] directed by [[Pupi Avati]], which he won a [[Flaiano Prize]] for Best Supporting Actor.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.premiflaiano.it/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=72&Itemid=89|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110722045214/http://www.premiflaiano.it/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=72&Itemid=89|url-status=dead|archive-date=22 July 2011|title=Flaiano Film Festival|publisher=premiflaiano.it|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> He was popular with teenagers in the 1970s<ref>{{cite book |last=Vitti |first=Antonio |date=2009 |title=The films of Gianni Amelio |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=S-gqAQAAIAAJ&q=Nino+D%27Angelo+singer |publisher=Metauro |page=235 |isbn=9788861560215 |access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> and into the 1980s,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/02/12/movies/12todie.html|title=The Mobsters of Palermo|date=11 February 2010|newspaper=The New York Times|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> charting ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' several times.<ref>{{cite magazine |date=22 June 1985 |title=Italy Albums |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KiQEAAAAMBAJ&q=Nino+D%27Angelo+singer&pg=RA1-PA24 |magazine=Billboard |volume=97 |issue=25 |pages=24 |access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine |date=21 June 1986 |title=Italy Albums |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gyQEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA61 |magazine=Billboard |volume=99 |issue=25 |pages=61 |access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref> In 2008, he also starred in a film directed and written by his son, Toni.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://variety.com/2008/film/reviews/a-night-1200472624/|title=A Night|date=29 December 2008|work=Variety|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref>

In 2013, he was one of the protesters after the mafia dumped toxic waste across the country including Naples.<ref>{{cite news|first=Tom|last=Porter|url=http://www.ibtimes.co.uk/naples-mafia-protest-camorra-dumping-toxic-waste-522823|title=Thousands Protest in Naples at Mafia Dumping of Toxic Waste|date=17 November 2013|work=International Business Times|access-date=3 July 2015}}</ref>

Nino D'Angelo is Roman Catholic.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Giordano|first=Lucio|title=Volevo farla finita con la vita ma Dio mi ha salvato e mi ha dato il successo|language=it|work=Dipiù|date=15 October 2021|number=41|pp=86-89}}</ref>
