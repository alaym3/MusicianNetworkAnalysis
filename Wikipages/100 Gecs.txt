'''100 gecs''' is an American [[hyperpop]] [[musical duo|duo]] formed in 2015 that consists of [[Dylan Brady]] and Laura Les.<ref name="pitchfork-1000-gecs-review">{{cite web |last1=Fitzmaurice |first1=Larry |title=100 gecs: 1000 gecs |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/100-gecs-1000-gecs/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=August 12, 2019 |date=July 27, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190730035926/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/100-gecs-1000-gecs/ |archive-date=July 30, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="paper-2019">{{cite web |last1=Moen |first1=Matt |title=You've Never Heard Anything Like 100 Gecs |url=https://www.papermag.com/100-gecs-money-machine-2638795117.html |work=Paper Mag |access-date=August 12, 2019 |date=June 13, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190812055432/https://www.papermag.com/100-gecs-money-machine-2638795117.html |archive-date=August 12, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="fader-july-19">{{cite web |last1=Wang |first1=Steffanee |title=Watch 100 gecs' unnervingly voyeuristic "800db cloud" video |url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/07/25/100-gecs-800db-cloud-video |work=[[The Fader]] |access-date=August 12, 2019 |date=July 25, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190801015754/https://www.thefader.com/2019/07/25/100-gecs-800db-cloud-video |archive-date=August 1, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> They self-released their debut [[studio album|album]], ''[[1000 Gecs]]'', in 2019 to critical acclaim, followed by a "companion"<ref name="nme-mylrea"/> remix album, ''[[1000 Gecs and the Tree of Clues]]'', in July 2020. Their music has been noted for its often chaotic yet catchy mixture of various styles,<ref name="pitchfork-1000-gecs-review"/><ref name="compelx-july-19">{{cite web|last1=Moore|first1=Jacob|last2=Gardner|first2=Alex|last3=Rindner|first3=Grant|last4=Price|first4=Joe|date=July 26, 2019|title=Best New Artists of the Month (July)|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2019/07/best-new-artists-july-2019/100-gecs|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190812055433/https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2019/07/best-new-artists-july-2019/100-gecs|archive-date=August 12, 2019|access-date=August 12, 2019|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]}}</ref> and has been described as exemplifying the 2010s genre [[hyperpop]].<ref name="independent"/>

==History==
===2015–2018: Formation and self-titled EP===
Brady and Les, who lived just miles apart in the St. Louis area ([[Kirkwood, Missouri|Kirkwood]] and [[Webster Groves]], respectively), first met during high school at a [[rodeo]].<ref name="outline-enis-2019">{{cite web |last1=Enis |first1=Eli |title=The batshit album that explains how 2019 feels |url=https://theoutline.com/post/7884/100-gecs-interview-post-internet |website=The Outline |access-date=September 4, 2019 |date=August 28, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200330113730/https://theoutline.com/post/7884/100-gecs-interview-post-internet |archive-date=March 30, 2020 |url-status=live }}</ref> <ref name="diy-watson">{{cite web |last1=Watson |first1=Elly |title=Who the fuck are 100 gecs? |url=https://diymag.com/2020/06/22/who-the-fuck-are-100-gecs-june-2020-interview |website=DIY Mag |access-date=27 August 2020 |date=June 22, 2020 |archive-date=August 12, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200812031346/https://diymag.com/2020/06/22/who-the-fuck-are-100-gecs-june-2020-interview |url-status=live }}</ref> However, they first had the idea to collaborate after meeting again at a house party in 2012.<ref name="mtv-roth">{{cite web |last1=Roth |first1=Madeline |title=How 100 Gecs Are Making Music 'Super Fun' Again, One Gec At A Time |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3151895/100-gecs-interview/ |website=MTV News |access-date=27 August 2020 |date=January 9, 2020 |archive-date=October 19, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201019221258/http://www.mtv.com/news/3151895/100-gecs-interview/ |url-status=live }}</ref> In the winter of 2015, Les and Brady first produced music together, recording in Chicago<ref name="fader-angel-2019">{{cite web|last1=Angell|first1=Jack|date=September 4, 2019|title=The brilliant pop anarchy of 100 gecs|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/09/04/100-gecs-1000-gecs-interview|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190914164724/https://www.thefader.com/2019/09/04/100-gecs-1000-gecs-interview|archive-date=September 14, 2019|access-date=September 15, 2019|publisher=The Fader}}</ref><ref name="diy-watson"/> and eventually self-releasing their first EP, ''100 Gecs'', on July 12, 2016. The origin of the name "100 Gecs" is disputed, as Brady and Les have given varying and contradictory explanations in interviews.<ref name="pigeons-planes-yt"/>

===2019–2020: ''1000 Gecs'' and ''1000 Gecs and the Tree of Clues''===
Despite plans to record more music, they were unable to find enough time until they performed a "breakout"<ref name="gq-pappademas">{{cite web |last1=Pappademas |first1=Alex |title=Into the Void with 100 Gecs |url=https://www.gq.com/story/gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs |website=GQ |access-date=27 August 2020 |date=July 6, 2020 |archive-date=August 20, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200820110542/https://www.gq.com/story/gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs-gecs |url-status=live }}</ref> DJ set together for the 2019 ''[[Minecraft]]'' Fire Festival.<ref name="outline-enis-2019" /> Following that collaboration, they continued to work on songs and released their debut album, ''[[1000 Gecs]]'', on May 31, 2019, to positive reviews.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Well|first=Johnny|date=July 8, 2019|title=Review: 100 Gecs - 1000 gecs|url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/79796/100-Gecs-1000-gecs/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191213231648/https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/79796/100-Gecs-1000-gecs/|archive-date=December 13, 2019|access-date=November 21, 2019|website=[[Sputnikmusic]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/100-gecs-1000-gecs/|title=100 gecs: 1000 gecs|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=November 21, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190730035926/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/100-gecs-1000-gecs/|archive-date=July 30, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="fader-secret-tour" /> According to Will Pritchard of ''[[The Independent]]'', the album helped to consolidate the eclectic 2010s "[[hyperpop]]" style by taking the genre "to its most extreme, and extremely catchy, conclusions: stadium-sized [[trap music|trap]] beats processed and distorted to near-destruction, overwrought [[emo]] vocals and cascades of [[rave music|rave]]y arpeggios."<ref name="independent">{{cite web |last1=Pritchard |first1=Will |title=Hyperpop or overhyped? ==Fandom==
Following the group's success, the pine tree depicted on the album covers of ''1000 Gecs'' and remix album ''Tree of Clues'' became popular among fans.<ref name="face-siegel">{{cite web |last1=Siegel |first1=Evan |title=Pilgrimage to Gecca |url=https://theface.com/culture/100-gecs-tree-fans-pilgrimage |website=The Face |access-date=27 August 2020 |date=August 5, 2020 |archive-date=October 3, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201003192805/https://theface.com/culture/100-gecs-tree-fans-pilgrimage |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="diy-watson"/> Soon, the "1000 Gecs tree" was found, revealed to be located in an [[Acuity Brands]]-owned office park in [[Des Plaines, Illinois]], near [[O'Hare International Airport]]. Fans of 100 Gecs began making "pilgrimages" to the tree and leaving items behind. In the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'', the tree and its fame were compared to the house on the cover of [[American Football (band)|American Football]]'s ''[[American Football (1999 album)|American Football]]'' (1999), located in [[Urbana, Illinois]].<ref name="chi-tribune-tree-sturm">{{cite web |last1=Sturm |first1=Verity |title=This little tree in Des Plaines is the center of a nationwide hyperpop pilgrimage for gecs love |url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-100-gecs-tree-des-plaines-20200806-cjurifmtbjektie4jlu7yn3gom-story.html |website=The Chicago Tribune |access-date=27 August 2020 |date=August 6, 2020 |archive-date=August 29, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200829121742/https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-100-gecs-tree-des-plaines-20200806-cjurifmtbjektie4jlu7yn3gom-story.html |url-status=live }}</ref> The music video for their single "Money Machine" was filmed in the same office park just before the photo was taken.<ref name="pigeons-planes-yt">{{cite web |title=100 gecs on Soulja Boy, How They Got Their Name and the Future of Virtual Concerts |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vI1DFv000OU |website=YouTube |publisher=Pigeons & Planes |access-date=1 September 2020 |date=June 17, 2020 |quote=...a little bit outside of the city [Chicago] where we shot the 'Money Machine' video, and we were like, 'Look at that tree!' |archive-date=August 7, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200807113833/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vI1DFv000OU |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Production style and influences==
The duo works on tracks by sending [[Logic Pro]] project files between the two,<ref name="stute-2019">{{cite web |last1=Kostelnik |first1=Jack |title=100 Gecs and bubblegum bass in the American consciousness |url=https://thestute.com/2019/09/13/100-gecs-and-bubblegum-bass-in-the-american-consciousness/ |website=The Stute |access-date=September 15, 2019 |date=September 13, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190915213400/https://thestute.com/2019/09/13/100-gecs-and-bubblegum-bass-in-the-american-consciousness/ |archive-date=September 15, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="outline-enis-2019"/> iterating songs each time<ref name="ha-fagen-2019">{{cite web |last1=Fagen |first1=Lucas |title=The Pranksters of Pop |url=https://hyperallergic.com/518162/the-pranksters-of-pop/ |website=Hyperallergic |access-date=September 22, 2019 |date=September 21, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190922024431/https://hyperallergic.com/518162/the-pranksters-of-pop/ |archive-date=September 22, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="reddit-ama-2019">{{cite web|date=June 27, 2019|title=100 gecs AMA <33|url=https://www.reddit.com/r/indieheads/comments/c6848z/100_gecs_ama_33/|access-date=August 12, 2019|website=reddit}}</ref>—Les called the process "sort of an [[exquisite corpse]] type thing".<ref name="guardian-ewens"/> Their music has been called an "anarchic assault on the ears"<ref name="compelx-july-19"/> that "[pulls] conventional pop [[Trope (music)|tropes]] in every direction possible",<ref name="eb-stems-2019"/> as well as "abrasive, maximalist pop"<ref name="pitchfork-1000-gecs-review"/> with "elements of [[pop punk]], [[nightcore]], [[ska]], [[dubstep]], [[deconstructed club]], [[Trance music|trance]], [[Metal (genre)|metal]], and [[happy hardcore]] all thrown into one big internet blender",<ref name="paper-2019"/> resulting in "[s]ongs [that] shift gears dozens of times, in a way that recalls... [[Kid606]], or [[Venetian Snares]]",<ref name="nyt-100-gecs-2019"/> also earning comparisons with label [[PC Music]]<ref name="eb-stems-2019">{{cite web |title=Flex Your Producer Skills With This Pack Of Stems From LA Avant-Pop Group 100 gecs |url=https://www.electronicbeats.net/the-feed/100-gecs-stems-free/ |website=Electronic Beats |access-date=September 4, 2019 |date=August 30, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190904021141/https://www.electronicbeats.net/the-feed/100-gecs-stems-free/ |archive-date=September 4, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="stute-2019"/> and band [[Sleigh Bells (band)|Sleigh Bells]]. Les explained that the "merging of genres" is "more natural than people think", adding that the group "didn't expect that [''1000 Gecs''] would resonate with people so much".<ref name="musicfeeds-welby">{{cite web |last1=Welby |first1=Augustus |title=100 gecs: "'Vanguards Of Pop' Or People Saying That We're Experimental… Those Aren't Things That We've Described Ourselves As" |url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/features/100-gecs/ |website=Music Feeds |access-date=27 August 2020 |date=August 3, 2020 |archive-date=September 21, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200921154002/https://musicfeeds.com.au/features/100-gecs/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

Brady has said 100 Gecs' style is influenced by [[Breathe Carolina]], [[John Zorn]],<ref name="fader-angel-2019"/> and [[I See Stars]], among others.<ref name="outline-enis-2019"/> Les calls their musical process "[v]ery much almost an [[Musical improvisation|improv]] mentality"<ref name="outline-enis-2019"/> and has said that they "try to have fun and write songs that we would want to listen to",<ref name="paper-2019"/> adding that "the whole idea of labeling genres is not super important to us".<ref name="fader-angel-2019"/> Les became interested in making music as a teenager when she got her first guitar; she has said that she "kind of always just wanted to be a songwriter" and "love[s] anything with a catchy melody".<ref name="uu-allanoff-2018"/><ref name="noisey-les-joyce">{{cite web |last1=Joyce |first1=Colin |title=Weather Our Dystopia With the Chaos Pop of Laura Les's Noisey Mix |url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/ne5qvk/laura-les-osno1-interview-noisey-mix |website=Noisey |access-date=September 22, 2019 |date=August 6, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190922033252/https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/ne5qvk/laura-les-osno1-interview-noisey-mix |archive-date=September 22, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> She has cited [[Naked City (band)|Naked City]], [[Playboi Carti]], [[3OH!3]], [[Cannibal Corpse]],<ref name="fader-angel-2019"/> and various PC&nbsp;Music artists as influences.<ref name="reddit-ama-2019"/> Both of them were heavily inspired by [[Skrillex]]'s song "[[Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites (song)|Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites]]".<ref name="purple-sneakers-jones">{{cite web |last1=Jones |first1=Emma |title=100 gecs on Skrillex, throwing out the rule book and loving that boomers hate their music |url=https://purplesneakers.com.au/2020/08/interview-100-gecs/ |website=Purple Sneakers |access-date=27 August 2020 |date=August 2020 |archive-date=September 25, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200925170056/https://www.purplesneakers.com.au/2020/08/interview-100-gecs/ |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="paper-skrillex-moen">{{cite web |last1=Moen |first1=Matt |title=Skrillex Interviews 100 gecs About the Future of Music |url=https://www.papermag.com/100-gecs-skrillex-pride-2646244919.html |website=Paper |access-date=27 August 2020 |date=June 24, 2020 |archive-date=August 6, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200806013031/https://www.papermag.com/100-gecs-skrillex-pride-2646244919.html |url-status=live }}</ref>
