'''James Vincent McMorrow''' (born 17 August 1981) is an Irish singer and songwriter.

== Career ==
McMorrow's debut album, [[Early in the Morning (James Vincent McMorrow album)|''Early in the Morning'']], was released in Ireland in February 2010, and in the United States and Europe in 2011, to widespread acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indierockreviews.com/2011/02/james-vincent-mcmorrow-early-in-the-morning-album-review/|title=James Vincent McMorrow 'Early In The Morning' Album Review|date=2011-01-25|publisher=Indie Rock Reviews|access-date=2011-07-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715234029/http://www.indierockreviews.com/2011/02/james-vincent-mcmorrow-early-in-the-morning-album-review/|archive-date=2011-07-15|url-status=dead}}</ref> He made his UK television debut on [[BBC Two]]'s ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]'' on 27 May 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b011lg09|title=Two Programmes - Later... with Jools Holland, Series 38, Episode 8|publisher=BBC|access-date=2011-07-26}}</ref> He played with [[Lisa Hannigan]] on 8 December 2011 at Dublin's Button Factory for the [[Choice Music Prize]] event.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.meteor.ie/about/news/news_2011/choicemusicaward/|title=Meteor Choice Music Prize|publisher=Meteor|access-date=2011-11-12}}</ref>

In 2011, he co-wrote the song "Shells of Silver" with [[Northern Ireland|Northern Irish]] electronic group [[The Japanese Popstars]], and his vocals are featured.<ref>{{Cite web|title=James Vincent McMorrow|url=https://www.facebook.com/jamesvincentmcmorrow/posts/183125061754206|access-date=2020-06-10|website=www.facebook.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Kari|date=2012-02-13|title=The Japanese Popstars feat. James Vincent McMorrow – "Shells of Silver"|url=https://theswillmerchants.com/2012/02/13/the-japanese-popstars-feat-james-vincent-mcmorrow-shells-of-silver/|access-date=2020-06-10|website=THE SWILL MERCHANTS|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2011-06-13|title=The Japanese Popstars - Shells Of Silver (Feat. James Vincent Mcmorrow)|url=https://blahblahblahscience.com/track-reviews/the-japanese-popstars-shells-of-silver-feat-james-vincent-mcmorrow/|access-date=2020-06-10|website=blahblahblahscience|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Shells Of Silver|url=https://www.pandora.com/artist/the-japanese-popstars/controlling-your-allegiance/shells-of-silver/TRKlbgK4cj35bb4|language=en|access-date=2020-06-10}}</ref> The song is featured in [[Gossip Girl (season 5)|Season 5]], Episode 7 of [[the CW]] teen drama ''[[Gossip Girl]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Music from Gossip Girl S5E07|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/gossip-girl/season-5/9972|access-date=2020-06-10|website=Tunefind|language=en-us}}</ref>

In 2014, he released his second album, ''[[Post Tropical]]'', which included the song "Glacier". The song was used in the 2014 [[Spanish Christmas Lottery]] advertising campaign.<ref>[https://archive.today/20141125211604/https://www.newstalk.ie/Irish-singer-James-Vincent-McMorrow-chosen-for-tearjerking-Christmas-ad] </ref> The album was recorded in 2013 in the small desert town of Tornillo, {{convert|55|mi|km|order=flip}} south of [[El Paso]] in Texas.<ref>''[[Early in the Morning (James Vincent McMorrow album)|Early in the Morning]]'' was recorded in [[Drogheda]], Ireland.</ref> The studio is located on a [[pecan]] farm, and McMorrow found the location "the most surreal place to work", and one that helped add to the tone of the album and inspire his creativity. He said "I think it's kind of engrained in the record, more than any pinpointed thing. I can hear the hot bedded air in this part. You can hear the birds in the pianos."<ref name="mir">Ward, James. "[http://www.irishmirror.ie/whats-on/music/james-vincent-mcmorrows-eagerly-awaited-3007796 Likely forecast looks as if Post Tropical will go down a storm]". ''[[Daily Mirror|Irish Daily Mirror]], January 10, 2014. Retrieved January 11, 2014.</ref>

In 2015, he composed a film score for the short, "Martha", directed by Sam Benenati. In 2016, his cover of the [[Chris Isaak]] song "[[Wicked Game]]" was used in the first official full-length trailer for the [[Game of Thrones (season 6)|sixth season]] of the [[HBO]] hit series ''[[Game of Thrones]]''. 
In April 2016, he was featured on the promotional single "I'm in Love" from [[Kygo]]'s debut album, ''[[Cloud Nine (Kygo album)|Cloud Nine]].''

In July 2016, McMorrow announced his third studio album and released "Rising Water", its lead single. ''[[We Move]]'' was produced by McMorrow, [[Nineteen85]], [[Frank Dukes]] and [[Two Inch Punch]], and was released on 2 September 2016. It marks a shift in McMorrow's style towards more [[Alternative R&B|R&B]]-tinged sounds. He released his fourth studio album ''[[True Care]]'' on 26 May 2017.

== Awards and nominations ==
In January 2012, McMorrow received a [[European Border Breakers Award]] in recognition of ''Early In The Morning''{{'s}} success beyond Ireland.<ref>[http://www.imro.ie/imro_article/james-vincent-mcmorrow-wins-ebba-2012 "James Vincent McMorrow wins ebba 2012"], imro.ie. October 7, 2011</ref>
