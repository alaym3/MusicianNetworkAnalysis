'''Sajjad Ali''' (born 1966) is a [[Pakistani]] [[Semi-classical music|semi-classical]], [[Pop music|pop]] and [[Rock music|rock]] [[singer]], [[poet]], [[actor]], [[film director]] as well as a film producer from [[Karachi, Pakistan]].<ref name=Dawn>[https://images.dawn.com/news/1177236 Sajjad Ali, Ali Azmat draw big crowds at Lahore Music Meet] Dawn (newspaper), Updated 12 March 2017, Retrieved 20 March 2018</ref><ref name=Tribune>{{cite web|url=https://tribune.com.pk/story/1147140/junoon-awaz-got-popular-original-songs-not-covers-sajjad-ali/|title=Junoon, Awaz and I got popular because of original songs not covers: Sajjad Ali|author=Rafay Mahmood|date=22 July 2016|publisher=The Express Tribune (newspaper)|access-date=20 March 2018}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
His father, Shafqat Hussain (Sajan), was a [[Pakistani]] [[film actor]] and also a [[cricketer]].<ref name=BR/>  his father appeared for the first time in a film, ''Badal Aur Bijli'' in 1971. Sajjad completed his F.A degree from National Arts College, Karachi. After that, his uncle, Tassaduq Hussain started teaching music to him with his classical collections.  During Sajjad's training, Hussain played music from classical artists such as [[Ustad Bade Ghulam Ali Khan]], Ustad [[Barkat Ali Khan]] and Ustad Mubarak Ali Khan who all were Sajjad Ali's grand-uncles also. He was also briefly trained by his uncle Ustad [[Munawar Ali Khan]] from [[India]] who himself was the son of Ustad Bade Ghulam Ali Khan. Sajjad Ali belongs to the [[Kasur Patiala Gharana]] of classical musicians.<ref>[http://www.tribuneindia.com/news/life-style/knowing-the-ustad/558337.html Knowing the Ustad] The Tribune (newspaper), Published 16 March 2018, Retrieved 20 March 2018</ref>

== Career ==
===Music===
Sajjad Ali's first classical hit album, ''[[Master Sajjad Sings Memorable Classics]]'' was released in 1979 by [[EMI]]-[[Pakistan]].<ref name=EMI>[http://www.emipakistan.com/artist/Sajjad-Ali-107 Sajjad Ali on EMI Pakistan website] Retrieved 20 March 2018</ref> The musicians were [[Bade Ghulam Ali Khan]], [[Mehdi Hassan]], [[Ghulam Ali (Ghazal singer)|Ghulam Ali]] and [[Amanat Ali Khan]], while most of the songs were written by [[Hasrat Mohani]], [[Momin Khan Momin|Momin]] and others.

Sajjad made his television debut in many programs before his debut program, ''Aap Janab'' by Athar Shah Khan. After this, he appeared in ''Rung Barangi Duniya'' by musician [[Sohail Rana]]. He also performed in the program ''Rag Rung'' in 1980 at the age of 14. He became famous at the 25th anniversary of [[Pakistan Television|PTV]]'s stage show, called Silver Jubilee by [[Shoaib Mansoor]], by singing the song "Banwari Chakori", originally sung by [[Noor Jehan]]. On 26 November 1983 during a show, Sajjad sung the songs "Lagi Ray Lagay Lagan Yehe Dil Mein" and "Bawari Chakuri".

He produced the music albums ''[[Babia 93]]'', ''[[Chief Saab]]'' and  ''[[Sohni Lag Di]]''.<ref name=cokestudio/>

[[Image:Sajjadali at paiman.jpg|thumb|left|Sajjad Ali performs at the launch of a US sponsored music video to promote health awareness about mothers and newborn's.]] His brother [[Waqar Ali]] is also in the music industry. Sajjad, along with his brother, wrote lyrics for most of the songs in his albums, while lyricists Sabir Zafar, Mohammad Nasir, Nadeem Asad, and Ali Moin have also written some songs.

In July 2006, Sajjad released a single called "Chal Rein De", a more classical song. On 3 September 2006, he released another single in a totally different genre with the help of Meloscience Corp.<ref name=BR>{{cite web|url=https://www.brecorder.com/2017/08/24/366450/singer-sajjad-ali-turns-51/|title=Singer Sajjad Ali turns 51|author=Parvez Jabri|date=24 August 2017|publisher=Business Recorder (newspaper)|access-date=21 March 2018}}</ref> The song is known as "Sajjad-Ali Sinsym Fligh", and it has an experimental, jazzy, Sufi beat. In late April 2008, Sajjad released his album, ''[[Chahar Balish]]'', which contained new tracks as well as some previous tracks such as "Chal Rein De".

Two new compositions of Sajjad Ali from the film ''[[Bol (film)|Bol]]'' by [[Shoaib Mansoor]] were released by [[Tips Industries|Tips]] music on 22 May 2011 in India and on 30 May 2011 by [[Fire Records (Pakistan)|Fire Records]] in Pakistan.<ref name=IMDb>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1891757/fullcredits?ref_=tt_cl_sm#cast Full Cast & Crew of film Bol (2011) on IMDb website] Retrieved 21 March 2018</ref> He also appeared in the second episode of ''[[Coke Studio (Pakistan)|Coke Studio]]'' with his song "Kir Kir", which was broadcast on major television channels across Pakistan on 5 June 2011.<ref name=cokestudio>[http://www.cokestudio.com.pk/season10/sajjad-ali.html Profile of Sajjad Ali on Coke Studio (Pakistan) website] Retrieved 20 March 2018</ref><ref name=Tribune/>

In 2017, Sajjad released his single with [[Bohemia (rapper)]] (Tamasha), a massive hit on YouTube and social media. It was his first collaboration.

=== Films and television ===
Sajjad directed his first telefilm, ''[[Love Letter (Sajjad Ali album)|Love Letter]]'' in 1989, and then his first full-length motion picture, ''[[Aik Aur Love Story]]'' in 1998. Before that, he also performed as an actor and a singer in a film,  ''Munda Tera Deewana'' which was directed by Zahoor Husain Gillani. Sajjad has also sung songs for drama serials, ''Teri Yaad (Album)'' in the ''[[Pakistan Television Corporation|PTV]]'' drama serial ''Thori Khushi Thora Ghum'', "[[Rangeen|Na Boloon Gi]]" in ''Na Tum Jano Na Hum Janain'' (''[[ARY Digital]]'') and "Sunn Leyna" in ''Sunn Leyna'' (''[[ARY Digital]]'').<ref>[https://arynews.tv/en/sajjad-alis-set-release-new-music-video/ Sajjad Ali all set to release new music video] ARY TV News website, Published 18 February 2015, Retrieved 21 March 2018</ref> His brother [[Waqar Ali]] is also credited with the making of these songs.

Sajjad composed songs in [[Shoaib Mansoor]]'s 2011 film ''[[Bol (film)|Bol]]''. Sajjad stated that the songs were recorded in his own studio in Dubai and by the name of Din Pareshan hai, having two versions. According to Sajjad Ali this song is different from the song in his album ''Chahar Balish'' which has also the same title.<ref name=IMDb/>

=== Critical acclaim ===
Musician [[A. R. Rahman]] has described Sajjad as the original crossover:
"From the realm of the classical, he metamorphosed into one of the brightest lights of Pakistani pop. Always striking the right note, and never missing a beat, even the most hardened purist has to give Sajjad his due. This man can breathe life in a [[Ghazal]] even as he puts the V back into verve. He is one of the very few singers in Pakistan who seems a complete singer. As far as skill is concerned I feel nobody compares to Sajjad Ali. He is simply too good at everything he chooses to create."<ref name="A.R. Rahman">[http://www.arrahman.com/ A.R. Rahman official website]  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090903030816/http://www.arrahman.com/ |date=3 September 2009 }}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Sajjad married his uncle's daughter Naureen in 1990. They have four children; two sons and two daughters. He lives in [[Dubai Marina]], United Arab Emirates with his family, where he has been based for many years.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://tribune.com.pk/story/1282093/sajjad-ali-fall-star/|title=Sajjad Ali: The fall of a star|work=The Express Tribune|date=3 January 2017|access-date=26 May 2018|first=Rafay|last=Mahmood}}</ref> His two brothers, named "Waqar Ali" and "Lucky Ali", have also made a name in the Pakistan music industry.<ref name=BR/>
