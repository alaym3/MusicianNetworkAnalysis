'''Hassan Jahangir''' ({{lang-ur|{{Nastaliq|حسن جہانگیر}}}}; born 1 July 1962) is a [[Pakistani people|Pakistani]] [[pop singer]].<ref>[http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/ENTERTAINMENT/Bollywood/News-Interviews/Hawa-Hawa-Aap-ke-liye/articleshow/4727320.cms Hawa Hawa: Aap ke liye!] by KUNAL M SHAH , MUMBAI MIRROR, Jul 2, 2009</ref><ref>[http://www.dawn.com/weekly/dmag/archive/080316/dmag7.htm Getting marr(i)ed] (By Mansoor Murad) - DAWN.com  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090421151904/http://www.dawn.com/weekly/dmag/archive/080316/dmag7.htm |date=April 21, 2009 }}</ref> He gained fame in the 1980s with hit singles such as "Hawa Hawa", "Hato Bacho", and "Shadi Na Karna Yaron".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://connect.in.com/hasan-jahangir/biography-114899.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2010-12-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100502031428/http://connect.in.com/hasan-jahangir/biography-114899.html |archive-date=2010-05-02 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He released his first single "Imran Khan is a Superman" in 1982 and in June 1987 went on to release his one and only internationally famous album ''Hawa Hawa''. It sold approximately 15 million copies in [[India]]. His music was acclaimed in [[South Asia]], most notably Pakistan and India.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iSzuusWTqc4|title=YouTube|publisher=|accessdate=28 February 2017}}</ref><ref>[http://www.catchnews.com/entertainment-news/30-years-of-hawa-hawa-hasan-jahangir-on-why-the-song-rocks-even-today-69708.html 30 years of Hawa Hawa: Hasan Jahangir on why the song rocks even today] by Aditya Menon, Catch News, July 5, 2017</ref>

In 2011, with a fresh twist to a Bengali folk song Dol Dol Doloni, he came back into Pakistani music after absent of a long period.<ref>{{cite web|author=Rafay Mehmood |url= https://tribune.com.pk/story/278248/hassan-jahangir-hawa-hawa-hitmaker-makes-a-comeback/|title= Hassan Jahangir: ‘Hawa Hawa’ hitmaker makes a comeback|publisher= The Express Tribune |date= 20 October 2011 |accessdate=24 November 2018}} </ref> Bollywood [[Mubarakan]] released in 2017, is also a redecorated of the Pakistani song “Hawa Hawa” written by Rehan Azmi and sung by Hassan Jahangir in 1980s.<ref>{{cite web|author=Shaheera Anwar |url=https://tribune.com.pk/story/1770067/4-5-pakistani-songs-remixed-ruined-bollywood/ |title=5 Pakistani songs that were remixed and ruined by Bollywood |publisher= The Express Tribune |date= 31 July 2018 |accessdate=24 November 2018}} & its Music track: https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/tracks/n2vr8v</ref>
