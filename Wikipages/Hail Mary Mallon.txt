'''Hail Mary Mallon''' was an American [[hip-hop]] group consisting of [[Aesop Rock]], [[Rob Sonic]], and DJ Big Wiz.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rhymesayers.com/hailmarymallon|title=Rhymesayers: Hail Mary Mallon|publisher=Rhymesayers.com|accessdate=31 December 2014}}</ref> The group's name is a reference to [[Mary Mallon|Typhoid Mary]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pitchfork.com/news/42305-aesop-rock-launches-new-group-hail-mary-mallon-tours-and-works-with-kimya-dawson|title=Aesop Rock Launches New Group Hail Mary Mallon, Tours and Works With Kimya Dawson|publisher=Pitchfork.com|accessdate=April 25, 2011}}</ref> Their first release was the song "D-Up", featured on the [[compilation album]], ''Definitive Jux Presents 4''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Definitive-Jux-Presents-Vol-4/dp/B002LW9IVW |title=Definitive Jux Presents 4|publisher=Amazon.com|accessdate=31 December 2014}}</ref>

==History==
Aesop Rock and Rob Sonic first heard of each other in the mid to late 1990s through the New York City hip hop scene. Soon after, the two would become label mates on [[El-P]]'s independent hip hop label [[Definitive Jux]], with DJ Big Wiz contributing to Aesop Rock and Rob Sonic's earlier albums.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bandwagmag.com/2015/01/soul-sonic-forces-aesop-rock-and-rob-sonic-are-hail-mary-mallon/|title=Soul Sonic Forces: Aesop Rock and Rob Sonic are Hail Mary Mallon - BandWagon Magazine|date=2015-01-15|newspaper=BandWagon Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2017-01-21}}</ref>

On April 20, 2011, it was announced that [[Rhymesayers Entertainment]] would release Hail Mary Mallon's debut album ''[[Are You Gonna Eat That?]]'' A music video for the album's first single, "Smock", was also announced that same day.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rhymesayers.com/news/rhymesayers_to_release_hail_mary_mallon_s_upcoming_album_are_you_gonna_eat_that_and_drops_first_sing|title=Rhymesayers Entertainment|publisher=Rhymesayers.com|accessdate=31 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/listen-hip-hop-supergroup-hail-mary-mallons-debut-single-smock-20110419|title=Listen: Hip-Hop Supergroup Hail Mary Mallon's Debut Single, 'Smock'|work=Rolling Stone|accessdate=31 December 2014}}</ref> ''Are You Gonna Eat That?'' was released digitally on May 3, 2011,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/are-you-gonna-eat-that/id432799861 |title=Are You Gonna Eat That?|publisher=Itunes.apple.com|accessdate=31 December 2014}}</ref> and on CD and double vinyl on June 7, 2011 by [[Rhymesayers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rhymesayers.com/hailmarymallon |title=Rhymesayers: Hail Mary Mallon|publisher=Rhymesayers.com|accessdate=31 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pitchfork.com/news/42305-aesop-rock-launches-new-group-hail-mary-mallon-tours-and-works-with-kimya-dawson |title=Aesop Rock Launches New Group Hail Mary Mallon, Tours and Works With Kimya Dawson|publisher=Pitchfork.com|accessdate=April 25, 2011}}</ref>

On May 13, 2014, Aesop Rock announced via his [[Facebook]] page that Hail Mary Mallon had finished mixing a new album with an undisclosed release date.

On September 9, 2014, the first single, "Jonathan", was released on the Rhymesayers website, as well as the release date for their second album, ''[[Bestiary (Hail Mary Mallon album)|Bestiary]]'', which was released on November 11, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rhymesayers.com/hailmarymallon/news/pre_order_hail_mary_mallon_s_bestiary_now_watch_new_video|title=Rhymesayers Entertainment|publisher=Rhymesayers.com|accessdate=31 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141105154817/http://www.rhymesayers.com/hailmarymallon/news/pre_order_hail_mary_mallon_s_bestiary_now_watch_new_video|archive-date=5 November 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On November 11, 2018, Rob Sonic announced via his Twitter account that there would not be another Hail Mary Mallon album. The tweet has since been deleted. <ref>{{cite tweet |user=Rob_Sonic |author-link=Rob Sonic |number=1061563584965046272 |date=November 11, 2018 |title=There will not be a 3rd HMM album, thanks for the love all the same.}}</ref>
