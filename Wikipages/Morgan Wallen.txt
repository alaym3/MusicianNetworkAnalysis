'''Morgan Cole Wallen''' (born May 13, 1993) is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter. He competed in the [[The Voice (American season 6)|sixth season of ''The Voice'']], originally as a member of [[Usher (musician)|Usher]]'s team, but later as a member of [[Adam Levine]]'s team. After being eliminated in the playoffs of that season, he signed to [[Panacea Records]], releasing his debut EP, ''Stand Alone'', in 2015.

In 2016, Wallen signed to [[Big Loud]], releasing his debut album, ''[[If I Know Me (album)|If I Know Me]]'', in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-08-15|title=BIG LOUD RECORDS ADDS MORGAN WALLEN TO ROSTER {{!}} Big Loud Records|url=https://bigloudrecords.com/big-loud-records-adds-morgan-wallen-to-roster/|access-date=2021-02-28|language=en-US}}</ref> The album includes the singles "[[Up Down (Morgan Wallen song)|Up Down]]" (featuring [[Florida Georgia Line]]), "[[Whiskey Glasses]]", and "[[Chasin' You]]". ''If I Know Me'' reached No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Top Country Albums chart.<ref>{{Cite web|last=KFDI Staff|date=August 13, 2020|title=Morgan Wallen’s "If I Know Me" Reaches No. 1 on Billboard Top Country Albums Chart|url=https://www.kfdi.com/2020/08/13/morgan-wallens-if-i-know-me-reaches-no-1-on-billboard-top-country-albums-chart/|access-date=2021-02-28|website=Country 101.3 KFDI|language=en-US}}</ref> Wallen's second album, ''[[Dangerous: The Double Album]]'', was released in January 2021, and in February 2021 became the only country album in the 64-year history of the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] to spend its first seven weeks at No. 1.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=February 28, 2021|title=Morgan Wallen's 'Dangerous' Becomes Only Country Album to Spend First Seven Weeks at No. 1 on Billboard 200|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9532572/morgan-wallen-dangerous-tops-billboard-200-seven-weeks|access-date=2021-02-28|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> It went on to spend a total of ten weeks at that spot, the first album to do so since [[Whitney Houston]]'s ''[[Whitney (album)|Whitney]]'' in 1987.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sisario|first=Ben|date=March 22, 2021|title=Morgan Wallen Tops Chart for a 10th Week|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/03/22/arts/music/morgan-wallen-dangerous-billboard.html|access-date=2021-04-06|website=NY Times|language=en}}</ref> The album included ''Billboard'' No. 1 singles "[[More Than My Hometown]]", "[[7 Summers]]", and "[[Wasted on You (Morgan Wallen song)|Wasted on You]]".<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Morgan Wallen on Country Digital Song Sales|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/morgan-wallen/chart-history/cdt/|access-date=2021-02-28|website=Billboard}}</ref>

Wallen has gained considerable notoriety for several incidents. He was arrested for [[disorderly conduct]] after being ejected from [[Kid Rock]]'s steakhouse in Nashville in May 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Tamburin|first=Adam|date=May 24, 2020|title=Country artist Morgan Wallen arrested after being kicked out of Kid Rock's bar, police say|url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/news/local/2020/05/24/morgan-wallen-arrested-nashville-bar/5253959002/|access-date=2021-02-28|website=The Tennessean|language=en-US}}</ref> and was suspended from a scheduled ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' performance that October after video evidence surfaced that showed Wallen was not adhering to [[NBC]]'s [[COVID-19]] protocols.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hudak|first=Joseph|date=2020-10-07|title=Morgan Wallen Was Supposed to Play 'SNL.' Then He Went to a Party|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/morgan-wallen-snl-covid-apology-1072552/|access-date=2021-02-28|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> In February 2021, a video was released that captured Wallen saying a racial slur. The incident resulted in suspension from his record label and removal of his music from several major radio networks in the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|last=France|first=Lisa Respers|date=February 4, 2021|title=Morgan Wallen apologizes for racial slur as backlash grows|url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/02/03/entertainment/morgan-wallen-racial-slur/index.html|access-date=2021-02-28|website=CNN}}</ref> Despite the controversy, Wallen's music experienced a surge in commercial success following the incident.<ref name="APHall"/>

==Early life==
Morgan Wallen was born to Tommy and Lesli Wallen on May 13, 1993 in [[Sneedville, Tennessee]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Randell|first=Louise|date=February 4, 2021|title=Everything we know about Morgan Wallen's parents|url=https://www.stylesrant.com/everything-we-know-about-morgan-wallen-parents/|access-date=February 28, 2021|website=StylesRant|language=en-US}}</ref> As a child, Wallen took piano and violin lessons. He aspired to play baseball in college, but after injuring his elbow he chose to pursue music.<ref name="nash">{{cite web|last=Casey|first=Jim|date=March 13, 2017|title=Who's New: Morgan Wallen|url=https://www.thecountrydaily.com/2017/03/13/whos-new-morgan-wallen-2/|access-date=February 28, 2021|work=The Country Daily}}</ref>

==Career==
===2014: ''The Voice''===
In 2014, Wallen competed on [[The Voice (U.S. season 6)|Season 6]] of ''[[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|The Voice]]'', initially as part of [[Usher (musician)|Usher]]'s team and later [[Adam Levine]]'s team. He was eliminated during the playoffs.<ref name="nash"/>

===2015: Panacea Records and ''Stand Alone'' EP===
While in California to appear on ''[[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|The Voice]]'', Wallen began working with Sergio Sanchez of [[Atom Smash]]. Sanchez introduced Wallen to Bill Ray and [[Paul Trust]] of [[Panacea Records]].<ref name="carolina">{{cite web|url=https://carolinacountrymusicfest.com/artist/morgan-wallen/|title=Morgan Wallen Artist Bio|work=[[Carolina Country Music Fest]]|access-date=May 6, 2020}}</ref> In 2015, Wallen signed to [[Panacea Records]] where he released an EP ''Stand Alone'' on August 24, 2015. "Spin You Around" was Wallen's debut single under [[Panacea Records]] and was certified gold by the [[Riaa|Recording Industry Association of America]] on April 21, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gold & Platinum|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=spin+you+around#search_section|access-date=2021-04-30|website=RIAA|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2015, Wallen's manager, Dirk Hemsath of Working Group Artist Management, sent a demo of Wallen to [[Big Loud Shirt|Big Loud Shirt’s]] Seth England, who had Wallen audition for his partners in [[Big Loud|Big Loud Records]]. They signed Wallen to the label and the publishing company.<ref name="carolina"/>

===2020–present: ''Dangerous''===
On May 27, 2020, Wallen released "[[More Than My Hometown]]" to country radio as the lead single to his upcoming second studio album. On August 14, Wallen released the song "[[7 Summers]]" and it broke the record for the most first day streams for a country song on [[Apple Music]]. It earned him his first top 10 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 chart, debuting and peaking at number six.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.countrynow.com/morgan-wallen-shatters-records-with-7-summers/amp/|title=Morgan Wallen Shatters Records With '7 Summers'|work=Country Now|last=Black|first=Lauren Jo|date=August 19, 2020|access-date=August 19, 2020}}</ref> The song was also chosen by the ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' as one of the best songs of 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The 10 Best Songs of 2020|url=https://time.com/5914984/best-songs-2020/|access-date=December 10, 2020|website=Time}}</ref> On November 11, Wallen announced that his second album would be a double album titled ''[[Dangerous: The Double Album]]'', set for release on January 8, 2021.<ref name="Dangerous">{{cite web|last=Dukes|first=Billy|date=November 11, 2020|title=Morgan Wallen Announces New 'Dangerous' Double Album, Featuring Chris Stapleton|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/morgan-wallen-dangerous-album/|access-date=November 12, 2020|work=Taste of Country}}</ref> Wallen announced the release date for the album as an Easter egg in the short film for "7 Summers".

==Controversies==
In May 2020, Wallen was arrested outside [[Kid Rock]]'s bar in downtown Nashville for public intoxication and disorderly conduct.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wsmv.com/news/country-music-artist-arrested-in-downtown-nashville/article_ea245f74-9dfc-11ea-b095-7feb5a7db419.html|title=Country music artist arrested in Downtown Nashville|first=Blake|last=Summers|website=Wsmv.com|date=May 24, 2020|access-date=January 12, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wkrn.com/news/crime-tracker/country-artist-morgan-wallen-arrested-after-incident-at-kid-rocks-honky-tonk/|title=Country artist Morgan Wallen arrested after incident at Kid Rock's Honky Tonk|website=Wkrn.com|date=May 24, 2020|access-date=January 12, 2021}}</ref> Wallen apologized on social media for his conduct.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/morgan-wallen-apologizes-arrest-drunk-disorderly/|title=Morgan Wallen Apologizes for Drunk and Disorderly Arrest: 'We Didn't Mean Any Harm'|first=Streling|last=Whitaker|website=Taste of Country|date=May 24, 2020}}</ref>

The game was played on October 3, 2020, a week before his scheduled debut on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' on October 10, 2020. In the videos, Wallen was shown maskless, violating [[social distancing]] guidelines, singing at a house party, and kissing women.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Low|first=Elaine|date=October 7, 2020|title=Morgan Wallen Under Scrutiny for Maskless Partying Ahead of 'SNL' Appearance|url=https://variety.com/2020/tv/news/morgan-wallen-saturday-night-live-covid-partying-1234795121/|access-date=December 6, 2020|website=Variety|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Wright|first=Megh|date=October 7, 2020|title=SNL's Next Musical Guest Spent the Weekend Kissing and Partying Mask Free|url=https://www.vulture.com/2020/10/snl-morgan-wallen-musical-guest-partying-covid-19.html|access-date=December 6, 2020|website=Vulture|language=en-us}}</ref><ref name="aderoju">{{cite web |last1=Aderoju |first1=Darlene |title=Morgan Wallen Faces Criticism After Partying amid Coronavirus Ahead of SNL Appearance |url=https://people.com/country/morgan-wallen-snl-criticism-after-partying/ |website=[[People (magazine)|People]] |access-date=October 9, 2020 |date=October 7, 2020 }}</ref> On October 7, Wallen announced that the producers of ''SNL'' had postponed his appearance for violating the show's COVID-19 protocols.<ref name="wilusz"/> Wallen went on to appear as musical guest on the December 5 episode.
