'''Lola Chantrelle Mitchell<ref name="ascap"/>''' (born August 7, 1979), better known by her [[stage name]] '''Gangsta Boo''', is an American rapper. She was the first female member of the [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]]-based [[hip hop music|hip hop]] group [[Three 6 Mafia]], where she initially rose to prominence. She left the group and label sometime in 2002, months after the release of her sophomore album, ''[[Both Worlds *69]]'', due to money disputes and issues regarding promotion of her album. She has since released [[mixtapes]] alongside artists like [[La Chat]] and [[Yelawolf]], as well as some solo music. She has also been featured on albums by [[clipping.]] and [[Run The Jewels]]. She has been referred to as a pioneer of female rap music.<ref>https://thegumbo.net/blog/2020/9/10/gangsta-boo-the-southern-rap-pioneer</ref><ref>https://www.vice.com/amp/en/article/rmj9vv/gangsta-boo-interview-kill-bitches-video-premiere</ref>

==Early life==
Lola Chantrelle Mitchell was born in the [[Whitehaven, Memphis|Whitehaven]] area of [[Memphis, Tennessee]], on August 7, 1979. She is from a [[middle class]] family that later "moved to the hood" after her parents had divorced. She started rapping at around age 16.<ref>https://i-d.vice.com/amp/en_uk/article/3kqq5b/twenty-years-in-the-rap-game-with-three-6-mafias-gangsta-boo</ref>

==Music career==
Boo's first solo album, ''[[Enquiring Minds]]'', was released in 1998 and reached number fifteen on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart and number 46 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/charts/1999-05-22/r-b-hip-hop-albums ''Enquiring Minds'' (Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart)], ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', May 22, 1999.</ref><ref>[http://www.billboard.com/charts/1998-11-21/billboard-200 ''Enquiring Minds'' (Billboard 200 chart)], ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', November 21, 1998.</ref>  The album featured the surprise hit ''Where Dem Dollas At!?''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.memphisflyer.com/CallingtheBluff/archives/2013/03/25/q-and-a-with-gangsta-boo | title=Q & A with Gangsta Boo | publisher=Memphis Flyer | date=March 25, 2013 | access-date=May 7, 2014 | author=Louis Goggans}}</ref>

Boo released her second album, ''[[Both Worlds *69]]'', in 2001, which reached number eight on the R&B/Hip-Hop chart and number 29 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/charts/2001-11-03/r-b-hip-hop-albums ''Both Worlds *69'' (Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart)], ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', November 3, 2001.</ref><ref>[http://www.billboard.com/charts/2001-10-06/billboard-200 ''Both Worlds *69'' (Billboard 200 chart)], ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', October 6, 2001.</ref> In 2003, she released her third album, ''[[Enquiring Minds II: The Soap Opera]]''.  The album peaked at number 53 on the R&B/Hip-Hop chart and 24 on the [[Independent Albums]] chart.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/charts/2003-11-22/r-b-hip-hop-albums Minds II: Soap Opera'' (Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart)'']'', ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', November 22, 2003.''</ref><ref>[http://www.billboard.com/charts/2003-11-15/independent-albums ''Enquiring Minds II: Soap Opera'' (Top Independent Albums chart)], ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', November 15, 2003.</ref>

In 2009, Boo released her third official mixtape ''The Rumors'' (following her previous mixtapes ''Street Ringers Vol. 1'' & ''Still Gangsta'').  She also released two mixtapes with DJ Fletch, ''Miss.Com'' (No DJ Version on iTunes) and ''4 Da Hood''. On June 27, 2011, she released her mixtape with Trap-A-Holics, ''Forever Gangsta''. In 2013, she joined the Three 6 Mafia spin-off group [[Da Mafia 6ix]], and was significantly featured on their debut mixtape [[6ix Commandments]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Da-Mafia-6ix-6ix-Commandments-mixtape.550198.html |title=Da Mafia 6ix - 6ix Commandments Hosted by Trap-A-Holics Mixtape - Stream & Download |publisher=Datpiff.com |access-date=August 10, 2015}}</ref> She departed from the group in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last=Baker |first=Soren |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.28690/title.dj-paul-reveals-gangsta-boo-s-departure-from-da-mafia-6ix |title=DJ Paul Reveals Gangsta Boo's Departure From Da Mafia 6ix |publisher=HipHopDX |date=May 29, 2014 |access-date=August 10, 2015}}</ref> On May 27, 2014, the [[extended play]] ''Witch'', a collaboration with [[La Chat]], was released. On October 14, 2014, Gangsta Boo teamed up with BeatKing and released a collaborative mixtape, ''Underground Cassette Tape Music, Vol. 1''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Gangsta-Boo-Beatking-Underground-Cassette-Tape-Music/release/6227973|title=Gangsta Boo & Beatking* - Underground Cassette Tape Music|website=Discogs|language=en|access-date=April 24, 2019}}</ref> In 2018, a successor mixtape titled ''Underground Cassette Tape Music, Vol. 2'', was released.

She was featured on [[Run The Jewels]]' fourth album, ''[[RTJ4]]'', released in 2020, on the track ''walking in the snow''.
