"'''La mentira (Se te olvida)'''", known in English as "'''Yellow Days'''", is a [[bolero]] written by [[Álvaro Carrillo]] around 1965.<ref name="Cunningham2010">{{cite book|last=Cunningham|first=Michael G.|title=Gilded Songs (Berlin to Bacharach): The Gig Instrumentalist's Guide to the Golden Era of American Popular Song (1920 to 1979)|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=SyxG0sOXSbsC&pg=PA50|date=August 2010|publisher=AuthorHouse|isbn=978-1-4520-4527-6|page=50}}</ref> Alan Bernstein wrote the song's lyrics in English. It has become a [[Latin music (genre)|Latin]] and [[jazz standard]], having been featured as the theme song of films and ''[[telenovela]]s'',<ref>{{cite book|editor1-last=Balderston|editor1-first=Daniel|editor2-last=González|editor2-first=Mike|editor3-last=López|editor3-first=Ana M.|title=Encyclopedia of Contemporary Latin American and Caribbean Cultures|date=2000|publisher=Routledge|location=London, UK|page=285|chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kp6FAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA285|chapter=Álvaro Carrillo|isbn=9781134788521}}</ref> as well as being covered by artists such as [[Frank Sinatra]], who sang it to an arrangement by [[Billy May]].<ref>{{cite book|title=The Music of Billy May: A Discography|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QQarAAAAIAAJ|date=1 January 1998|publisher=Greenwood Press|isbn=978-0-313-30739-3|page=275}}</ref> It appeared on the ''[[Francis A. & Edward K.]]'' (Sinatra and [[Duke Ellington]]) album on [[Reprise Records|Reprise]]. It has also been covered by [[Jerry Vale]], [[the Sandpipers]], [[Anita Bryant]], [[Johnny Mann]], [[Lenny Dee (organist)|Lenny Dee]], [[Johnny Mathis]], and [[Jonah Jones]].<ref name="Inc.1968">{{cite book|title=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qggEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA6|date=4 May 1968|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|page=6|issn=0006-2510}}</ref>
