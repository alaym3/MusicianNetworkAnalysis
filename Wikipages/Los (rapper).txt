'''Carlos Coleman''' (born March 23, 1982), better known by his stage name '''King Los''' or simply '''Los''' is an American [[Rapping|rapper]] and [[lyricist]]. He originally signed to [[Bad Boy Records]] during the 2005 in conjunction with his Bloc Incorporated record deal. After his deal fell through he returned to releasing music independently. Los signed to [[Sean Combs|Diddy]]'s Bad Boy Records again in February 2012, which is now distributed by [[Interscope Records]]. However, on March 19, 2014, he would announce that he was again leaving Bad Boy and Interscope. He is currently signed to After Platinum, 88 Classic and [[RCA Records|RCA]].

== Musical career ==

===1999–2010===
As an 18-year-old high school student, Coleman started as a rapper with his main subject being the murder of his father. In 2002 Los auditioned for [[Sean Combs|Diddy]]'s ''[[Making the Band 2]]'', but after being selected to appear on the show, he refused to sign any of the paperwork required so he was unable to appear on it.<ref name="citypaper1">{{cite web|url=http://blogs.citypaper.com/noise/index.php/2012/02/los-signs-with-bad-boy-records-again/ |title=Los Signs With Bad Boy Records. . .Again &#124; Noise |publisher=Blogs.citypaper.com |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref><ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1678644/diddy-los-bad-boy-records-signee.jhtml |title=Exclusive: Diddy Adds Baltimore MC Los To Bad Boy Roster – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV |date=February 6, 2012 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> Later, Coleman would end up signed to [[Bad Boy Records]] in 2005 through Da Bloc Incorporated, a local Baltimore record label. Da Bloc Incorporated eventually disbanded due to a legal issue and Los lost his deal in 2008 before he was able to release an album.<ref name="hiphopwired1">{{cite web|author= by C. Vernon Coleman II February 7, 2012, 10:57am |url=http://hiphopwired.com/2012/02/07/diddy-re-signs-baltimore-rapper-los-to-bad-boy-records/ |title=Diddy Re-Signs Baltimore Rapper Los To Bad Boy Records |publisher=Hip-Hop Wired |date=February 7, 2012 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref>

In 2008 Los released a number of mixtapes and online freestyles. His first and second would be ''Guilty Until Proven Innocent'' and ''G5: Certified Fly'', both released in 2008.<ref name="stovall1">{{cite web|last=Stovall |first=Calvin |url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2012/02/22/los-credits-big-brother-diddy-for-bad-boy-return.html |title=Los Credits 'Big Brother' Diddy for Bad Boy Return  |publisher=BET |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> He followed that with ''G5.2'' in May 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Los-G52-mixtape.47806.html |title=Los – G5.2 Hosted by Dj Wonda // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |date=May 27, 2009 |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref> His third mixtape ''Zero Gravity'' was released during April 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/LoS-Zero-Gravity-mixtape.115410.html |title=LoS – Zero Gravity Hosted by Presented By After Platinum Records and The Aphilliates // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |date=April 26, 2010 |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref> Then, the mixtape was ''Welcome To Swaggsville'' was released on August 5, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Los-Welcome-To-Swaggsville-VeryHot-mixtape.59475.html |title=Los – Welcome To Swaggsville [VeryHot!!!&#93; // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref> His sixth mixtape ''Shooter'' was released on November 8, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Los-Shooter-mixtape.167467.html |title=Los – Shooter Hosted by DJ ill Will, DJ Rockstar // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> It featured collaborations with [[Rick Ross]] and his After Platinum label-mate [[Cory Gunz]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Paine |first=Jake |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.12866/title.los-explains-baltimore-influencing-raps-shooter-mixtape |title=Los Explains Baltimore Influencing Raps, "Shooter" Mixtape &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=November 4, 2010 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> On December 26, 2010, Los released his seventh mixtape ''The Louis Vuitton Gift Pack''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globalgrind.com/2010/12/26/los-the-louis-vuitton-gift-pack-mixtape-new-music/ |title=Los: The Louis Vuitton Gift Pack Mixtape (NEW MUSIC) |publisher=Global Grind |date=December 26, 2010 |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref> The mixtape was primarily composed of freestyles and featured a collaboration with [[Chris Brown]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Los-The-Louis-Vuitton-Gift-Pack-mixtape.182278.html |title=Los – The Louis Vuitton Gift Pack Hosted by After Platinum // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |date=December 26, 2010 |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref>

===2011–2013: ''The Crown Ain't Safe'' and ''Becoming King''===
On July 18, 2011, Los released a mixtape titled ''Worth the Wait'', mostly made of tracks cut from his mixtape ''The Crown Ain't Safe''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://thesource.com/2011/07/19/loschatswiththesourcecomonwhatsworththewait/ |title=Los Chats With TheSource.com On What's 'Worth The Wait' |publisher=TheSource |accessdate=January 9, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110000351/http://thesource.com/2011/07/19/loschatswiththesourcecomonwhatsworththewait/ |archive-date=January 10, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On December 5, 2011, Los released ''The Crown Ain't Safe''. The mixtape featured guest appearances by [[Lola Monroe]], [[Jazze Pha]], [[DMX (rapper)|DMX]], [[Kid Ink]], [[Twista]] and [[XV (rapper)|XV]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://thesource.com/2011/12/05/mixtapelos-thecrownaintsafe/ |title=Mixtape: Los – The Crown Ain't Safe |publisher=TheSource |accessdate=January 9, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110000510/http://thesource.com/2011/12/05/mixtapelos-thecrownaintsafe/ |archive-date=January 10, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>  Along with [[Record production|production]] by [[Jahlil Beats]], [[Lifted (record producer)|Lifted]], J. Oliver and Rick Steel, among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2011/12/los-drops-new-mixtape-the-crown-aint-safe-download-now/ |title=Los Drops New Mixtape The Crown Ain't Safe [Download Now&#93; – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 5, 2011 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> It was met with decent critical reception upon release.<ref name="allhiphop1">{{cite web|author=Jake Crates |url=http://allhiphop.com/2012/02/13/los-bad-boys-baltimore-billionaire-in-training/ |title=Exclusive: Los: Bad Boy's Baltimore Billionaire in Training |publisher=AllHipHop.com |date=February 13, 2012 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> On February 6, 2012, Diddy announced to [[MTV]] that his newest signee to Bad Boy Records was Los.<ref name="autogenerated1"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2012/02/06/diddysignslostobadboyrecords/ |title=Diddy Signs Los To Bad Boy Records |publisher=TheSource |accessdate=January 9, 2014|date=February 6, 2012 }}</ref> Right after the announcement Coleman began working on his debut mixtape for Bad Boy.<ref name="allhiphop1"/>

Bad Boy released the mixtape ''Becoming King'' on April 25, 2013.<ref>{{cite web | first=Wesley | last=Case | work=The Baltimore Sun | url=http://articles.baltimoresun.com/2013-04-25/entertainment/bal-baltimore-rapper-los-releases-becoming-king-mixtape_1_local-rapper-datpiff-baltimore | title=Baltimore rapper Los releases 'Becoming King' mixtape – Baltimore Sun | date=April 25, 2013 | accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref>  The mixtape featured all-original music and numerous guest artists. Production was handled by [[1500 or Nothin']], [[Harry Fraud]], J. Oliver, Rob Holladay and [[Sonny Digital]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|author=Los |url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/los-becoming-king |title=Los – Becoming King – Download & Stream |publisher=Djbooth.net |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> ''Becoming King'' was met with generally positive reviews from [[music critic]]s.<ref name="xxlmag1">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/reviews/2013/05/mixtape-review-los-becoming-king/ |title=Mixtape Review: Los, Becoming King – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=May 10, 2013 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://mimo.recordingconnection.com/los-becoming-king-mixtape-review/963808/ |title=Los 'Becoming King' (Mixtape Review) |publisher=Mimo.recordingconnection.com |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thedrop.fm/los-becoming-king-mixtape-review/ |title=Los, 'Becoming King' – Mixtape Review |publisher=Thedrop.fm |date=April 30, 2013 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> ''XXL'' gave the mixtape a positive review saying, "There are heartfelt tales touching on his family, love for music and the trials and tribulations of life. As usual, Los’ strongest asset is an ability to deliver vivid imagery through lyrics and a captivating flow[...] Lyrically...the mixtape is a continuation of the verbal assaults he's known for."<ref name="xxlmag1"/>

===2013–present: Departure from Bad Boy and signed to RCA ===
Los was featured on labelmate [[French Montana]]'s song "Ocho Cinco" also featuring labelmates Diddy, [[Red Cafe]] and [[MGK]] taken from the deluxe edition of Montana's album ''[[Excuse My French (album)|Excuse My French]]''. On August 13, 2013, Los released a freestyle over the controversial "[[Control (Big Sean song)|Control]]" song.<ref>{{cite web|last=Homie |first=Big |url=http://rapradar.com/2013/08/13/new-music-los-control-freestyle/ |title=New Music: Los "Control (Freestyle)" (Kendrick Response) |publisher=Rap Radar |date=August 13, 2013 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref>  He was then featured on "No Option" by [[Kid Ink]], a [[promotional single]] from Ink's second studio album ''[[My Own Lane]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2013/12/premiere-kid-ink-featuring-king-los-no-option/ |title=Premiere: Kid Ink Featuring King Los "No Option" – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=December 16, 2013 |accessdate=January 9, 2014}}</ref> On December 28, 2013, Los released ''Broken Silence'' a collaboration mixtape with rapper Mark Battles. The mixtape featured guest appearances from [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]] among others.<ref>{{cite web |last=Baker |first=Soren |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26828/title.king-los-mark-battles-broken-silence-release-date-cover-art-download-mixtape-stream |title=King Los & Mark Battles "Broken Silence" Release Date, Cover Art, Download & Mixtape Stream &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=December 30, 2013 |accessdate=January 9, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150113005509/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26828/title.king-los-mark-battles-broken-silence-release-date-cover-art-download-mixtape-stream |archive-date=January 13, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Following the announcement of the mixtape ''Zero Gravity II'', on March 19, 2014, Los revealed that he was leaving Bad Boy Records and Interscope.<ref>{{cite web|author=Emmanuel C.M. |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/03/king-los-parting-ways-bad-boy-records/ |title=King Los Is Parting Ways With Bad Boy Records – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=March 19, 2014 |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref> He later elaborated on the story to [[MTV]] saying, "I've chose to explore other endeavors just like my mentor; just like Puff."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1724453/king-los-leaves-bad-boy.jhtml |title=King Los Leaves Bad Boy: Was Diddy's 'Nobody' Rant The Reason? |publisher=MTV |date=March 19, 2014 |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref>

On March 24, 2014, Los released his twelfth official mixtape ''Zero Gravity II''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Diep |first=Eric |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/03/king-los-releases-new-mixtape-zero-gravitiy-ii/ |title=King Los Releases New Mixtape, 'Zero Gravitiy II' – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref> Following that, from April 1 to May 31, 2014, Los toured with collaborator Kid Ink as a supporting act on the My Own Lane [[concert tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopwired.com/2014/02/13/kid-ink-my-own-lane-tour-king-los-bizzy-crook/ |title=Kid Ink Announces My Own Lane Tour With King Los & Bizzy Crook [PHOTO |publisher=Hip-Hop Wired |date=February 13, 2014 |accessdate=March 24, 2014}}</ref>

On October 23, 2014 Los made an appearance on [[WQHT|HOT 97]]'s ''Ebro in the Morning'' radio show and confirmed a new deal with [[RCA Records]] and 88 Classic, with the only details revealed being the announcement of [[P. Diddy]] being an executive producer for his pre-debut album, titled ''[[God, Money, War]]'', with RCA and 88 Classic.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2014/10/23/king-los-signs-with-rca.html | title=King los Signs with 88 Classic, RCA Records| website=[[BET]]}}</ref> It was released June 23, 2015. On August 19, 2015, Los appeared on [[Why? with Hannibal Buress]], performing the title track from ''God, Money, War''. Los then went on to perform on the God, Money, War [[Concert tour|Tour]] along with fellow rapper [[Skate (rapper)|Skate Maloley]] and others. He was one of many features on [[P. Diddy]]'s mixtape ''[[MMM (Money Making Mitch)|MMM]]'', [[Lil Wayne]]'s mixtape ''[[No Ceilings 2]]'' and later [[Wiz Khalifa]]'s mixtape ''[[Cabin Fever 3]]''.

On July 3, 2015 Los appeared in Damond Blue music video "Lemme Talk My Sh*t"<ref>{{Citation|last=Damond Blue|title=Damond Blue: Lemme Talk My Sh*t feat. King Los produced by Jay Oliver|date=2015-07-03|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g2sVh-chUYQ|access-date=2018-07-07}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=August 2018}} In 2018, it was announced that Los would be his battle rap return with Rare Breed Entertainment. His opponent was originally slated to be the West Coast battle rap veteran, Daylyt. However a month before the event, Daylyt dropped out and was replaced by East Coast rapper and battle rap veteran, Head ICE.
