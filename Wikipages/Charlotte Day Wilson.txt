'''Charlotte Day Wilson''' is a Canadian [[contemporary R&B]] singer and songwriter.<ref name=boysclub>[https://nowtoronto.com/music/features/charlotte-day-wilson-wants-to-break-up-boys-club/ "Charlotte Day Wilson wants to break up the boys' club"]. ''[[Now (newspaper)|Now]]'', February 21, 2018.</ref> She came to prominence in the mid-2010s with her single "Work" and collaborations with other Toronto-based artists like [[BadBadNotGood]] and [[Daniel Caesar]]. Wilson released her debut album ''[[Alpha (Charlotte Day Wilson album)|Alpha]]'' in July 2021.

==Life and career==
A native of [[Toronto]], Ontario, Wilson studied classical piano in childhood before teaching herself production via [[GarageBand]] as a teenager.<ref name=fader>[http://www.thefader.com/2018/03/20/charlotte-day-wilson-stone-woman-interview-cdw-toronto "Charlotte Day Wilson is a one-person powerhouse"]. ''[[The Fader]]'', March 20, 2018.</ref> She moved to [[Halifax, Nova Scotia|Halifax]], Nova Scotia to study music at university,<ref>[https://www.thecoast.ca/halifax/charlotte-day-wilsons-homecoming/Content?oid=10167669 "Charlotte Day Wilson’s homecoming"]. ''[[The Coast (newspaper)|The Coast]]'', October 19, 2017.</ref> but left school to focus on her music career. Wilson identifies as [[queer]].<ref name="boysclub" />

She self-released the EP ''Palimpsest'' in 2012,<ref>[https://www.thecoast.ca/halifax/perfect-palimpsest/Content?oid=3460959 "Charlotte Day Wilson's perfect Palimpsest"]. ''[[The Coast (newspaper)|The Coast]]'', November 1, 2012.</ref> and followed up with the standalone singles "Avondale," "Stephen," and "Montreal" in 2013 and 2014. She was also part of the funk band The Wayo,<ref name="fader" /> which released an EP in 2014, among other releases. She contributed vocals, keys, and saxophone.

She spent some time living in [[Montreal]], Quebec, before returning to Toronto and interning at [[Arts & Crafts Productions]].<ref name=fader/> There, Wilson began collaborating with artists such as [[Daniel Caesar]], [[River Tiber (musician)|River Tiber]], and [[BadBadNotGood]]<ref name="fader" /> before releasing her second EP, ''CDW'', in 2016.<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/charlotte_day_wilson-cdw "Charlotte Day Wilson: CDW"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', August 31, 2016.</ref>

The EP's song "Work" was nominated for the [[SOCAN Songwriting Prize]] in 2017,<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/pup_win_the_2017_socan_songwriting_prize "PUP Win the 2017 SOCAN Songwriting Prize"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', July 12, 2017.</ref> the EP was a longlisted nominee for the [[2017 Polaris Music Prize]];<ref>[http://www.macleans.ca/culture/arts/the-polaris-music-prize-2017-longlist-by-the-numbers/ "The Polaris Music Prize 2017 longlist, by the numbers"]. ''[[Maclean's]]'', June 13, 2017.</ref> additionally, producer [[Howie Beck]] received a [[Juno Award]] nomination for [[Jack Richardson Producer of the Year Award|Producer of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2017]] for his contributions to "Work" and [[Dragonette]]'s "High Five".<ref>[https://indie88.com/the-2017-juno-nominees-are-in/ "THE 2017 JUNO NOMINEES ARE IN"]. [[CIND-FM|Indie 88]], February 7, 2017.</ref> Wilson's video for "Work", directed by [[Fantavious Fritz]], won the 2018 [[Prism Prize]].<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/charlotte_day_wilson_wins_2018_prism_prize "Charlotte Day Wilson Wins 2018 Prism Prize"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', May 14, 2018.</ref> Wilson and Fritz subsequently announced that they were using the prize money to create a special grant program for emerging female video directors.<ref>[https://nowtoronto.com/music/charlotte-day-wilson-prism-prize/ "Charlotte Day Wilson is creating a grant for female music video directors"]. ''[[Now (newspaper)|Now]]'', May 14, 2018.</ref>

Her third EP, ''Stone Woman'', was released in 2018.<ref name="boysclub" /> The song "Falling Apart" from that album was sampled on the 2020 [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]] track "I Keep Calling."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-14|title=Stream James Blake's Spectacular New Dance-Pop EP 'Before'|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2102225/james-blake-before-ep/music/|access-date=2020-10-15|website=Stereogum}}</ref> In 2018, Vinyl Me, Please released an exclusive album that compiled ''CDW'' and ''Stone Woman''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-07-23|title=VMP Rising: Charlotte Day Wilson|url=http://magazine.vinylmeplease.com/magazine/vmp-rising-charlottedaywilson/|access-date=2020-11-24|website=Vinyl Me Please|language=en}}</ref>

In 2021, she was nominated for the [[Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for Traditional R&B/Soul Recording of the Year|Traditional R&B/Soul Recording of the Year]] for her single "Take Care of You" featuring [[Syd (singer)|Syd]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Winners + Nominees|url=https://junoawards.ca/awards/winners-nominees/|access-date=2021-03-10|website=The JUNO Awards|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== ''Alpha'' ===
Following a May 2021 announcement, Wilson released her debut album ''Alpha'' on July 9, 2021 to favorable reviews.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Charlotte Day Wilson Announces Debut Album 'Alpha'|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/charlotte_day_wilson_announces_debut_album_alpha|date=May 20, 2021|author=Allie Gregory|website=[[Exclaim!]]|language=en-ca}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=ALPHA by Charlotte Day Wilson|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/alpha/charlotte-day-wilson|language=en|access-date=2021-07-10}}</ref>  Written and produced by Wilson, the album features a number of tracks co-written and produced with Toronto songwriter Jack Rochon (Jack Ro) in addition to contributions from other Toronto acts like [[Daniel Caesar]], [[BadBadNotGood]], [[Mustafa the Poet|Mustafa]], and Merna Bishouty. Other producers include retro soul artist [[Thomas Brenneck]], R&B producer [[D'Mile]] in collaboration with [[Babyface (musician)|Babyface]], Dylan Wiggins (Sir Dyan), and [[Teo Halm]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Van Nguyen|first=Dean|date=July 9, 2021|title=Charlotte Day Wilson: Alpha|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/charlotte-day-wilson-alpha/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-10|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref>
