'''Flyte''' are an English indie rock band from [[London]]. Its current members are lead singer and guitarist Will Taylor, and bassist Nicolas Hill. Jon Supran was formerly Flyte’s drummer but left in mid-2021. Sam Berridge was formerly Flyte's keyboardist. <ref>{{cite web|last1=Chamberlain|first1=Irune|title=Introducing: Flyte|url=http://spindlemagazine.com/2014/11/introducing-flyte/|website=Spindlemagazine.com|accessdate=17 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://weallwantsomeone.org/2020/10/22/flyte-share-new-song-ive-got-a-girl/ |title=Flyte Share New Song “I’ve Got A Girl”  |last=Oliver |first=Will |date=October 22, 2020 |website=We All Want Someone to Shout For |publisher= |access-date=March 18, 2021 |quote=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/aug/28/new-band-of-day-flyte |title=Flyte (No 1,584) |last=Lester |first=Paul|date=August 28, 2013 |website= |publisher= |access-date=March 18, 2021 |quote=}}</ref>

== Early years ==
Taylor and Supran started playing together when they were eleven at their local comprehensive school. Hill and Berridge joined later; the rest of the band first heard Berridge singing in the London Underground. The band takes its name from Sebastian Flyte of [[Evelyn Waugh]]'s ''[[Brideshead Revisited]]''. After a video of Taylor and Berridge covering [[Joni Mitchell]]'s "[[River (Joni Mitchell song)|River]]" went viral, the band signed a record deal with [[Island Records]].{{citation needed|date=December 2017}}

Flyte's first single "We Are the Rain" was released on [[Transgressive Records]] on 28 April 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Cragg|first1=Michael|title=Flyte – We Are The Rain: New music|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/musicblog/2014/mar/14/flyte-we-are-the-rain|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|accessdate=17 November 2014}}</ref> The single and B-side "Where Nobody Knows Your Name" were mixed and produced by Dan Grech-Marguerat and released digitally along with a limited run of 7-inch vinyl. The video for "We Are the Rain" was premiered by Michael Cragg on ''[[The Guardian]]''{{'}}s music blog. The single received support from Radio 1, [[XFM]], [[BBC 6Music]], [[BBC London]], and [[Amazing Radio]]. It was announced in 2013 that the band had been signed by [[Island Records]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Future Stars:Flyte Island Records|url= http://www.thismustbepop.com/2013/09/23/flyte-island-records/ |website=ThisMustBePop.com}}</ref>

The second single from ''Light Me Up'' was released on Turned Out Nice / Island Records on 17 November 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Day|first1=Laurence|title=Flyte announce new single "Light Me Up"|url=http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/flyte-announce-new-single-light-me-up|website=Thelineofbestfit.com|accessdate=17 November 2014}}</ref> The single was recorded by Hugh Fielding at the band's own recording facility within Goldmine Studios in [[Bethnal Green]] and mixed by [[Mike Crossey]].

The band released two further singles on Island Records: "Closer Together"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/listen-to-flyte-s-joyously-claustrophobic-closer-together-143|title=Flyte - 'Closer Together'|website=TheFourOhFive.com|access-date=19 December 2017}}</ref> and "Please Eloise".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2015/08/21/flyte-announce-new-single-please-eloise|title=Flyte announce new single, ‘Please Eloise’|website=Diymag.com}}</ref> "Closer Together" was remixed by [[Bombay Bicycle Club]] singer [[Jack Steadman]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://earmilk.com/2015/07/13/jack-steadman-brings-us-all-closer-together-with-his-remix-of-flyte-premiere/|title=Jack Steadman brings us all "Closer Together" with his remix of Flyte [Premiere] - EARMILK|date=13 July 2015|website=EarMilk.com|access-date=19 December 2017}}</ref>

== Debut album ==
In 2017, Flyte flew to Australia to work with [[Courtney Barnett]]'s producer Burke Reid on their debut album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/flyte-announce-debut-album-plans|title=Flyte Announce Debut Album Plans|website=ClashMusic.com}}</ref> Focusing on their live performances and the band's four voices around one microphone, they took their production cues from [[David Bowie]], [[Nick Drake]], [[The Beatles]] and [[Lou Reed]]. The album ''The Loved Ones'' was released on 25 August 2017.

== Live performances ==
Flyte have toured the UK and Europe extensively and supported the likes of [[The Lemon Twigs]] and [[Bombay Bicycle Club]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.seetickets.com/tour/the-lemon-twigs|title=The Lemon Twigs Tickets - See Tickets|website=Seetickets.com|access-date=19 December 2017}}</ref>

They have also performed at [[Jamie Oliver]] and [[Alex James (musician)|Alex James]]' 'The Big Feastival' in 2015. They have also performed at Boardmasters 2018 in Cornwall after having toured in the US.

On 11 November 2019, Flyte played live at the Village Underground in Shoreditch, London. They were supported by Matt Maltese. In attendance were Paul Sheehan and Adam Heley.
