'''Rayland Baxter''', stylized as '''rayLand baxter''', is an American [[alternative country]] musician from [[Nashville]], [[Tennessee]]. He is currently signed to [[ATO Records]]. Baxter is 6-feet 5-inches and is also the son of musician [[Bucky Baxter]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Schlansky|first1=Evan|title=Rayland Baxter|url=http://www.americansongwriter.com/2013/09/rayland-baxter/|publisher=American Songwriter|accessdate=February 26, 2015}}</ref>

==Early life==
Baxter was a very good high school lacrosse player. He was a midfielder and attended the Severn School in Maryland and later Suffield Academy (Conn.).<ref>{{Cite web|last=McMullen|first=Paul|title=Coaching changes affect recruiting|url=https://www.baltimoresun.com/news/bs-xpm-2001-11-30-0111300136-story.html|access-date=2020-10-13|website=baltimoresun.com|language=en-US}}</ref> He was recruited and played Division I Lacrosse at Loyola University Maryland before a knee injury sidelined him and ended his playing career.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ledger|first=Jay N. MillerFor The Patriot|title=MUSIC SCENE: A knee injury led Rayland Baxter to a career in music|url=https://www.patriotledger.com/article/20160204/entertainment/160207595|access-date=2020-10-13|website=The Patriot Ledger, Quincy, MA|language=en}}</ref> He was expelled from Loyola his Sophomore year for getting into a fight on campus.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dukoff|first=Spencer|date=|title=The Eternal Sunshine Of Rayland Baxter|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/spencerdukoff/2019/01/28/rayland-baxter-wide-awake-eternal-sunshine-interview/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-10-13|website=Forbes|language=en}}</ref> Remaining in Baltimore, he bartended at Jerry's Belvedere before relocating to Colorado as a snowboard instructor at Breckenridge. There he began playing open mics at Gold Pan Saloon, starting his music career before eventually moving back home to Nashville to pursue music full time.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dukoff|first=Spencer|title=The Eternal Sunshine Of Rayland Baxter|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/spencerdukoff/2019/01/28/rayland-baxter-wide-awake-eternal-sunshine-interview/|access-date=2020-10-13|website=Forbes|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
Baxter began performing in 2010, when he was featured on the song Shanghai Cigarettes by country musician [[Caitlin Rose]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=LeMay|first1=Joseph|title=Video: Caitlin Rose, "Shanghai Cigarettes"|url=http://www.americansongwriter.com/2010/09/video-caitlin-rose-shanghai-cigarettes/|publisher=American Songwriter|accessdate=February 26, 2015}}</ref> In 2012, Baxter released his debut full-length album, titled Feathers & Fishhooks (stylized as feathers & fishHooks), via [[ATO Records]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bort|first1=Ryan|title=Album Stream: Rayland Baxter – feathers & fishHooks|url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/blogs/av/2012/08/video-premiere-rayland-baxter---driveway-melody-1.html|publisher=Paste Magazine}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Oloizia|first1=Jeff|title=Rayland Baxter is Becoming|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/rayland-baxter-feathers-and-fishhooks|publisher=Interview Magazine|accessdate=February 26, 2015}}</ref> In 2013, Baxter released his first extended play, titled Ashkelon (stylized as ashkeLON) also via [[ATO Records]]. The title is named after the town [[Ashkelon]] in Israel.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Wilkins|first1=Joshua|title=Song Premiere: Rayland Baxter – "bad things"|url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/blogs/av/2013/06/song-premiere-rayland-baxter---bad-things.html|publisher=Paste Magazine|accessdate=February 26, 2015}}</ref> On August 14, 2015, Baxter released his second studio album titled ''Imaginary Man''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Powers|first1=Ann|title=Songs We Love: Rayland Baxter, 'Yellow Eyes'|url=https://www.npr.org/2015/05/19/407749567/songs-we-love-rayland-baxter-yellow-eyes|publisher=NPR|accessdate=11 June 2015}}</ref> In 2018, Baxter released his third full-length album titled ''Wide Awake''.<ref name="popmatters">{{cite web |last1=Finley |first1=Adam |title=On 'Wide Awake', Rayland Baxter Makes a Masterful Play for the Pop-rock Middle |url=https://www.popmatters.com/rayland-baxter-wide-awake-2592963436.html |publisher=[[PopMatters]] |accessdate=5 October 2018}}</ref> In 2019, Baxter released ''Good Mmornin,'' an album of seven Mac Miller cover songs. The record was released the day before he played the [[Newport Folk Festival|Newport Folk Fest]] where he debuted several of the songs live for the first time.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-07-29|title=Rayland Baxter Talks Mac Miller's Impact On Him|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/rayland-baxter-opens-about-mac-millers-impact-him-newport-folk-2019|access-date=2020-10-14|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref>

These days, Rayland is working on his fourth album.
