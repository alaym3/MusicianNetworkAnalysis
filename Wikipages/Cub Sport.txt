'''Cub Sport''' are an Australian pop group formed in [[Brisbane]] in early 2010. Founding mainstays are Zoe Davis on keyboards, bass guitar, lead guitar and vocals, Tim Nelson on lead vocals and keyboards, Sam "Bolan" Netterfield on keyboards and vocals, and Dan Puusaari on drums. They have released four studio albums, ''[[This Is Our Vice]]'' (March 2016), ''[[Bats (album)|Bats]]'' (September 2017), ''[[Cub Sport (album)|Cub Sport]]'' (January 2019) and ''[[Like Nirvana]]'' (July 2020). ''Like Nirvana'' is their highest charting release, peaking at No.&nbsp;2 on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Albums Chart]]. Nelson and Netterfield married in August 2018.

==History==

===2010–2013: Early years===

Cub Sport were originally formed as '''Tim Nelson & the Cub Scouts''' early in 2010 in Brisbane to promote Nelson's solo songs.<ref name="unearthed">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/156007/20190618-0049/www.triplejunearthed.com/artist/cub-sport.html | title=Cub Sport | publisher=[[Triple J|Triple J Unearthed]] | year=2013 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20190617161151/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/156007/20190618-0049/www.triplejunearthed.com/artist/cub-sport.html | archive-date=18 June 2019 | access-date=30 October 2021 | url-status=dead }}</ref> The original six-piece line-up,<ref name="Freeman">{{cite web | url = https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8494673/cub-sport-interview-artist-of-the-month | title = Cub Sport New Album Interview: Coming Out, Getting Married and Being a 'New Type of Pop Star' |  last = Freeman | first = Ben | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date = 24 January 2019 | access-date = November 1, 2021 }}</ref> was Zoe Davis on vocals and bass guitar, Tim Nelson on lead vocals and keyboards, Sam Netterfield on vocals and keyboards, Dan Puusaari on drums, Bek Stoodley on backing vocals,{{citation needed|date=October 2021}} and Andrew Williams on lead guitar. Davis, Nelson, Netterfield and Williams had attended the same secondary school, [[Northside Christian College]].<ref name="Donelson"/><ref name="Northside">{{cite web | archive-url = https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20140211230444/http://www.northside.qld.edu.au/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/NorthsiderAutumn2012.pdf | url = http://www.northside.qld.edu.au/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/NorthsiderAutumn2012.pdf | title = Northside Talent ''Unearthed'' | work = Northsider | archive-date = 12 February 2014 | date = Autumn 2012 | access-date = 30 October 2021 | page = 13 }}</ref><ref name="Bruce">{{cite web | url = https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/a-literal-homophobic-hellscape-cub-sport-call-out-their-high-school/ | title = 'A literal homophobic hellscape': Cub Sport call out their high school | last = Bruce | first = Jasper | work = The Brag | date = 1 March 2020 | access-date = 30 October 2021 }}</ref>

Tim Nelson & the Cub Scouts released a self-titled debut extended play (EP) in August 2010.<ref name="Roberts"/><ref name="Griffiths">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/63544/20110107-0005/www.mediasearch.com.au/music/cdreviews/timnelsonandthecubscouts-ep.html | title=Tim Nelson & the Cub Scouts | last=Griffiths | first=Dave | editor=Carmine Pascuzzi | website=Mediasearch | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20110109164331/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/63544/20110107-0005/www.mediasearch.com.au/music/cdreviews/timnelsonandthecubscouts-ep.html | archive-date=10 January 2011 | access-date=30 October 2021 }}</ref> ''Mediasearch''{{'}}s Dave Griffiths noticed, "Nelson's amazing voice that sets this group apart from the many other bands... Sweet and blissful this is for those who love the music of Decoder Ring or george."<ref name="Griffiths"/> Adam Roberts of ''Alt Media'' felt, "as for hooks and chops among the quiet indie set, [Nelson] has all the right ingredients to make something bigger and better."<ref name="Roberts"/> Tiffany Bridger of ''Rave Magazine'' praised "Their rainbow of soft, whispery tunes – a fluttering on the glockenspiel, a series of Nelson's emotive keys throughout – make for a magical instrumental arrangement."<ref name="Bridger">{{cite web | url=http://www.ravemagazine.com.au/content/view/21583/181/ | title=Tim Nelwon & the Cub Scouts – ''Tim Nelson & The Cub Scouts'' EP | last=Bridger | first=Tiffany | website=Rave Magazine | date=6 July 2010 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20100710021814/http://www.ravemagazine.com.au/content/view/21583/181/ | archive-date=10 July 2010 | access-date=30 October 2021 }}</ref>

Following a line-up change, they shortened their name to '''Cub Scouts''' in 2011. They issued two singles — "Evie" in 2011 and "[[Do You Hear]]" in 2012; both received high rotation on national youth radio, [[Triple J]].<ref name="Rico"/> They released their six-track second EP, ''Told You So'' in October 2012.<ref name="Lee">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/10761/20130218-0713/themusic.com.au/reviews/album/2012/10/26/cub-scouts-told-you-so-mat-lee/index.html | title=Cub Scouts - Told You So ♫ Album reviews | last=Lee | first=Mat | website=theMusic.com.au | date=26 October 2012 | via=[[National Library of Australia]] | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20130219105737/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/10761/20130218-0713/themusic.com.au/reviews/album/2012/10/26/cub-scouts-told-you-so-mat-lee/index.html | archive-date=19 February 2013 | access-date=30 October 2021 }}</ref> ''theMusic.com.au''{{'}}s Mat Lee observed, "[its] six songs are individual in their own right and the pace is effectively contrasted to highlight the shifts in context and emotion."<ref name="Lee"/> They supported the EP's release with their second Australian tour.<ref name="Rico">{{cite web | url=http://www.couriermail.com.au/questnews/fast-track-to-success-stuns-band/story-fn8ygho7-1226307034497 | title=Fast-track to success stuns band | last=Rico | first = Charley | website= The Courier-Mail | date=22 March 2012 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20120328170932/http://www.couriermail.com.au/questnews/fast-track-to-success-stuns-band/story-fn8ygho7-1226307034497 | archive-date=29 March 2012 | access-date=30 October 2021 }}</ref> The title track gained popularity on US college radio.<ref name="unearthed" /><ref name="Donelson">{{cite web | url = https://www.allmusic.com/artist/cub-sport-mn0003439044/biography?1635577784461 | title = Cub Sport Biography, Songs, & Albums | last = Donelson | first = Marcy | publisher = [[AllMusic]] | date = | access-date = 30 October 2021 }}</ref> Another track, "Do You Hear" won two awards at the 2012 [[Queensland Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.queenslandmusicawards.com.au/past-winners/2012|title=Past Winners 2012|website=Queensland Music Awards|access-date=25 March 2021}}</ref>

The band officially changed their name to Cub Sport in August 2013 following a legal injunction by [[Scouts Australia]].<ref name="scouts">{{cite web |title=Cub Scouts changing name after legal dispute |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130827182121/http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s3833548.htm |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s3833548.htm |publisher=Triple J (Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC)) |archive-date=27 August 2013 |access-date=30 October 2021 |date=26 August 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="Hall">{{cite web | url = https://issuu.com/spa_magazines/docs/themusic_wa_05 | title = The Music (Perth) Issue #5 by TheMusic.com.au - issuu | last = Hall | first = Carley | work = The Music | location = [[Perth]], WA | date = 10 September 2013 | access-date = 30 October 2021 | issue = 5 | page = 26 }}</ref> Nelson explained, "We got a cease and desist letter about a month ago asking us to stop using the name."<ref name="scouts"/> Also in August, they released their five-track third EP, ''Paradise'', which featured two singles: "Pool!" and the title track. On "Pool!" lead vocals were shared by Davis and Nelson.<ref name="Pool!">{{cite web | url=http://www.pressrecord.com.au/cub-scouts-release-new-video-for-pool-announce-east-coast-shows/ | title=Cub Scouts Release New Video for 'Pool!', Announce East Coast Shows | website=Press Record | date=March 2013 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20130419184055/http://www.pressrecord.com.au/cub-scouts-release-new-video-for-pool-announce-east-coast-shows/ | archive-date=20 April 2013 | access-date=31 October 2021 }}</ref> Initial pressings of ''Paradise'' still had the Cub Scouts band name, "it's annoying that [the injunction] came up in the middle of us releasing an EP, ''after'' we got 1500 copies" pressed.<ref name="Hall"/> Cub Sport undertook a tour of United States and Europe during 2013.<ref name="Wall">{{cite web | url=http://westender.com.au/holy-holy-cub-sport-playing-brisbane-week/ | title=HOLY HOLY and Cub Sport playing in Brisbane the same week | last=Wall | first=Jimmy | website=Westender | date=12 November 2013 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20180513062726/http://westender.com.au/holy-holy-cub-sport-playing-brisbane-week/ | archive-date=13 May 2018 | access-date=30 October 2021 }}</ref>

===2014–2018: ''This Is Our Vice'' and ''Bats''===

{{main|This Is Our Vice|Bats (Cub Sport album)}}
Lead guitarist Andrew Williams left Cub Sport in 2014 but was not replaced, they continued as a four-piece, with both Nelson and Davis shifting to additional guitar duties. In October 2015 they released the first single, "Only Friend",<ref name="AMG Only Friend">{{cite web | url = https://www.allmusic.com/album/only-friend-mw0002884014 | title = 'Only Friend' – Cub Sport {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits | publisher = AllMusic | access-date = 30 October 2021 }}</ref> from their debut album, ''[[This Is Our Vice]]'', which appeared on 4 March 2016.<ref name="jbvice">{{cite web|title=Cub Sport - This Is Our Vice|url=https://www.jbhifi.com.au/music/browse/alternative-indie-pop/this-is-our-vice/886893/|publisher=[[JB Hifi]]|accessdate=9 March 2016|date=November 2015}}</ref> It was produced by John Castle ([[Megan Washington]], [[Vance Joy]]) for [[Nettwerk Records]].<ref name="Yeung"/><ref name="theMusic Nettwerk">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/10761/20160229-0938/themusic.com.au/news/all/2015/10/09/cub-sport-pen-global-deal-with-nettwerk-music/index.html | title=Cub Sport Pen Global Deal with Nettwerk Music | author=staff writer | website=theMusic.com.au | date=9 October 2015 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20160229212810/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/10761/20160229-0938/themusic.com.au/news/all/2015/10/09/cub-sport-pen-global-deal-with-nettwerk-music/index.html | archive-date=1 March 2016 | access-date=30 October 2021 }}</ref> Jaymz Clements of [[Rolling Stone Australia|''Rolling Stone'' (Australia)]] rated it at two stars and explained, "they fail to fire. It's fine for ''This Is Our Vice'' to slew more towards self-reflective, downer territory, but that it relies so heavily on well-tread synth-pop is where it lets itself down."<ref name="Clements">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/159038/20160802-0947/rollingstoneaus.com/reviews/post/cub-sport-this-is-our-vice/3369.html | title=Album Review: Cub Sport – ''This Is Our Vice'' | last=Clements | first=Jaymz | website=[[Rolling Stone Australia]] | date=14 March 2016 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20160802093031/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/159038/20160802-0947/rollingstoneaus.com/reviews/post/cub-sport-this-is-our-vice/3369.html | archive-date=2 August 2016 | access-date=30 October 2021 }}</ref> Whereas [[AllMusic]]'s Neil Z. Yeung gave it three-and-a-half stars and detailed, "With more electronic flourishes and a funkier vibe than their past efforts, the album shimmers, injecting tropical dance elements into its indie rock backbone."<ref name="Yeung">{{cite web | url = https://www.allmusic.com/album/this-is-our-vice-mw0002912612 | title = ''This Is Our Vice'' – Cub Sport {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits | last = Yeung | first = Neil Z. | publisher = AllMusic | access-date = 30 October 2021 }}</ref>
[[File:Cub Sport in Melbourne.jpg|thumb|Cub Sport performing in Melbourne in 2016.]]

In August 2016, they performed a cover of Kanye West's "[[Ultralight Beam]]" on Triple J's programme, ''[[Like a Version]]''.<ref name="ultralight">{{cite web|title=Breakfast with Alex and Matt|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/breakfast/blog/s4518283.htm|publisher=Triple J (Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC)) |access-date=5 September 2016}}</ref> The third single, "[[Come On Mess Me Up]]", was listed at number&nbsp;24 on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2016|2016 Triple J Hottest 100]],<ref name="hottest100">{{cite web|title=2016 Triple J Hottest 100|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/16/|publisher=Triple J (Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC)) |accessdate=10 March 2017}}</ref> their first ever appearance in the annual listeners poll. Cub Sport toured nationally and internationally in support of ''This Is Our Vice'', both as head-liners and support act to [[Ball Park Music]],<ref name="unearthed" /> [[Loon Lake (band)|Loon Lake]],<ref name="unearthed" /> [[Andy Bull]],<ref>{{cite web|title=ANDY BULL AND CUB SPORT ARE TOURING TOGETHER|url=https://www.projectu.tv/andy-bull-and-cub-sport-are-touring-together/|publisher=Project U|accessdate=9 March 2017}}</ref> Saskwatch,<ref>{{cite web|title=LIVE REVIEW: SASKWATCH W/ CUB SPORT @ THE TRIFFID|url=http://aaabackstage.com/live-review-saskwatch-the-triffid/|publisher=AAA Backstage|accessdate=9 March 2017}}</ref> [[the 1975]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Live Review: The 1975 + Cub Sport – Hisense Arena, Melbourne (24.07.16)|url=http://music.theaureview.com/reviews/the-1975-cub-sport-hisense-arena-melbourne-24-07-17/|publisher=The AU Review|accessdate=15 March 2017}}{{Dead link|date=July 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> and [[Big Scary]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Big Scary touring 'Animal' for the last time with Cub Sport in tow|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4636247.htm/|publisher=Triple J (Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC)) |accessdate=15 March 2017}}</ref> ''This Is Our Vice'' was re-released in April 2021, with two bonus tracks, and peaked in the top&nbsp;60 on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Albums Chart]].<ref name="2021rerelease">{{cite web|url=http://cubsport.aracastores.com/index.php/cub-sport-this-is-our-vice-ltd-etd-transparent-red-vinyl.html|title=''This Is Our Vice'' Transparent Red Vinyl|website=Cub Sport|access-date=10 April 2021}}</ref><ref name=AUS/>

In June 2017 Cub Sport released the first single, "O Lord", from their second album, ''[[Bats (album)|Bats]]''. The [[Gospel music|gospel]]-influenced "O Lord", was written about Nelson's experiences after [[coming out]] and his fear of losing his relationship with Netterfield.<ref name="olord">{{cite web|title=Cub Sport |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4692629.htm |publisher=Triple J (Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC)) |access-date=1 July 2017 }}</ref> It was released on 22 September 2017 via [[MGM Distribution]], with Castle and Nelson co-producing, which reached the ARIA Albums Chart top&nbsp;100.<ref name=AUS/> ''Bats'' provided two further singles "[[Chasin']]" (August 2017) and "Good Guys Go" (January 2018). Charlotte Saxon of ''Music Insight'' caught their performance in Perth in February 2018, "[they are] not afraid to share their most intimate personal stories with fans as they experiment with genres from RnB to pop to gospel. Although their songs are gut wrenching, they are beautiful none the less."<ref name="Saxon">{{cite web | url=https://www.musicinsight.com.au/reviews/review-cub-sport-jack-rabbit-slims-perth-23-february-2018/ | title=Review: Cub Sport at Jack Rabbit Slims, Perth, 23 February 2018 | last=Saxon | first=Charlotte | website=Music Insight | date=26 February 2018 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20180404203824/https://www.musicinsight.com.au/reviews/review-cub-sport-jack-rabbit-slims-perth-23-february-2018/ | archive-date=5 April 2018 | access-date=31 October 2021 }}</ref>

The band toured in support of ''Bats'', including a headlining Australian tour in March 2018, a national support for Vance Joy in September and headlined [[Aurora (singer)|Aurora Aksnes]] in October. In August 2018 Nelson and Netterfield were married after campaigning for [[same-sex marriage in Australia|legalization of same-sex marriage]].<ref name="Jenke">{{cite web | url=http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/169055/20200616-1111/tonedeaf.thebrag.com/cub-sport-tim-sam-married-weekend/index.html | title=Cub Sport's Tim Nelson and Sam Netterfield got married over the weekend | last=Jenke | first=Tyler | website=The Brag | date=13 August 2018 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20200616151944/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/169055/20200616-1111/tonedeaf.thebrag.com/cub-sport-tim-sam-married-weekend/index.html | archive-date=17 June 2020 | access-date=30 October 2021 }}</ref> A video for album track, "Hawaiian Party" was released in September 2018, which was directed and produced by the [[Dolan Twins]]. The video surpassed one million views within a week of its upload.{{citation needed|date=October 2021}}

===2019 to current: ''Cub Sport'' to ''Like Nirvana''===

{{main|Cub Sport (album)|Like Nirvana}}
In October 2018 Cub Sport issued the lead single "[[Sometimes (Cub Sport song)|Sometimes]]", from their third studio album, ''[[Cub Sport (album)|Cub Sport]]''. In the following month they were inaugural winners of the ARIA Emerging Artist Scholarship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ariaawards.com.au/News/2018/Nominations-for-the-2018-ARIA-Emerging-Artist-Scho|title=Nominations for the 2018 ARIA Emerging Artist Scholarship, Presented by Virgin Australia, Are Now Open|publisher=ARIA Awards|date=5 November 2018|access-date=26 January 2019}}</ref> ''Cub Sport'' appeared on 18 January 2019 via the band's own imprint, Cub Sport Records and was distributed by MGM, again.<ref name=AUS/> It peaked at number&nbsp;12 on the ARIA Albums Chart.<ref name=AUS/> Also in that month ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' initiated Billboard Pride to celebrate an [[LGBT|LGBTQ]] act, the inaugural Artist of the Month was Cub Sport.<ref name="Freeman"/> During their Australian tour in April, a live review said that Nelson "is literally like Brisbane's [[Justin Bieber]] but more indie and more gay"{{citation needed|date=October 2021}}.

Cub Sport released a duet single, "[[I Never Cried So Much in My Whole Life]]" (October 2019) featuring [[Darren Hayes]].<ref name="16Oct19"/> An article in ''[[The Courier-Mail]]'' praised the collaboration and referenced Nelson and Netterfield's alma mater; the band were prompted to tweet, "I'd just like to specify that Northside Christian College is a literal homophobic hellscape."<ref name="Bruce"/> In December they issued their fourth EP ''333'', which provided three singles released weekly over the month.<ref name="EP333"/> Nelson explained, "''333'' is made up of three songs that fit together to conjure a feeling of deep and healing euphoria."<ref name="EP333">{{cite web|url=https://www.auspop.com.au/2019/12/triple-treat-for-cub-sport-fans/|title=Triple Treat for Cub Sport Fans |website=auspop |date=13 December 2019 |accessdate=14 December 2019 }}</ref>

''[[Like Nirvana]]'', released on 24 July 2020, was Cub Sport's fourth studio album.<ref name="AuspOp Mar 2020">{{cite web |url=https://www.auspop.com.au/2020/03/cub-sport-drive-forward/ |title= Cub Sport Drive Forward |website=auspOp |date=27 March 2020 |access-date=28 March 2020 }}</ref> Annabel Ross of the Australian edition of ''[[NME]]'' observed that on their "most stunning LP yet" Nelson describes how "liberation does not deliver permanent bliss, and that self-acceptance is a journey without a final destination."<ref name="Ross">{{cite web | url = https://www.nme.com/en_au/reviews/album/cub-sport-like-nirvana-review-2712598 | title = Cub Sport – ''Like Nirvana'' Album Review | last = Ross | first = Annabel | work = [[NME]] | date = 22 July 2020 | access-date = 31 October 2021 }}</ref> Its lead single, "Confessions", had appeared in March.<ref name="Nirvana">{{cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/cub-sport-tim-nelson-gender-free-come-out-confessions-song/12023670|title= Cub Sport's Tim Nelson comes out as gender 'free' ahead of new album and tour|website=ABC|date=4 March 2020|accessdate=7 March 2020}}</ref> ''Like Nirvana'' peaked at number&nbsp;2 on the ARIA Albums Chart – their highest placing.<ref name=AUS/> In October they covered Powderfinger's "[[These Days (Powderfinger song)|These Days]]" at the [[2020 AFL Grand Final]], which was held in Brisbane.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.smh.com.au/culture/music/all-aussie-music-lineup-announced-for-afl-s-grand-final-at-the-gabba-20201012-p564c7.html |title=All-Aussie music lineup announced for AFL's grand final at
