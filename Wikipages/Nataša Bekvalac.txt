'''Nataša Bekvalac''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Наташа Беквалац}}, {{IPA-sr|nǎtaʃa bekʋǎːlats|pron}}, born 25 September 1980) is a Serbian singer, model and actress from [[Novi Sad]].

==Life and career==
The daughter of the former [[Association football|footballer]] and coach, [[Dragoljub Bekvalac]], Nataša was born on September 25, 1980 in [[Novi Sad]] and grew up as a middle child alongside two sisters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.story.rs/celebrity-news/vesti/71451/koja-ste-sestra-bekvalac-natasa-objavila-fotku-sa-kristinom-i-draganom-ali-ovakve-reakcije-nije-ocekivala-foto|title =Koja ste sestra Bekvalac: Nataša objavila sliku sa Kristinom i Draganom|date=March 25, 2019|access-date= December 21, 2020|website=story.rs|language=sr}}</ref> She gained public attention from an early age performing at children's music festivals as well as for appearing on the popular variety show hosted by [[Minja Subota]], called ''Mužički tobogan'', in 1990.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nova.rs/zabava/showbiz/leontina-natasa-bekvalac-i-dzenan-kao-klinci-u-muzickom-toboganu-minje-subote/|title=Leontina, Nataša Bekvalac i Dženan kao klinci u Muzičkom toboganu Minje Subote|date=September 17, 2021|accessdate=November 8, 2021|author=Jovović, P.|website=Nova.rs|language=sr}}</ref> After graduating from a senior coaching school she decided pursue a music career by releasing her debut album ''Ne brini'' in 2001 under [[City Records]]. Regarded as a [[pop icon]], Bekvalac has become one of the most prominent Serbian singers with five studio albums and numerous hit-songs, including "Mali signali", "Nikotin", "Kraljica novih ljubavi", "Original" and "Mala plava". On [[Valentine's Day]] 2020, she held a solo concert at the [[Belgrade Arena]], titled ''S.O.S. za ljubav'' (''[[SOS]] for Love'').<ref>{{cite web|url=https://story.rs/flash-news/vesti/natasa-bekvalac-priredila-koncert-za-pamcenje/|title=Nema joj ravne: Nataša Bekvalac priredila koncert za pamćenje!|date=February 15, 2020|accessdate=November 8, 2021|website=story.rs|language=sr}}</ref>

Beyond music, Bekvalac has also made several cameos on television and had her theatre debut in 2016 as Queen [[Draga Mašin]] in the [[Musical theatre|musical]] ''Tajna Crne ruke'' (''The Secret of [[Black Hand (Serbia)|Black Hand]]'').<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mondo.rs/a946414/Zabava/Zvezde-i-tracevi/Natasa-Bekvalac-Tajna-Crne-ruke-premijera.html|title=Nataša Bekvalac Tajna Crne ruke, premijera|date=October 10, 2016|accessdate=November 8, 2021|website=Mondo.rs|language=sr}}</ref> Furthermore, in 2018 she served as a judge on the children's singing show ''Pinkove zvezdice'', replacing singer [[Jelena Tomašević]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tracara.com/clanovi-zirija-pinkovih-zvezdica-napustaju-takmicenje-zbog-kije-kockar/|title=Članovi žirija Pinkovih Zvezdica napuštaju takmičenje zbog Kije|website=Tracara.rs|date=September 22, 2018|accessdate=November 8, 2021|author=Aleksovski|language=sr}}</ref> 

Bekvalac also been known for supporting [[LGBT rights in Serbia|LGBT rights]] through her public appearances and performances, thus was honored with the title of a [[gay icon]] in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novosti.rs/vesti/scena.147.html:827649-Natasa-Bekvalac-dobila-titulu-Gej-ikona-2019-godine-I-dalje-se-borim-za-ljubav|title=Nataša Bekvalac dobila titulu Gej ikone 2019 godine: I dalje se borim za ljubav|website=[[Večernje  Novosti|Novosti.rs]]|date=November 1, 2019|accessdate=November 8, 2021|language=sr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://nova.rs/zabava/kako-je-natasa-bekvalac-postala-gej-ikona/|title=Kako je Nataša Bekvalac postala gej ikona|date=September 20, 2021|accessdate=November 8, 2021|author=Jovović, P.|website=Nova.rs|language=sr}}</ref> Moreover, she served as the "godmother" of the 2021 Belgrade Pride, where she stated that she is not an alley to the [[LGBT community|community]], but a "part of it".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-09-18|title=Nataša Bekvalac: Nisam na Prajdu kao podrška LGBT+ populaciji, već kao deo nje|url=https://rs.n1info.com/vesti/natasa-bekvalac-nisam-na-prajdu-kao-podrska-lgbt-populaciji-vec-kao-deo-nje/|access-date=2021-09-20|website=N1|language=sr-RS}}</ref>

Bekvalac has also received widespread media attention because of her personal life. Between 2006 and 2011, she was married to Serbian [[waterpolo]] player [[Danilo Ikodinović]], with whom she had her first daughter in 2007. Her second marriage was to [[handball]] player Ljubomir Jovanović, from 2015 till 2017. Bekvalac subsequently got married to [[information technology|IT]] entrepreneur Luka Lazukić, giving birth to her second daughter in 2018. However, she eventually filled for a divorce after getting physically assaulted by her then husband, just a month after she had given birth.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tracara.com/pretucena-natasa-bekvalac-luka-lazukic-nasilje/|title=Pretučena Nataša Bekvalas!|publisher=Tracara|language=sr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://tracara.com/natasa-bekvalac-zvanicno-razvedena-od-luke-lazukica/|title=Nataša Bekvalac zvanično razvedena od Luke Lazukića|publisher=Tracara|language=sr}}</ref>
