'''Darin Zanyar''' (born 2 June 1987), known simply as '''Darin''', is a Kurdish-Swedish singer-songwriter born in [[Stockholm]]. He is one of Sweden's best-selling artists, with seven number one albums.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/article3370505.ab?service=print |title=Darin om fortkörningen, tjejer, partyliv & nya skivan – och tiden efter Idol &#124; Nöjesbladet &#124; Aftonbladet |publisher=Aftonbladet.se |date=2 September 2008|access-date=13 August 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121014005202/http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/article3370505.ab?service=print |archive-date=14 October 2012 |url-status=live}}</ref>

==Life and career==

===Early life===
Darin was born in [[Stockholm]], Sweden, to [[Kurds|Kurdish]] parents Shwan and Ashti, who are from Iraqi Kurdistan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.expressen.se/noje/tv/darin-mina-foraldrar-flydde-fran-saddam/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180218152053/https://www.expressen.se/noje/tv/darin-mina-foraldrar-flydde-fran-saddam/|url-status=dead|title=Darin: Mina föräldrar flydde från Saddam|archive-date=18 February 2018|website=Expressen|access-date=3 August 2020}}</ref> He grew up in a musical family and began singing at an early age, citing [[Michael Jackson]], [[Stevie Wonder]] and [[Whitney Houston]] as some of his biggest inspirations. He started writing and recording songs at the age of 14 after being discovered by a music producer in school after a performance.

=== 2004–2005: Breakthrough in ''Idol'', "The Anthem" and "Darin" ===
Darin became nationally famous in 2004 when he participated in the [[Idol 2004 (Sweden)|first season]] of the [[Idol (Swedish TV series)|Swedish Idol]] at the age of 16. He quickly became one of the favorite contestants in the show and was the only one to receive 78% of the public votes at the semi-final. He ended up as the runner-up and was signed by SonyBMG the day after the finale.
In February 2005 his debut album ''[[The Anthem (Darin album)|The Anthem]]'' was released. The album entering the [[Sverigetopplistan|Swedish Album Charts]] at number 1 and was certified gold by the [[IFPI]]. His first single "[[Money for Nothing (Darin song)|Money for Nothing]]" was written by [[Robyn]] and went straight to No. 1. In 2005 Darin was the most exposed person in the media in the country.<ref name="sverigesradio">{{cite web|url=http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2151&artikel=3438438|publisher=sverigesradio.se|title=Dagens gäst – Darin Zanyar – P4 Extra &#124; Sveriges Radio|access-date=9 November 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160126174334/http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2151&artikel=3438438|archive-date=26 January 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> They often wrote about his hysterical fans and referred to him as "The perfect popstar".

Later the same year Darin released his second, self-titled, album, ''[[Darin (album)|Darin]]'' which also peaked at number one.<ref name="Sophomore Success">[http://www.swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Darin&titel=Darin&cat=a Sophomore Success] {{Webarchive|url=https://www.webcitation.org/6CbrlPUha?url=http://www.swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Darin&titel=Darin&cat=a |date=2 December 2012 }} "swedishcharts". Retrieved 2 January 2010.</ref> It was his first to be released outside of Sweden, being released in [[Finland]]. ''Darin'' was certified platinum. Darin worked with record producer [[RedOne]] while creating the album, subsequently releasing the platinum selling "[[Step Up (Darin song)|Step Up]]", which spent a total of 26 weeks on [[Sverigetopplistan]] and was also the first single to be released in Finland. Furthermore, the album included the singles "Who's That Girl" and "[[Want Ya!]]".

===2006: ''Break the News''===
[[File:Tour2005darin.JPG|thumb|300px|Step Up to the Party Tour 2006]]
In 2006, Darin won Song of the Year at the Swedish Grammis for "[[Money for Nothing (Darin song)|Money for Nothing]]". Later in 2006, he released his first DVD, ''Tour Videos Interview'', which was mainly a concert DVD from a 2006 tour.

In November 2006, Darin released his third album studio album,''[[Break the News]]'', which debuted at number 1 on the Swedish Charts. The album included the singles "Perfect", "Everything But the Girl", "Desire" and "Insanity".

===2007–2009: Europe and ''Flashback''===
[[Image:Darin 2009.jpg|thumb|left|240px|Darin (2009)]]
In 2007, Darin signed a contract with [[EMI]] Germany to release music in Germany, Austria and [[Switzerland]] and in August 2007, "[[Insanity (song)|Insanity]]" was released.

In 2008, Darin started to work on his fourth studio album, ''[[Flashback (Darin album)|Flashback]],'' together with [[RedOne]]. The album was released in December 2008 and charted at number 10 in Sweden, making it Darin's first album not to reach number one in the country. The album included the singles "[[Breathing Your Love]]", a duet with American singer [[Kat DeLuna]], "Runaway" and "What If", a song which was released in collaboration with ''Friends'', a Swedish anti-bullying charity.

In 2009, Darin performed the song [[Se på mej]] at [[Melodifestivalen 2009]] as an interval act. In October 2009, Darin performed a cover of [[Coldplay]]'s "[[Viva la Vida]]" on Idol. The song was released as a single, and reached the number one on the Swedish singles chart, making it more successful than Coldplay's original in Sweden.<ref name="Gold For Darin">{{cite web|url=http://www.darin.nu/?sid=article&pid=read&id=13399 |title=Viva la Vida Goes Gold| website=www.darin.nu|date=2 July 2011|access-date=18 December 2009}}</ref>

===2010: Melodifestivalen and ''Lovekiller''===
{{Main|Lovekiller (album)}}
In November 2009, it was announced that Darin would be participating in [[Melodifestivalen 2010]],<ref name="Darin For Melodifestivalen">{{cite web|url=http://www.scandipop.co.uk/?p=1741|title=Darin to Enter Melodifestivelan| website=www.scandipop.co.uk|date=3 November 2009|access-date=3 November 2009}}</ref> Darin entered with the mid-tempo ballad "[[You're Out of My Life]]", which was written by [[Tony Nilsson]] and [[Henrik Janson]].<ref name="Darin's Royal Song">{{Cite web|url=http://svt.se/2.79613/1.1754672/darin_ar_forsta_jokern_i_melodifestivalen_2010?lid=puff_1754625&lpos=lasMer |title=Darin's The First Wild Card|website= svt.se|date=8 November 2009|access-date=9 November 2009}}</ref> Darin performed 7th in Heat 3 of the contest and progressed to the final<ref name="Darin Till Globen">{{cite web|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=9223&_t=Melodifestivalen%3A+Globen+awaits+Timoteij+and+Darin|title=Globen Awaits Darin|website=www.eurovision.tv|date=2010|access-date=4 June 2011 }}</ref> ultimately placing 4th.

In June 2010, Darin began a 17 date Summer Tour in Sweden.<ref name="Darin's Tour">{{cite web|url=http://www.darin.nu/?sid=article&pid=read&id=14171|title= Darin Sommarturné|website=www.darin.nu|date=9 June 2010| access-date=9 June 2010}}</ref>

In August 2010, Darin would soon be released the single "[[Lovekiller (song)|Lovekiller]]".<ref name="Universal Music">{{Cite web|url=http://www.universalmusic.se/blogg/nyheter/2010/6/1/darin-slapper-nytt-album|title= Darin to Release New Album|website= universalmusic.se|date= 9 June 2010|access-date=29 September 2011 }}</ref> The album ''[[Lovekiller (album)|Lovekiller]]'' was released in August 2010 and included the singles [[Viva la Vida#Darin version|Viva la Vida]], [[You're Out of My Life]], "Can't Stop Love", "[[Microphone (Darin song)|Microphone]]" and "[[Lovekiller (song)|Lovekiller]]". The album was certified gold in the first week of its release and peaked at number one on the [[Sverigetopplistan|Swedish album chart]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sverigetopplistan.se/chart/54?dspy=2010&dspp=35|title=Veckolista Album, vecka 35, 2010|publisher=[[Sverigetopplistan]]|access-date=3 April 2020}}</ref>

[[Image:Darin (Zanyar) and Jim Beanz in studio.jpg|thumb|right|240px|Darin with producer Jim Beanz working on his album ''EXIT'']]

===2012: ''Så mycket bättre'' and Chart record===
In February 2012 Darin released the single "[[Nobody Knows (Darin song)|Nobody Knows]]", which was the first single from his (then) upcoming album ''[[Exit (Darin album)|Exit]]''. In October 2012 Swedish television network [[TV4 (Sweden)|TV4]] aired the first episode of the third season of ''[[Så mycket bättre]]'' featuring Darin, a reality TV show in which each artist attempts to do their own version of another artists well-known songs, with each person getting an episode featuring a selection of their songs being performed by the other musicians. Darin performed six songs in the show: "Stockholm", "En Apa Som Liknar Dig", "I Can't Get You Off My Mind", "Astrologen", "Magdalena" and "Seven Days a Week". He broke the Swedish singles chart record and had 6 top 10 songs on [[iTunes]] at the same time.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

On 21 November 2012 [[Sony Music]] Sweden released a collection of Darin's songs from his four albums released by Sony. ''[[Det bästa av]]''.

===2013: ''Exit''===
[[Image:Cirkus2013.jpg|thumb|right|240px|Darin on tour in Stockholm in 2013]]
In January 2013, Darin released his sixth studio album, ''[[Exit (Darin album)|Exit]]'' which went straight to number one on the Swedish album chart; becaming his fifth number one album. Darin had spent time in the US working with worldwide known producers and songwriters such as [[Jim Beanz]], [[The Jackie Boyz]], [[Victoria Horn|Victoria "Lady V" Horn]] to make the album. ''Exit''.

In May 2013 a new single entitled "[[So Yours]]" was released in Europe due to a performance as the interval act during the second semi-final of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2013]] in [[Malmö]].

In mid 2013, Darin travelled to the Philippines to film the documentary ''En resa för livet'' together with SOS Children's Villages and three other artists, [[Eagle-Eye Cherry]], [[Sophie Zelmani]] and [[Uno Svenningsson]] to bring attention to the poverty in the country and also try to gain and collect as many sponsors as possible for the children in the children's villages. Darin and Eagle-Eye Cherry also wrote and sang the lead song "Dream Away" for the documentary that was aired on [[TV4 (Sweden)|TV4]]. All the revenues went to SOS Children's Villages.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

=== 2014–2016: ''Fjärilar i Magen'' and Dex Music ===
[[Image:Darin Zanyar 2014-01-18 001.jpg|thumb|right|240px|Darin (2014)]]
In 2014, Darin started writing new songs in Swedish for the first time.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}} He got inspired by recording a tribute song to the late [[Ted Gärdestad]] in the Atlantis-studio in Stockholm, where [[ABBA]] also recorded most of their albums and he wanted to record the same way they did.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

In March 2015 Darin released his first single "[[Ta mig tillbaka]]" from his (then) upcoming Swedish album ''[[Fjärilar i magen]]''. It was the first time he recorded a song with only acoustic instruments, which gave the production a different sound to what he had before. The lyrics was an autobiographical story about his childhood and growing up in the 1990s. The single went 4x platinum and was the most streamed song in Swedish that year.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

The album ''[[Fjärilar i magen]]'' was released in September 2015 by Darin's own record company Dex Music and became his sixth number one album in Sweden. In 2015, Darin was signed by [[Sony Music]] for all the Asian countries, including the Middle East for his previous English album ''Exit''.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

=== 2017–2020: ''Tvillingen'' and new singles ===
Darin continued to make music in Swedish and released his eighth studio album ''[[Tvillingen]]'', on 24 November 2017. The album peaked at number one on the Swedish Chart. Singles "Tvillingen" and" "Ja må du leva" were both certified 2x platinum.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mynewsdesk.com/se/united-stage/pressreleases/darin-slaepper-nytt-album-och-aaker-paa-vaarturne-2212626|title=Darin släpper nytt album och åker på vårturné|date=October 16, 2017|website=[[Mynewsdesk]]|url-status=live|access-date=February 28, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200227131853/https://www.mynewsdesk.com/se/united-stage/pressreleases/darin-slaepper-nytt-album-och-aaker-paa-vaarturne-2212626|archive-date=February 27, 2020}}</ref>

Darin has since then released a handful of singles: "Astronaut", "Identitetslös", "Hög", "Finns inga ord" and "[[En säng av rosor]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tv4play.se/program/nyhetsmorgon/12526657|title=Darin framför "En säng av rosor"|date=15 February 2020|website=[[TV4 (Sweden)]]|language=sv|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200228085604/https://www.tv4play.se/program/nyhetsmorgon/12526657|archive-date=February 28, 2020|access-date=28 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Veckolista Singlar, vecka 16|url=https://www.sverigetopplistan.se/chart/41?dspy=2020&dspp=16|publisher=Sverigetopplistan|access-date=9 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Veckolista Singlar, vecka 18|url=https://www.sverigetopplistan.se/chart/41?dspy=2020&dspp=18|publisher=Sverigetopplistan|format=Scroll down to number eight on the chart|access-date=9 May 2020}}</ref>

Darin has described his song "Identitetslös" as his most personal song yet and has mentioned that the song is about being lost and not knowing where you belong. He further stated that there had always been that "inner conflict" in him, referring to his history being an immigrant in Sweden.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.expressen.se/noje/darins-arliga-ord-om-sin-bakgrund/|title=Darins ärliga ord om sin bakgrund|last=Oxblod|first=Aino|date=22 November 2018|website=[[Expressen]]|language=sv|url-status=live|access-date=15 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181122212343/https://www.expressen.se/noje/darins-arliga-ord-om-sin-bakgrund/|archive-date=22 November 2018}}</ref>

=== 2021–present: ''Can't Stay Away'' and new album ===
On 28 May 2021, Darin released the single "[[Can't Stay Away (Darin song)|Can't Stay Away]]", his first single in English, since 2013.
