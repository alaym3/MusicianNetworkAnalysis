'''Ijahman Levi''' (born '''Trevor Sutherland''': 21 June 1946 in Christiana, [[Manchester, Jamaica]]) is a [[reggae]] musician. In his early years, Levi was tutored by musician and vocal teacher, [[Joe Higgs]].<ref name="Harris">Harris, Craig "[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/ijahman-mn0000645576/biography/ Artist Biography by Craig Harris]", ''[[Allmusic]]'', Retrieved 28 July 2014</ref> His first album, ''Haile I Hymn'', was released on [[Island Records]] in 1978. He became Ijahman Levi after a religious conversion to [[Rastafari]] when he was in prison between 1972 and 1974. It and his following records preach Rastafari movement as well as [[Mansions of Rastafari|Twelve Tribes of Israel]] [[doctrine]].

==Biography==
Levi moved with his parents to the [[United Kingdom]] in 1963.<ref>[http://incolor.inebraska.com/cvanpelt/ijahman.html Ijahman Levi: I & Jah]</ref> In 1966 and 1969 he released two singles under the name of '''The Youth'''. The first one was in 1966 for [[Polydor Records|Polydor]] 56121 "As Long As There is Love" c/w "Your One and Only Man", both covers of [[Jimmy Ruffin]] and [[Otis Redding]] songs. Ijahman has mentioned he is a great Otis Redding fan. They were cut in a typical mod R&B soul style. His second single was for [[Deram Records|Deram]] 226, released 17 January 1969, and featured "Meadow of My Love" c/w "Love Me or Leave Me". The B-side was self-written, being credited to 'Sutherland'. In 1985 Levi released "I Do", a duet he recorded with his second wife, Madge.
