'''Ricardo Valdez Valentine Jr.''' (born June 24, 1992), known professionally as '''6lack''' (stylized as '''6LACK,''' pronounced "black"<ref name="rs"/>), is an American singer, and songwriter.

He initially gained notable recognition following the release of his single, "[[Prblms]]", taken from his debut album, ''[[Free 6lack]]'', which was released in November 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/discover/2016/06/daily-discovery-6lack |title=Daily Discovery: 6LACK |publisher=Pigeons and Planes |access-date=November 17, 2016}}</ref> He released his second album, ''[[East Atlanta Love Letter]]'' in September 2018, which peaked at number three on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. Later that year, he released the single "[[OTW (Khalid, Ty Dolla Sign and 6lack song)|OTW]]" (with [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] and [[Ty Dolla Sign]]). 6lack was featured on the [[Normani]] single "[[Waves (Normani song)|Waves]]", which earned him a [[MTV Video Music Awards|MTV Video Music Award]]. In June 2020, he released the EP ''[[6pc Hot]]'', which included the single, "[[Know My Rights]]" (featuring [[Lil Baby]]). Later that year he was featured on the [[Gorillaz]] track "[[The Pink Phantom]]", with [[Sir Elton John]]. In 2021, his single "[[Calling My Phone]]" (featuring [[Lil Tjay]]), topped the charts in Canada, and peaked at number three in the US.

6lack is currently signed to [[Love Renaissance]] and [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/11/17/6lack-interview-rules |title=Meet 6LACK |magazine=Fader |access-date=November 18, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://djbooth.net/news/entry/2016-10-17-meet-6lack |title=LoveRenaissance's New R&B Star In The Making |publisher=DJ Booth |access-date=November 18, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7548366/6lack/biography |title=6LACK - Biography |magazine=Billboard |access-date=November 27, 2016}}</ref> He is also a member of the Atlanta-Baltimore music collective [[Spillage Village]], founded by [[EarthGang]] and [[JID]]. 6lack has received three [[Grammy Award]] nominations and has won an [[MTV Video Music Award]].

==Early life==
Ricardo Valdez Valentine Jr. was born on June 24, 1992 in [[Baltimore]], [[Maryland]], and moved to [[Atlanta]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], with his parents in 1997.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7573628/6lack-free-debut-album-interview |title=6lack Talks Industry Growing Pains, Love Songs & Moody Debut Album 'Free 6lack' |magazine=Billboard |access-date=November 18, 2016}}</ref> He attended [[Stone Mountain High School]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/everything-you-need-to-know-about-6lack-news.27901.html |title=All Access+ 6lack|date=January 25, 2017}}</ref> He is the oldest of 3 siblings. Valentine's first [[Sound recording and reproduction|recording]] experience was when he was 4 years of age, at his father's studio.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://hobmusicforward.org/news/all-access-with-6lack/#:~:text=6lack%20is%20an%20alumnus%20of,an%20exclusive%20Q%26A%20with%206lack. |title=Everything You Need To Know About 6LACK |date=October 28, 2019 }}</ref> He began rapping in middle school as a [[battle rap]]per and was involved in many battles in his youth including against rapper [[Young Thug]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url=https://www.gq.com/story/6lack-interview-breakout-atlanta-rapper-singer |title=How 6lack Went From Sleeping in a Recording Studio to Touring with The Weeknd |last=Schmelling |first=Clay Skipper,Michael |date=2017-03-30 |work=GQ |access-date=2017-09-18 |language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
===2011–2017: Beginnings and ''Free 6lack''===
[[File:6LACK.jpg|180px|left|thumbnail|6lack in January 2017]]

6lack signed a [[record deal]] with [[Flo Rida]]'s International Music Group and Strong Arm Records in July 2011. He left [[Valdosta State University]] once he signed, and spent the next few years learning about the industry. 6lack spent five years with the label, putting music out on his [[SoundCloud]] account. 6lack had little financial security and spent most of his time sleeping in the studio or outside on the street after moving to [[Miami]] to work on music with the record label.<ref name=":0" /> 6lack eventually left his label due to issues over artistic license and management.<ref name="auto">{{Cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7573628/6lack-free-debut-album-interview |title=6lack Talks Industry Growing Pains, Love Songs & Moody Debut Album 'Free 6lack' |work=Billboard |access-date=2017-09-18}}</ref> In 2015, 6lack announced that he had joined the musical collective [[Spillage Village]] after living with the members of [[EarthGang]], and appeared on four tracks of Spillage Village EP called ''Bears Like This Too'' in 2015.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kramer |first1=Kyle |title=This New Spillage Village EP Is About to Trigger a Worldwide Weed Shortage |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/64yabw/spillage-village-bears-like-this-too-ep-premiere |website=Noisey Vice}}</ref>

Once he left the label, he signed with [[Love Renaissance]] and [[Interscope Records]].<ref name="Genius">{{cite web |url=https://genius.com/a/meet-6lack-the-atlanta-artist-whose-song-prblms-is-destined-to-be-a-hit |title=Meet Rising Atlanta Artist: 6LACK |publisher=Genius |access-date=November 17, 2016}}</ref> In November 2016, ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' included 6lack in their list of "10 New Artists You Need to Know".<ref name="rs">{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-november-2016-w449519/6lack-w449628 |title=10 New Artists You Need to Know: November 2016 |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=November 17, 2016 |access-date=November 14, 2017}}</ref> He then went on to release his debut studio album, ''[[Free 6lack]]'', which peaked at number 34 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/music/6lack/chart-history/billboard-200 |title=6LACK Chart History: Billboard 200 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=September 22, 2018}}</ref> The album's single "[[Prblms]]" peaked at number 73 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart and became his first platinum record.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/6lack-sells-1-million-records-for-prblms-news.34761.html? |title=6lack Sells 1 Million Records For "Prblms" |date=July 8, 2017 |publisher=Urbanlinx Media |language=en |access-date=17 July 2017}}</ref> In April 2017, he joined Canadian singer [[The Weeknd]] as opening act for his [[Starboy: Legend of the Fall Tour|Legend of the Fall]] tour in North America.

===2018–present: ''East Atlanta Love Letter'' and ''6pc Hot''===
In April 2018, he released the single "[[OTW (Khalid, Ty Dolla Sign and 6lack song)|OTW]]" with [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] and [[Ty Dolla Sign]], which has reached at number 57 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref name=":1">{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/6lack/chart-history/hot-100 |title=6LACK Chart History: Billboard Hot 100 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=May 1, 2018}}</ref> "OTW" became 6lack's highest peaking song on the Hot 100.<ref name=":1" /> The first [[single (music)|single]] for his sophomore album, "Switch", was released on June 22, 2018 with a music video released on July 16.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hussein |first1=Wandera |title=6LACK crashes and burns in the "Switch" music video |url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/07/16/6lack-switch-music-video |website=The FADER |access-date=14 September 2018 |language=en}}</ref> "Nonchalant" was released as the album's second single on August 17, with its accompanying music video.<ref name="NonchalantHypebeast">{{cite web |last1=Montes |first1=Patrick |title=6LACK Drops New 'East Atlanta Love Letter' Single "Nonchalant" |url=https://hypebeast.com/2018/8/6lack-nonchalant-single-stream |website=HYPEBEAST |date=August 16, 2018 |access-date=14 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Ch |first1=Devin |title=6LACK's "Nonchalant" Video Is A Peaceful Refrain |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/6lacks-nonchalant-video-is-a-peaceful-refrain-new-video.44280.html |website=HotNewHipHop |date=August 17, 2018 |access-date=14 September 2018}}</ref> On September 14, 2018, 6lack released his second studio album, ''[[East Atlanta Love Letter]]'', featuring [[guest appearance]]s from [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[J. Cole]], [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]] and Khalid.  The album's release was preceded by the singles "Switch" and "Nonchalant."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/08/6lack-east-atlanta-love-letter-cover-art-release-date/ |title=6lack 'East Atlanta Love Letter' Cover Art and Release Date |work=XXL |date=August 29, 2018 |access-date=September 3, 2018}}</ref> In October 2018, 6lack started a tour for the promotion of the album, with opening acts [[THEY.]], [[Tierra Whack]], [[Boogie (rapper)|Boogie]], [[Deante' Hitchcock]], [[Summer Walker]], and [[Ari Lennox]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/6lack-announces-world-tour-featuring-tierra-whack-boogie-and-more-news.57301.html |title=6LACK Announces World Tour Featuring Tierra Whack, Boogie & More |work=HotNewHipHop |date=14 August 2018 |access-date=September 3, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.eventbrite.ca/e/48898288096?aff=efbneb |title=6LACK Presents From East Atlanta with Love Tour With Guest Deante Hitchcock |website=Eventbrite}}</ref> 6lack later featured on several other songs, such as "[[Waves (Normani song)|Waves]]" by [[Normani]].<ref>{{Citation |last=NormaniVEVO |title=Normani - Waves (feat. 6LACK) (Official Music Video) |date=2019-02-12 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x-x-eyZf8Ko |access-date=2019-03-07}}</ref> and "Crowded Room" by [[Selena Gomez]], in 2019 and early 2020, respectively.<ref>{{Citation |last=SelenaGomezVEVO |title=Selena Gomez - Crowded Room (Official Lyrics) ft. 6LACK |date=2020-01-10 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qi8TS13SlMM |access-date=2020-01-10}}</ref> 
In May 2020, 6lack released the track "ATL Freestyle". A few weeks later, on June 24, 2020, 6lack celebrated his birthday with the release of the single "Float". He also announced his EP ''[[6pc Hot]]'', which was released on June 26 and included the [[Lil Baby]]-assisted single, "[[Know My Rights]]". He also launched his own hot sauce brand 600 Degrees Original.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/6lack-celebrates-birthday-with-float-single-and-his-own-hot-sauce-brand-new-song.1987684.html?_amp |title=6LACK Celebrates Birthday With "Float" Single & His Own Hot Sauce Brand |work=HotNewHipHop |last=Marie |first=Erika |date=June 24, 2020 |access-date=June 24, 2020}}</ref>
6lack appeared on two tracks from [[Spillage Village]]'s ''[[Spilligion]]'' album, released in September 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/earthgang-reveals-spillage-villages-spilligion-tracklist-news.118073.html |title=EarthGang Reveals Spillage Village's "Spilligion" Tracklist |work=HotNewHipHop |date=September 18, 2020 |access-date=September 26, 2020}}</ref> During 2020, he collaborated with British virtual band [[Gorillaz]] and singer-songwriter [[Sir Elton John]] on the track "[[The Pink Phantom]]", for the band's seventh studio album, ''[[Song Machine, Season One: Strange Timez]]''.{{Citation needed|date=November 2021}}

In 2021, he released "[[Calling My Phone]]" with American rapper and singer [[Lil Tjay]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bloom|first=Madison|title=Lil Tjay and 6LACK Share New Song "Calling My Phone"|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/lil-tjay-and-6lack-share-new-song-calling-my-phone-listen/|access-date=2021-02-15|website=Pitchfork|date=February 12, 2021|language=en-us}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
6lack's music focuses or is based on personal and business relationships frequently, especially the topic of [[Broken heart|heartbreak]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/pgzykn/atlantas-6lack-wants-to-talk-about-real-life |title=Atlanta's 6LACK Wants to Talk About Real Life |website=Noisey |language=en-uk |access-date=2017-09-18}}</ref> 6lack has said that his songwriting is influenced mainly by his personal experiences and failed relationships.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.highsnobiety.com/2017/04/24/6lack-prblms-interview/ |title=6lack Illustrated: The Brooding Newcomer Finally Lives in Color |date=2017-09-18 |work=Highsnobiety |access-date=2017-09-18 |language=en-US}}</ref> ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine has described 6lack's music as "moody hip hop" that "spotlights vulnerability and honesty in a way the emoji generation can understand."<ref name="auto"/> 6lack has cited [[Sade (singer)|Sade]], [[T-Pain]], [[The-Dream]], and [[Usher (musician)|Usher]] as his influences.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2017/01/6lack-complex-interview-talks-new-music-past-and-more |title=How 6LACK Went From Atlanta Battle Rapper To Budding Superstar |work=Complex |date=January 24, 2017 |access-date=June 23, 2018}}</ref>
