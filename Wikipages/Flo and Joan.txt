'''Flo & Joan''' (or '''Flo and Joan''', a name they took from their grandmothers, who shared the sisterly bond the comedians present on stage) is an English [[comedy music]] act consisting of sisters, Rosie and Nicola Dempsey.<ref name="chortle2019">{{Cite web |last=Bennett |first=Steve |title=Flo & Joan: Alive On Stage : Reviews 2019 |url-status=live |url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/review/2019/08/29/44218/flo_&_joan:_alive_on_stage |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190831022338/https://www.chortle.co.uk/review/2019/08/29/44218/flo_%26_joan%3A_alive_on_stage |archive-date=31 August 2019 |access-date=2020-06-23 |website=[[Chortle]] }}</ref> The act generally consists of comedy songs.<ref>{{Cite web |last=O'Connor |date=2016-11-24 |first=Amy |title=This wonderfully sweary song perfectly captures what a load of shite 2016 has been |url=https://www.dailyedge.ie/flo-joan-2016-song-3099373-Nov2016/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170421172715/http://www.dailyedge.ie/flo-joan-2016-song-3099373-Nov2016 |archive-date=2017-04-02 |access-date=2020-06-24 |website=[[The Daily Edge]]}}</ref><ref name="chortle2019"/> Flo & Joan has been featured on [[BBC One]]'s ''[[Live at the Apollo (TV series)|Live at The Apollo]]'', the 2019's ''[[Royal Variety Performance]]''<ref>{{Cite web |last=Maxwell |first=Dominic |date=2020-01-13 |title=Victoria Wood taught us that Hobnob is funnier than biscuit |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/victoria-wood-taught-us-that-hobnob-is-funnier-than-biscuit-r889sf8r7 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live |access-date=2020-06-24 |language=en |website=[[TheTimes.co.uk]]}}</ref> and [[Richard Herring]] [[Leicester Square Theatre Podcast]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Edinburgh 2019 - Flo & Joan & John Kearns - RHLSTP with Richard Herring |url=https://www.comedy.co.uk/podcasts/richard_herring_lst_podcast/rhlstp_edinburgh_2019_21/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200623163927/https://www.comedy.co.uk/podcasts/richard_herring_lst_podcast/rhlstp_edinburgh_2019_21/ |archive-date=2020-06-23 |access-date=2020-06-23 |website=[[Comedy.co.uk]]}}</ref> In 2018, they were featured in a [[Nationwide Building Society|Nationwide]] [[building society]] television advert.<ref name="Metro">{{Cite web |date=2018-01-29 |title=Who are the Nationwide advert sisters, Flo and Joan? |url=https://metro.co.uk/2018/01/29/nationwide-advert-sisters-flo-joan-7270095/ |url-status=live |first=Jessica |last=Lindsay |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180203011320/http://metro.co.uk/2018/01/29/nationwide-advert-sisters-flo-joan-7270095 |archive-date=2018-02-03 |access-date=2020-06-23 |website=[[Metro.co.uk]] |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Nationwide Building Society on Facebook Watch |url=https://www.facebook.com/NationwideBuildingSociety/videos/a-sister-can-drive-you-around-the-bend-but-would-you-have-it-any-other-way-flo-j/10159738345305113/ |access-date=2020-06-23 |publisher=[[Nationwide Building Society]] |url-status=live |via=[[Facebook]]}}</ref> They grew up in [[Portsmouth]], lived in [[Toronto]] for a few years,<ref name="CultureCalling"/><ref name="Metro"/><ref>{{Cite web |date=2018-03-09 |title=Who are Flo and Joan? Facts about Nationwide singing advert pair you didn't know |url=https://www.ok.co.uk/celebrity-pictures/gallery/who-flo-joan-facts-nationwide-14962244 |url-status=live |access-date=2020-06-23 |website=[[OK! Magazine]]}}</ref> and {{as of|February 2019|alt=as of February 2019}}, now live in London.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Flo and Joan |date=2019-02-10 |title=Flo & Joan: 5 reasons why we love Toronto|url=https://www.toronto.com/opinion-story/9155233-flo-joan-5-reasons-why-we-love-toronto/ |access-date=2020-06-23 |website=Toronto.com}}</ref>

They released an [[Amazon Prime Video]] special, ''Alive on Stage'', based on their [[Edinburgh Fringe]] show in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Donaldson |first=Brian |title=Laugh your way through coronavirus lockdown: Try these comedies for size |url-status=live |url=https://www.thecourier.co.uk/fp/lifestyle/entertainment/1226023/laugh-your-way-through-coronavirus-lockdown-try-these-comedies-for-size/ |access-date=2020-06-23 |website=The Courier |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref name="chortle2019"/><ref>{{Cite news |last=Maxwell |first=Dominic |title=Flo & Joan review — on‑song sisters can up schtick and shine |language=en |date=2019-08-23 |url-access=subscription |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/flo-joan-review-on-song-sisters-can-up-schtick-and-shine-j8vqxvbc0 |url-status=live |access-date=2020-06-24 |issn=0140-0460 |website=[[TheTimes.co.uk]]}}</ref> Their song "Drank too much", which recounts a fictitious night drinking with a nonexistent friend Dino, has been featured on many comedy shows.
