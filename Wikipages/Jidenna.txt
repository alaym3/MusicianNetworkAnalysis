'''Jidenna Theodore Mobisson''' (born May 4, 1985), better known [[Mononymous person|mononymously]] as '''Jidenna''', is an American rapper and singer.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.pulse.ng/buzz/jidenna-why-is-us-singer-such-a-big-deal-in-nigeria-id5447865.html|title=Jidenna: Why is US singer such a big deal in Nigeria?|last=Akan|first=Joey|access-date=February 4, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2015, Jidenna released two singles, "[[Classic Man]]" and "[[Yoga (Janelle Monáe and Jidenna song)|Yoga]]", promoting [[Janelle Monáe]]'s label Wondaland Records' compilation EP ''[[Wondaland Presents: The Eephus|The Eephus]]'' with [[Epic Records]].<ref name="spin">{{cite news|last1=Brown|first1=Harley|title=Jidenna: The Classic Man With a (Semi-Secret) Plan|url=http://www.spin.com/2015/07/jidenna-classic-man-all-eyes-on/|access-date=November 9, 2015|work=[[SPIN (magazine)|SPIN]]|date=July 1, 2015}}</ref> His debut album, ''[[The Chief (album)|The Chief]]'', was released on February 17, 2017, and peaked at number 38 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2016/08/23/jidenna-long-live-the-chief-listening-party/|title=Jidenna Presents Debut Album at L.A. Listening Party|website=Rap-Up|access-date=March 18, 2017}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Jidenna Theodore Mobisson was born on May 4, 1985, in [[Wisconsin Rapids, Wisconsin]],<ref>{{cite web|last1=Forgenie|first1=Cacy|title=A Star Is Found: Jidenna Mobisson: A "Classic Man"|url=http://flavorpill.com/ntl/article/music/a-star-is-found-jidenna-mobisson-a-classic-man|website=Flavorpill|publisher=Flavorpill Media|access-date=April 21, 2015|date=March 20, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150424034044/http://flavorpill.com/ntl/article/music/a-star-is-found-jidenna-mobisson-a-classic-man|archive-date=April 24, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="billboard.com">Amaya Mendizabal, [https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6568270/tomorrows-hits-jidenna-ashley-clark-jasmine-thompson "Tomorrow's Hits: Jidenna, Ashley Clark & Jasmine Thompson"], ''Billboard'', May 15, 2015: "Born in Wisconsin, and raised for a period in Nigeria, the nomadic singer has since resided in several stateside cities and is currently based in Brooklyn."</ref> to Tama Mobisson, an accountant, and [[Oliver Mobisson]], a [[Nigeria]]n [[Igbo people|Igbo]] academic. Jidenna grew up partially in Nigeria, where his father was working as a professor of [[computer science]] at [[Enugu State University of Science and Technology|Enugu State University]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Fitzmaurice|first=Larry|date=March 17, 2017|title=Hail to the Chief: Jidenna will be performing at the University of Minnesota-Duluth on April 29th. GO DOGS! Jidenna's Incredible, Globe-Spanning Journey|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/hail-to-the-chief-jidennas-incredible-globe-spanning-journey|access-date=March 18, 2017|website=Noisey|language=en-us}}</ref> When Jidenna was six years old, the family moved back to the United States.<ref name=":1" /> In 1995, the family moved to [[Norwood, Massachusetts]], and then to [[Milton, Massachusetts]], in 2000.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Drake|first1=David |title=Bout to Blow: 10 Dope Songs You Should Be Hearing Everywhere Soon |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2015/03/bout-to-blow-best-new-songs-march/jidenna-classic-man |access-date=March 12, 2015|magazine=Complex|date=March 1, 2015}}</ref> His father died in 2010.<ref name=":1" />

In high school he became a co-founder of the rap group Black Spadez, and began producing, arranging and writing. Jidenna released his first album with Black Spadez as their final project at [[Milton Academy]], where Jidenna graduated in 2003.<ref name=":0">Murray, Nick. "[https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/magazine-feature/6836030/jidenna-classic-man-success-debut-album-style 'Classic Man' Singer Jidenna on How His Father's Death Inspired His Dandy Style: 'It Was My Way of Grieving']" January 16, 2016. ''Billboard Vol 128.1''</ref> In 2008, after graduating with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in [[Cross-cultural studies|Comparative Studies in Race and Ethnicity]] from [[Stanford University]],<ref name="People">{{cite news|url=https://www.peoplemagazine.co.za/celebrity-news/happy-birthday-jidenna/ |publisher=[[People Magazine]] |title=Happy Birthday Jidenna |date=April 5, 2016 |access-date=February 28, 2020}}</ref><ref name="CCSRE">{{cite web |url=https://ccsre.stanford.edu/20th-anniversary-fall-2016 |publisher=Stanford University Center for Comparative Studies in Race & Ethnicity |title=20th Anniversary: Fall 2016 |access-date=February 28, 2020}}</ref> he pursued his music career while working full-time as a teacher, moving between Los Angeles, [[Oakland]], [[Brooklyn]] and [[Atlanta]], before signing a deal with recording artist Janelle Monáe's Wondaland Records.<ref>{{cite news|title=Janelle Monáe's Artist Jidenna Releases New Music Video 'Classic Man'|url=http://necolebitchie.com/2015/02/janelle-monaes-artist-jidenna-releases-new-music-video-classic-man/|access-date=March 12, 2015|publisher=Necole Bithcie|date=February 23, 2015}}</ref>

== Career ==
[[File:Jidenna Seattle2018.jpg|thumb|Jidenna performing in Seattle]]

Jidenna is signed to [[Janelle Monáe]]'s Wondaland Records label and distributed through [[Epic Records]].<ref name=BillboardAsward>{{cite magazine|last1=Aswad|first1=Jem|title=Janelle Monáe Becomes a Mini-Mogul With Her Revamped Label|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/6472849/janelle-monae-mini-mogul-wondaland-label-epic-la-reid|access-date=February 13, 2015|magazine=Billboard|date=February 13, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Diaz|first1=Evelyn|title=Janelle Monáe Appears in Classic Man by Jidenna|url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2015/02/21/janelle-monae-appears-in-classic-man-by-jidenna.html|access-date=March 27, 2015|publisher=BET|date=February 21, 2015}}</ref> He has collaborated with a number of artists that are signed to this label; including [[Roman GianArthur]], [[St. Beauty]], [[Deep Cotton]] and Janelle Monáe herself, and then began recording a five-song compilation of its label's first [[extended play]] (EP), titled ''[[Wondaland Presents: The Eephus|The Eephus]]''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Janelle Monáe: I Come in Peace, But I Mean Business |url=http://www.bet.com/news/music/2015/03/26/janelle-monae-i-come-in-peace-but-i-mean-business.html|access-date=March 27, 2015|publisher=BET|date=March 26, 2015}}</ref> In February 2015, Jidenna released his first official single, called "[[Classic Man]]"<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.soulcentralmagazine.com/classic-man-feat-roman-gianarthur-single-jidenna-2/ |title=Classic Man feat Roman GianArthur |website=Soul Central Magazine |access-date=February 13, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150217082007/http://www.soulcentralmagazine.com/classic-man-feat-roman-gianarthur-single-jidenna-2/ |archive-date=February 17, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref> featuring GianArthur. The song was in heavy rotation throughout the United States,<ref>{{cite news|title=Hot New Music! Jidenna – Classic Man |url=http://www.jamn945.com/onair/maverik-2576/hot-new-music-jidenna-classic-man-13241336 |access-date=February 13, 2015 |publisher=[[WJMN (FM)|WJMN Jam'n 94.5]] |date=February 10, 2015}}</ref> and debuted at number 49 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay]] chart.<ref name="Billboard Classic Man Debut">{{cite news|last=Trust |first=Gary |title=Chart Highlights: Madonna's Ghosttown Debuts on Adult Contemporary |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6509485/chart-highlights-madonnas-ghosttown-debuts-on-adult-contemporary |access-date=March 30, 2015 |work=Billboard |date=March 23, 2015}}</ref>

On March 31, 2015, the second single from the EP was released – "[[Yoga (Janelle Monáe song)|Yoga]]" by Janelle Monáe and Jidenna.<ref name="Yoga Rolling Stone">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/janelle-monae-flows-through-new-song-yoga-20150331 |title=Janelle Monae Flows Through New Song 'Yoga' |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |first=Jon |last=Blistein |date=March 31, 2015 |access-date=April 1, 2015}}</ref> His song "Classic Man" was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap/Sung Collaboration|Best Rap/Sung Collaboration]] at the [[58th Annual Grammy Awards|58th Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/grammys/6785974/grammy-nominations-2016-full-list | title=Grammy Nominations 2016: See the Full List of Nominees | publisher=Prometheus Global Media | work=Billboard | date=December 7, 2015 | access-date=December 7, 2015}}</ref> In June of the same year, Jidenna performed the song with Monáe at the [[BET Awards 2015|BET Awards]].<ref name=":0" /> Jidenna also received an award for best new artist at the [[2015 Soul Train Music Awards]] in November.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/videos/popular/6777934/the-weeknd-uptown-funk-jidenna-2015-soul-train-awards|title=The Weeknd, 'Uptown Funk' and Jidenna Big Winners at 2015 Soul Train Awards|last=Mitchell|first=Gail|date=November 29, 2015|magazine=Billboard|access-date=August 24, 2016}}</ref>

Jidenna is a founding member of Fear & Fancy, a social club that began in California in 2006.<ref name=":1" /> The society, reminiscent of the social aid and pleasure clubs of [[New Orleans]], is an international collective of entrepreneurs, activists, educators, scientists, and artists who host soirees, dinner parties, and demonstrations.<ref name="Apunk">{{cite news|title=The Door That Opens You: Why Whippa Wiley Brings You Magic, Fear and Fancy|url=http://afropunk.com/2014/11/interview-the-door-that-opens-you-why-whippa-wiley-brings-you-magic-fear-and-fancy/|access-date=February 13, 2015|work=Afropunk|date=November 10, 2014}}</ref>

In June 2016 he released the single "Chief Don't Run".<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/hear-jidennas-confident-new-song-chief-dont-run-20160628|title=Hear Jidenna's Confident New Song 'Chief Don't Run'|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|date=June 28, 2016|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=August 24, 2016}}</ref> On February 17, 2017, Jidenna released ''[[The Chief (album)|The Chief]]'', his first studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/jidenna-announces-release-date-for-debut-album-the-chief-news.28446.html|title=Jidenna Announces Release Date For Debut Album "The Chief"|last=Feig|first=Zakk|access-date=December 18, 2017|date=February 3, 2017}}</ref> The album cover pays homage to [[Boz Scaggs]]' ''[[Middle Man (album)|Middle Man]]'' album. The lead singles "Sufi Woman" and "Tribe" were released on July 26, 2019, and will appear on his second studio album ''[[85 to Africa]]''.

In 2019 Jidenna released his second album, ''85 to Africa''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://freemusicempire.com/2019/11/13/my-favorite-album-of-2019/|title=My Favorite Album of 2019|author=Dan-O|publisher=Free Music Empire|date=November 13, 2019}}</ref>

== Style ==
Jidenna stated that his major influences include [[KRS-One]] and [[Big Daddy Kane]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7BufatLFvcA|title=Jidenna Details His Choice to Wear Tailored Suits as a Rapper|website=YouTube|publisher=VladTV|author=DJ Vlad|access-date=November 6, 2015}}</ref> as well as the Nigerian [[Highlife]] music genre.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6693282/jidenna-made-in-america-classic-man-nigerian-music|title=Jidenna Talks Classic Men and How He's Influenced by Nigerian Music|last=Weiner|first=Natalie|date=September 11, 2015|magazine=Billboard|access-date=August 24, 2016}}</ref>

Over the years, the singer developed his personal style in college, where he learned about the power of fashion from his psychology professor [[Philip Zimbardo]]. However, he would not adopt his signature [[dandy]] style until the death of his father in 2010.<ref name=":0" /> Jidenna describes his look as "heavily inspired by the [[Harlem Renaissance]] with hints of traditional [[West Africa]]n design," and a "marriage of European and African aesthetics."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://fashionbombdaily.com/fashion-bomber-of-the-day-jidenna-from-brooklyn-behind-the-scenes-of-classic-man/|title=Fashion Bomber of the Day: Jidenna from Brooklyn|date=April 17, 2015|website=Fashion Bomb Daily|publisher=The Fashion Bomb LLC|language=en-US|access-date=August 24, 2016}}</ref> Jidenna currently resides in [[East Flatbush, Brooklyn|East Flatbush]], Brooklyn.<ref name=XXLClassic>{{cite news|last1=Coleman|first1=C. Vernon|title=Watch Jidenna and Roman GianArthur's 'Classic Man' Video|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2015/02/watch-jidenna-roman-gianarthurs-classic-man-video/|access-date=March 12, 2015|publisher=XXL Mag|date=February 21, 2015}}</ref>

== Appearances in popular media ==

In September 2016, Jidenna was featured in an episode of the [[Netflix]] original series [[Luke Cage (TV series)|''Luke Cage'']]. Season 1 episode 5, "Just to Get a Rep", opens with Jidenna performing "Long Live the Chief" at the fictitious Harlem's Paradise. He performed "Long Live the Chief" and "Little Bit More" with a full band on ''[[List of The Daily Show episodes (2016)|The Daily Show with Trevor Noah]]'' in October 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/marvels-luke-cage/season-1/37806|title=Music from Marvel's Luke Cage S1E05|website=Tunefind}}</ref> Jidenna guest starred on ''[[Insecure (TV series)|Insecure]]'' in the episodes "Thirsty as Fuck," and "Shady as Fuck," which aired in November 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.avclub.com/tvclub/issa-loses-momentum-stagnant-insecure-245087|title=Issa loses momentum in a stagnant Insecure|first=Ashley|last=Ray-Harris|access-date=December 18, 2017}}</ref>

A [[chopped and screwed]] version of "[[Classic Man]]" featured in the [[Academy Award]] winning 2016 film [[Moonlight (2016 film)|''Moonlight'']].
