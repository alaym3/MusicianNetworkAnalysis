'''Yana Kedrina''' ({{lang-ru|Яна Кедрина}}; born 4 October 1990), better known by her stage name '''Kedr Livanskiy''',{{efn|{{lang-ru|Кедр Ливанский|translation=[[Cedrus libani|Lebanese cedar]]}}}} is a Russian [[electronic music]]ian, singer-songwriter, [[record producer]] and [[Disc jockey|DJ]].

==Life and career==
Kedrina was born on 4 October 1990<ref name="fader-keep-your"/> in [[Moscow]], [[Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic|Russian SFSR]].<ref name="allmusic-bio"/> She started her music career as a singer in the short-lived [[punk rock]] band Hesburger,<ref name="allmusic-bio"/><ref name="aughtmag"/> after briefly playing drums in a [[sludge metal|sludge]] group. During this time, she majored in literature at college, before enrolling to Moscow School of New Cinema.<ref name="vice-interview"/>

In the early 2010s, Kedrina and her friends started the underground [[musical collective]] Johns' Kingdom, which spawned her interest in electronic music.<ref name="vice-interview"/> After debuting her tracks under the name Kedr Livanskiy on [[SoundCloud]], she signed to New Jersey-based record label 2MR, which was co-founded by Mike Simonetti of [[Troubleman Unlimited Records|Troubleman Unlimited]] and Mike Sniper of [[Captured Tracks]]. In late 2015, Kedrina released her debut single, "Sgoraet", which was followed by her 2016 EP, ''January Sun''.<ref name="allmusic-bio"/><ref name="fader-keep-your"/> 2MR issued her debut full-length album, ''Ariadna'' in September 2017; her second studio album, [[Dub music|dub]]- and [[deep house]]-influenced ''Your Need'', was released in 2019.<ref name="allmusic-bio"/> In August 2021, she announced a new album, titled ''Liminal Soul'', due to be released on October 1, 2021.<ref name="liminal-news"/>

==Musical style and influences==
[[AllMusic]]'s Paul Simpson characterized Kedr Livanskiy's music as [[lo-fi music|lo-fi]] [[electropop|electronic pop]] and [[indie electronic]] that draws on '90s [[techno]] and [[jungle music|jungle]] influences,<ref name="allmusic-bio"/> while labeling her early songs as "wintry [[Outsider house|lo-fi house]] and jungle tracks with supremely haunting vocal melodies."<ref name="allmusic-your-need"/> Aimee Cliff of ''[[The Guardian]]'' described her style as "[[shoegaze]]-y electronic music that sounds as if it has arrived in a [[time capsule]] from a [[Retrofuturism|retro-futurist]] era."<ref name="guardian-your-need-review"/> Her debut EP, ''January Sun'', featured elements from "'90s-era [[Music of the United Kingdom (1990s)#Electronic music|UK electronic music]], dreamy [[alternative rock|alt-rock]] and [[Pop music|radio pop]]."<ref name="ra-january-sun"/> Her subsequent releases eschewed [[software synthesizer]]s in favor of analog gear, including [[Roland SH-101]] and [[Roland Juno-106]]; 2017's ''Ariadna'' was compared to the works of techno producers Patricia and Huerco S.<ref name="p4k-ariadna"/> Her second album, ''Your Need'', employed [[Roland TR-808]] as a backbone<ref name="guardian-your-need-review"/> and featured production input from Russian producer Flaty.<ref name="p4k-your-need"/> Kedr Livanskiy's lyrics are sung in Russian and English.<ref name="guardian-your-need-review"/>

Kedrina has cited [[Autechre]], [[Aphex Twin]], [[Boards of Canada]] and [[Mazzy Star]] as influences.<ref name="id-vice"/> She also noted [[MTV]] and [[1990s in music|90s musical aesthetic]] as influences on her early releases,<ref name="vice-interview"/> while her second album ''Your Need'' was driven by her love of "DJing classic [[house music|house]], [[breakbeat]], and [[UK garage|garage]],"<ref name="guardian-your-need-review"/> as well as [[Bassline (music genre)|bassline]], [[speed garage]] and [[Grime (music genre)|grime]].<ref name="daily-bandcamp"/>
