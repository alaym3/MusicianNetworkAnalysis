==Artwork==

===Album artwork===

==== Foster the People ====
In 2011, Young & Sick designed the [[album cover]] for [[Foster the People]]â€™s album ''[[Torches (album)|Torches]]''.<ref name="lost" /><ref name="ff">{{cite web|last=Smith|first=Cameron|title=Autoheart Exclusive Interview|url=http://www.femalefirst.co.uk/music/interviews/autoheart-280472.html|publisher=Female First}}</ref> The artwork was later expanded and used extensively as a backdrop in live shows, on merchandise, and on the band's website.<ref name="ff" /> The characters depicted in the artwork came to be an integral part of the band's identity.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lutheruk1.wordpress.com/2016/02/19/album-cover-analysis-foster-the-people-torches/|title=Album cover analysis-Foster the People "Torches"|date=February 19, 2016|website=lutherfilmproject|language=en|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref>

Young & Sick went on to create the visual identity for [[Foster the People]]'s 2014 second album ''[[Supermodel (album)|Supermodel]]'', which features an [[Abstract art|abstract]] [[Surrealism|surreal]] illustration of a model surrounded by a crowd of people and [[paparazzi]] armed with cameras, who take photographs as she poses on a pedestal. A mural of the artwork for the album was painted over the facade of a building located on 539 S Los Angeles Street in Los Angeles, California. It became the largest mural ever constructed on the [[West Coast of the United States|West Coast]] of the United States.<ref>{{Citation|title=Supermodel (album)|date=October 30, 2018|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Supermodel_(album)&oldid=866530791|work=Wikipedia|language=en|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.redbull.com/us-en/check-out-foster-the-peoples-new-supermodel|title=Foster The People's New 'Supermodel'|website=Red Bull|language=en|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref>

In 2018, Young & Sick designed the cover art for the [[Foster the People]] singles "[[Worst Nites]]."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bpzwv29ANZy/|title=Foster The People on Instagram: "New single "Worst Nites" out tomorrow"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref> He created the cover art for the single "Style," which was released on March 22, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BvPiQwJA303/|title=Foster The People on Instagram: "New song, who dis? ==== Maroon 5 and Robin Thicke ====

In 2012, Young & Sick drew the album cover for [[Maroon 5]]â€™s album ''[[Overexposed (album)|Overexposed]]''.<ref name="lost" /><ref name="new">{{cite web|title=Young + Sick House of Spirits|url=http://thenewblack.tumblr.com/post/30529753332/young-sick-house-of-spirits-weak-in-the-knees|publisher=The New Black|date=August 30, 2012}}</ref> [[Jimmy Fallon]] "loved" the artwork, even creating an [[Musical improvisation|improv]] song about the art entitled "kill the boogers" with [[Adam Levine]] after learning that the drawing first featured mucus coming from the nose.<ref name="jimmy">{{cite web|last=Fallon|first=Jimmy|title=Adam Levine, Part 1|url=http://www.latenightwithjimmyfallon.com/video/adam-levine-part-1-62812/1408184/|publisher=LateNight with Jimmy Fallon|date=June 28, 2012}}</ref> Young & Sick's artwork done for Maroon 5 was featured by [[Apple Inc.]] during the September 2012 launch of the [[iPhone 5]].<ref name="lost" /> Young & Sick was also retained by recording artist [[Robin Thicke]] to design the cover of Thicke's 2013 album ''[[Blurred Lines (album)|Blurred Lines]]''.<ref name="thicke" /> The album was the 4th consecutive album designed by Young & Sick to reach No. 1 on the [[Billboard Charts]].<ref name="thicke" />

===Branding===
In 2013, Young & Sick designed the branding and [[Cover art|art]]work for the [[New Orleans]] based music and [[art festival]] [[BUKU Music + Art Project|BUKU]].<ref name=buku>{{cite web|last=Shapiro|first=Benjamin|title=TRINIDAD JAME$, JAPANDROIDS, ACTION BRONSON, FLYING LOTUS AND PUBLIC ENEMY ADDED TO NEW ORLEANS' BUKU MUSIC AND ART PROJECT, NOISEY TEAMS UP FOR COVERAGE|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/trinidad-jame-japandroids-action-bronson-flying-lotus-and-public-enemy-added-to-new-orleans-buku-music-and-art-project|publisher=Noisey (Vice)}}</ref> Van Hofwegen went on to further develop his designs, expanding the festivals identity to create a Buku experience, continuing to design the branding for the festival in 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, and 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://2018.thebukuproject.com/past-lineups/|title=Past Lineups|website=BUKU Music + Art Project {{!}} March 22 + 23, 2019 {{!}} NOLA|language=EN|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref>

In June 2015, van Hofwegen was commissioned to reimagine the classic [[Pringles|Pringles can]] for an artistic campaign the brand did alongside [[Fergie (singer)|Fergie]] of the [[Black-eyed Peas|Black Eyed Peas]].<ref name=fergie>{{cite web|last1=Hamad|first1=Marwa|title=Fergie's comeback: A cornucopia of music|url=http://gulfnews.com/life-style/music/fergie-s-comeback-a-cornucopia-of-music-1.1535648|publisher=Gulf News}}</ref>

Later in 2015, [[The Fader]] and [[Coors Light]] retained van Hofwegen to create artwork for a three event concert series in LA featuring [[Kali Uchis]], [[Health (band)|HEALTH]], and [[Shlohmo]].<ref name=faderart>{{cite web|last1=MANDEL|first1=LEAH|title=Kali Uchis and Groundislava Slayed At Coors Light reFRESH L.A.|url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/08/21/kali-uchis-and-groundislava-slayed-at-coors-refresh-la|publisher=The Fader}}</ref> van Hofwegen created three unique posters for the event which were used during a six-week wheat paste campaign around Los Angeles.<ref name=faderart2>{{cite web|title=Watch HEALTH Talk About Their Moody Aesthetic With Young & Sick|url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/09/23/health-interview-young-and-sick-video|publisher=The Fader}}</ref>

In 2017, Young & Sick created the poster for [[Phoenix, Arizona|Phoenix]]-based 100% non-profit music festival M3F. In 2018, Van Hofwegen went on to design the poster and translate it into the physical branding for festival.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.m3ffest.com/|title=M3F Fest|website=M3F Fest|language=en|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref>

===Fine art===
In October 2013, [[Katz's Deli]] featured Young & Sick's artwork in the grand opening of their new art space, "The Space."<ref>{{cite web|title=Katz's Deli Is Hosting A Mouth Watering Art Show|url=http://www.complex.com/art-design/2013/10/katz-deli-gallery-the-space-art-show|publisher=Complex}}</ref> Young & Sick's piece was purchased by actor and comedian [[Jerry Stiller]], father of [[Ben Stiller]].<ref name=bedford/>

In coordination with the release of Foster the People's 2014 album ''[[Supermodel (album)|Supermodel]]'', Young & Sick was commissioned to design a mural to be painted on the side of a building in downtown LA spanning 148&nbsp;ft X 126&nbsp;ft.<ref name=rsm>{{cite web|last=EDWARDS|first=GAVIN|title=Foster the People Unveil 'Supermodel' Mural in Los Angeles|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/foster-the-people-unveil-supermodel-mural-in-los-angeles-20140124|publisher=Rolling Stone}}</ref> The mural was the largest mural constructed on the [[West Coast of the United States|West Coast]] of the United States.<ref name=mural>{{cite web|title=TIMELAPSE OF YOUNG AND SICK'S CREATING THE BIGGEST MURAL ON THE WEST COAST|url=http://www.lostateminor.com/2014/01/15/young-sicks-biggest-mural-west-coast/|publisher=Lost at E Minor}}</ref> A time lapse of the mural's creation was used as the lead music video for the band's first single off ''Supermodel'', "[[Coming of Age (Foster the People song)|Coming of Age]]".<ref name=rsm/>

Young & Sick presented his first solo gallery show at LA's FF-1051 Gallery in August 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/the-405-meets-young-sick-153|title=The 405 meets Young & Sick|last=http://thefourohfive.com|website=The 405|language=en|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref>

===Festivals===
In early 2014, it was announced Young & Sick would perform at the 2014 [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival]].<ref name=coach>{{cite web|title=Coachella 2014 Lineup Announced: OutKast, Muse & Arcade Fire Headlining|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/01/09/coachella-2014-lineup_n_4565618.html|publisher=Huffington Post}}</ref> He later performed at festivals including ACL Music Festival in Austin, Texas and Corona Capital Festival in Mexico City, Mexico.

====SXSW====
In March 2014, Young & Sick did a series of showcases at Austin's [[South by Southwest]] including a show exclusively for dogs at the Austin Pets Alive! shelter with [[Ryan Hemsworth]] and [[Mahaut Mondino]].<ref name=dogs>{{cite web|last=Schwartzberg|first=Lauren|title=Here are All of the Feelings I Felt at SXSW|url=https://thump.vice.com/en_us/article/pg8ea8/we-had-a-lot-of-feelings-at-sxsw|publisher=VICE}}</ref>

Young & Sick also showcased at other well-known events during SXSW including Fader Fort, HypeHotel, Another Planet, Noise Pop, and opened for [[Foster the People]] at the main outdoor stage for SXSW at Butler Park.<ref name="blare 2">{{cite web|last=Rupolo|first=Marie|title=INTERVIEW: YOUNG & SICK|url=http://blaremagazine.com/2014/03/27/interview-young-and-sick/|publisher=Blare Magazine}}</ref><ref name=faderfort>{{cite web|title=Video: Young & Sick, "Glass" (Live at The FADER FORT Presented by Converse)|url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/03/19/video-young-sick-glass-live-at-the-fader-fort-presented-by-converse/|publisher=The Fader}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=HOFFBERGER|first=CHASE|title=SXSW Free Butler Park Concert Series|url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/daily/music/2014-03-06/sxsw-free-butler-park-concert-series/|publisher=Austin Chronicle}}</ref>
