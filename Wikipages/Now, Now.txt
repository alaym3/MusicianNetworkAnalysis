'''Now, Now''', formerly known as '''Now, Now Every Children''', is an American [[indie rock]] duo formed in [[Blaine, Minnesota]], United States, and based in [[Minneapolis]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Kowal |first=Rachel |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=102302846 |title=Now, Now Every Children: 'Everyone You Know' |website=Npr.org |date=March 24, 2009 |accessdate=August 31, 2010}}</ref> The band is composed of Cacie Dalager (vocals, guitar, keyboard) and Bradley Hale (drums, backing vocals).

==History==
===Formation===
The band was formed in roughly 2003. Classmates Cacie Dalager and Bradley Hale met in their high school marching band when they were both sixteen years old; they eventually started writing songs together, starting with an acoustic song dedicated to a college-bound friend.<ref>{{cite web|last=Rosen |first=Craig |url=http://new.music.yahoo.com/programs/the-new-now/405/get-to-know-now-now-children-every-with-a-free-mp3 |title=Get To Know Now, Now Every Children With A Free MP3 |website=New.music.yahoo.com |date=May 1, 2009 |accessdate=August 31, 2010}}</ref> The band's name, according to Dalager, was an [[in-joke]] resulting from a typo in an online chat. The band considered using the name for an [[Extended play|EP]] title, but it stuck as the band name when they officially started the project.<ref name="Ableson">{{cite web |last=Ableson |first=Jon |url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2010/01/in-spotlight-now-now-every-children.html |title=In The Spotlight: Now, Now Every Children |website=AlterThePress.com |date=January 2010 |accessdate=August 31, 2010 |archive-date=August 27, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100827200105/http://www.alterthepress.com/2010/01/in-spotlight-now-now-every-children.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> Dalager and Hale were joined by Brad's sister Britty on keyboards, and school friend Justin Schweim on bass for the recording of their first EP and their first few shows in the Minneapolis area.{{Citation needed|date=April 2012}} Shortly before the band began recording their first album, Schweim left their live setup, and Christine Sako stepped in.<ref name=Ableson /> Jess Abbott joined the band in Summer 2009 after moving from Maine to Minnesota, initially just for the summer until she left for college, however she became a permanent member shortly after. In 2017, after a new musical release, it was announced that Jess Abbott had left the band.<ref name="npr.org">{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/05/11/527605612/songs-we-love-now-now-sgl|title=Now, Now Is Back With Its First Song In 5 Years, The Instant Classic 'SGL'|website=Npr.org|accessdate=January 13, 2018}}</ref>

===''Cars'' (2007–2009)===
In 2007, Now, Now signed to local Minneapolis-based [[Independent record label|indie record label]] Afternoon Records, founded by friend [[Ian M. Anderson (musician)|Ian Anderson]].<ref name="Ableson"/> The band recorded and released two EPs on the label in early 2008, titled ''Not One, But Two'' and ''In The City''.<ref name="Salisbury">{{cite web |last=Salisbury |first=Vanita |url=http://www.blackbookmag.com/article/now-now-every-childrens-cacie-dalager-takes-our-pop-quiz/7451 |title=Pop Quiz: Now, Now, Every Children’s Cacie Dalager |publisher=BlackBook |date=May 1, 2009 |accessdate=August 31, 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090506011859/http://www.blackbookmag.com/article/now-now-every-childrens-cacie-dalager-takes-our-pop-quiz/7451 |archivedate=May 6, 2009 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> In September 2008, the band opened for [[Mates of State]] in Minneapolis, a performance that Dalager then regarded as "easily my favorite show we have ever played."<ref name="Brusie">{{cite web|last=Brusie |first=David |url=http://www.avclub.com/twincities/articles/now-now-every-children,1277/ |title=Interview: Now, Now Every Children |publisher=The A.V. Club Twin Cities |date=December 9, 2008 |accessdate=August 31, 2010}}</ref> Anderson produced their full-length debut album ''Cars'', released digitally on February 27, 2009<ref>{{cite web |url=https://nownoweverychildren-official.bandcamp.com/album/cars |title=Cars - Now, Now Every Children |website=Bandcamp |access-date=March 9, 2021}}</ref> and on CD on July 9, 2009.<ref name="Salisbury" /> Its release was accompanied by an album release show on December 12 at Minneapolis venue [[First Avenue (nightclub)|7th Street Entry]].<ref name="Brusie" /> The band played [[The Bamboozle]] festival on May 3, 2009<ref>{{cite web|last=Gaston |first=Peter |url=http://www.spin.com/gallery/15-must-hear-bands-bamboozle-fest?page=2#main |title=15 Must-Hear Bands at Bamboozle Fest |website=Spin.com|date=April 29, 2009 |accessdate=August 31, 2010}}</ref> and supported [[Paramore]] on tour across Europe in December 2009.<ref>{{cite web |last=Henderson |first=Steve |url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=1316262 |title=Now, Now Every Paramore |website=AbsolutePunk.net |date=September 9, 2009 |accessdate=August 31, 2010 |archive-date=June 30, 2012 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20120630152121/http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=1316262 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===Label changes and ''Neighbors'' (2010–2011)===
After about three years at Afternoon Records, Now, Now left the label, which they announced on their official blog on April 16, 2010.<ref>{{cite web |last=Ableson |first=Jon |url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2010/04/now-now-every-children-leave-afternoon.html |title=Now, Now Every Children Leave Afternoon Records |website=AlterThePress.com |date=April 2010 |accessdate=August 31, 2010 |archive-date=April 20, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100420082109/http://www.alterthepress.com/2010/04/now-now-every-children-leave-afternoon.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> Since then, the band has shortened their name from "Now, Now Every Children" to simply "Now, Now".<ref>{{cite web |last=Reid |first=Sean |url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2010/10/now-now-every-children-name-change.html |title=Now, Now Every Children Name Change |website=AlterThePress.com |date=October 2010 |accessdate=October 27, 2010 |archive-date=March 2, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120302234044/http://www.alterthepress.com/2010/10/now-now-every-children-name-change.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> Of the name change, Dalager explained to SPIN: "We felt like we needed to disconnect ourselves from any childish image we had. It was a way of starting over and simplifying things for us."<ref name="Gaston">{{cite web|last=Gaston |first=Peter |url=https://www.spin.com/2010/11/exclusive-new-music-grown-now-now/ |title=EXCLUSIVE: New Music from a Grown-Up Now, Now |website=Spin.com|date=November 23, 2010 |accessdate=November 23, 2010}}</ref>  Under their new name, they self-produced an EP titled ''Neighbors'', which was digitally released on September 7, 2010 for 24 hours.<ref>{{cite web |last=Reid |first=Sean |url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2010/09/now-now-every-children-release-new-ep.html |title=Now, Now Every Children Release New EP for 24 Hours Only and Sign with No Sleep Records |website=Alterthepress.com |date=September 2010 |accessdate=September 7, 2010 |archive-date=March 2, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120302233853/http://www.alterthepress.com/2010/09/now-now-every-children-release-new-ep.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> There were also 140 physical copies of the EP made, which sold out within 8 minutes of going on sale. The album was re-released on December 7 via No Sleep Records.<ref name="Gaston" /> The band performed at the first Popsickle Festival, presented by [[Motion City Soundtrack]], at [[First Avenue (nightclub)|First Avenue]] on December 18, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|last=Sciarretto |first=Amy |url=http://www.artistdirect.com/entertainment-news/article/motion-city-soundtrack-launches-popsickle-festival/7920764 |title=Motion City Soundtrack Launches Popsickle Festival |website=Artistdirect.com |date= November 11, 2010 |accessdate=November 12, 2010}}</ref>

Now, Now began 2011 as a support act on all dates of [[Hellogoodbye|Hellogoodbye's]] winter tour, along with [[Jukebox the Ghost]], [[You, Me, and Everyone We Know]] and [[Gold Motel]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Common |first=Tyler |url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/hellogoodbye_announce_2011_headlining_tour/ |title=Hellogoodbye announce 2011 headlining tour with You Me And Everyone We Know, Gold Motel and more |publisher=Alt Press |date=December 16, 2010 |accessdate=January 16, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110131191735/http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/hellogoodbye_announce_2011_headlining_tour/ |archive-date=January 31, 2011 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref> The band was also scheduled to be one of the support acts for [[Fake Problems]] on their Spring 2011 tour, but pulled out to prepare new material for their next full-length record and to "clear [their] heads after so many straight months of touring"; they still performed on the Minnesota date.<ref>{{cite web |last=Kohli |first=Rohan |url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2180262 |title=Fake Problems Headlining Tour |website=AbsolutePunk.net |date=February 17, 2011 |accessdate=February 22, 2011 |archive-date=July 16, 2012 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20120716074732/http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2180262 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Zarrillo |first=Zack |url=http://propertyofzack.com/post/4585886048/now-now-pull-off-fake-problems-tour-for-new-record |title=Now, Now Pull Off Fake Problems Tour For New Record |website=PropertyOfZack.com |date=April 13, 2011 |accessdate=April 18, 2011 |archive-date=August 25, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110825053405/http://propertyofzack.com/post/4585886048/now-now-pull-off-fake-problems-tour-for-new-record |url-status=dead }}</ref> On May 3, 2011, Now, Now released a collection of remixes of the ''Neighbors'' tracks, called ''Neighbors: The Remixes''. In the same announcement, it was confirmed that the band would be recording their second full-length album shortly thereafter, produced by [[Howard Redekopp]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://filtermagazine.com/index.php/media/entry/mp3_now_now_remix_jesus_camp_filter_exclusive |title=MP3: Now, Now Remix ‘Jesus Camp’ (FILTER Exclusive) |publisher=FILTER Magazine |date=April 21, 2010 |accessdate=April 21, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110426054541/http://filtermagazine.com/index.php/media/entry/mp3_now_now_remix_jesus_camp_filter_exclusive |archive-date=April 26, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The band gained some mainstream exposure when their song "Neighbors" appeared in an episode of Grey's Anatomy ("It's a Long Way Back"), which aired on April 28, 2011. In fall 2011, Now, Now performed a short tour supporting All Get Out,<ref>{{cite web |last=Ableson |first=Jon |url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2011/08/now-now-announce-us-shows-w-all-get-out.html |title=Now, Now Announce US Shows w/ All Get Out |website=AlterThePress.com |date=August 2011 |accessdate=September 15, 2011 |archive-date=March 3, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303235500/http://www.alterthepress.com/2011/08/now-now-announce-us-shows-w-all-get-out.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> with an additional 2 dates supporting Mansions.

===''Threads'' (2012–2013)===
On December 8, 2011, it was announced via their Facebook page that they had signed to [[Chris Walla]]'s Trans Record Label - an imprint of Atlantic Records, and that their second album ''[[Threads (Now, Now album)|Threads]]'' would be released on March 6, 2012.<ref>{{cite web |last=Darwin |first=Liza |url=http://www.nylonmag.com/?section=article&parid=7281 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120521192316/http://www.nylonmag.com/?section=article&parid=7281 |url-status=dead |archive-date=May 21, 2012 |title=Premiere: Now, Now |publisher=Nylon Magazine |date=December 8, 2011 |accessdate=December 8, 2011 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://music.pacsun.com/blog/2011/12/09/big-things-coming-now-now/ |title=Big Things in 2012 for Now, Now |publisher=PacSun Music |date=December 9, 2011 |accessdate=December 9, 2011 }}{{dead link|date=February 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

Since the release of ''Threads'', Now, Now have been an opening act for [[The Naked And Famous]]' US Spring Tour, and joined [[fun.]] on their Summer US tour in May 2012. In September 2012, they embarked on a 7 date run of the UK, in support of the UK release of Threads, and are supporting Motion City Soundtrack on most of their UK and US tour dates.

Now, Now made their television debut on [[Late Night with Jimmy Fallon]] on November 7, 2012, performing "Thread".<ref>{{cite web|last=Ilvonen |first=Keagan |url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2915262 |title=Now, Now To Perform on Late Night with Jimmy Fallon |website=AbsolutePunk.net |date=October 8, 2012 |accessdate=October 8, 2012}}</ref>

In early 2013 Now, Now joined [[To Write Love on Her Arms]]'s Heavy and Light US tour.<ref>{{cite web|last=Wagner |first=Christian |url=http://absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2979522 |title=To Write Love on Her Arms Announces Heavy and Light Tour |website=AbsolutePunk.net |date=December 11, 2012 |accessdate=April 7, 2013}}</ref>

===Delays and ''Saved'' (2014–Present)===
In 2014, the band announced that they were in the process of writing a new record.

2015 saw no new touring or new releases for Now, Now, but the band continued to interact with fans through social media, maintaining that the new record was still being written. They were announced to appear at both the 2016 and 2017 editions of the [[South by Southwest|SXSW]] festival, however in both instances they withdrew without commenting on the scheduled appearances.

On May 8, 2017 the band deleted all previous content from their Twitter and Facebook page and posted a short teaser video. No comments were provided with the video. Furthermore, the band's new homepage nownowmusic.com was opened. The following day an eight-date US Summer tour was announced - their first since 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nownowmusic.com/tour/|title=Tour|website=Nownowmusic.com|accessdate=January 13, 2018}}</ref>

On May 11, 2017 the band released a new single, "SGL." It was also announced that [[Jess Abbott]] amicably parted ways with the band.<ref name="npr.org"/>

The band released another single, "Yours," on September 15, 2017. They had posted a number of teaser videos to their social media accounts in advance of the release and premiered the song September 14 on [[The Fader]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/09/14/now-now-band-yours-sgl-video|title=Now, Now Is America’s Next Great Band|website=Thefader.com|accessdate=January 13, 2018}}</ref>

The band released a third single, "AZ," on March 1, 2018, along with announcing the title of their new album ''[[Saved (Now, Now album)|Saved]]'', set for a May 18 release date.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/03/01/now-now-saved-album-interview|title=Now, Now just keep getting better|website=Thefader.com}}</ref>

On May 2, 2018, the band released a fourth and final single, "MJ."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1994360/now-now-mj/music/|title=Now, Now – “MJ”|website=Stereogum.com|date=May 2, 2018}}</ref>

On April 9, 2019, the band released their first single of 2019 called "Enda"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2039140/now-now-enda/music/|title=Now, Now - "Enda"|website=Stereogum.com|date=April 9, 2019}}</ref>
