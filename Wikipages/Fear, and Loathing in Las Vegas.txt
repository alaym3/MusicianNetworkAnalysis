'''Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas''' ''([[sic]])'' (フィアー·アンド·ロージング·イン·ラスベガス) is a Japanese [[electronicore]] band from [[Kobe]] that was formed in summer 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegas-jp.com/en/bio/ |title=Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas Biography |website=Lasvegas-jp.com |access-date=6 April 2017}}</ref> The band currently consists of vocalist So, keyboardist Minami, guitarist Taiki, drummer Tomonori, and bassist Tetsuya. They are best known for their heavy use of [[vocoder]] and [[synthesizer]] blended with [[emo]]/[[metalcore]] music and rave beats.<ref name="Rockin'On Japan">{{cite web|magazine=[[Rockin'On Japan]]|language=ja|url=https://rockinon.com/artist/5444|title=Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegasの記事まとめ|trans-title=Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas Overview|access-date=15 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/2015-the-burning-questions-are-babymetal-japan-s-weirdest-band|title=2015 – the burning questions: are Babymetal Japan's weirdest band?|website=[[Classic Rock (magazine)|Louder]]|first=Paul|last=Brannigan|date=27 November 2015|access-date=29 October 2018}}</ref>

==History==
[[File:FALIV logo.png|thumb|left|Band's first logo]]
Founded in 2008 summer, the band was originally formed by members of the bands Ending for a Start and Blank Time. A year later, after an extensive search, So (former member of Bombreligion) joined as the sixth member.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jpopasia.com/fearandloathinginlasvegas/ |title=Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas|website=JpopAsia.com |access-date=6 April 2017}}</ref>

In 2010, [[Pac (wrestler)|PAC]] started using their song "Evolution (Entering the New World)" as his theme music when wrestling in [[Dragon Gate (wrestling)|Dragon Gate]].<ref name="Pac song">{{cite news|url=https://www.gaora.co.jp/dragongate/record/cd/028.html|title=Open The Music Gate 2012|language=ja|website=Dragon Gate Official|date=2 December 2011|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref>

In 2011, the song "Jump Around" from the band's [[extended play|EP]] ''[[Nextreme]]'' was selected to appear in the video game ''[[Pro Evolution Soccer 2012]]'' (in Asia: ''World Soccer: Winning Eleven 2012'') as the fourth track.<ref>[http://pes.neoseeker.com/wiki/List_of_songs_on_Pro_Evolution_Soccer_2012 List of songs on Pro Evolution Soccer 2012]; PES Neoseeker</ref> The song "Chase the Light!" was used as the opening theme for ''[[Kaiji (manga)|Gyakkyō Burai Kaiji: Hakairoku-hen]]'' (逆境無頼カイジ 破戒録篇 lit. Suffering Outcast Kaiji: Maverick Arc). In late 2011, the band's song "Just Awake" was featured as the closing song in the ''[[Hunter × Hunter]]'' [[Reboot (fiction)|reboot]] [[anime]]; as a single it was released later in 2012.<ref name="Just Awake HxH">{{cite news|last=Loo|first=Egan|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/interest/2011-09-16/galneryus-ono-fear-and-loathing-in-las-vegas-perform-hunter-x-hunter-songs|title=Galneryus' Ono, Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas perform 'Hunter X Hunter' songs|website=[[Anime News Network]]|date=16 September 2011|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/2011/09/hunterxhunter-anime-reveals-details-on-opening-ending-theme-songs/|title="HUNTERxHUNTER" anime reveals details on opening & ending theme songs|website=Tokyo Hive|access-date=17 September 2011}}</ref>

On 22 June 2012, the band announced that they will be releasing their second studio album on 8 August, titled ''[[All That We Have Now]]'', with a tour in September.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/2012/06/fear-and-loathing-in-las-vegas-announce-new-album-and-tour/|title=Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas announce new album and tour|website=Tokyo Hive|access-date=22 June 2012}}</ref>

In April 2013, the band announced the release of their first DVD, called ''The Animals in Screen'', scheduled to be out on 26 June.<ref>[http://www.jpopasia.com/news/fear-and-loathing-in-las-vegas-to-release-new-dvd-the-animals-in-screen::13498.html Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas to Release New DVD "The Animals in Screen"]; JPop Asia, 11 April 2013</ref> On 26 October, they announced a new single, "Rave-up Tonight", expected to be released on 15 January 2014, which is to be the theme song for ''[[Mobile Suit Gundam: Extreme Vs.|Gundam Extreme VS. Maxi Boost]]''.<ref>[http://www.jpopasia.com/news/fear-and-loathing-in-las-vegas-announces-new-single-for-january::15069.html Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas Announces New Single for January]; Jpop Asia, 26 October 2013</ref><ref name="Gundam Maxi Boost Rave-up">{{cite news|last=Green|first=Scott|url=https://www.crunchyroll.com/anime-news/2014/09/03-1/video-mobile-suit-gundam-extreme-vs-maxi-boost-introduces-nobel-gundam|title=Video: "Mobile Suit Gundam: Extreme Vs. Maxi Boost" introduces Nobel Gundam|website=[[Crunchyroll]]|date=4 September 2014|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.4gamer.net/games/234/G023420/20140214033/ |title=［JAEPO2014］「機動戦士ガンダムEXTREME VS. MAXI BOOST」，最新のスクリーンショットが公開 |website=[[4Gamer.net]] |access-date=6 April 2017|language=ja}}</ref> In September 2013, the founding bassist Mashu left the band, as a result, on their official website announced on 23 September 2013 that Kei replaced Mashu as the new bassist.

On 23 March 2014, the band announced that they will be releasing a new album this summer. The album release was announced at the [[World Memorial Hall|Kobe World Memorial Hall]] live during their first headlining show. As a result, they will start a new tour. The song "Virtue and Vice" is featured as the opening theme for ''[[Brynhildr in the Darkness|Gokukoku no Brynhildr]]'' and the song "Thunderclap" was made by the band for the third anime season of ''[[Sengoku Basara]]'', subtitled ''[[Sengoku Basara: End of Judgement|Judge End]]''. Both songs are taken from their third album, ''[[Phase 2 (album)|Phase 2]]''. On 5 September, the band announced on their official site that they will release a new limited single on 7 January 2015 called "Let Me Hear". On 8 October, the short version of their new single premiered as the opening theme of ''[[Parasyte]]''.<ref name="Parasyte song">{{cite news|last=Green|first=Scott|url=https://www.crunchyroll.com/anime-news/2014/08/29/fear-and-loathing-in-las-vegas-to-perform-parasyte-anime-opening|title=Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas to perform "Parasyte" anime opening|website=Crunchyroll|date=29 August 2014|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref>

On 13 May 2015, the band released a new limited singles "Starburst" and "Struggle to Survive". A few days after the release of "Starburst", the band announced on their official site that they would be releasing their fourth studio album this autumn including the two main songs from their last two singles, "Starburst" and "Let Me Hear". On 12 August, the band released a new PV for the song "Cast Your Shell" which also was included to the album. On 16 August, they announced the title of the album as being ''[[Feeling of Unity (album)|Feeling of Unity]]'' which was released on 30 September 2015.

In 2017, the band signed onto a new label with [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music Japan]]. On 2 April 2017, the band announced on their official site the trailer for their new single, "Shine" on 23 June. On 26 May, they released a trailer for a PV for the song "Shine" with the full version being released the following month. The second single "Return to Zero" was released on 11 July. On 11 October, an PV was released for their new song "LLLD" which is featured on their newest fifth studio album ''[[New Sunrise]]''. On 23 October, they released another new PV titled "The Sun Also Rises" which is the final track of the album. ''New Sunrise'' was released 25 October 2017 and includes the songs "Shine" and "Return to Zero".

On 31 January 2018, the band released a new PV for the song "Keep the Heat and Fire Yourself Up" which is also the opening for the anime ''[[Hoshin Engi|Hakyū Hōshin Engi]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.crunchyroll.com/anime-news/2017/12/15/tv-anime-hakyu-hoshin-engi-new-pv-introduces-op-song-by-fear-and-loathing-in-lasvegas|title=TV anime "Hakyu Hoshin Engi" new PV introduces OP song by Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas|date=15 December 2017|work=[[Crunchyroll]]|access-date=14 October 2018|last=Komatsu|first=Mikikazu}}</ref> The full single was released 2 May 2018. The band also provided "The Gong of Knockout" for the second opening for [[Netflix]] and [[TMS Entertainment]]'s anime adaptation of ''[[Baki the Grappler]]'' in 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://manga.tokyo/news/baki-recap-trailer-released-before-second-half-of-series/|title=Baki Recap Trailer Released Before Second Half of Series {{!}} MANGA.TOKYO|date=27 September 2018|work=MANGA.TOKYO|access-date=2 October 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> On 30 June, their official website announced that the guitarist, Sxun had left the band due to personal circumstances.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://jame-world.com/us/news-130692-sxun-leaves-fear-and-loathing-in-las-vegas.html|title=Sxun leaves Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas|date=30 June 2018|work=JaME USA|access-date=14 October 2018|author=Christine}}</ref>

[[File:New Logo 2019.jpg|thumb|right|Band's 2019 logo]]
On 16 January 2019, the band's official website announced the bassist, Kei had died due to an [[Heart failure|acute heart failure]] at his home on midnight of 12 January. They discontinued a release of a new album and their Japanese tour in the spring of 2019. However, they announced that this will not stop the band's activity.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2019-01-16/fear-and-loathing-in-las-vegas-band-bassist-kei-passes-away/.142138|title=Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas Band Bassist Kei Passes Away|date=16 January 2019|website=Anime News Network|access-date=17 January 2019|last=Ressler|first=Karen}}</ref>

On 19 March, the band's official website announced to hold a tribute concert "Thanks to You All" in the memory of Kei on 7 June 2019 at the "Namba Hatch". The reason they chose "Namba Hatch" was because it was Kei's first live performance for the band. On 29 June, the band's official website announced that Tetsuya replaced Kei as the new bassist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegas-jp.com/topics/5278|title=新メンバー加入のお知らせ|publisher=Official website|trans-title=Announcement of New Member|date=28 June 2019|access-date=28 June 2019|language=ja}}</ref> On 4 December, the band released their sixth studio album titled ''[[Hypertoughness]]'' in Japan with an announcement that the worldwide release date is set on 15 January 2020.
