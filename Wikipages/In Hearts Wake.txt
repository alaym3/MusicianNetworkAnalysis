'''In Hearts Wake''' are an Australian [[metalcore]] band from [[Byron Bay]], formed in 2006. The band comprises vocalist Jake Taylor, guitarists Eaven Dall and Ben Nairne, bassist Kyle Erich, and drummer Conor Ward. They have released five studio albums: ''[[Divination (album)|Divination]]'', ''[[Earthwalker]]'', ''[[Skydancer (In Hearts Wake album)|Skydancer]]'', ''[[Ark (In Hearts Wake album)|Ark]]'' and ''[[Kaliyuga (album)|Kaliyuga]]'', which was released on 7 August 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-in-hearts-wake-detail-fifth-album-kaliyuga/|title=NEWS: In Hearts Wake detail fifth album, 'Kaliyuga'!|publisher=Dead Press!|access-date=24 April 2020|date=24 April 2020}}</ref> The band's lyrical themes include [[environmentalism]], [[environmental justice]], [[global warming]], [[Conservation movement|conservation]], and other social issues.

==History==
===Early years and first releases (2006–2010)===
In Hearts Wake were formed in early 2006 composed of five friends that originate from Byron Bay, Australia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/in-hearts-wake-mn0002997787/biography|title=In Hearts Wake {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref> In July 2006, the band recorded their first demo track "True Love Is Hard to Find". In December 2006, they recorded their debut 7-track EP, entitled ''Into the Storm''. In early 2007, the band welcomed new drummer Caleb Burton and began to refine their sound, writing faster, heavier, more technical and melodic songs. In July 2007, they embarked on a tour that covered three Australian states and three major cities.

In December 2007, with an average age of just seventeen, the band began their 5-month studio recording schedule at [[SAE Institute]] in Byron Bay to produce their second EP with Scottish producer/engineer James Lyall, entitled ''The Gateway'', which was self-financed by the producer and musicians and mixed by respected US engineer Daniel Castleman ([[As I Lay Dying (band)|As I Lay Dying]], [[Winds of Plague]]).<ref>''[[Triple J]]'': [http://www.triplejunearthed.com/InHeartsWake In Hearts Wake: Biography]</ref> In 2010, the band produced a split-EP together with the hardcore band The Bride.<ref>Nick Lalak: ''The AU Review'': [http://www.theaureview.com/the-goss/the-bride-and-in-hearts-wake-announce-the-split-ep THE BRIDE AND IN HEARTS WAKE ANNOUNCE THE SPLIT EP]</ref>

===''Divination'' (2011–2013)===
In Hearts Wake signed their first professional recording deal with [[UNFD]] in July 2012. Their debut studio album, ''[[Divination (album)|Divination]]'', was released on 31 August 2012. Josh Schroeder produced the album at Random Awesome Studios in the [[United States of America]].<ref>Coops: ''Chucking a Mosh'': [http://www.chuckingamosh.com/2012/07/13/unfd-signs-in-hearts-wake-and-releases-debut-album/ UNFD Signs In Hearts Wake and Releases Debut Album]</ref> Divination peaked at 27 on the [[ARIA charts]].<ref name="Charts">Charts: [http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=In+Hearts+Wake&titel=Divination&cat=a AU]</ref>

In March 2013, they played four shows in New Zealand.<ref>''The Blackout Agency'': [http://www.theblackoutagency.com/in-hearts-wake-new-zealand-tour/ In Hearts Wake – 2013 New Zealand Tour] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203032403/http://www.theblackoutagency.com/in-hearts-wake-new-zealand-tour/ |date=3 December 2013 }}</ref> In June, another Australia tour followed with support from [[Counterparts (band)|Counterparts]], The Storm Picturesque and [[Stories (band)|Stories]].<ref>Mike Hohnen: ''Music Feeds'': [http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/in-hearts-wake-announce-australian-tour-june-2013/ In Hearts Wake Announce Australian Tour, June 2013]</ref> The first tour throughout Australia was held in September. In Hearts Wake toured with [[Landscapes (band)|Landscapes]] as support for [[The Amity Affliction]] in [[Europe]]. The tour led the bands throughout the [[United Kingdom]], [[Germany]], [[Belgium]], [[France]] and [[Austria]].<ref>Dark Lord: ''Stormbringer.at'': [http://www.stormbringer.at/news_39378/the-amity-affliction-landscapes-in-hearts-wake-europa-tour-dates-inkl-wien-konzert.html THE AMITY AFFLICTION, LANDSCAPES, IN HEARTS WAKE - Europa Tour Dates inkl. Wien Konzert!] (German)</ref> The band continued touring together with The Amity Affliction on their "Brothers in Arms Australia Tour" alongside [[Chelsea Grin]] and [[Stick to Your Guns (band)|Stick to Your Guns]] as support.<ref>Mike Hohnen: ''Music Feeds'': [http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/the-amity-affliction-announce-brothers-in-arms-australian-tour-with-chelsea-grin-stick-to-your-guns/ The Amity Affliction Announce ‘Brothers In Arms’ Australian Tour With Chelsea Grin & Stick To Your Guns]</ref>

Between 30 August 2013 and 8 September 2013, the band toured [[Australia]] alongside [[Like Moths to Flames]] and label mates [[Hand of Mercy]] in support of the "Homebound Tour" of [[Dream On, Dreamer]].<ref>Bomber: ''Bombshell Zine'': [http://www.bombshellzine.com/blog/2012/07/video-clip-in-hearts-wake-traveller-the-fool/ Video Clip – In Hearts Wake – ‘Traveller (The Fool)’] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006154913/http://www.bombshellzine.com/blog/2012/07/video-clip-in-hearts-wake-traveller-the-fool/ |date=6 October 2014 }}</ref> After the "Homebound Tour", the band played four shows with British [[electronicore]] group [[Enter Shikari]].<ref>Alex Wilson: ''The Music'': [http://themusic.com.au/news/all/2012/09/06/in-hearts-wake-announced-as-enter-shikari-support/ IN HEARTS WAKE SCORE ENTER SHIKARI SUPPORT]</ref>

===''Earthwalker'' and ''Skydancer'' (2013–2015)===
In February and March 2014, they played [[Soundwave (Australian music festival)|Soundwave Festival]] in Australia for the first time.<ref>Anwar Rizk: ''Metal Obsession'': [http://www.metalobsession.net/2013/11/08/soundwave-festival-2014-second-announcement-revealed/ News : Soundwave Festival 2014 second announcement revealed]</ref> Before playing at Soundwave, the band headlined the "Skydancer Australia Tour" with support from [[Hand of Mercy]] and [[Hellions (band)|Hellions]]. The earnings of the tour went to the band's own Skydancer project.<ref>Greg Moskovitch: ''Music Feeds'': [http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/in-hearts-wake-announce-skydancer-regional-tour-with-hand-of-mercy-hellions/ In Hearts Wake Announce ‘Skydancer’ Regional Tour With Hand Of Mercy & Hellions]</ref><ref>Mike Hohnen: ''Music Feeds'': [http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/in-hearts-wake-launch-the-skydancer-project-in-the-name-of-charity/ In Hearts Wake Launch 'The Skydancer Project' In The Name Of Charity]</ref>

The musicians announced they would release a new album, ''[[Earthwalker]]'', on 2 May 2014 and in Europe at the end of May. The album peaked at No. 5 at official [[ARIA Charts]].<ref name="Charts" /> In June, the band toured Australia again to promote ''Earthwalker'' with support from [[Being as an Ocean]], [[Dream On, Dreamer]], Endless Heights, and [[Sierra (band)|Sierra]].<ref>Greg Moskovitch: ''The Music'': [http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/in-hearts-wake-earthwalker-national-tour-announced/ In Hearts Wake ‘Earthwalker’ National Tour Announced]</ref>

In early March 2015, the band announced that they would be releasing a new album entitled ''[[Skydancer (In Hearts Wake album)|Skydancer]]'' in May 2015. Jake Taylor stated in a documentary that the band actually recorded the new album alongside their second studio album ''Earthwalker''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/in-hearts-wake-announce-new-album-skydancer/|title=In Hearts Wake Announce New Album 'Skydancer' - Music Feeds|date=4 March 2015}}</ref>

The band support [[Northlane]] on their "North American Node Tour" in the summer of 2015, [[Oceans Ate Alaska]] and Like Moths to Flames were support. The band then returned to North America the same year in the fall to support [[Parkway Drive]] on the "IRE Tour". [[Thy Art Is Murder]] and [[Miss May I]] joined as support.

On 31 March 2016, as an [[April Fools' Day|April Fool's Day]] joke, the band unveiled the final piece in their three album series entitled ''Seaskimmer'' to be released 6 May 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/inheartswake/photos/a.457581732712.232782.203218602712/10153813679917713/?type=3&theater|title=In Hearts Wake|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=16 April 2017}}</ref>

===''Equinox'' (2016–2017)===
On 19 April 2016, the band announced that they had been working on a collaborative EP with Northlane entitled ''[[Equinox (EP)|Equinox]]''. It was recorded in January in Melbourne with producer Will Putney. The EP features three songs: "Refuge", "Equinox" and "Hologram" and was released on 20 April, through UNFD. To promote the EP, Northlane and In Hearts Wake co-headlined a tour in June.<ref name="mf">{{cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/northlane-hearts-wake-release-surprise-joint-ep-equinox/|title=Northlane & In Hearts Wake Release Surprise Joint EP, 'Equinox'|publisher=Music Feeds|first=Zanda|last=Wilson|date=20 April 2016|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> Supporting them were [[Hands Like Houses]] and [[Ocean Grove (Australian band)|Ocean Grove]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unfdcentral.com/news/northlane-in-hearts-wake-announce-equinox-australian-tour/|title=Northlane & In Hearts Wake Announce Equinox Australian Tour|website=UNFD Central|date=2 March 2016|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> On 5 December, touring member Conor Ward was officially announced as the band's new drummer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unfdcentral.com/news/hearts-wake-officially-welcome-new-drummer-conor-ward/|website=UNFD Central|title=In Hearts Wake officially welcome new drummer Conor Ward|date=5 December 2016|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref>

===''Ark'' and ''Ark Prevails'' (2017–2019)===
On 2 April 2017, the band unveiled a new song "Warcry" during their UK tour in support of [[While She Sleeps]]. On 26 April, another single, "Passage", was released, and it was announced that both tracks were taken from their upcoming album ''[[Ark (In Hearts Wake album)|Ark]]'', released on 26 May 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teamrock.com/news/2017-04-26/in-hearts-wake-announce-new-album-ark |title=In Hearts Wake announce new album Ark |publisher=Teamrock.com |date=25 April 2017 |access-date=4 May 2017 }}</ref>

Prior to the album's release, the band announced an Australian headline tour with While She Sleeps and [[Crossfaith]] in July 2017. The band also announced their We Are Waterborne Initiative, aiming to clean up beaches in Australia and around the world with their fans. Due to overwhelming support and praise for the Initiative, the band released a third track from ''Ark'', "Waterborne", on 23 May.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/inheartswake/posts/10154967474852713|title=In Hearts Wake|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=7 August 2017}}</ref> After the release of ''Ark'' on 26 May, the album debuted at No. 3 on the Australian [[ARIA Charts]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/inheartswake/photos/a.457581732712.232782.203218602712/10155006218162713/?type=3|title=In Hearts Wake|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=7 August 2017}}</ref> In North America the band opened up for [[August Burns Red]] on their ''Messengers'' 10th Anniversary Tour with [[Protest the Hero]] and '68 as support.

To celebrate the release of ''Ark'', the band embarked on their first beach clean-ups with fans for their newly created We Are Waterborne Initiative. Subsequent events were organized in New Zealand and regional Australia throughout 2017 and 2018 with the help of local [[marine conservation]] organizations Tangaroa Blue and [[Sea Shepherd Conservation Society|Sea Shepherd]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.unfdcentral.com/news/hearts-wake-launch-waterborne-cleanup-initiative/|title=In Hearts Wake Launch "We Are Waterborne" - A Cleanup Initiative {{!}} UNFD Central|date=27 April 2017|work=UNFD Central|access-date=7 August 2017|language=en-US}}</ref>

On 6 July, In Hearts Wake released a music video for the single "Nomad".<ref>{{Citation|last=UNFD|title=In Hearts Wake - Nomad [Official Music Video]|date=5 July 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XKaS-TM1gUc|access-date=7 August 2017}}</ref> The music video features an all-girl mosh pit and is accompanied by a message from the band regarding safety and self-expression for all fans at their shows.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/inheartswake/posts/10155102416172713|title=In Hearts Wake|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=7 August 2017}}</ref> Shortly after, the band embarked on their Australian "Ark Tour", and also toured extensively in Europe and the United States in late 2017, co-headlining with [[Fit for a King (band)|Fit for a King]], and support from [[Like Moths to Flames]] and [[Phinehas (band)|Phinehas]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://inheartswake.com/|title=In Hearts Wake|website=inheartswake.com|language=en-US|access-date=7 August 2017}}</ref> At the start of 2018, the band kicked off their 28-date "Great Southern Land Tour", which included shows all across Australia.<ref name=":0"/>

On 2 February 2018, the band released ''Ark Prevails'', a four-track ''Ark'' companion EP that includes three "re-imagined" acoustic versions of the songs "Frequency", "Waterborne", and "Arrow", the latter of which features guest vocals from CJ Gilpin, frontwoman of the UK band [[Dream State]], as well as a new ambient instrumental track entitled "Adrift (Outro)". The release date of the EP was alluded to in the ''Ark'' album tray, which contained a hidden secret message that could be decoded using a [[cipher]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://www.unfdcentral.com/news/hearts-wake-release-ark-prevails-ep/|title=In Hearts Wake Release Ark Prevails EP {{!}} UNFD Central|date=2 February 2018|work=UNFD Central|access-date=6 February 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> According to frontman Jake Taylor:

{{Quote|text=Last year we hid 02.02.18 within the Ark CD booklet and it could only be revealed by using the cypher (only a few figured it out!). So today we finally reveal to you Ark Prevails, bonus re-imaginings of three tracks as well as a special ambient piece. Some of these renditions you would have heard us play acoustically at our Marine Debris clean ups, which took place on the shores of waterways around the world. This four track offering is a thank you to everyone helping to re-steer the ship. May the Ark Prevail.|sign=Jake Taylor|source=}}

All proceeds from the sale of ''Ark Prevails'', via [[Bandcamp]]'s "name your price" model, goes to Sea Shepherd Australia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://inheartswakeband.bandcamp.com/album/ark-prevails|title=Ark Prevails, by In Hearts Wake|website=In Hearts Wake|access-date=6 February 2018}}</ref> In Hearts Wake also joined the final touring portion of the Vans Warped Tour in 2018. They were joined along with [[Motionless in White]], [[Simple Plan]], [[Unearth]], [[Every Time I Die]], [[Wage War]], [[Chelsea Grin (band)|Chelsea Grin]], [[Crown the Empire]], [[Nekrogoblikon]] and much more. After which, in June they performed an all ages "Ark Tour" in Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/hearts-wake-announce-2018-ark-ages-tour/|title=In Hearts Wake Announce 2018 'Ark' All Ages Tour|first=Tom|last=Williams|publisher=Music Feeds|date=12 April 2018|access-date=19 March 2020}}</ref>

In January 2019, the band performed their Summer Setlist Tour and performed in Geelong, Albury, Canberra, Wollongong, Penrith and Newcastle, while also performing at UNIFY Gathering 2019 along the way. The tour was supported by [[Alpha Wolf (band)|Alpha Wolf]] and Drown This City, and was In Hearts Wake's only NSW, ACT & VIC shows for the year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/in-hearts-wake-fans-choose-setlist-2019-summer-setlist-tour/|title=In Hearts Wake To Let Fans Choose The Setlist On 2019 'Summer Setlist' Tour|first=Tom|last=Williams|publisher=Music Feeds|date=11 October 2018|access-date=19 March 2020}}</ref>

===''Kaliyuga'' (2020–present)===
On 17 March 2020, the band unveiled a new single titled "Worldwide Suicide" along with an accompanying music video in which the band intend to plant a tree for every 1 thousand views the below video for it generates.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2020/03/18/news/in-hearts-wake-debut-new-track-worldwide-suicide-launch-environmental-initiative/|title=In Hearts Wake Debut New Track "Worldwide Suicide", Launch Environmental Initiative|publisher=ThePRP|access-date=18 March 2020|date=25 April 2020}}</ref> On 1 April, like with the album cover ''Seaskimmer'', as an April Fool's Day joke the band unveiled another album cover entitled ''Hellbringer''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/inheartswake/status/1245155448279318529|title=April Fools?|website=Twitter|language=en|access-date=24 April 2020}}</ref> On 24 April, the band released the second single "Son of a Witch" from their upcoming fifth studio album, ''[[Kaliyuga (album)|Kaliyuga]]'', which is scheduled to be released on 7 August 2020 along with a corresponding music video. At the same time, the band announced the album itself, the album cover, the track list, and release date.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2020/04/24/news/in-hearts-wake-set-august-release-for-new-album-kaliyuga-premiere-son-of-a-witch/|title=In Hearts Wake Set August Release For New Album "Kaliyuga", Premiere "Son Of A Witch"|publisher=ThePRP|access-date=25 April 2020|date=24 April 2020}}</ref>

On 27 May, the band released the third single "Hellbringer" featuring Jamie Hails of [[Polaris (Australian band)|Polaris]]. The single was inconspicuously teased by the band as an April Fool's Day joke with revelation that on the "album cover" of the single they already teased that Hails will feature on the song as his face was on the cover but halfway hidden. That same day, the band also revealed other guest musicians that will feature on the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2020/05/27/in-hearts-wake-team-up-with-jamie-hails-for-hellbringer/|title=In Hearts Wake Team Up With Jamie Hails For 'Hellbringer'|website=Wall of Sound|access-date=27 May 2020|date=27 May 2020}}</ref> On 8 July, the band released their fourth single "Dystopia".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/in-hearts-wake-release-new-single-dystopia-share-music-video-2704140|title=In Hearts Wake release new single 'Dystopia', share music video|work=NME|access-date=9 July 2020|date=9 July 2020}}</ref> On 5 August, two days before the album release, the band released their fifth single "Moving On".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://metalnoise.net/2020/08/news-in-hearts-wake-are-moving-on|title=NEWS: In Hearts Wake are "Moving On"?|publisher=Metal Noise|access-date=6 August 2020|date=5 August 2020}}</ref>
