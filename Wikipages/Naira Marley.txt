'''Azeez Adeshina Fashola''' (born 10 May, 1991), known professionally as '''Naira Marley''', is a British-[[Nigerians|Nigerian]] singer and songwriter.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/pidgin/tori-48347911|title=Naira Marley: Tins you suppose know about Afeez Fashola aka Naira Beans|publisher=[[BBC]]|date=21 May 2019|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pmnewsnigeria.com/2020/06/16/we-wont-fly-with-your-hungry-airline-again-naira-marley-replies-executive-jets|title=We won't fly with your 'hungry airline' again: Naira Marley replies Executive Jets|date=16 June 2020|website=[[P.M. News]]|access-date=16 June 2020}}</ref> He is known as the president of his controversial fan base, "Marlians".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.pmnewsnigeria.com/2019/12/29/naira-marley-premieres-new-dance-moves-in-tesumole|title=Naira Marley premieres new dance moves in "Tesumole"|publisher=[[P.M. News]]|last=Okanlawon|first=Taiwo|date=29 December 2019|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://leadership.ng/2019/11/09/marlian-movement-and-their-crazy-lifestyle|title=Marlian Movement And Their Crazy Lifestyle|publisher=[[Leadership (newspaper)|Leadership Newspaper]]|last=Abraham|first=Anthony Ada|date=9 November 2019|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref>

== Education ==
At the age of 11, he moved to [[Peckham]], [[South London]], [[England]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://notjustok.com/artists/naira-marley|title=Naira Marley, Career, Background, Biography|publisher=[[NotJustOk]]|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref>  Niara Marley studied, at Porlock Hall before attending Walworth School in the UK, where he obtained his General Certificate of Secondary Education (GCSE). Naira Marley furthered, his studies at Peckham Academy and graduated with a distinction in Business.  He continued his studies and enrolled for business law at Crossways College now known as the Christ the King Sixth Form College.  

'''Career'''  

While growing up, Naira Marley had plans of becoming an [[Master of ceremonies|MC]] and a [[voice-over]] artist. He began singing in 2014 after discovering his passion for music and was encouraged by close-knit friends to pursue his music career. He released the Max Twigz-assisted track "Marry Juana" before releasing his debut EP ''Gotta Dance'' in 2015.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://mixmag.net/feature/get-to-know-naira-marley-the-lagos-via-peckham-mc-pioneering-afrobashment|title=Get to know Naira Marley, the Lagos via Peckham MC pioneering afrobashment|publisher=[[Mixmag]]|last=Mcquaid|first=Ian|date=2 March 2018|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://nigerianguide.com.ng/naira-marley-biography-net-worth-and-more|title=Naira Marley Biography, Net Worth and More|publisher=Nigerian Guide|last=Obialo|first=Maduawuchi|date=15 November 2019|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref>

In December 2017, he released the [[Olamide]] and [[Lil Kesh]]-assisted single "Issa Goal", which became the theme song for the [[Nigeria national football team|Super Eagles]] at the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://guardian.ng/art/how-naira-marleys-issa-goal-became-nigerias-world-cup-2018-theme-song|title=How Naira Marley's Issa Goal became Nigeria's World Cup, 2018 theme song|publisher=[[The Guardian (Nigeria)|The Guardian Nigeria]]|last=Onyekwena|first=Chiagoziem|date=23 June 2018|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=editor|date=2019-05-03|title=From Naija Issa Goal to Leggo, Coca-Cola Keeps the Ball Rolling in Music|url=https://www.thisdaylive.com/index.php/2019/05/03/from-naija-issa-goal-to-leggo-coca-cola-keeps-the-ball-rolling-in-music/|access-date=2020-09-25|website=THISDAYLIVE|language=en-US}}</ref> The official remix of "Issa Goal" was released on 16 June 2018; it features vocals by [[Olamide]], [[Lil Kesh]], [[Falz]], [[Simi (singer)|Simi]] and Slimcase.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://tooxclusive.com/song-naira-marley-falz-olamide-simi-lil-kesh-and-slimcase-naija-issagoal-remix|title=[Song] Naira Marley, Falz, Olamide, Simi, Lil Kesh and Slimcase - Naija IssaGoal (Remix)|publisher=[[tooXclusive]]|date=16 June 2018|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref>

He continued, his journey and owned 2018 when he released the  remix of "Issa Goal". which was released, on the16th of June in 2018. Naira Marley sings in [[English language|English]], [[Nigerian Pidgin|Pidgin]] and [[Yoruba language|Yoruba]]; his music is a blend of [[Afrobeats]] and [[Hip hop music|hip-hop]]. He derived his stage name from Jamaican singer [[Bob Marley]], whom he admires; his dreadlocks are also a tribute to the singer.

==Controversies==
===EFCC arrest===
On the 10th May, 2019 Naira Marley was caught up in a scandal with, The Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC) and was arrested, alongside with his friends namely; [[Zlatan (musician)|Zlatan]], Tiamiu Kayode, Adewunmi Moses, Micheal "Taqueesh" Adenuga and Abubakar Musa. The arrest, was made a day after Naira Marley released the video for his controversial single "Am I A Yahoo Boy". His friends were released, three days after their arrest but Naira Marley remained in their custody due to overwhelming evidence against him. In May 2019, Naira Marley, was charged by the EFCC with 11 counts of fraud. He later appeared before the Federal High Court and pleaded "not guilty" and he had his bail hearing on the 30th of May, 2019. Naira Marley was granted bail on the 14th of June, 2019 and was set to be released, "on two million naira bail ". Naira Marley released, the single <em>"4 Nights In Ekohtiebo"</em> while still in prison In it, he talks about his industry friends foes and other people who have admired him.        

In October 2019, he returned to the Federal High Court to face his charges.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/celebrities/naira-marley-back-in-court-over-alleged-cyber-fraud-video/rc1gzby|title=Naira Marley back in court over alleged cyber fraud [Video]|publisher=Pulse NG|last=Okonofua|first=Odion|date=22 October 2019|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref> His case was later adjourned to 27 February 2020 after an EFCC witness testified against him.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.premiumtimesng.com/news/more-news/367944-efcc-witness-testifies-against-naira-marley.html|title=EFCC witness testifies against Naira Marley|publisher=[[Premium Times]]|date=12 December 2019|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref>

===Flouting lockdown order===
He was detained by the Nigerian Police Force for contravening the lockdown order imposed on [[Lagos]] State by President [[Muhammadu Buhari]] to contain the spread of [[COVID-19 pandemic in Nigeria|coronavirus]] in the country.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://guardian.ng/news/police-detains-naira-marley-over-funke-akindeles-house-party/|title=Police detains Naira Marley over Funke Akindele's house party|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=6 April 2020|access-date=26 June 2020}}</ref> On 13 June 2020, he performed at a concert in Abuja despite the interstate travel ban and social distancing rules aimed at curbing the spread of [[COVID-19]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.premiumtimesng.com/entertainment/music/397583-amidst-covid-19-naira-marley-holds-abuja-concert.html|title=Amidst COVID-19, Naira Marley holds Abuja concert|publisher=[[Premium Times]]|last=Augoye|first=Jayne|date=14 June 2020|access-date=26 June 2020}}</ref> He was arraigned by the FCT Administration before a mobile court in [[Abuja]] over the concert.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-07|title=FCT Administration Arraigns Naira Marley Before Mobile Court Over Abuja Concert|url=http://saharareporters.com/2020/08/07/fct-administration-arraigns-naira-marley-mobile-court-over-abuja-concert|access-date=2020-09-19|website=Sahara Reporters}}</ref>
