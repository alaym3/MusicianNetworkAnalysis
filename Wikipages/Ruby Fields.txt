'''Ruby Fields''' is an Australian singer-songwriter, guitarist, and occasional radio host.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/ruby-fields-triple-j-breakfast |title=Ruby Fields will be taking over the triple j Breakfast slot next week |work=Tone Deaf
|date=25 June 2019 |accessdate=16 October 2019 |author=Jenke, Tyler}}</ref> In 2018, she released her debut EP ''Your Dad's Opinion for Dinner'', followed by the popular singles "I Want", "P Plates", and "Ritalin". Her single "[[Dinosaurs (song)|Dinosaurs]]" reached number nine on [[Triple J]]'s [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2018|Hottest 100 of 2018]].

In 2019, she released her second EP ''Permanent Hermit'', which contained the singles "[[Dinosaurs (song)|Dinosaurs]]", "Climate", and "Trouble".<ref name="PH2019" /> Her debut album ''[[Been Doin' It for a Bit]]'' was released in September 2021 and debuted atop the [[ARIA Albums Chart]].<ref name="ARIA 4 October"/>

==Early life==
Fields' hometown is Cronulla in southern [[Sydney]]. She began songwriting when she was 11<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3uDV7Ifjj_g|title=Meet The Overnight Sensation, Ruby Fields|author=triple j|via=YouTube}}</ref> and [[Street performance|busking]] when she was 13.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.russh.com/ruby-fields/|title=Ruby Fields|first=WORDS Gigi Perry PHOTOGRAPHY Sarah|last=Adamson|date=22 August 2018|website=Russh}}</ref> Fields spent several years learning how to build guitars and repair music equipment from her friend's father.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.monsterchildren.com/72478/ruby-fields-growing-real-time/|title=Ruby Fields is Growing up in Real Time|date=8 June 2018|website=Monster Children}}</ref><ref name="auto"/> She grew up listening to bands such as AC/DC, Fleetwood Mac, and Guns N' Roses.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/artist/ruby-fields|title=RUBY FIELDS|website=triple j Unearthed|language=en|access-date=2019-04-17}}</ref>

==Music career==
===2016–2018: Early years and ''Your Dad's Opinion for Dinner''===
Since 2016, Fields has been performing live with supporting band members; Adam Newling on [[lead guitar]], Tas Wilson on [[bass guitar]], and Patrick Rogers on [[drum kit|drums]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/pg/rubyfieldsmusic/about/?ref=page_internal|title=RUBY FIELDS - About|publisher=Facebook|accessdate=13 February 2019}}</ref>

In 2017, Fields uploaded her debut single "I Want" to the [[Triple J Unearthed]] website, which was played on nationwide radio station [[Triple J]] on the same day.<ref name="laneway">{{cite web|url=https://melbourne.lanewayfestival.com/line-up/artist/ruby-fields|title=St Jerome's Laneway Festival - Melbourne|website=melbourne.lanewayfestival.com}}</ref> The track, along with her follow-up single "P Plates", reached the top 200 in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2017]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/first-spin:-ruby-fields-announces-debut-ep-with-ritalin/9489502|title=First Spin: Ruby Fields announces debut EP with 'Ritalin'|first=Al|last=Newstead|date=27 February 2018|publisher=ABC|work=triple j}}</ref>

Fields performed at [[Groovin' the Moo#The Plot|The Plot]] 2017 and [[South by Southwest]] 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theindustryobserver.thebrag.com/sxsw-2018-1st-round/|title=Ruby Fields, Sloan Peterson, Keith Urban headed to SXSW 2018|date=11 October 2017|publisher=The Brag|work=The Industry Observer}}</ref> She also supported [[Ball Park Music]] and [[San Cisco]] on tour in 2018, and performed at [[St Jerome's Laneway Festival]] in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/ruby-fields-new-song/|title=Triple J staff & listeners are going nuts for the new Ruby Fields song|date=18 September 2018|publisher=The Brag|work=Tone Deaf}}</ref>

===2019: ''Permanent Hermit''===
On 27 January 2019, her song "Dinosaurs" reached number 9 in [[Triple J]]'s [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2018|Hottest 100 of 2018]]. In March 2019, Fields released "Climate" and announced the release of her second EP ''Permanent Hermit'', which was released on the 3 May 2019.

===2020: ''Been Doin' It for a Bit''===
In 2020, Fields released "Pretty Grim" and in April 2021, "R.E.G.O.", the second single from her forthcoming debut studio album.<ref name="REGO">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/ruby-fields-to-tour-nationally-from-this-month-2914333|title=Ruby Fields releases new single, "R.E.G.O." announces tour|website=NME|date=6 April 2021|access-date=10 April 2021}}</ref>

In July 2021, Fields released "Song About a Boy" and announced her debut album is due for release on 24 September 2021.<ref name="Been NME">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/ruby-fields-announces-debut-album-been-doin-it-for-a-bit-shares-new-single-2992080|title=Ruby Fields announces debut album ''Been Doin It for a Bit'', shares new single|website=[[NME Australia]]|date=13 July 2021|access-date=16 July 2021|author=Young, David James}}</ref> ''[[Been Doin' It for a Bit]]'' debuted at number 1 on the [[ARIA Charts]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.aria.com.au/charts/news/ruby-fields-lands-debut-aria-1-album|title=Ruby Fields lands debut ARIA #1 album|website=[[ARIA Charts]]|date=1 October 2021|access-date=16 October 2021}}</ref>
