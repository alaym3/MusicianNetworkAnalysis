'''Mitchell Quilleon Brown''', known by his stage name '''Kid Quill''', is an American rapper from [[Shelbyville, Indiana]]. The name ''Kid Quill'' originates from his middle name Quilleon with the addition of ''Kid'' to the beginning.<ref name="Armstrong">{{Cite web|url=http://www.nuvo.net/indianapolis/kid-quill-at-irving-theater-tonight/Content?oid=2863116|title=Kid Quill at Irving Theater tonight|last=Armstrong|first=Teryn|website=NUVO|access-date=April 14, 2016}}</ref> He made his first notable appearance with his 2016 album ''The Name Above The Title'' charting on the Top 10 iTunes Hip-Hop/Rap charts and Billboard Heatseekers. In September 2017, Brown released his second studio album, 94.3 The Reel, that returned to the Top 10 on iTunes Hip-Hop/Rap chart.

== Soccer career ==
While attending [[Shelbyville Senior High School]], Brown was a 3-time Hoosier Heritage All-Conference First Team, 2-time Indiana Soccer Coaches Association Top Team Player Awards, 2-time Indiana All-District First Team, 3-time ''Top Drawer'' Soccer Player to Watch, 2-time ''[[The Indianapolis Star|Indianapolis Star]]'' Super Team, 2011 Indiana All-State 3rd Team Selection, and 2012 [[National Soccer Coaches Association of America|NSCAA]] Soccer All-American Scholar.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nscaa.com/news/2011/12/2011-boys-high-school-scholar-all-west-team|title=2011 Boys High School Scholar All-West Team|website=www.nscaa.com|access-date=April 13, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://depauwtigers.com/sports/msoc/2013-14/bios/brown_mitch_01ce|title=Mitch Brown ‚Äì  DePauw Athletics|website=depauwtigers.com|access-date=April 13, 2016}}</ref>

He decided to continue his soccer career by playing for [[DePauw University]] at the collegiate level. He had three appearances in his freshman year, including playing in the NCAA Tournament, and two in his sophomore year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.depauwtigers.com/sports/msoc/2012-13/releases/depauw-bows-out-of-ncaa-play-after.html|title=DePauw Bows out of NCAA Play after Second-Round Loss  ‚Äì  DePauw Athletics|website=www.depauwtigers.com|access-date=April 13, 2016}}</ref>

His junior year, Brown decided to quit soccer because the balance of music, soccer, and school was too much.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.shelbynews.com/news/article_78c856a6-09eb-11e4-8853-001a4bcf887a.html|title=Kid Quill grinning from 'Ear to Ear'|website=Shelby News|access-date=April 14, 2016}}</ref>

== Music career ==
While in high school, Brown would rap battle friends in his basement. One of his friends decided to record Brown rapping and uploaded it to YouTube where it received a good number of views.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hercampus.com/school/depauw/mitch-brown-16|title=Mitch Brown '16|website=Her Campus|access-date=April 14, 2016}}</ref> Noticing that there was potential, he decided to pursue rapping.

On October 14, 2016, Brown released his debut album ''The Name Above The Title''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.shelbynews.com/sports/article_1827d025-32a3-5ed3-aeb4-018f6bf3d667.html|title=Sportswrap|newspaper=Shelby News|access-date=October 15, 2016}}</ref> It charted Top 10 on iTunes Hip-Hop/Rap. It also charted as a Billboard Heatseaker.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Flint|first1=Ross|title=Local Artist Reaches Top 10|url=http://www.shelbynews.com/news/article_553fb4f0-50e3-5552-bb0b-e212c708bd1c.html|website=shelbynews.com|publisher=Paxton Media Group|accessdate=October 29, 2016}}</ref>

In September 2017, Brown followed his first album with ''94.3 The Reel.'' This also charted Top 10 on iTunes Hip-Hop/Rap Charts. He spent the following year as support on nationals tours with [[Devvon Terrell]], [[SoMo]], [[Skizzy Mars]] and [[Bryce Vine]].

On September 6, 2019, Brown released his fourth album, titled Sunset Diner, inspired by his adolescence, 80s and 90s music and his life experiences.

On September 25, September 26 and September 27th 2020, Brown opened for [[Quinn XCII]] and [[Chelsea Cutler]] at their drive in shows at [[Charlotte Motor Speedway]] and The Westland Mall.<ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1309209881761722368|user=KidQuill|title=This weekend üöò‚ù§Ô∏è|date=Sep 24, 2020|author=}}</ref>
