'''Wolf & Bear''' is an American [[post-hardcore]] band formed in [[Sacramento]], [[California]], in 2013. The band currently consists of vocalists Marcus Cisneros and Tyler Watt, lead guitarist Cameron Nunez, rhythm guitarist Louie Baltazar, drummer Jacob Koval, and bass guitarist Tim Feerick. The band originated in the [[Central Valley (California)|Central Valley]] [[hardcore punk]] music scene. The band signed to [[Blue Swan Records]] and released a [[split album|split]] with Adventurer in May 2016 and their debut studio album, ''Everything Is Going Grey'', in October 2017.

== Career ==

Wolf & Bear formed in [[Sacramento, California]] aiming to "expand on the [[progressive rock]] genre", in 2014.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=http://switchbitchnoise.com/wolf-bear-sign-to-blue-swan-records/|title=Wolf & Bear Sign to Blue Swan Records|first=Carly|last=Stuber|date=January 8, 2016|website=Switchbitchnoise.com|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref> The band originally consisted of vocalists Sam Kohl and Tyler Watt, lead guitarist Steven Fernandes, rhythm guitarist Cameron Nunez, and bass guitarist Ben Jovi. The band released their debut song, "Oil Cup", on January 27, 2015, featuring clean vocals from Sam Kohl, now in Sea in the Sky.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Oil-Cup/dp/B06VSQPW16|title=Oil Cup|accessdate=June 5, 2021|via=Amazon}}</ref> It was recorded at Pus Cavern Recording Studios in late 2014 with engineers Trent Hollingsworth and Nick Scott.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wolfandbearmusic.bandcamp.com/track/oil-cup|title=Oil Cup, by Wolf & Bear|website=Wolf & Bear|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref>

2015 brought a line-up change following the departure of Sam Kohl, Steven Fernandes, and Ben Jovi. They were subsequently replaced by vocalist Marcus Cisneros, guitarist Louie Baltazar, and bass guitarist Nathaniel Duarte. They released their second single, "Sight", on September 8, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wolfandbearmusic.bandcamp.com/track/sight|title=Sight, by Wolf & Bear|website=Wolf & Bear|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref>

On January 6, 2016, they signed to [[Will Swan (musician)|Will Swan]]'s record label [[Blue Swan Records]].<ref name="auto"/> On March 29, 2016, the band released their single, "Different Fires", on the label. They released ''Wolf & Bear x Adventurer'', a [[split album|split]] [[extended play|EP]] with label-mates Adventurer, on May 20, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blueswanrecords.bandcamp.com/album/wolf-bear-x-adventurer|title=Wolf & Bear x Adventurer, by Blue Swan Records|website=Blue Swan Records|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref>

The band appeared on American [[post-hardcore]] band [[Dance Gavin Dance]]'s 10-year anniversary tour in the United States in December 2015, alongside [[Strawberry Girls]], [[Slaves (American band)|Slaves]], [[A Lot Like Birds]], and [[Dayshell]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://imgur.com/bCGGUD7|title="For the Sacramento, CA date (Dec. 19th) on the DGD-10 Year Anniversary Tour, the band, Wolf &amp; Bear, are making a guest appearance on the lineup." by HelloDepression in dancegavindance|website=Imgur.com|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref>

On June 8, 2017, they released the single "GreyBlood", their first with bass guitarist Tim Feerick, along with its music video, which premiered on ''[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/wolf_and_bear_greyblood_music_video/|title=Wolf & Bear premiere new video "GreyBlood"—and, yes, that's a Dance Gavin Dance member you see|date=June 8, 2017|website=Altpress.com|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref> On September 10, 2017, it was confirmed by Blue Swan Records that Wolf & Bear is scheduled to released their debut studio album, ''Everything Is Going Grey'', on October 3, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/wolfandbearband/posts/1819775564714195|title=Wolf & Bear|website=Facebook.com|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref>

On June 5, 2018, the band released a five-track [[live album]], ''Grey Sessions,'' recorded at Pus Cavern in Sacramento, California.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/joshunitt/posts/1386543948112011|title=Joshua Unitt|website=Facebook.com|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://wolfandbearmusic.bandcamp.com/album/grey-sessions|title=Grey Sessions, by Wolf & Bear|website=Wolf & Bear|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref> From January 11 to 19, 2019, they toured in support of [[Sianvar]]'s 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/wolfandbearband/posts/2534520429906368|title=Wolf & Bear|website=Facebook.com|accessdate=February 23, 2019}}</ref> On February 28, 2019, they released the lead single, "Deleto", along with its music video, from an upcoming EP,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1IZekKaWFcE|title=Wolf & Bear - "Deleto" (Official Music Video) &#124; BVTV Music|accessdate=June 5, 2021|via=www.youtube.com}}</ref> and the second and third singles, "Red Hen" and "Lifeguard", on June 4, and July 18, 2019, respectively. The group's self-titled EP followed on August 14, 2019, becoming their first release since being dropped by Blue Swan.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/ep-ep/1475909152|title=Ep - EP by Wolf & Bear|accessdate=June 5, 2021|via=music.apple.com}}</ref>
