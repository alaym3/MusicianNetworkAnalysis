'''Jeffry Wayne''' (born 1 July 1943) is an American-born naturalized British composer, musician and lyricist.<ref name="Larkin">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Virgin Encyclopedia of Popular Music]]|editor=Colin Larkin|editor-link=Colin Larkin (writer)|publisher=[[Virgin Books]]|date=1997|edition=Concise|isbn=1-85227-745-9|page=1237}}</ref> In 1978, he released ''[[Jeff Wayne's Musical Version of The War of the Worlds]]'', his musical adaptation of [[H. G. Wells]]' science-fiction novel ''[[The War of the Worlds]]''. Wayne wrote approximately 3,000 advertising jingles in the 1970s which appeared on television in the United Kingdom, including a [[Gordon's Gin]] commercial which was covered by [[The Human League]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/money-jeff-wayne|title=Money: Jeff Wayne|last=Lester|first=Paul|date=16 July 2014|website=Classic Rock Magazine|language=en|access-date=2020-04-24}}</ref> Wayne also composed numerous television themes, including ''[[Good Morning Britain (1983)|Good Morning Britain]]'' ([[TV-am]]), ITV's ''[[The Big Match]]'' and ''[[World of Sport (UK TV series)|World of Sport]]'', BBC's ''[[Sixty Minutes (UK TV programme)|Sixty Minutes]]'', and for 24 years, the UK's first news radio station, [[LBC]].

Wayne wrote feature film and documentary film scores and was musical director for various artists. Wayne published a book called ''The Book of Tennis'' and created, produced and scored eight thirty-minute episodes of ''The Book of Tennis Chronicles'' that was distributed by [[Fox Sports (USA)|Fox Sports]] in approximately twenty countries, and was broadcast in the US on [[The Tennis Channel]] between 2005 and 2008.

==Early life==
Jeff Wayne was born on 1 July 1943 in [[Forest Hills, Queens|Forest Hills]], [[Queens]], [[New York (state)|New York]], United States.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sonybmgmusic.co.uk/artists/jeff_wayne/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090616152020/http://www.sonybmgmusic.co.uk/artists/jeff_wayne/|url-status=dead|archive-date=16 June 2009|title=Sony Music UK &#124; Artists &#124; Jeff Wayne|date=16 June 2009}}</ref> His father, Jerry Wayne ''(né'' Jerome Marvin Krauth; 1916–1996), was an actor, singer, and theatre producer. Jeff Wayne spent four years of his childhood in the UK when his father played romantic gambler Sky Masterson in the original West End musical production of ''[[Guys and Dolls]]''.{{citation needed|date=December 2016}}

Four years later they returned to New York. Wayne graduated from Stephen Halsey Jr. High and attended [[Forest Hills High School (New York)|Forest Hills High School]] for one year, and played for its tennis team, before moving to California. Wayne graduated from [[Grant High School (Los Angeles)|Grant High School]], then from [[Los Angeles Valley College]] with a journalism degree. He played keyboards in local bands and coached tennis to support himself. After completing his journalism degree he switched to music, playing keyboards briefly with [[The Sandpipers]].<ref name=GB>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_NNmFiUnSmUC&q=jeff+wayne+the+sandpipers&pg=PA3839|date=2011|publisher=Ominbus Press|title=The Encyclopedia of Popular Music|page=3839|author=Larkin, Colin|isbn=9780857125958}}</ref>

==Musical career==
In 1966, Wayne composed the score for his father's West End musical ''Two Cities'' based on [[Charles Dickens]]' ''[[A Tale of Two Cities]]'' which ran at London's Palace Theatre.<ref name="Larkin"/> The musical was successful, winning for [[Edward Woodward]] the ''[[Evening Standard]]'' Award for Best Male Performance in a Musical for 1968–69. Returning to the UK, Wayne became a record producer and helped produce [[David Essex]]'s album ''[[Rock On (David Essex album)|Rock On]]''.<ref name="Larkin"/> Essex was a [[voice actor]] in ''The War of the Worlds'', playing the part of the Artilleryman. Wayne also provided the score for the 1977 action thriller ''[[Golden Rendezvous]]'', which starred [[Richard Harris]].

===''The War of the Worlds''===
In 1978, ''[[Jeff Wayne's Musical Version of The War of the Worlds]]'' was released, achieving international success.<ref name="Larkin"/> Wayne's two-disc composition sold millions of copies around the world<ref name="The Story">[http://www.thewaroftheworlds.com/thestory/default.aspx ''Jeff Wayne's Musical Version of The War of the Worlds'' website], ''The Story'', Chap.14.</ref> and was the 40th best selling album of all time in the UK by 2009.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.bpi.co.uk/assets/files/top%2040%20albums.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=23 March 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100924031126/http://bpi.co.uk/assets/files/top%2040%20albums.pdf |archive-date=24 September 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref> It included worldwide hit singles: "The Eve of the War" and "[[Forever Autumn (song)|Forever Autumn]]", both sung by [[Justin Hayward]], and included narration throughout by [[Richard Burton]].  It won two [[Ivor Novello Award]]s and The Best Recording in Science Fiction and Fantasy (the judges included [[Steven Spielberg]], [[George Lucas]], and [[Alfred Hitchcock]]). "The Eve of the War" was used by the offshore radio station [[Radio Delmare]] as its theme tune. The album was re-released on SACD in 2005 [[Super Audio CD|audio CD]]; five tracks were remastered and placed on the second disc (mostly without Wayne's input). A Limited Edition 7-disc Collector's Edition CD and DVD box set, which included remixes, outtakes, and documentary, was issued in 2005.

Wayne announced a new ''Musical Version of The War of the Worlds'' album to be released in June 2012 titled ''[[Jeff Wayne's Musical Version of The War of the Worlds – The New Generation]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thewaroftheworlds.com/news/newsstory.aspx?id=161 |title=The War of the Worlds...The New Generation|publisher=Official Website|date=18 November 2011}}</ref> The album was released in November 2012.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/jeff-waynes-musical-version-of-the-war-of-the-worldsthe-new-generation-134105953.html |title=Jeff Wayne's Musical Version of The War of the Worlds...The New Generation!|publisher=PR Newswire United Business Media|date=18 November 2012}}</ref>

===Later work===
After ''The War of the Worlds'', Wayne continued to compose and produce music, such as the music for the 1979 television film ''[[The Knowledge (film)|The Knowledge]]'' and The Who's movie ''[[McVicar (film)|McVicar]]'' released in 1980. Wayne composed music for the [[Fry's Turkish Delight]] advertisement in 1981, the theme for ''[[Good Morning Britain (1983)|Good Morning Britain]]'' in 1982 on [[TV-am]], and music for the 1984 album ''Beyond the Planets'',<ref name="Larkin"/> Kevin Peek and [[Rick Wakeman]]'s [[progressive rock]] recording of [[Planet Suite|Holst's orchestral suite]].

In 1992 Wayne released ''[[Jeff Wayne's Musical Version of Spartacus]]'', his first major release since ''The War of the Worlds''.<ref name="Larkin"/> Its cast included [[Anthony Hopkins]], [[Catherine Zeta-Jones]], [[Ladysmith Black Mambazo]], and [[Marillion]]'s [[Fish (singer)|Fish]].<ref name="Larkin"/> It featured a [[libretto]] by [[Gary Osborne]] and a story combining powerful themes of oppression, desperation, love and death

Wayne was involved in producing ''[[Jeff Wayne's The War of the Worlds (1998 video game)|Jeff Wayne's The War of the Worlds]]'' in 1998, a computer game that included 45 minutes of music from his ''Musical Version of The War of the Worlds'', re-scored and [[remix]]ed in an [[electronica]] style with [[techno]] beats. The game also featured computer-generated visualisations of numerous scenes from the album – an idea Wayne would later revisit. In 1999, a related game, also titled ''[[Jeff Wayne's The War of the Worlds (1999 video game)|Jeff Wayne's The War of the Worlds]]'', was released for the [[PlayStation (console)|Sony PlayStation]].

''The War of the Worlds'' was re-released in the summer of 2005. It spent ten consecutive weeks in the Top 10 of the UK Album charts. Including this re-release, the album achieved sales to 3 million in the UK, and approximately 14 million worldwide. Also in 2005, it was announced that the musical would be going on a UK tour in April 2006. The live show was taken to Australia and New Zealand and returned the UK during December 2007. The show, produced by [[Damian Collier]], used a ten-piece band and a 48-piece string orchestra, voice actors, screen-projected images and [[animatronics]]. [[Universal Pictures]] released a DVD of the show, filmed at [[Wembley Arena]] using 23 [[high-definition video|high definition]] cameras and directed by [[David Mallet (director)|David Mallet]].

Wayne's first television series, ''The Book of Tennis Chronicles'', was broadcast worldwide in 2005 and distributed by Fox Sports. It features well-known tennis players and events, set against events in world history from 1877 to 2005. Wayne created and produced eight half-hour episodes and scored its music. The series' executive producer was Damian Collier.

==Tennis==
A keen player and follower of tennis throughout his life, Wayne remains involved with the sport; he has won the British National Indoor Veterans singles and doubles titles, and the National Clay Court doubles. In 1992 he partnered former Great Britain No. 1 and [[Davis Cup]] captain [[Roger Taylor (tennis)|Roger Taylor]] at the European Veteran Championships. In 1999, Wayne represented Great Britain in the Austria Cup in Spain, and achieved an ITF world ranking in his age group. In 2008 Wayne was elected into the International Lawn Tennis Club of Great Britain. Wayne has also played in numerous fundraising pro-am tournaments, including for [[Muscular dystrophy]] and [[Save the Children]].

==Personal life==
Wayne and his wife, Geraldine, live in [[Hertfordshire]], England, and have two daughters: Anna-Marie (an actress) and Jemma; and two sons: Zeb (a DJ, and married to [[Olivia Wayne]]), and Joab (a junior tennis player).<ref name="WOW Website">{{cite web|title=The War of the Worlds Official Website|url=http://www.thewaroftheworlds.com/thestory/default.aspx|pages=210–211|website=Thewaroftheworlds.com|access-date=13 March 2011}}</ref>
