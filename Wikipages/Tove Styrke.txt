'''Tove Anna Linnéa Östman Styrke'''<ref name="tv4">{{cite web | url = http://tove.idol.tv4.se/2009/10/21/fragestund-del-1/ | title = Frågestund – del 1 | language = sv | access-date = 20 December 2011 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20120426062944/http://tove.idol.tv4.se/2009/10/21/fragestund-del-1/ | archive-date = 26 April 2012 | url-status = dead | df = dmy-all }}</ref> ({{IPA-sv|ˈtǔːvɛ ˈstʏ̂rːkɛ}}; born 19 November 1992<ref name="tv4" />) is a [[Swedes|Swedish]] singer and songwriter. She gained popularity as a contestant on ''[[Idol (Swedish TV series)|Swedish Idol 2009]]'', finishing in third place.<ref>[[:sv:Idol 2009#Utröstningsschema|Third place on Idol 2009]]</ref> After the show, she started a solo career in [[electropop]]. She released her debut album ''[[Tove Styrke (album)|Tove Styrke]]'' in 2010.  The ''[[New York Post]]'' included Styrke in its list of "10 Artists to Know in 2011".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nypost.com/p/blogs/popwrap/artists_to_watch_in_XI4rWo1GWKVR3TW2CouR5J |title=10 Artists to Know in 2011: New Music Musts |publisher=Nypost.com |access-date=13 December 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111204200034/http://www.nypost.com/p/blogs/popwrap/artists_to_watch_in_XI4rWo1GWKVR3TW2CouR5J |archive-date=4 December 2011 }}</ref> In 2015, she released her second album, ''[[Kiddo (album)|Kiddo]]''. Three years later, in 2018, she released her third album, ''[[Sway (Tove Styrke album)|Sway]]''. Throughout the 2010s Styrke gained an increasing critical acclaim as an independent artist resulting in her single "[[Say My Name (Tove Styrke song)|Say My Name]]" being included in ''[[Rolling Stone]]''{{'}}s 100 best songs of the decade.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/the-100-best-songs-of-the-2010s-917532/|title=The 100 Best Songs of the 2010s|website=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=4 December 2019}}</ref>

==Biography==
Styrke was born in [[Umeå]], [[Sweden]]. She has two sisters, one younger and one older.<ref name="tv4" /><ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.expressen.se/noje/1.2356715/tove-styrke-hastigt-sjuk | title = Tove Styrke hastigt sjuk | language = sv | access-date = 20 December 2011 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110918124541/http://www.expressen.se/noje/1.2356715/tove-styrke-hastigt-sjuk | archive-date = 18 September 2011 | url-status = dead | df = dmy-all }}</ref> Her father, musician Anders Östman, scored a number one hit on the Swedish radio chart, [[Svensktoppen]], in 1975 with the song "Vindens melodi" performed by his [[dansband]] Max Fenders. Her mother was a [[ballet]] instructor and Styrke took classes from her at an early age.

==Music career==

===''Tove Styrke'' and "Call My Name" (2009–2011)===
After competing on ''Swedish Idol 2009'' and finishing in third place, Styrke was signed by [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony Music]] label in December 2009.<ref name="toveblog">{{cite web|url=http://tovestyrke.weebly.com |title=Tove Styrke – Home | publisher=Tovestyrke.weebly.com |date=30 June 2010 |access-date=13 December 2011}}</ref> In June 2010, she released her first single, "[[Million Pieces]]", which was co-written by Adam Olenius from [[Shout Out Louds]] and [[Lykke Li]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mynewsdesk.com/se/pressroom/sonybmg/pressrelease/view/sony-music-presenterar-stolt-tove-styrke-428586 |title=Sony Music – Sony Music presenterar stolt: Tove Styrke | language=sv | publisher=Mynewsdesk |date=22 June 2010 |access-date=13 December 2011}}</ref>
[[File:Tove Styrke2.jpg|thumb|Styrke in 2010]]
On 12 November 2010, she released her debut album, ''[[Tove Styrke (album)|Tove Styrke]]''. The album was produced by Patrik Berger, Lotus & 2manyfreckles, [[Peter Ågren]], Janne Kask and Paw. It peaked at number 10 on the Swedish single chart, spending 35 weeks on the top album charts in Sweden.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://swedishcharts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Tove+Styrke|title=swedishcharts.com - Discography Tove Styrke|website=swedishcharts.com|access-date=30 January 2019}}</ref> It was first certified gold, then later received platinum certification in Sweden. The majority of the album was co-written by Styrke.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://swedishcharts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Tove+Styrke |title=Swedish Charts: Tove Styrke | publisher=Swedish Charts |date=2012|access-date=13 November 2014}}</ref> The album was later re-released in March 2012 in [[Germany]] with two new songs: "[[Call My Name (Tove Styrke song)|Call My Name]]" and "Sticks and Stones". Her second single, "[[White Light Moment]]", charted at number five on the Swedish top singles chart and received gold certification. It eventually peaked at number two.<ref>{{cite web|author=Steffen Hung |url=http://hitparad.se/showitem.asp?interpret=Tove+Styrke&titel=White+Light+Moment&cat=s |title=Tove Styrke – White Light Moment |publisher=hitparad.se |language=sv|access-date=13 December 2011}}</ref> "[[White Light Moment]]" was later nominated for "Best Song of the Year" at [[Grammis]]galan 2012, the Swedish equivalent of the [[Grammy Award]]s.

In January 2011, Styrke won the "Best Newcomer" award at P3 Guld.<ref>[[:sv:P3 Guld#2011|"Best Newcomer" awards at P3 Guld]]</ref> At ceremony, she performed a remixed version of "[[Million Pieces]]" by Swedish record producer [[Familjen]]. In February 2011, Styrke issued her first [[extended play|EP]], ''High and Low'', featuring six remixes of the title track. In April 2011, Styrke was featured in an [[IKEA]] commercial, where she performed the Swedish lullaby "Byssan Lull".<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.resume.se/nyheter/2011/04/26/vaggvisor/index.xml | title = Ikeazzz nya kampanj | language = sv | access-date = 20 December 2011 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20141129051055/http://www.resume.se/nyheter/2011/04/26/vaggvisor/index.xml | archive-date = 29 November 2014 | df = dmy-all}}</ref>

On 19 August 2011, the promotional single "[[Call My Name (Tove Styrke song)|Call My Name]]" was released on [[music download|digital download]]. The song peaked at number six on the Swedish Singles Chart. "[[Call My Name (Tove Styrke song)|Call My Name]]" was later nominated for "Best Song of the Year" at P3 Guld.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sverigesradio.se/sida/gruppsida.aspx?programid=3964&grupp=13044 |title=Årets låt – P3 Guld 2012 &#124; Sveriges Radio |publisher=Sverigesradio.se |language=sv|access-date=13 December 2011}}</ref> That same month, Stryke joined other Swedish artists, collectively called Kedjan, to release a [[charity record|charity single]] called "Ringar på vattnet" ("Ripple Effect" in English), benefiting [[Radiohjälpen]]'s charity efforts fighting [[famine]] in the [[Horn of Africa]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tovestyrkemusic.com/en/node/213 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120723090530/http://tovestyrkemusic.com/en/node/213 |url-status=dead |archive-date=23 July 2012 |title=Tove medverkar i Kedjans Ringar på vattnet |language=sv|access-date=2 January 2012}}</ref> In September 2011, the Swedish band Caotico collaborated with Styrke on their single released  "Brains Out". She also appeared in the [[music video]] for the single.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.scandipop.co.uk/?p=6963 | title = Scandipop.co.uk – Caotico & Tove Styrke: Brains Out | access-date = 20 December 2011}}</ref>

===''Borderline'' (2013–2014)===
In 2013, Styrke signed a contract with [[Sony Music UK]]. On 22 May 2014 she released her comeback single, "Even If I'm Loud Doesn't Mean I'm Talking To You", on iTunes in Sweden. On 9 October 2014, her next single "Borderline", the first from her upcoming EP of the same title, was released on iTunes. A remixed version of "Borderline" was previously released for the Swedish [[compilation album]], ''F!'' on 22 April 2014 on iTunes in Sweden and for online streaming on [[Spotify]]. The music video for "Borderline" was filmed in [[Pyramiden]] in September 2014 and premiered exclusively on website, "Fader", on 15 October 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url =http://www.thefader.com/2014/10/15/video-tove-stryke-borderline| title = Tove Styrke Travels To The End Of The World In "Borderline" Video| access-date = 15 October 2014}}</ref>

Styrke performed songs from the EP and new material at two debut UK shows in [[London]] on 5 and 6 November. The song "Brag" premiered exclusively for [[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]] on 20 November 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url =http://www.nylon.com/articles/song-premiere-tove-styrke-brag| title = Tove Styrke Is Sweden's Next Big Music Export| access-date = 20 November 2014}}</ref> The ''[[Borderline (Tove Styrke EP)|Borderline EP]]'' was released for digital download on 23 November 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/listen-tove-styrke-borderline| title = Sweden's Tove Styrke returns with ballsy new single "Borderline"| access-date = 9 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url = http://feministisktinitiativ.se/valskivan-f/| title = Här är alla artister på vår valskiva "F!"| access-date = 9 October 2014| url-status = dead| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20141019183618/http://feministisktinitiativ.se/valskivan-f/| archive-date = 19 October 2014| df = dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/the-morning-after/tove-styrke-the-old-blue-last| title = Tove Styrke plays The Old Blue Last for sold out comeback show| access-date = 19 November 2014}}</ref> On 17 November 2014, [[RCA Records]] announced that Styrke  had signed with the label. The label confirmed Styrke's plans to release her first US album via RCA and Sony Sweden in the spring of 2015.<ref name="rcarecords.com">{{cite web | url =http://www.rcarecords.com/news/rca-records-signs-critically-acclaimed-swedish-recording-artist-tove-styrke| title =RCA RECORDS SIGNS CRITICALLY-ACCLAIMED SWEDISH RECORDING ARTIST TOVE STYRKE|website=Rcarecords.com| access-date = 17 November 2014}}</ref>

===''Kiddo'' and ''Sway'' (2015–present)===
[[File:Tove Styrke på Malmöfestivalen 2018 01.jpg|thumb|Tove Styrke at Malmöfestivalen 2018.]]
On 23 January 2015, Styrke premiered the single "Ego" from her second album, ''[[Kiddo (album)|Kiddo]]''. The single was available for streaming via her official [[SoundCloud]] account and later was released that same day on iTunes. The music video for "Ego" was filmed in [[Tokyo]] and premiered on Styrke's Vevo channel on 2 February 2015.<ref name="time.com">{{cite news |url=http://time.com/3691280/tove-styrke-ego-video/ |title=Meet the Swedish Pop Star You Need to Know This Spring |access-date=23 February 2015 | website=Time.com |first=Nolan |last=Feeney |date=2 February 2015}}</ref>

''Kiddo'' was released in digital, vinyl, and physical formats on 8 June 2015.<ref name=itunes>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/kiddo/984516091|title=Kiddo by Tove Styrke|website=[[Apple Music]]}}</ref> The next single "Number One" was released for streaming on [[Spotify]] and digital download for [[iTunes]] on 13 May 2015 and was featured on the [[The CW|CW]]'s ''[[Riverdale (2017 TV series)|Riverdale]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/here-s-all-the-music-from-riverdale-season-3|title=Here's All The Music From 'Riverdale' Season 3|work=[[Genius (website)|Genius]]|first=Eddie|last=Fu|date=15 October 2018}}</ref>  To promote the album, Styrke attended the annual [[South by Southwest|SXSW]] festival in March 2015;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.populartv.com/music/tove-styrke-sxsw-diary/|title=SXSW Outfit Diary: Tove Styrke|work=[[Popular, Inc.|Popular TV]]|first=Liza|last=Darwin|date=24 March 2015}}</ref> she later embarked on a small tour of the US in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/tove-styrke|title=Tove Styrke|work=[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]]|first=Emily|last=McDermott|date=8 June 2015}}</ref>

Four days prior to its release, ''Kiddo'' exclusively streamed in its entirety for Spotify listeners in Sweden on 5 June 2015.<ref name="gp.se">{{cite web|url=https://www.gp.se/kulturnoje/recensioner/musik/musik4fyrar/1.2737157-tove-styrke-kiddo|title=Tove Styrke - Kiddo|work=[[Göteborgs-Posten]]|first=Johan|last=Lindqvist|date=15 June 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160201074230/https://www.gp.se/kulturnoje/recensioner/musik/musik4fyrar/1.2737157-tove-styrke-kiddo|archive-date=1 February 2016|access-date=5 June 2015}}</ref><ref name="pigeonsandplanes">{{cite web |url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2015/05/tove-styrke-interview|title=Tove Styrke Speaks on Her Re-Emergence in Music and Her Upcoming Album and Tour|work=[[Pigeons and Planes]]|publisher=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|first=Katie|last=Kelly|date=1 May 2015|access-date=5 June 2015}}</ref><ref name="thelineofbestfit.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/tove-styrke-announces-sophomore-lp-kiddo-streams-new-track-ego|title=Tove Styrke announces sophomore LP Kiddo, streams new track "Ego"|work=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|first=Laurence|last=Day|date=23 January 2015|access-date=23 January 2015}}</ref><ref name="vk.se">{{cite web|url=http://www.vk.se/1447426/ny-singel-fran-tove-styrke|title=Ny singel från Tove Styrke|work=[[Västerbottens-Kuriren]]|first=Evelina|last=Åberg|date=13 May 2015|access-date=13 May 2015}}</ref>

On 23 July 2015, Stryke released a cover version of "[[...Baby One More Time (song)|...Baby One More Time]]" by [[Britney Spears]] as a single along its music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/kelleydunlap/tove-styrke-britney-spears-baby-one-more-time-cover|title=This Alt-Pop Cover Of Britney Spears' "... Baby One More Time" Is Everything|work=[[BuzzFeed]]|first=Kelley|last=Dunlap|date=24 June 2015}}</ref>

On 28 April 2017, Styrke released "[[Say My Name (Tove Styrke song)|Say My Name]]", which would serve as the first single from her third album, ''[[Sway (Tove Styrke album)|Sway]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/tove-styrke-say-my-name-2061960|title=New Music Of The Day: Tove Styrke: ‘Say My Name’|work=[[NME]]|first=Thomas|last=Smith|date=28 April 2017}}</ref> "Mistakes" followed as the second single on 15 September,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/tove-styrke/news/tove-styrke-is-a-runaway-bride-in-the-epic-mistakes-video|title=Tove Styrke Is A Runaway Bride In The Epic 'Mistakes' Video|work=[[MTV UK]]|first=Ross|last=McNeilage|date=4 October 2017}}</ref> with "Liability (Demo)" being released as a promotional track on 15 December.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/tove-styrke/13682|title=Listen: Tove Styrke covers Lorde’s 'Liability'.|work=Coup De Main|first=Rose|last=Riddell|date=14 December 2017}}</ref> "Changed My Mind" was released as the third single alongside the album announcement on 2 February 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://time.com/5128910/5-songs-you-need-to-listen-to-this-week-40/|title=5 Songs You Need to Listen to This Week|work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|first=Raisa|last=Bruner|date=2 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mynewsdesk.com/se/sonybmg/pressreleases/p3-guld-vinnaren-tove-styrke-slaepper-singeln-changed-my-mind-och-releasedatum-foer-kommande-albumet-sway-2386820|title=P3 Guld-vinnaren Tove Styrke släpper singeln ”Changed My Mind” och releasedatum för kommande albumet ”Sway”|work=[[Mynewsdesk]]|date=2 February 2018|language=sv}}</ref>  Before the album's release, the single "On the Low" came out.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mynewsdesk.com/se/sonybmg/pressreleases/tove-styrke-slaepper-singeln-on-the-low-fraan-kommande-albumet-sway-paa-scen-med-lorde-sjungandes-robyn-cover-2469855|title=Tove Styrke släpper singeln ”On The Low” från kommande albumet ”Sway” - på scen med Lorde sjungandes Robyn cover|work=Mynewsdesk|date=6 April 2018|language=sv}}</ref> ''Sway'' was released on 4 May 2018 through Sony Music.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8454628/tove-styrke-interview-sway|title=Tove Styrke Talks New Album 'Sway,' Touring With Lorde and Katy Perry: 'Oh My God, Is This Real Life?'|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Gab|last=Ginsberg|date=4 May 2018}}</ref> It was re-released digitally, including the single "Vibe", unveiled on 19 October 2018 ahead of a tour through United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://readdork.com/news/tove-styrke-has-released-a-vibe-new-single-ahead-of-her-upcoming-tour-dates/|title=Tove Styrke has released a vibey new single ahead of her upcoming tour dates|work=[[Dork (magazine)|Dork]]|date= 19 October 2018}}</ref>

Through 2018 and 2020 she released several collaborations, including a feature on [[Alma (Finnish singer)|Alma]]'s mixtape ''[[Heavy Rules (mixtape)|Heavy Rules]]'' called "Good Vibes",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/alma-heavy-rules-mixtape-2541787334.html|title= ALMA Is a Legend For Life|work=Paper|first=Justin|last=Moran|date=2 March 2018}}</ref> "Last Goodbye" by [[Clean Bandit]] also featuring [[Stefflon Don]] from their album ''[[What Is Love? (Clean Bandit album)|What Is Love?]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/clean-bandit-what-is-love-review-rita-ora-sean-paul-demi-lovato-ellie-goulding-a8655831.html|title=Clean Bandit, What is Love? review: A shallow, algorithm-friendly array of synthetic pop devotion|work=[[The Independent]]|first=Mark|last=Beaumont|date=29 November 2018|access-date=13 January 2019}}</ref> "Stuck" with [[Lost Kings]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.iheart.com/featured/maxwell/content/2018-08-07-maxwells-music-video-cameo-in-lost-kings-stuck/|title=Maxwell's Music Video Cameo in Lost Kings' - Stuck|work=[[iHeartRadio]]|author=Maxwell|date=8 August 2018}}</ref> "Been There, Done That" with [[NOTD]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8472119/notd-tove-styrke-been-there-done-that|title=Notd & Tove Styrke Are Stuck on Love With 'Been There Done That': Listen|work=Billboard|first=Kat|last=Bein|date=August 24, 2018}}</ref> and "Love Me Back" with [[Ritual (electronic band)|Ritual]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.idolator.com/7691047/tove-styrke-ritual-love-me-back-video-review|title=Tove Styrke Teams Up With RITUAL For Massive Banger, “Love Me Back”|work=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|first=Mike|last=Wass|date=29 January 2019}}</ref> She also participated in the eleventh series of the Swedish [[reality television]] show ''[[Så mycket bättre]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tv4.se/artikel/5ec3ead04bd8e56f4819e13c/tove-styrke-ansluter-till-sa-mycket-battre-2020|title=Tove Styrke ansluter till Så mycket bättre 2020|date=20 May 2020|work=[[TV 4 (Sweden)|TV 4]]|language=sv}}</ref> 

On 19 June 2021 she released the single "Mood Swings", teasing an upcoming project.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/tove-styrke-unveils-first-single-of-2021-mood-swings|title=Tove Styrke unveils first single of 2021 “Mood Swings”|work=The Line of Best Fit|first=Cerys|last=Kenneally|date=18 June 2021}}</ref>

==Influences==
According to [[AllMusic]], Styrke combines "the bubbling electronica of [[Robyn]] with the hipster synth pop of [[Annie (Norwegian singer)|Annie]] and the hook-laden Euro-disco of [[September (singer)|September]]".<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tove-styrke-p2301962/biography |title=Tove Styrke – Allmusic |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=13 December 2011}}</ref> The German ''Kulturspiegel'', a monthly extra to ''[[Der Spiegel]]'', called Styrke's music "nonchalant, catchy boom-pop" and compared it to Robyn and [[Lykke Li]].<ref>{{cite journal|title=Wumms-Pop |first=Christoph |last=Dallach | language=de |journal=[[Der Spiegel|Kulturspiegel]] |issue=4 |year=2012 |page=34 |quote=Ihr... Debütalbum 'Tove Styrke', voller unterkühlt eingängiger Wumms-Pop-Songs... [Her debut album which is full of nonchalant, catchy boom-pop songs...]}}</ref>
