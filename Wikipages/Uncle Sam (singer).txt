{{short description|American R&B singer}}
'''Uncle Sam''' (born '''Sam Turner''';<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p14592/biography|pure_url=yes}} Uncle Sam Biography] ''[[AllMusic]]''</ref> May 31, 1971 in [[Detroit]], [[Michigan]], United States)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hiponline.com/2209/uncle-sam.html|title=Uncle Sam|website=Hiponline.com|date=16 February 2008|access-date=16 October 2021}}</ref> is an [[United States|American]] [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] [[singing|singer]]. He is best known for being signed to [[Boyz II Men]]'s Stonecreek Records imprint (distributed by [[Epic Records]]) in the late 1990s.<ref name="AMG">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p14592/biography|pure_url=yes}} |title=Biography by John Bush |website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=20 June 2009}}</ref>

His only [[1990s One-hit wonders in the United States|Top 40 hit]] was the [[Nathan Morris]]-penned "[[I Don't Ever Want to See You Again]]" released October 14, 1997, which reached No. 6 on the [[United States|U.S.]] ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] [[record chart|chart]], and No. 2 on the U.S. [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|R&B]] chart in 1997. The same track reached No. 30 in the [[UK Singles Chart]] in May 1998.<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums">{{cite book

Uncle Sam also sang a [[cover version|cover]] of "[[When I See You Smile]]", originally recorded by [[Bad English]], for the [[television program]], ''[[Touched by an Angel]].''

Uncle Sam performed a live global stream in [[Nairobi]], Kenya on February 18, 2017, following the final bout of the Making of a Champ boxing series.  His set included:
# "Free"
# "When I See You Smile"
# "You" (pre-release debut of new single)
# "I Don't Ever Want to See You Again"
