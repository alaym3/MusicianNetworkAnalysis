'''Sea Girls''' are an English [[indie rock]] band formed in London in 2015, comprising four members: Henry Camamile (vocals, guitar), Rory Young (lead guitar), Andrew Dawson (bass), and Oli Khan (drums).<ref name="formation_date"/><ref name="band_members"/> 

The band, named after a misheard lyric from a [[Nick Cave]] song,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://chuffmedia.com/artists/sea-girls|title= Sea Girls|access-date=21 November 2021}}</ref> are known for their indie anthems and energetic live shows.<ref name="signing"/> Their first release under Polydor, "Damage Done", was released on 7 May 2019.<ref name="damagedone"/>  Their debut single ("Call Me Out") was released on 1 June 2017,<ref name="callmeout"/> followed by their debut [[Extended play|EP]] of the same name on 8 June 2017.<ref name="callmeoutep"/> They have since released their second EP “Heavenly War” on 4 January 2018.<ref name="heavenlywarep"/> "Adored", the band's third EP was released on 5 June 2018.<ref name="adored"/> Their first EP under Polydor, ''Under Exit Lights'' was released 6 March 2020 and their debut album ''Open Up Your Head'' was released in August 2020.<ref name="underexitlights"/>

==Background==
The members individually hail from Leicestershire, Kent, and Lincolnshire.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xsnoize.com/review-sea-girls-adored-ep/|title= REVIEW: Sea Girls – 'Adored' EP|website=www.xsnoize.com|date= 13 June 2018|access-date=11 March 2020}}</ref> Although the band was not officially formed until 2015, it was in high school that the members had all met.<ref name="q1043.iheart.com">{{cite web|url=https://q1043.iheart.com/featured/out-of-the-box/content/2019-12-16-how-sea-girls-celebrated-their-first-arrival-in-america/|website=www.q1043.iheart.com|title= Sea Girls' First Arrival in America| access-date=11 February 2020}}</ref> During this time each member had played in separate bands, and it was not until they all eventually settled in London that they come together to form their own.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://oneononemusic.co.uk/2019/09/04/we-fired-some-questions-at-rising-indie-rock-stars-sea-girls-and-they-had-the-good-grace-to-answer-them/|website=www.q1043.iheart.com|title = Questions For Rising Indie-Rock Stars Sea Girls|access-date=11 February 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
Their debut single, "Call Me Out", was self-released on 1 June 2017.<ref name="callmeout"/> This live fan favourite was succeeded by the release of a four-track EP of the same name on 8 June 2017.<ref name="callmeoutep"/> Their second EP, ''Heavenly War'', was released on 4 January 2018.<ref name="heavenlywarep"/> A third EP, ''Adored'', was released on 5 June 2018 as a tribute to the band's first single, "Call Me Out".<ref name="adored"/>  To celebrate this anniversary, Sea Girls released this EP as a special edition 7" vinyl.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.roughtrade.com/gb/sea-girls/adored-ep|work=Rough Trade|title= Sea Girls Adored EP|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> These EPs were all self-released before the band eventually signed with [[Polydor Records]] in 2019.<ref name = "signing"/>

Sea Girls started out the 2018 festival season at the [[BBC Music]] festival The Biggest Weekend 26 May 2018 in Swansea.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/efj3v2/acts/aj84rz/|title=BBC Music Presents The Biggest Weekend - Sea Girls|work=BBC|access-date=29 January 2020}}</ref> They went on to play the [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]] on 25 and 26 August 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/sea-girls-psyche-return-stomping-ground-reading-leeds-2369169/|title=Sea Girls at Reading & Leeds|work=BBC|access-date=4 February 2020}}</ref> The band was invited back to play the festival the following year in 2019.<ref name="signing"/> The band is also set to play at [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]] 2020 on the BBC Radio 1 stage, making this their third year in a row on the festivals’ lineup.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.radiotimes.com/news/2020-02-11/reading-leeds-festival-tickets-2/ |title=Reading & Leeds 2020 Lineup|work=BBC|access-date=11 February 2020}}</ref>

Their last single of 2018, "All I Want To Hear You Say", released 11 September, set the band up to be featured as part of predicted successful acts of 2019 by multiple sources.<ref name = "All I Want To Hear You Say" /> In December 2018, it was announced that Sea Girls had been included in the [[BBC]]'s [[Sound of...|Sound of 2019]] poll.<ref name="soundof2019"/> The band were also included on predictors of successful acts in 2019 by [[Radio X (United Kingdom)|Radio X]], It's All Indie, and Dork.<ref name="radiox"/><ref name="indie"/><ref name="dork"/> It was announced in May 2019 that the group had signed a worldwide multi-album deal with [[Polydor Records]].<ref name="signing"/> Their first release under Polydor, "Damage Done", was released on 7 May 2019.<ref name="damagedone"/> It was then named by [[Annie Mac]] as the hottest record in the world on [[BBC Radio 1]] [[Annie Mac (radio show)|Annie Mac]] show.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.totalntertainment.com/music/sea-girls-unveil-anthemic-new-single-damage-done/|title=Sea Girls Unveil Anthemic New Single Damage Done|access-date=11 February 2020}}</ref> The band's second single released under [[Polydor Records]] was "Violet" on 20 August of the same year, and was once again praised as the hottest record in the world by Annie Mac.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2019/08/26/754316645/do-londons-sea-girls-have-the-hottest-record-in-the-world|title=Do Sea Girls Have the Hottest Record in the World |access-date=11 February 2020}}</ref>

Shortly after the band's first signed release it was also announced that they would be supporting Russian and Ukraine tour dates in August of [[Foals (band)|Foals]]'s [[Everything Not Saved Will Be Lost World Tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.indieisnotagenre.com/sea-girls-damage-done/|work=Indie Is Not A Genre|title= Sea Girls Reveal New Single 'Damage Done'|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> In December 2019 the band completed their first American tour, playing shows at the [[Mercury Lounge]] in New York City and at the [[Hotel Café]] in Los Angeles.<ref name="q1043.iheart.com"/>  Sea Girls closed out 2019 with an inclusion in the  [[MTV]] Push: Ones To Watch 2020 shortlist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/mtv-push-ones-to-watch-2020/news/introducing-our-mtv-push-ones-to-watch-2020-shortlist|title=MTV PUSH: Ones Two Watch 2020|work=MTV|access-date=29 January 2020}}</ref>

Their first release of 2020, "Ready For More", was released in January 2020,<ref name="readyformore"/> along with the announcement of their first EP under Polydor; ''Under Exit Lights'' which was released on 6 March 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.indieisnotagenre.com/sea-girls-under-exit-lights-announcement//|title=Sea Girls Announce New EP|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref><ref name="underexitlights"/> Their debut album, ''Open Up Your Head'', was released on 14 August 2020 and peaked at number 3 on the [[UK Albums Chart]];<ref name="album"/> having been announced on 29 April 2020 along with the release of single "Do You Really Wanna Know?".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/SEA-GIRLS-Announce-Debut-Album-and-Drop-New-Single-20200429|title=SEA GIRLS Announce Debut Album and Drop New Single|website=broadwayworld.com|date=29 April 2020|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref>

==Members==
* Henry Camamile - vocals, guitar (2015–present)
* Rory Young - lead guitar, backing vocals (2015–present)
* Andrew Dawson - bass, backing vocals (2015–present)
* Oli Khan - drums, percussion (2015–present)
