'''Nicholas Britell''' (born October 17, 1980) is an American composer, pianist, and film producer based in New York City. He has scored both of [[Barry Jenkins]]'s studio films, ''[[Moonlight (2016 film)|Moonlight]]'' (2016) and [[If Beale Street Could Talk (film)|''If Beale Street Could Talk'']] (2018), both of which received nominations for [[Academy Award for Best Original Score|Best Original Score]] at the [[Academy Awards]]. He has also worked with [[Adam McKay]], scoring his two most recent films, [[The Big Short (film)|''The Big Short'']] (2015) and [[Vice (2018 film)|''Vice'']] (2018). The [[HBO]] original series ''[[Succession (TV series)|Succession]]'' (2018–present) marked Britell's entry into television. Britell scored every episode of the first and second season, earning the [[Emmy Award]] for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Original Main Title Theme Music|Outstanding Original Main Title Theme Music]]<ref name="imdb.com">{{cite web|title=Nicholas Britell - Awards|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1615109/awards?ref_=nm_ql_2|access-date=December 12, 2017|website=IMDb}}</ref> and the [[Hollywood Music in Media Awards|Hollywood Music in Media Award]] for Best Original Score – TV Show/Limited Series. His score for the second season of ''Succession'' was nominated in 2020 for the [[Emmy Awards|Emmy Award]] for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Music Composition for a Series|Outstanding Music Composition for a Series]]. His score for [[The Underground Railroad (miniseries)|''The Underground Railroad'']] was nominated for Outstanding Music Composition For A Limited Or Anthology Series, Movie Or Special (Original Dramatic Score) at the [[73rd Primetime Emmy Awards]].

At the [[World Soundtrack Award for Soundtrack Composer of the Year|World Soundtrack Awards]], Britell was awarded [[World Soundtrack Award for Soundtrack Composer of the Year|Film Composer of the Year]] in 2019 for his scores for [[Vice (2018 film)|''Vice'']] and [[If Beale Street Could Talk (film)|''If Beale Street Could Talk'']] and [[World Soundtrack Awards|Television Composer of the Year]]<ref>{{Cite web|last=Awards|first=World Soundtrack|title=Television Composer of the Year - Awards|url=https://www.worldsoundtrackawards.com/en/awards/television-composer-of-the-year/4|access-date=2020-11-03|website=World Soundtrack Awards}}</ref> in 2020 for ''[[Succession (TV series)|Succession]]''. Britell also won Best Original Song at the 2021 ceremony alongside [[Florence Welch]] for [[Call Me Cruella]], written for [[Cruella (film)|Cruella]]. His works, as described by Soraya McDonald of Film Comment, "seem to organically straddle accessibility and sophistication in a way that goes beyond the typical programming of a big-city pops orchestra...That might have something to do with the fact that Britell has long had one foot in the world of hip-hop and another in the world of classical music."<ref>{{Cite web|title=New American Songbook|url=https://www.filmcomment.com/article/new-american-songbook-nicholas-britell/|access-date=2021-05-06|website=Film Comment|language=en}}</ref>

February 13, 2017</ref> in [[New York City]].<ref name=CrimsonBritell>{{cite news | url=https://www.thecrimson.com/article/2004/2/26/nicholas-j-britell-03-04-for-nick/# | title=Nicholas J. Britell '03–'04 | access-date=August 25, 2013 | author=Thompson, A. Haven | date=February 26, 2004 | publisher=The Harvard Crimson}}</ref> He graduated from the college preparatory school, [[Hopkins School]], in 1999.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Distinguished Alumni/ae and Fellows {{!}} Hopkins School|url = http://www.hopkins.edu/Page/Alumniae/Distinguished-Alumniae-and-Fellows|website = www.hopkins.edu|access-date = November 26, 2015|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20190331181522/https://www.hopkins.edu/Page/Alumniae/Distinguished-Alumniae-and-Fellows|archive-date = March 31, 2019|url-status = dead}}</ref> Britell is a graduate of the [[Juilliard School]]'s Pre-College Division and a [[Phi Beta Kappa]] graduate of [[Harvard University]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ascapfoundation.org/news/alumni-news |title=Nicholas Britell |website= ASCAP Foundation.com|publisher= ASCAP |access-date=February 5, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2004/06/phi-beta-kappa-elects-92-seniors-to-harvard-chapter/ | title=Phi Beta Kappa elects 92 seniors to Harvard chapter |access-date=August 26, 2013 |work=Harvard University Gazette| date=June 10, 2004 }}</ref> in 2003.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2017/02/moonlight-reflection/ | title='Moonlight' reflection - Oscar-nominated composer Britell '03 found inspiration in film's poetic depth |date=February 22, 2017|access-date=February 5, 2017 |work=Harvard University Gazette}}</ref> At school, he was a member of the instrumental hip-hop group, The Witness Protection Program, where he played keyboards and synthesizers.<ref name=CrimsonBritell /> Britell is part of an emerging generation of composers and artists who draw from an eclectic range of influences. He composed the title song from [[Christina Aguilera]]'s eighth studio album ''[[Liberation (Christina Aguilera album)|Liberation]]'' (2018).<ref name="US iTunes">{{cite web|title=''Liberation'' by Christina Aguilera|url=https://itunes.apple.com/ca/album/liberation/1378454470|website=[[iTunes Store]]|access-date=February 27, 2019|location=United States}}</ref><ref name="UK iTunes">{{cite web|title=Liberation by Christina Aguilera|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/liberation/1378454470|website=iTunes Store|access-date=February 27, 2019|location=United Kingdom}}</ref>

Britell scored [[Fox Searchlight]]'s tennis biopic ''[[Battle of the Sexes (2017 film)|Battle of the Sexes]]'', directed by [[Jonathan Dayton and Valerie Faris]], and released in 2017.<ref name="ew">{{cite web|url=https://ew.com/article/2016/04/13/battle-sexes-emma-stone-steve-carell-photo/|title=Battle of the Sexes: Emma Stone, Steve Carell are tennis pros in first photo|website=EW.com|access-date=December 12, 2017}}</ref>

His score for ''[[If Beale Street Could Talk (film)|If Beale Street Could Talk]]'' has received wide critical acclaim and awards including a 2019 [[Academy Award]] nomination, a [[British Academy of Film and Television Arts|BAFTA]] nomination,<ref name="imdb.com" /> a [[Critics' Choice Movie Awards|Critics' Choice]] nomination,<ref name="imdb.com"/> and Best Original Score awards from each of the [[Los Angeles Film Critics Association|Los Angeles]],<ref name="imdb.com"/> [[Boston Society of Film Critics|Boston]],<ref name="imdb.com"/> [[Chicago Film Critics Association|Chicago]],<ref name="imdb.com"/> Central Ohio,<ref name="imdb.com"/> Iowa,<ref name="imdb.com"/> [[Washington D.C. Area Film Critics Association|Washington DC]],<ref name="imdb.com"/> Phoenix,<ref name="imdb.com"/> [[Los Angeles Online Film Critics Society|LA Online]],<ref name="imdb.com"/> [[New York Film Critics Online|NY Online]],<ref name="imdb.com"/> and [[Online Film Critics Society|Online Film Critics Associations]].<ref name="imdb.com"/>

Britell is the composer of the HBO series ''[[Succession (TV series)|Succession]]'', winning a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Original Main Title Theme Music]] for the series. He will compose season 3 of ''Succession'', set to air in fall 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Kosin|first1=Julie|last2=Gonzales|first2=Erica|date=2021-02-10|title='Birds of Prey' Director Cathy Yan Will Helm a 'Succession' Season 3 Episode|url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/film-tv/a28612177/succession-season-3-news-date-cast-spoilers/|access-date=2021-02-18|website=Harper's BAZAAR|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== 2020–present ===
On February 6, 2019, Britell confirmed he is composing the score for Barry Jenkins' next project, ''[[The Underground Railroad (miniseries)|The Underground Railroad]]'', an original series on Amazon based on [[Colson Whitehead|Colson Whitehead's]] Pulitzer Prize winning [[The Underground Railroad (novel)|novel of the same name]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=D'Alessandro|first=Anthony|date=2019-02-07|title=Oscar Nominees Barry Jenkins & Nicholas Britell On The Beat Of 'Beale Street' – Crew Call Podcast|url=https://deadline.com/2019/02/if-beale-street-could-talk-oscar-nominees-barry-jenkins-nicholas-britell-interview-podcast-1202523297/|access-date=2021-02-18|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref> The show premiered on [[Amazon Video]] on May 14, 2021 to critical acclaim for both Jenkins and Britell. For his score, Britell received a [[Primetime Emmy Awards|Primetime Emmy Award]] nomination for Outstanding Music Composition For A Limited Or Anthology Series, Movie Or Special (Original Dramatic Score) at the [[73rd Primetime Emmy Awards]].

Britell is also composing the music for Adam McKay's upcoming film ''[[Don't Look Up (2021 film)|Don't Look Up]],'' expected to be released in 2021. Britell also scored Disney's ''[[One Hundred and One Dalmatians]]'' live-action spin-off ''[[Cruella (film)|Cruella]],'' released in 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nicholas Britell|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1615109/|access-date=2021-02-18|website=IMDb}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://filmmusicreporter.com/2021/03/31/nicholas-britell-scoring-craig-gillespies-cruella/|title = Nicholas Britell Scoring Craig Gillespie's 'Cruella' &#124; Film Music Reporter}}</ref>

== Other endeavors ==
Britell is a [[Steinway Artist]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nicholas Britell - Steinway & Sons|url=https://www.steinway.com/artists/nicholas-britell|access-date=2021-02-18|website=www.steinway.com|language=en}}</ref> and a Creative Associate of the [[Juilliard School]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nicholas Britell {{!}} The Juilliard School|url=https://www.juilliard.edu/node/146951|access-date=2021-02-18|website=www.juilliard.edu}}</ref> In December 2018, it was announced that Britell would be a part of [[Esa-Pekka Salonen]]'s newly formed creative collective "brain trust" as Salonen takes the reins as music director of the [[San Francisco Symphony]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-12-11|title=Esa-Pekka Salonen bringing brain trust to SF Symphony — who are they?|url=https://www.mercurynews.com/2018/12/11/esa-pekka-salonen-bringing-brain-trust-to-sf-symphony-heres-whos-on-board/|access-date=2021-02-18|website=The Mercury News|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He is married to cellist Caitlin Sullivan.<ref>[https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/arts/la-et-cm-moonlight-movie-live-music-20170111-story.html Los Angeles Times: "'Moonlight' screens to live music, packed crowd at downtown L.A.'s Million Dollar Theatre" by Tim Greiving] January 11, 2017</ref>
