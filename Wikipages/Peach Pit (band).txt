'''Peach Pit''' is an [[indie pop]] band from [[Vancouver]], [[British Columbia]], [[Canada]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tealmagazine.com/articles/interview-with-peach-pit|title=Interview with Peach Pit: Guitar Solo Revival and the Vancouver Music Scene|website=Teal Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-09-04}}</ref> The band consists of singer and [[Rhythm guitar|rhythm guitarist]] Neil Smith, [[Lead guitar|lead guitarist]] Christopher Vanderkooy, [[bassist]] Peter Wilton, and [[drummer]] Mikey Pascuzzi. They describe their own music as "chewed bubblegum pop",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wepluggoodmusic.com/2018/02/16/wpgm-reviews-peach-pit-live-moth-club/|title=WPGM Reviews: Peach Pit Live At Moth Club - WE PLUG GOOD MUSICWE PLUG GOOD MUSIC|website=www.wepluggoodmusic.com|date=16 February 2018|access-date=2019-08-27}}</ref> whereas critics have described their sound as sad [[pop music|pop]], and [[surf rock]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/best-surf-rock-bands/|title=Top 10 modern bands keeping surf rock alive and well in 2019|last=Quiles|first=Alyssa|date=2019-07-29|website=Alternative Press|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-27}}</ref> and is characterized by a soft spoken vocal style and guitar driven rock melodies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://substreammagazine.com/2017/09/peach-pit-editorial/|title=Nectar and chewed bubblegum: the not too sweet pop of Peach by videographer Lester Lyons-Hookham.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/little-gentlemen-the-405-meets-peach-pit-150|title=Little Gentlemen: The 405 meets Peach == History ==
The group formed when high school friends, Neil Smith and Chris Vanderkooy, decided to collaborate on a musical project in 2014. Peter Wilton and Mikey Pascuzzi joined them on bass and drums. In June 2016, the band released its debut EP, ''Sweet FA'', produced and recorded by Smith's former roommate Harley Small at The Space Studios in Vancouver BC. They quickly began work on their follow up release and debut [[LP album|LP]], ''Being So Normal''. The album was again produced and recorded by Harley Small at The Space Studios<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Peach-Pit-Being-So-Normal/release/11454821|title=Being So Normal, album credits|website=Discogs|access-date=2020-01-02}}</ref>  and was released in September 2017 on the Vancouver indie label Kingfisher Bluez.'<ref>{{cite web|url=http://trendprivemagazine.com/2017/09/19/peach-pit-being-so-normal-album-review-normalizing-normal/|title=Peach Pit: Being So Normal Album Review – Normalizing Normal|last=Cheng|first=Mason|date=19 September 2017|website=Trend Prive|access-date=9 January 2018}}</ref>

Vocalist/rhythm guitarist Neil Smith was formerly part of the folk band Dogwood and Dahlia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dogwoodanddahlia.bandcamp.com/|title=Dogwood and Dahlia, by Dogwood and Dahlia|website=Dogwood and Dahlia|access-date=2019-08-19}}</ref> Smith and bassist Peter Wilton formerly worked as Amazon delivery drivers, guitarist Christopher Vanderkooy worked at a local Vancouver brewery, and drummer Mikey Pascuzzi worked as a carpenter. The band quit their jobs to go on their first tour of North America, Europe<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://justmusicallyspeaking.com/peach-pit-concert-review/|title=Peach Pit - Concert Review • Musically Speaking|date=2018-01-30|website=Musically Speaking|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-27}}</ref> and Asia<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://asialive365.com/indie-pop-peach-pit-singapore-bangkok/|title=Indie pop band Peach Pit to perform in Singapore and Bangkok|last=Team|first=AL365|date=2018-01-16|website=Asia Live 365|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-27}}</ref> from 2017-2018. They have been touring steadily ever since the release of their debut album.<ref name="Little">{{cite web|url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/little-gentlemen-the-405-meets-peach-pit-150|title=Little Gentlemen: The 405 meets Peach Pit|last=Tomer|first=William|date=29 September 2017|website=The 405|access-date=8 January 2018}}</ref>
