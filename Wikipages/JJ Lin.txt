'''Wayne Lim Junjie''' ({{zh|t=林俊傑|s=林俊杰|p=Lín Jùnjié}}; born 27 March 1981) better known by his stage name '''JJ Lin''', is a [[Singaporeans|Singaporean]] singer, songwriter, record producer, and actor.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.jpopasia.com/celebrity/jjlin/ | title=JJ Lin – singer/actor | website=jpopasia.com/ | access-date=15 November 2014 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141129014414/http://www.jpopasia.com/celebrity/jjlin/ | archive-date=29 November 2014 | url-status=live }}</ref> Lin launched his musical career by releasing his debut album, ''[[Music Voyager]]'' (2003). Lin has received three [[Golden Melody Awards]], including one [[Golden Melody Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] and two [[Golden Melody Award for Best Male Vocalist Mandarin|Best Mandarin Male Singer]].<ref name="15GMA">{{in lang|zh}} GIO, Taiwan [http://info.gio.gov.tw/ct.asp?xItem=20284&ctNode=4824&mp=3 15th Golden Melody Awards winners list] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111125093238/http://info.gio.gov.tw/ct.asp?xItem=20284&ctNode=4824&mp=3|date=25 November 2011}} 12 May 2004. Retrieved 20 June 2011</ref>

==Early life==
JJ Lin was born to an ethnic [[Kinmen|Kim-mûi]] [[Hokkiens|Hokkien]] family ([[Hokkiens|Minnan]] ethnicity)<ref>{{cite web | url=https://new.qq.com/omn/20190519/20190519A08RX3.html | title=林俊杰演唱会说出小时候的秘密 被妈妈打 所有的男孩都会犯的错 | publisher=qq.com | access-date=3 November 2019}}</ref> in Singapore and studied at {{ill|Jing Shan Primary School|lt=|zh|靜山小學}}, [[Anglo-Chinese School (Independent)]] and [[Saint Andrew's Junior College]] before completing his [[National service in Singapore|National Service]]. Lin's family consists of his brother, father and mother who have a background in Chinese Classical Music, hence influencing the artist's music. Furthermore, his brother was a major influence in his career choice.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://miner8.com/en/25536|title=Profile and Facts of JJ Lin: Bio, Net Worth, Fun Facts etc.|date=18 May 2017|website=MIJ Miner8|access-date=3 February 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181101100508/http://miner8.com/en/25536|archive-date=1 November 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:林俊傑 學不會慶功記者會.jpg|thumb|Lin in 2012]]
In addition to [[Mandarin Chinese|Mandarin]], Lin also sings in English, [[Taiwanese Hokkien|Hokkien]] (his native dialect) and [[Cantonese]] using romanized pronunciation system, and has released his songs in Cantonese. In Taiwan, he was awarded the Best New Artist award at the [[Golden Melody Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p1027328|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography: JJ Lin|last=Birchmeier|first=Jason|publisher=[[All Media Guide|AMG]]|access-date=16 May 2010}}</ref>

After completing his national service, Lin signed with [[Ocean Butterflies International]], which launched the then 22-year-old Lin's debut album in 2003.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/entertainment/billy-koh-made-jj-lin-a-big-mandopop-star|title=Billy Koh made JJ Lin a big Mandopop star|last=Paige Lim|date=11 June 2015|website=The Straits Times|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180310203545/http://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/entertainment/billy-koh-made-jj-lin-a-big-mandopop-star|archive-date=10 March 2018|url-status=live|access-date=3 September 2019}}</ref> In 2011, Lin signed a new contract with the record label [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music Taiwan]].
 
He is well known for his songwriting and singing skills. He wrote songs for various musical artists while he was still a trainee under [[Ocean Butterflies]] under his mentors, Billy Koh and Sunkist Ng. In total, he has written over a hundred songs for other artists. His notable compositions include "Remember" (記得) for Taiwanese singer [[A-Mei]], [[A-Do]]'s "Let Go" (放手), [[Harlem Yu]]'s "What's Wrong With You?" (幹嘛你看不爽我), [[Cyndi Wang]]'s "When You" (当你), [[Vivian Hsu]]'s "Smiling Eyes" (爱笑的眼睛), and [[Comic Boyz]]'s "Heart of Superman" (超人心).
 
In Singapore, he was selected to perform the remixed version of "Home", the theme song of the [[Singapore National Day Parade|National Day Parade]] in 2004. Lin has sold 1 million copies in less than a week and is extremely successful throughout Asia.
 
In July 2007, he broke a Guinness record by signing 3,052 CDs in 2 hours and 30 minutes. During the signing, he was not allowed to drink or eat. The average time for him to sign a CD was 2.7 seconds.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://english.cri.cn/3086/2007/07/23/1261@252907.htm|title=JJ Lin Earns Autograph-Signing World Record|publisher=CRIENGLISH|date=23 July 2007|access-date=8 May 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303224901/http://english.cri.cn/3086/2007/07/23/1261@252907.htm|archive-date=3 March 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>
 
In May 2008, he performed at the [[China Central Television|CCTV]] charity event ''The Giving of Love'', dedicated to helping victims of the [[2008 Sichuan earthquake]]. Lin also donated a large sum of money to the rescue efforts and composed a song "Love and Hope" ({{zh|t=愛與希望|p=Ài Yǚ Xī Wàng}}) commemorating the tragedy.
 
In February 2008, Lin released a compilation album entitled ''Waiting for Love'' ({{zh|t=期待爱|p=Qīdài Ài}}). It has selected tracks from several of his albums spanning from his debut album to 2007's ''[[West Side (album)|West Side]]''.<ref>Duzhe Issues 9–16 – Page 69 2008 "期待你的愛/林俊杰 "</ref> Later in October of that year, his sixth album, ''[[Sixology]]'' ({{zh|t=陸|p=Liù}}), was released, and it sold 280,000 copies in a week.
 
His own fashion line, SMUDGE,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smudgestore.com/live/store.php|title=SMUDGE STORE – ABOUT THE STORE|access-date=2 February 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716095617/http://www.smudgestore.com/live/store.php|archive-date=16 July 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> was opened in Singapore during 2008 Christmas with an unofficial launch. The store's official launch was on 13 March 2009.
 
In May 2009, Lin won three awards at the 14th annual Composers and Authors Society of the UK (Compass) Awards Presentations,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.compass.org.sg/cIndex266.aspx|title=COMPASS – 14th Compass Annual Awards Presentation|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120722231751/http://www.compass.org.sg/cIndex266.aspx|archive-date=22 July 2012}}</ref> a ceremony that honors performers in the music scene. It was held at the [[Royal Albert Hall]], [[Hyde Park, London|Hyde Park]], Buckingham Palace and [[Raffles City Convention Centre]], Singapore. He was honored with the Top Local Artiste of the Year award, which is given out to Singaporean artists who generated the highest royalty earnings for the year. In that same year, Lin won the Singapore Youth Award.<ref>[http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/439783/1/.html JJ Lin among recipients of Singapore Youth Award 2009] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090905034409/http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/singaporelocalnews/view/439783/1/.html |date=5 September 2009 }} 1 July 2009</ref>
 
In 2009, his singing career was put on hold due to his voice being damaged by [[acid reflux]] and flu, as well as his hectic touring and recording schedules. His poor health forced him to return to Singapore to recover and receive treatments. He released the seventh album, Hundred Days, on 18 December 2009.
 
In May 2010, Lin performed in America for the first time at the [[Asian Pacific American Heritage Month]] Concert Tour (APAHM).<ref>{{cite web | url = http://asiapacificarts.usc.edu/article@apa?connecting_with_american_fans_an_interview_with_jj_lin_and_evonne_hsu_15264.aspx | title = Connecting with American Fans: an interview with JJ Lin and Evonne Hsu | publisher = Asia Pacific Arts | date = 18 August 2010 | access-date = 14 February 2011 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130826035558/http://asiapacificarts.usc.edu/article@apa?connecting_with_american_fans_an_interview_with_jj_lin_and_evonne_hsu_15264.aspx | archive-date = 26 August 2013 | url-status = dead }}</ref>
 
In June 2010, Lin performed together with American singer [[Sean Kingston]] at the opening concert of the [[Marina Bay Sands]] to thousands of invited VIPs guests. But both were absent at the [[2010 Summer Youth Olympics|inaugural]] [[Youth Olympic Games|YOG]] Opening Ceremony. Lin's eighth album, ''[[She Says (album)|She Says]]'' (她說), was released on 8 December 2010.

Lin's ninth studio album, [[Lost N Found (JJ Lin album)|Lost N Found]] (學不會) was released on 31 December 2011. A preview of his 9th album was made available on 12 December 2011 on various Chinese internet radio channels.<ref name="test">{{cite web|url=http://www.jj-lin.com/en/newsdetail.aspx?cid=4&id=94|title=林俊傑 – 官方網站|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120405102809/http://www.jj-lin.com/en/newsdetail.aspx?cid=4&id=94|archive-date=5 April 2012}}</ref>
 
Lin's tenth anniversary album, [[Stories Untold (album)|Stories Untold]] (因你而在) was released on 13 March 2013. A compilation of micro films were released in conjunction with the album, serving to expand the stories behind the songs. He worked with a few notable people on the album, including [[Ashin]], Harry Chang (张怀秋), and his older brother Eugene Lim (林俊峰). He also started his world tour "Timeline" in July 2013 in [[Taipei]], Taiwan.
 
Lin co-wrote the song "Dreams" with Goh Kheng Long and [[Corrinne May]] and performed the song live during the grand finale at the [[Chingay Parade (South East Asia)|Chingay Parade]] on 27 and 28 February 2015.<ref>{{cite news|title=Singer-songwriter JJ Lin is star of this year's Chingay parade|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/music/story/jj-lin-star-years-chingay-parade-20150227|work=[[The Straits Times]]|date=27 February 2015|access-date=1 March 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150403012917/http://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/music/story/jj-lin-star-years-chingay-parade-20150227|archive-date=3 April 2015|url-status=live}}</ref>
 
One of his recent ventures in the world of music was a duet with [[Ayumi Hamasaki]], for the latter's music video, "The Gift" (and as of 27 April 2015, has about 1,882,459-page views).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://sg.entertainment.yahoo.com/news/jj-lin-stars-ayumi-hamasakis-mv-062800656.html |title=JJ Lin stars in Ayumi Hamasaki's new MV |access-date=27 April 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150822081945/https://sg.entertainment.yahoo.com/news/jj-lin-stars-ayumi-hamasakis-mv-062800656.html |archive-date=22 August 2015  }}</ref><ref>{{cite av media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6Z13emJ6IFc|title=浜崎あゆみ / The GIFT（ayumi hamasaki – The GIFT feat. JJ Lin）【Music Video】|date=16 March 2015|via=YouTube|access-date=27 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150421204157/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6Z13emJ6IFc|archive-date=21 April 2015|url-status=live}}</ref>
 
In 2015, he was selected to perform the [[Singapore National Day Parade#Theme songs|National Day Theme Song]], "Our Singapore", which was composed by [[Dick Lee]]. His experimental debut album ''[[From M.E. to Myself]]'' was released on 25 December 2015.
 
On 7 July 2016, Lin released "If Miracles had a Sound", a 96-minute music documentary created over the course of 455 days, taking place throughout studios in the U.S., Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong, and Taiwan. The theme song of the documentary, "Infinity and Beyond" (超越无限) was released on 21 June 2016.
 
On 19 December 2017, JJ Lin posted on his Instagram page that the past two years "had not been easy" and that he had been "searching constantly for a clear direction", needing a "shelter for the soul, a 'SANCTUARY'". He "penned songs and finished his [[Message in a Bottle (album)|Message in a Bottle album]]", which was released on 29 December 2017. At the same time, he and his team and "shared a common vision, starting on project "SANCTUARY" and built the space from ground zero."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bc4hgawDm9F/|title=Instagram post by JJ Lin • Dec 19, 2017 at 12:05&nbsp;pm UTC|website=Instagram|access-date=28 December 2017}}</ref> In early 2018, he announced his "Sanctuary" concert tour began in Shanghai on 17 March.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicacrossasia.blogspot.com/2018/01/jj-lin-2018-tour-and-concert-dates.html|title=JJ Lin 林俊傑 2018 Tour and Concert Dates|website=Musicacrossasia|access-date=2 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180623193938/https://musicacrossasia.blogspot.com/2018/01/jj-lin-2018-tour-and-concert-dates.html|archive-date=23 June 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2019, he released ''The Story of Us'' ({{Zh|c=将故事写成我们|s=|t=|p=}}), and actress [[Wu Jinyan]] starred in the music video.<ref name=":02">{{Cite web|date=2019-12-31|title=吴谨言MV扮玉兔 林俊杰：她的眼睛会说故事|url=https://www.zaobao.com.sg/zentertainment/music/story20191231-1017239|access-date=2020-10-04|website=早报|language=zh-Hans}}</ref>

In January 2020, Lin released a song "I'll Stay With You" dedicated to the medical staff in [[Wuhan]], China, who were working hard to save patients infected with [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID-19]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessinsider.sg/ill-stay-with-you-grateful-netizens-thank-jj-lins-and-stefanie-sun-for-encouraging-wuhan-medical-staff-with-touching-song/|title='I'll stay with you': Grateful netizens thank JJ Lin and Stefanie Sun for encouraging Wuhan medical staff with touching song, Business Insider – Business Insider Singapore|last=Smith|first=Lamont Mark|website=www.businessinsider.sg|language=en-US|access-date=22 February 2020|archive-date=22 February 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200222111800/https://www.businessinsider.sg/ill-stay-with-you-grateful-netizens-thank-jj-lins-and-stefanie-sun-for-encouraging-wuhan-medical-staff-with-touching-song/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Other work==
Lin has appeared in various advertisements for brands such as [[Sprite (soft drink)|Sprite]], [[Durex]], ''[[Dota 2]], [[Beats by Dre|Beats]]'' and Cornetto Royale.
 
He is also a tourist ambassador for Singapore. He was also involved in an anti-drugs event as an ambassador, in Taiwan. The theme song for this event is "Baby Baby", which is from his album Westside.
 
On 14 August 2015, Lin was selected to be the advisor for Team [[Harlem Yu]] on ''The Voice of China'' (season 4).

On 4 November 2016, Lin joined the variety show "[[Sound of My Dream|Dream Voice]]" as one of the mentors alongside [[A-mei]] Zhang, [[Jam Hsiao]], [[Hebe Tien|Hebe Tian]], and [[Yu Quan]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.zaobao.com.sg/zentertainment/music/story20161116-690872|script-title=zh:《梦想的声音》第二期收视大涨 萧敬腾、林俊杰玩合体|date=16 November 2016|work=联合早报网|access-date=21 April 2018|language=zh}}</ref> He stayed on as a mentor for the second season of the show, which was released in October 2017,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.jnnc.com/ent/2017/1027/605587.shtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180418092838/http://news.jnnc.com/ent/2017/1027/605587.shtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=18 April 2018|title=《梦想的声音2》导师林俊杰回归，什么歌曲让JJ表示压力山大？_娱乐_新闻_新视听网站|website=news.jnnc.com|language=zh-CN|access-date=17 April 2018}}</ref> and the third season, which was released in October 2018.

In 2017, Lin established Team Still Moving under Gunfire (Team SMG), a professional [[esports]] team.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.hypeandstuff.com/jj-lins-e-sports-malaysia-team/|title=JJ Lin's E-sports Team Breaks Through Southeast Asian Market by Acquiring Malaysian Team|date=2 February 2020|website=HYPE & STUFF|language=en-GB|access-date=3 March 2020}}</ref> Initially focused on [[Arena of Valor]],<ref name=":0" /> Team SMG has expanded itself into other games such as [[PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds]] in 2018, [[Mobile Legends: Bang Bang]] in 2020<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/lifestyle/jj-lin-esports-team-mobile-legends-bang-mlbb-12468138|title=Singer JJ Lin's e-sports organisation enters Southeast Asia with Malaysian team|website=CNA|language=en|access-date=3 March 2020}}</ref> and [[Dota 2]] in 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=JJ Lin's Team SMG is entering Dota with new squad led by Mushi and ninjaboogie {{!}} ONE Esports|url=https://www.oneesports.gg/dota2/jj-lins-team-smg-is-entering-dota-with-new-squad-led-by-mushi-and-ninjaboogie/|access-date=2021-09-26|website=www.oneesports.gg|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2019, he took part in the Chinese food travelogue show, [[Chef Nic]].

On 24 February 2021, Lin performed a medley of songs ("[[A Whole New World]]", "[[Can You Feel the Love Tonight]]", "[[Let It Go]]" and "Embark") to commemorate the official launch of the digital streaming service [[Disney+]] in Singapore, which launched a day earlier on the 23rd.<ref>{{cite web |title=Join me for A Night of Disney+, with a music performance like never before!
