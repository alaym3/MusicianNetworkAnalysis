'''Donald Joseph Klang''' (born January 23, 1985) is an American singer, songwriter, producer and model. He won ''[[Making the Band 4]]'' and was awarded his own solo contract by [[Sean Combs|Diddy]]. He released his debut album, ''[[Just a Rolling Stone]]'' in late 2008.

==Life and career==
Klang was born in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]].<ref name=amg>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p1058364|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography: Donnie Klang|last=Collar|first=Matt|publisher=[[All Media Guide]]|access-date=8 May 2010}}</ref> He began modeling as a baby and doing television and film work from a young age, getting minor roles on ''[[NYPD Blue]]'' to ''[[The Nanny]]''.<ref name=nyt/> He was in a band in middle school, later attending chorus in high school.<ref name=levi>{{cite web |url=http://www.antonnews.com/levittowntribune/2007/08/03/news/ |title=Levittowner Competes on MTV's Making the Band 4 |publisher=Antonnews.com |date=2007-08-03 |access-date=2010-08-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707151048/http://www.antonnews.com/levittowntribune/2007/08/03/news/ |archive-date=2011-07-07 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was part of pop groups in [[St. Dominic High School (Oyster Bay, New York)|St. Dominic High School]] and [[Island Trees High School]], namely Playa Deception and INT. The latter which released an independent record called ''Hip-Pop''.<ref name=nyt/> He attended [[Hofstra University]] briefly studying music management and business and minoring in music.<ref name="nyt"/><ref name=levi/><ref name=full/> Klang decided to take a semester off of college to pursue a music career with his group.<ref name=levi/>

Klang had been in INT for nine years before it broke up because their parents wanted them to go back to school as the band was going nowhere.<ref name=full>{{cite web|url=http://fullblastmagazine.com/2010/03/the-re-making-of-donnie-klang/ |title=AMPED UP &#124; The Re-Making of Donnie Klang « Full Blast Magazine |publisher=Fullblastmagazine.com |access-date=2010-08-31}}</ref> He auditioned for ''[[American Idol]]'' twice but never got past the first round.<ref name=nyt/> These events led to him being apprehensive of pursuing music further.<ref name=crushable/> Not long after returning to Hofstra and working at a warehouse, the opportunity came for ''Making the Band 4'' and he again took time off from college in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|last=Gamboa |first=Glenn |url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/article/donnie-klang-gets-a-push-from-diddy/ |title=Donnie Klang gets a push from Diddy < PopMatters |publisher=Popmatters.com |access-date=2010-08-31}}</ref><ref name=full/> An acquaintance had entered his [[MySpace]] account into an audition which he later attended at the encouragement of his family.<ref name=crushable>{{cite web|url=http://crushable.com/entertainment/interview-with-making-the-band-4s-donnie-klang/ |title=Interview with Making the Band 4’s Donnie Klang « Crushable |publisher=Crushable.com |date=2008-08-21 |access-date=2010-08-31}}</ref> He was awarded his own solo contract by Diddy instead of a spot in the group. Klang released his debut album, ''Just a Rolling Stone'', in late 2008 which peaked at number nineteen on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. He later opened for [[Janet Jackson]] in the fall.<ref name=nyt>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/21/nyregion/long-island/21klangli.html | work=The New York Times | title=From Levittown, a New Voice Emerges | first=Tammy | last=La Gorce | date=2008-09-21}}</ref>

Klang split with [[Bad Boy Records]] in mid-2010, signing with independent label Eight72 Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/957862/billboard-bits-big-boi-lollapalooza-donnie-klang-daniel-lanois-and-more |title=Bits: Big Boi, Lollapalooza, Donnie Klang, Daniel Lanois and More |website=Billboard.com |access-date=2020-04-05}}</ref> He began working on his second album at the same time.<ref>{{cite web|author=Date: 05/24/10 |url=http://www.singersroom.com/news/5806/Bad-Boy-No-More-Donnie-Klang-Readies-New-Album |title=Bad Boy 'No More,' Donnie Klang Readies New Album |publisher=Singersroom.com |access-date=2010-08-31}}</ref> He later started his own record label, Donnie Klang Entertainment and released his first single "Falling 4U" in 2011. He also released a mixtape on Valentine's Day.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Donnie-Klang-Valentines-Day-Mixtape.199186.html |title=Valentine's Day Mixtape Mixtape by Donnie Klang Hosted by DJ Spin King & DJ Swivet |website=Datpiff.com |date=2011-02-14 |access-date=2020-04-05}}</ref> "X-Miss" was also released as a single for Christmas in 2011.

==Artistry==
Klang has listed [[Boyz II Men]], [[Dru Hill]], [[NSYNC]], [[Backstreet Boys]], [[Usher (entertainer)|Usher]], [[The Fray]], [[OneRepublic]],  [[Justin Timberlake]], [[Brian McKnight]], [[Michael Jackson]] and [[James Brown]] as musical influences.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.alloy.com/5/86/7903/1/ |title=Donnie Klang - Interviews - Entertainment |publisher=ALLOY.com |access-date=2010-08-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707113617/http://www.alloy.com/5/86/7903/1/ |archive-date=2011-07-07 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name=singers>{{cite web|author=Date: 09/01/08 |url=http://www.singersroom.com/interview/123/Donnie-Klang-One-Man-Band |title=Donnie Klang: One Man Band |publisher=Singersroom.com |date=2008-10-07 |access-date=2010-08-31}}</ref> He co-wrote most of ''Just a Rolling Stone''.<ref name=singers/>
