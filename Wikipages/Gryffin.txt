'''Dan Griffith''' (born September 29, 1987), known by his stage name '''Gryffin''' (often stylized in [[all caps]]), is an American musician, DJ, songwriter, and record producer. He gained recognition for remixing some well-known songs such as [[Tove Lo]]'s "[[Talking Body]]", [[Maroon 5]]'s "[[Animals (Maroon 5 song)|Animals]]" and [[Years & Years]]' "[[Desire (Years & Years song)|Desire]]".<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=http://gryffinofficial.com/about|title=Gryffin {{!}}   About|website=gryffinofficial.com|access-date=2016-09-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160919152825/http://gryffinofficial.com/about|archive-date=September 19, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7557750/gryffin-w-hotel-next-up-new-artists-in-music-series|title=Next Up: Gryffin Brings His Whole Heart Tour to D.C. for Private Show at W Hotel|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref>

== Background ==
Gryffin was born in San Francisco, California to an American father and a Japanese mother.<ref name="Galore">{{cite web |url=https://galoremag.com/gryffin-edm-producer-sounds-like-good-boyfriend/ |title=Gryffin is an EDM Producer Who Also Sounds Like a Really Good Boyfriend |last=Cohen |first=Abeline |date=June 29, 2016 |website=Galore |publisher= |access-date=June 21, 2021}}</ref> Gryffin grew up as a classically trained pianist and learned to play the guitar at an early age.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/6737444/gryffin-tour-dates-announcement-live|title=Gryffin Announces First-Ever Live Dates, Talks Transition to Multi-Instrumental Act: Exclusive|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.windishagency.com/artists/gryffin|title=The Windish Agency: Gryffin|website=www.windishagency.com|access-date=2016-09-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.youredm.com/2016/02/08/your-edm-exclusive-gryffin-talks-opening-for-kygo-musicality-his-new-single-heading-home-more/|title=Your EDM Exclusive: Gryffin Talks Opening For Kygo, Musicality, His New Single 'Heading Home' + More|last=Yau|first=Jeffrey|date=February 8, 2016|website=Your EDM|access-date=2016-09-01}}</ref> He played in bands to initially hone his instrumental abilities. He is a graduate of Saint Francis High School in Mountain View, California. He studied [[electrical engineering]] at the [[University of Southern California]] where he spent much of his leisure time in his dorm room to work on music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/kyle-stevens/gryffin-debuts-whole-hear_b_11769758.html|title=Gryffin Debuts &quot;Whole Heart&quot; and Paints an Intimate Portrait of his Life|last=equality|first=Kyle Stevens Fighter for|last2=Br|first2=justice BUILD Series|date=August 30, 2016|website=The Huffington Post|access-date=2016-09-01|last3=Ambassador.}}</ref> 

When asked on his key lessons of [[Music production (music industry)|music production]], he said: "At the beginning, I just released music with the main goal of having my friends play it at college parties and I never would have imagined it would get to this point". He made his 'world' debut as Gryffin at the SnowGlobe Music Festival 2015.<ref name="auto"/>

His stage name was inspired by both his surname "Griffith" and the mythical bird [[Griffin|griffin]].<ref name="Galore" />

===2017===
2017 saw three singles for Gryffin. "[[Feel Good (Gryffin song)|Feel Good]]", a collaboration with DJ [[Illenium]] and singer [[Daya (singer)|Daya]], was released on March 3.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7709343/gryffin-illenium-feel-good-daya-interview-2017|title=Gryffin Talks New Track 'Feel Good' With Illenium & Daya, Dream Collabs & What's Next|website=Billboard}}</ref> "Love in Ruins" with singer [[Sinéad Harnett]] was released on July 7<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7881770/gryffin-new-song-love-in-ruins-ep-interview|title=Gryffin Talks New Single 'Love In Ruins' With Sinead Harnett, Upcoming EP & More: Exclusive|website=Billboard}}</ref> and "Nobody Compares to You" with Katie Pearlman was released on October 6.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7990124/gryffin-nobody-compares-to-you|title=Stream Gryffin's Uplifting Single 'Nobody Compares to You'|website=Billboard}}</ref>

===2018===
2018 proved to be a big year for Gryffin, releasing five singles throughout the year. His first single of the year, "Winnebago" with [[Quinn XCII]] and [[Dan Wilson (musician)|Daniel Wilson]] was released on April 20.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8357957/gryffin-winnebago-new-song-quinn-xcii-daniel-wilson|title=Gryffin Goes Reggae on 'Winnebago' Feat. Quinn XCII and Daniel Wilson: Listen|website=Billboard}}</ref> He followed this up with single "Just for a Moment" with [[Iselin Solheim|Iselin]], released June 22,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2018/06/gryffin-teams-iselin-emotional-just-moment/|title=Gryffin teams up with Iselin on emotional 'Just For A Moment'|date=June 25, 2018|website=Dancing Astronaut}}</ref> and "[[Tie Me Down (Gryffin and Elley Duhé song)|Tie Me Down]]" with [[Elley Duhé]], released on August 3.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8468529/gryffin-elley-duhe-tie-you-down|title=Gryffin & Elley Duhe 'Tie Me Down' With Energetic Dance-Pop Melodies: Listen|website=Billboard}}</ref> Two months following the release of "Tie Me Down", Gryffin released a song with Zohara. "[[Remember (Gryffin and Zohara song)|Remember]]" was released on October 26 and eventually topped the [[Dance Club Songs|US Dance Charts]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://edm.com/music-releases/gryffin-debut-album-remember-zohara|title=Gryffin Shares Lead Single from Debut Album, "Remember" ft. ZOHARA|first=EDM com|last=Staff|website=EDM.com - The Latest Electronic Dance Music News, Reviews & Artists}}</ref> marking Griffith's first number one hit song.

===2019===
In 2019, Gryffin released his full-length debut album, ''[[Gravity (Gryffin album)|Gravity]]'', to critical acclaim.

===2020===
On July 26, 2020, Gryffin announced the release of "[[Cry (Gryffin and John Martin song)|Cry]]", with [[John Martin (singer)|John Martin]], on his Instagram page.<ref>{{Cite web|url=www.https://www.instagram.com/p/CDH-m7SnEJL/|title=Gryffin (@gryffinofficial) · Instagram photos and videos|website=Instagram}}</ref> He hinted that the track would be the first single from his upcoming second album, and announced July 30, 2020 as the single's release date. On November 19, 2020, Gryffin released “[[Safe with Me (Gryffin and Audrey Mika song)|Safe With Me]]” with [[Audrey Mika]].

===2021===
Gryffin released "I Want Love" featuring [[Two Feet]] on Feb 10, 2021. He announced the release one day prior on his instagram page.

== Personal life ==
He moved to Los Angeles in 2016 to be closer to his label and management.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7487814/gryffin-talks-live-performances-la-whole-heart|title=Gryffin Talks Live Shows, Move to L.A. & Shares Touching Track 'Whole Heart'|newspaper=Billboard|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref> He married his wife, Stephanie, in April 2018.<ref name="Wedding">{{cite web |url=https://www.zola.com/wedding/steph-dan |title=Wedding: Steph & Dan |last= |first= |date=April 7, 2018 |website=Zola |publisher= |access-date=June 21, 2021}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
Gryffin produces music in a melodic house style and classifies himself as a melodic house artist.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://edmchicago.com/2016/12/07/gryffin-talks-going-live-new-ep-selling-venues/|title=Gryffin Talks Going Live, a New EP and Selling Out Venues|last=Sheban|first=Molly|date=7 December 2016|website=EDM Chicago|access-date=2016-12-10}}</ref> He incorporates sounds of piano and guitar for a 'hybrid of electric and organic' musical style.<ref name=":0" />
