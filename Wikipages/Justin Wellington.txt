'''Justin Wellington''' sometimes also known by the initials '''JW''' (born 1978) is a [[Papua New Guinea]]n singer<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nrj.fr/artistes/justin-wellington/biographie|title=NRJ France: Justin Wellington biographie}}</ref> presenting a blend of [[pop music|pop]], [[reggae]], [[R&B]], [[dancehall]] and island music. He is most famous for his version of "[[Iko Iko]]" as "[[Iko Iko#Justin Wellington version|Iko Iko (My Bestie)]]"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9587968/justin-wellington-small-jam-iko-iko-global-charts|title=New Around the World: Justin Wellington & Small Jam Bring New Orleans Classic 'Iko Iko' to Global Charts|website=Billboard}}</ref> He has released three studio albums.

==Career==
Wellington was born to Australia-born parents who had immigrated to Papua New Guinea. He attended schools in Texas, USA and in Canada. He then began working as an R&B and reggae pop singer and has released several singles since 2002. Hits include "I Wanna Give You Some Lovin'", "Better Off", "One Night With You", "Much Love", "Fire", "In Love with U", "Runaway", "Tell Me", "Reminiscing", "Rain of Morobe" and others.<ref name=pngnews>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pngnewsupdate.com/2021/04/23/justin-wellington-set-to-release-new-tracks/|title=PNG New Update: Justin Wellington set to release new tracks (23 April 2021)}}</ref> He has worked with many Papua New Guinea artists such as AK-47, Tattz from NakaBlood, [[Jokema]] and [[O-Shen]], Robbie T, Mekere Crew, Steven M, Gravity, DJ Travy etc.<ref name=pngnews/>

His international breakthrough came with a cover of "Iko Iko", originally recorded by [[James "Sugar Boy" Crawford]] in 1953 and popularised by [[The Dixie Cups]], [[Dr. John]], [[Captain Jack (group)|Captain Jack]] amongst others. Justin Wellington's adapted version of the song as "Iko Iko ((My Bestie)", which he actually released featuring [[Solomon Islands]] group Small Jam in 2017. It received worldwide attention as part of the social platform [[TikTok]] challenge. As a result, the cover was released by [[Sony Music UK]] on June 3, 2019 and started to gain international popularity in 2021.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ruelle|first=Yohann|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Justin-Wellington/news-117525.html|title="Iko Iko" : le tube phénomène de Justin Wellington fait danser TikTok et la France|website=chartsinfrance.net|language=fr|date=May 11, 2021|access-date=May 13, 2021}}</ref><ref name=los40>{{cite news|url=https://los40.com/los40/2021/05/19/musica/1621449555_807703.html|author=Ivi Venegas|title='Iko Iko': esta es la canción de moda en TikTok que está basada en un clásico de los ãnos 50|publisher=Los40.com|date=19 May 2021|accessdate=12 October 2021|language=es}}</ref> His version makes various changes to the lyrics of the verses, and has its own original sections, but keeps the chorus the same. It was later added alongside the TikTok dance into the video game ''[[Fortnite Battle Royale]]''. He was nominated for "International Revelation of the Year" during the 2021 [[NRJ Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nrj.fr/music-awards/actualites/qui-sont-les-nommes-71355541|title=NRJ France: NRJ Music Awards: Qui sont les nommés}}</ref>
