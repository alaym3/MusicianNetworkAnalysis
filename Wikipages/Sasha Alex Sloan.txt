'''Alexandra Artourovna Yatchenko'''<ref name="ACE">{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/search/writer/tinashe/title/faded+love|title=ACE Repertory|last=ACE|website=ascap.com|access-date=October 29, 2018}}</ref> (born March 11, 1995<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bu4vEjuhWcI/|title=sasha sloan on Instagram: "i turned 24 today which means ive basically hit menopause in the music biz lol also i got a new phone thank u so much @kinghenry rip my…"|website=Instagram|access-date=May 12, 2019}}</ref>), known professionally as '''Sasha Alex Sloan''' (previously '''Sasha Sloan'''), is an American singer and songwriter.<ref name="onestowatch.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/sasha-sloan-quick-6-interview|title=Sasha Sloan - Quick 6 Interview|website=[[Ones to Watch]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | author=Thomas, Fred | title=Sasha Sloan – Biography & History | publisher=[[AllMusic]] | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/sasha-sloan-mn0003435850/biography | access-date=October 29, 2018}}</ref>

==Life and career==

===1995–2014: Early life and background===
Sloan grew up outside of Boston and taught herself to play on a piano her mother purchased when she was five.<ref name="BeatMagazine">{{cite web|title=New Favourite: Get to Know Low-key Pop Queen Sasha Sloan|url=http://www.thebeatjuice.com/2018/03/06/new-favourite-get-know-low-key-pop-queen-sasha-sloan/|website=Beat Magazine|access-date=May 14, 2018|date=March 6, 2018}}</ref><ref name="billboard.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8487041/sasha-sloan-interview-guide-to-her-songs|title=Sasha Sloan's Guide to Becoming a Sasha Sloan Fan|first=Tatiana|last=Cirisano|website=Billboard|date=November 29, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Billboard">{{cite web|url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8097669/sasha-sloan-new-singles-normal-fall-listen|title= Camila Cabello, Charli XCX Collaborator Sasha Sloan Has Introverts Covered With 'Normal' and 'Fall'|first= Tatiana|last= Cirisano|work= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date= February 2, 2018}}</ref> Her grandparents lived in [[Siberia]], Russia, and she spent summers on their farm there as a child.<ref name="onestowatch.com"/> When Sloan was nineteen, she moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career as a songwriter; she worked at a coffee shop to support herself.<ref name="billboard.com"/> She made her US national TV debut on ''[[The Late Show with Stephen Colbert]]'' on February 6, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8497071/sasha-sloan-older-performance-late-show-video|title=Sasha Sloan Makes TV Debut With Captivating Performance of 'Older' on 'Late Show': Watch|first=Angelica|last=Acevedo|website=Billboard|date=February 7, 2019}}</ref>

===2015–2017: Beginnings===
In 2015, Sloan was featured in the song "Phoenix" by [[Kaskade]], as well as being credited for songwriting. Sloan continued her career by writing for artists including [[Idina Menzel]], [[Maggie Lindemann]] and [[Camila Cabello]].
In 2017, Sloan was featured in the song "[[This Town (Kygo song)|This Town]]" by [[Kygo]], which she also cowrote.

===2018: ''Sad Girl'' and ''Loser''===
Her debut EP, ''[[Sad Girl (EP)|Sad Girl]]'', was released by RCA on April 18, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/release/sad-girl-mr0004886135|title=Sad Girl - Sasha Sloan - Release Info|website=AllMusic}}</ref>

Her second EP, ''[[Loser (EP)|Loser]]'', was released by RCA on November 29, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rcarecords.com/news/sasha-sloan-releases-new-ep-loser-2019-north-american-headlining-tour-announced/|title=Sasha Sloan Releases New EP "Loser"; 2019 North American Headlining Tour Announced|website=RCA Records}}</ref> On the same day, she announced her first headlining tour to support the album. Her single "Older" from the album was performed on ''The Late Show with Stephen Colbert'' on February 6, 2019.

===2019–present: ''Self-Portrait'' and ''Only Child'' ===
Sloan released her third EP ''Self-Portrait'' on October 18, 2019. She stated that "this EP is more about being OK with the fact that I have a lot of anxiety, and that I don’t want to go to parties."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8533238/sasha-sloan-ep-self-portrait-interview|title=Sasha Sloan's Parents Pranked Her, Then She Went Viral|website=Billboard|first=Ilana|last=Kaplan|date=October 18, 2019|access-date=October 19, 2019}}</ref>

On April 3, 2020, Sloan released the single "[[I'll Wait (Kygo and Sasha Sloan song)|I'll Wait]]" produced by [[Kygo]] for his album ''[[Golden Hour (Kygo album)|Golden Hour]]''. The following day, a music video was released starring American couple [[Rob Gronkowski]] and [[Camille Kostek]] containing personal footage of their life together.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://edm.com/music-releases/kygo-sasha-slon-ill-wait|title=Kygo and Sasha Sloan Warm Souls with "I'll Wait"|last=Stone|first=Katie|website=EDM.com|language=en|access-date=April 4, 2020}}</ref>

On August 3, Sloan announced that her debut album ''Only Child'' would be released in the fall,<ref>{{Cite tweet|title=pretty on brand to be releasing my debut album during one of the worst years of all time lol but I’m really excited about it and can’t wait for y’all to hear it|number=1290090043596582912|date=August 3, 2020|user=sadgirlsloan|author=Sasha Sloan}}</ref> with the lead single "Lie" released on August 7.<ref>{{Cite tweet|title=coming august 7th {{emoji|1F5A4|theme=none}}|number=1290318048667328512|date=August 3, 2020|user=sadgirlsloan|author=Sasha Sloan}}</ref> On August 25 she released the song "House With No Mirrors" as the second single from her debut album.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-25|title=Sasha Sloan Tackles Body Image On "House With No Mirrors"|url=https://www.idolator.com/7913774/sasha-sloan-tackles-body-image-on-house-with-no-mirrors|access-date=2020-08-31|website=idolator}}</ref> On August 28 she announced that her debut album ''Only Child'' would be released on October 16.<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 28, 2020|title=Sasha Sloan Unveils Cover Of Debut LP ‘Only Child’|url=https://www.idolator.com/7913859/sasha-sloan-unveils-cover-of-debut-lp-only-child|access-date=August 31, 2020|website=idolator}}</ref>

On November 17, Sloan announced a collaboration with singer/songwriter [[Charlie Puth]], a song called "Is It Just Me?", which would be released on November 19, 2020.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=sadgirlsloan|author=Sasha Sloan|number=1328814843583979523|title={{emoji|1F974|theme=none}} @charlieputh|date=November 17, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|user=charlieputh|author=[[Charlie Puth]]|number=1328817940515364864|title=Wait until you hear this one... @sadgirlsloan|date=November 17, 2020}}</ref>

On April 22, 2021 Sasha teamed up with country superstar [[Sam Hunt]] to release an acoustic-driven song titled "when was it over?" that finds Hunt and Sloan in a post-breakup state of misery questioning the cause of their respective romantic splits.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-23|title=Sam Hunt, Sasha Sloan Team Up For Haunting Breakup Song, 'when was it over?'|url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/music/sam-hunt-sasha-sloan-breakup-song-when-was-it-over/|access-date=2021-04-26|website=Sounds Like Nashville}}</ref> Sloan said that the track is about not being able to let go of someone even when you know there's nothing left.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2021-04-23|title=Sasha Sloan Teams Up With Sam Hunt For ’When Was It Over?’|url=https://uproxx.com/pop/sasha-sloan-when-was-it-over-sam-hunt/|access-date=2021-04-26|website=UPROXX|language=en-US}}</ref> The song was co-written with [[Shane McAnally]] who came up with the song's title.<ref name=":0" /> Other co-writers include [[King Henry (producer)|King Henry]] and Emi Dragoi.<ref>{{Cite web|title=SASHA SLOAN RELEASES “when was it over?” FEAT. SAM HUNT – Sony Music Canada|url=https://www.sonymusic.ca/press_release/sasha-sloan-releases-when-was-it-over-feat-sam-hunt|access-date=2021-04-26|website=www.sonymusic.ca}}</ref>
