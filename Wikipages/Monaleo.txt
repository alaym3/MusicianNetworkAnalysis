'''Leondra Roshawn Gay''',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chartoo.com/apple-music/song/1554447522-beating-down-yo-block|access-date=2021-11-02|website=www.chartoo.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Shazam|url=https://www.shazam.com/track/558675346/beating-down-yo-block|access-date=2021-11-02|website=Shazam}}</ref> known by her [[stage name]] '''Monaleo''' is an American rapper and songwriter based in [[Houston]]. She released her debut single, "Beating Down Yo Block," in 2020, which subsequently went viral on [[TikTok]] in 2021.

== Early life ==
Monaelo is from [[Houston]]. Monaleo attended The High School for Law and Justice, located downtown Houston in a neighborhood called River Oaks. She was pursuing a degree at [[Prairie View A&M University]] before putting her studies on pause due to her musical career.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|last=Stewart|first=Shelby|date=May 28, 2021|title=Houston's rising female rapper Monaleo earns a spot on Rolling Loud lineup|work=Houston Chronicle|url=https://www.chron.com/culture/music/article/houston-rapper-Monaleo-rolling-loud-lineup-16210358.php|access-date=2021-10-07}}</ref>

== Career ==
Monaelo began working in music intermittently since January 2020. In September 2020, Monaelo released a snippet of her debut single "Beating Down Yo Block" with producer Merion Krazy.<ref name=":0" /> The song samples "Knockin Pictures Off Da Wall"  by Yungstar. She wrote the song while struggling with a bad [[breakup]].<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-13|title=Genius: Monaleo "Beating Down Yo Block" Official Lyrics & Meaning|url=https://hiphopcanada.com/genius-monaleo-beating-down/|access-date=2021-10-07|website=HipHopCanada|language=en-CA}}</ref> The song went viral on social media January 2021 with over 3 million streams on Spotify and 3.3 million views on the music video on YouTube. Monaelo later released the singles "Girls Outside" and "Suck It Up."<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Seabrook III|first=Robby|date=September 15, 2021|title=The Break Presents - Monaleo|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/monaleo-interview-the-break/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-10-07|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> "Girls Outside" samples "Outside (Better Days)" by [[MO3 (rapper)|MO3]] & OG Bobby Billions.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Stewart|first=Shelby|date=2021-07-08|title=Houston's Monaleo releases 'Girls Outside,' a summer revenge anthem|url=https://www.chron.com/culture/music/article/Houston-artist-Monaleo-girls-outside-music-video-16301151.php|access-date=2021-10-07|website=Chron|language=en-US}}</ref> Monaelo released a music video for the single, "Suck It Up."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Strauss|first=Matthew|date=2021-08-11|title=Monaleo Shares Video for New Song "Suck It Up"|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/monaleo-shares-video-for-new-song-suck-it-up-watch/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-10-07|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|date=2021-08-17|title=Listen to Monaleo's "Suck it Up": The Ones|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/monaleo-suck-it-up-new-song-listen/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-10-07|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref> 

Monaelo is represented by the Stomp Down collective.<ref name=":0" />  

== Personal life ==
Monaleo's younger brother is rapper Yung Rampage. She has shared publicly about her past [[Suicide attempt|suicide attempts]] and [[mental health]].<ref name=":0" />
