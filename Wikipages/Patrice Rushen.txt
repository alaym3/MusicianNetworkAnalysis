'''Patrice Louise Rushen''' (born September 30, 1954) is an American jazz pianist and R&B singer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Film Academy Invites 819 New Members, With 36 Percent People of Color|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/race/film-academy-invites-819-new-members-36-percent-people-color-1301209|access-date=2020-07-29|website=The Hollywood Reporter|language=en}}</ref> She is also a composer, record producer, multi-instrumentalist, songwriter, and music director.

Her 1982 single "[[Forget Me Nots]]" received a [[25th Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] nomination for [[Best Female R&B Vocal Performance]]. The instrumental "Number One" from her album ''[[Straight from the Heart (Patrice Rushen album)|Straight from the Heart]]'' earned an additional Grammy nomination for best instrumental. Her 12th album ''[[Signature (Patrice Rushen album)|Signature]]'' also received a [[40th Grammy Awards|Grammy nomination]] for best instrumental in 1998.<ref name="Grammy" />

Rushen also serves as an ambassador for artistry in education at the [[Berklee College of Music]] and the chair of the popular music program at the [[USC Thornton School of Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.berklee.edu/people/patrice-rushen|title=Patrice Rushen: Ambassador for Artistry in Education|publisher=[[Berklee College of Music]]|website=berklee.edu}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.usc.edu/patrice-rushen/|title=Patrice Rushen: Chair, Popular Music Program|publisher=[[University of Southern California]]|website=usc.edu}}</ref>

==Biography==
Rushen is the elder of two daughters born to Allen and Ruth Rushen.<ref name='CA-Birth-Index'/> Patrice was three years old when she began playing the piano, and by the time she was six, she was giving classical recitals.<ref name=Russonello>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/07/17/arts/music/patrice-rushen-jazz.html|title=Patrice Rushen Found Success in Jazz and Dance Music. She Hasn't Been Forgotten|first=Giovanni |last=Russonello|date=July 17, 2019|access-date=March 25, 2020|df=mdy-all}}</ref> In her teens, she attended [[Locke High School]] and later earned a degree in music from the University of Southern California.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usc.edu/schools/music/alumni/scenter_stage/prushen.html/|title=Patrice Rushen MM '76|access-date=2008-10-06|publisher=University of Southern California|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081128161904/http://www.usc.edu/schools/music/alumni/scenter_stage/prushen.html/|archive-date=November 28, 2008|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

After winning a competition at the age of 17 that enabled her to perform with her band at the [[Monterey Jazz Festival]], Rushen signed with the [[Prestige Records|Prestige]] label, releasing three albums with them – ''[[Prelusion]]'' (1974), ''[[Before the Dawn (Patrice Rushen album)|Before the Dawn]]'' (1975), and ''[[Shout It Out (Patrice Rushen album)|Shout It Out]]'' (1977).<ref name=Russonello /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Patrice Rushen {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/patrice-rushen-mn0000743968/biography|access-date=2020-08-07|website=AllMusic|language=en-us}}</ref> In 1978, when she was 23, she began recording with [[Elektra Records|Elektra]].<ref name=Russonello />

Rushen married Marc St. Louis, a concert tour manager and live show production specialist,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.toimoore.com/unbreakable.php |title=Celebrity Author |publisher=Toi Moore |access-date=2013-10-28}}</ref> in 1986.<ref name="minglecity.com">{{cite web |url=http://minglecity.com/group/realmenofiseecolor/forum/topics/rhythm-blues-legend-5 |title=Rhythm & Blues Legend = Patrice Rushen |publisher=Mingle City |access-date=2013-10-28 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029200058/http://minglecity.com/group/realmenofiseecolor/forum/topics/rhythm-blues-legend-5 |archive-date=October 29, 2013 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> They have one son, Cameron and one daughter named Jadyn.<ref name="minglecity.com"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1999/06/15/classified/paid-notice-deaths-st-louis-marie.html |title=Paid Notice: Deaths ST. LOUIS, MARIE – New York Times |work=Nytimes.com |date=June 15, 1999 |access-date=2013-10-28}}</ref> The name of her publishing company, Baby fingers Inc., is pulled from her nickname Babyfingers for her tiny hands.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Baby Fingers, Inc.|url=https://www.discogs.com/label/592565-Baby-Fingers-Inc|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Discogs|language=en}}</ref> In 2005, Rushen received an honorary doctorate of Music degree from Berklee College of Music. She is the chair of popular music at USC and the ambassador of artistry in Education at the Berklee College of Music.<ref>{{cite web |title=Honorary Degree Recipients| url=http://www.berklee.edu/about/honorary-degree-recipients|website=Berklee.edu}}</ref> She has served as the film composer for numerous movies television shows and documentaries. She has been a member of [[jazz fusion]] band [[CAB (band)|CAB]], The Meeting (GRP Records) with [[Leon "Ndugu" Chancler|Ndugu Chancler]], [[Alphonso Johnson]] and Ernie Watts.<ref name="CAB">{{cite web | url=http://news.allaboutjazz.com/cab-with-bunny-brunel-and-tony-macalpine-live-at-the-baked-potato.php | title="C.A.B." with Bunny Brunel and Tony MacAlpine Live at the Baked Potato | publisher=[[All About Jazz]] | date=May 29, 2014 | access-date=September 5, 2014}}</ref> Rushen is also a music director, having worked on various television events as well as [[Janet Jackson|Janet Jackson's]] world tour '[[Janet (album)|Janet]]'.

Her song "Hang It Up" was featured on the 2005 video game ''[[Fahrenheit (2005 video game)|Fahrenheit]]''.<ref name="Fahrenheit">{{cite web | url=http://www.ign.com/faqs/2005/indigo-prophecy-soundtrack-lyrics-faq-669326 | title=Indigo Prophecy – Soundtrack Lyrics FAQ | publisher=[[IGN]] | date=November 22, 2005 | access-date=September 5, 2014 | author=Rack, Donald}}</ref>

==Cultural impact==
Rushen's songs are sampled often in other artists music. The chorus from "[[Forget Me Nots]]" was used as the music for the 1997 song "[[Men in Black (song)|Men in Black]]". Rushen was credited as writer and composer, along with [[Will Smith]] and Terri McFadden. The same chorus can be heard in [[George Michael]]'s song "[[Fastlove]]". "Forget Me Nots" previously found its way into the trampolining scene in the film [[Big (1988 film)|Big]]. Her song "[[Haven't You Heard (Patrice Rushen song)|Haven't You Heard]]" was sampled in Kirk Franklin's Looking For You from the [[Norbit]] soundtrack.

Rushen was the first woman to serve as music director for the [[46th Annual Grammy Awards|46th]], [[47th Annual Grammy Awards|47th]], and [[48th Annual Grammy Awards|48th]] Grammy Awards. She was the only woman music director/conductor/arranger for a late-night show titled ''The Midnight Hour'', which aired on CBS in 1990.
