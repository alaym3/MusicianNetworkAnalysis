'''Fabien Marsaud''' (born 31 July 1977<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Durieu|first=Laurence|date=2008-12-31|title=Grand Corps Malade|url=http://archive.wikiwix.com/cache/index2.php?url=http://www.vsd.fr/contenu-editorial/en-coulisses/cv-de-stars/1148-grand-corps-malade|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-22|website=archive.wikiwix.com|publisher=[[VSD (French magazine)|VSD]]|language=fr}}</ref>), known professionally as '''Grand Corps Malade''' (GCM), is a French [[Poetry slam|slam]] poet and lyricist. He has released seven studio albums so far, and all of them reached top-five on the [[Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique|SNEP]] chart. GCM has started writing and performing [[A cappella|a capella]] at slam events in 2003. Three years later, in 2006, he signed with [[Universal Music Group|Universal]]'s affiliate [[AZ (label)|AZ]] and released his debut album ''[[Midi 20]]'', which became top-ten selling record of the year in France.

Its follow-up ''[[Enfant de la ville]]'' (2008) and the third album ''[[3ème temps]]'' (2010) were less successful commercially. In 2013, he released family-motivated ''[[Funambule (album)|Funambule]]'', while ''Il nous restera ça'' (2015) featured 11 collaborations with other musicians. His sixth album, ''Plan B'' (2018), reached number two on the SNEP album chart. In 2020, he released ''Mesdames'' (Ladies), featuring ten duets with women, which topped the French chart. Its lead single, "Mais je t'aime" (But I love you) – a duet with [[Camille Lellouche]], has become his highest-charting song on the French singles chart.

GCM's accolades include three [[Victoires de la Musique|Victories of Music]] awarded by French [[Ministry of Culture (France)|Ministry of Culture]]. His stage name means "tall sick body" in French, a reference to his height (nearly 6'4" or 1.94m) and to a spine injury that forces him to walk with a crutch, due to a 1997 accident that originally had doctors tell him he would never walk again.

==Early life==
Marsaud was born on 31 July 1977, in [[Le Blanc-Mesnil]], [[Seine-Saint-Denis]].<ref name=":0" /> His mother was a librarian. His father, Jacques Marsaud, was a regional civil servant: general [[Communes of France|commune]] secretary in [[Noisy-le-Sec]] and [[Saint-Denis, Seine-Saint-Denis|Saint-Denis]], later on director general of services at [[Val-de-Marne]]'s [[Departmental council (France)|departmental council]] and then at Plaine Commune [[Agglomeration communities in France|agglomeration community]] ([[:fr:Établissement public territorial Plaine Commune|fr]]: [[:fr:Établissement public territorial|EPT]]).<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Walter|first=Emmanuelle|date=2007-02-08|title="Saint-Denis où j'ai grandi"|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090106074759/http://parisobs.nouvelobs.com/article/culture/saint-denis-ou-jai-grandi/754,2571,folder-754,page1.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-22|website=[[Wayback Machine]]|publisher=[[L'Obs]]|language=fr}}</ref> They lived in Saint-Denis. Marsaud excelled in his classes, particularly in literary courses, he wrote his first texts aged 14-15. But sports won, at one point he was simultaneously in three clubs: tennis, athletics and basketball; the latter became his greatest passion.<ref name=":0" /> At 17, he was offered to join training center in [[Toulouse]], but preferred to stay in Saint-Denis. After playing in [[Nanterre 92|JSF Nanterre]] and in Saint Denis, Marsaud signed with 3rd division level team in [[Aubervilliers]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Joby|first=Stéphane|date=2008-04-06|title=Grand Corps Malade: "Le sport plus fort que la musique"|url=https://www.lejdd.fr/Sport/Grand-Corps-Malade-Le-sport-plus-fort-que-la-musique-94890-3089372|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130521034229/http://www.lejdd.fr/cmc/sport/200814/grand-corps-malade-le-sport-plus-fort-que-la-musique_108470.html|archive-date=2013-05-21|access-date=2021-05-22|website=[[Le Journal du Dimanche]]|publisher=}}</ref> He earned a [[Diplôme d'études universitaires générales|DEUG]] degree majoring in physical education.<ref name=":1" />

In July 1997, during a sports camp where Marsaud was a supervisor, a diving accident in a swimming pool caused him to displace his spine; he was later told he would never walk again.<ref name=":0" /> However in 1999, after a year of intensive treatment, he regained the ability to walk, although he usually has to use a crutch.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Morbin|first=Ludivine|date=2006-10-19|title=Grand Corps Malade : L'art du rebond|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120209171625/http://www.sportweek.fr/sports/basket/0/grand-corps-malade-art-rebond-5761.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=9 February 2012|access-date=22 February 2012|website=[[Wayback Machine]]|publisher=sportweek.fr}}</ref> Marsaud continued studying and earned [[Master of Advanced Studies#France and francophone countries|DESS]] master degree in sport management. He worked in marketing for [[Stade de France]] between 2001 and 2005, but became disillusioned with the job.<ref name=":1" />

==Career==
Marsaud discovered [[Poetry slam|slam]] in 2003, and started writing poems.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Lefranc|first=Odile|date=2019-07-19|title=Grand Corps Malade|url=https://lelephant-larevue.fr/thematiques/politique-et-societe/grand-corps-malade/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-23|website=L'Éléphant lelephant-larevue.fr|language=fr}}</ref> On 23 October, he made his first appearance as a [[Poetry slam|slam]] artist in a bar at the [[Place de Clichy]], Paris, where he recited his first artistic piece titled "Cassiopée".<ref>[http://www.universalmusic.fr/artiste/grand--corps--malade/ Universal Music: Biography of Grand Corps Malade] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091222224203/http://www.universalmusic.fr/artiste/Grand--Corps--Malade |date=22 December 2009 }}</ref> And that is when he has chosen his stage name Grand Corps Malade. He then took part in many slam events with Collectif 129H and John Pucc'Chocolat. In 2004, he conducted "Slam'Alikoum", a monthly feature at the "Café Culturel de Saint-Denis" with his friend John Pucc'. He co-founded "Le Cercle des Poètes sans Instru" ("The Circle of Poets Without Instruments"), a group of 7 slam artists including John Pucc’, Droopy, Techa, the 129H members and himself.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Le Vaillant|first=Serge|date=2006-04-11|title=Sous les étoiles exactement – Grand Corps Malade du 11 avril 2006|url=https://www.franceinter.fr/emissions/sous-les-etoiles-exactement/sous-les-etoiles-exactement-11-avril-2006|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-23|website=[[France Inter]]|language=fr}}</ref>

Grand Corps Malade gained more fame in slam circles in 2005 through appearances at Reservoir, a club that featured upcoming artists like Jamel Comedy Club. He opened for [[Cheb Mami]] at [[Stade de France]], and for Mouss and Hakim at the "Boule Noire". That same year, his friend [[S Petit Nico]] offered to make a musical track to accompany his poetry.

[[File:Grand Corps Malade 20060614 Fnac 07.jpg|thumb|upright=0.9|Grand Corps Malade signing for fans (2006)|left]]
GCM signed with [[AZ (label)|AZ]] label, and on 26 March 2006 released his debut studio album, ''[[Midi 20]]'', which became top ten selling album of the year in France. To promote the record, he went on a tour with 120 shows, including two sold-out concerts in [[La Cigale]] in Paris, and a performance at the [[Les Francofolies de La Rochelle]] festival.

In 2007, he won two [[Victoires de la Musique]] awards for 'Album revelation' and 'Stage artist stage revelation'. GCM appeared on many television shows and in [[Edouard Baer]]'s ''Grand Cabaret''.

In 2008, he released his second album ''[[Enfant de la ville]]''. The same year, he performed at the [[Festival d'été de Québec]] gaining popularity throughout the [[Geographical distribution of French speakers|French-language speaking world]]. He also conducted many workshops to introduce youngsters in his community to slam poetry in Saint Denis and elsewhere. The result was ''Génération Slam'' album, released in November 2008 by a diversified group of amateur slam artists.<ref>[http://www.philippevallin.com/article-25158097.html Benoît Lagarrigue article – Événement à la ligne 13 – "Génération slam"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110502073415/http://www.philippevallin.com/article-25158097.html |date=2 May 2011 }} (in French)</ref>

2010 saw the release of his third studio album, ''[[3ème temps]]'', featuring collaboration with [[Charles Aznavour]], and song "Roméo kiffe Juliette" – a [[Shakespeare]]'s adaptation. The two adolescent lovers were transposed into a suburb of Paris, and the family divide transformed into a religious divide between a Jewish Juliette and the Muslim Roméo. The song's video clip choreography was arranged by hip hop dancer [[Bintou Dembélé]], one of the pioneer figures of hip hop in France.<ref>{{Cite journal|year=2014|title=Un témoignage de Bintou Dembele: S/T/R/A/T/E/S. Trente ans de Hip-Hop dans le corps|journal=Africultures|volume=n° 99 – 100|pages= 250–261}}</ref>

In 2011, GCM released the single "[[Inch'Allah (Grand Corps Malade song)|Inch'Allah]]" featuring [[Reda Taliani]], which had become his most successful single until then.

In 2013, he released his fourth studio album, ''[[Funambule (album)|Funambule]]'', after quitting his former label [[AZ (label)|AZ]] and working with the independent labels Believe Recording and Anouche Productions.<ref>{{cite web|title=Grand Corps Malade sort "Funambule" : un nouvel album plein d'émotion... et de surprises|url=http://leplus.nouvelobs.com/contribution/961284-grand-corps-malade-sort-funambule-un-album-poetique-a-forte-decharge-emotionnelle.html|work=Le Nouvel Observateur|access-date=22 May 2014}}</ref> His fifth album titled ''Il nous restera ça'' was released in 2015.

GCM's first feature film, [[Patients (film)|''Patients'']] (2016), based on his own [[autobiographical novel]] – describing his accident and rehabilitation period, was released commercially in 2017. He co-directed with Mehdi Idir, and it was filmed in the center where he carried out his rehabilitation. The movie was a success in France and garnered 4 nominations for [[César Awards]].

In 2018, GCM released sixth studio album, ''Plan B'', with producer Jean-Rachid. It was first record on which he sang, and also his first to top the French [[SNEP]] albums chart.<ref name=":2" /> In support of ''Plan B'', he embarked on tour with more than 100 shows. The album was certified platinum in France.

His second film, teen [[Comedy film|comedy]] [[Drama film|drama]] ''[[School Life (2019 film)|School Life]]'' (2019), received positive reviews from critics. It was co-directed with Mehdi Idir again.

In April 2020, Grand Corps Malade released charity single, "Effets secondaires", with all proceeds going to two hospitals in Parisian region. It was a collaboration with [[Quentin Mosimann|Mosimann]] and producer Rachid Kallouche.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-10|title=Coronavirus : Grand Corps Malade sort une chanson au profit de deux hôpitaux|url=https://www.cnews.fr/culture/2020-04-10/coronavirus-grand-corps-malade-sort-une-chanson-au-profit-de-deux-hopitaux-945722|url-status=live|access-date=2021-06-03|website=[[CNews]]|language=fr}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Citation|title=Effets secondaires - Single by Grand Corps Malade|date=2020-04-10|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/effets-secondaires-single/1506639368|publisher=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=2021-06-03}}</ref> On 19 June 2020, he released the lead single from his upcoming seventh album: "Mais je t'aime" (But I love you), a duet with [[Camille Lellouche]]. The single became his highest-charting song on the SNEP singles chart to date, peaking at number nine. The album titled ''Mesdames'' (Ladies), a tribute to women, was released on 11 September. It features also 9 other collaborations, namely with [[Véronique Sanson]], [[Louane (singer)|Louane]], Suzane, [[Laura Smet]], Camille Berthollet and her sister Julie, as well as Manon, Amuse-Bouche, and Alicia. ''Mesdames'' reached number 1 on both French and Belgium albums charts, and was certified triple platinum in France.

In 2021, he won [[Victoires de la Musique]] award for the song "Mais je t'aime".

==Artistry==
Grand Corps Malade helps [[Poetry slam|slam]] go beyond the intimate setting of cafes and typical slam scenes. The texts on his albums are spoken sometimes [[A cappella|a cappella]], but mostly they are accompanied by a minimalist melody in the background. He collaborates with musicians such as [[String quartet|string quartets]], and [[flute]] players.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lejard|first=Laurent|date=2006-06-02|title=Grand Corps Malade, le slameur qui monte|url=https://www.yanous.com/tribus/moteur/moteur060602.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-23|website=Yanous ! Le magazine francophone du handicap|language=fr}}</ref>

==In popular culture==
*In 2007, French hip hop / jazz band [[Hocus Pocus (group)|Hocus Pocus]] mentioned him in the track "Place 54" on their similarly titled album ''Place 54''.
*In 2007, French parody rap group [[Fatal Bazooka]] mentioned him in their album ''[[T'As Vu ?]]'' in a slam or in the track "Crêpes au froment" (a version of "Fous ta cagoule") in which they parody his name by saying "Vas-y dis leur toi aussi Grand Cul Malade!" 
*In 2010, French singer [[Dorothée]] in her show at [[Olympia (Paris)|l'Olympia]] in Paris introduced her song "Valise 2010", a slam, by giving her songwriter and friend [[Jean-Luc Azoulay]] the nickname "Grand Cerveau Malade" in reference to Grand Corps Malade
*His adopted name is used in parodies of paronymes and antonymes, like what happened in the program ''Groland'' in its parody "Petit Corps Normal", or by Fabrice Eboué in Jamel Comedy Club talking about [[Amelle Chahbi]] calling her "P'tit corps salade". He is also parodied in les ''Guignols de l'Info'' broadcast on [[Canal+]]
