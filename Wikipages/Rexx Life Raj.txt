'''Faraji Omar Wright''', also known as '''Rexx Life Raj''', is an American rapper and singer. He has released four albums, including the ''Father Figure'' trilogy, and four EPs.

== Early life ==
Faraji Omar Wright grew up in [[Berkeley, California]]. His mother was in a gospel group called The Marshall Quartet with his aunts, uncle, and grandmother. His father was a Black Panther. In middle school, Wright began recording his own music, making the beats on a Casio keyboard and singing with a karaoke machine, making CDs he sold at school.<ref name="East Bay Express">{{cite news |last1=St. John |first1=Matt |title=How Rexx Life Raj Went from Being a Delivery Man to Hip-Hop's Next Big Thing |url=https://www.eastbayexpress.com/oakland/how-rexx-life-raj-went-from-being-a-delivery-man-to-hip-hops-next-big-thing/Content?oid=11372559 |accessdate=June 6, 2020 |work=East Bay Express |date=December 6, 2017}}</ref>  Wright went to middle and high school with [[G-Eazy]].<ref name="Billboard">{{cite news |last1=Saponara |first1=Michael |title=Rexx Life Raj Is Back in Control of His Happiness & It's Paying Off in the Music |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8543797/rexx-life-raj-father-figure-3-interview) |accessdate=June 4, 2020 |work=Billboard |date=November 26, 2019}}</ref><ref name="MTV">{{cite news |last1=Holmes |first1=Charles |title=REXX LIFE RAJ AND G-EAZY WENT FROM HIGH SCHOOL CLASSMATES TO COLLABORATORS |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3047499/rexx-life-raj-g-eazy-collaborators-interview/ |accessdate=June 4, 2020 |work=MTV.com |date=November 17, 2017}}</ref>

In 2010, he played offensive tackle and guard for the [[Fiesta Bowl]]-winning [[Boise State University]] football team.<ref name="Forbes">{{cite news |last1=Payne |first1=Ogden |title=3 Hip-Hop Artists Colleges Should Book This Semester |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/ogdenpayne/2017/08/31/3-hip-hop-artists-colleges-should-book-this-semester/#2addeddc2a43 |accessdate=June 4, 2020 |work=Forbes |date=August 31, 2017}}</ref> After graduation, Wright returned home to California to pursue music while working at his parents' package delivery business.<ref name="KQED">{{cite news |last1=Voynovskaya |first1=Nastia |title=In the Algorithm Age, Rexx Life Raj Charts a Path Without Gimmicks |url=https://www.kqed.org/arts/13869513/rexx-life-raj-father-figure-3-empire |accessdate=June 4, 2020 |work=KQED |date=November 7, 2019}}</ref>
 
"Neo-soul music became my favorite when I could begin to appreciate it. I was into Lauryn Hill, Erykah Badu, and Musiq Soulchild. [Kid Cudi's] ''Man on the Moon: End of Day'' is one of, if not my favorite album of all-time", he told [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]].<ref name="Billboard" />

== Career ==
Wright adopted the professional stage name Rexx Life Raj for his rap and singing career. He released his first EP for sale, ''Hidden Clouds'', in 2014, which featured the single "Put On". That same year, he released the ''Portraits'' EP with producer Tele Fresco.<ref name="SF Weekly">{{cite news |last1=Casagrande |first1=Tim |title=Three Must-See Acts This Week — 11/14-11/21 |url=https://www.sfweekly.com/culture/three-must-see-acts-this-week-1114-1121/ |accessdate=June 6, 2020 |work=SF Weekly |date=November 14, 2019}}</ref> In 2015, he released another two projects, the full-length album, ''The Escape'',<ref name="East Bay Express" /> with guests [[Iamsu!]], Will Fraker, City Shawn, and the EP, ''Dreamland: Telegraph Ave.'', with YMTK, Ellen Purtell, and [[Lolo Zouaï]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |title=Rexx Life Raj |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/rexx-life-raj-mn0003549675/biography |website=allmusic.com |accessdate=June 6, 2020}}</ref>

In 2016, with his first album, ''Father Figure'',<ref name="Forbes" /> Raj developed his sound, combining rhymes with singing "confessional" and political lyrics in tracks like "Handheld GPS" and, the album's first single, "Moxie Java"<ref name="SF Weekly" /> featuring another Vallejo-area rapper [[Nef the Pharaoh]].<ref name="allmusic" />

Raj's sequel album, ''Father Figure 2: Flourish'', came out in 2017 with features from G-Eazy,<ref name="MTV" /> Russ, and Iman Europe.<ref name="Vice">{{cite news |title=Rexx Life Raj's 'Father Figure 2: Flourish' Is Perfect for The Long Weekend |url=https://www.vice.com/en_ca/article/43nzwg/rexx-life-rajs-father-figure-2-flourish-is-perfect-for-the-long-weekend |accessdate=June 10, 2020 |work=Vice |date=November 22, 2017}}</ref> That year he toured with Mr. Carmack.<ref name="Stereogum">{{cite news |last1=Trewn |first1=Pranav |title=Rexx Life Raj – "Handheld GPS" Video |url=https://www.stereogum.com/1933158/rexx-life-raj-handheld-gps-video/video/ |accessdate=June 10, 2020 |work=Stereogum |date=April 6, 2017}}</ref>

In 2018, he released EP ''California Poppy'', produced entirely by Kyle Betty,<ref name="Fader 2">{{cite news |last1=Dandridge-Lemco |first1=Ben |title=Rexx Life Raj pays homage to a Bay Area classic with help from E-40 on "Sprinkle Me" |url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/21/rexx-life-raj-e-40-sprinkle-me-premiere |accessdate=June 10, 2020 |work=The Fader |date=November 21, 2018}}</ref> and toured twice in support of both [[Marc E. Bassy]] and [[Bas (rapper)|Bas]], as well as his first headlining tour in Europe in 2019.<ref name="KQED" />

In 2019, Raj released ''Father Figure 3: Somewhere Out There'' produced by Sango, [[Kenny Beats]],<ref name="BB2">{{cite news |last1=Saponara |first1=Michael |title=Rexx Life Raj Connects With Kehlani for Woozy 'Your Way': Listen |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8541592/rexx-life-raj-kehlani-your-way |accessdate=June 11, 2020 |work=Billboard |date=October 30, 2019}}</ref> JULiA LEWiS, Avedon<ref name="dj booth">{{cite news |last1=Rindner |first1=Grant |title=Rexx Life Raj Hopes Rap Never Becomes a Job: Interview |url=https://djbooth.net/features/2019-11-26-rexx-life-raj-father-figure-3-interview |accessdate=June 12, 2020 |work=djbooth.net |date=November 26, 2019}}</ref> and Bay Area producers Kyle Betty,<ref name="Fader 2" /> DTB, Wax Roof and Drew Banga.<ref name="Power">{{cite news |last1=Barnes |first1=Deion |title=Rexx Life Raj Drops New Album "Father Figure 3: Somewhere Out There" |url=https://power1051.iheart.com/content/2019-11-07-rexx-life-raj-drops-new-album-father-figure-3-somewhere-out-there/ |accessdate=June 11, 2020 |work=Power1051.iheart.com |date=November 7, 2019}}</ref> Songs included "Burgundy Regal," about his two childhood best friends, one of whom was eventually killed, the other incarcerated; "Your Way", an acoustic duet with [[Kehlani]] that also featured. [[Russ (rapper)|Russ]], and was premiered on [[Zane Lowe]]'s [[Beats 1]] show. ''Father Figure 3: Somewhere Out'' was recorded most of the vocals in hotels and Airbnbs on the road touring as opening act for Bas.<ref name="KQED" /><ref name="Fader">{{cite news |last1=Dandridge-Lemco |first1=Ben |title=Rexx Life Raj connects with Kehlani for new single "Your Way" |url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/10/29/rexx-life-raj-kehlani-your-way-father-figure-3 |accessdate=June 6, 2020 |work=The Fader |date=October 29, 2019}}</ref>

Raj did a headline tour of the United States in 2019.<ref name="Complex">{{cite news |last1=Espinosa |first1=Joshua |title=Rexx Life Raj's 'Father Figure 3: Somewhere Out There' Has Arrived |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/11/rexx-life-raj-father-figure-3-somewhere-out-there-stream |accessdate=June 10, 2020 |work=Complex |date=November 6, 2019}}</ref>
