music#Crossover as a mix of genres|crossover]] style that incorporates elements of [[hip hop music|hip hop]], [[Dub music|dub]], [[ska]], [[reggae]], [[rock music|rock]], [[punk rock|punk]], ==History==
Dubioza kolektiv was formed in 2003<ref name=aboutus/><ref name=dvom>{{Cite news | url=https://www.rtvslo.si/kultura/glasba/dubioza-kolektiv-sejejo-svoj-dvom/134191 | title=DUBIOZA KOLEKTIV SEJEJO SVOJ DVOM | trans-title=THE DUBIOSIS COLLECTIVE SOWING TWO | work=[[Radiotelevizija Slovenija]] | date=January 6, 2005 | language=sl-si}}</ref> by Brano Jakubović,<ref name=prevara/><ref name=ziggi>{{Cite web | url=https://www.ziggipapers.com/?subpageid=134&contid=878 |title=Ziggi - INTERNATIONAL - Products | website=www.ziggipapers.com |language=en}}</ref> Vedran Mujagić, Almir Hasanbegović and Adis Zvekić with the merger of two musical groups, ''Ornament'' and ''Gluho doba''.<ref name=":14">{{Cite news | url=http://hushhushbiz.com/spotlight-interview-with-seven-piece-bosnian-band-dubioza-kolektiv/ | title=Spotlight Interview with Seven Piece Bosnian Band Dubioza Kolektiv | work=Hush Hush Biz| date=November 24, 2016 | language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=osem/><ref name=ziggi/> The band originates from [[Sarajevo]]<ref name=ziggi/> and [[Zenica]].<ref name=Apsurdistan/><ref name=ziggi/> Dubioza kolektiv initially also featured a female vocal, but the singer, Adisa Zvekić, eventually left the group to pursue a solo career.<ref name=osem/> The group's performances have been multilingual (Bosnian and English) ever since the band's inception.<ref name=Apsurdistan>{{Cite news | url=https://www.rtvslo.si/zabava/glasba/dobrodosli-v-apsurdistanu-fikcijska-drzava-kot-odgovor-na-mojstre-ignorance-politike/307314 | title=Dobrodošli v Apsurdistanu, fikcijska država kot odgovor na "mojstre ignorance" - politike | trans-title=Welcome to Absurdistan, a fictional state in response to the "masters of ignorance" - politics | first=Claudia | last=Kopina | work=[[Radiotelevizija Slovenija]] | date=April 23, 2013}}</ref>

Singer Adis Zvekić stated that he first set up a band that served as a forerunner for Dubioza in 1993, when he was 13 years old. Due to the [[Bosnian War]], there was no electricity; the band used to meet in the evenings and have [[jam session]]s in the dark.<ref name=europe>{{Cite news | url=https://www.balcanicaucaso.org/eng/Areas/Bosnia-Herzegovina/Dubioza-about-Europe-93862 | title=Dubioza about Europe | first=Luka | last=Zanoni   | work=[[Osservatorio Balcani e Caucaso Transeuropa]] | date=May 13, 2011 | language=it}}</ref> All band members declare themselves to have had a working-class background, being the children of workers and peasants.<ref name=":14" />

Though the group considered contending to represent Bosnia in the [[Eurovision Song Contest]], they eventually decided against the idea as they would have to sign a contract obligating them to refrain from making any political statements. Instead, they recorded and released a highly critical and political song; ''Euro song''.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.rtvslo.si/zabava/glasba/video-dubioza-kolektiv-prerasla-balkan-bo-dorasla-zahodu/264532 | title=Video: Dubioza kolektiv prerasla Balkan. Bo dorasla Zahodu? | trans-title=Video: Dubioza collective outgrows the Balkans. Will it reach the West? | work=[[Radiotelevizija Slovenija]] | date=August 21, 2011}}</ref><ref name=europe/>

===Dubioza kolektiv, and Open Wide (2004–2006)===
Dubioza kolektiv released their self-titled first album in April 2004. Two of the songs also featured accompanying music videos.<ref name=dvom/>

The ''Open Wide'' EP was released in December 2004, and featured four remixes of songs from the ''Dubioza kolektiv'' album, and two up until then unpublished tracks featuring English dub poet [[Benjamin Zephaniah]], and Mush Khan of the Pakistani-British group, [[Fun-Da-Mental]].<ref name=dvom/>

===Dubnamite (2006–2008)===
Dubnamite features 10 songs.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://dubioza.org/music/dubnamite/ | title=Dubnamite | publisher=Dubioza kolektiv | language=en-US}}</ref>

===Firma ilegal (2008–2010)===
''Firma ilegal'' (English: ''Illegal Enterprise'') was the first album where all lyrics were written in the Bosnian language. The album addresses the social and political situation and [[Transition economy]] in Balkan societies, discussing corruption, [[privatization]] vs. [[social ownership]], and the opportunistic political oligarchy.<ref name=vse/><ref name=ilegalno/><ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.rtvslo.si/kultura/poslusamo/dubioza-kolektiv-firma-ilegal/156023 | title=Dubioza kolektiv: Firma ilegal | trans-title=Dubiose Collective: Firm Illegal | work=[[Radiotelevizija Slovenija]] | date=July 28, 2008}}</ref>

===5 do 12 (2010–2011)===
Dubioza's fourth album, ''5 do 12'', was the first to be released online in full and for download free of charge. The album featured [[Plavi orkestar]] vocalist [[Saša Lošić]], and Macedonian ska punk group, [[Superhiks]]. The CD release also featured remixes of the songs by other artists and recordings from MTV Live @ Skirlibaba Studio sessions. The work continued on the path set by Firma illegal, featuring socially critical lyrics written in the Bosnian language.<ref name=osem/><ref name=5do12/><ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.zurnal24.si/magazin/film-glasba-tv/albuma-dubioze-kolektiv-ze-zastonj-77914 |title=Albuma Dubioze kolektiv že zastonj | trans-title=Album Dubioze kolektiv for free | work=[[Žurnal24]] | date=April 12, 2010 | language=hr}}</ref>

===Wild Wild East (2011–2013)===
''Wild Wild East'' was the first album released for an international audience/market, marking the group's entry onto the international stage some 8 years after its founding.<ref name=Apsurdistan/>

===Apsurdistan (2013–2015)===
Apsurdistan features 13 songs.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://dubioza.org/music/apsurdistan/ | title=Apsurdistan | publisher=Dubioza kolektiv | language=en-US}}</ref>

===Happy Machine (2015–2017)===
Lyrics for the songs on the ''Happy Machine'' album were penned in response to events that unfolded over the 2 years prior to its publication, including the [[Gezi Park protests]], [[The Pirate Bay trial]], and the [[European refugee crisis]], and featured numerous collaborating artists from around the globe and lyrics in English, Spanish, Punjabi, and Italian. The album title is an allusion to the makeshift distillation apparatus used to distill [[Rakia|rakija]], a regionally popular variety of moonshine. The drink is also mentioned in the song ''No Escape (From Balkan)'', and a blueprint for the distillation contraption is also featured on the album cover (in protest of new EU regulations requiring a permit even for small scale spirit production intended for domestic use).<ref name=happymachine>{{Cite web | url=https://dubioza.org/music/happy-machine/ | title=Happy Machine | publisher=Dubioza kolektiv | language=en-US}}</ref>

The group also saw waxing international success, appearing at [[Glastonbury Festival 2015]],<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.rtvslo.si/zabava/glasba/dubioza-kolektiv-za-podporo-beguncem-ta-tezava-se-ne-bo-resila-sama-od-sebe/373508 | title=Dubioza kolektiv za podporo beguncem: Ta težava se ne bo rešila sama od sebe | trans-title=Dubioza Refugee Support Collective: This problem will not solve itself | first=Claudia | last=Kopina | work=[[Radiotelevizija Slovenija]] | date=September 9, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.24sata.hr/show/dubioza-kolektiv-nastupala-je-na-glastonbury-festivalu-425880 | title=Dubioza kolektiv nastupala je na Glastonbury festivalu... | trans-title=Dubioza collective performed at the Glastonbury Festival | first=Ines | last=Madunic | work=[[24sata (Croatia)|24sata]]| date=June 29, 2015 | language=hr}}</ref> and at [[South by Southwest]] in 2016, marking their US debut.<ref name=sxsw>{{Cite web | url=https://schedule.sxsw.com/2016/events/event_MS33856 |title=Dubioza Kolektiv - SXSW 2016 Event Schedule | publisher=[[South by Southwest]]}}</ref><ref name=aboutus/>

===Pjesmice za djecu i odrasle (2017–)===
Dubioza's 2017 album, ''Pjesmice za djecu i odrasle'' (English: ''Songs for Youths and Adults'') features songs written exclusively in [[Bosnian language]].{{Citation needed|date=October 2018}} The album's singles include ''Himna generacije'', ''Treba mi zraka'',<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.24ur.com/ekskluziv/glasba/video-dubioza-kolektiv-tokrat-s-pokojno-hrvasko-glasbeno-legendo-dinom-dvornikom.html | title=VIDEO: Dubioza Kolektiv tokrat s pokojno hrvaško glasbeno legendo Dinom Dvornikom | trans-title=VIDEO: Dubioza Kolektiv this time with the late Croatian music legend Dino Dvornik | work=[[Pop TV]] | date=September 25, 2017 | language=sl}}</ref> and ''Rijaliti''.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.muzika.hr/singl-tjedna-dubioza-kolektiv-rijaliti/ | title=Singl tjedna: Dubioza kolektiv – "Rijaliti" | work=muzika.hr | date=March 5, 2018 | language=hr-HR}}</ref>

==Musical style==
Dubioza kolektiv has been described as a "band without a [[Music genre|genre]]".<ref name=genre>{{Cite news | url=https://cafebabel.com/en/article/dubioza-kolektiv-the-bosnian-band-without-a-genre-5ae00a40f723b35a145e6144/ | title=Dubioza Kolektiv: the Bosnian band without a genre | last=Touriel | first=Aubry | work=[[Cafébabel]] | date=August 21, 2015 | language=en}}</ref> The group's distinct eclectic musical style has been shaped by the members' dissimilar and varied musical influences and backgrounds.<ref name=interview/><ref name=osem/> The group never attempted to define a genre, preferring instead to focus on the ideas they wish to communicate to the audience and tailoring their music accordingly.<ref name=genre/><ref name=prevara>{{Cite news | url=https://www.dnevnik.si/1042729574 | title=Brano Jakubović, Dubioza kolektiv: Glasbena industrija je velika prevara | trans-title=Brano Jakubovic, Dubioza Collective: The music industry is a big scam | first=Žiga | last=Brdnik | work=[[Dnevnik (Slovenia)|Dnevnik]] | date=February 5, 2016}}</ref><ref name=":14" /><ref name=Apsurdistan/>

The group's music often combines serious lyrical themes and content with satirical and humorous lyrical style and presentation, and a lighthearted melody and simple and dancable rhythm, attempting to appeal to as wide an audience as possible in disseminating its art and ideas.<ref name=buzz/><ref name=prevara/><ref name=Apsurdistan/> As keyboardist Brano Jakubović put it: "[Our songs] prove that people can dance and think at the same time."<ref name=prevara/>

The content of many Dubioza songs is addressed and especially pertinent to the wider Balkan region, addressing the shared experience of (post-)[[Transition economy|transitional]] societies, their [[collective consciousness]]es characteristically marred by the perception of widespread corruption, of failed privatisation of [[Social ownership|socially owned]] property, and opportunistic political oligarchy.<ref name=interview/><ref name=vse/><ref name=ilegalno/> With their expanding reach, the band has also begun to address social and political topics common to all Western and global contemporary society, often with lyrics written in English.<ref name=interview/><ref name=Apsurdistan/><ref name=happymachine/><ref>{{Cite news |url= http://www.rtvslo.si/zabava/glasba/dubioza-kolektiv-za-podporo-beguncem-ta-tezava-se-ne-bo-resila-sama-od-sebe/373508|title=Dubioza kolektiv za podporo beguncem: Ta težava se ne bo rešila sama od sebe |work=Prvi interaktivni multimedijski portal, MMC RTV Slovenija |access-date= 2018-10-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url= https://www.rtvslo.si/zabava/glasba/video-dubioza-kolektiv-prerasla-balkan-bo-dorasla-zahodu/264532 |title=Video: Dubioza kolektiv prerasla Balkan. Bo dorasla Zahodu? |work= Prvi interaktivni multimedijski portal, MMC RTV Slovenija |access-date= 2018-10-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url= http://www.sarajevotimes.com/dubioza-kolektiv-is-the-most-wanted-band-at-the-eurosonic-festival-in-holland |title=Dubioza Kolektiv is the most wanted Band at the Eurosonic Festival in Holland - Sarajevo Times |date=2016-01-19 |work= Sarajevo Times |access-date= 2018-10-20 |language=en-US}}</ref> The group nonetheless consistently incorporates a "Balkan sound" into its music, in part to combat common prejudices based on stereotypes regarding the musical form of the region,<ref name=interview/> and to make their music instantly recognisable.<ref name=genre/><ref name=":14" />

The group has also adopted a distinctive on-stage image, featuring yellow-black dress replete with the band's, [[Ziggi]] rolling papers', and [[The Pirate Bay]]' logos, a tight stage choreography, lively and rambunctious performance antics, and proactive audience interaction.<ref name=":9">{{Cite news|url=https://www.delo.si/kultura/glasba/dubioza-kolektiv-rumeno-crna-glasbena-tranzicija.html|title=Dubioza kolektiv - rumeno črna glasbena tranzicija|last=Matoz|first=Zdenko|access-date=2018-10-20|language=sl-si}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.zurnal24.si/magazin/film-glasba-tv/koncert-zakljucili-v-trenirkah-143921|title=Koncert zaključili v trenirkah|access-date=2018-10-20|language=hr}}</ref><ref name=buzz>{{Cite news | url=https://www.buzzmag.co.uk/dubioza-kolektiv-music-interview/ | title=Dubioza Kolektiv - Music Interview | work=Buzz Magazine | date=November 29, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news | url=https://siol.net/trendi/glasba/dubioza-kolektiv-194171 | title=Dubioza Kolektiv | first=Urška | last=Z. | work=[[Siol]] |  date=March 4, 2009 | language=sl}}</ref>

Members of the group embrace a "[[DIY]]" approach to music, writing, recording and producing it in their own studio, self-designing disc covers and websites, and carrying out most of the remaining band-related work like administering the band's social media presence and producing music videos largely by themselves.<ref name=genre/><ref name=interview/> The band has an internal contest where band members who successfully contribute lyrics receive a monetary reward so as to self-encourage the group's songwriting.<ref name=":14" />

Dubioza has consistently embraced socially and politically conscious texts, speaking out about injustices befalling ordinary people, political corruption, and attempting to raise public political awareness.<ref name=pozitivne/><ref name=Apsurdistan/><ref name=":9" /><ref name=sxsw/> Band members believe that it is the duty of artists to utilise their media to promote positive ideas and values.<ref name=pozitivne/>

==Activism==
The group's activism against "right-wing parties, xenophobia and fascism" is in part based on [[Bosnian War|their home country's experience with the repercussions of such ideologies]].<ref name=interview/><ref name=pozitivne/><ref name=":12">{{Cite news |url= https://www.rtvslo.si/zabava/glasba/dubioza-kolektiv-za-podporo-beguncem-ta-tezava-se-ne-bo-resila-sama-od-sebe/373508 |title=Dubioza kolektiv za podporo beguncem: Ta težava se ne bo rešila sama od sebe|work=Prvi interaktivni multimedijski portal, MMC RTV Slovenija|access-date=2018-10-20}}</ref>

Dubioza has supported various civic movements and non-governmental organisations (including by advocating them during live performances), having attended and performed at the [[2012 Slovenian protests]] and [[2015 Macedonian protests]], and supported the [[Dosta!]] Roma awareness raising campaign,<ref name=pozitivne/><ref name=Apsurdistan/><ref name=":9" /> the Refugees Welcome campaign,<ref name=":12" /> a Slovenian environmentalist campaign to preserve the [[Mura River]],<ref name=":9" /> and a campaign to revive the Bosnian Dita Tuzla laundry detergent factory<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.sarajevotimes.com/great-support-to-the-workers-of-dita-tuzla-an-incredible-demand-for-products/|title=Great Support to the Workers of Dita Tuzla: An Incredible Demand for Products - Sarajevo Times|date=2015-06-19|work=Sarajevo Times|access-date=2018-10-20|language=en-US}}</ref> wrecked by the Bosnian War and subsequent privatisation.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.mladina.si/167720/dita-tuzla-bosanski-cudez-in-upanje/|title=Dita Tuzla, bosanski čudež in upanje {{!}} MLADINA.si|work=Mladina.si|access-date=2018-10-20}}</ref> Dubioza also actively advocates for the [[Marijuana legalisation|legalisation of marijuana]] through their lyrics and during live performances.<ref name=":9" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://balkanrock.com/intervjui/dubioza-kolektiv-zaista-pokusavamo-da-izbjegnemo-peglanje-ljudi/|title=Dubioza kolektiv: "Zaista pokušavamo da izbjegnemo 'peglanje' ljudi"|date=2013-04-18|work=Balkanrock.com|access-date=2018-10-20|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=prevara/>

Dubioza kolektiv has made all their recent albums available for download free of charge on their website,<ref name=osem/> also uploading them onto [[The Pirate Bay]].{{Citation needed|date=October 2018}} Their song ''Free.mp3 (The Pirate Bay Song)'' pays homage to the peer-to-peer website and its jailed founders, lampoons global pop culture and political figures including [[Kim Kardashian]], [[Miley Cyrus]], and [[Barack Obama]], and explores copyright in the digital age, internet privacy, and freedom of information. The song and music video were subsequently featured on Pirate Bay's homepage.<ref name=interview/><ref name=":8">{{Cite news|url=https://www.mladina.si/170672/dubioza-kolektiv-podprli-pirate-bay-ta-jim-je-vrnil-z-naslovnico/|title=Dubioza kolektiv podprli Pirate Bay, ta jim je vrnil z naslovnico {{!}} MLADINA.si|work=Mladina.si|access-date=2018-10-20}}</ref><ref name=happymachine/><ref name=prevara/>

Instead of monetising their songs through intellectual property, Dubioza has instead been committed to exploring alternate modes of generating income as artists, including promoting their live performances instead of record sales, marketing the group's merchandise, and soliciting cryptocurrency donations, while regarding the traditional mode of music industry income generation as "outdated" and unfair to the artists.<ref name=interview/><ref name=buzz/> Dubioza believes in free exchange of art, ideas, and knowledge, with the audience voluntarily deciding whether they wish to monetarily reward the authors depending on whether they like their work.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rtvslo.si/zabava/glasba/dubioza-kolektiv-za-podporo-beguncem-ta-tezava-se-ne-bo-resila-sama-od-sebe/373508|title=Dubioza kolektiv za podporo beguncem: Ta težava se ne bo rešila sama od sebe|work=Prvi interaktivni multimedijski portal, MMC RTV Slovenija|access-date=2018-10-20}}</ref> The band has also stated they found releasing albums online has strengthened their bond with their audience.<ref name=osem/><ref name=prevara/>

Dubioza has dedicated multiple concerts to charitable causes and donated the earnings, including to victims of [[2014 Southeast Europe floods]] in Bosnia, and the children's [[Mostar]] Rock School.<ref name=interview/>
