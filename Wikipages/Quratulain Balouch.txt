'''Quratulain Balouch''' ({{lang-ur|{{Nastaliq|قرۃ العین بلوچ}}}}) is a [[Pakistani American]] singer-songwriter.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://propakistani.pk/lens/pakistani-actors-with-dual-citizenship/|title=28 Pakistani Actors Who Hold Dual Citizenship|date=April 28, 2020|website=Lens}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://dailytimes.com.pk/162610/pakistani-industrys-future-good-hands-thanks-talented-lot/|title=Pakistani industry’s future is in good hands... thanks to this talented lot - Daily Times|date=2017-12-19|work=Daily Times|access-date=2018-05-07|language=en-US}}</ref> Also known as QB or the Humsafar Girl, she became popular for her title track [[Woh Humsafar Tha]] in [[Hum TV]]'s serial ''[[Humsafar]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/263981/quratulain-balouch-shines-internationally/.html|title=Quratulain Balouch shines internationally|work=[[The Express Tribune]]|date=30 September 2011|author=Sher Khan|access-date=14 December 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
Balouch has no formal music education. She grew up listening to [[Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan]], [[Muhammad Juman]], Pathanay Khan and others.<ref name=":1"/>

Her singing career started with her cover of [[Reshma]]'s "Ankhian Nu Ren De" in 2011. She came to be known when she was featured in ''[[Coke Studio (Pakistan)|Coke Studio]]'' with [[Jal (band)|Jal]] for a song "Panchi".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://tribune.com.pk/story/226920/quratulain-balouch-entering-the-murky-waters-of-the-music-industry/|title=Quratulain Balouch: Entering the murky waters of the music industry|date=8 August 2011|work=The Express Tribune|access-date=23 September 2017|author=Rafay Mahmood}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thenews.com.pk/magazine/instep-today/195243-Ali-Azmat-and-QB-kill-it-with-Chal-Diye|title=Ali Azmat and QB kill it with 'Chal Diye'|author=Maheen Sabeeh|work=[[The News International|The News]]|access-date=23 September 2017|date=29 March 2017}}</ref> She rose to fame after her award-winning performance of "Woh Humsafar Tha" for 2011 drama serial ''[[Humsafar]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/music/they-call-me-the-humsafar-girl-qurat-ul-ain-balouch/story-KwD8d3pI7NtwcCiQ5A85wM.html|title=They call me the Humsafar Girl: Qurat-ul-ain Balouch|date=10 April 2015|author=Samarth Goyal|access-date=24 September 2017|work=[[Hindustan Times]]}}</ref>

In 2012, she represented her country in a live performance with the [[BBC Philharmonic Orchestra]].<ref name=":0">{{cite news|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/entertainment/pakistani-singers-career-almost-cut-short-after-car-accident-theatened-vocal|title=Pakistani singer's career almost cut short after car accident theatened vocal cords|author=Deepika Shetty|date=29 December 2015|work=[[The Straits Times]]|access-date=23 September 2017}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{cite news|author=Kasmin Fernandes|date=24 April 2015|url=http://timesofindia.com/entertainment/hindi/music/news/Qurat-ul-ain-Balouch-Sufism-answers-my-questions-by-all-means/articleshow/47031576.cms|title=Qurat-ul-ain Balouch: Sufism answers my questions by all means|work=[[The Times of India]]|access-date=23 September 2017}}</ref> In 2016, she made her debut in [[Bollywood]] with song "Kaari Kaari" in film ''[[Pink (2016 film)|Pink]]''.<ref>{{cite news|author=IANS|date=31 August 2016|work=[[Indian Express]]|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/singing-for-bollywoods-pink-became-mandatory-for-pakistan-singer-quratulain-balouch-3006060/|title=Singing for Bollywood's 'Pink' became 'mandatory' for Pakistan singer Quratulain Balouch|access-date=23 September 2017}}</ref>
