'''indigo la End''' is a Japanese rock band led by vocalist and songwriter [[Enon Kawatani]]. The band released their debut extended play in 2012 with independent label Space Shower Records, and were later signed by [[Warner Music Group|Warner]] sub-label [[Unborde]].

== Biography ==
The band first began in April 2009, however after a member change began full activities from February 2010.<ref name="Jackman">{{cite magazine | url=http://jackmanrecords.ro69.jp/contents/artist/ro69jack1011 | title=JACKMAN RECORDS COMPILATION ALBUM vol.4 『RO69JACK 10/11』 | language=Japanese |year=2011 |magazine=[[Rockin' On Japan]] | accessdate=September 21, 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140921012719/http://jackmanrecords.ro69.jp/contents/artist/ro69jack1011|archivedate=September 21, 2014}}</ref> The original bassist, E ni Naranai Kachō, left the band in July 2011.<ref name="Daiji"/> In January he was replaced with Mariko Wada,<ref name="Geocities"/> and the band released their debut extended play ''Sayōnara, Subarashii Sekai'' in April 2012. Mariko Wada left soon after in June, with the band releasing a second extended play ''Nagisa nite'' in September.

The band released their debut album ''Yoru ni Mahō o Kakerarete'' in February 2013. Two months later saw the debut of another band of vocalist [[Enon Kawatani]]'s, [[Gesu no Kiwami Otome]], when they released the [[extended play]] ''Dress no Nugikata''.<ref name="SSnews">{{cite web | url=http://music.spaceshower.net/artists/gesunokiwamiotome | script-title=ja:ゲスの極み乙女。 | language=Japanese |year=2014 |publisher=Space Shower | accessdate=September 21, 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140201204445/http://music.spaceshower.net/artists/gesunokiwamiotome |archivedate=February 1, 2014 }}</ref> Gesu no Kiwami Otome featured former indigo la End bassist E ni Naranai Kachō. In December, the band was signed to [[Warner Music Group|Warner]] sub-label [[Unborde]] simultaneously with Gesu no Kiwami Otome.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.barks.jp/news/?id=1000096871 | script-title=ja:ゲスの極み乙女。、2014年にワーナーよりメジャーデビュー決定 | language=Japanese |publisher=Barks |date=December 4, 2013 | accessdate=September 21, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/108057 | title=ゲスの極み乙女。とindigo la End、4月に同時デビュー決定 | language=Japanese |publisher=Natalie |date=January 19, 2014 | accessdate=September 21, 2014}}</ref> Both musical units released their major label debut released on April 2, 2014: ''Minna Normal'' for Gesu no Kiwami Otome and ''Ano Machi Record'' for Indigo la End.

In the remainder of 2014, the band released two singles, ''Hitomi ni Utsuranai'' and ''Sayonara Bell'' the end of the band's performance at the Countdown Japan 14/15 new years music festival, band drummer Yūsuke Ōta announced his retirement from the band, citing differences in opinion for the future of the band. His final recordings with the band are featured on their second studio album, ''Shiawase ga Afuretara'' (2015).<ref name="OtaLeaving">{{cite web | url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/135901 | title=indigo la Endドラマー・オオタユウスケが脱退 | language=Japanese |date=January 10, 2015 |publisher=Natalie | accessdate=January 10, 2015}}</ref>

Later in 2015, the band released the singles ''Kanashiku Naru Mae Ni'' and the [[A-side and B-side#Double A-side|double A-Side]] single ''Shizuku ni Koi Shite/Wasurete Hanataba'', which was followed by another single in 2016, ''Kokoro Ame''. In July 2016, the band released their third studio album, and second major label album, ''Aiiro Music'' (2016).<ref>{{cite web|title=藍色ミュージック（通常盤）|url=https://wmg.jp/artist/indigolaend/WPCL000012336.html|website=Warner Music Japan|accessdate=14 April 2018|language=Japanese|date=6 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180415063743/https://wmg.jp/artist/indigolaend/WPCL000012336.html|archive-date=15 April 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2017, the band's song ''Kane Naku Inochi'' was featured as the opening theme song of the Japanese television drama ''[[Inside Mari]]'', and was released as part of the band's third major label album, and fourth studio album, ''Crying End Roll'' (2017).<ref>{{cite web|title=Crying End Roll（通常盤）|url=https://wmg.jp/artist/indigolaend/WPCL000012676.html|website=Warner Music Japan|accessdate=14 April 2018|language=Japanese|date=12 July 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180415063435/https://wmg.jp/artist/indigolaend/WPCL000012676.html|archive-date=15 April 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> That same year, band leader Kawatani Enon's birthday on December 3 served as the release date for the band's first digital single, ''Touya no Magic''.<ref>{{cite web | title=indigo la End 「冬夜のマジック」ワーナーミュージック・ジャパン | url=https://wmg.jp/artist/indigolaend/PKG0000018956.html | work=ワーナーミュージック・ジャパン {{!}} Warner Music Japan | language=Japanese | date=December 3, 2017 | accessdate=March 16, 2018 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180317035947/https://wmg.jp/artist/indigolaend/PKG0000018956.html | archive-date=March 17, 2018 | url-status=dead }}</ref> This was followed in April 2018 by a second digital single, ''Haru no Iutōri'', and the release of their fifth studio and fourth major label album, ''PULSATE'' (2018), in July 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=indigo la End、7/18リリースの4thフル・アルバム『PULSATE』収録内容＆ジャケット写真公開。収録曲「蒼糸」先行配信開始も |url=http://skream.jp/news/2018/06/indigo_la_end_pulsate_detail.php|trans-title=indigo la End's 4th full album "PULSATE" released 18 July. Track-listing and album art revealed. New song "Aoi Ito" receives advance digital streaming |website=skream.jp |publisher=Geki-Rock Entertainment |accessdate=14 July 2018 |language=Japanese |date=15 June 2018}}</ref>

Their fifth full major label album, ''Nureyuku Shishousetsu'' (2019), was released on October 9, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=indigo la Endが10月にアルバム「濡れゆく私小説」発表、ホールツアーも開催|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/337825|trans-title=indigo la End to release album "Nureyuku Shishousetsu" in October, Hall Tour to be held|website=natalie.mu|publisher=音楽ナタリー|accessdate=1 July 2019 |language=Japanese |date=1 July 2019}}</ref>

The band's name was inspired by Japanese band [[Spitz (band)|Spitz]] and their album ''Indigo Chiheisen'' (1996).<ref>{{cite web | url=http://zasshi.news.yahoo.co.jp/article?a=20140401-00010003-realsound-musi | title=indigo／ゲス極のキーマン川谷絵音登場「バンドシーンを通過して、唯一の存在になりたい」 | language=Japanese |date=April 1, 2014 |author=Real Sound |publisher=Yahoo | accessdate=September 22, 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140922094121/http://zasshi.news.yahoo.co.jp/article?a=20140401-00010003-realsound-musi |archivedate=September 22, 2014}}</ref>

== Members ==
*{{nihongo|Enon Kawatani|川谷 絵音|Kawatani Enon}}, real name {{nihongo|Kenta Kawatani|川谷 健太|Kawatani Kenta}},<ref name="OldMySpace">{{cite web | url=http://www.myspace.com/indigolaend | title=indigo la End | language=Japanese |year=2010 |author=indigo la End |publisher=MySpace | accessdate=September 21, 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100626004440/http://www.myspace.com/indigolaend |archivedate=June 26, 2010}}</ref> is the band's vocalist, guitarist and main songwriter, who also fronts the band [[Gesu no Kiwami Otome]].
*{{nihongo|Curtis Osada|長田 カーティス|Osada Kātisu}}, real name {{nihongo|Yoshitaka Osada|長田 佳孝|Osada Yoshitaka}},<ref name="OldMySpace"/> is the band's guitarist.
*{{nihongo|Ryōsuke Gochō|後鳥 亮介|Gochō Ryōsuke}} is the band's bassist. Formerly a support member, he was promoted to being a fully-fledged member on August 10, 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://wmg.jp/artist/indigolaend/news_58507.html | title=メジャー1stシングル「瞳に映らない」9月24日（水）発売決定！新境地へと歩み出す、切なさ溢れる名曲ここに誕生！そして、後鳥亮介（Bass）正式メンバー加入発表！ | language=Japanese | publisher=Warner Music Group | date=August 10, 2014 | accessdate=September 22, 2014 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304025121/http://wmg.jp/artist/indigolaend/news_58507.html | archive-date=March 4, 2016 | url-status=dead }}</ref>
*{{nihongo|Eitarō Satō|佐藤 栄太郎|Satō Eitarō}} is the band's drummer. Formerly a support member for the band's 2015 Shiawase ga Afuretara tour, he was promoted to being a fully-fledged member on March 17, 2015.<ref>{{cite magazine | url=http://ro69.jp/news/detail/120671 | title=indigo la End、ドラム・佐藤栄太郎が正式加入。「これからは4人でindigo la End」 | language=Japanese |magazine=[[Rockin' On Japan]] |date=March 18, 2015 | accessdate=September 22, 2014}}</ref>

=== Former members ===
*{{nihongo|Yūsuke Ōta|オオタ ユウスケ|Ōta Yūsuke}}, real name {{nihongo2|太田 悠介}},<ref name="OldMySpace"/> was the band's drummer. In December 2014 he left the band, after recording the ''Shiawase ga Afuretara'' album and performing at the Countdown Japan 14/15 festival.<ref name="OtaLeaving"/>
*{{nihongo|E ni Naranai Kachō|絵にならない課長||"Unpicturable Manager"}}, real name {{nihongo|Masao Wada|和田 理生|Wada Masao}}, was the band's bassist from 2010 until July 2011.<ref name="Jackman"/><ref name="Daiji">{{cite web | url=http://eninaranai.blog.fc2.com/blog-entry-31.html | script-title=ja:大事な話 |trans-title=Important Message | language=Japanese |date=July 19, 2011 |publisher=Masao Wada| accessdate=September 21, 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140921012923/http://eninaranai.blog.fc2.com/blog-entry-31.html|archivedate=September 21, 2014}}</ref> From 2007 to 2009, Wada was a member of the band Aomune, performing under the name {{nihongo|Waden|ワデン}}.<ref name="OldMySpace"/><ref>{{cite web | url=http://aomune.exblog.jp/7632814/ | script-title=ja:遅れました！ |trans-title=Delayed! | language=Japanese |date=October 28, 2007 |author=Masao Wada |publisher=Excite | accessdate=September 21, 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140921014017/http://aomune.exblog.jp/7632814 |archivedate=September 21, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://bin.onpoo.net/artist_detail.cgi?artist_id=284 | title=aomune | language=Japanese |publisher=Onpoo | accessdate=September 21, 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140921014319/http://bin.onpoo.net/artist_detail.cgi?artist_id=284 |archivedate=September 21, 2014}}</ref> He later collaborated with Kawatani in 2012 as {{nihongo|Kyūjitsu Kachō|休日課長||"Weekend Manager"}}, the bassist for the band Gesu no Kiwami Otome.
*{{nihongo|Mariko Wada|和田 茉莉子|Wada Mariko}} was the band's bassist from January 2012, but announced her retirement in June 2012. During her time with the band, she also worked as a member of the bands Boots on Avalanche and Far France, and was formerly a member of Halt.<ref name="Geocities">{{cite web | url=http://www.geocities.jp/indigolaend/mobile_news.html | script-title=ja:新メンバー加入のお知らせ | language=Japanese |date=January 27, 2012 |publisher=Indigo la End | accessdate=September 22, 2014 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140922075127/http://www.geocities.jp/indigolaend/mobile_news.html |archivedate=September 22, 2014}}</ref>
