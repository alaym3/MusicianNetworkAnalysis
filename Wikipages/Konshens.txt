'''Garfield Spence''' (born 11 January 1985), also known as '''Konshens''', is a [[Jamaicans|Jamaican]] [[dancehall]] [[Musician|recording artist]].

==Biography==
Garfield Delano Spence was born on 11 January 1985.{{citation needed|date=June 2020}} Prior to his solo career, he was a member of the duo [[SoJah]] with his brother [[Delus]].<ref name="marriage">"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/konshens-ties-knot-in-florida_117633?profile=1116 Konshens ties knot in Florida]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 21 November 2017. Retrieved 1 December 2017</ref> His hits have included "Winner", "Rasta Impostor", "This Means Money", "Good Girl Gone Bad", "Gal Dem A Talk", "Realest Song", "Represent", "Do Sumn" and "Forward", "Gal a bubble".<ref name="Star1">"[http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20110423/ent/ent2.html Konshens lives in the heart of the streets] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131111140300/http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20110423/ent/ent2.html |date=11 November 2013 }}", ''Jamaica Star'', 23 April 2011. Retrieved 10 June 2011</ref>

His 2005 single "Pon Di Corner" was a major hit in [[Japan]], and led to a month-long tour of the country and a Japan-only album release.<ref name="Obs1">"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/Konshens-falls-in-love-with-reggae-music_8503463 Konshens falls in love with reggae music] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131111140938/http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/Konshens-falls-in-love-with-reggae-music_8503463 |date=11 November 2013 }}", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 11 March 2011. Retrieved 10 June 2011</ref>

He made a guest appearance on [[Tarrus Riley]]'s ''Contagious'' album, deejaying on "Good Girl Gone Bad", which was also released as a single.<ref name="Kenner">Kenner, Rob (2009) "[https://www.nytimes.com/2009/08/23/arts/music/23kenn.html?pagewanted=1&ref=reggaemusic Reggae Singer With a Legacy, a Following and a Mission]", ''[[The New York Times]]'', 21 August 2009. Retrieved 10 June 2011</ref>

He has a large fanbase in [[Trinidad and Tobago]], [[Guyana]] and Europe, where his "Gal Dem Talk" single was a major hit, and he performed at Guyana's National Stadium in May 2011.<ref name="Obse1">"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Konshens-thrills-Guyana_8749113 Konshens thrills Guyana]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 5 May 2011. Retrieved 10 June 2011</ref>

His album, ''[[Real Talk (Konshens album)|Real Talk]]'', was released by Japanese label Koyashi in 2010.<ref name="Jackson">Jackson, Kevin (2010) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/news/CATCH-DI-RIDDIM_8121309 Catch Di Riddim: Konshens to release two albums] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131111140233/http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/news/CATCH-DI-RIDDIM_8121309 |date=11 November 2013 }}", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 5 November 2010. Retrieved 10 June 2011</ref> A second album was also announced to be released in Jamaica on Tad Dawkins' Tad's International label.<ref name="Jackson" /> Konshens also launched his own [[Subkonshus]] label in 2010, working with new acts including his brother [[Delus]].<ref name="Jackson" />

In May 2011, he was one of several high-profile Jamaican celebrities to take part in a march in [[Torrington Park]] calling for an end to political rivalry in the area, commemorating [[Eventide Home fire|a fire in May 1980 at the Eventide Home]] which killed over 150 elderly women, for which politically motivated arson was suspected.<ref name="Robinson">Robinson, Corey (2011) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/news/Eventide-fire-remembered_8844433 Eventide fire remembered: Torrington Park marks anniversary with demand for an end to political rivalry] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120616153811/http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/news/Eventide-fire-remembered_8844433 |date=2012-06-16 }}", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 23 May 2011. Retrieved 10 June 2011</ref>

In 2012, he released his second album, ''[[Mental Maintenance]]''.

In March 2014, he became an official brand ambassador for [[Pepsi]].<ref>"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Konshens-is-Pepsi-brand-ambassador Konshens is Pepsi brand ambassador]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 19 March 2014. Retrieved 23 March 2014</ref>

In July 2018, he was featured on [[I Don't Dance (Without You)]] with [[Matoma]] and [[Enrique Iglesias]].

==Personal life==
Spence married [[Latoya Wright]] in Miami, Florida, in November 2017.<ref name="marriage" /> Spence has two children, Liam and Sajhi Spence<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thetropixs.com/konshens-spice-sean-paul-share-family-photos-for-christmas/|title=Konshens, Spice, Sean Paul, Share Family Photos For Christmas|last=Tropixs|first=The|date=25 December 2017|website=The Tropixs|access-date=8 March 2019}}</ref>
