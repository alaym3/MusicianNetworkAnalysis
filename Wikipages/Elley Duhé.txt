'''Elley Duhé''' (born February 14, 1992) is an American singer-songwriter. Born in [[Mobile, Alabama]],<ref>Matt Collar, [https://www.allmusic.com/artist/elley-duh%C3%A9-mn0003536718/biography, "Artist Biography by Matt Collar"], Allmusic, retrieved December 1, 2017</ref> she was raised on the [[Gulf Coast of the United States|Gulf Coast]] in the small towns of [[Vancleave, Mississippi]] and [[Dauphin Island, Alabama]].<ref>Maxamillion Polo (July 18, 2017). [http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/new-artist-discovery-elley-duh%C3%A9-is-the-future-of "New Artist Discovery: Elley Duhé Is The Future of Pop"]. Ones to Watch. Retrieved December 1, 2017.</ref>

Duhé felt inspired by her musician father and uncles who are in the New Orleans Music Scene.

==Biography==
After her father got Elley her first guitar when she was 14, she began her career at 15 singing in coffee shops. Graduating to paying gigs in bars, restaurants, and at private parties, she gained enough exposure to be booked to open for national acts and connect with songwriters in [[Nashville]], Los Angeles, and [[Austin]], where, after dropping out of high school and getting her GED, she spent three years honing her craft. She now lives in Los Angeles.<ref>Mark Braboy (December 12, 2016). [https://www.vibe.com/2016/12/elley-duhe-delivers-captivating-melodies-on-immortal/ "Elley Duhe Delivers Captivating Melodies On "Immortal""]. ''Vibe''. Retrieved December 1, 2017.</ref>

The single "Millennium", her collaboration with electronic producer Tarro, released in October 2016, was viewed over two million times on YouTube and streamed 1.4&nbsp;million times on [[Spotify]].

After ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' premiered her single "Immortal" in December 2016,<ref>Alexa Shouneyia (December 7, 2016). [https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7603870/elley-duhe-immortal-exclusive-premiere "Elley Duhe Drops Debut Solo Single 'immortal': Exclusive Premiere"]. ''Billboard''. Retrieved December 1, 2017</ref> it gained 4.5&nbsp;million streams on [[Spotify]], while the video netted nearly a million views on YouTube. A remix by DJ duo [[Snakehips (duo)|Snakehips]] netted an additional 770,000 [[Spotify]] streams.

Her follow-up single "Fly", released in July 2017, featured production from ILLA and [[Cool & Dre]]<ref>[https://www.hillydilly.com/2017/07/elley-duhe-fly/], Hillydilly, retrieved December 1, 2017</ref> ([[Christina Milian]], [[Lil Wayne]]).

In July 2018, she collaborated with Russian-German music producer [[Zedd]] on the single "[[Happy Now (Zedd and Elley Duhé song)|Happy Now]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2018/music/news/zedd-elley-duhe-happy-now-1202877325/|title=Zedd Drops New Single 'Happy Now,' Featuring Elley Duhe|last=Bitran|first=Tara|date=July 19, 2018|work=Variety|access-date=August 15, 2018}}</ref>

On August 3, 2018, her collaborative single with [[Gryffin]], "Tie Me Down", was released.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8468529/gryffin-elley-duhe-tie-you-down|title=Gryffin & Elley Duhe 'Tie Me Down' With Energetic Dance-Pop Melodies: Listen|work=Billboard|access-date=August 15, 2018}}</ref> On August 10, 2018, she released the EP ''Dragon Mentality''.<ref name="EP">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/dragon-mentality-ep/1421950069|title=Dragon Mentality – EP by Elley Duhé on iTunes|work=iTunes Store (AU)|access-date=August 17, 2018}}</ref>
