'''Satinder Pal Singh''', popularly known as '''Satinder Sartaaj''', is an Indian singer, songwriter, actor and poet of [[Punjabi language|Punjabi]] language films and songs.<ref name=Tribune1>{{Cite news| title=Sufiana Spell | url=http://www.tribuneindia.com/2006/20060217/ttlife.htm#8 | work=The Tribune Lifestyle | location=Chandigarh | date=17 February 2006 | accessdate=16 October 2010 | first=S.D. | last=Sharma}}</ref><ref name="Thaindian">{{cite web | url=http://www.thaindian.com/newsportal/feature/dr-satinder-sartaj-takes-sufism-to-music-lovers-across-the-world_100240366.html | date=30 August 2009 | publisher=Thaindian | title=Dr. Satinder Sartaj takes Sufism to music lovers across the world | first=Karan | last=Kapoor | accessdate=11 April 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180619113034/http://www.thaindian.com/newsportal/feature/dr-satinder-sartaj-takes-sufism-to-music-lovers-across-the-world_100240366.html |archive-date=19 June 2018 }}</ref> He is a famous punjabi singer since 1999. Since then he has performed across the world.<ref name= Tribune2>{{Cite news| title=Stirring the soul with Sufi music |  url = http://www.tribuneindia.com/2010/20100814/ldh1.htm#6 | work=The Tribune | location=Ludhiana | date=14 August 2010 | accessdate=16 October 2010 | first=Charu | last=Chhibber}}</ref> He made his film debut as [[Duleep Singh|Maharaja Duleep Singh]] in [[The Black Prince (movie)|''The Black Prince'']] in 2017.

== Early life ==
Satinder Pal Singh Saini was born in the village of Bajrawar in [[Hoshiarpur]], [[Punjab, India|Punjab]]. He attended school at the government elementary school in his village. While in the third standard, he began performing in local Bal Sabhas.<ref>{{cite web|title=Satinder Sartaaj|url=http://www.desiblitz.com/content/satinder-sartaaj|publisher=desiblitz.com|date=9 September 2012}}</ref>{{better source needed|reason=Not considered reliable as per WP:ICTF, WP:RS|date=September 2020}}

==Education==
Sartaaj obtained an honours degree in music from Government College, [[Hoshiarpur]]. He concentrated on his musical career while completing his [[Master of Philosophy|MPhil]] in Sufi music singing and later a [[Doctorate of Philosophy|PhD]] in Sufi singing (gayan) from [[Panjab University, Chandigarh]]. He also taught music at Panjab University for six years.  Sartaaj also completed a certificate course and Diploma in [[Persian language|Persian]] language.<ref name=Tribune1 />  He started writing poetry and adopted his [[Takhallus]](Pen name), Sartaaj, while at college.

== Career==
Sartaaj pursued a professional music career in his 20s. Prior to this, he has stated he was a farmer and had no interest in pursuing a career as a performer. 

His big performance break was in 2008 when he got booked for a gig in [[Toronto, Ontario]]. The show's organizers had heard him singing on YouTube and wanted him to perform for the Punjabi-Canadian audience.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Exclusive SATINDER SARTAAJ Interview {{!}} In Conversation with Amin Dhillon (Ep. 10)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zSIqufb7pto|work=YouTube|access-date=9 April 2021}}</ref>

In 2011, Sartaaj won "Best International Act" at the [[Brit Asia TV Music Awards]] (BAMA).<ref>{{cite news |title=Brit Asia Music Awards 2011 |url=https://www.asianimage.co.uk/news/9256387.brit-asia-music-awards-2011/ |accessdate=15 September 2020 |work=Asian Image |date=16 September 2011 |language=en}}</ref>

On 2 May 2014, Sartaaj performed in the [[Royal Albert Hall]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Satinder Sartaaj to visit UK for premiere of his film 'Ikko Mikke' |url=https://www.bizasialive.com/satinder-sartaaj-to-visit-uk-for-premiere-of-his-film-ikko-mikke/ |accessdate=15 September 2020 |work=BizAsia {{!}} Media, Entertainment, Showbiz, Brit, Events and Music |date=3 March 2020}}</ref> He won  "Best Songwriter" again at BAMA 2017 and "Music Video of the Year" at BAMA 2018 for "Udaarian".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Baddhan |first1=Raj |title=Brit Asia World Music Awards 2017: Winners |url=https://www.bizasialive.com/brit-asia-world-music-awards-2017-winners/ |accessdate=15 September 2020 |work=BizAsia {{!}} Media, Entertainment, Showbiz, Brit, Events and Music |date=5 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=BritAsia TV Music Awards 2018: Winners List |url=https://www.bizasialive.com/britasia-tv-music-awards-2018-winners-list/ |accessdate=15 September 2020 |work=BizAsia {{!}} Media, Entertainment, Showbiz, Brit, Events and Music |date=8 October 2018}}</ref>

He debuted in the American film industry with the lead role in movie ''[[The Black Prince (film)|The Black Prince]],'' a historic biopic of Maharaja [[Duleep Singh]] which was released on 21 July 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/movie-reviews/the-black-prince-movie-review-strong-subject-poorly-executed/story-0zGk3ioz8TDbblaS2jnWNM.html|title=The Black Prince movie review: Strong subject, poorly executed|date=22 July 2017|work=Hindustani Times|access-date=11 March 2018}}</ref>

== Philosophy ==
Although he has taught at the university and is an accomplished [[Bhangra (dance)|bhangra]] performer, composer and singer, Sartaaj has said that he considers ''[[shayari]]'' (poetry) as his first love.<ref>{{cite web|title=PTC Punjabi - Showcase Black Prince|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ukzEBtMalv0&t=10s|website=YouTube|publisher=PTC Punjabi|accessdate=18 February 2018}}</ref>
