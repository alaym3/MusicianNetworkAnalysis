'''Donell  Jones''' (born May 22, 1973) is an American singer, songwriter and record producer. He is most notable for the [[hit record|hits]] "[[U Know What's Up]]," "[[Where I Wanna Be (Donell Jones song)|Where I Wanna Be]]," and his [[cover version|cover]] of [[Stevie Wonder]]'s "[[Knocks Me Off My Feet]]." Jones' first studio album ''[[My Heart (Donell Jones album)|My Heart]]'', was released in 1996, but he is best known for his late 1990s release of his second album ''[[Where I Wanna Be (Donell Jones album)|Where I Wanna Be]]''.

==Music career==
Jones is a son of a gospel singer and was raised in Chicago, IL.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/donell-jones-mn0000987777/biography|title=Donell Jones &#124; Biography & History|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=2020-03-26}}</ref> He later met DJ [[Eddie F]] of [[Heavy D]] and The Boyz and signed with Untouchables/[[LaFace Records]], writing hits for [[Usher (musician)|Usher]] and [[702 (band)|702]].

===1996–2007: Breakthrough with LaFace===
In June 1996, Jones released his debut album, ''[[My Heart (Donell Jones album)|My Heart]]'', on LaFace Records. While most of the album was produced by Jones himself, it also featured help from Eddie Ferrell's Untouchables Entertainment production team, involving Mookie, Marks Sparks, Kenny Tonge, and Darin Whittington. A moderate commercial success, it peaked at number 30 on ''Billboard''{{'}}s [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]], but failed to reach the upper half of the US ''Billboard'' 200.<ref name="Allmusic albums" /> Its first two singles, "In the Hood" and a cover of [[Stevie Wonder]]'s 1976 ballad "[[Knocks Me Off My Feet]]," became top thirty hits on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs as well as the [[New Zealand Singles Chart]].<ref name="Allmusic albums">{{cite web | title=Joe > Charts & Awards > ''Billboard'' Albums | url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/joe-p39682/charts-awards | website=[[AllMusic]] | access-date=January 10, 2012}}</ref>

In 1999, Jones enjoyed major success with the release of his second album, ''[[Where I Wanna Be (Donell Jones album)|Where I Wanna Be]]''. Boosted by its hit single "[[U Know What's Up]]," which peaked at number seven on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. "U Know What's Up" was a global hit, peaking within the top ten of the charts in Belgium (Flanders), the Republic of Ireland, and the United Kingdom. In the latter country, "U Know What's Up" entered and peaked at number two on the [[UK Singles Chart]], where it remained for two consecutive weeks behind "[[The Masses Against the Classes]]" by [[Manic Street Preachers]] and "[[Born to Make You Happy]]" by [[Britney Spears]]. 
"Where I Wanna Be" peaked at number six on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums in the United States.<ref name="Allmusic albums" /> The album produced three further singles, including "Shorty (Got Her Eyes On Me)" and "[[Where I Wanna Be (Donell Jones song)|Where I Wanna Be]]," the latter of which reached number two on ''Billboard''{{'}}s [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart, and was eventually certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA) for sales in excess of 1.0 million copies.<ref name="Allmusic albums" /> In 2000, Jones recorded the song "[[I'll Go]]" for the [[Love & Basketball (soundtrack)|soundtrack]] of the romantic drama film ''[[Love and Basketball]]'', which earned him a [[Black Reel Award]] nomination for [[Black Reel Award for Best Original or Adapted Song|Best Original or Adapted Song]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Awards for Donell Jones|website=IMDb.com|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1244934/awards?ref_=nm_awd|access-date=July 18, 2018}}</ref> The same year, he was also awarded an [[American Music Award]] in the [[American Music Awards of 2001|Favorite Soul/R&B New Artist]] category.<ref>{{cite web|title=28th American Music Awards|website=Rockonthenet.com|url=http://www.rockonthenet.com/archive/2001/amas.htm|access-date=July 18, 2018}}</ref>

Jones reteamed with his regular team of contributors, including Eddie F., Sheldon Goode, Jamie Hawkins, G-Wise, Kyle West, and Darren Lighty, to work on his third album ''[[Life Goes On (Donell Jones album)|Life Goes On]]''. Released in June 2002, it debuted number 3 on the ''Billboard'' 200 and at number three on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart, becoming his highest-charting effort yet.<ref name="Allmusic albums" /> A steady seller it earned gold status from the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).<ref name="RIAA">{{cite web | url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=Donell+Jones| title=RIAA – Searchable Database: Donell Jones| publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]] | access-date=July 18, 2018 }}</ref> Its three singles were less successful however, though lead single "[[You Know That I Love You (Donell Jones song)|You Know That I Love You]]" became a top twenty hit on the R&B charts.<ref name="Allmusic albums" /> Jones' fourth album, ''[[Journey of a Gemini]]'' was released in 2006 and marked his first album to include a diverse roster of collaborators including production credits from [[Tim & Bob]], [[Sean Garrett]], [[Ryan Leslie]], [[Tank (American singer)|Tank]], [[Mike City]], and [[The Underdogs (duo)|The Underdogs]]. It became Jones' highest-charting album on the ''Billboard'' Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart where it topped at number one, while entering the top 20 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref name="Allmusic albums" />

===2009–present: Independent releases===
In 2009, Jones released ''[[The Lost Files (Donell Jones album)|The Lost Files]]'', a collection of unreleased songs that were recorded between 1996 and 2004, through his own label Candyman Music Inc and TuneCore.<ref name="yknig">{{cite web|title=YouKnowIGotSoul Interview With Donell Jones|website=YouKnowIGotSoul.com|url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/youknowigotsoul-interview-with-donell-jones|access-date=July 18, 2018}}</ref> Serving as prelude to his next studio album, ''The Lost Files'' failed to chart.<ref name="yknig"/> ''[[Lyrics (album)|Lyrics]]'', Jones' fifth regular album, was released in September 2010 via Candyman and [[E1 Music|eOne Music]].<ref>[http://blog.thenext2shine.com/2009/interview-with-rnb-veteran-donell-jones EXCLUSIVE Interview: with R&B Veteran, Donell Jones - TheNext2Shine.com] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100301043410/http://blog.thenext2shine.com/2009/interview-with-rnb-veteran-donell-jones |date=March 1, 2010 }}. Blog.thenext2shine.com. Retrieved on 2012-04-09.</ref> Chiefly produced by Jones' himself, it debuted and peaked at number 28 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and at number nine on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]], becoming his fourth entry on the latter chart.<ref name="Allmusic albums" /> ''Lyrics'' produced two singles, including "[[Love Like This (Donell Jones song)|Love Like This]]" which reached the top five of ''Billboard''{{'}}s [[Adult R&B Songs]].<ref name="Allmusic albums" />

Jones' sixth album ''Forever'' was released in July 2013.<ref name="yknig2">{{cite web|title=Interview: Donell Jones Talks New Album "Forever", Living His Music, Finding Inspiration|website=YouKnowIGotSoul.com|url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/interview-donell-jones-talks-new-album-forever-living-his-music-finding-inspiration|access-date=July 18, 2018}}</ref> Another project conceived under Candyman and eOne Music, it debuted and peaked at number 20 on the ''Billboard'' 200 and reached number eight on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums.<ref name="Allmusic albums" /> The album spawned two singles. Its title track which peaked at number 14 on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Adult R&B Songs.<ref name="Allmusic albums" /> In 2016, Jones premiered the single "Never Let Her Go," a collaboration with rapper [[David Banner]]. In support of the single, he went on a promo tour during spring 2017.<ref name="yknig3">{{cite web|title=New Music: Donell Jones – Never Let Her Go (Featuring David Banner)|website=YouKnowIGotSoul.com|url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/new-music-donell-jones-never-let-her-go-featuring-david-banner|access-date=July 18, 2018}}</ref> In 2018, Jones provided vocals, produced and co-wrote on "23" by [[Jacquees]] off the album ''[[4275]]'' (2018).

In 2019, Donell Jones collaborated with [[Jon B.]] on the duet "Understand".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://youknowigotsoul.com/jon-b-and-donell-jones-shoot-video-for-new-single-understand|title=Jon B. and Donell Jones Shoot Video for New Single "Understand" |website=YouKnowIGotSoul.com|date=March 7, 2019}}</ref> He also announced plans for an upcoming album to release in 2019 during an appearance on the SoulBack R&B Podcast.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://youknowigotsoul.com/donell-jones-talks-new-single-with-jon-b-upcoming-album-working-with-jacquees-exclusive|title=Donell Jones Talks New Single with Jon B., Upcoming Album, Working with Jacquees (Exclusive)|website=YouKnowIGotSoul.com|date=March 4, 2019}}</ref> The project, called ''100% Free'', was expected to be released in December 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://youknowigotsoul.com/donell-jones-returns-with-new-single-karma-payback|title=Donell Jones Returns With New Single "Karma (Payback)"|website=YouKnowIGotSoul.com|access-date=November 14, 2020}}</ref> but eventually came out on digital services (and for free on his official website) on February 14, 2021.<ref>https://ratedrnb.com/2021/02/donell-jones-releases-new-album-100-free/</ref>
