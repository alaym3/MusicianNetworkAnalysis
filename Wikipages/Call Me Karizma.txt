'''Morgan Parriott''' (born 1995), known professionally as '''Call Me Karizma''', is an American singer-songwriter and rapper from [[New Prague, Minnesota]]. His music tends to be within the [[hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[Punk music|punk]] genres.

His music career started in 2015, when Parriott toured with American rappers [[Mod Sun]] and [[Blackbear (musician)|Blackbear]] and released his debut album, ''Uninvited'', a year later. In 2018, he signed with [[Arista Records]] and released his first [[extended play]] (EP) ''The Gloomy Tapes, Vol. 1'' with ''Vol. 2'' following a year later. In 2020, he left Arista and released his second album ''To Hell With Hollywood''.

== Early life ==
Morgan Parriott was born on the 28th of April 1995 and is originally from [[New Prague, Minnesota]].<ref name="Engelman 2018" /><ref name="Bailey 2019" /> He started writing songs at age 12.<ref name="Engelman 2018" /><ref name="Radio.com 2019" />

== Career ==
In February 2015, whilst studying as [[sophomore]] (second year of study) in college he assisted American rappers [[Mod Sun]] and [[Blackbear (musician)|Blackbear]] in a 35-date tour as a result of fans [[tweeting]] at them.<ref name="Engelman 2018" /><ref name="Armstrong 2018" /> Parriott released his debut album ''Uninvited'' in 2016.<ref name="Engelman 2018" />

In summer 2018, he released his first [[extended play]] (EP) called ''The Gloomy Tapes, Vol. 1'', as part of his three part ''The Gloomy Tapes'' EP series.<ref name="Morin 2018" />

In 2018, Parriott signed to [[Arista Records]].<ref name="Engelman 2018" /> He released the single "Serotonin" on 30 November 2018 which explores themes of mental health, depression and anxiety, the first song he released with Arista.<ref name="Engelman 2018" /><ref name="Armstrong 2018" /> Also in November 2018, Parriott released a collaboration called "God Damnit" with American DJ, [[Illenium]].<ref name="Crestejo 2018" /> Under Arista, ''The Gloomy Tapes, Vol 2'' was released in February 2019.<ref name="Engelman 2018" /> His lead single "Monster (under my bed)" of ''Vol. 2'' was featured on the television show ''[[The Purge (TV series)|The Purge]]'' on the seventh episode of the second season.<ref name="The Purge" /> Additionally, the song according to ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', peaked at number 33 on the "Mainstream Rock Airplay" on 30 March 2019.<ref name="Billboard Chart History" />

Parriott left Arista Records in March 2020.<ref name="Edtimes press release" /> On 10 December 2020, his second album ''To Hell With Hollywood'' was released.<ref name="Edtimes press release" /> Additionally, he has opened for [[Wiz Khalifa]] and has worked with producer [[David Pramik]].<ref name="Armstrong 2018" /> As of 2018, he has completed four tours of the United States.<ref name="Armstrong 2018" />

In July 2021, Parriott signed to [[Thriller Records]].<ref name="McMahon 2021" /> Thriller is a new label founded by Bob Becker who also founded [[Fearless Records]].<ref name="McMahon 2021" /> Later in the year, Parriott is toured in the United States to promote a new EP entitled ''Bleached Serpent''. Several of the tour dates were sold out.<ref name="McMahon 2021" />

His music has been described as [[Pop music|pop and post-pop]],<ref name="Bailey 2019" /><ref name="McMahon 2021" /> [[hip hop music|hip hop]]<ref name="Engelman 2018" /><ref name="Bailey 2019" /><ref name="Crestejo 2018" /><ref name="McMahon 2021" /> and [[Punk music|punk]].<ref name="Engelman 2018" /><ref name="Bailey 2019" /><ref name="McMahon 2021" /> He is regarded as a singer,<ref name="Bailey 2019" /><ref name="Morin 2018" /> songwriter,<ref name="Engelman 2018" /><ref name="Bailey 2019" /><ref name="Morin 2018" /> and rapper.<ref name="Engelman 2018" /><ref name="Bailey 2019" /><ref name="Morin 2018" /> According to ''Substream Magazine'', he has a strong fanbase in Germany, Russia, and Ukraine.<ref name="McMahon 2021" />
