'''Iñigo Dominic Lazaro Pascual''' (born September 14, 1997) is a Filipino actor, singer and songwriter. He is popularly known for his 2016 hit single, "[[Dahil Sa'yo]]", the first number one song on ''[[Billboard Philippines]]''{{'}} then-new [[Philippine Top 20]] chart which also won "Song of the Year" at the 30th Awit Awards, the Philippine version of the Grammys. He is the son of actor [[Piolo Pascual]].

== Early life ==
Iñigo Pascual was born on September 14, 1997, in the Philippines.<ref>{{cite magazine |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Inigo Pascual Cast in Hollywood Series Opposite Susan Sarandon |url=https://www.esquiremag.ph/culture/movies-and-tv/inigo-pascual-hollywood-monarch-cast-a00203-20210916 |magazine=Esquire Philippines |location= |publisher= |date=September 16, 2021 |access-date=September 28, 2021}}</ref> Pascual moved to the United States when he was 8 years old.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://younghollywood.com/scene/artist-to-watch-inigo.html |title=Artist to Watch: Inigo |last=Minnig |first=Jenna |date=June 28, 2019 |website=[[Young Hollywood]] |publisher= |access-date=September 28, 2021 |quote=}}</ref> At the age of 17, he moved back to the Philippines to pursue his career in entertainment.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2019/08/02/1939735/becoming-his-own-pascual-iigo-carves-career-music|title=Becoming his own Pascual: Iñigo carves a career in music|date=August 2, 2019|publisher=[[The Philippine Star]]|accessdate=March 5, 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2014–2015: Career beginnings ===
Pascual's first lead role in film came in 2014 when he starred in the teen romance film ''[[Relaks, It's Just Pag-ibig]],'' which was directed by Antoinette Jadaone and Irene Villamor.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Relaks, It's Just Pag-Ibig|url=https://www.clickthecity.com/movies/detail/SxD0b9/relaks-it-s-just-pag-ibig|website=Click The City}}</ref> He played the role of Josh Brillantes, a teenage boy who joined in the journey of a girl who wanted to meet a pair of lovers during a blue moon after she a picked up a love letter written by a man named Elias. Piolo Pacual, Inigo's father, has a special participation in the film as the mysterious letter writer, Elias. The film was produced by Spring Films and distributed by Star Cinema.

In 2015, Pascual starred in another teen romance film, ''[[Para sa Hopeless Romantic]]'' alongside [[James Reid (actor)|James Reid]], [[Nadine Lustre]], and [[Julia Barretto]]. The film was directed by Andoy Ranay and was based on the Marcelo Santos III's bestselling novel of the same name. Pascual played the role of Ryan Sebastian, a fictional character from a story written by Becca, Lustre's character. Pascual then starred in Mae Cruz-Alviar's ''[[Crazy Beautiful You]]'' with another pair of popular loveteam, Kathryn Bernardo and Daniel Padilla. He played the role of Marcus Alcantara, the stepbrother of Padilla's character who had feelings for Bernardo's character.

After appearing in several episodes of anthology shows such as ''[[Maalaala Mo Kaya]]'' and ''[[Wansapanataym (1997 TV series)|Wansapanataym]]'', Pascual landed a role in the afternoon drama series, ''[[And I Love You So (TV series)|And I Love You So]].'' He starred in the series alongside Miles Ocampo, [[Julia Barretto]], Dimples Romana, and Angel Aquino. He played the role of Justin Santiago, who was a love interest for both Ocampo's and Barretto's characters.

=== 2016–2018 : Breakthrough in music ===
In 2016, Pascual released his self-titled debut album under [[Star Music]], which included the hit single ''[[Dahil Sa'yo|Dahil Sa'Yo]].'' The song became the first number 1 track of the short-lived Billboard Philippines Top 20 chart.<ref>{{Cite news|date=June 19, 2017|title=Iñigo Pascual tops first Billboard Philippine Charts|work=Manila Standard|url=https://manilastandard.net/mobile/article/239752}}</ref> In 2017, Pascual was tapped by Disney Philippines to record the Philippine version of "Remember Me," the theme song from Disney-Pixar's animated movie, [[Coco (2017 film)|''Coco'']].<ref>{{Cite news|date=November 14, 2017|title=Iñigo Pascual sings official PH version of 'Coco' theme|work=ABS-CBN News|url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/11/14/17/iigo-pascual-sings-official-ph-version-of-coco-theme}}</ref> In 2019, Pascual released his first international solo single, Options. The track was produced by LA-based instrumentalist Bernard "HARV" Harvey.<ref>{{Cite news|date=June 28, 2019|title=Inigo Pascual launches first internationally-produced single "Options"|work=ABS-CBN News|url=https://www.abs-cbn.com/newsroom/news-releases/2019/6/28/inigo-pascual-launches-first-internationally-produ?lang=en}}</ref> On May 6, 2020, amidst the COVID-19 pandemic, Pascual was featured in [[88rising]]'s online music festival called ''Asia Rising Forever.''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Santos|first=Camille|date=April 30, 2020|title=INIGO PASCUAL TO JOIN 88RISING ARTISTS IN VIRTUAL FESTIVAL 'ASIA RISING FOREVER'|url=https://myx.abs-cbn.com/features/23695/inigo-pascual-asia-rising-festival|website=Myx}}</ref> During the livestream event, Pascual performed a cover of [[Post Malone]]'s Better Now.  In June 2020'','' Pascual appeared as a musical act in Filipino-American stand-up comedian [[Jo Koy|Jo Koy's]] Netflix special entitled ''Jo Koy: In HIs Elements''.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Asilo|first=Rito|date=June 14, 2020|title=Iñigo Pascual on how he ended up performing in Jo Koy's Netflix special|url=https://entertainment.inquirer.net/379127/inigo-pascual-on-how-he-ended-up-performing-in-jo-koys-netflix-special|work=Philippine Daily Inquirer}}</ref>

===American television debut===
On September 15, 2021, ''[[Deadline Hollywood]]'' announced that Pascual had joined the cast of [[Fox (network)|Fox]] drama series ''Monarch'' in his American television debut.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Del Rosario |first1=Alexandra |title=‘Monarch’: Filipino Singer-Actor Inigo Pascual Joins Lead Cast Of Fox Musical Drama |url=https://deadline.com/2021/09/filipino-inigo-pascual-monarch-fox-musical-drama-1234834218/ |website=Deadline |access-date=25 September 2021 |date=15 September 2021}}</ref>
