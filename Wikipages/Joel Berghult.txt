'''Joel Gustaf Berghult''' (born 8 April 1988),<ref>{{cite tweet|user=roomieofficial|author=Joel Berghult|number=1248093524085497857|title=Celebrating my birthday by singing a little bit pitchy|date=8 April 2020|access-date=3 July 2020}}</ref> better known as '''Roomie''' or '''Lil Pitchy''', is a Swedish singer, songwriter, producer, and YouTuber. He is best known for his YouTube channel '''''RoomieOfficial''''', which consists of music commentary, original songs, covers, comedy, and more music-related content. He has posted several [[viral videos]] and collaborations with PewDiePie ([[PewDiePie|Felix Kjellberg]]), TheOdd1sOut ([[TheOdd1sOut|James Rallison]]) and Boyinaband ([[David Brown (British musician)|David Brown]]). His most popular video is "One Guy, 43 Voices", featuring vocal imitations of singers such as [[Justin Bieber]], [[Charlie Puth]], and [[Brendon Urie]]. His YouTube channel has 7.43 million subscribers as of December 2021.

== Personal life ==
Berghult was born and raised in Lerum, [[Gothenburg]], Sweden.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Joel Berghult|url=http://curatorsofsweden.com/curator/joel-berghult/|website=Curators of Sweden|access-date=8 May 2020}}</ref> His father, Bosse Berghult, is also a musician and had encouraged his musical endeavors growing up by providing him with instruments and high tech music production software, including drums. He used to play in a small band with his college friends.<ref name=tumblr/>

Berghult graduated in 2014 from {{ill|Musikmakarna|sv}}, where he studied songwriting and music production.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Joel "Roomie" Berghult {{!}} Musikmakarna|url=https://www.musikmakarna.se/sv/framgangar/joel-roomie-berghult|access-date=25 September 2019|website=Musikmakarna}}</ref> Berghult moved to the United Kingdom in 2015.<ref name=":1" /> He moved to Los Angeles in 2021.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=roomieofficial|author=Joel Berghult|number=1367469554918699024|title=Sorry if I miss some daily uploads soon, I got my US visa and am moving to Los Angeles for some exciting opportunities! |date=4 March 2021|access-date=26 April 2021}}</ref>

In 2014, Berghult began to release music with fellow Swedish YouTuber [[PewDiePie]] (who is also from [[Gothenburg]]). The single "His Name is Pewdiepie" was released in 2014 and was made using samples of PewDiePie's voice;<ref name=":0" /> later that year, "Fabulous" was released, followed by "Brofist" in 2016. In 2019, Berghult again teamed up with PewDiePie and longtime collaborator [[Boyinaband]] on the song "[[Congratulations (Roomie, PewDiePie and Boyinaband song)|Congratulations]]".<ref>{{cite news|date=2 April 2019|title=PewDiePie vs T-Series: The battle for the biggest YouTube channel – ABC News|language=en-AU|work=ABC News|url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2019-04-02/pewdiepie-tseries-battle-for-biggest-youtube-channel/10962870|access-date=8 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Perang Berakhir! PewDiePie Mengakui Kekalahannya Terhadap T-Series|url=https://mediaformasi.com/2019/04/perang-berakhir-pewdiepie-mengakui-kekalahannya-terhadap-t-series/|last=Ramadhan|first=Rafli|date=1 April 2019|website=Media Formasi|language=id-ID|access-date=11 May 2020}}</ref> The song peaked at number one on the US Comedy Digital Track Sales published by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''<ref>{{Cite web|date=27 April 2019|title=Comedy Digital Track Sales|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/comedy-digital-track-sales/2019-04-13|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191013180319/https://www.billboard.com/charts/comedy-digital-track-sales/2019-04-27|archive-date=13 November 2019|access-date=17 November 2019|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> and has over 200 million views on YouTube as of 2021.<ref>{{Citation|title=Congratulations|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PHgc8Q6qTjc|language=en|access-date=2021-11-29}}</ref>

In December 2019, Berghult uploaded daily videos as part of a series called "Daily December". In response to positive feedback, he continued releasing daily videos. He grew discontent with the daily upload schedule, and announced a hiatus from YouTube on 11 June 2020, which lasted until 22 July.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Allen|first=Siân|date=15 June 2020|title=Roomie Announces Break From Making Videos|url=https://teneightymagazine.com/2020/06/15/roomie-announces-break-from-making-videos/|access-date=15 June 2020|website=TenEighty|language=en-GB}}</ref> On 20 July 2020, while still on break, Berghult released an album single "Livin' For That" on Spotify under the name "Lil Pitchy". The album consists of the song "Livin' For That", plus its instrumental and [[a cappella]] versions.<ref>{{Cite web|date=20 July 2020|title=Livin' for That [Explicit]|url=https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B08D7YHW8B/|access-date=23 December 2020|website=amazon.com}}</ref> On 31 May 2021, Berghult announced another hiatus from YouTube. He cited fatigue from uploading daily videos, and wanting to shift focus to larger-scale projects.<ref>{{cite web |title=RoomieOfficial reveals reason behind sudden YouTube break |url=https://www.dexerto.com/entertainment/roomieofficial-reveals-reason-behind-sudden-youtube-break-1585899/ |website=Dexerto|language=en |date=31 May 2021}}</ref> He ended that hiatus on 28 September, with the video "We Bought 100 Microphones To Do This...".<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 September 2021|website=YouTube|language=en|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GJYSk_SxqV0|title=We Bought 100 Microphones To Do This...|last=Berghult|first=Joel}}</ref>
