'''Vallis Alps''' are an electronic music duo based in Sydney, Australia, composed of producer David Ansari and vocalist Parissa Tosif, who are originally from [[Seattle]] and [[Canberra]] respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idolator.com/7581825/new-find-vallis-alps-young-who-are|title=New Find: Vallis Alps Make Their Mark With Drowsy Electro-Anthem "Young"|publisher=Idolator|date=18 February 2015|first=Mike|last=Wass|access-date=26 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{citation|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4254995.htm|website=Triple J|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|date=15 June 2015|title=Vallis Alps announce debut Australian tour}}</ref>

==Career==
===2015-2016: ''Vallis Alps''===
In January 2015, They released the tracks "Young" and "Thru" on [[Triple J]] and their first [[Extended play]], ''Vallis Alps''. Following the success of their debut EP, Vallis Alps announced their first Australian tour dates in August 2015, including a performance at [[Splendour in the Grass]] in July.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4254995.htm|title=Vallis Alps announce debut Australian tour {{!}} Music News {{!}} triple j|website=www.abc.net.au|access-date=2017-04-05}}</ref> The pair moved to Sydney in late 2015.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/article/meet-vallis-alps-start-splendour|title=Meet Vallis Alps: from start to Splendour|date=2015|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|website=Triple J Unearthed}}</ref> Their 2015 was capped off with a nomination for the [[J Award]] for Unearthed Artist of the Year in 2015. "Young" went on to place number 27 on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2015]]. In November 2016, the duo released a new track titled "Fading". The track placed at number 94 on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2016]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/16/countdown/|title=1-100 {{!}} Hottest 100 2016 {{!}} triple j|date=2016-11-11|website=www.abc.net.au|language=en-AU|access-date=2017-04-05}}</ref>

===2017-present: ''Fable''===
In March 2017, the duo released "East", the lead single from the duo's second EP, ''Fable''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://stoneyroads.com/2017/03/vallis-alps-share-dreamy-sophomore-ep-fable|title=Vallis Alps Share Dreamy Sophomore EP 'Fable'|work=Stoney Roads|access-date=2017-04-05|language=en}}</ref>

On 31 August 2017, the duo released a single, "Oceans", which tells the sacrifice women take and is also inspired upon the 19th century Iranian woman [[Tahirih]] who fought for women's rights in Iran.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://eatthismusic.com/2017/09/01/vallis-alps-2/|title=Sacrifice women make in fighting equality in their new single 'Oceans'|work=Steve Likoski|access-date=2017-09-01|language=en}}</ref>
