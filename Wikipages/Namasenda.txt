'''Naomi Namasenda''' (born 14 November 1993), known mononymously as '''Namasenda''', is a Swedish singer and songwriter from [[Stockholm]]. She is most well known for releases on British [[Pop music|pop]] and [[Electronic music|electronic]] label, [[PC Music]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Namasenda raises the stakes on her new A. G. Cook-produced single 'Dare'|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/04/29/namasenda-dare-pm-premiere-ag-cook-pc-music|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref>

In 2012, she was awarded a vocal scholarship by the [[Hollywood Chamber of Commerce]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hollywood Chamber of Commerce Community Foundation Vocal Scholarship Winner: Naomi Namasenda|url=https://musiciansfoundation.wordpress.com/2012/10/23/hollywood-chamber-of-commerce-community-foundation-vocal-scholarship-winner-naomi-namasenda/|last=Foundation|first=THE Musicians|date=2012-10-23|website=The Musicians Foundation Blog|language=en|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref> She graduated from the [[Musicians Institute]] and moved back to Sweden to work on new music and released her debut single "Here" in 2016, featuring BFOTY.<ref>{{Cite web|title="Sverige är för litet för mig"|url=https://www.sydsvenskan.se/2016-08-24/sverige-ar-for-litet-for-mig|website=Sydsvenskan|language=sv|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref> There, she was hand picked as a breakthrough act by Red Bull Select.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Malmöhus|first=P4|date=2016-10-14|title=Veberöd var för litet för Namasenda|language=sv|work=Sveriges Radio|url=https://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=96&artikel=6540449|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Spångatalangen Anna coachas av Cherrie|url=https://mitti.se/nyheter/spangatalangen-coachas-cherrie/|website=mitti.se|language=sv-SE|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Namasenda Brings Her Dance Party Vibes To The Regent Theater [REVIEW+PHOTOS]|url=http://blurredculture.com/namasenda-brings-her-dance-party-vibes-to-the-regent-theater-reviewphotos/|website=Blurred Culture|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref> In 2017, she released her debut EP, ''hot_babe_93'', and presented awards at the Swedish Grammys.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Årets Grammis – egendomligt fantasifattig|url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/a/vok0L|website=Aftonbladet|language=sv|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref>

In 2019, with the release of her single "24/7", she became the first black artist to sign to PC Music.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The 10 best moments, dramas and trends in 2019’s weird world of pop|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/47308/1/the-10-best-moments-dramas-and-trends-in-2019-s-weird-world-of-pop|last=Dazed|date=2019-12-31|website=Dazed|language=en|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Review: Namasenda - 24/7|url=https://www.residentadvisor.net/reviews/24521|website=Resident Advisor|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref> In the same year she featured on American singer [[Liz (musician)|LIZ]]'s ''[[Planet Y2K]]'' album.<ref>{{Cite web|title=We Listened To Namasenda’s "24/7" For 24 Hours And This is What We Think|url=https://celebmix.com/namasenda-247/|date=2019-12-04|website=CelebMix|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref> She released "Dare (AM)" and "Dare (PM)" in April 2020, both singles produced by A. G. Cook,<ref name=":0" /> followed by "Wanted" in November 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-12|title=Namasenda's New Song "Wanted" Is The Wildest Ride of the Day|url=https://celebmix.com/namsenda-wanted/|access-date=2021-07-16|website=CelebMix|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Namasenda released "Demonic", "Banana Clip", "Finish Him", and "No Regrets" as singles off of her debut ''[[Unlimited Ammo]]'' mixtape, which was released on 28 October 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-22|title=Namasenda and La Zowi on Their 'Demonic' Single|url=https://www.papermag.com/namasenda-la-zowi-demonic-2653896261.html?rebelltitem=8#rebelltitem8?rebelltitem=8|access-date=2021-07-23|website=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Dazed|date=2021-08-20|title=Mowalola and PC Music’s Namasenda on fake hoes and funeral songs|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/fashion/article/53903/1/mowalola-namasenda-collaboration-pc-music-hannah-diamond-banana-clip-ag-cook|access-date=2021-09-16|website=Dazed|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Subscribe|title=Namasenda unveils new bop 'Finish Him'|url=http://diymag.com/2021/09/16/namasenda-finish-him-pc-music|access-date=2021-09-17|website=DIY|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=10 songs you need in your life this week|url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/10/07/best-new-songs-playlist-namasenda-arca-porches-wiki|access-date=2021-10-07|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref>
