'''Adrian Galvin,''' better known by his moniker '''Yoke Lore''', is a multi-instrumentalist, dancer, and visual artist from New York, United States.<ref>{{Cite web|date=January 3, 2018|title=Yoke Lore isn't afraid of becoming a popstar|url=https://ontheaside.com/music/yoke-lore-isnt-afraid-of-becoming-a-popstar/|access-date=March 20, 2020|website=A.Side|language=en-US}}</ref> Before going solo Galvin was a member of [[Walk the Moon]] and Yellerkin.<ref name=voice>{{Cite web|title=Prepare to Find Religion With Yoke Lore at Baby's All Right {{!}} The Village Voice|url=https://www.villagevoice.com/2017/07/06/find-religion-with-yoke-lore-at-babys-all-right-in-brooklyn/|access-date=March 20, 2020|website=www.villagevoice.com|date=July 6, 2017}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Galvin is from [[Katonah, New York]], and both his parents were creative professionals who encouraged him to explore the arts.<ref name=amg>[https://www.allmusic.com/artist/yoke-lore-mn0003540885/biography Yoke Lore] at [[Allmusic]]</ref> His brother is actor and singer [[Noah Galvin]]. His father is Catholic and his mother is Jewish.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://forward.com/culture/469137/yoke-lore-adrian-galvin-musician-banjo-player/|title=Musician Yoke Lore thrives as a pandemic hermit. Here's what he's gotten done}}</ref> Galvin attended Kenyon College where he began writing songs and playing drums for the band [[Walk the Moon]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Cleared for Liftoff – Kenyon Alumni Magazine|url=https://bulletin.kenyon.edu/feature/cleared-for-liftoff/|access-date=April 14, 2020|website=bulletin.kenyon.edu}}</ref> He split with the band to form a short-lived project called Yellerkin in 2013.<ref name=amg/> Galvin lived in a monastery in India for a time after college.<ref name=voice/> Yellerkin, which was based in Brooklyn, was co-founded with producer Luca Buccellati.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Song of the Day: Yellerkin – Tools|url=https://www.kexp.org/read/2015/01/19/song-of-the-day-yellerkin-tools/|access-date=April 14, 2020|website=www.kexp.org}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=September 2, 2014|title=Yellerkin – "Tools" (|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1706569/yellerkin-tools-stereogum-premiere/mp3s/|access-date=April 14, 2020|website=Stereogum}}</ref>

== Yoke Lore ==
He released his first EP as Yoke Lore in 2016, and soon after performed at [[SXSW]] and toured internationally.<ref>[https://atwoodmagazine.com/yoke-lore-2017-interview/ Fighting the Happy War: Catching Up with Yoke Lore]. ''Atwood Magazine'', June 13, 2017.</ref> Galvin is also a dancer and founded a dance group called Boomerang in 2016.<ref name=amg/>

In late 2017, he released a video for the song "Beige".<ref>[https://nylon.com/articles/yoke-lore-beige-video Yoke Lore Pushes Against The Pop Machine In “Beige” Video]. ''[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]]'', December 4, 2017.</ref> Late in 2019, he released the single "Dead Ringer".<ref>[https://ew.com/music/2019/11/08/yoke-lore-dead-ringer-in-the-basement/ Yoke Lore performs 'Dead Ringer' for EW's In The Basement]. ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'', November 8, 2019.</ref> Early in 2020, he collaborated with [[Jax Anderson]] on the single "Sensitive Heart".<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8550967/yoke-lore-jax-anderson-sensitive-heart Yoke Lore and Jax Anderson Are Just a Couple of 'Sensitive Hearts' on Sweet New Track: Exclusive]. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', February 14, 2020.</ref>
