{{nihongo|'''Gen Hoshino'''|星野 源|Hoshino Gen|born January 28, 1981}} is a Japanese singer-songwriter, musician, actor, and writer.

==Career==
===Music===
Since 2000, Hoshino has led the [[instrumental]] band [[Sakerock]], where he plays [[marimba]] and guitar. They released over ten albums before disbanding in 2015. As a solo artist, he debuted with his first album ''[[Baka no Uta]]'' on June 23, 2010. His first physical single, "Kudaranai no Naka ni" was released on March 2, 2011 and peaked at number 17 on the [[Oricon Singles Chart]]. The subsequent singles – "Film", "Yume no Soto e", "Shiranai" (2012), and "Gag" (2013) – all charted in the top 10. His second album, ''[[Episode (Gen Hoshino album)|Episode]]'', released on September 28, 2011, peaked at number five. His third album, ''[[Stranger (Gen Hoshino album)|Stranger]]'', released on May 1, 2013, peaked at number two on the [[Oricon Albums Chart]] and was certified gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of Japan]].

Since 2011, he's been co-hosting a [[Ustream]] program called {{nihongo|''Sake no Sakana''|サケノサカナ}} together with [[Ichirō Yamaguchi]] of [[Sakanaction]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.en.barks.jp/news/?id=1000001910|title=Sakanaction : Ichiro Yamaguchi and Gen Hoshino's USTREAM Program 'Sake no Sakana', Back on Air for First Time in 9 Months &#124; BARKS News|publisher=En.barks.jp|access-date=27 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171227235313/http://www.en.barks.jp/news/?id=1000001910|archive-date=December 27, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2014 the program officially changed its name to ''Yoru no Television''.

His fourth album, ''[[Yellow Dancer]]'', was released on December 2, 2015 in Japan on [[Victor Entertainment|Speedstar Records]], and debuted atop the Oricon and ''[[Billboard Japan|Billboard]]'' Album charts. ''Yellow Dancer'' was certified platinum by the [[RIAJ]] for physical shipments, and became one of the few albums to receive a gold certification for digital downloads.

His fifth album, ''[[Pop Virus]]'', was released on December 17, 2018.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.cinra.net/interview/201902-ariizumiyanatake | title=星野源『POP VIRUS』が変えたJ-POPの常識 有泉智子×YANATAKE / February 28 2019  | access-date=March 8, 2019 | language=ja| publisher=cinra.net}}</ref> It topped the Oricon Album charts four weeks in a row and was certified double platinum by the [[RIAJ]] one month after its release. The commercial success of the album, within a couple of months, earned him a Best Album prize at the 33rd [[Japan Gold Disc Awards]], alongside a Best Song prize for the digital success of the single, and ''[[asadora]]'' theme song, "Idea".<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.golddisc.jp/award/33/Prize_2.html#gd33_23 | title=Best 5 Songs By Download  | access-date=March 8, 2019 | language=ja | publisher=RIAJ}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.golddisc.jp/award/33/Prize_1.html#gd33_10 | title=Best 5 Albums in Japan | access-date=March 8, 2019 | language=ja | publisher=RIAJ}}</ref> He also became the first artist to win a total of four trophies at the [[Space Shower Music Awards]] of 2019.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://awards.spaceshower.jp/2019/winners/#year | title=Winners of the Space Shower Music Awards 2019 | access-date=March 8, 2019 | language=ja| publisher=Space Shower Networks}}</ref> On August 30, 2019, all of his music was made available on streaming services.<ref>{{cite web |title=All of Gen Hoshino's Music Now Available for Streaming. |url=https://www.hoshinogen.com/en/news/190830_subscription/}}</ref>

On February 18, 2018, Hoshino uploaded a music video of his song 'Doraemon', which served as a tribute song for the [[Doraemon|Japanese franchise of the same name.]] It was used as the theme to the 38th Doraemon movie, "Nobita's Treasure Island," and, since fall 2019, has been used as the current opening theme of the [[Doraemon (2005 TV series)|Doraemon anime]].

===Acting===
Hoshino's movie debut, [[Lee Sang-il (film director)|Lee Sang-il]]'s ''[[69 (film)|69]]'', was an [[Film adaptation|adaptation]] of the [[Ryū Murakami]] novel of the [[69 (novel)|same name]]. He previously acted in various [[Japanese television drama|television dramas]] and [[stage play]]s. In 2012, he debuted as a voice actor, voicing Buddha in the [[original video animation]] (OVA) [[Film adaptation|adaptation]] of [[Hikaru Nakamura (manga artist)|Hikaru Nakamura]]'s [[manga]] ''[[Saint Young Men]]'', and also provided a theme song called "Gag" for its 2013 theatrical version, where he reprised his role.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/interest/2013-03-21/gen-hoshino-to-perform-saint-young-men-film-theme-song|title=Gen Hoshino to Perform Saint Young Men Film's Theme Song – Interest |publisher=Anime News Network|date=September 30, 2013|access-date=27 December 2017}}</ref> In 2013, he played the lead character in Masahide Ichii's {{nihongo|''Blindly in Love''|箱入り息子の恋|Hakoiri Musuko no Koi}} alongside [[Kaho (actress)|Kaho]], and starred in the [[Sion Sono]] film ''[[Why Don't You Play in Hell?]]''. In 2016, Hoshino starred in TBS's ''[[Nigeru wa Haji da ga Yaku ni Tatsu|The Full-Time Wife Escapist]]''. With [[Yui Aragaki]] as his co-star, he portrayed a salaryman named Hiramasa Tsuzaki. The drama achieved steadily rising ratings, with a real-time viewership peak rating of 20.8% on the final episode, and an overall rating of 14.5%.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.sponichi.co.jp/entertainment/news/2016/12/21/kiji/20161220s00041000229000c.html | title=「逃げ恥」最終回は視聴率20.8%！初回から右肩上がり、大台超えで有終の美 | newspaper=[[Sponichi]] | language=ja | date=December 21, 2016 | access-date=March 20, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | url=https://mainichi.jp/english/articles/20161216/p2a/00m/0na/018000c | title=Overall rating of popular TV drama 'Nigehaji' hits 30% | newspaper=[[The Mainichi]] | date=December 16, 2016 | access-date=March 20, 2019}}</ref> Hoshino's performance earned him several Best Supporting Actor awards.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://confidence-award.jp/vol06.html | title=第6回 コンフィデンスアワード・ドラマ賞 | access-date=September 21, 2018 | language=ja | publisher=Oricon}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://thetv.jp/feature/drama-academy/91/awards/ | title=受賞結果総評 第91回ドラマアカデミー賞 | access-date=September 21, 2018 | language=ja | publisher=[[Kadokawa Corporation]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.nikkansports.com/entertainment/award/ns-dramagp/20/ns-dgp-result_autumn.html | title=日刊スポーツ・ドラマグランプリ 第20回秋投票結果 : 日刊スポーツ | access-date=December 1, 2018 | language=ja | newspaper=[[Nikkan Sports|Nikkan Sports News]]}}</ref> He also provided the theme song for the drama, titled "[[Koi (song)|Koi]]".

Gen Hoshino created the parody character "Akira Nise" (ニセ明, ''Nise Akira'', literally ''Fake Akira'') as a tribute to [[Akira Fuse]].<ref>{{cite web |title=星野源　今度はニセ明　大人気キャラを１週間アップ |url=https://headlines.yahoo.co.jp/hl?a=20200420-00000120-dal-ent}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Hoshino Gen Int'l @hoshinogen_intl |url=https://twitter.com/hoshinogen_intl/status/814288115795836928}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Watch Gen Hoshino's Quirky Music Video for 'Doraemon' Japanese Comic |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/international/8215545/watch-gen-hoshino-music-video-doraemon-japan}}</ref>

In 2017, Hoshino lent his voice to the main character of award-winning animated film ''[[Night Is Short, Walk On Girl]]''. He also became the voice of the Father in [[Mamoru Hosoda]]'s ''[[Mirai (film)|Mirai]]'', which was released into theaters in Japan on July 20, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mirai-no-mirai.jp/|title=「未来のミライ」公式サイト|website=Mirai-no-mirai.jp}}</ref>

''[[Idaten (TV series)|Idaten]]'', NHK's 2019 Taiga drama, chosen for the theme to encourage [[2020 Summer Olympics|the Tokyo 2020 Olympic Games]], Hoshino portrayed Kazushige Hirasawa, who delivered the persuasive speech that helped determine the venue for [[1964 Summer Olympics|the Tokyo 1964 Olympic Games]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.sponichi.co.jp/entertainment/news/2018/12/15/kiji/20181214s00041000265000c.html | title=星野源　大河「いだてん」で滝クリ級スピーチ　カンペ見ずに流ちょうな英語で五輪招致導く  / December 15, 2018| access-date=April 19, 2019 | language=ja| publisher=Suponichi Annex}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://opop.tsukuba.ac.jp/en/archive/olympic-and-bc/  | title=Olympic Invitation and Bunkyo City, Professor Hisashi SANADA (Faculty of Health and Sport Sciences, University of Tsukuba)/Kazushige HIRASAWA| access-date=March 8, 2019 | publisher=University of Tsukuba(OPOP)}}</ref>

On April 30, 2018, it was announced that Hoshino will star in ''Samurai Shifters'', the film adaptation of Akihiro Dobashi's historical novel ''Hikkoshi Daimyo Sanzenri'', set to premier on August 30, 2019. In the movie, he plays a bookworm samurai called Harunosuke Katagiri, who receives the mission to help a daimyo move. It will be Hoshino's first lead role in a live-action movie since ''Blindly in Love'' in 2013.

==Personal life==
On December 22, 2012, Hoshino was diagnosed with a [[subarachnoid hemorrhage]] and underwent surgery.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/article/2012/12/hoshino-gen-announces-his-hiatus-due-to-subarachnoid-hemorrhage/|title=Hoshino Gen announces his hiatus due to subarachnoid hemorrhage|date=December 23, 2012|publisher=tokyohive.com|access-date=27 December 2017}}</ref> He officially returned to public life on February 28, 2013 with his appearance at the Tokyo [[J-Wave]] Awards.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.daily.co.jp/newsflash/gossip/2013/02/28/0005776806.shtml|title=くも膜下出血で手術の星野源が復帰/芸能速報/デイリースポーツ online|author=デイリースポーツ社|publisher=Daily.co.jp|language=ja|access-date=27 December 2017}}</ref>

In 2021, Hoshino married actress [[Yui Aragaki]], his co-star in the television series ''[[Nigeru wa Haji da ga Yaku ni Tatsu|The Full-Time Wife Escapist]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2193908/full/|title= 新垣結衣と星野源が結婚を発表「互いに支え合い豊かな時間を積み重ねていけたら」|access-date= May 19, 2021|work= Oricon}}</ref>
