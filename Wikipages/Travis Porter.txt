===2010-2012: ''From Day 1''===
On December 22, 2010, the trio released their debut single, "Make it Rain", which peaked at number 50 on the Urban radio charts and debuted on the charts at number 97 before climbing to number 46. On October 7, 2011, [[RCA Music Group]] announced the disbandment of [[Jive Records]], along with [[Arista Records]] and [[J Records]]. With the shutdown, the group (and all other artists previously signed to these three labels) announced that they would have to released their future materials to [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/industry/record-labels/rca-s-new-executive-team-named-under-ceo-1005324782.story |title=RCA's New Executive Team Named Under CEO Peter Edge Amid Layoffs (Update) |website=Billboard.biz |date=2011-08-23 |access-date=2012-04-09}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fmqb.com/article.asp?id=2268707 |title=Radio Industry News, Music Industry Updates, Arbitron Ratings, Music News and more! |publisher=FMQB |access-date=2012-04-09}}</ref> On February 7, 2011, Travis Porter released their second single, titled "[[Bring It Back (Travis Porter song)|Bring It Back]]".

In 2012, the trio released their third single, "[[Ayy Ladies]]", a collaboration with [[Young Money Entertainment|Young Money]] rapper [[Tyga]]. The single peaked at number 56 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, and has topped the [[Top Heatseekers|Top Heatseekers Songs]] charts, becoming Porter's most successful song to date.<ref>{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=travis porter|chart=all}} |title=Top 100 Music Hits, Top 100 Music Charts, Top 100 Songs & The Hot 100 |website=Billboard.com |access-date=2012-04-09}}</ref> The group's debut album, ''[[From Day 1]]'' was released on May 29, 2012, under [[RCA Records]] and the group's own Porter House Music [[record label]].<ref name="fromday1">{{cite web|last=Ryon|first=Sean|title=Travis Porter Announce Release Date And Title Of Debut LP|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.19096/title.travis-porter-announce-release-date-and-title-of-debut-lp/|website=HipHopDX.com|access-date=March 23, 2012|date=March 22, 2012}}</ref> ''[[From Day 1]]'' charted at number 16 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and number 2 on the [[Top Rap Albums]] charts.

==Controversy==
===Roscoe Dash===
The track "[[All the Way Turnt Up]]" has caused controversy with [[Roscoe Dash]]. Dash stated that he came up with the song, including the hook and the lyrics and even asked the group Travis Porter to appear on this song. Travis Porter then added this song to their mixtape, which caused fans to believe that this was not Roscoe Dash's song, but Travis Porter's. Roscoe Dash then released the inverted version to the song through his label [[Interscope Records]], adding a verse by a fellow rapper [[Soulja Boy]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Shaheem Reid |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1641410/20100611/porter_travis.jhtml |title=Travis Porter Are 'Killing' Indie Scene With Proud To Be A Problem |publisher=MTV |date=2010-06-11 |access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref>

===Young Dro and Yung L.A.===
The group's song, titled "Black Boy, White Boy" caused a controversy when rappers [[Young Dro]] and [[Yung L.A.]] said that the song title was initially theirs.<ref>Williford, Fredrickus. "Travvvy! Travis Porter From ATL to the World." Ezine Articles 6 March 2010</ref>
