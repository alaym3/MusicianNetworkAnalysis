'''Walter William Cobb''' (born 29 September 1940), known as '''Billy Cobb''', was an English [[association football|footballer]] who scored 38 goals from 199 appearances in [[the Football League]] playing for [[Nottingham Forest F.C.|Nottingham Forest]], [[Plymouth Argyle F.C.|Plymouth Argyle]], [[Brentford F.C.|Brentford]] and [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]]. He played in [[midfielder|midfield]].<ref name=NB>{{cite web |url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player/billycobb.html |title=Billy Cobb |work=UK A–Z Transfers |publisher=Neil Brown |access-date=22 April 2013}}</ref> He went on to play for [[Boston United F.C.|Boston United]] in the [[Northern Premier League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bufc.drfox.org.uk/rollcall.html#Cobb |title=Boston United Roll Call |work=Boston United FC&nbsp;– the original website |publisher=Ken Fox |access-date=22 April 2013}}</ref>

Cobb was the scorer of Nottingham Forest's first goal in European football, in a 5–1 defeat to [[Valencia CF|Valencia]] in the [[1961–62 Inter-Cities Fairs Cup|1961–62 Fairs Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.newarkadvertiser.co.uk/articles/sport/Keeping-it-in-the-family |title=Keeping it in the family |work=Newark Advertiser |date=4 January 2008 |access-date=29 March 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130927134943/http://www.newarkadvertiser.co.uk/articles/sport/Keeping-it-in-the-family |archive-date=27 September 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He scored a [[hat-trick]] on his Lincoln City debut, in an 8–1 defeat of [[Luton Town]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.redimps.com/archive3/index.mv?cat=hattrick&drop=matchsearch |title=Hat-trick heroes |work=The Lincoln City FC Archive |publisher=Lincoln City F.C |access-date=29 March 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130926193012/http://www.redimps.com/archive3/index.mv?cat=hattrick&drop=matchsearch |archive-date=26 September 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> After retiring from football he kept a pub in [[Nottingham]]<ref>{{cite web |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120722131701/http://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/page/WhereNow/0%2C%2C10308~233794%2C00.html |url=http://www.nottinghamforest.co.uk/page/WhereNow/0,,10308~233794,00.html |title=Where are they now&nbsp;– from A to I |publisher=Nottingham Forest F.C |archive-date=22 July 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> and managed the bars at [[Nottingham Ice Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://docs.newsbank.com/openurl?ctx_ver=z39.88-2004&rft_id=info:sid/iw.newsbank.com:AWNB:TND1&rft_val_format=info:ofi/fmt:kev:mtx:ctx&rft_dat=131FED8DAEDF4098&svc_dat=InfoWeb:aggregated5&req_dat=0D0CB57AB53DF815 |title=Where are they now? |first=Jon |last=Culley |work=The Independent |location=London |date=21 November 1995 |access-date=22 April 2013}}</ref>
