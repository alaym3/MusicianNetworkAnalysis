'''Dougie MacLean''', [[Officer of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire|OBE]] (born 27 September 1954)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rateyourmusic.com/artist/dougie_maclean |title=Albums by Dougie MacLean |publisher=Rate Your Music |access-date=6 April 2013}}</ref> is a Scottish singer-songwriter, composer, multi-instrumentalist and record producer. Described by [[AllMusic]] as "one of Scotland's premier singer-songwriters", MacLean has performed both under his own name, and as part of multiple [[Folk music|folk]] bands, since the mid 1970s. His most famous pieces include "[[Caledonia (song)|Caledonia]]", which is often dubbed Scotland's "unofficial national anthem"; and "The Gael", which became the main theme to the 1992 film ''[[The Last of the Mohicans (1992 film)|The Last of the Mohicans]]''.

MacLean's songs have been covered by numerous popular artists. Aside from his career as a touring singer-songwriter, MacLean founded the Dunkeld Records label and recording studio with his wife Jennifer in 1983.

==Origins and early work==
MacLean grew up in the [[Perthshire]] countryside, where his father was a gardener. His mother played [[melodeon (accordion)|melodeon]], and his father played the fiddle.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dougiemaclean.com/index.php/about-dougie/full-biography|title="Dougie MacLean: Full Biography|access-date=2018-09-02}}</ref>
To support himself in the 1970s, MacLean was a driver for [[Doc Watson]] and [[Merle Watson]] during their tour around Europe.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/entertainment-us-music-dougiemaclean-idUSBRE90T0HT20130130 |title=From Doc Watson to lifetime folk award, Dougie MacLean sings on |date=30 January 2013 |publisher=Reuters |access-date=2013-02-05}}</ref> He maintained a friendship afterward and has appeared at [[Merlefest]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.merlefest.org/content.aspx?id=8237 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121023211505/http://www.merlefest.org/content.aspx?id=8237 |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-10-23 |title=2013 Merlefest Artist list }}</ref>

In 1976, MacLean began touring with Scottish folk band [[The Tannahill Weavers]], with whom he also participated in a studio recording. In the late 1970s, he spent six months touring with Scottish band [[Silly Wizard]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dougiemaclean.com/index.php/about-dougie/full-biography|title="Dougie MacLean: Full Biography|access-date=2018-09-02}}</ref> "[[Caledonia (song)|Caledonia]]", from [[Caledonia (Alan Roberts and Dougie MacLean album)|MacLean's first album]] with Alan Roberts (1978), is often dubbed Scotland's "unofficial national anthem".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p01qlqd4|title=Dougie MacLean performs 'This Love Will Carry'|website=[[BBC Music]]|access-date=28 March 2021|quote=MacLean... is perhaps best known for his track, 'Caledonia' that is often referred to as Scotland's unofficial national anthem.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/news/14839577.checklist-10-things-to-do-in-scotland-this-weekend/|title=10 things to do in Scotland this weekend|last=Castle|first=Jill|date=3 November 2016|website=[[The Herald (Glasgow)|The Herald]]|access-date=28 March 2021|quote=Dougie MacLean, the man who wrote Scotland's unofficial national anthem, Caledonia.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thecourier.co.uk/lifestyle/entertainment/music/129368/review-dougie-maclean-at-bruce-festival/|title=Review: Dougie MacLean at Bruce Festival|date=20 August 2010|website=[[The Courier (Dundee)|The Courier]]|access-date=28 March 2021|quote=Who better then to open a festival steeped in Scottish history than MacLean... unofficial national anthem Caledonia came early in the set.}}</ref>

==Solo career==
MacLean is described by [[AllMusic]] as "one of Scotland's premier singer-songwriters".<ref name="allmusic">{{cite news |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/dougie-maclean-mn0000199194/biography |title=Dougie MacLean: Biography |first=Craig |last=Harris |work=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=8 April 2012}}</ref> His solo career started in 1981 and since then he has recorded numerous albums. He plays multiple instruments, including guitar, violin, [[mandola]], [[viola]], [[bouzouki]], [[banjo]] and [[Bass (instrument)|bass]] as well as being a singer and composer.<ref>Emblen, Frank (23 February 1986). New Jersey Guide. "The New York Times". Accessed on 14 August 2007.</ref>

MacLean composed "The Gael" (1990), which became the main theme to the 1992 film ''[[The Last of the Mohicans (1992 film)|The Last of the Mohicans]]''. He also served as music director for [[TAG Theatre Company]]'s 1993 production of ''[[A Scots Quair]]'',<ref name="allmusic"/> releasing his contributions on the ''Sunset Song'' LP (1994). MacLean's songs have been recorded by many artists including [[Dolores Keane]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/scotland-now/scots-anthem-caledonia-adopted-soldiers-22032407|title=Scots anthem Caledonia adopted by US soldiers in emotional pandemic performance|last=Murphy|first=Sean|date=15 May 2020|website=[[Daily Record (Scotland)|Daily Record]]|access-date=28 March 2021}}</ref> [[Ronan Keating]], [[Paolo Nutini]], [[Amy Macdonald]], [[Kathy Mattea]], [[Frankie Miller]], [[Cara Dillon]], and [[Mary Black]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thecourier.co.uk/fp/lifestyle/entertainment/music/600062/musical-treasure-dougie-maclean-heads-fife/|title=Musical treasure Dougie MacLean heads to Fife|last=Ritchie|first=Gayle|date=14 February 2018|website=[[The Courier (Dundee)|The Courier]]|access-date=28 March 2021}}</ref> who covered "Turning Away" (1991) for the soundtrack of the 2001 film ''[[Angel Eyes (film)|Angel Eyes]]''.

MacLean was the subject of the 1993 [[BBC]] documentary film ''The Land: The Songs of Dougie MacLean''.<ref name="allmusic"/> He has organised and performed in the [[Perthshire Amber]] festival, [[Birnam, Perth and Kinross|Birnam]] & [[Dunkeld]], alongside multiple performances at [[Celtic Connections]], Glasgow.

MacLean founded the Dunkeld Records label and recording studio with his wife Jennifer in 1983.<ref name="allmusic"/>

==Awards==
In 2011, MacLean was invested as an [[Officer of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire]] (OBE).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-tayside-central-12094777 |title='Caledonia' folk writer receives OBE |date=31 December 2010|publisher=BBC |access-date=4 February 2011}}</ref>

In 2013, MacLean was awarded the [[BBC Radio 2]] Folk Award for Lifetime Achievement for Contribution to Songwriting. The award was presented by First Minister [[Alex Salmond]] at [[Glasgow Royal Concert Hall]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.scotsman.com/lifestyle/music/news-and-features/bbc-radio-2-folk-awards-recognises-scots-legends-1-2768574 |title=BBC Radio 2 Folk Awards recognises Scots legends |date=31 January 2013 |publisher=Scotsman |access-date= 5 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/entertainment/music/music-news/dougie-maclean-celebrates-folk-award-1568325 |title=Dougie Maclean celebrates Folk Award with star-studded Caledonia rendition |date= 1 February 2013 |publisher=Daily Record and Sunday Mail |access-date= 5 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00yrkrj/features/folk-awards-2013-nominees |title= BBC2 Folk Awards 2013 Winners |access-date= 5 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00yrkrj |title= BBC2 Folk Awards 2013 Winners |access-date= 5 February 2013}}</ref>
