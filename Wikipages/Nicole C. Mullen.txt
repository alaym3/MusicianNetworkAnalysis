'''Aileen Nicole Coleman-Mullen''', known professionally as '''Nicole C. Mullen''', (born January 3, 1967)<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thezimbabwean.co.uk/articles/38380|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120913081113/http://www.thezimbabwean.co.uk/articles/38380|url-status=dead|archive-date=September 13, 2012|title=Nicole C. Mullen's return|access-date=January 11, 2011|work=The Zimbabwean}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter, and [[choreographer]]. She was born and raised in [[Cincinnati, Ohio]].

==Music career==

While studying at [[Christ for the Nations Institute]] in [[Dallas]], she joined a vocal group called Living Praise and began pursuing a music career.<ref name="charisma">{{cite web|last1=Bonham|first1=Chad|title=She's Just Everyday People|url=http://www.charismamag.com/life/117-j15/covers/unorganized/1727-shes-just-everyday-people|website=www.charismamag.com}}</ref> Under the name Nicole, she recorded her first solo album, ''Don’t Let Me Go'', in 1991 with the [[Indie (music)|independent label]] [[Frontline Records (1980s)|Frontline Records]]. It was produced by [[Tim Miner]]. She continued her singing-songwriting-choreography career in the 1990s, singing backup for [[Michael W. Smith]] and the [[Newsboys]], writing for [[Jaci Velasquez]], and working as a dancer and [[choreography|choreographer]] with [[Amy Grant]]. She also provided backup vocals for the [[music video]] at the end of the [[VeggieTales]] program, ''[[Larry-Boy and the Fib from Outer Space!]]'' and the opening of the VeggieTales presentation, ''[[Larry-Boy and the Rumor Weed]]''. She was signed to [[Word Records]] in 1998 by VP of A&R [[Brent Bourgeois]]. Her fourth album, ''[[Talk About It (Nicole C. Mullen album)|Talk About It]]'', was certified gold for sales in excess of 500,000 copies on April 15, 2008.{{citation needed|date=August 2016}}

==Personal life==
Mullen has been married twice. During her first marriage, she endured physical and mental abuse.<ref>{{cite web|title=Nicole C. Mullen Biography |url=http://www.todayschristianmusic.com/artists/nicole-c-mullen/biography/|website=www.todayschristianmusic.com}}</ref> In 1993, she married singer, songwriter and music producer [[David Mullen (singer)|David Mullen]]. They divorced in 2014. In a Facebook post, she cited "Biblical reasons" for the divorce.<ref>{{cite web|title=New couple alert? Gospel singer Donnie McClurkin shows off his Stellar Awards 'date'—CCM singer Nicole Mullen - EEW Magazine - News from a faith-based perspective|url=http://buzz.eewmagazine.com/eew-magazine-buzz-blog/2016/2/21/new-couple-alert-gospel-singer-donnie-mcclurkin-shows-off-hi.html|website=buzz.eewmagazine.com}}</ref> They have three children, one daughter and two sons, one of whom was adopted.<ref name="charisma"/>

In 2016, it was reported that Mullen had entered into a relationship with minister/singer [[Donnie McClurkin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.christianpost.com/news/donnie-mcclurkin-dating-ccm-singer-nicole-c-mullen-158577/|title=Donnie McClurkin Dating CCM Singer Nicole C Mullen?|website=www.christianpost.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.centrictv.com/entertainment/celebrity-news/articles/2016/08/17/donnie-mcclurkin-says-he-and-girlfriend-need-counseling-before-getting-married.html|title=Donnie McClurkin Says He and Girlfriend Need Counseling Before Getting Married|first=Centric|last=TV|website=CentricTV}}</ref>

=== Activism ===
Mullen established a mentor group for girls called "The Baby Girls Club" where she mentors youth through the arts.  Mullen has also been active with the International Needs Network Ghana, an organization that works to free [[Ritual servitude|Trokosi slaves]] in Ghana.{{citation needed|date=August 2016}}

==Awards and honors==
Mullen was inducted into the Christian Music Hall of Fame<ref>[http://hallmuseum.com Christian Music Hall of Fame] website  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101229220325/http://hallmuseum.com/ |date=December 29, 2010 }}</ref> on November 5, 2011. Mullen is the only African-American artist to win the [[GMA Dove Awards|Dove Award]] for Song of the Year and Songwriter of the Year.{{citation needed|date=August 2016}}
