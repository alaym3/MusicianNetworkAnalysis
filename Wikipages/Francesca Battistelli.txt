'''Francesca Battistelli''' (born May 18, 1985) is an American [[contemporary Christian music|Christian]] singer and songwriter from New York City.<ref name="JFH">{{cite web |url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/artists/FrancescaBattistelli.asp |title=Francesca Battistelli Artist Database, Francesca Battistelli Discography |publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout |access-date=June 11, 2008}}</ref> She was originally an independent artist and had released an independent album, ''Just a Breath'', in 2004. Her first studio album on [[Fervent Records]], ''[[My Paper Heart (album)|My Paper Heart]]'', was released on July 22, 2008. Her first single, "[[I'm Letting Go]]", was released to radio stations on July 15, 2008, and charted on national Christian [[Contemporary hit radio|CHR]] charts. The song was the 16th most played song of 2008 on Christian radio stations according to R&R magazine.<ref name=RR2008CHR>[http://www.radioandrecords.com/Formats/Charts/YearEnd/08/Christian_CHR_songs.asp 2008 Year End Charts/Top Christian CHR songs] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090525080129/http://www.radioandrecords.com/Formats/Charts/YearEnd/08/Christian_CHR_songs.asp |date=May 25, 2009 }}, [[R&R (magazine)|R&R magazine]]. Retrieved January 15, 2009</ref> Battistelli received her first Grammy Award nomination in 2009, for Best Gospel Performance with her single, "[[Free to Be Me]]". She later received her first Grammy Award in 2016 at the [[58th Annual Grammy Awards]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Christian Music Performance/Song|Best Contemporary Christian Music Performance/Song]] with her single "Holy Spirit" from her album ''[[If We're Honest]]''.<ref name=2016-Grammy>{{cite web |url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/1653-francesca-battistelli-wins-first-career-grammy-award-for-holy-spirit |title=Francesca Battistelli Wins First Career GRAMMY Award For 'Holy Spirit' |date=February 17, 2016 |publisher=TheChristianBeat.org |access-date=February 19, 2016}}</ref>

== Biography ==

=== Early life ===
Battistelli was born on May 18, 1985, in New York City.<ref name="JFH" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mvnu.edu/wnzr/featured.html |title=Featured Artist: Francesca Battistelli |access-date=May 4, 2009 |publisher=[[WNZR]] |first=Ashley |last=Erin |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081007134429/http://mvnu.edu/wnzr/featured.html |archive-date=October 7, 2008 }}</ref> She grew up in a musical family with both of her parents involved in musical theater.<ref name="about.com">{{cite web |url=http://christianmusic.about.com/b/2008/05/21/introducing-francesca-battistelli.htm |title=Introducing Francesca Battistelli |date=May 21, 2008 |publisher=[[About.com]] |access-date=June 11, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080607150349/http://christianmusic.about.com/b/2008/05/21/introducing-francesca-battistelli.htm |archive-date=June 7, 2008  }}</ref> "There was just something about live theater—especially musical theater—that has always resonated with me", she said.<ref name="cmspin" /> She "knew that she was going to spend her life performing" after seeing a performance of the musical ''[[The Secret Garden (musical)|The Secret Garden]]'' on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] at the age of six.<ref name="about.com" /> "So I thought that was it. I was going to be [[Mary Lennox]] in ''The Secret Garden''."<ref name="ymz-bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.yourmusiczone.com/go/ymz/music_artist_more/battistelli_francesca/ |title=Francesca Battistelli Biography |publisher=Your Music Zone |access-date=June 12, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080906174449/http://www.yourmusiczone.com/go/ymz/music_artist_more/battistelli_francesca/ |archive-date=September 6, 2008 }}</ref>

When she was 15, she became a member of the [[Orlando, Florida]] all-girl mainstream pop group Bella. The group's touring performances included singing and dancing, which were "two things I loved", she said.<ref name="ymz-bio" /> After the group disbanded, Battistelli picked up the guitar and began writing songs.

=== Career ===
As an independent artist, Battistelli released her debut album, ''Just a Breath'', in 2004. In early 2008, she released the ''I'm Letting Go EP'' exclusively through [[iTunes Store|iTunes]] on [[Fervent Records]], which contains four songs, including the title track. All four songs were included on her first major label studio album, ''[[My Paper Heart (album)|My Paper Heart]]'', which was released on July 22, 2008.<ref name="lightvox">{{cite web |url=http://www.lightvox.com/francesca-battistelli-im-letting-go-my-paper-heart.html |title=Francesca Battistelli My Paper Heart – Releases July 22nd |date=May 18, 2008 |publisher=Word Music Group; Lightvox.com |access-date=June 11, 2008|archive-url  = https://web.archive.org/web/20080615140604/http://www.lightvox.com/francesca-battistelli-im-letting-go-my-paper-heart.html|archive-date =June 15, 2008}}</ref> The album was produced by [[GMA Dove Award|Dove Award]]-winning producer [[Ian Eskelin]] and published through Word Distribution.<ref name="cmspin">{{cite web |url=http://www.cmspin.com/newsmanager/anmviewer.asp?a=4951&z=26 |title=Francesca Battistelli Opens Her Heart With July {{sic|22|rd|nolink=yes}} Release |date=May 15, 2008 |publisher=CMSpin.com |access-date=June 11, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708170651/http://www.cmspin.com/newsmanager/anmviewer.asp?a=4951&z=26 |archive-date=July 8, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Her first radio single, "[[I'm Letting Go]]", was released on July 15, 2008. The song had reached the top 20 on [[R&R (magazine)|R&R]]'s Christian [[Contemporary hit radio|CHR]] charts by May 30, 2008.<ref name="ymz-5/30">{{cite web|url=http://www.yourmusiczone.com/go/ymz/news_more/francesca_battistellis_july_22nd_release_my_paper_heart_already_garnering_m/ |title=Francesca Battistelli's July 22nd Release, My Paper Heart, Already Garnering Major Buzz |publisher=Your Music Zone |date=May 30, 2008 |access-date=June 11, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080908091845/http://www.yourmusiczone.com/go/ymz/news_more/francesca_battistellis_july_22nd_release_my_paper_heart_already_garnering_m/ |archive-date=September 8, 2008 }}</ref> In early July 2008, the song reached No. 10 on Christian CHR charts.<ref name="r&r">{{cite web |url=http://www.radioandrecords.com/rrwebsite20/Members/Charts.aspx?ChartId=25 |title=Christian CHR National Airplay |date=April 3, 2009 |access-date=April 6, 2009 |work=[[R&R (magazine)|R&R]] |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090422094326/http://www.radioandrecords.com/RRWebSite20/Members/Charts.aspx?ChartId=25 |archive-date=April 22, 2009  }}</ref>

The song "[[Free to Be Me]]", which received a Grammy nomination in 2009, was partially inspired when she backed her car into a lawyer's car, resulting in the line "'cause I got a couple dents in my fender".<ref>{{cite news|last=Davis|first=Kevin|title=#39 – "Free To Be Me" by Francesca Battistelli|url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/article.php?article_id=225|access-date=April 24, 2013|newspaper=newreleasetuesday.com|date=June 16, 2009}}</ref>

Battistelli began a period of touring in the U.S. in early 2008; she played solo concerts as well as performing with fellow Fervent Records band [[Big Daddy Weave]].<ref name="ymz-2" /> She performed at several Christian music festivals throughout mid-2008, such as [[Spirit West Coast]] in California and Alive Fest.<ref name="about.com" /><ref name="ymz-2">{{cite web|url=http://www.yourmusiczone.com/go/ymz/news_more/francesca_battistelli_opens_her_heart_with_july_22nd_release_my_paper_heart/ |title=Francesca Battistelli Opens Her Heart With July 22nd Release, "My Paper Heart" |publisher=Your Music Zone |date=May 28, 2008 |access-date=June 11, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080529172126/http://www.yourmusiczone.com/go/ymz/news_more/francesca_battistelli_opens_her_heart_with_july_22nd_release_my_paper_heart/ |archive-date=May 29, 2008 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> In late 2008, she guested on a tour with [[Aaron Shust]] and [[NewSong]].

Her debut single, "I'm Letting Go", reached No. 3 on [[R&R (magazine)|''R&R'']]'s Christian [[Contemporary hit radio|CHR]] and [[Adult contemporary|AC]] charts during mid to late 2008. Her next single, "[[Free to Be Me]]", reached No. 1 on the ''R&R'' Christian CHR format in March 2009 and held the top position for nine consecutive weeks through the beginning of May.<ref name="r&r" />

Battistelli joined [[TobyMac]], [[Hawk Nelson]], [[Brandon Heath]] and others on the [[Winter Jam 2009]] tour, the nation's largest annual [[Christian music]] tour, which ran from January through March 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jamtour.com |title=The Winter Jam 2012 Tour Spectacular |publisher=Jamtour.com |access-date=March 5, 2012}}</ref> On November 23, 2009, Battistelli's song "[[It's Your Life (Francesca Battistelli song)|It's Your Life]]" was featured on the series finale of ''[[Jon & Kate Plus 8]]'' and ''[[The Biggest Loser (American TV series)|The Biggest Loser]]'', the same day ''It's Your Life'', featuring all of the singles, was released on iTunes US.{{citation needed|date=October 2015}} ''It's Your Life Dented Fender Session'' was also released at that time.{{citation needed|date=October 2015}} "It's Your Life" was also used in the film ''[[Soul Surfer (film)|Soul Surfer]]''.{{Citation needed|date=October 2015}}

Battistelli toured on Winter Jam again for 2011 and released a second studio album, ''[[Hundred More Years]]'', on March 1, 2011. The album was preceded by the release of a single, "This is the Stuff", in early 2011, which was followed in July by "Motion of Mercy".<ref name="auto">{{cite news|last=Davis|first=Kevin|title=#240 – "Motion of Mercy" by Francesca Battistelli|url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/article.php?article_id=630|access-date=April 24, 2013|newspaper=newreleasetuesday.com|date=July 26, 2011}}</ref> On October 16, 2012, Battistelli released ''Christmas'', a collection of traditional holiday music.<ref>{{cite news|title=Francesca Battistelli: Christmas|url=https://www.amazon.com/Christmas-Francesca-Battistelli/dp/B008YMNXKA|access-date=April 24, 2013|date=October 16, 2012}}</ref>

On April 22, 2014, Battistelli's third studio album was released and titled ''[[If We're Honest]]''.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|title=Francesca Battistelli Preps for Release of Third Studio Album, 'If We're Honest,' with Pre-Order at Itunes Today|url=http://www.christiannewswire.com/news/6192073493.html|publisher=ChristianNewsWire|access-date=January 14, 2014}}</ref>

Battistelli released "Future" exclusively to [[Pizza Ranch]] on June 1, 2015. The song was written by Battistelli and Casey Brown and was produced by Ian Eskelin.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.pizzaranch.com/livinghope/track/future/|title = Pizza Ranch – "Living Hope" – "Future"|access-date = June 2, 2015|date = June 1, 2015|work = Pizza Ranch|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150607044457/http://www.pizzaranch.com/livinghope/track/future/|archive-date = June 7, 2015|url-status = dead}}</ref>{{better source|date=June 2015|reason=This is a primary source. We don't want to treat this article as a coatrack for everything the subject has ever done, we only want notable events to be listed. Find a secondary source.}}
[[File:Francesca2017.jpg|thumb|right|upright=0.7|Battistelli performing in 2017]]
In late 2016, Battistelli toured with [[Bethel Music]] and was featured on two songs on their 2017 release, ''[[Starlight (Bethel Music album)|Starlight]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hallels.com/articles/17107/20170318/francesca-battistelli-sings-lead-vocals-on-two-songs-on-bethel-musics-new-album.htm|title=Francesca Battistelli Sings Lead Vocals on Two Songs on Bethel Music's New Album|first=Timothy|last=Yap|publisher=Hallels|date=March 18, 2017|access-date=March 18, 2017}}</ref> In late 2017, she released ''Greatest Hits: The First Ten Years''.

On June 15, 2018, Battistelli released "[[The Breakup Song (Francesca Battistelli song)|The Breakup Song]]", the first single from her fourth studio album, ''[[Own It (album)|Own It]].''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newreleasetoday.com/news_detail.php?newsid=3261|title=Francesca Battistelli Drops 'The Breakup Song'|publisher=New Release Today|date=June 15, 2018|access-date=July 7, 2018}}</ref> The second single, "This Could Change Everything", was released on October 12, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hallels.com/articles/20230/20181015/watch-francesca-battistells-new-video-this-could-change-everything.htm|title=Francesca Battistelli Drops Single 'This Could Change Everything'|publisher=New Release Today|date=October 15, 2018|access-date=October 15, 2018}}</ref>

== Style ==
Battistelli's music is influenced by several genres. One reviewer wrote that "Battistelli's soulful piano-pop sound resembles a blend of her music interests—[[jazz]], rock, [[gospel music|gospel]] and [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]]."<ref name="musicremedy">{{cite web |url=http://www.musicremedy.com/f/Francesca_Battistelli/videos/Im_Letting_Go-22601.html |title=Francesca Battistelli I'm Letting Go music video |publisher=Music Remedy |access-date=June 11, 2008}}</ref> Battistelli stated that when she began writing music, "I really longed to bring something authentic to the [Christian music] scene that even non-Christians could listen to."<ref name="ymz-bio" />

Her track "Blue Sky" was featured on the Hollister Playlist for December 2009.

== Personal life ==
[[File:Francesca2016.jpg|thumb|right|upright=0.7|A pregnant Battistelli performs in 2016]]
Battistelli is married to, and managed by, Matt Goodwin, whom she met on the [[Winter Jam Tour Spectacular|Winter Jam]] tour when he was playing drums in the band [[NewSong]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mcall.com/entertainment/mc-winter-jam-francesca-batistelli-20150114-story.html|title=Christian singer Francesca Battistelli is honest on Winter Jam tour|first=John J.|last=Moser|work=The Morning Call|date=January 14, 2015}}</ref> Battistelli announced at the 2019 [[K-Love]] Fan Awards that she was pregnant with their fifth child.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ccmmagazine.com/k-love-fan-awards/francesca-battistelli-is-pregnant-with-baby-number-five/|title=Francesca Battistelli Is Pregnant With Baby Number Five|first=Brandon|last=Woolum|work=CCM Magazine|date=June 4, 2019}}</ref>
