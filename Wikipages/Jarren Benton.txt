'''Jarren Giovanni Benton''' (born October 26, 1981), is an American rapper from [[Decatur, Georgia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2015/10/happy-birthday-jarren-benton/|title=Happy Birthday, Jarren Benton!|work=XXL|date=October 26, 2015|access-date=January 11, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170701222927/http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2015/10/happy-birthday-jarren-benton/|archive-date=July 1, 2017}}</ref>

In early 2012, he signed to rapper [[Hopsin]]'s independent record label [[Funk Volume]] and released a mixtape titled, ''Freebasing with Kevin Bacon'' in June 2012.<ref name="FWKB">{{cite web|url=https://www.datpiff.com/Jarren-Benton-Freebasing-With-Kevin-Bacon-mixtape.367225.html|title=Freebasing With Kevin Bacon Mixtape by Jarren Benton|work=DatPiff|date=June 28, 2012|access-date=January 5, 2020}}</ref> A year later, on June 11, 2013, he released his debut studio album, ''[[My Grandma's Basement]]'', which received positive critical reviews and debuted at number 152 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref name="BB200">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/jarren-benton/chart-history/billboard-200|title=Jarren Benton – Chart History: Billboard 200|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 22, 2015|access-date=January 5, 2020}}</ref> After Funk Volume split up, Benton found himself between labels until 2018 when he was signed to [[Roc Nation]], founded by [[Jay-Z]] in 2008.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://rocnation.com/jarrenbenton/|title=Jarren Benton – ROCNATION|work=Roc Nation|access-date=October 11, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life ==
From his early youth, Benton aspired to be a rapper. He told ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', "I started trying to rap pretty young and the shit was kind of whack and then I got a little older and I started going around Atlanta performing at certain showcases. I got a little name for myself, but it was kind of hard in Atlanta, just due to the type of music that comes from there, which is you know, typical down south shit."<ref>[http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/06/the-come-up-jarren-benton/2/ The Come Up: Jarren Benton – Page 2 of 7 – XXL<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He credits his musical influences as [[OutKast]], [[Goodie Mob]], [[Dr. Dre]], [[Ludacris]], [[EL-P]], [[Busta Rhymes]], [[Sean Price]], [[Redman (rapper)|Redman]], [[Wu-Tang Clan]], [[Eminem]], [[Ras Kass]], [[Nine Inch Nails]], [[Chino XL]], [[Deftones]], and [[Marilyn Manson]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/06/the-come-up-jarren-benton/3/|title=The Come Up: Jarren Benton|work=XXL}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2013/06/14/source-exclusive-jarren-benton-speaks-on-his-funk-volume-family-redman-his-latest-album/|title=Source Exclusive: Jarren Benton Speaks On His Funk Volume Family, Redman & His Latest Album|work=thesource.com|date=June 14, 2013}}</ref><ref name="xxlmag1">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/08/jarren-benton-conquers-his-stage-fright/ |title=Jarren Benton Conquers His Stage Fright – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=August 15, 2013 |access-date=August 25, 2013}}</ref>

== Musical career ==

=== 2009–2012: Mixtapes and signing with Funk Volume ===
On August 5, 2009, Benton released his first official mixtape titled, ''Jarren Benton: The Mixtape'', produced entirely by The Beatgods.<ref name="djbooth">{{cite web|url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/news/entry/booth-favorite-jarren-benton-drops-self-titled-mixtape-download-0805093|title=Booth Favorite Jarren Benton Drops Self-Titled Mixtape &#91;Download&#93; &#124; DJBooth.net|publisher=djbooth.net|access-date=April 4, 2014}}</ref> On January 25, 2011, Benton released a collaboration mixtape with producer SMKA.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Jarren-Benton-Meets-Smka-Hasselhoff/dp/B004IXGQVW|title=Amazon.com: Jarren Benton Meets Smka: Huffing Glue With Hasselhoff [Explicit]: Jarren Benton: MP3 Downloads|work=amazon.com}}</ref> The mixtape titled ''Huffing Glue With Hasselhoff'' was supported by the lead single "Get a Load of Me".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/jarren-benton-huffing-glue-01261101#review|title=Jarren Benton Meets SMKA: Huffing Glue With Hasslehoff|work=DJBooth.net|access-date=August 14, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140105010810/http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/jarren-benton-huffing-glue-01261101#review|archive-date=January 5, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> He soon begun to gain attention after the release of his song "Skitzo".<ref name="xxlmag">{{cite web|title=The Come Up: Jarren Benton|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/06/the-come-up-jarren-benton/|work=XXL}}</ref> American underground rapper [[Hopsin]] would watch the music video, and passed it on to [[Funk Volume]] CEO Dame Ritter. Ritter would contact Benton a few months later and then in May 2012, Hopsin and Ritter signed Benton to their Funk Volume record label.

He had previously been courted by industry heavyweights such as [[Erick Sermon]] and [[No ID]] for major label record deals, with labels such as [[Def Jam Recordings]] and [[Interscope Records]], however he chose to sign with the independent record label Funk Volume.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/06/the-come-up-jarren-benton/6/|title=The Come Up: Jarren Benton|work=XXL}}</ref> His first project on Funk Volume, a mixtape titled ''Freebasing with Kevin Bacon'' would be released on June 28, 2012. The mixtape featured guest appearances from [[Dizzy Wright]], [[Rittz]], [[Freeway (rapper)|Freeway]], [[Gangsta Boo]], Jon Connor, and [[Planet VI]] among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/jarren-benton-freebasing-with-kevin-bacon|title=Jarren Benton – Freebasing With Kevin Bacon|work=DJBooth.net|access-date=August 14, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140105011137/http://www.djbooth.net/index/albums/review/jarren-benton-freebasing-with-kevin-bacon|archive-date=January 5, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== 2012–2015: ''My Grandma's Basement'' and ''Slow Motion'' ===
{{Main|My Grandma's Basement}}
In late 2012 he toured internationally with Hopsin, Dizzy Wright, [[SwizZz]] and [[DJ Hoppa]] for two-months, which included 54 shows in 60 days in the United States, Europe, and Australia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2012/08/hopsin-announces-funk-volume-2012-tour-with-dizzy-wright-swizzz-jarren-benton-video/ |title=Hopsin Announces Funk Volume 2012 Tour with Dizzy Wright, SwizZz & Jarren Benton [Video&#93; &#124; XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=August 16, 2012 |access-date=July 18, 2013}}</ref> He explained his mindset going into his debut album saying, "This album has a different vibe. It's a lot more personal stuff so people can learn who Jarren is." He also said it will feature a more refined production sound that translates more to his ambitions of being a household name.<ref name="myfunkvolume" /> On June 11, 2013, Benton would release his debut studio album, ''[[My Grandma's Basement]]'', on Funk Volume. The album featured guest appearances by Dizzy Wright, Hopsin, [[R.A. the Rugged Man]], [[Vinnie Paz]] and SwizZz.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/06/the-come-up-jarren-benton/7/|title=The Come Up: Jarren Benton|work=XXL}}</ref> ''My Grandma's Basement'' debuted and peaked at number 152 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and number four on the ''Billboard'' [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref name="BB200" /><ref name="Heatseeker">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/music/jarren-benton/chart-history/heatseekers-albums|title=Jarren Benton – Chart History: Heatseekers Albums|work=Billboard|access-date=January 5, 2020}}</ref> He released music videos for multiple songs including "Go Off", "Life In the Jungle" and "We On (My Own Dick)" among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/06/video-jarren-benton-f-hopsin-swizzz-go-off|title=Video: Jarren Benton f/ Hopsin & SwizZz "Go Off"|author=Tyler Keyes|work=Complex}}</ref>

On August 2, 2013, [[R.A. the Rugged Man]] released the remix to his song "Underground Hitz" featuring [[Hopsin]] and Benton.<ref name="UHR">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/underground-hits-remix-feat-hopsin-jarren-benton-single/685056290|title=Underground Hits (Remix) [feat. Hopsin, Jarren Benton] – Single by R.A. the Rugged Man|work=Apple Music|date=August 2, 2013|access-date=January 11, 2020}}</ref> From August 1–19, 2013, Benton toured the United States on the ''Grandma's Basement Tour'', his first solo headlining tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24432/title.jarren-benton-announces-grandma-s-basement-tour-dates|title=Jarren Benton Announces "Grandma's Basement Tour" Dates|author=HipHopDX|date=June 24, 2013|work=HipHopDX|access-date=August 14, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203084908/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24432/title.jarren-benton-announces-grandma-s-basement-tour-dates|archive-date=December 3, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> Then, Benton went on "The Life And Times Tour" with [[Strange Music]] rapper [[Rittz]] and [[Atlantic Records]] rapper [[Snow Tha Product]], the tour went from October 31 to December 8, 2013. Benton also featured on Hopsin's third studio album ''[[Knock Madness]]'', on the song "Who's There" with Funk Volume label mate Dizzy Wright. Benton received the most fan-votes votes, earning a spot on the 2014 ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' freshman class and was named the "People's Champ".<ref name="xxlmag2">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/05/xxl-freshmen-2014-cover-revealed/|title=XXL Freshmen 2014 Cover Revealed – XXL|publisher=xxlmag.com|access-date=July 14, 2014}}</ref> He was the oldest rapper that had ever been selected to the list at 32.<ref name="xxlmag3">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/05/xxl-freshmen-2014-numbers-chart/2/|title=XXL Freshmen Class: By The Numbers|publisher=xxlmag.com|access-date=July 14, 2014}}</ref> From April 9 to June 28, 2014, Benton was touring with [[Tech N9ne]], [[Freddie Gibbs]] and [[Krizz Kaliko]] on the Independent Grind Tour 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/01/tech-n9ne-freddie-gibbs-new-tour/ |title=Tech N9ne, Freddie Gibbs Are Hitting 70 Cities For New Tour – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |access-date=January 30, 2014}}</ref><ref name="xxlmag4">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/06/learned-jarren-bentons-ask-anything-session-reddit/2/|title=What We Learned: Jarren Benton&#039;s 'Ask Me Anything' Session On Reddit – Page 2 of 2 – XXL|publisher=xxlmag.com|access-date=July 14, 2014}}</ref> On January 27, 2015, Benton released his debut extended play, "Slow Motion" named after an old friend and fellow rapper who had recently died at the time. The EP debuted and peaked at number 168 on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart.<ref name="BB200" />

=== 2016–2017: ''Slow Motion, Vol. 2'' and ''The Mink Coat Killa'' ===
{{Main|The Mink Coat Killa}}
On July 22, 2016, Benton released his second studio album, and his first one under his newfound independent record label Benton Enterprises, ''Slow Motion, Vol. 2'', the sequel to his 2015 EP, ''Slow Motion''.<ref name="SMV2">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/slow-motion-vol-2/1130026517|title=Slow Motion, Vol. 2 by Jarren Benton|work=Apple Music|date=July 22, 2016|access-date=January 5, 2020}}</ref> On June 23, 2017, Benton released his third studio album titled, "[[The Mink Coat Killa]]", and supported by two singles from the album "C.R.E.A.M. 17" featuring Nick Grant being released on June 1, and "Again" featuring Aleon Craft being released on June 19, from the album. Initially, Benton stated that this project was intended to be a Wu-Tang Clan tribute project, the title of the project being a reference to his Wu-Tang name "The Mink Coat Killa."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wSAekaH5OM4&t=5s|title=Jarren Benton talks Mink Coat Killa LP, "Fuck" Atlanta, Post Funk Volume Life w/ Adrian Hernandez|publisher=The Beat 101.1|access-date=January 11, 2018}}</ref>

=== 2018–present: ''One Week Notice'' and ''Yuck Fou'' ===
On January 5, 2018, Benton, with the record label StillMovin, released a collaboration project titled, ''One Week Notice''. The album was created when StillMovin label owner Damian Ritter got Benton together with [[Dizzy Wright]], [[Audio Push]], [[Demrick]], [[Emilio Rojas]], Reezy, [[DJ Hoppa]], and [[Kato (producer)|Kato]] to record an album in one week. On June 29, 2018, Benton and [[Kato (producer)|Kato]] released the single "Don't Need You" featuring [[Hopsin]] and announced a collaborative album. On July 25, Benton and Kato released the single "Money Bag" featuring [[Jay Park]], and announced that their collaborative album, ''Yuck Fou'', would be released under [[Roc Nation]].<ref name="YF">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/yuck-fou/1411700030|title=Yuck Fou by Jarren Benton|work=Apple Music|date=July 27, 2018|access-date=January 5, 2020}}</ref>
