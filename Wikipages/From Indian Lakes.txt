'''From Indian Lakes''' is an American [[indie rock]] band formed in 2009. Vocalist Joey Vannucchi writes, performs, and produces the music. The band's sound changes between releases but generally incorporates elements of pop, acoustic, emo, shoegaze, and rock. 

In interviews, Vannucchi has [[Mew (band)|Mew]], [[Radiohead]], [[Feist (band)|Feist]], [[The Dodos]], and other indie musicians as influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uofsdmedia.com/interview-joey-vannucchi-of-from-indian-lakes/|title=Interview: Joey Vannucchi of From Indian Lakes|date=October 13, 2014|website=Uofsdmedia.com}}</ref> As a touring act the group consists of Vannucchi (vocals, guitar, keyboards), Justin Stanphill (guitar, vocals), Enrique Gutierrez (keyboards, samples), and Chris Kellogg (bass, vocals). Vannucchi began songwriting in a small mountain town near [[Yosemite National Park]] and later enlisted the help of friends to perform his material. From Indian Lakes have released two albums independently: ''The Man with Wooden Legs'' in late 2009 and ''[[Able Bodies (album)|Able Bodies]]'' in 2012. Their third album, ''[[Absent Sounds]]'', was released on October 7, 2014 on [[Triple Crown Records]]<ref name="triplecrownrecords.com">{{cite web|url=http://triplecrownrecords.com/artists/from-indian-lakes/|title=From Indian Lakes|website=Triplecrownrecords.com|accessdate=July 25, 2015}}</ref> Their fourth album, "Everything Feels Better Now", was released on October 14, 2016 via Triple Crown Records.

== History ==
Joey Vannucchi grew up on forty acres of land near [[Yosemite National Park]]. Vannucchi spent most of his youth reading books, playing drums, learning new instruments, and exploring the land around his rural home.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teamrock.com/feature/2015-05-18/new-blood-from-indian-lakes|title=New Blood: From Indian Lakes|date=May 18, 2015|website=Teamrock.com}}</ref> He received jazz drum lessons and played on a borrowed church drumset for hours daily. Vannucchi said: "I could play my drums for hours. There was no one to bother. No one around cared about the sound."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.recordnet.com/article/20120621/A_ENTERTAIN/206210302?template=printart|title=Natural rock formation|website=Recordnet.com|accessdate=July 25, 2015}}</ref>

Throughout high school and into adulthood, Vannucchi participated in various bands. At age 20, he started writing and recording songs at a friend's studio in [[Indian Lakes Estates, California]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.masqueradeatlanta.com/artist/from-indian-lakes|title=The Masquerade  - artist  - from-indian-lakes|website=Masqueradeatlanta.com|accessdate=July 25, 2015}}</ref> which he released to [[Myspace]] as "Songs From Indian Lakes". This reference was later shortened to the current band name.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://intothecrowdmagazine.com/2014/01/09/interview-from-indian-lakes/|title=INTERVIEW: From Indian Lakes|website=Intothecrowdmagazine.com|date=January 9, 2014}}</ref>

After enlisting friends to perform his material, From Indian Lakes began playing local shows and independently released a full-length record, ''The Man With Wooden Legs'', in late 2009.<ref name="triplecrownrecords.com"/>

From Indian Lakes recorded and released the ''Acoustic EP'' in 2011 and performed solo on a month-long living room tour across the US.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fromindianlakes.bandcamp.com/album/acoustic-ep|title=Acoustic EP - From Indian Lakes|date=October 25, 2011|website=Fromindianlakes.bandcamp.com|accessdate=July 25, 2015}}</ref>

On November 2012, From Indian Lakes released its second album, ''[[Able Bodies (album)|Able Bodies]]'', which was well-received by both fans and press.<ref name="triplecrownrecords.com"/> ''Able Bodies'' was also selected to be on Google Play's Top Unsigned List for 2012 releases and remained on the cover of iTunes as a "rising star of alt-rock" for three weeks.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.behance.net/gallery/6867217/From-Indian-Lakes-Album-Release|title=Behance|website=Behance.net|accessdate=July 25, 2015}}</ref>

From Indian Lakes signed to [[Triple Crown Records]] in 2013 and released their third album, ''[[Absent Sounds]]'' on October 7, 2014<ref name="triplecrownrecords.com"/> and it entered the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], reached number 8 on the [[Top Heatseekers]] chart, and hit number 36 on the [[Independent Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/search/albums/absent+sounds|title=Album Search for "Absent Sounds"|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author= |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/6282015/from-indian-lakes |title=From Indian Lakes |website=Billboard.com |date= |accessdate=2020-03-17}}</ref>

From Indian Lakes posted a link to their official website and a new EP, ''Wanderer''. It features three new songs and two acoustic renditions of previous songs written and recorded by Vannucchi at his home studio in Northern California. It was produced and engineered by long-time friend Matthew Bedrosian who helped with "The Acoustic EP" in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fromindianlakes.com|title=home|website=Fromindianlakes.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://fromindianlakes.tumblr.com/post/137831090356/one-of-the-first-tours-i-did-years-ago-was-a|title=One of the first tours I did years ago was a... -|website=Fromindianlakes.tumblr.com}}</ref>

On July 23, 2016, a documentary, ''To and from Indian Lakes'', filmed by longtime friend, Guy Samuelson, was released. It was filmed over several months of touring and spending time in Vannucchi's and Samuelson's childhood areas in and around Indian Lakes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/fromindianlakes/photos/a.111352362250738.8401.100856976633610/1202179233168040/?type=3&theater|title=From Indian Lakes - Timeline |website=Facebook.com}}</ref>

On August 18, 2016, [[NPR]] featured a new song, "The Monster", off ''Everything Feels Better Now'', scheduled to be released October 14, 2016 on [[Triple Crown Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundfiction.net/2016/08/08/indian-lakes-announces-everything-feels-better-now-tour-dates|title=From Indian Lakes Announces 'Everything Feels Better Now' + Tour Dates|website=Soundfiction.net|date=August 8, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/allsongs/2016/08/18/490357689/vikings-choice-from-indian-lakes-the-monster|title=Hear From Indian Lakes' Dreamy 'The Monster'|website=Npr.org}}</ref>

On October 14, 2016 "Everything Feels Better Now", a fourth full-length album, was released on Triple Crown Records. The album was produced by Kevin Augunas, engineered/mixed by Gavin Paddock, and mastered by Mikchael Fassbender.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/everything-feels-better-now-mw0002977417 |title=Everything Feels Better Now - From Indian Lakes &#124; Songs, Reviews, Credits |website=[[AllMusic]] |date=2016-10-14 |accessdate=2020-03-17}}</ref> EFBN was recorded partly in a coffee shop basement near Vannucchi's brief time home in Chico, CA and finished over several weeks at Fairfax Recordings in the old [[Sound City Studios]] space.<ref name="consequenceofsound.net">{{cite web|last=Geslani |first=Michelle |url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2016/10/stream-from-indian-lakes-new-album-everything-feels-better-now/ |title=Stream: From Indian Lakes' new album Everything Feels Better Now |publisher=[[Consequence of Sound]] |date=2016-10-13 |accessdate=2020-03-17}}</ref> It was met with praise by fans and critics and featured in [[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]],<ref>{{cite web|author=Ian Cohen |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/22377-everything-feels-better-now/ |title=From Indian Lakes: Everything Feels Better Now Album Review |website=Pitchfork.com |date=2016-10-24 |accessdate=2020-03-17}}</ref> [[Consequence of Sound]],<ref name="consequenceofsound.net"/> [[Stereogum]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1898889/from-indian-lakes-the-monster-video/video/ |title=From Indian Lakes – "The Monster" Video |website=Stereogum.com |date=2016-09-14 |accessdate=2020-03-17}}</ref> as well as charting at 105 in the top 200 on [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] and debuting at number five on the [[Top Heatseekers|heat seekers chart]].<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7549138/from-indian-lakes-premiere-american-dreams-video |title=From Indian Lakes Takes 'American Dreams' Video to the Desert: Exclusive Premiere |website=Billboard.com |date=2016-10-20 |accessdate=2020-03-17}}</ref>

From Indian Lakes digitally released "EFBN Tapes" on December 5, 2016. The release features demos, alternative versions, and initial interpretations of the songs from "Everything Feels Better Now". It was previously released only on cassette with deluxe pre-orders, but Vannucchi decided to put the songs online stating "These were meant to be released as a deluxe digital add on to the record at a time when new promotion was appropriate, but it continues to feel necessary to put into action words of love and positivity." All proceeds are donated were donated to the [[Southern Poverty Law Center]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/fromindianlakes/posts/1320435998009029 |title=EFBN TAPES available on Bandcamp now - From Indian Lakes |website=Facebook.com |date= |accessdate=2020-03-17}}</ref>
