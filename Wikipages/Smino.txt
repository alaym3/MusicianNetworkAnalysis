'''Christopher Smith Jr.''' (born October 2, 1991), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Smino''', is an British, American and Australian rapper, [[Angry Birds]] fan and singer from [[St. Louis, Missouri]], [[London, England]] and [[Sydney, Australia]]. Smino is the founder of the musical collective [[Zero Fatigue]] with Bari, [[Monte Booker]], Jay2, and [[Ravyn Lenae]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Chicago's Very Own: Zero Fatigue |url=https://medium.com/fwrd/chicagos-very-own-zero-fatigue-23b8213f1e74 |website=Medium.com|date=9 October 2017 }}</ref> He is a member of the hip-hop supergroup Zoink Gang, with [[JID]], [[Buddy (rapper)|Buddy]] and [[Guapdad 4000]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Duck |first1=Steve |title=Interview: Keanu Beats is the Producer Reppin for Australia on 'Revenge Of The Dreamers III' |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/07/keanu-beats-australian-producer-dreamville-jcole-migos/ |website=Complex.com |access-date=26 July 2019}}</ref> He is also one third of the supergroup Ghetto Sage, with [[Saba (rapper)|Saba]] and [[Noname (rapper)|Noname]].<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Renshaw |first1=David |title=Noname, Saba, and Smino form supergroup Ghetto Sage |url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/10/15/ghetto-sage-saba-noname-smino |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref> Smino is signed to [[Downtown Records]] and [[Interscope Records|Interscope]].

Smino initially gained local recognition in 2012 where he released a mixtape called ''Smeezy Dot Com'', and a collaboration mixtape with Bari called ''Retail''. In 2015, he released two EPs: ''S!Ck S!Ck S!Ck'' and ''[[blkjuptr]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Smino News|url=http://djbooth.net/mobile/artist/chris-smith-jr|website=Djbooth.net|access-date=2020-03-31}}</ref> His debut studio album, ''[[blkswn]]'' was released to critical acclaim on March 14, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last=Tanzer|first=Myles|title=Smino Announces Debut Album 'blkswn'|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/03/08/smino-blkswn-announce-album/amp|website=Thefader.com|date=March 8, 2017}}</ref> His second studio album, ''[[Noir (Smino album)|NØIR]]'', was released on November 8, 2018.<ref>{{Citation|title=NOIR by Smino|date=8 November 2018|url=https://itunes.apple.com/nz/album/noir/1441333559|language=en-au|access-date=2019-04-02}}</ref>

==Early life==
Chris Smith Jr. was born in [[St. Louis, Missouri]] into a musical family: his father plays keys, his mother sings, and his grandfather played bass guitar for [[Blues Hall of Fame]] musician [[Muddy Waters]]. Both his father and grandfather were given opportunities to take their music far, but they gave up their success to be with their families. When he was seven, his father gave him a set of drums, which he played in his church's band, and also began rapping. He later moved to [[Chicago, Illinois]] for college under the wing of his older cousin, singer Drea Smith.<ref name="VICE">{{cite web|last=Mahadevan|first=Tara|title=Smino is making a midwest musical moment|url=http://m.noisey.vice.com/en_ca/blog/smino-3m-premiere-interview-profile|website=M.noisey.vice.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Burney|first=Lawrence|title=Smino Isn't Singing to Himself Anymore|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/smino-isnt-singing-to-himself-anymore|website=Noisey.vice.com|date=May 13, 2017}}</ref>

==Musical career==
===2009–2015: Career beginnings and various projects===
Between 2009 and 2013, Smino went by the stage name C. Smi, and was in a rap duo with his friend Bari Allen called "Young Dumb and Outta Control" (YDOC), where they released three mixtapes including ''Retail''. As they started to gain popularity locally, Smino released his first solo mixtape, ''Smeezy Dot Com'' in 2012 and moved back to Chicago.<ref name="VICE"/> At [[Columbia College Chicago]], he met engineer Chris "Classick" Inumerable, who became his manager.<ref>{{cite web|title=Meet Smino, the rapper about to put St. Louis back on the map|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/2015/12/smino-interview-2015/|website=Pigeons and Planes}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Frazer|first=Wes|title=Meet Smino, The Deeply Loyal Rapper Putting His People First|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/05/04/smino-blkswn-interview-ferguson-gen-f|website=Thefader.com|access-date=May 5, 2017}}</ref>

On October 2, 2015 Smino released his first EP, ''S!Ck S!Ck S!Ck''.<ref name="p4k-Zero">{{cite web|last=Drake|first=David|title=Zero Fatigue: Chicago's Next Hip-Hop Visionaries|url=http://pitchfork.com/features/rising/9802-zero-fatigue-chicagos-next-hip-hop-visionaries/|website=Pitchfork.com|access-date=2020-03-31}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Moore|first=Jacob|title=Listen to Smino's 'S!CK S!CK S!CK' EP|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/2015/10/smino-sick-sick-sick/|website=Pigeons And Planes}}</ref> On December 11, 2015, he released his second EP titled ''[[Blkjuptr]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Smino Releases Debut Video "Blkjuptr"|url=http://hiphopdx.com/videos/id.23422/title.smino-brown-releases-debut-video-blkjuptr|website=HipHopDX.com|date=2 June 2016|access-date=2020-03-31}}</ref>

===2016–2017: blkswn===
[[File:Smino.jpg|thumb|Smino at Lollapalooza in 2016]]
{{Main|blkswn}}
On July 31, 2016, Smino appeared on [[Noname (rapper)|Noname]]'s mixtape, ''[[Telefone (mixtape)|Telefone]]'', with [[Saba (rapper)|Saba]] and Phoelix on the track "Shadow Man".<ref>{{cite web|last=Daramola|first=Israel|date=August 8, 2016|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/22218-telefone/|title=Noname: Telefone Album Review|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|access-date=August 9, 2016}}</ref> In the fall of 2016, Smino was alongside [[Mick Jenkins (rapper)|Mick Jenkins]] on the A Quest For Love Tour. On October 27, he made another guest appearance on ''[[Bucket List Project]]'' on the song "World in My Hands".<ref>{{cite web|last=Mench|first=Chris|title=Stream Saba's New Project 'Bucket List'|url=http://ca.complex.com/music/2016/10/saba-new-mixtape-stream-bucket-list|website=Complex.com|date=October 27, 2016}}</ref>

On December 2, 2016, Smino released the title track to serve as the first single from his debut album, ''[[blkswn]]'', which was released on March 14, 2017. The release date holds a special significance to the St. Louis rapper because 314 is the area code of his hometown. The album includes guest appearances from [[Ravyn Lenae]], Bari, Noname, theMIND, Jay2, Via Rosa, Drea Smith, and Akenya, with production coming from [[Monte Booker]], THEMpeople, [[Sango (musician)|Sango]], Phoelix, and J. Bird.<ref>{{cite web|last=Pickens|first=Ashley|title=Smino Wants To Reflect A New Light On St. Louis With Debut Album 'blkswn'|url=http://www.vibe.com/2017/03/smino-blkswn-album-announcement/|website=Vibe.com|date=March 8, 2017}}</ref>

Following the album's release, he headlined in his month-long Swanita Tour, which included 22 dates with guests Monte Booker, Jay2, Bari, and Jean Deaux.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ketchum III|first=William E.|title='I Know Exactly What I Want': St. Louis Rapper Smino on New Album 'blkswn' and Focusing on What Comes Next|url=http://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/columns/hip-hop/7793206/smino-interview-blkswn-new-album|website=Billboard.com|date=16 May 2017|access-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref> On July 6, it was announced that Smino and Ravyn Lenae will be opening for [[SZA (singer)|SZA]] on The CTRL Tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7857764/sza-ctrl-tour-dates|title=SZA Announces 'CTRL' Tour|date=July 6, 2017|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> He later released music videos for "Anita", "Netflix & Dusse", and "Wild Irish Roses". During October, he was opening act for [[T-Pain]] on his Acoustic Tour, who appeared on the remix for "Anita".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7940883/t-pain-acoustic-tour-dates|title=T-Pain Announces Acoustic Tour|date=August 22, 2017|website=Billboard.com}}</ref><ref>[https://www.hotnewhiaphop.com/smino-lands-t-pain-for-the-anita-remix-new-song.1976074.html] {{dead link|date=March 2020}}</ref>

==Artistry==
===Influence===
Smino describes his sound as futuristic funk and soulful rap. He grew up listening to a lot of jazz, gospel, and hip hop, with rappers including [[Busta Rhymes]], [[Ludacris]], [[Nelly]], [[Bone Thugs-n-Harmony]] and [[Field Mob]]. Smino names [[Kanye West]], [[André 3000]] and [[Lil Wayne]] as his biggest musical influences, as well as his cousin, Drea Smith.<ref>{{cite web|last=C. M.|first=Emmanuel|title=The Break Presents: Smino|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2016/04/smino-interview-the-break|website=XXL Magazine}}</ref> In an interview with ''Pitchfork'' he stated, "I grew up in the church, so it was a bunch of gospel along with Musiq Soulchild, Em, T-Pain—just a bunch of soulful dudes that are country. My first favorite rapper was Ludacris."<ref name="p4k-Zero"/>

===Musical style===
Scott Glaysher of ''[[Exclaim!]]'' described his music is a "mix of smart rapping and crowd-pleasing, futuristic R&B grooves."<ref>{{cite web|last=Glaysher|first=Scott|title=Smino - Blkswn|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/smino-blkswn|website=Exclaim.ca|date=March 20, 2017}}</ref> Jay Balfour from ''[[Pitchfork (magazine)|Pitchfork]]'' said he focuses "on his incredibly versatile voice within a warm palette of sludgy R&B and neo-funk" and "throws a million different voices into the mix."<ref>{{cite web|last=Balfour|first=Jay|title=Smino - NOIR|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/smino-noir/|website=Pitchfork.com|date=November 12, 2018}}</ref> ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' said "his rap makes room for southern R&B and imperfect harmony singing; the drums are inevitably cooled out, like nth-wave neo soul."<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Leight |first1=Elias |title=By the Time Smino Releases an Album, He Already Hates It |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/smino-new-album-noir-752507/ |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] |access-date=3 December 2018}}</ref>

==Other ventures==
===Zero Fatigue===
Smino is a founder and part of a musical collective and record label called [[Zero Fatigue]] with Monte Booker, Jay2, Bari, [[Ravyn Lenae]], Elton "L10MixedIt" Chueng, Chris "Classick" Innumerable, and Nosidam. The collective's origins began when Smino met Classick at [[Columbia College Chicago]], who owned a recording studio in his house in Chicago and later became his manager. Interning for Classick, engineer and mixer L10MixedIt Chueng met Smino in that studio where they began working together.<ref>{{cite web |title=Meet L10MixedIt, the Engineer Behind Chance The Rapper, Noname & Smino's Breakthrough Projects |url=https://djbooth.net/features/2017-04-06-l10mixedit-interview |website=Djbooth.net |access-date=29 December 2018}}</ref> Unofficial members and affiliates of the collective include Jean Deaux, Drea Smith, Phoelix, & JayBaby TheGreaty.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Niespodziany |first1=Ben |title=Home Team • Jean Deaux |url=https://www.thesedays.news/features/2015/12/3/home-team-jean-deaux |website=Thesedays.news |access-date=29 December 2018}}</ref>

===Kribmas===
Kribmas is an annual Christmas concert and fundraising event hosted by Smino in his hometown of [[St. Louis, Missouri]], which began in 2016. Ymani Wince wrote about the event saying, "Kribmas is more than just Smino's homecoming weekend; the rapper's team employs local photographers, videographers, stylists, businesses and artists to put each element of the show together."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wince |first1=Ymani |title=Smino Celebrates St. Louis at Kribmas |url=https://www.riverfronttimes.com/stlouis/smino-spreads-st-louis-love-at-kribmas/Content?oid=32759614 |website=River Front Times}}</ref>
