'''Passion''' was a short-lived [[disco]] and [[boogie (genre)|post-disco]] music band featuring convicted cult leader [[Dwight York]]. Their style mixed [[funk]] and [[Latin American music|Latin]] rhythms combined with sensual lyrics. An eponymous debut album was released in 1979. They continued publishing music until 1984.<ref name=OMG>[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/passion-p368747 (((Passion)))] - Artists - Biography, Discography. ''Rovi Corporation'', '''Allmusic'''. Retrieved 2012-23-04</ref><ref>[http://www.allmusic.com/album/dont-stop-my-love-r298704/charts-awards (((Don't Stop My Love)))] - Album - Charts, Credits. ''Rovi Corporation'', '''Allmusic'''. Retrieved 2012-23-04</ref>

Their first single "Don't Bring Back Memories" gained substantial club play when it was released in 1980. The song, written by multi-instrumentalist [[Ray Martínez|Ray Martinez]] and remixed by [[François Kevorkian]], featured background vocals of Dara Norman, David Romero and Martha Roque. The B-side was "In New York", over eleven minutes in length.

In 1982, they released their "biggest"<ref name=OMG/> dance hit "Don't Stop My Love", a song written by [[Kashif (musician)|Kashif Saleem]]. The band followed up with "You Can't Hide It", a song recorded under the creative control of [[Eric Matthew]] in 1984.

Dwight York afterwards released two albums titled ''New'' and ''Re-New''  under his own label York's Records. The albums contained songs  like "Life Is But a Dream" and "I Hurt". He was later imprisoned for multiple  crimes including child sexual abuse.<ref>Billboard (r): Dr. York. ''Billboard/Nielsen Business Media, Inc.'' '''97:''' 41. 4 May 1985.
