'''Jeremy Phillip Felton''' (born July 17, 1987),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lyrics.com/lyric/22018909/Jeremih/Wanna+Get+Up|title=Wanna Get Up Lyrics|website=Lyrics.com|access-date=April 13, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/jeremih-mn0001545319|title=Jeremih &#124; Biography, Albums, Streaming Links|website=AllMusic}}</ref> known professionally as '''Jeremih''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|dʒ|ɛr|ə|m|aɪ}} {{respell|JERR|əm|eye}}),<ref>{{cite news |title=Jeremih: Pronounced Je Ruh My |url=https://allhiphop.com/features/jeremih-pronounced-je-ruh-my-fzbIbcZ5t0GwQfEMd9_sJA/ |access-date=December 19, 2018 |publisher=All Hip Hop |date=December 4, 2010}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cnn.com/2014/12/15/travel/feat-singer-jeremih-arrested-newark-airport/index.html|title=R&B singer Jeremih arrested in plane incident - CNN.com|first=Katia |last=Hetter|date=December 15, 2014|publisher=CNN}}</ref> In 2009, he signed a record deal with [[Def Jam Recordings]]. Jeremih's commercial debut single, "[[Birthday Sex]]", peaked at number four on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart—leading his [[Jeremih (album)|self-titled debut album]] released in June that year, reaching number six on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. Jeremih's success continued with the release of his second album, ''[[All About You (album)|All About You]]'', led by the single "[[Down on Me (Jeremih song)|Down on Me]]" (featuring [[50 Cent]]), which also reached the top five of the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. In 2014, his single "[[Don't Tell 'Em]]" (featuring [[YG (rapper)|YG]]) became his third top-ten hit on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. The song became the lead single for his third studio album, ''[[Late Nights]]'' released in December 2015. A collaborative album between Jeremih and California singer [[Ty Dolla Sign]], titled ''[[MihTy]]'', was released on August 24, 2018.

==Early life==
Born in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]], Jeremih began playing drums at the age of three and is able to play the saxophone, several percussion instruments and piano/keyboards.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1615523/20090708/jeremih.jhtml|title=Jeremih Talks About 'Nervous' Def Jam Audition|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|date=July 8, 2009|work=[[MTV News]]|access-date=July 15, 2009}}</ref> He attended [[Morgan Park High School]], where he was part of the [[marching band]] as well as a [[Latin jazz]] band. He also learned to play percussion instruments like [[conga]]s and [[timbales]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bluesandsoul.com/feature/444/\jeremih_perfect_presence|title=Perfect Presence - Pete Lewis Interviews Jeremih|last=Lewis|first=Pete|work=[[Blues & Soul]]|access-date=September 9, 2009}}</ref> Felton was a good student and earned academic honors at Morgan Park High School. His strong academic performance enabled him to graduate from high school a year early.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.lastchanceharvey.com/everything-need-know-jeremih/ | title=Everything You Need To Know About Jeremih | publisher=Last Chance Harvey | access-date=April 14, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2741361/jeremih-the-black-knight/ | title=Jeremih: The Black Knight | date=February 6, 2016 | access-date=April 14, 2016 | author=Meaghan Garvey| website=[[MTV]] }}</ref> He enrolled at the [[University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign|University of Illinois]] and [[Parkland College]] in Urbana-Champaign.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.today.com/id/31925623#.VxAfDkfQ46M | title=Jeremih wants Diddy-style success | publisher=Today Music | access-date=April 14, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://amp.cbslocal.com/2011/04/19/audio-carson-daly-down-on-me-singer-jeremih-reps-his-south-side-chicago-pride-on-the-amp-radio-morning-show/ | title=Carson Daly: "Down On Me" Singer Jeremih Reps His South Side Chicago Pride On The AMP Radio Morning Show | publisher=Amp | access-date=April 14, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://newsok.com/article/3385734 | title=Soul singer Jeremih sees No. 1 success with first song | publisher=NewsOk | date=July 17, 2009 | access-date=April 14, 2016}}</ref> After spending a semester at the university studying engineering, he wanted to change his education to something related to music. He transferred to [[Columbia College Chicago]] in 2007 to pursue a degree in music business.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://djbooth.net/interviews/entry/jeremih-interview-0623091 | title=Jeremih Interview | publisher=DJBooth | access-date=April 14, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Sun-Times">{{cite web|url=http://www.suntimes.com/entertainment/music/hiphop/1644525,CST-FTR-jeremih30.article|title=Engineering a career with a 'Birthday' hit|last=Houlihan|first=Mary|date=June 30, 2009|work=Chicago Sun-Times|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090703111528/http://www.suntimes.com/entertainment/music/hiphop/1644525,CST-FTR-jeremih30.article|archive-date=July 3, 2009|access-date=June 30, 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref> After performing a tribute to [[Ray Charles]] at one of the campus's talent shows, he received positive feedback and realized his vocal ability, saying that the "music was just taking over".<ref name="Sun-Times"/>

==Musical style==
Jeremih cited [[Michael Jackson]], [[Stevie Wonder]] and [[R. Kelly]] as his biggest influences.<ref name="Dallas News">{{cite web|url=http://www.dallasnews.com/sharedcontent/dws/ent/stories/DN-jeremih_0822gd.ART.State.Edition1.4bdc20b.html|title=Jeremih performs in Dallas riding the wave of his big hit, 'Birthday Sex'|last=Burkes|first=Dawn M.|date=August 22, 2009|work=[[The Dallas Morning News]]|publisher=[[A. H. Belo]]|access-date=August 25, 2009}}</ref> He described their music as "timeless", calling them "true musician-artists".<ref name="Dallas News"/> With the release of his debut album, Andy Kellman of [[Allmusic]] compared Jeremih's vocals to [[Slim (112)|Slim]] of [[112 (band)|112]] and [[Raphael Saadiq]], noting that his "charmingly sly voice...is hard to not like".<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1611544|pure_url=yes}}|title=Jeremih - Overview|work=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=August 25, 2009}}</ref> In contrast, Ken Capobianco of ''[[The Boston Globe]]'' felt that Jeremih's voice was "thin", showing "little range".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.boston.com/ae/music/cd_reviews/articles/2009/06/29/jeremih_jeremih/|title=Jeremih, 'Jeremih'|last=Capobianco|first=Ken|date=June 29, 2009|work=[[The Boston Globe]]|publisher=[[The New York Times Company]]|access-date=August 25, 2009}}</ref> Jody Rosen expressed that Jeremih "has a way with tunes and hooks".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/artists/jeremih/albums/album/28847806/review/29237139/jeremih|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100221071145/http://www.rollingstone.com/artists/jeremih/albums/album/28847806/review/29237139/jeremih|url-status=dead|archive-date=February 21, 2010|title=Jeremih: Jeremih: Music Reviews|last=Rosen|first=Jody|date=July 20, 2009|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|publisher=[[Jann Wenner|Wenner Media]]|access-date=August 25, 2009}}</ref> He received comparisons to [[The-Dream]], R. Kelly and Stevie Wonder.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.straight.com/article-246917/songwriting-puts-jeremih-fast-track|title=Songwriting puts Jeremih on the fast track|last=Turenne|first=Martin|date=August 13, 2009|work=[[The Georgia Straight]]|publisher=Vancouver Free Press|access-date=August 25, 2009}}</ref>

==Health==
In November 2020, Jeremih was hospitalized in [[Chicago]] after testing positive for [[COVID-19]]. By November 14, he was reported to be on a ventilator in the intensive care unit.<ref>[https://www.tmz.com/2020/11/14/singer-jeremih-hospitalized-icu-covid-50-cent/ Jeremih hospitalized in the ICU with COVID]</ref> He was taken off the ventilator on November 19 but remained in critical condition.<ref>[https://variety.com/2020/music/news/jeremih-coronavirus-critical-condition-icu-1234835977/ Jeremih in 'Critical Condition' in the ICU, Agent Says]</ref> His spokesman said Jeremih has no underlying conditions.<ref>[https://deadline.com/2020/11/jeremih-has-severe-case-of-covid-19-on-ventilator-in-icu-1234619382/ R&B Artist Jeremih Has "Severe Case" Of COVID-19 And Is On A Ventilator In ICU]</ref> Jeremih was transferred out of intensive care on November 21, into a general hospital to make a full recovery.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jeremih Transferred Out of ICU After COVID Scare, On Road to Recovery|url=https://www.tmz.com/2020/11/21/jeremih-transferred-out-icu-covid-road-to-recovery/|access-date=November 21, 2020|website=TMZ|language=en}}</ref> He was released from the hospital on December 4.<ref>[https://abc7chicago.com/jeremih-singer-covid-update/8498879/ Jeremih update: Chicago R&B singer released from hospital following COVID-19 battle]</ref>
