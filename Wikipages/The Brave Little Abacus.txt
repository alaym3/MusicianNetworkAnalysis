'''The Brave Little Abacus''' was an American [[emo]] band formed in [[Sandown, New Hampshire]] in 2007. The band consisted of vocalist, guitarist, and drummer Adam Demirjian, bassist Andrew Ryan, and keyboardist Zach Kelly Onett, but were later joined by Nick Morrone on drums in 2011. Before they had a live drummer they performed with [[Backing track|backing tracks]], that were often played through a [[Dreamcast|Sega Dreamcast]].

==Career==
The Brave Little Abacus first released a demo in 2008 titled ''Demo?''.<ref name="sputnik1">{{cite web|title=The Brave Little Abacus: Demo?|url=http://www.sputnikmusic.com/soundoff.php?albumid=170687|publisher=Sputnikmusic|accessdate=22 March 2017}}</ref> That same year, they released a split with fellow New Hampshire musician Matt Aspinwall.<ref name="sputnikmusic">{{cite web|title=Matt Aspinwall/The Brave Little Abacus|url=http://www.sputnikmusic.com/soundoff.php?albumid=170890|publisher=[[Sputnikmusic]]|accessdate=21 March 2017}}</ref> In 2009, the band self-released their first album, titled ''Masked Dancers: Concern In So Many Things You Forget Where You Are'' (often shortened to ''Masked Dancers''.) <ref name="sputnik2">{{cite web|title=The Brave Little Abacus: Masked Dancers|url=http://www.sputnikmusic.com/soundoff.php?albumid=99156|publisher=Sputnikmusic|accessdate=22 March 2017}}</ref> In 2010, the band self-released their final album, titled ''Just Got Back From the Discomfort—We're Alright''. (often shortened to ''Just Got Back,'' or simply ''Discomfort)''<ref name="xfdrmag">{{cite web|last1=Seraydarian|first1=Thomas|title=Crossfader’s Emo Primer|url=http://www.xfdrmag.net/crossfaders-emo-primer/|work=XFDR Magazine|accessdate=22 March 2017}}</ref><ref name="sputnik3">{{cite web|title=The Brave Little Abacus: Just Got Back from the Discomfort|url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/57490/The-Brave-Little-Abacus-Just-Got-Back-from-the-Discomfort/|publisher=Sputnikmusic|accessdate=22 March 2017}}</ref> The album was listed at number 27 on [[Spin (magazine)|Spin Magazine's]] list of the "30 Best [[Emo revival|Emo Revival]] Albums, Ranked".<ref name="spinmagazine">{{cite web|title=30 Best Emo Revival Albums, Ranked|url=https://www.spin.com/featured/best-emo-revival-albums-ranked/|publisher=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|accessdate=6 October 2017}}</ref> The Brave Little Abacus's final release, an EP titled ''Okumay'', was released in 2012 on [[Quote Unquote Records]].<ref name="ocweekly">{{cite web|last1=Chemotti|first1=Lucas|title=Top 10 Emo Revival Bands You'll Never Hear From Again|url=http://www.ocweekly.com/music/top-10-emo-revival-bands-youll-never-hear-from-again-6570437|publisher=[[OC Weekly]]|accessdate=22 March 2017}}</ref> They played their last show at the Vic Geary Center in Plaistow, New Hampshire on January 9th, 2012. Demirjian is currently a member of Boston-based [[power pop]] band Me in Capris. In recent years, many publications and musicians have cited the Brave Little Abacus as an under-appreciated band.<ref name="Pitchfork">{{cite web|last1=Cohen|first1=Ian|title=(파란노을) Parannoul: To See the Next Part of the Dream|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/parannoul-to-see-the-next-part-of-the-dream/|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|accessdate=15 May 2021}}</ref> Demirjian has discussed in interviews that there have been talks between him, Onett, and Ryan since the band's dissolution in 2012 of forming a new band similar in sound to The Brave Little Abacus under the name ''Okumay,'' named after the title of their final EP. {{Citation needed|date=August 2021}}

== Musical style and influences ==
{{Unreferenced section|date=July 2021}}
The Brave Little Abacus have been described as [[emo]], [[art rock]], [[math rock]], [[experimental rock]], [[synth-pop]], [[progressive rock]], and [[post-hardcore]].

Onett used an [[Alesis Ion#Alesis%20Micron|Alesis Micron]] for the band's entire run.

Demirjian has cited [[The Flaming Lips]], [[They Might Be Giants]], [[Liz Phair]], [[Deftones]], [[R.E.M.]], and [[Cursive (band)|Cursive]] as influences. On ''Just Got Back From the Discomfort—We're Alright'', Demirjian makes direct references to Liz Phair, and has said that Deftones inspired him to play [[seven-string guitar]] on ''Masked Dancers.''
