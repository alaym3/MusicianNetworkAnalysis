'''Richard Siken''' (born February 15, 1967) is an American [[poet]], painter, and filmmaker. He is the author of the collection ''Crush'' ([[Yale University Press]], 2005), which won the [[Yale Series of Younger Poets Competition]] in 2004.<ref name="pf" /> His second book of poems, ''War of the Foxes'', was published by [[Copper Canyon Press]] in 2015.

==Early life and education ==
Siken was born in [[New York City]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Richard Siken - Western Illinois University|url=http://www.wiu.edu/cas/english/case/biographies/siken.php|website=www.wiu.edu|access-date=2020-05-05}}</ref> He studied at and received a B.A. in psychology and later a [[Master of Fine Arts]] in poetry from the University of Arizona.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.poetryfoundation.org/features/articles/detail/68487|title=Nerve-Wracked Love by Nell Casey|date=2016-11-16|website=Poetry Foundation|language=en-us|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2001, Siken co-founded [[Spork Press]], where he continues to work as an editor.

Siken received a Literature Fellowship in Poetry from the [[National Endowment for the Arts]], and his book ''Crush'' was awarded the [[Lambda Literary Award]] for "Gay Men's Poetry" in 2005,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lambdaliterary.org/awards/previous_winners/paw_2004_2006.html|accessdate=2008-08-06|title=Previous Lammy Award Winners|publisher=Lambda Literary Foundation |archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080504161813/http://www.lambdaliterary.org/awards/previous_winners/paw_2004_2006.html <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archivedate = 2008-05-04}}</ref> and the [[Thom Gunn Award]] from [[Publishing Triangle]]. The 1991 death of his boyfriend influenced his writing of the book.<ref name="tucson">{{Cite news |date=2006-04-13 |title=Poetry Personas: Three well-known writers reflect on new, contemporary work |first=Joan |last=Schuman |periodical=[[Tucson Weekly]] }}</ref>

Siken's book ''War of the Foxes'' became a recipient of two residencies with the Lannan Residency Program, and a Lannan Literary Selection.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lannan.org/programs/literary/|title=Lannan Literary Program - Lannan Foundation|last=Robinson|first=Shane|website=www.lannan.org|access-date=2016-11-18}}</ref>

Siken currently lives in [[Tucson, Arizona]]. On March 19, 2019, Siken reported on his Facebook that he had recently suffered a stroke.
