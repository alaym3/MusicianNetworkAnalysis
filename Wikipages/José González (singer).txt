'''José Gabriel González''' (born 31 July 1978) is a Swedish [[indie folk]] singer-songwriter and guitarist from [[Gothenburg]].<ref>{{cite web|title=José González on ''Mountain Stage''|url=https://www.npr.org/event/music/402774459/jos-gonz-lez-on-mountain-stage|website=[[NPR]]|access-date=7 July 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150501080507/http://www.npr.org/event/music/402774459/jos-gonz-lez-on-mountain-stage|archive-date=1 May 2015}}</ref> González is also a member of the band [[Junip]], along with Tobias Winterkorn.

== Early life and education ==
In 1976, the González family – made up of González's father, a [[National University of San Luis]] psychology student, González's mother, a fellow student studying biochemistry, both of whom were politically active, and González's older sister, then an infant – fled [[Argentina]] after a right-wing military coup ([[military junta]]) seized power in March 1976, the beginning of the "[[Dirty War]]".<ref name=SouthFlorida-VestigesClaws-2015>{{cite news|last1=Crandell|first1=Ben|title=Jose Gonzalez, man of the world|url=http://www.southflorida.com/music/sf-jose-gonzalez-hollywood-artspark-20150923-story.html|work=SouthFlorida.com|date=23 September 2015}}</ref> José was born two years later, in 1978,<ref name="newway">{{cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/music/a-new-way-for-jose/2007/09/20/1189881687722.html|title=A new way for Jose|work=[[The Age]]|date=23 September 2007|access-date=13 May 2011}}</ref> in a suburb of [[Gothenburg, Sweden]]. He has a younger sibling.<ref name=BayBridged-VestigesClaws-2015>{{cite news|last1=Luoma|first1=Jess|title=TIMF Interview: Getting to know José Gonzaléz on a personal level|url=http://thebaybridged.com/2015/10/15/get-know-jose-gonzalez/|work=The Bay Bridged|date=15 October 2015}}</ref> He commented, "It's a very small town. It has about a half-million people living there. It's a pretty good music city by the ocean. It rains a lot there, but it's beautiful in the summertime."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicsnobbery.com/2006/07/jose_gonzalez_t.html|title=Jose Gonzalez: The MusicSnobbery.com Interview|work=MusicSnobbery.com|date=3 July 2006|access-date=12 August 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716185652/http://www.musicsnobbery.com/2006/07/jose_gonzalez_t.html|archive-date=16 July 2011}}</ref>

González grew up listening to Latin folk and pop music and has named Cuban singer-songwriter [[Silvio Rodríguez]] as a favourite artist.<ref name="newway"/> He said the first concert he went to was [[Bob Marley and the Wailers|The Wailers]]. "I got their autographs and everything," he said in an interview. "I was about 12 or so. At the time, my favourite music was [[Bob Marley]] and [[Michael Jackson]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.timeout.com/london/music/features/235.html|title=Jose Gonzalez: Interview|first=Chris|last=Parkin|work=[[Time Out (company)|Time Out]]|date=13 April 2006|access-date=12 August 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081012103232/http://www.timeout.com/london/music/features/235.html|archive-date=12 October 2008}}</ref>

González was in a PhD program for biochemistry at the [[University of Gothenburg]].<ref name=BBCRadio1-Veneer-2008>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio1/guerilla/jose/|title=Sweety Gonzalez: Jose has a quiet word with Guerilla|work=[[BBC Radio 1]]|access-date=31 July 2008}}</ref> In 2003, he stopped working on the PhD, as his musical career became a new focus.<ref name=Fader-Yoshis-2008>{{cite news|last1=Evers|first1=Derek|title=Interview – Jose Gonzalez|url=http://www.thefader.com/2008/10/01/interview-jose-gonzalez|work=[[The Fader]]|date=1 October 2008|language=en}}</ref><ref name=PlugInMusic-InOurNature-2007>{{cite news|last1=Hakimzadeh|first1=Dara|title=José González: The Science of Music|url=http://www.pluginmusic.com/features.php?page=josegonzalez|work=PlugInMusic.com|date=19 December 2007|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
The first band he played in was Back Against the Wall, a Gothenburg [[hardcore punk]] band influenced by [[Black Flag (band)|Black Flag]], [[Misfits (band)|The Misfits]] and the [[Dead Kennedys]].<ref name="exclaimmag">{{cite web|url=http://www.exclaim.ca/articles/points.aspx?csid1=116|title=José González Is Quietly Hardcore|last=Rajagopalan|first=Pras|work=[[Exclaim!]]|year=2007|access-date=31 October 2007|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071109072435/http://www.exclaim.ca/articles/points.aspx?csid1=116|archive-date=9 November 2007}}</ref> He later played bass guitar in another hardcore band, Renascence, between 1993 and 1998.<ref>[http://wayback.vefsafn.is/wayback/20060526150659/http://www.grapevine.is/undirflokkar.aspx?id=1116 José González] interview with ''[[The Reykjavík Grapevine]]''</ref> Between 1997 and 1998 José played guitar with rock band Only if You Call Me Jonathan.

In June 2003 González released his debut solo release, a two-track 7" single. The single was discovered by Joakim Gävert, co-founder of the then fledgling label Imperial Records who then signed González as their first official artist.<ref name="hitquarters.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/opar/intrview_Magnus_Bohman_Interview.html|title=Interview with Magnus Bohman|first=Lukasz |last=Polowczyk|work=[[HitQuarters]]|date=25 June 2006|access-date=10 December 2010}}</ref> In October he released his debut album, ''[[Veneer (album)|Veneer]]'', in Europe. The album was subsequently released in the UK on 25 April 2005, and in the United States on 6 September 2005. The album was made while González was a PhD student at the [[University of Gothenburg]].<ref name=BBCRadio1-Veneer-2008 />

González' trademark sound is solo classical guitar with soft vocal melody. His work, although mostly original, also includes acoustic covers of such hits as "[[Heartbeats (song)|Heartbeats]]" by his fellow Swedes [[The Knife]], "[[Love Will Tear Us Apart]]" by [[Joy Division]], "[[Born in the U.S.A. (song)|Born in the U.S.A.]]" and "[[The Ghost of Tom Joad (song)|The Ghost of Tom Joad]]" by [[Bruce Springsteen]], "[[Hand on Your Heart]]" by [[Kylie Minogue]], "[[Smalltown Boy]]" by [[Bronski Beat]], "[[Teardrop (Massive Attack song)|Teardrop]]" by [[Massive Attack]] and "Last Snowstorm of the Year" by [[Low (band)|Low]].

His second album, ''[[In Our Nature]]'', was released internationally on 22 September 2007.  The album's lyrical content was in part influenced by his reading of books like ''[[The God Delusion]]'' by evolutionary biologist [[Richard Dawkins]] and ''[[Practical Ethics]]'' by ethicist [[Peter Singer]].<ref name="exclaimmag"/><ref>[http://www.groundcontrolmag.com/detail/1/547/ Jose Gonzalez interview] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071008174336/http://www.groundcontrolmag.com/detail/1/547/ |date=8 October 2007 }} with Mark Ziemke for GroundControlMag.com, 4 September 2007</ref> In 2007, González won a [[European Border Breakers Award]] Award. Every year the European Border Breakers Awards (EBBA) recognize the success of ten emerging artists or groups who reached audiences outside their own countries with their first internationally released album in the past year.

In 2010, a documentary about González called ''The Extraordinary Ordinary Life Of Jose Gonzalez'' was released.<ref name=ExtraordinaryOrdinaryLife-Doc-2010>{{cite web|last1=Egerstrand & Blund|title=The Extraordinary Ordinary Life Of José González|url=https://vimeo.com/43518985|format=Documentary short|date=2010}}</ref><ref name=Variety-Documentary-Review-2010>{{cite news|last1=Simon|first1=Alissa|title=The Extraordinary Ordinary Life of Jose Gonzalez|url=https://variety.com/2010/film/reviews/the-extraordinary-ordinary-life-of-jose-gonzalez-1117942090/|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=7 February 2010}}</ref> In December 2014, González performed at the sustainable lifestyle festival, [[Wonderfruit]] in Thailand.

His album ''[[Vestiges & Claws]]'' was released in February 2015. It was critically acclaimed and received the IMPALA Album of the Year Award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://impalamusic.org/content/%E2%80%9Cvestiges-claws%E2%80%9D-jos%C3%A9-gonz%C3%A1lez-wins-impala-album-year-award | title="Vestiges & Claws" by José González wins IMPALA Album of the Year Award" | publisher=sur impalamusic.org}}</ref>

On 17 February 2021, he released a single with Spanish lyrics, "El Invento".<ref>"José González - El Invento (Official Music Video)" https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j3_Zz0O4pDo</ref> In April he released "Visions", the second single from his album ''[[Local Valley]]'', which was released on 17 September.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Elder |first1=Sajae |title=José González announces new album Local Valley, shares "Visions" |url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/04/14/jos-gonzlez-announces-new-album-local-valley-shares-visions |website=Pitchfork |access-date=14 April 2021 |date=14 April 2021}}</ref>

== Television and other media appearances==
{{BLP sources section|date=January 2018}}
[[File:Jose Gonzalez live at Shepherds Bush Empire London.jpg|left|thumb|200px|José González performing at O2 Shepherds Bush Empire in London, UK, April 2008]]
González has performed on several television programs, including ''[[Late Night with Conan O'Brien]]'' (29 March 2006 and 28 September 2007), ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]]'' (12 October 2007), the BBC's ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]'' (9 June 2006), ''[[Last Call with Carson Daly]]'' (21 June 2006), [[Seven Network]]'s ''[[Sunrise (Australian TV program)|Sunrise]]'' (15 August 2007) and Canada's ''[[MTV Live (Canada)|MTV Live]]'' (5 December 2007).

The song "Crosses", from González's ''Crosses'' [[Extended play|EP]] and the ''Veneer'' album, appeared on the finale of the second season of the popular American television [[comedy-drama]] ''[[The O.C.]]'', as well as on a trailer for a [[BBC Four]] programme.{{Which|date=August 2011}} Also taken from the ''Veneer'' album, "Stay in the Shade" was used in ''[[The O.C.]]'', midway through season three, in the episode ''The Pot Stirrer''. The song "Storm," also from ''Veneer'', was used in season 1 episode called "Best Laid Plans" in the [[Friday Night Lights (TV series)|Friday Night Lights]]'' TV series (12 December 2006).

His cover of [[The Knife]]'s "[[Heartbeats (song)|Heartbeats]]" has been used many times. It featured in a [[Sony]] [[Bravia (brand)|BRAVIA]] commercial featuring 250,000 coloured [[bouncing ball]]s in San Francisco;<ref>[http://blogotheque.net/article.php3?id_article=1541 "José González: sparking heartbeats in the charts"], 22 January 2006 interview with [[La Blogothèque]]</ref><ref>[http://www.bravia-advert.com/balls/] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061121181149/http://www.bravia-advert.com/balls/ |date=21 November 2006 }}</ref> in episode 409 and 505 of the CW's ''[[One Tree Hill (TV series)|One Tree Hill]]'' and is included on the album ''The Road Mix: One Tree Hill Volume 3''; it featured in season 7, episode 2 of ''[[Scrubs (TV series)|Scrubs]]''; in season 1, episode 21 of ''[[Brothers & Sisters (2006 TV series)|Brothers & Sisters]]''; in season 4, episode 17 of ''[[Bones (TV series)|Bones]]''; in ''[[Stargate Universe]]''; in ''[[90210 (TV series)|90210]]''; in season 1, episode 21 of ''[[Whitney (TV series)|Whitney]]''; and in an episode of the British TV show; ''Sun, Sea and A+E''. ''[[Gone (2007 film)|Gone]]'', a 2007 film directed by Ringan Ledwidge, and ''[[With Every Heartbeat (film)|Kyss Mig]]'', a 2011 film directed by Alexandra Therese Keining, both feature the track "Lovestain", from the ''[[Veneer (album)|Veneer]]'' album.''

Music from González' latest album has also been used in other television shows. His version of [[Massive Attack]]'s "[[Teardrop (Massive Attack song)|Teardrop]]" was used in the fourth [[season finale]] of ''[[House (TV series)|House]]'' entitled "Wilson's Heart" (the Massive Attack version is used as the show's theme music). It features in the fifth-season episode of ''[[Numb3rs]]'' entitled "The Fifth Man". The song can be heard at the end of ''[[Friday Night Lights (TV series)|Friday Night Lights]]'' season 4, episode "A Sort of Homecoming".

González features prominently on the soundtrack to Ben Stiller's "[[The Secret Life of Walter Mitty (2013 film)|''The Secret Life of Walter Mitty'']]", whether with [[Junip]] or by himself, including choir-backed opener "Step Out", "Stay Alive" and similarly lush John Lennon cover "#9 Dream".

His original song "Far Away" appeared in the [[Western (genre)|Western]] video game ''[[Red Dead Redemption]]'' by [[Rockstar Games]]. The song's [[Western music (North America)|Western music]] feel garnered "Best Song" at [[Spike Video Game Awards]] for its featured appearance, the game itself took home the award for "Game of the Year".

== Collaborations ==
González plays in the band [[Junip]] with Tobias Winterkorn (and previously Elias Araya). To date Junip have released two EPs, ''[[Black Refuge EP|Black Refuge]]'' and the free internet release ''[[Rope and Summit]]'', along with two full-length albums, ''[[Fields (Junip album)|Fields]]'' and ''[[Junip (Junip album)|Junip]]''.

He collaborated with UK downtempo duo [[Zero 7]] on their 2006 album, ''[[The Garden (Zero 7 album)|The Garden]]'', providing guest vocals on the album's tracks, along with Zero 7 regular [[Sia Furler]]. González performs vocals on four songs: "Futures", "Left Behind", "Today" and "Crosses". His song "Crosses" is featured in the popular video game [[Life Is Strange (video game)|Life Is Strange]].

González collaborated with Finnish house DJ [[Jori Hulkkonen]] on the track "Blinded By The Numbers" from Hulkkonen's album ''Dualizm'', released in 2005, and his song 'Crosses' was sampled by British hiphop artist [[Plan B (musician)|Plan B]] for the song "Cast a Light" which appears on the ''Paint It Blacker'' EP. He has also collaborated with the Swedish hip hop DJ and producer [[Embee]] on the track "Send Someone Away", the album mixed by [[Soundism]] received a [[Grammis]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundism.com/ |title=Music Producers, Sound Engineers, Songwriters and Music Production Studios |publisher=Soundism.com |access-date=7 April 2015}}</ref> González's song "Crosses" was remixed in 2006 by popular Dutch DJ [[Tiësto]]. González can also be heard in the song "I want you Back" by the Swedish indie-rock group Niccokick.

González collaborated with [[The Books]] on [[Red Hot Organization]]'s ''[[Dark Was the Night (album)|Dark Was the Night]]'' compilation album, fundraising for [[HIV/AIDS|HIV]] and AIDS awareness, which was released in February 2009.<ref>{{cite web|last=Hall |first=Tara |title=Jose Gonzalez maps North American jaunt |url=http://www.livedaily.com/news/19193.html |work=Livedaily |date=27 May 2009 |access-date=24 June 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090529171542/http://www.livedaily.com/news/19193.html |archive-date=29 May 2009 }}</ref> In 2011, he contributed to the organization's follow-up album "Red Hot+Rio 2" with the song "Um Girassol Da Cor Do Seu Cabelo," recorded with [[Mia Doi Todd]].

González participated in [[The Göteborg String Theory]] project in 2009–2011, an experimental music and art project that involved artists from Göteborg ([[Gothenburg]]), Sweden and classical composers from [[Berlin]], Germany. A new sound piece of his song "Cycling Trivialities" was arranged by composer Nackt for a classical orchestra. It was recorded and performed together with González, and finally released on the album The Göteborg String Theory (Kning Disk) in April 2010. González continued his collaboration with The Göteborg String Theory in 2011. Nackt wrote 11 new arrangement for González' songs, and in spring 2011 José González and The Göteborg String Theory went on a European tour in 19 cites.

== Personal life ==
González is in a long-term relationship with his partner, Swedish designer Hannele Fernström, with whom he has a daughter born in 2017.<ref name=Murmur75-ArtistAsParent-2018>{{cite news|last1=Milazzo|first1=Robert|last2=González|first2=José|title=Murmur 75: José González "They Choose Where We Live"|url=http://www.murmurradio.com/archive/2018/2/10/murmur-75-jos-gonzlez-they-choose-where-we-live|work=Murmur Digital Radio|date=12 February 2018|format=Audio podcast}}</ref> González is an [[atheism|atheist]]<ref name=Murmur75-ArtistAsParent-2018 /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://nymag.com/daily/entertainment/2008/09/jos_gonzlez_on_learning_portug.html|title=José González on Learning Portuguese and the Likelihood of Hearing 'Kumbaya' at His Concert This Weekend|last=Salazar|first=Lauren|work=[[New York (magazine)|New York]]|date=26 September 2008|access-date=9 September 2010}}</ref> and a "flexible" vegetarian.

A firm believer in [[effective altruism]], González is since August 2017 a member of [[Giving What We Can]], a community of people who have pledged to give at least 10% of their income to [[effective altruism|effective charities]].{{citation needed|date=October 2020}}
