'''Knutsen & Ludvigsen''' (English: Tootson and Ludiwood) was a [[Norway|Norwegian]] singing duo consisting of [[Øystein Dolmen]] ("Knutsen") and [[Gustav Lorentzen]] ("Ludvigsen").<ref name=allmusic>{{Cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p1064772/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Allmusic bio|publisher=[[All Media Guide]]|accessdate=2010-02-25}}</ref>

Writing and performing various songs mostly aimed at children, they released seven albums, two of which received [[Spellemannprisen]] - the Norwegian Grammy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.spellemann.no/index.php?option=com_winner&Itemid=29&year=1972|title=Spellemannprisen winners and nominations 1972|publisher=Spellemannprisen|accessdate=2010-02-25|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110720134505/http://www.spellemann.no/index.php?option=com_winner&Itemid=29&year=1972|archivedate=2011-07-20}}</ref> They had several #1 hits, including "Grevling i taket", "Hallo! Hallo!" and "Dum og deilig". Since the late '90s, they have enjoyed a cult status among young Norwegians, performing a series of reunion concerts.<ref name=allmusic/>

October 26, 2006, [[TV 2 (Norway)|TV 2]] broadcast the program "Knutsen & Ludvigsen - The Full Story". The Norwegian Pop & Rock Encyclopedia says: "This was more than a famous duo - Knutsen & Ludvigsen was an institution. The whole rock generation of the nineties was nourished by their records."<ref>Norsk Pop & Rock Leksikon, Oslo 2005: "Dette var mer enn en berømt duo - Knutsen & Ludvigsen var et begrep. Hele rockegenerasjonen fra 90-tallet var oppfostret på platene deres."</ref>

On 21 April 2010, Gustav Lorentzen died from a heart attack while participating in an [[orienteering]] race near [[Bergen]]. He was 62.
