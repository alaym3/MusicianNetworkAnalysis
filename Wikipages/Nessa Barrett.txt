'''Nessa Barrett''' is an American singer and songwriter from [[New Jersey]]. Barrett rose to prominence on the video sharing app [[TikTok]] in 2019. Barrett began her music career in mid-2020 with the release of her debut single "Pain."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nessa's New Music Video Is So Sad & Beautiful At The Same Time|url=https://www.elitedaily.com/p/nessa-barretts-pain-lyrics-music-video-are-melancholy-but-beautiful-30518337|access-date=2021-07-02|website=Elite Daily}}</ref> Barrett released her debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''Pretty Poison'' on September 10, 2021.<ref name="Poison">{{cite web|url=https://www.j-14.com/posts/nessa-barrett-pretty-poison-ep-release-date-tracklist-more-details/|title=Nessa Barrett 'Pretty Poison' EP: Release Date, Tracklist, More Details|work=[[J-14 (magazine)|J-14]]|last=Stivale|first=Shelvg|date=August 6, 2021|access-date=August 7, 2021}}</ref> Her song "[[I Hope Ur Miserable Until Ur Dead]]" entered the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] at number 88 in August 2021, becoming her first entry on the chart.<ref name="Hot 100"/>

==Career==
In 2019, Barrett joined the social media platform [[TikTok]], accumulating over 16.5 million followers {{asof|2021|8|lc=y}} and over 197.8 million likes on the platform.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ness (@nessaabarrett, nessa barrett) Official TikTok {{!}} Watch Ness's Newest TikTok Videos|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@nessaabarrett?lang=en|access-date=2021-07-02|via=TikTok}}</ref>

Barrett began her music career in July 2020, when she released her piano-based ballad debut single "Pain". Barrett's debut single was released shortly after signing with [[Warner Records]], the label reaching out and eventually signing her after discovering clips of her singing on TikTok.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Shafer|first=Ellise|date=2020-12-30|title=Meet the Creators Turning TikTok Fame Into Record Deals|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/tiktok-stars-music-industry-jxdn-nessa-barrett-jufu-tayler-holder-1234867855/|access-date=2021-07-02|website=Variety}}</ref> Barrett continued her music career, intending to embody a "dark, punk rock kind of vibe", with the release of her second single "If U Love Me" in October 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Warner Records Press {{!}} Nessa Barrett|url=https://press.warnerrecords.com/nessa-barrett/|access-date=2021-07-02}}</ref> Shortly after, Barrett followed the song with a dark interpretation of "[[Santa Baby]]", released in December 2020. In February 2021, Barrett released the track "[[La Di Die]]", which explores the downfalls of fame with fellow alt-pop artist [[Jxdn]], produced by [[Travis Barker]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=TotalNtertainment|date=2021-02-20|title=Nessa Barrett & jxdn collaborate for new track 'la di die'|url=https://www.totalntertainment.com/music/nessa-barrett-jxdn-collaborate-for-new-track-la-di-die/|access-date=2021-07-02|website=TotalNtertainment|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Nessa & Jaden's New Rock Collab Is Really, Really Dark, Y'all|url=https://www.elitedaily.com/p/nessa-barrett-jaden-hosslers-la-di-die-lyrics-explore-the-dark-side-of-fame-62841396|access-date=2021-07-02|website=Elite Daily}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/nessa-barrett-links-with-jxdn-on-new-single-la-di-die|title=Nessa Barrett Links With jxdn On New Single 'la di die'|work=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]|last=Murray|first=Robin|date=February 19, 2021|accessdate=July 4, 2021}}</ref> Barrett and collaborator Jxdn, along with Barker, performed the single live for the first time on the April 7, 2021 episode of ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]]'' and performed the song again on ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'' on April 12, 2021.

On June 25, 2021, Barrett released "Counting Crimes", a song said by Barrett to be "about moving on from something toxic with an empowering bad bitch energy. Admitting that you have made mistakes, but the other person has done worse than you and understanding who really is the bad guy in the situation."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alt-Pop Siren Nessa Barrett is "Counting Crimes"|url=https://popnerdlounge.com/read/alt-pop-siren-nessa-barrett-is-counting-crimes|access-date=2021-07-02|website=Pop Nerd Lounge}}</ref> Barrett confirmed on the ''[[Zach Sang Show]]'' in July 2021 that her debut EP would be titled ''Pretty Poison''. The EP contains seven new tracks and was released on September 10, 2021.<ref name="Poison" /> This date coincides with [[World Suicide Prevention Day]], which Barrett states is "to show that there's faith behind everything and that everyone is able to be happy whenever it's right for them."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pick Your ‘Poison’: Nessa Barrett on Owning Her Darkness & Finding a Brighter Day With Debut EP 'Pretty Poison'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/9626860/nessa-barrett-interview-pretty-poison-ep|access-date=2021-09-10|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> Barrett earned her first entry on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in August 2021, when her song "[[I Hope Ur Miserable Until Ur Dead]]" debuted at number 88.<ref name="Hot 100"/>
