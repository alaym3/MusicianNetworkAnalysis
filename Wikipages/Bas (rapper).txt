'''Abbas Hamad''' (born May 27, 1987), better known by his stage name '''Bas''', is a Sudanese-American rapper. He is signed to [[J. Cole]]'s [[Dreamville Records]] and [[Interscope Records]]. His first studio album ''[[Last Winter]]'', was released on April 29, 2014, followed by his second studio album, ''[[Too High to Riot]]'' that was released on March 4, 2016. His third album, ''[[Milky Way (album)|Milky Way]]'', was released on August 24, 2018.

Bas was born in [[Paris]] in 1987, to Sudanese parents. At the age of 8, his family moved to [[New York City]]. He attended [[St. Francis Preparatory School]] in [[Queens]], [[New York (state)|New York]].

==Musical career==
===2011–2013: Career beginnings and mixtapes===
In 2011, Bas released his debut mixtape titled ''Quarter Water Raised Vol. 1''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Bas-Quarter-Water-Raised-Me-Vol-I-mixtape.238276.html|title=Bas - Quarter Water Raised Me Vol. I|work=DatPiff}}</ref> In 2013, his second mixtape was released entitled, ''Quarter Water Raised Vol. 2''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Bas-Quarter-Water-Raised-Me-Vol-II-mixtape.492678.html|title=Bas - Quarter Water Raised Me Vol. II|work=DatPiff}}</ref>

In 2013, Bas was featured on [[J. Cole]]'s "New York Times" along with [[50 Cent]] from Cole's album ''[[Born Sinner]]'', and then on [[DJ Khaled]]'s "Hells Kitchen" from ''[[Suffering from Success]]''.<ref name="edwinortiz"/> He then appeared on the Dreamville compilation mixtape ''[[Revenge of the Dreamers]]''. That mixtape was released in celebration of Dreamville's partnership with [[Interscope Records]], resulting in Bas being signed to Interscope.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kramer|first1=Kyle|url=http://www.complexmag.ca/music/2014/01/j-cole-releases-revenge-of-the-dreamers-mixtape-announces-dreamville-interscope-partnership|website=Complex Music|title=J. Cole Releases A New Mixtape "Revenge of the Dreamers" and Announces Partnership Between His Dreamville Imprint and Interscope}}</ref> Two weeks prior to the release of his debut album ''Last Winter'', Bas released a free EP titled ''Two Weeks Notice''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/04/bas-releases-new-mixtape-two-weeks-notice/|title=Bas Releases New Mixtape âTwo Weeks Noticeâ|work=XXL}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/04/download-bas-two-weeks-notice-ep|title=Download Bas' "Two Weeks Notice" EP|author=Zach Frydenlund|work=Complex}}</ref>

===2014–2015: ''Last Winter''===
{{Main|Last Winter}}
[[File:Bas2013.jpg|180px|thumb|right|Bas performing during What Dreams May Come Tour in January 2014.]]
''[[Last Winter]]'' was released on April 29, 2014 by Dreamville Records and [[Interscope Records]]. The album was a conceptual project that touches on the cold days in New York City recording the album. The album was supported by the single "My Nigga Just Made Bail" produced by GP808 and featured guest appearances by [[J. Cole]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2014/04/bas-charles-de-gaulle-jfk/|title=Bas "Charles de Gaulle To JFK" - XXL|website=XXL Mag}}</ref> ''Last Winter'' would debut at 103 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with 3,601 copies sold in its first week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.28644/title.hip-hop-album-sales-week-ending-05-04-2014|title=Hip Hop Album Sales: Week Ending 05/04/2014|first=HipHopDX -|last=http://hiphopdx.com|access-date=2014-06-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140508061251/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.28644/title.hip-hop-album-sales-week-ending-05-04-2014|archive-date=2014-05-08|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Following its release, Bas went on a 10-city nationwide tour titled after the album.<ref name="edwinortiz"/> Bas followed his own "[[Last Winter]]" tour by going on a 24-city nationwide and international tour with TDE rapper [[Ab-Soul]]. In 2015, Bas supported J. Cole on '2014 Forest Hills Drive Tour' alongside Omen, Cozz, Jeremih, YG, and Big Sean.

===2016–2017: ''Too High to Riot''===
{{Main|Too High to Riot}}
Bas released his second album, ''[[Too High to Riot]]'' on March 4, 2016. The album also includes guest appearances from his labelmates J. Cole and [[Cozz]], as well as [[the Hics]]. The album would debut at 49 on the [[Billboard 200]] chart, selling 10,965 copies in its first week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2016/02/05/listen-to-bas-newest-release-methylone-off-his-forthcoming-too-high-to-riot/|title=Listen to Bas' Newest Release, "Methylone," Off His Forthcoming 'Too High to Riot' - The Source|date=5 February 2016}}</ref> He also later released a music video for each song on the album. Bas went on tour titled after the album in June 2016 with [[Cozz]] and [[EarthGang]]. It included 26 cities in North America and 10 cities in Europe. On January 29, 2017, the documentary highlighting the tour was released on Tidal.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Dreamville|title=Too High To Riot (Documentary)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P93txyXVbm0|website=[[YouTube]]|access-date=March 12, 2017|date=February 4, 2017}}</ref>

===2018–present: ''Milky Way''===
{{Main|Milky Way (album)}}
On April 10, 2018, Bas released the one-off single "Pinball II", featuring Correy C.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2018/04/bas-pinball-ii|title=Bas Joins Forces With Correy C on New Song "Pinball II'|website=Complex}}</ref> The first single for his third album "Boca Raton" was released on June 19, and featured [[ASAP Ferg]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/06/bas-asap-ferg-boca-raton|title=Stream Bas' Summer-Ready Single "Boca Raton" f/ ASAP Ferg|website=Complex}}</ref> On August 22, he released the second single "[[Tribe (Bas song)|Tribe]]" featuring J. Cole.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8471694/bas-tribe-video-j-cole|title=Bas & J. Cole Travel Through Miami's Lil Haiti for New 'Tribe' Video: Watch}}</ref> Bas released his third album, ''[[Milky Way (album)|Milky Way]]'' on August 24, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.47970/title.bas-announces-milky-way-album-drops-video-with-asap-ferg|title=Bas Announces "Milky Way" Album & Drops Video With A$AP Ferg|first=HipHopDX -|last=http://hiphopdx.com|date=9 August 2018}}</ref> The album debuted at number 35 on the ''Billboard'' 200, selling 13,150 album-equivalent units in the first week. Bas announced a 43-city tour to support the album, which began in November 2018 and concluded in February 2019. In late 2018, Bas announced he was working on an album with frequent collaborators, [[The Hics]], the album is due out late 2021 to early 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/bas-announces-milky-way-tour-news.60659.html|title = Bas Announces "Milky Way" Tour}}</ref>
