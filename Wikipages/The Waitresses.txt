  | last = Huey
  | first = Steve
  | url = {{BillboardURLbyName|artist=the waitresses|bio=true}}

  | publisher = Billboard.com
  | access-date = October 25, 2012
}}</ref><ref name="Moore_2006">{{cite book
  | last = Moore
  | first = Christie
  | chapter-url = https://books.google.com/books?id=HsSo4WC2FmAC&q=%22i+know+what+boys+like%22&pg=PA408
  | title = The Unultimate Rockopedia
  | chapter = "W: The Waitresses"
  | publisher = AuthorHouse

  | access-date = October 25, 2012
}}</ref> best known for their singles "[[I Know What Boys Like]]" and "[[Christmas Wrapping]]". They released two [[album]]s, ''[[Wasn't Tomorrow Wonderful?]]'' and ''[[Bruiseology]]'', and two [[Extended play|EP]]s, ''[[I Could Rule the World If I Could Only Get the Parts]]'' and ''Make the Weather''.<ref name="Billboard_bio_Waitresses"/>

The group was led by guitarist-songwriter [[Chris Butler (musician)|Chris Butler]], with lead [[singing|vocals]] performed by [[Patty Donahue]].<ref name="Billboard_bio_Waitresses"/><ref name="Talevski_2006">{{cite book

==History==
The Waitresses were formed by Butler (formerly of [[the Numbers Band]]) in 1978 as a side project while he was still a member of [[Tin Huey]].<ref name="Billboard">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/419396/waitresses/biography |title=The Waitresses - Biography |magazine=Billboard |date=1996-12-09 |access-date=2017-09-07}}</ref> He wrote and recorded "I Know What Boys Like" that year, with guest vocals by friend Donahue (as "Patty Darling") and saxophone from Tin Huey member [[Ralph Carney]], although the song remained unreleased at the time.<ref name="Billboard" /> A debut single, ''In "Short Stack"'' (featuring the songs "Slide" and "Clones"), recorded solely by Butler, was issued by Clone Records in 1978.<ref name="Pouncey">{{Cite magazine |last=Pouncey |first=Edwin |date=March 6, 1982 |title=The Waitresses: What The Butler Said |url=https://www.rocksbackpages.com/Library/Article/the-waitresses-what-the-butler-said |magazine=[[Sounds (magazine)|Sounds]] |access-date=June 21, 2018 |via=[[Rock's Backpages]] |url-access=subscription }}</ref> Both tracks from the single, plus another early song, "The Comb", appeared that year on ''The Akron Compilation'', issued by [[Stiff Records]].<ref name="Pouncey"/>

Butler moved to [[New York City]] and shopped "I Know What Boys Like".<ref name="Billboard" /> The song landed him a deal with [[ZE Records]] (an affiliate of [[Antilles Records]]), who released the single in 1980.<ref name="Billboard" /> It was an underground hit, but did not chart.<ref name="Popoff_2010">{{cite book
  | last = Popoff
  | first = Martin
  | authorlink = Martin Popoff
  | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=XUI1R7jJz2YC&q=%22i+know+what+boys+like%22
  | title = Goldmine Standard Catalog of American Records 1948–1991
  | page = 1233
  | publisher = Krause
  | date = September 2, 2010
  | isbn = 978-1-440216-21-3
  | access-date = October 25, 2012
 }}</ref><ref name="Billboard_1982-02-06">{{cite journal
  | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=FyUEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22i+know+what+boys+like%22+billboard&pg=PA71

  | access-date = October 25, 2012
}} "The self-made single, 'I Know What Boys Like', has been a New York club favorite for ''a couple of years now''..." (emphasis added).</ref>

With the deal in place, Butler put together an actual band lineup for the Waitresses, featuring lead vocalist Donahue, jazz saxophonist [[Mars Williams]], former [[Television (band)|Television]] drummer [[Billy Ficca]], keyboardist Dan Klayman, bassist Dave Hofstra and backing vocalist Ariel Warner. The Waitresses played their debut concert on New Year's Eve 1980.<ref name="Billboard" />

The Waitresses released their debut album, ''[[Wasn't Tomorrow Wonderful?]]'' on January 11, 1982 on the [[Polydor Records|Polydor]] label, licensed from ZE.<ref name="Talevski_2006"/> "I Know What Boys Like" was included on the album, and when the song was re-released as a single in 1982, it peaked at No. 62 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]],<ref name="Billboard_1982-06-12">{{cite web
  | url = http://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/1982-06-05?order=drop
  | title = Hot 100: Week of June 5, 1982
  | work = Billboard.com
  | date = June 12, 1982
  | access-date = October 25, 2012
}}</ref>
No.&nbsp;23 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'''s [[Hot Mainstream Rock Tracks|Top Tracks]] chart,<ref name="Billboard_1982-02-27">{{cite journal
  | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=2yQEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22i+know+what+boys+like%22+billboard&pg=PA24
  | title = Billboard Rock Albums & Top Tracks
  | journal = Billboard
  | volume = 94
  | issue = 8
  | issn = 0006-2510
  | page = 24
  | date = February 27, 2012
  | access-date = October 25, 2012
}}</ref>
No.&nbsp;14 on the Australian Singles Chart (Kent Music Report),<ref name="Billboard_1982-02-06"/>
and also charted in the UK.<ref name="Moore_2006"/>

During the recording sessions for the album, which peaked at No. 41 in the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, Warner resigned from the band due to stage fright, and Hofstra quit before its release, to be replaced by [[Tracy Wormworth]].<ref name="Billboard" />

A [[Christmas song]] by the band, "Christmas Wrapping", had originally been released on the ZE Records album ''[[A Christmas Record]]'' in 1981,<ref name="Billboard_bio_Waitresses"/> and became a No. 45 hit in the [[United Kingdom]] in 1982.<ref name="Talevski_2006"/><ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums">{{cite book

The song was subsequently covered by the [[Spice Girls]] in 1998, as the [[A-side and B-side|B-side]] of their single "[[Goodbye (Spice Girls song)|Goodbye]]"<ref name="Billboard_bio_Waitresses"/><ref name="Billboard_holiday-songs">{{cite web

and on the television show ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]'' sung by the character Brittany for their 2011 Christmas special.<ref>{{cite web
  | url = https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2001513/combined

The Waitresses recorded the [[theme music|theme song]] to the [[television program]] ''[[Square Pegs]]'',<ref name="Talevski_2006"/><ref name="Discogs.com">{{cite web
  | url = http://www.discogs.com/Waitresses-I-Could-Rule-The-World-If-I-Could-Only-Get-The-Parts/release/472650

starring [[Sarah Jessica Parker]] and [[Amy Linker]], which aired during the 1982–1983 season,<ref name="IMDb_Waitresses">{{cite web
  | url = https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1382004/

and the band appeared as themselves in the pilot episode.<ref name="IMDb_Waitresses"/> The song was issued by Polydor as a single in 1982, and was included (along with "Christmas Wrapping") on the EP ''I Could Rule the World If I Could Only Get the Parts'' that same year.<ref name="Billboard" />

The band's second album, ''Bruiseology'', was released by Polydor in May 1983, followed by the ''Make the Weather'' EP in 1984.

In the summer of 1984, Donahue left the band and was replaced by [[Holly Beth Vincent]], formerly of [[Holly and the Italians]]. But Vincent herself left after just two weeks, and Donahue returned.<ref name="Moore_2006"/><ref name="Talevski_2006"/>

The Waitresses split up in December 1984.<ref name="Billboard" />

==Post-breakup==
Donahue died of lung cancer at the age of 40 on December 9, 1996.<ref name="Talevski_2006"/>

Butler later worked as a producer, and played with numerous bands and artists including Half Cleveland, Purple K'nif and [[Richard Lloyd (guitarist)|Richard Lloyd]].

Ficca played in [[Gods and Monsters (band)|Gods and Monsters]] and returned to his former group Television when they reunited in 1991. Williams played with [[the Psychedelic Furs]], [[NRG Ensemble]], [[Liquid Soul]], [[Hal Russell]] and [[Ken Vandermark]]. Wormworth has played bass for [[the B-52's]] since 1992.

''[[King Biscuit Flower Hour Presents the Waitresses]]'', a live album recorded in 1982 at [[My Father's Place]] in [[Roslyn, New York]], was issued in 1997 by [[King Biscuit Flower Hour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/king-biscuit-flower-hour-mw0000095139|title=King Biscuit Flower Hour - The Waitresses - Songs, Reviews, Credits - AllMusic|website=AllMusic}}</ref>

Polydor issued two compilation albums, ''[[The Best of The Waitresses (1990 album)|The Best of the Waitresses]]'' (1990)<ref name="AM1990">{{AllMusic |class=album |id=mw0000309357 |tab=Overview |label=The Best of the Waitresses |first=Chris |last=Woodstra |access-date=January 15, 2019 }}</ref> and ''[[20th Century Masters – The Millennium Collection: The Best of The Waitresses]]'' (2003).<ref name="AM2003">{{AllMusic |class=album |id=mw0000022240 |tab=Overview |label=20th Century Masters – The Millennium Collection: The Best of The Waitresses |first=Heather |last=Phares |access-date=January 15, 2019 }}</ref>

In 2013, [[Omnivore Recordings]] released the compilation ''Just Desserts: The Complete Waitresses'', collecting virtually all of the band's recordings for Polydor,<ref name=AM>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/just-desserts-the-complete-waitresses-mw0002556230|title=Just Desserts: The Complete Waitresses|first=David |last=Jeffries
|access-date=10 December 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2013/10/the-waitresses-just-desserts-the-complete-waitress.html|title=The Waitresses: Just Desserts: The Complete Waitresses|publisher=}}</ref> while [[ZE Records]] issued a digital collection of their ZE releases, ''Deluxe Special: Ze Complete Recordings''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.zerecords.com/release/28/deluxe-specialze-complete-recording.html|title=The Waitresses – Deluxe Special: Ze Complete Recordings|first=Ze|last=Records|website=www.zerecords.com}}</ref>
