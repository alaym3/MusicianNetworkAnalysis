'''Laura Pausini''' {{postnominals|post-noms=[[Order of Merit of the Italian Republic|OMRI]]}} ({{IPA-it|ˈlaura pauˈziːni|lang}}; born 16 May 1974) is an Italian singer. As a child, she was encouraged by her father to join him during his performances in local [[piano bar]]s. After competing in local singing contests, Pausini signed her first recording contract. She rose to fame in 1993, winning the newcomer artists' section of the 43rd [[Sanremo Music Festival]] with her debut single "[[La solitudine]]",<ref name="Sanremo1993">{{cite web|url= http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1993/marzo/01/aria_nuova_Sanremo_per_ora_co_0_93030110053.shtml|access-date=8 September 2013|title=Aria nuova a Sanremo? Per ora solo spifferi|author = Mario Luzzatto Fegiz, Gloria Pozzi|date = 1 March 1993|work=[[Corriere della Sera]]|language=it|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130723011723/http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1993/marzo/01/aria_nuova_Sanremo_per_ora_co_0_93030110053.shtml|archive-date=23 July 2013}}</ref> which became an Italian standard<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.it/musica/artisti/archivio/artista.asp?idArt=5906#biografia |publisher=[[MTV Italy]] |language=it |access-date=22 July 2011 |title=Laura Pausini: La biografia |archive-date= 20 September 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120920025157/http://www.mtv.it/musica/artisti/archivio/artista.asp?idart=5906 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> and an international hit, topping the [[Dutch Top 40|charts in the Netherlands]], and [[Ultratop 50|Belgium]].<ref name=DutchTop40LaSolitudine>{{cite web|url=http://www.top40.nl/index.aspx?week=2&jaar=1994 |publisher=[[Dutch Top 40]] |access-date=1 September 2012 |title=Top40 week 2 van 1994 (08-01-1994) |language=nl |archive-date= 1 September 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120901165352/http://www.top40.nl/top40/1994/week-2 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref><ref name=VRTTop30>{{cite web | url = http://www.ultratop.be/nl/showitem.asp?interpret=Laura+Pausini&titel=La+solitudine&cat=s | publisher = [[Ultratop]]. Hung Medien | language = nl | access-date = 7 September 2013| title = Charts Vlaanderen – Laura Pausini – La solitudine}}</ref> Her [[Laura Pausini (1993 album)|self titled debut album]] was released in Italy on 23 April 1993<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wuz.it/disco/0745099238520/pausini-laura/laura-pausini.html |publisher=Wuz.it |language=it |access-date=6 August 2012 |title=Scheda Disco – Laura Pausini – Laura Pausini |archive-date= 18 February 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130218120532/http://www.wuz.it/disco/0745099238520/pausini-laura/laura-pausini.html |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> and later became an international success, selling two million copies worldwide.<ref name="SalesFirstAlbum">{{cite web|url=http://www.italica.rai.it/eng/principal/topics/bio/pausini.htm |publisher=[[RAI]] |access-date=1 September 2012 |title=Biography of Laura Pausini |archive-date= 15 March 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120315153244/http://www.italica.rai.it/eng/principal/topics/bio/pausini.htm |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref>

Its follow-up, ''[[Laura (Laura Pausini album)|Laura]]'', was released in 1994 and confirmed her international success, selling three million copies worldwide.<ref name="LauraSales">{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2000/settembre/15/Laura_Pausini_alla_moda_del_co_0_0009159171.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | access-date =8 September 2013 | language = it | date = 15 September 2000 | author = Mario Luzzatto Fegiz | title = Laura Pausini: no alla moda del pop latino|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130722232616/http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2000/settembre/15/Laura_Pausini_alla_moda_del_co_0_0009159171.shtml|archive-date=22 July 2013}}</ref> During the same year, she released her first Spanish-language album, ''[[Laura Pausini (1994 album)|Laura Pausini]]'', composed of ten adapted songs originally included in her previous works. The album was certified diamond by the [[Productores de Música de España|Association of Phonographic and Videographic of Spain]],<ref name=Diamond1994Album>{{cite web|title=Laura Pausini actuará en Madrid y Barcelona para presentar su undécimo disco, luego irá a la capital de colombia (Soledad) a comer butifarra baranoera |url=http://www.republica.com/2011/05/17/laura-pausini-actuara-en-madrid-y-barcelona-para-presentar-su-undecimo-disco_336295/ |language=es |access-date=1 September 2012 |date=17 May 2011 |publisher=República.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120806201205/http://www.republica.com/2011/05/17/laura-pausini-actuara-en-madrid-y-barcelona-para-presentar-su-undecimo-disco_336295/ |archive-date= 6 August 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> making her the first non-Spanish artist to sell more than one million copies in Spain.<ref name="RepubblicaArticle">{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2002/02/14/intrigante-soul-della-regina-del-pop.html | archive-url = https://archive.today/20120518050924/http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2002/02/14/intrigante-soul-della-regina-del-pop.html | url-status = dead | archive-date = 18 May 2012 | work = [[la Repubblica]] | access-date =29 July 2011 | title = L'intrigante soul della regina del pop | date = 14 February 2002 | first = Ernesto | last = Assante }}</ref>

As of 2017, Pausini has released twelve studio albums, two international [[greatest hits album]]s and two compilation albums for the Hispanic and Anglophone market only, respectively. She mostly performs in Italian and Spanish, but she has also recorded songs in English, French, Portuguese and more recently, Catalan.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.elperiodico.com/es/noticias/tele/laura-pausini-canta-lamarato-tv3-solidaridad-3641722 | work = [[El Periódico de Catalunya]] | access-date = 20 December 2014 | title = Laura Pausini canta por 'La Marató' de TV-3 | date = 28 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=MQ8EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA48 | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date = 1 September 2012 | title = Laura Pausini Tenth Anniversary – Italy's Multilingual Star Serenades The World | page = 48 | date = 15 March 2003 | first = Mark| last = Worden}}</ref> Her sole English-language album of original songs, ''[[From the Inside (Laura Pausini album)|From the Inside]]'', was released in 2002, but failed to satisfy commercial expectations both in North America and in the rest of the world. In 2006, her record ''[[Io canto]]'' was the best-selling album of the year in Italy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fimi.it/dett_comunicatistampa.php?id=15|publisher=[[Federation of the Italian Music Industry]]|date=10 January 2008|title=Classifiche Annuali 2007 FIMI-AC Nielsen: al primo posto Eros Ramazzotti con E2|access-date=19 January 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120506002747/http://www.fimi.it/dett_comunicatistampa.php?id=15|archive-date=6 May 2012}}</ref>

In 2004, [[Allmusic]]'s Jason Birchmeier considered Pausini's sales "an impressive feat for someone who'd never really broken into the lucrative English–language market".<ref name=allmusic /> In 2014 [[FIMI]] certified Pausini's sales of more than 70 million records with a FIMI Icon Award, making her the fourth best-selling female artist in Latin music, and the best-selling female non-Spanish speaking Latin music artist.<ref>{{cite web|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141204205948/http://www.webl0g.net/2014/07/07/icon-award-per-laura-pausini-e-il-world-tour-in-ottobre-sbarca-negli-stati-uniti/ |url=http://www.webl0g.net/2014/07/07/icon-award-per-laura-pausini-e-il-world-tour-in-ottobre-sbarca-negli-stati-uniti/ |publisher=Fimi & Italian Trade |access-date=27 November 2014 |archive-date= 4 December 2014 |title=Laura Pausini: Icon Award by Fimi & Italian Trade for her sales of more than 75 million records worldwide |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Pausini appeared as a coach on both [[La Voz... México|the Mexican]] and [[La Voz (Spanish TV series)|Spanish versions]] of international reality television singing competition franchise ''[[The Voice (TV series)|The Voice]]'', was a judge on the first and second series of ''[[La Banda (TV series)|La banda]]'', and is likewise a judge on [[Factor X (Spanish TV series)|the Spanish version]] of international franchise ''[[The X Factor]]''. In 2016, she also debuted as a variety show presenter, hosting the television show ''Laura & Paola'', with actress [[Paola Cortellesi]].

Throughout her career, she has won numerous music awards in Italy and internationally.
She has earned the first and third place at the [[Sanremo Music Festival]], two "Lunezia Awards", eighteen [[Wind Music Awards]], four awards at the [[Festivalbar]], seven [[Telegatti]], and an [[MTV Italian Music Awards|MTV Italian Music Award]]. Internationally, she has won four awards at the [[Viña del Mar International Song Festival]], four [[Lo Nuestro Awards]], four [[Latin Grammy Award]]s, a [[Billboard Latin Music Award]], six [[World Music Award]]s, and has received a nomination at the [[Emmy Award]]s. In 2006, she also became the first Italian female artist to win a [[Grammy Award]],<ref name="RepubblicaGrammy">{{cite web | url = http://www.repubblica.it/2006/b/sezioni/spettacoli_e_cultura/pausinigrammy/pausinigrammy/pausinigrammy.html?ref=search | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it | access-date =8 September 2013| title = Laura Pausini vince il Grammy. Premiata per il pop latino | date = 9 February 2006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121022123627/http://www.repubblica.it/2006/b/sezioni/spettacoli_e_cultura/pausinigrammy/pausinigrammy/pausinigrammy.html?ref=search|archive-date=22 October 2012}}</ref> receiving the accolade for [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album|Best Latin Pop Album]] for the record ''[[Resta in ascolto|Escucha]]''.<ref name="GrammyAward">{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/nominees/search?artist=laura+pausini&title=escucha&year=All&genre=All |publisher=Grammy.com |access-date=8 September 2013 |title=Past Winners Search – Laura Pausini – Escucha |archive-date=25 September 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120925205633/http://www.grammy.com/nominees/search?artist=laura%2Bpausini&title=escucha&year=All&genre=All |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2021, she was nominated for the [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]] for [[Academy Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]]  with "[[Io sì (Seen)]]" from the film ''[[The Life Ahead]]''. The single also won the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song]], making it the first Italian-language song to win the award.<ref name="GG">{{cite web|url=https://www.ilfattoquotidiano.it/2021/03/01/golden-globe-2021-laura-pausini-trionfa-con-io-si-seen-la-prima-volta-per-una-canzone-italiana/6117028/|title=Golden Globe 2021, Laura Pausini trionfa con Io sì (Seen). La prima volta per una canzone italiana|work=[[Il Fatto Quotidiano]]|language=it|date=1 March 2021|access-date=1 March 2021}}</ref> She has been honoured as a [[Order of Merit of the Italian Republic|Commander Order of Merit of the Italian Republic]] by President [[Carlo Azeglio Ciampi]] and as a [[Ambassador|World Ambassador]] of [[Emilia Romagna]].

==Biography==
===Childhood and early beginnings===
The elder of two daughters,<ref name=Family/> Laura Pausini was born in [[Faenza]], in the [[Province of Ravenna]], Italy,<ref name=Commander>{{cite web | url = http://www.quirinale.it/elementi/DettaglioOnorificenze.aspx?decorato=173657 | publisher = Quirinale.it | language = it | title = Pausini Sig.ra Laura – Commendatore Ordine al Merito della Repubblica Italiana | access-date = 1 September 2012 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110605221939/http://www.quirinale.it/elementi/DettaglioOnorificenze.aspx?decorato=173657 | archive-date = 5 June 2011}}</ref> to Fabrizio Pausini<ref name=Family>{{Cite magazine | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=MQ8EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA43 | magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date = 15 March 2003 | access-date = 1 September 2012 | title = Italy's multilingual star serenades the world | first = Mark| last = Worden}}</ref> and Gianna Ballardini. She grew up in [[Solarolo]], a small ''[[comune]]'' in the same region.<ref name="FirstAlbum">{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1993/maggio/25/Laura_Pausini_Sanremo_primo_disco_co_0_93052512464.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | access-date =29 July 2011 | date = 25 May 1993 | title = Laura Pausini da Sanremo al primo disco}}</ref><sup id="inlinea" class="reference">[[#notea|[A]]]</sup>
Her father is a former pianist<ref>{{cite web|url=http://archives.lesoir.be/pausini-d-oiseau-le-rossignol-de-ravenna-veut-tout-et-t_t-19961002-Z0CPDF.html |work=[[Le Soir]] |language=fr |access-date=8 September 2013 |archive-date= 7 September 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130907220601/http://archives.lesoir.be/pausini-d-oiseau-le-rossignol-de-ravenna-veut-tout-et-t_t-19961002-Z0CPDF.html |title=Pausini d'oiseau, le rossignol de Ravenna veut tout et tou |date=2 October 1996 |page=47 |first=Thierry |last=Coljon |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> who also played as a [[sessionman]] for [[ABBA]]'s [[Frida Lyngstad]] and entered a band whose members later founded the Italian pop group [[Pooh (band)|Pooh]].<ref name=Family/> After becoming a [[piano bar]] artist, he encouraged Pausini to start performing as a singer.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.corriere.it/Primo_Piano/Spettacoli/2006/02_Febbraio/08/grammy.html | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | access-date = 1 September 2012 | title = Grammy, trionfa Laura Pausini | date = 9 February 2006 | archive-date = 4 April 2006 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20060404132741/http://www.corriere.it/Primo_Piano/Spettacoli/2006/02_Febbraio/08/grammy.html}}</ref>
Her first live performance was on 16 May 1985, when she sang together with her father in a restaurant in [[Bologna]].<ref name="GianniMinà">{{cite web | url = http://www.giannimina.it/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=177&Itemid=53 | publisher = Giannimina.it | first = Gianni | last = Minà | access-date = 1 September 2012 | title = Laura Pausini: una ragazza di provincia con un respiro mondiale | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110716010503/http://www.giannimina.it/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=177&Itemid=53 | archive-date = 16 July 2011}}</ref>
Since then, her father started giving her singing lessons<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1994/maggio/26/imparato_papa_cantando_piano_bar_co_10_9405261778.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | date = 26 May 1994 | first = Sandra | last = Cesarale | title = Ho imparato da papà cantando al piano bar}}</ref> and she continued to perform alongside him in local piano bars. In the meanwhile, she also started singing in a [[church choir]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=1084008 | work = [[La Stampa]] | access-date = 29 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini. Maturità e poi tourneé. "Son studiosa e devota" | date = 23 April 1993 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20111005080709/http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=1084008 | archive-date = 5 October 2011 | df = dmy-all }}</ref>

In 1987 she recorded her first [[Demo (music)|demo album]], produced by her father and released to promote her live shows.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2009/11/25/laura-live-pausini-la-voce-del-pop.html | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it | access-date = 1 September 2012 | title = Laura live Pausini, la voce del pop successi intorno al mondo | first = Gino | last = Castaldo | date = 25 November 2009 | page = 15}}</ref> Titled ''I sogni di Laura'', it consisted of eight covers and five new songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rockit.it/album/15647/laura-pausini-i-sogni-di-laura |publisher=Rockit.it |language=it |access-date=8 September 2013 |title=Laura Pausini – I sogni di Laura |date=11 April 2011 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130907220752/http://www.rockit.it/laurapausini/album/i-sogni-di-laura/15647 |archive-date= 7 September 2013 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> In 1991 she participated in the [[Castrocaro Music Festival]] singing [[Liza Minnelli]]'s "[[Theme from New York, New York|New York, New York]]", but she failed to reach the final stage of the competition.<ref name="GianniMinà"/> During the same year, she took part in another singing competition, Sanremo Famosi, which should have served as a selection for the newcomers' of the following [[Sanremo Music Festival]].<ref name=RepubblicaSanremo1993>{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/1993/02/28/sanremo-vince-ruggeri.html | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it | access-date = 1 September 2012 | date = 28 February 1993 | title= Sanremo: vince Ruggeri | author = Maria Pia Fusco|page=25}}</ref> Despite being declared joint winner with another contestant,<ref name="GianniMinà"/><ref name=RepubblicaSanremo1993/> Pausini was not allowed to compete in the [[Sanremo Music Festival]] 1992.<ref name="GianniMinà"/>

===1993: Career breakthrough===
Thanks to her performances in local singing competitions, Pausini was noticed by Italian producer and songwriter Angelo Valsiglio, who introduced her to manager Marco Marati.<ref name="GianniMinà"/> Valsiglio suggested to her "[[La solitudine]]", a song he wrote with Pietro Cremonesi and Federico Cavalli. Pausini's rendition convinced Valsiglio and Marati, who wanted Pausini to audition for some major labels.<ref name="GianniMinà"/> During one of the auditions, she met Fabrizio Giannini of [[Warner Music Italy]]'s [[Compagnia Generale del Disco]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=MQ8EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA46 | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date = 2 September 2012 | title = Laura Pausini – The Billboard Interview | first = Mark | last = Worden | date = 15 March 2003 | page = 46}}</ref> After impressing him with a performance of an unreleased [[Mia Martini]] song, Pausini obtained her first recording contract, becoming one of the first artists discovered by Giannini, who later launched the careers of several Italian acts, including [[Irene Grandi]].<ref name="GianniMinà"/>

[[File:Sanremo0006.jpg|thumb|Pausini rose to fame in 1993, after winning the 43rd [[Sanremo Music Festival]], annually held at the [[Teatro Ariston]] in [[Sanremo]], Italy.]]
"La solitudine" was selected as one of the entries in the newcomer artists' section of the 43rd [[Sanremo Music Festival]]. Pausini performed it for the first time on 23 February 1993, during the first semifinal of the contest.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/1993/02/23/il-programma-delle-tre-serate.html | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it | access-date = 23 September 2011 | date = 23 February 1993 | title = Il programma delle tre serate}}</ref> After being admitted to the final, held on 27 February 1993, she won the competition, receiving 7,464 votes from the juries and beating [[Gerardina Trovato]] with "Ma non ho più la mia città", who took second place with 7,209 votes.
The song also became a commercial success in Italy, and it is still one of Pausini's best-known hits.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2005/06/30/la-regina-della-canzone-amore.html | work = [[la Repubblica]] | date = 30 June 2005 | access-date =29 July 2011 | title = La regina della canzone d' amore | author = Giusy La Piana}}</ref>

Following the success obtained with her debut single, Pausini started working on her first professional album, ''[[Laura Pausini (1993 album)|Laura Pausini]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://warnermusic.it/artista/laura-pausini/?s=read-all |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120320053811/http://warnermusic.it/artista/laura-pausini/?s=read-all|archive-date=20 March 2012| publisher = [[Warner Music Group]] | language = it | access-date =8 September 2013 | title = Laura Pausini – Biografia}}</ref>
The album was recorded while Pausini was still a high school student<ref name="Repubblica15092000"/> at the "Gaetano Ballardini" Institute of Ceramics in [[Faenza]], Italy,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.istitutoarteceramicafaenza.com/arch/docs/50/Il%20Resto%20del%20Carlino%20-%20Maturi.pdf |publisher=[[Il Resto del Carlino]] / Istituto Arte Ceramica Faenza |language=it |access-date=2 September 2012 |title=Istituto d'Arte per la Ceramica Ballardini |archive-date= 25 October 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101025220901/http://www.istitutoarteceramicafaenza.com/arch/docs/50/Il%20Resto%20del%20Carlino%20-%20Maturi.pdf |year=2010 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> where she got her [[High school diploma|diploma]] a few months after the release of her debut studio set.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1993/ottobre/06/Pausini_macche_solitudine_fans_assaltano_co_0_9310062804.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | date = 6 October 1993 | title = La Pausini: macchè solitudine, i fans mi assaltano | page = 33 | first = Gloria| last = Pozzi}}</ref>
Released by [[CGD Records]] in May 1993,<ref name="FirstAlbum"/> it sold 400,000 copies in Italy.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.elmundo.es/larevista/num157/textos/amore1.html | work = [[El Mundo (Spain)|El Mundo]] | language = es | access-date =24 July 2011 | title = La Revista: Desde Italia con Amore – Laura Pausini, La voz bonita | archive-date = 2 January 2008 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080102113555/http://www.elmundo.es/larevista/num157/textos/amore1.html}}</ref>

===1994–1995: Spanish-language debut and international success===
In February 1994, Pausini participated for the second time in the Sanremo Music Festival, competing in the "Big Artists" section with her entry "[[Strani amori]]".
The song ranked third in the competition, behind [[Aleandro Baldi]]'s "Passerà" and [[Giorgio Faletti]]'s "Signor tenente",<ref>{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/1994/02/27/vincono-baldi-bocelli.html | work = [[la Repubblica]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Vincono Baldi e Bocelli  | language = it | date = 27 February 1994 | author = Maria Pia Fusco | page = 28}}</ref> and became a hit in Italy,<ref>{{cite web | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=NQgEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA56 | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | page = 56 | date = 26 March 1994 | publisher = [[Nielsen Business Media, Inc]] | access-date = 9 August 2012|title = Hits of the World – Italy}}</ref> in the Netherlands<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.top40.nl/top40/1994/week-19 |publisher=[[Dutch Top 40]] |language=nl |access-date=9 August 2012 |title=Top40 week 19 van 1994 (07-05-1994) |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130611075632/http://www.top40.nl/top40/1994/week-19 |archive-date= 11 June 2013 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> and in Flanders.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.ultratop.be/nl/showitem.asp?interpret=Laura+Pausini&titel=Strani+amori&cat=s | publisher = [[Ultratop]] | language = nl | access-date = 8 September 2013 | title = Charts Vlaanderen Laura Pausini – Strani amori}}</ref> The single launched Pausini's second studio album, ''[[Laura (Laura Pausini album)|Laura]]'', released in February 1994. According to CGD Records, the album sold 150,000 copies in Italy in its first week, with initial shipments of 200,000 units.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=1219412 | work = [[La Stampa]] | title = Le vendite per una volta danno ragione alla classifica. During the summer of 1994, Pausini took part in the Italian itinerant TV show [[Festivalbar]], reaching the final stage of the music competition<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1994/agosto/06/Festivalbar_quattro_finalisti_co_0_9408068004.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | title = Festivalbar: quattro finalisti | date = 6 August 1994 | access-date = 2 September 2012 | page = 22}}</ref> and receiving the Premio Europa for her international success.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1994/settembre/04/Festivalbar_dopo_anni_Tozzi_trionfa_co_0_94090411455.shtml | title = Festivalbar, dopo 17 anni Tozzi trionfa di nuovo | date = 4 September 1994 | access-date = 2 September 2012 | language = it | page = 27}}</ref> In 1994 she was also awarded with her second [[Telegatto]], receiving the prize for Best Female Artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=1301159 |work=[[La Stampa]] |language=it |access-date=1 August 2011 |title=Su Canale 5 I Telegatti di Red e di Alba |date=14 September 1994 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120314004234/http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=1301159 |archive-date=14 March 2012 }}</ref> In the meanwhile, she started an Italian tour to promote her album.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/1994/05/27/dolce-laura-aiuto-il-successo-costa.html | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it | title = Dolce Laura: Aiuto, il successo costa caro | date = 27 May 1994 | access-date = 2 September 2012}}</ref>

=== 1996–1997: ''Le cose che vivi'' and World Wide Tour 1997 ===
Following the success obtained by Pausini's debut Spanish album, her third studio set was released on 12 September 1996 both in Italian and Spanish, under the titles ''[[Le cose che vivi]]'' and ''Las cosas que vives'', respectively.<ref name=LaStampaLeCoseCheVivi>{{cite web | url =http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=1611453 | work = [[La Stampa]] | date = 19 July 1996  |access-date = 8 September 2013 | page = 25 | title = Pausini e soci: i nuovi dischi|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120314004144/http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=1611453|archive-date=14 March 2012}}</ref><ref name=AdnkronosLeCoseCheVivi>{{cite web | url = http://www.adnkronos.com/Archivio/AdnAgenzia/1996/08/26/Spettacolo/MUSICA-ESCE-IN-32-PAESI-IL-DISCO-DELLA-PAUSINI_190600.php | publisher = [[Adnkronos]] | language = it | date = 26 August 1996 | title = Musica: esce in 32 Paesi il disco della Pausini | access-date = 20 September 2012}}</ref>
Starting from that moment, Pausini has recorded most of her songs both in her native language and in Spanish, in a practice that, according to [[Allmusic]]'s Jason Birchmeier, has "come to define her career and compound her success".<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web | url = http://www.allmusic.com/artist/laura-pausini-mn0000138235 |work = [[Allmusic]] | title = Laura Pausini: Biography | date = October 2009 | first = Jason| last = Birchmeier|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121021160045/http://www.allmusic.com/artist/laura-pausini-mn0000138235|archive-date=21 October 2012|access-date=7 September 2013}}</ref>
A special edition of the album was also released in Brazil, featuring three additional bonus tracks in Portuguese.<ref name=AdnkronosLeCoseCheVivi/>

The album was preceded by the single "[[Incancellabile]]", released to Italian radio stations on 26 August 1996<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1996/agosto/27/Laura_Pausini_settembre_alla_conquista_co_0_9608275922.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | access-date = 20 September 2012 | title = Laura Pausini da settembre alla conquista di 32 Paesi | page = 33 | date = 27 August 1996}}</ref> and titled "Inolvidable" in its version for the Hispanic market. ===1998–1999: ''La mia risposta'' / ''Mi respuesta'' and second World Tour===
In 1998, Pausini released her fourth studio album, ''[[La mia risposta]]'', together with its Spanish-language counterpart, ''Mi respuesta''. The album, which included a song penned by [[Phil Collins]],<ref name="StampaMiaRisposta">{{cite web|url=http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=2250188 |work=[[La Stampa]] |language=it |access-date=30 July 2011 |title=Dopo l'adolescenziale "La solitudine", un disco maturo con venature soul. Pausini, la metamorfosi: Canta Phil Collins, pensa agli Usa |first=Marinella |last=Venegoni |date=15 October 1998 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111005080920/http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=2250188 |archive-date=5 October 2011 }}</ref> was dubbed by Italian music critics as a mature work, with influences from [[soul music]],<ref name="StampaMiaRisposta"/> but, despite reaching the top spot of the [[Italian Albums Chart]],<ref>{{cite web | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=IQoEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA50 | publisher = [[Nielsen Business Media, Inc.]] | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date = 8 August 2012 | title = Hits of the World – Italy | page = 50 | date = 7 November 1998}}</ref> it was a moderate commercial success, selling two million copies worldwide.<ref name="LauraSales"/>
The lead single from the album, "[[Un'emergenza d'amore]]", was released in September 1998,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.adnkronos.com/Archivio/AdnAgenzia/1998/09/10/Spettacolo/LAURA-PAUSINI-EMERGENZA-DAMORE-DA-DOMANI-NELLE-RADIO_201100.php|publisher=[[Adnkronos]]|language=it|date=10 September 1998|title=Laura Pausini: Emergenza d'amore da domani nelle radio|access-date=8 September 2013}}</ref> and was followed by "[[In assenza di te]]" and "[[La mia risposta (song)|La mia risposta]]", the latter being performed during the [[Festivalbar]] in 1999.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.adnkronos.com/Archivio/AdnAgenzia/1999/06/21/Spettacolo/ITALIA-1-FESTIVALBAR_152900.php|publisher=[[Adnkronos]]|language=it|access-date=8 September 2013|date=21 June 1999|title=Italia 1: Festivalbar}}</ref>

To promote the album, Pausini began in early 1999 the [[La Mia Risposta World Tour '99]], during which she performed in theatres throughout Europe.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1999/marzo/02/Pausini_conquista_Firenze_Canto_anche_co_0_9903021395.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = La Pausini conquista Firenze "Canto anche Madre Teresa" | first = Emilia | last = Costantini | date = 2 March 1999}}</ref>
On 1 June 1999, she was one of the artists performing along with Italian [[tenor]] [[Luciano Pavarotti]] in [[Modena]] during his annual "Pavarotti and Friends" concert.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1999/maggio/01/Gianni_Morandi_detto_Festival_Pavarotti_co_0_9905016308.shtml | newspaper = [[Corriere della Sera]] | date = 1 May 1999 | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Gianni Morandi ha detto sì al Festival di Pavarotti | language = it}}</ref> Pavarotti and Pausini duetted in the Italian version of the [[aria]] "[[Dein ist mein ganzes Herz]]", titled "Tu che m'hai preso il cuor", from [[Franz Lehár]]'s [[operetta]] ''[[Das Land des Lächelns]]''. The live performance was later included in the album ''Pavarotti & Friends for the Children of Guatemala and Kosovo''.<ref name="PavarottiAndFirends">{{cite web | url = http://www.pop24.de/musik/musik-detail/product/73954/pavarotti-und-friends-for-the-children-of-guatemala-and-kosovo/| publisher = Pop24.de | access-date =8 September 2013 | title = Pavarotti & Friends – Pavarotti & Friends for the Children of Guatemala And Kosovo | language = de|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111001162952/http://www.pop24.de/musik/musik-detail/product/73954/pavarotti-und-friends-for-the-children-of-guatemala-and-kosovo/|archive-date=1 October 2011}}</ref>
In 1999 she also contributed the [[Richard Marx]] composition "[[One More Time (Laura Pausini song)|One More Time]]" to the ''[[Message in a Bottle (film)|Message in a Bottle]]'' soundtrack.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.rockol.it/news-5843/c-e-anche-la-voce-di-laura-pausini-nel-nuovo-film-di-kevin-costner | publisher = Rockol.it | access-date =8 September 2013| title = C'è anche la voce di Laura Pausini nel nuovo film di Kevin Kostner|date=2 April 1999}}</ref> The track was produced by [[David Foster]]<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.ultratop.be/fr/showitem.asp?interpret=Laura+Pausini&titel=One+More+Time&cat=s | publisher = [[Ultratop]] | language = fr | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini – One More Time (chanson)}}</ref> who was featured on piano.

===2000–2001: ''Tra te e il mare'' / ''Entre tú y mil mares'' and greatest hits album===
In 2000, she recorded the song "The Extra Mile" for the soundtrack of the movie ''[[Pokémon: The Movie 2000|Pokémon 2000: The Power of One]]''.<ref name="Repubblica15092000">{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2000/09/15/laura-pausini-canta-in-inglese-comincia-con.html | work = [[La Repubblica]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini canta in inglese e comincia con Pokemon | date = 15 September 2000 | first = Giacomo | last = Pellicciotti | language = it}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine | magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date = 15 July 2000 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=-g8EAAAAMBAJ&q=pausini+pokemon+soundtrack+billboard&pg=PA21 | title = Soundtracks and film score news | author = Charles Karel Bouley | access-date =30 July 2011}}</ref> The song was included in the album ''[[Tra te e il mare]]'', released on 11 September 2000 and preceded by the [[Tra te e il mare (song)|homonymous]] single, written by Italian pop singer [[Biagio Antonacci]].<ref name=TraTeEIlMareRelease>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2000/agosto/22/Esce_singolo_Laura_Pausini_firmato_co_0_0008228258.shtml | publisher = 30 July 2011 | title = Esce il singolo di Laura Pausini firmato da Biagio Antonacci | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | language = it}}</ref> Other singles from the album include "[[Il mio sbaglio più grande]]", which was a top 20 hit in Italy,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Laura+Pausini&titel=Il+mio+sbaglio+pi%F9+grande&cat=s|publisher=Italiancharts.com. Hung Medien|title=Italian Charts – Laura Pausini – Il mio sbaglio più grande (song)|access-date=8 September 2013}}</ref> and "Volevo dirti che ti amo", whose Spanish-language version "Quiero decirte que te amo" peaked at number 15 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Latin Pop Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/306634/laura%2Bpausini/chart?page=1&f=372 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=8 September 2013 |title=Laura Pausini Album & Song Chart History – Latin Pop Songs |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130908161519/http://www.billboard.com/artist/306634/laura%2Bpausini/chart?page=1&f=372 |archive-date= 8 September 2013 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref>
The album also features the songs "Viaggio con te", which was composed by her father and which was awarded in 2001 with an Italian Lunezia Award for Best Songwriter of the Year,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.premiolunezia.it/albo/index.htm | publisher = Premiolunezia.it | language = it| title = Premio Lunezia – Albo d'oro | access-date =8 September 2013|archive-date=12 February 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080212003147/http://www.premiolunezia.it/albo/index.htm}}</ref><ref name=FamigliaCristiana2000>{{cite web|url=http://www.stpauls.it/fc00/0039fc/0039fc96.htm |work=[[Famiglia Cristiana]] |language=it |access-date=8 September 2013 |first=Gigi|last=Vesigna|author-link=Gigi Vesigna |title=Spettacolo: Laura Pausini presenta il suo nuovo disco. Quella passione che ci unisce |date=1 October 2001 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130908160148/http://www.stpauls.it/fc00/0039fc/0039fc96.htm |archive-date= 8 September 2013 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> and "Per vivere", written from the point of view of a homeless child Pausini met in [[Rio de Janeiro]] and dedicated to two Brazilian children [[Child sponsorship|sponsored]] by her.<ref name=FamigliaCristiana2000/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.corriere.it/spettacoli/soundsandsummers/schede_artisti/pausini_1f61cba0-b7d2-11e1-a264-b99bbdd148d8.shtml|work=[[Corriere della Sera]]|language=it|access-date=8 September 2013|title=Laura Pausini|date=16 June 2012}}</ref>

''Tra te e il mare'' received a nomination for Album of the Year at the Premio Italiano della Musica, while Pausini was nominated for Best Female Artist during the same award ceremony,<ref name="PIM2001">{{cite web | url = http://www.rockol.it/news-28857/Rese-note-le-nominations-per-il-Premio-Italiano-della-Musica  | language = it | publisher = Rockol.it | access-date = 28 October 2011 | title = Rese note le nominations per il Premio Italiano della Musica | date = 16 February 2001}}</ref> as well as in the first edition of the [[Italian Music Awards]], held in February 2001.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.rockol.it/news-27502/Le-nomination-agli-Italian-Music-Awards--quasi-un-plebiscito-per-i-L%C3%B9napop | publisher = Rockol.it | access-date =8 September 2013| title = Le nomination agli Italian Music Awards: quasi un plebiscito per i Lùnapop | date = 12 January 2001|archive-date=7 July 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120707210924/http://www.rockol.it/news-27502/Le-nomination-agli-Italian-Music-Awards--quasi-un-plebiscito-per-i-L%C3%B9napop}}</ref>
A Spanish-language version of the album, titled ''Entre tú y mil mares'', was released shortly before the Italian-language edition, on 11 September 2000.<ref name=TraTeEIlMareRelease/> At the [[Latin Grammy Awards of 2001|2001 Latin Grammy Awards]], the record received two nominations for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Album|Best Female Pop Vocal Album]] and [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Engineered Album|Best Engineered Album]], while Pausini and Alfredo Cerruti were in the shortlist for [[Latin Grammy Award for Producer of the Year|Producer of the Year]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://articles.latimes.com/2001/jul/18/entertainment/ca-23444 | work = [[Los Angeles Times]] | title = The Full List of Nominations | date = 18 July 2001|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121026135501/http://articles.latimes.com/2001/jul/18/entertainment/ca-23444|archive-date=26 October 2012}}</ref><ref name=LatinGrammys2001>{{cite web | url = http://articles.latimes.com/2001/jul/18/entertainment/ca-23444/3 | work = [[Los Angeles Times]] | date = 18 July 2001 | access-date = 1 September 2012 | title = The Full List of Nominations (part 3)| url-status = dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121114011007/http://articles.latimes.com/2001/jul/18/entertainment/ca-23444/3|archive-date=14 November 2012}}</ref>

Pausini's first international [[greatest hits album]] was released in 2001, both in an Italian-language version and in a Spanish-language edition, titled ''[[The Best of Laura Pausini: E ritorno da te]]'' and ''Lo mejor de Laura Pausini: Volveré junto a ti'', respectively. The first single, "[[E ritorno da te (song)|E ritorno da te]]"—"Volveré junto a ti" in Spanish—was accompanied by a music video shot by Italian film director [[Gabriele Muccino]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.rockol.it/news-36261/Laura-Pausini,-il-suo-%27Best-of%27-esce-il-12-ottobre | publisher = Rockol.it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini, il suo 'Best of' esce il 12 ottobre | date = 16 September 2001 | language = it}}</ref> The album also includes the single "Una storia che vale" and features guest appearances by Brazilian singer [[Gilberto Gil]] in "Seamisai"<ref name="LaStampa9October2001">{{cite web | url = http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=3303853 | work = [[La Stampa]] | access-date = 30 July 2011 | title = Anche un CD in inglese con l'aiuto di Santana. Pausini: canto e non m'arrendo | first = Marinella | last = Venegoni | date = 9 October 2001 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20111005080939/http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=3303853 | archive-date = 5 October 2011 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> and by Italian singer [[Nek]], who plays bass in "[[Non c'è]]".<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.rockol.it/vetrina-529/Laura-Pausini-E-RITORNO-DA-TE---THE-BEST-OF-LAURA-PAUSINI| publisher = Rockol.it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini – E Ritorno da Te – The Best of Laura Pausini | language = it}}</ref>

Supported by the [[2001/2002 World Tour]], which started in Miami on 19 October 2001,<ref name="LaStampa9October2001"/> the greatest hits became one of Pausini's biggest commercial successes, selling 700,000 copies in Italy and 800,000 copies in France.<ref name="Sales">{{Cite magazine | magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | title = Laura Pausini matures with new studio set | access-date =27 July 2011 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=nRMEAAAAMBAJ&q=shipped+laura+pausini&pg=PA57 | first = Mark | last = Worden | date = 23 October 2003}}</ref>
During the concert she gave in [[Milan]] on 2 December 2001 as part of the tour, Pausini also recorded her first live [[video album]], titled ''[[Live 2001-2002 World Tour]]'' and released on 30 November 2002.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2003/gennaio/06/Boom_del_rock_vedere_Raddoppia_co_0_0301061855.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Boom del rock da vedere Raddoppia il mercato Dvd | first = Andrea | last = Laffranchi | date = 6 January 2003 | language = it}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.musicafilm.it/music-dvds/live-2001-2002-world-tour.html | publisher = Musicafilm.it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Live 2001–2002 World Tour – Laura Pausini|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120317052059/http://www.musicafilm.it/music-dvds/live-2001-2002-world-tour.html|archive-date=17 March 2012}}</ref>

===2002–2003: ''From the Inside'', the English-language debut===
In 2001, Pausini started working with producers such as [[Patrick Leonard]] and [[John Shanks]]<ref>{{cite web | url = {{BillboardURLbyName|artist=laura pausini|chart=all}} | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = From the Inside – Laura Pausini}}</ref> on her first English-language album, ''[[From the Inside (Laura Pausini album)|From the Inside]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2001/ottobre/09/Laura_Pausini_canta_inglese_co_0_0110092619.shtml| work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | author = Mario Luzzatto Fegiz | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Pausini canta in inglese | date = 9 October 2001}}</ref>
Released in Canada, Mexico and the United States by [[Atlantic Records]] on 5 November 2002, the album did not get the expected success, selling 50,000 copies in the U.S., according to Nielsen-Soundscan.<ref name="BillboardUSSales">{{cite magazine | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=MQ8EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA50 | magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | title = Primed for the U.S | first = Chuck | last = Taylor | access-date =27 July 2011 | date = 15 March 2011 | page = 50}}</ref>
The album singles "[[Surrender (Laura Pausini song)|Surrender]]" and "If That's Love" reached the top spot on the [[Hot Dance Club Songs]] Chart,<ref>{{cite web | url = {{BillboardURLbyName|artist=laura pausini|chart=Dance/Club Play Songs}} | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date =29 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini Album & Song Chart History – Dance/Club Play Songs}}</ref> but Pausini, disappointed at her English-language debut being ignored in the U.S. outside the club scene, abandoned the promotion for ''From the Inside'' due to her label promoting it as a dance album instead of a pop album as she requested.<ref name=Corriere>{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2003/gennaio/08/Pausini_americana_studiato_inglese_per_co_0_0301081590.shtml | title = Pausini l'americana: ho studiato inglese per scalare la classifica | access-date =30 July 2011 | first = Sandra | last = Cesarale | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | date = 8 January 2003}}</ref>
The album was later released in Europe too, selling 800,000 copies worldwide.<ref name="Sales"/>

In 2003 Luciano Pavarotti invited her for the second time to the "Pavarotti and Friends" concert, where they duetted again in "Tu che m'hai preso il cuor".<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.rockol.it/musicaitaliana.com/news/it/2003/03042903.html | publisher = Rockol.it | access-date = 30 July 2011 | title = Pavarotti & Friends, decima edizione: ecco il cast 2003 | language = it | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20110716033221/http://www.rockol.it/musicaitaliana.com/news/it/2003/03042903.html | archive-date = 16 July 2011 | df = dmy-all }}</ref>

===2004–2005: ''Resta in ascolto'' / ''Escucha'' and the Grammy===
[[File:LauraPausini.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Pausini during the presentation of her album [[Resta in ascolto|''Resta in ascolto'' / ''Escucha'']] in 2004]]

In October 2004 Pausini released her eighth studio album, ''[[Resta in ascolto]]'', and its Spanish-language counterpart, ''Escucha''. Influenced by international artists including [[Phil Collins]] and [[Celine Dion]],<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.billboard.com/album/laura-pausini/resta-in-ascolto/654563/review#/album/laura-pausini/resta-in-ascolto/654563/review | publisher = www.billboard.com | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Resta in Ascolto – Laura Pausini | first = Alex| last = Henderson}}</ref> the recording is on the subject of a break-up and was written in 2002, during her separation from her ex-boyfriend and producer Alfredo Cerruti.<ref name="Repubblica5February05">{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2005/02/05/pausini-che-grinta-non-posso-tacere-canto.html | work  = [[La Repubblica]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Pausini, che grinta 'Non posso tacere canto anche l'Iraq' | first = Lucia | last = Marchio | date = 5 February 2005}}</ref>

The album features the song "Mi abbandono a te", co-written by Pausini, [[Rick Nowels]] and [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]]. and the single "Benedetta passione", penned by Italian rock-star [[Vasco Rossi]].<ref name="Corriere23Sept04">{{cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2004/settembre/23/per_Laura_Pausini_inno_alla_co_9_040923072.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | date = 23 September 2004 | language = it | first = Andrea | last = Laffranchi | title = E per Laura Pausini inno alla pace scritto a quattro mani con Madonna}}</ref> Well received by music critics,<ref>{{cite web | url = The album debuted at number one on the [[Italian Albums Chart]]<ref>{{cite web  |url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2004/11/03/laura-pausini-prima-in-classifica.html | work = [[La Repubblica]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | language = it | date = 3 November 2004 | title = Laura Pausini prima in classifica}}</ref> and sold 350,000 copies in Italy.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.ilvaltrompianews.it/det_articolo.php?IdEd=19&IdArt=634 | publisher = www.ilvaltrompianews.it | language = it | access-date = 30 July 2011 | title = Word tour '05 con Laura Pausini | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20120328091133/http://www.ilvaltrompianews.it/det_articolo.php?IdEd=19&IdArt=634 | archive-date = 28 March 2012 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> Its Spanish version later won [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Female Pop Vocal Album|Best Female Pop Vocal Album]] at the [[Latin Grammy Awards of 2005]]<ref name="LatinGrammy2005">{{cite web | url = http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/60804/2005-latin-grammy-awards-winners | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date =29 July 2011 | title = 2005 Latin Grammy Awards Winners| date = 4 November 2005 }}</ref> and [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album|Best Latin Pop Album]] at the [[48th Grammy Awards]],<ref name="GrammyAward"/> making Pausini the first Italian female artist to win a [[Grammy Award]].<ref name="RepubblicaGrammy"/>
In January 2005, Pausini started [[World Tour '05|a new tour to promote the album]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.all4show.it/vis_dettaglio.php?primo_livello=menu&id_livello=235 |publisher=All4show.it |access-date=30 July 2011 |language=it |title=Date Tour Pausini 2005 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120720152726/http://www.all4show.it/vis_dettaglio.php?primo_livello=menu&id_livello=235 |archive-date=20 July 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> The concerts she gave at the [[Zénith de Paris]] on 22 and 23 March 2005 were filmed and released as a live album in November 2005, titled ''[[Live in Paris 05]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rockol.it/news-74656/Cd-e-DVD-live-per-Laura-Pausini |publisher=Rockol.it |language=it |access-date=8 September 2013 |title=Cd e DVD live per Laura Pausini |date=4 November 2005 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130908154436/http://www.rockol.it/news-74656/cd-e-dvd-live-per-laura-pausini |archive-date= 8 September 2013 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref>

The duet was placed on both the [[Compact Disc|audio CD]], and the full concert DVD that aired on [[Public Broadcasting Service|PBS]] as an episode of ''[[Great Performances]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rockol.it/recensione-3124/Michael-Bubl%C3%A9-CAUGHT-IN-THE-ACT|publisher=Rockol.it|language=it|access-date=15 November 2014|title=Michael Bublé – Caught in the Act – Recensione|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091108203835/http://www.rockol.it/recensione-3124/Michael-Bubl%C3%A9-CAUGHT-IN-THE-ACT|date=13 December 2005|archive-date=8 November 2009}}</ref>

At the [[Premios Lo Nuestro 2006|2006 Lo Nuestro Award]], Pausini was nominated in the sections Album of the Year for ''Escucha'', Song of the Year and Video of the Year for "Viveme"<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.hispanicbusiness.com/hispanicprwire/2005/12/13/univision_announces_premio_lo_nuestro_2006.htm| work = Hispanic Business | access-date =8 September 2013 | date = 13 December 2005 | title = Univision Announces 'Premio Lo Nuestro' 2006 Nominees|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120806013814/http://www.hispanicbusiness.com/2005/12/13/univision_announces_premio_lo_nuestro_2006.htm|archive-date=6 August 2012}}</ref> and won the award for Best Female Pop Artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.terra.com/musica/noticias/premios_lo_nuestro_2006_los_preferidos_de_los_usuarios_de_terra/oci74944 |publisher=[[Terra Networks]] |language=es |access-date=8 September 2013 |title=Premios Lo Nuestro 2006: Los Preferidos de los Usuarios de Terra |date=1 February 2006 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120911125828/http://www.terra.com/musica/noticias/premios_lo_nuestro_2006_los_preferidos_de_los_usuarios_de_terra/oci74944 |archive-date=11 September 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref>

===2006–2007: ''Io canto'' and the concert in San Siro===
[[File:Laura Pausini1 adjusted.jpg|thumb|right|180px|Pausini performing during the Festival Teatro-Canzone, in memory of Italian singer [[Giorgio Gaber]], in July 2007]]
In November 2006, Pausini released the album [[Io canto|''Io canto'' / ''Yo canto'']], consisting of covers of Italian [[pop rock]] songs.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.mtv.com/music/artist/pausini_laura/artist.jhtml#biographyEnd | publisher = MTV | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini – Full Biography}}</ref> On the album liner notes, Pausini wrote:
"here is the music I listen to when I'm at my saddest, or when I feel a moment is special, the songs I used to sing as a young girl when I first started performing, and above all those which taught me to love music, and how music can move you so deeply, regardless of its genre or style".<ref>{{cite web | url = https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/io-canto-spanish-premium-version/id203047936 | publisher = [[iTunes]] | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Io canto (Spanish Premium Version) by Laura Pausini}}</ref>

The album also features duets with [[Tiziano Ferro]], [[Juanes]] and [[Johnny Hallyday]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/B000IY04OU | publisher = www.amazon.co.uk | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Io Canto – Laura Pausini}}</ref> It debuted at number one on the [[Italian Albums Chart]] and held the top spot for 8 non-consecutive weeks.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Laura+Pausini&titel=Io+canto&cat=a | publisher = italiancharts.com | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini – Io canto (album)}}</ref> It also became the best-selling album of 2006 in Italy, selling 500,000 copies in less than two months.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/industry/global/homegrown-acts-dominate-2006-italy-record-1003530295.story | publisher = www.billboard.biz | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Homegrown Acts Dominate 2006 Italy Record Sales | first = Mark | last = Worden | date = 10 January 2007}}</ref> On 8 November 2007, the album won [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album, Female|Best Female Pop Vocal Album]] at the [[Latin Grammy Awards]].<ref name="LatinGrammy2007">{{cite web | url = http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1047366/juan-luis-guerra-dominates-latin-grammy-awards | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | title = Juan Luis Guerra Dominates Latin Grammy Awards| date = 8 November 2007 }}</ref>
Laura dedicated the award to the memory of Italian tenor [[Luciano Pavarotti]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=8080733 | work = [[La Stampa]] | language = it | access-date = 30 July 2011 | title = Latin Grammy Award Pausini vince e dedica il premio a Pavarotti | date = 10 November 2007 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20120314004221/http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=8080733 | archive-date = 14 March 2012 | df = dmy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.nytimes.com/2007/11/09/arts/music/09grammy.html?scp=11&sq=laura%20pausini&st=cse | work = [[The New York Times]] | access-date =13 August 2011 | date = 9 November 2007 | title = For One Dominican Songwriter, Top Music Honors | first = Jon| last = Pareles}}</ref> Later on during the show she sang "[[Vivere (Dare to Live)]]" alongside Italian singer [[Andrea Bocelli]].<ref name="LatinGrammy2007"/>

In Summer 2006, Pausini played a [[Juntos en concierto 2006|Juntos en concierto tour]] with [[Marc Anthony]] and [[Marco Antonio Solís]],<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/content_display/industry/news/e3i7b7c9db010011fac34dca14062def7c8 | publisher = www.billboard.biz | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Marc Anthony Plans Third Juntos Concert Series | date = 28 May 2008 | first = Leila| last = Cobo}}</ref> consisting of 20 concerts throughout the United States.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.adnkronos.com/Archivio/AdnAgenzia/2006/10/11/Spettacolo/Musica/MUSICA-LAURA-PAUSINI-ARRIVA-IL-NUOVO-SINGOLO-IO-CANTO-2_110450.php | publisher = [[Adnkronos]] | language = it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Musica: Laura Pausini, arriva il nuovo singolo Io canto | date = 11 October 2006}}</ref>

On 2 June 2007, Laura Pausini was the first female artist to play at the [[San Siro Stadium]] in Milan,<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.mtv.it/news/news-detail.asp?idnews=19488 | publisher = MTV | language = it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini a San Siro | date = 25 January 2007}}</ref> in front of a crowd of 70,000 spectators.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2007/06/04/un-trionfo-per-laura-pausini-settantamila-in.html | work = [[La Repubblica]] | language = it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Un trionfo per Laura Pausini settantamila in delirio a S.Siro | first = Gino | last = Castaldo | date = 4 June 2007}}</ref> On 30 November 2007, the concert was released on CD and DVD, under the title ''[[San Siro 2007]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.mtv.it/news/news-detail.asp?idnews=21877 | publisher = MTV | language = it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini in DVD | date = 20 November 2007}}</ref>

===2008–2010: ''Primavera in anticipo'', ''Amiche per l'Abruzzo'' and ''Laura Live''===
[[File:AmicheXlAbruzzo.jpg|thumb|left|250px|Concert of ''Amiche per l'Abruzzo'']]

Pausini spent the first months of 2008 recording her tenth studio album, [[Primavera in anticipo|''Primavera in anticipo'' / ''Primavera anticipada'']]. The Spanish language edition of the album was released on 11 November 2008,<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.eventosonline.cl/laura-pausino-lanza-primavera-anticipada-su-decimo-album-en-espanol/537.htm | publisher = www.eventosonline.cl | language = es | title = Laura Pausini lanza "Primavera Anticipada" su decimo album en español | date = 10 November 2008 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20090102064543/http://www.eventosonline.cl/laura-pausino-lanza-primavera-anticipada-su-decimo-album-en-espanol/537.htm | archive-date = 2 January 2009 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> while the Italian language edition was released in Italy on 14 November 2008.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.lastampa.it/cmstp/rubriche/stampa.asp?ID_blog=37&ID_articolo=1163 | archive-url = https://archive.today/20120904193917/http://www.lastampa.it/cmstp/rubriche/stampa.asp?ID_blog=37&ID_articolo=1163 | url-status = dead | archive-date = 4 September 2012 | work = [[La Stampa]] | language = it | access-date = 30 July 2011 | title = Il nuovo singolo della Pausini da venerdì in radio | date = 22 October 2008 }}</ref>
The album was preceded by the single [[Invece no|"Invece no" / "En cambio no"]], released on 24 October 2008<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.musicalnews.com/articolo.php?codice=13878&sz=5 | publisher = www.musicalnews.com | language = it | access-date = 30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini: Invece, no – il nuovo singolo in radio dal 24 ottobre | first = Laura | last = Gorini | date = 21 October 2008 | archive-url = https://archive.today/20120911173120/http://www.musicalnews.com/articolo.php?codice=13878&sz=5 | archive-date = 11 September 2012 | url-status = dead }}</ref> and promoted with an appearance in [[Spanish Steps|Piazza Trinità dei Monti]] in Rome on 14 November 2008.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://roma.repubblica.it/multimedia/home/3687367/21 | newspaper = [[La Repubblica]] | language = it| access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Piazza di Spagna, il concerto a sorpresa della Pausini | date = 14 November 2008}}</ref>
The album also features the single [[Primavera in anticipo (It Is My Song)|"Primavera in anticipo (It Is My Song)" / Primavera anticipada (It Is My Song)"]], a duet with British singer-songwriter [[James Blunt]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.amazon.com/dp/B001KQG4WQ | publisher = www.amazon.com | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Primavera anticipada (Spanish Version)}}</ref> Also in late 2008, French chansonnier [[Charles Aznavour]] and Pausini recorded Aznavour's 1965 song "Paris au mois d'août" for Aznavour's ''Duos'' album, both in French as well as the Italian version "Parigi in agosto". Pausini grew up listening to Aznavour's songs, and in a January 2009 interview on France 2's Vivement Dimanche hosted by [[Michel Drucker]], Aznavour said of Pausini, seated by his side after a live duet performance of "Paris au mois d'août", that "she knows the lyrics [to my songs] better than me."<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wmU1RSrtjcE | publisher = [[France 2]] | language = fr| access-date = 16 March 2014 | title = Laura PAUSINI & Charles AZNAVOUR Vivement Dimanche France 2 (18-01-2009) Paris au mois d'Août | date = 18 January 2009}}</ref> In November 2009 ''Primavera in anticipo'' went on to win Best Female Pop Vocal Album at the [[Latin Grammy Awards of 2009|Latin Grammy Awards]].<ref name="LatinGrammy2009">{{cite web | url = http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1263237/juan-gabriel-steals-show-calle-13-sweep-latin-grammys | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date =29 July 2011 | title = Juan Gabriel Steals Show, Calle 13 Sweep Latin Grammys | date = 5 November 2009 | first = Leila| last = Cobo}}</ref> In 2010 Pausini also won the [[Premio Lo Nuestro 2010|Lo Nuestro Award]] for Female Artist of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.univision.com/content/content.jhtml?chid=10383&schid=10646&secid=10649&cid=1794888 |publisher=www.univision.com |access-date=30 July 2011 |title=Premio Lo Nuestro – Premiados POP – Ganadores de Premio Lo Nuestro 2010 |language=es |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110714060805/http://www.univision.com/content/content.jhtml?chid=10383&schid=10646&secid=10649&cid=1794888 |archive-date=14 July 2011 }}</ref>

[[File:Laura Pausini 2009.04.30 025.jpg|thumb|Pausini performing during the World Tour 2009]]
On 21 June 2009, Pausini organized a mega-concert in the [[San Siro Stadium]] in Milan, raising money to support the victims of the [[2009 L'Aquila earthquake]]. In the meanwhile, on 5 March 2009, Pausini began her [[World Tour 2009]] in [[Turin]],<ref name="WorldTour09">{{cite web | url = http://www.musicalnews.com/articolo.php?codice=14725&sz=6 | publisher = Musicalnews.com | first = Laura | last = Gorini | title = Boom per il Laura Pausini World Tour. A CD of the tour, along with a DVD, was released on 27 November 2009 with the title [[Laura Live World Tour 09|''Laura Live World Tour 09'' / ''Laura Live Gira Mundial 09'']].<ref name="LauraLive">{{cite web | url = http://rockol.it/news-104319/Laura-Pausini,-Laura-Live--E-ci%C3%B2-che-pi%C3%B9-mi-rappresenta.-E-ora-mi-fermo... | publisher = Rockol.it | access-date =30 July 2011 | title = Laura Pausini, 'Laura Live': 'E' ciò che più mi rappresenta. ===2013–2014: ''20 – The Greatest Hits'' and World Tour===

The track was launched as a limited-edition digital single, available for purchase for a week only.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rockol.it/news-466116/Laura-Pausini,-edizione-speciale-di-'La-solitudine'-per-i-vent'anni-di-carriera |publisher=Rockol.it |language=it |access-date=7 September 2013 |title=Laura Pausini, edizione speciale di 'La solitudine' per i vent'anni di carriera |date=1 February 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130907200854/http://www.rockol.it/news-466116/laura-pausini-edizione-speciale-di-la-solitudine-per-i-vent-anni-di-carriera |archive-date= 7 September 2013 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref>

===2015–2016: ''Simili'', TV shows and ''Laura Xmas''===
[[File:Laura Pausini live @ Rio de Janeiro, 14 Sep 2016.jpg|thumb|left|Pausini during a show of the Latin American leg of the ''Simili'' World Tour, September 2016]]
At the [[Premio Lo Nuestro 2015|27th Lo Nuestro Awards]], Pausini received a special award in recognition of her music career trajectory.<ref name="lonuestro-winners">{{cite news|url=http://musica.terra.com.mx/lista-completa-de-ganadores-de-premio-lo-nuestro-2015,b4fd66a7135ab410VgnVCM4000009bcceb0aRCRD.html|title=Lista completa de ganadores de Premio Lo Nuestro 2015|work=[[Terra Networks]]|publisher=[[Telefónica]]|date=20 February 2015|access-date= 11 March 2015}}</ref> After performing a medley of songs including the salsa version of "[[Non c'è|Se fué]]" with [[Marc Anthony]], she stated that although she is Italian, "half of my heart beats Latino".<ref>{{cite news|title=Romeo Santos, el gran ganador de Premios Lo Nuestro 2015|url=http://elespecial.com/news/2015/feb/21/romeo-santos-el-gran-ganador-de-premios-lo-nuestro/?page=2|access-date=10 October 2015|work=El Especial|date=21 February 2015}}{{dead link|date=November 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

===2019-2021: LB Stadi Tour, Una. Nessuna. Centomila, and the Academy Award nomination===
In the Summer of 2019, Pausini performed 11 concerts throughout Italy, along with [[Biagio Antonacci]], as part of their collaborative show titled LB Stadi Tour. At the end of the tour, she announced a two years-brek from recording music, so she could listen to new melodies, to be with her daughter, and get some inspiration.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.it/entry/laura-pausini-si-prende-una-pausa-dalla-musica-per-due-anni-non-faro-dischi-mi-devo-un-attimo-fermare_it_5d162aaae4b082e5536858e9|work=[[The Huffington Post]]|language=it|title=Laura Pausini: Dopo il tour, prendo una pausa per due anni: non farò dischi. Ho bisogno di fermarmi|date=28 June 2019|access-date=26 August 2021}}</ref>

In 2020, she returned as a coach in the Spanish edition of ''[[La Voz (Spanish TV series)|La Voz]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lasprovincias.es/culturas/tv/la-voz-2020-coaches-asesores-20201030082244-nt.html|work=[[Las Provincias]]|language=es|access-date=26 August 2021|title=Quiénes son los coaches y asesores en La Voz 2020|date=30 October 2020}}</ref> 
On 5 September 2020, Pausini was set to perform a concert titled "Pausini BeMe" to celebrate the 25th anniversary of her official fan club. The Fan Club Party was later cancelled, due to the Italian Government's decision to forbid all live concerts through October 2020, as part of the measures to contain the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Italy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.optimagazine.com/2020/09/10/laura-pausini-festeggia-il-fan-club-per-i-25-anni-con-un-live-in-giardino-video/1920996|publisher=OptiMagazione|date=10 September 2020|access-date=26 August 2021|title=Laura Pausini festeggia il fan club per i 25 anni con un live in giardino (video)|author=Martina Dessì|language=it}}</ref>
In February 2020, while appearing as a guest in the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2020|70th Sanremo Music Festival]], she also announced a one-night-only concert in the RCF Campovolo in [[Reggio Emilia]], along with 6 other Italian female artists—[[Elisa (Italian singer)|Elisa]], [[Fiorella Mannoia]], [[Alessandra Amoroso]], [[Emma Marrone|Emma]], [[Gianna Nannini]], and [[Giorgia (singer)|Giorgia]]. The concert will be held to help women suffering from domestic violence. Titled "Una. Nessuna. Centomila", the event was later postponed to 11 June 2022 as a consequence of the Coronavirus pandemic in Italy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lastampa.it/spettacoli/musica/2021/04/16/news/una-nessuna-centomila-rinviato-al-2022-slitta-ancora-il-super-evento-di-pausini-co-al-campovolo-1.40159401|work=[[La Stampa]]|language=it|title=Una. Nessuna. Centomila rinviato al 2022, slitta ancora il super evento di Pausini & co al Campovolo|date=16 April 2021|access-date=26 August 2021}}</ref>

In October 2020, Pausini released "[[Io sì (Seen)]]", which was written in collaboration with [[Diane Warren]] and Niccolò Agliardi and was created for the [[Netflix]] feature film ''[[The Life Ahead]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-23|title=Diane Warren, Laura Pausini Release Original Song for 'The Life Ahead'|url=https://www.awardsdaily.com/2020/10/23/diane-warren-laura-pausini-release-original-song-for-the-life-ahead/|access-date=2020-11-01|website=Awardsdaily - The Oscars, the Films and everything in between.|language=en-US}}</ref> Pausini recorded the song in five languages.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Diane Warren On The Life Ahead's Original Song Contender 'Io Si (Seen)'|url=https://theplaylist.net/diane-warren-life-ahead-exclusive-20201030/|access-date=2020-11-01|website=theplaylist.net}}</ref> The music video for the single also features the film's star, [[Sophia Loren]].<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Davis|first1=Clayton|date=2020-10-30|title=Sophia Loren Could Break Oscar Acting Records With 'The Life Ahead'|url=https://variety.com/2020/film/awards/the-life-ahead-sophia-loren-oscars-chances-1234819984/|access-date=2020-11-01|website=Variety|language=en-US}}</ref> Thanks to the song, Pausini won the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song]], making "Io sì (Seen)" the first Italian-language song to win the prize, and was nominated for the [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]].<ref name="GG"/><ref name="oscar">{{cite web|first=Hugh|last=McIntyre|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2021/03/15/get-to-know-the-best-original-song-oscar-nominees/|title=Get To Know The Best Original Song Oscar Nominees|magazine=[[Forbes]]|date=15 March 2021|access-date=17 March 2021}}</ref>

In July 2021, Pausini announced she has been working on a film since February 2020. The film, directed by [[Ivan Cotroneo]] and set to be released on [[Amazon Prime Video]] in 2022, is based on an idea by Pausini herself. Pausini will also star in the film, thus debuting as an actress.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elle.com/it/showbiz/tv/a37093251/laura-pausini-film-amazon/|work=[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]]|language=it|title=Si è ufficiale, su Amazon è in arrivo un film su Laura Pausini e noi non vediamo l'ora|date=25 July 2021|access-date=26 August 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Pausini left her hometown in 1995, when she moved to [[Milan]] with her partner, manager and producer Alfredo Cerruti Jr.<ref name=laRepubblica14Dec1995>{{cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/1995/12/24/la-favola-di-laura-stella-dei-due.html | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it | access-date =13 August 2011 | title = La favola di Laura, stella dei due mondi | first = Laura | last = Putti | date = 24 December 1995}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2001/01/27/pausini-pensa-allamerica.html |work=[[la Repubblica]] |language=it |access-date=13 August 2011 |title=Pausini pensa all'America |date=27 January 2001 |first=Laura |last=Putti |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120518050444/http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2001/01/27/pausini-pensa-allamerica.html |archive-date=18 May 2012 }}</ref> Their relationship ended in 2002. Between 2002 and 2005 Pausini was romantically involved with her new manager, Gabriele Parisi.<ref name="Repubblica5February05"/>
She is currently engaged to the Italian guitarist, composer, music producer and former singer Paolo Carta.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.tgcom.mediaset.it/gossip/articoli/articolo284865.shtml | work = [[TGCOM]] | language = it | access-date = 13 August 2011 | title = Pausini torna dal suo chitarrista | date = 23 November 2005 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20101108221024/http://www.tgcom.mediaset.it/gossip/articoli/articolo284865.shtml | archive-date = 8 November 2010 | url-status = dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.tgcom.mediaset.it/spettacolo/articoli/1017053/pausini-paparazzata-sul-set-del-nuovo-video.shtml | work = [[TGCOM]] | language = it | access-date =13 August 2011 | title = Pausini paparazzata sul set del nuovo video | date = 29 July 2011}}</ref> On 15 September 2012, Pausini confirmed through her official Facebook page that she was expecting her first child with Carta.<ref>{{cite web|work=[[Il Secolo XIX]] |url=http://www.ilsecoloxix.it/p/magazine/2012/09/15/APL084RD-pausini_incinta_facebook.shtml |language=it |title=Pausini incinta, la foto su Facebook |date=15 September 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120918025859/http://www.ilsecoloxix.it/p/magazine/2012/09/15/APL084RD-pausini_incinta_facebook.shtml |archive-date=18 September 2012 |access-date=15 September 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref> Their daughter, Paola, was born on 8 February 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ilrestodelcarlino.it/ravenna/cronaca/2013/02/08/842942-laura-pausini-mamma-piccola-paola.shtml |work=[[Il Resto del Carlino]] |language=it |access-date=8 February 2013 |date=8 February 2013 |title=Laura Pausini è diventata mamma E' nata la piccola Paola |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130208210028/http://www.ilrestodelcarlino.it/ravenna/cronaca/2013/02/08/842942-laura-pausini-mamma-piccola-paola.shtml |archive-date= 8 February 2013 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref>

Pausini describes herself as a [[Catholic]] woman,<ref name="Repubblica15092000"/><ref>{{cite web | url = http://archivio.lastampa.it/LaStampaArchivio/main/History/tmpl_viewObj.jsp?objid=1084008 | work = [[La Stampa]] | language = it | access-date = 13 August 2011 | title = Laura Pausini. I just have a few doubts about the Catholic Church, such as the [[homophobia|discrimination against homosexuals]]. I don't understand how they condemn [[racism]], but at the same time take issue with gay people.}}
