"'''Vossi Bop'''" is a song by British rapper [[Stormzy]]. It was released as a single on 25 April 2019 through #Merky and [[Atlantic Records]] as the lead single from his second studio album, ''[[Heavy Is the Head (album)|Heavy Is the Head]]''. The music video was also released the same day. It is his first solo music since his ''[[Gang Signs & Prayer]]'' album in 2017.<ref name="BBC">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/av/entertainment-arts-48117631/stormzy-on-glastonbury-idris-and-his-new-single|title=Stormzy on Glastonbury, Idris and his new single|publisher=[[BBC]]|date=1 May 2019|access-date=3 May 2019}}</ref> The song debuted atop the [[UK Singles Chart]], becoming Stormzy's first number-one single.

==Background==
The song is Stormzy's first new music as a lead artist since 2017, when he released his UK number-one album ''[[Gang Signs & Prayer]]''. During his time away, he pursued interests outside of music.<ref name="BBC"/> Upon the song's release, Stormzy corrected several users on Twitter who had written the first line as "My bruddas don't die, we just [[Vossi]] bop", stressing that it was "[[Dab (dance)|dab]]" and not "die".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.capitalxtra.com/artists/stormzy/news/vossi-bop-lyrics-dab-die-confusion/|title=Stormzy Addresses Hilarious 'Vossi Bop' Lyrics Confusion On Twitter|website=[[Capital Xtra]]|date=1 May 2019|access-date=3 May 2019}}</ref>

==Music video==
The music video was directed by Henry Scholfield and released on 25 April 2019.<ref name="Noisey">{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/neanbb/stormzy-number-one-vossi-bop-stream-buy-2019|title=Stream "Vossi Bop," Buy "Vossi Bop" and Get Stormzy to Number 1|website=[[Noisey]]|last=Mokoena|first=Tshepo|date=30 April 2019|access-date=3 May 2019}}</ref> It features Stormzy rapping on [[Westminster Bridge]] and outside the [[Bank of England]].

==Remixes==
Four remixes of the song were released on 19 July 2019. Respectively, they featured Norwegian artist Lauren, Italian rapper [[Ghali (rapper)|Ghali]], Swedish duo Aden x Asme, and German rappers [[Bausa (rapper)|Bausa]] and [[Capo (rapper)|Capo]].

==Commercial performance==
On the midweek [[UK Singles Chart]], Stormzy was at number one, ahead of "[[Me!]]" by [[Taylor Swift]] featuring [[Brendon Urie]] by some 500 combined sales.<ref name="Noisey"/> Several media outlets, including the [[BBC]] and ''[[Noisey]]'', made note that it could be Stormzy's first UK number-one single.<ref name="Noisey"/><ref name="BBC"/> The song debuted at number one on the official chart ahead of previous number one, "[[Old Town Road]]" by [[Lil Nas X]], and Swift's "Me!" at numbers two and three, respectively.<ref name="UK"/> "Vossi Bop" also received 12.7 million streams in its debut week on sale, the highest ever by a rapper and the fifth-highest of all time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/stormzy-scores-debut-number-1-on-the-official-singles-chart-with-vossi-bop-words-don-t-really-do-it-justice-i-m-genuinely-for-once-in-my-life-speechless-__26241/|title=Stormzy scores debut Number 1 on the Official Singles Chart with Vossi Bop: 'Words don't really do it justice. I'm genuinely, for once in my life, speechless!'|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|last=Ainsley|first=Helen|date=3 May 2019|access-date=3 May 2019}}</ref>
