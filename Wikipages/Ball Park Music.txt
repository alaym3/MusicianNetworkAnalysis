'''Ball Park Music''' are a five-piece [[indie rock]] band from [[Brisbane]], [[Queensland]], Australia.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tweeddailynews.com.au/news/ball-park-music-to-tour-new-album/3047356/ |title=Ball Park Music to tour new album |work=[[Tweed Daily News]] |date=4 February 2016 |access-date=3 June 2020 |author=McKenzie, Daniel}}</ref> The band consists of lead vocalist and guitarist Sam Cromack, bassist and backing vocalist Jennifer Boyce, keyboardist and trombonist Paul Furness, guitarist and backing vocalist Dean Hanson, and guitarist and drummer Daniel Hanson (who are twin brothers).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fasterlouder.com.au/bands/Ball_Park_Music/ |title=Ball Park Music at FasterLouder |work=[[FasterLouder]] |date=27 January 2010 |access-date=3 June 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110807114253/http://www.fasterlouder.com.au/bands/Ball_Park_Music/ |archive-date=7 August 2011 |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theaustralian.com.au/arts/review/ball-park-music-rises-from-uni-beginnings-to-stardom/news-story/5a50477d5d0918aac0caf75714f820d9 |title=Ball Park Music rises from uni beginnings to stardom |work=[[The Australian]] |date=17 February 2018 |accessdate=4 February 2021 |author=McMillen, Andrew}}</ref>

Their 2011 single "It's Nice to Be Alive" was featured in the 2014 film ''[[Blended (film)|Blended]]'' and the [[Gossip Girl (season 5)|fifth season]] of ''[[Gossip Girl]]''.<ref name="WS">{{cite web |url=https://www.what-song.com/Artist/12589/Ball-Park-Music |title=Ball Park Music – TV & Movie Placements |publisher=WhatSong |date=23 May 2014 |access-date=25 June 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
{{More citations needed section|date=June 2020}}
===2008–2009: Formation and early years===
'''Ball Park Music''' was originally the name given to Sam Cromack's solo work. The band all met while they were studying music together at the Queensland University of Technology.

Ball Park Music independently released their debut EP ''Rolling on the Floor, Laughing Ourselves to Sleep'' in April 2009.<ref>{{Citation | date = 11 November 2009 | title = The Buzz today! | periodical = The Cairns Post }}</ref> The EP was quickly championed by [[triple j]] presenter Stephanie Hughes who gave an early version of "All I Want Is You" and "Black Skies" their first national airplay.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ball Park Music|url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/artist/ball-park-music|website=Triple J Unearthed|access-date=14 October 2015}}</ref> [[Richard Kingsmill]] said "[[Rufus Wainwright]] called. He wants his song back. So he should. This is a pretty good one." about their song "Black Skies" and gave it four out of five stars.

===2009–2010: ''Conquer the Town, Easy as Cake'' and "iFly"===
In 2010, Ball Park Music released their second EP ''Conquer the Town, Easy as Cake''. The EP spawned their first [[Triple J]] hit in "iFly" which immediately was added to high rotation and gained a five star rating from [[Richard Kingsmill]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Discover new Australian music|url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/|publisher=American Broadcasting Company|access-date=14 October 2015}}</ref> Another song "Western Whirl" was nominated for both Best Alternative and The Courier Mail People's Choice Awards at the 2010 Q Song Awards.<ref name="Caloundra">{{Citation | date = 27 October 2010 | title = Ball Park Music part of touring whirlwind | periodical = Caloundra Weekly }}</ref><ref>Q Music 22 July 2010, [http://www.qmusic.com.au/qsong/2010/index.cfm?contentID=451&articleID=9259 The 2010 Q Song Award Nominations]</ref>

In October 2010 the band embarked on their first Australian tour in October with [[Hungry Kids of Hungary]] and [[Big Scary]]<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.selectmusic.com.au/page/News_details?select=NewsList&rowid=2230 |title=Select Music News |access-date=18 September 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110706111924/http://www.selectmusic.com.au/page/News_details?select=NewsList&rowid=2230 |archive-date=6 July 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and released a brand new single "Sad Rude Future Dude"<ref name="mucho-bravado.com">{{Cite web |url=http://mucho-bravado.com/2010/10/14/ball-park-music-release-new-single-and-debut-live-show-around-the-country/ |title=Ball Park Music release new single and debut live show around the country |access-date=21 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120326032545/http://mucho-bravado.com/2010/10/14/ball-park-music-release-new-single-and-debut-live-show-around-the-country/ |archive-date=26 March 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> which was placed on rotation by [[Triple J|triple]] j.

In November 2010, they were named as a triple j New Crop artist – "one of the 20 best new bands in Australia" – and was also offered a slot at the [[triple j]] Ausmusic Month Party in Brisbane where they collaborated with Custard's [[David McCormack]] on a cover of [[Custard (band)|Custard]]'s hit song "Apartment". Following this, the band was announced as the Queensland winner of a [[triple j]] Unearthed competition and won the chance to play at [[Big Day Out]] 2011 on the Gold Coast. Additionally, the band played several festivals in summer of 2010/11 including [[Falls Festival]], South Bound, Sunset Sounds and Good Vibrations.

===2011: ''Happiness and Surrounding Suburbs''===
In June 2011, Ball Park Music released "Rich People Are Stupid" which was put on high rotation on triple j.<ref name="Bravado">{{cite web |url=http://mucho-bravado.com/ball-park-music-new-video-boy-bear-tour-support/ |title=Ball Park Music new video + Boy & Bear tour support |website=Mucho Bravado |date=2 November 2011 |access-date=3 June 2020}}</ref> On 24 June 2011, Ball Park Music signed to Sydney label Stop Start Music<ref name="Ball Park Music sign to Stop Start">{{cite web |url=http://old.themusic.com.au/newsletter/1481/p.street-press-logo |title=Your Daily SPA 24 June 2011 Ball Park Music sign to Stop Start |access-date=21 July 2012 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20121230192613/http://old.themusic.com.au/newsletter/1481/p.street-press-logo |archive-date=30 December 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref> to release their debut album. "It's Nice to Be Alive" was released in July 2011 and was once again supported by triple j.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jplay.com.au/song/|title=Song}}</ref> The video for the song achieved a Channel [V] Ripe Clip of the Week, beating out competition from [[The Kooks]]'s "Junk of the Heart" and [[Kanye West]]/[[Jay-Z]]'s "[[Otis (song)|Otis]]".<ref name="theinsoundfromwayout.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.theinsoundfromwayout.com/core/install.php|title=Choose language &#124; Drupal|website=www.theinsoundfromwayout.com}}</ref> "It's Nice to Be Alive" was featured in the film ''[[Blended (film)|Blended]]'' (2014).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://m.imdb.com/name/nm10171071/filmotype/soundtrack |title=Ball Park Music – IMDb |publisher=[[IMDb]] |year=2020 |access-date=3 June 2020}}</ref>

The band's debut studio album ''[[Happiness and Surrounding Suburbs]]'' was released in Australia and New Zealand on 9 September and debuted on the Australian ARIA Album Chart the following week at No. 36. Ball Park Music embarked on a four-date headline tour with Adelaide rock outfit [[City Riots]] on the ''How I Met Ball Park Music'' Tour which was followed by another headline tour to launch their debut album in October with [[Northeast Party House]] and [[The Jungle Giants]]. Immediately following that, they embarked as main support for [[Boy & Bear]] on their "Moonfire Tour".

In November, Ball Park Music were named the Unearthed Artist of the Year at [[triple j]]'s J Awards. They were also nominated for the 2011 J Award but were beaten by [[Gotye]] and his album ''[[Making Mirrors]]''.

In December it was announced that ''Happiness and Surrounding Suburbs'' was voted No. 10 in [[Triple J]]'s [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2014#Top 10 Albums of 2014|Top 10 Albums of 2014]] and No. 2 in ''The AU Review''{{'}}s album poll.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/kingsmill/albumpolls/ |title=Triple J Album Polls |access-date=3 June 2020}}</ref> The third single, "Literally Baby", was serviced to radio and TV, and it quickly achieved rotation on Nova Brisbane and [[Channel V|Channel [V]]].<ref name="novafm.com.au">{{Cite web |url=http://www.novafm.com.au/song/literally-baby |title=Nova FM Literally Baby |access-date=29 August 2012 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20121230160640/http://www.novafm.com.au/song/literally-baby |archive-date=30 December 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> To finish off the year, the band played [[Peats Ridge Festival]], [[Pyramid Rock]] and No Years on New Year's Eve in Brisbane.

===2012: ''Museum''===
Ball Park Music began 2012 by announcing The 180° Tour; "their most ambitious tour yet".<ref name="Ball Park Music's 180 Degree Tour">{{Cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/|title=Music News|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation}}</ref> They were initially to play 14 dates with supports [[Nantes]] and [[Cub Sport|Cub Scouts]] but added more shows in Brisbane, Sydney, Adelaide and Melbourne due to popular demand.<ref name="Ball Park Music's 3rd show due to popular demand!">[https://corner.ticketscout.com.au/gigs/453-ball-park-music---third-show Tonedeaf Jan 2011 3rd show due to popular demand!]</ref> The band was also added to the 2012 [[Groovin' the Moo]] line-up<ref name="Groovin' The Moo 2012 Line-up – Chiddy Bang Cancel, Ball Park Music Join">{{Cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/groovin-the-moo-2012-lineup-chiddy-bang-cancel-ball-park-music-join/|title=Groovin The Moo 2012 Line-up - Chiddy Bang Cancel, Ball Park Music Join|date=22 February 2012|website=Music Feeds}}</ref> as well as [[Splendour in the Grass]].<ref name="Splendour in the Grass 2012 Line-up">{{Cite web|url=http://www.beat.com.au/festivals/splendour-grass-2012-lineup|title=Musicfeed.com.au Feb 2012 Splendour in the Grass 2012 Line-up}}</ref>

On 23 March 2012, Ball Park Music were invited to do [[Triple J]]'s [[Like a Version]], where they performed their single "All I Want Is You" followed by a cover of the [[Flaming Lips]] song "[[Do You Realize??]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/media/s3462129.htm|title=Like A Version: Ball Park Music &#124; triple j Media|date=23 March 2012|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation}}</ref> This was also filmed for [[triple j tv]] at the time.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/like-a-version/|title=Like A Version|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation}}</ref>

On 20 July 2012, it was announced that the band would release new music in the form of a single called "Surrender" leading into their set at [[Splendour in the Grass]].<ref name="Surrender to the Power of Ball Park Music">{{Cite web|url=http://mucho-bravado.com/2012/07/20/surrender-to-the-power-of-ball-park-music/|title=Mucho Bravado July 2012 Surrender To The Power Of Ball Park Music}}</ref> In a radio interview on Triple J with [[Zan Rowe]], Cromack revealed that band were working on a new album, and they'd be releasing something "soon enough".<ref name="Album number two">{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/zan/blog/s3525880.htm|title=triple j June 2012 Sam Cromack of Ball Park Music Takes 5 with his guide to the 90's...}}</ref>

In August, Ball Park Music announced their second album, ''Museum'' and a headline tour with [[Loon Lake (band)|Loon Lake]] in support.<ref name="theinsoundfromwayout.com"/> In a press release, Sam Cromack said he was "inspired by the swift turn around in which the likes of [[The Beatles]] would deliver new records every six to 12 months" and issued the band a challenge to release another album in 12 months.<ref name="theinsoundfromwayout.com"/>

''Museum'' was released in Australia on 5 October 2012<ref>{{cite web|author=Paul Cashmere|work=Noise 11|title=Ball Park Music To Release Second Album Museum |url=http://www.noise11.com/news/ball-park-music-to-release-second-album-museum-20120824 |access-date=24 August 2012|date=24 August 2012}}</ref> and it was named [[Triple J]]'s Feature Album the following week.<ref>[http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/review/album/s3603966.htm Museum: triple j Feature Albums | Title: Museum: triple j music reviews]</ref> The album peaked at number 9 on the ARIA charts.

===2014–2015: ''Puddinghead''===
The band released their studio album ''[[Puddinghead]]'' on 4 April 2014 and the album peaked at number 2 on the ARIA charts. The album spawned the singles "[[She Only Loves Me When I'm There]]"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/au/album/she-only-loves-me-when-im-there/1512559635?i=1512559638|title=She Only Loves Me When I'm There (single)|website=Apple Music|date=14 February 2014|access-date=28 January 2021}}</ref> and "Trippin' the Light Fantastic".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/au/album/trippin-the-light-fantastic-single/1512536220|title=Trippin' the Light Fantastic (single)|website=Apple Music|date=23 May 2014|access-date=28 January 2021}}</ref>

===2016–2017: ''Every Night the Same Dream''===
The band's fourth studio album, ''[[Every Night the Same Dream]]'' was released on 19 August 2016. A change to their sound resulted in an album with more of a [[psychedelic rock]] feel, that was acclaimed by fans and critics and was toured nationally to sold out theatres across Australia.{{cn|date=January 2021}}

===2018–2019: ''Good Mood''===
The band's fifth studio album ''Good Mood'' was released on 16 February 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ballparkmusic.com/products/:identifier|title=Ball Park Music|website=Ball Park Music}}</ref> Ball Park Music subsequently embarked on a national tour in February and March being supported by Ali Barter and Hatchie. This was followed by a national tour in which they partnered with [[San Cisco]], [[Ruby Fields]] and Tia Gostelow. The single "[[Exactly How You Are]]" enjoyed success on [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2017|Triple J's Hottest 100 for 2017]] coming in at #18,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/17/countdown/1-100/ |title=Triple J's Hottest 100 |publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation |access-date=24 November 2018}}</ref> and the album track "Hands Off My Body" was added to the official [[NBA 2K19]] soundtrack curated by [[Travis Scott]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.newsweek.com/nba-2k19-soundtrack-release-spotify-tracklist-mix-travis-scott-1075334 |title='NBA 2K19' Soundtrack Released on Spotify With Tracklist Mixed By Travis Scott |author=Christopher Groux |date=15 August 2018 |work=Newsweek |access-date=24 November 2018}}</ref> Ball Park Music also released a limited edition 'Pizza Box' Boxed Set of vinyl LP's that contained their album catalogue from 2011's "[[Happiness and Surrounding Suburbs]]" to 2018's "[[Good Mood (album)|Good Mood]]". At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2018]], the album was nominated for three awards; [[ARIA Award for Producer of the Year|Producer of the Year]], [[ARIA Award for Engineer of the Year|Engineer of the Year]] and [[ARIA Award for Best Cover Art|Best Cover Art]]<ref name="ARIA Noms 2018">{{cite web | url = https://www.ariaawards.com.au/news/2018/2018-aria-award-nominees-announced | title = 2018 ARIA Award Nominees Announced | author = [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA) | publisher = Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) | date = 11 October 2018 | access-date = 14 October 2018 }}</ref>

===2020–present: ''Ball Park Music''===
The band's sixth album was announced with the title of ''Mostly Sunny'' in March 2020, alongside the release of single "Spark Up".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/ball-park-music-new-song-single-spark-up/12067698|title=First Spin: triple j textline reacts to new Ball Park Music stomper 'Spark Up!'|first=Al|last=Newstead|date=19 March 2020|website=triple j}}</ref> The following month, the band announced they were changing the title of the upcoming album to ''Ball Park Music''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/ball-park-music-are-changing-the-title-of-their-upcoming-album-2647785|title=Ball Park Music announce new title of their upcoming album &#124; NME Australia|date=23 April 2020}}</ref> A scheduled tour was cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Australia]], although the release of the self-titled album in October 2020 was followed by a [[concert residency]] at a venue in Brisbane.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/ball-park-music-announce-residency-at-the-triffid-for-brisbane-album-launch-2760985|title=Ball Park Music add two final dates to residency at The Triffid for Brisbane album launch &#124; NME Australia|date=30 September 2020}}</ref> The album included the track "Cherub", which was considered a strong contender to reach #1 in the lead-up to the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2020]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/the-race-for-1-in-triple-js-hottest-100-is-tight/|title=The Race For #1 In Triple J's Hottest 100 Is TIGHT|date=15 January 2021|website=Music Feeds}}</ref> It placed on the list at #4, beating their previous best, "Exactly How You Are", from 2017. On October 26th, 2021, the band released a new single "Sunscreen".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/ball-park-music-new-single-sunscreen/|title=Ball Park Music Release New Single ‘Sunscreen’ Just In Time For Summer|first=August|last=Billy|date=26 October 2021|website=music feeds}}</ref> Fans are eagerly awaiting a possible album drop.

===Other projects===
Sam Cromack now records his solo work under the moniker My Own Pet Radio and has released three solo albums, ''Suburban Lemon Shops and the Bruxer in My Bed'' (2009), ''Unidentified Flying Collection of Songs'' (2010) and ''Goodlum'' (2015).

Jennifer Boyce records solo work under the moniker Little Planes Land.
