'''Carlos St. John Phillips'''<ref>{{cite web |url=https://tsdr.uspto.gov/#caseNumber=88605875&caseSearchType=US_APPLICATION&caseType=DEFAULT&searchType=statusSearch |title=Trademark Status & Document Retrieval |access-date=21 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Saint Jhn |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/56017/saint-jhn/ |website=Official Charts |publisher=The Official UK Charts Company |access-date=8 July 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200331100855/https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/56017/saint-jhn/ |archive-date=31 March 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Saint Jhn – Profile |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/saint-jhn/profile/ |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=8 July 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200708011739/https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/saint-jhn/profile/ |archive-date=8 July 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref> (born August 26, 1986), known professionally as '''Saint Jhn''' (stylized as '''SAINt JHN'''; pronounced "Saint John"), is a Guyanese-American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer.<ref>{{cite web|date=21 March 2021|title=US-based Guyanese rapper SAINt JHN wins two Grammys|url=https://www.stabroeknews.com/2021/03/21/news/guyana/us-based-guyanese-rapper-saint-jhn-wins-two-grammys/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-24|website=StabroekNews.com|publisher=[[Stabroek News]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=21 March 2017|title=SAINt on Twitter|url=https://twitter.com/saintjhn/status/844326320196734977?s=21|url-status=live|website=Twitter|publisher=Saint Jhn}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=16 March 2021|title=Guyanese icon 'SAINt JHN' wins 2 Grammy awards|url=https://www.kaieteurnewsonline.com/2021/03/16/guyanese-icon-saint-jhn-wins-2-grammy-awards/|url-status=live|website=KaieteurNewsOnline.com|publisher=Kaieteur News}}</ref>  He released his first album, ''[[Collection One]]'', in March 2018.

He is best known for the 2019 [[deep house]] remix to his 2016 song "[[Roses (Saint Jhn song)|Roses]]". The remix, produced by Kazakh DJ [[Imanbek]], reached the top 5 of the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and topped the charts in Australia, the Netherlands, Ireland, New Zealand, and the United Kingdom. The song became the lead single for Saint Jhn's debut album, ''[[While the World Was Burning]]'' released in late 2020.

He has written songs for [[Jidenna]], [[Usher (musician)|Usher]], [[Hoodie Allen]], and [[Kiesza]], among others.<ref name="Interview">{{cite magazine | last=Hyman| first=Dan| title =Young rapper SAINt JHN goes with the flow| magazine =Interview Magazine| date =December 25, 2017 | url =https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/saint-jhn-usher-joey-badass-interview| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> He is a founding member of the Gødd Complexx music collective.<ref name="FADERRoses">{{cite magazine | last=Starling| first=Lakin| title =How Rapper Saint JHN Learned To Appreciate His Own Gifts| magazine =The FADER| date =October 7, 2016 | url =http://www.thefader.com/2016/10/07/saint-jhn-roses-premiere-interview| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref>

==Early life==
{{More citations needed section|date=June 2021}}
Phillips was born in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]] and raised in [[Georgetown, Guyana]]. Growing up, he split his time in three year intervals between Guyana and the neighborhood of [[East New York, Brooklyn|East New York]] in Brooklyn. He began creating music when he was twelve years old and was inspired by his older brother, who would rap in the neighborhood with friends. He wrote his first song in his first year of high school while living in Guyana.<ref name="FADERRoses"/><ref name="XXLBreak">{{cite magazine | last=C.M.| first=Emmanuel| title =The Break Presents: SAINt JHN| magazine =XXL| date =November 25, 2016 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/11/the-break-saint-jhn/| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Prior to adopting the Saint Jhn stage name, he performed and wrote using his birth name, Carlos St. John (or Carlos Saint John).<ref name="FADERRoses"/><ref name="PPRoses">{{cite news | title =Listen to Brooklyn Artist SAINt JHN's Intense "Roses"| publisher =Pigeons & Planes| date =July 22, 2016 | url =https://pigeonsandplanes.com/music/2016/07/saint-jhn-roses| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> In 2010, he released an EP, ''The St. John Portfolio'', and a mixtape, ''In Association'', under his birth name.<ref name="PPPortfolio">{{cite news | title =Carlos St. John - The St. John Portfolio (EP)| publisher =Pigeons & Planes| date =March 8, 2010 | url =https://pigeonsandplanes.com/news/2010/03/carlos-st-john-the-st-john-portfolio-ep| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref><ref name="PPAssociation">{{cite news | title =Carlos St. John - In Association (Mixtape)| publisher =Pigeons & Planes| date =December 25, 2010 | url =https://pigeonsandplanes.com/news/2010/12/carlos-st-john-in-association-mixtape| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> Soon after, he was flown to [[Los Angeles]], [[California]] by music executive Zach Katz. For two months, he wrote songs for [[Rihanna]], but none of his records were accepted. After returning home, Saint Jhn co-wrote the 2012 [[Hoodie Allen]] song, "No Interruption".<ref name="FADERRoses" /><ref name="DXAlbum">{{cite magazine | last=Wallace| first=Riley| title =SAINt JHN's Debut Album Took A Lifetime To Create| magazine =HipHopDX| date =March 30, 2018 | url =https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.46369/title.saint-jhns-debut-album-took-a-lifetime-to-create| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> In the following years, Saint Jhn wrote songs for [[Kiesza]], [[Gorgon City]], and [[Nico & Vinz]], among others.<ref name="AMCredits">{{cite web |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/carlos-st-john-mn0003315895|title=Carlos St. John - Credits|website=AllMusic |access-date=April 24, 2018}}</ref> In 2016, he earned a writer credit for the [[Usher (musician)|Usher]] songs, "[[Crash (Usher song)|Crash]]" and "[[Rivals (song)|Rivals]]", both of which appeared on the ''[[Hard II Love]]'' album.<ref name="DXAlbum"/> Also in 2016, he released his first song under the moniker, Saint Jhn, entitled "1999".<ref name="FADER1999">{{cite magazine | last=Starling| first=Lakin| title =SAINt JHN Lets Us In On His Journey With "1999"| magazine =The FADER| date =June 28, 2016 | url =http://www.thefader.com/2016/06/28/saint-jhn-1999-video| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> He followed that with two more songs in 2016, "[[Roses (Saint Jhn song)|Roses]]"<ref name="HNHHRoses">{{cite news | last=Smith| first=Trevor| title =SAINt JHN - Roses | publisher =HotNewHipHop| date =July 24, 2016 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/saint-jhn-roses-new-song.1970748.html| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> and "Reflex".<ref name="Yahoo">{{cite news | last=Moore| first=Jacob| title =Brooklyn's SAINt JHN Delivers Again With "Reflex"| publisher =Yahoo!| date =September 22, 2016 | url =https://www.yahoo.com/news/brooklyns-saint-jhn-delivers-again-170654916.html| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> In October 2016, it was announced that Saint Jhn would open up for [[Post Malone]] during a run of shows on the West Coast.<ref name="BWW">{{cite web | title =Rapper SAINt Jhn to Open for Post Malone on West Coast Run| website =Broadway World| date =October 31, 2016 | url =https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Rapper-SAINt-JHN-to-Open-for-Post-Malone-on-West-Coast-Run-20161031| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref>

In February 2017, Jidenna's album, ''[[The Chief (album)|The Chief]]'', was released featuring the song, "Helicopters / Beware", which Saint Jhn co-wrote.<ref name="DXAlbum"/> The following month, Saint Jhn released another original track, "3 Below".<ref name="XXL3">{{cite magazine | last=Hunter| first=Christopher| title =SAINt JHN Isn't Trying to Be a Hero on New Song "3 Below"| magazine =XXL| date =March 7, 2017 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2017/03/saint-jhn-3-below-new-song/| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> In October of that year, he played at two festivals, Rolling Loud and the [[Voodoo Music + Arts Experience|Voodoo Experience]]. He also released another new song, "Hermes Freestyle".<ref name="XXLHermes">{{cite magazine | last=C.M.| first=Emmanuel| title =Saint JHN Brags for New Song "Hermes Freestyle"| magazine =XXL| date =October 23, 2017 | url =http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2017/10/saint-jhn-hermes-freestyle/| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> In February 2018, Saint Jhn released "I Heard You Got Too Litt Last Night".<ref name="BBLitt">{{cite magazine | last=Aniftos| first=Rania| title =Saint JHN Delivers an Ode to the Wild Ones in 'Heard You Got Too Lit Last Night': Exclusive Premiere| magazine =Billboard| date =February 9, 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8097551/saint-jhn-heard-you-got-too-lit-last-night-premiere| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref><ref name="PFLitt">{{cite web | last=Balfour| first=Jay| title =SAINt JHN - "I Heard You Got Too Litt Last Night"| website =Pitchfork| date =February 13, 2018 | url =https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/saint-jhn-i-heard-you-got-too-litt-last-night/| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> In early March, he released the song, "Albino Blue",<ref name="PPAlbino">{{cite news | last=Moore| first=Jacob| title =SAINt JHN Drops "Albino Blue," Plans to Release Debut Album March 30| publisher =Pigeons & Planes| date = March 9, 2018| url =https://pigeonsandplanes.com/music/2018/03/saint-jhn-albino-blue| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref> and on March 30, 2018, his debut album was released. At that time, the already-released songs on the album had accrued 50 million total streams on various platforms. In addition to working on music and a tour in support of ''Collection One'', Saint Jhn was hired by [[Gucci]] as a model for its "Guilty" campaign alongside [[Adesuwa Aighewi]].<ref name="BBAlbum">{{cite magazine | last=Lyle| first=Ashley| title =Emerging Rapper SAINt JHN Talks Debut Project 'Collection One' and Being the 'Ghetto Lenny Kravitz'| magazine =Billboard| date =March 30, 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8269705/saint-jhn-interview-collection-one| access-date =April 24, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=SAINt JHN announces tour in support of debut album 'Collection 1'|url=https://www.axs.com/saint-jhn-announces-tour-in-support-of-debut-album-collection-1-129169|work=[[AXS (company)|AXS]]|date=March 28, 2018|access-date=August 23, 2018}}</ref> In April 2020, Saint Jhn's single "Roses" hit number one in the Australian singles chart as well as the UK singles chart, surged by the release of the [[Imanbek]] dance remix.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9366277/saint-jhn-the-smith-street-band-rule-australia-charts |title=SAINt JHN, The Smith Street Band Rule Australia's Charts |date=2020-04-27 |website=billboard.com |access-date=2020-04-27}}</ref> The song later received two other remixes, with [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and [[J Balvin]], respectively.<ref name="Star">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/10/saint-jhn-while-the-world-was-burning-features|title=Saint Jhn Unveils Star-Studded Feature List for Upcoming Album|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=Cowen|first=Trace William|date=October 25, 2020|access-date=October 26, 2020}}</ref>

In early October 2020, Saint Jhn was among the acts who performed live for the [[2020 Billboard Music Awards|2020 ''Billboard'' Music Awards]], following the success of "Roses".<ref name="Star" /> On October 23, 2020, he released the video for the single, "Gorgeous", which serves as the lead single for his third studio album, ''[[While the World Was Burning]]''. On October 26, he revealed the album's artwork, tracklist, and release date as November 20, 2020. The album includes the songs "High School Reunion, Prom", featuring [[Lil Uzi Vert]], "Monica Lewinsky, Election Year", featuring [[DaBaby]] and [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]], as well as "Pray 4 Me", featuring [[Kanye West]].<ref name="Star" />
