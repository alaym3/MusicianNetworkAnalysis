'''Bức Tường''' ("The Wall") is one of the first professional Vietnamese [[glam metal]] bands, formed in 1995 from Vietnam's [[National University of Civil Engineering]] in [[Hanoi]]. The band is considered to be the leader of Hanoi's rock scene.<ref>Dale Alan Olsen ''Popular Music of Vietnam: The Politics of Remembering'' 2008 "Bức Tường" pp100-104, 108–109, 127–128</ref><ref>Barley Norton ''Songs for the Spirits: Music and Mediums in Modern Vietnam ''- Page 51- 2009 "Following the karaoke craze in Vietnam in the mid-1990s, young popular musicians have found inspiration in diverse idioms including jazz, pop, rock, and rap: there is now a thriving rock scene in Hanoi led by the group Buc Tuong (The Wall);"</ref><ref>''Asian music'' journal of the Society for Asian Music: Volume 39 Society for Asian Music – 2008 "Love is a not a frequent subject for Vietnam's rock lyricists. Instead they engage, as one writer on an online bulletin board has said about Buc Tuong, with issues like "ideology" (tit tuong) and "education" (gido due), much as communist political ..."</ref>

== History ==
In early 1996, Bức Tường had the opportunity to introduce themselves to the students and national audience with the song "We are Bức Tường" or "We're the Wall" in the opening game of SV96 between three teams: [[Hanoi University of Technology]], [[National Economics University]], and [[National University of Civil Engineering]]. Then, four years later, in the SV2000 finale, with upbeat tunes, vibrant of "Bình minh sinh viên 2000" and "Đường tới ngày vinh quang" or "Road to the glory", Bức Tường had impressed the audiences. This event was remarkable and a turning point in their musical career.

The band was chosen as the representative of [[Vietnam]]ese contemporary music to attended the Festival "Vietnamese Faces – French Faces", which is organized in [[Cahors]] in the [[South of France]] from 26 September to 7 October 2003.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Wall dự Festival Cahors ở Pháp|url=http://vietbao.vn/Van-hoa/The-Wall-du-Festival-Cahors-o-Phap/20025683/181/|publisher=vietbao|accessdate=5 April 2013}}</ref>

On 25 April 2004, Bức Tường became the first band to perform a live concert in ''Music and friends of [[VTV3]]''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ban nhạc Bức tường "Nam tiến"|url=http://tuoitre.vn/Tuoi-tre-cuoi-tuan/Van-hoa-nghe-thuat/29083/ban-nhac-buc-tuong-nam-tien.html|accessdate=5 April 2013|newspaper=tuoitre|date=2004-04-17}}</ref>

In November 2005, Bức Tường was the first band in Vietnam (and only till this point) has a tour across Vietnam. 4 concerts "9 +" was continuously held in [[Hanoi]], [[Haiphong|Hai Phong]], [[Da Nang]] and [[Ho Chi Minh City]].

After nearly 12 years, on 2 December 2006 farewell concert called "The Last Saturday". It took place in front of 20,000 fans (it is consider as the largest concert with the biggest number of fans in all of Bức Tường's concerts) at the "Trung tâm Triển lãm Giảng Võ" or  "Giang Vo Exhibition Center" in [[Hanoi]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Ban nhạc Bức Tường biểu diễn live show cuối cùng|url=http://vietbao.vn/Van-hoa/Ban-nhac-Buc-Tuong-bieu-dien-live-show-cuoi-cung/65075166/107/|accessdate=5 April 2013|newspaper=vietbao|date=29 November 2006}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Huyen|first=Pham Thanh|title=Ban nhạc Bức Tường và "Ngày thứ 7 cuối cùng"|url=http://www.qdnd.vn/QDNDSite/vi-VN/61/43/6/6/65/5437/Default.aspx|accessdate=5 April 2013|newspaper=qdnd|date=2006-11-28|archive-url=https://archive.is/20120913193508/http://www.qdnd.vn/QDNDSite/vi-VN/61/43/6/6/65/5437/Default.aspx#|archive-date=13 September 2012|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Tran Lap's cancer and death==

In November 2015, the band's lead singer [[Trần Lập]] was diagnosed with [[colorectal cancer|rectal cancer]]. In January 2016, Bức Tường held their last live show with him, "Hand of Fire", in Hanoi, attracting 15,000 spectators. On 17 March 2016, five months after being diagnosed with cancer, Trần Lập passed away at his home in Hanoi.
