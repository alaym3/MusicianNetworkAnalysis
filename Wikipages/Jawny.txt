'''Jacob Lee-Nicholas Sullenger''' (born December 23, 1995),<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Jacob Leenicholas Sullenger, Born 12/23/1995 in California|url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/jacob_leenicholas_sullenger_born_1995_24046482|access-date=May 12, 2020|website=[[California Birth Index]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=June 6, 2014|title=Fort Mill High School to award 419 diplomas|url=https://www.heraldonline.com/news/local/community/fort-mill-times/article12000974.html|access-date=May 12, 2020|website=[[The Herald (Rock Hill)|Herald Online]]|publisher=Fort Mill Times}}</ref> known professionally by his stage name '''Jawny''' (stylized as '''JAWNY'''; formerly '''Johnny Utah'''), is an American singer, songwriter, and [[Record producer|producer]]. He first received recognition after the release of his 2019 single "Honeypie", and was signed to [[Interscope Records]] in January 2020. He made his major-label debut with the mixtape ''For Abby'' in late October 2020.

==Early life and education==

Jacob Sullenger was born and raised in the [[San Francisco Bay Area]].<ref name="Bill20">{{cite news |last=Ginsberg |first=Gab |title=New Interscope Signee Jawny on Why He Won't Just Be Another 'Viral' Moment|work=Billboard | date=14 February 2020 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8550829/jawny-signs-interscope-records-record-deal-interview |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> He first learned how to play guitar at age 6 and began composing songs in his early teens.<ref name="Diffus">{{cite AV media |people= Sullenger, Jacob (interviewee) |date=5 April 2020 |title=Jawny - Love Boy Pop Music From Los Angeles (Portrait) |medium=Video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1WqbcjReX-Y |access-date=8 July 2020 |publisher=Diffus }}</ref> He briefly attended college where he studied nursing, but dropped out to pursue music.<ref name="CUI">{{cite news |last=Titelbaum |first=Benny |title=Indie rockstars Goth Babe and Johnny Utah kickoff CU's Homecoming week|work=CU Independent | date=6 November 2019 |url=https://cuindependent.com/2019/11/06/cu-homecoming-goth-babe-and-johnny-utah/ |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> He also managed a pizzeria in [[New Jersey]] to pay bills.<ref name="Bill20"/>

==Career==

At age 20 in 2016, Sullenger moved to Philadelphia where he began making music under the moniker Johnny Utah. The pseudonym was a reference to [[Keanu Reeves]]' character in the 1991 film ''[[Point Break]]''.<ref name="Bill20"/>

In January 2018, he released a self-titled EP via Z Tapes Records.<ref name="DeliST">{{cite news |last=London |first=Dianca |title=The Deli Philly's February Record of the Month: Johnny Utah – Johnny Utah| work=The Deli Magazine| date=1 February 2018 |url=http://philadelphia.thedelimagazine.com/33512/deli-philly%E2%80%99s-february-record-month-johnny-utah-%E2%80%93-johnny-utah |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> He released several more singles that year, including "Folding Like Honey",<ref name="DeliHoney">{{cite news |title=New Track: "Folding Like Honey" - Johnny Utah|work=The Deli Magazine | date=1 May 2018 |url=http://philadelphia.thedelimagazine.com/34638/new-track-folding-honey-johnny-utah |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> "PATTY", and "Crazy For Your Love". His songs also appeared on the [[Spotify]] "Bedroom Pop" playlist.<ref name="Paper18">{{cite news |last=Wetmore |first=Brendan |title=Johnny Utah Will Never Stop Clowning Around|work=Paper | date=20 November 2018 |url=https://www.papermag.com/johnny-utah-2620751965.html |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> In November 2018, the tracks were compiled by Z Tapes Records into a second EP entitled ''Big Dogs''.<ref name="DeliDogs">{{cite news |title=New Johnny Utah EP Available for Streaming & Purchase|work=The Deli Magazine | date=28 November 2018 |url=http://philadelphia.thedelimagazine.com/38152/new-johnny-utah-ep-available-for-streaming-purchase |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> Another song from that EP, "Really Meant",<ref name="What">{{cite news |title=High Maintenance - S3E4 Soundtrack|work=WhatSong |url=https://www.what-song.com/Tvshow/186/High-Maintenance/e/113758 |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref> appeared in a 2019 episode of the [[HBO]] series ''[[High Maintenance]]''.<ref name="MilkHoney">{{cite news |last=La |first=Michelle |title=Johnny Utah is breaking out of his bedroom in "Honeypie"|work=EarMilk | date=25 April 2019 |url=https://earmilk.com/2019/04/25/johnny-utah-is-breaking-out-of-his-bedroom-in-honeypie/ |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref>

In April 2019, he released the single "Honeypie",<ref name="MilkHoney"/> which was later featured on the Spotify playlists, "Fresh Finds" and "Ultimate Indie". That exposure led to the song accruing millions of streams on the platform.<ref name="RS">{{cite news |last=Wang |first=Amy X. |title=How Do You Get a Hit Song? Spotify Says to Pitch It|work=Rolling Stone | date=27 February 2020 |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/pro/features/spotify-artists-playlist-submission-958384/ |access-date=8 July 2020 }}</ref>

Sullenger was signed to Interscope Records in January 2020<ref name="MilkWant">{{cite news|last=La|first=Michelle|date=2 March 2020|title=Jawny will do "Anything You Want" and we love him for it|work=[[Earmilk]]|url=https://earmilk.com/2020/03/02/jawny-will-do-anything-you-want-and-we-love-him-for-it/|access-date=8 July 2020}}</ref> and changed his stage name to Jawny around that time. He also relocated to [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="Bill20"/>

In February 2020, he released the single "Anything You Want".<ref name="MilkWant"/> He released his Interscope debut project, ''For Abby'', in October 2020.<ref name="OTWAbby">{{cite news|last=Scott|first=Ruby|date=27 October 2020|title=JAWNY Delivers a High-Spirited Mixtape in Major Label Debut 'For Abby'|work=[[Ones to Watch]]|url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/jawny-for-abby|access-date=1 November 2020}}</ref> Jawny described the 10-track project as a mixtape rather than an EP.<ref name="NMEAbby">{{cite news|last=Aubrey|first=Elizabeth|date=28 October 2020|title=Jawny – 'For Abby' review: one of lockdown's most innovative and colourful bedroom pop projects|work=[[NME]]|url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/reviews/album/jawny-for-abby-review-one-of-lockdowns-most-innovative-and-colourful-bedroom-pop-projects-2802737|access-date=1 November 2020}}</ref> One of the collection's singles, "Sabotage,"<ref name="Sabotage">{{cite news |title=JAWNY SHARES NEW TRACK 'SABOTAGE'|work=DIY Magazine | date=7 August 2020 |url=https://diymag.com/2020/08/07/listen-jawny-sabotage|access-date=1 November 2020 }}</ref> premiered at number 34 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Alternative Airplay]] chart in January 2021.<ref name="BillAA">{{cite news |title=Alternative Airplay|work=Billboard | date=23 January 2021 |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/alternative-airplay/2021-01-23|access-date=1 November 2020 }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Sullenger was in a romantic relationship with rapper, singer-songwriter, [[Doja Cat]] from mid 2019 to February 2020.
