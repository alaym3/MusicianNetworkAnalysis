'''Kim Owens''' (born July 23, 1969), better known by his stage name '''Kem''',<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kellman|first1=Andy|title=Kem Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/kem-mn0000087697|website=allmusic.com|publisher=All Media Network, LLC|access-date=2016-03-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Johnson|first1=Gwen B.|title=KEM: addicted to meth Hope|url=http://stylemagazine.com/news/2015/jul/14/kem-addiction-and-homelessness-25-years-sobriety-a/|website=stylemagazine.com|publisher=Minority Print Media, L.L.C.|access-date=2016-03-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Nero|first1=Mark Edward|title=Kem Biography|url=http://randb.about.com/od/classicartistsaz/p/Kem.htm|website=about.com|publisher=About Entertainment|access-date=2016-03-23}}</ref> is an [[United States|American]] [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]]/[[soul music|Soul]] [[Singing|singer]]–[[songwriter]] and [[record producer|producer]]. Born in [[Nashville]], [[Tennessee]], Kem was raised near [[Detroit]], [[Michigan]].<ref name="bio">[http://www.billboard.com/artist/305692/kem/biography Kem]. Billboards</ref>

==Career==
Kem wrote, produced, and financed his self-released debut album, ''[[Kemistry (album)|Kemistry]]'', with his [[American Express]] card and by singing top 40 cover tunes in a wedding band and waiting tables. He was signed by [[Motown Records]] in November 2001, which re-released the album on February 25, 2003. It sold more than 500,000 copies nationwide and was certified Gold by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]]. The album's only official single is "[[Love Calls (song)|Love Calls]]".

His second album, ''[[Album II (Kem album)|Album II]]'', was released on May 17, 2005, and was certified Platinum by the RIAA.  It includes the single "I Can't Stop Loving You", a #1 song at urban adult contemporary radio, and the song "You Might Win" featuring [[Stevie Wonder]] on harmonica. His third album, ''[[Intimacy: Album III]]'', was released on August 17, 2010, and was certified Gold by the RIAA.

In 2013, Kem was featured on "My Favorite Thing", with American recording artist [[Ronald Isley]] who Kem wrote and produced for. The song is the second single from Isley's album ''This Song Is For You''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1572964/ronald-isley-my-favorite-thing-exclusive-song-premiere | title=Ronald Isley, 'My Favorite Thing': Exclusive Song Premiere | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] | date=July 12, 2013}}</ref>

On August 25, 2014, Kem released his fourth studio album, ''[[Promise to Love]]''. He performed the [[Grammy Award|Grammy]]-nominated song "Nobody" from the album on the syndicated daytime talk show ''[[Steve Harvey (TV series)|Steve Harvey]]'' during the fourth season. On August 28, 2020, he released his fifth studio album, ''[[Love Always Wins]]'', which includes the singles "Lie To Me" and "Live Out Your Love" featuring [[Toni Braxton]].
