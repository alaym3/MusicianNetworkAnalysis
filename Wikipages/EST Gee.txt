'''George Albert Stone III''' (born May 11, 1994), known professionally as '''EST Gee''', is an American rapper and songwriter. He is signed to [[Yo Gotti]]'s [[Collective Music Group]] (CMG), [[Interscope Records]] and Warlike.<ref name="The Break Presents: EST Gee">{{Cite web|last1=March 3|first1=Robby Seabrook IIIPublished|last2=2021|title=The Break Presents: EST Gee|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/est-gee-interview-the-break/|access-date=July 23, 2021|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> He is perhaps best known for his feature on the 2021 single "[[Real as It Gets]]" by rapper [[Lil Baby]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=July 16, 2021|title=EST Gee Is Having a Breakout Year, So Why Is He So Apathetic?|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/est-gee-interview/|access-date=July 23, 2021|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref> His fifth mixtape, ''Bigger Than Life or Death'', released on July 21, 2021, and peaked at number 7 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sykes|first=Calvin|date=July 21, 2021|title=Louisville's EST Gee learned how to focus on the football field|url=https://theundefeated.com/features/louisvilles-est-gee-learned-how-to-focus-on-the-football-field/|access-date=July 23, 2021|website=The Undefeated|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=July 22, 2021|title=Jay-Z Told EST Gee Not To Bring His Ghostwriter To The Studio|url=https://uproxx.com/music/jay-z-est-gee-ghostwriter-studio/|access-date=July 23, 2021|website=UPROXX|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life ==
George Albert Stone III was born on May 11, 1994, in [[Louisville, Kentucky]] to George and Sheila Stone, and grew up at Tubman Court & Ellington Avenue in the southeastern region of the city.<ref>{{Cite web|title=George Stone III - Football|url=https://gosycamores.com/sports/football/roster/george-stone-iii/2870|access-date=July 27, 2021|website=Indiana State University Athletics|language=en}}</ref> After graduating [[St. Xavier High School (Louisville)|St. Xavier High School]] in 2012, Stone received a football scholarship to [[Indiana State University]] and majored in communications. He spent two years there before transferring to [[Stephen F. Austin University]]. Stone dropped out of college in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=George III - Football|url=https://sfajacks.com/sports/football/roster/george-iii/332|access-date=July 27, 2021|website=Stephen F. Austin State University Athletics|language=en}}</ref> He was arrested for drug trafficking in the same year and was sentenced to four months of house arrest.<ref name="The Break Presents: EST Gee"/><ref>{{Citation|title=EST Gee On Quitting Athletic Scholarship For Football To Facing 10 Years For Trafficking Marijuana|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z73lq_g-rBo|language=en|access-date=July 23, 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
During his house arrest, Stone became motivated to pursue a rap career after watching rapper [[Lil Baby]] perform on TV. He initially adopted the name "Big Gee" before changing it to "EST Gee", in which EST is an [[initialism]] for "Everybody Shines Together".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.shazam.com/track/403282394/stains|title=Shazam}}</ref> He released his debut song "Stains" as "Big Gee" on [[YouTube]] on December 17, 2017.<ref>{{Citation|title=EST Gee - Stains (Official Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M9rMhV6uK6Q|language=en|access-date=July 23, 2021}}</ref> He continued to release consistently through YouTube, gaining himself a local fanbase. He released his first and second mixtapes ''El Toro'' and ''Die Bloody'' in June and August 2019, respectively.

EST Gee would get mainstream attention with his third mixtape ''Ion Feel Nun'' on March 6, 2020, following it up with his fourth mixtape ''I Still Don't Feel Nun'' on December 18, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Vigil|first=Dom|title=EST Gee Shares New Project, "I Still Don't Feel Nun" {{!}} Prelude Press|url=https://preludepress.com/news/2020/12/18/est-gee-shares-new-project-i-still-dont-feel-nun/|access-date=July 23, 2021|language=en-US}}</ref> In January 2021, EST Gee signed a deal with [[Yo Gotti]]'s [[Collective Music Group]] (CMG), [[Interscope Records]] and Warlike. He featured on Lil Baby's single "[[Real as It Gets]]" which became his first [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] song, peaking at number 34.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Lil Baby Chart History (Hot 100)|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/lil-baby/chart-history/HSI|access-date=August 4, 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> His fifth mixtape ''Bigger Than Life or Death'' was released on July 21, 2021. It features guest appearances from [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Young Thug]], [[Pooh Shiesty]], [[Lil Durk]], and more.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Listen to EST Gee's new mixtape Bigger Than Life Or Death|url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/07/21/listen-to-est-gees-new-mixtape-bigger-than-life-or-death|access-date=July 23, 2021|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
EST Gee's mother died in March 2020 due to leukemia and his brother passed a week later in a shooting in Louisville.<ref name="youtube.com">{{Citation|title=EST Gee Talks About Getting Shot 5 Times, Louisville Police Not Wanting Him To Perform In The City|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UiKVd0aaRno|language=en|access-date=July 23, 2021}}</ref>

EST Gee was shot five times in September 2019 following a music video shoot, getting hit in the stomach four times and once on his left eye.<ref>{{Citation|title=EST Gee On Linking With Yo Gotti, Repping Kentucky, Getting Shot, New Project + More|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tfVZeQARstU|language=en|access-date=July 23, 2021}}</ref> His brother was hit in the leg. They were transported to a hospital and Gee was discharged after two weeks. Following surgery, Gee has retained most vision in his eye.<ref name="youtube.com"/>
