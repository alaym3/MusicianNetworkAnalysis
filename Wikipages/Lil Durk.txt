'''Durk Derrick Banks''' (born October 19, 1992), known professionally as '''Lil Durk''', is an American rapper and singer from [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://chicago.cbslocal.com/2013/06/05/rapper-lil-durk-charged-again-with-gun-violation/ |title=Rapper Lil Durk Charged Again With Gun Violation |website=CBS Chicago |date=June 5, 2013 |access-date=June 2, 2015}}</ref> He is the lead member and founder of the collective and record label [[Only the Family]] (OTF).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kollegekidd.com/news/lil-durk-loyal-fans-get-otf-tattoos/|title=Lil Durk's Loyal Fans Get OTF Tattoos|website=KollegeKidd |access-date=June 22, 2016 |date=April 28, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/02/lil-durk-still-on-def-jam/|title=Lil Durk Confirms He's Still On Def Jam After Leaked Song Contains Shots at Label|last=Weinstein|first=Max|website=[[XXL Magazine|XXL]]|publisher=Townsquare Media|date=February 29, 2016|access-date=June 22, 2016}}</ref> Durk garnered a cult following with the release of his ''[[Signed to the Streets]]'' mixtape series (2013–2014), leading to a record deal with [[Def Jam Recordings]]. The label would release his debut studio albums, ''[[Remember My Name (album)|Remember My Name]]'' (2015) and ''[[Lil Durk 2X]]'' (2016), before dropping the artist in 2018. In April 2020, Durk made his first appearance on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] with the single "[[Just Cause Y'all Waited 2|Viral Moment]]" off his fifth studio album, ''[[Just Cause Y'all Waited 2]]'' (2020). Lil Durk's commercial resurgence continued with singles such as "[[3 Headed Goat]]" (featuring [[Polo G]] and [[Lil Baby]]), "[[Backdoor (song)|Backdoor]]," and "[[The Voice (Lil Durk song)|The Voice]]"; his features on [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s 2020 single, "[[Laugh Now Cry Later]]" and [[Pooh Shiesty]]'s song, "[[Back in Blood (song)|Back in Blood]]", as well as his LPs, ''[[The Voice (Lil Durk album)|The Voice]]'' (2020) and his joint album with Lil Baby, ''[[The Voice of the Heroes]]'' (2021)—which became his first release to debut atop the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

== Early life ==
Born on October 19, 1992, Durk grew up with a great deal of responsibility at home as his father was incarcerated when he was seven months old.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/02/lil-durk-reunited-father-25-year-prison-sentence/|title=Lil Durk Reunited With His Father After 25 Years Behind Bars - XXL|last=Centeno|first=Tony M.|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=May 3, 2019 |date=February 14, 2019}}</ref> He recalls that there were times when he did not have enough food at home when he was younger. He started his first breakthrough on social media channels like [[Myspace]] and [[YouTube]], and grew fond of the idea of being a rapper as his online fan base started to expand.<ref name="chicagotribune.com"/> Durk took his career more seriously after becoming a father at 17 and dropping out of school at, [[Paul Robeson High School (Chicago)|Paul Robeson High School]], in order to join the [[Black Disciples]], a street gang in [[Chicago]].<ref name="chicagotribune.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/celebrity/ct-lil-durk-remember-by-name-20150601-column.html|title=Interview: Lil Durk on growing up on the South Side|last=Gomez|first=Luis|website=chicagotribune.com|language=en-US|access-date=May 3, 2019 |date=June 1, 2015}}</ref> Soon after joining, he began to get into trouble with the law and served time in October 2011 due to [[gun charges]], including possession of a firearm with a defaced serial number.<ref name="Nickeas">{{Cite web|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/local/breaking/chi-despite-vow-rapper-lil-durk-headed-back-to-court-on-another-gun-charge-20141105-story.html|title='He's not guilty,' Lil Durk's wife declares as rapper is jailed on gun charge|last=Nickeas|first=Rosemary Regina Sobol, Peter|website=chicagotribune.com|language=en-US|access-date=May 8, 2019 |date=November 6, 2014}}</ref> He later pleaded guilty to a reduced charge of aggravated unauthorized use of a weapon, according to court records.<ref name="Nickeas"/>

==Career==

===Career and rise to popularity===
In 2011, Banks would start taking music seriously.  After plans to sign with [[Chief Keef]] and [[French Montana]]'s labels fell through,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vladtv.com/amp/243699/lil-durk-on-why-he-left-def-jam-never-signed-a-contract-with-french|title=Lil Durk on Why He Left Def Jam, Never Signed a Contract with French Montana|website=www.vladtv.com |date=April 16, 2018}}</ref> he considered rapping as "a full-time career" after the releases of his two singles, "Sneak Dissin'" and "I'ma Hitta", each of which received generally positive feedback.<ref>{{cite web |last=Drake |first=David |title=Lil Durk Started When? | url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/11/who-is-lil-durk/growing-up-in-chicago | date=November 18, 2012 | work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] }}</ref> On October 19, 2012, Durk released his third mixtape, ''[[Life Ain't No Joke]]''. As of September 22, 2015, the mixtape has been downloaded over 216,000 times on [[DatPiff]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Durk – Life Aint No Joke Hosted by DJ Moondawg & DJ Victoriouz Mixtape – Stream & Download|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Lil-Durk-Life-Aint-No-Joke-mixtape.398485.html|website=[[DatPiff]]|date=October 19, 2012|access-date=June 22, 2016}}</ref> In December 2012, Durk released the track, called "L's Anthem", which was [[remix]]ed, featuring French Montana, as a re-release.<ref>{{cite web |last=Diep |first=Eric | title=Lil Durk to Feature Yo Gotti, Juelz Santana, Meek Mill & Future on "Ls Anthem (Remix)" | url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2012/12/lil-durk-to-feature-yo-gotti-juelz-santana-meek-mill-future-on-ls-anthem-remix/ | date=December 6, 2012 | work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] }}</ref>

===2013–2015: Signing to OTF and Def Jam Recordings and ''Signed to the Streets''===
{{main|Signed to the Streets|Signed to the Streets 2}}
Due to the popularity of "L's Anthem" and its follow-up single, "[[Dis Ain't What U Want]]" in 2013, Durk accepted a [[joint venture]] deal with his collective Only the Family and [[Def Jam Recordings]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/04/lil-durk-and-lil-reese-speak-on-signing-to-def-jam|title=Lil Durk and Lil Reese Speak on Signing to Def Jam|first=Jacob|last=Moore |work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=June 25, 2012|access-date=June 22, 2016}}</ref>

After a small number of delays, Durk's fourth mixtape ''[[Signed to the Streets]]'' was released on October 14, 2013, exclusively on DatPiff. It features two guest appearances from former Glo Gang affiliate [[Lil Reese]], along with production credits from Paris Bueller and [[Young Chop]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ortiz |first=Edwin |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/10/lil-durk-signed-to-the-streets-mixtape |title=Mixtape: Lil Durk "Signed To The Streets" |work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |date=October 10, 2013 |access-date=June 2, 2015}}</ref> Eight music videos were released to promote the mixtape: "Bars Pt. 2", "Oh My God", "100 Rounds", "Dis Ain't What U Want", "Bang Bros", "Traumatized", "Hittaz" and "Times". ''Signed to the Streets'' would later be named the eighth best mixtape of 2013 by ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-best-mixtapes-of-2013-20131209/lil-durk-signed-to-the-streets-19691231 |title=Lil Durk, 'Signed to the Streets' – 10 Best Mixtapes of 2013 |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=December 9, 2013 |access-date=June 2, 2015}}</ref> On October 22, 2013, in an interview with [[XXL (magazine)|XXL]], Lil Durk said he was working on his debut album under Def Jam.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/10/lil-durk-interview/ |title=Lil Durk Draws Inspiration From Chicago's Most Violent Neighborhoods |website=[[XXL Magazine|XXL]] |date=October 22, 2013 |access-date=June 2, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Sargent |first=Jordan |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/lil-durk-fuckery-delay-def-jam |title=Lil Durk Says Def Jam Put His "F**kery" Mixtape On Hold |website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |date=January 6, 2014 |access-date=June 2, 2015}}</ref> Also, Durk was named part of the 2014 XXL Freshmen Class.<ref>{{cite web|last=Sia|first=Niccole|title=XXL Freshmen 2014 Cover Revealed|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/05/xxl-freshmen-2014-cover-revealed/ |website=[[XXL Magazine|XXL]] |access-date=May 6, 2014}}</ref>

On July 7, 2014, Durk released his fifth mixtape, [[Signed to the Streets 2|the sequel to ''Signed to the Streets'']].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Lil-Durk-Signed-To-The-Streets-2-mixtape.620642.html |title=Lil Durk – Signed To The Streets 2 Hosted by DJ Drama & Don Cannon Mixtape – Stream & Download |access-date=June 2, 2015}}</ref>

===2015–2016: ''Remember My Name'' and ''Lil Durk 2X''===
{{main|Remember My Name (album)|Lil Durk 2X}}
On March 25, 2015, Durk announced the title of his debut studio album ''[[Remember My Name (album)|Remember My Name]]'' and revealed its original release date, May 12.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://respect-mag.com/2015/03/lil-durks-debut-album-remember-my-name-drops-may-12th/|title=Lil Durk's Debut Album "Remember My Name" Drops May 12th|website=[[Respect (magazine)|Respect]] |date=March 25, 2015}}</ref> On the same day, the album's first single, "[[Like Me (Lil Durk song)|Like Me]]", featuring [[Jeremih]], was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/like-me-feat.-jeremih-single/id978514389 |title=Like Me (feat. Jeremih) [Explicit] – Single by Lil Durk |website=[[iTunes Store]] (US) |access-date=July 17, 2015}}</ref> However, on April 20, 2015, Durk announced that the May 12 release date of ''Remember My Name'' was delayed; he also revealed the official album cover and announced that its release had been pushed back to June 2.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Zeichner|first1=Naomi|title=Lil Durk Announces Debut Album, Premieres "Like Me" Video With Jeremih|url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/04/20/lil-durk-jeremih-like-me-video|website=[[The Fader]]}}</ref>

On June 2, 2015, Durk's debut album, ''Remember My Name'', was released as planned. The album peaked at number fourteen on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with 28,000 [[Top Album Sales|equivalent album units]]; it sold 24,000 copies in its first week, with the remainder of its unit count attributed to [[Streaming media|streaming]] activity and [[Music download|track]] sales.<ref>{{cite web|last=Harling |first=Danielle |url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.34198/title.hip-hop-album-sales-lil-durk-jason-derulo-boosie-badazz |title=Hip Hop Album Sales: Lil Durk, Jason Derulo & Boosie Badazz |website=HipHopDX |date=June 10, 2015 |access-date=June 10, 2015}}</ref> Later that year, Durk released his sixth mixtape, ''300 Days, 300 Nights'', on December 15, 2015.<ref name=3D3N>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Lil-Durk-300-Days-300-Nights-mixtape.741374.html|title=Lil Durk – 300 Days 300 Nights Hosted by OTF Mixtape – Stream & Download |date=December 15, 2015 |access-date=January 22, 2016}}</ref> The only single released in promotion for the mixtape was "[[My Beyoncé]]", which features [[Detroit]] rapper [[Dej Loaf]]. The single was released on November 20, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/my-beyonce-feat.-dej-loaf/id1063896015 |title=My Beyoncé (feat. Dej Loaf) [Explicit] – Single by Lil Durk |website=[[iTunes Store]] (US) |date=December 2015 |access-date=June 22, 2016}}</ref> A music video for "My Beyoncé" had been released in early 2016.<ref>{{cite AV media |date=January 13, 2016 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i5LUMfJjW3Q |title=Lil Durk ft. Dej Loaf - My Beyoncé (Explicit) [Official Video] |publisher=LilDurkVEVO |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=November 16, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210130172301/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i5LUMfJjW3Q |archive-date=January 30, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> On June 5, 2017, the single was certified [[RIAA certification|gold]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] for single-equivalent units of over 500,000 copies.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=My+Beyonce#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum - RIAA|website=riaa.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://kollegekidd.com/news/lil-durk-and-dej-loaf-my-beyonce-certified-gold/|title=Lil Durk and Dej Loaf's 'My Beyonce' Certified Gold |date=June 7, 2017|website=kollegekidd.com}}</ref>

Lil Durk released his second album, ''[[Lil Durk 2X]]'', on July 22, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/lil-durk-2x/id1118089192|title=Lil Durk 2X [Explicit] by Lil Durk|website=[[iTunes Store]] (US) |access-date=June 22, 2016}}</ref> Previously, he released the first single for the album, "She Just Wanna", featuring [[Ty Dolla Sign|Ty Dolla $ign]], in May.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/she-just-wanna-feat.-ty-dolla/id1115923483|title=She Just Wanna (feat. Ty Dolla $ign) by Lil Durk on iTunes|publisher=[[Apple Inc.]]|date=May 24, 2016|access-date=June 22, 2016}}</ref> Other follow-up singles for the album include: "Money Walk", featuring [[Yo Gotti]], and "True". The album, unfortunately, failed to meet the same standards as his previous album, ''Remember My Name'', peaking at number twenty-nine on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/lil-durk/chart-history/tlp/|title=Lil Durk Lil Durk 2X Chart History|website=billboard.com}}</ref>

Later that November, Durk released his seventh mixtape, ''[[They Forgot]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/11/lil-durk-they-forgot-mixtape/|title=Premiere: Listen to Lil Durk's 'They Forgot' Mixtape f/21 Savage, Jadakiss, and More|website=[[XXL Magazine|XXL]]|date=November 26, 2016}}</ref> The mixtape features guest appearances from Lil Reese, [[Meek Mill]], [[Mozzy]], OTF Ikey, [[21 Savage]], [[Hypno Carlito]], Dej Loaf, YFN Lucci, and [[BJ the Chicago Kid]]. While the [[Hip hop production|production]] was handled by [[C-Sick]], ChopSquad DJ, Donis Beats, DP Beats, LeekeLeek, Kid Wonder, [[London on da Track]], TY Made It, and Young Chop.<ref>{{cite web |last=Morris |first=Jessie |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2016/11/lil-durk-new-mixtape-premiere-they-forgot|title=Lil Durk Premiere 'They Forgot'|website=[[Complex Magazine|Complex]]|date=November 26, 2016}}</ref> The mixtape was supported by the single "Baller".<ref>{{cite web|last=Walker|first=Angus|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-durk-baller-new-song.1971974.html|title=Lil Durk - "Baller"|website=HotNewHipHop|date=November 18, 2016|access-date=November 27, 2016}}</ref> On December 7, 2016, Durk's single, "Like Me", from his debut studio album, ''Remember My Name'', was certified gold by the RIAA.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=Lil+Durk#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum - RIAA|website=riaa.com}}</ref>

The album peaked at number four on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] in the United States.

===2020–present: ''Just Cause Y'all Waited 2 & The Voice'' ===
{{main|Just Cause Y'all Waited 2|The Voice (Lil Durk album)}}
His fifth album, ''[[Just Cause Y'all Waited 2]]'', followed on May 8, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lil Durk to Release 'Just Cause Y'all Waited 2' Album in May|url=https://thesource.com/2020/04/28/lil-durk-just-cause-yall-waited-2/|last=Grant|first=Shawn|date=April 28, 2020|website=[[The Source]]|access-date=May 7, 2020}}</ref> The album contains his second highest-charting song, "[[3 Headed Goat]]", featuring [[Polo G]] and [[Lil Baby]]. Welcome To KollegeKidd.com|date=May 7, 2014|website=kollegekidd.com}}</ref> However, this would force Game to respond with "Bigger than Me", which he targets Durk, 40 Glocc, 2014 XXL 's Freshman Class and many countless others.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7518026/the-game-rap-beef-history-meek-mill-50-cent|title=An In-Depth History of The Game's Many Hip-Hop Beefs|website=billboard.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1847302/game-bigger-than-me/|title=Game Says He's The Black Eminem On New Single 'Bigger Than Me': Listen|website=mtv.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bet.com/news/music/2014/06/20/game-talks-bigger-than-me-dissing-xxl-freshman-class.amp.html|title=Game Talks 'Bigger Than Me,' Dissing XXL 's Freshman Class|website=bet.com}}</ref> Just eight months following, Durk squashed his feuds with Game and a week later with Tyga.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sohh.com/after-killing-game-feud-lil-durk-ends-another-beef-that-st-aint-st-in-my-eyes/|title=After Killing Game Feud, Lil Durk Ends Another Beef: "That S**t Ain't S**t In My Eyes" – SOHH.com|date=July 8, 2014|website=sohh.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapbasement.com/lil-durk-2/070814-lil-durk-squashes-beef-tyga.html|title=Lil Durk Squashes Beef With Tyga - Rap Basement|date=July 8, 2014|website=rapbasement.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapbasement.com/lil-durk-2/070414-lil-durk-speaks-ending-feud-game.html|title=Lil Durk Speaks On Ending Feud With The Game - Rap Basement|date=July 4, 2014|website=rapbasement.com}}</ref>

In his adolescence, Durk and [[Famous Dex]] attended Paul Robeson High School, but after they both dropped out, the two seemingly lost contact.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kollegekidd.com/news/famous-dex-reveals-he-went-to-high-school-with-lil-durk/|title=Famous Dex Reveals He Went To High School With Lil Durk - Welcome To KollegeKidd.com|date=June 23, 2015|website=kollegekidd.com}}</ref> But after Durk became famous, Dex immediately revealed in an interview that he never saw him in his later days soon after, until they finally got a chance to meet with one another during the [[BET Awards 2016|2016 BET Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kollegekidd.com/news/famous-dex-talks-fbg-beef-and-bumping-into-lil-durk-at-bet-awards/|title=Famous Dex Talks FBG Beef and Bumping Into Lil Durk At BET Awards - Welcome To KollegeKidd.com|date=June 30, 2016|website=kollegekidd.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite AV media |date=June 30, 2016 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9t5LBhLPL6s |title=Famous Dex: "I was never in FBG, bumping into Lil Durk was a relief" |publisher=SAY CHEESE! |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=November 16, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201005201743/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9t5LBhLPL6s |archive-date=October 5, 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref> Durk also admitted that he was still "rockin'" with Dex.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kollegekidd.com/news/lil-durk-rocking-with-famous-dex/|title=Lil Durk Rocking With Famous Dex - Welcome To KollegeKidd.com|date=August 4, 2016|website=kollegekidd.com}}</ref> Musician Lil' Mister also alleged a collaborative project between the two rappers was in development.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopsrevival.com/interviews/lil-mister-hints-project-durk-famous-dex-talks-ending-beef-opps/|title=Lil Mister hints project with Durk & Famous Dex, talks ending beef with opps - Hip Hops Revival|date=September 10, 2016|website=hiphopsrevival.com}}</ref>

Welcome To KollegeKidd.com|date=December 24, 2016|website=kollegekidd.com}}</ref> Durk also released a remix to Meek Mill's "We Ball" with Tay's cousin, Booka600.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-durk-drops-off-a-remix-to-meek-mills-we-ball-with-booka600-new-song.1975723.html|title=Lil Durk Drops Off A Remix To Meek Mill's "We Ball" With Booka600|website=hotnewhiphop.com|date=September 19, 2017}}</ref> Infuriated of the fact that his ex-friend collaborated with a rival, Tay600 responded with his own remix of "We Ball", calling Durk's collective Only the Family "[[faggot (slang)|fags]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vladtv.com/amp/234245/tay600-disses-lil-durk-on-we-ball-remix|title=Tay600 Disses Lil Durk on 'We Ball' Remix|website=www.vladtv.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://kollegekidd.com/news/tay600-disses-lil-durk-in-we-ball-remix/|title=Tay600 Disses Lil Durk In 'We Ball' Remix - All For The Culture Show Episode 19|website=player.fm}}</ref> He also bragged that he was involved in a short-lived sexual relationship with the mother of two of Durk's six children.<ref name="HL">{{cite web|title=Tay600 & Lil Durk's Feud: Tay Says He F**ked His Baby Mama – Hollywood Life|url=http://hollywoodlife.com/2017/09/19/tay600-lil-durk-feud-fighting-slept-with-baby-mama-tweets/|author=Wilson, Samantha|date=September 19, 2017|access-date=April 18, 2018|website=Hollywood Life}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://bossip.com/1293293/heres-what-happened-when-rapper-lil-durk-threatened-to-take-his-kids-from-their-mothers-on-instagram/|title=Here's What Happened When Rapper Lil' Durk Threatened To Take His Kids From Their Mothers On Instagram…|date=March 16, 2016|website=bossip.com}}</ref>
