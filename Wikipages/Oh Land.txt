'''Nanna Øland Fabricius''' (born 2 May 1985),<ref>{{cite web |author=Oh Land |url=https://twitter.com/OHLANDmusic/status/65035084587208704 |title=Hey shorty it's my birthday! |publisher=[[Twitter]] |date=2 May 2011 |access-date=18 December 2014}}</ref> better known by her stage name '''Oh Land''', is a Danish musician, singer, songwriter, dancer and recording artist.

==Early life==
Oh Land was born in [[Copenhagen]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ohlandmusic.com/bio/ |title=Bio |publisher=ohlandmusic.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110717235348/http://www.ohlandmusic.com/bio/ |archive-date=17 July 2011 |access-date=23 March 2011}}</ref> the daughter of Bendt Fabricius,<ref>{{cite web |last=Svidt |first=Ole Rosenstand |url=http://gaffa.dk/anmeldelse/32729 |title=Oh Land : med Lyngby-Taarbæk Symfoniorkester, Spot Festival 2009, Musikhuset, Symfonisk Sal, Århus |language=da |work=[[Gaffa (magazine)|Gaffa]] |date=22 May 2009 |access-date=7 May 2011 |quote=... Oh Lands far, den klassiske komponist Bendt Fabricius ...}}</ref> an [[organist]] (not to be confused with the composer [[Bent Fabric|Bent Fabricius Bjerre]]), and Bodil Øland,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://cdon.dk/musik/oh_land/fauna-4050830 |title=Fauna – Oh Land |language=da |publisher=CDON.dk |access-date=7 May 2011 |quote=... Nannas egen mor, operasangerinden Bodil Øland.}}</ref> an [[opera]] singer.<ref name="944.com">{{cite web |last=Sterling |first=Scott T. |url=http://www.944.com/articles/oh-land-the-exploration-of-a-rising-pop-star/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707071057/http://www.944.com/articles/oh-land-the-exploration-of-a-rising-pop-star/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=7 July 2011 |title=The Exploration of A Rising Pop Star |work=[[944 Magazine]] |date=15 February 2009 |access-date=23 March 2011 }}</ref> She is the great-great-grandchild of Lutheran missionary and ethnographer [[Otto Fabricius]], who published ''Fauna Groenlandica'' in 1780, the first zoological observations of [[Greenland]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Jakobsen |first=Ole |url=http://www.dr.dk/Musik/Artikler/2008/11/12162107.htm |title=Oh Land – sådan et land hvor man bliver overrasket |language=da |publisher=[[DR (broadcaster)|DR]] |date=November 2008 |access-date=6 May 2011 |quote=... min tipoldefar havde skrevet en bog om Grønlands dyreliv, der hed "Fauna Grønlandica".}}</ref> She is a former student of the [[Royal Danish Ballet|Royal Danish]] and [[Royal Swedish Ballet]] schools.<ref>{{cite web |last=Smith |first=Douglas Q. |url=http://culture.wnyc.org/articles/music-hub/2010/nov/08/gig-alert-oh-land/ |title=Gig Alert: Oh Land |publisher=[[WNYC]] |date=8 November 2010 |access-date=24 February 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110321040646/http://culture.wnyc.org/articles/music-hub/2010/nov/08/gig-alert-oh-land/ |archive-date=21 March 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> However, an injury caused by a [[spinal disc herniation|slipped disc]] and [[spinal fracture]] put an end to her dancing career, which eventually led her to start making music.<ref name="944.com"/> She moved to [[New York City]] in January 2010 where she lived in the [[Williamsburg, Brooklyn|Williamsburg]] neighbourhood of [[Brooklyn]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Chapman |first=Alex |url=http://www.urb.com/2010/11/20/urb-presents-oh-land/ |title=URB Presents: Oh Land |work=[[URB (magazine)|URB]] |date=20 November 2010 |access-date=3 July 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101122102144/http://www.urb.com/2010/11/20/urb-presents-oh-land/ |archive-date=22 November 2010 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

==Career==

===2008–11: Debut, ''Fauna'' and ''Oh Land''===
[[File:Oh Land in Dublin 02.jpg|thumb|right|Oh Land performing in [[Dublin]], 2011]]
Oh Land's debut album, ''[[Fauna (album)|Fauna]]'', was released in her native Denmark on 10 November 2008 by Danish independent label Fake Diamond Records.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fakediamond.dk/#/231751/ |title=Oh Land – Fauna |publisher=Fake Diamond Records |access-date=3 April 2011}}</ref> For her [[Oh Land (album)|eponymous follow-up album]], she worked with producers [[Dan Carey (music)|Dan Carey]], [[Dave McCracken]] and [[Lester Mendez]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/oh-land-mw0002105560/credits |title=Oh Land – Oh Land : Credits |publisher=[[Allmusic]]. [[Rovi Corporation]] |access-date=4 May 2011}}</ref> The album was released on 14 March 2011<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fakediamond.dk/#/478969/ |title=Oh Land – Oh Land (album) |publisher=Fake Diamond Records |access-date=3 April 2011}}</ref> and peaked at number five on the [[IFPI Denmark|Danish Albums Chart]].<ref name="den">{{cite web |url=https://danishcharts.dk/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Oh+Land |title=Discography Oh Land |publisher=danishcharts.dk. Hung Medien |access-date=15 August 2011}}</ref> It also served as her debut in the United States, where it was released on 15 March 2011 by [[Epic Records]],<ref>{{cite web |last=Ennis |first=Jillian |url=http://fashion.elle.com/culture/2011/03/11/oh-lands-new-album-drops-next-week/ |title=Oh Land's New Album Drops Next Week |work=[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]] |publisher=[[Hachette Filipacchi Media U.S.|Hachette Filipacchi Media]] |date=11 March 2011 |access-date=3 April 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110317055945/http://fashion.elle.com/culture/2011/03/11/oh-lands-new-album-drops-next-week/ |archive-date=17 March 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> reaching number 184 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref name="bb200">{{cite web |url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=oh land|chart=Billboard 200}} |title=Oh Land – Chart history: Billboard 200 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |access-date=27 March 2011}}</ref> An EP with the same name was previously released on 19 October 2010, containing four tracks from the full-length album.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fakediamond.dk/#/445502/ |title=Oh Land – Oh Land EP |publisher=Fake Diamond Records |access-date=3 April 2011}}</ref> On 7 April 2011, she was given the Brink of Fame: Music Artist award at the 2011 [[NewNowNext Awards]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://my.news.yahoo.com/photos/singer-oh-land-accepts-brink-fame-music-artist-photo-20110407-232834-869.html |title=Singer Oh Land accepts the Brink of Fame: Music Artist award at the 2011 NewNowNext Awards in Los Angeles on Thursday, April 7, 2011. ===2012–13: ''Wish Bone''===
On 20 May 2013, "Renaissance Girls" was released as the lead single from Oh Land's third studio album, ''[[Wish Bone (album)|Wish Bone]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/dk/album/renaissance-girls-single/id645537251 |title=Renaissance Girls – Single by Oh Land |publisher=[[iTunes Store]] Denmark. [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] |access-date=16 June 2013}}</ref><ref name="idolator">{{cite web |last=Williott |first=Carl |url=http://idolator.com/7474573/oh-land-renaissance-girls |title=Oh Land's "Renaissance Girls": Hear The 'Wish Bone' Lead Single |publisher=Idolator. Spin Media |date=31 July 2013 |access-date=5 August 2013}}</ref> The album was released in Denmark on 16 September 2013,<ref>{{cite web |last=Gonzalez |first=Michael Jose |url=http://gaffa.dk/anmeldelse/75207 |title=Oh Land: Wishbone |language=da |work=Gaffa |date=14 September 2013 |access-date=21 September 2013}}</ref> and will be released in the United States on 24 September via [[Dave Sitek]]'s boutique label Federal Prism.<ref name="idolator"/><ref>{{cite web |last=McGovern |first=Kyle |url=https://www.spin.com/2013/07/oh-land-my-boxer-wish-bone-stream/ |title=Hear Oh Land's Sinewy 'My Boxer' |work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |date=10 July 2013 |access-date=5 August 2013}}</ref> The second single "Pyromaniac" was released on 2 September 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/dk/album/pyromaniac-single/id688269793 |title=Pyromaniac – Single by Oh Land |publisher=iTunes Store Denmark. Apple |access-date=18 September 2013}}</ref> On 20 September Oh Land kicked off her Wish Bone tour at the Music Hall of Williamsburg.  The show featured special guests Sun Rai and (Who Is) Anastasia,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.whoisanastasia.com |title=(Who Is) Anastasia |access-date=23 April 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141024091404/http://www.whoisanastasia.com/ |archive-date=24 October 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> who will both also appear with her at Gramercy Theatre on 24 September.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.songkick.com/artists/784566-oh-land/calendar|title=Oh Land|author=AroundSound|work=Songkick|access-date=24 October 2014}}</ref>

===2014: Acting, ''Voice Junior'' and ''[[Earth Sick]]''===
Oh Land also stars in the 2014 Danish western, ''[[The Salvation (film)|The Salvation]]'', as Marie, alongside [[Mads Mikkelsen]], [[Eva Green]] and [[Eric Cantona]], which premiered at the 2014 Cannes Film Festival on 17 May 2014.<ref>{{cite news|title=The Salvation (2014)|url=http://tuppencemagazine.co.uk/entertainment-news/the-salvation-2014/|access-date=15 June 2014|newspaper=Tuppence Magazine|date=26 May 2014}}</ref>

On 28 June 2014, Oh Land revealed plans for her fourth record, which would be recorded in her Brooklyn apartment and [[crowdfunded]] in collaboration with [[PledgeMusic]];<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/OhLandMusic/posts/10152203119225924|title=Oh Land - Oh Land shared a link. - Facebook|access-date=24 October 2014}}</ref> part of the proceeds would also be donated to [[Greenpeace]]'s [[Save the Arctic]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pledgemusic.com/projects/ohland|title=Oh Land|work=PledgeMusic|access-date=24 October 2014}}</ref> The album was ultimately titled ''[[Earth Sick]]'', and its lead single "Head Up High" was released on 6 October 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soundvenue.com/musik/2014/10/oh-land-head-up-high-117142|title=Oh Land ‘Head Up High’|author=Christian Wolkoff|work=SOUNDVENUE.COM|access-date=24 October 2014}}</ref> Oh Land debuted "Head Up High" live on 23 September 2014 on Denmark's ''[[Voice – Danmarks største stemme|Voice Junior]]'', for which she serves as a coach. The music video for "Head Up High" was released on 20 October 2014, with a world premiere on the ''[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]]'' website, which described it as "a victorious electro-jam smattered with twinkling bells and grounding percussion" and while that could be used to describe other songs, they felt the new track still demonstrated Oh Land's evolution and maturation as an artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nylon.com/articles/video-premiere-oh-land-head-up-high|title=Video Premiere: Oh Land "Head Up High" - NYLON MAGAZINE|access-date=24 October 2014}}</ref> ''Nylon'' also announced that ''Earth Sick'' would be released on 11 November 2014 on Oh Land's Tusk or Tooth label. On 27 October, the title track from the album was made available to download for people who had pre-ordered the album on her pledge site, and the following day it was released as the instant download track on other retailers.

===2015–present: Soundtracks and ''Family Tree''===
On 23 November 2015, Oh Land was announced as composer for the [[pantomime]] of [[Cinderella]] on [[Tivoli Pantomime Theatre]], royal ballet with [[Margrethe II of Denmark|Queen Margrethe II]] as [[scenographer]] and the award-winning Russian [[choreographer]] Yuri Possokhovs. On 4 November 2016, Oh Land first soundtrack album, "[[Askepot (soundtrack)|Askepot (Musikken fra forestllingen i Tivolis Pantomime teater)]]", was released digitally.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://politiken.dk/ibyen/scene/ECE2944333/oh-land-og-dronningen-saetter-askepot-op-i-tivoli/|title=Oh Land og dronningen sætter 'Askepot' op i Tivoli|publisher=Ibyen|access-date=9 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tivoli.dk/da/teater/2016/askepot|title=Askepot - Klassisk eventyrballet med dekorationer og kostumer af Dronning Margrethe|publisher=Tivoli.dk|access-date=9 November 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161110110947/http://www.tivoli.dk/da/teater/2016/askepot|archive-date=10 November 2016|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

On 2017 Oh Land collaborated with her husband, visual artist Eske Kath, in the interactive art exposition "SKIBET", available at [[Kunsthallen Nikolaj|Nikolaj Kunsthal]] from August 23, 2017 until January 7, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nikolajkunsthal.dk/da/udstillinger/skibet
|title=ESKE KATH & OH LAND: SKIBET NIKOLAJ KUNSTHAL 23. AUGUST 2017 TIL 7. JANUAR 2018|publisher=NikolajKunsthal.dk|access-date=20 January 2018}}</ref> On 30 March 2018 she released the soundtrack album from the exhibition, "[[Watermusic]]". In 2018 Oh Land recorded "Der Var Et Yndigt Land", a modified version of [[Der er et yndigt land|Denmark's national anthem]], to serve as the theme of the Netflix series [[The Rain (TV series)|The Rain]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.flixfilm.dk/hor-oh-land-fortolke-nationalsangen-i-the-rain/
|title=Hør Oh Land fortolke nationalsangen i ‘The Rain’|publisher=FlixFilm.dk|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref> 
On 27 May 2018 Oh Land performed at 50º [[Frederik, Crown Prince of Denmark|Danish Crown Prince]]’s birthday celebration on live tv. She played with the DR symphony orchestra , [[livgarden]], the DR junior choir and 25 dancers as well as musicians Adi Zukanovic and Hans Hvidberg at the Danish Royal Arena in Copenhagen.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/musik/saa-du-den-oh-lands-saerlige-overraskelse-til-kronprinsen
|title=Så du den? Oh Lands særlige overraskelse til kronprinsen|publisher=Dr.dk|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref> On July 2018 she became an ambassador from [[World Wide Fund for Nature|WWF]]´s Wild Schoolprojekt and travelled to [[Kenya]] to an action from the project.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billedbladet.dk/kendte/danmark/musik/oh-land-hjaelper-i-afrika
|title=Oh Land hjælper i Afrika|publisher=BilledBladet.dk|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref>

On 11 February 2019 she released the single "Human Error" and announced her new album "Family Tree" to be released 3 May 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/oh-land-announces-new-album-releases-lead-single-human-error
|title=Oh Land announces new album, releases lead single “Human Error”|publisher=TheLineOfBestFit.com|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref> That year, she also made her debut as a judge on the [[X Factor (Denmark season 12)|twelfth season]] of Danish TV show ''[[X Factor (Danish TV series)|X Factor]]''.

Oh Land wrote the music to a musical installment of the [[Hans Christian Andersen|H.C. Andersen]] Fairy Tale "[[The Snow Queen]]", premiering on 1 December 2019 in [[Tivolis Koncertsal|Tivoli Concert Hall]]. Consumes were designed by Queen Margrethe II.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dr.dk/nyheder/kultur/dronningen-har-lavet-kostumer-til-snedronningen|title=Dronningen har lavet kostumer til Snedronningen|last=Sommerand|first=Malthe|date=19 November 2019|work=DR|access-date=19 November 2019|language=da}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 2016, Oh Land moved back to Denmark.<ref name=365nyhede>{{cite web|url=http://365nyheder.dk/2016/02/07/oh-land-er-flyttet-hjem-til-soeborg/|title=Oh Land er flyttet hjem til Søborg|language=da|website=365nyheder.dk|access-date=17 August 2017}}</ref> She married artist and designer [[Eske Kath]] in 2013 at a zoo in Denmark.<ref>{{cite web |last=Lanham|first=Tom|url=http://archives.sfexaminer.com/sanfrancisco/nature-girl-oh-land-is-blazing-new-trails-on-the-streets-of-new-york/Content?oid=2609228 |title=Nature girl Oh Land is blazing new trails on the streets of New York|work=[[The San Francisco Examiner]] |date=23 October 2013 |access-date=24 February 2016}}</ref> On 14 February 2016 she gave birth to her first son Svend in New York.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BBvOF5CyKpD/ |title=Welcome to the world baby! Your parents love you already #boy (...og nej, det er ikke hans cpr nr. Men fødselstidspunkt )|publisher=[[Instagram]] |date=14 February 2016 |access-date=24 February 2016}}</ref> The family moved to [[Søborg]], a northern  suburb of Copenhagen after Svend was born.<ref name=365nyhede/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://borsen.dk/nyheder/avisen/artikel/11/177121/artikel.html?hl=YToxOntpOjA7czo5OiJL-GJlbmhhdm4iO30,|title=Blå Bog: Eske Kath|language=da|website=[[Dagbladet Børsen]]|access-date=17 August 2017}}</ref> A separation permit for the couple was issued on the 23rd of October 2018 by the Danish government. She has a small dog named Ujan that travels with her sometimes.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://daveymorgan.com/blog/?p=4883 |title=Oh Land! |publisher=Davey Morgan Photography |date=31 March 2011 |access-date=8 April 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Oh Land |url=http://plixi.com/p/81080862 |title=Ujan the goat-monkey is not thinking about David Letterman!!! |publisher=Plixi. [[Lockerz]] |date=2 March 2011 |access-date=8 April 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120720061725/http://plixi.com/p/81080862 |archive-date=20 July 2012 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> On 21 August, 2020 Oh Land gave birth to her second son by her boyfriend, Adi Zukanović, known to the public as Moust; the child's real name remains a family secret.<ref>{{cite web |author=Lykke Buhl |url=https://herognu.dk/oh-land-barsel-amme-soen-x-factor/ |title=Nybagt mor og ‘X Factor’-dommer: Tager baby med på job |publisher=her & nu |date=12 September 2020 |access-date=2 October 2020}}</ref>
