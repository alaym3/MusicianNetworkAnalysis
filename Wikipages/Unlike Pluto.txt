'''Armond Arabshahi''', known by his stage name '''Unlike Pluto''', is an American singer, songwriter, [[DJ]] and record producer.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://ventsmagazine.com/2018/07/10/unlike-pluto-releases-fake-smiles-real-memories-double-single/ |title=UNLIKE PLUTO RELEASES ‘FAKE SMILES, REAL MEMORIES’ DOUBLE SINGLE |date=2018-07-11 |website=Vents Magazine |language=en-US |access-date=2019-01-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://earmilk.com/2018/06/15/unlike-pluto-shares-a-snapshot-of-youth-in-double-single-late-bloomer-premiere/ |title=Unlike Pluto shares a snapshot of youth in double single "Late Bloomer" [Premiere] |last=Ceccarelli |first=Katey |date=2018-06-15 |website=EARMILK |language=en-US |access-date=2019-01-17}}</ref> The name Unlike Pluto developed from holding onto his outcast persona. He is known for his ''Pluto Tapes'' series of albums, where he challenges himself to release one song per week starting from May 2018.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youredm.com/2019/04/26/unlike-pluto-releases-his-second-album-of-the-year-and-its-only-april/|title=Unlike Pluto Releases His Second Album Of The Year... And It's Only April|last=Meadow|first=Matthew|date=2019-04-26|website=Your EDM|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-09}}</ref> Besides his Unlike Pluto project, he has a side project with ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]'' singer Joanna Jones called Why Mona where they create covers of old school classics.<ref name=":1" />

== Career ==
Originally from [[Atlanta]] and based in [[Los Angeles]], Arabshahi describes his style of music as [[electronic rock]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://edmidentity.com/2017/05/14/lowdown-unlike-pluto/|title=Unlike Pluto Talks Sloths, His Music, & More!|last=Lopez|first=Carlos|date=2017-05-14|website=EDM Identity|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-17}}</ref>

Arabshahi has performed at various music festivals, including Lollapalooza, Bonnaroo, and Euphoria Music Festival.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.songkick.com/artists/7119134-unlike-pluto/gigography|title=Unlike Pluto Gigography, Tour History & Past Concerts – Songkick|website=www.songkick.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-09}}</ref> In 2017 he also hosted a tour promoting one of his huge singles, "[[Everything Black]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7718107/unlike-pluto-everything-black-mike-taylor|title=Unlike Pluto Wants to Paint 'Everything Black' on Mike Taylor Collab: Listen|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-08-09}}</ref>

=== Why Mona ===
In 2017, Arabshahi announced a new side project called Why Mona with ''Hamilton'' singer Joanna Jones. The pair released a cover of [[Queen (band)|Queen]]'s "[[We Will Rock You]]" the same day.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7898101/unlike-pluto-side-project-why-mona-we-will-rock-you-cover |title=Unlike Pluto Reveals New Side Project Why Mona, Drops Smoldering Cover of 'We Will Rock You': Exclusive |website=Billboard |access-date=2019-01-17}}</ref> Why Mona combines rock and electronic music to create covers of old school classics. In contrast to Unlike Pluto's electronic driven production, Joanna hails from Broadway where she's currently starring in the roles of Maria Reynolds and Peggy Schuyler in the critically acclaimed Broadway hit musical [[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm4720292/|title=Joanna Jones|website=IMDb|access-date=2019-08-09}}</ref> In early 2019, the duo's cover of "[[Wannabe]]" by [[Spice Girls]] went viral on [[TikTok]] amassing over 10 million unique video creations by users on the platform.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/news/tiktoks-greatest-hits-old-songs-suddenly-going-viral/|title=TikTok’s greatest hits: why are these old songs suddenly going viral?|last=Vincent|first=Alice|date=2019-10-24|work=The Telegraph|access-date=2019-11-05|language=en-GB|issn=0307-1235}}</ref> In 2021 Dell computers released an ad that featured their track "Rabbit Hole" which introduced a lot of viewers to this song and group.<ref>{{Cite web|title=2021 Dell XPS Advert Song – Youniverse – TV Advert Songs|url=https://www.tvadsongs.uk/dell-xps-advert-music-youniverse/|access-date=2021-04-06|language=en-US}}</ref>
