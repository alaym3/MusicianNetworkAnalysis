WARNER MUSIC">{{cite web|url=http://www.gaymagazine.cl/musica/?contenido=1690|date=6 May 2008|title=LUIS MIGUEL: Complices_WARNER MUSIC|website=Gaymagazine.cl|language=es|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080512100613/http://www.gaymagazine.cl/musica/?contenido=1690 |archive-date=12 May 2008|access-date=27 September 2011}}</ref><ref name="Buzz Briefs: Luis Miguel, Bon Jovi">{{cite news|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2008/07/08/entertainment/main4240526.shtml?source=RSSattr=Entertainment_4240526|title=Buzz Briefs: Luis Miguel, Bon Jovi|publisher=[[CBS News]] | date=8 July 2008}}</ref> Luis Miguel has sung in multiple genres and styles, including [[Latin pop|pop]] songs, [[Latin ballad|ballads]], [[bolero]]s, [[Tango (flamenco)|tangos]], [[jazz]], [[big band]] and [[mariachi]]. Miguel is also recognized as the only Latin singer of his generation to not crossover to the [[Anglo]] market during the "Latin Explosion" in the 1990s.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://articles.latimes.com/1995-09-20/entertainment/ca-47892_1_luis-miguel-gallego|title=One World Will Do, for Now : Pop music: 'My language and my world is Spanish,' says Luis Miguel, when asked about his crossover aspirations.|last=LOPETEGUI|first=ENRIQUE|date=20 September 1995|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|access-date=10 June 2018|language=en-US|issn=0458-3035}}</ref>

Despite recording only in [[Spanish language|Spanish]], he continued to be the best selling Latin artist in the 1990s, and was credited for popularizing the [[bolero]] genre into the mainstream market.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://articles.latimes.com/1997/sep/20/entertainment/ca-34128|title=Luis Miguel's Crossroad|last=HILBURN|first=ROBERT|date=20 September 1997|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|access-date=10 June 2018|language=en-US|issn=0458-3035}}</ref> He has sold around 60 million records worldwide,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://web.archive.org/save/https://www.bbc.com/mundo/cultura_sociedad/2010/07/100719_luis_miguel_regresa_escenarios_lav|title=Luis Miguel regresa a los escenarios|publisher=[[BBC News]]|date=20 July 2010|access-date=17 June 2020|language=es}}</ref> making him one of the best-selling Latin music artists.

[[Latin pop music]], along with his personal life and showmanship on stage, has made Luis Miguel popular for nearly his entire career, which started in Mexico in 1981. Having won his first Grammy Award at the age of fourteen for his duet "Me Gustas Tal Como Eres" with [[Sheena Easton]],<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> he is the [[Grammy Award records#Youngest winners|youngest male artist]] in music history to have received this accolade. In 1991, the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] recognized the high sales for his 1991 album ''[[Romance (Luis Miguel album)|Romance]]'', itself being the third best-selling music album in Mexico, and one of the best-selling [[Spanish language]] albums of all time.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> He was the first Latin artist to have been awarded two [[RIAA certification|Platinum certified]] Spanish-language albums in the United States with ''[[Romance (Luis Miguel album)|Romance]]'' and ''[[Segundo Romance]]'' the latter earning him 35 platinum records throughout Central and South America. He is also recognized by Billboard, as the artist with the most top 10 hits on the [[Hot Latin Songs|''Billboard's'' Hot Latin Songs]] chart. His album ''[[Cómplices|Complices]]'' was released in 2008 and peaked at No.10 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], and his most recent album ''[[¡México Por Siempre!]]'' was released in 2017, and it earned him his second No. 1 on the [[Regional Mexican Albums|''Billboard'' Regional Mexican Albums]] and achieved double platinum status.

Luis Miguel is also known for his high-grossing live performances. He is the first-highest grossing Latin touring artist since Boxscore began tracking touring data in 1990, with a towering total of $278.5 million.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/touring/8486024/marc-anthony-becomes-second-highest-grossing-latin-touring-artist|title=Luis Miguel First-Highest Grossing Latin Touring Artist|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181122110915/https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/touring/8486024/marc-anthony-becomes-second-highest-grossing-latin-touring-artist|archive-date=29 October 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=20 November 2018}}</ref>
With the [[Luis Miguel Tour]], which took place in 2010, he visited 22 countries in North America, South America and Europe, where he performed in a three-year span with a total of 223 shows all over the world, making it the longest and highest-grossing tour ever made by a Latin artist. He also holds the record for the most consecutive presentations in the [[National Auditorium|Auditorio Nacional]] (National Auditorium) with a total of [[México En La Piel Tour|30 consecutive]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jornada.com.mx/2006/03/01/index.php?section=espectaculos&article=a09n1esp|title=El Auditorio Nacional entregó a Luis Miguel la Estela de Plata por imponer una marca - La Jornada|website=Jornada.com.mx}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/luismiguelofficial/posts/1618701578179593|title=Luis Miguel|website=Facebook.com}}</ref> concerts as well as the record for the most presentations in the same venue with a total of 258 concerts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BfXbcCwj2ie/|title=Kiko Cibrian on Instagram: "Con el corazón contento por que estoy de regreso al Auditorio Nacional con LM. God is good!!!"|website=Instagram.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.quien.com/espectaculos/2011/03/11/luis-miguel-logra-200-conciertos-en-el-auditorio|title=Luis Miguel logra 200 conciertos en el Auditorio|date=12 March 2011|website=Quien.com}}</ref> {{as of|2020|10}}, Luis Miguel ranks number two on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Greatest of All-Time Latin Artists chart.<ref>{{cite magazine |title=Enrique Iglesias Tops Billboard's Greatest of All Time Latin Artists Chart |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/9466868/enrique-iglesias-tops-greatest-of-all-time-latin-artists-chart#:~:text=Enrique%20Iglesias%20crowns%20Billboard's%20first,Wednesday%20night%2C%20Oct.%2021. |magazine=Billboard |access-date=20 October 2020 |date=19 October 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
===First recordings and commercial breakout===
During his childhood years, his father, who was also his manager, encouraged him to watch and analyze nearly every movie, recording, and concert performance of [[Elvis Presley]].<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de">{{cite web|url=http://www.elixic.de/Luis-Miguel|title=Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de|website=Elixic.de|language=de}}</ref> In 1982, he released his first album, ''[[Un Sol]]'', by the Mexican branch of EMI Records, which won him his first gold disc at the age of 11.<ref name="Luis Miguel: Grandes Éxitos on Apple Music">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/luis-miguel-grandes-exitos/id96190948|title=Luis Miguel: Grandes Éxitos|date=4 March 2003|publisher=Apple Music|language=en}}</ref> Two years later, he started touring Latin American countries including Colombia, Venezuela, Chile, and Argentina. He scored a major hit with his single, "1+1=Dos Enamorados".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tmz.com/people/luis-miguel/|title=Luis Miguel|website=Tmz.com|access-date=16 December 2019}}</ref>

In 1984, Luis Miguel turned into [[Spanish rock|Spanish rock-pop style]], with the international release of his fourth studio album ''[[Palabra de honor (album)|Palabra de Honor]]'' produced by the then Spanish Hispavox director Honorio Herrero.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://terceracultura.cl/2013/11/este-hombre-invento-la-musica-para-encerar-y-nadie-lo-sabe-parte-i/|title=Este hombre inventó la música para encerar… y nadie lo sabe (Parte I)|last=Martinez|first=Ricardo|date=11 November 2013|website=Terceracultura.cl/|language=es|access-date=24 October 2016}}</ref> In 1985, at age of 15, he took part at the [[Sanremo Music Festival]], where he won the second place award with his song "Noi Ragazzi Di Oggi".<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/> That same year, he was also presented with the "Antorcha de Plata" Award in the [[Viña del Mar International Song Festival|Viña del Mar Music Festival]] for his duet single "Me Gustas Tal Como Eres" ("I Like You Just As You Are") with Scottish singer [[Sheena Easton]].<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> In 1983, he co-starred in the film ''[[Ya nunca más (film)|Ya Nunca Más]]'' (released 1984) and in 1985, ''[[Fiebre de Amor (Film)|Fiebre de Amor]]''.<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/>

In 1986, he took a brief hiatus as his relationship with his father was souring due to alleged mismanagement, poor financial decisions and risking the earnings of his career. Shortly after signing with Warner Records, in 1987, he fired his father and started to work with Juan Carlos Calderón.<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/> Luis Miguel's album ''[[Soy Como Quiero Ser]]'' in May 1987 sold well over 2.5 million records.<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/> The album featured covering hits such as "[[Cuando calienta el sol (song)|Cuando Calienta El Sol]]", "[[I Only Want to Be with You|Ahora Te Puedes Marchar]]", and "[[You Don't Have to Say You Love Me|Yo Que No Vivo Sin Ti]]".{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}

On 25 November 1988, Luis Miguel's second album ''[[Busca Una Mujer]]'' was released. The first single "[[La Incondicional]]" became a top-ten hit throughout Latin America in the first half of 1989,<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/> and thanks in part to the music video, it spent over seven months on the top ten of many Latin American charts. The video was shocking to many fans as he had cut his signature long hair. This cemented his transition from a child star into an adult superstar. By the end of 1989, the second single "[[Fría Como el Viento]]" reached No.&nbsp;1 on the [[Hot Latin Tracks]].<ref name="Artist Chart History: Luis Miguel"/> He also had a top-ten single with "Separados" in early 1990, peaking at #8.

In 1990, he made a successful transition from a child singer to an adult showman with the album ''[[20 Años (Luis Miguel album)|20 Años]]'' and an ensuing string of sold-out shows first in Mexico then throughout Latin America and elsewhere in the world. ''[[20 Años (Luis Miguel album)|20 Años]]'' sold 600,000 copies in its first week of release<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/> and resulted in the release of three singles: "Tengo Todo Excepto A Ti", "Entrégate", and "Amante Del Amor", with the two former hitting number 1 on Billboard's Hot Latin Tracks in 1990.<ref name="Artist Chart History: Luis Miguel">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=luis miguel|chart=all}}|title= Artist Chart History: Luis Miguel|work=Billboard Magazine}}</ref>

===International superstardom===
[[File:Luismiguelconcierto2.png|thumb|right|260px|Luis Miguel in concert live with [[Mariachi]]]]
In 1991, Luis Miguel's career went to even greater heights and earned him the respect of a wider audience with the release of ''[[Romance (Luis Miguel album)|Romance]]'', an album of romantic boleros, most of them from the 1950s. He has been credited with reinventing the bolero for modern audiences. The album ''Romance'', which became his most successful material ever, eventually sold 7 million units worldwide.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.diariolibre.com/revista/2013/11/02/i409359_luis-miguel-altos-chavn-sur-profundo-una-sonrisa-para-los-turistas.html|title=Luis Miguel en Altos de Chavón, y en el Sur profundo, una sonrisa para los turistas|trans-title=Luis Miguel in Altos de Chavon, and in the Deep South, a smile for tourists
|work=Diario Libre|date=2 November 2013|access-date=21 September 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://diario.latercera.com/2012/02/21/01/contenido/cultura-entretencion/30-101299-9-los-mentores-de-luis-miguel-revelan-sus-mitos-y-obsesiones.shtml|title=Los mentores de Luis Miguel revelan sus mitos y obsesiones|language=es|trans-title=Luis Miguel's mentors reveal his myths and obsessions|last=Vergara|first=Claudia|work=[[La Tercera]]|publisher=[[Copesa]]|date=21 February 2012|access-date=15 July 2014}}</ref>

<!-- Deleted image removed: [[File:luismiguelvivo6.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Luis Miguel at the Amarte Es Un Placer Tour]] -->
In 1993, Luis Miguel's fifth studio album, ''[[Aries (album)|Aries]]'', was released, which won him that year's [[Grammy Award]] for Best Latin Pop Album. That same year Luis Miguel was personally invited by [[Frank Sinatra]] to join him on his ''[[Duets II (Frank Sinatra album)|Duets II]]'' album and to perform live in a nationally televised special in honor of Sinatra's 80th birthday; along with other stars such as [[Stevie Wonder]] and [[Natalie Cole]].<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/>

In 1994, he released a sequel to ''Romance'', ''[[Segundo Romance]]'', featuring Luis Miguel as a convincing pop singer with mastery over timeless Latin classics such as "Solamente Una Vez" and "Historia de un Amor". The album went on to earn another Grammy Award, as well as skyrocketing Platinum status in the United States.

In 1996, for his contribution to the recording industry, Luis Miguel received a star and was inducted to the [[Hollywood Walk of Fame]], being at that time the youngest male singer to receive a star. Soon after, Luis Miguel quickly returned to the studios and released ''[[Nada Es Igual (Luis Miguel album)|Nada Es Igual]]'', a pop album featuring "[[Someday (Disney song)#Luis Miguel version|Sueña]]", the main theme for Disney's ''[[The Hunchback of Notre Dame (1996 film)|The Hunchback of Notre Dame]]''.

In 1997, Luis Miguel released his third album of the bolero series ''[[Romances (Luis Miguel album)|Romances]]'', which sold over 5 million copies. "Por Debajo de la Mesa" was released as the [[lead single]] from the album. The single quickly reached number one on the [[Hot Latin Songs|''Billboard'' Hot Latin Songs]] chart, two months later; it would spend twenty-six weeks on the chart. At the [[40th Annual Grammy Awards]] in 1998, Luis Miguel won the award for [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album|Best Latin Pop Performance]]. He also received the [[Billboard Latin Music Awards|Billboard Latin Music Award]] for "Male Pop Album of the Year" and the [[World Music Awards|World Music Award]] for "Best Selling Latin Artist" in the same year.

In 1999, Luis Miguel released his ninth studio album ''[[Amarte Es un Placer (album)|Amarte Es Un Placer]]'' which, in 2000, earned him two Latin Grammys for being Best Album of the Year and Best Pop Album of the Year.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> The single "O Tú, O Ninguna" of ''Amarte Es Un Placer'' became number 1 on the Billboard Hot Latin Tracks and perched at the top position for eight consecutive weeks.<ref name="Artist Chart History: Luis Miguel"/> Following the success of ''Amarte Es Un Placer'', Luis Miguel released ''[[Mis Romances]]'' in 2001, his fourth album in the bolero series. The album, which sold over two million copies worldwide was followed by the launch of ''[[Mis Romances Tour]]'' which took place throughout the U.S., Europe, and Latin America.

In 2003, Luis Miguel released his first pop album in over four years entitled ''[[33 (Luis Miguel album)|33]]'', indicative of his age. The album, which featured a collection of [[ballad]]s and [[uptempo]] songs climbed to number 1 on Billboard's Latin Charts earning Luis Miguel two Billboard Awards as well as Grammy and Latin Grammy award nominations.

On the ''[[33 Tour]]'', Luis Miguel filled the largest halls in the U.S., toured throughout Latin America, including Chile where his album ''33'' sold 2.5&nbsp;million copies and was recognized that same year by Warner Chile as the artist with the most sold records sold in the history of Chile.<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on German Warner Music">{{cite web|url=http://www.warnermusic.de/luismiguel/bio|title=Biography: Luis Miguel on German Warner Music|publisher=Warner Music Germany|language=de|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081126164738/http://www.warnermusic.de/luismiguel/bio/|archive-date=26 November 2008}}</ref> He also performed at Mexico's sold-out [[National Auditorium]], and various sold-out shows at [[Buenos Aires]], as well as other prestigious international venues such as [[Plaza de Toros de Las Ventas]] in Spain. That same year, [[Felipe, Prince of Asturias|Prince Felipe]] of Spain presented him with a special award for being the best-selling foreign artist in his country's history, and hosted a special party in his honor in [[Madrid]].<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de" />

In addition to his album ''33'' Luis Miguel was bestowed by ''[[Lo Nuestro Awards]]'' in 2003 a "Premio Lo Nuestro a la Excelencia", (literally, The "Ours" award for Excellence) a lifetime achievement award for his outstanding career at the mere age of 33 being the youngest artist to receive this award.
[[File:Luismiguelconcierto1.png|thumb|left|260px|Luis Miguel in Mexico City in 2006]]
In 2004, Luis Miguel released his ''[[México en la Piel (album)|Mexico En La Piel]]''. The album, which was a collection of traditional Mexican ''mariachi'' songs went on to sell five million copies worldwide. For this album he received a diamond disc and won the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Ranchero Album]] at the [[Latin Grammy Awards of 2005]] and also the [[Grammy Award for Best Mexican/Mexican-American Album]]. He launched his ''[[Mexico En La Piel Tour]]'' in 2005. In 2006, Luis Miguel performed thirty shows between 18 January and 27 February at the [[National Auditorium]] in Mexico City.<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/> The 123-date tour started in September 2005 in Mexico which then stretched to Argentina, Chile and Uruguay. The ''[[Mexico En La Piel Tour]]'' won the ''[[Billboard Touring Awards|Billboard "Tour of the Year"]]'' and positioned itself as No.1 on the ''Billboard World Top Boxscore''.

In 2005, Luis Miguel released ''[[Grandes éxitos (Luis Miguel album)|Grandes éxitos]]'', his first greatest hits album, that featured his greatest songs he recorded during all his career, including two never-released singles: "Misterios Del Amor" and "Si Te Perdiera".

That same year, he released a Christmas album called ''[[Navidades]]''. The playlist includes many Christmas standards in Spanish. The album reached number one on Billboard's Top Latin Albums chart during the first week of its release.<ref name="Artist Chart History: Luis Miguel (Albums)">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=luis miguel|chart=all}}|title= Artist Chart History: Luis Miguel (Albums)|work=Billboard Magazine}}</ref> The album released two singles "Santa Claus Llegó A La Ciudad" ("[[Santa Claus Is Coming to Town]]"), and "Mi Humilde Oración" ("My Grown-up Christmas List").

On 6 May 2008, Luis Miguel released ''[[Cómplices]]'', produced by him and written by Spanish composer [[Manuel Alejandro]]. It sold almost 350,000 copies in the first 24 hours.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.utpac.org/media/press/Luis_Miguel |title=Luis Miguel |publisher=University of Texas at Austin Performing Arts Center |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090227180809/http://utpac.org/media/press/Luis_Miguel |archive-date=27 February 2009}}</ref> The first single, "[[Si Tú Te Atreves]]" was released on 7 April 2008, the second single of the album was "[[Te Desean]]". Luis Miguel made history with his album ''[[Cómplices|Complices]]'' which debuted at No.10 on the [[Billboard 200|Billboard Top 200]], being the highest position a Latin artist has ever reached on the [[Billboard 200|Billboard Top 200]] with a fully Spanish composed album, it also reached No.1 on the [[Billboard Top Latin Albums]] making him the Latin artist with the most No.1 albums on the chart with a total of 9 albums. His ''[[Complices Tour]]'' began in [[Seattle]], Washington on 3 September 2008.

Luis Miguel released his [[Luis Miguel (album)|self-titled]] studio album on 14 September 2010. Its first single "Labios de Miel" is a smooth Latin pop song. The [[Luis Miguel Tour|tour]] started on 4 November 2010 in the city of [[Lima, Peru]] and covered the United States, South America, Mexico and Spain among other countries.<ref>[http://www.luismigueloficial.com/main_ncaln.html Luis Miguel Sitio Oficial]. Luismigueloficial.com. Retrieved on 13 August 2011.</ref> On 22 February 2012, he sang at the [[Viña del Mar International Song Festival]]. With one million dollars for his performance, he became the most expensive artist in the history of the festival. Luis Miguel performed at the [[Curaçao]] [[North Sea Jazz Festival]] on 31 August 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.curacaonorthseajazz.com/es/prensa/comunicados-de-prensa/ |title=Tarima adicional en el Curaçao North Sea Jazz y nombres nuevos! |language=es |access-date=7 January 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130402182658/http://www.curacaonorthseajazz.com/es/prensa/comunicados-de-prensa/ |archive-date=2 April 2013 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> In August 2014, the song "Déjà Vu" was leaked on the web.

On 4 May 2017, Telemundo signed a deal for the exclusive U.S. broadcast rights to the "officially authorized TV series" based on Luis Miguel's life story and announced it will air in 2018. On the same day, Netflix also revealed it has the rights to stream the yet-unnamed bio-series in Latin America and Spain with the same target date.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}

In his official [[Facebook]] page it was announced that his new single "La Fiesta del Mariachi" was set to release on 27 October 2017. On 24 November, the full album, titled ''[[¡México Por Siempre!]]'' was released, it features 14 tracks of traditional Mexican music. The [[México Por Siempre Tour|tour]] began on 21 February 2018, in the [[National Auditorium]] of [[Mexico City]] with 8 shows and will continue throughout Mexico, United States and Spain.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}

On 22 April, Netflix and Telemundo started airing the self-titled series ''[[Luis Miguel (TV series)|Luis Miguel]]''. Together with revealing many parts both known and unknown of the singer, it also brought nostalgic moments for those watching it, like the mirroring of his [[Sabritas]] ad, called in the series "Saboritas".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/fanalopez/luis-miguel-comercial-sabritas|title=El nuevo episodio de la serie de Luis Miguel nos dejó a todos con ganas de comer 'Saboritas'|date=29 April 2018|website=Buzzfeed.com|language=es|access-date=30 April 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
[[File:Luismiguel9900.jpg|thumb|right|250px|Luis Miguel 2008]]
His father was a Spanish singer, songwriter and guitarist, Luis Gallego Sánchez, known as "[[Luisito Rey]]", and his mother was an Italian actress, Marcela Basteri.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.antesydespues.com.ar/luis-miguel/ |title=El Antes y Despues de Luis Miguel {{!}} Biografia, Fotos y Familia |access-date=14 November 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203022040/http://www.antesydespues.com.ar/luis-miguel/ |archive-date=3 December 2013 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> His father was from [[Cádiz]], a city in southwestern Spain, and his mother was from the [[Province of Massa-Carrara]], Italy. He was named Luis Miguel in honor of Spanish bullfighter [[Luis Miguel Dominguín]].<ref name="Quien">{{cite web|url=http://www.quien.com/perfiles/luis-miguel|title=LuisMiguel|website=Quien.com|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120125085714/http://www.quien.com/perfiles/luis-miguel|archive-date=25 January 2012}}</ref> Luis Miguel has two younger brothers, Alejandro and Sergio.

His birth date is on 18 April, but he celebrates his birthday on 19 April as that was the day his father registered him at Puerto Rico's Civil Registry.<ref>{{cite book |title=Quien |date=19 April 2010|publisher=Revista Quien |pages=18}}</ref>
Due to his early fame, Luis Miguel had a complicated childhood. He had a difficult relationship with his father, who was also his manager. His father was very strict and demanding during incessant rehearsals, although his father's strict discipline has also been credited with playing a large role in his success. In 1986, Luis Miguel's mother disappeared mysteriously and her whereabouts are still unknown.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://us.hola.com/actualidad/201701126732/marcela-basteri-madre-luis-miguel-fotos/|title=Hace 30 años que desapareció Marcela Basteri, la madre de Luis Miguel|date=12 January 2017|website=Us.hola.com}}</ref> In the late 1980s, Luis Miguel fired his father due to financial problems caused by poor representation. After their estrangement, his father, who was a [[heavy drinker]], fell into depression and died on 9 December 1992 in Spain.<ref name="Biography: Luis Miguel on elixic.de"/>

Luis Miguel is recognized for his taste for [[haute cuisine]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://blog.fantasyspringsresort.com/2015/09/luis-miguel-real-most-interesting-man.html|title=Luis Miguel - The REAL Most Interesting Man In The World}}</ref> and for being a self-taught [[sommelier]]. Luis Miguel is the father of three children: Michelle Salas, born on 13 June 1989, from his relationship with [[Stephanie Salas]]; Miguel, born on 1 January 2007 and Daniel, born on 18 December 2008, both from his relationship with actress [[Aracely Arámbula]]. ==Artistry and image==
Luis Miguel rarely grants interviews or attends award ceremonies. He is always escorted by a diligent security team and he is transported in several trucks to distract paparazzi and reporters.<ref>[https://www.tmz.com/2007/06/14/who-is-chows-mystery-vip/ TMZ website]. Tmz.com (2007-06-14). Retrieved on 13 August 2011.</ref> He is quoted as saying, "I maintain my sanity by keeping my distance."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ybarra |first1=Rose |title=Balladeer reigns for two-nights at Dodge Arena |url=http://www.luismiguelsite.com/pubhtml/ingles/monitor.htm |access-date=18 October 2021 |work=www.luismiguelsite.com |publisher=The Monitor |date=30 October 2003}}</ref>
[[File:Luis Miguel Hollywood Star.jpg|thumb|right|Luis Miguel's star on the [[Hollywood Walk of Fame]]]]

Luis Miguel is considered one of the top male pop singers worldwide today. His voice has been estimated to span three octaves, six notes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vintagevinylnews.com/2014/05/digging-deeper-axl-rose-is-not-singer.html|title=Digging Deeper: Axl Rose is NOT the Singer With the Widest Range|website=Vintagevinylnews.com|access-date=16 December 2019}}</ref> In a career that has spanned almost forty years, he has become the main male singer from Latin America, having performed successfully pop music, [[bolero]], [[mariachi]] and [[romantic ballad]]s.<ref name="articles.latimes.com">{{cite news| url=https://articles.latimes.com/1997/sep/20/entertainment/ca-34128 | title=Luis Miguel's Crossroad|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]| first=Robert | last=Hilburn | date=20 September 1997}}</ref><ref name="ReferenceA">{{cite news| url=https://articles.latimes.com/2002/jan/31/news/wk-gurza31 | newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]] | first=Agustin | last=Gurza | date=31 January 2002|title=Crooner in Cruise Control}}</ref> In the late 1980s, Luis Miguel made the transition from child star to consolidated international singer, and since then, he became one of the most revered and popular Latin American artists ever.<ref name="articles.latimes.com"/><ref name="ReferenceA"/> Also dubbed as a crooner, his wide vocal range and performance has been praised by critics and other artists all over the world.<ref name="articles.latimes.com"/><ref name="ReferenceA"/> Producer [[Gustavo Santaolalla]] described him as "one of the greatest singers of all time" and stated that "Luis Miguel is not just a singer, he's an artist."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.peopleenespanol.com/article/gustavo-santaolalla-luis-miguel-no-es-un-cantante-es-un-artista|title=Gustavo Santaolalla: "Luis Miguel no es un cantante, es un artista"|website=Peopleenespanol.com|access-date=31 January 2016}}</ref>

[[Frank Sinatra]] personally invited Luis Miguel to participate on a duet in the album ''[[Duets II (Frank Sinatra album)|Duets II]]''. Luis Miguel has been dubbed several times by the press and the media as the "Latin Frank Sinatra".<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2008/07/08/entertainment/main4240526.shtml?source=RSSattr=Entertainment_4240526 | work=CBS News | title=Buzz Briefs: Luis Miguel, Bon Jovi | date=8 July 2008}}</ref>

The press stated that at the time of his capture, the dictator [[Saddam Hussein]] had the album ''[[Segundo Romance]]'', among his belongings.<ref>[http://www.terra.com/musica/noticias/luis_miguel_es_el_preferido_de_saddam_hussein/oci81495 Luis Miguel es el preferido de Saddam Hussein]. Terra. Retrieved on 13 August 2011.</ref>

His net worth is estimated at US$180 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fotolog.com/luis-miguel-net-worth/|title=Luis Miguel Net Worth 2020 - How Much is He Worth?|date=23 February 2020}}</ref>

===Summary of distinctions===
At the age of 14, Luis Miguel received a [[Grammy Award]] for his duet "Me Gustas Tal Como Eres" ("I Like You Just The Way You Are") with [[Sheena Easton]].<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> In 1991, the [[RIAA]] gave him a recognition for the high sales of the albums ''[[Romance (Luis Miguel album)|Romance]]'' and ''[[Segundo Romance]]''.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> Luis Miguel is the only Latin artist to have two Spanish-language albums (''Romance'' and ''Segundo Romance'') go platinum in the USA.

He was the only Latin artist to perform at the show ''"[[Frank Sinatra|Sinatra]]: 80 Years My Way"'', along with other pop icons such as [[Bruce Springsteen]], [[Natalie Cole]] and [[Bob Dylan]].<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> His 1997 album ''[[Romances (Luis Miguel album)|Romances]]'' became the first Spanish-language album ever debuting at No.&nbsp;14 on the Billboard Top 200.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> [[Warner Music Group]] recognized Luis Miguel as the best-selling artist in the history of Chile with over 2.5&nbsp;million records sold.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> In 2003, [[Felipe, Prince of Asturias|Prince Felipe]] of Spain presented him with a special award for being the best-selling foreign artist in his country's history. In 2008, Luis Miguel's album ''[[Navidades]]'' became the first [[Christmas music|Christmas album]] ever being nominated to the [[Grammy]] in the category of Pop.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> His album ''[[Cómplices]]'' broke records in Mexico, by selling over 320,000 copies in its first day of release.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> Just three days after its release, his new [[Luis Miguel (album)|album]] achieved Quadruple Platinum certification in Mexico, Platinum in Argentina and gold records in Chile and the United States.

Luis Miguel is also known by his high-grossing tours in Chile, Uruguay, Venezuela, Brazil and Spain between 1999 and 2000. The tour consisted of 99 concerts and was attended by approximately 1.4&nbsp;million fans. It was the highest-grossing tour ever made by a Latin artist, as well as the most extended. These two records have been broken by his [[Mexico En La Piel Tour]]. His [[33 Tour]] peaked at No.&nbsp;1 in the ''Billboard World Top Boxscore''.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> The [[Mexico En La Piel Tour]] of 2005–2007, with a total of 124 concerts, peaked at No.&nbsp;1 in the ''Billboard World Top Boxscore''. The tour spanned two years with a total of 129 concerts attended by over 1.5 million spectators, and grossed around $95&nbsp;million.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> In 2006, during the ''Mexico En La Piel Tour'', after giving 30 consecutive sold-out concerts at the [[National Auditorium|Auditorio Nacional]] in Mexico, gathering over 260,000 spectators he was awarded with the ''"Estela de Plata"'' at the last concert.<ref name="LUIS MIGUEL: Complices - WARNER MUSIC"/> His [[Luis Miguel Tour]] is ranked at number five of the highest-grossing tours of 2011 in North America.<ref>[http://www.pollstarpro.com/files/Charts2011/07181Ttop100NorthAmericanTours.pdf 071811top100.pmd] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110815160637/http://www.pollstarpro.com/files/Charts2011/07181Ttop100NorthAmericanTours.pdf |date=15 August 2011 }}. (PDF) . Retrieved on 13 August 2011.</ref>
