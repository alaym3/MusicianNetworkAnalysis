'''Tommee James Profitt''' (born November 12, 1984) is an American songwriter and producer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://doveawards.com/2016-winners/|title=2016 Winners {{!}} The 49th Annual GMA Dove Awards|website=doveawards.com|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> 

== Biography ==
Profitt is originally from [[Grand Rapids, Michigan]], United States.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.westmichiganchristian.com/local/436-tommee-profitt-s-new-season.html|title=Tommee Profitt's New Season - West Michigan Christian News {{!}} West Michigan Christian News|last=DeBoer|first=Terry|website=Westmichiganchristian.com|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> He moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] in 2017 to continue his music career.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://fullcirclemusic.org/fcm082/|title=FCM082 - Producing Music for Film and TV with Tommee Profitt - Full Circle Music|date=October 3, 2017|work=Full Circle Music|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> He has worked on the production and sound development of rapper [[NF (rapper)|NF]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/artists/Tommee-profitt|title=Tommee Profitt|website=Genius|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> In later years, he has also worked in film and television music.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.songspub.com/News/1773/HBO's%20Road%20to%20Canelo%20vs.%20Smith%20(TV)%20'I'm%20Seeing%20Red'%20(Tommee%20Profitt)|title=HBO's Road to Canelo vs. Smith (TV) // 'I'm Seeing Red' (Tommee Profitt)|work=SONGS Music Publishing|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> His music has been featured in  ''[[24 (TV series)|24]]'', ''[[Legacies (TV series)|Legacies]]'', ''[[Quantico (TV series)|Quantico]]'', ''[[The Good Doctor (TV series)|The Good Doctor]]'', ''[[Prison Break]]'', ''[[The Rookie (TV series)|The Rookie]]'', ''[[America's Got Talent]]'', ''[[Empire (2015 TV series)|Empire]]'', ''[[Bones (TV series)|Bones]]'', ''[[The Handmaid's Tale (TV series)|The Handmaid's Tale]]'', ''[[The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 2|The Hunger Games: Mockingjay Part 2]]'', [[National Football League|NFL]], [[Tyr Sport, Inc.|TYR Sport]], and others.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.northstarmedia.com/artist/MTg0MjA0LWI0ODMzNQ/|title=North Star Media - Tommee Profitt|website=northstarmedia.com|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> He has won two [[GMA Dove Award|Dove Awards]] and has been nominated for five.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://doveawards.com/awards/past-winners/|title=Past Winners {{!}} The 50th Annual GMA Dove Awards|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-07}}</ref>

His music has also been used for video game promos and scores including ''[[Tom Clancy's Ghost Recon Breakpoint|Ghost Recon Breakpoint]]'', ''[[Apex Legends]]'', ''[[Assassin's Creed]]'', ''UFC2'' and ''[[Madden NFL|Madden]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://resin8music.com/tommee-profitt|title=Tommee Profitt|website=Resin8 Music|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-14}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://williamsonsource.com/franklin-songwriter-celebrates-number-1-song-ascap/|title=Franklin Songwriter Celebrates Number 1 Song at ASCAP|last=Vissman|first=Donna|date=2018-04-15|website=Williamson Source|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-14}}</ref> In 2018 Profitt held the Top TV Song for two consecutive weeks with covers of [[Linkin Park]]'s "[[In the End|In The End]]," featuring singer-songwriter [[Fleurie (musician)|Fleurie]] and American rapper Jung Youth.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spotifycharts.com/|title=Spotify Charts|website=Spotifycharts.com|access-date=2019-11-14}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nashvilleunsigned.com/featured/in-the-end-linkin-park-cover-tommee-profitt/|title="In The End" Linkin Park Cover// Produced By TOMMEE PROFITT|date=2018-11-07|website=Nashville Unsigned|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-14}}</ref> Another collaboration, "[[Wake Me Up (Avicii song)|Wake Me Up]]''',"''' featuring Fleurie, charted on [[iTunes]] Top 50 and on [[Spotify]]'s Top 50 Viral chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://blog.tunefind.com/2018/12/top-tv-song-wake-me-up-tommee-proffit-fleurie-legacies/|title=Top TV Song Last Week: Wake Me Up by Tommee Proffit, feat. Fleurie|last=Amanda Byers|date=2018-12-11|website=Tunefind|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-14}}</ref> 

== Collaborations ==
Profitt is known for his production work with American rapper NF.<ref name="podchaser.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.podchaser.com/podcasts/full-circle-music-show-80858/episodes/fcm082-producing-music-for-fil-21674035|title=FCM082 – Producing Music for Film and TV with Tommee Profitt by Full Circle Music Show on Podchaser|website=Podchaser|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> The two met in [[Michigan]] while NF was starting out as a rapper and Profitt was transitioning from being a full-time artist to a producer. Profitt signed his publishing deal with [[Capitol CMG]] Publishing in 2014, the same year that NF signed to [[Capitol Christian Music Group]] as an artist.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.rapzilla.com/rz/features/story/8841-how-nf-became-the-1st-hip-hop-artist-on-capitol-cmg|title=How NF Became the First Hip-Hop Artist on Capitol CMG|last=Daniels|first=David|work=Rapzilla.com|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> They have continued to be frequent collaborators, with Profitt collaborating on the writing and production on NF's four full-length releases that have been released under Capitol CMG.<ref name="podchaser.com"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.capitolcmgpublishing.com/LicensingPages/SongSearch.aspx?SSong=tomme|title=Capitol CMG Publishing|website=capitolcmgpublishing.com|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref>

The first album, ''[[Mansion (album)|Mansion]]'', debuted at No.&nbsp;1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Christian Albums chart and peaked at No.&nbsp;62 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] Albums chart.<ref name="Billboard">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/nf|title=NF|work=Billboard|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> The second album, ''[[Therapy Session]]'', also debuted at No.&nbsp;1 on the Christian Albums Chart<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com//cdreviews/TherapySession.asp|title=Jesusfreakhideout.com: NF, "Therapy Session" Review|website=jesusfreakhideout.com|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> and peaked at No.&nbsp;12 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref name="Billboard"/> NF's third studio album, ''[[Perception (NF album)|Perception]]'', debuted at No.&nbsp;1 overall on the ''Billboard'' 200;<ref name="billboard.com">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7998826/nf-first-no-1-album-billboard-200-chart-perception|title=NF Scores First No. 1 Album on ''Billboard'' 200 Chart With ''Perception''|work=Billboard|access-date=December 13, 2017}}</ref> his fourth studio album, ''[[The Search (NF album)|The Search]]'', did so too.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8526019/nf-scores-second-no-1-album-billboard-200|title=NF Scores Second No. 1 Album on Billboard 200 Chart With 'The Search'|work=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=August 4, 2019|accessdate=August 5, 2019}}</ref> With over one billion global streams, their song, “[[Let You Down (NF song)|Let You Down]],” claimed the No.&nbsp;1 spot on the ''Billboard'' Pop Chart<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.themcollective.com/press-releases/2018/4/13/let-you-down-eclipses-top-40-chart-as-nf-hit-co-written-produced-by-tommee-profitt-marks-first-pop-no-1-for-capitol-cmg-publishing|title="Let You Down" Eclipses Top 40 Chart As NF Hit Co-Written & Produced By Tommee Profitt Marks First Pop No. 1 For Capitol CMG Publishing|website=the media collective|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-07}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://musicrow-do.datadriven.design/category/publishing/|title=Publishing Archives - Page 17 of 234|website=MusicRow.com|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-07}}</ref> and went on to become an 8x [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] Certified Platinum single.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.themcollective.com/press-releases|title=Press Releases|website=the media collective|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-07}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/|title=Gold & Platinum|website=RIAA|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-12}}</ref> In addition to his work with NF, Tommee has worked with [[Hunter Hayes]], [[Migos]]''',''' [[The Score (band)|The Score]], [[Avril Lavigne]], [[Social Club Misfits]], Angie Rose, [[TobyMac]], [[Chris Tomlin]], [[Crowder (musician)|Crowder]], [[Britt Nicole]], [[SVRCINA]], [[Colton Dixon]], [[Nichole Nordeman|Nicole Nordeman]], [[Blanca (musician)|Blanca]], [[Ruelle (singer)|Ruelle]], Jung Youth, Fleurie, and others.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/tommee-profitt-mn0001431651/credits|title=Tommee Profitt {{!}} Credits|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=2019-11-14}}</ref>
