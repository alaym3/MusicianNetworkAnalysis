'''Imelda Mary Higham''' (born '''Imelda Mary Clabby'''; 10 July 1974), professionally known as '''Imelda May''', is an [[Irish people|Irish]] singer, songwriter and multi-instrumentalist. Known primarily as a singer, she also plays the [[bodhrán]], [[guitar]], [[bass guitar]] and tambourine. Described as "a unique vocal talent", May is known for her musical style of rockabilly revival and has also been compared to female [[jazz]] musicians such as [[Billie Holiday]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.state.ie/26519-features/choice-music-prize-preview/2|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120912193502/http://www.state.ie/26519-features/choice-music-prize-preview/2|url-status=dead|archive-date=12 September 2012|title=Choice Music Prize Preview|last=Forbes|first=Anna|date=3 March 2011|work=[[State (magazine)|State Magazine]] |access-date=19 December 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/imelda-may-p1022669 |title=Imelda May |last=True |first=Chris |year=2010 |work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=19 December 2011}}</ref>

Born and raised in [[the Liberties, Dublin|The Liberties]] area of [[Dublin]], May began her career in music at 16 by performing with a number of local bands and musicians. She formed her own band in 2002, and released her debut studio album, ''[[No Turning Back (Imelda May album)|No Turning Back]]''. After the release, May relocated to [[London]] with her then-husband, guitarist [[Darrel Higham]].

Following an appearance on the [[BBC]] music programme ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]'' in 2008,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/av/entertainment-arts-11128987/singer-imelda-may-on-records-and-rockabilly |title=Singer Imelda May on records and rockabilly |website=BBC News |access-date=12 June 2018}}</ref> she released her second studio album, ''[[Love Tattoo]]'' (2009). She collaborated and toured with a number of artists following its release. Her third studio album, ''[[Mayhem (Imelda May album)|Mayhem]]'', was released in 2010 and earned her a nomination for the [[Choice Music Prize]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/choice-music-prize-1.582284 |title=Choice Music Prize |last=Gleeson |first=Sinead |date=25 February 2011 |work=The Irish Times |access-date=12 June 2018}}</ref> Her fourth studio album, ''[[Tribal (Imelda May album)|Tribal]]'', came out in 2014, and her fifth, ''[[Life Love Flesh Blood]]'', in 2017. [[11 Past the Hour]] was released on 16 April 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Imelda-Mays-New-Album-11-Past-the-Hour-Out-Tomorrow-20210415|title=Imelda May's New Album '11 Past the Hour' Out Tomorrow|website=[[BroadwayWorld]]|date=15 April 2021|access-date=16 April 2021}}</ref>

==Early life and career==
May was born in Dublin on 10 July 1974 in [[The Liberties, Dublin|The Liberties]] in the south inner city.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/thats-alright-mama-singer-imelda-may-on-family-life-30212408.html |title=That's Alright, Mama: Singer Imelda May on family life - Independent.ie |last=Egan |first=Barry |date=23 April 2014 |work=[[Irish Independent]] |access-date=12 June 2018}}</ref> She is the youngest of five siblings, with sisters Edel Foy and Maria O'Reilly and brothers Brendan Clabby and Fintan Clabby. In 1991, she attended Senior College Ballyfermot, where she studied art, graphics and printmaking.

Her career began at age 16 when she began touring the Dublin club circuit and she was occasionally barred from her own shows at Dublin's Bruxelles club for being under-aged.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}} She moved to [[Great Britain]] in 1998 and shortly after toured with [[Mike Sanchez]].

==Professional career==
[[File:Imelda May, Cambridge Festivals 2001-2014 (4854631312).jpg|thumb|May at the [[Cambridge Folk Festival]] 2010]]

In 2003, May released ''[[No Turning Back (Imelda May album)|No Turning Back]]''. In 2007 she signed a recording contract with Ambassador Records, and recorded her second album, ''[[Love Tattoo]]'', which reached No.1 in Ireland and received wide critical acclaim. She caught the attention of [[Jools Holland]], whom she later supported on tour, which led him to request she appear on his music show ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]''. She won Female Artist of the Year at the [[2009 Meteor Awards]].

{{Quote| quote=Some people think the only way of doing well or of having a career in music is to go the ''X Factor'' route, but a lot of people lose the joy out of music by going that way, possibly because they're so incredibly focused on other people's ideas of success. |source=Imelda May in 2009, selected as a quote of that year by ''[[The Irish Times]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/features/2009/1231/1224261472213.html |title=Quotes of the year |date=31 December 2009 |access-date=2 January 2010 |work=[[The Irish Times]]}}</ref>}}

On 31 January 2010, she performed at the [[52nd Grammy Awards]] with [[Jeff Beck]] in tribute to [[Les Paul]] and [[Mary Ford]]. She released her third studio album, ''[[Mayhem (Imelda May album)|Mayhem]]'', in Ireland on 3 September 2010, again reaching No. 1 in the Irish Album Charts, and in the United Kingdom on 4 October 2010.

[[File:Imelda May en Madgarden Festival 2015 - 12.jpg|thumb|left|May at the Madgarden Festival 2015]]
May soon after appeared on ''[[The Tonight Show with Jay Leno]]'', ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]'' and ''[[The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson]]''. In 2016, May performed at [[RTÉ]]'s Centenary Concert to mark the 100th anniversary of the [[1916 Rising]]. In the UK May appeared on ''[[Never Mind the Buzzcocks]]'' with the late [[Terry Wogan]] and ''[[The Graham Norton Show|Graham Norton Show]]'' and sang live on ''[[Strictly Come Dancing]]''. May worked with BIMM music college in Dublin to provide a scholarship (which they named after her) for up-and-coming artists.

May's fifth album, ''[[Life Love Flesh Blood]]'', was released on 7 April 2017. She collaborated with American musician [[T Bone Burnett]] on the record, who produced the album. Throughout the creative process, May received guidance from [[U2]] vocalist [[Bono]]. ''Life Love Flesh Blood'' won critical acclaim and reached no. 4 in the UK charts. On 18 May 2017 May performed ''Life Love Flesh Blood'' at a special concert in the prestigious [[London Palladium]].

In Ireland, she also had her own music TV show, ''[[The Imelda May Show]]'', showcasing the best of Irish and international talent. May performed the Irish national anthem on 26 August 2017 at the [[T-Mobile Arena]] in Las Vegas, Nevada, prior to the [[Floyd Mayweather Jr. vs. Conor McGregor]] fight. In June 2020, she released the original composition ''You Don’t Get To Be Racist And Irish'' which was used by the Irish government for the nationwide billboard campaign [https://rethinkireland.ie/ ReThink Ireland].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-02-23|title=Interview with Imelda May about music, art, poetry and the new duet with Noel Gallagher and Ronnie Wood|url=https://the-shortlisted.co.uk/imelda-may-interview/|access-date=2021-05-10|website=The Shortlisted|language=en-GB}}</ref> In 2020 she also published a new album, ''[[Slip of the Tongue]]'', whose style shifts from rock to [[spoken word]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-29|title=Album Review: Imelda May trades rockabilly for spoken word on “Slip of the Tongue” |url=https://www.theyoungfolks.com/music/147840/album-review-imelda-may-trades-rockabilly-for-spoken-word-on-slip-of-the-tongue/ |access-date=2021-11-08|website=The Young Folks|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Her second solo album apart from her former husband, ''[[11 Past the Hour]]'' was released on 16 April 2021 on the [[Decca Records|Decca]] label.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Imelda-Mays-New-Album-11-Past-the-Hour-Out-Tomorrow-20210415|title=Imelda May's New Album '11 Past the Hour' Out Tomorrow|website=[[BroadwayWorld]]|date=15 April 2021|access-date=16 April 2021}}</ref> Also in 2021, May contributed a cover of the Metallica song "[[The God That Failed (song)|The God That Failed]]" to the charity tribute album ''[[The Metallica Blacklist]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=He|first=Richard S.|date=2021-09-10|title=Every Metallica Blacklist cover ranked from worst to best|url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/every-metallica-blacklist-cover-ranked-from-worst-to-best|access-date=2021-10-24|website=loudersound|language=en}}</ref>

===Performances with other artists===
[[File:Imelda May and Jeff Beck 28 Mar2011.jpg|thumb|Imelda May and [[Jeff Beck]] at the [[Beacon Theatre (New York City)|Beacon Theatre]], 28 March 2011]]

May has recorded with, and performed with, a variety of artists. She has worked with producers [[Mike Crossey]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mikecrossey.com/bio/ |title=Bio |last=Crossey |first=Mike |website=www.mikecrossey.com|access-date=19 April 2019}}</ref> [[Tony Visconti]], [[Peter Asher]] and [[T Bone Burnett]].<ref name="Ind-Mugan">{{Cite news|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/imelda-may-life-love-flesh-blood-bob-dylan-darrel-higham-divorce-ireland-a7764531.html |title=Imelda May on her new album, having Bob Dylan as a fan and divorce |last=Mugan |first=Chris |date=1 June 2017 |work=[[The Independent]] |access-date=19 April 2019}}</ref>

She performed at a tribute concert for [[Bill Wyman]]'s 80th birthday, a live recorded concert for [[Jacques Dutronc]], and a live recorded concert with and for Tony Biscontu for [[Sky Arts]].{{citation needed|date=October 2018}} In 2010 she performed with [[Jeff Beck]] honoring [[Les Paul]] at the [[Iridium Jazz Club]] in [[New York City]], where the session was recorded for DVD and [[HBO]] and subsequently toured the US with [[Jeff Beck]] for five weeks. In December 2011 she sold out two nights at [[O2 Dublin]], with [[Bono]] making a surprise appearance as her guest. May was the surprise guest in Dublin for U2's homecoming show on the band's 2015 Innocence + Experience tour: Bono called her "the other queen of Ireland" as she came on stage to perform "Desire", streamed live online to millions of viewers.<ref>{{Cite web|last=U2News |title=U2 – Desire (ft. Imelda May) – Dublin 28/11/2015 |date=28 November 2015 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dt7z5yvZpAs |website=YouTube |access-date=12 June 2018}}</ref>

In 2020, May was part of an Irish collective of female singers and musicians called [[Irish Women in Harmony]] that recorded a version of "[[Dreams (The Cranberries song)|Dreams]]" in aid of the charity SafeIreland, which deals with [[domestic abuse]], which had reportedly risen significantly during the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the Republic of Ireland|COVID-19 lockdown]].<ref name="it-1">{{cite news |last=Bol |first=Rosita |title=Irish Women in Harmony record Cranberries song in aid of Safe Ireland |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/irish-women-in-harmony-record-cranberries-song-in-aid-of-safe-ireland-1.4283348 |access-date=3 July 2020 |work=The Irish Times}}</ref>

In 2021, she dueted with [[Noel Gallagher]] and [[Ronnie Wood]] of [[The Rolling Stones]] in the single ''Just One Kiss'' from the album [[11 Past the Hour|''11 Past the Hour.'']]<ref>{{Cite web|last=Peacock|first=Tim|date=2021-01-27|title=Watch The Video For Imelda May & Noel Gallagher’s Just One Kiss|url=https://www.udiscovermusic.com/news/imelda-may-noel-gallagher-just-kiss/|access-date=2021-05-10|website=uDiscover Music|language=en-US}}</ref> and, together with [[The Waterboys]] and the Joanna Eden Band, she joined the line-up for [[Van Morrison]]'s outdoor show at [[Audley End House|Audley End House and Gardens]] expected for 14 August 2021 in [[Saffron Walden]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=GCE Heritage Live: Van Morrison at Audley End|url=https://www.english-heritage.org.uk/visit/whats-on/audley-end-gce-van-morrison-sat-14-aug-2021/|access-date=2021-05-10|website=English Heritage}}</ref>

==Personal life==
May married her guitarist and band member, [[Darrel Higham]], in 2002. She gave birth to a daughter in August 2012.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.herald.ie/news/imelda-mays-delight-at-birth-of-baby-girl-violet-28016114.html |title=Imelda May's delight at birth of baby girl Violet |first=Laura |last=Butler |date=25 August 2012 |newspaper=[[The Herald (Ireland)|The Herald]] |location=Dublin |access-date=11 February 2017}}</ref> On 17 July 2015, May announced the couple's decision to separate after 13 years of marriage.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/2017/0408/866281-imelda-may-on-marriage-break-up-weve-both-moved-on/ |title=Imelda May on marriage break-up: 'We've both moved on' |date=9 April 2017 |work=[[Raidió Teilifís Éireann|RTÉ]] |location=Dublin |access-date=8 October 2019}}</ref>
