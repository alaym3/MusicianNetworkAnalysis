'''Auktyon''' ({{lang-ru|АукцЫон}}, {{IPA-ru|ɐʊktsɨˈon|pron}}) is a Russian [[alternative rock]] band from [[Saint Petersburg]].

The band was founded by [[Leonid Fyodorov (musician)|Leonid Fyodorov]] at the [[Saint Petersburg Polytechnical University|Polytechnic Institute of Leningrad]].<ref name=Beumers>{{cite book|last=Beumers |first=Birgit |title=Pop Culture Russia! |year=2005 |place=Santa Barbara |publisher=ABC-CLIO |isbn=1-85109-459-8}}</ref> Though they originally played [[post-punk]] and [[New wave music|new wave]], the group came to be influenced by European and Central Asian folk music, avant-garde jazz, the poetry of Russian futurist [[Velimir Khlebnikov]], and aspects of Russian high culture and literature.<ref name=Beumers/>

Auktyon was particularly popular from 1987 through 1995, but became less active in the late 1990s when Fyodorov began to produce records for [[Leningrad (band)|Leningrad]].<ref name=Beumers/> The group continues to play together and to release new albums. They toured the [[United States]] in 2006<ref>Mitter, Siddhartha (24 March 2006). "By way of Russia, art-rock for art's sake." ''Boston Globe'' Boston, Massachusetts. pg. D.1</ref> in support of their first US release, the album ''Pioneer''. The group returned to the US in 2008 when they released the album ''Girls Sing.''<ref>Vigil, Delfin (27 March 2008). "Auktyon. Eclectic band formed in the former Soviet Union and, 20 years later, strange, loud rock plays a key role in the group's musical stylings." ''San Francisco Chronicle''. San Francisco, California. pg. G.4</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Drozdowski |first=Ted |url=http://thephoenix.com/Boston/music/58205-auktyon-girls-sing/ |title=Auktyon - CD Reviews |publisher=The Phoenix |date=18 March 2008 |accessdate=2012-09-19 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121014185438/http://thephoenix.com/Boston/music/58205-auktyon-girls-sing/ |archivedate=2012-10-14 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Horowitz |first=Steve |url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/auktyon-girls-sing/ |title=Auktyon: Girls Sing |publisher= PopMatters |date=31 March 2008 |accessdate=2012-09-19}}</ref>
