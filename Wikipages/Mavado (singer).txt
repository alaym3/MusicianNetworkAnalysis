'''David Constantine Brooks''' (born 30 November 1980),<ref name=":0" /> better known by his [[stage name]] '''Mavado''', is a Jamaican [[dancehall]] singer, who is signed to [[We the Best Music Group]] and formerly to [[Cash Money Records]].

==Biography==
David Constantine Brooks was raised in [[Kingston, Jamaica]]'s Cassava Piece community.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/mavado-mn0000475682/biography|title=Mavado {{!}} Biography & History|last=Jeffries|first=David|publisher=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> He cites the music of [[Bounty Killer]] as an early influence.<ref name=":0" /> Bounty took him under his wing to show him the ropes of the music industry and introduced him to his manager, Julian Jones-Griffith.<ref name=billboard>[http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1049714/mavado-life-after-death Mavado: Life After Death]. ''Billboard''. Retrieved 2011-3-8.</ref> He decided to name himself "Mavado" after the Swiss watch company [[Movado]], with his manager altering the spelling.<ref name="cooke">Cooke, Mel. Mavado: dancehall lovers/haters' fantasy. {{Cite web |url=http://jamaica-gleaner.com/gleaner/20080427/ent/ent4.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=8 March 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121008175331/http://jamaica-gleaner.com/gleaner/20080427/ent/ent4.html |archive-date=8 October 2012 |url-status=bot: unknown }}. ''Jamaica Gleaner''. 27 April 2008. Retrieved 2011-3-8.</ref>

In 2005, Mavado had his breakthrough with his first single, "Real McKoy".<ref name="cooke" /> He further established himself with the follow-up record "Weh Dem a Do", on the Red Bull & Guinness riddim, that had the distinction of hitting Billboard. After a string of hits in 2005 and 2006,{{Citation needed|date=December 2019}} Mavado released the album ''[[Gangsta for Life: The Symphony of David Brooks]]'' 10 July 2007 on [[VP Records]].<ref>Cole, Debra. [http://www.yardflex.com/archives/001362.html Mavado - "Gangsta For Life: The Symphony of David Brooks"]. YardFlex. 6 June 2007. Retrieved 2011-3-8.</ref> The single "Dying" from the album was featured heavily on New York's [[WQHT|WQHT (Hot 97)]] R&B/hip-hop radio station.<ref name=billboard/>

That same year brought Mavado off-stage controversy, as he was detained by Jamaican police.<ref name=billboard/><ref name=cooke/> While in police custody, the singer claims he was thrown through a glass window, slicing three fingers in the process, which were reattached at a local hospital. Police claimed Mavado escaped from custody while at the hospital.<ref name=billboard/> He was later arrested and charged with shooting with intent and illegal possession of firearm in relation to an event occurring 27 July 2007.<ref name=copsseek>[http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20101110/news/news2.html Cops seek Mavado, Kartel] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713075026/http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20101110/news/news2.html |date=13 July 2011 }}. ''Jamaica Star''. 10 January 2010. Retrieved 2011-3-8.</ref> Mavado was released two days later on a $3000 bail<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/music-mavado-dc-idUSN0419664620080405|title=Controversial Jamaican singer faces the music|author=Patrica Meschino|date=5 April 2008|access-date=31 July 2020}}</ref> and was subsequently refused entry into the United States.<ref name="cooke" />

Mavado did not sign the [[Reggae Compassionate Act]] and has been criticised for anti-gay lyrics (in such songs as <nowiki>''</nowiki>Batty Bwoy Termination<nowiki>''</nowiki> where the lyrics say "Battyboy must die, Lesbian must die, Sodomite must die"), as well as alleged promotion of gun violence. The issue caused him to be banned at times from several Caribbean countries.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://shadowproof.com/2012/06/17/who-is-mavado-and-what-is-he-doing-now/|title=Who Is Mavado And What Is He Doing Now?|date=2012-06-18|website=[[Shadowproof]]|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://blog.svd.se/kultur/2012/04/04/protester-mot-ny-reggaespelning/|title=Sveriges kvalitetssajt för nyheter|website=SvD.se|language=sv|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.tt/neo/NeoProxy.dll?app=NeoDirect&com=6/2/443456/d32e3019ab|title=Banned Mavado gets 'one-off' show in T&T|last=Alexander|first=Gail|date=2011-06-01|website=[[Trinidad and Tobago Guardian]]|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.caribbean360.com/news/guyana-lifts-ban-on-jamaican-reggae-artist-mavado|title=Guyana lifts ban on Jamaican reggae artist Mavado|date=2009-09-11|website=Caribbean360|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref>

In March 2008, Mavado was featured performing a rendition of his song "Real McKoy" for [[Grand Theft Auto IV]] Trailer #4 "Everyone's a Rat". "Real McKoy", along with "Last Night" (both from the album ''Gangsta for Life''), were also featured in the game on the radio station "Massive B Radio".<ref name=hypelifemagazine>[http://hypelifemagazine.com/celebritynews/mavado-vybz-kartel-rich-net-worth/ Is Mavado Richer Than Vybz Kartel?]. ''Hype Life Magazine'', 27 October 2008 . Retrieved 29 October 2016.</ref> Mavado was also featured on the [[G-Unit]] track 'Let It Go' along with [[Tony Yayo]] and [[Lloyd Banks]] for the album: ''[[T·O·S (Terminate on Sight)]]'' in 2008. He was the only non-G-Unit feature on the album. In 2008 Mavado was given the UK [[MOBO Awards|MOBO Award]] for Best Reggae Act.

In April 2010, Mavado starred in a music video as a gangster in [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]]'s song "[[Find Your Love]]" for Drake's album ''[[Thank Me Later]]''. Mavado formed his own label, Mansion Records, in 2011, which debuted with the single "Delilah".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20110517/ent/ent1.html |title=Mavado forms own label - Set to release new single soon |author=Brooks, Sadeke |date=17 May 2011 |work=Jamaica Star|access-date=18 May 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110520230504/http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20110517/ent/ent1.html |archive-date=20 May 2011 }}</ref>
Along with Bounty Killer, he has cited [[2Pac]] as a childhood idol, comparing the late rapper's life to his own.<ref name=billboard/>

In 2011, Mavado recorded with U.K. [[Grime (music)|grime]] artist [[Chip (rapper)|Chipmunk]] on the track "Every Gyal".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.soulculture.co.uk/blogs/chipmunk-feat-mavado-every-gyal-new-music/ |title=Chipmunk feat. Mavado – "Every Gyal" |author=Sparks, Marvin |date=13 April 2011 |publisher=Soul Culture |access-date=2011-06-07}}</ref> On 1 September 2011, Mavado appeared on the [[Angie Martinez]] show on New York's Hot 97 radio station to announce that he had signed a deal with [[DJ Khaled]]'s record label [[We the Best Music Group]]. Mavado released three singles "Emergency" featuring [[Ace Hood]], Soulja girl and "Survivor" featuring [[Akon]] on [[We the Best Music Group]].

In May 2012 he was convicted of assault occasioning actual bodily harm and malicious destruction of property after a driving incident, and was fined a total of $100,000.<ref name="ABH">"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Mavado-loses-conviction-appeal_15283682#ixzz2iF6aJVzY Mavado loses conviction appeal] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131019191709/http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Mavado-loses-conviction-appeal_15283682 |date=19 October 2013 }}", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 19 October 2013. Retrieved 20 October 2013</ref> An appeal against the sentence was unsuccessful.<ref name="ABH" /> In 2012, it was announced that Mavado would feature in a "Shottas 2" a sequel to the [[Shottas]] movie, which had starred Ky-Mani Marley and Spragga Benz.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://old.jamaica-star.com/thestar/20120424/ent/ent2.html|title=Mavado to star in Shottas 2    - Entertainment - Jamaica Star - April 24, 2012|last=Online|first=Jamaica Star|website=old.jamaica-star.com|access-date=2016-12-16}}</ref>

In 2016, Mavado's song "Progress" on the Mineral Boss Records produced "Money Boss Riddim" was featured in [[DJ Khaled]]'s ''[[Major Key (album)|Major Key]]'' album. The album was nominated for the 2017 [[59th Annual Grammy Awards|Grammy Awards]] on the "best rap" album category. In 2017, Mavado, [[Alkaline (musician)|Alkaline]] and Jahmiel formed a camp known as MVP, which Mavado claimed to be the "biggest thing" in dancehall at that time.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://thetropixs.com/mavado-says-mvp-is-biggest-thing-in-dancehall/|title=Mavado Says MVP Is Biggest Thing in Dancehall|date=2017-08-20|work=The Tropixs|access-date=2018-03-12|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2018, Mavado collaborated with [[Sean Paul]] for an inspirational song called "I'm Sanctify".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://jamaica-star.com/article/entertainment/20180309/sean-paul-mavado-cry-peace-through-im-sanctify|title=Sean Paul, Mavado cry for peace through 'I'm Sanctify'|website=jamaica-star.com|language=en|access-date=2018-03-12}}</ref> In 2019, Mavado released a song called "Top Shotta Is Back" On a Riddim called The Aircraft Riddim produced by Chimney Records.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2019/12/06/music/mavado-declares-war-with-new-track-top-shotta-is-back-listen.html|title=Mavado Declares War With New Track 'Top Shotta Is Back': Listen|website=dancehallmag.com|language=en|access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref>

In 2020, Mavado recorded and released a Drake diss song called "Enemy Line".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mavado Accuses Drake of Appropriating Dancehall on New Song "Enemy Line"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/07/mavado-enemy-line-accuses-drake-appropriating-dancehall|access-date=2020-08-12|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>
