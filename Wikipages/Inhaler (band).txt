'''Inhaler''' are an Irish rock band originating from [[Dublin]]. The band consists of vocalist/guitarist Elijah Hewson, bassist Robert Keating, guitarist Josh Jenkinson and drummer Ryan McMahon.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.radiox.co.uk/features/who-are-inhaler-band-bono-son-elijah-hewson/|title=Everything you need to know about Inhaler, featuring Bono's son Elijah Hewson|date=6 January 2020|work=[[Radio X (United Kingdom)|Radio X]]|url-status=live|accessdate=10 January 2020}}</ref> The band were tipped for success in 2020 when they ranked at number 5 in [[BBC]]'s [[Sound of 2020|Sound of...]] music poll.<ref name="Sound of 2020">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-50712447|title=BBC Sound of 2020: Bono's son and his band Inhaler tipped for success|last=Youngs|first=Ian|date=5 January 2020|work=[[BBC News]]|url-status=live|accessdate=10 January 2020}}</ref> Inhaler have released nine singles, "I Want You", "It Won't Always Be Like This", "My Honest Face", "Ice Cream Sundae",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-blogs/inhaler-dublin-band-interview-2019-2564286|title=Inhaler are the Dubliners who want rock to rage again – and shake off those U2 comparisons|last=Hunt|first=El|date=4 November 2019|work=[[NME]]|url-status=live|accessdate=10 January 2020}}</ref> "We Have to Move On", "Falling In", "When It Breaks", "Cheer Up Baby", and, most recently, "Who's Your Money On (Plastic House)". Their debut album, ''[[It Won't Always Be Like This]]'', was released on 9 July 2021.  It entered the Irish Albums Chart at number one,  it also entered the top 10 and top 20 album charts in other European countries such as the Netherlands and Germany <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/inhaler-claim-first-ever-irma-number-1-award-as-it-won-t-always-be-like-this-tops-official-irish-albums-chart__33600/|title = Inhaler debut at Number 1 with It Won't Always be Like This}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dutchcharts.nl/weekchart.asp?cat=a|title = Dutch Charts - dutchcharts.nl}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/charts/album?rCH=2|title=Offizielle Deutsche Charts - Offizielle Deutsche Charts}}</ref>
It entered the UK Album Chart at number one.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/inhaler-score-number-1-debut-album-with-it-won-t-always-be-like-this__33606/|title = Inhaler score Number 1 debut album: "It means the absolute world"}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2015–2017: Early years ===
Originally formed in 2012 at [[St Andrew's College, Dublin|St Andrews College]] in [[Blackrock, Dublin|Blackrock]], [[Dublin]], the band only decided on the name Inhaler in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://faroutmagazine.co.uk/introducing-inhaler-band/|title=Introducing Inhaler, a breathless new band with some power-pop prowess|date=21 July 2019|website=Far Out Magazine|language=en-GB|url-status=live|access-date=12 January 2020}}</ref><ref name=":0" /> Jenkinson joined the band right after the band name was decided.

=== 2018–present: ''It Won't Always Be Like This'' ===
The group self-released their debut single, "I Want You", in 2017.  The single was featured on ''Garageland Volume 1'', released on 14 April 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.top6.ie/garageland-volume-1/|title=Garageland. Volume 1 – Top 6|access-date=2020-01-22}}</ref> This was later followed by three more singles in 2019, namely "It Won't Always Be Like This", "My Honest Face" and "Ice Cream Sundae". They placed fifth on [[BBC]]'s annual music poll, [[Sound of 2020]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/articles/4hf3JpfgFysgsBdpf7R0SbJ/sound-of-2020|title=Sound of 2020 Shortlist|website=BBC|access-date=2021-01-16}}</ref> On 21 January 2020, the group released their fourth single, titled "We Have to Move On".<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/inhaler-dublin-irish-band-bono-u2-909647/|title=Inhaler: How an Asthmatic Irish Band Went From Talent Shows to Interscope|last=Ehrlich|first=Brenna|date=2020-01-21|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=2020-01-23}}</ref>

On their 2019–2020 tours, the band released a self-titled debut [[Extended play|EP]] featuring the singles "It Won't Always Be Like This", "Oklahoma" (Late Night Version), "My Honest Face" and "There's No Other Place".
 
For [[Record Store Day]] 2020, the band released their singles as limited-edition vinyl records.<ref>{{Cite web|last=White|first=Jack|date=5 March 2020|title=Christy Moore, Dermot Kennedy and Inhaler among Irish acts releasing limited edition vinyl for Record Store Day 2020|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/christy-moore-dermot-kennedy-and-inhaler-among-irish-acts-releasing-limited-edition-vinyl-for-record-store-day-2020__28947/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-06-24|website=Official Charts|language=en}}</ref>

The band were one of the winners of the Music Moves Europe Talent Award 2021<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotpress.com/music/inhaler-win-at-music-moves-european-talent-awards-2021-22838902?fbclid=IwAR2DtzH18J8qtb61LNziIoc1Cj49PZQm8hGN8ku423lNxgXqYKlEDivPyus|title=Inhaler Win At Music Moves European Talent Awards 2021|date=2021-01-15|website=HotPress|access-date=2021-01-16}}</ref> and were also shortlisted for the MTV PUSH UK & Ireland 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.music-news.com/news/Underground/136146/MTV-PUSH-UK-IRE-announce-the-2021-shortlist|title=MTV PUSH UK & IRE announce the 2021 shortlist|date=2020-11-19|website=Music News|access-date=2020-11-20}}</ref>

On 17 March 2021, the band released a single titled "Cheer Up Baby" and announced that their debut album, ''[[It Won't Always Be Like This]]'', would be released on 16 July 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-17|title=Inhaler talk debut album and new single 'Cheer Up Baby': "It's a love letter to all our fans who are feeling isolated"|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/inhaler-debut-album-it-wont-always-be-like-this-cheer-up-baby-interview-2901846|access-date=2021-03-17|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref> On 13 May 2021, the band announced on their social media that the album release would be pushed forward by a week, to be released on 9 July 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/inhaler-it-wont-always-be-like-this-review-radar-2986572 |title=Inhaler – 'It Won't Always Be Like This' review: defiance, optimism and plenty of guitars |work=NME |date=8 July 2021 |access-date=10 July 2021 |author=Kemp, Ella}}</ref>
