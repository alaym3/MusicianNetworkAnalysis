'''GFriend''' (stylized in [[all caps]]; {{korean|hangul=여자친구|rr=Yeoja Chingu}}) was a six-member South Korean [[girl group]] formed by [[Source Music]] in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/85237/new-rookie-group-gfriend-makes-debut-with-glass-bead-mv|title=New Rookie Group GFriend Makes Debut with ′Glass Bead′ MV|publisher=Mwave|date=January 16, 2015|access-date=January 16, 2015|archive-url=https://archive.today/20170105172440/http://mwave.interest.me/en/kpop-news/article/85237/new-rookie-group-gfriend-makes-debut-with-glass-bead-mv|archive-date=January 5, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> The group consisted of Sowon<!--DO NOT WIKILINK AS NO ARTICLE EXISTS YET DUE TO FAILING TO MEET [[WP:SINGER]] GUIDELINES -->, [[Yerin (entertainer)|Yerin]], [[Eunha (singer)|Eunha]], [[Yuju (singer)|Yuju]], [[SinB]], and [[Umji]]. They made their debut with the [[Extended play|EP]] ''[[Season of Glass (EP)|Season of Glass]]'' on January 15, 2015. GFriend won several 2015 female rookie awards and has garnered momentum since their debut despite being from a small company.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/86112/interview-gfriend-spills-on-snsd-comparisons-role-models-and-more|title=GFriend Spills on SNSD Comparisons, Role Models and More|publisher=Newsen|date=January 31, 2015|access-date=January 31, 2015|archive-url=https://archive.today/20150823215622/http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/86112/interview-gfriend-spills-on-snsd-comparisons-role-models-and-more|archive-date=August 23, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> GFriend also became the first-ever girl group to be interviewed for the [[Grammy Awards|Grammy Mini Masterclass]], an interview series organized by the US [[The Recording Academy|Grammy's Museum Experience Prudential Center]] where artists, music industry workers, and musicians talk about music to influence the youth.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.dispatch.co.kr/2119228 | title=여자친구, 그래미 뮤지엄 인터뷰..."K팝 걸그룹 최초, 초대 받았다" | publisher=Dispatch | language=ko | access-date=December 16, 2020 | archive-date=December 27, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201227160255/https://www.dispatch.co.kr/2119228 | url-status=live }}</ref>

In 2016, they continued their success with their third EP, [[Snowflake (EP)|''Snowflake'']], and won first place on many music shows with the title song, "[[Rough (song)|Rough]]". They released their first full-length album, ''[[LOL (GFriend album)|LOL]]'', in July 2016. In 2017, GFriend made a comeback with a new concept with their fourth EP, [[The Awakening (EP)|''The Awakening'']], and the pre-orders for the album exceeded 100,000 units.<ref name="auto">{{cite news|url=http://mwave.interest.me/en/kpop-news/article/112981/gfriends-the-awakening-sells-over-100000-copies-in-pre-sales-all-kpop-news|publisher=mwave.interest.me|access-date=April 22, 2017|date=March 6, 2017|first=Susan|last=Min|title=GFriend′s ′The Awakening′ Sells Over 100,000 Copies in Pre-Sales|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170422130300/http://mwave.interest.me/en/kpop-news/article/112981/gfriends-the-awakening-sells-over-100000-copies-in-pre-sales-all-kpop-news|archive-date=April 22, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> GFriend released their fifth EP, [[Parallel (EP)|''Parallel'']], in August 2017, and the extended play was re-released a month later under the title [[Parallel (EP)#Rainbow|''Rainbow'']]. In 2018, GFriend held their first concert since debut called ''Season of GFriend''<ref>{{cite news|url=http://english.yonhapnews.co.kr/news/2017/11/28/0200000000AEN20171128006400315.html|publisher=Yonhap News|access-date=Jan 11, 2018|date=Nov 28, 2017|title=Gfriend to hold first solo concerts|archive-date=January 1, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180101071256/http://english.yonhapnews.co.kr/news/2017/11/28/0200000000AEN20171128006400315.html|url-status=live}}</ref> and later their first Asia Tour with the same name.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://english.yonhapnews.co.kr/culturesports/2018/01/11/0701000000AEN20180111005600315.html?sns=tw|publisher=Yonhap News|access-date=Jan 11, 2018|date=Jan 11, 2018|title=Gfriend to hold first-ever Asia tour|archive-date=January 11, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180111165232/http://english.yonhapnews.co.kr/culturesports/2018/01/11/0701000000AEN20180111005600315.html?sns=tw|url-status=live}}</ref>

GFriend released their sixth EP, ''[[Time for the Moon Night]]'', in April 2018 and the special EP ''[[Sunny Summer]]'' in July 2018. GFriend made an official Japanese debut with a compilation album, ''GFriend 1st Best'', in May 2018. In January 2019, GFriend released their second full-length album, ''[[Time for Us|Time For Us]]'', and released their seventh extended play album, ''[[Fever Season (EP)|Fever Season]]'' in July that year. The group's final releases consisted of the "回" series, which consisted of two extended plays and their third studio album. GFriend disbanded on May 22, 2021 after all members left Source Music upon the completion of their six-year contract.

The group garnered international attention after a fan-taken video of GFriend members continuing and completing a performance of "Me Gustas Tu" despite continuously falling on a slippery stage went viral in September 2015. The group was praised for their professionalism, especially [[Yuju (singer)|Yuju]], who fell a total of five times and suffered a twisted finger during the performance.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Regan|first1=Helen|url=http://time.com/4024228/k-pop-gfriend-fall-korea-yuju/|title=This Video of a K-Pop Singer Falling 8 Times in 1 Song Will Inspire You to Keep Going No Matter What|publisher=Time|date=September 7, 2015|access-date=September 7, 2015|archive-date=September 17, 2015|archive-url=https://archive.today/20150917161430/http://time.com/4024228/k-pop-gfriend-fall-korea-yuju/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Jang Mi-ran|title=GFriend′s Yuju Talks About Viral Video of Her Falling on Stage|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/102104/gfriends-yuju-talks-about-viral-video-of-her-falling-on-stage|publisher=CJ E&M Corp.|access-date=February 25, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160213015635/http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/102104/gfriends-yuju-talks-about-viral-video-of-her-falling-on-stage|archive-date=February 13, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On September 17, 2015, GFriend made headlines again as the only girl group to be nominated in the [[2015 MTV Europe Music Awards]] "Best Korean Act" category alongside boy groups [[B1A4]], [[BTS]], [[Got7]], and [[VIXX]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.chosun.com/site/data/html_dir/2015/09/17/2015091700699.html|title=여자친구, MTV EMA 韓최우수 가수 부문 후보..'걸그룹 유일'|publisher=Chosun|date=September 17, 2015|language=ko|access-date=February 7, 2016|archive-date=July 13, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180713134520/http://news.chosun.com/site/data/html_dir/2015/09/17/2015091700699.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2016: ''Snowflake'', first music show wins, ''Showtime'' and ''LOL''===
On January 25, 2016, GFriend released their third EP titled ''[[Snowflake (EP)|Snowflake]]'' with the lead single "[[Rough (song)|Rough]]". "Rough" is the last song to complete GFriend's high school concept trilogy along with "Glass Bead" and "Me Gustas Tu". GFriend started the promotions for ''Snowflake'' on January 26 on [[SBS MTV]]'s music program ''[[The Show (South Korean TV series)|The Show]]''. The album debuted at number ten on [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']]'s World Albums Chart,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/world-albums/2016-02-13|title=World Albums (The week of February 13, 2016)|publisher=Billboard|date=February 13, 2016|access-date=November 13, 2019|archive-date=May 23, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200523062404/https://www.billboard.com/charts/world-albums/2016-02-13|url-status=live}}</ref> and the music video of "Rough" on [[YouTube]] ranked number three in top ten "Most Viewed K-Pop Videos Around the World in January 2016".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/6874855/most-viewed-k-pop-videos-mvs-america-world-january-2016|title=Most Viewed K-Pop Videos in America, Around the World: January 2016|publisher=Billboard|date=February 11, 2016|access-date=March 11, 2016|archive-date=July 17, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180717234609/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/6874855/most-viewed-k-pop-videos-mvs-america-world-january-2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Awards and nominations==
{{Main|List of awards and nominations received by GFriend}}

GFriend got their first-ever major award for their debut extended play ''[[Season of Glass (EP)|Season of Glass]]'', released in January 2015, as the "Best New Female Artist" at [[Melon Music Awards]] on November 7, 2015.<ref name="melon20152016">{{cite news|url=http://english.yonhapnews.co.kr/kwave/2016/11/19/16/3001000000AEN20161119003200315F.html|date=November 20, 2016|access-date=January 1, 2017|publisher=Yonhap News Agency|title=BTS, EXO, TWICE top 2016 Melon Music Awards|author=Chung Joo-won|archive-date=February 20, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180220103322/http://english.yonhapnews.co.kr/kwave/2016/11/19/16/3001000000AEN20161119003200315F.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The group also won several new artist awards between late 2015 to early 2016, including at the 5th [[Gaon Chart Music Awards]], the 30th [[Golden Disc Awards]] and the 25th [[Seoul Music Awards]]. The group received their first Grand Prize (Daesang) award at the 2016 Korean PD Awards.

Their first music show win was on ''[[The Show (South Korean TV series)|The Show]]'' with "Rough" on February 2, 2016, after which the group went on to receive a total of 15 music show wins during the song's promotion. During their "Navillera" promotions they got a total of 14 music show wins, making them the girl group with most wins gained within a year with a total of 29 wins.<ref>{{cite news|language=ko|url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=109&aid=0003375483|title=[뮤직톡톡]'걸그룹 새 역사'...여자친구, #29관왕 #트리플 #더블텐|publisher=entertain.naver.com|access-date=December 19, 2016|date=August 14, 2016|archive-date=June 17, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190617074713/https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=109&aid=0003375483|url-status=live}}</ref>

The group's third extended play ''[[Snowflake (EP)|Snowflake]]'' single "[[Rough (song)|Rough]]" won the group several awards between late 2016 to early 2017, including Song of the Year – January at the 7th Gaon Chart Music Awards. The single also won the Best Dance award and Digital Bonsang award at the 2016 [[Melon Music Awards]], the [[2016 Mnet Asian Music Awards]] and the 31st Golden Disc Awards, respectively. In 2020, Dingo Music has given them a 'Special Grand Prize' trophy for their exemplary contribution for the Original Contents created by the channel throughout the years.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://youtube.com/watch?feature=youtu.be&v=w0pN_w0LJsQ | publisher= Dingo Music YouTube Channel | title= Dingo Music awarding GFriend a Special Grand Prize Trophy for their Contribution in Dingo Channel | access-date=December 3, 2020 | date=November 30, 2020 }}</ref>
