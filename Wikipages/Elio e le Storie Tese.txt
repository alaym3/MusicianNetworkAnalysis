'''Elio e le Storie Tese''' ({{IPA-it|ˈɛːljo e lle ˈstɔːrje ˈteːze|lang}}; literally "Elio and the Troubled Stories"), often abbreviated '''EelST''', was an Italian [[comedy rock]] band from [[Milan]], formed in 1980.<ref name=treccani>{{cite web|title=Elio e le storie tese|url=http://www.treccani.it/enciclopedia/elio-e-le-storie-tese/|website=[[Treccani]]|access-date=22 August 2015|language=it}}</ref><ref name=stampa>{{cite web|last1=Amore|first1=Maurizio|title=Elio e Le Storie Tese|url=http://www.lastampa.it/2013/01/15/spettacoli/festival-di-sanremo/2013/artisti-in-gara/artisti-big/elio-e-le-storie-tese-fm1mhID8fy6000ULqce23M/pagina.html|website=[[La Stampa]]|access-date=22 August 2015|language=it|date=15 January 2013}}</ref> Its leader was Stefano Belisari, better known as [[Elio]]. They announced their split on 17 October 2017, on Italian TV program ''[[Le Iene]]''.<ref name=split/>

Elio e le Storie Tese acquired national fame after their second-place finish at the [[Sanremo Music Festival 1996]] with the song "[[La terra dei cachi]]", a humorous take on Italian lifestyle.<ref name=treccani/><ref name=stampa/> They also won the [[Sanremo_Music_Festival#"Mia_Martini"_Critics_Award|"Mia Martini" Critics Awards]] for their performance.<ref name=ansa>{{cite web|title=Sanremo: i vincitori del Premio della Critica|url=http://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/cultura/2015/02/04/sanremo-i-vincitori-del-premio-della-critica_ff9c84a8-8cc1-4501-9d90-4b3ff17a2f66.html|website=[[Agenzia Nazionale Stampa Associata|ANSA]]|access-date=22 August 2015|language=it|date=4 February 2015}}</ref>

In 1999, they were awarded as Best Italian Act at the [[1999 MTV Europe Music Awards|MTV Europe Music Awards]], and in 2003 they won the Best Italian Videoclip award at the [[Italian Music Awards]] of [[Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana]] for "[[Shpalman®]]".<ref name=stampa/> In 2011, they were elected as the best group band of the 2001–2010 decade through a referendum announced by the website Rockol.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dieci! Il referendum della musica italiana del decennio|url=http://www.rockol.it/news-197174/dieci-il-referendum-sulla-musica-del-decennio-ecco-tutti-i-risultati|website=Rockol|access-date=22 August 2015|language=it|date=11 January 2011}}</ref> In 2012, the album ''[[Elio samaga hukapan kariyana turu]]'' has been ranked the 15th best Italian album of all time by the magazine ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Il miglior disco italiano? Bollicine di Vasco Rossi|url=http://www.linkiesta.it/rolling-stone-dischi-italiani-piu-belli|website=Linkiesta.it|access-date=22 August 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140312065013/http://www.linkiesta.it/rolling-stone-dischi-italiani-piu-belli|archive-date=12 March 2014|language=it}}</ref>

The group participated to the [[Sanremo Music Festival]] for the second time in the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2013|2013 edition]] with the song "[[La canzone mononota]]", achieving again the second place and winning the "Mia Martini" Critics Awards for the second time, the award for the best arrangement, and the Radio and TV Press-Room award.<ref name=sony>{{cite news|title=Sanremo 2013: tre premi ad Elio e le Storie Tese|url=http://www.sonymusic.it/news/sanremo-2013-tre-premi-ad-elio-e-le-storie-tese/|access-date=22 August 2015|work=[[Sony Music]]|language=it}}</ref>

==History==
The origins of Elio e le Storie Tese go back to the 1970s, when Stefano Belisari (Elio), Luca Mangoni and Marco Conforti (Sergio's brother and future manager of the group) were classmates in a high school in Milan.<ref name=book />

The first line-up debuted in Milan in 1980. It consisted of Elio on guitar, Paolo Cortellino on bass and Pier Luigi Zuffellato on drums. Cortellino was then replaced firstly by Fabio Gianvecchio (Chiosco), later by Dario Mazzoli (Scaffale), and finally, in 1986, by Nicola Fasani (Faso). In the meantime, Zuffellato had left the group, and was replaced by [[Vittorio Cosma]] until 1982, when, after EelST were joined by the pianist Sergio Conforti (Rocco Tanica), the role of drummer was filled by a [[E-mu Drumulator|Drumulator]] operated by Rocco Tanica himself until 1988, when a Swiss drummer called Christian Meyer joined the group.<ref name=bio />

At the beginning, Elio was singer and guitarist, but in 1983 the group hired rock guitarist Davide Civaschi (Cesareo). From that moment on, Elio could focus on being the front-man, and, sparingly, play the [[Western concert flute]], for which he had passed the music academy's examination.<ref name=bio /> In 1988, the group was also joined by the multi-instrumentalist Paolo Panigada (Feiez),<ref name=book /><ref name=bio /> who died ten years later of a brain hemorrhage during a concert.<ref name=bio />

In 1992, Elio decided to include architect and former classmate Mangoni in the band. Mangoni, described in the official website as "artist and architect",<ref name=biography>{{cite web|title=Biography |url=http://elioelestorietese.it/english-bio/ |website=ElioeleStorieTese.it |access-date=25 August 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150825224720/http://elioelestorietese.it/english-bio/ |archive-date=25 August 2015 }}</ref> would have various roles, such as dancing and singing during live performances.<ref name=bio />

The first studio album, ''[[Elio samaga hukapan kariyana turu]]'' was published in 1989, and it was followed by ''[[The Los Sri Lanka Parakramabahu Brothers Featuring Elio e le Storie Tese]]'' (1990), ''[[İtalyan, rum casusu çikti]]'' (1992), ''[[Esco dal mio corpo e ho molta paura: Gli inediti 1979–1986]]'' (1993), ''[[Eat the Phikis]]'' (1996), ''[[Peerla]]'' (1998), ''[[Craccracriccrecr]]'' (1999), ''[[Cicciput]]'' (2003), ''[[Studentessi]]'' (2008) and ''[[L'album biango]]'' (2013). During their 35-year career, EelST have been awarded several prizes; they came second twice in the [[Sanremo Music Festival]] (in ([[Sanremo Music Festival 1996|1996]] and [[Sanremo Music Festival 2013|2013]]);<ref name=treccani /><ref name=stampa /> they also won two "Mia Martini" Critics' Awards.<ref name=ansa /><ref name=sony />

In March 2014, Elio e le Storie Tese presented six episodes of ''Il Musichione'', a comedy musical show on [[Rai 2]].<ref name=fq>{{cite news|last1=Gandus|first1=Valeria|title=Il Musichione, Elio e le Storie tese portano "bruttezza" (e molta musica) su Rai2|work=[[Il Fatto Quotidiano]]|date=5 March 2014|language=it}}</ref>

On 17 October 2017, the band announced that they would disband at the end of the year, after their last concert in Milan on 19 December.<ref name=split>{{Cite web |url=http://www.corriere.it/video-articoli/2017/10/18/elio-storie-tese-annunciano-l-addio-il-18-dicembre-l-ultimo-concerto/54ca75f2-b3cc-11e7-b73f-b517701f3ad7.shtml |title=Elio e le storie tese si sciolgono: «Il 19 dicembre l’ultimo concerto» |last=Laffranchi |first=Andrea |date=18 October 2017 |website=[[Corriere della Sera]] |language=it |access-date=18 October 2017}}</ref>

==Style==
[[File:Mangoni.jpg|thumb|left|Rocco Tanica, Mangoni and Elio]]
The music of the group is openly inspired, among other sources, by the style of [[Frank Zappa]], both in music and lyrics.<ref name="ondarock">{{cite web|last1=Lo Giudice|first1=Antonio|title=Elio e le Storie Tese. 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140811214453/http://www.bravonline.it/la-vita-tesa-di-elio-un-concerto-di-elio-e-le-storie-tese/|archive-date=11 August 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> Elio e le Storie Tese's musical work is characterized by eclecticism and competence, in the melodic, harmonic and orchestration (arrangement) aspects, augmented by the members' technical proficiency.<ref name="ondarock" /><ref name="bravo" /> They are widely considered as some of the best musicians in Italy.<ref name=book /><ref name="bravo" /><ref>{{cite web|last1=Villa|first1=Marco|title=Elio e le Storie Tese miglior gruppo italiano di sempre: il trollaggio |website=The Bottom Up |access-date=24 August 2015 |language=it |date=2 June 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150713125838/http://elioelestorietese.it/bio/ |archive-date=13 July 2015 |access-date=24 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=E gli Elii lanciano In their official discography, and in their gigs, EelST have covered many of the genres typified in popular music: rock, [[Latin music (genre)|Latin]], [[progressive rock]], [[disco]] and [[Soul music|soul]], to name a few.<ref name=book>{{cite book|last1=Di Mambro|first1=Angelo|title=L'importanza di chiamarsi Elio. Storia e gloria del più importante gruppo italiano|date=2004|publisher=Castelvecchi|language=it}}</ref><ref name="ondarock" /> A very strong attitude to affectionately mock and modify to their needs the clichès of Italian pop music has always been part of their musical career, and nonetheless many Italian pop artists have been proud guests in their recordings, acknowledging their value.
Their songs are typically interspersed by a rude, coarse humour which lies in stark contrast to their extreme varied and competent musical talent; once the band was considered part of the 'demential' rock underground spawned after the demise of [[punk rock|punk]] and cross-fertilized by situationist dadaism,<ref name="bravo" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Elio e le Storie Tese in concerto|url=http://www.trentinofamiglia.it/Attualita/Archivio-2012/Marzo/Elio-e-le-Storie-Tese-in-concerto|website=Trentinofamiglia.it|access-date=24 August 2015}}</ref> but they have soared well past above that scene.<ref name=book /> One of their favourite themes is adolescent life,<ref name="ondarock" /> seen as a golden period of fun, friendship, music and sexual discoveries.

The band has also been the first one, in 2004, to sell instant CDs of their live performances immediately after their end (which they dubbed ''CD brulé'', as they were 'burned' on the spot, like [[mulled wine]], in Italian "''vin brulé''").<ref>{{cite news|last1=Torelli|first1=Umberto|title=Elio e le Storie Tese lanciano l'instant cd|url=http://www.corriere.it/Primo_Piano/Spettacoli/2004/07_Luglio/03/elio.shtml|access-date=24 August 2015|work=[[Corriere della Sera]]|date=3 July 2004|language=it}}</ref>
