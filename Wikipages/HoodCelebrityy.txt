'''Tina Pinnock''', (born July 4, 1991) known professionally as '''HoodCelebrityy''', is a [[Jamaica|Jamaican]] [[dancehall]] singer and songwriter. Born and raised in [[Portmore, Jamaica|Portmore]], [[Jamaica]], Tina migrated to The Bronx, New York at the age of 12. Currently signed to [[Epic Records]], she released two mixtapes in 2017, ''Can't Believe It's Just a Girl'' and ''Trap Vs. Reggae''. The latter album peaked at number 9 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Reggae Albums chart. Her 2017 single, "Walking Trophy," peaked at number 22 on the [[R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay]] chart.

==Early life==
Tina Pinnock was born and raised in [[Saint Catherine Parish]] and [[Portmore, Jamaica|Portmore]], [[Jamaica]].<ref name="NYER">{{cite magazine | last=Trammell| first=Matthew| title =HoodCelebrityy and Dancehall’s New, Global Faces| magazine =The New Yorker| date =March 6, 2018 | url =https://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/hoodcelebrityy-and-dancehalls-new-global-faces| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref><ref name="MNT">{{cite news | last=Jerome| first=Cristina| title =The Five Top Acts at Best of the Best 2018| newspaper =Miami New Times| date =May 25, 2018 | url =http://www.miaminewtimes.com/music/best-of-the-best-2018-miami-lineup-10350598| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref> When she was 12, Pinnock and her family moved to The Bronx, New York.<ref name="SourceRise">{{cite magazine | last=Borik| first=Mike| title =On The Rise: Check Out HoodCelebrityy And Her Standout Single "The Takeover"| magazine =The Source| date =January 27, 2017 | url =http://thesource.com/2017/01/27/on-the-rise-check-out-hoodcelebrityy-and-her-standout-single-the-takeover/| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Circa">{{cite news | last=Dunlea| first=Reed| title =Cardi B ruled 2017. In 2018, expect to discover her collaborator, HoodCelebrityy.| publisher =Circa| date =January 4, 2018 | url =https://www.circa.com/story/2018/01/04/music/hoodcelebritty-cardi-b-music-collaborator-looks-to-be-discovered-in-2018| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref> Prior to achieving fame, she worked at a clothing store and uploaded 15-second freestyle videos on her [[Instagram]] account.<ref name="Noisey2017">{{cite news | title =Listen to HoodCelebrityy’s Cardi B-Approved New Mixtape, ‘Trap Vs. Reggae’| publisher =Noisey| date =August 11, 2017 | url =https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/neev9q/listen-to-hoodcelebrityys-cardi-b-approved-new-mixtape-trap-reggae| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
HoodCelebrityy's breakout came in 2015 when friend and future collaborator, [[Cardi B]], posted about her song, "Wine Pon It". The song went viral soon after.<ref name="HHWEpic">{{cite news | last=Chandler| first=D.L.| title =Epic Records Signs Dancehall & Hip-Hop Artist HoodCelebrityy| publisher =Hip-Hop Wired| date =May 3, 2018 | url =http://hiphopwired.com/751465/epic-records-signs-dancehall-hip-hop-artist-hoodcelebrityy/| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Singers">{{cite news | last=Breezy| first=Elle| title =Rising Trap-Reggae Artist HoodCelebrityy Coins New Sound On ‘The Takeover’| publisher =Singersroom| date =January 24, 2017 | url =https://singersroom.com/content/2017-01-24/rising-trap-reggae-artist-hoodcelebrityy-coins-new-sound-on-the-takeover/| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref> In 2016, HoodCelebrityy was featured on the Jubilee song, "Wine Up."<ref name="PF">{{cite web | last=Sherburne| first=Philip| title = Jubilee - "Wine Up" (ft. Hoodcelebrityy)| website =Pitchfork| date =September 16, 2016 | url =https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/18529-jubilee-wine-up-ft-hoodcelebrityy/| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref> She would later be signed by the management company and record label, KSR Group.<ref name="Vulture">{{cite news | last=Serwer| first=Jesse| title =Women Are Keeping Dancehall Fresh| publisher =Vulture| date =April 26, 2018 | url =http://www.vulture.com/2018/04/women-are-keeping-dancehall-fresh.html| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Grunge">{{cite news | title =Epic Records signs rising Dancehall/Hip-Hop artist HoodCelebrityy| publisher =GrungeCake| date =May 3, 2018 | url =https://grungecake.com/hoodcelebrityy/articles/44948| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref> In January 2017, she appeared on Cardi B's mixtape, ''[[Gangsta Bitch Music, Vol. 2]]'', on the song "Back It Up" which also featured [[Konshens]].<ref name="Noisey2018">{{cite news | last=Corry| first=Kristin| title =HoodCelebrityy's "Walking Trophy" Is a Party for Influencers, Minus Instagram| publisher =Noisey| date = February 13, 2018| url =https://noisey.vice.com/en_au/article/a343yz/hoodcelebrityy-walking-trophy-video-premiere-watch| access-date =June 6, 2018}}</ref> That same month, she released the lead single, "The Takeover", off of her debut mixtape, ''Can't Believe It's Just a Girl''. The mixtape was released in the spring of that year.<ref name="SourceRise"/>

In August 2017, HoodCelebrityy released her second mixtape, ''Trap Vs. Reggae'', which featured the song "Island Girls," a collaboration with Cardi B and [[Josh X]].<ref name="Noisey2017"/> The mixtape would later peak at number 9 on the ''Billboard'' Reggae Albums chart.<ref name="BBReg">{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/hood-celebrityy/chart-history/reggae-albums |title=Hood Celebrityy - Chart History - Reggae Albums|magazine=Billboard |access-date=June 7, 2018}}</ref> Later in 2017, she released the single, "Walking Trophy."<ref name="Fader12">{{cite magazine | last=Kochhar| first=Nazuk| title =12 songs you need in your life this week| magazine =The FADER| date =November 21, 2017 | url =http://www.thefader.com/2017/11/21/lil-uzi-vert-20-min-mr-eazi-songs-you-need| access-date =June 7, 2018}}</ref> She was also featured on the remix of the [[French Montana]] song, "Famous."<ref name="Vulture"/> The video for "Walking Trophy" was directed by Mazi O and released in February 2018.<ref name="Noisey2018"/> The song has thus far peaked at number 22 on the ''Billboard'' R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay chart.<ref name="BBRBHHAir">{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/hood-celebrityy/chart-history/hot-r-and-b-hip-hop-airplay |title=Hood Celebrityy - Chart History - R&B/Hip-Hop Airplay|magazine=Billboard |access-date=June 26, 2018}}</ref>

In May 2018, HoodCelebrityy was signed to Epic Records.<ref name="HHWEpic"/><ref name="Grunge"/> Later that month, she and [[Tory Lanez]] were featured on the Megan Ryte track, "On & On."<ref name="Complex">{{cite magazine | last=Ortiz| first=Edwin| title =Premiere: DJ Megan Ryte Hits the Club in "On & On" Video f/ Tory Lanez and Hoodcelebrityy| magazine =Complex| date =May 29, 2018 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2018/05/dj-megan-ryte-tory-lanez-hoodcelebrityy-on-and-on-video| access-date =June 7, 2018}}</ref> She performed at the 2018 [[Summer Jam (festival)|Summer Jam]].<ref name="Vulture"/>

On August 30, 2019 HoodCelebrityy released a single called "Bum Pon It" on her Official Youtube page. The song is caters to women and is being played at numerous events worldwide.
