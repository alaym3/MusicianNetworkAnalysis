'''Khulane Morule''' (born August 27, 1982), better known by his stage name '''Khuli Chana''', is a [[South Africa]]n [[Motswako]] [[rapper]].

==Early life and career==
Morule was born in the [[Mmabatho]], [[North West (South African province)|North West Province]] and started rapping motswako at a young age.<ref name="trace">{{cite web|url=http://trace.tv/artist/khuli-chana/|title=Khuli Chana Archives|publisher=Trace TV|accessdate=27 January  2016}}</ref> He was a member of a rap group called Morafe.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.news24.com/MoveMag/Archive/khuli-chana-20170728|title=KHULI CHANA|publisher=News 24}}</ref> He then proceeded to go solo, after the rap group took a break from releasing music as a unit.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iol.co.za/entertainment/music/local/khuli-chana-talks-about-his-creative-revolution-12194421|title=Khuli Chana talks about his creative revolution|publisher=IOL SA}}</ref>

Khuli Chana rose to fame after releasing his major successful album ''Motswakoriginator'', which infuses both the [[Setswana]] and [[English language]]s. [[Motswako]] is a mixture of both languages and the sound and style of music originates from [[Mahikeng]], a small town in the North West Province.

In 2014, Chana was nominated for Most Valuable Artist and Video of the Year at 2014 [[South African Hip Hop Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|title=SA Hip Hop Awards nominees 2014|url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/sa-hip-hop-awards-nominees-2014/|last=The Journalist}}</ref>

He has shared a stage with US rapper [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], as an opening act.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Marshall |first1=Rhode |title=Khuli Chana: The MotswakOriginator |url=https://mg.co.za/article/2012-10-15-khuli-chana-the-motswakoriginator |website=Mail & Guardian |accessdate=15 March 2019}}</ref>

Khuli Chana partnered with vodka brand [[Absolut Vodka|Absolut]] as one of their ambassadors for the South Africa market.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://adage.com/article/agency-news/finalists-ad-age-s-creativity-awards/312472/|title=CREATIVITY AWARDS FINALISTS|magazine=Ad Age}}</ref>

Khuli Chana was nearly killed when members of the South African police mistook him for a criminal and proceeded to fire nine bullets at his vehicle.<ref name="thesouthafrican">{{cite web|url=https://www.thesouthafrican.com/rapper-khuli-chana-mistakenly-shot-by-police/|title=Rapper Khuli Chana mistakenly shot by police in South Africa|publisher=The South African|accessdate=15 July  2018}}</ref><ref name="ENCA">{{cite web|url=https://www.enca.com/south-africa/khuli-chana-settles-with-police-after-shooting-ordeal|title=Khuli Chana settles with police over shooting|publisher=ENCA|accessdate=15 July  2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 2013, Khuli was shot by police while in his car due to mistaken identity. This was a major blow to his name and career as there were speculations about the incident. Luckily he survived the shooting, fully recovered and cleared his name. He settled it out of court and got himself R1.8 million.<ref>{{cite web|title=SAPS must pay star R1.8m over shooting
|url=https://www.iol.co.za/amp/capetimes/news/saps-must-pay-star-r18m-over-shooting-2029342|website=www.iol.co.za|accessdate=2 June 2016}}</ref> Khuli Chana married DJ and television personality Lamiez Holworthy in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|title=‘Khuli living it up with new bae’|url=https://www.dailysun.co.za/News/Eidos/khuli-living-it-up-with-new-bae-20190112
|website=dailysun.co.za|accessdate=9 April 2019}}</ref>
