'''Njomza Vitia''' ({{IPAc-en|Àà|n|j|…í|m|z|…ô|_|Àà|v|iÀê|t|i|…ô}}; {{IPA-sq|Àà…≤…îmza viÀàtia}}; born April 22, 1994),<ref name="Phoenix Times Feb 2019" /> known [[mononymously]] as '''Njomza''', is an American singer and songwriter. She is currently signed with [[Capitol Records|Capitol]], [[Motown Records|Motown]], and [[SinceThe80s]]. Since 2013, she has released one mixtape and three EPs.

==Early life==
Njomza Vitia was born in [[Ludwigsburg]], [[Baden-W√ºrttemberg]], Germany to [[Kosovo Albanian]] parents, and grew up in [[Chicago, Illinois]]. Her father, Faik Vitia, and her mother, Sheki Vitia (n√©e Haziri) were born in [[Pristina]], [[Kosovo]]. Her parents left Kosovo in 1992.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Trust|first=Gary|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8507787/albanian-artists-charts-dua-lipa-bebe-rexha-ava-max|title=Why Albanian Artists Are Having a Moment On the Charts|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=April 22, 2019|accessdate=August 30, 2019}}</ref> She has an older sister who is five-years older than her. As a child, Njomza was introduced to R&B music through her sister, and began listening to rock music and pop rock during middle school.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VZCIphN-eFs</ref>

==Musical career==
Njomza began her career in music as the front woman for an alternative band called Scarlett.  The band had a strong following and played Warped Tour in 2010.  Once the band dismantled in 2011, Njomza went on to pursue her career as a solo artist.  She posted [[cover version]]s on [[YouTube]] of [[Mac Miller]], [[Kid Cudi]], and various other artists. It was through covers that Njomza was discovered by Mac Miller, who signed Njomza to his [[Warner Bros. Records]] imprint, REMember Music.<ref name="newmotown">{{Cite web|url=https://www.laweekly.com/new-motown-artist-njomza-talks-mac-miller-and-more/|title=New Motown Artist NJOMZA Talks Mac Miller and More|first=Shirley|last=Ju|date=February 19, 2019|website=[[LA Weekly]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/njomza-talks-gov-ball-high-transcending-genres-friendship-mac-miller-more|title=NJOMZA Talks Gov Ball, Dropping Genres, Mac Miller|date=June 1, 2019|website=[[Grammy Award]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/news/njomza-on-working-with-ariana-grande-best-advice-from-mac-miller/|title=Singer NJOMZA on Working With Ariana Grande, Best Advice From Mac Miller|date=August 20, 2019|website=[[Us Weekly]]}}</ref>

She also co-wrote [[Ariana Grande]]'s songs "[[7 Rings]]" and "[[Thank U, Next (song)|Thank U, Next]]".<ref name="Phoenix Times Feb 2019">{{Cite web|url=https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/music/pop-artist-njomza-ariana-grandes-thank-u-next-co-writer-steps-into-the-spotlight-11201378|title=NJOMZA, Ariana Grande's "thank u, next" Co-Writer, Steps Into the Spotlight|first=Gerrit|last=Feenstra|date=February 20, 2019|accessdate=August 30, 2019|website=[[Phoenix New Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|last1=Trust|first1=Gary|last2=Anderson|first2=Trevor|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/podcasts/8499376/ariana-grande-njomza-interview-chart-beat-podcast|title=Ariana Grande's Record Week & Guest Njomza on Co-Writing '7 Rings' & 'Thank U, Next': Chart Beat Podcast|magazine=Billboard|date=February 21, 2019|accessdate=August 30, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/celebs/a26826540/njomza-7-rings-under-the-covers/|title=Go Under the Covers With Ariana Grande's BFF Njomza|date=March 15, 2019|website=[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]]}}</ref>
