'''Picture This''' are<!--This article is written in Irish English. Do not change to is!--> an Irish pop rock musical band based in [[Athy|Athy, County Kildare, Ireland]], comprising Ryan Hennessy, Jimmy Rainsford, Owen Cardiff and Cliff Deane. In 2017, they released their [[Picture This (Picture This album)|eponymous debut album]], which entered atop the [[Irish Albums Chart]]. In early 2019, they released their follow up album ''Mdrn Lv''. Their third studio album was released in June 2021.

==Biography==
Picture This originate from [[Athy]], County Kildare, Ireland. The band consists of  Ryan Hennessy (vocals), Jimmy Rainsford (drums), Owen Cardiff (Guitar) and Cliff Deane (Bass). Rainsford had previously toured with [[Ryan Sheridan (musician)|Ryan Sheridan]], whilst Hennessy had been a member of another local band. Deane, Cardiff and Rainsford were childhood friends. Deane and Cardiff joined the band when Hennessy and Rainsford asked them to play with Picture This.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/student-hub/picture-this-a-band-to-watch-out-for-1.2610942|title=Picture This: A band to watch out for!|work=The Irish Times|access-date= 13 July 2016}}</ref>

==History==
===2015–2017: Breakthrough and ''Picture This''===
{{Main|Picture This (Picture This album)}}
In October 2015, Ryan Hennessy recorded a sample of the song "Take My Hand" on his iPhone, which he and Rainsford later recorded in the studio. The video was uploaded to Facebook and YouTube, and has amassed over 4 million views across all platforms. The band's debut gig was to be played in the Grand Social venue in [[Dublin]], which they sold out in under 30 minutes. To meet the demand, the gig was moved to [[The Academy (music venue)|The Academy]], which has a capacity of 850 people. The band became the first act to sell out the venue for their debut gig.

The band announced a 20-date tour of Ireland in spring 2016. The tour began in [[Leixlip]] on 4 June and culminated with three dates at the [[Olympia Theatre (Dublin)|Olympia]], a 1,600 capacity venue in Dublin on 1–3 November. By 22 July 2016 tickets for 16 of the shows had sold out, including two dates at the Olympia. The band later announced that they would be playing a third and final date at the Olympia on 3 November 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotpress.com/Picture-This/news/Picture-This-adds-a-third-show-at-Olympia-after-selling-out-previous-two/17855627.html |title=Picture This add a third show at Olympia after selling out previous two |work=Hot Press |date=18 July 2016 |access-date=22 July 2016}}</ref>

In July 2016, the band announced that they would be releasing their debut self-titled EP on 12 August. The track list featured five original songs including "Take My Hand". The EP debuted at number 1 on the [[Irish Albums Chart]] for the week ending 18 August 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.chart-track.co.uk/index.jsp?c=p%2Fmusicvideo%2Fmusic%2Farchive%2Findex_test.jsp&ct=240002&arch=t&lyr=2016&year=2016&week=33 |title=Top 100 Artist Album, Week Ending 18 August 2016 |publisher=Chart-Track |access-date=19 August 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160823001852/https://www.chart-track.co.uk/index.jsp?c=p%2Fmusicvideo%2Fmusic%2Farchive%2Findex_test.jsp&ct=240002&arch=t&lyr=2016&year=2016&week=33 |archive-date=23 August 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In May 2017, Picture This released "Never Change", the lead single from their debut studio album. Rainsford told Billboard the song was inspired by "the feeling of loving somebody exactly how they are and never wanting them to change a thing. The video signifies that we could go anywhere in the world but we always end up back home and will never change that."<ref name="NC"/>

They released their [[Picture This (Picture This album)|self-titled debut album]] on 25 August 2017.<ref name="Hot Press"/>

===2018–present: ''Mdrn Lv'' & ''Life in Colour''===
On 23 March 2018, Picture This released "This Morning". On 15 June 2018, they released "When We Were Young". On 17 October 2018, they announced their second album, ''Mdrn Lv'', and released its lead single, "One Drink". Following the release, they also announced their second headline tour. On 7 December 2018, they released the second single from ''Mdrn Lv'', "Everything or Nothing". The album was released on 19 February 2019, with a performance atop the [[Empire State Building]] in [[New York City]]. On 18 October 2019 they released their single "One Night". On 19 October they announced three concert dates in Ireland in Cork, Dublin and Belfast for June 2020.

In June 2021, the band released their third studio album, ''Life in Colour'' which debuted at number 2 on the Irish charts.

==Band members==
* Ryan Hennessy – lead vocals and rhythm electric guitar
* Jimmy Rainsford – drum kit and backing vocals 
* Owen Cardiff – lead electric guitar and backing vocals 
* Cliff Deane – electric bass guitar and backing vocals
