'''Noah Hawley''' (born {{circa|1967}}) is an American television director, producer, and writer for television and film who created and wrote the [[FX (TV channel)|FX]] television series ''[[Fargo (TV series)|Fargo]]'' (2014–present) and ''[[Legion (TV series)|Legion]]'' (2017–2019). Hawley earlier worked on the television series ''[[Bones (TV series)|Bones]]'' (2005–2008), ''[[The Unusuals]]'' (2009), and ''[[My Generation (TV series)|My Generation]]'' (2010). He is set to create a new television series based on the ''[[Alien (franchise)|Alien]]'' film franchise. He has also written five novels.

==Early life==
Hawley was born and raised in [[New York City]], [[New York (state)|New York]].<ref name="telegraph2012"/><ref name="thr2014">{{cite magazine | url = https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/fargo-creator-noah-hawley-how-694492 | title = 'Fargo' Creator Noah Hawley: How I Made It in Hollywood | magazine = The Hollywood Reporter | date = April 15, 2014 | access-date = December 10, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150204224217/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/fargo-creator-noah-hawley-how-694492 |archive-date=February 4, 2015 |url-status=live}}</ref> His mother, [[Louise Armstrong]],<ref name="louisearmstrong">{{cite news | url = https://www.theguardian.com/books/2008/sep/24/childprotection | title = Louise Armstrong | newspaper = The Guardian | access-date = December 10, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141211013156/http://www.theguardian.com/books/2008/sep/24/childprotection |archive-date=December 11, 2014 |url-status=live}}</ref> was a non-fiction writer and activist, and his maternal grandmother was a playwright. His father, Tom Hawley, was a businessman.<ref name="louisearmstrong"/> He has a twin brother, Alexi, who is a writer for the television show ''[[The Following]]'' and the creator of ''[[State of Affairs (TV series)|State of Affairs]]''.<ref name="thr2014"/><ref name="louisearmstrong"/>

Hawley graduated from [[Sarah Lawrence College]] with a degree in political science in 1989.<ref>[http://alum.slc.edu/page.aspx?pid=273 "News and Announcements from Alums"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110117123148/http://alum.slc.edu/page.aspx?pid=273 |date=January 17, 2011 }}, Sarah Lawrence College website, accessed May 30, 2013.</ref><ref name="sfgate1998">{{cite news | url = http://www.sfgate.com/style/article/Success-stalks-thriller-writer-3305223.php | title = Success stalks thriller writer | newspaper = [[The San Francisco Examiner]] | via = SFgate.com | date = August 31, 1998 | first = Jane | last = Ganahl |access-date=December 10, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141211053023/http://www.sfgate.com/style/article/Success-stalks-thriller-writer-3305223.php |archive-date=December 11, 2014 |url-status=live}}</ref> He worked for the [[Legal Aid Society]] in New York City, dealing with cases involving child abuse and neglect.<ref name="sfgate1998"/><ref>{{cite web | url = http://alum.slc.edu/page.aspx?pid=273 | title = News and Announcements from Alums | publisher = Sarah Lawrence College | access-date = December 10, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110117123148/http://alum.slc.edu/page.aspx?pid=273 |archive-date=January 17, 2011 |url-status=live}}</ref> He later moved to [[San Francisco]]. He worked in computer programming at law firms<ref name="sfgate1998"/> and as a [[paralegal]].<ref name="thr2014"/>

==Career==

===Books===
He has published five novels: ''A Conspiracy of Tall Men'' (1998), ''Other People's Weddings'' (2004), ''The Punch'' (2008), ''The Good Father'' (2012), and ''Before the Fall'' (2016).<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.fantasticfiction.com/h/noah-hawley/ | title = Noah Hawley |access-date=March 15, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160315204841/https://www.fantasticfiction.com/h/noah-hawley/ |archive-date=March 15, 2016 |url-status=live}}</ref> He is currently at work on a sixth novel, ''Anthem'', which is set to be published in January 2022.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://deadline.com/video/noah-hawley-fargo-interview-behind-the-lens/ | title = Noah Hawley On Why He Came Back To ‘Fargo’, How Covid Changed Its Course, Plus News On His ‘Star Trek’ Demise And If ‘Alien’ Awaits – Behind The Lens |first1=Pete|last1=Hammond|publisher=Deadline.com|date=June 14, 2021|access-date=June 15, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210615085309/https://deadline.com/video/noah-hawley-fargo-interview-behind-the-lens/ |archive-date=June 15, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref>

===Television===
[[File:Noah Hawley by Gage Skidmore.jpg|right|thumb|upright|Hawley at the 2019 [[WonderCon]], promoting ''Legion'']]
Hawley was a writer and producer on the first three seasons of the television series ''[[Bones (TV series)|Bones]]'' (2005–2008).<ref>Elks, Jennifer, ''The Skinny'', accessed June 21, 2011.</ref> He was also creator and executive producer of ''[[The Unusuals]]'' (2009) and ''[[My Generation (TV series)|My Generation]]'' (2010).<ref>[http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news/2010/01/20/development-update-wednesday-january-20/8488/ "Development Update:Wednesday, January 20"], ''The Futon Critic'', accessed June 21, 2011.</ref>

Hawley is the creator, primary writer, and executive producer of the [[FX (TV channel)|FX]] [[Anthology series|anthology]] television series ''[[Fargo (TV series)|Fargo]]'' (2014), based on the [[Coen brothers]]' 1996 [[Fargo (1996 film)|film of the same name]]. On August 25, 2014, ''Fargo'' won the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Miniseries]], along with 17 additional nominations at the [[66th Primetime Emmy Awards]]. In total, the series has been [[List of awards and nominations received by Fargo (TV series)|nominated for 113 awards]] since its premiere, winning 32 of them. The fourth season of ''Fargo'' premiered on September 27, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://variety.com/2020/tv/news/fargo-season-4-premiere-date-fx-2-1234730391/ | title = 'Fargo' Season 4 Premiere Date Set at FX | last = Thorne| first = Will | website = Variety | language = en-US|access-date=September 29, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200812194056/https://variety.com/2020/tv/news/fargo-season-4-premiere-date-fx-2-1234730391/|archive-date=August 12, 2020|url-status=live | date = August 10, 2020}}</ref>

In December 2015, Hawley extended his production deal with FX.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web | url = https://variety.com/2015/tv/news/fx-inks-new-deal-with-fargo-creator-noah-hawley-sets-new-development-projects-1201658532/ | title = FX Inks New Deal with 'Fargo' Creator Noah Hawley, Sets New Development Projects | last = Wagmeister | first = Elizabeth | website = Variety | language = en-US|access-date=April 20, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170628085616/http://variety.com/2015/tv/news/fx-inks-new-deal-with-fargo-creator-noah-hawley-sets-new-development-projects-1201658532/|archive-date=June 28, 2017|url-status=live | date = December 10, 2015}}</ref> He wrote and served as executive producer for ''[[Legion (TV series)|Legion]]'', an FX television series based on the [[Legion (Marvel Comics)|Marvel comic book character]].<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.ew.com/article/2015/10/14/legion-series-marvel | title = 'Fargo' creator developing 'Legion' series with Marvel and FX | website = Entertainment Weekly's EW.com|access-date=April 20, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160409002836/http://www.ew.com/article/2015/10/14/legion-series-marvel|archive-date=April 9, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

On December 10, 2020, it was announced that Hawley will be creating a television series based on the ''[[Alien (franchise)|Alien]]'' franchise for FX, for which he will serve as the showrunner, primary writer, and executive producer.<ref>{{Cite web|last=D'Alessandro|first=Anthony|date=2020-12-10|title=‘Alien’ Series In The Works At FX With ‘Fargo’ Creator Noah Hawley; Ridley Scott In Advance Talks To EP|url=https://deadline.com/2020/12/alien-series-in-the-works-at-fx-with-fargo-creator-noah-hawley-ridley-scott-in-advance-talks-to-ep-1234654111/|access-date=2021-07-04|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref> The show is scheduled to be released some time in 2023.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-08-13|title=Noah Hawley’s ‘Alien’ TV Series Won’t Premiere Until 2023, Earliest|url=https://decider.com/2021/08/13/alien-fx-series-noah-hawley-2023-premiere-date/|access-date=2021-10-04|website=Decider|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Film ===
Hawley wrote the original screenplay for the film ''[[The Alibi]]'' (2006).<ref>Elks, Jennifer [https://www.comingsoon.net/films.php?id=13000 "Lies and Alibis"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120327063548/http://www.comingsoon.net/films.php?id=13000 |date=March 27, 2012 }}, ''Coming Soon'', accessed June 21, 2011.</ref>

In September 2014, Hawley signed a deal with [[Universal Pictures]] to script an untitled project for their upcoming [[Universal Monsters|Dark Universe]].<ref name=":1" /> Sony Pictures has acquired the rights to Hawley's latest novel, ''Before the Fall'', with him writing the screenplay.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web | url = https://deadline.com/2014/09/fargo-noah-hawley-novel-before-the-fall-sony-836510/ | title = 'Fargo's Noah Hawley To Adapt His Novel 'Before The Fall' In Sony Pic Deal | last = Fleming | first = Mike Jr. | website = Deadline | language = en-US|access-date=April 20, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160504094433/http://deadline.com/2014/09/fargo-noah-hawley-novel-before-the-fall-sony-836510/|archive-date=May 4, 2016|url-status=live | date = September 18, 2014}}</ref> In 2016, his 26 Keys production company signed a deal with 20th Century Fox to do films.<ref>{{Cite web|last=McNary|first=Dave|date=2016-04-25|title=Noah Hawley Signs First-Look Deal With Fox|url=https://variety.com/2016/film/news/noah-hawley-fox-first-look-deal-1201759981/|access-date=2020-12-04|website=Variety|language=en-US}}</ref> 

On July 20, 2017, Hawley announced at [[Comic Con]] that he was writing and directing a ''[[Doctor Doom in other media#Film|Doctor Doom]]'' movie with [[20th Century Fox]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://screenrant.com/doctor-doom-movie-announcement-noah-hawley-fantastic-four/|title=Doctor Doom Movie In Development From Legion's Noah Hawley|last=Leadbeater|first=Alex|publisher=Screen Rant|date=July 20, 2017|access-date=July 20, 2017}}</ref> However, the project was shelved following [[Acquisition of 21st Century Fox by Disney|Disney's acquisition of 21st Century Fox]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2018/06/noah-hawley-doctor-doom-movie-is-written-but-on-hold.html|title=Noah Hawley's Doctor Doom Movie Is Written, But Don't Expect to See It Anytime Soon|website=Vulture|last=Riesman|first=Abraham|date=June 4, 2018|access-date=June 4, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://deadline.com/2019/08/marvel-spoilers-legion-noah-hawley-interview-series-finale-fargo-fx-doctor-doom-movie-cats-cradle-1202666661/|title='Legion's Noah Hawley Talks Tonight's Series Finale, 'Fargo', 'Doctor Doom' Movie & 'Cat's Cradle' Adaptation|work=Deadline|date=August 13, 2019}}</ref>

In 2019, he made his feature film directorial debut with ''[[Lucy in the Sky (film)|Lucy in the Sky]]'', a drama film starring [[Natalie Portman]] as an astronaut, for [[Fox Searchlight]].<ref>{{Cite web | last = Kroll | first = Justin | url = https://variety.com/2018/film/news/natalie-portman-fox-searchlights-pale-blue-dot-1202645031/ | title = Natalie Portman in Talks to Replace Reese Witherspoon in 'Pale Blue Dot' (EXCLUSIVE) | date = January 11, 2018 | website = Variety | access-date = January 24, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180125134737/http://variety.com/2018/film/news/natalie-portman-fox-searchlights-pale-blue-dot-1202645031/|archive-date=January 25, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

In November 2019, it was announced that Hawley would be writing and directing the fourth installment in the rebooted ''[[Star Trek]]'' franchise.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/film/news/noah-hawley-star-trek-1203409795/|title = Noah Hawley to Write and Direct Next 'Star Trek' Movie|last = Kroll|first = Justin|date = 2019-11-19|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> He finished the script in September 2020. It would feature a new crew, although set in the same universe.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://trekmovie.com/2020/09/15/noah-hawley-confirms-star-trek-film-would-feature-new-crew-project-currently-in-stasis/|title=Noah Hawley Confirms Star Trek Film Would Feature New Crew, Project in "Statis"|last=TrekMovieStaff|date=2020-09-15|website=TrekMovie|language=en|access-date=2020-09-21}}</ref> In late November 2020, the project was cancelled and Hawley had left.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://movieweb.com/star-trek-4-noah-hawley-update/|title=Star Trek Is Not In Director Noah Hawley's Immediate Future|last = Fuge|first = Jon|date = 2020-12-01|website = MovieWeb|language=en|access-date=2020-12-02}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hawley resides in [[Austin, Texas]] and [[Los Angeles, California]], with his wife, Kyle Hawley, and their two children.<ref name="thr2014"/>
