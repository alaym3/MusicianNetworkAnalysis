'''Evan Sewell Wallace''' (November 1, 1982 – February 13, 2017) best known by his stage name '''E-Dubble,''' often stylized '''e-dubble''', or shortened to '''e-dub''', was an American [[rapper]]. He was best known for his Freestyle Friday series in which he released a new song each Friday throughout 2010, with one final unofficial release in 2012. He was the founder of Black Paisley Records.<ref name="BlackPaisleyRecords">{{Cite web |title=Black Paisley Records |url=http://www.blackpaisleyrecords.com/}}</ref> 

Over the course of his career, E-Dubble released two studio albums, ''Hip Hop Is Good'' (2009), and ''Two Tone Rebel'' (2016); One studio EP ''Reset EP'' (2012); one collaborative EP with the band 27 Lights, ''Surrounded By Giants'' (2014); and one mixtape ''Straight Outta St. Mary's'' (2006).

== Background ==

Wallace grew up outside of Philadelphia, the youngest child of a principal and a schoolteacher. His mother was a schoolteacher, and taught music. In the 1990s, Wallace and his friends fell under the spell of hip-hop cast by [[Snoop Dogg]] and [[Dr. Dre]]; his music would later be known for its common celebrations of this love of hip-hop. He played center on the [[Wissahickon High School]] basketball team, but dreamed of being a rapper. In an interview with the [[Baltimore (magazine)|Baltimore magazine]], Wallace cited [[Eminem]]'s breakout success in the late 90s as a major inspiration to him.<ref>{{Cite web |title=A towering presence in-person and online, Evan Wallace shoots for breakout success |url=http://www.baltimoremagazine.net/2014/1/13/evan-wallace-aka-e-dubble-shoots-for-breakout-success |access-date=February 24, 2017}}</ref>

Wallace graduated from [[St. Mary's College of Maryland]] with a degree in political science.<ref>{{Cite web|last=January 2014|first=Marion Winik {{!}}|date=January 13, 2014|title=Making It Big|url=https://www.baltimoremagazine.com/section/artsentertainment/evan-wallace-aka-e-dubble-shoots-for-breakout-success/|access-date=July 2, 2021|website=Baltimore Magazine}}</ref> The ''Straight Outta St. Mary's'' mixtape was released on April 12, 2006, and was written by E-Dubble and Glaze who were both members of the online group Irishtoothache.<ref>{{Cite web|title=irishtoothache - funny songs - funny videos - funny cartoons - funny shit - ALL FREE!|url=http://irishtoothache.com/about.php|access-date=July 2, 2021|website=irishtoothache.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=October 26, 2007|title=CD Baby: E-DUBBLE: Straight Outta St. Mary's|url=http://cdbaby.com/cd/edubble/from/edubble/|access-date=July 2, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071026075854/http://cdbaby.com/cd/edubble/from/edubble/|archive-date=October 26, 2007}}</ref>

E-dubble moved to Baltimore to live with his friends and collaborators. Together they formed the hip-hop band Young English, and played their first show in July 2008. The group went on to purchase a renovated warehouse, dubbed "The Hampden Mansion," where E-dubble would later go on to write, record, and produce his debut album ''Hip-Hop is Good'', released on October 27, 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|title=E-Dubble|url=https://genius.com/artists/E-dubble|access-date=July 2, 2021|website=Genius}}</ref> The next year, in August 2010, E-dubble created his [[YouTube]] channel.<ref>{{Cite web |title=e-dubble |url=https://www.youtube.com/user/EdubHipHop |access-date=February 24, 2017 |website=YouTube}}</ref> He went on to produce his Freestyle Friday series, a weekly series of free songs.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=e-dubble |url=https://genius.com/artists/E-dubble |website=Genius}}</ref>

The first ''Freestyle Friday'' track—not true [[freestyle rap]]s, but composed songs—was released on February 5, 2010, with a new song released every Friday until on February 3, 2011.<ref>{{Citation|title=e-dubble - Robots Can't Drink (Freestyle Friday #53)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AeurKtKCtiw|access-date=July 2, 2021}}</ref> He also occasionally collaborated with his band, Young English, to make some of his tracks.<ref>{{Citation|title=e-dubble & Young English - Anything You Want|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C9y_h7Jbpmw|access-date=July 2, 2021}}</ref> A final track, "Last Man Standing", was released in 2012.<ref>{{Citation|title=e-dubble - Last Man Standing (Freestyle Friday #54)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=chKFnVH6rCY|access-date=July 2, 2021}}</ref> The series was noted for its effective use of samples, lyrics and E-dubble's spoken word outros, in which he communicated with listeners.<ref>{{Cite web |title=E-Dubble brings the Freestyle Friday Feeling |url=http://www.mychemicaltoilet.com/2010/12/e-dubble-freestyle-friday/ |access-date=February 24, 2017 |website=mychemicaltoilet}}</ref> The pressure writing and recording a new song every week took its toll.<ref>{{Cite web |last=magazine |first=Baltimore |date=January 13, 2014 |title=Making It Big |url=https://www.baltimoremagazine.com/2014/1/13/evan-wallace-aka-e-dubble-shoots-for-breakout-success |access-date=May 12, 2019 |website=Baltimore magazine}}</ref> 

''Reset EP'' was released on November 6, 2012, was well received by his fans and it focused on his recovery following the Freestyle Friday era<ref>{{Cite web |title=Stream e-dubble's "Reset" EP |url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2012/11/stream-e-dubbles-reset-ep |access-date=May 12, 2019 |website=Complex}}</ref> He released his final album, "Two Tone Rebel", in 2016.<ref>{{Citation|title=e-dubble - Two Tone Rebel|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2bFFGKSSs-c|access-date=July 2, 2021}}</ref>

== Death ==

Wallace died of an infection which started in his hand and spread throughout his body on February 13, 2017.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Schwadron |first=Eli |date=February 15, 2017 |title=Philadelphia Rapper E-Dubble Has Died |work=XXL Magazine |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/02/e-dubble-has-died/ |access-date=June 19, 2017}}</ref> [[Sepsis]] was reported as the cause of death, but this was not confirmed. Wallace claimed that his illness caused him to "throw up half of his body's blood" and for "his hands to swell up." He also said that he had to have "10 blood transfusions."<ref>{{Cite web |title=Edubble's instagram post from the hospital |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BPivRXhjS0x/ |access-date=February 24, 2017 |website=Instagram}}</ref> He is buried in the [[Abington_Township,_Montgomery_County,_Pennsylvania|Abington]] Presbyterian Church.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Evan Sewell Wallace memorial |url=https://memorial.fitzpatrickabington.com/Wallace-Evan/2846341/service-details.php |access-date=December 3, 2019 |website=Fitzpatrick Funeral Home}}</ref>
