'''Quinlan Sharif McAfee''' (born January 18, 2001),<ref>{{Cite web|title=MCAFEE, QUINLAN AQUIRRE Inmate 1906526: Travis County Jail in Austin, TX|url=https://www.bailbondsearch.com/texas/travis-inmate-MCAFEE/1906526|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-12}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Quin NFN''' (formerly '''Quincho'''),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/quin-nfn-mn0003824663/biography|title=Quin NFN {{!}} Biography & History|last=|first=|date=|website=AllMusic|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter from [[Austin, Texas]]. He is best known for his singles "Talkin' My Shit," "Straight Thru," and "Poles" featuring [[NLE Choppa]].

He released his debut commercial mixtape, ''4Nun'', on October 25, 2019.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/4nun/1483271235|title=4Nun - Album by Quin NFN on Apple Music|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> He released his second mixtape, ''Quincho'', on March 27, 2020.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/quincho/1502660824|title=QUINCHO - Album by Quin NFN on Apple Music|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Quinlan McAfee was born in [[Austin, Texas]], on January 18, 2001. He started rhyming and writing rap songs at six years old.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|title=The Break Presents: Quin NFN|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/10/quin-nfn-interview-the-break/|last=Seabrook III|first=Robert|date=October 17, 2019|website=XXL|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Quin NFN Is a Texas Rapper Making Both Melodic and High-Energy Songs|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/10/quin-nfn-interview|last=Schube|first=Will|date=October 31, 2019|website=Complex|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Exploring Austin's Emerging Rap Scene With Quin NFN|url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/exploring-austins-emerging-rap-scene-with-quin-nfn/|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=2020-05-14}}</ref> As stated in interviews, he cites [[Lil Wayne]] as "the first inspiration to basically make me wanna rap."<ref name=":4" /> He has also said that rapper Young Pappy inspired him "to get in the studio and talk shit." The first album he purchased was Lil Wayne's ''[[Tha Carter III]]''.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|title=LL Presents: The Quin Nfn Q&A|url=https://lyricallemonade.com/p/ll-presentsl-the-quin-nfn-qa|last=|first=|date=|website=Lyrical Lemonade|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2016-2017: Early fame and debut EP ===
According to McAfee, he did not start recording music until the end of 2016.<ref name=":5" /> By that time, he started gaining attention in his hometown of Austin, Texas. However, his first hit song did not come until he released the single "Game Time Pt. 2" in August 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/10/quin-nfn-interview|title=Quin NFN Is a Texas Rapper Making Both Melodic and High-Energy Songs|last=|first=|date=|website=Complex|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> He followed this up with the releases of songs such as "Gang Shit", "Revenge", and "Lil Gangsta", with the former two being featured on his debut EP ''Stain or Starve''.

=== 2018-2019: Big success and debut mixtape ===
McAfee gained national success after the release of two of his biggest singles to date, "Talkin' My Shit" and "Straight Thru", which both had music videos uploaded onto the popular Jmoney1041 [[YouTube]] channel in late 2018, helping them gain traction.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MEAy8jwsUsk|title=Quin NFN - Talkin' My Shit (Official Music Video)|last=|first=|date=|website=YouTube|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XlS7Q0yR-Sc|title=Quin NFN - Straight Thru (Official Music Video)|last=|first=|date=|website=YouTube|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> These singles helped McAfee sign a record deal with 10K Projects.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://celebrityaccess.com/2019/03/14/record-label-10k-projects-signs-rapper-quin-nfn/|title=Record Label 10K Projects Signs Rapper Quin NFN|last=|first=|date=|website=CelebrityAccess|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> He followed this success up with hit singles such as "How I'm Living" and "NFN ENT", both of which accumulated over a million views on YouTube.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7dE8H01oYpc|title=Quin NFN - NFN ENT (Official Music Video)|last=|first=|date=|website=YouTube|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sZh-Yp5azEQ|title=Quin NFN "How I'm Living" (WSHH Exclusive - Official Music Video)|last=|first=|date=|website=YouTube|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

On October 25, 2019, McAfee released his debut mixtape, ''4Nun.''<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2" /> The tape included features from [[NLE Choppa]] and [[PnB Rock]], and one of the singles from the tape, "Poles" featuring NLE Choppa, went on to become one of McAfee's most popular singles.

=== 2020-present: Second mixtape ===
McAfee released his second mixtape, ''Quincho'', on March 27, 2020. The tape included no features and four previously released tracks.<ref name=":3" />
