==Early life==

Garg was born in [[Tura, Meghalaya]] to Mohini Borthakur and Ily Borthakur. He was named after the music composer [[Zubin Mehta]].<ref name="Birthday"/> His father Mohini Borthakur is a lyricist and poet under the name  ''Kapil Thakur''.<ref name="Birthday"/><ref name="Jongki"/> Garg pursued a degree in [[Bachelor of Science]] but [[Dropping out|dropped out]] to concentrate in his singing career.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nenow.in/north-east-news/zubeens-kanchanjangha-a-rebel-without-a-cause.html|title=Zubeen's Kanchanjangha: A rebel without a cause|website=NORTHEAST NOW|language=en-US|access-date=12 October 2019|archive-date=12 October 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191012063801/https://nenow.in/north-east-news/zubeens-kanchanjangha-a-rebel-without-a-cause.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==

===Beginning singing career after dropout his college (1992-1995)===
{{unreliable sources|date=March 2020}}
Garg got his confidence to become a singer when he got gold medal for his western solo performance in youth festival held in 1992. And after this he entered into professional music with his debut Assamese album ''Anamika'', which was released in 1992.<ref name="futuresounds">{{cite web | url=http://www.futuresounds.in/store/anamika/ | title=About Zubeen's debut album Anamika(অনামিকা) at futuresounds.com | publisher=futuresounds.com | access-date=4 September 2015 | archive-date=5 October 2015 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151005084005/http://www.futuresounds.in/store/anamika/ | url-status=live }}</ref> His first composed song from this album was "Gaane Ki Aane" which he composed at the age of 13.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Zubeen hits high notes on podium {{!}} Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Singing Superstar|url=https://www.indiaforums.com/forum/topic/346471|access-date=27 July 2021|website=India Forums|language=en}}</ref>  He later released many other albums and film songs such as ''Maya'' (1994), ''Asha'' (1995), ''Ujaan Piriti'' (1995), etc.

===Bollywood singing career (1995-present)===
In 1995, Garg moved to Mumbai to work in the [[Bollywood]] music industry where he recorded few Hindi albums and films songs like ''Chandni Raat'', ''Yuhi Kabhi'', ''[[Dil Se..|Dil Se]]'', ''[[Fiza]]'', ''Sparsh'', ''Mere Liye'',<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mere Liye (2001) - Apart from singing in his bollywood and Assamese industries, he debuted in Bengali music industry in 2003 where he sung two songs in the movie ''Mon''. In the next year he sung three songs in the movie ''[[Shudhu Tumi]]'' and also was the music director in the film.<ref>{{Citation|last=Array|title=Ektuku Chhoan Lage (Shudhu Tumi / Soundtrack Version) (Full Song) - Zubeen Garg - Download or Listen Free - JioSaavn|url=https://www.jiosaavn.com/song/ektuku-chhoan-lage-shudhu-tumi--soundtrack-version/NA48ZSFaelc|access-date=8 March 2020}}</ref>

In 2005 he sung "''O bondhure"'' and "''Lagena Bhalo"'' in the film  ''[[Premi]]''. In 2008 he got a big break in the movies ''[[Mon Mane Na (2008 film)|Mon Mane Na]]'', ''[[Love Story (2008 film)|Love story]]'', ''[[Chirodini Tumi Je Amar]]'' and ''[[Golmaal (2008 film)|Golmaal]]''. His songs like "''Mon mane Na" from'' the movie [[Mon Mane Na (2008 film)|''Mon Mane Na'']] ,<ref>{{Citation|title=Mon Maane Naa (2008) - Jeet Ganguly|url=https://mio.to//album/Mon+Maane+Naa+%282008%29|language=en|access-date=8 March 2020|archive-date=22 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180822060057/http://mio.to/album/Mon+Maane+Naa+(2008)|url-status=live}}</ref> "''Piya re Piya re"''<ref>{{Citation|title=Chirodini Tumi Je Amar (2008) - Jeet Ganguly|url=https://mio.to//album/Chirodini+Tumi+Je+Amar+%282008%29|language=en|access-date=8 March 2020|archive-date=22 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180822060751/http://mio.to/album/Chirodini+Tumi+Je+Amar+(2008)|url-status=live}}</ref> from the movie ''[[Chirodini Tumi Je Amar|Chirodini Tumi je Amaar]]'', and "''Mon jete chay shudhu"''  from the movie  ''[[Love Story (2008 film)|Love story]]'' became very popular.

He got the biggest break in Bollywood from the movie ''[[Gangster (2006 film)|Gangster]]'' where he sang the song ''"Ya ali"''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Music Hits 2000-2009 |url=http://www.boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=286&catName=MjAwMC0yMDA5 |website=Boxofficeindia.com |access-date=30 April 2017 |date=22 January 2009 |url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090122120910/http://www.boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=286&catName=MjAwMC0yMDA5 |archive-date=22 January 2009 }}</ref> The movie and the song was one of the biggest hits of 2006. The song brought him the best playback singer Global Indian Film Awards (GIFA) in 2006. His next Hindi album ''[[Zindagi (album)|Zindagi]]''<ref>{{cite web|title=Zubeen Garg launches new album|url=http://www.radioandmusic.com/content/editorial/news/zubeen-garg-launches-new-album|publisher=radioandmusic|access-date=30 April 2017|archive-date=28 July 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170728105756/http://www.radioandmusic.com/content/editorial/news/zubeen-garg-launches-new-album|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Zubeen Garg New Hindi Album|url=http://www.assamtimes.org/node/376|publisher=assamtimes|access-date=30 April 2017|date=26 August 2007|archive-date=8 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170908111522/https://www.assamtimes.org/node/376|url-status=live}}</ref> was released in 2007 which was highly appreciated. Some of his [[discography]] songs in various Indian languages along with his various co-artists includes bollywood singers [[Sonu Nigam]], [[Shaan (singer)|Shaan]], [[Sagarika]], [[Shweta Shetty]], [[Suneeta Rao]], [[Alisha Chinai]], [[Mahalakshmi Iyer]], [[Tarali Sarma]], [[Bhitali Das]], [[Lata Mangeshkar]], [[Asha Bhosle]], [[Shankar Mahadevan]], [[Raageshwari]], Zublee Baruah, Diganta Bharati, [[Queen Hazarika]], [[Debojit Saha]], Shanta Uzir, Mitali Chaudhary, [[Shweta Mohan]], Deeplina Deka etc.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fridaymoviez.com/celebrity/biography/zubeen-garg|title=About Zubeen Garg|website=fridaymoviez.come|access-date=30 April 2017|archive-date=8 September 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170908063808/http://www.fridaymoviez.com/celebrity/biography/zubeen-garg|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Garg's younger sister Jonkey Borthakur was an actress and singer who died in a car accident in February 2002 in [[Sonitpur district]] while they were going to perform stage show along with her co-artists.<ref name="Jongki">{{cite news | url=http://www.nenanews.com/NEE%20Feb.7-21,%2002/oh11.htm | title=Jonkey Barthakur dies in car mishap | work=North East News Agency (NENA) | date=21 February 2002 | agency=NENA | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130313031027/http://www.nenanews.com/NEE%20Feb.7-21,%2002/oh11.htm |archive-date=13 March 2013 |access-date=19 January 2013}}</ref> He had released two albums ''Akou Hiyamon'' and ''Xixhu'' in 2002, in the memory of Jonkey Borthakur.<ref>{{Cite web|date=15 May 2017|title=The Language of Music|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/lifestyle/art-and-culture/the-language-of-music-4654378/|access-date=2 May 2021|website=The Indian Express|language=en}}</ref>

Garg married Garima Saikia, a [[fashion designer]] from [[Golaghat]], Assam on 4 February 2002.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://thenortheasttoday.com/assam-zubeen-to-celebrate-his-birthday-by-releasing-music-album-of-mission-china/| title=Assam: Zubeen Garg to celebrate his birthday by releasing music album of 'Mission China'!| publisher=thenortheasttoday.com| access-date=8 September 2017| archive-date=23 May 2017| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170523122714/http://thenortheasttoday.com/assam-zubeen-to-celebrate-his-birthday-by-releasing-music-album-of-mission-china/| url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.magicalassam.com/2013/02/the-valentine-couple-of-jollywood-zubeen-and-garima.html|title=The valentine couple of Jollywood Zubeen and Garima|publisher=magicalassam.com|work=Hussain Ahmed|access-date=14 February 2013|date=14 February 2013|archive-date=30 October 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161030091517/http://www.magicalassam.com/2013/02/the-valentine-couple-of-jollywood-zubeen-and-garima.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.nelive.in/assam/entertainment/assam-zubeen-garg-celebrate-his-birthday-releasing-music-album-mission-china| title=Assam: Zubeen Garg to celebrate his birthday by releasing music album of 'Mission China'!| publisher=nelive.in| access-date=18 November 2016| archive-date=8 September 2017| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170908063736/https://www.nelive.in/assam/entertainment/assam-zubeen-garg-celebrate-his-birthday-releasing-music-album-mission-china| url-status=live}}</ref>
