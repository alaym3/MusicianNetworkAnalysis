'''Seth Haley''', known by his stage name '''Com Truise''' is an American [[electronic musician]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/04/com-truise-announces-new-album-shares-space-themed-single-memory-listen/ | title=Com Truise announces new album, Iteration, shares space-themed "Memory" — listen | last=Ravits | first=Sarah | website=Consequence of Sound | date=April 5, 2017 | access-date=April 14, 2017}}</ref> His stage name is a [[spoonerism]] of the name of American actor [[Tom Cruise]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/reviews/qznv |title=Music – Review of Com Truise – Galactic Melt |publisher=[[BBC]] |date=2011-07-05 |access-date=2017-07-09}}</ref>

==Biography==
Pre-2010, Com Truise had released music under the [[pseudonym]]s '''Sarin Sunday''', '''SYSTM''', and '''Airliner'''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://comtruise.com/about/ | title=About | website=ComTruise.com | access-date=May 7, 2016}}</ref>

In 2010, the first Com Truise recording, the ''Cyanide Sisters'' EP, was released.  Initially, the EP was a free download from the AMDISCS record label.<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/DanceAndElectronic/com_truise-galactic_melt Interview], [[Exclaim!]]</ref> [[Ghostly International]] digitally reissued it in January 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/com-truise-mn0002532578/biography|title=Com Truise &#124; Biography & History|website=AllMusic}}</ref> He resigned from his job as [[art director]] prior to this release. Com Truise [[remix|remixed]] [[Daft Punk]]'s song "ENCOM, Part II" for the ''[[Tron: Legacy Reconfigured]]'' album soon after. In June 2011, he released his debut album, ''[[Galactic Melt]]''.<ref>[http://www.prefixmag.com/news/stream-com-truises-galactic-melt/53223/ Com Truise's Galactic Melt]. Prefix Magazine.</ref> ''[[In Decay]]'' was the immediate full-length followup in 2012. Com Truise's songs are represented by [[Downtown Music Publishing]].{{When|date=October 2018}}

He is from [[Oneida, New York|Oneida]], [[New York (state)|New York]] and now resides in [[Orlando, Florida|Orlando]], [[Florida]].

==Style==
Truise's sound is [[synthesizer]]-heavy [[Synthwave (2000s genre)|synthwave]], influenced by 1980s musical styles, as first offered on the ''Cyanide Sisters'' EP.

===The "Com Truise"-saga===
All of the Com Truise albums, starting with ''Cyanide Sisters'' and ending with ''Iteration'' tell the story of a space traveller named Com Truise;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.magneticmag.com/2016/04/com-truise-interview/|title=We Talk to Com Truise About a Robot Flying in Space and His Latest EP "Silicon Tare"|first=Kirk|last=Williams|website=Magnetic Magazine}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.stufftoblowyourmind.com/blogs/space-music-com-truise-iteration.htm |title=Archived copy |access-date=2019-03-29 |archive-date=2019-03-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190329210814/https://www.stufftoblowyourmind.com/blogs/space-music-com-truise-iteration.htm |url-status=dead }}</ref> Haley described the character as a "synthetic astronaut",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/18711/1/com-truise-wave-1|title=Com Truise – Wave 1|date=February 3, 2014|website=Dazed}}</ref> and the story as a tale about escaping an [[oppression|oppressed]] society.<ref name = "NewRetroWave">{{Cite web|url=https://newretrowave.com/2017/06/12/an-interview-with-com-truise/|title=An Interview with Com Truise An Interview with Com Truise|date=November 11, 2017|website=NewRetroWave|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171111094855/https://newretrowave.com/retrowave-articles/2017/6/12/an-interview-with-com-truise|archive-date=November 11, 2017|access-date=March 29, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Equipment==
Since 2017, Truise has incorporated [[Dave Smith Instruments]] Ob-6 analogue keyboard on some tracks and for live performances.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://drownedinsound.com/in_depth/4151109-com-truises-track-by-track-guide-to-iteration|title=Com Truise's Track By Track Guide to Iteration|website=DrownedInSound|access-date=2018-10-14|archive-date=2018-10-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181014091607/http://drownedinsound.com/in_depth/4151109-com-truises-track-by-track-guide-to-iteration|url-status=dead}}</ref>
