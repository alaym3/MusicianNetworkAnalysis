'''Dua Lipa''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|d|uː|ə|_|ˈ|l|iː|p|ə}}; {{IPA-sq|ˈdua ˈlipa|lang}}; born {{Birth date|df=yes|1995|8|22}}) is an English singer, songwriter and model<!-- Do not add Albanian as ethnicity does not belong in the lead section per [[MOS:BLPLEAD]] and nor is there a reliable source that confirms she also has Albanian citizenship. -->. After working as a [[Model (person)|model]], she signed with [[Warner Records|Warner Bros. Records]] in 2014 and released her [[Dua Lipa (album)|eponymous debut album]] in 2017. The album peaked at number three on the [[UK Albums Chart]] and yielded eight singles, including "[[Be the One (Dua Lipa song)|Be the One]]", "[[IDGAF (song)|IDGAF]]", and the UK number-one single "[[New Rules]]", which also peaked at number six in the US. The album has been certified platinum in numerous countries worldwide and won Lipa the Brit Awards for [[Brit Award for British Female Solo Artist|British Female Solo Artist]] and [[Brit Award for British Breakthrough Act|British Breakthrough Act]] in [[2018 Brit Awards|2018]].

Her 2018 single "[[One Kiss (song)|One Kiss]]" with [[Calvin Harris]] peaked at number one in the UK and became the year{{'}}s [[List of UK Singles Chart number ones of the 2010s|longest-running number-one single]] by a female artist, winning the 2019 [[Brit Award for Song of the Year]]. In 2019, she won the [[Grammy Award]] for [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], as well as the [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording]] for her [[Silk City (duo)|Silk City]]-collaboration "[[Electricity (Silk City and Dua Lipa song)|Electricity]]". Later that year, Lipa released "[[Don't Start Now]]" as the lead single from her second studio album. Reaching number two in the UK and US, and number four on the US year-end chart, it was the most commercially successful song by a female artist in the US in 2020. Its follow-up singles "[[Levitating (song)|Levitating]]", "[[Physical (Dua Lipa song)|Physical]]" and "[[Break My Heart (Dua Lipa song)|Break My Heart]]" also charted in the top ten.

Lipa released her second studio album, ''[[Future Nostalgia]]'' (2020), to critical acclaim. It earned six Grammy nominations—including [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]], [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]] and [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]]—and became her first UK number-one album. The album helped Lipa win British Female Solo Artist and [[Brit Award for British Album of the Year|British Album of the Year]] at the [[2021 Brit Awards]]. She has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Dua Lipa|many accolades]], including recognition on the [[Time 100#Time 100 Next list|''Time'' 100 Next list]] (2021). She made her modelling debut at [[Versace]]'s Spring/Summer 2022 show at [[Milan Fashion Week]].

===2013–2017: Career beginnings and ''Dua Lipa''===
[[File:Dua Lipa-0761.jpg|thumb|right|Lipa in 2016|262x262px]]
In 2013, Lipa signed a contract with Tap Management, directed by Ben Mawson and Ed Millett, while working as a waitress in a cocktail bar. Lipa was introduced to Mawson by her lawyer, who discouraged her from signing another publishing deal that was offered to her. After endorsing, and campaigning for, U.S. Senator [[Bernie Sanders]] in the [[2020 Democratic Party presidential primaries|2020 Democratic primaries]], Lipa expressed support for [[Joe Biden]] to become the U.S. president at a virtual event aimed at [[Albanian Americans]] later that year during the general election season, arguing that the [[Kosovo Albanians|Kosovars]] "owed" support to Biden because he was against the [[War crimes in the Kosovo War|massacre and ethnic cleansing]] of [[Albanians]] by having supported the [[NATO bombing of Yugoslavia]].<ref name="Mejdini">{{cite news|last=Mejdini|first=Fatjona|date=30 October 2020|title=Biden Counts on History to Help Win Albanian American Vote|work=|agency=Balkan Insight|url=https://balkaninsight.com/2020/10/30/biden-counts-on-history-to-help-win-albanian-american-vote/|url-status=live|access-date=30 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201101010446/https://balkaninsight.com/2020/10/30/biden-counts-on-history-to-help-win-albanian-american-vote/|archive-date=1 November 2020}}</ref> In January 2021, she published a call to her Instagram followers to encourage Kosovo citizens to vote in the [[2021 Kosovan parliamentary election]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=19 January 2021|title=Dua Lipa dhe babai i saj u bëjnë thirrje qytetarëve të Kosovës që të dalin të votojnë më 14 shkurt|url=https://telegrafi.com/dua-lipa-dhe-babai-saj-u-bejne-thirrje-qytetareve-te-kosoves-qe-te-dalin-te-votojne-14-shkurt/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210208135525/https://telegrafi.com/dua-lipa-dhe-babai-saj-u-bejne-thirrje-qytetareve-te-kosoves-qe-te-dalin-te-votojne-14-shkurt/|archive-date=8 February 2021|access-date=8 February 2021|website=Telegrafi|language=Albanian}}</ref>

In May 2021, after expressing solidarity for the death of innocent civilians due to the [[2021 Israel–Palestine crisis|Israel–Palestine crisis]], Lipa criticised ''[[The New York Times]]'' for publishing an advertisement claiming her, [[Bella Hadid|Bella]] and [[Gigi Hadid]] to be [[antisemitic]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Dua Lipa criticises ad claiming her views on Israeli-Palestinian conflict are antisemitic |website=[[NME]] |date=23 May 2021 |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/dua-lipa-criticises-ad-claiming-her-views-on-israeli-palestinian-conflict-are-antisemitic-2946420 |access-date=6 June 2021}}</ref>

In July 2021, through her Sunny Hill Foundation, Lipa joined an initiative that was launched by the Municipality of [[Tirana]], called "Adopt a Kindergarten". The aim of the project was to reconstruct a kindergarten that was severely damaged by the [[2019 Albania earthquake]]. She was quoted saying, "The kindergarten will be very beautiful and I am very proud that Sunny Hill Foundation is a part of it. Let's see better things for our country. I am very proud to be Albanian.".<ref>{{Cite web|date=23 July 2021|title=Dua Lipa in Tirana for reconstruction initiative "Adopt a Kindergarten"|url=https://euronews.al/en/albania/2021/07/23/dua-lipa-in-tirana-for-reconstruction-initiative-adopt-a-kindergarten/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-10-15|website=www.euronews.al|publisher=[[Euronews#Franchises|Euronews Albania]]}}</ref> The kindergarten was completed in October 2021, and was named "Sunny Hill Kindergarten".<ref>{{Cite instagram|author=Dua Lipa|author-link=Dua Lipa|user=dualipa|postid=CU-R-_osTMN|date=14 October 2021|title=a very proud moment|access-date=14 October 2021}}</ref>

An advocate for the rights of displaced people, Lipa endorsed [[Kosovo]]'s president [[Vjosa Osmani]] in August 2021 in the search for the missing civilians of the [[Kosovo war]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Dua Lipa endorses Vjosa Osmani |work=Gazeta Blic |url=https://gazetablic.com/dua-lipa-shperndan-postimet-e-vjosa-osmani-per-diten-nderkombetare-te-te-zhdukurve/ |access-date= 30 August 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Her first name "Dua" was her grandmother's suggestion and means "love" in [[Albanian language|Albanian]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/apr/15/dua-lipa-pop-has-to-be-fun-you-cant-get-upset-about-every-little-thing-new-rules|title='Pop has to be fun. You can't get upset about every little thing'|website=The Guardian |date=15 April 2018}}</ref>

She is occasionally referred to as Dula Peep, which originated as a [[mispronunciation]] by [[Daytime television|daytime TV]] [[celebrity|personality]] [[Wendy Williams]] in 2018.<ref name="Wendy Dula Peep">{{cite news |last1=Syd |first1=Robinson |title=Wendy Williams Just Tried To Say Dua Lipa's Name And It's Everything |url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/sydrobinson1/wendy-williams-just-mispronounced-dua-lipas-name |access-date=27 November 2021 |agency=Buzzfeed |publisher=Buzzfeed |date=23 May 2018}}</ref> She has since positively embraced the [[nickname]].<ref name="Dula Peep Fallon">{{cite news |last1=Wang |first1=Steffanee |title=DUA LIPA IS COOL WITH YOU CALLING HER "DULA PEEP" |url=https://www.nylon.com/dua-lipa-dula-peep |access-date=27 November 2021 |agency=Nylon |publisher=Nylon |date=19 December 2019}}</ref>
