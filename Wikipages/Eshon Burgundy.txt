'''Anton Eshon Hairston''', who goes by the stage name '''Eshon Burgundy''', is an American [[Christian hip hop]] musician. His first album, ''Blood Rushing to My Head'', was released in 2012 with Salvation Armie Music Group. He released his second album, 2015's [[The Fear of God (Eshon Burgundy album)|''The Fear of God'']], with Humble Beast Records. This was a [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] chart breakthrough release.

==Early life==
Eshon Burgundy was born Anton Eshon Hairston in [[Abington Township, Montgomery County, Pennsylvania|Abington, Pennsylvania]],<ref name="AllMusic Biography">{{cite web|title=Eshon Burgundy: Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/eshon-burgundy-mn0000463903/biography|first=Paul|last=Simpson|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 15, 2015}}</ref><ref name="TW Wife Bday">{{cite web|title=Happy (3 1/2 hours early) Birthday @eshonburgundy! The girls can't wait for you to open your gifts… |url=https://twitter.com/Tandeace/status/325783142621409282|first=Tandeace M.|last=Hairston|publisher=[[Twitter]]|access-date=March 20, 2015|date=April 20, 2013}}</ref> He was raised by his mother Rochelle in [[Philadelphia|Philadelphia, Pennsylvania]].<ref name="NRT Bio">{{cite web|title=Eshon Burgundy Artist Profile|url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/artistdetail.php?artist_id=4969|first=Dwayne|last=Lacy|publisher=New Release Tuesday|access-date=March 20, 2015|date=March 18, 2015}}</ref> He was raised in an environment of constantly moving from residence-to-residence across Philadelphia after his mother was in an abusive relationship with a boyfriend at the time.<ref name="NRT Bio"/> They eventually settled in a housing complex, Passyunk Homes.<ref name="NRT Bio"/> This would lead him to become the responsible oldest sibling in his family, where he has two younger brothers, Seifuddin and Ameer, and a younger sister, Aleema.<ref name="NRT Bio"/> Eshon was shot three times when he was 15 years old, coming within minutes of dying.<ref name="NRT Bio"/><ref name="Geo Clan">{{cite web|title = An Interview with Eshon Burgundy|url = http://www.geoclan.com/music/articles/06/interviews/AnInterviewwithEshonBurgundy.htm|first1 = Josey|last1 = El|first2 = Clayton|last2 = Ruley|publisher = Geo Clan|access-date = March 20, 2015}}</ref> This was the impetus for him to dedicate the next five years of his life to being involved in the hip hop underground community in his hometown of Philadelphia.<ref name="NRT Bio"/> God put up some roadblocks to his success, when he signed two record deals with drug kingpins around 2000 that went nowhere.<ref name="NRT Bio"/> He welcomed Jesus Christ as his personal Lord and Savior in 2001.<ref name="NRT Bio"/> He started making hip hop music that would try to reach the lost in his hometown, until he relocated to [[Jacksonville, Florida]].<ref name="NRT Bio"/>

==Music career==
His professional music career got started in 2007, when he was featured along with poet Black Ice on Dj Jazzy Jeff's Return Of The Magnificent album. The song was entitled Run that back. Eshon Burgundy released his debut album ''Blood Rushing to My Head'' with Salvation Armie Music Group on July 24, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|title=Blood Rusing to My Head: Eshon Burgundy |url=https://www.amazon.com/Blood-Rushing-Head-Eshon-Burgundy/dp/B008OI3DF4/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&qid=1426823980&sr=8-2&keywords=Eshon+Burgundy|author=[[Amazon.com]]|access-date=March 20, 2015|date=July 24, 2012}}</ref> His second album, [[The Fear of God (Eshon Burgundy album)|''The Fear of God'']], was released by Humble Beast Records on March 3, 2015, just shy of his thirty-fifth birthday.<ref name="NRT Album">{{cite web|title=The Fear of God by Eshon Burgundy |url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=14994|author=New Release Tuesday|publisher=New Release Tuesday|access-date=March 20, 2015|date=March 3, 2012}}</ref> This was his [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] chart breakthrough album, with it charting at No. 15 on the [[Christian Albums]], No. 20 on the [[Top Rap Albums|Rap Albums]], No. 25 on the [[Independent Albums]], and on the [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Albums]] at No. 4.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite journal|url=https://www.billboard.com/files/index/artistindex_03_21_2015.pdf|title=Artist Index: March 21, 2015|journal=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 20, 2015|date=March 21, 2015}}</ref> On May 27, 2016, Eshon Burgundy released his highly anticipated third album called The Passover. The album was inspired by the Passover of the [[The Exodus|Exodus]] of the [[Israelites]] out of [[Egypt]]. He then released a fourth and most recent album which he named For The Love of Money on September 20, 2019.

==Personal life==
Eshon Burgundy resides just outside Atlanta, Georgia, in the community of Hampton, with his wife, Zara,<ref name="NRT Bio"/> and their three children. Harlem, Evyr and Kinsington.<ref name="NRT Bio"/><ref name="DO 512">{{cite web|title = Eshon Burgundy|url = http://do512.com/artists/eshon-burgundy|author = Humble Beast Records|publisher = Do512|access-date = March 20, 2015}}</ref>
