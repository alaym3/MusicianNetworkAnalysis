'''Annabella Avery Thorne''' (born October 8, 1997) is an American actress, model, and singer.<ref name="Allmusic">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/bella-thorne-mn0002731953|title=Bella Thorne|last=Erlewine|first=Stephen Thomas|publisher=AllMusic.com|year=2016|access-date=August 3, 2016}}</ref> She is the recipient of several accolades, including an [[Imagen Awards|Imagen Award]], a [[Shorty Awards|Shorty Award]], a [[Teen Choice Awards|Teen Choice Award]] and three [[Young Artist Award]]s.

Thorne first received recognition for her roles as Margaux Darling in the series ''[[Dirty Sexy Money]]'' (2007–2008) and as Ruthy Spivey in the drama series ''[[My Own Worst Enemy (TV series)|My Own Worst Enemy]]'' (2009), the latter of which earning her a [[Young Artist Award]]. She gained prominence for her role as [[CeCe Jones]] on the [[Disney Channel]] series ''[[Shake It Up (American TV series)|Shake It Up]]'' (2010–2013), for which she won an [[Imagen Awards|Imagen Award]].<ref>{{cite web |first=Tehrene|last= Firman |title=Bella Thorne Refuses to Auto-Tune Her Music Like Other Disney Stars |url=http://www.j-14.com/posts/bella-thorne-refuses-to-auto-tune-her-music-like-other-disney-stars-38559 |publisher=J-14 |access-date=September 28, 2014}}</ref> Thorne subsequently led the drama series ''[[Famous in Love]]'' (2017–2018), for which she received two [[Teen Choice Awards|Teen Choice Award]] nominations, and has appeared in numerous feature films, including ''[[Blended (film)|Blended]]'' (2014), ''[[Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Road Chip]]'' (2015), [[The Babysitter (2017 film)|''The Babysitter'' film series]] (2017–2020). She received praise for her roles in ''[[The DUFF]]'' (2015), ''[[Amityville: The Awakening]]'' (2017), ''[[Infamous (2020 film)|Infamous]]'' (2020) and [[Girl (2020 film)|''Girl'']] (2020).

Outside of acting, Thorne has ventured into music; she released her debut single, "[[Watch Me (Bella Thorne and Zendaya song)|Watch Me]]" in 2011, which charted at 86 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. She has since released the EP [[Made in Japan (EP)|''Made in Japan'']] in 2012 and the EP [[Jersey (EP)|''Jersey'']] in 2014. She made her directorial debut in 2019, directing the [[adult film]] ''[[Her & Him]]'', which garnered her a mixed reception, although she won a [[Pornhub|Pornhub Visionary Award]].

==Career==

=== 2003–2009: Early work and acting beginnings ===
Thorne's first film appearance was an uncredited role as a sidelines fan in the 2003 film ''[[Stuck on You (film)|Stuck on You]]''. She has since appeared in film and television projects including [[Entourage (U.S. TV series)|''Entourage'']] and ''[[The O.C.]]'' as a younger version of [[Taylor Townsend (The O.C.)|Taylor Townsend]]. In 2007, she joined the recurring cast of ''[[Dirty Sexy Money]]''{{'}}s second season as Margaux Darling. This is her first major television role. The series revolves around lawyer and family man Nick George when Nick's father mysteriously dies in a plane crash, he agrees to take his position as the Darling family's lawyer, while trying to discover who committed the murder.<ref name="Bella Thorne Interview" />

In 2008, Thorne starred alongside [[Christian Slater]] and [[Taylor Lautner]] in the short-lived drama series [[My Own Worst Enemy (TV series)|''My Own Worst Enemy'']], for which she won a [[Young Artist Awards 2010|Young Artist Award]] for her portrayal of the character Ruthy Spivey; Thorne described her ''Enemy'' casting as a major breakthrough as this was the first recurring role in her career. The same year, she starred in the third-last episode of ''[[October Road (TV series)|October Road]]'' as Angela Ferilli, the pre-adolescence crush of the main characters. Her older brother Remy also guest-starred in the same episode as a younger [[Eddie Latekka]].{{citation needed|date=August 2020}}

In 2009, she starred the web series ''[[Little Monk]]'', which depicted characters from the series ''[[Monk (TV series)|Monk]]'', as Wendy, one of Monk's classmates. The webisodes are available only on the "Best of Monk" DVD. Also in 2009, she played the role of the vengeful antagonist in the horror film ''[[Forget Me Not (2009 film)|Forget Me Not]]''. Thorne took on a supporting role in the family drama ''[[Raspberry Magic]]'', the movie premiered at the [[Cinequest Film Festival]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cinequest.org/media/CQ20Guide.pdf |title=Mavericks: Official Guide to Cinequest Film Festival 20 |date=March 7, 2010 |access-date=January 26, 2011 |page=46}}{{dead link|date=July 2017 |bot=Jennica |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> and the [[San Francisco International Asian American Film Festival]] in 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://filmguide.festival.asianamericanmedia.org/tixSYS/2010/filmguide/Director/PP |title=Events by director: detail view |publisher=2010 San Francisco Asian American Film Festival |access-date=January 26, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150912104538/http://filmguide.festival.asianamericanmedia.org/tixSYS/2010/filmguide/Director/PP |archive-date=September 12, 2015}}</ref>

Thorne played CeCe Jones, a dancer with heavy ambitions for a career in the spotlight despite having [[dyslexia]].<ref name="Variety.com" /> The show is a [[buddy comedy]] centered on a teen dance show (in a show-within-a-show format) co-starring Thorne and [[Zendaya]].<ref name="OceanUp.com" /> The multi-camera series began production in Hollywood, California, in July 2010 and premiered on November 7, 2010, on Disney Channel. While she had a substantial portfolio of work in television and film, Thorne had no experience in professional dancing before being cast. After signing onto the show in October 2009, she began taking three dance classes every night.
