'''Dan + Shay''' (pronounced as "Dan and Shay") is an American [[country pop]] music duo composed of vocalists and songwriters Dan Smyers and Shay Mooney.<ref>{{cite web|last=Wyland |first=Sarah |url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2013/10/16/dan-shay-release-debut-single-to-country-radio/ |title=Dan + Shay Release Debut Single To Country Radio &#124; Great American Country |publisher=Blog.gactv.com |date=2013-10-16 |access-date=2015-07-29}}</ref> They are signed to [[Warner Records|Warner Records Nashville]] and have released four albums, ''[[Where It All Began (Dan + Shay album)|Where It All Began]]'', ''[[Obsessed (album)|Obsessed]]'', ''[[Dan + Shay (album)|Dan + Shay]]''. and ''[[Good Things (Dan + Shay album)|Good Things]]''. Of their nine singles; five have topped the [[Country Airplay]] chart and two have topped the [[Hot Country Songs]] chart.  In addition to their own material, the group's members have collaborated with [[Justin Bieber]], [[Rascal Flatts]], [[Lindsey Stirling]], [[RaeLynn]], and [[Kelly Clarkson]].  From 2019 to 2021, the duo won three consecutive [[Grammy Award for Best Country Duo/Group Performance|Grammy Awards for Best Country Duo/Group Performance]] for the songs "[[Tequila (Dan + Shay song)|Tequila]]", "[[Speechless (Dan + Shay song)|Speechless]]", and "[[10,000 Hours]]".

==History==
===2013–2017: ''Where It All Began'' and ''Obsessed''===
Before the duo's foundation, Mooney was a solo artist on [[T-Pain]]'s [[Nappy Boy Entertainment]] label and Smyers was a member of a group called Bonaventure as well as a previous member of the band Transition on [[Floodgate Records]].<ref name="taste"/> The two met in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], on December 7, 2012, and started writing the day after they met. The first song they ever wrote together got put on hold for [[Rascal Flatts]]. Within two months, the duo had multiple publishing offers and signed with [[Warner/Chappell Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/5785939/inside-guy-oseary-and-paul-mcguinness-30m-live-nation-deal-new-roles-more |title=Inside Guy Oseary and Paul McGuinness' $30M Live Nation Deal |publisher=Billboard.com/ |date=November 13, 2013 |access-date=November 13, 2014}}</ref>

On October 14, 2013, the debut single, "[[19 You + Me]]", was released to country radio. Dan + Shay wrote the song with [[Danny Orton]]. The song received a positive review at ''[[Taste of Country]]'' which praised the "warm details" and "emotion."<ref name="taste">{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/dan-and-shay-19-you-and-me/ |title=Dan + Shay, '19 You + Me' [Listen&#93; |publisher=Tasteofcountry.com |access-date=July 29, 2015}}</ref> Their debut album, ''[[Where It All Began (Dan + Shay album)|Where It All Began]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboot.com/dan-shay-where-it-all-began-cover-track-listing/ |title=Dan + Shay Reveal Title, Cover + Track Listing for Debut |publisher=Theboot.com |access-date=2015-07-29}}</ref> was released on April 1, 2014, which Orton produced with [[Scott Hendricks]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.warnermusicnashville.com/news/2014-acm-award-nominees-dan-shay-to-release-debut-album-april-1st-on-warner-bros-records |title=2014 ACM Award Nominees Dan + Shay to Release Debut Album April 1st on Warner Bros. Records' |publisher=Warnermusicnashville.com |date=February 4, 2014 |access-date=February 6, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140222221340/http://www.warnermusicnashville.com/news/2014-acm-award-nominees-dan-shay-to-release-debut-album-april-1st-on-warner-bros-records |archive-date=February 22, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The album's second single, "[[Show You Off]]", was released to country radio on May 12, 2014.<ref name="Show You Off Adds">{{cite web|title=R&R :: Going For Adds :: Country|url=http://gfa.radioandrecords.com/PublishGFA/GFANextPage.asp?sDate=05/12/2014&Format=4|work=[[Radio & Records]]|access-date=April 28, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140429044230/http://gfa.radioandrecords.com/PublishGFA/GFANextPage.asp?sDate=05%2F12%2F2014&Format=4|archive-date=April 29, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> They performed on ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'', performing "19 You + Me" in 2014. They made their ''[[Grand Ole Opry]]'' debut on February 1, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.opry.com/artist/dan-shay|title=Dan + Shay Grand Ole Opry'|date=January 15, 2014|publisher=opry.com|access-date=January 15, 2014}}</ref> They also received a 2014 [[Academy of Country Music Awards]] Vocal Duo of the Year nomination.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.acmawards.com/nominees/ |title=ACM Awards Nominees' |publisher=acmcountry.com/ |date=January 29, 2014 |access-date=January 29, 2014}}</ref> The album's third single, "[[Nothin' Like You]]", was released to country radio on February 23, 2015. It reached number one on the [[Country Airplay]] chart in December 2015.

Dan + Shay was the opening act for [[Hunter Hayes]] on his [[We're Not Invisible Tour]], which began in March 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hunterhayes.com/news/breaking-news-tour-announcement |title=Breaking News - Tour Announcement |publisher=Hunterhayes.com |date=January 26, 2014 |access-date=January 26, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140202095702/http://www.hunterhayes.com/news/breaking-news-tour-announcement |archive-date=February 2, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Dan + Shay opened up for [[Blake Shelton]] on the 2014 leg of his [[Ten Times Crazier Tour]]. The tour began in June 2014 and ended in October 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.blakeshelton.com/news/blake-announces-ten-times-crazier-tour-433701 |title=Blake Announces Ten Times Crazier Tour' |publisher=BlakeShelton.com |date=January 17, 2014 |access-date=January 17, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140120051827/http://www.blakeshelton.com/news/blake-announces-ten-times-crazier-tour-433701 |archive-date=January 20, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Following the end of the [[Ten Times Crazier Tour]], Dan + Shay embarked on their own 10-date headlining tour, entitled the "Where It All Began Tour." The tour began on October 9, 2014, in [[Minneapolis]] and concluded in [[Columbus, Ohio]], on October 26, 2014.<ref name="2014 tour">{{cite web | url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2014/05/24/dan-shay-announce-where-it-all-began-tour/ | title=Dan + Shay Announce Where It All Began Tour | publisher=Great American Country | date=May 24, 2014 | access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> After the conclusion of the "Where It All Began Tour," Dan + Shay set out on the road once more with [[Hunter Hayes]] on his [[Tattoo (Your Name) Tour]].

In August 2015, the duo announced the Just the Right Kind of Crazy Tour across the Midwest and eastern United States.<ref name="2015 tour">{{cite web|url=http://radio.com/2015/08/10/dan-shay-just-the-right-kind-of-crazy-tour/|title=Dan + Shay Announce 'Just The Right Kind of Crazy' Tour| publisher=Radio.com|date=August 13, 2015|access-date=August 10, 2015}}</ref> Their sophomore album's first single, "[[From the Ground Up (song)|From the Ground Up]]", was released to country radio in February 2016. The album's second single, "[[How Not To]]", was released to country radio on September 26, 2016. It reached number one on the Country Airplay chart in July 2017. The album's third single, "[[Road Trippin' (Dan + Shay song)|Road Trippin']]", was released to country radio on July 17, 2017.

===2018: ''Dan + Shay''===
In January 2018, the duo released "[[Tequila (Dan + Shay song)|Tequila]]", the lead single from their [[Dan + Shay (album)|self-titled third studio album]], which was released on June 22, 2018. "Tequila" has since become their highest-charting effort on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], with it reaching the top 40. The album also features a collaboration with [[Kelly Clarkson]] on "Keeping Score".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/danandshay/status/997281346727890944?s=21|title=SELF-TITLED ALBUM &#124; JUNE 22 Pre-order starts tonight at 11 PM CT!pic.twitter.com/es5uTgFKXm|first=Dan +|last=Shay|date=May 17, 2018}}</ref> In August, they released ''Dan + Shay (The Vocals)'', which includes the vocal tracks of the two singles from the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/album/dan-shay-the-vocals-single/1420630009|title=Dan + Shay (The Vocals) - Single by Dan + Shay on Apple Music|work=iTunes|access-date=August 9, 2018}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Good Things''===
On October 4, 2019, they released "[[10,000 Hours]]", a collaboration with Canadian singer-songwriter [[Justin Bieber]], after his and [[Hailey Bieber]]'s wedding in South Carolina on September 30, 2019. The song debuted at number 4 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] and also won the Grammy Award for Best Country Duo/Group Performance. The duo co-wrote [[Eric Ethridge]]'s 2020 single "[[Dream Girl (Eric Ethridge song)|Dream Girl]]".<ref>{{cite news|title=Eric Ethridge's 'Dream Girl' Becomes a Precious Memory in Dan + Shay-Penned Single [Exclusive Premiere]|first=Cillea|last=Houghton|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/eric-ethridges-dream-girl-dan-and-shay/|work=Taste of Country|date=February 20, 2020|access-date=January 5, 2021}}</ref> On July 31, 2020, the duo returned with the single, "[[I Should Probably Go to Bed]]". They then came back with the single "[[Glad You Exist]]", which was released on February 5, 2021. On July 16, 2021, the duo announced their fourth album ''Good Things'' while releasing the album's title single "[[Good Things (Dan + Shay song)|Good Things]]" and revealing the album's release date of August 13, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/news/dan-shay-fourth-studio-album-good-things/|title=Dan + Shay Reveal Details of Fourth Studio Album, ‘GOOD THINGS’|last=Parton|first=Chris|publisher=Sounds Like Nashville|date=July 16, 2021|accessdate=July 20, 2021}}</ref>In August 2021, the duo collaborated with [[Carrie Underwood]] on a cover of "Only Us" from ''[[Dear Evan Hansen]]'', which they recorded for the soundtrack album of the musical's [[Dear Evan Hansen (film)|2021 film adaptation]]. It was released as a single on September 3, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1837541/carrie-underwood-dan-shay-collaborate-on-new-single-only-us/|work=CMT News|title=Carrie Underwood, Dan + Shay Collaborate On New Single "Only Us"|first=Marcus K.|last=Dowling|date=September 1, 2021|access-date=September 4, 2021}}</ref> On November 2, 2021, the duo revealed they had to cancel two shows, Orlando and Atlanta, on their Dan + Shay The (Arena) Tour due to a positive COVID-19 test within their touring camp.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nicholson|first=Jessica|title=Dan + Shay Cancel Two Arena Concerts Due to COVID-19|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/9654520/dan-and-shay-cancel-concerts-covid-19/|date=November 2, 2021|access-date=November 2, 2021|website=Billboard}}</ref>

==In popular culture==
Dan + Shay song "Stop Drop + Roll" was featured on the ''[[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]]'' season two episode, "Your Good Girl's Gonna Go Bad". They were also featured on ''[[The Bachelorette (American TV series)|The Bachelorette]]'' in an episode that aired on June 7, 2016.

The duo also collaborated with violinist, dancer, and YouTuber [[Lindsey Stirling]] on a song from her third album ''[[Brave Enough]]''. The song was entitled "Those Days", and the album was released on August 19, 2016.

==Members==
===Dan Smyers===
[[File:Dan Smyers of Dan + Shay in 2017.jpg|thumb|upright=0.9|Dan Smyers]]
Daniel "Dan" Smyers, born {{birth date and age|1987|8|16|mf=y}} was raised in [[Wexford, Pennsylvania]], where he attended [[North Allegheny Senior High School]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pghcitypaper.com/pittsburgh/in-between/Content?oid=1337222 |title=Local rockers Transition straddle commercialism and conviction' |publisher=Pghcitypaper.com |date=June 8, 2006 |access-date=June 8, 2006}}</ref> He attended [[Carnegie Mellon University]] with plans to study finance and play football.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://lescheminsdurock.wordpress.com/2012/05/12/bonaventure-our-dream-is-to-touch-people-with-our-music/ |title=Bonaventure: 'Our dream is to touch people with our music' |publisher=Patriciatanne.com/ |date=May 12, 2012 |access-date=May 12, 2012}}</ref>

Prior to joining Dan + Shay, Smyers fronted an [[emo]] band called Transition, which released one full-length record in 2006 on [[floodgate records]] produced by [[Mike Herrera]], who is best known for his work with [[MxPx]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/dan-and-shay-dan-smyers-emo-band-all-time-low/|title = Dan Smyers' emo band before Dan + Shay once opened for All Time Low|date = 21 January 2021}}</ref> Transition appeared on the [[Vans Warped Tour]] in both 2004 and 2006, and had opened for bands including [[All Time Low]], MxPx, [[Streetlight Manifesto]] & [[Reel Big Fish]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/transition/2006/congress-theater-chicago-il-6bf5da6a.html|title = Transition Setlist at Congress Theater, Chicago}}</ref>

Instruments: Vocals, Guitar, Bass, Piano

Smyers and Abby Law were in a relationship for many years before becoming engaged in 2016 and were married May 13, 2017.{{Citation needed|date=October 2020}}  Law made a cameo in a Dan + Shay videos for their songs "Nothin' Like You", “Speechless” and "10,000 Hours".{{Citation needed|date=October 2020}} Smyers and Abby work with Wags & Walks Rescue Nashville to save dogs and place them in their forever homes. They have five dogs together as of March 2021.

===Shay Mooney===
[[File:Shay Mooney of Dan + Shay in 2017.jpg|thumb|upright=0.9|Shay Mooney]]
James Shay Mooney, born {{birth date and age|1991|12|27|mf=y}} was raised in [[Natural Dam, Arkansas]], and attended [[Union Christian Academy]] and [[Van Buren High School (Van Buren, Arkansas)|Van Buren High School]]. Following high school, he attended [[University of Valley Forge|Valley Forge Christian College]] for one year before completely pursuing his music career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cnnradio.cnn.com/2013/03/17/cnn-radio-music-notes-shay-mooney-a-redneck-in-an-urban-world|title=Shay Mooney – 'a redneck in an urban world'|work=Cnnradio.cnn.com|access-date=March 17, 2013}}</ref>

Instruments: Vocals, Piano, Guitar

On January 24, 2017, his fiancée Hannah Billingsley gave birth to their son, Asher James Mooney.<ref name="asher">{{cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/shay-mooney-hannah-billingsley-welcome-son-asher-james/|title=Shay Mooney and Hannah Billingsley Welcome Son Asher James|work=people.com|access-date=January 24, 2017}}</ref> Mooney and Billingsley were married on October 20, 2017, in Arkansas.<ref name="Hannah Mooney">{{cite web|url=http://people.com/country/shay-mooney-marries-hannah-billingsley/|work=people.com|title=Dan + Shay's Shay Mooney Marries Hannah Billingsley — All the Wedding Details!|access-date=October 21, 2017}}</ref>  On February 21, 2020, the couple welcomed their second son, Ames Alexander Mooney.<ref name="ames">{{cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/shay-mooney-welcomes-son-ames-alexander-first-photo/|title=Meet Ames Alexander! Dan + Shay's Shay Mooney and Wife Hannah Welcome Second Son|work=people.com|access-date=February 22, 2020}}</ref>
