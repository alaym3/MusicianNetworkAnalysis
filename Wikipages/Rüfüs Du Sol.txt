'''Rüfüs Du Sol''', stylised as '''RÜFÜS DU SOL''' and formerly known as simply '''Rüfüs''' (stylized '''RÜFÜS''') from 2010 to 2018,<ref>{{cite web|last1=Ma|first1=Roger|title=RUFUS forced to change name in US|url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/rufus-forced-to-change-name-in-us-20140130-31nqq.html|website=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|access-date=5 August 2014|date=30 January 2014}}</ref><ref name="Name Change">{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/rufus-have-changed-their-name-ahead-of-a-new-album/9791964|title=RÜFÜS are dead, long live RÜFÜS DU SOL|website=[[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]]|date=24 May 2018 |access-date=26 May 2018}}</ref> is an Australian group from [[Sydney]], that consists of Tyrone Lindqvist, Jon George and James Hunt. Their debut album ''[[Atlas (Rüfüs album)|Atlas]]'' peaked at number one in Australia, while their second album ''[[Bloom (Rüfüs album)|Bloom]]'' debuted atop the Australian albums chart in early 2016. Their single "[[You Were Right (Rüfüs song)|You Were Right]]" won the [[ARIA Award for Best Dance Release]] in 2015. While still known as Rüfüs elsewhere, they performed as Rüfüs Du Sol in the United States because Rufus [[Rufus (band)|was already taken]]. In 2018 they changed their name to Rüfüs Du Sol internationally.

They have performed at the [[Byron Bay Bluesfest]] in NSW in 2013, Byron Bay's [[Splendour in the Grass]] in 2014, [[Electric Forest Festival]] at Double JJ Resort in 2016 and 2018, [[Mountain Sounds (festival)|Mountain Sounds]] in 2017 in NSW, [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] in 2016, 2017, and 2019, as well as [[Field Day (Sydney festival)|Field Day]] on New Years Day in Sydney in 2019.

== Career ==
=== 2010–2012: Formation ===
The band formed consisting of Jon George, Tyrone Lindqvist and James Hunt in November 2010. On 1 January 2011 they released their debut EP ''Rüfüs''.<ref name="RufusEP">{{cite web|title=Rufus EP|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/rufus-ep/id412480557|work=[[Apple Inc.]]|publisher=[[iTunes Store]] Australia|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref> "We Left" their debut single was released on 25 July 2011.<ref name="WeLeft">{{cite web|title=We Left – EP|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/we-left-ep/id451436776|work=Apple Inc.|publisher=iTunes Store Australia|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref> Two tracks from the EP, "Paris Collides" and "We Left" reached Nos. 8 and 13 on the Hype Machine charts simultaneously, while the video for "We Left" was nominated as one of 12 finalists internationally in the 2012 Vimeo awards for best music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gigpiglet.com.au/recordings/child-no-3/630-2|title=RÜFÜS |publisher=gigpiglet|date=2012|access-date=29 May 2018}}</ref>

In April 2012 they released their second EP ''[[Rüfüs (Blue EP)|Rüfüs (Blue)]]''.<ref name="RufusEPBlue">{{cite web|title=Rufus EP (Blue)|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/rufus-ep-blue/id514526309|work=Apple Inc.|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref> The double A-sided single "This Summer"/"Selena" was released on 16 July 2012.<ref name="ThisSummer">{{cite web|title=This Summer / Selena (Remixes)|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/this-summer-selena-remixes/id541159227|work=Apple Inc.|publisher=iTunes Store Australia|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref>

=== 2013–2014: ''Atlas'' ===
{{Main|Atlas (Rüfüs album)}}
Their debut studio album, ''[[Atlas (RÜFÜS album)|Atlas]]'', was released on 9 August 2013.<ref name="Atlas">{{cite web|title=Atlas|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/atlas/id678235188|work=Apple Inc.|publisher=iTunes Store Australia|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref> It debuted at number 1 on the [[ARIA Charts|Australian Albums Chart]] on 25 August 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=RÜFÜS – Atlas|url=http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=R%DCF%DCS&titel=Atlas&cat=a|work=Hung Medien|publisher=australian-charts.com|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref> It was preceded by "[[Take Me (Rufus song)|Take Me]]" as the lead single on 8 March 2013,<ref>{{cite web|title=Take Me|url=http://www.beatport.com/release/take-me/1052183|publisher=[[Beatport]]|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref> and "[[Desert Night]]" as the second single on 2 August 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Desert Night|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/desert-night/id680280502|work=Apple Inc.|publisher=iTunes Store Australia|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref> "[[Tonight (Rufus song)|Tonight]]" was released as the third single 22 November 2013,<ref>{{cite web|title=Tonight – Single|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/tonight-single/id735891937|work=Apple Inc.|publisher=iTunes Store Australia|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref> followed by "[[Sundream (song)|Sundream]]" as the fourth single on 21 March 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sundream – EP|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/sundream-ep/id878541541|work=Apple Inc.|publisher=iTunes Store Australia|access-date=5 August 2014}}</ref>

=== 2015–2017: ''Bloom'' ===
{{Main|Bloom (Rüfüs album)}}
In June 2015, RÜFÜS released "[[You Were Right (Rüfüs song)|You Were Right]]", the lead single from their forthcoming second album. It peaked at number 22 on the Australian [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Singles Chart]] and was certified double platinum. The album ''[[Bloom (RÜFÜS album)|Bloom]]'' was released in January 2016 and become the band's second number-one album.

=== 2018–2020: ''Solace'' and ''Solace Remixed'' ===
{{Main|Solace (Rüfüs Du Sol album)|Solace Remixed}}
In May 2018, the band changed its Australian name to Rüfüs Du Sol. Band member Tyrone Lindqvist explained the name change: "I guess we don't really know any other bands that have two names throughout the entire world so it just seemed fitting."<ref name="Name Change"/> They released "[[No Place (song)|No Place]]" on 25 May 2018. They later released the singles "[[Underwater (Rüfüs Du Sol song)|Underwater]]" and "[[Lost in My Mind (Rüfüs Du Sol song)|Lost in My Mind]]", along with announcing their third album ''[[Solace (Rüfüs album)|Solace]]''.<ref name="LIMM">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8473702/rufus-du-sol-lost-in-my-mind-listen|title=RÜFÜS DU SOL Go Deep in the Desert on 'Lost in My Mind': Listen|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Ochoa|first=John|date=5 September 2018|access-date=7 September 2018}}</ref> A [[Solace Remixed|remix album]] was released in September 2019. At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2019]], ''Solace'' was nominated for three [[ARIA Music Awards]].<ref name="ARIA noms 2019">{{cite web | url = https://ariaawards.com.au/news/2019/2019-aria-awards-nominated-artists-revealed | title = ARIA Awards: 2019 ARIA Awards Nominated Artists Revealed | publisher = Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) | date = 10 October 2019 | access-date = 10 October 2019 }}</ref> 

=== 2021: ''Surrender''===
On 13 July 2021, the band released "[[Alive (Rüfüs Du Sol song)|Alive]]"; their first song in three years. In a press statement, frontman Tyrone Lindqvist said "It's a heavier song in some ways, but at its core it's hopeful."<ref name="Alive">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/rufus-du-sol-return-with-first-song-in-three-years-alive-2992069|title=RÜFÜS DU SOL return with first song in three years, "Alive"|website=[[NME Australia]]|date=13 July 2021|access-date=3 August 2021|author=Langford, Jackson}}</ref> "[[Next to Me (Rüfüs Du Sol song)|Next to Me]]" was released on 11 August 2021. Jon George said it "is a song of devotion, caring compassion and pure love."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://weraveyou.com/2021/08/rufus-du-sol-next-to-me/|title=Next to Me|website=weraveyou.com|date=12 August 2021|access-date=13 August 2021}}</ref> On 24 September 2021, they released the single "[[On My Knees (Rüfüs Du Sol song)|On My Knees]]".<ref name="OnMyKnees and Announce">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/rufus-du-sol-announce-fourth-album-surrender-3053635|title= RÜFÜS DU SOL announce fourth album, ''Surrender''|website=[[NME Australia]]|date=24 September 2021|access-date=25 September 2021}}</ref> On the same day, they also announced their fourth studio album, ''Surrender'', which was released on 21 October 2021.<ref name=onmyknees>{{cite web|url=https://edm.com/news/rufus-du-sol-new-album-surrender-release-date|title=RÜFÜS DU SOL Announce Release Date for Fourth Full-Length Album, "Surrender"|last=Scilippa|first=Phil|website=EDM.com|date=23 September 2021|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref>
