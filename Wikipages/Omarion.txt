'''Omari Ishmael Grandberry''' (born November 12, 1984), known by his stage name '''Omarion''', is an American singer, songwriter, actor, and dancer. He is best known as the lead singer of the American [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] boy band [[B2K]]. The group achieved success in the early 2000s with singles like "[[Bump, Bump, Bump]]", "[[Uh Huh (B2K song)|Uh Huh]]", and "[[Girlfriend (B2K song)|Girlfriend]]", which all reached success on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. After the group disbanded in 2004, Omarion embarked on a solo career and released his debut album, ''[[O (Omarion album)|O]]'' in 2005, which debuted at the top of the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, and received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination for Best Contemporary R&B Album at the [[48th Grammy Awards]].

His second solo album, ''[[21 (Omarion album)|21]]'' (2006), contained "[[Ice Box (song)|Ice Box]]", which reached number 12 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. His third solo album, ''[[Ollusion (album)|Ollusion]]'' (2010), was released on January 12, 2010, with the lead single, "[[I Get It In (Omarion song)|I Get It In]]".<ref name="singersroom">{{cite web|url=http://www.singersroom.com/news/4924/Omarion-Says-New-Label-Album-Beautiful-Thing|title=Omarion Says New Label, Album 'Beautiful Thing'|website=Singersroom.com|date=December 15, 2009|access-date=December 15, 2009}}</ref> His fourth solo album, ''[[Sex Playlist]]'' (2014), spawned the single, "[[Post to Be]]", which was certified 3x platinum and reached number 13 on the Hot 100 chart in May 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6568258/omarion-tops-mainstream-r-b-hip-hop|title=Omarion Returns to No. 1 on Mainstream R&B/Hip-Hop Chart|date=May 14, 2015|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> Omarion has also ventured into acting, with appearances in films such as ''[[You Got Served]]'', ''[[Fat Albert (film)|Fat Albert]]'', ''[[Somebody Help Me (film)|Somebody Help Me]]'' and ''[[The Proud Family Movie]]''.

==Early life and career==
Omarion was born Omari Ishmael Grandberry on November 12, 1984 in [[Inglewood, California]] to Leslie Burrell and Trent Grandberry. His younger brother, [[O'Ryan (singer)|O'Ryan]], is also a singer.<ref name="billboard">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=omarion|bio=true}}|title=Omarion Biography|last=Kellman|first=Andy|publisher=[[Billboard magazine|Billboard]]}}</ref> He appeared in commercials for both [[Corn Pops|Kellogg's Corn Pops]] and [[McDonald's]]. He started his dancing career as a background dancer for the R&B girl group [[Before Dark]] and appeared in their music videos. Omarion soon began pursuing a career in music, and would go on to become the lead singer of the boy band [[B2K]].

===1999–2004: B2K===
Omari joined the group [[B2K]] in 1999 and took the stage name '''Omarion'''. The group named themselves B2K, an acronym for "Boys of The New Millennium". They began touring with [[Lil Bow Wow]], and eventually released their debut single, "[[Uh Huh (B2K song)|Uh Huh]]," in 2001. They soon after released their debut album, the self-titled [[B2K (album)|B2K]] on March 12, 2002. The album peaked at No. 2 on the [[Billboard 200]] and No. 1 on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop albums]] chart. They followed the album up in December with their second album, ''[[Pandemonium! (album)|Pandemonium!]]'', supported by their most successful single, [[Bump, Bump, Bump]] featuring [[P. Diddy]]. The single peaked at No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100. This would prove to be the group's last album, as they would disband in early 2004.

===2004–09: ''O'', ''21'', and ''Face Off''===
[[File:Omarion LF.JPG|right|thumb|200px|Omarion in 2007]]
In early 2004, Omarion appeared in several films, most notably ''[[You Got Served]]'' in which he starred alongside [[Marques Houston]], as well as his former [[B2K]] bandmates.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphop-elements.com/article/read/6/7216/1/ |title=OMARION Interview |publisher=HipHop-Elements.com |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref> The movie centered on a group of friends in a [[hip hop dance]] crew who take part in underground dance competitions. That same year, he played the role of the school bully "Reggie" in the comedy ''[[Fat Albert (film)|Fat Albert]],'' based on the animated series ''[[Fat Albert and the Cosby Kids]].''<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/12/28/AR2006122800330.html |title=R& B's Omarion: On His Own and Loving It |work=washingtonpost.com |date= December 29, 2006|access-date=May 12, 2011 |first=Richard |last=Harrington}}</ref> On February 22, 2005, Omarion released his debut solo album ''[[O (Omarion album)|O]]''. The album debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 and Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums charts. The first single from the album was "[[O (Omarion song)|O]]". The single peaked at No. 27 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. The second single "[[Touch (Omarion song)|Touch]]", peaked at No. 94 on the Billboard Hot 100. The album received a third and final release with, "[[I'm Tryna]]". ''O'' has sold over 758,000 copies in the United States since its release. Omarion received a Grammy nomination for the album at the [[48th Grammy Awards]] for Best Contemporary R&B Album. On December 26, 2006 Omarion released his second album ''[[21 (Omarion album)|21]]''. The album debuted at No. 1 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] charts. The first single from the album was "[[Entourage (song)|Entourage]]". The second single "[[Ice Box (song)|Ice Box]]", produced by [[Timbaland]], peaked at No. 12 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and is Omarion's most successful solo single to date,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/omarion-p528354/charts-awards/billboard-singles |title=Omarion |publisher=AllMusic |date=November 12, 1984 |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref> reaching gold certification by the RIAA.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?resultpage=1&table=SEARCH_RESULTS&title=&artist=omarion&perPage=100 |title=Recording Industry Association of America |publisher=RIAA |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref> ''21'' has sold over 390,000 copies in the United States since its release.

Towards the end of 2007, he displayed his acting skills in the thriller ''[[Somebody Help Me (film)|Somebody Help Me]].'' This movie was released exclusively on DVD and aired on BET in celebration of [[Halloween]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bet.com/OnTV/BETShows/blackbuster/blackbustermoviesomebodyhelpme.htm|title=BlackBuster Movie: Somebody Help Me|last=Jefferson|first=Kia E.|date=October 29, 2007|website=[[Black Entertainment Television|BET]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071102054832/http://www.bet.com/OnTV/BETShows/blackbuster/blackbustermoviesomebodyhelpme.htm|archive-date=November 2, 2007|access-date=October 7, 2018}}</ref> Omarion has also made several appearances in a number of TV shows, including ''One on One''. Omarion also played the voice of Fifteen Cent in ''[[The Proud Family Movie]].''<ref>{{cite news|last=McDonough|first=Kevin|title=Famous voices animate new features|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=AmBWAAAAIBAJ&pg=6655,4042719&dq=omarion+proud-family&hl=en|access-date=May 12, 2011|newspaper=[[Eugene Register-Guard]]|date=August 19, 2005}}</ref> His portrayal of a rapper who gets involved with thugs in the movie ''[[Feel the Noise]]'' received mixed reviews from critics.<ref>{{cite news|last=Scheck |first=Frank |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/review-film-noise-dc-idUSN0625265920071006 |title=Feel the Noise appealing despite cliches |publisher=Reuters |date= October 6, 2007|access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Howell |first=Peter |url=https://www.thestar.com/Movies/MovieReview/article/264339 |title='Feel the noise': This movie barely even lets us hear it |work=thestar.com |date=October 7, 2007 |access-date=May 12, 2011 |location=Toronto}}</ref> Omarion also co-produced ''Feel the Noise''.<ref>{{cite web |last=Bentley |first=Rick |url=http://www.dispatch.com/live/content/life/stories/2007/10/10/2_FEEL_THE_NOISE.ART_ART_10-10-07_D3_498506G.html?sid=101 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130208040802/http://www.dispatch.com/live/content/life/stories/2007/10/10/2_FEEL_THE_NOISE.ART_ART_10-10-07_D3_498506G.html?sid=101 |url-status=dead |archive-date=February 8, 2013 |title=Movie Review &#124; Feel the Noise: Story discovers its rhythm after slow start &#124; The Columbus Dispatch |publisher=Dispatch.com |date=October 10, 2007 |access-date=May 12, 2011 }}</ref> In late 2007, Omarion and [[Bow Wow (rapper)|Bow Wow]] recorded a collaboration album entitled ''[[Face Off (Bow Wow & Omarion album)|Face Off]]''. The album sold over 100,000 copies in its first week and went on to sell over 500,000 copies, reaching gold certification by the RIAA.<ref>[https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?resultpage=1&table=SEARCH_RESULTS&title=&artist=omarion&perPage=100 Recording Industry Association of America]. ===2009-11: EMI and ''Ollusion''===
On March 30, 2009, [[Lil Wayne]] confirmed that Omarion was affiliated (though not officially signed) with [[Young Money Entertainment]]. Four months later he parted ways with the label; both Lil Wayne and Omarion confirmed that his departure had been for business reasons.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2009/08/20/omarion-splits-from-young-money/ |title=Omarion Splits From Young Money |publisher=Rap-Up.com |date=August 20, 2009 |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/artists/news/1619186/20090820/omarion.jhtml |title=Omarion : Omarion Parts Ways With Young Money – Rhapsody Music Downloads |publisher=VH1.com |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i294Z2AoCsE |title=Omarion explains why he didn't want to join Lil Wayne, Drake, & Bow Wow at Young Money |publisher=YouTube |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref> In early 2010, Omarion signed with [[EMI]] and created his own label, StarrWorld Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web|author=Denise |url=http://www.hiphoprx.com/2009/10/02/omarion-signs-with-emi-preps-new-album-ollusion-and-label-imprint/ |title=Omarion Signs With EMI, Preps New Album 'Ollusion' and Label Imprint |publisher=Hiphoprx.com |date=October 2, 2009 |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Rodriguez |first=Jayson |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1622301/omarions-new-lp-ollusion-due-november-24.jhtml |title=Omarion's New LP, Ollusion, Due November 24 – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV |date=September 24, 2009 |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref> Omarion later re-released the single "[[I Get It In (Omarion song)|I Get It In]]" with Lil Wayne replaced by [[Gucci Mane]]. Then he released his second single "[[Speedin' (Omarion song)|Speedin']]". He released his third studio album ''[[Ollusion (album)|Ollusion]]'', which sold 19,400 copies in its first week.<ref>{{cite web|last=Langhorne |first=Cyrus |url=http://www.sohh.com/2010/01/keha_finds_her_instinct_at_no_3_alicia_k.html |title=Ke$ha Finds Her Instinct At No. 3, Alicia Keys Roams Free In Top 5 & Omarion Lands On The Charts |publisher=Sohh.Com |date=January 20, 2010 |access-date=May 12, 2011}} :-</ref>

===2011–2017: ''Sex Playlist''===
In 2010, Omarion became a judge on the [[America's Best Dance Crew (season 5)|fifth season]] of ''[[America's Best Dance Crew]]'', replacing former judge [[Shane Sparks]]. He left after Season 5 to focus on his music career. In early 2011, Omarion worked on his first mixtape presented by [[DJ Drama]], titled ''The Awakening''. Originally to be released on April 27, 2011, the mixtape was instead released on May 11, 2011. In November 2011, Omarion was seen on set with Japanese star [[Koda Kumi]]. On December 6, 2011, it was revealed that he collaborated with the star on the song "Slow" for her 2012 album ''[[Japonesque (Kumi Koda album)|Japonesque]]'' and appeared in the music video. On May 2, 2012, Omarion signed with [[Rick Ross]] label [[Maybach Music Group]] and [[Warner Bros. Records]]. He then appeared on ''[[Self Made Vol. 2]]'' which made the Top 10 on Billboard 200. Also in 2012, Omarion signed to [[Jay-Z]]'s [[Roc Nation]] Management.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Omarion-The-Awakening-mixtape.227956.html |title=Omarion – The Awakening Hosted by DJ Drama // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |access-date=May 12, 2011}}</ref> On November 29, 2012, Omarion released his debut EP ''Care Package''. September 2013 saw the release of ''[[Self Made Vol. 3]]'' featuring Omarion which also reached the Top 10 on Billboard 200 at number 4. On November 5, 2013 Omarion released his second EP ''Care Package 2''.

On August 18, 2014, Omarion was cast to be a part of ''[[Love & Hip Hop: Hollywood]]'' along with his mother, and girlfriend Apryl Jones while awaiting the arrival of their first child. In November 2014, Omarion released the single "You Like It" in promotion of his fourth studio album "SexPlaylist" on February 14, 2014. The second single, "[[Post to Be]]," features [[Chris Brown]] and [[Jhené Aiko]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Benton-Martin|first1=Erika|title=Omarion Releases Full Version of 'Post To Be' Featuring Jhené Aiko And Chris Brown|url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/15467/20141112/omarion-releases-full-version-post-featuring-jhené-aiko-chris-brown.htm|website=Musictimes.com|date=November 12, 2014}}</ref> "Post to Be" gained substantial commercial success, reaching No. 13 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Cantor|first1=Brian|title=Omarion, Chris Brown, & Jhene Aiko's "Post to Be" Claims #1 at Urban Radio|url=http://headlineplanet.com/home/2015/05/10/omarion-chris-brown-jhene-aikos-post-to-be-claims-1-at-urban-radio/|website=Headlineplanet.com|date=May 10, 2015}}</ref> The song also peaked in the top ten on Urban Radio chart, Rhythmic chart, and the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart and reached gold status by the RIAA in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Williams|first1=Brillion|title=Omarion's 'Post to Be' is Officially Gold|url=http://musicsnake.com/omarions-post-to-be-is-officially-gold/|website=Musicsnake.com|date=May 13, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150623193630/http://musicsnake.com/omarions-post-to-be-is-officially-gold/|archive-date=June 23, 2015}}</ref> He released his fourth studio album, ''[[Sex Playlist]]'', on December 2, 2014.

In June 2015, Omarion stated in an interview that he wanted to open his own dance studio by the end of the year.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Hot97|title=Omarion Interview From Summer Jam 2015|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44hjgOm5DQQ|website=[[YouTube]]|date=June 11, 2015}}</ref> On November 12, 2015, he released his third EP ''Care Package 3''. November 2017 was met with the release of the fourth EP ''Care Package 4''.

===2017-present: ''The Millennium Tour'' and ''The Kinection''===
Omarion released the song "[[Distance (Omarion song)|Distance]]" in 2017 for which he filmed a music video in [[South Africa]].

In 2018, he was featured on the track "Tell Me" from [[Anatii]]'s album ''[[Artiifact]]''. That same year, he also appeared in the "Get It Now" remix music video by [[Tiwa Savage]] as a featured artist.

In 2019, B2K briefly reformed to go on [[The Millennium Tour]]; joined by supporting acts Mario, Ying Yang Twins, Chingy, Pretty Ricky, Bobby V and Lloyd. The tour kicked off on March 8 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania and wrapped up on April 28 in Louisville, Kentucky. It was later announced that Omarion would be continuing The Millennium Tour without his bandmates and with [[Bow Wow (rapper)|Bow Wow]] and other acts added to the lineup for 2020 dates.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Ashanti Joins Millennium Tour|url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/columns/hip-hop/8545506/ashanti-millennium-tour-2020-omarion-bow-wow|magazine=Billboard|date=December 7, 2019}}</ref> The group was nominated for Top R&B Tour at the [[2020 Billboard Music Awards]].

In 2020, Omarion released the single, "Can You Hear Me?" featuring a guest verse from [[T-Pain]]. The single is the lead song from the upcoming Omarion ''The Kinection'' album. On October 29, ''Omarion presents Thriller Night'' premiered on [[YouTube]] in celebration of the album release. The event included Prince Jackson live from [[Michael Jackson]]'s [[Hayvenhurst]] Estate.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/omarion/videos/656825231644000/ |title=OMARION presents THRILLER NIGHT. The Kinection Album Release |date= |website=facebook}}</ref> He released his fifth album on October 30, 2020.

In 2021, Omarion competed in [[The Masked Singer (American season 5)|season five]] of ''[[The Masked Singer (American TV series)|The Masked Singer]]'' as the wild card contestant "Yeti". Certain performances has the Yeti's feet swapped out with different footwear. He finished in fourth place.

On May 31, 2021, "We Will Never Forget" with [[Lalah Hathaway]],  [[Kierra Sheard]] and Omarion was released being featured in the documentary film, ''[[Dreamland: The Burning of Black Wall Street]]'' executive produced by [[LeBron James]], with all proceeds going to [[Color of Change]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Omarion Recruits Lalah Hathaway and Kierra Sheard for 'We Will Never Forget'|url=https://ratedrnb.com/2021/05/omarion-recruits-lalah-hathaway-and-kierra-sheard-for-we-will-never-forget/|website=ratedrnb|date=May 11, 2021}}</ref>

As of 2021, Omarion is set to headline [[The Millennium Tour]] in the fall of 2021 with plans to concurrently release a single with [[Soulja Boy]] and [[Bow Wow (rapper)|Bow Wow]] entitled "EX" from his forthcoming album ''Full Circle'', which he announced through [[Songtrust]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://m.facebook.com/songtrust/photos/pcb.4745185142158305/4745184988824987/?type=3&source=48&refid=52&__tn__=EH-R|title = Songtrust|website = [[Facebook]]}}</ref>
