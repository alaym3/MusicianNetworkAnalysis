'''Raymond Howard''' "'''Ray'''" '''Boltz''' (born June 14, 1953) is a singer and songwriter who first came to wide notice in [[contemporary Christian music]]. Many of his songs tell stories of faith and inspiration.

== Background ==

Raised by his parents William and Ruth Boltz in Muncie, Indiana, Ray Boltz is the [[Birth order|middle child]] of his parents' three children (a fourth child died shortly after birth).<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Jones|first=Kim|last2=|first2=|last3=|first3=|last4=|first4=|date=|title=The Ray Boltz Story|url=https://www.learnreligions.com/ray-boltz-biography-708465|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-12-26|website=Learn Religions|language=en}}</ref> Boltz was married to Carol (nee Brammer)<ref name=":0" /> Boltz<ref name="NYT">{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2010/05/15/us/15religion.html | work=The New York Times | title=Christian Singer Resumes Career, Relieved of a Secret | first=Samuel G. | last=Freedman | date=2010-05-14}}</ref> for 33 years (1975-2008), and they have four children: Karen, Philip, Elizabeth and Sara.<ref name=":0" /> Boltz graduated from Ball State University with a degree in business and marketing.<ref name=":0" />

Boltz was virtually unknown when he wrote "Thank You", which won the [[Dove Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] prize at the 1990 [[GMA Dove Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=15996 |title=Thank You Songfacts |publisher=Songfacts.com |date= |accessdate=2013-12-05}}</ref> His song "I Pledge Allegiance to the Lamb" also won a Dove Award for Inspirational Recorded Song of the Year at the [[25th GMA Dove Awards]] in 1994. After the release of ''Songs from the Potter's Field'' in 2002, and his last tour in 2004, Boltz retired from the music industry. He separated from his wife in 2005 before moving to [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida]], with their [[divorce]] being finalized in early 2008.<ref name=":0" />

On September 12, 2008, during an interview with the ''[[Washington Blade]]'', Boltz [[coming out|disclosed]] that he was [[gay]].<ref name=Interview>{{cite news|url=http://www.washingtonblade.com/print.cfm?content_id=13258|title=Key changes|last=DiGuglielmo|first=Joey|date=12 September 2008|work=Washington Blade|accessdate=2009-02-19}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> Since then, Boltz has performed at several churches of the [[Metropolitan Community Church]], a [[LGBT-welcoming church programs|gay-affirming]] Christian denomination.<ref>{{cite news|last=Lopez |first=Rich |date=2009-09-10 |url=http://www.dallasvoice.com/artman/publish/article_11808.php |work=Dallas Voice |title=Ray of Light |accessdate=2009-01-01 }}{{dead link|date=December 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

In 2010, he released the album ''True'', which won Album of the Year at the OUTMusic Awards.<ref name="NYT" /> Boltz currently lives in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, with his husband, who is also his [[talent agent]].

==Awards==
Boltz has been the recipient of two song awards from the Gospel Music Association's [[GMA Dove Award|Dove Awards]]. "Thank You" received the 1990 [[Dove Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] award, and "I Pledge Allegiance to the Lamb" received the 1994 award for Inspirational Song. He also was one of various artists contributing to ''God with Us: A Celebration of Christmas Carols & Classics'', which won the 1997 award for Special Event Album.
