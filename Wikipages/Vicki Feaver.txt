'''Vicki Feaver''' (born 1943) is an [[English people|English]] poet. She has published three poetry collections.  Feaver's poem "Judith", from her book, ''Handless Maiden'', was awarded the [[Forward Prizes for Poetry|Forward Prize]] for Best Single Poem. The book was also the recipient of a Heinemann Prize and shortlisted for the [[Forward Prizes for Poetry|Forward Prize]].  Feaver was also a recipient of a [[Cholmondeley Award]].<ref name="Poetry Foundation">{{cite web |title=Vicki Feaver b. 1943 |url=https://www.poetryarchive.org/poet/vicki-feaver |website=The Poetry Archive |access-date=22 March 2019}}</ref>

==Biography==

Feaver was born in 1943 in [[Nottingham]], [[England]]. She studied music at [[Durham University]] and English at [[University College, London]], and later worked as a lecturer and tutor in English and Creative Writing at [[University of Chichester|University College, Chichester]], where she is an emeritus professor.<ref name="Poetry Foundation" />

On 14 January 2014, Feaver participated in the BBC Radio 3 series "The Essay - Letters to a Young Poet". Taking [[Rainer Maria Rilke]]'s classic text ''[[Letters to a Young Poet]]'' as inspiration, leading poets wrote a letter to a protege.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b03pdg58 | work=The Essay | publisher=BBC | title=Vicki Feaver - Letters to a Young Poet | date=20 August 2014 | access-date=31 January 2019 }}</ref>

Feaver lives in [[South Lanarkshire]], [[Scotland]].<ref name="Poetry Foundation" />

==Collections of poetry==
* 1978: ''Monograph'' (Pamela Robertson-Pearce)
* 1981: ''Close Relatives'' (Secker & Warburg)
* 1986: ''Clean Sheets'' (Cathcart Press)
* 1991: ''Penguin Modern Poets Vol 2'' (with Carol Ann Duffy and Eavan Boland)
* 1992: ''Crab Apple Jelly'' (Somers Press)
* 1994: ''The Handless Maiden'' (Jonathan Cape, shortlisted for [[Forward Poetry Prize|Forward Prize]])
* 2003: ''Girl in Red and Other Poems'' (Scottish Book Trust)
* 2006: ''The Book of Blood'' (Jonathan Cape, shortlisted for [[Forward Poetry Prize|Forward Prize]] and [[2006 Costa Book Awards|Costa Award]])
* 2015: ''Second Wind'' (Saltire Society, with Douglas Dunn and Diana Henry)
* 2019: ''I Want! I Want!'' (Jonathan Cape, forthcoming autumn 2019)

== Other works ==
* 2005: ''Machinery of Grace: A Tribute to Michael Donaghy (1954-2004)'' (contributor)
* 2011: ''This Moment Will Never Come Again: A Selection of Daily Photographs from the Attic Salt Exhibition'' (co-author)

==Prizes==
* 1992: Arvon Competition Prize for the poem "Lily Pond"
* 1993: [[Forward Poetry Prize]], Best Single Poem, for the poem 'Judith'
* 1994: W. H. Heinemann Award for ''The Handless Maiden''
* 1998: [[National Poetry Competition]] for the poem 'Bats'
* 1999: [[Cholmondeley Award]]
