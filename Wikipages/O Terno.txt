'''O Terno''' ({{IPA-pt|u ˈteɾ.nu}}, "The Suit") is a Brazilian rock band from [[São Paulo]], consisting of singer-songwriter Tim Bernardes, bassist Guilherme D'Almeida and drummer Gabriel Basile. Formed in 2009, the trio began performing covers of songs by [[Os Mutantes]], [[The Beatles]] and [[The Kinks]] before releasing original music.<ref>{{Cite news |date=18 July 2017 |title=Em novo clipe, O Terno brinca com humor com referências da internet |language=pt-BR |work=[[Correio Braziliense]] |url=https://www.correiobraziliense.com.br/app/noticia/diversao-e-arte/2017/07/18/interna_diversao_arte,610623/novo-clipe-do-terno.shtml |access-date=8 July 2021}}</ref> The band is considered a fixture of the contemporary Brazilian [[independent music]] scene, being one of the founders of the independent music label {{ill|Selo RISCO|pt}}.

== History ==
O Terno is a rock band from São Paulo, formed by Tim Bernardes (vocals and guitar), Guilherme D'Almeida (bass) and Victor Chaves (drums). In June 2012, they independently released their first album, ''66''. Since its release, the album has been highly rated, being considered by the newspaper ''O Globo'' as "one of the most impressive debut albums of a Brazilian band" and ranked among the 25 best Brazilian albums of 2012 by the magazine ''[[Rolling Stone|Rolling Stone Brasil]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Dados artísticos |url=http://www.dicionariompb.com.br/o-terno/dados-artisticos |access-date=19 May 2015 |publisher=[[Dicionário Cravo Albin da Música Popular Brasileira]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Listas - Os melhores discos nacionais de 2012 - O Terno |url=http://rollingstone.uol.com.br/listas/os-melhores-discos-de-2012/o-terno/ |access-date=19 May 2015 |publisher=[[Rolling Stone|Rolling Stone Brasil]]}}</ref> The album contains five original songs and five songs written by Bernardes's father, [[Maurício Pereira]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=O Terno - Toque no Brasil |url=http://tnb.art.br/rede/bandaoterno/ |access-date=21 August 2014}}</ref> with arrangements by the trio.

The title track, ''66'', produced the band's first video, which was awarded in the category "Clipe do Ano" of the 2012 [[Multishow Brazilian Music Award]]. The band also won the Aposta MTV award at the [[2012 MTV Video Music Brazil]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Veja os vencedores do Prêmio Multishow 2012 |url=http://multishow.globo.com/especiais/premio-multishow-2012/materias/veja-os-vencedores-do-premio-multishow-2012.htm |access-date=2 May 2015}}</ref>

Also in 2012, O Terno performed as part of [[TV Cultura]]'s year-end special, on the programme ''Cultura Livre''. At the 2012 Multishow Brazilian Music Award, they performed with [[Nando Reis]] and [[Arnaldo Antunes]]. They also participated in the &nbsp;<em href="Arnaldo Antunes">Som Brasil Tropicália</em>&nbsp;series of [[TV Globo|Rede Globo]].

In 2013, the trio recorded two songs for the EP of&nbsp;[[Tom Zé]],&nbsp;<em href="Tom Zé">''Tribunal do Feicebuqui''</em>. The band also competed at the 2013 Multishow Brazilian Music Award in the category ''Melhor Canção'' with <em href="Prêmio Multishow de Música Brasileira 2013">Harmonium,</em>&nbsp;their EP ''TicTac-Harmonium''. The EP featured three songs. A music video was produced for the track "Tic-Tac".<ref>{{Cite web |last=Pedro Antunes |date=21 May 2014 |title=Exclusivo: O Terno corre contra o tempo no clipe de "Tic Tac", com participação de Paulo Miklos |url=http://rollingstone.uol.com.br/blog/sobe-o-som/exclusivo-o-terno-corre-contra-o-tempo-no-novo-clipe-de-tic-tac-com-participacao-de-paulo-miklos |access-date=3 May 2015 |publisher=[[Rolling Stone|Rolling Stone Brasil]]}}</ref>

In 2014, they formed the collective music label selo RISCO, along with seven other bands, such as Charlie e os Marretas and {{ill|Luiza Lian|pt}}. In August of the same year, through [[crowdfunding]] website {{ill|Projeto Catarse|pt|Catarse (site)}}, the band released their self-titled second studio album, featuring twelve songs written by the band members themselves.<ref>{{Cite web |last=André Felipe de Medeiros |date=20 August 2014 |title=O Terno - O Terno - Monkeybuzz |url=http://monkeybuzz.com.br/resenhas/albuns/11934/o-terno---o-terno// |access-date=21 August 2014 |publisher=Monkeybuzz}}</ref> O disco foi gravado no estúdio Canoa e lançado de forma independente.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Grupo O Terno vai além do 'power trio' em novo disco |url=http://www1.folha.uol.com.br/ilustrada/2014/08/1503860-grupo-o-terno-vai-alem-do-power-trio-em-novo-disco.shtml |access-date=19 May 2015 |publisher=[[Folha de S. Paulo]]}}</ref>

In March 2015, the band changed its lineup: Gabriel Basile replaced Victor Chaves on drums. At the end of the same month, the band made their debut at the festival [[Lollapalooza]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=André Felipe de Medeiros |date=17 March 2015 |title=O Terno anuncia nova formação. |url=http://monkeybuzz.com.br/noticias/14524/o-terno-anuncia-nova-formacao/ |access-date=17 March 2015 |publisher=Monkeybuzz}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=O Terno agrada ao público com espontaneidade |url=http://cultura.estadao.com.br/noticias/musica,o-terno-agrada-ao-publico-com-espontaneidade,1660210 |access-date=19 May 2015 |publisher=[[O Estado de S. Paulo]]}}</ref>

In 2016, they participated in the production of the first selo RISCO compilation album, recording a cover of the song "Ávida Dúvida", by the band Memórias de um Caramujo. In addition, they released the video for ''Ai, Ai, Como Eu Me Iludo'', produced by Alasca Filmes, the band's third. Between the end of May and the beginning of June 2016, O Terno made its first international tour, passing through Portugal and Spain ([[Primavera Sound]]), in festivals and solo performances spread across local cities. In September 2016, they released their third studio album, ''Melhor Do Que Parece'', a fusion of [[Tropicália]], rock, soul and [[música popular brasileira]]. The album was ranked first by ''[[O Estado de S. Paulo]]'' on its list of the 12 best Brazilian albums of 2016.<ref>{{Cite news |date=21 December 2016 |title=Os 12 melhores discos nacionais de 2016 |language=pt-BR |work=[[O Estado de S. Paulo]] |url=https://cultura.estadao.com.br/galerias/musica,os-12-melhores-discos-nacionais-de-2016,29327 |access-date=8 July 2021}}</ref>

In 2019, the trio released ''<atrás/além>'', their fourth studio album, on 23 April.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rolling Stone · Com disco < Atrás / Além >, O Terno reflete sobre o vazio de não ser jovem nem velho demais |url=https://rollingstone.uol.com.br/noticia/com-disco-atras-alem-o-terno-reflete-sobre-o-vazio-de-nao-ser-jovem-ou-velho-demais/ |access-date=31 August 2020 |website=[[Rolling Stone|Rolling Stone Brasil]] |language=pt-BR}}</ref> The album featured international guest appearances by [[Devendra Banhart]] and [[Shintaro Sakamoto]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rolling Stone · O Terno revela primeiros detalhes do novo disco e duas participações internacionais |url=https://rollingstone.uol.com.br/noticia/o-terno-revela-primeiros-detalhes-do-novo-disco-e-duas-participacoes-internacionais/ |access-date=28 March 2019 |website=[[Rolling Stone|Rolling Stone Brasil]] |language=pt-BR}}</ref> and was selected as one of the 25 best Brazilian albums of the first half of 2019 by the [[Associação Paulista de Críticos de Arte]].<ref name="apcamelhores19">{{Cite web |last=Antunes |first=Pedro |date=16 August 2019 |title=Os 25 melhores discos de 2019 até agora, segundo a APCA [LISTA] |url=https://rollingstone.uol.com.br/noticia/os-25-melhores-discos-de-2019-ate-agora-segundo-apca-lista/ |access-date=2 January 2021 |website=[[Rolling Stone|Rolling Stone Brasil]] |publisher=Grupo Perfil}}</ref> Mauro Ferreira of [[G1 (website)|G1]] ranked the album 7th on his list of the 17 best Brazilian albums of 2019.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Ferreira |first=Mauro |date=31 December 2019 |title=RETROSPECTIVA 2019 – Os álbuns brasileiros que sobressaíram ao longo do ano |language=pt-BR |work=[[G1 (website)|G1]] |publisher=[[Grupo Globo]] |url=https://g1.globo.com/pop-arte/musica/blog/mauro-ferreira/post/2019/12/31/retrospectiva-2019-os-albuns-brasileiros-que-sobressairam-ao-longo-do-ano.ghtml |access-date=8 July 2021}}</ref>
