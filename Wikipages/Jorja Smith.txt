'''Jorja Alice Smith''' (born 11 June 1997) is an English singer. Born and raised in [[Walsall]], [[West Midlands (county)|West Midlands]], she has been writing songs since the age of 11. In 2012, Smith's friend uploaded her cover to [[YouTube]], which led to her discovery by record producer Guy Moot. After her first two singles received broader recognition, she signed with [[Sony/ATV]] in 2016, releasing two [[Extended play|EPs]] throughout 2016 and 2017.

Her debut studio album, ''[[Lost & Found (Jorja Smith album)|Lost & Found]]'', was released in 2018 to critical acclaim, and peaked at number three on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. The same year, Smith won the [[Critics' Choice (Brit Award)|Brit Critics' Choice Award]]. In 2019, she was named [[Brit Award for British Female Solo Artist|Best British Female Artist]] at the [[2019 Brit Awards|Brit Awards]] and was also nominated for the [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist]]. Her third EP ''[[Be Right Back (EP)|Be Right Back]]'', which was released in May 2021 received favorable reviews.

On 14 May 2021, she released her third extended play ''[[Be Right Back (EP)|Be Right Back]]'', which received generally positive reviews.

== Influences ==
Smith grew up listening to reggae, punk, hip-hop, and R&B, and wrote her first song at the age of 11.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/london/2018/09/centre-stage-jorja-smith-lost-and-found-stormzy-music|title=Centre Stage: Jorja Smith|last1=Thompson|first1=Isobel|date=5 September 2018|website=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]|access-date=4 October 2018|archive-date=12 August 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200812184941/https://www.vanityfair.com/london/2018/09/centre-stage-jorja-smith-lost-and-found-stormzy-music|url-status=live}}</ref> She describes being "obsessed" with [[Amy Winehouse]]'s 2003 debut album ''[[Frank (Amy Winehouse album)|Frank]]'' as a teenager and was inspired by the singer's raw approach to songwriting.<ref name="Hyman">{{cite magazine |last1=Hyman |first1=Dan |title=Meet Jorja Smith, the Mercury Prize-Shortlisted Favorite of Drake and Kendrick |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/meet-jorja-smith-the-mercury-prize-nominated-favorite-of-drake-and-kendrick-703496/ |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] |access-date=4 October 2018 |date=1 August 2018 |archive-date=4 October 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181004185710/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/meet-jorja-smith-the-mercury-prize-nominated-favorite-of-drake-and-kendrick-703496/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Smith said her songs are about social issues: "When things are going on in the world, I think it's important to touch on them, because as a musician, you can make people listen. As soon as people press play, you've got their attention."<ref>{{cite web |author=Celebretainment |title=Jorja Smith gets 'best advice' from Stormzy |url=https://www.celebretainment.com/music/jorja-smith-gets-best-advice-from-stormzy/article_1e80f630-4ea0-5116-be6c-6f00370b2829.html |website=Celebretainment |access-date=4 October 2018 |date=22 September 2018 |archive-date=23 September 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180923161522/https://www.celebretainment.com/music/jorja-smith-gets-best-advice-from-stormzy/article_1e80f630-4ea0-5116-be6c-6f00370b2829.html |url-status=live }}</ref>
She cites [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Adele]], [[Amy Winehouse]], [[Sade (singer)|Sade]], [[Nina Simone]], [[Alicia Keys]], [[Mos Def]] and [[the Streets]] as influences.<ref name="Bains">{{cite web|first=Sanjeeta|last=Bains|url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/who-is-jorja-smith-drake-12659644|title=Who is Jorja Smith? All you need to know about the Brit Critics Choice Award winner from Walsall|work=[[Birmingham Mail]]|date=16 February 2018|access-date=22 July 2018|archive-date=22 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180722100112/https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/who-is-jorja-smith-drake-12659644|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.playboy.com/read/fall-in-love-with-jorja-smith|title=Fall in Love With Jorja Smith, Music's Rising Seductress|work=[[Playboy]]|first=Ariela|last=Kozin|date=1 June 2018|access-date=10 June 2018|archive-date=12 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612142818/https://www.playboy.com/read/fall-in-love-with-jorja-smith|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Kay|last=Montano|url=http://thandiekay.com/2016/07/08/jorja-smith/|title=Q+A with Singer Jorja Smith|work=ThandieKay|date=8 July 2016|access-date=10 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180611055443/http://thandiekay.com/2016/07/08/jorja-smith/|archive-date=11 June 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> Style wise, Smith cites [[Rihanna]] as the sole fashion icon she is inspired by.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elle.com/uk/life-and-culture/a27307331/jorja-smith-interview/| title=Jorja Rising | date=30 April 2019 }}</ref>
