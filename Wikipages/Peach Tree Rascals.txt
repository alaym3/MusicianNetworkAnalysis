'''Peach Tree Rascals'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/whats-band-called-mate-get-know-peach-tree-rascals-2554078|title=What’s Your Band Called, Mate? Get to know Peach Tree Rascals|date=October 6, 2019|author=Smith, Thomas|website=[[NME]]}}</ref> are a music collective from [[San Jose, California]]. The group achieved fame with their song, "[[Mariposa (song)|Mariposa]]," which went viral on [[TikTok]]. The group has been featured on [[Global Release Day|New Music Friday]] and has received critical acclaim from [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]], [[Pigeons and Planes]], and [[MTV]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.10kprojects.com/artists/peach-tree-rascals|title=PEACH TREE RASCALS|website=10K Projects}}</ref>

The group is composed of singer-rappers Isaac Pech, Tarrek Abdel-Khaliq and Joseph Barros as well as producer and mixer Dominic “Dom” Pizano and creative director Jorge Olazaba.<ref name = eighteen>{{cite web|url=https://1883magazine.com/peach-tree-rascals/|title=Peach Tree Rascals|author=Olivo, Lauren Ablondi|website=[[1843 (magazine)|1883]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/peach-tree-rascals/16596|title=Interview: 2020 Must-Know – Peach Tree Rascals|date=April 30, 2020|author=Riddell, Rose|website=Coup de Main}}</ref><ref name = tsis>{{cite web|url=https://thissongissick.com/post/peach-tree-rascals-interview/|title=INTERVIEW – Peach Tree Rascals Verge On Stardom With New Single “Things Won’t Go My Way”|date=March 27, 2020|author=Thomas, Langston|website=Thissongissick}}</ref><ref name = affinity>{{cite web|url=http://culture.affinitymagazine.us/an-interview-with-peach-tree-rascals-sunshine-songwriting/|title=An Interview with Peach Tree Rascals – Sunshine & Songwriting|date=December 1, 2019|author=Ehrlich, Helen|website=Affinity}}</ref>

== History ==
The group formed as a group of friends in high school, with many of them being second-generation children of immigrants.<ref name = affinity/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tmrwmagazine.com/features/music/peach-tree-rascals-on-the-rise|title=PEACH TREE RASCALS: ON THE RISE|date=May 25, 2020|author=Sharp, Toni|website=TMRW}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://b-sides.tv/interviews/20200527-video-interview-peach-tree-rascals-friendship-led-to-blossoming-music-career|title=VIDEO: INTERVIEW – Peach Tree Rascals Friendship Led To Blossoming Music Career|date=May 25, 2020|website=b-sides.tv}}</ref><ref name = clash>{{cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/next-wave/next-wave-949-peach-tree-rascals|title=Next Wave #949: Peach Tree Rascals|date=March 23, 2020|author=Ahmed, Narzra|website=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]}}</ref> Pech moved to Khaliq's and Pizano's high school and became a part of their friend group, creating CD's and inspiring the group to create music.<ref name = eighteen/><ref name = euphoria>{{cite web|url=https://www.euphoriazine.com/blog/2019/09/interviews-peach-tree-rascals-interview/|title=Peach Tree Rascals|date=September 11, 2019|author=Myers, Alana|website=Euphoria}}</ref> A week before their release of "Glide," the group were camping trying to figure out a name, to which Olabaza suggested "Peach Tree Village." Pech suggested that "village" be replaced with "rascals."<ref name = bruin>{{cite web|url=https://dailybruin.com/2020/08/17/qa-peach-tree-rascals-look-back-on-rise-to-popularity-through-tiktok-high-school-roots|title=Q&A: Peach Tree Rascals look back on rise to popularity through TikTok, high school roots|author=Xu, Vivian|date=August 17, 2020|website=[[Daily Bruin]]}}</ref> The group began releasing music in 2018 with the debut single "Glide."<ref name = bmi>{{cite web|url=https://www.bmi.com/news/entry/indie-spotlight-peach-tree-rascals|title=Indie Spotlight: Peach Tree Rascals|date=March 20, 2020|website=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elicitmagazine.com/peach-tree-rascals-mariposa/|title=PEACH TREE RASCALS SHARE SINGLE, “MARIPOSA”|website=Elicit Magazine}}</ref> 

The group returned in 2019 with the single "Plus,"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/peach-tree-rascals-return-with-plus|title=Peach Tree Rascals return with “Plus”|date=February 12, 2019|website=Elevator Magazine}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thebaybridged.com/2019/02/08/peach-tree-rascals-plus-is-a-taste-of-sweet-san-jose-soul/|title=Peach Tree Rascals’ “Plus” is a taste of sweet San Jose soul|date=February 8, 2019|author=Tabios, Nina|website=The Bay Bridged}}</ref> moving in together.<ref name = office>{{cite web|url=http://officemagazine.net/trying-catch-peach-tree-rascals|title=Trying to Catch Up with Peach Tree Rascals|author=Niami, Saam|website=Office}}</ref> On August 27, 2019, the group released the song "[[Mariposa (song)|Mariposa]]," which went viral on [[TikTok]], being used for more than 1.6 million videos.<ref name = bruin/><ref name = office/> 

In 2020, they were signed to 10K Projects and released the single "[[Deer (song)|Deer]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://happymag.tv/introducing-peach-tree-rascals-the-la-crew-youll-wish-your-label-signed/|title=Introducing Peach Tree Rascals, the LA crew you’ll wish your label signed|date=April 1, 2020|website=Happy}}</ref> In August 2020, the group moved to [[Los Angeles]] and released an acoustic version of "Mariposa."<ref name = office/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/07/25/digital-fort-kick-back-with-peach-tree-rascals|title=Digital FORT: Kick back with Peach Tree Rascals|date=July 25, 2020|website=[[The Fader]]}}</ref>

== Influences ==
The group has been described as mixing alternative jazz, [[funk]], and [[hip hop]] with inspiration from [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Frank Ocean]], [[the Beatles]], and [[John Mayer]].<ref name = clash/><ref name = bmi/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://alt1053.radio.com/blogs/dallas/peach-tree-rascals-chat-influences-and-more|title=San Jose's Peach Tree Rascals Talk About Life In San Jose, Their Influences & Favorite Bay Area Venues|date=August 30, 2020|website=[[KITS]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2020/03/27/get-to-know-peach-tree-rascals|title=GET TO KNOW… PEACH TREE RASCALS|date=March 27, 2020|website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]}}</ref>
