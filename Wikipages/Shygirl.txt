'''Blane Muise''',<ref name="face">{{cite web |last1=Thomas |first1=Katie |title=Shygirl’s living her best life |url=https://theface.com/music/dr-martens-shygirl-blane-muise-cruel-practise |website=The Face |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> better known by her stage name '''Shygirl''', is an English rapper, DJ, singer, songwriter and co-head/founder of record label and collective NUXXE. Shygirl's music incorporates elements of [[hip house]], [[Industrial hip hop|industrial hip-hop]], [[Grime (music genre)|grime]] and [[deconstructed club]]. She has also been associated with the [[hyperpop]] music scene. Shygirl rose to prominence after working with close collaborator and friend [[Sega Bodega]], as well as other well-known [[experimental pop]] producers [[Arca (musician)|Arca]] and [[Sophie (musician)|SOPHIE]], gaining attention from the likes of [[Rihanna]], using various NUXXE tracks for her [[Fenty Beauty]] commercials and fashion shows. Shygirl has released various singles since 2016, and two EPs titled ''Cruel Practice'' and ''[[Alias (EP)|Alias]]''.

==Early life and career==
Shygirl was born in South London and grew up around [[Blackheath, London|Blackheath]].<ref name="coeval">{{cite web |last1=Kleih |first1=Helene |title=SHYGIRL |url=https://www.coeval-magazine.com/coeval/shygirl |website=Coeval Magazine |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Madden|first=Emma|date=2020-01-30|title=Shygirl // Interview {{!}} BERLIN IN STEREO|url=https://berlininstereo.com/shygirl-interview/|access-date=2021-02-04}}</ref> She describes herself as "delving into fantasy and reading constantly" growing up.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Shygirl can do anything|url=https://mixmag.net/feature/shygirl-alias-ep-cover-interview|access-date=2021-06-14|website=Mixmag}}</ref> She was her secondary school's head girl.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Madden|first=Emma|date=2020-01-30|title=Shygirl // Interview {{!}} BERLIN IN STEREO|url=https://berlininstereo.com/shygirl-interview/|access-date=2021-06-14}}</ref> She went on to study practical photography at the [[University of Bristol]], travelling back to London on the weekends for parties.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2105959/artist-to-watch-shygirl/interviews/band-to-watch/|title=Artist To Watch: Shygirl|last=Farrell|first=Michael|work=[[Stereogum]]|date=11 November 2020|access-date=31 March 2021}}</ref>

Shygirl released her first single "Want More", produced by [[Sega Bodega]] in 2016.<ref name="RA">{{cite web |last1=Mickles |first1=Kiana |title=RA Reviews: Shygirl |url=https://www.residentadvisor.net/reviews/25340 |website=Resident Advisor |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> This was the first song released on the NUXXE label, founded by Shygirl, Sega Bodega, and [[Coucou Chloe]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Belut |first1=Selim |title=Nuxxe: future forever |url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/40130/1/nuxxe-shygirl-new-ep |website=Dazed}}</ref> She went on to work with Sega Bodega on her singles "Msry" and "Nvr" in 2017, having featured on his song "CC" earlier in the year.<ref name="mixmag">{{cite web |last1=Kent-Smith |first1=Jasmine |title=ESSENTIAL: SHYGIRL’S DYNAMITE NEW SINGLE, ‘MSRYNVR’ |url=https://mixmag.net/read/essential-shygirls-dynamite-dual-single-msrynvr-essential-listening |website=Mixmag |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> In 2018, Shygirl quit her day job at a modelling agency to pursue her music career.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dazed|date=2020-11-17|title=Shygirl is the shapeshifting rapper for head-splitting times|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/51076/1/shygirl-interview-autumnwinter-2020-issue-alias|access-date=2021-06-14|website=Dazed}}</ref>

In May 2018, Shygirl released her debut EP, ''Cruel Practice'', on NUXXE. The EP received positive reviews from ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|title=Shygirl : Cruel Practice|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/shygirl-cruel-practice/|access-date=2020-11-19|website=Pitchfork}}</ref> ''[[Crack Magazine]]''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Shygirl's cybernetic tracks and downplayed style is constructed for the club|url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/aesthetic/aesthetic-shygirl/|access-date=2020-11-19|website=Crack Magazine}}</ref> and ''[[Tiny Mix Tapes]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Music Review: Shygirl - Cruel Practice|url=https://www.tinymixtapes.com/music-review/shygirl-cruel-practice|access-date=2020-11-19|website=Tiny Mix Tapes}}</ref>

She featured on [[Arca (musician)|Arca]]'s song "Watch" on her 2020 album, [[Kick I|''KiCk i'']].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bell |first1=Kaelen |title=Arca's Stunning 'KiCk i' Is a Fully Realized Vision of the Future |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/arca_kick_i_album_review |website=Exclaim |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> The pair had previously collaborated on the song "Unconditional", with all of the proceeds from that single going towards [[Black Lives Matter]] and [[Inquest (charity)|Inquest UK]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kreps |first1=Daniel |title=Shygirl Recruits Arca for ‘Unconditional,’ Donating Proceeds to Black Lives Matter |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/shygirl-arca-unconditional-1010615/ |website=Rolling Stone |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> In November 2020, she released her second EP, ''Alias'' on the [[Because Music]] label. This EP received positive attention from ''Pitchfork'',<ref name="pitchforkalias">{{cite web |last1=Bardhan |first1=Ashley |title=Shygirl - Alias EP |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/shygirl-alias-ep/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]''<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hess |first1=Liam |title=The Many Faces of Shygirl, London’s Most Stylish Breakout Rapper |url=https://www.vogue.com/article/shygirl-alias-ep-profile |website=Vogue |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> and ''[[NME]]''.<ref name="nme">{{cite web |last1=Jolley |first1=Ben |title=Shygirl – ‘Alias’ review: futuristic club-rap bangers from underground queen’s fantasy universe |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/shygirl-alias-review-radar-2824268 |website=NME |access-date=9 December 2020}}</ref> ''Alias'' was listed as one of the best EPs of 2020 by audio and music publication ''MusicNGear''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Goodbye 2020: A lookback at some of our favourite albums|url=https://www.musicngear.com/blog/goodbye-2020-lookback-some-our-favourite-albums|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=MusicnGear}}</ref>

In June 2021, Shygirl released a performance film titled "Blu". It featured performances of songs from her ''Alias'' EP, as well as a new song, titled "BDE" featuring British rapper [[slowthai]]. She released the song as a single the next day.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Brady|first=Keegan|date=2021-06-29|title=The U.K. Rapper Shygirl Makes Futuristic Club Anthems for Whatever Comes Next|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/shygirl-new-video-interview-1190649/|access-date=2021-09-08|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Shygirl on exploring her many aliases and getting filthy with Slowthai on "BDE"|url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/07/09/shygirl-interview-alias-bde-slowthai-podcast|access-date=2021-09-08|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref> She would later release the ''BDE 3XL'' remix EP in September. Shygirl and British producer [[Mura Masa]] were additionally featured on a remix of [[Lady Gaga]] and [[Blackpink|BLACKPINK]]'s "[[Sour Candy (Lady Gaga and Blackpink song)|Sour Candy]]" on Lady Gaga's ''[[Dawn of Chromatica]]'' remix album.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Curto|first=Justin|date=2021-09-03|title=Get in Girls, We’re Going Back to Chromatica|url=https://www.vulture.com/2021/09/lady-gaga-dawn-of-chromatica-remix-album.html|access-date=2021-09-08|website=Vulture|language=en-us}}</ref>

== Influences ==
Shygirl has listed [[Mariah Carey]], [[Aphex Twin]], [[Madonna]], [[Björk]], and [[Róisín Murphy]] as her biggest musical influences.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Get To Know: Shygirl {{!}} MTV UK|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/mtv-push/news/get-to-know-shygirl|access-date=2021-06-14|website=www.mtv.co.uk|language=en}}</ref>
