'''The 88''' is an American pop rock band from [[Los Angeles]], California. The group consists of [[Keith Slettedahl]] (vocals and guitar), [[Adam Merrin]] (keyboards and vocals), Anthony Zimmitti (drums and percussion), and [[Todd O'Keefe]] (bass and vocals). Meeting in high school in [[Calabasas, California]], Adam Merrin and Keith Slettedahl formed the band in 2002. The band's popularity grew from high-profile song placements in video games, films, commercials, and popular TV shows.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://diymusician.cdbaby.com/2010/02/top-sellers-interview-the-88/|title=Top Sellers Interview: The 88|date=26 February 2010|work=DIY Musician|publisher=CDbaby}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ascap.com/playback/2013/07/faces-places/pop-rock/ascap-new-member-the-88.aspx|title=ASCAP New Members: The 88|work=ASCAP|last=Edmonston|first=Tyler|date=22 July 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm2164624/|title=The 88|work=IMDb}}</ref>

From 2010 to 2012, the band toured and recorded with [[the Kinks]] leader [[Ray Davies]], serving as his opening act and backing Davies for his set. The band performed with Davies at the [[Royal Albert Hall]] in [[London]] in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.ocweekly.com/heardmentality/2010/02/the_88_keith_slettedahl_ray_davies.php|title=The 88 front man Keith Slettedahl on Backing Ray Davies|first=Arrissia|last=Owen|date=12 February 2010|work=OC Weekly}}{{Dead link|date=July 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://life.royalalberthall.com/2012/05/16/ray-davies-to-return-to-the-hall/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150407012500/http://life.royalalberthall.com/2012/05/16/ray-davies-to-return-to-the-hall/|archivedate=7 April 2015|title=Ray Davies to return to the Hall|work=Life at the Hall|date=16 May 2012|first=Matt|last=Griffin|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Other acts The 88 have performed with include [[Elliott Smith]], [[The Smashing Pumpkins]], [[The B-52's]], [[Elvis Costello]], [[Black Francis]],  [[The Zombies]], and [[The Flaming Lips]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://laist.com/2009/12/08/winston_calling_-_tonight_at_the_ec.php|title=Winston Calling – Tonight at the Echo|first=Heath|last=Biter|work=laist|date=8 December 2009|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20171106011231/http://laist.com/2009/12/08/winston_calling_-_tonight_at_the_ec.php|archivedate=6 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammymuseum.org/events/detail/homegrown-the-88|title=Homegrown: The 88 – GRAMMY Museum|work=GRAMMY Museum}}</ref>

Their track "At Least It Was Here" is the opening theme song to the television series ''[[Community (TV series)|Community]]''. In 2012, the band co-scored the film ''[[Friends with Kids]]''.

== Work with Ray Davies ==

In 2010, the band acted as the support act and back up band for [[Ray Davies]] on his American tour.  For the next two years, the band performed with Davies, including performances at London's [[Royal Albert Hall]] and Japan's [[Fuji Rock Festival]].  The 88 performed with Davies and [[Elvis Costello]] at [[Clive Davis]]'s pre-Grammy party in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/clive-davis-pre-grammy-party-goes-on-in-memory-of-whitney-houston-20120212|title=Clive Davis Pre-Grammy Party Goes on in Memory of Whitney Houston|work=Rolling Stone|last=Baltin|first=Steve|date=12 February 2012}}</ref>  The band recorded with Davies at [[Konk Studios]] in London on Davies' duets album ''[[See My Friends (album)|See My Friends]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/see-my-friends-mw0002058264|title=See My Friends – Ray Davies|work=AllMusic}}</ref>  The 88 played on "Long Way From Home" with Lucinda Williams and "[[Till the End of the Day]]" with Alex Chilton (Chilton's final recorded performance),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/ray-davies-on-alex-chilton|title=Ray Davies On Alex Chilton|work=Clash|last=Murray|first=Robin|date=11 December 2010}}</ref> as well as recording a version of [[David Watts (song)|David Watts]] with Davies.  The band did a promotional tour for ''See My Friends'' with Davies including performances on ''[[Late Night with Jimmy Fallon]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/ray-davies-til-the-end-of-the-day-on-fallon-20110726|title=Ray Davies 'Til the End of the Day' on 'Fallon'|work=Rolling Stone|date=26 July 2011}}</ref> and The Regis and Kelly show.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2011/jul/26/entertainment/la-et-tvtalk26-20110726|title=Tuesday's TV talk shows|date=26 July 2011|work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref>

== Television, film and video games ==
The 88 wrote and performed "At Least It Was Here", the opening theme song for the television series ''[[Community (TV series)|Community]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/The-88-at-least-it-was-here-lyrics|title=The 88 – At Least It Was Here|work=Genius}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1439629/|title=Community|date=17 September 2009|work=IMDb}}</ref>

Their song "You Belong to Me" is on the soundtrack of ''[[How I Met Your Mother]]'''s episode "[[Belly Full of Turkey]]". The band was also featured on the episode "[[Best Prom Ever]]" of that same series.  Their song "No One Here" was used during the Season 2 finale "[[Something Blue (How I Met Your Mother)|Something Blue]]". 

Their song "How Good it Can Be" is on the soundtrack of ''[[The O.C.]]''. "All 'Cause of You" is on the soundtrack for ''[[You, Me and Dupree]]''.

"No One Here" and also "All 'Cause of You" were used in ''[[Blue Lagoon: The Awakening]]'', the 2012 remake of ''[[The Blue Lagoon (1980 film)]]''.

In film, the band co-scored the 2012 movie ''[[Friends with Kids]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1720616/soundtrack|title=Friends with Kids|date=16 March 2012|work=IMDb}}</ref> That same year, The 88 performed on the soundtrack to the film ''[[The Lorax (film)|The Lorax]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/dr-seuss-the-lorax-original-songs-from-the-motion-picture-mw0002320938|title=Dr. Seuss' the Lorax [Original Songs from the Motion Picture] – Original Soundtrack|work=AllMusic}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1482459/soundtrack|title=The Lorax|date=2 March 2012|work=IMDb}}</ref>

The song "Sons and Daughters" appeared as downloadable content for the video game ''[[Rock Band (video game)|Rock Band]]''.
