'''Melody's Echo Chamber''' is the main project of French musician [[Melody Prochet]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Freeman|first=John|title=Reviews: Melody's Echo Chamber|url=http://thequietus.com/articles/10569-melody-s-echo-chamber-review|work=The Quietus|publisher=The Quietus|accessdate=13 May 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.lexpress.fr/culture/musique/melody-s-echo-chamber-specialiste-des-ballades-psyche_1243579.html|title=Melody's Echo Chamber, spécialiste des ballades psyché|last=Médioni|first=Gilles|date=25 April 2013|work=[[L'Express (France)|L'Express]]|language=French|accessdate=26 May 2013}}</ref>

==History==
When Melody Prochet's previous project My Bee's Garden supported [[Tame Impala]] on their European tour in 2010, Prochet collaborated with [[Kevin Parker (musician)|Kevin Parker]] to produce her new solo material as Melody's Echo Chamber. The material was recorded in Parker's makeshift studio in [[Perth]], Australia and Prochet's grandmother's seaside home in the south of France.<ref>{{cite web|last=Traynor|first=Cian|title=Interview: Melody's Echo Chamber|url=http://www.thestoolpigeon.co.uk/features/interview-melodys-echo-chamber.html|work=The Stool Pigeon|publisher=The Stool Pigeon|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120715030929/http://www.thestoolpigeon.co.uk/features/interview-melodys-echo-chamber.html|archivedate=15 July 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref> The [[self-titled]] [[Melody's Echo Chamber (album)|debut album]] was released under [[Fat Possum Records]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|title=Fat Possum Records: Melody's Echo Chamber|url=http://www.fatpossum.com/artists/melodys-echo-chamber|work=Fat Possum Records|publisher=Fat Possum Records}}</ref>

In 2013, the debut album peaked at 61 on the US ''Billboard'' Heatseekers Album Chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Billboard: Heatseekers Albums|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/1493203/melodys-echo-chamber/chart|work=Billboard|publisher=Billboard|accessdate=13 May 2013|archive-date=29 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190329221342/https://www.billboard.com/artist/1493203/melodys-echo-chamber/chart|url-status=dead}}</ref> ''[[Q Magazine]]'' rated the album 8/10, calling it an "intoxicating listen that's well worth experiencing for yourself."<ref>{{cite news|title=Melody's Echo Chamber|newspaper=Q Magazine|date=December 2012|agency=Bauer Media Group|page=109}}</ref> ''[[Drowned in Sound]]''{{'}}s Dom Gourlay awarded the album 9/10 and stated: "Whatever happens next, she can rest assured safe in the knowledge that together with her beau they've conjured up one of 2012's--or any other year in recent memory--finest debuts."<ref>{{cite web|last=Gourlay|first=Dom|title=Melody's Echo Chamber – Melody's Echo Chamber|url=http://drownedinsound.com/releases/17352/reviews/4145765|work=Drowned in Sound|publisher=Drowned in Sound|accessdate=13 May 2013|archive-date=5 April 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130405203943/http://drownedinsound.com/releases/17352/reviews/4145765|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Melody's Echo Chamber released "Shirim" in October 2014, which was set to be featured in her next album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2014/10/melodys-echo-chamber-returns-with-dreamy-new-single-shirim-listen/|title=Melody’s Echo Chamber returns with dreamy new single “Shirim” — listen|date=15 October 2014|website=Consequenceofsound.net|accessdate=21 June 2018}}</ref> In December, it was announced that Melody's Echo Chamber would play at the 2015 [[Levitation (festival)|Levitation Festival]] in [[Austin, Texas]], but her appearance was later cancelled due to visa issues.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2015/05/melodys_echo_ch_2.html|title=Melody's Echo Chamber cancels Levitation Fest appearance due to visa issues, reschedules NYC show for August|website=BrooklynVegan|accessdate=21 June 2018}}</ref>

In 2017, Prochet released a new track on [[YouTube]] titled "Cross My Heart", with the announcement of her independently produced album ''[[Bon Voyage (Melody's Echo Chamber album)|Bon Voyage]]''. It was set to be released in the same year, but was delayed as Prochet had suffered a "serious accident", followed by the cancellation of her world tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/melodys-echo-chamber-cancels-tour-due-to-serious-accident/|title=Melody's Echo Chamber Cancels Tour Due to Serious Accident|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Noah|last=Yoo|date=27 June 2017|access-date=27 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/melodys-echo-chamber-cancels-tour-suffering-2095191|title=Melody’s Echo Chamber cancels tour after suffering 'serious accident'|first=Luke Morgan|last=Britton|work=[[NME]]|date=27 June 2017|access-date=27 June 2017}}</ref>

In April 2018, Prochet released the single “Breathe In, Breathe Out”, and ''Bon Voyage'' was released in June.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/jun/15/melodys-echo-chamber-bon-voyage-review-a-psych-pop-hotchpotch|title=Melody’s Echo Chamber: Bon Voyage review – a psych-pop hotchpotch|first=Rachel|last=Aroesti|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=15 June 2018|access-date=21 June 2018}}</ref>
