'''Fabio Bartolo Rizzo''', better known by his [[stage name]] '''Marracash''' ({{IPA-it|marraˈkɛʃ|IPA}}), is an Italian [[Rapping|rapper]], [[record producer]] and [[TV personality]]. Starting his career in the rap group Dogo Gang, he debuted as a solo artist rising in fame in the 2010s releasing six studio albums that reached the top ten of the [[Fimi|Italian Albums Chart]], including two top positions with [[Santeria (album)|''Santeria'']] (2016) and ''Persona'' (2019). The rapper has also released numerous successful singles and collaborations scoring seventeen times the top ten of the Italian Singles Chart. Thanks to his work he has sold 3 million copies in Italy, becoming one of the top selling Italian rappers in the 2010s.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fimi.it/top-of-the-music/certificazioni/certificazioni.kl|title=Certificazioni - FIMI|website=www.fimi.it|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref>

In the course of his musical projects he has sung and written songs with numerous artists including rap artists like [[Gué Pequeno]], [[J-Ax]], [[Fabri Fibra]], [[Emis Killa]], [[Club Dogo]], [[Baby K (artist)|Baby K]], and pop influences artist as [[Tiziano Ferro]], [[Giusy Ferreri]], [[Elisa (Italian singer)|Elisa]], [[Federica Abbate]] and [[Elodie (singer)|Elodie]].

In 2013, together with producer Shablo, he founded the independent record label Roccia Music, which involves established figures and newcomers to rap, producers and DJs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://rocciamusic.com/|title=Roccia Music {{!}} Sito ufficiale|last=Music|first=Roccia|website=Roccia Music {{!}} Sito ufficiale|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> From 2012 to 2014 he host [[MTV (Italy)|MTV Italy]]'s [[freestyle music]] tv program ''MTV Spit.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.it/video/cgy75u/MTV-Spit-3x00-Marracash-ti-invita-a-MTV-Spit-3|title=MTV Spit - 3x00 - Marracash ti invita a MTV Spit 3 - MTV Spit {{!}} Video {{!}} MTV Italia|website=www.mtv.it|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Fabio Rizzo Bartolo was born in [[Nicosia, Sicily]] from a mother who worked as a custodian and a father who worked as a truck driver. He has a younger brother. His family then moved to [[Milan]] in a house in Bramante;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2010/10/24/marracash-canto-il-lato-oscuro-della-vita.html|title=Marracash Canto il lato oscuro della vita e a casa mia parlo in dialetto - la Repubblica.it|website=Archivio - la Repubblica.it|language=it|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref> when he was ten, he was expelled from his home with his family, who moved to the suburbs of [[Barona]], where he grew up and still lives.<ref>{{Citation|title=Personal: Marracash racconta il suo lungo silenzio, la psicoterapia e 'Persona'|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mkcR1SO8kfk|language=en|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref>

After graduating as an electronical technician, Rizzo recorded his first verses under the pseudonym '''Yuza delle Nuvole''', appearing in Prodigio's 1999 demo "The Royal Rumble", alongside Il Guercio, Fame, Aken and Corvo D'Argento. He adopted the stage name Marracash after his childhood nickname, {{lang|it|Marocchino}} ({{langnf||Italian|"Moroccan"}}, jokingly because of his Sicilian origins), as a pun on the name of [[Marrakesh]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2010/10/24/marracash-canto-il-lato-oscuro-della-vita.html|title=Marracash Canto il lato oscuro della vita e a casa mia parlo in dialetto - la Repubblica.it|website=Archivio - la Repubblica.it|language=it|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref>

==Career==
Its debut dates back to 2004 in the PMC VS Club Dogo mixtape - the official Mixtape, product born from the collaboration between the [[Club Dogo]] and the Massive Crew Portion, contacts in Milan and Bologna. In the same year the collective Dogo Gang came to life, within which Marracash's militancy is gradually consolidating, marking a new stage with the participation within ''Regular'', album of [[Club Dogo]], and of ''Hashishinz Sound Vol. 1'', by [[Gué Pequeno]] and Deleterio.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Club-Dogo-PMC-PMC-Vs-Club-Dogo-The-Official-Mixtape/release/1454308|title=Club Dogo / PMC (3) - PMC Vs. Club Dogo: The Official Mixtape|website=Discogs|language=en|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref>

In 2005, under the Contagion Area label, Marracash published "Popolare", a single product by Don Joe made available for free download for network users.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.produzionioblio.com/|title=Produzioni Oblio - Area di Contagio|website=www.produzionioblio.com|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref> The song anticipated the release of the mixtape ''Roccia Music I'',  mixtape in which the Dogo Gang (Club Dogo, Vincenzo da Via Anfossi, Deleterio and Ted Bundy) collaborated and guests such as Inoki, Co'Sang, Rischio, Shablo, Misa and Royal Mehdi.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.soundvillage.it/interviste_hiphop/view.php?id=161|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081011194618/http://www.soundvillage.it/interviste_hiphop/view.php?id=161|url-status=dead|archive-date=2008-10-11|title=Soundvillage Music - HIPHOP ZONE - INTERVISTE: MARRACASH : Predestinato dal prefabbricato|date=2008-10-11|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref>

On June 13, 2008 the first solo album entitled [[Marracash (album)|''Marracash'']] is released, released by [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music]], which reaches the ninth position of the Italian Albums Chart receiving the gold certification.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://italiancharts.com/search.asp?search=marracash&cat=a|title=italiancharts.com - Italian charts portal|website=italiancharts.com|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> Guests of the album are [[Gué Pequeno]] and [[J-Ax]], [[Vincenzo da Via Anfossi]] and [[Jake La Furia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.soundvillage.it/interviste_hiphop/view.php?id=161|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081011194618/http://www.soundvillage.it/interviste_hiphop/view.php?id=161|url-status=dead|archive-date=2008-10-11|title=Soundvillage Music - HIPHOP ZONE - INTERVISTE: MARRACASH : Predestinato dal prefabbricato|date=2008-10-11|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> Two singles were extracted from the album: "[[Badabum Cha Cha]]", which becomes the singer's first top ten, and "Estate in città".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.panorama.it/news/musica/marracash-ascolto-status-lalbum|title=Marracash: il primo ascolto di Status, l'album|date=2015-01-20|website=Panorama|language=en|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> 

In 2009 he participated together with 55 other Italian singers in the realization of "Domani 21/04.2009", a musical track published in a charitable way to remember the victims affected by the [[2009 L'Aquila earthquake]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Artisti+uniti+per+l%27Abruzzo&titel=Domani+21+Aprile+2009&cat=s|title=italiancharts.com - Artisti uniti per l'Abruzzo - Domani 21 Aprile 2009|website=italiancharts.com|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> Towards the end of the year the song Cani pazzi, which anticipated Marracash's second album ''Fino a qui tutto bene'', out on July 13, 2010.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tgcom.mediaset.it/spettacolo/articoli/articolo487328.shtml|title=Fino a qui tutto bene|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110208040241/http://www.tgcom.mediaset.it/spettacolo/articoli/articolo487328.shtml|archive-date=2011-02-08|access-date=}}</ref> The collaborations present are with [[Giusy Ferreri]] in "Rivincita" and [[Fabri Fibra]] in "Stupidi",  with less success than the previous project.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://italiancharts.com/search.asp?search=marracash&cat=s|title=italiancharts.com - Italian charts portal|website=italiancharts.com|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> 

On September 7, 2011, he releases the song "King of rap", which anticipates the eponymous album released in October.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rnbjunk.com/intervista-esclusiva-marracash-nuovo-album-king-del-rap-652/|title=King del Rap|last=|first=|date=|website=|access-date=}}</ref> The album debuts at the third position of the FIMI chart receiving the platinum certification.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fimi.it/top-of-the-music/certification-form.kl|title=Richiedi targhetta|website=www.fimi.it|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> Later in 2012 he released the collaboration "Giusto un giro" with [[Emis Killa]] and announced the inauguration of his own record label, La Roccia Music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fimi.it/top-of-the-music/certificazioni/certificazioni.kl|title=Certificazioni - FIMI|website=www.fimi.it|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rapburger.com/2013/09/26/roccia-music-un-collettivo-per-alzare-lasticella-nel-mercato-urban-italiano/|title=Roccia Music, un collettivo per alzare l'asticella nel mercato urban italiano|date=2013-09-26|website=RapBurger|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> On December 22, Marracash conducts the first episode of ''[[MTV Spit]],'' a television program focused on the rap battle between artists of the underground scene.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://spit.mtv.it/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171110170912/http://spit.mtv.it/|url-status=dead|archive-date=2017-11-10|title=MTV Spit|date=2017-11-10|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref>  

After the release of the collective album ''Genesis'' with the artists of the Rock Music of 2013,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ilgiorno.it/pavia/cronaca/roccia-musica-marracash-1.72094|title="Roccia Music", il collettivo di Marracash fa tappa a Pavia: il meglio del rap è di scena al PalaRavizza|last=|first=|date=2014-07-23|website=Il Giorno|language=it|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> on January 20, 2015 he published his fourth studio album ''Status'' which reached the number 2 position of the Italian Albums Chart and 32 of the Swiss Charts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fimi.it/top-of-the-music/classifiche.kl|title=Classifiche - FIMI|website=www.fimi.it|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> From the album he releases the homonymous single "Status", which peaked at number 2 of Italian Singles Chart,  "Senza un posto nel mondo" with [[Tiziano Ferro]], "In radio" and "Niente canzoni d'amore" with [[Federica Abbate]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.earone.it/news/marracash_in_radio_radio_date_16_01_2015_13294644/|title=Marracash - In radio (Radio Date: 16-01-2015)|website=EarOne|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusicitalia.it/news/marracash-esce-il-22-gennaio-status-vendetta-edition-2-cd-e-1-dvd-a-prezzo-speciale.html|title=MARRACASH: esce il 22 gennaio "Status Vendetta Edition" 2 cd e 1 dvd a prezzo speciale|date=2015-12-21|website=All Music Italia|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> 

In 2016 the rapper announced the release of a studio album with [[Gué Pequeno]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tgcom24.mediaset.it/spettacolo/gue-pequeno-e-marracash-un-disco-insieme-durante-il-2016_2152357-201602a.shtml|title=Gué Pequeno e Marracash, un disco insieme durante il 2016|website=Tgcom24|language=IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> Published on June 24 of the same year, the album is entitled [[Santeria (album)|''Santeria'']] and is composed of fifteen tracks, which became his first number one album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.soundsblog.it/post/445195/marracash-gue-pequeno-marra-gue-album-canzoni-tracklist-cover|title=Marracash e Guè Pequeno, Marra/Guè: L'album esce il 24 giugno 2016|date=2016-05-20|website=Soundsblog.it|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref> The forerunner single "[[Nulla accade]]", released on June 7, is certified double platinum and reaches position number 16 of the Italian Singles Chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fimi.it/top-of-the-music/certificazioni/certificazioni.kl|title=Certificazioni - FIMI|website=www.fimi.it|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.it/musica/news-musica/marracash-e-gue-paqueno-pubblicano-santeria/317026/|title=Marracash e Guè Pequeno pubblicano "Santeria"|date=2016-06-07|website=Rolling Stone Italia|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-02}}</ref>

== Personal life ==

== Music influences and style ==
In a TV interview with [[AllMusic]] on July 7, 2008, Marracash claimed to have regularly listened to [[Metallica]] and [[883 (band)|883]] as a child.<ref>{{Citation|title=Lucilla Agosti|date=2020-01-18|url=https://it.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Lucilla_Agosti&oldid=110214124|work=Wikipedia|language=it|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref> The rapper takes inspiration from American artists such as [[Jay-Z]], [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Kanye West]], [[DJ Khaled]], [[Nas]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Wu-Tang Clan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.outpump.com/la-playlist-che-ha-accompagnato-marracash-nella-scrittura-di-persona/|title=La playlist che ha accompagnato Marracash nella scrittura di "Persona"|date=2019-11-15|website=Outpump|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref> From Italy, he cities [[Frankie Hi-NRG MC]].

The rapper describes his writing and writing process for the 2019 album ''Persona'' in an interview on [[Rolling Stone|Rolling Stone Italy]]: "''It develops over time, in the sense that I mature stuff, it's something I do on an introspective level, I do it without writing. I just take notes and then at a certain point I throw them out, and when I started writing the pieces flowed out like blood from a wound.''" He then describes his feelings and the way he analyzes the situations he reports in the texts: "''I exercise a constant self-consciousness that leads me to experience emotions very intensely. [...] But each time I tend to pull the rope a bit to its extreme and it is perhaps also a way to feel alive, a search for adrenaline, because in the end I detach myself enough to find them stronger than before.''"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.it/digital-cover/marracash-e-differente/483512/|title=Marracash è differente|date=2019-10-29|website=Rolling Stone Italia|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref>

== Criticism and controversy ==
In 2006, when Marracash criticized [[Nesli]]'s way of rapping through the song "Popular", a kind of rivalry between the two began. The following year, in fact, Nesli responded to Marracash with the song "Life is just one", accusing him of making neighborhood rhymes and being a chatterbox. Marracash's response came in 2008 with the song "Dritto al punto", in which he reversed the quotations made by the opposing rapper, who replied with "Riot", closing the debate.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.it/recensioni/marracash-10-anni-dopo-e-ancora-il-king/|title=Marracash, 10 anni dopo è ancora il King|date=2018-06-22|website=Rolling Stone Italia|language=it-IT|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref>

On January 14, 2017, six months after the publication of Santeria, Marracash and [[Gué Pequeno]] gave an interview to [[Corriere della Sera]], in which they commented on the musical style adopted by their colleagues [[Fedez]] and [[J-Ax]], at the time close to the publication of ''[[Comunisti col Rolex]]'', criticizing the artists and accusing them of masking their intent to make numbers and money with false intentions.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.corriere.it/spettacoli/17_gennaio_14/marracash-gue-pequeno-rap-siamo-noi-show-ispirato-gangster-movie-ef0bbe0c-da90-11e6-817c-c522bb7cbdb6.shtml|title=Marracash-Gué Pequeno: il rap siamo noi. Show ispirato ai gangster movie|last=Laffranchi|first=Andrea|date=2017-01-14|website=Corriere della Sera|language=it|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref> Fedez responded to the accusations by explaining that the two rappers criticized the duo on purpose to have visibility, as well as calling Marracash a coward to not meet him during [[Moschino]]'s fashion show; Marracash, referring to Fedez as "''dwarf with [[Napoleon complex]]''", denied what Fedez said about the fashion show, claiming that the colleague had not even sought eye contact with him on that occasion, and ironic about the choice to attend the parades with bodyguards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.adnkronos.com/intrattenimento/spettacolo/2017/01/16/lite-insulti-tra-rapper-marracash-gue-pequeno-contro-fedez_6Nml6WaUgYi7qTSe7tvCoJ.html|title=Lite e insulti tra rapper: Marracash e Gué Pequeno contro Fedez|website=Adnkronos|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.corriere.it/spettacoli/17_gennaio_15/guerra-rapper-fedez-j-ax-contro-marracash-gue-pequeno-b21f2916-db58-11e6-8da6-59efe3faefec.shtml|title=È guerra tra rapper: Fedez (e J-Ax) contro Marracash e Gué Pequeno|last=Franco|first=Renato|date=2017-01-15|website=Corriere della Sera|language=it|access-date=2020-02-03}}</ref>
