'''Sananda Francesco Maitreya''' (born '''Terence Trent Howard'''; March 15, 1962), who started his career with the [[stage name]] '''Terence Trent D'Arby''', is an American singer and songwriter who came to fame with his debut studio album, ''[[Introducing the Hardline According to Terence Trent D'Arby]]'' (1987). The album included the singles "[[If You Let Me Stay]]", "[[Sign Your Name]]", "[[Dance Little Sister (Terence Trent D'Arby song)|Dance Little Sister]]",  and the number one hit "[[Wishing Well (Terence Trent D'Arby song)|Wishing Well]]".

== Early life ==
Terence Trent D'Arby was born Terence Trent Howard in Manhattan in 1962.<ref name="ReferenceC">{{cite web|url=http://www.sanandamaitreya.com/biography.html |title=Biography / Facts & Figures |publisher=Sanandamaitreya.com |access-date=November 7, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180315223642/http://www.sanandamaitreya.com/biography.html|archive-date=March 15, 2018}}</ref> His mother is Frances Howard, a [[gospel singer]],<ref name="RollingStone">Gilmore, Mikal (June 16, 1988). "Can Terence Trent D'Arby Be As Good As He Thinks He Is". ''[[Rolling Stone]]''. Issue 528.</ref> teacher and counselor. Frances Howard married Bishop James Benjamin Darby, who became his stepfather and raised him. He took this stepfather's last name and later added the apostrophe.<ref name="ReferenceC"/><ref>{{cite news|first=Kate|last=Mossman|title='I was killed when I was 27': the curious afterlife of Terence Trent D'Arby|date=October 9, 2015|work=New Statesman|url=https://www.newstatesman.com/politics/uk/2015/10/i-was-killed-when-i-was-27-curious-afterlife-terence-trent-d-arby|access-date=January 18, 2021}}</ref>

He trained as a [[boxing|boxer]] in Orlando and in 1980 won the Florida [[Golden Gloves]] [[lightweight]] championship.<ref name="sanandamaitreya.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.sanandamaitreya.com/presskit/SANANDA%20MAITREYA_Artist%20Bio%202012.pdf |title=Sananda Maitreya – Bio |publisher=Sanadamaitreya.com |access-date=February 10, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170702015046/http://www.sanandamaitreya.com/presskit/SANANDA%20MAITREYA_Artist%20Bio%202012.pdf|archive-date=July 4, 2017}}</ref> He received an offer to attend boxing school in the [[United States Army]], but went to college instead. After enrolling at the [[University of Central Florida]], he quit a year later and enlisted in the U.S. Army. He was posted at [[Fort Sill]], [[Oklahoma]], and then served in the [[3rd Armored Division (United States)|3rd Armored Division]], near [[Frankfurt]], West Germany.<ref name="RollingStone"/> He was formally court-martialed and dishonorably discharged by the army in April 1983 after going [[Desertion|absent without leave]].<ref name=Corcoran>Michael Corcoran. [https://books.google.com/books?id=gAcetOZSUdcC In the Ring With Terence Trent D'Arby], ''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]'' June 1988, Vol. 4, No. 3</ref>  While in West Germany, he  worked as a band leader with the band The Touch, releasing an album of material called ''Love on Time'' (1984).<ref name="The Great Rock Discography">{{cite book

In 1986, he left West Germany for London, where he briefly played with The Bojangles. In London, he signed a recording contract with CBS Records.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Terence Trent D'Arby: Vibrator|last=Richliano|first=James|date=May 28, 1995|work=[[In Newsweekly]]}}</ref>

== Fame as Terence Trent D'Arby ==
D'Arby's debut solo album, ''[[Introducing the Hardline According to Terence Trent D'Arby]]'', was released in July 1987.<ref name="The Great Rock Discography"/> The album produced hits including "[[If You Let Me Stay]]", "[[Sign Your Name]]", "[[Dance Little Sister (Terence Trent D'Arby song)|Dance Little Sister]]", and the number one hit "[[Wishing Well (Terence Trent D'Arby song)|Wishing Well]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/introducing-the-hardline-according-to-terence-trent-darby-mw0000197584|title=Introducing the Hardline According to Terence Trent d'Arby - Terence Trent D'Arby &#124; Songs, Reviews, Credits|website=AllMusic}}</ref>

In an interview, D'Arby expressed a high opinion of his first album, claiming that it was the most important album since the Beatles' ''[[Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band|Sgt. Pepper]]''.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Thomas |first=Stephen |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/terence-trent-darby-mn0000017877 |title=Terence Trent D'Arby – Music Biography, Credits and Discography |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=November 7, 2012}}</ref> After the comments leaked to US media outlets, he stated that most of what he said was exaggerated, but that it is sometimes necessary to "hit people over the head" to get their attention.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1988-03-20-ca-2404-story.html|title=POP MUSIC : An Enigma Called Terence Trent D'Arby|date=March 20, 1988|work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref> The album earned him a [[Grammy Award]] in the category [[Grammy Award for Best Male R&B vocal performance|Best R&B Vocal Performance, Male]] (1989)<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.orlandosentinel.com/news/os-xpm-1993-04-18-9309171063-story.html|title=TERENCE TRENT D'ARBY-- GRAMMY WINNER FROM DELAND|website=OrlandoSentinel.com}}</ref> and a BRIT Award for International Breakthrough Act, and he also received Grammy and ''[[Soul Train]]'' nominations for Best New Artist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kickmag.net/2017/05/28/throwback-terence-trent-darby-sign-name/|title=Throwback: Terence Trent D'Arby-Sign Your Name|website=Kick Mag The Urban Eclectic}}</ref>

D'Arby's follow-up album was ''[[Neither Fish Nor Flesh]]'' (1989),<ref name="The Great Rock Discography"/> which was very different from his previous one and ahead of his time, as stated in a 2021 interview with producer Martyn Ware.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://podcasts.apple.com/gb/podcast/ep30-sananda-maitreya-part-1/id1542104367?i=1000521646051|title=Electronically Yours with Martyn Ware: EP30: Sananda Maitreya, Part 1 on Apple Podcasts|website=Podcasts.apple.com|access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref><ref name="auto"/> It took four more years and a move to Los Angeles until his next album, ''[[Symphony or Damn]]'' (1993), was released. The record contained the singles "Delicate" and "She Kissed Me". It peaked at No. 4 on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref name="The Great Rock Discography"/> In 1995, D'Arby released ''[[Vibrator (album)|Vibrator]]'', which was followed by a world tour.<ref name="The Great Rock Discography"/>

D'Arby's music has been included on several movie and television soundtracks. He sang the theme song of 1991's ''[[Frankie and Johnny (1991 film)|Frankie and Johnny]]''. "Right Thing, Wrong Way" featured prominently in the end credits of ''[[Beverly Hills Cop III]]''. "What Shall I Do?" was featured in an episode of the [[UPN]] television series ''[[Girlfriends (U.S. TV series)|Girlfriends]]''. He sang the ending song, "Letting Go", in the 1996 film ''[[The Fan (1996 film)|The Fan]]''. D'Arby's songs were also used in ''[[Prêt-à-Porter (film)|Prêt-à-Porter]]'' and the 1995 miniseries ''The Promised Land''.{{citation needed|date=September 2019}}

In 1999, D'Arby collaborated with [[INXS]] to replace his friend, the late vocalist [[Michael Hutchence]], so the band could play at the official opening of [[Stadium Australia]] (a major venue for the [[2000 Summer Olympics|Sydney Olympics]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/354190.stm |title=INXS plan Olympic comeback |publisher=BBC News |date=May 27, 1999 |access-date=April 17, 2014}}</ref>

==Later career as Sananda Maitreya==
{{more citations needed section|date=October 2021}}
D'Arby legally changed his name to Sananda Maitreya on October 4, 2001, explaining "Terence Trent D'Arby was dead... he watched his suffering as he died a noble death. After intense pain I meditated for a new spirit, a new will, a new identity".<ref name="bgreenman">{{cite web|first=Ben |last=Greenman |author-link=Ben Greenman |url=http://www.newyorker.com/online/blogs/culture/2013/06/where-have-you-gone-terence-trent-darby.html |title=Whatever Happened to Terence Trent D'Arby? |website=[[The New Yorker]] |date=June 4, 2013 |access-date=November 25, 2014}}</ref> Maitreya has said that his name change resulted from a series of dreams he had in 1995. Though the name does not have any religious significance, Maitreya explained that he understood it to mean "rebirth" in [[Sanskrit]].<ref name="lester1">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/oct/05/why-terence-trent-darby-became-sananda-maitreya-it-was-that-or-death|title=Why Terence Trent D'Arby became Sananda Maitreya: 'It was that or death'|first=Paul|last=Lester|date=October 5, 2017|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> {{lang|sa-Latn|Sānanda}} ({{wikt-lang|sa|आनन्द}}) means 'possessed of happiness',<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.sanskrit-lexicon.uni-koeln.de/scans/MWScan/tamil/index.html|title=Sanskrit and Tamil Dictionaries|website=Sanskrit-lexicon.uni-koeln.de|access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref> and {{lang|sa-Latn|[[maitreya]]}} ({{wikt-lang|sa|मैत्रेय}}) means 'friendly, kind, loving, benevolent'.<ref name="auto1"/>

From 2001 to 2021, Maitreya released nine studio albums and four live albums.

2001 also marked the release of the ''Wildcard'' album. Initially downloadable for free from the artist's official website,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sanandamaitreya.com/|title=Sananda Maitreya's Official Website!|website=Sanandamaitreya.com|access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref> the album received great support from international critics in particular for its single, the song "O Divina". At the beginning of 2002 Maitreya moved to Milan for love, where he married Italian Architect and TV presenter Francesca Francone in 2003, and began his sixth project, ''[[Angels & Vampires - Volume I]]''. The artist initially chose to publish the project on the official website in chapters, as the recordings continued and then released it on June 29, 2005 in Mp3 format.

In July 2005, Maitreya began the second volume of the project: ''[[Angels & Vampires - Volume II]]'', successfully continued the division into chapters. On 29 April 2006 the second mastered volume is published. ''The Angels & Vampires'' album contains 40 songs, including a cover of "Angie", a tribute to [[the Rolling Stones]]. The genre of the album is Post Millennium Rock. Maitreya played all the instruments during the recordings and entirely produced, wrote and arranged the entire project by himself.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Sananda-Maitreya-Angels-Vampires/release/2205764|title=Sananda Maitreya – Angels & Vampires (2007, CD)|access-date=1 July 2021|website=Discogs.com}}</ref>

In 2007, three of his songs were played in [[Judd Apatow]]'s movie ''[[Knocked Up]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0478311/soundtrack|title=Knocked Up (2007) |website=IMDb.com|access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref>

After the 2007 European tour, new concerts followed in 2008 and television participation in the 2008 Christmas concert. In addition to the studio albums, Maitreya has released four live albums from 2007 to 2012: ''Influenza in Firenze'', ''Camels At The Crossroads'', ''Lovers & Fighters'', and ''Confessions of a Zooathaholic''; a selection of the best songs from live concerts and tours performed in the same year.

Maitreya released ''[[Nigor Mortis]]'' in 2008, which followed the same evolutionary process as ''Angels & Vampires''. The album was first published in chapters during the recordings, and then came out in the mastered version at the end of 2008 and is available on CD and MP3 at the link of his website. ''[[The Sphinx (album)|The Sphinx]]'' album was released in March 2011; in the same month the instrumental version of ''The Sphinx'' and the new live album by the artist related to the 2010 concerts of the ''Post Millennium Rock: Confessions of a Zooathaholic'' has been released.

In March 2013, ''[[Return to Zooathalon]]'' was released, followed in 2015 by the double album ''[[The Rise of the Zugebrian Time Lords]]''. 2017 marked the release of a monumental work: ''Prometheus & Pandora'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Sananda-Maitreya-Prometheus-Pandora/release/11962800|title=Sananda Maitreya – Prometheus & Pandora (2017, CD)|access-date=1 July 2021|website=Discogs.com}}</ref> 53 songs divided into three volumes, the artist declared that this album has become so important and impressive because through music he has elaborated the mourning for the loss of his great friends and idols, David Bowie, Prince, George Michael, and Tom Petty.

In December 2020, a new live album, ''Some Sake In Osaka'', was released,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=OLAK5uy_lzvtYC-yCW8YS9EY2qL4BTShYp81xu5ro|title=Some Sake In Osaka! (Live) |publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref>  it showcases a Japan tour with his historic American band.

On 15 March 2021, Maitreya released his 12th studio album, ''Pandora's PlayHouse'', which included three collaborations, the song "Reflecting Light",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/the-avalanches-reflecting-light-ft-sananda-maitreya-and-vashti-bunyan/ |title=The Avalanches |website=Pitchfork.com |date=August 3, 2020}}</ref> composed with the Australian duo [[The Avalanches]]; "Time Is On My Side" with Irene Grandi and the opening song  of the project: "Pandora's Plight" with jazz pianist Antonio Faraò. The project has an instrumental song called "Prince",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theprincefanalbum.com/sananda-maitreya|title=Sananda Maitreya dedicated a piano piece to Master Prince. Audio and Interview.|website=Theprincefanalbum.com|access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref> which honours the memory and the friendship of Maitreya and [[Prince (musician)|Prince]].

==Film career==
Maitreya has appeared in two films and in the TV mini-series ''[[Shake, Rattle and Roll: An American Love Story]]'', in which he played the part of [[Jackie Wilson]].{{citation needed|date=October 2021}}

==Personal life==
Maitreya married Italian television host and architect Francesca Francone in 2003.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/whatever-happened-to-terence-trent-darby|title=Whatever Happened to Terence Trent D'Arby?|first=Ben|last=Greenman|date=June 4, 2013|website=Newyorker.com}}</ref> They have two sons.<ref name="lester1"/> Maitreya has a daughter from a previous relationship, London-based musician Seraphina Simone.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vanyaland.com/2020/08/26/seraphina-simone-explores-the-smoke-and-mirrors-behind-hollywood/|title=Seraphina Simone explores the smoke and mirrors behind 'Hollywood $$$'|date=26 August 2020|website=Vanyaland.com|access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref>
