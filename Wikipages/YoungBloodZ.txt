'''YoungBloodZ''' is an American [[Southern hip hop]] [[Duet (music)|duo]] from [[Atlanta, Georgia]] composed of J-Bo (born Jeffrey Raymond Grigsby on October 4, 1977) and Sean P (born Sean Paul Ryan Joseph on March 7, 1978). The duo was signed to [[LaFace Records]]. They are one of many commercially successful [[Atlanta hip hop|Atlanta hip hop artists]].

== Career ==
Jeffrey Raymond Grigsby and Sean Paul Ryan Joseph met at Decatur's Miller Grove Middle School, where they started the Attic Crew with some friends.<ref>''[[Rap Pages]]'' magazine, Volume 8, p. 136, 1999</ref>

Their debut album was ''Against Da Grain'', released in 1999 on LaFace. The duo are best known for singles like "U-Way" (1999) and "85" (2000), which featured [[Big Boi]]. After a 3-year absence, they returned with ''Cadillac Pimpin''' in 2003, scoring a moderate hit. The duo's sophomore effort featured Grammy-nominated single "[[Damn! (song)|Damn!]]", reaching #4 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 and becoming their only top 10 hit. It was produced by [[Lil Jon]].

YoungBloodz released ''[[Ev'rybody Know Me]]'' in 2005. It featured guest vocals from [[Young Buck]] and another [[Lil Jon]]-produced hit "[[Presidential (song)|Presidential]]" and production by [[Scott Storch]]. Their song "I'mma Shine" was featured in the 2006 film ''[[Step Up (film)|Step Up]]'' and on its soundtrack.

YoungbloodZ has made many appearances in a number of hit singles including [[Cherish (band)|Cherish]]'s "[[Do It to It]]", [[Sammie]]'s "[[You Should Be My Girl]]", both produced by [[Jazzy Pha]]. Sean P appeared on the ''Billboard'' R&B/Hip-Hop number one song "[[Snap Yo Fingers]]" by [[Lil Jon]] featuring [[E-40]]. YoungBloodz have also been featured in songs with [[T.I.]], [[Nelly]], [[Gucci Mane]], [[Lil Scrappy]], [[Twista]], [[DJ Khaled]] and [[T-Pain]]. The group are working on a new album ''Back From the Liquor Sto'', that is scheduled for release soon. The first official singles from the EP are entitled "Foolish" and "She Drank, She Smoke" both produced by Rawbeatzz.<ref>{{cite news|last=Mason|first=Kim|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Youngbloodz/+wiki|title=Youngbloodz Bsck in the Studio|date=2013-11-23|publisher=LastFm.com|access-date=2013-01-25}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
Both members were arrested on June 1, 2006, on drugs and weapons charges in Atlanta after a traffic stop involving a tour bus with an expired license plate. All charges were eventually dropped and subsequently the driver was charged.{{citation needed|date=June 2014}} J-Bo and Sean P never had any trouble with the law before this arrest.<ref>{{cite news|last=Harris|first=Chris|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1533517/youngbloodz-booked-on-drug-weapons-charges.jhtml|title=Youngbloodz Booked On Drug, Weapons Charges|date=2006-06-04|publisher=mtv.com|access-date=2013-01-10}}</ref>
