''' Erika de Casier e Ramos Lizardo''' is a Portuguese-born Danish singer, songwriter, and record producer. She began her career performing as half of the duo Saint Cava. After the duo disbanded, she independently released her debut studio album, ''Essentials'', in 2019. She signed to British record label [[4AD]] in 2020, and released her second studio album, ''[[Sensational (Erika de Casier album)|Sensational]]'', in 2021.

==Early life==
Erika de Casier was born in 1989 or 1990<ref name="Clash profile">{{cite web |last1=Hussain |first1=Shahzaib |title=Astral Realm: An Alternative Roundup #2 |url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/astral-realm-an-alternative-roundup-2 |website=Clash |access-date=4 June 2021 |date=27 May 2021 |quote="I’m 31! I’ve tried to correct it on Wiki. People are shocked when I tell them I’m not a twenty something."}}</ref> in Portugal<ref name="bv4adsign">{{cite web |last1=Hatfield |first1=Amanda |title=Erika de Casier signs to 4AD with new single "No Butterflies, No Nothing" |url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/erika-de-casier-signs-to-4ad-with-new-single-no-butterflies-no-nothing/ |website=[[BrooklynVegan]] |access-date=22 January 2021 |language=en |date=20 October 2020 |archive-date=30 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210130034932/https://www.brooklynvegan.com/erika-de-casier-signs-to-4ad-with-new-single-no-butterflies-no-nothing/ |url-status=live }}</ref> to a Belgian mother and Cape Verdean father. She attended [[Catholic school]] as a child, and at age eight, she and her family moved to [[Aarhus]], Denmark. She has stated that she was a target of bullying due to her and her brother being the only black students at her school. She spent time painting throughout her adolescence. At age 16, she spent a year living with a [[Homestay|host family]] in the United States. Upon returning to Denmark, she joined her school's choir and band and began producing her own beats. After graduating from school, she moved to [[Copenhagen]].<ref name="crackint" /> Before performing as a solo artist, she worked at a [[kindergarten]].<ref name="metal" />

==Career==
Starting in 2014, Casier performed with Andreas Vasegaard as part of the R&B duo Saint Cava. The duo performed at the [[Roskilde Festival]] in 2015 and released a string of singles through Danish record label Forbandet Ungdom before eventually disbanding.<ref name="metal" /> During this time, she also worked with the Danish electronic collective Regelbau.<ref name="crackint" />

==Personal life==
As of 2020, Casier is studying to earn a [[master's degree]] in Music Creation at the [[Rhythmic Music Conservatory]]. She has described herself as "not a spiritual person at all".<ref name="metal">{{cite web |last1=Reed |first1=Matt |title=Erika de Casier |url=https://metalmagazine.eu/en/post/interview/erika-de-casier |website=Metal Magazine |access-date=January 21, 2021 |language=en |date=17 March 2020 |archive-date=21 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210121182519/https://metalmagazine.eu/en/post/interview/erika-de-casier |url-status=live }}</ref>
