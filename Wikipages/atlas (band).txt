'''Atlas''' were a [[New Zealand]] [[Rock music|rock]] [[Band (music)|band]] which was formed in 2005 and disbanded in late 2008.

==Founding==
In April 2005, in [[Hollywood, Los Angeles, California|Hollywood, California]], producer [[Hank Linderman]] ([[The Beach Boys]], [[Eagles (band)|Eagles]]) introduced New Zealand rocker [[Ben Campbell (musician)|Ben Campbell]] (a former member of the band [[Zed (band)|Zed]])<ref name="3 News1" /> and his sister Beth Campbell (a [[backing vocalist]] for the band [[Chicago (band)|Chicago]]),<ref name="3 News1" /><ref name=onenews1>{{cite web|last1=Yeoman|first1=Paula|title=Atlas cruises into the big time|url=http://tvnz.co.nz/content/1458746/3362663.xhtml|website=[[One News (New Zealand)]]|date=22 November 2007}}</ref> to 19-year-old American solo artist [[Sean Cunningham (musician)|Sean Cunningham]].<ref name=onenews1/> Together with Joe McCallum,<ref name=onenews1/> the group decided to form the band Atlas.<ref name="3 News1" />

Located in Auckland, New Zealand,<ref name=NZmusician1>{{cite web|last1=Thorne|first1=Richard|title=Feature: Atlas - Running To Follow Crawl|url=http://www.nzmusician.co.nz/index.php/ps_pagename/article/pi_articleid/1264|website=NZ Musician magazine|accessdate=9 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141109063155/http://www.nzmusician.co.nz/index.php/ps_pagename/article/pi_articleid/1264|archive-date=9 November 2014|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Atlas released their  second single "[[Crawl (Atlas song)|Crawl]]" in March, 2007, and on 5 March, "Crawl" debuted at #9<ref name=OfficialNZchart1>{{cite web|title=Singles 5 MARCH 2007|url=http://nztop40.co.nz/chart/singles?chart=1420|website=[[Official New Zealand Music Chart]]|accessdate=9 November 2014}}</ref> before moving up to claim the top spot of the [[Official New Zealand Music Chart]] the next week,<ref name=OfficialNZchart2>{{cite web|title=Singles 12 MARCH 2007|url=http://nztop40.co.nz/chart/singles?chart=1421|website=[[Official New Zealand Music Chart]]|accessdate=9 November 2014}}</ref> where it stayed for a total of seven weeks.<ref name=crawlchart>{{cite web|title=ATLAS - CRAWL (SONG) |url=https://charts.nz/showitem.asp?interpret=Atlas&titel=Crawl&cat=s |website=charts.nz |publisher=Hung Medien |accessdate=9 November 2014 |df=dmy }}</ref> Their next single was "Magic 8".

Atlas released their debut album ''[[Reasons for Voyaging]]'' on 19 November 2007,<ref name="3 News1">{{cite web|title=Full interview with kiwi band Atlas|url=http://www.3news.co.nz/entertainment/full-interview-with-kiwi-band-atlas-2007112015|website=[[3 News]]|date=20 November 2007}}</ref> entering the Official New Zealand Music Chart the following week at #4, which was its peak position.<ref name=NZChartalbums>{{cite web|title=Albums 26 NOVEMBER 2007|url=http://nztop40.co.nz/chart/albums?chart=1458|website=[[Official New Zealand Music Chart]]|accessdate=9 November 2014}}</ref>
