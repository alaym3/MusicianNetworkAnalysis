'''Ye Banished Privateers''' are a pirate band from [[Umeå]], [[Sweden]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://rateyourmusic.com/artist/ye-banished-privateers|title=Ye Banished Privateers discography|website=RateYourMusic|access-date=2017-10-27}}</ref>

Their songs are inspired by traditional Irish and Scandinavian folk music and their lyrics are mostly based on events that happened in the 17th and 18th century.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://capcreativity.wordpress.com/2017/01/10/ye-banished-privateers-more-than-music/|title=Ye Banished Privateers: more than music|date=2017-01-10|website=Capturing creativity|access-date=2017-06-29}}</ref><ref name=Goteborgs>{{cite news |last=Edstam |first=Fanny |date=October 9, 2017 |title=Piraterna Ye Banished Privateers intar Brännö |trans-title=The pirates Ye Banished Privateers take on Brännö |url=https://www.gp.se/kultur/musik/piraterna-ye-banished-privateers-intar-br%C3%A4nn%C3%B6-1.4712667 |language=sv |work=[[Göteborgs-Posten]] |access-date=August 3, 2019}}</ref> During their shows they portray a crew of pirates, wearing historical clothes and acting while playing, making the show itself a mix of music and theatre.<ref>{{cite news |last=Hanneken |first=Markus |date=July 17, 2019 |title=Stilecht und rockig: Piratennacht am Freitag auf der Meile |trans-title=Stylish and Rocking: Pirate Night on Friday on the Mile |url=https://www.wa.de/hamm/night-pirates-freitag-meile-suedstrasse-hamm-banished-privateers-12823668.html |language=de |work=[[Westfälischer Anzeiger]] |access-date=August 3, 2019}}</ref>

== Biography ==
The band was started in 2008 by [[Björn Malmrös|Björn Malmros]] and [[Peter Mollwing]], who gathered several other musicians with the idea of playing pirate folk music.<ref>{{Citation|last=Tommeks-Musikwelt|title=Interview mit Ye Banished Privateers|date=2015-10-05|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F_tj1qHP6uU|access-date=2017-06-29}}</ref>

Today, the band amounts to nearly 30 members.

Their first self-produced album, [[Songs and Curses]], was released on 19 September 2012 for the [[International Talk Like a Pirate Day]].

After playing several shows in their home country, they started touring Germany in 2014.

In the same year, on 12 September, they released The Legend of Libertalia<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/YeBanishedPrivateers/photos/a.655019457867340.1073741829.423150107720944/709476689088283/?type=1&theater|title=Ye Banished Privateers|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2017-06-29}}</ref> their second studio album, on [[Totentanz Records]].

In 2015 and 2016 they toured mainly in Sweden, Germany and the Netherlands.

They announced their third album in 2016,<ref>{{Citation|last=Under The Crossbones|title=Ye Banished Privateers on Under The Crossbones Pirate Podcast|date=2016-09-27|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x55f9IzdEl8|access-date=2017-06-30}}</ref> but the release was delayed until 30 June 2017. [[First Night Back in Port]] finally came out when they signed a deal with [[Napalm Records]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://bravewords.com/news/ye-banished-privateers-sign-worldwide-deal-with-napalm-records-new-album-coming-in-june|title=YE BANISHED PRIVATEERS Sign Worldwide Deal With Napalm Records; New Album Coming In June|last=bravewords.com|work=bravewords.com|access-date=2017-10-27|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Video premiere: "I Dream Of You" by Ye Banished Privateers |url=https://sonic-seducer.de/index.php/en/3734-videopremiere-i-dream-of-you-von-ye-banished-privateers.html |work=[[Sonic Seducer]] |date=June 16, 2017 |access-date=August 3, 2019}} </ref>

In 2020 Ye Banished Privateers released their fourth album Hostis Humani Generis.<ref>Virginia Langum, How to Train Pirates, 26.04.2020, retrieved on 02.07.2020 https://www.umeatoday.com/how-to-train-pirates-umeas-ye-banished-privateers-swashbuckle-through-pandemic-with-kung-fu/</ref> The name of the album, Latin for "enemy of mankind"<ref>Dr Sonja Schillings talks to Tatiana Prorokova; Hostis Humani Generis- Enemy of all mankind? 25.08.2015, retrieved on 02.07.2020 http://podacademy.org/podcasts/hostis-humani-generis-enemy-of-all-mankind/</ref> is a term that was often used to describe maritime pirates; outsiders of society and a threat to the status quo.<ref>Salvatore Poier, 2009. Hostis Humani Generis: History of a multi-faceted Word https://www.academia.edu/692947/Hostis_Humani_Generis_History_of_a_multi-faceted_Word</ref> Due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic|coronavirus pandemic]], the band was not able to release the album as planned and live-streamed a concert instead, an online event to which 12000 fans participated.<ref>Virginia Langum, How to Train Pirates, 26.04.2020, retrieved on 02.07.2020 https://www.umeatoday.com/how-to-train-pirates-umeas-ye-banished-privateers-swashbuckle-through-pandemic-with-kung-fu/</ref>
