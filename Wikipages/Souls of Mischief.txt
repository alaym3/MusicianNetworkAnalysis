'''Souls of Mischief''' is a hip hop group from [[Oakland, California]], that is also part of the [[hip hop collective]] [[Hieroglyphics (band)|Hieroglyphics]]. The Souls of Mischief formed in 1991 and is composed of rappers [[A-Plus (rapper)|A-Plus]], [[Opio]], [[Phesto]], and [[Tajai]].

==History==
East Oakland native Tajai began rhyming with future bandmate A-Plus at age 8, while both were in [[elementary school]].<ref name="Tajai Interview">{{cite web | url = http://www.gorillamovement.com/Tajai.html | access-date = 2007-02-03 | title = Tajai Interview | publisher = Gorilla Movement.com | year = 2005}}</ref> Tajai and Phesto met later in [[junior high school]].<ref name="Phesto Interview">{{cite web | url = http://ensim.hieroglyphics.com/artists/souls/phesto-interview.html | access-date = 2007-02-03 | title = Phesto Interview | publisher = StinkE Productions | date = 1996-07-18 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20060719094917/http://ensim.hieroglyphics.com/artists/souls/phesto-interview.html | archive-date = 2006-07-19 }}</ref> Tajai recruited his best friend Phesto in middle school and A-Plus brought Opio into the budding act in [[high school]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mag.citizensofhumanity.com/blog/2015/12/10/souls-of-mischief/ |title=Souls Of Mischief - Humanity Magazine |website=Mag.citizensofhumanity.com |date=2020-03-02 |access-date=2020-03-06}}</ref> before making its [[major record label|major-label]] debut on [[Jive Records]] with the group's well-received album ''[[93 'til Infinity]]'', in 1993.<ref name="Phesto Interview"/>

The group is part of the hip-hop collective [[Hieroglyphics (band)|Hieroglyphics]], along with [[rapping|emcees]] [[Del tha Funkee Homosapien]], Casual, [[Pep Love]], Jaybiz, and [[record producer|producer]] [[Domino (producer)|Domino]].<ref name="Phesto Interview"/> The group participated as full members in all three of Hieroglyphics' studio albums: 1998's ''[[3rd Eye Vision]]'', [[2003 in music|2003's]] ''[[Full Circle (Hieroglyphics album)|Full Circle]]'', and 2013's [[The Kitchen (album)|The Kitchen]].

''93 ’til Infinity'' is the group's highest charting album to date (#17 Top R&B/Hip Hop Albums; #85 [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]) and, in January, 1998, was named one of the Top 100 Rap albums by ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]'' magazine.

The group stayed with Jive for one more album, 1995's ''[[No Man's Land (Souls of Mischief album)|No Man's Land]]'', before being released from the label during the same year.

The group released two albums on Hiero's self-owned [[independent record label|independent label]], [[Hieroglyphics Imperium Recordings]]: 1999's ''[[Focus (1999 album)|Focus]]'', and [[2000 in music|2000's]] ''[[Trilogy: Conflict, Climax, Resolution]]''.

On October 30, 2006, Hierocast (Hieroglyphics' official [[podcast]]), Del tha Funkee Homosapien reported that the Souls of Mischief was currently working on its next studio album with [[Prince Paul (producer)|Prince Paul]] ([[De La Soul]], [[Gravediggaz]], [[Handsome Boy Modeling School]]).{{Citation needed|date=February 2007}}

On January 6, 2009, Souls of Mischief released the ''Tour Stories'' EP produced by Domino and co-produced by Prince Paul. The song is taken from the group's fifth studio album, ''Montezuma's Revenge'', released on Hieroglyphics Imperium.

In 2013, a documentary about the band was released entitled ''Til Infinity: The Souls of Mischief''.<ref>{{Citation|last=Smith|first=Shomari|title=Til Infinity: The Souls of Mischief|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6314468/|type=Documentary|others=Stretch Armstrong, Adisa Banjoko, Yasiin Bey, Casual|publisher=SkyFre Productions|access-date=2020-10-31}}</ref> Produced and directed by Shomari Smith, it reflected on the 20 year anniversary of the band's debut album ''93 'Til Infinity'' and includes over 50 interviews with prominent MCs, DJs, and producers of the era.

During the [[2017 NBA Playoffs]], [[Gatorade]] released a television commercial promoting Gatorade Flow, which featured [[Indiana Pacers]] star forward [[Paul George]] and the instrumental music of Souls of Mischief' ''93 'Til Infinity''.<ref name="Ball is Life Article">{{cite web | url = http://ballislife.com/paul-george-gatorade/ | access-date = 2017-04-20 | title = Ball is Life Article | website = ballislife.com | year = 2017}}</ref>
