'''Jordan Mark Witzigreuter''' (born November 14, 1989), known professionally as '''Onlychild''' and formerly as '''The Ready Set''', is an American singer-songwriter from [[Fort Wayne, Indiana]], United States. He is the lead vocalist and sole member of the act, using a backup band while on tour. He has released five studio albums, ''Syntax and Bright Lights'', ''Tantrum Castle'', ''[[I'm Alive, I'm Dreaming]]'', ''[[The Bad & the Better]]'', and ''[[I Will Be Nothing Without Your Love]],'' eight extended plays and fourteen singles. He is best known for the commercially successful single "[[Love Like Woe]]" from his major label debut ''I'm Alive, I'm Dreaming''.

As of July 2019, Witzigreuter has begun a new project, now using the name "Onlychild." He has released twelve singles and a four-track EP Solstice under this name.<ref>{{cite web |title=Onlychild |url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCK2J4l5sLY5dVQ5p1eDrFuA/videos?disable_polymer=1 |website=YouTube.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=onlyonlychild |url=https://www.instagram.com/onlyonlychild/ |website=Instagram.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=GONE FISHKIN Announcement |url=https://twitter.com/thereadyset |website=Twitter.com |publisher=Fish}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=iamonlychild |url=https://twitter.com/iamonlychild |website=Twitter.com}}</ref>

Witzigreuter is also a part of the [[synthpop]] band [[Nekokat]], alongside [[Cameron Walker-Wright|Cameron Walker]], and Jess Bowen of [[The Summer Set]].

In 2021, in collaboration with [[Cameron Walker-Wright]], Witzigreuter created the music label ''Swim Team Records''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Swim Team Records YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCP656wZ7IeksuRRH2wRaNiw|url-status=live}}</ref> The company has featured a multitude of artists such as Future Coyote, TALKBAK, and Casey Abrams.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Swim Team Records Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/swimteamrecords/|url-status=live}}</ref> The label's music has been featured in promotion for things such as the PGA Tour.<ref>{{Cite web|title="Get To Work" PGA Tour|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CJwu_wBlO52/|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Early life==
Witzigreuter was born and raised in [[Fort Wayne, Indiana]], a location he frequently mentions in his music. At the age of 10, his mother signed him up for drum lessons. This led to him playing the drums in bands at the age of 14. After becoming acquainted with other instruments, he began touring with bands at the age of 16. As a seventeen-year-old, Witzigreuter participated in various bands playing drums for the [[ska|ska band]] Take Sides and the [[hardcore punk|hardcore band]] Saints Never Surrender.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6-eFI1e8Zhw|title=Ask The Ready Set 4|access-date=December 27, 2020}}</ref> He was also in the [[acoustic music|acoustic band]] brideandgroom.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reverbnation.com/brideandgroom|title=Brideandgroom|access-date=January 13, 2021}}</ref>

==Career==

===2007–09: ''Syntax and Bright Lights'' and ''Tantrum Castle''===
In 2007, Witzigreuter began making music independently under the name "The Ready Set." His first two albums ''Syntax and Bright Lights'' and ''Tantrum Castle'' were recorded in the basement of his childhood home. Initially uploading his music to [[Myspace]], Witzigreuter then began performing his music live. His first performances took place at The Mocha Lounge in [[Fort Wayne, Indiana]], a coffee shop where he worked.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0bSwuyikGZg|title=The Ready Set - Sixty-Eight (Live)|access-date=January 13, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ie3WLuaI8iA|title=The Ready Set - The Scientist (Live)|access-date=January 13, 2021}}</ref> His performances evolved to that of a live band, and he began performing for music festivals. These included [[The Bamboozle]] and [[Bamboozle Left]], the [[The Bamboozle|Bamboozle Roadshow]], and the [[MTV Video Music Award|MTV VMA Tour]].<ref>{{cite web |title=The Ready Set - MTV VMA TOUR!!! (Episode 8) [NO SECOND CHANCES] |url=https://www.bing.com/videos/search?q=mtv+vma+ready+set&view=detail&mid=64E00871786204286B3364E00871786204286B33&FORM=VIRE |website=YouTube.com |publisher=The Ready Set}}</ref> He also opened for bands like [[Boys Like Girls]].<ref>{{cite web |title=The Ready Set artist profile |url=https://www.last.fm/music/The+Ready+Set |website=last.fm}}</ref>

The Ready Set has also made performances on [[MTV]] on multiple occasions, including an appearance on MTV's ''TJ Search Live Finale'' and being the "One to Watch" on MTV's 10 on Top. Witzigreuter has also been featured twice on MTV's ''Buzzworthy''.

===2010–11: ''I’m Alive, I’m Dreaming''===
[[File:The Ready Set - live 2010.jpg|thumb|upright=0.85|The Ready Set performing at Emo's in 2010.]] 

[[Pete Wentz]] signed Witzigreuter to his label [[DCD2 Records|Decaydance Records]] in 2009.<ref name="a">{{cite web|url=http://underthegunreview.net/2009/11/24/thereadysetsignsandstreams|title=The Ready Set signs to Decaydance Records & Sire|publisher=Underthegunreview.net}}</ref> He would release two singles [[Love Like Woe]] and More Than Alive. Love Like Woe peaked at number 27 on ''[[Billboard Hot 100]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/HSI|title=The Ready Set Chart History - (Hot 100)|access-date=July 10, 2021}}</ref> The song lasted 21 weeks on chart and is certified platinum by [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=The+Ready+Set#search_section|title=RIAA – Gold & Platinum – November 29, 2010: The Ready Set certified singles|publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]|access-date=November 29, 2010}}</ref> This would mark his first song to have received a certification. It also peaked at number 30 on [[Billboard charts|Canada CHR/Top 40]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/CTF|title=The Ready Set Chart History – (Canada CHR/Top 40)|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|access-date=December 19, 2020}}</ref> The second single More Than Alive was released on May 25, 2010. ''[[I'm Alive, I'm Dreaming]]'', peaked at number 3 on US ''[[Top Heatseekers]]'' albums chart lasting 23 weeks chart and peaked at number 172 on Billboard Current Album Sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/heatseekers-albums|title=The Ready Set – Chart History: Heatseekers Albums|work=Billboard|access-date=January 7, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/TCL/song/657291|title=The Ready Set Chart History: Current Album Sales|access-date=December 19, 2020}}</ref>

On November 2010, his single, Love Like Woe was one of the recipients for the [[Broadcast Data Systems|BDS Spin Awards]] after receiving 50,000 spins.<ref name="BDS">{{cite web|url=https://worldradiohistory.com/Archive-All-Music/Billboard/00s/2011/BB-2011-02-05.pdf|title=BDS Certified Spin Awards November 2010|p=24|access-date=August 23, 2021}}</ref> On November 26, 2010, he released his Christmas EP, "Regifted".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/albums/The-ready-set/Regifted|title=The Ready Set - Regifted|website=Genius.com|access-date=December 26, 2020}}</ref> His song Wishlist from the EP, peaked at number 32 on Billboard [[Billboard charts|Holiday Digital Song Sales]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/XDT|title=The Ready Set Chart History - Holiday Digital Song Sales|access-date=December 19, 2020}}</ref> In 2011, he embarked on his first headlining tour called, The Glamour Kills Tour. Following the release of ''I’m Alive, I’m Dreaming'', he released two other singles from his ''[[Feel Good Now EP]]''. [[Young Forever (The Ready Set song)|Young Forever]] peaked at 39 on US Mainstream Top 40 and number 5 on US ''Bubbling Under Hot 100''.<ref name="Top40">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/TFM|title=The Ready Set Album & Song Chart History – Pop Songs|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|access-date=November 29, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/HBU|title=The Ready Set Chart History: Bubbling Under Hot 100|website=Billboard|access-date=2021-01-14}}</ref> Hollywood Dream was the second single released on August 22, 2011. ''Feel Good Now'' peaked at 179 on ''[[Billboard 200]]''.<ref name="Billboard200">{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/billboard-200|title=The Ready Set – Chart History: Billboard 200 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 7, 2018}}</ref> In 2012, Witzigreuter was featured in an episode of [[Disney Channel|Disney Channel's]] ''[[So Random!]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Ready set performs Young forever on So Random |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K1g1wqaeimo |website=YouTube.com |publisher=Disney}}</ref>

===2012–15: ''The Bad & The Better''===
[[File:The_Ready_Set_-_11.jpg|thumb|left|upright=0.85|The Ready Set performing live in 2011.]]

After leaving Decaydance following his second album, he subsequently signed with the label Razor & Tie, releasing ''[[The Bad & The Better]]'' which peaked at 75 on ''Billboard 200''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://razorandtie.com/the-ready-set-signs-to-razor-tie/|title=THE READY SET SIGNS TO RAZOR & TIE : Razor & Tie|website=razorandtie.com|access-date=2019-01-14}}</ref><ref name="b">{{cite web|url=http://decaydance.com/blog/we-have-a-new-addition-to-the-family|title=We Have a New Addition to the Family!|publisher=Decaydance.com|access-date=2010-05-08}}</ref><ref name="Billboard200"/> He released [[Give Me Your Hand]] in 2012 which peaked at number 30 on US Mainstream Top 40 and number 33 in New Zealand.<ref name="Top40"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://nztop40.co.nz/chart/singles?chart=2144|title=NZ Top 40 Singles Chart|access-date=December 19, 2020}}</ref> Higher, Freakin Me Out, and Fangz were other singles from the album. He would serve as an act on [[Warped Tour]] in 2014. He would headline a tour with [[Metro Station (band)|Metro Station]] called "The Outsiders Tour" with supporting acts from [[The Downtown Fiction]] and [[Against The Current (band)|Against The Current]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://idobi.com/contests/the-outsiders-tour-giveaway/|title=A signed record from The Ready Set or Metro Station|access-date=June 28, 2021}}</ref> In 2015, Witzigreuter started a side project band [[Nekokat]] with artist [[Cameron Walker-Wright]] and drummer Jess Bowen.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/the_ready_set_summer_set_members_unveil_new_nekokat_music_video_exclusive/|title=THE READY SET, SUMMER SET MEMBERS UNVEIL NEW NEKOKAT MUSIC VIDEO (EXCLUSIVE)|access-date=December 26, 2020}}</ref> 

===2016–18: ''I Will Be Nothing Without Your Love'' and ''V1 & V2''===
In 2016, Witzigreuter signed with [[Hopeless Records]] and released ''[[I Will Be Nothing Without Your Love]]'' that same year. The album peaked at number 24 on the US ''[[Independent Albums|Independent Albums Chart]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/independent-albums|title=The Ready Set – Chart History: Independent Albums |work=Billboard|access-date=January 7, 2018}}</ref> He would go on to release two singles, [[Good Enough (The Ready Set song)|Good Enough]] and Disappearing Act. He collaborated with Haitian DJ [[Michael Brun]] and made a remix to the song Good Enough as it peaked at number 40 on US '' Dance/Mix Show Airplay''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-ready-set/chart-history/DNC|title=The Ready Set Chart History – Dance Songs|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|access-date=December 19, 2020}}</ref> Witzigreuter stated in a 2016 interview with the [[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] that he almost ended The Ready Set following the release of his album The Bad & The Better.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ayGE26dyBG4|title=The Ready Set - I Will Be Nothing Without Your Love Interview|access-date=December 19, 2020}}</ref>

Following these albums, he began to release music independently in 2018 with two extended plays: ''V1'' and ''V2''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://earmilk.com/2018/08/09/electro-pop-sensation-the-ready-set-talks-creativity-for-v2-ep-interview-premiere/|title=Electro-pop sensation The Ready Set talks creativity for 'V2' EP [Interview + Premiere]|last=Cihak|first=Lennon|date=2018-08-09|website=EARMILK|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-14}}</ref> His single off of V1, Life In Pink was released on February 15, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8099897/the-ready-set-life-in-pink-premiere-new-ep-2018|title=The Ready Set Premieres Radiant Guitar-Pop Track 'Life in Pink,' Announces New EP|author=Chris Payne|website=Billboard.com|access-date=December 26, 2020}}</ref> He also released another single off of V2, Stitch, on July 27, 2018. V3 was originally supposed to be released in October 2018 but the EP was never released. During that time The Ready Set had collaborated with multiple other artists.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/2eRJjYEaWyGZbOBGYFLBoC/discography|title=The Ready Set Spotify|access-date=December 26, 2020}}</ref>

===2019–present: Onlychild===
In mid-2019, Witzigreuter adopted a new name, Onlychild. He would release his first EP "Solstice" under that name on July 27, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.galaxy-mag.com/post/onlychild-released-solstice|title=Onlychild released his debut EP "Solstice!"|access-date=December 26, 2020}}</ref> In 2020, he released a non-album single Chardonnay and Tangerine on May.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://youtube.com/c3mC69W6rr8|title=Onlychild- Chardonnay & Tangerine (Official Video)|access-date=December 26, 2020}}</ref> On October 23, 2020, Witzigreuter released another single Thank Me Later.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://youtube.com/z5jLqrwgduQ|title=Onlychild- Thank Me Later (Official Video)|access-date=December 26, 2020}}</ref> On April 16, 2021, he released his new single Sad Fantasy which featured [[Panic! at The Disco]] guitarist, Mike Naran.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=fVhPpthtFr0|title=Onlychild- Sad Fantasy|access-date=April 17, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/iamonlychild/status/1381710934163345408?s=20|title=Onlychild |work= Twitter |access-date=April 17, 2021}}</ref>

In 2021, Witzigreuter and Nekokat collaborator Cameron Walker contributed writing credits to the single "Rock with You" on South Korean band [[Seventeen (South Korean band)|Seventeen]]'s mini-album ''[[Attacca (EP)|Attacca]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Dongre |first1=Divyansha |title=SEVENTEEN Embrace the Global Punk Resurgence on “Rock With You” |url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/seventeen-embrace-the-global-punk-resurgence-on-rock-with-you/ |website=Rolling Stone}}</ref>
