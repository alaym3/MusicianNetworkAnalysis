'''Marika Louise Hackman''' (born 17 February 1992) is an English vocalist, multi-instrumentalist and songwriter. She is considered to fall within the alternative and English-[[Folk music|folk]] genres, and is noted for her dark, melancholic lyrics.

Hackman has released a [[Mini-LP|mini-album]], ''That Iron Taste'' (2013), and four full-length albums: ''We Slept at Last'' (2015); ''[[I'm Not Your Man]]'' (2017); ''[[Any Human Friend]]'' (2019) and ''Covers'' (2020). She has also released four [[extended play|EPs]]: ''Free Covers'' (2012), ''Sugar Blind'' (2013), ''Deaf Heat'' (2014), and ''Wonderland'' (2016).

==Early life==
Hackman was born in [[Hampshire]], England and raised in [[Selborne]] and [[Devon]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Introducing: Marika Hackman |url=http://sodwee.com/blog/2013/04/introducing-marika-hackman/ |website=sodwee.com |date=13 April 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Thane |first=Rich |title=Introducing: Marika Hackman |url=http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/introducing/marika-hackman-116179 |website=The Line of Best Fit |date=16 January 2013}}</ref><ref name="interview2">{{cite web |last=Mitchell |first=Elliot |title=Introducing... Marika Hackman // Interview |url=http://whenthegramophonerings.com/2013/12/20/marika-hackman-interview/ |website=When The Gramophone Rings |date=18 February 2014}}</ref> Her mother and [[Finns|Finnish]] father met during their work as animators.<ref>{{cite news |last=Hemmings |first=Jeff |title=Music: Marika Hackman |url=http://thelatest.co.uk/brighton/2013/02/26/music-marika-hackman/ |website=Latest Brighton |date=26 February 2013}}</ref> Hackman watched little television as a child. She and her older brother, Ben, a dance music producer who releases material under the name "Hackman", were instead encouraged to find other creative outlets. Hackman is a self-taught guitarist and began learning when she was 12. She had lessons in piano from the age of 4, and lessons in the bass guitar and drums from the age of 10.

She received a scholarship to attend [[Bedales School]] as a day pupil from ages 11 to 17, where she met and befriended model [[Cara Delevingne]].<ref>{{cite web  |last=Huston |first=Carol |title= Marika Hackman interview |url=http://carolhustondotcom.wordpress.com/2013/02/10/marika-hackman/|website=CarolHustondotcom |date=10 February 2013}}</ref> Together, they formed a short-lived [[cover band]] called "The Clementines",<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nylon.com/articles/band-crush-marika-hackman-7689|title=BAND CRUSH: MARIKA HACKMAN|access-date=29 July 2017|language=en}}</ref> with Hackman playing drums and Delevingne playing guitar and singing. Hackman performed at [[Mulberry (company)|Mulberry's]] Park Lane launch dinner, celebrating the release of Delevingne's collection in February 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tatler.com/bystander/events/2014/february/mulberry-cara-delevingne-dinner#!/13548/image/1 |title=Mulberry's Cara Delevingne collection launch dinner |website=[[Tatler]] |date=17 February 2014 |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref>

At 18, she moved to [[Brighton]], England, where she studied art foundation for a year and planned on earning a degree in fine arts. Instead, she later chose to pursue music full-time.

==Music career==

===Early career (2012–2013)===
[[File:Marika Hackman 2.jpg|thumb|left|Marika Hackman at Rough Trade]]
Hackman's first release was an EP of covers, which was available for digital download in October 2012.<ref name="free covers folk radio">{{cite web|url=https://www.folkradio.co.uk/2012/10/marika-hackman-free-covers-ep/|title=Marika Hackman – Free Covers EP and Headline Tour|last=Gallacher|first=Alex|date=4 October 2012|website=[[Folk Radio UK]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190501233920/https://www.folkradio.co.uk/2012/10/marika-hackman-free-covers-ep/|archive-date=1 May 2019|url-status=live|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref> The following February, she released her first mini-album, ''That Iron Taste'', featuring songs of her own composition. The album was released in physical formats and as a digital download. It was produced by [[alt-J]]'s [[Charlie Andrew]]. In 2013, she toured Australia and Europe as an opening act for [[Laura Marling]], to support the release of ''That Iron Taste''. The music video for her song "Cannibal" was filmed at [[Bedales School]] in March 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://beyondbedales.wordpress.com/tag/marika-hackman/ |title=OB returns to film music video |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=13 March 2013 |website=Beyond Bedales |accessdate=4 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="interview4">{{cite magazine |last=Dunn |first=Francesca |title=Marika Hackman, Beautiful Folk |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_gb/article/marika-hackman-beautiful-folk |magazine=[[i-D]] |date=14 March 2014}}</ref> She later released two more EPs, ''Sugar Blind'' on 9 December 2013 and ''Deaf Heat'' on 4 April 2014, via iTunes. Both EPs contain songs written by Hackman as well as one cover song.

===Debut album: ''We Slept at Last'' (2014–2017)===
Hackman began recording her full-length debut album in 2014 with producer [[Charlie Andrew]]. She debuted new material from the album, including the songs "Skin" and "Ophelia", while on tour in 2013 with [[Laura Marling]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://birminghampromoters.com/tag/marika-hackman/|title=Marika Hackman To Play The Sunflower Lounge Birmingham |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=9 July 2014 |website=Birmingham Promoters |accessdate=22 August 2014}}</ref> In late September 2014, the album's title was announced as ''We Slept at Last'' and was released on 16 February 2015. The album features 12 new songs written by Hackman and does not feature any material from her previous EPs. Hackman embarked on a headlining solo tour throughout the UK in support of the album in November 2014.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://diymag.com/2014/10/15/marika-hackman-previews-debut-album-with-drown |title=Marika Hackman Previews Debut Album With 'Drown' |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=15 October 2014 |magazine=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |accessdate=4 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="dirtyhit">{{cite web |url=http://www.dirtyhit.co.uk/news/marika-hackman-announces-debut-album-we-slept-at-last/ |title=Marika Hackman Announces Debut Album 'We Slept At Last' |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=9 October 2014 |website=Dirty Hit Records |accessdate=10 October 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141015085113/http://www.dirtyhit.co.uk/news/marika-hackman-announces-debut-album-we-slept-at-last/ |archive-date=15 October 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The album's lead single, "Drown", premiered in mid-October and was officially released on 8 December 2014. On 17 November 2014, "Drown" was announced as a shortlisted contender for DJ [[Zane Lowe]]'s "Hottest Record of 2014" on [[BBC Radio 1]]. It ranked No. 89 out of 100 songs.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b04smyvm/ |title=Zane's 100 Hottest Records 100-76 |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=27 November 2014 |website=BBC Radio 1 |accessdate=8 December 2014}}</ref><ref name="BBCVote2014">{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/articles/264TPgB4vCVP2pkRrJZSM9Z/vote-for-your-favourite-hottest-record-of-2014 |title=Vote for your favourite Hottest Record of 2014 |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=21 November 2014 |website=BBC Radio 1 |accessdate=21 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="dirtyhit"/> She also appeared as a featured vocalist on [[alt-J]]'s song "Warm Foothills", from their second album "This Is All Yours". On 8 January 2015 she premiered the album's second single, "Animal Fear", for DIY Magazine. On 13 January 2015, "Before I Sleep", another track from her debut album, was streamed exclusively on the website ''[[Earmilk]]'', as well as her [[SoundCloud]] account. Hackman previewed material from the album in the days leading up to its release. The album is available to stream in its entirely on her official SoundCloud account, and features a distinctive blue colour scheme. The deluxe edition of the album includes all of the songs from Hackman's EPs.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/we-slept-at-last-deluxe-edition/id963805668 |title="We Slept at Last (Deluxe Edition)" by Marika Hackman |website=iTunes |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref> "Animal Fear" was released as the album's second single on 16 February 2015, the same day as the release of ''We Slept at Last''.

On 18 February 2015, Hackman played material from her debut album at The Cob Gallery in [[London Borough of Camden|Camden]], London. An art exhibit, featuring the album's artwork of 24 photographs, was displayed during the show. She embarked on a solo headlining tour throughout the UK during March and April 2015. [[Fenne Lily]] provided support to Marika during these dates, which were generally well-received by the press<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.drunkenwerewolf.com/reviews/marika-hackman-thekla-bristol-09042015/ |title= Fenne Lily, Sophie Jamieson, Marika Hackman @ Thekla, Bristol, 09/04/2015 |last=Daniels |first=Tiffany |date=10 April 2015 |website=Drunken Werewolf |access-date=8 October 2019|quote=Previous Marika Hackman gigs have conjured up a feeling of indescribable glee, and though tonight is no different, there's also a sense of accomplishment in the air. She's at the top of her game, but Hackman shows no signs of relenting}}.</ref> The tour continued with shows throughout Europe during the spring and summer of 2015.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://diymag.com/2015/01/08/marika-hackman-previews-new-album-with-the-stunning-animal-fear |title=Marika Hackman Previews New Album With The Stunning 'Animal Fear' |first=Jamie |last=Milton |date=8 January 2015 |magazine=DIY |accessdate=12 January 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.earmilk.com/2015/01/13/marika-hackmans-before-i-sleep-premiere/|title=Marika Hackman's new track "Before I Sleep" is dark in scope and narrative [Premiere] |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->  |date=13 January 2015 |website=EarMilk |accessdate=13 January 2015}}</ref>

On 25 June 2015, [[Laura Marling]] announced that Hackman and [[Johnny Flynn (musician)|Johnny Flynn]] would join her on tour for a series of concerts in North America during July and August 2015. The concerts would be Hackman's first time performing material in North America. Hackman's fourth single, "Next Year", was released on 14 August 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.clashmusic.com/videos/premiere-marika-hackman-next-year-acoustic |title=Premiere: Marika Hackman – 'Next Year' (Acoustic) |first=Robin |last=Murray |date=29 July 2015 |website=Clash Music |accessdate=29 July 2015}}</ref>

The album was generally well-received,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/we-slept-at-last/marika-hackman |title=We Slept At Last by Marika Hackman |website=[[Metacritic]] |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/we-slept-at-last-mw0002808041 |title=We Slept At Last – Marika Hackman |website=AllMusic |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref> with ''[[The Guardian]]''{{'}}s review rating it four out of five stars, and calling Hackman's work "superbly understated and atmospheric", noting that the "unsettling quality" was a distinguishing factor.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/feb/12/marika-hackman-we-slept-at-last-review |title=Marika Hackman: We Slept at Last review – superbly understated and atmospheric |first=Dave |last=Simpson |date=12 February 2015 |newspaper=The Guardian |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref>

===Second album: ''I'm Not Your Man'' (2017)===
Hackman signed with [[Sub Pop]] for her second album, ''[[I'm Not Your Man]]''. "Boyfriend", the first single from the album, debuted on Hackman's VEVO channel on 22 February 2017 in the form of a music video.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FyVBFCmTuUM |title=Marika Hackman – Boyfriend |author=MarikaHackmanVEVO |date=22 February 2017 |website=YouTube |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref> The album features the London band [[The Big Moon]] as backing vocalists and instrumentalists.<ref name="diymag.com">{{cite magazine|date=22 February 2017|title=Marika Hackman details new album 'I'm Not Your Man'|url=http://diymag.com/2017/02/22/marika-hackman-im-not-your-man-debut-album-details|magazine=DIY|accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref> The cover art was designed by [[Tristan Pigott]].<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.creativereview.co.uk/work/work-tristan-piggotts-cover-art-marika-hackman/ |title=Tristan Pigott's artwork for Marika Hackman album I'm Not Your Man |first=Rachael |last=Steven |date=21 February 2017 |magazine=[[Creative Review]] |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref>

The album was released 2 June 2017. ''The Guardian''{{'}}s four-star (out of five) review praised Hackman's "sweetly sung cut-glass vocals" and for having "risen from the alt-folk scene".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/jun/01/marika-hackman-im-not-your-man-review-amr-records |title=Marika Hackman: I'm Not Your Man review – pop with a sultry swagger |first=Kate |last=Hutchinson |date=1 June 2017 |newspaper=The Guardian |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref> The Observer's review (rating 3/5 stars) called the album "witty, raucous and honest", noting that Hackman, despite a new sound, "keeps the best of her former incarnation", adding to the "balance and variety" of the album.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/jun/04/marika-hackman-im-not-your-man-review-witty-raucous-and-honest-boyfriend-the-big-moon |title=Marika Hackman: I'm Not Your Man review – witty, raucous and honest |first=Emily |last=Mackay |date=4 June 2017 |newspaper=The Guardian |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref> ''Pitchfork'' declared the album "bracing" and "darkly funny", "melodically strong" and "full of surprises", giving a rating of 7.5/10.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/23158-im-not-your-man/ |title=Marika Hackman: I'm Not Your Man Album Review |first=Laura |last=Snapes |date=2 June 2017 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |accessdate=6 June 2017}}</ref>

===Third album: ''Any Human Friend'' (2019)===
On 23 April 2019, Hackman shared a teaser video with the caption "A _ _  H _ _ _ _  F _ _ _ _ _", hinting at the title of her upcoming third album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/marika-hackman-teases-new-music-with-short-clip|title=Marika Hackman teases new music with short clip|last=Kenneally|first=Cerys|date=23 April 2019|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190523044355/https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/marika-hackman-teases-new-music-with-short-clip|archive-date=23 May 2019|url-status=live|access-date=23 May 2019}}</ref> The following day, she released the lead single, "I'm Not Where You Are".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2041038/marika-hackman-im-not-where-you-are/music/|title=Marika Hackman – 'I'm Not Where You Are'|last=Rettig|first=James|date=24 April 2019|website=[[Stereogum]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190523052126/https://www.stereogum.com/2041038/marika-hackman-im-not-where-you-are/music/|archive-date=23 May 2019|url-status=live|access-date=23 May 2019}}</ref> On 22 May, the album's title was revealed to be ''[[Any Human Friend]]'', and it was made available to pre-order. It was released on 9 August 2019. The album was produced by Hackman and [[David Wrench (singer)|David Wrench]], and significantly features synthesizers. Hackman has described the lyrical content of the album as "quite sexual" and "blunt, but not offensive".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/marika-hackman-announces-new-lp-and-tour-shares-im-not-where-you-are/|title=Marika Hackman announces new LP and tour, shares 'i'm not where you are'|last=Pearis|first=Bill|date=22 May 2019|website=[[BrooklynVegan]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190523052527/http://www.brooklynvegan.com/marika-hackman-announces-new-lp-and-tour-shares-im-not-where-you-are/|archive-date=23 May 2019|url-status=live|access-date=23 May 2019}}</ref> She wanted to write about sexuality in a "unifying and sexy" way, in contrast to sexual lyrics that objectify the subject.<ref name="INWYA DIY">{{cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2019/04/24/marika-hackman-new-single-im-not-where-you-are-listen|title=Marika Hackman returns with 'i'm not where you are'|date=24 April 2019|website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY Magazine]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190503102901/http://diymag.com/2019/04/24/marika-hackman-new-single-im-not-where-you-are-listen|archive-date=3 May 2019|url-status=live|access-date=3 May 2019}}</ref> The second single from the album, "The One", was released on 13 June 2019. Hackman has described it as "probably the poppiest song I’ve ever written".<ref name="the one single">{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2047670/marika-hackman-the-one/music/|title=Marika Hackman Shares New Song 'The One': Listen|last=Rettig|first=James|date=13 June 2019|website=[[Stereogum]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190713074743/https://www.stereogum.com/2047670/marika-hackman-the-one/music/|archive-date=13 July 2019|url-status=live|access-date=13 July 2019}}</ref>

''The Guardian'', in a four-star review, observed that, on Hackman's "most accomplished record to date", she "flits between self-reflection and self-loathing" in "glorious songs" characterised by "a general wry frankness".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/aug/11/marika-hackman-any-human-friend-review|title=Marika Hackman: Any Human Friend review – frank breakup album|first=Tara|last=Joshi|date=11 August 2019|website=Theguardian.com|accessdate=13 August 2019}}</ref> ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' called the album "a singular, extraordinarily horny, and occasionally bleak pop record", about "those quiet moments of reckoning with what it means to be alive, young, and cautiously enamoured of it all", also observing Hackman, with her "coolly unimpressed alto" as "not interested in being coy or mincing words".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/marika-hackman-any-human-friend/|title=Marika Hackman: Any Human Friend|website=Pitchfork.com|accessdate=13 August 2019}}</ref> ''[[The Independent]]'''s review calls the album "blunt and bold" with a "dark sexual energy" on which "Hackman’s beatific voice sits atop methodically messy instrumentals".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/marika-hackman-review-any-human-friend-album-stream-tracklist-tour-a9045176.html|title=Marika Hackman's bold album Any Human Friend has a dark sexual energy|date=7 August 2019|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|accessdate=13 August 2019}}</ref>

For this album cycle, Hackman toured in the United Kingdom, Europe and the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Records|first=Sub Pop|title=Marika Hackman North American Tour Begins Tomorrow, October 11th in Support of 'Any Human Friend'|url=https://www.subpop.com/news/2019/10/10/marika_hackman_north_american_tour_begins_tomorrow_october_11th_in_support_of_any_human_friend|access-date=2021-02-16|website=Sub Pop Records|language=en}}</ref>

===Fourth album: ''Covers'' (2020)===
This album of covers was produced during Hackman's time in lockdown during the [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]] pandemic, including songs from [[Beyoncé]], [[The Shins]], and [[Elliott Smith]]. Released on 13 November 2020, the album was noted by NME in its four-star review to be "intimate and inventive", representing "soothing familiarity with the excitement of the new". Hackman "straddles the line between... well-known tunes and something fresher", the reviewer observing that "the results are gorgeous".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/marika-hackman-covers-album-review-2020-2815928|title=Marika Hackman – 'Covers' review: familiarity meets excitement of the new|website=Nme.com|date=13 November 2020|accessdate=11 February 2021}}</ref>
