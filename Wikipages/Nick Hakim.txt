'''Nicolas Hakim''' is an American musician based in [[Brooklyn, New York]], United States.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1943146/qa-nick-hakim-on-his-debut-album-nick-drake-dealing-with-pressure/franchises/interview/|title=Q&A: Nick Hakim On His Debut Album, Dealing With Pressure, & Nick Drake|date=2017-05-26|website=Stereogum.com|access-date=2019-07-16}}</ref> His first EPs, ''Where Will We Go Part 1 & 2'', were self-released in 2014. His debut album, ''Green Twins'' was released in May 2017, followed by his second studio-album, ''[[Will This Make Me Good]]'', released in May 2020.<ref>{{Citation|title=Green Twins by Nick Hakim|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/green-twins/nick-hakim|website=Metacritic.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-11}}</ref>

== Career ==
Nick was born and raised in Washington, D.C. by his parents, a Chilean mother and Peruvian father, where he was surrounded by a diverse array of musical influences. At 17, a friend invited him to sing with her church choir, where Nick taught himself to play piano. Soon after, he was accepted to attend Boston’s [[Berklee College of Music]], and whilst there, he released 2014’s two-part EPs, ''Where Will We Go''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Brown|first=Author Tony|date=2019-04-10|title=Featured Article: Who is Nick Hakim?|url=https://www.thebirn.com/featured-article-who-is-nick-hakim/|access-date=2019-12-22|website=Thebirn.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2017, Nick released ''Green Twins'' through ATO Records and toured internationally.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://atorecords.com/artists/nick-hakim/|title=Nick Hakim one of the Ten Best R&B Albums of 2017 by [[NPR]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/12/18/571211664/the-10-best-r-b-albums-of-2017|title=The 10 Best R&B Albums Of In 2020 he released his second studio album ''[[Will This Make Me Good]]''.<ref>{{cite web|author=Noah Yoo |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/nick-hakim-announces-new-album-shares-video-for-new-song-qadir-watch/ |title=Nick Hakim Announces New Album, Shares Video for New Song “QADIR”: Watch |website=Pitchfork.com |date=2020-03-31 |accessdate=2020-04-05}}</ref> Nick collaborated with [[Erykah Badu]] and contributed to her live stream concert series ‘Too Sensitive’ in September 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Erykah Badu Announces 'TOO SENSITIVE'|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwtv/article/Erykah-Badu-Announces-TOO-SENSITIVE-Series-20200925|url-status=live|website=Broadway World}}</ref> A remixed version of ''[[Will This Make Me Good]]'' was released in 2021 and included remixes from [[BadBadNotGood]], KeiyaA, Slauson Malone, Pink Siifu and more.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Badbadnotgood Remix Nick Hakim|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/badbadnotgood-remix-nick-hakim-qadir-listen/|url-status=live|website=Pitchfork}}</ref>
