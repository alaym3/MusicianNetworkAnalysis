'''Matthew Steven Maeson''' (born January 17, 1993)<ref name="riff">{{cite news|url=http://www.riffmagazine.com/features/matt-maeson-long-winding-road/|title=Matt Maeson found the straight and narrow on a long and winding road|work=Riff Magazine|last=Gokhman|first=Roman|date=September 26, 2018|accessdate=August 23, 2019}}</ref> is an American singer-songwriter and musician from [[Virginia Beach, Virginia]], signed to [[Neon Gold Records]] and [[Atlantic Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/matt-maeson-i-just-dont-care-that-much-video/|title=Matt Maeson captures '90s vibes in new music video—watch|work=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]|last=Quiles|first=Alyssa|date=April 5, 2019|accessdate=August 23, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Maeson was raised in [[Norfolk, Virginia]]. His parents were juvenile delinquents who later became "musicianaries", eventually playing in [[Christian metal]] bands. He was banned from listening to secular rock music on the radio until he was a teen. Matt has never served time in prison. He did community service by working construction for 12 hours a day and performed at biker rallies and prisons with his parents' ministry.<ref name="ones">{{cite web|url=https://www.onestowatch.com/artist/matt-maeson|title=Matt Maeson|work=Ones to Watch|accessdate=August 23, 2019}}</ref><ref name="riff"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.paradigmagency.com/music/matt-maeson/|title=Matt Maeson|work=Paradigm Talent Agency|accessdate=August 23, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==

In November 2016, Maeson released his debut single "Cringe". In 2018, Maeson performed at festivals such as [[Bonnaroo]], [[Lollapalooza]], and [[Forecastle]]. Prior to the release of his debut album ''Bank on the Funeral'' on April 5, 2019, he released two EPs, ''Who Killed Matt Maeson'' in 2017, and ''The Hearse'' in 2018.<ref name="ones"/> In January 2019, his song "Cringe" reached No. 7 on the [[Adult Alternative Songs]] chart. The song later spent four weeks at No. 1 on the Billboard [[Alternative Songs]] chart in August that year, the longest running number-one single by a new artist in 2019.<ref name="billboard">{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8527941/matt-maeson-cringe-no-1-alternative-songs-chart|title=Matt Maeson Earns First Alternative Songs No. 1 With 'Cringe' in Record-Breaking Week For Soloists|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Rutherford|first=Kevin|date=August 21, 2019|accessdate=August 23, 2019}}</ref> "Cringe" was certified gold by the RIAA on February 11, 2020. In August 2020, Maeson returned to number one on the ''Billboard'' Alternative Songs chart with "[[Hallucinogenics]]", which went on to top the ''Billboard'' [[Rock Airplay]] chart one week later. He was featured in [[Illenium]]’s 2021 single “Heavenly Side” from his upcoming second full length studio album.
