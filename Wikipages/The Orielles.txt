'''The Orielles''' are an English [[indie rock]] band from [[Halifax, West Yorkshire|Halifax]], [[West Yorkshire]], England.<ref name="theguardian">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/feb/17/the-orielles-one-to-watch-indie-band|title=One to watch: The Orielles &#124; Music|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=7 November 2018}}</ref><ref name="theguardian2">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/mar/20/new-band-of-the-week-the-orielles-no-144|title=New band of the week: The Orielles (No 144) &#124; Music|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|access-date=7 November 2018}}</ref><ref name="highclouds">{{cite web|url=http://www.highclouds.org/meet-the-orielles-the-band-that-revolutionize-rock-music/|title=Meet The Orielles, the band that revolutionize rock music|website=HighClouds.org|date=16 February 2018|access-date=7 November 2018}}</ref>
The band features Sidonie "Sid" B. Hand-Halford on drums, Esmé Dee Hand-Halford on bass and vocals and Henry Carlyle Wade on guitar and vocals.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Lester|first=Paul|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/mar/20/new-band-of-the-week-the-orielles-no-144|title=New band of the week: The Orielles (No 144)|date=20 March 2017|work=The Guardian|access-date=5 March 2020|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>

== Career ==
Their debut album, ''Silver Dollar Moment'', was released in February 2018 on [[Heavenly Records]].<ref name="thequietus">{{cite web|url=http://thequietus.com/articles/24049-orielles-silver-dollar-moment-album-review|title=Reviews: The Orielles|work=The Quietus|access-date=7 November 2018}}</ref> [[AllMusic]] described the album as a "stunning debut... it does have a uniquely sweet spirit and lighthearted beauty all its own by a young U.K. trio that gives the baggy indie dance sound of the early '90s a serious upgrade."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/silver-dollar-moment-mw0003129492|title=Silver Dollar Moment - The Orielles - Songs, Reviews, Credits|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=7 December 2018}}</ref>

Their second album, ''Disco Volador'', was released in February 2020 on [[Heavenly Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://heavenlyemporium.com/release/144894-the-orielles-disco-volador |title=The Orielles - Disco Volador. Vinyl LP, CD. Heavenly Recordings |website=Heavenlyemporium.com |access-date=29 February 2020}}</ref> It was produced by [[Marta Salogni|Marta Sologni.]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Disco Volador by The Orielles|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/disco-volador/the-orielles|language=en|website=Metacritic.com|access-date=5 March 2020}}</ref> ''[[The Guardian]]'' gave it a three out of five stars rating, describing the album as the band "expanding the sound of their winsome but somewhat conventional debut by abandoning traditional song structures and voyaging into new textures."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/feb/28/the-orielles-disco-volador-review |title=The Orielles: Disco Volador review – taking cosmic pop to new galaxies |newspaper=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=29 February 2020}}</ref> It has a Metacritic rating of 73, with critics commenting that the album mixes "the euphoria of whooshing through infinite space past astral displays of imagined beauty via a blend of disco funk, dream pop, electronic exotica and '70s highlife" (Uncut) and ''Q'' saying that the album is "both instantly appealing and dazzling inventive."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Disco Volador by The Orielles|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/disco-volador/the-orielles|website=Metacritic.com|access-date=5 March 2020}}</ref>
