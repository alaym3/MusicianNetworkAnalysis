'''Mahalia Burkmar''' (born 1 May 1998), known [[Mononymous person|mononymously]] as '''Mahalia,'''<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=2019-04-17|title=Mahalia - New Songs, Playlists & Latest News|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/5751a689-8b70-4ba0-a9e6-f8a10f4f8531|website=BBC}}</ref> is a British Jamaican singer, songwriter and actress.<ref name=":0">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-47854958|title=Mahalia: Drunk-dialling her way to fame|first=Mark|last=Savage|work=BBC News|date=9 April 2019|publisher=}}</ref> Mahalia has released several EPs and two albums, ''Diary of Me'' (2016) and ''[[Love and Compromise]]'' (2019). She also acted in the film ''[[Brotherhood (2016 film)|Brotherhood]]'' (2016).<ref>{{cite news | url = https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/new-music-to-listen-to-this-week-mahalia-a7390916.html | date = 1 November 2016 | accessdate = 6 April 2017| first = Roisin | last = O'Connor  | newspaper = [[The Independent]] | location = London | title = New music to listen to this week: Mahalia}}</ref> She had her breakthrough in 2017 with her performance of "Sober" for the [[YouTube]] channel [[Colors (YouTube channel)|Colors]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Smyth|first=David|date=2019-04-08|title=Mahalia: 'I was ready to quit music in 2017'|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/culture/music/mahalia-interview-sober-london-album-a4110101.html|access-date=2021-11-08|website=www.standard.co.uk|language=en}}</ref> In 2018, she was ranked number one on YouTube's Ones to Watch list.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|date=2018-11-13|title=YouTube: Mahalia tops its first ever Ones To Watch list|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-46183500|access-date=2021-11-08}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Mahalia is from [[Syston]], [[Borough of Charnwood|Charnwood]], in [[Leicestershire]].<ref name="frodsham-mercury">{{cite news | url = http://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/leicester-singer-mahalia-burkmar-to-star-in-brotherhood/story-29507263-detail/story.html | date = 13 July 2016 | accessdate = 6 April 2017 | first = I | last = Frodsham | newspaper = [[Leicester Mercury]] | location = Leicester | title = Leicester singer Mahalia Burkmar to star in Brotherhood | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160714144649/http://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/leicester-singer-mahalia-burkmar-to-star-in-brotherhood/story-29507263-detail/story.html | archive-date = 14 July 2016 | url-status = dead }}</ref><ref name="collins-id">{{cite web | url = https://i-d.vice.com/en_gb/article/10-things-you-need-to-know-about-mahalia | date = 10 December 2015 | accessdate = 6 April 2017 | first = Hattie | last = Collins | publisher = [[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] | work = i-D| title = 10 things you need to know about mahalia}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/syston-teenager-set-share-stage-double-brit/story-18587292-detail/story.html | date = 3 April 2013 | accessdate = 6 April 2017 | first = Gemma | last = Peplow | newspaper = [[Leicester Mercury]] | location = Leicester | title = Syston teenager set to share stage with double-Brit winner Emeli Sandé }}{{Dead link|date=March 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> She was born to musician parents.<ref name=":1" /> Her father is British-Irish and her mother is Jamaican.<ref>[https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/artists/mahalia-messaging-jodie-comer-cancel-culture-problem-brits/ Mahalia messaging Jodie Comer cancel culture problem - Telegraph]</ref> Mahalia has described the town she grew up in as a predominantly white area with "a lot of racism".<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title=Come My Way: Mahalia Interviewed|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/come-my-way-mahalia-interviewed|access-date=2021-11-08|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> She has a brother.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Singer Mahalia tipped as star by YouTube Music but 'avoids comment section'|url=https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/singer-mahalia-tipped-as-star-by-youtube-music-but-avoids-comment-section-37522559.html|access-date=2021-11-08|website=independent|language=en}}</ref>

She attended [[The Roundhill Academy|Roundhill Community College]] until she signed a record deal aged 13, when she transferred to a performing arts school,<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last1=Fresh|first1=Mikey|date=2017-10-24|title=Mahalia Talks About Her Journey From Unknown Singer To Major Label Recording Artist|url=https://www.vibe.com/music/music-news/mahalia-interview-uk-singer-sober-545474/|access-date=2021-11-08|website=VIBE.com|language=en-US}}</ref> Birmingham Ormiston Academy,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.othervoices.ie/content/qa-mahalia}}</ref> where she took acting classes.<ref name=":1" />

As a child she would often spend her summer breaks at home writing music{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}—she wrote her first song at age eight<ref name=":2" /> and signed a deal with [[Asylum Records]] at age 13.<ref name="collins-id" /><ref>{{cite web | url = http://blog.urbanoutfitters.co.uk/?p=30827 | date = 11 December 2015 | accessdate = 6 April 2017 | publisher = [[Urban Outfitters]] | work = UO Blog | title = Future Noise: Mahalia | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170708132104/http://blog.urbanoutfitters.co.uk/?p=30827 | archive-date = 8 July 2017 | url-status = dead }}</ref><ref name=":1" /> She performed live for the first time aged "eleven, or maybe 12" at an open mic.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2018-12-14|title=The first with... Mahalia|url=https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/article/mahalia-interview|access-date=2021-11-08|website=British GQ|language=en-GB}}</ref> She released her debut EP, ''Head Space'', in 2012.<ref name=":3" /> Mahalia was later introduced to [[Ed Sheeran]],<ref name=":4" /> who she went on to perform as a supporting act for.<ref name=":1" />

In 2019, Mahalia was featured on [[BBC Radio 1]]'s Brit List.<ref name=":0" /> Her album ''Love and Compromise'' was released on marketed as her debut album. The album concept was influenced by an interview with [[Eartha Kitt]], which Mahalia's mother showed her when she was a child.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-09-07|title=Mahalia's not compromising her self-awareness|url=https://gal-dem.com/mahalia-interview-love-and-compromise/|access-date=2021-11-08|website=gal-dem}}</ref>

Mahalia won Best Female Act and Best R&B/Soul Act at the 2020 [[MOBO Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-17|title=Listen to Mahalia's soulful new single 'Whenever You're Ready'|url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/news/music/listen-to-mahalias-soulful-new-single-whenever-youre-ready-2996492|access-date=2021-11-08|website=NME|language=en}}</ref>

In 2021, she released "Jealous", a collaboration with American rapper [[Rico Nasty]].<ref name=":5" /> She was nominated for her first Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-15|title=Grammy awards 2021: the full list of winners|url=http://www.theguardian.com/culture/2021/mar/14/grammy-awards-2021-winners-list|access-date=2021-11-08|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref> Mahalia was included in the 2021 [[Forbes]] Under 30 list.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-08|title=Yomi Adegoke and other Black Brits honoured on Forbes 30 under 30 list|url=https://www.voice-online.co.uk/news/uk-news/2021/04/08/yomi-adegoke-nadine-white-and-other-black-brits-honoured-on-forbes-30-under-30-list/|access-date=2021-11-08|website=Voice Online|language=en}}</ref>

== Influences ==
Mahalia has listed [[Lauryn Hill]],<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|last=Callender|first=Samantha|date=2021-07-16|title=R&B Singer Mahalia Wants to Create Excellence|url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/art-books-music/a37046803/mahalia-whenever-youre-ready-interview/|access-date=2021-11-08|website=Harper's BAZAAR|language=en-US}}</ref> [[India Arie]],<ref name=":6" /> Ed Sheeran,<ref name=":4" /> and [[Kate Nash]]<ref name=":1" /> among her musical influences.
