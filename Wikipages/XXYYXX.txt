'''Marcel Everett''' (born October 31, 1995),<ref name="interview">{{cite web|url=http://www.speakersincode.com/2013/10/jam-of-day-shlomo-ghosts.html|title=Jam of the Day Shlohmo – Ghosts|last=Newcity|first=Dylan|publisher=Speakers in Code|access-date=June 11, 2014}}</ref> better known as '''XXYYXX''', is an [[Electronic music|electronic]] musician and record producer from [[Orlando, Florida]], formerly signed to the label [[Relief in Abstract]].

His style is likened to [[Clams Casino (musician)|Clams Casino]], [[Zomby]], [[Burial (musician)|Burial]], [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]] and [[The Weeknd]]. His musical influences include [[Lapalux]], [[Star Slinger]], [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]] and [[Shlohmo]].<ref name="acclaim">{{cite web|url=http://www.acclaimmag.com/music/artist-on-the-rise-xxyyxx/|title=Artist on the Rise: XXYYXX|last=Lopez|first=Seth|date=May 25, 2012|publisher=Acclaim Magazine|access-date=December 29, 2012}}</ref>

== Career ==
Everett released the 'Still Sound' mixtape in 2011. The next year at 16 years of age he would release his self-titled album, which included the hit 'About You' and gained him prominence within the [[indie music]] culture in late 2013.<ref name="interview" /><ref name="acclaim" /><ref name="SFWeekly" /><ref name="miaminewtimes.com">{{Cite news|url=https://www.miaminewtimes.com/music/xxyyxx-isnt-just-good-for-his-age-hes-just-good-8276746|title=XXYYXX Isn't Just Good for His Age — He's Just Good|first=Matt|last=Stieb|date=February 25, 2016|newspaper=[[Miami New Times]]|access-date=January 31, 2021}}</ref>

Everett has remixed for artists such as [[Tinashe]] and [[Usher (entertainer)|Usher]].<ref name="factmag1">{{cite web | url=http://www.factmag.com/2012/07/18/xxyyxx-remixes-rb-chanteuse-tinashe/ | title=XXYYXX Remixes R&B Chanteuse Tinashe | publisher=Fact Magazine | date=July 18, 2012 | access-date=December 29, 2012}}</ref><ref name="lyfstyl">{{cite web|url=http://lyfstylmusic.com/post/21598974537 |title=Usher – Climax (XXYYXX remix) |publisher=LYFSTYL |access-date=December 29, 2012 }}</ref><ref name="factmag2">{{cite web | url=http://www.factmag.com/2012/04/20/usher-climax-xxyyxx-remix/ | title=Usher – Climax (XXYYXX remix) | publisher=Fact Magazine | date=April 20, 2012 | access-date=December 29, 2012}}</ref> In 2014, he created the song "What We Want" for the video game ''[[Grand Theft Auto V]]''. This song was included for the expanded soundtrack that was a part of the re-release of the game on PC, [[Xbox One]], and [[PlayStation 4]] systems.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pcgamer.com/gta-5-pcs-expanded-soundtrack-revealed/|title=GTA 5 PC's expanded soundtrack revealed|first=Phil|last=Savage|website=Pcgamer.com|date=November 18, 2014|access-date=January 31, 2021}}</ref> He headlined the [[Noise Pop Festival|San Francisco Noise Pop Festival]] in February 2013.<ref name="SFWeekly">{{cite web|last=Port|first=Ian|title=Noise Pop 2013 Additions: Damien Jurado, XXYYXX, Wax Idols, Ceremony, and More|url=http://blogs.sfweekly.com/shookdown/2013/01/noise_pop_2013_additions_damie.php|publisher=SF Weekly|access-date=February 24, 2013}}</ref><ref name="SFChronicle">{{cite news|title=Noise Pop 21: XXYYXX, Teen Daze (DJ Set), DJ Dials, Nanosaur|url=http://events.sfgate.com/san_francisco_ca/events/show/307882787-noise-pop-21-xxyyxx-teen-daze-dj-set-dj-dials-nanosaur|access-date=February 24, 2013|newspaper=San Francisco Chronicle}}</ref>

In 2015, Everett released the single "Red",<ref>[http://hypetrak.com/2015/11/xxyyxx-red/] {{dead link|date=January 2021}}</ref> about which he commented that "I was trying to express a feeling, and I got that more effectively".<ref name="miaminewtimes.com"/>

He spent the majority of 2016 touring the U.S., playing notable festival gigs such as Shambhala Festival,<ref>[http://www.shambhalamusicfestival.com/full-shambhala-2016-lineup-revealed/] {{dead link|date=January 2021}}</ref> Beach Goth,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1887764/beach-goth-2016-lineup/news/|title=Beach Goth 2016 Lineup|website=Stereogum.com|date=July 13, 2016|access-date=January 31, 2021}}</ref> Mysteryland, and Ancient Future. He was also credited on Tory Lanez's 2016 single "Flex", featured on his album ''I Told You.'' <ref name="allmusic.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/flex-mw0002968754/credits|title=Flex - Tory Lanez &#124; Credits &#124; AllMusic|access-date=January 31, 2021|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref>

Samples from XXYYXX's tracks have been utilized by artists such as [[Chance the Rapper]] and [[SZA (singer)|SZA]], and in 2017 Everett began producing additional music once more.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://edm.com/music-releases/xxyyxx-i-dont|title=XXYYXX Releases the Atmospheric "I Don't," His First Track in Two Years|first=Nick|last=Yopko|website=EDM.com|access-date=January 31, 2021}}</ref>
