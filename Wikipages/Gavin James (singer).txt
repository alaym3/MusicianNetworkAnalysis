'''Gavin Wigglesworth''',<ref name="hopr"/> known professionally as '''Gavin James''', is an Irish singer-songwriter who has over 1.5 Billion global streams of his music to-date. Two time [[Choice Music Prize]] Irish Song of the Year award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.choicemusicprize.com/news.php?page=4|title=And the Winners Are ...|publisher=choicemusicprize.com}}</ref> and has had No.1 radio singles in Ireland, Brazil, Switzerland, Portugal and has 22 gold and platinum records in 8 countries. 

==Early life==
Gavin was born in Dublin on 5 July 1991 and grew up in a household surrounded by music. Gavins' dad would play [[Cat Stevens]], [[Sam Cooke]] and [[Bob Dylan]] whilst his sister Emma sang and toured with a musical in Ireland.<ref name="hopr"/> James' great-grandparents were opera singers and his grandfather was a stand-up comedian and whistler.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gavin James Plus Guest Support|url = http://www.brudenellsocialclub.co.uk/whats-on/gavin-james/|website = Brudenell Social Club|access-date = 4 February 2016|language = en-GB}}</ref>

James began playing music in a rock band at the age of 8 and writing his own songs. The rock band was short lived, after a falling out over an alleged "paranormal experience". However James continued to perform solo, playing numerous gigs for years and building his audience. By age 21 he had begun focusing on his songwriting and playing open mic nights. In 2013, James independently released the single "Say Hello", which won the Meteor Choice Award for Song of the Year. By 2015, James had signed deals with [[Sony]] in Europe and [[Capitol Records]] in the US.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gavin James signs to Sony Music in Europe – Collective Management & PR|url = http://collectivemgmt.com/gavin-james-signs-to-sony-music-in-europe/|website = Collective Management & PR|access-date = 4 February 2016|language = en-US}}</ref>

==Musical career==
In the spring of 2015, James appeared on US television's Shark Tank and released a four-track EP titled For You. Later that year, he was invited to open for chart-toppers [[Ed Sheeran]] and [[Sam Smith]] on their respective tours, and released singles including "Bitter Pill" from his full-length studio debut of the same name.{{citation needed|date=October 2018}}  Subsequently, ''Bitter Pill'' has won the [[Choice Music Prize]] for Best Single released in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|title = 2015 Shortlist {{!}} Choice Music Prize|url = http://choicemusicprize.ie/2015-shortlist/|website = choicemusicprize.ie|access-date = 4 February 2016|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160131224907/http://choicemusicprize.ie/2015-shortlist/|archive-date = 31 January 2016|url-status = dead}}</ref>

In May and November 2015, James appeared on [[James Corden]]'s ''[[The Late Late Show with James Corden|Late Late Show]]''<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gavin James renews acquaintances with James Corden {{!}} Music {{!}} News {{!}} Hot Press|url = http://www.hotpress.com/Gavin-James/news/Gavin-James-renews-acquaintances-with-James-Corden/15608416.html|website = Hot Press|access-date = 4 February 2016}}</ref> and [[Jimmy Kimmel]] in December 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gavin James to perform on Jimmy Kimmel tonight {{!}} Music {{!}} News {{!}} Hot Press|url = http://www.hotpress.com/Gavin-James/news/Gavin-James-to-perform-on-iJimmy-Kimmeli-tonight/16344203.html|website = Hot Press|access-date = 4 February 2016}}</ref> In January 2016 James appeared on the BBC One Show, C'est A Vous and RTÉ and was included in Buzzfeed's<ref>{{Cite web|title = 15 Artists To Look Out For in 2016|url = https://www.buzzfeed.com/spotifyuk/15-artists-to-look-out-for-in-2016#.sudzw0BKq|website = BuzzFeed|access-date = 4 February 2016}}</ref> and The Sun's 'Ones to Watch 2016' lists.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gavin James featured as 'One To Watch' by The Sun {{!}} Good Soldier Songs|url = http://goodsoldiersongs.com/news/gavin-james-featured-as-one-to-watch-by-the-sun/|publisher = goodsoldiersongs.com|access-date = 4 February 2016}}</ref>

He has been supporting act for [[Kodaline]], [[Tori Kelly]],<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gavin James: ‘There was no plan, let alone a master plan’|url = http://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/gavin-james-there-was-no-plan-let-alone-a-master-plan-1.2458857|website = The Irish Times|access-date = 4 February 2016}}</ref> [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]] and [[Ed Sheeran]] on their tours.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/stayingin/music/one-to-watch-gavin-james-10363833.html|title=One to watch: Gavin James|publisher=London Standard|date=3 July 2015}}</ref> 

[[Spotify]] selected Gavin James to be a 'Spotify Spotlight Artist of 2016,' and he has total streams of over 1.5 Billion. On 21 January 2016, James set off on his debut European headline tour, starting with three sold out back-to-back dates at the Olympia Theatre in Ireland. James travelled back to America to support [[Ben Rector]] on his US tour in March 2016. James' cover of [[David Bowie]]'s "[[Changes (David Bowie song)|Changes]]" relaunched the new Virgin Radio UK at 11&nbsp;am GMT on 30 March 2016, he later performed live and was interviewed for the show.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-03-30|title=VIDEO: We're back - relive the launch of Virgin Radio UK|url=https://virginradio.co.uk/highlights/video-were-back-relive-launch-virgin-radio-uk-1603302282|access-date=2020-06-15|website=Virgin Radio UK|language=en}}</ref>

On 7 October 2016, James sold out Shepherd's Bush Empire in London and he headlined his first arena show at [[3Arena]] on 9 December in Dublin and played to 15,000 fans.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kodaline and Gavin James set to rock the 3Arena tonight for 2FM Xmas Ball|url=https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/music-news/kodaline-and-gavin-james-set-to-rock-the-3arena-tonight-for-2fm-xmas-ball-35265838.html|access-date=2020-06-15|website=independent|language=en}}</ref> James also won the 'Breaking Artist' award at the 2016 Music Business Worldwide A&R Awards on 2 November.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-11-03|title=The A&R Awards: All the winners from a big night for the UK music biz|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/ar-awards-winners-big-night-uk-music-biz/|access-date=2020-06-15|website=Music Business Worldwide|language=en-US}}</ref>

James collaborated with Australian hip hop trio [[Bliss n Eso]] on the ARIA award-winning song four times platinum single "Moments" in 2017.

=== 2015–present: ''Bitter Pill'', ''Only Ticket Home'', and '' Boxes'' ===
Work began on James' debut album ''[[Bitter Pill (album)|Bitter Pill]]'' in 2015. It was recorded in London and Los Angeles with producers [[Fraser T. Smith]] and [[Cam Blackwood]]. James said of the album: "I've always wanted to hear how my songs would sound properly produced, but I didn't want them to lose the edge they had live. We recorded the song Bitter Pill first and once we found the sound for that the rest was easy." ''Bitter Pill'' was released in Ireland on in November 2015 and was subsequently certified Platinum. It was later released worldwide in March 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Gavin James – Biography {{!}} Billboard|url = http://www.billboard.com/artist/6448972/gavin-james/biography/|website = Billboard|access-date = 4 February 2016}}</ref> Album track "22" was later used in the 10th episode of the 13th season of the American TV series ''[[House Hunters]]''.

In October 2017, James released the lead single "Hearts on Fire" from his second album.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Hearts On Fire - Single by Gavin James on Apple Music|url = https://itunes.apple.com/ca/album/hearts-on-fire-single/1299371392|website = Apple Music|access-date = October 27, 2017}}</ref> In April 2018, he released the single "Always", which was a no. radio hit across Europe and Brazil.<ref name="Murtagh">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotpress.com/news/Gavin-James-releases-highly-anticipated-new-single-Always/21989168.html|title=Gavin James releases highly anticipated new single 'Always' {{!}} Music {{!}} News|work=[[Hot Press]]|last=Murtagh|first=Natasha|date=13 April 2018|access-date=14 April 2018}}</ref> Third single "Glow" was released in September 2018,<ref>{{Cite web|title = Glow by Gavin James on Amazon Music|url = https://www.amazon.co.uk/Glow/dp/B07GL59RJY|website = Amazon UK|access-date = September 6, 2018}}</ref> followed by the release of James' second studio album ''Only Ticket Home'' in October.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Only Ticket Home by Gavin James on Amazon Music|url = https://www.amazon.co.uk/Only-Ticket-Home-Gavin-James/dp/B07GL4VJ3L|website = Amazon UK|access-date = October 25, 2018}}</ref>

In October 2020, James released his album, "Boxes." It reached #1 on the Irish Recorded Music Association (IRMA) charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://irma.ie/index.cfm?page=irish-charts&chart=Albums|title=IRMA – Irish Charts|publisher=[[Irish Recorded Music Association]]|access-date=9 October 2020}}</ref>

===Singles and EPs===
The Mark McCabe remix of "Nervous (The Ooh Song)" has been streamed over 170 million times to date and certified double platinum in Ireland, Sweden, Norway and Holland.<ref>{{Citation|title=Nervous - The Ooh Song/Mark McCabe Remix|url=https://open.spotify.com/track/5FoYSQF0IaVwS6M9mlk6C4|language=en|access-date=2020-06-15}}</ref> Alan Walker remix of his single "Tired" has also reached over 200 million streams and his single 'Always" has reahed over 150 Million stream. 

==Charity==

James is a supporter of the [[Down Syndrome Centre]],{{fact|date=August 2020}} whose mission is to help children with Down Syndrome to reach their full potential by providing early intervention services and to create a family focused centre for parents of the children using the service.
