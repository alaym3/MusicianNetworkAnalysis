'''Aaron West and the Roaring Twenties''' is the solo project of [[The Wonder Years (band)|The Wonder Years]] frontman [[Dan Campbell (singer)|Dan "Soupy" Campbell]]. According to the project's Facebook page, it is defined as "a character study conducted through music". Campbell is taking on the persona of Aaron West. The album, ''[[We Don't Have Each Other]]'' was released in July 2014 and the EP [[Bittersweet (EP)|''Bittersweet'']] was released on May 20, 2016. Occasional singles were released by the group in between albums. [[Routine Maintenance (album)|''Routine Maintenance'']] is the second officially released album, on May 10, 2019.

==History==

===''We Don't Have Each Other'' (2014–15)===
{{Main|We Don't Have Each Other}}
[[The Wonder Years (band)|The Wonder Years]] frontman Dan Campbell began the project on May 22, 2014, via a video released by [[Hopeless Records]] titled "Aaron West and The Roaring Twenties - An Introduction To Aaron West".<ref name=RsAaron>{{cite web|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/the-wonder-years-soupy-announces-side-project|title=The Wonder Years’ Dan Campbell Announces Side-Project|work=Rock Sound Magazine|author=Rock Sound|date=May 23, 2014|accessdate=August 24, 2015}}</ref> With this project, Campbell pushed himself "to make a piece of fiction feel just as raw and personal as songs about my life".<ref name=RsAaron/> Campbell released the projects debut song, "Divorce and the American South", via ''[[The A.V. Club]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Anthony|first1=David|title=Aaron West And The Roaring Twenties take a southern approach to divorce in new video|url=http://www.avclub.com/article/aaron-west-and-roaring-twenties-take-southern-appr-205602|publisher=A.V. Club|accessdate=7 March 2015}}</ref> "You Ain't No Saint" was released as a single on May 27.<ref name=YANSsingle/> "Divorce and the American South" was released as a single on June 10.<ref name=DATASsingle/> The project's first album, ''[[We Don't Have Each Other]]'', was released on July 8 through Hopeless.<ref>{{cite web|title=Now Streaming: Aaron West And The Roaring Twenties - We Don’t Have Each Other|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/now-streaming-in-full-aaron-west-and-the-roaring-twenties-we-dont-have-each|publisher=Rock Sound|accessdate=7 March 2015}}</ref> The album received primarily positive reviews, with ''[[Alternative Press (music magazine)|Alternative Press]]'' giving it a rating of 4.5/5.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Manley|first1=Brendan|title=Aaron West And The Roaring Twenties - We Don't Have Each Other|url=http://www.altpress.com/reviews/entry/aaron_west_and_the_roaring_twenties_we_dont_have_each_other|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=7 March 2015}}</ref> On November 19, West released a music video for the song "Our Apartment" via ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Payne|first1=Chris|title=Aaron West and the Roaring Twenties (Wonder Years' Dan Campbell) Premieres 'Our Apartment' Video (Exclusive)|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6320046/aaron-west-roaring-twenties-our-apartment-video-wonder-years-dan-campbell-premiere|publisher=Billboard|accessdate=7 March 2015}}</ref> Campbell performed his project on five of the 2014 [[Vans Warped Tour]] dates.<ref name=RsAaron/> On March 4, 2015, it was announced that Campbell would be performing his project on all of the 2015 Warped Tour dates, on the acoustic basement stage.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sharp|first1=Tyler|title=Acoustic Basement performers announced for Warped Tour 2015|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/acoustic_basement_performers_announced_for_warped_tour_2015|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=7 March 2015}}</ref>

The album is a concept record detailing the worst year of Aaron's life from start to finish. The album consists of 9 original songs and 1 cover song, "Going to Georgia" by [[The Mountain Goats]]. Campbell is said to have added the song to the album not because the song continued the story, but because he liked the song.

=== ''Bittersweet'' and ''Orchard Park'' (2016–18) ===
{{Main|Bittersweet (EP)}}
On March 25, 2016, it was announced that an EP, titled [[Bittersweet (EP)|''Bittersweet'']], would be released on May 20.<ref name=AltPBittersweet>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/aaron_west_and_the_roaring_twenties_stream_new_song_announce_new_ep|title=Aaron West And The Roaring Twenties stream new song, announce new EP|work=Alternative Press|author=Ralph, Caitlyn |date=March 25, 2016|accessdate=March 25, 2016}}</ref> The EP's cover art and track listing was revealed, and one song on the EP, "'67, Cherry Red", was made available for streaming.<ref name=AltPBittersweet/> The release of the EP features vinyl pressings on three different colors, Green, Blue, and Red, attributing to the songs "Green Like the G Train, Green Like Sea Foam", "Goodbye, Carolina Blues", and "'67, Cherry Red" respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coldcutsmerch.com/collections/aaron-west-and-the-roaring-twenties-bittersweet-preorders/products/aaron-west-and-the-roaring-twenties-bittersweet-7|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161010215822/https://www.coldcutsmerch.com/collections/aaron-west-and-the-roaring-twenties-bittersweet-preorders/products/aaron-west-and-the-roaring-twenties-bittersweet-7|url-status=dead|archive-date=10 October 2016|title=Aaron West and the Roaring Twenties "Bittersweet" 7"|website=Internet Archive|access-date=11 October 2019}}</ref>

[[Orchard Park (song)|"Orchard Park"]] was released on October 5, 2017 as a non-album single. The song visualizes Aaron driving with his mother to spread his father's ashes at Orchard Park.

=== ''Routine Maintenance'' and live album (2019–present) ===
{{Main|Routine Maintenance (album)}}
The project's second LP, titled [[Routine Maintenance (album)|''Routine Maintenance'']], was officially announced on March 18, 2019 and was given a projected release date of May 10.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/aaron-west-the-roaring-twenties-announce-new-album-tour/|title=Aaron West & the Roaring Twenties announce new album & tour|website=BrooklynVegan|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-18}}</ref>

In January 2020, Campbell announced a UK tour that was scheduled to begin in May of that year.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Wilson-Taylor |first1=James |title=Aaron West and the Roaring Twenties have announced a UK tour |url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/aaron-west-and-the-roaring-twenties-have-announced-a-uk-tour |access-date=27 January 2021 |work=Rock Sound |date=29 January 2020}}</ref> Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the tour was rescheduled for August 2021.<ref>{{Cite tweet |author=Dan Campbell |user=ThisIsAaronWest |number=1298591024088924161 |title=We aren't giving up on bringing the full band AW20 experience to the UK!}}</ref>

On July 31, 2020, the project released ''Live From Asbury Park'', created from recordings of two December 2019 live performances.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Jay |first1=Melannie |title=Aaron West and the Roaring Twenties release bonus cover of Springsteen’s “Thunder Road” |url=https://thekey.xpn.org/2020/07/22/aaron-west-thunder-road/ |access-date=27 January 2021 |work=The Key |publisher=WXPN |date=22 July 2020}}</ref>

==Style and influences==
Timothy Monger of [[AllMusic]] wrote that the project "blended the passion and aggression of pop punk with more of an Americana and roots aesthetic."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/aaron-west-the-roaring-twenties-mn0003268944/biography|title=Aaron West & the Roaring Twenties {{!}} Biography & History |work=AllMusic|author=Monger, Timothy|accessdate=April 4, 2016}}</ref>

Campbelll said in an interview with ''[[Rock Sound]]'' that he was inspired by [[The Mountain Goats]]' ''[[All Hail West Texas]]'' and [[The Weakerthans]]' ''[[Reunion Tour (album)|Reunion Tour]]'', and the artists' ability to create "such real, whole characters that I found myself invested in their lives and their stories."<ref>{{cite news |title=The Wonder Years' Dan Campbell Announces Side-Project |url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/the-wonder-years-soupy-announces-side-project |access-date=30 January 2021 |work=Rock Sound |date=23 May 2014}}</ref>
