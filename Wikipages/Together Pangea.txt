'''Together Pangea''' (sometimes stylized as '''together PANGEA''') is an American rock band from [[Santa Clarita, California]] that is based out of [[Los Angeles, California]]. The group has released four full-length albums and is currently signed to [[Nettwerk|Nettwerk Records]].

==History==
Together Pangea was formed in 2009 when longtime friends William Keegan and Danny Bengston met drummer Erik Jimenez at Cal Arts.<ref name=LAWeekDrak/> The group began playing several shows on the [[California Institute of the Arts]] campus and as well as many local venues throughout [[Southern California]].<ref name=LAWeekDrak/><ref name=CityRoar/> Originally formed as Pangea, the group was later forced to change their name to Together Pangea.<ref name=CityRoar/> They released their debut record ''Jelly Jam'' in 2010 via Lost Sound Tapes.<ref name=CityRoar/> ''Living Dummy'', the group’s second full-length album was released in 2011 by the Burger Records label.<ref name=CityRoar/> Following the release of the album, they began performing with [[Ty Segall]], [[Mikal Cronin]], [[Wavves]], and [[The Black Lips]].<ref name=CityRoar/> Their first national tour, dubbed the Burgerama Caravan of Stars, saw them share the stage with Gap Dream, [[Cosmonauts]], The Resonars, and [[Curtis Harding]].<ref name=MTVHivePery>{{cite news|last=Ehrlich|first=Brenna|title=Together Pangea's Danny Bengston Talks Katy Perry And Erectile Dysfunction|url=http://www.mtvhive.com/2013/11/05/together-pangeas-danny-bengston-katy-perry-erectile-dysfunction/|accessdate=22 April 2014|newspaper=MTV Hive|date=5 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Levine|first=Joshua|title=The Burgerama Caravan of Stars 2013 Tour Hits Tucson Tonight|url=http://www.tucsonweekly.com/TheRange/archives/2013/10/15/the-burgerama-caravan-of-stars-2013-tour-hits-tucson-tonight|accessdate=22 April 2014|newspaper=Tucson Weekly|date=15 October 2013}}</ref>

In 2014, Together Pangea's third full length was released through [[Harvest Records]].<ref name=CityRoar>{{cite news|last=Ali|first=Reyan|title=Together Pangea, L.A. Band's Rock & Roll Rises With A Disorderly, Disillusioned Roar|url=http://www.cityweekly.net/utah/article-292-18806-together-pangea.html|accessdate=22 April 2014|newspaper=City Weekly|date=20 February 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Ehrlich|first=Brenna|title=Rock N Roll Is Not Dead - Here's Living Proof|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1725172/rock-n-roll-not-dead-heres-living-proof.jhtml|accessdate=22 April 2014|newspaper=MTV|date=2 April 2014}}</ref> Titled ''Badillac'', [[MTV]] called it one of the most anticipated indie releases of 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last=Ehrlich|first=Brenna|title=Broken Bells, St. Vincent and Skaters Top Most-Anticipated Indie Albums of 2014|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1720134/most-anticipated-indie-albums-2014.jhtml|accessdate=22 April 2014|newspaper=MTV|date=9 January 2014}}</ref> The band set out on a national tour with Mozes and The Firstborn following the album release.<ref>{{cite news|title=Together Pangea, Mozes & The Firstborn|url=http://www.portlandmercury.com/portland/together-pangea-and-mozes-and-the-firstborn/Event?oid=11856544|accessdate=8 May 2014|newspaper=Portland Mercury|date=27 February 2014}}</ref> Kicking off 2015 with a European tour throughout February and March, the following month saw the band support [[The Replacements (band)|The Replacements]] at the [[Hollywood Palladium]] in Los Angeles as part of their [[Back by Unpopular Demand]] tour.<ref>{{cite news|title=Together PANGEA ready THE PHAGE EP, tour (2015)|url=https://ghettoblastermagazine.com/news/together-pangea-ready-the-phage-ep-tour-2015/|accessdate=1 August 2020|website=Ghetto Blaster|date=13 August 2015}}</ref> Later that year also saw the release of the six-track EP, ''The Phage'' on 16th October, produced by [[Tommy Stinson]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Together PANGEA releasing new Tommy Stinson-produced EP, share "If You're Scared" (tour dates and stream)|url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/together-pangea-2/|accessdate=1 August 2020|website=Brooklyn Vegan|date=13 August 2015}}</ref>

In 2018, the band returned as a trio of Keegan, Bengston and Jimenez and released a single called "Non Stop Paranoia".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/together-pangea-share-potent-new-garage-punk-melter-non-stop-paranoia|title=Together Pangea Share Potent New Garage Punk Melter 'Non Stop Paranoia'|website=Clash Magazine}}</ref> In 2019, the trio released the ''Dispassionate'' EP, which featured the "Never Said I Wanna"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://buzzbands.la/2019/04/05/stream-together-pangea-never-said-i-wanna/|title=Stream: Together Pangea, 'Never Said I Wanna'|date=April 5, 2019}}</ref> single. In 2021, they released "Marijuana"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://buzzbands.la/2021/04/20/stream-new-4-20-appropriate-dispatches-from-sumeau-together-pangea-and-jhene-aiko/|title=Stream: New 4/20-appropriate dispatches from Sumeau, Together Pangea and Jhené Aiko|date=April 21, 2021}}</ref> and "Nothing to Hide", as previews to their fifth album ''DYE'', due to be released on October 22, 2021 by [[Nettwerk|Nettwerk Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://buzzbands.la/2021/06/07/video-together-pangea-nothing-to-hide/|title=Video: Together Pangea, 'Nothing to Hide'|date=June 7, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequence.net/2021/06/together-pangea-dye-nothing-to-hide-stream/|title=Together Pangea announce new album DYE, share "Nothing to Hide": Stream|date=June 3, 2021}}</ref><ref>[http://www.nettwerk.com/news/2021/together-pangea-announce-new-album-dye-out-oct-22-new-single-video-nothing-hide%E2%80%82%E2%81%9F%E2%80%81%E2%80%83%E2%80%83%E2%80%86%E2%81%9F]</ref>
