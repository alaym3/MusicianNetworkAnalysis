'''George Evelyn''' (born 15 January 1970), better known by his stage name '''Nightmares on Wax''' or '''DJ E.A.S.E.''', is an English DJ and record producer from [[Leeds]]. His music has been released by [[Warp (record label)|Warp Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2016/09/29/nightmares-on-wax-release-back-catalog-of-mixtapes-for-free/|title=Nightmares On Wax releases back catalog of mixtapes for free|work=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]|first=Miles|last=Bowe|date=29 September 2016|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> He is based in [[Ibiza]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.residentadvisor.net/news/31670|title=Nightmares on Wax lines up stateside DJ tour|work=[[Resident Advisor]]|first=Brian|last=Kolada|date=15 October 2015|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> Nightmares on Wax were originally a group consisting of Evelyn and John Halnon, and then later with Kevin Harper.

==Early life==
George Evelyn was born in [[Leeds]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2013/09/19/smoke-signals-nightmares-on-wax-talks-his-first-album-in-seven-years/|title=Smoke signals: Nightmares On Wax on his first album in seven years|work=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]|first=Tom|last=Jones|date=19 September 2013|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> His father and sister introduced him to the soul music of [[Quincy Jones]] and [[Curtis Mayfield]].<ref name=redeyeusa/> Later, he was fascinated by the hip hop tracks such as "[[Rapper's Delight]]" and "[[Buffalo Gals (Malcolm McLaren song)|Buffalo Gals]]".<ref name=redeyeusa>{{cite web|url=http://www.redeyeusa.com/artist/12315|title=Nightmares On Wax|work=[[Redeye Distribution]]|access-date=15 January 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090313105728/http://www.redeyeusa.com/artist/12315/|archive-date=13 March 2009}}</ref>

==Career==
Nightmares on Wax was originally formed in 1988 by George Evelyn as a group project with John Halnon, and later with Kevin Harper.<ref name=sfweekly>{{cite web|url=http://www.sfweekly.com/music/three-must-see-acts-this-weekend-drama-nightmares-on-wax-and-iceage/|title=Three Must-See Acts This Weekend: Drama, Nightmares on Wax, and Iceage|work=[[SF Weekly]]|first=Tim|last=Casagrande|date=6 June 2018|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> Their debut studio album, ''[[A Word of Science: The First and Final Chapter]]'', was released on [[Warp (record label)|Warp]] in 1991.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/4x8ygn/nightmares-on-wax-comes-full-circle|title=Nightmares On Wax Comes Full Circle|work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|author=DJ Pangburn|date=28 August 2013|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> After the release of the album, Harper left the group to pursue a career in DJing.<ref name=allmusic-bio>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/nightmares-on-wax-mn0000869417/biography|title=Nightmares on Wax - Biography|work=[[AllMusic]]|first=Sean|last=Cooper|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> [[Robin Taylor-Firth]] became a frequent collaborator with Evelyn.<ref name=sfweekly/>

The second studio album for Warp, titled ''[[Smokers Delight]]'', was released in 1995.<ref name=factmag2015/> It was placed at number 15 on ''[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]''{{'}}s "50 Best Trip-Hop Albums of All Time" list.<ref name=factmag2015>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2015/07/30/50-best-trip-hop-albums/|title=The 50 best trip-hop albums of all time|work=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]|first1=John|last1=Twells|first2=Laurent|last2=Fintoni|date=30 July 2015|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> Evelyn released ''[[Carboot Soul]]'' in 1999,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/5794-carboot-soul/|title=Nightmares on Wax: Carboot Soul|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Ryan|last=Schrieber|date=12 April 1999|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> and ''[[Mind Elevation]]'' in 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.popmatters.com/nightmaresonwax-mind-2496011700.html|title=Nightmares on Wax: Mind Elevation|work=[[PopMatters]]|first=Wayne|last=Franklin|date=28 August 2002|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> In 2005, Evelyn started his own record label Wax On.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/leeds/content/articles/2008/10/21/music_leeds_artists_1990s_feature.shtml|title=Leeds' Musical Heritage: 1990s|work=[[BBC]]|date=21 October 2008|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref>

He released ''[[In a Space Outta Sound]]'' in 2006,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/5795-in-a-space-outta-sound/|title=Nightmares on Wax: In a Space Outta Sound|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Jess|last=Harvell|date=20 March 2006|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> ''[[Thought So]]'' in 2008,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/12172-thought-so/|title=Nightmares on Wax: Thought So...|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Joe|last=Colly|date=10 September 2008|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> ''[[Feelin' Good (Nightmares on Wax album)|Feelin' Good]]'' in 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.residentadvisor.net/reviews/13674|title=Nightmares On Wax - Feelin Good|work=[[Resident Advisor]]|first=Kristan|last=Caryl|date=27 September 2013|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref>  ''[[Shape the Future]]'' in 2018,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.popmatters.com/nightmares-on-wax-shape-future-2529622638.html|title=Nightmares on Wax Has Environmental and Spiritual Concerns on 'Shape the Future'|work=[[PopMatters]]|first=Paul|last=Carr|date=30 January 2018|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> and ''[[Shout Out! To Freedom...]]'' (2021)
