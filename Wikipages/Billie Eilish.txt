'''Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O'Connell''' ({{IPAc-en|'|aI|l|I|S}} {{Respell|EYE|lish}};<ref name="BBCPirate">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-40580489|title=Billie Eilish: Is she pop's best new hope?|last=Savage|first=Mark|work=[[BBC News]]|date=July 15, 2017|quote="...It's eye-''lish'', like eyelash with a lish."|access-date=July 21, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170721111611/http://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-40580489|archive-date=July 21, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> born December 18, 2001) is an American singer and songwriter.<!--only occupation(s) that are integral to the person's notability per reliable sources; [[MOS:ROLEBIO]]--> She first gained public attention in 2015 with her debut single "[[Ocean Eyes (song)|Ocean Eyes]]", which was subsequently released by Darkroom, a subsidiary of [[Interscope Records]]. The song was written and produced by her brother, [[Finneas O'Connell]], with whom she frequently collaborates on music and in live shows. Her debut [[extended play]], ''[[Don't Smile at Me]]'' (2017), was commercially successful and charted within the top 15 in the US, UK, Canada, and Australia.

Eilish's first studio album, ''[[When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?]]'' (2019), debuted atop the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and reached number one in the UK. It was one of the best-selling albums of 2019, buoyed by the success of its fifth single and Eilish's first number-one hit on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], "[[Bad Guy (Billie Eilish song)|Bad Guy]]". In 2020, she co-wrote and performed the theme song "[[No Time to Die (song)|No Time to Die]]" for the [[James Bond]] film [[No Time to Die|of the same name]], which topped the UK singles chart. Her next singles "[[Everything I Wanted]]", "[[My Future]]", "[[Therefore I Am (song)|Therefore I Am]]", and "[[Your Power]]" each peaked within the top 10 in the US and UK; her sophomore album, ''[[Happier Than Ever]]'' (2021), reached number one in 25 countries.

Eilish has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Billie Eilish|several accolades]], including seven [[Grammy Award]]s, two [[American Music Awards]], one [[Guinness World Record]], three [[MTV Video Music Award]]s, and two [[Brit Awards]]. She is the youngest artist in Grammy history and second overall to win all four general field categories—[[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]], [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]], and [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]]—in the same year. She was named in ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine's inaugural [[Time 100#Time 100 Next list|''Time'' 100 Next list]] in 2019, and made it into the main [[Time 100|''Time'' 100 list]] in 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2021-09-15|title=Billie Eilish, Lil Nas X, Bad Bunny Make 2021 Time 100 List|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/billie-eilish-lil-nas-x-bad-bunny-make-2021-time-100-list/|access-date=2021-09-16|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref> According to the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] and ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', Eilish is the 26th-highest-certified digital singles artist<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=top_tallies&ttt=TAS|title=Recording Industry Association of America Gold & Platinum|website=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]|access-date=June 1, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160126081620/http://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=top_tallies&ttt=TAS|archive-date=January 26, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> and one of the most successful artists of the [[2010s in music|2010s]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Billboard's Top Artists of the 2010s|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/decade-end/top-artists|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210511033038/https://www.billboard.com/charts/decade-end/top-artists|archive-date=May 11, 2021|access-date=May 11, 2021|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>

==Controversies==
Throughout the month of June 2021, Eilish became a subject of significant media coverage after several videos and criticisms against her began getting attention.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Whelan|first=Ella|date=June 23, 2021|title=Not even children are safe from cancel culture, as Billie Eilish just found out|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/what-to-listen-to/not-even-children-safe-cancel-culture-billie-eilish-just-found/|access-date=July 21, 2021|issn=0307-1235}}</ref> One of the videos saw her mouthing the anti-Asian slur "[[chink]]" while lip-syncing the song "[[Goblin (album)|Fish]]" by [[Tyler, the Creator]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Squires|first=Bethy|date=June 21, 2021|title=Billie Eilish Apologizes for Lip-Syncing Racist Slur in Old Video|url=https://www.vulture.com/2021/06/billie-eilish-apologizes-on-instagram-for-racist-language.html|access-date=July 21, 2021|website=[[Vulture (magazine)|Vulture]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Yang|first=Rachel|date=June 22, 2021|title=Billie Eilish apologizes for mouthing anti-Asian slur in past video: 'I want to barf'|url=https://ew.com/music/billie-eilish-apologizes-anti-asian-slur/|access-date=July 21, 2021|website=[[Entertainment Weekly]]}}</ref> Others highlighted her use of [[blaccent]] and [[African-American Vernacular English|AAVE]].{{refn|<ref>{{Cite web|last=Coates|first=Tayla|date=June 17, 2021|title=Billie Eilish Under Fire For Allegedly Using Anti-Asian Slurs & "Blaccent"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/billie-eilish-under-fire-for-allegedly-using-anti-asian-slurs-and-blaccent-news.134074.html
|access-date=June 21, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last = Taylor|first=Zariah|date=February 7, 2020|title=We Need To Talk About Billie Eilish and Cultural Appropriation [OPINION]|url=https://voxatl.org/billie-eilish-cultural-appropriation/|access-date=July 21, 2021|website=VOX ATL}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Harrison|first=Ellie|date=June 18, 2021|title=Billie Eilish's reported boyfriend apologises after 'racist and homophobic' posts are resurfaced|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/billie-eilish-boyfriend-matthew-tyler-vorce-b1868340.html|access-date=July 21, 2021|website=[[The Independent]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Mier|first=Tomás|date=June 17, 2021|title=Billie Eilish's Boyfriend Matthew Tyler Vorce Apologizes After Racist and Homophobic Posts Resurface|url=https://people.com/music/billie-eilish-boyfriend-matthew-tyler-vorce-apologizes-offensive-comments/|access-date=July 21, 2021|website=[[People (magazine)|People]]}}</ref>}} Eilish was also accused of [[queerbaiting]] after using the caption "I love girls" to promote her music video for the single "[[Lost Cause (Billie Eilish song)#Music video|Lost Cause]]", which was also noted to contain "sapphic undertones and scenes".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Robledo|first=Jordan|date=June 12, 2021|title=Billie Eilish uploads "I love girls" Instagram post after queer-baiting accusations|url=https://www.gaytimes.co.uk/culture/billie-eilish-uploads-i-love-girls-instagram-post-after-queer-baiting-accusations/|access-date=July 21, 2021|website=[[Gay Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=June 14, 2021|title=Billie Eilish has been accused of queerbaiting in her 'Lost Cause' video|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/53183/1/billie-eilish-has-been-accused-of-queerbaiting-in-her-lost-cause-video|access-date=July 21, 2021|website=[[Dazed]]}}</ref>

On June 22, she posted an apology to [[Instagram Stories|Instagram stories]] for her usage of "chink", saying that she was "appalled and embarrassed" by the video and that she was "13 or 14" at the time and did not know the slur was a derogatory term.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|last=Kambhampaty|first=Anna P.|date=June 22, 2021|title=Billie Eilish Apologizes for Lip-Syncing Anti-Asian Slur|work=[[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/06/22/style/billie-eilish-asian-slur-apology.html|access-date=July 21, 2021|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> She also addressed a separate video of what was viewed as her mocking an Asian accent, writing that she was actually "speaking in a silly gibberish made up voice".<ref name=":0" /> Reflecting on the events in a July 2021 interview, Eilish stated: "I said so many things then that I totally don't agree with now, or think the opposite thing. The weirdest thing is how nothing ever goes away once it's on the internet ... When you're a fucking teenager, you don't really know yourself ... I didn't actually know how I really felt. So I just came up with this facade that I stuck to."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lancaster|first=Brodie|date=July 17, 2021|title=Billie Eilish on her complicated relationship with the internet, the price of fame and the "new Billie"|url=https://www.vogue.com.au/culture/features/billie-eilish-on-her-complicated-relationship-with-the-internet-the-price-of-fame-and-the-new-billie/news-story/1c6e34914c93e17dd7299a8bd9aa1b5c|access-date=July 21, 2021|website=[[Vogue Australia]]}}</ref>
