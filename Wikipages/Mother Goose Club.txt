'''''Mother Goose Club''''' is an educational [[Preschool|nursery school]] program that streams on its eponymous [[YouTube]] channel and is produced by Sockeye Media LLC. Its YouTube channel has acquired more than 8 billion views and 7 million subscribers since 2009. Episodes of the program have also aired on [[PBS]] stations and are available on streaming platforms like [[Netflix]], [[Amazon Prime Video]], and [[Tubi]]. The show is made up of a series of educational [[Live action|live-action]] and animated segments, with a cast of six recurring characters who introduce classic [[nursery rhymes]] and other songs to children through catchy tunes, play, and interactive lessons.

==Content==

Each episode of ''Mother Goose Club'' contains multiple segments and follows a cast of six recurring characters—Baa Baa Sheep, Eep the Mouse, Little Bo Peep, Jack B. Nimble, Mary Quite Contrary, and Teddy Bear—who sing and dance to a variety of nursery rhymes and original songs.<ref name="Broadcast">{{Cite web|last=|first=|url=https://www.broadcastnow.co.uk/hot-picks-mother-goose-club/5110372.article|title=Hot Picks: Mother Goose Club|work=Broadcast|date=October 14, 2016|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref><ref name="C21">{{Cite web|url=http://www.c21media.net/mother-goose-gets-cgi-treatment/|title=Mother Goose gets CGI treatment|publisher=C21 Media|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref> The live-action characters often perform in front of animated backdrops, although some sequences of the show feature fully-animated [[Computer-generated imagery|CGI]] versions of the cast.<ref name="Common">{{Cite web|last=Ashby|first=Emily|url=https://www.commonsensemedia.org/youtube-reviews/mother-goose-club|title=Mother Goose Club|work=Common Sense Media|date=January 28, 2019|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref> Episodes also often feature sketch-like segments that have narrative elements.<ref name="Muo">{{Cite web|last=Cawley|first=Christian|url=https://www.muo.com/tag/best-youtube-channels-for-kids/|title=The 8 Best YouTube Channels for Kids to Watch|work=Muo|date=October 16, 2019|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref> The program's emphasis on words is designed to help young children learn early language skills, while rhymes and melodies encourage song participation and physical movement.<ref name="Harvard">{{Cite web|last=Jorgensen |first=Marin| url=https://www.gse.harvard.edu/news/ed/12/05/rhythm-and-rhyme-sona-chong-jho|title=Rhythm and Rhyme: Sona Chong Jho|work=Harvard Ed. Magazine|language=en|date=June 2012|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref> The show also focuses on other early learning concepts such as letters, colors, and shape recognition.<ref name="Common"/>

== History ==
The show was created by educators and parents of four, Harry Jho and Sona Jho of Sockeye Media.<ref name="Broadcast"/> ''Mother Goose Club'' videos were initially uploaded to YouTube for the purpose of sharing content with industry professionals but developed an unexpectedly large following among the general population.<ref name="KidScreenData">{{Cite news|last=Dickson|first=Jeremy| url=http://kidscreen.com/2016/10/21/how-digital-data-is-shaping-creative-content/|title=How digital data is shaping creative content|work=KidScreen|date=October 21, 2016| access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref>
[[File:Mothergooseclubcast.jpg|left|thumb|250x250px|''Mother Goose Club'' cast and creators]]
By 2011, ''Mother Goose Club'' had inadvertently become one of the largest YouTube kids' channels in the world.<ref name="Harvard" /><ref name="KidScreenData"/> Episodes also appeared on PBS stations nationwide<ref name="C21"/> and in DVD compilations.<ref name="KidScreenData"/> In 2015, the ''Mother Goose Club'' channel was among the first content creators to appear on the [[YouTube Kids]] mobile app.<ref name="Guardian">{{Cite web|last=Dredge|first=Stuart|url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2015/feb/23/youtube-kids-app-android-us-only|title=YouTube Kids app launches in the US for Android and iOS|work=The Guardian|date=February 23, 2015|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref>

By 2016, a compilation of ''Mother Goose Club'' episodes had been licensed to Netflix.<ref name="Variety2016"/> Beginning that year, Sockeye Media started presenting the ''Mother Goose Club'' series at both [[MIPCOM]] and MIPJunior, international conferences that bring together entertainment content players worldwide. It also announced a partnership with Foothill Entertainment on a new 52-episode, CGI-animated ''Mother Goose Club'' series. Each episode would be 11 minutes long and feature a story-driven narrative.<ref name="KidScreenFoothill">{{Cite web|last=Foster|first=Elizabeth|url=https://kidscreen.com/2016/09/30/youtube-hit-mother-goose-club-to-get-cgi-series/|title=YouTube hit Mother Goose Club to get CGI series|work=KidScreen|date=September 30, 2016|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref> The series went into development in 2018.<ref name="LicenseGlobal">{{Cite web|last=|first=|url=https://www.licenseglobal.com/entertainment/foothill-distribute-mother-goose-club|title=Foothill to Distribute ‘Mother Goose Club’|work=License Global|date=April 6, 2018|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref>

In 2017, Sockeye Media, together with developer Story Toys Entertainment (now Touch Press Inc.), created an app that brought ''Mother Goose Club'' books, videos, and games to [[iOS]] mobile devices.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://appadvice.com/post/mother-goose-club-app/745063|title=Mother Goose Club App Out Now on iOS to Bring Preschool to the World|last=Calimlim|first=Aldrin|date=April 11, 2017|work=AppAdvice|access-date=February 24, 2021|language=en-US}}</ref> In June of that year, Sockeye Media announced its partnership with [[DHX Media]]'s online kids' network [[WildBrain]] for managing ''Mother Goose Club''{{'}}s YouTube channels and handling paid media campaigns.<ref name="KidScreenWild"/> The deal aims to increase audiences and ad revenue by providing rights management and access to technical support.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Burke|first=Jade| url=https://www.licensing.biz/news/read/wildbrain-secures-youtube-rights-to-mother-goose-club/046507|title=Wildbrain secures YouTube rights to Mother Goose Club|work=Licensing Biz|date=September 7, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170909111248/https://www.licensing.biz/news/read/wildbrain-secures-youtube-rights-to-mother-goose-club/046507|archive-date=September 9, 2017|url-status=dead|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref>

In December 2019, ''Mother Goose Club'' episodes were added to the library of [[Video on demand|AVOD]] provider, Kidoodle.TV.<ref name="KidScreenKidoodle"/> In November 2020, it was announced that ''Mother Goose Club'' content would be available on the upcoming interactive children's app and streaming service, Hellosaurus. The app would allow children to interact directly with "[[Gamification|gamified]]" episodes of the show.<ref name="KidScreenHello">{{Cite web|last=Tuchow|first=Ryan|url=https://kidscreen.com/2020/11/26/hellosaurus-snags-us3-5-million-investment/|title=Hellosaurus snags US$3.5-million investment|work=KidScreen|date=November 26, 2020|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref> ''Mother Goose Club'' was also announced as a content producer for the upcoming kid-focused streaming app, Sensical, developed by [[Common Sense Media]].<ref name="VarietySensical">{{Cite web|last=Spangler|first=Todd|url=https://variety.com/2020/digital/news/common-sense-sensical-free-kids-streaming-launch-1234853869/|title=Common Sense Media’s Sensical Free Kids Streaming Service Sets 2021 Launch|work=Variety|date=December 15, 2020|access-date=February 24, 2021}}</ref>
