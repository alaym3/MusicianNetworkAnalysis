'''Lamp''' (ランプ, ''Ranpu'') is a Japanese indie band formed in 2000.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.hmv.co.jp/news/article/1401300036/?|title=【インタビュー】 Lamp 『ゆめ』 （前編）|last=株式会社ローソンエンタテインメント|website=www.hmv.co.jp|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tower.jp/article/series/2014/02/04/nmnl|title=Lamp NO MUSIC, NO LIFE.メイキングレポート - TOWER RECORDS ONLINE|website=tower.jp|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref>

== History ==

=== Formation ===
Someya started playing guitar in middle school. In the folk club during high school,<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|url=http://www.webvanda.com/2003/08/lamp201-motel-bluembr-003-lamp.html|title=Lamp：『そよ風アパートメント201』 (MOTEL BLUE/MBR-003) Lampインタビュー|last=Uchi|first=投稿者 Takahide|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> he became friends with Nagai through their common love of 60’s music.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.hmv.co.jp/news/article/1101260030?|title=【コラム】Lamp 『東京ユウトピア通信』（第1回）|last=株式会社ローソンエンタテインメント|website=www.hmv.co.jp|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref> During college, when a friend introduced Sakakibara to Someya,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hmv.co.jp/news/article/1102030084/|title=【コラム】Lamp 『東京ユウトピア通信』（第2回）|last=株式会社ローソンエンタテインメント|website=www.hmv.co.jp|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref><ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url=https://tower.jp/article/interview/2003/06/23/100038672|title=Lamp - TOWER RECORDS ONLINE|website=tower.jp|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> he decided to start Lamp.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.biosmonthly.com/article/9665|title=乘著一陣鬆軟音波浪花：Lamp "A Distant Shore" 亞洲巡迴台北場 ｜ BIOS monthly|last=monthly|first=BIOS|website=www.biosmonthly.com|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.taaze.tw/static_act/201703/lamp2/index_2.htm|title=TAAZE讀冊生活 {{!}} 情人已滿／未滿|website=www.taaze.tw|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref>

=== 2003–2011: Early years and first six albums ===
On 9 April 2003, Lamp released their first album, ''Soyokaze Apartment Room 201'' (そよ風アパートメント201).<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":7" /> Someya has cited manga artist [[Yoshiharu Tsuge]] as influence for the first album's lyrics.<ref name=":7" /> 

On 11 February 2004, they released their second album, ''For Lovers'' (恋人へ).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.webvanda.com/2004/02/lamp-motel-bleumbrd-005.html|title=Lamp：『恋人へ』 （MOTEL BLEU/MBRD-005） ミサワマサノリ対談レビュー|last=Uchi|first=投稿者 Takahide|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> Despite selling less than their first album, ''For Lovers'' became a fan favorite over the years.<ref name=":10">{{Cite web|url=https://blow.streetvoice.com/32081-%e5%9c%a8%e5%86%ac%e6%9c%ab%e7%9b%b8%e9%81%87%ef%bc%81%e8%bf%91%e6%9c%9f%e5%a4%96%e5%9c%98%e4%be%86%e5%8f%b0%e6%bc%94%e5%87%ba%e7%b2%be%e9%81%b8/|title=在冬末相遇！近期外團來台演出精選|date=2017-02-18|website=Blow 吹音樂|language=zh-TW|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref>

On 25 May 2005, they released their third album, ''Komorebi Dori Nite'' (木洩陽通りにて).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.webvanda.com/2005/05/lamp-motel-bluembr-009.html|title=Lamp：『木洩陽通りにて』 (MOTEL BLUE/MBR-009) クノシンジ対談レビュー|last=Uchi|first=投稿者 Takahide|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref>

On 7 March 2007, they released their compilation album, ''Zankou'' (残光).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/333|title=[Lamp] アルバム未収録曲集リリース決定|last=Inc|first=Natasha|website=音楽ナタリー|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref>

On 3 December 2008, they released their fourth album, ''Lamp Genso'' (ランプ幻想).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://popscene.jp/foundit/033585.html|title=00'sシティポップの名盤、Lampの『ランプ幻想』がLP化！|website=popscene.jp|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-13}}</ref>

On 4 August 2010, they released their fifth album, ''The Poetry Of August'' (八月の詩情).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.webvanda.com/2010/07/lampmotel-bleumbrd023.html|title=Lamp：『八月の詩情』（MOTEL BLEU/MBRD023）|last=Uchi|first=投稿者 Takahide|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref>

On 9 February 2011, they released their sixth album, ''Tokyo Utopia Correspondence'' (東京ユウトピア通信).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.webvanda.com/2011/02/lampuvca-3005.html|title=Lamp/『東京ユウトピア通信』（ポリスター/UVCA-3005）|last=Uchi|first=投稿者 Takahide|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ototoy.jp/feature/2011021001|title=LampのNEW ALBUM『東京ユウトピア通信』配信開始！|website=ototoy.jp|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> The album cover was drawn by manga artist [[Ouji Suzuki]] ([[:ja:鈴木翁二|鈴木翁二]]).<ref name=":0" />

=== 2011–present: Botanical House, Tours, ''Yume'', ''Her Watch'' ===
On 5 February 2014, Lamp released their seventh album, ''Yume'' (ゆめ).<ref name=":3" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/107083|title=Lamp、新境地切り開く新作アルバム「ゆめ」リリース|last=Inc|first=Natasha|website=音楽ナタリー|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> The album cover was drawn by illustrator Sēichi Hayashi ([[:ja:林静一|林静一]]).<ref name=":0" /> In 2014, they started their own label Botanical House.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sohu.com/a/135526341_698916|title=我们在Lamp来巡演前和他们聊了下日本音乐(仮)|website=www.sohu.com|language=en|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref> They performed at BEATRAM MUSIC FESTIVAL in [[Toyama Castle|Toyama Castle Park]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ukproject.com/calendar/6698/|title=BEATRAM MUSIC FESTIVAL 2014. {{!}}|last=|first=|date=|website=UK.PROJECT|language=ja|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> They collaborated with San Francisco-based band [[The Bilinda Butchers]] on their debut album, ''Heaven''.<ref>{{Cite journal|date=2014-07-31|title=The Bilinda Butchers On ‘Heaven’, Japan & Animal Crossing!|url=https://www.soundsbetterwithreverb.com/feature/bilinda-butchers-interview/|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2015, they performed at HELLO INDIE in [[Sendai]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://helloindie.net/2015/|title=HELLO INDIE 2015|website=helloindie.net|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref>

On 13 March 2015, they released their compilation album, ''Ame ni Hana'' (雨に花).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.indienative.com/2016/10/1wint|title=LAのドリームポップ・バンド Winter、自主リリースのニューシングル「Dreaming」のMVが公開！ {{!}} indienative|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref>

In 2017, they toured in China, including Shenzhen, Beijing, and Shanghai.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mp.weixin.qq.com/s?__biz=MzA4MTQwNTQ0MA==&mid=2664619330&idx=1&sn=9967d0a8a43d3488943d0e110b0e38ea&chksm=84b30e2db3c4873b98be7c8a4d36dabb564b613656e233b6243cb1cb2a745ea1a2dbbc76e1df#rd|title=Lamp｜首次中国巡演「唱给恋人的歌」|website=WeChat Official Accounts Platform|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref> On 20 April, they performed in Taipei.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":10" />

On 15 May 2018, they released their seventh album, ''Her Watch'' (彼女の時計).<ref name=":5" /> On 23 July, they released the single, "Tabibito/Place in my dream (旅人／夢の国)".<ref name=":9">{{Cite web|url=https://www.indienative.com/2018/07/153l81|title=Lamp、北園みなみアレンジの両A面シングル「旅人/夢の国」をこの夏リリース！ {{!}} indienative|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> On 1 August 2018, they released the split single, "Blue/Girlfriend (ブルー／Girlfriend)," a collaboration with The Bilinda Butchers.<ref name=":9" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/289336|title=Lamp新作7inchアナログを2枚リリース、北園みなみがアレンジで参加|last=Inc|first=Natasha|website=音楽ナタリー|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> In August and September, Lamp held the Lamp Asia Tour 2018 "A Distant Shore", performing in cities such as Beijing, Tokyo, Fukuoka, Seoul, Hong Kong, and Taipei.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/286259|title=Lamp、リキッドワンマンから「愛の言葉」「街は雨降り」ライブ映像公開（動画あり）|last=Inc|first=Natasha|website=音楽ナタリー|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref><ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=https://www.indienative.com/2018/03/lamp-8th|title=Lamp、8作目となるニューアルバム『彼女の時計』を 5/15 リリース！ {{!}} indienative|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hk01.com/扭耳仔/218719/lamp-1998-被時光沖刷的哀傷之聲|title=Lamp - 1998：被時光沖刷的哀傷之聲|last=陳力琛|date=2018-08-03|website=香港01|language=zh-HK|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> In October, they performed at Kirari Music Festival in [[Fujimi, Saitama|Fujimi]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kirari-fujimi.com/program/view/556|title=キラリ音楽祭2018 {{!}} 年間プログラム 富士見市民文化会館 キラリ☆ふじみ|website=www.kirari-fujimi.com|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref>

== Style and influences ==
Lamp's style has been described as [[Shibuya-kei]], [[city pop]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://solomo.xinmedia.com/xinforum/80923|title=【講堂筆記】浪漫的逆襲：日本獨有的 city-pop-欣講堂-欣傳媒生活頻道|last=欣講堂|date=2016-06-27|website=欣傳媒|language=zh-Hant-TW|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> and café music,<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hmv.co.jp/news/article/1102030085/|title=【コラム】Lamp 『東京ユウトピア通信』（第3回）|last=株式会社ローソンエンタテインメント|website=www.hmv.co.jp|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref> but they have also been described as difficult to place into one genre.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://livejapanmusic.com/why-lamp/|title=WHY LAMP?|last=livejapanmusic|date=2020-01-28|website=LIVE JAPAN MUSIC|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> They often incorporated elements of [[bossa nova]], [[jazz]], [[Soul music|soul]], and [[funk]] into their music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jame-world.com/en/article/151916-lamp-her-watch.html|title=Lamp - Her Watch|date=2019-11-13|website=JaME|access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> They have cited [[Music of Brazil|Brazilian music]], [[The Beatles]], [[The Beach Boys]], and [[Simon & Garfunkel]] as influences.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://contents.lee-japan.jp/2019/04/101-vol05-lamp.html|title=101ストーリー「ミュージシャンが語るスタンダード 」 Vol.05 Lamp│MEN’S│Lee official contents media|website=contents.lee-japan.jp|language=ja|access-date=2020-03-02}}</ref>

== Members ==
* Taiyo Someya (染谷大陽; born 13 November 1979) – guitar, synthesizer, vibraphone
* Yusuke Nagai (永井祐介; born 22 July 1980) – vocals, guitar, bass, keyboard
* Kaori Sakakibara (榊原香保里; born 17 November 1979) – vocals, flute, accordion
