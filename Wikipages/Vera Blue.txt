'''Celia Pavey''' (born 23 January 1994), known professionally as ''' Vera Blue''', is an Australian singer-songwriter signed with Mercury Records Australia, which is part of [[Universal Music Australia]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.creammagazine.com/2013/08/interview-with-celia-pavey/ | title=Interview with Celia Pavey | date=14 August 2013 |  publisher = Cream Magazine| access-date=4 August 2014}}</ref> Her folk-inspired album ''[[This Music]]'' peaked at number 14 on the Australian ARIA Charts in July 2013.<ref name="aus">{{cite web | url = http://australian-charts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Celia+Pavey | title = Discography Celia Pavey | work = Australian Charts Portal | publisher = Hung Medien | access-date = 21 January 2017 }}</ref> Pavey sings, and plays the [[guitar]] and the [[violin]].<ref name="Instruments">{{cite web | url= http://www.dailyliberal.com.au/story/1570878/music-director-saw-celias-talent/ | title = Music director saw Celia's talent | date = 13 June 2013 | publisher = Daily Liberal | access-date = 29 July 2014 }}</ref> She placed third in [[The Voice (Australia series 2)|season 2 of ''The Voice Australia'']].

== Career ==
===2013‚Äì2014: Celia Pavey, The Voice and ''This Music''===
{{Main|This Music}}
During the 2013 season of [[The Voice Australia]], Pavey auditioned while playing the guitar and received acclaim from the judges and audience.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/tv-and-radio/the-voice-grand-finale-live-blog-20130617-2odej.html|title=The Voice grand finale live blog|website=smh.com.au|date=17 June 2013}}</ref> Her first audition clip of [[Simon & Garfunkel]]'s "Scarborough Fair / Canticle" has amassed over 12 million views online.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lUbiDYhGpHQ|title=Celia Pavey Sings Scarborough Fair Canticle: The Voice Australia Season 2|publisher=[[The Voice Australia]]|website=youtube.com}}</ref> She joined [[Delta Goodrem|Team Delta]] and placed third in the competition.

Following The Voice, Pavey released her debut studio album ''[[This Music]]'' in July 2013, which peaked at number 14 in Australia. In 2013, Pavey was nominated for [[Cosmopolitan Magazine]]'s Fun Fearless Female Awards.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://magazines.org.au/news/cosmopolitan-releases-2013-fun-fearless-female-nominees |title=Archived copy |access-date=2016-05-19 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160424031417/http://magazines.org.au/news/cosmopolitan-releases-2013-fun-fearless-female-nominees |archive-date=24 April 2016 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Celia Pavey released her debut EP ''[[Bodies (EP)|Bodies]]'' on 29 August 2014 which was produced by [[Eric J|Eric J. Dubowsky]]. Pavey toured the east coast of Australia in support of the EP.<ref>http://www.123agency.com.au/announces-bodies-coast/{{dead link|date=February 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

===2015‚Äì2017: Vera Blue ''Fingertips'' and ''Perennial''===
{{Main|Fingertips (EP)|Perennial (album)}}
In August 2015, Pavey announced that she would be releasing music under her new project 'Vera Blue'.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://righthandmanagement.com.au/vera-blue/celia-pavey-announces-new-project-vera-blue|title=Celia Pavey announced new project Vera Blue|publisher=righthandmanagement.com.au|date=17 August 2015|access-date=20 October 2015}}{{Dead link|date=November 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

Her EP entitled ''[[Fingertips (EP)|Fingertips]]'' was released on 13 May 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/fingertips-ep/id1099115633|title=Fingertips EP|publisher=iTunes AUS|access-date=16 April 2016}}</ref> It features five tracks, including the two singles "[[Hold (song)|Hold]]" and "[[Settle (Vera Blue song)|Settle]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/verabluemusic/photos/a.623894317655086.1073741825.429698377074682/1047151828662664/?type=3|title=Vera Blue Facebook|publisher=www.facebook.com|date=12 October 2015|access-date=20 October 2015}}</ref>

In 2016, she was featured on Australian rapper [[Illy (rapper)|Illy]]'s song "[[Papercuts (Illy song)|Papercuts]]", which peaked at #2 on the ARIA singles charts.

Her single "[[Hold (song)|Hold]]" peaked at number 5 on the US Spotify viral top 50 chart and number 1 on the Australian Viral 50 chart. In February 2017, Vera Blue released "[[Private (Vera Blue song)|Private]]". In June 2017, Vera Blue announced the release of her second studio album ''[[Perennial (album)|Perennial]]''. The album was released in July and peaked at number 6 on the ARIA Charts. Her song "[[Regular Touch]]" placed at number 15 on [[triple j]]'s [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2017|2017 Hottest 100]].

===2018‚Äìpresent: "All the Pretty Girls" & "Like I Remember You"===
On 26 October 2018, Vera Blue released the single "[[All the Pretty Girls (Vera Blue song)|All The Pretty Girls]]" after premiering the song a day before on Australian radio station Triple J. She also released the live album ''[[Lady Powers Live at the Forum]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dontboreus.thebrag.com/vera-blue-new-single-live-album/|title=Vera Blue releases new single "All The Pretty Girls" and new live album|publisher=Don't Bore Us|date=26 October 2018|access-date=26 October 2018}}</ref>

I feel a deep connection to our oceans and I know that the seafloor is no place for risky oil drilling".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BtkeqkFH885/|title=Vera Blue on Instagram: "IT'S TOMORROW!! üåäüíö‚òÆÔ∏è My new single 'Like I Remember You' in collaboration with @greenpeaceap is dropping and I'm so excited. == Touring ==
Vera Blue supported [[Conrad Sewell]] on his Australian tour September 2015. In April and May 2016 Vera Blue supported [[Matt Corby]] on his ''Telluric'' tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/matt-corby-announces-support-acts-upcoming-telluric-tour/|title=Matt Corby Announces Support Acts For Upcoming 'Telluric' Tour - Music Feeds|date=2016-04-08|website=Music Feeds|language=en-US|access-date=2016-05-04}}</ref> In July 2016 Vera Blue supported [[Broods]] on their Australian tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4445490.htm|title=BROODS announce new album 'Conscious' and headline Aussie tour {{!}} Music News {{!}} triple j|website=www.abc.net.au|access-date=2016-05-04}}</ref>

Vera Blue's own Australian headline ''Fingertips'' tour was completed in May 2016, with all 8 dates around the country sold out.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://themusic.com.au/news/all/2016/03/18/vera-blue-announces-first-run-of-headline-shows/|title = /}}</ref>
Vera Blue's second headline tour commenced in August through to October 2016, hitting metropolitan and regional cities.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/vera-blue-announces-another-2016-national-tour/|title=Vera Blue Announces 2016 National Tour|website=themusic.com.au}}</ref>

Vera Blue supported [[Flume (musician)|Flume]] at the 2016 [[Splendour in the Grass]] Festival, performing his number one hit ''[[Never Be Like You]]''.

The 'Private' Tour was announced on 16 February 2017 and toured Australia in support of her released single, "Private". After the rising popularity of March 2018 single "[[Lady Powers]]", Vera Blue set off on her biggest Australian tour, titled 'Lady Powers' in March and June 2018.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theherald.com.au/story/5301860/vera-blue-showing-off-her-lady-powers/|title=Vera Blue shares her love and Lady Powers with new five-track EP as she heads for Groovin|newspaper=Newcastle Herald|last=McIntyre|first=Isaac|date=23 April 2018|access-date=22 May 2018}}</ref>
