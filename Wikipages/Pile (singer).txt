{{Nihongo|'''Eriko Hori'''|堀 絵梨子|Hori Eriko|born 2 May 1988}},<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ota-suke.jp/seiyu/pile|work=Ota-suke|publisher=Nyu-Akiba|title=Pile|accessdate=23 September 2014|language=Japanese}}</ref> better known by her stage name '''Pile'''<!-- yes, she uses Roman characters -->, is a [[Koreans in Japan|Korean–Japanese]] singer, actress and [[Voice acting in Japan|voice actress]] from [[Tokyo]]. Her first roles were minor parts in TV series and films during 2006. She debuted as a singer after she was selected in a 2006 Japan-wide audition and she made her voice acting debut in the ''[[Love Live! School Idol Project]]'' series in 2010.

== Musical career ==
In 2006, Hori was chosen from a total of 700 applicants in the Japan-wide Asian Dolls Audition. She later wrote that she wanted to be seen as "warm and enveloping, an artist who can be loved by everyone",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ameblo.jp/pile0502/entry-11618190218.html|author=Pile|script-title=ja:ちょっとだけ昔のお話|date=21 September 2013|accessdate=1 September 2014|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140903073208/http://ameblo.jp/pile0502/entry-11618190218.html|archivedate=3 September 2014|website=[[Ameba (website)|Ameblo]]|publisher=[[CyberAgent]]|language=Japanese}}</ref> so she chose her stage name ''Pile'' after the [[pile (textile)|pile]] towels are made from.<ref>{{cite web|author=CDJournal|url=http://artist.cdjournal.com/a/pile/171525 |title=Pile (声優 / Singer)|language=Japanese|publisher= Ongaku Shuppunsha|accessdate=3 September 2014 |archiveurl= https://www.webcitation.org/6SJ4UssWO?url=http://artist.cdjournal.com/a/pile/171525%20Pile|archivedate=3 September 2014}}</ref> In October 2007, she debuted as a singer with the indie single "Your is All...";<ref name=yourisall/> this song was the opening theme for the third season of the drama TV series ''Ikari Oyaji Ai no Sekki Youtaikyoku''.<ref name=pileprofile/> In 2008, two of her songs were included in [[Dancemania]] albums, including "Flower", which was included in ''Hime Trance Best''.<ref name=pileprofile/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dancegroove.net/database/tracklist-ht.html|title=HIME TRANCE (姫トランス) : Track Listing|website=Dance Groove|accessdate=4 October 2014}}</ref>

== Acting career ==
In 2005, Hori appeared as a minor character in the TV series ''[[1 Litre no Namida (TV series)|1 Litre no Namida]]'', and in 2006 she appeared in ''Karera no Umi VII'',<ref name=ndgac/> and the films ''Ōkoku'', ''Shiroi Asa no Koi'', and ''Tokyo Real''.<ref name=pileprofile/>

In 2010, Pile made her [[voice acting]] debut for [[ASCII Media Works]]' ''Love Live!'' project, as Maki Nishikino. Maki is one of the main characters of the series, a member of the idol group μ's. Pile has featured in her role in a two-season anime television series, anime music videos, CDs, [[rhythm game]]s, and an animated film, among others.<ref name=makesolodebuts>{{cite web|url=http://headlines.yahoo.co.jp/hl?a=20140711-00000331-oric-ent|script-title=ja:アイドルプロジェクト 相次ぐ声優陣のソロデビュー|trans-title=Voice actresses of the successful ''Love Live!'' series make their solo debuts|publisher=Yahoo! == Personal life ==
Hori was born and grew up in [[Tokyo]], where she went to Shinagawa Women's Academy in [[Shinagawa, Tokyo|Shinagawa Ward]] for middle school and high school.<ref name=ndgac/> She attended a college in Tokyo.<ref>{{cite web|author=Pile|title=｡*ﾟPile｡*ﾟさんとつながろう♪ (無料)|url=http://profile.ameba.jp/pile0502/|website=[[Ameba (website)|Ameblo]]|publisher=CyberAgent|accessdate=7 September 2014|language=Japanese}}</ref> Her father is Japanese and her mother is [[Koreans in Japan|Korean]].<ref name=ndgac/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/pile_eric/status/323025743980396544|title=pile_eric: あ、知らない方もいるかもなので、私は母親が韓国、父親が日本の ...|accessdate=13 April 2013|publisher=[[Twitter]]|author=Pile|language=Japanese|date=13 April 2013}}</ref> She is fluent in [[Korean language|Korean]] and proficient in English. She studied [[ballet]], [[Hip-hop dance|hip-hop]], and [[jazz dance]] while in high school, as well as [[baton twirling]] and theater.<ref name=ndgac>{{cite web|url=http://www.ndg.ac.jp/blog/students/2007/11/112.html|title=学園祭初日の渋コラ音楽祭に「Pile」さん登場!!|date=2 November 2007|work=NDGBLOG: Gakusei blog|publisher=Nippon Design School|accessdate=3 September 2014|last=Yamamoto|first=Dai|language=Japanese}}</ref>
