'''Adila Sedraïa''' (born 26 June 1984),<ref>{{Cite web|title=Indila – Universal Music France|url=https://www.universalmusic.fr/artistes/30367495090|access-date=2021-04-01|website=www.universalmusic.fr|language=fr-FR}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Musée SACEM: Indila|url=https://musee.sacem.fr/index.php/Detail/entities/10821|access-date=2021-04-01|website=musee.sacem.fr}}</ref> best known by her stage name '''Indila''' ({{IPA-fr|indila}}), is a French singer and songwriter.<ref name="leparisien-3532007">{{cite web |last=Bureau |first=Éric |title=Indila : le tube surprise de l'hiver |url=http://www.leparisien.fr/musique/albums/video-indila-le-tube-surprise-de-l-hiver-27-01-2014-3532007.php|publisher=Le Parisien|archive-url=https://archive.today/20140127155413/http://www.leparisien.fr/musique/albums/video-indila-le-tube-surprise-de-l-hiver-27-01-2014-3532007.php |language=fr |date=27 January 2014 |url-status=live |archive-date=27 January 2014 |access-date=24 June 2014}}</ref> She collaborated with many musicians on vocals and songwriting prior to releasing her first single, "[[Dernière danse (Indila song)|Dernière danse]]" ("Last Dance"), in November 2013, which reached [[SNEP]] Top 2 in France. She released her first album ''[[Mini World (album)|Mini World]]'' in February 2014.

==Early life==
Adila Sedraïa was born on 26 June 1984, in [[Paris]]. Her mother was a caregiver, while her grandmother sang at weddings; she has two older sisters.<ref name="Gala" /> Indila describes herself as a "child of the world" as she is of Indian, Algerian, Egyptian, and Cambodian descent.<!--this should be expanded--><ref name="Bureau 2014">{{cite news|last1=Bureau|first1=Eric|title=VIDEO. Indila : le tube surprise de l'hiver|url=http://www.leparisien.fr/musique/albums/video-indila-le-tube-surprise-de-l-hiver-27-01-2014-3532007.php|access-date=17 April 2017|work=[[Le Parisien]]|date=27 January 2014|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Photos : Indila : 5 choses à savoir sur LA nouvelle chanteuse qui nous fait danser !|url=http://www.public.fr/News/Photos/Photos-Indila-5-choses-a-savoir-sur-LA-nouvelle-chanteuse-qui-nous-fait-danser-494700|access-date=17 April 2017|work=Public.fr|date=30 January 2014|language=fr}}</ref> Prior to her singing career, she worked as a tour guide at the [[Marché international de Rungis]].<ref name="Bureau 2014" /> She explained that her stage name comes from her insatiable love for [[India]].<ref name="Bureau 2014" /><ref name="EBBA">{{cite web|title=The Winners: Indila|url=https://www.europeanborderbreakersawards.eu/en/the-winners/indila/|website=European Border Breakers Awards|access-date=20 April 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170802210322/https://www.europeanborderbreakersawards.eu/en/the-winners/indila/|archive-date=2 August 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In addition to her native French, Indila has provided vocals in English, including "Dreamin{{'}}" with Youssoupha<ref>{{cite AV media |title=Youssoupha feat Indila & Skalpovich – Dreamin' – Clip (Officiel) |language=fr, en |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DwtAg1V_kQk|time=0:01–0:20, 1:03–1:46, 2:27–2:50|publisher=[[Bomayé Musik]]}}</ref> and "Bye Bye Sonyé" with DJ Abdel,<ref name="auto">{{cite AV media |title=Dj Abdel – Bye Bye Sonyé Feat. Indila  |language=en |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PSFb1IJYzhw}}</ref> and sung [[Hindi|Hindi-language]] [[refrain]]s in ''Criminel'' by TLF and ''Thug mariage'' by Rohff.<ref name="Booska-P">{{cite web|title=Indila : La révélation musicale de ce début d'année|url=https://www.booska-p.com/new-indila-la-revelation-musicale-de-ce-debut-d'année-n24489.html|website=Booska-P|access-date=17 April 2017|language=fr|date=20 January 2014}}{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==Career==
Her meeting with record producer [[Skalp]]ovich allowed her to enter into the world of rap and eventually collaborations with several French rappers.<ref name="Gala"/><ref>{{cite news|title=Indila performs twice in the same day in Bucharest|url=http://www.citycompass.ro/events/indila-performs-twice-in-the-same-day-in-bucharest/|access-date=20 April 2017|work=City Compass|date=28 November 2014}}</ref> Indila began her music career around 2009, providing vocals for (among others) ''Invitaation'' by [[Vitaa]], "Trinité" by [[L'Algérino]], "Thug mariage" by [[Rohff]], "Hiro" by [[Soprano (rapper)|Soprano]], and composing "J'ai besoin d'y croire" sung by [[Admiral T]].<ref name="Ados"/> "Hiro" was her first successful collaboration, reaching number 26 in the charts in France and 27 in Belgium.<ref name="lescharts - Hiro">{{cite web|title=SOPRANO – HIRO (CHANSON)|url=http://www.lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Soprano&titel=Hiro&cat=s|website=lescharts.com|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 April 2017|language=fr}}</ref> She continued to collaborate with several other French rappers and DJs over the next three years, including "Poussière d'empire" with [[Nessbeal]], "Criminel" with [[TLF (band)|TLF]], "Press pause" with OGB, "Bye Bye Sonyé" with [[DJ Abdel]], "Dreamin{{'}}" with [[Youssoupha]]—which peaked in July 2012 at number 14<ref name="lescharts - Dreamin">{{cite web|title=YOUSSOUPHA FEAT. INDILA & SKALPOVITCH – DREAMIN' (CHANSON)|url=http://www.lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Youssoupha+feat%2E+Indila+%26+Skalpovich=Dreamin'&cat=s|website=lescharts.com|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=19 April 2017|language=fr}}</ref> in France's SNEP charts—and "Ma Reine" with [[Axel Tony]].<ref name="Ados"/> She also composed the songs ''Plus Haut'' by [[Matt Pokora]] along with "Avec Toi" by Axel Tony featuring [[Tunisiano]], in which she is credited by her real name "Adila Sedraïa".<ref name="Ados"/><ref name="lescharts - Avec toi">{{cite web|title=AXEL TONY FEAT. TUNISIANO – AVEC TOI (CHANSON)|url=http://www.lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Axel+Tony+feat%2E+Tunisiano&titel=Avec+toi&cat=s|website=lescharts.com|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 April 2017|language=fr}}</ref> "Avec toi", which was produced by Skalp, reached number 7 in the charts in France in November 2012.<ref name="Ados"/><ref name="lescharts - Avec toi"/>

[[File:Indila - EBBA 2015.jpg|thumb|upright=0.9|Indila performing at the 2015 [[European Border Breakers Award|EBBA]] Awards]]
In November 2013, Indila released her first single—"[[Dernière danse (Indila song)|Dernière danse]]" ("Last Dance"), from the album ''Mini World''—which quickly found success. It reached the top 10 of the French charts on 4 January 2014 and reached number 2 on 18 January, where it remained through the week of 22 March.<ref name="lescharts - Dernière danse">{{cite web|title=INDILA – DERNIÈRE DANSE (CHANSON)|url=http://www.lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Indila&titel=Derni%E8re+danse&cat=s|website=lescharts.com|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 April 2017|language=fr}}</ref> The song remained in the top 200 in France through April 2015, for a total of 77 weeks in the top 200.<ref name="lescharts - Dernière danse"/> It was also a success outside France; by the end of February 2014, "Dernière danse" was the number one song on [[iTunes]] in Greece and Romania, and the number two song on iTunes in Turkey.<ref>{{cite web|title=Le succès international d'Indila !|url=http://www.mycontact.fr/news/le-succes-international-d-indila-1342|publisher=Contact FM|access-date=20 April 2017|date=24 February 2014}}</ref> The music video for ''Dernière danse'', released 4 December 2013,<ref name="Derniere Danse - YouTube">{{cite web|title=Indila – Dernière Danse (Clip Officiel)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K5KAc5CoCuk|website=YouTube|access-date=20 April 2017|language=fr}}</ref> reached 100 million YouTube views on 30 July 2014<ref name="Ados"/> and has over 800 million views as of {{date}}.<ref name="Derniere Danse - YouTube"/>

In February 2014, Indila released her first album, ''[[Mini World (Indila album)|Mini World]]'', which was produced by Skalp.<ref name="Gala"/> In addition to "Dernière danse", two other singles from the album reached the top-20 in the charts in France: "Tourner dans le vide" ("Spinning in the Emptiness"), which reached number 13, and "S.O.S", which reached number 8.<ref name="lescharts - Mini World">{{cite web|title=INDILA – MINI WORLD (ALBUM)|url=http://www.lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Indila&titel=Mini+World&cat=a|website=lescharts.com|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 April 2017|language=fr}}</ref> The album was the number 1 best-selling album in France the week of its debut and remained in the top-10 best-selling albums through the week of 11 October.<ref name="lescharts - Mini World"/> A limited-edition CD/DVD version of ''Mini World'' was released in November 2014, with two new songs, orchestral versions of "Tourner dans le vide" and "Love Story", an acoustic version of "S.O.S", and videos of live performances of several tracks from the album.<ref name="lescharts - Mini World"/> A second limited-edition release of ''Mini World'' in February 2015 contained the ten tracks from the original release, the two new songs from the first limited edition, and another new song.<ref name="lescharts - Mini World"/> ''Mini World'' found success both in France, where it was the third-best-selling album of 2014, and outside the country, where it was the best-seller of 2014 in Poland and second-best-seller of 2014 in Belgium.<ref name="Purebreak 20 Jan 2015">{{cite news|last1=Hamard|first1=Jonathan|title=Indila en tête du palmarès des meilleures ventes de l'année 2014... en Pologne !|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Indila/news-95467.html|access-date=20 April 2017|work=Purebreak Charts|date=20 January 2015|language=fr}}</ref><ref name="Purebreak 4 April 2014">{{cite news|last1=Hamard|first1=Jonathan|title=Indila part (déjà) à la conquête des charts internationaux...|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Indila/news-91377.html|access-date=20 April 2017|work=Purebreak Charts|date=4 April 2014|language=fr}}</ref><ref name="RTBF">{{cite news|title=Victoires: Indila album révélation de l'année|url=https://www.rtbf.be/culture/musique/detail_victoires-indila-album-revelation-de-l-annee?id=8908137|access-date=20 April 2017|work=rtbf.be|agency=[[Agence France Presse]]|date=16 February 2015|language=fr}}</ref>

In October 2014, Indila won the [[MTV Europe Music Awards|MTV Europe Music Award]] for [[MTV Europe Music Award for Best French Act|Best French Artist]] of 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=MTV EMA 2014 : Indila meilleure artiste française, le palmarès complet|url=http://www.bfmtv.com/mediaplayer/video/mtv-ema-2014-indila-meilleure-artiste-française-le-palmarès-complet-349285.html|website=BFMTV|access-date=20 April 2017|language=fr|date=10 November 2014}}</ref> She won Best New Album of the Year award (for ''Mini World'') at the 30<sup><small>th</small></sup> ''[[Victoires de la Musique]]'' in February 2015.<ref name="RTBF"/> Indila was among the ten artists to receive the [[European Border Breakers Award#Winners EBBA Awards 2015|European Border Breakers Award in 2015]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Hozier wins European Border Breakers Award|url=http://www.hotpress.com/Hozier/news/Hozier-wins-European-Border-Breakers-Award/12660482.html|access-date=20 April 2017|work=Hot Press}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=EBBA Music Award Show 2015|url=https://ec.europa.eu/programmes/creative-europe/events/20151401-ebba_en|publisher=[[Creative Europe]]|access-date=20 April 2017}}</ref>

On 23 August 2019, Indila returned with her new song "[[Parle à ta tête]]".<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1164884360136450049|user=Indila|title=At least 4 years of silence I would say? I apologize for those who waited for what has been an eternity... Long story short, I am back on the Dreamers Train. Welcome on board. Indila|author=Indila|date=23 August 2019}}</ref> On 14 November, the music video for the song was released.<ref>{{cite web|title=Indila – Parle à ta tête (Clip Officiel)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZthUo-Z97uw|website=YouTube|access-date=14 November 2019|language=fr}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Indila's vocal range spans from [[F (musical note)|F3]] – [[D-sharp minor|D#5]] and has [[alto]] type.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Indila – Artist vocal range {{!}} Singing carrots|url=https://singingcarrots.com/artist-range?artist=Indila|access-date=2020-10-31|website=singingcarrots.com}}</ref>

She has cited [[Enrico Macias]] as her first musical influence, with others influences being [[Michael Jackson]], [[Ismaël Lo]], [[Concha Buika|Buika]], [[Warda Al-Jazairia|Warda]], [[Jacques Brel|Brel]], [[Lata Mangeshkar]].<ref name="Gala" /><ref name="NRJ">{{cite web|title=Indila|url=http://www.nrj.re/NRJ_Artistes/fiche.do:id=38274&name=indila|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181004121104/http://nrj.re/NRJ_Artistes/fiche.do%3Aid%3D38274%26name%3Dindila|archive-date=4 October 2018|access-date=17 April 2017|website=NRJ|publisher=NRJ Réunion|language=fr}}</ref>
