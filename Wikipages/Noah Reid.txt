'''Noah Nicholas Reid'''<ref>{{Citation|last=The Arts Guild|title=Interview with Noah Reid and Melanie Leishman about Old Stock|date=May 31, 2013|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0hp2xjy8-VQ|access-date=May 12, 2019}}</ref> (born May 29, 1987) is a Canadian-American actor, musician, and former voice actor. He has performed in several films and television series, including ''[[Franklin (TV series)|Franklin]]'' and the [[CBC Television|CBC]] comedy ''[[Schitt's Creek]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2019/01/schitts-creek-netflix-pop-success-story.html|title=The Unlikely Rise of Schitt's Creek|last=Adalian|first=Josef|date=January 16, 2019|website=www.vulture.com|access-date=March 22, 2019}}</ref> In 2016, he received a [[Canadian Screen Awards|Canadian Screen Award]] nomination for [[Academy of Canadian Cinema and Television Award for Best Achievement in Music – Original Song|Best Original Song]] for his work in the feature film ''[[People Hold On (film)|People Hold On]]''.<ref name=":0" /> In 2019, he received a Canadian Screen Award for Best Supporting Actor in a Comedy for his work on ''Schitt's Creek''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BviKIzTBdrW/|title=Schitt's Creek on Instagram: "WINNER! Congratulations Noah Reid, for your Best Supporting Actor in a Comedy Canadian Screen Award! #CdnScreenAwards"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=May 19, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Reid was born and raised in [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.torontosun.com/2015/08/09/kevin-from-work-star-noah-reid-explores-the-art-of-awkwardness|title='Kevin From Work' star Noah Reid explores the art of awkwardness|newspaper=Toronto Sun|access-date=January 21, 2017}}</ref> The son of visual artists, he became involved in theatre around age six and regularly attended productions with his family throughout his childhood.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.myentertainmentworld.ca/2018/03/noah-reid/|title=Nominee Interview Series: Noah Reid|last=Kelly »|date=March 18, 2018|website=My Entertainment World|language=en-US|access-date=May 25, 2019}}</ref> He attended [[Etobicoke School of the Arts]]<ref>{{Cite web|last=ESA Contemporary Art|date=22 November 2020|title=Etobicoke School of the Arts Graduates|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sa4P_oDFzFw|access-date=24 November 2020|website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> and is a 2008 graduate<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ww2.ent-nts.ca/en/graduates-and-recent-cohorts/2008/|title=Graduates and recent cohorts|website=ww2.ent-nts.ca|language=en|access-date=May 17, 2019}}</ref> of the [[National Theatre School of Canada]].<ref name=":1" />

==Career==
Starting as a child actor, Reid did extensive voice work for Canadian and American children's television and was the original voice of the title character in ''[[Franklin (TV series)|Franklin]]'' from 1997 to 2004.<ref>{{cite book |last1=Perlmutter |first1=David |title=The Encyclopedia of American Animated Television Shows |date=2018 |publisher=Rowman & Littlefield |isbn=978-1538103739 |pages=219–220}}</ref> While at Etobicoke School of the Arts, he co-starred in ''[[Strange Days at Blake Holsey High]]'' (2002-2006). 

In 2005, Reid played the role of Darrell Fox in the made-for-television movie ''Terry'' which dramatized [[Terry Fox]]'s [[Terry Fox Run|historic run for cancer research]]. In 2007, he appeared in the ''[[Naturally, Sadie]]'' episode "[[List of Naturally, Sadie episodes|As the Whirly Turns]]". In 2009, Reid guest-starred in the ''[[Degrassi: The Next Generation]]'' episode "[[Degrassi: The Next Generation (season 8)|Danger Zone]]".

Reid's first leading role in a feature film was Farley Gordon in ''[[Score: A Hockey Musical]]'', which premiered at the [[2010 Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://news.nationalpost.com/posted-toronto/my-toronto-noah-reid-outdoor-rinks-hold-allure-for-score-star|title=My Toronto: Noah Reid — Outdoor rinks hold allure for Score star|newspaper=National Post|language=en|access-date=January 21, 2017}}</ref> He then played several guest roles on television, including in the [[Police procedural|police drama]] ''[[Rookie Blue]]'' (2012, the project on which he met future wife [[Clare Stone]]<ref>{{Cite web|title='Schitt's Creek' Actor Noah Reid's Laid-Back Micro Wedding on Lake Huron|url=https://www.brides.com/noah-reid-clare-stone-wedding-photos-5076822|access-date=2021-11-22|website=Brides|language=en}}</ref>) and the drama series ''[[Cracked (Canadian TV series)|Cracked]]'' (2013). In 2013, he also starred in the [[The CW|CW]] [[web series]] ''[[Backpackers (web series)|Backpackers]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cwtvpr.com/the-cw/shows/backpackers/bios?id=noah-reid|title=The CW Press {{!}} Backpackers|website=www.cwtvpr.com|language=en|access-date=January 14, 2018}}</ref> In 2015, Reid appeared briefly in the first episode of [[Annie Murphy]]'s CBC television series ''[[The Plateaus]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/theplateaus/m_episodes |title=The Plateaus |website=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation]] |access-date=June 19, 2021}}</ref> The same year, he played the lead role in the [[Freeform (TV channel)|ABC Family]] comedy series ''[[Kevin from Work|Kevin From Work]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/news/kevin-from-work-premiere-noah-reid-interview-1201568828/|title=ABC Family Taps Into 20-Something Mentality with Comedy 'Kevin From Work'|last=Wagmeister|first=Elizabeth|date=August 12, 2015|work=Variety|access-date=January 14, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> The series was canceled after one season.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://deadline.com/2016/03/kevin-from-work-canceled-freeform-one-season-1201714694/|title='Kevin From Work' Canceled By Freeform After One Season|last=Andreeva|first=Nellie|date=March 4, 2016|work=Deadline|access-date=January 14, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2016, Reid was nominated for a [[Canadian Screen Awards|Canadian Screen Award]] for [[Academy of Canadian Cinema and Television Award for Best Achievement in Music – Original Song|Best Original Song]] for his work in the feature film ''[[People Hold On (film)|People Hold On]]'' (2015), in which he also starred.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://globalnews.ca/news/2462969/2016-canadian-screen-awards-nominees-rookie-blue-vikings-big-brother-canada-nominated/|title=2016 Canadian Screen Awards nominees: 'Rookie Blue,' 'Vikings,' 'Big Brother Canada' nominated|last=Jancelewicz|first=Chris|website=Global News|access-date=April 7, 2016}}</ref> He also released a self-produced full-length original album, ''Songs From a Broken Chair''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BIa1WKIBuhh/|title=Noah Reid on Instagram: "Songs From A Broken Chair is now available on iTunes. Debut album. Playing a release show @burdockonbloor tonight, doors at 830. Thanks to…"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=May 23, 2019}}</ref>

In 2017, Reid played the recurring role of Patrick in the [[List of Schitt's Creek episodes#Season 3 (2017)|third season]] of the [[CBC Television|CBC]] series ''[[Schitt's Creek]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vulture.com/2017/11/pansexual-schitts-creek-character-wont-become-caricature.html|title=Dan Levy Won't Make Pansexual Schitt's Creek Character a 'Caricature'|last=Ferber|first=Taylor|work=Vulture|access-date=January 14, 2018|language=en}}</ref> That year, he was also cast in the independent film ''[[Cardinals (film)|Cardinals]]'', opposite [[Sheila McCarthy]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/kevin-work-star-noah-reid-879704|title='Kevin From Work' Star Noah Reid Nabs Lead in 'Cardinal' Drama (Exclusive)|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=January 14, 2018|language=en}}</ref> which premiered at the [[2017 Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tiff.net/tiff/cardinals/|title=Cardinals|website=www.tiff.net|language=en|access-date=January 14, 2018}}</ref>

In 2018, Reid was promoted to a series regular on ''Schitt's Creek'', beginning with the series' [[List of Schitt's Creek episodes#Season 4 (2018)|fourth season]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.tv-eh.com/2018/01/08/preview-schitts-creek/|title=Preview: The schitt hits the fan in Season 4 of CBC's Schitt's Creek|date=January 8, 2018|work=TV, eh?|access-date=January 14, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> He also performed in a production of ''[[Hamlet]]'' at the [[Tarragon Theatre]] in Toronto in the same year.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/theatre-and-performance/theatre-reviews/review-something-is-off-in-tarragons-hamlet/article37579917/|title=Review: Something is off in Tarragon's reimagining of Hamlet|access-date=January 14, 2018}}</ref> He appeared in the films ''[[Buffaloed]]'' and ''[[Disappearance at Clifton Hill]]'', which were both released in February 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/02/13/804804244/buffaloed-debt-collecting-for-fun-and-profit|title='Buffaloed': Debt Collecting For Fun And Profit|website=[[NPR]]|date=February 13, 2020|last=Jenkins|first=Mark|access-date=February 29, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Disappearance at Clifton Hill|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/disappearance_at_clifton_hill|language=en|access-date=2021-11-22}}</ref> Reid later embarked on a tour across North America to support his then-upcoming second album. However, two-thirds of the original dates had to be indefinitely postponed midway through the tour due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lau|first1=Melody|url=https://www.cbc.ca/music/noah-reid-s-double-life-how-the-schitt-s-creek-star-balances-acting-and-music-1.5568221|title=Noah Reid's double life: how the Schitt's Creek star balances acting and music|work=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation]]|date=May 28, 2020|access-date=June 3, 2020}}</ref> The album, titled ''Gemini'', was released on May 29, 2020.<ref name="EW">{{cite web|last1=Krochmal|first1=Shana Naomi|url=https://ew.com/music/noah-reid-exclusive-music-video-got-you/|title=Exclusive: Watch Schitt's Creek star Noah Reid dance in adorably distanced video for 'Got You'|work=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=May 27, 2020|access-date=June 3, 2020}}</ref>

Reid will make his Broadway debut in 2022, joining the original Broadway cast of [[Tracy Letts]]'s ''[[The Minutes (play)|The Minutes]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=''Schitt's Creek'' Star Noah Reid Will Make Broadway Debut in ''The Minutes''|url=https://www.playbill.com/article/schitts-creek-star-noah-reid-will-make-broadway-debut-in-the-minutes/|access-date=2021-10-15|website=www.playbill.com}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Reid announced his engagement to actress [[Clare Stone]] on December 31, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BsEKXG8no5K/|title=Noah Reid on Instagram: Roommates for life |date=December 31, 2018|website=Instagram|access-date=March 29, 2020}}</ref> They were married on July 25, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/pictures/schitts-creek-star-noah-reid-is-married-to-clare-stone/|title='Schitt's Creek' Star Noah Reid Marries Clare Stone in Romantic Beach Wedding|work=[[Us Weekly]]|first=Meredith|last=Nardino|date=August 14, 2020|access-date=August 24, 2020}}</ref> Reid is a dual Canadian-US citizen.<ref name=":2">{{cite web|last1=Granderson|first1=LZ|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/tv/story/2020-04-17/schitts-creeks-noah-reid-music-hold-on|title='Schitt's Creek' inspired Noah Reid's return to music. Now he has a soulful new single|work=[[The Los Angeles Times]]|date=April 17, 2020|access-date=June 3, 2020}}</ref>
