'''Ben J. Pierce''' (born February 19, 1999), also known as '''Miss Benny''', is an American singer-songwriter, [[YouTuber]] and actor.

== Early life ==
Ben J. Pierce was born in [[Dallas]], Texas on February 19, 1999.<ref name=":6" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.topnetworths.com/celebrities/ben-j-pierce/|title=Ben J. Pierce Snapchat, Twitter, Tumbler, Wiki, Net Worth In 2019|date=January 8, 2019|website=Top Networth|language=en|access-date=January 14, 2019}}</ref>{{unreliable source?|certain=y|date=January 2019|reason=Top Networth is a blog with no credibility}} He lived as a [[closeted]] teenager before coming out. As Pierce began posting on YouTube, he found an outlet with other similar individuals in the online community. He befriended fellow YouTube artists [[Troye Sivan]] and [[Tyler Oakley]].<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|url=https://www.them.us/story/miss-benny|title=Get Ready — Miss Benny Is Making Gay Glitter Pop Music About Kissing Boys|last=Geiger|first=JJ|date=November 28, 2018|website=them.|language=en|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==
Pierce began posting on YouTube using various channels when he was 11 years old in 2010 as a way to feel less isolated and lonely.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url=https://www.polygon.com/2018/6/26/17505592/youtube-creators-vidcon-conan-gray-ben-pierce-macdoesit-chandlernwilson|title=Four YouTube stars on being young, lonely, then wildly successful|last=Alexander|first=Julia|date=June 26, 2018|website=Polygon|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref> In 2012, he began posting under the YouTube channel, KidPOV. He began posting under his own name in 2016.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=https://celebmix.com/youtuber-week-ben-j-pierce/|title=YouTuber of the Week: Ben J. Pierce|last=Low|first=Celine|date=November 13, 2016|website=CelebMix|language=en-GB|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref> He frequently discusses gender and identity. Pierce has done makeup tutorials and collaborated with other media personalities including Tyler Oakley and [[James Charles (model)|James Charles]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/best-beauty-youtuber-ben-j-pierce|title=Why You Should Follow Beauty YouTuber Ben J. Pierce|last=Santiago|first=Sean|date=January 3, 2017|website=[[Teen Vogue]]|language=en|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dailydot.com/upstream/9-must-watch-youtube-channels/|title=9 underrated YouTube channels to watch in 2016|last=Votta|first=Rae|date=January 1, 2016|website=[[The Daily Dot]]|language=en|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref> His YouTube channel also includes humorous skits.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://celebmix.com/ben-j-pierce-releases-new-single-boys-will-boys/|title=Ben J. Pierce releases new single "Boys Will Be Boys"|last=Low|first=Celine|date=December 10, 2016|website=CelebMix|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref>

In 2014, Pierce released the song "Little Game". He wrote the lyrics and directed and produced the music video with assistance from Tumi Mphahlele. The song and video rebuff forcing children into traditional [[gender role]]s.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bustle.com/articles/48950-15-year-old-ben-j-pierces-little-games-music-video-takes-on-gender-roles-in-an-epic-way|title=15-Year-Old Ben J. Pierce's "Little Games" Music Video Takes on Gender Roles In an Epic Way|last=Cueto|first=Emma|date=November 12, 2014|website=[[Bustle (magazine)|Bustle]]|language=en|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref>

In 2016, Pierce was cast as Smiley in season 2 of [[go90]]'s [[Guidance (2015 series)|''Guidance'']].<ref name=":2" />

Pierce also produces music under the name Miss Benny. Pierce released the single "Boys Will Be Boys" in 2016. The song's music video was filmed and edited by YouTuber and filmmaker Chase vs Everything. [[Kalama Epstein]] is featured in the music video.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://ouchthathertz.com/2016/12/13/pop-singersongwriter-benny-highlights-the-toxicity-within-masculine-culture-in-new-single-boys-will-be-boys/|title=Pop Singer/Songwriter Benny Highlights the Toxicity Within Masculine Culture in New Single "Boys Will Be Boys"|date=December 13, 2016|website=Ouch That Hertz!|language=en|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref>{{unreliable source?|date=January 2019}} The song is about [[toxic masculinity]] and [[heteronormativity]].<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":3" />{{unreliable source?|date=January 2019}} It features melodic tones, smooth vocals, and drum beats.<ref name=":2" /> Nic Kelly of ''Project U'' had a positive review of the song, describing its style as somewhere between [[Odesza]] and Drumaq.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.projectu.tv/bennys-boys-will-be-boys-is-a-carefully-considered-triumph/|title=Benny's 'Boys Will Be Boys' is a carefully considered triumph|last=Kelley|first=Nic|date=December 10, 2016|website=Project U|language=en-US|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref>{{unreliable source?|date=January 2019|certain=y}}

In 2017, Pierce released the single "Never Apart". He directed the video which was shot by Chase vs Everything. The video earned praise from ''Project U'' which described it as having a "90s vibe."<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.projectu.tv/bennys-never-apart-video-is-so-cute/|title=Benny's 'Never Apart' video is so cute|last=Jake|first=Nathan|date=June 16, 2017|website=Project U|language=en-US|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref>{{unreliable source?|date=January 2019|certain=y}} Kelley of ''Project U'' describes the song as an dark R&B track similar to [[Leland (musician)|Leland]] and [[Tom Aspaul]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.projectu.tv/benny-never-apart-ben-j-pierce/|title=Benny's put out a plodding R&B song called 'Never Apart'|last=Kelley|first=Nic|date=June 2, 2017|website=Project U|language=en-US|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref>

In 2017, Pierce was managed by [[Lisa Filipelli]] of Flip Management.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2017/digital/news/tyler-oakley-ingrid-nilsen-lisa-filipelli-big-frame-1202425341/|title=Tyler Oakley and Ingrid Nilsen's Manager Lisa Filipelli Exits Awesomeness' Big Frame|last=Spangler|first=Todd|date=May 15, 2017|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|language=en|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref>

In 2018, Pierce appeared in the sitcom ''[[Fuller House (TV series)|Fuller House]]'' as Casey, the shows first openly gay character.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|url=https://www.advocate.com/television/2018/12/18/gay-fuller-house-writer-dishes-shows-queer-characters|title=A Gay 'Fuller House' Writer Dishes on the Show's Queer Characters|last=Broverman|first=Neal|date=December 18, 2018|website=[[The Advocate (LGBT magazine)|Advocate]]|language=en|access-date=January 13, 2019}}</ref> He was cast in the series ''Reverie''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonblade.com/2019/02/28/youtuber-ben-j-pierce-cast-as-gender-non-conforming-lead-on-cw-series/|title=YouTuber Ben J. Pierce cast as gender non-conforming lead on CW series|last=Cooper|first=Mariah|date=February 28, 2019|website=Washington Blade|language=en-US|access-date=June 14, 2019}}</ref> In 2019, he was cast as the lead for [[The CW]] pilot ''Glamorous'' playing Marco, an "ambitious and creative gender-nonconforming teenager who uses makeup and fashion to let his queer self bloom."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2019/02/the-cw-pilot-glamorous-gender-nonconforming-teen-lead-ben-j-pierce-cast-pierson-fode-michael-rauch-1202565692/|title=The CW Pilot 'Glamorous' Finds Its Gender-Nonconforming Teen Lead In Ben J. Pierce, Casts Pierson Fodé, Adds EP|last=Andreeva|first=Nellie|date=February 26, 2019|website=Deadline|language=en|access-date=June 14, 2019}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Pierce names [[Bob Mizer]] as one of his aesthetic influence.<ref name=":7" /> He strives to create music that is glittery synth-pop, bubbly, and "very gay and very [[queer]]." Pierce listens to pop musicians [[Charli XCX]] and [[Carly Rae Jepsen]]. He also listens to rap and hip hop.<ref name=":7" /> Pierce often goes by the nickname Miss Benny to portray a masculine but still feminine image.<ref name=":7" />

== Personal life ==
Pierce lives in Los Angeles, California {{As of|2018|June|df=|lc=|since=}}. Pierce identifies as gay and queer.<ref name=":7" /> He has three older siblings who assist in aspects of video production including crafting props and design hair and makeup.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dailydot.com/upstream/ben-j-pierce-little-games/|title=This 15-year-old created a hauntingly good music video about gender roles|last=Votta|first=Rae|date=November 12, 2014|website=[[The Daily Dot]]|language=en|access-date=January 14, 2019}}</ref>
