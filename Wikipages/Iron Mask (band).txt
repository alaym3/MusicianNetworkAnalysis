'''Iron Mask''' is a [[power metal]] band formed in 2002 by [[Belgium|Belgian]] guitarist {{Interlanguage link multi|Dushan Petrossi|nl}}, also known from [[Magic Kingdom (band)|Magic Kingdom]].<ref>[https://archive.today/20120909191159/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=75258 News Archives: Iron Mask] ''[[blabbermouth.net]].'' Retrieved on 2010-08-07</ref><ref>Tartarean Desire webzine. [http://www.tartareandesire.com/bands Iron Mask Band Page] tartareandesire.com. 2004-01-04. Retrieved on 2010-08-07</ref>

==History==
===''Revenge Is My Name'' (2002-2005)===
Petrossi signed with [[Lion Music]] in early 2002 to record a side project, Iron Mask. The band’s name and some songs of the first album were based on the book [[Man in the Iron Mask]] by [[Alexander Dumas]]. The album was titled ''[[Revenge Is My Name]]''. Bass was recorded by Vassili Moltchanov ([[Magic Kingdom (band)|Magic Kingdom]], Cryme), vocals were performed by Phil Letaw (Karyan, Cryme, Stormy Night), keyboards - by Youri De Groote.<ref>Martin Popoff. [http://www.bravewords.com/hardwares/1000461 Iron Mask - Revenge Is My Name] ''[[Brave Words & Bloody Knuckles|bravewords.com]].'' Retrieved on 2010-08-07</ref><ref>Stefan Glas. [http://www.rockhard.de/index.php?smod=p209MJ56rKOyMTyuWz1iMUIfo2qAo2D9pz9wn2uupzDhpzI2nJI3pl5xMKEunJkJnJI3Wzqlo3IjFHD9pzuspzI2nJI3WzAioaEyoaEWEQ0lBGL5BPMmqJAbp3ElnJ5aCFL%3D Iron Mask, Revenge is my Name]{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} ''rockhard.de''. Retrieved on 2010-08-07.</ref>

===''Hordes of the Brave'' (2005-2009)===
In April 2005, Iron Mask had a different line up for the album ''[[Hordes of the Brave]]''. The band inaugurated Goetz "Valhalla Jr" Mohr (Arrow, Hanz Damf, [[Rolf Munkes]]) as vocalist. Guest vocalist [[Oliver Hartmann]] ([[At Vance]], [[Avantasia]], [[Edguy]], [[Aina (band)|Aina]], [[Magic Kingdom (band)|Magic Kingdom]], [[Freedom Call]], [[Helloween]]) added vocals to three tracks. The  keyboardist [[Richard Andersson]] ([[Majestic (band)|Majestic]], [[Time Requiem]], [[Space Odyssey]], [[Karmakanic]], [[Evil Masquerade]], [[Adagio (band)|Adagio]], Silver Seraph) is responsible for all the keyboards solos on ''Hordes of the Brave''.<ref>Shoegazer Ross. [http://www.metalexpressradio.com/menu.php?main=reviews&id=1646 Iron Mask - Hordes of the Brave] metalexpressradio.com. Retrieved on 2010-08-07</ref><ref>Reckert Klaus. [http://www.gaesteliste.de/review/show.html?id=99007718&_nr=5523 Dushan Petrossi's Iron Mask - Hordes Of The Brave] gaesteliste.de. Retrieved on 2010-08-07</ref><ref>Stijn Lambert. [http://www.rockreport.be/review.asp?id=1111 Iron Mask, Hordes of the Brave] rockreport.be.  Retrieved on 2010-08-07.</ref>

In November 2005 the band gave several concerts in Europe to promote ''Hordes of the Brave'', touring with Canadian heavy metal veterans [[Anvil (band)|Anvil]] and [[Phantom X]].<ref>Doug G. ===''Shadow of the Red Baron'' (2009-2011)===
In 2009, the band's third album, entitled ''[[Shadow of the Red Baron]]'', was released. Petrossi wrote and produced eleven songs. The vocals were performed by Goetz "Valhalla Jr." Mohr on all songs, with the exception of "Dreams", which was sung by backing vocalist [[Oliver Hartmann]]. Another guest was [[Lars Eric Mattsson]] who played the third guitar solo on "Sahara". Still on bass guitar was Vassili Moltchanov, but a new drummer was found in Erik Stout ([[Vengeance (band)|Vengeance]], Joe Stump, Daize Shayne); keyboards were played by Andreas Lindahl ([[Wuthering Heights (band)|Wuthering Heights]], [[The Murder of My Sweet]], [[Narnia (band)|Narnia]], [[Loch Vostok]], [[Audiovision]], [[Manticora (band)|Manticora]], [[ZooL]], [[Platitude (band)|Platitude]]). As with the previous album, Roma Siadletski (Magic Kingdom) performed extreme vocals. [[Jens Bogren]] ([[Symphony X]], [[Opeth]], [[Soilwork]], [[Paradise Lost (band)|Paradise Lost]], [[Universum (band)|Universum]], [[Amon Amarth]], [[Collarbone (band)|Collarbone]]) was responsible for the sound.<ref>Lords of Metal e-zine. [http://www.lordsofmetal.nl/showreview.php?id=15874&lang=en Iron Mask - Shadow of the Red Baron] lordsofmetal.nl. Retrieved on 2010-08-07</ref><ref>Ty Arthur. [http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=43064 Iron Mask unveils new album "Shadow Of The Red Baron" Details] metalunderground.com. 2009-03-12. Retrieved on 2010-08-07</ref>

===''Black as Death'' (2011-2013)===
On 30 April 2011, the band played a concert at [[Power & Prog Metal Fest]] ([[Mons]], Belgium) alongside [[Europe (band)|Europe]], [[HammerFall]], [[Gamma Ray (band)|Gamma Ray]] and [[Vanden Plas (band)|Vanden Plas]].<ref>[http://www.ppmfest.com/bands02.php Prog & Power Metal Fest (Belgium)] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110206152118/http://www.ppmfest.com/bands02.php |date=6 February 2011 }} ppmfest.com. Retrieved on 2011-07-14</ref>

In July 2011 the band announced that for the forthcoming album ''[[Black as Death]]'' <ref>Iron Mask posts new track, unveils album artwork for Black as Death. [http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=73163 metalunderground.com] Retrieved on 2011-10-27</ref> part of vocal duties would be performed by ex-[[Yngwie Malmsteen]] singers [[Mark Boals]] ([[Royal Hunt]], [[Ring of Fire (band)|Ring of Fire]]) and [[Göran Edman]] ([[Karmakanic]], [[Brazen Abbot]], [[Jayce Landberg]], [[Time Requiem]]). [[Mats Olausson]] (Yngwie Malmsteen, [[Ark (Norwegian band)|Ark]], [[Evil Masquerade]], [[John Norum]]) will be responsible for all the keyboard parts. The mix and production will be done by [[Dennis Ward (musician)|Denis Ward]] ([[Pink Cream 69]]). For the album the band signed in September 2011 with [[AFM Records]]. ''Black as Death'' was released on 16 December 2011.<ref>Iron Mask official website. ===''Fifth Son of Winterdoom'' (2013-2014)===
In 2013 Iron Mask announced the forthcoming of 5th studio album ''[[Fifth Son of Winterdoom]]''. The album was released on 8 November 2013 by AFM Records with Mark Boals on vocals.

===''Diabolica'' (2014-present)===
In July 2014 the band started working on a new studio album, and two years later, it was announced that the band's next studio album, ''Diabolica'', would be released on 23 September 2016, on AFM Records with Diego Valdez on vocals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://metalodyssey.net/2016/07/17/iron-mask-to-release-new-album-diabolica-on-september-23rd/|title=IRON MASK – To Release New Album “Diabolica” On September 23rd!|publisher=Metal Odyssey|accessdate=July 19, 2016}}</ref>
