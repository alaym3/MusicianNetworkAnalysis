'''Isabella Sophie Tweddle''' (born 27 May 1999), who performs under the stage name '''Billie Marten''', is a British singer-songwriter and musician from [[Ripon]] in North Yorkshire. She first came to prominence at the age of twelve when a video on YouTube of her singing attracted thousands of views. {{cn|date=November 2020}} She released her first [[extended play|EP]] at the age of fifteen in 2014, and her second EP a year later. At the end of 2015 she was nominated for the [[Sound of...|Sound of 2016]] award.

==Music career==
In May 2014, just before her fifteenth birthday, Billie Marten released the single "Ribbon" via [[Burberry]]'s curation program.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hungertv.com/feature/premiere-billie-marten-ribbon/|title=Premiere: Billie Marten – Ribbon|publisher=Hunger TV|date=16 May 2014}}</ref><ref name="st1">{{cite web|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1791219/billie-marten-heavy-weather-stereogum-premiere/mp3s/|title=Billie Marten – "Heavy Weather" (Stereogum Premiere) |publisher=Stereogum|date=6 April 2015}}</ref> She wrote the single with [[Fiona Bevan]] the previous year.<ref name="jo1"/> According to Marten, one theme of "Ribbon" is "the elusiveness of the city, and how quickly you can lose something amongst the people and tube stations."<ref name="jo1"/> In June 2014 she released her debut [[extended play|EP]] ''Ribbon''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.justmusicilike.com/blog/stmusicthatilike.com/2014/06/billie-marten-new-music-introducing.html|title=Billie Marten – New Music "Introducing"|publisher=Just Music I Like|date=16 June 2014}}</ref> In August 2014 Marten played the [[BBC Introducing]] stage at the [[Reading Festival]].<ref name="bbc2016">{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/5h9Skn0H7srr17xsq8N9RqY/billie-marten|title=BBC Music Sound Of, 2016 – Billie Marten|publisher=BBC Music|accessdate=10 January 2016}}</ref> She described her music in December 2014 as "a mix between acoustic and quite folky and a little indie."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.teenvogue.com/story/billie-marten-interview|title=Sound Scout: Meet Billie Marten, the Songbird That's About to Soar Straight to the Top|work=Teen Vogue|date=5 December 2014}}</ref>

In January 2015 Billie Marten signed to [[Chess Club Records]], a division of [[Sony Music]] and their [[RCA Records]] recording label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://chessclub-records.co.uk/2015/01/signing-billie-marten/|title=Signing: Billie Marten|work=Chess Club Records|accessdate=22 January 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160310111021/http://chessclub-records.co.uk/2015/01/signing-billie-marten/|archive-date=10 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="st2">{{cite web|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1837390/billie-marten-bird-video/video/|title=Billie Marten – "Bird" Video|publisher=Stereogum|date=14 October 2015}}</ref> She released the single "Heavy Weather" in April 2015 which premiered on [[Huw Stephens]]' [[BBC Radio 1]] show.<ref name="st1"/> A [[7" vinyl]] version of "Heavy Weather" was released in May with her cover version of [[Royal Blood (band)|Royal Blood]]'s "[[Out of the Black (song)|Out of the Black]]" on the [[A-side and B-side|B-side]].<ref name="st1"/> She released the single "Bird" in September 2015.<ref name="tlobf">{{cite web|url=http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/billie-marten-bird|title=Billie Marten shares enchanting new single "Bird" ahead of new EP|publisher=The Line of Best Fit|date=23 September 2015}}</ref> She wrote the song with [[Olivia Broadfield]], and according to Marten, the song is about "how words can truly affect people, not always for the right reasons".<ref name="rt"/> She toured for the first time in October 2015 as the [[Opening act|supporting act]] for [[Lucy Rose]].<ref name="ne1"/> Marten released her second EP ''As Long As'' in November 2015.<ref name="st2"/>

In November 2015 she was nominated for the [[Sound of...#Sound of 2016|BBC Sound of 2016]].<ref name="bbc2016"/><ref name="telegraph1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/what-to-listen-to/bbc-sound-of-2016-longlist-announced/|title=BBC Sound of 2016 longlist announced |work=The Telegraph|date=30 November 2016}}</ref>

In 2018 Marten's Blue Sea, Red Sea featured as Annie Mac's hottest record on Radio 1.<ref name="bbc.co.uk">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/av/newsbeat-46405743/billie-marten-having-sad-made-me-write-the-happiest-pop-song |title=Billie Marten: Having SAD made me write the happiest pop song – BBC News |website=Bbc.co.uk |date=6 December 2018 |accessdate=9 May 2020}}</ref>

In January 2021, Marten announced her third album titled ''Flora Fauna'', set for a 21 May release.<ref name="Flora Fauna">{{cite news |last1=Krol, Charlotte |title=Billie Marten announces new album ''Flora Fauna'' with rollicking single ‘Garden Of Eden' |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/billie-marten-announces-new-album-flora-fauna-with-rollicking-single-garden-of-eden-2867109 |access-date=29 January 2021 |work=[[NME]] |date=January 28, 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Marten likes [[alpaca]]s and has expressed a wish to buy one.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/interviews/interview-2016-must-know-billie-marten|title=Interview: 2016 Must-Know – BILLIE MARTEN|publisher=Coup De Main Magazine|date=20 December 2015}}</ref> She has said "I have this weird obsession with alpacas, I can't explain it. I think they're beautiful and I love them".<ref name="so1"/>

When aged 18, Marten was diagnosed with severe [[seasonal affective disorder]] (SAD), causing some depression and anxiety.<ref name="bbc.co.uk"/>
