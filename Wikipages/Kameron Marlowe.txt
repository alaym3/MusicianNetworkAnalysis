'''Kameron Marlowe''', born in 1997 in [[Kannapolis, North Carolina]], is an American country music singer-songwriter signed to [[Columbia Nashville]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Hollabaugh|first=Lorie|date=2020-06-26|title=Kameron Marlowe Signs With Columbia Nashville|url=https://musicrow.com/2020/06/kameron-marlowe-signs-with-columbia-nashville/|access-date=2020-08-02|website=MusicRow|language=en}}</ref> He was a contestant on [[The Voice (American season 15)|season 15]] of NBC's ''[[The Voice (American TV series)|The Voice]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Heisinger|first=Melanie|title=What's Next for Kameron Marlowe After 'The Voice' {{!}} Cabarrus Magazine|url=http://www.cabarrusmagazine.com/2019/01/07/186527/whats-next-for-kameron-marlowe-after-the-voice|access-date=2020-08-02|website=Cabarrus Magazine|language=en}}</ref> His debut single, "Giving You Up" was released on June 14, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Buckwell|first=Tyler|date=2019-06-18|title='Giving You Up': Kameron Marlowe releases debut single|url=https://independenttribune.com/news/giving-you-up-kameron-marlowe-releases-debut-single/article_edb0a6cc-91ee-11e9-ba46-fb4d988f6c38.html|access-date=2020-08-02|website=The Independent Tribune|language=en-us}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Marlowe was born and raised in [[Kannapolis, North Carolina]]. He has a younger brother by the name of Aidan, who was also born and raised in [[Kannapolis, North Carolina]]. Aidan is a hard working camera operator for the Kannapolis Cannon Ballers, Low-A affiliate of the Chicago White Sox, at the beautiful Atrium Health Ballpark. As a child, Kameron became interested in music through his grandfather, who listened to country radio when they went hunting and fishing together. He started singing in church when he was 10 and later became a worship leader. Marlowe attended [[A.L. Brown High School]], where he joined a band and performed around town before graduating in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Wineka|first=Mark|date=2018-12-30|title=10 to Watch in 2019: Kameron Marlowe|url=https://www.salisburypost.com/2018/12/30/2019-10-to-watch-kameron-marlowe/|access-date=2020-08-01|website=Salisbury Post}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Kidd|first=Erin|title=A city behind him: Kameron Marlowe talks about appearance on The Voice|url=https://independenttribune.com/news/a-city-behind-him-kameron-marlowe-talks-about-appearance-on-the-voice/article_35ae4314-c33a-11e8-840f-7b914dd99297.html|access-date=2020-08-01|website=The Independent Tribune|language=en}}</ref>

In 2017, Marlowe's mother was diagnosed with a degenerative disc injury in her back and had to quit her job.<ref name=":0" /> To help support her, Marlowe dropped out of college and began working as an auto parts salesman for General Motors while playing gigs locally.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Northey|first=Jessica|date=2020-04-13|title=Kameron Marlowe releases video for debut song "Giving You Up"|url=http://cmchatlive.com/kameron-marlowe-video-giving-you-up/|access-date=2020-08-02|website=Country Music Chat & Sway|language=en-US}}</ref>

== ''The Voice'' ==
While working as a car parts salesman in Kannapolis, Marlowe received a call from a talent recruiter from ''The Voice'' who had seen his live performance videos on YouTube. They offered Marlowe a fast track to the series' live auditions.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sony signs Auditions]] premiere on September 24, 2018 and subsequently joined [[Blake Shelton]]'s Team Blake before being "stolen" by coach [[Adam Levine]] later in the season.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Blind Auditions Premiere, Night 1|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt8583746/?ref_=nm_flmg_slf_1|website=IMDB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Matthews|first=Colleen|date=2010-10-31|title=[Watch] Team Blake's Dave Fenley Stuns With Lionel Richie's "Stuck On You" on "The Voice" – One eliminated in the Live Playoffs Top 24 episode on November 13, 2018.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Parton|first=Chris|date=2018-11-14|title='The Voice' Recap: Kane Brown Performs, 13 Finalists == Career ==
In June 2019, Marlowe independently released his debut song, "Giving You Up" and followed it with "Take You Up On It" in October.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Custance|first=Sara|date=2019-10-15|title=Kameron Marlowe Releases Enjoyable New Single "Take You Up On It"|url=https://www.raisedrowdy.com/2019/10/15/kameron-marlowe-releases-enjoyable-new-single-take-you-up-on-it/|access-date=2020-08-02|website=Raised Rowdy|language=en-CA}}</ref> By April 2020, "Giving You Up" had amassed over 15 million on-demand streams, and Marlowe signed a management deal with [[Artist Management Group|AMG]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hollabaugh|first=Lorie|date=2020-04-01|title=Kameron Marlowe Signs With The AMG|url=https://musicrow.com/2020/04/kameron-marlowe-signs-with-the-amg/|access-date=2020-08-02|website=[[MusicRow]]|language=en}}</ref> That same month, he unveiled a music video for the song, which later premiered on [[CMT (American TV channel)|CMT]]'s 12 Pack Countdown.<ref>{{Cite web|title=CMT Music 12 Pack Countdown : Voting|url=http://www.cmt.com/shows/cmt-music-12-pack-countdown/vote|access-date=2020-08-02|website=CMT}}</ref> In May 2020, Marlowe signed a songwriting deal with [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nicholson|first=Jessica|date=2020-05-19|title=Kameron Marlowe Signs Publishing Deal With Sony/ATV|url=https://musicrow.com/2020/05/kameron-marlowe-signs-publishing-deal-with-sony-atv/|website=MusicRow|language=en}}</ref>

On June 26, 2020, Sony Music Nashville announced that Marlowe had signed a recording deal with its Columbia Nashville imprint. Marlowe released his first song through the label, "Burn 'Em All" on the same day.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sony Music Nashville press release|url=https://www.sonymusicnashville.com/sony-music-nashville-signs-kameron-marlowe-to-columbia-nashville/|access-date=2021-10-01}}</ref> On September 30, 2021, "Giving You Up" was certified Gold by the [[RIAA]], becoming Marlowe's first song to earn a certification.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gold & Platinum|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=Kameron+Marlowe#search_section|access-date=2021-10-01|website=RIAA}}</ref>
