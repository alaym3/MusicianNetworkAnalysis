'''Damini Ebunoluwa Ogulu''' (born 2 July 1991),<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ibile|first=Fagbo|date=3 October 2021|title=Burna Boy's Biography|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/burna-boy-mn0003297650/biography#|url-status=live|access-date=3 October 2021|website=AllMusic.com}}</ref> known professionally as '''Burna Boy''', is a Nigerian singer, rapper and songwriter. He is one of the biggest and most successful African artists.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://edition.cnn.com/2019/11/21/africa/gallery/africas-biggest-stars/index.html|title=Africa's biggest music stars|website=edition.cnn.com|access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/entertainment/archive/2019/07/burna-boy-african-giant-bombastic-nigerian-pop/594610/|title= The 'African Giant' Challenging Musical Boundaries|website=theatlantic.com|date= 26 July 2019|access-date= 27 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.guardian.ng/life/burna-boy-leads-billboards-top-15-sub-saharan-african-artists/|title=Burna Boy leads Billboard top 15 Sub-Saharan Artists|website=m.guardian.ng|date=6 June 2020|access-date=27 December 2020}}</ref>

He rose to prominence in 2012 after releasing "Like to Party", the lead single from his debut studio album ''[[L.I.F.E]]'' (2013). In 2017, Burna Boy signed with Bad Habit/[[Atlantic Records]] in the United States and [[Warner Music Group]] internationally. His third studio album ''[[Outside (Burna Boy album)|Outside]]'' marked his major-label debut.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Nick Duerden |title=How Burna Boy Became Nigeria's Surprise Success Story |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8520544/burna-boy-african-giant-interview |publisher=Billboard |access-date=28 July 2019 |date=19 July 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190730110455/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8520544/burna-boy-african-giant-interview |archive-date=30 July 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> In 2019, he won Best International Act at the [[BET Awards 2019|2019 BET Awards]], and was announced as an [[Apple Music Up Next]] artist. His fourth studio album ''[[African Giant]]'' was released in July 2019; it won Album of the Year at the 2019 [[All Africa Music Awards]] and was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best World Music Album|Best World Music Album]] at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.channelstv.com/2019/11/24/2baba-burna-boy-others-win-at-afrima-2019/|title=2Baba Burna Boy and Others Win at AFRIMA 2019|date=24 November 2019|website=ChannelsTV|language=en|access-date=24 November 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191127205921/https://www.channelstv.com/2019/11/24/2baba-burna-boy-others-win-at-afrima-2019/|archive-date=27 November 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=autogenerated3>{{cite web |author1=Damolo Durosomo |title=Burna Boy, Angelique Kidjo, Trevor Noah & More Earn 2020 Grammy Nominations |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/burna-boy-angelique-kidjo-trevor-noah-more-earn-2020-african-artists-grammy-nominations-full-list/ |publisher=OkayAfrica |access-date=20 November 2019 |date=20 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191121141704/https://www.okayafrica.com/burna-boy-angelique-kidjo-trevor-noah-more-earn-2020-african-artists-grammy-nominations-full-list/ |archive-date=21 November 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> He was awarded the African Artist of the year at the 2020 VGMA's.
In 2020, his album ''[[Twice as Tall]]'' was nominated for the same category at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]], making him the first [[Nigerians|Nigerian]] with back-to-back [[nomination]]s at the [[Grammy Award|Grammys.]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=GRAMMY.com|url=https://www.grammy.com/|access-date=27 November 2020|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref> In 2021, ''Twice as Tall'' won the [[Grammy Award for Best World Music Album|Best World Music Album]] at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=14 March 2021|title=Grammys 2021: Burna Boy win at music awards|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-56393983|access-date=14 March 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2012–2015: ''L.I.F.E'' and ''On a Spaceship'' ===
Burna Boy's debut studio album ''[[L.I.F.E]]'' was released on 12 August 2013, serving as the follow-up to his second mixtape ''Burn Identity'' (2011). The album sold 40,000 copies on the first day of its release. Aristokrat Records later sold its marketing rights to Uba Pacific for ₦10 million.<ref name="allAfrica.com">{{cite web|last1=Abulude|first1=Samuel|title=Nigeria: Burna Boy Sells Debut Album Rights for N10 Million|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/201309060110.html|website=allAfrica.com|publisher=allAfrica via Leadership Nigeria|access-date=1 July 2014|date=6 September 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714201953/http://allafrica.com/stories/201309060110.html|archive-date=14 July 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> The album's release was preceded by five singles: "Like to Party", "Tonight", "Always Love You", "Run My Race" and "Yawa Dey".

''L.I.F.E'' was produced entirely by [[Leriq]] and features guest artists such as [[2face Idibia]], [[M.I Abaga|M.I]], [[Timaya]], [[Olamide]], [[Reminisce (musician)|Reminisce]] and [[Wizkid (musician)|Wizkid]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Iwar|first1=Sute|title=Burna Boy's Album Debut 'L.I.F.E'|url=http://www.okayafrica.com/audio/burna-boy-life-debut-album/|website=Okayafrica.com|publisher=OkayAfrica|access-date=1 July 2014|date=23 August 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714131613/http://www.okayafrica.com/audio/burna-boy-life-debut-album/|archive-date=14 July 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> ''Nigeria Entertainment Today'' ranked the album 10th on its list of the 12th Best Albums of 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Alonge|first1=Osagie|title=12 best albums of 2013 – The definitive list from Nigerian Entertainment Today|url=http://thenet.ng/2013/12/12-best-albums-of-2013-the-definitive-list-from-nigerian-entertainment-today/|website=thenet.ng|publisher=Nigerian Entertainment Today|access-date=1 July 2014|date=29 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160510082520/http://thenet.ng/2013/12/12-best-albums-of-2013-the-definitive-list-from-nigerian-entertainment-today/|archive-date=10 May 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album's music draws heavily from the music of [[Fela Anikulapo Kuti|Fela Kuti]], [[King Sunny Ade]] and [[Bob Marley]]. It received generally positive reviews from music critics, who applauded its production. It was nominated for Best Album of the Year at the [[2014 Nigeria Entertainment Awards]]. In August 2013, ''L.I.F.E'' peaked at number 7 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Reggae Albums chart.<ref>{{cite web |title=Burna Boy tops billboard reggae album chart with 'Outside' |url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2018/02/burna-boy-tops-billboard-reggae-album-chart-outside/ |publisher=Vanguard |access-date=31 March 2019 |date=10 February 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331163914/https://www.vanguardngr.com/2018/02/burna-boy-tops-billboard-reggae-album-chart-outside/ |archive-date=31 March 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>
[[File:Afrobeats star Burna Boy performing with at Nativeland Concert, Lagos, Nigeria 2016.jpg|thumb|Burna Boy performing at Nativeland Concert, Lagos, Nigeria 2016]]
In 2014, Burna Boy split from Aristokrat records.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thecable.ng/burnaboy-leaves-aristokrat-records|title=Burnaboy leaves Aristokrat Records|date=24 July 2014|website=TheCable|language=en-US|access-date=13 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190113232130/https://www.thecable.ng/burnaboy-leaves-aristokrat-records|archive-date=13 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> In February 2015, he founded the record label [[Spaceship Entertainment]] eight months after parting ways with Aristocrat Records.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Funsho Arogundade |title=Burna Boy Floats SpaceShip Entertainment |url=https://www.pmnewsnigeria.com/2015/02/27/burna-boy-floats-spaceship-entertainment/ |website=P.M News |access-date=16 February 2020 |date=27 February 2015}}</ref> Burna Boy released his second album ''On a Spaceship'' on 25 November 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://silverbirdtv.com/uncategorized/22965/burna-boy-releases-on-a-spaceship-album/|title=Burna Boy Releases 'On a Spaceship' Album|date=26 November 2015|website=SilverbirdTV|language=en-US|access-date=13 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190113182439/http://silverbirdtv.com/uncategorized/22965/burna-boy-releases-on-a-spaceship-album/|archive-date=13 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> His 7-track debut EP ''Redemption'' was released in September 2016; its lead single "Pree Me" debuted on [[Vice (magazine)|Noisey]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/09/23/burna-boy-redemption-ep|title=Listen To Burna Boy's New EP Redemption|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=13 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190113182337/https://www.thefader.com/2016/09/23/burna-boy-redemption-ep|archive-date=13 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thenet.ng/burna-boy-drops-7-track-ep-redemption/|title=Burna Boy drops 7-track EP 'Redemption'|date=23 September 2016|website=Nigerian Entertainment Today|language=en-GB|access-date=13 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190113232222/http://thenet.ng/burna-boy-drops-7-track-ep-redemption/|archive-date=13 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== 2018: ''Outside'' and O2 Academy Brixton performance ===
In January 2018, Burna Boy was featured on "Sunshine Riptide", a track from [[Fall Out Boy]]'s seventh studio album ''[[Mania (Fall Out Boy album)|MANIA]]'' (2018).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.konbini.com/ng/entertainment/listen-to-burna-boy-kill-it-on-fall-out-boys-sunshine-riptide/|title=Listen To Burna Boy Kill It On Fall Out Boy's 'Sunshine Riptide'|last=Orubo|first=Daniel|date=19 January 2018|website=Konbini Nigeria|language=en-NG|access-date=13 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190113182340/http://www.konbini.com/ng/entertainment/listen-to-burna-boy-kill-it-on-fall-out-boys-sunshine-riptide/|archive-date=13 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> He released his third studio album ''[[Outside (Burna Boy album)|Outside]]'' on 26 January 2018. Described as a mixtape, ''Outside'' consists mostly of [[Afrobeats]], [[dancehall]], [[reggae]] and [[road rap]]. It features guest vocals from English musicians [[J Hus]], [[Lily Allen]] and [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]]. ''Outside'' was supported by six singles: "Rock Your Body", "Streets of Africa", "Koni Baje", "Sekkle Down", "Heaven's Gate" and "[[Ye (song)|Ye]]". Its production was handled by [[Leriq]], Baba Stiltz, Jae 5, Juls, Chopstix, [[Steel Banglez]], [[Fred Gibson (record producer)|Fred Gibson]], Phantom and FTSE. The album received positive critical acclaim and was ranked by Pulse Nigeria and ''[[Nigerian Entertainment Today]]'' as the best Nigerian album of 2018.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Ehis Ohunyon |title=Pulse List 2018: Top 10 Nigerian albums |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/pulse-list-2018-top-10-nigerian-albums/tzhymhd |publisher=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=17 January 2019 |date=12 December 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190117122306/https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/pulse-list-2018-top-10-nigerian-albums/tzhymhd |archive-date=17 January 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Atayero |first1=Adeoluwa |title=Ranking The 10 Best Albums Of 2018 |url=http://thenet.ng/ranking-10-best-albums-2018-net-list/ |publisher=Nigerian Entertainment Today |access-date=17 January 2019 |date=7 December 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181212172736/http://thenet.ng/ranking-10-best-albums-2018-net-list/ |archive-date=12 December 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> It won Album of the Year at the 2018 [[Nigeria Entertainment Awards]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ohunyon |first1=Ehis |title=Davido, Tiwa Savage and Burna Boy win at 2018 Nigerian Entertainment Awards [See full list |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/davido-tiwa-savage-and-burna-boy-win-at-2018-nigerian-entertainment-awards-see-full/77zs365 |publisher=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=30 March 2019 |date=19 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190330233255/https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/davido-tiwa-savage-and-burna-boy-win-at-2018-nigerian-entertainment-awards-see-full/77zs365 |archive-date=30 March 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>
In February 2018, ''Outside'' debuted at number 3 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Reggae Albums chart.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Praise |first1=Billy |title=Burna Boy's "Outside" Album Debuts On Billboard's Reggae Chart |url=https://guardian.ng/life/music/burna-boys-outside-album-debuts-on-billboards-reggae-chart/ |publisher=Guardian Life |access-date=31 March 2019 |date=7 February 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331031345/https://guardian.ng/life/music/burna-boys-outside-album-debuts-on-billboards-reggae-chart/ |archive-date=31 March 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> The album's biggest single "Ye" ended up atop most Nigerian publications year-end list as the biggest song of 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/burna-boys-ye-is-the-hottest-nigerian-song-of-2018/yq2vd9l|title=Pulse List 2018: Here is the hottest Nigerian song of the year|date=17 December 2018|website=pulse.ng|language=en-US|access-date=13 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190113232340/https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/burna-boys-ye-is-the-hottest-nigerian-song-of-2018/yq2vd9l|archive-date=13 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/nigerian-songs-best-2018-year-listen-stream/|title=The Best Nigerian Songs of 2018|last=Isama|first=Antoinette|date=11 January 2019|website=Okay Africa|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190114002140/https://www.okayafrica.com/nigerian-songs-best-2018-year-listen-stream/|archive-date=14 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== 2019–present: ''Steel & Copper'', ''African Giant'' and ''Twice as Tall'' ===
On 3 January 2019, he was announced alongside [[Mr Eazi]] as one of the artists performing at the 2019 [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2019/01/burna-boy-mr-eazi-to-make-coachella-debut/|title=Burna Boy, Mr Eazi to make Coachella debut|last=adekunle|date=3 January 2019|website=Vanguard News Nigeria|language=en-US|access-date=3 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190103210355/https://www.vanguardngr.com/2019/01/burna-boy-mr-eazi-to-make-coachella-debut/|archive-date=3 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> He won four awards at the [[Soundcity MVP Awards Festival]], including African Artiste of the Year, Listener's Choice and Best Male MVP.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2019/01/soundcity-mvp-burna-boy-wins-african-artiste-of-the-year/|title=Soundcity MVP: Burna Boy wins African Artiste of the Year|last=adekunle|date=6 January 2019|website=Vanguard News Nigeria|language=en-US|access-date=13 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190113174217/https://www.vanguardngr.com/2019/01/soundcity-mvp-burna-boy-wins-african-artiste-of-the-year/|archive-date=13 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 21 March 2019, Burna Boy released a 4-track collaborative EP with Los Angeles-based [[electronic music|electronic]] duo [[DJDS]], titled ''[[Steel & Copper]]''.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Evan Minsker |title=DJDS and Burna Boy Release New Steel & Copper EP: Listen |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/djds-and-burna-boy-release-new-steel-and-copper-ep-listen/ |publisher=Pitchfork |access-date=5 April 2019 |date=21 March 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331131747/https://pitchfork.com/news/djds-and-burna-boy-release-new-steel-and-copper-ep-listen/ |archive-date=31 March 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> It blends Burna Boy's upbeat melodies with DJDS' slinky trap beats.<ref name="Native 1">{{cite web |author1=Debola Abimbolu |title=Essentials: Burna Boy and DJDS's 'Steel and Copper'|url=https://thenativemag.com/music/burna-boy-teams-los-angeles-production-duo-djds-new-ep-steel-cooper/ |website=Native |access-date=21 January 2020 |date=22 March 2019}}</ref><ref name="Pulse Nigeria 1" /> ''Steel & Copper'' combines elements of [[dancehall]] and [[reggae]] music with [[Afropop]] and [[trap music|trap]].<ref name="Native 1" /><ref name="Pulse Nigeria 1">{{cite web |author1=Ehis Ohunyon |title=Burna Boy's ''Steel and Copper'' with DJDS is his most experimental project yet |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/burna-boy-djds-steel-and-copper-review/60ymg0w |website=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=21 January 2020 |date=25 March 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190707025958/https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/burna-boy-djds-steel-and-copper-review/60ymg0w |archive-date=7 July 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 24 June 2019, Burna Boy won Best International Act at the [[BET Awards 2019|2019 BET Awards]].<ref name=":0">{{cite news |title=Burna Boy wins Best International Act at 2019 BET awards |url=https://oak.tv/newstrack/burna-boy-wins-best-international-act-2019-bet-awards/ |access-date=24 June 2019 |agency=Oak Tv |date=24 June 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190624141540/https://oak.tv/newstrack/burna-boy-wins-best-international-act-2019-bet-awards/ |archive-date=24 June 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> In July 2019, Burna Boy was announced as an [[Apple Music Up Next]] artist. His inclusion into the program was accompanied by a [[Beats 1]] interview with [[Julie Adenuga]] and a short documentary.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Kam Tambini |title=Burna Boy Is Apple Music's New 'Up Next' Artist |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/burna-boy-apple-music-up-next/ |publisher=OkayAfrica |access-date=20 July 2019 |date=9 July 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190710153004/https://www.okayafrica.com/burna-boy-apple-music-up-next/ |archive-date=10 July 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> He recorded "Ja Ara E" ([[Yoruba language|Yoruba]]: "wise up" or "use your head") for [[Beyoncé]]'s ''[[The Lion King: The Gift]]'' and was the only guest artist with their own track on the soundtrack album.<ref name="Chicago Sun Times">{{cite web |author1=Gary Gerard Hamilton |title=Afrobeat pioneer Burna Boy, to play House of Blues, making a name for himself in the U.S. |url=https://chicago.suntimes.com/2019/8/22/20823183/burna-boy-beyonce-lion-king-afro-fusion-african-giant |website=Chicago Sun Times |access-date=1 December 2019 |date=22 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190913181734/https://chicago.suntimes.com/2019/8/22/20823183/burna-boy-beyonce-lion-king-afro-fusion-african-giant |archive-date=13 September 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author1=Antoinette Isama |title=The Crossover: How Burna Boy's Grammy Nod Proves The Power of Consistency |url=https://www.vibe.com/2019/11/burna-boy-grammy-nomination-opinion |website=Vibe |access-date=1 December 2019 |date=30 November 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191130204248/https://www.vibe.com/2019/11/burna-boy-grammy-nomination-opinion |archive-date=30 November 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In November 2019, Burna Boy became the first Afrobeat artist to sell out the [[The SSE Arena, Wembley|SSE Arena]]( formerly known as the Wembley arena). He was given a special plaque to mark his achievement.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Burna Boy first afrobeat star to sell out at Wembley Arena - P.M. Burna Boy's fourth studio album ''[[African Giant]]'' was released on 26 July 2019. It has been supported by six singles: "Gbona", "On the Low", Killin Dem", "Dangote", "Anybody" and "Pull Up". He began recording the album in 2018. He told ''Billboard'' magazine the album is his most personal yet.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Nick Duerden |title=How Burna Boy Became Nigeria's Surprise Success Story |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8520544/burna-boy-african-giant-interview |publisher=Billboard |access-date=20 July 2019 |date=July 19, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190720033715/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8520544/burna-boy-african-giant-interview |archive-date=20 July 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> He first revealed plans to release the album in April 2019, and held a private listening session in Los Angeles.<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 13">{{cite web |author1=Odion Okonofua |title=Pulse Exclusive: Burna Boy set to drop new album 'African Giant' |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/pulse-exclusive-burna-boy-set-to-drop-new-album-african-giant/9jzh15r |publisher=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=26 May 2019 |date=17 April 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190526134727/https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/pulse-exclusive-burna-boy-set-to-drop-new-album-african-giant/9jzh15r |archive-date=26 May 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> Photos and videos from the listening session were shared on social media. ''African Giant'' was initially announced as a 16-track album.<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 13" /> In order to promote the album, Burna Boy headlined the African Giant Returns tour, the second leg of his African Giant tour.<ref>{{cite web |title=Burna Boy Announces 'The African Giant Returns Tour' |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/burna-boy-tour-african-giant-returns/ |publisher=OkayAfrica |access-date=20 July 2019 |date=June 17, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190720200529/https://www.okayafrica.com/burna-boy-tour-african-giant-returns/ |archive-date=20 July 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> Burna Boy recorded "My Money, My Baby", a track that appeared on ''[[Queen & Slim]]''{{'}}s soundtrack album.<ref name="OkayAfrica 21">{{cite web |author1=Damola Durosomo |title=Burna Boy Samples Fela's 'Shakara' on New Track, 'My Money, My Baby' From 'Queen & Slim' Soundtrack |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/listen-burna-boys-new-song-my-money-my-baby-from-the-queen-slim-soundtrack/ |website=OkayAfrica |access-date=1 December 2019 |date=November 15, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191122131305/https://www.okayafrica.com/listen-burna-boys-new-song-my-money-my-baby-from-the-queen-slim-soundtrack/ |archive-date=22 November 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> Described as an "Afrobeat-tinged track", "My Money, My Baby" contains a sample of [[Fela Kuti]]'s 1972 song "Shakara".<ref name="OkayAfrica 21" />

In April 2020, Burna Boy performed in the ''One World: Together at Home'' special.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Burna Boy Highlights Pandemic Relief Efforts During 'One World' Concert: Watch|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9361276/burna-boy-one-world-together-home-performance|last=Ginsberg|first=Gab|website=Billboard}}</ref>

On 19 June 2020, following the release of multiple singles during early 2020, by himself and on featured credits, Burna Boy was featured on the remix of [[South Africa]]n producer [[Master KG]]'s [[viral video|viral]] song "[[Jerusalema]]". The remix led to the song entering various charts.<ref name="News24">{{cite web|url=https://www.news24.com/amp/drum/celebs/news/master-kgs-jerusalem-gets-30-million-views-on-youtube-20200629|title=Master KG's Jerusalem gets 30 million views on YouTube!|work=[[News24 (website)|News24]]|last=Mavuso|first=Karabo|date=29 June 2020|access-date=27 July 2020}}</ref> Burna Boy utilized his signature [[Afrobeats]] style on the song<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/amp/master-kg-jerusalema-remix-feat-burna-boy-nomcebo-2646208258|title=Listen to Master KG and Nomcebo's 'Jerusalema' Remix Featuring Burna Boy|work=[[OkayAfrica]]|last=Samanga|first=Rufaro|date=19 June 2020|access-date=27 July 2020}}</ref> and also partly sings in the [[isiZulu]] language.<ref name="News24" />

Burna Boy was featured on [[Sam Smith]]'s single "[[My Oasis]]", which was released on 30 July 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopza.com/burna-boy-featured-in-sam-smiths-new-single-my-oasis/|title=Burna Boy Featured In Sam Smith's New Single 'My Oasis'|date=29 July 2020|access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref>

Burna Boy's fifth studio album, ''[[Twice as Tall]]'', was released on 14 August 2020, executive produced by [[Sean Combs|Diddy]] and Mama Burna [[Bose Ogulu]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/05/arts/music/burna-boy-twice-as-tall.html|last=Pareles|first=Jon|title=Burna Boy Has the Whole World Listening|date=5 August 2020|work=[[The New York Times]]|access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/news/2020/8/11/21363227/burna-boy-twice-as-tall-tracklist|last=Moorwood|first=Victoria|title=Burna Boy shares tracklist for Diddy-executive produced 'Twice As Tall'|date=11 August 2020|publisher=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|access-date=18 August 2020}}</ref> The album became his highest-charting project, debuting at number 1 on the Billboard World Albums Chart.

On 24 November 2020, Burna Boy album ''Twice as Tall'' was nominated under the World Music Album category in the 63rd Annual Grammy Awards alongside other entertainers like Antibalas, Bebel Gilberto, Anoushka Shankar and Tianariwen. This is the second year in a row that Burna Boy is getting a nomination in this category.<ref>{{Cite web|date=23 November 2020|title=Burna Boy|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/burna-boy/251682|access-date=24 November 2020|website=GRAMMY.com}}</ref> He won an [[Edison award]] in the World Album category for ''African Giant'', and the Best International Act at the [[MOBO Awards]] on 9 December, shaking off competition from [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Megan Thee Stallion]], [[Lil Baby]] and [[Roddy Ricch]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metro.co.uk/2020/12/09/mobos-2020-full-winners-list-highlights-performance-from-awards-13728485/amp/|title=MOOBOs 2020: Full winners list, highlights performance from awards|website=metro.co.uk|access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref>

Burna Boy′s ″Destiny″ was included in the playlist at the [[inauguration of Joe Biden]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Augoye |first1=Jayne |title=Nigeria: Burna Boy's Song Makes Joe Biden, Kamala Harris' Inauguration Playlist |url=https://allafrica.com/stories/202101200027.html |access-date=21 January 2021 |work=allAfrica.com |publisher=Premium Times |date=20 January 2021 |language=en}}</ref>

He added another achievement to his repertoire at the BET awards 2021 where he won the Best International Act. With this win, Burna Boy becomes the first African artiste to bag three nominations and wins consecutively, having won the BET Award in 2019, 2020 and now in 2021.

Burna Boy was featured on [[Jon Bellion]]'s single "I Feel It", released on 17 September 2021.

== Controversies ==

Burna Boy's outspoken views have made him a controversial figure in the Nigerian and African entertainment industry. He has been in a feud with South African rapper [[AKA (rapper)|AKA]] over anti-Nigerian [[Twitter|tweets]]. In July 2019, Burna Boy posted controversial and threatening tweets after South Africa lost to Nigeria at the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations]].<ref>{{cite web|date=4 September 2019|title=Burna Boy vs. AKA: A brief history of their relationship and their top 5 songs together|url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/burna-boy-vs-aka-a-brief-history-of-their-friendship-and-their-top-5-songs-together/trf34sm|access-date=29 September 2020|website=Pulse Nigeria|language=en-US}}</ref> On 28 November 2019, Burna Boy was involved in an altercation with fellow Nigerian musician [[Davido]] at a nightclub in [[Accra|Accra, Ghana]]. The altercation resulted in the night club's bouncers escorting Burna Boy and his entourage out of the building. This was a result of a feud between the two that started over [[Instagram]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Davido, Burna Boy 'clash' for Ghana nightclub|work=BBC News Pidgin|url=https://www.bbc.com/pidgin/tori-55467815|access-date=10 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=28 December 2020|title=Ghanaian bouncers forced out Burna Boy after brawl with Davido –Eyewitness|url=https://punchng.com/ghanaian-bouncers-forced-out-burna-boy-after-brawl-with-davido-eyewitness/|access-date=10 November 2021|website=Punch Newspapers|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Burna Boy describes his music as [[Afro-fusion]], a genre that blends [[Afrobeats]], [[dancehall]] riddims, [[reggae]], [[Hip hop music|American rap]] and [[R&B]].<ref name="Chicago Sun Times" /><ref name="SSense">{{cite web |author1=Safra Ducreay |title=Burna Boy, Afrofusion Superhero |url=https://www.ssense.com/en-us/editorial/music/burna-boy-afrofusion-superhero |website=SSense |date=4 April 2018 |access-date=1 December 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190506033627/https://www.ssense.com/en-us/editorial/music/burna-boy-afrofusion-superhero |archive-date=6 May 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> August Brown of the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' said Burna Boy's sound is "savvy and modern but undistracted by obvious crossover moves".<ref>{{cite web |author1=August Brown |title=Review: Burna Boy celebrates L.A.'s African diaspora at star-making Wiltern show |url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2019-08-30/burna-boy-afrobeat-nigerian-wiltern-review |website=Los Angeles Times |access-date=1 December 2019 |date=30 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191127212623/https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2019-08-30/burna-boy-afrobeat-nigerian-wiltern-review |archive-date=27 November 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>
