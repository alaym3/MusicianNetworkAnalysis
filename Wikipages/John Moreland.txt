'''John Robert Moreland''' (born June 22, 1985)<ref name=FamilySearch-TxBirthIndex-1985>{{cite web|title=John Robert Moreland –Texas Birth Index|url=https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/V8R9-7J4|work=[[FamilySearch]]|date=June 22, 1985}}</ref> is an American singer-songwriter from [[Tulsa, Oklahoma|Tulsa]], Oklahoma.<ref name=NewsOK-Carney-2013>{{cite news|last=Carney|first=Matt|title=Here's why you should buy John Moreland's excellent new record 'In The Throes'|url=http://newsok.com/heres-why-you-should-buy-john-morelands-excellent-new-record-in-the-throes/article/3860859|newspaper=[[The Oklahoman]]|date=July 10, 2013}}</ref><ref name=OKLefty-2012>{{cite web|last=Brown|first=Dave|title=10 Questions with Justin Orcutt of Okie Tone Records|url=http://oklahomalefty.blogspot.com/2012/02/10-questions-with-justin-orcutt-of-okie.html|work=Oklahoma Lefty|date=February 9, 2012}}</ref><ref name=GQ-BigBadLuv-2017>{{cite news|last1=Martin|first1=Clay Skipper, Matt|title=The New Face of Folk Rock on Why He's Glad He Didn't Grow Up "Skinny and Good-Looking"|url=https://www.gq.com/story/john-moreland-singer-songwriter-interview|work=[[GQ]]|date=June 15, 2017|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Moreland was born in [[Longview, Texas|Longview]], Texas,<ref name=TFGAD:71:2014>{{cite web|last=Gibbs|first=Otis|title=Episode 71: John Moreland|url=http://otisgibbs.com/episode-71-john-moreland/|work=Thanks for Giving a Damn with [[Otis Gibbs]]|format=podcast|date=March 10, 2014}}</ref> the son of Robert Lloyd Moreland, an engineer, and Connie May Moreland (née Brandon), a school librarian.<ref name=FamilySearch-TxBirthIndex-1985 /><ref name=GardenGun-BigBadLuv-2017>{{cite news|last1=Hendrickson|first1=Matt|title=From the Heart: John Moreland|url=http://gardenandgun.com/articles/john-moreland/|work=[[Garden & Gun]]|date=May 23, 2017}}</ref> Moreland's father worked for [[Sunoco]] as an [[Electrical engineering|electrical engineer]], and because of this job the family moved a lot.<ref name=TFGAD:71:2014/> His was a conservative [[Southern Baptist]] family.<ref name=ND-ITT-2013 /> When he was a baby they moved to [[Northern Kentucky]], across the river from [[Cincinnati]], Ohio. Moreland credits his love for [[Cincinnati Reds]] to this time in Kentucky.<ref name=TFGAD:71:2014/>

When Moreland was 10 years old Moreland's family moved from [[Boone County, Kentucky]] to [[Tulsa, Oklahoma]] and, with the help of his father, he started playing the guitar. When he was 12 or 13 he started playing with a child he went to church with who wrote songs and inspired him to start writing songs.<ref name=ARTimes-Live-2013>{{cite web|last1=Millar|first1=Lindsay|title=Live from Fellowship Hall Sound: John Moreland talks about becoming a singer/songwriter, plays '3:59 AM' |url=http://www.arktimes.com/RockCandy/archives/2013/10/24/john-moreland-talks-about-becoming-a-singersongwriter-plays-359-am|work=[[Arkansas Times]]|last2=Spradlin|first2=Greg|last3=Camp Friday Films|format=video|date=October 24, 2013}}</ref>

== Career ==
Moreland played in his first show when he was 13 or 14 years old.<ref name=OKLefty-2011 /> In the early 2000s during high school, Moreland played in local punk and hardcore bands,<ref name=9B-MichelleInt-2013>{{cite web|last=Evans|first=Michelle|title=Michelle Interviews John Moreland|url=http://ninebullets.net/archives/john-moreland-the-black-gold-band-things-i-cant-control|work=Nine Bullets|date=May 14, 2013}}</ref> including local [[metalcore]] Oklahoma band, Thirty Called Arson.<ref name=NewsOK-Carney-2013 />

Moreland put together the Black Gold Band in 2005, and released ''Endless Oklahoma Sky'' on Oklahoma City label Little Mafia Records in 2008. In 2009, he recorded the follow-up ''Things I Can't Control'' at Armstrong Recording in Tulsa with producer and musician [[Stephen Egerton (guitarist)|Stephen Egerton]] ([[Descendents]], [[All (band)|All]]).

Largely self-performed and self-produced,<ref name=ND-NGADASA-2013 /> Moreland produces music that is influenced by his Oklahoma roots,<ref name=Knoxville-2013>{{cite news|last=Cole|first=Jer|title=Band Scene: Tulsa singer-songwriter John Moreland walks alone ... and likes it that way|url=http://repubhub.icopyright.net/freePost.act?tag=3.5413?icx_id=472980|newspaper=[[Knoxville News Sentinel]]|date=December 27, 2013}}</ref> music that is "gloriously and joyfully heartbreaking."<ref name=FtM-ITT-2013>{{cite web|title=Album Review: John Moreland – In The Throes|url=http://www.farcethemusic.com/2013/06/album-review-john-moreland-in-throes.html|work=Farce the Music|date=June 12, 2013}}</ref> Moreland has released a constant stream of records (in 2011 he released two full length albums and two EPs), saying "I write a lot of songs. And I guess I feel like your most recent release kind of represents you."<ref name=OKLefty-2011>{{cite web|last=Brown|first=Dave|title=10 Questions with John Moreland|url=http://oklahomalefty.blogspot.com/2011/04/10-questions-with-john-moreland.html|work=Oklahoma Lefty|date=April 17, 2011}}</ref><ref name=UTW-2012 /> Moreland has cited [[Steve Earle]] as his "gateway"<ref name=AmericanaMusicShow-HoTH-2015>{{cite news|last1=Powers|first1=Calvin|title=Ep246 John Moreland writes for the church kids outside the punk rock show|url=https://americanamusicshow.com/ep246-john-moreland-writes-for-the-church-kids-outside-the-punk-rock-show/|work=Americana Music Show|date=May 12, 2015|format=Podcast}}</ref> to folk music. He switched genres from hardcore to folk when he heard Earle's song "Rich Man's War".<ref name=YahooMusic-HOTH-2015>{{cite news|last1=DiMartino|first1=Dave|title=John Moreland Live: All Het Up, Tulsa Style|url=https://www.yahoo.com/music/john-moreland-live-all-het-up-tulsa-style-115957262771.html|work=[[Yahoo! Music]]|date=April 9, 2015|format=Includes video interview}}</ref> His father was also a big Earle fan. Other influences were [[Guy Clark]] and [[Townes Van Zandt]].<ref name=AmSongwriter-FolkAliianceQA-2015>{{cite news|last1=Margolis|first1=Lynne|title=No Crappy Job: A Q&A with John Moreland|url=http://www.americansongwriter.com/2015/04/john-morelands-heat-wave/|work=[[American Songwriter]]|date=April 24, 2015}}</ref>

Moreland runs his own mail order business and ships his own records (packing the records, taking them to the post office himself) because all of his favorite labels ([[Ebullition Records]], [[Level Plane Records]], [[Dischord Records]]) used that method.<ref name=TFGAD:71:2014 />

In 2015, Moreland released ''High on Tulsa Heat,'' his third full-length solo record release.<ref name=WSJ-HTHStream-2015>{{cite news|last1=Danton|first1=Eric R.|title=John Moreland Takes 'Off the Cuff' Approach to 'High on Tulsa Heat' (Exclusive Album)|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2015/04/14/john-moreland-takes-off-the-cuff-approach-to-high-on-tulsa-heat-exclusive-album/|work=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=April 14, 2015}}</ref><ref name=NYTimes-HOTH-2015>{{cite news|last1=Caramanica|first1=Jon|title=Review: On John Moreland's 'High on Tulsa Heat,' World-Weariness and Clarity|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/04/21/arts/music/review-on-john-morelands-high-on-tulsa-heat-world-weariness-and-clarity.html?referrer&_r=0|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=April 20, 2015}}</ref> It was produced by Moreland and features Jesse Aycock, John Calvin Abney, Chris Foster, Jared Tyler, and Kierston White. The album was recorded quickly and informally over the course of a few days in July 2014. Moreland used his parents' home in [[Bixby, Oklahoma|Bixby]], Oklahoma, as a studio while they were out of town on vacation.<ref name=NewsOK-HTH-2015>{{cite news|last1=Carman|first1=Becky|title=This Land is Moreland's: Tulsa's John Moreland opens up on new album|url=http://newsok.com/this-land-is-morelands-tulsas-john-moreland-opens-up-on-new-album/article/5408184|work=[[The Oklahoman]]|date=April 7, 2015}}</ref> A video of the song "Cherokee" was conceived and shot by Joey Kneiser, and features bass player Bingham Barnes. Both are from the band Glossary.<ref name=NPR-Cherokee-Video-2015>{{cite news|last1=Powers|first1=Ann|title=John Moreland, 'Cherokee'|url=https://www.npr.org/event/music/398124312/john-moreland-cherokee|work=[[NPR]]|date=April 8, 2015|format=Music video}}</ref> Moreland said the song was inspired by a dream.<ref name=NPR-Cherokee-Video-2015 />

Moreland participates in the [[Folk Alliance]] International Conference, a non-profit folk music conference that is held annually in [[Kansas City, Kansas|Kansas City]], Kansas.<ref name=AmSongwriter-FolkAliianceQA-2015 /> He participated in fellow singer-songwriter [[Jason Isbell]]'s 2013 national tour.<ref name=ThisLand-2014>{{cite web|last=Wendle|first=Abby|title=John Moreland is Nervous|url=http://thislandpress.com/02/20/2014/john-moreland-is-nervous/|work=This Land Radio|format=podcast|date=February 20, 2014}}</ref>

In 2017, Moreland released his seventh album, ''Big Bad Luv,'' on 4AD.<ref name=RollingStone-BigBadLuv-2017>{{cite news|last1=Bernstein|first1=Jonathan|title=How John Moreland Became Miranda Lambert's Favorite Songwriter|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/premieres/how-john-moreland-became-miranda-lamberts-favorite-writer-w480511|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=May 4, 2017}}</ref> The title is a nod to the book by that name by [[Larry Brown (author)|Larry Brown]].<ref name=NewYorker-BigBadLuv-2017>{{cite news|last1=Petrusich|first1=Amanda|title=John Moreland's Sad National Anthems|url=http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2017/07/10/john-morelands-sad-national-anthems|work=[[The New Yorker]]|date=July 10, 2017}}</ref> The record is the first where Moreland recorded with a full band. Musicians from the bands [[Dawes (band)|Dawes]] (Griffin and [[Taylor Goldsmith]]) and [[Shovels And Rope]] (Carrie Ann Hearst and Michael Trent) contributed vocals.<ref name=PopMatters-BigBadLuv-2017>{{cite news|last1=Spencer|first1=Slaone|title=John Moreland's Feeling Some Big Bad Luv|url=http://www.popmatters.com/post/john-morelands-feeling-some-big-bad-luv/|work=[[PopMatters]]|date=May 1, 2017}}</ref>

His fifth solo album, ''LP5'', was released February 7, 2020. It's produced by [[Matt Pence]] of [[Centro-Matic]] and marks his return to the label [[Thirty Tigers]], after his previous release via 4AD.<ref name=Saving-Country-Music-2019>{{cite news|last=Trigger|title=John Moreland Announces New Album "LP5" – Releases New Song|url=https://www.savingcountrymusic.com/john-moreland-announces-new-album-lp5-releases-new-song/|work=[[Saving Country Music]]|date=October 18, 2019}}</ref>

== Performance style ==
[[File:John Moreland and the Black Gold Band.jpg|thumb|John Moreland and the Black Gold Band<br/>Oklahoma City Conservancy<br/>(June 14, 2009)]]
Moreland sometimes plays solo with an acoustic guitar, but was often accompanied by two different bands: the Black Gold Band (now defunct)<ref name=UTW-2012>{{cite news|last=Hizer|first=G.K.|title=Doing It His Own Way: John Moreland returns with a new chapter in his book of rock|url=http://www.urbantulsa.com/gyrobase/Content?oid=oid%3A50761|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120813114613/http://www.urbantulsa.com/gyrobase/Content?oid=oid%3A50761|archivedate=August 13, 2012|newspaper=Urban Tulsa Weekly|date=July 11, 2012}}</ref><ref name=9B-ThingsCantControl-2011>{{cite web|last=RomeoSidVicious|title=John Moreland & The Black Gold Band – Things I Can't Control|url=http://ninebullets.net/archives/john-moreland-the-black-gold-band-things-i-cant-control|work=Nine Bullets|date=March 22, 2011}}</ref> or the Dust Bowl Souls.<ref name=ND-ITT-2013>{{cite web|last=Reiley|first=Rick|title=Oklahoma's John Moreland- A Man of Measured Words – A Review of 'In the Throes'|url=http://www.nodepression.com/profiles/blogs/oklahoma-s-john-moreland-a-man-of-measured-words-a-review-of-in|work=[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]|date=July 27, 2013}}</ref> Though his earlier music was more rock-based, his more recent releases are characterized as being sparsely acoustic.<ref name=SCM-2013>{{cite web|last=Trigger|title=John Moreland's "In The Throes" Proves People Still Care About Songs|url=http://www.savingcountrymusic.com/john-morelands-in-the-throes-proves-people-still-care-about-songs|work=Saving Country Music|date=June 22, 2013}}</ref>

He is characterized as a songwriter's songwriter.<ref name=9B-ITT-2013>{{cite web|last=Hale|first=Charles|title=John Moreland – In the Throes|url=http://ninebullets.net/archives/john-moreland-in-the-throes|work=Nine Bullets|date=June 10, 2013}}</ref> American television host and political commentator [[Rachel Maddow]] tweeted praise of Moreland's work: "If the American music business made any sense, guys like John Moreland would be household names."<ref name=NewsOK-Year-2013>{{cite news|last=Gordon|first=Grace|title=The year in Oklahoma music|url=http://newsok.com/the-year-in-oklahoma-music/article/3917442|newspaper=[[The Oklahoman]]|date=December 23, 2013}}</ref> Moreland posits that Maddow probably saw him opening for [[Lucero (band)|Lucero]], a band Maddow likes.<ref name=TFGAD:71:2014 /> Moreland jokes that her remark was "the first time his dad has agreed with Rachel Maddow."<ref name=TFGAD:71:2014 /> During the summer of 2015, Moreland opened for [[Jason Isbell]], [[Dawes (band)|Dawes]], and [[Patty Griffin]].<ref name=WSJ-SummerTour-2015>{{cite news|last1=Dougherty|first1=Steve|title=The Sad, Sweet Songs of Oklahoma's John Moreland|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-sad-sweet-songs-of-oklahomas-john-moreland-1435676610|work=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=June 30, 2015}}</ref>

== ''Sons of Anarchy'' ==
Three of Moreland's songs, "Heaven", "Gospel", and "Your Spell", have been featured on the TV show, ''[[Sons of Anarchy]].''<ref name=ND-NGADASA-2013>{{cite web|last=Gilded Palace|title=John Moreland – 'Nobody Gives A Damn About Songs Anymore'|url=http://www.nodepression.com/profiles/blogs/gilded-palace-radio-show-john-moreland-nobody-gives-a-damn-about|work=[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]|date=June 17, 2013}}</ref><ref name=NewsOK-SoA-2013>{{cite news|last=Hall|first=Richard|title=Oklahoma musician featured in 'Sons of Anarchy' television show|url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-musician-featured-in-sons-of-anarchy-television-show/article/3898463|newspaper=[[The Oklahoman]]|date=October 28, 2013}}</ref><ref name=Tulsa-SoA-2013>{{cite news|last=Hall|first=Richard|title=Tulsa musician featured in 'Sons of Anarchy' television show|url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/scene/music/tulsa-musician-featured-in-sons-of-anarchy-television-show/article_87b446b0-4179-11e3-bab0-001a4bcf6878.html|newspaper=[[Tulsa World]]|date=October 30, 2013}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Moreland is married to visual artist Pearl Rachinsky, who did the album layout for ''Big Bad Luv.''<ref name=NewYorker-BigBadLuv-2017 /><ref name=GardenGun-BigBadLuv-2017 /><ref name=PopMatters-BigBadLuv-2017 />

Around 2015, Moreland relocated to [[Norman, Oklahoma]] but then later that year moved back to Tulsa.<ref name=AustinChronicle-Tearjerker-2015>{{cite news|last1=Graham|first1=William Harries|title=Tearjerker John Moreland|url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/daily/music/2015-01-09/tearjerker-john-moreland/|work=[[The Austin Chronicle]]|date=January 9, 2015}}</ref>
