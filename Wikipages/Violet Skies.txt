'''Hannah Berney''' (born 14 February 1991), known as '''Violet Skies''', is a singer-songwriter from [[Chepstow]] in [[Monmouthshire]], [[Wales]].<ref name=horizons />

==Musical career==
In 2013, Violet Skies released her first song, "How the Mighty". ''WalesOnline'' called it "a pulsing, classically arranged electro slow-burner of a song that merges Massive Attack with a haunting piano and the singer's equally stark and soulful vocals", and it included the song on its list of the 51 greatest Welsh pop records of 2013.<ref>Owens, David (30 December 2013). "[https://www.walesonline.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/51-greatest-welsh-pop-records-6454771 The 51 greatest Welsh pop records of 2013]". ''WelshOnline''.</ref> The same year, independent label Awkward signed her.<ref>Alimadi, Paul (12 March 2013). "[https://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/bwoywonder/violet-skies-one-to-watch-in-2014_b_4323487.html One To Watch In 2014: Violet Skies]". ''Huffington Post''.</ref>

In June 2014 she released her debut solo EP, ''Dragons'',<ref>{{cite web|title=Violet Skies – "Dragons" EP|url=https://earthboyblog.wordpress.com/2014/07/12/violet-skies-dragons-ep/|website=Earth Boy Blog|date=12 July 2014|access-date=23 January 2016}}</ref> and played at the [[Glastonbury Festival]] on the [[BBC Introducing]] Stage.<ref>{{cite web|title=Glastonbury – Violet Skies|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/errnc8/acts/anbrbp|website=BBC|access-date=23 January 2016}}</ref> That same summer, she played a headline London show and later opened for [[A Great Big World]] at [[Bush Hall]] in [[Shepherd's Bush]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Le Nurb check out Great Big World|url=http://bru-media.co.uk/lenurb/gig-review-a-great-big-world-at-bush-hall/|website=Le Nurb|access-date=23 January 2016}}</ref> In January 2015 she released a new song, "Liar".<ref>{{cite web|title=New Music: Violet Skies "Liar"|url=http://www.mobo.com/news-blogs/new-music-violet-skies-liar|website=MOBO|date=16 January 2015|access-date=23 January 2016}}</ref>

Violet Skies' records have been played by [[Huw Stephens]] on [[BBC Radio 1]], [[Jamie Cullum]] on [[BBC Radio 2]] and across a number of other stations, as well as featured in Hunger Magazine and [[NME]]. She was selected as part of BBC [[Horizons Gorwelion]] 2015, a BBC Wales and Welsh Arts Council Initiative.<ref name=horizons>{{cite web|title=Gorwelion Horizons – Violet Skies|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/1kbMSj2V5zzFHcrpBGybq84/violet-skies|website=BBC|access-date=23 January 2016}}</ref>

Violet also released an album and EP with [[Bristol]]-based producer Stumbleine through Monotreme Records in early 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Stumbleine Feat. Violet Skies 'Chasing Honeybees' 5-track CD and digital EP|url=http://monotremerecords.limitedrun.com/products/521536-stumbleine-feat-violet-skies-chasing-honeybees-5-track-cd-and-digital-ep|website=Monotreme Records|access-date=23 January 2016}}</ref>

In June 2019, skies released her new EP ''I'll Buy a House'', which contains four of her singles. A few months later, she released the acoustic version of her EP.

She has also co-written the song "[[God Is a Dancer]]" for the British singer [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]] and the Dutch DJ [[Tiësto]]. "God Is a Dancer" spent eight weeks in the United Kingdom Top 40, peaking at number 15.<ref name= amazingyear/>

On 3 April 2020 Skies released an EP, ''Lonely'', containing five songs: "Lonely", "Nothing at Night", "Half My Life", "Born On Valentine's Day" and "This Could Be Love".

==Personal life==
Born Hannah Berney in [[Chepstow]] in [[Monmouthshire]], [[Wales]],<ref name= evolving/> she was a student at [[Wyedean School]] in [[Sedbury]], where she wrote songs with friends during lunch breaks.<ref name= amazingyear>Thomas, Nicholas (23 November 2019). "[https://www.southwalesargus.co.uk/news/18055929.chepstow-singer-songwriter-violet-skies-talks-amazing-year-rise-success/ Chepstow singer-songwriter Violet Skies talks about her amazing year and rise to success]". ''South Wales Argus''.</ref> After graduating, Violet attended [[Exeter University]], where she studied French and history.<ref name= amazingyear/> While at Exeter, she was a contestant on [[The Voice UK]] in 2012.<ref>Donelson, Marcy "[https://www.allmusic.com/artist/violet-skies-mn0003228252/biography Violet Skies]". ''AllMusic''. Retrieved 5 November 2020.</ref>

She and Charlie McClean founded sheWrites, a series of woman-only camps for songwriters, producers, and artists, that convene in different places around the world, in order to encourage women to make connections, make songs, and make an impact in the music industry.<ref>Borhan, Nargis (2020). "[https://ldnmagazine.com/2020/04/30/qa-with-violet-skies/ Q&A with Violet Skies]". ''LDN''.</ref>

When asked about her musical pseudonym, Violet Skies, she said her great-grandmother's name was Violet, and Skies was her mother's idea.<ref>Davies, Tara (August 14, 2020). "[https://thetaragraphonline.wordpress.com/2020/08/14/meet-violet-skies/ Interview: Meet Violet Skies]". ''Vibez Music''.</ref>
