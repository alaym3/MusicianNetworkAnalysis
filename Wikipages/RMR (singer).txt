'''RMR''' (pronounced "rumor") is an American singer, rapper and songwriter. He first attained notoriety when his song "[[Rascal (song)|Rascal]]" went viral on [[YouTube]] in February 2020.<ref name="Rolling Stone">{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/rmr-rascal-rascal-flatts-viral-959688/|title=RMR's Rascal Flatts Homage 'Rascal' Is a Viral Hit – As Long As It Can Stay Online|first1=Charles|last1=Holmes|date=March 2, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/06/13/876073848/rmr-pre-fabulous-breakout-of-the-year-is-nothing-but-smiles|title=RMR, Pre-Fabulous Breakout Of The Year, Is Nothing But Smiles|website=NPR.org}}</ref>

He is signed to [[Warner Records]] and Cmnty Rcrds.<ref name="Billboard">{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/9359389/deals-rmr-warner-records-six-course-rca-daniel-tiger-arts-music|title=The Deals: 'Rascal' Artist RMR Joins Warner Records|date=April 16, 2020|website=Billboard}}</ref>

==Career==
In February 2020, he [[Independent music|independently released]] the song "[[Rascal (song)|Rascal]]", which quickly went viral on YouTube overnight, due to the video's content and portrayal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/music/rmr-rascal-video-going-viral/|title=RMR's Hilarious 'Rascal' Video Is A Literal Overnight Success|date=February 27, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Rolling Stone"/> He signed to [[Warner Records]] and Cmnty Rcrds in April 2020.<ref name="Billboard"/> He dropped the single "Dealer", debuting its remix days later. The remix features American rappers [[Future (rapper)|Future]] and [[Lil Baby]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/5/rmr-interview-dealer-remix-music-video-premiere-future-lil-baby|title=RMR on the Endless Influences That Move the Man Behind the Mask|website=HYPEBEAST|date=May 11, 2020}}</ref>

On June 12, 2020, he released his debut extended play, ''Drug Dealing is a Lost Art''. The eight-track EP contains features from American rappers Future, Lil Baby, [[Westside Gunn]] and [[Young Thug]].<ref name="EP"/>

On November 12, he released "The Wishing Hour", a reworking of the [[Matchbox Twenty]] song "[[3AM (Matchbox Twenty song)|3AM]]."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ross |first1=Alex Robert |title=RMR hornily reworks Matchbox Twenty's "3AM" on "The Wishing Hour" |url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/11/12/rmr-the-wishing-hour-premiere-stream-matchbox-20-3am |website=The Fader}}</ref> It was subsequently announced RMR's next project would be called ''4th Qtr Medley'', a three-part series of re-imagined songs and interconnected videos similar to "Rascal," set to be released on November 24.<ref>{{cite web |last1=2DOPEBOYZ |title=RMR – "The Wishing Hour" |url=https://2dopeboyz.com/2020/11/12/rmr-the-wishing-hour/ |website=2DOPEBOYZ}}</ref> On November 24, ''4th Qtr Medley'' was released and featured reworkings of songs from Matchbox Twenty, [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], and [[The Goo Goo Dolls]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Williams |first1=Aaron |title=RMR Completes His '4th Qtr Medley' With One Last Classic Cover |url=https://uproxx.com/music/rmr-4th-qtr-medley-full-film/ |website=Uproxx|date=November 24, 2020 }}</ref> As of 2021, the music video for ''4th Qtr Medley'' is no longer on YouTube, having been removed from RMR's channel, although the three individual songs are still accessible online.

On January 8, 2021, RMR released "Her Honeymoon", the first single off of his upcoming debut album, ''Hotel''. The accompanying music video featured three possible endings to the narrative, concluding with RMR encouraging fans to call or text his personal cell-phone to vote for their preferred ending.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mahadevan |first1=Tara C. |title=Watch RMR's 'Choose Your Own Adventure' Style Video for "Her Honeymoon" |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2021/01/rmr-her-honeymoon-video |website=Complex}}</ref>

On May 21, 2021, RMR released "Vibes" featuring [[Tyla Yaweh]]. The song is the second single from RMR's upcoming debut album ''Hotel.''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Powell|first=Jon|date=2021-05-21|title=RMR and Tyla Yaweh bring the "Vibes" on new single|url=https://www.revolt.tv/new-music/2021/5/21/22447393/rmr-tyla-yaweh-vibes-song|access-date=2021-05-23|website=REVOLT|language=en}}</ref>

On June 11, 2021, Billy Strings released a single for "Wargasm" that features RMR.

== Personal life ==
RMR was born in 1996 in [[Atlanta]], Georgia. He is secretive about his life, and remains professionally anonymous.<ref name="Rolling Stone" /> As of 2020, RMR is currently living in Los Angeles, California, having been relocated from Atlanta.<ref name="Rolling Stone" />

RMR was a panel member on A View From The Front Line, an introspective panel discussion hosted by one of his labels [[Warner Records]]. Moderated by Gail Mitchell, Executive Director of R&B/Hip-Hop for [[Billboard charts|Billboard]], the panel included artists involved in the [[George Floyd protests]] and Paul Henderson, Director of Police Accountability for the City of San Francisco.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=A View From The Front Line – a conversation about making change|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K9towiLud7k|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Warner Records YouTube channel}}</ref>

===Artistry===
RMR has received comparisons to [[Lil Nas X]] for his musical style, fusing elements of [[hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[Country music|country]] music. In addition to Rascal Flatts, he cites [[Kanye West]], [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], [[Lil Wayne]] and [[Michael Jackson]] as influences on his music.<ref>{{Cite web|title=RMR Is More Than a Mask and a Viral Moment|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/06/rmr-interview-drug-dealing-is-a-lost-art|website=Complex}}</ref>
