'''Jamell Maurice Demons''' (born May 1, 1999), known professionally as '''YNW Melly''' (backronym for '''Young Nigga World Melly'''),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-blogs/ynw-melly-everything-know-upcoming-rapper-charged-murder-2447806|title = Who is YNW Melly? Everything to know about the upcoming rapper charged with murder|website = [[NME]]|date = February 17, 2019}}</ref> is an American rapper and singer. He is best known for his songs "[[Murder on My Mind]]", "[[Mixed Personalities]]" (featuring [[Kanye West]]), "[[Suicidal (song)|Suicidal]]" (featuring [[Juice Wrld]]), and "[[223's]]" (featuring [[9lokkNine]]). The first is considered his breakout, which garnered him further attention after being charged with the double-murder of two fellow rappers in the "YNW" collective. In November 2019, he released his debut album, ''[[Melly vs. Melvin]]'', peaking number eight on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

In February 2019, he was arrested and charged with two counts of first-degree murder and faces [[life imprisonment]] or the [[Capital punishment in Florida|death penalty]] if convicted.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/ynw-melly-arrested-double-murder-794588/|title=YNW Melly Charged With Two Counts of First-Degree Murder|first1=Charles|last1=Holmes|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=February 14, 2019|access-date=February 14, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/ynw-melly-double-murder-kanye-west-1203139808/|title=YNW Melly, Kanye West Collaborator, Arrested for Double Murder|first=Jem|last=Aswad|date=February 14, 2019|publisher=Variety|access-date=February 15, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/florida-prosecutors-to-seek-death-penalty-for-ynw-melly/amp/|title=Florida Prosecutors to Seek Death Penalty for YNW Melly|access-date=April 22, 2019|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref> He is also a suspect in the 2017 murder of a sheriff's deputy in Gifford.<ref name="Complex">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/02/ynw-melly-suspect-deputy-shooting-death|title=YNW Melly Reportedly Now a Suspect in Deputy's Shooting Death|first=Trace William|last=Cowen|date=February 22, 2019|publisher=Complex|access-date=February 24, 2019}}</ref> In March 2019, Demons pleaded not guilty to the double-murder charges and is currently awaiting trial.<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8501228/ynw-melly-pleads-not-guilty-double-murder-charges YNW Melly Pleads Not Guilty to Double Murder Charges | Billboard<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

==Early life==
Jamell Demons was born on May 1, 1999, in [[Gifford, Florida]].<ref name=bill>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8471446/ynw-melly-i-am-you-mixtape-tee-grizzley-lion-king-interview|title=YNW Melly on Breakout Mixtape 'I Am You,' Interpolating Chris Brown Throwbacks & His Favorite 'Lion King' Solos|date=August 21, 2018|author=Charles Holmes|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=October 14, 2018}}</ref> He was raised by his single mother, Jamie Demons-King,<ref>{{cite web |last1=FNR TIGG |title=YNW Melly's Mom Claims She Was Threatened by Rapper's Alleged Murder Victim |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/03/ynw-melly-mom-threatened-by-rapper-alleged-murder-victim |website=[[Complex Networks|Complex]] |access-date=September 26, 2020 |date=March 13, 2019}}</ref> and does not know who his father is.  Another rapper, Donte "Tha Gift" Taylor, claims to be YNW Melly's father.<ref>{{cite web |title=YNW Melly's father remixes "Murder on My Mind" to proclaim son's innocence |url=https://www.revolt.tv/2019/4/30/20823777/ynw-melly-s-father-remixes-murder-on-my-mind-to-proclaim-son-s-innocence |website=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]] |access-date=September 26, 2020 |date=April 30, 2019}}</ref> Demons-King was 14 years old when she became pregnant with him, giving birth to him in ninth grade. Later moving to a poorer part of Gifford, his mother struggled to pay for housing and necessaries. Demons joined the [[Bloods]] gang at a young age,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tcpalm.com/story/news/crime/2019/02/15/who-florida-rapper-ynw-melly-accused-murdering-childhood-friends/2881599002/|title=Gifford rapper YNW Melly documentary gives eerie look at friendship with murdered artists|last=Moore|first=Mary Helen|website=TCPalm|language=en|access-date=November 22, 2019}}</ref> later posting his songs on [[SoundCloud]] when he was 15. In late 2015, Demons was arrested for shooting at a group of students near [[Vero Beach High School]] and subsequently convicted of aggravated battery, discharging a firearm in public, and two counts of aggravated assault, for which he served several months in prison.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/08/02/ynw-melly-virtual-i-am-you-gen-f-interview|title=How YNW Melly turned his pain into beautiful rap ballads|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=January 18, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wlna-webservice.gannettdigital.com/articleservice/view/2868483002/michigan-state-spartans/24.3.57/iphone?apiKey=57646bc6bca4811fea00000126a000bb69414f2a44f75039173cf104|title=Grand jury indictment: YNW Melly fired the gun, killing the friends he grew up with|website=USA Today|language=en|access-date=April 22, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==

=== 2016–2018: Early fame and debut projects ===
Demons adopted the stage name YNW Melly in 2016. YNW (an [[initialism]] of "Young Nigga World" or "Young New Wave") is a [[hip-hop collective]] that included Demons, Anthony "YNW Sakchaser" Williams, Christopher "[[YNW Juvy]]" Thomas Jr., and Cortlen "YNW Bortlen" Henry.<ref>{{cite web|first1=Marc|last1=Hogan|access-date=April 15, 2020|title=YNW Melly Is Charged With the Murder of Two Friends. What Happened?|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/ynw-melly-is-charged-with-the-murder-of-two-friends-what-happened/|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=February 20, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Bernadette|last=Giacomazzo|access-date=April 15, 2020|title=Everything To Know About YNW Melly & His Pending Murder Charges|url=https://hiphopdx.com/editorials/id.4240/title.everything-to-know-about-ynw-melly-his-pending-murder-charges|date=February 28, 2019|website=[[HipHopDX]]}}</ref>

In late 2017, while still incarcerated, Demons released his first project, an [[extended play|EP]] called ''Collect Call'', which contained features from numerous well-known artists, including [[Lil B]] and John Wicks. In 2018, he released the singles "Virtual (Blue Balenciagas)", "Melly the Menace", and "Slang That Iron". Other singles include "4 Real", "Butter Pecan", and "Medium Fries".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fred |first1=Thomas |title=YNW Melly |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ynw-melly-mn0003736777/biography |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=August 5, 2018}}</ref> The respective music videos have amassed 26 million, 16 million and 11 million views on [[YouTube]], as of January 2019.

In August 2018, Demons released his debut mixtape ''I Am You,'' which later appeared on the [[Billboard 200]] at number 192 on January 10, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.49905/title.hip-hop-album-sales-a-boogie-wit-da-hoodie-scores-his-first-no-1-album-on-billboard-200|title=Hip Hop Album Sales: A Boogie Wit Da Hoodie Earns His 1st No. 1 Album On Billboard 200|date=January 14, 2019|website=HipHopDX|access-date=January 18, 2019}}</ref>

=== 2019–present: Second mixtape and debut studio album ===
On January 18, 2019, while incarcerated, Demons released ''We All Shine'', his second commercial mixtape, consisting of 16 tracks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ynw-melly-drops-we-all-shine-featuring-kanye-west-new-mixtape.118819.html|title=YNW Melly Drops "We All Shine" Featuring Kanye West|website=HotNewHipHop|date=January 18, 2019|access-date=January 18, 2019}}</ref> The project featured collaborations with [[Kanye West]] and Fredo Bang.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.49950/title.ynw-melly-releases-we-all-shine-project-with-kanye-west-guest-appearance|title=YNW Melly Releases "We All Shine" Project With Kanye West Guest Appearance|date=January 18, 2019|website=HipHopDX|access-date=January 18, 2019}}</ref> A music video for "Mixed Personalities" featuring West was released with the album.

As of March 2019, Demons had amassed over 200 million streams on [[Spotify]] with over 10 million monthly listeners. His most streamed song was "[[Murder on My Mind]]", which was originally released as a single before being added to ''I Am You''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/1cNDP5yjU5vjeR8qMf4grg|title=YNW Melly|website=Spotify|access-date=February 14, 2019}}</ref>

On November 22, 2019, Demons released his debut studio album, ''[[Melly vs. Melvin]]''. It peaked at number eight on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], making it his highest charting album at the time. The album included the singles "[[223's]]", which peaked at number 34 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].

On March 13, 2020, Demons released a remix of his track "[[Suicidal (song)|Suicidal]]", making it the second single from ''Melly vs. Melvin''. The remix included a feature from late American rapper and singer [[Juice Wrld]], making it his third official posthumous feature. The song has peaked at number 20 on the Hot 100, becoming Melly's second top 20 entry.

==Legal issues==

Demons was arrested on October 19, 2015 on three counts of aggravated assault with a deadly weapon and one count of discharging a firearm in public, after firing shots at three people near [[Vero Beach High School]].<ref>{{cite news|title = Teen charged with firing gun at group near Vero Beach High School|url = http://archive.tcpalm.com/news/teen-charged-with-firing-gun-at-group-near-vero-beach-high-school-ep-1329429693-337666951.html/|work = TC Palm|date = October 20, 2015|access-date = July 1, 2021}}</ref> Demons spent a year in jail before being released on probation.<ref name="thefader.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/08/02/ynw-melly-virtual-i-am-you-gen-f-interview|title=How YNW Melly turned his pain into beautiful rap ballads|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=July 1, 2021}}</ref>

In 2017, Demons was arrested for violating probation, and spent several months in jail before being released in March 2018.<ref name="thefader.com"/>

Demons was arrested on June 30, 2018, in [[Fort Myers, Florida]] for possession of [[Cannabis (drug)|marijuana]], possession of weapon or ammunition by a convicted felon, and [[drug paraphernalia]].<ref>{{cite book|title=Jamell Maurice Demons Arrest Information|date=June 30, 2018|publisher=Lee County Sheriff's Office |location=Ft Myers, FL|url=http://www.sheriffleefl.org/how_do_i/learn_more_about/arrest_search/index.php?view=inmateDetail&id=881998 |access-date=June 30, 2018}}</ref>

Demons was again arrested on January 3, 2019, in Fort Myers for possession of marijuana.<ref>{{cite book|title=Jamell Maurice Demons Arrest Information|date=January 3, 2019|publisher=Lee County Sheriff's Office |location=Ft Myers, FL|url=http://www.sheriffleefl.org/how_do_i/learn_more_about/arrest_search/index.php?view=inmateDetail&id=894574 |access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref>

On February 12, 2019, Demons was charged with two counts of [[first-degree murder]] in connection with the October 2018 shooting deaths in [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida]] of two YNW associates described as his close friends, rappers YNW Sakchaser and [[YNW Juvy]].<ref>{{cite book|url=http://apps.sheriff.org/ArrestSearch/InmateDetail/371900050|title=Jamell Maurice Demons Arrest Information|date=February 13, 2019|publisher=Miramar Police, FL|location=Miramar, FL|access-date=February 13, 2019}}</ref> Authorities purport that Demons conspired with fellow YNW rapper Cortlen Henry to stage the double-murder of Williams and Thomas Jr. and make it appear as if they were fatally injured in a [[drive-by shooting]]. Henry allegedly drove the victims to the hospital where they later succumbed to their wounds. Demons turned himself in on February 13, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blavity.com/florida-rapper-ynw-melly-turns-himself-in-after-being-charged-in-the-killings-of-his-best-friends|title=Florida Rapper YNW Melly Turns Himself In After Being Charged In The Killings Of His Best Friends - Blavity|last1=Johnson|first1=Jazzi|website=blavity.com|access-date=August 5, 2019}}</ref> He announced on his [[Instagram]]:<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/02/14/us/ynw-melly-rapper-murder/index.html|title=Rising rapper YNW Melly grieved publicly for two friends. Police say he killed them|first=Eric |last=Levenson|website=CNN|date=February 14, 2019|access-date=April 23, 2020}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
On April 2, 2020, Demons (via management) told fans on [[Twitter]] that he tested positive for [[COVID-19]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/ynw-melly-tests-positive-for-covid-19-in-jail/|title=YNW Melly Tests Positive for COVID-19 in Jail|website=Pitchfork|date=April 2, 2020|language=en|access-date=April 3, 2020}}</ref> He was in the process of trying to get an early release from prison due to health concerns, as [[6ix9ine]] was released from prison due to [[asthma]] and [[bronchitis]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.insider.com/tekashi-6ix9ine-home-confinement-request-approved-coronavirus-outbreak-2020-4|title=Tekashi 6ix9ine has been released from prison to serve the rest of his sentence at home because of the coronavirus|last=McLaughlin|first=Kelly|website=Insider|access-date=April 3, 2020}}</ref> On April 14, his motion was denied.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/04/ynw-melly-denied-prison-release-after-contracting-coronavirus|title=Judge Reportedly Orders YNW Melly to Remain in Jail Despite COVID-19 Diagnosis|last=Espinoza|first=Joshua|date=April 15, 2020|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref>
