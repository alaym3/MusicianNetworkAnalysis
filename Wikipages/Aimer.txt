{{nihongo|'''Aimer'''|エメ|Eme|{{IPA-ja|eme|}}}} is a [[J-pop|Japanese pop]] singer and [[lyricist]] signed to [[Sacra Music|SACRA MUSIC]] and managed by FOURseam. Her name comes from the verb "Aimer" in [[French language|French]], which means "to love".<ref name="J-Network" /> Aimer uses the name '''Aimerrhythm''' for lyrics credits.<ref name="Aimr" />

==Early life==
Her father was the bassist of a band, so Aimer grew up surrounded by music from a very young age. She began studying the piano in elementary school and highly looked up to [[Ringo Sheena]] and [[Utada Hikaru]]. In junior high school, listening to [[Avril Lavigne]] inspired her to take up guitar and began writing English lyrics. At the age of 15, Aimer lost her voice due to over-usage of her vocal cords and was forced to undergo silence therapy for treatment. However, that did not stop her, and after she recovered, she acquired her distinctive husky voice.<ref name="J-Network">{{cite web|url=https://jnetworkonline.com/2016/12/07/stepping-into-the-light-an-interview-with-aimer/|title=Stepping Into the Light: An Interview with Aimer|website=J-Network|date=December 7, 2016|access-date=November 11, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161222234709/https://jnetworkonline.com/2016/12/07/stepping-into-the-light-an-interview-with-aimer/|archive-date=December 22, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Barks">''Barks''. ==Career==
Aimer teamed up with the "Agehasprings" group, which has worked with, produced, or provided music for various artists, including [[Yuki (singer)|Yuki]], [[Mika Nakashima]], [[Flumpool]], [[Superfly (band)|Superfly]], [[Yuzu (band)|Yuzu]], and [[Genki Rockets]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ageha.net/#!agehatop|title=Agehasprings|website=Agehasprings Official|access-date=December 18, 2013|language=ja}}</ref> In 2011, her musical career began in earnest. In May 2011, they released the [[concept album]] ''Your favorite things''. It covered numerous popular works, including works in various genre such as [[jazz]] and [[Country music|country]] western music. The number 1 record [[Cover version|cover]] was based on [[Lady Gaga]]'s "[[Poker Face (Lady Gaga song)|Poker Face]]", and was the first song to appear in the lead album chart for the jazz category in the [[iTunes Store]]. The album reached number 2.

On September 7, 2011, Aimer debuted for [[Defstar Records]], with the song "Rokutousei no Yoru", which was selected by Fuji TV as the ending theme of the 2011 anime series ''[[No. 6]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/54543|title=新人シンガーAimer「NO.6」EDテーマでメジャーデビュー|trans-title=Major debut of rookie singer Aimer for ''No.6'' ED theme|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=August 9, 2011|access-date=May 16, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> "Rokutousei no Yoru" recorded its highest ranking at number 9 on the [[Recochoku]] music distribution chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://recochoku.jp|title=Recochoku chart|access-date=October 10, 2011|language=ja}}</ref> The second single was released on December 14, 2011. "Re:pray/ Sabishikute Nemurenai Yoru wa" reached number 1 in the [[mora (music store)|Mora]] music download charts site.<ref>{{cite news|website=Utamap News|url=https://news.utamap.com/music/114539/|title=Aimerインターネットライブに２万人が感動と涙!!スペシャルゲストに話題のくまモンが登場!?|date=December 18, 2011|access-date=May 15, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> The song "Re: pray" was chosen as the 29th ending theme for the anime ''[[Bleach (anime)|Bleach]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/56444|title=Aimer、2ndシングルが「BLEACH」エンディング曲に抜擢|trans-title=Aimer's 2nd single is selected for ''Bleach'' ending song|website=Natalie|date=September 13, 2011|access-date=May 16, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> The single includes a cover of [[Lady Gaga]]'s "Poker Face". The song was previously featured in Aimer's ''Your Favorite Things'', a cover album that was released during her indie era.

On February 22, 2012, she released her third single, "Yuki no Furumachi/Fuyu no Diamond", which had the theme of "winter" on all tracks. On May 11, 2012, Aimer released a digital single called "Hoshikuzu Venus". This single was intended to be the theme song and background music for [[Sasaki Nozomi (fashion model)|Sasaki Nozomi]]'s drama, ''Koi nante Zeitaku ga Watashi ni Ochite Kuru no daro ka?''. This drama aired on April 16, 2012. On August 15, Aimer released her fourth single, "Anata ni Deawanakereba: Kasetsu Toka / Hoshikuzu Venus", which included a cover of [[Neil Sedaka]]'s "[[Breaking Up Is Hard to Do]]". The first track of this single "Anata ni Deawanakereba: Kasetsu Toka" is an ending song of [[Fuji TV]]'s anime series ''[[Natsuyuki Rendezvous]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.comtrya.com/2012/aimer-anata-ni-deawanakereba-natsuyuki-fuyuhana-ed-anime-natsuyuki-rendezvous/|title=Aimer - "Anata ni Deawanakereba Natsuyuki Fuyuhana" - ED anime ''Natsuyuki Rendezvous''|website=Comtrya.com|date=August 13, 2012|access-date=May 16, 2018|archive-date=May 17, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180517082016/https://www.comtrya.com/2012/aimer-anata-ni-deawanakereba-natsuyuki-fuyuhana-ed-anime-natsuyuki-rendezvous/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On March 20, 2013, Aimer released the single "[[Re: I Am]]", which was used as the ending theme for the penultimate episode of ''[[Mobile Suit Gundam Unicorn]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/82988|title=Aimer新曲は「ガンダムUC」新エピソード主題歌|trans-title=Aimer's new song be the theme song of ''Gundam UC'' 's new episode|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=January 11, 2013|access-date=May 16, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> According to an interview, the song title "is an anagram of [her] name (Aimer), and has the meaning of breaking down a word and constructing it into another."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/pp/aimer06|last=Narimatsu|first=Satoshi|title=Aimer・インタビュー|trans-title=Aimer・Interview|website=Natalie|date=June 25, 2014|access-date=December 18, 2014|language=ja}}</ref> Aimer's eighth single, "[[Brave Shine]]", appeared as the second opening theme to the ''[[Fate/stay night: Unlimited Blade Works]]'' anime, and was released on June 3, 2015.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2015-02-15/aimer-to-sing-fate-stay-night-unlimited-blade-works-2nd-season-opening/.85004|title=Aimer to sing ''Fate/stay night Unlimited Blade Works'' ' 2nd Season opening|website=[[Anime News Network]]|date=February 16, 2015|access-date=May 16, 2018|last=Loveridge|first=Lynzee}}</ref> Another song, "Last Stardust", which was also a candidate for the opening, appears as an insert song in episode #20.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.crunchyroll.com/anime-news/2015/08/08-1/takashi-takeuchi-fatestay-nightunlimited-blade-works-ii-blu-ray-set-box-art-previewed|title=Takashi Takeuchi ''Fate/stay night[Unlimited Blade Works] II'' Blu-ray Set Box Art previewed|website=[[Crunchyroll]]|date=August 8, 2015|access-date=May 16, 2018|last=Green|first=Scott}}</ref>

On August 18, 2016, Aimer announced her special 4th studio album ''[[Daydream (Aimer album)|Daydream]]'' to be released on September 21 that features collaboration songs between her and popular artists such as [[Takahiro Moriuchi|Taka]] ([[One Ok Rock|ONE OK ROCK]]), [[Yojiro Noda]] ([[Radwimps|RADWIMPS]]), [[Toru Kitajima|TK]] ([[Ling tosite Sigure|Ling tosite sigure]]), chelly ([[Egoist (band)|EGOIST]]), Takahito Uchisawa ([[androp]]), [[Hiroyuki Sawano]], [[Sukima Switch]], and [[Mao Abe]]. Taka additionally produced four new songs for this album, while TK provided two. Also includes the ending theme of ''[[Kabaneri of the Iron Fortress]]'' (anime) made in collaboration with chelly ([[Egoist (band)|EGOIST]]) and Hiroyuki Sawano and more for 13 tracks total. The album was released in three versions: a limited CD+Blu-ray (Type-A), a limited CD+DVD edition (Type-B), and a regular CD only edition. The song "Falling Alone" was used as lead track and was previously released as her 10th digital single as advance of the album.<ref>{{cite news|website=[[Rockin'On Japan]]|url=https://rockinon.com/news/detail/147379?rtw/|title=Takaが新たに4曲提供！ Aimer、新アルバムリリース決定。野田洋次郎、TKらがコメント|trans-title=Taka offers 4 new songs! Yojiro Noda, TK and others comment|date=August 18, 2016|access-date=August 30, 2016|language=ja}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/198686/|title=AimerアルバムにワンオクTaka、androp内澤、時雨TK、RAD野田ら提供曲|trans-title=Aimer's collaborations with One Ok Rock's Taka, Androp's Nakazawa, TK, Radwimp's Noda and others|date=August 18, 2016|access-date=August 30, 2016|language=ja}}</ref>

On August 20, 2016 Aimer revealed her face for the first time on ''[[Music Station]]'' with the song "Chouchou Musubi".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/198056|title=次週「Mステ」に初登場Aimer、Cocco、ユニコーン、AKB48、中山優馬、リトグリ|trans-title=First appearance in "M. Station" next week Aimer, Cocco, Unicorn, AKB 48, Yuma Nakayama, Ritoguri|language=ja|website=Natalie|date=August 29, 2016|access-date=May 16, 2018}}</ref> Following the announcement of her 4th album ''[[Daydream (Aimer album)|Daydream]]'', Aimer performed the ending song to the Season 5 of anime series ''[[Natsume's Book of Friends]]'', "Akane Sasu".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/207814|title=Aimer、茜色の空が美しく広がる「茜さす」MV|trans-title=The sky spreads beautifully in Aimer's "Akane Sasu" MV|website=Natalie|language=ja|date=November 2, 2016|access-date=December 10, 2017}}</ref>

On January 5, 2017 it was announced that Aimer wrote a song for the Japanese culinary exhibition "Tabegamisama no Fushigina Resutoranten" directed by the multimedia entertainment studio [[Moment Factory]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s_Wh9Cmde8A|title=「食神さまの不思議なレストラン展」CM (feat. Aimer)|trans-title="A magical restaurant exhibition of food God" CM (featuring Aimer)|website=[[YouTube]]|publisher=アートプロジェクト日本橋 兜町・茅場町 (Art project Nihonbashi Kabutocho · Kayabacho)|date=January 5, 2017|access-date=March 25, 2017|language=ja}}</ref> A day after it was revealed on Aimer's official Twitter profile that the name of the song for the exhibition would be "Kachō Fūgetsu".<ref>{{cite web|quote=Aimer's new song "Kachō Fūgetsu" will be used for the culinary exhibition "Tabegamisama no Fushigina Resutoranten"|url=https://twitter.com/Aimer_and_staff/status/817280037070127104|title=Aimer&staff post on Twitter|website=Official [[Twitter]]|language=ja|date=January 6, 2017|access-date=May 10, 2017}}</ref> Later, it was also revealed another single, which would be used as opening theme for the Japanese drama ''Ubai ai, fuyu'', titled "Kogoesōna Kisetsu Kara", and would be released on February 10, 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/217052|title=Aimer、悲しく切ない「奪い愛、冬」OPテーマ配信リリース|trans-title=Aimer, ''Ubai ai, fuyu'' OP theme release|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=January 16, 2017|access-date=May 1, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> The compilation albums ''[[Best Selection (Aimer album)#Best Selection "blanc"|Best Selection "blanc"]]'' and ''[[Best Selection (Aimer album)#Best Selection "noir"|Best Selection "noir"]]'' were released on May 3, 2017. On November 11, Aimer released the single "[[One (Aimer song)|ONE]]", which peaked at #2 on both [[Oricon]] and [[Billboard Japan Hot 100|Billboard Japan]] charts.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/542958/products/1245353/1/ |title=ONE/花の唄/六等星の夜 Magic Blue ver.(初回生産限定盤) |trans-title=ONE / Hana no Uta / Rokutosei no Yoru Magic Blue ver. |publisher=[[Oricon]] |access-date=2019-11-11}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/japan-hot-100/2017-10-28 |title=Japanese Music: Top Japanese Songs Chart |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=2017-10-28|access-date=2019-11-11}}</ref>

Her single "Ref:rain" was released digitally on February 18, 2018 and received a physical release on February 21, 2018. The song is used as the ending theme to the 2018 anime television series ''[[Koi wa Ameagari no You ni]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=''Koi wa Ameagari no You ni'' TV Anime Reveals New Visual, Main Staff|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2017-11-02/koi-wa-ameagari-no-you-ni-tv-anime-reveals-new-visual-main-staff/.123502|publisher=[[Anime News Network]]|access-date=November 2, 2017|date=November 2, 2017|last=Hodgkins|first=Crystalyn}}</ref> On September 7, 2018, Aimer released the single "Black Bird", which was used as the theme song for the Japanese [[Live action|live-action film]] adaptation of the manga ''[[Kasane (manga)|Kasane]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2018-07-06/live-action-kasane-film-trailer-previews-aimer-song/.133916|title=Live-Action Kasane Film's Trailer Previews Aimer Song|website=Anime News Network|date=July 6, 2018|access-date=September 12, 2018|last=Sherman|first=Jennifer}}</ref>

On January 8, 2019, Aimer released the single "I beg you" for the anime film ''[[Fate/stay night: Heaven's Feel II. lost butterfly]]''. The song became her first #1 single on Oricon's chart.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/542958/products/1303114/1/ |title=I beg you/花びらたちのマーチ/Sailing(期間生産限定盤) |trans-title=I beg you / Hanabiratachi no March / Sailing |publisher=[[Oricon]] |access-date=2019-11-11}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2019-01-15/aimer-fate-stay-night-heaven-feel-ii-song-tops-weekly-chart/.142061 |title=Aimer's Fate/stay night Heaven's Feel II Song Tops Weekly Chart |first=Rafael Antonio |last=Pineda |website=[[Anime News Network]] |date=2019-01-15 |access-date=2019-11-15}}</ref> Aimer released two simultaneous albums, ''[[Sun Dance (album)|Sun Dance]]'' and ''[[Penny Rain]]'', on April 10, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/international/8496142/japan-aimer-sun-dance-penny-rain-album-release-date-april |title=Japanese Singer Aimer's 'Sun Dance' & 'Penny Rain' Albums Due in April |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=2019-02-01 |access-date=2019-11-11}}</ref> She recorded the ending theme song "Torches" for the anime adaptation of the manga series ''[[Vinland Saga (manga)|Vinland Saga]]''.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Rafael Antonio Pineda |title=Aimer Performs Vinland Saga Anime's Ending Theme Song |url=http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2019-06-09/aimer-performs-vinland-saga-anime-ending-theme-song/.147665 |website=[[Anime News Network]] |access-date=June 10, 2019 |date=June 9, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.moshimoshi-nippon.jp/223288 |title=TV Anime Vinland Saga's New Trailer Shows ED Theme 'Torches' by Aimer |website=Moshi Moshi Nippon |date=June 16, 2019 |access-date=March 20, 2020}}</ref> She released her 18th single "Haru wa Yuku" on March 25, 2020. The song serves as the main theme of the anime film ''[[Fate/stay night: Heaven's Feel III. spring song]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2019-12-14/aimer-sings-final-fate-stay-night-heaven-feel-anime-film-theme/.154372 |title=Aimer Sings ''Final Fate/stay night: Heaven's Feel'' Anime Film's Theme |first=Egan |last=Loo |website=[[Anime News Network]] |date=December 15, 2019 |access-date=March 20, 2020}}</ref> Aimer's most recent song, "Spark-Again" has become the opening theme to the beginning to season 2 of the hit series [[List_of_Fire_Force_episodes|''Fire Force'']].
