'''Jeon Jung-kook''' ({{Korean|hangul=전정국}}; born September 1, 1997), known mononymously as '''Jungkook''', is a South Korean singer and songwriter. He is the youngest member and<!-- Do NOT add MAIN; it will be deleted as per consensus--> vocalist of the South Korean boy band [[BTS]].

== Career ==
=== 2013–present: BTS ===
{{Main|BTS}}
On June 12, 2013, Jungkook made his debut as a member of [[BTS]] with the release of the single ''[[2 Cool 4 Skool]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/38336/bts-releases-debut-video|title=BTS Asks about Your Dreams in 'No More Dream' MV|author=Grace Danbi Hong|publisher=Mwave|date=June 12, 2013|access-date=January 31, 2016|archive-date=February 5, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150205172617/http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/38336/bts-releases-debut-video|url-status=live}}</ref> Under BTS, he has sung three solo songs; the first, pop track "Begin" from the 2016 album ''[[Wings (BTS album)|Wings]]'', told his story of moving to Seoul at a young age to become an idol and expresses his gratitude towards his fellow members for taking care of him during that time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fuse.tv/2016/10/bts-wings-album-the-significance-of-solo-members-songs|title=The Significance of Each BTS Member Having Their Own Solo on 'Wings'|work=[[Fuse (magazine)|Fuse]]|date=October 13, 2018|access-date=October 13, 2018|last=Benjamin|first=Jeff|archive-date=March 29, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190329025300/https://www.fuse.tv/2016/10/bts-wings-album-the-significance-of-solo-members-songs|url-status=live}}</ref> The second, a [[future bass]] song titled "[[Euphoria (BTS song)|Euphoria]]", was released with an accompanying nine-minute short film on April 5, 2018, as the introduction to the third part of BTS' "Love Yourself" series.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8289670/bts-love-yourself-series-euphoria-theme-tease-next-album|title=BTS Tease Next Album In 'Love Yourself' Series With 'Euphoria' Theme Video|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Herman|first=Tamar|date=April 5, 2018|access-date=April 24, 2018|archive-date=January 31, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200131092543/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8289670/bts-love-yourself-series-euphoria-theme-tease-next-album|url-status=live}}</ref> The full studio version was included on their ''[[Love Yourself: Answer]]'' compilation album, released August 24, 2018.<ref name="FB" /> "Euphoria" was produced by [[DJ Swivel]] and charted at number five on the ''Billboard'' [[Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles|Bubbling Under Hot 100]] chart. The third solo, off the band's 2020 studio album ''[[Map of the Soul: 7]]'', is a strobing R&B song about forgoing teenage experiences because of his career.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/bts-map-of-the-soul-7/|title=BTS: Map of the Soul: 7|first=Sheldon|last=Pearce|date=February 25, 2020|access-date=February 8, 2021|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|archive-date=May 7, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200507154538/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/bts-map-of-the-soul-7/|url-status=live}}</ref> "My Time" charted at number 84 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. "Euphoria" and "My Time" are the first and second longest charting solo tracks among K-pop singers on ''Billboard''{{'}}s [[World Digital Song Sales]] chart, having spent 85 and 81 weeks respectively on the ranking.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/312/0000507214|script-title=ko:방탄소년단 정국 솔로 2곡 美 빌보드 차트 韓아이돌 솔로 최장 1위·2위|trans-title=BTS Jungkook's 2 solo songs topped the US Billboard Chart for the longest time as a Korean idol solo|last=Kim|first=Soon-shin|date=October 24, 2021|website=TenAsia|language=ko|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211024041912/https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/312/0000507214|archive-date=October 24, 2021|url-status=live|access-date=October 24, 2021|via=Naver}}</ref>

Jungkook has been credited as the main producer for two of BTS' songs: "Love is Not Over" and "Magic Shop".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://kpopherald.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=201805210954156737846_2|title=[V Report] BTS' Jimin talks about returning to BBMAs, Jungkook-sketched track 'Magic Shop'|access-date=October 24, 2018|archive-date=October 24, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181024112945/http://kpopherald.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=201805210954156737846_2|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=052&aid=0001121484|title=[지승훈&케이지의 세팍타크로] 방탄소년단·엑소의 숨은 명곡을 찾아라|access-date=October 24, 2018|language=ko|archive-date=May 21, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200521143924/https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=052&aid=0001121484|url-status=live}}</ref>

On October 25, 2018,  Jungkook (together with the rest of the BTS members) was ordained with the fifth-class Hwagwan [[Order of Cultural Merit (Korea)|Order of Cultural Merit]] by the [[President of South Korea]].<ref>{{Citation|last=SBS 뉴스|title='문화훈장' 방탄소년단(BTS) 7인7색 소감 "국가대표의 마음으로" (풀영상) / SBS|date=October 24, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UgyBS8FU2lY|access-date=October 25, 2018|archive-date=February 29, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200229042826/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UgyBS8FU2lY|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://entertain.naver.com/ranking/read?oid=312&aid=0000354692|title='대중문화예술상' BTS, 문화훈장 화관 최연소 수훈...유재석부터 故 김주혁까지 '영광의 얼굴들' (종합)|access-date=October 25, 2018|language=ko|archive-date=June 16, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200616023357/https://entertain.naver.com/ranking/read?oid=312&aid=0000354692|url-status=live}}</ref> In July 2021, he was appointed [[Office of the President (South Korea)|Special Presidential Envoy for Future Generations and Culture]] by [[Moon Jae-in|President Moon Jae-in]], along with the other members of BTS, to help "lead the global agenda for future generations, such as sustainable growth" and "expand South Korea's diplomatic efforts and global standing" in the international community.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2021/07/21/skorea-BTS-President-Moon-Jae-in-special-envoy/6491626854465/|title=BTS named special presidential envoy by South Korea|last=Maresca|first=Thomas|date=July 21, 2021|work=[[United Press International]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210721091025/https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2021/07/21/skorea-BTS-President-Moon-Jae-in-special-envoy/6491626854465/|archive-date=July 21, 2021|url-status=live|access-date=October 3, 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
In 2017, he collapsed during a concert in Chile. It was revealed afterwards he had been feeling unwell that day and there were no underlying issues.<ref>{{cite web |title=BTS: When Jungkook collapsed during K-pop group's Chile concert and members panicked, watch |url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/entertainment/music/bts-when-jungkook-collapsed-during-k-pop-group-s-chile-concert-and-members-panicked-watch-101631460832756.html |website=Hindustan Times |access-date=22 October 2021 |date=September 13, 2021}}</ref> In 2018, while on the [[Love Yourself World Tour]] he suffered an injury to his heel making him unable to participate in choreography.
