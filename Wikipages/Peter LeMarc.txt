'''Peter LeMarc''' (born '''Peter Lenn Marc Fransson''', 23 October 1958) is a [[Sweden|Swedish]] singer and songwriter.<ref>[http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/article15485570.ab Aftonbladet: Peter LeMarc: "Jag kände att jorden rämnade"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120925045716/http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/article15485570.ab |date=2012-09-25 }} {{in lang|sv}}</ref>
He made his record debut in 1982 and broke through in 1987, when the album ''Peter LeMarc'' sold gold. Since then he has released several successful albums that have sold [[Music recording certification|gold and platinum]], and been awarded seven [[Grammis|Swedish Grammy Awards]].<ref>[https://grammis.se/om-grammis/grammisvinnarna-genom-aren/ "Grammisvinnare genom åren".] Swedish Grammy. Read 23 november 2020.</ref>

==Biography== 
===Background=== 
Peter LeMarc was born in 1958 in the industrial town of [[Trollhättan]], to parents May and Lennart. His middle name, Lenn-Marc (also spelled ''Lenmarc'') was a compromise; his mother wanted his name to be Marcus, while his father prompted on the name Lennart.
<ref>[https://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/a/ngy4X5/nar-jag-val-kom-upp-pa-scenen-fick-dom-slita-bort-mej Aftonbladet: Peter LeMarc: "När jag väl kom upp på scenen fick dom slita bort mej"] 2005-08-24 {{in lang|sv}}</ref> 

At a young age, LeMarc discovered music through [[The Beatles]], [[Bob Dylan]], [[The Rolling Stones]] and other bands of the 1960s and 70s. In his teenage years, he played in a number of bands in the area around [[Trollhättan]]. <Br>
His father died of cancer in 1977, something LeMarc often returns to in his songs, such as "Jag ska gå hel ur det här", "Närmare gränsen" and "Drivved".<ref>[http://www.lemarc.se/biografi Peter LeMarc: "Peter LeMarc, Biografi"] 2020-11-23 {{in lang|sv}}</ref> 

LeMarc moved to [[Gothenburg]] in 1979 and joined the band Box 81. In 1982 he moved to [[Stockholm]]. Soon after he released his debut album ''Buick''. The record was well received in the press, but struggled to achieve commercial success. Two more albums met the same fate.<ref>[http://www.lemarc.se/biografi Peter LeMarc: "Peter LeMarc, Biografi"] 2020-11-23 {{in lang|sv}}</ref>

===Breakthrough=== 
By 1987 LeMarc had created a reputation as a prolific live artist. In august LeMarc released his fifth album, which would become his huge breakthrough, selling gold (50,000 copies) and earned him his first [[Grammis|Swedish Grammy Award]].<ref>[https://grammis.se/om-grammis/grammisvinnarna-genom-aren/ "Grammisvinnare genom åren".] Swedish Grammy. Read 23 november 2020.</ref> 

In February 1990 ''Välkommen hem!'' was released, earning him another gold record.<ref>[http://www.lemarc.se/biografi Peter LeMarc: "Peter LeMarc, Biografi"] 2020-11-23 {{in lang|sv}}</ref>  The album was followed by an acclaimed tour, resulting in LeMarc receiving another Grammy, as lyricist of the year.<ref>[https://grammis.se/om-grammis/grammisvinnarna-genom-aren/ "Grammisvinnare genom åren".] Swedish Grammy. Read 23 november 2020.</ref> 

In August 1991, ''Sången dom spelar när filmen är slut'' was released to wide acclaim. It contained some of his most prominent songs, such as "Little Willie John", "Evelina" and "Ett av dom sätt". The record soon sold platinum (100,000 copies).<ref>[http://www.lemarc.se/biografi Peter LeMarc: "Peter LeMarc, Biografi"] 2020-11-23 {{in lang|sv}}</ref> <br> ''Det finns inget bättre'' came out in December 1992 and gave him his second platinum record, as well as two Grammys : one for lyricist of the year and one for video of the year.<ref>[http://www.lemarc.se/biografi Peter LeMarc: "Peter LeMarc, Biografi"] 2020-11-23 {{in lang|sv}}</ref>

LeMarc premiered his most acclaimed tour to date in his hometown of Trollhättan, in February 1993. The concert met immediate critical praise. THis appearances at [[Cirkus (Stockholm)|Cirkus]] in Stockholm were later released as the live album ''Buona Sera! Peter LeMarc Livslevande''.<ref>[http://www.lemarc.se/biografi Peter LeMarc: "Peter LeMarc, Biografi"] 2020-11-23 {{in lang|sv}}</ref> 

In March 1995, ''Bok med blanka sidor'' was released, featuring prominent songs such as "Tess" and "Fyra steg i det blå". The record sold gold and LeMarc received another Grammis, as Male artist of the year. In 1996 the two collections ''LeMarcologi'' and ''LeMarxism'' were released. The former sold platinum. In March 1997, LeMarc entered the Decibel studio in [[Stockholm]] to record his eleventh studio album - ''Nio broars väg''. The album would go on to sell platinum.<ref>[http://www.lemarc.se/biografi Peter LeMarc: "Peter LeMarc, Biografi"] 2020-11-23 {{in lang|sv}}</ref>

===Later career=== 
After enjoying a hugely popular career from 1987 to 1997, and after a hiatus, he enjoyed a comeback starting 2003 onwards with a series of new albums reaching the top of the Swedish Albums Chart.   

In addition to a prolific solo career, he has also written a number of songs for a variety of artists including [[Anna-Lotta Larsson]], [[Jerry Williams (singer)|Jerry Williams]], [[Totta Näslund]], [[Christina Lindberg (singer)|Christina Lindberg]], [[Sven-Ingvars]] and has as well produced the [[Eldkvarn (band)|Eldkvarn]] album ''Limbo''.

==Personal life==
Peter LeMarc has two children and resides with his wife in Stockholm.<ref>[https://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/musik/peter-lemarc-vissa-man-tycker-att-jag-gor-sentimentalt-krafs Dagens Nyheter: Peter LeMarc: "Peter LeMarc: Vissa män tycker att jag gör sentimentalt krafs"] {{in lang|sv}}</ref>

It was announced that he would perform his last concert at the Stockholm Music & Arts festival in August 2014.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.svd.se/kultur/3796138.svd |title=Archived copy |access-date=2015-05-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140804225309/http://www.svd.se/kultur/3796138.svd |archive-date=2014-08-04 |url-status=live}}</ref>
