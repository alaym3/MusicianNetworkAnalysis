'''Cheat Codes''' is an American [[electronic music]] DJ trio. Consisting of [[KEVI]] (Kevin Ford / Prince$$ Rosie), Trevor Dahl, and Matthew Russell based in Los Angeles, the group is notable for their 2016 single "[[Sex (Cheat Codes and Kris Kross Amsterdam song)|Sex]]", which samples the chorus from "[[Let's Talk About Sex]]" by [[Salt-N-Pepa]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Cheat Codes {{!}} Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/cheat-codes-mn0003391646/biography|work=Allmusic|access-date=June 6, 2016}}</ref> and their 2017 single "[[No Promises (Cheat Codes song)|No Promises]]", which featured American popstar [[Demi Lovato]] and peaked within the top 40 of the US, UK and Australia. The track was [[Certified platinum|certified Platinum]] by the [[RIAA]].

==History==
===2014–2015: Formation and debut singles===
Prior to the 2014 formation of the trio, Trevor and Matt lived together and later wrote and recorded music with Kevin. Their name was inspired by Kevin's brother who told him that he had found the "[[Cheating in video games#Cheat codes|cheat code]]" to getting anything he wanted in life. "We really embody the idea that anything is possible. To us, the real 'cheat code' to life is loving what you do and we hope to convey that energy through our music," they said in an interview.<ref name="Free Bike Valet">{{cite web|last=Frazier|first=Dan|title=Cheat Codes (Interview)|url=http://freebikevalet.com/2015/08/10/cheat-codes-interview/|date=August 10, 2015|access-date=October 20, 2016}}</ref>

In 2015, they released their debut single titled "Visions", which reached number one on the [[Hype Machine]] chart. It was followed by the singles "Don't Say No", "Senses", and "Adventure". "Adventure" reached number three on the Hype Machine chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kick Kick Snare – Cheat Codes – Visions (Boehm Remix)|url=http://kickkicksnare.com/2015/07/27/cheat-codes-visions-boehm-remix/|website=Kick Kick Snare|date=July 27, 2015|access-date=October 20, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161021004429/http://kickkicksnare.com/2015/07/27/cheat-codes-visions-boehm-remix/|archive-date=October 21, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> The group gained over 15&nbsp;million views on [[YouTube]] and later toured with [[The Chainsmokers]].<ref name="iTunes">{{cite web|title=Cheat Codes on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/cheat-codes/id957521979|website=iTunes|access-date=October 20, 2016}}</ref>

===2016–present: Other singles, first EPs and ''Hellraisers'' ===
In July 2016, they released "[[Let Me Hold You (Turn Me On)]]" with [[Dante Klein]], which samples [[Kevin Lyttle]]'s song "[[Turn Me On (Kevin Lyttle song)|Turn Me On]]".<ref>{{cite web|last=Melendez|first=Monique|title=Cheat Codes Party With Sword Swallowers in 'Let Me Hold You (Turn Me On)': Video Premiere|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7423254/cheat-codes-party-let-me-hold-you-turn-me-on-video|website=Billboard|access-date=October 20, 2016}}</ref> The song has since gained over 100&nbsp;million streams.<ref name="Billboard 2">{{cite web|last=Bein|first=Kat|title=Cheat Codes Joins Young Thug, Fetty Wap on 300 Entertainment Roster|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7525634/cheat-codes-sign-to-300-entertainment-migos-fetty-wap|website=Billboard|access-date=October 20, 2016}}</ref>

Before "Let Me Hold You (Turn Me On)" was released, In February 2016, they released "[[Sex (Cheat Codes and Kris Kross Amsterdam song)|Sex]]" with [[Kris Kross Amsterdam]], quickly gaining over 300&nbsp;million streams.<ref name="Billboard 2"/> Talking about "Sex", band member Matt admitted that the song was affected by divine intervention as it only took 45 minutes to write.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://firetunes.co/2016/05/06/cheat-codes-interview-2/|title=Cheat Codes Interview|date=6 May 2016}}</ref> In August 2016, a music video was released for the song, and the trio performed at the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] festival.<ref name="People">{{cite web|last=Nelson|first=Jeff|title=Cheat Codes Kris Kross Amsterdam Sex Music Video: Salt-N-Pepa|url=http://people.com/celebrity/cheat-codes-kris-kross-amsterdam-sex-music-video-salt-n-pepa/|website=PEOPLE.com|date=August 11, 2016|access-date=October 20, 2016}}</ref>

They signed to [[300 Entertainment]] in September 2016.<ref name="Billboard 2" /> In November, they released "Queen Elizabeth", co-produced by "Foreign Noi$e".<ref>{{cite web|last=Russell|first=Erica|title=Cheat Codes Deliver Breezy Electro Pop on 'Queen Elizabeth': Premiere|url=http://popcrush.com/cheat-codes-queen-elizabeth-premiere/|website=PopCrush|access-date=November 30, 2016}}</ref> They released "[[No Promises (Cheat Codes song)|No Promises]]", a collaboration with [[Demi Lovato]], in March 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Malone|first1=Dolph|title=Cheat Codes & Demi Lovato's "No Promises" Heading To Pop Radio|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2017/03/30/cheat-codes-demi-lovatos-no-promises-heading-pop-radio/|website=Headline Planet|access-date=31 March 2017|date=30 March 2017}}</ref> In May 2017, the single "Stay With You" was released.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cheat Codes And Cade Team Up For 'Stay With You'|url=http://elbroide.com/2017/05/28/cheat-codes-cade-team-stay/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200804015454/http://elbroide.com/2017/05/28/cheat-codes-cade-team-stay/|url-status=dead|archive-date=August 4, 2020|website=El Broide|access-date=5 September 2017|date=28 May 2017}}</ref> The single "Sober" was released in July 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bein|first1=Kat|title=Cheat Codes' Nicky Romero Collab 'Sober' Tempts You to Text All Your Exes: Listen|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7882010/cheat-codes-nicky-romero-sober|website=Billboard|access-date=5 September 2017}}</ref> The group released "[[Feels Great]]", a collaboration with [[Fetty Wap]] and CVBZ, on October 13, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Armstrong|first1=Megan|title=Cheat Codes Unveil Latest Collaboration 'Feels Great' With Fetty Wap & CVBZ: Listen|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7998521/cheat-codes-new-song-feels-great-fetty-wap-cvbz|website=Billboard|access-date=17 October 2017}}</ref>

The group released the single "Put Me Back Together" with [[Kiiara]] on March 2, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bein|first1=Kat|title=Cheat Codes & Kiiara Find Healing on 'Put Me Back Together': Listen|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8225504/cheat-codes-kiiara-put-me-back-together|website=Billboard|access-date=6 March 2018}}</ref> Cheat Codes collaborated with [[U2]] and released a remixed version of U2's single "[[Love Is Bigger Than Anything in Its Way]]" on May 29, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/U2-Cheat-Codes-Release-LOVE-IS-BIGGER-THAN-ANYTHING-IN-ITS-WAY-20180529|title=U2 & Cheat Codes Release LOVE IS BIGGER THAN ANYTHING IN ITS WAY|author=BWW News Desk|access-date=2018-06-06|language=en}}</ref>

In June 2018, they released their extended play, ''Level 1'' consisting of 6 songs, include "I Love It" and "Balenciaga".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rounsefell |first1=Kierstin |title=Cheat Codes Tease Debut EP with New Single "Balenciaga" |url=https://edm.com/music-releases/cheat-codes-level-1-ep |website=EDM.com – The Latest Electronic Dance Music News, Reviews & Artists |access-date=30 November 2018 |language=en}}</ref> On June 22, 2018, Cheat Codes released "[[Only You (Cheat Codes and Little Mix song)|Only You]]"<!-- see comment at beginning of paragraph --> with [[Little Mix]]<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mele |first1=Sofia |title=Cheat Codes and Little Mix Tell a Mermaid Love Story in Vibrant 'Only You' Video: Watch |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8465364/cheat-codes-little-mix-video-only-you |website=Billboard |access-date=30 November 2018}}</ref> as a part of the compilation album ''Love Island: The Pool Party'', which was released on July 6, 2018 by [[Ministry of Sound]]. They released the song "[[Feeling of Falling]]" with [[Kim Petras]] on November 30, 2018.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Piedra |first1=Xavier |title=Kim Petras & Cheat Codes Team Up For 'Vulnerable' Dance Track 'Feeling of Falling': Listen |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8487058/kim-petras-cheat-codes-feeling-of-falling |website=Billboard |access-date=30 November 2018}}</ref> On July 26, 2019, they released their second extended play, ''Level 2''.

On June 26, 2020, the group released the single "Heaven".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sani|first=Niko|title=Cheat Codes Return with Pop-Infused Single "Heaven"|url=https://edm.com/music-releases/cheat-codes-heaven|access-date=2020-07-22|website=EDM.com - The Latest Electronic Dance Music News, Reviews & Artists|language=en}}</ref> The music video was released the following month.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Cheat Codes Premieres New Song "Heaven"|url=https://www.pmstudio.com/music/music.html?page=20200717-11742|access-date=2020-07-22|website=pm studio world wide news|language=ja}}</ref> In August, they released "No Time" featuring [[Wiz Khalifa]] and [[Dvbbs|DVBBS]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Padin|first=Malvika|date=2020-08-18|title=Cheat Codes release perfect summer quarantine track "No Time" with Wiz Khalifa, DVBBS|url=https://earmilk.com/?p=686394|access-date=2020-08-31|website=EARMILK|language=en-US}}</ref> On September 2, 2020, they announced their upcoming three-part debut studio album ''Hellraisers'' and released the lead single "Between Our Hearts" featuring Australian singer [[Cxloe]].<ref name=hellraisers/> On November 4, they released the single "Washed Up".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Carlotti|first=Hannah|title=Cheat Codes release new single "Washed Up"|url=http://www.nusoundonline.com/cheat-codes-release-new-single-washed-up/|access-date=2020-11-13|language=en-US}}</ref>
