'''Jacob Lee Christian Blowes''' (born 4 November 1994), who performs as '''Jacob Lee''', is an Australian pop, singer-songwriter from the [[Gold Coast, Queensland]]. In May–June 2014 he was a contestant on the third Australian series of [[The Voice (Australia season 3)|''The Voice'']], and was coached by [[will.i.am]]. Previously he had been a member of radio station, [[Sea FM]]'s boy band, Oracle East, in 2012. In January 2016, Lee supported [[Justice Crew]] on their Live & Local Tour in New South Wales. Lee is the founder and owner of Philosophical Records & Lowly Lyricist.

===2011- : ''The X Factor'' and Oracle East===
In 2011, Lee progressed to bootcamp of Season 3 of Australian TV talent show, ''[[The X Factor (Australian TV series)|The X Factor]]'', after [[Guy Sebastian]] believed he was too young. 

Lee was briefly a member of the boy band, Oracle East, which was devised in 2012 by Gold Coast radio station, [[Sea FM]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = 90.9 Sea FM - Check out our new Boy Band!! In June 2014 Lee released his debut single, "Chariot". 

=== 2015-present: Current activities ===

In June 2014 Lee released his debut single, "Chariot". 

In January 2016 Lee accompanied Australian boy band, [[Justice Crew]], on their New South Wales tour from [[Coffs Harbour]] to Sydney.

Lee's released ''Sine Qua Non'' on 6 February 2016.  

Lee was the featured vocalist on "Need to Feel", a single by Social Hooliganz & Trifo, which peaked at No.&nbsp;12 on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Club Tracks]] in May 2016.<ref name="ARIA Report 1369">{{cite journal | date = 23 May 2016 | archiveurl = https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20160523140035/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/23790/20160524-0000/Issue1369.pdf | title = Week Commencing ~ 23rd May 2016 ~ Issue #1368 | last = Wallace | first = Ian | work = The ARIA Report | format = PDF | publisher = [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA) | issue = 1369 | page = 20 | url = http://www.aria.com.au/pages/documents/issue1369.pdf | archivedate = 24 May 2016 | accessdate = 5 October 2019 }}</ref> 

The singer's third EP, ''Clarity'', was released in 2017. 

In March 2017, Lee featured on the Social Hooliganz single "Stars at Night", which peaked at No.&nbsp;36 on the ARIA Club Tracks.<ref name="ARIA Report 1414">{{cite journal | date = 3 April 2017 | archiveurl = https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20170410140029/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/23790/20170404-0000/Issue1414.pdf | title = Week Commencing ~ 3rd April 2017 ~ Issue #1414 | last = Wallace | first = Ian | work = The ARIA Report | format = PDF | publisher = Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) | issue = 1414 | page = 20 | url = http://www.aria.com.au/pages/documents/issue1414.pdf | archivedate = 11 April 2017 | accessdate = 6 October 2019 }}</ref>

In December 2020, Lee released his second studio album, ''Conscience''.
