'''Turnstile''' is an American [[hardcore punk]] band from [[Baltimore, Maryland]], formed in 2010. They have released five EPs<ref>{{cite web|title=Turnstile announces new album|url=http://lambgoat.com/news/23448/Turnstile-announces-new-album|publisher=Lambgoat|accessdate=December 25, 2014}}</ref> and three studio albums.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kraus|first1=Brian|title=Turnstile announce debut album, 'Non Stop Feeling'|date=September 26, 2014|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/turnstile_announce_debut_album_non_stop_feeling|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=December 25, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Turnstile Knows Hardcore's Rules. That's Why It Can Break Them|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/21/arts/music/turnstile-time-and-space-ronny-j-omgronny-review.html|work=New York Times|date=February 21, 2018|accessdate=February 22, 2018|last1=Caramanica|first1=Jon}}</ref>

==History==

===2010–2016: Early years and ''Nonstop Feeling''===
Turnstile formed in 2010, based out of Baltimore's emerging hardcore scene. They released their debut EP, titled ''Pressure to Succeed'', in 2011,<ref>{{cite web|title=Turnstile – Pressure to Succeed|url=http://www.songsandcigarettes.com/2011/05/turnstile-pressure-to-succeed/|publisher=Songs + Cigarettes|access-date=June 19, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150619103244/http://www.songsandcigarettes.com/2011/05/turnstile-pressure-to-succeed/|archive-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref> and their second EP, ''Step 2 Rhythm'', in 2013, both via Reaper Records.<ref name="Hit the Floor">{{cite web|last1=Matthews|first1=James Paul|title=Review: Turnstile – Step 2 Rhythm (Vinyl Only EP)|url=http://www.hitthefloor.com/music/review-turnstile-step-2-rhythm-vinyl-only-ep/|publisher=Hit the Floor|date=April 4, 2013|accessdate=November 21, 2015}}</ref> On January 13, 2015, Turnstile released their debut full-length album ''[[Nonstop Feeling]]'', also via Reaper Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=Stream Turnstile's Debut LP 'Nonstop Feeling' in Full Now|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/turnstile-nonstop-feeling-album-stream|publisher=Vice|accessdate=June 19, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Sharp|first1=Tyler|title=Listen to Turnstile's insane debut album, 'Nonstop Feeling'|date=January 6, 2015|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/listen_to_turnstiles_insane_debut_album_nonstop_feeling|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=June 19, 2015}}</ref> The album was recorded at Salad Days Studios with producer [[Brian McTernan]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Mackay|first1=Sebastian|title=Stream Turnstile's "Nonstop Feeling" Now!|url=http://substreammagazine.com/2015/01/stream-turnstiles-nonstop-feeling-now/|publisher=Substream Magazine|accessdate=June 19, 2015}}</ref> Supporting the release of the album, Turnstile went on both an East Coast and a West Coast tour with [[Superheaven]], titled the Nonstop Feeling Tour.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sharp|first1=Tyler|title=Turnstile announce 'Nonstop Feeling' tours|date=November 11, 2014|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/turnstile_announce_nonstop_feeling_tours|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=December 25, 2014}}</ref> They also supported [[New Found Glory]] on their spring 2015 tour. Following this tour their guitarist Sean Coo stepped down, and in 2016 a replacement was found in Pat McCrory of fellow Maryland based hardcore band Angel Du$t.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sharp|first1=Tyler|title=New Found Glory, Turnstile, This Wild Life, Turnover announce tour|date=December 9, 2014|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/new_found_glory_turnstile_this_wild_life_turnover_announce_tour|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=December 25, 2014}}</ref>

===2016–2020: ''Move Thru Me'' and ''Time & Space''===
On September 16, 2016, Turnstile released their third EP titled ''Move Thru Me''. The record charted at number 14 on the [[Top Heatseekers|Billboard Heatseekers Album Chart]] and number 19 on the [[Billboard charts|Hard Rock Albums chart]]. The band began recording for their second album under [[Roadrunner Records]] in the fall of 2017, completing recording in early 2018. The record was produced by [[Will Yip]] at his Studio 4 recording studio. On February 23, 2018, the band released the album, titled ''[[Time & Space]]''; it is their first release under a [[Record label|major label]].<ref>[https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/turnstile_new_song_moon "album recording"]''altpress'' retrieved March 2, 2018</ref> Three of the tracks from this album were reworked together with DJ and producer Mall Grab and released as an EP, titled ''Share A View'', in January 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hear Mall Grab Remixing Turnstile songs on 'Share A View' EP|date=January 8, 2020|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/mall-grab-remix-turnstile-songs-share-a-view-ep-2594115|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2021–present: ''Glow On''===
On June 27, 2021, the band released their fifth extended play, ''Turnstile Love Connection'', alongside an accompanying short film directed by Yates.<ref>{{Citation|title="TURNSTILE LOVE CONNECTION" by TURNSTILE|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WdJZlmhQXug|access-date=July 14, 2021}}</ref> On July 15, 2021, the band announced the release of another album, ''Glow On''. The first single from the album, Alien Love Call, was released, featuring British singer [[Dev Hynes|Blood Orange]].<ref>{{Citation|title=TURNSTILE - ALIEN LOVE CALL (FT. BLOOD ORANGE) [OFFICIAL VIDEO]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9EXzz9QyWd0|access-date=July 14, 2021}}</ref>
