'''Wreck and Reference''' is an [[experimental rock]] group based in [[Los Angeles|Los Angeles, California]]. Their sound is characterized by experimentation with a wide variety of vocal styles over electronic instrumentation, composed of samples and synthesizers, and acoustic drums. The group has been closely associated with San Francisco-based record label [[The Flenser]].

== History ==
Wreck and Reference (sometime stylized Wreck & Reference or W&R) begun as a garage project in [[Davis, California|Davis, CA]] in 2009.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.cvltnation.com/black-cassette-cvlt-nation-interviews-wreck-reference/|title=BLACK CASSETTE...CVLT Nation interviews WRECK & REFERENCE -|date=2011-04-26|newspaper=CVLT Nation|language=en-US|access-date=2016-12-01}}</ref> The band began drawing mainstream attention after the release of the ''Y̶o̶u̶t̶h̶''  LP and the ''C̶o̶n̶t̶e̶n̶t̶''  EP (pronounced "No Youth" and "No Content") in 2012.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/allsongs/2013/04/16/177489238/were-not-sure-whats-happening-in-this-wreck-and-reference-video|title=Close Your Eyes: Wreck And Reference's Creepy Video|newspaper=NPR.org|access-date=2016-12-01}}</ref> In 2014 their second full length ''Want'' was met with some critical acclaim and featured on "The Best Metal Albums of 2014" list by [[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.tinymixtapes.com/music-review/wreck-and-reference-want|title=Music Review: Wreck and Reference - Want|newspaper=Tiny Mix Tapes|access-date=2016-12-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19492-wreck-reference-want/|title=Wreck and Reference: Want Album Review {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|access-date=2016-12-01}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/features/show-no-mercy/9567-the-best-metal-albums-of-2014/|title=The Best Metal Albums of 2014 {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|access-date=2016-12-01}}</ref> ''Want'' was followed by a 75 track online-only release of the samples used to create the electronic instrumentals on the record.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.tinymixtapes.com/features/wreck-and-reference|title=Interview: Wreck and Reference|newspaper=Tiny Mix Tapes|access-date=2016-12-01}}</ref> Common themes on the albums include depression, anxiety, and pessimism, drawing influence largely from personal events, philosophy, and literature, especially [[Cormac McCarthy]].<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.invisibleoranges.com/interview-wreck-and-reference/|title=Interview: Wreck and Reference|website=[[Invisible Oranges]] - The Metal Blog|access-date=2016-12-01}}</ref> The band has a notable [[Twitter]] account.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ponto-alternativo.com/wreck-reference-or-the-best-twitter-account-ever/|title=Wreck & Reference – Or the best Twitter account ever|website=www.ponto-alternativo.com|access-date=2016-12-01}}</ref>
