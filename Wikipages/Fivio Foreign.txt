'''Maxie Lee Ryles III''' (born March 29, 1990), known by his stage name '''Fivio Foreign''', is an American rapper and songwriter. In June 2019, he rose to fame with his single "Big Drip", which spawned a remix with rappers [[Lil Baby]] and [[Quavo]]. He is signed to fellow New York rapper [[Mase]]'s RichFish Records and [[Columbia Records]].<ref name="RichFish">{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/03/fivio-foreign-talks-mase-record-deal|title=Fivio Foreign Sets the Record Straight About His Deal With Mase|website=Complex|access-date=May 26, 2020|archive-date=April 27, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200427173332/https://www.complex.com/music/2020/03/fivio-foreign-talks-mase-record-deal|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.okayplayer.com/culture/50-cent-mase-fivio-foreign.html|title=50 Cent Accuses Mase of Signing Fivio Foreign to a Predatory Deal|date=February 19, 2020|website=Okayplayer|access-date=May 26, 2020|archive-date=August 10, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200810191056/https://www.okayplayer.com/culture/50-cent-mase-fivio-foreign.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

In May 2020, Ryles was featured on [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s song "[[Dark Lane Demo Tapes|Demons]]", which charted at 34 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], earning him his first ''Billboard''-charting single. Later that month, he collaborated with [[Lil Tjay]] and [[Pop Smoke]] on the song "Zoo York", which peaked at 65 on the Hot 100. In August 2021, he reached further recognition from his feature on [[Kanye West]]'s song "[[Off the Grid (song)|Off the Grid]]" from his tenth studio album ''[[Donda]]'', which peaked at number 11 on the Hot 100.

== Early life ==
Ryles started rapping under the name "Lite Fivio" in 2011. In 2013, he changed his name to Fivio Foreign and formed a music collective with his friends under the name 800 Foreign Side.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Fivio Foreign Interview: Always Viral|url=https://www.complex.com/music/fivio-foreign-interview-brooklyn-drill|website=Complex|access-date=May 11, 2020|archive-date=May 1, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200501050553/https://www.complex.com/music/fivio-foreign-interview-brooklyn-drill|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Career ==

Ryles started gaining traction after the release of his single "Big Drip". The song was featured on his 2019 [[Extended play|EP]]s ''Pain and Love'' and ''[[800 B.C. (EP)|800 B.C.]]'' In November, he signed a one million dollar record deal with [[Columbia Records]], in conjunction with American rapper [[Mase]]'s record label [[Mase|RichFish Records]].<ref name="RichFish" /><ref name=":0" />

In May 2020, Ryles earned his first two ''Billboard''-charting singles, with features on "Demons" by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] and "Zoo York" by [[Lil Tjay]]. The same month, Ryles launched the non-profit organization Foreignside Foundation, "geared towards providing beneficial resources and programs for at-risk youth, the homeless, current & former gang-affiliated individuals, incarcerated individuals".<ref>{{Cite web|title=FIVIO FOREIGN LAUNCHES NON-PROFIT ORGANIZATION, FOREIGNSIDE FOUNDATION|url=https://thesource.com/2020/05/20/fivio-foreign-foundation/|website=[[The Source]]|date=May 20, 2020|access-date=August 18, 2020|archive-date=September 3, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200903055440/https://thesource.com/2020/05/20/fivio-foreign-foundation/|url-status=live}}</ref>

On August 11, 2020, Ryles was included on ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''{{'}}s 2020 [[XXL (magazine)#Annual Freshman Class List|Freshman Class]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/xxl-2020-freshman-class-revealed/|title=XXL 2020 FRESHMAN CLASS REVEALED|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=Cline|first=Georgette|date=August 11, 2020|access-date=August 11, 2020|archive-date=August 12, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200812141625/https://www.xxlmag.com/xxl-2020-freshman-class-revealed/|url-status=live}}</ref> Throughout the rest 2020, he appeared on a number of songs by other artists, including [[Nas]]' "Spicy", [[Tory Lanez]]'s "K Lo K", [[French Montana]]'s "That's a Fact (Remix)", and&nbsp;[[King Von]]'s "I Am What I Am".<ref name="Baddie">{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2020/12/3/22149992/fivio-foreign-baddie-on-my-wish-list-song|title=Fivio Foreign drops new "Baddie On My Wish List" song|work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|last=Cho|first=Regina|date=December 3, 2020|access-date=December 7, 2020|archive-date=April 16, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210416141825/https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2020/12/3/22149992/fivio-foreign-baddie-on-my-wish-list-song|url-status=live}}</ref> In November 2020, he released the single "Trust", from his upcoming project, ''B.I.B.L.E''.<ref name="Baddie" /> He released the Christmas song "Baddie on My Wish List" on December 3, as part of [[Apple Music]]'s&nbsp;holiday project ''Carols Covered''.<ref name="Baddie" />

In 2021, He appeared on [[Kanye West]]’s ''[[Donda]]'' album along with [[Playboi Carti]].<ref> https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/soulja-boy-slams-kanye-west-left-off-donda-9621975/</ref>

==Personal life==
Ryles has a son born in April 2021.<ref>{{Citation|title=Fivio Foreign Details Making Of Kanye West 'Donda' Collab & Speaks On Relationship With Pop Smoke|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1vLzk5GPvbc|language=en|access-date=2021-09-12|archive-date=September 12, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210912144229/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1vLzk5GPvbc|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2018, Ryles' mother died from a stroke.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fivio Foreign Wants to Make New York's Rap Scene Fun Again|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/akwx8j/fivio-foreign-wants-to-make-new-yorks-rap-scene-fun-again-new-mixtape-800-bc|website=VICE|access-date=May 23, 2020|archive-date=May 25, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200525173039/https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/akwx8j/fivio-foreign-wants-to-make-new-yorks-rap-scene-fun-again-new-mixtape-800-bc|url-status=live}}</ref> 
Ryles has one child with his girlfriend.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Price|first1=Joe|date=October 13, 2020|title=Fivio Foreign Denies Allegation He Assaulted His Pregnant Girlfriend|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/10/fivio-foreign-assault-arrest|access-date=December 11, 2020|website=Complex|archive-date=January 24, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210124065815/https://www.complex.com/music/2020/10/fivio-foreign-assault-arrest|url-status=live}}</ref> 
Ryles was friends with the late [[Pop Smoke]] and [[King Von]] who were both murdered in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fivio Foreign on the late Pop Smoke: "I learned everything from him"|url=https://www.nme.com/music-interviews/fivio-foreign-interview-800-bc-pop-smoke-2020-drill-2653220|website=NME|date=April 24, 2020|access-date=May 23, 2020|archive-date=May 20, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200520102126/https://www.nme.com/music-interviews/fivio-foreign-interview-800-bc-pop-smoke-2020-drill-2653220|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>https://youtube.com/PbrLG5ZEV8s</ref>
