'''Olivia Isabel Rodrigo''' (born February 20, 2003) is an American singer-songwriter and actress. She is known for her roles as Paige Olvera on the [[Disney Channel]] series ''[[Bizaardvark]]'' and Nini Salazar-Roberts on the [[Disney+]] mockumentary series ''[[High School Musical: The Musical: The Series]]''. 

She signed with [[Interscope Records|Interscope]] and [[Geffen Records]] in 2020 and released her debut single, "[[Drivers License (song)|Drivers License]]", in 2021, which quickly topped various charts worldwide and gave Rodrigo her breakthrough. It was followed by successful singles "[[Deja Vu (Olivia Rodrigo song)|Deja Vu]]" and "[[Good 4 U]]", leading up to the release of her debut studio album, ''[[Sour (album)|Sour]]'', to critical acclaim. Rodrigo has earned seven [[Grammy]] nominations, including [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] for ''Sour'', and [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]] and [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] for "Drivers License".

==Other ventures==
[[File:President Joe Biden and Olivia Rodrigo.jpg|thumb|right|Rodrigo posing with U.S. President [[Joe Biden]] as part of a campaign promoting [[COVID-19 vaccination in the United States|COVID-19 vaccinations]] to youth|alt=Olivia Rodrigo with the U.S. President Joe Biden at the Oval Office, posing to press cameras with sunglasses, as part of a government campaign that aims to encourage the American youth to get vaccinated against COVID-19]]
On July 13, 2021, Rodrigo was involved in a [[Presidency of Joe Biden|White House]] effort to promote [[COVID-19 vaccination in the United States|COVID-19 vaccinations]] among young people in the US. She met with President [[Joe Biden]], Vice President [[Kamala Harris]] and [[Chief Medical Advisor to the President|Chief Medical Advisor]] [[Anthony Fauci]], to discuss her efforts.<ref name=":4">{{Cite news |last=Klein |first=Betsy |date=July 13, 2021 |title=Pop star Olivia Rodrigo coming to the White House to promote vaccines |url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/07/13/politics/olivia-rodrigo-white-house/index.html |access-date=July 14, 2021 |publisher=[[CNN]] |archive-date=July 14, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210714002930/https://www.cnn.com/2021/07/13/politics/olivia-rodrigo-white-house/index.html |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Olivia Rodrigo partners with the White House to promote vaccines|url=https://www.today.com/video/olivia-rodrigo-partners-with-the-white-house-to-promote-vaccines-116766789786|access-date=July 15, 2021|work=[[Today (American TV program)|Today]]|archive-date=July 15, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210715004407/https://www.today.com/video/olivia-rodrigo-partners-with-the-white-house-to-promote-vaccines-116766789786|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=July 14, 2021|title=Olivia Rodrigo boosts White House youth vaccine push|url=https://apnews.com/article/olivia-rodrigo-visits-white-house-vaccines-07aeaeb169aa7daed999dd48d57e06a0|access-date=July 15, 2021|work=[[Associated Press]]|archive-date=July 14, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210714205913/https://apnews.com/article/olivia-rodrigo-visits-white-house-vaccines-07aeaeb169aa7daed999dd48d57e06a0|url-status=live}}</ref> According to a [[CNN]] report, Rodrigo will record videos about the importance of young people getting vaccinated, including answering questions the youth have regarding the vaccination.<ref name=":4" />
