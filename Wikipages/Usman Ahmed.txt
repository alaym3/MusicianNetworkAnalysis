'''Usman "Uzzy" Ahmed''' ({{lang-ur|عثمان احمد}}; born 21 November 1981) is a British former [[professional boxer]] who competed from 2006 to 2016. He challenged once for the [[British Boxing Board of Control|British]] and [[Commonwealth Boxing Council|Commonwealth]] [[flyweight]] titles in 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/proboxer/373092|title=BoxRec: Usman Ahmed|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref>

==Professional career==
Ahmed lost on his professional debut via points decision over six rounds to [[Chris Edwards (boxer)|Chris Edwards]] on 30 September 2006, at the Kings Hall in [[Stoke-on-Trent]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/event/509652/1139040|title=BoxRec: Bout|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref>

In his eighth fight, with a record of 5–1–1, he fought Chris Edwards in a rematch on 29 May 2009 at the Fenton Manor Sports Complex, Stoke-on-Trent, with Edwards' [[British Boxing Board of Control|British]] and [[Commonwealth Boxing Council|Commonwealth]] [[flyweight]] titles on the line. Ahmed lost the fight by [[unanimous decision]], with two judges scoring the bout 118–109 and the third scoring it 117–111.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/event/575035/1412248|title=BoxRec: Bout|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref>

His next fight was a four round points decision victory over Kyle King in December 2009, at the [[Newport Centre (Wales)|Newport Centre]] in [[Newport, Wales|Newport]], Wales.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/event/589182/1471198|title=BoxRec: Bout|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref> One month later he challenged [[Ashley Sexton]] for the vacant [[British Boxing Board of Control#Councils|English]] flyweight title, losing via first round [[knockout]] (KO).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/event/590664/1471909|title=BoxRec: Bout|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref> Footage from the fight, showing Ahmed dancing to the ring and then being knocked unconscious with an overhand right became a [[Viral video|viral hit]] online.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.whtimes.co.uk/news/hatfield-boxer-s-knockout-is-youtube-hit-1-802688|title=Hatfield boxer's knockout is YouTube hit|last=Hutchinson|first=By Ross Logan and John|website=Welwyn Hatfield Times|date=16 February 2011|language=en|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dailypost.co.uk/sport/other-sport/youtube-sensation-usman-ahmed-out-2686057|title=YouTube sensation Usman Ahmed out for Prizefighter glory|last1=Live|first1=North Wales|last2=00:00|first2=23 Sep 2011Updated04:16|date=2011-09-22|website=dailypost|language=en|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ringsideboxingnews.com/usman-ahmed-finally-speaks-about-that-infamous-viral-video/|title=USMAN AHMED SPEAKS OUT ON THAT INFAMOUS VIRAL VIDEO {{!}}|last=says|first=Ringside Boxing News|date=2019-06-21|website=Ringside Boxing News|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/boxing/news/20876/7191909/ahmed-gets-prizefighter-call|title=Ahmed gets Prizefighter call|website=Sky Sports|language=en|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref>

On 12 October 2011, he fought [[Don Broadhurst]] at the [[Liverpool Olympia|Olympia]] in [[Liverpool]] as part of the [[Prizefighter series|Prizefighter Series]]' [[super flyweight]] edition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/boxing/news/20876/7191909/ahmed-gets-prizefighter-call|title=Ahmed gets Prizefighter call|website=Sky Sports|language=en|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref> Ahmed lost by unanimous decision over three rounds.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/boxing/15293257|title=Don Broadhurst hopes for career boost following Prizefighter final|date=2011-10-13|access-date=2019-11-17|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/event/627709/1625099|title=BoxRec: Bout|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref>

On 23 May 2015, 18 months after his last fight, Ahmed lost a six round points decision to Jamie Williams at the Britannia Hotel in [[Nottingham]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/event/711667/1965234|title=BoxRec: Bout|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref> 7 months later, on 13 December, he faced Williams in a rematch at the Hermitage Leisure Centre in [[Whitwick]], this time with the vacant [[British Boxing Board of Control#Councils|Midlands Area]] flyweight title on the line, fighting to a points [[Tie (draw)|draw]] over ten rounds.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/event/721984/2014688|title=BoxRec: Bout|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref> He then fought Williams for a third time on 23 October 2016, at the Hermitage Leisure Centre, again for the vacant Midlands Area flyweight title, this time losing by eighth round [[technical knockout]] (TKO).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://boxrec.com/en/event/740053/2107350|title=BoxRec: Bout|website=boxrec.com|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref> This would be Ahmed's final fight of his professional career.
