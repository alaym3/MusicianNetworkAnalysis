'''Weathers''' is an American [[rock music|rock]] band from [[Los Angeles]].

== History ==

===Formation===
Lead vocalist [[Cameron Boyer]] and guitarist [[Cameron Olsen]] first met in 2012 at a local [[Battle of the Bands]] in [[Manhattan Beach, California|Manhattan Beach]]. They later recruited bassist [[Brennen Bates]] and drummer [[Cole Carson]]. The band officially formed in Los Angeles in October 2015.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[RCA Records]]|url=https://cdn.smehost.net/rcarecordscom-usrcaprod/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/Weathers-Bio.docx|title=Weathers – Bio|accessdate=November 30, 2016|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161130111238/https://cdn.smehost.net/rcarecordscom-usrcaprod/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/Weathers-Bio.docx|archivedate=November 30, 2016}}</ref>

===Debut singles (2015)===
Weathers released singles "I Don't Wanna Know" and "Happy Pills" in 2015. "Happy Pills" was the most added song on [[alternative rock|alternative]] radio on its impact date, receiving the most adds for a new artist in the 2010s. "Happy Pills" peaked at No. 21 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Alternative Songs]] chart.<!--referenced below-->

===<i>Kids in the Night</i>===
Weathers debuted their album ''Kids In The Night'' (2016) in two separate EPs, ''Kids In The Night Part 1'' and ''Kids In The Night Part 2''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Weathers Release "Kids In The Night" Part 1 EP Today |url=https://www.rcarecords.com/news/weathers-release-kids-in-the-night-part-1-ep-today/ |website=www.rcarecords.com |publisher=RCA Records |access-date=10 June 2021}}</ref> Lead singer [[Cameron Boyer]] said about the release of the first EP "It's about accepting your flaws and realizing that the so-called ‘bad' things about you are actually what shape you into who you are now — a stronger, better person. It's asking, ‘Are the bad things really bad or are they just temporarily hard?'".

===<i>Pillows and Therapy</i>===
Following their debut album <i>Kids in the Night</i>, they released the singles "Dirty Money", "Problems" (and its acoustic version), "Lonely Vampire", and "Always Tired" in 2019, "Feel Good", "C'est la vie" and "Lucky" (together with [[Songs That Saved My Life]]) in 2020, "Losing Blood", "C'est la vie (Cinematic Mix)", and "Rehab" in 2021 of which "C'est la vie", "Losing Blood", and "Rehab" will be part of their upcoming album ''Pillows and Therapy''. Weathers has toured supporting many alternative acts, including [[Saint Motel]],<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[RCA Records]]|url=https://rcarecords.com/news/weathers-join-st-motel-north-american-tour-fall-single-happy-pills-1-added-alt-radio/|title=Weathers To Join St. Motel On North American Tour This Fall; Single "Happy Pills" Is #1 Most Added At Alt Radio|accessdate=November 30, 2016|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20161130110856/https://rcarecords.com/news/weathers-join-st-motel-north-american-tour-fall-single-happy-pills-1-added-alt-radio/|archivedate=November 30, 2016}}</ref> [[Nothing but Thieves]], [[Dreamers (band)|Dreamers]], [[Palaye Royale]], [[One Ok Rock]], [[Badflower]], and [[Echosmith]]. Their headline American national tour of February 2021 has been moved to start in September 2021.
