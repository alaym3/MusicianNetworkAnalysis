'''Destroy Boys''' are an American [[punk rock]] band formed in [[Sacramento]], [[California]], in 2015. In 2017, they were nominated for awards in the 25th Sacramento Area Music Awards in the categories "New Artist", "Punk/post-punk" and "Teen"<ref name="SMA">{{cite web |last1=Leibrock |first1=Rachel |title=Vote for your favorite 25th annual Sacramento Area Music Award nominees |url=https://www.newsreview.com/sacramento/vote-for-your-favorite-25th/content?oid=23946429 |website=[[The News-Review]] |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref> and in 2018 was the highest-voted contestant in  Do The Bay's contest to play [[Noise Pop Festival]].<ref name="DotheBay" /> Their second album "Make Room" was included in [[Maximumrocknroll]]'s top 10 albums of 2018.<ref>{{cite journal |title=Top 10 Albums of 2018 |journal=[[Maximumrocknroll]] |issue=429}}</ref>

==History==
The band was formed by Roditis and Mayugba on 6 October 2015<ref>{{cite podcast |url=https://player.fm/series/the-music-meetup/destroy-boys |title=Destroy Boys|date=26 November 201|access-date=21 August 2019}}</ref> as an acoustic project, intending for Roditis to play drums and Mayugba to sing and play guitar.<ref name="Melted">{{cite web |last1=ROOD |first1=NASH |title=AN INTERVIEW WITH DESTROY BOYS |url=https://www.meltedmagazine.com/blog/2017/9/29/an-interview-with-destroy-boys |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref> Their name was taken from the words that Mayugba wrote on her chalkboard at home during a period of relationship troubles.<ref name="rain">{{cite web |last1=Rain |first1=Kyla |title="Nowhere To Hide" – An Interview With DESTROY BOYS |url=https://seafoaming.com/2017/03/25/nowhere-to-hide-an-interview-with-destroy-boys/ |accessdate=2 December 2018}}</ref> They released their debut EP "Mom Jeans" digitally in 2015 with Roditis singing and Mayugba on acoustic guitar.<ref name="Sacramento Bee" /> Not long after, Knight's mother said she would not continue paying for his drums lesson unless he joined a band, leading to him contacting Mayugba, whom he knew as the child of [[Phallucy]] guitarist Sonny Mayugba and the Skirts vocalist Lynn Mayugba.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rodriguez |first1=Steph |title=Girls just wanna rock in Destroy Boys The teen band focuses on the music, not ideology |url=https://www.newsreview.com/sacramento/girls-just-wanna-rock-in/content?oid=21212068 |website=[[The News-Review]] |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref> She replied by sending him their EP, which he was impressed by, leading to him joining the group, and Mayugba moving onto electric guitar.<ref name="Melted" />

On April 6, 2016, they released the EP "Grimester", their first release with Knight.<ref name="Sacramento Bee" /> On February 4, 2017 they released two singles, "Methatonin" and "Gold Medal".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gonzalez |first1=Sean |title=Song Premiere: Destroy Boys – "Soundproof" |url=https://newnoisemagazine.com/song-premiere-destroy-boys-soundproof/ |accessdate=2 December 2018}}</ref> Enzo Malaspina of Mt. Eddy joined the band briefly in 2017, as a bass player, recording on their second album and performing with them live,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lane |first1=Casey |title=Malaspina strikes a chord with the audience |url=https://tphnews.com/2017/11/13/malaspina-strikes-a-chord-with-the-audience/ |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref> later joined by his brother Chris on drums, after Knight's departure<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lore |first1=Mark |title=Sorry, boys Sacramento’s Destroy Boys ready to crush all |url=https://www.newsreview.com/chico/sorry-boys/content?oid=24395733 |website=[[The News-Review]] |accessdate=2 December 2018}}</ref> They were nominated for awards in the 2017 Sacramento Area Music Awards in the categories "New Artist", "Punk/post-punk" and "Teen".<ref name="SMA" /> On February 4, 2018, they played at Submerge magazine's tenth-anniversary show, along with Sam I Jam, Horseneck and Screature.<ref name="Submerge">{{cite web |last1=CARABBA |first1=JONATHAN |title=A NIGHT TO REMEMBER: GET TO KNOW THE ARTISTS PLAYING OUR 10-YEAR ANNIVERSARY PARTY! |url=http://submergemag.com/music/get-to-know-the-artists-playing-the-submerge-10-year-anniversary-party/ |accessdate=2 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://eventseeker.com/artist/72836-destroy-boys|title=Destroy Boys - Artist Profile|last=|first=|date=|website=eventseeker.com|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-04-14}}</ref> On April 6, 2018, they released the singles "Crybaby" and "Vixen",<ref name="Picks:Destroy Boys" /> around this time Roditis began playing rhythm guitar live for the band.<ref>{{cite podcast
|url=https://player.fm/series/serious-talk-seriously/episode-168-destroy-boys| title= Episode 168: Destroy Boys| date= 9 April 2018| access-date= 12 August 2019}}</ref> In June 2018 they released the fifth single, "American River" from their second album<ref>{{cite web |last1=Darus |first1=Alex |title=WSTR SIGN TO HOPELESS RECORDS AND OTHER NEWS YOU MIGHT HAVE MISSED TODAY |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/wstr-cane-hill-less-than-jake-recap/ |website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref> opened for [[Sons of an Illustrious Father]] at a sold-out gig at [[Chicago]]'s Schubas venue,<ref name="Gleeson">{{cite web |last1=Gleeson |first1=Cailey |title=Destroy Boys, Sons of an Illustrious Father rock Schubas |url=https://depauliaonline.com/36030/artslife/destroy-boys-sons-of-an-illustrious-father-rock-schubas/ |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref> and opened for [[The Regrettes]] on their "Come Through" North American tour.<ref>{{cite web |last1=MITAL |first1=SACHYN |title=The Regrettes + Destroy Boys: Governors Ball After Dark |url=https://www.popmatters.com/regrettes-govball-after-dark-2577490497.html |website=[[PopMatters]] |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=This Punk Pop Buzz Band Has No Regrettes |url=https://timesofsandiego.com/arts/2018/07/07/this-punk-pop-buzz-band-has-no-regrettes/ |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref> On October 27, they played at the [[UC Theatre]] for ''Uncool Halloween III'', with [[SWMRS]], [[Bleached (band)|Bleached]], Beach Goons, Mt. ==Musical style and influence==
The band's musical style has been categorized as [[punk rock]],<ref name="Destroy Boys" /><ref name="Gleeson" /><ref>{{cite web |last1=Catajan |first1=Chloe |title=REVIEW: SWMRS return with Uncool Halloween III at the UC Theatre |url=http://www.riffmagazine.com/reviews/swmrs-20181027/ |accessdate=1 December 2018}}</ref> [[garage punk (fusion genre)|garage punk]],<ref name="Submerge" /><ref name="rain" /><ref name="Picks:Destroy Boys">{{cite journal |title=Picks: Destroy Boys |journal=[[East Bay Express]] |date=6 April 2018}}</ref> [[hardcore punk]],<ref name="SFChronicle" /><ref name="DotheBay">{{cite web |title=8 LOCAL ARTISTS TO HEAR RIGHT NOW |url=https://dothebay.com/p/bay-area-artist-guide |accessdate=4 December 2018}}</ref> [[garage rock]],<ref name="Rollin Stone">{{cite web |last1=HIATT |first1=BRIAN |title=Inside Green Day’s Revolutionary Rebirth |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/inside-green-days-revolutionary-rebirth-111108/ |website=[[Rolling Stone]] |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref> [[rock and roll]]<ref name="BTRToday">{{cite web |last1=Childers |first1=Elena |title=Despite Their Name, Destroy Boys Wants Equality |url=http://www.btrtoday.com/read/musicmeetup/despite-their-name-destroy-boys-wants-equality/ |accessdate=4 December 2018}}</ref> and [[riot grrrl]].<ref name="SFChronicle">{{cite web |title=SWMRS – Uncool Halloween III |url=https://datebook.sfchronicle.com/event/music/swmrs-uncool-halloween-iii |website=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref><ref name="Destroy Boys">{{cite journal |title=Destroy Boys |date=6 November 2017}}</ref><ref name="Sacramento Bee" /> They cite influences including 80s punk rock bands like [[Operation Ivy (band)|Operation Ivy]], the [[Misfits (band)|Misfits]], as well as contemporary musical groups like [[Dog Party (band)|Dog Party]] and much of the Uncool Records line-up,<ref name="Sacramento Bee">{{cite web |last1=GILLESPIE |first1=BLAKE |title=How did this Sacramento garage band end up in Rolling Stone? |url=https://www.sacbee.com/entertainment/music-news-reviews/article108041037.html |website=[[The Sacramento Bee]] |accessdate=17 November 2018}}</ref> in addition to [[Against Me]], [[The Who]], [[The Garden (band)|The Garden]], [[Sleater-Kinney]], [[Lady Gaga]],<ref name="rain" /> [[Mannequin Pussy]], [[Deftones]], [[the Distillers]], [[Black Sabbath]] and [[the Police (band)|the Police]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=WARDLAW |first1=MIKE |title=Stereo Six: Punk Trio Destroy Boys and the Records that Helped Them Crush |url=https://v13.net/2020/06/stereo-six-punk-trio-destroy-boys-and-the-records-that-helped-them-crush/?fbclid=IwAR1pjzKH7BezxstE_8IOB6wKo7_PnsSjxiWGne1rhDWSLhhA2osMnI2dW80 |accessdate=19 June 2020}}</ref> [[San Francisco Chronicle]] described them as "equal parts hardcore and fun",<ref name="SFChronicle" /> while having self-described their sound as "What would happen if [[Blondie (band)|Blondie]] stumbled into a Misfits recording session".<ref name="Submerge" /> Roditis says her biggest influences vocally are [[Siouxsie Sioux]] from [[Siouxsie and the Banshees]], Brooks Nielsen from [[The Growlers]] and [[Ezra Koenig]] from [[Vampire Weekend]].<ref name="Melted" />

Despite their frequent comparisons to riot grrrl bands like [[Bikini Kill]], the members of the band deny any associations with it, saying that they love the genre, but they shouldn't be categorized as it just because they're female-fronted.<ref name="BTRToday" />
