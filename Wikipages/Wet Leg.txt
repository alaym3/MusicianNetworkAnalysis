'''Wet Leg''' are a British indie band from the [[Isle of Wight]], founded by Rhian Teasdale and Hester Chambers.

== History ==
Teasdale and Chambers first met at [[Isle of Wight College]].<ref name="NME">{{cite news|last1=Williams|first1=Sophie|date=28 September 2021|title=Wet Leg: “We want to be recognised as guitar heroes”|work=NME|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/wet-leg-chaise-longue-interview-2021-3055835|access-date=29 September 2021}}</ref><ref name="iwcollege">{{cite web|title=Diploma In Music Level 3|url=https://iwcollege.ac.uk/course-areas/commercial-music/diploma-in-music-level-3/|access-date=29 September 2021|website=iwcollege.ac.uk}}</ref> After ten years of friendship, they became a musical duo in 2019 under the name Wet Leg, and signed with [[Domino Recording Company]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.countypress.co.uk/news/19373615.quirky-debut-ensures-splash-isle-wight-band-wet-leg/ |title=Quirky debut release ensures splash for Isle of Wight band Wet Leg |website=Isle of Wight County Press |access-date=29 September 2021}}</ref><ref name="guardianonetowatch"></ref>

They have released four singles. Their debut single, "[[Chaise Longue (song)|Chaise Longue]]", was released on 15 June 2021,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Deville |first=Chris |date=15 June 2021 |title=Wet Leg - "Chaise Longue" |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2151122/wet-leg-chaise-longue/music/ |access-date=30 November 2021 |website=[[Stereogum]]}}</ref> and gained media notice for earning millions of streams and video views.<ref name="Variety">{{cite news |last1=Aswad |first1=Jem |title=Oddball British Duo Wet Leg Return With Their Second Great Single This Year, ‘Wet Dream’ |url=https://variety.com/2021/music/news/wet-leg-single-video-dream-1235076142/ |access-date=29 September 2021 |work=Variety |date=28 September 2021}}</ref> Their second single, "Wet Dream", was released on 28 September 2021. ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' noted, "It's rare that a new group releases two songs and they're both great". The band has scheduled a US tour to begin in December 2021.<ref name="Variety" /> They appeared on ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]'' on BBC2, on 30 October 2021.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Little |first1=Lori |title=Isle of Wight band Wet Leg to star on Later With Jools Holland |url=https://www.countypress.co.uk/news/19680132.isle-wight-band-wet-leg-star-later-jools-holland/ |access-date=30 October 2021 |work=Isle of Wight County Press |date=28 October 2021}}</ref> On 29 November 2021, they announced their debut self-titled album, scheduled for release on 8 April 2022 via Domino. The same day, Wet Leg released the double-single "Too Late Now" / "Oh No".<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jones |first=Damian |date=29 November 2021 |title=Wet Leg announce debut album and share two new songs 'Too Late Now' and 'Oh No' |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/wet-leg-announce-debut-album-and-share-two-new-songs-too-late-now-and-oh-no-3107104 |access-date=30 November 2021 |website=[[NME]]}}</ref>
