'''Zachary Lane Bryan''' (born April 2, 1996) is an American singer-songwriter from [[Oologah, Oklahoma]].

==Early life==
Bryan hails from [[Oologah, Oklahoma]].<ref name="opry">{{Cite web|title=Zach Byran - Grand Ole Opry|url=https://www.opry.com/artists/zach-bryan/|access-date=2021-05-09|website=The Grand Ole Opry|language=en}}</ref> He is the son of Dewayne Bryan and Annette DeAnn (Mullen) Bryan, and has a sister, MacKenzie.<ref name="obituary">{{Cite web|title=Obituary for Annette DeAnn (Mullen) Bryan|url=https://www.lehmanfuneralhome.net/obituary/Annette-Bryan|access-date=2021-05-09|website=Lehman Funeral Home|language=en}}</ref> Continuing a family tradition, Bryan was an active-duty member of the U.S. Navy. He used his spare time to write music for his enjoyment.<ref name="study">{{cite news| url=https://studybreaks.com/culture/music/zach-bryan/| work=Study Breaks | first=Nora | last=Markey| title=Zach Bryan Brings Authenticity And Soul To The World Of Country | date=2020-06-17 |access-date=2021-05-09}}</ref>

==Musical career==
Bryan started uploading his music to [[YouTube]] in 2017, with his friends recording him perform using his iPhone. One of his songs, "Heading South", eventually went viral.<ref name="study" />

His debut studio album, ''DeAnn'', was dedicated to his late mother and was released on August 24, 2019. It was written in two months and recorded in his barn.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Stories of Zach Bryan's debut album, 'DeAnn'|url=https://stonyjammer.com/interviews/zachbryan |access-date=2021-05-09|website=Stony Jammer|language=en}}</ref><ref name="study" /> A second album, ''Elisabeth'', was released on May 8, 2020.

On April 10, 2021, Bryan made his [[Grand Ole Opry]] debut. He has since signed a deal with [[Warner Records]] to release his music.<ref name="Tennessean">{{cite news| url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/2021/04/09/zach-bryan-makes-grand-ole-opry-debut-how-to-watch/7130030002/| work=The Tennessean | first=Matthew | last=Leimkuehler | title=Fast-rising country artist Zach Bryan makes his Opry debut this weekend | date=2021-04-09}}</ref>

On October 14, 2021, Bryan announced that he was [[Military discharge|honorably discharged]] by the [[United States Navy|U.S. Navy]] after 8 years of service to pursue his career in music just ahead of his nationwide Fall 2021 "Ain't For Tamin' Tour".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Young|first=Casey|date=October 14, 2021|title=Zach Bryan Honorably Discharged From Active-Duty Service In The U.S. Navy To Pursue Music Full Time|url=https://www.whiskeyriff.com/2021/10/14/zach-bryan-honorably-discharged-from-active-duty-service-in-the-u-s-navy-to-pursue-music-full-time/|url-status=live|website=Whiskey Riff}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Bryan's mother, Annette DeAnn, died in 2016.
