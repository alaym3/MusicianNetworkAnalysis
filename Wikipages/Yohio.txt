==Family and early life==
Yohio was born in [[Stockholm]], Sweden on 12 July 1995 and is the son of Tommy Rehn of the Swedish [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band [[Corroded (band)|Corroded]], and Johanna Eires.<ref name="autogenerated1"/> Shortly after, his family moved to [[Sundsvall]], where he grew up. When he was six years old he started learning to play the piano, and later learned the guitar at age eleven. He wrote his first song when he was six years old.

He is the grandson of Jan-Eric Rehn, who was a guitarist in 1960s band The Panthers<ref>{{cite web |author=Anna-Karin Saad |url=http://st.nu/familj/stgrattar/1.5112316-hen-har-minglat-med-the-who |title=Hen har minglat med The Who - ST Grattar |language=sv |publisher=St.nu |access-date=2013-02-23 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130703154310/http://st.nu/familj/stgrattar/1.5112316-hen-har-minglat-med-the-who |archive-date=2013-07-03 |url-status=live }}</ref> and the nephew of Chris Rehn of Swedish [[post-grunge]]/[[pop rock]] band [[Takida]]. Chris and Tommy Rehn were also both in the band [[Angtoria]]. Yohio speaks [[Japanese language|Japanese]], after becoming interested in Japanese culture and [[visual kei]] at a young age, and also spending time in Japan on his several trips to the country performing his music.<ref>{{cite web |author=Erik Gripenholm |url=http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/det-konstigaste-ar-att-jag-ar-svensk |title=Det konstigaste är att jag är svensk |publisher=DN.SE |date=2012-08-28 |access-date=2013-02-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130104102843/http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/det-konstigaste-ar-att-jag-ar-svensk |archive-date=2013-01-04 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Career in Sweden==
Yohio and his band Seremedy gained some recognition in Sweden starting in 2011. The band consisted of lead vocalist SEIKE, YOHIO on lead guitar, Ray on guitar, JENZiiH on bass and LINDER on drums.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/seremedy/info?tab=page_info |title=Seremedy |publisher=Facebook |access-date=2016-03-15}}</ref> The band released their first EP ''Seasons Will Change'' in 2011<ref>{{cite web |title=Seremedy Seasons Will Change (EP)- Spirit of Metal Webzine (en) |url=http://Spirit-of-metal.com |access-date=2016-03-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160312110231/http://www.spirit-of-metal.com/ |archive-date=2016-03-12 |url-status=live }}</ref>  and their first album ''Welcome to our Madness'' on July 25, 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.spirit-of-metal.com/album-groupe-Seremedy-nom_album-Welcome_to_Our_Madness-l-en.html |title=Seremedy Welcome to Our Madness (CD Album)- Spirit of Metal Webzine (en) |website=Spirit-of-metal.com |date=2012-07-25 |access-date=2016-03-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160316070020/http://www.spirit-of-metal.com/album-groupe-Seremedy-nom_album-Welcome_to_Our_Madness-l-en.html |archive-date=2016-03-16 |url-status=live }}</ref> The band then broke up and made a one time return for a last live show and EP ''Re:Madness'' on September 20, 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.spirit-of-metal.com/album-groupe-Seremedy-nom_album-ReMadness-l-en.html |title=Seremedy Re:Madness (EP)- Spirit of Metal Webzine (en) |website=Spirit-of-metal.com |date=2014-09-20 |access-date=2016-03-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160317080216/http://www.spirit-of-metal.com/album-groupe-Seremedy-nom_album-ReMadness-l-en.html |archive-date=2016-03-17 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Yohio received special attention for wearing a dress when performing.<ref name=ab1>{{cite news|last=Löf|first=Anders|title=De kallar oss konstiga bögar|url=http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/musik/hardrock/article12830212.ab|access-date=2 December 2013|newspaper=Aftonbladet|date=4 April 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203083737/http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/musik/hardrock/article12830212.ab|archive-date=3 December 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2012 Yohio released his first [[English language|English]] song as a solo performer, "Our Story".<ref>{{cite web|title=Yohio - japansk popstjärna från Sundsvall|url=http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=4067&artikel=5312758|publisher=Sveriges Radio|access-date=2 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131202235013/http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=4067&artikel=5312758|archive-date=2 December 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> Yohio has made four music videos – for "Sky Limit", "Our Story", "Heartbreak Hotel" (his entry for ''[[Melodifestivalen 2013]]'') and "Revolution".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aftonbladet.se/webbtv/noje/musik/musikvideor/article15690418.ab |title=Kolla in Japansuccén Yohios nya musikvideo! &#124; Musikvideor &#124; Musik &#124; Nöjesbladet &#124; Webbtv &#124; Aftonbladet |publisher=Aftonbladet.se |date=2012-10-29 |access-date=2014-02-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140203120447/http://www.aftonbladet.se/webbtv/noje/musik/musikvideor/article15690418.ab |archive-date=2014-02-03 |url-status=live }}</ref>
 	
In 2013, it was announced that Yohio was lending his voice to a [[Vocaloid]] voicebank within the [[PowerFX]] range that contains both an English and Japanese vocal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://twitter.com/YOHIO_Seremedy/status/303618748718399489 |title=Twitter / YOHIO_Seremedy: I will become a new bilingual |publisher=Twitter.com |date=2013-02-18 |access-date=2014-02-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140112220148/https://twitter.com/YOHIO_Seremedy/status/303618748718399489 |archive-date=2014-01-12 |url-status=live }}</ref> He provided the voicebank for YOHIOloid.

===Melodifestivalen 2013===
Yohio was one of the contestants in the [[Melodifestivalen 2013|2013 edition]] of ''[[Melodifestivalen]]'', the Swedish national selection for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2013]] to be held in [[Malmö]]. His song was "[[Heartbreak Hotel (Yohio song)|Heartbreak Hotel]]",<ref name="svt">{{cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/yohio-heartbreak-hotel|publisher=SVT.se|title=Yohio – Heartbreak Hotel|access-date=2013-02-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304205500/http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/yohio-heartbreak-hotel|archive-date=2016-03-04|url-status=live}}</ref> which he wrote along with Johan Fransson, Tobias Lundgren, Tim Larsson and Henrik Göranson.<ref name="svt" /> On February 2, Yohio was qualified for the contest's finals. Soon after, on the 9th of March, he came second place with Robin Stjernberg leading him as the final winner with 166 points. Yohio earned 133 points with his successful song; Heartbreak Hotel.<ref>Melodifestivalen, SVT. [https://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/melodifestivalen-2013-den-totala-resultatlistan/ "Winner and finalists of Melodifestivalen"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171026055756/https://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/melodifestivalen-2013-den-totala-resultatlistan/ |date=2017-10-26 }}, ''[[Sveriges Television|SVT]]'', full list of the scores.</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/david-lindgren-ar-vidare-i-melodifestivalen-2013|title=YOHIO och David Lindgren är i final i Melodifestivalen|publisher=SVT|date=2 February 2013|access-date=2 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130205075702/http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/david-lindgren-ar-vidare-i-melodifestivalen-2013|archive-date=5 February 2013|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/robin-stjernberg-ar-vinnaren-av-melodifestivalen-2013|title=Robin Stjernberg är vinnaren av Melodifestivalen 2013|author=Emma Petersson|publisher=SVT|date=9 March 2013|access-date=31 May 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130517171637/http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/robin-stjernberg-ar-vinnaren-av-melodifestivalen-2013|archive-date=17 May 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> Yohio was the Swedish spokesperson and announcer of the Swedish voting result at the 2013 Eurovision final in Malmö.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/yohio-presenterar-de-svenska-rosterna-i-eurovision-song-contest|title=YOHIO presenterar de svenska rösterna i Eurovision Song Contest|author=Mirja Bokholm|date=22 April 2013|work=SVT.se|access-date=22 April 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130509034225/http://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/yohio-presenterar-de-svenska-rosterna-i-eurovision-song-contest|archive-date=9 May 2013|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Melodifestivalen 2014===
Yohio took part in the first heat of [[Melodifestivalen 2014]] at the [[Malmö Arena]] with the song "[[To the End (Yohio song)|To the End]]", performing first. Yohio was announced as the first act to go directly to the final at the [[Friends Arena]] on 8 March.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/melodifestivalen/article18281226.ab|title=n: De gick vidare|date=1 February 2014|work=Aftonbladet|access-date=1 February 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304051435/http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/melodifestivalen/article18281226.ab|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> In the final, he came in sixth place.

===DISREIGN===
Yohio formed a new band that consists of Valentin on guitar, Tias on drums and former Seremedy bandmate, JENZiiH on bass. They released their first single and PV Until The Fade on May 8, 2015 and released the single worldwide on August 5, 2015. The PV was directed by Die/may band member Riotcolor. This marked Yohio's return to a band and his return to singing in Japanese since Shiraha in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/disreign/info?tab=page_info |title=Disreign |publisher=Facebook |access-date=2016-03-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Yeriw_b-pDA} |title=Disreign - Until The Fade (Official Pv) |publisher=YouTube |date=2015-05-08 |access-date=2016-03-15}}</ref>

==Producing career==
Yohio has mixed a song for former Seremedy bandmate Seike's band Die/May single The Return, making this their first time working together since the break up of Seremedy (before the reunion in 2014)<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sAIUt8WW1mA |title=Die/May - The Return (Official Pv) |publisher=YouTube |date=2014-05-04 |access-date=2016-03-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160310012000/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sAIUt8WW1mA |archive-date=2016-03-10 |url-status=live }}</ref> Yohio also wrote the lyrics to the song "I'm Sorry" tweeting "Writing some R&B right now for another artist. Not my territory at all, but very interesting to create. Love how you can play with vocals!" The song was sung by Oskar Bruzell.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xTtPK9DOf1E |title=Oskar Bruzell - I'm Sorry (OFFICIAL MUSIC VIDEO) |publisher=YouTube |date=2015-02-20 |access-date=2016-03-15}}</ref>

==Career in Japan==
In 2012, Yohio released his first [[Extended play|EP]] "Reach the Sky" in Japan.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://poplight.zitiz.se/artikel/fraemsta-latarna-2012-yohio-skylimit |title=Poplight &#124; Främsta låtarna 2012: YOHIO - SKYLiMIT &#124; Poplight |publisher=Poplight.zitiz.se |access-date=2013-02-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131202235147/http://poplight.zitiz.se/artikel/fraemsta-latarna-2012-yohio-skylimit |archive-date=2013-12-02 |url-status=live }}</ref> His first [[album]], ''[[Break the Border]]'' was released in June, 2013, three months after the Swedish release. It entered the [[Oricon]] weekly charts on one occasion, at position 285.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/artist/559522/products/music/1011764/1/ |title=「ブレイク・ザ・ボーダー」 ＹＯＨＩＯ│オリコン芸能人事典-ORICON STYLE |publisher=Oricon.co.jp |access-date=2014-02-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131207210649/http://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/artist/559522/products/music/1011764/1/ |archive-date=2013-12-07 |url-status=live }}</ref>
