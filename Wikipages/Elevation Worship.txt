'''Elevation Worship''' is a [[contemporary worship music]] band from [[Elevation Church]] in [[Charlotte, North Carolina]]. The band leads worship in weekend church services, as well as performing concerts and tours around the United States.

== History ==

Led by Wade Joye and Chris Brown, Elevation Worship began in 2007 at [[Elevation Church]] in [[Charlotte, North Carolina]].<ref name=ALLMUSIC/><ref name=JESUS/><ref>Luchae Williams, gatewaynews.co.za, [http://gatewaynews.co.za/elevation-worship-wake-up-the-wonder-review/ Elevation Worship – Wake up the wonder: Review], South Africa, March 5, 2015</ref> The band released four independent albums prior to signing with Essential Records. ''The Sound'' was released in 2007 (under the name Elevation Church Live), ''We Are Alive'' in 2008, ''God With Us'' in 2009, and ''Kingdom Come'' in 2010.<ref name="JESUS" /> Featuring the song "Give Me Faith", ''Kingdom Come'' was the band's first album to break through on the ''Billboard'' charts, reaching No. 5 on Heatseekers, No. 42 on Independent Albums and at No. 17 on the Christian Albums chart.<ref>Timothy Yap, hallels.com, [http://www.hallels.com/articles/2971/20140731/darlene-zschech-israel-houghton-elevation-worship-share-their-sunday-worship-song-sets.htm Darlene Zschech, Israel Houghton & Elevation Worship Share Their Sunday Worship Song Sets], USA, July 31, 2014</ref><ref name="ALLMUSIC" />

The band released their debut studio album, ''[[For the Honor]]'', under [[Sony Music Nashville]]'s [[Essential Records (Christian)|Essential Records]] label on November 21, 2011.<ref name=ALLMUSIC/><ref name=JESUS/> It saw chart success on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine charts, including No. 1 on Heatseekers, No. 19 on the Christian Albums, and the ''Billboard'' 200 at No. 193.<ref name="ALLMUSIC" /><ref name="JFHFIRST">{{cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/ForTheHonor.asp|title=Elevation Worship, "For The Honor" Review|last=Caldwell|first=Alex "Tincan"|date=November 21, 2011|publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout|access-date=March 1, 2013}}</ref>

The band's second album under the Essential label,<ref name=ALLMUSIC/><ref name=JESUS/> ''[[Nothing Is Wasted (album)|Nothing Is Wasted]]'', was released on February 19, 2013. A deluxe edition of the album also included studio recordings of each song. It also saw chart success – reaching No. 1 on the Christian Albums chart and at No. 41 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref name="ALLMUSIC" /><ref name="JFHSECOND" /><ref>EMILY HARRIS, charlottemagazine.com, [http://www.charlottemagazine.com/Charlotte-Magazine/January-2014/The-Story-of-Elevation-Church/?cparticle=2&siarticle=1 On God's Stage: Elevation Church] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161229032716/http://www.charlottemagazine.com/Charlotte-Magazine/January-2014/The-Story-of-Elevation-Church/?cparticle=2&siarticle=1 |date=December 29, 2016 }}, USA,December 19, 2013</ref><ref>JEFF SCHAPIRO, christianpost.com, [http://www.christianpost.com/news/new-elevation-worship-album-becomes-an-instant-chart-topper-90522/ New Elevation Worship Album Becomes an Instant Chart-Topper], USA, February 21, 2013</ref>

Their third album, ''[[Only King Forever]]'', was released on January 14, 2014, debuting at their highest charting position, No. 23 on the ''Billboard'' 200<ref name="BILLBOARD">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/301611/elevation-worship/chart|title=Elevation Worship Charts|work=Billboard|access-date=October 10, 2014}}</ref> and featured guest vocalist [[Darlene Zschech]] from [[Hillsong Worship]].

On August 1, 2014, the band recorded their fourth album, ''[[Wake Up the Wonder]]'', live at [[Spectrum Center (arena)|the Spectrum in Charlotte]]. It was released on November 25 that same year. The album debuted at No. 58 on the ''Billboard'' 200 in its first week, as well as No. 1 on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Christian Album chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/301611/Elevation+Worship/chart?f=310|title=Elevation Worship - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=Billboard|access-date=February 12, 2017}}</ref> On July 31, 2015, they held another live recording at Time Warner Cable Arena for their fifth album, ''[[Here as in Heaven]]'', their first album released on their own label, Elevation Worship Records. It was released on February 5, 2016, and peaked No. 1 on US album charts.<ref name="ALLMUSIC" /><ref name="JFHSECOND">{{cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/ElevationWorshipNothingIsWasted.asp|title=Elevation Worship, "Nothing Is Wasted" Review|author=JFH Admin|date=February 19, 2013|publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout|access-date=March 1, 2013}}</ref> Their fifth album, ''[[There Is a Cloud]]'', was released on March 17, 2017, after being recorded at Elevation Ballantyne during the church's revival, "Code Orange Revival". After ''There is a Cloud'', Elevation Worship released their first Spanish album, ''[[Lo Harás Otra Vez]]'', on August 18, 2017. This album peaked at No. 2 on the US Latin album chart. On September 28, 2018, Elevation Worship released their eleventh live album, ''[[Hallelujah Here Below]]'', which was nominated for the 2019 [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Christian Music Album|Best Contemporary Christian Music Album]]. On April 12, 2019, Elevation Worship released ''Paradoxology'', which was a reimagined album with different takes on the songs from ''Hallelujah Here Below'', like "Here Again", "Echo", and "Won't Stop Now". ''Paradoxology'' included a single, "With You".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/5915-elevation-worship-releases-new-project-paradoxology|title=Elevation Worship Releases New Project 'Paradoxology'|last=Longs|first=Herb|website=TheChristianBeat.org|language=en-gb|access-date=2019-12-10}}</ref> ''[[Aleluya (En La Tierra)]]'' is Elevation Worship's second album in Spanish and was released on July 19, 2019. It peaked at eight on US Latin Album Sales. ''[[At Midnight (EP)|At Midnight]]'', their seventh EP, was released on August 30, 2019.

They have previously toured with other [[contemporary worship music|contemporary worship]] or [[contemporary Christian music|contemporary Christian]] acts including [[Hillsong Worship]], [[Lauren Daigle]], [[Bethel Music]], [[Passion Conferences|Passion]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.premierproductions.com/blog/outcry-tour-announces-dates-and-artists-2016|title=OUTCRY Tour Announces Dates and Artists for 2016!|date=December 7, 2015|newspaper=Premier Productions|access-date=February 9, 2017}}</ref>
