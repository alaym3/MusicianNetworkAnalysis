'''Jeremy Biddle''' (born April 4, 1994), known professionally as '''Yung Bleu''', is an American rapper and singer. He is best known for his 2020 single "[[You're Mines Still]]", which peaked at number 18 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] after it was remixed by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ju|first=Shirley|date=April 19, 2021|title=Yung Bleu opens up about Drake hopping on "You're Mines Still"|url=https://www.revolt.tv/2021/4/19/22392355/yung-bleu-music-interview|url-status=live|access-date=July 23, 2021|website=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Drake Chart History (Billboard Hot 100)|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Drake/chart-history/HSI|url-status=live|access-date=July 23, 2021|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>

== Early life and career ==
Biddle was born and raised in [[Mobile, Alabama]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yung Bleu & Davido Stay Cold Hearted On "Unforgiving"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/yung-bleu-and-davido-stay-with-a-cold-heart-on-unforgiving-new-song.1992109.html|access-date=July 25, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop|date=July 25, 2021}}</ref> He started making music in 2013, releasing a string of [[mixtape]]s that had his name circulate throughout the [[Southern hip hop|Southern scene]], and eventually sign a deal with [[Columbia Records]] through Boosie Badazz's label [[Boosie Badazz#Bad Azz Music Syndicate|Bad Azz Music Syndicate]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=September 27, 2020|title=Yung Bleu Says 'He's Badazz For Life' After Boosie Badazz Let Him Out Of His Deal To Get Bigger|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.58128/title.yung-bleu-says-hes-badazz-for-life-after-boosie-badazz-let-him-out-of-his-deal-to-get-bigger|url-status=live|access-date=July 29, 2021|website=[[HipHopDX]]}}</ref>

In 2017, he came back on the scene with the singles "Miss It" and "Ice on My Baby" from the mixtape ''Investments 4'', which featured a guest appearance from [[YoungBoy Never Broke Again]].<ref name=":0">{{Citation|website=My Mixtapez|title=Mixtape of Investments 4 by Yung Bleu|date=September 12, 2017|url=https://mymixtapez.com/album/142710|access-date=July 29, 2021}}</ref> In 2018, he released a remix for "Miss It" featuring [[Kid Ink]] and for "Ice on My Baby" featuring [[Kevin Gates]].

In 2020, he released the mixtape ''Inv 5'' and the EP ''Bleu Vandross'', which were his last releases under Columbia Records and Bad Azz Music. Later that year, he signed a deal with [[Empire Distribution]] and a management deal with [[Meek Mill]]'s label [[Dream Chasers Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yung Bleu Clarifies Label Deal With Boosie Badazz & Meek Mill|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/yung-bleu-clarifies-label-deal-with-boosie-badazz-and-meek-mill-news.118783.html|access-date=July 29, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop|date=September 30, 2020}}</ref> The same year, he released the EP ''Love Scars: The 5 Stages of Emotions'', which included the single "[[You're Mines Still]]". A remix for "You're Mines Still" featuring Canadian rapper [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] was released through [[OVO Sound]] on October 16, 2020, after the two artists were connected by basketball player [[DeMarcus Cousins]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=How Drake Ended Up on Yung Bleu's New Remix (With Help From DeMarcus Cousins)|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/10/yung-bleu-remix-drake-interview|url-status=live|access-date=July 29, 2021|website=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]}}</ref> The remix peaked at number 18 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and helped ''Love Scars: The 5 Stages of Emotions'' to enter the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Yung Bleu Scores First Top 10 on Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9531672/yung-bleu-top-10-rb-hip-hop-songs/|url-status=live|access-date=July 29, 2021|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Billboard 200 Chart (Week of October 31, 2020)|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2020-10-31|url-status=live|access-date=July 29, 2021|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>

In 2021, Biddle released the singles "Thieves in Atlanta" featuring [[Coi Leray]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yung Bleu & Coi Leray Team Up For "Thieves In Atlanta"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/yung-bleu-and-coi-leray-team-up-for-thieves-in-atlanta-new-song.1990432.html|access-date=July 29, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop|date=February 26, 2021}}</ref> "[[Baddest (Yung Bleu song)|Baddest]]" featuring [[Chris Brown]] [[https://tecronet.com/chris-brown-net-worth/ 11]]and [[2 Chainz]],[12] and "Way More Close (Stuck in a Box)" featuring [[Big Sean]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yung Bleu Reveals Big Sean Collab Came About From Accidental Studio Mixup|url=https://www.complex.com/music/yung-bleu-big-sean-collab-way-more-close|url-status=live|access-date=July 29, 2021|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]}}</ref> All of the three songs appeared on Biddle's debut album ''[[Moon Boy (Yung Bleu album)|Moon Boy]]'', released on July 23 of the same year.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yung Bleu: Moon Boy|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/yung-bleu-moon-boy/|url-status=live|access-date=July 29, 2021|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|first=|title=Yung Bleu, Dave, EST Gee and More – New Projects This Week|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/yung-bleu-dave-est-gee-new-projects/|url-status=live|access-date=July 29, 2021|website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]}}</ref> The album features guest appearances from Drake, [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]], Big Sean, [[Kehlani]], [[Chris Brown]], [[2 Chainz]], [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]], [[Moneybagg Yo]], [[H.E.R.]] and [[Kodak Black]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yung Bleu Shares Debut Album 'Moon Boy' f/ Drake, Gunna, Big Sean, and More|url=https://www.complex.com/music/yung-bleu-moon-boy-album-stream|access-date=July 29, 2021|website=Complex}}</ref>
