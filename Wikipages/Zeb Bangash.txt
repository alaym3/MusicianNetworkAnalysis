'''Zebunisa Bangash''' (born March 7, 1982), better known as '''Zeb Bangash''', is a Pakistani singer-songwriter from Lahore, Punjab. Her family originally hails from [[Khyber Pakhtunkhwa]]. Apart from her solo career, she was the member of music group [[Zeb and Haniya]] along with Haniya Aslam who is her first cousin.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Rehman|first1=Maliha|title=FirstPerson: "Music is not a competitive sport" – Zeb Bangash|url=http://www.dawn.com/news/1280322|website=[[Dawn (newspaper)|Dawn]]|access-date=3 September 2016|date=28 August 2016}}</ref>

She has been active since 2008 and is the first Pakistani artist who served as a music director of Bollywood movie [[Lipstick Under My Burkha]], which received 18 film awards.<ref>{{cite news |title=Zeb Bangash, 'Lipstick Under My Burkha' director to reunite in Japan {{!}} Pakistan Today |url=https://archive.pakistantoday.com.pk/2018/02/12/zeb-bangash-lipstick-under-my-burkha-director-to-reunite-in-japan/ |access-date=21 October 2021 |work=[[Pakistan Today]] |date=12 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=instepinterview! Music matters with Zeb Bangash {{!}} Instep {{!}} thenews.com.pk |url=https://www.thenews.com.pk/tns/detail/565166-instepinterview-music-matters-zeb-bangash |access-date=21 October 2021 |work=[[The News International]] |language=en}}</ref> During her career, Bangash has recorded songs in Urdu, [[Pashto]], [[Punjabi language|Punjabi]], Turkish, [[Persian language|Persian]], [[Saraiki language|Saraiki]] and others. She has been often seen on the Pakistani television shows [[Coke Studio (Pakistani TV program)|Coke Studio]] and [[Pepsi Battle of the Bands]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Zeb Bangash performs on Pepsi Battle of the Bands stage |url=https://nation.com.pk/27-Aug-2017/zeb-bangash-performs-on-pepsi-battle-of-the-bands-stage |access-date=21 October 2021 |work=The Nation |date=26 August 2017 |language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
Zebunisa Bangash began her career with her cousin [[Haniya Aslam]] as [[Zeb and Haniya]].<ref name="website">{{cite web|url=http://www.zebandhaniya.com/theband.html|title=Zeb and Haniya- The Band|website=Zebandhaniya.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080101032137/http://www.zebandhaniya.com/theband.html|archive-date=1 January 2008|url-status=dead|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref> They released their debut album ''[[Chup!]]'' in 2008.<ref>[http://www.muziq.net/songs/Zeb-and-Haniya/Chup/ Pakistani Music Channel review]</ref> In 2013, Zeb did a song for the film ''[[Madras Cafe]]'' and made her debut in Bollywood. She continued her solo career after Haniya left for studies in Canada. She sung for the soundtrack of ''[[Highway (2014 Hindi film)|Highway]]'' (2014), ''[[Bin Roye]]'' (2015), ''[[Manto (2015 film)|Manto]]'' (2015) , ''[[Ho Mann Jahan]]'' (2016) , ''[[Fitoor]]'' (2016) , ''[[Lala Begum]]'' (2016), ''[[Verna (film)|Verna]]'' (2017) , ''[[Parwaaz Hai Junoon]]'' (2018) , ''[[Baaji]]'' (2019) , ''[[Parey Hut Love]]'' (2019) and ''[[Superstar (2019 film)|Superstar]]'' (2019).<ref>{{cite web|last1=Faisal|first1=Sabeen|title=In conversation with Zeb Bangash, the voice of 'Ho Mann Jahaan'|url=http://www.hipinpakistan.com/news/1148154|website=HIP|access-date=3 September 2016|date=22 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title='Fitoor' experience was special for me: Zeb Bangash|url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/1034616/fitoor-experience-was-special-for-me-zeb-bangash/|website=[[The Express Tribune]]|access-date=3 September 2016|date=26 January 2016}}</ref> She also sung the title track of television series ''[[Diyar-e-Dil]]'' which won [[15th Lux Style Awards|Lux Style Award]] for Best Original Soundtrack and [[Hum Award for Best Original Soundtrack]] in 2016.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sabeeh|first1=Maheen|title=Haniya is my sister. I have to support every decision she makes...|url=http://tns.thenews.com.pk/zeb-of-zeb-and-haniya-interview/#.V8qQeTTXfCQ|website=[[The News International]]|access-date=3 September 2016|date=23 February 2016}}</ref>

She has sung several songs for ''[[Coke Studio (Pakistan)|Coke Studio]]''.,<ref>{{cite web|last1=Salahuddin|first1=Zahra|title=Coke Studio review: Episode 1 saved by Zeb Bangash's lovely ballad|url=http://images.dawn.com/news/1176047|website=[[Dawn (newspaper)|Images]]|access-date=3 September 2016|date=14 August 2016}}</ref> including Paimona, Chup, Chal Diyay, Bibi Sanam Janem, Tann Dolay and more. She then also performed a single in the second season ''[[Pepsi Battle of the Bands]]''.<ref>{{cite news|author=Ahmed Sarym|date=28 August 2017|access-date=17 September 2017|work=DAWN Images|url=https://images.dawn.com/news/1178267|title=The exploitation in Pakistan's music industry is really appalling: Zeb Bangash}}</ref> In 2019, she performed "Roshe", a Kashmiri ode to loss in [[CokeStudio12]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thenews.com.pk/magazine/instep-today/545831-|title=Coke Studio second episode to drop tomorrow |date=24 October 2019 |author=Maheen Sabeeh |work=The News}}</ref>

Zeb Bangash is the first Pakistani artist to serve as music director of an award-winning Bollywood film;<ref>{{Cite web|title=Zeb Bangash in Concert at Yale {{!}} Yale MacMillan Center South Asian Studies|url=https://southasia.macmillan.yale.edu/event/zeb-bangash-concert-yale|access-date=2020-11-06|website=southasia.macmillan.yale.edu}}</ref> she made her debut with, "Lipstick under my Burkha," a Prakash Jha production whose music was snapped up by music marketing giant T-Series in India. The film won several awards, including FilmDe Femmes Festival, France 2017, Glasgow Film Festival 2017, London Asian Film Awards 2017, Minneapolis St. Paul International Festival 2017, Mumbai Film Festival 2016, Screen Awards, India 2018 and Tokyo International Film Festival 2017.

She is part of the Brooklyn-based ensemble Sandaraa, with the renowned klezmer clarinetist/composer Michael Winograd and a number of other leading Brooklyn-based musicians.<ref>{{cite news |title=Zeb Bangash on band culture and Sandaraa's upcoming music |url=https://www.thenews.com.pk/magazine/instep-today/331507-zeb-bangash-on-band-culture-and-sandaraas-upcoming-music |access-date=21 October 2021 |work=[[The News International]] |language=en}}</ref> The group was recently awarded the prestigious grants from Chamber Music America and the MAP fund to compose and record music that features the works of brilliant Urdu poets in the subcontinent. Their first viral single, Farz Karo, written by the poet, Ibn-e-Insha, was released at the beginning of 2018, and their forthcoming songs are slated for release.

Since the past few years, Bangash has been under the tutelage of Ustaad Naseeruddin Saami, a master of the centuries-old Dehli Gharana. She is currently working on a documentary recording of Ustaad Saami with GRAMMY-winning producer Ian Brennan, to explore his ancestral legacy of Khayal and Qawwali, and its sufic roots and lineage from the times of Hazrat Amir Khusro.

Bangash has been profiled by international media such as the BBC, TIME, Asia Society, Filmfare Middle East, Rolling Stone India, NPR, The News International, DAWN, and many others. She is a fellow at the US Pakistan Leadership Forum, has been invited to speak and perform at the prestigious events such as the PopTech in Maine, at the Brookings Institution in Washington, D.C., at the Harvard Pakistan Conference in Cambridge, Massachusetts, and at TedX events in Pakistan. She has performed worldwide, including on a US State Department sponsored tour Center Stage which included a performance at Kennedy Centre, Washington DC. She also performed at the UN Centre at Pakistan Embassy Washington and at Asia Centre New York on occasion of Lahore Literary Festival NY 2018.  

Given her prolific career and her ability to sing in various languages and appeal to audiences of all ages across cultures and continents, it is no wonder that Zeb has a huge international fan following be it in Chennai or Canada, and she has been invited to perform to large audiences across the globe, in places such as, London, Norway, New York, Washington, Chicago, Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Malaysia, Singapore, Japan, Chennai, Poona, Delhi, Agra, Mumbai, Kabul and others.

During Covid, she released a single Kuch to Kategi Zindagi.
