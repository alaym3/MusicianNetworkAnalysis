'''Vetiver''' is an American [[Folk music|folk]] band headed by songwriter [[Andy Cabic]].

==History==
Vetiver was formed in [[San Francisco]] in 2002.  The band released their self-titled debut album in [[2004 in music|2004]] on the small indie folk label [[DiCristina]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dustedmagazine.com/reviews/1484 |title=Dusted Reviews: Vetiver - Vetiver |publisher=Dustedmagazine.com |date=2004-06-10 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> Since the album's release, Vetiver has toured extensively, opening for and collaborating with [[Devendra Banhart]] and [[Joanna Newsom]]. Vetiver released another album, ''[[To Find Me Gone]]'', on DiCristina in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/9053-to-find-me-gone |title=Vetiver: To Find Me Gone &#124; Album Reviews |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2006-05-26 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> Banhart and Cabic also launched their own label, [[Gnomonsong|Gnomonsong Recordings]], releasing [[Jana Hunter]]'s ''[[Blank Unstaring Heirs of Doom]]'' in 2005 and ''There's No Home'' in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.philadelphiaweekly.com/music/2011/02/24/tonight-in-shows-444 |title=Tonight In Shows &#124; Make Major Moves |publisher=Blogs.philadelphiaweekly.com |date=2011-02-24 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> The label also released in 2008 Vetiver's ''[[Thing of the Past]]'', a collection of cover songs that have influenced Cabic's aesthetic.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/11539-thing-of-the-past/ |title=Vetiver: Thing of the Past &#124; Album Reviews |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2008-06-05 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> [[Sub Pop|Sub Pop Records]] (US) and [[Bella Union]] (UK) released Vetiver's ''[[Tight Knit]]'' (2009)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/reviews/vetiver-tight-knit-sub-pop |title=Vetiver, 'Tight Knit' (Sub Pop) |publisher=SPIN |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Simmy Richman |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/album-vetiver-tight-knit-bella-union-1639909.html |title=Album: Vetiver, Tight Knit, (Bella Union) - Reviews - Music |publisher=The Independent |date=2009-03-08 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> ''The Errant Charm'' (2011),<ref>{{cite web|last=Scott |first=Jim |url=http://www.undertheradarmag.com/reviews/the_errant_charm |title=Vetiver: The Errant Charm (Sub Pop) &#124; Under The Radar |publisher=Undertheradarmag.com |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Andy Gill |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/album-vetiver-the-errant-charm-bella-union-2295232.html |title=Album: Vetiver, The Errant Charm (Bella Union) - Reviews - Music |publisher=The Independent |date=2011-06-10 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> and ''Complete Strangers'' (2015).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://vetiverse.com/vetiver-announce-new-album-complete-strangers/ |title=Vetiver announce new album Complete Strangers |access-date=2015-03-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150315051754/http://vetiverse.com/vetiver-announce-new-album-complete-strangers/ |archive-date=2015-03-15 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The band shared the bill with [[Vashti Bunyan]] on her US tour in early 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2006/12/vetiver_vashti.html |title=Vetiver & Vashti Bunyan &#124; 2007 Tour Dates |publisher=Brooklynvegan.com |date=2006-12-07 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> Over the years, the band has toured with artists like [[Fleet Foxes]],<ref>{{cite web|author=Alex |url=http://www.folkradio.co.uk/2011/10/vetiver-uk-tour |title=Vetiver UK Tour &#124; Folk Radio UK |publisher=Folkradio.co.uk |date=2011-10-13 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> [[The Shins]], [[Fruit Bats (band)|Fruit Bats]]<ref>{{cite web|author=World Cafe |url=https://www.npr.org/2011/09/27/140815530/fruit-bats-and-vetiver-on-world-cafe |title=Fruit Bats And Vetiver On World Cafe |publisher=NPR |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> and [[Wilco]].<ref>{{cite web|author=L.A. Record |url=http://larecord.com/photos/2015/08/07/wilco-vetiver-the-greek-theatre |title=Wilco + Vetiver @ The Greek Theatre |publisher=L.S.Record |access-date=2015-09-09}}</ref>

Cabic's music has also been featured in numerous TV commercials, including an original song for Birds Eye.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hellocotton.com/birds-eye-it-s-always-vegetable-season-commercial-original-song-by-andy-cabic-1737934 |title=Birds Eye It's Always Vegetable Season Commercial Original Song by Andy Cabic. - by tvcmsong |publisher=Hellocotton.com |date=2011-09-23 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> He also works as a composer, including the documentary ''The Family Jams''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.relix.com/features/2011/05/12/kevin-barker-shares-the-family-jams-of-devendra-banhart-joanna-newsom-and-vetiver |title=Features - Kevin Barker Shares _The Family Jams_ of Devendra Banhart, Joanna Newsom and Vetiver |publisher=Relix |date=2011-05-12 |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref> and the film ''[[Smashed (film)|Smashed]]'', which was shown at the [[2012 Sundance Film Festival|Sundance Film Festival in 2012]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://filmguide.sundance.org/film/120030/smashed |title=Smashed - Online Film Guide &#124; Sundance Institute |publisher=Filmguide.sundance.org |access-date=2012-04-02}}</ref>
