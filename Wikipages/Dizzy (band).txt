'''Dizzy''' is a [[Canada|Canadian]] [[indie pop]] band from [[Oshawa]], [[Ontario]],<ref name=nme>[https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/dizzy-album-canada-great-escape-2323481 "The Great Escape: Dizzy’s brand of pop offer a dreamy escape from monotonous suburban life"]. ''[[NME]]'', May 23, 2018.</ref> whose debut album ''Baby Teeth'' won the [[Juno Award for Alternative Album of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2019]].<ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/junos-2019-the-complete-list-of-winners-1.5056461 "Junos 2019: the complete list of winners"]. [[CBC Music]], March 16, 2019.</ref>

Consisting of vocalist Katie Munshaw and instrumentalists Alex, Mackenzie and Charlie Spencer,<ref name=nme/> the group released ''Baby Teeth'' in 2018.<ref>[https://exclaim.ca/music/article/dizzy-baby_teeth "Dizzy: Baby Teeth"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', August 14, 2018.</ref> The album was promoted with a concert tour in Canada, the [[United States]] and [[Europe]],<ref name=backstroke>[https://www.stereogum.com/2007681/dizzy-backstroke-ontario-katie-munshaw/music/ "Dizzy: Backstroke"]. ''[[Stereogum]]'', July 27, 2018.</ref> and the tracks "Swim", "Pretty Thing"<ref>[https://www.stereogum.com/1988040/dizzy-pretty-thing/premiere/ "Dizzy: Pretty Thing"]. ''[[Stereogum]]'', March 22, 2018.</ref> and "Backstroke"<ref name=backstroke/> were released as singles.

In addition to the Alternative Album of the Year category, the group were also nominated for [[Juno Award for Breakthrough Group of the Year|Breakthrough Group of the Year]].<ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/shawn-mendes-and-the-weeknd-lead-the-2019-juno-nominations-1.4992669 "Shawn Mendes and the Weeknd lead the 2019 Juno nominations"]. [[CBC Music]], January 29, 2019.</ref>

On February 26, 2020 Dizzy released the single "Sunflower",<ref>{{cite web |title=Dizzy - "Sunflower" |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2074843/dizzy-sunflower/music/ |website=Stereogum |language=en |date=February 26, 2020 |accessdate=November 13, 2020 }}</ref> and on July 31, 2020 the band released their sophomore album, ''The Sun and Her Scorch''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Dizzy Discuss Following Up Award Winning Album With 'The Sun and Her Scorch' |url=https://americansongwriter.com/the-sun-and-her-scorch-dizzy-album-interview/ |website=American Songwriter |accessdate=November 13, 2020 |date=July 31, 2020}}</ref> The album received another Juno Award nomination for Alternative Album of the Year at the [[Juno Awards of 2021]].<ref name="cbc">Holly Gordon, [https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/the-weeknd-jp-saxe-jessie-reyez-and-justin-bieber-lead-2021-juno-award-nominations-1.5940551 "The Weeknd, JP Saxe, Jessie Reyez and Justin Bieber lead 2021 Juno Award nominations"]. [[CBC Music]], March 9, 2021.</ref>
