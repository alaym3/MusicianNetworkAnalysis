'''Tyler James Williams''' (born October 9, 1992) is an American actor and rapper best known for playing the title character on the [[UPN]]/[[The CW|CW]] [[sitcom]] ''[[Everybody Hates Chris]]'', songwriter Cyrus DeBarge in the [[Disney Channel]] movie ''[[Let It Shine (film)|Let It Shine]]'' and Noah on [[AMC (TV channel)|AMC]]'s ''[[The Walking Dead (TV series)|The Walking Dead]].'' He has also appeared in films such as [[Detroit (film)|Detroit]] and [[The United States vs. Billie Holiday]].

==Early life==
Williams was born in [[Westchester County, New York]] and grew up in [[Yonkers, New York]].<ref name=filmr/> His mother, Angela Williams, is a singer and songwriter, and his father, Le’Roy Williams, is a police sergeant.<ref name=filmr>[http://www.filmreference.com/film/94/Tyler-James-Williams.html Tyler James Williams] filmreference.com</ref><ref>[https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/tyler-james-williams/bio/193951 Tyler James Williams Biography]. Tvguide.com. Retrieved on 2012-06-18.</ref> Williams has two younger brothers; [[Tyrel Jackson Williams]] (b. 1997) and [[Tylen Jacob Williams]] (b. 2001). Both his younger brothers are also actors; Tyrel starred on the [[Disney XD]] series ''[[Lab Rats (U.S. TV series)|Lab Rats]]'' as Leo Dooley and Tylen starred on the [[Nick at Nite]] sitcom  ''[[Instant Mom]]'' as James Phillips. Williams also appeared on ''Lab Rats'', portraying his brother's character's future self and ''Instant Mom'' as his younger brother's family cousin Jamal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kidzworld.com/article/27075-tyler-james-williams-bio|title=Tyler James Williams Bio|website=Kidz World|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
Williams began his acting career at age 4.<ref name=ebony>{{cite web | url=https://www.questia.com/read/1G1-140092999 | title=Tyler James Williams: star of Everybody Hates Chris. | work=Ebony | date=January 1, 2006 | access-date=}}{{dead link|date=July 2021}}</ref> He later starred in ''[[Little Bill]]'' as the voice of Bobby, briefly replacing Devon Malik Beckford in 2000, and played himself (or an eponymous character, "Tyler") on the [[PBS]] series ''[[Sesame Street]]'' from 2000 to 2005. He rose to fame in September 2005 by playing the title character in the hit series ''[[Everybody Hates Chris]]'',<ref>{{cite web|title=Everybody Hates Chris|url=http://teacher.scholastic.com/scholasticnews/indepth/everybodyhateschris.asp|website=teacher.scholastic.com|access-date=21 September 2016}}</ref> which finished in May 2009. He won a [[Young Artist Award]] in 2007 for his work on the show. He was also a guest star in ''[[Two for the Money (2005 film)|Two for the Money]]'', ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'', and the show ''[[Hi-Jinks]]''.{{citation needed|date=March 2021}} In late 2009, Williams appeared in the second season of ''[[True Jackson, VP]]'' as True's love interest Justin "Lil' Shakespeare" Weber in the two-part episode "Flirting with Fame".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.j-14.com/posts/natasha-bedingfield-and-tyler-james-williams-guest-star-in-new-true-jackson-vp-movie-1508|title=Natasha Bedingfield and Tyler James Williams Guest Star in New True Jackson, VP Movie!|author=J-14 Magazine|website=J-14|date=May 6, 2013|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref>

In 2012, Williams got the role of Owen in the series [[Go On (TV series)|''Go On'']].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2015/01/tyler-james-williams-cast-criminal-minds-spinoff-cbs-1201361056/|title=Tyler James Williams Cast in 'Criminal Minds' Spinoff|date=28 January 2015}}</ref> He also played the lead role of Cyrus DeBarge in the [[Disney Channel]] original movie ''[[Let It Shine (film)|Let It Shine]]''<ref>{{cite web|title=Tyler James Williams Reveals His Musical Talents In Disney's 'Let It Shine'|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/tyler-james-williams-musical-talents-disney-let-it-shine_n_1601141|website=HuffPost|date=15 June 2012|access-date=20 June 2020}}</ref> alongside [[Coco Jones]], [[Trevor Jackson (actor)|Trevor Jackson]], and [[Brandon Mychal Smith]]. Williams was also featured on nine songs on the film's soundtrack, showing off his rapping skills. The movie premiered on June 15, 2012, and his songs "Don't Run Away", "Me and You", "Guardian Angel", "Let It Shine", and "Moment of Truth" all received airplay on [[Radio Disney]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/walt-disney-records-let-it-shine-soundtrack-boasts-songs-by-coco-jones-tyler-james-williams-and-other-stars-from-the-disney-channel-original-movie-152490215.html|title=Walt Disney Records' Let It Shine Soundtrack Boasts Songs By Coco Jones, Tyler James Williams And Other Stars From The Disney Channel Original Movie|author=Walt Disney Records|website=PR News Wire|date=May 22, 2012|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref> The videos were also played frequently on the [[Disney Channel]]. Williams also guest starred in the [[Disney XD]] series ''[[Lab Rats (American TV series)|Lab Rats]]'' as a future version of the character his little brother [[Tyrel Jackson Williams]] played.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ontheblacklist.net/5-quick-facts-actor-hip-hop-recording-artist-tyler-williams/|title=6 Fantastic Facts About Actor and Hip Hop Recording Artist, Tyler Williams|website=On the Black List|author=jaej|date=September 6, 2017|access-date=November 29, 2017}}</ref>

In 2014, Williams starred in [[Justin Simien]]'s independent film ''[[Dear White People]]'' as Lionel Higgins,<ref>{{cite web|title="Dear White People" Actor Tyler James Williams Talks To BOSSIP About Race & Sexuality|url=http://bossip.com/1049783/bossip-exclusive-tyler-james-williams-talks-gay-kissing-scenes-in-dear-white-people-and-interracial-dating-in-2014/|website=bossip.com|date=26 October 2014|access-date=21 September 2016}}</ref> and was cast as the recurring character Noah on the television series ''[[The Walking Dead (TV series)|The Walking Dead]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/walking-dead-tyler-james-williams-735504|title='Walking Dead' Adds 'Go On' Alum in New Mystery Role|author=Lesley Goldberg|work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=24 September 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title='Walking Dead's' Latest Fatality: Savage Death Will "Rock" Glenn|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/walking-dead-noah-dies-tyler-781577|website=www.hollywoodreporter.com|date=15 March 2015|access-date=21 September 2016}}</ref>

In 2016, Williams played Russ "Monty" Montgomery in the ''[[Criminal Minds]]'' spin-off ''[[Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title = 'Criminal Minds' Episode 10.19 Photos: Meet the FBI's International Unit|url = http://www.buddytv.com/slideshows/criminal-minds/criminal-minds-episode-1019-photos-meet-the-fbis-international-unit-98755.aspx|website = www.buddytv.com|access-date = 2016-01-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Tyler James Williams Cast In 'Criminal Minds' Spinoff|url = https://deadline.com/2015/01/tyler-james-williams-cast-criminal-minds-spinoff-cbs-1201361056/|website = Deadline|access-date = 2016-01-30|language = en-US|first = Nellie|last = Andreeva|date = 28 January 2015}}</ref>

In 2017, Williams was diagnosed with [[Crohn's disease]] after experiencing symptoms for three years. Since his diagnosis, he has undergone treatment, including multiple surgeries.<ref name="Crohns">{{cite web |last1=Crohn's and Colitis Foundation |title=#myIBD: Tyler James Williams' Story |url=https://www.facebook.com/ccfafb/videos/549028692226145 |website=Facebook |access-date=16 November 2020 |date=1 December 2018}}</ref>

In 2019, Williams was cast as Jake in the movie ''[[The Wedding Year]]'', teen [[romantic comedy]] film directed by [[Robert Luketic]] and starring [[Sarah Hyland]], [[Jenna Dewan]], [[Matt Shively]], and [[Anna Camp]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/the_wedding_year|title = The Wedding Year}}</ref>

In 2020, Williams played the role of Paul in ''[[The Argument (film)|The Argument]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/the-argument-film-review|title='The Argument': Film Review|date=4 September 2020}}</ref>
