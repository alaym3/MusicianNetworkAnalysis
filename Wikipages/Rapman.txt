'''Andrew Onwubolu''' (born 6 June 1989), better known by his stage name '''Rapman''',<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/sep/18/move-over-bodyguard-shiros-story-youtube-crime-drama-joivan-wade-percelle-ascott-rapman|title=Move over Bodyguard: how crime drama Shiro's Story became a YouTube hit|first=Ben|last=Beaumont-Thomas|date=18 September 2018|newspaper=The Guardian|accessdate=8 November 2019}}</ref> is a British rapper, music producer, actor, writer and film director. He grew up in [[Deptford|Deptford, London]], [[London Borough of Lewisham|Lewisham]] and from age 11 attended [[Sacred Heart Catholic School, Camberwell]].<ref>de Semlyen, Phil (19 Nov, 2019). [https://www.timeout.com/london/film/behind-the-scenes-of-blue-story-the-most-important-london-film-of-2019 Behind the scenes of ‘Blue Story’ – the most important London film of 2019]. Time Out London.</ref><ref name="GuardianInterview">Leigh, Danny (8 November 2019). [https://www.theguardian.com/film/2019/nov/08/rapman-the-london-film-maker-who-gatecrashed-hollywood-in-style Rapman: the London film-maker who gatecrashed Hollywood in style]. The Guardian.</ref>

Rapman is best known for creating the three-part [[YouTube]] musical drama series ''[[Shiro's Story]],'' which has since received a [[cult following]] among the music scene in the [[United Kingdom]]. The series has amassed over 20 million views combined since Part 1 was uploaded in April 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H_6ZJrg-E3Q&list=PLWEtKf1Ud6vKOROH4CDbQM9EyQN0xOwhM|title=Rapman - Shiro's Story [Music Video] Link Up TV|last=Link Up TV|date=8 April 2018|publisher=|via=YouTube}}</ref> The final episode received over a million views in less than five hours.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-45594313|title=Rapman: I want to take Shiro's Story to Netflix|first=De'Graft|last=Mensah|date=21 September 2018|publisher=|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> Rapman narrates the majority of the story through [[rapping]].<ref name="auto"/> Part 1 was self-financed by Rapman and created by calling in favours with friends.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cFtWj1oN-pw|title=How Real Life Inspired Youtube Hit Shiro's Story - Good Morning Britain|last=Good Morning Britain|date=20 September 2018|publisher=|via=YouTube}}</ref> After the release of ''Shiro's Story'' he was offered a contract by [[Jeffrey Katzenberg]] to work for his short form content platform [[Quibi]]. He ultimately turned down the offer to work on ''Blue Story'', but accepted a writing project for Katzenberg.<ref name="GuardianInterview"/>

In November 2018, Rapman signed a deal with the [[Roc Nation]] entertainment agency for management, film and TV, with the opportunity to start his own record label. The deal involved travelling to Los Angeles to meet Jay-Z, the company's owner.<ref>Griffin, Louise (16 Nov 2018).[https://metro.co.uk/2018/11/16/shiros-story-creator-rapman-signs-life-changing-deal-with-jay-zs-roc-nation-8148951/ Shiro’s Story creator Rapman signs ‘life-changing’ deal with Jay-Z’s Roc Nation]. [[Metro (British newspaper)|Metro]]</ref> In March 2019, Rapman announced he was developing a previous YouTube series he made in 2014, ''[[Blue Story]]'', into a [[Blue Story|full-length movie]], in partnership with [[BBC Films]] and [[Paramount Pictures]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Shiro's Story creator Rapman to make Blue Story film|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-47412450 |accessdate=9 July 2019 |publisher=BBC News |date=1 March 2019}}</ref>
