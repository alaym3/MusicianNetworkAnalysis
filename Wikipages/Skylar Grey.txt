'''Holly Brook Hafermann''' (born February 23, 1986), known professionally as '''Skylar Grey''', is an American singer, songwriter and record producer from [[Mazomanie, Wisconsin]]. In 2004, at the age of 17, Grey signed a publishing deal with [[Universal Music Publishing Group]] and a [[recording contract]] with [[Machine Shop Recordings]] under the name '''Holly Brook'''. In 2006, she released her debut studio album, ''[[Like Blood Like Honey]]'', under the aforementioned labels.

In 2010, Grey co-wrote [[Eminem]] and [[Rihanna]]'s hit single, "[[Love the Way You Lie]]", with Eminem and British record producer [[Alex da Kid]], who subsequently signed Grey to his [[Interscope Records]] label imprint, [[Kidinakorner]]. Grey's second album, ''[[Don't Look Down (Skylar Grey album)|Don't Look Down]]'', was released in July 2013. The album reached the top 10 in the US and produced four singles. Grey's third studio release, ''[[Natural Causes (Skylar Grey album)|Natural Causes]]'', was issued in September 2016.

Grey is perhaps best known for providing guest vocals on several [[hit single]]s, such as [[Fort Minor]]'s "[[Where'd You Go (Fort Minor song)|Where'd You Go]]", [[Sean Combs|Diddy]]'s "[[Coming Home (Diddy – Dirty Money song)|Coming Home]]", [[Dr. Dre]]'s "[[I Need a Doctor]]", [[Nicki Minaj]]'s "[[Bed of Lies (song)|Bed of Lies]]", and [[Macklemore]]'s "[[Glorious (Macklemore song)|Glorious]]". Additionally, she has written songs for a number of prominent artists in the music industry, including [[Kehlani]], [[G-Eazy]], [[Alicia Keys]], [[Christina Aguilera]], [[Zedd]], [[Nick Jonas]], [[Céline Dion]], and [[CeeLo Green]].

==Life and career==
===1994–2004: Early life and Generations===
As a child, Grey performed professionally in a [[folk music|folk]] duo with her mother, Candace Kreitlow,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cookcountynews-herald.com/news/2010-10-30/lives_remembered|title=Lives Remembered - www.cookcountynews-herald.com - Cook County News Herald}}</ref> called Generations.<ref>Samara Kalk, [https://web.archive.org/web/20121105221628/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-81154171.html Rhythm Profile], ''[[Wisconsin State Journal]]'', December 27, 2001.</ref> The group released three studio albums together.

===2005–10: ''Like Blood Like Honey'' and EPs===
{{further|Like Blood Like Honey}}

Grey co-wrote "Done With Like" and "She Said" with Jon Ingoldsby and American actress [[Brie Larson]] for Larson's first and only album, ''[[Finally Out of P.E.]]'' (2005). In 2005, Grey was featured on American hip hop group [[Fort Minor]]'s songs, "[[Where'd You Go (Fort Minor song)|Where'd You Go]]" and "Be Somebody".{{citation needed|date=October 2013}} "Where'd You Go" was released as a single on April 14, 2006, with an accompanying [[music video]] following soon after. The song gained commercial success and ended up becoming a top four hit on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart and also became [[RIAA certification|certified platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). After working with music producer Jonathan Ingoldsby, Grey released her debut album, titled ''[[Like Blood Like Honey]]'', on June 6, 2006, under [[Warner Bros. Records]]. The album peaked at number 35 on [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]{{'s}} [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=holly brook|chart=all}}|title=Holly Brook Album & Song Chart History: Heatseekers Albums|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|access-date=May 2, 2011}}</ref> During this time, Grey had landed opening spots for [[concert tour]]s with [[Jamie Cullum]], [[k.d. lang]], [[Daniel Powter]], [[Teddy Geiger]] and [[Duncan Sheik]].{{citation needed|date=October 2013}}

Through Machine Shop Records, Grey worked with Linkin Park affiliates [[Styles of Beyond]] and [[Apathy (rapper)|Apathy]]. She was featured on two tracks, "Victim" and "No Sad Tomorrow", from Apathy's second studio album, ''[[Wanna Snuggle?]]'' (2009). Grey toured as part of [[Duncan Sheik]]'s band and contributed substantially to his 2009 album, ''[[Whisper House]]''. In 2009, Grey sang backing vocals on Eurovision contestant [[Yohanna]]'s debut album, ''[[Butterflies and Elvis]]''. In August 2009, still under the name Holly Brook, she lent both her song "It's Raining Again" and her image to a promotional campaign for Ciao Water.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ciaowater.com/|title=c i a o|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.complex.com/music/2011/02/who-is-skylar-grey-the-secret-history-of-raps-new-chorus-queen/ciao-water-commercial|title=Ciao Water Commercial - Who Is Skylar Grey? - Complex UK|first=Ernest|last=Baker|work=Complex UK|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref> In early 2010, she performed in the theatrical version of "Whisper House," playing one of the two lead ghost vocalists, along with David Poe.<ref name='LATimes1'>{{cite news | first=Charles | last=McNulty | title=Theater review: 'Whisper House' at San Diego's Old Globe* | date=January 24, 2010 | url =http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/culturemonster/2010/01/theater-review-whisper-house-at-san-diegos-old-globe.html | work=The Los Angeles Times | access-date =January 31, 2010 }}</ref> On June 10, 2010, she self-released the seven-song [[extended play]] (EP), ''O’Dark:Thirty''. Production of the EP was handled by Duncan Sheik and Jon Ingoldsby.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://magazine.localsounds.org/2010/07/27/holly-brook-odark-thirty/|title=HOLLY BROOK – O'Dark Thirty|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref>

===2010–11: Songwriting and name change===
[[File:Skylar Grey at Tibet Talk for World Peace (cropped).jpg|thumb|right|Grey singing at the Tibet Talk for World Peace in July 2011]]
Brook later changed her stage name to Skylar Grey. While still living in [[Oregon]], Grey visited New York to meet her publisher [[Jennifer Blakeman]], to seek help presenting her work. Blakeman had suggested that she work with English producer [[Alex da Kid]]. Alex da Kid then sent Grey a few tracks that he had been working on, the first song finished being "[[Love the Way You Lie]]".<ref name="CHANGLEING">{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2011/06/skylar-grey-changeling/|title=Skylar Grey: The Changeling|work=SPIN|date=June 10, 2011|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite episode | series=The KLOS Sessions | title=Chic, "Good Times" | station=[[KLOS]], [[Los Angeles]]  | url=https://soundcloud.com/theklossessions/chic-good-times | air-date = March 25, 2019 }}</ref> The song was given to American rapper [[Eminem]] and Barbadian singer [[Rihanna]]. Their version became a worldwide hit, reaching number one on 26 charts and being nominated for four [[Grammy Award]]s. Grey received a [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] Grammy nomination for "Love the Way You Lie".<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/949954/eminem-bruno-mars-lady-gaga-lead-grammy-nominations Eminem, Bruno Mars, Lady Gaga Lead Grammy Nominations] via Billboard</ref> Grey wrote the [[refrain|hook]] to all three versions of "Love the Way You Lie" by Eminem and Rihanna, and even recorded a solo version that was later featured on her fourth EP, ''The Buried Sessions of Skylar Grey'' (2012).

Alex da Kid went on to sign Skylar Grey to a production deal on his KIDinaKORNER [[imprint label|record label imprint]].<ref>[http://m.billboard.com/features/hitmaker-alex-da-kid-talks-eminem-s-lie-1004109717.story#/features/hitmaker-alex-da-kid-talks-eminem-s-lie-1004109717.story Alex Da Kid signs Skylar Grey to Wonderland Entertainment] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708001318/http://m.billboard.com/features/hitmaker-alex-da-kid-talks-eminem-s-lie-1004109717.story#/features/hitmaker-alex-da-kid-talks-eminem-s-lie-1004109717.story |date=July 8, 2011 }} via Billboard</ref> In 2010, Grey also co-wrote and was featured on [[Diddy – Dirty Money]]'s single "[[Coming Home (Diddy – Dirty Money song)|Coming Home]]", which gained massive commercial success.<ref>[http://www.rap-up.com/2010/11/15/dawn-and-kalenna-call-diddy-dirty-moneys-coming-home-a-gift/ Diddy-Dirty Money feat. Skylar Grey – "Coming Home"] via Rap-Up</ref> Also in 2010, Grey co-wrote "[[Castle Walls]]" by American rapper [[T.I.]] and American pop singer [[Christina Aguilera]].<ref>[http://www.rap-up.com/2010/11/23/rap-up-tv-alex-da-kid-builds-castle-walls-for-ti-and-christina-aguilera/ Alex Da Kid names Skylar Grey as writer for "Castle Walls" by T.I. & Christina Aguilera] via Rap-Up</ref> On February 1, 2011, American rapper and renowned hip hop producer [[Dr. Dre]] released a song titled "[[I Need a Doctor]]", featuring Grey and Eminem.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20101120211427/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/68404/235196 Dr. Dre reunites with Eminem] via RollingStone</ref> "I Need a Doctor" managed to peak at number four on the US ''Billboard'' Hot 100 chart and was certified double platinum by the RIAA. The song garnered Grey several Grammy nominations.

Grey made her national television debut under her new alias during the [[53rd Annual Grammy Awards]], where she performed "I Need a Doctor", with Eminem and Dr. Dre. During this time, Grey announced that she had begun working on her album then-titled ''[[Invinsible]]''. In March 2011, it was revealed Grey signed to [[Interscope Records]], through Alex da Kid's KIDinaKORNER and announced she would be releasing a single in the spring.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2011/03/10/skylar-grey-signs-to-interscope-single-due-this-spring/|title=Skylar Grey Signs to Interscope, Single Due This Spring|work=Rap-Up|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref> Later in 2011, Grey was featured on a song titled "[[Words I Never Said]]", the second single from [[Lupe Fiasco]]'s ''[[Lasers (album)|Lasers]]'' (2011).

===2015–17: ''Natural Causes'' and soundtracks===
{{main|Natural Causes (Skylar Grey album)}}

Also in February, Grey confirmed she has a song on the ''[[Furious 7]]'' soundtrack, called "I Will Return." In March 2015, she released her version of "[[Addicted to Love (song)|Addicted to Love]]" on iTunes, and also re-released the song "Words," which was deleted from the [[iTunes Store]] in 2013. On March 29, 2015, Grey appeared at her third [[WrestleMania]] event, performing a medley of "[[Listen (David Guetta album)|Rise]]" and "Money and the Power" at the [[WrestleMania 31|thirty-first annual event]], with [[Travis Barker]] and [[Kid Ink]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Martin|first1=Adam|title=Skylar Grey, Kid Ink, Travis Barker will be at WrestleMania 31|url=http://www.wrestleview.com/wwe-news/53687-skylar-grey-kid-ink-travis-barker-to-perform-at-wrestlemania|website=wrestleview.com|date=March 23, 2015|access-date=March 24, 2015}}</ref>

On May 18, 2015, house producer [[deadmau5]] released a short demo on [[SoundCloud]] in collaboration with [[Kaskade]], featuring vocals by Grey, titled "[[Beneath with Me]]".<ref>{{Cite tweet |user=deadmau5 |number=600189019536908288 |date=May 18, 2015 |title=deadmau5 retweeting Kaskade's tweet about the "Beneath With Me" collab }}</ref> On October 6, 2016, a music video for the song was released.<ref name="mtsk324">{{cite journal | url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/73783/20161007/deadmau5-kaskade-skylar-grey-beneath-with-me.htm | title=deadmau5, Kaskade, Skylar Grey Finally Release 'Beneath With Me' | journal=Music Times | date=October 7, 2016 | access-date=November 4, 2016 | author=Middleton, Ryan}}</ref>

''[[Natural Causes (Skylar Grey album)|Natural Causes]]'', Grey's third studio album, was released on September 23, 2016,. The album received positive reviews and was a moderate commercial success. It was preceded by the singles "Moving Mountains", the Eminem-produced  "Come Up For Air",<ref name="ibt1sk">{{cite web | url=http://www.ibtimes.com.au/music-video-eminem-produced-single-skylar-greys-come-air-goes-online-1527637 | title=Music video of Eminem-produced single, Skylar Grey's 'Come Up for Air,' goes online | website=International Business Times | date=September 5, 2016 | access-date=September 6, 2016 | author=Bayos, Kris}}</ref> and "Lemonade".<ref name="baemusic12445">{{cite web | url=http://www.baeblemusic.com/musicblog/10-31-2016/skylar-grey-is-a-bad-ass-karma-god-in-her-new-video-for-lemonade.html | title=Skylar Grey is a Bad Ass Karma God in Her New Video For 'Lemonade' | publisher=Baeble Music | date=October 31, 2016 | access-date=November 4, 2016 | author=Spruch, Kirsten}}</ref><ref name="nc4vc">{{cite web | url=http://vergecampus.com/2016/09/skylar-grey-eminem-kill-for-you-natural-causes/ | title=Listen to Eminem & Skylar Grey's Bonnie and Clyde-Inspired 'Kill for You | publisher=Verge Campus | date=September 22, 2016 | access-date=November 4, 2016}}</ref> In the fall of 2016, Grey went on her Natural Causes Tour with morgxn.<ref>[http://blog.fanzoot.com/2016/08/19/skylar-grey-natural-causes-tour-dates/ "Skylar Grey Announces Natural Causes Tour Dates,"] Fanzoot, August 19, 2016.</ref>

In 2017, Grey provided vocals for the song "Wreak Havoc" and wrote Kehlani's "[[Gangsta (Kehlani song)|Gangsta]]" for the ''[[Suicide Squad (film)|Suicide Squad]]'' soundtrack.<ref name="billbnc">{{cite magazine | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7469618/skylar-grey-reveals-sophomore-album-title-release-date | title=Singer/Songwriter Skylar Grey Reveals Sophomore Album Title & Release Date: Exclusive | magazine=Billboard | date=August 15, 2016 | access-date=August 19, 2016}}</ref> Grey later co-wrote Kehlani and [[G-Eazy]]'s song "Good Life" for ''[[The Fate of the Furious: The Album]]'' soundtrack. Grey collaborated with [[G-Eazy]] again, writing "Pick Me Up" for his album ''[[The Beautiful & Damned]]''.<ref>[https://listen.tidal.com/album/82477168 "The Beautiful & Damned / G-Eazy TIDAL"]. [[Tidal (service)|Tidal]]. Retrieved February 7, 2018.</ref>

On March 31, 2017, American rock band [[Papa Roach]] released their song, "Periscope", featuring guest vocals by Grey. Grey was also featured on American rapper [[Macklemore]]'s single "[[Glorious (Macklemore song)|Glorious]]", released on June 25, 2017. The platinum selling single topped the charts in numerous countries.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ariacharts.com.au/charts/singles-chart|title=ARIA Australian Top 50 Singles|publisher=[[Australian Recording Industry Association]]|date=November 6, 2017|access-date=November 9, 2017}}</ref><ref name=Brokenrecord>{{cite podcast|url=https://art19.com/shows/broken-record/episodes/1777c7ba-d9a4-449d-8201-8d2c9d4d1586|title=Broken Record|website=Art19|publisher=Art19|host=Gladwell, Malcom|access-date=November 11, 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171111152254/https://art19.com/shows/broken-record/episodes/1777c7ba-d9a4-449d-8201-8d2c9d4d1586|archive-date=November 11, 2017}}</ref> Grey collaborated with Eminem on his album ''Revival'', co-writing the collaborations "Walk On Water" with [[Beyoncé]], "Like Home" with [[Alicia Keys]], "Need Me" with [[Pink (singer)|Pink]], and "Tragic Endings" with Grey herself.<ref>[https://listen.tidal.com/album/82119363 "Revival / Eminem TIDAL"]. Tidal. Retrieved February 7, 2018.</ref>

Previous collaborator [[Kaskade]] released his debut [[Christmas]] album ''Kaskade Christmas'' on November 24 and featured Grey singing a cover of "[[O Come, O Come, Emmanuel]]" retitled "O Come Emmanuel".<ref>[https://listen.tidal.com/album/80944874 "Kaskade Christmas / Kaskade TIDAL"]. [[Tidal (service)|Tidal]]. Retrieved February 7, 2018.</ref>

===2018–present: ''Angel with Tattoos'' and ''Dark Thoughts''===
On January 25, 2018, Grey released a cover of [[Ben E. King]]'s "[[Stand by Me (Ben E. King song)|Stand by Me]]",<ref>[https://listen.tidal.com/track/83645227 "Stand By Me / Skylar Grey TIDAL"]. Retrieved February 7, 2018.</ref> which was featured in a  [[Budweiser]] [[Super Bowl LII]] commercial.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CxGUmtRLm5g Budweiser 2018 Super Bowl Commercial | “Stand By You”.] [[YouTube]]. Retrieved February 7, 2018.</ref> A portion of the proceeds from the song went to the [[American Red Cross]] to benefit those affected by natural disasters.<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/super-bowl/8096489/skylar-grey-super-bowl-commercial-budweiser-stand-by-me ''Skylar Grey Helps Highlight 'A Good Cause on a Large Scale' With New Budweiser Super Bowl Commercial: Watch'']. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', Julia Marzovilla. Retrieved February 7, 2018.</ref> A music video was released on February 2, 2018.<ref>[https://www.vevo.com/watch/skylar-grey/stand-by-me-(official)/USUV71800267 Skylar Grey - "Stand By Me" (Official)]. [[Vevo]]. Retrieved February 7, 2018.</ref>

In an interview with ''[[Uproxx]]'' published on January 26, 2018, Grey revealed that she was working on her fourth album and is planning on including her own version of "[[Walk on Water (Eminem song)|Walk on Water]]", which she had written for [[Eminem]] and [[Beyoncé]], on the album.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://uproxx.com/music/skylar-gray-interview-super-bowl-budweiser-commerical/|title=Skylar Grey Talks About Her New Super Bowl Spot And Her Joni Mitchell-Inspired Solo Album|publisher=[[UPROXX]]|first=Corbin|last=Reiff|date=January 26, 2018|access-date=February 7, 2018}}</ref> Later that year, Grey co-wrote and performed the single "Everything I Need", for James Wan's superhero blockbuster film ''[[Aquaman (2018 film)|Aquaman]]''.

In May 2019 Grey, along with fellow artists [[Jamie N Commons]] and [[Gallant (singer)|Gallant]], released a new version of [[Soul Asylum]]'s 1993 "[[Runaway Train (Soul Asylum song)|Runaway Train]]". The video for the song was made in collaboration with the [[National Center for Missing and Exploited Children]] and utilizes [[geotargeting]] to show viewers missing children in their local area.<ref>[https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/soul-asylum-runaway-train-video-25-anniversary-838659/ "Songwriters Remake Soul Asylum's ‘Runaway Train’ for Video's 25th Anniversary"]. ''[[Rolling Stone]]''. Retrieved July 8, 2019.</ref> The video was later nominated for a [[MTV Video Music Award|VMA]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/mtv-vmas-2019-see-the-full-list-of-nominees-here/|title=MTV VMAs 2019: See The Full List of Nominees Here|website=Pitchfork|date=July 23, 2019|language=en|access-date=2019-07-26}}</ref>

On July 19, 2019, Grey announced on social media that she had left [[Interscope Records]]/[[Kidinakorner|KIDinaKORNER]] and that she would release her fourth studio album ''Angel with Tattoos.'' The first single released was titled "Shame On You".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Skylar Grey on Instagram: "Can u believe it's been a year since this little hotel room rehearsal of "Shame On You" and this song still hasn't come out?!... Dropping…"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B1fqP77JWZx/|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2019-08-23}}</ref> ''Angel with Tattoos'' was released as an EP on October 18, 2019 and she released the music video for the title-song of the album on the same day.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Interview: Skylar Grey Talks 'Angel With Tattoos' & New Beginnings|url=https://www.idolator.com/7899736/interview-skylar-grey-talks-angel-with-tattoos-new-beginnings?andro=1&chrome=1|website=Idolator|date = October 18, 2019|access-date=2019-10-22}}</ref> Grey announced that the album is open ended, and songs will be added over time, stating; "Who knows? It could end up with a hundred songs, it could stop at five.”.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Skylar Grey Discusses 'Open-Ended' Album & Returns To Her Genre-Bending, 'Eclectic Musical Upbringing'|url=https://hollywoodlife.com/2019/09/27/skylar-grey-angel-with-tattoos-interview/|last=Stagnitta|first=Ali|date=2019-09-27|website=Hollywood Life|language=en|access-date=2020-05-11}}</ref>

On August 7, 2019, Grey released a new song titled "New Kind Of Love" that was featured on ''[[Hulu|Hulu's]]'' re-make of [[Four Weddings and a Funeral (miniseries)|"Four Weddings And A Funeral"]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://skylargreynews.com/2019/08/07/skylar-grey-mcxciii-new-kind-of-love-now-available-buy/|title=Skylar Grey MCXCIII :: 'New Kind of Love' – from Hulu's 'Four Weddings and a Funeral', Now Available {{!}} Buy {{!}} SKYLAR GREY NEWS|access-date=2019-08-23}}</ref> In the Aug. 24 issue of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', Grey revealed that she had left [[Interscope Records]] due to "creative differences;" that her departure had been "amicable;" and that she had signed with Crush Music in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/legal-and-management/8528091/skylar-grey-signs-crush-music-management-signed|title=Inside Skylar Grey's New Deal With Crush Music Management: Exclusive|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-08-26}}</ref>

In 2019, Grey and her fiancé Elliott Taylor wrote "Falling In Love Again" for [[Celine Dion]]'s ''[[Courage (Celine Dion album)|Courage]]''. [[Train (band)|Train]] also released a song featuring Grey titled "Mai Tais". The song was featured on the [[CBS]] show [[Magnum P.I. (2018 TV series)|''Magnum P.I'']].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8543834/train-mai-tais-featuring-skylar-grey|title=Train Unveils the Tropical 'Mai Tais' Featuring Skylar Grey|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-01-25}}</ref>

On January 17, 2020, Eminem released his eleventh studio album, ''[[Music to Be Murdered By|Music To Be Murdered By]]'', featuring a song featuring Grey titled "Leaving Heaven". Furthermore, Grey co-wrote and produced the song, making her the first sole female to produce an Eminem song.

On July 14, 2020, Grey revealed her then-upcoming album title, ''Dark Thoughts''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lipps Service with Scott Lipps: Skylar Grey on Apple Podcasts|url=https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/skylar-grey/id1440565435?i=1000484869279|access-date=2020-07-19|website=Apple Podcasts|language=en-us}}</ref> The project was preceded by the singles "Dark Thoughts", a cover of "[[Goosebumps (Travis Scott song)|Goosebumps]]",<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ahlgrim|first=Callie|title=Skylar Grey explains why Travis Scott is a 'legend' and how he's inspired her new solo music|url=https://www.insider.com/skylar-grey-interview-goosebumps-travis-scott-cover-new-music-2020-6|access-date=2020-07-04|website=Insider}}</ref> and "Sunscreen".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-03|title=Skylar Grey Rolls Out Summery Single "Sunscreen"|url=https://www.idolator.com/7912259/skylar-grey-rolls-out-summery-single-sunscreen|access-date=2020-07-04|website=idolator}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Skylar Grey - SUNSCREEN (Official Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dWhsWLHjawY|website=YouTube}}</ref> The project was eventually scrapped as Grey announced she started working on a new album via various social platforms on January 16, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Login • Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/accounts/login/|access-date=2021-01-20|website=www.instagram.com}}</ref>

On September 30, 2021, Grey's song ''"Last One Standing"'' was featured on the soundtrack for the film [[Venom: Let There Be Carnage]]  with [[Eminem]], [[Polo G]], and [[Mozzy]] featured on the song.

===Stage name===
Grey explained that she thought her stage name "represents the unknowns in life. People seem to be afraid of the unknowns, but I'm the complete opposite. I dive into the unknown because I feel like that's where all your possibilities come from."<ref>{{cite web|title=Skylar Grey talks about her name change|url=http://music-mix.ew.com/2011/02/22/skylar-grey-interview-eminem-dr-dre/|work=EW|access-date=May 12, 2011|first=Mandi|last=Bierly|archive-url=http://music-mix.ew.com/2011/02/|archive-date=Feb 2011|page=4}}</ref> She explained to Beatweek that she chose "grey" with an ''e'' rather than an ''a'' because "I like to do things the original way" and because "it's more masculine. I'm not a very feminine person."<ref>[http://www.beatweek.com/coverstory/9445-skylar-grey-the-beatweek-interview-with-the-future-of-pop-music/ Skylar Grey: the Beatweek interview with the future of pop music] via Beatweek Magazine  {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20120731163726/http://www.beatweek.com/coverstory/9445-skylar-grey-the-beatweek-interview-with-the-future-of-pop-music/ |date=July 31, 2012 }}</ref>
