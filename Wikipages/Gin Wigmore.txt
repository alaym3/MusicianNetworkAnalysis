'''Virginia Claire Wigmore''' (born 6 June 1986<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/news/article.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=10602242 |title=Gin Wigmore: A free spirit |work=[[The New Zealand Herald]]|first=Joanna|last=Hunkin|date=10 October 2009|access-date=26 March 2010}}</ref>) is a New Zealand singer and songwriter. Featured on the  [[Smashproof]] single "[[Brother (Smashproof song)|Brother]]" in 2009, Wigmore went on to release four albums ''[[Holy Smoke (Gin Wigmore album)|Holy Smoke]]'' (2009), ''[[Gravel & Wine]]'' (2011), ''[[Blood to Bone]]'' (2015) and ''[[Ivory (Gin Wigmore album)|Ivory]]'' (2018), with the first three having been chart-toppers on the [[Recorded Music NZ|New Zealand Albums Chart]]. She is known for her high pitched and raspy voice.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/50716/gin-wigmore-you-oughta-know/|title=You Oughta Know Artist Gin Wigmore Kicks Up Grit, Excitement And Gravel & Wine In U.S.|work=VH1 News|access-date=24 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.culturebrats.com/2012/11/the-girl-with-weirdo-voice-our.html|title=Gin Wigmore: The Culture Brats Interview – Culture Brats|work=culturebrats.com|access-date=24 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://inkedaustralia.com/2013/07/30/whisky-wine-gin/|title=Whisky, Wine & Gin|work=Inked|access-date=24 October 2015}}</ref>

== Career ==

===International Songwriting Competition===
Inspired by [[David Gray (musician)|David Gray]]'s album ''[[White Ladder]]'', Wigmore wrote her first song, "Angelfire", at the age of 14. Two years later her father died of cancer and Wigmore stopped writing and playing music. She went to Argentina on an exchange program to teach at a kindergarten. On her return to New Zealand, she wrote "Hallelujah", a tribute to her father.<ref name=max>{{cite web |url=http://www.maxfoundation.co.nz/default,191,gin_wigmore.sm |title=Gin Wigmore |publisher=Max Foundation for New Zealand Women |access-date=20 October 2012 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6CsfYzsIA?url=http://www.maxfoundation.co.nz/default,191,gin_wigmore.sm |archive-date=13 December 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Her sister entered the song in the US-based [[International Songwriting Competition]] in 2004,<ref name=ISC/> and Wigmore beat 11,000 songwriting aspirants from 77 countries to become the youngest and only unsigned Grand Prize winner in the history of the competition.<ref name=max/> She also won the Teen category with "Angelfire".<ref name=ISC>{{cite web |url= http://www.songwritingcompetition.com/winners2004.htm |title=International Songwriting Competition – Winners 2004 |access-date=23 July 2008 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080617070657/http://www.songwritingcompetition.com/winners2004.htm |archive-date = 17 June 2008}}</ref>

<blockquote>
Hallelujah was my way of telling my family I had dealt with dad's passing and honestly, I didn't want to share these feelings with anyone else. I wanted the moment for myself and my family.

— Gin Wigmore<ref name=wigmore>{{cite web|url=http://ginwigmore.com.au/|title=GIN WIGMORE|work=ginwigmore.com.au|access-date=24 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190319192721/http://ginwigmore.com.au/|archive-date=19 March 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref></blockquote>

===''Extended Play''===
{{main|Extended Play (Gin Wigmore EP)}}
''Extended Play'' was Wigmore's first EP. It was produced by [[Tony Buchen]] (Blue King Brown, The Whitlams, Macromantics, Kid Confucius) and contains the tracks "Hallelujah", "These Roses", "SOS", "Under My Skin", and "Easy Come Easy Go". Australian musician [[John Butler (musician)|John Butler]] played [[ukulele]] on song "SOS".<ref name=wigmore /> Released in 2008, the EP peaked at number ten in the New Zealand charts. It was certified Platinum in New Zealand on 2 May 2010, selling over 15,000 copies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.radioscope.net.nz/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=77&Itemid=61|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110724195845/http://www.radioscope.net.nz/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=77&Itemid=61|url-status=dead|title=Radioscope: New Zealand Platinum Certification|archive-date=24 July 2011}}</ref>

''Extended Play'' was released in [[Australia]] on 2 August 2008 by [[Island Records]]. Wigmore was the first artist signed with Island Records, a partner company with [[Universal Music Group]], Australia.<ref name=wigmore /> She toured with Australian artist [[Pete Murray (Australian singer-songwriter)|Pete Murray]] on his national tour in August/September 2008. Wigmore toured in 2008, including at the Cross Town Revue in Auckland, and in December 2008, toured with musicians [[John Mellencamp]] and [[Sheryl Crow]] in New Zealand. Her hit single "Under My Skin" was featured on an [[Air New Zealand]] advertising campaign, ''Nothing to Hide''.

Wigmore featured in the 2009 song and video of "[[Brother (Smashproof song)|Brother]]" by new group [[Smashproof]], also from Auckland.

===''Holy Smoke''===
{{main|Holy Smoke (Gin Wigmore album)}}
In 2009, Wigmore released her debut full-length album ''Holy Smoke'', recorded with [[The Cardinals (rock band)|The Cardinals]] of [[Ryan Adams]] & the Cardinals fame.<ref>{{cite web | author= Lindsay, Andrew | title=Gin Wigmore and the Cardinals |date= 27 July 2009 |url=http://stereokill.net/2009/07/27/gin-wigmore-and-the-cardinals/ | website=Stereokill.net | access-date=27 July 2009 }}</ref> The first single from the album, "[[Oh My (Gin Wigmore song)|Oh My]]", debuted on the New Zealand Singles Chart at number twenty-one on 24 August 2009, moving to number seven the following week. It peaked at number four.

Three other singles were released during 2010, "I Do" charted at no. 14 on the Official New Zealand Singles Chart, while third single "Hey Ho" missed out on a top 20 position by charting just outside at no. 21. The fourth single taken from ''Holy Smoke'' was "Too Late For Lovers", and despite hitting no. 3 on the NZ40 Airplay chart, the single failed to crack the national Top 40. During the week of 30 May 2010, all four of Wigmore's singles from ''Holy Smoke'' appeared on the NZ40 Airplay Chart<ref>[http://www.radioscope.net.nz/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=2963&Itemid=70 ]{{dead link|date=February 2012}}</ref> 'Too Late For Lovers' #7, 'Oh My' #23, 'Hey Ho' #24,  'I Do' #37

The single "Oh My" is the theme track for the series [[The Almighty Johnsons]]. Wigmore's single "Hey Ho" appeared during the closing scenes of the ''[[Weeds (TV series)|Weeds]]'' Season 6 Finale. In September 2011, Home Improvement Retailer [[Lowe's]] began a new brand campaign featuring Wigmore's single "Don't Stop".<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.thestreet.com/story/11252332/1/lowes-never-stop-improving-brand-spot.html | title=Lowe's Never Stop Improving Brand Spot | website=[[TheStreet.com]] | date=19 September 2011 | access-date=20 September 2011 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121014180344/http://www.thestreet.com/story/11252332/1/lowes-never-stop-improving-brand-spot.html | archive-date=14 October 2012 | url-status=dead }}</ref> Her song "Stealing Happiness" appeared in the TV movie "Tangiwai".

===''Gravel & Wine''===
{{main|Gravel & Wine}}Wigmore travelled for two months in  [[Mississippi]] and [[Alabama]] to get a [[Southern United States]] inspiration for her second album which would be recorded in [[California]] under producer [[Butch Walker]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/dominion-post/culture/performance/5871441/Gin-Wigmore-up-close-and-personal|title=Gin Wigmore up close and personal|website=Stuff.co.nz|date=28 October 2011}}</ref> In September 2011, Wigmore revealed the title and release of her new album ''[[Gravel & Wine]]'' on her official Facebook page.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/GinWigmore |title=Gin Wigmore |website=Facebook.com |access-date=15 February 2012}}</ref> The first single "Black Sheep" was released 26 September 2011, debuting at #13 in the [[Official New Zealand Music Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nztop40.co.nz/chart/singles?chart=1997 |title=NZ Top 40 Singles Chart &#124; The Official New Zealand Music Chart |website=Nztop40.co.nz |date=2011-10-03 |access-date=2018-05-03}}</ref> The album was released on 7 November 2011. It debuted atop the New Zealand charts, and at #29 on the Australian Albums Chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=australian-charts.com – Gravel & Wine – Gin Wigmore|url=http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Gin+Wigmore&titel=Gravel+%26+Wine&cat=a|work=[[ARIA Charts]]|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=17 April 2013}}</ref> Wigmore also released music videos for "Man Like That" and "If Only".

"Black Sheep" appeared in episode 12, season 8 of television series ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]'', in episode 2, season 2 of ''[[Teen Wolf (2011 TV series)|Teen Wolf]]'', in episode 5, season 5 of ''[[The Good Wife (TV series)|The Good Wife]]'', and in episode 1 season 1 of ''[[Wynonna Earp (TV series)|Wynonna Earp]].'' She appeared singing it on ''Gift of Revenge'', a segment of ABC's television show ''[[Revenge (TV series)|Revenge]]'' season 2, episode 7. The song was also used in television commercials for [[Guinness Black Lager]] and Nissan Rogue Midnight Edition (2017) in the United States.{{Citation needed|date=February 2020}}

"Kill of the Night" was also featured during season 2 of ''Teen Wolf'', along with the series ''[[Pretty Little Liars (TV series)|Pretty Little Liars]]'' and  ''[[666 Park Avenue]]'', and [[AMC (TV channel)|AMC]] during its annual airing of Halloween movies and shows titled ''[[FearFest]]''.<br>The track was also the theme song to the 2016 [[E4 (TV channel)|E4]] and [[Netflix]] mini series ''[[Crazyhead (TV series)|Crazyhead]]''. Commercials with the song include [[Alfa Romeo]] in Australia, [[Nationwide Insurance]] in the United States, and a worldwide short by [[Johnnie Walker]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nativetongue.com.au/writers/gin-wigmore/|title=Gin Wigmore|work=nativetongue.com.au|access-date=24 October 2015}}</ref>

Wigmore was featured in a ''[[Skyfall]]'' tie-in commercial from [[Heineken]] performing the song "Man Like That", which also featured [[James Bond]] portrayer [[Daniel Craig]] and [[Bond girl]] [[Bérénice Marlohe]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/film/7713000/Gin-Wigmore-sings-for-007|title=Gin Wigmore sings for 007|work=Stuff|date=21 September 2012|access-date=24 October 2015}}</ref>

''Gravel & Wine'' was released in the U.S. on 2 April 2013, and Wigmore appeared on the entire 2013 [[Vans Warped Tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.contactmusic.com/press/gin-wigmore-announces-april-2nd-2013-for-gravel |title=Gin Wigmore Announces April 2nd 2013 For 'Gravel & Wine' Us Album Release |website=Contactmusic.com |date=8 December 1980 |access-date=25 March 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://punkmusic.about.com/od/warpedtour2013/a/The-2013-Vans-Warped-Tour-Lineup.htm |title=The 2013 Vans Warped Tour Lineup |website=Punkmusic.about.com |date=19 November 2012 |access-date=25 March 2013}}</ref>

=== ''Blood to Bone''===
On 14 August 2014, Wigmore had confirmed she had begun studio production on her third LP. Throughout 15 to 22 August, Wigmore had worked in West Hollywood. But on 25 August 2014, Wigmore posted a picture of the beginning of her thirteen-day studio work at Blakeslee Recording Studios in North Hollywood, California. Stephen Rusch is engineering the album. On 29 January 2015, Wigmore posted the album cover and title of her the third LP, ''Blood to Bone'', which was scheduled for release by [[Island Records]] on 26 June 2015.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Weiner|first1=Natalie|title=Exclusive: Watch New Zealander Gin Wigmore Get a 'New Rush' on Latest Video|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6553075/gin-wigmore-new-rush-video-blood-to-the-bone-exclusive|website=Billboard.com|access-date=30 April 2015}}</ref> It was Wigmore's third straight chart-topper in New Zealand, and also debuted at #13 at the [[ARIA Charts]], her biggest Australian performance.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.aria.com.au/documents/ARIAChartsmediarelease5September2015.pdf|title=Fresh chart toppers for this week's ARIA Charts: Justin Bieber and The Weeknd both hit the heights on debut!}}</ref> In the United States, ''Blood to Bone'' came out in August,<ref>{{AllMusic | class= album | id= mw0002857867| label= ''Blood to Bone'' | access-date= 30 March 2017}}</ref> and entered the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Alternative Albums]] chart the following month.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/alternative-albums/2015-09-19?chartDate=2009-01-03/en-en/|title=Alternative Music: Top Alternative Albums Chart|website=Billboard.com}}</ref>
Her song "New Rush" was featured on the soundtrack of ''[[FIFA 16]]''. On 6 June 2015, she released a new promotional single entitled "Willing To Die" featuring the American rapper [[Logic (rapper)|Logic]] & the Australian rapper [[Suffa]] of [[The Hilltop Hoods]]. The song samples the [[Alan Lomax]] recording "Rosie", which [[David Guetta]] also sampled on his track "[[Hey Mama (David Guetta song)|Hey Mama]]", released in March 2015.

=== ''Ivory'' ===
On 2 September 2016, Wigmore announced on Facebook she had begun work on her fourth studio album. Later on 3 November, Wigmore dropped "Dirty Mercy", the lead single from her upcoming fourth album, which she had expected to release in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://myspace.com/article/2016/12/5/gin-wigmore-end-of-year-interview|title=Gin Wigmore Looks Back on a Wild 2016|website=Myspace.com}}</ref> Wigmore said that she started working in May, and soon came up with ten new songs, while described the album as "a very eclectic mash of styles as I seem to struggle at settling for one particular genre of music with my albums." Also in November, Wigmore started another North American tour, beginning at the [[Corona Capital]] festival in [[Mexico City]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://style.kiwi/five-minutes-with-gin-wigmore/|title=Interview: Five Minutes with Gin Wigmore|access-date=13 April 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170414162100/http://style.kiwi/five-minutes-with-gin-wigmore/|archive-date=14 April 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://rollingstone.com.mx/g_r_conciertos/gin-wigmore-a-la-deriva/|title=Gin Wigmore a la deriva|website=Rollingstone.com.mx}}</ref> and extending to cities in the United States up until December.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7572715/gin-wigmore-dirty-mercy-babysitting-music-video|title=Gin Wigmore is a Bad Babysitter, Righteous Role Model in 'Dirty Mercy' Video: Exclusive Premiere|website=Billboard.com}}</ref>

In 2017, she was a presenter at [[TEDx]]ScottBase, filmed in [[Scott Base|New Zealand's Antarctic research facility]].<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.tedxscottbase.com/speakers-1/| title=TEDx Scott Base – Speakers|website=Tedxscottbase.com| date=22 January 2017}}</ref> There Wigmore revealed another new song, "Thoughts of You".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebreeze.co.nz/home/music/2017/01/gin-wigmore-debuts-new-song-with-intimate-antarctic-performance.html|title=Gin Wigmore debuts new song with intimate Antarctic performance|website=Thebreeze.co.nz}}</ref>

On 26 June, Wigmore released the album's second single "Hallow Fate". The song was inspired by an artist's individuality in the music industry. Later in the summer she released the promotional single, "Beatnik Trip"

On 16 November, Wigmore released the promotional single "Cabrona", along with the announcement that her fourth album was titled ''[[Ivory (Gin Wigmore album)|Ivory]]''. Released on April 2018, the album was named after Wigmore's son, even if all the songs were written prior to his birth.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/news/article.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=12021735|title=Gin Wigmore on motherhood, forming a Girl Gang, and her 'brighter' new album|author=George Fenwick|date=5 April 2018|website=Nzherald.co.nz}}</ref>

=== ''Others'' ===
In addition, Gin has collaborated with [[Air New Zealand]], with songs such as "[[Tomorrow (song from Annie)|Tomorrow]]" on the "Where to Next" ad campaign; her hit single, "Under My Skin", for [[Air New Zealand#Nothing to hide campaign|"Nothing to Hide"]]; and, most recently, her rendition of [[My Little Corner of the World]] on the "a Fantastical Journey" [[Pre-flight safety demonstration|flight safety video]] in 2017.

==Personal life==
Wigmore was born in Auckland, New Zealand to Peter and Adrienne Wigmore.  She has two older siblings: brother [https://www.oliverwigmore.com/ Oliver Wigmore] and sister [[Lucy Wigmore]], a professional actress known for starring as [[Justine Jones]] in the long-running New Zealand soap opera ''[[Shortland Street]]'' and a role in ''[[Underbelly: Razor]]''. She married [[Jason Aalon Butler|Jason Butler]], ex-lead singer of the band [[Letlive]] and currently lead singer of [[Fever 333]], during a private ceremony in [[Hawaii]] in September 2014.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/letlive-interview-with-jason-aalon-butler-rock-music-was-birthed-in-heresy-and-rebellion-a7020016.html|title=Letlive. interview with Jason Aalon Butler: 'Rock music was birthed in heresy and rebellion'|work=Independent|date=May 2015}}</ref> The couple's first child, Pascal Nashoba Butler, was born on 7 September 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.morefm.co.nz/home/goss/2017/09/kiwi-singer-gin-wigmore-gives-birth-to-her-first-child.html|title=Kiwi singer Gin Wigmore gives birth to her first child|website=Morefm.co.nz}}</ref> Her second son, Izaiah River Butler, was born on 20 March 2020.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/national/programmes/sunday/audio/2018747741/gin-wigmore-i-might-have-bitten-off-more-than-i-can-chew|title=Gin Wigmore: 'I might have bitten off more than I can chew'|publisher=Radio New Zealand|date=24 May 2020}}</ref>

Wigmore is vegetarian and, in 2015, was shortlisted for SAFE's "New Zealand's Hottest Vegetarians" award.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/lifestyle/news/article.cfm?c_id=6&objectid=11521499| title=NZ's hottest vegetarians crowned| publisher=NZ Herald| date=1 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.safe.org.nz/articles/011112/vegetarian-new-black| title=VEGETARIAN is the new black!| website=Safe.org.nz| date=1 November 2012| access-date=10 April 2016| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160423134601/http://www.safe.org.nz/articles/011112/vegetarian-new-black| archive-date=23 April 2016| url-status=dead}}</ref>

Wigmore and her husband reside in [[Palm Springs, California]], where they operate a boutique hotel.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-05-24|title=Gin Wigmore: 'I might have bitten off more than I can chew'|url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/national/programmes/sunday/audio/2018747741/gin-wigmore-i-might-have-bitten-off-more-than-i-can-chew|access-date=2021-09-05|website=RNZ|language=en-nz}}</ref>
