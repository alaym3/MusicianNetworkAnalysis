'''Vaudeville Smash''' are an Australian funk/disco/pop band from [[Coburg, Victoria]], formed in 2009. The band consists of Marc Lucchesi, Dan Lucchesi, Luca Lucchesi, Martin Lubran and James Bowers.<ref name="BM">{{cite web |url=https://www.beat.com.au/vaudeville-smash-3/ |title=Vaudeville Smash |website=Beat Magazine |accessdate=27 January 2021 |author=Maxx, Denver}}</ref>

The group rose to international attention after they collaborated with sports commentator [[Les Murray (broadcaster)|Les Murray]] for a tribute song honoring the French football player [[Zinedine Zidane]].<ref name="HS">{{cite web |url=https://www.heraldsun.com.au/leader/north/what-has-this-melbournemade-music-video-got-that-has-the-whole-world-cup-talking/news-story/d65834da06d05f5be2d86396402d9326.|title=What has this Melbourne-made music video got that has the whole World Cup talking? |work=[[Herald Sun]] |date=4 July 2014 |accessdate=27 January 2021 |author=Heath, Tamara}}</ref> The song was named by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' as one of ten unofficial World Cup 2014 anthems.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin-notas/6121340/top-ten-alternative-2014-world-cup-songs/ |title=Top Ten Alternative 2014 World Cup Songs |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=13 June 2014 |accessdate=27 January 2021 |author=Cantor-Navas, Judy}}</ref>
