'''Windhand''' is an American [[doom metal]] band formed in [[Richmond, Virginia]] in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.windhand.band/|title=WINDHAND|author=|date=|accessdate=December 2, 2015}}</ref> Currently signed to [[Relapse Records]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.relapse.com/windhand/|title=WINDHAND "Grief's Infernal Flower" – OUT NOW|publisher=Relapse Records|date=|accessdate=December 2, 2015}}</ref> Windhand released their most recent album,  ''[[Eternal Return (Windhand album)|Eternal Return]]'', in October 2018.

== History ==
Windhand formed in 2009 with singer [[Dorthia Cottrell]], guitarists Asechiah Bogdan (also a member of [[Alabama Thunderpussy]]) and Garrett Morris, bassist Nathan Hilbish, and drummer Jeff Loucks.<ref name="WindhandPast">{{cite web |title=Windhand: Past Members |url=https://www.metal-archives.com/bands/Windhand/3540313433#band_tab_members |website=Encyclopaedia Metallum |accessdate=October 16, 2018}}</ref>

In 2010 they released a two-song demo titled ''Practice Space Demo''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Windhand – Windhand (Practice Space Demo) [Demo] |url=http://www.metalstorm.net/bands/album.php?album_id=69243&band_id=7670&bandname=Windhand |website=Metal Storm |accessdate=October 16, 2018}}</ref> Loucks was then replaced by former The Might Could drummer Ryan Wolfe.<ref name="WindhandPast" /> The band signed with Forcefield Records and released their [[Windhand (album)|eponymous debut album]] in 2012. Hilbish left the band and was replaced by Parker Chandler.<ref name="WindhandPast" /> Shortly after, the band left Forcefield and signed to [[Relapse Records]], which released their second album ''[[Soma (Windhand album)|Soma]]'' in 2013. The album reached No. 24 on the ''Billboard'' [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart, making it the band's first release to make a ''Billboard'' chart,<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5733483/windhand/chart?f=324 |title=Windhand – Chart history |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=March 6, 2017}}</ref> and was named one of the year's best heavy metal albums by ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/20-best-metal-albums-of-2013-20131211/windhand-soma-19691231 |title=Windhand, 'Soma' &#124; 20 Best Metal Albums of 2013 |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=December 11, 2013 |accessdate=March 6, 2017}}</ref>

In 2013–14 Windhand released the split albums ''[[Reflection of the Negative]]'' with Cough and another with the band Salem's Pot, plus the live album ''[[Live at Roadburn 2014]]''.<ref name="Relapse">{{cite web |title=Biography: Windhand |url=http://label.relapse.com/artist/windhand/ |website=Relapse Records |accessdate=October 16, 2018}}</ref> In 2015, with [[Jack Endino]] as producer, they recorded and released their third album ''[[Grief's Infernal Flower]]''. The album peaked at No. 16 on the Billboard [[Hard Rock Albums]] chart and No. 7 on the Billboard Heatseekers chart,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5733483/windhand/chart?f=795|title=Windhand|author=|date=|work=billboard.com|accessdate=December 2, 2015}}</ref> and was named the 17th best album of 2015 by ''[[Consequence of Sound]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2015/12/top-50-albums-of-2015/4/|title=Top 50 Albums of 2015|author=|date=December 2, 2015|work=Consequence of Sound|accessdate=December 2, 2015}}</ref> Founding guitarist Asechiah Bogdan then left the band and was not replaced.<ref name="WindhandPast" />

In early 2018, Windhand released the split EP ''[[Windhand / Satan's Satyrs]]'' with fellow Virginia band [[Satan's Satyrs]]. This EP reached No. 11 on the ''Billboard'' [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref name = 
"Heatseekers">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/windhand/chart-history/heatseekers-albums/song/1067010|work=[[Billboard.com]]|title=Windhand Chart History|accessdate=July 16, 2018}}</ref> Their fourth full-length album ''[[Eternal Return (Windhand album)|Eternal Return]]'', again with Endino producing, was released in October 2018.<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Kaufman |first1=Spencer |title=Windhand Announce New album, Eternal Return, Unveil "Grey Garden" |magazine=Consequence of Sound |accessdate=October 15, 2018 |date=August 1, 2018|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/08/windhand-grey-garden-video-details-new-album-eternal-return/}}</ref> That album reached No. 3 on the ''Billboard'' [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref name = 
"Heatseekers" />
