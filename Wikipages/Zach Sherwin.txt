'''Zach Sherwin''' (born July 1, 1980) is an American comedian, rapper, writer, and actor best known for writing for and performing in the ''[[Epic Rap Battles of History]]'' [[Web series|YouTube series]], as well as writing for ''[[Crazy Ex-Girlfriend (TV series)|Crazy Ex-Girlfriend]]'' on [[The CW]]. He specializes in [[Comedy hip hop|comedic rap]]. He previously performed with The Late Night Players, and under the stage name '''MC Mr. Napkins'''.

==Early life and education==
Sherwin was born in Cleveland, Ohio and raised in Cincinnati, Ohio and southwest Missouri.<ref name="ethicsnow" /> His mother was a rabbi, and he continues to identify as [[Judaism|Jewish]].<ref name="jj"/>

He attended college at [[Brandeis University]] and graduated in 2002. At Brandeis, Sherwin became an Ethics Center Student Fellow (known as the Sorensen Fellowship since 2009) while interning at Ikamva Labantu in [[Cape Town]], [[South Africa]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.brandeis.edu/ethics/atbrandeis/sorensenfellowship/africainternships.html|title=Internships in Africa|publisher=International Center for Ethics, Justice, and Public Life at [[Brandeis University]]|access-date=October 18, 2016}}</ref><ref name="ethicsnow">{{cite web|url=http://www.brandeis.edu/ethics/atbrandeis/ecsf/now.html|title=Where Are They Now?|publisher=International Center for Ethics, Justice, and Public Life at [[Brandeis University]]|access-date=October 18, 2016}}</ref> While in college, Sherwin started a sketch comedy group, The Late Night Players. His fellow members included Andrew Slack and Seth Reibstein, founders of the [[Harry Potter Alliance]], and Aaron Kagan.<ref>{{cite web|title=URI Hillel to bring Late Night Players comedy troupe to campus|url=http://today.uri.edu/news/uri-hillel-to-bring-late-night-players-comedy-troupe-to-campus/|publisher=[[University of Rhode Island]]|date=September 14, 2006|access-date=October 28, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
Sherwin continued to perform with The Late Night Players after graduating from Brandeis until the group's dissolution in 2007. The group was the subject of a documentary directed by Joshua Goren which screened at the November 2006 New York International Independent Film and Video Festival.<ref>{{cite web|website=New York International Independent Film and Video Festival|url=http://www.nyfilmvideo.com/2006/nynov2006/schedule/ny-nov-2006-schedule.html|title=3 Screens Schedule NY November 2006|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100809112932/http://www.nyfilmvideo.com/2006/nynov2006/schedule/ny-nov-2006-schedule.html|archive-date=August 9, 2010|access-date=October 18, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Sherwin began to perform solo as MC Mr. Napkins in Boston from 2007 until giving up the name completely in 2013 in a video posted to his YouTube channel.<ref>{{cite video|title=Stage Name|date=July 1, 2013|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6hLmjGwbENE|people=Zach Sherwin (writer and performer)|access-date=October 18, 2016}}</ref> As a solo comedian, Sherwin has performed multiple times at [[Knitting Factory|The Knitting Factory]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://comedyattheknittingfactory.tumblr.com/post/4975605254/this-sunday-night-5111-comedy-at-the-knitting|title=This Sunday Night 5/1/11 @ Comedy At The Knitting Factory|publisher=[[Knitting Factory|The Knitting Factory]]|date=April 26, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://comedyattheknittingfactory.tumblr.com/post/42596930554/this-sunday-night|title=This Sunday Night|publisher=[[Knitting Factory|The Knitting Factory]]|date=February 8, 2013|access-date=October 28, 2016}}</ref> He has appeared in multiple shows at [[Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre|UCB]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://east.ucbtheatre.com/show/3371|title=Undefined - A Variety Show|publisher=[[Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre|Upright Citizens Brigade]]|access-date=October 28, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://east.ucbtheatre.com/performance/26093|title=If You Build It: A Stand-Up Comedy Show|publisher=[[Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre|Upright Citizens Brigade]]|access-date=October 28, 2016}}</ref> Sherwin appeared on the debut episode of [[Cameron Esposito]]'s [[ASpecialThing Records|ASpecialThing]]-produced stand-up comedy podcast ''Put Your Hands Together''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laweekly.com/arts/put-your-hands-together-debuts-tomorrow-filling-the-spot-of-beloved-ucb-stand-up-show-comedy-bang-bang-4183495|title=Put Your Hands Together Debuts Tomorrow, Filling the Spot of Beloved UCB Stand-up Show Comedy Bang! Bang!|author=Julie Seabaugh|publisher=[[LA Weekly]]|date=January 7, 2013|access-date=October 28, 2016}}</ref>

Sherwin was one of the original performers involved with the [[Epic Rap Battles of History]] YouTube series. As of December 2016, he has appeared in ten episodes, having portrayed [[Albert Einstein]], [[Emmett Brown]], [[Sherlock Holmes]], [[Ebenezer Scrooge]], [[Stephen King]], [[Wayne Gretzky]], [[Egon Spengler]], [[Voltaire]], [[Walt Disney]], and [[Alexander the Great]]. Along with co-creators [[Peter Shukoff]] and [[Lloyd Ahlquist]], Sherwin has acted in, and written for, episodes in every season of the series. In 2014, [[Epic Rap Battles of History]] won a [[Streamy Awards|Streamy Award]] for Best Writing. In 2015, the series was nominated for the same award. In 2016, the series was nominated for Streamy Awards for Best Collaboration and Best Writing.

Since 2015, Zach Sherwin has written for the [[The CW|CW]] series [[Crazy Ex-Girlfriend (TV series)|Crazy Ex-Girlfriend]]. He co-wrote the songs "JAP Battle", "JAP Battle (Reprise)", "I Give Good Parent", and "So Maternal".<ref>{{cite web|title='Crazy Ex-Girlfriend' Brings You the Best Jewish Rap Battle Ever|url=http://forward.com/the-assimilator/334657/crazy-ex-girlfriend-brings-you-the-best-jewish-rap-battle-ever/|author=Lior Zaltzman|date=March 1, 2016|access-date=October 28, 2016|publisher=[[The Forward]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Rachel Bloom Tells the Stories Behind 8 Crazy Ex-Girlfriend Songs|url=http://www.vulture.com/2016/04/crazy-ex-girlfriend-songs-backstories.html|author=Devon Ivie|date=April 15, 2016|access-date=October 28, 2016|website=[[Vulture.com]]|publisher=[[New York (magazine)|New York Magazine]]}}</ref> The first season of the series was nominated for multiple awards and holds a 96% positive rating on [[Rotten Tomatoes]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.rottentomatoes.com/tv/crazy-ex-girlfriend/s01/ |title= Crazy Ex-girlfriend: Season 1 (2015) |publisher=[[Rotten Tomatoes]] |access-date=October 10, 2015}}</ref>

Sherwin auditioned for [[America's Got Talent (season 11)|Season 11]] of [[America's Got Talent]] in 2016. His performance in the Judge Cuts round was televised, however he did not proceed to the Quarterfinals round.

Sherwin writes, produces, and hosts a series of live [[Crossword|crossword puzzle]] comedy shows entitled "The Crossword Show", in which three panelists race to solve a crossword puzzle on stage. The shows include comedy raps, videos, and humorous facts prepared for each clue. The format is similar to [[NPR]]'s [[Wait Wait... Don't Tell Me!]].<ref name="jj">{{cite interview|last=Sherwin|first=Zach|url=https://jewishjournal.com/culture/just-asking/302402/zach-sherwins-live-jew-ish-crossword-rap-shows/|interviewer=Esther D. Kustanowitz|title=Zach Sherwin’s Live Jew-ish Crossword/Rap Shows|date=July 31, 2019|access-date=October 24, 2019|work=[[The Jewish Journal of Greater Los Angeles|The Jewish Journal]]|publisher=TRIBE Media Corp.}}</ref> Notable guest solvers have included [[Mayim Bialik]], [[Pete Holmes]], [[Rachel Bloom]], [[Lisa Loeb]], [[Josh Gondelman]], and [[Aparna Nancherla]].

==Personal life==
Sherwin lives in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]]. He is a [[vegan]].
