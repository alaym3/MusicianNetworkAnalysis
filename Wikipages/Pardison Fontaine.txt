'''Jorden Kyle Lanier Thorpe'''<ref>{{cite web |url=https://archive.org/details/bmi-songview-search-jorden-kyle-lanier-thorpe-feb-2021 |title=BMI Songview Search Jorden Kyle Lanier Thorpe |date=February 25, 2021 |website=repertoire.bmi.com |publisher=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]] |access-date=February 25, 2021}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Pardison Fontaine''', is an American rapper and songwriter originally from [[Newburgh, New York]]. He is best known for his 2018 single "[[Backin' It Up]]" featuring [[Cardi B]], which peaked at number 40 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His debut album ''Under8ed'' was released November 15, 2019. Along with his solo career, Thorpe has credits as co-writer in multiple top 20 hits with other artists, including [[Kanye West]] ("[[Yikes (Kanye West song)|Yikes]]", [[Ghost Town (Kanye West song)|Ghost Town]]" and "[[All Mine (Kanye West song)|All Mine]]"), Cardi B ("[[Bodak Yellow]]", "[[I Like It (Cardi B, Bad Bunny and J Balvin song)|I Like It]]", "[[Wild Side (Normani song)|Wild Side]]" and "[[Up (Cardi B song)|Up]]"), and [[Megan Thee Stallion]] ("[[Savage Remix]]" and "[[WAP (song)|WAP]]").<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/pardison-fontaine-and-the-plight-of-the-ghostwriter |title=Pardison Fontaine and the Plight of the Ghostwriter |date=November 29, 2019 |access-date=July 16, 2021 |archive-date=April 5, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210405194430/https://www.newyorker.com/culture/culture-desk/pardison-fontaine-and-the-plight-of-the-ghostwriter |url-status=live }} {{Bare URL inline|date=September 2021}}</ref> In 2021, Thorpe won the [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Song]] for his contribution on "Savage Remix".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jordan Thorpe|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/jordan-thorpe/188756|url-status=live|website=Grammys|date=April 22, 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
In August 2015, Fontaine released his debut mixtape, ''Not Supposed to Be Here''.<ref name="NSTBH" /> During 2017-2018, he worked with rapper [[Cardi B]]. In 2018, Fontaine co-wrote songs for rapper [[Kanye West]]'s eighth studio album, ''[[Ye (album)|Ye]]'', such as [[Violent Crimes (song)|Violent Crimes]]". In August 2018, he released the single "Say What I Want".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://karencivil.com/2018/08/24/pardison-fontaine-drops-rap-charlamange-say-what-i-want/|title=Pardison Fontaine Drops "Rap Charlamange (Say What I Want)"|date=2018-08-24|website=[[Karen Civil|]]|access-date=2019-11-28|archive-date=November 28, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191128124049/http://karencivil.com/2018/08/24/pardison-fontaine-drops-rap-charlamange-say-what-i-want/|url-status=live}}</ref> In September 2018, he released the single "[[Backin' It Up]]" featuring Cardi B, which reached number 40 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/cardi-b-shares-old-clip-of-her-and-pardison-fontaine-turning-up-before-the-fame-news.60221.html|title=Cardi B Shares Old Clip Of Her & Pardison Fontaine Turning Up Before The Fame|work=HotNewHipHop|date=September 20, 2018|accessdate=October 10, 2018|archive-date=September 20, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180920134936/https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/cardi-b-shares-old-clip-of-her-and-pardison-fontaine-turning-up-before-the-fame-news.60221.html|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="Hot100" /> They performed the song live for the first time at the [[2018 BET Hip Hop Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8480326/cardi-b-get-up-10-performance-2018-bet-hip-hop-awards|title=Watch Cardi B Perform 'Get Up|access-date=October 17, 2018|archive-date=October 17, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181017082200/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8480326/cardi-b-get-up-10-performance-2018-bet-hip-hop-awards|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Pardison Fontaine has a daughter. In February 2021 it was confirmed that he is dating [[Megan Thee Stallion]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Megan Thee Stallion Confirms She's Dating Pardison Fontaine: 'That's My Boo'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9528996/megan-thee-stallion-dating-pardison-fontaine/|access-date=July 18, 2021|magazine=Billboard|archive-date=July 19, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210719040916/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9528996/megan-thee-stallion-dating-pardison-fontaine/|url-status=live}}</ref>
