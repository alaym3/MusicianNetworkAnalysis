'''James Michael Bay'''<ref name=BMIDATA>{{cite web|url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/Catalog.aspx?detail=writerid&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&keyid=1349658&subid=0|title=Songwriter/Composer: BAY JAMES MICHAEL|last=Bay|first=James Michael|publisher=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]] (BMI)|access-date=4 July 2017}}</ref> (born 4 September 1990) is an English singer-songwriter and guitarist.<ref>{{cite web|title=New Music – Kimberley Anne, James Bay, Little Bear|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/blogs/ontherecord/2012/11/29/new-music-kimberly-anne-james-bay-little-bear/|website=The Irish Times|access-date=28 August 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=BBC Introducing – On the playlist: James Bay|date=19 August 2014|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/introducing/posts/On-the-playlist-James-Bay-Let-It-Go|publisher=BBC|access-date=28 August 2014}}</ref><ref name="Young">{{cite web|url=http://www.thecomet.net/what-s-on/hitchin_guitarist_one_to_watch_says_emi_boss_1_348547|title=Hitchin guitarist 'One to watch' says EMI boss|last=Young|first=Richard|date=28 May 2010|work=The Comet|publisher=Archant Community Media|access-date=2 September 2014}}</ref> In 2014, he released his single "[[Hold Back the River (James Bay song)|Hold Back the River]]", which was certified platinum, before releasing his debut studio album ''[[Chaos and the Calm]]'' (2014). The album went to number one in the UK and number 15 in the US. In February 2015, Bay received the [[Brit Awards]] "Critics' Choice" award.<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-30839967 "Brit Awards 2015: Winners list"]. BBC. Retrieved 26 February 2015</ref> At the [[2016 Brit Awards]] he received the award for [[Brit Award for British Male Solo Artist|Best British Male Solo Artist]].<ref name="2016 Brits">{{cite news|title=Brit Awards 2016: Adele dominates with four awards|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-35656227|publisher=BBC|date=25 February 2016}}</ref> Bay also received three nominations at the [[2016 Grammy Awards]], for [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Album|Best Rock Album]], and [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Song|Best Rock Song]]. In May 2018, he released his second studio album, ''[[Electric Light (album)|Electric Light]]''.

==Early life==
James Bay was born to Jill, a former fashion illustrator for [[Marie Claire]], and Nick Bay, a wine merchant.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/features/6738196/british-singer-james-bay-conquering-america-classic-rock|title = British Singer James Bay is Ready to Conquer 'the Biggest Prize of All' -- America}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/james-bay-interview-solo-artist-guitar-i-can-only-write-so-many-weepie-little-bedroom-songs-10121453.html?amp|title = Can James Bay prove he's not just another middle-of-the-road, boring boy with a guitar?|date = 20 March 2015}}</ref> He has an older brother named Alex.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/james-bay-what-i-know-about-women-20180712-p4zqz6.html|title = James Bay: What I know about women|date = 14 July 2018}}</ref> Bay's parents brought him up in [[Hitchin]], Hertfordshire where he attended [[Hitchin Boys' School]]. Aged 11, Bay was inspired to play classical guitar after hearing [[Eric Clapton]]'s "[[Layla]]".<ref name="Telegraph"/> He used an old rusty guitar with five strings he found in a cupboard in his house.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.irishmirror.ie/whats-on/music/modest-james-bay-not-carried-6605055 |title=Modest James Bay not carried away with his quick-fire success |author=Maeve Quigley |date=9 October 2015 |work=irishmirror |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151031135132/http://www.irishmirror.ie/whats-on/music/modest-james-bay-not-carried-6605055 |archive-date=31 October 2015 }}</ref> Bay is a supporter of [[Newcastle United F.C.]]<ref>{{cite news|last=Mitchinson|first=Rory|url=https://www.nufc.co.uk/news/latest-news/the-atmosphere-was-incredible-in-there-james-bay-on-uniteds-win-at-spurs/|title='The atmosphere was incredible in there' - James Bay on United's win at Spurs|date=2 September 2019|website=Newcastle United F.C.|access-date=30 January 2021}}</ref>

When he moved to study in [[Brighton]] at the [[British and Irish Modern Music Institute]] seven years later at age 18 he played the city's open mic nights, on which he said, "That taught me a lot about writing and performing on my own and trying to hold my own". "I'm trying to make songs that make people feel something, and if I'm lucky, even move them".

Bay caught the attention of [[Republic Records]] A&R after a fan uploaded a video to YouTube of him performing at an open mic in London, and within a week, he signed to the label. His first EP, ''The Dark of the Morning'', was released in 2013. Within a year, he was selling out his first UK headline tour.<ref name="Telegraph">{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/new-music/11271254/James-Bay-Brits-Critics-Choice-2015.html|title=Who is James Bay?|date=4 December 2014|access-date=18 April 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
===2013–2017: Career beginnings and ''Chaos and the Calm''===
Bay released his debut [[extended play]], ''The Dark of the Morning'', on 18 July 2013. His second EP, ''[[Let It Go (James Bay song)|Let It Go]]'', was released on 12 May 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=A Name to Remember: James Bay is a voice that you just won't forget |url=http://www.her.ie/entertainment/a-name-to-remember-james-bay-is-one-voice-that-you-just-wont-forget/ |website=Her |access-date=28 August 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140911203109/http://www.her.ie/entertainment/a-name-to-remember-james-bay-is-one-voice-that-you-just-wont-forget/ |archive-date=11 September 2014 }}</ref> The ''Let It Go'' EP debuted in the top 10 iTunes album chart<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.music-news.com/news/UK/84021/Read|title=James Bay premieres new single 'Hold Back The River'|website=Music-News.com}}</ref> and the lead single, titled "[[Let It Go (James Bay song)|Let It Go]]", peaked at number 10 in the UK charts.<ref>{{cite web|title=James Bay, artist|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/_/james%20bay/|publisher=Official Charts Company|access-date=8 October 2014}}</ref> Bay has performed live on the [[Burberry]] runway and has also recorded a Burberry Acoustic session.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bayley|first1=Leanne|title=James Bay sang at Burberry SS15 show – but who is he?|url=http://www.glamourmagazine.co.uk/news/celebrity/2014/09/15/who-is-james-bay-burberry-fashion-show-introduction-singer|website=Glamour Magazine UK|access-date=14 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150106100711/http://www.glamourmagazine.co.uk/news/celebrity/2014/09/15/who-is-james-bay-burberry-fashion-show-introduction-singer#|archive-date=6 January 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> He toured with [[Hozier (musician)|Hozier]] in 2014.<ref name="soundofboston.com">{{cite web|last1=Bedian|first1=Knar|title=Painted Faces and Fantastic Pipes: James Bay|url=http://soundofboston.com/painted-faces-fantastic-pipes-james-bay/|website=Sound of Boston|date=14 November 2014|access-date=14 November 2014}}</ref>

Bay recorded his entire debut studio album ''[[Chaos and the Calm]]'' at Blackbird Studios in [[Nashville]], with producer [[Jacquire King]].<ref name="nme.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/various-artists/81680|title=James Bay announces debut album|work=[[NME]]|date=11 December 2014}}</ref> It debuted at number one in the UK charts and has been certified X2 platinum.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bpi.co.uk/award/13205-4002-2# |title=Archived copy |access-date=14 March 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180315133328/https://www.bpi.co.uk/award/13205-4002-2# |archive-date=15 March 2018 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> His single "[[Hold Back the River (James Bay song)|Hold Back the River]]" peaked at number 2 in the UK charts and was certified X2 platinum.<ref>[http://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/20150329/7501/ "Official Singles Chart Top 100 (29 March 2015 – 4 April 2015)]. Official Charts. Retrieved 21 July 2015</ref>
[[File:James Bay 2016 (9 von 12).jpg|260px|thumb|upright|Bay performing in 2016]]
In January 2015, Bay released his second [[Extended play|EP]], ''Other Sides.'' He performed on the Pyramid Stage at [[Glastonbury Festival]] in June 2015.<ref name="telegraph.co.uk">{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/glastonbury/11697251/James-Bay-review.html|title=James Bay, Glastonbury, review: 'One Direction didn't steal the show'|work=[[The Telegraph (U.K.)|The Telegraph]]|date=26 June 2015}}</ref> At the [[2016 Brit Awards]] in London on 24 February, he received the award for Best British Male Solo Artist, and also performed "[[Love Yourself (Justin Bieber song)|Love Yourself]]" with [[Justin Bieber]] at the ceremony.<ref name="2016 Brits"/>  Bay recorded a cover of the [[Beatles]] song "[[Hey Bulldog]]" for the 2016 TV series ''[[Beat Bugs]]''. He recorded a cover of the [[Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers|Tom Petty]] song "King's Highway" for the 2017 [[Pixar|Disney/Pixar]] film ''[[Cars 3]]''. It is heard in the film and is included on the official soundtrack album.

===2018–present: ''Electric Light'' and upcoming third studio album ===
Bay released his second studio album, ''[[Electric Light (album)|Electric Light]]'', on 18 May 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/electric-light/1354016490|title=Electric Light by James Bay|via=itunes.apple.com}}</ref> It peaked at number 2 on the UK Albums Chart and 21 on the US Billboard 200.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/james-bay|title=James Bay Chart History|website=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/albums-chart/20180525/7502/|title=Official Albums Chart Top 100 &#124; Official Charts Company|website=www.officialcharts.com}}</ref> The album has notable pop and R&B influences,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://inews.co.uk/culture/james-bay-interview-new-album-electric-light-wild-love-review-image/|title=James Bay interview: 'Do I really want to wander around the world with the hat and the long hair for another four years? No'|first=Nick|last=Duerden|date=8 March 2018|website=inews.co.uk}}</ref> and was noted for its "hip hip style spoken-word skits" during intervals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/james-bay-electric-light-review|title=James Bay – 'Electric Light' album review|first=Jordan|last=Bassett|website=NME|date=18 May 2018}}</ref> The lead single, "[[Wild Love (James Bay song)|Wild Love]]", was released on 8 February 2018, and peaked at 39 on the UK Singles Chart and 17 on the US Adults Top 40. Bay released a duet version of the album's third single, "[[Us (James Bay song)|Us]]", performed on ''[[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|The Voice]]'' with [[Alicia Keys]] in May 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ss-tNM2NXd0|title=James Bay, Alicia Keys - Us (The Voice 2018)|date=23 May 2018|via=YouTube}}</ref>
Bay released a single, "[[Peer Pressure (James Bay song)|Peer Pressure]]", on February 22, 2019, featuring artist [[Julia Michaels]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Gc8EPDTF4Ho|title=James Bay - Peer Pressure (Audio) ft. Julia Michaels|date=21 February 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> On 10 May 2019, he released his third [[Extended play|EP]], ''Oh My Messy Mind,'' the first record of his to be immediately released on [[streaming]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Moss |first1=Rich |title=Atwood Magazine |url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/jbmm-james-bay-oh-my-messy-mind-ep/ |website=JAMES BAY’S BEAUTIFULLY HEARTBREAKING ‘OH MY MESSY MIND’ EP|date=13 May 2019 }}</ref>  On the same day, the single "[[Bad (James Bay song)|Bad]]" was released.

In the spring of 2020, Bay began making his third studio album in Nashville at the historic [[RCA Studio A]] alongside producer [[Dave Cobb]], and collaborated with Brandon Flowers of [[the Killers]] during recording.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/music-interviews/james-bay-chew-on-my-heart-new-album-brandon-flowers-tyler-creator-2703290|title=James Bay on new single 'Chew On My Heart'|date=9 July 2020|via=NME}}</ref> He released the single "Chew On My Heart" in July 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pipa |first1=Alexa |title=Song You Need to Know: James Bay, 'Chew On My Heart' |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/james-bay-chew-on-my-heart-1028347/ |website=Rolling Stone|date=14 July 2020 }}</ref> Later that month, he streamed a live performance in support of the #SaveOurVenues campaign, to support efforts against the economic threat posed to grassroots venues during the [[COVID-19]] pandemic.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Moore |first1=Sam |title=Music Venue Trust launches 'Save Our Venues' campaign |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/music-venue-trust-launches-save-our-venues-campaign-2654484 |website=NME |date=27 April 2020 |access-date=13 November 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=In support of the #SaveOurVenues campaign, I'm going to be performing a live set online for you this Thursday! |website=Instagram }}</ref>

==Gear==
Bay endorses and plays [[Tone King]] amplifiers, a boutique amplifier brand of [[Premier Builders Guild]].<ref name="guitar world.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.guitarworld.com/features-news-interviews/chaos-and-calm-james-bay-talks-songwriting-opening-stones-and-more/%0924583|title='Chaos and the Calm': James Bay Talks Songwriting, Opening for the Stones and More |work=[[Guitar World]]|date=12 June 2015}}</ref>
In 2017, he released his Epiphone Signature "1966" Century Outfit, an accurate recreation of his customized 1966 Epiphone Century guitar which has James Bay's "Hat Man" logo on the headstock and custom strap.<ref name="Epiphone.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.epiphone.com/Products/Electrics/Archtop/Ltd-Ed-James-Bay-Signature-1966-Century-Outfit.aspx|title=James Bay Epiphone Century |work=[[Epiphone]]|date=30 June 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Bay has been in a relationship with Lucy Smith, a music promoter, since he was 17 years old.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/amp/entertainment-arts-53363675|title = James Bay on the 'fantastic drama' of fame|work = BBC News|date = 11 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://amp.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/james-bay-what-i-know-about-women-20180712-p4zqz6.html|title = James Bay: What I know about women}}</ref> The couple have a daughter, born in October 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Slater |first1=Georgia |title=James Bay Welcomes First Baby, Daughter Ada Violet with Partner Lucy Smith: 'We Are So in Love' |url=https://people.com/parents/james-bay-welcomes-first-baby-daughter-ada-violet/ |website=People |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref>
