'''Tomas Henry Gaynor''' (born 21 February 1991<ref>{{cite web |url=https://music.apple.com/us/artist/allday/635350916/ |title=Allday on Apple Music |publisher=[[Apple Music]] |access-date=28 April 2021}}</ref>), known professionally as '''Allday''', is an Australian rapper, singer and songwriter from [[Adelaide]], [[South Australia]].<ref name="SMH">{{cite web |url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/where-did-allday-come-from-20140926-10lrzq.html |title=Where did Allday come from? |work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]] |date=25 September 2014 |access-date=25 June 2018 |author=Bacon, Erika}}</ref> Gaynor became active in 2011, when he began uploading his music for free online. Following his works becoming increasingly popular, he moved to [[Melbourne]], Victoria, to pursue a serious career before later relocating to [[Los Angeles]], California, in February 2017.<ref name="Ripitup">{{cite web |url=http://www.ripitup.com.au/music/article/allday-im-not-trying-to-bring-anyone-down#.VIcRyWSsUrM |title=Allday: "I'm not trying to bring anyone down" |work=Rip It Up |date=3 July 2014 |access-date=29 May 2021 |author=Byzantine, Jimmy |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141217184912/http://www.ripitup.com.au/music/article/allday-im-not-trying-to-bring-anyone-down#.VIcRyWSsUrM |archive-date=17 December 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref> His works have been influenced by acts such as [[Silverchair]], [[Frank Ocean]], and [[TLC (group)|TLC]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/artist/allday/ |title=Allday – Triple J Unearthed |publisher=[[Triple J Unearthed]] |access-date=29 May 2021 |archive-url= |archive-date= |url-status=live}}</ref> Gaynor released his debut album, ''[[Startup Cult]]'', in July 2014 to Australian audiences. ''Startup Cult'' achieved success and reached number three on the [[ARIA Charts]].

==Life and career==
Tom's mother is from England. He previously attended [[Scotch College, Adelaide|Scotch College]] in Adelaide.{{citation needed|date=July 2019}}

===2011–2013: Early life and career origins and mixtapes===
Tom previously played in a harmonica band called Sissycunt at school, which generated his interest in music.<ref name="SMH" /> He began producing works under the name Allday in January 2011, after dropping out of art school. In 2011 Tom said "I basically grew up in the hip hop scene; I would have been about eleven years old when I went to my first show. I've done the shaved head and the air max thing when I was younger."<ref name="NousYesue"/> 

In September 2011, Allday released his free for download debut mixtape ''Noue Yesue'' (pronounced "No-way Yes-Way")<ref name="NousYesue"/> Followed by ''Soon I'll Be in Cali''.<ref name="Cali1"/>

In 2011, Tom was a runner-up [[Raw Comedy Award|Raw Comedy]] national final.<ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p3uPjiQsgWY|title=Tom Gaynor - RAW Comedy 2011|date=3 December 2014|access-date=12 April 2015}}</ref> 

On 3 May 2012, Allday released ''A Skateboard Soirée'' for free on his [[Bandcamp]].<ref name="ASSEP">{{cite web|url=https://alldaytunes.bandcamp.com/album/a-skateboard-soiree-e-p |title=ASS EP|website=AlldayTunes|date=3 May 2012|access-date=28 May 2021}}></ref> ''A Skateboard Soirée'' received nationwide airplay on [[Triple J]] and earned him & producer C1 "Unearthed Artist of the Week" honours.<ref name="Euphoria"/> The single "So Good" was released, and peaked at number 91 on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2012]].<ref name="Cali2">{{Cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/allday-releases-new-mixtape-and-announces-australian-tour-dates-september-october-2013/|title= Allday Drops ''Soon I'll Be in Cali 2'' Mixtape Alongside New Tour Dates|website=MusicFeeds|date=5 August 2013|access-date=28 May 2021}}</ref>

In December 2012 Allday released his fourth free for download mixtape, ''Euphoria''. The mixtape features production from C1, Soul Marauder, Savo and Woodz from Sweden.<ref name="Euphoria">{{cite web|url=https://allaussiehiphop.com/2012/12/11/allday-euphoria/|title=Allday Euphoria|website=All Aussie Hip Hop|date=11 December 2012|access-date=28 May 2021}}</ref> 

In April 2013, Allday released his debut EP, ''[[Loners Are Cool]]'', which peaked at number 18 on the ARIA Charts.<ref name="auschart"/> In July 2013, Allday released his fifth free for download mixtape, ''Soon I'll Be in Cali 2''.<ref name="Cali2"/>

===2014–2015: OneTwo Records, ''Loners Are Cool'' and ''Startup Cult''===
In December 2013, Allday signed with OneTwo Records and released "Claude Monet".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/allday-releases-new-single-claude-monet/|title= Allday Releases New Single "Claude Monet"|website=Music Feeds|date=December 2013|access-date=28 May 2021}}</ref>

He went on a national tour of Australia in May and June 2014. Labelled the Right Now National Tour, he held concerts in Western Australia, South Australia, Victoria, New South Wales and Queensland. His debut album to be released in-stores nationally was ''Startup Cult'' in July 2014, which a week after its release reached number 3 on the ARIA Albums Chart.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url = http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/music/rapper-allday-out-to-end-boredom-of-hiphop-shows-with-startup-cult-tour/story-e6frfn29-1226999101691|title = Rapper Allday out to end 'boredom' of hip-hop shows with Startup Cult tour|date = 23 July 2014|website = news.com.au|publisher = News Corp|last = McCabe|first = Kathy}}</ref> It was supported by a headlining tour around Australia.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.ripitup.com.au/music/article/allday-announces-startup-cult-tour#.VIcO8mSsUrM|title = Allday announces 'Startup Cult' Tour|date = 14 July 2014|website = ripitup.co,.au|publisher = Rip It Up|last = Frangos|first = Daniela|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20141217184316/http://www.ripitup.com.au/music/article/allday-announces-startup-cult-tour#.VIcO8mSsUrM|archive-date = 17 December 2014|url-status = dead|df = dmy-all}}</ref> In December 2014 he released the single "Wolves" featuring Sunni Colón.<ref>Newsitem 'Allday Wolves feat. Sunni Colón (Ladies' Sun Hat Edition)' on ''allaussiehiphop.com'', 1 December 2014.</ref> He featured on Tasmanian singer [[Asta (musician)|Asta]]'s song "Dynamite" in 2015.

In July 2015, Allday released another mixtape titled ''Soft Grunge Love Rap''. Allday said "This is a mixtape I made really quick because my album is taking forever and I wanted to put some new songs out." A single "Grammy" was released.<ref name="SGLR">{{cite web|url=https://www.scenezine.com.au/allday-releases-new-mixtape-called-soft-grunge-love-rap/|title= Allday releases new mixtape called ''Soft Grunge Love Rap''|website=scenezine|date=21 July 2015|access-date=28 May 2021}}</ref>

In 2015, he was featured on the [[Troye Sivan]] song "For Him."<!-- Note: The period is part of the song title. --> on his album ''[[Blue Neighbourhood]]'', which was released 4 December 2015.

===2016–2017: ''Speeding''===
On 23 June 2016—after a delay from his new label, Wind Up Records—Allday released "Monster Truck", which was produced by [[Bill Nye|Bill Nye the Science Guy]], one of his frequent collaborators.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QN3-LiYMIis</ref> On 15 July 2016 he released the lead single from his upcoming second album, the single was premiered on Triple J and is called "Sides" (featuring Nyne). He later released the single "Send Nudes" on 24 August 2016 which hit the top ten on the iTunes hip-hop charts. He released his fourth single of 2016 on 15 December, titled "Raceway".
In March, Allday announced his second studio album, titled ''[[Speeding (album)|Speeding]]'', and released a third single "[[In Motion (Allday song)|In Motion]]", with fellow Australian producer [[Japanese Wallpaper]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7743573/aussie-cult-rapper-allday-stateside-breakthrough-new-album-interview|title=Aussie Cult Rapper Allday Talks Stateside Breakthrough & Forthcoming Sophomore LP}}</ref> The album was released on April 21, 2017. He toured his album ''Speeding'' around [[Regional Australia]] with Arno Faraji and Kinder as supports.

===2018–2019: ''Starry Night Over the Phone''===
In November 2018, Allday released "[[Wonder Drug]]", the lead single from his third studio album.<ref name="WD"/> The album, titled ''[[Starry Night Over the Phone]]'', was released on 12 July 2019, peaked at #7 in Australia; becoming his third top ten album. Allday mentioned in an interview with ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine that he got jumped for his phone one starry night, and this is why the album was so personal to him.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Allday|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Allday/chart-history/ALP/song/1154709|access-date=21 August 2020 |magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

===2020–present: ''Drinking with My Smoking Friends''===
In March 2021, Allday announced the release of his fourth studio album ''[[Drinking with My Smoking Friends]]'', released on 28 May 2021. The album peaked at number 13 on the ARIA Charts. 

In November 2021, Allday returned with the standalone single, "Eyes on the Prize".<ref name="EyesPrize">{{cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/allday-eyes-on-the-prize/|title=Listen To Allday's Breezy, Triumphant New Single "Eyes on the Prize"|website=musicfeeds.com.au|date=19 November 2021|access-date=20 November 2021}}</ref>

The Fowler's Live Music Awards took place from 2012 to 2014 to "recognise success and achievement over the past 12 months [and] celebrate the great diversity of original live music" in South Australia.
