'''Lavell William Crump ''' (born April 11, 1974), known professionally as '''David Banner''', is an American rapper, record producer, activist, magician, and actor.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://davidbanner.com/db/biography/ |title=Biography |publisher=davidbanner.com |access-date=2018-09-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180919132259/https://davidbanner.com/db/biography/ |archive-date=2018-09-19 }}</ref>

Born in [[Brookhaven, Mississippi]], Banner's family moved to [[Jackson, Mississippi]] where he was raised. Banner graduated from [[Southern University]] and pursued a masters of education at the [[University of Maryland Eastern Shore]]. He started his music career as a member of the rap duo, [[Crooked Lettaz]], before going solo in 2000 with the release titled ''[[Them Firewater Boyz, Vol. 1]]''.

In 2003, Banner signed to [[Universal Records]] releasing four albums: ''[[Mississippi: The Album]]'' (2003), ''[[MTA2: Baptized in Dirty Water]]'' (2003), ''[[Certified (David Banner album)|Certified]]'' (2005), and ''[[The Greatest Story Ever Told (David Banner album)|The Greatest Story Ever Told]]'' (2008).

Banner is also a noted [[record producer|producer]], having produced music for himself, [[Trick Daddy]], [[T.I.]], [[Lil Boosie]] and [[Lil Wayne]] among others.

==Music career==

===1995–2000: Beginnings and ''Them Firewater Boyz, Vol. 1''===
Crump's stage name "David Banner" is taken from the lead character of the television series, ''[[The Incredible Hulk (1978 TV series)|The Incredible Hulk]]''.<ref name="VH1 interview">{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/artists/interview/1474718/20030724/banner_david.jhtml|title=David Banner: Mississippi Burning|last=Bottomley|first=C|date=July 24, 2003|publisher=VH1|access-date=December 28, 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071001130531/http://www.vh1.com/artists/interview/1474718/20030724/banner_david.jhtml|archive-date=October 1, 2007|url-status=dead}}</ref> With several of his friends, Banner sent some demo tapes to Jackson, Mississippi radio station, [[WJMI]]. In 1999, Banner and rapper Kamikaze as the duo, Crooked Lettaz, released ''Grey Skies'' ([[Penalty Recordings]]).<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p366511/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Crooked Lettaz: Biography|last=DaRonco|first=Mike|publisher=allmusic|access-date=December 28, 2009}}</ref> In 2000, Banner released his solo debut album, ''Them Firewater Boyz, Vol. 1''. Released on the independent label, Big Face Records, the album sold around 7,000 copies.<ref name="XXL">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=12526|title=David Banner: Can't Tell Me Nothin'|date=July 30, 2007|work=XXL|access-date=December 28, 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090410174944/http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=12526|archive-date=April 10, 2009}}</ref>

===2003&ndash;06: ''Mississippi: The Album,'' ''MTA2: Baptized in Dirty Water'' and ''Certified''===
After assessing various offers, Banner and manager, Scott Johnson, decided to sign with [[Universal Records]] subsidiary, [[SRC Records]], which was founded by [[Steve Rifkind]] who had previous success as CEO of the heavyweight hip-hop label, [[Loud Records]].<ref name="hitquarters.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/opar/intrview_ScottJohnson_int.html |title=Interview with Scott Johnson|publisher=[[HitQuarters]]|date=10 Mar 2004 |access-date=21 Dec 2010}}</ref> In 2003, Banner produced [[T.I.]]'s single, "[[Rubberband Man]]", which reached #30 on the Hot 100, #15 on the R&B chart, and #11 on the Rap chart. In 2003, Banner released his first major label album, ''[[Mississippi: The Album]]''. ''Mississippi'' included the hit single, "[[Like a Pimp]]", featuring [[Lil Flip]]. "Like a Pimp" peaked at #48 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart, #15 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart, and #10 on the [[Hot Rap Tracks]] chart.<ref name="singles">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p441465/charts-awards/billboard-singles|pure_url=yes}}|title=David Banner: Charts & Awards: Billboard Singles|publisher=allmusic|access-date=December 28, 2009}}</ref> He released the follow-up album the same year titled ''[[MTA2: Baptized in Dirty Water]]'' which yielded the hit, "Crank It Up", featuring [[Static Major]].

In 2005, Banner released his third major label album, ''[[Certified (David Banner album)|Certified]]''. The album's first single was "Ain't Got Nothing" which featured Magic & [[Lil Boosie]], followed by the second single, "[[Play (David Banner song)|Play]]", which reached #7 on the Hot 100 chart, #5 on the R&B chart, and #3 on the Rap chart.<ref name="singles"/> The third single, "Touching", featured Jazze Pha and reached #54 on the R&B chart. In 2006, Banner appeared on "Seein' Thangs", a song about Hurricane Katrina featured on DJ Shadow's album "The Outsider". A remix featuring Bay Area hyphy rappers Nump and Gold also was made. Outside projects for Banner have included writing the theme song to the video game, ''[[Saints Row (video game)|Saints Row]]'', as well as contributing to the music for a commercial promoting the video game, ''[[Marvel vs Capcom 3: Fate of Two Worlds]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://music.ign.com/articles/727/727105p1.html|title=Saints Row Snags David Banner|work=IGN|access-date=9 March 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120126072208/http://music.ign.com/articles/727/727105p1.html|archive-date=26 January 2012}}</ref> Banner played the part of Tehronne in ''[[Black Snake Moan (film)|Black Snake Moan]]''.

===2007&ndash;present: ''The Greatest Story Ever Told'' and ''The God Box''===
Banner appeared on the [[Adult Swim]] cartoon show [[That Crook'd 'Sipp]], which premiered Sunday, May 13, 2007. His single "[[Play (David Banner song)|Play]]" was used as the background music in the pilot's first television promo. In 2007, he played the character of Mo, in the film ''[[This Christmas (film)|This Christmas]]''. Banner also starred as Jay, a gang leader from the hood in ''[[Stomp the Yard: Homecoming]]''. On July 15, 2008, Banner released his fourth major label album, ''[[The Greatest Story Ever Told (David Banner album)|The Greatest Story Ever Told]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theblackspotlight.com/news/David_Banner_Releases_The_Greatest_Story_Ever_Told.html|title=The Black Spotlight|access-date=9 March 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111004104633/http://www.theblackspotlight.com/news/David_Banner_Releases_The_Greatest_Story_Ever_Told.html|archive-date=4 October 2011}}</ref> The album's first single titled "9mm" featured [[Akon]], [[Lil Wayne]], and [[Snoop Dogg]]. Banner then produced his next two singles: "[[Get Like Me (David Banner song)|Get Like Me]]" featuring [[Chris Brown (American singer)|Chris Brown]] and [[Yung Joc]], "Get Like Me" reached #16 on the Hot 100, #7 on the R&B chart, and #2 on the Rap chart.<ref name="singles"/> and "[[Shawty Say]]", featuring Lil Wayne. In 2008, Banner was featured on the track, ''Superfriend'', from the 2008 album, ''[[The Sound (Mary Mary album)|The Sound]]'', by [[gospel music|gospel]] [[contemporary R&B|R&B]] duo, [[Mary Mary]].

In 2010, he played Bosch in the film [[The Experiment (2010 film)|The Experiment]]. He has also worked with international artists such as Seeda, a Japanese Rap Artist where he was featured in the song,  Life Song in 2010 on Seeda's Breathe Album. Banner released his debut mixtape titled ''[[Sex, Drugs and Video Games]]'' on May 22, 2012 as a free download.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/David-Banner-Sex-Drugs-Video-Games-mixtape.349642.html|title=David Banner - Sex, Drugs & Video Games|work=DatPiff|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref> It is also available on his website, davidbanner.com, where fans are encouraged to make a donation of at least $1 for the sixteen song mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T9QMrfBKUB8|title=David Banner's Epic Interview|date=9 April 2012|work=YouTube|access-date=9 March 2015}}</ref> The first single off the mixtape is titled "Amazing" featuring [[Chris Brown (American singer)|Chris Brown]]. It was announced that [[Universal Republic Records]] was going defunct, all of the artists on the roster moved from the label including (David Banner) was being moved to [[Republic Records]] making the label itself revived.

In 2013, David Banner appeared in the music video for the song ''[[Confessions (Lecrae song)|Confessions]]'' by [[Lecrae]]. Banner played Earl Gaines, Cecil Gaines' father, in the 2013 film ''[[The Butler]]''. Banner plays Jay, a criminal who is interrogated, in the 2014 film ''[[Ride Along (film)|Ride Along]].'' David Banner was featured on a remix of Mexican American rapper [[Kap G]]'s single "La Policia", which stirred hype throughout the media and the internet.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/04/09/kap-g-ti-david-banner-la-policia-remix|title=T.I. and David Banner Join Kap G For Powerful "La Policia" Remix|work=The FADER}}</ref>

==Humanitarian and political work==

In November 2006, Banner was awarded a Visionary Award by the [[National Black Caucus of State Legislators]] in [[Jackson, Mississippi]]  in recognition of his humanitarian work in response to [[Hurricane Katrina]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wapt.com/video/10424269/index.html?%20taf=jac/|title=David Banner Speech|date=29 March 2012|work=WAPT|access-date=9 March 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070926235155/http://www.wapt.com/video/10424269/index.html?%20taf=jac%2F|archive-date=26 September 2007}}</ref><ref>[https://top40-charts.com/mnews.php?nid=29056&cat=  National Black Caucus Honors David Banner For His Hurricane Katrina Relief Efforts With Humanitarian Award] November 2006</ref>
On September 25, 2007, Banner testified before Congress at a hearing about [[racism]] and [[misogyny in hip hop culture|misogyny in hip hop music]] titled ''From [[Don Imus#Rutgers women's basketball controversy|Imus]] to Industry: The Business of Stereotypes and Degrading Images''.<ref name="washington">Abrams, Jim. [https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/09/25/AR2007092500908.html "House Panel Debates Hip-Hop Lyrics"]. ''[[The Washington Post]]'', September 25, 2011.</ref> He defended his use of offensive language and argued: "Change the situation in my neighborhood and maybe I'll get better."<ref name="washington"/> In his opening statement, Banner stated: "I can admit there are some problems in hip hop but it is only a reflection of what's taking place in our society. Hip hop is sick because America is sick."<ref name="Hearing">{{cite news|last=Leeds|first=Jeff|title=Hearing Focuses on Language and Violence in Rap Music |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/09/26/washington/26rap.html|work=The New York Times|date=2007-09-26}}</ref>
