'''girl in red''' is the [[indie pop]] music project<ref name=":8" /><ref>{{Cite web|date=27 November 2018|title=Get To Know... Girl In Red|url=http://diymag.com/2018/11/27/get-to-know-girl-in-red-interview|access-date=3 March 2021|website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]|quote=Next up, we’re with Girl In Red, the project of 19-year-old Marie Ulven from Oslo.}}</ref><ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Morris|first=Ashley|date=2 February 2020|title=Interview: Norwegian bedroom singer Girl in Red|url=https://www.euphoriazine.com/blog/2020/02/interviews-girl-in-red-interview/|access-date=3 March 2021|website=Euphoria|quote='''What made you decide to call yourself Girl in Red instead of using your real name?''' When I started the project, I already had a Norwegian project under my own name, so it was mostly just to start something new!}}</ref> of Norwegian singer-songwriter and record producer '''Marie Ulven''' '''Ringheim''' (born 16 February 1999). She rose to prominence with her homemade [[bedroom pop]] songs about romance and [[mental health]] featured on the early [[Extended play|EPs]] ''Chapter 1'' (2018) and ''Chapter 2'' (2019).<ref name="thelineofbestfit">{{cite web|title=A teenage queer icon for queer teenagers, Marie Ulven is breaking out of the bedroom as girl in red.|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/interviews/on-the-rise-girl-in-red|last1=Hannah|first1=Andrew|date=28 November 2018|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|access-date=16 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Johnston|first=Maura|date=September 20, 2019|title=Home Recording Gear Haul: girl in red – Spotify for Artists|url=https://artists.spotify.com/blog/home-recording-gear-haul-girl-in-red|access-date=March 3, 2021|website=[[Spotify]]}}</ref> Her debut studio album ''[[If I Could Make It Go Quiet]]'' was released through [[AWAL]] on 30 April 2021.<ref name=":9">{{Cite web|last=Trendell|first=Andrew|date=1 March 2021|title=Girl In Red announces debut album 'If I Could Make It Go Quiet' and new single 'Serotonin'|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/girl-in-red-announces-debut-album-if-i-could-make-it-go-quiet-and-new-single-serotonin-finneas-2891481|access-date=3 March 2021|website=[[NME]]}}</ref>

Girl in Red has been cited as a [[Gay icon|queer icon]] by ''[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]'',<ref name=":0" /> and a "phenomenon" who is "one of the most astute and exciting singer-songwriters working in the world of guitar music" by ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref name=":2" /> She was nominated for Best New Artist at the 2020 [[Spellemannprisen|Norwegian Grammy Awards]]. In January 2021, her singles "I Wanna Be Your Girlfriend" (2017) and "[[We Fell in Love in October]]" (2018) were [[RIAA certification|certified gold]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).

== Early life ==
Marie Ulven Ringheim<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/writer/780561136/RINGHEIM%20MARIE%20ULVEN|title=ASCAP Ace Search|last=Ringheim|first=Marie Ulven|publisher=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] (ASCAP)|access-date=29 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=TheDirtyHarry|title=11. Marie Ulven Ringheim v.s. Fabian Kronsteiner|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D2RMgqLJUsI|work=[[YouTube]]|language=en|access-date=2019-11-17}}</ref> was born in the town of [[Horten]], [[Norway]] on 16 February 1999.<ref name=":14">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/big-reads/girl-in-red-interview-nme-100-2020-star-2592144|title=The Big Read – Girl In Red: "World domination. That's what I want"|last=Trendell|first=Andrew|date=3 January 2020|website=[[NME]]|language=en-GB|access-date=10 January 2020}}</ref> She grew up in the town with her sisters and her divorced parents,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.them.us/story/girl-in-red-chapter-2-interview|title=Girl in Red's Music is Touching, Gorgeous, and Unabashedly Queer|last=Bennett|first=Willa|date=2 September 2019|website=them.|access-date=2 October 2019}}</ref> and described it as "quiet and kinda boring" in an interview with [[Triple J]] in October 2019.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/programs/2019-with-richard-kingsmill/girl-in-red/11615666|title=GIRL IN RED: from a Norwegian bedroom to beyond|date=October 18, 2019|website=[[triple j]]|access-date=2019-10-24}}</ref> Her mother worked with technology and her father worked as a policeman.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/03/27/arts/music/marie-ulven-girl-in-red.html|title=She's Becoming the Gay Musical Role Model She Never Had|last=Caramanica|first=Jon|date=27 March 2019|work=The New York Times|access-date=2 October 2019|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Her grandfather could play the guitar and piano; however, she grew up without instruments in the home.<ref name="thelineofbestfit" /> Ulven received her first guitar as a Christmas gift from her grandfather in 2012, but didn't start playing it until 2013 after losing interest in [[Fingerboard (skateboard)|fingerboarding]].<ref name=":6" /> She credits her grandfather for sparking her interest in music.<ref name=":6" /> While in high school, Ulven was curious about becoming a teacher before she was introduced to the guitar and songwriting at the age of 14.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/girl-in-red-2639222000.html|title=Meet Girl in Red: The Queer Artist Writing Teen Love Songs|last=Kaplan|first=Ilana|date=17 July 2019|website=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|access-date=2 October 2019}}</ref> She had taught herself piano, guitar and [[Record producer|music production]] from the comfort of her bedroom.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2019/03/25/neu-recommended-girl-in-red-scalping-nov3l-more|title=Neu Recommended (Girl In Red, SCALPING, NOV3L & more)|date=March 25, 2019|website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]|access-date=2019-10-11}}</ref> Ulven began by writing and releasing [[Music of Norway|Norwegian music]] and planned to study music, but never concluded that she was going to become a musician.<ref name=":0" />

== Career ==
=== 2015–2017: SoundCloud and "I Wanna Be Your Girlfriend" ===
After getting a [[Blue Microphones|Blue Yeti]] microphone from her father in 2015,<ref name=":6" /> Ulven began writing and releasing [[Music of Norway|Norwegian music]] to [[SoundCloud]] under the moniker of "Lydia X".<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bewaremag.com/girl-in-red-king-princess-question-du-genre-sexualite/|title=De Girl in Red à King Princess, qui sont ces jeunes artistes qui redéfinissent les questions du genre et de la sexualité ?|date=27 September 2019|website=Beware Magazine|language=fr-FR|access-date=2 October 2019}}</ref> She stopped attending guitar lessons after six months once her teacher refused to acknowledge her interest in songwriting and producing.<ref name=":6" /> Ulven coined the stagename "Girl in Red" after attempting to identify herself in a crowd to a friend via [[Text messaging|text]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2019/02/girl-in-red-interview|title=No Mixed Signals: An Interview With Girl in Red|last=Aquino|first=Tara|date=14 February 2019|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|access-date=2 October 2019}}</ref> Using the new moniker, she published her debut single "I Wanna Be Your Girlfriend" on SoundCloud in November 2016,<ref>{{Citation|last=girl in red|title=i wanna be your girlfriend|date=November 11, 2016|url=https://soundcloud.com/lyfsuxx/i-wanna-be-your-girlfriend|work=[[SoundCloud]]}}</ref> where it got about 5 thousand streams within five months.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|date=October 21, 2019|title=SPACES: girl in red Hit 150M Streams With Inclusive Self-Love And A $150 Mic|url=https://www.awal.com/blog/spaces-girl-in-red|access-date=October 24, 2019|website=[[AWAL]]}}</ref> Following the single's feature on the Norwegian music website ''NRK Urørt'', "I Wanna Be Your Girlfriend" amassed thousands of streams and gained Ulven a large online following.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/girl-in-red-mn0003783307/biography|title=girl in red {{!}} Biography & History|last=Donelson|first=Marcy|publisher=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''If I Could Make It Go Quiet'' ===
In December 2019, Ulven signed a worldwide recording contract with British music distribution company [[AWAL]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Stassen|first=Murray|date=3 December 2019|title=AWAL signs worldwide recordings deal with girl in red|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/awal-signs-worldwide-recordings-deal-with-girl-in-red/|access-date=5 March 2021|website=[[Music Business Worldwide]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=3 December 2019|title=Welcoming girl in red to the AWAL Family|url=https://www.awal.com/blog/welcoming-girl-in-red-to-the-awal-family|access-date=5 March 2021|website=[[AWAL]]}}</ref> In a rare phenomenon for most new label signees, her entire existing discography, which had all originally been [[Self-publishing|self-released]], was subsequently registered under this label on all digital music platforms. Ulven appeared on the front cover of both ''[[Gay Times]]'' and ''[[Dork (magazine)|Dork]]'' in December 2019,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://buy.gaytimes.co.uk/products/gay-times-magazine-issue-503|title=Gay Times Magazine • Issue 503|website=[[Gay Times]]|language=en|access-date=29 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.readdork.com/magazine/december-2019-january-2020|title=December 2019 / January 2020|website=Dork|language=en|access-date=10 January 2020}}</ref> as well as the cover of ''[[NME]]'' in January 2020.<ref name=":14" /> She provided the single "Kate's Not Here" for the original soundtrack of the 2020 supernatural horror film ''[[The Turning (2020 film)|The Turning]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/girl-in-red-shares-surging-new-song-kates-not-here-2601073|title=Girl In Red shares surging new song 'Kate's Not Here'|last=Daly|first=Rhian|date=25 January 2020|website=[[NME]]|language=en-GB|access-date=2 April 2020}}</ref> Ulven was nominated for the Newcomer of the Year award and, as a result, the Gramo scholarship at the 2020 [[Spellemannprisen]], also known elsewhere as the Norwegian Grammy Awards.<ref name=":15" /> In May 2020, Ulven was included in the annual ''[[Dazed]]'' 100 list after her hit single "I Wanna Be Your Girlfriend" clocked 150 million streams.<ref>{{Cite web|title=girl in red on the #Dazed100|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/projects/article/48821/1/girl-in-red-musician-biography-dazed-100-2020-profile|last=Dawson|first=Brit|date=April 28, 2020|website=[[Dazed]]|language=en|access-date=May 18, 2020}}</ref> In mid 2020, she became a popular symbol of queer identification on the online platform [[TikTok]], where users would adopt the common phrase, "Do you listen to Girl in Red?" as a way of asking if someone was a [[lesbian]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Wilson|first=Lena|date=29 June 2020|title=For Lesbians, TikTok Is 'the Next Tinder'|language=en-US|work=[[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/06/29/style/lesbian-tiktok-dating.html|access-date=7 July 2020|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Isherwood|first=Alisha|date=28 June 2020|title="Yes, I listen to Girl In Red" – The Symbiotic Nature of Sexuality and Music|url=http://cubmagazine.co.uk/2020/06/yes-i-listen-to-girl-in-red-the-symbiotic-nature-of-sexuality-and-music/|access-date=7 July 2020|website=[[Queen Mary University of London|CUB Magazine]]|language=en-US}}</ref> In November, she released the standalone [[Christmas music|holiday single]] "Two Queens in a King Sized Bed",<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Ehrlich|first=Brenna|date=18 November 2020|title=Girl in Red Drops Cozy New Christmas Song 'Two Queens in a King-Sized Bed'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/girl-in-red-queens-bed-christmas-1092216/|access-date=2 March 2021|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref> shortly before she was awarded Artist of the Year at the 2020 [[P3 Gull]] Awards.<ref name=":4" /> 

Ulven released her debut studio album, titled ''[[If I Could Make It Go Quiet]]'', on 30 April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Minsker|first=Evan|date=3 March 2021|title=girl in red Details Debut Album, Shares New Song 'Serotonin'|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/girl-in-red-details-debut-album-shares-new-song-serotonin-listen/|access-date=3 March 2021|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref> The album has thus far been supported by four singles, "Midnight Love", in April 2020,<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Ehrlich|first=Brenna|date=15 April 2020|title=Girl in Red's 'Midnight Love' Is a Dreamy Ode to F-k Buddies|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/girl-in-red-midnight-love-983701/|access-date=4 March 2021|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref> "Rue" in August 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Almeida|first=Nicole|date=14 September 2020|title=Today's Song: girl in red's Essential and Euphoric 'rue'|url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/girr-rue-girl-in-red-song-review/|access-date=2 March 2021|website=[[Atwood Magazine]]}}</ref> "Serotonin" in March 2021,<ref name=":9" /> and "You Stupid Bitch" in April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dowd|first=Rachael|date=2021-04-14|title=girl in red channels 'The Perks Of Being A Wallflower' in "You Stupid Bitch"|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/girl-in-red-new-single-you-stupid-bitch/|access-date=2021-05-11|website=Alternative Press|language=en-US}}</ref> The cover artwork for the album was made by the Norwegian painter and artist Fredrik Wiig Sørensen. Originally an oil painting on a 80x80 cm canvas, by the title of The Antizero. Ulven named [[Taylor Swift]] as one of her biggest musical influences.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ruth|first=Wolfgang|date=2021-04-30|title=7 Influences on girl in red's Debut, From (Yes) Taylor Swift to Low Serotonin|url=https://www.vulture.com/2021/04/girl-in-red-interview-serotonin-if-i-could-make-it-go-quiet.html|access-date=2021-05-01|website=Vulture|language=en-us}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Ulven now resides in the [[Grünerløkka]] district of [[Oslo]]. She is openly [[queer]]<!-- Don't change to lesbian, Ulven uses gay instead. See the source for details. -->.<ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1321790104420163584|user=_girlinred_|title=For those who need to know:|author=Girl in Red|date=2020-10-29}}</ref> She studied music production and songwriting at [[Westerdals Oslo School of Arts, Communication and Technology]].<ref name=":2" />
