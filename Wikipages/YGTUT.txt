'''Kevin Adams, Jr.''' (born December 19, 1991), better known by his stage name '''YGTUT''' (or simply, '''TUT'''), is an American rapper, singer and record producer. He first gained recognition and acclaim after the release of his 2015 mixtape, ''Preacher's Son''. His most recent project, an EP titled ''I.O.U.'', was released in January 2019. He is signed to Same Plate Entertainment and [[Sony Music]]. He is also a founding member of the Chattanooga art collective, TheHouse.

==Early life and education==
Kevin Adams Jr.<ref name="Vice2015">{{cite news | last=Kramer| first=Kyle| title =Chattanooga Gospel: TUT's Corner Stories Are Bringing a New Spirit to Hip-Hop| work=Noisey| date =7 July 2015 | url =https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/r3za9n/tut-preachers-son-interview-profile-2015-throwed-video-premiere| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> was born and raised in Chattanooga, Tennessee. His father is a [[Baptists|Baptist]] preacher and his mother a [[Gospel music|gospel]] singer.<ref name="Bull">{{cite news | last=Bell| first=Max| title =Interview: Rising Chattanooga Rapper YGTut| publisher=Red Bull Music Academy| date =18 March 2015 | url =https://daily.redbullmusicacademy.com/2015/03/ygtut-interview| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> Adams grew up attending church and would often play the [[Organ (music)|organ]] or [[drums]] after service.<ref name="Vice2015"/> He first started rapping when he was 13 or 14 after his father brought him to a studio.<ref name="Bull"/> In around 2010, he began attending [[Middle Tennessee State University]]. While there, he met fellow recording artist, [[Isaiah Rashad]], and the two formed an artist collective called "TheHouse".<ref name="Vice2015"/> Both artists ended up dropping out and moving back to Chattanooga to focus on music full time.<ref name="Bull"/>

==Career==
YGTUT began working on and releasing music in around 2011.<ref name="ComplexTrill">{{cite news | last=Espinoza| first=Joshua| title =Premiere: YGTUT Drops His Long-Lost "Trill Dreams" Track f/ Isaiah Rashad| work=Complex| date =14 February 2018 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2018/02/ygtut-isaiah-rashad-trill-dreams| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> He also went by the stage name, "Kev Adams", for a time.<ref name="Bull"/> He first garnered significant interest in late 2014 by releasing a series of tracks, including "Hangin'", "Holy Water" (featuring Angel Mae),<ref name="DJBHoly">{{cite news | last=Spadine| first=Richard| title =YGTUT ft. Angel Mae - Holy Water| work=DJ Booth| date =16 December 2014 | url =https://djbooth.net/songs/ygtut-holy-water| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> and "Live From Chattanooga".<ref name="ElevLive">{{cite news | last=S-k| first=Julian| title =YGTUT "Live From Chattanooga" Prod. By K-Toven| work=Elevator Magazine| date =30 December 2014 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/ygtut-live-from-chattanooga-prod-by-k-toven/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> Those songs would form the basis of his first mixtape, ''Preacher's Son'', which was released in January 2015. The album featured guest appearances and production work from several members of TheHouse including Isaiah Rashad, Michael Da Vinci, and Ktoven.<ref name="ElevPreacher">{{cite news | last=S-k| first=Julian| title =YGTUT "Preacher's Son" (Mixtape)| work=Elevator Magazine| date =6 January 2015 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/ygtut-preachers-son-mixtape/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> Another House member, Swayyvo, played saxophone on the mixtape.<ref name="TFP">{{cite news | last=Gienapp| first=Emmett| title =Chattanooga native is triple threat with rap, beat production and saxophone| newspaper=Times Free Press| date =26 December 2016 | url =https://www.timesfreepress.com/news/life/entertainment/story/2016/dec/26/chattanoognative-triple-threrap-beproductiand/404369/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> The album received a favorable review from ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]''.<ref name="Pitch">{{cite news | last=Turner| first=David| title = TUT - Preacher's Son| work=Pitchfork| date =14 January 2015 | url =https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/20124-preachers-son/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref>

In August 2016, TUT released the single "G35" featuring Isaiah Rashad.<ref name="Bill35">{{cite news | last=Platon| first=Adelle| title =Chattanooga Rapper Tut Rides Out on 'G35' Feat. Isaiah Rashad: Premiere| work=Billboard| date =9 August 2016 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7461976/chattanooga-rapper-tut-g35-isaiah-rashad-premiere| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> Later that year, he was featured on the Zzz track, "The Cost".<ref name="EDM">{{cite news | title =Don't Sleep On Zzz's "The Cost" ft. YGTut| work=EDM Sauce| date =15 November 2016 | url =https://www.edmsauce.com/2016/11/15/zzz-cost-ft-ygtut-prod-keem-cipher/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> In June 2017, TUT was featured on a track ("Long Day") as part of TheHouse collective. Fellow members Brian Brown, Michael Da Vinci, and Chris P also delivered verses on the song.<ref name="ElevLong">{{cite news | title =Chattanooga collective The House returns for first time in years with video for "Long Day"| work=Elevator Magazine| date =13 June 2017 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/chattanooga-collective-the-house-returns-for-first-time-in-years-with-video-for-long-day/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> In December of that year, TUT released the solo single, "$leezy Money".<ref name="ElevSleezy">{{cite news | last=Robertson| first=Devin| title =YGTut is counting up '$leezy Money'| work=Elevator Magazine| date =14 December 2017 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/ygtut-is-counting-up-leezy-money/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> Later that month, he appeared on another release by TheHouse, this time an EP entitled ''Four Two Three'' (a reference to the [[Area code 423|area code in Chattanooga]]). YGTUT and $hoey were the only two members to appear on all the collection's songs.<ref name="ElevHouse">{{cite news | last=Robertson| first=Devin| title =What is TheHouse?| work=Elevator Magazine| date =3 January 2018 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/what-is-thehouse/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref>

In February 2018, he released a track called "Trill Dreams" featuring Isaiah Rashad. The song had been recorded in 2011 when the two were still attending Middle Tennessee State University together.<ref name="ComplexTrill"/> In April 2018, TUT was featured on the Hippy Soul song, "Tides".<ref name="ElevHippy">{{cite news | last=Robertson| first=Devin| title =Hippy Soul and YGTUT ride the wave on "Tides"| work=Elevator Magazine| date =12 April 2018 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/hippy-soul-and-ygtut-ride-the-wave-on-tides/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> In June of that year, it was announced that he had been signed to Same Plate Entertainment and [[Sony Music]].<ref name="BillSony">{{cite news | last=Mitchell| first=Gail| title =Sony Music & Jonathan Master Partner for SamePlate Joint Venture: Exclusive| work=Billboard| date =4 June 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8459056/sony-music-jonathan-master-partner-sameplate-joint-venture| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> Beginning in August, he started periodically releasing new songs in a [[SoundCloud]] playlist called ''Save It''.<ref name="ElevSave">{{cite news | last=Gregory| first=Jack| title =YGTUT returns with brand new EP "Save It"| work=Elevator Magazine| date =23 August 2018 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/ygtut-returns-with-brand-new-ep-save-it/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> These tracks included "Loyalty 2 da Fam",<ref name="Pitch2018">{{cite news | last=Pierre| first=Alphonse| title =The Ones: 5 Best New Rap Songs From Quando Rondo, Yung Bans, YGTUT, Sheff G, and Octavian| work=Pitchfork| date =12 September 2018 | url =https://pitchfork.com/levels/the-ones-5-best-new-rap-songs-from-quando-rondo-yung-bans-ygtut-sheff-g-and-octavian/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> "Late", and "Top of the World".<ref name="Elev2">{{cite news | last=Gregory| first=Jack| title =YGTUT blesses us with two new tracks on his "Save It" Playlist| work=Elevator Magazine| date =30 September 2018 | url =https://www.elevatormag.com/ygtut-blesses-us-with-two-new-tracks-on-his-save-it-playlist/| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref>

In December 2018, he premiered the song, "Get It".<ref name="FaderGet">{{cite news | last=Maicki| first=Salvatore| title =YGTUT aims for anthem status on new single "Get It"| work=The Fader| date =12 December 2018 | url =https://www.thefader.com/2018/12/12/ygtut-new-single-get-it-premiere| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> It would serve as the lead single<ref name="Vice2019">{{cite news | last=Phillips| first=Yoh| title =The Gospel According to YGTUT| work=Noisey| date =18 January 2019 | url =https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/qvyda5/the-gospel-according-to-ygtut| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref> on his Same Plate/Sony debut EP, ''I.O.U.'', which was released in January 2019.<ref name="DJB2019">{{cite news | last=Chesman| first=Donna-Claire| title =A Very Genuine Interview with Chattanooga Rapper YGTUT| work=DJ Booth| date =17 January 2019 | url =https://djbooth.net/features/2019-01-17-ygtut-interview-iou| access-date =5 September 2019}}</ref>

In 2021, YGTUT appeared on [[Isaiah Rashad|Isaiah Rashad's]] album ''[[The House Is Burning (album)|The House is Burning]]'', featuring on the track "Chad".
