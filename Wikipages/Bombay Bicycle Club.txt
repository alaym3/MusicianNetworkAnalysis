'''Bombay Bicycle Club''' are an English [[indie rock]] band from [[Crouch End]], [[London]], consisting of [[Mr Jukes|Jack Steadman]], Jamie MacColl, Suren de Saram, and Ed Nash.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/0ae49abe-d6af-44fa-8ab0-b9ace5690e6f |title=Bombay Bicycle Club - New Songs, Playlists & Latest News |publisher=BBC|access-date=18 January 2020}}</ref> They are guitar-fronted and have experimented with different genres, including [[folk music|folk]], [[electronic music|electronica]], [[world music]] and [[indie rock]].

The band were given the opening slot on 2006's [[V Festival]] after winning a competition. They subsequently released two [[Extended play|EP]]s and their debut single "[[Evening/Morning]]". Since then, the band has released five albums including ''[[So Long, See You Tomorrow (album)|So Long, See You Tomorrow]]'' which topped the album charts in February 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/bombay-bicycle-club-score-first-ever-number-1-album-2283/|title=Bombay Bicycle Club score their first ever Number 1 |date= 9 February 2014}}</ref> The band has toured worldwide as a headlining act, playing North America, Australia, Europe and Asia.

In January 2016, the four members announced that they would pursue solo projects.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/news/bombay-bicycle-club/91081|title=Bombay Bicycle Club are going on hiatus|last=Renshaw|first=David|date=29 January 2016|website=NME|language=en-GB|access-date=2016-07-01}}</ref> Ed Nash recorded music under the name Toothless, and released his debut album in early 2017.<ref>[https://www.instagram.com/p/BMBbeR8hpYW/] {{dead link|date=January 2020}}</ref> Frontman Jack Steadman, under the name [[Mr Jukes]], released his debut album in 2017.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/03/21/mr-jukes-teams-up-with-bj-the-chicago-kid-for-the-joyful-angelsyour-love|title=Mr Jukes Teams Up With BJ The Chicago Kid for the Joyful "Angels/Your Love"|website=Thefader.com|access-date=2017-03-21}}</ref>

In January 2019, Bombay Bicycle Club announced their return to making new music and performing live after a three-year hiatus: "Since late last year we've been getting back into the swing of playing music together. More than anything it just felt great to be in the same room playing again. It made us realise what a good thing we have and has given us renewed energy and enthusiasm for the future".<ref name=autogenerated1>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/BombayBicycle/status/1084842688036462592|title=pic.twitter.com/7ryt3zr5aL|last=Club|first=Bombay Bicycle|date=2019-01-14|website=@BombayBicycle|access-date=2019-01-14}}{{Primary source inline|date=December 2020}}</ref>

==History==
===Beginnings (2005–06)===
Steadman, MacColl (son of former [[The Bible (band)|Bible]]/Liberty Horses guitarist/producer/session player Neill MacColl, nephew of singer [[Kirsty MacColl]] and grandson of folk singers [[Ewan MacColl]] and [[Peggy Seeger]])<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/jan/25/ewan-maccoll-godfather-folk-adored-and-feared|title=Ewan MacColl: the godfather of folk who was adored – and feared|first=Neil|last=Spencer|date=25 January 2015|website=The Guardian}}</ref> and de Saram (son of Sri Lankan cellist [[Rohan de Saram]])<ref>{{cite web |title=Rohan de Saram |url=http://www.rohandesaram.co.uk/main.asp?varFunction=Artists&varArtistid=33 |website=Rohandesaram.co.uk |access-date=17 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=LUNCHBREAK CONCERT |url=http://www.rohandesaram.co.uk/reviews/aberdeen_20091114.pdf |website=Rohandesaram.co.uk |access-date=17 September 2019 |date=14 November 2009}}</ref> met at age 15 as students at [[University College School]], [[Hampstead]] (in the same year as the members of the band [[Cajun Dance Party]]),<ref>{{cite web |title=Bombay Bicycle Club interview: Saddled with success |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/bombay-bicycle-club-interview-saddled-with-success-9066682.html |website=The Independent |access-date=17 September 2019 |date=17 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=No 217: Bombay Bicycle Club |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2007/nov/01/popandrock1 |website=The Guardian |access-date=17 September 2019 |date=1 November 2007}}</ref> and began playing under the name ''The Canals'' after performing together in a school assembly, with an additional keyboardist.  They switched between various aliases until they picked Bombay Bicycle Club, named after a (now defunct) [[Bombay Bicycle Club (restaurant chain)|chain of Indian restaurants]] in North London. The line-up changed regularly until summer 2006, when Ed Nash (a student at [[Camden School for Girls]]),<ref>{{cite web |title=Bicycle boys on the frame game |url=http://www.thecnj.com/review/2009/111209/music111209_01.html |website=Thecnj.com |access-date=17 September 2019 |date=12 November 2009}}</ref> joined the band after they met him at a funeral. Initially they played gigs at small London venues such as The Old Blue Last, Lark in the Park and Jacksons Lane (as well as winning Exeter's Got Talent 2009). They released demos of their music on [[Myspace]], with early versions of 'The Hill' and 'Autumn' as well as the song, 'City Lights'.

The band was entered into [[Virgin Mobile]]'s "Road to V" competition on Channel 4 in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idiomag.com/peek/92786/bombay_bicycle_club|title=Bombay Bicycle Club - Dust on the Ground|access-date=2009-07-24|date=21 July 2009|publisher=[[idiomag]]}}</ref> They were announced as one of two winners of the competition on 16 August 2006, beating competition from fellow [[London]] band - and favourites to win the competition - [[The Holloways]] in the final round of fourteen bands.<ref name="BBC MusicOMH">{{cite web|url=http://www.musicomh.com/music/features/bombay-bicycle-club_0307.htm |title=Bombay Bicycle Club - MusicOMH |website=musicOMH|access-date=2008-12-28 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090108161858/http://www.musicomh.com/music/features/bombay-bicycle-club_0307.htm |archive-date=8 January 2009 }}</ref> Previous participants include [[Young Knives|The Young Knives]], who were winners of the inaugural competition one year before. They were given the opening slot of the 2006 [[V Festival]], playing on the [[V Festival#V2006|Channel 4 Stage]] in [[Chelmsford]] on 19 August, before opening the [[Staffordshire]] site the following day.

===''The Boy I Used to Be'' and ''How We Are'' (2007–08)===
On 16 May 2007, ''[[NME]]'' published an article naming Bombay Bicycle Club "...the hottest band to come from North London for quite some time".<ref name="NME">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/artists/bombay-bicycle-club|title=Bombay Bicycle Club Archives &#124; NME}}</ref> Bombay Bicycle Club released their debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''[[The Boy I Used to Be]]'', on 12 February 2007. The band released it on their own [[independent record label]], Mmm... Records.<ref name="zunior.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.zunior.com/product_info.php?products_id=902 |title=Zunior.com |website=Zunior.com |access-date=18 January 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928024444/http://www.zunior.com/product_info.php?products_id=902 |archive-date=28 September 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="Bombay Bicycle Club">{{cite web|url=http://www.bombaybicycleclubmusic.com/|title=Bombay Bicycle Club|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100620065741/http://www.bombaybicycleclubmusic.com/|archive-date=20 June 2010}}</ref> The EP was produced by [[Jim Abbiss]] at his own personal request, and was recorded and mixed by Ewan Davies at [[Lincolnshire]]'s Chapel Studios. To support the release of the EP they played a headline show at the [[Dingwalls]] in Camden as well as supporting fellow "Road to V" winners [[Young Knives|The Young Knives]] across some of their UK Tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.channel4.com/music/features/interview.jsp?featureId=270|title=Categories - All 4|publisher=Channel 4}}</ref> The band also played the Carling Stage at [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]] in 2007.

The band's second EP, ''[[How We Are]]'', was released on 22 October 2007, again through Mmm... Records.<ref name="zunior.com"/><ref name="Bombay Bicycle Club"/> It was produced by Jim Abbiss, and recorded and mixed by Richard Wilkinson at Konk Studios. The band headlined their first UK tour<ref name="Bombay Bicycle Club"/> in support of this release and the EP debuted at number 2 on the UK Indie Singles Chart of 5 November 2007.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/music-magazine/live-reviews/bombay-bicycle-club-the-roundhouse-london-772761.html | newspaper=[[The Independent]]| location=London | title=Bombay Bicycle Club, The Roundhouse, London | first=Elisa | last=Bray | date=24 January 2008 | access-date=26 April 2010}}</ref>

At the beginning of 2008, Bombay Bicycle Club appeared at the third annual [[Artrocker]] Festival,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.artrocker.com/node/1034|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090207154531/http://www.artrocker.com/node/1034|url-status=dead|title=Artrocker 3rd annual festival 15-19 Jan &#124; Artrocker|archive-date=7 February 2009}}</ref> the [[Camden Crawl]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gigwise.com/news/40996/Camden-Crawl-Line-Up-Is-Announced|title=Camden Crawl Line-Up Is Announced|author=Scott Colothan|website=Gigwise.com}}</ref> and The Great Escape Festival in Brighton.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.efestivals.co.uk/news/080219a.shtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080821143836/http://www.efestivals.co.uk/news/080219a.shtml|url-status=dead|title=more acts for Great Escape - Pete and the Pirates, Bombay Bicycle Club, and many more|archive-date=21 August 2008}}</ref> The band also played the [[Shockwaves NME Awards]] Show at London's [[KOKO (venue)|KOKO]] where they were main support for [[The Hold Steady]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/awardsshows|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080928083824/http://www.nme.com/awardsshows|url-status=dead|title=Shockwaves NME Awards 2008|archive-date=28 September 2008}}</ref>

===''I Had the Blues But I Shook Them Loose'' (2008–09)===
In June 2008, all current members of the band finished secondary school education, which meant that they could commit full-time to music – and enjoy freedom from the conflicting responsibilities of being in a band versus education. The band also headlined the first Club NME in [[Paris]] at La Fleche D'or venue, where they were supported by Bitchee Bitchee Ya Ya Ya, with DJ sets from South Central, [[Shitdisco]] and Vicarious Bliss.

The band's first single, "Evening/Morning", was released on 4 August 2008 through Young and Lost Club.<ref name="Young and Lost Club">{{cite web|url=http://www.youngandlostclub.com/|title=YOUNG AND LOST CLUB|website=Youngandlostclub.com|access-date=21 June 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081113143401/http://youngandlostclub.com/|archive-date=13 November 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref> The single was produced by Jim Abbiss and was recorded and mixed by Richard Wilkinson at The Garden, London. The band embarked on a 23 date UK tour in July and August, including festival appearances at the 2008 [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]], the 2008 [[T in the Park]] Festival, [[The Edge Festival]] in [[Edinburgh]], [[Scotland]] and the second [[Underage Festival]].<ref name="Young and Lost Club"/>

In September 2008, Bombay Bicycle Club took part in the [[Levi Strauss|Levi]]'s Ones to Watch 5 Night Revue at The Macbeth in London.<ref name="Levis Ones To Watch">{{cite web|url=http://eu.levi.com/en_GB/lotw/|title=Levi's Jeans, Jackets & Clothing - Official Levi's® Site|website=Levi.com}}</ref> The band headlined the first night with support from Sky Larkin and an interview and highlights from the show were broadcast on [[Channel 4]] on 9 October 2008.<ref name="Levis Ones To Watch"/>

Bombay Bicycle Club's debut album ''[[I Had the Blues But I Shook Them Loose]]'' was recorded between late October and late November 2008 at Konk Studios in London. The album was produced by Jim Abbiss. The band also played the Levi's Ones To Watch tour at the end of October, which included dates in Brighton, London, Birmingham, Liverpool, and Glasgow.

At the end of 2008, Bombay Bicycle Club signed a deal with Island Records to release future singles and albums with the record label. All releases were to be published through the Mmm...Records/Island Records offprint, the first release being the single "[[Always Like This]]". The single was released in April 2009 and reached #97 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref name="Chart">{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/5501/bombay-bicycle-club/|title=Bombay Bicycle Club full Official Chart history|publisher=Official Charts Company|access-date=6 September 2019}}
* "Dust on the Ground": {{cite web|first=Tobias |last=Zywietz |url=http://www.zobbel.de/cluk/CLUK_B.HTM |title=Chart Log UK 1994–2010 Darren B – David Byrne |website=Zobbel.de |access-date=6 September 2019}}</ref> The band toured throughout April in support of the release.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/news/bombay-bicycle-club/42957|title=NME News Bombay Bicycle Club announce UK tour |date=23 February 2009|website=NME}}</ref>

===''Flaws'' (2010–11)===
On 9 May 2010, Bombay Bicycle Club released the track "[[Ivy & Gold]]" in the United Kingdom as a [[music download|digital download]]. The single, which was released as a double A-part with the track "Flaws" served as the lead single from their second studio album; ''Flaws''. Having been added to [[BBC Radio 1]]'s A Playlist, the single debuted on the [[UK Singles Chart|singles chart]] at number 97, climbing to number 56 the following week; marking the band's most successful single to date.

The band released ''[[Flaws (album)|Flaws]]'' in the United Kingdom on 12 July 2010; where it debuted on the [[UK Albums Chart]] at number 8;<ref name="Chart"/> beating the debut's peak of number 46. The album included 11 acoustically recorded tracks, including "Swansea"; a cover of the [[Joanna Newsom]] song that appears on her album ''[[The Milk-Eyed Mender]]'' and "Fairytale Lullaby"; a cover of the [[John Martyn]] song that appears on the album ''[[London Conversation]]''. On 10 December, the album was certified ''Silver'' by the [[British Phonographic Industry]] for selling over 60,000 copies.<ref name="Certifications">{{cite web|url=https://www.bpi.co.uk/brit-certified/|title=BRIT Certified - bpi|publisher=[[British Phonographic Industry]]|access-date=22 October 2018|format=To access, enter the search parameter "Bombay Bicycle Club and select "Search by Keyword"}}</ref>

On 20 September, the band released the second single "Rinse Me Down" as a music download and 7" vinyl alongside a track entitled "Dorcas", which served as the second A-side.<ref name="RinseMeDown">[https://www.amazon.co.uk/Rinse-Me-Down-Dorcas/dp/B0041XG8ZI/ref=dm_ap_alb7/ Bombay Bicycle Club - 'Rinse Me Down / Dorcas'] [[Amazon.com|Amazon]]. Retrieved 2011-01-27.</ref> The single managed to reach Radio 1's B Playlist, although failed to chart in the UK.

On 19 April 2011 ''Flaws'' was nominated for the 2011 [[Ivor Novello Award]] for best album.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ivor Novello Nominations|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/6music/news/20110419_ivors.shtml|publisher=BBC|access-date=18 January 2020}}</ref>

===''A Different Kind of Fix'' (2011–12)===
In September 2010 the band began working on their third studio album,<ref name="Third Album">Bombay Bicycle Club - Third Album - NME Magazine - Issue: 15 January 2011. Retrieved 2011-01-27.</ref> returning to electric guitars following acoustic recordings for ''[[Flaws (album)|Flaws]]''. On 7 June 2011, Zane Lowe revealed on BBC Radio 1 that their new album would be called ''A Different Kind of Fix''. He also said that he would be playing a new song from the album, entitled "[[Shuffle (Bombay Bicycle Club song)|Shuffle]]", on 22 June before its release as a single the following day. The single reached #69 in the charts. The album was released on 29 August 2011 and included 12 tracks.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/preorder/a-different-kind-of-fix/id444314509 |title=iTunes Music - A Different Kind of Fix by Bombay Bicycle Club |publisher=iTunes Store |date=26 August 2011 |access-date=2012-04-03}}</ref> "Beg" rounded off the album as its final release on 9 July 2012. The track failed to make the original physical version of the album but was available as a bonus track on the digital version.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://musiceyz.co.uk/2012/05/24/review-of-beg-by-bombay-bicycle-club/ |title=Music Eyz Beg Review |website=Musiceyz.co.uk |date=24 May 2012 |access-date=24 May 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130827071752/http://musiceyz.co.uk/2012/05/24/review-of-beg-by-bombay-bicycle-club/ |archive-date=27 August 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> [[Lucy Rose]] sang on ''A Different Kind of Fix'', but the band recruited Amber Wilson instead for their 2012 tour due to Lucy's solo project. Louis Bhose, a keyboardist also joined the group.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.yorkpress.co.uk/leisure/music/9647487.bombay-bicycle-club-york-barbican-april-19-sold-out/|title=Bombay Bicycle Club, York Barbican, 19 April, sold out|website=York Press}}</ref>

Bombay Bicycle Club won the Best New Band award at the [[NME Awards|2010 NME Awards]] on 24 February. In June 2010, their song "How Can You Swallow So Much Sleep" was included as a bonus track on ''[[The Twilight Saga: Eclipse (soundtrack)|The Twilight Saga: Eclipse]]'' soundtrack.

In 2011, a portrait of lead singer Jack Steadman painted by British artist [[Joe Simpson (artist)|Joe Simpson]] was exhibited around the UK, including a solo exhibition at [[The Royal Albert Hall]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/photos/musician-portraits-joe-simpson-s-paintings-of-rock-stars-1405918|title=Musician Portraits - Joe Simpson's paintings of rock stars &#124; NME|date=14 October 2011}}</ref>

''[[A Different Kind of Fix]]'' was released in the UK on 26 August 2011 and in the US on 17 January 2012.

Bombay Bicycle Club were among the bands chosen to perform at the BT London Live concert in Hyde Park, which marked the end of the 2012 London Summer Olympics.

===''So Long, See You Tomorrow'' (2013–2016)===
On 4 November 2013, the band aired "Carry Me", the first single from their next album that was yet to be named, on [[BBC Radio 1]]. On the same night, an interactive video was released, which allowed viewers to manipulate the band members' actions. The album was released on 3 February 2014 and was followed by a tour. The new sounds of the album were influenced by Steadman's travels to the Netherlands, Turkey, and India.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/reviews/bombay-bicycle-club/15056|title=NME Reviews - Bombay Bicycle Club - So Long, See You Tomorrow |date=29 January 2014|website=NME}}</ref> On 4 December 2013, the band announced that the new album would be called ''So Long, See You Tomorrow'', with album artwork designed by La Boca.

On 10 December, "It's Alright Now" was aired on BBC Radio 1.

On 6 January 2014, the band aired the album's third single, "Luna", on the [[Zane Lowe]] show, on BBC Radio 1. The single features solo artist [[Rae Morris]] and was accompanied by a music video. It also featured as soundtrack in [[Konami]] game, ''[[Pro Evolution Soccer 2015]]''. The track also appeared in ''[[Forza Horizon 2]]''.

On 3 February 2014. ''[[So Long, See You Tomorrow (album)|So Long, See You Tomorrow]]'' was released.

Their 2014 tour finished on 13 December at [[Earl's Court Exhibition Centre|Earls Court Arena]], this concert being the final event ever to take place there before its demolition. [[Pink Floyd]] member [[David Gilmour]] played with the band on their song "Rinse Me Down" before a performance of Pink Floyd's ''[[Wish You Were Here (Pink Floyd song)|Wish You Were Here]]''.

On 29 January 2016, the band announced an indefinite hiatus, citing that "after ten years of doing this we thought it was time for all of us to try something else", with Steadman and Nash intending to record solo material.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.itsallindie.com/2016/01/jack-steadman-is-recording-solo-album.html|title=It's All Indie :: Jack Steadman is recording a solo album|website=Itsallindie.com|access-date=2016-07-01}}</ref>

===''Everything Else Has Gone Wrong'' (2019–present)===
On 14 January 2019, the band announced<ref name=autogenerated1 /> their return to making music and performing together, ending a three-year hiatus.

On 27 August 2019, the band released "Eat, Sleep, Wake (Nothing but You)" as the first song from their fifth studio album ''[[Everything Else Has Gone Wrong]]'', which was released on 17 January 2020. A single with the same name of the album ("Everything Else Has Gone Wrong") was released on 27 November 2019. On 19 December 2019, the band released the album's third single, "Racing Stripes".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/bombay-bicycle-clubs-racing-stripes-2589715|title=Bombay Bicycle Club release airy new single 'Racing Stripes'|date=19 December 2019}}</ref>

On 24 July 2020, the band released an EP titled ''Two Lives''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Brimstin |first=Chelsea |date=24 July 2020 |title=Stream: Bombay Bicycle Club releases acoustic 'Two Lives' EP |url=https://indie88.com/stream-bombay-bicycle-club-releases-acoustic-two-lives-ep/ |access-date=10 August 2020 |website=[[Indie88]]}}</ref> The EP features three acoustic versions of songs from ''Everything Else Has Gone Wrong'' and a cover version of Bonnie Raitt's "[[Sweet Forgiveness|Two Lives]]". It was recorded primarily at Steadman's house in quarantine during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. The band is donating all proceeds from the EP to the charities [[Chineke! Foundation]] and Youth Music.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Moore |first=Sam |date=12 June 2020 |title=Listen to Bombay Bicycle Club's 'Let You Go' from new 'Two Lives' EP |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/bombay-bicycle-club-two-lives-ep-let-you-go-2687103 |access-date=10 August 2020 |website=[[NME]]}}</ref> The EP was preceded by the singles "Let You Go (Acoustic)",<ref>{{Cite web |last=Kenneally |first=Cerys |date=12 June 2020 |title=Bombay Bicycle Club announce new EP with acoustic version of 'Let You Go' |url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/bombay-bicycle-club-announce-new-ep-with-acoustic-version-of-let-you-go |access-date=10 August 2020 |website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]}}</ref> "Racing Stripes (Acoustic)"<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 June 2020 |title=Bombay Bicycle Club release acoustic recording of 'Racing Stripes' |url=https://diymag.com/2020/06/26/listen-bombay-bicycle-club-racing-stripes |access-date=10 August 2020 |website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]}}</ref> and "Two Lives".<ref>{{Cite web |last=Lavin |first=Will |date=11 July 2020 |title=Listen to Bombay Bicycle Club's shimmering cover of Bonnie Raitt's 'Two Lives' |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/bombay-bicycle-club-cover-bonnie-raitt-two-lives-2705710 |access-date=10 August 2020 |website=[[NME]]}}</ref>
