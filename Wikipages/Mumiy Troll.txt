'''Mumiy Troll''' ({{lang-ru|Му́мий Тро́лль}} {{IPA-ru|ˈmumʲɪj ˈtrolʲ|}}) is a [[Russia]]n [[rock music|rock group]], founded in [[1983 in music|1983]] in [[Vladivostok]] by vocalist and songwriter [[Ilya Lagutenko]] ({{Lang-ru|Илья Лагутенко}}). The band's name translates as "mummy troll" and is a pun on ''[[Moomins|Mumintroll]]'', the children's books series by [[Tove Jansson]].<ref name="Davidson">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p653084|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography: Mumiy Troll|last=Davidson|first=Neil|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=6 June 2010}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Castillo|first=Arielle|date=2010-08-19|title=Mumiy Troll, Russian independent rock band, plays two-night stand in Miami Beach|url=https://www.miaminewtimes.com/music/mumiy-troll-russian-independent-rock-band-plays-two-night-stand-in-miami-beach-6367507|access-date=2021-02-10|website=Miami New Times}}</ref>

==Career==
Ilya Lagutenko founded Mumiy Troll in Vladivostok on 16 October 1983.<ref name="forbes" /> In 1985, the group recorded their first album, ''Novaya luna aprelya'', which was distributed as ''[[magnitizdat]]''.<ref name="ria">{{cite web|url=http://ria.ru/spravka/20131114/976864469.html|title=Рок-группа "Мумий Тролль"|date=14 November 2013|publisher=[[RIA Novosti]]|language=ru|access-date=23 February 2016}}</ref> 

Mumiy Troll disbanded when Lagutenko was conscripted into the Russian navy.<ref name="wsj">{{cite news |last1=Walzer |first1=Robert P. |title=A Russian Band Looks for a Home Abroad |work=[[Wall Street Journal]] |date=2012-04-26}}</ref> In 1990, they briefly reunited and released their second album, ''Delay Yu-Yu'', on tape.<ref name="ria"/> Having studied Chinese and English at the Oriental Studies Institute of the [[Far Eastern Federal University]], Lagutenko worked in China and London from 1991 to 1995.<ref name="forbes" /><ref name="variety">{{cite news |last1=Holdsworth |first1=Nick |title=Russian rock band takes on America |url=https://variety.com/2009/music/markets-festivals/russian-rock-band-takes-on-america-1118011002/ |access-date=2021-02-09 |work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=2009-11-07}}</ref> In 1995, he returned to Russia and reformed the band.<ref name="variety" />

In May 1997, Mumiy Troll released their first studio album, ''Morskaya'' ({{Lang-ru|«Морская»|lit=Marine}}), which brought them wide popularity.<ref name="ria" /><ref name="forbes"/> Six months later, they released their second studio album, ''Ikra'' ({{Lang-ru|«Икра»|lit=Caviar}}).
In 1998, [[MTV (Russian TV channel)|MTV]] began broadcasting in Russia and Mumiy Troll's music video for the song "Vladivostok 2000" was the first music video by a Russian artist shown on the channel.<ref>{{cite web |title=MTV в России |url=http://www.mtv.ru/about/ |website=[[MTV (Russian TV channel)|MTV]] |access-date=2021-02-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130116141936/http://www.mtv.ru/about/ |archive-date=2013-01-16 |language=ru |url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2001, the group represented Russia in the [[Eurovision Song Contest]] and came in 12th place.<ref name="ria"/><ref name="forbes">{{cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Мумий Тролль|url=http://www.forbes.ru/profile/-mumii-troll|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304135444/https://www.forbes.ru/profile/-mumii-troll|archive-date=2016-03-04|access-date=24 February 2016|work=[[Forbes]]|language=ru}}</ref> ''Time Out'' ([[London]]) wrote that they 'stole the show' by standing out as snakeskin heroes amongst all the taffeta and tuxedos.{{Verify source|date=February 2021}}

The group wrote and produced soundtracks for a full-length cartoon movie and gave a new sound to classic Russian silent sci-fi movie and donated tracks to feature films including Russian blockbuster ''[[Night Watch (2004 film)|Night Watch]]'', where Lagutenko also plays the Vampire. His face is on the cover of the American release.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}

Their 2005 album ''Sliyaniye i pogloshcheniye'' ({{Lang-ru|Слияние и Поглощение|lit=Merger and Acquisition}}) was called back from the printers on the day of release when distributors realized that the bride and groom on the cover were wearing masks of Putin and [[Mikhail Khodorkovsky]]. The band changed them into symbols of hearts and [[United States dollars]] respectively.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}
In 2008, the band signed with the Agency Group.<ref name="wsj" /> In 2009, they released the album ''Comrade Ambassador'', their first album to be made commercially available in North America, and toured North America to promote it.<ref name="variety" /> In 2012, Mumiy Troll released their first English-language album, ''Vladivostok''.<ref name="wsj" />

In 2013, they released the album ''SOS matrosu'' ({{Lang-ru|SOS матросу|lit=SOS to the Sailor}}), written during the band's round-the-world trip on a 19-century sailing ship in locations.<ref name="ria" /><ref>{{Cite web|date=2013-07-20|title=Барк "Седов" завершает кругосветку с группой "Мумий Тролль" на борту|url=https://ria.ru/20130720/950980491.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-09|publisher=[[RIA Novosti]]|language=ru}}</ref> 

In 2015, they released album ''Piratskie kopii'' ({{Lang-ru|Пиратские копии|lit=Pirate Copies}}) and in 2016, the English-language album ''Malibu Alibi.''

In 2011, the first Mumiy Troll Music Bar was opened in Vladivostok.<ref>{{Cite web|title=About|url=https://mumiytrollbar.com/en/about|access-date=2021-02-09|website=Mumiy Troll Music Bar}}</ref> In 2015, Lagutenko opened a second Mumiy Troll Music Bar in Moscow.<ref name="forbes" />

The group has participated in various international festivals and music conferences, including [[SXSW]], Culture Collide, [[Zandari Festa]], Mu:Con, Sound City, and Visual Japan Summit.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}} In 2013, they founded their own showcase festival "Vladivostok Rocks" (V-ROX).<ref>{{Cite news|last=Ryzik|first=Melena|date=2013-08-28|title=East by Far East: Vladivostok Rocks (Published 2013)|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/08/29/arts/music/east-by-far-east-vladivostok-rocks.html|access-date=2021-02-05|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> The festival was dubbed "a small Pacific Woodstock" by [[Russia Beyond The Headlines]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Shenkman|first=Yan|date=2013-09-01|title=V-ROX: A small Pacific Woodstock|url=https://www.rbth.com/arts/2013/09/01/v-rox_a_small_pacific_woodstock_29397.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-05|website=[[Russia Beyond The Headlines]]|language=en-US}}</ref> V-ROX was held annually from 2013 to 2019. The 2020 edition of the festival was postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2020-05-05|title=V-ROX 2020 = V-ROX 2021|url=http://vrox.org/en/news/2014/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-05|website=V-ROX}}</ref> 

Mumiy Troll was the first to support the activities of PSI organization fighting AIDS in Russia and performed at the No Name Fever exhibition for AIDS in [[Gothenburg]], [[Sweden]] in 2005. They are also well known for conservation activities on wild life in Far-East taiga and helping local minorities to survive. Ilya Lagutenko is a patron of the British-Russian Amur fund for the protection of Amur tigers and leopards.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}

== Musical style ==
Lagutenko lived in London in the 1990s and was influenced by the [[Britpop]] scene at the time.<ref>{{Cite web|title="Questions and Answers: Ilya Lagutenko" — Evil Monito Magazine|url=http://www.mumiytroll.com/mtinpress/1477|access-date=2021-02-10|website=Мумий Тролль}}</ref><ref name="Davidson" /> Lagutenko has described the band's style as "rockapops."<ref name="wsj" />
