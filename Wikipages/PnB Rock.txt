'''Rakim Hasheem Allen''' (born December 9, 1991), better known by his stage name '''PnB Rock''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter from [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kelsie Smith|url=https://schedule.sxsw.com/2019/speakers/2007812|access-date=August 5, 2020|website=SXSW 2020 Schedule|language=en}}</ref> He is best known for his 2015 single "Fleek" and 2016 single "[[Selfish (PnB Rock song)|Selfish]]", which peaked at number 51 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. He has released two studio albums; ''[[Catch These Vibes]]'' (2017) and ''[[Trapstar Turnt Popstar]]'' (2019). In 2017, Allen was chosen as part of the [[XXL Freshman List|''XXL'' Freshman Class]].

==Early life==
Rakim Hasheem Allen was born on December 9, 1991,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/pnb-rock/profile/|title=PnB Rock – Profile|publisher=HotNewHipHop|access-date=January 9, 2017}}</ref><ref>[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/pnb-rock-mn0003449221 PnB Rock], [[AllMusic]]</ref> in the [[Germantown, Philadelphia|Germantown]] neighborhood of [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania]]. Allen's father was murdered when Allen was 3 years old, so Allen was primarily raised by his mother. In his teen years, he lived in [[Northeast Philadelphia]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url= https://globalgrind.cassiuslife.com/4168432/pnb-rock-discusses-his-criminal-past-being-named-after-hip-hop-legend-rakim-the-philly-curse |title= PnB Rock Discusses His Criminal Past, Being Named after Hip-Hop Legend Rakim, & The Philly Curse. He was also related to XXXTENTACION and he shows this in the music video, "MIDDLE CHILD" |date= April 25, 2016|work=Global Grind |access-date= July 2, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> He grew up listening to rapper [[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]] and R&B group [[Jodeci]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2015/08/break-presents-pnb-rock |title= The Break Presents: PnB Rock |work=XXL |date= August 15, 2015 |access-date= January 9, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Bio">{{cite web |url= http://www.pnbrockmusic.com/bio |title= Bio – PnB Rock |work= Atlantic Records |access-date= January 9, 2017 |url-status= bot: unknown |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170110014800/http://www.pnbrockmusic.com/bio |archive-date= January 10, 2017 }}</ref> At the age of 13, Allen was sent to a youth detention program for committing robberies and fighting in school. When he turned 19, he was sentenced to 33 months in prison for drug possession and other crimes. Allen was homeless for a short period after being released from prison.<ref>{{Cite news |url= http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.43285/title.pnb-rock-explains-journey-from-homelessness-to-hot-100# |title= PnB Rock Explains Journey From Homelessness To Hot 100 & Credits Instagram For His Success|last=http://hiphopdx.com|first=HipHopDX -|work=HipHopDX|access-date=July 2, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> He never finished high school.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/06/23/pnb-rock-interview-philadelphia-selfish|title=Meet PnB Rock, The Philly Prince Who Will Steal Your Girl|work=The Fader|access-date=January 9, 2017}}</ref> Allen later adopted the stage name PnB Rock, which pays homage to Pastorius and Baynton, a street corner near where he grew up in the Philadelphia neighborhood [[Germantown, Philadelphia|Germantown]].<ref name=":1" />

==Career==
On June 24, 2014, PnB Rock released his debut mixtape, ''Real N*gga Bangaz''.  He wrote the mixtape while he was incarcerated.<ref name="Bio"/> In 2015, PnB Rock signed a record deal with [[Atlantic Records]], and under the label, he released his first project with Atlantic titled ''RnB3'' which is his third mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/pnb-rock-rnb-3-new-mixtape.116105.html|title=PnB Rock – RnB 3 – Download And Stream|work=HotNewHipHop|date=November 3, 2015|access-date=January 8, 2017}}</ref> In June 2016, he released the hit single, "[[Selfish (PnB Rock song)|Selfish]]". The song peaked at number 51 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="hot100">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7545862/pnb-rock/chart?f=379|title=PnB Rock – Chart History: Billboard Hot 100|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|access-date=January 8, 2017}}</ref> In October 2016, ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' included him in their list of "10 New Artists You Need to Know".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/pictures/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-october-2016-w445291|title=10 New Artists You Need to Know: October 2016|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=October 21, 2016|access-date=January 17, 2017}}</ref>

On January 10, 2017, he released his second retail mixtape album, ''[[GTTM: Goin Thru the Motions]]'' through Atlantic Records and [[Empire Distribution]]. The album debuted at number 28 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7545862/pnb-rock/chart?f=305|title=PnB Rock – Chart history|work=Billboard|access-date=September 8, 2017}}</ref> Subsequently, in April 2017, he participated in the [[The Fate of the Furious: The Album|soundtrack]] of ''[[The Fate of the Furious]]'' with two singles: "[[Gang Up]]" with [[Young Thug]], [[2 Chainz]] and [[Wiz Khalifa]] and "[[Horses (song)|Horses]]" with [[Kodak Black]] and [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]]. In June 2017, PnB Rock was named as one of the ten of ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL's]]'' "2017 Freshman Class" along with A Boogie wit da Hoodie, [[Playboi Carti]], [[Ugly God]], [[Kyle (rapper)|Kyle]], [[Aminé]], [[MadeinTYO]], [[Kamaiyah]], [[Kap G]], and [[XXXTentacion]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/2017-xxl-freshman-cover/|title=XXL Freshman 2017 Cover Revealed|publisher=XXL|date=June 13, 2017|access-date=June 13, 2017}}</ref>

PnB Rock has also admitted that his single, "Nowadays", was co-written by Bronx rapper, Carlos, who has featured PnB on his summer hit, "Let's Get Personal". His debut album ''[[TrapStar Turnt PopStar]]'' was released in May 2019.

PnB Rock is featured on [[Ed Sheeran]]'s "[[Cross Me]]", which is a part of Sheeran's album ''[[No.6 Collaborations Project]]'', along with [[Chance the Rapper]].

In January 2020, PnB Rock released a track titled "Ordinary" featuring late rapper [[Pop Smoke]] and also went on to feature another late rapper in January 2021, [[King Von]], in a song titled "Rose Gold".
