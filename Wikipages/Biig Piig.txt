'''Jessica Smyth,''' known by the stage name '''Biig Piig,''' is an Irish singer and rapper, based in West London. She performs in English and Spanish and is signed to [[RCA Records]].

==Early life==
Smyth was born in Cork, Ireland, but lived in [[Spain]] from the age of four to twelve,<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.readdork.com/features/biig-piig-interview-nov19 |title= Biig Piig: Finding a home in music |website=Dork |last= Freyaldenhoven |first=Laura |date=20 November 2019 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> after her family had moved on advice that the climate would be better for her brother's asthma.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://diymag.com/2019/12/10/class-of-2020-biig-piig |title= CLASS OF 2020: BIIG PIIG |website=DIY |last=Williams |first=Jenessa |date=10 December 2019 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> Upon moving with her family back to Ireland, she was at first able to read and write only in Spanish, and had to reintegrate into Irish culture. After living in Waterford and then Kerry, she moved with her family to West London at the age of 14<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.readdork.com/features/biig-piig-interview-nov19 |title= Biig Piig: Finding a home in music |website=Dork |last= Freyaldenhoven |first=Laura |date=20 November 2019 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> where Smyth worked as a late-night poker dealer,<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.loudandquiet.com/interview/behind-biig-piigs-minimal-songwriting-is-an-ex-poker-dealer-embracing-a-messy-life/ |title= Behind Biig Piig’s minimal songwriting is an ex-poker dealer embracing her own messy world |website=Loud and quiet |last=Cochrane |first=Greg |date=12 September 2017 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> and where her parents continue to run a pub.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/oct/12/biig-piig-jess-smyth-one-to-watch-cork-hip-hop-soul-vocals |title= One to watch: Biig Piig |website=The Guardian |last=Joshi |first=Tara |date=12 October 2019 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref>

In her first six months in London, prior to starting school, Smyth began writing folk-style songs on guitar and ukulele,<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2018/05/10/biig-piig/ |title= Biig Piig |website=Wonderland |last= Cahill |first=Ryan |date=10 May 2018 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> influenced by acoustic balladeers like Lewis Watson and Ben Harper.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.loudandquiet.com/interview/behind-biig-piigs-minimal-songwriting-is-an-ex-poker-dealer-embracing-a-messy-life/ |title= Behind Biig Piig’s minimal songwriting is an ex-poker dealer embracing her own messy world |website=Loud and quiet |last=Cochrane |first=Greg |date=12 September 2017 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> While taking a music technology class at Richmond College, Smyth met Lava La Rue, the founder and Creative Director<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.goldenplec.com/featured/biig-piig-plec-picks-2019/ |title= BIIG PIIG|website=Golden Plec |last= Byrne |first=Stephen |date=28 January 2019 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> of NiNE8, a collective of like-minded young DIY musical artists.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/39611/1/biig-piig-interview |title= Biig Piig: Finding a home in music |website=Dazed |last= Bulut |first=Selim |date=6 April 2018 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> Smyth subsequently joined NiNE8, and during this period, performed at Soho open mics, finding a new voice at a freestyling session in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/new-artist-discovery-biig-piig-is-the-dreamy |title= Biig Piig: Finding a home in music |website=[[Ones to Watch]] |last= Ramos |first=Chuck |date=28 August 2018 ==Career==
In 2016 Smyth began uploading her music to Soundcloud. In 2017 her single "Vice City" caught the attention of digital music platform COLORS, who invited her to record a live video session of the song at their Berlin studios. Released on 13 April, it was a breakthrough for her, and has been viewed over seven million times on YouTube<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4-M3-Y7_I54 |title= Biig Piig - Vice City - A COLORS SHOW |website=YouTube |date=13 April 2017 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> and streamed more than five million times on [[Spotify]]. She followed it up with the singles "24K" and "Perdida". The latter gained her further notice and has netted more than eleven million Spotify streams.

On 27 April 2018 she released the first of a trilogy of projects, the five-track EP ''Big Fan of the Sesh, Vol 1''.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://diymag.com/2019/12/10/class-of-2020-biig-piig |title= CLASS OF 2020: BIIG PIIG |website=DIY |last=Williams |first=Jenessa |date=10 December 2019 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> Its sequel, ''A World Without Snooze, Vol 2'', followed on 22 March 2019. In June of that year she signed to [[RCA Records]].,<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://diymag.com/2019/10/21/biig-piig-neu-october-2019-interview |title= INTRODUCING BIIG PIIG |website=DIY |last=Kerwick |first=Sean |date=21 October 2019 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> releasing the track "Roses and Gold" 2 October 2019 in advance of a headlining European tour<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://diymag.com/2019/10/02/biig-piig-new-track-roses-and-gold |title= BIIG PIIG SHARES HAZY NEW TRACK ‘ROSES AND GOLD’ |website=DIY |date=2 October 2019 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> and her first official label release "Sunny",<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://crackmagazine.net/article/long-reads/biig-piig-together-apart/ |title= Biig Piig: Together, apart |website=Crack Magazine |last=Samways |first=Gemma |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> produced by Zach Nahome.

The third in her trilogy, ''No Place For Patience, Vol. 3'', her first EP on RCA, was released in November 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/biig-piig-showcases-her-versatility-and-dreamlike |title= Biig Piig Showcases Her Versatility and Dreamlike Vocals in ‘No Place for Patience, Vol. 3’ |website=[[Ones to Watch]] |last=Rudas |first=Matthew |date=4 November 2019 |accessdate=26 February 2020}}</ref>

==Stage name==
Smyth found the pseudonym 'Biig Piig' on a pizza menu.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/interviews/london-rapper-biig-piig-got-her-name-from-a-pizza-menu-and-embodies-the-spi |title= On The Rise: Biig Piig |website=The Line of Best Fit |last=Biddles |first=Claire |date=15 March 2018 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref> She has explained that the name 'puts no pressure on me to be a certain way – I can be a mess, and I can also be cute and put together.'<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/39611/1/biig-piig-interview |title= Biig Piig: Finding a home in music |website=Dazed |last= Bulut |first=Selim |date=6 April 2018 |accessdate=25 February 2020}}</ref>
