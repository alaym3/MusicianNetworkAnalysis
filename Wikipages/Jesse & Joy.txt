'''Jesse & Joy''' ({{IPA-es|ˈɟʝesi i ˈʝoj}}) is a [[Mexico|Mexican]] [[Latin pop music|pop]] [[Duet (music)|duo]] formed in 2005 by brother and sister '''Jesse''' (born December 31, 1982, as Jesse Eduardo Huerta Uecke<ref name=sacm>{{cite web|url=http://www.sacm.org.mx/archivos/biografias.asp?txtSocio=29631&offset=20|title=Biografía de Jesse|publisher=Sociedad de Autores y Compositores de México|access-date=February 13, 2013|language=es|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131225111650/http://www.sacm.org.mx/archivos/biografias.asp?txtSocio=29631&offset=20|archive-date=December 25, 2013|df=mdy-all}}</ref>) and '''Joy''' (born June 20, 1986,<ref name=sacm2>{{cite web|url=http://www.sacm.org.mx/archivos/biografias.asp?txtSocio=29630|title=Biografía de Joy|publisher=Sociedad de Autores y Compositores de México|access-date=February 13, 2013|language=es|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140308064753/http://www.sacm.org.mx/archivos/biografias.asp?txtSocio=29630|archive-date=March 8, 2014|df=mdy-all}}</ref> as Tirzah Joy Huerta Uecke<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.trademarkia.com/company-huerta-uecke-tirzah-joy-3137420-page-1-2|title=Huerta Uecke, Tirzah Joy|publisher=Trademarkia.com. Trademarkia, Inc|access-date=February 13, 2013}}</ref>), in [[Mexico City]]. The duo have released five studio albums, one live album and one EP on [[Warner Music|Warner Music Latin]], have toured internationally, and have accumulated a Best Latin Pop Album [[Grammy Award]] and six [[Latin Grammy Award]]s in various categories.

"Jesse & Joy are probably some of the purest, most talented artists of our generation," wrote ''[[Latin Times]]''.<ref name="Latin Times" />

==Early life and career==

The Huerta Uecke siblings were born in [[Mexico City]] to a Mexican father and American mother.<ref name="Huff" /> In 2001, inspired by their parents' love for classic, rock and folk music, when they were 18 and 15 years old, respectively, they began writing music and songs with their father using instruments from his church, which they attended when they were small.

Jesse & Joy signed with the [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music Latina]] group on April 18, 2005.<ref name=sacm/> Jesse plays piano and guitar, while Joy plays guitar, drums and is the lead vocalist.<ref name="NPR">{{cite news |last1=Kahn |first1=Carrie |title=Mexican-American Duo Jesse & Joy Advocate For Migrants In The U.S. |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/altlatino/2018/06/27/623313792/mexican-american-duo-jesse-joy-advocate-for-migrants-in-the-u-s |access-date=7 July 2019 |work=NPR |date=June 27, 2018}}</ref>

===''Esta Es Mi Vida''===
Introduced by the duo Sin Bandera at the 2005 Mexican [[Teletón (Mexico)|Teletón]],<ref name="Billboard 4">{{cite news |title=Latin Grammy Best New Artist Winners: Where Are They Now? |url=https://www.billboard.com/photos/6762398/latin-grammy-best-new-artist-winners-where-are-they-now |access-date=7 July 2019 |work=Billboard |date=November 17, 2015}}</ref> Jesse & Joy released their debut album ''[[Esta Es Mi Vida]]'' in 2006 through [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music Mexico]]. Their debut single, "Espacio Sideral", was certified gold by the [[Asociación Mexicana de Productores de Fonogramas y Videogramas]] (AMPROFON). The album reached the top twenty on the Mexican Album Chart, was certified platinum by the AMPROFON, and helped the duo to win the [[Latin Grammy Award]] for [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] the next year.<ref name="billboard.com">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/grammys/7686244/jesse-joy-win-first-grammy-dedicate-hispanics|title=Jesse & Joy Win First Grammy, Dedicate Award to 'All Hispanics Out There'|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>

===''Esto Es Lo Que Soy'' EP, ''Electricidad''===
In 2008, the duo released the ''[[Esto Es Lo Que Soy]]'' [[Extended play|EP]] featuring "Espacio Sideral", "Ya No Quiero", "Volveré,” "Llegaste Tú", and a new song called "Esto Es Lo Que Soy.

In 2009, their second studio album, ''[[Electricidad]],'' was released. The album was certified gold by the AMPROFON and included singles "Adiós” and "Chocolate".

===''¿Con Quién Se Queda El Perro?''===
''[[¿Con Quién Se Queda El Perro?]]'' was released as their third studio album in 2011. The album was certified 2× platinum, and included singles "[[Me Voy (Jesse & Joy song)|Me Voy]]", "[[¡Corre!]]" and "[[La de la Mala Suerte]]". It received six Latin Grammy Awards and five nominations, as well as the group's first Grammy Award nomination for Best Latin Pop Album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.univision.com/miami/las-gotitas-de-amor-de-jesse-joy|title=Las gotitas de amor de Jesse & Joy|website=www.univision.com|language=es|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>

At the [[Latin Grammy Awards of 2012|2012 Latin Grammy Awards]], Jesse & Joy were nominated for four Grammy Awards, winning three of them: [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Pop Vocal Album|Best Contemporary Pop Album]] for ''¿Con Quién Se Queda El Perro?'', and [[Latin Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song]] and [[Latin Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]] for "¡Corre!". Additionally, together with Carlos López Estrada they received the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Short Form Music Video]] for directing "Me Voy". On December 5, 2012, ''¿Con Quién Se Queda El Perro?'' received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album|Best Latin Pop Album]].<ref name="grammy2013">{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/nominees?genre=17|title=55th Annual Grammy Awards Nominees|date=December 5, 2012|publisher=[[National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences]]|access-date=December 5, 2012}}</ref>

===''Un Besito Más'', ''Jesse & Joy''===
[[File:Jesse_&_Joy_Querétaro_2013.jpg|thumb|left|250px|Jesse and Joy in 2013.]]
At the end of 2015, the pop duo released their fourth studio album titled “[[Un Besito Más]] ,”<ref name="Billboard 3">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/6708226/jesse-joy-new-album-un-besito-mas-exclusive-interview|title=Jesse & Joy Reveal New Album Title, Release Date & Heartfelt Juan Luis Guerra Duet: Exclusive|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref> which went to Number One on the [[Billboard Top Latin Albums|Latin Albums Chart]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/6812874/jesse-joy-first-number-1-top-latin-albums-chart|title=Jesse & Joy Earn First No. 1 on Top Latin Albums Chart|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref> The group toured the United States, Latin America, and Spain to support the album,<ref name="ReferenceA">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7496141/jesse-joy-us-tour-dates|title=Jesse & Joy Announce U.S. Tour Dates|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref> as well as solidifying their base with shows in Latin America. The album, produced by [[Fraser T Smith]] and [[Martin Terefe]] ([[Jason Mraz]], [[James Morrison (singer)|James Morrison]], and [[Shawn Mendes]]), included the song, "Un Besito Mas", which Jesse & Joy wrote after their father's death and is depicted in the video highlighting the US immigration issues.<ref name="Rolling Stone" />

''Un Besito Mas'' earned four [[Latin Grammy Award|Latin GRAMMY®]] Nominations<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-awards-latingrammys-nominations/jesse-joy-djavan-and-fonseca-lead-latin-grammy-nominations-idUSKCN11R2R3|title=Jesse & Joy, Djavan and Fonseca lead Latin Grammy nominations|date=2016-09-21|work=Reuters|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref> (in the categories of [[Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the year]], [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]], [[Latin Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] and [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Pop Vocal Album|Best Contemporary Pop Album]]). That year, Jesse & Jay performed as part of the Latin Recording Academy's  “Person of the Year” Gala honoring [[Roberto Carlos]].<ref name="Fox News">{{cite news |title=On Latin Grammy's eve, Brazilian singer Roberto Carlos honored as 'Person of the Year' |url=https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/on-latin-grammys-eve-brazilian-singer-roberto-carlos-honored-as-person-of-the-year |access-date=7 July 2019 |work=Fox News Channel |date=November 18, 2015}}</ref> The album, ''Un Besito Mas'' went on to win the Latin Grammy (2016) for [[Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album|Best Pop Vocal Album]].<ref name="Los40">{{cite news |title=JESSE & JOY GANAN PREMIO GRAMMY A MEJOR ÁLBUM LATINO |url=https://los40.com.mx/los40/2017/02/13/musica/1486957708_074347.html |access-date=20 August 2019 |work=los40.com}}</ref> 

The release, shortly after, of their first-ever English songs, "Echoes of Love" and "[[More than Amigos|More Than Amigos]]", extended the band's reach to the [[United Kingdom]] when the songs were playlisted on [[BBC Radio 1]].<ref name="AZ Republic">{{cite news |last1=Cordova |first1=Randy |title=Jesse & Joy cross boundaries, blend languages, in concert 11/19 in Phoenix |url=https://www.azcentral.com/story/entertainment/music/2016/11/14/jesse-joy-grupo-duo-latin-pop-music/93295948/ |access-date=20 August 2019 |work=Arizona Republic |date=November 14, 2016}}</ref>

[[The Independent]] in the UK described album opener "Echoes of Love" as “true to their Latin/folk roots while introducing distinctly [[Adele]]-style epicness to the vocals”.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/exclusive-latin-america-s-biggest-band-jesse-and-joys-new-english-language-music-video-a7061536.html|title=Latin America's biggest band Jesse and Joy's new music video|date=2016-06-02|work=The Independent|access-date=2018-02-20|language=en-GB}}</ref>

In mid-2016, Jesse & Joy gained mainstream attention with the release of their first dual-language album, ''Jesse & Joy'' which contained English versions of songs from their past four studio releases and biggest hits such as “Corre! (Run!)”,  “[[Esta Es Mi Vida|Espacio Sideral]] (Outer Space)", "Ecos de Amor (Echoes of Love)”,<ref name="Huff">{{cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.in/entry/jesse-y-joy-un-besito-mas-deportation_us_596916cbe4b03389bb173f72|title=Mexican-American Duo's New Music Video Captures Tragic Effects Of Deportation|date=2017-07-17|work=Huffington Post India|access-date=2018-02-20|language=en-IN}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7438885/jesse-joy-new-english-tracks-echoes-of-love-run-listen|title=Jesse & Joy Tease New English Tracks 'Echoes of Love' & 'Run': Listen|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref> among others. The English/Spanish album was a logical step for the siblings, as Jesse later said: "I was blessed to be raised in a bi-cultural home. It showed me, from a young age, the beauty and power that lies in diversity, and working with my sister every day I experience the power that lies in unity: ‘Together we are stronger.’ All it takes is a little more time to understand and learn from one another."<ref name="Rolling Stone">{{cite news |last1=Exposito |first1=Suzy |title=Karol G, Jesse and Joy, Crazy Legs Honored at 2018 Hispanic Heritage Awards |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/karol-g-jesse-joy-crazy-legs-hispanic-heritage-awards-2018-731051/ |access-date=6 July 2019 |work=Rolling Stone |date=September 29, 2018}}</ref>

At the 2017 American Grammy Award ceremony they received further mainstream recognition with their first-ever Grammy Award for [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album|Best Latin Pop Album]] honoring ''Un Besito Más'', and in Joy's acceptance speech she dedicated the achievement to "all the Hispanics out there."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vibe.com/2017/02/jesse-joy-celebrate-first-ever-grammy/|title=Jesse & Joy Celebrate First Grammy With Message Of Love|date=2017-02-14|work=Vibe|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2012/11/16/jesse--joy-take-4-latin-grammys-juanes-wins-too/1708703/|title=Jesse & Joy take 4 Latin Grammys, Juanes wins too|work=USA TODAY|access-date=2018-02-20|language=en}}</ref><ref name="billboard.com"/>

Jesse & Joy rounded out 2017 with appearances at the [[Latin American Music Award]]s,<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/883628/2017-latin-american-music-awards-performers-announced-chris-brown-prince-royce-more-stars|title=2017 Latin American Music Awards Performers Announced|work=E! Online|access-date=2018-02-20|language=en-US}}</ref> and at the Latin Grammy's "Person of the Year Gala" honoring [[Alejandro Sanz]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8039175/alejandro-sanz-latin-grammys-person-of-the-year-honoree|title=Alejandro Sanz Honored by His Peers During Person of the Year Ceremony: 'Don't Forget That We Are What We Dream'|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7556706/gente-de-zona-jesse-joy-latin-grammys-marc-anthony|title=Gente de Zona, Jesse & Joy and More to Perform at Latin Grammys' Person of the Year Gala|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>

With their live ''Un Besito Más'' Tour, Jesse & Joy visited more than 15 countries and nearly 100 cities, including [[Buenos Aires]], [[Madrid]], [[Barcelona]], [[Santiago|Santiago de Chile]], [[New York City|New York]] and [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="ReferenceA"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7832721/marco-antonio-solis-announces-us-tour-dates-camila-jesse-joy|title=Marco Antonio Solis Announces U.S. Tour With Camila And Jesse & Joy: See Dates & Venues|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref>

In 2018, Jesse & Joy embarked on another international tour, beginning at [[Chile]]’s [[Viña del Mar International Song Festival|Viña Del Mar Festival]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.publimetro.cl/cl/entretenimiento/2017/10/02/jesse-joy-vina-monstruo-amor.html|title=Jesse & Joy: "Viña es un monstruo de amor"|work=Publimetro Chile|access-date=2018-02-20|language=es-ES}}</ref> followed by the band’s homecoming to [[Mexico City]]'s Arena Ciudad de Mexico.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://gritaradio.com/jesse-joy-regresan-a-casa-12-de-mayo-2018-arena-ciudad-de-mexico/|title=¡¡¡¡JESSE & JOY REGRESAN A CASA!!! 12 DE MAYO 2018 ARENA CIUDAD DE MÉXICO - GritaRadio|date=2017-11-22|work=GritaRadio|access-date=2018-02-20|language=es-MX|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180825220044/http://gritaradio.com/jesse-joy-regresan-a-casa-12-de-mayo-2018-arena-ciudad-de-mexico/|archive-date=August 25, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.telehit.com/noticias/jesse-joy-anuncian-fecha-en-arena-ciudad-de-mexico/|title=Jesse & Joy anuncian fecha en Arena Ciudad de México -|last=Loaiza|first=Carolina|date=2017-11-21|access-date=2018-02-20|language=es-ES}}</ref>[[File:Jesse Joy Concierto 2012.jpg|thumbnail|Jesse & Joy in concert in 2012]]

The duo received the 2018 Vision Award that September at the 31st Annual Hispanic Heritage Awards, which aired on [[PBS]] that month. The award recognizes Latinos with major contributions in music.<ref name="Rolling Stone" />

===Upcoming fifth studio album: ''Aire''===
The two artists signed a deal with Kobalt Music for their worldwide publishing beginning with their 2018 single, "Te Esperé".<ref name="Billboard 2">{{cite news |last1=Ockenfels III |first1=Frank W. |title=Jesse & Joy Sign Worldwide Agreement with Kobalt Music |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8486952/jesse-joy-kobalt-music-publishing-worldwide-agreement |access-date=7 July 2019 |work=Billboard |date=November 28, 2018}}</ref>

In April 2019, Jesse & Joy collaborated with Colombian singer [[J Balvin]] for the single, "Mañana es Too Late.” The song was written by the duo, along with [[Poo Bear]] (who has worked with [[David Guetta]], [[Matoma]], [[Justin Bieber]], [[Mariah Carey]], [[Skrillex]], [[Juanes]], [[Jennifer Lopez]], [[Fifth Harmony]]) and produced by Jesse and [[Charlie Heat]] ([[Kanye West]], [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]]).<ref name="Billboard">{{cite news |last1=Ockenfels |first1=Frank |title=Plus: Morat & Aitana release catchy pop song, Diana Fuentes launches new album. |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8506964/viva-friday-playlist-jesse-joy-j-balvin |access-date=7 July 2019 |work=Billboard |date=April 12, 2019}}</ref> "Mañana es Too Late" reached #1 in Mexico.<ref name="La Revista">{{cite news |last1=Feijoo |first1=Bolivar |title=JESSE & JOY triunfan con otro sold out en Auditorio Nacional |url=http://wowlarevista.com/jesse-joy-triunfan-con-otro-sold-out-en-auditorio-nacional/ |access-date=18 July 2019 |work=La Revista |date=June 6, 2019}}</ref>

Throughout May 2019, Jesse & Joy toured Mexico including another sold-out performance at Auditorio Nacional.<ref name="Publimetro">{{cite news |last1=Acosta |first1=Gabriela |title=Jesse & Joy hacen de cada álbum una historia de vida |url=https://www.publimetro.com.mx/mx/entretenimiento/2019/05/02/jesse-joy-album-una-historia-vida.html |access-date=17 July 2019 |work=Publimetro |date=May 2, 2019}}</ref>
 
Joy was recognized by the Latin Recording Academy as one at the Leading Ladies of Entertainment at a gala June 2019 that took place in Mexico City. The honor, for Latinas who "during their lives have demonstrated enthusiasm, determination, leadership, pride in their roots and an indomitable spirit in continuous form,"<ref name="El Universal">{{cite news |last1=Escobar |first1=Elizabeth |title=Reconocen empoderamiento de Martha Debayle, Joy Huerta y Alondra de la Parra |url=https://www.eluniversal.com.mx/espectaculos/musica/reconocen-empoderamiento-de-martha-debayle-joy-huerta-y-alondra-de-la-parra |access-date=7 July 2019 |work=El Universal |date=June 20, 2019}}</ref> that included Tatiana Bilboa, Martha de Bayle, [[Alondra de la Parra]], and Soumaya Slim Domit.<ref name="El Heraldo">{{cite news |title=La Acadamia Latina reconce a Joy Huerta y Alondra de la Perra |url=https://www.elheraldo.hn/entretenimiento/1294602-466/la-academia-latina-reconoce-a-joy-huerta-y-alondra-de-la-parra |access-date=7 July 2019 |work=El Heraldo |publisher=Associated Press |date=June 19, 2019}}</ref>

Jesse & Joy continue to tour in 2019, with shows in Latin American and the United States.<ref name="Chi Trib AP">{{cite news |title=Los Dells recibirán a Ozuna, Carlos Vives y Café Tacvba |url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/hoy/entretenimiento/ct-hoy-el-programa-de-los-dells-2019-20190710-y777wrzwszdmras33hgybmzwau-story.html |work=Chicago Tribune |publisher=AP Spanish |date=July 10, 2019}}</ref><ref name="El Universo">{{cite news |title=Jesse & Joy y Ha*Ash cantarán en Guayaquil |url=https://www.eluniverso.com/entretenimiento/2019/05/30/nota/7352475/jesse-joy-haash-cantaran-guayaquil |access-date=18 July 2019 |work=El Universo |date=May 30, 2019}}</ref><ref name="La Nacion">{{cite news |last1=Rojas |first1=Jessica |title=El dúo Jesse & Joy volverá a cantar en Costa Rica |url=https://www.nacion.com/viva/musica/el-duo-jesse-joy-volvera-a-cantar-en-costa-rica/7BB3KAFSWVF37KQO3OIKZ37YEA/story/ |access-date=18 July 2019 |work=La Nacion |date=June 3, 2019}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
Jesse & Joy maintain an ongoing partnership with [[UnidosUS]] (formerly NCLR), the Hispanic civil rights and advocacy organization, and their “Electrify The Vote!". They performed acoustic shows in California, Texas, and Arizona intended to mobilize Latinos to vote.<ref name="Pollstara">{{cite news |last1=Borba |first1=Ryan |title=Jesse & Joy To 'Electrify The Vote' With Southwest U.S. Dates |url=https://www.pollstar.com/article/jesse-joy-to-electrify-the-vote-with-southwest-us-dates-136304 |access-date=7 July 2019 |work=Pollstar |date=September 14, 2018}}</ref> In addition, the musicians initiated personal recovery efforts following [[earthquake]]s in [[Mexico]].<ref name="Latin Times">{{Cite news|url=http://www.latintimes.com/jesse-joy-honor-victims-natural-disasters-latin-amas-break-down-tears-426182|title=Jesse & Joy Honor Victims Of Natural Disasters At Latin AMAs, Break Down In Tears|date=2017-10-27|work=Latin Times|access-date=2018-02-20|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://people.com/chica/eva-longoria-global-gift-gala-2017-benefited-mexico-earthquake-victims/|title=Eva Longoria Gets Help from Celeb Friends to Aid Mexico's Earthquake Victims|work=PEOPLE.com|access-date=2018-02-20|language=en}}</ref>
