'''Xenia Rubinos''' (born July 24, 1985) is an [[United States|American]] singer, songwriter and multi-instrumentalist. 

== Background and early life ==
Xenia Rubinos was born in [[Hartford, CT]] in 1985 to a [[Puerto Rico|Puerto Rican]] mother and a [[Cuba]]n father.<ref name="Guard">{{cite web|url= https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/oct/24/xenia-rubinos-black-terry-cat-identity |work=The Guardian|date=October 24, 2016|title= Xenia Rubinos: ‘I'm saying things about being a brown girl in America' |first=Laura|last=Snapes}}</ref> She studied [[jazz]] composition at the [[Berklee College of Music]].<ref name="PBS Art">{{cite web|url=http://www.pbs.org/video/xenia-rubinos-ocwtr4/| title = Articulate: Xenia Rubinos | work = PBS }}</ref> She spent most of her 20s acting as the primary caregiver for her father as he dealt with a degenerative illness, which inspired her song "Black Stars."<ref name= "npr2016" >{{cite web|url= https://www.npr.org/2016/07/03/483262449/in-black-stars-xenia-rubinos-memorializes-many-lives-in-one |work = NPR | date = July 3, 2016 |title = In 'Black Stars,' Xenia Rubinos Memorializes Many Lives In One }}</ref> She has lived in Brooklyn since 2006.

== Career ==
Her album ''Black Terry Cat'' was released to critical acclaim and was named the 11th best album of 2016 by [[NPR]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=5 December 2016|title=NPR Music: Best 50 Albums of 2016|url=https://apps.npr.org/best-music-2016/list/top-albums/|url-status=live|website=NPR}}</ref>

== Music ==
Rubinos' early music influences include composers like [[Prokofiev]] and [[Ravel]],<ref name="Voice">{{cite web|url = https://www.villagevoice.com/2016/05/31/unboxed-genre-hopping-with-xenia-rubinos/ |work = The Village Voice | date = May 31, 2016 | title = Unboxed: Genre-Hopping with Xenia Rubinos | first = Isabela | last = Raygoza}}</ref> as her father was a fan of classical music and opera.<ref name="npr2016" />  [[Salsa music|Salsa]], [[Cuban rumba|rumba]] and [[merengue music|merengue]], including releases by [[Fania Records]], were popular in her house while growing up.<ref name="Voice" /><ref>{{cite web |last1=Brown |first1=Helen |title=Xenia Rubinos, Black Terry Cat, review: 'the sound of the summer' |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/what-to-listen-to/xenia-rubinos-black-terry-cat-review-the-sound-of-the-summer/ |website=The Telegraph |access-date=May 2, 2019 |date=June 17, 2016}}</ref> Later, she became enthralled with [[hip-hop]], [[R&B]] and [[Miles Davis]] in particular, which led her to study jazz at the Berklee College of Music.

She is inspired by her [[Latin American culture|Latin American]] heritage and [[Santería]] practices.<ref name = "npr2013" /> She is also inspired by the [[Black Lives Matter]] movement,<ref name="npr2016" /> and discusses her experiences as a woman of color in her songs, but she sees her music as broader than the category of [[Protest song|protest music]].<ref name="Guard" />

Rubinos' music is not easily categorized, as she crosses many genres in both her lyrics and her sound.<ref name="Guard" /><ref name= "npr2013">{{cite web | url=https://www.npr.org/2013/04/28/179281913/xenia-rubinos-adventures-in-syncopation| work=NPR | title = Xenia Rubinos: Adventures in Syncopation | date = April 23, 2013 | first = Rachel | last = Martin }}</ref><ref name="Toronto">{{cite web|url=http://www.pbs.org/video/xenia-rubinos-ocwtr4/ |work=Now Toronto|date=July 13, 2016|title= Album Review Black Terry Cat |first=Chaka|last=Grier}}</ref><ref name="Pitchfork">{{cite web| url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/18119-xenia-rubinos-magic-trix/|work=Pitchfork |title = Magic Trix Review | date = May 24, 2013 | first=Laura|last=Snapes}}</ref>
