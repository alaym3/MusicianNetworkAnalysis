'''Joseline Hernandez''' (born November 3, 1986), also known as "The Puerto Rican Princess",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/news/love-and-hip-hop-atlantas-joseline-hernandez-sizes-up-her-castmates-w211241|title=Love and Hip Hop: Atlanta's Joseline Hernandez Sizes Up Her Castmates – Us Weekly|work=US Weekly|date=June 27, 2016}}</ref> is a [[Puerto Ricans|Puerto Rican]] reality television personality, rapper, and actress. She came to prominence as a main cast member of the [[VH1]] reality series ''[[Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta]]''. She appeared on the show for six seasons alongside her boyfriend [[Stevie J]], with whom she also starred in the spin-off ''[[Stevie J & Joseline: Go Hollywood]]''.

== Early life ==
Joseline Hernandez was born on November 3, 1986, in [[Ponce, Puerto Rico]],<ref name="Source15">{{cite web|url=http://www.latina.com/entertainment/celebrity/joseline-hernandez-facts-bio|title=5 Things You Never Knew About Joseline Hernandez|last=Fernandez|first=Celia|date=November 3, 2015|work=[[Latina (magazine)|Latina]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180228123752/http://www.latina.com/entertainment/celebrity/joseline-hernandez-facts-bio|archive-date=February 28, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="Source3">{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2013/07/22/joseline-hernandez-spits-about-her-boricua-culture/|title=Joseline Hernandez Spits About Her Boricua Culture|last=TheLadyLana|date=July 22, 2013|work=[[The Source]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141116182409/http://thesource.com/2013/07/22/joseline-hernandez-spits-about-her-boricua-culture/|archive-date=November 16, 2014|url-status=live|access-date=September 4, 2018}}</ref> and grew up in the [[Public housing in Puerto Rico|public housing system of Puerto Rico]].<ref name="Source2">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5DYHEfKK3O0|title=The Joseline You Don't Know|date=May 3, 2017|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=September 4, 2018}}</ref> Her biological father died from a [[heroin]] overdose. She moved to [[Florida]] at the age of 6, along with her mother Carmen, stepfather Luis, her older sister Yanira, and her four brothers, Jorge, Hisael, Kermit and Luis Jr.<ref name="Source2" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/311553/joseline-hernandezs-family-sister-yanira-abneris-brothers-kermit-jorge-hisael-luis/|title=Who's Who? Get to Know Joseline Hernandez's Family Including Yanira, Abneris, and Brother Kermit|access-date=July 26, 2018}}</ref> Her youngest brother has [[autism]] and requires special care.<ref name="globalgrind">{{cite web|url=http://globalgrind.com/1862685/joseline-hernandez-reveals-troubled-past-snever-tweet-naked-picture-again-interview/|title=Joseline Hernandez Reveals Her Troubled Past|work=GlobalGrind|date=July 16, 2012}}</ref> From the age of 16, she began [[stripper|stripping]] in order to provide for her family.<ref name="globalgrind" /> During this time, she was arrested in 2003 and 2007 under the name Shenellica Bettencourt, for [[lewd]] and [[lascivious behavior]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://starcasm.net/archives/235783|title=Joseline Hernandez mugshot photos as Shenellica Bettencourt, arrested for lewd behavior in 2003 and 2007|work=Starcasm|date=August 2, 2013}}</ref>

=== Breakthrough, ''Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta'' (2012–2017)===
Hernandez was discovered by [[Grammy Award]]-winning record producer [[Stevie J]] while performing as a [[stripper]] at the Onyx Club in [[Atlanta]].<ref name="globalgrind" /> In 2012, she became part of the original cast of ''[[Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta]]'', appearing as Stevie J's new artist, an aspiring rapper, singer and actress. It was later revealed that the two were romantically involved, much to the anger of his girlfriend, [[Mimi Faust]]. The trio's love triangle became ''Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta'''s leading storyline and Hernandez continued as a main cast member for six seasons. It became the highest rated show of the ''[[Love & Hip Hop]]'' franchise and is one of the highest rating shows on VH1 overall, averaging 3.5&nbsp;million viewers per episode.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/133011/love-hip-hop-atlanta-season-3-ratings/ |title=5.6M Fans Tuned in to Love & Hip Hop Atlanta's premiere|work=VH1}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/network-press-releases/vh1s-love-hip-hop-atlanta-ranks-as-the-summers-number-1-cable-reality-series-in-adults-18-49-women-18-49/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161220163035/http://tvbythenumbers.zap2it.com/network-press-releases/vh1s-love-hip-hop-atlanta-ranks-as-the-summers-number-1-cable-reality-series-in-adults-18-49-women-18-49/|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-12-20|title=VH1's 'Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta' Ranks as the Summer's Number 1 Cable Reality Series in Adults 18–49 & Women 18–49 |work=Zap2it.com}}</ref>

The show's success and notoriety catapulted Hernandez to stardom, leading to appearances at the 2013 ''AVN Awards'', the 2013 ''[[2013 BET Hip Hop Awards|BET Hip Hop Awards]]'' and the 2015 ''[[BET Awards 2015|BET Awards]]'', as well as guest spots on ''[[This Is Hot 97]]'', ''[[K. Michelle: My Life]]'', ''[[The Wendy Williams Show]]'' and ''[[Hip Hop Squares]]''. She also made several cameo appearances with Stevie in various [[hip hop]] and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] videos, including the music videos for [[Trey Songz]]'s "[[Chapter V (Trey Songz album)|Hail Mary]]", [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]]'s "[[Jesus Piece (album)|I Remember]]", [[Ciara]]'s "[[Body Party]]" and [[Faith Evans]]' "[[I Deserve It]]".

During the course of the show, Hernandez released a series of [[reggaeton]] and [[dancehall]]-influenced singles and music videos, many of which are sung and spoken in Spanish, her native tongue. She performed her songs "Church" and "Stingy with My Kutty Katt" live at ''Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta'''s fourth season reunion. She also appeared on [[Rocko (rapper)|Rocko]]'s mixtape ''Wordplay 2'' on the track "Girls Gone Wild" (with [[Young Dro]]). In 2016, Hernandez starred with Stevie in the spin-off show ''[[Stevie J & Joseline: Go Hollywood]]'', which premiered to 2.6&nbsp;million viewers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bossip.com/1275742/bossip-exclusive-stevie-j-and-joseline-go-hollywood-debuts-to-a-whopping-2-6-million-viewers-on-vh1/ |title=Stevie J and Joseline Go Hollywood debuts at 2.6&nbsp;million viewers|work=Bossip|date=January 27, 2016}}</ref> In 2017, she starred in her own television special, ''Joseline's Special Delivery'', which premiered on [[VH1]] on May 1, 2017, and documented the birth of her child.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/292369/joseline-hernandez-baby-girl-name-bonnie/|title=Joseline Hernandez Reveals Baby Girl Will Be Named Bonnie|work=VH1 News}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BQF4GhJAqRu/|title=Joseline on Instagram: "#specialdelivery #ExecutiveProducer #JoselineHernandez #MayFirst #Vh1 I cant wait for everyone to see my very first executive producer…"|website=Instagram|access-date=July 26, 2018}}</ref>

On June 5, 2016, Hernandez revealed that she had filmed an acting role on [[Lee Daniels]]' television series ''[[Star (TV series)|Star]]'', which aired from December 14, 2016, on [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/262272/joseline-hernandez-lee-daniels-star-fox-series-queen-latifah/|title=Joseline Hernandez Discusses Filming Lee Daniels' New Series|work=VH1}}</ref> She appeared in the pilot episode as Michelle, a stripper. She returned in a recurring role for three more episodes in 2017. Hernandez appeared as a guest co-host on the [[Broadcast syndication|syndicated]] talk show ''[[The Real (TV series)|The Real]]'' from January 23 to 27, 2017, and again from May 1 to 5, 2017. On June 1, 2017, Hernandez quit ''Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta'' during the taping of the sixth season's reunion, amid tensions with creator [[Mona Scott-Young]] and the show's producers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.intouchweekly.com/posts/joseline-hernandez-love-hip-hop-atlanta-mona-scott-young-133801/photos/joseline-hernandez-stevie-j7-246389|title=Joseline Hernandez Leaves 'Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta' Amid Feud With Mona Scott-Young|date=June 2, 2017|access-date=July 26, 2018}}</ref>

On August 6, 2017, Hernandez released a preview of the song "[[Run Me My Money]]" on her [[Instagram]] account, along with a tag #MONAFEA ("fea" meaning "ugly" in Spanish). It was immediately alleged that "Run Me My Money" is a [[Diss (music)|diss record]] aimed at Scott-Young.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bossip.com/1576739/hip-hop-no-love-joseline-hernandez-drops-a-new-track-that-sounds-a-lot-like-a-mona-scott-diss/|title=Hip Hop & No Love: Joseline Hernandez Drops A New Track That Sounds A Lot Like A Mona Scott Diss|date=August 6, 2017|website=[[Bossip]]|access-date=October 14, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hip-hopvibe.com/2017/08/06/joseline-hernandez-run-me-my-money-mona-scott-young-diss/|title=Joseline Hernandez – "Run Me My Money" (Mona Scott-Young diss)|date=August 6, 2017|access-date=July 26, 2018}}</ref> Hernandez commented "The show people, let's just get this very clear, all them bitches that go through ''Love & Hip Hop'', they work for me. I get royalties on all them asses, so that was the inspiration." However, she did not directly confirm whether she was taking aim at Scott-Young.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphollywood.com/2017/08/joseline-hernandez-reveals-who-shes-talking-about-on-new-track-run-me-my-money/|title=Joseline Hernandez Reveals Who She's Talking About On New Track "Run Me My Money"|date=August 10, 2017|website=HipHollywood|access-date=October 14, 2017}}</ref>

[[The Rap Game|Miss Mulatto]]). On October 8, 2017, she released "Run Me My Money" in full as a [[Digital download (music)|digital download]]. ==Personal life==
Hernandez is openly [[bisexual]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.advocate.com/arts-entertainment/television/2012/08/04/love-and-hip-hop-star-comes-out-bisexual|title=Love and Hip Hop Star Comes Out as Bisexual - Advocate.com|work=The Advocate|date=August 4, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vladtv.com/article/165325/joseline-on-bisexuality-calling-stevie-j-ga|title=EXCLUSIVE: Joseline on Bisexuality & Calling Stevie J "Gay"}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2015/08/love-and-hip-hop-atlanta-recap-season-4-episode-16|title = 'Love and Hip-Hop Atlanta' Recap: "There Isn't Peace in Palace"}}</ref>

She and Stevie initially claimed to have married in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/133417/love-hip-hop-atlanta-stevie-j-joseline-married/|title=Are Stevie J + Joseline Married or Not? – VH1|work=VH1}}</ref> However, Stevie J admitted in 2016 that the marriage was faked for publicity<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rickeysmileymorningshow.com/1647868/stevie-j-joseline-hernandez-never-married/|title=Stevie J Reveals He & Joseline Hernandez Were Never Married|work=The Rickey Smiley Morning Show|date=February 5, 2016}}</ref> and in court documents released later that year, Joseline confirmed they were in a long-term relationship and never legally married.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2016/11/joseline-hernandez-never-married-stevie-j/|title=Joseline Hernandez Says She Was Never Married to Stevie J|work=Vibe|date=November 9, 2016}}</ref> The couple's break up was documented in ''Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta'''s fifth season, although they have reconciled on-and-off since then.

During filming of ''Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta'''s fifth season reunion, Hernandez revealed she was pregnant with Stevie's sixth child. She gave birth to their daughter on December 28, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/292926/stevie-j-and-joseline-are-baby-shower-this-weekend/|title=Stevie J and Joseline are getting along in time for baby Bonnie Bella's baby shower|work=VH1 News}}</ref>

In April 2019, it was reported that Hernandez was dating music producer Robin Ingouma, known professionally as Balistic Beats.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://atlantablackstar.com/2019/07/08/what-report-says-joseline-hernandez-slated-to-appear-on-we-tvs-upcoming-season-15-of-marriage-boot-camp/|title=What?
