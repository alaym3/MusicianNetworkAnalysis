'''Marvin Bernard''' (born March 31, 1978),<ref name="allmusic" /> better known by his stage name '''Tony Yayo''', is an American [[rapper]] and [[hype man]]. He is best known as a member of [[G-Unit]], a [[hip hop]] group he formed with his childhood friends [[50 Cent]] and [[Lloyd Banks]]. Yayo released his debut studio album, ''[[Thoughts of a Predicate Felon]]'', in 2005, which debuted at number 2 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His stage name is derived from the 1983 film ''[[Scarface (1983 film)|Scarface]]'', referencing the main character [[Tony Montana]], and Yayo, the slang word for [[cocaine]].

== Early life ==
Yayo was born Marvin Bernard in [[South Jamaica, Queens]], New York City to [[Haitian Americans|Haitian]] parents, the youngest of three children.<ref>Reid, Shaheem. (January 15, 2010) [http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1629845/20100115/g_unit.jhtml Tony Yayo, DJ Whoo Kid, More Haitian Artists Discuss Earthquake – Music, Celebrity, Artist News]. MTV. Retrieved on 2011-07-31.</ref> Bernard's parents divorced when he was 16. As a child, he met his lifelong friends [[Lloyd Banks]] and [[50 Cent]], and the trio eventually formed a group, [[G-Unit]], and began working on music together, releasing a number of mixtapes in the early 2000s, including 2002's [[50 Cent is the Future]], [[No Mercy, No Fear]], and [[God's Plan (album)|God's Plan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/tony-yayo-mn0000157088|title=Tony Yayo – Biography, Albums, Streaming Links – AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=October 28, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.today.com/popculture/tony-yayo-talks-about-life-g-unit-prison-wbna9258063|title=Tony Yayo talks about life in G-Unit, prison|website=Today.com|access-date=October 28, 2018}}</ref>

== Music career ==
=== G-Unit ===
While Yayo was incarcerated in 2003, [[G-Unit]] boss [[50 Cent]] was granted his own record label, "[[G-Unit Records]]" by [[Interscope]] after the success his debut studio album ''[[Get Rich or Die Tryin' (album)|Get Rich or Die Tryin']]''. 50 immediately signed G-Unit members Yayo and [[Lloyd Banks]] to the label, and eventually [[Young Buck]] was signed and added to the group as the official fourth member. G-Unit released their first official group album ''[[Beg for Mercy]]'' in November 2003, which went on to be certified 4x Platinum in the United States of America.<ref name="Beg for Mercy">Lamy, Jonathan (December 18, 2003). [https://www.riaa.com/newsitem.php?news_year_filter=2003&resultpage=&id=D567432C-1DD7-2B70-27E8-161B2A3E9F22 2003 Ends With a Bang!]. RIAA. Accessed July 10, 2007.</ref><ref>[http://arts.enotes.com/contemporary-musicians/50-cent-biography 50 Cent Biography: Contemporary Musicians] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070527013252/http://arts.enotes.com/contemporary-musicians/50-cent-biography |date=May 27, 2007 }}. eNotes. Accessed July 18, 2007.</ref> The album was released whilst Yayo was in prison serving time on weapons charges, but Yayo was still featured on the album. The second G-Unit album [[T.O.S: Terminate on Sight]] was released in July 2008 and went on to sell 102,000 copies in the first week.

=== Solo career ===
[[File:2005 1104hoopsmania0016.JPG|thumb|right|Tony Yayo performing at Villanova Hoops Mania in 2005]]
Though his appearances on numerous mixtapes during and upon his release from prison announced his return on a street level, it was not until the release of his debut single, "[[So Seductive]]" featuring 50 Cent, that Yayo received widespread recognition.
In August 2005, Tony Yayo released his debut studio album, ''[[Thoughts of a Predicate Felon]]'', debuting at No. 2 on the [[Billboard 200]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tonyyayo.gunitworld.com.com/|title=Search|website=Tonyyayo.gunitworld.com.com|access-date=October 28, 2018}}</ref>

Tony Yayo has confirmed that work is in progress for his second studio album.<ref name=AHH>Steve Raze (November 5, 2007). [http://allhiphop.com/blogs/features/archive/2007/11/05/18815854.aspx Lloyd Banks & Tony Yayo: Shooters, Part 2] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071130060918/http://allhiphop.com/blogs/features/archive/2007/11/05/18815854.aspx |date=November 30, 2007 }}. AllHipHop. Accessed November 6, 2007.</ref> No official name for the album has been decided yet.  The first single from the album is "[[Pass the Patron]]", and it features 50 Cent. The album will be released with [[G-Unit Records]] / [[EMI]] who will both promote and distribute the album.
On March 2, 2011, Yayo released the second single of off his upcoming album called, "[[Haters (Tony Yayo song)|Haters]]". The song features [[Shawty Lo]], [[50 Cent]], & [[Roscoe Dash]]. The single was released onto Thisis50.com after being premiered on Hot 97 by [[Funkmaster Flex]]. The single rapidly started to gain success on the urban radio charts and soon debuted on the [[Billboard R&B chart|Billboard Hip-Hop/R&B chart]].

== Labels ==
Tony Yayo was signed to [[Interscope Records]] in 2003, where he released his debut album ''[[Thoughts of a Predicate Felon]]''. He was later dropped from the label in 2009. [[Lloyd Banks]] announced a deal was signed with [[50 Cent]]'s label, [[G-Unit Records]] on Friday, August 13, 2010, on [[MTV News]], in which [[EMI]] Label Services would distribute and promote releases on the G-Unit roster in North America including Yayo's untitled second album.

== Legal issues ==

=== Felony jail time ===
Along with 50 Cent, Yayo was arrested on December 31, 2002, for weapons possession. He had a [[Glock 20]] handgun and a [[Colt AR-15]] in the trunk of his car. When police discovered that Yayo had an outstanding warrant for a previous charge, Yayo was sentenced for bail jumping and spent all of 2003 in prison.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p555596|pure_url=yes}}|title=Tony Yayo > Biography|last=Jeffries|first=David|year=2005|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 13, 2009}}</ref> Yayo served time in the [[Lakeview Shock Incarceration Correctional Facility]] and was paroled on January 8, 2004.<ref name="NYSDOCS">{{cite web|url=http://nysdocslookup.docs.state.ny.us/kinqw00|title=Inmate Population Information Search|publisher=[[New York State Department of Correctional Services]]|access-date=September 26, 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090921063616/http://nysdocslookup.docs.state.ny.us/kinqw00|archive-date=September 21, 2009}} Department ID Number: 03-A-2339</ref> The next day, he was arrested for possessing a forged passport and served time in federal prison until February 24, 2004.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1485337/tony-yayo-pleads-guilty-to-passport-fraud/|title=Tony Yayo Pleads Guilty To Passport Fraud|last=Patel|first=Joseph|date=February 25, 2004|publisher=[[MTV News]]|access-date=October 14, 2017}}</ref> When Yayo was incarcerated, G-Unit and [[Eminem]] began a campaign called "Free Yayo". Yayo has said he was unaware of all the attention the campaign was getting until he saw Eminem wearing a "Free Yayo" T Shirt on stage at the [[2003 Grammy Awards]].<ref>[http://www.xxlmag.com/magazine/2005/07/what-i-gotta-do/ What I Gotta Do]. Xxlmag.Com. Retrieved on July 31, 2011.</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/tony-yayo-mn0000157088/biography|title=Tony Yayo – Biography & History – AllMusic|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=October 28, 2018}}</ref> The first song Yayo appeared on after being released from prison was "Ain't No Click'" from [[Lloyd Banks]]' debut album ''[[The Hunger For More]]'' on June 2004.

=== Assault charge ===
On March 24, 2007, Tony Yayo was arrested for allegedly assaulting the 14-year-old son of [[Jimmy Henchman|Jimmy "Henchmen" Rosemond]]. Tony Yayo and 50 Cent were accused of approaching the child, with members of their entourage, and pushing him against a nearby wall. This was when Tony Yayo reportedly slapped the boy several times and said "Fuck Czar Entertainment". Tony Yayo has pleaded "not guilty" and 50 Cent has since proven his innocence with video footage showing him at his [[Connecticut]] mansion at the time of the alleged incident.<ref>Rich Rock (March 24, 2007). [http://www.sohh.com/articles/article.php/11224 Tony Yayo Arrested For Teen Assault, Young Buck Release Distracted] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070508215227/http://www.sohh.com/articles/article.php/11224 |date=May 8, 2007 }}. SOHH. Accessed July 29, 2007.</ref>

Tony Yayo made his first court appearance on the morning of April 25, 2007, in New York. The case was adjourned to July 24. Tony Yayo's lawyer has expressed his belief that Yayo is innocent and commented on the situation in an interview with MTV:
{{quote|After a thorough investigation, I steadfastly and adamantly state to you that Tony Yayo did not slap anyone. I am hopeful that when [authorities] complete their investigation, they will come to the same conclusion. Yayo, as a father, understands the allegations and would never do anything to harm a child.<ref>[http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1558104/20070426/50_cent.jhtml Tony Yayo's lawyer Commenting on Alleged Incident]. MTV. Accessed July 29, 2007.</ref>}}

On April 18, 2007, the house of Tony Yayo's mother was "sprayed with bullets". The attack is believed to be in connection with Tony Yayo's house arrest. Yayo references the attack on the G-Unit song "Straight Out of Southside", off ''T.O.S.'', the group's second album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2007/04/20/home-of-tony-yayos-mother-sprayed-with-bullets/ |title=Home Of Tony Yayo's Mother Sprayed With Bullets |website=BallerStatus.com |date=April 20, 2007}}</ref>

On July 24, 2007, Tony Yayo attended a court hearing regarding his alleged assault. The prosecution offered him a nine-month prison sentence if he pleaded guilty which he rejected.<ref>[http://www.sohh.com/articles/article.php/12153 Update: Tony Yayo Rejects Deal In Rosemond Assault Case, "It's Not Even A Good Offer For A Guilty Person," Says Lawyer] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070911145422/http://www.sohh.com/articles/article.php/12153 |date=September 11, 2007 }}. SOHH. Accessed July 29, 2007</ref>

Prosecutors dropped the charges in February 2008.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tony Yayo Cleared Of Child Assault Charges|url=http://www.rapbasement.com/tony-yayo/tony-yayo-cleared-of-child-assault-charges.html|work=Rap Basement|access-date=February 5, 2011|date=February 14, 2008}}</ref> The mother of Rosemond's son sued 50 Cent and Yayo two months later.<ref>{{cite web|last=Rodriguez|first=Jayson|title=50 Cent, Tony Yayo Named In Assault-And-Battery Suit Involving Jimmy Rosemond's Son|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1585097/50-cent-tony-yayo-named-assaultandbattery-civil-suit.jhtml|publisher=[[MTV News]]|access-date=February 5, 2011|date=April 9, 2008}}</ref>
