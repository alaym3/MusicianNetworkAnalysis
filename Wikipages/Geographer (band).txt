'''Geographer''' is the band name for Mike Deni's American [[Synthpop|synth pop]]/[[indie rock]] band based in [[San Francisco]], [[California]], United States.

Formed in 2007, by Deni<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dontpaniconline.com/magazine/music/geographer|title=Geographer: The band maps out their future|last=Mahnty|first=Shannon|date=8 May 2011|work=Don't Panic|access-date=24 February 2013}}</ref> who has described his sound as being "soulful music from outer space" using analog, electronic and acoustic elements to craft dense layers and unique sound textures.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indieshuffle.com/geographer-animal-shapes/|title=Geographer – Animal Shapes – What's So Good?|last=Kahn|first=Jennifer|date=20 August 2010|publisher=IndieShuffle|access-date=18 February 2013}}</ref>

As Geographer, Deni has released three albums, ''Innocent Ghosts'' in 2008, ''Myth'' in 2012, and ''Ghost Modern'' in 2015; along with two [[extended play|EPs]], ''Animal Shapes'' in 2010, ''Endless Motion'' in 2015; and a number of [[7-inch single]]s including, "Kites" in 2010.

== History ==
Deni relocated to San Francisco from [[New Jersey]] to escape strife within his family, and after finding a [[synthesizer]] on the street, he began to channel his experiences into the songs that would become ''Innocent Ghosts''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Kish|first=Molly|title=Geographer Interview: Noise complaints, reclaimed synths and courtesy cookies|url=http://showbams.com/2012/10/04/geographer-interview-noise-complaints-reclaimed-synths-and-courtesy-cookies/|publisher=Show Bams|access-date= 4 October 2012}}</ref> He began playing his songs locally in San Francisco at open mic nights—most notably The Hotel Utah, where he met cellist Nathan Blaz and drummer Brian Ostreicher.<ref>{{cite web|last=Johnson-Igra|first=David|title=Interview with Geographer: Q&A with Michael Deni|url=http://www.sfcritic.com/2010/04/19/interview-with-geographer-q-with/394|publisher=SFCritic|access-date= 20 August 2009}}</ref> Blaz and Ostreicher originally met while attending the [[Berklee College of Music]], in [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]], before moving to San Francisco. After being introduced to Deni at an [[open mic|open-mic]] night by mutual friend Kacey Johansing, the two would join Deni's live band.<ref>{{cite web|last=Kuo|first=Fenny|title=Video: Geographer Interview|url=http://www.thebaybridged.com/2009/08/20/video-geographer-interview-and-age-of-consent-new-order-cover/|publisher=The Bay Bridged|access-date= 19 April 2010}}</ref>

On August 17, 2008, Deni released ''Innocent Ghosts'' independently and the band began playing around the [[San Francisco Bay Area]]. The release garnered local and national attention with ''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]'' magazine voting the band "''[o]''ne of the three undiscovered bands you need to hear now".<ref>{{cite web|last=Gaston|first=Peter|title=Three Undoscovered Bands You Need To Hear Now|url=https://www.spin.com/2008/10/three-undiscovered-bands-you-need-hear-now/| work = [[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|access-date= 14 October 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=CD of the Month: Geographer – Innocent Ghosts|url=http://thedelimagazine.com/sf/index.php?itemId=204320| work = [[The Deli Magazine]] |access-date= 14 August 2008}}</ref>

He then signed with local Bay Area label Tricycle Records, and in December 2009, released a 7-inch single for the song "Kites" with a cover of the [[English people|English]] rock band [[New Order (band)|New Order]]'s "[[Age of Consent (song)|Age of Consent]]" as the B-side.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://indiemusicfilter.com/listen-age-of-consent-by-geographer-new-order-cover|title=Listen: "Age of Consent" by Geographer (New Order Cover) - Indie Music Filter|date=8 December 2015|website=Indie Music Filter|language=en-US|access-date=18 June 2016}}</ref>

In 2010, he followed up the single with the release of the six-song EP ''Animal Shapes''. The [[digital distribution|digital-download]] version of the record also contained two bonus [[remix]]es of the songs "Kites" and "Paris" by fellow Bay Area artists [[Ricky Reed|Wallpaper]] and The Limousines.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/geographer-kites|title=Geographer, Kites|last=Gillespie|first=Blake|work=Impose Magazine|access-date=27 August 2010}}</ref> The album received favorable reviews, landing the band a "Best of" award by the [[SF Weekly]].<ref>{{cite web| title=Best Local Version of Radiohead – 2010 – Geographer|url=http://www.sfweekly.com/bestof/2010/award/best-local-version-of-radiohead-1983128/ | work = [[SF Weekly]] |access-date= 18 February 2013}}</ref> November 2010 marked the first major tour for the band, supporting [[Stars (Canadian band)|Stars]] on dates across the United States beginning in the Bay Area and ending in Buffalo, NY.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thebaybridged.com/2010/11/08/geographer-tours-the-us-with-stars/|title=Geographer tours the US with Stars|last=Van Houten|first=Ben November|date=8 November 2010|website=The Bay Bridged - San Francisco Bay Area Indie Music|access-date=18 June 2016}}</ref>

In March 2011, the band signed with Modern Art Records, based in [[New York City]], [[New York (state)|New York]].<ref>{{failed verification|date=February 2013|reason=no mention of "march 2011" or even mention of the band signing with the label.}}  [http://modernartrecords.virb.com/geographer "Geographer"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110822084734/http://modernartrecords.virb.com/geographer |date=22 August 2011 }}. Modern Art Records. Retrieved 18 February 2013.</ref>

In December 2011, ''[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]'' magazine released the single "Life of Crime" from forthcoming full-length album ''Myth'', to be released by Modern Art Records.<ref>{{cite web|last=Alwahab|first=Clint|title=Song Premiere: Geographer – 'Life of Crime'|url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/blogs/av/2011/12/song-premiere-geographer---life-of-crime.html| work = [[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |access-date= 20 December 2011}}</ref>

O February 28, 2012, Deni released ''Myth''. In March of the same year, the band embarked on a five-week U.S. tour with labelmate indie-pop band [[Miniature Tigers]].<ref name="auto">(18 January 2012).  [http://consequenceofsound.net/2012/01/consequence-of-sound-presents-the-modern-art-tour/ "Consequence of Sound presents The Modern Art Tour"].  consequenceofsound.com.</ref> On 11 August 2012, the band played a set on the Twin Peaks Stage at the [[Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival]], in San Francisco, the largest audience for which the band had thus far performed.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/08/06/geographer_n_1749669.html |title=Geographer's Mike Deni Talks Long Walks in Noe Valley, The Agony of Open Mic Nights and the Novel He Wrote About Lance Bass |last=Sankin |first=Aaron |work= [[The Huffington Post]] |date=7 August 2012 |access-date= 18 February 2013 |quote = 'We've done smaller stuff like [Live 105's] BFD, but OSL is going to be the biggest festival we've ever played.'}}</ref> The set featured new songs "Life of Crime," "Lover's Game" and "Kites".<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/08/06/geographer_n_1749669.html |title=Geographer's Mike Deni Talks Long Walks in Noe Valley, The Agony of Open Mic Nights and the Novel He Wrote About Lance Bass |last=Sankin |first=Aaron |work= [[The Huffington Post]] |date=7 August 2012 |access-date= 18 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.glidemagazine.com/articles/58746/outside-lands-music-and-arts-festival-saturday.html |title=Show Review: Outside Lands&nbsp;– Day Two: Saturday, August 11, 2012 |last=Zimmerman |first=Peter |work=Glide Magazine |date=12 August 2012 |access-date=18 August 2012}}</ref>

In September 2012, the band announced that it would head out on a 21-date U.S. tour with indie-rock band [[Freelance Whales]]. The tour began 4 October at [[Lincoln Hall Chicago|Lincoln Hall]] in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]], and ended 3 November at the [[Paradise Rock Club]] in Boston.<ref name="auto2">{{cite web|url=http://sf.thedelimagazine.com/11478/geographer-releases-lovers-game-video-tours-freelance-whales |title=Geographer Releases "Lover's Game" Video, Tours with Freelance Whales — San Francisco |website=Sf.thedelimagazine.com |date=2012-10-17 |access-date=2020-03-12}}</ref>

The band embarked on their first headlining tour of the western United States in 2013 with the band [[On An On]]. The tour began 12 January at [[Doug Fir Lounge]] in Portland, OR, and ended 1 February at [[The Echo (venue)|The Echo]] in Los Angeles, California.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.glidemagazine.com/22341/geographer-announce-january-tour-dates-with-on-an-on/|title=Geographer Announce January Tour Dates With On An On - Glide Magazine|date=7 April 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140407093258/http://www.glidemagazine.com/22341/geographer-announce-january-tour-dates-with-on-an-on/|access-date=20 November 2020|archive-date=April 7, 2014}}</ref> The tour was a success for the band, selling out venues across the U.S. including their homecoming show at the historic [[Fillmore West|Fillmore]] on 31 January.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.sfweekly.com/shookdown/2013/02/geographer_comes_home_to_the_f.php|title=Geographer Comes Home to the Fillmore, 1/31/13|last=Weekly|first=SF|website=SF Weekly|access-date=18 June 2016}}</ref> The band continued touring throughout 2013 in the U.S. and Canada with support from [[GRMLN]] and [[Royal Bangs]].

The band announced on its website in March 2014 that they would be supporting [[Tokyo Police Club]] on their North American tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://geographermusic.com/news/205003|title=ON TOUR WITH TOKYO POLICE CLUB|website=Geographermusic.com|access-date=18 June 2016}}</ref> On 20 January 2015 ''[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]'' magazine premiered the single "I'm Ready" and announced his forthcoming full-length LP 'Ghost Modern' to be released in March 2015.<ref>(20 January 2015).  [http://www.pastemagazine.com/blogs/av/2015/01/song-premiere-geographer---im-ready-1.html "Song Premiere: Geographer - "I'm Ready""]. Pastemagazine.com.</ref> Prior to the release of 'Ghost Modern', the band headlined a sold-out show at the [[Fox Oakland Theatre|Fox Theater]] in Oakland, CA as part of the [[Noise Pop Festival]]. The show debuted new songs including "Need" and "I'm Ready" which would appear on the upcoming full-length album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stanforddaily.com/2015/03/06/san-franciscos-geographer-enthralls-at-the-fox-theater/|title=San Francisco's Geographer enthralls at the Fox Theater|last=Groth|first=Gabriela|website=Stanford Daily|access-date=18 June 2016}}</ref>

''Ghost Modern'', Deni's third album was released on 23 March through Roll Call Records. The album featured the previously released single "I'm Ready" as well as recently released singles "Need" and "The Guest". Prior to the release of ''Ghost Modern'', Blaz and Osteicher stepped down as the touring band pursue other projects that prevented them from maintaining the band's rigorous touring schedule.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reddit.com/r/listentothis/comments/317tmf/ama_hi_its_mike_deni_aka_geographer_here_im_ready/ |title=[AMA&#93; Hi, it's Mike Deni (aka Geographer) here. I'm ready for you - Ask Me Anything : listentothis |website=Reddit.com |date=2009-05-06 |access-date=2020-03-12}}</ref> Deni then recruited cellist Joyce Lee, guitarist and bass player Duncan Nielsen and drummer Cody Rhodes as his touring band, headlining shows throughout the United States and SXSW.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://geographermusic.com/news/245453|title=March Tour Recap : Geographer|date=16 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150416233334/http://geographermusic.com/news/245453|access-date=20 November 2020|archive-date=April 16, 2015}}</ref>

Deni released a cover of Paul Simon's "I Know What I Know" through ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' on October 26, 2015, announcing the release of the covers EP ''Endless Motion'', which features additional covers of Kate Bush's "Cloudbusting", New Order's "Age of Consent", Felix da Housecat's "Ready 2 Wear" and Arthur Russell's "This Is How We Walk on the Moon".<ref>(October 26, 2015).  [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6738563/geographer-covers-ep-paul-simon-exclusive "Geographer Announces New Covers EP, Debuts Paul Simon Cover: Exclusive Premiere"]. Billboard.com</ref>

On December 16, 2016, Deni independently released the Geographer single "I Want It All".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://geographermusic.com/news/285953|title=New Track - I Want It All|website=Geographer.com|access-date=14 February 2017|language=en}}</ref>

==Live==
In live performances, Deni is known to sample and loop his vocals, saxophone, and synthesizers using an [[Akai]] [[MIDI controller]]. During performances, the cellist uses an [[Ned Steinberger|NS Designs]] [[electric cello]], often combined with [[effects pedals]] to create unique sounds.<ref>(October 15, 2011).  [http://www.thebaybridged.com/2011/10/15/live-blogging-treasure-island-music-festival-2011-day-1 "Live Blogging Treasure Island Music Festival 2011: Day 1"]. Thebaybridged.com.  Retrieved 18 February 2013.</ref>
