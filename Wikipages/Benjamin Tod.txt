'''Benjamin Tod Flippo<ref name="chicago reader ragged" />''' (born November 11, 1990), is an American singer-songwriter. He is lead singer and guitarist for the '''Lost Dog Street Band''' with his wife Ashley Mae (vocals, fiddle) and Jeff Loops (bass).<ref name="lostdog facebook"/> The band has released several albums through [[crowdfunding]] platforms, with their album ''Weight of a Trigger'' reaching number five on the [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' Bluegrass Albums chart]] in 2019.

== Biography ==
=== Early life ===
Tod grew up in [[Cottontown, Tennessee]]. At the age of fourteen, he was expelled from school.<ref name="rolling stone" /> Tod began hopping freight trains as a way to see the country.<ref name="kosu"/>

=== Lost Dog Street Band ===
After being part of a music group called Barefoot Surrender, Tod and bandmate Ashley Mae formed the Lost Dog Street Band in 2010, named after their yellow Labrador, Daisy. Tod would do guitar and vocals while Mae would play the fiddle.<ref name="do303">https://do303.com/events/2019/7/26/lost-dog-street-band</ref>

Tod and Mae met Nicholas and Shannon Jae Ridout. The two couples formed the street-quartet, Spitshine, and began touring together. In 2013 Nicholas Ridout committed suicide and Tod and Mae rekindled Lost Dog Street Band.<ref name="chicago reader ragged" /><ref name="do303" /> The band has crowdfunded multiple album through [[Indiegogo]] and [[Kickstarter]], as well as self-publishing their music videos on the YouTube channel GemsOnVHS.<ref name="rolling stone"/><ref name="americana highways">{{Cite web |url=https://americanahighways.org/2019/08/17/show-review-lost-dog-street-band-matt-heckler-and-the-tillers/ |title=Show Review: Lost Dog Street Band, Matt Heckler, and the Tillers |date=August 17, 2019 |website=Americana Highways | first=David | last=Nowels | access-date=9 September 2019}}</ref> Lost Dog Street Band toured and released their fifth studio album ''Weight of a Trigger'' in 2019, and in April of that year, the album reached number five on the [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' Bluegrass Album chart]].<ref name="billboard chart" />

=== Solo career ===
In 2017, Tod released the solo album ''I Will Rise'' which was recorded with "two mics and no overdubbing." In early 2019, Tod announced his second solo album, ''A Heart of Gold is Hard to Find'', which was released November 22, 2019.<ref name="ear"/>

== Personal life ==
In 2008, Tod met his wife Ashley Mae when they were 17 and 18 years old living in [[Muhlenberg County]], Kentucky.<ref name="rolling stone" />
