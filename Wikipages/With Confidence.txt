'''With Confidence''' are an Australian [[pop punk]] band from [[Sydney]], formed in 2012. The band currently consists of lead vocalist/bassist Jayden Seeley, guitarist/backing vocalist Inigo Del Carmen, drummer Joshua Brozzesi and guitarist Scotty Mac.

==History==

===Formation, EPs, line-up changes (2012–15)===
With Confidence formed in mid-2012 with Jayden Seeley, Josh Brozzesi, and Samuel Haynes, who had all attended high school together. Shortly thereafter, member Inigo Del Carmen joined, and the four began posting videos on [[YouTube]] where they released their debut single "Stand Again" for free download on 27 April 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Don3-gh0kCE |title=Stand Again - With Confidence FREE DOWNLOAD IN DESCRIPTION |publisher=YouTube |date=27 April 2013 |access-date=2014-05-05}}</ref> The band then released their debut EP ''Youth'' independently on 1 July 2013,<ref name="youthrelease">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/withconfidence/posts/539495136087869?stream_ref=10 |title=With Confidence |publisher=Facebook.com |access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref> and saw reasonable success, receiving national radio-play on Australian stations [[2Day FM]] and [[Triple J]].<ref>{{citation|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/shortfastloud/playlists/s3865861.htm|author=Stu Harvey|title=short.fast.loud playlists|publisher=[[Triple J]]"|date=9 October 2013|access-date=5 May 2014}}</ref> Alongside the ''Youth'' EP release, With Confidence played their first Australian tour supporting The Never Ever.<ref>{{citation|url=http://www.ripitup.com.au/music/article/the-never-ever-lead-2013-hoodie-weather-tour#.U2ZsRNw1fwI|title=THE NEVER EVER LEAD 2013 HOODIE WEATHER TOUR|publisher=Miranda|date=7 May 2013|access-date=5 May 2014}}</ref> The band went on to play local supports for [[5 Seconds of Summer]],<ref name=5sossupport>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/events/444766678890911/permalink/461843140516598/ |title=5 Seconds of Summer - Twenty Twelve Tour |publisher=Facebook.com |date=2012-08-05 |access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref> [[The Getaway Plan]],<ref>{{citation|url=http://www.liveguide.com.au/Events/890230/The_Getaway_Plan_Your_Weight_In_Gold_Thesis_With_Confidence/Hot_Damn_The_Getaway_Plan|title=Hot Damn! The Getaway Plan @ Spectrum|publisher=Live Guide|date=5 September 2013|access-date=5 May 2014}}</ref> and [[The Red Jumpsuit Apparatus]],<ref>{{citation|url=http://www.killyourstereo.com/news/18576/the-red-jumpsuit-apparatus-local-tour-supports-announced/|title=The Red Jumpsuit Apparatus local tour supports announced|publisher=Kane_H|date=27 August 2013|access-date=5 May 2014}}</ref> continuing to build a following.

On 8 January 2014, guitarist Samuel Haynes was announced as no longer being a part of With Confidence.<ref name=memberleaving>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/withconfidence/photos/a.363323990371652.73960.349806188390099/621014121269303/?type=1&stream_ref=10 |title=With Confidence - Hey guys, unfortunately Sam has decided... |publisher=Facebook |date=2014-01-07 |access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref> The band continued writing as a three-piece and on 31 March released the new single and debut music video "I Will Never Wait".<ref>{{citation|url=http://www.killyourstereo.com/news/20946/with-confidence-release-new-music-video/|title=With Confidence release new music video|publisher=Rob Foster|date=31 March 2014|access-date=5 May 2014}}</ref> They supported this release with their first national headline tour in April, selling out multiple shows including Sydney's "The Lair" ([[The Metro Theatre]]).<ref name=lairsellout>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/withconfidence/photos/a.646543448716370.1073741826.349806188390099/667001180003930/?type=1&stream_ref=10 |title=With Confidence |publisher=Facebook.com |access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref> On 3 September, the band returned to four-piece status when previously touring guitarist Luke Rockets was announced as an official member.<ref name=newmember>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/withconfidence/photos/a.646543448716370.1073741826.349806188390099/725089494195098/?type=1 |title=With Confidence |publisher=Facebook.com |access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref> They continued keeping the year busy touring and supporting the likes of [[You Me at Six]], [[Tonight Alive]],<ref name="YMAS Support">{{cite web|url=http://www.the59thsound.com/you-me-at-six--tonight-alive-announce-tour-supports-04092014.html |title=You Me At Six & Tonight Alive announce tour supports (04/09/2014) |publisher=The59thsound.com |access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref> [[Marianas Trench (band)|Marianas Trench]], [[The Maine (band)|The Maine]], and [[Kids in Glass Houses]].<ref name="KIGH Support">{{cite web|url=http://www.tonedeaf.com.au/404989/kids-in-glass-houses-farewell-australian-tour-2014-announced.htm |title=Kids In Glass Houses Farewell Australian Tour 2014 Announced |publisher=Tonedeaf.com.au |access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref> The band released their second EP ''Distance'' on 13 January 2015.<ref>{{citation|url=http://www.killyourstereo.com/news/1045384/with-confidence-announce-tour-release-ep|author=Kane_H|title=Kill Your Stereo!|date=23 January 2015|access-date=20 May 2015}}</ref> This was shortly followed by their second headlining tour, again selling out multiple venues throughout Australia.

=== Hopeless Records, ''Better Weather'' (2016–17) ===
On 4 January 2016 the band announced they had signed to American independent record label [[Hopeless Records]]. They coincided this news with new single and music video "We'll Be Okay".<ref>{{citation|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/with_confidence_sign_to_hopeless_records_to_tour_with_state_champs_and_neck|author=Caitlyn Ralph|title=With Confidence sign to Hopeless Records, to tour with State Champs and Neck Deep|date=4 January 2016|access-date=16 February 2016}}</ref> Following this the band announced an Australian headline tour set to take place in April, with support from Harbours.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://themusic.com.au/news/all/2016/2/18/with-confidence-announce-australian-headline-tour-1455771241/|title=With Confidence Announce Australian Headline Tour|website=theMusic|language=en-GB|access-date=2016-11-29}}</ref> On 5 April they released their new single "Keeper" along with the announcement that their debut album [[Better Weather]] was going to be released on 17 June worldwide. Following their Australian headline tour, the band travelled to Japan to tour with [[The Wonder Years (band)|The Wonder Years]], [[Roam (band)|ROAM]] & Cautioners. Directly after the Japanese tour, With Confidence travelled to the UK to support [[As It Is (band)|As It Is]] alongside American band Jule Vera. After the UK tour the band voyaged across to the US to appear on the entirety of the [[Vans Warped Tour]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/tonight-alive-hearts-wake-lead-aussie-contingent-vans-warped-tour-lineup/|title=Tonight Alive, In Hearts Wake Lead Aussie Contingent On Vans Warped Tour Lineup - Music Feeds|date=2016-03-23|newspaper=Music Feeds|language=en-US|access-date=2016-11-29}}</ref>

On 21 July, the band released a music video for their [[Harry Potter]]-referencing song "Voldemort".<ref>https://www.altpress.com/news/with_confidence_drop_harry_potter_themed_songlisten/</ref> Bassist Jayden Seeley said at the time that "“’Fear of a name only increases fear of the thing itself.’ Voldemort is my personification of mental illness and this song is a big, happy and carefree fuck you to Voldemort.“"<ref>https://www.altpress.com/news/with_confidence_drop_harry_potter_themed_songlisten/</ref> On 17 October, the band announced they were releasing two new versions of the songs. One would be an acoustic version and the other features John Floreani of [[Trophy Eyes]]. The songs were released on 18 October.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://celebmix.com/confidence-release-two-new-versions-voldemort/|title=With Confidence to Release Two New Versions of 'Voldemort' - CelebMix|date=2016-10-17|newspaper=CelebMix|language=en-GB|access-date=2017-04-11}}</ref>

Upon returning to Australia the band were on the road almost immediately again, supporting [[Motion City Soundtrack]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/motion-city-soundtrack-announce-last-ever-australian-tour/|title=Motion City Soundtrack Announce Last Ever Australian Tour - Music Feeds|date=2016-05-16|newspaper=Music Feeds|language=en-US|access-date=2016-11-29}}</ref> & [[5 Seconds of Summer]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://celebmix.com/confidence-support-5sos-australian-leg-tour/|title=With Confidence to support 5SOS on Australian leg of tour - CelebMix|date=2016-08-29|newspaper=CelebMix|language=en-GB|access-date=2016-11-29}}</ref> before heading out on their own album tour in October to celebrate the album that charted at #22 on the ARIA Charts. In December, it was announced they would be a part of [[Kerrang!]]'s American Superhits [[Green Day]] Tribute CD, covering "[[Wake Me Up When September Ends]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.kerrang.com/46362/pre-order-kerrang-green-day-cd-isssue/|title=Pre-Order Kerrang!'s Green Day American Superhits Cover CD + Issue Now!|date=2016-12-02|newspaper=Kerrang!|language=en-GB|access-date=2017-04-11}}</ref>

In February 2017, the band headlined a tour in the UK and Europe, with support from Milestones, [[Broadside (band)|Broadside]], and Safe To Say.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.kerrang.com/46238/confidence-announce-ukeuropean-headline-tour/|title=With Confidence Announce UK/European Headline Tour|date=2016-11-23|newspaper=Kerrang!|language=en-GB|access-date=2017-04-11}}</ref> In April, they embarked on tour in the US to support [[State Champs]], along with [[Don Broco]] and [[Against the Current (band)|Against the Current]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/state_champs_announce_tour_with_against_the_current_with_confidence_and_don|title=State Champs Announce Tour with Against The Current, With Confidence and Don Broco|date=2016-12-14|newspaper=Alternative Press|language=en-US|access-date=2017-04-11}}</ref> In May, they returned to the UK for a co-headlining tour with [[Set It Off (band)|Set It Off]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://teamrock.com/news/2017-03-21/set-it-off-with-confidence-announce-uk-tour|title=Set It Off, With Confidence Announce UK Tour|date=2017-03-21|newspaper=Team Rock|language=en-GB|access-date=2017-04-11}}</ref>

In November 2017, the band put out a statement that they had parted ways with lead guitarist, Luke Rockets, after alleged sexual misconduct. <ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/with_confidence_luke_rockets_sexual_misconduct_allegations|title=With Confidence part ways with guitarist Luke Rockets amidst sexual misconduct allegations|date=2017-11-13|newspaper=Alternative Press|language=en-GB|access-date=2017-11-13}}</ref> <ref>{{cite web|last1=Vidler|first1=piper|title=In light of the recent events|url=https://www.facebook.com/nearlypiper/posts/10208459721608981|website=Facebook|access-date=13 November 2017}}</ref>
The band subsequently dropped the remaining dates of their US tour. Shortly after Rockets was fired from the band, frontman Jayden Seeley responded to allegations made against himself.<ref>{{cite web|title=With Confidence's Jayden Seeley addresses sexual misconduct allegations - News - Alternative Press|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/with_confidence_jayden_seeley_sexual_misconduct_allegations|website=Alternative Press|access-date=2 December 2017|language=en}}</ref> Following these allegations, the band announced that they would be taking "a step back" from touring.<ref name="seeley1">{{cite web|title=With Confidence Loses Festival Spot After Another Sexual Misconduct Claim|url=https://www.pedestrian.tv/news/with-confidence-loses-festival-spot/|website=Pedestrian TV|access-date=2 December 2017|date=16 November 2017}}</ref>

=== Return, ''Love and Loathing'', and line-up change (2018-2021) ===
Following their break from touring With Confidence announced a late February return show at the Chain Reaction in Anaheim, shortly followed by the announcement of their spot on Warped Tour 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/2018-vans-warped-tour-lineup-revealed/|title=2018 VANS WARPED TOUR Lineup Revealed|date=2018-03-01|work=Blabbermouth.net|access-date=2018-07-06}}</ref> They announced their second studio album ''Love and Loathing'' on 11 June shortly before their run on Warped Tour, along with its lead single "That Something".<ref name=":0" /> On 17 July, the band released their second single, "Jaded" along with a music video.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20181206022817/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LhnQGFX2Z2E] </ref>

The album was released on 10 August 2018.

On 26 March 2021, the band announced via Instagram the addition of guitarist Scotty Mac.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-08|title=With Confidence Come Back Strong In New Single • Soundigest|url=https://soundigest.com/2021/04/08/with-confidence-big-cat-judgement-day-review/|access-date=2021-06-21|website=Soundigest|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Style and influences==
[[AllMusic]] biographer Neil Z. Yeung wrote that the band "carries the blink-182 torch of pop-punk."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/with-confidence-mn0003445208/biography|title=With Confidence {{!}} Biography & History|work=AllMusic|author=Yeung, Neil Z. |access-date=16 June 2016}}</ref>

With Confidence have cited influences from bands [[The Dangerous Summer (band)|The Dangerous Summer]], [[Kings of Leon]], [[Blink-182]], [[The Wonder Years (band)|The Wonder Years]], [[The Strokes]], and [[La Dispute]] on their Facebook. Along with [[Blink-182]], [[All Time Low]], [[Red Hot Chili Peppers]], and The Dangerous Summer mentioned in their interview with Beers with the Band on YouTube.<ref name=influences>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/withconfidence/info |title=With Confidence |publisher=Facebook.com |date=2015-01-13 |access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20170719000658/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nHYCbWrSG9o] </ref>

The band uses mental health as theme throughout their debut album, as shown in songs such as "Voldemort".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/with_confidence_drop_harry_potter_themed_songlisten|title=With Confidence drop Harry Potter-referenced song "Voldemort"—listen|date=2016-06-09|work=Alternative Press|access-date=2017-04-17}}</ref>
