'''Mariah Buckles''', known professionally as '''Mariah the Scientist''', is an American singer-songwriter. She is signed to [[RCA Records]] and Canadian rapper [[Tory Lanez]]'s record label [[One Umbrella]]. She released her debut studio album ''Master'', on August 23, 2019.

==Early life==
Mariah Buckles was born in [[Atlanta]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]]. She graduated high school a year early and attended college at St. Johnâ€™s University in [[New York City]] in hopes of becoming an anesthesiologist, but later dropped out to pursue music.<ref name="Bio"/>

==Career==
Mariah discovered singing when she joined her elementary school chorus. She began writing songs while attending [[St. John's University (New York City)|St. John's University]] in New York on a [[biology]] scholarship, which she later dropped out of to concentrate on her music career.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/mariah-the-scientist-interview/ |title=Mariah the Scientist Is an R&B Upstart Making Her Way Through a Dumb Man's World |website=pitchfork |last=Schnipper |first=Matthew |date=15 January 2020 |accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref> Mariah's stage name came from her genuine love of science.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Inman|first=DeMicia|date=2021-08-25|title=Mariah the Scientist Talks â€˜Ry Ry Worldâ€™ And Making Honest Music|url=https://www.vibe.com/features/editorial/mariah-the-scientist-ry-ry-world-interview-1234626716/|access-date=2021-10-18|website=VIBE.com|language=en-US}}</ref> She released her debut EP, the six-track ''To Die For'', on [[SoundCloud]] in January 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.allmusic.com/artist/mariah-the-scientist-mn0003890056/biography |title= Mariah the Scientist |website=allmusic |last=Kellman |first=Andy |date=21 August 2019 |accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref>

She was discovered by Canadian rapper [[Tory Lanez]], later signing her to his record label [[One Umbrella|One Umbrella Records]]. She later went on to tour with him, while honing her sound in preparation for releasing her debut studio album.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://rain-mag.com/mariah-the-scientist-releases-her-debut-album-master/ |title= Mariah the Scientist Releases Her Debut Album, 'Master' |website=pitchfork |last=Benjamin |first=Mark |date=28 August 2019 |accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref> The Canadian artist took on a mentor role in her career.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/mariah-the-scientist-earns-tory-lanez-approval-on-reminders-new-song.1984228.html |title= Mariah The Scientist Earns Tory Lanez Approval On "Reminders" |website=hotnewhiphop |last=Findlay |first=Mitch |date=21 August 2019 |accessdate=29 January 2020}}</ref> In February 2019 she was signed to [[RCA Records]], in conjunction with One Umbrella.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://rollingout.com/2019/08/23/rcas-new-artist-mariah-the-scientist-is-ready-to-master-the-music-industry/ |title=RCA's new artist Mariah the Scientist is ready to master the music industry |website=glittermagrocks |last=Sparks |first=Cassidy |date=23 August 2019 |accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref> She released her debut on the label, the ten-track album ''Master'', on 23 August 2019, executive produced by [[Tory Lanez]]. The debut single, "Beetlejuice,"<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.complex.com/music/2019/08/mariah-the-scientist-reminders-video |title=Premiere: Mariah the Scientist Shares "Reminders" Video |website=complex |last=Cowen |first=Trace William |date=21 August 2019 |accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref> made [[NPR]]'s Heat Check weekly roundup in August 2019,<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.npr.org/sections/allsongs/2019/08/12/750458126/heat-check-sticky-synths-relatable-heartache-sweltering-bars |title= Heat Check: Sticky Synths, Relatable Heartache, Sweltering Bars |website=NPR |last=Madden |first=Sidney |date=12 August 2019 |accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref> and the video for the track "Reminders" premiered on ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' on 21 August 2019.

Her touring schedule in 2020 includes bookings at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]], [[Primavera Sound]], and [[Pitchfork Music Festival]]. On May 22, 2020, Mariah released the single "RIP".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/rip-single/1513042478|title=RIP - Single by Mariah the Scientist on Apple Music|via=[[Apple Music]]|date=May 22, 2020|accessdate=May 22, 2020}}</ref>

==Influences==
Mariah has cited [[OutKast]] and [[Frank Ocean]] as major songwriting influences, and [[Whitney Houston]] and [[Mariah Carey]] for inspiration as a performer.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://glittermagrocks.com/connect/2019/11/15/interview-mariah-the-scientist-get-to-know-her-musical-evolution/ |title= Interview: Mariah the Scientist, Get to Know Her Musical Evolution |date=15 November 2019 |website=glittermagrocks |accessdate=28 January 2020}}</ref>
