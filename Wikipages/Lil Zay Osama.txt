'''Isaiah Dukes''', known professionally as '''Lil Zay Osama''', is an American [[rapper]]. His music, which he describes as "pain music", reflects his violent upbringing on the [[South Side, Chicago|South Side of Chicago]] and explores subjects including grief, trauma and survival.<ref name="RS">{{Cite web|last=Holmes|first=Charles|date=April 19, 2019|title=Song You Need to Know: Lil Zay Osama, 'Changed Up'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/lil-zay-osama-changed-up-824158/|access-date=January 24, 2021|website=Rolling Stone}}</ref><ref name="AM">{{Cite web|last=Simpson|first=Paul|title=Lil Zay Osama {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lil-zay-osama-mn0003822073/biography|access-date=January 18, 2021|website=AllMusic}}</ref>

==Early life==
Osama was born and raised in [[Chicago|Chicago, Illinois]]. He lived at the [[Robert Taylor Homes]] in the [[Douglas, Chicago#Bronzeville|Bronzville]] neighborhood on the South Side until he was 10.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rising Chicago Rappers You Need to Know|url=https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/rising-chicago-rappers-know-161958912.html|access-date=January 26, 2021|website=www.yahoo.com}}</ref><ref name="chicago reader">{{Cite news|last=Galil|first=Leor|date=March 7, 2019|title=Lil Zay Osama is part of an emerging wave of MCs changing Chicago rap|work=The Chicago Reader|url=https://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/lil-zay-osama-is-part-of-an-emerging-wave-of-mcs-changing-chicago-rap/Content?oid=68843129|url-status=live|access-date=January 23, 2021}}</ref> His mother was the primary caretaker for Osama and his five siblings; although his parents did not formally separate until he was nine, his father was in and out of jail and rarely present.<ref name="DJS">{{Cite web|title=Lil Zay Osama Parents Split by Age 9 and How It Affected Him, Father in State Prison 3 Years|last=DJ Smallz Eyes 2|date=June 21, 2020|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ONAmJcw_ebE|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>

==Career==
Inspired by his older brother, Osama began rapping when he was 8 and recording at 10. At 12, simultaneous to the release of his first tracks and videos, he joined a gang.<ref name="DJS" /> "Part of an emerging wave of MCs changing Chicago rap", he was initially associated with Chicago's [[Drill music|drill scene]].<ref name="chicago reader" /> He used the name Lil Zay until 2010, when, as his gang-related activity ramped up, he was christened on the street as Lil Zay Osama.<ref name="GQ">{{Cite web|last=Barshad|first=Amos|title=Why Are So Many New Rappers Named After Osama Bin Laden?|url=https://www.gq.com/story/why-are-so-many-new-rappers-named-after-osama-bin-laden|access-date=January 25, 2021|website=GQ}}</ref><ref name="VLAD" >{{Cite web|title=EXCLUSIVE: Lil Zay Osama Details Getting Shot in the Chest at 15, Sentenced to Juvenile Life|url=https://www.vladtv.com/article/258307/lil-zay-osama-details-getting-shot-in-the-chest-at-15-sentenced-to|access-date=January 26, 2021|website=www.vladtv.com}}</ref>

In 2015 Osama was shot in the chest, and while in the hospital, he was arrested on gun charges. At 16, he was sentenced to five years in a juvenile detention center. Committed to keeping away from the streets when he was freed, he focused on music for the duration of his incarceration, writing rap as well as songs influenced by pop and R&B.<ref name="DJS" /><ref name="VLAD" />

Osama's sentence was reduced to three years, and he was released in 2017. At 19, he returned to the South Side with a "new style, a new perspective, and a new level of determination."<ref name="XXL">{{Cite web|last=Seabrook|first=Robby|title=The Break Presents: Lil Zay Osama - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/lil-zay-osama-interview-the-break/|access-date=January 27, 2021|website=XXL Mag}}</ref> In 2018, he put 8 new songs on [[SoundCloud]], and in 2019, he released ''Hood Bible,'' a collection of 14 songs that came out one by one.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lil Zay Osama|url=https://soundcloud.com/lilzayosama|access-date=January 27, 2021|website=SoundCloud}}</ref> ''Hood Bible'' included his first hit, "Changed Up". The video for "Changed Up" received more than 20 million views on YouTube over the course of several months. ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' attributed Osama's success to "relatable struggles in his lyrics" and "a realness that resonated".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|title=The Ones: Lil Zay Osama's "2 Years"|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/lil-zay-osama-2-year-new-song-listen/|access-date=January 27, 2021|website=Pitchfork}}</ref>

In August 2019, Osama signed with [[Warner Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lil Zay Osama Signs With Warner Records, Releases 'Talk to Me Crazy' Video: Exclusive|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8528032/lil-zay-osama-warner-records-talk-to-me-crazy-video|access-date=January 25, 2021|website=Billboard}}</ref> Among other songs, he has since released "Like a Pimp", a collaboration with [[Stunna 4 Vegas]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=Grant|first=Shawn|date=November 17, 2020|title=Lil Zay Osama and Stunna 4 Vegas Link for 'Like A Pimp' Video|url=https://thesource.com/2020/11/17/lil-zay-osama-like-a-pimp/|access-date=January 25, 2021|website=The Source
}}</ref> "Rumors", "Ride 4 Me" featuring [[Jackboy]] and "61st to 64th".<ref>{{Cite web|date=December 12, 2020|title=Lil Zay Osama Addresses "Rumors" In New Video|url=https://allhiphop.com/music/lil-zay-osama-addresses-rumors-in-new-video/|access-date=January 25, 2021|website=AllHipHop.com}}</ref> His mixtape, ''Trench Baby'', was released on February 19, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Interview with Lil Zay Osama|url=https://www.bringinitbackwards.com/pod/lilzayosama|access-date=January 27, 2021|website=BRINGIN' IT BACKWARDS}}</ref>
