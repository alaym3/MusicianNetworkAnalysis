'''Ramin Djawadi''' ({{IPAc-en|r|Q|'|m|i:|n|_|'|dZ|A:|v|@|d|i}}, {{lang-fa|رامین جوادی}}, pronounced ''Java-Dee'', born 19 July 1974)<ref name=BBC>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ramin-djawadi-mn0000940397/biography|first=James Christopher |last= Monger|title=Ramin Djawadi Biography | publisher=[[AllMusic.com]]|access-date=May 9, 2019|archive-date=April 22, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190422071840/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ramin-djawadi-mn0000940397/biography|url-status=live}}</ref> is a [[Germans|German]]<!-- Please see the talk page if you're going to change his nationality --> [[film score|score]] [[composer]]. He is known for his scores for the 2008 Marvel film ''[[Iron Man (2008 film)|Iron Man]]'' and the HBO series ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', for which he was nominated for [[Grammy Award]]s in 2009, 2018 and 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/search/Ramin%20djawadi|title=Search Results for Ramin djawadi|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en|access-date=June 29, 2019}}</ref> He has scored films such as ''[[Clash of the Titans (2010 film)|Clash of the Titans]]'', ''[[Pacific Rim (film)|Pacific Rim]]'', ''[[Warcraft (film)|Warcraft]]'' and ''[[A Wrinkle in Time (2018 film)|A Wrinkle in Time]]'', television series including ''[[Prison Break]]'', ''[[Person of Interest (TV series)|Person of Interest]]'', ''[[Jack Ryan (TV series)|Jack Ryan]]'', and ''[[Westworld (TV series)|Westworld]]'', and video games such as ''[[Medal of Honor (2010 video game)|Medal of Honor]]'', ''[[Gears of War 4]]'', and ''[[Gears 5]]''. He won two consecutive [[Primetime Emmy Award|Emmy Awards]] for ''Game of Thrones'', in 2018 for the episode "[[The Dragon and the Wolf]]" and in 2019 for "[[The Long Night (Game of Thrones)|The Long Night]]".<ref name="emmy"/>

==Early life==
Djawadi was born in [[Duisburg]], to an [[Iranian peoples|Iranian]] father and a [[Germany|German]] mother.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Welle (www.dw.com)|first=Deutsche|title=No flutes allowed: Composer Ramin Djawadi on the music of Game of Thrones {{!}} DW {{!}} 17.05.2018|url=https://www.dw.com/en/no-flutes-allowed-composer-ramin-djawadi-on-the-music-of-game-of-thrones/a-19201563|access-date=2020-06-07|website=DW.COM|language=en-GB}}</ref> He went to Krupp Gymnasium in [[Duisburg]], [[West Germany]] and studied at [[Berklee College of Music]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-ramin-djawadi-game-of-thrones-concert20170201-story.html|title=When music and dragons meet: 'Game of Thrones' comes to the Forum|last=Ali|first=Lorraine|date=March 21, 2017|work=Los Angeles Times|access-date=December 4, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171206005816/http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-ramin-djawadi-game-of-thrones-concert20170201-story.html|archive-date=December 6, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.dailyherald.com/article/20170217/entlife/170219304/|title='Game of Thrones' composer brings immersive tour to United Center|last=Stangland|first=Sean|date=February 17, 2017|work=Daily Herald|access-date=December 4, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171205092955/http://www.dailyherald.com/article/20170217/entlife/170219304/|archive-date=December 5, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
After graduating from [[Berklee College of Music]] in 1998,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.berklee.edu/people/ramin-djawadi?ss=203546|title=Ramin Djawadi {{!}} Berklee College of Music|website=www.berklee.edu|access-date=January 24, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180125015941/https://www.berklee.edu/people/ramin-djawadi?ss=203546|archive-date=January 25, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Djawadi garnered the attention of [[Hans Zimmer]], who recruited him to [[Remote Control Productions (American company)|Remote Control Productions]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2017/10/ramin-djawadi|title=From *Game of Thrones* to Idris Elba's Love Scenes, Composer Ramin Djawadi Is Responsible for the Music That Makes Your Heart Thud|last=Keegan|first=Rebecca|date=October 6, 2017|work=HWD|access-date=December 4, 2017|publisher=Vanity Fair}}</ref> Djawadi moved to Los Angeles and worked as an assistant to [[Klaus Badelt]]. From there on he made additional music and arrangements for Badelt and Zimmer movies, such as ''[[Pirates of the Caribbean: The Curse of the Black Pearl]]'', ''[[The Time Machine (2002 film)|The Time Machine]]'', and the [[Academy Award]]-nominated film ''[[Something's Gotta Give (film)|Something's Gotta Give]]''. He co-composed the music for ''[[System Shock 2]]'' (1999). In 2003, he and Badelt composed the score of ''[[Beat the Drum]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.filmmusicmag.com/?p=11418|title=Audio: On the Score with Ramin Djawadi :: Film Music Magazine|last=Schweiger|first=Daniel|date=July 2, 2013|website=Film Music Magazine|access-date=December 4, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171206005756/http://www.filmmusicmag.com/?p=11418|archive-date=December 6, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2004, Djawadi went out on his own with ''[[Blade: Trinity]]'', collaborating with [[RZA]] for director [[David S. Goyer]]. This was the beginning of his relationship with Goyer for both film and television. The following year Djawadi continued making additional music for Zimmer in films such as ''[[Batman Begins]]'' and ''[[The Island (2005 film)|The Island]]'', which was his last time working in the background of another composer. The same year, he composed the Emmy-nominated main title themes and scores for ''[[Prison Break]]'' and the related show ''[[Breakout Kings]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.express.co.uk/showbiz/tv-radio/593565/Prison-Break-season-5-Ramin-Djawadi-Wentworth-Miller|title=Prison Break season 5: Ramin Djawadi says he would 'absolutely' return to the show|last=Debnath|first=Neela|work=[[Daily Express]]|date=July 24, 2015|access-date=March 17, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170419132127/http://www.express.co.uk/showbiz/tv-radio/593565/Prison-Break-season-5-Ramin-Djawadi-Wentworth-Miller|archive-date=April 19, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2006, Djawadi scored the first Sony Animation project, ''[[Open Season (2006 film)|Open Season]]'', followed by the sequel ''[[Open Season 2]]'' (2008). Djawadi's ethereal score for the film ''[[Mr. Brooks]]'' (2007) earned him a [[World Soundtrack Award for Discovery of the Year]] nomination. His other scores include ''[[Deception (2008 film)|Deception]]'', [[Robert Towne]]'s ''[[Ask the Dust (film)|Ask the Dust]]'', and ''[[Iron Man (2008 film)|Iron Man]]''. Djawadi was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Score Soundtrack for Visual Media]]<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-11-26|title=Ramin Djawadi|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/ramin-djawadi/2992|access-date=2020-11-14|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref> for his work on ''Iron Man'''.

Djawadi wrote the score for Goyer's horror thriller ''[[The Unborn (2009 film)|The Unborn]]'' (2009), produced by [[Michael Bay]]. Djawadi collaborated with Goyer on the television show ''[[FlashForward]]'' that year, earning him his second [[Emmy Award|Emmy]] nomination.

In 2010, Djawadi completed Warner Brothers' ''[[Clash of the Titans (2010 film)|Clash of the Titans]]''. The same year, he scored the soundtrack for the video game ''[[Medal of Honor (2010 video game)|Medal of Honor]]''.

In 2011, he was selected to score HBO's fantasy drama ''[[Game of Thrones]]''.<ref>{{cite news |author=Victoria Ellison |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/game-thrones-composer-ramin-djawadi-437085 |title=''Game of Thrones'' Composer Ramin Djawadi: 'I'm Just Trying to Create Something Magical' (Q&A) |work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |date=April 15, 2013 |access-date=August 22, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131225155807/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/game-thrones-composer-ramin-djawadi-437085 |archive-date=December 25, 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref> His work on ''Game of Thrones'' has garnered him several industry awards and recognition including a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Music Composition for a Series]] in September 2018 for the score "The Dragon and the Wolf".<ref>{{cite web |title=Outstanding Music Composition For A Series (Original Dramatic Score) - 2018 |url=https://www.emmys.com/awards/nominees-winners/2018/outstanding-music-composition-for-a-series-original-dramatic-score |website=Emmys |publisher=Academy of Television Arts & Sciences |access-date=May 21, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190330111016/https://www.emmys.com/awards/nominees-winners/2018/outstanding-music-composition-for-a-series-original-dramatic-score |archive-date=March 30, 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> For his work on [[Game of Thrones (season 7)|season 7]], he was nominated for the [[Grammy Award for Best Score Soundtrack for Visual Media]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1014697/awards|title=Ramin Djawadi|website=IMDb|access-date=June 29, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170818215121/http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1014697/awards|archive-date=August 18, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> This was his second nomination in this category after being nominated before for ''Iron Man'' in 2009. In 2011, he worked on the CBS crime drama ''[[Person of Interest (TV series)|Person of Interest]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://filmmusicreporter.com/2011/05/31/game-of-thrones-soundtrack-details/ |title=Game of Thrones Soundtrack Details |publisher=Film Music Reporter |date=May 31, 2011 |access-date=June 15, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160725184403/http://filmmusicreporter.com/2011/05/31/game-of-thrones-soundtrack-details/ |archive-date=July 25, 2016 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In 2013, Djawadi composed for the science fiction film ''[[Pacific Rim (film)|Pacific Rim]]''. He scored FX's vampire drama ''[[The Strain (TV series)|The Strain]]'', created by ''Pacific Rim'' director [[Guillermo del Toro]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://filmmusicreporter.com/2013/09/27/ramin-djawadi-to-score-guillermo-del-toros-the-strain/|title=Ramin Djawadi to Score Guillermo Del Toro's ''The Strain''|publisher=Film Music Reporter|date=September 27, 2013|access-date=March 17, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171112021821/http://filmmusicreporter.com/2013/09/27/ramin-djawadi-to-score-guillermo-del-toros-the-strain/|archive-date=November 12, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2016, Djawadi composed for the fantasy film ''[[Warcraft (film)|Warcraft]]'' and the HBO science fiction show ''[[Westworld (TV series)|Westworld]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url = http://filmmusicreporter.com/2014/10/09/ramin-djawadi-to-score-duncan-jones-warcraft-movie/ |title = Ramin Djawadi to Score Duncan Jones' 'Warcraft' Movie |publisher = filmmusicreporter.com |date = October 17, 2014 |access-date = October 27, 2016 |archive-url = https://www.webcitation.org/6XqCcfwVT?url=http://filmmusicreporter.com/2014/10/09/ramin-djawadi-to-score-duncan-jones-warcraft-movie/ |archive-date = April 16, 2015 |url-status = live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://filmmusicreporter.com/2014/12/29/ramin-djawadi-to-score-hbos-westworld/ |title=Ramin Djawadi to Score HBO's 'Westworld' |work=Film Music Reporter |date=December 30, 2014 |access-date=December 30, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141230111311/http://filmmusicreporter.com/2014/12/29/ramin-djawadi-to-score-hbos-westworld/ |archive-date=December 30, 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref> The same year, Djawadi composed the score for the fantasy action monster film [[The Great Wall (film)|''The Great Wall'']].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://filmmusicreporter.com/2015/08/18/ramin-djawadi-to-score-the-great-wall/|title=Ramin Djawadi to Score 'The Great Wall'|publisher=Film Music Reporter|date=August 18, 2015|access-date=March 17, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161220010655/http://filmmusicreporter.com/2015/08/18/ramin-djawadi-to-score-the-great-wall/|archive-date=December 20, 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

Djawadi scored ''[[The Queen's Corgi]]'', an animation film directed by frequent collaborator [[Ben Stassen]].<ref>{{Citation|title=The Queen's Corgi (2019) - IMDb|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt6563576/fullcredits|access-date=June 29, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190908135516/https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6563576/fullcredits|archive-date=September 8, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> He co-wrote "Hollow Crown " alongside [[Ellie Goulding]] in ''[[For the Throne: Music Inspired by the HBO Series Game of Thrones]]''.<ref>{{Citation|title=For the Throne: Music Inspired by the HBO Series Game of Thrones|date=June 22, 2019|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=For_the_Throne:_Music_Inspired_by_the_HBO_Series_Game_of_Thrones&oldid=902987210|work=Wikipedia|language=en|access-date=June 29, 2019}}</ref> In 2019, he won his second Emmy Award in a row for the ''Game of Thrones'' episode "[[The Long Night (Game of Thrones)|The Long Night]]".<ref name="emmy">{{Cite web|url=https://www.emmys.com/bios/ramin-djawadi|title=Ramin Djawadi|website=Television Academy|language=en|access-date=September 16, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190831203802/https://www.emmys.com/bios/ramin-djawadi|archive-date=August 31, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2019, Djawadi also made the soundtrack for the Microsoft Studios and The Coalition video game, Gears 5. The soundtrack was copyrighted and cannot be used even while playing the game online. 

He received a third Grammy nomination of his career for his work in [[Game of Thrones (season 8)]] in the [[Grammy Award for Best Score Soundtrack for Visual Media]] category.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/2020-grammy-awards-complete-nominees-list|title=2020 GRAMMY Awards: Complete Nominees List|date=2019-11-18|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref>

Djawadi composed the score for the 2021 [[Marvel Studios]] film ''[[Eternals (film)|Eternals]]'', which marked his return to the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] (MCU) since 2008's ''Iron Man''. Djawadi composed music for the trailer of [[Magic: The Gathering|Magic: The Gathering: Theros Beyond Death]], a card game. Djawadi co-composed music with  [[Brandon Campbell]] for the second episode of [[Apple TV+]]'s series ''[[Amazing Stories (2020 TV series)|Amazing Stories]]'' titled "The Heat". He composed music for the [[Disney+]]'s [[nature documentary]] film ''[[Elephant (2020 film)|Elephant]]''. Djawadi is set to compose the score for [[Lisa Joy|Lisa Joy's]] feature film debut [[Reminiscence (2021 film)|''Reminiscence'']]. He also co-composed the music for the [[Amazon Games|Amazon Game's]] [[New World (video game)|''"New World"'']] with [[Brandon Campbell]].

==Personal life==
Djawadi is married to Jennifer Hawks, a music executive in the film industry.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://variety.com/2013/dirt/real-estalker/late-thursday-afternoon-this-and-thats-390/ |title=Late Thursday Afternoon This And Thats |first=Mark |last=David |date=August 22, 2013 |work=Variety |access-date=December 13, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171205065540/http://variety.com/2013/dirt/real-estalker/late-thursday-afternoon-this-and-thats-390/ |archive-date=December 5, 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref> They are parents of twins.<ref>[https://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2017/10/ramin-djawadi Vanity Fair: Ramin Djawadi]</ref> According to Djawadi, he experiences the perceptual phenomenon known as [[synesthesia]] whereby he may "associate colours with music, or music with colours", and it allows him to visualize music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/game-of-thrones-music-composer-ramin-djawadi-interview-2016-7|title=Meet the musical genius behind the 'Game of Thrones' soundtrack who watches each season before anyone else|first=Kim|last=Renfro|work=Business Insider|date=July 7, 2016|access-date=December 22, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170131113921/http://www.businessinsider.com/game-of-thrones-music-composer-ramin-djawadi-interview-2016-7|archive-date=January 31, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>
