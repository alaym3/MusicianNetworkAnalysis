'''Wind Rose''' is an Italian [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band founded in 2009 in [[Pisa]], [[Tuscany]]. The band has released four studio albums, most recently ''Wintersaga'' in September 2019. Their sound is [[heavy metal music|heavy metal]] but can also be described using the sub-genres [[power metal]] and [[folk metal]] with orchestration and vocal harmonies.  Their lyrics take inspiration in the works of [[J. R. R. Tolkien]], and their aesthetic is based on Tolkien's [[Dwarf (Middle-Earth)|Dwarves]].<ref name="Metal Hammer">{{cite web|url=http://www.metalhammer.it/band/wind-rose/ |title=Wind Rose |work=Metal Hammer |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref>

==History==
In March 2010, a few months after their formation, Wind Rose released an EP called "Demo 2010" which was produced by [[Cristiano Bertocchi]]. On 28 August 2012 Wind Rose released their first full-length ‘Shadows Over Lothadruin’ via [[Bakerteam Records]] (secondary division of [[Scarlet Records]]).<ref name="Metal Hammer"/>

In 2013/2014 they performed a number of promotional live shows in Europe directly supporting [[Wintersun]],<ref name="Rock Hard Video">{{cite web |url=https://www.rockhard.de/artikel/wind-rose-veroeffentlichen-_the-returning-race_-video_366834.html |title=Wind Rose veröffentlichen 'The Returning Race'-Video |language=German |work=[[Rock Hard]] |date=17 October 2017 |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref> [[Finntroll]]<ref name="Masters of Rock">{{cite web |url=https://www.mastersofrock.cz/en/Wind-Rose |title=Wind Rose |work=[[Masters of Rock (festival)|Masters of Rock]] |year=2017 |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref> and [[Epica (band)|Epica]].<ref name="Rock Hard Erebor">{{cite web |url=https://www.rockhard.de/artikel/wind-rose_-_to-erebor_-clip-ist-online_369070.html |title=Wind Rose: 'To Erebor'-Clip ist online |language=German |work=[[Rock Hard]] |date=16 May 2017 |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref> Cristiano Bertocchi joined the band as bass player during 2014 before the band went to the studio to record the second full-length album.

In February 2015, they took part in [[Eluveitie]]'s ''Origins'' European tour<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.metropoli.com/b/conciertos/67582/eluveitie-skalmold-wind-rose.html |title=Eluveitie + Skálmöld + Wind Rose |language=Spanish |work=El Mundo Metropoli |year=2015 |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref> and their second studio album ''Wardens of the West Wind'' was released by [[Scarlet Records]].<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.rockhard.de/reviews/wind-rose-wardens-of-the-west-wind_39318.html |title=Wind Rose – Wardens of the West Wind |language=German |work=[[Rock Hard]] |first=Alexandra |last=Michels |issue=334 |year=2015}}</ref> In October they also played as support for [[Ensiferum]]'s Spanish shows during their ''One Man Army'' European tour.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/events/sala-razzmatazz-2/ensiferum-wind-rose-barcelona/1660523867497348/ |title=Esiferum + Wind Rose: Barcelona |work=Sala Razzamatazz 2 |language=Spanish |publisher=[[Facebook]] |date=15 October 2015 |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref> After these shows, Wind Rose dedicated the entire year 2016 to writing new material. In 2017, the band signed a deal with [[Inner Wound Recordings]] for their third album ''Stonehymn''.<ref name="rock Hard Stonehymn">{{cite magazine |title=Wind Rose – Stonehymn |first=Marcus |last=Schleutermann |work=[[Rock Hard]] |issue=361 |url=https://www.rockhard.de/reviews/wind-rose-stonehymn_20842.html |language=German |year=2017}}</ref> It was released in May. The first video to be released was "The Wolves' Call" followed by "To Erebor"<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rockhard.de/artikel/wind-rose_-_the-wolves_-call_-lyricvideo-veroeffentlicht_366746.html |title=Wind Rose: 'The Wolves' Call'-Lyricvideo veröffentlicht |language=German |work=[[Rock Hard]] |date=11 April 2017 |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref><ref name="Rock Hard Erebor"/> which gave more popularity to the band, reaching millions of contacts on their [[Facebook]] and on [[YouTube]] outlets. The virality of the video sparked the curiosity of the managers of music events.{{citation needed|date=July 2019}} Thus Wind Rose was invited to participate in important metal festivals such as [[Bloodstock Open Air|Bloodstock]] (United Kingdom) and [[Masters of Rock (festival)|Masters of Rock]] (Czech Republic).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bloodstock.uk.com/events/boa-2017/bands/wind-rose |title=Band line up 2017 |work=Bloodstock Open Air |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref><ref name="Masters of Rock"/>

In January 2018, the band was asked to tour Japan with Ensiferum, and in April and May 2018 they joined the ''Path to Glory'' European tour with Ensiferum and [[Ex Deo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.metal-hammer.de/metal-hammer-praesentiert-ensiferum-ex-deo-998077/ |title=Metal Hammer präsentiert: Ensiferum + Ex Deo + Wind Rose |language=German |work=[[Metal Hammer]] |date=21 December 2017 |first=Thomas |last=Sonder |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref> In December 2018, Wind Rose announced a deal with [[Napalm Records]] and the recording of a new album.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/windroseofficial/posts/1991915077522811?__xts__[0]=68.ARBp-DhfjcNMe69UkdsgzZCWo6ETgERrxb1lWxiF5NCxSI4Y_BM9PWo2BRbZha_lJ0qI31DHCLG8g3R4sCYbbOqJuFRSrhIrH9CpN-BoFk-pbXyyXeyqR2ira0M9g3CUbk2bsnZP0hWvPPabuPGS-0_ZCcB9L2W3SQnu7yJGp0wZmypn8M--oviaQg7tglr24g8_6x4j-T6eG0HzAERCf3IEP0kziHemwtlcM7KAjtubiP3JpGMwNr7A0cbsAMDo3aAT544DB0F4odcugncBZYazJnPdAKoGBdmnLv4KWlFkYzyyLtjKltwu_vDV_OPXY2MVIUycT0PPSEolea1ZZGk1NKLtXHCtmVjKzOIW3F9Hu6REjUrmMoFwJhV5VsNHacz-Wiiv8MrpF8vyTGLu8RcBNnPVYLLFNdopWHUjpQeDN9uNhRbA1OpXs38L441iyU76VI5NQzFpyTx9OlGIC1TtD-UU_FR-UtSFMQpM5bBs_nExsEFi5-k&__tn__=-R |title=Wind Rose – Sign With Napalm Records! |work=Wind Rose |publisher=[[Facebook]] |date=21 December 2018 |accessdate=12 July 2019}}</ref> On 6 June 2019 Wind Rose released the first single on Napalm: "Diggy Diggy Hole", a song originally written and performed by gaming YouTubers [[The Yogscast]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicalypse.net/single-of-the-day-diggy-diggy-hole-by-wind-rose/|title=SINGLE OF THE DAY: "Diggy Diggy Hole" by Wind Rose|first=Bear|last=W|date=1 January 2020}}</ref> The album ''Wintersaga'' was released on 27 September 2019.
