'''Whores''' (stylized as '''WHORES.''') is an American [[noise rock]] band based in [[Atlanta]], Georgia consisting of vocalist and guitarist Christian Lembach, bass guitarist Casey Maxwell and drummer Joel Willis. The band has released two records through Brutal Panda Records<ref name="allmusic"/> and is known for its "intensely cathartic live shows."<ref>{{cite web | url=http://exclaim.ca/Interviews/WebExclusive/whores-not_easiest_band_name_to_google | title=Whores: Not the Easiest Band Name to Google | publisher=[[Exclaim!]] | date=November 2013 | accessdate=April 8, 2014 | author=Walschots, Natalie Zina}}</ref>

==History==
In 2011, the band released their debut EP Ruiner through Brutal Panda Records.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/whores-mn0002882219/biography | title=Whores | publisher=[[Allmusic]] | accessdate=April 8, 2014 | author=Heaney, Gregory}}</ref> This was followed by ''[[Clean (Whores EP)|Clean]]'' EP in 2013, which received attention from music websites such as ''[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]'',<ref name="p4k-review1">{{cite web | url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/18687-whores-clean/ | title=Whores - Clean | publisher=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] | date=November 15, 2013 | accessdate=April 8, 2014 | author=O'Connor, Andy}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://pitchfork.com/news/52709-stream-new-releases-from-laurel-halo-and-russian-circles-via-pitchfork-advance/ | title=Stream New Releases From Laurel Halo, Russian Circles, and Whores Via Pitchfork Advance | publisher=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] | date=October 21, 2013 | accessdate=April 8, 2014}}</ref> ''[[Exclaim!]]'',<ref name="exclaim-review">{{cite web | url=http://exclaim.ca/Reviews/Metal/whores-clean | title=Whores - Clean | publisher=[[Exclaim!]] | date=October 15, 2013 | accessdate=April 8, 2014 | author=Walschots, Natalie Zina}}</ref> and [[Sputnikmusic]].<ref name="sput"/> A split single with the band Rabbits, which mainly consists of [[The Cure]] covers, were also released in 2014.,<ref>{{cite web | url=http://brutalpandarecords.com/shop/whores-rabbits-split-7/ | title=Whores / Rabbits – Split 7 | publisher=Brutal Panda Records | accessdate=April 8, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2014/02/24/whores-jump-someone-elses-train-cure-cover/ | title=Whores Jump Someone Else’s Train (The Cure Cover) | publisher=[[MetalSucks]] | date=February 24, 2014 | accessdate=April 8, 2014 | author=Neilstein, Vİnce}}</ref> On October 28, 2016, they released their new album “Gold“ via eOne Music.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.radioneutron.com/2016/11/03/ep090-christian-lembech-whores/ | title=Protonic Reversal Ep090 Christian Lembach (Whores.) - The band has also extensively toured United States, alongside acts such as [[Red Fang]], [[Melvins]], [[Retox (band)|Retox]], [[Torche (band)|Torche]], [[The Sword]], [[Black Tusk (band)|Black Tusk]], [[Deafheaven]], [[Floor (band)|Floor]], [[Kylesa]], [[Obliterations (band)|Obliterations]], [[Iron Reagan]], [[Fight Amp (band)|Fight Amp]], [[No Way (band)|No Way]] and [[Creepoid]].<ref name="Moore, Doug">{{cite web | url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/tag/Whores | title=The Heavy Lowdown: 40 Watt Sun LP news, new Autopsy, Misery Index at MDF +++ more | publisher=[[BrooklynVegan]] | accessdate=April 8, 2014 | author=Moore, Doug}}</ref>

==Musical style==
The band's music is mainly described as [[noise rock]].<ref name="p4k-review1"/><ref name="exclaim-review"/><ref name="sput">{{cite web | url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/59505/Whores.-Clean/ | title=Whores. - Clean (staff review) | publisher=[[Sputnikmusic]] | date=October 28, 2013 | accessdate=April 8, 2014 | author=Fisher, Greg}}</ref> Pitchfork Media reviewer Andy O'Connor noted the influences of [[Melvins]], [[Helmet (band)|Helmet]], [[Karp (band)|Karp]], [[Pissed Jeans]], and [[Amphetamine Reptile Records]] artists.<ref name="p4k-review1"/> The band also incorporates  [[Sludge Metal|sludge]] in its sound.<ref name="sput"/>

The band also collaborated with many figures of the noise rock scene. The band's first EP was mastered by [[Harvey Milk (band)|Harvey Milk]] drummer Kyle Spence<ref>{{cite web | url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/12921-daddys-money/ | title=Whores - "Daddy's Money" | publisher=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] | date=January 3, 2012 | accessdate=April 8, 2014 | author=Stosuy, Brandon}}</ref> and their sophomore EP was produced by Ryan Boesch, who worked with various acts such as [[Melvins]], [[Fu Manchu (band)|Fu Manchu]], [[Helmet (band)|Helmet]] and [[Foo Fighters]].<ref name="exclaim-review"/>
