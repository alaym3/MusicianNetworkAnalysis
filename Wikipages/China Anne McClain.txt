'''China Anne McClain''' (born August 25, 1998) is an American actress and singer. McClain's career began when she was seven years old, portraying Alexis in the film ''[[The Gospel (film)|The Gospel]]'' (2005), and then China James in ''[[Daddy's Little Girls]]'' (2007). She then received recognition for starring as Jazmine Payne in the television series ''[[Tyler Perry's House of Payne]]'' (2007–2012; 2020–present) and as Charlotte McKenzie in the film ''[[Grown Ups (film)|Grown Ups]]'' (2010); and became internationally known for starring as Chyna Parks in the [[Disney Channel]] television series ''[[A.N.T. Farm]]'' (2011–2014), and as Uma in the Disney Channel films ''[[Descendants 2]]'' (2017) and ''[[Descendants 3]]'' (2019). In 2018, McClain began starring in [[The CW]] superhero series [[Black Lightning (TV series)|''Black Lightning'']] (2018–2021) as [[Lightning (DC Comics)|Jennifer Pierce / Lightning]]. She also reprised her character Jazmine Payne on [[Oprah Winfrey Network|OWN]]'s revival of ''[[The Paynes]]'' (2018).

McClain began her musical career in 2005 as a member of the sister girl group the [[Thriii|3mcclaingirls]] with her sisters [[Sierra McClain|Sierra]] and [[Lauryn McClain|Lauryn]]. Her first professional single was "Your Biggest Fan" with [[Nick Jonas]] on the soundtrack album [[Jonas L.A. (soundtrack)|''Jonas L.A.'']] (2010). In 2011, McClain signed with [[Hollywood Records]] both as a solo artist and with her sister girl group, known as the [[Thriii|McClain Sisters]]. Her debut single as a solo artist was "[[Dynamite (China Anne McClain song)|Dynamite]]" which peaked at no. 2 on the US [[Kid Digital Songs]], and her second single, "[[Calling All the Monsters]]", peaked at no. 1 on the same chart and earned 25,000 downloads within its first week of release. The [[A.N.T. Farm (soundtrack)|''A.N.T. Farm'' soundtrack album]] spent five weeks on the [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'']] Kids chart in 2011 and sold 14,000 copies in its first week of release. ''Billboard'' also named her the sixth best-selling artist for digital kids' songs in 2012. In 2017, her single "[[What's My Name (Descendants song)|What's My Name]]" from ''[[Descendants 2]]'' peaked on several [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] charts, and was certified [[Gold (music sales)|gold]], with 500,000 units sold, on November 10, 2017. In June 2020 her sister girl group changed their name to [[Thriii]].

== Personal life ==
In 2014, Lauryn and China created a [[YouTube]] channel where they posted singing videos, challenges, tags, and Q&As. Sierra appeared in several of the channel's videos. As of August 2020, the channel has approximately 701,000 subscribers and 29 million views. They have since renamed the channel ''[[Thriii]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lauryn & China|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCiil8pQHTtMjFg2W72AfqjQ|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200412011155/https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCiil8pQHTtMjFg2W72AfqjQ|archive-date=April 12, 2020|access-date=2020-04-12|publisher=YouTube|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Lauryn & China - About|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/laurynandchina/about|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200412020240/https://www.youtube.com/user/laurynandchina/about|archive-date=April 12, 2020|publisher=YouTube}}</ref>

As of 2011, she resided in [[Los Angeles]].<ref name=":9" /> McClain cites her parents as her biggest influences.<ref>{{cite web|title=Teen star China Anne McClain's African-American influencers|url=http://www.theroot.com/articles/culture/2013/02/teen_star_china_anne_mcclains_africanamerican_influencers.html|year=2012|publisher=The Root|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304110359/http://www.theroot.com/articles/culture/2013/02/teen_star_china_anne_mcclains_africanamerican_influencers.html|archive-date=March 4, 2016|access-date=February 1, 2012}}</ref>
