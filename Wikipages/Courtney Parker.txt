'''Courtney Parker''' (born July 7, 1985 in [[Oklahoma City]], Oklahoma)<ref name="bio1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ishow.gr/personBio.asp?guid=341503C6-93DE-4221-9B44-949FDBA10A26|title=Κόρτνεϊ Πάρκερ (Courtney Parker) – iShow.gr|website=ishow.gr|access-date=July 4, 2019}}</ref><ref name="bio2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ladylike.gr/articles/synentefxeis/courtney-parker-apo-thn-oklaxoma-sthn-ellada-me-eisithrio-th-moysikh.2908932.html|title=Courtney Parker: Από την Οκλαχόμα στην Ελλάδα με εισιτήριο τη μουσική|last=Ιουλίου 09 2014 15:16|first=ΣΥΝΕΝΤΕΥΞΕΙΣ ΙΩΑΝΝΑ ΜΑΜΑΗ|website=ladylike.gr|access-date=July 4, 2019}}</ref> usually referred to simply as '''Courtney''' is an American singer and dancer that currently lives and works in Greece. She is currently signed to the Panik Records. She is known for her collaborations with [[Elena Paparizou]], Playmen and HouseTwins at the songs "All the Time", "Breakin' Me Up", and "Rhythm Is A Dancer" respectively.

== Career ==

Before moving to Greece and starting her music career, Courtney was a dancer. Her love for dancing led her to teaching and choreographing for the last 11 years. She studied classical and contemporary ballet, [[Horton technique|Horton]] and [[Graham technique|Graham]] modern techniques, jazz, lyrical, contemporary and hip hop. After graduating from the [[University of Central Oklahoma]] where she studied dance major for one year, she was hired as an Equity dancer at [[Walt Disney World]].<ref name="people-interview">{{Cite web|url=https://www.peoplegreece.com/celebrity/courtney-parker-ti-tin-ekane-na-afisi-sinergasies-rihanna-ke-beyonce-ke-na-erthi-stin-ellada/|title=Courtney Parker: Tι την έκανε να αφήσει συνεργασίες με Rihanna και Beyonce και να έρθει στην Ελλάδα|date=July 13, 2017|website=Peoplegreece.com|language=el|access-date=July 4, 2019}}</ref> Later, Courtney moved to Los Angeles under [[Brian Friedman]]'s instruction who became her mentor after he met her at the Walt Disney World. Courtney has danced with such artists as [[Destiny's Child]], [[will.i.am]], [[Chris Brown (American entertainer)|Chris Brown]], [[Trey Songz]], [[Rihanna]] and [[Nelly Furtado]].<ref name="people-interview"/> She appeared in several music videos and national commercials. She has worked with choreographers such as Frank Gatson ([[Kelly Rowland]]), Brian Friedman (Monster's of Hip Hop), Fatima Robinson (Will.i.am), Rosero McCoy (National Verizon Commercial), [[Robin Antin]], Zachary Woodley ([[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]) and more.

In 2008, Courtney became a [[The Pussycat Dolls|Pussycat Doll]] and started performing on various television shows in L.A. as a Pussycat Doll.<ref name="bio2"/> She also appeared at the [[The Viper Room|Viper Room]]. Later, she appeared at [[Glee (TV series)|Glee]] where she performed with Zachary Woodley. Afterwards, Courtney traveled around Europe as a dancer and singer with some of Greece's biggest artists. She has also been working with choreographer and instructor Chali Jennings as her assistant choreographer. Courtney used her time overseas to share her passion for dance by teaching students and professional dancers in Greece.

Courtney's first appearance in the music industry was in 2010 with her appearance at Thalassa in Greece.<ref name="bio2"/> After she was done with her appearances at Thalassa she went back to the United States but returned again in September for her collaboration with [[Helena Paparizou]] and [[Antonis Remos]] in Diogenis Studio.

Two years later, in 2012, she collaborated once again with [[Helena Paparizou]] at the song "[[All the Time (Playmen song)|All the Time]]" with Playmen and [[RiskyKidd]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thesout.gr/2010-2012/playmen-feat-elena-paparizou-all-the-time-neo-tragoudi-6628|title=Playmen feat. Έλενα Παπαρίζου – All the time νέο τραγούδι|work=thesout.gr|accessdate=June 21, 2012|language=Greek|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130721111157/http://www.thesout.gr/2010-2012/playmen-feat-elena-paparizou-all-the-time-neo-tragoudi-6628|archivedate=July 21, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tralala.gr/Party-All-The-Time-To-neo-tragoudi-ton-Playmen-me-thn-Elena-Paparizou/|title="Party All The Time" Το νέο τραγούδι των Playmen με την Έλενα Παπαρίζου!|work=tralala.gr|accessdate=June 21, 2012|language=Greek}}</ref> After the success of the song, Courtney collaborated once again with Playmen, in 2013, at the song "Breakin' Me Up" which was the music theme of the MadWalk 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hit-channel.com/playmen-ft-courtney-breakin-me-up/37812|title=Νέο τραγούδι: Playmen ft. Courtney – Breakin" me up|accessdate=March 21, 2013|language=Greek|work=hit-channel.com}}</ref>

Along with [[Tamta]], RiskyKidd, Midenistis and HouseTwins, Courtney presented her new song with RiskyKidd and HouseTwins, "Rhythm Is A Dancer", at the [[2013 MAD Video Music Awards]]. The song is a remake of Snap's song.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hit-channel.com/rythm-is-a-dancer-housetwins-courtney-riskykidd/48118|title=Rythm(sic) is a dancer: HouseTwins, Courtney & RiskyKidd ενώνουν τις δυνάμεις τους|accessdate=June 29, 2013|language=Greek|work=hit-channel.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tralala.gr/to-ekriktiko-remake-tou-rhythm-is-a-dancer-apo-tous-housetwins-feat-courtney-parker-riskykidd/|title=Το εκρηκτικό remake του "Rhythm is a dancer" από τους Housetwins feat. Courtney Parker & RiskyKidd!|work=tralala.gr|accessdate=June 29, 2013|language=Greek}}</ref> Courtney won two awards at the MAD VMA, all for her collaboration in the song "All the Time".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.madata.gr/diafora/showbiz/282621.html|title=Mad Video Music Awards 2013: Τα βραβεία|work=madata.gr|language=Greek|accessdate=June 25, 2013}}</ref>

In 2014, she released her debut [[Greek-language]] solo single, "Agapise Me" (love me), with music and lyrics written by fiancée and lead vocalist of [[Melisses]], Chris Mastoras.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.art-oholic.com/music-room/courtney-parker |title=Archived copy |accessdate=February 19, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150219075424/http://www.art-oholic.com/music-room/courtney-parker |archivedate=February 19, 2015 }}</ref>
