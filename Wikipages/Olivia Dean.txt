'''Olivia Lauryn Dean''' (born 14 March 1999) is an English singer. She began her career in collaboration with [[Rudimental]]. She was named [[Amazon Music]]'s 2021 breakthrough artist of the year<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://inews.co.uk/culture/music/london-soul-singer-olivia-dean-named-amazon-music-2021-breakthrough-artist-953137|title=London soul singer Olivia Dean named Amazon Music 2021 Breakthrough Artist|journal=INews|first=Adam|last=Sherwin|date=12 April 2021|accessdate=8 August 2021}}</ref> and recorded an exclusive version of Nat King Cole's "[[The Christmas Song]]" for the company's 2021 Christmas Originals line-up.<ref>https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/george-ezra-camila-cabello-and-alessia-cara-announced-for-amazon-exclusive-christmas-songs-line-up__34365/</ref>

==Early life==
Dean was born in the [[London Borough of Enfield|London Borough of Haringey t]]<nowiki/>o an English father and a Jamaican-Guyanese mother and grew up in [[Walthamstow]]. She took musical theatre lessons and participated in a gospel choir from a young age. She then attended the [[BRIT School]].<ref name="wonderland 1">{{Cite journal|url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2018/10/26/premiere-olivia-reason-stay/|title=Premiere: Olivia – 'Reason to Stay'|journal=Wonderland|date=26 October 2018|accessdate=9 August 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://1883magazine.com/olivia-dean/|title=Olivia Dean|journal=1883 Magazine|first=Kelsey|last=Barnes|accessdate=8 August 2021}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Dean has cited [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Amy Winehouse]], [[Carole King]], and [[The Supremes]] as inspirations.<ref name="wonderland 1"/><ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://schonmagazine.com/interview-olivia-dean/|title=interview olivia dean|journal=Schön!|first=Shama|last=Nasinde|date=10 August 2020|accessdate=8 August 2021}}</ref>
