'''Lehlogonolo Ronald Mataboge''' (born 27 March 1997), known professionally as '''A-Reece''', is a South African rapper, songwriter and record producer. He was born and raised in [[Atteridgeville]], [[Pretoria]], [[Gauteng]]. He is the younger brother of P-Jay, a rapper who is one half of the South African hip hop duo B3nchmarQ]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.soundcloud.com/pistolwhipapi |title=PistolWhipPapi}}</ref>

A-Reece was signed under [[Ambitiouz Entertainment]] in 2015. After disputes with management, he left to become an independent artist. He gained popularity in 2015 following the release of his single, "Couldn't", which featured the rapper [[Emtee]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cutfm.co.za/entertainment/entertainment-news/a-reece-biography-songs-new-album-houses-brother-girlfriend-age-cars/196/|title=A-Reece Biography|website=Cut FM}}</ref>

A-Reece released his debut studio album, ''[[Paradise (A-Reece album)|Paradise]]'' (2016), which produced the singles, "Couldn't" and "Mgani".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/a-reece-releases-paradise/|title=A-Reece RELEASES Paradise!|website=Zkhipani}}</ref> In 2017, A-Reece released his second album ''From Me to You & Only You'', He then released music video of Feelings with features Flvme his former colleague. In 2018 he released a collaborative album with Ecco and Wordz titled "''Long Lost Letters''" (L3) , the lead single was "Welcome To My Life". In 2019, he released ''Reece Effect''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/review-a-reece-from-me-to-you-only-you/|title=REVIEW:A-Reece-From Me To You & The World|website=Zkhipani}}</ref> He also released several [[extended plays|EPs]] notably being ''Gwan Big Up Urself'', Cutaways EP ''And I'm Only 21''.{{cn|date=November 2021}}

Following a financial squabble with Ambitiouz Entertainment, The Wrecking Crew (TWC) was formed with Mashbeatz. The Wrecking Crew consists of MashBeatz, Ex Global, Wordz, and other former members, Flvme, Ecco and Mellow. The Wrecking Crew changed names to Rubberband Records after the departure of Flvme, Ecco and Mellow.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.peoplemagazine.co.za/celebrity-news/a-reece-explains-why-he-left-ambitiouz-entertainment/|title=A Reece Explains Why He Left Ambitiouz Entertainment|website=People Magazine}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/chit-chat-reece-wrecking-crew-awesomefoursome/|title=Chit Chat with A-Reece &.The Wrecking Crew #AwesomeFoursome|website=Zkhipani}}</ref>

== Early life and career ==
Lehlogonolo Mataboge was born on 27 March 1997, and came from [[Pretoria]], Gauteng. He started writing music in primary school following the footsteps of his older brother Phologo Jody Mataboge, known as P-Jay from the hip hop duo B3nchMarQ and is close friends With PistolWhipPapi, Wordz, Mashbeatz & Hlumelo Moni. He got his stage name "A-Reece" from his star sign [[Aries (astrology)|Aries]].<ref>{{cite web|title=5 Things You Probably Didn't Know About A-Reece|url=http://www.youthvillage.co.za/2016/07/5-things-probably-didnt-know-reece/|website=Youth Village|access-date=November 17, 2016}}</ref>

In 2015, he signed to [[Ambitiouz Entertainment]] and released his first single "Couldn't" under the label, featuring Emtee. He released ''Cutaways'', an EP, on 29 September 2016, which consists of songs that did not make the cut for his debut album ''[[Paradise (A-Reece album)|Paradise]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=A-Reece Releases CUTaways EP|url=http://www.zkhiphani.co.za/a-reece-releases-cutaways-ep/|website=Zkhipani|access-date=November 17, 2016}}</ref> His debut album was later released on October 21, 2016, and topped the No.1 spot on [[iTunes]] just under 24 hours after its release.<ref>{{cite web|title=A-Reece's Album Goes Straight To Number ONE|url=http://www.zkhiphani.co.za/a-reeces-album-goes-straight-to-numberone/|website=Zkhipani|access-date=November 17, 2016}}</ref> He went on to release his second studio album ''From Me To You And Only You'' to celebrate the anniversary of his debut album. He release the collaboration album in 2019 with Mashbeatz named "Reece Effect". In the same year A-Reece released the songs "Carele$$" and "Blood In Blood Out".{{cn|date=November 2021}}

In 2021, he released the album ''Today's Tragedy, Tomorrow's Memory'' containing 13 tracks.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-27|title=A-Reece battles remorse on Today’s Tragedy, Tomorrow’s Memory|url=https://pan-african-music.com/en/a-reece-todays-tragedy-tomorrows-memory/|access-date=2021-08-28|website=PAM - Pan African Music|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Ambitiouz Entertainment controversy==
[[Ambitiouz Entertainment]] went under much scrutiny after artists [[Fifi Cooper]], B3nchMarQ, A-Reece and Flame left the label in a very public and ugly fight.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.peoplemagazine.co.za/celebrity-news/fifi-cooper-b3nchmarq-reece-leave-ambitiouz-entertainment|title=Fifi Cooper, B3nchmarq and A Reece Leave Ambitiouz {{!}} People Magazine|website=www.peoplemagazine.co.za|language=en-ZA|access-date=December 29, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.zalebs.com/whats-hot/leaving-ambitious-entertainment-artists-shouldnt-come-surprise/|title=The leaving of Ambitiouz Entertainment artists shouldn't come as a surprise|work=ZAlebs|access-date=December 29, 2017|language=en-GB}}</ref> Artists claimed that they decided to part ways with the record label because of financial disagreements. Fifi Cooper went on to say that she had been signed for over two years to the label yet had not received a single cent from her performances, as well as royalties.<ref>{{cite web|title=Shocker! Top rappers Fifi Cooper, B3nchmarq and A Reece Leave Ambitiouz|url=http://citizen.co.za/your-life/your-life-entertainment-your-life/entertainment-celebrities/1431475/shocker-top-rappers-fifi-cooper-b3nchmarq-reece-leave-ambitiouz/|website=The Citizen|access-date=February 18, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Breaking!!! Fifi Cooper, B3nchMarQ And A -Reece Leaves Ambitiouz Ent… Fifi Unveils Own Record Label|url=https://hiphopza.com/a-reece-reveals-the-unknown-truth-about-ambitiouz-entertainment-all-it-does-against-artists/|website=Hiphopza|access-date=February 18, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170218234523/http://www.zatrends.co.za/breaking-fifi-cooper-b3nchmarq-and-a-reece-leaves-ambitiouz-ent-fifi-unveils-own-record-label/|archive-date=February 18, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> The record label also went on to remove all of A-Reece's music videos from their YouTube channel and his Facebook page was also deleted. A-Reece Release "Loyal" where he address there reason why he left Ambitious Entertainment. In the song A-Reece slammed his former colleague Emtee for staying in the record label. 7 months later on his album ''Manando'', Emtee responded with a diss track called [[Crown]] calling A-Reece whack and emotional on songs.<ref>{{cite web|title=A-Reece Hits Back At Ambitiouz For Removing His Videos From YouTube|url=http://www.youthvillage.co.za/2017/03/a-reece-hits-back-at-ambitiouz-for-removing-his-videos-from-youtube/|website=Youth Village|access-date=May 8, 2018}}</ref>
