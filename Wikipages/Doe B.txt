'''Glenn Thomas''' (June 13, 1991 – December 28, 2013), better known by his stage name '''Doe B''', was an American rapper from [[Montgomery, Alabama|Montgomery]], Alabama. He was perhaps best known for being signed to fellow American [[Rapping|rapper]] [[T.I.]]'s Atlanta-based record label, [[Grand Hustle Records|Hustle Gang]].<ref name=cnn>{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2013/12/29/justice/alabama-rapper-killing/|author=Ben Brumfield|title=Rapper Doe B killed in Alabama|publisher=CNN|date=December 29, 2013|access-date=December 29, 2013}}</ref> Thomas was also known for his signature [[eye patch]], the result of a shooting that occurred in 2009. During his career he released several [[mixtape]]s, including ''Trap Life'' (2012) and ''Baby Jesus'' (2013); he was also featured on the 2013 Grand Hustle compilation ''[[G.D.O.D. (Get Dough or Die)]]''. Thomas was shot and killed on December 28, 2013, at the Centennial Hill Bar & Grill, in his hometown of Montgomery. There have been two [[wikt:posthumous|posthumous]] projects, ''D.O.A.T. 3 (Definition of a Trapper)'' and the compilation mixtape ''Doe B Presents C.B.M.: Choppaz, Brickz & Money'', released by Doe B's own label, Cote Block Music.

== Musical career==
On November 22, 2012, Doe B released his first full-length project, a [[mixtape]] titled ''Trap Life'', with Hoodrich Entertainment.<ref name="traplife">{{cite web | url=http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/18591/doe_b_trap_life.html | title=Mixtapes " DJs " DJ Frank White, DJ Scream " Doe B – Trap Life Download & Stream | publisher=LiveMixtapes | date=November 22, 2013 | access-date=January 15, 2014}}</ref> The mixtape, which hosted by American disc jockeys DJ Frank White and DJ Scream, includes [[hip hop production|production]] from [[Lex Luger (producer)|Lex Luger]], [[Zaytoven]], M16 and Will-A-Fool, among others.<ref name="traplife"/>

He was signed to [[Grand Hustle Records]] by the label's founder [[T.I.]] in 2012 and appeared on Hustle Gang's ''[[G.D.O.D. (Get Dough Or Die)]]'' compilation mixtape, which was released May 2013.<ref name=billboard>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5855046/doe-b-shot-killed-alabama-rapper-glenn-thomas-dead|title=Rapper Doe B Shot and Killed in Alabama|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=December 28, 2013|access-date=December 29, 2013}}</ref> He appeared on four tracks, "Kemosabe", "G.D.O.D.", "Real Niggas" and the [[remix]] of his song "Let Me Find Out". Shortly after signing to Grand Hustle, Doe B had secured a recording contract with [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Tardio |first=Andres |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26802/title.doe-b-s-management-releases-statement-regarding-rapper-s-passing/ |title=Doe B's Management Releases Statement Regarding Rapper's Passing &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |access-date=December 31, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131229053923/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26802/title.doe-b-s-management-releases-statement-regarding-rapper-s-passing/ |archive-date=December 29, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Doe B later released his own mixtape, the 18-track ''Baby Jesus'', in 2013. An alternative 21-track mixtape with 18 of the original and three more tracks was released and hosted by [[Maybach Music Group]]'s DJ Scream. Doe B also released music videos for "Let Me Find Out" featuring T.I. and Juicy J, "2 Many" featuring Rich Homie Quan, and "Trap Muzik".<ref name=billboard/>

His debut studio album had been expected to be released in early 2014, on Grand Hustle and Interscope.<ref name=hollywoodlife>{{cite news|url=http://hollywoodlife.com/2013/12/29/doe-b-shot-killed-last-tweet-before-death/|author=Andrew Gruttadaro|title=Doe B: His Tragic Final Tweet Before Being Shot & Killed|publisher=Hollywood Life|date=December 29, 2013|access-date=December 29, 2013}}</ref> Following his death, his management confirmed that his music would be released [[wikt:Posthumous|posthumously]] and that he had over 200 songs recorded.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/12/doe-bs-management-releases-statement-regarding-rappers-death/ |title=Doe B's Management Releases Statement Regarding Rapper's Death – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |access-date=December 31, 2013}}</ref> On December 30, 2013, a new [[Raekwon]] song, featuring Doe B and T.I., titled "I Wanna Know", was released. Later that day, Atlanta-based rapper [[Pastor Troy]], released a tribute song to Doe B, titled "Y'all Ain't Do Him Right".  T.I. released his own tribute song, which features Doe B, titled "Flying with Angels".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/singles/id.26343/title.T.I.+%26+Doe+B-Flying+With+Angels+%28RIP+Doe+B%29 |title=T.I. & Doe B – Flying With Angels (RIP Doe B) &#124; New Hip Hop Music & All The New Rap Songs 2011 |publisher=HipHop DX |date=January 2, 2014 |access-date=February 7, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/ti-doe-b-flying-with-angels |title=T.I. f/ Doe B "Flying With Angels" |magazine=Complex |access-date=February 7, 2014}}</ref> On January 9, 2014, T.I. revealed a [[teaser trailer|teaser]] for a Doe B song titled "Why", featuring himself. It was said to be a track featured on Doe B's upcoming posthumous debut album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/01/t-i-previews-new-doe-b-record/ |title=T.I. Previews New Doe B Record – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=January 9, 2014 |access-date=February 7, 2014}}</ref> Then five days later, "Why" was released for digital download.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2014/01/14/new-music-doe-b-f-ti-why/ |title=New Music: Doe B f/ T.I. – 'Why' |publisher=Rap-Up.com |date=January 14, 2014 |access-date=February 7, 2014}}</ref><ref name="apple1">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/why-feat.-t.i.-single/id797057107?ls=1 |title=iTunes – Music – Why (feat. T.I.) – Single by Doe B |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=January 14, 2014 |access-date=February 7, 2014}}</ref>

On April 1, 2014, Doe B's first full posthumous project titled ''D.O.A.T. 3 (Definition of a Trapper)'' was released. The mixtape features vocal guest appearances from T.I., [[Young Dro]], [[Trae Tha Truth]] and others. The production was handled by [[Zaytoven]], M16, [[K.E. on the Track]] and more producers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/03/doe-bs-definition-trapper-track-list-revealed/|title=Doe B's 'Definition Of A Trapper 3′ Track List Revealed – XXL|first=C. Vernon Coleman|last=II|website=XXL Mag}}</ref> On [[iTunes]] a Deluxe Edition of ''D.O.A.T. 3 (Definition of a Trapper)'' was released on the same day.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/d-o-a-t-3-definition-of-a-trapper-deluxe-edition/851600971|title=D.O.A.T. 3 (Definition of a Trapper) [Deluxe Edition] by Doe B|website=Music.apple.com}}</ref> The Deluxe Edition features 6 more tracks than the original, having 24 tracks in a total. [[Big Kuntry King]], T.I., [[Problem (rapper)|Problem]], Shad Da God are some of the guest appearances featured on the Deluxe Edition, that aren't on the original. One of those 6 tracks "Homicide" featuring T.I. was turned into the second single of ''D.O.A.T. 3 (Definition of a Trapper)''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/homicide-feat-t-i-single/872848016|title=Homicide (feat. T.I.) – Single by Doe B|website=Music.apple.com}}</ref>
On June 13, 2014 ''Doe B Presents C.B.M.: Choppaz, Brickz & Money'' was released. It's a compilation mixtape by Doe B's own label Cote Block Music (CBM). The production was handled by Kartlin Bankz, [[808 Mafia]], [[Zaytoven]] and others. Doe B appears on 7 tracks of the mixtape.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/24021/doe-b-presents-cbm-choppaz-brickz-money.html|title=LiveMixtapes.com}}</ref> A mixtape entitled ''The Best Of Doe B So Far'' was released at the end of 2015. Karltin Bankz later on released remixes of both "Trap Muzik" and "Hood Memories". In early 2017, a new Doe B album cover of Doe B lying in his casket was released. The cover caused controversy and was later changed. Doe B's manager DJ Frank White, along with Doe B's mother announced a new Doe B album, that would be released on June 13, 2017, Doe B's birthday. The album's name is titled "No life After Death". RIP to the late Doe B

==Personal life==
Doe B was born Glenn Thomas in Montgomery, Alabama, on June 13, 1991. He was the son of Shirley Thomas and Kenneth Aaron. He wore an eye patch as a result of a shooting in 2009. He had 3 daughters.

==Death==
On December 28, 2013, Doe B was shot at the Centennial Bar and Grill in Montgomery, Alabama. He was rushed to a nearby hospital where he was pronounced dead. A bystander, Kimberle Johnson, was also killed,<ref name=cnn/> and six other patrons were injured, including rapper Young Mayo, who is currently serving time for drug possession.<ref>{{cite web|last=Barrett |first=Annie |url= https://ew.com/article/2013/12/29/rapper-doe-b-killed-in-club-shooting-at-22/|title=Rapper Doe B killed in club shooting at 22 |website=Entertainment Weekly|date=December 29, 2013 |access-date=February 26, 2020}}</ref> The Centennial was housed in what was earlier known as the "Rose Supper Club", which was the subject of a shooting in December 2012 that left six people injured.<ref name="billboard1">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5855046/doe-b-shot-killed-alabama-rapper-glenn-thomas-dead |title=Rapper Doe B Shot and Killed in Alabama |magazine=Billboard |access-date=December 31, 2013}}</ref> It had also been the location of multiple shootings over the past seven years.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/news/national/rapper-doe-b-shot-dead-saturday-montgomery-ala-article-1.1560760 |title=Doe B, member of T.I.'s Grand Hustle, dead at 22 after fatal shooting |newspaper=NY Daily News |date=October 21, 2013 |access-date=December 31, 2013}}</ref> The name of the venue had been changed after the most recent incident. After the latest shooting, Mayor [[Todd Strange (politician)|Todd Strange]] ordered the club to close immediately.<ref name="billboard1"/>

On December 30, 2013, 25-year-old Jason McWilliams of Montgomery turned himself in to MPD detectives. He would then be charged with two counts of [[capital murder]] and held without bond.<ref>{{cite web|last=Jinright |first=Rachel |url=http://www.wsfa.com/story/24327363/mpd-makes-arrest-in-fatal-weekend-shooting |title=Gunman charged in rapper Doe B slaying – WSFA.com: News Weather and Sports for Montgomery, AL |publisher=WSFA.com |date=January 2, 2013 |access-date=December 31, 2013}}</ref> Montgomery Police Department detectives concluded that the "shooting stemmed from an ongoing dispute between McWilliams and Doe B."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/nation/2013/12/30/alabama-nightclub-shooting/4248753/ |title=Suspect accused of killing 2 at Ala. nightclub arrested |publisher=Usatoday.com |date=December 27, 2013 |access-date=December 31, 2013}}</ref> A second local 25-year-old man, named Darius Thomas, was also arrested for the murders and charged accordingly. On January 3, 2014, a third shooting victim, 20-year-old Tim Hamilton, died from the gunshot wounds.<ref>{{cite web|first=Nolan|last=Strong|url=http://allhiphop.com/2014/01/04/third-victims-dies-in-doe-b-shooting-incident/ |title=Third Victim Dies In Doe B. Shooting Incident |publisher=AllHipHop.com |date=January 4, 2014 |access-date=February 7, 2014}}</ref> On January 10, 2018, Darius Thomas was sentenced to 85 years in prison after pleading guilty to the deaths of Doe B, Kim Johnson, and Tim Hamilton.

=== Burial ===
Doe B's funeral was held in Montgomery, Alabama on January 4, 2014. T.I. spoke at the funeral praising Doe B and speaking about how bright of a future in music he really had.<ref>{{cite web|last=Balfour |first=Jay |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.26884/title.t-i-speaks-at-doe-b-s-funeral |title=T.I. Speaks At Doe B's Funeral &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=January 5, 2014 |access-date=February 7, 2014}}</ref> On January 11, 2014, it was reported that three of the five surviving victims of the shooting were suing the Centennial Hill Bar & Grill. The plaintiffs, Keldric Landon, Clarence Young, Jr. and Ferro Bennett, have filed lawsuits against Centennial, the Flight LLC, its owner Nicole Bankhead and the club's security company. Landon was the most injured, suffering multiple gunshot injuries requiring surgery. All three victims claimed in the lawsuit that the club's bouncers were bribed into allowing weapons into the building. They are seeking compensation for medical expenses, loss of work and pain and suffering.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/01/victims-gunfire-doe-b-shooting-suing-venue-claim-security-bribed/ |title=Victims Of Gunfire In Doe B Shooting Suing Venue, Claim Security Was Bribed – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=January 11, 2014 |access-date=February 7, 2014}}</ref>

On September 30, 2014, it was discovered that someone had tried to disturb Thomas' grave. His mother has expressed concern and is considering moving his body to an unmarked grave {{citation needed|date=July 2021}}.
