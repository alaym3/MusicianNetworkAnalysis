==Early life==
Dphrepaulezz was born in western [[Massachusetts]], the eighth of fifteen children.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.sfgate.com/music/article/Fantastic-Negrito-Oakland-musician-lives-up-to-6187896.php |title=Fantastic Negrito: Oakland musician lives up to new name |last=Vaziri |first=Aidin |website=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |date=2015-04-28}}</ref> His father was a deeply religious Somali Muslim who, Dphrepaulezz recalls, had "a lot of rules" for his children.<ref name=guardian/> Dphrepaulezz and his family relocated to [[Oakland, California|Oakland]], California, when he was 12 years old.<ref name=npr/> He began selling drugs as a teenager in Oakland, telling ''the Guardian'', "We were all selling drugs, man. We all carried pistols. There was a crack epidemic. [....] I was the kind of kid who would sell fake weed [...]. Sometimes I would use tea."<ref name=guardian/> He became inspired to teach himself how to play music after listening to [[Prince (musician)|Prince]]'s album ''[[Dirty Mind]]'' and hearing that Prince was a self-taught musician. He learned to play music by sneaking into music classrooms at the [[University of California Berkeley]] despite not being a student there.<ref name=guardian>{{Cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/aug/02/fantastic-negrito-the-drug-dealing-hustler-who-became-bernie-sanders-favourite-bluesman |title=Fantastic Negrito: the drug-dealing hustler who became Bernie Sanders' favourite bluesman |last=Paphides |first=Pete |website=[[The Guardian]] |date=2016-08-02}}</ref>

==Career==
Dphrepaulezz signed an early record deal with Prince's former manager. In 1993, he signed with [[Interscope Records]].<ref name=guardian/> On January 9, 1996, he released his first album, ''The X Factor'', under the mononym Xavier. The album was released on [[Lexington House Records]] and distributed by Interscope.<ref name=guardian/><ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Borzillo |first=Carrie |title=Popular Uprisings |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3g4EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA17 |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=1996-01-20 |volume=108 |issue=3 |page=17}}</ref> He was in a near-fatal car crash in 1999, which left him in a coma for three weeks.<ref name=wired/> He has said that he felt that this crash "released" him because Interscope terminated their contract with him, after which he set up an illegal nightclub in South Central Los Angeles.<ref name=guardian/> In 2007, he stopped making music, but he returned in 2014, describing his new musical style as "black roots music for everyone".<ref name=wired/> In 2015, he won ''[[NPR]]''{{'}}s [[Tiny Desk Contest]].<ref name=npr>{{Cite web |url=https://www.npr.org/2016/05/26/478235309/first-listen-fantastic-negrito-the-last-days-of-oakland |title=Review: Fantastic Negrito, 'The Last Days Of Oakland' |last=Heller |first=Jason |website=[[NPR]] |date=May 26, 2016}}</ref> In 2016, his album ''[[The Last Days of Oakland]]'' was released on the [[Blackball Universe]] label.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.popmatters.com/column/fantastic-negritos-working-poor-is-the-soundtrack-to-the-summer-of-16/ |title=Fantastic Negrito's "Working Poor" Is the Soundtrack to the Summer of '16 |last=Loss |first=Robert |website=[[PopMatters]] |date=2016-07-13}}</ref> Dphrepaulezz won his first Grammy in 2017, when ''The Last Days of Oakland'' received the [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Blues Album]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.sfgate.com/music/article/Fantastic-Negrito-wins-first-Grammy-for-Best-10927506.php |title=Fantastic Negrito wins first Grammy for Best Contemporary Blues album |last=Pereira |first=Alyssa |website=[[San Francisco Chronicle]] |date=February 12, 2017 |access-date=February 19, 2017}}</ref> He won his second Grammy with his next album, ''Please Don't Be Dead'', which won "Best Contemporary Blues Album" at the [[61st Annual Grammy Awards|2019 Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://datebook.sfchronicle.com/music/oaklands-fantastic-negrito-continues-winning-streak-at-grammy-awards|title=Oakland's Fantastic Negrito continues winning streak at Grammy Awards|website=Datebook.sfchronicle.com|accessdate=January 20, 2020}}</ref>
