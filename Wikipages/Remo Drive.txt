'''Remo Drive''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Bloomington, Minnesota]].<ref name="mndaily">{{cite web|last1=Cristo|first1=Joe|title=Minneapolis emo brand, Remo Drive, garners local acclaim|url=http://www.mndaily.com/article/2017/04/minneapolis-emo-brand-remo-drive-garners-local-acclaim|publisher=[[Minnesota Daily]]|accessdate=16 August 2017}}</ref> They are currently signed to Epitaph Records. They released their first full length album titled ''[[Greatest Hits (Remo Drive album)|Greatest Hits]]'' on March 16, 2017. Their second studio album ''[[Natural, Everyday Degradation]]'' was released on May 31, 2019. Their third, ''[[A Portrait of an Ugly Man]]'', was released on June 26, 2020.

==History==
Remo Drive began in 2013, Erik and Stephen Paulson were in High School when they saw kids wearing [[Title Fight]] shirts, They both got into the band and decided they wanted to make music like them. The band started recording demos in 2013, also known as EP 1. They met their drummer Sam Mathys when Stephen was in a different band with him and asked if he wanted to jam with them, Later in 2014 they started releasing a collection of songs on the website [[Bandcamp]] under the title ''Demos 2014,'' This was the band's first time recording with Mathys including EPs such as ''Away'' and ''Stay Out Longer''.<ref name="citypages">{{cite web|last1=Diamond|first1=Peter|title=You can't spell Remo Drive without 'emo', but this band likes Sting too|url=http://www.citypages.com/music/you-cant-spell-remo-drive-without-emo-but-this-band-likes-sting-too/418462193|publisher=[[City Pages]]|accessdate=16 August 2017}}</ref> The band got its name when bassist Stephen Paulson decided to incorporate the drum brand, [[Remo]], into a band name.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DCWoOa2dYdY</ref> In 2015 Mathys left the band due to creative differences, They had Austin Voigt Fill in for him. The same year Remo Drive released an EP titled ''Wait For The Sun'' on Lost State Records. Also in 2015, Remo Drive released a split with the band Unturned<ref name="citypages"/> as well as a split with the band Weathered. In Early 2016 Voigt left the band due to personal hardship, and Mathys rejoined the band. In March 2016 they began working a their first LP. <ref name="36vultures">{{cite web|last1=Fugger|first1=Scott|title=Remo Drive: A Band Reborn|url=http://36vultures.net/interview/remo-drive-a-band-reborn|publisher=36vultures|accessdate=16 August 2017}}</ref> In March 2017, Remo Drive self-released their first full-length album, ''Greatest Hits'', featuring tracks "Yer Killin Me", "Art School", and "Crash Test Rating". 

In Early 2018, Mathys announced on social media that he had been removed as a member of Remo Drive in December 2017.

In February 2018, the band announced that they had signed with [[Epitaph Records]]. This announcement came alongside the release of a single, Blue Ribbon, from their EP Pop Music, which was released on March 9.<ref name="punknews">{{cite web|last1=Crawford|first1=Sean|title=Remo Drive: Greatest Hits (2017)|url=https://www.punknews.org/review/14991/remo-drive-greatest-hits|publisher=Punknews|accessdate=16 August 2017}}</ref><ref name="sputnikmusic">{{cite web|title=Remo Drive: Greatest Hits|url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/73295/Remo-Drive-Greatest-Hits/|publisher=[[Sputnikmusic]]|accessdate=16 August 2017}}</ref><ref name="thebatesstudent">{{cite web|last1=Lemus|first1=Nicolas|title=Remo Drive's Greatest Hits delivers a momentous debut|url=http://www.thebatesstudent.com/2017/04/remo-drives-greatest-hits-delivers-a-momentous-debut/|publisher=[[The Bates Student]]|accessdate=16 August 2017}}</ref>

In late August of 2018, Remo Drive announced that they were planning on recording a new album that December. On April 22, 2019, the band released a double A-side single, "Two Bux/The Grind," ahead of their sophomore effort ''Natural, Everyday Degradation'' on May 31st.

In May of that year, Erik Paulson announced that he hid promo CDs of the next single around Twin Cities, Minnesota, before releasing "Around the Sun" as a single on May 14th.

In late 2019, Remo Drive posted on social media that they were working on their third album, ''[[A Portrait of an Ugly Man]]'', which was properly announced on April 28th, 2020 for a June release, along with the first single, "Star Worship" on the same day. Two other singles were released, "Ode to Joy 2" on May 18th, 2020 and "A Flower and a Weed" on June 17th, 2020. The album released on June 26th, 2020, along with another single "Easy as That".

==Musical style==
Remo Drive is typically described as an [[emo]] band<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.citypages.com/music/you-cant-spell-remo-drive-without-emo-but-this-band-likes-sting-too/418462193|title=You can't spell Remo Drive without 'emo', but this band likes Sting too - City Pages|first=Peter|last=Diamond|website=City Pages}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.punknews.org/review/14991/remo-drive-greatest-hits|title=Remo Drive - Greatest Hits|last=Punknews.org|website=www.punknews.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/73295/Remo-Drive-Greatest-Hits/|title=Remo Drive - Greatest Hits (album review ) - Sputnikmusic|website=www.sputnikmusic.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://36vultures.net/interview/remo-drive-a-band-reborn|title=Remo Drive: A Band Reborn - 36vultures|date=21 March 2017|publisher=}}</ref> who incorporate elements of [[indie rock]], [[power pop]], [[pop punk]], [[dance punk]], [[post-hardcore]], and in some tracks/recordings [[grunge]] and [[punk rock]]. The group has also self described themselves as emo and associated themselves with the emo revival.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://overblown.co.uk/remo-drive-interview-talking-the-needle-drop-emo-music-and-minnesota/|title=Remo Drive Interview: Talking The Needle Drop, Emo Music, & Minnesota|first=Sam|last=Rosean|date=7 February 2017|publisher=}}</ref> The band has listed a number of acts as influences including [[Title Fight]], [[The Police]], [[Pup (band)|PUP]], [[Jeff Rosenstock]], [[Vampire Weekend]], and [[Weezer]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wordsformusic.blog/2017/04/02/interview-with-remo-drive/|title=Interview with Remo Drive!|date=2 April 2017|publisher=}}</ref>
