'''Nicholas James Murphy''' (born 23 June 1988), known professionally as '''Chet Faker''', is an Australian singer and songwriter. In 2012, as Chet Faker, he issued an extended play, ''[[Thinking in Textures]]'', and signed to [[Downtown Records]] in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|last=Mann|first=Tom|title=The Rise and Rise of Melbourne's Soul Scene|url=http://www.fasterlouder.com.au/features/34600/The-rise-and-rise-of-Melbournes-soul-scene|publisher=Faster Louder|date=10 January 2013|access-date=27 January 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130218141301/http://www.fasterlouder.com.au/features/34600/The-rise-and-rise-of-Melbournes-soul-scene|archive-date=18 February 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Tame Impala Win at Rolling Stone Awards|url=http://au.news.yahoo.com/thewest/a/-/breaking/15854382/tame-impala-win-at-rolling-stone-awards/|publisher=The West|date=16 January 2013|access-date=27 January 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130128004540/http://au.news.yahoo.com/thewest/a/-/breaking/15854382/tame-impala-win-at-rolling-stone-awards/|archive-date=28 January 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> In October 2012, he won Breakthrough Artist of the Year and ''Thinking in Textures'' won Best Independent Single/EP at the [[Australian Independent Record Labels Association#AIR Awards|Australian Independent Records Awards]].<ref name="Roberts">{{cite news |url= http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/chet-faker-wins-big-at-music-awards-20121016-27pjk.html |title=Chet Faker wins big at music awards |last=Roberts |first=Jo |work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]  |date=16 October 2012 |access-date=31 January 2013}}</ref> In January 2013, the work won Best Independent Release at the [[Rolling Stone Australia Awards|''Rolling Stone'' Australia Awards]] for 2012.<ref name="Rolling Stone Awards 2012" />

Murphy's cover of [[Blackstreet]]'s "[[No Diggity]]" was featured in a 2013 [[Super Bowl advertising|Super Bowl commercial]] for [[Beck's Brewery|Beck's]] Sapphire.<ref>{{cite web|title=Beck's Sapphire Commercial – Super Bowl 2013|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6tREvbQqBoA|access-date=24 September 2013|date=31 January 2013}}</ref> In April 2014, ''[[Built on Glass]]'', his debut studio album, was released to generally positive reviews and debuted at number one on the Australian [[ARIA Charts]].<ref name="BuiltonGlassMetacritic">{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/built-on-glass/chet-faker|title=Built on Glass Reviews|publisher=[[Metacritic]]|access-date=20 April 2014}}</ref><ref name="AUS-albums"/> Three tracks from the album were voted into the top&nbsp;ten of [[Triple J]]'s [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2014|Hottest 100 of 2014]], including the number one spot for "[[Talk Is Cheap (Chet Faker song)|Talk Is Cheap]]".<ref>{{cite web|title=Triple J's Hottest 100 of 2014|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/14/|access-date=27 January 2015}}</ref>

In September 2016, Murphy dropped the Chet Faker name to start performing and releasing material under his birth name, beginning with the release of a pair of singles later that year, and the ''[[Missing Link (EP)|Missing Link]]'' extended play in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Moskovitch|first=Greg|date=2016-09-09|title=Chet Faker Has Officially Changed His Name|url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/chet-faker-officially-changes-name/|access-date=2020-10-04|website=Tone Deaf|language=en-AU}}</ref> Murphy's second studio album – the first under his birth name – ''Run Fast Sleep Naked'' was released in April 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Newstead|first=Al|date=2019-04-26|title=Nick Murphy has made an album that Chet Faker never could|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/nick-murphy-run-fast-sleep-naked-feature-album-interview-review/11049428|access-date=2020-10-04|website=triple j|language=en}}</ref> This was followed up by a surprise [[instrumental]] album, ''Music for Silence'', in March 2020, initially released via the [[Calm (company)|Calm]] meditation app.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Young|first=David James|date=2020-03-10|title=Nick Murphy releases surprise instrumental album, 'Music For Silence'|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/nick-murphy-surprise-album-music-for-silence-2622792|access-date=2020-10-04|website=[[New Musical Express|NME]]|language=en-AU}}</ref>

In October 2020, four years after dropping the moniker, Murphy revived the Chet Faker name for the release of a new single, "Low".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fuamoli|first=Sose|date=2020-10-02|title=Nick Murphy revives Chet Faker name for new song, the textline reacts|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/first-spin-chet-faker-comes-out-of-retirement-low/12725296|access-date=2020-10-04|website=[[triple j]]|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== Early career ===

Murphy played under his stage name after people came to his shows thinking he was a different and already established musician, Nick Murphy. He settled on the name as a homage to [[Chet Baker]]: "I listened to a lot of jazz and I was a big fan of ... the way he sang, when he moved into mainstream singing. He had this really fragile vocal style—this really, broken, close-up and intimate style. The name is kind of just an ode to Chet Baker and the mood of music he used to play—something I would like to at least pay homage to in my own music."<ref name=InterviewMag>{{cite web|last=Brown|first=Emma|title=Discovery: Chet Faker|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/discovery-chet-faker#_|publisher=Interview|access-date=24 September 2013}}</ref> Other influences he has cited include [[Bob Dylan]], as well as his mother's [[Motown]] albums and his father's "chilled out [[Ibiza]] CDs".<ref name=InterviewMag/><ref>{{cite web|last=Emery|first=Patrick|title=Chet Faker|url=http://www.beat.com.au/music/chet-faker|work=Beat Magazine|access-date=24 September 2013|date=6 December 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180203064100/http://www.beat.com.au/music/chet-faker|archive-date=3 February 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== ''Thinking in Textures'' and collaborations (2011–13) ===

He first rose to prominence after his cover of [[Blackstreet]]'s "[[No Diggity]]" went viral online, reaching number&nbsp;one on the [[Hypemachine]] chart in May 2011.<ref name=InterviewMag/> He released his first extended play, ''[[Thinking in Textures]]'', on 22 March 2012 to positive reviews, being described as "wonderfully loungey" and praised for its ability to "mix subtlety with impressive beauty".<ref>{{cite web|last=Carrow|title=Chet Faker – Thinking in Textures EP|url=http://thebluewalrus.com/2012/03/29/chet-faker-thinking-in-textures-ep/|publisher=The Blue Walrus|access-date=24 September 2013|date=29 March 2012}}</ref> The EP was also popular with fans, with its second single, "I'm into You", landing at number&nbsp;24 on [[Triple J]]'s [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2012|Hottest 100 of 2012]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Scott|title=Chet Faker Interview|url=http://www.ripitup.com.au/music/article/chet-faker-interview1|publisher=Rip It Up|access-date=24 September 2013|date=13 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141205185839/http://www.ripitup.com.au/music/article/chet-faker-interview1|archive-date=5 December 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Murphy has collaborated with [[Flume (musician)|Flume]] and remixes of songs by [[MS MR]] and [[The Temper Trap]]. He was a featured vocalist on [[Say Lou Lou]]'s "Fool of Me", which was named Best New Track by ''[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]'' in May 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last=Zoladz|first=Lindsay|title=Say Lou Lou: "Fool of Me" [ft. Chet Faker]|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/15398-say-lou-lou-fool-of-me-ft-chet-faker/|access-date=24 September 2013|date=6 May 2013}}</ref>

=== ''Lockjaw EP'' and ''Built on Glass'' (2013–15) ===

[[File:Chet Faker, Positivus Music Festival, Latvia 2014.jpg|thumb|Murphy performing at the [[Positivus Festival|Positivus Music Festival]], [[Salacgrīva]], Latvia in July 2014.]]
On 12 August 2013, Murphy released a new single, "Melt", featuring American vocalist [[Kilo Kish]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Crax|title=Chet Faker Ft. Kilo Kish – "Melt"|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/2013/08/chet-faker-ft-kilo-kish-melt/|publisher=Pigeons & Planes|access-date=24 September 2013|date=12 August 2013}}</ref> In November 2013 Flume and Murphy released an EP, titled ''[[Lockjaw (EP)|Lockjaw]]''. Murphy released his debut studio album, ''[[Built on Glass]]'', on 11 April 2014.<ref name="The Ripe">{{cite web|title=CHET FAKER – Talk Is Cheap|url=http://www.theripe.tv/chet-faker-talk-is-cheap/|publisher=The Ripe|access-date=12 February 2014}}</ref> The album's full release was preceded by its lead single, "[[Talk Is Cheap (Chet Faker song)|Talk Is Cheap]]", and an accompanying music video on 11 February 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hear: Chet Faker's sensuous new single 'Talk Is Cheap'|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s3942959.htm|publisher=[[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]]|access-date=12 February 2014}}</ref> The album debuted at number&nbsp;one on the [[ARIA Charts]].<ref name="AUS-albums"/>

Murphy performed at the Boston Calling Music Festival in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ollman|first=Jonah|title=Boston Calling Announces May 2015 Lineup|url=http://soundofboston.com/boston-calling-announces-may-2015-lineup/|publisher=Sound of Boston|access-date=15 January 2015}}</ref> Later that month, "Talk Is Cheap" was named the number-one song in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2014|2014 Triple J Hottest 100]], while two other singles, "[[Gold (Chet Faker song)|Gold]]" and "[[1998 (Chet Faker song)|1998]]", also reached the top ten, placing at number&nbsp;7 and 8, respectively.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hottest 100 2014|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/14/countdown/|work=[[Triple J]]|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|access-date=29 January 2015|date=26 January 2014}}</ref> In June, Murphy released a new single, "[[Bend (song)|Bend]]", a previously unreleased track intended for ''Built on Glass''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/aus/artists/music/chet-faker/songs/bend/|title= Chet Faker – Songs – Bend|publisher=itunescharts.net|date=20 June 2015|access-date=22 June 2015}}</ref> It was promoted by his Australian ''Built on Live'' tour in October.<ref>{{cite web|title=Update: Chet Faker premieres 'Bend', announces national tour|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/musicnews/s4256118.htm|work=Triple J|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|access-date=28 June 2015|date=17 June 2015}}</ref>

Murphy released a new EP, ''[[Work (EP)|Work]]'', a collaboration with London-based DJ Marcus Marr on 4 December 2015.<ref>{{cite web|last=Cashmere|first=Paul|title=New Chet Faker With Marcus Marr 'The Trouble With Us' #LISTEN|url=http://www.noise11.com/news/new-chet-faker-with-marcus-marr-the-trouble-with-us-listen-20151016|publisher= Noise11|access-date=27 October 2015|date=16 October 2015}}</ref>

=== Nick Murphy (2016–2019) ===

On 8 September 2016, the artist wrote on his Facebook page, "It's been half a decade since I started releasing music as Chet Faker and all of you have been the driving force behind the music since. There's an evolution happening and I wanted to let you know where it's going. The next record will be under my own name, Nick Murphy. Chet Faker will always be a part of the music. This is next."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/Chetfaker/posts/1088075637897050|title=Nick Murphy fka Chet Faker|via=Facebook|language=en|access-date=2017-11-12}}{{Primary source inline|date=December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.noise11.com/news/nick-murphy-shares-fear-less-new-music-20160913|title=Nicholas Murphy Shares Fear Less New Music|work=PAUL CASHMERE|publisher=Noise11|date=13 September 2016|access-date=14 September 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/chet-faker-will-now-make-music-real-name-nick-murphy/|title= Chet Faker Will Now Make Music Under His Real Name Nicholas Murphy |work=Zanda Wilson|publisher=Music Feeds|date=9 September 2016|access-date=14 September 2016}}</ref>

===Chet Faker revival (2020)===
In October 2020, Murphy revived the Chet Faker social media accounts and released "Low", his first single under the Chet Faker moniker since 2016. This marked his first release through [[BMG Music Australia|BMG Australia & New Zealand]], with whom he had recently signed a record deal.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bruce |first1=Jasper |title=Nick Murphy to readopt Chet Faker moniker, teases new music |url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/nick-murphy-to-readopt-chet-faker-moniker-teases-new-music-2766225 |website=NME |access-date=4 October 2020 |date=1 October 2020}}</ref>
