'''Dafina Zeqiri''' ({{IPA-sq|daˈfina ˈzɛˈciɾi|lang}}; born 14 April 1989), also known mononymously as '''Dafina''', is a Kosovo-Albanian singer, songwriter and composer.<ref name="TeksteShqip">{{cite web |publisher=TeksteShqip |title=Dafina Zeqiri–Biografia |url=https://www.teksteshqip.com/dafina-zeqiri |access-date=9 February 2020 |language=sq}}</ref><ref name="Top Channel">{{cite web |publisher=[[Top Channel]] |title=Dafina Zeqiri #Bio |url=http://topawards.top-channel.tv/index.php/artist/dafina-zeqiri/ |access-date=9 February 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201021183049/http://topawards.top-channel.tv/index.php/artist/dafina-zeqiri/ |archive-date=21 October 2020 |language=sq}}</ref> Zeqiri is noted for her versatility in music and considered an influential figure in contemporary Albanian music.

== Life and career ==

Dafina Zeqiri was born on 14 April 1989 into an [[Albanians|Albanian]] family from [[Kosovo]] in the city of [[Varberg]], Sweden.<ref name="TeksteShqip"/><ref name="Top Channel"/> Her siblings are Tringa Zeqiri and Besa Tafari ''née'' Zeqiri.<ref>{{cite web |last=Hoxha |first=E. |publisher=Iconstyle |title='Babai im!' In 2007, Dafina performed in Polifest Festival and is nominated for her song, "Rrallë e Përmallë," and eventually won the competition. In 2008, Dafina received a BOOM Award Nomination for her song "Adios," and was awarded with the audience award. In the same year, she launched her first album titled "Knock Down". Shortly afterwards, in her continued participation in "Kënga Magjike," she received the "First Magic" award with her song, "Bateritë".

Zeqiri's success continued with two collaborations with known singers Blero & F-Kay with the song "La Vida Loca" and Capital T & 2po2, "Shumë Naltë". Both songs turned into hits of the summer. In May 2009, she won "Best RNB" in Top Fest 6 with the song "Dua që ta di," a song which was a great success. Then Duffy'e also made the a mix of the song with the acclaimed DJ-Zap. During 2009, two of her clips were very successful: "Shumë Larg" (directed by Stamps Pictures) and "Amazing Girl" (Directed by Entermedia).<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.teksteshqip.com/dafina-zeqiri/lajme_1103.php |title=Dafina nuk din të ndalet |access-date=2013-09-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121016134825/http://teksteshqip.com/dafina-zeqiri/lajme_1103.php |archive-date=2012-10-16 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.teksteshqip.com/dafina-zeqiri/lajme_2306.php |title=E lumtur pas suksesit të klipit të ri |access-date=2013-09-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121016134418/http://teksteshqip.com/dafina-zeqiri/lajme_2306.php |archive-date=2012-10-16 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2010, she traveled to Australia to shoot the music video for "Rock This Club." The video clip of the song "Amazing Girl" by Dafina was the main winner of this year's edition of Music Videofest, sweeping away the award for best videospot, 'Best of All'. She was nominated in eight categories and won four of them. In December 2011, Dafina Zeqiri released her newest album. In her album, the song D&G was included, a song she has made with Albanian rapper Getoar Selimi. She has already launched "Tonight," which is also included in the new album "Just Me".
She launched 2 new music videos "Veç Ti" and "Bloodah" which were successful.
