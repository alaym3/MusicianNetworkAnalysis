'''Darak Kings Figueroa''' (born August 6, 2001), professionally known as '''StaySolidRocky''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. Born in [[San Antonio]], Texas, he was raised in [[Richmond, Virginia]]. He rose to fame in 2020 with his single "[[Party Girl (StaySolidRocky song)|Party Girl]]", which peaked at number 21 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].

==Early life==
Figueroa was born in [[San Antonio]], Texas. His father is of Puerto Rican descent. He was raised by his mother, a former rapper herself.<ref name="Nine">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/columns/hip-hop/9380970/staysolidrocky-party-girl-interview|title=Nine Things To Know About 'Party Girl' Star StaySolidRocky, As Explained By the Rapper Himself|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Lamarre|first=Carl|date=May 19, 2020|accessdate=June 3, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Bleu">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bleumag.com/2020/05/30/staysolidrocky-the-rapper-behind-hit-single-party-girl/|title=StaySolidRocky: The Rapper Behind Hit Single "Party Girl"|work=Bleu Mag|last=Rodriguez|first=Tommy|date=May 30, 2020|accessdate=June 3, 2020}}</ref> He started expressing an interest in music when he moved in with his father, and in high school in [[Richmond, Virginia]], he began free styling and started using his [[Instagram]] platform to share snippets of music he had been working on. He soon began building a fanbase on Instagram.<ref name="Bleu" />

==Career==
Figueroa started to gain popularity after he released the music video for his single "[[Party Girl (StaySolidRocky song)|Party Girl]]" in December 2019.<ref name="Nine" /> The track sat at 2 million views on YouTube before it went viral on the video-sharing platform [[TikTok]], which propelled Figueroa to stardom. He got involved with Solomon Sobande, the former manager of [[XXXTentacion]], and shortly afterwards, he signed a deal with [[Columbia Records]].<ref name="Nine" /> His debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''[[Fallin' (EP)|Fallin'<nowiki/>]]'' was released on July 17, 2020. The 7-track EP included "Party Girl".<ref name="Fall" />

==Influences==
Growing up, StaySolidRocky would listen to veteran hip hop artists [[Bone Thugs-n-Harmony]] and [[Lil Wayne]]. As he started making his own music, he began listening to [[NBA YoungBoy]], [[Kevin Gates]] and [[Kodak Black]].<ref name="Nine" />
