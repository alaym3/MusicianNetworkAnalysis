'''PrettyMuch''' (stylized in [[all caps]]) are an American-Canadian [[Pop music|pop]] and [[contemporary R&B|R&B]] [[boy band]] based in [[Los Angeles]], California. Formed by [[Simon Cowell]] in 2016, the band consists of Brandon Arreaga, Edwin Honoret, Austin Porter, Nick Mara, and Zion Kuwonu.

==Career==
===2016&ndash;2017: Formation and early career===
The members of PrettyMuch were pursuing solo careers until [[Syco Music]] founder Simon Cowell and former label president Sonny Takhar merged them into a group.<ref name="Variety">{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2017/music/news/prettymuch-simon-cowell-boy-band-teen-choice-1202509967/|title=PrettyMuch, Simon Cowell's Latest Boy Band Discovery, Poised to Pop|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|first=Michele Amabile|last=Angermiller |date=July 28, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Billboard">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7940930/prettymuch-why-dont-we-cnco-boy-bands-interview|title=Meet the Next Wave of Boy Bands: Prettymuch, Why Don't We and CNCO|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Steven J.|last=Horowitz|date=August 24, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref> Since 2016, the members of PrettyMuch have been based in [[Los Angeles]] where they have shared a home since March 18, 2016, in which they have worked on their dancing skills in addition to recording their debut EP.<ref name="Variety" /><ref name="NYDailyNews">{{cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/life-style/new-simon-cowell-boy-band-pretty-latest-article-1.3502651|title=New Simon Cowell boy band is Prettymuch the latest thing|website=[[New York Daily News]]|first=Ray|last=Monell|date=September 17, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref>

In 2017, PrettyMuch gained attention on social media with their cover songs and dancing videos, including a rendition of [[Charlie Puth]]'s "[[Attention (Charlie Puth song)|Attention]]" and choreography tributes to [[Michael Jackson]] and [[Bruno Mars]].<ref name="MTV">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3036230/prettymuch-would-you-mind-video/|title=Prettymuch's Sunny 'Would You Mind' Video Looks Like It's From 1997|website=[[MTV]]|first=Patrick|last=Hosken|date=September 15, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref><ref name="People">{{cite web|url=http://people.com/music/simon-cowell-new-boy-band-pretty-much/|title=Meet Simon Cowell's New Boy Band, Prettymuch (Spoiler: They're Adorable and They Can Really Dance)|website=[[People (magazine)|People]]|first=Aurelie|last=Corinthios|date=May 13, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref><ref name="EW">{{cite web|url=http://ew.com/music/2017/09/19/prettymuch-would-you-mind-music-video/|title=Simon Cowell's boy band Prettymuch drops 'Would You Mind' music video — watch now|website=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|first=Thatiana|last=Diaz|date=September 19, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref> PrettyMuch's debut single, "Would You Mind", written by Savan Kotecha and Jacob Kasher, was released in July 2017.<ref name="EW" /><ref name="Idolator">{{cite web|url=http://www.idolator.com/7665887/prettymuch-debut-single-would-you-mind-snippet|title=Meet Your New Favorite Boy Band: Prettymuch Teases "Would You Mind" |website=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|first=Mike|last=Wass|date=July 7, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref> A month later, the group performed the song at the [[2017 Teen Choice Awards]] and sang an [[a cappella]] version on the red carpet at the [[2017 MTV Video Music Awards]].<ref name="NYDailyNews" /><ref name="MTV" /> A music video for the song was then released in September. Directed by [[Emil Nava]], it depicts the members dancing and jumping over each other in empty alleyways, along with scenes from a road trip the group had taken.<ref name="Billboard1">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7965767/prettymuch-would-you-mind-video|title=Prettymuch Drops 'Would You Mind' Video|website=Billboard.com|first=Megan|last=Armstrong|date=September 15, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref><ref name="MTV1">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3036757/prettymuch-emil-nava-would-you-mind-behind-the-scenes/|title=Prettymuch Director Breaks Down The Influences Behind 'Would You Mind' Video |website=MTV|first=Patrick|last=Hosken|date=September 19, 2017|access-date=September 23, 2017}}</ref> The clip was inspired by boy band videos of the late 1990s and early 2000s, such as [[Backstreet Boys]]' "[[We've Got It Goin' On]]" and [[NSYNC]]'s "[[I Want You Back (NSYNC song)|I Want You Back]]".<ref name="MTV1" /> The single peaked in the top 40 on [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'s [[Mainstream Top 40]] charts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/prettymuch/chart-history|title=Prettymuch|website=Billboard.com}}</ref>

PrettyMuch performed "Would You Mind" on MTV's ''[[Total Request Live]]'' in October 2017 and released their second single, "Teacher".<ref name="MTV2">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3040047/prettymuch-teacher-women-empowerment/|title=Prettymuch's New Single Is All About 'Women Empowerment, 100 Percent'|website=MTV|first=Madeline|last=Roth|date=October 5, 2017|access-date=October 6, 2017}}</ref> This single and its music video have been accused of being very similar to those of [[Dead Obies]]' "Where They @".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.journaldemontreal.com/2017/10/13/video-dead-obies-plagie-par-un-boys-band-americain|title=[VIDÉO] Dead Obies plagié par un boys band américain?|last=Plante|first=Stéphane|work=Le Journal de Montréal|access-date=2018-04-21|language=fr-CA}}</ref> On October 27, 2017, they released their single, "Open Arms". In November 2017, they confirmed via Twitter that they would perform during the final of [[The X Factor (UK series 14)|Series 14]] of ''[[The X Factor (UK TV series)|The X Factor]]'' in the [[United Kingdom]], performing their single, "No More".<ref name="Twitter">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/PRETTYMUCH/status/935948105064878080|title=Twitter|website=Twitter|date=November 29, 2017|access-date=June 24, 2018}}</ref> PrettyMuch had their own tour called Prettymuch Everywhere Tour across the United States. The group performed on Good Morning America on January 15. PrettyMuch performed their singles "Open Arms" and "No More" on MTV's ''[[Total Request Live]] ''on January 17.

===2018&ndash;2019: ''Prettymuch an EP'', ''Phases'', and ''INTL:EP''===
They released their debut EP, ''Prettymuch an EP'' on April 19, 2018, including their single "10,000 Hours". On June 21, 2018, they released a summer single "Summer On You", written by [[Ed Sheeran]] and produced by [[Steve Mac]].<ref name="Billboard2">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8462126/prettymuch-summer-on-you|title=Prettymuch Drops Sunny Ed Sheeran-Penned Single 'Summer on You': Listen|website=Billboard.com|first=Sofia|last=Mele|date=June 21, 2018|access-date=June 24, 2018}}</ref> They also performed it on the Late Late Show with [[James Corden]]. On August 20, 2018, the group performed at the [[MTV Video Music Awards|2018 MTV Video Music Awards]] on the Push Artist Stage.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.pressparty.com/pg/newsdesk/administrator/view/179013/|title=Prettymuch confirmed to perform at 2018 MTV VMAs|publisher=Pressparty|date=14 August 2018}}</ref> The single peaked in the top 50 on [[Pop radio]].<ref>{{cite web |first=Brian |last=Cantor |title=Songs By Marshmello & Bastille, Panic! At The Disco, Prettymuch Join Pop Radio's Top 50 |website=Headline Planet |url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2018/08/26/songs-marshmello-bastille-panic-disco-prettymuch-join-pop-radios-top-50/ |access-date=2019-11-22}}</ref> On September 28, 2018, they released their single "Solita" featuring [[Rich the Kid]].<ref name="Rolling Stone">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/prettymuch-new-song-solita-rich-the-kid-730179/|title=Hear PrettyMuch's Feisty New Song 'Solita'|website=Rolling Stone|first=Brittany|last=Spanos|date=September 28, 2018|access-date=October 3, 2018}}</ref>

They released their song "Real Friends" on October 17, 2018, to promote their fall tour, the Funktion Tour, and afterward "Jello", on December 14, 2018, accompanied by an official video. In the start of 2019, "Blind" (stylized as "BLiND") was released on January 25. Their second single of 2019, "Phases", was released on April 26, alongside a music video featuring model Justine Mae Biticon. On May 17, PrettyMuch announced their second EP would come out a week later. On May 20, the band released "Gone 2 Long", written and produced completely by Brandon Arreaga. The group's second EP, the ''Phases - EP'', was released on May 24, 2019. The EP consists of six songs: two already released songs ("Phases" and "Gone 2 Long"), and four new songs called "Eyes Off You", "Temporary Heart", "4U" and "One Shot".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.idolator.com/7783168/prettymuch-phases-ep-listen|title=Prettymuch Gets Things Very Right On 'Phases,' Listen Here|work=Idolator|date=24 May 2019}}</ref>

On July 11, PrettyMuch kicked off their FOMO Tour with opening act [[Kenzie Ziegler]] in [[Dallas]], [[Texas]]. A week later, on July 19, the band released "Lying", a collaboration with [[Lil Tjay]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/prettymuch-lying-lil-tjay-new-video-fomo-tour-861070/|title=Watch PrettyMuch, Lil Tjay's Graffiti-Covered 'Lying' Video|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|date=2019-07-19|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-15}}</ref>

On November 22, PrettyMuch released ''INTL:EP'', which features "Me Necesita", a collaboration with Latin-American boy band [[CNCO]], and three other songs, with guest vocals from [[Luísa Sonza]], [[NCT Dream]], and [[Iñigo Pascual]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Spanos |first=Brittany |title=PrettyMuch Discuss Guest-Filled 'Intl:EP,' Ambitious 2020 Plans |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/prettymuch-interview-intl-ep-cnco-nct-dream-911886/ |access-date=2019-11-22}}</ref> On March 10, 2020, PrettyMuch was announced as an opening act for select US dates of [[Camila Cabello]]'s [[The Romance Tour]], but the tour was later cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9332170/camila-cabello-announces-prettymuch-trevor-daniel-ant-saunders-romance-tour-guests|title=Camila Cabello Announces Romance Tour Opening Acts|date=March 10, 2020|website=Billboard.com}}</ref>

=== 2020&ndash;present: New label and ''Smackables'' ===
Following a year of more participation in their music, PrettyMuch was musically silent for the entirety of 2020, but continued to remain active on social media. Later in the year, it was revealed that [[Syco Music]], the band's record label, had been dissolved.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Syco analysis: The low-key demise of Simon Cowell's former label|url=https://www.musicweek.com/labels/read/syco-analysis-the-low-key-demise-of-simon-cowell-s-former-label/081311|access-date=2020-12-21|website=www.musicweek.com|language=en}}</ref> In December 2020, PrettyMuch announced they had signed to [[Sire Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sire Records Signs Prettymuch Following Syco Music Closure: Exclusive|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/9493336/prettymuch-signing-sire-records-following-syco-music-closure|access-date=2020-12-21|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> They began teasing their comeback to releasing music on their social media, as well as posting a countdown on their website to January 22, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prettymuch's Instagram profile post: "new label. new music. new everything. Prettymuch 2.0 coming 2021 🔥 don't call it a comeback. Link in bio!!!"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CIV90cglE8o/|access-date=2020-12-21|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref>

On January 17, 2021, the band revealed that a new single was set to be released that week, posting the cover art with the title hidden by asterisks (stars).<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prettymuch on Twitter: "⭐👀 01/22"|url=https://twitter.com/PRETTYMUCH/status/1350534073781899265|website=[[Twitter]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Prettymuch (@prettymuch) posted on Instagram: "⭐️👀 01/22" • Jan 16, 2021 at 7:56pm UTC|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CKHk2pPsmfQ/|access-date=2021-01-19|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref> Five days later, "Stars" was released to all streaming platforms, along with a music video. On the same day, PrettyMuch announced their label debut EP, ''Smackables''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Mier|first=Tomás|date=January 22, 2021|title=Prettymuch Isn't Here to Be Your Typical Boy Band — We're 'Not Afraid to Take Risks'|url=https://people.com/music/prettymuch-isnt-here-to-be-your-typical-boy-band-stars-smackables/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-30|website=People|language=en}}</ref> The EP was released a week later on January 29, along with a music video for the song "Parking Spot" featuring actress and model Alexia Castillo.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-29|title=Prettymuch Rolls Out "Parking Spot" Video, Drops 'Snackables' EP|url=https://www.idolator.com/7917814/prettymuch-rolls-out-parking-spot-video-drops-snackables-ep|access-date=2021-01-30|website=idolator}}</ref>

In an interview with GQ,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Armstrong|first=Megan|title=PrettyMuch Wants to Be the Anti-Boy Band|url=https://www.gq.com/story/prettymuch-smackables-interview|access-date=2021-03-08|website=GQ|language=en-us}}</ref> it was revealed that "Corpus Christi", a song initially thought to be on the original ''Smackables'' EP would be released on a deluxe version of ''Smackables''. This also served as a simultaneous, unofficial announcement of ''Smackables (Deluxe Version)''. On February 19, the music video for "Lonely" was premiered on PrettyMuch's YouTube channel.<ref>{{Citation|title=Prettymuch - Lonely (Official Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5YVtWD_o58g|language=en|access-date=2021-03-08}}</ref> On February 26, 2021, the deluxe version of the ''Smackables'' EP was released on Sire Records. The deluxe EP included "Corpus Christi", a song written mainly by Brandon Arreaga featuring a production credit from [[Lido (musician)|Lido]], and "Smackables", the long-awaited title track teased by the band in various YouTube videos throughout 2020.

Prior to the release of ''Smackables (Deluxe Edition)'', PrettyMuch announced a "Smackables EP Release Party",<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prettymuch on Instagram: "Smackables Release Party Airing March 4th and 5th !!! mark your calendars 😈"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CLXMzNQMgf4/|access-date=2021-03-08|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref> hosted by Moment House.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Moment House|url=https://www.momenthouse.com/about|access-date=2021-03-08|website=www.momenthouse.com|language=en}}</ref> The release party was held in three installments, each for a different region of the world on March 4 or 5th, 2021, to accommodate their global fans. The party consisted of the band doing short commentaries on three songs ("Free", "Lonely", and "Corpus Christi") and playing them for fans, performing three of the songs off the complete ''Smackables'' EP ("Stars", "Parking Spot", and "Smackables"), and a special premiere of the "Corpus Christi" music video. The official "Corpus Christi" video, directed by [http://www.bonimata.com/ Boni Mata], was released on March 9, 2021. PrettyMuch also performed their song "Stars" on [[Good Morning America]] on March 16.<ref>{{Citation|last=America|first=Good Morning|title=Prettymuch performs 'Stars' on 'GMA'|url=https://www.goodmorningamerica.com/culture/video/prettymuch-performs-stars-gma-76486366|language=en|access-date=2021-03-17}}</ref>

After sharing a teaser on [[TikTok]], Prettymuch released "Trust" on July 30, 2021. The official video, which was released on the same day, is directed by [[Ian Eastwood]] and edited by Brandon Arreaga.<ref>{{Cite web|title=‘Trust’ Prettymuch To Release A Hit Like This!|url=https://thehoneypop.com/2021/07/31/trust-prettymuch-to-release-a-hit-like-this/|language=en|date=2021-07-31}}</ref>

== Members ==
=== Brandon Arreaga ===
Brandon Michael Lee Arreaga (born December 14, 1999) grew up in Corinth, Texas. He began doing acting and modeling jobs at around eleven years old, and used money earned from the jobs to get equipment for a home studio.<ref>[https://schonmagazine.com/interview-prettymuch/ "interview<nowiki> |</nowiki> prettymuch"], ''Schön!''. Retrieved 2019-11-04.</ref> Arreaga would rent the studio out to other local musicians.<ref name="Zach Sang Show 2017">[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l7gRuTme74w Prettymuch talks Would You Mind, One Direction and French Montana], ''Zach Sang Show''.</ref> He was the last member to join the group, as he did not initially get involved because his parents thought the scouting was fake.<ref name="Idolator 2017">[https://www.idolator.com/7666497/prettymuch-interview-would-you-mind "Prettymuch On Boy Band Life & Debut Single “Would You Mind”: Interview"], ''Idolator''. Retrieved 2019-11-04.</ref> As of 2019, Arreaga has been dating [[Maggie Lindemann]], another singer from Texas.<ref>[https://www.instagram.com/p/B0KmXMtnO4L/ ☆ Brandon Arreaga ☆ on Instagram: "u are actually the most beautiful thing I think I’ve ever seen in my life no cap..."]. ''Instagram''. Retrieved 2019-11-04.</ref>

=== Edwin Honoret ===
Edwin Joel Honoret (born February 12, 1999) is from [[The Bronx]], New York.<ref name="Zach Sang Show 2017" />

=== Austin Porter ===
Austin Dale Porter (born August 14, 1997) grew up in [[Charlotte, North Carolina|Charlotte]], North Carolina. Prior to the audition, Porter had a solo career, having released a pop album titled "One Love" in 2013<ref>{{cite web|title="One Love" by Austin Porter on Deezer|url=https://www.deezer.com/us/album/9747286}}</ref> and made an unreleased rap EP.<ref name="Zach Sang Show 2017" /> He is inspired by a variety of different genres including lo-fi hip-hop and jazz. He has listed [[Elton John]], [[James Taylor]], [[Chicago (band)|Chicago]] and [[The Beatles]] as some of his musical influences.<ref>[http://www.digitaljournal.com/entertainment/music/prettymuch-talks-new-single-summer-on-you-ed-sheeran-beyonce/article/526766 Prettymuch talks new single 'Summer on You,' Ed Sheeran, Beyonce], ''Digital Journal''.</ref>

=== Nick Mara ===
Nicholas Carter Mara (born November 8, 1997) was born in [[New York City|New York]], but grew up in [[Englishtown, New Jersey|Englishtown]], New Jersey. He began dancing at around nine years old, and was formerly in the dance crew ICONic Boyz, which was featured in the sixth season of [[America's Best Dance Crew (season 6)|America's Best Dance Crew]]. Mara describes his musical interest as "Bad Boy-era type music", being inspired by artists such as [[Notorious B.I.G]].<ref name="Teen Vogue 2017">[https://www.teenvogue.com/story/prettymuch-interview-musical-influences-being-in-a-boy-band Prettymuch Gets Real About Musical Influences and Being in a Boy Band], ''Teen Vogue''. Retrieved 2019-11-04.</ref>

=== Zion Kuwonu ===
Caleb Zion Kuwonu (born June 29, 1999) is from [[Ottawa]], Canada. He was inspired to become a singer after watching [[Justin Bieber: Never Say Never]], and lists [[Justin Bieber]] as one of his musical inspirations, among other artists such as [[PartyNextDoor]], [[One Direction]], and [[Michael Jackson]].<ref name="Idolator 2017" /><ref name="Teen Vogue 2017" /> Kuwonu was noticed by [[Sony Music]] after he began posting song covers on [[Vine (service)|Vine]] in order to gain popularity at school. He has noted that he only had around 150 followers at the time.<ref name="Zach Sang Show 2017" />
