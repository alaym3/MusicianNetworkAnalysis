'''Lilliane Catherine Diomi''' (born 2001),<!--please do not add exact dob without multiple reliable sources--> known professionally as '''ppcocaine''' (previously known as '''trapbunniebubbles'''<ref>{{cite web |title=ppcocaine |url=https://genius.com/20129980 |website=Genius}}</ref>), is an American [[social media personality]] and [[rapper]]. They are best known for their song "3 Musketeers" that gained popularity on the video-sharing platform [[TikTok]].

==Early life==
Diomi was born in 2001<!--please do not add exact dob without multiple reliable sources--> in the [[Panorama City, Los Angeles|Panorama City]] neighborhood of Los Angeles and raised in the [[Santa Clarita, California|Santa Clarita]] area.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=McCrudden|first=Michael|date=August 6, 2020|title=PPCocaine – Before They Were Famous|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zajE8eXRQI8|access-date=September 2, 2020|website=YouTube|publisher=Before They Were Famous|archive-date=September 6, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200906154002/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zajE8eXRQI8|url-status=live}}</ref> They are biracial and were born to a [[Democratic Republic of the Congo|Congolese]] father and a white mother.<ref name=":0" /> Raised primarily by their grandparents and father, they attended [[Canyon High School (Santa Clarita, California)|Canyon High School]], [[Birmingham High School]] and [[Golden Valley High School (Santa Clarita, California)|Golden Valley High School]], graduating from the latter.<ref name=":1" /> While in high school, they were enrolled in dance classes and later worked as an erotic dancer.<ref name=":1" /><ref name="caramanicaNYT28aug2020" />

==Career==
In their early career, ppcocaine went by the pseudonym trapbunniebubbles.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Blake|first=Emily|date=September 2, 2020|title=RS Charts: Burna Boy, Money Man and Victoria Monet Lead the Breakthrough 25 Chart: Kaash Paige, Priscilla Block and ppcocaine also make Rolling Stone's list of the 25 fastest-rising new artists of the month.|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/charts-burna-boy-money-man-breakthrough-1054322/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200903061648/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/charts-burna-boy-money-man-breakthrough-1054322/|archive-date=September 3, 2020|access-date=September 6, 2020|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref>

A snippet of their song "PJ" was posted to their account on the video-sharing platform [[TikTok]] in June 2020 and subsequently went viral on the platform.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Wang|first=Jessica|date=August 5, 2020|title=Everything You Need To Know About PPCocaine, TikTok Star & Rapper|url=https://www.bustle.com/entertainment/who-is-ppcocaine-the-rapper-is-tiktoks-current-soundtrack|access-date=September 2, 2020|website=Bustle|archive-date=September 6, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200906153947/https://www.bustle.com/entertainment/who-is-ppcocaine-the-rapper-is-tiktoks-current-soundtrack|url-status=live}}</ref> Their debut single, "DDLG", was released in the same month and also gained attention on TikTok.<ref>{{cite web |title=DDLG / ppcocaine |url=https://listen.tidal.com/album/146160326 |website=TIDAL |access-date=September 7, 2020 |language=en}}</ref> A snippet from an unreleased song, "For That Cash", was posted on their TikTok in July 2020 and was heralded as a "lesbian anthem" on the platform.<ref name=":0" /> They released their single "3 Musketeers" featuring fellow rapper NextYoungin in July 2020. The song also gained traction on TikTok.<ref name="haylockVULTURE10aug2020" /><ref name="caramanicaNYT28aug2020">{{Cite news|last=Caramanica|first=Jon|date=August 28, 2020|title='WAP' Is Good, Raunchy Fun. On TikTok, It's at Home.|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/28/arts/music/wap-cardi-b-megan-thee-stallion-ppcocaine.html|access-date=September 2, 2020|issn=0362-4331|archive-date=September 1, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200901044936/https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/28/arts/music/wap-cardi-b-megan-thee-stallion-ppcocaine.html|url-status=live}}</ref> It became their first single to chart in the US and elsewhere, peaking at number 22 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Bubbling Under Hot 100]] chart and at number 76 on the [[Irish Singles Chart]] in August 2020.<ref name="isc" /><ref name="buh100" /> They later released "PJ" in August 2020.<ref name="haylockVULTURE10aug2020" /> They appeared in the top 10 on ''[[Rolling Stone]]'''s August 2020 [[Rolling Stone Breakthrough 25|Breakthrough 25]] chart of the fastest-rising new artists of the month, and in the same month, signed with [[Columbia Records]].<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Rolling Stone|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/charts/breakthrough/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200827104043/https://www.rollingstone.com/charts/breakthrough/|archive-date=August 27, 2020|access-date=September 6, 2020|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":3" /> In September 2020, they released a music video for "3 Musketeers".<ref name=":4">{{cite web |title=ppcocaine releases official video for "Three Musketeers" featuring NextYoungin|url=https://radiofacts.com/ppcocaine-releases-official-video-for-three-musketeers-featuring-nextyoungin/ |website=Digital and Radio Facts |access-date=September 19, 2020 |language=en |date=September 18, 2020}}</ref> {{As of|2020|September}}, they have over one million followers on TikTok.<ref name=":3" />

==Public image==
Jessica Wang of ''[[Bustle (magazine)|Bustle]]'' said ppcocaine is best known for "aggressive vocal deliveries".<ref name=":0" /> According to Zoe Haylock of ''[[Vulture.com|Vulture]]'', they have a "nasally cartoon-character voice".<ref name="haylockVULTURE10aug2020" /> [[Jon Caramanica]] of ''[[The New York Times|New York Times]]'' said their music has a "rascally joy and bad-kid energy", calling it "playfully lewd" and noting that it has become "TikTok grammar" since its release.<ref name="caramanicaNYT28aug2020" /> ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' called their vocals "energetic, if chipmunk-esque".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lang|first=Cady|date=August 29, 2020|title=The Best TikTok Dances of 2020 So Far|url=https://time.com/5880779/best-tiktok-dances-2020/|access-date=September 2, 2020|website=Time|archive-date=September 1, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200901145945/https://time.com/5880779/best-tiktok-dances-2020/|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2020, their song "3 Musketeers" was described by Caramanica as "one of the definitive songs of this summer" and "something of a lesbian anthem on TikTok".<ref name="caramanicaNYT28aug2020" /> Wang called their song "PJ" "an anthem for women psyching each other up" and called their unreleased song "For That Cash" "a lesbian anthem".<ref name=":0" /> They gained popularity on both "alt" TikTok and "straight" TikTok.<ref name="haylockVULTURE10aug2020">{{Cite web|last=Haylock|first=Zoe|date=August 10, 2020|title=Alt and Straight TikTok Both Agree on Rapper Ppcocaine|url=https://www.vulture.com/2020/08/ppcocaine-tiktok-hits-pj-ddlg-3-musketeers-explained.html|access-date=September 2, 2020|website=Vulture|archive-date=August 29, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200829154558/https://www.vulture.com/2020/08/ppcocaine-tiktok-hits-pj-ddlg-3-musketeers-explained.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' called their songs "so over-the-top sexual that they make '[[WAP (song)|WAP]]' read like ''[[The Notebook (novel)|The Notebook]]''."<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=Brown|first=August|date=September 10, 2020|title=You think 'WAP' is dirty? Meet 19-year-old L.A. rapper ppcocaine, the filth queen of TikTok|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2020-09-10/ppcocaine-tiktok-rapper|access-date=September 11, 2020|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US}}</ref>

''Vulture'' compared them favorably to rappers [[Megan Thee Stallion]], [[Flo Milli]], and [[Rico Nasty]].<ref name="haylockVULTURE10aug2020" /> According to Wang, ppcocaine's songs became popular on TikTok due to dance videos made during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. The publication called them "the TikTok star behind, among other trends, the viral 'Shake Some Ass' dance trend".<ref name=":0" />

==Artistry==
Diomi has cited American rappers [[Rico Nasty]], Stunna Girl, [[Bali Baby]], and [[Flo Milli]] as influences on her music,<ref name=":0" /> the first of which is featuring Diomi in the remix of the song "Smack a Bitch" from her album ''[[Nightmare Vacation (album)|Nightmare Vacation]].'' Diomi is also known for their trademark color pink, usually wearing pink clothes and or bubblegum pink hair.<ref name="haylockVULTURE10aug2020" />

==Personal life==
Diomi is lesbian<ref name="caramanicaNYT28aug2020" /> and is in a relationship with fellow rapper NextYoungin, also known as "Daddy Kaine".<ref name="KNC Instagram">{{cite web |title=Back when y'all thought we was just best friends |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CKY9248j4Fg/ |website=Instagram |publisher=kainencocaine |access-date=January 28, 2021}}</ref> Diomi is [[Non-binary gender|gender-fluid]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/ppcoca1ne/status/1440807749076488195|url=https://twitter.com/ppcoca1ne/status/1440807749076488195|access-date=2021-10-02|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> and uses they/them pronouns.<ref>{{Cite web|title=ppcocaine on Twitter|url=https://twitter.com/ppcoca1ne|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-12|website=[[Twitter]]|language=en}}</ref>
