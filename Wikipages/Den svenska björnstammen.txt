'''Den svenska björnstammen''' ([[Swedish language|Swedish]] for "The Swedish Bear Population") is a Swedish musical [[pop music|pop]]-[[electronic dance music|EDM]] band from [[Norrköping]], formed in 2010 and consisting of Dan Brännvall, Kim Dahlberg, Petter Frisendahl, Ambjörn Göransson, Mattias Göransson, Klas Isaksson and Åke Olofsson. The band is signed to Pope Records who has a special distribution agreement with [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Sweden]] since 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Universal Music distribuerar Pope Records|url=http://www.musikindustrin.se/2009/04/22/universal_music_distribuerar_pope_records/|last=Olson|first=Claes|date=22 April 2009|website=Musikindustrin|language=sv|url-status=live|access-date=5 May 2020}}</ref>

==History==
The band was one of three finalists for the Metro on Stage music competition. In 2011, it had a breakthrough with the song "Vart jag mig i världen vänder" ("Wherever I Turn In The World"), topping the Swedish national [[record chart]] [[Sverigetopplistan]] for six weeks. The band's follow-up song, "''Svalkar vinden''" ("Does The Wind Cool") and "''Trumma Trumma''" ("Drum Drum") also charted.
