'''The Districts''' are an American [[Rock music|rock]] band originally from [[Lititz, Pennsylvania]]. The group formed in 2009 while members Rob Grote, Mark Larson, Connor Jacobus, and Braden Lawrence were all still in high school.

==History==
The Districts were formed in 2009 by four high schoolers from Lititz, a small town in central Pennsylvania.<ref>[https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-districts-mn0002742344/biography The Districts] at [[Allmusic]]</ref> The group self-released two EPs and a full-length album, ''Telephone,'' over the course of 2011 and 2012. Late in 2013, after a stint in viral success from a live studio session, The Districts signed with [[Fat Possum Records]], and released an EP in early 2014 consisting of three remastered tracks from their previous releases and two new songs.<ref>[https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/the-districts-are-set-to-go-national-ep-premiere-20140123 The Districts Are Set to Go National - EP Premiere ]. ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', January 23, 2014.</ref> By 2014 the group had relocated from Lititz to [[Philadelphia]].<ref>[http://drownedinsound.com/in_depth/4147798-the-districts-wolf-alice-lana-del-rey-dis-does-singles-27-05-14 Interview] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150920051755/http://drownedinsound.com/in_depth/4147798-the-districts-wolf-alice-lana-del-rey-dis-does-singles-27-05-14 |date=2015-09-20 }}, [[Drowned in Sound]]</ref> In February 2015, the group's second full-length album, ''A Flourish and a Spoil,'' produced by [[John Congleton]], also appeared on the Fat Possum label.<ref>[https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/20142-a-flourish-and-a-spoil/ Review], [[Pitchfork Media]]</ref><ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/feb/05/the-districts-a-flourish-and-a-spoil-review Review], [[The Guardian]]</ref><ref>[https://www.nme.com/reviews/the-districts/15906 Review], [[NME]]</ref> This album peaked at #7 on the [[Billboard magazine|Billboard]] Heatseekers chart and #28 on the Top Independent Albums chart.<ref>[https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-districts-mn0002742344/awards Awards], Allmusic</ref> In August 2017, the band released their third full length album, ''Popular Manipulations,'' once again under the Fat Possum label.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Geslani|first1=Michelle|title=The Districts release new album Popular Manipulations: Stream|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/08/the-districts-release-new-album-popular-manipulations-stream/|website=Consequence of Sound|accessdate=22 February 2018}}</ref>
