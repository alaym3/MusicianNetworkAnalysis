'''Joywave''' is an American [[indie rock]] band from [[Rochester, New York]], formed in 2010. Their lineup consists of Daniel Armbruster (vocals), Joseph Morinelli (guitar), and Paul Brenner (drums).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/joywave-mn0002855911/biography |title=Joywave |work=[[AllMusic]] |publisher=[[Rovi Corporation]] |access-date=July 25, 2014 |author=Thomas, Fred}}</ref> The band first became known for its collaboration with electronic music project [[Big Data (band)|Big Data]], "[[Dangerous (Big Data song)|Dangerous]]", which peaked at number one on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Alternative Songs]] chart in 2014.<ref name="lfoglia">Lou Foglia, [http://www.columbiachronicle.com/arts_and_culture/article_2421e4f2-696f-11e5-9b04-a31af3b5fff1.html "Joywave talks Disney, 'Dangerous,' late night shows,"] ''[[Columbia Chronicle]]'', October 5, 2015.</ref> After releasing two EPs, their debut album, ''[[How Do You Feel Now?]]'', was released through their own Cultco Music label, an imprint of [[Hollywood Records]], in 2015. Their second album, ''[[Content (Joywave album)|Content]]'', was released on July 28, 2017. It was followed by their third album, ''[[Possession (Joywave album)|Possession]]'', which was released on March 13, 2020 and finally ''Every Window Is A Mirror'' on the June 25, 2021.

==History==
===Early years (2010–2013)===
All five members of Joywave grew up in [[Rochester, New York]].<ref name="jspevak">Jeff Spevak, [https://www.usatoday.com/story/lifestyle/2015/11/25/riding-joywave/75660196/ "Riding the Joywave,"] ''[[USA Today]]'', November 25, 2015.</ref><ref name="athatcher">Autumn Thatcher, [http://www.sltrib.com/home/2249403-155/concert-preview-joywave-on-a-decidedly "Joywave on a decidedly upward trend,"] ''[[Salt Lake Tribune]]'', March 5, 2015.</ref> Armbruster, Brenner and Morinelli played together in various local bands growing up, including The Hoodies.<ref name="lfoglia"/><ref name="jspevak"/> They formed Joywave in Rochester in 2010.<ref name="lfoglia"/><ref name="tsmith">Troy L. Smith, [http://www.cleveland.com/music/index.ssf/2014/09/buzz_band_joywave_opens_for_th.html "Buzz band Joywave opens for The Killers, visits 'Late Night with Seth Meyers',"] ''[[The Plain Dealer]]'', September 24, 2014.</ref> Donnelly joined the band soon after, and they released their first mixtape, ''77777'', in 2011.<ref name="tsmith"/> In 2012, they released their first EP, ''Koda Vista''.<ref name="jspevak"/>

==="Dangerous" and ''How Do You Feel Now?'' (2014–2016)===
{{Main|How Do You Feel Now?}}
Originally a member of Big Data with Alan Wilkis, Armbruster wrote the song “Dangerous”, which was recorded by Wilkis with Armbruster on vocals, and credited to Big Data featuring Joywave.<ref>Roman Gokhman, [http://thebaybridged.com/2016/02/25/noise-pop-qa-success-buys-pink-suits-new-bed-joywave/ "Noise Pop Q&A: Success buys pink suits, new bed for Joywave,"] The Bay Bridged, February 25, 2016.</ref> Following the success of “Dangerous”, Joywave's notability continued to rise with the release of their 2014 EP ''How Do You Feel?'' The two singles from the EP, "[[Tongues (song)|Tongues]]" (ft. KOPPS) and "[[Somebody New (Joywave song)|Somebody New]]", achieved considerable popularity on the radio and on YouTube, with the respective music videos each having over 1.8 million views on [[YouTube]] as of June 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AqmwKi6KOSw&list=PLDDrB7ulTMq-CbET77rmTmQH-H7O2eO-N |title=Joywave – Tongues (Official video) feat. KOPPS |publisher=[[YouTube]] |access-date=July 25, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ep9ppY7u6Ug |title=Joywave – Somebody New (Official Video) |publisher=[[YouTube]] |access-date=April 24, 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AqmwKi6KOSw|title=Joywave – Tongues (Official Video) ft. KOPPS|website=YouTube|access-date=April 6, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=JoywaveVEVO|title=Joywave – Somebody New (Official Video)|date=February 24, 2015 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ep9ppY7u6Ug|access-date=April 6, 2016}}</ref> "Tongues" was featured on a [[Google Nexus]] commercial and in [[EA Sports]] football game, ''[[FIFA 15]]'',<ref name="jspevak"/> and the music video for the song was directed by the directing duo [[Daniels (directors)|Daniels]].<ref>Claire Lobenfeld, [http://www.spin.com/2014/06/joywave-kopps-tongues-video/ "Joywave and Kopps Lead NSFW Romp in 'Tongues' Video,"] ''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]'', June 3, 2014.</ref>

Joywave's debut LP, ''[[How Do You Feel Now?]]'', was released on April 21, 2015 in the US and June 22, 2015 in the UK.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.inveterate.co.uk/music/interview-joywave-destruction-is-one-of-our-favourite-on-the-record-its-aggressive-and-scary/146000/ | title=Interview: Joywave – "Destruction Is One Of Our Favourite On The Record; It's Aggressive And Scary" | publisher=Inveterate | date=May 21, 2015 | access-date=May 21, 2015 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150524021024/http://www.inveterate.co.uk/music/interview-joywave-destruction-is-one-of-our-favourite-on-the-record-its-aggressive-and-scary/146000/ | archive-date=May 24, 2015 | url-status=dead }}</ref> It reached #25 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Alternative Albums]] chart, #34 on the [[Top Rock Albums]] chart, and #3 on the [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/artist/6195772/joywave/chart?f=794 Joywave Chart History], ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. Accessed June 15, 2017.</ref> In 2016, the band released ''[[Destruction (song)#Swish|Swish]]'', which features alternate versions of "[[Destruction (song)|Destruction]]". The title is a reference to [[Kanye West]]'s 2016 album ''[[The Life of Pablo]]'', which was originally going to be titled ''Swish''.<ref>Tyler Harvey, [https://303magazine.com/2016/06/review-joywave/ "Joywave Shows Denver Some Destruction,"] ''303 Magazine'', June 14, 2016.</ref>

===''Content'' (2017–2018)===
{{Main|Content (Joywave album)}}
In April 2017, they released "Content",<ref>Sydney Sweeney, [http://atwoodmagazine.com/content-joywave-review/ "This Just In: Joywave's 'Content' is a Creepy, Double-Edged Sword,"] ''Atwood Magazine'', April 21, 2017.</ref> the first single off their upcoming second album, also titled ''[[Content (Joywave album)|Content]]''.<ref name="lhoward">Lindsay Howard, [http://variancemagazine.com/sounds/7779-joywave-its-a-trip "Joywave Teases Next Album with New Song 'It's a Trip',"] ''[[Variance Magazine]]'', May 5, 2017.</ref> In May 2017, they released the album's second single, "It's a Trip!".<ref name="lhoward"/> Three other singles were released in the months that followed: "Shutdown," "Doubt," and "Going to a Place." ''Content'' was released on July 28, 2017.<ref name="Content by Joywave">{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T3usOhr_gWY |title=Content by Joywave |publisher=[[YouTube]] |location=United States |access-date=July 27, 2017}}</ref> On June 1, 2018, the band released the song "Compromise", an unreleased song from the ''How Do You Feel Now?'' era.<ref name="Compromise – Single by Joywave">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/compromise-single/1386975763 |title=Compromise – Single by Joywave |work=[[iTunes Store]] |location=United States |publisher=[[Apple Inc.|Apple]] |access-date=June 7, 2018}}</ref>

===''Possession'' (2018-2020)===
{{Main|Possession (Joywave album)}}
On July 12, 2018, the band released the single "Blastoffff".<ref name=BO>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/blastoffff-single/1409994601 |title=Blastoffff – Single by Joywave |work=iTunes Store |location=United States |access-date=July 17, 2018}}</ref> It appears in the ''[[Fortnite]]'' Season 5 trailer.<ref>{{cite web |title=Fortnite Season 5 Is Now Live, Brings Golf Courts, Map Changes And ATK With A Bounce Padded Roof |url=https://www.futuregamereleases.com/2018/07/fortnite-season-5-is-now-live-brings-golf-courts-map-changes-and-atk-with-a-bounce-padded-roof/ |website=futuregamereleases.com |access-date=17 July 2018}}</ref> On June 21, 2019 the single "Like a Kennedy" was released alongside a teaser for the music video.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/joywave/status/1147226687630766080|title=Tuesday.pic.twitter.com/3OEV5OI9Lh|last=Joywave|date=2019-07-05|website=@joywave|language=en|access-date=2019-07-07}}</ref> On August 9, 2019, the band released the single "Obsession", in advance of their third album.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Spruch |first1=Kirsten |title=Joywave Team Up With Marvel Comics to Unveil New Song 'Obsession' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8526946/joywave-obsession-listen |access-date=19 August 2019 |publisher=Billboard |date=9 August 2019}}</ref> On November 1, 2019, they released the single "Blank Slate".<ref name="vents">{{cite web |last1=Frometa |first1=RJ |title=Joywave reveal "Blank Slate" |url=https://ventsmagazine.com/2019/11/01/joywave-reveal-blank-slate/ |website=ventsmagazine.com |access-date=7 November 2019}}</ref> On January 7, 2020, the band released the song "Half Your Age" and revealed that their third album, ''Possession'', would be released on March 13. They also announced dates for a North American headlining tour, which was ultimately cancelled due to the COVID-19 pandemic. <ref>{{Cite web |url=https://ventsmagazine.com/2020/01/07/joywave-announce-new-album-possession-to-release-march-13-north-american-headline-tour-announced-new-single-half-your-age-out-today/ |title=Joywave announce new album "Possession" to release March 13, North American headline tour announced, new single "Half Your Age" out today |last=Frometa |first=RJ |date=January 7, 2020 |website=Vents |access-date=January 9, 2020}}</ref>

===''Every Window Is A Mirror'' (2021)===
Their four-song EP ''Every Window Is a Mirror'' was released on June 25, 2021.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Frometa |first1=RJ |title=Joywave releases "Every Window Is A Mirror" of their upcoming EP set to release June 25 |url=https://ventsmagazine.com/2021/05/07/joywave-releases-every-window-is-a-mirror-of-their-upcoming-ep-set-to-release-june-25/ |access-date=24 May 2021 |publisher=Vents Magazine}}</ref> The EP include 4 new songs which were released over a course of around 3 months and the EP includes the songs: After Coffee, Every Window Is A Mirror, The Inversion and Pray For The Reboot. Joywave also released a Russian version of Every Window Is A Mirror when they noticed an uptick in streams coming from Russia.

It is also to note that after the EP went live, Joywave started a Q&A series called Every Question Has An Answer.

=== ''Cleanse'' (2022) ===
On October 29th, 2021, the band announced their fourth album alongside the single "Cyn City 2000".<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/joywave/status/1454101013573640198|url=https://twitter.com/joywave/status/1454101013573640198|access-date=2021-11-02|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> The album is set to be released on February 11th, 2022, containing songs from their EP a year prior.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Login • Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/accounts/login/|access-date=2021-11-02|website=www.instagram.com}}</ref> The band also announced a tour across the United States to accompany the release of the album during the spring of 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/joywave/status/1454129203159437317|url=https://twitter.com/joywave/status/1454129203159437317|access-date=2021-11-02|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>

Thanks For Coming Tour With 2018 Dates,"] ''The Prelude Press'', November 16, 2017.</ref> They toured with [[Thirty Seconds to Mars]], [[Walk the Moon]], and [[MisterWives]] on Thirty Seconds to Mars's North American tour in the summer of 2018,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Dickman |first1=Maggie |title=THIRTY SECONDS TO MARS SHARE MASSIVE NORTH AMERICAN TOUR, DETAIL NEW ALBUM |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/thirty_seconds_to_mars_north_american_tour_new_album_april_6/ |website=Alt Press |access-date=17 July 2018}}</ref> and in September 2018 they toured with [[Bishop Briggs]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Bishop Briggs schedules fall tour dates with Joywave |url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2018/6/13/bishop-briggs-schedules-fall-tour-dates-with-joywave.html |website=ABC News Radio |access-date=17 July 2018}}</ref>
