'''Pom Pom Squad''' is an [[indie rock]]/[[grunge]] band from [[Brooklyn]], New York.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hu|first=Elli|date=2018-11-20|title=Local Sounds Presents: Pom Pom Squad|url=https://nyulocal.com/local-sounds-presents-pom-pom-squad-2324b1b9a3e5|access-date=2021-03-06|website=Medium|language=en}}</ref> Begun in 2015 as a solo project of [[Lead vocalist|frontwoman]] Mia Berrin, Pom Pom Squad evolved into a four-piece band consisting of Berrin, bassist Mari Alé Figeman, drummer Shelby Keller, and guitarist Alex Mercuri.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Co|first=Truth &|date=2021-03-06|title=Pom Pom Squad|url=https://cityslang.com/artists/pom-pom-squad|access-date=2021-03-06|website=City Slang|language=en}}</ref>

== History ==
Mia Berrin began Pom Pom Squad when she was eighteen years old. She soon moved to New York City where she performed solo under the name Pom Pom Squad and with an early iteration of the band, with whom she released the EP ''Hate It Here'' in 2017.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2019-09-05|title=Pom Pom Squad Dig Deep Into Those Gut Feelings on 'Ow'|url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/pom-pom-squad-ow-stream/|access-date=2021-03-06|website=Highsnobiety|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=HATE IT HERE, by Pom Pom Squad|url=https://pompomsquad.bandcamp.com/album/hate-it-here|access-date=2021-03-06|website=Pom Pom Squad}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=New York's Pom Pom Squad, Better Than Your High School Cheer Team|url=https://www.pond-mag.com/music/2017/10/3/pom-pom-squad-better-than-your-high-school-cheer-team|access-date=2021-03-06|website=POND Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

Berrin later met bassist Mari Alé Figeman and drummer Shelby Keller at club show and reformed the band. Guitar duties fluctuated between Ethan Sass and Alex Mercuri for a period until Mercuri became the permanent fourth member.<ref name=":0" /> Pom Pom Squad released their EP ''Ow'' in September 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ow, by Pom Pom Squad|url=https://pompomsquad.bandcamp.com/album/ow|access-date=2021-03-06|website=Pom Pom Squad}}</ref> The band built critical buzz during this time and were booked for multiple [[South by Southwest|SXSW showcases]] as well as supporting tour dates with [[The Front Bottoms]] and Disq;<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pom Pom Squad Announces Tour w/ The Front Bottoms + SXSW Shows w/ AV Club, Neon Gold & More • WithGuitars|url=https://www.withguitars.com/pom-pom-squad-announces-tour-w-the-front-bottoms-sxsw-shows-w-av-club-neon-gold-more/|access-date=2021-03-06|website=WithGuitars|language=en-GB}}</ref> these dates in 2020 were ultimately cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2021/the-lily/career-achievements-interrupted/</ref> During 2020, Pom Pom Squad released a series of covers and the original single "Red with Love."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-19|title=Pom Pom Squad - "Crimson And Clover" (Tommy James And The Shondells Cover)|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2088142/pom-pom-squad-crimson-and-clover-tommy-james-and-the-shondells-cover/music/|access-date=2021-03-06|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref>

On March 2, 2021, it was announced that Pom Pom Squad had signed with the Berlin [[Independent record label|independent label]] [[City Slang]]. With the announcement, the band released the single "Lux."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Co|first=Truth &|date=2021-03-05|title=We Signed Pom Pom Squad!|url=https://cityslang.com/news/we-signed-pom-pom-squad|access-date=2021-03-06|website=City Slang|language=en}}</ref> Their first full-length album ''[[Death of a Cheerleader (album)|Death of a Cheerleader]]'' was released on June 25, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pom Pom Squad - Death of a Cheerleader - LP+|url=https://www.roughtrade.com/us/pom-pom-squad/death-of-a-cheerleader/lp-plus?utm_source=CJ&cjevent=7410c4d7a1e511eb80f204450a82b82a|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Rough Trade}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-20|title=Band To Watch: Pom Pom Squad|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2144459/pom-pom-squad-death-of-a-cheerleader-mia-berrin/interviews/band-to-watch/|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref>
