'''Adelitas Way''' is an independent American {{nowrap|[[hard rock]]}} band formed in Las Vegas in 2006.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Adelitas+Way/+wiki |title=Adelitas Way's Biography – Free listening, concerts, stats, & pictures at |publisher=Last.fm |date=January 15, 2013 |access-date=September 24, 2013}}</ref> The band's debut single "[[Invincible (Adelitas Way song)|Invincible]]", broke them into the mainstream scene after the song made numerous television appearances in commercials and live sporting events.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.axs.com/adelitas-way-s-invincible-catapulted-the-band-from-obscurity-to-succes-28706 |title=Adelitas Way's 'Invincible' catapulted the band from obscurity to success|access-date=November 17, 2014}}</ref> As of 2017, the band has toured with notable acts such as [[Guns N' Roses]], [[Creed (band)|Creed]], [[Papa Roach]], [[Godsmack]], [[Theory of a Deadman]], [[Seether]], [[Three Days Grace]], [[Breaking Benjamin]], [[Deftones]], [[Puddle of Mudd]], [[Sick Puppies]], [[Shinedown]], [[Staind]], [[Alter Bridge]], [[Skillet (band)|Skillet]], [[Halestorm]], [[Thousand Foot Krutch]] and others.<ref name="autogenerated1"/>

==History==
{{sources|section|date=April 2021}}
The band began working on their debut album in the fall of 2008. After inking with Virgin Records, Rick DeJesus, and drummer Trevor Stafford headed for Groovemaster Studios in Chicago, Illinois where producer [[Johnny K]] awaited their arrival. Before the band started touring in support of their new record, the band expanded with the additions of bassist Derek Johnston and additional guitarist, [[Keith Wallen]], completing the original line up. On April 17, 2009, the band released their debut single "[[Invincible (Adelitas Way song)|Invincible]]" which, became the official [[theme song]] for ''[[WWE Superstars]]'' on [[WGN America]] as well as being featured in the [[Smackdown vs. Raw 2010]] video game, [[MTV]] as well as the [[CSI Miami]] Finale.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.answers.com/topic/adelitas-way |title=Adelitas Way: Information from |publisher=Answers.com |date=July 14, 2009 |access-date=September 24, 2013}}</ref> They also wrote a song called "It's A New Day" which was the theme song for [[WWE]] [[The Legacy (professional wrestling)|The Legacy]] from June 15, 2009 – September 13, 2010.

On July 14, 2009, their debut [[Adelitas Way (album)|self-titled album]] was released. The second single off the ''[[Adelitas Way (album)|Adelitas Way]]'' album, "[[Last Stand (song)|Last Stand]]" was released on February 23, 2010. The video for "Last Stand" was directed by [[Paul Boyd (director)|Paul Boyd]] and which premiered on March 2, 2010. The song "Scream" was featured as a soundtrack for ''[[Saw 3D]]'' on October 2010.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://tunelab.com/2010/09/06/adelitas-way-headed-to-the-studio/ |title=Adelitas Way Headed to the Studio |website=Tunelab.com |date=September 6, 2010 |access-date=March 28, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160503121031/http://tunelab.com/2010/09/06/adelitas-way-headed-to-the-studio/ |archive-date=May 3, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
Adelitas Way has over 130 Million Streams Independently and has released 3 albums on Their own label Vegas Syn "Getaway" "Notorious" and "Live Love Life" They have released "What It Takes", "Stay Ready", "Down for
Anything", "Habit" and "Shine On" from their upcoming 6th album. The album, Shine On was released on August 14th of 2020.

In January 2011, the band revealed their second album's title, ''[[Home School Valedictorian]]''. The album was produced by [[Dave Bassett (songwriter)|Dave Bassett]] and released June 7, 2011. Adelitas Way's first single off the second album Home School Valedictorian, "[[Sick (song)|Sick]]", was released on March 11, 2011. It reached No. 1 at Active Rock Radio, becoming the band's first No. 1 single. It was followed up by "The Collapse" in late August that same year which hit No. 2 at active rock radio. On February 16, 2012 "Criticize" was released as the third single, and reached No. 1 on the active rock chart, becoming the band's second No. 1 single from the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.concertboom.com/adelitas-way/tour-dates/ |title=Adelitas Way Tour Dates 2014, Adelitas Way Concert Tickets 2014 |publisher=Concertboom |access-date=September 24, 2013}}</ref> "Alive" was announced as the fourth and final single, released on August 13, 2012, and reached No. 4 on the U.S active Rock chart in early 2013.

Writing began at the very end of 2012 and continued through July 2013. On May 7, 2013, guitarist [[Keith Wallen]] revealed through his Facebook page that he was leaving the band to pursue his own personal goals and became the new guitarist for [[Breaking Benjamin]] In late July, it was announced that [[Grammy Award]] winning rock producer [[Nick Raskulinecz]] would be producing their third album. Recording began in early September at Rock Falcon Studio in [[Nashville]], [[Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/stuck-deluxe-version/id878682291 |title=iTunes - Music - Stuck (Deluxe Version) by Adelitas Way |date=January 2014 |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |access-date=May 24, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.Instagram.com/adelitasway |title=Instagram |publisher=Instagram |access-date=May 24, 2014}}</ref> In December, bassist Derek Johnston left the band. Their first single off of the album, "[[Dog on a Leash]]", was released on April 8, 2014, followed by a music video. "Dog on a Leash" reached No. 7 on the Active Rock chart.

''[[Stuck (Adelitas Way album)|Stuck]]'' was released on July 29, 2014, through Virgin Records/CMG. On August 15, 2014, it was officially announced that "Save the World" would be the second single off the album and was released on September 12, 2014.

On January 14, 2015, the band announced the release of a new single, a five-song EP, and a full-length album. In an interview [[Rick DeJesus]] revealed the new single, "[[I Get Around (Adelitas Way 2015 song)|I Get Around]]". On February 12, "I Get Around" was released on for exclusive download. The band's EP ''[[Deserve This EP]]'' was released on March 11, 2015, just a week earlier than its original release date March 17. The band toured with [[Red (band)|Red]] in the Summer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://loudwire.com/red-adelitas-way-2015-u-s-tour-bad-seed-rising/ |title=RED To Embark on 2015 U.S. Tour With Adelitas Way and Bad Seed Rising|access-date= May 14, 2015}}</ref> 

The lead single, "Bad Reputation", off their follow-up album, ''Getaway'', was released on December 18, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://loudwire.com/adelitas-way-bad-reputation-exclusive-song-stream/ |title= Adelitas Way, Bad Reputation - Exclusive Song Stream |publisher= loudwire.com |access-date= December 23, 2015}}</ref> "Bad Reputation" gained generally positive reviews and rose to No. 12 on the Active Rock chart. "Bad Reputation" also reached at No. 9 on Big Uns Countdown. ''Getaway'' charted at No. 8 in Hard Rock albums on ''Billboard''.

The first single, "Ready for War (Pray for Peace)", of off their fifth studio album, ''Notorious'', charted at No. 18 on Active Rock Radio and No. 3 on Big UNS Countdown. "Ready for War (Pray for Peace)" was the official theme song of [[WWE]] [[TLC: Tables, Ladders & Chairs (2016)]]. On December 16, "Tell Me" released as the second single. On February 22 the band started their second [[PledgeMusic]] campaign for the 2017 album, and they released a song, "Black Diamond", exclusively for their pledge supporters. The title-track, "Notorious", was released on July 7, 2017. It was written and produced by DeJesus and former [[New Medicine]] member Jake Scherer.{{Citation needed|date=August 2018}}

"Still Hungry" was released as a single on April 6, 2018. And another single "Drifting" was released on July 20, 2018. An Ep Live Love Life was released on November 2.{{nigc|date=June 2020}}

The band released their sixth studio album '' Shine On'', on August 14, 2020.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/shine-on/1515822190|title=Shine On by Adelitas Way|website=Apple Music|date=August 14, 2020}}</ref><ref>h{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wn5UhwFyYM8 |title=Video |website=www.youtube.com |access-date=2020-06-13}}</ref>
