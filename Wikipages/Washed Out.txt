'''Ernest Weatherly Greene Jr.''' (born October 3, 1982), known professionally as '''Washed Out''', is an American singer, songwriter and record producer.

==Life and career==
Ernest Weatherly Greene Jr. was born on October 3, 1982 in [[Perry, Georgia]], and currently lives in [[Atlanta, Georgia]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Reges |first=Margaret |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/washed-out-mn0002136598 |title=Washed Out |publisher=[[AllMusic]]. [[Rovi Corporation]] |access-date=February 6, 2013}}</ref><ref name="CreLoaf">{{cite web |last=Williams |first=Wyatt |url=http://clatl.com/atlanta/how-washed-out-became-the-poster-boy-of-electros-chillwave-movement/Content?oid=1430865 |title=How Washed Out became the poster boy of electro's chillwave movement |work=[[Creative Loafing (Atlanta)|Creative Loafing Atlanta]] |date=March 23, 2010 |access-date=December 20, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thelepantoleague.blogspot.com/2009/07/washed-out-is-south-carolina-born-and.html|title=Washed Out|date=2009-07-27|website=The Lepanto League|access-date=2017-09-07}}</ref> After earning an undergraduate degree from the [[University of Georgia]], he obtained a [[Master of Library and Information Science]] degree but was unable to find a job as a librarian. Greene moved back in with his parents and started producing songs in his bedroom studio,<ref name="CreLoaf"/><ref>{{cite web |last=Oder |first=Norman |url=http://www.libraryjournal.com/lj/community/libraryeducation/855989-272/story.csp |title=LJ Talks to Ernest Greene, Unemployed LIS Grad, Breakout Musician |work=[[Library Journal]] |date=October 22, 2009 |access-date=December 20, 2011}}</ref> as well as working on recordings of dance music with Bedroom, a local band.<ref name=biz-bio>{{cite web|title=Washed Out - Biography|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/277847/washed-out/biography|publisher=billboardbiz|access-date=10 April 2013}}</ref>  During 2008 he recorded lo-fi rock music under the name Lee Weather, but the following summer he found more success with his new project, Washed Out.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fredperry.com/subculture/article-washed-out|title=Washed Out Chillwave / dreampop from the US|website=www.fredperry.com|language=en|access-date=2017-09-07}}</ref> He was soon discovered by a number of influential music bloggers after they found his music on his [[Myspace]] page.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/news/36358-rising-washed-out/ |title=Rising: Washed Out |publisher=[[Pitchfork Media]] |date=September 1, 2009 |access-date=December 20, 2011}}</ref> His first recordings have been described as "drowsy, distorted, dance pop-influenced tracks that brought to mind [[Neon Indian]] and [[Memory Tapes]]".<ref name=biz-bio/>

Greene's first two [[extended play]]s were released in August and September 2009. He held his debut New York City performance (his second live show ever) at Santos Party House. He has since performed at the 2010 [[Pitchfork Music Festival]]<ref>{{cite web|author=Pitchfork, July 20, 2010 |url=http://pitchfork.com/features/articles/7837-photos-pitchfork-music-festival-2010/ |title=Articles: Photos: Pitchfork Music Festival 2010 |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2010-07-20 |access-date=2011-12-20}}</ref> and his song "Feel it All Around" is used as the opening theme for the television series ''[[Portlandia (TV series)|Portlandia]]''.<ref name=tampabay>{{cite news|last=Cridlin|first=Jay|title=SoundBytes: LMFAO, Washed Out, Dan Aykroyd and more|url=http://www.tampabay.com/content/soundbytes-lmfao-washed-out-dan-aykroyd-and-more/2053647|access-date=5 Feb 2018|newspaper=Tampa Bay Times|date=February 29, 2012}}</ref>

[[File:Washed Out at Ogden Theatre(10.04.11)(2).jpg|thumb|Washed Out performing at the [[Ogden Theatre]] in [[Denver]], 2011]]
In April 2011 it was announced that he had been signed to [[Sub Pop]]. His debut, ''[[Within and Without (album)|Within and Without]]'', then released on July 12, 2011. The album peaked at #26 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and #89 on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. He was chosen by [[Battles (band)|Battles]] to perform at the [[All Tomorrow's Parties (music festival)|ATP Nightmare Before Christmas]] festival that they co-curated in December 2011 in [[Minehead]], [[England]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atpfestival.com/events/nightmare2011.php |title=ATP Nightmare Before Christmas |publisher=Atpfestival.com |access-date=2011-12-20}}</ref>

Washed Out's second album, ''[[Paracosm (album)|Paracosm]]'', was released on August 13, 2013. The first single was "It All Feels Right," followed by "Don't Give Up."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtvhive.com/2013/06/11/washed-out-it-all-feels-right-paracosm/ |title=Listen to Washed Out's Sunny New Single "It All Feels Right" |publisher=MTV |access-date=2013-06-11}}</ref> The same year, "New Theory" from ''[[Life of Leisure]]'' featured in-background on a scene from ''[[The Spectacular Now]]''. On May 2014, he was reporting to be working on a third studio album, but he stated, "I'm figuring out the next step."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://radio.com/2014/05/29/washed-out-interview-new-album-im-figuring-out-the-next-step/|title=Washed Out Talks Soundscape of New Album: 'I'm Figuring Out the Next Step'|date=29 May 2014|publisher=Radio.com|access-date=15 December 2016}}</ref>

On May 19, Washed Out announced dates for his "Get Lost" tour, taking place in July 2017. Subsequently, he released his third studio album, ''[[Mister Mellow]]'', on June 30, 2017. The album was produced at Stones Throw with the help of Cole M.G.N.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/washed-out-mn0002136598/biography |title = Washed Out &#124; Biography & History}}</ref>

In April 2018, Washed Out released a new single, "Face Up" as part of Adult Swim's Singles Series.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/04/washed-out-returns-with-new-song-face-up-stream/|title=Washed Out returns with new song "Face Up": Stream|date=4 April 2018|access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/04/washed-out-face-up/|title=Washed Out – "Face Up"|date=4 April 2018|website=Spin.com|access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref> The single was later included on his fourth album ''[[Purple Noon (album)|Purple Noon]]''.<ref name="PurpleNoon">{{cite web|url=http://www.subpop.com/releases/washed_out/full_lengths/purple_noon|title=Sub Pop - Purple Noon by Washed Out |website=Subpop.com|access-date=July 24, 2020}}</ref>

In April 2020, Washed Out released a new single, “Too Late”.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2020/04/stream-washed-out-too-late-song-video/|title=Washed Out finds connection on new single "Too Late": Stream|website=[[Consequence of Sound]]|date=9 April 2020|access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref>

On June 30, 2020, Washed Out announced that a new album called ''[[Purple Noon (album)|Purple Noon]]'' would be released on August 7, 2020. He also released a new single, "Time to Walk Away".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/washed-out-announces-new-album-purple-noon-shares-new-song-listen/|title=Washed Out Announces New Album Purple Noon, Shares New Song|first=Evan|last=Minsker|website=Pitchfork.com|access-date=8 August 2020}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
Washed Out's style has been identified with the [[chillwave]] movement.<ref name="CreLoaf" /> He has said [[hip hop music|hip hop]] influences the way he writes songs.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lifelounge.com.au/music/interview/interview-with-washed-out.aspx |title=Interview with Washed Out |website=Lifelounge.com.au|date=2010-11-19 |access-date=2011-12-20}}</ref>
