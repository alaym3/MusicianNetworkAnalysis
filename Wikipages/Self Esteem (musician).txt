'''Self Esteem''' is an [[experimental pop|experimental-pop]] project, consisting solely of musician '''Rebecca Lucy Taylor'''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-48369910|title=Self Esteem is better off on her own|first=Mark|last=Savage|date=7 June 2019|website=Bbc.co.uk}}</ref> The inspiration for her [[nom de plume|stage name]] comes from the evolution of Taylor's confidence from her early 20s.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Subscribe|title=Self Esteem: Independent Woman, Part 2|url=https://diymag.com/2019/03/07/self-esteem-compliments-please-interview|access-date=2020-10-18|website=diymag.com|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
[[File:WideAwake030921 (244 of 307) (51422989887).jpg|thumb|Self Esteem, Brockwell Park, 2021]]
Taylor was previously a member of folk duo [[Slow Club]], which formed in [[Sheffield]] in 2006. The band consisted of multi-instrumentalists Charles Watson and Taylor, with Watson on the piano, Taylor on the drums, and both performing guitars and vocals.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/slow-club-we-feel-like-a-different-band-9608744.html|title=Slow Club: 'We feel like a different band'|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|access-date=2018-08-28|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://theverse.co.uk/homepage/music-interview-slow-club/|title=Music Interview: Slow Club - The Verse|date=2015-02-11|work=The Verse|access-date=2018-08-28|language=en-GB}}</ref> The band split up in 2017, following an extensive tour to support their last album, owing to differing musical interests.<ref name="Noisey2019">{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/d3bqkz/self-esteem-rebecca-taylor-interview-2019-first-date|title=Self Esteem and I Cheered Each Other Up at Winter Wonderland|date=14 January 2019|first=Daisy|last=Jones|work=Noisey}}</ref>

A big fan of [[Queen (band)|Queen]], her logo is based on [[Freddie Mercury]]'s signature.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-10-01|title=Elis James & John Robins BBC Radio 5 Show|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/m0010034|access-date=2021-10-02|website=BBC|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Taylor released "Your Wife", her first single under the Self Esteem moniker, in 2017. Her debut album under the name, ''Compliments Please'', was released on 1 March 2019 on [[Fiction Records]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Snapes|first=Laura|date=2019-03-01|title=Self Esteem: Compliments Please review – sly, covetous pop|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/mar/01/self-esteem-compliments-please-pop-rebecca-taylor-review-debut-solo-album|access-date=2019-03-20|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> The album was preceded by the singles "Wrestling", "Rollout", "The Best" and "Girl Crush".{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}} 

Self Esteem completed a UK tour and played at festivals such as [[Glastonbury Festival|Glastonbury]], [[British Summer Time (concerts)|British Summer Time]] and [[Latitude Festival|Latitude]] during 2019.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=SELF ESTEEM Gigography, Tour History & Past Concerts – Songkick|url=https://www.songkick.com/artists/9686864-self-esteem/gigography|access-date=2021-11-05|website=www.songkick.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Digital|first=Pretty Good|title=Glastonbury Festival - 2019|url=https://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/line-up/line-up-2019/|access-date=2021-11-07|website=Glastonbury Festival - 21st-25th June, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> 

In April 2021, Self Esteem released "I Do This All The Time", the first single from her forthcoming second album as Self Esteem.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-28|title=Self Esteem returns with powerful new song 'I Do This All The Time'|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/self-esteem-returns-with-powerful-new-song-i-do-this-all-the-time-2929679|access-date=2021-05-06|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref> Esteem released the third single, "How Can I Help You".<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2021-08-23|title=Self Esteem takes aim at the objectification of women on new single 'How Can I Help music videos for those singles, filming all three at the [[Almeida Theatre]].<ref name=":0" /> In September, the single "Moody" was released.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-09-16|title=Self Esteem shares ''[[Prioritise Pleasure]]'' was released on 22 October 2021. The UK tour started on 6 November 2021 in Edinburgh.<ref name=":1" />

[[The Guardian]] named "I Do This All The Time" as the best song of 2021, following a vote by 31 of the newspaper's music writers<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-11-29|title=The 20 best songs of 2021|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2021/nov/29/the-20-best-songs-of-2021|access-date=2021-12-01|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref>.    

===Collaborations===

Taylor released her first single as Self Esteem on Kick + Clap, a label run by [[Django Django]] member Dave MacLean.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Django Django's Dave MacLean Launches New Label|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/django-djangos-dave-maclean-launches-new-label|access-date=2019-03-20|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> Taylor then went on to feature on the Django Django track "Surface to Air", which appeared on the band's album ''[[Marble Skies]].{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}''

She provided guest vocals for "Strangle of Anna," a song by fellow Sheffield act [[The Moonlandingz]] which appeared on their second album, ''[[Interplanetary Class Classics]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-03-20|title=The Moonlandingz's new 'Strangle of Anna' video is a greasy and surreal melodrama|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/moonlandingzs-new-strangle-anna-video-greasy-surreal-melodrama-20229212|access-date=2021-08-16|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref> Taylor also performed with the band live.

== Personal life ==
