'''Noah McBeth''' (born April 27, 1991 in Heidelberg, Germany), is the son of a German music manager and an American fashion model. He is better known by his [[stage name]] '''NoMBe.''' McBeth holds both German and US citizenship and is currently working as a Independent recording artist, composer and record producer based in [[Los Angeles]] as well as [[Hawaii]].<ref name="ew">{{cite web|last1=VAIN|first1=MADISON|title=Pharrell-supported NoMBe loves the 'Bad Girls' in his irresistible new jam|url=http://ew.com/music/2017/07/25/nombe-bad-girls-premiere/|publisher=Entertainment Weekly}}</ref><ref name="acclaim">{{cite web|last1=Rowe|first1=Madeleine|title=Upfront: NoMBe|url=http://acclaimmag.com/music/upfront-nombe|publisher=Acclaim Magazine}}</ref>

==Career==
Noah grew up in Germany and starting with the age of 6, he received a classic piano education by the [[Russia|Russian]] concert pianist Nathalia Worm in Germany. After graduation from high school in Germany in 2010 he left Germany to stay with his mother in Florida and he studied Music in Miami from 2011. {{Citation needed|date=August 2021}}

NoMBe released his 18-track debut album in 2018, "'''''They Might’ve Even Loved Me"''''' now under [[Jake Udell|TH3RD BRAIN Records]]. His song '''"Wait"''', premiered worldwide on the popular Australian radio station [[Triple J]].<ref name="pp">{{cite web|title=Listen To NoMBe's Sultry New Single "Wait"|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2017/01/nombe-wait|website=Complex|language=en|accessdate=22 March 2019}}</ref> '''NoMBe''' calls his music psychedelic infused electro-soul"

In February 2018, it was announced that NoMBe was selected to [[Sirius XM Satellite Radio|Sirius XM]]'s Alt Nation's Advanced Placement Tour, along with [[Mikky Ekko]] and [[Mansionair]], an 18-city North American tour, which ran from April to May 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://blog.siriusxm.com/alt-nations-advanced-placement-tour-to-feature-mansionair-mikky-ekko-and-nombe/|title=Alt Nation’s Advanced Placement Tour to feature Mansionair, Mikky Ekko and NoMBe|date=2018-02-27|work=Hear & Now|access-date=2018-07-06|language=en-US}}</ref>

Multiple songs gained airplay around the world, including "Man Up", "Wait",<ref>{{Citation|title=NoMBe "Wait" ▲ Triple J World Premiere 1.16.2017 ▲|url=https://soundcloud.com/th3rdbrain/nombe-wait-triple-j-world-premiere-162017|language=en|access-date=2018-07-06}}</ref> "Freak Like Me", "Drama" and "Milk & Coffee".

In April 2018, McBeth released “Can’t Catch Me” which was selected to be the theme for [[Pharrell Williams|Pharrell]]'s [[HBO]] docu-series ''Outpost''.<ref name="pps">{{cite web|title=NoMBe Releases Pharrell-Approved New Single "Can't Catch Me"|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/music/2017/04/nombe-cant-catch-me|publisher=Pigeons & Planes}}</ref> McBeth has had multiple syncs in the TV series ''[[Ballers]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=NoMBe music featured in movies and TV shows:|url=https://www.tunefind.com/artist/nombe|publisher=TuneFind}}</ref>

In July, McBeth released an EP with fellow [[Jake Udell|TH3RD BRAIN]] musician [[Thutmose (musician)|Thutmose]]. The two have previously collaborated to create "Run Wild", which was selected as [[EA Sports FIFA 18]]'s official "World Cup" commercial track.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8412262/thutmose-run-wild-nombe-fifa-world-cup|title=Thutmose & NoMBe 'Run Wild' for EA Sports 'FIFA World Cup 2018' Game: Listen|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-07-06}}</ref>

His 2018 full-length debut, They Might’ve Even Loved Me, earned praise from '''NPR, Billboard, Afropunk, WWD,''' '''Complex''', and '''Flaunt Magazine'''. '''Elton John''' featured him in Rocket Hour, and '''Pharrell Williams''' championed him on OTHERTONE and selected “Can’t Catch Me” as the theme for his '''HBO''' documentary series OUTPOST. Other syncs followed on '''Shameless, Ozark, The Mighty Ducks: Game Changers, Marvel’s Cloak & Dagger, The Resident, Ballers,''' and in '''Kylie Jenner’s "Life of Kylie".''' 

The '''Rolling Stone Magazin''' dedicated a page in 2020 to him under the title '''“Weirdo”''' is the Song You Need to Know Right Now '''| Rolling Stone'''  . 

'''CHROMATOPIA'''. 

In 2020 his US tour was canceled due to concerns over '''COVID-19'''.  Despite this, he used the time to creatively realign himself and during 2020, he rented an Animal Farm in Sunland, CA. where he assembled '''CHROMATOPIA'''. It garnered acclaim from PAPER Magazine, American Songwriter, and OnesTo Watch. Beginning of 2021 '''NOMBE''' moved from Hollywood CA to Hawai where he built his new studio.  He is currently working on his 3rd Album.
