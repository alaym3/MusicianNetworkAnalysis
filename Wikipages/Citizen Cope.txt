'''Clarence Greenwood''' (born May 20, 1968), also known by his stage name, '''Citizen Cope''', is an American songwriter, producer and performer.<ref name=last.fm>{{cite web|title=Citizen Cope|url=https://www.last.fm/music/Citizen+Cope/+wiki|website=[[Last.fm]]|access-date=October 17, 2013}}</ref> His music is commonly described as a mix of [[blues music|blues]], [[soul music|soul]], [[folk music|folk]], and [[rock music|rock]]. Citizen Cope's compositions have been recorded by [[Carlos Santana]], [[Dido (singer)|Dido]], [[Pharoahe Monch]] and [[Richie Havens]]. He currently records and produces for his own record label, Rainwater Recordings, which he founded in 2010. He had previously been signed to [[Capitol Records|Capitol]], [[Arista Records|Arista]], [[DreamWorks Records|DreamWorks]] and [[RCA Records|RCA]].<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web|last=Greg Prato|title=Citizen Cope - Artist Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/citizen-cope-mn0000110791/biography|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=17 October 2013}}</ref> On March 1, 2019, he self-released his first album in six years, ''Heroin and Helicopters''.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.musicnewsnet.com/2018/11/citizen-cope-announces-1st-album-in-7-years-heroin-and-helicopters-out-march-1st-on-rainwater-record.html|date=November 30, 2018|title=Citizen Cope Announces 1st Album In 7 Years, ‘Heroin And Helicopters,’ Out March 1st On Rainwater Recordings/Thirty Tigers|website=Music News Net|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Born in [[Memphis, Tennessee]], Greenwood spent parts of his childhood in [[Texas]] and [[Mississippi]], ultimately ending up in [[Washington, D.C.]], where he was primarily raised. He graduated from [[Woodrow Wilson High School (Washington, D.C.)|Wilson High School]] and attended [[Texas Tech]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Segal|first=David|title=Citizen Cope's Record Year |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/2002/01/28/citizen-copes-record-year/e98cc70f-5e5b-49e8-bbaa-70915b638880/ |work=[[The Washington Post]] |date=January 28, 2002 |page=C1}}</ref>

==Recording career==
Greenwood was initially the DJ for Washington, D.C.-based [[alternative hip-hop]] group [[Basehead]]. He was signed to Capitol Records in 1997.<ref name=artistdirect>{{cite web|title=Citizen Cope Biography|last=Prato|first=Greg| url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/citizen-cope-mn0000110791/biography|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=March 23, 2020}}</ref> 
In 2000, Citizen Cope signed with DreamWorks and released a self-titled album in 2002.

In 2004, he released ''[[The Clarence Greenwood Recordings]]'', which he also produced. ''[[Every Waking Moment]]'' followed in 2006 (also self-produced), and debuted at #69 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. In 2010, Greenwood established his own record label, Rainwater Recordings. The move allowed him full creative control over his music and career.<ref name=artistdirect /> He released ''[[The Rainwater LP]]'' that year.

"Let the Drummer Kick" from his 2002 album ''[[Citizen Cope (album)|Citizen Cope]]'' achieved [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] gold disc status. Greenwood released his following album, ''One Lovely Day'' on July 17, 2012.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/song-premiere-citizen-cope-one-lovely-day-232914/|title=Song Premiere: Citizen Cope, ‘One Lovely Day’|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|first=Natalie|last=Morin|date=July 16, 2012|access-date=March 23, 2020}}</ref>

On March 1, 2019 his first album in six years, ''Heroin and Helicopters'' was released.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2019/05/03/719957716/catching-up-with-citizen-cope|title=Catching Up With Citizen Cope|date=May 3, 2019|work=[[NPR]]|first1=Stephen|last1=Kallao| first2=John| last2=Myers|access-date=March 23, 2020}}</ref> He released his first single from the album, "Justice," on November 30, 2018.<ref name="swings">{{cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2018/11/30/671173386/citizen-cope-swings-for-the-fences-and-scores-on-justice|title=Citizen Cope Swings For The Fences And Scores On 'Justice'|first=Bruce|last=Warren|date=November 30, 2018|work=[[NPR]]|access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref>

==Songwriting and producing==
[[File:Citizen Cope, Cat's Cradle (8553518573).jpg|thumb|Citizen Cope performing at [[Cat's Cradle (venue)|Cat's Cradle]] in [[Carrboro, North Carolina]]]]
Greenwood's work has been recorded by a number of musicians. In 2002, [[Santana (band)|Santana]] recorded Cope's song "Sideways" for their ''[[Shaman (album)|Shaman]]'' album.<ref name="swings" /> He performed "Sideways" in concert with Santana on his 2003 European tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.blumenthalarts.org/news/detail/tunesday-citizen-cope|first=Jenna| last=Harris|title="TUNES"day--Citizen Cope|date=March 7, 2017|publisher=[[North Carolina Blumenthal Performing Arts Center]]|access-date=March 23, 2020}}</ref> Greenwood sang on and produced the track. [[Sheryl Crow]] asked to record the song in 2010 for her ''[[100 Miles from Memphis]]'' album, and invited Greenwood to join her in concert on several occasions, including the [[Crossroads Guitar Festival]] in [[Chicago]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nhregister.com/entertainment/article/Citizen-Cope-at-Klein-Memorial-Auditorium-in-11352772.php|title=Citizen Cope at Klein Memorial Auditorium in Bridgeport Thursday|first=Mark|last=Zaretsky|date=March 27, 2015|access-date=March 23, 2020}}</ref> At the same festival, [[Eric Clapton]] brought Greenwood onstage to perform "Hands of the Saints" together.<ref>{{cite av media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TU772lwIOwM|title=Citizen Cope & Eric Clapton Hands of the Saints Crossroads 2010|date=March 16, 2019|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=March 23, 2020}}</ref> [[Corey Taylor]] also recorded a version of the song. [[Brett Dennen]] recorded a version of the song "Healing Hands" for ''The Voice Project Presents Home Recordings Volume 1'' in 2012.<ref>{{cite av media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LeyKnObbdUM|title=Brett Dennen » Citizen Cope "Healing Hands"|date=September 15, 2010|access-date=March 23, 2020|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>

In 2008, Greenwood wrote and recorded "Burnin' Love" with [[Dido (singer)|Dido]] for her ''[[Safe Trip Home]]'' album.{{fact|date=May 2021}} That same year, [[Richie Havens]] released his version of "Hurricane Waters" and the hip-hop artist [[Rhymefest]] recorded "Bullet," featuring Greenwood.{{fact|date=May 2021}}

His work appeared in a number of soundtracks to films including ''[[Trust the Man]]'', ''[[Ghost Town (2008 film)|Ghost Town]]'', ''Coach Carter (2005 film)'', ''[[Accepted]]'', ''[[The Lincoln Lawyer (film)|The Lincoln Lawyer]]'', and ''[[Fracture (2007 film)|Fracture]]''. His song "Bullet and a Target" appeared in 2006's ''[[The Sentinel (2006 film)|The Sentinel]]'', ''[[Alpha Dog]]'' as well as in the television show, ''[[Cold Case]]''.{{fact|date=May 2021}} Greenwood's songs have also been included on ''[[Scrubs (TV series)|Scrubs]]'', ''[[One Tree Hill (TV series)|One Tree Hill]]'', ''[[Entourage (U.S. TV series)|Entourage]]'', ''[[So You Think You Can Dance (United States)|So You Think You Can Dance]]'', and ''[[Smallville]]''. "One Lovely Day" appeared in the film ''[[Battleship (film)|Battleship]].''{{fact|date=May 2021}}
