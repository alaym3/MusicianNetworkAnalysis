'''Marquis Amonte King''' (born December 12, 1992),<ref name="WaPo" /> better known by his stage name '''Shy Glizzy''', is an American rapper from [[Southeast (Washington, D.C.)|Southeast]], [[Washington, D.C.]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Shy Glizzy – Fxck Rap [Mixtape Review]|url=http://metrojolt.com/shy-glizzy-fxck-rap-mixtape-review|website=Metrojolt|accessdate=November 13, 2014}}</ref>

== Background ==
Shy Glizzy was born Marquis Amonte King on December 12, 1992, at [[Greater Southeast Community Hospital]] and grew up on 37th avenue in [[Southeast Washington, D.C.]]<ref name="WaPo">{{cite web|last1=Richards|first1=Chris|title=Six months on the trail with Shy Glizzy, Washington's latest budding rap star|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/six-months-on-the-trail-of-shy-glizzy-the-most-thrilling-rapper-dc-has-ever-seen/2015/05/21/e91062ba-fe3b-11e4-833c-a2de05b6b2a4_story.html|website=The Washington Post|accessdate=January 9, 2017|date=May 21, 2015}}</ref> His father was shot and killed at 19 years old ,<ref name="WCP" /> before Shy's first birthday. He grew up in a household with his mother, grandmother, and his younger brother, {{nowrap|3 Glizzy}}. At age 9, he was arrested for petty [[larceny]].<ref name="WaPo" /> At age 16, he was arrested for a robbery and spent 14 months in juvenile detention,<ref name="fader2013">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.thefader.com/2013/03/19/gen-f-shy-glizzy |title=Gen F: Shy Glizzy |magazine=[[Fader (magazine)|Fader]] |last1=Nosnitsky |first1=Andrew |date=March 19, 2013 |accessdate=March 14, 2019}}</ref> where he also earned his [[GED]].<ref name="WCP" />

He received his nickname "Shy" during early childhood.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/ypvy7y/jefe-formerly-shy-glizzy-has-become-dcs-most-important-new-rap-voice |title=Jefe, Formerly Shy Glizzy, Has Become DC's Most Important New Rap Voice |publisher=[[Noisey]] |date=January 11, 2017 |accessdate=March 14, 2019}}</ref> He is [[Muslim]].<ref>{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190315121401/https://twitter.com/ShyGlizzy/status/217079318571003907?lang=en |title=ShyGlizzy |url-status=dead |url=https://twitter.com/ShyGlizzy/status/217079318571003907?lang=en |website=[[Twitter]] |date=June 24, 2012 |archive-date=March 15, 2019}}</ref> As a teenager, he began reading the [[Quran]], then the daily newspaper and stacks of biographies, before starting to write raps.<ref name="WaPo" /> He was initially "trying to write a book, but it ended up being a song."<ref name="fader2013" /> Shy has stated he did not listen to rap music growing up but rather [[go-go]] music, which he credits for helping to create his own unique sound.<ref name="WaPo" /> https://www.abss.k12.nc.us/cms/lib02/NC01001905/Centricity/Domain/3797/number-the-stars-lois-lowry.pdf

== Career ==
=== 2011–2013 ===
In 2011, Shy Glizzy released his debut project, titled ''No Brainer'', in January. He released his second mixtape, ''Streets Hottest Youngin'', on October 31.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/wj4wwn/shy-glizzy-march-madness-bracket |title=I Made a Shy Glizzy Madness Bracket Because No One Else Is Brave Enough To |publisher=Noisey |last1=Laurels |first1=True |date=March 16, 2018 |accessdate=March 30, 2019}}</ref>

On June 6, 2012, Shy released his third mixtape titled ''Law''. He was also featured by ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' on its list of "10 New [[Baltimore–Washington metropolitan area|DMV]] Rappers To Watch Out For."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/06/10-new-dmv-rappers-to-watch-out-for/shy-glizzy |last1=Kimble|first1=Julian |title=10 New DMV Rappers To Watch Out For |website=Complex |date=June 6, 2012 |accessdate=November 13, 2014}}</ref> The ''[[Washington City Paper]]''{{'}}s Ramon Ramirez referred to it as "a well-connected tape [which] sometimes feels like it wants to be a major-label-debut", featuring guest appearances from [[Project Pat]] and [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]] and production from [[Beat Billionaire]], but described Shy as being "less polished, and more {{wt|en|frenetic}}, while his hooks are repetitive and droning but also hungry and catchy."<ref name="WCP">{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtoncitypaper.com/arts/music/blog/13077782/shy-glizzys-law-reviewed |title=Shy Glizzy's ''Law'' Reviewed |newspaper=[[Washington City Paper]] |date=June 15, 2012 |accessdate=March 12, 2019}}</ref>

On November 14, 2012, Shy released his fourth mixtape, ''Fly Money'', an 11-track collaborative project with Jose Guapo. On December 12, he released his fifth mixtape, ''Fxck Rap'', featuring a guest appearance from [[Trinidad James]] and production from Speaker Knockerz.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Zeichner|first1=Naomi|title=Download Shy Glizzy's Fxck Rap Mixtape|url=https://www.thefader.com/2012/12/12/download-shy-glizzys-fxck-rap-mixtape|website=Fader|accessdate=November 13, 2014}}</ref>

On March 16, 2013, Shy Glizzy was listed by ''Complex'' as one of "15 Unsigned Rappers Who Should Get a Deal After [[SXSW]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2013/03/15-unsigned-rappers-who-should-get-a-deal-after-sxsw/shy-glizzy |title=15 Unsigned Rappers Who Should Get a Deal After SXSW |website=Complex |date=March 16, 2013 |accessdate=March 17, 2019}}</ref> On April 3, he announced his sixth mixtape, ''Law 2'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/ShyGlizzy/status/319446307716599809 |title=ShyGlizzy |website=Twitter |date=April 3, 2013}}</ref> which went on to be released on August 1, and included guest appearances from the likes of [[Migos]], [[Yo Gotti]], [[Starlito]] and [[Kevin Gates]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Goddard|first1=Kevin|title=Shy Glizzy – Law 2|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/shy-glizzy-law-2-mixtape.91790.html?song-1|website=Hot New Hip Hop|date=August 2, 2013|accessdate=November 13, 2014}}</ref>

=== 2014–2016 ===
On January 7, 2014, Shy Glizzy was listed by ''[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]'' as one of "10 Rappers to Watch in 2014".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2014/01/07/10-rappers-to-watch-in-2014/7/ |title=10 Rappers to Watch in 2014 |website=Complex |date=January 7, 2014 |accessdate=March 19, 2019}}</ref> On January 20, he announced his seventh mixtape, ''Young Jefe'', which went on to be released on February 17, and included guest appearances from the likes of [[Young Scooter]], [[PeeWee Longway]], [[Young Thug]], [[Plies (rapper)|Plies]] and [[Gudda Gudda]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lilah|first1=Rose|title=Shy Glizzy – Young Jefe|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/shy-glizzy-young-jefe-new-mixtape.108777.html|website=Hot New Hip Hop|date=February 17, 2014|accessdate=November 13, 2014}}</ref> From that mixtape, the hit single "Awwsome" turned into the biggest song of his career and peaked at number 45 on the US [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6297054/shy-glizzy/chart?f=367|title=Shy Glizzy – Chart history |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=December 19, 2016}}</ref> It spawned a remix off the record that features [[A$AP Rocky]] and [[2 Chainz]] that grabbed more than 750,000 streams on SoundCloud in the first week of its release.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Krastz|first1=Roger|title=Shy Glizzy Says Birdman Inspired Him To Get In The Studio With Boosie Badazz|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/10/shy-glizzy-birdman-boosie-studio/|website=XXL Mag|accessdate=November 11, 2014}}</ref>

On June 3, 2015, Shy Glizzy was selected as part of the [[XXL (magazine)|''XXL'' magazine]] Freshman Class list.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2176762/2015-xxl-freshman-class-cover/ |title=Here's Who Made the ''2015 XXL Freshman Class'' Cover |publisher=[[MTV News]] |date=June 3, 2015 |accessdate=March 30, 2019}}</ref> That same day he released his ninth mixtape, ''Be Careful'', a 16-track collaborative project with his Glizzy Gang collective which included members 30 Glizzy, Goo Glizzy and 3 Glizzy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://respect-mag.com/2015/06/shy-glizzy-drops-be-careful-mixtape-with-glizzy-gang/ |title=Shy Glizzy Drops "Be Careful" Mixtape with Glizzy Gang |publisher=[[Respect (magazine)|Respect]] |date=June 4, 2015 |accessdate=March 29, 2019}}</ref> On August 28, Shy announced his 10th mixtape, ''For Trappers Only'', entirely produced by [[Zaytoven]] to be released on September 30.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2015/08/shy-glizzy-announces-new-mixtape-zaytoven/ |title=Shy Glizzy Announces New Mixtape With Zaytoven |magazine=XXL |date=August 28, 2019 |accessdate=March 30, 2019}}</ref> It included guest appearances from the likes of [[Boosie Badazz]], [[Yo Gotti]] and [[Ty Dolla $ign]].

On July 8, 2016, Shy Glizzy announced his 11th mixtape, ''Young Jefe 2'', to be released on July 15.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/07/shy-glizzy-release-date-young-jefe-2/ |title=Shy Glizzy Reveals Release Date for 'Young Jefe 2' |magazine=XXL |date=July 8, 2016 |accessdate=April 4, 2019}}</ref>

In a December 2014 interview with ''[[The Fader]]'', when asked about his thoughts on "DMV rap", Shy responded "DMV still didn't become a thing. Nobody opened up a lane for DMV rap {{small|[...]}} I opened up my lane. I opened up Glizzy Gang lane, the southeast DC lane."<ref name="Fader2014">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.thefader.com/2014/12/10/shy-glizzy-interview |title=Shy Glizzy On Being "Awwsome" |magazine=[[The Fader]] |last1=Younger |first1=Briana |date=December 10, 2014 |accessdate=March 20, 2019}}</ref> However, in a January 2017 interview with ''The Fader'', when asked about the state of DC's rap scene, Shy responded "I'm trying to open up that lane so more youngsters can come through. No one else ever opened that door for me {{small|[...]}} I got a whole culture—{{nowrap|the D, [[Maryland|the M]], and [[Virginia|the V]]}}—to put on for."<ref name="Fader2017" />

=== 2017–present ===
On January 6, 2017, Shy Glizzy released an [[Extended play|EP]], ''The World is Yours'', under the stage name 'Jefe'.<ref name="Fader2017">{{cite web|last1=Dandridge-Lemco|first1=Ben|title=Shy Glizzy Is Changing His Name To Jefe And Releasing A New Project|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/01/03/shy-glizzy-the-world-is-yours-interview-one-stream|website=The FADER|accessdate=January 6, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Cook-Wilson|first1=Winston|title=Shy Glizzy Changes Name to Jefe, Releases Zaytoven-Produced "One"|url=http://www.spin.com/2017/01/shy-glizzy-changes-name-to-jefe-releases-zaytoven-produced-one/|website=Spin|accessdate=January 6, 2017|date=January 3, 2017}}</ref> [[HipHopDX]]'s Aaron McKrell described it as "an energetic but hollow offering" but praised Shy Glizzy's delivery and "ability to manipulate vocal sonics." He rated the project 2.7 out of 5.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/reviews/id.2895/title.review-shy-glizzys-switch-to-jefe-doesnt-help-the-world-is-yours-ep|title = Review: Shy Glizzy's Switch to Jefe Doesn't Help "The World is Yours" EP|date = January 14, 2017}}</ref> [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'s Sheldon Pearce stated that it "isn't King at the peak of his powers {{small|[...]}} Here, he mostly seems content to roll leisurely through verses" and showed "no sense of urgency." He rated the project 6.9 out of 10.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/22808-the-world-is-yours/|title = Jefe: The World is Yours}}</ref>

The single "Take Me Away" was released on September 29.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Weinstein|first1=Max|title=Shy Glizzy Is Back With New Song 'Take Me Away' – XXL|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/09/shy-glizzy-take-me-away/|website=XXL Mag|accessdate=September 29, 2017}}</ref> He released his 12th mixtape ''Quiet Storm'' on December 12,<ref name="Quiet Storm">{{cite web|last1=Goddard|first1=Kevin|title=Stream Shy Glizzy's "Quiet Storm" Project|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/stream-shy-glizzys-quiet-storm-project-new-mixtape.117689.html|website=HotNewHipHop|date=December 12, 2017|accessdate=December 18, 2017}}</ref> which he described as his "best body of work yet."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/01/31/shy-glizzy-talks-grammy-nom-ep-quiet-storm-losing-brother-06009f8ad7 |title=Shy Glizzy talks his Grammy nom, new 'Quiet Storm' EP and losing his brother |publisher=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt TV]] |date=January 31, 2018 |accessdate=March 21, 2019}}</ref>

In 2018, Shy Glizzy released his debut [[studio album]], ''Fully Loaded'', on October 18, which included guest appearances from [[YoungBoy Never Broke Again]], [[Lil Uzi Vert]], [[Young Thug]], [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]], and [[Rick Ross]].<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Lamarre |first1=Carl |title=Stream Shy Glizzy's New Album 'Fully Loaded' Featuring Lil Uzi Vert, Young Thug & Gunna: Exclusive |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8479614/shy-glizzy-fully-loaded-album |magazine=Billboard |accessdate=October 19, 2018}}</ref> The album received positive reviews<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pearce |first1=Sheldon |title=Shy Glizzy: Fully Loaded review |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/shy-glizzy-fully-loaded/|website=Pitchfork |accessdate=March 3, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Blackwell |first1=Marcus |title=Review: Shy Glizzy Covers All Bases On "Fully Loaded" |url=https://hiphopdx.com/reviews/id.3280/title.review-shy-glizzy-covers-all-bases-on-fully-loaded#|website=HipHopDX |date=October 29, 2018 |accessdate=March 3, 2019}}</ref> and peaked at number 35 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], selling 14,446 [[album-equivalent units]] in the first week.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.48912/title.hip-hop-album-sales-quavos-quavo-huncho-catapults-to-no-2-on-billboard-200|title=Hip Hop Album Sales: Quavo's "Quavo Huncho" Catapults To No. 2 On Billboard 200|last=http://hiphopdx.com|first=HipHopDX-|date=October 22, 2018|website=HipHopDX|access-date=March 3, 2019}}</ref> It was announced that Glizzy will embark on nationwide tour with Atlanta rapper Gunna on his [[Drip or Drown 2]] tour.

In an April interview with HipHopDX's Daniela Evgenivna, Glizzy announced he would be releasing his second studio album titled ''Covered 'N Blood'' on April 19.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.50955/title.shy-glizzy-discusses-new-album-covered-in-blood-nipsey-hussles-life-lessons|title=Shy Glizzy Discusses New Album "Covered In Blood" & Nipsey Hussle's Life Lessons|last=http://hiphopdx.com|first=HipHopDX-|date=April 7, 2019|website=HipHopDX|access-date=April 21, 2019}}</ref> He described the album as being more personal than the feature packed ''Fully Loaded'' stating "It's a really deep album. A lot of my music is like a diary. This one is about what I've been going through the last few months."<ref name=":0" /> On April 19, ''Covered n' Blood'' was released as Glizzy's second [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]-charting record, peaking at 156. Shy ended 2019 with a second mixtape titled ''''Aloha'''', inspired by and recorded on the island of [[Hawaii (island)|Hawaii]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Release |first1=Press |title=SHY GLIZZY RELEASES NEW EP 'ALOHA' |url=http://www.elicitmagazine.com/shy-glizzy-releases-new-ep-aloha/ |website=Elicit Magazine |date=August 30, 2019 |publisher=Elicit Magazine |accessdate=April 16, 2020}}</ref> On April 10, 2020, he released the single "Lonely Vibes".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/04/shy-glizzy-new-track-lonely-vibes|title=Shy Glizzy Is Back With New Track "Lonely Vibes"|website=Complex|accessdate=April 29, 2020}}</ref> He released the song "Right Or Wrong" with [[Lil Uzi Vert]] on June 19.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lil Uzi Vert Joins Shy Glizzy on New Song "Right or Wrong"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/06/lil-uzi-vert-shy-glizzy-right-or-wrong|accessdate=June 22, 2020|website=Complex}}</ref>
On September 18, 2020, Shy Glizzy released his new project, ''Young Jefe 3''. It was preceded by the single "Forever Tre 7", featuring No Savage, a new signee to Glizzy's label, Glizzy Gang Records, an imprint under [[300 Entertainment]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2020/9/14/21436562/shy-glizzy-forever-tre-7-no-savage|title=Shy Glizzy drops new video "Forever Tre 7" featuring No Savage|work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|last=Regina|first=Cho|date=September 14, 2020|accessdate=September 17, 2020}}</ref> In September 2020, he released the album ''Young Jefe 3''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Shy Glizzy Returns With His New Album 'Young Jefe 3'|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/9/shy-glizzy-young-jefe-3-new-album-stream|access-date=September 24, 2020|website=HYPEBEAST|date=September 18, 2020}}</ref>
