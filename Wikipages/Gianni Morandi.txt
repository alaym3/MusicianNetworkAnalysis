'''Gianni Morandi''' ({{IPA-it|ˈdʒanni moˈrandi}}; born 11 December 1944) is an [[Italy|Italian]] pop singer, actor and entertainer.<ref name="MPC-object" />

== Early life ==
Gian Luigi Morandi was born in a little village called [[Monghidoro]] on the Tuscan-Emilian Apennines. His father Renato was active within the [[Italian Communist Party]] and Gianni used to help him sell the party newspapers. At an early age Morandi worked as a shoe-shiner, cobbler and as a candy vendor in the village's only cinema. His vocal abilities led him to a number of small gigs, some of which were during the Communist Party’s activities.{{citation needed|date=September 2019}}

== Career ==
{{More citations needed section|date=September 2019}}
[[File:Gianni Morandi (Italie), Bestanddeelnr 923-3708.jpg|thumb|left|180px|Gianni Morandi during [[Eurovision Song Contest 1970]]]]

He made his debut in 1962 and quickly placed high at or won a number of Italian popular song festivals, including the ''[[Canzonissima]]'' festival in 1969. In 1962 he was signed by [[RCA Italiana]] and achieved national stardom with the song "Fatti mandare dalla mamma", and remained Italy’s darling throughout that decade.

In 1970, he represented Italy at the [[Eurovision Song Contest]] with "[[Occhi di ragazza]]". His career went into a decline in the 1970s but underwent a revival in the 1980s. He won the [[Music of Italy#San Remo Festival|Festival of Sanremo]] in 1987 with "Si può dare di più" together with [[Enrico Ruggeri]] and [[Umberto Tozzi]], placed second in 1995 and third in 2000.

It is estimated that Morandi has sold 50 million recordings.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musica.virgilio.it/news/pop/gianni_morandi_in_numeri.html |title=Gianni Morandi in numeri - Pop - News - Virgilio Musica |publisher=Musica.virgilio.it |date=2012-09-20 |access-date=2012-11-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140903085054/http://musica.virgilio.it/news/pop/gianni_morandi_in_numeri.html |archive-date=2014-09-03 }}</ref>

He has written a number of autobiographical books and appeared in 18 films. In TV he played [[Claude Jade]]'s husband Davide in the 1984 [[television program|TV series]] ''[[Voglia di Volare|Voglia di volare]]''. He also played as actor in several TV series, as well as the host in popular Italian television shows.

In 1966 he married actress [[Laura Efrikian]], with whom he played in several [[Musicarello|Musicarelli films]] together.<ref>{{cite book|last=Enrico Lancia, Roberto Poppi|title=Dizionario del cinema italiano - Le Attrici|publisher=Gremese Editore, 2003|isbn=888440214X}}</ref> The couple divorced in 1979.<ref>{{cite news|last=Vivì Zizzo|title=Laura Efrikian torna a parlare. Intervista esclusiva alla prima moglie di Gianni Morandi|url=http://www.oggi.it/focus/personaggi/2011/02/01/laura-efrikian-torna-a-parlare-intervista-esclusiva-alla-prima-moglie-di-gianni-morandi/|access-date=8 January 2014|newspaper=[[Oggi (magazine)|Oggi]]|date=1 February 2011}}</ref>

Morandi was chosen to be the presenter of [[Sanremo Music Festival 2011]], together with [[Belén Rodriguez]] and [[Elisabetta Canalis]]. They were joined by comedians Luca Bizzarri and Paolo Kessisoglu from [[Italia 1]]'s satire show ''[[Le Iene]]''.<ref>{{cite news|author=Renato Franco|url=http://www.corriere.it/spettacoli/10_ottobre_05/morandi-stop-trattativa-sanremo-rai_c3a0c644-d098-11df-9b01-00144f02aabc.shtml|title=Festival di Sanremo, ok a Morandi. Ci saranno anche Belen ed Elisabetta|work=Corriere della Sera|location=Italy|date=May 10, 2010|page=41|access-date=February 6, 2011|language=it}}</ref> In October 2011, he was confirmed as the presenter of the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2012]].<ref name=LaStampa13Oct2011>{{cite web | url = http://www3.lastampa.it/spettacoli/sezioni/articolo/lstp/424698/ | work = [[La Stampa]] | language = it | access-date = 2 February 2012 | title = Il Morandi bis sul palco di Sanremo. Adesso è ufficiale: lo condurrò io | date = 13 October 2011 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20111217030523/http://www3.lastampa.it/spettacoli/sezioni/articolo/lstp/424698/ | archive-date = 17 December 2011 | url-status = dead }}</ref>

At the beginning of 2016, Gianni Morandi started a tour called "Capitani Coraggiosi Tour" with [[Claudio Baglioni]]. A double album will be published on February 5.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Baglioni e Morandi, il gran ritorno: "Capitani coraggiosi" di nuovo in tour|url = http://www.repubblica.it/spettacoli/musica/2016/01/08/news/baglioni_morandi_capitani_coraggiosi-130853094/|website = Spettacoli - La Repubblica|access-date = 2016-02-23}}</ref> In 2017 he sang with Fabio Rovazzi the song "Volare", a big hit.

In 2019 his 1964 song "[[In ginocchio da te]]" gained renewed popularity as it was featured in one of the main scenes of the [[South Korea|South Korean]] movie [[Parasite (2019 film)|Parasite]], which won four [[Academy Awards|Oscars]] the following year, including [[Best Picture Academy Award|Best Picture]].

== Awards and honors ==
* Gianni Morandi has been honorary president of [[Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna Football Club 1909]] since 2010.
* His songs "[[In ginocchio da te]]", "Non son degno di te" and "Scende la pioggia" were certified as having each sold over one million copies, and were awarded [[music recording sales certification|gold discs]].<ref name="The Book of Golden Discs" />
* Asteroid [[248970 Giannimorandi]], discovered by Italian amateur astronomer [[Vincenzo Casulli]] in 2007, was named in his honor.<ref name="MPC-object" /> The official {{MoMP|248970|naming citation}} was published by the [[Minor Planet Center]] on 25 November 2015 ({{small|[[Minor Planet Circulars|M.P.C.]] 96938}}).<ref name="MPC-Circulars-Archive" />
