'''Volbeat''' are <!-- Please refer to WP:ENGVAR. This article is written in British English, which uses the plural format while referring to music groups. Volbeat may also refer to a Pokémon, but is completely different. Please do NOT change "are" to "is". Thank you. --> a Danish [[Rock music|rock]] band formed in [[Copenhagen]] in 2001. They play a fusion of [[rock and roll]], [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] and [[rockabilly]]. Their current line-up consists of vocalist and guitarist [[Michael Poulsen]], guitarist [[Rob Caggiano]], drummer [[Jon Larsen (Danish musician)|Jon Larsen]] and bassist Kaspar Boye Larsen. The band is signed to Dutch label Mascot Records and has released seven studio albums and one DVD. All of their studio albums have been [[Music recording sales certification|certified gold]] in Denmark. Their second album ''[[Rock the Rebel/Metal the Devil]]'' received platinum status, and their 2010 release ''[[Beyond Hell/Above Heaven]]'' was subject to widespread international critical acclaim, receiving double platinum in Denmark, platinum in Finland and Germany, and gold in the United States,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=volbeat#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum - RIAA|work=RIAA|access-date=18 March 2017}}</ref> Sweden and Austria. Volbeat's seventh album, ''[[Rewind, Replay, Rebound]]'', was released on 2 August 2019.

==History==

===Early years (2001–2009)===
[[File:Volbeat Michael Poulsen 2009.jpg|thumb|left|Michael Poulsen, singer, guitarist and main songwriter of Volbeat]]
Singer [[Michael Poulsen]]'s first band was [[death metal]] act, [[Dominus (band)|Dominus]].<ref name="katt">{{cite web|url=http://www.katt.com/2014/04/22/jake-daniels-tuesdays-broadcast-4-22-14/|title=Jake Daniels Tuesday's Broadcast 4-22-14|date=22 April 2014|access-date=16 September 2019}}</ref><ref name="allmusic1">{{cite web|last=True|first=Chris|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/volbeat-mn0000725175/biography|title=Volbeat Biography|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=16 September 2019}}</ref> In 2000, Poulsen became fed up with the death metal music scene, causing Dominus to break up.<ref name="allmusic1"/><ref name="katt"/> In 2001, Poulsen went on to form a new band with some friends and other former Dominus members.<ref name="allmusic1"/> This was the beginning of Volbeat.<ref name="allmusic1"/>

The band name "Volbeat" was derived from Dominus's third album name, ''Vol.Beat'' (read as: Volume Beat).<ref name="katt"/> After selling 1,000 copies of their ''Beat the Meat'' demo tape, Volbeat was signed to a record deal by Rebel Monster Records, a sub-label of Mascot Records.<ref name="allmusic1"/>

Volbeat released their first album, ''[[The Strength/The Sound/The Songs]]'' in 2005.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Volbeat-The-Strength-The-Sound-The-Songs/release/955581|title=Volbeat - The Strength / The Sound / The Songs|website=Discogs|language=en|access-date=25 January 2017}}</ref> The line-up for the album consisted of Michael Poulsen (vocals), Jon Larsen (drums), Franz Gottschalk (guitar) and Anders Kjølholm (bass).<ref name="allmusic1"/> The album was a huge success in Denmark achieving a spot at No. 18 in the charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalstorm.ee/bands/biography.php?band_id=2788&bandname=Volbeat |title=Volbeat - Biography - Metal Storm |publisher=MetalStorm.ee |access-date=20 February 2010}}</ref> Critics were generally positive towards ''The Strength / The Sound / The Songs''. The German [[hard rock]] magazine RockHard gave it 10/10 in a review.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rockhard.de/index.php?smod=p209Wz1iMUIfo2qAo2D9pz9wn2uupzDhpzI2nJI3pl5xMKEunJkJnJI3Wzqlo3IjFHD9pzuspzI2nJI3WzAioaEyoaEWEQ00ZQV4AlMmqJAbp3ElnJ5aCKMioTWyLKDz |title=Rock Hard Online rockt das Web |publisher=RockHard.de |access-date=20 February 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927235922/http://www.rockhard.de/index.php?smod=p209Wz1iMUIfo2qAo2D9pz9wn2uupzDhpzI2nJI3pl5xMKEunJkJnJI3Wzqlo3IjFHD9pzuspzI2nJI3WzAioaEyoaEWEQ00ZQV4AlMmqJAbp3ElnJ5aCKMioTWyLKDz |archive-date=27 September 2007 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> ''The Strength / The Sound / The Songs'' also received a handful of awards, including Best Album in the Danish Metal Musik Awards in 2005.<ref name=ansvol>{{cite web|url=http://www.answers.com/topic/volbeat |title=Volbeat: Information from Answers.com |publisher=Answers.com |access-date=20 February 2010}}</ref> Volbeat was praised for its [[concert|live performance]]s. Their concert at [[Roskilde Festival]] 2006 received a 6/6 stars review in the Danish newspaper [[B.T. (tabloid)|BT]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Volbeat |title=Volbeat - Discover music, videos, concerts, & pictures at Last.fm |publisher=Last.fm |access-date=20 February 2010}}</ref>

[[File:20140613-067-Nova Rock 2014-Volbeat-Anders Kjølholm.JPG|thumb|right|upright=0.6|Bassist Anders Kjølholm at [[Nova Rock Festival|Nova Rock]] 2014]]

The band, featuring the same line-up as their first album, released their second album, ''[[Rock the Rebel/Metal the Devil]]'', in week 8 of 2007, where it debuted as No. 1 in the Danish Top 40 for CD-sales.<ref name=ansvol />

They opened the [[Roskilde Festival]] in 2007 and also supported [[Metallica]] together with [[Mnemic]] at their concert in Denmark 13 July of that year.<ref name="allmusic1"/> They were to support [[Megadeth]] in Finland and in their home town, [[Copenhagen]], and to make a few festival appearances mid-2008.<ref name="allmusic1"/> They hired [[Thomas Bredahl]] following the recording of ''Rock the Rebel / Metal the Devil'' as a replacement for Gottschalk on guitar.<ref name="allmusic1"/>

In 2008, Volbeat released their third album, ''[[Guitar Gangsters & Cadillac Blood]]'' featuring guitarist Thomas Bredahl. It topped the Finnish Album Chart straight after its publication.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://yle.fi/lista/listat/tuote.php?id=8898 |title=Suomen virallinen lista |publisher=yle.fi }}</ref> In 2009, Volbeat supported the symphonic metal band [[Nightwish]] on their U.S tour in May.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.metalfromfinland.com/news/2008-10-28_23:47/volbeat_to_support_nightwish_on_spring_2009_us_tour |title=Volbeat To Support Nightwish on Spring 2009 U.S. Tour - Metal from Finland |publisher=MetalFromFinland.com |date=28 October 2008 |access-date=20 February 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120220013008/http://www.metalfromfinland.com/news/2008-10-28_23:47/volbeat_to_support_nightwish_on_spring_2009_us_tour |archive-date=20 February 2012 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> On 31 May 2009 they were on the main stage of the [[Pinkpop Festival]]. They also supported Metallica on the North American leg of Metallica's [[World Magnetic Tour]] from October to December 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.metallica.com/index.asp?item=602410 |title=Metallica.com - More Opening Act News |publisher=Metallica.com |date=27 July 2009 |access-date=20 February 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100125041505/http://www.metallica.com/index.asp?item=602410 |archive-date=25 January 2010 |url-status=dead  }}</ref>
In 2009 the band played at the biggest open-air festival in Europe (400,000–500,000 rock fans every year), [[Przystanek Woodstock]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.en.wosp.org.pl/woodstock/|title=Home - Wielka Orkiestra Świątecznej Pomocy|work=Wielka Orkiestra Swiątecznej Pomocy|access-date=10 January 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100325213029/http://www.en.wosp.org.pl/woodstock/|archive-date=25 March 2010}}</ref> in Poland.

===  Line-up changes (2010–2015)===
[[File:20140613-064-Nova_Rock_2014-Volbeat-Michael_Schøn_Poulsen_and_Rob_Caggiano.JPG|thumb|right|Rob Caggiano and Michael Poulsen at [[Nova Rock Festival|Nova Rock]] 2014]]
Poulsen stated about ''[[Beyond Hell/Above Heaven]]'', "The album title refers to the overall theme of the album, and it continues the storyline from where ''Guitar Gangsters & Cadillac Blood'' left off...".<ref name=voldk>{{cite web |url=http://volbeat.dk/2010/02/15/volbeat-reveals-album-title/ |title=Volbeat: Official Homepage - Volbeat Reveals Album Title! |publisher=Volbeat.dk |date=15 February 2010 |access-date=20 February 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100222225514/http://volbeat.dk/2010/02/15/volbeat-reveals-album-title/ |archive-date=22 February 2010 }}</ref>

On 25 January 2010, it was confirmed that Volbeat would be performing at [[Download Festival]] in June 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/download-2010-stage-times-revealed|title=Download 2010 Stage Times Revealed|publisher=Rock Sound|date=8 June 2018|access-date=16 September 2019}}</ref> It was also confirmed that Volbeat were to join [[Metallica]], [[Megadeth]], [[Anthrax (American band)|Anthrax]], [[Slayer]] and others in the "Big Four" show at Sonisphere Switzerland.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://sz.sonispherefestivals.com/2010/01/volbeat-definitiv-bestatigt/ |title=VOLBEAT DEFINITIV BESTÄTIGT: Sonisphere 2010 Switzerland |publisher=SonisphereFestivals.com |date=10 January 2010 |access-date=20 February 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100724130615/http://sz.sonispherefestivals.com/2010/01/volbeat-definitiv-bestatigt/ |archive-date=24 July 2010 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> It was also announced that the new album, ''Beyond Hell/Above Heaven'' was to be released on 10 September 2010. The band also announced their "Beyond Hell/Above Heaven Live 2010" tour.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://volbeat.dk/2010/05/14/album-release-and-fall-dates-confirmed/ |title=Volbeat: Official Homepage - Album release and fall dates confirmed! |publisher=Volbeat.dk |date=14 May 2010 |access-date=30 May 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100521131137/http://volbeat.dk/2010/05/14/album-release-and-fall-dates-confirmed/ |archive-date=21 May 2010 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> In 2011, Volbeat announced their Grand Summer Tour across North America. The tour began in Toronto, Ontario on 24 July at Heavy T.O., and concluded with a two-night stand on 3 & 4 September at the House of Blues in Anaheim, CA. Volbeat co-headlined this tour with rock powerhouse [[Cold (band)|Cold]] on all US dates, but not in Canada. On 28 November 2011, Volbeat announced the departure of Bredahl via their website.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.volbeat.dk/2/news.php?id=70&lang=en |title=Under Construction |publisher=Volbeat.dk |access-date=20 September 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120429015255/http://www.volbeat.dk/2/news.php?id=70&lang=en |archive-date=29 April 2012 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> Volbeat committed to playing all 2011-2012 as scheduled either as a trio, or with a replacement guitarist. On 26 January 2012, Volbeat joined [[Megadeth]], [[Motörhead]], and [[Lacuna Coil]] on [[Gigantour]]. The tour started in Camden, New Jersey, and culminated on 28 February 2012 in Denver, Colorado, at The Fillmore Auditorium. On the tour, Hank Shermann ([[Mercyful Fate]]) filled in as the guitarist for Volbeat. On their North American Summer tour, which began in June 2012, [[Iced Earth]] and [[Hellyeah]] were the supporting acts. The tour included dates in Baltimore, Toronto, Detroit, Billings, Boston, Tempe, San Francisco, and Weyburn and Lazerfest in Boone, Iowa, and in June, Orion Music Festival, headlined by Metallica, at Bader Field in Atlantic City, New Jersey.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://lineup.orionmusicandmore.com/events/2012/06/23/ |title=Orion Music + More 2013 |publisher=Lineup.orionmusicandmore.com |access-date=20 September 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130225102702/http://lineup.orionmusicandmore.com/events/2012/06/23/ |archive-date=25 February 2013 |url-status=dead  }}</ref>

[[File:Volbeat 2013.jpg|thumb|left|Volbeat performing in 2013]]
Volbeat were confirmed to be playing Download festival June 2013. Volbeat announced in December that their new album would be released during 2013. A spring tour with [[Danko Jones]] and [[Spoken (band)|Spoken]] was announced in December. In January 2013, Volbeat revealed the name of the new album, ''[[Outlaw Gentlemen & Shady Ladies]]'', and that was released in the spring of 2013.<ref name="allmusic1"/>

On 4 February 2013, it was announced that Rob Caggiano, formerly of [[Anthrax (American band)|Anthrax]], had officially joined the band as second guitarist. He had been producing and recording guest solos on the band's upcoming album, ''Outlaw Gentlemen and Shady Ladies'', which the band also announced will be released on 8 April 2013 (GSA on 5 April and US on 9 April), before being asked to join as a full-time member. Singer and guitarist [[Michael Poulsen]] said of the chemistry between Caggiano and the rest of the band "The collaboration with Rob in the studio was so inspiring and in good spirit that we the decided to keep him. Basically we went into the studio as a three piece and came out as a whole band!"<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.volbeat.dk/2/news.php?id=98&lang=en |title=Under Construction |publisher=Volbeat.dk |access-date=20 September 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130208043040/http://www.volbeat.dk/2/news.php?id=98&lang=en |archive-date=8 February 2013 }}</ref>

Michael Poulsen confirmed during the band's 2015 tour with [[Anthrax (American band)|Anthrax]] and [[Crobot]], that a new album was in the works, hinting that it would be released in 2016, and demoed a new song throughout "The Devil's Bleeding Crown" tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/volbeat-performs-new-song-at-san-antonios-river-city-rockfest-video/ |title=Video: Volbeat Performs New Song |publisher=blabbermouth.net |date=25 May 2015 |access-date=22 July 2015}}</ref>

In April 2015, singer Poulsen revealed that he had so far written seven songs for the next album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bt.dk/musik/volbeat-interview-folk-tror-jeg-er-den-falske-michael-poulsen|title=Volbeat-interview: Folk tror, jeg er den falske Michael Poulsen|access-date=3 June 2016}}</ref> In November 2015, the band announced that Kjølholm would leave Volbeat. They emphasised, that he left in good terms with the band. Poulsen and Larsen said that "Anders has been a loyal friend and bandmate since 2001. ... We're very thankful for this and wish Anders all the best in the future." Kjølholm likewise wished all the best for the rest of the band.<ref>[http://www.volbeat.dk/3/news/a-message-from-volbeat---44-.html A Message from Volbeat]. Volbeat.dk. Retrieved 14 November 2015</ref><ref>[http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/volbeat-parts-ways-with-bassist-anders-kjolholm/ Volbeat Parts Ways With Bassist Anders Kjølholm]. [[Blabbermouth.net]]. Retrieved 9 December 2015</ref><ref>[http://gaffa.dk/nyhed/101316 Volbeat mister bassist]. ''[[Gaffa (magazine)|GAFFA]]''. Retrieved 9 December 2015</ref>

*Volbeat were nominated for the [[Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance]] for "Room 24".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/awards|title=Awards Nominations & Winners|date=30 April 2017|website=Grammy.com}}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
The band's musical style has been described as [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]],<ref name="lehighvalleylive">{{cite web|first=Dustin|last=Schoof|url=https://www.lehighvalleylive.com/music/index.ssf/2014/04/volbeat_singer_discusses_fan_a.html|title=Volbeat singer discusses fan appreciation, influences and Young the Giant|date=25 April 2014|access-date=3 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Toni|last=Ruberto|url=https://buffalonews.com/2017/03/20/volbeat-headlines-second-edgefest-show-at-artpark/|title=Volbeat headlines second Edgefest show at Artpark|publisher=Buffalo News|date=20 March 2017|access-date=3 July 2018}}</ref> [[hard rock]],<ref name="lehighvalleylive"/> [[psychobilly]],<ref>{{cite web|first=Chad|last=Vergine|url=https://nysmusic.com/2015/11/15/anders-kjolholm-parts-from-volbeat/|title=Anders Kjølholm Parts From Volbeat|publisher=NYS Music|date=15 November 2015|access-date=3 July 2018}}</ref> [[rockabilly]],<ref>{{cite web|first=Polly|last=Glass|url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/tracks-of-the-week-54|title=Tracks Of The Week: music and videos from Black Star Riders, Volbeat and more...|date=7 April 2017|access-date=3 July 2018}}</ref><ref name="loudwire genres">{{cite web|url=https://loudwire.com/volbeat-announce-2012-north-american-tour-dates-with-hellyeah/|title=Volbeat Announce 2012 North American Tour Dates With HellYeah|date=18 April 2012|access-date=25 June 2019}}</ref> [[groove metal]],<ref name="loudwire genres"/> and [[rock and roll]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/cdreviews/the-strength-the-sound-the-songs/|title=The Strength/The Sound/The Songs - Volbeat|date=23 October 2005|newspaper=Blabbermouth.net|language=en-US|access-date=16 September 2019}}</ref> Chris True of [[AllMusic]] stated, "[the band's] sound weds hard rock, classic metal, glam, [[Punk rock|punk]], and rockabilly."<ref name="allmusic1"/> Some of the band's influences include [[Elvis Presley]], [[Johnny Cash]], [[Metallica]], [[AC/DC]], and [[Social Distortion]].<ref name="allmusic1"/>
