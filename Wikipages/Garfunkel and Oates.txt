'''Garfunkel and Oates''' is an American [[comedy folk]] duo consisting of actresses [[Riki Lindhome]], who performs as Garfunkel, and [[Kate Micucci]], who performs as Oates. Their fast-paced songs typically combine raunchy [[observational comedy]] with sweet-sounding melodies and vocals.

The duo was formed in 2007 in [[Los Angeles]], after Lindhome and Micucci met at an improv show at the [[Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre]]. They began releasing songs on [[YouTube]] in 2007, several of which became popular on the website, including "Pregnant Women are Smug", "The Loophole", and "This Party Took a Turn for the Douche". Their debut studio album ''All Over Your Face'' was released in 2011, while their second studio album ''Slippery When Moist'' was released in 2012 and topped the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Comedy Albums]] chart. They released their third studio album, ''Secretions'', in 2015.

Garfunkel and Oates starred in [[Garfunkel and Oates (TV series)|an eponymous comedy TV series]], which aired on [[IFC (American TV channel)|IFC]] for one season in 2014. Their 2016 [[Vimeo]] comedy special, ''Garfunkel and Oates: Trying to Be Special'', was nominated for a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Original Music and Lyrics]]. The duo wrote the music for the [[Netflix]] children's TV series ''[[Waffles + Mochi]]'' in 2021, and will executive produce and write the music for the upcoming animated film ''Steps''.

They soon became close friends and started writing comedy songs together.<ref name="amsongint">{{cite web |last1=Uitti |first1=Jacob |title=Riki Lindhome and Kate Micucci Receive Approval From Michelle Obama to Write Kids Music for 'Waffles + Mochi' |url=https://americansongwriter.com/garfunkel-oates-receives-approval-from-michelle-obama-to-write-kids-music-for-waffles-mochi/ |website=[[American Songwriter]] |access-date=November 13, 2021 |language=en |date=April 14, 2021}}</ref><ref name="wsjshow">{{cite web |last1=Jurgensen |first1=John |title='Garfunkel and Oates' — Art Mimics Life in New IFC Series |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/BL-SEB-82892 |website=[[Wall Street Journal]] |access-date=November 18, 2021 |language=en |date=August 14, 2014}}</ref>

In 2009, Rebecca Dana of ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'' wrote that Garfunkel and Oates were "fast becoming the darlings of the Los Angeles comedy scene...with their pretty-girl, dirty-mouth act."<ref name="wsjint2009" /> Chrissie Dickinson of the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' called their music "funny and sharply pointed", adding that it "can also be disarmingly touching".<ref name="chictrib" /> [[NPR]]'s David Greene called the duo "experts at wrapping ugly, hilarious truths into sweet melodies".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Greene |first1=David |title=Working And Dating In LA, Living To Sing The Tale |url=https://www.npr.org/2014/08/07/338305684/working-and-dating-in-l-a-living-to-sing-the-tale |website=[[NPR]] |access-date=November 18, 2021 |language=en |date=August 7, 2014}}</ref> ''[[The Daily Dot]]''{{'}}s Audra Schroeder called them "a Swiss Army knife, at a time when artists often have to be many things to stay afloat."<ref name="ddotspesh" /> Neal Justin of the ''[[Star Tribune]]'' compared the tone of their songs to ''[[Sesame Street]]''.<ref name="startrib" /> For ''[[New York (magazine)#Vulture|Vulture]]'', Joe Berkowitz wrote that their songs "are filled with sharp observations and hilarious stories that are distinctly feminine and quirky", adding, "The blunt, detail-packed way they rebuke potential suitors or reveal embarrassing personal insights seems brutally honest."<ref name="aoyfvult" />

Clara Olshansky and Milan Polk of ''[[Men's Health]]'' named Garfunkel and Oates's song "Present Face" one of the funniest Christmas songs of all time.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Olshansky |first1=Clara |last2=Polk |first2=Milan |title=The Funniest Christmas Songs of All Time |url=https://www.menshealth.com/entertainment/g34820058/funny-christmas-songs/ |website=[[Men's Health]] |access-date=November 19, 2021 |language=en |date=October 26, 2021}}</ref> ''[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]''{{'}}s Hudson Hongo named Micucci and Lindhome two of the best comedians of 2014 for their work as Garfunkel and Oates, while the two were included on ''[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]]''{{'}}s list of "funny women to watch" in 2014.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hongo |first1=Hudson |title=The 20 Best Comedians of 2014 |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/comedy/the-20-best-comedians-of-2014/#11-10-kate-micucci-and-riki-lindhome |website=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |access-date=November 19, 2021 |language=en |date=December 4, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Zulkey |first1=Claire |title=13 Funny Women to Watch in 2014 |url=https://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/celebs/videos/a17778/funny-women-to-watch-2014/ |website=[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]] |access-date=November 19, 2021 |language=en |date=January 1, 2014}}</ref>
