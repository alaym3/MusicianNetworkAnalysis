'''NxWorries''' (pronounced as "no worries") is an American music [[Supergroup (music)|superduo]] consisting of recording artist [[Anderson .Paak]] and record producer [[Knxwledge]]. They are signed to [[Stones Throw Records]] and released their debut studio album, ''[[Yes Lawd!]]'', in 2016.

== History ==

===2015–16: Formation and debut album===
[[Knxwledge]] and [[Anderson .Paak]]'s first song under the name NxWorries, "[[Suede (song)|Suede]]" was released on February 10, 2015, via [[Stones Throw Records]]' [[SoundCloud]] page.<ref> name=Stones>{{cite web|title=Anderson.Paak & Knxwledge – NxWorries – "SUEDE"|url=http://www.stonesthrow.com/news/2015/02/anderson-paak-knxwledge-nxworries-suede|website=Stones Throw|accessdate=Jan 22, 2016|date=Feb 10, 2015}}</ref> To produce it, Knxwledge sampled "[[The Bottle]]" by [[Gil Scott-Heron]].<ref name=NPR>{{cite web|last1=Aku|first1=Timmhotep|title=WATCH: NxWorries' Anderson .Paak And Knxwledge Take A Wild Ride In 'Suede'|url=https://www.npr.org/event/music/434612623/nxworries-suede|website=NPR.org|accessdate=Jan 20, 2016|date=Aug 25, 2015}}</ref> The song reached more than a million SoundCloud listens and nearly as many [[YouTube]] plays.<ref name=Billboard>{{cite web|last1=Martins|first1=Chris|title=Anderson Paak on Working With Dr. Dre and New Album 'Malibu'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/magazine-feature/6842874/anderson-paak-on-malibu-album-working-with-dr-dre-life-struggles|website=Billboard.com|accessdate=Jan 15, 2016|date=Jan 14, 2016}}</ref>

In August of 2015, Paak and Knxwledge, billed as NxWorries, joined rappers [[Earl Sweatshirt]] and [[Remy Banks]] on Sweatshirt's ''Ready to Leave Now Tour''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Huynh|first=Davis|date=July 25, 2015|title=Earl Sweatshirt Announces '2015 Ready To Leave Now Tour'|url=https://hypebeast.com/tags/2015-ready-to-leave-now-tour|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-29|website=hypebeast.com}}</ref> On August 25, the music video for "Suede" was released on the official Stones Throw [[YouTube]] channel while another song, "Link Up", was first broadcast on [[Zane Lowe]]'s [[Beats 1]] radio show on December 1, 2015. The duo's debut EP ''[[Link Up & Suede]]'' was then released on December 4 under Stones Throw Records. The music video for "Link Up" was released on the official Stones Throw YouTube channel on March 3, 2016, featuring cameos from comedian [[Eric Andre]] and Earl Sweatshirt.

On June 19, 2016, Paak announced that NxWorries' debut album is finished and ready to be released.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/06/anderson-paak-nxworries-finished/ |title=Anderson .Paak Says 'NxWorries' Album Is Finished - XXL |website=Xxlmag.com |accessdate=2016-07-27}}</ref> The album title ''[[Yes Lawd]]'' was confirmed on July 25, 2016.<ref>{{cite news|last=Hermann |first=Andy |url=http://www.laweekly.com/music/anderson-paaks-long-hard-road-to-fame-was-worth-the-wait-7169706 |title=Anderson .Paak's Long, Hard Road to Fame Was Worth the Wait |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Weekly]] |accessdate=2016-07-27}}</ref> On September 19, 2016, ''Yes Lawd!'' was set for a release date of October 21, 2016, while the lead single, "Lyk Dis", was also released with a music video. The album was surprise released early on October 14.<ref name = "Stones Throw Records">{{cite web|title= NxWorries - Anderson.Paak& Knxwledge - to release YES LAWD! on October 21|url=https://www.stonesthrow.com/news/2016/09/nxworries-yes-lawd|accessdate=Sep 19, 2016|date=Sep 19, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/listen-to-nxworries-debut-album-yes-lawd-a-week-early-news.24738.html? |title=NxWorries release debut album earlier |last=Goddard |first=Kevin |website=HNHH |date=14 October 2016 |access-date=14 October 2016}}</ref>

=== 2017–present: Upcoming second album ===
Besides releasing a ''Yes Lawd! Remixes'' album in 2017, and more music videos until 2018, NxWorries seemed to be on an unannounced hiatus. It was not until March 27, 2020, when Knxwledge released his ''1988'' album containing the track "itkanbe[sonice]" featuring vocals from Anderson .Paak, that the NxWorries name was revived.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2078348/knxwledge-itkanbesonice-feat-anderson-paak/music/|title = Anderson .Paak Reunites with NxWorries Partner Knxwledge on New Song "Itkanbe&#91;Sonice&#93;": Listen|date = 27 March 2020}}</ref> During an interview with [[Zane Lowe]] on his [[Apple Music]] show in October 2020, Paak revealed that he and Knxwledge were currently working on their upcoming second album.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Williams |first1=Aaron |title=Anderson .Paak Says A New NxWorries Album Is In The Works |url=https://uproxx.com/music/anderson-paak-new-nxworries-album-coming-soon/ |website=UPROXX |date=14 October 2020 |access-date=11 March 2021}}</ref> On December 2, 2020, NxWorries debuted a new song called "Where I Go" during their virtual performance on [[88rising]]'s Double Happiness Winter Wonder Festival.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Breihan |first1=Tom |title=Watch NxWorries Debut New Song "Where I Go" At Double Happiness Festival |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2109388/watch-nxworries-debut-new-song-where-i-go-at-double-happiness-festival/news/ |website=Stereogum |date=3 December 2020 |access-date=11 March 2021}}</ref>
