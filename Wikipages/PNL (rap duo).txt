'''PNL''' ({{IPA-fr|pe ɛnɛl}}; acronym for '''P'''eace '''N'''<nowiki/>' '''L'''ovés, {{literally|Peace and Money}}) is a French rap duo consisting of siblings '''Ademo''' (born Tarik Andrieu, {{IPA-fr|taʁik ɑ̃dʁijø|lang}}) and '''N.O.S''' (born Nabil Andrieu, {{IPA-fr|nabil ɑ̃dʁijø|}}),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://next.liberation.fr/musique/2016/09/15/dans-la-legende-la-fascination-pnl_1498473|title="Dans la légende", la fascination PNL|publisher=[[Libération]]|date=September 15, 2016|accessdate=September 24, 2016|language=fr}}</ref> from [[Corbeil-Essonnes]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mxdwn.co.uk/news/french-rap-duo-pnl-rise-rapidly-through-the-itunes-charts/|title=French rap duo PNL rise rapidly through the Itunes charts|website=MXDWN UK|date=November 6, 2015|accessdate=June 18, 2016}}</ref> Formed in 2014, they released their debut studio album, ''[[Le Monde Chico]]'' next year. In 2016, the duo released their second album ''[[Dans la légende]]'', which has peaked at number one on the [[Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique|French Albums chart]] and sold over a million copies worldwide. PNL's third album, ''[[Deux frères (album)|Deux frères]]'' (2019), supported by the number one singles "[[À l'ammoniaque]]", "[[91's]]" and "[[Au DD]]". has also topped the chart in France.

==Early life==
Tarik and Nabil Andrieu grew up in the difficult neighbourhood of Les Tarterêts in [[Corbeil-Essonnes]], in the southern suburbs of [[Paris]]. Tarik was born on December 26, 1986, and Nabil on April 25, 1989. Their father, René Andrieu, a [[Pieds-noirs|Pied-Noir]] native of [[Corsica]], was involved in a [[bank robbery]] and served an eight years sentence at the ''Maison centrale''— the designation of prisons for dangerous and/or long sentence detainees in France— of [[Poissy]]. René is an important figure in his community. The brothers were brought up in the absence of their mother, a native of Algeria (Kabylie).
Ademo (Tarik)  went to jail twice in 2010 and 2013 for drug trafficking.<ref name="gonzai" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rapcity.fr/rappeurs-francais-prison/5/|title=10 rappeurs français qui ont déjà été en prison • Page 5 sur 5 • RapCity|website=www.rapcity.fr|language=fr}}</ref>

Before forming PNL, the two brothers first evolved solo. Tarik released his first mixtape ''Le Son des Halls Vol. 1'' in 2008, followed by the second volume in 2011. Nabil released a mixtape titled ''Calmement (365 Jours Pour Percer)'' in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://intrld.com/ademo-n-o-s-guizmo/|title=Quand Ademo et N.O.S faisaient leur premier pas dans le Rap au côté de Guizmo|work=Interlude|date=July 24, 2017|accessdate=November 9, 2020}}</ref> While comparing their early solo works to PNL, ''Abcdr du Son'' editor-in-chief Mehdi Maizi said: "What impressed me was their metamorphosis. It's as if they had passed through a rap laboratory and came out better".<ref name="thefader" />

==Career==
===2014–18: ''Le Monde Chico'' and ''Dans la légende''===
[[File:PNL live Deux frères tour.jpg|thumb|200px|right|PNL performing during the Dans la légende tour in 2017.]]
The duo released their first song together in early 2014 after Ademo got out of the prison.<ref name="FranceRadio">{{cite web|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/culture/musique/rap/entrez-dans-le-monde-de-pnl-5-choses-a-savoir-sur-le-phenomene-rap_3340801.html|title=Entrez dans le monde de PNL : 5 choses à savoir sur le phénomène rap|work=[[France Info (radio network)|France Info]]|date=September 15, 2016|accessdate=November 13, 2020|language=fr}}</ref> They released a mixtape titled ''[[Que la famille]]'' in March 2015, followed by the debut studio album ''[[Le monde Chico]]'' in October of the same year. The album was certified 2x platinum for selling over 200,000 copies in France.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.melty.fr/pnl-est-disque-d-or-avec-le-monde-chico-a511287.html|title=PNL est disque d'or avec Le Monde Chico !|publisher=Melty|date=March 19, 2016|accessdate=November 13, 2020|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lesechos.fr/tech-medias/medias/0211317249777-pnl-le-phenomene-rap-de-la-rentree-est-disque-dor-en-une-semaine-2029788.php |title=PNL, le phénomène rap de la rentrée est disque d'or en une semaine |publisher=[[Les Échos (France)|Les Échos]] |date=September 23, 2016|accessdate=November 13, 2020|language=fr}}</ref> One of the singles from the album, "Le monde ou rien", went viral becoming a political slogan for the [[Nuit debout]] social movement against proposed labor reforms known as the [[El Khomri law]].<ref name="thefader">{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/06/14/pnl-cover-story-french-rap|title=PNL's World Or Nothing|work=[[The Fader]]|date=June 14, 2016|accessdate=November 12, 2020}}</ref>

Their second album ''[[Dans la légende]]'' released on September 16, 2016 was certified diamond for selling over 1,000,000 copies in total in more than 5 countries around Europe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.melty.fr/amp/pnl-entre-dans-la-legende-en-battant-ce-nouveau-record-a579787.html|title=PNL entre Dans La Légende en battant ce nouveau record|publisher=Melty|date=December 13, 2016|accessdate=February 20, 2017|language=fr}}</ref> To promote the album, the duo went on a six-city tour in France through 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.huffingtonpost.fr/2017/02/14/lidee-de-genie-de-pnl-pour-annoncer-ses-dates-de-concert-a-pari_a_21713740/|title=L'idée de génie de PNL pour annoncer ses dates de concert à Paris, Lille, Toulouse, Marseille, Nantes et Lyon|work=HuffPost|date=February 14, 2019|accessdate=November 22, 2020}}</ref>

PNL were set to perform at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]], one of the biggest [[music festival]]s in the [[United States]] during its 2017 edition. However, due to Ademo's criminal record, he was denied entry into the country.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-pnl-coachella-visa-20170421-story.html|title=French rap duo PNL won't perform at Coachella despite last-ditch efforts to fix visa issue|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=April 21, 2017|accessdate=November 13, 2020}}</ref> Through 2018, PNL released the singles, "[[À l'ammoniaque]]" and "[[91's]]", both have reached number one on the [[Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique|French singles chart]].<ref name=fra />

===2019–present: ''Deux frères''===
On April 5, 2019, PNL released their third album, ''[[Deux frères (album)|Deux frères]]'', which sold more than 100,000 copies in its first week of release, a record for French rap.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mouv.fr/rap-fr/pnl-les-chiffres-stratospheriques-de-la-premiere-semaine-de-deux-freres-349420|title=PNL : les chiffres stratosphériques de la première semaine de "Deux Frères"|work=Mouv'|date=April 12, 2019|accessdate=November 13, 2020|language=fr}}</ref> The album's third single, "[[Au DD]]", has topped the French singles chart.<ref name=fra>{{cite web|url=http://lescharts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=PNL |title=lescharts.com - Discographie PNL|publisher=Hung Medien|accessdate=November 17, 2020}}</ref> They shot the music video for "Au DD" on the [[Eiffel Tower]], becoming the first duo or band to do so.<ref>{{cite web |title=PNL: Deux Frères review – France's rap kings scale brooding new heights|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/apr/05/pnl-deux-freres-review|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=April 5, 2019|accessdate=November 13, 2020}}</ref> The album was nominated for [[Independent Music Companies Association|IMPALA]]'s European Independent Album of the Year Award.<ref name="IMPALA" /> In June 2019, the duo presented their QLF collection of sneakers, tracksuits, jeans and T-shirts, followed by their appearance in the [[Off-White]] fashion show in Paris.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mouv.fr/sneakers-style/pnl-la-nouvelle-collection-qlf-est-dispo-361234|title=PNL : la nouvelle collection QLF est dispo|work=Mouv'|date=June 12, 2019|accessdate=November 13, 2020|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/vogueworld/article/french-rap-group-pnl-ademo-and-nos-at-off-white|title=This French Rap Group Stole the Show at Off-White|work=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]|date=June 20, 2019|accessdate=November 13, 2020}}</ref> In July, it was announced that [[Apple Music]] has signed PNL and will co-brand their music videos and promote special joint events.<ref name="RollingStone"/> In the same month, PNL released the song "Tahia" in honor of Algeria after the country's victory in the [[2019 Africa Cup of Nations]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.fr/entry/pnl-algerie-tahia-can-2019_fr_5d3245f3e4b0419fd32d13eb|title=PNL sort "Tahia" pour le sacre de l'Algérie à la CAN|work=[[HuffPost]]|date=July 20, 2019|accessdate=November 14, 2020|language=fr}}</ref>

In July 2020, PNL released an hour-long tour documentary on [[Netflix]] France from a 2017 concert.<ref name="Netflix">{{cite web|url=https://www.mouv.fr/musique/rap-fr/pnl-le-concert-de-bercy-2017-est-dispo-sur-netflix-361809|title=PNL x Netflix : le concert de Bercy 2017 est disponible|publisher=Mouv'|date=July 6, 2020|accessdate=November 13, 2020|language=fr}}</ref> In September 2020, Ademo was arrested in Paris on drug use and public disorder charges. The arrest was filmed by several fans and appeared to show him being held on the ground by police officers, and then handcuffed.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.leparisien.fr/faits-divers/paris-le-rappeur-ademo-de-pnl-en-garde-a-vue-pour-usage-de-stupefiants-outrage-et-rebellion-05-09-2020-8379333.php|title=Paris : le rappeur Ademo, de PNL, en garde à vue pour usage de stupéfiants, outrage et rébellion|work=Le Parisien|date=September 6, 2020|accessdate=November 22, 2020|language=fr}}</ref>

PNL's music is labeled as [[cloud rap]] and their style has been described as "raw, hypnotic and moving" by French journalist Olivier Cachin.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://leplus.nouvelobs.com/contribution/1413658-pnl-le-phenomene-rap-cru-hypnotique-et-emouvant-ils-reinventent-leur-art.html|title=PNL, le phénomène rap : cru, hypnotique et émouvant... Ils réinventent leur art|publisher=[[L'Obs]]|date=August 30, 2015|accessdate=November 8, 2020|language=fr}}</ref> According to [[France Info (radio network)|France Info]]'s Laure Narlian "the contrast between the softness of the music and the rawness of the lyrics is what characterizes them best now".<ref name="FranceRadio" /> The main themes of the duo's songs are money, [[Illegal drug trade|drug trafficking]], family and the feeling of being different from other people.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telerama.fr/musique/une-prof-de-francais-examine-les-paroles-de-pnl,139231.php|title=Quand une prof de français examine les paroles de PNL|publisher=[[Télérama]]|date=March 30, 2016|accessdate=November 8, 2020|language=fr}}</ref> [[Apple Music 1]]’s [[Zane Lowe]] has described the duo as melding modern U.S. hip-hop production sonics with “that struggling sound of a community [which is] burdened with unemployment, austerity measures, drugs, gangs, racism, and a whole host of other social issues”.<ref name="RollingStone">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/apple-music-artist-independence-french-rap-duo-855694/|title=How Apple Music is Striking a Blow for Artist Independence – by Betting Big on This French Rap Duo|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=July 4, 2019|accessdate=November 17, 2020}}</ref> Writing for [[openDemocracy]], Adem Ferizaj said: "On the one hand, PNL stands out sound-aesthetically from other rap artists through their choice of atmospheric and often sentimental instrumentals. On the other hand, they are innovative, in terms of flows signifying the rap technique in hip hop culture, in their use of technical voice tools – especially the [[vocoder]]. Through these means, they are constantly crossing boundaries between music genres".<ref name="opendemocracy" /> ''[[Juice (magazine)|Juice]]'' magazine's editor Joram Vuille wrote; "[PNL is] the first French rap group to really master [[Autotune]] and finally use it like an instrument, adapting their flows and lyrics to beats borrowed from the internet. PNL gave their French compatriots what they could only get from the Americans. They made you forget the lyrics and brought a certain atmosphere to the fore; an atmosphere that can also be found in their videos. It seemed as if for the first time a French group had managed to completely translate the new codes from across the [[Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic]] into French. And French rap finally proved that it could be sad, melancholy, depressed without using the otherwise inevitable and the utmost dead piano [[Sample (music)|samples]]."<ref>{{cite web|title=HipHope: PNL – Alles oder Nichts|url=https://juice.de/hiphope-pnl-alles-oder-nichts/#|work=[[Juice (magazine)|Juice]]|date=March 24, 2016|access-date=July 13, 2021|language=de}}</ref>

As practising Muslims, [[Islam]] is one of their major artistic influences. In the song "Chang" from their third album, theŕe's a line by Ademo rapping “I'm not leading a life according to [[Din (Arabic)|Din]] but I'm always trying to become a better person”.<ref name="opendemocracy">{{cite web|url=https://www.opendemocracy.net/en/can-europe-make-it/pnl-and-european-others/|title=PNL and 'European Others'|work=[[openDemocracy]]|date=May 18, 2019|accessdate=November 9, 2020}}</ref> PNL often make references to cartoons (''[[The Lion King]]'', ''[[The Jungle Book (1967 film)|The Jungle Book]]''), films (''[[Scarface (1983 film)|Scarface]]'') and video games; naming one of their singles "Onizuka" after the Japanese [[manga]] ''[[Great Teacher Onizuka]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.underlined.fr/2016/09/pnl-famille-monde-legende/|title=PNL : la famille, le monde, la Légende|work=Underlined|date=September 17, 2016|accessdate=November 8, 2020|language=fr}}</ref>
