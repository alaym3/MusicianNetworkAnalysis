'''Beach Bunny''' is an American rock band formed in 2015 in [[Chicago|Chicago, Illinois]]. The group released its debut studio album, ''[[Honeymoon (Beach Bunny album)|Honeymoon]]'', in February 2020 on [[Mom + Pop Music]].

==History==
Beach Bunny began as a bedroom-based solo project in 2015 when Lili Trifilio recorded a song titled "6 Weeks". The same year, Trifilio released her first EP, titled ''Animalism''.<ref name="chicagotribune">{{cite web |last1=Stewart |first1=Allison |title=Chicago's Beach Bunny is about to face being grown up and becoming the next big thing at the same time chile as she should|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-ott-beach-bunny-0104-story.html |work=[[Chicago Tribune]] |accessdate=31 January 2019}}</ref><ref name="chicagoreader">{{cite web |last1=De La Cerda |first1=Marissa |title=Beach Bunny's indie pop goes from dorms to DIY shows to Thalia Hall |url=https://www.chicagoreader.com/Bleader/archives/2018/10/31/beach-bunnys-indie-pop-goes-from-dorms-to-diy-shows-to-thalia-hall |publisher=[[Chicago Reader]] |accessdate=31 January 2019}}</ref> She released her second EP titled ''Pool Party'' in 2016.<ref name="hooliganmagazine1">{{cite web |last1=Gates |first1=Meggie |title=Beach Bunny and the Passage of Time |url=http://www.hooliganmagazine.com/blog/2018/8/30/beach-bunny-and-the-passage-of-time |work=Hooligan Magazine |accessdate=1 February 2019}}</ref> In 2017, she released her third EP titled ''Crybaby'', and Beach Bunny expanded to a full four-piece lineup shortly after.<ref name="chicagoreader1">{{cite web |last1=Galil |first1=Leor |title=Chicago power-pop band Beach Bunny show why they're one of the best in town with Prom Queen |url=https://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/chicago-power-pop-band-beach-bunny-show-why-theyre-one-of-the-best-in-town-with-prom-queen/Content?oid=65987181 |accessdate=1 February 2019}}</ref> In 2018, Beach Bunny released their fourth EP, titled ''Prom Queen''.<ref name="chicagoreader1"/><ref name="pastemagazine">{{cite web |last1=Kamp |first1=Justin |title=Daily Dose: Beach Bunny, "Prom Queen" |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2019/01/daily-dose-beach-bunny-prom-queen.html |work=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |accessdate=1 February 2019}}</ref><ref name="fourteeneastmagazine">{{cite web |last1=Happold |first1=Madeline |title=Beach Bunny Leans Into Growing Pains With 'Prom Queen' EP |url=http://fourteeneastmag.com/index.php/2018/08/10/beach-bunny-leans-into-growing-pains-with-prom-queen-ep/ |publisher=Fourteen East Magazine}}</ref><ref name="hooliganmagazine">{{cite web |last1=Corrall |first1=Cody |title=PREMIERE: Beach Bunny's 'painkiller' Lets You Dance Away the Heartbreak |url=http://www.hooliganmagazine.com/music/2018/7/13/premiere-beach-bunnys-painkiller-lets-you-dance-away-the-heartbreak |work=Hooligan Magazine |accessdate=1 February 2019}}</ref>

On October 31, 2019, the band announced that they had signed to [[Mom + Pop Music]] and released their first full-length album, ''[[Honeymoon (Beach Bunny album)|Honeymoon]]'', on February 14, 2020.<ref name="consequenceofsound">{{cite web |last1=Kaye |first1=Ben |title=Beach Bunny announce debut LP Honeymoon, share Origins of "Dream Boy": Stream |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2019/10/beach-bunny-dream-boy-origins-honeymoon-stream/ |publisher=[[Consequence of Sound]] |accessdate=3 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="pastemagazine1">{{cite web |last1=Vasandani |first1=Rachita |title=Beach Bunny Announce Debut Album Honeymoon, Share "Dream Boy" |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2019/10/beach-bunny-announce-debut-album-honeymoon.html |work=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |accessdate=3 December 2019}}</ref> The album was produced by Joe Reinhart  at [[Electrical Audio]]; the Chicago studio owned by [[Steve Albini]], and subsequently mixed at Reinhart's [[Headroom Studios]]. ''Honeymoon'' was met with widespread critical acclaim, appearing on the Best Albums of 2020 lists in both ''[[The New York Times]]'', and ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref>https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/best-albums-2020-1096814/</ref> In November 2020, the band released a new single titled "Good Girls (Don't Get Used)". Their fifth EP, ''[[Blame Game (EP)|Blame Game]]'', was released in 2021.<ref name="avclub1">{{cite web|last1 = Colburn|first1 = Randall|title=Beach Bunny decries "dumb boy talk" in the video for "Good Girls (Don't Get Used)"|url=https://news.avclub.com/beach-bunny-decries-dumb-boy-talk-in-the-video-for-g-1845776687|work=[[The A.V. Club]]|accessdate=30 November 2020}}</ref>

==Band members==
'''Current'''
*Lili Trifilio – vocals, guitar (2015–present)
*Jon Alvarado – drums (2016–present)
*Matt Henkels – guitar (2016–present)
*Anthony Vaccaro – bass (2019–present)

'''Former'''
*Aiden Cada – bass (2016–2019)
