'''Chukwuka Ekweani''',<ref name="OCC">{{cite web |last1=Ainsley |first1=Helen |title=Who is CKay? The Emo-Afrobeat artist flying up the charts with Love Nwantiti |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/who-is-ckay-the-emo-afrobeat-artist-flying-up-the-charts-with-love-nwantiti__34100/ |website=Official Charts Company |access-date=1 October 2021}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Ckay''' ({{respell|SEE|kay}}), is a Nigerian singer-songwriter and record producer signed to Warner Music South Africa. He was formerly signed to [[Chocolate City (record label)|Chocolate City]], a member of [[Warner Music Group]] independent label services [[Alternative Distribution Alliance|ADA]]. His international hit single "[[Love Nwantiti]]", also known as "Love Nwantiti (Ah Ah Ah)", was released in 2019 by Chocolate City.

==Early life==
Chukwuka Ekweani was born in [[Kaduna]], but of Igbo origin particularly [[Anambra state]]. His earliest contact with music was at age 7, where he was introduced to the thing he would come to know as his life. 'Music'. He completed his secondary school education at Zamani College, Kaduna before proceeding for his undergraduate degree at the University of Lagos.

==Career==
[[File:CKay Yo.jpg|thumb|CKay in 2021]]
In 2014, he relocated to Lagos, after he got discovered. While signed to Loopy Music, he began working with [[Chocolate City (music label)|Chocolate City]], early 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Olorunsola|first=Michael|date=2017-06-09|title=Chocolate City's CKay discusses His New Single, Coming Projects & More on Exclusive Interview with BN Music|url=https://www.bellanaija.com/2017/06/ckay-exclusive-interview-bn-music/|access-date=2020-08-12|website=BellaNaija|language=en-US}}</ref> Following the merger of Loopy Music in 2016, he officially joined Chocolate City artist roster.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ckay Signs To Chocolate City Music|url=https://www.thenigerianvoice.com/movie/227693/ckay-signs-to-chocolate-city-music.html|access-date=2020-08-12|website=Nigerian Voice}}</ref> He is a musician who has made tracks in a variety of genres including [[Afrobeats]], [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[dancehall]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-06-04|title=I'm the future of Afrobeat music – CKay|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2019/06/im-the-future-of-afrobeat-music-ckay/|access-date=2020-08-12|website=Vanguard News}}</ref> On 11 September 2017, he released his first extended play ''Who the Fuck Is CKay?'',<ref>{{Cite web|last=Idowu|first=Tomi|date=2017-09-08|title=Ckay Leverages The Publicity With Release of 'Who The Fuck Is Ckay EP'|url=https://culturecustodian.com/who-the-fuck-is-ckay/|access-date=2020-08-12|website=The Culture Custodian (Est. 2014)}}</ref> through Chocolate City. On 30 August 2019, he released his second extended play ''[[CKay the First]]'' through [[Chocolate City (music label)|Chocolate City]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=BellaNaija.com|date=2019-09-06|title=Ckay Releases new EP "Ckay the First" {{!}} Stream on BN|url=https://www.bellanaija.com/2019/09/ckay-releases-new-ep-ckay-the-first-stream-on-bn/|access-date=2020-08-12|website=BellaNaija|language=en-US}}</ref> and on 11 February 2021, he released his third extended play ''Boyfriend'' through Warner Music South Africa.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ckay releases new EP, 'Boyfriend' |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/new-ep-ckay-boyfriend-ep/9nqnspk |website=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=1 October 2021}}</ref> On 4 May 2021, he confirmed on Twitter that he is no longer signed to Chocolate City, but signed to Warner Music South Africa.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Daramola |first1=Kunle |title=Ckay: I'm signed to WMG South Africa NOT Chocolate City |url=https://lifestyle.thecable.ng/ckay-im-signed-to-wmg-south-africa-not-chocolate-city/ |website=TheCable |access-date=1 October 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=CKay |url=https://www.warnermusic.co.za/artist/ckay/ |website=Warner Music South Africa |access-date=1 October 2021}}</ref>

On 2 March 2018 he released "Container", an Afrobeat record influenced by the South African gwara gwara dance style. The song became a breakthrough record, and earned him substantial airplay in Nigeria. It was released through Chocolate City, and was produced by Tempoe.<ref>{{cite web |title=New Music: CKay – Container |url=https://www.bellanaija.com/2018/03/new-music-ckay-container/ |website=BellaNaija |access-date=2 October 2021 |date=2 March 2018}}</ref> On 6 July 2018, he released the music video, directed and shot by the Myth in various locations in Lagos.<ref>{{cite web |title=New Video: CKay – Container |url=https://www.bellanaija.com/2018/07/new-video-ckay-container/ |website=BellaNaija |access-date=2 October 2021 |date=9 July 2018}}</ref>

In 2019, following the release of ''[[CKay the First]]'', "[[Love Nwantiti]]" became a major record in Nigeria. On 14 February 2020, CKay released a remix titled "Love Nwantiti (Ah Ah Ah)" featuring [[Joeboy]] and [[Kuami Eugene]]. In 2021, it went viral on [[TikTok]]<ref>{{cite web |last1=C.J |first1=Nelson |title=CKay's 'Love Nwantinti' Took Over TikTok -- But He's Only Now Getting Full Credit |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/ckay-love-nwantinti-tik-tok-1231221/ |website=Rolling Stone |access-date=2 October 2021 |date=24 September 2021}}</ref> and became an international hit, charting across Europe, Africa, Australia and New Zealand, also reaching number 23 in the UK<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-09-21|title=Ckay's 'Love Nwantiti' charts at No. 23 on the official UK top 40|url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/ckays-love-nwantiti-charts-at-no-23-on-the-official-uk-top-40/he1b2h0|access-date=2021-10-02|website=Pulse Nigeria|language=en}}</ref> and earned him his first entry on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. A North African remix featuring [[ElGrande Toto]] became popular in the Maghreb countries and in Germany. A French remix featured [[Franglish]], a German remix featuring Frizzo, Joeboy and Kuami Eugene, the Spanish version with De La Ghetto, the East African remix featuring Rayvanny and a South African remix featuring Tshego and Gemini Major.

He was featured on the song "La La" from [[Davido]]'s studio album ''[[A Better Time]]'', released on 13 November 2020, through [[Davido Music Worldwide]], [[RCA Records]], and [[Sony Music]]. and "Beggie Beggie" from [[Ayra Starr]]'s debut studio album ''[[19 & Dangerous]]'', released on 6 August 2021, through [[Mavin Records]]. On 28 July 2021, he received his first YouTube plaque for reaching 100k subscribers.<ref>{{cite web |title=CKay Receives Youtube Plaque for Crossing New Milestone |url=https://notjustok.com/news/ckay-youtube-plaque-for-milestone/ |website=NotjustOk |access-date=1 October 2021 |date=28 July 2021}}</ref> On 8 October 2021, Ckay became first African artist to hit 20 Million Spotify Listeners, with over 21 million monthly listeners on his Spotify page.<ref>{{cite web |title=CKay Makes History As First African Artist to Hit 20 Million Spotify Listeners |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/ckay-20-million-spotify-history/ |website=OkayAfrica |access-date=11 October 2021 |language=en |date=8 October 2021}}</ref>

In 2021 his first single [[Love Nwantiti]] (2019) was playlisted on [[TikTok|Tiktok]] for having 15 billons views. The single was remix by many [[Disc jockey|Disk Jockey]], Singers and upcoming acts

==Artistry==
On 4 June 2019, in an interview with Ayo Onikoyi of [[Vanguard (Nigeria)|Vanguard Nigeria]], CKay described his style of music as Afro-pop from the future (2056 A.D specifically).<ref>{{cite web |title=I'm the future of Afrobeat music – CKay |url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2019/06/im-the-future-of-afrobeat-music-ckay/ |website=Vanguard News |access-date=2 October 2021 |date=4 June 2019}}</ref> On 24 September 2021, Helen Ainsley of the [[Official Charts Company]] describes CKay's music as fusing Afrobeats with pop, electronic music, and even interpolating classical music.<ref name="OCC" />
