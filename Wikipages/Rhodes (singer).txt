'''David Rhodes''', stagename '''Rhodes''' (stylised '''RHODES''') (born 1988), is an English singer and songwriter from [[Baldock]], [[Hertfordshire]], England.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/mar/14/rhodes|title=New band of the day RHODES (No 1,720)|first=Paul|last=Lester|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=14 March 2014|accessdate=8 July 2014}}</ref> He released his debut EP ''Raise Your Love'' on Hometown Records in October 2013 and released his second EP ''Morning'' on Rhodes Music on 12 May 2014. His debut album ''[[Wishes (Rhodes album)|Wishes]]'' was released on 18 September 2015.

==Music career==
===2013: Early career and ''Raise Your Love'' EP===
Rhodes's music began to spread early 2013 when his demo track "Always" was playlisted on [[Amazing Radio]] and then picked up plays from [[BBC Radio 1]].<ref name="Hometown Records">{{cite web|url=http://www.hometown-records.co.uk/news/view/4/introducing-rhodes|title=Introducing RHODES|publisher=Hometown Records|date=14 September 2013|accessdate=8 July 2014}}</ref> Following a period supporting [[Rufus Wainwright]], [[Laura Marling]] and [[Nick Mulvey]] in the UK, his debut EP ''Raise Your Love'' was released on Phil Taggart's label Hometown Records in October 2013.<ref name="Hometown Records"/>

===2014: ''Morning'' EP and live performances===
On 12 March 2014, Rhodes released the song "Your Soul" – which was premiered by Zane Lowe on BBC Radio 1 as his 'Next Hype'<ref name="Jamie Milton">{{cite web|url=http://diymag.com/archive/listen-rhodes-streams-new-track-your-soul/|title=LISTEN: RHODES STREAMS NEW TRACK, 'YOUR SOUL'|first=Jamie|last=Milton|work=DIY|date=12 March 2014|accessdate=8 July 2014}}</ref> – taken from his second EP 'Morning' released on 11 May 2014.
On this EP, Rhodes recorded alongside Ian Grimble and Tim Bran/Roy Kerr, producers who have been previously working with the likes of [[London Grammar]] and [[Daughter (band)|Daughter]].<ref name="Jamie Milton"/>

In March and April 2014, Rhodes completed his first UK headline tour, including 3 sold out gigs at London's Sebright Arms on 8, 9 & 10 April 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughtrade.com/events/2014/5/495|title=RHODES|publisher=Rough Trade|year=2014|accessdate=8 July 2014|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140709022752/http://roughtrade.com/events/2014/5/495|archivedate=9 July 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref> He supported [[London Grammar]] on their UK tour (including one of the O2 Academy Brixton Academy shows<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.o2academybrixton.co.uk/event/63633/london-grammar-tickets|title=O2 Academy Brixton – London Grammar|author=O2 Academy Brixton|work=O2 Academy Brixton|date=12 May 2014|accessdate=8 July 2014}}</ref>) as well as [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]] in the UK (at the Roundhouse<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roundhouse.org.uk/whats-on/2014/sam-smith/|title=Sam Smith – Roundhouse|author=Roundhouse|work=Roundhouse|year=2014|accessdate=8 July 2014}}</ref>) and in Europe (Amsterdam and Paris).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/introducing/posts/On-the-playlist-Rhodes-Your-Soul|title=On the playlist – Rhodes – Your Soul|first=Zoë|last=Nicholson|publisher=BBC Introducing|date=12 May 2014|accessdate=8 July 2014}}</ref>
Rhodes also performed at [[BBC Radio 1]] Big Weekend in Glasgow on the BBC Introducing stage on 25 May<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/ep3g9r/acts/abpbj5#p01zsjvc|title=RHODES – Supported by BBC Introducing in Beds, Herts and Bucks|author=BBC Radio 1|author-link=BBC Radio 1|work=[[BBC Radio 1]]|date=25 May 2014|accessdate=8 July 2014}}</ref> and at the [[Glastonbury Festival]] on 29 June on the Rabbit Hole stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/line-up/line-up-2014/|title=Glastonbury Festival – Line-up|publisher=Glastonbury Festival|year=2014|accessdate=8 July 2014}}</ref>

=== 2015– present: ''Wishes'' ===
On 26 June 2015, he played at the Park Stage at [[Glastonbury Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/line-up/line-up-2015/|title=Glastonbury Festival – 2015|last=Digital|first=Pretty Good|website=Glastonbury Festival – 21–25 June 2017|access-date=26 June 2017}}</ref> In July, the first single to be taken from his album ''[[Wishes (Rhodes album)|Wishes]]'' , "Close your Eyes", charted at 85 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/31329/rhodes/|title=rhodes {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en|access-date=26 June 2017}}</ref> A week before the release of his debut album, Rhodes released a duet with [[Birdy (singer)|Birdy]], "[[Let It All Go (Rhodes & Birdy song)|Let It All Go]]". It charted in many countries and is his most successful single to date.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/48385/rhodes-and-birdy/|title=rhodes-and-birdy {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en|access-date=26 June 2017}}</ref> He then released ''Wishes'', which peaked at 24 on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref name=":0" />

Rhodes featured on [[Kygo]]'s debut studio album ''[[Cloud Nine (Kygo album)|Cloud Nine]]'', providing the vocals for the track "Not Alone".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://play.spotify.com/track/5nQojVsRxImTExfSXQ9qU2?play=true|title=Spotify|website=play.spotify.com|access-date=19 July 2017}}</ref>

On 9 June 2017, he released the single, "Sleep Is a Rose",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/sleep-is-a-rose-single/id1241424850|title=Sleep Is a Rose – Single by RHODES on Apple Music|publisher=iTunes Store|access-date=23 June 2017}}</ref> and the music video for the song, which was recorded live, was released the same day on the Rhodes [[Vevo]] channel on YouTube.<ref>{{Citation|last=RhodesVEVO|title=RHODES – Sleep Is a Rose|date=9 June 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xovjLzqQY3A|accessdate=23 June 2017}}</ref> The song came to him one sleepless night in the [[Lake District]]. He was lying awake in a small cottage when he came up with the song.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/listen-rhodes-sleep-is-a-rose|title=Listen: Rhodes – 'Sleep Is A Rose'|work=Clash Magazine|access-date=17 August 2017|language=en}}</ref> On 23 March he released a collaboration with German DJ [[Alle Farben]] of a cover of American [[country music]] duo [[Florida Georgia Line]]'s song "[[H.O.L.Y.]]".<ref>{{Citation|title=H.O.L.Y. - Single by Alle Farben & RHODES|date=2018-03-23|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/h-o-l-y-single/1360376513|language=en-US|accessdate=2018-04-24}}</ref>
