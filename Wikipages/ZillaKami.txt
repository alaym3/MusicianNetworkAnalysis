'''Junius Rogers''', professionally known as '''ZillaKami''', is an American rapper and songwriter. He is a member of the [[hip hop]] group [[City Morgue]] and a former songwriter for [[6ix9ine]].<ref name="Darkweb Insider">{{cite web |last1=King |first1=Seth |title=Why you love Zillakami, not 6IX9INE |url=https://darkwebinsider.com/lifestyle-culture/2018/9/3/34wqj9axyhwjgupao0ai87uqfwvbvg |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref>

He has been noted by publications such as ''HotNewHiphop'' as a pioneer of [[trap metal]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-09-17|title=ZillaKami Delivers Experimental New Album "Dog Boy"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/zillakami-delivers-experimental-new-album-dog-boy-new-mixtape.121956.html|access-date=2021-11-17|website=HotNewHipHop|language=en}}</ref>

==Biography==
Rogers was born in [[Bay Shore, New York]].<ref name="No Jumper" /> In his teens, Rogers formed a [[punk rock]] band called Scud Got Quayle,<ref>{{cite web |title=CITY MORGUE rapper [[6ix9ine]].<ref name="Darkweb Insider" /> During this period, he wrote a song which he intended to feature two vocalists, to which 6ix9ine responded by telling him that he should feature on the track, which would eventually be released under the name "Yokai", and the pair followed up with by releasing another collaborative track called "Hellsing Station".<ref name="No Jumper">{{cite |last1=– |title=HERE'S A RUNDOWN OF EVERY PUBLIC BEEF INVOLVING 6IX9INE |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/08/rundown-public-beef-6ix9ine/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=14 December On 30 April 2017, he released his debut EP "LifeIsAHorrorMovie", which he later took down due to him no longer feeling a connection with the music that is contained.<ref>{{cite web |title=ZillaKami's New Twisted Music Video Will Give You Nightmares ( Warning: Disturbing) |url=https://www.tophotmuzik.com/news/zillakamis-new-twisted-music-video-will-give-you-nightmares-warning-disturbing/ |access-date=13 December 2018}}</ref>

Soon after, ZillaKami got in contact with the son of the owner of the tattoo parlour that his older brother Righteous P worked at, who went by the stage name SosMula. A few days after his release from prison on drug charges, SosMula formed [[City Morgue]] with ZillaKami.<ref name="No Jumper" /> In August 2018, after numerous singles scattered throughout the couple years before, they released their debut EP as a group, titled "Be Patient".<ref>{{cite web |last1=DREW |first1=SLIM |title=ZILLAKAMI & SOSMULA AKA CITY MORGUE SHARES 'BE PATIENT' EP AHEAD OF LONG AWAITED DEBUT |url=http://therapfest.com/zillakami-sosmula-aka-city-morgue-shares-patient-ep-ahead-long-awaited-debut/ |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Sternberg |first1=Bret |title=CITY MORGUE RELEASES NEW EP, "BE PATIENT" |url=http://dailychiefers.com/city-morgue-releases-new-ep-be-patient/ |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref>

On 5 September 2018, he featured on the track "Vengeance" from [[Denzel Curry]]'s third album [[Ta13oo]], which also featured [[JPEGMafia]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=KAYE |first1=BEN |title=Denzel Curry seeks "Vengeance" in graphic new music video: Watch |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/09/denzel-curry-vengeance-video/ |website=[[Consequence of Sound]] |access-date=15 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Top 100 Songs of 2018 |url=https://www.treblezine.com/top-100-songs-of-2018/ |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Madden |first1=Michael |title=Denzel Curry reshapes the SoundCloud-rap phenomenon on one of the year's most ambitious albums |url=http://www.citypages.com/music/denzel-curry-reshapes-the-soundcloud-rap-phenomenon-on-one-of-the-years-most-ambitious-albums/489902311 |website=[[City Pages]] |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref> which was followed, in the same month, by a feature on Lil Gnar's track "Man Down".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Zidel |first1=Alex |title=Lil Gnar Proves His Versatility On "Gnar Lif3" With Lil Skies, IDK, & More |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-gnar-proves-his-versatility-on-gnar-lif3-with-lil-skies-idk-and-more-new-mixtape.118454.html |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref>

On 12 October 2018, [[City Morgue]] released their debut album "''City Morgue Vol 1: Hell or High Water''",<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fantano |first1=Anthony |title=City Morgue - Vol. 1: Hell or High Water |url=http://www.theneedledrop.com/articles/2018/10/city-morgue-vol-1-hell-or-high-water |website=[[Anthony Fantano|The Needle Drop]] |access-date=15 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=EPSTEIN |first1=DAN |last2=BENNETT |first2=J |last3=NAVIDSON |first3=WILL |last4=CAMP |first4=ZOE |last5=PESSARO |first5=FRED |last6=CHICHESTER |first6=SAMMI |last7=CHAPSTICK |first7=KELSEY |last8=ANGLE |first8=BRAD |last9=GEIST |first9=BRANDON |title=30 BEST ALBUMS OF 2018 |url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/30-best-albums-2018 |website=[[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]] |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref> and soon after, their headline tour in support of it sold out.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://allmetaleverything.com/2018/11/27/city-morgue-punk-is-alive/|title=City Morgue in Brooklyn: Punk is Alive|last=Møtion|first=Noah|date=2018-11-27|website=All Metal Everything|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-05}}</ref>

On 29 November 2018, he featured on Powers Pleasant's track "Please Forgive", along with [[Jay IDK]], [[Zombie Juice]] and [[Denzel Curry]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pierre |first1=Alphonse |title=The Ones: 5 Best New Rap Songs From BandGang, Polo G, Splurge, Cdot Honcho, and Powers Pleasant |url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/the-ones-5-best-new-rap-songs-from-bandgang-polo-g-splurge-cdot-honcho-and-powers-pleasant/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=12 December 2018}}</ref>

From July 24 to August 23, he joined [[Suicideboys]]' ''Grey Day'' tour as a part of City Morgue, along with [[Turnstile (band)|Turnstile]], Denzel Curry, [[Trash Talk (band)|Trash Talk]], [[Pouya (rapper)|Pouya]], Germ, [[Shoreline Mafia]] and [[Night Lovell]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Suarez |first1=Gary |title=GREY DAY: A WHO'S WHO GUIDE TO THE HIP-HOP AND HARDCORE TOUR OF THE SUMMER |url=https://www.kerrang.com/features/grey-day-a-whos-who-guide-to-the-hip-hop-and-hardcore-tour-of-the-summer/ |website=[[Kerrang!]] |access-date=19 February 2020}}</ref>

On 13 December 2019, City Morgue released their second album ''City Morgue Vol 2: As Good As Dead'' The deluxe version of said album, featuring several new tracks, was released on May 15, 2020.

On 31 July 2020, City Morgue released their mixtape "''Toxic Boogaloo''".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fantano |first1=Anthony |title=City Morgue - Toxic Boogaloo |url=https://www.theneedledrop.com/articles/2020/8/city-morgue-toxic-boogaloo |website=[[Anthony Fantano|The Needle Drop]] |access-date=7 September 2020}}</ref>

On 17 September 2021, Rogers released his debut solo album DOG BOY

==Musical style and influence==
ZillaKami's music has been described as merging elements of [[hardcore punk]] and [[heavy metal music|heavy metal]] with [[trap music]],<ref name="Rolling Stone" /> by incorporating electric guitars and guttural shouted vocals and aggressive lyrical themes.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Guzmán |first1=José |title=ZillaKami: A creator of nightmares and responsible for 6ix9ine |url=https://heabbi.com/zillakami-6ix9ine |access-date=13 December 2018}}</ref><ref name="undergroundunderdogs">{{cite web |last1=SHARMA |first1=SAMYAK |title=How NYC's Zillakami is Redefining Rap |url=https://undergroundunderdogs.com/articles/zillakami/ |access-date=13 December 2018}}</ref> His lyrics often depict themes of extreme violence, death, [[Sadomasochism|masochism]] and drug-use.<ref name="undergroundunderdogs" /><ref name="Complex">{{cite web |last1=MOORE |first1=JACOB |title=New York Rapper Zillakami is Taking Hip-Hop to Brutal New Extremes |url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2017/12/zillakami |website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |access-date=15 December 2018}}</ref> In an article by [[Complex (magazine)|Complex magazine]], writer Jacob Moore described his music as "the most merciless rap music since [[Necro (rapper)|Necro]]".<ref name="Complex" />

He cites his biggest influences as [[Title Fight]]'s albums ''[[Floral Green]]'' and ''[[Hyperview (album)|Hyperview]]'', [[DMX (rapper)|DMX]], as well as [[Radiohead]]'s album ''[[In Rainbows]]'' and [[Onyx (hip hop group)|Onyx]].<ref name="No Jumper" />
