'''Cody Robert Simpson'''<ref>{{cite book|last=Simpson|first=Cody R.|title=Prince Neptune: Poetry and Prose|date=7 April 2020|publisher=Andrews McMeel Publishing|publication-date=7 April 2020|isbn=9781524853990}}</ref> (born 11 January 1997)<ref>{{cite news|date=11 January 2020|title=UPI Almanac for Saturday, Jan. 11, 2020|work=[[United Press International]]|url=https://www.upi.com/Top_News/2020/01/11/UPI-Almanac-for-Saturday-Jan-11-2020/6871578622259/|url-status=live|access-date=3 February 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200112071743/https://www.upi.com/Top_News/2020/01/11/UPI-Almanac-for-Saturday-Jan-11-2020/6871578622259/|archive-date=12 January 2020|quote=…musician Cody Simpson in 1997 (age 23)}}</ref> is an Australian singer, songwriter, dancer, actor, model, swimmer and environmentalist.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.atlanticrecords.com/codysimpson |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120722160235/http://www.atlanticrecords.com/codysimpson |url-status=dead |archive-date=22 July 2012 |title=Atlantic Records – Cody Simpson |publisher=atlanticrecords.com |access-date=15 August 2010 }}</ref>

Since his debut, he has released three solo studio albums: ''[[Paradise (Cody Simpson album)|Paradise]]'' (2012), ''[[Surfers Paradise (album)|Surfers Paradise]]'' (2013), and ''[[Free (Cody Simpson album)|Free]]'' (2015).

He portrayed the lead role of Dmitry in the Broadway musical ''[[Anastasia (musical)|Anastasia]]'' from November 2018 through April 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/cody-simpson-extends-run-in-broadways-anastasia|title=Cody Simpson Extends Run in Broadway's Anastasiaia|work=[[Playbill]]|date=29 January 2019}}</ref> The same year he won the first season of ''[[The Masked Singer (Australian season 1)|The Masked Singer Australia]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.smh.com.au/culture/music/cody-simpson-won-the-masked-singer-so-now-what-20191022-p53332.html|title=Cody Simpson won The Masked Singer. So now what?|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=22 October 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Simpson was born on the [[Gold Coast, Queensland|Gold Coast]], Queensland, Australia, to Brad and Angie Simpson. He attended [[All Saints Anglican School]]<ref>{{cite news|first=Shaya|last=Laughlin|title=Former Gold Coaster popstar Cody Simpson wants to be Commonwealth Games ambassador|url=https://www.goldcoastbulletin.com.au/entertainment/former-gold-coaster-popstar-cody-simpson-wants-to-be-commonwealth-games-ambassador/news-story/68996cf34ec6dc4ef6271559330248a6|access-date=6 January 2019|newspaper=Gold Coast Bulletin|date=12 September 2016}}</ref> throughout his upbringing and was a talented swimmer in his younger years. Simpson trained at the [[Miami, Queensland|Miami]] Swimming Club under coach Ken Nixon<ref>{{cite news|title=2009 Qld Championships a Huge Success!|url=http://www.clubsonline.com.au/news/index.cfm?fuseaction=display_main&NewsID=11584&orgid=4033&cfid=42832036&cftoken=86561938&dts=29120105725|access-date=13 March 2014|newspaper=Brisbane Swimmning Association|date=22 January 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110723214610/http://www.clubsonline.com.au/news/index.cfm?fuseaction=display_main&NewsID=11584&orgid=4033&cfid=42832036&cftoken=86561938&dts=29120105725|archive-date=23 July 2011}}</ref> and won two gold medals at the 2009 Queensland Swimming Championships.<ref>{{cite news|last=Dew|first=Jake|title=Rickard motivated by loss|url=http://www.goldcoast.com.au/article/2009/12/14/169285_gold-coast-sport.html|access-date=13 March 2014|newspaper=Goldcoast.com.au|date=14 December 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121004081259/http://www.goldcoast.com.au/article/2009/12/14/169285_gold-coast-sport.html|archive-date=4 October 2012}}</ref> His mother Angie also worked as a volunteer at the swimming club.<ref>{{cite news|first1=Geoff|last1=Chambers|first2=Jake|last2=Dew|title=$500 a year to watch kids swim|url=http://www.goldcoast.com.au/article/2009/05/12/77885_gold-coast-news.html|access-date=13 March 2014|newspaper=Goldcoast.com.au|date=12 May 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130324235801/http://www.goldcoast.com.au/article/2009/05/12/77885_gold-coast-news.html|archive-date=24 March 2013}}</ref> He has two younger siblings, Tom and Alli.<ref>{{cite news|last=Grant|first=Dwayne|title=The Cody Simpson Effect|url=http://www.goldcoast.com.au/article/2010/06/19/229321_news.html|access-date=13 March 2014|newspaper=Goldcoast.com.au|date=19 June 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121004081255/http://www.goldcoast.com.au/article/2010/06/19/229321_news.html|archive-date=4 October 2012}}</ref>

Simpson began to record songs in his bedroom during the summer of 2009 on YouTube, performing "[[I'm Yours (Jason Mraz song)|I'm Yours]]" by [[Jason Mraz]],<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2vnp2e2O8P0 | title=Cody Simpson singing "I'm Yours" by Jason Mraz | publisher=YouTube | access-date=8 August 2010}}</ref> "[[Cry Me a River (Justin Timberlake song)|Cry Me a River]]"<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CkO1RoX6qWA | title=Cry Me a River – Cody Simpson Cover | publisher=YouTube | access-date=8 August 2010}}</ref> and "[[Señorita (Justin Timberlake song)|Señorita]]"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h8x0quR56aU | title=Justin Timberlake – Señorita cover – Cody Simpson|publisher=YouTube|access-date=19 August 2010}}</ref> by [[Justin Timberlake]], "[[I Want You Back]]" by [[The Jackson 5]],<ref>He also made covers of "Wanted Dead or Alive" by Bon Jovi, and "The Man Who Can't Be Moved" by The Script{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xkwaAdt0Rh8 | title=I Want You Back by Michael Jackson – Cody Simpson Cover|publisher=YouTube|access-date=19 August 2010}}</ref> and his own songs, "One"<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J-ZuRlvggMo | title='One' – Cody Simpson | publisher=YouTube | access-date=8 August 2010}}</ref> and "Perfect".<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UMZb1iP9WhY | title=Perfect – Original by Cody Simpson | publisher=YouTube | access-date=8 August 2010}}</ref>

The music video for Simpson's second single, "Summertime", was released on 20 September 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i287hxbZ8-k|title=Cody Simpson – Summertime (Official Video) |publisher=YouTube|access-date=20 September 2010}}</ref> Simpson relocated to Los Angeles in June 2010 with his family to record his songs with [[Atlantic Records]]' Shawn Campbell.<ref>{{cite news|last=Dickinson|first=Alex|title=Teen singer Cody Simpson hits US big time|url=http://www.couriermail.com.au/news/queensland/teen-singer-cody-simpson-hits-us-big-time/story-e6freoof-1225867546162|access-date=13 March 2014|newspaper=[[The Courier-Mail]]|date=17 May 2010}}</ref> That same month, Simpson appeared on ''[[Sunrise (Australian TV program)|Sunrise]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://au.tv.yahoo.com/sunrise/video/play/-/7330775/cody-goes-global/|title=Cody goes global|date=2 June 2010|work=au.tv.yahoo.com/sunrise|access-date=15 June 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718135233/http://au.tv.yahoo.com/sunrise/video/play/-/7330775/cody-goes-global/|archive-date=18 July 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> On 22 June 2010, it was announced that Simpson would participate in the ''Camplified 2010 Tour'', along with other artists, touring across the United States. The EP included five tracks in total, four of them being previously unreleased.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.codysimpson.com/blog/new-cody-music/|title=New Cody Simpson Music – 4 U|date=4 December 2010|publisher=codysimpson.com|access-date=7 December 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101208232055/http://www.codysimpson.com/blog/new-cody-music/|archive-date=8 December 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> Simpson recorded a remake of the song "[[I Want Candy]]" by [[The Strangeloves]] as the main theme song for the Easter-themed live-action/[[Computer-generated imagery|CGI]]-animated film ''[[Hop (film)|Hop]]''. ''[[Coast to Coast (Cody Simpson EP)|Coast to Coast]]'' was released on 20 September 2011 by Atlantic Records. It reached number 12 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and sold 24,000 copies.<ref>{{cite news|last=Kaufman|first=Gil|title=Tony Bennett Scores First No. 1 on Billboard 200|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1671676/tony-bennett-duets-billboard-sales.jhtml|access-date=13 March 2014|publisher=[[MTV]]|date=28 September 2011}}</ref> On 23 April 2011, Simpson released the single "[[On My Mind (Cody Simpson song)|On My Mind]]". He played nine dates around the United States and finished it in [[Orange, California]] on 18 September 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.codysimpson.com/blog/cody-announces-coast-coast-tour-updated-times |title=Cody Announces the 'Coast to Coast' Tour! *Updated Times* |access-date=18 August 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131005062158/http://www.codysimpson.com/blog/cody-announces-coast-coast-tour-updated-times |archive-date=5 October 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On 22 September, it was announced that Cody Simpson hired [[Justin Bieber|Justin Bieber's]] manager, [[Scooter Braun]], as his own.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thehothits.com/news/26000/it's-official---scooter-braun-is-cody-simpson's-manager |title=It's Official – Scooter Braun Is Cody Simpson's Manager |publisher=The Hot Hits |access-date=20 July 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130913232801/http://www.thehothits.com/news/26000/it's-official---scooter-braun-is-cody-simpson's-manager |archive-date=13 September 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>[http://www.topjustinbieber.com/category/justin-bieber-september-21-2011/]  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130723000737/http://www.topjustinbieber.com/category/justin-bieber-september-21-2011/ |date=23 July 2013 }}</ref> In December 2011, [[The Wish Factory]] released a Cody Simpson doll in two formats.<ref>{{cite web|last=Cashmere|first=Paul|title=Cody Simpson Is A Doll|date=10 December 2011|url=http://www.noise11.com/news/cody-simpson-is-a-doll-20111210|publisher=noise11.com|access-date=10 December 2011}}</ref>

====2012–2014: ''Paradise'', ''Surfers Paradise'' and ''Dancing with the Stars''====
[[File:Cody Simpson 2013.jpg|thumb|Simpson performing in [[Montreal]] in 2013]]
On 12 June 2012, Simpson released an EP titled ''Preview to Paradise'' containing four songs from his upcoming debut studio album, ''[[Paradise (Cody Simpson album)|Paradise]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.artistdirect.com/entertainment-news/article/cody-simpson-preview-to-paradise-review-4-5-out-of-5-stars/10124863 |title=Preview to Paradise – EP by Cody Simpson |access-date=18 August 2012}}</ref> which was released on 2 October 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nickutopia.com/2012/06/30/cody-simpson-releasing-his-paradise-album-on-october-2-2012/ |title=Cody Simpson Releasing His "Paradise" Album on October 2, 2012 |access-date=18 August 2012}}</ref> In February 2013, Simpson toured Europe, doing his own tour dates as well as supporting Justin Bieber on many of the European Believe tour dates. ''[[Surfers Paradise (album)|Surfers Paradise]]'', which was released on 16 July 2013, is Simpson's first album to debut in the top 10 of the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard 200|200]].
During the summer of 2013, Simpson headlined the [[Paradise Tour (Cody Simpson)|Paradise Tour]], featuring opening acts Ryan Beatty and Before You Exit.
On 19 November 2013, Simpson released an acoustic album called ''The Acoustic Sessions'', featuring the songs "Pretty Brown Eyes", "All Day", "La Da Dee", "Wish U Were Here", and a cover of "Please Come Home for Christmas" recorded acoustically.

On 4 March 2014, Simpson was announced as one of the celebrities to participate on the [[Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 18)|18th season]] of ''[[Dancing with the Stars (U.S. TV series)|Dancing with the Stars]]'' during [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC's]] ''[[Good Morning America]]''. He was partnered with professional dancer [[Witney Carson]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/517094/dancing-with-the-stars-cast-revealed-cody-simpson-nene-leakes-and-james-maslow-are-among-the-names-see-the-full-list |title=Dancing With the Stars Cast Revealed! Cody Simpson, Nene Leakes and James Maslow Are Among the Names—See the Full List! &#124; E! Online UK |publisher=E! |date=4 March 2014 |access-date=21 April 2014}}</ref> He was eliminated on Week 5 of the competition and finished in 9th place.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/blogs/entertainment/2014/04/dancing-with-the-stars-cody-simpson-voted-off-in-week-5/|title='Dancing With the Stars': Cody Simpson Voted Off in Week 5|work=ABC News Blogs|publisher=ABC News}}</ref>

From 30 June 2014 to 14 July 2014 Simpson did an acoustic tour through Europe with singer [[Jackson Harris]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jacksonharrismusic.com/tour-with-cody-simpson-starts-tonight|title=Tour With Cody Simpson Starts Tonight! – Jackson Harris|publisher=jacksonharrismusic.com|access-date=31 October 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160529183501/http://www.jacksonharrismusic.com/tour-with-cody-simpson-starts-tonight/|archive-date=29 May 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In August 2014 Simpson announced he had departed Warner/Atlantic. He cited creative differences as the reason for his departure and as a result, is currently unsigned.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fanlala.com/news/cody-simpson-leaves-record-label-and-becomes-independent-artist|title=Cody Simpson Leaves Record Label And Becomes Independent Artist!|publisher=fanlala.com|access-date=23 August 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160131025856/https://www.fanlala.com/news/cody-simpson-leaves-record-label-and-becomes-independent-artist|archive-date=31 January 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

====2015–2018: ''Free'', Cody Simpson and the Tide, and ''Wave One''====

In 2017, Simpson formed a new band billed as Cody Simpson and the Tide, making their debut with the single "Waiting for the Tide."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8013949/cody-simpson-and-the-tide-waiting-for-the-tide-video|title=Cody Simpson & The Tide Make a Wavy Debut With 'Waiting for the Tide' Video: Premiere|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=25 October 2017}}</ref> Cody and the Tide is composed of Cody Simpson on vocals and guitar, Adrian Cota on drums and Shareef "Reef" Addo on bass.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2017-11-21-interview-cody-simpson-the-tide-talk-wave-one-ep-their-formation/|title=INTERVIEW: Cody Simpson & The Tide Talk 'Wave One' EP & Their Formation |website=iHeart.com|access-date=17 June 2018|language=en}}</ref>
Cody and the Tide released their debut EP, entitled ''Wave One'', in the fall of 2017. The music is a blend of pop, rock, blues, and surf-rock genres. The band used their varying influences and musical tastes to create ''Wave One.'' In their four-track EP, Simpson sings of love, unity, environmentalism and ocean conservation. The EP's debut single, "Waiting for the Tide," was inspired by a poem Simpson wrote that tackled topics such as rising water levels and ocean conservation.<ref name=":0" /> In August 2018, the band released "Underwater" music video song.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Cody Simpson & The Tide's 'Underwater' Video: Premiere|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8468414/cody-simpson-the-tide-underwater-video-premiere|date=2018-08-03|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2020-05-06}}</ref> In October, Cody and the Tide went on a short tour of southern California to perform their EP.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7973938/cody-simpson-new-band-the-tide|title=Cody Simpson Announces 'Zen' New Band, The Tide|work=Billboard|access-date=17 June 2018}}</ref> In early 2019, after almost three years together, the band split up.

====2019–present: Return to solo work====
After parting ways with his band and focusing on his solo career again, Simpson released the song ''Golden Thing'' he wrote for [[Miley Cyrus]] on 18 October 2019 which crossed over 1 million views on Spotify.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8532857/cody-simpson-details-romance-with-miley-cyrus-confirms-plans-to-release-new-song|title=Cody Simpson Details Romance With Miley Cyrus, Confirms Plans to Release New Song: Report|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=12 October 2019}}</ref> In the same year, he participated on ''[[The Masked Singer (Australian TV series)|The Masked Singer Australia]]'', as the "Robot" and went on to win the show.
