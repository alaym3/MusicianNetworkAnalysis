'''Lawrence''' is a US-based [[Soul music|soul-pop]] group founded by [[New York City]]-raised sibling duo Clyde and Gracie Lawrence. The two who have been singing, performing, and writing songs together since their early childhood. Today, Lawrence is an eight-piece band that has released seven albums.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.townandcountrymag.com/leisure/arts-and-culture/a9640832/lawrence-band/|title=Meet Lawrence, the World Economic Forum’s Favorite Rock Band|last=Rathe|first=Adam|date=2017-05-11|website=Town & Country|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-07}}</ref>

==Lawrence family==
Clyde and Gracie Lawrence, siblings aged three and a half years apart, have created music and performed together since childhood.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.dalton.org/page/alumni-news-detail?pk=1080433|title=Gracie and Clyde of "Lawrence" Return to Perform for High School Assembly|date=2017-01-11|website=Dalton School|language=en|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref> Their father, [[Marc Lawrence (filmmaker)|Marc Lawrence]], is a filmmaker, screenwriter, and director, best known for movies such as ''[[Miss Congeniality (film)|Miss Congeniality]]'' and ''[[Music and Lyrics]].''<ref name="auto1" /> Their younger brother, Linus has also featured in songs such as "Come On, Brother" on their album ''Breakfast.'' He has also appeared in Lawrence music videos.<ref name=":3" />

===Clyde Lawrence===
{{Self-published|section|date=July 2020}}
Clyde is a singer, pianist, and songwriter. He began writing music soon after he started playing the piano at age 4. In the band he plays guitar, organ, and keyboard.<ref name=":0" /> He graduated from the [[Dalton School]] in 2011 and completed an undergraduate degree at [[Brown University]] in 2015.<ref name=":2" />

When Marc Lawrence was working on the 2000 film ''Miss Congeniality'' and was not pleased with his options for the Miss United States pageant theme song, he turned to Clyde to see what he thought was wrong. At only 5, Clyde wrote his own version, and the film's other producers chose it without knowing its writer.<ref name="auto1" /> The Miss United States pageant anthem that Clyde composed is prominently featured in the film, and his composition led him to become the youngest member of the [[Songwriters Guild of America]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.downtownmagazinenyc.com/lawrence-founders-clyde-and-gracie-lawrence-to-headline-rockwood-music-hall-on-may-6/|title=Lawrence Founders Clyde and Gracie Lawrence to Headline Rockwood Music Hall on May 6|last=Downtown Magazine|first=|date=2016-04-27|website=Downtown Magazine|language=en-US|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref> In recent years, a line from this song that Clyde wrote, "She's beauty, she's grace" has become widely used on the Internet.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spreadshirt.com/shes+beauty+shes+grace+t-shirts|title=Shop Shes Beauty Shes Grace T-Shirts online|website=Spreadshirt|language=en|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref>

While attending high school at Dalton, Clyde continued to compose music for major motion pictures. He wrote parts of the original score for the 2007 film ''[[Music and Lyrics|Music & Lyrics]]'' as well as "Dance With Me Tonight", a song that [[Hugh Grant]] performs.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://clydelawrence.bandcamp.com/album/homesick|title=Homesick, by Clyde Lawrence|website=Clyde Lawrence|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref> He wrote two tracks, one of which he performed himself, for 2009 movie ''[[Did You Hear About the Morgans?]]'' Clyde has written songs for additional films including ''[[The Rewrite]]'' (2015) and ''[[Landline (film)|Landline]]'' (2017).<ref name="auto2" /> He also composed the songs featured in the Season 2 episode of ''[[Instinct (American TV series)|Instinct]]'' entitled "Broken Record" which aired on July 7, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm2753085/|title=Clyde Lawrence|website=IMDb|access-date=2020-04-26}}</ref> Clyde, along with Cody Fitzgerald, composed the entire score for the 2019 [[Disney+]] film, ''[[Noelle (2019 film)|Noelle]],'' starring [[Bill Hader]] and [[Anna Kendrick]]. They are the youngest people to serve as composers for a [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]] film.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6T-PKw8uaaY|title=Clyde Lawrence, Cody Fitzgerald - Noelle (Behind the Scenes)|last=|first=|date=February 13, 2020|website=YouTube|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

===Gracie Lawrence===
Gracie is a singer and actress. She attended Dalton School, graduating in 2015.<ref name=":2" /> She attended Brown after taking a gap year to tour with the band.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=http://features.browndailyherald.com/2015/soundingoff/|title=Sounding Off|website=Brown Daily Herald Features|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref> Gracie was set to graduate from Brown in 2020 but has since left college to play with Lawrence full time.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.brownnyc.org/article.html?aid=515|title=SOLD OUT: Brown student/alumni band, Lawrence, rocks the Bowery Ballroom!|website=www.brownnyc.org|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref>

Beyond music, she has acted on Broadway, television, and films for over a decade.<ref name=":8">{{Citation|title=Gracie Lawrence - One Dollar Cast Member|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200202204942/https://www.cbs.com/shows/one-dollar/cast/215937/|language=en|access-date=2021-11-17}}</ref> In 2009, she acted in the Broadway show ''[[Brighton Beach Memoirs]]'' and also played Lucy Granger in the film [[Did You Hear About the Morgans?|''Did You Hear About The Morgans?'']]. She has also acted in the 2011 film ''[[The Sitter]]'' as well as TV shows such as ''[[The Good Wife]],'' ''[[The Americans]],'' and ''[[Younger (TV series)|Younger]].'' In 2018, she played Julie Gardner on the main cast of thriller [[CBS]]-All Access series ''[[One Dollar (TV series)|One Dollar]]'', which also featured [[Leslie Odom Jr.|Leslie Odom Jr]]. and [[John Carroll Lynch]].<ref name=":8" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm2789151/|title=Gracie Lawrence|website=IMDb|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref> She was featured in ''[[Town & Country (magazine)|Town & Country]]'' magazine's "Modern Swans" September 2018 issue.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.townandcountrymag.com/society/a22561513/modern-swan-2018-gracie-lawrence/|title=Meet Gracie Lawrence, Actress and Half of a Sibling Pop Duo|author=The Editors|date=2018-08-01|website=Town & Country|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-01}}</ref>

==Band formation==
While studying at Brown University, Clyde started a band originally called The Clyde Lawrence Band for which he sung lead vocals and played keyboard. The band featured a rotating crew of Brown students as members. Gracie, still in high school, also travelled to perform with the band.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brownalumnimagazine.com/articles/2015-06-12/music-is-what-i-do|title=Music Is What I Do|website=www.brownalumnimagazine.com|language=en|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.townandcountrymag.com/leisure/arts-and-culture/a9640832/lawrence-band/|title=Meet Lawrence, the World Economic Forum’s Favorite Rock Band|last=Rathe|first=Adam|date=2017-05-11|website=Town & Country|language=en-US|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref> The Clyde Lawrence Band played events on campus as well as at colleges and venues across the Northeast.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":5" />

The Clyde Lawrence Band performed on Clyde's 2013 album, ''Homesick''.<ref name=":0" /> When Gracie became co-lead singer after many years playing together the band's name was changed to Lawrence.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.brownalumnimagazine.com/articles/2015-06-12/music-is-what-i-do|title=Music Is What I Do|last=Goodman|first=Lawrence|date=2015-08-15|website=Brown Alumni Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-23}}</ref>

Seven of the eight members of Lawrence attended Brown.<ref name=":4" />

==Music==
Lawrence released its first full-length album, ''Breakfast'', in 2016, which reached #6 on the iTunes R&B charts on its first day of release.<ref name=":3" /> Produced by Grammy winner [[Eric Krasno]], ''Breakfast'' includes musical appearances from members of [[Snarky Puppy]] and [[Lettuce (band)|Lettuce]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://lawrencetheband.com/bio|title=Bio — Lawrence the Band|website=Lawrence|language=en-US|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref> Their hit single from the album, "Do You Wanna Do Nothing with Me?" has several million plays on Spotify.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/5rwUYLyUq8gBsVaOUcUxpE|title=Lawrence|website=Spotify|language=en|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref>

Their sophomore album, ''Living Room'', was released in September 2018. ''Living Room'' reached #2 on the iTunes R&B/Soul chart, earned a featured placement on Spotify’s “New Music Friday” playlist, and led the band to its late-night television debut on NBC’s ''[[Last Call with Carson Daly]]''.<ref name="auto" /> The album, which combines elements of Lawrence's old and new influences while featuring themes of childhood and family issues,<ref name="auto" /> was co-produced by bandmates Jordan Cohen (tenor/baritone saxophone) and Jonny Koh (guitar), Brooklyn-based producer Eli Crews, and Clyde and Gracie themselves.<ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/04/13/523759335/brother-sister-duo-lawrence-on-touring-and-the-meaning-of-home|title=Siblings Gracie And Clyde Lawrence On Touring And The Meaning Of Home|last=Cala|first=Christina|date=2017-04-13|website=NPR|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-08}}</ref>

Lawrence has performed both in the United States and internationally. The band has headlined several tours across the USA and also has appeared at [[Bonnaroo Music Festival|Bonnaroo]], [[Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival|Outside Lands]], [[South by Southwest|SXSW]], [[Electric Forest Festival|Electric Forest]], [[Firefly Music Festival|Firefly]], [[Okeechobee Music & Arts Festival|Okeechobee]], and [[Hangout Music Festival|Hangout]] festivals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.michigandaily.com/section/arts/let’s-make-pop-music-cool-again-conversation-band-lawrence|title=Let’s make pop music cool again: A conversation with the band Lawrence|last=Zak|first=Mimi|date=2016-01-16|website=Michigan Daily|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-23}}</ref> The band has also opened for [[Jon Bellion]], [[Lake Street Dive]], [[Vulfpeck]], [[Soulive]], [[Jacob Collier]], [[Blues Traveler]], [[O.A.R.]], and [[Jarle Bernhoft|Bernhoft]].<ref name="auto1" />

===Jon Bellion collaboration and record deal===
In February 2019, Lawrence announced via social media that the band would be an opener for [[Jon Bellion]] on his summer 2019 national tour, the ''[[Glory Sound Prep]]'' Tour.<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/lawrencetheband/posts/2349042015120574|title=Lawrence|website=www.facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref> In June, Lawrence released its first of many Jon Bellion-produced singles, "Casualty". In July, Bellion and Lawrence announced that Bellion had launched his own record label, Beautiful Mind Records and had signed Lawrence as its first artist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/BMindRecords|title=Beautiful Mind Records (@BMindRecords) {{!}} Twitter|website=twitter.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Lawrence – Casualty|url=https://genius.com/Lawrence-casualty-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/lawrencetheband/status/1137131314539323392?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E1137131314539323392&ref_url=https://genius.com/Lawrence-casualty-lyrics|title=HERE’S THE BIG ANNOUNCEMENT: We’ve been in the studio with @jonbellion making a whole bunch of new Lawrence songs!! The first single is called "Casualty" and it comes out June 21st pic.twitter.com/bc4FNXk8Q6|last=Lawrence|date=2019-06-07|website=@lawrencetheband|language=en|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref> Bellion has stated that his intention with this new label is to "take care of artists for the rest of their careers" and to create a family of musical creators who he supports and with whom he collaborates.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://303magazine.com/2019/07/review-jon-bellion-fillmore/|title=Review – Jon Bellion Electrified and Improvised at the Fillmore|last=Stephan|first=Tiara|date=2019-07-23|website=303 Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-06}}</ref>
