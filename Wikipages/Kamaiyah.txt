'''Kamaiyah Jamesha Johnson''' (born March 13, 1992)<ref name="Wallace">{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/features/rising/9792-supa-dupa-fly-kamaiyah-bosses-up/|title=Supa Dupa Fly: Kamaiyah Bosses Up|first=Carvell|last=Wallace|date=January 20, 2016|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BFr_BOpN7dY/|title=Instagram photo by CTBeats RealMusic • May 21, 2016 at 11:11&nbsp;pm UTC}}</ref> is an American rapper and singer from [[Oakland, California]]. Her debut mixtape, ''[[A Good Night in the Ghetto]]'', was released in 2016 to critical acclaim. In 2017, Kamaiyah was named as one of the ten of ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''{{'}}s [[XXL Freshman|2017 Freshman Class]]. She released her second mixtape, ''Before I Wake'', in 2017. In 2020, she launched her own label, GRND.WRK, releasing her third mixtape, ''Got It Made'', through the label.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Joshi|first=Tara|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/feb/22/one-to-watch-kamaiyah-got-it-made|title=One to watch: Kamaiyah|date=2020-02-22|work=The Guardian|accessdate=2020-03-20|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>

==Career==
Kamaiyah Johnson was born in [[Oakland, California]], but raised in [[Sacramento]]. She is inspired by her favorite childhood 1990s artists such as [[TLC (group)|TLC]], [[Missy Elliott]], and [[Aaliyah]].<ref name="Wallace" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2794761/kamaiyah-oakland-best-new-rapper/|title=Meet Kamaiyah, Oakland's Best New Rapper|publisher=[[MTV]]|first=Meaghan|last=Garvey|date=March 16, 2016|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thehundreds.com/meet-kamaiyah-oaklands-newest-rap-queen/|title=Meet Kamaiyah, Oakland's Newest Rap Queen|publisher=[[The Hundreds]]|first=Anna|last=Dorn|date=June 14, 2006|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref> Her debut single "How Does It Feel" premiered late in 2015 and was ranked one of the year's best songs by [[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] and [[NPR]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/9765-the-100-best-tracks-of-2015/|title=The 100 Best Tracks of 2015|date=December 13, 2015|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2015/12/15/459719483/the-complete-list-npr-musics-favorite-songs-of-2015|title=The Complete List: NPR Music's Favorite Songs Of 2015|publisher=[[NPR]]|date=December 15, 2015|accessdate=June 19, 2016}}</ref>

She released her debut mixtape, ''[[A Good Night in the Ghetto]]'', in March 2016 to critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.factmag.com/2016/03/26/rap-round-up-kamiayah-lil-yachty/2/|title=The Rap Round-up: Kamaiyah puts the bounce back into the Bay|work=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]|first1=John|last1=Twells|first2=Chris|last2=Kelly|first3=Claire|last3=Lobenfeld|date=March 26, 2016|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/03/kamaiyah-good-night-in-the-ghetto/|title=Kamaiyah Drops 'A Good Night in the Ghetto' Mixtape|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|first=Peter|last=Walsh|date=March 14, 2016|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/kamaiyah-a_good_night_in_the_ghetto|title=Kamaiyah – A Good Night in the Ghetto|work=[[Exclaim!]]|first=James|last=Wilt|date=March 22, 2016|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/21712-a-good-night-in-the-ghetto/|title=Kamaiyah: A Good Night in the Ghetto|first=Jayson|last=Greene|date=March 24, 2016|access-date=June 19, 2016}}</ref> ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' placed it at number 21 on the "50 Best Albums of 2016" list.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/best-albums-of-2016/kamaiyah-a-good-night-in-the-ghetto|title=The 50 Best Albums of 2016 – 21. After releasing her debut mixtape, Kamaiyah performed at [[South by Southwest]] that month in [[Austin, Texas]]. That year she was also featured, along with [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], on [[YG (rapper)|YG]]'s "[[Why You Always Hatin?]]", a single from his 2016 ''[[Still Brazy]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine | title=YG Recruits Drake & Kamaiyah For 'Why You Always Hatin?'  | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7378473/yg-why-you-always-hatin-drake-kamaiyah | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |last=Platon |first=Adelle | date=May 23, 2016 | accessdate=June 19, 2016}}</ref> Later that year, Kamaiyah performed as an opener for YG on his Fuck Donald Trump Tour.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7588766/yg-kamaiyah-anti-trump-protests-fuck-donald-trump-tour-portland|title=YG, Kamaiyah and More Continue Anti-Trump Protests on F—k Donald Trump Tour Stop in Oregon|work=Billboard|access-date=December 6, 2017}}</ref> Kamaiyah signed to [[Interscope Records]] in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/kamaiyah-got-it-made/|title=Kamaiyah: Got It Made|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=2020-03-20}}</ref>

On June 13, 2017, Kamaiyah was named as one of the ten of ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL's]]'' "[[XXL Freshman|2017 Freshman Class]]" along with [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]], [[PnB Rock]], [[Playboi Carti]], [[Ugly God]], [[Kyle (rapper)|Kyle]], [[Aminé]], [[MadeinTYO]], [[Kap G]], and [[XXXTentacion]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/2017-xxl-freshman-cover/|title=XXL Freshman 2017 Cover Revealed|magazine=XXL|date=June 13, 2017|accessdate=June 17, 2017}}</ref> Kamaiyah announced a mixtape, ''Don't Ever Get it Twisted'', which was delayed and eventually shelved, due to sample clearance issues and problems with her major label contract.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2017/04/20/kamaiyah-dont-ever-get-it-twisted-mixtape-delayed-due-to-sample-clearance-issues/|title=Kamaiyah's Don't Ever Get It Twisted mixtape delayed due to sample clearance issues|date=2017-04-20|website=FACT Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.eastbayexpress.com/oakland/kamaiyahs-major-label-blues/Content?oid=11027979|title=Kamaiyah's Major-Label Blues|last=Lefebvre|first=Sam|website=East Bay Express|language=en|access-date=2020-03-20}}</ref> She instead self-released a second mixtape, ''Before I Wake'', in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theface.com/music/kamaiyah-got-it-made-album-rap-hip-hop|title=Kamaiyah exclusively speaks to The Face on her new album Got it Made|website=The Face|language=en-gb|access-date=2020-03-20}}</ref>

Kamaiyah appeared in a [[Sprite (drink)|Sprite]] commercial alongside [[LeBron James]], in early 2018.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8097430/kamaiyah-interview-sprite-commercial-woke-mixtape|title=Kamaiyah Talks Sprite 'Big Taste Cuts Through' Commercial With LeBron James, Bringing Creativity Back to Hip-Hop|magazine=Billboard.com|date=January 21, 2018}}</ref> Kamaiyah left [[Interscope Records]] and [[4Hunnid Records]] after her project ''Something To Ride To,'' was delayed multiple times''.''<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2020/02/kamaiyah-got-it-made-feature|title=Kamaiyah On Long-Awaited Debut 'Got It Made,' Independent Status|date=2020-02-21|website=Vibe|language=en|access-date=2020-03-20}}</ref> Kamaiyah launched her own label GRND.WRK and released her third mixtape, ''Got it Made'', in 2020.<ref name=":0" /> The project was preceded by the single "1-800-IM-HORNY".

On September 18, 2020, Kamaiyah released ''Oakland Nights'', a collaborative EP with fellow Bay Area artist, Capolow. The 10-track EP marks Kamaiyah's second project released under her label, GRND.WRK.<ref name="Nights">{{cite web|url=https://respect-mag.com/2020/09/kamaiyah-announces-new-music-oakland-nights/|title=Kamaiyah Announces New Music, "Oakland Nights"|work=[[Respect (magazine)|Respect]]|last=Rashed|first=Ayana|date=September 17, 2020|accessdate=September 17, 2020}}</ref>
