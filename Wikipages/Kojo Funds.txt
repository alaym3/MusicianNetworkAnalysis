'''Errol Kojo Bellot''', better known professionally as '''Kojo Funds''' ({{IPAc-en|k|oʊ|dʒ|oʊ|_|f|ʌ|n|d|z}} {{Respell|KOH|joh|_|FUNDZ}}; born 30 March 1995),<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=Kojo Funds - Earth Agency|url=https://earth-agency.com/artists/kojo-funds/|website=Earth Agency}}</ref> is an English singer-songwriter and rapper.<ref name="guardian">{{cite news|first1=David|last1=Renshaw|accessdate=30 August 2018|title=Kojo Funds review – champagne, confetti and raw street life|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/apr/06/kojo-funds-review-afro-swinger-grime-rap-trap-electric-brixton|date=6 April 2018|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref><ref name="vice">{{cite web|accessdate=30 August 2018|title=Kojo Funds Is the Afro-Swing King of the UK's Black Diaspora|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/ne9mxq/kojo-funds-afro-swing-interview-2018-stallin|website=Noisey|date=17 May 2018}}</ref> His music blends West African and Caribbean sounds with [[road rap]], [[dancehall]] and [[British pop music|pop]].

Kojo Funds featured on [[Yxng Bane]]'s single "Fine Wine" and on [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]]'s "[[Finders Keepers (Mabel song)|Finders Keepers]]". His own singles include "[[Check (Kojo Funds and Raye song)|Check]]" with [[Raye (singer)|Raye]].

== Career ==
Kojo Funds was born in 1995 in [[London]]. He is of mixed Ghanaian and [[Dominica]]n heritage.<ref name="vice"/><ref name="mtv">{{cite news|accessdate=1 September 2016|title=Get to Know: Kojo Funds|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/get-to-know/news/get-to-know-kojo-funds|website=MTV}}</ref> He grew up in [[Custom House, Newham|Custom House]] in East London and was raised by his mother.<ref name="vice"/>

A number of his songs have been produced by GA, including "Dun Talkin".<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=GA on how he made Dun Talkin by Kojo Funds, Afrobeats, Afro Bashment or Afro Swing, so what do we call it?, Twin B - BBC Radio 1Xtra|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p052jfz7|website=BBC}}</ref> In early 2017, he had a feature on the [[Yxng Bane]] single "Fine Wine" which was certified silver by the BPI,<ref>"[https://www.bpi.co.uk/award/14694-4552-1 Yxng Bane Ft Kojo Funds: Fine Wine: All About The Melody]". British Phonographic Industry. Accessed 2 September 2018.</ref> and on [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]]'s "[[Finders Keepers (Mabel song)|Finders Keepers]]" which reached number 8 in the UK Official Charts Company Singles chart.<ref>{{cite web|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=Mabel ft Kojo Funds|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/52027/mabel-ft-kojo-funds/|website=[[Official Charts]]}}</ref> On 9 February 2018 he released the single "[[Check (Kojo Funds and Raye song)|Check]]" with British singer-songwriter [[Raye (singer)|Raye]].

In 2017 Kojo Funds was nominated for Best Newcomer at the [[MOBO Awards]].<ref>{{cite news|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=This year's MOBO award nominations have been announced|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/mobo-awards-2017-nominations-stormzy-j-hus-a8006441.html|newspaper=The Independent}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=Here are all the nominations for the 2017 MOBO Awards|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/10/17/2017-mobo-awards-full-nominees|newspaper=The Fader}}</ref> In 2018 he played [[Bestival]]<ref>{{cite news|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=Grace Jones, Ghetts and a lot of glitter: the best of Bestival 2018 – in pictures|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/gallery/2018/aug/06/grace-jones-ghetts-glitter-the-best-of-bestival-2018|newspaper=The Guardian|date=6 August 2018|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> and [[Wireless Festival]].<ref>{{cite news|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=Wireless Festival 2018 announces bonkers lineup ft. J Cole and Stormzy|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/wireless-festival-lineup-headliners-j-cole-stormzy-dj-khaled-j-hus-cardi-b-how-to-get-tickets-a8172061.html|newspaper=The Independent}}</ref>

He calls his musical style [[afroswing]].<ref name="Mixmag">{{cite news|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=UK Afrobeats: The new sound reinventing British club music|url=https://mixmag.net/feature/uk-afrobeats-the-new-sound-reinventing-british-club-music|newspaper=Mixmag}}</ref><ref name="guardian-simpson">{{cite web|first1=Dave|last1=Simpson|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=Yxng Bane review – genre-hopping rapper relentlessly pleases the crowd|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/mar/26/yxng-bane-review-gorilla-manchester|date=26 March 2018|website=The Guardian}}</ref> It has also been called "afro-bashment".<ref name="guardian-adegoke"/> The genre originated in [[London]] in late 2000s; it blends West African and Caribbean sounds with [[road rap]], [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], [[dancehall]] and [[British pop music]].<ref name="guardian"/><ref name="vice"/><ref name="Mixmag" /><ref name="guardian-adegoke">{{cite web|first1=Yomi|last1=Adegoke|accessdate=1 September 2018|title=Grime, Afro bashment, drill ... how black British music became more fertile than ever|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/jun/01/grime-afro-bashment-drill-how-black-british-music-became-more-fertile-than-ever|date=1 June 2018|website=The Guardian}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He is an avid supporter of [[Manchester United F.C.]]<ref>{{Cite news|last=Davey|first=Jacob|title=VERSUS Jerseys: Kojo Funds |url=https://versus.uk.com/2019/01/versus-jerseys-kojo-funds/ |website=versus.uk.com |date=9 January 2019 |access-date=3 March 2021}}</ref>
