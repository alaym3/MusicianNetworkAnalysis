'''Sercan İpekçioğlu''' (born 1 July 1991), better known by his stage name '''Ezhel''' ({{IPA-tr|ezɛl|lang}}), is a Turkish [[rapper]], singer and [[songwriter]] from [[Ankara]] whose music blends [[trap music (hip hop)|trap]], [[hip-hop]] and [[reggae]]. His song "AYA" has been streamed more than 80 million times on Spotify. He has been given the title 'Freestyle King' by Hip Hop Life in Turkey.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hiphoplife.com.tr/haberler/865-freestyle-king-2-sampiyonu-ais-ezhel.html|title=Freestyle King 2 Şampiyonu Ais Ezhel!|last=Ulaş|website=Hiphoplife.com.tr {{!}} Türkiye`den Hip Hop Kültürü.|language=tr-TR|access-date=2018-03-16|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180317164810/https://www.hiphoplife.com.tr/haberler/865-freestyle-king-2-sampiyonu-ais-ezhel.html|archive-date=2018-03-17|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Life and career ==
Ezhel came from a musical family. His mother was an Anatolian folk dancer, and his uncle was a musician and sound and light engineer.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.milliyet.com.tr/rap-hep-askti--gundem-2529002/|title='Rap hep aşktı'|last=sitesi|first=milliyet.com.tr Türkiye'nin lider haber|work=MİLLİYET HABER - TÜRKİYE'NİN HABER SİTESİ|access-date=2018-03-14}}</ref> He received a scholarship to TED school, and began listening to US rappers like [[Eminem]], [[50 Cent]] and [[Tupac Shakur]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bantmag.com/magazine/issue/post/59/982|title="Bir rap iyi başlamazsa, iyi rap değildir": Ezhel ve Büyük Ev Ablukada|last=Dostgül|first=Busen|website=bantmag.com|access-date=14 March 2018}}</ref>

At 18, he began performing with a [[reggae]] band called Afra Tafra. After this group split, he formed his own reggae band, Kökler Filizleniyor, along with some of his friends from Ankara.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.saloniksv.com/en/event/814/ezhel|title=Salon İKSV {{!}} Ezhel|website=Salon İKSV|language=en|access-date=2018-03-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180531225914/http://www.saloniksv.com/en/event/814/ezhel|archive-date=2018-05-31|url-status=dead}}</ref> He performed under the name Ais Ezhel until 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.weloveist.com/turkish-hip-hop|title=The Story of Turkish Hip Hop|date=2016-05-22|work=We Love Istanbul|access-date=2018-03-14|language=en-GB}}</ref>

Ezhel can also play the [[bağlama]] and guitar, and sings reggae with the band Kökler Filizleniyor. His debut album ''[[Müptezhel]]'' came out in 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.basedistanbul.com/ay-gunesten-daha-guzel/|title=Ay Güneşten Daha Güzel! {{!}} Based Istanbul|date=2017-11-13|work=Based Istanbul|access-date=2018-03-14|language=en-US}}</ref>

Ezhel was arrested on 24 May 2018 and sent to pretrial detention accused of "inciting drug use" in his songs.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.hurriyet.com.tr/gundem/son-dakika-fenomen-rapci-ezhel-tutuklandi-40847455|title=Fenomen rapçi Ezhel tutuklandı {{!}} |work=Hürriyet|date=2018-05-24|access-date=2018-05-24|language=tr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/ezhel-arrested-turkey-rapper-lyrics-drug-use-promotion-wiz-khalifa-latest-updates-a8369296.html|title=Popular Turkish rapper Ezhel arrested over lyrics 'promoting drug use'|work=The Independent|first=Roisin|last=O'Connor|date=25 May 2018|accessdate=24 May 2019}}</ref> On June 19, Ezhel was acquitted within 9 minutes of the opening of his first trial hearing. Ezhel's lawyer, Fuat Ekin, stated that, "We got an acquittal. We can say that justice has been done," according to the Media and Law Studies Association (MLSA).<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.alaraby.co.uk/english/news/2018/6/19/turkish-rapper-ezhel-acquitted-of-first-day-of-trial|title=Turkish rapper and government critic Ezhel acquitted of drug charges|last=agencies|first=The New Arab &|work=alaraby|access-date=2018-06-21|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/turkish-rap-artist-ezhel-acquitted-in-drug-case-133478|title=Turkish rap artist Ezhel acquitted in drug case|website=Hürriyet Daily News|language=en|access-date=2018-06-21}}</ref>

In May 2019, ''[[The New York Times]]'' named him among the European pop acts that everyone should know.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2019/05/22/arts/music/15-european-pop-acts-who-matter-right-now.html|title=15 European Pop Acts You Might Not Know, but Should|work=The New York Times|date=22 May 2019|accessdate=24 May 2019}}</ref>
