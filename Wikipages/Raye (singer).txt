'''Rachel Agatha Keen'''<ref name="WarnerChappell"/> (born on 24 October 1997), known professionally as '''Raye''' (stylised in [[all caps]]), is a British singer and songwriter from South London. In 2016, she had her breakthrough by featuring on the singles; "[[By Your Side (Jonas Blue song)|By Your Side]]" by [[Jonas Blue]] and "[[You Don't Know Me (Jax Jones song)|You Don't Know Me]]" by [[Jax Jones]], the latter peaking at number three on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and both being certified platinum or higher by the [[British Phonographic Industry|BPI]]. Raye was short-listed for the BBC Music [[Sound of...]] award for 2017 and was named in third place. Her debut [[Mini-LP|mini-album]], ''[[Euphoric Sad Songs]]'' was released in November 2020 and spawned in the UK top-10 single "[[Secrets (Regard and Raye song)|Secrets]]" with [[Regard (DJ)|Regard]]. In 2021, she was co-lead artist on the single "[[Bed (Joel Corry, Raye and David Guetta song)|Bed]]" with musicians [[Joel Corry]] and [[David Guetta]]. In July 2021, Raye parted ways with her record label [[Polydor Records]].

==Early life ==
Rachel Agatha Keen was born on 24 October 1997 in [[Tooting]], London,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.simonjonespr.com/clients-1/2016/3/21/61icqm95415xuw3t4n9wkmu19r1iub|title=RAYE|website=Simonjonespr.com|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref> to a [[Ghanaian people|Ghanaian]]-[[Swiss people|Swiss]] mother and an [[English people|English]] father. She moved to [[Croydon]], where she studied at [[Woodcote High School]] before being accepted to attend the [[BRIT School]] for two years before dropping out after she felt "confined...despite learning an extensive amount."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-38402963|title=BBC Sound of 2017: Raye interview|first=Mark|last=Savage|date=4 January 2017|access-date=18 July 2020|website=Bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

==Career==
===2014–2015: Beginnings and ''Welcome to the Winter''===
On 11 December 2014, Raye released her debut extended play ''Welcome to the Winter''.<ref name="WTTW">{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Raye-Welcome-To-The-Winter/release/9005394|title=Raye (3) - Welcome To The Winter|website=Discogs.com|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref> Raye released the singles "Flowers" and "Alien" on 7 February 2015 and 28 April 2015 respectively, the latter featuring London based rapper [[Avelino (rapper)|Avelino]].<ref name="Flowers">{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Raye-Flowers/release/9005430|title=Raye (3) - Flowers|website=Discogs.com|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref><ref name="Alien">{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Raye-Alien/release/9005463|title=Raye (3) - Alien|website=Discogs.com|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref>

===2016–2018: Breakthrough and EPs===
On 11 August 2016, Raye released her second extended play, ''[[Second (Raye EP)|Second]]''.<ref name="Second">{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/second-ep/1444861442|title=Second - EP by RAYE|website=Music.apple.com|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref> The EP was preceded by promotional singles "Distraction" and "Ambition", the latter featuring rapper [[Stormzy]]. "I, U, Us" was released as the lead single from the EP on 14 October 2016.<ref name="IUUS">{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/i-u-us-the-remixes-ep/1444878700|title=I, U, Us (The Remixes) - EP by RAYE|website=Music.apple.com|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref> A video was released which was directed by British singer/songwriter [[Charli XCX]]. A live performance video for the song "Shhh" was released 17 November 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Raye - Shhh (Official Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=06aiE3dZ2q4|access-date=2020-07-12|website=Youtube.com}}</ref> Raye was featured on [[Jonas Blue]]'s single "[[By Your Side (Jonas Blue song)|By Your Side]]". It was released on 28 October 2016 and peaked at number 15 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. She also featured on [[Jax Jones]]' single "[[You Don't Know Me (Jax Jones song)|You Don't Know Me]]", released on 9 December 2016. The song, co-written by Raye herself, peaked at number three on the UK Singles Chart and has been certified double Platinum by the BPI. 

On 9 February 2017, Raye performed her first headline concert at [[XOYO]] in [[Shoreditch]], London.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://xoyo.co.uk/whats-on/|title=XOYO &#124; Whats on Shoreditch|website=Xoyo.co.uk|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref> Raye was featured alongside [[Starrah]] on the track "Dreamer" from [[Charli XCX]]'s ''[[Number 1 Angel]]'' mixtape, which was released on 10 March 2017. Stand-alone singles, "[[The Line (Raye song)|The Line]]" and "Sober (Stripped)" were released on 19 May 2017 and 4 August 2017 respectively. On 3 November 2017, Raye released "[[Decline (song)|Decline]]" featuring [[Mr Eazi]], which peaked at Number 15 on the UK Chart and has been certified Platinum in the same country. 

On 22 February 2018, Raye released the single titled "Cigarette" with [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]] and [[Stefflon Don]]. The song peaked at Number 41 on the UK Chart and has been certified Silver by the BPI. In March 2018, Raye collaborated with [[Kojo Funds]] and released "Check" which peaked at Number 26 on the UK Chart and has been certified Gold in the United Kingdom. Both "Decline" and "Cigarette" would appear on Raye's third [[extended play]], titled ''Side Tape'', which was released on 4 May 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Side Tape - EP by RAYE|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/side-tape-ep/1382754257|access-date=2020-07-12|website=Music.apple.com|language=en-gb}}</ref> A video for promotional single "Confidence" with [[Maleek Berry]] and Nana Rogues, was released 16 May 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Raye feat. Maleek Berry & Nana Rogues - 'Confidence'|url=https://www.capitalfm.com/artists/raye/confidence/|access-date=2020-07-12|website=Capitalfm.com|language=en}}</ref> Later in 2018, Raye released the stand-alone single titled, "Friends", which peaked at Number 66 on the UK Chart. In September 2018, Raye supported [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]] on the European leg of the [[Hopeless Fountain Kingdom Tour]]. In October 2018, Raye embarked on her own headlining tour, visiting a number of cities around the UK and Ireland.{{fact|date=June 2020}}

=== 2019–2020: ''Euphoric Sad Songs'' ===
In February 2019, Raye was featured on the song, "Tipsy" by Odunsi.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/tipsy-feat-raye-single/1450174228|title=Tipsy (feat. RAYE) - Single by Odunsi (The Engine)|website=Music.apple.com|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref> In April 2019, it was announced Raye would support [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] on his [[Free Spirit World Tour]] in Autumn 2019, alongside [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]]. Raye collaborated with [[David Guetta]] on the single "[[Stay (Don't Go Away)]]" which was released in May 2019 and peaked at Number 41 on the UK Chart. Raye was revealed to be a co-writer on the track "[[Bigger (Beyoncé song)|Bigger]]" sung by American singer [[Beyoncé]], which was featured on the soundtrack album ''[[The Lion King: The Gift]]'' released on 19 July 2019. In August 2019, Raye released the single "[[Love Me Again (Raye song)|Love Me Again]]" whilst a remix of the song with [[Jess Glynne]] was later released on 30 August 2019. The single later peaked at Number 55 on the UK Chart and has been certified Silver. Another Guetta collaboration, "[[Make It to Heaven]]" was later released on 22 November 2019 along with producer [[Morten Breum|Morten]]. Follow up promotional singles "Please Don't Touch" and "All of My Love", the latter featuring rapper [[Young Adz]], were released on 13 December 2019 and 6 February 2020 respectively.  

"[[Tequila (Jax Jones, Martin Solveig and Raye song)|Tequila]]" was released as a single in collaboration with DJs [[Jax Jones]] and [[Martin Solveig]] on 21 February 2020. The song peaked at Number 21 on the UK Chart as well as peaking inside the top 20 on the Ireland and Scotland charts. The track received a Gold certification in the UK. A collaboration with DJ [[Regard (DJ)|Regard]] titled "[[Secrets (Regard and Raye song)|Secrets]]" was released on 23 April 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/secrets-single/1506786986|title=Secrets - Single by Regard & RAYE|website=Music.apple.com|access-date=18 July 2020}}</ref> The song peaked at number 6 on the UK Singles Chart and topped the UK Dance Chart.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Official Singles Chart Top 100 {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/|access-date=2020-06-12|website=Officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Official Dance Singles Chart Top 40 {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/dance-singles-chart/|access-date=2020-06-12|website=Officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref> The song also reached the top 10 in Ireland and Scotland as well as on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart. "Secrets" charted in numerous countries such as the Netherlands, France and Germany making it Raye's first chart appearance as a lead artist. On 10 July 2020, Raye released the single "[[Natalie Don't]]", alongside a music video.<ref>[https://twitter.com/raye/status/1280909510966575104] {{dead link|date=July 2020}}</ref> In an interview with BBC News, Raye announced that her fifth EP titled, ''Her Heart Beats in 4/4'', would be released later that same year. The project would be centred around the theme of the "seven stages of grief" and will include previously released singles "Love Me Again" and "Natalie Don't".<ref>{{Cite news|last=Savage|first=Mark|date=2020-07-15|title=How updating Dolly Parton helped Raye find her voice|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-53403474|access-date=2020-07-16}}</ref> In an interview with [[Kate Thornton]] for her podcast White Wine Question Time, Raye said she wrote the album during each stage of grief and that ultimately the creative process had healed her broken heart. <ref>{{Cite web|title=RAYE reveals how her new EP helped heal her broken heart|url=https://uk.news.yahoo.com/raye-new-ep-helped-heal-broken-heart-060147193.html|access-date=2020-09-07|website=uk.news.yahoo.com|language=en-GB}}</ref> The project would later be retitled in favour for ''[[Euphoric Sad Songs]]''.    

On 6 November 2020, Raye released the single "Love of Your Life"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/raye-shares-new-disco-anthem-love-of-your-life|title=Raye Shares New Disco Anthem "Love of Your Life"|website=The Line of Best Fit|date=6 November 2020|access-date=19 March 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pressparty.com/pg/newsdesk/SimonJonesPR/view/233963/?isworld=y|title=Raye Releases The Stunning Video To "Love of Your Life"|website=Press Party|date=12 November 2020|access-date=19 March 2021}}</ref> and later announced the release of ''Euphoric Sad Songs'', which would include the previous released songs; "[[Love Me Again (Raye song)|Love Me Again]]", "Please Don't Touch", "[[Secrets (Regard and Raye song)|Secrets]]" and "[[Natalie Don't]]". The mini-album was released on 20 November 2020. The album's fourth official single, "[[Regardless (Raye and Rudimental song)|Regardless]]" with [[Rudimental]], was released alongside the mini-album, with a music video premiering on 8 January 2021. The single peaked inside the top 40 in the United Kingdom as well on the US Dance/Electronic Songs chart.

===2021: Departure from Polydor and upcoming debut studio album===

A collaborative single, "[[Bed (Joel Corry, Raye and David Guetta song)|Bed]]" with [[Joel Corry]] and [[David Guetta]], was released on 26 February 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title=iTunes - Music - BED - Single by Joel Corry, RAYE & David Guetta|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/bed-single/1552434655|publisher=[[iTunes Store]]}}</ref> The song would peak at number three on the UK Singles Chart, becoming Raye's highest charting single as a lead artist on the chart. On 11 June 2021, Raye released the single "Call On Me", which serves as the lead single for her upcoming debut studio album.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2021-06-11|title=Exclusive: RAYE on 'levelling up' with her massive new dance anthem Call On Me|url=https://www.gaytimes.co.uk/culture/exclusive-raye-on-levelling-up-with-her-massive-new-dance-anthem-call-on-me/|access-date=2021-06-11|website=GAY TIMES|language=en-GB}}</ref> Raye co-wrote and co-produced the song "[[Let Them Know]]" by [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]], which was released on 18 June 2021. On 29 June 2021, Raye revealed that her record label, [[Polydor Records]], has been withholding her debut album for several years.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-30|title='Tears and pain' as Polydor holds Raye debut album|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-57663925|access-date=2021-06-30|website=BBC News|language=en-GB}}</ref> The statement received support from fellow artists including [[Charli XCX]], [[MNEK]] and [[Rina Sawayama]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Clarke|first=Patrick|date=2021-06-30|title=RAYE says she hasn't "been allowed" to release her debut album|url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/news/music/raye-says-she-hasnt-been-allowed-to-release-her-debut-album-2981496|access-date=2021-06-30|website=NME|language=en}}</ref> Raye later went on a hiatus following the statements.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-04|title=Raye quits social media amid claims label won’t let her release debut album|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/raye-quits-social-media-claiming-label-wont-let-her-release-debut-album-2984993|access-date=2021-07-11|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref> On 19 July 2021, she announced via Instagram that she has parted ways with Polydor and is now working as an independent artist.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Login • Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CRhDJY8hvG9/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-19|website=www.instagram.com}}</ref>
