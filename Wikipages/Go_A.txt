'''Go_A''' ({{lang-uk|Ґоу_Ей}}) is a Ukrainian [[Folktronica|electro-folk]] band first formed in 2012 who were set to represent [[Ukraine in the Eurovision Song Contest 2020|Ukraine]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2020]] in [[Rotterdam]], [[The Netherlands|Netherlands]], with the song "[[Solovey (Go A song)|Solovey]]". Following the cancellation of the 2020 contest due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], the band represented the country in [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021|2021]], this time with "[[Shum (song)|Shum]]", with which they placed fifth in the final.

==History==
The idea to form a band that would combine modern electronic music with ethnic motifs first came to Taras Shevchenko in 2011, but the first attempts to work with actual musicians took place in 2012. At the end of that year, in December, the first song "Koliada" (Коляда) was released. The band's name means "return to the roots" and was made by combining the English word "Go" with the Greek letter "Alpha", which symbolizes the beginning of everything.<ref name="Vezha">{{Cite web|url=https://www.vezha.org/gurt-go-a/|title=Про гурт "Go-A" &#124; Телерадіокомпанія "ВЕЖА"|language=uk |access-date=22 February 2020}}</ref>

The band first gained attention after the release of the single "Vesnianka" (Веснянка), which won the national competition The Best Track in Ukraine 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://nightlife.tochka.net/articles/n/8270-konkurs-the-best-track-in-ukraine-2015-objavil-pob/ |title=Конкурс The Best Track in Ukraine 2015 объявил победителей! |language=ru |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=15 December 2015 |website=tochka.net  |access-date=25 February 2020 }}</ref> For six weeks, the song stayed at number one on the 10Dance chart of the Kiss FM radio station in Ukraine and was awarded Discovery of the Year by the radio station.<ref name="Vezha"/>

In the fall of 2016, Go_A released their debut album ''Idy na zvuk'' (Іди на звук; Follow the Tune) under [[Moon Records Ukraine|Moon Records]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://musicinua.com/elektrofolk-gurt-go-predstavyv-debyutnyj-albom/ |title=Електрофолк-гурт Go-A представив дебютний альбом |language=uk |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=2 November 2016 |website=musicinua.com  |access-date=26 February 2020 }}</ref> The album consists of ten songs, including the successful "Vesnianka".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://music.apple.com//album/ідиназвук/1181020206 |title=#Ідиназвук by Goa on Apple Music |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=29 November 2016 |publisher= [[Apple Music]] |access-date=25 February 2020 }}</ref> In early 2017 they released a Christmas single "Shchedryi vechir" (Щедрий вечір) in collaboration with [[Katya Chilly|Katia Chilly]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://112.international/ukraine-top-news/winners-of-ukraines-national-selection-for-eurovision-2020-ten-facts-about-goa-48837.html|title=Winners of Ukraine’s National Selection for Eurovision 2020: Ten facts about Go_A |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=23 February 2020 |publisher=[[112 Ukraine|112 International]] |access-date=25 February 2020 }}</ref>

==Eurovision Song Contest==
In early 2020, it was announced that Go_A was among the 16 acts participating in the [[Ukraine in the Eurovision Song Contest 2020|Ukrainian national selection for Eurovision 2020]] with the song '"[[Solovey (Go A song)|Solovey]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ivona.bigmir.net/showbiz/news/482762-Nacotbor-na--Evrovidenie-2020---Gruppa-Go-A-prezentovala-konkursnuju-pesnju|title=Нацотбор на “Евровидение 2020”: Группа Go_A презентовала конкурсную песню|author=<!--Not stated-->|date=28 January 2020|website=ivona.bigmir.net|language=ru|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> The band performed in the first semi-final, which took place on February 8, where they placed second and thus qualified to the final. On February 22, in the final they won both the public vote and the jury vote. Hence, they were supposed to represent Ukraine at the Eurovision Song Contest 2020 in May.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eurovision.tv/story/ukraine-national-selection-vidbir-2020|title=Go_A will represent Ukraine in Rotterdam|author=<!--Not stated-->|date=22 February 2020|website=[[Eurovision.tv]]|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> However, on 18 March 2020, the event was cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. On the same day [[UA:PBC]] announced that the band will represent the country in [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021|2021]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://eurovoix.com/2020/03/18/ukraine-goa-eurovision-2021/|title=Ukraine: Go_A Will Represent Ukraine at Eurovision 2021|last=Herbert|first=Emily|date=18 March 2020|website=Eurovoix|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200318185448/https://eurovoix.com/2020/03/18/ukraine-goa-eurovision-2021/|archive-date=18 March 2020|access-date=20 March 2020}}</ref> On 4 February 2021, it was announced that "[[Shum (song)|SHUM]]" would be the song that Go_A would be performing in Rotterdam. At the Eurovision final on 22 May 2021, SHUM placed fifth, with 364 combined public and jury points. However, in the public vote alone SHUM placed second with 267 points.<ref name="Eurovision2021">{{Cite web|date=2021-02-04|title=Latest news: Go_A has new song for Eurovision 2021!|url=https://eurovision.tv/story/latest-news-february-2021|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210204142321/https://eurovision.tv/story/latest-news-february-2021|archive-date=2021-02-04|access-date=2021-02-04|website=[[Eurovision.tv]]|publisher=[[European Broadcasting Union|EBU]]}}</ref>
