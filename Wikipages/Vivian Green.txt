'''Vivian Sakiyyah Green'''<ref>{{cite web|title=Vivian Green – biography|url=http://www.soulwalking.co.uk/Vivian%20Green.html|website=soulwalking.com|publisher=Soul Walking|access-date=May 3, 2015}}</ref> (born May 22, 1979)<ref>{{cite web|last1=Brown|first1=Tony|title=Happy Birthday Shirley Murdock, Donell Jones, Johnny Gill, Leela James, Vivian Green, and Avery Sunshine!|url=http://wdasfm.iheart.com/onair/tony-brown-the-quiet-storm-4353/happy-birthday-shirley-murdock-donell-jones-13614485/|website=wdasfm.iheart.com|publisher=iHeartMedia, Inc.|access-date=April 10, 2016}}</ref> is an American [[R&B]] singer-songwriter and pianist.

== Early life ==
Green was born May 22, 1979 in the [[East Oak Lane, Philadelphia|East Oak Lane]] neighborhood of [[Philadelphia]], and took an interest in singing, playing the [[piano]], and songwriting at a very young age. At the age of thirteen, she became a member of a female [[quintet]] called Younique. She is a graduate of what is now Parkway Northwest High School for Peace and Social Justice.

Green has credit for writing "Dear God" by [[Boyz II Men]], from their 1997 album ''[[Evolution (Boyz II Men album)|Evolution]]''.<ref name="You Know I Got Soul">{{cite web|title=Interview: Vivian Green Talks New album "Vivid", Fresh Energized Sound, Signing With Kwame|url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/interview-vivian-green-talks-new-album-vivid-fresh-energized-sound-signing-with-kwame|website=youknowigotsoul.com|publisher=You Know I Got Soul|access-date=July 12, 2015}}</ref> Green received her big break at the age of nineteen when she became a [[backing vocalist|backup singer]] for [[Jill Scott]], who took her on an international tour.<ref name="You Know I Got Soul" /> She signed to [[Columbia Records]] in late 2000.

== Career ==

=== 2001–2003: ''A Love Story'' ===
In 2002, Green released her debut album, ''[[A Love Story (Vivian Green album)|A Love Story]]'', which featured the number-one single "[[Emotional Rollercoaster (song)|Emotional Rollercoaster]]". In the meantime, Green made a cameo appearance in the [[Cole Porter]] biopic ''[[De-Lovely]]'' singing a [[cover version]] of Porter's 1930 song "[[Love for Sale (song)|Love for Sale]]", which made the film's soundtrack album. She also played [[Brenda Holloway]] in an episode of the first season of [[NBC]]'s drama series ''[[American Dreams]]'', entitled "The Carpetbaggers" (originally aired on April 6, 2003), performing a rendition of Holloway's 1964 hit "[[Every Little Bit Hurts]]".

=== 2004–2006: ''Vivian'' ===
In 2005, she appeared on [[Cyndi Lauper]]'s ''[[The Body Acoustic]]'' album on the tracks "I'll Be Your River" and "[[Sisters of Avalon (song)|Sisters of Avalon]]".

During the spring of 2005, three years after the release of her debut album, Green released her second studio album ''[[Vivian (Vivian Green album)|Vivian]]''. It featured the lead single "[[Gotta Go Gotta Leave (Tired)]]", which peaked at No. 24 on the R&B chart and No. 1 on the dance charts The song also charted No. 1 on the Hot Adult Airplay.<ref>[{{BillboardURLbyName|artist=vivian green|chart=Adult R&B Airplay}} Vivian Green – Gotta Go, Gotta Leave (Tired)] Billboard. Retrieved:February 11, 2006</ref> The track known as "I Like It (But I Don't Need It)" followed up as the album's second single. The song followed up the previous single's success by also reaching #1 on the dance charts.

=== 2007–2011: ''Beautiful'' ===
In 2007, Green appeared on [[Guru (rapper)|Guru]]'s album ''[[Jazzmatazz, Vol. 4: The Hip-Hop Jazz Messenger: Back to the Future]]'' on the track "Fine and Free". While performing at a gig in support of her second album, Green announced that she is currently recording her third full-length album on [[Koch Records]]. In April 2009, she signed a 3-album deal with [[E1 Music]] with the first album under the deal set to be released in Summer 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.marketwire.com/press-release/E1-Music-AIM-ETO-970395.html |title=E1 Music Signs R&B Sensation Vivian Green |access-date=April 14, 2009 |date=April 3, 2009}}</ref> Her third album titled ''[[Beautiful (Vivian Green album)|Beautiful]]'' was released April 6, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.viviangreen.com/ |title=Vivian Green |publisher=Vivian Green |access-date=June 30, 2016}}</ref> The songs explore the ins and outs of love from a variety of perspectives, from the youthful romanticism of "Somewhere" to the playful insight of "So Good" and "Better Man" to the hard-won intensity of "Masterpiece" and "Beautiful."

In contrast to her first two albums, which were recorded in a variety of studios with multiple producers, Vivian approached the recording of Beautiful in a more intimate, organic manner.  With the exception of "Save Me," which she cut with Jason Farmer ([[Keyshia Cole]], [[Wyclef Jean]], [[Rihanna]]) in the producer's seat, Vivian recorded the entire album with Grammy-nominated producer Anthony Bell, a longtime friend and collaborator who made key production contributions to her first two albums, and whose extensive resume also includes work with [[Jazmine Sullivan]], [[Jewel (singer)|Jewel]], [[Musiq]], [[Raheem DeVaughn]] and [[Jill Scott]].

In 2011, she collaborated with Phoe Notes for a winter single titled "Missing You". In 2013, she undertook a few festivals and concerts in addition to TV appearances on [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]] and [[BET Her|Centric]]'s music series.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://singersroom.com/content/2013-08-07/Jill-Scott-and-Mary-Mary-Set-For-Season-3-of-Verses-and-Flow/ |title=Jill Scott and Mary Mary Set For Season 3 of 'Verses and Flow' |website=Singersroom.com |date=August 7, 2013 |access-date=June 30, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.centrictv.com/lifestyle/culturelist/?tag=vivian-green |title=Fashion, Beauty, Trends and Style |website=Blogs.centrictv.com |date=July 22, 2013 |access-date=June 30, 2016}}</ref>

=== 2014–2016:  ''Vivid'' ===
In 2014, Green became the first artist signed to rapper/producer [[Kwamé]]'s record label, Make Noise Recordings. ''[[Vivid (Vivian Green album)|Vivid]]'', her fifth studio album was distributed via [[Caroline Records]]. On April 7, 2015, the first single released from the album was "Get Right Back to My Baby". The song went to No. 2 on Billboard's UAC chart. In 2015, Green was nominated for a [[Soul Train Award]]. In 2016, she released "Grown Folks Music (Work)", which went to the top 15 on Billboard's UAC chart.

=== 2017–2019: ''VGVI'' ===
On July 7, 2017, Green released the first single off her sixth album ''VGVI'', titled "I Don't Know". The song became a top-10 hit on Billboard's Adult R&B chart. ''VGVI'' was released on October 6, 2017.<ref name="You Know I Got Soul" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Vivian Green – news article|url=http://caroline.com/?page=news_item&NewsID=3765647630851&last_page=home|website=caroline.com|publisher=Caroline Records|access-date=May 3, 2015}}</ref>

=== 2020–Present: ''Love Absolute'' ===
In October 2019, Green and Kwamé signed with the SoNo Recording Group through Kwamé's record label, Make Noise Recordings. Her seventh studio album, ''Love Absolute'', was released on November 13, 2020.

== Personal life ==
Between her first and second albums, she took a three-year hiatus. In 2004, she gave birth to her son Jordan. Green became an advocate for [[special needs]] children, publicly speaking about her experiences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hellobeautiful.com/2010/04/27/video-vivian-green-discusses-raising-a-special-needs-child/ |title=VIDEO: Vivian Green Discusses Raising A Special Needs Child |website=HelloBeautiful.com |date=April 27, 2010 |access-date=June 30, 2016}}</ref>
