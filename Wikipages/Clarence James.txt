'''Clarence Eldridge James''', [[CBE]] (August 27, 1931 â€“ April 16, 2016) was a Bermudian [[surgeon]] and politician. He was a member of the [[House of Assembly of Bermuda]], representing the Pembroke West Central constituency for the former the [[United Bermuda Party]] (UBP), for 21 years from 1968 to 1989.<ref name=rgazette>{{cite news|first=Owain |last=Johnston-Barnes|title='Truly loved' former Deputy Premier dies |url=http://www.royalgazette.com/news/article/20160417/truly-loved-former-deputy-premier-dies |work=[[The Royal Gazette (Bermuda)|Royal Gazette]] |date=2016-04-17 |accessdate=2016-05-08}}</ref> He served as the head of several government ministries, including Minister of Transport, Minister of Finance (becoming the first [[Black Bermudian]] to lead the Ministry of Finance), and Minister of Health.<ref name=rgazette/> He then served as Deputy Premier of Bermuda from 1983 to 1989, during the government of [[Premier of Bermuda|Premier]] [[John Swan (Bermudian politician)|John Swan]].<ref name=rgazette/>

James was born in Bermuda in August 1931.<ref name=rgazette/> He married his wife, Shirley, with whom he had three children: Joanne, Robert, and Charles.<ref name=rgazette/>
