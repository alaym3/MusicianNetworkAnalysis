'''Taylor C. McLendon''' (born 1995), known professionally as '''Ivy Sole''', is an American rapper and record producer from [[Philadelphia|Philadelphia, PA]]. Ivy Sole has been a member of Indigold, Liberal Art, and Third Eye Optiks.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Ivy Sole Is the Philly Rapper Who Wants You to Forgive Yourself|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/xwpw73/ivy-sole-overgrown-interview|last=Corry|first=Kristen|date=September 24, 2018|website=Vice|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200424184332/https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/xwpw73/ivy-sole-overgrown-interview|archive-date=April 24, 2020|access-date=April 24, 2020}}</ref> Ivy Sole has been mentioned as an example of the growing movement of non-binary artists.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chesman|first=Donna-Claire|date=January 7, 2019|title=5 Women in Hip-Hop to Watch in 2019|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2019-01-07-five-women-in-hip-hop-2019|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200428030042/https://djbooth.net/features/2019-01-07-five-women-in-hip-hop-2019|archive-date=April 28, 2020|access-date=April 27, 2020|website=DJBooth|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/01/what-experts-think-is-next-for-rap|title=What’s Next in Rap? Experts Predict What Hip-Hop Will Sound Like in 2018|last=Setaro|first=Shawn|date=January 16, 2018|website=Complex|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180308125411/https://www.complex.com/music/2018/01/what-experts-think-is-next-for-rap|archive-date=March 8, 2018|access-date=April 27, 2020}}</ref>

== Early life ==
McLendon grew up [[queer]] in a [[Southern Baptist Church]] and received all of their vocal training in a church setting. McLendon was raised in a predominantly black neighborhood, but attended a predominantly white magnet school.<ref name=":1" />

A week after her 18th birthday, McLendon attended a [[Mac Miller]] concert featuring [[Rapsody]] and [[Nicki Minaj]], which they cites as her inspiration for pursuing a musical career.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8475975/ivy-sole-interview-overgrown|title=Ivy Sole Talks Growing Up Queer in the Southern Baptist Church & Mac Miller's Impact: 'He Bridged Generations'|last=Kuga|first=Mitchell|date=September 19, 2018|website=Billboard|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200424183929/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8475975/ivy-sole-interview-overgrown|archive-date=April 24, 2020|access-date=April 24, 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==
Ivy Sole began her musical career by joining three different collaborations called Indigold, Liberal Art, and Third Eye Optiks.<ref name=":0" />

In 2016, Ivy Sole began her solo career with the debut mixtape entitled ''Eden'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2016/04/14/on-the-rise-ivy-sole-bares-it-all-on-her-debut-ep-eden/|title=The Source {{!}}On The Rise: Ivy Sole Bares It All On Her Debut EP, 'EDEN'|last=Victoria|first=Da'ryl|date=April 14, 2016|website=The Source|language=en-US|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200428024633/https://thesource.com/2016/04/14/on-the-rise-ivy-sole-bares-it-all-on-her-debut-ep-eden/|archive-date=April 27, 2020|access-date=April 27, 2020}}</ref> which would be followed by the extended plays ''West'' and then ''East''.<ref name=":0" /> [[NPR]] noted that Ivy Sole tackles mental health issues in her music video for the track entitled "Life" off of the extended play ''East''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2017/06/01/531030253/ivy-soles-life-reassures-that-pain-is-temporary|title=Ivy Sole's 'Life' Reassures That Pain Is Temporary|last=Carmichael|first=Rodney|date=June 1, 2017|website=NPR|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200424185828/https://www.npr.org/2017/06/01/531030253/ivy-soles-life-reassures-that-pain-is-temporary|archive-date=April 24, 2020|access-date=April 24, 2020}}</ref>

In 2018, Ivy Sole released her debut full-length album entitled ''Overgrown''.<ref name=":0" /> [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] did a review of the track entitled "Achilles" off of the album ''Overgrown''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/ivy-sole-achilles/|title=Ivy Sole "Achilles"|last=Horn|first=Olivia|date=September 24, 2018|website=Pitchfork|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200424185339/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/ivy-sole-achilles/|archive-date=April 24, 2020|access-date=April 24, 2020}}</ref>

In 2020, a live recording of ''Overgrown'' entitled ''Overgrown* (Live from Philadelphia)'' was released.

== Personal life ==
McLendon moved to Philadelphia in 2011 to attend the [[Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania]]. She graduated with the Class of 2015 with a degree in business.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.inquirer.com/philly/entertainment/music/ivy-sole-wharton-pe-20170807.html|title=Why Wharton grad Ivy Sole is the hip-hop artist you should be listening to|last=Heinzerling|first=Kelly|date=August 7, 2017|website=The Philadelphia Inquirer|language=en-US|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200114155417/https://www.inquirer.com/philly/entertainment/music/ivy-sole-wharton-pe-20170807.html|archive-date=January 14, 2020|access-date=April 27, 2020}}</ref>

Describing her sexual orientation to ''Billboard'', McLendon stated: "I like women and I like men. I like women a lot though. Women have me on my ass, which is rare -- I feel like I’m a very calm and collected person.”<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=2018-09-19|title=Ivy Sole Talks Growing Up Queer in the Southern Baptist Church & Mac Miller's Impact: 'He Bridged Generations'|url=http://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/news/pride/8475975/ivy-sole-interview-overgrown|access-date=2021-06-23|website=www.billboard.com|language=en}}</ref>
