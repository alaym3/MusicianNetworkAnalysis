'''Volcano Choir''' is an American [[indie rock]] band from [[Wisconsin]], formed in 2005. It has released two albums, ''[[Unmap]]'' and ''[[Repave]].''

==History==
The band began as a collaboration between [[Bon Iver]]'s [[Justin Vernon]] and members of [[Collections of Colonies of Bees]]. Their debut album, ''[[Unmap]]'', was released on September 22, 2009, reaching number 92 on the [[Billboard Top 200]] [[record chart|chart]].<ref name=":0">{{cite magazine|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=volcano choir|chart=all}}|title=Volcano Choir - Chart History|magazine=Billboard.com|access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref> The band toured on the success of this release, with a large string of shows around Japan in November 2010 being their most notable run.<ref>{{cite web |title=Volcano Choir Setlist |url=https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/volcano-choir/2010/shibuya-o-west-tokyo-japan-5bd28bb0.html |website=Setlist.fm |publisher=Setlist.fm |access-date=9 November 2018 |date=8 November 2010}}</ref>

Since the band's formation, Jon Mueller (Death Blues) and Thomas Wincek (of All Tiny Creatures) have left Collections of Colonies of Bees, but continue to be members of Volcano Choir.<ref>{{cite web|last=Hyden |first=Steven |url=http://www.avclub.com/milwaukee/articles/jon-mueller-leaves-collections-of-colonies-of-bees,43052/ |title=Jon Mueller leaves Collections Of Colonies Of Bees ... by the end of the year &#124; Music &#124; The A.V. Club Milwaukee |publisher=Avclub.com |date=2010-07-13 |access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Shanahan |first=Joel |url=http://www.avclub.com/articles/all-tiny-creatures-thomas-wincek,45725/ |title=All Tiny Creatures' Thomas Wincek &#124; Music &#124; Interview |newspaper=The A.V. Club |date=2010-10-06 |access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref> They have also added Matthew Skemp on bass, who also plays in All Tiny Creatures.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jagjaguwar.com/blog/2013/05/volcano-choir-are-back/ |title=Volcano Choir Are Back! New Album ' REPAVE' Out In September, New Tour Dates Set For Summer & Fall Blog |publisher=Jagjaguwar |date=2013-05-28 |access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref>

It was revealed in a 2014 interview with band members that the name "Volcano Choir" originated from a [[vaporizer (inhalation device)|vaporizer]] called the "Volcano".<ref>{{cite video |url= http://www.bdcwire.com/boston-calling-volcano-choir-interview/boston-calling-volcano-choir-interview-2 |title= Boston Calling: Volcano Choir Interview |publisher= BDCWire |date= 2015-02-26 |access-date=2018-06-30}}</ref>

The cover art for ''Unmap'' was created by Adreienne DeBoer, and insert art was illustrated by [[Dave Godowsky]].

Volcano Choir released their second studio album, ''[[Repave]]'',<ref name="Wolfe">Jake Wolfe (2013-09-13). "[http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/volcano-choir-review You Should Definitely Listen To The New Volcano Choir Album]". Retrieved 2013-09-05.</ref> on September 3, 2013, on [[Jagjaguwar]]. The album was recorded at Vernon's April Base studio in [[Eau Claire, Wisconsin]].<ref>{{cite web |title=April Base Publishing |url=https://www.discogs.com/label/325955-April-Base-Publishing |website=Discogs |publisher=Discogs |access-date=4 November 2018}}</ref>

Andrew Fitzpatrick from All Tiny Creatures became a touring member in 2013, supporting the album ''Repave'' and is now a member of the band. He then became a touring member of [[Bon Iver]], supporting the album ''[[22, A Million]]''.

Thomas Wincek joined [[Field Report]] in 2014.

Volcano Choir's song "Byegone" was used in the 4th episode of the first season of ''[[The 100 (TV series)|The 100]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/the-100/season-1/17928|title=Music from The 100 S1E04|website=Tunefind}}</ref>

The band finished touring in support of ''Repave'' in November 2014,<ref>{{cite web |title=Volcano Choir at Turner Hall Ballroom, Milwaukee, WI, USA |url=https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/volcano-choir/2014/turner-hall-ballroom-milwaukee-wi-3cde5cb.html |website=Setlist.FM |access-date=10 July 2019}}</ref> and have since been on hiatus as Vernon focuses on Bon Iver.
