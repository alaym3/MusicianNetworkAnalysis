'''Porridge Radio''' are a British [[indie rock]] band formed in [[Brighton]] in 2015.<ref name=vice/> They are fronted by vocalist, songwriter and lead guitarist Dana Margolin.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Rankine |first1=Ollie |title=Porridge Radio – A DIY band learn to take their time |url=https://www.loudandquiet.com/interview/porridge-radio-a-diy-band-learn-to-take-their-time/ |work=[[Loud and Quiet]] |date=31 May 2019}}</ref> The other members are keyboardist Georgie Stott, bass guitarist Maddie Ryall and drummer Sam Yardley.<ref name=vice>{{cite news |last1=O'Neill |first1=Lauren |title=Porridge Radio's Indie-Pop Is Wild, Contradictory and *So* Great |url=https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/vb9dxd/porridge-radio-interview-2019-dont-ask-me-twice |work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |date=1 May 2019 |language=en}}</ref>

''[[The Guardian]]'' listed them among their top 40 new artists of 2018 and described their music as 'slacker [[indie rock|indie]]'.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Aroesti |first1=Rachel |last2=Beaumont-Thomas |first2=Ben |title=From shamstep to drill: the top 40 newcomers of 2018 |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/dec/28/from-shamstep-to-drill-the-top-40-newcomers-of-2018 |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=28 December 2017}}</ref>

They performed a live session for [[Marc Riley]]'s show on [[BBC Radio 6 Music]] in May 2019 and again in February 2020.<ref>{{cite episode |network=BBC Radio|station=6 Music|series=Marc Riley|title=Porridge Radio Live In Session |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/m0004t51|date=7 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite episode |network=BBC Radio|station=6 Music|series=Marc Riley|title=Porridge Radio Live In Session |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/m000fgwq|date=18 February 2020}}</ref>

They announced signing to US independent label [[Secretly Canadian]] in December 2019.<ref name=clash>{{cite news |last=Murray |first=Robin |title=Porridge Radio Sign To Secretly Canadian, Share New Single 'Lilac' |url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/porridge-radio-sign-to-secretly-canadian-share-new-single-lilac |work=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]] |date=5 December 2019}}</ref> In January 2020, they announced their second album and their label debut, ''[[Every Bad]]'', which was released on 13 March 2020 to critical acclaim.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/porridge-radio-announce-new-album-every-bad-single-sweet-video-2597566|title=Porridge Radio announce new album 'Every Bad' and share grungy video for ‘Sweet’|website=Nme.com|date=January 16, 2020}}</ref> Later that year they released three singles; "Good For You" was co-released on 2 July 2020 with the artist [[Lala Lala]], "7 Seconds" was released on 14 September 2020, and "The Last Time I Saw You (O Christmas)" was released on 8 December 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Subscribe|title=Porridge Radio team up with Lala Lala for 'Good For You' {{!}} News|url=http://diymag.com/2020/07/02/listen-porridge-radio-lala-lala-good-for-you|access-date=2021-02-05|website=diymag.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hussey|first=Allison|title=Porridge Radio Share New Song "7 Seconds"|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/porridge-radio-share-new-song-7-seconds-listen/|access-date=2021-02-05|website=Pitchfork|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Strauss|first=Matthew|title=Listen to Porridge Radio’s New Song "The Last Time I Saw You (O Christmas)"|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/listen-to-porridge-radio-new-song-the-last-time-i-saw-you-o-christmas/|access-date=2021-02-05|website=Pitchfork|language=en-us}}</ref>

==Formation==

Porridge Radio was started by Dana Margolin, who prior to starting the band was attending open mics and songwriting in her bedroom. She formed Porridge Radio as a way to express herself by using inspiration from the sea and her seaside home of Brighton. The band was formed with Maddie Ryall on bass, Georgie Stott on keyboard, and Sam Yardley on drums.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Richards|first=Will|date=2020-01-16|title=Band To Watch: Porridge Radio|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2069874/porridge-radio-every-bad-sweet/interviews/band-to-watch/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-02|website=[[Stereogum]]|language=en}}</ref>
