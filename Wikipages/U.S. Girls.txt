'''U.S. Girls''' is a Toronto-based [[experimental pop]] project formed in 2007, consisting solely of American musician and record producer '''Meghan Remy'''.<ref name=noisey>[http://noisey.vice.com/blog/us-girls-meg-remy-interview-toronto-pop-2015 "U.S. Girls’ Meg Remy Is an American Badass Living in Canada"]. ''[[Noisey]]'', September 24, 2015.</ref> She had released music on a variety of independent record labels before signing to [[4AD]] in 2015.<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/apr/17/new-band-of-the-week-us-girls-no-50 "New band of the Week: U.S. Girls"]. ''[[The Guardian]]'', April 17, 2015.</ref>

''[[Half Free]]'', her first record for 4AD, was released the same year.<ref>[http://www.undertheradarmag.com/interviews/u.s._girls "U.S. Girls: Soaking It All In"]. ''[[Under the Radar (magazine)|Under the Radar]]'', February 4, 2016.</ref> It garnered a [[Juno Award]] nomination for [[Juno Award for Alternative Album of the Year|Alternative Album of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2016]],<ref>[http://www.cbc.ca/news/arts/juno-nominees-list-2016-1.3430406 "Junos 2016: Complete list of Juno Award nominees"]. [[CBC News]], February 2, 2016.</ref> and was a shortlisted finalist for the [[2016 Polaris Music Prize]].<ref name="shortlist">{{cite web |title=2016 Polaris Music Short List Is Here |url=http://polarismusicprize.ca/blog/2016-polaris-music-short-list-is-here/ |last=Brophy |first=Aaron |date=14 July 2016 |website=Polarismusicprize.ca |access-date=15 January 2017}}</ref>

Remy collaborates with a number of Toronto-based musicians on both songwriting and music production.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Hua|first1=Hsu|title=U.S. Girls’ Collection of Characters|url=https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2018/03/05/us-girls-collection-of-characters|magazine=[[The New Yorker]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Berman|first1=Stuart|title=Welcome to the U.S. Girls Universe|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/welcome-to-the-us-girls-universe-listening-guide/|website=Pitchfork.com|access-date=3 March 2018}}</ref>

== Background ==
Remy grew up in Illinois and attended a Catholic high school. She was raised mostly by her mother. She was in her first punk band as a teenager. She cites [[Riot grrrl]] and [[Crass]] as some of her early influences.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/oct/19/us-girls-meghan-remy-halt-free|title=US Girls: ‘I like to do the opposite of everything that is making money’|last=O'Connell|first=Sharon|date=2015-10-19|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|language=en|access-date=2018-03-05}}</ref>

She attended an art college in Oregon, concentrating on paper arts and graphic design.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://adhoc.fm/post/interview-us-girls/|title=Artist Profile: U.S. Girls|website=AdHoc.fm|language=en|access-date=2018-03-05}}</ref>

The name "U.S. Girls" originated from a casual conversation she was having with a friend talking about a European band coming to town. She joked, "Wait 'til they get a look at these U.S. girls!" and the phrase stuck.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/the-out-door/7804-the-out-door-3/?page=3|title=Losing the Little Labels {{!}} Pitchfork|website=Pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2018-03-05}}</ref>

== Early career ==
Remy began making music in the mid-2000s, playing in bands in Chicago and Portland. In 2008 she started recording solo at home.<ref name="auto"/> In 2018, Remy's sixth studio album, ''[[In a Poem Unlimited]],'' was released on 4AD''.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://4ad.com/|title=The official website for independent record label 4AD.|website=4ad.com|access-date=April 17, 2020}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Remy later moved the band to [[Toronto]] from [[Chicago]] in 2010 after marrying Canadian musician [[Slim Twig|Max "Slim Twig" Turnbull]].<ref>[https://nowtoronto.com/music/features/u-s-girls/ "U.S. Girls: focusing on the darkness in many women's lives"]. ''[[Now (newspaper)|Now]]'', December 9, 2015.</ref> Alongside Turnbull, she operates record label Calico. Corp, and sometimes performs as a guest vocalist with Turnbull's [[Badge Époque Ensemble]].<ref>Stuart Berman, [https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/badge-epoque-ensemble-self-help/ "Badge Époque Ensemble, Self Help"]. ''[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]'', November 21, 2020.</ref>

She is a permanent resident of Canada.
