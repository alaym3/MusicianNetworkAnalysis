'''Lynda Tait Randle''' (born February 7,<ref name="Birthday">{{cite web|author=Lynda Tait Randle (@LyndaTaitRandle) |title=I am so blessed to be celebrating another BIrthday today! God has been so good to me and I know that the Best is still yet to come! Yeah!!! |url=https://twitter.com/LyndaTaitRandle/status/166909800419033088 |publisher=Twitter |access-date=May 10, 2016 |date=February 7, 2012}}{{Primary source inline|date=February 2020}}</ref> 1962), is an [[alto]] singer of [[southern gospel]] music.

==Early years==
The daughter of Nate and Maxine Tait,<ref name="ww">{{cite news |last1=Brown |first1=Vicki |title=Lynda Randle shares story, performs at HLGU |url=https://wordandway.org/2013/11/27/lynda-randle-shares-story-performs-at-hlgu/ |access-date=May 3, 2020 |work=Word&Way |date=November 27, 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20200503010436/https://wordandway.org/2013/11/27/lynda-randle-shares-story-performs-at-hlgu/ |archive-date=May 3, 2020}}</ref> Randle grew up in the inner city of Washington, DC. Her bi-vocational minister father drove a cab. Public schools provided her education through the eighth grade, after which she attended Riverdale Baptist School. Her choir teacher there persuaded her to enhance her solo talents. In 1989 she graduated from [[Liberty University]].<ref name="gsg">{{cite book |last1=Harper |first1=Ryan P. |title=The Gaithers and Southern Gospel: Homecoming in the Twenty-First Century |date=2017 |publisher=Univ. Press of Mississippi |isbn=978-1-4968-1093-9 |page=224 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ZhmSDgAAQBAJ&q=%22Lynda+Randle%22&pg=PA224 |access-date=May 3, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
Randle is known as a [[Gaither Homecoming#Homecoming Artists|Gaither Homecoming]] artist since she was recruited by Bill Gaither in 1998.<ref name="bb" /> She also markets a number of singing videos<ref>[http://www.martinrothonline.com/sgcentral/artists/LyndaRandle.htm Southern Gospel Beat on Lynda Randle.]</ref> featuring her mellow alto voice in [[gospel music]], particularly southern gospel.

She also heads Lynda Randle Ministries in [[Kansas City, Missouri]]. Randle on March 21, 2009, became one of the main artists featured by [[Feed the Children]] as a [[fundraising]] solicitation for contributions.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20100617015624/http://www.feedthechildren.org/site/PageServer?pagename=usw_randle Randle on Feed the Children web site.]</ref>

Randle wrote a children's book, ''Cab Driver’s Daughter'', that is partly autobiographical.<ref name="ww" />

==Personal life==
Randle and her husband, Michael, live in [[Liberty, Missouri]], where they settled after their 1989 marriage.<ref name="gsg" /> They have two daughters, Patience and Joy.<ref>[http://lyndarandle.weebly.com/ Lynda Randle’s official web site with photos.]</ref> Randle has four sisters. Her brother is [[Michael Tait]] of [[dc Talk]] and [[Newsboys]].<ref>http://www.lyndarandle.com/testimonials.html</ref>

==Recognition==
Randle's ''A Tribute to Mahalia Jackson'' recording won a [[GMA Dove Award]] for traditional gospel album.<ref name=bb>{{cite news |last1=Price |first1=Deborah Evans |title=Randle's on her way |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nBQEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22Lynda+Randle%22&pg=PA44 |access-date=May 3, 2020 |work=Billboard |date=May 7, 2005 |page=44}}</ref>
