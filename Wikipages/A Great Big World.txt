'''A Great Big World''' is an American musical duo from New York<ref>{{cite news | last =Lovett | first =Matthew | title =Preview: A Great big World At Rumba | newspaper =Columbus Alive | date = 23 May 2013| url =http://www.columbusalive.com/content/stories/2013/05/23/preview-a-great-big-world-at-rumba.html | access-date =4 June 2013 }}</ref> made up of singer/songwriters [[Ian Axel]] and Chad King and signed to [[Epic Records]]. The group is best known for their single "This Is the New Year", which was performed by the cast in an episode of ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]'' and reached the [[Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)|''Billboard'' Mainstream Top 40]] chart in May 2013,<ref>{{cite magazine | last =Caulfield | first =Keith | title =Bubbling Under: Passenger's Worldwide Ride Continues | magazine =Billboard Magazine | date = 17 May 2013| url =http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/chart-alert/1562813/bubbling-under-passengers-worldwide-ride-continues | access-date =4 June 2013 }}</ref> and their international hit "[[Say Something (A Great Big World song)|Say Something]]", particularly after recording it as a duet collaboration with [[Christina Aguilera]].

==Career==

===Early beginnings===
[[Ian Axel]] and Chad King met at [[New York University]], where they were attending the Steinhardt Music Program.<ref name="RockRevoltMay4">{{cite news|title=A Great Big World To Release Three Song EP May 21 |newspaper=Rock Revolt Magazine |date=4 May 2013 |url=http://www.rockrevoltmagazine.com/a-great-big-world-to-release-three-song-ep-may-21/ |access-date=7 May 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130507183047/http://www.rockrevoltmagazine.com/a-great-big-world-to-release-three-song-ep-may-21/ |archive-date=7 May 2013 }}</ref> Both were music business students and Axel is said to have convinced King to write a song together. King agreed to work with Axel after hearing him sing. They both wrote and performed songs together prior to Axel embarking on a solo career in which he released an independent album featuring songs that were all co-written by King.<ref name="RockRevoltMay4"/>

===2011–2013: Breakthrough and ''Glee''===
In 2011, their song "This Is the New Year" was licensed to various television networks and was used as the theme song for [[MTV]]'s ''[[I Used to Be Fat]]'' as well as being featured on ''[[The Amazing Race]]'', [[ESPN]], [[One Tree Hill (TV series)|''One Tree Hill'']], and ''[[Good Morning America]]''.<ref name="ExaminerBigMoves"/> Ian Axel and Chad King went on national tour and performed as the openers for [[Ingrid Michaelson]], [[Matthew Morrison]] (''Glee''), and [[Five for Fighting]].<ref name="RockRevoltMay4"/>

The duo re-branded itself in 2012, when they took the official name ''A Great Big World''. They released a six-song EP after obtaining funding from the crowdsource funding website [[Kickstarter]].<ref name="ExaminerBigMoves">{{cite news | last =Gibson | first =Yolanda | title =A Great Big World Is Making Moves This New Year| newspaper =Examiner | date =7 May 2013 }}</ref>

In 2013, "This Is the New Year" was performed by the cast in an [[Naked (Glee)|episode]] of [[Glee (TV series)|''Glee'']].<ref name="RockRevoltMay4"/>

===2013–2014: ''Is There Anybody Out There?''===
[[File:A Great Big World Performing Live 2 (cropped).jpg|thumb|right|A Great Big World performing in April 2014]]
In 2013, the group signed with [[Epic Records]] and released a three-song EP under the Epic label in May 2013.<ref name="ArtistDirect">{{cite news | title =A Great Big World To Release "This Is The New Year" on May 21 | newspaper =Artist Direct | date =10 May 2013 | url =http://www.artistdirect.com/entertainment-news/article/a-great-big-world-to-release-three-song-ep-on-may-21/10550113 | access-date =4 June 2013 }}</ref> They completed a headline tour in conjunction with their EP release.<ref name="AnnexMag">{{cite news | last =Pikka | title =Great Big World | newspaper =Annex Magazine | date =2 May 2013 | url =http://www.annexmagazine.com/great-big-world/#sthash.EBQRnOfL.ZfHT7U4Y.dpbs | access-date =7 May 2013 | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20130525055836/http://www.annexmagazine.com/great-big-world/#sthash.EBQRnOfL.ZfHT7U4Y.dpbs | archive-date =2013-05-25 | url-status=dead }}</ref>

In June 2013, they were picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] and were featured on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'' where they performed live their song "This Is the New Year". The song "[[Say Something (A Great Big World song)|Say Something]]" was featured in the final episode of the US dance competition ''[[So You Think You Can Dance (U.S. season 10)|So You Think You Can Dance]]'' on September 3, 2013 and released digitally the same day. It climbed to number 20 on iTunes in the United States the next day. The song was subsequently re-recorded featuring [[Christina Aguilera]] after she heard the song, and this version was released on November 4, 2013, and that version reached number 1 on the iTunes charts.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://music-mix.ew.com/2013/11/03/a-great-big-world-christina-aguilera-say-something/ |title=A Great Big World feat. Christina Aguilera, 'Say Something' |date=November 3, 2013 |work=EW.com }}</ref> The following evening, A Great Big World and Aguilera gave the premiere live TV performance of the song on the NBC television series ''[[The Voice (U.S. season 5)|The Voice]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ryanseacrest.com/2013/11/01/christina-aguilera-duets-with-a-great-big-world-on-say-something/ |title=Christina Aguilera Duets With A Great Big World on 'Say Something' |author=Kathleen Perricone |date=November 1, 2013 |work=RyanSeacrest.com }}</ref> The song reached No. 1 on the digital song chart the next day with 189,000 copies sold for the week.<ref name=billboard>{{cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/5785918/eminem-debuts-at-no-1-on-billboard-200-celine-dion-and-avril-lavigne-in-top |title=Eminem Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200, Celine Dion and Avril Lavigne in Top Five|author= Keith Caulfield |date= November 13, 2013 |work=Billboard }}</ref> The video was released on November 19, 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.etonline.com/music/140996_Say_Something_Music_Video_A_Great_Big_World_Featuring_Christina_Aguilera/ |title=World Premiere: 'Say Something' Music Video |author= Robert Pace |date= November 19, 2013 |work=ETonline }}</ref> In November 2013, it was announced that A Great Big World would be performing at the annual ''[[Victoria's Secret Fashion Show]]''. The same month, they performed for the first time at the American Music Awards with Aguilera.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5800696/a-great-big-world-christina-aguilera-combine-to-say-something-at|title=A Great Big World, Christina Aguilera Combine To 'Say Something' At American Music Awards |author= Jason Lipshutz |date= November 24, 2013 |work=Billboard }}</ref>

Their debut album, ''[[Is There Anybody Out There? (album)|Is There Anybody Out There?]]'', was released on January 21, 2014.

The group made an appearance on the song "Over You" on [[Ingrid Michaelson]]'s 2014 album, ''Lights Out''.

"Say Something" was also used in the nineteenth season of South Park (Episode 6) Titled "Tweek x Craig"

===2015: ''When the Morning Comes''===
On April 21, 2015, it was announced through the A Great Big World Facebook page that Ian and Chad were working on a second album. The group announced the release of their first single off of the new album via Instagram in mid-July. The single "Hold Each Other" was released on July 22, 2015, featuring the rapper Futuristic. The non-rap version was released on July 24, 2015. The album was released on November 13, 2015, with the title ''[[When the Morning Comes (A Great Big World album)|When the Morning Comes]]''. While visiting the UK in June 2015 they recorded at Sarm Studios in London.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/AGreatBigWorld/status/609764814236315649 |title=A Great Big World on Twitter: "Recording @SarmStudios in London today. New music is coming" |publisher=Twitter.com |date=2015-06-13 |access-date=2015-08-10}}</ref> Several musicians and producers collaborated on the album including [[Dave Eggar]], Derek Fuhrmann, [[Gregg Wattenberg]], [[Greg Holden]], [[Kevin Kadish]], [[Josh Kear]], [[Dan Romer]] and [[Mozella]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/AGreatBigWorld/status/619601083107831808 |title=A Great Big World on Twitter: "Working with the incredible @daveeggar on some new music!" |publisher=Twitter.com |date=2015-07-10 |access-date=2015-08-10}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/AGreatBigWorld/status/607652839322710017 |title=A Great Big World on Twitter: "Making some magic with these guys @DerekAEF #greggwattenberg #agreatbigalbum " |publisher=Twitter.com |date=2015-06-07 |access-date=2015-08-10}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/AGreatBigWorld/status/603348188129579008 |title=A Great Big World on Twitter: ".@Dan_romer is totally blowing it " |publisher=Twitter.com |date=2015-05-26 |access-date=2015-08-10}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/AGreatBigWorld/status/588856122104107008 |title=A Great Big World on Twitter: "Just finished writing a beautiful song w/ @mozellamusic. Can't wait to share what we've been working on with you all. " |publisher=Twitter.com |date=2015-04-16 |access-date=2015-08-10}}</ref>

They have been writing a musical for several years. The show is about family and is called ''Strokes of Genius''.<ref name="Out">{{cite journal|title=Chad King of A Great Big World discussing about loving a man |journal=Out |date=14 August 2015 |url=http://www.out.com/music/2015/8/14/chad-king-great-big-world-discusses-singing-about-loving-man3 |access-date=2 September 2015 }}{{dead link|date=May 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
