'''Aya Coco Danioko''' ({{IPA-fr|aja danjɔko}};<ref>{{Cite web|last=Vandromme|first=Frederick|date=2020-11-16|title=Aya Nakamura kan niet bevestigen met nieuwe plaat 'AYA'|url=https://www.humo.be/gs-bc8a7883|access-date=2020-11-16|website=Humo|language=nl-NL}}</ref> born 10 May 1995), known by her stage name '''Aya Nakamura''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|aɪ|ə|_|ˌ|n|æ|k|ə|ˈ|m|ʊər|ə
}} {{respell|EYE|ə|_|NAK|ə|MOOR|ə}}), is a [[Malians in France|French-Malian]] pop singer.<ref name="guardian2019">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/jan/23/aya-nakamura-interview-french-afropop-reluctant-face-of-empowerment|title=Aya Nakamura: Afropop's reluctant face of empowerment|work=[[The Guardian]]|first=Iman|last=Amrani|date=23 January 2019|access-date=23 January 2019}}</ref> She is best known for her hit song "[[Djadja (song)|Djadja]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iPGgnzc34tY|date=11 May 2020|title=Aya Nakamura - Djadja (Clip officiel)|website=youtube.com}}</ref>

She was born in [[Bamako]] and immigrated to France with her family, growing up in [[Aulnay-sous-Bois]].<ref name="fader">{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/13/aya-nakamura-is-flipping-frances-rigid-rules-beautifully|title=Aya Nakamura is flipping France's rigid rules, beautifully|website=[[The Fader]]|first=Christelle|last=Oyiri|date=13 November 2018|access-date=24 January 2019}}</ref> Coming from a family of [[griot]]s (West African storytellers, praise singers / poets of oral tradition), she is the oldest of five siblings. She studied fashion at [[La Courneuve]]. She later launched into music with the stage name Aya Nakamura, after the character [[Hiro Nakamura]] of the [[NBC]] ''[[Heroes (U.S. TV series)|Heroes]]'' science fiction [[drama]] series.<ref name="guardian2019" />

Nakamura published her music online, gaining a following with "Karma" and "J'ai mal". Dembo Camara, a long-time friend, became her producer and manager. Notably, her song "Brisé", composed by Christopher Ghenda, garnered 34 million views on [[YouTube]], and a duo with rapper [[Fababy]] "[[Love d'un voyou]]" resulted in her charting in France. She released her debut album with a number of collaborations. She also had a big concert at the Modibo-Keïta stadium in Bamako, where she opened for the Nigerian star [[Davido]].

== Early life ==
Born Aya Coco Danioko in [[Bamako]], [[Mali]] on May 10, 1995 from a family of [[griot]] (west African storytellers, praise singers, poets of oral traditions). She is the oldest of five siblings. At an early age, her family came to France and moved to Paris Northern suburbs in [[:simple:Aulnay-sous-Bois|Aulnay-sous-Bois]]. She took the name Nakamura after the character [[Hiro Nakamura]] of the NBC science fiction drama series ''[[Heroes (American TV series)|Heroes]]''.

== Career ==
=== 2014–2017: Debut and first success with ''Journal intime'' ===
In 2014, at the age of 19, she released her first single "Karma" on Facebook. With the help of the producer [[:fr:Seysey|Seysey]], she composed a break-up song, "J'ai mal", with a [[zouk (musical movement)|zouk]] type of melody. The song's video reached more than 1 million YouTube views by that time. An old-time friend, Dembo Camara, became her producer and artistic agent.

In 2015, she made the song "Brisé" with the composer [https://www.mouv.fr/emissions/mouv-13-actu/rencontre-avec-christopher-ghenda-producteur-de-talents-video-323274 Christopher Ghenda].. Then she released another song, "[[:fr:Love d'un voyou|Love d'un Voyou]]", featuring the rapper [[Fababy]].

As a tribute to her cultural heritage and roots, she did a concert at the [[Stade Modibo Kéïta|Modibo-Keïta stadium]] in Bamako, opening for the American-Nigerian star [[Davido]]. And she dedicated a song to one of the most famous Malian singer [[Oumou Sangaré]], who was born in Bamako just like her.

In January 2016, the singer signed a deal with Rec. 118, a label from Warner music France. During the same year, she kept on doing collaborations and she released her second single "Super Héros", featuring the rapper [[Gradur]].

On 25 August 2017, she released her debut album ''[[:fr:Journal intime (album)|Journal Intime]]'', led by her first Platinum hit, "[[:fr:Comportement (chanson)|Comportement]]".

The album was [https://snepmusique.com/les-certifications/?interprete=aya%20nakamura&titre=journal%20intime certified Platinum in France]. On 23 September 2017, she participated to La Nuit du Mali in [[AccorHotels Arena|Bercy]] organized by the Wati-Boss, Dawala in order to celebrate the Independence Day of Mali in Paris. She shared the stage with Oumou Sangaré and other Malian artists such as [[Cheick Tidiane Seck]], Lassana Hawa or [[Mokobé]] among others.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rfi.fr/video/20170924-premiere-nuit-mali-electrise-accorhotels-arena-paris-bercy|title=La première Nuit du Mali électrise Paris-Bercy|last=René-worms|first=Pierre|date=24 September 2017|website=RFI|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== 2018–2020: ''Nakamura'' ===
On 6 April 2018, Aya Nakamura released "[[Djadja (song)|Djadja]]"—the first single from her second album—which stayed two consecutive weeks at number one on the French chart, and was later certified Diamond.<ref name="fr" />

The song quickly became a summer hit in France and soon became an international hit. She became the first French female artist to reach number one in the Netherlands since [[Édith Piaf|Edith Piaf]] with "[[Non, je ne regrette rien|Non je ne regrette rien]]" in 1961.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/musique/2018/10/11/03006-20181011ARTFIG00167-aya-nakamura-l-europe-est-totalement-gaga-de-la-chanteuse-francaise-de-djadja.php|title=Aya Nakamura, l'Europe est totalement gaga de la chanteuse française de Djadja|last=Cannone|first=Robin|date=11 October 2018|work=Le Figaro|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref>

"Djadja" was also the first Francophone song since 2009 to reach the top of the Dutch charts, the last one being "[[Alors on danse]]" from Belgian artist [[Stromae|Stromaé]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraaf.nl/entertainment/2448958/voor-het-eerst-in-8-jaar-franstalige-hit-op-1|title=Voor het eerst in 8 jaar Franstalige hit op 1|last=Redaction|first=team|date=17 August 2018|work=De Telegraaf}}</ref>

"Djadja" then conquered charts and radios all over Europe (Germany, Sweden, Portugal, Spain, Turkey, Romania, Bulgaria, Greece, Spain, Belgium, Switzerland...)<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":3" /><ref name="bel-wa" /><ref name="SWE" /><ref name=":4" /><ref name=":5" />

The following single "[[:fr:Copines|Copines]]" released in August 2018 entered at number four in France before climbing to number one in November 2018, and is also certified Diamond.<ref name="fr" />

On 2 November 2018, Aya Nakamura released her second album ''[[:fr:Nakamura (album)|Nakamura]]''.

In January 2019 she won the [https://www.lebureauexport.fr/info/2018/11/europe-aya-nakamura-remporte-les-music-moves-europe-talent-awards/ European Music Moves Talent Award] for Best Urban album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ec.europa.eu/programmes/creative-europe/content/music-moves-europe-talent-awards-2019-12-winners-selected_fr|title=Music Moves Europe Talent Awards 2019|website=European commission}}</ref>

In February 2019 she was nominated for Song of the Year and Best Urban Album at the [[:fr:Victoires de la musique|France Music Awards]].

In April 2019, she released the video for "[[:fr:Pookie (chanson)|Pookie]]", which became the most viewed French video in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nrj.fr/artistes/aya-nakamura/actus/aya-nakamura-arrive-en-tete-des-videos-les-plus-vues-sur-youtube-en-2019-et-en-france-71327092|title=Aya Nakamura arrive en tête des vidéos les plus vues sur YouTube en 2019 et en France|date=6 December 2019|website=NRJ}}</ref>

In May 2019, she was portrayed by ''[[The New York Times]]'' as "one of the most important act in Europe now, musically and socially".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2019/05/22/arts/music/15-european-pop-acts-who-matter-right-now.html|title=One of the most important act in Europe now, musically and socially|last=Peltier|first=Elian|date=22 May 2019|work=New York Times}}</ref>

In June 2019, she earned her first nomination at the [[BET Awards]] as Best International Act.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.7television.fr/aya-nakamura-nominee-au-bet-awards/|title=Aya Nakamura nominée au BET Awards|date=17 May 2019|website=7 television}} {{Dead link|date=June 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

In Summer 2019 she gained momentum with the single "Pookie" (with over 240 million YouTube views to date), including high-profile international versions with multi-platinum rappers [[:it:Capo Plaza|Capo Plaza]] and [[Lil Pump]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rfm.fr/news/Aya-Nakamura-devoile-le-remix-de-Pookie-avec-Lil-Pump-16857|title=Aya Nakamura dévoile le remix de Pookie avec Lil Pump|date=2 August 2019|website=RFM}}</ref>

In October, "Djadja" went Platinum in Spain<ref name=":6" /> and Portugal, while "Pookie" was certified double Platinum in Italy.<ref name="FIMI" />

On 25 October 2019, Nakamura released a re-issue of the now double platinum ''Nakamura'' with five new songs, including the top-five single "40%".{{Citation needed|date=August 2021}}

In the December year-end recaps, she was named Most Watched French female artist in 2019 by YouTube, and Most Streamed French female artist in 2019 by Spotify.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://face2faceafrica.com/article/becoming-the-most-streamed-french-female-artist-in-france-and-the-world-meet-malian-born-star-aya-nakamura|title=Becoming the most-streamed French female artist in France and the world, meet Malian-born star Aya Nakamura|last=Mildred Europa|first=Taylor|date=12 December 2019|work=Face 2 Face Africa|access-date=12 December 2019}}</ref>

On 3 January 2020, she was announced to perform at Coachella Music & Arts Festival 2020.{{Citation needed|date=August 2021}}

On 12 June, Nakamura released a Spanish-language version of "Djadja" with Colombian singer Maluma.{{Citation needed|date=August 2021}}

=== 2020-2021 ===
On 17 July 2020, she released the single "[[Jolie nana]]" as the lead single from her third studio album ''[[Aya (album)|Aya]]''. It debuted at number one on the French singles chart, and achieved Gold status in 2 weeks.<ref>https://twitter.com/snep/status/1290179731610951680 {{Bare URL inline|date=November 2021}}</ref> It also reached the top 10 in Belgium and Switzerland as well as top 40 in the Netherlands. On the UK Afrobeats chart, it reached number 7. {{Citation needed|date=August 2021}}

On the 9th of October, she released the second single from the album, "Doudou". It peaked at number 6 in France, in the top 40 in Belgium and number 16 on the UK Afrobeats chart.<ref>https://www.instagram.com/p/CH7cecHpAwH/</ref>

She announced the album on 15 October, with a release date of 13 November. The tracklist was released on 4 October, revealing collaborations with Stormzy, Ms Banks and Oboy. {{Citation needed|date=August 2021}}

In 2021, Nakamura featured in the music video for ‘Sans Moi,’ a track in the [[Franglish (rapper)|Franglish]]'s album ''Vibe'',<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rickman|first=Catherine|date=2021-08-18|title=Franglish & Aya Nakamura Have a New Music Video Out|url=https://frenchly.us/franglish-aya-nakamura-have-a-new-music-video-out/|access-date=2021-08-31|website=Frenchly|language=en-US}}</ref> and in the music video for ‘C’est Cuit’ by [[Major Lazer]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rickman|first=Catherine|date=2021-08-06|title=Major Lazer Releases New Music Video for 'C'est Cuit' featuring Aya Nakamura|url=https://frenchly.us/major-lazer-releases-new-music-video-for-cest-cuit-featuring-aya-nakamura/|access-date=2021-08-31|website=Frenchly|language=en-US}}</ref>
