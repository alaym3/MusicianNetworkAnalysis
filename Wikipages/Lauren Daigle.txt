'''Lauren Ashley Daigle''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|d|eɪ|.|ɡ||ə|l||}}) (born September 9, 1991)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://kgeb.net/getting-to-know-lauren-daigle/|title=Getting to Know Lauren Daigle|work=[[KGEB]]}}</ref><ref>https://music.apple.com/cz/artist/lauren-daigle/722177758 Lauren Daigle on Apple Music</ref> is an American [[contemporary Christian music]] singer and songwriter. After being signed to the label [[Centricity Music]], she released her debut album, ''[[How Can It Be (album)|How Can It Be]],'' in 2015. It reached No. 1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Christian Albums]] chart, has been certified Platinum by the [[RIAA]] and produced three No. 1 singles on the ''Billboard'' [[Christian Airplay]] chart ("First", "[[Trust in You (song)|Trust in You]]", and "[[O'Lord]]").

Daigle's third studio album, ''[[Look Up Child]]'', was released in September 2018. Bolstered by the [[Crossover music|pop crossover]] success of the single "[[You Say]]", it went on to debut at No. 3 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, becoming the highest-charting Christian album by a woman in over 20 years, and No. 1 on the Top Christian Albums chart, with 115,000 album-equivalent units sold in the first week.<ref name="BB200">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8475341/paul-mccartney-first-no-1-album-in-over-36-years-on-billboard-200-chart|title=Paul McCartney Earns First No. 1 Album in Over 36 Years on Billboard 200 Chart With 'Egypt Station'|work=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=September 16, 2018|access-date=September 17, 2018}}</ref>  The album's lead single, "You Say", peaked at No. 29 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart and has broken the record for the number of weeks at No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Christian Songs]] chart at 129 weeks for any solo artist. The album and single earned Daigle two [[Grammy Awards]].

In addition to two [[Grammy Awards]], Daigle has won seven [[GMA Dove Awards]], five [[Billboard Music Awards|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], two [[American Music Awards]], and has had four No. 1 singles on both the ''Billboard'' Christian Airplay and Hot Christian Songs chart.

== Early life ==
Daigle was born in [[Lake Charles, Louisiana]], and grew up in [[Lafayette, Louisiana]]. She was influenced by [[zydeco]], [[blues]], and [[Cajun music]] in her environment.<ref name="SESAC">{{cite web |first=Lauren Ashley |last=Daigle |title=Lauren Ashley Daigle |url=http://www.sesac.com/Repertory/SongsByWriter.aspx?affilNum=436147&writerAKAName=LAUREN%20ASHLEY%20DAIGLE |publisher=[[SESAC]] |access-date=January 16, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924100800/http://www.sesac.com/Repertory/SongsByWriter.aspx?affilNum=436147&writerAKAName=LAUREN%20ASHLEY%20DAIGLE |archive-date=September 24, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="MTVBio">{{cite web |title= About Lauren Daigle |url= http://www.mtv.com/artists/lauren-daigle/biography/ |publisher=MTV |access-date= November 17, 2014}}</ref> Her mother called her house "the music box" because she was always singing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.klove.com/music/artists/lauren-daigle/|title=Lauren Daigle on K-LOVE|website=Klove.com|access-date=March 3, 2018}}</ref> 

Daigle did not consider music seriously until contracting [[cytomegalovirus]] at age 15. This diagnosis kept her out of school for the next two years. During that time, Daigle took voice lessons that provided her with a creative outlet.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://laurendaigle.com/about/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180204191023/http://laurendaigle.com/about/|url-status=dead|archive-date=February 4, 2018|title=About Lauren - Lauren Daigle|date=February 4, 2018}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=2019-09-25|title=Illness, 'no' on 'American Idol' didn't stop Daigle|url=https://news.gcu.edu/2019/09/lauren-daigle-concert-grand-canyon-university/|access-date=2021-01-14|website=GCU Today|language=en-US}}</ref> During college prep school, she planned to enter the medical field and do mission work. Daigle attended a charter school and completed a year and a half of class work in six months, graduating early. She took a year off from school and did mission work in Brazil before attending [[Louisiana State University]] (LSU) for Child and Family Studies.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://laurendaigle.com/about/|title=About Lauren |work=Lauren Daigle|access-date=February 19, 2018}}</ref><ref name=":1" />

== Music career ==
=== Career beginnings ===
Daigle started singing in her choir<ref name=MTVBio /> and continued to lead the choir at LSU.<ref name=MTVBio /> She tried out for ''[[American Idol]]'' in 2010 and 2012 after she was encouraged by her family to compete in the singing competition.<ref name=MTVBio /> She was cut just before the final 24 contestants in 2010,<ref>{{cite web |title= Top 24 Revealed: Feb 17, 2010 |url= http://archive.americanidol.com/recaps/season_9/feb_17 |publisher= [[American Idol]] |access-date= November 17, 2014 |url-status= dead |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20141129105841/http://archive.americanidol.com/recaps/season_9/feb_17 |archive-date= November 29, 2014 |df= mdy-all }}</ref> and she did not make it to Hollywood in 2011. She made it to the Hollywood round in 2012, but was cut in the first Las Vegas round.<ref name=Hallels />

She made her debut on [[North Point InsideOut]]'s album, ''Hear'', where she recorded "Close" and "You Alone" live at [[North Point Community Church]] in Alpharetta, Georgia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/north-point-insideout-hear-mw0002830123|title=North Point InsideOut: Hear – North Point InsideOut {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits {{!}} AllMusic|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref> She later appeared on North Point Music's album ''North Point Beginnings'' where she recorded "It is Well" in 2015.<ref>{{Citation|title=North Point Music: Beginnings by Various Artists|date=January 20, 2015|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/north-point-music-beginnings/956331915|access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref>

She was asked to sing background vocals on an [[extended play|EP]] for a local band called "The Assemblie" and the EP was briefly the most sold album in the US [[iTunes Store]].<ref name=Hallels>{{cite web |last1=Tap |first1=Timothy |date= September 7, 2014 |title= Lauren Daigle Talks to Hallels About Her American Idol Experience, Her New EP, Human Trafficking, and More |publisher= Hallels |url= http://www.hallels.com/articles/5376/20140907/lauren-daigle-talks-to-hallels-about-her-american-idol-experience-her-new-ep-human-trafficking-and-more.htm |access-date= November 17, 2014}}</ref> After the release of the EP, [[Centricity Music]] invited her to attend a workshop. When the singer for the main event at the workshop became ill, she was asked to fill in.<ref name=MTVBio /> This led to her singing on the [[Jason Gray (musician)|Jason Gray]] single "Nothing is Wasted".<ref name=MTVBio /> Centricity Music signed her on the label in 2013.<ref name=MTVBio />

Daigle's first single, "Light of the World" came from the 2013 album ''Christmas: Joy To The World,'' a collection of holiday songs from various Centricity artists.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.newreleasetoday.com/article.php?article_id=1231 |title= #477 Light of the World Lauren Daigle |publisher= New Release Today |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref> Serving as Daigle's solo introduction to the Christian music scene, the single garnered an initial fanbase and much attention for Daigle. That year, Daigle was asked to be a part of the Morning Rises Fall Tour with [[Aaron Shust]] and [[Mikeschair]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://aaronshust.com/aaron-shust-to-headline-morning-rises-tour/|title=Aaron Shust to Headline Morning Rises Tour|date=March 1, 2014|access-date=January 2, 2019}}</ref> Daigle released the EP ''How Can It Be'' in September 2014.<ref name=NewReleaseTuesday>{{cite web |last1=Davis |first1= Kevin |title=Behind the Song with Kevin Davis: #555 – "How Can It Be" by Lauren Daigle |url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/article.php?article_id=1408 |publisher= New Release Tuesday |access-date= November 17, 2014}}</ref>

=== 2015–2017: ''How Can It Be'' ===
Daigle's debut full-length album, ''[[How Can It Be (album)|How Can It Be]]'' was released April 14, 2015, debuting at No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Christian Albums Chart. The album also broke Top 20 on the ''Billboard'' Top Albums Chart for all genres (No. 16).<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.hallels.com/articles/12919/20150507/lauren-daigle-reflects-on-her-1-album-how-can-it-be-it-is-born-in-passion.htm|title=Lauren Daigle Reflects on Her No. 1 Album "How Can It Be:"  "It is Born in Passion"|work=hallels.com}}</ref> Daigle was named one of Christian music's break-out artists, and her album's first single "How Can It Be" was named by iTunes as one of the Top Christian songs of 2014.<ref>{{cite web |title= Lauren Daigle worship song of year |url=http://centricitymusic.com/site/lauren-daigle-worship-song-year/ |access-date= June 8, 2015}}</ref> The single "How Can It Be" also reached Top 10 at NCA, AC Monitored, and AC Indicator. As a whole, the album logged six weeks at No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Christian Albums Chart. Eighteen months after its release, ''How Can It Be'' achieved Gold Record status.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://laurendaigle.com/site/lauren-daigle-achieves-first-gold-record-album-can/ |title= Lauren Daigle Achieves First Gold Record For Her Album "How Can It Be?" | date= August 30, 2016 |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref> The album is certified platinum.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://centricitymusic.com/lauren-daigles-how-can-it-be-album-certified-platinum/|title=LAUREN DAIGLE'S "HOW CAN IT BE" ALBUM CERTIFIED PLATINUM|date=June 25, 2018|website=Centricitymusic.com|access-date=December 9, 2018}}</ref>

The additional singles from ''How Can It Be'' also gained success for Daigle. "First" reached No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Christian Airplay Chart, where it remained for seven weeks; "Trust in You" also reached the No. 1 spot, remaining for eight weeks.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.billboard.com/artist/6202557/lauren-daigle/chart?f=1762 |title= Lauren Daigle Chart History |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref> Both songs achieved the No. 1 spot on the NCA radio chart.<ref>{{cite web| url= http://laurendaigle.com/site/lauren-daigle-achieves-first-gold-record-album-can/ | title= Lauren Daigle Achieves Her First Gold Record for 'How Can It Be' |date= August 30, 2016 |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref> "Come Alive (Dry Bones)" peaked at No. 5 on the ''Billboard'' Christian Airplay chart.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.billboard.com/artist/6202557/lauren-daigle/chart?f=1762 |title= Lauren Daigle |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref>

On June 1, 2015, Daigle accepted her first award, Worship Song of the Year, for her single "How Can It Be" at the [[K-Love]] Awards.<ref name="K-LOVE The Tennessean">{{Cite news |url= http://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/2015/05/31/king-country-win-top-honors-love-fan-awards/28287407/ |title= For King & Country win top honors at K-LOVE Fan Awards |last= Paulson |first=Dave |date= May 8, 2015 |work= The Tennessean |access-date= March 14, 2017 |publisher= USA Today Network}}</ref> This was also the first K-Love Fan Award for her label, [[Centricity Music]].<ref>{{cite web |title= Lauren Daigle&nbsp;– Worship Song of the Year |url= http://centricitymusic.com/site/lauren-daigle-worship-song-year/|access-date= August 8, 2015}}</ref> The album ''How Can It Be'' won the 2016 Billboard Award for Top Christian Album and was nominated for the 2016 [[Grammy Award]] for [[Best Contemporary Christian Music Album]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://laurendaigle.com/site/lauren-daigle-achieves-first-gold-record-album-can/ |title= Lauren Daigle Achieves Her First Gold Record for Album How Can It Be |date= August 30, 2016 |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref> Along with being named Best New Artist at the 2015 Dove Awards, Daigle earned awards for Song of the Year and Contemporary Song of the Year (for "How Can It Be). At the next year's ceremony, she took home the top honor of Artist of the Year, plus Songwriter of the Year, and Contemporary Recorded Song of the Year (for "Trust in You").<ref>{{cite web |url= http://doveawards.com/2016-winners/ |title= 2016 Winners |access-date= March 21, 2017 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20190407015426/https://doveawards.com/2016-winners/ |archive-date= April 7, 2019 |url-status= dead }}</ref> That year she also won two K-Love Fan Awards, for Artist of the Year and Female Artist of the Year.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.klovefanawards.com/winners |title= Winners |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref> She received another [[Grammy]] nomination in 2017, for [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Christian Music Performance/Song|Best Contemporary Christian Music Performance/Song]], for "Trust in You".<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.grammy.com/nominees |title= 59th Annual Grammy Awards Winners and Nominees |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref> She won Female Artist of the Year at the K-Love Fan Awards in 2017 for the second year in a row.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.klovefanawards.com/nominees | title= 2017 Nominees |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url =https://twitter.com/Lauren_Daigle/status/869590873150980096 | title= Congrats to Lauren for Winning Female Artist of the Year |access-date= June 1, 2017}}{{Primary source inline|date=November 2018}}</ref>

The album's overall success resulted in the release of ''How Can It Be: Deluxe Edition'' in May 2016. The deluxe version features the original album's tracks, along with two new songs, acoustic versions of singles "First" and "Come Alive", and a live performance version of "How Can It Be".<ref>{{cite web | url= http://centricitymusic.com/site/lauren-daigle-how-can-it-be-deluxe-edition/ | title= Lauren Daigle How Can It Be Deluxe Edition |date= February 23, 2016 |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref>

On October 21, 2016, she released ''[[Behold: A Christmas Collection]]'', a [[Christmas music|Christmas album]]. Three singles were released to [[Christian radio]] on November 25, 2016: "Jingle Bells", "Have Yourself a Merry Little Christmas," and "What Child Is This", with "O Holy Night" released to Christian radio the following year, November 24, 2017, and "Winter Wonderland" released the following year, October 19, 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://daily.plaympe.com/lauren-daigle-lauren-daigle-christmas/ |title= Lauren Daigle – Lauren Daigle Christmas – Daily Play MPE®Daily Play MPE® |date= October 7, 2016 |website= Daily Play MPE® |publisher= Destiny Media Technologies Inc. |access-date= January 2, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= http://daily.plaympe.com/lauren-daigle-o-holy-night/ |title= Lauren Daigle – O Holy Night – Daily Play MPE®Daily Play MPE® |date= October 16, 2017 |website= Daily Play MPE® |publisher= Destiny Media Technologies Inc. |access-date= January 2, 2019}}</ref>

The album debuted at No. 77 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] on November 12, 2016, peaking at No. 29 on December 17, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2016-11-12|title=Top 200 Albums, Billboard 200 chart – November 12, 2016|access-date=October 9, 2018|date=November 12, 2016|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2016-12-17|title=Top 200 Albums, Billboard 200 chart – December 17, 2016|access-date=October 9, 2018|date=December 17, 2016|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> The album debuted at No. 18 on the US [[Christian Albums|''Billboard'' Christian Albums Chart]] on November 5, 2016, reaching No. 1 on December 31, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/christian-albums/2016-11-05|title=Christian Albums: Top Praise Music Chart – November 5, 2016|access-date=October 9, 2018|date=November 5, 2016|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/christian-albums/2016-12-31|title=Christian Albums: Top Praise Music Chart – December 31, 2016|access-date=October 9, 2018|date=December 31, 2016|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>

In 2017, she was chosen to sing, "Almost Human", for the ''[[Blade Runner 2049 (soundtrack)|Blade Runner 2049: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack]]''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Blade Runner 2049 (2017)|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1856101/soundtrack|access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref>

=== 2018–present: ''Look Up Child'' ===
"[[You Say]]" was released on July 13, 2018, as the lead single for her third studio album, ''[[Look Up Child]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/us/artists/music/lauren-daigle/songs/you-say/|work=iTunesCharts|title=Lauren Daigle – 'You Say' American iTunes Chart Performance}}</ref> The song was released to Christian radio on July 9.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://daily.plaympe.com/lauren-daigle-you-say/|title=Lauren Daigle – You Say |publisher=DailyPlayMPE|access-date=July 16, 2018}}</ref> The album was also available for pre-order.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/look-up-child/1409149359|title=Look Up Child Lauren Daigle|access-date=July 16, 2018|publisher=iTunes Store}}</ref> It was released on September 7, 2018, via [[Centricity Music]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.patheos.com/blogs/reelfaith/2018/07/listen-new-lauren-daigle-song-you-say-new-album-coming-soon.html|title=LISTEN: New Lauren Daigle Song ‘You Say,’ New Album Coming Soon |publisher=Patheos|access-date=July 16, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/look-up-child/1409149359|title=Look Up Child by Lauren Daigle |publisher=iTunes Store|access-date=July 16, 2018}}</ref> It was her first studio album in three years and served as a follow-up to her debut studio album, ''How Can It Be'' (2015).

"You Say" reached No. 1 on the [[Hot Christian Songs]] chart, where it has spent 60 weeks at No. 1, beating "[[Something in the Water (Carrie Underwood song)|Something in the Water]]" by [[Carrie Underwood]] for the longest reign by any [[solo artist]] in the chart's history.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8494645/lauren-daigle-ties-carrie-underwood-record|title=Lauren Daigle Ties Carrie Underwood For No. 1 Record on Hot Christian Songs Chart |publisher=Billboard|access-date=January 23, 2019}}</ref> After becoming a huge [[Christian radio]] success (peaking at No. 1 on multiple different charts, including the [[Christian Airplay]], where it spent 17 weeks at No. 1<ref>ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/lauren-daigle/chart-history/christian-airplay/song/1088310 |title=Lauren Daigle You Say Chart History|work=Billboard}}</ref>), the song was released to [[mainstream radio]] on January 15, 2019 by [[Warner Bros. Records|Warner Bros]]. From there, it reached the [[Adult Top 40]], [[Adult Contemporary]], and [[Mainstream Top 40]] charts. It was the second biggest Christian song of 2018, according to ''Billboard''. It has further reached a peak No. 29 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8467018/lauren-daigle-you-say-no-1-hot-christian-songs-chart|title=Lauren Daigle's You Say Soars to No. 1 on Hot Christian Songs Chart|work=Billboard|access-date=July 30, 2018}}</ref> "You Say" won the [[61st Annual Grammy Awards|2019 Grammy Award]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Christian Music Performance/Song|Best Contemporary Christian Music Performance/Song]].<ref name=Grammy2019>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/grammys/8489045/2019-grammy-nominees-full-list|title=Grammys 2019 Nominees: The Complete List|work=Billboard|date=December 7, 2018|first=Joe|last=Lynch|access-date=December 7, 2018}}</ref>

''Look Up Child'' debuted at No. 3 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with 115,000 [[album-equivalent unit]]s, of which 103,000 were pure album sales.<ref name="BB200" /> It is her highest-charting album and the highest-charting Christian album overall since ''[[Hard Love (album)|Hard Love]]'' by [[Needtobreathe]] reached No. 2 on the chart in 2016. The album became the highest-charting Christian album by a woman on the ''Billboard'' 200 since [[LeAnn Rimes]]' ''[[You Light Up My Life: Inspirational Songs|You Light Up My Life]]'' peaked at No. 1 in 1997.<ref name="BB200" /> The album won [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Christian Music Album|Best Contemporary Christian Music Album]] at the 2019 Grammy Awards.<ref name=Grammy2019 />

As of January 2019, the album has sold 208,000 copies in the U.S.<ref>{{cite web|title=BuzzAngle Music 2018 Year-End Report|url=http://www.buzzanglemusic.com/wp-content/uploads/BuzzAngle-Music-2018-US-Report-Industry.pdf|website=[[BuzzAngle Music]]|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190106040346/http://www.buzzanglemusic.com/wp-content/uploads/BuzzAngle-Music-2018-US-Report-Industry.pdf|archive-date=January 6, 2019}}</ref>

=== Criticism ===
On November 7, 2020, Daigle sang as part of [[Sean Feucht]]'s "Let Us Worship" tour protesting [[Coronavirus disease 2019|COVID-19]] restrictions.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Guidry|first=Leigh|title=New Orleans concert featuring Lauren Daigle under fire for ignoring COVID-19 restrictions|url=https://www.theadvertiser.com/story/news/2020/11/09/new-orleans-jackson-square-concert-lauren-daigle-covid-19/6225445002/|access-date=2021-01-14|website=The Daily Advertiser|language=en-US}}</ref> Her participation elicited criticism from New Orleans mayor [[LaToya Cantrell]], including requesting that [[Dick Clark Productions]] not book her to perform for its annual New Year's Eve television special ''[[Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve|New Year's Rockin' Eve]]'' (which has included segments broadcast from New Orleans).<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-15|title=Cantrell says Daigle cannot be part of New Year's broadcast|url=https://www.radio.com/wwl/news/local/cantrell-says-daigle-cannot-be-part-of-new-years-broadcast|access-date=2021-01-14|website=www.radio.com|language=en}}</ref> Daigle responded with a statement that said she was riding her bike when she saw the worship protest and "was asked to sing." She said she was "disappointed that my spontaneous participation has become part of the political discourse and I’m saddened by the divisive agendas of these times." Daigle also stated that while she had been under consideration for the program, she had not been officially part of the lineup.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Spera|first=Keith|date=|title=Lauren Daigle's first take on rally, New Year's Eve: 'Saddened' by divisiveness|url=https://www.nola.com/entertainment_life/music/article_22d328d6-40ba-11eb-8900-cb1ba8ecd8af.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-14|website=NOLA.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|first=|date=2020-12-16|title=Source: Lauren Daigle was not a confirmed performer for Dick Clark Productions New Years Rockin' Eve|url=https://www.wdsu.com/article/source-lauren-daigle-was-not-a-confirmed-performer-for-dick-clark-productions-new-years-rockin-eve/34992703|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-14|website=WDSU|language=en}}</ref>
== Tour and other projects ==
In mid-2015, Daigle was a member of the Outcry Tour along with Hillsong United.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ccmmagazine.com/media/outcry-tour-2015-in-photos/|title=Outcry Tour 2015 in photos|last=Staff|date=August 25, 2015|work=CCM Magazine|access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref> She was also part of the Winter Jam Tour 2016 in the central and eastern parts of the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/1163-winter-jam-2016-lineup-announced|title=Winter Jam 2016 Lineup Announced|last=Longs|first=Herb|website=Thechristianbeat.org|access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref>

In late 2016, she headlined her first tour, ''A Night With Lauren Daigle,'' which sold out.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://teamjesusmag.com/congrats-lauren-daigle-artist-year-sold-debut-tour/|title=Congrats Lauren Daigle: Artist of the Year, Sold Out Debut Tour – Team Jesus Magazine|date=October 16, 2016|work=Team Jesus Magazine|access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/2269-lauren-daigle-announces-first-headlining-tour|title=Lauren Daigle Announces First Headlining Tour|last=Clarks|first=Jessie|website=Thechristianbeat.org|access-date=November 18, 2018}}</ref> She joined [[For King & Country (band)|for King and Country]] on their holiday tour later in the year. She was part of the Outcry Tour again in early 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url = http://laurendaigle.com/site/category/news/ |title= Lauren Receives Second Grammy Nomination |date= December 7, 2016 |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref>

In October 2016, she released her first Christmas album, ''Behold: A Christmas Collection'',<ref>{{cite web |url= http://centricitymusic.com/site/lauren-daigle-christmas-album/ |title= Lauren Daigle Christmas Album {{!}} Centricity Music |date= August 9, 2016 |access-date= March 21, 2017}}</ref> and reached No. 29 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite web | url= http://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2016-12-17 |title= Top 200 Albums {{!}} Billboard |date= December 17, 2016 |access-date= March 21, 2017}} "</ref> In 2017, she was featured in the song "Hard Love" by [[Needtobreathe]] for ''[[The Shack (2017 film)|The Shack]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.needtobreathe.com/news/hard-love-featuring-lauren-daigle-shack-soundtrack-available-now-23166|title='Hard Love' featuring Lauren Daigle on The {{sic|hide=y|reason=double the' error in source title}} Shack soundtrack available now!|website=Needtobreathe Official Website|access-date=January 12, 2018}}</ref> She co-wrote and performed "Almost Human", the only original song, on the ''[[Blade Runner 2049 (soundtrack)|Blade Runner 2049]]'' soundtrack.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.soundtrack.net/news/article/?id=2463|title=Alcon Sleeping Giant (ASG) Records & Epic Records to Release 'Blade Runner 2049'  Soundtrack – Soundtrack.Net|website=Soundtrack.net|access-date=January 23, 2018}}</ref> The album, produced by [[Michael Hodges (producer)|Michael Hodges]], Kayla Morrison and Ashley Culp, reached No. 1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Soundtrack Sales Charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ventsmagazine.com/2017/10/03/alcon-sleeping-giant-asg-records-epic-records-warner-bros-pictures-announce-blade-runner-2049-original-motion-picture-soundtrack-october-5/|title=ALCON SLEEPING GIANT (ASG) RECORDS, EPIC RECORDS & WARNER BROS. PICTURES ANNOUNCE BLADE RUNNER 2049 ORIGINAL MOTION PICTURE SOUNDTRACK OUT OCTOBER 5 -|website=Ventsmagazine.com|access-date=January 23, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/2017-10-28/soundtrack-sales|title=Soundtrack Album Sales : Oct 28, 2017 – Billboard Chart Archive|website=Billboard.com|access-date=January 23, 2018}}</ref>

She performed as part of Austin City Limits in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aclfestival.com/lineup/poster/|title=LINEUP POSTER|website=ACL Music Festival|access-date=April 30, 2019}}</ref> She sang the [[The Star-Spangled Banner|national anthem]] at the [[2020 College Football Playoff National Championship]] in New Orleans on January 13, 2020. Earlier that month she sang at the Festival One Christian Music Festival in New Zealand, and then in Christchurch, New Zealand. <ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/6935-lauren-daigle-to-sing-national-anthem-at-college-football-playoff-national-championship |title=Lauren Daigle To Sing National Anthem At College Football Playoff National Championship |first=Herb |last=Longs |website=thechristianbeat.org |date=November 23, 2019 |access-date=November 24, 2019}}</ref>
