'''Gorki''' was a [[Belgium|Belgian]] [[Rock band|rock group]], in its most recent line-up consisting of [[Luc De Vos]] ([[lead singer]] and [[guitarist]]), Luc Heyvaerts ([[Musical keyboard|keyboard]] and [[clarinet]]), Erik  Van Biesen ([[bass guitar]]), Thomas Vanelslander ([[guitarist]]) and Bert Huysentruyt ([[drum]]s). Three songs by Gorki were number one hits in the [[Studio Brussel]] alternative hitlist ''De Afrekening'' in Belgium: 'Lieve kleine piranha' (Gorky, 1992), 'Schaduw in de schemering (from the CD 'Plan B') and 'Joerie' in 2006. Luc De Vos died on 29 November 2014.

==Biography==

===Gorky===
The band was originally named ''Gorky'', which is [[Russian language|Russian]] for 'the bitter'. The first members were Luc De Vos, Wout De Schutter and Geert Bonne. In 1990, the group finished 3rd in [[Humo's Rock Rally]], which is a contest for upcoming Belgian talent organised by the popular magazine Humo. In 1991 came the band's breakthrough with the single "Anja", referring to a Dutch singer from the 1960s with that name.<ref name=archives>{{cite web|url=http://houbi.com/belpop/groups/gorky.htm|title=Gorky/Gorki|work=The Belgian Pop & Rock Archives|accessdate=22 August 2011}}</ref><ref name=biography>{{cite web|url=http://www.gorki.be/newsite/index.php/bio |title=Official biography |language=Dutch |accessdate=22 August 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110823140209/http://www.gorki.be/newsite/index.php/bio |archivedate=23 August 2011 }}</ref>

==="Mia"===
After "Anja", several other singles were released from the debut album ''Gorky''. Among them was "Soms vraagt een mens zich af" ''(Sometimes a man wonders)'', the B-side of which was "Mia". By making number one in the "Tijdloze" ''(the Timeless)'', the "best of all times" charts in [[Flanders]] by the music station [[Studio Brussel]], for three consecutive years, "Mia" became known as their largest hit. Likewise, it made number 1 in a similar chart of Radio 1 in Flanders, and named best Belgian song on the chart by Radio Donna in 2005. {{citation needed|date=November 2014}}

In 2006, it was elected the best song about girls on a TV-show on the public network [[één]]. In 2008, the early music consort [[Capilla Flamenca]] issued "Rosa (Mia)", a polyphonic adaptation of "Mia" sung in Latin. In 2008 a new award show was founded called "de MIA's" ''(the MIAs)''. It was named after the best Flemish song of all times and officially stands for the [[Music Industry Awards]]. {{citation needed|date=November 2014}}

===Death of Luc De Vos===
Luc De Vos died of acute organ failure on 29 November 2014, between 17:00 and 17:30 at the age of 52, leaving a wife and son.<ref name=news>{{cite web|url=http://deredactie.be/cm/vrtnieuws/cultuur%2Ben%2Bmedia/muziek/1.2164202|title=News from the Flemish public television|language=Dutch|accessdate=29 November 2014}}</ref><ref name=tvnews>{{cite web|url=http://deredactie.be/cm/vrtnieuws/videozone/programmas/journaal/2.36632?playlist=7.39644&video=1.2164225|title=News from the Flemish public television|language=Dutch|accessdate=29 November 2014}}</ref> The band ceased to exist thereafter.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.gorki.be/#biografie|title=Gorki - Luc De Vos|website=Gorki - Luc De Vos|language=nl-NL|access-date=2016-03-31}}</ref>
