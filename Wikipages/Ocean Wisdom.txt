'''Ocean Alexander Alouwishas Wisdom''' (born 17 May 1993),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/11727470/filing-history|title=BEYOND MEASURE RECORDS LTD - Filing history (free information from Companies House)|website=beta.companieshouse.gov.uk|language=en|access-date=2019-10-05}}</ref> known professionally as '''Ocean Wisdom''', is an English rapper from [[Camden, London|Camden Town, London]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.high-focus.com/home/artists/ocean-wisdom/|title=Ocean Wisdom|work=Official Website of High Focus Records|access-date=2018-04-06}}</ref> His largest claim to fame is his debut single "Walkin'", in which he delivered lyrics faster than [[Eminem]]'s "[[Rap God]]" (which itself has received acclaim for its rapping speed), at 4.45 words per second, as opposed to "Rap God"'s 4.31.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/may/08/ocean-wisdom-review-gorilla-manchester|title=Ocean Wisdom review – rewinds, nursery rhymes and rapid-fire raps|last=Wray|first=Daniel Dylan|date=8 May 2018|work=The Guardian}}</ref>

==Career==
Ocean Wisdom was born in [[Camden Town|Camden]], London but spent some of his teenage years in [[Brighton]] before relocating back to his hometown.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ocean-wisdom-mn0003423670/biography|title=Ocean Wisdom: Biography|last=Martin|first=Liam|work=AllMusic}}</ref> His debut single "Walkin'" was released in 2014 on [[High Focus Records]].

The song was noted as having been delivered at a faster speed than [[Eminem]]'s "[[Rap God]]" after Ocean managed to fit 4.45 words per second in his track compared to the 4.31 words per second Eminem fit in "[[Rap God]]".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/may/08/ocean-wisdom-review-gorilla-manchester |title=Ocean Wisdom review – rewinds, nursery rhymes and rapid-fire raps |first=Daniel Dylan|last= Wray|date= 8 May 2018 |work=The Guardian }}</ref> 

He released an EP, ''Splittin' the Racket'', on 20 August 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://hiphopinenglish.com/albums/splittin-the-racket/ |title =Album/E.P.: Splittin' The Racket |work=HHIE UK Hip Hop}}</ref> 

His debut full-length LP, ''Chaos ‘93'', was released in February 2016 and entered the official iTunes album charts the same day, peaking at number 10.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/uk/artists/music/ocean-wisdom/albums/chaos-93/|title=iTunesCharts.net: 'Chaos 93'' by Ocean Wisdom (British Albums iTunes Chart)|website=www.itunescharts.net|language=en|access-date=2018-04-06}}</ref> ''Chaos ‘93'' was entirely produced by Dirty Dike.

Ocean Wisdom's second album, ''Wizville'', was released in February 2018, peaking at number 39 in the Official UK Album Charts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/49131/ocean-wisdom/|title=OCEAN WISDOM {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en|access-date=2018-07-21}}</ref> It featured collaborations with artists such as [[Method Man]] (of the [[Wu-Tang Clan]]), Rodney P of [[London Posse]], [[Dizzee Rascal]], and [[Roots Manuva]]. Production came from multiple producers, including Dirty Dike, Muckaniks, Leaf Dog, 184 and Pete Cannon. Later, his song "Tom & Jerry" was featured on the soundtrack of the [[EA Sports]]' [[FIFA 19]].

In March 2019, after releasing his final single "Revvin'" on the High Focus Records label, Ocean formed his new record label, Beyond Measure, in cooperation with [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music]]. He has since released three singles on the label – "4AM", "Blessed", and "Breathin'", all of which are featured on his album ''Big Talk, Vol. 1''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Big Talk, Vol. 1 by Ocean Wisdom on iTunes|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/big-talk-vol-1/1479064653|website=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=2019-10-05}}</ref>

In 2020, Ocean Wisdom collaborated with the [[Italy|Italian]] rapper Nitro on his single "MURDAMURDAMURDA", from the album ''GarbAge''.

In March 2021, Ocean Wisdom released his fourth album, ''Stay Sane,'' featuring artists such as [[Maverick Sabre]], [[Novelist (musician)]] and [[Kojey Radical]].
