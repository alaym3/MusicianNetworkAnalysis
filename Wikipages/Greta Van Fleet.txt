'''Greta Van Fleet''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Frankenmuth, Michigan]], formed in 2012. It consists of Kiszka brothers Josh (vocals), Jake (guitar) and Sam (bass guitar, keyboards); and Danny Wagner (drums). They were signed to [[Lava Records]] in March 2017,<ref name=chartbreaker>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8030232/greta-van-fleet-led-zeppelin-interview-chartbreaker|title=Chartbreaker: Greta Van Fleet on Led Zeppelin's Influence and 'Living In the Resurgence of Rock'|work=Billboard|date=November 10, 2017|access-date=January 21, 2018}}</ref> and a month later they released their debut studio [[extended play|EP]], ''[[Black Smoke Rising]]''. Their debut single, "[[Highway Tune]]", topped the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' U.S. [[Mainstream Rock (chart)|Mainstream Rock]] and [[Active Rock]] charts in September 2017 for four weeks in a row.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/greta-van-fleets-highway-tune-claims-1-spot-at-u-s-active-and-mainstream-rock-radio/|title=GRETA VAN FLEET's 'Highway Tune' Claims #1 Spot At U.S. Active And Mainstream Rock Radio|date=September 6, 2017|work=Blabbermouth.net|access-date=September 22, 2017}}</ref> Their second EP, ''[[From the Fires]]'', containing the four songs from ''Black Smoke Rising'' and four new songs, was released on November 10, 2017, alongside a second single, "[[Safari Song]]". ''From the Fires'' went on to win the 2019 [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Album]].<ref name="Grammy">{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/greta-van-fleet-wins-best-rock-album-fires-2019-grammys|title=Greta Van Fleet Wins Best Rock Album|date=2019-02-07|publisher=National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences|access-date=2019-02-26}}</ref>

Their debut full-length studio album, ''[[Anthem of the Peaceful Army]]'', was released on October 19, 2018, and topped the ''Billboard'' Rock Album charts in the first week after its release. The album's first single, "[[When the Curtain Falls]]", was released ahead of it in July 2018 and became the band's third number-one single on the U.S. ''Billboard'' Mainstream Rock charts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8482945/greta-van-fleet-disturbed-dominate-rock-album-charts|title=Greta Van Fleet, Disturbed Dominate Rock Album Charts|website=Billboard|access-date=December 10, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ultimateclassicrock.com/greta-van-fleet-anthem-of-the-peaceful-army/|title=Greta Van Fleet Announce Debut LP, 'Anthem of the Peaceful Army'|last1=SmithSeptember 6|first1=Rob|last2=2018|website=Ultimate Classic Rock|access-date=December 10, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/greta-van-fleet/chart-history/hot-mainstream-rock-tracks|title=Greta Van Fleet Chart History|work=Billboard|access-date=November 6, 2018}}</ref> ''Anthem of the Peaceful Army'' also debuted atop the ''Billboard'' Hard Rock charts and reached the number one spot on the ''Billboard'' Top Album Sales charts in the first week after its release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hard-rock-albums|title=Hard Rock Music: Top Hard Rock Albums|website=Billboard|access-date=December 10, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/top-album-sales/2018-11-03|title=Top Album Sales : Page 1|work=Billboard|access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref> A second studio album, ''[[The Battle at Garden's Gate]]'' was released on April 16, 2021.

==History==
===Formation and early years (2012–2015)===
The band was formed in [[Frankenmuth, Michigan]], in 2012 by twin brothers Josh and Jake Kiszka, their younger brother Sam, and Kyle Hauck.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/saginaw/index.ssf/2014/06/local_lowdown_greta_van_fleet.html|title=Here's the lowdown on Frankenmuth band Greta Van Fleet|last=White|first=Sue|date=June 6, 2014|website=Mlive.com|access-date=April 27, 2017}}</ref> The band name was created when Kyle Hauck heard a relative mention Gretna Van Fleet, a resident of Frankenmuth; their use of the variation on her name was done with her (subsequent) blessing.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/5902bfcee4b05279d4edbad7|title=Rock Band 'Greta Van Fleet' Call Frankenmuth, Michigan Their Home and Unleash a Bluesy Wonderland with New EP "Black Smoke Rising"|last=Frazier|first=Kelly|date=April 28, 2017|work=[[Huff Post]]|access-date=April 28, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170429021941/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/5902bfcee4b05279d4edbad7|archive-date=April 29, 2017}}</ref><ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DpG8kgspBGQ&t=3m48s Greta Van Fleet Talk Classic Rock Influences, the Woman Behind the Band Name, More] (Q1043 New York interview, published to YouTube on December 18, 2017)</ref> Van Fleet also stated in a later interview that while the band's music is not her type, she supports the band and thinks they are very talented.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/greta-van-fleets-namesake-admits-she-doesnt-like-bands-music/|title=GRETA VAN FLEET's Namesake Admits She Doesn't Like Band's Music|last=Blabbermouth|date=January 4, 2019|website=BLABBERMOUTH.NET|access-date=January 7, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8457950/greta-van-fleet-gretna-van-fleet-band|title=87-Year-Old Gretna Van Fleet on Inspiring Greta Van Fleet's Name: 'I Kind of Laughed It Off'|date=2018-05-25|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-03-16}}</ref> At the time Hauck was the drummer, the band recorded three songs: "Cloud Train", "Highway Tune" and "Standing On". Hauck was forced to leave the band in October 2013 and was replaced as drummer by the brothers' good friend Danny Wagner the same year. The initial [[guitar riff]] for "[[Highway Tune]]" was cited by guitarist Jake Kiszka as being written as early back as 2010,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://loudwire.com/greta-van-fleet-jake-kiszka-black-smoke-rising-ep-british-invasion-influence-more/|title=Greta Van Fleet's Jake Kiszka Talks 'Black Smoke Rising' EP + More|website=Loudwire|access-date=November 6, 2018}}</ref> was rereleased as a single in March 2017.<ref>{{Citation|title=Highway Tune|url=https://soundcloud.com/gretavanfleet/highway-tune-1|access-date=November 6, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.axs.com/the-future-of-rock-and-roll-is-here-with-greta-van-fleet-116955|title=The future of rock and roll is here with Greta Van Fleet|work=AXS|access-date=November 6, 2018}}</ref> On February 28, 2014, a live EP was recorded in one take and released on June 7, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pledgemusic.com/projects/gretavanfleet/updates/33686|title=The Greta Van Fleet Live Video is Posted!|date=March 24, 2014|website=Pledgemusic.com|access-date=April 27, 2017}}</ref>

In October 2017, the band won Best New Artist at the [[Loudwire]] Music Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://loudwire.com/greta-van-fleet-win-best-new-artist-2017-loudwire-music-awards/|title=Greta Van Fleet Win Best New Artist – 2017 Loudwire Music Awards|website=Loudwire|access-date=April 17, 2018}}</ref> The band released an eight-song double EP titled ''[[From the Fires]]'' on November 10, 2017. ===''Anthem of the Peaceful Army'' (2018–2019)===
The group performed at [[Elton John]]'s [[Elton John AIDS Foundation Academy Award Party|Academy Award Party]] on March 4, 2018 at the host's personal request. John joined Greta Van Fleet onstage for his "[[Saturday Night's Alright for Fighting]]" and their "[[You're the One (Greta Van Fleet song)|You're the One]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://loudwire.com/elton-john-on-greta-van-fleet-they-have-an-amazing-future/|title=Watch Greta Van Fleet Jam With Elton John|work=[[Loudwire]]|date=March 5, 2018|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> After the set Elton John recommended the band get more dramatic and flamboyant with their performance and wardrobe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://loudwire.com/greta-van-fleets-jake-kiszka-advice-elton-john-gave/|title=Greta Van Fleet's Jake Kiszka Reveals the Advice Elton John Gave|website=Loudwire.com|access-date=October 19, 2018}}</ref> On July 26, the band made their TV debut on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' performing "[[When the Curtain Falls]]", the first single off of their upcoming debut album.<ref name="Billboard2018">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8467342/tonight-show-greta-van-fleet-when-the-curtain-falls-watch|title=Greta Van Fleet Get Standing Ovation For Blistering 'Tonight Show' Debut With 'When the Curtain Falls': Watch|website=Billboard|access-date=August 7, 2018}}</ref>

Their debut album, ''[[Anthem of the Peaceful Army]]'', was released on October 19, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/the-metal-hammer-11|title=The Metal Hammer 11: The best new music in rock and metal|website=Loudersound.com|access-date=October 19, 2018}}</ref> According to Josh Kiszka, the name of the album "Anthem of the Peaceful Army" was taken from the title of a poem.<ref>{{Citation|last=Amazon Music|title=Greta Van Fleet: The Today in Music Interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=geVtekIpcIY|access-date=December 15, 2018}}</ref> It was the top selling album in its debut week in the US, selling 80,000 copies. Factoring in [[album equivalent units]], it debuted at number three on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="BB200">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200|title=Billboard 200 chart|date=November 3, 2018|website=Billboard|access-date=January 23, 2019}}</ref>

In December 2018, the band was nominated for four [[Grammy Awards]] – [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Performance|Best Rock Performance]] for "[[Highway Tune]]", [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Song|Best Rock Song]] for "[[Black Smoke Rising (song)|Black Smoke Rising]]", and [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Album|Best Rock Album]] for ''[[From the Fires]]'',<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.mlive.com/life/2018/12/greta-van-fleet-earns-4-grammy-nominations-including-best-new-artist.html|title=Greta Van Fleet earns 4 Grammy nominations including Best New Artist|website=mlive.com|date=December 7, 2018|access-date=December 7, 2018}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/awards/61st-annual-grammy-awards|title=61st Annual GRAMMY Awards|date=December 6, 2018|publisher=National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences|access-date=December 10, 2018}}</ref> the latter of which they won.<ref name="Grammy" />

On January 19, 2019, Greta Van Fleet appeared as a musical guest on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'', and performed the song "Black Smoke Rising" and second single from ''Anthem of the Peaceful Army'' "[[You're the One (Greta Van Fleet song)|You're the One]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8494181/greta-van-fleet-saturday-night-live-performance|title=Greta Van Fleet Perform 'Black Smoke Rising' and 'You're the One' on 'SNL': Watch|website=Billboard|access-date=January 23, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/music/greta-van-fleet-snl-performance-video-black-smoke-rising/|title=Watch Greta Van Fleet's Performance Of 'Black Smoke Rising' On 'sNL'|date=January 20, 2019|website=Uproxx.com|access-date=January 23, 2019}}</ref> A third single, "[[Lover, Leaver]]" was later released in May 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/greta-van-fleet|title=Greta Van Fleet Chart History|website=Billboard}}</ref> The band contributed "Always There", an outtake from the album, to the soundtrack of the film ''[[A Million Little Pieces (film)|A Million Little Pieces]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/greta-van-fleet-always-there-million-little-pieces-881025/|title=Hear Greta Van Fleet's New Stomper 'Always There' From 'A Million Little Pieces' |date=September 6, 2019|website=[[Rolling Stone]]|access-date=October 5, 2019}}</ref>

Jake and Sam Kiszka told ''New Musical Express'' magazine in an interview in July 2019 that they are working on a new album to put out in the coming year and that their musical style has evolved.<ref>{{cite web |title=Greta Van Fleet interview: "we're working on something quite different for album two" |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X3vQ4bO6FYM |website=YouTube |publisher=NME |access-date=12 March 2020}}</ref>

===''The Battle at Garden's Gate'' (2020–present)===
On October 9, 2020, the band released a new single, "[[My Way, Soon]]", accompanied by a music video that was shot, edited and directed by the band. The song was expected to appear on their upcoming second album, which, according to Wagner, drew on the band's touring over the previous two years, and how it opened their eyes: "We realized that while growing up, we had been shielded by a lot of things, we were unaware of a lot of things".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/greta-van-fleet-new-song-my-way-soon-1073292/amp/|title=Greta Van Fleet Return With New Song 'My Way, Soon'|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|last=Blistein|first=Jon|date=October 9, 2020|access-date=October 11, 2020}}</ref>

On December 4, 2020, the band announced their second studio album would be titled ''[[The Battle at Garden's Gate]]''. A single titled "Age of Machine" was also released alongside the announcement. The album was released on April 16, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://loudwire.com/greta-van-fleet-age-of-machine-lyrics-battle-at-gardens-gate-album/|title=Greta van Fleet Release 'Age of Machine' Video + Announce New Album}}</ref>

==Musical style==
[[File:Joshkiszka.jpg|thumb|upright|Josh Kiszka performing at the [[Mercury Lounge]] on August 24, 2017]]

Greta Van Fleet's music has been categorized as [[hard rock]] and [[blues rock]].<ref name=Bullet>{{cite web|url=http://www.bulletmusic.net/features-1/2017/greta-van-fleet-interview|title=[Interview] Greta Van Fleet Shed Light On Their Surprise Success And Life On The Road |work=Bullet Music|date=September 3, 2017|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> Although all four members bonded over [[blues]], each does have his own musical tastes: Jake gravitates towards rock and roll, Sam likes [[jazz]], Danny prefers [[Folk music|folk]], and Josh likes [[world music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/greta-van-fleet-vocalist-on-led-zeppelin-comparisons-well-take-it/|title=GRETA VAN FLEET Vocalist On LED ZEPPELIN Comparisons: 'We'll Take It'|work=[[Blabbermouth.net]]|date=September 16, 2017|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> Jake said in an interview, "that all three brothers had listened to their parents' vinyl which included blues and folk music."<ref>{{Citation|last=102.1 the Edge|title=Greta Van Fleet's Jake Kiszka on their childhood upbringing and the renaissance of guitar rock|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Ap5SF41E_k|access-date=December 15, 2018}}</ref> Sam stated that, "It's not like we set out to be a rock and roll band, it's just that sound that comes out [when] we get together and play."<ref name=Bullet/> Songwriting is done by the band as a whole after one of the four members finds the concept for a song.<ref name=BluesInterview>{{cite web|url=http://bluesrockreview.com/2017/10/greta-van-fleet-interview-danny-wagner.html|title=Greta Van Fleet Interview: Danny Wagner|work=Blues Rock Review|date=October 27, 2017|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> Danny stated that a lot of their songs are first written in a "folk set up" before evolving into something else.<ref name=Bullet/>

Greta Van Fleet is often compared to [[Led Zeppelin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.immusicmag.com/2017/05/interview-sam-kiszka-of-greta-van-fleet/|title=Interview ~ Sam Kiszka of Greta Van Fleet|work=I'm Music Magazine|date=May 9, 2017|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> Jake related that he "... went through a year of really intensely studying what [[Jimmy Page|[Jimmy] Page]] did to the point I knew how he thought," but he has studied other classic rock guitarists in a similar manner, specifically mentioning [[Pete Townshend]].<ref name=StoneInterview>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/greta-van-fleet-on-led-zeppelin-influence-blues-roots-w515153|title=Greta Van Fleet's Misty Mountain Revival|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=January 9, 2018|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> Likewise, on his voice being compared to [[Robert Plant]]'s, Josh said that Plant has certainly been an influence, "though it's not what I was going for."<ref name=voice>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7940889/greta-van-fleet-interview-highway-tune-meet-the-band|title=Greta Van Fleet Bring Rock Back to the Future on Debut EP, Talk Influences & Brotherly Love|work=Billboard|date=August 23, 2017|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> Stating that he did not even know who Led Zeppelin were until high school, he explained that his singing style came out naturally one day while struggling to be heard over the rest of the band.<ref name=StoneInterview/> In a March 2018 interview, Plant said of Greta Van Fleet, "they are ''[[Led Zeppelin (album)|Led Zeppelin I]]''" and described Josh as "a beautiful little singer."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freep.com/story/entertainment/music/2018/03/31/robert-plant-greta-van-fleet-led-zeppelin/475696002/|title=Robert Plant on Greta Van Fleet: 'They are Led Zeppelin I'|work=[[Detroit Free Press]]|access-date=April 3, 2018}}</ref>

Some of Jake's guitar influences include [[John Lee Hooker]], [[Elmore James]], [[Bert Jansch]], [[Eric Clapton]] and [[Keith Richards]].<ref name=GoVenue>{{cite web|url=http://www.govenuemagazine.com/interview-jake-kiszka-and-danny-wagner-of-greta-van-fleet/|title=INTERVIEW: Jake Kiszka and Danny Wagner of Greta Van Fleet|work=Go Venue Magazine|date=December 8, 2017|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> Some of the drummers that inspired Danny are [[Carmine Appice]], [[John Bonham]], [[Mitch Mitchell]] and [[Michael Shrieve]].<ref name=GoVenue/> Sam's favorite bass player is [[Motown]] session bassist [[James Jamerson]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://audioinkradio.com/2017/11/greta-van-fleet-interview/|title=Greta Van Fleet Talks Bob Dylan, 'Highway Tune' + More – Interview|work=Audio Ink Radio|date=November 16, 2018|access-date=March 16, 2018}}</ref> Josh Kiszka has imitated the movements and onstage antics of [[Joe Cocker]].<ref>{{Citation|last=Two Doods Reviews|title=Greta Van Fleet Called Out Over Led Zeppelin Likeness In Interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kyj-agfMsm4|access-date=December 15, 2018}}</ref>
