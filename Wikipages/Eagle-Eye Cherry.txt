'''Eagle-Eye Lanoo Cherry'''<ref name=active>{{cite web |url=http://www.activemusician.com/music--eagle-eye_cherry |title=Eagle-Eye Cherry |website=ActiveMusician.com |access-date=2012-11-04 |quote=Eagle-Eye Lanoo Cherry, (born 7 May 1971 in Skåne, Sweden) |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130630105735/http://www.activemusician.com/music--eagle-eye_cherry |archive-date=30 June 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name=Larkin>{{cite encyclopedia| first= Colin| last= Larkin| encyclopedia= The Virgin Encyclopedia of Nineties Music |title= Cherry, Eagle-Eye | publisher= Virgin| year= 2000 |page= 86| quote= b. 7 May 1971, Stockholm, Sweden.}}</ref> (born 7 May 1968) is a Swedish singer and stage performer. His 1997 single "[[Save Tonight]]" achieved commercial success in Ireland, the United States and the United Kingdom, and was voted song of the year in New Zealand. Cherry is the son of American jazz artist [[Don Cherry (trumpeter)|Don Cherry]] and Swedish artist and designer [[Moki Cherry]]. 

He pursued acting during high school, though Cherry's acting career was eventually set aside as his musical career took precedence in his life with his debut album ''[[Desireless (album)|Desireless]]'' (1997). He has released several other albums since his debut, and has co-written for other artists and films, and has guest-starred on reality television. Cherry's music is of the [[Acoustic guitar|acoustic]], [[Rock music|rock]], [[post-grunge]] and [[alternative rock]] genres.

He is fluent in both English and [[Swedish language|Swedish]].<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ow0gVbEhOQI</ref>

==Early life==
Cherry was born on 7 May 1968, in [[Stockholm]], Sweden,<ref name=Larkin/><ref name=active/> the son of American [[jazz]] artist [[Don Cherry (trumpeter)|Don Cherry]] and [[Sweden|Swedish]] painter/textile artist [[Moki Cherry|Monika Cherry]] (''née'' Karlsson). Of [[Choctaw]] descent through his paternal grandmother and African-American through his grandfather,<ref>{{cite book |last1=Dennis |first1=Yvonne Wakim |last2=Hirschfelder |first2=Arlene B. |last3=Rothenberger |first3=Shannon |title=Native American Almanac |date=2016 |publisher=Invisible Ink Press |location=Canton, Michigan |isbn=9781578595075 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=OnBrDQAAQBAJ&q=%22eagle-eye+cherry%22+%22native+american%22&pg=PT863 |access-date=March 12, 2019}}</ref> Cherry is the fourth of five siblings. He is the half-brother of singer [[Neneh Cherry]] (his mother's daughter with Ahmadu Jah).<ref name="AMG">{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p309955|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography|first=Steve|last=Huey|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=20 January 2009}}</ref><ref name="bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.pauseandplay.com/titiyo.htm|title='Come Along' with Titiyo|first=Gerry|last=Galipault|website=Pauseandplay.com|date=27 November 2002|access-date=20 October 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100925185627/http://pauseandplay.com/titiyo.htm|archive-date=25 September 2010|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Other half-siblings are violinist Jan Cherry, Christian Cherry, and jazz musician David Ornette Cherry. At the age of 12, Cherry was sent to school in New York City. He remained there to work as an actor and a drummer in various bands.<ref name="AMG"/> At 16, he enrolled in the [[High School of Performing Arts]] in New York in the same class as [[Jennifer Aniston]] and [[Chaz Bono]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/eagle-eye-cherry-born-to-be-a-wild-child-281422.html|title=Eagle-Eye Cherry: Born to be a wild child|date=20 April 2000|newspaper=[[The Independent]]}}</ref>

==Career==
In 1988, Cherry was credited as "Teenager" in the film ''[[Arthur 2: On the Rocks]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Arthur 2: On the Rocks (1988)|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0094678/?ref_=nm_flmg_act_11|website=Internet Movie Database|publisher=IMDb.com Inc.|access-date=8 June 2016}}</ref> In 1989 he was credited as "Vet #1" in Born on the Fourth of July. In 1993 he starred on the short-lived [[NBC]] action/adventure television show ''[[South Beach (1993 TV series)|South Beach]]''.

Cherry's father died in 1995. He returned to Stockholm to focus on his music rather than an acting career.<ref name="hitquarters">{{cite web |url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/opar/intrview_TManzi.html |title=Interview With Tommy Manzi |publisher=[[HitQuarters]] |date=7 May 2001 |access-date=6 May 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120609212527/http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview%2Fopar%2Fintrview_TManzi.html |archive-date=9 June 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He began writing and recording his debut album, ''[[Desireless (album)|Desireless]]'', in his bedroom studio on an acoustic guitar.<ref name="hitquarters"/> According to manager Tommy Manzi, Cherry's perfectionism made him keep the recordings under wraps until the album was virtually complete.<ref name="hitquarters"/> The album became a commercial success throughout the world.<ref name="AMG"/> ''Desireless'' sold four million copies worldwide and was [[Music recording certification|certified platinum]] in the United States.<ref name="AMG"/>

Cherry co-wrote and sang on "Wishing It Was" on [[Santana (band)|Santana]]'s 1999 album ''[[Supernatural (Santana album)|Supernatural]]'',<ref name="AMG"/> as well as in films such as [[Wim Wenders]]' ''[[The Soul of a Man]]'', ''[[Y tu mamá también]]'', ''The Love of the Game'', ''GO'', ''[[Best Laid Plans (1999 film)|Best Laid Plans]]'', ''[[Holes (film)|Holes]]'', ''[[Billy Elliot]]'' and ''[[Over Her Dead Body]]''. Some of his best-known songs include "[[Save Tonight]]", "[[Falling in Love Again (Eagle-Eye Cherry song)|Falling in Love Again]]", "[[Are You Still Having Fun?]]", "Long Way Around", "Feels So Right", "Skull Tattoo" and "Don't Give Up".

On 27 November 2012, he appeared as musical guest on ''[[Vocea României (season 2)|The Voice of Romania]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.libertatea.ro/detalii/articol/eagle-eye-cherry-nicoleta-luciu-nu-e-genul-meu-424622.html|title=EXCLUSIV: Eagle Eye Cherry, invitatul special de la "Vocea României", nu a fost impresionat de prezentatoare: "Nicoleta Luciu nu e genul meu"|trans-title= Eagle Eye Cherry, special guest from 'The Voice of Romania' was not impressed by the presenter: 'Nicoleta Gloss — not my type' | website=[[Libertatea]].ro| first= Claudiu |last= Petrisor |date=29 November 2012|language=ro}}</ref> His fifth album, ''Streets of You'', was released on 26 October 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elle.fr/Loisirs/Musique/Concert/ELLE-LIVE-65-Eagle-Eye-Cherry-a-la-redaction-3719835|title=ELLE LIVE #65 : Eagle-Eye Cherry à la rédaction - Elle|date=15 November 2018|website=Elle.fr}}</ref>
