'''Sarah Connor''' (born '''Sarah Marianne Corina Lewe'''; 13 June 1980) is a German singer and songwriter. She rose to prominence after she signed with X-Cell Records in 2000 and released her debut album ''[[Green Eyed Soul]]'' (2001) the following year. She followed it with a series of successful albums, including ''[[Unbelievable (Sarah Connor album)|Unbelievable]]'' (2002), ''[[Key to My Soul]]'' (2003), ''[[Naughty but Nice (album)|Naughty but Nice]]'' (2005), ''[[Christmas in My Heart (Sarah Connor album)|Christmas in My Heart]]'' (2005), ''[[Soulicious (Sarah Connor album)|Soulicious]]'' (2007), ''[[Sexy as Hell]]'' (2008) and ''[[Real Love (Sarah Connor album)|Real Love]]'' (2010). Several songs from these albums became hit singles on the pop record charts, including the number-one hits "[[From Sarah with Love]]", "[[Music is the Key]]", "[[Just One Last Dance]]", "[[Living to Love You]]" and "[[From Zero to Hero]]". In 2015, Connor's first German language project ''[[Muttersprache (album)|Muttersprache]]'' became her second chart topper after a decade.

Her popularity was further enhanced by the [[ProSieben]] reality television series ''[[Sarah & Marc in Love]]'' (2005) and ''[[Sarah & Marc in Love|Sarah & Marc Crazy in Love]]'' (2008) alongside her then-husband [[Marc Terenzi]], chronicling their marriage and careers. In 2010, she returned to television to become a judge on the debut season of the German ''[[X Factor (German TV series)|X Factor]]'' adaptation of which she remained part for two further seasons. In 2014, she joined the cast of the debut season of ''[[Sing meinen Song – Das Tauschkonzert]]'', the German version of the series ''[[The Best Singers (series)|The Best Singers]]'' whose accompanying compilation album topped the [[Austrian Albums Chart|Austrian]] and [[German Albums Chart]]. The following year, she served as a guest coach on the [[RTL II]] remake of the talent series ''[[Popstars 2015]]''.

Connor has sold seven million albums worldwide and is recognized as one of the most successful German vocalists to emerge in the 2000s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.universal-music.de/sarah-connor/biografie|title=Sarah Connor – Universal Music Group|access-date=6 October 2018|language=de}}</ref> According to [[GfK Entertainment]], she is the first recording act to ever have scored four consecutive chart-topping hits on the [[Media Control Charts|German Singles Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Yvonne|last=Beister|title=Zum 30. Geburtstag Ein TV-Comeback|publisher=Stuttgarter-Nachrichten.de|url=http://www.stuttgarter-nachrichten.de/inhalt.sarah-connor-zum-30-geburtstag-ein-tv-comeback.51ce6d0e-a60d-402d-8a4d-9803a09fcf4c.html|date=12 September 2010|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> In 2017, [[Deutsche Welle]] ranked her third on its list of the top ten German solo acts who have enjoyed international success.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ranking: Die 10 erfolgreichsten deutschen Sängerinnen und Sänger|publisher=[[DW.com]]|url=https://www.dw.com/de/ranking-die-10-erfolgreichsten-deutschen-s%C3%A4ngerinnen-und-s%C3%A4nger/g-19420855|access-date=6 October 2018}}</ref> A 13-time nominee, Connor won two [[Echo Music Prize]] and has been the recipient of four [[Comet Award|Comet]]s, a [[Top of the Pops|Top of the Pops Award]], a [[Goldene Kamera]], a [[Goldene Europa]], and a [[World Music Award]]. Separated from Terenzi, father of her first two children, Connor has been in a relationship with her manager [[The Boyz (German band)|Florian Fischer]] since 2010. The couple have another two children of their own.

==Early life==
Connor is the eldest of six siblings born to Michael Lewe, a [[copywriter]] from [[New Orleans]], and his wife Soraya Lewe-Tacke (''née'' Gray), a former fashion model and homemaker of [[Scottish people|Scottish]], Irish, and German descent, in [[Hamburg]].<ref name="focus2002">{{cite web|title=Von Niedersachsen nach New York|website=[[Focus.de]]|url=http://www.focus.de/panorama/boulevard/entertainment-von-niedersachsen-nach-new-york_aid_205335.html|access-date=6 October 2018|language=de}}</ref> She has four sisters, including [[Bushido (rapper)#Personal life|Anna-Maria Lagerblom]], the wife of German rapper [[Bushido (rapper)|Bushido]], as well as Marisa, Sophia-Luisa, and Valentina; and one brother, Robin.<ref name="vf">{{cite web|last=Jessen|first=Philipp|date=5 April 2007|title=Sarah Connor: Das nackte Leben|work=Vanity Fair Magazine|url=http://www2.vanityfair.de/articles/leute/sarah-connor/2007/04/05/1/00236/|access-date=12 June 2007 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20070515180119/http://www.vanityfair.de/articles/leute/sarah-connor/2007/04/05/1/00236/ |archive-date = 15 May 2007}}</ref> Her maternal half-brothers Mick and Lex were born in 2008,<ref>{{cite news|title=Sarah Connors Mutter bekommt Zwillinge|website=[[Welt.de]]|date=29 May 2008|url=https://www.welt.de/vermischtes/article2045523/Sarah_Connors_Mutter_bekommt_Zwillinge.html|access-date=19 June 2008}}</ref> Connor grew up listening to [[soul music]], mostly influenced by her paternal grandfather, a former jazz and R&B [[pianist]] from [[New Orleans]].<ref name="focus2002"/> A church choir provided her with her first experience in [[gospel music]] at the age of six.<ref name="focus2002"/> In 1989, the family relocated from Hamburg to [[Delmenhorst]], where Connor attended the Max-Planck-Gymnasium (high school).<ref name="weser2015">{{cite web|first=Uwe|last=Dammann|title=Doppeltes Heimspiel für Sarah Connor|website=Weser-Kurier.de|url=https://www.weser-kurier.de/bremen/bremen-kultur_artikel,-Doppeltes-Heimspiel-fuer-Sarah-Connor-_arid,1162402.html|date=8 July 2015|access-date=6 October 2018|language=de}}</ref> She also enrolled at the local performing arts school, where she received dancing, singing, and acting lessons, which she paid for through waitressing and modelling jobs.<ref name="weser2015"/> After dropping out of school, she completed an internship with [[RTL Television]].<ref name="weser2015"/>

In 1997, Connor and her sister Anna-Maria were picked to sing with the [[backing singer|backing choir]] during the performances of "[[Earth Song]]" and "[[Heal the World]]" at [[Michael Jackson]]'s [[HIStory World Tour]] in [[Bremen]], Germany.<ref name="vf"/> Overwhelmed by a personal meeting with Jackson at the end of the show,<ref name="vf"/> she began to record a number of demo tapes under the pseudonym ''Sarah Gray'',<ref name="ym">{{cite web|last=Bonacich|first=Drago|title=Sarah Connors – Biography|work=Yahoo! Music|url=http://music.yahoo.com/ar-305393-bio--Sarah-Connor|access-date=21 June 2008}}</ref> including [[cover version]]s such as "[[Silent Night]]" and "[[This Christmas (Donnie Hathaway song)|This Christmas]]" as well as the local radio success "Ula la Ula".<ref name="delorado">{{cite web|date=11 August 2005 |title=Sarah Connors Weg zum Erfolg |work=Delorado |url=http://www.deldorado.borgmeier-media.de/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=33&Itemid=33 |access-date=26 July 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070517165250/http://www.deldorado.borgmeier-media.de/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=33&Itemid=33 |archive-date=17 May 2007 }}</ref> In 1998, her boyfriend Martin Scholz, member of the boy band [[Touché (band)|Touché]], arranged for Connor to sign a management deal with the group's manager.<ref name="delorado"/> She subsequently served as their opening act for a [[Bremen]] concert in July 1998.<ref name="weser2015"/> Unhappy with her management though, Connor transitioned to the manager Carlo Vista and began work on the single "This Christmas" with the producers Oliver Hintz and Thomas Nöhre. It was released through Villa Records in 1999.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Gray – This Christmas|website=[[Discogs]].com|url=https://www.discogs.com/de/Sarah-Gray-This-Christmas/release/7576366|access-date=6 October 2018|language=de}}</ref> Also that year, she provided the leading vocals for the German producer Marc van Linden's [[trance music|trance]] remix of the 1982 record "[[The Last Unicorn (soundtrack)|Last Unicorn]]", which reached number eighty-six on the [[German singles chart]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Marc van Linden - The Last Unicorn|website=AustrianCharts.at|url=https://austriancharts.at/showitem.asp?interpret=Marc+van+Linden&titel=The+Last+Unicorn&cat=s|access-date=6 October 2018|language=de}}</ref> In 2000, music manager [[George Glueck]] eventually signed her as ''Sarah Connor'' with the [[Sony]] subsidiary X-Cell Records.<ref name="delorado"/>

==Career==
===2001–2003: Breakthrough with "From Sarah with Love"===
Connor fluctuated between Berlin, [[Hamburg]], and [[Hanover]] to work on her [[contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[hip hop]]-driven debut album, involving German music producers Bülent Aris, Rob Tyger and Kay Denar as well as international collaborators such as [[Kwasi Jones Martin|Eddie Martin]] and rapper [[TQ (singer)|TQ]].<ref name="focus2002"/> Its release was preceded by the top five and top 20 hit singles "[[Let's Get Back to Bed – Boy!]]" and "[[French Kissing]]", respectively, as well as her breakthrough hit, the ballad "[[From Sarah with Love]]", which reached the top position in Germany, Poland, Portugal and Switzerland and raised Connor's fame all over Europe.<ref name="focus2002"/> Eventually released in November 2001, ''[[Green Eyed Soul (Sarah Connor album)|Green Eyed Soul]]'' generally had mixed reception from music critics, who declared it a mixed bag but considered it a solid career launcher.<ref name="laut.de">{{cite web |last=Frömmer |first=Michael |url=http://www.laut.de/lautstark/cd-reviews/c/connor_sarah/green_eyed_soul/index.htm |title=Gängige R'n'B Grooves, zeitgemäße Hip Hop-Muster, Pop Beats à la Britney|language=de |publisher=laut.de |access-date=7 October 2018}}</ref> The album debuted at number two on the [[German albums chart]], and charted within the top five in Austria, Finland and Switzerland, reaching gold status in Austria, Czechia, Poland, Portugal and Switzerland, while earning platinum certifications in Finland and Germany.<ref name="focus2002"/> Connor was subsequently awarded a [[Goldene Europa]] and won the [[Echo Music Prize|Echo Music Prize for Best National Rock/Pop Female Artist]], among three nominations.<ref name="focus2002"/>

[[File:Sarah Connor 4.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Connor in 2002 at ''[[Verstehen Sie Spaß?]]'']] The following year, Connor embarked on the Green Eyed Soul Tour in 2002 and began production on her second studio album ''[[Unbelievable (Sarah Connor album)|Unbelievable]]''. A continuation of ''Green Eyed Soul'', she reteamed with Aris, Denar and Tyger to work on the majority of the album, with American musicians [[Wyclef Jean]], [[Jerry Duplessis]], and [[Diane Warren]] also joining the project.<ref name="focus2002"/> Jean wrote, produced and rapped on the album's [[lead single]], "[[One Nite Stand (of Wolves and Sheep)]]", which once again entered the top 5. ''Unbelievable'' simultaneously achieved gold status in Germany within 48 hours and spawned another three singles, including "[[Skin on Skin (Sarah Connor song)|Skin on Skin]]", "[[He's Unbelievable]]" and "[[Bounce (Sarah Connor Song)|Bounce]]".

In October 2003, Connor released a live DVD branded ''Sarah Connor Live – [[A Night to Remember: Pop Meets Classic]]'', whose performance had been recorded at the Alten Kasselhaus in [[Düsseldorf]] on 24 January, including individual performances of own songs with a classical orchestra backing her up. In November 2003, in spite of being heavily pregnant with her first child, Connor released her third album called, ''[[Key to My Soul]]''. The album's first single, "[[Music Is the Key]]" featuring New York [[a cappella]] singers [[Naturally 7]], became her second number-one hit in Germany. "[[Just One Last Dance]]", the album's second and final single, was a re-recorded edition of the album's original version, including new vocals by Marc Terenzi's band [[Natural (band)|Natural]]. The duet once again reached number-one in Austria, Germany, and Switzerland and the top 20 on a composite World Top 40.

The album ''[[Sarah Connor (album)|Sarah Connor]]'' featured the first single, "Bounce". It was released in many countries, including Japan, Australia, New Zealand, Canada and the United States. In Germany, the single made it to number 14 on the charts. In England and Ireland, it peaked at number 14 and was certified gold in Australia. In America, the song only managed a number 54 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. The album made it to number 106 and took off in the U.S., selling almost 100.000 copies.

===2004–2008: International success===
After a lengthy hiatus that saw the birth of her son Tyler, Connor returned to music in fall 2004 with the release of her single "[[Living to Love You]]". The ballad reached number-one in Germany and Switzerland, becoming her third consecutive chart-topper, and preceded her fourth album ''[[Naughty But Nice (album)|Naughty But Nice]]'' (2005).<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor – Living To Love You|publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/song/3226|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> Connor re-teamed with longtime contributors Booya, Kay Denar und Rob Tyger to work on the album, whose release coincided with the broadcast of her [[ProSieben]] reality series ''[[Sarah & Marc in Love]]''. Her highest-charting effort yet, ''Naughty But Nice'' debuted on top of the German Albums Chart and reached the top three in Austria and Switzerland, selling 100,000 copies within the first two days of its release.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor – ''Naughty But Nice'' |publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/album/18027|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> Critical reaction towards the album was generally mixed, with [[Allmusic]] rating it three starts out of five,<ref>{{cite web|title=''Naughty but Nice'' review|website=[[Allmusic]]|url={{Allmusic|class=album|id=r740510|pure_url=yes}}|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> and ''laut.de'' summing it as "underclass music for the disco around the corner".<ref>{{cite web|first=Michael|last=Schuh|title=Unterschichtenmusik Für Die Dorfdisco Um Die Ecke|publisher=laut.de|url=http://www.laut.de/Sarah-Connor/Naughty-But-Nice-(Album)|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> Spawning just one another single, "[[From Zero to Hero]]" was picked to be used as the theme song to the [[computer-animated]] movie ''[[Robots (2005 film)|Robots]]'' (2005),The song became her fifth chart-topping single in Germany and the fourth number-one hit single in a row.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor – From Zero To Hero|publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/song/3300|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref>

[[File:SarahConnorEinZuhausefürKinder.jpg|thumb|right|upright|Connor performing live in 2008]]
In fall 2005, Connor embarked on the [[Naughty But Nice (album)|Naughty but Nice concert tour]]. Compiling twenty-three dates in Austria, Germany and Switzerland, the tour concluded a month later. In December 2005, she released a [[holiday album]] named ''[[Christmas in My Heart (Sarah Connor album)|Christmas in My Heart]]''. Containing a mix of traditional but contemporary English standards and re-written German songs in English language, both the album and its [[Christmas in My Heart (Sarah Connor song)|same-titled lead single]] reached number four on the charts.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor – ''Christmas in My Heart''|publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/album/18170|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor – 'Christmas in My Heart'|publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/song/7345|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> Eventually certified platinum by the [[IFPI]],<ref name="cert">{{cite certification|region=Germany|artist=Sarah Connor|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> the album was re-released the following year, including the previously unreleased single "[[The Best Side of Life]]", another top four success.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor – 'The Best Side of Life'|publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/song/11581|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref>

Returning from yet another hiatus after the birth of her daughter Summer in 2006, Connor released her sixth studio album ''[[Soulicious (Sarah Connor album)|Soulicious]]'' in March 2007. Mainly made up of 1960s and 70s [[Motown]] covers, but also including two original songs, recording for the entire album took place at the former main building of the [[Berliner Rundfunk]], where Connor was backed by a symphonic orchestra of forty-eight musicians.<ref>{{cite web|title=Information on ''Soulicious''|publisher=SarahConnor.com|url=http://www.sarah-connor.com/de/media/discography/album/soulicious.html|access-date=29 October 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110107185321/http://www.sarah-connor.com/de/media/discography/album/soulicious.html|archive-date=7 January 2011}}</ref> Upon its release, the album reached the top ten in Austria, Germany and Switzerland, where it each reached gold status.<ref name="cert"/> Critics, again, gave ''Soulicious'' a mixed response, with ''CDStarts.de'' calling it "a sterile soul adaption for ''[[The Dome (television program)|The Dome]]'' stage".<ref>{{cite web|first=Matthias|last=Reichel|title=''Soulicious'' Review|publisher=CDStarts.de|url=http://www.cdstarts.de/kritiken/88956-Soulicious.html|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> Leading single "[[The Impossible Dream (The Quest)]]", which was featured in a publicity campaign for the comeback fight of German boxer [[Henry Maske]], and "[[Sexual Healing#Sarah Connor version|Sexual Healing]]", a duet with American singer [[Ne-Yo]], reached the top ten and top twenty of the singles charts respectively.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor – 'The Impossible Dream '|publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/song/12488|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor and Ne-Yo – 'Sexual Healing'|publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/song/26859|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref>

Heading into a new musical direction, Connor intensified work on her next original album with producers such as [[Remee]], [[Thomas Troelsen]] and [[J. R. Rotem]]. In May 2008, it was announced Connor and her family would once again participate in an eight-part reality show titled ''Sarah and Marc: Still Crazy in Love"'' on [[ProSieben]]. The special began airing on July 3, 2008, and followed the recording and promotional events for the new album, branded ''[[Sexy as Hell]]''.<ref>[http://www.prosieben.de/music_cd/samil/artikel/52525/ TV: Serien & Sendungen von ProSieben]. Prosieben.de. Retrieved on 2012-01-15.</ref><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20080601073820/http://unterhaltung.de.msn.com/stars/news-artikel.aspx?cp-documentid=8346012 Sarah und Marc mit neuer Liebesdoku]. MSN.com (in German). May 21, 2008</ref> Its first single, "[[Under My Skin (Sarah Connor song)|Under My Skin]]", co-written by Troelsen, was released on 1 August 2008, in [[German-speaking Europe]], with the album following on 22 August 2008. Subsequently, she was nominated for an ECHO.{{citation needed|date=November 2011}}

===2009–present: ''X-Factor'', ''Real Love'' and ''Muttersprache''===
[[File:Sarah Connor Bremen1.jpg|thumbnail|Connor performing at the Muttersprache Tour in March 2016]]
In 2009, Connor contributed to a re-recorded version of [[Enrique Iglesias]]' duet "[[Takin' Back My Love]]", replacing American singer [[Ciara]]'s original vocals on the song. Released as the second single from Iglesias' 2008 ''[[Greatest Hits (Enrique Iglesias album)|Greatest Hits]]'' album in German-speaking Europe, Poland and the Czech Republic, it reached the top ten of the German singles chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Enrique Iglesias and Sarah Connor – Takin Back My Love |publisher=aCharts.us|url=http://acharts.us/song/43299|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> In April 2010, it was announced that Connor would appear alongside mentor George Glueck and musician [[Till Brönner]] as a judge on the debut series of ''[[X Factor (Germany)|X Factor]]'', broadcast on the German [[VOX (German TV channel)|VOX]] network.<ref name="gala">[http://www.gala.de/stars/news/96648/Sarah-Connor-Jurorin-bei-X-Factor.html Sarah Connor: Jurorin bei "X Factor" – News auf]. Gala.de. 8 March 2010. Retrieved on 15 January 2012.</ref> Connor was given the 16-24s category (made up of solo contestants between 16 and 24) but failed to reach the final show when her contestant [[Mathia-Mathithiahu Gavriel|Mati Gavriel]] was eliminated in his seventh week and [[Edita Abdieski]] was crowned the winner of ''X Factor'' instead.<ref name="gala"/> Amid her appearances on ''X Factor'', Connor released her eighth studio album ''[[Real Love (Sarah Connor album)|Real Love]]'' on 22 October 2010. It earned a lukewarm reception by critics,<ref>{{cite web|first=Sarah-Nina|last=Rademacher|url=http://www.laut.de/Sarah-Connor/Real-Love-(Album)|title=Sarah, du hast den X-Factor ...|access-date=6 November 2010|work=laut.de}}</ref> and debuted at number eight on the German albums chart.<ref name="acharts">{{cite web |url=http://acharts.us/album/58333|title=Sarah Connor – ''Real Love'' Music Charts |access-date=6 November 2010|work=αCharts.us}}</ref> Altogether, ''Real Love'' produced two singles, including "[[Cold as Ice (Sarah Connor song)|Cold as Ice]]", which reached the top twenty on the German singles chart, and [[Real Love (Sarah Connor song)|its title track]], both of which became her lowest-charting singles in years.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://acharts.us/song/58072|title=Sarah Connor – 'Cold As Ice' Music Charts |access-date=6 November 2010|work=αCharts.us}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.beatblogger.de/vo-vorschau/|title=VÖ-Vorschau|access-date=6 November 2010|work=Beatblogger.de}}</ref> Connor returned for the second and third ''X Factor'' series in 2011 and 2012 and was given the groups and over 25s categories, respectively. Again, her contestants Nica & Joe and Björn Paulsen each placed third, losing to [[David Pfeffer]] and [[Mrs. Greenbird]].<ref name="gala"/>

Following a hiatus, in 2014, Connor appeared in the reality television series ''[[Sing meinen Song - Das Tauschkonzert]]'', the German version of ''[[The Best Singers (series)|The Best Singers]]'' series. In 2015, Connor released her ninth studio album ''[[Muttersprache (album)|Muttersprache]]'', her first album to be entirely recorded in German.<ref name="google">{{cite web|date=1 February 2006|title=Muttersprache (Deluxe Version)|publisher=Play.Google.de|url=https://play.google.com/store/music/album/Sarah_Connor_Muttersprache_Deluxe_Version?id=Byjmelola4mbdegqtkjif6zbom4&PAffiliateID=11lxBw|access-date=20 April 2015}}</ref> Encouraged to record songs in her native language, following her performances on ''Sing meinen Song'', Connor worked with a smaller range of producers on the album, including Daniel Faust, [[Peter Plate]], and Ulf Leo Sommer. Upon its release, ''Muttersprache'' debuted on top of on the German albums chart, becoming her second number-one album following 2005's ''[[Naughty but Nice (album)|Naughty but Nice]]''. With first week sales of more than 100,000 copies in Germany, ''Muttersprache'' was certified [[List of music recording certifications|gold]] by the [[Bundesverband Musikindustrie]] (BVMI) just days after its release.<ref name="pp">{{cite web|date=3 June 2006|title=SARAH CONNOR: Platz #1 in den Offiziellen Deutschen Albumcharts|publisher=Presseportal|url=http://www.presseportal.de/pm/58621/3038018|access-date=5 June 2015}}</ref> It has since been certified [[List of music recording certifications|platinum]].<ref name="uv">{{cite web|title=Universal-Vertriebstagung 2015 – Briegmann: "Wachstum ohne Streaming ist nicht denkbar"|publisher=Musikmarkt.de|url=http://www.musikmarkt.de/Aktuell/News/Universal-Vertriebstagung-2015-Briegmann-Wachstum-ohne-Streaming-ist-nicht-denkbar|access-date=5 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151019032132/http://www.musikmarkt.de/Aktuell/News/Universal-Vertriebstagung-2015-Briegmann-Wachstum-ohne-Streaming-ist-nicht-denkbar|archive-date=19 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album also debuted at number-one on the [[Swiss Albums Chart]], her first album to do so.<ref name="pp"/> "[[Wie schön du bist]]" became Connor's highest-charting single in a decade, reaching number two on the German singles chart, and sold over 260,000 copies.<ref name="uv">{{cite web|title=Universal-Vertriebstagung 2015 – Briegmann: "Wachstum ohne Streaming ist nicht denkbar"|publisher=Musikmarkt.de|url=http://www.musikmarkt.de/Aktuell/News/Universal-Vertriebstagung-2015-Briegmann-Wachstum-ohne-Streaming-ist-nicht-denkbar|access-date=5 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151019032132/http://www.musikmarkt.de/Aktuell/News/Universal-Vertriebstagung-2015-Briegmann-Wachstum-ohne-Streaming-ist-nicht-denkbar|archive-date=19 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Connor met [[Marc Terenzi]], the lead singer of American boy band [[Natural (band)|Natural]], at the Bravo Happy Holidays festival in [[Rust, Baden-Württemberg|Rust]], Germany in July 2002.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor Und Marc Terenzi Gmeinsam Im Exklusiven Interview|url=http://www.presseportal.de/pm/13440/373742/bravo|date=20 August 2002|work=BRAVO|publisher=Presseportal.de|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> The pair began dating and in June 2003, it was announced they were engaged and expecting their first child, Tyler Marc Terenzi, who was born on 2 February 2004 in [[Orlando, Florida]].<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Popstar-Liebe: Sarah Connor Ist Mutter|url=http://www.spiegel.de/panorama/0,1518,284737,00.html|magazine=[[Der Spiegel]]|date=2 March 2004|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> On 29 February 2004 the couple invited their friends to a dinner supposedly to celebrate the birth of their son; however they announced that they in fact had eloped. The couple renewed their vows this time with a wedding ceremony on 18 August 2005 in Altafulla, [[Tarragonès]], near [[Barcelona]], Spain. The wedding was the subject of their ProSieben reality show ''[[Sarah and Marc in Love]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor (24) – Ein Pop-Produkt Wird Besichtigt|url=http://www.dk-online.de/Nachrichten/Archiv/index.csp?artikel=784027|publisher=DK-Online.de|date=5 May 2005|access-date=29 October 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718231933/http://www.dk-online.de/Nachrichten/Archiv/index.csp?artikel=784027|archive-date=18 July 2011}}</ref> On 23 June 2006, Connor gave birth to their second child, a girl, named Summer Antonia Soraya.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Herzfehler – Sarah Connor Bangt Um Tochter|url=http://www.stern.de/lifestyle/leute/herzfehler-sarah-connor-bangt-um-tochter-564391.html|magazine=[[Stern (magazine)|Stern]]|date=19 June 2006|access-date=29 October 2010}}</ref> In November 2008, Connor announced her separation from Terenzi.

In April 2010, Connor announced that she is in a relationship with her manager, Florian Fischer, the singer of the 1990s pop group The Boyz.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sarah Connor Ist Nach Scheidung Wieder Liiert|url=http://www.abendblatt.de/vermischtes/article1673152/Sarah-Connor-ist-nach-Scheidung-wieder-liiert.html|date=24 October 2010|work=[[Hamburger Abendblatt]]|publisher=Abendblatt.de|access-date=30 October 2010}}</ref> In February 2011, the two confirmed that they were expecting a child.<ref>{{in lang|de}} [http://www.bild.de/BILD/unterhaltung/leute/2011/02/21/sarah-connor/im-vierten-monat-schwanger-baby-mit-florian-fischer.html Pop-Sängerin freut sich auf ihr 3. Baby: Sarah Connor im 4. Monat schwanger – Leute]. Bild.de. 21 February 2011. Retrieved on 15 January 2012.</ref> Their daughter Delphine Malou was born in September 2011. They welcomed their son Jax Llewyn in January 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.zeit.de/news/2017-01/18/leute-sarah-connor-bringt-soehnchen-jax-llewyn-zur-welt-18072008|title=ZEIT ONLINE &#124; Lesen Sie zeit.de mit Werbung oder im PUR-Abo.
