'''Austin Richard Post''' (born July 4, 1995),<ref name=Syr /> known professionally as '''Post Malone''', is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, and musician. Malone has gained acclaim for blending a range of genres including [[Pop music|pop]], [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], [[Trap music|trap]], [[pop-rap]], [[rap rock]], and [[cloud rap]]. Malone is known for his introspective songwriting and variegated vocal styles on his music. He first attained recognition in 2015 following the release of his debut single "[[White Iverson]]".<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://soundcloud.com/postmalone/white-iverson |title=White Iverson by Post Malone |access-date=October 3, 2015 |website=SoundCloud |publisher=Post Malone|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151005114933/https://soundcloud.com/postmalone/white-iverson|archive-date = October 5, 2015|url-status = live}}</ref> He subsequently signed a recording contract with [[Republic Records]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/post-malone-signs-with-republic-records-news.17053.html |title=Post Malone Signs With Republic Records |date=August 14, 2015 |access-date=October 3, 2015 |website=HotNewHipHop |last=Schwartz |first=Danny|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151004185327/http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/post-malone-signs-with-republic-records-news.17053.html|archive-date = October 4, 2015|url-status = live}}</ref> His stage name is derived from his last name and from a rap name generator.<ref name="vh1.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.vh1.com/news/333717/post-malone-explains-how-he-got-his-name-martha-and-snoop/ |title=Find Out How Post Malone Got His Name (and All of His Tattoos) in This Martha & Snoop Bonus Clip |last1=Wurzburger |first1=Andrea |website=VH1 News |access-date=November 22, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171121142207/http://www.vh1.com/news/333717/post-malone-explains-how-he-got-his-name-martha-and-snoop/|archive-date=November 21, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

Malone's debut album ''[[Stoney (album)|Stoney]]'' (2016) featured the hit single "[[Congratulations (Post Malone song)|Congratulations]]",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2018/07/what-if-post-malone-is-here-forever.html|title=What If Post Malone Is Here Forever?|last=Fitzmaurice|first=Larry|date=July 25, 2018|website=Vulture|language=en-us|access-date=December 20, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191026224545/https://www.vulture.com/2018/07/what-if-post-malone-is-here-forever.html|archive-date=October 26, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> and set the record for most weeks on the US [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|''Billboard'' Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart, with 77.<ref name=":7" /> His second album, ''[[Beerbongs & Bentleys]]'' (2018), debuted at number one on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and broke several [[Streaming media|streaming]] records upon release.<ref name=":7">{{Cite news |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/post-malone-broke-a-34-year-michael-jackson-record-how-705514/ |title=Post Malone Broke a 34-Year-Old Michael Jackson Record. How? |last=Wang |first=Amy X. |date=August 18, 2018 |work=Rolling Stone|access-date=August 19, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180819113158/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/post-malone-broke-a-34-year-michael-jackson-record-how-705514/|archive-date=August 19, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Featuring the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] number-one singles "[[Rockstar (Post Malone song)|Rockstar]]" and "[[Psycho (Post Malone song)|Psycho]]", it was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]] at the [[61st Annual Grammy Awards|2018 Grammy Awards]]. In 2018, Malone collaborated with American singer-rapper [[Swae Lee]] on the song "[[Sunflower (Post Malone and Swae Lee song)|Sunflower]]" for [[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (soundtrack)|the soundtrack]] to the film ''[[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse]]'', which reached the top of the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. His third album, ''[[Hollywood's Bleeding]]'', explored [[indie pop]] and was released in late 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.uatrav.com/lifestyles/article_5c518030-d117-11e9-a5e4-572936caffca.html|title=Post Malone Album Brings Indie-Pop Flair, Other Singles Not Quite There|first=Grant|last=Lancaster |website=The Arkansas Traveler|language=en|access-date=December 20, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191220080143/http://www.uatrav.com/lifestyles/article_5c518030-d117-11e9-a5e4-572936caffca.html|archive-date=December 20, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> It became his second number-one album on the ''Billboard'' 200 and featured the Hot 100 number-one single "[[Circles (Post Malone song)|Circles]]", which spent a record 39 weeks in the top ten on the Hot 100.<ref>{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8528329/post-malone-announces-new-album-hollywoods-bleeding |title=Post Malone Announces New Album 'Hollywood's Bleeding' |magazine=Billboard|access-date=September 4, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190828161136/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8528329/post-malone-announces-new-album-hollywoods-bleeding|archive-date=August 28, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

Malone has sold more than 80 million records worldwide, making him one of the [[List of best-selling music artists|best-selling music artists]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Check out pictures of six famous American Rappers before and after they got tattoos|url=https://ng.opera.news/ng/en/entertainment/e9637fa21a84c57dc65ff79a78503bb9|website=ng.opera.news|access-date=August 6, 2021|archive-date=July 27, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210727030138/https://ng.opera.news/ng/en/entertainment/e9637fa21a84c57dc65ff79a78503bb9|url-status=live}}</ref> His [[List of awards and nominations received by Post Malone|accolades]] include three [[American Music Awards]], 10 [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Awards]], and an [[MTV Video Music Awards|MTV Video Music Award]]. Additionally, he has received six [[Grammy Award]] nominations during his career.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/post-malone |title=Post Malone |date=May 12, 2018 |website=GRAMMY.com|access-date=June 12, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190208112544/https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/post-malone|archive-date=February 8, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> In August 2020, Malone became the first ever solo artist to top both the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Rap Airplay]] and [[Adult Contemporary (chart)|Adult Contemporary]] charts as a lead artist when "Circles" reached number one on the latter, with "Rockstar" having reached number one on the former in 2017. Additionally, having reached the top spot in its 41st week on the chart, "Circles" holds the record for the longest climb to number one on the US Adult Contemporary airplay chart among songs by solo artists.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9430625/post-malone-circles-tops-ac-chart |title=Post Malone's 'Circles' Sets More Records With Run to No. 1 on Adult Contemporary Chart |access-date=August 10, 2020 |magazine=Billboard |last=Trust |first=Gary |url-status=live |archive-date=August 10, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200810003325/https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9430625/post-malone-circles-tops-ac-chart }}</ref> Malone also received a [[RIAA certification|Diamond]] certification by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA) in the United States for "Congratulations".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Post Malone & Quavo's "Congratulations" Certified Diamond|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/post-malone-and-quavos-congratulations-certified-diamond-news.69255.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190603154417/https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/post-malone-and-quavos-congratulations-certified-diamond-news.69255.html|archive-date=June 3, 2019|access-date=June 12, 2019|website=HotNewHipHop|date=January 14, 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2011–2016: Career beginnings and ''Stoney'' ===
[[File:Postmalone-ritual-may7-DREWYORKE-56 (17275323379).jpg|thumb|Malone performing in 2015|upright|left]]
According to Malone, he chose "Post Malone" as his [[stage name]] when he was 14 or 15.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://youtube.com/watch?v=J2sO5AKGfKo?t=1882 |title=Post Malone – Stone Cold Podcast |last=Scare Wolf |date=August 15, 2017 |via=YouTube |access-date=December 20, 2017 |archive-date=March 5, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210305201611/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J2sO5AKGfKo%3Ft%3D1882 |url-status=live }}</ref> The name was rumored to be a reference to the professional basketball player [[Karl Malone]],<ref name=":4" /> but Malone later explained that  "Post" is his last name, and he used a "rap name generator" to get "Malone".<ref name="vh1.com"/> At 16, using audio editor [[Audacity (audio editor)|Audacity]], Malone created his first [[mixtape]], ''Young and After Them Riches''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w-34Bnpbzkg?t=268 |title=GGN Post Malone Has The Sauce |last=westfesttv |date=February 9, 2016 |via=YouTube |access-date=December 20, 2017}}</ref> He showed it to some of his classmates at [[Grapevine High School]].<ref name="rollingstone" /><ref name=":2">{{cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/05/14/everything-you-need-to-know-about-post-malone |title=How Post Malone Became The Most Random Success In Rap |last=Golden |first=Zara |date=May 14, 2015 |website=The FADER |access-date=October 3, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150926073108/http://www.thefader.com/2015/05/14/everything-you-need-to-know-about-post-malone|archive-date=September 26, 2015|url-status=live}}</ref> He was voted "Most Likely to Become Famous" by his classmates as a senior in high school. He worked at a [[Chicken Express]] as a teenager.<ref name="rollingstone" />

Malone later enrolled in [[Tarrant County College]], but dropped out.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VP_ki-W3cLY&t=8m42s |title=Post Malone Goes Undercover on Twitter, Facebook, Quora, and Reddit |date=December 21, 2017 |work=[[GQ]] |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=December 21, 2017 |quote=I didn't go to Syracuse ... Malone has a tattoo of U.S. president [[John F. Kennedy]] on his arm, and has said that Kennedy was "the only President to speak out against the crazy corruption stuff that's going on in our government nowadays."<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7632407/post-malone-jfk-assassination-trump-inauguration-breakfast-club |title=Post Malone Talks JFK Assassination & Trump Inauguration on 'The Breakfast Club' |work=Billboard|access-date=August 8, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170818040311/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7632407/post-malone-jfk-assassination-trump-inauguration-breakfast-club|archive-date=August 18, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> In December 2016, Malone stated that if asked to perform at the [[inauguration of Donald Trump]] he would not have been opposed,<ref name="Trump">{{cite web |url=http://uproxx.com/realtalk/post-malone-trump-inauguration/ |title=Post Malone Isn't Exactly Opposed To Performing At Trump's Inauguration |date=December 20, 2016 |access-date=October 15, 2017 |publisher=Uproxx|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171016122759/http://uproxx.com/realtalk/post-malone-trump-inauguration/|archive-date=October 16, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> though he stated that neither Trump nor [[Hillary Clinton]] were fit for [[president of the United States]] and expressed his support of [[Bernie Sanders]] during the election cycle.<ref name="Trump" />
