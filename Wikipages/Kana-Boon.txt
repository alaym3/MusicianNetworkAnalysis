'''Kana-Boon''' (stylised as '''KANA-BOON''' when [[Romanization of Japanese|romanized]]) are a Japanese [[Rock music|rock]] band formed in 2008.<ref name="Real Sound">{{cite web|website=Real Sound|url=https://realsound.jp/artist/name/kana-boon|title=KANA-BOON|language=ja|accessdate=September 29, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180805084146/http://realsound.jp/artist/name/kana-boon|archive-date=August 5, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> They made their major debut with [[Ki/oon Music]] in 2013. Ever since, they've had four [[album]]s reach the top ten on the weekly [[Oricon]] [[Oricon Albums Chart|Albums Chart]], with ''Doppel'' being their best-charting album, reaching the third place on the chart.<ref name="oricon">{{cite web |url= http://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/587112/products/1039723/1/|title= DOPPEL|accessdate= March 30, 2016|work= Oricon Style|publisher= [[Oricon]]|language= Japanese}}</ref> They have also had five [[single (music)|single]]s reach the top ten on the weekly [[Oricon]] [[Oricon Singles Chart|Singles Chart]], with {{Nihongo|"Full Drive"|フルドライブ}} being their best-charting single, reaching the sixth place on the chart.<ref name="oricon2">{{cite web |url= http://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/587112/products/1074946/1/|title= フルドライブ|accessdate= March 30, 2016|work= Oricon Style|publisher= [[Oricon]]|language= Japanese}}</ref> Three of their singles have been used in the ''[[Naruto]]'' franchise, with "[[Silhouette (Kana-Boon song)|Silhouette]]" being used as the 16th ''[[Naruto Shippuden]]'' opening theme, and "[[Diver (Kana-Boon song)|Diver]]" as the theme song for ''[[Boruto: Naruto the Movie]]'' and [[Baton Road]] as the first season opening for ''[[Boruto: Naruto Next Generations]]''. <ref name="Silhouette, Diver">{{cite news|last=Loo|first=Egan|website=[[Anime News Network]]|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2015-06-14/rock-band-kana-boon-performs-boruto-naruto-the-movie-theme-song/.89272|title=Rock band Kana-Boon performs ''Boruto -Naruto the Movie-'' theme song|date=June 15, 2015|accessdate=April 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Real Sound Kana-Boon - Naruto">{{cite news|website=Real Sound|language=ja|url=https://realsound.jp/2017/07/post-91647.html|title=『NARUTO』から『BORUTO』、「シルエット」から「バトンロード」へーーKANA-BOONが描く鮮やかな"継承"|trans-title=From ''Naruto'' to ''Boruto'', "Silhouette" to "Baton Road" — Kana-Boon's brilliant "inheritance"|date=July 15, 2017|accessdate=September 29, 2017}}</ref> The [[B-side]] for "Diver", "Spiral", was the theme song for ''[[Naruto Shippuden: Ultimate Ninja Storm 4]]'' video game.<ref name="Real Sound Kana-Boon - Naruto"/> This group also made a single for the anime ''[[Subete ga F ni Naru|The Perfect Insider]]'' titled "Talking".<ref name="Talking">{{cite news|last=Hodgkins|first=Crystalyn|website=[[Anime News Network]]|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2015-10-01/the-perfect-insider-anime-opening-ending-theme-song-videos-streamed/.93618|title=''The Perfect Insider'' anime's opening, ending theme song videos streamed|date=October 1, 2015|accessdate=April 22, 2018}}</ref> Their single "Fighter" was chosen to be the fourth opening of ''[[Mobile Suit Gundam: Iron-Blooded Orphans]]''.<ref name="Fighter">{{cite news|last=Green|first=Scott|website=[[Crunchyroll]]|url=https://www.crunchyroll.com/anime-news/2016/12/03-1/kana-boon-and-uru-to-perform-new-gundam-iron-blooded-orphans-themes|title=Kana-Boon and Uru to perform new ''Gundam Iron-Blooded Orphans'' themes|date=December 4, 2016|accessdate=April 22, 2018}}</ref> Shortly after that, their new single titled "Baton Road" was chosen to be the first opening of the ''[[Naruto]]'' [[sequel]], ''Boruto: Naruto Next Generations''.<ref name="Real Sound Kana-Boon - Concerts that were scheduled for October 31 and November 8, 2020 have been canceled as a result.<ref>{{cite web|last=Mateo|first=Alex|title=KANA-BOON Band's Maguro Taniguchi Takes Hiatus Due to Poor Physical Health|publisher=Anime News Network|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2020-10-22/kana-boon-band-maguro-taniguchi-takes-hiatus-due-to-poor-physical-health/.165498|date=October 22, 2020|accessdate=October 23, 2020}}</ref>

==History==
===2006–2013: Origins and early history===
The group was initially formed in 2006 as part of Osaka Prefectural Izumisougou Senior High School's light music club in [[Sakai|Sakai, Osaka]],<ref name="lmaga">{{cite web|url=https://www.lmaga.jp/news/2016/09/15875/|script-title=ja:ＫＡＮＡ−ＢＯＯＮ「音楽で堺に貢献したい」|date=September 22, 2016|website=lmaga.jp|accessdate=February 4, 2021}}</ref> and officially formed as a band in 2008. Maguro Taniguchi initially invited Takahiro Koizumi to join the club, and later invited Hayato Koga and Yuuma Meshida. The band's name derives from popular internet slang.<ref name="Real Sound"/> The band began performing live at the Mikunigaoka Fuzz [[live house]] in Osaka from 2010.<ref name="lmaga"/> During August to September 2011, bassist Hiroaki Hirose left the band, with Yuuma Meshida replacing Hirose. In December 2011, the band won the eo Music Try special prize award.<ref name="eo-2011"/>

In April 2012, Kana-Boon won the ''[[Ki/oon Music|Ki/oon]] 20 Years Audition'' out of 4000 contestants, and served as the opening act for [[Asian Kung-Fu Generation]]. On April 13, 2013, the band released their first music video, "Naimononedari", directed by Shinta Yamagishi, and announced their first nationally distributed work, {{Nihongo|''Boku ga CD o dashitara''|僕がCDを出したら}}, for release on April 24, 2013 through their management [[Hip Land Music Corp.|Hip Land Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/88683|script-title=ja:「僕がCDを出したら」KANA-BOON初全国流通盤リリース|date=April 13, 2013|publisher=[[Natalie (website)|Music Natalie]]|accessdate=February 5, 2021|language=Japanese}}</ref> The album performed successfully, placing 14th on the [[Oricon Charts]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/587112/products/1015431/1/|script-title=ja:僕がCDを出したら|publisher=[[Oricon]]|accessdate=February 5, 2021|language=Japanese}}</ref>
