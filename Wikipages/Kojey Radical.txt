'''Kwadwo Adu Genfi Amponsah''' (born 4 January 1993), known professionally as '''Kojey Radical''', is a British music artist, creative director and mixed media visual artist.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/aug/20/kojey-radical-one-to-watch-poet-socially-conscious-rap-interview |title=One to watch: Kojey Radical |last=Hutchinson |first=Kate |date=2017-08-20 |website=The Guardian |language=en-GB |access-date=2017-12-30}}</ref> His style has been described as a mix of [[Grime (music genre)|grime]]-y [[hip hop]], [[alternative rap]] and [[spoken word]]. Since 2018, he has received three nominations at the [[MOBO Awards]], including Best Newcomer. He performed at the 2020 MOBOs ceremony.

==Early life==
Radical was raised in [[Shoreditch]] and [[Hoxton]], [[London]], the son of [[Ghana]]ian immigrants.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/ne3vpg/kojey-radical-in-gods-body-interview-2017 |title=Kojey Radical and the Search for Inner Peace |last=Ekpoudom |first=Aniefiok  |date=2017-10-24 |website=Noisey |language=en-us |access-date=2017-12-30}}</ref> He began as a [[spoken word poet]] and mixed media illustrator, graduating from [[London College of Fashion]] with a [[British undergraduate degree classification|First Class Honours]] Bachelor of Arts degree in Fashion Illustration.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://showtime.arts.ac.uk/KOJEYRADICAL |title= Kojo Amponsah SHOWTIME |last= UAL |date=2014|website=UAL |language=en |access-date=2017-12-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Ekpoudom |first1=Aniefiok |title=Rodney P meets Kojey Radical: 'I don't want my son to always have to fight' |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/jun/25/rodney-p-meets-kojey-radical-i-dont-want-my-son-to-always-have-to-fight |publisher=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref>

==Career==
===2014-2016: Dear Daisy : Opium & career beginnings===
In 2014, Radical released his first musical project ''Dear Daisy : Opium'' just after graduating.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.gal-dem.com/kojey-radical-in-conversation/ |title= 2017 artist to watch: Kojey Radical |last= |first= |date=2016-12-16 |website= Gal Dem|language=en-GB |access-date=2017-12-30}}</ref> Media commentators stated ''Dear Daisy'' was Radical's first music project in which he touched on topics of love, social media and religion.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://earmilk.com/2014/06/18/kojey-radical-premieres-his-brilliant-new-ep-dear-daisy-opium-premiere/#fT0i59YZGYs71oJz.99 |title= Kojey Radical premieres his brilliant new EP 'Dear Daisy : Opium' [Premiere] |last= Wood |first= Mike  |date=2014-05-11 |website=Earmilk |language=en-us |access-date=2017-12-30}}</ref> It spawned from a book with the same name Radical was illustrating during his time at [[university]]. Radical linked up with UK artist and producer Jay Prince, who produced Radical's first official record, ''The Garden Party.''<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.passionweiss.com/2017/05/11/kojey-radical-interview/ |title= I’m Always Going to do What I Want to do": An Interview with Kojey Radical  |last= Weis |first=Jeff  |date=2017-05-11 |website=PassionWeis |language=en-us |access-date=2017-12-30}}</ref> Radical followed up with a record called "Bambu", which subsequently became the first single on his sophomore EP, ''23Winters.'' Bambu made use of [[blackface]], a persona Radical would later adopt in majority of his early visuals.

After supporting [[Young Fathers]] on tour, Radical later released "Open Hand" a record that saw him adopt a much more political stance in his music. premiering the visual at [[Tate Britain]] in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/6w8vnz/premiere-kojey-radical-open-hand |title= Fresh From Premiering Over at the Tate Britain, Here's Kojey Radical's "Open Hand"  |date=2015-05-10 |website=NOISEY|language=en-us |access-date=2017-12-30}}</ref> Radical aimed to offer an alternative outlook on socio-political issues.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.stampthewax.com/2015/10/19/stamp-tv-kojey-radical-an-open-hand-revolution/|title=Stamp TV: Kojey Radical: An Open Hand Revolution -|date=2015-10-19|access-date=2018-02-26|language=en-US}}</ref> He later followed up with a record called "Kwame Nkrumah" before releasing the ''23Winters'' EP in February 2016. "Kwame Nkrumah" was written in honour of the first Ghanaian president and Ghana's independence.

The ''23Winters'' EP was a personal analysis of a relationship between father and son, with themes of religion, society, family, love, new-age revolution and African diaspora narrated by Kojey's father.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://thesource.com/2016/02/23/first-listen-kojey-radical-23winters-ep/|title=First Listen: Kojey Radical '23Winters' EP {{!}} The Source|date=2016-02-23|work=The Source|access-date=2018-02-26|language=en-US}}</ref> The project includes production from KZ The Producer, Fwdslxsh, Lupus Cain, Selvsse, Niels Kirk & New Machine as well as collaborations from Tom Grenan, Ray Blk & Bobii Lewis. It has been described as "a supreme phonic proclamation of one’s ethnicity, history, and future."{{citation needed|date=August 2017}} In an interview with ''The Source Magazine'' Radical stated his intention was to "...create a body of work that voices my ideas and philosophies while also offering another sense of perspective... "<ref name=":0" /> ''23Winters'' also saw Kojey nominated for two [[MOBO Awards]], one for Best Newcomer and one for Best Video.<ref name=mobo>{{Cite news|url=http://thelinkup.com/meet-moboawards-best-newcomer-nominee-kojeyradical/|title=Meet MOBO Best Newcomer Nominee – Kojey Radical|work=THELINKUP.COM|access-date=2018-02-26}}</ref> The project independently debuted in 3rd position in the [[Official Charts Company|Rap & Hip Hop Albums Charts]] and also entered the [[UK Singles Chart|UK top 40]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.gigsoupmusic.com/reviews/ep/edited-kojey-radical-23-winters-ep-review/|title=Kojey Radical '23 Winters' - EP REVIEW - GIGsoup|date=2016-03-16|work=GIGsoup|access-date=2018-02-26|language=en-GB}}</ref>

===2017-present: In Gods Body and Cashmere Tears===
In 2017, Radical returned with his longest project to date, ''In Gods Body.''<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/kojey-radical-talks-to-clash-about-in-gods-body|title=Kojey Radical Talks To Clash About 'In God's Body'|work=Clash Magazine|access-date=2018-02-26|language=en}}</ref> It includes features from Shola Ama, [[Ghetts]], Tamera Foster, Miloh Smith, Dance music producer Potè, [[Obongjayar]] and British actress and screenwriter, [[Michaela Coel]]. Coel recites a poem written by Kojey that serves as a central narration to the project.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://nationofbillions.com/kojey-radical-returns-in-gods-body|title=Kojey Radical Returns ‘In God’s Body’|work=A Nation of Billions|access-date=2018-02-26}}</ref>

Media commentators stated the project was a continuation of ''23Winters,'' honing in on some of the messages recited by Radical's father and developed into a journey of self-discovery.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Stanley |first1=Jack |title=Kojey Radical, the London Artist Ascending the Throne on His Own Terms |url=https://hypebeast.com/2017/9/kojey-radical-in-gods-body-interview |publisher=Hype Beast |date=September 1, 2017}}</ref> Radical deconstructed the politics of black identity and race while introducing conversations about sexuality and love.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dummymag.com/new-music/new-music-kojey-radical-in-gods-body-ep|title=New Music: Kojey Radical - IN GODS BODY EP|last=Abiade|first=Yemi|website=www.dummymag.com|language=en|access-date=2018-02-26}}</ref> Since the release of ''23Winters,'' Radical has toured the world independently playing in Australia, New Zealand, Brazil, South Africa, Russia, and Europe.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://fault-magazine.com/2017/11/kojey-radical-speaks-fault-gods-body/|title=Kojey Radical Speaks to FAULT About Latest Project, 'In Gods Body'|date=2017-11-24|work=FAULT Magazine|access-date=2018-02-26|language=en-US}}</ref> Radical has a long-lasting brand relationship has been with Adidas, which began in 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/in-depth/2017/11/brand-cosigns-more-impactful-than-artist-cosigns|title=Are Brand Co-Signs More Impactful Than Artist Co-Signs?|work=PigeonsandPlanes|access-date=2018-02-26|language=en}}</ref> In 2018, Radical collaborated with [[Mahalia (singer)|Mahalia]] on her single, ''“Water”'', which was also featured in the soundtrack for the video game ''FIFA 19.''<ref>{{cite web |last1=O'Connor |first1=Roisin |title=Kojey Radical is the renaissance man |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/kojey-radical-interview-music-water-mahalia-trump-eminem-tour-dates-a8367251.html |publisher=[[The Independent]] |date=May 24, 2018}}</ref> This was followed up with another collaboration with the [[Leicester]]-born musician on the single, "One Night Only." The track went onto feature on [[Mahalia (singer)|Mahalia]]'s EP, ''Seasons''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Mahalia and Kojey Radical Drop Cinematic New Visual for ‘One Night Only' |url=https://mixmag.net/read/mahalia-kojey-radical-drop-cinematic-new-visual-for-one-night-only-video-blog |publisher=Mix Magazine}}</ref> During the same period, Radical and [[MJ Cole]] collaborated on the track, ''“Soak It Up."''<ref>{{cite web |last1=Renshaw |first1=David |title=MJ Cole and Kojey Radical’s "Soak It Up" is an exploration of grief |url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/07/18/mj-cole-kojey-radical-soak-it-up-video-premiere |publisher=The Fader}}</ref> Radical and [[Adidas]] teamed up to create a short film about mental health and depression alongside director Max Luz and international fashion platform, SSense.<ref>{{cite web |title=Infinite Silence: A Film for Adidas |url=https://www.ssense.com/en-us/editorial/fashion/breaking-the-infinite-silence-a-film-for-adidas |publisher=SSense}}</ref>

Away from music, in 2019 Radical was responsible for the bottle design of the spirits brand, [[1800 Tequila]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Kojey Radical review – flexing his musicality big time |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/sep/28/kojey-radical-scala-review-cashmere-tears |publisher=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> In late 2019, Radical released his fourth EP, ''Cashmere Tears''. In the review by magazine [[NME]] it received five out of five stars and was described as ''"tale of deeply emotional tales of everyday life."''<ref>{{cite web |last1=Williams |first1=Kyann-Sian |title=Kojey Radical – ‘Cashmere Tears’ review: an extraordinary tale of turmoil, love – and funk |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/kojey-radical-cashmere-tears-review-2547356 |publisher=[[NME]] |date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> In January 2020, it was announced that Radical would be teaming up with US-musician [[Mereba]] on his latest single, ''"Same Boat."''<ref>{{cite web |title=Kojey Radical Enlists Mereba for New Single "Same Boat" |url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/6/kojey-radical-mereba-same-boat-single-stream |publisher=HypeBeast}}</ref>

Radical was a nominee at the [[MOBO Awards]] in 2020 following a two-year break of the ceremony in the Best Video category.<ref name=mobo2>{{cite web |title=MOBO Awards 2020: See the full list of nominees |url=http://mobo.com/news/mobo-awards-2020-see-full-list-nominees |publisher=[[MOBO Awards]]}}</ref> Subsequently, he was announced in the British media as one of the headline performances at the ceremony in December 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=MOBOs 2020: 'More important than ever to showcase black culture' |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/newsbeat-55055619 |publisher=[[BBC News]] |date=November 24, 2020}}</ref> He released the single "Good" in late 2020, which was part of a wider collaboration with [[Sony]] and the use of 360 Reality Audio.<ref>{{cite web |title=How immersive audio will transform virtual experiences |url=https://www.wired.co.uk/article/sony-360-reality-audio-music |publisher=[[Wired (magazine)|Wired]] |date=December 22, 2020}}</ref>
