'''Summer Marjani Walker''' (born April 11, 1996)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www-musixmatch-com.cdn.ampproject.org/v/s/www.musixmatch.com/lyrics/Summer-Walker/BP/amp?usqp=mq331AQA&amp_js_v=0.1|title=Summer Walker – BP Lyrics {{!}} Musixmatch|website=musixmatch-com.cdn.ampproject.org|access-date=May 24, 2019}}</ref> is an American singer-songwriter. In 2017, she was signed to [[Love Renaissance]] and [[Interscope Records]]. The following year, she released the mixtape ''[[Last Day of Summer (Summer Walker album)|Last Day of Summer]],'' which spawned the single "[[Girls Need Love (song)|Girls Need Love]]", that became her first top 40 entry on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart following a remix featuring [[Drake (musician)|Drake]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Johnson |first1=Zoe |title=Take A Walk With Summer Walker On The 'Last Day Of Summer'|url=https://www.vibe.com/2018/10/summer-walker-last-day-of-summer-album |website=Vibe |date=October 19, 2018 |access-date=March 3, 2019}}</ref> In October 2019, Walker released her debut studio album ''[[Over It (album)|Over It]],'' which spawned the US top 20 single "[[Playing Games (song)|Playing Games]]".<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1183838124453126144|user=billboardcharts|title=.@IAMSUMMERWALKER has eight songs on...|date=October 14, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Top 100 Songs: October 19, 2019|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2019-10-19|access-date=October 15, 2019|work=Billboard}}</ref> 

''Over It'' was released to universal acclaim from music critics,<ref>{{cite web|last=Wicker|first=Jewel|date=October 1, 2019|title=Chartbreaker: Summer Walker Readies Star-Studded New Album After Drake Co-Sign|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8531763/summer-walker-debut-album-over-it-interview-drake|access-date=October 4, 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> it also debuted at number-two on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, breaking the record for the biggest streaming debut-week ever for a female [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] artist;<ref>{{Cite web|title=Summer Walker's 'Over It' Earns Biggest Streaming Week Ever for an R&B Album by a Woman|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8532880/summer-walker-over-it-biggest-streaming-week-r-and-b-album-woman|work=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media, LLC}}</ref> while also being [[RIAA certification|certified platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2020/05/09/summer-walkers-over-it-officially-goes-platinum/|title=Summer Walker's 'Over It' Officially Goes Platinum|first=Roman|last=White|date=May 9, 2020}}</ref>

On November 5, 2021, she released her sophomore studio album ''[[Still Over It]]'', which debuted at number-one on the ''Billboard'' 200. The album broke the record for most album streams in a single day by a female artist on [[Apple Music]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=Records|first=Interscope|title=Summer Walker Shatters Apple Music Record For Most Album Streams In A Day By A Female-Identifying Artist Ever|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/summer-walker-shatters-apple-music-record-for-most-album-streams-in-a-day-by-a-female-identifying-artist-ever-301420027.html|access-date=2021-11-20|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en}}</ref> and broke her previous record for largest streaming debut-week for a female R&B artist; while she also tied with [[Taylor Swift]] as the only two female artists that have held 18 songs concurrently on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Zellner|first=Xander|date=2021-11-16|title=Summer Walker Ties Taylor Swift for This Billboard Hot 100 Record|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/pop/summer-walker-taylor-swift-hot-100-record-10140051/|access-date=2021-11-17|website=Billboard|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Walker was born in [[Atlanta]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]]. From 2016 to 2018, she had a small cleaning business, and she was also stripping in Atlanta. She taught herself how to play the guitar by watching tutorials on [[YouTube]] and posted various videos on the platform performing covers.<ref name=LA>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2019-10-21/summer-walker-over-it-drake|title=Summer Walker went from housekeeper to R&B it girl — and she's still a mystery|work=Los Angeles Times|last=Kennedy|first=Gerrick D.|date=October 21, 2019}}</ref>

== Musical career ==
=== 2018–2019: Career beginnings and ''Last Day of Summer'' ===
Walker was discovered by a woman, who has the same name as her and works as the studio manager of an Atlanta-based label, called [[Love Renaissance]].<ref name=LA /> On October 19, 2018, Walker released her debut commercial mixtape, titled ''[[Last Day of Summer (Summer Walker album)|Last Day of Summer]]'', supported by her lead single, "[[Girls Need Love]]".<ref>{{cite web |last1=P. |first1=Milca |title=Summer Walker Officially Arrives With "Last Day Of Summer" Project |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/summer-walker-officially-arrives-with-last-day-of-summer-project-new-mixtape.118530.html |website=HotNewHipHop|date=October 20, 2018 }}</ref> Her album contained her thoughts on love, doubt, and womanhood.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/5-times-we-fell-in-love-with-summer-walker-on-her|title=5 Times We Fell In Love With Summer Walker on Her 'Last Day of Summer' Debut|website=[[Ones to Watch]]|access-date=May 6, 2019}}</ref> Towards the end of 2018, she went on tour with [[6lack]] on the From East Atlanta With Love Tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/6lack-announces-world-tour-featuring-tierra-whack-boogie-and-more-news.57301.html |title=6LACK Announces World Tour Featuring Tierra Whack, Boogie & More |work=HotNewHipHop |date=August 14, 2018}}</ref> Following the success of her mixtape, [[Apple Music]] named Walker as its newest [[Apple Music Up Next|Up Next]] artist in 2019, and became the number 8 [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] artist worldwide across Apple Music.<ref>{{cite web |title=Summer Walker Named the First Apple Music Up Next Artist of 2019 |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Summer-Walker-Named-the-First-Apple-Music-Up-Next-Artist-of-2019-20190115 |website=Broadway World |access-date=March 3, 2019}}</ref> On January 25, 2019, she released her first EP titled ''Clear'', consisting of four tracks of acoustic recordings.<ref>{{cite web |last1=P. |first1=Milca |title=Summer Walker Shares Soulful "CLEAR" EP |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/summer-walker-shares-soulful-clear-ep-new-mixtape.118835.html |website=HotNewHipHop|date=January 26, 2019 }}</ref> On February 27, 2019, she released the remix to her song, "Girls Need Love", with [[Drake (musician)|Drake]].<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Blistein |first1=Jon |title=Hear Drake Drop First New Verse of 2019 on Summer Walker's 'Girls Need Love' Remix |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/drake-summer-walker-girls-need-love-remix-801045/ |magazine=Rolling Stone |date=February 27, 2019 |access-date=March 3, 2019}}</ref>

=== 2019–2020: Breakthrough and ''Over It'' ===
On August 23, 2019, Walker released a track, called "[[Playing Games (song)|Playing Games]]" as the first official single for her debut studio album, titled ''[[Over It (album)|Over It]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ratedrnb.com/2019/08/summer-walker-releases-new-song-playing-games/|title=Summer Walker Releases New Song 'Playing Games'|website=ratedrnb.com|date=August 23, 2019|access-date=September 17, 2019}}</ref> The song, which contains an interpolation of [[Destiny's Child]]'s number-one hit "[[Say My Name]]", was produced by [[London on da Track]]. On September 12, Walker revealed that ''Over It'' would be release on October 4 via [[Love Renaissance|LVRN]] and [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ratedrnb.com/2019/09/summer-walker-announces-release-date-for-over-it/|title=Summer Walker Announces Release Date for Debut Album|website=ratedrnb.com|date=September 12, 2019|access-date=September 17, 2019}}</ref> Along with her album announcement, she shared the official artwork. ''Over It'' debuted at number two on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with 134,000 [[album-equivalent unit|units]] in its first week. Its debut week marked the largest streaming week for an R&B album by a female artist, in terms of on-demand audio streams.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8532879/superm-the-1st-mini-album-billboard-200-no-1|title=SuperM Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Albums Chart With 'The 1st Mini Album'|work=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=October 13, 2019|access-date=October 14, 2019}}</ref> The album also topped the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|R&B Albums]] chart for 14 nonconsecutive weeks, and as of July 2020, has yet to chart below number 7.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9422631/summer-walker-life-earth-number-1-top-rb-albums-chart|title=Summer Walker's 'Life on Earth' Debuts at No. 1 on Top R&B Albums Chart|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Trevor|first=Anderson|date=July 17, 2020|access-date=July 21, 2020}}</ref> She supported ''Over It'' with her accompanying The First and Last Tour, which kicked off on October 20.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ratedrnb.com/2019/08/summer-walker-plots-the-first-and-last-tour/|title=Summer Walker Plots 'The First and Last Tour'|website=ratedrnb.com|date=August 19, 2019|access-date=September 17, 2019}}</ref> Due to her social anxiety, Walker canceled the remaining 20 out of 29 dates on the tour committing to 9 of them.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-50401508|title=Summer Walker cancels US gigs: 'I get tired, I get sad and it's just a lot'|work=BBC|date=November 13, 2019}}</ref> On November 17, Walker won her first [[Soul Train Music Awards|Soul Train Music Award]] for Best New Artist, she later received backlash from fans for her short speech and was accused of faking her social anxiety.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.essence.com/lifestyle/health-wellness/summer-walker-addresses-social-anxiety-claims/|title=Summer Walker Addresses Critics Who Don't Believe She Has Social Anxiety|work=Essence}}</ref>

During early 2020, Walker collaborated with an American rapper [[21 Savage]] on a song, titled "Secret". She also appeared on a remix for [[Justin Bieber]]'s song [[Yummy (Justin Bieber song)|Yummy]]. In April 2020, she was featured on [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]]'s remix of "Eleven". On June 29, Walker announced the release of an EP, titled ''Life on Earth'' to be released on July 10. The announcement came a day after she performed "Session 32" and "[[Come Thru]]" with [[Usher (musician)|Usher]] at the [[BET Awards 2020|2020's BET Awards]], where she was nominated for Best New Artist and Best Female R&B/Pop Artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/6/summer-walker-life-on-earth-ep-announcement-cover-art|title=Summer Walker Announces New 'Life on Earth' EP|work=Hypebeast|last=Ingvaldsen|first=Torsten|date=June 29, 2020|access-date=July 3, 2020}}</ref> ''Life on Earth'' debuted atop the ''Billboard''{{'s}} Top R&B Albums chart, becoming Walker's second number one. It also debuted at number 8 on the ''Billboard'' 200 becoming her second top 10 project on the chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9422631/summer-walker-life-earth-number-1-top-rb-albums-chart|title=Summer Walker's 'Life on Earth' Debuts at No. 1 on Top R&B Albums Chart|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Trevor|first=Anderson|date=July 17, 2020|access-date=July 24, 2020}}</ref> Two tracks from the EP charted on the [[Billboard Hot 100|"Billboard" Hot 100]], "Let It Go", and "My Affection" featuring [[PartyNextDoor]], at numbers 84 and 86, respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/summer-walker/chart-history/HSI|title=Summer Walker Hot 100 Chart History|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 24, 2020}}</ref> "[[Come Thru]]" featuring [[Usher (musician)|Usher]] was certified platinum on August 20 by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). A few days later, on August 25, her song "Body" was a certified gold, "[[Playing Games]]" was a certified two-time platinum, and "[[Girls Need Love]]" was a certified three-time platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/|title=Gold & Platinum|website=RIAA}}</ref> On September 14, Walker was a musical guest on [[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]], performing "Body" from her debut album ''Over It''. The performance received praise from music-critics and fans alike, with [[Rolling Stone]] describing the performance as "beatific". On October 14, Summer won the [[Billboard Music Award]] for the Best Female R&B Artist at the [[2020 Billboard Music Awards|2020's Billboard Music Awards]] beating out [[Beyoncé]] and [[Lizzo]]. It became her first Billboard Music Award win from three nominations respectively. She was also nominated for [[Billboard Music Award for Top R&B Artist|Top R&B Artist]] and Top R&B Album for ''Over It''. On November 23, she released a repackaged version of the ''Over It'' album, titled ''Over It (Complete Edition)''. The repackaged version of the album features all 18 songs from the standard version of the album plus additional [[instrumentals]], [[a capella]] versions, live renditions and more. On December 10, her debut song, "Session 32" was certified gold and her collaboration featuring [[Jhené Aiko]], titled "I'll Kill You" was a certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), respectively. On the ''Billboard'' 200 year-end chart of 2020, ''Over It'' was the second best performing R&B album of the year behind [[The Weeknd]]'s ''[[After Hours (The Weeknd album)|After Hours]]'' and was the seventeenth best performing album overall.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/year-end/top-billboard-200-albums|title=Billboard 200 Albums – Year-End|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

2021: Summer Walker releases her second album "Still Over It" which has since broken the record for biggest #1 R&B debut on the US albums chart since Beyonce's album "Lemonade." The album has surpassed 185-210k units within the first week of release. It was announced in late November 2021, that Summer Walker would perform at the [[2021 Soul Train Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Grein|first=Paul|title=Summer Walker to Perform at 2021 Soul Train Awards|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/awards/2021-soul-train-awards-performers-summer-walker-1235000607/|date=November 19, 2021|access-date=November 21, 2021|website=Billboard}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
While teaching herself guitar through [[YouTube]] tutorials, Summer worked as a stripper in an [[Atlanta]] strip club. In an interview with ''The Face'' she stated that the experience was "helping her grow out of her shell" and that it was "a fun experience".
Summer has talked about her various previous jobs in a lengthy 'thank you post' to her fans in which she stated: "Beyond Blessed. My whole life changed in a year & a half, like literally year ago I was scrubbing toilets and stripping. I'm now damn near financially free with a paid off house & car without a care in the world, and it's all cause of y'all so thank you," posted on her Instagram in Jan. 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hollywoodlife.com/feature/who-is-summer-walker-singer-3359398/|title=Summer Walker: 5 Things To Know About The Pregnant Singer Calling BF London On Da Track 'A Bum'|first=Samantha|last=Wilson|date=November 28, 2020}}</ref>

She is also known to actively voice her personal opinions on issues throughout both of her [[Instagram]] pages.

In 2020 Walker announced that she was expecting her first child, with on-off boyfriend [[London on da Track]] by posing her baby bump on her social media platforms.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.essence.com/love/summer-walker-pregnant-london-on-da-track/|title=Summer Walker Is Pregnant With Her First Child|magazine=Essence|date=November 20, 2020}}</ref> Walker and London started their on-off again relationship after he worked on the production of her debut album, ''Over It'', of which he is also the executive producer. In March 2021, Walker gave birth to a baby girl.

=== Tattoos ===
Walker has approximately 30 tattoos, several of which are on her face, including her current boyfriend's name.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://people.com/style/summer-walker-and-rapper-boyfriend-lvrd-pharaoh-debut-face-tattoos-of-each-others-names/|title=Summer Walker and Rapper Boyfriend LVRD Pharaoh Debut Face Tattoos of Each Other's Names|work=People|date=November 23, 2021|last=Jenkel|first=Olivia|publisher=Meredith Corporation}}</ref>

== Controversies ==
On March 3, 2020, Summer took to Instagram to give her opinion on a video pertaining to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. It garnered a lot of backlash due to people claiming that she was spreading misinformation and being "[[Xenophobia|xenophobic]]". After one commenter questioned the validity of the two-year-old video, she replied by saying, "They trifling & nasty af too so what's your point?"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/summer-walker-coronavirus-criticism-2645402619.html|title=Summer Walker Criticized For 'Racist' Coronavirus Post|date=March 6, 2020|website=PAPER}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Walker has said that she draws inspiration from [[Amy Winehouse]], [[Jimi Hendrix]], and [[Erykah Badu]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/5-times-we-fell-in-love-with-summer-walker-on-her|title=5 Times We Fell In Love With Summer Walker on Her 'Last Day of Summer' Debut / Ones To Watch|website=Ones To Watch|access-date=May 6, 2019}}</ref>
