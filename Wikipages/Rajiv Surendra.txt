'''Rajiv Surendra''' (born 1984) is a Canadian actor, artist, and writer. He is best known for his portrayal of Kevin Gnapoor in the 2004 teen comedy film ''[[Mean Girls]]'' and for his 2016 memoir, ''The Elephants in My Backyard'', which chronicles his bid to win the lead role in the 2012 film ''[[Life of Pi (film)|Life of Pi]]''.<ref name="cbcbooks">[https://web.archive.org/web/20170421021701/http://www.cbc.ca/books/2016/11/the-elephants-in-my-backyard.html "The Elephants in My Backyard"]. [[CBC Books]], November 18, 2016.</ref>

== Career ==

=== Acting ===
As an actor, Surendra played Chuck Singh in the third season of the [[YTV (TV channel)|YTV]] sitcom ''[[System Crash (TV series)|System Crash]]'' (2000), Max in the film comedy ''Fast Food High'' (2003), Barney Oscarson in an episode of Canadian teen comedy-drama television series [[Radio Free Roscoe]] (2003), Kevin Gnapoor in ''[[Mean Girls]]'' (2004), and Jajeev in the short film ''6 ft. in 7 min'' (2005)''.''

=== Writing ===
In 2016, Surendra published the book, ''The Elephants in My Backyard'', a memoir of his failed attempt to win the lead role in the 2012 film ''[[Life of Pi (film)|Life of Pi]]''.<ref name="globe">[https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/books-and-media/memoir-by-rajiv-surendra-traces-actors-parallels-with-life-of-pi/article32930166/ "Memoir by Rajiv Surendra traces actor's parallels with Life of Pi"]. ''[[The Globe and Mail]]'', November 18, 2016.</ref> He read ''Life of Pi'' for the first time on the set of ''Mean Girls'' in 2004 and noticed many parallels between his own life and that of the novel's lead character, Piscine Molitor "Pi" Patel, including the fact that Surendra himself grew up in a home immediately adjacent to the [[Toronto Zoo]].<ref name="globe" /> To prepare for the role, Surendra temporarily dropped out of school at the University of Toronto and travelled to [[Pondicherry]], [[India]] to learn the specific dialect of the character. Upon returning to Canada and returning to school, he learned how to swim. While he did meet with the casting director of the film, Surendra was not selected to play the role of Pi.<ref>{{Citation|title=The Elephants in My Backyard {{!}} Rajiv Surendra {{!}} TEDxUofT|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nUn4iTzYPfw|language=en|access-date=2021-09-16}}</ref> He reflected on these experiences in ''The Elephants in My Backyard.'' The book was longlisted for the 2017 edition of ''[[Canada Reads]]''<ref name="cbcbooks" /> and Surendra was nominated for the [[Kobo Emerging Writer Prize]] in 2017.<ref>[http://www.cbc.ca/books/2017/05/teva-harrison-rajiv-surendra-nominated-for-10k-kobo-emerging-writer-prize.html "Teva Harrison, Rajiv Surendra nominated for $10K Kobo Emerging Writer Prize"]. [[CBC Books]], May 1, 2017.</ref> 

=== Fine arts and crafts ===
Surendra is the founder of ''Letters in Ink'', a bespoke [[calligraphy]] and [[graphic design]] service based in [[Manhattan]]. He uses pen and ink and chalk to create art and branding for restaurants and other businesses.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Letters In Ink|url=https://lettersinink.com/|access-date=2021-09-16|language=en-US}}</ref>

While working at [[Black Creek Pioneer Village]], in [[Toronto]] as a teenager, Surendra developed an interest in traditional crafts and antiques. As a result, he practices a number of traditional arts and crafts, including letter writing, calligraphy, [[bookbinding]], and [[painting]]. He is an [[Apprenticeship|apprentice]] to Connecticut-based [[Pottery|potter]] Guy Wolff, whose work has appeared in [[Martha Stewart Living]] magazine.

In 2020, Surendra created a two-video [[YouTube]] series on the art of letter writing for [[Morgan Library & Museum|The Morgan Library & Museum]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Letter-writing is not dead! Part 2: Handwriting is Not Dead!|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fTC_z4kRwbs|language=en|access-date=2021-09-16}}</ref> In 2021, he appeared in a series of videos posted on the HGTV Handmade YouTube channel highlighting his personal collection of handmade objects,<ref>{{Citation|title=Tour Rajiv Surendra’s NYC Apartment Filled With Handmade Decor...and Chalk Art! {{!}} Handmade Home Tour|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Uo2nomf4I0|language=en|access-date=2021-09-16}}</ref> chalk art,<ref>{{Citation|title=A Beginner’s Guide To Chalk Art With Rajiv Surendra {{!}} Chalk Art for Beginners|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4rmwQIL4W0Y|language=en|access-date=2021-09-16}}</ref> bookbinding,<ref>{{Citation|title=Rajiv Surendra’s Bookbinding Guide for Beginners {{!}} Intro to Bookbinding|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ER9FIyYr_QA|language=en|access-date=2021-09-16}}</ref> and [[paper marbling]].<ref>{{Citation|title=A Beginner’s Guide to Paper Marbling, With Rajiv Surendra {{!}} Paper Marbling Tutorial|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3x36f713jZA|language=en|access-date=2021-09-16}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Surendra's parents immigrated to Canada from [[Sri Lanka]]. He grew up in [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]], in a neighbourhood close to [[Toronto Zoo]]. Rajiv attended [[Wexford Collegiate School for the Arts]] as a Musical Theatre student and performer. He graduated in 2003.  He is currently based in [[Manhattan]], where he works as a calligrapher, potter, painter, and chalk artist.<ref name="globe" /> He is gay.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/books/rajiv-surendra-traveled-from-mean-girls-to-chalk-artist/2016/11/15/7c58ee88-909f-11e6-a6a3-d50061aa9fae_story.html|title=Rajiv Surendra traveled from 'Mean Girls' to chalk artist|last=Lee|first=Nicole|date=2016-11-15|work=[[The Washington Post]]|access-date=2019-12-04|language=en-US|issn=0190-8286}}</ref>
