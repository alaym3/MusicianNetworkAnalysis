<!-- Do NOT add "Igorevich". Zaslavski himself has denied it multiple times -->'''Anton Zaslavski''' ({{lang-ru|Антон Заславский}};{{efn|[[romanisation of Russian|Romanised]] ''Anton Zaslavskij'' ({{IPA-ru|ɐnˈton zɐsˈlafskʲɪj|pron}})}} born 2 September 1989), known professionally as '''Zedd''' ({{IPAc-en|z|ɛ|d}}), is a German-Russian DJ, record producer, and songwriter. According to the world's charts, Zedd's most listened songs are "[[The Middle (Zedd, Maren Morris and Grey song)|The Middle]]", "[[Stay (Zedd and Alessia Cara song)|Stay]]", "[[Stay the Night (Zedd song)|Stay the Night"]], "[[Clarity (Zedd song)|Clarity]]", and "[[I Want You to Know (Zedd song)|I Want You to Know]]".  

Zedd grew up and began his musical journey in [[Kaiserslautern]], Germany.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/431777/zedd/biography|title=Zedd – Biography |work=Billboard|access-date=28 October 2016}}</ref> His stage name, Zedd, was derived from ''zed'', the [[Z#Name and pronunciation|English pronunciation]], barring [[American English]], for the first letter of his surname, [[Z]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://askzedd.tumblr.com/post/9949113478/why-did-you-choose-the-name-zedd|title=Why did you choose the name Zedd?|website=Zedd|access-date=2018-04-03}}</ref> In 2012, he released "[[Clarity (Zedd song)|Clarity]]" (featuring [[Foxes (singer)|Foxes]]), which propelled him to mainstream success, reaching the 8th position on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and earning him a [[Grammy Award|Grammy]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording|Best Dance Recording]] at the [[56th Annual Grammy Awards|56th Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/events/grammys-2014/5885369/zedd-cedric-gervais-take-home-first-grammys |title=Zedd & Cedric Gervais Take Home First Grammys |publisher=Billboard |date=26 January 2014 |access-date=30 January 2014}}</ref> Later in 2012, he released his debut studio album ''[[Clarity (Zedd album)|Clarity]]'', with hit single "[[Stay the Night (Zedd song)|Stay the Night]]" (featuring [[Hayley Williams]]) appearing on the 2013 deluxe edition of the album.

In 2015, his second studio album ''[[True Colors (Zedd album)|True Colors]]'' was released, supported by its lead single "[[I Want You to Know (Zedd song)|I Want You to Know]]" featuring [[Selena Gomez]]. Subsequent well-known songs include "[[Stay (Zedd and Alessia Cara song)|Stay]]" with [[Alessia Cara]], peaking at number 7, "[[The Middle (Zedd, Maren Morris and Grey song)|The Middle]]" with [[Maren Morris]] and [[Grey (duo)|Grey]], and, as a featured artist, "[[Break Free (song)|Break Free]]" with [[Ariana Grande]], peaking at number 4 in the US.

==Early life==
Anton Zaslavski<ref>{{cite tweet|title=I don't have a middle name|first=Anton|last=Zazlavski|user=Zedd|number=650783082728042497|date=4 October 2015|access-date=24 February 2018}}</ref> was born on 2 September 1989 in [[Saratov]], [[Soviet Russia]], to a [[Jewish]] family.<ref>{{cite tweet |user=zedd |number=1268096678189383680 |date= 3 June 2020 |title=I grew up in Germany and my family is Jewish. Trust me. I know what a dictator is.}}</ref> He grew up in [[Dansenberg]], [[Kaiserslautern]], Germany,<ref name="Allmusic: Zedd (Biography)">{{cite web|last=Heaney|first=Gregory|title=Zedd Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/zedd-mn0002782403/biography|access-date=1 September 2013|work=[[AllMusic]]|publisher=[[Rovi Corporation]]}}</ref> after his family moved there when Anton was three years old.<ref name="forbes1">{{cite web|last1=O'Malley Greenburg|first1=Zack|title=Zedd Ahead: Meet The Next Big Star Of Electronic Music|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2013/08/14/zedd-ahead-meet-the-next-big-star-of-electronic-music|website=[[Forbes]]|access-date=10 October 2018|date=14 August 2013}}</ref> He reportedly is a classically trained musician, the son of two musicians. His father, Igor Zaslavski, is a guitarist and schoolteacher, and his mother is a piano teacher.<ref name="forbes1"/><ref name="family1">{{cite web|title=Watch Zedd Playing Music As A Child, Along With His Brother And Father|url=http://edm.com/articles/2014-02-21/zedd-family|website=EDM.com|access-date=26 June 2015|date=21 February 2014}}</ref> He began playing the piano at the age of four and drums at twelve years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theworstguy.com/2010/11/04/zedd-exclusive-interview|title=Zedd Exclusive Interview|publisher=The Worst Guy|date=23 December 2011|access-date=7 November 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.upcoming-soon.com/exclusive-interview-with-zedd|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130313173745/http://www.upcoming-soon.com/exclusive-interview-with-zedd|archive-date=13 March 2013|title=Zedd – 'The important point of the actual music is being neglected a bit'|publisher=Upcoming-Soon|date=5 June 2011|access-date=7 November 2012}}</ref> He has an older brother, Arkadi, and a younger half brother, Daniel.<ref name="family1"/> In 2002, Zaslavski began playing for the German [[deathcore]] band Dioramic, signed by Lifeforce Records. Zaslavski's interest in producing electronic music was piqued after hearing ''[[Cross (Justice album)|Cross]]'' by the French electronic duo [[Justice (band)|Justice]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.310electro.com/2010/12/an-early-christmas-gift-an-interview-with-zedd|title=An Early Christmas Gift! An Interview with Zedd|publisher=310electro.com|access-date=7 November 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120331162901/http://www.310electro.com/2010/12/an-early-christmas-gift-an-interview-with-zedd/|archive-date=31 March 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==
=== 2010–2011: Beginnings ===
[[File:Zedd Logo.png|thumb|Zedd logo]]
After leaving Dioramic in 2010, Zedd won two [[Beatport]] remix contests in 2010;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/digital-and-mobile/6176867/beatports-new-president-lloyd-starr-on-download-farms|title=Beatport's New President Lloyd Starr on Download Farms, What EDM Even Is|publisher=Billboard|date=23 July 2014|access-date=7 August 2014|first=Hayley|last=Brown}}</ref> his first original release, "The Anthem", entered the Beatport Electro house chart in the top 20.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pro.beatport.com/artist/zedd/126776|title=Zedd – May 2010 Chart|website=pro.beatport.com|access-date=25 August 2015}}</ref>

He also remixed [[Skrillex]]'s "[[Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites (song)|Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites]]". He has since produced remixes of well-known artists, including [[Justin Bieber]], [[Lady Gaga]], [[the Black Eyed Peas]], [[Periphery (band)|Periphery]] and others. Zedd now sits as one of the top producers of the decade.<ref>{{cite web|first=Christina|last=Lee|url=http://www.idolator.com/7529355/zedd-magic-rude-remix-lollapalooza|title=MAGIC!'s "Rude" Gets Remixed By Zedd At Lollapalooza|publisher=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|date=2 August 2014|access-date=7 August 2014}}</ref> Zaslavski creates his music using the [[Steinberg Cubase|Cubase]] suite of music production applications, and uses plug-ins such as the Sylenth1, Nexus, SynthMaster 2.6, and Omnisphere synthesizers, and the Kontakt sampler.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://djtimes.com/in-the-studio-with-zedd-going-for-clarity|title=In The Studio With Zedd: Going for Clarity|first=Lina|last=Abascal|work=DJ Times|date=1 December 2012|access-date=7 August 2014}}</ref>

Zedd's remix of Lady Gaga's track "[[Born This Way (song)|Born This Way]]" appeared on the special edition of her third studio album, ''[[Born This Way (album)|Born This Way]]'' (2011).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idolator.com/6051871/lady-gagas-born-this-way-the-remix-the-collection-cover|title=Lady Gaga's 'Born This Way: The Remix' & 'The Collection' Covers Revealed|publisher=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|date=21 October 2011|first=Robbie|last=Daw|access-date=7 August 2014}}</ref> In 2011, his single "[[Shave It Up]]" (then known as "Shave It") became Zedd's first release on [[OWSLA]]. Zedd went on to release "Slam the Door" and "Shotgun" with OWSLA before departing to release his debut album in 2013.{{citation needed|date=December 2015}}

===2012–2014: ''Clarity''===
{{Multiple image|perrow = 1|image1=Porter Robinson, Zedd, and Skrillex at the 2012 SXSW cropped.jpg|caption1=[[Porter Robinson]], Zedd, and [[Skrillex]] performing at [[SXSW]] on 16 March 2012|image2=Zedd at 2013 Moment of Clarity at the Aragon, Chicago.jpg|caption2=Zedd at 2013 Moment of Clarity World Tour at the Aragon, Chicago}}
In 2012, Zedd signed with [[Interscope Records]] and released his debut single from the label, "[[Spectrum (Zedd song)|Spectrum]]", with lyrics and vocals by [[Matthew Koma]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Zedd Bio, Music, News & Shows|url=http://djz.com/featured_djs/zedd/|publisher=DJZ.com|access-date=11 March 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130330000050/http://djz.com/featured_djs/zedd/|archive-date=30 March 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref> It shot to the top of [[Dance Club Songs|''Billboard''{{'}}s Hot Dance Club Songs]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2012-09-01/dance-club-play-songs|title=Dance/Club Play Songs – Week of 1 September 2012|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=13 October 2012}}</ref> He then produced the [[Eva Simons]] song, "[[I Don't Like You]]", which was also released on Interscope. He also produced and co-wrote the third single "[[Beauty and a Beat]]" with [[Max Martin]] for Justin Bieber's third studio album, ''[[Believe (Justin Bieber album)|Believe]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Vena|first=Jocelyn|title=Justin Bieber Didn't 'Convince' 'Beauty And A Beat' Producer Zedd At First|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1695750/justin-bieber-zedd-beauty-beat.jhtml|publisher=MTV|access-date=19 November 2013}}</ref>

On 12 October 2012, Zedd released his debut studio album, ''[[Clarity (Zedd album)|Clarity]]'', with "[[Shave It]]" as the album's first single and "[[Spectrum (Zedd song)|Spectrum]]" as its second single. "[[Clarity (Zedd song)|Clarity]]", featuring British singer-songwriter [[Foxes (singer)|Foxes]], was released on 1 February 2013 as the album's third single. It charted in the top 10 of [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], sold 2,238,000 copies, and was [[Music recording sales certification|certified platinum]], making it Zedd's most successful single to date. The song also became the #1 most played on American radio on 10 September 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Zedd's 'Clarity' Finally Hits #1 On US Radio|url=http://www.edmtunes.com/2013/09/zedds-clarity-finally-hits-1-on-us-radio|website=EDMTunes|access-date=21 August 2015}}</ref> Zedd concluded 2013 with his first worldwide tour, selling out venues across 5 continents.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Zedd – Moment Of Clarity World Tour|url = http://www.momentofclaritytour.com|website=www.momentofclaritytour.com|access-date=20 August 2015}}</ref>

Zedd worked with Japanese singer [[Namie Amuro]] and produced her promotional single, "[[Heaven (Namie Amuro song)|Heaven]]" for her album ''[[Feel (Namie Amuro album)|Feel]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://dimension-point.jp/amuro/_genic/ |title=安室奈美恵 NEW ALBUM「_genic」 SPECIAL WEBSITE |access-date=10 April 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171025011318/http://dimension-point.jp/amuro/_genic/ |archive-date=25 October 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Zedd would later work with her again in 2015 for her album, ''[[genic]]'', although none of his songs were actually featured on the album.<ref>{{cite AV media notes|type=Album liner notes|title=Genic|date=10 June 2015|language=ja|publisher=[[Avex Trax]]|location=Tokyo, Japan}}</ref>

"[[Stay the Night (Zedd song)|Stay the Night]]", featuring [[Hayley Williams]], was released as a single on 10 September 2013, as part of the deluxe version of Zedd's album. The song met similar success, although it was criticized for its lack of uniqueness and less powerful lyrics compared to "Clarity".<ref>{{cite web|last=Shotwell|first=James|title=Zedd Streams "Stay the Night" Featuring Hayley Williams of Paramore|url=http://www.underthegunreview.net/2013/09/10/zedd-streams-stay-the-night-featuring-hayley-williams-of-paramore|work=Under The Gun Review|publisher=[[Spin Media]]|access-date=18 October 2013}}</ref> It ranked in ''Billboard'' Hot 100's top 20, and certified platinum as well.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Zedd Announces 'Moment of Clarity' Documentary Release for 18 December|url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/20068/20141214/zedd-announces-moment-clarity-documentary-release-dec-18.htm|access-date=20 August 2015}}</ref> In 2013, Zedd produced "[[Aura (song)|Aura]]", "[[G.U.Y.]]", and "Donatella" on Lady Gaga's 2013 album, ''[[Artpop]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1700630/lady-gaga-zedd-artpop-album-update.jhtml|title=Lady Gaga Has 'A Lot Of Work Left' On ARTPOP Songs |publisher=MTV|access-date=21 June 2013}}</ref> On 26 January 2014, he released the single "[[Find You (Zedd song)|Find You]]" featuring Matthew Koma and [[Miriam Bryant]], which is included on [[Divergent (soundtrack)|the soundtrack]] for the film ''[[Divergent (film)|Divergent]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a558392/zedd-premieres-divergent-music-video-for-find-you-watch.html#~oMfpGaHLQ2nuGm|title=Zedd premieres Divergent music video for 'Find You'|publisher=[[Digital Spy]]|date=18 March 2014|access-date=7 August 2014|first=Sam|last=Rigby}}</ref>

On 3 July 2014, [[Ariana Grande]] released a song with Zedd entitled "[[Break Free (song)|Break Free]]",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6128769/ariana-grande-break-free-zedd-interview-video-new-single-problem|title=Ariana Grande's 'Break Free': Zedd Discusses The 'Problem' Follow-Up|last=Lipshutz|first=Jason|publisher=Billboard|date=23 June 2014|access-date=28 July 2014}}</ref> which peaked at No. 4 on the Billboard Hot 100 and peaked at No. 1 on the [[Hot Dance/Electronic Songs]].<ref name="TripleHot100">{{cite web|last=Trust|first=Gary|title=Ariana Grande, Iggy Azalea Triple Up In Hot 100's Top 10, MAGIC! Still No. 1|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6221978/hot-100-ariana-grande-iggy-azalea-top-10-magic|work=Billboard|access-date=20 August 2014}}</ref> The song was nominated at the [[Teen Choice Awards]] in the category of "Break-Up Song".<ref name="TCA2014">{{cite web|url=http://popcrush.com/2014-teen-choice-awards-nominees|title=2014 Teen Choice Awards Nominees Announced|website=PopCrush|access-date=11 November 2015}}</ref> On 18 December 2014, ''Moment of Clarity'', a documentary about Zedd and his 2013 world tour, was released through his YouTube channel, with [[Sandra Bullock]], [[Rachel Weisz]] and [[Julia Roberts]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=a2AjlTLdU1M|title=Zedd-Moment of Clarity (Documentary)|access-date=20 August 2015|website=Youtube}}</ref>

===2017–present: Upcoming third studio album===
[[File:Zedd at Veld 2017.jpg|thumb|upright|Zedd performing at the 2017 [[VELD Music Festival]]]]
In 2017, he organized a benefit concert for the American Civil Liberties Union, prompted in response to US President Donald Trump's executive order to "suspended all refugee admissions for 120 days and blocked citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries, refugees or otherwise".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7677763/zedd-aclu-benefit-welcome-lineup-skrillex-incubus-macklemore|title=Zedd's ACLU Benefit Concert to Feature Skrillex, Incubus, Macklemore & More|website=billboard.com|access-date=2 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://radio.com/2017/02/02/zedd-organizes-welcome-concert-aclu/|title=Zedd Organizes 'Welcome' Concert to Benefit ACLU|website=radio.com|access-date=2 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.youredm.com/2017/02/02/zedd-rolls-details-aclu-benefit-concert-welcome/|title=Zedd Drops Stacked Lineup With Skrillex, Halsey, Macklemore & More For ACLU Benefit Concert|first=Matthew|last=Meadow|date=2 February 2017|website=youredm.com|access-date=2 June 2017}}</ref> The line-up included [[Daya (singer)|Daya]], [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]], [[Macklemore]], [[Tinashe]] and [[Skrillex]]. On 23 February 2017, Zedd released his collaboration along with [[Alessia Cara]], "[[Stay (Zedd and Alessia Cara song)|Stay]]" which debuted at number 28 on the Billboard Hot 100 and 33 on the UK Singles Chart.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Alessia Cara and Zedd's New Single "Stay" Is Finally Here|url = http://www.teenvogue.com/story/alessia-cara-zedds-new-single-stay|access-date = 23 February 2017}}</ref> On 6 July 2017, Zedd released his single with [[Liam Payne]], "[[Get Low (Zedd and Liam Payne song)|Get Low]]".<ref>[https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/entertainthis/2017/07/06/get-low-liam-payne-zedd-release-summer-anthem/454969001/ Liam Payne, Zedd invite you to 'Get Low' in catchy summer anthem].</ref>

On 23 January 2018, Zedd released his single with [[Maren Morris]] and [[Grey (duo)|Grey]], "[[The Middle (Zedd, Maren Morris and Grey song)|The Middle]]", which rose to No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' [[Pop Songs]] radio chart and peaked at No. 5 spot on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8279839/zedd-maren-morris-grey-the-middle-number-one-pop-songs-chart|title=Zedd, Maren Morris & Grey's 'The Middle' Hits No. 1 on Pop Songs Airplay Chart|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-04-20}}</ref> In March 2018, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' named Zedd as number seven on their 2018 ranking of dance musicians titled Billboard Dance 100.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8257429/billboard-dance-100-2018 |title=Billboard Dance 100 Artists of 2018: The Complete List |website=Billboard |access-date=2018-03-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.wjbdradio.com/music-news/2018/03/22/the-chainsmokers-named-top-djs-in-the-world-by-billboard |title=The Chainsmokers named top DJs in the world by Billboard |website=WJBD Radio |access-date=2018-03-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180322221157/http://www.wjbdradio.com/music-news/2018/03/22/the-chainsmokers-named-top-djs-in-the-world-by-billboard |archive-date=22 March 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Zedd was tapped by National Geographic to make a song inspired by the documentary series ''[[One Strange Rock]]'' and released a video to the song, also titled "One Strange Rock" on his YouTube channel on 9 April 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zedd Lets Fans Inside His Head While Creating Song Inspired By 'One Strange Rock' National Geographic Series: Watch |first=Kat |last=Bein |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8292769/zedd-national-geographic-one-strange-rock-song-making-of |date=6 April 2018 |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=30 May 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180524104015/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8292769/zedd-national-geographic-one-strange-rock-song-making-of |archive-date=24 May 2018 }}</ref> On 17 July 2018, Zedd released his single with [[Elley Duhé]], "[[Happy Now (Zedd and Elley Duhé song)|Happy Now]]". The accompanying music video was later released 23 August 2018.

On 27 September 2018, Zedd released his remix of the [[Shawn Mendes]] single "[[Lost in Japan]]".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Spanos |first1=Britney |title=Hear Zedd's Glitchy Remix of Shawn Mendes' 'Lost in Japan' |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/zedd-shawn-mendes-lost-in-japan-remix-729917/ |website=Rolling Stone}}</ref> This single quickly rose onto the Top 40 charts and was the first collaboration between the two artists. They performed this song at the 2018 American Music Awards on 10 October 2018 and then performed it again at the [[Victoria's Secret Fashion Show]] on 8 November 2018. The accompanying music video was released following both performances on 9 November 2018.

On 14 February 2019, Zedd released his collaboration with [[Katy Perry]], "[[365 (Zedd and Katy Perry song)|365]]", along with the song's music video.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Spanos |first1=Brittany |title=Watch Robot Katy Perry Learn to Love Human Zedd in '365' Video |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/katy-perry-zedd-collaboration-365-video-794260/ |website=Rolling Stone |date=14 February 2019}}</ref>

Zedd performed at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] on the main stage 21 April 2019, bringing out [[Katy Perry]], [[Alessia Cara]], and [[Maren Morris]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8508042/zedd-maren-morris-alessia-cara-coachella|title=Zedd Brings Out Maren Morris, Alessia Cara Out For Coachella Weekend 2 Set|website=Billboard}}</ref> On 1 May 2019, Zedd announced the Orbit Tour, which kicks off in the fall of 2019. He will tour along with duo [[NOTD]] and [[Jax Jones]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/zedd-orbit-world-tour-829589/|title=Zedd Announces 'Orbit' Tour in North America|first1=Claire|last1=Shaffer|date=1 May 2019}}</ref>

On 31 May 2019, [[Katy Perry]] released her single "[[Never Really Over]]" in which Zedd is a producer and a songwriter on the track. The song debuted at number 15 on the US Billboard Hot 100.<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/artist/katy-perry/chart-history/hsi/ ]</ref>

On 27 September 2019, Zedd released the single "[[Good Thing (Zedd and Kehlani song)|Good Thing]]" with [[Kehlani]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Legaspi |first1=Althea |title=Hear Zedd and Kehlani's Bouncy Singles Anthem 'Good Thing' |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/zedd-kehlani-good-thing-song-listen-891406/ |website=Rolling Stone |date=27 September 2019}}</ref>

On 10 October 2019, Zedd [[Twitter|tweeted]] that he had been "permanently banned" from China due to his liking of a tweet related to ''[[South Park]]'', an American [[Adult animation|adult]] [[animated sitcom]] which has been banned in the country several days prior as a result of the episode "[[Band in China]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/Zedd/status/1182376966495838208|title=I just got permanently banned from China because I liked a @SouthPark tweet.|date=10 October 2019}}</ref>
This seemingly only applies to live performances however, as much of his music remains easily accessible within the country.

On 16 July 2020, Zedd released the single "[[Funny (Zedd and Jasmine Thompson song)|Funny]]" with [[Jasmine Thompson]], along with the song's music video.

On 23 October 2020, Zedd released the single "[[Inside Out (Zedd and Griff song)|Inside Out]]" with [[Griff (singer)|Griff]].

On 7 September 2021, [[Valorant]] announced the collaboration between Zedd and Valorant to create different cosmetic weapon skins which featured exclusive sound effects, as well as a custom tune on the weapons when inspected.
