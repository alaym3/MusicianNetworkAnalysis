'''Kari Brooke Jobe''' (born April 6, 1981) is an American [[contemporary Christian music]] singer and [[songwriter]]. Since her first album in 2009, she has received two [[Grammy Award]] nominations and ten [[Dove Award]] nominations, six of which she won.

== Biography ==

Kari Jobe was born in 1981, in [[Waco, Texas]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Heath|first=Brandon|title=New Artist Spotlight: Kari Jobe|url=http://www.crosswalk.com/music/11605124/|work=CCMagazine.com|publisher=Salem Communications Corporation|access-date=March 14, 2011}}</ref> to parents Mark and Caroline "Sandy" (née Bragg) Jobe and was raised in [[Watauga, Texas]], and [[Hurst, Texas]]; both suburbs of [[Fort Worth]]. She has two siblings, Kristen and Caleb. Jobe began singing at the age of three<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.karijobe.com/kari.php |title=About |publisher=Kari Jobe |date=January 24, 2012 |access-date=May 13, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120120164343/http://www.karijobe.com/kari.php |archive-date=January 20, 2012 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> and became a Christian at the age of five. As she grew older, she attended [[Oral Roberts University]], [[Christ for the Nations Institute]] and [[Dallas Baptist University]] and obtained her degree in [[biblical studies|pastoral studies]] and [[psychology]].<ref> Steve Wildsmith, [https://www.thedailytimes.com/entertainment/winter-jam-artist-spotlight-kari-jobe-combines-a-love-of/article_1fdb6922-3ad8-5b57-9300-d56cdb95f295.html ‘Winter Jam’ artist spotlight: Kari Jobe combines a love of ministry and music], thedailytimes.com, USA, March 14, 2012</ref>

She became known with the successes "Revelation Song" and "Holy Spirit" released in 2009. <ref> Micah Smith, [http://www.jacksonfreepress.com/news/2017/mar/01/kari-jobe-growth-garden/ ‘Kari Jobe: Growth in ‘The Garden’], jacksonfreepress.com, USA, March 1, 2017 </ref>

After graduation, Jobe accepted an invitation to serve as associate worship pastor at [[Gateway Church (Texas)|Gateway Church]] in [[Southlake, Texas]], where she had been attending for six years. She worked alongside her father, Mark, who serves as campus pastor of Gateway's flagship campus in Southlake.<ref> Nancy De Gennaro, [https://www.dnj.com/story/life/2015/01/29/kari-jobe-concert-world-outreach/22550181/ Kari Jobe in concert at World Outreach], dnj.com, USA, January 29, 2015 </ref> She has traveled internationally on mission trips to lead worship.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.delicatefade.com/artist.asp?ID=1104 |title=Kari Jobe Chord Charts &#124; Kari Jobe Lyrics &#124; Kari Jobe Biography and Discography |publisher=Delicatefade.com |access-date=March 21, 2012}}</ref> Jobe was also a member of [[Gateway Worship]], a Christian worship band associated with Gateway Church. Their live album, ''Wake Up the World'', debuted at No. 2 on ''Billboard'''s Christian Albums chart in 2008.

Aside from being a worship leader and pastor, Jobe released her first major label self-titled debut album in February 2009 by [[Integrity Music]]/[[Columbia Records]]/Gateway Create.

== 2009–2011: ''Kari Jobe'', ''Le Canto'' ==

Jobe released her [[Kari Jobe (album)|self-titled debut album]] on February 10, 2009. The album charted at No. 67 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]<ref>[http://billboard.biz/bbcom/discography/index.jsp?pid=837252&aid=1207755]{{dead link|date=March 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.gospelmusicweek.com/?p=226 |title=SoGospel.Com &#124; Southern Gospel Music Lifestyles, News And Reviews Online |publisher=Gospelmusicweek.com |access-date=March 21, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110102230404/http://www.gospelmusicweek.com/?p=226 |archive-date=January 2, 2011 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> and No. 3 on ''Billboard'''s Christian music chart. A Spanish-language version of the album was released under the name ''[[Le Canto (album)|Le Canto]]''. The album ''Le Canto'' won a [[Dove Award]] in the category, Spanish Album of the Year at the [[41st GMA Dove Awards]].<ref>[http://blogs.tennessean.com/tunein/2010/02/18/41st-annual-gma-dove-awards-nominees-announced/ 41st Annual GMA Dove Awards nominees announced] on [[The Tennessean]] (February 18, 2010)</ref>
Prior to releasing ''Kari Jobe'', Jobe had released a compilation album, ''Kari Jobe'', a live concert album, ''Throneroom Worship: Live Acoustic Worship'', and a Christmas album, ''Bethlehem''.

From her self-titled album, "[[I'm Singing]]" debuted at No. 13 on ''Billboard'''s Christian singles chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gannsdeen.com/2009/04/30/mandisa-earns-5th-top-10-hit/ |title=Mandisa earns 5th Top 10 hit! &#124; Filipino-Christian Blogger Ganns Deen |publisher=Gannsdeen.com |date=April 30, 2009 |access-date=March 21, 2012}}</ref> Jobe's second single, "Healer", was released in 2009 and debuted at No. 33 on Billboard's Soft A/C chart.<ref>[https://archive.today/20110809131933/http://www.breathecast.com/Christian.Music.News-%E2%80%9CHealer%E2%80%9D.Becoming.New.Hit.Song.For.Kari.Jobe.And.Subject.Of.Inspiring.Video/1_3183.htm]</ref> She released the single "Adore Him" in 2009, which appeared as part of Integrity Music's compilation album ''Worship and Adore: A Christmas Offering''.

She won two [[Dove Awards]] for Special Event Album of the Year and Spanish Language album and was nominated for New Artist of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/news/2010/02/23.INTEGRITY%20MUSICS%2018%20DOVE%20AWARD%20NOMINATIONS%20INCLUDE%20MULTIPLE%20NODS%20FOR%20KARI%20JOBE%20ISRAEL%20HOUGHTON%20AND%20PAUL%20BALOCHE.asp |title=Music News, February 2010: Integrity Music's 18 Dove Award Nominations Include Multiple Nods for Kari Jobe, Israel Houghton and Paul Baloche |publisher=Jesusfreakhideout.com |access-date=March 21, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/karijobe/status/12614496299 |title=Twitter / Kari Jobe: Well guys..I get to come h |publisher=Twitter.com |date=April 21, 2010 |access-date=March 21, 2012}}</ref>

In 2011, Jobe signed with EMI CMG Sparrow Records.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cmspin.com/newsmanager/anmviewer.asp?a=6309&z=26 |title=Kari Jobe Signed To Sparrow Records |publisher=Gospelmusicweek.com |access-date=August 1, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130723231316/http://www.cmspin.com/newsmanager/anmviewer.asp?a=6309&z=26 |archive-date=July 23, 2013 |url-status=dead  }}</ref>

== 2012–2017: ''Where I Find You'', ''Majestic'', ''Donde Te Encuentro'' and ''Majestic Revisited'' ==
[[File:Kari Jobe at Burning Lights (cropped).jpg|thumb|right|Jobe performing in 2013]]
Jobe released her second recording project, ''Where I Find You'', on January 24, 2012. The album debuted at No. 75 on the [[Canadian Albums Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Charts/ALBUMS.html |title=CANOE - JAM! Music SoundScan Charts |publisher=Jam.canoe.ca |access-date=March 21, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20041226005640/http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Charts/ALBUMS.html |archive-date=December 26, 2004 }}</ref> The album also debuted at No. 10 on the ''[[Billboard 200]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2012-02-11 |title=Billboard Top 200 Albums, Week of February 11, 2012|publisher=Billboard.com |access-date=April 3, 2017}}</ref> Kari released the album ''[[Where I Find You]]'' in Spanish called ''[[Donde Te Encuentro]]'', her second album released on January 24, 2012.<ref>{{cite web |title=Kari Jobe Donde Te Encuentro |url=https://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/DondeTeEncuentro.asp |publisher=Jesusfreakhideout |access-date=26 May 2021}}</ref> The album debuted at chart No. 6 on the [[Latin Pop Albums]]<ref>{{cite web |title=Kari Jobe {{!}} Billboard |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/kari-jobe/chart-history/LPP |publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=31 May 2021}}</ref> and on the chart No. 19 on the [[Top Latin Albums]]<ref>{{cite web |title=Kari Jobe {{!}} Billboard |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/kari-jobe/chart-history/LCM |publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=31 May 2021}}</ref> by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''.

Jobe later released her first EP, ''The Acoustic Sessions (Live)'', later that year.

Jobe received her first [[Grammy]] nomination for ''Where I Find You'' as Best Contemporary Christian Album in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/karijobe/status/276558609901551616 |title=Twitter / karijobe: Cannot believe I'm nominated |publisher=Twitter.com |access-date=May 13, 2013}}</ref>

In May 2013, Jobe competed in season two of [[Game Show Network|GSN]]'s ''[[The American Bible Challenge]]'', along with [[Sheila Walsh (singer)|Sheila Walsh]] and author Lisa Harper. Their charity was [[The A21 Campaign]], an anti-trafficking organization that aims to abolish slavery.<ref>{{cite web|title=Women of Faith on The American Bible Challenge|url=http://www.womenoffaith.com/2013/04/women-of-faith-on-american-bible-challenge/|access-date=May 13, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130524023228/http://www.womenoffaith.com/2013/04/women-of-faith-on-american-bible-challenge/|archive-date=May 24, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Jobe announced the recording of a live worship album that was recorded on November 20 and 21, 2013, at the [[Majestic Theatre (Dallas, Texas)|Majestic Theater]] in [[Dallas, Texas]].<ref>"[http://theundergroundsite.com/kari-jobe-announces-new-album-headlines-tour/ Kari Jobe announces new album, headlines tour.]" The Underground. Retrieved February 28, 2014.</ref> ''Majestic'' was released on March 25, 2014, and was preceded by its first single, "Forever", on February 18.<ref>{{cite web |title=Kari Jobe - Majestic (Deluxe Edition/Live) |url=http://www.capitolchristiandistribution.com/products/detail.aspx?iid=2015920 |access-date=February 18, 2014}}</ref>

''[[Majestic: Revisited|Majestic Revisited]]'' was released on September 25, 2015. The album include songs from ''Majestic'' rerecorded in the studio and in a different style.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Kari Jobe Announces 'Majestic Revisited' for September Release; New Cover Unveiled [PICTURE]|date = July 24, 2015|url = http://www.breathecast.com/articles/kari-jobe-announces-majestic-revisited-for-september-release-new-cover-unveiled-picture-29964/|access-date = September 9, 2015}}</ref>

==2017–present: ''The Garden''==

"The Cause of Christ" was released on November 4, 2016, as the lead single from her fourth studio album, ''[[The Garden (Kari Jobe album)|The Garden]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/new-music/2797-kari-jobe-the-cause-of-christ-lyric-video|title=Kari Jobe Releases New Single "The Cause of Christ"|publisher=The Christian Beat|access-date=November 11, 2016}}</ref> Three other singles: "Heal Our Land", "Fall Afresh" and "The Garden"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/new-music/2903-kari-jobe-releases-new-song-heal-our-land |title=Kari Jobe Releases New Single "Heal Our Land"|publisher=The Christian Beat |access-date=February 24, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/official-videos/3023-kari-jobe-fall-afresh-lyric-video |title=Kari Jobe "Fall Afresh" Lyric Video |publisher=The Christian Beat|access-date=February 24, 2017}}</ref> were released before the album's release on February 3, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.933fmthefish.com/content/music/kari-jobe-announces-her-new-studio-album-the-garden|title=Kari Jobe Announces Her New Studio Album, "The Garden", Set To Release February 3 |date=December 27, 2016 |publisher=93.3 Fish on Air|access-date=February 4, 2017}}</ref>

== Personal life ==

In August 2014, Jobe confirmed via Instagram that she became engaged to fellow Gateway Worship artist [[Cody Carnes]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://instagram.com/p/rm0d96BoXI |title=Instagram |publisher=Instagram |access-date=August 13, 2014}}</ref> The two married on November 21, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://instagram.com/p/vtln58BoUi|title=Instagram|access-date=April 2, 2016}}</ref>

On the red carpet at the Dove Awards 2015, Jobe announced she and her husband were expecting a son.<ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AToaB-oWk34|title=Kari Jobe, #DoveAwards 2015 red carpet|date=October 13, 2015}}</ref> Jobe announced in June 2018 that she and her husband were expecting their second child,<ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/kari-jobe-shares-heartwarming-baby-announcement-ahead-of-fourth-of-july-photo-225689/|title=Kari Jobe Shares Heartwarming Baby Announcement Ahead of Fourth of July|author=Law, Jennie|publisher=Christian Post|date=July 4, 2018|access-date=July 7, 2018}}</ref> who was born on February 1, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Kari Jobe & Cody Carnes Welcome Baby #2 |url=http://www.gospelmusic.org/kari-jobe-cody-carnes-welcome-baby-2/ |website=Gospel Music Association |date=February 7, 2019 |publisher=GMA |access-date=October 7, 2019}}</ref>

In December 2019, the couple visited the White House and prayed for President [[Donald Trump]]. They also appeared in a video praising the Trump administration.<ref>{{Cite web|last=RELEVANT|date=December 9, 2019|title=Kari Jobe: "I'm So Thankful to ...See What God's Doing in Our White House."|url=https://relevantmagazine.com/culture/kari-jobe-im-so-thankful-to-see-what-gods-doing-in-our-white-house/|access-date=September 27, 2020|website=RELEVANT|language=en-US}}</ref> In response to criticism about the visit, the pair said, "We didn't know that we were going to take a picture in the Oval Office or know they were going to post it. I didn't know they were going to use a video interview that we did talking about how amazing it was to be in a White House leading worship."<ref>{{Cite web|date=July 2, 2020|title=Kari Jobe and Cody Carnes on Injustice, 'The Blessing' and Their New Album|url=https://relevantmagazine.com/culture/kari-jobe-and-cody-carnes-on-injustice-the-blessing-and-their-new-album/|access-date=September 27, 2020|website=RELEVANT}}</ref>
