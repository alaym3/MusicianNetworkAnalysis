'''Flow''' (styled as '''FLOW''') is a Japanese [[Rock music|rock]] band formed in 1998 as a five-piece band made up of two vocalists, a drummer, a bassist, and a guitarist.<ref name="JpopAsia">{{cite web|url=http://www.jpopasia.com/flow/|title=Flow Biography|publisher=JpopAsia|access-date=September 19, 2017}}</ref> They are signed to [[Sacra Music]].<ref>{{cite web|archive-url=https://archive.today/20200612011614/https://www.flow-official.jp/news/info.php|url=https://www.flow-official.jp/news/info.php|title=FLOWレーベル移籍のお知らせ|publisher=Amuse|work=Flow|date=June 6, 2020|archive-date=June 12, 2020|access-date=June 11, 2020|language=ja}}</ref> As of December 2021, the band has released 37 singles, 2 collaboration singles with [[Granrodeo]] and 11 studio albums. Their songs have been featured in the opening sequences of several [[anime]] and Japanese drama series.<ref name="AllMusic"/>

==History==
The group began in 1993 when brothers Kōshi (elder: vocals, rhythm guitar) and Take (younger: lead guitar) began playing together.<ref name="Barks"/> They formed Flow in 1998, and by 2000, were joined by Keigo (vocals), Gotō (bass) and Iwasaki (drums).<ref name="Flow official website">{{cite web|url=http://www.flow.mu/bio/|title=Flow Biography|publisher=Flow|access-date=September 19, 2017|language=ja}}</ref> In 2001, the band released its first [[maxi single]], ''Flow #0''. In 2002, the group released two mini-albums within the same year, followed by "Okuru Kotoba", Flow's first [[Cover version|cover]] single, released in January 2003,<ref name="Nihongogo">{{cite web|url=https://nihongogo.com/2015/06/an-interview-with-japanese-rock-band-flow/|title=An Interview with Japanese Rock Band FLOW|publisher=Nihongogo|date=June 30, 2015|access-date=May 11, 2018|last=Asperilla|first=Lawrence}}</ref> which remained on the [[Oricon]] indie chart for seven consecutive weeks and also hit No.&nbsp;6 on the overall singles chart. In the spring of that year, their first full-scale album ''[[Splash (Flow album)|Splash!!!]]'' debuted at No.&nbsp;2 on the Oricon album chart. In July 2003, Flow released the single "[[Blaster (song)|Blaster]]" on the major label [[Ki/oon Music|Ki/oon Records]]. In April 2004, they released "[[Go (Flow song)|GO!!!]]", which stayed on the Oricon Top 10 Chart for three weeks. In May 2004, Flow released their first major label album ''[[Game (Flow album)|Game]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/280660/products/546592/1/|title=''Game'' {{!}} Flow|publisher=[[Oricon]]|access-date=May 11, 2018|language=ja}}</ref>

A string of singles followed and in July 2005 the band released its third album, ''[[Golden Coast (Flow album)|Golden Coast]]''. Since the release of ''Golden Coast'', Flow has released two singles with new A-sides, but one of these ''[[Around The World / Kandata]]'' was a [[double A-side]] release. The songs "[[Go (Flow song)|GO!!!]]" and "[[Re:member]]" both served as opening themes to the anime series ''[[Naruto]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/280660/products/543129/1/|title=Go!!! {{!}} Flow|publisher=[[Oricon]]|access-date=May 11, 2018|language=ja}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/280660/products/651665/1/|title=Re:member {{!}} Flow|publisher=[[Oricon]]|access-date=May 11, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> as well as "[[Sign (Flow song)|Sign]]" for ''[[Naruto Shippuden]]''.<ref name="Sign">{{cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/23795|title=FLOW新作シングルは「NARUTO」を励ますロックチューン|trans-title=FLOW's new single is rock tune encouraging "Naruto"|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=November 15, 2009|access-date=May 11, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> "[[Days (Flow song)|Days]]" was the first opening for [[Bones (studio)|Bones]]' ''[[Eureka Seven]]'' anime,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/280660/products/595625/1/|title=Days {{!}} Flow|publisher=[[Oricon]]|access-date=May 11, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> and "Realize" was the opening for the [[PlayStation 2]] video games based on the same series. Flow performed live in America for the first time in Dallas, Texas on September 2, 2006 at [[AnimeFest]] which was held at the [[Hyatt|Hyatt Hotel]] and [[Kay Bailey Hutchison Convention Center|Convention Center]] in downtown Dallas.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://animecons.com/news/article.shtml/341|title=AnimeFest announces music guest of honor|website=[[Anime convention|AnimeCons.com]]|date=July 25, 2006|access-date=May 11, 2018}}</ref> Flow then released "[[Colors (Flow song)|Colors]]" in 2006, the first opening for [[Sunrise (company)|Sunrise]]'s original series, ''[[Code Geass|Code Geass: Lelouch of the Rebellion]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/280660/products/677969/1/|title=Colors {{!}} Flow|publisher=[[Oricon]]|access-date=May 11, 2018|language=ja}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.barks.jp/news/?id=1000036967|script-title=ja:ガンダムシリーズ、コードギアス、ケロロ軍曹の映像をいつでもどこでも！|trans-title=''Gundam'' series, ''Code Geass'', ''Sergeant Keroro'' anytime, anywhere!|website=Barks Japan|date=January 9, 2008|access-date=May 14, 2018|language=ja}}</ref>

Their song "[[Answer (Flow song)|Answer]]" was the first opening theme for the live action Japanese drama ''[[Tantei Gakuen Q|Detective School Q]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/1955|title=[FLOW] 5周年記念の新曲は初ドラマ主題歌|trans-title=FLOW's 5th anniversary new song be their first drama theme song|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=May 28, 2007|access-date=May 14, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> They performed "[[Fuyu no Amaoto / Night Parade|Night Parade]]" with the hip-hop band [[Home Made Kazoku]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.livedoor.com/article/detail/3336201/|title=FLOW × HOME MADE 家族 ＝ 無限大の可能性|trans-title=FLOW × HOME MADE Kazoku = possibility of infinity|website=[[Livedoor]]|date=October 8, 2007|access-date=May 14, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> In February 2008, they released a new single titled "[[Arigatō (Flow song)|Arigatō]]", followed by ''[[Persona -trinity soul-]]''{{'}}s new opening, "[[Word of the Voice]]" in June 2008. They performed ''[[Code Geass|Code Geass: Lelouch of the Rebellion R2]]'''s second opening theme "[[World End (Flow song)|World End]]" released in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/eiga/news/274900|title=「コードギアス 皇道」OPテーマは「WORLD END」、キービジュアル完成|trans-title=''Code Geass'' OP theme "World End", Key Visual Completed|website=Natalie|date=March 24, 2018|access-date=May 14, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> In 2009, the band performed "Sign", the sixth opening for ''[[Naruto Shippuden]]''.<ref name="Sign"/> The band released a [[A-side and B-side|B-side]] compilation album on November 4, 2009. Their single "[[Calling (Flow song)|Calling]]" was featured as the ending for the anime ''[[Heroman]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tower.jp/article/news/2010/03/08/3764|title=FLOWがテレビ東京系アニメ「HEROMAN」のエンディング・テーマ"CALLING"を5月12日にリリース!|trans-title=FLOW releases "Calling" for the ending theme of TV Tokyo's anime ''Heroman'' on May 12!|website=[[Tower Records Japan]]|date=March 8, 2010|access-date=May 14, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> Flow returned to North America to perform at [[Anime Central]], in Rosemont, Illinois on May 20, 2011, and [[FanimeCon]] in San Jose, California on May 28, 2010 and in May 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2011-04-23/j-pop-unit-flow-to-perform-at-anime-central-fanimecon|title=Flow to Perform at Anime Central and FanimeCon|website=[[Anime News Network]]|last=Loo|first=Egan|date=April 23, 2011|access-date=May 14, 2018}}</ref> Their song "[[Hey (Flow song)|Hey!!!]]" was the third opening theme for the anime ''[[Beelzebub (manga)|Beelzebub]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/88525/full/|title=FLOWの新曲がアニメ「べるぜバブ」主題歌に起用|trans-title=FLOW's new song be a theme song for anime "Beelzebub"|website=[[Oricon|Oricon News]]|date=June 6, 2011|access-date=May 14, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> and the song "[[Brave Blue]]" was used as the second opening theme for the anime ''[[Eureka Seven AO]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/71509|title=「エウレカAO」新テーマはFLOW、新人5人組joyが担当|trans-title=FLOW, and a 5-rookie-person Joy are in charge for ''Eureka AO'' 's new theme|website=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=June 21, 2012|access-date=May 14, 2018|language=ja}}</ref> In 2012, they performed in France for the first time at [[Japan Expo]].

They provided a cover of "[[Cha-La Head-Cha-La]]" as the main theme song for the film ''[[Dragon Ball Z: Battle of Gods]]'';<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.crunchyroll.com/anime-news/2012/12/11-1/flow-to-cover-cha-la-head-cha-la-for-dragon-ball-anime-movie|title=Flow to Cover "Cha-La-Head-Cha-La" for Dragon Ball Anime Movie|website=[[Crunchyroll]]|date=December 12, 2012|access-date=May 11, 2018|last=Green|first=Scott}}</ref> the song, coupled with an insert song from the film, was released as a double A-side single on March 20, 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tokyohive.com/2013/01/flow-announces-new-single-album-nationwide-tour/|title=FLOW announces new single + album + nationwide tour|publisher=Tokyohive|access-date=January 14, 2013}}</ref> and subsequently would be featured as the opening theme for ''[[Dragon Ball]]'' video games such as ''[[Dragon Ball Z: Battle of Z]]'' and ''[[Dragon Ball XenoVerse]]''. Flow released their eighth studio album ''[[Flow The Max|Flow The Max!!!]]'' on March 27, 2013. Flow went to Brazil for the first time to perform at Ressacca Friends in 2013. Their single "Innosense" was released on February 8, 2017; the song was used as the second ending theme of ''Tales of Zestiria the X''. The band covered "[[Classic (Mucc song)|Classic]]" on the album ''Tribute of Mucc -en-'' as a tribute to the band [[Mucc]] released on November 22, 2017.
