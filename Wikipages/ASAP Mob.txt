'''ASAP Mob''' (stylized as '''A$AP Mob''', ASAP being an acronym for "Always Strive and Prosper")<ref name="hypetrak1" /> is an American [[hip hop collective]] formed in 2006 in [[Harlem, New York]],<ref name="villagevoice">{{cite web|last=Pablo|first=J.|title=Meet The A$AP Mob: Talking To Ant, Ferg, J. Scott, Nast, Twelvy, And Yams|url=http://blogs.villagevoice.com/music/2012/08/meet_asap_mob.php|access-date=18 December 2012|archive-date=27 May 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150527034452/http://blogs.villagevoice.com/music/2012/08/meet_asap_mob.php|url-status=dead}}</ref> that consists of New York-based rappers (most of whom carry the "ASAP" [[moniker]]), [[record producer]]s, [[music video director]]s and [[fashion designer]]s.<ref name="hypetrak1">{{cite web |last=Huynh |first=Davis |url=http://hypetrak.com/2012/01/asap-rocky-always-strive-and-prosper/ |title=A$AP Rocky – Always $trive and Prosper |publisher=Hypetrak |access-date=2013-07-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131016011341/http://hypetrak.com/2012/01/asap-rocky-always-strive-and-prosper/ |archive-date=2013-10-16 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

On August 28, 2012, the collective released the [[mixtape]] ''[[Lords Never Worry]]''. They released the single "[[Trillmatic]]" in December 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/a-ap-mob-s-trillmatic-video-count-the-aa99-s-references-20131204|title=A$AP Mob's 'Trillmatic' Video: Count the 1990s References|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref><ref name="rap-up.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/12/03/video-asap-mob-f-asap-nast-method-man-trillmatic/|title=Video: A$AP Mob f/ A$AP Nast & Method Man – 'Trillmatic'|work=Rap-Up|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopleague.com/aap-nast-ft-method-man-trillmatic/|title=A$AP Nast Ft Method Man - Trillmatic - HipHopLeague|work=HipHopLeague|access-date=19 January 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141024074814/http://www.hiphopleague.com/aap-nast-ft-method-man-trillmatic/|archive-date=24 October 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> followed by their debut [[studio album]], ''[[Cozy Tapes Vol. 1: Friends]]'', in October 2016.{{citation needed|date=April 2020}}

==History==

===Formation and early beginnings (2006&ndash;2012)===
In 2006, Steven Rodriguez, professionally known as A$AP Yams, formed the collective with fellow [[New York City|New Yorkers]] A$AP Bari, A$AP Kham, and A$AP Illz.<ref name="villagevoice"/> [[Harlem]] rapper A$AP Rocky joined later.<ref name="villagevoice"/> In the summer of 2011, the group released  [[music video]]s for Rocky's singles "[[Peso (song)|Peso]]" and "Purple Swag," [[Hip hop production|produced]] by A$AP Ty Beats. Rocky followed up with his [[mixtape]] ''[[Live. Love. ASAP|Live.Love.A$AP]]'' in October,{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}} signing a record deal with [[Sony Music Entertainment]] that month.<ref name="Adaso">{{cite web|last=Adaso|first=Henry|url=http://rap.about.com/od/artists/p/Asap-Rocky-Bio.htm|title=A$AP Rocky Biography|publisher=[[About.com]]. The New York Times Company|access-date=June 3, 2012}}</ref><ref name="Coleman">{{cite web|last=Coleman II|first=C. Vernon|date=October 14, 2011|url=http://hiphopwired.com/2011/10/14/asap-rocky-signs-record-deal-with-polo-grounds-musicrca-records/|title=A$AP Rocky Signs Record Deal With Polo Grounds Music/RCA Records|publisher=Hip-Hop Wired|access-date=June 3, 2012}}</ref>

===''Lords Never Worry'' (2012&ndash;2014)===
[[File:Joey Badass x ASAP Mob - Under the Influence Tour 2013 Toronto.jpg|thumb|right|Members of ASAP Mob with [[Joey Badass]] (left) at the Under the Influence Tour in [[Toronto]], [[Canada]] on 10 August 2013]]
On August 27, 2012, A$AP Mob released their debut project, a mixtape titled ''[[Lords Never Worry]]'', as a free download.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/47648-download-the-new-mixtape-lords-never-worry-from-aap-rockys-aap-mob/|title=Download the New Mixtape Lords Never Worry From A$AP Rocky's A$AP Mob|publisher=Pitchfork Media|last=Pelly|first=Jenn|date=August 27, 2012|access-date=August 28, 2012}}</ref> From September to November, Rocky toured with opening acts [[Schoolboy Q]], [[Danny Brown (rapper)|Danny Brown]], and A$AP Mob in support of his solo debut album.<ref name="Tour Information">{{cite magazine|last=McDermott|first=Tyler K.|location=New York|date=July 25, 2012|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/480772/aap-rocky-announces-longliveaap-tour|title=A$AP Rocky Announces 'LONGLIVEA$AP Tour'|magazine=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|access-date=July 26, 2012}}</ref>

In January 2013, [[ASAP Ferg|A$AP Ferg]] signed a joint venture deal with [[RCA Records|RCA]] and [[Polo Grounds Music|Polo Grounds]], which released his single "Work" for retail on iTunes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/01/asap-ferg-signs-solo-deal-with-polo-grounds-musicrca-records|title=A$AP Ferg Signs Solo Deal with Polo Grounds Music/RCA Records|author=Daniel Isenberg|work=Complex|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref> An official [[remix]] of "Work", featuring Rocky, [[French Montana]], [[Schoolboy Q]] and [[Trinidad James]], followed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/asap-ferg-says-work-remix-to-feature-asap-rocky-and-trinidad-james-video.10043.html |title=A$AP Ferg Videos - Says "Work" Remix To Feature A$AP Rocky & Trinidad James |publisher=Hotnewhiphop.com |access-date=2013-07-04}}</ref>

Rocky's debut album ''[[Long. Live. ASAP|Long. Live. A$AP]]'', was released on January 15, 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/longliveaap/aap-rocky|title=Long.Live.A$AP Reviews|website=Metacritic|access-date=March 9, 2013}}</ref> debuting at number one on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, with first-week sales of 139,000 copies in the United States.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22640/title.hip-hop-album-sales-the-week-ending-1-20-2013|title=Hip Hop Album Sales: The Week Ending 1/20/2013 |magazine=HipHopDX |access-date=January 23, 2013}}</ref>

A$AP Ferg announced that A$AP Mob would release their debut album after the release of his solo debut album ''[[Trap Lord]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/07/asap-ferg-teases-new-asap-mob-album/|title=A$AP Ferg Teases New A$AP Mob Album|work=XXL|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref><ref name="hdd">{{cite web|url=http://www.hitsdailydouble.com/sales/sales_ur.cgi|title=HITSDailyDouble: Upcoming Releases|publisher=Hitsdailydouble.com|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref> on August 20, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Trap-Lord-Asap-Ferg/dp/B00DQRHMM8/ref=tmm_acd_title_0?ie=UTF8&qid=1373653161&sr=8-1 |title=Trap Lord: Asap Ferg: Music |website=Amazon |access-date=2013-07-20}}</ref> The album peaked at number nine on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and number four on the [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart, with first-week sales of 32,000 copies in the United States.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.25240/title.hip-hop-album-sales-the-week-ending-8-25-2013|title=Hip Hop Album Sales: The Week Ending 8/25/2013|magazine=HipHopDX|access-date=August 25, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130828151703/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.25240/title.hip-hop-album-sales-the-week-ending-8-25-2013|archive-date=August 28, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> In November 2013, ASAP Rocky announced that ASAP Mob's debut album would be titled ''Lords''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/video/2013/11/powerhouse-2013-crazy/|title=Powerhouse 2013 Was Crazy!|publisher=Xxlmag.com|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref> On December 4, 2013, the album's first [[Single (music)|single]], entitled "[[Trillmatic]]."<ref name="mv">{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/53247-video-aap-mob-trillmatic-ft-aap-nast-and-method-man/|title=Video: A$AP Mob: "Trillmatic" [ft. A$AP Nast and Method Man]|publisher=Pitchfork Media|last=Zoladz|first=Lindsay|date=December 5, 2013|access-date=January 17, 2014}}</ref> The album title was then changed to ''[[L.O.R.D. (album)|L.O.R.D.]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/01/asap-ant-plans-a-robbery-in-see-me-video/|title=A$AP Ant Plans A Robbery In "See Me" Video - XXL|work=XXL|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.27038/title.rap-release-dates-juicy-j-meek-mill-murs-curtiss-king-killa-sha|title=Rap Release Dates: Juicy J, Meek Mill, Murs & Curtiss King, Killa Sha|publisher=Hiphopdx.com|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref> On January 13, 2014, the single "See Me" by ASAP Ant was released.<ref name="seeme">{{cite web|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1628181/aap-ant-see-me-video/video/|title=A$AP Ant – "See Me" Video|work=[[Stereogum]]|publisher=[[Spin Media]]|last=Breihan|first=Tom|date=January 14, 2014|access-date=January 17, 2014}}</ref>

On September 26, 2014, A$AP Yams revealed that ''L.O.R.D.'' had been shelved.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://yamborghini.tumblr.com/post/98450145494/you-my-dude-and-a-ap-is-my-fav-rap-fashionable-but|title=You my dude and A$AP Is my fav rap / fashionable ...|publisher=Yamborghini.tumblr.com|access-date=19 January 2015}}</ref>

===''Cozy Tapes'' series (2015&ndash;present)===
On January 18, 2015, A$AP Mob revealed that A$AP Yams had died<ref name=MTV>{{cite web|last1=White|first1=Caitlin|title=ASAP Mob founder cozy ASAP Yams dead at 26|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2052616/asap-yams-dead-at-26/|publisher=[[MTV News]]|access-date=January 18, 2015|date=January 18, 2015}}</ref> of acute mixed drug intoxication.<ref name="yams"/> On October 15, 2016, A$AP Rocky confirmed that the group's compilation album ''[[Cozy Tapes Vol. 1: Friends]]'' was completed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/asap-rocky-says-asap-mobs-cozy-tape-is-finally-finished-news.24766.html?|title=ASAP Rocky Says ASAP Mob's "Cozy Tape" Is "Finally Finished"|website=HNHH}}</ref> Dedicated to A$AP Yams, the album was released on October 31, 2016, and featured several artists, including [[Playboi Carti]], [[Skepta]] and [[Tyler, The Creator]].

On August 1, 2017, A$AP Rocky announced that ''[[Cozy Tapes Vol. 2: Too Cozy]]'' would be released on August 25, preceded by [[ASAP Twelvyy]]'s ''12'' album on August 4 and [[ASAP Ferg]]'s ''[[Still Striving]]'' album on August 18.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/08/01/asap-mob-cozy-tapes-vol-2-asap-ferg-twelvvy-release-dates |title=A$AP Mob's Cozy Tapes Vol. 2: Too Cozy Is Coming Out This Month|magazine=The Fader|date=2017-08-01}}</ref>

On January 13, 2019, ASAP Ant announced that he was leaving the collective to focus on his solo career.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/asap-ant-has-left-asap-mob-went-solo-news.69189.html |title=A$AP Ant Has Left A$AP Mob: "Went Solo" |website=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=2019-01-13 |access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref> He returned in April, confirming that the collective had begun work on their third studio album, ''Cozy Tapes 3''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/cozy-tapes-3-coming-soon-according-to-asap-ant-news.77863.html |title="Cozy Tapes 3" Coming Soon According To A$AP Ant |website=Hotnewhiphop.com |date=2019-04-21 |access-date=2020-04-09}}</ref>

On January 18, 2015, ASAP Yams was found dead at the age of 26.<ref>Ducker, 
Eric. [https://www.npr.org/blogs/therecord/2015/01/28/382123001/a-rational-conversation-with-asap-yams A Rational Conversation with ASAP Yams]. ''NPR''. Retrieved January 31, 2015.</ref><ref>Matthew Trammell, [http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2016/01/18/also-known "Also Known"] ''[[The New Yorker]]'', January 18, 2016.</ref> The cause of death was ruled an [[Drug overdose|overdose]] due to [[mixed drug intoxication]],<ref name="yams">{{cite web|last1=CARAMANICA|first1=JON|title=Staff Writer|url=http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2015/03/20/asap-yams-died-of-accidental-drug-intoxication-says-chief-medical-examiner/|work=New York Times}}</ref> although ASAP Mob members and affiliates claimed that Yams had died of [[asphyxiation|asphyxiating]] on vomit brought on by [[sleep apnea]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6450852/asap-rocky-asap-yams-death-sundance-2015-dope|title=A$AP Rocky Speaks In-Depth on Death of A$AP Yams For the First Time|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mstarz.com/articles/59509/20150324/asap-rocky-asap-yams-suffocated-vomit-sleep-apnea-killed-video.htm|title=ASAP Rocky Says ASAP Yams Suffocated On Vomit & Sleep Apnea Killed Him |last=Mstarz|date=24 March 2015|access-date=29 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304120703/http://www.mstarz.com/articles/59509/20150324/asap-rocky-asap-yams-suffocated-vomit-sleep-apnea-killed-video.htm|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodtake.com/aap-yams-death-cause-revealed-joey-fatts-denies-overdose-talks-sleep-apnea-steven-69862|title=A$AP Yams' Death Cause Revealed? Joey Fatts Denies Overdose, Talks Sleep Apnea, As Steven Rodriguez Tribute Song Is Released}}</ref>

On February 2, 2020, A$AP J. SCOTT (aka A$AP Snacks) died. He was killed and robbed.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/music/j-scott-of-asap-mob-has-died-asap-rocky-and-more-pay-tribute/|title=J. Scott of A$AP Mob Has Died, A$AP Rocky and More Pay Tribute to the DJ: 'I'm Lost for Words'}}</ref>

Chynna was found dead on April 8, 2020, of an accidental drug overdose. She was 25 years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nbcnews.com/pop-culture/pop-culture-news/philadelphia-rapper-chynna-rogers-dies-25-n1180051|title=Chynna Rogers, Philadelphia rapper, dies of drug overdose at 25|website=[[NBC News]]}}</ref>
