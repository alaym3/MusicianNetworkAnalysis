'''Jaswinder Singh Bains''' ([[Punjabi language|Punjabi]]: ਜਸਵਿੰਦਰ ਸਿੰਘ ਬੈਂਸ, born 1 April 1975), better known by his stage name '''Jazzy B''' is an Indo-Canadian Punjabi singer and songwriter.

==Early life and career==
Jazzy B was born in [[Jalandhar]], [[Punjab, India]]. When he was five years old, his family moved to [[Surrey, British Columbia|Surrey]], [[British Columbia]], Canada.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dailyhive.com/vancouver/canucks-jazzy-b-diwali-night|title=Bhangra superstar Jazzy B to perform at Canucks game next week {{!}} Offside|website=dailyhive.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-26}}</ref> 
His first album ''Gugiyan Da Jora'' was released in 1993. The rhythm was played by [[Sukhshinder Shinda]] who suggested that he move to [[England]] if he wanted a prominent career in [[Punjabi music]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bhangra.org/6-interesting-facts-about-jazzy-b/776801/|title=6 interesting facts about Jazzy B|date=8 September 2016}}</ref>
He is a big fan of the late singer [[Kuldeep Manak]], which is one of the reasons why Jazzy's album, Maharajas, released in 2011,  with Kuldeep's son, Yudhvir, also a singer, is a tribute to Kuldeep Manak, with some of Kuldeep's original vocals retained, and many other songs sung by Jazzy and Yudhvir, are also a tribute to Kuldeep Manak.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.darpanmagazine.com/2009/07/one-on-one-with-the-legendary-jazzy|title=One on One with the Legendary JAZZY B|publisher=Darpan Magazine|date=1 April 1975|access-date=31 January 2012}}</ref>

Jazzy has released 12 studio albums, and two religious ones. He has collaborated on many tracks.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/All-Jazzed-up/articleshow/29091271.cms|title=All Jazzed up!|work=[[The Times of India]]|date=22 November 2002|access-date=18 February 2012|author=Singh, Harneet}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/How-ice-hockey-made-Jaswinder-into-Jazzy/articleshow/1339356087.cms|title=How ice hockey made Jaswinder into Jazzy|work=[[The Times of India]]|date=15 September 2001|access-date=18 February 2012|author=Bhandari, Geetika Sasan}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tribuneindia.com/2001/20011119/login/music.htm|title=Born in India, brought up in Canada – Oh! Keda?|work=[[The Tribune (Chandigarh)|The Tribune]]|date=19 November 2001|access-date=18 February 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tribuneindia.com/2002/20021123/windows/zero.htm|title=The king of bhangra pop|work=[[The Tribune (Chandigarh)|The Tribune]]|date=23 November 2002|access-date=18 February 2012|author=Pal, Dharam}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tribuneindia.com/2008/20080704/ttlife1.htm|title=More than just JAZZ|work=[[The Tribune (Chandigarh)|The Tribune]]|date=4 July 2008|access-date=18 February 2012|author=Khurana, Manpriya}}</ref>

==Film career==
In 2000 Jazzy made his film debut in ''[[Shaheed Udham Singh (film)|Shaheed Udham Singh]]''.
In 2006 he appeared in [[Sunny Deol]]'s movie ''[[Teesri Aankh: The Hidden Camera]]'' with [[Amisha Patel]] in song Chug De Punjabi.

In 2012 he made his debut with movie Best of Luck as an actor with [[Punjabi people|Punjabi]] singer and actor [[Gippy Grewal]] and Miss India Universe 2008 [[Simran Kaur Mundi]]. The shooting of this film commenced in [[Vancouver]], [[British Columbia]], [[Canada]] in May 2012. He made special appearance in [[Diljit Dosanjh]]'s ''[[Jatt & Juliet 2]]'' in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=Irv9CfDWgkY|title = - YouTube}}</ref>

In 2014, he appeared in ''[[Romeo Ranjha]]''.
In a news story published in April, it was revealed that while doing stunts for [[Romeo Ranjha]], Jazzy underwent serious injuries wherein he even had "near-death" experience. He was quoted as saying, "I literally left my body. I could feel everyone trying to resuscitate me. I remember my whole life flashing before my eyes, and surprisingly, I wasn't thinking about winning awards or anything like that. The only thing I cared about was that I wanted to live because I did not want to leave my family so soon."<ref>{{cite web|last=Ballewood.in|title=When Jazzy B met with Death|url=http://ballewood.in/movies/news/jazzy-b-met-with-death|publisher=Ballewood.in|access-date=3 April 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140405232204/http://ballewood.in/movies/news/jazzy-b-met-with-death|archive-date=5 April 2014}}</ref>

In 2014 he made a special appearance in [[Dharmendra]]'s and [[Gippy Grewal]]'s movie [[Double Di Trouble]].<ref>https://
https://punjabimania.com/jazzy-b-does-a-cameo-in-double-di-trouble/</ref>

In 2015 he featured in title song "Zalim Dilli"of movie ''[[Dilliwali Zaalim Girlfriend]]''  with [[Hard Kaur]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.india.com/viral/|title=Viral|website=India News, Breaking News &#124; India.com}}</ref>

In 2019 he worked on the [[Akshay Kumar]] starer Kesari as a playback singer on the song<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6264938/fullcredits|title=Kesari (2019) - IMDb|via=m.imdb.com}}</ref>

In 2020 he featured in song "Gallan Kardi" with [[Saif Ali Khan ]] for movie [[Jawaani Jaaneman]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=mSY1QrJd1f8|title = Gallan Kardi - Jawaani Jaaneman &#124; Saif Ali Khan, Tabu, Alaya F&#124;Jazzy B, Jyotica, Mumzy, Prem-Hardeep}}</ref>
