'''Miniature Tigers''' is an American [[indie pop]] band based in the [[Brooklyn|Brooklyn borough of New York City]] that combines elements of [[indie rock]], [[synth-pop]], and [[dream pop]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.myspace.com/miniaturetigers |title=Miniature Tigers &#124; Listen and Stream Free Music, Albums, New Releases, Photos, Videos |publisher=Myspace.com |date=2012-12-06 |accessdate=2014-07-13}}</ref> The band is made up of Charlie Brand (lead vocals, guitar), Rick Schaier (keyboards and guitar), Algernon Quashie (guitar), and Brandon Lee (bass guitar).<ref name=spin72908>[https://www.spin.com/2008/07/catch-buzz-miniature-tigers/ "Catch the Buzz: Miniature Tigers"], ''Spin'', William Goodman, July 29, 2008</ref>

Miniature Tigers began their first national tour in October 2008,<ref>[http://blogs.phoenixnewtimes.com/uponsun/2008/09/miniature_tigers_at_modified_a.php "Miniature Tigers at Modified Arts"], ''Phoenix New Times'', "Up on the Sun" blog for Sep. 15 2008</ref> which included playing a set at the 2008 [[CMJ]] Music Marathon.<ref>{{cite web|last=Leatherman|first=Benjamin|title=Miniature Tigers: Almost Everyone Loves Charlie Brand|url=http://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/2009-03-12/music/miniature-tigers-almost-everyone-loves-charlie-brand/|website=[[Phoenix New Times]]|accessdate=17 January 2012|date=12 March 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Wallace|first=Lynn|title=Miniature Tigers. Something to Roar About|url=http://www.ink19.com/issues/june2009/interviews/miniatureTigers.html|publisher=Ink 19|accessdate=17 January 2012}}</ref>
Miniature Tigers toured with [[Ben Folds]] in February and played the 2009 Monolith Festival in September.  They also toured with [[Fun.]] in support of their latest album, ''[[Some Nights (album)|Some Nights]]''.

==Media==
In December 2006, ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' listed Miniature Tigers as "one of the 25 best bands on [[Myspace]]."<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20070106160452/http://www.rollingstone.com/rockdaily/index.php/2006/12/22/rolling-stone-declares-the-25-best-bands-on-myspace "Rolling Stone Declares the 25 Best Bands on MySpace"], December 22, 2006</ref>  The band has also received favorable attention from [[Spin (magazine)|''Spin'' magazine]],<ref name=spin72908/> which included Miniature Tigers in its list of "The 9 Hottest New Bands Playing CMJ 2008."<ref>[https://www.spin.com/2008/10/9-hottest-new-bands-playing-cmj-2008/ "The 9 Hottest New Bands Playing CMJ 2008"], ''Spin'', by Peter Gaston, October 20, 2008</ref> ''Death + Taxes'', a music magazine, awarded the band's ''Black Magic'' and ''White Magic'' EPs 4 stars, calling the band "terribly endearing."<ref>[http://deathandtaxesmagazine.com/reviews-music.php?indReview=86 "MINIATURE TIGERS : BLACK & WHITE MAGIC EPS: MODERN ART"] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20090629190747/http://deathandtaxesmagazine.com/reviews-music.php?indReview=86 |date=2009-06-29 }}, ''Death + Taxes'',</ref>
The music video for "Cannibal Queen" has been featured on mtvU and MTV2's Subterranean.

The band's 4th album ''Cruel Runnings'' was released June 17, 2014 with cover art inspired by [[Memphis Group]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Miniature Tigers Discuss the Future, fun., and Fighting|url = http://www.transversomedia.com/articles/2015/5/26/miniature-tigers-discuss-the-future-fun-and-fighting|website = TRANSVERSO|accessdate = 2015-11-05}}</ref>

The band contributed music to and appear in the 2015 romantic comedy ''[[Sleeping with Other People]]''.<ref>[http://welistenhear.com/interviews/interview-miniature-tigers/ "Interview: Miniature Tigers"], July 9, 2015</ref>
