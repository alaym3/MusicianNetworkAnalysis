'''Lera Lynn''' (born December 5, 1984) is an American singer, songwriter, musician, and actress.

==Early life==
Born in [[Houston, Texas]], Lynn was raised in [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]]. She has a [[bachelor's degree]] in [[anthropology]] from the [[University of Georgia]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/michael-bialas/lera-lynn_b_2003744.html | work=Huffpost Entertainment | title=From Athens to Austin, Lera Lynn Gets High Marks in Old School | date=October 23, 2012}}</ref> Before attending the university, she briefly attended [[Young Harris College]] in [[Young Harris, Georgia]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.stompandstammer.com/content/view/5975/51/ |title=Lera Lynn (Sept.14 issue) |publisher=Stomp And Stammer |accessdate=2015-10-15 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150705012943/http://www.stompandstammer.com/content/view/5975/51 |archivedate=July 5, 2015 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

==Career==
Lynn's first LP, ''Have You Met Lera Lynn'', was released in 2011. In 2014, she released an EP entitled ''Lying in the Sun'' and also began creating music for the [[True Detective (season 2)|second season]] of the crime drama series ''[[True Detective (TV series)|True Detective]]''. Working with songwriter and music producer [[T Bone Burnett]], the two collaborated with singer-songwriter [[Rosanne Cash]] at Burnett's home in [[Los Angeles, California]] with two of the resulting tracks being included on Cash's 2018 album ''[[She Remembers Everything]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://leralynn.com/about |title=ABOUT |publisher=Lera Lynn |date= |accessdate=2015-10-15}}</ref> After approving the musical tracks, series creator [[Nic Pizzolatto]] cast Lynn as a recurring character who performs the songs in a [[dive bar]] frequented by the main characters.<ref>{{cite news| title=‘True’ Chanteuse Is the Secret Singer | author=John Jurgensen | newspaper=Wall Street Journal | date=June 26, 2015 | page=D6}}</ref> Her 2016 album ''Resistor'' was reviewed in ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref>{{cite web |author= Marissa R. Moss |url= https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/hear-lera-lynns-punchy-shape-shifter-20160217 |title= Hear Lera Lynn's Punchy 'Shape Shifter' |date= February 17, 2016 |publisher= [[Rolling Stone]] |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170318090835/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/hear-lera-lynns-punchy-shape-shifter-20160217 |archive-date= March 18, 2017 |url-status= live |accessdate= June 21, 2017}}</ref>

In 2016, Lynn performed at the [[Edmonton Folk Music Festival]].<ref>[http://www.edmontonsun.com/2016/08/07/2016-edmonton-folk-fest-an-understated-wonderful-weekend " 2016 Edmonton Folk Fest an understated, wonderful weekend"]. ''Edmonton Sun'', By Fish Griwkowsky. August 07, 2016</ref>
