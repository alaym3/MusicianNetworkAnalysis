'''Larkin Poe''' is an American [[roots rock]] band originally from north [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], currently based in [[Nashville, Tennessee]],<ref>
{{Citation|url=https://www.middlesborodailynews.com/2019/08/26/larkin-poe-excited-to-appear-in-the-bluegrass-state-thursday-at-levitt/|title=Larkin Poe excited to appear in the Bluegrass State Thursday at Levitt|work=[[Middlesboro Daily News]]|access-date=September 1, 2019}}</ref> and fronted by sisters Rebecca Lovell (born January 30, 1991) and Megan Lovell (born May 12, 1989). Featuring strong southern harmonies, heavy electric guitar riffs, and [[slide guitar]], they are often touted as "the little sisters of the [[The Allman Brothers Band|Allman Brothers]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.udiscovermusic.com/big-names-in-the-making-at-maverick-festival-2014|title=Big Names in the Making at Maverick Festival 2014 - uDiscover Music|website=www.udiscovermusic.com}}</ref> The band performed at the [[Glastonbury Festival 2014|2014]] and [[2016 Glastonbury Festival]] and were voted "Best Discovery of Glastonbury 2014" by the UK's ''[[The Observer]]''. The sisters have also toured as backing musicians for a variety of other bands, most notably [[Elvis Costello]], [[Conor Oberst]] of [[Bright Eyes (band)|Bright Eyes]], [[Kristian Bush]] of [[Sugarland]], and [[Keith Urban]].

==Career==
Rebecca and Megan Lovell began their musical careers in 2005 as teenagers with the formation of a [[Bluegrass music|bluegrass]]/[[Americana music|Americana]] group, the [[Lovell Sisters]], with their older sister, Jessica Lovell.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r-nQ1q7XET0 |title=
An Interview With Larkin Poe {{!}} Baeble Music}}</ref> After self-releasing two independent albums and touring successfully for four years – appearing on [[Garrison Keillor]]'s ''[[A Prairie Home Companion]]'', the ''[[Grand Ole Opry]]'', and performing at [[Bonnaroo Music Festival]] – the Lovell Sisters announced the group's disbandment in December 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem2.asp?xid=3581 |title=Lovell Sisters call it quits as a trio|website=www.countrystandardtime.com}}</ref> In January 2010, Rebecca and Megan regrouped as Larkin Poe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://flyinshoes.ning.com/profiles/blogs/the-lovell-sisters-are-now|title=The Lovell Sisters are now Larkin Poe!!|website=flyinshoes.ning.com|access-date=5 May 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140812110543/http://flyinshoes.ning.com/profiles/blogs/the-lovell-sisters-are-now|archive-date=12 August 2014|url-status=dead}}.</ref> "Larkin Poe" was the name of the sisters' great-great-great-grandfather.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://nodepression.com/interview/larkin-poe-co-writing-edgar-allan-poe-would-be-drag-band%E2%80%99s-happy-levels |title=Larkin Poe: 'Co-Writing with Edgar Allan Poe Would be a Drag on the Band's Happy Levels' |work=No Depression |date=2015-02-22 |access-date=2018-11-19 |author=Michael Bialas |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150318000132/http://nodepression.com/interview/larkin-poe-co-writing-edgar-allan-poe-would-be-drag-band%E2%80%99s-happy-levels |archive-date=18 March 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Over the course of 3 years (2010–2013), Larkin Poe self-released 5 independent [[Extended play|EP]]s, 2 collaborative albums, and a live performance DVD. ''Spring'' EP (2010), ''Summer'' EP (2010), ''Fall'' EP (2010), ''Winter'' EP (2010), ''Thick as Thieves'' EP (2011), ''The Sound of the Ocean Sound'' a collaboration with [[Thom Hell]] (2013), ''Killing Time'' EP a collaboration with [[Blair Dunlop]] (2013).

In late 2013, Larkin Poe signed a record deal with RH Music, the music division of San Francisco-based company [[Restoration Hardware]]. Following this signing, the band began work on their first full-length album, ''Kin'', which was released in 2014.

In March 2014, producer [[T Bone Burnett]] tapped the sisters to record harmonies and instrumentation on [[The New Basement Tapes]]' album ''[[Lost on the River: The New Basement Tapes]]'' alongside [[Marcus Mumford]] of [[Mumford & Sons]], [[Elvis Costello]], [[Jim James]] of [[My Morning Jacket]], [[Taylor Goldsmith]] of [[Dawes (band)|Dawes]], and [[Rhiannon Giddens]]. Rebecca and Megan also appear in the 2014 [[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime]] documentary ''Lost Songs: The Basement Tapes Continued''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.sho.com/sho/reality-docs/titles/3411641/lost-songs-the-basement-tapes-continued#/index |title=Archived copy |access-date=2015-05-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150512120929/http://www.sho.com/sho/reality-docs/titles/3411641/lost-songs-the-basement-tapes-continued#/index#/index |archive-date=2015-05-12 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In June 2014, Larkin Poe performed at the [[Glastonbury Festival]], which resulted in them being named "best discovery of Glastonbury" by ''The Observer''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/michael-bialas/larkin-poe-how-megan-and_b_5975300.html |title=Larkin Poe: How Megan and Rebecca Lovell Got Their Sister Act Together |work=Huffington Post |date=2014-10-13 |access-date=2018-11-19 |author=Michael Bialas}}</ref>

In April 2016, Larkin Poe reissued their debut album under the title ''Reskinned'', with a different order of songs and 5 new tracks replacing songs from the original version.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gratefulweb.com/articles/larkin-poe-releases-new-album-reskinned|title=Larkin Poe Releases New Album Reskinned|website=Grateful Web}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clichemag.com/music/larkin-poe-open-up-about-their-new-album-reskinned|title=Larkin Poe Open Up About Their New Album 'RESKINNED'|date=22 June 2016|work=Cliche Mag}}</ref> They performed their song "Trouble in Mind" on ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://americansongwriter.com/2016/04/songwriter-u-tour-diary-larkin-poe-gets-a-surprise-call-from-conan/ |title=Tour Diary: Larkin Poe Gets A "Surprise Call" From Conan |work=American Songwriter |date=2016-04-12 |access-date=2018-11-19 }}</ref> They also appeared on [[Steven Tyler]]'s album ''[[We're All Somebody from Somewhere]]'', which was released in July of the same year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://guitargirlmag.com/interviews/larkin-poe-has-trouble-in-mind-2016-11-06/|title=Larkin Poe Has Trouble In Mind|work=Guitar Girl Magazine|last=Zuro|first=Che|date=6 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://specials.myajc.com/ages-atlanta-music/|title=Ages may differ, but music is a common tie for Atlantans of every generation|first=Melissa|last=Ruggieri|work=The Atlanta Journal-Constitution|date=August 18, 2016}}</ref>

In February 2017, Larkin Poe performed as part of the backing band at the [[MusiCares]] tribute to [[Tom Petty]] in Los Angeles, accompanying such artists as [[Jackson Browne]] and [[Don Henley]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7686186/tom-petty-heartbreakers-all-star-tribute-musicares-grammy-awards-recording-academy|title=Tom Petty Joined by Foo Fighters, Stevie Nicks & Many More at Epic MusiCares Tribute Show|last1=Willman|first1=Chris|last2=Newman|first2=Melinda|date=February 11, 2017|work=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://music.blog.ajc.com/2017/02/11/musicares-person-of-the-year-tribute-features-tom-petty-stevie-nicks-and-atlantas-larkin-poe/|title=MusiCares Person of the Year tribute features Tom Petty, Stevie Nicks and Atlanta's Larkin Poe|first=Melissa|last=Ruggieri|date=February 11, 2017|work=The Atlanta Journal-Constitution}}</ref>

Larkin Poe's cover of the traditional song "[[John the Revelator (song)|John the Revelator]]" was used as the Series 3 episode 13 ending music for the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] TV show ''[[Lucifer (TV series)|Lucifer]]''.

In 2017, Larkin Poe released their self-produced sophomore album, ''Peach'', which was nominated for the [[Blues Foundation]] Best Emerging Artist Album in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://blues.org/2018-blues-music-award-nominees-announced/ |title=2018 Blues Music Award nominees announced: Taj Mahal, Mavis Staples, Keb' Mo', N. Mississippi Allstars among nominees. |work=Blues Foundation |date=2018-01-09 |access-date=2018-11-19 }}</ref>

Larkin Poe released their third studio album, ''Venom & Faith'', on November 9, 2018. The album was recorded in Nashville, produced by Rebecca and Megan, and engineered and mixed by Roger Alan Nichols.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2018/08/larkin-poe-album-announcement.html |title=Larkin Poe Announce Fourth Album, Venom & Faith |work=Paste |date=August 24, 2018 |access-date=October 7, 2018 |author=Korstanje, Emma}}</ref> ''Venom & Faith'' reached #1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' blues album chart for the week of November 24, 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/blues-albums/2018-11-24 |title=Blues Music: Top Blues Albums Chart - Billboard |work=Billboard |date=2018-11-24 |access-date=2018-11-30 }}</ref> The album was nominated for the 2020 [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Blues Album]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.npr.org/2019/11/20/781115326/lizzo-billie-eilish-lead-2020-grammy-nominations |title=See The Full List Of 2020 Grammy Nominations |work=NPR |date=2019-11-20 |access-date=2019-11-24 }}</ref>

Larkin Poe were one of the headliners at the 2020 Mahindra Blues Festival in Mumbai, India. Although relatively unknown in that part of the world, the duo left a mark with their performance of "When God Closes a Door". They then surprised their fans with the first live performance of the title track from their next album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.firstpost.com/entertainment/mahindra-blues-festival-2020-was-filled-with-stellar-performances-but-it-may-be-time-to-retire-the-all-star-jam-8029991.html|title=Mahindra Blues Festival 2020 was filled with stellar performances, but it may be time to retire the all-star jam- Entertainment News, Firstpost|date=2020-02-13|website=Firstpost|language=en|access-date=2020-02-26}}</ref> Shortly after that, the duo announced the dates of their USA tour via their social media pages.

Larkin Poe's fourth studio album ''Self Made Man'' was released on June 12, 2020, on Tricki-Woo Records.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.withguitars.com/larkin-poe-announce-new-single-holy-ghost-fire-new-album-self-made-man-out-12th-june/ |title=Larkin Poe – Announce New Single 'Holy Ghost Fire' {{!}} New Album 'Self Made Man' out 12th June |work=With Guitars |date=March 30, 2020 |access-date=April 1, 2020 |author=Johnson, Neil}}</ref> The lead single, "She's a Self Made Man", was released in March 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.stereoboard.com/content/view/227755/9 |title=Larkin Poe To Release New Album 'Self Made Man' In May, Share New Song She's A Self Made Man |work=Stereoboard.com |date=March 13, 2020 |access-date=March 24, 2020 |author=Stickler, Jon}}</ref> ''Self Made Man'' reached #1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' blues album chart for the week of June 27, 2020,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/blues-albums/2020-06-27 |title=Blues Music: Top Blues Albums Chart - Billboard |work=Billboard |date=2020-06-27 |access-date=2020-06-24 }}</ref> making it their second consecutive number-one album.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/larkin-poe/chart-history/BLU |title=Larkin Poe Chart History - Billboard |work=Billboard |date=2020-06-27 |access-date=2020-06-24 }}</ref>

On November 20, 2020, Larkin Poe released their fifth studio album, ''Kindred Spirits'', a covers album featuring renditions of songs by [[Lenny Kravitz]], [[Neil Young]], [[Elvis Presley]], [[Phil Collins]], [[Elton John]], and others.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Larkin-Poe-Kindred-Spirits/master/1846409|title=Larkin Poe - Kindred Spirits|work=[[Discogs]]|access-date=2021-02-09}}</ref>
