'''Aaron Jennings Puckett''',<ref>{{cite web |title=Aaron "lil aaron" Jennings Puckett |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/aaron-lil-aaron-jennings-puckett |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> better known by his [[stage name]] '''Lil Aaron''' (stylized as '''lil aaron'''), is an American rapper, singer and songwriter from [[Indianapolis]], [[Indiana]]. Merging elements of [[pop punk]] and [[emo]] with [[hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[pop music|pop]], he has released five solo EPs, as well as two EPs as a member of the [[alternative hip hop]] collective Boyfriendz. He has also written songs for artists such as [[Blackbear (musician)|Blackbear]],<ref name="Aaron Jennings">{{cite web |title=Aaron Jennings |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/aaron-jennings-mn0001943674 |website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=16 February 2021}}</ref> [[Liam Payne]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=King |first1=Clive |title=Liam Payne smoked 'a bunch of cigarettes' before studio session |url=https://www.contactmusic.com/liam-payne/news/liam-payne-smoked-a-bunch-of-cigarettes-before-studio-session_5941610 |access-date=16 February 2021}}</ref> [[Hailee Steinfeld]], [[Dev (singer)|Dev]] and [[Kiiara]].<ref name="Projectu" /> He took part in the writing of [[Lizzo]]'s 2019 album ''[[Cuz I Love You]]'',<ref name="Aaron Jennings"/> which won a [[Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/2020-grammy-awards-complete-nominees-list |title=2020 GRAMMY Awards: Complete Nominees List|date=November 20, 2019|website=Grammy.com|access-date=November 20, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Puckett grew up in [[Goshen, Indiana|Goshen]], [[Indiana]].<ref name="Hypefresh">{{cite web |title=LIL AARON HAS HIS EYES ON BECOMING BIG IN THE MUSIC INDUSTRY WITH NO LABEL |url=https://www.hypefresh.co/aaron-has-eyes-on-becoming-big-with-no-label/ |access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref> As a child, his [[Christians|Christian]] parents didn't allow him to listen to [[secular]] music, however he eventually became a fan of secular [[punk rock]] and [[emo]] bands through websites such as [[MySpace]] and [[PureVolume]], without his parents knowing.<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web |last1=Martin |first1=Liam |title=Lil Aaron Biography by Liam Martin |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lil-aaron-mn0003591332/biography?cmpredirect |website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref><ref name="AP">{{cite web |title=AN INTERVIEW WITH SOUNDCLOUD RAPPER LIL AARON: "I’VE BEEN EMO MY WHOLE FUCKING LIFE" |url=https://www.altpress.com/features/lil_aaron_interview/ |website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> First he discovered groups likes [[Never Shout Never]], [[Owl City]] and [[The Ready Set]], before becoming a fan of bands like [[Hit the Lights]] and [[Boys Like Girls]].<ref name="AP" /> Looking back in adulthood, he has stated "I’ve been emo my whole fucking life".<ref>{{cite web |last1=HUNTER |first1=ELIZABETH |title=Lil Aaron is 2019’s answer to pop-punk |url=https://brignews.com/2019/04/04/lil-aaron-is-2019s-answer-to-pop-punk/ |access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref> In middle school and high school, he was the vocalist of a [[pop punk]], that was influenced by Hit the Lights and [[Metro Station (band)|Metro Station]] and saw Puckett using overt auto-tune.<ref name="AP band">{{cite web |last1=Heinz |first1=Natasha |title=LIL AARON’S HIGH SCHOOL BAND SOUNDED LIKE "HIT THE LIGHTS MEETS METRO STATION" |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/lil_aaron_high_school_band_hit_the_lights_meets_metro_station/ |website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> As he grew older, Puckett became increasingly interested in the independent [[hip hop music|hip hop]] scene that was coalescing on the internet. This led to him dropping out of high school and moving to [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="AllMusic" />

==Career==
Puckett began his career as a songwriter, working with artists such as [[Travis Mills]], [[DRAM (musician)|DRAM]], [[Icona Pop]] and [[Kiiara]].<ref name="AllMusic" /> However, he soon wished to perform the tracks he was writing himself, beginning a solo-career in 2016 with the "Damn" produced by [[Y2K (record producer)|Y2K]].<ref name="AllMusic" /> On April 10, he was featured on the Travis Mills song "Trouble".<ref>{{cite web |title=Travis Mills & lil aaron have done a song called ‘Trouble’ together |url=https://projectu.tv/travis-mills-lil-aaron-have-done-a-song-called-trouble-together/ |access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref> On June 23, he released the track Prolly featuring Ilovemakonnen.<ref>{{cite web |title=lil aaron & ILOVEMAKONNEN have made a song about things they should PROLLY do |url=https://projectu.tv/lil-aaron-ilovemakonnen-have-made-a-song-about-things-they-should-prolly-do/ |access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref> On September 22, he was featured on the [[Joy (Australian musician)|Joy]] song "Birthday".<ref>{{cite web |title=JOY. just dropped ‘Birthday’ ft lil aaron |url=https://projectu.tv/joy-just-dropped-birthday-ft-lil-aaron/ |access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref> For his debut EP ''Gloing Pain$'', he enlisted the members of [[Polyphia]] to record the instrumentals.<ref name="AllMusic" /> Puckett's music first began gaining attention in mid-2017, through songs "Hot Topic" and "Warped Tour" that made references to cornerstones of pop punk and emo culture, namely [[Hot Topic]] and [[Warped Tour]], and through sampling bands like [[Paramore]] and [[Panic! at the Disco]].<ref name="AP band" /><ref>{{cite web |title=RAPPER LIL AARON SAMPLES PARAMORE, PANIC! ON NEW SONGS "WARPED TOUR" AND "HOT TOPIC" |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/lil_aaron_samples_paramore_panic_at_the_disco/ |website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> In late 2017, Puckett formed the group Boyfriendz, with Smrtdeath and [[Lil Lotus]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=McCarthy |first1=Liam |title=Lil Aaron, Smrtdeath & Lil Lotus share ‘Boyfriendz’ mixtape |url=https://www.elevatormag.com/lil-aaron-smrtdeath-lil-lotus-share-boyfriendz-mixtape |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Pierre |first1=Alphonse |title=10 Emo Rap Artists To Know |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/10-emo-rap-artists-to-know-news.51939.html |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> They released their debut self-titled EP in December 2017.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rick |first1=Vince |title=10 Punk-Rap & Punk-Pop Artists You Should Listen To |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/10-punk-rap-and-punk-pop-artists-you-should-listen-to-news.40979.html |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> The EP was recorded in a single night and was released within a week through [[SoundCloud]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Angell |first1=Jack |title=LIL LOTUS IS REWRITING THE EMO PLAYBOOK AND THERE ARE NO RULES |url=https://www.altpress.com/features/lil-lotus-if-i-die-first-boyfriendz-interview/ |website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> In 2017, Puckett was featured on the song "Faded" by [[Kim Petras]]. ==Musical style==
Puckett's music had been categorised as [[emo rap]]<ref name="Pitchfork">{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Nathan |title=Big Baby Scumbag Big Baby Earnhardt |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/big-baby-scumbag-big-baby-earnhardt/ |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=SHARP |first1=TYLER |title=THE DEATH METAL TURTLENECK IS REAL |url=https://loudwire.com/death-metal-turtleneck-is-real/ |access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref> and [[rap rock]].<ref name="K Quit" /><ref name="Pitchfork rap rock" /> It includes elements of [[pop punk]], [[SoundCloud rap]] and [[pop music|pop]].<ref name="Atwood Magazine">{{cite web |last1=Crowley |first1=James |title=THE NEW ROCK$TAR$: A CONVERSATION WITH LIL AARON |url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/lil-aaron-2018-interview/ |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> [[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] called his music "genre-defying".<ref name="AP blink" />

He has cited influences including [[Panic! at the Disco]], [[Blink-182]], [[Brokencyde]] and Dot Dot Curve<ref name="Atwood Magazine" /> In an interview with Projectu.TV, he stated that his goal was to appeal to both "the kids who listened to [[ASAP Mob|A$AP]], [[Kid Cudi|Cudi]] and [[Kanye West|Kanye]] and the kids who listened to [[Black Veil Brides]], [[Blood on the Dance Floor (band)|Blood On The Dance Floor]], the [[Warped Tour]] kind of music".<ref name="Projectu">{{cite web |title=an interview with lil aaron |url=https://projectu.tv/an-interview-with-lil-aaron/ |access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref> His music often merges [[trap music|trap]] instrumentals with [[autotune]]d and distorted vocals using melodies reminiscent of pop punk and emo.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Basler |first1=Patrick |title=Review: Lil Aaron’s "Gloing Pain$" is rap rock done right |url=https://dbknews.com/2016/11/01/review-lil-aaron-gloing-pains/ |access-date=16 February 2021}}</ref> [[Complex Networks|Complex]] writer Joe Price described his sound as "[[Travis Scott]] meets [[Green Day]]".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Price |first1=Joe |title=Is Pop-Punk Rap's Next Big Obsession? |url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2017/05/pop-punk-rap-music-lil-uzi-vert-blink-182 |website=[[Complex Networks]] |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref> [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] writer Jayson Greene described it as "sampl[ing] Panic! At the Disco, alternately whining like Blink’s [[Tom DeLonge]] and warbling like, well, [[Chief Keef]] over [[Roland TR-808|808]] claps. It’s all mixed together and atomized, a fever dream of Adidas tracksuits and dreads and emo power ballads".<ref name="Pitchfork rap rock">{{cite web |last1=Greene |first1=Jayson |title=The Unlikely Resurgence of Rap Rock |url=https://pitchfork.com/features/overtones/the-unlikely-resurgence-of-rap-rock/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref> Hypefresh.co described his music as "mixing pop-punk attitude with the new genre of autotuned rap".<ref name="Hypefresh" />

His music was cited as an influence by [[Yungblud]] for his album ''[[Weird!]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Carter |first1=Emily |title= YUNGBLUD Curates Spotify Playlist Of "Misfits" Who Inspire Him |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/yungblud-curates-spotify-playlist-of-misfits-who-inspire-him/ |website=[[Kerrang!]] |access-date=14 February 2021}}</ref>
