'''The Walters''' are a band from [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]], composed of lead vocalist Luke Olson, lead guitarist Walter Kosner, rhythm guitarist/vocalist MJ Tirabassi, bassist Danny Wells, and drummer Charlie Ekhaus.<ref name="Fox 32 Chicago interview">{{cite web|url=http://www.fox32chicago.com/mornings/228976770-video|title=The Walters on growing popularity, finding success without a label|website=FOX 32|accessdate=28 April 2017}}</ref> They have released two EPs and three singles, and were touring the United States on their first national tour in 2017.<ref name="columbiachronicle">{{cite web|url=http://www.columbiachronicle.com/arts_and_culture/article_34f44464-0b6c-11e7-8e1d-5746658c46e3.html|title=REVIEW: The Orwells, The Walters shake it down at home show on US tour kickoff (PHOTOS)|last=Parrella-Aureli|first=Ariel|date=March 17, 2017|website=The Columbia Chronicle|accessdate=28 April 2017}}</ref><ref name="WGNradio">{{cite web|url=http://wgnradio.com/2017/03/14/live-from-studio-435-the-walters/|title=Live from Studio 435: The Walters|date=14 March 2017|website=WGN Radio|accessdate=28 April 2017}}</ref>

== History ==
The Walters formed in Chicago, Illinois in 2014 after the band's guitarist and namesake Walter Kosner moved from Connecticut to Chicago to attend DePaul University. After dropping out of the school, Kosner recruited the rest of the band members who eventually would form The Walters.<ref name="Lollapalooza bio">{{cite web|url=https://www.lollapalooza.com/artist/the-walters|title=The Walters|website=Lollapalooza|accessdate=28 April 2017}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=November 2021}} That same year, the band released their first recorded effort, ''Songs for Dads''. The release saw the band charting on Spotify's ''United States Viral Top 50'' which caught the attention of [[Atlantic Records|Canvasback Records]]. As part of the label's ongoing monthly singles series, Canvasclub, The Walters released two songs, entitled "Hunk Beach" and "I Want to Be Good".<ref name="thesedays">{{cite web|url=http://www.thesedays.news/features/2015/9/23/hunky-dads-an-introductory-interview-with-the-walters|title=Hunky Dads: An Introductory Interview with The Walters|last=NIESPODZIANY|first=BEN|date=September 25, 2015|website=These Days|accessdate=28 April 2017}}</ref>

In 2015, the band also recorded and independently released their second full-length effort, ''Young Men''.{{citation needed|date=November 2021}} This was followed in 2017 by the single "She's Gonna Leave You".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/sweat-out-your-heartbreak-to-the-walterss-video-for-shes-gonna-leave-you|title=Sweat Out Your Heartbreak to The Walters's Video for "She's Gonna Leave You"|last=Domanick|first=Andrea|date=January 12, 2017|website=Noisey|language=en-us|access-date=2017-05-02}}</ref> In 2017, the band began their first national tour and also performed at the Lollapalooza music festival. On September 23, the band announced via their Facebook page that they "currently had no future plans to release new music as The Walters".<ref>The band's official statement: https://www.facebook.com/TheWaltersband/</ref>{{better source needed|date=November 2021}}

Following the band's breakup, lead vocalist Luke Olson started a solo career under the moniker "L. Martin",<ref name="Kenosha"/> while the remaining members formed a new band called Corduroy.<ref name="The Walters Facebook">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/TheWaltersband/photos/rpp.1503089106597766/2440077022898965/?type=3&theater|title=The Walters|website=Facebook|accessdate=27 March 2020}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=November 2021}} In an April 2021 interview, Olson stated that "the band broke up with [him]" and that it was "a pretty tough pill to swallow."<ref name="Kenosha">{{cite web |title=The Olson Brothers 'Reunite' As an Unlikely Musical Duo |url=https://www.kenosha.com/2021/04/15/the-olson-brothers-reunite-as-an-unlikely-musical-duo/ |website=kenosha.com |access-date=14 June 2021}}</ref>

In late-2021, their song "I Love You So" from the EP ''Songs for Dads'' went viral on [[TikTok]], leading to the song earning nearly 4 million on-demand streams in the United Stated in the first week of November.<ref name="BillboardInterview"/> On November 3, 2021, the band announced their reunion alongside a three-show tour running through December and subsequently signed with [[Warner Records]].<ref name="BillboardInterview">{{cite web |title=The Walters Sign With Warner Records Following Reunion: Exclusive |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/record-labels/9658509/the-walters-reunion-warner-records-exclusive |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=12 November 2021}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', the band said that they "had been talking for a while about getting in the studio or doing some shows together" and that the success of "I Love You So" led to them officially reuniting.<ref name="BillboardInterview"/>
