'''Khia Shamone Finch''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|k|aɪ|.|ə}} {{respell|KY|ə}}; ''{{née}}'' '''Chambers'''; born November 8, 1977)<ref name="Public Records Index">''U.S. Public Records Index'' Vol 1 (Provo, UT: Ancestry.com Operations, Inc.), 2010.</ref><ref name="SPTimes" /> is an [[United States|American]] [[rapping|rapper]], [[songwriter]], singer, media personality, and record producer. 
She is best known for her worldwide hit single "[[My Neck, My Back (Lick It)]]" as well as other notable street hits such as "Snatch the Cat Back", "Respect Me", "Been a Bad Girl", and "Next Caller".

== Early life ==
Khia was born Khia Shamone Chambers in [[Philadelphia]] and was raised in its [[Germantown, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania|Germantown]] neighborhood,<ref name="SPTimes" /><ref name="Myspace">{{cite web|url=http://www.myspace.com/khia|title=Khia|publisher=Myspace|access-date=November 10, 2010}}</ref> and she moved to [[Tampa, Florida]] when she was 11.<ref name="SPTimes">{{cite news|last=Green |first=Tony |url=http://www.sptimes.com/2002/07/14/Floridian/Big_hit_was_no_surpri.shtml |title=Big hit was no surprise for rapper |work=St. Petersburg Times |date=July 14, 2002 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20030710171939/http://www.sptimes.com/2002/07/14/Floridian/Big_hit_was_no_surpri.shtml |archive-date=July 10, 2003 |access-date=May 3, 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Khia attended Dowdell Middle School and [[Hillsborough High School (Tampa, Florida)|Hillsborough High School]]. She was expelled from Hillsborough during ninth grade and attended an alternative school afterwards.<ref name="Vibe" /> In 1991, Khia had her first child, a daughter also named Khia.<ref name ="Vice">{{citation|last=Dorn|first=Anna|url=https://www.vice.com/sv/article/ex5vvk/my-neck-my-back-my-pussy-and-my-life|title=An Interview with Khia|work=Vice|date=March 8, 2012}}</ref> In 1992, she moved to [[Hawaii]] when the father was stationed there and had her second child, a son named Rashawn, while she was living with him.<ref name="SPTimes" /> Before becoming a rapper, she worked as a bartender at Club XS in [[Tampa, Florida]].<ref name="Vibe" /> Her mother Carol Belinda Chambers, who was a data entry specialist, died in 2001.<ref name="SPTimes" /><ref name="Vibe">{{citation|last=Jacobs|first=Sonji|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uSUEAAAAMBAJ|title=Risqué Business|work=Vibe|date=November 2002|volume=10|issue=11|page=116}}</ref>

== Music career ==

=== 2000–2002: ''Thug Misses'' ===
{{Main|Thug Misses}}
Khia released her debut album, ''[[Thug Misses]]'' in 2002 on Dirty Down/Artemis Records as a re-release from the original version by Divine Records.<ref name="MTV" />  The lead single "[[My Neck, My Back (Lick It)]]" became a breakthrough single, charting at #42 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] chart and #12 on the [[Hot Rap Tracks]] chart.<ref name="Billboard singles">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p515126|pure_url=yes}}|title=Khia: Charts & Awards: Billboard Singles|publisher=allmusic|access-date=January 22, 2010}}</ref> MTV News reported that she wrote "My Neck, My Back" in 15 minutes.<ref name="MTV">{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1455425/20020626/khia.jhtml|title=Khia Shocked By Response To 'My Neck, My Back'|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|date=June 26, 2002|publisher=MTV News|access-date=January 22, 2010}}</ref>

=== 2003–2006: ''Gangstress'' ===
{{Main|Gangstress}}
In 2004, Khia appeared on a track with [[Trick Daddy]] and Tampa Tony called "Jump On It" from Trick's album ''[[Thug Matrimony: Married to the Streets]]''.
Khia's second album was supposed to be released in 2003 by the title "Street Preacher". The album was complete by mid-2003, and was set for released by the end of 2003, but the album got shelved for unknown reasons. In 2006 Khia recorded another album to be her second album, Gangstress.

Khia's second album, ''[[Gangstress]]'', was released on July 11, 2006 on Phase One Communications.<ref>{{cite web|last=Orzeck|first=Kurt|title=New Releases: Thom Yorke, Muse, Sufjan Stevens, Seether, Peaches, Butch Walker, Phish, Pink Floyd & More|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1536020/20060710/yorke_thom_radiohead_.jhtml|publisher=MTV News|access-date=July 23, 2010|date=July 11, 2006}}</ref> It debuted at #67 on the ''Billboard'' R&B chart. Later in 2006, Khia also collaborated with [[Janet Jackson]] on her hit "[[So Excited]]", which peaked at #90 on the Hot 100 and #1 on the [[Hot Dance Club Play]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=20 Y.O. > Charts & Awards > Billboard Singles|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r840427|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=allmusic|access-date=July 23, 2010}}</ref>

=== 2007–2008: ''Nasti Muzik, Boss Lady Mixtape'' ===
{{Main|Nasti Muzik}}
After signing to [[Big Cat Records (U.S. record label)|Big Cat Records]], and still with her own label Thug Misses Entertainment, Khia finished working on her third studio album, ''[[Nasti Muzik]]'', released on July 22, 2008. "[[What They Do]]" was the first promotional single on the album, which featured Atlanta rapper [[Gucci Mane]]. The first single, "Be Your Lady", was produced by Tampa's Push-a-Key Productions. Khia promoted the up-and-coming album by releasing a mixtape entitled ''The Boss Lady'', which was hosted by DJ Scream.

Khia participated in the [[VH1]] reality show ''[[Miss Rap Supreme]]'' in 2008.<ref name="HHDX2008">{{cite web|last=Hatfield|first=Quinton|title= Miss Rap Supreme Castaways: Khia|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.1102/title.miss-rap-supreme-castaways-khia|work=HipHopDX|access-date=July 23, 2010|date=April 22, 2008}}</ref> Khia was eliminated from the competition for rapping a song recorded prior to the show, "Respect Me".<ref>{{Cite episode|series=Ego Trip's Miss Rap Supreme|title=Miss Thang|series-link=Ego Trip's Miss Rap Supreme|network=VH1|number=2}}</ref>

=== 2009–2012: ''Motor Mouf/Khia Shamone'' ===
{{Main|MotorMouf aka Khia Shamone}}
Khia began working on her fourth album, ''[[MotorMouf aka Khia Shamone]]'', in 2009. On May 4, 2010, she released the lead single from the album, "Been a Bad Girl". A video for the single was shot in New York by Clifton Bell.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uxvtA90R_kc |title=Been a Bad Girl&nbsp;– Khia [OFFICIAL MUSIC VIDEO |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2012-04-13}}</ref> The song showed a different, more sensitive side of the rapper.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BLg8OaqZumI |title=Khia&nbsp;– Motor Mouf AKA Khia Shamone (Special) |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2012-04-13}}</ref> Following its release, Khia stopped by a local ABC News show to promote the single.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TiUWbZKYaV4 |title=Khia on ABC NEWS NOW |publisher=YouTube |date=2010-06-09 |access-date=2012-04-13}}</ref> In 2010, she told the website ''HipHopDX'' that the album will feature her singing instead of rapping on some of its cuts.<ref>{{cite web|last=Arnold|first=Paul W.|title= Khia Launches Singing Career|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.10727/title.khia-launches-singing-career|work=HipHopDX|access-date=July 23, 2010|date=February 28, 2010}}</ref> A second single, a smooth R&B song, "So Addicted", followed "Been a Bad Girl". That track was compiled on the ''Khia Shamone'' section of the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MJ4JnN73zd8 |title=Khia&nbsp;– Touch Me / So Addicted ( in the studio) |publisher=YouTube |date=2010-06-27 |access-date=2012-04-13}}</ref> She's since released the singles, "Pay Your Pussy Bill" and "Georgia" from the album.<ref name="facebook.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.facebook.com/khiatmeceo |title=Khia Shamone |publisher=Facebook |access-date=2012-04-13}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I88op5_cZVQ |title=Khia&nbsp;– 4real (new single) |publisher=YouTube |date=2010-12-03 |access-date=2012-04-13}}</ref><ref name="ustream.tv">{{cite web |url=http://www.ustream.tv/recorded/9208901 |title=hot or not?, quran and hemp mag. ovntvradio on USTREAM. Magic |publisher=Ustream.tv |access-date=2012-04-13 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121020074027/http://www.ustream.tv/recorded/9208901 |archive-date=2012-10-20 |url-status=dead }}</ref> ''MotorMouf aka Khia Shamone'' was released on July 17, 2012.

=== 2013–2016: ''Love Locs'' and ''Queendom Cum'' ===

The official "Love Locs" album cover was revealed on March 30, 2014 on Khia's Twitter, Instagram and Facebook pages. Khia released the ''You Deserve It'' video onto YouTube on May 29, 2014. On June 26, Khia announced via Twitter that the deluxe version was to contain 30 songs. She also revealed the deluxe edition cover art. The album was released on July 4.

=== 2017–present : ''Twerkanomics'' and focus on vlogging ===

Starting in 2017, Khia began hosting a weekly gossip web series entitled ''The Queen’s Court'' alongside friend [[Ts Madison]]. The show garners over 300,000 views per video. The two collaborated on the single "Next Caller," which also serves as the show's theme song. Khia left The Queen's Court after her disagreements with TS Madison after the February 5, 2018 show. Starting on March 5, 2018, Khia returned to solo vlogging with her new weekly webseries titled ''GAG ORDER''.

Throughout Fall 2018, Khia took a break from ''GAG ORDER'' to concentrate on creating new music. She released a single called "Kash Only" in October 2018. Without promotion, Khia released her seventh album, and first greatest hits album, ''TwerkAnomics'' in December 2018.
