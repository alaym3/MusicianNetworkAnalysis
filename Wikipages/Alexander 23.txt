'''Alexander Glantz''' (born January 23, 1995), known professionally as '''Alexander 23''', is an American singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist, engineer, and record producer. Since releasing his first string of singles and EP in 2019, he has collaborated with artists such as [[Selena Gomez]], [[Olivia Rodrigo]], [[Jeremy Zucker]], [[Noah Kahan]] and [[mxmtoon]], and has accumulated hundreds of millions of streams on Spotify.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alexander 23|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/6sFHvCyqklnJpXC9Nh1aag|access-date=2021-01-12|website=Spotify|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Glantz was raised in [[Deerfield, Illinois]]<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2020-08-18|title=Alexander 23 Discusses Songwriting, What Goes Into Your Favorite Bedroom Pop Tunes|url=https://americansongwriter.com/alexander-23-songwriting-idk-you-yet/|access-date=2021-01-14|website=American Songwriter|language=en-US}}</ref> a suburb of [[Chicago|Chicago, Illinois]]. At the age of 8, Glantz saw his father playing guitar which inspired him to learn as well.<ref name=":0" /> As a teenager, he joined a band where he also learned how to play bass, piano, and drums.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rising artist Alexander 23 releases debut album|url=https://www.jhunewsletter.com/article/2019/10/rising-artist-alexander-23-releases-debut-album|access-date=2021-01-14|website=The Johns Hopkins News-Letter}}</ref>

After high school, Glantz went to the University of Pennsylvania for engineering, but dropped out a year later to pursue music more seriously.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=2020-08-05|title=Get To Know Alexander 23 And His Track IDK You Yet|url=https://www.augustman.com/my/culture/music/get-to-know-alexander-23-and-his-track-idk-you-yet/|access-date=2021-01-14|website=Augustman|language=en-US}}</ref> Glantz then moved to [[Los Angeles|Los Angeles, CA]] to begin songwriting and producing for others and soon after he decided to write songs for himself and to pursue a career as an artist.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-10-16|title=Interview: Alexander 23 breaks down the barriers of bedroom pop|url=https://vanyaland.com/2019/10/16/interview-alexander-23-breaks-down-the-barriers-of-bedroom-pop/|access-date=2021-01-14|website=Vanyaland|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Career ==
Glantz signed to [[Interscope Records|Interscope]] in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sound|first=Stitched|date=2019-06-07|title=Alexander 23 discusses touring with Alec Benjamin, performing this year at Lollapalooza and what's next|url=https://stitchedsound.com/alexander-23-discusses-touring-with-alec-benjamin-his-debut-single-and-whats-next/|access-date=2021-01-14|website=Stitched Sound|language=en-US}}</ref> He released his first single "Dirty AF1s" in 2019 which made it to Spotify's New Music Friday playlist and was recognized as Apple Music's "Bop of the Week".<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=I'm watching Alexander 23 at Ones To Watch!|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/artist/alexander-23|access-date=2021-01-14|website=Ones To Watch}}</ref> After releasing five subsequent singles, his first EP ''I'm Sorry I Love You'' was released by Interscope in October 2019<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alexander 23 Breaks Down Debut EP 'I'm Sorry I Love You': Exclusive|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8540455/alexander-23-im-sorry-i-love-you-ep-breakdown|access-date=2021-01-14|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

Since the release of his first EP, Glantz has released a number of singles as well as collaborations with artists such as [[Selena Gomez]], [[Jeremy Zucker]], [[Chelsea Cutler]], and [[mxmtoon]]. He has also written, produced, and engineered songs for a number of other artists such as Role Model, [[Olivia Rodrigo]], [[AJ Mitchell]] and [[Louis the Child (DJs)|Louis The Child]].

He made his first live debut opening for [[Alec Benjamin]] on his Outrunning Karma Tour in 2019<ref>{{Cite web|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|date=|title=Alexander 23 Yearns for Love in Animated 'IDK You Yet' Video|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/alexander-23-idk-you-yet-video-997302/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-14|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> and has since opened for artists such as mxmtoon,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mxmtoon & Alexander 23 Bring the Joy to Los Angeles' Roxy Theatre|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8531672/mxmtoon-alexander-23-los-angeles-roxy-theatre-recap/|access-date=2021-01-14|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> Chelsea Cutler,<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|last2=|first2=|last3=|first3=|first8=|last9=|first9=|date=|title=Alexander 23 Shares Piano Version Of "IDK You Yet"|url=https://www.beyondthestagemagazine.com/alexander-23-shares-mesmerizing-piano-version-of-idk-you-yet/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-14|website=Beyond The Stage Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> and [[Omar Apollo]].<ref name=":2" />Alexander was set to open for [[Lauv]] during his How I'm Feeling Tour in August, 2020, however, due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic|Covid-19 pandemic]] these tour dates were cancelled.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Castel|first=Gregory|date=2020-08-18|title=Alexander23 gets "Caught in the Middle" [Video]|url=https://earmilk.com/2020/08/18/alexander23-gets-caught-in-the-middle-video/|access-date=2021-01-14|website=EARMILK|language=en-US}}</ref> Glantz instantly sold out his first two headline shows in Los Angeles in February 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alexander 23 Shows|url=http://www.teragrampresents.com/e/sold-out-alexander-23-81866848949/|url-status=live}}</ref>

When describing his decision to go by Alexander 23, Glantz said, "I was born on the 23rd, I grew up in Chicago and saw Michael Jordan as a huge figure in basketball and was obsessed with him, and he wore number 23. Plus, I was 23 when I started writing these songs. I knew that I wanted to go by my real first name and I just thought I’d spice it up by adding number 23 at the end."<ref name=":1" />

Glantz is also a member of the ten-member band Top Bunk.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Look Out For Alexander 23|url=https://kindacoolmagazine.com/features/look-out-for-alexander-23|access-date=2021-01-14|website=kinda cool magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

His second EP, ''Oh No, Not Again!'' was released on February 19, 2021 on Interscope Records.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chavez|first=Adriana|date=2021-02-21|title=Alexander 23 Shares Heartfelt New EP 'Oh No, Not Again!'|url=https://vacancy-mag.com/alexander-23-shares-heartfelt-new-ep-oh-no-not-again/|access-date=2021-02-21|website=Vacancy Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

Glantz co-produced "[[Good 4 U]]" by [[Olivia Rodrigo]] with [[Dan Nigro]]<ref>url=https://genius.com/Olivia-rodrigo-good-4-u-lyrics</ref> which was released on May 14, 2021 through [[Geffen Records|Geffen]] and [[Interscope Records|Interscope]] and debuted atop the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].
