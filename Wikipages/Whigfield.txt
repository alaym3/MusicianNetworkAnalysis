'''Whigfield''' is an Italian dance act fronted by Danish singer, songwriter and record producer Sannie Charlotte Carlson (born 11 April 1970), known by her various stage names including '''Whigfield''', '''Naan''', or simply '''Sannie'''.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rgsEAAAAMBAJ&q=Whigfield+Italian&pg=PA75|title=Billboard: Italian Acts|work=Billboard|access-date=2020-08-19|date=1995-07-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rgsEAAAAMBAJ&q=Whigfield+Italian+Dance+act&pg=PA71|title=Billboard: Italy's Music Industry Is Rising Above Political and Economical Uncertainty|work=Billboard|access-date=2020-08-20|date=1995-07-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rg8EAAAAMBAJ&q=Whigfield+Italy&pg=PA86|title=Billboard: For Italo Dance Pop "Business Isn't Great, It's Huge"|work=Billboard|access-date=2020-08-20|date=2004-06-26}}</ref><ref name="Naan">{{cite web|url=http://www.sanniecarlson.com/website.htm|title=Sannie Carlson (official web site)|access-date=2016-01-01|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160301124747/http://www.sanniecarlson.com/website.htm|archive-date=1 March 2016|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref name="Larkin2000">{{cite book|last=Larkin|first=Colin|title=The Virgin Encyclopedia of Nineties Music|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=crs7AQAAIAAJ|access-date=14 July 2015|year=2000|publisher=Virgin|isbn=9780753504277}}</ref> She is best known for her 1993 single "[[Saturday Night (Whigfield song)|Saturday Night]]", which became an international hit the following year.

Based in Italy at the time, Carlson worked with Italian producer [[Larry Pignagnoli]] and "[[Saturday Night (Whigfield song)|Saturday Night]]" entered the top five in Italy.<ref name="Billboard: Italian Acts">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rgsEAAAAMBAJ&q=Whigfield+from+Italy&pg=PA75|title=Billboard: Italian Acts (page-74)|publisher=[[Billboard magazine]] |date=1995-07-01 |access-date=2014-02-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YQgEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA58|title=Billboard: Hits of the World (page-58)|publisher=[[Billboard magazine]] |date=1994-10-29 |access-date=2014-02-01}}</ref> Her single "[[Another Day (Whigfield song)|Another Day]]" also managed to peak at number three in Italy as well.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tQsEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA38|title=Billboard: Hits of the World (page-38)|publisher=[[Billboard magazine]] |date=1995-01-14 |access-date=2014-02-01}}</ref> The single "[[Sexy Eyes (Whigfield song)|Sexy Eyes]]" also charted in a number of other markets, along with "Another Day" and "[[Think of You (Whigfield song)|Think of You]]" which entered the top ten in the United Kingdom, Switzerland, Norway, and her native Denmark.<ref name="Official Chart Company: Whigfield">{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/_/whigfield/ |title=Official Chart Company: Whigfield |publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=2014-02-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://swisscharts.com/song/Whigfield/Another-Day-2924|title=Hitparade.ch: Whigfiled (Another Day)|publisher=Hitparade.ch |access-date=2014-02-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=sgsEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA61|title=Billboard: Hits of the World (page-61)|publisher=[[Billboard magazine]] |date=1995-01-14 |access-date=2014-02-01}}</ref><ref>https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/30681/whigfield/</ref>

She competed in the ''[[Denmark in the Eurovision Song Contest 2018|Dansk Melodi Grand Prix 2018]]'' with the song "Boys on Girls" but didn't qualify for the superfinal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dr.dk/event/melodigrandprix/her-er-deltagerne-i-dansk-melodi-grand-prix-2018-overraskende-comeback-og|title=Denmark: DR reveals the ten Dansk Melodi Grand Prix 2017 hopefuls|date=22 January 2018|access-date=22 January 2018|publisher=DR|language=da}}</ref>

==Early life==
Carlson was born in [[Skælskør]], Denmark. She spent several years in [[Africa]] as a child before returning to her native country. Before singing Carlson worked as a model and studied music.<ref name="Billboard: Italian Acts"/> Carlson played in a duo<ref name="Larkin2000"/> before she met the producer Larry Pignagnoli and took on the name "Whigfield" as a tribute to her school music teacher.<ref name="Billboard: Italian Acts"/><ref name="Sannie Carlson (a.k.a Whigfield)">{{cite web|url=http://zapbangmagazine.com/music/interviews/sannie-carlson |title=Sannie Carlson (a.k.a Whigfield)|publisher=zapbangmagazine.com |access-date=2015-04-30}}</ref>
