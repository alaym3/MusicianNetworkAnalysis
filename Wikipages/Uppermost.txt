'''Behdad Nejatbakhshe''', better known by his stage name '''Uppermost''', is a [[France|French]] [[Electronic dance music|electronic music]] producer and multi-instrumentalist based in [[Paris]], [[France]].

==Career==
[[File:Upperwordmark.jpg|thumb|right|Uppermost's wordmark]]
[[File:Uppermost logo png.jpg|thumb|right|Uppermost's logo]]
Uppermost started producing music using [[FL Studio]] at the age of 17. His music has been released by [[Sony BMG]], [[Ministry of Sound#Record label|Ministry of Sound]], BugEyed Records, Starlight Records, and his own label, Uppwind.<ref>{{cite web|title=Discogs Uppermost Discography|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/uppermost|access-date=18 February 2015}}</ref> His song "Equivocal" reached 3rd place in the [[Beatport]] electro-house chart in 2009 and in 2011 his ''Biscuit Factory EP'' ranked 1st on the JunoDownload electro-house chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=JunoDownload {{!}} Bestsellers This Week|url=http://www.junodownload.com/electro-house/charts/bestsellers/this-week/releases/|access-date=8 March 2011}}</ref> Uppermost has remixed [[Daft Punk]], [[deadmau5]], [[Burial (musician)|Burial]], [[Crystal Castles (band)|Crystal Castles]], [[Johnathan Coulton]], [[Syl Johnson]], Congorock, Gregor Tresher, Cyberpunkers, OniMe, [[Jesus Luz]], Spencer & Hill and [[Felguk]]. He has been playlisted by artists such as [[Tiësto]], [[Armin van Buuren]] and [[Steve Angello]] and remixed by the popular Swedish dance duo [[Dada Life]]. In 2010, Uppermost released 41 tracks for free on his [[MySpace]] page under the aliases Uppermost, Alonely and Downed.<ref>{{cite web|title=Uppermost on MySpace|url=http://www.myspace.com/uppermost|access-date=2 March 2011}}</ref> Uppermost is currently working to promote his record label, Uppwind. His goal is to "...create a network where people can share art with each other; an artistic community where all talents can meet and learn from each other."<ref>{{cite web|title=Uppermost LessThan3 Interview|url=http://lessthan3.com/#!/2011/02/interview-uppermost/|access-date=27 February 2011}}</ref> Uppermost's idea to create music came from "an urge to express what I (Uppermost) felt at the time, some kind of nostalgia, music seemed to be the only language to translate this emotion."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Chant |first1=Louis |title=Interview: Uppermost Talks Beginnings, Soundcloud and More |url=https://frenchshuffle.com/2016/02/25/uppermost-talks-beginnings-soundcloud-and-more/ |website=The French Shuffle |access-date=25 February 2016}}</ref>

On 19 September 2011, Uppermost released his debut album, ''Action'', on his own label, Uppwind. It was made available for purchase on [[Beatport]] and [[Amazon (company)|Amazon]] on 28 November. On 19 December 2011, he released his second album, ''Polis'', for free on his Uppwind [[blog]], just two months after the release of ''Action''. Uppermost began performing at live events in early 2012, starting with his gig at Alte Kaserne in [[Zurich]].
He made his first French radio interview on a student's radio named CommonWave on 21 January 2012 with Steige and Zach Mayer.

==Influences==
He cites [[Burial (musician)|Burial]] and [[Daft Punk]] as some of his greatest influences.<ref>{{cite web|title=Uppermost on Facebook|url=http://www.facebook.com/uppermost?v=info|access-date=27 February 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Uppermost LessThan3 Interview|url=http://lessthan3.com/#!/2011/02/interview-uppermost/|access-date=27 February 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Interview de Uppermost dans le studio de Paris Culture Club|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hFjxEugxxvs|access-date=15 December 2012}}</ref>
