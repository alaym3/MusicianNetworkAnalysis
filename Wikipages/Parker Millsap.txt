'''Parker Millsap''' is an American singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist from [[Purcell, Oklahoma]], playing a blend of blues, country, rock, Americana, and folk music. Named one of [[Americana Music Association]]'s 2014 Emerging Artists of the Year after the release of his [[eponymous]] album, Millsap garnered attention with his song "Truck Stop Gospel", which has been featured on NPR's music program ''The Record''.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Powers|first1=Ann|title=An Emerging Voice of Americana (And Oklahoma)|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/therecord/2014/09/11/347466848/an-emerging-voice-of-americana-and-oklahoma|newspaper=NPR|date=11 September 2014|accessdate=28 July 2015}}</ref> Playing with childhood friend Michael Rose on bass and Daniel Foulks on the fiddle, Millsap's musical style has drawn comparisons to early [[Elvis Presley]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Haymes|first1=Greg|title=Parker Millsap at the Ale House 7/14/15|url=http://blog.timesunion.com/localarts/review-parker-millsap-the-ale-house-71415/38492/|website=timesunion.com|date=15 July 2015|publisher=The Times Union|accessdate=28 July 2015}}</ref> In addition to singing, he plays guitar, harmonica and piano.

He attributes his musical influence to growing up in a [[Pentecostal Church]] and listening to blues with his parents.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wake|first1=Matt|title=Who the heck is Jason Isbell's opening act Parker Millsap?|url=http://www.al.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2014/05/who_the_heck_is_jason_isbells.html|accessdate=28 July 2015|publisher=AL.com|date=May 7, 2014}}</ref>

He has opened for [[Patty Griffin]], [[Old Crow Medicine Show]], [[Shovels & Rope]], [[Lake Street Dive]], [[John Fullbright]], [[Jason Isbell]], and [[Sarah Jarosz]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Parker Millsap|url=http://longspark.org/summer-music-series/2015-performers/parker-millsap/|website=Longs Park Amphitheater Foundation}}</ref> as well as headlining tours of his own, and counts [[Elton John]] as a fan. Millsap's television appearances include ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]'' and ''[[Austin City Limits]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nondoc.com/2016/10/04/elton-john-parker-millsap-restored-my-faith-in-music/|title = Elton John: Parker Millsap and Sarah Jarosz 'restored my faith in music'|date = 4 October 2016}}</ref>

Millsap has released five studio albums.  His 2016 release, ''The Very Last Day,'' was declared the 11th best roots album of the year by ''[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]''.<ref>http://nodepression.com/article/your-50-favorite-albums-2016</ref> His fourth album, ''Other Arrangements'', was  released on May 4, 2018. ''Other Arrangements'' features a more blues, rock, and pop-focused sound than his previous work and is his first release to feature electric instruments.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://newsok.com/article/5583472|title=Acclaimed Oklahoma singer-songwriter Parker Millsap releasing new album 'Other Arrangements' in spring|date=14 February 2018}}</ref> Millsap's fifth album, ''Be Here Instead'' was released on April 9, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://americansongwriter.com/parker-millsap-is-back-with-be-here-instead-first-single-the-real-thing-premieres/|title = Parker Millsap is Back with 'BE HERE INSTEAD'; First Single, "The Real Thing," Premieres|date = 14 January 2021}}</ref>
