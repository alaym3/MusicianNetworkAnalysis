'''Jane Marczewski''' (born 1990),<ref name=Story>{{cite web |author=Rebeca |url=https://thestorywatch.com/nightbirde-is-it-really-okay/ |title=Nightbirde, is it really okay? |website=Story Watch |date=August 2021 |access-date=2021-10-19 |type=interview }}</ref> known professionally as '''Nightbirde''', is an American [[singer-songwriter]]. After releasing two [[extended play|EP]]s and two singles, she received a Golden Buzzer at her audition on ''[[America's Got Talent (season 16)|America's Got Talent]]'' in 2021, but was unable to compete in the quarterfinals because of worsening health due to cancer.

== Career ==
Marczewski uploaded her first songs and gave her first live performance in 2011, while a student at Liberty.<ref name=Lynchburg>{{cite news |first=Casey |last=Gillis |url=https://newsadvance.com/entertainment/features/musician-jane-claudio-after-beating-cancer-will-return-to-the-stage-for-lynchburg-show/article_9c3a7878-528c-11e9-91ea-1bf5cb28cadb.html |title=Musician Jane Claudio, after beating cancer, will return to the stage for Lynchburg show |newspaper=The News & Advance |date=2019-03-30 |access-date=2021-09-20 }}</ref> She released a three-song EP in 2012, called ''Lines'', on which she sang and played acoustic guitar.<ref name=six/><ref name=student/> In 2013, she released ''Ocean & Sky'', a six-song EP that she funded on [[Kickstarter]].<ref name=hurts/> 

Remaining in [[Lynchburg, Virginia]], after graduating, Marczewski was a popular performer locally. She returned to Ohio in 2014, and following her marriage, moved in 2015 to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], where she later resumed performing under her married name, Jane Claudio, with her husband acting as her producer.<ref name=resurrection>{{cite news |first=Suzanne |last=Ramsey |url=https://lynchburgliving.com/the-ressurection-of-jane/ |title=The Resurrection of Jane |magazine=Lynchburg Living |date=2019-07-01 |access-date=2021-09-20 }}</ref> [[Billing (performing arts)|Credited as]] Jane Marczewski-Claudio, she contributed music for the 2015 [[documentary film]], ''[[Leonard Knight: A Man & His Mountain]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.watchmode.com/c/01584725/cast-crew |title=''Leonard Knight: A Man & His Mountain'' (2015): Full Cast & Crew |website=Watchmode |access-date=2021-09-21 }}</ref>

She adopted the professional name Nightbirde in 2019,<ref name=Lynchburg/> based on a repeated dream: "It felt so poetic that these birds were singing as if it were morning, and yet there was no sign of it yet, and that’s what I want to embody."<ref name=Story/> Her first single under that name was "Girl in a Bubble", released in March 2019.<ref name=Liberty>{{cite magazine |first=Drew |last=Menard |url=https://www.liberty.edu/journal/article/taking-flight/ |title=Taking Flight: Alumna musician Jane 'Nightbirde' Claudio on her rebirth after beating cancer |magazine=Liberty Journal |publisher=[[Liberty University]] |date=2019-06-12 |access-date=2021-09-20 }}</ref><ref name=Bubble>{{cite web |first=Gregory |last=Castel |url=https://earmilk.com/2019/03/26/meet-nightbirde-the-girl-in-a-bubble/ |title=Meet Nightbirde, the 'Girl in a Bubble' |website=[[Earmilk]] |date=2019-03-26 |access-date=2021-09-20 }}</ref> In April 2019, she opened for [[Tori Kelly]] at Liberty.<ref name=Lynchburg/><ref name=resurrection/> In August 2020, following her second cancer diagnosis and remission, she released "It's OK".<ref name=OK>{{cite web |first=Valeria |last=Dulava |url=https://earmilk.com/2020/08/21/after-a-tumultuous-year-nightbirde-finally-declares-its-ok-in-an-electrifying-follow-up-to-her-debut/ |date=2020-08-21 |title=After a tumultuous year, Nightbirde finally declares 'It's OK' in an electrifying follow-up to her debut |website=Earmilk |access-date=2021-09-20 }}</ref>

Her style, a mix of [[folk music|folk]] and [[pop music|pop]],<ref name=hurts/> has evolved to become [[electropop]].<ref name=Bubble/>

=== ''America's Got Talent'' ===
In June 2021, she performed as Nightbirde during the [[America's Got Talent (season 16)|16th season]] auditions on ''[[America's Got Talent]]''.  She subsequently received a Golden Buzzer from [[Simon Cowell]] for her performance of her original song "It's OK".<ref name=songwriter/><ref name=Huff>{{Cite web|last=Dicker|first=Ron|date=June 9, 2021|title=Cancer Patient Jane Marczewski Melts Simon Cowell With 'Stunning' Voice On 'America's Got Talent'| url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/jane-marczewski-cancer-americas-got-talent-nightbirde_n_60c09689e4b059c73bd3f9de|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-20 |website=HuffPost}}</ref><ref name=iTunes>{{cite news |first1=Andy |last1=Long |first2=Stephanie |last2=Thompson |url=https://www.nbc4i.com/news/local-news/original-nightbirde-song-now-topping-itunes-download-charts/ |title=After AGT Golden Buzzer, original Nightbirde song 'It's OK' topping iTunes download charts |publisher=WCMH-TV |date=2021-06-10 |access-date=2021-09-20 }}</ref> During her post-performance interview with the judges, Marczewski offered this inspiration to the audience, "You can't wait until life isn't hard anymore before you decide to be happy."<ref name="Huff" /> The song became No. 1 on [[iTunes]]<ref name=iTunes/> and No. 2 trending on [[YouTube]].<ref name=JW/> 

In August 2021, before she could compete in the show's quarterfinal round, Marczewski withdrew from the competition because of worsening health.<ref name=JW/><ref>{{cite news |first=Chloe |last=Melas |url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/08/03/entertainment/jane-marczewski-nightbirde-americas-got-talent-trnd/index.html |title='America's Got Talent' contestant departs show due to cancer battle |publisher=CNN |date=2021-08-03 |access-date=2021-10-19 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://metro.co.uk/2021/08/02/americas-got-talent-nightbirde-pulls-out-as-cancer-battle-takes-turn-15025821/|title=America's Got Talent: Simon Cowell's Golden Buzzer act Nightbirde forced to pull out as cancer takes a 'turn for the worse'|work=[[Metro (British newspaper)|Metro]]|location=UK|first=Nola |last=Ojomu|date=August 2, 2021|access-date=August 2, 2021}}</ref> Cowell encouraged her not to return to the competition.<ref>{{cite news |first=Talia |last=Naquin |url=https://fox8.com/news/entertainment/simon-cowell-shares-update-on-ohio-singer-nightbirde-amid-cancer-battle/ |title=Simon Cowell shares update on Ohio singer 'Nightbirde' amid cancer battle |publisher=WJW TV |location=Cleveland, Ohio |access-date=2021-10-19 }}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Marczewski was married to musician Jeremy Claudio.<ref name=resurrection/> The couple separated in 2020, after which she moved from Nashville to [[Long Beach, California]].<ref name=opens/><ref>{{cite news |first=Deepa |last=Bharath |url=https://www.ocregister.com/2020/07/24/hundreds-gather-in-huntington-beach-to-be-baptized-with-cold-dunk-in-the-ocean/ |title=Hundreds gather in Huntington Beach to be baptized with cold dunk in the ocean |newspaper=[[The Orange County Register]] |date=2020-07-25 |orig-year=July 24, 2020 |access-date=2021-09-20 }}</ref>

She was diagnosed with [[breast cancer]] in 2017,<ref name=opens/><ref name=Lynchburg/><ref name=resurrection/> and declared cancer-free in July 2018.<ref name=Liberty/> Her cancer recurred in 2019, and she was given 3 to 6 months to live,<ref name=opens/> but she was again declared cancer-free in 2020.<ref name=OK/> Prior to the ''America's Got Talent'' audition, she was told that the cancer had [[Metastatic breast cancer|metastasized]] to her lungs, spine, and liver and her chance of survival was 2%.<ref name=Huff/><ref>{{cite news |first=Kayla |last=Keegan |url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/health/medical/agt-star-nightbirde-delivers-a-health-update-after-sharing-cancer-diagnosis/ar-AAKUnay |title='AGT' Star Nightbirde Delivers a Health Update After Sharing Cancer Diagnosis |work=Good Housekeeping |via=[[MSN]] |date=2021-06-10 |access-date=2021-09-20 }}</ref>
