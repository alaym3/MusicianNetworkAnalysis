'''Derrick Lemel Stewart''' (born March 26, 1969),<ref>California Births, 1905 - 1995 [http://www.familytreelegends.com/records/39461?c=search&first=Derrick&last=Stewart Derrick L. Stewart]</ref><ref>FATLIP IS CELEBRATING HIS 40TH BIRTHDAY! [http://www.openxmoney.com/2009/03/dance-right-fatlip-is-celebrating-his-40th-birthday/ When: Thursday March 26th <s>2006</s> 2009 (Published: March 22, 2009)]{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes}}</ref> known professionally as '''Fatlip''', is a Los Angeles-born [[hip hop music|hip hop]] musician. He started his career in the hip hop group [[The Pharcyde]].

==Early career==
===The Pharcyde===
As a member of The Pharcyde, Fatlip contributed to the critically acclaimed ''[[Bizarre Ride II the Pharcyde]]'' as well as the follow-up release ''[[Labcabincalifornia]]''. After the release of the ''Labcabincalifornia'' album, Fatlip was kicked out of the group, in part for leaving the supporting tour for their second album in favor of working on his own solo efforts.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.the411online.com/fatlip.html |title=The 411/Fat Lip |website=www.the411online.com |last=Ratcliffe |first=Joshua "Fahiym" |date=May 2000 |access-date=2017-07-18}}</ref>

In the group, Fatlip could be recognized for his conversational-style delivery of his verses and for having the deepest voice in the group.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}}

==Solo career==
In 2005, Fatlip released his solo debut, ''[[The Loneliest Punk]]''. It includes his single from 2000, "What's Up Fatlip?" (which was included in the soundtrack for ''[[Tony Hawk's American Wasteland]]'' and the ''[[Youth in Revolt (film)|Youth In Revolt]]'' soundtrack.) The music video for "What's Up Fatlip?" was directed by [[Spike Jonze]]. During the filming process, Jonze compiled a series of interviews with Fatlip and put them together in a documentary also titled "What's Up{{not a typo|,}} Fatlip?".<ref><!-- Note this is the label's official copy of the video, not a copyright violation. -->{{cite web|title=FatLip - What's Up Fat Lip? |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SK_WfF6hf2E |website=[[YouTube]] |date=2006-12-03 |access-date=2017-07-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Fatlip: What's Up Fatlip? (Video 2000) |website=[[IMDb]] |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6810042/ |access-date=2017-07-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=What's Up, Fatlip? (Video 2003) |website=[[IMDb]] |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0417259/ |access-date=2017-07-18}}</ref> The album features guest appearances by [[Chali 2na]] (on the song "Today's Your Day", which is included in the skateboarding film ''[[Yeah Right!]])'', as well as [[Shock G]] (on the song "Freaky Pumps"). The album contains songs produced by [[J-Swift]], who was responsible for the production of The Pharcyde's first album ''Bizarre Ride II the Pharcyde''.

Fatlip appeared on [[MTV]]'s ''[[Jackass (TV series)|Jackass]]'' sliding down an [[escalator]] rail, and this was edited into his video for "Worst Case Scenario".

===Collaborations===
{{BLP unsourced section|date=June 2021}}
In 2001, Fatlip was featured on [[L.A. Symphony]]'s ''Big Broke L.A.'' EP on the track "What You Say".

Fatlip had a [[Cameo appearance|cameo]] in [[Southern rap]]per [[Ludacris]]'s "Get Back" video.

In 2007, he collaborated with [[The Chemical Brothers]] on their song "[[The Salmon Dance]]" which is featured on their album ''[[We Are the Night (album)|We Are the Night]]''.

Fatlip has collaborated with a crew from [[French hip hop|France]] known as the Jazz Liberatorz. In 2008, he appeared on their album ''Clin D'Oeil''. He was featured on two tracks, "Ease My Mind" and "Genius At Work", the latter of which also featured [[Tre Hardson]], Omni, and T-Love. He also appeared twice on the subsequent Jazz Liberatorz album, ''Fruit of the Past'', on "My Style Is Fly" and "Back Packers".

Fatlip is featured on the song "Luxury Pool" from the 2008 album ''[[Stainless Style]]'' by [[Neon Neon]].

In early 2009, Fatlip also appeared on two tracks on ''The Spirit of Apollo'', the debut album by N.A.S.A., which was a collaboration project between [[Sam Spiegel (musician)|Sam Spiegel]] and [[DJ Zegon]]. The two songs were "Hip Hop" (also featuring [[KRS-One]] and [[Slimkid3|Tre Hardson]]) and "Strange Enough" (also featuring [[Karen O]] and [[Ol' Dirty Bastard]]). Other artists who appeared on the album included [[Method Man]], [[E-40]], Chali 2na, and [[David Byrne]], among others.

2016 saw Fatlip collaborate with [[Edo G]] on a song called "Playtime Over", produced by Tone Spliff. Then, in 2017, Fatlip was featured on the track "Mr. Lonely" by Portland-based band [[Portugal. The Man]]. The song was included on the band's album ''[[Woodstock (Portugal. The Man album)|Woodstock]]'', and Fatlip received a writing credit for it.

In 2020, Fatlip released a collaboration with [[Blu (rapper)|Blu]] and [[Samuel T. Herring]], the latter credited as Hemlock Ernst. The song was called "Good For The Soul". That year also saw Fatlip collaborate with [[Percee P]] and [[Phil Da Agony]] on the song "For You".

===Reunion with Tre Hardson===
Fatlip has reunited with former Pharcyde member Tre Hardson to perform on "The Over 30, Dirty Old Men Tour" together.<ref name="Fatlipandslimkid3">{{cite web |url=http://www.fatlipandslimkidtre.com/ |title=The Over 30 Dirty Old Men Tour |work=Fatlipandslimkid3.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071018021715/http://www.fatlipandslimkid3.com/ |archive-date=2007-10-18 |access-date=2017-10-03}}</ref>
