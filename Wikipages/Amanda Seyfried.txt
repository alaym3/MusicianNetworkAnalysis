'''Amanda Michelle Seyfried''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|s|aɪ|f|r|ɛ|d}} {{respell|SY|fred}}; born December 3, 1985) is an American actress, singer, and model. Born and raised in [[Allentown, Pennsylvania]], she began modeling at eleven and ventured into acting at fifteen with recurring roles as [[Lucy Montgomery (As the World Turns)|Lucy Montgomery]] on the [[CBS]] [[soap opera]] ''[[As the World Turns]]'' (1999–2001) and Joni Stafford on the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] soap ''[[All My Children]]'' (2003). Seyfried came to prominence following her feature film debut in the teen comedy ''[[Mean Girls]]'' (2004) and her recurring role as [[List of Veronica Mars characters|Lilly Kane]] on the [[UPN]] television series ''[[Veronica Mars]]'' (2004–2006). She went on to have supporting roles in independent films such as ''[[Nine Lives (2005 film)|Nine Lives]]'' (2005) and ''[[Alpha Dog]]'' (2006), and portrayed [[Sarah Henrickson]] on the [[HBO]] drama series ''[[Big Love]]'' (2006–2011).

Seyfried has had starring roles in a number of successful films, including ''[[Mamma Mia! (film)|Mamma Mia!]]'' (2008), ''[[Solstice (film)|Solstice]]'' (2008), ''[[Jennifer's Body]]'' (2009), ''[[Chloe (2009 film)|Chloe]]'' (2009), ''[[Dear John (2010 film)|Dear John]]'' (2010), ''[[Letters to Juliet]]'' (2010), ''[[Red Riding Hood (2011 film)|Red Riding Hood]]'' (2011), ''[[In Time]]'' (2011), ''[[Gone (2012 film)|Gone]]'' (2012), ''[[Lovelace (film)|Lovelace]]'' (2013), ''[[The Big Wedding]]'' (2013),  ''[[A Million Ways to Die in the West]]'' (2014), ''[[Ted 2]]'' (2015),  ''[[Pan (2015 film)|Pan]]'' (2015), ''[[Fathers and Daughters]]'' (2016), ''[[First Reformed]]'' (2017), ''[[The Clapper (film)|The Clapper]]'' (2017), ''[[Anon (film)|Anon]]'' (2018), ''[[Gringo (2018 film)|Gringo]]'' (2018), ''[[Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again]]'' (2018), ''[[The Art of Racing in the Rain (film)|The Art of Racing in the Rain]]'' (2019), and  ''[[Things Heard & Seen]]'' (2021)''.'' She has also occasionally done voice-over work in animation films, such as ''[[Epic (2013 film)|Epic]]'' (2013) and ''[[Scoob!]]'' (2020).

For her role in the musical film ''[[Les Misérables (2012 film)|Les Misérables]]'' (2012), Seyfried won the [[Satellite Award for Best Cast – Motion Picture]] and was nominated for the [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture]]. For her portrayal of actress [[Marion Davies]] in the biopic ''[[Mank]]'' (2020), Seyfried received nominations for the [[Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Supporting Actress|Critics' Choice Movie Award]], the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture|Golden Globe Award]], and the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Academy Award]] for Best Supporting Actress.

==Career==
===1996–2005: Early work===
During Seyfried's time of modeling she appeared in the printed ads for clothing companies including [[Limited Too]] with [[Leighton Meester]], and was featured on three covers of the ''[[Sweet Valley High]]'' novel series.<ref name="allureinterview" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Amanda Seyfried biography|url=http://www.people.com/people/amanda_seyfried/biography|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|access-date=August 14, 2015|archive-date=August 29, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160829202726/http://www.people.com/people/amanda_seyfried/biography/|url-status=live}}</ref> She stopped modeling when she was 17,<ref name="allureinterview" /> and worked as a [[waitress]] in a [[retirement community]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mcpkLfUBzeU|title=Amanda Seyfried (Letterman)|via=www.youtube.com|access-date=August 24, 2013|archive-date=March 5, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305213709/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mcpkLfUBzeU|url-status=live}}</ref> Seyfried took vocal lessons, studied opera, and trained with a Broadway coach while still a teen. She began acting as an extra in the daytime drama television series ''[[Guiding Light]]''.<ref name="askmeninterview">{{cite web |url=http://www.askmen.com/celebs/women/actress/amanda-seyfried/index.html |title=Amanda Seyfried Profile |work=[[Ask Men|AskMen.com]] |access-date=January 1, 2010| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100125235106/http://www.askmen.com/celebs/women/actress/amanda-seyfried/index.html| archive-date= January 25, 2010 <!--DASHBot-->| url-status=live}}</ref> From 2000 to 2001, she portrayed the recurring character [[Lucy Montgomery (ATWT)|Lucy Montgomery]] on the [[CBS]] soap opera ''[[As the World Turns]]''.<ref name="askmeninterview" /> She played Joni Stafford on the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] soap ''[[All My Children]]'' from 2002 to 2003.

In 2003, Seyfried auditioned to play the role of Regina George in ''[[Mean Girls]]''; the role eventually went to [[Rachel McAdams]]. While she was initially considered to play the lead role of Cady Heron, played by [[Lindsay Lohan]], the producers of the film decided that Seyfried should play Karen Smith, Regina's dim-witted "Plastic" friend and sidekick.<ref name="askmeninterview" /> The film was a box office success, grossing over $129&nbsp;million in its theatrical run.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=meangirls.htm |title=Mean Girls (2004) |work=[[Box Office Mojo|BoxOfficeMojo.com]] |access-date=January 1, 2010 |archive-date=December 29, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201229043421/https://www.boxofficemojo.com/release/rl3395520001/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Seyfried's performance in the film earned her, along with Lohan, [[Lacey Chabert]], and McAdams, an [[MTV Movie Award]] in the category of "Best On-Screen Team". Seyfried auditioned to play the title character on [[UPN]]'s television series ''[[Veronica Mars]]''.<ref name="askmeninterview" /> The role eventually went to [[Kristen Bell]], and Seyfried portrayed Veronica's murdered best friend Lilly Kane.<ref name="askmeninterview" /> Her character was only shown in [[Flashback (narrative)|flashbacks]].<ref name="askmeninterview" /> The show's creator, Rob Thomas, felt that Seyfried's portrayal as Lilly Kane was so outstanding that he used her more times in the show than he initially planned in the first season.{{citation needed|date=July 2018}} Seyfried appeared in ten episodes from 2004 to 2005.

In 2005, Seyfried played the lead character, Samantha, a role written by director [[Rodrigo García (director)|Rodrigo García]] specifically for her, in one of the nine parts of the film ''[[Nine Lives (2005 film)|Nine Lives]]'', composed of nine short films with different themes and an ensemble cast.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://movies.radiofree.com/interviews/ninelive_amanda_seyfried.shtml |title=Amanda Seyfried on 'Nine Lives' |author=Michael J. Lee |work=Movies. RadioFree.com |publisher=Radio Free Entertainment |date=June 21, 2005 |access-date=February 5, 2010 |archive-date=July 15, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110715150024/http://movies.radiofree.com/interviews/ninelive_amanda_seyfried.shtml |url-status=live }}</ref> For her performance, Seyfried, along with the film's other female leads, won an award from the Locarno International Film Festival, for Best Actress. The same year she played supporting character, Mouse, in the independent film ''[[American Gun (2005 film)|American Gun]]''. In 2006, Seyfried appeared in five episodes of ''[[Wildfire (2005 TV series)|Wildfire]]'' as Rebecca and had a lead role as Chrissy in the short film ''Gypsies, Tramps & Thieves'', by writer-director [[Andrea Janakas]]. Seyfried also contributed a minor role as Julie Beckley in ''[[Alpha Dog]]''. From 2004 to 2006 Seyfried made multiple guest appearances on television series, including ''[[House (TV series)|House]]'', ''[[Justice (2006 TV series)|Justice]]'', ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'', ''[[American Dad!]]'' and ''[[CSI: Crime Scene Investigation]]''.<ref name="askmeninterview" />

===2006–2010: Breakthrough===
Seyfried's profile grew considerably with her role in the highly acclaimed [[HBO]] drama television series ''[[Big Love]]''. The series centers on a fictional [[Mormon fundamentalism|fundamentalist Mormon]] family, in which Seyfried plays [[Sarah Henrickson]], Bill and Barb's first daughter, who struggles with her family's [[polygamy|polygamous]] faith.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.observer.com/node/51964 |author =Rebecca Dana |title=Raise the Red-State Lantern |work=[[The New York Observer]] |date=March 12, 2006 |access-date=January 2, 2010| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100107053432/http://www.observer.com/node/51964| archive-date= January 7, 2010 <!--DASHBot-->| url-status=dead}}</ref> ''Big Love'' premiered in the United States on March 12, 2006. In December 2009, HBO confirmed that Seyfried would return for the show's fourth season, but that it would be her last, as Seyfried wished to concentrate on her film career and upcoming projects.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.eonline.com/uberblog/watch_with_kristin/b158732_this_just_in_amanda_seyfried_says_bye.html |title=This Just In: Amanda Seyfried Says Bye to Big Love |work=[[E! Following ''Big Love'' Seyfried had a supporting role, as Zoe, in the 2008 horror drama film ''[[Solstice (film)|Solstice]]'', and co-starred alongside [[Meryl Streep]] in ''[[Mamma Mia! (film)|Mamma Mia!]]'', a [[romantic comedy]] [[film adaptation]] of the 1999 [[Mamma Mia! (musical)|musical of the same name]]. ''Mamma Mia!'' was Seyfried's first leading role. The film was the fifth highest-grossing film of 2008,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=mammamia.htm |work=[[Box Office Mojo]] |title=Mamma Mia!
