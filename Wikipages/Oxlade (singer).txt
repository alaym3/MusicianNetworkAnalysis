'''Ikuforiji Olaitan Abdulrahman''' (born 22 April 1997), known professionally by his stage name '''Oxlade''', is a Nigerian singer and songwriter. He rose to prominence with the release of the song "[[Away (Oxlade song)|Away]]", which appeared on [[Rolling Stone]] 50 best songs of 2020.

==Life and Career==
Ikuforiji Olaitan Abdulrahman was born on 22 April 1997 in [[Surulere]], a residential area of [[Lagos]].<ref name="Now Entertainment Magazine">{{cite web |title=Fast-growing Across The Europe - Oxlade |url=https://www.nowentertainment.net/fast-growing-across-the-europe-oxlade/ |website=Now Entertainment Magazine |access-date=11 November 2021}}</ref> After the passing of his mother at a tender age, he was raised by his father and grandmother.<ref name="Now Entertainment Magazine" /> His father was a lecturer at [[Lagos State University]], where he gained admission to study [[History]] and [[International Relations]]. He enrolled for two years before dropping off to purse his musical career.<ref name="Now Entertainment Magazine" />

In 2018, he was approached by A&R executive Bizzle Osikoya, right after the release of "Mamiwota" by [[Blaqbonez]], off the ''Bad Boy Blaq'' ep, released through 100 Crowns, an imprint of [[Chocolate City (record label)|Chocolate City]]. After his meeting with Bizzle, he signed a management deal with The Plug.<ref>{{cite web |title=Oxlade signed to The Plug |url=https://plugng.com/music/Oxade |website=plugng.com |access-date=10 November 2021 |language=en}}</ref> Thereafter he signed a publishing, licensing and distribution deal with The Plug Entertainment,<ref name="The Guardian Nigeria">{{cite web |title=Oxygene… A Bold Beginning For Oxlade |url=https://guardian.ng/life/music/oxygene-a-bold-beginning-for-oxlade/ |website=The Guardian Nigeria |access-date=10 November 2021 |date=9 May 2020}}</ref> and a record deal with Troniq Music. On 27 December 2018, [[Davido]] allowed Oxlade perform at City of David concert in [[Lagos]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Oxlade of Afrobeats |url=https://www.redbull.com/ng-en/oxlade-of-afrobeats |website=www.redbull.com |access-date=10 November 2021}}</ref> and he opened for [[Wizkid]] in London at Star Boy Fest in 2019.

On 13 April 2020, he released the music video for Away, directed by [[TG Omori]] for Troniq Music. On 15 April 2020, he tells, Tami Makinde of [[The Native]], he was instructed by [[TG Omori]], to sit on his lady's lap to personify the popular saying "behind every successful man is a woman".<ref>{{cite web |title=Oxlade's latest video "Away" is a celebration of a woman's power |url=https://thenativemag.com/oxlade-takes-us-gold-coast-video-latest-single-away/ |website=The NATIVE |access-date=10 November 2021 |date=15 April 2020}}</ref> On 7 November 2020, [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] posted a screenshot on his [[Instagram]] story, listening to Away.<ref>{{cite web |title=Why should anyone care that Drake is listening to Oxlade |url=https://thenativemag.com/anyone-care-drake-listening-oxlade/ |website=The NATIVE |access-date=10 November 2021 |date=8 November 2020}}</ref> Right after the Canadian rapper made the post, he gained 3 million streams with 24 hours.<ref>{{cite web |last1=https://hiphopdx.com |first1=HipHopDX- |title=Drake Co-Sign Gave Nigerian Artist Oxlade 3M Streams In A Day |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.65454/title.drake-co-sign-nigerian-artist-oxlade-3-million-streams |website=HipHopDX |access-date=10 November 2021 |date=27 October 2021}}</ref> He achieved mainstream recognition with his hit track "[[Away (Oxlade song)|Away]]"  which peaked at number twenty three on the Nigeria's [[TurnTable Top 50 chart|TurnTable Top 50]] Yearly chart,<ref name="ReferenceA" /> off his ep [[Oxygene (EP)|Oxygene]], released through Troniq Music, and The Plug Entertainment, on 27 March 2020. He released "DKT" independently on 3 September 2020, and peaked at number 13 on [[Billboard Global 200|Billboard Top Triller Global]] chart.<ref name="ReferenceB" /> He released the music video of "DKT" on 10 May 2021, shot and directed by Dammy Twitch.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Onabanjo |first1=Adedamola |title=Watch The Dramatic Dammy Twitch Directed Video For Oxlade’s ‘DKT’ |url=https://culturecustodian.com/oxlade-dkt-video/ |website=The Culture Custodian (Est. 2014.) |access-date=10 November 2021 |date=11 May 2021}}</ref>

In January 2021, his collaboration with Dolapo, and [[Ms Banks]] on their song "Interest", earned him his first entry on [[Capital XTRA]] Airplay Chart at number eleven on 18 February 2021. and also earned him, his first career entry on [[UK Afrobeats Singles Chart]] at number ten, and spent twelve consecutive weeks on chart.<ref name="ReferenceE" /> On 15 January 2021, the music video for "Interest", was released and directed by Capone.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tv |first1=Bn |title=New Video: Dolapo feat. Ms Banks & Oxlade – Interest |url=https://www.bellanaija.com/2021/01/dolapo-ms-banks-oxlade-interest-video/ |website=BellaNaija |access-date=11 November 2021 |date=16 January 2021}}</ref> In October 2021, he earned his second entry on the [[UK Afrobeats Singles Chart]] with his track "Ojuju", which is currently on chart for five consecutive weeks, since 3 October.<ref name="ReferenceD" /> It also peaked at number thirteen on Nigeria's [[TurnTable Top 50 chart|TurnTable Top 50]] weekly chart.<ref name="ReferenceC" /> On 3 September 2021, he released the official music video for "Ojuju", directed and shot by Ademola Falomo. He was featured on the single "Kolo" by [[Ice Prince]], which spent eighteen consecutive weeks on chart, and peaked at number eight on [[TurnTable Top 50 chart|TurnTable Top 50]] weekly chart.<ref name="ReferenceF" /> On 11 March 2021, the music video for "Kolo", was released and directed by Director UAX.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tv |first1=Bn |title=New Music + Video: Ice Prince feat. Oxlade – Kolo |url=https://www.bellanaija.com/2021/03/ice-prince-oxlade-kolo/ |website=BellaNaija |access-date=11 November 2021 |date=11 March 2021}}</ref>

He was featured on the song "Overdue" from [[Coming 2 America]] studio album [[Rhythms of Zamunda]], curated by Sipho Dlamini, and released on 5 March 2021, through Def Jam Africa, and [[Def Jam Recordings]]. On 20 August 2021, Troniq Music, and Oxlade, released the four-tracks extended play ''Eclipse'', produced solely by [[DJ Coublon]], through Troniq Music, and [[Epic Records]] France.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Alake |first1=Motolani |title=Oxlade serves a timely reminder of his potential on ‘Eclipse’ [Pulse EP Review] |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/review-oxlade-and-troniq-music-eclipse-ep/xyw2dfq |website=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=11 November 2021 |language=en |date=23 August 2021}}</ref> On 24 August 2021, Oxlade performed "Slow Down" by [[Skip Marley]] for [[Grammy Awards|The Grammy]] web series popularly known as Reimagined At Home.<ref>{{cite web |title=Oxlade Performs Skip Marley's "Slow Down" |url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/reimagined-at-home-oxlade-performs-skip-marley-cover-slow-down |website=GRAMMY.com |access-date=11 November 2021 |language=en |date=24 August 2021}}</ref>

==Artistry==
His voice as described by Efe Ukpebor of [[Nigeria Entertainment Today]], as "smooth", with seamless blend of musical genres.<ref>{{cite web |title=Meet Oxlade, the Nigerian Pop Artiste You Should Look Out For |url=https://thenet.ng/meet-oxlade-nigerian-pop-artiste/ |website=Nigerian Entertainment Today |access-date=10 November 2021 |date=11 May 2020}}</ref> He describes his sound as a bridge between the Alte (Afro-fusion/experimental music expressions) and the mainstream.<ref name="The Guardian Nigeria" /> His lyrics are a mixture of English, and Pidgin English, often exploring contemporary topics such as love.
