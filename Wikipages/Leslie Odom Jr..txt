'''Leslie Lloyd Odom Jr.''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|oʊ|d|ə|m}}; born August 6, 1981)<ref>{{Cite web|title=You are being redirected...|url=https://pennsylvanianewstoday.com/leslie-odom-jr-explains-the-role-of-his-big-many-saints-of-newark/232859/|access-date=2021-11-20|website=pennsylvanianewstoday.com}}</ref> is an American actor, singer and songwriter. He made his acting debut on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] in 1998 and first gained recognition for his portrayal of [[Aaron Burr]] in the musical ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]'', which earned him a [[70th Tony Awards|2016]] [[Tony Award for Best Actor in a Musical|Tony Award for Best Actor in a Leading Role in a Musical]] and a [[Grammy Award for Best Musical Theater Album]] in the [[58th Annual Grammy Awards|same year]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=You are being redirected...|url=https://bostonpopsjuly4th.org/leslie-odom-jr/|access-date=2021-07-14|website=bostonpopsjuly4th.org}}</ref>

Odom is also known for his roles in the television series ''[[Smash (TV series)|Smash]]'' (2012–2013) and ''[[Person of Interest (TV series)|Person of Interest]]'' (2013–2014), as well as the films ''[[Murder on the Orient Express (2017 film)|Murder on the Orient Express]]'' (2017), ''[[Harriet (film)|Harriet]]'' (2019), and ''[[Hamilton (2020 film)|Hamilton]]'' (2020). Odom currently voices the character of Owen Tillerman in the [[Apple TV+]] animated musical-comedy series ''[[Central Park (TV series)|Central Park]],'' for which he was nominated for an [[Emmy Award]] in 2020.

For his performance as singer [[Sam Cooke]] in ''[[One Night in Miami...]]'' (2020), Odom earned [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor|Academy Award]], [[BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role|BAFTA Award]], [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Male Actor in a Supporting Role|Screen Actors Guild Award]], [[Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Supporting Actor|Critics' Choice Movie Award]], and [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Motion Picture|Golden Globe Awards]] nominations for Best Supporting Actor. He was also nominated for an [[Academy Award for Best Original Song|Oscar]] and [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Golden Globe]] for writing the film's original song "[[Speak Now (Leslie Odom Jr. and Sam Ashworth song)|Speak Now]]," for which he won [[Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Song|Best Song]] at the 2021 Critics' Choice Awards.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title='Speak Now' Wins Critics Choice Award for Best Song, Muddying the Oscar Race|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/awards/9536133/critics-choice-award-music-winners|access-date=2021-04-22|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> Later [[73rd Primetime Emmy Awards|that year]], he received a nomination for a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Limited or Anthology Series or Movie|Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Actor in a Leading Role in a Limited Series or Movie]] for his performance in the 2020 [[Disney+]] [[Hamilton (2020 film)|live stage recording]] of ''Hamilton''. Also in 2021, he received critical praise for his role as Harold McBrayer in ''[[The Sopranos]]'' prequel film ''[[The Many Saints of Newark]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Woods|first=Kathia|title=Leslie Odom Jr. embraces the darkness in 'Sopranos' prequel|url=https://www.phillytrib.com/entertainment/movies/leslie-odom-jr-embraces-the-darkness-in-sopranos-prequel/article_0a85d5c1-1b47-5c82-8324-bedf8fbd1f64.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-10-11|website=The Philadelphia Tribune|language=en}}</ref>

He has released four albums and a book.<ref name="USNews" />

==Early life and education ==
Odom was born in [[Queens]], [[New York City]].<ref name=Playbill-bio/> His family later moved to the [[East Oak Lane, Philadelphia|East Oak Lane]] section of [[Philadelphia]], where he grew up. He attended [[Julia R. Masterman School]] for middle school and [[Philadelphia High School for Creative and Performing Arts]] for high school.<ref name=Whelan2015/><ref name=Gray2013/> He and his family attended Canaan Baptist Church in the [[Germantown, Philadelphia|Germantown]] section of Philadelphia, where he sang solos in the church choir.<ref name=Whelan2015/>

He earned a degree with honors at [[Carnegie Mellon University]] in [[Pittsburgh]], Pennsylvania, and then moved to Los Angeles in the summer of 2003.

==Career==
===Acting career===

====Theater====
Odom attended the [[New Freedom Theatre]]  where he studied theater and performed in musicals. He then went on to study musical theater in college. At the age of 17 he made his [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] debut as Paul in ''[[Rent (musical)|Rent]]''. In 2001, he appeared in the ensemble of the one-night Broadway concert version of ''[[Dreamgirls]]''.

He spent much of his time doing theatre in Los Angeles, including in 2010 with ''[[Leap of Faith (musical)|Leap of Faith]]'',<ref name=ibdbcite/> which moved to Broadway in 2012, with him starring as Isaiah Sturdevant. When the show closed shortly afterward, he relocated to New York City.

After starring [[Off-Broadway]] in ''[[Venice (musical)|Venice]]'' and in ''[[Witness Uganda]]'' (later retitled ''[[Invisible Thread (musical)|Invisible Thread]]'') at A.R.T. and in workshops, Odom worked with [[Lin-Manuel Miranda]] in the [[Encores!|Encores! Off-Center]] production of ''[[Tick, Tick... Boom!]]'', playing Michael. Odom also played [[Nat King Cole]] in the 2015 one-night [[Actors Fund of America]] benefit concert of ''[[Bombshell (musical)|Bombshell]]''.<ref name=ActorsFund/>

[[File:Leslie_Odom,_Jr.,_in_Hamilton_costume,_July_2015.jpg|thumb|250px|Odom in ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]'' in July 2015.]]
Odom was nominated for a 2015 [[Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Featured Actor in a Musical]]<ref name=ibdbcite/> for the role of [[Aaron Burr]] in the off-Broadway production of ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]'' at The Public Theater. He continued in the same role at the [[Richard Rodgers Theatre]] after the show transferred to Broadway later that year. He also won a 2016 Grammy Award for the cast album, and won the [[Tony Award for Best Actor in a Musical]].

Odom's final appearance in the role of Aaron Burr in ''Hamilton'' occurred on July 9, 2016.<ref name=Paulson2016/>

====Television====
Odom had a recurring role on ''[[CSI: Miami]]'', appearing as Joseph Kayle in nine episodes between 2003 and 2006.

He followed with a recurring role on ''[[Vanished (TV series)|Vanished]]'', appearing in 10 episodes as Maliko Christo. From 2006 to 2007, he held a recurring role as Freddy on ''[[Big Day]]'', appearing on nine episodes prior to its cancellation. He also had a supporting role in the TV movie ''Poe'' in 2011.

In the musical television series ''[[Smash (U.S. TV series)|Smash]]'', Odom had a recurring role as Sam Strickland in 2012, and was promoted to a starring role for the show's final season in 2013.

In late 2013, Odom was offered the leading role of Lucas Newsome on ''[[State of Affairs (TV series)|State of Affairs]]''. He accepted the role, but ended up backing out to take the part of [[Aaron Burr]] in the workshops of the musical ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]''.

In 2013 and 2014, he had a recurring role on ''[[Person of Interest (TV series)|Person of Interest]]'' as Peter Collier, appearing in eight episodes. He also played Reverend Curtis Scott in seven episodes of ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'' between 2013 and 2015.

Beginning on May 19, 2016, Odom hosted the [[Broadway.com]] web series ''Aaron Burr, Sir: Backstage at Hamilton with Leslie Odom Jr.'', with a new episode being uploaded every Thursday for eight weeks. Odom was also set to play [[Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis|Jimmy Jam]] in a 2017 miniseries about [[New Edition]], but ended up leaving the project.

In 2020, Odom starred and executive produced the four-part, television miniseries ''[[Love in the Time of Corona]]'' opposite his wife [[Nicolette Robinson]].<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Ramos|first1=Dino-Ray|last2=Ramos|first2=Dino-Ray|date=2020-06-29|title='Love In The Time Of Corona': Freeform Sets Leslie Odom Jr., Nicolette Robinson, Tommy Dorfman, Rainey Qualley And 4 More For Limited Series Event|url=https://deadline.com/2020/06/love-in-the-time-of-corona-freeform-leslie-odom-jr-nicolette-robinson-tommy-dorfman-rainey-qualle-gil-bellows-rya-kihlstedt-ava-bellows-l-scott-caldwell-1202972774/|access-date=2021-04-22|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref> ''Love in the Time of Corona'' follows four interwoven stories about the hopeful search for love and connection during the quarantine as a result of the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United States|COVID-19 pandemic]]. Production began virtually on June 29, 2020, in [[Los Angeles]] using remote technologies.<ref>{{Cite web|last=White|first=Peter|date=2020-05-07|title='Love in the Time of Corona': Freeform Orders COVID-19 Scripted Comedy From 'Good Trouble' EP Joanna Johnson & Anonymous Content|url=https://deadline.com/2020/05/love-in-the-time-of-corona-freeform-joanna-johnson-1202928885/|access-date=2021-04-22|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref> The limited series premiered on [[Freeform (TV channel)|Freeform]] on August 22, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pedersen|first=Erik|date=2020-07-27|title='Love In The Time Of Corona': Premiere Date & Teaser For Freeform's Rom-Com Limited-Series|url=https://deadline.com/2020/07/love-in-the-time-of-corona-premiere-date-teaser-for-freeform-comedy-series-1202996136/|access-date=2021-04-22|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref>

Odom currently voices the character of Owen Tillerman in the [[Apple TV+]] animated musical-comedy series ''[[Central Park (TV series)|Central Park]],'' for which he was nominated for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Character Voice-Over Performance|Outstanding Character Voice-Over Performance]] at the 2020 [[72nd Primetime Emmy Awards|Primetime Emmy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Leslie Odom, Jr.|url=https://www.emmys.com/bios/leslie-odom-jr|access-date=2021-04-22|website=Television Academy|language=en}}</ref>

He has made guest appearances on several shows including ''[[Gilmore Girls]]'', ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]'', ''[[Supernatural (U.S. TV series)|Supernatural]]'', and ''[[The Good Wife]]''. In 2016, Odom was also featured in a commercial for [[Nationwide Insurance]].<ref name="nationwide" /><ref name="nationwide2" />

On February 4, 2018, Odom sang "[[America the Beautiful]]" during the [[NBC]] broadcast of [[Super Bowl LII]].<ref name=Gans2018/>

====Film====
Odom has appeared in short films, and had a supporting role as Walter Hall in the 2012 war film ''[[Red Tails]]''.

He played Dr. Arbuthnot in [[Kenneth Branagh]]'s 2017 adaptation of ''[[Murder on the Orient Express (2017 film)|Murder on the Orient Express]]''.<ref name=Fleming2016/> He also starred as abolitionist [[William Still]] in the 2019 film ''[[Harriet (film)|Harriet]]'', about abolitionist [[Harriet Tubman]]. In 2019, Odom starred in ''[[Only (film)|Only]]'', a post-apocalyptic romance.

In 2021, Odom starred as Ebo in musical film [[Music (2021 film)|''Music'']], which was co-written and directed by Australian singer-songwriter [[Sia (musician)|Sia]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-19|title=Kate Hudson And Leslie Odom Jr. Star In Sia's Directorial Debut 'Music' [VIDEO]|url=https://bossip.com/1981605/kate-hudson-and-leslie-odom-jr-star-in-sias-directorial-debut-music-video/|access-date=2020-12-22|website=Bossip}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Ramachandran|first=Naman|date=2020-10-12|title=Sia's 'Music,' Starring Kate Hudson, Begins Global Sales Journey With HanWay Films|url=https://variety.com/2020/film/global/sia-music-kate-hudson-hanway-1234801228/|access-date=2020-12-22|website=Variety|language=en-US}}</ref> It was released in February 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Curto|first=Justin|date=2020-11-19|title=Sia Made a Movie Called Music (and an Album Too)|url=https://www.vulture.com/2020/11/sia-music-movie-trailer.html|access-date=2020-12-22|website=Vulture|language=en-us}}</ref>

In 2020, Odom was cast as legendary [[soul music|soul]] singer [[Sam Cooke]] in the [[Regina King]]–directed film adaptation of ''[[One Night in Miami...]]'', which was released in theaters and [[Amazon Prime]] on January 15, 2021. Odom received rave reviews from critics for his portrayal of Cooke and has since earned [[Academy Award]], [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor - Motion Picture|Golden Globe]], and [[SAG Award]] nominations including for the [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Male Actor in a Supporting Role|Best Supporting Actor]] and an additional nomination for [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture|Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture]]. With [[Sam Ashworth (songwriter)|Sam Ashworth]] as co-writer, Odom co-wrote and performed "[[Speak Now (Leslie Odom Jr. and Sam Ashworth song)|Speak Now]]", which also received a nomination for [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]] at the [[78th Golden Globe Awards]] to be held on February 28, 2021. "Speak Now" is played during the end credits of ''One Night in Miami''.

In May 2021, Odom was cast in the [[Knives Out#Sequels|sequel to ''Knives Out''.]]<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Kit|first1=Mia Galuppo,Borys|last2=Galuppo|first2=Mia|last3=Kit|first3=Borys|date=2021-05-18|title=Leslie Odom Jr. Joins Daniel Craig in 'Knives Out 2' (Exclusive)|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/leslie-odom-jr-knives-out-sequel-1234954701/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-18|website=The Hollywood Reporter|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Recording career===
In 2014, Odom self-released his debut album as a jazz singer, ''[[Leslie Odom Jr. (album)|Leslie Odom Jr.]]'' on CD and SoundCloud, and promoted the album with several concerts at [[The Public Theater]]. In February 2016, he released a version of [[Selena Gomez]]'s "[[Good For You (song)|Good For You]]" featuring his ''Hamilton'' castmate [[Daveed Diggs]].<ref name=Elle2016/>

Odom was signed to a four-album deal with [[S-Curve Records]] in 2016.<ref name=CBS2016/> He and producer [[Steve Greenberg (record producer)|Steve Greenberg]] narrowed down 200 potential tunes to ten tracks, and Odom recorded an updated and improved version of ''Leslie Odom Jr.'' during days off and afternoons before Broadway performances, in order to release the album before Odom left ''Hamilton''.<ref name=Lee2016/> Released in June 2016, the album charted at No. 1 on [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] Jazz and No. 147 on [[Billboard 200]].

On November 11, 2016, Odom released his second album, ''[[Simply Christmas]]'', which featured jazz interpretations of Christmas standards. A deluxe edition of the album was released in October 2017, with four additional songs.<ref name=Snyder2017/> ''Simply Christmas'' hit #1 on iTunes and the Billboard Jazz charts, reaching No. 4 on the Billboard Holiday chart and No. 31 on the [[Billboard 200|Billboard Top 200]] chart.

Odom released his third album and first of original material, ''Mr,'' in November 2019, and in October 2020, he debuted a new version of the album's standout song "Cold" featuring [[Sia (musician)|Sia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Mr-Leslie-Odom-Jr/dp/B07Y4K73GB|access-date=2021-04-22|website=www.amazon.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Leslie Odom Jr. Taps Sia to Heat Up His Song 'Cold': Listen|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9459326/leslie-odom-jr-sia-cold/|access-date=2021-04-22|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

His critically acclaimed second holiday album, ''The Christmas Album,'' was released in November 2020. ''The Christmas Album'' is Odom's fourth studio album.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-30|title='Hamilton' star Leslie Odom Jr. releases new Christmas album ahead of Sam Cooke role in 'One Night in Miami'|url=https://www.ocregister.com/2020/11/30/hamilton-star-leslie-odom-jr-releases-new-christmas-album-ahead-of-sam-cooke-role-in-one-night-in-miami|access-date=2021-04-22|website=Orange County Register|language=en-US}}</ref>

Odom tours to promote his albums, performing concerts backed by a jazz quintet that includes a drummer, percussionist, bassist, guitarist and a pianist who is also Odom's musical director.<ref name=Roa2017/>

In December 2017, Odom returned to the New York City stage in a solo concert at [[Jazz at Lincoln Center]]. The cabaret-style performance was crafted around signature songs and music that shaped this artist's journey, all performed with a world-class band in front of a live audience. The show was filmed for broadcast as an hour-long [[PBS]] special as part of the 17-time Emmy Award-winning series, ''[[Live from Lincoln Center]]'', and premiered in April 2018.

===Writing===
In June 2017, it was announced that Odom had a book deal for ''Failing Up: How to Take Risks, Aim Higher, and Never Stop Learning'', which he was writing with the intention to inspire younger readers.<ref name=GansBook/> The book, {{Show by date|2018|3|27|to be published|which was published|hour=-5}} by an imprint of [[Macmillan Publishers|Macmillan]] in March 2018, "outlines the setbacks and rejections that preceded his success."<ref name=USNews/> Odom modeled his writing on the style of a commencement speech, exploring what he had learned throughout his life and the importance of pursuing passions.<ref name=GansBook/>

Odom has been married to actress [[Nicolette Kloe Robinson]] since December 1, 2012.<ref name=Playbill2015/> Their daughter, Lucille Ruby, was born on April 23, 2017.<ref name=Playbill2017/> In November 2020, Leslie and his wife announced that they are expecting their second child, a boy, in March 2021. The couple met in 2008 while Robinson was a student at [[UCLA]] and auditioned for ''[[Once on This Island]]'' in Los Angeles.<ref name=Playbill2015/> When she replaced a cast member who left unexpectedly, assistant director Odom became responsible for bringing her quickly up to speed, and a romantic relationship ensued.<ref name=Playbill2015/> Robinson has since appeared in the off-Broadway musical ''[[Invisible Thread (musical)|Invisible Thread]]'' (2015) and the television series ''[[The Affair (TV series)|The Affair]]'',<ref name=Playbill2015/> and played the lead role in the Broadway musical ''[[Waitress (musical)|Waitress]]'' from September 4 through December 9, 2018.<ref name=Hart2018/>
