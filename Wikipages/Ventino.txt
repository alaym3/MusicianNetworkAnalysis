'''Ventino''' is a [[Latin pop]] [[girl group]], formed in [[Bogotá]], Colombia, that first gained popularity on [[YouTube]], singing [[cover version]]s before becoming known for their original material.<ref>{{cite web|first=Selene|last=Moral|url=http://los40.com/los40/2017/05/17/musica/1495041504_337051.html|title=Conoce a Ventino, la girl band colombiana que triunfa con sus covers|publisher=[[Los 40]]|date=May 17, 2017|accessdate=September 18, 2018|language=Spanish}}</ref> They released their self-titled debut album in 2018. Its members are María Cristina "Makis" de Angulo, Camila Esguerra, Natalia Afanador and Olga Lucía Vives.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.eltiempo.com/cultura/musica-y-libros/planes-y-conciertos-para-el-fin-de-semana-en-bogota-118490|title=Encuentre aquí los mejores planes para el fin de semana|newspaper=[[El Tiempo (Colombia)|El Tiempo]]|date=August 10, 2017|accessdate=September 19, 2018|language=Spanish}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== Formation ===
Prior to their formation, each of the group's members performed in musical theater productions at their respective high schools.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elespectador.com/entretenimiento/unchatcon/sacame-los-ojos-nuevo-sencillo-de-santiago-deluchi-articulo-648656|title='Sácame los ojos', nuevo sencillo de Santiago Deluchi|newspaper=[[El Espectador]]|date=August 11, 2016|accessdate=September 19, 2018|language=Spanish}}</ref> Natalia Afanador and Juliana Pérez attended the same high school (Gimnasio La Montaña), while Camila Esguerra, Makis de Angulo and Olga Vives attended a neighboring school (Colegio Los Nogales).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ritmoromantica.pe/noticias/musica/ventino-me-equivoque-75361#1|title=Conoce más de Ventino, el grupo revelación de Ritmo Romántica|work=Radio Ritmo Romántica|date=June 21, 2017|accessdate=September 19, 2018|language=Spanish}}</ref> On October 22, 2014, Camila Esguerra and Natalia Afanador uploaded their first video to [[YouTube]], a [[mashup (music)|mashup]] cover of [[Sia (musician)|Sia]]'s "[[Chandelier (song)|Chandelier]]" and [[Rihanna]]'s "[[Diamonds (Rihanna song)|Diamonds]]".<ref>{{cite web|title=Chandelier/Diamonds Cover - Camila Esguerra & Natalia Afanador|date=October 22, 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w3Ns7qyxHH8|publisher=YouTube|accessdate=September 18, 2018}}</ref>

=== YouTube covers and rising popularity ===
In 2015 a friend of all five then future-members, Juan David Muñoz gathered the five young women together to record a video of them singing a mashup of various Disney Princess songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bluradio.com/107232/ojala-podamos-ser-reconocidas-en-todo-el-mundo-grupo-juvenil-ventino|title=Ojalá podamos ser reconocidas en todo el mundo: grupo juvenil Ventino|work=[[Bluradio]]|date=August 9, 2015|accessdate=September 19, 2018|language=Spanish}}</ref> On April 8, 2015 they uploaded the video to YouTube. Within a month "Disney Princess Mashup" surpassed a million views. It was this experience that led the five members to pursue music as a group with Muñoz continuing to work with them as a producer and mentor.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.eluniversal.com.co/farandula/conozca-ventino-las-chicas-que-entonaron-el-himno-nacional-en-barranquilla-237651|title=Conozca a 'Ventino', las chicas que entonaron el Himno Nacional en Barranquilla|newspaper=[[El Universal (Cartagena)|El Universal]]|date=October 13, 2016|accessdate=September 19, 2018|language=Spanish}}</ref> They spent much of 2015 and 2016 recording and uploading covers of artists such as [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]], [[Queen (band)|Queen]], [[Adele]] and [[Beyoncé]].

After a year together, as the group's popularity grew, they met Pedro Malaver who, at the time, managed [[Cali & El Dandee]] and Morat. He arranged a meeting with [[Sony Music]] Colombia who signed them to a record deal.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://blogs.eltiempo.com/natalia-gnecco-blog/2016/09/28/ventino-a-la-conquista-del-publico-hispano/|title=Ventino: a la conquista del público hispano|date=2016-09-28|work=Natalia Gnecco Blog|access-date=2018-09-18|language=Spanish}}</ref>

=== Christmas album and debut studio album ===
Following their record deal with Sony Music Colombia, Ventino began working on their debut album.

On October 14, 2016 they released their debut single, "Me Equivoque". Its video reached over 95 million views.<ref>{{Citation|last=VentinoVEVO|title=Ventino - Me Equivoqué (Video Oficial)|date=2016-10-14|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Me7sZrL6OhM|publisher=YouTube|access-date=2018-09-18}}</ref> They spent the remainder of 2016 and 2017 releasing a string of singles leading up to their self-titled debut album. The singles "Apaga y Vamonos" and "Si Decides (Baby)" spread their popularity to Spain and throughout Latin America.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://myipop.net/2017/10/27/apaga-y-vamonos-es-el-nuevo-single-de-ventino/|title=‘Apaga y Vámonos’ es el nuevo single de Ventino|date=2017-10-27|work=www.MyiPop.net|access-date=2018-09-18|language=es-ES}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.sonymusic.com.mx/ventino-lanza-su-nueva-cancion-si-decides-baby/|title=VENTINO lanza su nueva canción "SI DECIDES (BABY)" - Sony Music Entertainment México|date=2018-05-04|work=Sony Music Entertainment México|access-date=2018-09-18|language=es-MX}}</ref> Prior to releasing their debut studio album, the group recorded and released a Christmas album, ''Ya Es Navidad,'' on November 17, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cnnespanol.cnn.com/video/cnnee-zona-pop-dig-orig-huston-ventino-ya-es-navidad/|title=Así suena la Navidad con Ventino|date=2017-12-25|website=CNN|language=es-ES|access-date=2019-04-02}}</ref> The album was produced by Colombian singer, songwriter and producer Santiago Deluchi.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elheraldo.co/entretenimiento/ventino-lanza-album-de-navidad-y-presenta-video-de-el-tamborilero-429080|title=Ventino lanza álbum de Navidad y presenta video de ‘El tamborilero’|website=www.elheraldo.co|language=es|access-date=2019-04-02}}</ref>

They released their debut studio album ''Ventino'' on August 17, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Ventino-Ventino/release/12449467|title=Ventino - Ventino|website=Discogs|language=en|access-date=2018-09-18}}</ref>

=== Continued singles and second studio album ===
Following the release of their self-titled album, the group continued recording new music and released "Yo Te Quiero Más" in November 2018.<ref>{{Citation|last=VentinoVEVO|title=Ventino - Yo Te Quiero Más (Video Oficial) ft. Mike Bahía|date=2018-11-02|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=64EhJbCMwe0|access-date=2019-04-02}}</ref> The release was a collaboration with Colombian singer Mike Bahia.  In February 2019 they followed "Yo Te Quiero Mas" with a new single, "Ya Te Perdone",<ref>{{Citation|last=VentinoVEVO|title=Ventino - Ya Te Perdoné (Video Oficial)|date=2019-02-08|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7vLsoBhaTPo|access-date=2019-04-02}}</ref> "Andan Diciendo",<ref>{{Citation|last=VentinoVEVO|title=Ventino - Andan Diciendo (Video Oficial)|date=2019-05-03|url=https://youtube.com/watch?v=phH9MCNObhM|access-date=2019-06-29}}</ref> and "Don Juan."

===New Ep - ''Otra Noche'' ===
On September 13, 2019, the group released a new 4 track ep entitled ''Otra Noche''. The EP's tracks expanded on the ballad and midtempo sound that they are popular for and the group was lauded for their versatility in sound and for the group's vocals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://colombia.as.com/colombia/2019/09/14/tikitakas/1568418561_795152.html|title=Ventino llega cargada de nuevos sonidos con 'Otra Noche'|date=2019-09-13|website=AS Colombia|language=es-co|access-date=2019-11-07}}</ref> It features collaborations with Jeon and Roman. In support of this new project, the group embarked of a tour throughout [[Colombia]], including their first acoustic concert.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wradio.com.co/noticias/sociedad/el-grupo-ventino-presentara-su-primer-show-acustico/20190902/nota/3948634.aspx|title=El grupo Ventino presentará su primer show acústico|date=2019-09-02|website=www.wradio.com.co|language=es|access-date=2019-11-07}}</ref>
