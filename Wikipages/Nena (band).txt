'''Nena''' was a German [[Neue Deutsche Welle]] band. In 1983 and 1984, their German-language song "[[99 Luftballons]]" (and its English version, "99 Red Balloons") reached number one in the singles charts of countries around the world.

==History==
===Formation and rise===
The band was formed in 1982 when vocalist Gabriele Kerner ([[Nena]]) came to [[West Berlin]] with drummer [[:de:Rolf Brendel|Rolf Brendel]], her boyfriend at the time.<ref name="Allmusic biography">{{cite web|title=AllMusic biography – Nena|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/nena-mn0000395187/biography|website=AllMusic.com|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref> There they assembled the other members of the band, which took its name from their lead singer's nickname (Spanish for "little girl"), which she had acquired as a toddler during a family holiday to Spain.<ref>In the RTL documentary ''100 prozent Nena'', Nena explains the derivation of her nickname.</ref>

The band wrote all of their songs themselves, typically working in pairs. They became overnight sensations in Germany when they performed their debut single "[[Nena (album)#"Nur geträumt"|Nur geträumt]]" on German TV in August 1982, Nena herself wearing a distinctive short red skirt.<ref name="Allmusic biography" /> The single reached number 2 in the German charts, a position it occupied for 6 weeks, and also climbed high in the Austrian, Belgian, Dutch and Swiss charts.<ref name="BEL (FL)" /><ref name=swiss /><ref name=nld /><ref name=austria /> In early 1983 the follow-up single "[[99 Luftballons]]" spent a further 7 weeks at number 2 before finally reaching the top slot, a position [[Nena (album)|the debut album]] matched.<ref name="German charts">{{cite web|title=Official German charts – Nena|url=http://www.officialcharts.de/suche.asp?search=nena&x=8&y=3&country=de|website=charts.de|publisher=GfK Entertainment|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref>

By the time the band released its second album ''[[? (Nena album)|? (Fragezeichen)]]'' in Germany in January 1984, "99 Luftballons" was starting to climb the US charts, where it reached number 2 and became one of the most successful non-English-language tracks in US Billboard chart history. With the English version of the song ("99 Red Balloons") achieving the number 1 position in the UK,<ref name="UK charts">{{cite web|title=UK charts – Nena|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/_/nena/|website=Official charts.com|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref> the band became internationally famous and in 1984 gave concerts and made TV appearances in Scandinavia, the UK, Spain and France as well as a 7-date tour (on consecutive nights) of Japan.<ref>{{cite web|title=Nena tour dates 1984|url=http://www.nena.de/tour/live-1984|website=Nena.de|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Nena Japan tour dates|url=http://www.nena.de/tour/japan-tour-1984|website=Nena.de|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref> An international album ''[[99 Luftballons (album)|99 Luftballons]]'' which comprised a combination of English and German versions of tracks from the first two German albums, performed respectably, reaching number 31 in the UK charts in mid-1984.<ref name="UK charts" />

Whilst enjoying international prominence on the back of "99 Luftballons", the band continued to enjoy domestic success in Germany with a string of hits in 1984 and the first half of 1985. The ''? (Fragezeichen)'' album matched the band's debut album in making it to the top spot<ref name="German charts" /> and spawned two of the band's best-known and most successful singles (the title track and "Rette mich"). They also had success with a couple of songs which would eventually be included on their third studio album, ''[[Feuer und Flamme]]'', most notably "[[Irgendwie, irgendwo, irgendwann]]".

===Decline and discontinuation===
The band was unable to follow up the international success of "99 Luftballons". An English version of "Nur geträumt" ("Just a Dream") failed to make the Billboard Hot 100 and only reached number 70 in the UK charts.<ref name="UK charts" /> A second English album (''[[Feuer und Flamme#It's All in the Game track listing|It's All in the Game]]'', an English version of ''Feuer und Flamme'') failed to chart.

The band's fortunes in its homeland also began to turn in the second half of 1985, with disappointing attendance levels at their concerts<ref name="Allmusic biography" /> and their successful chart run ending when the last two singles from ''Feuer und Flamme'' failed to chart.

The band's fourth and final studio album ''[[Eisbrecher (Nena album)|Eisbrecher]]'' ("Icebreaker"), apparently so named because it was intended to smash the mounting indifference the band was encountering, was released in late 1986.<ref name="Allmusic Eisbrecher">{{cite web|title=Allmusic "Eisbrecher" review|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/eisbrecher-mw0000535686|website=Allmusic.com|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref> Relative to its three predecessors it was a commercial failure as were the two last singles released from the album.<ref name="German charts" /> Without ever formally announcing a breakup, the band discontinued in 1987. In the same year the marriage of Nena's sister to the band's bassist [[Jürgen Dehmel]] ended in divorce<ref>{{cite web|title=Nena then and now|url=http://www.lostidols.com/files/n/nena.html|website=lostidols.com|access-date=6 November 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20020907235717/http://www.lostidols.com/files/n/nena.html|archive-date=7 September 2002}}</ref> and Nena's 8-year relationship with the band's drummer, Rolf Brendel, also ended.<ref name="Bunte - Brendel">{{cite web|title=Wir stritten uns bis aufs Blut|url=http://www.bunte.de/stars/nena-wir-stritten-bis-aufs-blut-106093.html|website=Bunte.de|publisher=Bunte|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref>

===Epilogue===
After a break from the music scene during which she starred in a film and had a baby boy who died at the age of 11 months, Nena launched her solo career in 1989 going on to enjoy considerable success in Germany, Austria and Switzerland, particularly since 2002 when she re-recorded the band's biggest successes, newly arranged and produced by her former band colleague and composer of most of the band's hit songs, Uwe Fahrenkrog-Petersen. Since the birth of her fifth and final child in 1997, she has toured Germany more or less constantly, at least 50% of her concert setlists typically comprising tracks from the Nena band era.<ref name="Nena tours">{{cite web|title=Nena tours history and setlists|url=http://www.nena.de/gigografie|website=Nena.de|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref>

Throughout her solo career, Nena has continued to collaborate with former band members. Firstly, Nena's former boyfriend, Rolf Brendel, supported her as a drummer in the ''[[Bongo Girl]]'' tour in 1993.<ref>See Neue Welle TV program "100 Grad" broadcast 30 March 1993</ref> Most enduring has been the link with the band's former keyboard player [[Uwe Fahrenkrog-Petersen]] who appeared with her on stage during the 2005 tour promoting the triple-gold achieving ''[[Willst du mit mir gehn]]'' album which he had also produced and co-written the singles for.<ref name=WDMMG>{{cite web|title=Willst du mit mir gehn credits|url=http://www.nena.de/discografie/willst-du-mit-mir-gehn|website=Nena.de|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref> He also collaborated with Nena for the 2009 album ''[[Made in Germany (Nena album)|Made in Germany]]'', which also achieved gold status.<ref name=MIG>{{cite web|title=Made in Germany credits|url=http://www.nena.de/discografie/made-germany|website=Nena.de|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref> The band's former guitarist, [[Carlo Karges]], also wrote tracks for some of Nena's solo albums in the 1990s. He died in 2002, just months before all the band's surviving members reunited on stage to perform "[[Nena (album)#"Leuchtturm"|Leuchtturm]]" during Nena's triumphant Frankfurt concert which coincided with the release of the triple-platinum ''[[Nena feat. Nena]]'' album which regalvanised her solo career.

The surviving members next publicly reunited in 2017 to perform "[[Nena (album)#"Nur geträumt"|Nur geträumt]]" in a television programme marking the 40th anniversary of Nena's first stage appearance.<ref>{{cite web|title=Nena: Reunion mit der alten Band|url=http://www.klatsch-tratsch.de/2017/09/23/nena-reunion-mit-der-alten-band/321351|website=Klatsch-tratsch.de|access-date=28 September 2017}}</ref>

* [[Jürgen Dehmel]] – bass, [[Chapman Stick]], keyboards, synthesizer
* Rolf Brendel – drums, percussion

===International albums===
Following the international success of the single "[[99 Luftballons]]", the band released ''99 Luftballons'', a compilation of tracks from its first two albums, with five of the songs in new English-language versions. ''It's All in the Game'' is the English-language version of ''Feuer und Flamme'', with lyrics by Canadian singer [[Lisa Dalbello]].
