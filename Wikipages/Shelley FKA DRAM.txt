'''Shelley Marshaun Massenburg-Smith''' (born August 3, 1988), known professionally as '''Shelley FKA DRAM''' (formerly '''DRAM''' {{IPAc-en|'|d|r|ɑː|m}}; an [[acronym]] for '''D'''oes '''R'''eal '''A'''ss '''M'''usic),{{efn|Formerly stylized as '''D.R.A.M.'''; previously '''DRAMA J'''}}<ref name="One Epic Summer" /><ref>{{cite web | url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/dram/962129771 | title=DRAM | publisher=[[iTunes Store|iTunes]] | access-date=November 10, 2017}}</ref> is an American rapper and singer from [[Hampton, Virginia]]. He is signed to [[Atlantic Records]] and [[Empire Distribution]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fashionably-early.com/2015/07/10/d-r-a-m-signs-to-atlantic-records/|title=D.R.A.M Signs to Atlantic Records – Fashionably-Early|date=July 10, 2015}}</ref> His debut [[Single (music)|single]] was "Cha Cha", followed by his breakout single "[[Broccoli (song)|Broccoli]]", featuring [[Lil Yachty]], which peaked at number five on the [[Billboard Hot 100|US ''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/18173-dram-broccoli-ft-lil-yachty/|title="Broccoli" [ft. Lil Yachty] by D.R.A.M. Review - and his album titled "Big Baby D.R.A.M." is set to be released on the 21st Of October 2016 Pitchfork|access-date=July 1, 2016}}</ref> In October 2016, he released his debut studio album, ''[[Big Baby DRAM]]''.

==Early life==
Shelley Marshaun Massenburg-Smith was born in [[Neustadt an der Weinstraße]], West Germany. Before the age of one, he moved to the United States and was raised in [[Hampton, Virginia]]. His mother was in the military.<ref name="One Epic Summer">Lawrence Burney, [http://noisey.vice.com/blog/dram-hampton-virginia-1epicsummer-interview-profile Meet D.R.A.M., the Virginia Artist Who Stopped Following the Rules of Rap and Had One Epic Summer] [[Vice Media]], October 8, 2014</ref> As a teenager, Massenburg-Smith was an ardent fan of the singer [[Bilal (American singer)|Bilal]] and recalls discovering his unreleased second album ''[[Love for Sale (Bilal album)|Love for Sale]]'' when it [[Internet leak|leaked]] online in 2006, later citing the song "White Turns to Grey" as his favorite "hidden gem" in music.<ref>{{cite web|author=Anon|date=April 8, 2016|url=https://www.standardhotels.com/culture/standard-sounds-hidden-gems-2|title=Standard Sounds Goes Searching for Hidden Gems|website=[[The Standard, High Line|The Standard Hotels]]|access-date=August 20, 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
In March 2015, DRAM released his debut EP, ''#1Epic'' through [[Atlantic Records]] and [[Empire Distribution]].<ref name="EpicEP" /> The EP included the single "Cha Cha", which peaked at number one on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs#Bubbling Under R&B/Hip-Hop Singles|Bubbling Under Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Singles]] chart.<ref name="Chacha">{{cite web | title= D.R.A.M.: Chart History for Cha Cha| url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?f[0]=ss_chart_search_title%3A%22Cha%20Cha%22&f[1]=itm_field_chart_id%3A345&refine=1|access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref> In October 2015, his debut mixtape, ''Gahdamn!'' was released.<ref name="Gahdamn" />

In April 2016, DRAM released his hit single "[[Broccoli (song)|Broccoli]]" featuring rapper [[Lil Yachty]]. The song gained international success and peaked at number five on the [[Billboard Hot 100|US ''Billboard'' Hot 100]], becoming his first top five song in the country. In October 2016, he appeared on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'', performing "Blessings (Reprise)" with [[Chance the Rapper]], [[Ty Dolla Sign]], [[Anthony Hamilton (musician)|Anthony Hamilton]] and [[Raury]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/tv/news/watch-chance-the-rappers-all-star-performance-on-fallon-w443246|title=Watch Chance the Rapper Send Epic 'Blessings (Reprise)' on 'Fallon'|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=October 4, 2016|access-date=October 4, 2016}}</ref> His debut studio album, ''[[Big Baby DRAM]]'', was released on October 21, 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dram-reveals-debut-album-big-baby-dram-release-date-and-cover-art-news.24565.html? |title=D.R.A.M. announces his first album |last=Lilah |first=Rose |date=October 5, 2016 |website=Hotnewhiphop |access-date=October 5, 2016}}</ref> The album received many positive reviews.

DRAM was featured on the single "[[Andromeda (Gorillaz song)|Andromeda]]" from British band [[Gorillaz]], for their fifth album, ''[[Humanz]]''. He also provided uncredited guest vocals on the track "[[We Got the Power (Gorillaz song)|We Got the Power]]" from the same album.

He is the voice of Wallace from ''[[Lazor Wulf]]''.
