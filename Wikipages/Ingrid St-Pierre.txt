'''Ingrid St-Pierre''' (born in 1985) is a French Canadian singer-songwriter from [[Quebec]].

== Biography ==
Ingrid St-Pierre was born and raised in [[Cabano]], a town within the [[Bas-Saint-Laurent]] region of [[Quebec]], [[Canada]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ingridstpierre.com/|title=Ingrid St-Pierre|website=Ingrid St-Pierre|language=fr-CA|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref> Her father is a businessman while her mother is a painter and an artist. Since a young age, Ingrid took piano and [[Gregorian chant|Gregorian]] classical singing classes.  At age 17, she left her hometown to go study in [[Quebec City]]. She later on settled in [[Trois-Rivières]] where she decided to study [[psychology]]. Ultimately, she left university to pursue a musical career.

== Musical career ==
She joined independent Canadian record label "[[La Tribu]]" in 2011 and still works with them to this day<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.latribu.ca/?lc=1&ai=171|title=La Compagnie Larivée Cabot Champagne {{!}} Les disques de La Tribu|website=www.latribu.ca|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref>

She released her first album ''Petite mam’zelle de chemin'' on May 24, 2011. The album sold relatively well in Quebec and it even won St-Pierre her first nomination for the Breakthrough Award at the [[ADISQ]], the main awards for music in Quebec. Ingrid was called ''Francophone discovery'' by the Public Francophone Radio Association.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ici.radio-canada.ca/emissions/les_lionnes/2011-2012/document.asp?idDoc=175000|title=Les invités du 21 septembre {{!}} Les lionnes {{!}} Radio-Canada.ca|last=Radio-Canada.ca|first=Télévision -|website=Les lionnes {{!}} Radio-Canada.ca|language=fr-ca|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref> On January 20, 2012, Ingrid released a [[music video]] for her song "Ficelles" on [[YouTube]], a video on her Grandmother and her struggle with Alzheimer's<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.alzheimer.ca/en/quebec/Get-involved/Spokesperson/Ingrid%20St-Pierre|title=Ingrid St-Pierre {{!}} Alzheimer Society of $name|website=www.alzheimer.ca|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref> which now has nearly three million views.<ref>{{Citation|last=Disques La Tribu|title=INGRID ST-PIERRE - Ficelles (Vidéoclip Officiel)|date=2012-01-20|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fnPlcr6zG3k|accessdate=2016-11-16}}</ref>  In 2012, she released her second album ''L’Escapade'', receiving three ADISQ nominations: album of the year, singer-songwriter of the year and the Contemporary adult award, an award she lost to [[Celine Dion]]. After a three-year hiatus due to personal reasons, during which St-Pierre suffered a miscarriage in 2014, she released her third album ''Tokyo'' in November 2015.  Ingrid with her partner and drummer, Liu-Kong Ha, had a baby boy on September 9, 2015 who they decided to name  Polo.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/IngridStPierreofficielle/photos/a.146901705369533.29644.116923645034006/974730132586682/?type=3&theater|title=On répète pour le lancement du  26... - Ingrid St-Pierre Page Officielle {{!}} Facebook|website=www.facebook.com|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref> Her son was conceived in Tokyo hence the name she gave to her new album. Later that year, she was nominated once again for the ADISQ Gala and finally won her first Felix for artist of the year.<ref name="ADISQ">{{Cite web |url=http://palmaresadisq.ca/fr/artiste/ingrid-st-pierre/ |title=Palmarès - Ingrid St-Pierre: |website=ADISQ}}</ref> Following her vocality on the subject, The Federation of Quebec Alzheimer's society named Ingrid St-Pierre their Spokesperson for the Alzheimer's movement.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.alzheimer.ca/en/quebec/Get-involved/Spokesperson/Ingrid%20St-Pierre|title=Ingrid St-Pierre {{!}} Alzheimer Society of $name|website=www.alzheimer.ca|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.lapresse.ca/le-nouvelliste/arts/201401/09/01-4726972-ingrid-st-pierre-porte-parole-pour-la-federation-des-societes-alzheimer.php|title=Ingrid St-Pierre porte-parole pour la Fédération des Sociétés Alzheimer {{!}} Marie-Josée Montminy {{!}} Arts & Spectacles|newspaper=La Presse|language=fr-CA|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref>

Ingrid is presently on tour for her third album ''Tokyo'' since January 2016. She also joined the Canadian celebrity dancing show ''Les Dieux de la danse''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://lesdieuxdeladanse.radio-canada.ca/couples/ingrid-st-pierre-et-joel-legendre|title=Ingrid St-Pierre et Joël Legendre - Les dieux de la danse|last=Radio-Canada.ca|first=Zone @@@4 - ICI|website=Radio-Canada|language=fr-ca|access-date=2016-11-16}}</ref> with her long-time friend, singer and television host [[Joël Legendre]].
