'''Hannes Wader''' (born '''Hans Eckard Wader''' on 23 June 1942) is a German singer-songwriter ("[[Liedermacher]]"). He has been an important figure in German leftist circles since the 1970s, with his songs covering such themes as socialist and communist resistance to oppression in Europe and other places like Latin America. He both wrote new songs and played versions of older historical works.

== Life and work ==
[[File:Com L24-0331-0006-0003.tif|thumb|Hannes Wader 1975 at the Lenzburg Folkfestival in Switzerland]]
Wader was born in Bethel, near [[Bielefeld]], [[Province of Westphalia|Westphalia]], Germany. His works are mostly based on German [[Folk song]]s. Aside from his own lyrics, he also performs works of famous poets like [[Eichendorff]]. He now rarely sings the workers' songs and socialist hymns that used to be a large part of his repertoire. In 1997 he published an album exclusively with songs by [[Franz Schubert]]. He also performed translated works from [[Carl Michael Bellman]] on the album ''Liebe, Schnaps & Tod''.

In the 1970s, Hannes Wader became one of the stars of the political left through his provocative songs. He was a member of the [[German Communist Party]] from 1977 to 1991. Wader even came under suspicions of [[terrorism]] because of his song ''Der Tankerkönig'', a spoken song about kidnapping a tycoon.

In 1973, he moved to [[Struckum]], in [[Nordfriesland]], where he published some of his later albums. In 1998, he and his family moved to the [[Steinburg]] district of [[Schleswig-Holstein]]. He now lives in [[Kassel]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hna.de/kultur/hannes-wader-singen-lebensrettend-mich-4710019.html |title="Singen war lebensrettend für mich" – Interview mit Hannes Wader |trans-title=Interview with Hannes Wader |work=HNA |language=German |first=Matthias |last=Lohr |date=6 February 2015 |accessdate=5 December 2017}}</ref>

He has published numerous albums and appeared in [[open-air concert]]s and clubs until 2017. His last concert was on 30 November 2017, in [[Berlin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tagesspiegel.de/kultur/hannes-wader-live-in-berlin-morgen-dort/20660388.html |title=Morgen dort – Hannes Wader verabschiedet sich von der Bühne |trans-title=Hannes Wader's final concert |work=Tagesspiegel |language=German |first=Johannes |last=Schneider |date=1 December 2017 |accessdate=5 December 2017}}</ref>
