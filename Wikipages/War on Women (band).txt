'''War On Women''' is a [[Mixed-sex education|co-ed]], [[Feminism|feminist]], [[hardcore punk]] band from [[Baltimore]], [[Maryland]], formed in 2010. Themes in their music have included [[Abortion-rights movements|abortion rights]], access to [[Birth control|contraception]], [[rape]] and online [[misogyny]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newsweek.com/war-women-hardcore-punk-feminism-511714|title=War on Women fuses feminism and hardcore punk|first=Lucy Westcott On 10/20/16 at 1:21 PM|last=EDT|date=October 20, 2016|website=Newsweek}}</ref>

== History ==
In 2003, Brooks Harlan recruited Shawna Potter to sing and play guitar for AVEC, an indie-rock band project that was based in Baltimore.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/war-women-pissed-feminist-punk-band-aims-lasting-impact|title=War On Women: Pissed-Off Feminist Punk Band Aims for "Lasting Impact"|date=June 4, 2018|website=Revolver}}</ref> Seeing [[Courtney Love]] playing guitar in "[[Doll Parts]]" inspired Potter to play music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/09/01/arts/music/rock-bands-women.html|title=Rock's Not Dead, It's Ruled by Women: The Round-Table Conversation|first=Joe|last=Coscarelli|date=September 1, 2017|via=NYTimes.com}}</ref>

In 2010 Potter and Harlan formed War On Women, a band “inspired by the [[riot grrrl]] bands [Potter] adored as a teenager.”<ref name="auto"/>

War On Women released their debut album, a six-song EP titled ''Improvised Weapons,'' in 2012 on Exotic Fever Records.<ref name="WOWBandcampImprovisedWeapons">{{Cite web|url=https://waronwomen.bandcamp.com/album/improvised-weapons|title=Improvised Weapons, by War On Women|website=War On Women}}</ref>

In 2015 the band released their first full-length album, the self-titled ''War On Women,'' on [[Bridge 9 Records|Bridge Nine Records]]. The album was recorded, mixed and produced in collaboration with [[J. Robbins]] of the band [[Jawbox]].<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.bridge9.com/waronwomen|title=War On Women|website=Bridge Nine Records}}</ref>

Their second and third full-length albums were also released under by Bridge Nine Records. The second album, ''Capture the Flag,'' was again recorded with J. Robbins and released in 2018.<ref name="auto1"/> The third album, ''Wonderful Hell'', was released in 2020.<ref name="auto1"/>

In August 2021 Robbins’ band Jawbox announced that Harlan had joined Jawbox, replacing guitarist Bill Barbot. <ref name="JawboxFacebookHarlanAnnouncement">{{Cite web|url= https://www.facebook.com/100044335843914/posts/374205994067259/?d=n |title=Jawbox Facebook Harlan Announcement|date=August 10, 2021 |website=Facebook}}</ref>
