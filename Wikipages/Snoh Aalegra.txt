'''Snoh Sheri Nowrozi'''<ref>{{cite web |title=Nowrozi Snoh Sheri {{!}} ACE Repertory |url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#/ace/writer/292660057/NOWROZI%20SNOH%20SHERI |website=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP]] |access-date=9 March 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Joshi |first1=Tara |title=One to watch: Snoh Aalegra |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/aug/17/snoh-aalegra-swedish-iranian-r-and-b-prince-drake-common-rza-ugh-those-feels-again |work=The Guardian |date=August 17, 2019}}</ref> (born '''Shahrzad Fooladi''',<ref name="teen-vogue"/> 13 September 1987), better known by her [[stage name]], '''Snoh Aalegra''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|s|n|oʊ|ˈ|æ|l|i|ɡ|r|ɑː}}), is a Swedish<!--DO NOT add Iranian, ethnicity does not belong in the lead section per [[MOS:BLPLEAD]]--> singer and songwriter based in Los Angeles. Her debut album ''Feels'' was released in 2017, followed by ''[[Ugh, Those Feels Again]]'' in 2019.

==Life and career==
===1987–2008: Early life and career beginnings===
Aalegra was born on 13 September 1987 in [[Uppsala]], Sweden to [[Persian people|Persian]] parents.<ref name="teen-vogue">{{cite web |last1=Kwateng-Clar |first1=Danielle |title=Snoh Aalegra and Michael B. Jordan's Steamy "Whoa" Video Sparks Dating Rumors |url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/snoh-aalegra-whoa-video |website=[[Teen Vogue]] |access-date=15 December 2019 |language=en |date=14 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="gal-dem">{{cite web |last1=Ijaduola |first1=Deborah |title=Snoh Aalegra: on connecting the dots of her Swedish-Persian heritage |url=http://gal-dem.com/snoh-aalegra-swedish-persian-heritage |website=[[gal-dem]] |access-date=17 March 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191208011736/http://gal-dem.com/snoh-aalegra-swedish-persian-heritage |archive-date=8 December 2019 |date=17 November 2017}}</ref> She grew up in [[Enköping]], [[Sweden]],<ref name="finest.se">{{cite web |last1=Erwik |first1=Alexander |title=Sheri - nästa års stora svenska stjärna - Finest.se |url=http://www.finest.se/articles/article.php?aid=2306/ |website=Finest.se |access-date=11 October 2018 |date=10 December 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120228222346/http://www.finest.se/articles/article.php?aid=2306/ |archive-date=28 February 2012 |language=sv}}</ref> moving there with her mother following her parents' divorce.<ref name="okayplayer">{{cite web |title=First Look Friday: Snoh Aalegra's Fresh Take On R&B |url=http://www.okayplayer.com/news/new-artist-snoh-aalegra-photos-interview.html |website=Okayplayer |access-date=15 October 2018 |date=16 January 2015}}</ref><ref name="pb1">{{cite news |last1=Domanick |first1=Andrea |title=Snoh Aalegra and the Beauty of Otherness |url=https://www.playboy.com/read/get-lost-in-snoh-aalegra |work=Playboy |date=September 24, 2019}}</ref> They later moved to [[Stockholm]].<ref name="wwd1">{{cite news |last1=Wally |first1=Maxine |title=Snoh Aalegra Finds Home on Her New Album, 'Ugh, Those Feels Again' |url=https://wwd.com/eye/people/snoh-aalegra-finds-home-on-her-new-album-ugh-those-feels-again-1203239656/ |work=WWD |date=August 16, 2019}}</ref> She began writing music at the age of 9.<ref name="tidal1">{{cite news |last1=Ju |first1=Shirley |title=Snoh Aalegra on Prince's Lessons - And Her New Album |url=http://read.tidal.com/article/snoh-aalegra-ugh-feels |work=Tidal |date=August 16, 2019}}</ref>

In 2001, at the age of 13, Aalegra signed an [[artist development deal]] with [[Sony Music|Sony Music Sweden]].<ref name="pb1"/> She would ultimately leave Sony Sweden with no music being released.<ref name="highsnobiety">{{cite web |last1=Gore |first1=Sydney |title=Snoh Aalegra on Writing Heavy Songs That Resist The Mainstream |url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/snoh-aalegra-interview-2 |website=[[Highsnobiety]] |access-date=1 August 2019 |date=21 November 2018}}</ref><ref name="interviewmagazine">{{cite web |last1=Brara |first1=Noor |title=Discovery: Snoh Aalegra - Interview Magazine |url=https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/discover-snoh-aalegra |website=Interview Magazine |access-date=12 October 2018 |date=24 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="billboard">{{cite web |last1=Shouneyia |first1=Alexa |title=Snoh Aalegra Talks New Album 'Feels,' Working With No I.D. & the Best Advice She Received From Prince |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8014782/snoh-aalegra-interview-new-album-feels-prince-no-id |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=7 October 2018 |date=26 October 2017}}</ref>

===2009–2013: Career beginnings as Sheri, ''First Sign''===
[[File:Sheri 2009.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Aalegra at [[Stockholm Pride]] in 2009]]
In 2009, Aalegra began her musical career using the mononym Sheri.<ref name="finest.se"/> She released her debut single "Hit and Run" on 16 February 2009, with production by [[Andreas Carlsson]]. The single peaked at number 12 on the [[Sverigetopplistan|Swedish Single Charts]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hung |first1=Steffen |title=swedishcharts.com - Sheri - Hit And Run |url=https://swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Sheri&titel=Hit+And+Run&cat=s |website=swedishcharts.com |access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref> She released her second single "U Got Me Good" on 4 December 2009, reaching number 2 on the Swedish Single Charts.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hung |first1=Steffen |title=swedishcharts.com - Sheri - U Got Me Good |url=https://swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Sheri&titel=U+Got+Me+Good&cat=s |website=swedishcharts.com |access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref>

On 6 April 2010, under the name Sheri, she released the studio album ''First Sign'', on Universal Music Sweden. It features a cover of the 1984 song "[[Smooth Operator]]" by [[Sade (band)|Sade]], and the singles "Hit And Run" and "U Got Me Good".<ref name="first-sign"/>

She signed with [[No I.D.]]'s [[ARTium Recordings]] in 2013.<ref name="interviewmagazine"/>

===2014–2018: Debut as Snoh Aalegra, ''Feels''===
In July 2014, she debuted her new name, Snoh Aalegra, and appeared on [[Common (rapper)|Common]]'s tenth studio album, ''[[Nobody's Smiling]]'', featuring on the song "Hustle Harder".<ref>{{cite web |title=Nobody's Smiling by Common |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/nobodys-smiling/891968114 |access-date=1 January 2019 |language=en-us}}</ref><ref name="variety1">{{cite news |last1=Aswad |first1=Jem |title=Album Review: Snoh Aalegra's 'Ugh, Those Feels Again' |url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/album-review-snoh-aalegras-ugh-those-feels-again-1203304738/ |work=Variety |date=August 16, 2019}}</ref> She released her debut single, "Bad Things" featuring Common, in October 2014, and  her debut EP, ''There Will Be Sunshine'', on 17 November 2014 through ARTium/[[Epic Records|Epic]].<ref name="interviewmagazine"/> The EP includes "Stockholm, Pt. II (Outro)" featuring [[Cocaine 80s]].<ref>{{cite web |title=There Will Be Sunshine - EP by Snoh Aalegra |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/there-will-be-sunshine-ep/938986790 |access-date=7 October 2018 |date=17 November 2014}}</ref>

In 2014, she became a protégé of [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] after he discovered her music and reached out to her.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://eventseeker.com/artist/55036-snoh-aalegra|title=Snoh Aalegra - Artist Profile|website=eventseeker.com|language=en|access-date=2020-03-24}}</ref> He mentored her until his death in 2016.<ref name="pb1"/><ref name="hb1">{{cite news |last1=Gonzalez |first1=Erica |title=Snoh Aalegra Talks Getting Donuts with Prince and a Possible Frank Ocean Collaboration |url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/art-books-music/a29134062/snoh-aalegra-interview/ |work=Harpers Bazaar |date=September 20, 2019}}</ref>

In February 2015, Aalegra said she had changed her legal name to Snoh Nowrozi.<ref name="teenvogue">{{cite web |last1=Weston |first1=Liana |title=Sound Scout: Meet Snoh Aalegra, the Enchanting Soulstress With a Hypnotic Voice |url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/snoh-aalegra-interview |website=[[Teen Vogue]] |access-date=7 October 2018 |date=7 February 2015 |language=en}}</ref> She added "Aalegra" to her stage name because it means "joyful" in Italian (changing the spelling of "allegra" to make it her own), and because there was already an artist named Snoh.<ref name="sm1">{{cite news |last1=Haque |first1=Eshika |title=Interview: Snoh Aalegra |url=https://schonmagazine.com/SnohAalegra/ |work=Schon |date=February 13, 2015}}</ref><ref name="goat1">{{cite news |last1=Abraham |first1=Tameka |title=Snoh Aalegra |url=https://www.goat.com/greatest/snoh-aalegra |access-date=3 January 2020 |work=GOAT}}</ref> On 11 February 2015, Aalegra released her first single as Snoh Aalegra, "Emotional", produced by [[RZA]].<ref name="sm1"/> She also appeared on [[Vince Staples]]' debut album, ''[[Summertime '06]]'' in June 2015, providing vocals for the song, "Jump Off the Roof".<ref name="cos1">{{cite news |last1=Kivel |first1=Adam |title=Vince Staples – Summertime '06 |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2015/07/album-review-vince-staples-summertime-06/ |work=Consequence of Sound |date=June 30, 2015}}</ref>

Aalegra released the EP ''Don't Explain'' on 8 April 2016 through ARTium Recordings, with features and production from [[James Fauntleroy]], [[No I.D.]], [[Boi-1da]], [[Christian Rich]] and [[DJ Dahi]].<ref name="wwd1"/><ref name="tidal1"/> The EP features a cover of the 1944 song, "[[Don't Explain (song)#Notable cover versions|Don't Explain]]" by [[Billie Holiday]] and [[Arthur Herzog Jr.]]<ref name="guardian1">{{cite news |last1=Adewunmi |first1=Bim |title=Why I love... Snoh Aalegra |url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2016/aug/13/why-i-love-snoh-aalegra-band-bim-adewunmi |work=The Guardian |date=August 13, 2016}}</ref> [[John Mayer]] plays guitar on "Under the Influence",<ref name="highsnobiety"/> and the final track, "Chaos", was written by [[Sia (musician)|Sia]].<ref name="teenvogue"/> In 2017, she went on her first North American tour, in support of [[Daniel Caesar]].<ref name="billboard"/>

On 20 October 2017, Aalegra released her debut album, ''Feels'', with features from [[Vince Staples]], [[Vic Mensa]], [[Logic (rapper)|Logic]], and [[Timbuktu (musician)|Timbuktu]].<ref name="billboard"/> The album is a "nostalgic blend of soul and R&B" which she calls "cinematic soul".    Her single "Time" was sampled by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] on "Do Not Disturb", the closing track on his 2017 mixtape ''[[More Life]]''.<ref name="billboard"/><ref name="hb1"/> On September 12, 2018, her single "Nothing Burns Like the Cold" produced by [[Christian Rich]] and featuring Vince Staples, was used by [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] for their [[iPhone XS]] announcement video and commercials.<ref>{{cite web |title=iPhone XS — Illusion — Apple |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l96MQKwwHOE |website=[[YouTube]] |access-date=7 October 2018 |date=15 September 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Apple iPhone XS TV Commercial, 'Illusion' Song by Snoh Aalegra, Vince Staples |url=https://www.ispot.tv/ad/djH1/apple-iphone-xs-illusion-song-by-snoh-aalegra |website=iSpot.tv |access-date=7 October 2018 |language=en}}</ref>   In the fall of 2018, Aalegra headlined her first North American tour with support from [[Mereba]] and Leven Kali.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lukarcanin |first1=Emina |title=Leven Kali Releases Euphoric Two-Song Project 'NunWrong With a Lil Good Lovin': Listen |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8478514/leven-kali-nunwrong-with-a-lil-good-lovin |work=Billboard |date=October 5, 2018}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Ugh, Those Feels Again'' and ''Temporary Highs in the Violet Skies''===

In August 2019, Aalegra released her second album, ''[[Ugh, Those Feels Again]]'', on ARTium Recordings.<ref name=Guardian-Ugh-Review-2019>{{cite news|last1=Joshi|first1=Tara|title=One to watch: Snoh Aalegra|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/aug/17/snoh-aalegra-swedish-iranian-r-and-b-prince-drake-common-rza-ugh-those-feels-again|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=17 August 2019}}</ref> The album is considered to be a sequel to ''Feels'', similar in mood and thematically.<ref name="variety1"/><ref name="pf1">{{cite news |last1=Kearse |first1=Stephen |title=Ugh, those feels again |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/snoh-aalegra-ugh-those-feels-again/ |work=Pitchfork |date=September 6, 2019}}</ref> The album peaked at number 3 on the ''Billboard'' R&B Album Sales chart, number 6 on the ''Billboard'' [[Top R&B Albums]] chart,<ref name="bbrap">{{cite web |title=Snoh Aalegra Chart History: Top R&B Albums |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/snoh-aalegra/chart-history/r-and-b-albums |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=28 August 2019}}</ref> and number 73 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref name="bb200"/> The single "[[I Want You Around]]" reached number 1 on the ''Billboard'' [[Adult R&B Songs]] chart.<ref name="bbrba"/> She recorded the song "Wolves Are Out Tonight", produced by [[Swizz Beatz]], for the soundtrack to the 2019 [[EPIX]] television series ''[[Godfather of Harlem]]''.<ref>{{cite news |title=Snoh Aalegra Graces New "Godfather Of Harlem" Track "Wolves Are Out Tonight" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/snoh-aalegra-graces-new-godfather-of-harlem-track-wolves-are-out-tonight-new-song.1985149.html |work=Hot New Hip Hop |date=November 9, 2019}}</ref> She released the video to her single "Whoa", starring herself and actor [[Michael B. Jordan]], on December 13, 2019.<ref name="teen-vogue"/> In the fall of 2019, she headlined a European and North American tour, with support from [[Baby Rose]] and [[Giveon]],<ref name="defpen1">{{cite news |last1=Inman |first1=DeMicia |title=Snoh Aalegra Announces North American Tour With Baby Rose |url=https://defpen.com/snoh-aalegra-north-american-tour/ |work=Def Pen |date=September 24, 2019}}</ref> and reached number 1 on the Bandsintown + Billboard Global Rising Artists Index.<ref>{{cite news |title=Snoh Aalegra: Bandsintown |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/snoh-aalegra/chart-history/BRG/song/1100671 |access-date=3 January 2020 |work=Billboard}}</ref>

In February 2020, [[NPR]]'s [[Tiny Desk Concerts]] released Aalegra's performance where she sang "Love Like That", "I Want You Around", "Whoa", "Fool For You", and "Find Someone Like You".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Snoh Aalegra: Tiny Desk Concert|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/02/18/805478361/snoh-aalegra-tiny-desk-concert|access-date=2020-11-19|website=NPR.org|language=en}}</ref>

On 9 March 2020, it was announced that Aalegra had signed a recording contract with [[Roc Nation]]/[[Universal Music Group]] with a partnership with her current label ARTium Records, and released "Dying 4 Your Love", her first single, through both labels on July 10. She announced that she was working on her next album in an interview.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Moorwood |first1=Victoria |title=Snoh Aalegra signs with JAY-Z’s Roc Nation |url=https://www.revolt.tv/news/2020/3/10/21173073/snoh-aalegra-signs-roc-nation-tour |website=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]] |access-date=15 January 2021 |language=en |date=10 March 2020}}</ref>

Aalegra was featured on "Last Time", on [[Giveon]]'s second EP, ''[[When It's All Said and Done]]''.

On 9 July 2021, Aalegra released her third album, ''Temporary Highs in the Violet Skies'', on ARTium Records and [[Roc Nation]]. The album was preceded by singles "Dying 4 Your Love", released on 10 June 2020, and "Lost You", released 21 June 2021, which was produced by Maneesh and No I.D.

==Artistry==
Aalegra cites [[James Brown]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Whitney Houston]], [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[Brandy Norwood|Brandy]], [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Missy Elliott]], [[Robyn]], and [[Mariah Carey]] as musical influences.<ref name="wonderland">{{cite web |last1=Walker |first1=Lily |title=New Noise: Snoh {{!}} Wonderland Magazine |url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2014/12/09/new-noise-snoh-2/ |website=Wonderland |access-date=7 October 2018 |date=9 December 2014}}</ref><ref name="teenvogue" /><ref name=Guardian-Ugh-Review-2019/><ref>{{cite news |last1=Penrose |first1=Nerisha |title=Snoh Aalegra Is The Soundtrack Of Sad Girl Fall |url=https://www.elle.com/culture/music/a29107966/snoh-aalegra-ugh-those-feels-again-album-interview/ |work=Elle |date=September 24, 2019}}</ref><ref name="paper1">{{cite news |title=Snoh Aalegra Is Right at Home |url=https://www.papermag.com/snoh-aalegra-2640037511.html |work=Paper |date=August 26, 2019}}</ref><ref name="w1">{{cite news |last1=Marine |first1=Brooke |title=New Faces: Snoh Aalegra Delves Deep with her Sophomore Album, -Ugh, those feels again |url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/snoh-aalegra-ugh-those-feels-again |work=W Magazine |date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> She also credits film score orchestras and arrangements with influencing her sound.<ref name="w1"/> She has said she first fell in love with music when she was 7 and heard Whitney Houston's ''[[The Bodyguard (soundtrack)|The Bodyguard]]'' soundtrack.<ref name="pb1"/><ref name="paper1"/>

==Personal life==
After briefly living in [[London]], Aalegra has lived in [[Los Angeles]] since 2012.<ref name="teenvogue" /> She speaks [[Persian language|Persian]], [[Swedish language|Swedish]] and [[English language|English]].<ref name="gal-dem" />
