'''Francisco Javier Bautista Jr.''' (born December 7, 1975),<ref>{{cite web |url=http://twitpic.com/1uvv69 |title=Share photos and videos on Twitter |publisher=Twitpic |access-date=August 10, 2013}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Frankie J''', is a [[Mexican-American]] singer, songwriter, and former member of the musical group [[Kumbia Kings]].

Born in Mexico, he grew up in San Diego and became a freestyle artist under the stage name Frankie Boy in the late 1990s. He then joined Kumbia Kings before re-embarking on a solo career. Frankie J's solo debut album, ''[[What's a Man to Do]]'', was released in 2003, followed by more English and Spanish language albums. He received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination for his album ''[[Faith, Hope y Amor]]'' in 2013 for Best Latin Pop Album.

==Early life==
Bautista was born in [[Tijuana]], [[Mexico]] and raised in [[San Diego, California]] from the age of two, after his uncle brought him and his siblings to the United States.<ref name="rolling stone">{{cite web |last=Devenish |first=Colin |title=Frankie J. Back At School |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/artists/frankiej/articles/story/5935487/frankie_j_back_at_school |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=May 29, 2003 |access-date=February 1, 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081204070129/http://www.rollingstone.com/artists/frankiej/articles/story/5935487/frankie_j_back_at_school |archive-date=December 4, 2008}}</ref> He grew up listening to both traditional Latin music as well as American [[urban music]].<ref name="Allmusic">{{cite web |last=Kellman |first=Andy |title=Frankie J – Biography |url={{AllMusic |class=artist |id=p89577/biography |pure_url=yes}}|year=2006|access-date=February 10, 2008}}</ref> He attended [[Southwest Senior High School]] in San Diego.<ref name="rolling stone"/> He later began to develop more interest in singing, with the encouragement of his family. At high school age, he began participating in talent shows and performing at school functions.<ref name="musician guide">{{cite web |last=Winters |first=Kelly |title=Frankie J. Biography |url=http://www.musicianguide.com/biographies/1608004783/Frankie-J.html |publisher=MusicianGuide.com |year=2006 |access-date=February 1, 2008}}</ref> He graduated high school in 1995.<ref name="rolling stone"/>

==Career==
Bautista's first taste of the music business was in 1993, when he was briefly signed to Canadian independent record label Ventura International Records, featuring on a song called "Margarita" by Turkish artist Atilla. In 1997, Bautista signed with the now-defunct Hola Recordings which at the time had only one artist signed to the label, the brainchild of dance music legend and producer [[Jellybean Benitez]]. At the time, he was a [[Freestyle music|freestyle]] artist known as Frankie Boy and his music was released on three ''Maximum Freestyle'' compilations but his solo CD was shelved before release.<ref name="Allmusic"/>

===Kumbia Kings (1999–2003)===
In 1999 he became a member of the [[Kumbia Kings]] created by [[A.B. Quintanilla]], brother of [[Selena]]. He performed under Cisko.

===Solo career: ''What's a Man to Do''===
Frankie J signed as a solo artist with [[Columbia Records]] and released his first album ''[[What's A Man To Do]]'' on May 27, 2003. His self-titled first Spanish-language album ''[[Frankie J (album)|Frankie J]]'' was also released later in the same year.<ref name="frankie j spanish">{{cite web |last=Kellman |first=Andy |title="Frankie J" – Overview |url={{AllMusic |class=album |id=r647895 |pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|year=2003|access-date=February 1, 2008}}</ref> The album's lead single "[[Don't Wanna Try]]" peaked at #19 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] Singles Chart, while "Ya No Es Igual" went Top 10 on the World Latin charts.<ref name="billboard singles"/> Later that year, he was featured in the Top 10 Billboard Hot 100 hit "[[Suga Suga]]" by rapper [[Baby Bash]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Sendra |first=Tim |title="Now, Vol. 14" – Overview |url={{AllMusic |class=album |id=r669696 |pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|year=2003|access-date=February 1, 2008}}</ref>

===''The One'' and breakthrough===
His second album ''[[The One (Frankie J album)|The One]]'' was released on March 22, 2005.<ref>{{cite web |last=Kellman |first=Andy |title="The One" – Overview |url={{AllMusic |class=album |id=r732331 |pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|year=2005|access-date=February 1, 2008}}</ref> It was led by the #3 ''Billboard'' Hot 100 hit "[[Obsession (No Es Amor)]]" featuring rapper [[Baby Bash]], and followed by the Top40 singles "How To Deal" and "[[More Than Words]]". To coincide with the release of "[[More Than Words]]", the album was re-released on the [[DualDisc]] format in October.<ref>{{cite web |last=Kellman |first=Andy |title="The One [DualDisc Bonus Track]" – Overview |url={{AllMusic |class=album |id=r807345 |pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|year=2005|access-date=February 1, 2008}}</ref> By January 2006, ''[[The One (Frankie J album)|The One]]'' was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] for shipping over a million copies.<ref>{{cite web |title=RIAA searchable database |url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?table=SEARCH |publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]] |access-date=September 11, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070626050454/http://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?table=SEARCH |archive-date=June 26, 2007 |df=mdy}}</ref> His second Spanish-language album ''[[Un Nuevo Dia]]'' was released on June 13, 2006, and featured the single "Pensado En Ti".<ref name="billboard singles"/>

Frankie's third English-language album, ''[[Priceless (Frankie J album)|Priceless]]'', was released on October 17, 2006. It debuted at #30 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="billboard albums">{{cite web |title=Frankie J – Billboard Albums |url={{AllMusic |class=artist |id=p89577/charts-awards/billboard-albums |pure_url=yes}}|year=2006|access-date=February 1, 2008}}</ref> The lead single "[[That Girl (Frankie J song)|That Girl]]" featured [[Chamillionaire]] and [[Mannie Fresh]] and peaked at number 43 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. A second single, "Daddy's Little Girl", charted at number 23 on the ''Billboard'' Bubbling Under Hot 100 chart.

===Independent route===
Following his departure from Columbia Records, Frankie J released a few singles ("Pictures", "If You Were My Girlfriend", "Crush") between 2008 and 2011, as well as starting his own label, Soulsick Records.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.emusic.com/label/Soulsick-Records-TuneCore-MP3-Download/746414.html |title=Browse Albums &#124; Discover & Download Music |publisher=eMusic |access-date=August 10, 2013}}</ref> He has also written songs for other artists, most recently the number 2 hit song "[[Take a Chance on Me (JLS song)|Take a Chance on Me]]" in the [[United Kingdom]] for boyband [[JLS]].

He released his fourth English-language studio album ''Courage'' on December 7, 2011. The lead single "How Beautiful You Are" was released to digital retailers on October 24, 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.amazon.com/How-Beautiful-You-Are-Single/dp/B00605YMG2/ref=sr_shvl_album_3?ie=UTF8&qid=1321860910&sr=301-3 |title=How Beautiful You Are – Single: Frankie J.: MP3 Downloads |access-date=August 10, 2013}}</ref> The music video was directed by Mike Ho and premiered on YouTube on September 11, 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YB2-Ta3hknQ |title=Frankie J (How Beautiful You Are) Official Music Video |publisher=YouTube |date=September 11, 2011 |access-date=August 10, 2013}}</ref> The video for the album's second single "And I Had You There" premiered on Frankie's YouTube channel on November 26, 2011. Frankie J and [[Baby Bash]] shot a video for the album's third single "That's Wussup" in December 2011 in San Diego, California. The video premiered on January 8, 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A2R0h4fEeU8 |title=Frankie J feat Baby Bash That's Wussup |publisher=YouTube |date=January 8, 2012 |access-date=August 10, 2013}}</ref>

On July 12, 2012, it was announced that Frankie J will join [[Jennifer Lopez]] and [[Enrique Iglesias]] on their joint North America tour, serving as a special guest, replacing [[Wisin & Yandel]] who were initially the opening act.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/industry/touring/frankie-j-joins-jennifer-lopez-enrique-iglesias-1007563152.story |title=Frankie J. Joins Jennifer Lopez, Enrique Iglesias Summer Tour After Wisin & Yandel Drop Out &#124; Billboard |publisher=Billboard.biz |date=July 12, 2012 |access-date=August 10, 2013}}</ref>

===''Faith, Hope y Amor'' and Grammy success===
In 2011 he signed with Universal Music and began work on a new Spanish-language album. He released two digital singles ("Ay, Ay, Ay" and "Tienes Que Creer En Mi"/"Take a Chance on Me") in late 2012.

In 2013, he released his new album, ''Faith, Hope y Amor'' on May 28, 2013. The album was nominated for the 56th annual [[Grammy Award]] under the category of Best Latin Pop Album. It was preceded by the release of the singles "No Te Quiero Ver Con Él" and "Beautiful" (featuring Pitbull). "Impossible" was also released as a single.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://twitter.com/therealfrankieJ/status/340250223165181952 |title=Twitter / therealfrankieJ: #impossible RT @Express1self |publisher=Twitter.com |access-date=August 10, 2013}}</ref>

On November 26, 2015, he premiered the single "Breakin'",<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tkYC7uwgdBs |title=Frankie J "Breakin" |last=therealfrankiej1 |date=November 26, 2015 |via=YouTube}}</ref> with a music video (in English and Spanglish) premiering on December 14.<ref name="youtube.com">{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3htdluiZCYc |title=Frankie J "Breakin" (English) |last=therealfrankiej1 |date=December 14, 2015 |via=YouTube}}</ref> The digital single was released on December 25, 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/breakin-single/id1064239550 |title=Breakin' - Single by Frankie J on Apple Music |date=December 25, 2015 }}</ref> Four additional singles — "Let the Music Take Control", "I Promise You", "Riches in Heaven", and "Body Rock" — followed in 2016. He announced his next album would be titled ''More Than the Music''.<ref name="youtube.com"/> Another single "Not So Dangerous" was released on December 25, 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01NCJPO4C/ |title=Not so Dangerous |via=Amazon}}</ref>

In March 2017, he scored a radio hit with "Si Una Vez (If I Once)" which was a collaboration with Play-N-Skillz, Wisin, and Leslie Grace.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M4hDvM3gZHA |title=Play-N-Skillz - Si Una Vez (If I Once)[Official Video] ft. Wisin, Frankie J, Leslie Grace |last=PlayNSkillzVEVO |date=January 20, 2017 |via=YouTube}}</ref> In June, he collaborated with [[Baby Bash]] for a new single titled "Vamonos."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.amazon.com/Vamonos-Explicit/dp/B072M6HRW7/ |title=Vamonos |via=Amazon}}</ref> The following month in July, he released a new single "Lowrider" with Baby Bash, C Kan, Ozomatli & Kid Frost.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.amazon.com/Lowrider-feat-Baby-Ozomatli-Frost/dp/B073TF4JNZ/ |title=Lowrider |via=Amazon}}</ref>

On July 27, he released his new studio album titled ''Eleven'' through his own label Soulsick Musick Inc.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.amazon.com/Eleven-Explicit-Frankie-J/dp/B073W75MQ7/ |title=Eleven |via=Amazon}}</ref>

===Acting===
Frankie appeared as a celebrity guest at the 16th birthday party of [[Marissa Mishelle]] on MTV's ''[[My Super Sweet 16]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.com/ontv/dyn/sweet_16/episode/episode.jhtml?episodeId=97598 |title=MTV Original TV Shows, Reality TV Shows - MTV |website=MTV}}</ref>

In 2011, he joined the [[mun2]] television series ''RPM Miami'' as mechanic Ramon.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2011/04/rpm-miami-the-first-biligual-one-hour-drama-premieres-in-may.html |title='RPM Miami': The first bilingual one-hour drama premieres in May – Zap2it |publisher=Blog.zap2it.com |date=April 15, 2011 |access-date=August 10, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131224114921/http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2011/04/rpm-miami-the-first-biligual-one-hour-drama-premieres-in-may.html |archive-date=December 24, 2013 |df=mdy-all}}</ref> The series was quickly renewed for a second season.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.variety.com/article/VR1118037232 |work=Variety |first=Sam |last=Thielman |title=Telemundo, Mun2 amp up series |date=May 17, 2011}}</ref>
