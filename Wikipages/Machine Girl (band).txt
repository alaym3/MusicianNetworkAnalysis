'''Machine Girl''' (sometimes stylized as ''machin3gir1'') is an American [[electronic music]] project created in 2013 by Matt Stephenson in [[Long Island]], [[New York (state)|New York]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Simpson|first=Paul|date=|title=Machine Girl|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/machine-girl-mn0002143234/biography|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 November 2020|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Adame|first=Oscar|date=13 January 2020|title=Machine Girl, the future of digital hardcore|url=https://warp.la/editoriales/machine-girl-bandsyouneedtoknow|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 November 2020|website=Warp}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Smith|first=Nathan|date=14 September 2020|title=A Guide to the Relentless Hardcore of Machine Girl|url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/lists/machine-girl-guide|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 November 2020|website=[[Bandcamp]]}}</ref> In 2015, the project became a duo, with Stephenson recruiting percussionist Sean Kelly to play live drums.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pettis|first=Josh|date=29 May 2019|title=Lobsterfest 2019 Q&A: Machine Girl|url=https://acrn.com/2019/05/29/lobsterfest-2019-qa-machine-girl/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 November 2020|website=ACRN.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Machine Girl|url=https://www.discogs.com/es/artist/3707279-Machine-Girl-2|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=10 November 2020|website=[[Discogs]]}}</ref>

== Style and themes ==
In an interview with [[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]] magazine, Stephenson defined Machine Girl as "fucked-up electronic [[Punk rock|punk]]" and stated that they didn't like the "[[Industrial music|industrial]]" tag for being "very [[Goth subculture|goth]], and very black and white" considering the project "a lot more colorful."<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Enis|first=Eli|date=11 May 2020|title=Machine Girl: "Alien music" duo on spitting blood, mashing genres, transcending self|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/machine-girl-alien-music-duo-spitting-blood-mashing-genres-transcending-self|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 November 2020|website=Revolver}}</ref> [[Kerrang!]] listed them as one of the "bands expanding the definition of [[Hardcore (electronic dance music genre)|hardcore]]", and described the project as "a particularly punky and ferocious breed of the electronic sub-genre [[breakcore]] that could easily pass for hardcore when they rip it live."<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Enis|first=Eli|date=24 May 2019|title=5 bands expanding the definition of hardcore|url=https://www.kerrang.com/features/5-bands-expanding-the-definition-of-hardcore/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 November 2020|website=[[Kerrang!]]}}</ref> [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] defined their style as "relentlessly smashing together bits of punk, [[grindcore]], [[rave]], industrial, and more" and "unpredictable and dangerous, full of animalistic rage and uncontrollable energy".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Joyce|first=Colin|date=10 March 2020|title=Machine Girl: U-Void Synthesizer|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/machine-girl-u-void-synthesizer/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 November 2020|website=Pitchfork}}</ref>  

The project was named after the 2008 Japanese film ''[[The Machine Girl]].''<ref name=":1" />
