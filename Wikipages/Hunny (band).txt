'''Hunny''' (stylized as '''HUNNY''') is an American [[rock music|rock]] band from [[Newbury Park, California]], formed in 2014. The band consists of Jason Yarger, Jake Goldstein, Kevin Grimmett, and Joey Anderson.<ref name="allmusic.com">{{cite web|last1=Deming |first1=Mark |title=Artist Biography by Mark Deming |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/hunny-mn0003379675/biography |accessdate=18 December 2019}}</ref>

==History==
Lead singer and guitarist Jason Yarger and bassist Gregory Horne had been friends since high school. After all the members had been in multiple bands around the local area (notably with other members of [[Bad Suns]] and [[The Neighbourhood]]), they decided to join forces and form Hunny in 2014. They released their first single "Honey Blonde". Their second single, "Cry For Me", was released in January 2015 and became a breakthrough hit, gaining over 1 million streams within a year.<ref name="allmusic.com" /> On 9 October 2015, they released their debut EP, ''Pain / Ache / Loving''.<ref name="atwoodmagazine">{{cite web |last1=Sweeney |first1=Sydney |title=Music You Should Know: Two Years Later, ‘Pain / Ache / Loving’ is Still HUNNY’s Best |url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/pal-hunny-mysk/ |publisher=Atwood Magazine |accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref><ref name="thelineofbestfit">{{cite web |last1=Day |first1=Laurence |title=L.A. indie rockers HUNNY stream B3SCI debut Pain/Ache/Loving [Premiere] |url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/l.a.-indie-rockers-hunny-stream-b3sci-debut-pain-ache-loving-premiere |publisher=[[The Line of Best Fit]] |accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref> In 2016, Hunny released the singles "Vowels (and The Importance of Being Me)" <ref name="consequenceofsound">{{cite web |last1=Brennan |first1=Collin |title=SoCal synth rockers HUNNY share new single “Vowels (and The Importance of Being Me)” — listen |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2016/04/socal-synth-rockers-hunny-share-new-single-vowels-and-the-importance-of-being-me-listen/ |publisher=[[Consequence of Sound]] |accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref> and "Colder Parts".<ref name="onestowatch">{{cite web |title=HUNNY Gets Real Real In “Colder Parts” |url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/hunny-gets-real-real-in-colder-parts |publisher=Ones To Watch |accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref> Guitarist Jake Munk left the band in December 2016.<ref name="atwoodmagazine" /> In May 2017, Hunny released their second EP ''Windows I''.<ref name="newenglandsounds">{{cite web |last1=Johnson |first1=Colleen |title=INTERVIEW: HUNNY chats “Windows 2” and tour with Made Violent! |url=https://newenglandsounds.com/2018/03/31/interview-hunny-chats-windows-2-and-tour-with-made-violent/ |publisher=New England Sounds |accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref> 
In early 2018, bassist Gregory Horne left the band.<ref name="miaminewtimes">{{cite web
|last1=Hardee |first1=Howard |title=Hunny Frontman Jason Yarger Spills on His Influences, Also Spills Paint |url=https://www.miaminewtimes.com/music/hunny-at-omalley-sports-bar-in-margate-march-31-10184745 |publisher=Miami New Times |accessdate= 18 December 2019}}</ref> Hunny signed to [[Epitaph Records]] in July 2018, before releasing their third EP ''Windows II''.<ref name="thepreludepress">{{cite web |last1=Vigil |first1=Dom |title=HUNNY Sign to Epitaph & Release New EP, "Windows II" |url=https://thepreludepress.com/news/2018/7/6/hunny-sign-to-epitaph-release-new-ep-windows-ii |publisher=The Prelude Press |accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref> In May 2019, Hunny released their first full-length album, ''Yes. Yes. Yes. Yes. Yes.'' Hunny worked with producer [[Carlos de la Garza (music producer)|Carlos de la Garza]] on the album.<ref name="hunnytheband.co">{{cite web|url=https://hunnytheband.co/about|title=hunnytheband.co|accessdate= 18 December 2019}}</ref>
