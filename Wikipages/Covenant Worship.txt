'''Covenant Worship''' is an American [[Christian music]] [[contemporary worship music|worship]] band from [[Dallas]], [[Texas]]. Their group formed at Covenant Church, an interdenominational congregation, while the church was established by Pastors Mike and Kathy Hayes, in 1976. The group released, ''Heaven on Earth'' in 2009 and ''Never Going Back'' in 2010, independently. They have released 4 [[studio albums|live albums]], ''Standing'' in 2012,  ''[[Kingdom (Covenant Worship album)|Kingdom]]'' in 2014, Take Heart in 2016 and Sand and Stars in 2017.  In May 2017, after the release of Sand and Stars, worship pastors [[David and Nicole Binion]] (Along With Daughter Madison "Gracie" Binion or MDSN) felt the urging from God that their time with covenant was coming to an end.<ref>{{Cite web|title=David & Nicole Binion Talk About Leaving Covenant Worship & the Creation of "Dwell"|url=http://www.hallels.com/articles/19849/20180809/david-nicole-binion-talk-about-leaving-covenant-worship-the-creation-of-dwell.htm|access-date=2021-01-25|website=www.hallels.com|language=en}}</ref> Since then Covenant Worship has released 2 live albums, 1 studio albums(with Covenant Church youth worship group Cov Kid), and 1 instrumental album.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Covenant Worship|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/2F2932LEa8ahflK2xDYKWj|access-date=2021-01-25|website=Spotify|language=en}}</ref>

==Background==
Covenant Worship is from [[Dallas]], [[Texas]], where they were established in 2009,<ref name="AM Bio">{{cite web|first=Steve|last=Leggett|title=Covenant Worship : Biography |publisher=[[AllMusic]]|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/covenant-worship-mn0003280029/biography |accessdate=November 8, 2015}}</ref><ref name="AaW Interview">{{cite web |first=Matt |last=Perkins |title=Interview with David Binion of Covenant Worship |url=http://www.allaboutworship.com/blog/interview-with-david-binion-of-covenant-worship |publisher=All about Worship |accessdate=November 8, 2015 |date=June 26, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160107190213/http://www.allaboutworship.com/blog/interview-with-david-binion-of-covenant-worship |archive-date=January 7, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> with four members, David Binion, Nicole Binion, Joshua Dufrene, and Colin Edge, while they are members of Covenant Church, an interdenominational congregation, founded by Pastors Mike and Kathy Hayes, in 1976.<ref name="AM Bio"/><ref name="AaW Interview"/><ref name="NRT Profile">{{cite web|author=NRTTeamAdmin|title=Covenant Worship Artist Profile |publisher=New Release Today|accessdate=November 8, 2015|date=July 7, 2014 |url=http://www.newreleasetoday.com/artistdetail.php?artist_id=4746}}</ref>

==Music history==
The band started as a musical entity in 2009, with their first independently-made album, ''Heaven on Earth'', that was released on October 27, 2009, from Covenant Worship.<ref name="AM One">{{cite web|author=AllMusic|author-link=AllMusic|title=Heaven on Earth - Covenant Worship|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/covenant-worship-heaven-on-earth-mw0001780760 |accessdate=November 8, 2015}}</ref> This album was their breakthrough release upon the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'' magazine]] charts, where it peaked at No. 29 on the [[Top Gospel Albums|Gospel Albums]] chart.<ref name="AM One Awards">{{cite web|author=''Billboard''|author-link=Billboard (magazine)|title=Heaven on Earth - Covenant Worship : Awards|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/covenant-worship-heaven-on-earth-mw0001780760/awards |accessdate=November 8, 2015}}</ref> Their subsequent album, a [[studio album]], ''Standing'',<ref name="NRT Album Two Review">{{cite web|first=Dwayne|last=Lacy|title=Covenant Church releases their debut on Integrity Music |publisher=New Release Today|accessdate=November 8, 2015|url=http://www.newreleasetoday.com/userprofile_reviewssinglepost.php?review_id=20444&user_id=43067|date=August 26, 2012}}</ref> was released on August 7, 2012, by [[Integrity Media|Integrity Music]].<ref name="NRT Album Two">{{cite web|author=NRTTeamAdmin|title=Standing by Covenant Worship |publisher=New Release Today|accessdate=November 8, 2015|url=http://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=11901}}</ref> The album charted on three ''Billboard'' magazine charts, where it peaked at No. 138 on [[Billboard 200|The ''Billboard'' 200]], No. 2 on [[Christian Albums]], and No. 19 on the [[Independent Albums]] chart.<ref name="AM Two Awards">{{cite web|author=''Billboard''|author-link=Billboard (magazine)|title=Standing - Covenant Worship : Awards|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/standing-mw0002414694/awards |accessdate=November 8, 2015}}</ref> They released, ''[[Kingdom (Covenant Worship album)|Kingdom]]'', on July 1, 2014, with Integrity Media.<ref name="AM Three">{{cite web|first=Matt|last=Collar|title=Kingdom - Covenant Worship : Review|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/kingdom-mw0002698564 |accessdate=November 8, 2015}}</ref> This album peaked on The ''Billboard'' 200 at No. 113,<ref name="AM Three Awards">{{cite web|author=''Billboard''|author-link=Billboard (magazine)|title=Kingdom - Covenant Worship : Awards|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/kingdom-mw0002698564/awards |accessdate=November 8, 2015}}</ref> and at No. 2 on the Christian Albums chart.<ref name="chart search one">[http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?f%5b0%5d=ss_chart_search_title%3A%22Kingdom%22&f%5b1%5d=itm_field_chart_id%3A310&refine=1 Chart search for Kingdom] at billboard.com on Christian Albums</ref> They released, ''Take Heart'', on February 26, 2016, with Integrity Music.<ref name="WL Review TH">{{cite magazine |first=Jeremy |last=Armstrong |title=Covenant Worship: ''Take Heart'' |url=http://media.worshipleader.com/lib/2016mar/html5/index.html?page=79 |magazine=[[Worship Leader (magazine)|Worship Leader]] |publisher=Chuck Fromm, PhD (Worship Leader Partnership) |editor1-first=Jeremy |editor1-last=Armstrong |location=[[San Juan Capistrano, California]] |issn=1066-1247 |access-date=March 1, 2016 |volume=March/April 2016 |page=79}}</ref>
