'''Margaret Elizabeth Lindemann'''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Maggie Lindemann Reveals Her Celebrity Crush & Discloses Her Real Name|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tBgN9XZxVKs|access-date=October 11, 2020|website=YouTube}}</ref> (born July 21, 1998)<ref name="itunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/maggie-lindemann/id1035916223|title=Maggie Lindemann|publisher=iTunes|access-date=November 3, 2017}}</ref> is an American singer-songwriter. She is best known for her 2016 breakout single "[[Pretty Girl (Maggie Lindemann song)|Pretty Girl]]", which peaked at number 4 in [[Sverigetopplistan|Sweden]], number 6 in [[Irish Singles Chart|Ireland]], and number 8 the [[UK Singles Chart|United Kingdom]] and the [[Dutch Charts|Netherlands]].

== Early life ==
Margaret Elizabeth Lindemann was born July 21, 1998, in [[Dallas, Texas]], into a German-Scottish family. She began posting recordings of her singing on the social media app [[Keek]], gaining a following both on the app and her other social media accounts.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.phoenixmag.co.uk/article/meet-maggie-lindemann-the-gen-z-pop-star-whos-not-afraid-to-call-out-the-haters/|title=Meet Maggie Lindemann, the Gen Z Pop Star Who's Not Afraid to Call Out The Haters|date=September 1, 2017|work=PHOENIX Magazine|access-date=December 3, 2017|language=en-GB}}</ref> Her career in music began after her manager, Gerald Tennison, discovered a video of her singing on her [[Instagram]] page. She then relocated to Los Angeles to pursue music.<ref name="Teen Vogue">{{cite web|last1=Ceron|first1=Ella|title=Maggie Lindemann on #KnockingOnYourHeart And Meeting Instagram Followers IRL|url=http://www.teenvogue.com/story/maggie-lindemann-instagram-music-knocking-on-your-heart|website=Teen Vogue|date=September 4, 2015|access-date=September 22, 2016}}</ref>

== Career ==
Lindemann's debut single "Knocking On Your Heart" was released in September 2015. The song earned her a Top 20 spot on the [[iTunes]] [[Alternative rock|Alternative]] Chart within 24 hours of its release.<ref name="iTunes">{{cite web|title=Maggie Lindemann on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/maggie-lindemann/id1035916223#fullText|website=iTunes|access-date=September 22, 2016}}</ref> Her second single "Couple Of Kids" was released on October 30, 2015. On January 29, 2016, her third single "Things" was released, earning her another successful top 25 hit on the iTunes Alternative Chart within a day of its release.<ref name="iTunes" /> The song also broke the Top 50 on the [[Spotify]] [[Billboard charts|Viral 50]] worldwide chart and the top 5 of the Canada viral chart. The official music video of her single "Things" was released on her [[YouTube]] channel on February 5, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|title=Maggie Lindemann – Things [Official Video]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aADqFviP7wU|website=YouTube|access-date=September 22, 2016|date=February 5, 2016}}</ref>

On September 29, 2016, Lindemann released the single "[[Pretty Girl (Maggie Lindemann song)|Pretty Girl]]", her first single since signing to [[300 Entertainment]].<ref name="Cosmo">{{cite web|last1=Thompson|first1=Eliza|title=Exclusive: Listen to Maggie Lindemann's New Single "Pretty Girl"|url=http://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/music/a4259945/maggie-lindemanns-pretty-girl/|website=Cosmopolitan|access-date=September 30, 2016|date=September 29, 2016}}</ref> The song peaked at number 4 on the [[Next Big Sound]] chart, and at number 26 on the Spotify Viral 50 chart.<ref name="Billboard chart">{{cite web|title=Maggie Lindemann – Chart history {{!}} Billboard|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7534507/maggie-lindemann/chart?f=2206|website=www.billboard.com|access-date=October 25, 2016}}</ref> Lindemann wrote the song to "show people that there's more to girls than just being pretty. We have so much to offer. People should get past the physical appearance to something deeper."<ref name="Galore">{{cite web|last1=Cordoba|first1=Maddie|title=Maggie Lindemann's Song 'Pretty Girl' Isn't What You Think|url=https://galoremag.com/maggie-lindemanns-song-pretty-girl-isnt-think/|website=Galore|access-date=November 4, 2016|date=October 31, 2016}}</ref> The music video for "Pretty Girl" premiered through [[People (magazine)|''People'']] on March 9, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Nelson|first1=Jeff|title=Social Media Star Maggie Lindemann Premieres Music Video for Defiant New Single 'Pretty Girl'|url=http://people.com/music/maggie-lindemann-pretty-girl-music-video-premiere/|website=PEOPLE.com|access-date=March 10, 2017|date=March 9, 2017}}</ref> "Pretty Girl" is Lindemann's first song to ever chart on the US Pop Radio Charts as it peaked at 50.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Cantor|first1=Brian|title=Stargate, Chance The Rapper, Maggie Lindemann Enter Pop Radio's Top 50|url=http://headlineplanet.com/home/2017/03/19/stargate-chance-rapper-maggie-lindemann-enter-pop-radios-top-50/|website=Headline Planet|access-date=April 6, 2017|date=March 19, 2017}}</ref> It also peaked at #8 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. Lindemann featured in [[The Vamps (British band)|The Vamps]]' single "[[Personal (The Vamps song)|Personal]]", released October 13, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Wass|first1=Mike|title=The Vamps Recruit Maggie Lindemann For Catchy New Single "Personal"|url=http://www.idolator.com/7669844/vamps-personal-maggie-lindemann-review?view-all|website=idolator|access-date=October 17, 2017|date=October 13, 2017}}</ref> Lindemann released the single "Obsessed" on November 17, 2017.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Stubblebine|first1=Allison|title=Maggie Lindemann is 'So Obsessed' With New Single and Pat McGrath Beauty Campaign|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8039291/maggie-lindemann-interview-new-single-obsessed-pat-mcgrath-beauty-campaign|magazine=Billboard|date=November 17, 2017|access-date=November 21, 2017}}</ref>
On October 26, 2018, she released the single "Human".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Nied |first1=Mike |title=Spooky: Maggie Lindemann Struggles To Connect On "Human" |url=https://www.idolator.com/7687391/spooky-maggie-lindemann-chills-with-the-undead-in-her-human-video?chrome=1 |website=www.idolator.com |access-date=October 26, 2018}}</ref> She released the single "Would I" on November 16, 2018.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wetmore |first1=Brendan |title=Maggie Lindemann Asks, 'Would I?' |url=http://www.papermag.com/maggie-lindenmann-would-i-premiere-2619886732.html |website=PAPER |access-date=November 19, 2018 |language=en |date=November 15, 2018}}</ref> In March 2019, Lindemann was the supporting act for [[Sabrina Carpenter]] on the North American leg of the [[Singular Tour]], and has also opened for The Vamps, as well as [[Madison Beer]] on her [[Life Support (Madison Beer album)|Life Support]] Tour in 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.windycitymediagroup.com/lgbt/Maggie-Lindemanns-not-just-a-Pretty-Girl/65796.html|title=Maggie Lindemann's not just a "Pretty Girl" – Gay Lesbian Bi Trans News Archive|website=Windy City Times|date=April 7, 2019|access-date=April 12, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicmayhemmagazine.com/maggie-lindemann-channels-90s-no-doubt-on-new-song-friends-go/|title=Maggie Lindemann Channels 90's No Doubt on New Song, "Friends Go"|date=March 22, 2019|website=Music Mayhem Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=April 12, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.laweekly.com/event/sabrina-carpenter-maggie-lindemann-10142281|title=Sabrina Carpenter, Maggie Lindemann|last=Callwood|first=Brett|website=L.A. Weekly|access-date=April 12, 2019}}</ref>

Lindemann released the single "Friends Go" on April 24, 2019.<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Daw |first1=Stephen |title=Maggie Lindemann Wonders Where All Her Friends Went On Ska-Tinged Track: Listen |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8503667/maggie-lindemann-friends-go |magazine=Billboard |access-date=May 15, 2019 |date=March 22, 2019}}</ref> The song was later rereleased, featuring [[Travis Barker]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/travis-barker-friends-go-maggie-lindemann/|title=Travis Barker Drums Up Rework of 'Friends Go' With Maggie Lindemann|work=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]|access-date=January 7, 2021}}</ref> Through 2020, she released four singles; "Knife Under My Pillow", "Gaslight!", "Scissorhands" and "Loner". All four of the songs, as well as four new songs, were included on Lindemann's debut [[extended play|EP]], ''PARANOIA,'' released on January 22, 2021. The EP and Lindemann herself received praise from ''[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]'', who acknowledged her "[[Pop music|pop]] background" and appreciated Lindemann "blending genres and experimenting with rock and metal sounds", and credited her with "keeping [[emo]] alive".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/maggie-lindemann-emo-paranoia-ep-release|title=10 WAYS MAGGIE LINDEMANN IS KEEPING EMO ALIVE IN A WHOLE NEW WAY|work=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]|access-date=January 7, 2021}}</ref>

PARANOIA debuted at number 5 at top US album debuts of the week of its release on [[Spotify]]. Her song, "Crash and Burn", was [https://www.audacy.com/ Radio.com] Alternative Pick Of The Week from March 20–27, which landed Maggie Lindemann's first debut on [[Mediabase]] radio chart peaking at number 33.

Lindemann hosts a [[podcast]] called "swixxzaudio," sharing the name of her record label which she founded in 2020. On the podcast, which currently has 8 episodes and began on April 20, 2020, she has discussed various topics such as the making and release of PARANOIA.

== Personal life ==
In 2015, a video leaked on [[Tumblr]] which showed Lindemann's then boyfriend [[Vine (service)|Vine]] star Carter Reynolds attempting to convince Lindemann into oral sex, despite her continuously saying "this makes me so uncomfortable".<ref>{{Cite web|date=July 23, 2015|title=Hashtag-crossed lovers Carter Reynolds and Maggie Lindemann struggle with social media that made them celebs|url=https://www.smh.com.au/technology/hashtagcrossed-lovers-carter-reynolds-and-maggie-lindemann-struggle-with-social-media-that-made-them-celebs-20150723-giipct.html|access-date=August 21, 2020|website=The Sydney Morning Herald|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=June 24, 2015|title=Twitter reacts to leaked video of Vine star 'pressuring 16-year-old into giving oral sex'|url=https://metro.co.uk/2015/06/24/twitter-reacts-to-leaked-video-of-vine-star-pressuring-16-year-old-into-giving-oral-sex-5262409/|access-date=August 21, 2020|website=Metro|language=en}}</ref>

In 2016, Lindemann publicly came out as [[bisexual]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.divamag.co.uk/Diva-Magazine/Celebs/Maggie-Lindemann-A-lot-of-people-have-a-hard-time-believing-Im-bisexual/|title=Maggie Lindemann: "A lot of people have a hard time believing I'm bisexual"|last=Lyell|first=Carrie|date=June 22, 2018|website=Diva|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180622082036/http://www.divamag.co.uk/Diva-Magazine/Celebs/Maggie-Lindemann-A-lot-of-people-have-a-hard-time-believing-Im-bisexual/|archive-date=June 22, 2018}}</ref> As of 2019, Lindemann has been dating Brandon Arreaga, a member of the boy band [[PrettyMuch|PRETTYMUCH]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.popsugar.co.uk/node/46952964|title=BRB, I'm Melting at These Pics of Brandon Arreaga and Maggie Lindemann|last=Dolan|first=Chelsea|date=November 26, 2019|website=POPSUGAR Celebrity UK|language=en|access-date=March 18, 2020}}</ref>

In 2019, while performing in Malaysia, Lindemann was escorted off the stage and arrested for performing without a professional visit pass, as required by all foreigners performing work in the country. She was released on bail the next day, with the event organizers pleading guilty. She has described the incident as "five days of living hell".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/entertainment/singer-maggie-lindemann-arrested-in-malaysia-days-before-she-was-due-to-play|title=Singer Maggie Lindemann arrested in Malaysia days before she was due to play in Singapore|last=hermesauto|date=July 5, 2019|website=The Straits Times|language=en|access-date=July 6, 2019}}</ref>

== Influences ==
Lindemann credits [[Lana Del Rey]], [[Banks (singer)|Banks]], and [[Spooky Black]] as some of her main influences, calling them "the 'anti pop stars'...off-kilter artists who do their individual thing and are unconcerned with being mainstream."<ref name="noisey">{{cite web|last1=Kaplan|first1=Ilana|title=Social Media Star Turned Singer Maggie Lindemann Talks Outsider Pop and Overcoming Cyberbullying – Noisey|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/maggie-lindemann-couple-of-kids-interview|website=noisey|access-date=September 22, 2016}}</ref> She has credited rock and alternative acts of the early 2000s, such as [[Gwen Stefani]], [[Avril Lavigne]], [[Paramore]], [[Evanescence]] and [[Flyleaf (band)|Flyleaf]], as influences, saying, "That's actually my favorite time in music, but I felt like I had to hide that side of myself in order to fit this pop princess vibe."<ref>{{cite web |last1=George |first1=Cassidy |title=When Did Emo Become Cool Again? |url=https://www.wmagazine.com/culture/emo-music-tiktok-gen-z-its-not-a-phase-its-a-lifestyle |website=W Magazine |access-date=August 26, 2021 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Ginsberg |first1=Gab |title=Maggie Lindemann's Boots Were Made For Creeping |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9514052/maggie-lindemann-interview-paranoia-ep |magazine=Billboard |date=January 22, 2021 |access-date=January 27, 2021 |quote=Lindemann found inspiration in Gwen Stefani and No Doubt, Avril Lavigne and Paramore -- as well as Evanescence and Flyleaf}}</ref>
