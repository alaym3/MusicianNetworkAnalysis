'''Grace Davies''' (born 1 February 1997) is an English singer-songwriter. She is an independent artist best known for her original song "Roots". She was runner-up on the [[The X Factor (UK series 14)|fourteenth series]] of ''[[The X Factor (British TV series)|The X Factor]]'' in 2017 mentored by [[Sharon Osbourne]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-42217568|title=Rak-Su: Boy band beat Grace Davies to win X Factor 2017|date=3 December 2017|publisher=[[BBC News]]|access-date=14 December 2017}}</ref>

==Career==

=== 2017: ''The X Factor'' ===
In Davies' first audition for ''[[The X Factor (British TV series)|The X Factor]]'', she performed an original song titled "Roots", and received four yes votes from the judges.<ref>{{cite web|title=Langho singer Grace Davies sails through first part of X Factor bootcamp|url=http://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/news/15568662.Amazing_Grace_Davies_sails_through_first_part_of_X_Factor_bootcamp/|date=30 September 2017|publisher=Lancashire Telegraph|last=Thompson|first=Steven}}</ref> She was mentored by [[Sharon Osbourne]], along with fellow acts [[Alisah Bonaobra]], Holly Tandy and Rai-Elle Williams. She was voted the winner of the Prize Fight in week 1 against [[Rak-Su]], winning a trip to [[New York City|New York]] and a meet and greet with [[Pink (singer)|Pink]] at [[Madison Square Garden]]. Following the eliminations of Bonaobra in week 6 and Tandy and Williams in Week 8, Davies became Osbourne's last remaining act in the competition. She made the final on the weekend of 2 December 2017, alongside Kevin Davy White and Rak-Su. After Davy White was eliminated on Saturday's show, she was in the top two with Rak-Su and finished as the runner-up.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2019/08/19/what-happened-to-grace-davies-x-factor-audition-album/?amp|work=HITC|title=What happened to Grace from X Factor? Singing sensation's 2017 audition re-surfaces!|access-date=25 June 2020}}</ref>

=== 2018-2020: ''Friends with the Tragic'' ===

On 12 January 2018, Davies confirmed she had signed to [[Simon Cowell]]'s record label [[Syco Music]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://tellymix.co.uk/reality-tv/the-x-factor/342522-x-factors-grace-davies-record-label-syco.html/amp |title=X Factor's Grace Davies has signed a record deal with Syco |publisher=TellyMix|access-date=14 December 2017}}</ref> In September 2019, Davies and [[Tokio Myers]] performed as guests on ''[[America's Got Talent]]'' along with [[Stewart Copeland]], performing "[[Safe and Sound (Capital Cities song)|Safe and Sound]]", written by Tokio Myers featuring vocals from Grace Davies.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://metro.co.uk/2019/09/12/simon-cowell-sends-incredible-10-year-old-opera-singer-emanne-beasha-americas-got-talent-finals-10727534/|first=Sarah|last=Deen|title=Simon Cowell sends 'incredible' 10-year-old opera singer Emanne Beasha to America's Got Talent finals|publisher=[[Metro (British newspaper)|Metro]]|date=12 September 2019}}</ref> On 24 January 2020, Davies announced that her debut single will be called "Invisible". The song was released on 31 January 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/news/18186646.x-factor-star-grace-davies-finally-release-first-single/|title=X Factor star Grace Davies to finally release first single|work=[[Lancashire Telegraph]]|access-date=25 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-31|title=GRACE DAVIES RELEASES 'INVISIBLE'|url=https://ventsmagazine.com/2020/01/31/grace-davies-releases-%e2%80%8binvisible/|access-date=2021-03-14|website=Vents Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> On 13 March 2020, Davies announced her second single called "Addicted to Blue", which was released on 20 March 2020. On the same day, she announced the release of her debut [[extended play]], titled ''Friends with the Tragic'', which was released on 26 June 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.entertainment-focus.com/music-section/music-news/grace-davies-debuts-addicted-to-blue-and-announces-ep/|title=Grace Davies debuts Addicted to Blue and announces EP|work=Entertainment Focus|access-date=25 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Grace Davies announces EP and shares brand new single|url=https://heatworld.com/entertainment/music/grace-davies-friends-with-tragic-addicted-blue/|access-date=2021-03-14|website=Heat}}</ref> "Amsterdam" was released as the third single from the EP on 1 May 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-05-01|title=Grace Davies Presents 'Amsterdam'|url=https://ourculturemag.com/2020/05/01/grace-davies-presents-amsterdam/|access-date=2021-03-14|website=Our Culture|language=en-GB}}</ref> "Just a Girl" was released as the fourth and final single from the EP on 26 June 2020 alongside the EP and the official music video.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-27|title=Grace Davies unveils debut EP 'Friends With The Tragic'|url=https://celebmix.com/grace-davies-unveils-debut-ep-friends-with-the-tragic/|access-date=2021-03-14|website=CelebMix|language=en-GB}}</ref>

=== 2021–present: ''I Wonder If You Wonder'' ===
After her label Syco had ceased operations, Davies announced her first independent single called "i met a boy online". The song was released on 19 February 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Premiere: Grace Davies performs new single i met a boy online|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/grace-davies-shares-lockdown-dating-anxieties-with-live-session-of-new-single-i-met-a-boy-online-premiere__32599/|access-date=2021-03-14|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Ackroyd|first=Stephen|date=2021-03-05|title=Grace Davies has shared a 'live @ home' session for her new single 'i met a boy online' {{!}} Dork|url=https://readdork.com/news/grace-davies-i-met-a-boy-online/|access-date=2021-03-14|website=readdork.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

On 26 March 2021, Davies released a cover of [[Goo Goo Dolls]]’ song "[[Iris (song)#Grace Davies version|Iris]]".

On 24 May 2021, Davies announced her new song called "Testosterone". The song was released on 28 May 2021.

On 16 July 2021, Davies announced her new song called "toothbrush". It was released on 23 July 2021.

On 4 August 2021, it was announced that Davies will support [[Janet Devlin]] on her Confessional Tour.

On 20 August 2021, Davies announced her new song called "used to you". The song was released on 27 August 2021. The official video for the song was released on 31 August 2021.

On 28 September 2021, Davies announced the official release of her song "Roots". The song was released on 8 October 2021. On the same day, Davies announced of the title of her third EP ''I Wonder If You Wonder'', which was released independently on 26 November 2021.
