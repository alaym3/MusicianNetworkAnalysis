'''Megan Bülow''' ({{IPA-de|ˈmeːɡan ˈbyːloː}}; born December 25, 1999), known professionally as '''bülow''' (stylized in [[lowercase letters]]), is a German-Canadian pop singer who had success with her debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''Damaged Vol. 1''.<ref name="umusic">{{cite web|title= Meet: bülow, the rising teen pop star from the Netherlands|url=http://www.umusic.ca/2018/01/19/meet-bulow/|website=Umusic|publisher=Universal Music Canada|date=19 January 2018|access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Bülow was born in 1999 in [[Berlin, Germany]]. She grew up and lived in the [[United Kingdom]], [[Canada]], the [[United States]], and the [[Netherlands]].<ref name="ventsinterview">{{cite web|last1=Frometa|first1=Rafael Jóvine|title=INTERVIEW: Megan Bülow|url=http://ventsmagazine.com/2017/11/09/interview-megan-bulow/|website=ventsmagazine.com|publisher=Vents Magazine|date=9 November 2017}}</ref><ref name="recordsite1">{{cite web|title=bülow|url=http://www.casablanca-music.com/artist/bulow/#|website=Casablanca Records|publisher=Casablanca Records}}</ref> After being discovered at a summer camp in 2016, when she was 16,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/ywexqk/bulow-profile-2018|title=Bülow Is Already a Big Deal and Now the Pop World Has to Catch Up|last=Weekes|first=Jabbari|last2=MacDonald|first2=Sarah|date=2018-06-12|website=Noisey|access-date=2019-04-08}}</ref> Bülow began collaborating with a number of Canadian writers and producers.<ref name="recordsite1"/> Bülow currently resides in the Netherlands, where she recently completed high school at the American School of the Hague.<ref name="ventsinterview"/>

== ''Damaged Vol. 1'' ==
In November 2017, Bülow released her debut EP ''Damaged Vol. 1'', garnering her the title of "New Artist of the Week" by Apple Music Canada.<ref name="lemonwire">{{cite web|last1=Young|first1=Jessica|title=Megan Bülow: the rising star you've never heard of - LemonWire|url=https://www.lemonwire.com/2018/03/26/megan-bulow-the-rising-star-youve-never-heard-of/|website=LemonWire|date=26 March 2018}}</ref> The EP featured three tracks, with the track "Not a Love Song" released as a single first.<ref name="noisey">{{cite web|last1=Pacholik|first1=Devin|title=Bülow Wants You To Know "This Is Not A Love Song"|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/zmz8py/bulow-wants-you-to-know-this-is-not-a-love-song|website=Noisey|date=13 November 2017}}</ref> In a statement to [[Noisey]], Bülow described her inspiration for the track as "the excitement of meeting someone for the first time" and eventually realizing that she "just wasn't ready for that commitment yet."<ref name="noisey"/> The rest of her EP also consists of "smart, poignant tracks about guys".<ref name="interns">{{cite web|last1=Murphy|first1=Sam|title=Bülow's 'Damaged Vol.1' Is Your Next Pop Obsession|url=https://theinterns.net/2017/11/14/bulows-damaged-vol-1-next-scandi-pop-obsession/|website=The Interns|date=14 November 2017}}</ref>

''Damaged Vol. 1'' was praised by critics for its "real and authentic" portrayal of bülow's voice and use of dynamic percussion elements.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Stack|first1=Chris|title=Bülow - Damaged Vol. 1 EP - rBeatz Radio|url=https://rbeatz.com/bulow-damaged-vol-1-ep/|website=rbeatz.com|date=20 December 2017|access-date=25 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612112936/https://rbeatz.com/bulow-damaged-vol-1-ep/|archive-date=12 June 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> "Not a Love Song" was also praised for its "bouncy" and "synth-saturated" style.<ref name="noisey"/>

== Tours ==
On 9 April 2019, Bülow announced on Instagram that she would be touring with [[Lauv]] in the fall, visiting India and the American cities of [[Washington, D.C.]], [[Philadelphia]], [[Boston]], [[New York City]], [[Chicago]], [[Oakland, California|Oakland]], and [[Los Angeles]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BwAyJ95gFJV/|title=bülow on Instagram: 'i'm going on tour with @lauvsongs in the fall! ✨'|via=Instagram|access-date=2019-04-08}}</ref> On 9 February 2020, [[Conan Gray]] announced that she would be supporting him on the European leg of his [[Kid Krow World Tour]], starting on 26 April 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B8YW23vJxcr/|title=Conan Gray on Instagram: "KID KROW WORLD TOUR IS COMING TO EUROPE. FIRST TIME PLAYING THE SONGS OFF MY DEBUT ALBUM. WITH GUEST @bulow . TIX ON SALE THIS FRIDAY 2/14…"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-02-14}}</ref> The tour was however postponed indefinitely due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.conangray.com/tour|title=Tour|website=Conan Gray Official Website|language=en|access-date=April 23, 2020|archive-url=https://archive.today/20200505111858/http://www.conangray.com/tour|archive-date=May 5, 2020|url-status=dead}}</ref> On 29 October 2021, [[Conan Gray]] announced that Bülow would be joining him on the North America branch of his 2022 World Tour.
