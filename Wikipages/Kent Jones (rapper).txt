'''Daryl Kent Jones''' (born April 14, 1993) is an American rapper.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kent Jones- Biography|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6894171/kent-jones/biography|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 27, 2015}}</ref> He is best known for his commercial debut single "[[Don't Mind (Kent Jones song)|Don't Mind]]", which reached number eight on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart. His first mixtape, ''Tours'', was released in the summer of 2015 by [[Cool & Dre#Epidemic Records|Epidemic Records]], [[We the Best Music Group]] and [[Epic Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://artistecard.com/kentjones/|title=Kent Jones|access-date=July 1, 2016}}</ref>

==Early life==
Daryl Kent Jones was born on April 14, 1993, in [[Tallahassee, Florida]].<ref name="birthday">{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/kent-jones-mn0003195075/biography | title=Kent Jones Biography | publisher=AllMusic | access-date=May 15, 2015|author=Jeffries, David}}</ref> As a youngster he came in contact with music in his local church, while he was also taught to play the drums by his aunt.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6894171/kent-jones/biography | title=Kent Jones – Biography | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date=May 16, 2016}}</ref> Later on Jones started taking formal music lessons, learning to play the piano and the [[Hammond organ]], and studying music theory.<ref name="genius">{{cite web | url=https://genius.com/a/meet-kent-jones-the-dj-khaled-protege-whose-hit-single-will-soundtrack-the-summer | title=Meet Kent Jones, The DJ Khaled Protégé Whose Hit Single Will Soundtrack The Summer | publisher=[[Genius (website)|Genius]] | date=May 10, 2016 | access-date=May 17, 2016 | author=Nostro, Lauren}}</ref> He also started developing an interest in jazz music, appreciating the music of [[Quincy Jones]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.vibe.com/2015/12/rapper-kent-jones-cool-dre-interview/ | title=We The Best Artist Kent Jones Explains The Success Behind His 'Tours' Mixtape | work=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]] | date=December 11, 2015 | access-date=May 16, 2016 | author=Centeno, Tony}}</ref> Jones later moved to Miami, Florida to work more with [[Cool & Dre]].

==Career==
===2013–present: Beginnings and rise to fame===
Jones first started working with record producers and songwriters [[Cool & Dre]], signing a record deal with their label [[Cool & Dre#Epidemic Records|Epidemic Records]].<ref name="genius" /> While at Epidemic he collaborated with the likes of [[Fat Joe]], [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]], [[Currensy]], [[Queen Latifah]], and [[Busta Rhymes]].<ref name="genius" /> Cool & Dre introduced him to DJ Khaled in 2015, who eventually signed him in a joint venture record deal to his own label [[We the Best Music Group]] and [[Epic Records]].<ref name="genius" /><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ooyuz.com/geturl?aid=8491818 | title=Who is Kent Jones? | publisher=OOYUZ.com | date=September 26, 2015 | access-date=May 17, 2016}}</ref> Jones released his first mixtape titled ''Tours'' in July 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kent-jones-dont-mind-new-song.1965928.html | title=Kent Jones makes his HNHH debut with "Don't Mind." | publisher=HotNewHipHop.com | date=August 18, 2015 | access-date=May 17, 2016 | author=Lyons, Patrick}}</ref> The mixtape's most successful song "[[Don't Mind (Kent Jones song)|Don't Mind]]", which contains lyrics in French, Spanish, Japanese and [[Haitian Creole]], was later released as a single.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=40009 | title=Don't Mind by Kent Jones | publisher=SongFacts.com | access-date=May 17, 2016}}</ref> "Don't Mind", a song which was about to be left out of the mixtape, was also remixed by [[Trina]] later on.<ref name="genius" /> The song debuted at number 63 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart in May 2016,<ref name="genius" /> reached number 46 on [[Contemporary hit radio|pop radio]] in the first week of May 2016,<ref>{{cite web | url=http://headlineplanet.com/home/2016/05/08/kent-jones-dont-mind-disturbeds-the-sound-of-silence-reach-top-50-at-pop-radio/ | title=Kent Jones' "Don't mind," Disturbed's "The sound of silenc" reach top 50 at Pop Radio | publisher=Headline Planet | date=May 8, 2015 | access-date=May 16, 2015 | author=Cantor, Brian}}</ref> further rising to number 40 in the following weeks.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://headlineplanet.com/home/2016/05/15/belly-the-weeknds-might-not-kent-jones-dont-mind-enter-pop-radios-top-40/ | title=Belly's "Might Not (featuring The Weeknd)" and Kent Jones' "Don't Mind" are now inside the Top 40 at pop radio. | publisher=headlineplanet.com | date=May 15, 2016 | access-date=May 17, 2015 | author=Cantor, Brian}}</ref> Jones was also featured in the soundtrack of ''[[NBA 2K16]]'', participating in [[DJ Khaled]]'s track "365" along with [[Ace Hood]] and [[Vado (rapper)|Vado]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.vg247.com/2015/07/24/heres-the-official-nba-2k16-track-list-curated-by-dj-mustard-dj-khaled-and-dj-premier/ | title=Get your groove on with the official NBA 2K16 soundtrack | date=July 24, 2015 | access-date=May 17, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/dj-khaled-365-feat-ace-hood-kent-jones-and-vado-new-song.1966459.html | title=DJ Khaled – 365 Feat. Ace Hood, Kent Jones & Vado [New Song] | publisher=HotNewHipHop.com | date=September 26, 2015 | access-date=May 17, 2015 | author=Walker, Angus}}</ref>
