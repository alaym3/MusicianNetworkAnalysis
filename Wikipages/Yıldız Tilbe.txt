'''Yıldız Tilbe''' (born 16 July 1966) is a Turkish singer of [[Kurdish people|Kurdish]]-[[Zazas|Zaza]] descent and one of the best-selling musical artists in Turkey,<ref name="vaybee.de">{{cite web|url=http://www.vaybee.de/thema/yildiz-tilbe.php|title=Yildiz Tilbe|work=vaybee.de}}</ref> known especially for her eastern-infused ballads.

==Life and career==
Yıldız Tilbe was born in the Gültepe district of [[Konak, İzmir]], and is of [[Kurds|Kurdish]] origin.<ref name="m.habervitrini.com">http://m.habervitrini.com/medya/stardaki-yildiz-tilbenin-programinda-kurt-turk-gerginligi-716004</ref> She is the youngest of her parents' six children.<ref>[http://www.sabah.com.tr/Pazar/2010/05/23/hastayiz_sana_dermansiziz Hastayız sana, dermansızız] ''[[Sabah (newspaper)|Sabah]]. 23 May 2010.</ref> She was known by the nickname "Yadigar" within her family.<ref>[http://www.sabah.com.tr/Pazar/2011/08/28/hep-tasrali-hep-bizden-hep-kadin Hep taşralı, hep bizden, hep kadın] ''[[Sabah (newspaper)|Sabah]]. 28 August 2011</ref> Her mother Altun was from [[Tunceli]], while her father Ali was from [[Ağrı]].<ref name="m.habervitrini.com"/><ref name="vaybee.de"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.biyografi.info/kisi/yildiz-tilbe|title=Yıldız Tilbe|work=biyografi.info}}</ref> Her father Ali Tilbe was a seasonal worker. She attended the Mustafa Rahmi Balaban School, but left after the second grade,<ref>[http://www.ttnetmuzik.com.tr/artist/Yildiz_Tilbe/32777/biyografi Yıldız Tilbe, Biyografi] ''[[TTNET]], Retrieved: 29 December 2012''</ref> and started singing from an early age.<ref>[http://haber.mynet.com/iphone/foto-analiz/unlulerin-eski-meslekleri/579469 Ünlülerin Eski Meslekleri] ''[[Mynet]], Retrieved: 30 December 2012''</ref> At the age of 18, she married Güngör Karahan.<ref>[http://www.milliyet.com.tr/ekler/gazete_pazar/990307/haber/hab4.html Artık Bütün Kapılar Yüzüne Kapatılıyor] ''[[Milliyet]], 14 March 1999''</ref> From this marriage she has a daughter, named Sezen Burçin. After five years the couple divorced. She then started singing in various nightclubs in İzmir.

She began her career in 1990<ref>[http://arama.hurriyet.com.tr/arsivnews.aspx?id=166285 Bir zamanlar... ünlü değillerdi] ''Hürriyet, Kültür Sanat. Yayın tarihi: 19 Ağustos 2003''</ref> in a nightclub owned by Cengiz Özşeker. While performing at the nightclub in 1991, she met [[Sezen Aksu]], who asked Tilbe to become her backing [[vocalist]].<ref>[http://www.fozdemir.com/yildiz-tilbe-iste-boyle-sohret-oldu Yıldız Böyle Şöhret Oldu] ''29 September 2010, Fozdemir''</ref> After accepting her offer, Tilbe moved to [[Istanbul]] and worked for Sezen Aksu for a while. After parting her way from Aksu, Tilbe started performing at Istanbul's nightclubs. She subsequently got an offer from [[Cem Özer]] to perform as a soloist in the program ''Laf Lafı Açıyor''. With her appearance on this show, she became famous<ref>[http://bizimkahve.gazetevatan.com/haberdetay.asp?hkat=1&hid=18375 Benim bu ülkede gördüğüm ilgiyi ne İbrahim Tatlıses ne de Tarkan gördü] ''[[Vatan (newspaper)|Vatan]] Magazin. 7 April 2012''</ref> and released her first album, ''[[Delikanlım]]'', in 1994, which became a hit in Turkey.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.milliyet.com.tr/2002/09/03/sanat/san20.html |title=Aşk laftan anlamaz ki... |date=3 September 2002 |website=Milliyet.com.tr |publisher=Milliyet |access-date=31 July 2014 |url-status=bot: unknown |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050210160519/http://www.milliyet.com.tr/2002/09/03/sanat/san20.html |archive-date=10 February 2005 }}</ref> In addition to her singing career, she started songwriting. In her songs, she addresses subjects such as sadness, love and separation and primarily uses romantic themes in her songs and compositions.<ref>[http://hurarsiv.hurriyet.com.tr/goster/ShowNew.aspx?id=6058207 Magazin, Hürriyet] ''Hürriyet Archive, 4 March 2007''</ref> In her romantic songs, she uses an imaginative and powerful language. Tilbe has composed many songs for several other artists and collaborated with them on their music projects.<ref>[http://www.odatv.com/n.php?n=adi-sezen-aksu-olsaydi-mansetlerden-inmezdi--3105121200 Adı Sezen Aksu Olsaydı Manşetlerden İnmezdi] ''Oda Tv.'' 31 May 2012 15:14</ref><ref name="europopmusic.eu">{{cite web|url=http://www.europopmusic.eu/Turkey_pages/Tilbe.html|title=Yildiz Tilbe - Europopmusic|work=europopmusic.eu}}</ref> About her songs Tilbe said:

{{quote|"My songs do not just tell my story, they tell everybody's story. That is why people listen to them. There is Yıldız who sings these songs, and there is someone who listens to them. I can not even listen to some of my songs because they are really painful. 
Also I'm a person who has something to say not only in those songs but in other ways. I'm not a person limited to just about 40 songs..."<ref name="notgoing">{{cite web | url = http://www.tilbemarket.com | title = Tilbe Market | access-date = 2010-12-28 | publisher = Can Dündar | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151120075018/http://www.tilbemarket.com:80/ | archive-date = 20 November 2015}}</ref>}}

In 1996, the anti-[[narcotics]] police found some cannabis at her house. Tilbe was held in custody and was released after three days. To get rid of her addiction to cannabis, she was hospitalized at [[Balıklı Greek Hospital]] for a while.<ref>[http://www.sabah.com.tr/fotohaber/yasam/unlulerin_kirli_sirlari/23315 Ünlülerin Kirli Sırları] ''[[Sabah (newspaper)|Sabah]], 7 October 2010, Thursday''</ref> In the same year, she released her new album, ''Aşkperest''. In the late 1990s, she worked at various nightclubs in Istanbul, [[Bursa]] and [[Eskişehir]]. In 2001, the release of her new album ''Gülüm'' brought her back to the stardom, which was followed by the album ''Haberi Olsun'' in 2002. In 2003, all of the songs on her album ''Yürü Anca Gidersin'' became hits in Turkey. Throughout her career, she has won many awards and received various nominations.

In 2004, Tilbe was diagnosed with [[uterine cancer]]<ref>[http://magazin.bugun.com.tr/karsiliksiz-ask-kanser-yapti-178584-makalesi.aspx Karşılıksız aşk kanser yaptı!] ''[[Bugün (newspaper)|Bugün]]. 19 December 2011 Monday''</ref> and underwent surgery and treatment at [[Hacettepe University Medical School]]. Tilbe has related her illness to the personal issues she was dealing with at the time and said:

{{quote|I did not say this to anyone except my family. Fortunately, I had a child before. Otherwise, this issue could tear me up. I had a great love in my life during that period of time, I was deeply in love. I was just thinking about him. He was constantly on my mind. When a person is in pain, it could actually be a problem in her body. I completely forgot him after my illness was over. Now I know that everything is not worth hanging around, and I learned that I should not feel despaired for unnecessary things.<ref>[http://www.memesaglik.com/hastalarimiz-icin-/kanseri-yenen-unluler.html Kanseri Yenen Ünlüler]''Dr. Hamdi Koçer Meme Sağlığı. 25 November 2011''</ref>}}
Once she received a marriage offer from [[Azer Bülbül]] but she did not accept his proposal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnnturk.com/haber/kultur-sanat/yildiz-tilbe-azer-bulbulu-anlatti|title = Yıldız Tilbe Azer Bülbül'ü anlattı}}</ref>

==Controversies==
After the [[2006 Lebanon War|2006 Israeli airstrike]] on Lebanon, during a television appearance Tilbe said "May God bring down one disaster after another upon Israel," to which the studio audience answered "Amen."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jcpa.org/article/present-day-anti-semitism-in-turkey/|title=Present-Day Anti-Semitism in Turkey|work=Jerusalem Center For Public Affairs}}</ref> In response to [[Operation Protective Edge|Israeli airstrikes]] on Gaza in early July 2014, Tilbe is reported to have said on social media site Twitter: "God bless Hitler, it was even too few what he did to the Jews, he was right" and "The Jews will be destroyed by Muslims, in the name of Allah, not much time left for it to be done".<ref>[http://www.milliyet.com.tr/yildiz-tilbe-den-irkci-mesajlar-magazin-1910034/ Yıldız Tilbe'den ırkçı mesajlar]</ref><ref name="hurriyetdailynews.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/popular-turkish-singer-causes-outrage-with-anti-semitic-gaza-tweets.aspx?pageID=238&nID=68996&NewsCatID=341|title=Popular Turkish singer causes outrage with anti-Semitic Gaza tweets - LOCAL|work=hurriyetdailynews.com}}</ref> Her tweets received support from [[Melih Gökçek]], the mayor of [[Ankara]], who is a member of the ruling [[Justice and Development Party (Turkey)|Justice and Development Party]] and himself a controversial figure.<ref name="hurriyetdailynews.com"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.timeturk.com/tr/2014/07/11/melih-gokcek-yildiz-tilbe-seni-alkisliyorum.html#.U7_BrpIaySM|title=Melih Gökçek: Yıldız Tilbe, seni alkışlıyorum|work=timeturk.com}}</ref> Later she tweeted that did not mean to praise Hitler and that she also has some Jewish friends and acquaintances.

Yıldız Tilbe, who was a guest on the ''İbo Show'' music program broadcast on the Turkish channel [[ATV (Turkey)|ATV]] in 2009, got angry and reacted when her song was interrupted by Turkish singer and the program's presenter [[İbrahim Tatlıses]]. Tatlıses found Tilbe's behavior disrespectful, reminding her that "I saved you from the pimps." This further caused tension between them and Tilbe left the program.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hurriyet.com.tr/kelebek/jilet-parasi-bile-istedi-11076456|title=Jilet parası bile istedi|date=2009-02-25|publisher=Hurriyet|language=tr}}</ref>

During an interview in 2012 she called a journalist bitch for which she later went on trial.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.haberler.com/yildiz-tilbe-hakaretten-ifade-verdi-3464420-haberi/|title=Yıldız Tilbe, Hakim Karşısında|date=2012-03-20|publisher=haberler.com|language=tr}}</ref>
