'''HIMALAYAS''' are a [[Wales|Welsh]] [[Rock music|rock group]] from [[Cardiff]], Wales, formed in 2015. The group currently consists of Mike Griffiths (lead guitar and vocals), Joe Williams (rhythm guitar and vocals), Louis Heaps (bass) and James Goulbourn (drums).

The group are described as energetic live performers,<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.itsallindie.com/2018/08/interview-with-himalayas.html|title=[INTERVIEW] with... Himalayas|last=link|first=Get|last2=Facebook|access-date=2019-08-30|last3=Twitter|last4=Pinterest|last5=Email|last6=Apps|first6=Other}}</ref> offering music with "seductive guitar lines, hip bass parts that will groove you to the core all driven by an avalanche of drums."<ref name=":1" />

== History ==
Goulbourn, Griffiths, and Williams all met in school and were later joined in the group by Heaps, who was an acquaintance of Goulbourn. The group cite interests from The Beatles, Otis Redding, Kelly Jones, Green Day, John Bonham, and Cher.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsoundwales.com/interviews/himalayas/|title=Himalayas {{!}} New Sound Wales|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref>

The band began producing music on [[SoundCloud]] in July 2016, with their first track titled "Cheap Thrills".<ref>{{Citation|title=Cheap Thrills|url=https://soundcloud.com/user-83036502/cheap-thrills-master|language=en|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> This was followed a few months later by their debut EP release "Ecstasy",<ref>{{Citation|title=Ecstasy|url=https://soundcloud.com/user-83036502/ecstasy-master|language=en|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> "How Do You Sleep",<ref>{{Citation|title=How Do You Sleep|url=https://soundcloud.com/user-83036502/how-do-you-sleep-master|language=en|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> and "Intoxicate Me".<ref>{{Citation|title=Intoxicate Me|url=https://soundcloud.com/user-83036502/intoxicate-me-master|language=en|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> The group began touring at venues including [[Clwb Ifor Bach]] for [[Cardiff University|Cardiff University's]] [[Xpress Radio|Xpresstival]],<ref name=":0" /> [[Music of Newport|Warehouse 54]] in [[Newport, Wales|Newport]],<ref name=":0" /> and Fiddler's Elbow in [[Camden Town|Camden]].<ref name=":0" /> The band have been managed since August 2018 by Stephanie Brennan; formerly of [[Warner Music Group|WME]] and Kobalt Records; and James Brennan, both of whom are based with the Dark Horses agency.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thedarkhorses.com/weapons/|title=The Dark Horses - Staff|website=www.thedarkhorses.com|access-date=2019-09-22}}</ref> Their upcoming release The Masquerade is set to be released independently.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/71NBOcJ9lMeXqnbnya1z0x|title=Himalayas|website=Spotify|language=en|access-date=2019-09-22}}</ref>

The band's music is largely written by Griffiths and Williams and the band have worked closely with former [[Estrons]] member Steffan Pringle, who also works with rising Welsh names [[Adwaith]] and [[Jack Perrett]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.itsallindie.com/2018/08/interview-with-himalayas.html|title=[INTERVIEW] with... Himalayas|last=link|first=Get|last2=Facebook|access-date=2019-08-30|last3=Twitter|last4=Pinterest|last5=Email|last6=Apps|first6=Other}}</ref>

The band drew large scale attention with the release of their track "Thank God I'm Not You".<ref>{{Citation|title=Himalayas - Thank God I'm Not You|url=https://soundcloud.com/user-83036502/himalayas-thank-god-im-not-you-master|language=en|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> [[BBC Cymru Wales]] presenter Adam Walton reportedly called the band for permission to play the track on air within a day of its release,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.redbrick.me/single-review-himalayas-thank-god-im-not/|title=Single Review: Himalayas - Thank God I'm Not You|date=2017-03-31|website=Redbrick|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> and the band went on to appear on [[BBC Radio 1]] with [[Edith Bowman]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.itsallindie.com/2018/08/interview-with-himalayas.html|title=[INTERVIEW] with... Himalayas|last=link|first=Get|last2=Facebook|access-date=2019-08-30|last3=Twitter|last4=Pinterest|last5=Email|last6=Apps|first6=Other}}</ref> [[Virgin Radio UK|Virgin Radio]], [[Amazing Radio]], and [[Radio X (United Kingdom)|Radio X]], with the track named by the latter as the ''[http://northern-exposure.co/this-feeling-60-seconds-with-himalayas-this-feeling-bigin2018/ This Feeling] Track Of The Week'' for the week commencing January 8, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://northern-exposure.co/this-feeling-60-seconds-with-himalayas-this-feeling-bigin2018/|title=THIS FEELING: 60 SECONDS WITH HIMALAYAS – THIS FEELING #BIGIN2018|website=Northern Exposure|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref>

The band toured extensively in 2018 with appearances at [[BBC Music Introducing|BBC Music Introducing Stage]] at [[Reading and Leeds Festivals|Reading and Leeds Festival]], [[Isle of Wight Festival 2018|Isle of Wight Festival]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mcr.live/new-artist-showcase-himalayas/|title=New Artist Showcase: Himalayas - Manchester - MCR Live Blog|date=2017-10-20|website=MCR Live|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> the [[BBC Music Introducing]]/[[PRS for Music]] stage at [[South by Southwest]] 2018,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p0615444|title=BBC Music - BBC Music Introducing, Himalayas - Thank God I'm Not You (SXSW 2018)|website=BBC|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> Cardiff [[Sŵn|Sŵn Festival]], the [[Horizons Gorwelion]] stage at [[Festival N°6]], a two venue run with [[Kaiser Chiefs]], and sets at [[Truck Festival]], [[Y Not Festival]], [[Tramlines Festival]], HOYfest, and a headline set at Nambuccapalooza in London.<ref name=":1" />

To date, the band's headline track "Thank God I'm Not You" has over 25 million streams on [[Spotify]]<ref>{{Citation|title=Thank God I'm Not You|url=https://open.spotify.com/track/4YZVtRbdGOhHdGNi4JpCL5|language=en|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref> and the related music video is their most viewed upload on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Himalayas - Thank God I'm Not You|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J_TL02vGQaM|language=en|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref>

The band announced their 2019 single, "The Masquerade", during their [[BBC Radio 2]] debut on the [[Johnnie Walker (DJ)|Johnnie Walker]] show, which will be their first independent release after leaving their previous label.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00jlwqd|title=BBC Radio 2 - Sounds Of The 70s with Johnnie Walker|website=BBC|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-08-30}}</ref>

In 2021 Himalayas signed to Wild MGMT (Manic Street Preachers, AC/DC, Pink, Courteeners)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://https://nation.cymru/culture/manics-mentor-aims-to-guide-another-welsh-band-to-rock-n-roll-success/}}</ref> 

In summer 2021 Himalayas had their a debut TV performance at The Hundreds on BBC sports <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/live/cricket/57919921/page/10}}</ref> Himalayas supported Manic Street Preachers towards the end of the year.  <ref>{{Cite web|url= https://nation.cymru/culture/meet-the-welsh-band-handpicked-to-support-the-manics-in-cardiff/}}</ref>
