'''Clara Hagman''' (born 9 July 1991), known professionally as '''Clara Mae''', is a Swedish singer and songwriter currently signed to [[Big Beat Records (American record label)|Big Beat]] and [[Atlantic Records]]. She has released two major label singles, "I'm Not Her" and "I Forgot." From 2009 to 2012, Clara Mae was a member of the Swedish band, [[Ace of Base]]. In 2016, she was featured on and co-wrote the [[Kream|KREAM]] song, "Taped Up Heart," which peaked at number 21 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Dance/Electronic Songs|Hot Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart.

==Early life==

Clara Mae was born on 9 July 1991 in [[Gävle]], [[Sweden]].<ref name="GDAce">{{cite news | last1=Ränttilä| first1=Angelica|last2=Linderstam |first2=Madelene | title =Nytt ess i Ace of Base| newspaper =Gefle Dagblad|language =sv | date =August 8, 2010 | url =http://www.gd.se/gastrikland/gavle/nytt-ess-i-ace-of-base-1| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref><ref name="OTW">{{cite news | last=Damoui| first=Yasmin| title =EXCLUSIVE PREMIERE + Q&A: Artist To Watch Clara Mae Exemplifies Individuality in "I’m Not Her" Acoustic| publisher =[[Ones to Watch]]| date =January 4, 2018 | url =http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/exclusive-premiere-qa-artist-to-watch-clara| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> In 2002, she participated in the Lilla Melodifestivalen songwriting competition, performing an original song, "Hej, vem är du," and placing fourth.<ref name="GDAce"/> She also studied jazz vocals and piano for three years. She moved to [[Stockholm]] to pursue a career in music.<ref name="OTW"/>

==Career==

Early in her career, Clara Mae worked with producers [[David Guetta]] and [[Tiësto]].<ref name="OTW"/> In 2008, she appeared on the singing competition program, ''[[Next Star (Sweden)|Next Star]]'' where she performed [[Whitney Houston]]'s "[[I Will Always Love You]]" and placed second.<ref name="DP">{{cite news | last=Egefur| first=Christian| title =Allsång med kroppsövning| newspaper =Göteborgs-Posten|language =sv | date =July 28, 2008 | url =http://www.gp.se/kultur/alls%C3%A5ng-med-kropps%C3%B6vning-1.1154771| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> The following year, she competed on another singing competition program, ''[[Idol 2009 (Sweden)|Idol 2009]]''. She performed [[Duffy (singer)|Duffy]]'s "[[Warwick Avenue (song)|Warwick Avenue]]" for her audition and [[Oh Laura]]'s "[[Release Me (Oh Laura song)|Release Me]]" during the semi-final round, but failed to advance to the finals.<ref name="GDAce"/><ref name="ArbIdol">{{cite news | last=Ekenberg| first=Kristian| title =Idol-Clara räckte inte – men hoppet lever| newspaper =Arbetarbladet|language =sv | date =September 30, 2009 | url =https://www.arbetarbladet.se/noje/idol-clara-rackte-inte-men-hoppet-lever| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> Also in 2009, Clara Mae was recruited by [[Ulf Ekberg]] and [[Jonas Berggren]] to join Ace of Base. She and fellow new member, Julia Williamson, replaced former members, [[Jenny Berggren|Jenny]] and [[Linn Berggren]], and began recording a new album with the band in September 2009.<ref name="GDAce"/><ref name="ArbAce">{{cite news | last=Westrin| first=Stefan| title =Clara Hagman blir ny sångerska i Ace of base| newspaper =Arbetarbladet|language =sv | date =July 23, 2010 | url =https://www.arbetarbladet.se/noje/musik/clara-hagman-blir-ny-sangerska-i-ace-of-base| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> The band released ''[[The Golden Ratio (album)|The Golden Ratio]]'' in September 2010,<ref name="COS">{{cite magazine | last=Young| first=Alex| title =Ace of Base announce new album. Seriously.| magazine =Consequence of Sound | date =September 7, 2010 | url =https://consequenceofsound.net/2010/09/ace-of-base-announce-new-album-seriously/| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> and Clara Mae officially left Ace of Base in 2012.<ref name="LAMB">{{cite news| last =Park| first =Lesley| title =Throwback Thursday: 25 Years of Ace of Base| publisher =LA Music Blog| date =July 30, 2015| url =http://lamusicblog.com/2015/07/news/throwback-thursday-25-years-of-ace-of-base/| accessdate =May 5, 2018| archive-date =October 3, 2015| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20151003051815/http://lamusicblog.com/2015/07/news/throwback-thursday-25-years-of-ace-of-base/| url-status =dead}}</ref>

In 2014, she officially adopted "Clara Mae" as her stage name and released her first song as a solo artist, "Changing Faces." She had been going by her birth name, Clara Hagman, prior to that.<ref name="GD14">{{cite news | last=Sandén| first=Johanni| title =Clara Hagman blir Clara Mae| newspaper =Gefle Dagblad|language =sv | date =July 29, 2014 | url =http://www.gd.se/noje/musik/clara-hagman-blir-clara-mae| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Scandipop">{{cite news| title =Clara Mae: ‘Changing Faces’| publisher =Scandipop| date =July 17, 2014| url =https://www.scandipop.co.uk/clara-mae-changing-faces/| accessdate =May 5, 2018| archive-date =May 7, 2018| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20180507003255/https://www.scandipop.co.uk/clara-mae-changing-faces/| url-status =dead}}</ref> In 2015, she released a new single, "Avalon."<ref name="JMIL">{{cite news | title =Clara Mae - Avalon| publisher =Just Music I Like | date =July 16, 2015 | url =http://www.justmusicilike.com/blog/2015/7/15/clara-mae| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> In 2016, she was featured on the Steve Void & No Mondays song, "Chemistry,"<ref name="DAVoid">{{cite news | title =Steve Void & No Mondays feat. Clara Mae – Chemistry (Original Mix) | publisher =Dancing Astronaut | date =September 10, 2016 | url =https://dancingastronaut.com/2016/09/steve-void-mondays-feat-clara-mae-chemistry-original-mix/| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> and also released another solo single, "STRIP."<ref name="Hilly">{{cite news| title ="STRIP" - Clara Mae| publisher =Hilly Dilly| date =June 2016| url =https://www.hillydilly.com/2016/06/clara-mae-strip/| accessdate =May 5, 2018| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20180507085306/https://www.hillydilly.com/2016/06/clara-mae-strip/| archive-date =May 7, 2018| url-status =dead}}</ref>

That same year, she also co-wrote and was featured on the KREAM song, "Taped Up Heart," which would go on to peak at number 21 on the ''Billboard'' Hot Dance/Electronic Songs chart<ref name="OTW"/><ref name="Idol">{{cite news | last=Wass| first=Mike| title =New Find: KREAM & Clara Mae’s "Taped Up Heart" Is A Moody Bop| publisher =Idolator | date =November 9, 2016 | url =https://www.idolator.com/7650672/new-find-kream-clara-mae-taped-up-heart| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Bill">{{cite magazine | last=Bein| first=Kat| title =Clara Mae Steps Out of the Songwriter Shadows with Poppy 'I'm Not Her': Exclusive| magazine =Billboard | date =November 9, 2017 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8031050/clara-mae-im-not-her-video| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> and number 70 on the [[Sverigetopplistan|Swedish chart]].<ref name="SWE">{{cite web |url=http://swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Kream+feat%2E+Clara+Mae&titel=Taped+Up+Heart&cat=s |title=KREAM Feat. Clara Mae - Taped Up Heart (song)|publisher=Swedish Charts |accessdate=May 5, 2018}}</ref> She also provided backing vocals for [[Gabriela Gunčíková]], who represented the [[Czech Republic in the Eurovision Song Contest 2016|Czech Republic]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2016]] with the song, "[[I Stand (song)|I Stand]]". Clara Mae signed to Big Beat Records (an imprint of Atlantic Records) in 2017.<ref name="R29">{{cite news | last=Lindsay| first=Kathryn| title =Clara Mae's "I Forgot" Is The Anthem You Need The Next Time You Run Into An Ex| publisher =Refinery29 | date =March 13, 2018 | url =https://www.refinery29.com/2018/03/193378/clara-mae-i-forgot-music-video-ex-boyfriend?bucketed=true| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> She released her first major label single, "I'm Not Her," in November of that year.<ref name="Bill"/> She released an acoustic version of the song in January 2018.<ref name="OTW"/> Clara Mae was a featured artist on the [[Felix Jaehn]] song, "Better," which was released in February 2018.<ref name="DAFelix">{{cite news | last=Fleisher| first=Grace| title =Felix Jaehn releases highly anticipated 25-track LP, ‘I’ | publisher =Dancing Astronaut | date =February 19, 2018 | url =https://dancingastronaut.com/2018/02/felix-jaehn-releases-highly-anticipated-25-track-lp/| accessdate =May 5, 2018}}</ref> Her second single on Big Beat, "I Forgot," was released in March 2018.<ref name="R29"/>
