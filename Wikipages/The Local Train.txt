'''[https://www.celebanything.com/the-local-train/ The Local Train']'' is a [[India]]n rock band formed in [[Chandigarh]] in 2008, and based in [[New Delhi]] since 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-10-15|title='For us, it has always been about putting Hindi in indie and making original music'|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/for-us-it-has-always-been-about-putting-hindi-in-indie-and-making-original-music/|access-date=2021-04-15|website=The Indian Express|language=en}}</ref> The band’s lineup comprises lead vocalist, lyricist and guitarist Raman Negi, lead and acoustic guitarist Paras Thakur, bassist Ramit Mehra, and drummer and percussionist Sahil Sarin.<ref>{{Cite web|title='H'indie rock: gear up for The Local Train's performance in Hyderabad|url=https://www.indulgexpress.com/culture/music/2017/aug/31/hindie-rock-gear-up-for-the-local-trains-performance-in-hyderabad-3358.html|access-date=2021-04-15|website=www.indulgexpress.com|language=en}}</ref>

Having started their career by releasing a stream of singles,<ref>{{Cite journal|title=Performance of Coupled-Cluster Singles and Doubles on Modern Stream Processing Architectures|url=http://dx.doi.org/10.1021/acs.jctc.0c00336.s001|access-date=2021-04-15|website=dx.doi.org|doi=10.1021/acs.jctc.0c00336.s001}}</ref> the band’s debut album ''Aalas Ka Pedh'' was released in 2015 and their second album ''Vaaqif'' followed in 2018. Both the albums have been among the top 5 most-streamed rock music albums on [[Apple Music]] India.<ref>{{Cite web|title=India Apple Music Top 100 Rock Albums|url=https://www.top-charts.com/albums/rock/india/apple-music/2021-W09|access-date=2021-04-15|website=www.top-charts.com|language=en}}</ref> The band is known for its Hindi and [[Urdu]] lyrics, often touching on topics like [[communalism]] and [[faith]]<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-02-12|title=The Local Train on their second album Vaaqif, changing the way Hindi rock music is perceived - Living News , Firstpost|url=https://www.firstpost.com/living/the-local-train-on-their-second-album-vaaqif-changing-the-way-hindi-rock-music-is-perceived-4347333.html|access-date=2021-04-15|website=Firstpost}}</ref> which has made them popular with younger audiences across India.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-07|title=REVIEW {{!}} Aalas Ka Pedh by The Local Train|url=https://ahummingheart.com/reviews/aalas-ke-pedh-the-local-train/|access-date=2021-04-15|website=A Humming Heart|language=en-GB}}</ref>

The Local Train frequently performs at live events and music festivals in India, including Bacardi NH7 Weekender, SULAFEST, One Plus Music Fest, Red Bull Tour Bus and Grub Fest. Their music has been featured in [[Pan Nalin]]'s 2015 film [[Angry Indian Goddesses|Angry Indian Goddesses]], which premiered at the [[Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-09-21|title='Angry Indian Goddesses' wins at Toronto International Film Festival|url=https://www.news18.com/news/movies/angry-indian-goddesses-wins-at-toronto-international-film-festival-1107033.html|access-date=2021-04-15|website=www.news18.com|language=en}}</ref>

==History==

=== Early Years ===
The Local Train's journey began in [[Chandigarh]] in 2008 when Ramit, who was working in radio, theatre and performing with several local bands,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-02-15|title=Keeping it Original & Candid: The Local Train|url=http://mondaymorning.nitrkl.ac.in/article/2016/02/15/476-keeping-it-original--candid-the-local-train/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=Monday Morning}}</ref> collaborated with Raman on a song.<ref>{{Cite web|title=In tune with soulful symphonies|url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/cities/bengaluru/2019/dec/09/in-tune-with-soulful-symphonies-2073335.html|access-date=2021-05-03|website=The New Indian Express}}</ref> Soon after, Ramit introduced Raman to Sahil, a well-known independent musician and educator in the circuit,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rock ON! - Indian Express|url=http://archive.indianexpress.com/news/rock-on-/447079/|access-date=2021-05-28|website=archive.indianexpress.com}}</ref> who had known Ramit since their school days and they formed The Local Train. In 2011, Paras joined the band, replacing Abhinav, and dropping out of his [[civil engineering]] course.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Tribune, Chandigarh, India - The Tribune Lifestyle|url=https://www.tribuneindia.com/2011/20110710/ttlife1.htm|access-date=2021-05-03|website=www.tribuneindia.com}}</ref> In 2012, Raman quit his job at TCS to pursue music full time. With this final lineup, the band relocated to [[Delhi]].<ref name="article23384470">{{Cite news|last=Kusnur|first=Narendra|date=2018-03-29|title=Heady beats and soulful lyrics|language=en-IN|work=The Hindu|url=https://www.thehindu.com/entertainment/music/heady-beats-and-soulful-lyrics/article23384470.ece|access-date=2021-05-03|issn=0971-751X}}</ref> Throughout this period, they functioned independently, handling the management and logistics while simultaneously working on singles and releasing them online. Their single Choo Lo was released on [[Myspace|MySpace]] in 2009 and continued to be refined until its music video release in 2011.Other singles released then include Aaoge Tum Kabhi and Kaisey Jiyun in 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-10-15|title='For us, it has always been about putting Hindi in indie and making original music'|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/for-us-it-has-always-been-about-putting-hindi-in-indie-and-making-original-music/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=The Indian Express|language=en}}</ref>

The Local Train had recorded most of their debut album Aalas Ka Pedh when they won [[Sennheiser|Sennheiser's]] first Top 50 band talent hunt in India in 2015, bagging an endorsement from Sennheiser.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-07-28|title=Delhi Rockers The Local Train Announced Winners of Sennheiser Top50 -|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/delhi-rockers-the-local-train-announced-winners-of-sennheiser-top50/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=My Site|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=sennheiser top 50 contest winner 'The local train' {{!}} Metalbase {{!}} Taking Indian Rock, Metal Global {{!}} Provides Best Digital Solution in India|url=http://metalbase.in/2015/09/26/sennheiser-top-50-contest-winner-the-local-train/|access-date=2021-05-03|language=en-US}}</ref> They finished the album, which contained two of their previously released singles, with Flying Carpet Productions and released it in September 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-09-09|title=The Local Train releases debut album|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/the-local-train-release-debut-album-set-out-on-a-nationwide-tour/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=My Site|language=en-US}}</ref> The album was mastered by [[Chris Hesse (musician)|Chris Hesse]]<ref name="article23384470"/> and was one of the highest selling of the year,<ref>{{Cite web|title=You are being redirected...|url=https://www.thedailypao.com/these-are-the-best-selling-indian-indie-albums-of-2015/?fbclid=IwAR0RzAkvC7PPQIl7XvE5YPViSZq1fiqkzSaPHSAsLFQU5J_L8kNso2rxfrw|access-date=2021-05-03|website=www.thedailypao.com}}</ref> featuring on the “Most Streamed Rock Albums<nowiki>''</nowiki> list on [[Apple Music|Apple Music India]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=India Apple Music Top 100 Rock Albums|url=https://www.top-charts.com/albums/rock/india/apple-music/2021-W09|access-date=2021-05-03|website=www.top-charts.com|language=en}}</ref> In November 2015 they signed on with [https://www.instagram.com/spectal.management/ Spectal Management], run by Himanshu Chowdhry.<ref>{{Cite The Local Train worked extensively with [https://www.instagram.com/themorpheusproductions/?hl=en The Morpheus Productions] for their initial music videos. The first video was for Aaoge Tum Kabhi, which featured in [[Pan Nalin]]'s 2015 film [[Angry Indian Goddesses]]<ref>{{Cite web|title='Angry Indian Goddesses' album is a mix of genres - Times of India|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/music/news/angry-indian-goddesses-album-is-a-mix-of-genres/articleshow/49947811.cms|access-date=2021-05-03|website=The Times of India|language=en}}</ref>  that premiered at The [[Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-09-21|title='Angry Indian Goddesses' wins at Toronto International Film Festival|url=https://www.news18.com/news/movies/angry-indian-goddesses-wins-at-toronto-international-film-festival-1107033.html|access-date=2021-05-03|website=www.news18.com|language=en}}</ref> Their second music video for Kaise Jiyun featured Ryan Martyr.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Theurbandancecommunity|date=2016-04-26|title=Ryan Martyr (India, Delhi)|url=https://theurbandancecommunity.wordpress.com/2016/04/26/ryan-martyr/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=theurbandancecommunity|language=en}}</ref> The Dil Mere video was shot in [[Solang Valley]] and [[Manali, Himachal Pradesh|Manali]], and was also produced with Snow Leopard Productions,<ref>{{Citation|title=Dil Mere {{!}} Short Film of the Day {{!}} Shorted|date=2021-03-14|url=https://shorted.in/short-films/dil-mere-the-local-train/|access-date=2021-05-03}}</ref> and Atharva Studios.

=== 2017 - Present ===
The Local Train released Khudi in 2017, a single from their second album. Its music video starred [[Arjun Mathur]], was directed by Vijesh Rajan and produced by Jungle Book Entertainment.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Behance|title=Showreel|url=https://www.behance.net/gallery/17286337/Showreel|access-date=2021-05-03|website=Behance|language=english}}</ref>  It won Platinum Film of The Year at the 2017 [[India Film Project|India Film Project Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-10-06|title=The Local Train Detail Their Upcoming Album 'Vaaqif' -|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/local-train-detail-upcoming-album-vaaqif/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=My Site|language=en-US}}</ref> The second album Vaaqif was released in January 2018, with mixing and mastering done by [https://soundbetter.com/profiles/4788-erich-talaba Erich Talaba].<ref>{{Cite web|last1=ahummingheart·Interviews·February 2|last2=2018|date=2018-02-02|title=The Local Train on their latest album 'Vaaqif'|url=https://ahummingheart.com/features/interviews/local-train-on-their-latest-album-vaaqif/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=A Humming Heart|language=en-GB}}</ref> The band toured extensively in 21 cities before the album launch. In 2018, they released Mizaaj's music video, their first foray into animation that was conceptualised and animated by Ekabhuya Animation.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-06-27|title=Ekabhuya Animation raises strong voice for conserving nature & looking beyond virtual life|url=https://www.animationxpress.com/latest-news/nature-conservation-looking-beyond-virtual-life-ekabhuya-animation-raises-strong-voice/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=AnimationXpress|language=en-US}}</ref> The cyber-punk animated video for Gustaakh soon followed, produced by Plexus studios <ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-12-31|title=Gustaakh asserts that blind faith in a leader might turn out to be dangerous|url=http://www.animationxpress.com/latest-news/plexus-animated-short-gustaakh-asserts-that-blind-faith-in-a-leader-might-turn-out-to-be-dangerous/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=AnimationXpress|language=en-US}}</ref> and edited by Shreyas Beltangdy. It was selected for the 18th Miami International Science Fiction Film Festival,<ref>{{Cite web|title=AN EVENING OF ANIMATION {{!}} SciFi Film Festival|url=https://scififilmfestival.com/an-evening-of-animation/,%20https://scififilmfestival.com/an-evening-of-animation/|access-date=2021-05-03|language=en-US}}</ref> and chosen as one of [[Rolling Stone]]’s 11 best Indian music videos for 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-12-26|title=11 Best Indian Music Videos of 2019 -|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/10-best-indian-music-videos-of-2019/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=My Site|language=en-US}}</ref>

The band is reported to be currently working on a third album.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-11-15|title=There are Good Days and Bad But Four of Us Really Work Well Together: Rock Band The Local Train|url=https://www.news18.com/news/movies/there-are-good-days-and-bad-but-four-of-us-really-work-well-together-rock-band-the-local-train-2382657.html|access-date=2021-05-03|website=www.news18.com|language=en}}</ref>

== Style and Lyrical Themes ==
The Local Train’s music uses a blend of [[Urdu]] and [[Hindi]] lyrics, and has been described as soulful,<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Local Train|url=https://www.platform-mag.com/music/the-local-train.html|access-date=2021-05-19|website=www.platform-mag.com}}</ref>  evocative, and anthemic.<ref name="perceived-4347333">{{Cite web|date=2018-02-12|title=The Local Train on their second album Vaaqif, changing the way Hindi rock music is perceived-Living News , Firstpost|url=https://www.firstpost.com/living/the-local-train-on-their-second-album-vaaqif-changing-the-way-hindi-rock-music-is-perceived-4347333.html|access-date=2021-05-19|website=Firstpost}}</ref> The band cites their musical influences to include [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]], [[Aerosmith]], [[U2]], [[Porcupine Tree]], [[Lucky Ali]], [[The 1975]], and [[Alt-J]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-07|title=REVIEW {{!}} Aalas Ka Pedh by The Local Train|url=https://ahummingheart.com/reviews/aalas-ke-pedh-the-local-train/|access-date=2021-05-19|website=A Humming Heart|language=en-GB}}</ref> They’ve expressed a desire to “put the Hindi in the indie”, and to make a mark on audiences looking to switch from mainstream [[Bollywood]] to other genres of music.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-10-15|title='For us, it has always been about putting Hindi in indie and making original music'|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/for-us-it-has-always-been-about-putting-hindi-in-indie-and-making-original-music/|access-date=2021-05-19|website=The Indian Express|language=en}}</ref>

While Aalas ka Pedh was more [[Pop rock|pop-rock]] inclined,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-09-09|title=The Local Train releases debut album|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/the-local-train-release-debut-album-set-out-on-a-nationwide-tour/|access-date=2021-05-19|website=My Site|language=en-US}}</ref> showing the evolution of the band's music,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Borthakur|first=Pritisha|title=In Conversation With The Local Train {{!}} Pritisha Borthakur|url=http://www.pritishaborthakur.com/2016/03/19/in-conversation-with-the-local-train/|access-date=2021-05-19|language=en-US}}</ref> the band started to experiment with larger themes in their second album Vaaqif. The tracks and accompanying videos explore diverse topics and stories including those about [[communalism]], [[faith]], [[existentialism]],<ref name="perceived-4347333"/> [[Self-actualization|self-actualisation]], over-dependence on technology,<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Potta·Videos·August 31|first1=Meghana|last2=2018|date=2018-08-31|title=WATCH {{!}} The Local Train's new artistic video 'Mizaaj'|url=https://ahummingheart.com/fresh/videos/watch-the-local-trains-new-artistic-video-mizaaj/|access-date=2021-05-19|website=A Humming Heart|language=en-GB}}</ref> and oppressive regimes.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-08-08|title=gustaakh: the local train|url=https://shreyasbeltangdy.com/portfolio/gustaakh-the-local-train/|access-date=2021-05-19|website=SHREYAS BELTANGDY|language=en}}</ref>
