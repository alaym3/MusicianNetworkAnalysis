'''Crystal Lake''' is a Japanese [[metalcore]] band from [[Tokyo]], formed in 2002. The band comprises guitarists Yudai Miyamoto and Hisatsugu Taji, vocalist Ryo Kinoshita, bassist Mitsuru, and drummer Gaku Taura, who is a former member of [[Nocturnal Bloodlust]]. After Shinya Hori's departure, Miyamoto is the only member of the original line-up who remains in the band. Crystal Lake have released five studio albums. Their latest studio album, ''[[Helix (Crystal Lake album)|Helix]]'', was released on 28 November 2018.

==History==

===Formation and first releases (2002–2005)===
Crystal Lake was formed in 2002 in [[Tokyo]], Japan. Their first [[Demo (music)|demo]], ''Freewill'', was released in February in time for the show in Korea which they were invited to by GMC Records. The second demo, ''One Word Changes Everything'', came out in July, and a [[Self-publishing|self-released]] four-track EP, also entitled ''Freewill'' was released in September with 1000 copies made and sold. In September 2003, Crystal Lake played five shows in Japan with Australian band Day of Contempt.

From March to June 2004, they toured with Loyal to the Grave and Extinguish the Fire. Following the end of the tour, the bands released a split album titled ''Blood of Judas'' on [[Compact disc|CD]] and [[DVD]].

On 29 April 2005, the band released their second split album with Risen and Unboy titled ''3 Way Split''. They also toured in [[Malaysia]].<ref>{{Cite web|author=Alliance Trax|date=31 December 2005|url=http://alliance-trax.shop-pro.jp/?pid=649013|title=3 Way Split|access-date=28 May 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110722124401/http://alliance-trax.shop-pro.jp/?pid=649013|archive-date=22 July 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===''Dimension'' and Taste of Chaos 2007 (2006–2007)===
In February 2006, they performed at Independence D. On 5 July 2006, the band released their first studio album titled ''[[Dimension (Crystal Lake album)|Dimension]]'' through Imperium Recordings. After the release of the album, they went on "Dimension Tour" in Japan.<ref>{{Cite web|website=[[Amazon (company)|Amazon]]|date=31 July 2006|url=https://www.amazon.co.jp/DIMENSION-CRYSTAL-LAKE/dp/B000FPWYLI|title=Dimension CD|access-date=29 May 2009}}</ref>

In February 2007, they performed at Metal Presentation with Australian band [[I Killed the Prom Queen]] and [[Hatebreed]] on "Hatebreed Japan Tour" in March.<ref name="MP 2007">{{Cite web|website=howling-bull.co.jp|date=31 January 2007|url=http://www.howling-bull.co.jp/news/news_4.html|title=Crystal Lake with I Killed The Prom Queen|access-date=29 May 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090625151829/http://www.howling-bull.co.jp/news/news_4.html|archive-date=25 June 2009}}</ref><ref name="Hatebreed Japan Tour">{{Cite web|website=creativeman.co.jp|date=1 March 2007|url=http://www.creativeman.co.jp/archives/2007/hatebreed/index.html|title=Crystal Lake performed with Hatebreed|access-date=29 May 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111006182343/http://www.creativeman.co.jp/archives/2007/hatebreed/index.html|archive-date=6 October 2011}}</ref> In November 2007, they played on [[Rockstar (drink)|Rockstar]]'s "[[Taste of Chaos]]" in Japan.<ref>{{Cite web|website=smash-jpn.com|date=28 October 2007|url=http://www.smash-jpn.com/band/2007/11_toc/index.php|title=Crystal Lake taste of chaos 2007|access-date=29 May 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081203194143/http://www.smash-jpn.com/band/2007/11_toc/index.php|archive-date=3 December 2008}}</ref>

===Third split album and Taste of Chaos 2008 (2008–2009)===
In May 2008, they released their third split album on CD with the Japanese punk band Cleave.<ref>{{Cite web|website=crystallake.bigcartel.com|date=1 June 2008|url=http://crystallake.bigcartel.com/product/crystal-lake-cleave-split-cd|title=Split CD with Cleave|access-date=29 May 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090412122604/http://crystallake.bigcartel.com/product/crystal-lake-cleave-split-cd|archive-date=12 April 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref> In November 2008, they performed again at Rockstar's "Taste of Chaos" in Japan.<ref>{{Cite web|website=hurley.jp|date=28 October 2008|url=http://hurley.jp/archives/2008/10/rockstar_taste_of_chaos_2.html|title=Crystal Lake taste of chaos 2008|access-date=29 May 2009}}{{dead link|date=August 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

In January 2009, they toured in Japan with [[Parkway Drive]] and [[Shai Hulud (band)|Shai Hulud]].<ref>{{Cite web|website=alliance-trax.com|date=1 January 2009|url=http://www.alliance-trax.com/tour/spc.html|title=Crystal Lake, Parkway Drive, Shai Hulud Japan Tour 2009|access-date=29 May 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081231160203/http://www.alliance-trax.com/tour/spc.html|archive-date=31 December 2008}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|website=lambgoat.com|date=19 November 2008|url=http://www.lambgoat.com/news/view.aspx?id=11780|title=Japan Tour 2009|access-date=29 May 2009}}</ref>

===''Into the Great Beyond'' and Nishimura's departure (2010–2011)===
On 27 May 2010, Crystal Lake released free download demo ''Endeavor'' on their [[MySpace]] page.<ref>{{Cite web|website=[[MySpace]]|date=27 May 2010|url=http://www.myspace.com/crystallake|title=Endeavor demo 2010|access-date=30 May 2010}}</ref> Crystal Lake also announced through MySpace, Twitter, Facebook and YouTube a new music video "Twisted Fate" from their upcoming second studio album titled ''[[Into the Great Beyond]]''. On 11 June 2011, the founding vocalist Kentaro Nishimura left the band.

===Line-up change and ''Cubes'' (2012–2015)===
On 24 March 2012, the original drummer Yusuke Ishihara left the band. On 3 July, they announced their new vocalist Ryo Kinoshita on their Facebook page with a statement: "''Today, we are pleased to announce the newest addition to the Crystal Lake Family, please give a warm welcome the new vocalist Ryo''."<ref>{{Cite web|website=[[Facebook]]|date=3 July 2012|url=https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10150934002447842&set=a.399521332841.179929.79179407841&type=1&theater|title=New vocalist announced|access-date=3 July 2012}}</ref> The next day, the band announced that Gaku Taura will be their support on drums. They also announced that they were going to release a new single in summer.<ref>{{Cite web|website=crystallakeofficial.tumblr.com|date=30 July 2012|url=http://crystallakeofficial.tumblr.com/post/28261820885/new-single-out-this-summer|title=New Single|access-date=30 July 2012|archive-url=https://archive.is/20121202055756/http://crystallakeofficial.tumblr.com/post/28261820885/new-single-out-this-summer|archive-date=2 December 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> On 5 September, they released two new singles "The Fire Inside" and "Overcome" which was mixed and mastered by [[Brian Hood]] at 456 Recordings.

From 26 January to 1 February 2013, they toured with [[As Blood Runs Black]] and [[Confession (band)|Confession]]. In May 2013, they toured with [[The Ghost Inside (band)|The Ghost Inside]] and Continents. After that they shared the stage with [[Emmure]] and they toured across Japan on the Sumerian Tour 2013 with [[Born of Osiris]], [[Upon a Burning Body]] and Her Name in Blood. Later in 2013, the band went on a supporting tours for [[Coldrain]], Her Name in Blood, [[Crossfaith]], Before My Life Fails, [[SiM (band)|SiM]], and [[Totalfat]].

On 9 February 2014, they played at the Scream Out Fest 2014 with [[The Devil Wears Prada (band)|The Devil Wears Prada]], [[Periphery (band)|Periphery]], [[The Word Alive]], [[Fear, and Loathing in Las Vegas]], and Her Name in Blood at [[AgeHa|Shinkiba Studio Coast]], Tokyo. On 12 April, they played on the Monster Energy Outburn Tour 2014 with Coldrain, Crossfaith, and [[Miss May I]] in Sapporo.

Later in 2014, the band announced that their first [[Extended play|EP]] titled ''Cubes'' via YouTube page, is set to release on 6 August 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|website=[[YouTube]]|date=13 June 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gapIf2EIP30|title=Crystal Lake - Cubes|access-date=13 June 2014}}</ref> A music video for the new song "Ups & Downs" was released on 28 July. On 4 August, a music video was released for their cover of [[Limp Bizkit]]'s song "[[Rollin' (Limp Bizkit song)|Rollin']]". On 6 August, they released the new EP ''Cubes'' via Cube Records. ''Cubes'' debuted at #5 on the [[Oricon]] (Japanese music chart) indie weekly album chart, at #45 on the weekly album chart and at #27 on the daily album chart.

On 8 August, they headlined the "True North Project Tour", with support from Infection, Prompts, Sailing Before the Wind, Scarface, and Shark Ethic. They toured on the Crossfaith's "ACROSS THE FUTURE Tour" with [[We Came as Romans]] and [[While She Sleeps]] in September.<ref>{{Cite web|website=acrossthefuture.com|date=30 May 2014|url=http://acrossthefuture.com|title=Across The Future|access-date=31 May 2014}}</ref> On 25 October, they started their headlining tour "Cubes Tour" 2014. On 15 November, they performed at the [[Knotfest#2014 line-up|Knotfest Japan 2014]].<ref>{{Cite web|website=knotfestjapan.com|date=16 November 2014|url=http://knotfestjapan.com|title=KNOTFEST JAPAN 2014|access-date=16 November 2014}}</ref>

On 3 August 2015, it was announced that bassist Yasuyuki Kotaka had decided to leave the band due to his illness.

===Signing to Artery Recordings, ''The Sign'' and ''True North'' (2015–2017)===
On 7 September 2015, Crystal Lake announced they had signed to [[Artery Recordings]] in the U.S. and [[JPU Records]] in the UK and Europe and would be releasing their following third studio album ''[[The Sign (Crystal Lake album)|The Sign]]'' on 7 October 2015 through these labels. On 30 November 2016, Crystal Lake released their fourth studio album ''[[True North (Crystal Lake album)|True North]]''. They later released two new singles, "Apollo" and "Machina" in 2017.

===Signing to SharpTone Records and ''Helix'' (2018–2019)===
Crystal Lake released the single and accompanying video for "The Circle" on 6 August 2018. The three-track release for ''The Circle'' was released on 8 August.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=[[Blabbermouth.net]]|title=Japanese Metalcore Band CRYSTAL LAKE Releases Music Video For 'The Circle'|url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/japanese-metalcore-band-crystal-lake-releases-music-video-for-the-circle/|date=9 August 2018|access-date=10 September 2018}}</ref>

On 28 November, [[SharpTone Records]] announced that they had signed Crystal Lake to the label. The band released a new single and accompanying video for the song "Aeon".<ref>{{cite web|website=The PRP|title=Crystal Lake Sign with SharpTone Records, Release "Aeon" Music Video|url=https://www.theprp.com/2018/11/28/news/crystal-lake-sign-with-sharptone-records-debut-aeon-music-video/|date=28 November 2018|access-date=30 November 2018}}</ref> On the same day, the band released their fifth studio album, ''[[Helix (Crystal Lake album)|Helix]]'', exclusively in Japan with no release dates yet for a worldwide release as of their country. In 2019, Crystal Lake will support [[August Burns Red]], [[Miss May I]], and [[Fit for a King (band)|Fit for a King]] on "The Dangerous Tour" in North America. In March, the group went on [[Adept (band)|Adept]]'s Russian Tour as a warm up. They also headlined the 2019 "Never Say Die Tour" with [[In Hearts Wake]], King, Our Hollow Our Home, Polar, [[Alpha Wolf (band)|Alpha Wolf]], and Great American Ghost.

===Hori's departure and ''The Voyages'' (2020–present)===
On 8 July 2020, the band released a brand new single titled "Watch Me Burn" and its corresponding music video. On 9 July, a day after the new release, the band announced that the founding rhythm guitarist Shinya Hori would be taking a hiatus for personal reasons.<ref>{{cite web|website=Dead Press!|title=NEWS: Crystal Lake surprise release ‘Watch Me Burn’ EP!|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-crystal-lake-surprise-release-watch-me-burn-ep/|date=8 July 2020|access-date=2 August 2020}}</ref>

On 3 August, the band re-released their song "Into the Great Beyond" from their second studio album of the same name alongside an accompanying music video. At the same time, the band announced that an album consisting of re-recorded material from their Kentaro Nishimura era, titled ''The Voyages'', which released on 5 August 2020, while also revealing the album cover and the track list.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://killyourstereo.com/news/1109413/ten-years-on-crystal-lake-return-to-into-the-great-beyond-with-a-fresh-redux/|title=Ten years on, Crystal Lake return to ‘Into The Great Beyond’ with a fresh redux|work=KillYourStereo|access-date=2 September 2020|date=5 August 2020}}</ref> On 21 November, the band officially announced that they had parted ways with Shinya Hori due to "personal reasons".

In July 2021, the band released the single "Curse", accompanied by an official music video for the song, uploaded onto their [[YouTube]] channel.
