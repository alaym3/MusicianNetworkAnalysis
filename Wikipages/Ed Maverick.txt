'''Eduardo Hernández Saucedo''' (born January 19, 2001), known professionally as '''Ed Maverick'''  is a Mexican folk singer-songwriter.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.eluniversal.com.mx/espectaculos/ed-maverick-se-vuelve-el-artista-mas-viral-en-spotify|title=Ed Maverick se vuelve el artista más viral en Spotify (Ed Maverick became the most viral artist on Spotify)|last=Escobar|first=Elizabeth|website=El Universal (Mexico City)|url-status=live|access-date=September 13, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imer.mx/reactor/ed-maverick-estrena-video-musical-acurrucar-enterate-del-nuevo-fenomeno/|title=Ed Maverick, estrena video musical "Acurrucar" (Ed Maverick, premieres music video of "Acurrucar")|website=Reactor 105.7 FM|language=es|url-status=live|access-date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> He became famous mainly by the sharing of his songs on social media platforms.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lifeboxset.com/nota/199709/editorial-02-un-fenomeno-llamado-ed-maverick/|title=Un fenómeno llamado "Ed Maverick" (A phenomenon called Ed Maverick)|last=Rozales|first=Berenice|date=September 4, 2019|website=LifeBoxset|language=es|url-status=live|access-date=September 13, 2019}}</ref>

== Bio ==
Ed Maverick taught himself how to play the guitar. The first song he learned was, "Simple as this" by [[Jake Bugg]]. He began to compose songs in 2017 at the age of 16, in his hometown in Chihuahua. Among his influences are the rising Mexican musicians Dromedarios Mágicos, [[Juan Cirerol]], and [[Little Jesus]]. His career has grown very quickly and he has achieved fame in a relatively short period of time.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.telehit.com/musica/ed-maverick-cuenta-las-dificultades-de-saltar-de-lo-independiente-a-lo-mainstream|title=Ed Maverick cuenta las dificultades de saltar de lo independiente a lo mainstream (Ed Maverick talks about goes from independence to mainstream)|last=TIM|first=Televisa|website=Telehit|language=es|url-status=live|access-date=September 13, 2019}}</ref>

In 2018 [[Universal Music Group]] signed Maverick, and he released the album, ''Mix pa' llorar en tu cuarto'' (transl. ''Mix to cry in your room''). The album also included the hit, ''Fuentes de Ortiz'' (transl. ''Ortiz Fountains''), the second song that Maverick composed. In 2019 he became one of the most viral artists on [[Spotify]].<ref name=":0" /> That year he also performed at the Lunario del [[Auditorio Nacional (Mexico)|Auditorio Nacional]] and the [[Teatro Metropólitan|Teatro Metropolitan]]. At the latter location, he set a record for selling out his concert dates faster than any other Mexican artist. Also that year, Maverick earned an [[AMPROFON]] [[Music recording certification|Gold Disc]] for his debut album, ''Mix pa' llorar en tu cuarto.''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://marvin.com.mx/ed-maverick-mix-pa-llorar-disco-de-oro-en-tu-cuarto-nuevo-video-2019/|title=Ed Maverick estrenó nuevo video y recibió un disco de oro (Ed Maverick premier new video and received a Gold Disc)|last=Homero|date=September 6, 2019|website=Revista Marvin|language=es-MX|url-status=live|access-date=September 13, 2019}}</ref>
