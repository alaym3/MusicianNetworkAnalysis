'''Omar Jeffery Pineiro'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/Catalog.aspx?detail=writerid&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&keyid=550484157&subid=0 |title=Songwriter/Composer Pineiro Omar Jeffery |access-date=September 30, 2020}}</ref> (born May 15, 1997), known professionally as '''Smokepurpp''', is an American rapper and singer. Originally a record producer, Pineiro later found success on the audio distribution platform [[SoundCloud]] in 2017 from his surge in popularity due to his track "Audi".

Pineiro released his debut commercial mixtape ''[[Deadstar (mixtape)|Deadstar]]'' on September 28, 2017,<ref name="XXL201709">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2017/09/smokepurpp-deadstar-mixtape/|title=Smokepurpp's 'Deadstar' Is Finally Here|website=XXL Mag|language=en-US|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> and the mixtape peaked at number 42 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] albums chart.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/music/smokepurpp/chart-history/billboard-200/song/1047588|title=Smokepurpp Deadstar Chart History|work=Billboard|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Smokepurpp was born on May 15, 1997,<ref name=Carter>{{cite web|last1=Carter|first1=Brooke|title=SmokePurpp Net Worth 2017 – How Much Is He Worth Now?|url=http://gazettereview.com/2017/06/smokepurpp-net-worth-much-worth-now/|website=Gazette Review|access-date=November 24, 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170602100101/http://gazettereview.com/2017/06/smokepurpp-net-worth-much-worth-now/|archive-date=June 2, 2017}}</ref> in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]].<ref name=RS /> He and his family moved to  [[Miami]], [[Florida]] when he was three years old.<ref name=RS>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/look-at-me-the-noisy-soundcloud-revolution-changing-rap-w485101|title=Look At Me!: The Noisy, Blown-Out SoundCloud Revolution Redefining Rap|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> Speaking on his upbringing, Pineiro described himself during high school as a calm individual who surrounded himself with diverse groups of people.<ref>{{Citation|last=No Jumper|title=The SmokePurpp Interview – No Jumper|date=October 6, 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rQNQezzYq3U|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref>

Pineiro began his music career as a producer, a hobby he picked up due to boredom. While doing this, he had a major influence in getting his friend [[Lil Pump]] into rapping as well. He began to rap when no one bought his produced instrumentals and due to the success of several tracks on the audio distribution platform SoundCloud, Pineiro dropped out of high school in his senior year.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/smokepurpp-mn0003622501/biography|title=Smokepurpp {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref>

His musical influences are [[Kanye West]], [[Taking Back Sunday]], [[Every Avenue]], [[Young Thug]] and [[Lucki]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Billboard |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8508958/smokepurpp-video-interview |title=Get to Know 'Remember Me' Rapper Smokepurpp: Watch |publisher=Billboard |date=April 29, 2019 |access-date=September 30, 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2014–2016: Early work ===
Pineiro originally started to pursue music in high school, starting as a producer, as many others, Pineiro started publishing his art on [[SoundCloud]]. Pineiro began to rap due to the lack of people actually buying his produced instrumentals.<ref>{{Citation|last=Mass Appeal|title=Open Space: SmokePurpp|date=May 10, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vy_qRCQr1ag|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> Pineiro's first song was released on SoundCloud in 2015, but he quickly deleted it due to its lack of quality, soon uploading his second song, "Live Off a Lick" featuring Florida native rapper [[XXXTentacion]].<ref>{{Citation|title=smokepurpp ft. xxxtentacion – Live Off A Lick [prod. YUNG TREL]|url=https://soundcloud.com/smokepurpp/smokepurpp-ft-xxxtentacion-live-off-a-lick-prod-yung-trel|language=en|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> Pineiro eventually dropped out of high school due to his budding music career.<ref name=":1" /> Pineiro later released his first music video on September 23, 2014, titled "It's Nothin" with rapper Lil Ominous, who is also Pineiro's cousin.

=== 2017–2018: Breakthrough success and charting albums ===
In March 2017, Pineiro signed a joint venture record deal with [[Todd Moscowitz|Alamo Records]] and [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/03/smokepurpp-signs-record-deal/|title=Smokepurpp Signs to Todd Moscowitz's Alamo Records – XXL|website=XXL Mag|language=en-US|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> Following this, he announced his debut commercial mixtape on March 9, 2017, on Twitter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/smokepurpp/status/839684278921347074?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw&ref_url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/03/smokepurpp-signs-record-deal/|title=New tape on the way... pic.twitter.com/1gLi1sEY5h|last=purpp|first=lil|date=<!--7:48 PM - -->March 8, 2017|website=@smokepurpp|language=en|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> Several days later on March 14, 2017, Pineiro announced that the mixtape would be titled ''Deadstar''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2017/03/smokepurpp-deadtstar-mixtape-woah/|title=Smokepurpp Announces 'Deadtstar' Mixtape, Drops 'Woah' – XXL|website=XXL Mag|language=en-US|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref>

Pineiro released his commercial debut single, "Audi", in May 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2017/05/smokepurpp-goes-nuts-on-new-song-audi/|title=Smokepurpp Goes Nuts on New Song 'Audi'|website=XXL Mag|language=en-US|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> The single soon became his biggest song with over 25 million plays on SoundCloud<ref>{{Citation|title=Audi.|url=https://soundcloud.com/smokepurpp/audi|language=en|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> and 122 million streams on Spotify<ref>{{Citation|title=Audi.|date=May 19, 2017|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/3l1nALhJZP4WiIwLEluCC6|language=en|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> as of October 2017. In September 2017, Pineiro announced that the release date for ''Deadstar'' was September 22, releasing the single "Bless Yo Trap" following the announcement.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7957085/smokepurpp-deadstar-release-date-bless-yo-trap|title=Smokepurpp Announces 'DEADSTAR' Release Date, Premieres New Song 'Bless Yo Trap'|work=Billboard|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref> Pineiro announced later on that ''Deadstar'' would be delayed, stating no expected release date.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/smokepurpp-reveals-deadstar-delay-tm88-production-new-video.42683.html|title=Smokepurpp Reveals "DEADSTAR" Delay, TM88 Production|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=October 11, 2017}}</ref>

On September 28, 2017, Pineiro released ''Deadstar''. The mixtape includes features from artists such as [[Chief Keef]], [[Juicy J]] and [[Travis Scott]].<ref name="XXL201709" /> The mixtape debuted at number 42 on the ''Billboard'' 200 albums chart.<ref name=":0" /> Following the release of the mixtape, on November 8, 2017, Pineiro was on the MTV show ''[[Total Request Live|TRL]]'', where he announced that he had a collaboration mixtape coming soon with [[Murda Beatz]] titled ''[[Bless Yo Trap]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://trl.mtv.com/news/3046226/smokepurpp-tamar-braxton-trl-appearance/|title=SMOKEPURPP & TAMAR BRAXTON LIVED UP TO THE HYPE ON TRL – MTV|website=MTV|language=en-US|access-date=April 2, 2018}}</ref> He later released two singles from the mixtape called "123" and "Do Not Disturb", the latter which features [[Lil Yachty]] and [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]] from rap group [[Migos]]. The mixtape was released on April 13, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/04/stream-smokepurpp-murda-beatz-bless-yo-trap-project|title=Listen to Smokepurpp and Murda Beatz's Joint Project 'Bless Yo Trap'|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=March 10, 2019}}</ref>

On March 20, 2018, while Pineiro was at [[SXSW]], he did an interview with [[Nardwuar the Human Serviette]], and in that interview, Nardwuar exposed that he had a project coming in 2018 called ''Sound of Space'', along with his ''Deadstar 2'' project.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q|title=Nardwuar vs. Smokepurpp|date=March 20, 2018|website=YouTube}}</ref>

=== 2019–present: Debut studio album ===
On April 19, 2019, Pineiro released his EP ''Lost Planet'' via Alamo Records. The EP includes [[guest appearances]] from fellow rappers [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]], [[Lil Pump]], and [[NLE Choppa]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/albums/Smokepurpp/Lost-planet|title=Lost Planet by Smokepurpp|website=Genius|language=en|access-date=April 23, 2019}}</ref> On December 13, he released his debut studio album, ''[[Deadstar 2]]'', after many delays.<ref>{{cite web|title=Smokepurpp's 'Deadstar 2' Album Has Arrived|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/12/smokepurpp-deadstar-2-stream|access-date=June 19, 2020|website=Complex}}</ref> Although it did not perform as commercially well as his previous projects, it did peak at number 86 on the [[Canadian Albums Chart|Canadian Albums]] chart.

Pineiro released his second studio album, ''Florida Jit'', on June 19, 2020.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=https://hiphopdx.com|first=HipHopDX-|date=July 9, 2020|title=Review: Smokepurpp Unevenly Rehabs His Soundcloud Rap Origins On 'Florida Jit'|url=https://hiphopdx.com/reviews/id.3702/title.review-smokepurpp-unevenly-rehabs-his-soundcloud-rap-origins-on-florida-jit|access-date=December 1, 2020|website=HipHopDX}}</ref>
