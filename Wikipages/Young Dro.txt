'''D'Juan Montrel Hart''' (born January 15, 1979), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Young Dro''', is an American rapper. After gaining recognition with his regional hit song "Yes Sir", from his 2002 independent album ''I Got That Dro'', Young Dro aligned himself with fellow Atlanta-based rapper [[T.I.]] and signed to his label, [[Grand Hustle Records|Grand Hustle]], in 2004. Young Dro's debut ''[[Best Thang Smokin']]'', was released in August 2006, under Grand Hustle and [[Atlantic Records]]. The album was preceded by his debut single "[[Shoulder Lean]]", which served as the album's [[lead single]] and proved to be a [[hit single|hit]] in the urban community and [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' charts]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/young-dro-p788789/biography|title=Young Dro > Biography|last=Cordor|first=Cyril|year=2006|publisher=allmusic|access-date=September 6, 2011}}</ref> After amassing an abundance of [[mixtape]]s, Young Dro released his second album ''[[High Times (Young Dro album)|High Times]]'', 7 years later on October 15, 2013, under Grand Hustle, Atlantic and [[eOne Music|E1]]. It spawned the single "[[FDB (song)|FDB]]", his highest charting single since "Shoulder Lean".

==Biography==

===1979–2006: Early life and ''Best Thang Smokin'''===
On January 15, 1979, Young Dro was born D'Juan Hart in Bankhead, a neighborhood on Atlanta's west side.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite web|last=Langhorne |first=Cyrus |url=http://www.sohh.com/2012/10/tis_grand_hustle_proteges_cash_woes_expo.html |title=T.I.'s Grand Hustle Protégé's Cash Woes Exposed, "Just Had His Bad A** Maserati Repo'd" |publisher=Sohh.Com |date=2012-10-15 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> His inspiration for wanting to [[Rapping|rap]] came from the success of one of his best friends, Chris "Daddy Mack" Smith, one half of [[Kris Kross]].<ref name="autogenerated1"/> Musically speaking however, [[Goodie Mob]]—also west Atlanta natives—had the biggest impact on him.<ref name="autogenerated1"/> Hart embarked on his professional career around 1999 when he signed to bass music rapper Raheem the Dream's local Atlanta label, Tight 4 Life, under the [[moniker]] Dro.<ref name="hiphopdx1">{{cite web|last=Byron |first=Dennis |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.609/title.young-dro-best-thang-smokin |title=Young Dro: Best Thang Smokin' &#124; Rappers Talk Hip Hop Beef & Old School Hip Hop |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2006-08-09 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> He released the regional hit "Yes Sir", and an album titled ''I Got That Dro'', around the same time T.I. released his debut, ''[[I'm Serious]]''.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> Although Hart had known T.I. since the early '90s, they grew apart for some time.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> In 2004, Young Dro signed a recording contract with T.I.'s label imprint, [[Grand Hustle Records|Grand Hustle]].<ref name="hiphopdx1"/><ref>http://www.rapweekly.com/?m=200607</ref> Young Dro released his commercial debut single "[[Shoulder Lean]]", in the summer of 2006.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> The song had heavy rotation on [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]] and [[MTV2]], and its cellular phone ringtone sold over 500,000 units.<ref name="hiphopdx1"/> The hit single was featured on his major-label debut ''[[Best Thang Smokin']]'', released on August 29, 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/best-thang-smokin/id183020596 |title=iTunes - Music - Best Thang Smokin' by Young Dro |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2006-06-27 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> The album debuted at number three on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/431671/young-dro/chart?f=305 |title=Young Dro - Chart history |publisher=Billboard |access-date=2014-01-23}}</ref>

===2007–2013: Mixtapes and ''High Times''===
In 2007, Dro was included in [[XXL (magazine)|''XXL'']]'s first annual [[XXL (magazine)#Top 10 Freshmen|Top 10 Freshmen]] list, featured on their cover alongside fellow American rappers [[Lupe Fiasco]], [[Lil Boosie]], [[Joell Ortiz]], [[Plies (rapper)|Plies]], [[Saigon (rapper)|Saigon]], [[Rich Boy]], [[Gorilla Zoe]], [[Papoose (rapper)|Papoose]] and [[Crooked I]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2012/03/where-are-they-now-2007-freshman-recap/ |title=Where Are They Now?: 2007 Freshman Recap - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=2012-03-27 |access-date=2014-01-23}}</ref> On March 17, 2009, Hart released a single titled "[[Take Off (Young Dro song)|Take Off]]", featuring his protege [[Yung L.A.]]. The song was originally released as the first single from his then-titled second studio album ''P.O.L.O. (Players Only Live Once)''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/take-off-feat.-yung-l.a.-single/id306995204 |title=iTunes - Music - Take Off (feat. Yung L.A.) - Single by Young Dro |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2009-03-17 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> On February 23, 2011, he released a mixtape titled ''Equestrian Dro''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Homie |first=Big |url=http://rapradar.com/2011/02/25/new-mixtape-young-dro-equestrian-dro/ |title=New Mixtape: Young Dro Equestrian Dro |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2011-02-25 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> On March 17, 2011, he released a mixtape titled ''I Co-Sign Myself''.<ref>{{cite web|author=damaya919 |url=http://rapradar.com/2011/03/17/new-mixtape-young-dro-i-co-sign-myself/ |title=New Mixtape: Young Dro I Co-Sign Myself |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2011-03-17 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> On July 8, 2011, he released a mixtape titled ''Drocabulary''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Young-Dro-Drocabulary-mixtape.249224.html |title=Young Dro - Drocabulary Hosted by DJ Smallz // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> On January 16, 2012, he released a mixtape titled ''We Outchea''.<ref>{{cite web|author=damaya919 |url=http://rapradar.com/2012/01/16/new-mixtape-young-dro-we-outchea/ |title=New Mixtape: Young Dro We Outchea |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2012-01-16 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> On July 24, 2012, he released a mixtape titled ''R.I.P. 2 (I Killed That Shit)''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Homie |first=Big |url=http://rapradar.com/2012/07/24/new-mixtape-young-dro-r-i-p-2-i-killed-that-shit/ |title=New Mixtape: Young Dro R.I.P. 2 (I Killed That Shit) |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2012-07-24 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> On September 26, 2012, he released a mixtape titled ''Ralph Lauren Reefa''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Homie |first=Big |url=http://rapradar.com/2012/09/26/new-mixtape-young-dro-ralph-lauren-reefa/ |title=New Mixtape: Young Dro Ralph Lauren Reefa |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2012-09-26 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> On March 28, 2013, the first single from his second studio album "[[FDB (song)|FDB]]" was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/fdb-single/id627402302 |title=iTunes - Music - FDB - Single by Young Dro |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2013-03-28 |access-date=2013-09-30}}</ref> On June 17, 2013, Young Dro released the mixtape ''Day Two'', in promotion for his second studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapradar.com/2013/06/17/new-mixtape-young-dro-day-two/ |title=New Mixtape: Young Dro Day Two |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2013-06-17 |access-date=2013-09-30}}</ref> On July 10, 2013, Young Dro announced he would be releasing his second studio album, titled ''[[High Times (Young Dro album)|High Times]]'', in October 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last=Attia |first=Amir |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24616/title.young-dro-high-tymes-release-date |title=Young Dro "High Tymes" Release Date |publisher=HipHop DX |date=2013-07-10 |access-date=2013-09-30}}</ref> On September 18, 2013, the album cover was released and it was announced that the album would be released on October 15, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rapradar.com/2013/09/18/young-dro-high-times-cover/ |title=Young Dro High Times Cover |publisher=Rap Radar |date=2013-09-18 |access-date=2013-09-30}}</ref> On September 30, 2013, the track-listing was unveiled, revealing [[guest appearance]]s on the album from Forgeeauto, [[Mac Boney]], [[T.I.]], Spodee, [[Problem (rapper)|Problem]], Natasha Mosley, Blu June, [[Doe B]] and Miloh Smith.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/09/young-dro-reveals-high-times-cover-art/ |title=UPDATE: Young Dro Reveals 'High Times' Cover Art & Track List - XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |access-date=2013-09-30}}</ref> On September 30, 2013, the second single from ''High Times'', titled "Strong", was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/strong-single/id717197266 |title=iTunes - Music - Strong - Single by Young Dro |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2013-09-27 |access-date=2013-10-10}}</ref> ''[[High Times (Young Dro album)|High Times]]'' would chart at #57 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and at #9 on the [[Top Rap Albums]] chart.

===2015–present: ''Da Reality Show''===
On February 27, 2015, the first single from his third studio album "[[We in da City]]" was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/We-In-Da-City-Explicit/dp/B00U246BAC|title=We In Da City|access-date=9 October 2016|via=Amazon}}</ref> On April 27, 2015, Young Dro announced he would be releasing his third studio album, titled ''[[Da Reality Show]]'', in the summer of 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ugdigital.com/blog/2015/4/27/atlanta-rapper-young-dro-preps-new-album-da-reality-show-and-releases-new-video-for-single-we-in-da-city|title=Atlanta Rapper Young Dro Preps New Album, 'Da Reality Show', and Releases New Video for single, 'We In Da City'...|access-date=9 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.parlemag.com/2015/04/young-dro-preps-new-album-releases-we-in-da-city-video/|title=Young Dro Preps New Album, Releases "We In Da City" Video - Parlé Magazine — The Voice of Urban Entertainment|date=27 April 2015|access-date=9 October 2016}}</ref> It was released on September 18, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/da-reality-show/id1031704299|title=Da Reality Show by Young Dro on Apple Music|access-date=9 October 2016}}</ref>

On May 17, 2016, he released a joint mixtape with [[Zaytoven]] called Boot Me Up.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/39696/young-dro-boot-me-up.html |title=Boot Me Up  |date=May 17, 2016 |website=LiveMixtapes |access-date=May 20, 2016}}</ref>

In August 2016, he was arrested at Georgia Southern University for possessing a gun and drugs.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/young-dro-arrested-on-drugs-and-gun-charges-news.23792.html? |title=Young Dro arrested after being found with guns and a drug |last=Lilah |first=Rose |date=August 30, 2016 |website=Hotnewhiphop |access-date=August 30, 2016}}</ref>

As of May 2019, he is working on a project with upstart gangsta/trap rapper, Romo 11, for Just 11 Records.
