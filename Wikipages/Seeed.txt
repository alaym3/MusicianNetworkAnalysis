'''Seeed''' is a German [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], [[reggae]] and [[dancehall]] band based in [[Berlin]]. Founded in 1998, they have become well known in Germany and its neighboring countries.

== Musical style and lyrics ==
[[File:Seeed-MikaV.jpg|thumb|Hip Hop Open Stuttgart 2001]]
[[File:Seeed Lollapalooza 2015-2.jpg|thumb|See performing in 2015]]
Seeed consists of eleven band members, including three singers, a horn section and a DJ. Seeed is recognized for their catchy fusional mix of [[German hip hop]], reggae and dancehall styles and their use of horns, which is unusual in contemporary popular music. They have worked with [[Cee-Lo Green]], [[Anthony B]], [[Tanya Stephens]], [[General Degree]] as well as other Jamaican artists and producers. Almost all of their music releases feature a popular guest experience.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/Seeed|title=Seeed|website=[[Discogs]]|accessdate=1 June 2018}}</ref> Seeed's lyrics are in German, English and [[Jamaican Patois|Patois]]. Their biggest hits in the German [[Sprachraum]] were "Dickes B", "Aufstehn", "Ding" and "Music Monks".  Although their lyrics are in English and German, most of the songs in German were completely translated into English for the international releases of the Albums ''Next!'' and ''Music Monks''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Seeed|title=Seeed music, videos, stats, and photos|website=Last.fm|accessdate=1 June 2018}}</ref>

== History ==
Their first hit was "Dickes B", an ode to Berlin. They won three [[Echo (music award)|ECHO Awards]]. With the international versions of ''Music Monks'' and ''Next!'', they found broader success in Germany's neighboring countries, especially France.  After their initial success with "Dickes B",  in the year 2000, they released their first full-length album a few months later, in 2001: ''New Dubby Conquerors''.

In September 2012, Seeed released their fourth studio album, ''Seeed'', their first release in six years. In the meantime, members [[Peter Fox (musician)|Peter Fox]], [[:de:Dellé|Dellé]] and Demba Nabé (Boundzound) each released solo albums. The album ''Seeed'' went straight to number 1 in the [[German Albums Chart]]. It was the first time an album of Seeed achieved the top of the albums chart.

On 31 May 2018, Demba Nabé (Boundzound) died at the age of 46.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tagesspiegel.de/berlin/boundzound-alias-ear-seeed-frontmann-demba-nabe-gestorben/22631120.html|title=Seeed-Frontmann Demba Nabé gestorben|first=Laura|last=Hofmann|date=1 June 2018|accessdate=1 June 2018|website=Tagesspiegel.de|language=German}}</ref>

On 18 April 2019, Seeed released their first Single "Ticket" after Nabé's death. The band dedicated this song to their former member.<ref>{{Citation|title=Seeed – TICKET (official Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_UEHmxKEJUc|language=en|access-date=2 December 2019}}</ref>

The fifth album "Bam Bam" was released 4 October 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Seeed-Bam-Bam/release/14222798|title=Seeed – Bam Bam|website=Discogs|language=en|access-date=2 December 2019}}</ref>
