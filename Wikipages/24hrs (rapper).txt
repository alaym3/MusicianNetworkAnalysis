'''Robert Dozier Davis III''' (born December 1, 1990), better known as '''24hrs''', is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and DJ from [[Atlanta]], Georgia.<ref name="gr-carter">{{cite web|first1=Brooke|last1=Carter|accessdate=May 30, 2019|title=24hrs Net Worth 2018|url=https://gazettereview.com/2017/08/24hrs-net-worth-wealthy-rapper-now/|date=August 28, 2017|website=gazettereview.com}}</ref> Davis is best known for his single "What You Like" (featuring [[Ty Dolla Sign]] and [[Wiz Khalifa]]) from his album ''Night Shift'' (2017). He is the older brother of Atlanta rapper [[MadeinTYO]].

==Early life==
Robert Davis III was born in Oakland, California.<ref name="gr-carter"/> He is the oldest brother to fellow recording artist [[MadeinTYO]], with whom he worked early in his career.<ref name="gr-carter"/> In the mid 2000s, they both moved together to [[Atlanta]], Georgia.<ref name="gr-carter"/>

==Career==

24hrs began his artistry under the name Royce Rizzy in 2011.<ref name="billboard">{{cite magazine|accessdate=May 30, 2019|title=24hrs Talks New 'Houses on the Hill' Album, Working With Ty Dolla $ign & Reinventing Himself|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8485483/24hrs-houses-on-the-hill-ty-dolla-sign-hit-boy-interview|magazine=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|accessdate=May 30, 2019|title=The Politics Of Being Yourself, Explained By Royce Rizzy|url=https://www.vibe.com/2016/06/interview-royce-rizzy|date=June 6, 2016|website=Vibe}}</ref>
He released his first mixtape, ''Bag Muzik'' under [[Lil Scrappy]]'s G$UP record label.<ref name="gr-carter"/> His first change came about when he signed to [[Jermaine Dupri]]'s [[So So Def Recordings]] record label.<ref name="gr-carter"/><ref name="billboard"/>

On November 16, 2018, Davis released his debut album, ''House on the Hill''. The album featured executive production by [[Hit-Boy]] and [[Ty Dolla Sign]], as well as guest appearances from [[Lil Pump]], [[YG (rapper)|YG]], [[Moneybagg Yo]], [[Smokepurpp]] and [[Dom Kennedy]], among others.
