'''Khemmis''' is an American [[doom metal]] band from [[Denver|Denver, Colorado]], that formed in 2012. Their second studio album, ''[[Hunted (album)|Hunted]]'', was ranked 11th on ''[[Rolling Stone]]''′s 20 best metal albums of 2016<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/20-best-metal-albums-of-2016-w454251/khemmis-hunted-w454264 | title=20 Best Metal Albums of 2016 | work=RollingStone.com | date=December 9, 2016 | accessdate=July 5, 2017}}</ref> and 1st on [[Decibel (magazine)|''Decibel'' magazine]]'s Top 40 Albums of 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.decibelmagazine.com/2016/11/17/spoiler-here-are-decibel-s-top-40-albums-of-2016/|title=SPOILER: Here Are Decibel's Top 40 Albums of 2016 {{!}} Decibel Magazine|date=November 17, 2016|work=Decibel Magazine|access-date=September 11, 2017}}</ref>

In July 2017, the band announced that they had signed to [[Nuclear Blast]] (via licensing deal with 20 Buck Spin) for the release of their third album.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nuclearblast.de/en/label/music/band/news/details/4839401.4108178.khemmis-sign-to-nuclear-blast-worldwide.html|title=KHEMMIS - sign to Nuclear Blast worldwide!|date=July 17, 2017|work=Nuclear Blast|access-date=September 11, 2017}}</ref> The band toured North America alongside [[Enslaved (band)|Enslaved]], [[Wolves in the Throne Room]], and [[Myrkur]] as part of the 2018 Decibel Magazine Tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/enslaved-wolves-in-the-throne-room-myrkur-and-khemmis-set-for-2018-decibel-magazine-tour/|title=ENSLAVED, WOLVES IN THE THRONE ROOM, MYRKUR And KHEMMIS Set For 2018 'Decibel Magazine Tour'|date=October 24, 2017|website=Blabbermouth.net|access-date=April 21, 2019}}</ref> They released their third album, ''Desolation'', on June 22, 2018. The album was ranked 9th on [[Decibel (magazine)|Decibel Magazine]]'s Top 40 Albums of 2018<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.decibelmagazine.com/2018/11/15/spoiler-here-are-decibels-top-40-albums-of-2018/|title=SPOILER: Here Are Decibel's Top 40 Albums of 2018|date=November 15, 2018|website=Decibel Magazine|access-date=April 21, 2019}}</ref> and 12th on [[Consequence of Sound]]'s Top 25 Metal + Rock Albums of 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/12/top-25-metal-hard-rock-albums-of-2018/|title=Top 25 Metal + Hard Rock Albums of 2018|date=December 5, 2018|website=Consequence of Sound|access-date=April 21, 2019}}</ref>

In February 2019, the band announced that they had signed a worldwide deal with Nuclear Blast.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.westword.com/music/khemmis-signs-to-nuclear-blast-records-11255499|title=Khemmis Signs to Nuclear Blast Records|last=Cabrera|first=Adam|date=March 5, 2019|website=Westword|access-date=April 21, 2019}}</ref> In August that year, the band contributed a cover of the folk song "[[O Death]]" (titled "A Conversation with Death") for the video game series ''[[The Dark Pictures Anthology]]''.<ref>[https://ggwpreport.com/the-sound-of-death-man-of-medan-debuts-another-oh-death-cover/ The Sound of Death: Man of Medan Debuts Another 'Oh Death' Cover] Good Game Report. September 3, 2019. Retrieved September 9, 2019.</ref> In 2020, they covered "[[Down in a Hole]]" from [[Alice in Chains]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://metalinjection.net/av/new-music/khemmis-streams-crushing-cover-of-alice-in-chains-down-in-a-hole|title=KHEMMIS Streams Crushing Cover of ALICE IN CHAINS' "Down in a Hole"|first=Greg|last=Kennelty|date=August 7, 2020|website=Metal Injection|accessdate=November 18, 2020}}</ref>

They are named after an ancient city located in Egypt, now known as [[Akhmim]].<ref>{{cite encyclopedia|url=https://www.metal-archives.com/bands/Khemmis/3540375987|title=Khemmis|encyclopedia=EncyclopaediaMetallum|accessdate=July 5, 2017}}</ref>
