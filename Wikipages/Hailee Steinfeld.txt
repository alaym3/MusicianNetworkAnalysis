'''Hailee Steinfeld''' (born December 11, 1996) is an American actress and singer. She had her breakthrough with the [[Drama (film and television)|western drama film]] ''[[True Grit (2010 film)|True Grit]]'' (2010), which earned her nominations for the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Academy Award]], [[BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role|BAFTA Award]] and the [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Supporting Role|SAG Award]] for Best Supporting Actress.

Steinfeld rose to mainstream prominence for her lead roles in ''[[Ender's Game (film)|Ender's Game]]'' (2013), ''[[Romeo & Juliet (2013 film)|Romeo & Juliet]]'' (2013), ''[[Begin Again (film)|Begin Again]]'' (2013), and ''[[3 Days to Kill]]'' (2014). She received critical acclaim for her roles in the [[Pitch Perfect (film series)|''Pitch Perfect'' film series]] (2015–2017) and the [[Coming-of-age story|coming-of-age]] comedy-drama film ''[[The Edge of Seventeen]]'' (2016), the latter of which earned her a [[Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe Award]] and [[Critics' Choice Movie Awards|Critics' Choice Movie Award]] nomination. Steinfeld subsequently provided the voice of [[Spider-Woman (Gwen Stacy)|Gwen Stacy / Spider-Woman]] in the animated film ''[[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse]]'' (2018), and starred as Charlie Watson in the action film ''[[Bumblebee (film)|Bumblebee]]'' (2018). She currently plays [[Emily Dickinson]] in the [[Apple TV+]] comedy-drama series ''[[Dickinson (TV series)|Dickinson]]'' (2019–present) and [[Kate Bishop (Marvel Cinematic Universe)|Kate Bishop / Hawkeye]] in the 2021 [[Disney+]] series [[Hawkeye (2021 TV series)|''Hawkeye'']], set in the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]].

Steinfeld gained recognition in music after performing "[[Flashlight (Jessie J song)|Flashlight]]" in ''[[Pitch Perfect 2]]'' (2015). She signed with [[Republic Records]] soon after and released her debut single, "[[Love Myself (Hailee Steinfeld song)|Love Myself]]", followed by her debut extended play ''[[Haiz (EP)|Haiz]]'' (2015). She went on to release a series of singles, including the collaborations "[[Starving (song)|Starving]]", which peaked at number 12 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart, and "[[Let Me Go (Hailee Steinfeld and Alesso song)|Let Me Go]]" with [[Alesso]], [[Florida Georgia Line]] and [[Andrew Watt (musician)|Watt]], which peaked at number two on the US [[Dance/Electronic Songs|Dance songs]] chart. In May 2020, she released her second extended play, ''[[Half Written Story]]'', supported by the singles "[[Wrong Direction (song)|Wrong Direction]]" and "[[I Love You's]]".

== Artistry and media reception ==
===Musical and acting styles===
[[File:Jesse Eisenberg & Hailee Steinfeld (5491251554).jpg|thumb|right|Steinfeld (right) pictured at the [[83rd Academy Awards]] where she was nominated for [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Best Supporting Actress]]]]
''[[V (American magazine)|V magazine]]'' writer Dylan Kelly called Steinfeld an "artist of ambidexterity, showcasing supremacy across an impressive repertoire of on-screen roles in film and television".<ref name="VHS">{{Cite web|url=https://vmagazine.com/article/hailee-steinfeld-finds-clarity-through-acting-and-music-making/|title=Hailee Steinfeld Finds Clarity Through Acting and Music-Making|access-date=October 26, 2020|website=V}}</ref> He went on to say that her "musical prowess is one of melodic self-growth and good-natured, lyrical wisdom".<ref name="VHS"/> When making music, Steinfeld explained in interview for ''[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]]'' that, "With [my] music, I feel like I get to be myself, tell my own story, and take my life experiences and not put them into another story but into my own words."<ref name="InterviewMagHS">{{Cite web|url=https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/hailee-steinfeld|title=Hailee Steinfeld|website=Interview|date=March 28, 2016|access-date=October 26, 2020}}</ref> She went on to say that "music is such a big influence in [her] acting" and that the two "entwine".<ref name="InterviewMagHS"/> ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' writer Natalie Weiner stated that Steinfeld has long been injecting "some vague notion of female empowerment [...] into her music.<ref name="VarietyHS">{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/reviews/hayley-williams-hailee-steinfeld-albu-review-petals-armor-half-written-story-1234601348/|title=Hayley Williams' 'Petals for Armor' and Hailee Steinfeld's 'Half Written Story': Album Reviews|access-date=October 26, 2020|website=Variety|date=May 8, 2020}}</ref>

Steinfeld herself has stated that she hopes to be remembered as "an artist that truly cared about the art".<ref name="FNHS1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.film-news.co.uk/news/UK/41971/Hailee-Steinfeld-hopes-to-leave-behind-a-musical-legacy|title=Hailee Steinfeld hopes to leave behind a musical legacy|accessdate=May 30, 2021|website=Film News}}</ref> She went on to note the "differences between working on a film set and in a studio are multiple" and stated that "she loves the freedom of making music".<ref name="FNHS1"/> Steinfeld quoted,
<blockquote>"There's something so freeing about going into a studio. It doesn't matter what time of day it is, doesn't matter what you're wearing or whether you're wearing make-up or not. You go into a room with people that you love and you feel comfortable with, and can be yourself around and be vulnerable and open up and talk about experiences. Magic happens in those moments".<ref name="FNHS1"/></blockquote>

''[[Gold Derby]]'' writer, Christopher Rosen, wrote that Steinfeld "has grown up to become an incredibly versatile lead performer".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goldderby.com/feature/hailee-steinfeld-dickinson-video-interview-1204230509/|title='Dickinson' star Hailee Steinfeld looks back on her 1st Oscar nomination|accessdate=May 30, 2021|website=Gold Derby|date=April 28, 2021}}</ref> [[Self-love]] is common theme Steinfeld has looked at within her music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pop.inquirer.net/92780/multi-platinum-recording-artist-hailee-steinfeld-releases-new-single-and-album-while-on-lockdown|title=Multi-platinum recording artist Hailee Steinfeld releases new single and album while on lockdown|accessdate=September 14, 2021|website=Pop!}}</ref>

===Influences===
Steinfeld has cited [[Madonna]] and [[Rihanna]] as her biggest musical influences.<ref name="BillboardInfluencesHS">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/video/hailee-steinfeld-at-billboard-women-in-music-2015-i-have-so-many-musical-influences-6805713|title=Hailee Steinfeld at Billboard Women in Music 2015: 'I Have So Many Musical Influences'|access-date=October 26, 2020|website=Billboard}}</ref> Other artists Steinfeld has called an influence include [[Britney Spears]], [[Tori Kelly]], [[Selena Gomez]], [[Bruno Mars]] and [[Justin Timberlake]].<ref name="BillboardInfluencesHS"/><ref name="ttribunjateng.com 2017-05-16" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ew.com/article/2016/07/26/hailee-steinfeld-tour/|title=Hailee Steinfeld's tour: How Justin Bieber and Britney Spears inspired her|accessdate=August 12, 2021|website=Entertainment Weekly}}</ref> Steinfeld noted their "strength, courage and their ability to go out [...] and express themselves".<ref name="ttribunjateng.com 2017-05-16" /> She has also said that she grew up listening to [[Boyz II Men]] and [[Luther Vandross]] due to her mother's love for them.<ref name="InterviewMagHS"/>

===Achievements===
{{main|List of awards and nominations received by Hailee Steinfeld}}

In 2016, she was nominated for a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Musical or Comedy|Golden Globe Award for Best Actress]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goldenglobes.com/winners-nominees/2017?page=1|title=Golden Globe Awards Winners and Nominees 2017|accessdate=September 18, 2020}}</ref>
