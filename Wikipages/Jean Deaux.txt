'''Zoi Harris''' (born May 4, 1995), better known by her stage name '''Jean Deaux''' is an artist, musician and filmmaker from [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]] who is "known for her mastery of multiple genres and creative mediums".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/janeclairehervey/2018/11/08/why-singer-rapper-and-artist-jean-deaux-wrote-an-album-about-failure/|title=Why Chicago-Based Hip-Hop Artist Jean Deaux Wrote An Album About Failure|last=Hervey|first=Jane Claire|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> Her debut EP ''Krash'' was released in October 19, 2018.

==Musical career==
Deaux has appeared on tracks by other artists including [[Smino]], [[Saba (rapper)|Saba]], [[Mick Jenkins (rapper)|Mick Jenkins]], and [[Isaiah Rashad]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/05/04/jean-deaux-energy-premiere|title=Jean Deaux demands good vibes on her new single "Energy"|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> She has noted [[Missy Elliott]] as one of her inspirations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dbknews.com/pb/2018/10/24/jean-deaux-krash-ep-album-rap-hip-hop-rb-smino-noname-chicago-review/|title=Review: Jean Deaux is fully formed on her newest EP 'Krash'|last1=October 24|first1=Ayana Archie {{!}}|last2=2018|website=Arc Publishing|language=en-US|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> Jean Deaux is a stage name inspired by the line 'that's John Doe' in a track by [[Rick Ross]] which she previously used as a [[Twitter]] handle before adopting it as a stage name.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thirdcoastreview.com/2018/11/19/interview-last-friday-night-jean-deaux-threw-a-party/|title=Interview: Last Friday Night, Jean Deaux Threw a Party|last=Haynes|first=Arthur|date=2018-11-19|website=Third Coast Review|language=en-US|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> She is part of the hip hop collective The Village 777 with [[Alex Wiley]], Monster Mike, [[Isaiah Rashad]], Spiff, and The Magician. She is also part of the musical collective Medicine Woman with [[Ravyn Lenae]], Drea Smith, and Via Rosa.<ref name="Reader2">{{cite news|last1=Galil|first1=Leor|last2=Nelson|first2=J. R.|title=This year's Winter Block Party celebrates women in Chicago hip-hop|url=http://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/winter-block-party-jamila-psych-frozen-mind-fpe-zigtebra/Content?oid=25248790|access-date=17 November 2017|work=Chicago Reader|date=January 25, 2017}}</ref>

In 2017 she released a track titled ''Wikipedia'', telling HotNewHipHop that "People are going to try and tell you who you are every step of the way, they'll even knock you down to convince you. But you get stronger every time you get up on your feet".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/jean-deaux-wikipedia-new-song.1975459.html|title=Jean Deaux - Wikipedia|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> The lead single Way Out from the ''Krash'' EP was positively reviewed by [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/the-ones-5-best-new-rap-songs-from-jean-deaux-calboy-lancey-foux-noir-brent-and-soldier-kidd/|title=The Ones: 5 Best New Rap Songs From Jean Deaux, Calboy, Lancey Foux, Noir Brent, and Soldier Kidd|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> The EP received positive reviews from Rolling Out,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://rollingout.com/2018/11/04/jean-deauxs-krash-is-a-style-mashup-living-in-rb/|title=Jean Deaux's 'Krash' is a style mashup living in R&B|last=Lamarre|first=Eddy "Precise"|language=en-US|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> DJBooth<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://djbooth.net/charts/2018-10-23-new-songs-you-need-this-week|title=10 New Songs You Need This Week|last=Varan|first=Brendan|website=DJBooth|language=en|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> and [[Chicago Reader]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/jean-deaux-krash-barron-young-hurry-lightfoils-chambers/Content?oid=62694961|title=In a season crowded with great Chicago music, rapper-singer Jean Deaux drops a standout EP|last=Galil|first=J. R. Nelson, Leor|website=Chicago Reader|language=en|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref> [[NPR]] listed Deaux as one of 20 'artists to watch' in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2019/01/17/683321675/slingshot-artists-to-watch-in-2019|title=Slingshot: 20 Artists To Watch In 2019|website=NPR.org|language=en|access-date=2019-02-05}}</ref>
