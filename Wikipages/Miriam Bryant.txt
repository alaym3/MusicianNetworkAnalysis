'''Miriam Melanie Bryant''' (born 8 March 1991) is a Finnish-Swedish singer and songwriter.

==Biography==
Influenced by her British father's musical heritage and her Finnish mother's melancholy, Miriam Bryant began to write songs in November 2011 together with her childhood friend, [[Victor Rådström]] who is a producer and songwriter. Four months later, she produced three songs released in her single debut "Finders, Keepers" in March 2012 through the record label 100 songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.100songs.com/intro/|title=About Us}}</ref> The song received worldwide attention among bloggers and music sites.{{citation needed|date=March 2016}} She's signed to [[Warner Music]] in Sweden and to [[Interscope Records]] in the United States.

She has collaborated with music producer and DJ [[Zedd (musician)|Zedd]], who first heard her on the German radio.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.neontommy.com/news/2013/10/next-adele-meet-miriam-bryant |title=Is This The Next Adele? Meet Miriam Bryant |publisher=Neon Tommy |date=10 October 2013 |accessdate=4 December 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170816150314/http://www.neontommy.com/news/2013/10/next-adele-meet-miriam-bryant |archive-date=16 August 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Zedd made a remix of her song "Push Play" on his re-released album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Zedd-Clarity-Deluxe-Edition/release/4945708|title=Zedd – Clarity (Deluxe Edition)|publisher=discogs|access-date=16 August 2017}}</ref>

Bryant released her ''Push Play'' EP on 8 October 2013 through Interscope Records, her debut release in the United States. In 2015, Bryant was one of eight artists being involved in the programme ''[[Så mycket bättre]]'' on the Swedish commercial channel [[TV4 (Sweden)|TV4]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tv4.se/s%C3%A5-mycket-b%C3%A4ttre/artiklar/vilka-%C3%A4r-artisterna-i-s%C3%A5-mycket-b%C3%A4ttre-2015-5538c753fca38fd1ac0002ce|title=Vilka är artisterna i Så mycket bättre 2015?å mycket bättre – tv4.se|publisher=[[TV4 (Sweden)|TV 4]]|accessdate=3 January 2016|date=23 April 2015|first=Lina|last=Åhman}}</ref>

The single Black Car became a hit in homeland Sweden winning Song of the Year in 2016.<ref>https://thepartae.com/miriam-bryant-black-car-single-video/</ref> In 2017 Bryant was nominated for Best Live Act on the [[Swedish Grammis]].<ref>http://www.myguide2stockholm.com/miriam-bryant-is-back-with-rocket/</ref> In 2018, British press wrote the following about her single—[[Clash (magazine)|''Clash'' magazine]] wrote "Highly Intelligent and completely infectious"<ref>https://www.clashmusic.com/news/miriam-bryants-black-car-is-poised-with-optimism</ref> and ''[[The Line of Best Fit]]'' wrote "Miriam Bryant is your next pop superhero".<ref>https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/miriam-bryant-black-car</ref> In 2018 she has played at [[Roskilde Festival]],<ref>https://www.roskilde-festival.dk/music/2018/miriam-bryant?view=poster</ref> [[The Great Escape Festival]]<ref>https://greatescapefestival.com/artists/miriam-bryant/{{Dead link|date=May 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> to name a few.

In December 2019 she hosted the TV and radio fundraising project [[Musikhjälpen]] in [[Västerås]] together with [[Daniel Hallberg]] and [[Farah Abadi]].
