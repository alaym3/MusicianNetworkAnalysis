'''Angel Dust''' (stylized as '''Angel Du$t''')<ref name="Chicago">{{cite web |last1=Galil |first1=Leor |title=Baltimore's Angel Dust upend hardcore on Pretty Buff |url=https://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/baltimores-angel-dust-upend-hardcore-on-pretty-buff/Content?oid=68652881 |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> is an American [[rock music|rock]] supergroup formed in [[Baltimore]], [[Maryland]] in 2013,<ref name="AllMusic" /> made up of members of [[Turnstile (band)|Turnstile]] and [[Trapped Under Ice]].<ref name="Revolver" /> They have released four albums.<ref name="AllMusic" /> In 2018, they signed to [[Roadrunner Records]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=RENSHAW |first1=DAVID |title=Baltimore punks Angel Du$t return with two super catchy new songs |url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/12/angel-dust-big-ass-love-take-away-the-pain |website=[[The Fader]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> In 2019, [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] noted them as one of the most important bands in broadening the scope of what [[hardcore punk]] is.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Enis |first1=Eli |title=Is Hardcore Punk's Current Boom at Odds With Its Outsider Ethos? |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8545789/hardcore-boom-popularity-2019 |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> [[Kerrang!]] included their 2019 album ''Pretty Buff'' as one of their "25 Best Albums of 2019"<ref>{{cite web |title=THE 25 BEST ALBUMS OF 2019 (SO FAR) |url=https://www.kerrang.com/features/the-25-best-albums-of-2019-so-far/ |website=[[Kerrang!]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> and  ''[[Loudwire]]'' named it one of the 50 best metal albums of 2019.<ref name="Ltop5019">{{cite web |title=The 50 Best Metal Albums of 2019 |url=https://loudwire.com/best-metal-albums-2019/ |website=[[Loudwire]] |publisher=[[Townsquare Media]] |access-date=March 7, 2021 |date=December 3, 2019}}</ref>

==Musical style==
The band have been categorised as [[soft rock]],<ref name="Revolver">{{cite web |last1=HILL |first1=JOHN |title=ANGEL DU$T: HOW TURNSTILE/TRAPPED UNDER ICE SUPERGROUP KEEPS FANS GUESSING |url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/angel-dut-how-turnstiletrapped-under-ice-supergroup-keeps-fans-guessing |website=[[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> [[pop rock]],<ref name="NME">{{cite web |last1=Connick |first1=Tom |title=Angel Du$t are the fun-first hardcore group bringing ’90s pop-rock to the pit |url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-blogs/angel-dust-interview-album-2464417 |website=[[NME]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Breihan |first1=Tom |title=The 10 Best Hardcore Albums Of 2019 |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2067703/best-hardcore-albums-2019/franchises/2019-in-review/ |website=[[Stereogum]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> [[hardcore punk]],<ref name="Revolver" /><ref name="NME" /><ref>{{cite web |last1=Breihan |first1=Tom |title=Band To Watch: Angel Du$t |url=https://www.stereogum.com/1877374/band-to-watch-angel-dut/franchises/band-to-watch/ |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> and [[melodic hardcore]].<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web |last1=Monger |first1=James Christopher |title=Angel Du$t |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/angel-dut-mn0003279363/biography |website=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="Pitchfork">{{cite web |last1=Cohen |first1=Ian |title=Rock the Fuck on Forever |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/21910-rock-the-fuck-on-forever/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Haney |first1=Maisie |title=Angel Du$t expands the hardcore palate at Soundbar |url=https://www.orlandoweekly.com/Blogs/archives/2019/06/12/angel-du-t-expands-the-hardcore-palate-at-soundbar |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> Their music often incorporates elements of [[surf rock]], [[grunge]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Padmore |first1=Billy |title=Album Review: Angel Du$t – Pretty Buff |url=https://mindnoisenetwork.com/2019/03/22/album-review-angel-dut-pretty-buff/ |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> [[alternative rock]]<ref name="Chicago" /> and [[pop music|pop]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rosso |first1=Eric |title=Angel Du$t Pretty Buff (2019) |url=https://www.punknews.org/review/16381/angel-dut-pretty-buff |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref>

Their 2013 demo ''Xtra Raw'' was described by [[Kerrang!]] as a "scrappy, [[Lo-fi music|lo-fi]] bundle of punk", while their subsequent material was more melodic.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Enis |first1=Eli |title=ANGEL DU$T: ALBUM BY ALBUM WITH FRONTMAN JUSTICE TRIPP |url=https://www.kerrang.com/features/angel-du-t-album-by-album-with-frontman-justice-tripp/ |website=[[Kerrang!]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> [[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]] magazine described the band's music as a "curve ball", due to its relationship to the hardcore scene, despite also including "heartfelt saxophone", "strummy acoustic guitar[s]" and "an overwhelming amount of positivity",<ref name="Revolver" /> and in another article as "about as catchy and fun as catchy and fun get".<ref>{{cite web |title=45 MOST ANTICIPATED ALBUMS OF 2019 |url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/45-most-anticipated-albums-2019#angel-dut |website=[[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> In an article for [[Clash (magazine)|Clash]], writer Robin Murray described their music as "between a rough-around-the-edges [[The Lemonheads|Lemonheads]] with [[Elvis Costello]] vocal melodies".<ref name="Clash">{{cite web |last1=MURRAY |first1=ROBIN |title=Angel Du$t Defy Categorisation |url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/angel-dut-defy-categorisation |website=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref>

Many of the tracks on the band's 2019 album ''Pretty Buff'' were written about the death of Tripp's dog.<ref>{{cite web |last1=GELFAND |first1=ZAC |title=Baltimore Hardcore Buffs Angel Du$t Made A Complete Sonic Overhaul Sound Easy |url=https://uproxx.com/music/angel-dust-pretty-buff-interview/ |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref>

They cited influences including [[The Lemonheads]], [[The Replacements (band)|The Replacements]], [[The Feelies]],<ref name="Thrasher">{{cite web |title=Angel Du$t |url=https://www.thrashermagazine.com/articles/music-interviews/angel-du-t-interview/ |website=[[Thrasher (magazine)|Thrasher]] |accessdate=27 December 2019}}</ref> the [[Bad Brains]], [[Violent Femmes]] and [[Greg Sage]].<ref name="Clash" />
