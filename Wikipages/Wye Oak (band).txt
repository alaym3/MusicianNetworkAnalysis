'''Wye Oak''' is an American [[indie rock]] duo from [[Baltimore]], [[Maryland]], United States, composed of [[Andy Stack (musician)|Andy Stack]] (drums, keyboards, backup vocals) and [[Jenn Wasner]] (vocals, guitars and bass).<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |author=Monger, James Christopher |title=Wye Oak |website=Allmusic |url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p1029177/biography|pure_url=yes}} |access-date=14 April 2008 }}</ref> Their sound has been described as "earnest folk-influenced indie rock with touches of noise and dream pop"<ref name="pitchreview">{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/11430-if-children/|title=If Children Album Review |last=Powell |first=Mike |date=25 April 2008 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=21 February 2010 }}</ref> as well as [[indie folk]].<ref>{{cite web|work=A.V. Club|title=Wye Oak: Civilian|url=http://www.avclub.com/review/wye-oak-iciviliani-52830|last=Hyden|first=Steven|date=8 March 2011}}</ref> Wasner sings lead vocals and plays electric or acoustic guitar, while Stack plays both drums and keyboards, playing the drums with his feet and right hand, and the bass line with his left hand.<ref name="paste">{{cite magazine |url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2009/06/band-of-the-week-wye-oak.html |title=Band of the Week: Wye Oak |last=Newby |first=Tim |date=29 June 2009 |magazine=Paste magazine |access-date=21 February 2010 }}</ref><ref name="npr">{{cite web |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=113797945 |title=Untying The Knot with Wye Oak |last=Reilly |first=Matt |date=15 October 2009 |publisher=[[NPR]] |access-date=21 February 2010 }}</ref>

==History==
The band was formed as Monarch in mid-2006,<ref name="allmusic"/> before changing their name to Wye Oak, a reference to the former [[Wye Oak|state tree of their home state of Maryland]]. They released their first album, ''[[If Children]]'', independently in 2007 and subsequently signed to [[Merge Records]] in 2008. The label re-released ''If Children'' that year; a second album, ''[[The Knot (album)|The Knot]]'', followed in 2009.<ref name="pitchreview2">{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/13219-the-knot/ |title=The Knot – Album Review |last=Powell |first=Mike |date=21 July 2009 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=21 February 2010 }}</ref>  Both albums were recorded at the recording studio at [[University of Maryland Baltimore County]], where Stack was a student, with ''[[The Knot (album)|The Knot]]'' submitted as Stack's senior project.

Their third album, ''[[Civilian (Wye Oak album)|Civilian]]'', was released in March 2011 by Merge in the US and [[City Slang]] in Europe. The title track was featured in [[The Walking Dead (TV series)|The Walking Dead]] during the tenth episode of season 2, titled “18 Miles Out”, and also in [[Safety Not Guaranteed]].

They followed with ''[[Shriek (Wye Oak album)|Shriek]]'' in April 2014.<ref name="spinarticle">{{cite web |url=https://www.spin.com/2013/10/wye-oak-new-album-2014-guitar-interview/|title=Wye Oak Embrace Reinvention, Abandon Guitar on Upcoming Fourth Album |last=McGovern |first=Kyle |date=30 October 2013 |publisher=[[Spin Media|Spin.com]] |access-date=18 November 2013 }}</ref> In 2016, they released ''[[Tween (Wye Oak album)|Tween]]'', consisting of leftover material recorded between ''Civilian'' and ''Shriek''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.avclub.com/review/wye-oaks-tween-enthralling-glimpse-roads-not-taken-240272|title=Wye Oak’s Tween is an enthralling glimpse into roads not taken|first=Annie|last=Zaleski|website=Avclub.com|access-date=6 April 2018}}</ref>

On April 6, 2018, their next full-length album, ''[[The Louder I Call, the Faster It Runs]]'', was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/01/wye-oak-new-album-the-louder-i-call-the-faster-it-runs-title-track/|title=Wye Oak Announce New Album The Louder I Call, the Faster It Runs, Release Title Track|date=30 January 2018|website=Spin.com|access-date=6 April 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1980777/wye-oak-the-louder-i-call-the-faster-it-runs/music/|title=Wye Oak – 'The Louder I Call, The Faster It Runs'|date=30 January 2018|website=Stereogum.com|access-date=6 April 2018}}</ref> For their ''The Louder I Call, the Faster It Runs'' tour during the spring of 2018, Wye Oak added bassist William Joseph Hackney to the live line-up.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/2018/04/09/wye-oak-the-louder-i-call-the-faster-it-runs-review/|title=Album of the Day: Wye Oak, "The Louder I Call, The Faster It Runs"|date=9 April 2018|website=Daily.bandcamp.com|access-date=28 May 2018}}</ref>
