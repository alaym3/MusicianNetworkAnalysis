'''Ruby Ibarra''' (born February 25, 1988) is a [[Filipino American]] rapper, [[music producer]], and [[spoken word]] artist from [[San Lorenzo, California]]. She raps in [[Tagalog language|Tagalog]], [[Waray language|Waray]], and English.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Kruschewsky|first1=Gabriela|title=9 Underground Female Rappers You Need To Know About|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/gabrielakruschewsky/undereground-female-rappers-you-need-to-know-about|work=Buzzfeed|date=November 6, 2013}}</ref> Her raps concern her cultural heritage and her experiences as an immigrant to the United States from the Philippines.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bernier|first1=Lisa|title=11 Asian Musicians Proving That Great Music Knows No Race|url=http://mic.com/articles/85035/11-asian-musicians-proving-that-great-music-knows-no-race|work=Mic|date=March 12, 2014}}</ref>

==Early life==
Ibarra was born in the [[Philippines]]. Ruby spent around four years of her life in the city of [[Tacloban]], she was inspired by a television performance by Filipino rapper [[Francis Magalona]]. Her family immigrated to [[San Lorenzo, California]] and she was raised in the Bay Area. She attended the [[University of California, Davis]] and performed with spoken word collective, SickSpits.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Pandika|first1=Melissa|title=This Girl is Breaking the Silence of the Female Rap Scene|url=http://www.ozy.com/rising-stars/ruby-ibarra-dynamite-mc/30853|work=OZY|date=July 5, 2014}}</ref> Ruby attributes her identity in music from growing up in the early nineties with hip-hop influences of Tupac, Eminem, Wu-Tang Clan, etc. <ref>{{cite web |title=BIO |url=https://www.rubyibarra.com/ |website=Ruby Ibarra |access-date=8 August 2021}}</ref>

==Music career==
Ibarra released her ''Lost In Translation'' mixtape, hosted by [[DJ Kay Slay]], on December 12, 2012. The mixtape debuted that evening on [[Eminem]]'s Shade45 channel on [[Sirius XM Radio]], where she was interviewed live by [[DJ Kay Slay]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Fleischer|first1=Adam|title=The Break Presents: Ruby Ibarra|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2011/10/the-break-presents-ruby-ibarra/|work=XXL|date=October 3, 2011}}</ref>

On November 5, 2015, she officially signed to independent record label, Beatrock Music, and announced that she would release her full-length debut album with them in 2016. She began recording the album with [[Fatgums]] in [[Inglewood, California]] in March 2016. On October 3, 2017, she released her album “Circa 1991” - where she documents social justice issues like immigration and trauma and made her mark in her local music industry. She is described by people as reflective of who they are and the experiences they’ve had in the United States.

In January 2018, Ruby was featured in a national [[MasterCard]] TV commercial with singer [[SZA (singer)|SZA]] and other breakout artists to promote the Start Something Priceless campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/01/30/sza-documentary-self-love-mastercard|title=The Fader. (2018, Jan). Watch SZA team up with 6 breakout artists to talk struggle, self-love}}</ref>

In just 2018 alone, Ruby Ibarra has done 6 projects with The Filipino Channel: ''Cinematografo: Performance and Music Licensing- Discovering Routes: Music Licensing- Kasayahan Festival in Daly City: Performance- 24x24: Feature not yet released- Balitang America: Jocelyn Enriquez x Ruby Ibarra Feature- Gifts of Love: Performance for ABS CBN Foundation.'' Ruby Ibarra’s 2018 documentary - Nothing on US: Pinays Rising - was featured in multiple countries & film festivals including Guam,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.guamfilmfestival.org/nothing-on-us-pinays-rising/|access-date=2021-07-06|website=www.guamfilmfestival.org}}</ref> Toronto, & San Francisco + Boston in the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nothing on US: Pinays Rising|url=https://filmfreeway.com/NothingonUSPinaysRising|access-date=2021-07-06|website=FilmFreeway|language=en}}</ref>

On June 29, 2019, Ibarra performed at the [[Smithsonian Folklife Festival]] alongside DJ Mister REY on the National Mall in Washington, D.C.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ruby Ibarra – "Taking Names" at the Smithsonian Folklife Festival|url=https://www.si.edu/es/object/yt_He8lFvHgdsc|access-date=2021-06-30|website=Smithsonian Institution|language=es}}</ref> Ibarra performed at [[J. Paul Getty Museum|The Getty]] on February 15, 2020 in collaboration with the Smithsonian Folklife Festival.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sounds of L.A.: Ruby Ibarra and the Balikbayans {{!}} Getty360 Calendar|url=https://www.getty.edu/visit/cal/events/ev_3085.html|access-date=2021-07-06|website=The J. Paul Getty in Los Angeles|language=en}}</ref>

In addition to Ruby’s individual platforms, she has team support from the following platforms to distribute her content: Myx TV (CableTv & Online broadcast in more than 15 million households on cable providers such as Comcast, Time Warner, Cox, Bright House and DIRECTV) and DASH Radio (a global digital radio broadcasting platform with more than 80 original stations and 10 million+ listeners around the world). Dash Radio also partnered with General Motors last year to have the channel built into one million of its car models as a part of the podcast application.

Ibarra has 45,400 subscribers on [[YouTube|Youtube]] and combined presence of nearly 3 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ruby Ibarra - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/c/rubyibarra/about|access-date=2021-07-06|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>

Ruby Ibarra is one of a few Filipino artists from the USA that have gained traction among Filipinos from around world, as reflected by her weekly bookings to perform all around the world, including next week’s Malisimbo music festival in the Philippines.

==Style==
Ibarra describes her style as reminiscent of 1990s hip hop; she cites [[Lupe Fiasco]] and [[Raekwon]] as her influences.<ref>{{cite news|title=8 Asian Entertainers Who Are Making Names For Themselves In The States|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/10/06/asian-entertainers-in-music-film_n_5772148.html|work=Huff Post|date=October 6, 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==

Ibarra is a UC Davis graduate.<ref>https://medium.com/@julaipi/ruby-ibarra-scientist-by-day-rapper-by-night-61384bb25d59</ref> She lives in Hayward and works as a scientist in the quality-control department of a Bay Area biotech firm.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sanfrancisco.cbslocal.com/2020/08/09/ruby-ibarras-world-bay-area-rapper-by-night-covid-19-researcher-by-day/|title = Ruby Ibarra's World: Bay Area Rapper by Night; COVID-19 Researcher by Day|date = 9 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sfgate.com/sf-culture/article/Bay-Area-rapper-COVID-vaccine-Ruby-Ibarra-15450383.php|title=Meet the Bay Area rapper working on a COVID vaccine|date=3 August 2020}}</ref>
