'''Cory Hunter Asbury''' is an American Christian musician, [[worship pastor]], songwriter, and member of the [[Bethel Music]] collective.

Starting as an intern with the [[International House of Prayer]] in [[Kansas City, Missouri]], in 2005, Asbury's career as a musician grew as he collaborated with [[Matt Gilman]] leading to the release of ''Holy'' in 2008, followed by his first solo album, ''Let Me See Your Eyes'', in 2009 by Forerunner Music, and numerous appearances on IHOPKC albums,<ref name="Alabaster">{{cite web |url= http://www.alabastergroup.com/speakers/cory-asbury |title= Cory Asbury {{!}} Alabaster Group |website= Alabaster Group |access-date= January 22, 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180213195113/http://www.alabastergroup.com/speakers/cory-asbury |archive-date= February 13, 2018 |url-status= dead }}</ref><ref name="Forerunner">{{cite web |url= https://www.ihopkc.org/music/featured-artists/cory-asbury/ |title= Cory Asbury - Forerunner Music |website= Forerunner Music |publisher= [[International House of Prayer|International House of Prayer, Kansas City, MO.]] |access-date= February 12, 2018}}</ref> with songs such as "Where I Belong", "Shekinah", "All Is for Your Glory" and "Holy Ghost Party". In June 2012, Asbury moved to [[Colorado Springs, Colorado]], to join [[New Life Church (Colorado Springs, Colorado)|New Life Church]] where he began as an associate worship pastor, becoming a member of its ensembles, [[Desperation Band]] and [[New Life Worship]].

After signing with [[Bethel Music]] in 2015, Asbury made his first appearance with the Collective on ''[[Have It All (Bethel Music album)|Have It All]]'' (2016). He became more known with the release of "[[Reckless Love (song)|Reckless Love]]" in late 2017, a hit [[Single (music)|single]] that preceded his breakthrough second full-length album, also titled ''[[Reckless Love (Cory Asbury album)|Reckless Love]]'', in early 2018 which was a commercially successful release, having charted in the United States and internationally. "Reckless Love" was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Christian Music Performance/Song|Best Contemporary Christian Music Performance/Song]] in the [[61st Annual Grammy Awards|2019 Grammy Awards]]. His third studio album, ''[[To Love a Fool]]'', was released on July 31, 2020, which featured the singles, "[[The Father's House]]", "[[Sparrows (Cory Asbury song)|Sparrows]]", and "[[Dear God (Cory Asbury song)|Dear God]]".

==Early life==
Cory Asbury was born on October 15, 1985.<ref name="NLW Profile">{{cite web |url= http://nlw.dylanroopdesign.com/artists/cory-asbury/ |title= Cory Asbury {{!}} New Life Worship |website= New Life Worship |publisher= New Life Ministries |access-date= January 22, 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180213200748/http://nlw.dylanroopdesign.com/artists/cory-asbury/ |archive-date= February 13, 2018 |url-status= dead }}</ref> Music affected him profoundly in his childhood, and he developed his songwriting ability growing up in North Carolina.<ref name="Forerunner" /><ref name="NLW Profile" /> Asbury began leading worship at his local church when he was seventeen years old.<ref name="Alabaster" /><ref name="Forerunner" /> When Asbury finished high school at [[Franklin High School (North Carolina)|Franklin High School]] in 2004, he considered studying at [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill|UNC Chapel Hill]], but his friends that graduated at the Onething internship with International House of Prayer (IHOPKC) in Kansas City persuaded Asbury to attend IHOPKC instead,<ref name="Alabaster" /> so Asbury began his internship at IHOPKC on January 1, 2005,<ref name="NLW Profile" /> where he started his musical career.

==Music and ministry career==
===2005–2012: ''Holy'', ''Let Me See Your Eyes'' and IHOPKC releases===
Asbury began his career during his seven-year stay in Kansas City with the International House of Prayer, starting in the Onething internship program. He made his first appearance on an IHOPKC compilation titled ''Immersed'', released in December 2007, alongside several artists including [[Misty Edwards]] and David Brymer.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/immersed/ |title= Immersed - Forerunner Music |date= December 5, 2007 |website= Forerunner Music |publisher= International House of Prayer |access-date= February 12, 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180223110935/https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/immersed/ |archive-date= February 23, 2018 |url-status= dead }}</ref> Forerunner Music then released ''Holy'', a collaboration by Asbury with [[Matt Gilman]], on July 10, 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/products/Matt_Gilman__Cory_Asbury/Holy/117431/ |title= Holy - Matt Gilman & Cory Asbury {{!}} Cross Rhythms |website= [[Cross Rhythms]] |access-date= February 12, 2018}}</ref> The album consists of twelve tracks with Asbury and Gilman performing six each.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/holy/285930696 |title= Holy by Matt Gilman & Cory Asbury on Apple Music |date= July 10, 2008 |website= [[Apple Music]] |version= United States |publisher= [[Apple Inc.]] |access-date= February 13, 2018}}</ref>

On September 1, 2009, Asbury released his first solo album with Forerunner Music, called ''Let Me See Your Eyes'', which included appearances by Jaye Thomas, Edwin Botero and Nathan Prior.<ref name="LMSYE">{{cite web |url= https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/let-me-see-your-eyes/ |title= Let Me See Your Eyes - Forerunner Music |date= July 28, 2009 |website= Forerunner Music |publisher= International House of Prayer |access-date= February 13, 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180223110946/https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/let-me-see-your-eyes/ |archive-date= February 23, 2018 |url-status= dead }}</ref> Produced by Seth Yates,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.melodiesofheaven.com/about/ |title= ABOUT — MELODIES OF HEAVEN |website= Melodies of Heaven |access-date= February 20, 2018}}</ref> ''Let Me See Your Eyes'' is an [[indie pop]] collection which also incorporates various musical styles including [[Soul music|soul]], [[Rap music|rap]] and [[Electronic music|electro]].<ref name="LMSYE" /> The song "My Beloved", which featured Jaye Thomas, had been released as Asbury's first single from the album in July 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/my-beloved-single/ |title= My Beloved - Single - Forerunner Music |date= July 2, 2009 |website= Forerunner Music |publisher= International House of Prayer |access-date= February 13, 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180223110920/https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/my-beloved-single/ |archive-date= February 23, 2018 |url-status= dead }}</ref>

In 2010, Asbury was featured on two IHOPKC albums, ''Where I Belong'' by [[Onething conference|Onething Live]] and ''Joy'' by Forerunner Music, alongside Jaye Thomas and [[Laura Hackett Park]], on both releases. The song "Shekinah", which Asbury performed live with Jaye Thomas and was released as a part of ''Joy'' in November,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/joy/ |title= Joy - Forerunner Music |date= November 26, 2010 |website= Forerunner Music |publisher= International House of Prayer |access-date= February 12, 2018}}</ref> became their first ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' chart entry, as it peaked at No. 11 on the Gospel Digital Song Sales chart.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/gospel-digital-song-sales/2010-12-11 |title= Gospel Songs: Top Digital Song Sales Chart |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |version= Gospel Digital Song Sales |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= February 13, 2018}}<br />- {{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/music/cory-asbury/chart-history/gospel-digital-song-sales |title= Cory Asbury Chart History |website= Billboard |version= Gospel Digital Song Sales |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= February 13, 2018}}</ref> Released in 2011, Asbury appeared on ''Before His Eyes'' by Onething Live, performing "Reason to Dance".<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/onething-live-before-his-eyes/ |title= Before His Eyes (Live) - Forerunner Music |date= July 12, 2011 |website= Forerunner Music |publisher= International House of Prayer |access-date= February 12, 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180223110858/https://www.ihopkc.org/music/albums/onething-live-before-his-eyes/ |archive-date= February 23, 2018 |url-status= dead }}</ref>

On July 24, 2012, Onething Live released its third live album, ''Magnificent Obsession'', with Asbury featuring on "All Is for Your Glory" and "Never Gonna Leave Me Dry".<ref name="Magnificent Obsession NRT">{{cite web |url= http://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=11830 |title= Magnificent Obsession by onething Live |website= NewReleaseToday |publisher= NRT Media Inc. |access-date= February 12, 2018}}</ref> This album was Onething Live's breakthrough release, with the album making its debut on ''Billboard's'' [[Christian Albums]] chart at No. 17,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/christian-albums/2012-08-11 |title= Christian Albums |date= August 11, 2012 |website= Billboard |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= February 12, 2018}}</ref> and on [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Albums]] chart at No 10,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/heatseekers-albums/2012-08-11 |title= Heatseekers Albums: Up and Coming Musicians Chart|date= August 11, 2012 |website= Billboard |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= February 12, 2018}}</ref> credited as IHOPKC Worship.

Asbury released "[[Dear God (Cory Asbury song)|Dear God]]" as the third single from ''[[To Love a Fool]]'', on October 23, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/new-music/8772-cory-asbury-reimagines-dear-god-with-wife-anna |title= Cory Asbury Reimagines "Dear God" With Wife Anna |last= Longs |first= Herb |date= October 24, 2020 |website= The Christian Beat |access-date= November 4, 2020}}</ref> "Dear God" debuted at number 34 on the Hot Christian Songs chart.

On November 6, 2020, Asbury released his first live album, ''[[To Love a Fool – A Rooftop Experience]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url= https://vacancy-mag.com/cory-asbury-releases-live-album-to-love-a-fool/ |title= Cory Asbury Releases Live Album 'To Love A Fool' |last= Chavez |first= Adrianna |date= November 7, 2020 |website= Vacancy Magazine |access-date= December 4, 2020}}</ref> which contains live versions of the songs that initially appeared on ''To Love a Fool'' studio recording.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/new-music/8894-cory-asbury-drops-to-love-a-fool-a-rooftop-experience-album |title= Cory Asbury Drops 'To Love A Fool' Live Album |last= Clarks |first= Jessie |date= November 6, 2020 |website= The Christian Beat |access-date= December 4, 2020}}</ref> The album debuted at number 20 on the Top Christian Albums Chart.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/christian-albums/2020-04-11 |title= Top Christian Albums Chart {{!}} Billboard |date= April 7, 2020 |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= December 4, 2020}}</ref>

Asbury received two nominations for the [[52nd GMA Dove Awards|2021 GMA Dove Awards]],<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/awards/9613134/2021-gma-dove-awards-nominations |title= 2021 GMA Dove Award Nominees: Full List {{!}} Billboard |last= Grein |first= Paul |date= August 11, 2021 |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= August 14, 2021}}</ref> being nominated for Song of the Year for "The Father's House" and Pop/Contemporary Album of the Year for ''To Love a Fool''.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.thechristianbeat.org/bethel-music-snags-16-nominations-at-52nd-gma-dove-awards/ |title= Bethel Music Snags 16 Nominations At 52nd GMA Dove Awards - TCB |last= Clarks |first= Jessie |date= August 12, 2021 |website= The Christian Beat |access-date= August 14, 2021}}</ref> In August 2021, Asbury announced that he would be headlining the Songs and Stories tour in the United States, alongside Benjamin Hastings of [[Hillsong United]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.newreleasetoday.com/news_detail.php?newsid=5515 |title= Cory Asbury Announces Fall Tour |date= August 20, 2021 |website= NewReleaseToday |publisher= NRT Media Inc. |access-date= October 19, 2021}}</ref>
