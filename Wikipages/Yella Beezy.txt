'''Markies Deandre Conway''',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/search/workID/904029091|title=ACE Repertory|website=ascap.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dfw.cbslocal.com/2018/10/29/rapper-yella-breezy-released/|title=Rapper Yella Beezy Released From Hospital After Shooting On Dallas Highway|date=October 29, 2018|access-date=September 16, 2019}}</ref> known by the stage name '''Yella Beezy''', is an American rapper from [[Dallas]], [[Texas]]. He is best known for the singles "[[That's On Me (Yella Beezy song)|That's On Me]]",<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2018/08/07/yella-beezys-thats-debuts-billboard-hot-100/|title=Yella Beezy's "That's On Me" Debuts On Billboard Hot 100|date=August 7, 2018}}</ref> "[[Bacc At It Again]]",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/gucci-mane/chart-history/bubbling-under-hot-100/song/1125570|title=Gucci Mane|website=Billboard}}</ref> "Up One", and "Goin Through Some Thangs".

== Early life ==
Conway was raised in the [[Oak Cliff]] neighborhood of Dallas, TX. When Conway was 12, his father was murdered at their home in Oak Cliff on [[Mother's Day]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/08/yella-beezy-house-father-murdered-documentary/|title=YELLA BEEZY REVISITS HOUSE WHERE HIS FATHER WAS MURDERED IN NEW DOCUMENTARY SERIES}}</ref> Throughout his childhood he played basketball and football and his original aspiration was to become a professional football player.<ref name=":1" /> Conway became a [[Crips|Crip]] during his teenage years,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thebeatdfw.com/playlist/which-famous-rappers-are-crips-photo-gallery/|title=Which Famous Rappers Are Crips? (PHOTO GALLERY)|date=May 30, 2018|website=97.9 The Beat|language=en|access-date=August 30, 2019}}</ref> and was selling drugs to support his family.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dallasobserver.com/music/after-being-shot-in-a-drive-by-yella-beezy-is-grinding-away-11418960|title=Yella Beezy Isn't Slowing Down, Nor Is the Rise of Rapper's Popularity, After October Shooting|last=Bostick|first=Nicholas|date=December 18, 2018|website=Dallas Observer|access-date=August 30, 2019}}</ref> He was often kicked out of school for fighting and ended up [[dropping out]] of high school without his diploma.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://usatodayhss.com/2019/chop-up-yella-beezy-sits-on-the-random-hot-seat-family-feud-nfl|title=Chop-Up: Yella Beezy sits on the random/hot seat|date=February 13, 2019|website=USA TODAY High School Sports|language=en|access-date=August 30, 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== 2017–present: ''Billboard'' success and charting mixtapes ===
[[File:Yella Beezy performing.png|thumb|left|180px|Yella Breezy performing at a concert in 2018]]
Conway began writing song lyrics and rapping at the age of 13. He was inspired by artists such as [[Boosie Badazz]] and [[Kevin Gates]]. At the age of 14 he released his first [[mixtape]], ''Mash Mode Overload''. In 2012, his second mixtape, ''Lil Yella Mane'', was released. Conway's first [[hit single]] came in 2015 with "Trap in Designer" off of his mixtape ''Broke Nights Rich Days''.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/video/2019/08/yella-beezy-trap-in-designer/|title=YELLA BEEZY DETAILS THE RISE OF HIS LOCAL HIT "TRAP IN DESIGNER"}}</ref>

In 2017, Yella Beezy released his mixtape ''Lite Work, Vol. 2''. It featured the song "[[That's On Me (Yella Beezy song)|That's On Me]]", which peaked at number 56 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and also led to Yella Beezy getting signed by Chris Turner (A&R) to [[L.A. Reid]]'s label [[Hitco]].<ref name="auto1" />

In 2018, the mixtape ''Ain't No Goin Bacc'' was released. It included the hits "What I Did" (featuring [[Kevin Gates]]), "Up One" (featuring [[Lil Baby]]) and a remixed version of "That's On Me" (featuring [[2 Chainz]], [[T.I.]], and [[Boosie Badazz]]).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/albums/Yella-beezy/Aint-no-goin-bacc|title=Ain't No Goin' Bacc by Yella Beezy|website=Genius}}</ref> That same year, Yella Beezy began earning fame from opening for [[Jay-Z]] and [[Beyoncé]] in Dallas and [[Houston]] during the [[On the Run II Tour]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2018/music/news/yella-beezy-shot-three-times-l-a-reid-hitco-1202980429/|title=Yella Beezy, Rapper Signed to L.A. Reid's Hitco, Shot Three Times|last=Aswad|first=Jem|date=October 15, 2018|website=Variety|language=en|access-date=August 25, 2019}}</ref>

In 2019, the mixtape ''Baccend Beezy'' was the first mixtape by Yella Beezy that was released under his label Hitco. The mixtape featured the hit singles "[[Bacc At It Again]]" (featuring [[Quavo]] and [[Gucci Mane]]) and "Restroom Occupied" (featuring [[Chris Brown]]).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/albums/Yella-beezy/Baccend-beezy|title=Baccend Beezy by Yella Beezy|website=Genius}}</ref>

He was featured on Eritrean model [[Rubi Rose]]'s single "Hit Yo Dance" featuring [[NLE Choppa]].

== Personal life ==
On October 14, 2018, Conway was shot on the [[Sam Rayburn Tollway]] in [[Lewisville, Texas]] at around 3:30 AM. Reports say that his vehicle was fired at 23 times and Conway was hit 4 times. Conway was hospitalized and survived, while the shooter was never found.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/yella-beezy-shot-three-times-738007/|title=Yella Beezy Shot Three Times in Texas|date=October 15, 2018}}</ref>

Conway was arrested in [[Dallas]] on February 14, 2021, on weapons charges. He reportedly had five guns at the time of his arrest and was released from jail on the same day he was booked.<ref>https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/yella-beezy-arrested-in-dallas-report-news.126668.html</ref> On August 11, 2021, the rapper was arrested again with drug and weapons charges.<ref>https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/yella-beezy-arrested-on-drug-and-weapons-charges-report-news.137337.html</ref>

Conway was arrested in Plano, TX on Thursday 5 Nov 2021 and faces charges of sexual assault, child endangerment or abandonment, and unlawful carrying of a weapon.
<ref>https://www.nbcdfw.com/news/local/dallas-rapper-yella-beezy-arrested-in-collin-county/2804632/?amp</ref>
