'''Lucero''' is an [[United States|American]] [[country-rock]] band based in [[Memphis, Tennessee]], United States. Lucero's sound has been described as a "synthesis of soul, rock, and country [that] is distinctly Memphisian."<ref>{{cite web|last=Ryan |first=Kyle |url=http://www.avclub.com/articles/lucero-women-work,70673/ |title=Lucero: Women & Work &#124; Music &#124; MusicalWork Review |publisher=The A.V. Club |date=2012-03-13 |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref> They have released 11 albums and one live DVD, mostly through their own label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allmusic.com/artist/lucero-p476877/discography |title=Lucero &#124; Music Biography, Credits and Discography |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref> The band mainly tours around North America.

==Biography==
===1998-2007: Formation and early years===
Lucero had their start in Memphis, TN and played for the first time in early 1998. Since then, they have been called "one of the hardest working bands of the last 10 years—on tour significantly more days than they are not."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2012/03/album-review-lucero-women-work/ |title=Album Review: Lucero – Women & Work « Consequence of Sound |publisher=Consequenceofsound.net |date=2012-03-20 |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref>

===2000: The Attic Tapes===
The Attic Tapes was recorded in 2000 at Guitarist, Brian Venable's Dads attic. It was recorded straight to an 8 track cassette and will be self-released. This album will later be reissued in 2006 by Liberty & Lament. It includes the cover of the Jawbreaker song, “Kiss The Bottle.”

===2001: Lucero===
The Self titled album was recorded by Empty Road Music in 2000. The record includes such popular tracks as “My Best Girl”, “Raising Hell”, “Drink ‘Til We’re Gone” & “All Sewn Up.”

===2002: Tennessee===
Tennessee was recorded in 2001 by Madjack Records. It's one of Lucero’s most beloved albums by fans. It's a thirteen song album that includes such tracks as “Sweet Little Thing”, “Nights Like These”, “The Last Song” & “Here At the Starlite.”

===2003: That Much Further West===
"That Much Further West" was recorded in 2003 by Tiger Style Records. It includes 12 tracks. Guitarist Brian Venable took a break from the band so Todd Gill filled in on guitar for this record. The record includes popular songs such as "That Much Further West", "Across the River", "Tonight Ain't Gonna Be Good" & a fan favorite "Tears Don't Matter Much."

===2005: Nobody’s Darlings===
Nobody’s Darlings was released in 2005 & recorded by Liberty & Lament. It's a twelve track record that includes such tracks as “Bikeriders”, “Last Night In Town” & “The War.” "Bikeriders" is based on the book "The Bikeriders" by author Danny Lyon. “The War” is about, Lucero frontman, Ben Nichols grandfather & what he went through during his time serving in the army in Europe during World War II.

===2006: Rebels, Rogues & Sworn Brothers===
"Rebels, Rogues & Sworn Brothers" was recorded in 2006 by Liberty & Lament. This is a twelve song album that includes such tracks as “What Else Would You Have Me Be?”, “I Don’t Wanna Be the One”, “I Can Get Us Out of Here”, “1979”, & “She’s Just That Kinda Girl.”

===2008-2009: ''1372 Overton Park''===
In late 2008, the band announced they had signed a four-album deal with [[Universal Music Group]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Horton |first=Becca |url=http://www.memphisflyer.com/memphis/lucero-goes-major---has-picnic/Content?oid=1146776 |title=Lucero Goes Major, &#124; Has Picnic &#124; Music &#124; Memphis News and Events |publisher=Memphis Flyer |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref> though the relationship with Universal was short-lived.<ref>{{cite web|author=Jac Chebatoris |url=http://articles.latimes.com/2012/mar/21/entertainment/la-et-0321-lucero-20120321 |title=Rocking Memphis-style - Los Angeles Times |publisher=Articles.latimes.com |date=2012-03-21 |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref> ''1372 Overton Park'' was released October 6, 2009 by [[Universal Music Group]] and was the first Lucero album to feature a horn section.

===2012: ''Women & Work''===
Lucero's album, ''Women & Work'', was released on March 13, 2012 by ATO Records. This album integrates more of the horn section as well as the pedal steel guitar, keyboards and a gospel chorus.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052702304450004577279692149441670 |title=Lucero's 'Women & Work' Recalls Scaggs and Seger &#124; The Short List - WSJ.com |publisher=Online.wsj.com |date=2012-03-15 |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.luceromusic.com/site/about/ |title=About &#124; Lucero |publisher=Luceromusic.com |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref>

===2013: Texas & Tennessee-EP===
This four song EP was released in the spring of 2013 by ATO Records. It includes the songs; “Texas & Tennessee”, “Union Pacific Line”, “Breathless Love” & “The Other Side of Lonesome.”

===2015: ''All a Man Should Do''===
Lucero's ninth album, ''[[All a Man Should Do]]'', was released on September 18, 2015. It continued the Memphis-soul influence as seen in the previous two albums. The album included a cover of ''I'm In Love With A Girl'', originally recorded by fellow Memphis band, [[Big Star]]. The cover, which includes a line taken for the album title, featured backing vocals from Big Star drummer, [[Jody Stephens]]. It was recorded in [[Ardent Studios]] which is based in Memphis and managed by Stephens.<ref>{{cite web|author=Mark Deming|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/all-a-man-should-do-mw0002856070 |title=AllMusic - All A Man Should Do|publisher=allmusic.com|access-date=2019-11-12}}</ref>

===2018: ''Among the Ghosts''===
''Among the Ghosts'' was released August 3, 2018. The album was produced by [[Matt Ross-Spang]] and recorded live in a single room at [[Phillips Recording|Sam Phillip's Recording]], Memphis.<ref name="NPRAmongTheGhosts">{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/07/26/629767639/first-listen-lucero-among-the-ghosts 
 |title=Lucero's 'Among The Ghosts' Does A Little Musical Demolition |publisher=[[NPR]]| first=Brittney | last=McKenna |date=2018-07-26 |access-date=2020-05-07}}</ref> The sound is more stripped back than the previous three, with no brass section. The track "Back to the Night" features spoken word lyrics from actor [[Michael Shannon]]. Shannon is a frequent collaborator of frontman Ben Nichols brother, film director [[Jeff Nichols]]. The song ''Loving'', had previously featured on the soundtrack to Jeff Nichols film, ''[[Loving (2016 film)|Loving]],'' as a solo Ben Nichols song.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2016/11/tyyuguygi.html|title=A track from the Loving soundtrack|website=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]|last=Paste Editors|date=November 9, 2016|access-date=January 8, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Mark Deming|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/among-the-ghosts-mw0003179857 |title=AllMusic - Among The Ghosts|publisher=allmusic.com|access-date=2019-11-12}}</ref>

2019 saw Lucero undertake their first ever Australian headlining tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lovepolice.com.au/lucero-2019/ |title=LUCERO 2019|website=Lovepolice.com.au|access-date=2019-11-12}}</ref>

===2021: ''When You Found Me''===
On October 23, 2020, Lucero announced the release of their eleventh album, ''When You Found Me'', alongside the launch of a video for the song ''Outrun The Moon''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/lucero-announce-new-album-when-you-found-me-stream-a-track/|title=Lucero announce new album, ‘When You Found Me’ (stream a track)|website=[[BrooklynVegan]]|last=Sacher|first=Andrew|date=October 23, 2020|access-date=February 1, 2021}}</ref> ''When You Found Me'' was released on January 29, 2021. Like their previous album, ''Among the Ghosts'', ''When You Found Me'' was produced by [[Matt Ross-Spang]], recorded at [[Phillips Recording|Sam Phillip's Recording]] and released on their own label, Liberty & Lament. [[Rolling Stone]] states that he album "continues the darker trend started by ''Among the Ghosts'' and feels more intimate than raucous, horn-driven Lucero records like ''Women & Work'' and ''1372 Overton Park''" and that it "updates Lucero’s sound in interesting ways with the addition of synthesizers".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/lucero-new-album-when-you-found-me-1120598/|title=Lucero Look to Warren Zevon and Synthesizers on New Album ‘When You Found Me’
|website=[[Rolling Stone]]|last=Freeman|first=Jon|date=January 29, 2021|access-date=February 1, 2021}}</ref>

==Collaborations and other projects==

The first solo release from frontman Ben Nichols, ''[[The Last Pale Light in the West]]'', was released in January 2009 on Lucero's label [[Liberty & Lament]]. On November 17, 2013 the title song was featured in the episode "[[Live Bait (The Walking Dead)|Live Bait]]" of the AMC series ''[[The Walking Dead (TV series)|The Walking Dead]]''. The seven-song record was inspired by [[Cormac McCarthy]]'s book ''[[Blood Meridian]]'' and recorded with Rick Steff on piano and accordion and Todd Beane on pedal steel.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/The-Last-Pale-Light-West/dp/B001MW0IWU/ref=sr_1_fkmr0_1?s=music&ie=UTF8&qid=1332446691&sr=1-1-fkmr0 |title=The Last Pale Light In The West: Music |publisher=Amazon.com |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref>

In May 2009, Nichols co-starred in MTV's [[$5 Cover]], a Craig Brewer-produced, quasi-fictionalized series about the Memphis music scene. A performance of Lucero's song "San Francisco" at the Young Avenue Deli in Memphis was featured in the trailer for the series.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://5dollarcoveramplified.com/picture/ben/ |title=$5 Cover Amplified |publisher=5dollarcoveramplified.com |access-date=2013-08-08 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110906203324/http://5dollarcoveramplified.com/picture/ben/ |archive-date=2011-09-06 }}</ref>

Ben Nichols's previous band was Red 40 in which he played alongside [[Colin Brooks (drummer)|Colin Brooks]] and Steve Kooms.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Red+40 |title=Red 40 – Free listening, concerts, stats, & pictures at |publisher=Last.fm |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref>

Drummer Roy Berry is half of experimental duo Overjoid<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ninebullets.net/archives/guest-post-overjoid-roy-luceros-drummerss-side-project |title=Guest Post: Overjoid (Roy, Lucero’S Drummers’S, Side Project) |publisher=ninebullets.net |date=2009-02-11 |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref> and was a member of the Memphis-based band The Simple Ones before joining Lucero.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Worth-Weight-The-Simple-Ones/dp/B00000218U |title=Worth the Weight: The Simple Ones: Music |publisher=Amazon.com |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref>

John C. Stubblefield has recorded with Jello 1-2-3 (live at some high school dance), North Mississippi Allstars, Jim Dickinson and BigAssTruck (Sack Lunch EP), among others. He co-produced Hill Country Revue’s 2010 album Zebra Ranch.<ref>{{cite web|author=John C. Stubblefield |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/john-c-stubblefield-p518552/credits |title=John C. Stubblefield &#124; Credits |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref>

Rick Steff has recorded with Cat Power, Hank Williams Jr. and Dexys Midnight Runners, among others.

Todd Beene is also currently a member of the Tennessee band Glossary <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.glossary.us/about |title=ABOUT &#124; Glossary |publisher=Glossary.us |access-date=2013-08-08}}</ref> and [[Chuck Ragan]]'s band.
