'''Zombie Ghost Train''' (sometimes seen as '''ZGT''') were an [[Australian]] [[psychobilly]] and [[gothabilly]] band located in [[Sydney]], NSW, Australia. The band formed with Stu Arkoff (guitars and vocals), Azzy T (drums), and Captain Reckless (bass and backing vocals). In 2007, Azzy T left to pursue other musical ventures and was replaced by JM. The band released two albums, ''Glad Rags & Body Bags'' (April 2004) and ''Dealing the Death Card'' (May 2007),<ref name=ravemag-iv>{{cite web|last=Valentino|first=Bianca|title=Zombie Ghost Train|url=http://www.ravemagazine.com.au/content/view/4013/30/|work=Rave Magazine|access-date=30 May 2011|date=12 June 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120316120148/http://www.ravemagazine.com.au/content/view/4013/30/ |archive-date=2012-03-16}}</ref> and a seven-track extended play, ''Monster Formal Wear'' (2003).

==History==

Zombie Ghost Train were formed in Sydney as a [[gothabilly]] and [[psychobilly]] group by Stu Arkoff (a.k.a. Stuart Hibberd) on lead vocals, guitar and [[theremin]], Captain Reckless (a.k.a. Aaron Mol) on [[double bass|slap bass]] and backing vocals, and AzzA-T on drums and percussion.<ref name="NLA Glad">{{cite web | url = http://trove.nla.gov.au/work/36659575?sortby=dateAsc&q=%22Zombie+Ghost+Train%22&c=music&versionId=47541485 | title = ''Glad Rags & Body Bags'' &#91;sound recording&#93; / Zombie Ghost Train – Version Details | work = Trove | publisher = Shrunken Head Records | access-date = 5 November 2017 | via = [[National Library of Australia]] }}</ref> Arkoff and Reckless were former members of the Ark Off Experiment.<ref name="Syd Crew">{{cite web | url = http://www.oocities.org/sydneyriotcrew/OldNews.htm | title = Old News | date = 17 February 2003 | work = Sydney Riot Crew | access-date = 5 November 2017 }}</ref> The band toured Australia, Europe and the United States.<ref name=elpaso-usa-tour>{{cite web|title=PRESS RELEASE for Zombie Ghost Train USA Tour 2006|url=http://www.elpasomusicscene.com/press-releases/zombie-ghost-train-110206.html|work=El Paso Music Scene|access-date=31 May 2011}}</ref><ref>[http://www.ravemagazine.com.au/component/option,com_mamblog/Itemid,116/task,show/action,view/id,3754/ Rave Magazine<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070607014740/http://www.ravemagazine.com.au/component/option,com_mamblog/Itemid,116/task,show/action,view/id,3754/ |date=7 June 2007 }}</ref><ref name=fasterlouder2007>{{cite web|last=melbjuz|title=Zombie Ghost Train return to Australia!|url=http://www.fasterlouder.com.au/news/local/10851/Zombie-Ghost-Train-return-to-Australia|work=Faster Louder|publisher=FasterLouder Pty Ltd|access-date=30 May 2011|date=2007-10-14}}</ref> The band members, when appearing on stage, dress in "zombified" clothes, featuring tears and bloodstains. Each member has his hair in an extreme style of a [[quiff]], [[teddy boy cut]] or [[Pompadour (hairstyle)|pompadour]]. They wore zombie make up, complete with stitches across the face.<ref>[http://www.hairball8.com/images/ZGT.htm Zombie Ghost Train photos<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In 2008, one of Sydney's city guides, TwoThousand, called the band, "Australia's best kept secret. . . These mortuary musicians are the best thing psychobilly has seen since [[The Cramps]]".<ref name=twothousand>{{cite web|last=Morgan|first=Hayley|title=Zombie Ghost Train|url=http://www.twothousand.com.au/out/zombie-ghost-train/|work=TwoThousand|access-date=30 May 2011|date=2008-04-14}}</ref>

Zombie Ghost Train were featured in the [[Horror punk]] segment of interviews on the ''Tribal Area 8'' DVD compiled by German video-magazine, ''Tribal Area''.<ref name=tribalarea>{{cite web|title=Tribal Area 8 - DVD|url=http://www.tribalarea.de/en/aeltere-ausgaben/item/21-tribal-area-8-dvd.html|work=Tribal Area|access-date=31 May 2011}}</ref> In an interview with Voltage Media, Sydney band, ''The Horrorwood Mannequins'' listed playing with Zombie Ghost Train as one of their biggest moments in their music career.<ref name=voltage>{{cite web|last=ByronJC|title=HWM Sydney's Sickest Symphony|url=http://www.voltagemedia.com.au/news/2010/10/30/hwm-sydneys-sickest-symphony|work=Voltage Media|publisher=VOLTAGEMEDIA PTY LTD|access-date=31 May 2011|date=2010-10-30}}</ref> Collapse Board classed the band's style as being part of "the horror aesthetic and supernatural lyric themes" of psychobilly.<ref name=collapse>{{cite web|last=Fiend|first=Tom|title=Journey to the centre of a band|url=http://www.collapseboard.com/journey-to-the-centre-of-a-band/|work=Collapse Board|access-date=31 May 2011|date=10 September 2010}}</ref>

Zombie Ghost Train released an EP called ''Monster Formal Wear'' in 2003 and licensed to German label, ''Crazy Love Records'' (<small>CLLP 64204</small>).<ref name=dicogs-monster-formal-wear>{{cite web|title=Zombie Ghost Train – Monster Formal Wear|url=http://www.discogs.com/Zombie-Ghost-Train-Monster-Formal-Wear/release/2378893|work=Discogs|publisher=Discogs|access-date=31 May 2011}}</ref> Their first album, ''Glad Rags & Body Bags'', was released in 2005 on German Horrorpunk label, [[:de:Fiend Force Records|Fiend Force Records]] (<small>FF-033</small>).<ref name=discogs-bodybags>{{cite web|title=Zombie Ghost Train – Glad Rags & Body Bags|url=http://www.discogs.com/Zombie-Ghost-Train-Glad-Rags-Body-Bags/release/2378911|work=Discogs|publisher=Discogs|access-date=31 May 2011}}</ref> The song ''Girl U Want'' received the most airplay on National Australian radio station Triple J from this album, with a ranking of 7763.<ref name=jplay>{{cite web|title=Zombie Ghost Train song listing @ J Play|url=http://www.jplay.com.au/JSite/ViewArtist.aspx?ArtistID=934|work=J Play|access-date=31 May 2011}}</ref>

''Dealing the Death Card'' was the second and final studio album by Zombie Ghost Train. It was released on 19 May 2007 on [[Resist Records]] in Australia and in Germany on ''Fiend Force Records''.<ref name=discogs-deathcard>{{cite web|title=Zombie Ghost Train – Dealing The Death Card|url=http://www.discogs.com/Zombie-Ghost-Train-Dealing-The-Death-Card/release/2492512|work=Discogs|publisher=Discogs|access-date=31 May 2011}}</ref> The band launched this album during an Australian tour between May and June 2007.<ref name=liveguide-austour>{{cite web|title='Dealing The Death Card' Album Launch Tour Zombie Ghost Train|url=http://www.liveguide.com.au/Tours/491893/Zombie_Ghost_Train/Dealing_The_Death_Card_Album_Launch_Tour?event_id=491903#details|work=Live Guide|access-date=31 May 2011}}</ref>

'''Reformation and final shows:''' The original lineup of Stu, Reckless and Azzy T toured with Tiger Army in March 2008 playing shows in Brisbane, Sydney, Adelaide, and Melbourne. The final ZGT show was at Sydney's Oxford Art Factory on 24 April 2008.

The band announced their breakup on 26 April 2009 on their Myspace blog.<ref name=myspace-blog>{{cite web|last=ZGT|title=Farewell from Zombie Ghost Train|url=http://blogs.myspace.com/index.cfm?fuseaction=blog.view&friendId=13538121&blogId=485533523|work=Myspace - Zombie Ghost Train's Blog|publisher=Myspace|access-date=31 May 2011|date=2009-04-26}}</ref> Their logo can be spotted on the leather jacket of an extra in the film [[Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 1]]
