'''[[Kate McGarrigle]]''' (February 6, 1946 – January 18, 2010) and '''[[Anna McGarrigle]]''' (born December 4, 1944) were a duo of Canadian singer-songwriters (and sisters) from [[Quebec]], who performed until Kate McGarrigle's death on January 18, 2010.

{{TOClimit|3}}

==Music career==
In the 1960s, in Montreal, while Kate was studying engineering at [[McGill University]] and Anna art at the [[École des beaux-arts de Montréal]], they began performing in public and writing their own songs. From 1963 to 1967 they teamed up with [[Jack Nissenson]] and Peter Weldon to form the folk group [[Mountain City Four]].

Their songs have been covered by a variety of artists including [[Linda Ronstadt]],<ref name="star">"McGarrigle sisters writing a memoir". ''Toronto Daily Star'', April 14, 2014, E2.</ref> [[Emmylou Harris]],<ref name="star" /> [[Judy Collins]],<ref name="star" /> and others. These covers led to the McGarrigles getting their first recording contract in 1974. Although associated with Quebec's [[Anglo-Quebecer|anglophone]] community, they also recorded and performed many songs in French. Two of their albums, ''Entre la jeunesse et la sagesse'' and ''La vache qui pleure'', are entirely in French.

Their version of [[Wade Hemsworth]]'s song, "[[The Log Driver's Waltz]]" grew famous as the soundtrack for a 1979 animated film directed by [[John Weldon (animator)|John Weldon]] at Canada's [[National Film Board]]. They provided backing vocals on [[Nick Cave and the Bad Seeds]]'s 2001 album ''[[No More Shall We Part]]''.

They continued to write, record and perform music into the 21st century, with assorted accompanying artists including [[Gerry Conway (musician)|Gerry Conway]], [[Pat Donaldson]], Ken Pearson, Michel Pépin, Chaim Tannenbaum and [[Joel Zifkin]].<ref name="Lanken"/>

==Personal lives==
[[Anna McGarrigle|Anna]] and [[Kate McGarrigle]] were born in [[Montreal]] of mixed Irish- and French-Canadian background. They grew up in [[Saint-Sauveur-des-Monts, Quebec|Saint-Sauveur]], where they learned piano from nuns. 

Kate McGarrigle was married in 1971 to singer-songwriter [[Loudon Wainwright III]]. Their children, [[Rufus Wainwright|Rufus]] and [[Martha Wainwright|Martha]], are both singers too. The two divorced in 1976. Kate McGarrigle died in 2010, aged 63, of sarcoma, a rare form of cancer.<ref name="KMcGGuardianOb" /><ref name="reuters-kate-obit">{{cite web |author1=Reuters Staff |title=Kate McGarrigle, Rufus Wainwright's mother, dies |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-canada-mcgarrigle-idUSTRE60I4LM20100119 |publisher=Reuters |access-date=1 January 2021 |date=19 January 2010}}</ref>

Anna McGarrigle is married to Canadian journalist and author [[Dane Lanken]]. The couple have two children, Lily Lanken and Sylvan Lanken, and live in [[North Glengarry, Ontario|North Glengarry]], Ontario, just west of the Quebec border. Dane appeared as a vocalist on several of the sisters' albums and in 2007 wrote their career biography.

Another sister, [[Jane McGarrigle]], is a film and television composer who acted as business manager for Kate and Anna, and also wrote and performed several songs with the duo.<ref name="Lanken"/>{{rp|114}}

==Honours and awards==
They were appointed Members of the [[Order of Canada]] in 1993 and received the [[Governor General's Performing Arts Award]] in 2004.<ref>{{cite web|author=Betty Nygaard King|url=https://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/kate-and-anna-mcgarrigle-emc|title=Kate and Anna McGarrigle|publisher=[[Encyclopedia of Music in Canada]]|access-date=August 17, 2019}}</ref>

On November 22, 2006, they received the Lifetime Achievement Award at the 2006 [[SOCAN]] Awards in Toronto.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.socan.ca/about/awards/2006-socan-awards |title=Archived copy |access-date=June 10, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160810141132/http://www.socan.ca/about/awards/2006-socan-awards |archive-date=August 10, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

On November 18, 2010, they were recipients of the [[Mojo (magazine)|Mojo Roots Award]], presented by Emmylou Harris. The award was accepted by Anna McGarrigle and Kate's two children Rufus and Martha Wainwright, as Kate had died early in the year on January 18.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The MOJO Roots Award presented to Kate and Anna McGarrigle by Emmylou Harris|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jED2YMKiP3E|language=en|publisher=YouTube|access-date=October 4, 2019}}</ref>
