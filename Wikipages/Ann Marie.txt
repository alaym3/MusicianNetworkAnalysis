'''Joann Marie Slater''' (born November 20, 1995),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ann-marie-mn0003836079/biography|title=Ann Marie {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=November 16, 2019}}</ref> known professionally as '''Ann Marie''', is an American singer signed to [[Interscope Records]]. She is best known for her single "Secret", which has garnered over 50 million views on [[YouTube]] and peaked at number 22 on the [[R&B Songs|''Billboard'' Hot R&B Songs]] chart.<ref name=":0">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/r-and-b-songs|title=R&B Songs Chart|magazine=Billboard|date=January 2, 2013|access-date=November 16, 2019}}</ref> Her debut EP ''Pretty Psycho'' was released on July 10, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/biz/news/rb-singer-ann-marie-signs-with-interscope-records-exclusive-1203245141/|title=R&B Singer Ann Marie Signs With Interscope Records (EXCLUSIVE)|last=Diep|first=Eric|date=June 17, 2019|website=Variety|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Ann Marie was born Joann Marie Slater in [[Englewood, Chicago]], [[Illinois]]. She is one of eight children raised by a single mother. She began singing at an early age, crediting her uncle for encouraging her to recite [[Whitney Houston]]'s "[[My Love Is Your Love (song)|My Love Is Your Love]]" repeatedly.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/ann-marie-806472/|title=Ann Marie Is an Insanely Popular R&B Singer Hiding in Plain Sight|last=Holmes|first=Charles|date=March 22, 2019|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
In December 2020, Slater was charged with possession of a gun and aggravated assault with a deadly weapon in connection to an Atlanta hotel shooting of childhood friend and alleged boyfriend, Jonathon "Sandillo" Wright. Slater allegedly told an officer that a "gun fell off the table in the hotel room where the shooting happened".<ref>{{Cite web|last=https://hiphopdx.com|first=HipHopDX-|date=December 8, 2020|title=Ann Marie's Alleged Shooting Victim Officially Identified|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.59420/title.ann-maries-alleged-shooting-victim-officially-identified|access-date=December 8, 2020|website=HipHopDX}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=R&B singer arrested in connection to a Buckhead hotel shooting|url=https://www.cbs46.com/news/r-b-singer-arrested-in-connection-to-a-buckhead-hotel-shooting/article_ee8eedd8-38be-11eb-81d2-2bc2cb979de3.html|access-date=December 8, 2020|website=CBS46 News Atlanta|language=en}}</ref> She was later released on bond.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Abusaid|first1=Shaddi|last2=Journal-Constitution|first2=The Atlanta|title=Singer charged in friend's shooting at Buckhead hotel released on bond|url=https://www.ajc.com/news/singer-charged-in-friends-shooting-at-buckhead-hotel-released-on-bond/MQIC2CV2RBBE3LISIUZ4DLBTWE/|access-date=December 12, 2020|website=ajc|language=English}}</ref>

== Influences ==
Slater has stated that she draws inspiration from 90s R&B, especially from artists like [[Aaliyah]], [[Jagged Edge (American group)|Jagged Edge]], [[SWV]], [[Xscape (vocal group)|Xscape]], [[TLC (group)|TLC]], and [[Lil' Kim]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Ann Marie Takes The 90s R&B Quiz|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=t6Bl3JXV4iQ&feature=youtu.be}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Up Close & Personal: Ann Marie (lost interview)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oBU4whq_RAI|language=en|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref>
