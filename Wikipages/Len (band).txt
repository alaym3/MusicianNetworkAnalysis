'''Len''' is a Canadian [[alternative rock]] [[duet (music)|duo]] based in [[Toronto]], Ontario. The band consists of siblings [[Marc Costanzo]] (vocals, guitar) and Sharon Costanzo (vocals, bass) and a revolving lineup of touring and studio musicians.<ref name="Inc.1999">{{cite magazine|author=Carrie Bell|title=The Modern Age|magazine=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fAgEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA97|date=13 November 1999|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|page=97|issn=0006-2510}}</ref>

==History==
Len was formed in 1991 in Toronto as a punk band.<ref>[https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1999-sep-12-ca-9142-story.html "POP MUSIC : Len’s Day in the Sun"].  ''Los Angeles Times'', By STEVE APPLEFORD. Sep. 12, 1999</ref> Switching to mainly rock and pop music, over the next several years they released two albums, an EP and several singles on Funtrip Records,<ref>[https://www.stereogum.com/1877413/behind-the-music-steal-my-sunshine/franchises/weird-90s/ "Behind The Music: Steal My Sunshine"]. ''Stereogum'', Peter Helman | May 19, 2016</ref> including 1995's "Candy Pop".<ref>[https://www.vice.com/en_ca/article/exvx4z/a-completely-biased-ranking-of-the-60-best-canrock-songs-ever "A Completely Biased Ranking of the 60 Best CanRock Songs Ever, Part I"]. ''Vice'', by Cam Lindsay. Sep 26 2016</ref>

Len released a more hip-hop oriented album, ''[[You Can't Stop the Bum Rush]]'' in 1999;<ref>[https://newspaperarchive.com/winnipeg-free-press-jun-30-2017-p-68/ "STEAL MY SUNSHINE LEN You Can’t Stop the Bum Rush (1999)"]. ''Winnipeg Free Press'', via Newspaper Archives. June 30, 2017 - Page 68</ref> a song from the album, "[[Steal My Sunshine]]", was also released that year, and climbed the popular charts both in Canada and the United States.<ref>[http://www.bac-lac.gc.ca/eng/discover/films-videos-sound-recordings/rpm/Pages/item.aspx?IdNumber=5948& "Top Singles"]. ''RPM Magazine'',  - Volume 69, No. 11. Jul 05, 1999</ref><ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/calgary/how-steal-my-sunshine-became-an-accidental-summer-hit-1.1373237 "How Steal My Sunshine became an accidental summer hit"]. Chris dela Torre, ''Calgary Eyeopener'', CBC Radio One, Jul 25, 2013</ref>  Len was nominated for a 2000 [[Juno Award]] as best new group,<ref name="Inc.2000">{{cite journal|last=LeBlanc|first=Larry|title=Morissette leads Canada's Juno nomination list|journal=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=4Q0EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA45|date=12 February 2000|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|pages=45–|issn=0006-2510}}</ref> and the album was nominated as Best Alternative Album.<ref>[https://archive.macleans.ca/article/2000/3/13/rocking-good-news "Rocking Good News"]. ''Macleans Magazine'', page 64. Nicholas Jennings, March 13, 2000</ref>

At one point, the band's lineup included [[Brendan Canning]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://altmusic.about.com/od/reviews/fr/brendancanning.htm |title=Brendan Canning Something for All of Us - Review of Brendan Canning's Album Something for All of Us |publisher=Altmusic.about.com |date=2008-07-22 |access-date=2014-08-11 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140711013236/http://altmusic.about.com/od/reviews/fr/brendancanning.htm |archive-date=2014-07-11 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stereogum.com/8670/video_hangover_drinking_in_la_bran_van_3000/video/ |title=Video Hangover: "Drinking In L.A." / "Steal My Sunshine" |publisher=Stereogum |date= April 2008|access-date=2014-08-11}}</ref> who co-founded the indie rock band [[Broken Social Scene]] shortly after leaving Len.

Len produced no further hits, but continued to release albums until 2005, followed by a seven-year hiatus.<ref>[https://aestheticmagazinetoronto.com/2012/10/27/len-looking-forward-with-no-regrets/ "Len: Looking Forward With No Regrets"]. ''Aesthetic Magazine'' ⋅ October 27, 2012 By: Curtis Sindrey –</ref>

The group became active again in 2011. The following year the group released a music video, "It's My Neighbourhood",<ref>Steve Kupferman. [https://torontoist.com/2012/10/len-a-neglected-local-one-hit-wonder-releases-a-new-toronto-centric-music-video/ "Len, a Neglected Local One-Hit Wonder, Releases a New Toronto-Centric Music Video"]. ''Torontoist'', October 16, 2012</ref> and an album ''It's Easy if You Try''.<ref name=kane>[https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2012/10/23/remember_len_onehit_wonder_toronto_band_is_back_with_new_music.html "Remember Len? One-hit wonder Toronto band is back with new music"]. ''Toronto Star'', By Laura Kane, Oct. 23, 2012</ref>
