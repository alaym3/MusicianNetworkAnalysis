'''Gazzy Garcia''' (born August 17, 2000), known professionally as '''Lil Pump''', is an American rapper. He is one of the most prominent members of the [[mumble rap|SoundCloud rap]] scene and is known for his minimalist music and hyperactive public persona, where he is often portrayed taking drugs such as [[marijuana]], [[Purple drank|lean]], and [[Alprazolam|xanax]], actions that have garnered controversy.<ref>{{cite news |last1=F |first1=Matt |title=Lil Pump Tells Fans How Much Weed He's Smoking Per Day |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-pump-tells-fans-how-much-weed-hes-smoking-per-day-news.40892.html |access-date=April 6, 2019 |agency=hotnewhiphop |date=December 14, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Anís |first1=Ariela |title=Lil Pump Celebrates Weight Loss After Quitting Lean Addiction For Good |url=https://thehollywoodunlocked.com/lil-pump-weight-loss-quitting-lean-addiction/ |access-date=April 6, 2019 |agency=thehollywoodunlocked |date=December 19, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Lamarre |first1=Carl |title=Lil Pump Says He's Giving up Xanax for the New Year: 'F--- Xanax 2018' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8085795/lil-pump-instagram-post-quits-xanax-smokepurpp-2018 |access-date=April 6, 2019 |agency=Billboard |date=January 2, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://metro.co.uk/2019/05/24/lil-pump-smoking-petrol-station-one-dumbest-things-seen-9676494/|title=Lil Pump smoking in a petrol station is one of the dumbest things we've seen|date=May 24, 2019|website=Metro|language=en-US|access-date=July 22, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Gray |first1=Geordie |title=Lil Pump has posted a video of himself pissing over $100k |url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/lil-pump-pissing-on-money/ |access-date=April 6, 2019 |agency=Tone Deaf |date=November 29, 2018}}</ref>

Lil Pump rose to popularity in 2017 with the single "[[Gucci Gang]]", which peaked at number 3 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and was certified [[Recording Industry Association of America certification|five-times platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]].<ref name=platinum>{{cite web |title=Gucci Gang |url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=&ti=gucci+gang&lab=&genre=&format=&date_option=release&from=&to=&award=&type=&category=&adv=SEARCH#search_section |publisher=Recording Industry Association of America |access-date=September 28, 2018}}</ref> The single was featured on his debut album ''[[Lil Pump (album)|Lil Pump]]'' (2017), which peaked at number 3 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. He has since released the singles "[[I Love It (Kanye West and Lil Pump song)|I Love It]]" (with [[Kanye West]]), "[[Esskeetit]]", "[[Drug Addicts (song)|Drug Addicts]]", "[[Butterfly Doors]]", "[[Racks on Racks]]", "[[Be Like Me]]" (featuring [[Lil Wayne]]), and "[[Welcome to the Party (Diplo, French Montana and Lil Pump song)|Welcome to the Party]]" from the ''[[Deadpool 2]]'' soundtrack.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Margaritoff |first1=Marco |title=Watch Scenes From 'Deadpool 2' in Diplo and French Montana's "Welcome to the Party" Video |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/05/watch-scenes-deadpool-2-diplo-french-montana-welcome-to-the-party-video |access-date=February 3, 2019 |agency=Complex |date=May 21, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Hot100">{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/music/lil-pump/chart-history |title=Lil Pump – Chart History: Billboard Hot 100 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=October 30, 2017}}</ref> His second studio album ''[[Harverd Dropout]]'' (2019) peaked at number 7 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

==Early life==
Garcia was born August 17, 2000, in [[Miami]], [[Florida]].<ref name="Happy Birthday, Lil Pump! - XXL"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lil-pump-mn0003626782/biography|title=Lil Pump {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=November 7, 2018}}</ref> In a 2018 interview with [[J. Cole]], Garcia stated that his parents are from [[Colombia]] and they divorced when he was six years old.

When Garcia was thirteen years old, his cousin, Lil Ominous, introduced him to Omar Pineiro, better known as [[Smokepurpp]]; the two eventually became collaborators. Garcia and Pineiro were expelled from multiple district schools. Garcia, thereafter, enrolled in an [[Opportunity school|opportunity high school]] but was expelled in the tenth grade for fighting and inciting a [[riot]].<ref>{{Citation|first=Cole |last=Bennett|title=Smokepurpp & Lil Pump: The Lyrical Lemonade Interview|date=September 18, 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aDq88uhypL4&t=12s| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211117/aDq88uhypL4| archive-date=2021-11-17 | url-status=live|access-date=December 30, 2017 |via=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

==Career==
===Career beginnings (2016)===
Garcia's rap career began when Smokepurpp produced a track and asked him to [[Freestyle rap|freestyle]] over it.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://gulf-times.com/story/578039|title=Lil Pump, hottest American Rapper|date=January 14, 2018|website=Gulf-Times|language=ar|access-date=February 25, 2019}}</ref> The single produced was independently released in 2016 on the music streaming website [[SoundCloud]], as his debut single, "Lil Pump".<ref name="Turner 2017">{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/look-at-me-the-noisy-soundcloud-revolution-changing-rap-w485101|title=Look At Me!: The Noisy, Blown-Out SoundCloud Revolution Redefining Rap|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=October 10, 2017|date=June 1, 2017|first=David|last=Turner}}</ref> Garcia quickly followed the song with singles "Elementary", "Ignorant", "Gang Shit", and "Drum$tick", each garnering over three million streams.<ref name="soundcloud.com">{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/wavey-hefner/tracks|title=Lil Pump's Soundcloud profile|publisher=SoundCloud|access-date=November 19, 2017}}</ref> The success of his tracks on SoundCloud earned him recognition among the [[South Florida]] [[underground rap]] scene, in a style known as "[[SoundCloud rap]]". He co-headlined the No Jumper tour in 2016, and also performed at the [[Rolling Loud]] Festival.<ref name="HNHH">{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/everything-you-need-to-know-about-lil-pump-news.31687.html?|title=Everything You Need To Know About Lil Pump|last=Schwartz|first=Danny|date=April 26, 2017|access-date=July 15, 2017|publisher=HotNewHipHop}}</ref><ref name="XXLChief">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/05/chief-keef-lil-pump-hit-the-studio/|title=Chief Keef and Lil Pump Hit the Studio|last=Berry|first=Peter A.|date=May 18, 2017|access-date=July 15, 2017|magazine=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL Mag]]}}</ref>

===Rising popularity and ''Lil Pump'' (2017)===
Garcia began gaining attention in 2017 by releasing the singles "[[D Rose (song)|D Rose]]" and "[[Boss (Lil Pump song)|Boss]]", which were major hits on SoundCloud, collecting a combined 70 million streams.<ref name="soundcloud.com"/> The popularity of "D Rose" led to a [[music video]] being produced by Chicago-based director [[Cole Bennett]], also known as Lyrical Lemonade.<ref>{{Citation|first=Cole|last=Bennett|title=Lil Pump – D Rose (Shot by @_ColeBennett_)|date=January 30, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6Prfg3vyZkA| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211117/6Prfg3vyZkA| archive-date=2021-11-17 | url-status=live|access-date=October 10, 2017|via=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref> On June 9, 2017, Garcia signed a record deal with Tha Lights Global and [[Warner Records]], just two months before his seventeenth birthday.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-pump-signs-to-warner-bros-records-news.33541.html|title=Lil Pump Signs To Warner Bros Records Imprint Tha Lights Global|publisher=HotNewHipHop|date=June 9, 2017|first=Danny|last=Schwartz|access-date=November 19, 2017}}</ref> However, in January 2018, his contract with Warner Bros. Records was voided because he had been a minor at the time of signing.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8094047/lil-pump-free-agent-contract-voided-warner-bros|title=Lil Pump Now Free Agent, Contract Voided With Warner Bros.|work=Billboard|date=November 1, 2018|first=Colin|last=Stutz|access-date=February 15, 2018}}</ref>

In July 2017, Garcia announced through [[Twitter]] that his debut album was in the works and would be released in August.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/07/lil-pump-announces-debut-project-dropping-august/|title=Lil Pump Announces Debut Project Dropping in August – XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=November 19, 2017}}</ref> Although the album was not released in August it was pushed back and he instead released the song "[[Gucci Gang]]", which became his first ''Billboard'' Hot 100 entry, peaking at number three on November 8, 2017.<ref name="Hot100"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/09/lil-pump-billboard-hot-100-chart/|title=Lil Pump Lands His First Song on the Billboard Hot 100 Chart – XXL|website=XXL Mag|date=September 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/video/early-release-billboard-hot-100-top-10-november-18th-2017-countdown-official-8023256|title=Early Release! Billboard Hot 100 Top 10 November 18th 2017 Countdown – Official|access-date=November 19, 2017}}</ref> The song was [[Recording Industry Association of America certification|certified gold and platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] on January 11, 2018, and as of July 31, 2018, is certified triple platinum.<ref name=platinum/>

On October 6, 2017, Garcia released his debut commercial [[studio album]], ''[[Lil Pump (album)|Lil Pump]]'', featuring [[Smokepurpp]], [[Gucci Mane]], [[Lil Yachty]], [[Chief Keef]], [[Rick Ross]], and [[2 Chainz]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/10/listen-lil-pump-debut-album/|title=Listen to Lil Pump's Self-Titled Debut Album – XXL|website=XXL Mag|date=October 2017}}</ref>

=== Subsequent ventures (2020–present) ===
On February 13, 2020, Garcia stated on his Instagram story "I'm done doing music I quit..."; he did not elaborate.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2020/02/lil-pump-retiring-music-quit/|title=Lil Pump Says He's Retiring From Music|last=Centeno|first=Tony M.|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=February 14, 2020}}</ref> However days later he said on the same platform, "Yall Thought I Quit Bitch Im Back", and previewed a new song.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/02/lil-pump-ends-extremely-brief-retirement|title=Lil Pump Ends Extremely Brief Retirement|website=Complex|access-date=February 21, 2020}}</ref> He also announced that ''Lil Pump 2'' was the title of his upcoming album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-pump-announces-new-album-news.109355.html|title=Lil Pump Announces New Album|website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref>

On September 16, 2020, Garcia released a new single "Life Like Me" on his SoundCloud account. The song was first previewed in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-pump-finally-releases-life-like-me-new-song.1988613.html|title=Lil Pump Finally Releases "Life Like Me"|website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref> Garcia released a new single "Lil Pimp Big MAGA Steppin" on his SoundCloud on November 17 that year.<ref name="BillboardLilPimp">{{cite web |last1=Aniftos |first1=Rania |title=Lil Pump Unveils His Pro-Trump Anthem 'Lil Pimp Big MAGA Steppin' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9486234/lil-pump-lil-pimp-big-maga-steppin |website=Billboard |access-date=November 30, 2020}}</ref>

===Politics===
On October 26, 2020, Garcia endorsed the [[Donald Trump 2020 presidential campaign|campaign]] of [[President of the United States|President]] [[Donald Trump]] in the [[2020 United States presidential election]], citing [[Joe Biden]]'s tax plan as his reasoning.<ref>{{Cite news|date=October 26, 2020|title=The Countdown: Gen Z, why they vote and Amy v Hillary memes|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/election-us-2020-54689293|access-date=November 3, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=October 26, 2020|title=Lil Pump endorses Donald Trump for president, says "f*** sleepy Joe"|url=https://www.newsweek.com/lil-pump-rapper-donald-trump-endorsement-taxes-1542045|access-date=November 3, 2020|website=Newsweek|language=en}}</ref> Pump has made posts on social media wearing the [[Donald Trump 2016 presidential campaign|Trump campaign]]'s [[Make America Great Again]] baseball cap. On November 2, Trump brought Pump out to speak at his rally in [[Michigan]], mistakenly calling him "Little Pimp".<ref>{{Cite web|date=November 2, 2020|title=Trump Invites Rapper Lil Pump to Final Rally, Calls Him 'Little Pimp'|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/lil-pump-trump-rally-little-pimp-1234822241/|access-date=November 2, 2020|website=Variety|language=en}}</ref> Despite this endorsement, it was later revealed that Garcia was not even a registered voter.<ref>{{Cite web|date=November 14, 2020|title=Trump supporter Lil Pump didn't actually register to vote in 2020 election|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/trump-supporter-lil-pump-didnt-actually-register-to-vote-in-2020-election-2816741|access-date=November 16, 2020|website=NME Music News, Reviews, Videos, Galleries, Tickets and Blogs {{!}} NME.COM|language=en-GB}}</ref>

==Legal issues==
[[File:Lil Pump's mugshot.jpg|thumb|right|upright|Lil Pump's [[mug shot]] after being arrested for disorderly conduct in December 2018]]
On February 15, 2018, Garcia was arrested for discharging a weapon in an inhabited place.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2018/02/lil-pump-arrested-for-firing-weapon|title=Lil Pump Arrested for Firing Weapon at Home|website=Complex|date=February 2018}}</ref> According to his manager, three men attempted to break into his home in San Fernando Valley at around 4 pm before shooting into the door. Police found the bullet may have come from inside the home, coming back later with a search warrant before finding an unloaded handgun below the balcony with ammunition elsewhere in the residence.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-pump-reportedly-arrested-for-firing-gun-in-his-home-news.43898.html|title=Lil Pump Reportedly Arrested For Firing Gun In His Home|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=February 15, 2018}}</ref> Garcia's mother was subsequently investigated for endangering a minor and having an unsecured firearm at the home.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://uproxx.com/hiphop/lil-pump-arrested-for-shooting-gun-in-his-home/|title=Lil Pump Has Been Arrested For Firing A Gun Inside His Home|date=February 15, 2018|work=UPROXX|access-date=February 15, 2018}}</ref>

On August 29, 2018, Garcia was arrested for driving without a license in Miami.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.local10.com/news/local/miami/rapper-lil-pump-arrested-for-driving-without-licence-miami-police-say|title=Rapper Lil Pump arrested for driving without license, Miami...|last=Swift|first=Tim|website=www.local10.com|access-date=September 4, 2018|date=August 30, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/08/lil-pump-lawyer-says-rappers-arrest-was-due-to-profiling|title=Lil Pump's Lawyer Says the Rapper's Arrest Was Due to Racial Profiling|website=Complex|access-date=August 31, 2018}}</ref>
On September 3 he announced that he would be going to jail for 'a few months' for a parole violation stemming from the arrest.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8473297/lil-pump-tells-fans-hes-going-to-jail-for-a-couple-months-after|title=Lil Pump Tells Fans He's Going to Jail for a 'Couple Months' After Violating Probation: Watch|work=Billboard|access-date=September 4, 2018}}</ref> However, he appeared on the live American television show ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' on September 29.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/09/lil-pumps-saturday-night-live-performance-proves-hes-not-in-jail/|title=Lil Pump's 'SNL' Performance Proves He's Not in Jail – XXL|work=XXL Mag|access-date=September 30, 2018}}</ref> Garcia's manager told [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] in October 2018 that the rapper had served a prison sentence, but did not give any further details.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lockett |first1=Dee |title=21 Under 21: Lil Pump on Recording 'I Love It' With Kanye West and Breaking Through the SoundCloud Noise |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/grammys/8479042/lil-pump-kanye-west-21-under-21-interview |access-date=October 12, 2018 |work=Billboard |date=October 10, 2018}}</ref>

On December 4, 2018, Garcia was arrested by Danish police after a performance in [[Vega, Copenhagen]] for possessing marijuana; he was fined $700–800.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tmz.com/2018/12/05/lil-pump-released-denmark-police-weed-fine/|title=Lil Pump Released from Custody in Denmark After Paying Fine for Marijuana|website=TMZ|language=en|access-date=May 31, 2019}}</ref> He later live streamed himself flipping his middle finger at a police officer while being detained. He was subsequently banned from entering the country for two years.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://ekstrabladet.dk/flash/udlandkendte/politiet-bekraefter-international-stjerne-udvist-af-danmark/7427356 |title=Politiet bekræfter: International stjerne udvist af Danmark |last1=Jespersen |first1=Linette |last2=Hellensberg |first2=Laura |website=[[Ekstra Bladet]] |language=da |access-date=December 7, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2024938/lil-pump-detained-taunting-danish-cops-show-cancelled/news/|title=Lil Pump Detained For Taunting Danish Cops, Show Cancelled|date=December 4, 2018|website=Stereogum|access-date=May 31, 2019}}</ref>

On December 13, 2018, Garcia was arrested at a Miami airport for [[disorderly conduct]] as he was about to take off on a flight; security wanted to search Garcia's luggage for cannabis but Garcia insisted he did not have any. While no drugs were found, during the encounter with police Garcia became angry and began arguing loudly with the security employees. He was subsequently taken into custody.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/12/lil-pump-arrest-disorderly-conduct/|title=Lil Pump arrested for disorderly conduct after being accused of bringing drugs on airplane|date=December 14, 2018|website=Consequence of Sound|access-date=December 18, 2018}}</ref>

In December 2020 during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], he was banned from [[JetBlue]] after refusing to wear a mask on a flight from [[FLL|Fort Lauderdale]] to [[LAX|Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://thehill.com/blogs/in-the-know/in-the-know/531908-rapper-lil-pump-banned-from-jet-blue-after-refusing-to-wear-a  |date=December 29, 2020 |website=[[The Hill (newspaper)|The Hill]]|title=Rapper Lil Pump banned from Jet Blue after refusing to wear a mask |access-date=December 29, 2020}}</ref>
