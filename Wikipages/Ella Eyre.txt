'''Ella McMahon''' (born 1 April 1994), better known by her stage name '''Ella Eyre''', is a British singer and songwriter. She is best known for her collaborations with [[Rudimental]] on their UK number-one single "[[Waiting All Night]]" (2013), which won the [[2014 Brit Awards|2014 Brit Award]] for British Single of the Year, with [[DJ Fresh]] on his single "[[Gravity (DJ Fresh song)|Gravity]]" (2015), and with [[Sigala]] on their singles "[[Came Here for Love]]" (2017) and "[[Just Got Paid (Sigala song)|Just Got Paid]]" (2018). Her debut EP, ''Deeper'', was released in 2013 and her debut album, ''[[Feline (Ella Eyre album)|Feline]]'', was released in 2015. Eyre's musical influences include [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Etta James]], [[Basement Jaxx]] and [[Hans Zimmer]].

== Early life and education ==
Eyre was born on 1 April 1994 and was raised in [[Ealing]], [[West London]].<ref>Amelia Abraham, [http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/blog/a-first-date-with-ella-eyre "A First Date with... Ella Eyre"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151026122914/http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/blog/a-first-date-with-ella-eyre |date=26 October 2015 }}, Noisey.</ref> Her father was [[British Jamaicans|Jamaican]] and her mother is [[Maltese people|Maltese]]. Her father was a chef who lived in his home country of Jamaica, his health too poor to travel to the UK,<ref name="auto">{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/ella-eyre-interview-new-me-single-jamaica-privilege-a9288881.html|title=Ella Eyre interview: 'I have a posh accent, so people assume I am privileged'|date=20 January 2020|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|accessdate=9 February 2021}}</ref> and her mother, who raised Eyre in London, is a cake designer.<ref name=ID /><ref>Tom Lamont, [https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/jun/22/ella-eyre-wake-up-every-day-life-different-drumnbass-singer "Ella Eyre: 'I wake up every day and life is different'"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161223071252/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/jun/22/ella-eyre-wake-up-every-day-life-different-drumnbass-singer |date=23 December 2016 }}, ''The Guardian'', 22 June 2014.</ref> Eyre trained as a competitive swimmer before she began singing professionally.<ref name=ID>{{cite web |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_gb/article/heir-to-the-pop-throne-ella-eyre |title=heir to the pop throne, Ella Eyre |work=i-D Magazine |date=18 March 2014 |access-date=30 May 2014 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518100103/https://i-d.vice.com/en_gb/article/heir-to-the-pop-throne-ella-eyre |archive-date=18 May 2015 }}</ref>

Eyre was educated at [[Millfield School]] in Somerset; refuting the idea that she came from a privileged background, she observed that her mother could not have afforded the fees, and she went there on a "massive swimming scholarship".<ref name="auto" /> She later attended the [[BRIT School]] for Performing Arts and Technology, where she studied musical theatre.<ref>David Smyth, [https://www.standard.co.uk/goingout/music/another-ella-to-remember-ella-eyre-interview-9583434.html "Another Ella to remember: Ella Eyre interview"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518074858/http://www.standard.co.uk/goingout/music/another-ella-to-remember-ella-eyre-interview-9583434.html |date=18 May 2015 }}, ''Egypt Standard'', 4 July 2014.</ref><ref>[http://www.hungertv.com/feature/interview-ella-eyre/ "ONE FROM THE ARCHIVES – THE INTERVIEW: ELLA EYRE"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518073416/http://www.hungertv.com/feature/interview-ella-eyre/ |date=18 May 2015 }}, Hunger TV, 19 June 2015.</ref> Discovered by her management through a vocal coach in 2011, McMahon juggled school with songwriting. She signed to the publisher [[Warner/Chappell Music]] in July 2012 and a record deal with [[Virgin EMI]] followed shortly after.

== Music career ==
=== 2012–2016: Breakthrough and ''Feline'' ===
In December 2012, Eyre featured on [[Bastille (band)|Bastille]]'s cover and mash up of [[TLC (group)|TLC]]'s "[[No Scrubs]]" and [[the xx]]'s "[[Angels (The xx song)|Angels]]". Titled "No Angels", the song featured on the band's 2012 [[mixtape]], ''Other People's Heartache, Pt. 2''. In April 2013, she featured on drum and bass band [[Rudimental]]'s single "[[Waiting All Night]]", which peaked at number one on the [[UK Singles Chart]], becoming Eyre's first chart entry. In August 2013, she appeared alongside rapper [[Wiz Khalifa]] on the track "[[Think About It (Naughty Boy song)|Think About It]]" from producer [[Naughty Boy]]'s 2013 album ''[[Hotel Cabana]]''. The song was released as the fourth single from the album in November 2013. Eyre also features on rapper [[Tinie Tempah]]'s album ''[[Demonstration (Tinie Tempah album)|Demonstration]]'' on the track "[[Someday (Place in the Sun)]]", which entered the UK Singles Chart at number 87 but was never released as a single.

Eyre's first EP, ''Deeper'', was released in December 2013, and the title track reached number 72.<ref>Sarah Jane Griffiths, [https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-25577251 "BBC Sound of 2014: Ella Eyre"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160918233918/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-25577251 |date=18 September 2016 }}, BBC News – Entertainment & Arts, 9 January 2014.</ref><ref>[http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/29969/ella-eyre/ "Ella Eyre"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150717153954/http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/29969/ella-eyre/ |date=17 July 2015 }}, Official Charts.</ref> She was the runner-up for the 2014 [[Brit Awards|BRIT Critics' Choice Award]] and came second on the BBC [[Sound of...|Sound of 2014]] shortlist.<ref>Jimmy Blake, [http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/30363809/sound-of-2015-what-the-class-of-2014-did-next "Sound Of 2015: What the class of 2014 did next"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171204034523/http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/30363809/sound-of-2015-what-the-class-of-2014-did-next |date=4 December 2017 }}, Newsbeat, 5 January 2015.</ref> Her next three solo singles—"[[If I Go (Ella Eyre song)|If I Go]]", "[[Comeback (Ella Eyre song)|Comeback]]" (both 2014) and "[[Together (Ella Eyre song)|Together]]" (2015)—reached the top twenty on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref>Rob Copsey, [http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/ella-eyre-announces-new-single-good-times__9859/ "Ella Eyre announces new single Good Times"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924171958/http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/ella-eyre-announces-new-single-good-times__9859/ |date=24 September 2015 }}, Official Charts, 17 June 2015.</ref> She co-wrote the UK number one single "[[Changing (Sigma song)|Changing]]" for drum and bass duo [[Sigma (duo)|Sigma]] featuring British recording artist [[Paloma Faith]], which was released in the United Kingdom in September 2014. The following month, Eyre won Best Newcomer at the [[MOBO Awards]] 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.whatsgoodonline.co.uk/events/mobo-awards-2014-highlights/ |title=Ella Eyre Wins Best Newcomer at the MOBO Awards 2014 |publisher=What's Good? |date=22 October 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141023213138/http://www.whatsgoodonline.co.uk/events/mobo-awards-2014-highlights/ |archive-date=23 October 2014 |access-date=23 October 2014 }}</ref>

=== 2017–present: Forthcoming second studio album ===
On 18 February 2017, Eyre announced her comeback concert at [[KOKO (music venue)|KOKO]], London on 4 April 2017.<ref>[http://www.getwestlondon.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/ella-eyre-announces-live-return-12629259 Ella Eyre announces live return with KOKO London show – how to get tickets] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170226231142/http://www.getwestlondon.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/ella-eyre-announces-live-return-12629259 |date=26 February 2017 }}, ''Get West London'', 20 February 2017.</ref> At the comeback concert at KOKO, Eyre performed four new songs: "LOV(E)", "Drink with Your Name on It", "I Swear" and "Ego". On 29 May 2017, Eyre and [[Sigala]] announced a new collaborative single, "[[Came Here for Love]]", released on 9 June 2017. As of 29 June the official YouTube video has over 2.5 million views and 11 million streams on Spotify. On 31 July 2017, Eyre announced her new single 'Ego' featuring [[Ty Dolla $ign]], released on 11 August 2017. Eyre supported [[Little Mix]] on their [[The Glory Days Tour|Summer Shout Out Tour]] and supported [[The Script]] on their "Freedom Child Tour" in early 2018. When supporting [[The Script]], Eyre promoted a new song called "Answerphone" yet to be released.{{cn|date=April 2021}}

In February 2018, Eyre was confirmed for the celebrity version of the [[The Great British Bake Off|Great British Bake Off]] 2018. On 5 March 2018, Eyre announced her new single "[[Answerphone (Banx & Ranx and Ella Eyre song)|Answerphone]]" with [[Banx & Ranx]] which features [[Yxng Bane]] which was released on 16 March and peaked at number 5 on the UK Chart. On 20 June 2018, Eyre teased, whilst in [[Copenhagen]], a new song on her social media. On 7 September 2018, Eyre released another collaboration with [[Sigala]] called "[[Just Got Paid (Sigala song)|Just Got Paid]]" featuring [[Meghan Trainor]] and [[French Montana]]. It peaked at Number 11 on the UK Chart.

In July 2019, Eyre announced she had signed to [[Island Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicweek.com/talent/read/now-is-her-time-ella-eyre-signs-to-island/076652|title='Now is her time': Ella Eyre signs to Island|website=www.musicweek.com}}</ref> She subsequently released her new single titled "Mama" featuring [[Banx & Ranx]] and [[Kiana Ledé]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/ella-eyre-blasts-back-with-new-single-mama|title=Ella Eyre blasts back with new single "Mama"|website=The Line of Best Fit}}</ref>

In January 2020, Eyre released her new single "New Me", with a following UK tour in November and December the same year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/gq-hype/article/ella-eyre-interview|title=Ella Eyre on getting out of toxic situations|website=British GQ|date=24 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-51235282|title=How Ella Eyre fought her way out of the pop wilderness|first=Mark|last=Savage|date=29 January 2020|work=BBC News}}</ref> Following the release of "New Me", Eyre announced the release of her third single from her second studio album, "L.O.V.(e)", to be released on 8 May 2020, having been performed numerous times prior to release and has been noted{{by whom|date=April 2021}} as a 'fan favourite'. Following this, Eyre dropped the singles "Dreams" featuring [[Yxng Bane]] and "Careless", before dropping her [[extended play]] "Quarter Life Crisis" in August 2020.{{cn|date=April 2021}}

== Personal life ==
In February 2017, Eyre and Lewi Morgan, from the British [[pop rock]] band [[Push Baby|Rixton]], ended their two-year relationship.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cosmopolitan.co.za/celebrity-news/ella-eyre-and-lewi-morgan-split/|title=Ella Eyre and Lewi Morgan split|date=2 February 2017}}</ref> A week after the comeback show at KOKO in April 2017, Eyre announced the death of her father.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/ella-eyre-interview-new-me-single-jamaica-privilege-a9288881.html|title=Ella Eyre interview: 'I have a posh accent, so people assume I am privileged'|date=18 January 2020|website=The Independent}}</ref>

Eyre revealed she suffered from a kidney infection during the making of her 'New Me' music video.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-51235282|title=How Ella Eyre fought her way out of the pop wilderness|last=Savage|first=Mark|date=29 January 2020|work=BBC News|access-date=29 January 2020|language=en-GB}}</ref>
