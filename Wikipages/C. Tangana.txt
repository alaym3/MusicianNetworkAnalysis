'''Antón Álvarez Alfaro''' (born July 16, 1990), better known as '''C. Tangana''', is a Spanish rapper from [[Madrid]], Spain.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.elmundo.es/cultura/musica/2017/10/16/59e49ae8468aeb4a768b45cf.html|title=C. Tangana, el 'Ídolo' musical del año|work=ELMUNDO|access-date=April 25, 2018|language=es}}</ref> He is a member of the rap group Agorazein. He began his musical career in 2005 under the pseudonym Crema,<ref>"C. Tangana antes se llamaba Crema".</ref> with a demo called ''Él Es Crema''. Tangana has headlined the [[Primavera Sound]] festival.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Freire|first=Juan Manuel|date=2018-03-03|title=C. Tangana, ídolo con futuro|url=https://www.elperiodico.com/es/ocio-y-cultura/20180303/c-tangana-cronica-razzmatazz-idolo-6664433|access-date=2020-11-06|website=elperiodico|language=es}}</ref> In 2020, he was named one of the most influential people and most creative artists in Spain by ''[[Forbes]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=PLAYZ|date=2020-09-02|title=C.Tangana, uno de los españoles más creativos según Forbes {{!}} RTVE|url=https://www.rtve.es/playz/20200902/tangana-espanoles-mas-creativos-segun-forbes/2041265.shtml|access-date=2020-11-06|website=RTVE.es|language=es}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Álvarez was born in July 1990 in Madrid from [[Galicians|Galician]] and [[Madrid|Madrileño]] parents.<ref>{{Cite web title=C. Tangana - Biografía, historia y legado musical {{!}} BuenaMusica.com|url=https://www.buenamusica.com/c-tangana/biografia|access-date=2020-11-06|website=www.buenamusica.com|language=es}}</ref> In 2005 he started making music under the alias Crema.

== Career ==

=== 2005–2007: First steps as Crema ===
In 2005, he gained recognition as Crema as his freestyle demos began spreading online. He focused on rap music and released a seven-track [[Extended play|EP]] titled ''Él Es Crema'' (2005) and then two more (''Madrid Files'' and ''Septiembre'') in 2006. After finishing his [[Baccalaureate service|baccalaureate]] he studied [[philosophy]] at the [[Complutense University of Madrid]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=C. Tangana, de trabajar en el Pans&Company a número uno en Spotify|language=es|work=El Español|url=https://www.elespanol.com/cultura/musica/20170613/223478176_0.html|access-date=April 25, 2018}}</ref>

=== 2011–2016: Agorazein ===
Álvarez formed the band Agorazein with a group of friends in 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-26|title=Conoce a C. Tangana, el cantante español del momento|url=https://www.glamour.mx/celebrities/articulos/quien-es-c-tangana/15771|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Glamour Mexico|language=es-MX}}</ref> That year he released a work entitled "C. Tangana", which was generally well-received, attracted many new followers, and was adopted by Álvarez as his new stage name. In 2011 the band released their debut album in Spanish as independent artists titled ''Kind of Red''. Between 2011 and 2012 Álvarez self-released two albums: ''C. Tangana'' (2011) and ''LOVE'S'' (2012).<ref>{{Cite web|date=2012-12-10|title=C. TANGANA: "LO▼E'S es lo mejor que he hecho en todos los aspectos" {{!}} UMO Magazine|url=https://umomag.com/revista/entrevistas/c-tangana-lo▼es-es-lo-mejor-que-he-hecho-en-todos-los-aspectos/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=UMOMAG™|language=es}}</ref>

Tangana had replaced his particular style of short and direct phrases with short Anglicized phrases.{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}} While playing music, he made a living working in a Pans and Company and in a [[Vodafone]] call center. He began to shy away from the media in January 2013 because he did not feel comfortable with it.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Sancho|first=Xavi|date=2018-04-27|title=C. Tangana: "Lo siento mucho, pero me considero un impresario"|language=es|work=El País|url=https://elpais.com/elpais/2018/04/25/icon/1524669924_121944.html|access-date=2020-11-06|issn=1134-6582}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-07-22|title=C.Tangana: "Cuando haces algo de ruido, te ladran"|url=http://revistamito.com/c-tangana-cuando-haces-algo-de-ruido-te-ladran/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Mito {{!}} Revista Cultural|language=es}}</ref> In 2014 he released his first EP as an independent artist titled ''Trouble + Presidente''. In 2015 he left his job to fully work on music. Agorazein released their final album ''Siempre'' in November 2016 and have been on an indefinite hiatus since then.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-11-22|title=Agorazein / Siempre|url=https://jenesaispop.com/2016/11/22/281681/agorazein-siempre/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=jenesaispop.com|language=es}}</ref>

=== 2016–2017: Record deal and ''Ídolo'' ===
In 2016, Tangana signed a record deal with [[Warner Chappell Music]] and started working regularly with friend and producer [[Alizzz]]. His first release as a signed artist was "[[Antes de morirme|Antes de Morirme]]", a flamenco-pop collaboration with then-girlfriend [[Rosalía (singer)|Rosalía]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Tena|first=Raül De|date=2016-09-12|title=La exitosa alianza viral entre el rap y el flamenco|language=es|work=El País|url=https://elpais.com/cultura/2016/09/08/actualidad/1473354762_074504.html|access-date=2020-11-06|issn=1134-6582}}</ref> The song was a [[sleeper hit]] until late 2018, when Rosalía's career started growing internationally and the song was included in the soundtrack of the Spanish Netflix show ''[[Elite (TV series)|Élite]]''. Tangana continued releasing songs throughout 2016. In 2017, Álvarez embarked on a tour in Latin America called C. Tangana Latino Tour. When the tour finished, he announced his official agreement with the record company [[Sony Music|Sony Music Entertainment]]. In April he released the lead single off his debut album titled "Espabilao". One month later, "Mala Mujer" was released on digital platforms as the album's second single. The song became wildly popular in Spain and in parts of Latin America. The track became a top-five hit in Spain, was named song of the summer, certified three times platinum there, and was also certified gold in the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|last=20minutos|date=2017-09-08|title=C. Tangana consigue el doble disco de platino por su canción 'Mala Mujer'|url=https://www.20minutos.es/noticia/3129716/0/c-tangana-mala-mujer-doble-disco-platino/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=www.20minutos.es - Últimas Noticias|language=es}}</ref> A remix featuring [[Farruko]] and [[French Montana]] was released in September.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-09-22|title=C. Tangana estrena remix de "Mala Mujer" con Farruko y French Montana|url=https://www.mondosonoro.com/noticias-actualidad-musical/c-tangana-remix-mala-mujer-farruko-french-montana/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=MondoSonoro|language=es}}</ref> Later, C. Tangana performed the track at [[LOS40 Music Awards 2017]], where he was awarded Best New Artist of the Year.<ref>{{Cite web|last=LOS40|date=2017-11-08|title=C. Tangana, confirmado para LOS40 Music Awards|url=https://los40.com/los40/2017/11/08/radio/1510136066_943784.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=LOS40|language=es}}</ref> The music video for "Mala Mujer" also received a nomination for "Best National Video". He was also nominated at the [[2017 MTV Europe Music Awards]] for [[MTV Europe Music Award for Best Spanish Act|Best Spanish Act]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=LOS40|date=2020-10-06|title=¿Qué artistas españoles han sido nominados y han ganado los MTV EMAs en toda su historia?|url=https://los40.com/los40/2020/10/06/musica/1601987521_572587.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=LOS40|language=es}}</ref> His first studio album ''Ídolo'' was released on digital download and streaming platforms on October 5, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-10-05|title=C. Tangana publica 'Ídolo'|url=https://www.dodmagazine.es/c-tangana-publica-idolo/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Dod Magazine|language=es}}</ref> In December 2017 he released "Guerrera" alongside Dellafuente, which became a top 20 hit in Spain and was certified two times platinum.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Indiescretos|date=2018-02-28|title=La Guerrera de C.Tangana y Dellafuente ya es disco de oro|url=https://www.indiescretos.com/guerrera-c-tangana-dellafuente-videoclip-disco-de-oro/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Indiescretos|language=es}}</ref>

=== 2018–2019: ''Avida Dollars'' ===
In January 2018, Tanagana released the lead single of his debut mixtape as a signed artist: "Still Rapping" with Steve Lean. The song peaked at 86th on the [[Productores de Música de España|PROMUSICAE]] chart. In March he released the mixtape's second single "Llorando en la Limo", produced and featuring Alizzz, which peaked at number twelve on the chart and became another summer hit in Spain. The mixtape, ''Avida Dollars'', was released on digital platforms on April 20, 2018, presented with two sold-out shows at La Riviera in Madrid.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-04-26|title=Crónica: C. Tangana en Madrid, sala La Riviera (26-04-2018)|url=https://www.dodmagazine.es/cronica-c-tangana-en-madrid-sala-la-riviera-26-04-2018/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Dod Magazine|language=es}}</ref> and was well received by music critics.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-05-29|title=Reseñas cruzadas: C. Tangana - Avida Dollars (Sony)|url=https://muzikalia.com/resenascruzadas-c-tangana-avida-dollars-sony/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Muzikalia|language=es-ES}}</ref> Since then, Tangana started releasing independent singles such as "Bien Duro", released in June 2018, which peaked at number six in Spain and became the 32nd most sold song in the country. It was certified double platinum. In October, he released his first international collaboration with American singer [[Becky G]]. The song, titled "Booty" peaked at number 3 in Spain and became the 93rd best-selling song in Spain in only two months. "Booty" was also certified double platinum in Spain and platinum in the United States. Its music video was nominated for Best Music Video at the [[1st Annual Premios Odeón|2020 Premios Odeón]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-21|title=Premios Odeón 2020: lista de ganadores completa|url=https://www.elcorreo.com/culturas/musica/premios-odeon-2020-ganadores-20200121090320-nt.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=El Correo|language=es}}</ref> In November 2018, Tangana was awarded with two [[Latin Grammy Award]]s for writing Rosalía's "[[Malamente]]". He took home the awards for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Song|Best Alternative Song]] and [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Urban Fusion/Performance|Best Urban Fusion/Performance]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Mallorca|first=Noticias de|title=Rosalía se lleva dos Grammy latinos con su Malamente|url=https://www.mallorcadiario.com/rosalia-se-lleva-dos-grammy-latinos-con-su-malamente|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Noticias de Mallorca|language=es}}</ref> The track was also nominated for [[Latin Grammy Award for Record of the Year|Record of the Year]] and [[Latin Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]]. He later received the [[Latin Grammy Award for Album of the Year]] and for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Pop Vocal Album|Best Contemporary Pop Vocal Album]] for his contribution on eight songs on Rosalía's sophomore album ''[[El mal querer|El Mal Querer]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rosalía gana el Latin Grammy al Álbum del año por "El mal querer"|url=https://www.efe.com/efe/espana/cultura/rosalia-gana-el-latin-grammy-al-album-del-ano-por-mal-querer/10005-4111488|access-date=2020-11-06|website=www.efe.com|language=es}}</ref> In November, he also released "Un Veneno", a minimal, new flamenco track featuring Niño de Elche. It received critical acclaim due to its rawness. On November 21 he performed it on ''[[Operación Triunfo (series 10)|Operación Triunfo 2018]]''. After the performance, he left the stage aggressively despite being scheduled to give an interview,<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-11-22|title=C Tangana se ríe de OT 2018 en Instagram, tras su desplante a Roberto Leal|url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/television/20181122/453088910256/ot-2018-operacion-triunfo-c-tangana-roberto-leal-un-veneno-maricarmen.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=La Vanguardia|language=es}}</ref> which was received negatively on [[Twitter]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-11-22|title=C. Tangana se ríe de OT 2018 y evita hablar con los concursantes|url=https://www.abc.es/play/television/noticias/abci-operacion-triunfo-tangana-2018-y-evita-hablar-concursantes-201811220916_noticia.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=abc|language=es}}</ref> The media coverage the situation received caused an increase in "Un Veneno" streams, making it reach the number two spot on the PROMUSICAE chart and be certified gold in Spain.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-11-23|title=C.Tangana y su juego de espejos en 'Un veneno', su último single|url=https://us.hola.com/actualidad/20181123133441/ctangana-veneno-nuevo-single/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=HOLA USA|language=es-US}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=Música|first=El portal de|title=Un Veneno - C.Tangana & Niño de Elche {{!}} EPDM|url=https://www.elportaldemusica.es/single/ctangana-nino-de-elche-un-veneno|language=en-US|access-date=2020-11-06}}</ref>

During 2019, Tangana continued releasing independent singles. In January he collaborated with Brazilian rapper [[MC Bin Laden]] and once again with Alizzz on "Pa' Llamar Tu Atención".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-01-24|title='Pa' Llamar Tu Atención', el nuevo videoclip de C Tangana|url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/musica/20190124/454286908922/c-tangana-videoclip-pa-llamar-tu-atencion-alizzz-mc-bin-laden-video-seo-ext.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=La Vanguardia|language=es}}</ref> The track reached number three on the PROMUSICAE chart. In February he collaborated with Alizzz and Dellafuente once again on "París", which eventually became Tangana's fifth top 10 single. In March 2019 he performed at [[Lollapalooza|Lollapalooza Argentina]] and [[Lollapalooza Chile|Chile]]. He also offered to do a show in [[Lima]] and did a showcase in [[Miami]]. In April 2019 he moved to [[Los Angeles]]. He attended the [[2019 Billboard Latin Music Awards|2019 ''Billboard'' Latin Music Awards]] and performed at the after-party. While in [[Americas|America]], he worked with Chilean-American singer [[Paloma Mami]] on his song "No Te Debí Besar", released in September 2019, which marked Mami's debut on the Spanish music charts as well as a new top three hit for Tangana. In February he collaborated with [[Natti Natasha]] on his own track "Viene Y Va" and alongside [[Tainy]] and former [[Fifth Harmony]] member [[Lauren Jauregui]] on the Puerto Rican's "Nada", included in his album ''The Kids That Grew Up On Reggaeton'' (2020).<ref>{{Cite web|last=LOS40|date=2020-02-19|title=Colaboración sorpresa: Lauren Jauregui y C. === 2020–present: ''Bien'' and ''El Madrileño'' ===
In April 2020, Tangana moved to [[Pop rap|rap-pop]] and [[sadcore]] music with [[Folk music|folk]] elements, leaving trap and hip hop. On April 24 he released "Nunca Estoy", which was very well received by critics. It reached the top spot on the PROMUSICAE chart, and became Tangana's first number one hit in his career.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-05-05|title=Rosario Flores brinda a C. Tangana su primer número 1|url=https://www.elmundo.es/loc/famosos/2020/05/06/5eb188e2fc6c83cc288b45f7.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=ELMUNDO|language=es}}</ref> The track sampled both "Cómo Quieres Que Te Quiera" by [[Rosario Flores]] and "[[Corazón Partío]]" by [[Alejandro Sanz]]. In May, Tangana announced his first EP as a signed artist, ''[[Bien (EP)|Bien]]'', which was released through Sony Music on May 14, 2020, and debuted number one in Spain. He allied with Little Spain Productions to produce, write and direct his upcoming music videos. In September, Tangana revealed to ''[[Forbes]]'' that his upcoming studio album would be very different from his previous releases and that it would be named ''[[El Madrileño]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lenin|first=Jesús Rodríguez|date=2020-09-01|title=C. Tangana: "Tengo más capacidad para detectar talento que talento propio"|url=https://forbes.es/lifestyle/74402/c-tangana-creativos-forbes/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Forbes España|language=es}}</ref>

On October 8, Tangana released the lead single of ''El Madrileño''. "Demasiadas Mujeres" became popular in Spain; it debuted at the top of the [[Productores de Música de España|PROMUSICAE]] chart and being gold-certified in only a week for selling over 20,000 copies. Its music video included a scene of Tangana's own funeral and references to his past relationships with Rosalía and [[Berta Vázquez]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=PLAYZ|date=2020-10-09|title=C. Internationally, "Tú Me Dejaste de Querer" debuted at number 31 on the Spotify Global chart, marking the first entry of Tangana on the list.<ref>{{Cite web|title=C. Tangana rompe récords globales con "Tú Me Dejaste De Querer"|url=https://www.filo.news/musica/C.-Tangana-rompe-records-globales-con-Tu-Me-Dejaste-De-Querer-20201107-0035.html|access-date=2020-11-11|website=Filo News|language=es-ES}}</ref>

In January 2021, Tangana released the single "Comerte Entera" featuring [[Toquinho]] on ''El Madrileño''.

== Musical style ==
{{More citations needed section|date=March 2021}}
With Agorazein, he attracted the attention of people due to a blend of good rhythms and melodies that conveyed teenage rebelliousness and sadness. In contrast with rap, Tangana's soft sounds became as important as the voice, which started to gain acoustic treatment.

In 2015, he adopted a more traditional lyric style, but continued with the most creative part of his music. In his songs, Tangana expresses individualistic ideals. On occasion, some mass media have interpreted sexist sentences<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elidealgallego.com/articulo/sociedad/ctangana-acusar-machismo-preguntan-eres-nazi/20171012002805352631.html|title=C.Tangana: "Acusar de machismo es como si te preguntan si eres nazi"|last=gallego|first=El Ideal|website=El Ideal gallego|language=en|access-date=April 25, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cuatro.com/chesterinlove/Tangana-transexual-machista-aplaudido-comentario-video-entrevista-risto-mejide_2_2506905067.html|title="Yo no soy machista, yo soy transexual": C. Tangana aclara su aplaudido comentario|work=cuatro|access-date=April 25, 2018}}</ref> in his songs. Regarding to his political ideas, he said that he does not believe in the representative democracy.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://iamrap.es/c-tangana-ideologia-dice-pp-no-derechas/|title=C. Tangana y su ideología: ¿Por qué dice que el PP no es de derechas?|date=June 16, 2017|work=I am Rap|access-date=April 25, 2018|language=es-ES}}</ref>

== Influences ==
{{More citations needed section|date=March 2021}}
Tangana discovered rap music from the [[Beastie Boys]] album ''[[Ill Communication|III Communication]]'' and the feature film ''Fear of a Black Hat'', as mentioned in his song "Diez Años". Among all the influences, he also mentioned ''[[If You're Reading This It's Too Late]]'' by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], and other singers like [[Pharrell Williams]] and [[Kanye West]] because of their ability and inclination to innovate and to attract the public's attention. He stated a few times that he listens to all musical genres because "everything has an interesting side".{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}} Tangana's music is not the result of particular singers, but the product of musical movements and trends; although he recently said in an interview that his idol was the "King of Pop" [[Michael Jackson]].{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}

=== Feuds ===
Tangana has been involved in several feuds with other artists during his career.

The most infamous one was the beef with the rap group Los Chikos del Maíz. In May 2014, he recorded the video clip of his song "Alligators" where he blatantly promoted [[Lacoste]]. In September of that year, Los Chikos del Maíz released their song "Tú al Gulag y yo a California" to parodize Tangana because of the advertising. The group gave a concert in Madrid on May 17, 2015, where Tangana allegedly attacked the singer of the rap group, Nega. Tangana confirmed the allegations later that year with his song "Nada", throughout which he describes the fight. He later confessed that he regretted the punch in a radio interview for the program ''Yu Vodafone''. In May 2016, Los Chikos del Maíz released their new song "Los Pollos Hermanos". Four hours later, Tangana released "Los Chicos de Madriz" as a response. Many other people saw themselves involved in that conflict, like Pablo Iglesias, a Spanish leftist politician, who supported Nega.

In 2017, Tangana's new album was released and he feuded against [[Kaydy Cain]] with his song "No Te Pegas". Cain replied with his song "Perdedores del Barrio".

Tangana also fought with Spanish singer [[Yung Beef]] after a joint interview for the [[Primavera Sound Festival]] 2018 in which they argued about the music industry and their position in it. Later, Tangana released song "El Rey Soy Yo/I Feel Like Kanye", to which Yung Beef answered with "Yes indeed / I feel like Kim K-RIP Pucho". Tangana then released "Forfri" and a statement in which he claimed that Yung Beef "was just being used by the industry and he didn't even notice".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fortuny|first=Ignasi|date=2018-06-23|title=C. Tangana-Yung Beef: puñaladas traperas|url=https://www.elperiodico.com/es/ocio-y-cultura/20180623/c-tangana-yung-beef-punaladas-traperas-6899441|access-date=2021-04-14|website=elperiodico|language=es}}</ref>
