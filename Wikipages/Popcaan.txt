'''Andre Hugh Sutherland''' (born 19 July 1988), known professionally as '''Popcaan''', is a [[Jamaicans|Jamaican]] [[Toasting (Jamaican music)|deejay]], singer and songwriter from the parish of [[St. Thomas, Jamaica|St. Thomas]]. 

In 2008, Popcaan joined [[Vybz Kartel]]'s Gaza Music Empire. In 2010, he released his breakthrough international hit "Clarks" with Kartel, an ode to the popular shoe brand.

In 2014, Popcaan signed with [[Mixpak Records]] and released his debut studio album, ''[[Where We Come From]]''. 2016 Popcaan signed with Canadian rapper [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s [[record label]] [[OVO Sound]] and [[Warner Records]]. Popcaan's international singles include "Only Man She Want" (2011), "Everything Nice" (2014), "Ova Dweet" (2016), "El Chapo" (2017), and "Family" (2017). Popcaan made his acting debut in the film ''[[The Intent 2: The Come Up]]'' (2018).

Popcaan has collaborated with a number of international artists, most notably [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Jamie xx]], [[Pusha T]], [[Giggs (rapper)|Giggs]], [[Matoma]], [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], [[Gorillaz]] and [[Davido]].

His second album for [[Mixpak Records]], entitled [[Forever (Popcaan album)|''Forever'']], was released on 20 July 2018.

==Early life==
Born in [[Saint Thomas Parish, Jamaica]], Sutherland grew up in [[Portmore, Jamaica|Greater Portmore]], [[Saint Catherine Parish]].

==Career==
===2007–10: Early career===
In 2007, Popcaan approached [[Vybz Kartel]] at a local jam called "My Scheme" where Kartel recruited the young deejay to his Portmore Empire music group, becoming both a producer and mentor. Besides showing Popcaan the ropes of the music game, Kartel took him on major shows like Sting and [[Reggae Sumfest]].

Popcaan began making music for Adidjaheim Productions with then in-house producer NotNice, the first person to voice him professionally. He got off to a strong start with "Gal Wine" on the Gal Farm riddim, "Clarks" featuring Vybz Kartel, Popcaan and Gaza Slim and more following up with early hits including "It Nuh Work So", "Jah Jah Protect Me", "Gangsta City", "Dream",  "Gangsta City Part Second" and "Hot Grabba".

===2010–13: Growing popularity===
Popcaan's breakthrough came in 2010 with "Clarks", a collaboration with Kartel.<ref>"[http://www.mydancehallmadness.com/2010/03/videovybz-kartel-ft-popcaan-slim-clarks.html Official Video Vybz Kartel ft Popcaan &Gaza Slim – Clarks]", ''Dancehall Madness'', 30 March 2010</ref> The success of the song caused a nationwide spike in sales of the British shoe.<ref name=Metro>{{cite web|title=Vybz Kartel rap leads to booming Clarks shoes sales in Jamaica|url=http://metro.co.uk/2010/07/07/vybz-kartel-rap-leads-to-booming-clarks-shoes-sales-in-jamaica-439321/|website=Metro.co.uk|access-date=8 April 2015|date=7 July 2010}}</ref> In 2011, Popcaan received the Excellence in Music and Entertainment (EME) Award for Best New Artist, and Collaboration of the Year for "Clarks", which also won Song of the Year (Dancehall).<ref name="EME2011">"[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/Kartel-takes-home-four-awards Kartel takes home four awards] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120614153704/http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/Kartel-takes-home-four-awards |date=14 June 2012 }}", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 13 March 2011, retrieved 10 June 2011</ref>

At the end of January 2012, Popcaan released a new mixtape, ''Chromatic presents Yiy Change''—the follow-up to his 2010 mixtape ''Hot Skull, Fry Yiy, Boil Brainz''. In March, "Only Man She Want" gave Popcaan his first ''Billboard'' entry at No. 89 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart. The song was later officially remixed by rapper [[Busta Rhymes]].

Popcaan was also featured in ''[[The New York Times]]'' speaking on the growing impact of "Only Man She Want".<ref>Caramanica, Jon (13 April 2012) [https://www.nytimes.com/2012/04/15/arts/music/new-releases-from-ka-popcaan-vena-and-bleeding-knees-club.html?_r=0 achata Royalty, Long-Haul Rapper, Australian Brats]. New York Times</ref>

At the Youth View Awards, Popcaan won three awards: Favourite Summer Song and Local Chart-Topping Song of the Year for "Ravin", and Young, Hot and Hype Male of the Year.<ref>[http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20120206/ent/ent1.html Popcaan, Tifa win big at YVAs – Entertainment – Jamaica Star – 7 February 2012] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20130717081850/http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20120206/ent/ent1.html |date=17 July 2013 }}. Jamaica Star (7 February 2012). Retrieved on 15 July 2013.</ref>

Popcaan has toured the world since, in Europe, Canada, and the Caribbean and South American region. While in Canada, Popcaan connected with Canadian rapper [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], who has publicly expressed his admiration.<ref name=JamStar>{{cite web|title=Popcaan teams up with Drake|url=http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20120815/ent/ent1.html|website=Jamaica Star|access-date=8 April 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130528040140/http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20120815/ent/ent1.html|archive-date=28 May 2013}}</ref> In 2013, Popcaan released the video for "Unruly Rave", which was shot in Toronto and directed by [[OVO Sound|October's Very Own]] label head Niko.<ref name=OKPlay>{{cite web|title=LargeUp News: Is Drake Behind Popcaan's New 'Unruly Rave' Video?|url=http://www.okayplayer.com/news/drake-popcaan-unruly-rave-official-video.html|website=OkayPlayer|access-date=8 April 2015|date=27 June 2013}}</ref>

In 2012, Popcaan was featured on [[Pusha T]]'s song "Blocka". His voice was then sampled by [[Kanye West]] on ''[[Yeezus]]'' in 2013.

===2014–present: ''Where We Come From'', ''Forever'' and subsequent fame===
In 2014, Popcaan signed to [[Mixpak Records]] for a multi-record deal. His debut studio album, ''[[Where We Come From]]'', was released on Mixpak on 10 June 2014. The project was executive-produced by [[Dre Skull]]. The first single from the album, "Everything Nice", was produced by Dubbel Dutch and released on 1 October 2013.<ref name="Jackson">Jackson, Kevin (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/A-new-day-for-Popcaan-_17038413 A New Day for Popcaan]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 29 June 2014. Retrieved 29 June 2014</ref><ref>[http://www.mixpakrecords.com/blog/2013/10/popcaan-everything-nice-out-today/ Popcaan – Everything Nice Out Now!] mixpakrecords.com. 1 October 2013</ref> It entered the ''Billboard'' Top Reggae Albums chart at number 2, and the video gained over 5 million views on [[YouTube]].<ref name="Jackson" />

In June 2014, Popcaan first appeared on the cover of ''[[The Fader]]'' in its 92nd issue.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/06/03/popcaan-dancehall-cover-story-feature/ | title=Cover Story: Popcaan Is Making Dancehall Nice Again | publisher=The FADER | work=The FADER | date=3 June 2014 | access-date=3 July 2014}}</ref> He featured on [[Melissa Steel]]'s "Kisses for Breakfast" single, which was a top ten hit in the UK in August 2014.<ref>"[http://www.officialcharts.com/uk-top-40-singles-chart/ Official Singles Chart UK Top 40 – 9th August 2014]", Official Charts Company, 3 August 2014. Retrieved 5 August 2014</ref><ref>Jackson, Kevin (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Loving--Popcaan-s-breakfast-in-Britain_17302099 Loving 'Popcaan's breakfast in Britain]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 5 August 2014. Retrieved 5 August 2014</ref>

In September 2014 he was nominated for a [[MOBO Awards|MOBO Award]] in the Best Reggae Act category, his fourth consecutive nomination.<ref name="MOBO">Jackson, Kevin (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/5-for-MOBO-REGGAE 5 For MOBO Reggae]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 24 September 2014. Retrieved 27 September 2014</ref> He won the MOBO Award in 2015 and 2016.<ref>Jackson, Kevin (2015) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Popcaan-is-king-of-the-MOBOs-_19237195 Popcaan is king of the MOBOs]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 6 November 2015. Retrieved 7 November 2015</ref>

In May 2015, Popcaan was featured on [[Jamie xx]]'s "[[I Know There's Gonna Be (Good Times)]]" with American rapper [[Young Thug]].

In January 2016, Popcaan was featured on English electronic duo [[AlunaGeorge]]'s single "[[I'm in Control]]".<ref>{{cite web|last=Sahim |first=Sarah |url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2016/01/alunageorge-and-popcaan-command-attention-on-new-single-im-in-control-listen/ |title=AlunaGeorge and Popcaan command attention on new single "I'm In Control" — listen |publisher=[[Consequence of Sound]] |date=20 January 2016 |access-date=3 October 2016}}</ref> On 29 March 2016, a song entitled "[[Controlla]]" by Drake was leaked, with Popcaan being featured on it. A version without Popcaan was later featured on Drake's fourth studio album ''[[Views (album)|Views]]''.

On 28 April 2016, Popcaan released the single "Ova Dweet" which gained popularity both in Jamaica and abroad.

On 2 May 2016, Popcaan was reportedly held by police after a performance in [[Antigua]] involving an altercation with an officer on stage.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/popcaan-reportedly-arrested-in-antigua-news.21410.html? |title=My Favorite Things, Part II |last=Lilah |first=Rose |date=3 May 2016 |website=Hotnewhiphop |access-date=3 May 2016}}</ref>

In June 2016, Popcaan made his first UK appearance alongside Mixpak for the annual Red Bull Culture Clash, and won the clash over Wiz Khalifa, Taylor Gang, Eskimo Dance and UKG.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.redbull.com/gb-en/culture-clash-line-up-london-2016|title=Mixpak win Culture Clash London 2016!|newspaper=Red Bull|access-date=23 February 2017}}</ref>

In March 2017, Popcaan performed guest vocals for the [[Gorillaz]] track "[[Saturnz Barz]]" off of the album ''[[Humanz]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/72312-watch-gorillazs-video-for-new-song-saturnz-barz-with-popcaan/ |title=Watch Gorillaz's Video for New Song "Saturnz Barz" With Popcaan |date=24 March 2017|website=Pitchfork |access-date=24 March 2016}}</ref> The track reached number five on the [[List of number-one Billboard Rock Songs|''Billboard'' Hot Rock Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gorillaz Chart History|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/303029/Gorillaz/chart?f=902|website=Billboard|access-date=7 September 2017}}</ref> He also spent time in Europe as the opening act on [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s [[Boy Meets World Tour]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Popcaan Officially Joins Drake on 'More Life' Tour|url=https://dancehallhiphop.com/2017/03/02/popcaan-officially-joins-drake-boy-meets-world-tour/|access-date=7 September 2017|agency=DancehallHipHop|date=2 March 2017}}</ref>

Popcaan announced his second album ''Forever'' on 11 May 2018. It was preceded by the singles "Body So Good", "Wine For Me", and "Firm and Strong".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Hussein |first1=Wandera |title=Popcaan shares two new singles "Wine For Me" and "Firm & Strong" |url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/06/25/popcaan-new-songs-wine-for-me-firm-and-strong |access-date=23 July 2018 |agency=The Fader}}</ref> The album was released on 20 July, premiered on a live stream hosted by [[Boiler Room (music broadcaster)|Boiler Room]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Popcaan Live Boiler Room Kingston |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?time_continue=3&v=mp9Bed0t0nE}}</ref> Popcaan headlined [[Reggae Sumfest]] the following day.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://reggaesumfest.com/|title=Reggae Sumfest &#124; Our Music, Our Festival}}</ref> ''Forever'' entered the ''Billboard'' Reggae Albums chart at number 2.<ref>Jackson, Kevin (2018) "[http://301-joweb.newscyclecloud.com/entertainment/popcaan-s_140034?profile=1116 Popcaan's Forever debuts at two]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 31 July 2018. Retrieved 11 August 2018</ref>

On 23 December 2018, [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] announced that Popcaan would be signed to [[OVO Sound]] and [[Warner Records]].  Popcaan's third album ''Vanquish'', released on 19 December 2019, was the first project produced with OVO Sound.<ref>{{cite web |title=Popcaan's 'Vanquish' Mixtape Out Now On Drake's OVO Sound |url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2019/12/20/music/popcaans-vanquish-mixtape-out-now-on-drakes-ovo-sound.html |website=DancehallMag}}</ref> Popcaan's fourth album, ''[[Fixtape (Popcaan album)|Fixtape]]'', was released on 7 August 2020. It includes the Drake collaborations "All I Need" and "Twist & Turn" featuring [[Partynextdoor]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/PopcaanMusic/status/1291393241108144128?s=20|title=FIXTAPE TONIGHT|via=Popcaan Music on [[Twitter]] (confirmed account)|date=6 August 2020|access-date=6 August 2020}}</ref>  On 16 October 2020, Popcaan joined forces with OVO affiliate [[Preme]] for their ''Link Up'' EP, which featured [[Wiz Khalifa|Wiz Khalfia]], [[Davido]], [[Tyshane|BEAM]] and [[French Montana]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=18 October 2020|title=Popcaan, Preme 'Link Up' EP Album Review|url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2020/10/18/features/popcaan-preme-link-up-ep-review.html|access-date=20 October 2020|website=DancehallMag|language=en-US}}</ref>
