'''''Shiesty Season''''' is the debut mixtape by American rapper [[Pooh Shiesty]]. It was released on February 5, 2021, through [[Atlantic Records]] and [[1017 Records]]. The mixtape features [[guest appearance]]s from [[Lil Durk]], [[Gucci Mane]], [[Big30]], [[21 Savage]], Veeze, [[Foogiano]], Lil Hank, Choppa Wop, and [[Tay Keith]]. The "Spring Deluxe" edition of the mixtape was released on May 21, 2021. It features additional guest appearances from [[G Herbo]], No More Heroes, and [[Lil Baby]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty Packs On 4 New Songs To "Shiesty Season" For A Spring Deluxe|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/pooh-shiesty-packs-on-4-new-songs-to-shiesty-season-for-a-spring-deluxe-new-mixtape.121546.html|url-status=live|access-date=May 21, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref>

==Background==
The mixtape was announced on January 15, 2021, alongside the cover art and the release of the single "Guard Up".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/pooh-shiesty-announces-debut-mixtape-shares-video-for-new-song-guard-up-watch/|title=Pooh Shiesty Announces Debut Mixtape, Shares Video for New Song "Guard Up": Watch|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|last=Bloom|first=Madison|date=January 15, 2021|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref> Following its release, Shiesty stated that it was "well overdue and well anticipated".<ref name="HNHH">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/pooh-shiesty-drops-debut-mixtape-shiesty-season-ft-gucci-mane-lil-durk-21-savage-and-more-new-mixtape.121202.html|title=Pooh Shiesty Drops Debut Mixtape "Shiesty Season" Ft. Gucci Mane, Lil Durk, 21 Savage & More|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Marie|first=Erika|date=February 5, 2021|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref> It was released less than a year after he signed to [[Gucci Mane]]'s [[1017 Records]], thanks to his viral track "Shiesty Summer".<ref name="Complex">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/pooh-shiesty-shiesty-season-stream|title=Stream Pooh Shiesty's Debut Mixtape 'Shiesty Season' f/ Gucci Mane, 21 Savage, and More|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=Joshua|first=Espinoza|date=February 5, 2021|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref> Prior to its release, Shiesty had released various singles through the label and appeared on the label compilation ''So Icy Summer''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theboxhouston.com/10169680/pooh-shiesty-interview/|title=Pooh Shiesty Details 'Shiesty Season', Signing To 1017 & More! [Exclusive Interview]|via=[[KBXX|The Box Houston]]|last=Caldwell|first=Brandon|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref>

According to Shiesty, the mixtape sees him "switching it up a lot. I got songs from every genre".<ref name="Complex" /> In an interview with [[Zane Lowe]], he said, "You getting Big Shiest on this mixtape — I'm touching on all categories. Expect me getting versatile. It's lit. You can expect some big features, shooting a video to every song on there. We're going all the way up".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2021/2/5/22265381/pooh-shiesty-shiesty-season-mixtape|title=Pooh Shiesty releases debut mixtape 'Shiesty Season'|work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|author=DJ First Class|date=February 5, 2021|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref> ''[[Stereogum]]''{{'}}s Tom Breihan said the mixtape contains "eerie beats, weirdly catchy death-threats, tons of atmosphere. Rather than shooting for crossover ubiquity, Pooh is doing one thing really well";<ref name="Stereogum" /> as noted by ''[[The Fader]]''{{'}}s Jordan Darville, the mixtape mainly sees Pooh Shiesty "display a slouched flow hinting at an effortlessness to his skills",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/02/05/albums-stream-the-weeknd-pooh-shiesty-weather-station-hayley-williams/|title=The 9 albums you should stream right now|work=The Fader|last=Jordan|first=Darville|date=February 5, 2021|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref> as he details his heavy weaponry and his love for guns in his signature [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]] drawl.<ref name="AM">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/shiesty-season/1548999027|title=Shiesty Season by Pooh Shiesty on Apple Music|via=[[Apple Music]]|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref> Shiesty stated, "I got big features, big producers on here and I got my main producers I been rocking with".<ref name="HNHH" />

==Singles==
On November 6, 2020, Pooh Shiesty released the mixtape's lead single "[[Back in Blood (song)|Back in Blood]]", featuring fellow American rapper [[Lil Durk]], which became his first [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] entry, peaking at number 13 on the chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lil Durk Joins Pooh Shiesty on "Back in Blood"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lil-durk-joins-pooh-shiesty-on-back-in-blood-new-song.1989251.html|website=HotNewHipHop|last=Findlay|first=Mitch|date=November 6, 2020|accessdate=February 5, 2021}}</ref> "Guard Up" was released as the second single along with the announcement of the project on January 15, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty Announces Debut Mixtape, Shares Video for New Song "Guard Up": Watch|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/pooh-shiesty-announces-debut-mixtape-shares-video-for-new-song-guard-up-watch/|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|last=Bloom|first=Madison|date=January 15, 2021|accessdate=February 5, 2021}}</ref> "[[Neighbors (Pooh Shiesty song)|Neighbors]]", featuring fellow American rapper [[Big30]], was released as the third single on February 3, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pooh Shiesty Connects With BIG30 On "Neighbors": Watch|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/pooh-shiesty-connects-with-big30-on-neighbors-new-song.1990144.html|website=HotNewHipHop|last=Zidel|first=Alex|date=February 3, 2021|accessdate=February 5, 2021}}</ref>

===Other songs===
Pooh Shiesty released "Twerksum" as an official single on September 9, 2020, as a part of his label boss [[Gucci Mane]]'s fifth compilation album, ''So Icy Gang, Vol. 1''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/gucci-mane-drops-so-icy-gang-vol-1-ft-mulatto-pooh-shiesty-key-glock-tay-keith-new-mixtape.120811.html|title=Gucci Mane Drops "So Icy Gang Vol. 1" Ft. Mulatto, Pooh Shiesty, Key Glock, Tay Keith|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Marie|first=Erika|date=October 16, 2020|access-date=February 5, 2021}}</ref>

==Commercial performance==
''Shiesty Season'' debuted at number four on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with 62,000 [[album-equivalent unit]]s in its first week of release.<ref name="BB200">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9526109/morgan-wallen-dangerous-tops-billboard-200-for-fifth-week/|title=Morgan Wallen's 'Dangerous' No. 1 for Fifth Week on Billboard 200 While 'If I Know Me' Hits Top 10 for First Time|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=February 14, 2021|accessdate=February 15, 2021}}</ref> The mixtape also spent three weeks atop [[Apple Music]]'s pre-add chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://defpen.com/pooh-shiesty-shiesty-season/|title=Album Stream: Pooh Shiesty – Shiesty Season|work=Def Pen|last=Shepard|first=Ryan|date=February 5, 2021|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=February 2, 2021|title=Pooh Shiesty Spends Another Week Atop Apple Music Pre-Add Chart|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/pooh-shiesty-pre-add-apple-second-week-1122394/|url-status=live|accessdate=February 6, 2021|work=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|last=|first=|date=February 9, 2021|title=Pooh Shiesty Tops Apple Music Pre-Add Chart for Third Straight Week|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/pooh-shiesty-tops-apple-music-pre-add-chart-for-third-straight-week-1125678/|url-status=live|access-date=March 23, 2021|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref> A month later, the mixtape peaked at three on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2021-03-20 Billboard 200 Week of March 20, 2021]</ref>
