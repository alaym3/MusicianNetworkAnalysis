'''Georgio Allentini''', born '''George Allen''' (born 1966), better known by the [[stage name]] and mononym '''Georgio''', is an American [[funk music|funk]] and [[dance music]] singer, songwriter and [[multi-instrumentalist]] based in California. Allentini also produced, directed and starred in the film ''Tapped Out'' in 2003.<ref name="auto3">{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0250023/|title=Tapped Out|website=IMDb.com|access-date=2020-04-20}}</ref>

==Music career==
After giving his [[Demo (music)|demos]] to [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] in a bid to join Prince's [[Paisley Park Records|Paisley Park]], he recorded his own track "Sexappeal" (sometimes "Sex Appeal") on his own Georgio Records.<ref name="LarkinSM">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Soul Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-733-9|page=101}}</ref> "A lot of people in Los Angeles just gave me the run- around and didn't want to make a commitment." Allentini stated.<ref name="auto">[http://www.americanradiohistory.com/hd2/IDX-Business/Music/Archive-Billboard-IDX/IDX/80s/1987/Billboard-1987-03-07-OCR-Page-0026.pdf#search=%22georgio%20allentini%22] {{dead link|date=April 2020}}</ref>

In 1984, [[Motown Records]] showed interest in Allentini and he was eventually signed to the label.<ref name="LarkinSM"/> Said Motown's then-vice-president of [[Artists and repertoire|A&R]] [[Russ Regan]] "I heard ''Sexappeal'' for myself on my car radio one day and felt it was a record Motown had to go after, but I wanted to meet the artist first. I didn't want to buy just a record. We had dinner and I got excited. There was just something about him."<ref name="auto"/>

In 1987, Allentini had several songs charting on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]],<ref name="LarkinSM"/> although none climbed any higher than number 58. He was much more successful on the [[Hot Dance Music/Club Play]] chart, where he earned four Top 10 hits, including "Tina Cherry" which went to number  1.<ref>{{cite book |title= Hot Dance/Disco: 1974-2003|last=Whitburn |first=Joel |authorlink=Joel Whitburn |year=2004 |publisher=Record Research |page=109}}</ref><ref>[http://www.mixedfolks.com/singers2.htm Mixedfolks.com] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120415091209/http://www.mixedfolks.com/singers2.htm |date=2012-04-15 }}</ref> His 1988 release, "Lover's Lane" reached number 54 in the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums">{{cite book

Allentini recorded two albums on Motown Records in the 1980s and one on RCA Records in the 1990s. He also worked at MIG Records.{{citation needed|date=October 2013}}

==Film career==
Georgio made the transition to filmmaker with the 2003 release of ''Tapped Out'', which starred [[Coolio]], Alex Avant and [[Jimmy Bridges (actor)|Jimmy Bridges]]. The film was written, co-produced and co-directed by Allentini, who also composed music for its soundtrack.<ref name="auto3"/>

Allentini also appeared in the 2000 French documentary entitled ''XX elles'' with Estelle Desanges, Lisa Guerlain, Dany Verissimo-Petit and others.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0390797/|title=XX elles|website=IMDb.com|access-date=2020-04-20}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Allentini was born in [[Texas]] in 1966.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.myheritage.com/research/record-10182-623198/|title=Georgio - Biographical Summaries of Notable People - MyHeritage|date=20 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180120124029/https://www.myheritage.com/research/record-10182-623198/|access-date=2021-07-03|archive-date=2018-01-20}}</ref> His mother was a [[Retail clerk|retail salesperson]], his father a [[Petty officer|Naval officer]].<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0021210/bio|title=Georgio|website=IMDb.com|access-date=2020-04-20}}</ref><ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/archive/his-last-500-gambled-on-a-sexy-tape-princely-georgio-allentini-signs-on-to-make-millions-with-motown-vol-27-no-26/|title=His Last $500 Gambled on a Sexy Tape, Princely Georgio Allentini Signs on to Make Millions with Motown|website=People.com}}</ref> Georgio moved to [[Los Angeles]] at age 17.<ref name="auto1"/><ref name="auto2"/> He is married to actress Kelly Jo Minter.
