'''Angel Del Villar II'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/2016/05/homeboy-sandman-kindness-for-weakness-interview/|title=Gimme Some Truth: Life Studies With Homeboy Sandman|website=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|first=Andrew|last=Unterberger|date=May 6, 2016|access-date=January 2, 2017}}</ref> (born September 24, 1980<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/homeboy-sandman-mn0001082592|title=Homeboy Sandman - Overview|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=January 2, 2017}}</ref>), better known by his stage name '''Homeboy Sandman''', is an American rapper from [[Elmhurst, Queens]], New York, United States.

==Career==
Homeboy Sandman recorded and released his first EP, ''Nourishment'', in March 2007.  His debut album, entitled ''Nourishment (Second Helpings)'', was released in August 2007.  In early 2008, Homeboy Sandman's work began to be featured on underground radio programs the Squeeze Radio Show on WKCR 89.9 FM and the Halftime Radio Program on WNYU 89.1 FM in New York.  In June 2008, he was featured in Source Magazine's "Unsigned Hype" column.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://img685.imageshack.us/img685/8250/homeboysandmanthesource.jpg|title=The Source - Unsigned Hype June 2008|format=JPG|website=Img685.imageshack.us}}</ref>

His second album, ''Actual Factual Pterodactyl'' was acclaimed upon its release in August 2008.  The album received an entry in ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''{{'}}s ''Chairman's Choice'' column, which praised Homeboy Sandman's "sharp lyrics and irresistibly melodic flow, which, together, form an elastic instrument few MCs can match."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://img525.imageshack.us/img525/2729/homeboysandmanxxlmagazi.jpg|title=XXL Magazine - Chairman's Choice Jan/Feb 2009|format=JPG|website=Img525.imageshack.us}}</ref>  Actual Factual Pterodactyl received critical acclaim in a multitude of other print and online publications, including Blender Magazine, Beyond Race Magazine, Okayplayer.com and MSN.com.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/music|title=News, reviews, interviews and more for top artists and albums – MSN Music|website=Msn.com}}</ref>  At the end of 2008, Homeboy Sandman was named "Best Hip Hop Act in NYC 2008" by New York Press.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://img710.imageshack.us/img710/8690/homeboysandmannewyorkpr.jpg|title=New York Press: Best Hip Hop Act in NYC 2008|format=JPG|website=Img710.imageshack.us}}</ref>  Recently his music has received rotation on NYC's Power 105.1 FM and Hot 97.1 FM, the city's #1 Hip Hop and R&B radio station, as well as airplay on Spitkicker Radio (Channel 65 on XM Satellite Radio), DJ Premier's "Live From HeadQCourterz" (Sirius Satellite Radio's #1 ranked Hip Hop show) and BBC 1Xtra's Hip Hop M1X (Hosted by DJ Sarah Love).

In addition to his studio releases, Homeboy Sandman has garnered attention for his live performances.  From January 2008 to August 2009, Homeboy Sandman served as the host of “ALL THAT! Hip Hop, Poetry and Jazz” at the [[Nuyorican Poets Café]], New York City’s longest running open mic session (originally hosted by Hip Hop icon [[Bobbito García]]).  He has performed at the biggest hip hop shows around the country, including the [[South by Southwest]] (SXSW) music festival in [[Austin, Texas]], the Brooklyn Hip Hop Festival in Brooklyn, New York, the [[Rock the Bells]] hip hop festival in [[Long Island, New York]], the A3C Hip Hop Festival in [[Atlanta, Georgia]] and New York City's [[CMJ Music Marathon]].  Homeboy Sandman has recorded video interviews with other performers at his live shows, which are captured in his YouTube video series "Homeboy Sandman ''presents'' Live From..."{{citation needed|date=January 2017}}

In addition to his own releases, Homeboy Sandman has appeared on records by other artists, frequently with members of the AOK Collective, with whom he is affiliated.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}  He recently appeared on Fresh Daily's 2009 album ''The Gorgeous Killer: In Crimes of Passion'' and the 2008 album ''For Your Consideration'' released by P.SO (formerly P. Casso).  Homeboy Sandman collaborated with both Fresh Daily and P.SO for the song "Get On Down," which is featured on ''Sonic Smash'', the album released in 2009 by music producer [[DJ Spinna]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://vimeo.com/groups/nahh/videos/7915827|title=DJ Spinna - "Get On Down" (Video) Ft. Fresh Daily, P.SO and Homeboy Sandman|website=Vimeo.com}}</ref> Homeboy Sandman has also laid down tracks for a series of features with RIDES Magazine.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rides-mag.com/|title=Rides Magazine &#124; Custom Cars, Donks, Rims & Car Culture|website=Rides Magazine}}</ref> The track that Homeboy Sandman created for the Loud.com challenge, "Gun Control," was cited as one of the Top 25 Songs of 2009 by Hot 97 DJ [[Peter Rosenberg]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rosenbergradio.com/2009/12/28/my-top-25-of-2009/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091231042611/http://www.rosenbergradio.com/2009/12/28/my-top-25-of-2009/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2009-12-31 |title=Homeboy Sandman - "Gun Control" (Hot 97 DJ Peter Rosenberg Top 25 of 2009)}}</ref>

On June 1, 2010, Homeboy Sandman released his third album, ''The Good Sun'', which features production from 2 Hungry Bros, [[Ski Beats]], Thievin' Stephen, DJ Spinna, Psycho Les ([[The Beatnuts]]) and Core Rhythm, among others.  The first single from ''The Good Sun'' was "Angels with Dirty Faces," produced by [[J57]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.okayplayer.com/news/Audio-Homeboy-Sandman-Angels-with-Dirty-Faces.html|title=Audio: Homeboy Sandman "Angels with Dirty Faces"|date=November 24, 2009|website=Okayplayer.com}}</ref>   In September 2010, Homeboy Sandman released his first official video for ''The Good Sun'' for the track, "The Essence".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/videos/id.6305/title.homeboy-sandman-the-essence/|title=The Essence|publisher=Hiphopdx.com|access-date=2014-09-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120929235255/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/videos/id.6305/title.homeboy-sandman-the-essence|archive-date=2012-09-29|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In late 2010, Homeboy Sandman was featured as the coach in an episode of MTV's ''[[Made (TV series)|Made]]'', which premiered in October 2010.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/videos/made-season-11-ep-14-rapper/1649522/playlist.jhtml|title=MTV's MADE (Season 11) l Ep. 14 l Rapper (feat. Homeboy Sandman)|website=Mtv.com}}</ref>

In August 2011, Homeboy Sandman signed with [[Stones Throw Records]] and released his debut project, ''Subject: Matter EP'', with the California-based record label on January 24, 2012. Homeboy Sandman released his second EP with Stones Throw Records, titled ''Chimera'' in April 2012 and on September 18, 2012 released his debut full-length LP with Stones Throw entitled ''[[First of a Living Breed]]''. The album featured production from [[Oddisee]], J57, 6th Sense, RTNC and long-time production partners 2 Hungry Bros., along with label mates [[Jonwayne]] and [[Oh No (musician)|Oh No]], amongst others.{{citation needed|date=January 2017}}

In 2014, Homeboy Sandman released the EP, ''White Sands'', which was produced by [[Paul White (producer)|Paul White]]. Nate Patrin of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' commented that the producer and rapper had "a mutually beneficial set of styles" in a positive review.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19034-homeboy-sandman-white-sands/|title=Homeboy Sandman: White Sands|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Nate|last=Patrin|date=February 25, 2014|access-date=January 2, 2017}}</ref> In that year, Homeboy Sandman released the album, ''[[Hallways (album)|Hallways]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uproxx.com/smokingsection/homeboy-sandman-america-beautiful/|title=Homeboy Sandman Checks The U.S. On "America, The Beautiful"|website=[[Uproxx]]|first=Beware|last=Bowdon|date=July 27, 2014|access-date=January 2, 2017}}</ref>

In the wake of the [[Donald Sterling|Donald Sterling scandal]], Homeboy Sandman wrote for [[Gawker]] an op-ed called "Black People are Cowards," which attracted more than 1.3 million views in the first seven days of its publication.<ref name=Gawker>{{cite news|last=Sandman |first=Homeboy |title=Black People are Cowards |url=http://gawker.com/black-people-are-cowards-1568673014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140429081447/http://gawker.com/black-people-are-cowards-1568673014 |url-status=dead |archive-date=April 29, 2014 |access-date=May 4, 2014 |newspaper=Gawker |date=April 28, 2014 }}</ref>

In 2015, Homeboy Sandman released a collaborative EP with [[Aesop Rock]], titled ''Lice''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.36478/title.aesop-rock-homeboy-sandman-lice-release-date-cover-art-tracklist-download-ep-stream|title=Aesop Rock & Homeboy Sandman "Lice" Release Date, Cover Art, Tracklist, Download & EP Stream|website=[[HipHopDX]]|first=Soren|last=Baker|date=December 1, 2015|access-date=January 2, 2017}}</ref> In 2016, he released the album, ''[[Kindness for Weakness]]'', which included [[Edan (musician)|Edan]]-produced "Talking (Bleep)".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.38013/title.homeboy-sandman-announces-kindness-for-weakness-album|title=Homeboy Sandman Announces "Kindness For Weakness" Album|website=[[HipHopDX]]|first=Victoria|last=Hernandez|date=March 22, 2016|access-date=January 2, 2017}}</ref> In that year, he released a collaborative EP with Aesop Rock, titled ''Lice Two: Still Buggin'''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1902189/download-aesop-rock-homeboy-sandmans-free-ep-lice-two-still-buggin/mp3s/|title=Download Aesop Rock & Homeboy Sandman's Free EP Lice Two: Still Buggin'|website=[[Stereogum]]|first=Tom|last=Breihan|date=September 29, 2016|access-date=January 2, 2017}}</ref>

In 2019, Homeboy Sandman announced he had signed a new deal with Mello Music Group <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2045474/homeboy-sandman-aesop-rock-west-coast-new-song-listen/music/|title=Homeboy Sandman - "West Coast" (Prod. Aesop Rock)|website=Stereogum.com|date=May 29, 2019}}</ref> and released the new single "West Coast" produced by Aesop Rock to celebrate the new label signing.
