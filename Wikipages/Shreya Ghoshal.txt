'''Shreya Ghoshal''' (born 12 March 1984) is an Indian [[playback singer]].<!--NOTE:Her Indian nationality is already in the infobox---> She has received four<!--NOTE:Before changing any awards number, see the 'Awards and nominations' section.---> [[National Film Awards]], four [[Kerala State Film Awards]], two [[Tamil Nadu State Film Awards]], seven [[Filmfare Awards]] and ten [[Filmfare Awards South]]. She has recorded songs for films and albums in various Indian languages and has established herself as a leading playback singer of [[Indian cinema]].

Shreya aspired to become a playback singer from an early age. At the age of four, she started learning music. At the age of six, she started her formal training in classical music. At the age of sixteen, she was noticed by the mother of filmmaker [[Sanjay Leela Bhansali]] when she entered and won the television singing reality show ''[[Sa Re Ga Ma Pa|Sa Re Ga Ma]]''. Following that, she made her Bollywood playback singing debut with Bhansali's romantic drama ''[[Devdas (2002 Hindi film)|Devdas]]'' (2002) for which she received a [[National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer|National Film Award]], a [[Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] and a [[Filmfare RD Burman Award for New Music Talent]].

Apart from playback singing, Shreya has appeared as a judge on several television reality shows and she also appears in music videos. She performs in musical concerts around the world. She has been honoured by the United States [[state (polity)|state]] of [[Ohio]], where Governor [[Ted Strickland]] declared 26 June 2010 as "Shreya Ghoshal Day". In April 2013, she was honoured in London by the selected members of [[House of Commons of the United Kingdom]]. She has also featured five times in the [[Forbes]] list of the top 100 celebrities from India. In 2017, Shreya became the first Indian singer to have her wax figurine in [[Madame Tussauds]] Museum.

=== Bollywood debut (2002–04) ===
Shreya caught the attention of director [[Sanjay Leela Bhansali]] when she participated in the 75th children's special episode of ''Sa Re Ga Ma''. Bhansali's mother was watching the show and during Ghoshal's performance, she called him to watch her performance, after which he decided to give her a chance in his next film.<ref name="facts"/> According to Bhansali, Shreya's voice had the innocence needed for the character of Paro in ''[[Devdas (2002 Hindi film)|Devdas]]'' (2002).<ref name="voice of paro"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/shreya-ghoshal-mn0000600596/biography|title=Shreya Ghoshal|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=22 September 2015|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151001172221/http://www.allmusic.com/artist/shreya-ghoshal-mn0000600596/biography|archive-date=1 October 2015}}</ref>

In 2000, Bhansali and music director [[Ismail Darbar]] offered her the opportunity to be the voice of Paro, the lead female character of ''Devdas'', who was portrayed by [[Aishwarya Rai]].<ref name="voice of paro"/><ref>{{cite news|title=Singing in Devdas was God's greatest gift: Shreya Ghoshal|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/calcutta-times/Singing-in-Devdas-was-Gods-greatest-gift-Shreya-Ghoshal/articleshow/16690191.cms|last=Dasgupta|first=Priyanka|work=The Times of India|access-date=23 July 2012|date=21 July 2002|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121104005632/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/calcutta-times/Singing-in-Devdas-was-Gods-greatest-gift-Shreya-Ghoshal/articleshow/16690191.cms|archive-date=4 November 2012}}</ref> Shreya sang five songs in the film, namely, "Silsila Ye Chaahat Ka", "[[Bairi Piya]]", "Chalak Chalak", "Morey Piya", and "[[Dola Re Dola]]", with established singers such as [[Kavita Krishnamurthy]], [[Udit Narayan]], [[Vinod Rathod]], [[KK (singer)|KK]], and [[Jaspinder Narula]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Original Soundtrack Devdas|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/devdas-original-soundtrack-mw0001261240|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=29 September 2015|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151001173146/http://www.allmusic.com/album/devdas-original-soundtrack-mw0001261240|archive-date=1 October 2015}}</ref> She was sixteen when she recorded the first song for the film, "Bairi Piya" with Udit Narayan.
