'''Charlotte Cardin''' (born November 9, 1994) is a Canadian [[Pop music|pop]], [[Electronic music|electro]] and [[jazz]] singer and songwriter from [[Montreal]], [[Quebec]].<ref name=passion>[http://www.journaldequebec.com/2016/07/16/la-passion-de-charlotte-cardin "La passion de Charlotte Cardin"]. ''[[Le Journal de Québec]]'', July 16, 2016.</ref> Cardin began her career as a model at the age of 15, where she appeared in numerous advertising campaigns such as ''Barilà''.<ref>[http://www.journaldemontreal.com/2013/03/06/charlotte-mannequin "CANDIDATE À LA VOIX – Charlotte : mannequin"]. ''[[Le Journal de Montréal]]'', March 7, 2013.</ref>

== Career ==
A Top 4 finalist in the [[La Voix (season 1)|first season]] of the [[TVA (Canadian TV network)|TVA]] singing competition ''[[La Voix]]'' in 2013,<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/charlotte_cardin_talks_life_after_reality_tv_trusting_her_instincts_on_new_big_boy_ep "Charlotte Cardin Talks Life After Reality TV and Trusting Her Instincts on New 'Big Boy EP'"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', July 22, 2016.</ref> later the same year she was featured on [[Garou (singer)|Garou]]'s album ''[[Au milieu de ma vie]]'' as a duet vocalist on the single "Du vent, des mots".<ref>[http://www.journaldemontreal.com/2013/10/19/garou-recrute-charlotte-pour-son-nouvel-album "Garou recrute Charlotte pour son nouvel album"]. ''[[Le Journal de Montréal]]'', October 19, 2013.</ref> On March 1, 2014, she appeared on the sister series ''[[The Voice: la plus belle voix]]'' in France to perform the song with Garou, a judge on that edition.<ref>[http://www.staragora.com/news/the-voice-3-revivez-le-duo-de-garou-et-charlotte-cardin-finaliste-de-the-voice-canada-lors-des-battles-video/480287 "The Voice 3 : Revivez le duo de Garou et Charlotte Cardin, finaliste de The Voice Canada lors des Battles VIDEO"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161018200616/http://www.staragora.com/news/the-voice-3-revivez-le-duo-de-garou-et-charlotte-cardin-finaliste-de-the-voice-canada-lors-des-battles-video/480287 |date=October 18, 2016 }}. ''Cosmopolitan'' (France), March 2, 2014.</ref>

She released her solo debut EP, ''[[Big Boy (EP by Charlotte Cardin)|Big Boy]]'', in 2016 on Cult Nation Records.<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/charlotte_cardin-big_boy_ep "Charlotte Cardin: Big Boy EP"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', July 13, 2016.</ref> The EP featured songs in both English and French,<ref name=passion/> and included rapper [[Nate Husser]] as a featured artist on "Like It Doesn't Hurt".<ref>Matt Joycey, [https://www.vice.com/en/article/64dynp/charlotte-cardin-x-husser "Love and Death Are Intertwined in Charlotte Cardin's Video for 'Like It Doesn't Hurt'"]. ''[[Noisey]]'', July 29, 2016.</ref> She was a shortlisted [[SOCAN Songwriting Prize]] finalist in the French division for her song "Les échardes",<ref>[http://www.chartattack.com/news/2016/06/09/these-are-the-10-best-canadian-songs-of-the-year-according-to-socan/ "These are the 10 best Canadian songs of the year according to SOCAN's Songwriting Prize"]. [[chartattack.com]], June 9, 2016.</ref> and the title track "Big Boy" was playlisted on [[CBC Radio 2]] and charted on the ''[[Radio 2 Top 20]]''.<ref>[http://www.cbcmusic.ca/posts/12790/top-20-sep-30-charlotte-cardin-debuts-kings-leon-1 "Radio 2 Top 20, Sept. 30: Frank Ocean & Charlotte Cardin debut, Kings of Leon #1"]. [[CBC Music]], October 4, 2016.</ref> She made an appearance on the talk show ''[[Tout le monde en parle (Quebec)|Tout le monde en parle]]''<ref>[http://ici.radio-canada.ca/tele/tout-le-monde-en-parle/2016-2017/episodes/367036/patrick-lagace-charlotte-cardin-monic-neron-marie-maude-denis-felix-seguin-la-soiree-est-encore-jeune-denis-coderre-france-caster-john-parisella-fabien-cloutier?isautoplay=true "ÉPISODE DU DIMANCHE 6 NOVEMBRE 2016"]. ''[[ICI Radio-Canada Télé]]'', November 6, 2016.</ref> to discuss her mini-album in November 2016.<ref>[http://ici.radio-canada.ca/tele/tout-le-monde-en-parle/2016-2017/segments/entrevue/10635/charlotte-cardin "LE MONDE DE CHARLOTTE"]. ''[[ICI Radio-Canada Télé]]'', November 6, 2016.</ref>

For the 2017 SOCAN Songwriting Prize, she was a nominee in the English category for "Big Boy" and in the French category for "Faufile", becoming the first artist in the history of the award to be nominated in both categories in the same year.<ref>[[Alan Cross]], [http://ajournalofmusicalthings.com/music-fans-cast-votes-2017-socan-songwriting-prize/ "Music Fans: Cast Your Votes for the 2017 SOCAN Songwriting Prize"]. ''A Journal of Musical Things'', June 17, 2017.</ref>

In 2017 she released her second EP, ''Main Girl''.<ref>[https://beta.theglobeandmail.com/arts/music/big-curtain-about-to-rise-for-montreals-charlotte-cardin/article36353130/ "Big curtain about to rise for Montreal's Charlotte Cardin"]. ''[[The Globe and Mail]]'', September 21, 2017.</ref> The title track reached No. 1 on the Radio 2 Top 20.<ref>[http://www.cbcmusic.ca/posts/19091/radio-2-top-20-sept-27-fast-romantics-debut "Radio 2 Top 20, Sept. 28: Fast Romantics, Elliott Brood debut and Charlotte Cardin is your new #1"]. ''[[Radio 2 Top 20]]'', September 28, 2017.</ref>

She received two [[Juno Award]] nominations at the [[Juno Awards of 2018]], for [[Juno Award for Breakthrough Artist of the Year|Breakthrough Artist of the Year]] and [[Juno Award for Songwriter of the Year|Songwriter of the Year]] for "Main Girl", "Paradise Motion" and "The Kids".<ref>[http://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/junos-news/arcade-fire-and-daniel-caesar-lead-2018-juno-award-nominations-1.4520940 "Arcade Fire and Daniel Caesar lead 2018 Juno Award nominations"]. [[CBC Music]], February 6, 2018.</ref>

In 2018, she received another SOCAN Songwriting Prize nomination for "Main Girl".<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/2018_socan_songwriting_prize_nominees_revealed "2018 SOCAN Songwriting Prize Nominees Revealed"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', May 24, 2018.</ref>

In 2019, Cardin and producer [[CRi]] collaborated on a cover of [[Daniel Bélanger]]'s "Fous n'importe où",<ref>[https://voir.ca/nouvelles/actualite-musicale/2019/01/16/charlotte-cardin-et-cri-reprennent-daniel-belanger/ "Charlotte Cardin et CRi reprennent Daniel Bélanger"]. ''[[Voir]]'', January 16, 2019.</ref> and Cardin appeared on [[Loud (rapper)|Loud]]'s album ''Tout ça pour ça'' as a duet vocalist on the track "Sometimes, All the Time".<ref>[https://ici.radio-canada.ca/nouvelle/1169106/tout-ca-pour-ca-loud-deuxieme-album-pop-assumee "Tout ça pour ça : Loud revient avec un deuxième album à la pop assumée"]. [[Ici Radio-Canada]], May 10, 2019.</ref> In 2020 she released the singles "Passive Aggressive"<ref>Calum Slingerland, [https://exclaim.ca/music/article/charlotte_cardin_returns_with_new_song_passive_aggressive "Charlotte Cardin Returns with New Song 'Passive Aggressive'"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', September 18, 2020.</ref> and "Daddy"; both tracks were recorded for her full-length debut album ''[[Phoenix (Charlotte Cardin album)|Phoenix]]'', which was released on April 23, 2021.<ref>Calum Slingerland, [https://exclaim.ca/music/article/charlotte_cardin_readies_debut_album_phoenix?&utm_source=TwitterEN&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=ENTwitter "Charlotte Cardin Readies Debut Album 'Phoenix'"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', February 12, 2021.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Weil-Brenner|first=Luc|date=March 1, 2021|title=Charlotte Cardin reporte la sortie de son album Phoenix|url=https://www.iheartradio.ca/nouvelles/charlotte-cardin-reporte-la-sortie-de-son-album-phoenix-1.14678398|url-status=live|access-date=April 3, 2021|website=www.iheartradio.ca|language=fr-CA}}</ref> ''Phoenix'' is co-written with Cardin's longtime manager Jason Brando of Cult Nation.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Stanley|first=Laura|date=March 23, 2021|title=Charlotte Cardin Rises from the Ashes and Finds Her Authentic Self|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/charlotte_cardin_phoenix_cover_story_interview|url-status=live|access-date=April 3, 2021|website=exclaim.ca|language=en-ca}}</ref>

''Phoenix'' won the [[Felix Award]] for Anglophone Album of the Year at the [[43rd Félix Awards]]. Cardin also won the awards for Anglophone Concert of the Year and Most Successful Artist Outside Quebec.<ref>Valérie Marcoux, [https://www.lesoleil.com/2021/11/08/gala-de-ladisq-une-annee-riche-en-felix-pour-klo-pelgag-d01bf16c0c9d351f00a20f280527d6d8 "Gala de l’ADISQ: une année riche en Félix pour Klô Pelgag"]. ''[[Le Soleil (Quebec)|Le Soleil]]'', November 7, 2021.</ref>

==Personal life==

Cardin grew up in the Montreal suburb of [[Mount Royal, Quebec|Mount Royal]]. She is in a relationship with actor [[Aliocha Schneider]].<ref name=naked>Marissa Groguhé, [https://www.lapresse.ca/arts/musique/2020-03-21/naked-d-aliocha-aller-ailleurs "Naked d’Aliocha: aller ailleurs"]. ''[[La Presse (Canadian newspaper)|La Presse]]'', March 21, 2020.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mondedestars.com/nouvelles/charlotte-cardin-presente-officiellement-son-amoureux|title=Charlotte Cardin présente officiellement son amoureux|date=April 14, 2021|website=Monde de Stars|accessdate=September 9, 2021}}</ref>
