'''Kelsey Elizabeth McJunkins''', known professionally as '''Kelsey Lu''' (born May 12, 1991) is a African-American singer and cellist based in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/story/2019-08-20/kelsey-lu-la-black-art-scene|title=Solange. Blood Orange. Kelsey Lu. There's a new wave of genre-bending black artists|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]|first=Makeda|last=Easter|date=August 20, 2019|accessdate=April 29, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Kelsey Lu was born in [[Charlotte]], [[North Carolina]] and had a strict [[Jehovah's Witnesses|Jehovah's Witness]] upbringing.<ref name="nylon1"/> Both of Lu's parents are musicians: their father is a percussionist, their mother a pianist.<ref name="elle1"/> Lu began studying classical composition at age 6, learning piano, violin, and cello.<ref name="nylon1">{{cite web|url=https://nylon.com/articles/kelsey-lu-interview|title=Cellist Kelsey Lu Will Have You Under Her Spell|website=[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]]|first=Sydney|last=Gore|date=February 15, 2016|accessdate=April 29, 2019}}</ref> At age 18, Lu left their family home to attend the [[University of North Carolina School of the Arts]] on a scholarship.<ref name="elle1">{{cite web|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/music/news/a37641/kelsey-lu-interview/|title=Kelsey Lu Doesn't Sound Like Anyone Else|website=[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]]|first=Emma|last=Hager|date=July 15, 2016|accessdate=April 29, 2019}}</ref> A year later, they dropped out of the school.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/9934-kelsey-lus-dreams-are-better-than-yours/|title=Kelsey Lu's Dreams Are Better Than Yours|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Alex|last=Frank|date=August 12, 2016|accessdate=April 2, 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
Kelsey Lu recorded their debut EP, ''Church'', in a church in [[Greenpoint, Brooklyn]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/25/fashion/kelsey-lu-mcjunkins-hitting-the-stages-of-afropunk-and-barclays-center.html|title=Kelsey Lu McJunkins, Hitting the Stages of Afropunk and Barclays Center|website=[[The New York Times]]|first=Joanna|last=Nikas|date=August 25, 2016|accessdate=April 2, 2020}}</ref> During this time, they toured with the band [[Wet (band)|Wet]] as their opening act.<ref name="nylon1"/> They released ''Church'' in 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2016/11/10/kelsey-lu-cello-solange-blood-orange/|title=Kelsey Lu is the maverick cellist whose loop experiments have wooed Blood Orange and Solange|website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]|first=Claire|last=Lobenfeld|date=November 10, 2016|accessdate=April 29, 2019}}</ref> They released their debut album, ''Blood'', in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/ywygay/kelsey-lu-interview-2019-blood-album|title=Kelsey Lu Had to Manifest Hopeful Pop from Pain|website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|first=Tshepo|last=Mokoena|date=April 24, 2019|accessdate=April 29, 2019}}</ref> In that year, they also released ''Blood Transfusion'', a collection of remixes of tracks from ''Blood''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2019/11/15/kelsey-lu-enlists-lafawndah-omar-s-and-skrillex-for-blood-transfusion-remix-ep/|title=Kelsey Lu enlists Lafawndah, Omar S and Skrillex for Blood Transfusion remix EP|website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]|first=Henry|last=Bruce-Jones|date=November 15, 2019|accessdate=April 29, 2019}}</ref>
