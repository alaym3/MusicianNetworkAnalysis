'''Medina Danielle Oona Valbak'''<ref>https://www.seoghoer.dk/nyheder/nu-hedder-medina-ikke-laengere-medina</ref> (born '''Andrea Fuentealba Valbak''';<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Medina på spansk|url=https://www.seoghoer.dk/nyheder/medina-paa-spansk|access-date=2021-03-22|website=SE og HØR|language=da}}</ref> 30 November 1982), known as '''Medina''' ({{IPA-da|me̝ˈtiːnæ|lang}}; {{IPA-es|meˈðina|lang}}), was born in Denmark, and is a Danish singer and songwriter. She currently has released one EP, and seven studio albums. She became popular with various singles such as "[[Kun for mig]]" in Scandinavia, and "Addiction" in various European and English-speaking countries. At the 2010 Danish Music Awards, she won female artist of the year, Album of the year, and Songwriter of the year.<ref name="Medina's Biography on Allmusic">{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/medina-mn0001747693|title=Allmusic: Medina (Overview)|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=1 March 2013}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2007: ''Tæt på'' ===

She released her first two single's in Denmark titled "Flå" ("Rip") and "Et Øjeblik" ("One Moment") in 2007. This was followed by her debut album, ''[[Tæt på]]'' (''Close by'').

=== 2008–2010: ''Welcome to Medina'' and rise to fame ===

She rose to fame primarily within several countries in Western Europe in 2008 with the release of "Kun for mig" (which was later released in English as "You and I"), the lead single off her second album, ''[[Velkommen til Medina]]'' (''Welcome to Medina'').<ref name="Medina's Biography on Allmusic"/> The single spent six weeks at number one on the [[Danish Singles Chart]], eventually reaching triple-platinum status.<ref name="Denmark's position for Kun for mig">{{cite web |url=https://danishcharts.dk/showitem.asp?interpret=Medina&titel=Kun+for+mig&cat=s|title=Hung Medien: Medina "Kun for mig"|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=10 December 2010}}</ref><ref name="Denmark's certification-awards for 2010">{{cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.dk/index.php?pk_menu=284 |title=Guld og platin 2010 2014 }}</ref> The second single off the same album,  "[[Velkommen til Medina (song)|Velkommen til Medina]]" also charted number one in Denmark which spent five weeks at the top of the singles chart and reached a platinum status there.<ref name="Denmark's position for Velkommen til Medina (song)">{{cite web |url=https://danishcharts.dk/showitem.asp?interpret=Medina&titel=Velkommen+til+Medina&cat=s|title=Hung Medien: Medina "Velkommen til Medina (song)"|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=10 December 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141223062515/http://www.ifpi.dk/index.php?pk_menu=262 |archive-date=23 December 2014 }}</ref> Medina's third and fourth single, "Ensom" ("Lonely") and "Vi to" ("The two of us") peaked at number two in Denmark, and they both earned her a platinum-award.<ref name="Denmark's certification-awards for 2010"/><ref name="Denmark's positions In September 2009, Medina released an English-language version of "Kun for mig", titled ''[[You and I (Medina song)|You and I]]'', in the UK, Germany, Austria and Switzerland. This version reached thirty-nine on the [[UK Singles Chart]] and entered the top-ten on the German Singles Chart.<ref name="UK's positions for Medina">{{cite web |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artists/|title=The Official Charts Company: Medina|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=10 December 2010}}</ref><ref name="Germany's positions for Medina (Singles)">{{cite web |url=http://www.officialcharts.de/search.asp?search=Medina&x=9&y=8&cat=s&country=de|title= Charts.de (Singles)|publisher=Charts.de [[Media Control Charts|Media Control]]|access-date=26 August 2011}}</ref>

In 2009, two collaborative singles of Medina have also reached the top of the Danish charts. First is "100 dage" by Medina and [[Thomas Helmig]] and the second is "[[Mest ondt]]" by [[Burhan G]] featuring Medina.

Awards won by Medina include ''Best Danish Act'' at the [[MTV Europe Music Awards 2009|2009]] and [[MTV Europe Music Awards 2011|2011 MTV Europe Music Awards]].<ref name="Labelmade: Medina">{{cite web|url=http://www.labelmade.dk/Medina.html |title=Labelmade: Medina |publisher=Hung Medien |access-date=10 December 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100703031445/http://labelmade.dk/Medina.html |archive-date=3 July 2010 }}</ref> In 2010 Medina won six awards at the [[Danish Music Awards 2010|Danish Music Awards]], she was awarded Danish Female Artist of the Year, Danish Album of the Year (Velkommen til Medina), New Danish Name of the Year, Danish Hit of the Year (Vi to), Danish Songwriter of the Year (together with [[Providers]]) and Danish Producer of the Year ([[Providers]] for Medina).<ref>[http://www.soundvenue.com/nyheder/2010/11/15/medina-ryddede-bordet-til-danish-music-awards Soundvenue.com] (''in Danish'')</ref>

In July 2010, Medina's international debut album, ''Welcome to Medina'' was released. It reached 9th in Germany, 45th in Austria, and 24th in Switzerland.<ref name="Germany's positions for Medina (Albums)">{{cite web|url=http://www.musicline.de/de/chartverfolgung_summary/artist/Medina/?type=longplay|title=Chartverfolgung / Medina /Longplay|publisher=Musicline.de|access-date=10 December 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121009141604/http://www.musicline.de/de/chartverfolgung_summary/artist/Medina/?type=longplay|archive-date=9 October 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="Medina's positions for Welcome to Medina">{{cite web |url=http://www.swisscharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Medina&titel=Welcome+To+Medina&cat=a|title=Hung Medien: Medina "Velkommen til Medina (album)"|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=10 December 2010}}</ref> Apart from seven new songs, the album also includes English-language versions of the four singles off its Danish counterpart, including "Lonely" (English version of "Ensom"), it was released as the second single from the album.

=== 2011–2012: ''For Altid'' and ''Forever'' ===

In 2011 her Danish studio album ''For Altid'' was released, including the single's "Synd for Dig", "For Altid", "Kl. 10", "12 Dage", "Lyser i Morke", and "Har du Glemt". The repackaged special edition included the final single "Junkie (featuring Svenstrup)", the latter released single's take on a more EDM, Dance-Pop sound, reminiscent of ''Velkommen til Medina''. 
It was followed up in 2012 by the English version of the same name, titled ''Forever''.

=== 2013–2018: Hiatus, small projects, and return with ''We Survive'' and ''Grim'' ===

From 2013 to 2015 Medina went on a short hiatus between 2012–14, later embarking on smaller projects, including the release of an urban inspired EP, and a live album, which are both primarily in Danish.

Since 2016 she has released two studio albums, ''We Survive'' (English studio album) in 2016, and ''Grim'' (Danish studio album) in 2018.

=== 2019–present: New singles, ''Det Bliver Altid Forår'', and upcoming album ===

Medina has released several new singles (featuring a single with the band Zookeeper's). In the first half of 2020 she released a new live album titled ''Det Bliver Altid Forår'', the album includes several of her most famous Danish singles recorded and performed amidst a live audience, prior to the outbreak of Covid-19. She also married her partner in mid 2020. In July 2020 Medina announced via social media that new music was coming, leading to speculation that she would be releasing a new album, it is set to include multiple languages, a first for Medina. However, it has not been confirmed by Medina whether those rumours are true. Medina's new album is set to have a more urban, and EDM sound compared to her previous studio albums.

In the latter half of 2020 Medina would go onto release several new singles, mostly in Danish, including several Danish covers from the new season of "Toppen Af Poppen" (Danish TV Show), where she makes a return for the new season of the show.

Her fans call themselves "Medivas".<ref name=":1" />

== Personal life ==
Medina's father is Chilean,<ref name=":0" /> her mother is a Dane that grew up in Malaysia.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Medina|url=https://www.seoghoer.dk/vipedia/medina|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-22|website=[[Se og hør]]|language=da}}</ref> She changed her name in 2005 to Medina Danielle Oona Valbak after visiting a [[numerologist]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> The name "Medina" is not a reference to the Saudi Arabian city of [[Medina]], rather it is a common name in Chile.<ref name=":1" />

In 2007 she was in a relationship with Samir Soud. The coupled had planned to get married,<ref name=":1" /> but they broke up in the wake of the success of Medina's first album.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Lundtang|first=Anders|title=Medinas forliste forhold gennem tiden|url=https://www.seoghoer.dk/nyheder/medinas-forliste-forhold-gennem-tiden|access-date=2021-03-22|website=SE og HØR|language=da}}</ref> Shortly after that she had a relationship with dancer Mathias Arvedsen until 2011.<ref name=":2" /> She has also dated Danish singer [[Xander (Danish singer)|Xander]].<ref name=":2" /> From 2012 to 2014 she dated and lived with Danish singer [[Christopher (singer)|Christopher]],<ref name=":2" /> who is 9 years younger.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=dkeditorial|date=2012-09-03|title=Medina og Christopher flytter sammen|url=https://www.billedbladet.dk/kendte/danmark/musik/medina-og-christopher-flytter-sammen|access-date=2021-03-22|website=BILLED-BLADET|language=da}}</ref> As of 2014 she was in a relationship with model Mikkel Gregers Jensen;<ref name=":0" /> they broke up in 2015.<ref name=":2" />

Medina was confirmed in August 2020 to be expecting her first child with husband Malo Chapsal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.seoghoer.dk/kendte/medina-gravid|title=Medina er gravid|website=Se og Hør|date=8 August 2020|accessdate=26 March 2021}}</ref>
