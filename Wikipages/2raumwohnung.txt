'''2raumwohnung''' ({{IPA-de|t͡svaɪ ʁaʊm ˈvoːnʊŋ|lang}}; meaning: ''2-Room Apartment'') are a German [[pop duo|electro-pop duo]] that was formed in 2000 in [[Berlin]]. Its two members are singer, Inga Humpe, and her life partner, Tommi Eckart. They reached the high point of their career to date with the album "36 Grad" ("36 Degrees"), which remained in the German music charts for a total of 33 weeks.<ref name="sommerhit" /> Their most successful single has been the album's eponymous title song. 2raumwohnung have also appeared internationally, e.g. at [[Expo 2010]] and as a DJ team.<ref name="shanghai" /><ref name="dj-team" /> The band releases its music on their own record label ''it-sounds''.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.discogs.com/label/2043-Itsounds | title = it-sounds | publisher = Discogs | accessdate = 2014-09-03}}</ref>

== History ==
[[File:2raumwohnung Darmstadt 2014.jpeg|thumb|2raumwohnung in Darmstadt (2014)]]

2raumwohnung first met in [[East Berlin]] where they both settled shortly after [[German reunification]] in 1990.<ref>{{cite news | author = Ralph Geisenhanslüke | title = Fliegen möglicherweise | newspaper = Die Zeit | issue = 37 | year = 2004 | url = http://www.zeit.de/2004/37/2raumwohnung | accessdate = 2013-06-01 | language = de}}</ref> The band's first appearance was in ''Sternradio'' in 2001, a legendary club at [[Berlin Alexanderplatz|Alexanderplatz]].<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.tagesspiegel.de/berlin/clubwechsel-sternradio-geht-weekend-baut-aus/849742.html | title = Clubwechsel: Sternradio geht, Weekend baut aus | newspaper = Der Tagesspiegel | date = 2007-05-16 | accessdate = 2013-06-04 | language = de}}</ref> Before their formation as a band, the duo had been collaborating as a studio project, releasing their music under a pseudonym.

Recordings from that period were used in advertisements, such as for [[Imperial Tobacco|Imperial Tobacco's]] "Cabinet" brand ("Wir trafen uns in einem Garten" ("We Met in a Garden")) and for [[HypoVereinsbank]]. The latter used the song "2 von Millionen von Sternen" ("Two Out of Millions of Stars") as a jingle in a [[TV spot|TV advertisement]] for the introduction of the [[Euro]], although the wider public remained unaware of the song's performers at that point in time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shortnews.de/id/315617/2raumwohnung-gibt-millionen-von-sternen-fuer-euro |title=2raumwohnung gibt Millionen von Sternen für Euro |website=ShortNews |date=2001-10-23 |accessdate=2013-06-02 |language=de |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140303213857/http://www.shortnews.de/id/315617/2raumwohnung-gibt-millionen-von-sternen-fuer-euro |archivedate=2014-03-03 }}</ref>

The group released its debut album under the title "Kommt zusammen" ("Come Together") on July 2, 2001. It included a total of 13 songs and was ultimately released by Goldrush on the record label [[Bertelsmann Music Group|BMG]]. The reviews were mostly positive and ranged from a "new feeling of lightness" to "esoteric children's disco".<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.intro.de/platten/kritiken/23028086/2raumwohnung-kommt-zusammen | title = Kommt Zusammen | author = Jens Friebe | newspaper = Intro Magazin | accessdate = 2013-07-15 | date = 2007-09-07 | language = de | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140407061749/http://www.intro.de/platten/kritiken/23028086/2raumwohnung-kommt-zusammen | archive-date = 2014-04-07 | url-status = dead }}</ref> Due to successful sales, a remixed version of "Kommt zusammen", which combined house and electro elements, was also produced in January 2002.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.plattentests.de/rezi.php?show=862 | title = Housebesetzer | website = Plattentests | accessdate = 2013-07-15 | author = Oliver Ding | language = de}}</ref> "In wirklich" ("In Real") appeared in September of the same year and furthered the band's success that had been established with the debut album. Although some critics felt it was lacking the characteristic charm of their previous album,<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.plattentests.de/rezi.php?show=1158 | title = Echt unecht | website = Plattentests | author = Oliver Ding | accessdate = 2013-05-15 | language = de}}</ref> it nevertheless quickly rose to #5 in the German charts. 2raumwohnung had the following to say during the release of "In wirklich":

{{Quote | text = We make music for people who reflect on life – those who are not so influenced by RTL television. | sign = Inga Humpe | source = KulturSpiegel 10/2002}}

"In wirklich" earned 2raumwohnung the [[German Dance Award]] for "Best Album". Their next album, "Es wird Morgen" ("It Will Be Tomorrow" / "It Will Be Morning"), soon followed in August 2004, contained twelve songs and was sold in three different CD editions.<ref>{{cite news | title = Sommernachtsmusik | newspaper = UniSpiegel | issue = 5 | year = 2004 | url = http://www.spiegel.de/spiegel/unispiegel/d-32472684.html | accessdate = 2013-07-15 | language = de}}</ref> Reviewers felt the songs echoed the musical stylings of the '80s. Reviewers also felt that 2raumwohnung exhibited an impressive musical maturity, even though a certain lack of relevance was simultaneously mentioned.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.spiegel.de/kultur/musik/abgehoert-die-wichtigsten-cds-der-woche-a-315784.html | title =  Abgehört: Die wichtigsten CDs der Woche | newspaper = Spiegel Online | date = 2004-08-30 | accessdate = 2013-07-15 | language = de}}</ref> The record was performed live on tour throughout Germany and various neighboring European countries, with Jansen and Kowalski appearing as the opening act.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.medienkonverter.de/story-2raumwohnung_es-wird-morgen-50.html | title = Es wird Morgen | newspaper = Musik Magazin | publisher = Der Medienkonverter | date = 2004-10-29 | accessdate = 2013-07-15 | language = de | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140407072244/http://www.medienkonverter.de/story-2raumwohnung_es-wird-morgen-50.html | archive-date = 2014-04-07 | url-status = dead }}</ref> The summer album, "Melancholisch schön" ("Melancholically Beautiful"), followed in 2005 and featured twelve of the band's most well-known songs, which were re-recorded and remixed in [[Bossa Nova]] style. The album included "Sexy Girl", a single that had been previously released on "Kommt zusammen", where it had become somewhat popular nationwide. In 2005, 2raumwohnung also received the [[Goldene Stimmgabel]] for "Best Duo".<ref name="MOPO.de">{{Cite news|url=https://www.mopo.de/musik-goldene-stimmgabel-fuer-soehne-mannheims-und-peter-maffay-19572894|title=Musik: Goldene Stimmgabel für Söhne Mannheims und Peter Maffay|work=MOPO.de|access-date=2018-06-12|language=de-DE}}</ref>

In February 2007, following after a short break, 2raumwohnung composed two singles with [[Peter Plate]] from [[Rosenstolz]] and producer [[Ulf Leo Sommer]] for the album "36 Grad".<ref>{{cite web | url = http://blog.zeit.de/tontraeger/2007/02/23/zu-viele-gluckspillen_316 | title = Zu viele Glückspillen | author = Susanne El-Nawab | date = 2007-03-22 | accessdate = 2013-07-15 | website = Zeit Online | language = de}}</ref> With the first single, "Besser geht's nicht" ("It Can't Get Any Better Than This"), the duo made it into the top thirty of the German charts for the first time. The song was also featured in an [[Allgemeine Ortskrankenkasse|AOK]] commercial.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://musikradar.de/musikstuck-besser-gehts-nicht-aus-der-aok-werbung/ | title = Musikstück Besser geht's nicht aus der AOK-Werbung | newspaper = MusikRadar | date = 2013-01-22 | accessdate = 2013-06-30 | language = de}}</ref> The second single, "36 Grad" ("36 Degrees"), was even more successful and placed directly in the top ten of the charts. It became a massive summer hit in 2007 and continues to be frequently played during the summer on national radio. Inga Humpe compared the song to [[George Michael|George Michael's]] "Last Christmas", stating that it has maintained its summer popularity throughout the years, with ongoing possibilities of re-recordings and remixes.<ref>{{cite news | url = https://www.faz.net/aktuell/gesellschaft/jugend-schreibt/im-gespraech-inga-humpe-ueber-36-grad-dann-verlangsamt-man-so-ein-bisschen-11009279.html | title = Im Gespräch: Inga Humpe über 36 Grad | newspaper = Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung | author = Nina Belz | date = 2010-07-02 | accessdate = 2013-07-14 | language = de}}</ref> One such re-recording of "36 Grad" was produced in 2008 for the record [[Rhythms del Mundo]], in which 2raumwohnung collaborated with Cuban musicians.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.swr3.de/musik/poplexikon/Rhythms-Del-Mundo-2raumwohnung/-/id=927882/did=426704/v6bat3/index.html | title = Biografie: Rhythms Del Mundo | website = Poplexikon | publisher = Südwestrundfunk | date = 2012-01-31 | accessdate = 2013-07-12 | language = de | archive-date = 2013-10-15 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20131015023147/http://www.swr3.de/musik/poplexikon/Rhythms-Del-Mundo-2raumwohnung/-/id=927882/did=426704/v6bat3/index.html | url-status = dead }}</ref> Other German bands featured on this record included Rosenstolz, [[Culcha Candela]], [[Ich + Ich]] and [[Jan Delay]].

"36 Grad" has been 2raumwohnung's most commercially successful album to date, entering the charts on #7 and reaching #63 in the 2007 year end charts.<ref name="sommerhit">{{cite news | url = http://www.hna.de/nachrichten/kultur/grad-sommerhit-842696.html | title = 36 Grad, ein Sommerhit | newspaper = Hessische/Niedersächsische Allgemeine | author = Juliane Sattler | date = 2007-10-15 | accessdate = 2013-07-19 | language = de}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.abendblatt.de/kultur-live/article1567001/Heiss-2raumwohnung-feiert-Comeback-bei-36-Grad.html | title = Heiß: 2raumwohnung feiert Comeback bei 36 Grad | newspaper = Hamburger Abendblatt | date = 2010-07-14 | accessdate = 2013-07-19 | language = de}}</ref> Various remixes of the album's songs were produced with [[Paul van Dyk]] and [[Oliver Huntemann]], among others.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0R0xBn9csMM | title = Paul van Dyk Remix of 36 GRAD by 2RaumWohnung | website = YouTube | author = Paul van Dyk | date = 2011-09-19 | accessdate = 2013-07-15}}</ref> During the summer of 2007 and leading up to the tour for "36 Grad", it became known that Inga Humpe and Tommi Eckart were planning to live separately from each other. At that time, they were still living together in Berlin's Mitte district. Both artists spoke publicly about their age and the necessary distance without which their creativity was at risk.<ref>{{cite news | title = Getrennt wohnen | author = Anne-Dore Krohn | newspaper = Die Zeit | issue = 6 | year = 2007 | url = http://www.zeit.de/2007/06/W-2Raumwohnung | accessdate = 2013-07-02 | language = de}}</ref> The tour covered a total of 17 cities, including Berlin, [[Cologne]] and [[Munich]], as well as [[Vienna]] und [[Zurich]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.depechemode.de/2raumwohnung-neues-album-36-grad-im-februar-tourdaten-5062/ | title = 2raumwohnung – Neues Album 36 grad im Februar | website = Depeche Mode | date = 2006-12-07 | accessdate = 2013-06-04 | language = de}}</ref> 2raumwohnung subsequently released a live "36 Grad" video DVD that included both the entire concert at Berlin's Columbia Hall as well as performances from 2003 to 2005.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.ae-pool.de/buch/2raumwohnung-36-grad-live-dvd/ | title = 2raumwohnung 36 Grad live DVD | website = aepool | date = 2007-10-31 | accessdate = 2013-07-16 | language = de}}</ref>

Although a new record was not released in 2008, "Lasso" finally came out in August 2009.<ref>{{cite news | newspaper = Der Spiegel | year = 2009 | issue = 30 | title = Alterslose Jugend | author = Tobias Rapp | url = http://www.spiegel.de/spiegel/print/d-66133699.html | language = de}}</ref> The name was chosen with reference to [[Orlando di Lasso]]. The album contained a total of 13 songs, including the singles "Wir werden sehen" ("We Will See") and "Rette mich später" ("Save Me Later"). Although Lasso could not quite match the success that "36 Grad" had acquired, reviewers saw the album as an expression of "Berliners' sense of and approach to life in understandable musical language". Inga Humpe admitted in an interview that the [[urban jungle]] has significantly influenced "Lasso".<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.fairaudio.de/artikel/musik-rezension/08-2009/2-raum-wohnung-lasso-cd-rezension-1.html | title = Victoriah's Music | website = fairaudio | author = Victoriah Szirmai | date = 2009-08-02 | accessdate = 2013-07-15 | language = de}}</ref> 2raumwohnung also went on tour with the album (Lasso Tour 2009), along with German-American-Persian musician [[Malakoff Kowalski]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.nachtagenten.de/event/20.02.2010/stadtgarten/2raumwohnung-lasso-tour-2010.135786.html | title = 2Raumwohnung - Lasso Tour 2010 | website = Nachagenten | date = 2010-02-20 | accessdate = 2013-07-13 | language = de | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20131021015332/http://www.nachtagenten.de/event/20.02.2010/stadtgarten/2raumwohnung-lasso-tour-2010.135786.html | archive-date = 2013-10-21 | url-status = dead }}</ref> Furthermore, a remix album of Lasso appeared in 2010, in collaboration with [[Paul Kalkbrenner]], [[Blake Baxter]] and [[Abe Duque]], among others.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://itunes.apple.com/de/album/lasso-remixe/id457285490 | title = Lasso Remixe | website = iTunes Store | publisher = Apple | accessdate = 2013-07-16 | language = de}}</ref>

In 2010, 2raumwohnung had two guest appearances in the television [[soap opera]] [[Gute Zeiten, schlechte Zeiten|GZSZ]]. The episodes were aired on February 12 and 15, with 2raumwohnung performing both "36 Grad" and "Rette mich später" in the so-called ''Mauerwerk''.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.tagesspiegel.de/berlin/stadtleben/stadtmenschen-2raumwohnung-spielen-bei-gzsz/1678388.html | title = 2raumwohnung spielen bei GZSZ | newspaper = Der Tagesspiegel | date = 2010-02-08 | accessdate = 2013-07-02 | language = de}}</ref> The appearance helped promote the new album and the following tour. In addition to television, 2raumwohnung have also found their way into contemporary [[German literature]]: specifically, in [[Oliver Bendel|Oliver Bendel's]] "Nachrückende Generationen" ("Coming Generations"). In the novel, there is a scene of a boy dancing to the song "Wir sind die anderen" ("We Are The Others"). In addition, the band received the "Kulturpreis"-award (a cultural award) by [[Berliner Zeitung]] in January 2010.<ref name="kulturpreis">{{cite news | url = http://www.bz-berlin.de/kultur/19-b-z-kulturpreis-mit-gala-gefeiert-article708965.html | title = 19. B.Z.-Kulturpreis mit Gala gefeiert | date = 2010-01-21 | accessdate = 2013-07-19 | newspaper = B.Z. | language = de}}</ref> In 2011, 2raumwohnung played [[Gustav Mahler|Gustav Mahler's]] [[Symphony No. 5 (Mahler)|5th Symphony]] together with the [[Frankfurt Radio Symphony Orchestra]] conducted by the composer [[Moritz Eggert]]. The purpose of this collaboration, called "Music Discovery Project", aimed to merge different musical worlds.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hr-online.de/website/rubriken/kultur/index.jsp?rubrik=59183&key=standard_document_40085425 |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130723162456/http://www.hr-online.de/website/rubriken/kultur/index.jsp?rubrik=59183&key=standard_document_40085425 |url-status=dead |archive-date=2013-07-23 |title=Music Discovery Project 2011 mit 2raumwohnung, Eggert und Mahler |publisher=Hessischer Rundfunk |date=2011-01-12 |accessdate=2013-07-22 |language=de }}</ref>

=== Inga Humpe ===
[[File:Inga Humpe 2013.jpg|thumb|Inga Humpe (2013)]]

Humpe grew up in [[Herdecke|Herdecke on the Ruhr]], where her parents owned a [[Pastry|pastry shop]].<ref>{{cite news | title = Ich fand eigentlich alle blöd | newspaper = Zeit Campus | date = 2011-05-30 | accessdate = 2014-07-21 |url=http://www.zeit.de/campus/2011/03/mensa-interview-inga-humpe/komplettansicht | language = de}}</ref> After graduating from high school, she began her studies in [[Art History]] and [[Comparative Literature]] at the [[RWTH Aachen]] in 1975.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.klenkes.de/meldungen/29885.in-der-mensa-mit-inga-humpe.html | title = In der Mensa mit... Inga Humpe | website = Klenkes | date = 2010-12-08 | accessdate = 2014-02-02 | language = de}}</ref> The following year, she switched to the [[Free University of Berlin]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Inga Humpe, Deutschlands musikalischste 2raumausstatterin |newspaper=wir |year=2011 |issue=1 |url=https://www.fu-berlin.de/sites/alumni/alumni-buero/publikationen/alumni-magazin-wir/2011-01-wir.pdf |publisher=fu-berlin.de |page=46 |language=de |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141109095727/http://www.fu-berlin.de/sites/alumni/alumni-buero/publikationen/alumni-magazin-wir/2011-01-wir.pdf |archivedate=2014-11-09 }}</ref> Occasionally she attended acting courses at the ''Max Reinhardt School''.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.swr.de/swr1/bw/-/id=233362/nid=233362/did=1566144/ggm5pd/index.html | title = Careless love | publisher = SWR1 Baden-Württemberg | accessdate = 2014-04-11 | language = de}}</ref> Following her first musical projects, she founded the punk band ''Neonbabies'' in the late 1970s,<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.bild.de/unterhaltung/musik/2raumwohnung/von-neonbabies-bis-2raumwohnung-9073850.bild.html | title = Humpe, Humpe, tätärää – von Neonbabies bis 2raumwohnung | newspaper = Bild | date = 2009-07-19 | accessdate = 2014-02-02 | language = de}}</ref> in which she was active as a singer until 1983. Humpe became known to a larger public as a member of the group [[Deutsch-Österreichisches Feingefühl|DÖF]] with their hit "Codo".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ampya.com/Inga-Humpe |title=Inga Humpe |publisher=Ampya |accessdate=2014-02-02 |language=de |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140304081216/http://www.ampya.com/Inga-Humpe |archivedate=2014-03-04 }}</ref> In the second half of the 1980s she recorded two albums in a duo with her sister [[Annette Humpe]].

=== Tommi Eckart ===
[[File:Tommi Eckart Studio Session Dez 2011.jpg|thumb|Tommi Eckart (2011)]]

Eckart was born in [[West Berlin]] and spent parts of his childhood in [[Munich]].<ref name="2004-09-03">{{cite news | author = Michael Pilz | title = Neue Deutsche Wellness | newspaper = Die Welt | date = 2004-09-03 |url= https://www.welt.de/print-welt/article338091/Neue-Deutsche-Wellness.html|publisher=welt.de | language = de | accessdate = 2014-02-03}}</ref> Together with colleagues, he founded the punk band ''Alternative Arschlöcher''.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.ksta.de/ratgeber/hintergrund-das-pop-duo-2raumwohnung,15189524,12881836.html | title = Das Pop-Duo 2Raumwohnung | newspaper = Kölner Stadt-Anzeiger | date = 2009-08-11 | accessdate = 2014-02-03 | language = de}}</ref> His collaboration with Andreas Dorau began in the late 1980s and lasted into the 1990s.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.morgenpost.de/kultur/berlin-kultur/article124108388/Andreas-Dorau-Deutschlands-Dadaist-kommt-nach-Berlin.html | title = Andreas Dorau - Deutschlands Dadaist kommt nach Berlin | newspaper = Berliner Morgenpost | author = Birgit Reuther | date = 2014-01-22 | accessdate = 2014-02-03 | language = de}}</ref> After the fall of the [[Berlin Wall]], Eckart moved to [[East Berlin]], where he lived in a former wiretapped apartment of the Ministry of State Security.<ref name="2004-09-03" /> In 1992, he and Ralf Hertwig from [[Palais Schaumburg]] founded the [[trance music|trance projects]] ''Transform'' and ''Perry & Rhodan'', releasing the single "The Beat Just Goes Straight On & On" (Rising High Records) in 1993, among others.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.deejay.de/The_Beat_Just_Goes_Straight_On__98317 | title = The Beat Just Goes Straight On | website = deejay | accessdate = 2014-04-01 | language = de}}</ref> Eckart also worked with Klaus Löschner.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://sz-magazin.sueddeutsche.de/blogs/musikblog/1177/2raumwohnung-im-interview-man-kann-cool-und-trotzdem-erfolgreich-sein/ |title=2raumwohnung im Interview: Man kann cool und trotzdem erfolgreich sein. |author=Johannes Waechter |newspaper=SZ Magazin |accessdate=2014-02-03 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140304154328/http://sz-magazin.sueddeutsche.de/blogs/musikblog/1177/2raumwohnung-im-interview-man-kann-cool-und-trotzdem-erfolgreich-sein/ |archivedate=2014-03-04 }}</ref>

== Miscellaneous ==
In April 2008, 2raumwohnung and about 200 other artists signed an open letter of the [[German Music Industry Association]] to [[chancellor]] [[Angela Merkel]], which was published in the [[Süddeutsche Zeitung]] and [[Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung]] newspapers, among others. In the letter they argued in favor of increased protection of [[intellectual property]], which was being prejudiced by file sharing in particular.<ref>{{cite news | url = http://www.heise.de/newsticker/meldung/Offener-Brief-Bundeskanzlerin-soll-Kuenstlerrechte-schuetzen-202865.html | title = Offener Brief: Bundeskanzlerin soll Künstlerrechte schützen | newspaper = heise online | author = Peter-Michael Ziegler | date = 2008-04-24 | accessdate = 2013-07-12 | language = de}}</ref> In the wake of the [[Fukushima nuclear disaster]], 2raumwohnung and other artists initiated another open letter and a held a vigil in front of the Federal Chancellery, with the aim of phasing-out [[nuclear power]] without delay.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.zoolamar.com/2011/03/16/kunstler-mahnwache-vor-dem-bundeskanzleramt-am-16-marz-um-14-00-uhr/ | title = Künstler-Mahnwache vor dem Bundeskanzleramt am 16. März um 14.00 Uhr | website = zoolamar | date = 2011-03-16 | accessdate = 2013-09-23 | language = de | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20140407072520/http://www.zoolamar.com/2011/03/16/kunstler-mahnwache-vor-dem-bundeskanzleramt-am-16-marz-um-14-00-uhr/ | archivedate = 2014-04-07 }}</ref>

2raumwohnung have repeatedly represented German music at major international events, such as a concert on the [[German Unity Day|German National Day]] at [[Expo 2010]] in [[Shanghai]],<ref name="shanghai">{{cite web|url=http://www.emimusic.de/presse/2415 |title=2raumwohnung: Im Mai auf der EXPO, im Juni bei der WM, im Juli die Lasso Remixe |website=EMI Music Germany |date=2010-05-14 |accessdate=2013-07-10 |language=de |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100606184202/http://www.emimusic.de/presse/2415 |archivedate=2010-06-06 }}</ref> along with other artists such as [[Nobelpenner]] and [[Konrad Chef]]. In June 2010, at the invitation of the [[Goethe Institute]] and the German Embassy in [[Pretoria]], 2raumwohnung were guests at the [[FIFA World Cup 2010]] in [[South Africa]]. They appeared together with [[Frank Dellé]] at the ''Soccer Meets Culture'' event.

The pseudonym 2raumwohnung was developed due to the fact that the two artists did not want to be associated with music for commercials. Working in this field was frowned upon in certain circles. The duo initially used the spelling "Zweiraumwohnung", but later changed this since the corresponding [[domain name|domain]] had already been registered.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.bloom.de/articles/article_001303.htm | title = Das große Interview | website = bloom | date = 2001-06-20 | accessdate = 2013-06-03 | language = de}}</ref>

2raumwohnung have been active throughout their career as a [[DJ|DJ team]], appearing in [[New York City]] among other cities.<ref name="dj-team">{{cite web | url = http://deutscheshaus.as.nyu.edu/docs/IO/6833/cult_cal_20071108.pdf | title = Berlin – New York Dialogues | date = 2011-07-08 | accessdate = 2013-07-19}}</ref> In 2008, they played as part of Moguai & Friends at Weststadthalle in the city of Essen,<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.nachtagenten.de/event/05.04.2008/weststadthalle-e/moguai-friends-mit-2raum-dj-team.69944.html | title = Moguai & Friends mit 2Raum DJ-Team | website = Nachtagenten | date = 2008-04-05 | accessdate = 2013-07-19 | language = de | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140913103951/http://www.nachtagenten.de/event/05.04.2008/weststadthalle-e/moguai-friends-mit-2raum-dj-team.69944.html | archive-date = 2014-09-13 | url-status = dead }}</ref> and on [[Munich|Munich's]] [[Praterinsel]] during the ''[[Smirnoff]] Experience'' in 2003 (also with [[Moguai]]).<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.t-pro.net/presse/undercover_0505.html | title = 2Raumwohnung, ideale Lage | website = Undercover | publisher = T-Pro Events | accessdate = 2013-07-19 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140913105818/http://www.t-pro.net/presse/undercover_0505.html | archive-date = 2014-09-13 | url-status = dead }}</ref>
