'''Maria Lea Carmen Imutan Salonga''', {{Post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Lakandula|OL]]}}, ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|l|eɪ|ə|_|s|ə|ˈ|l|ɒ|ŋ|ɡ|ə}}; born February 22, 1971) is a [[Filipinos|Filipina]] singer and actress, best known for her roles in [[musical theatre]], for supplying the singing voices of two [[Disney Princess]]es ([[Princess Jasmine|Jasmine]] and [[Mulan (Disney character)|Mulan]]), and as a recording artist and television performer.

At age 18, she originated the lead role of Kim in the musical ''[[Miss Saigon]]'' in the [[West End theatre|West End]] and then reprised the role on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]],<ref name=broadwaycomstarfiles>{{Cite web | url = http://www.broadway.com/gen/Buzz_Star_File.aspx?ci=23104 | title = Lea Salonga, Star File: Broadway.com Buzz | access-date = March 10, 2016 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20050221175130/http://www.broadway.com/gen/Buzz_Star_File.aspx?ci=23104 | archive-date = February 21, 2005 | url-status = usurped}}</ref> winning the [[Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical|Olivier]] and [[Theatre World Award]]s, and becoming the first Asian woman to win a [[Tony Award]].<ref name=FirstTony>Gioia, Michael. [http://www.playbill.com/article/im-hoping-there-is-a-shift-says-lea-salonga-on-diversity-and-the-united-colors-of-this-season-com-354199 "'I'm Hoping There is a Shift', Says Lea Salonga On Diversity and the 'United Colors' of This Season"], Playbill, July 24, 2015, accessed April 30, 2016</ref> Salonga is the first Filipino artist to sign with an international record label ([[Atlantic Records]] in 1993).

Salonga was the first actress of Asian descent to play the roles of [[Éponine]] and [[Fantine]] in the musical ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]'' on Broadway.<ref name=newskey52>
* {{Cite press release | url = http://www.lesmis.com/pages/news/newsdetail.php?newskey=52 | title = Tony Award winner to join the cast as Fantine | website = Les Misérables | access-date = July 11, 2016 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20070808195117/http://www.lesmis.com/pages/news/newsdetail.php?newskey=52 | archive-date = August 8, 2007 | url-status=dead}}
* {{Cite web | url = http://www.broadway.com/buzz/96323/tony-winner-lea-salonga-gets-early-start-as-new-fantine-of-les-miz | title = Tony Winner Lea Salonga Gets Early Start as New Fantine of ''Les Miz'' | website = Broadway.com | date = March 2, 2007 | access-date = July 11, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | last = Wingfield | first = Garth | url = http://www.newyork.com/articles/broadway/10-stars-who-broke-barriers-on-broadway-17674 | title = 10 Stars Who Broke Barriers on Broadway | website = NewYork.com | date = June 25, 2014 | access-date = July 11, 2016 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140706041340/http://www.newyork.com/articles/broadway/10-stars-who-broke-barriers-on-broadway-17674/ | archive-date = July 6, 2014 | url-status=dead}}</ref> She also portrayed Éponine and Fantine, respectively, in the musical's [[Les Misérables: The Dream Cast in Concert|10th]] and [[Les Misérables: 25th Anniversary Concert|25th anniversary]] concerts in London. She provided the singing voices of two official Disney Princesses: [[Princess Jasmine|Jasmine]] in ''[[Aladdin (1992 Disney film)|Aladdin]]'' (1992) and [[Mulan (Disney character)|Fa Mulan]] in ''[[Mulan (1998 film)|Mulan]]'' (1998). She was named a [[Disney Legend]] in 2011 for her work with [[The Walt Disney Company]].<ref name=DLegend>{{Cite news|url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/08/21/11/lea-salonga-becomes-disney-legend|title=Lea Salonga becomes a Disney legend|first=Yong|last=Chavez|work=ABS-CBN News}}</ref> Salonga starred as Mei-li in the 2002 Broadway version of ''[[Flower Drum Song]]''. She has played numerous other stage, film and TV roles in the US, the Philippines and elsewhere. She has toured widely in the theatre roles and as a concert artist. From 2015 to 2016, she returned to Broadway in ''[[Allegiance (musical)|Allegiance]]'', and from 2017 to 2019 she appeared in the Broadway revival of ''[[Once on This Island]]''.

==Life and career==
===1971–1989: Early life and career===
Salonga was born in [[Ermita]], [[Manila]], to Feliciano Genuino Salonga, a naval [[rear admiral]] and shipping company owner (1929–2016),<ref>[http://www.rappler.com/entertainment/news/120909-lea-salonga-father-feliciano-dies "Lea Salonga's father Feliciano Salonga dies"], ''Rappler'', February 1, 2016</ref> and his wife, María Ligaya Alcantara, ''née'' Imutan.<ref name="birth"/> She spent the first six years of her childhood in [[Angeles City]] before moving to Manila.<ref name="filipinoweb.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.filipinoweb.com/leasalonga.html|website=Filipino Web|title=Lea Salonga|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090307111532/http://www.filipinoweb.com/leasalonga.html|archive-date=March 7, 2009}}</ref> Her brother, [[Gerard Salonga]], is a conductor.<ref name=EncyclopediaCom>[http://www.encyclopedia.com/doc/1G2-3444200168.html "Salonga, Lea 1971–"], ''Contemporary Theatre, Film and Television'', Encyclopedia.com, 2005, accessed November 4, 2015</ref>

She made her professional debut in 1978 at the age of seven in the musical ''[[The King and I]]'' with [[Repertory Philippines]].<ref name="books.google.com" /> She played the title role in ''[[Annie (musical)|Annie]]'' in 1980 and appeared in other productions such as ''[[Cat on a Hot Tin Roof]]'', ''[[Fiddler on the Roof]]'', ''[[The Sound of Music]]'', ''[[The Rose Tattoo|The Rose T]]'', ''[[The Goodbye Girl]]'' (1982), ''[[Paper Moon (film)|Paper Moon]]'' (1983) and ''[[The Fantasticks]]'' (1988).<ref name=EncyclopediaCom/> In 1981, she recorded her first album, ''[[Small Voice]]'', which was certified gold in the Philippines.<ref name=D23>[https://d23.com/walt-disney-legend/lea-salonga/ "Lea Salonga"], [[D23 (Disney)|D23]], accessed October 21, 2015</ref> In 1985, she and her brother took part in the [[Metro Manila Popular Music Festival#The 8th Metro Manila Popular Music Festival|eighth Metro Manila Popular Music Festival]] as the interpreters for the song entry titled "Musika, Lata, Sipol at La La La" which was composed by Tess Concepcion.

During the 1980s, Salonga also had several television projects through [[GMA Network|GMA Radio Television Arts]] where she worked as a child actor. After the success of her first album, from 1983 to 1985, she hosted her own musical television show, ''Love, Lea'',<ref name="filipinoweb.com" /> and was a member of the cast of [[German Moreno]]'s teen variety show ''[[That's Entertainment (TV series)|That's Entertainment]]''. She acted in films, which included the family-oriented ''Tropang Bulilit'', ''Like Father, Like Son'', ''Ninja Kids'', ''Captain Barbell'' and ''[[Pik Pak Boom]]''. As a young performer, Salonga received a Filipino Academy of Movie Arts and Sciences ([[FAMAS Awards|FAMAS]]) award nomination for Best Child Actress and three [[Aliw Award]]s for best child performer in 1980, 1981 and 1982.<ref name="filipinoweb.com"/><ref name=EncyclopediaCom/> She released her second album, ''[[Lea (album)|Lea]]'', in 1988.<ref name=D23/>

She also opened for, and performed with, international acts such as [[Menudo (band)|Menudo]] and [[Stevie Wonder]] in their concerts in Manila in 1985 and in 1988, respectively.<ref name="tv.com">{{Cite web|url=http://www.tv.com/lea-salonga/person/92158/biography.html|website=TV.com|title=Lea Salonga}}</ref>

She finished her secondary education in 1988 at the O. B. Montessori Center in Greenhills, San Juan, Metro Manila.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsflash.org/2004/02/ht/ht005316.htm|title=Article on Preciosa Soliven, with notes on Lea Salonga}}</ref> She also attended the [[University of the Philippines]] College of Music's extension program aimed at training musically talented children in music and stage movement.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}} A college freshman studying biology at the [[Ateneo de Manila University]] when she auditioned for ''[[Miss Saigon]]'', she intended to have a medical career. Later, in between jobs in New York, she took two courses at [[Fordham University]]'s [[Lincoln Center]] campus.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}}

===1989–1992: ''Miss Saigon'' and ''Aladdin''===
In 1989, Salonga was selected to play Kim in the debut production of the musical ''[[Miss Saigon]]'' in London.<ref name=broadwaycomstarfiles/> Unable to find a strong enough East Asian actress/singer in the United Kingdom, the producers scoured many countries looking for the lead.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1991/03/17/theater/theater-the-iron-butterfly-within-miss-saigon.html|title=Theater: The Iron Butterfly within Miss Saigon | work=The New York Times | first=Alex | last=Witchel | date=March 17, 1991 | access-date=May 7, 2010}}</ref> For her audition, the then 17-year-old Salonga chose to sing [[Alain Boublil|Boublil]] and [[Claude-Michel Schönberg]]'s "[[On My Own (Les Misérables)|On My Own]]" from ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]'' and was later asked to sing "Sun and Moon", impressing the audition panel.<ref name=antiqbook>{{Cite web|url=http://www.antiqbook.com/boox/bookco/707188.shtml|title=Behr, Edward and Mark Steyn, The Story of Miss Saigon, New York: Arcade Publishing, 1991|access-date=2007-11-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071215095251/http://www.antiqbook.com/boox/bookco/707188.shtml|archive-date=2007-12-15|url-status=dead}}</ref> Salonga has sometimes credited "On My Own" as the starting point of her international career.<ref>Pierce, Scott. [http://archive.sltrib.com/story.php?ref=/sltrib/lifestyle/53820026-80/song-changed-byutv-cardon.html.csp "Stars reveal song that changed their lives in new TV series"], ''[[The Salt Lake Tribune]]'', March 30, 2012, accessed July 10, 2016</ref><ref name=Gelfand>Gelfand, Janelle. [http://www.cincinnati.com/story/entertainment/2016/04/23/disney-princess-dancing-queen-lea-salonga-charms-pops/83387668/ Review: Disney princess to 'Dancing Queen,' Lea Salonga charms with Pops], ''[[Cincinnati.com]]'', April 26, 2016</ref> Salonga won the lead role, while  her childhood friend and fellow Repertory Philippines performer [[Monique Wilson]] was named her [[understudy]] and cast as the bar girl Mimi.<ref name=antiqbook/><ref>{{cite web| url=http://moniquewilson.com/bio2.php| title=Monique Wilson Biography| url-status=dead| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080104130955/http://moniquewilson.com/bio2.php| archive-date=2008-01-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine | url = http://www.asiaweek.com/asiaweek/magazine/2000/0218/people.html | title = A Broadway belle hits the books again | magazine = [[Time (magazine)|Time]] (Asia Edition) | via = www.asiaweek.com | date = February 18, 2000 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20010627072123/http://www.asiaweek.com/asiaweek/magazine/2000/0218/people.html | archive-date = June 27, 2001 | url-status = usurped}}</ref>

For her performance as Kim, Salonga won the 1990 [[Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical]], becoming one of the youngest winners of the award. When ''Miss Saigon'' opened on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] in 1991, she again played the role of Kim,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9C0CE3D8163AF931A35750C0A966958260| title='Miss Saigon' finds home on Broadway | work=The New York Times | first=Mervyn | last=Rothstein | date=March 2, 1990 | access-date=May 7, 2010}}</ref> winning the [[Drama Desk]], [[Outer Critics Circle Award|Outer Critics Circle]] and [[Theatre World Award|Theatre World]] Awards<ref name=broadwaycomstarfiles/> and becoming the first woman of Asian descent to win a [[Tony Award]].<ref name=FirstTony/><ref>Lee, Ashley. [http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/features/asian-actors-onstage-lea-salonga-842293 "Asian Actors Onstage: Lea Salonga, Phillipa Soo Sound Off on Broadway Representation, Cultural Perceptions"], ''The Hollywood Reporter'', November 24, 2015</ref> In 1993 and 1996, she returned to play Kim on Broadway.<ref name=newskey52/> In 1999, she was invited back to London to close the West End production, and in 2001, at the age of 29 and after finishing the Manila run of the musical,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://archives.cnn.com/2000/ASIANOW/southeast/12/03/philippines.miss.saigon/index.html|work=CNN|title=Musical ''Miss Saigon'' opens in the Philippines, home of its leading ladies | access-date=May 7, 2010 | date=December 4, 2000}}</ref> Salonga returned to Broadway to close that production.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2001/SHOWBIZ/Arts/01/16/miss.saigon/index.html|title=Miss Saigon star returns |work=CNN |date=January 16, 2001 | access-date=May 7, 2010}}</ref>

In 1990, Salonga performed in a homecoming concert in [[Manila]] entitled ''A Miss Called Lea''.<ref name="filmreference.com">{{cite web| url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/69/Lea-Salonga.html|website=Film Reference|title=Lea Salonga}}</ref> She also received a Presidential Award of Merit from [[President of the Philippines|President]] [[Corazon Aquino]].<ref>Porcalla, Delon. [http://www.philstar.com/headlines/2014/12/06/1399540/aquino-confers-awards-33-outstanding-pinoys "Aquino confers awards on 33 outstanding Pinoys"], ''The Philippine Star'', December 6, 2014, accessed March 10, 2016</ref> In 1991, she was named as one of ''People'' magazine's [[50 Most Beautiful People]].<ref>[http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20122069,00.html "Beautiful Through the Years"], ''People'' magazine, May 12, 1997, Vol. 47, No. 18, accessed October 21, 2015</ref> In 1992, she performed the singing voice of [[Princess Jasmine]] in [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney's]] animated film ''[[Aladdin (1992 Disney film)|Aladdin]]''.<ref name=DLegend/>

===1993–1996: ''Les Misérables'', films and other musicals===
In 1993, Salonga played the role of [[Éponine]] in the Broadway production of ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]''.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.broadway.com/buzz/96178/lea-salonga-to-replace-daphne-rubin-vega-in-les-miserables/ | title = Lea Salonga to Replace Daphne Rubin-Vega in Les Misérables | website = Broadway.com | date = January 3, 2007 | access-date = March 10, 2016}}</ref> She performed the song "[[A Whole New World]]" from ''Aladdin'' with [[Brad Caleb Kane|Brad Kane]] at the 65th Annual [[Academy Awards]] in [[Los Angeles]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LPmK0_1l-Bg| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211107/LPmK0_1l-Bg| archive-date=2021-11-07 | url-status=live|title=A Whole New World|via=www.youtube.com}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last=Wiley|first=Mason|title=Inside Oscar: The Unofficial History of the Academy Awards|year=1996| publisher=Ballantine Books| location=New York|isbn=0-345-40053-4|page=883|edition=5|author2=Damien Bona}}</ref> where the song won an [[Academy Awards|Oscar]], having already won a [[Golden Globe Award]].<ref name=D23/> That same year, she released her self-titled international debut album with [[Atlantic Records]]. In 1994, Salonga played in various musical theatre productions in the Philippines and [[Singapore]],<ref name=broadwaycomstarfiles/><ref name="books.google.com">{{cite book| url=https://books.google.com/books?id=aIu8qmB_wxEC&q=lea+salonga+age+of+seven+biography&pg=PA4| title="The Right Way to Sing" by Linda Marquart: Lea Salonga Biography|isbn=978-1-58115-407-8|last1=Marquart| first1=Linda|last2=Salonga|first2=Lea|date=2005-04-01}}</ref> such as Sandy in ''[[Grease (musical)|Grease]]'', Eliza Doolittle in ''[[My Fair Lady]]'' and the Witch in ''[[Into the Woods]]''.<ref name=EncyclopediaCom/>

Back in the US in 1995, Salonga played the role of Geri Riordan, an 18-year-old adopted [[Vietnamese American]] child in the [[Hallmark Hall of Fame]] [[TV movie]] ''[[Redwood Curtain (film)|Redwood Curtain]]'', which starred [[John Lithgow]] and [[Jeff Daniels]]. She then flew back to the Philippines to star with Filipino matinée idol [[Aga Muhlach]] in the critically acclaimed film ''[[Sana Maulit Muli (film)|Sana Maulit Muli]]'', which gave her a second Filipino Academy of Movie Arts and Sciences ([[FAMAS Awards|FAMAS]]) award nomination, this time for Best Actress. She played the role of Éponine in the 10th anniversary production of ''Les Mis'' called ''[[Les Misérables: The Dream Cast in Concert]]'' at London's [[Royal Albert Hall]].<ref name=newskey52/>

In 1996, Salonga was in ''Les Misérables'' once again as Éponine in the London production of the musical, then continued on to perform the role in the musical's US national tour.<ref name=broadwaycomstarfiles/> In the Philippines in 1999, and again in 2000, she played Sonia Walsk in ''[[They're Playing Our Song]]''.<ref>"Lea Salonga, at Home and Playing Her Own Song", ''International Herald Tribune'', August 15, 2000</ref>

===1997–2004: Recordings, concerts, TV and ''Flower Drum Song''===
From 1997 to 2000, Salonga did recordings and concerts in the Philippines and another engagement in London, in addition to a few returns to ''Miss Saigon'' in London and on Broadway. In 1997, she released ''[[I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing (album)|I'd Like to Teach The World to Sing]]'' (recordings from her childhood days) to gold sales in the Philippines.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.mp3.com/albums/528331/summary.html | title = I'd Like to Teach the World to Sing - Lea Salonga - Song Listings | website = MP3.com | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080415043244/http://www.mp3.com/albums/528331/summary.html | archive-date = April 15, 2008 | url-status = usurped}}</ref> That recording was followed by ''[[Lea... In Love]]'' in 1998<ref>{{Cite news|work=Philippine Daily Inquirer|url=http://www.inquirer.net/saturday/jan99wk1/spc_main.htm|title=10: The Best of 1998|access-date=2007-10-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080123193112/http://www.inquirer.net/saturday/jan99wk1/spc_main.htm|archive-date=2008-01-23|url-status=dead}}</ref> and ''[[By Heart (Lea Salonga album)|By Heart]]'' in 2000, with both albums reaching multiple platinum status in the Philippines.<ref name="tv.com"/> In 1998, she again lent her voice to a major Disney animated film, singing [[Mulan (Disney character)|the title character]] in ''[[Mulan (1998 film)|Mulan]]'', also providing the character's singing voice in the 2004 sequel, ''[[Mulan II]]''.<ref name=DLegend/> At the age of 28, Salonga moved to New York City, purchasing her own apartment (which she still owns up to at least 2013).<ref name="PhilStarInterview2013">{{cite news |last=Gonzalez |first=Bianca |date=29 September 2013 |title=Lea Salonga on life as Kim, Sonia, Eponine, wife & mom |url=http://www.philstar.com/sunday-life/2013/09/29/1239225/lea-salonga-life-kim-sonia-eponine-wife-mom |work=The Philippine Star|access-date=28 March 2015 }}<br/>{{cite book|author=Kevin L. Nadal and the Filipino-American National Historical Society Metropolitan New York Chapter|title=Filipinos in New York City|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=V6HOBgAAQBAJ&pg=PA8|date=30 March 2015|publisher=Arcadia Publishing| isbn=978-1-4671-2308-2|page=8}}</ref> She participated in the 1998 tribute concert to Sir [[Cameron Mackintosh]] in London called "Hey Mr. Producer: The Musical World of Cameron MacKintosh", where she did numbers from several of his musicals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/39076.html|website=Playbill|title=Judi Dench, Bernadette Peters to join Lea Salonga in London benefit}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.albemarle-london.com/Archive/ArchiveShow.php?Show_Name=Hey%20Mr%20Producer!|website=Albermarle of London|title=Hey Mr. Producer!|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100917091433/http://albemarle-london.com/Archive/ArchiveShow.php?Show_Name=Hey%20Mr%20Producer!|archive-date=2010-09-17}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.broadway.com/gen/Buzz_Story.aspx?ci=33514 | title = DVDs: Mackintosh's Salad Days | first = Ken | last = Mandelbaum | website = Broadway.com | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080610191722/http://www.broadway.com/gen/Buzz_Story.aspx?ci=33514 | archive-date = June 10, 2008 | url-status = usurped}}</ref> She also performed in four concerts: ''The Homecoming Concert'', ''The Millennium Concert'', ''The Best of Manila'' and ''Songs from the Screen'' – the last two being benefit shows.<ref name="filmreference.com" /> Salonga returned to Manila in ''Miss Saigon'', staged at the [[Cultural Center of the Philippines]] at the end of 2000.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/54813.html|website=Playbill|title=She will be Miss Saigon again: Salonga to be Kim in October Manila mounting}}</ref>

After a last stint in ''Miss Saigon'' for its closing on Broadway in 2001, Salonga recreated the role of [[Lien Hughes]] originally played by [[Ming-Na|Ming-Na Wen]] in the [[soap opera]] ''[[As the World Turns]]''. After completing her contract that year, she was asked to return to the role in 2003.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.soapcentral.com/atwt/theactors/salonga.php|website=Soap Central|title=Lea Salonga|access-date=2007-10-18|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120914125909/http://www.soapcentral.com/atwt/theactors/salonga.php|archive-date=2012-09-14|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/77449.html|website=Playbill|title=Lea Salonga does double-duty: "Flower Drum Song" and "As the World Turns"|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080612033059/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/77449.html|archive-date=2008-06-12}}</ref> She guested on Russell Watson's ''The Voice'' concert, narrated for the television special ''My America: A Poetry Atlas of the United States'', and appeared on the Christmas episode of the TV medical drama ''[[ER (TV series)|ER]]'', playing a patient with [[lymphoma]].<ref name="filmreference.com" />

In 2002, Salonga returned to Broadway to play the leading role of Mei-li, a Chinese immigrant in a reinterpretation of [[Rodgers and Hammerstein]]'s ''[[Flower Drum Song]]'' opposite [[Jose Llana]]. This was after the reinvented musical had a run at the Mark Taper Forum in Los Angeles in 2001 with Salonga playing the role<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.broadway.com/gen/Buzz_Story.aspx?ci=5316 | title = Lea Salonga confirmed for Flower Drum Song | website = Broadway.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = http://asianweek.com/2002_10_11/feature.html | title = Making Musical History | website = asianweek.com | date = October 11, 2002 | last = Hong | first = Terry | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20021127010212/http://asianweek.com/2002_10_11/feature.html | archive-date = November 27, 2002 | url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/72773.html|website=Playbill|title=The 'Flower' blooms: Rodgers and Hammerstein's 'Drum Song' opens Oct. 17 on Broadway}}</ref> and in 2002 winning Lead Actress in a Musical from the Los Angeles theatre [[Ovation Awards]].<ref>Ehren, Christine. ==Voice and musical ability==
Salonga has been praised for control over her vocals, which can evoke a wide array of emotion and soundscapes.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.theaureview.com/adelaide/live-review-lea-salonga-adelaide-festival-theatre-adelaide-10-06-12|title=Live Review: Lea Salonga – Adelaide Festival Theatre, Adelaide (10 June 2012)|date=June 11, 2012|first=Sosefina|last=Fuamoli|publisher=theaureview.com |access-date=September 24, 2012}}</ref> In her popular music releases, Salonga has sung "simple love songs", which are common in [[Original Pilipino Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/by-heart-mw0001032816|title=Lea Salonga: By Heart|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=September 24, 2012}}</ref> In both [[Disney Princess]] singing roles, [[Jasmine (Disney character)|Jasmine]] and [[Mulan (Disney character)|Mulan]], Salonga uses head tones that reach up to F<sub>5</sub>.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.musicnotes.com/sheetmusic/mtdFPE.asp?ppn=MN0057034&ref=google|title=Reflection Lea Salonga Digital Sheet Music|publisher=musicnotes.com| access-date=September 29, 2012|date=April 2, 2007}}{{link note|note=Purchase required|date=September 2012}}</ref>
