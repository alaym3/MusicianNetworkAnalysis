'''Khaled Hadj Ibrahim''' ({{lang-ar|خالد حاج إبراهيم}}, {{IPA-ar|ˈxaːled ħaːd͡ʒ ɪbraːˈhiːm|}}; born 29 February 1960), better known by his [[mononym]] '''Khaled''' ({{lang-ar|link=no|خالد}}), is an [[Algeria|Algerian]] [[raï]] singer, musician and songwriter born in [[Oran]]. He began recording in his early teens under the name '''Cheb Khaled''' ({{lang|ar|الشاب خالد}}, Arabic for "Young" Khaled, with "Cheb" as a common title for male Raï singers).

Khaled is one of the most important musicians in the history of Raï music in his native Algeria and is one the world's best-known Arab singers.<ref name="Swedenburg 2010" /> To date, Khaled has sold over 80.5 million albums (10 diamond, platinum, and gold) worldwide, making him one of the bestselling Arabic-language singers in history.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Cheb Khaled: 15 interesting facts about the 'Didi' singer|url=https://www.iloveqatar.net/news/entertainment/cheb-khaled-15-interesting-facts-about-the-didi-singer|access-date=2021-04-28|website=www.iloveqatar.net|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Cheb Khaled for Citizens of the World {{!}} Equus World|url=http://www.equus-world.com/en/citizens-of-the-world/the-artists/cheb-khaled.html|access-date=2021-01-11|website=www.equus-world.com}}</ref> Among his most famous songs are "[[Didi (song)|Didi]]", "El Arbi", "[[Abdel Kader (song)|Abdel Kader]]", "[[La Poupée qui fait non]]", "Wahran Wahran", "Bakhta", "[[Aïcha]]", "[[C'est la vie (Khaled song)|C'est la vie]]", and "Alech Taadi".<ref name="Cornea2007">{{cite book|author=Dr Christine Cornea|title=Science Fiction Cinema|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_d8kDQAAQBAJ&pg=PA207|date=6 June 2007|publisher=Edinburgh University Press|isbn=978-0-7486-2870-4|page=207}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Khaled Hadj Ibrahim was born in 1960 in [[Oran]]'s Eckmühl neighborhood, [[French Algeria|Algeria]].<ref>[http://next.liberation.fr/culture/1996/11/12/bio-rai_188806 "Bio raï"], ''[[Libération]]''</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=Dictionary of African Biography: Abach – Brand|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=39JMAgAAQBAJ&pg=RA2-PA348|year=2012|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=978-0-19-538207-5|page=2}}<br>{{cite book|author=Andy Gregory|title=The International Who's Who in Popular Music 2002|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gZIjT8PgJMEC&pg=PA276|year=2002|publisher=Psychology Press|isbn=978-1-85743-161-2|page=276}}<br>{{cite book|author=Donald Clarke|title=The Penguin encyclopedia of popular music|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zSsKAQAAMAAJ|year=1998|publisher=Penguin Books|page=960|isbn=9780140513707}}<br>{{cite book|author=Steve Sullivan|title=Encyclopedia of Great Popular Song Recordings|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GQglDwAAQBAJ&pg=PA692|date=17 May 2017|publisher=Rowman & Littlefield Publishers|isbn=978-1-4422-5449-7|page=692}}</ref>

Khaled was a playboy and a partier, rejecting the polite traditions of Algeria's poetry. When a traditional Oranese poet wants to describe love, Khaled says he prefers to take a different approach.

== Musical career ==
At the age of 14, he founded the ''Cinq Étoiles'' (Five Stars) band and started performing in nightclubs and at weddings. In the 1980s, Khaled started producing and singing songs in the [[Raï]] genre.

His rise to national fame was mainly due to the efforts of Lieutenant-Colonel Hosni Snoussi, director of the state-supported arts and culture Office Riadh el Feth, who took Khaled under his wing and invited him along with other rai stars to perform at the state-sponsored Festival de la Jeunesse pour la Fête Nationale in Algiers in July 1985.<ref name="AkyeampongGates2012">{{cite book|author1=Emmanuel Kwaku Akyeampong|author2=Henry Louis Gates|author3=Mr. Steven J. Niven|title=Dictionary of African Biography|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=39JMAgAAQBAJ&pg=RA2-PA349|date=2 February 2012|publisher=OUP USA|isbn=978-0-19-538207-5|page=349}}</ref><ref name="Swedenburg 2010">{{cite web | last=Swedenburg | first=Ted | title=Khaled and the myth of rai | website=Foreign Policy | date=10 September 2010 | url=https://foreignpolicy.com/2010/09/10/khaled-and-the-myth-of-rai/ | access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref> In the same year, he was crowned king of rai in the first official festival of rai which was staged in Oran.<ref name="DorianDuane1999">{{cite book|author1=Frederick Dorian|author2=Orla Duane|author3=James McConnachie|title=World Music: Africa, Europe and the Middle East|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gyiTOcnb2yYC&pg=PA420|year=1999|publisher=Rough Guides|isbn=978-1-85828-635-8|page=420}}</ref>
[[File:Khaled a la Coupole 2014 Algerie o.jpg|thumb|Khaled a la Coupole 2014 Algerie]]
Hosni Snoussi and [[Martin Meissonnier]], who met at the Festival, convinced France's Minister of Culture [[Jack Lang (French politician)|Jack Lang]] that the export of rai from Algeria to France was in the French government's interest and together they organized the first rai festival in France at Bobigny in 1986. Cheb Khaled, who had been avoiding his mandatory military service, was able to perform at Bobigny only after Colonel Snoussi intervened with the Algerian military authorities to secure him a passport.<ref name="AkyeampongGates2012" /><ref name="Swedenburg 2010" /> Shortly thereafter, Snoussi arranged for Cheb Khaled to record in France, with funding from the Office Riadh el Feth. The album, [[Kutché]], released in 1988, a collaboration between Khaled and the Algerian jazz musician [[Safy Boutella]], expanded his reputation in France, where he soon settled.<ref name="AkyeampongGates2012" />
[[File:Steve Hillage 1978.jpg|thumb|Guitarist [[Steve Hillage]] produced many of Khaled's career.]]
By 1986, Khaled (who'd now dropped the Cheb) had been forced to move to Paris, as violence in Algeria claimed lives and he'd been threatened several times (producer Ahmed would, in fact, be murdered, as would several Raï performers). Arriving with a reputation, he attempted to take his music to a global level, although his real debut, Kutche, did him no favors with its jazz-rock arrangements. He fared much better with 1992's Khaled, with some tracks produced by Michael Brook and others by [[Don Was]]. It yielded his first big hit, "[[Didi (song)|Didi]]" and helped consolidate his reputation as Raï's first superstar. That was firmly cemented the following year with the Was-produced [[N'ssi N'ssi]], which mixed funk, rock, and an Egyptian orchestra behind Khaled's persuasive voice and winning smile for a major commercial breakthrough in France, winning a European, as well as North African, audience. In 1996, he hit again with Sahra, whose first single, "[[Aicha (song)|Aïcha]]," written for his daughter, gave Khaled a French number one hit. A mix of producers gave varying sounds, with French hip-hoppers Akhenton & Imhotep proving the hardest on the track "Oran Marseille" with its rapid-fire rap. There was even some reggae on "Mektoubi," which merged relatively seamlessly with the North African sound. While well-received critically, it sold little in the U.S. however, unlike France, where the album became Khaled's biggest success. Perhaps the moment that justified his title of King of Raï, however, came in 1999, when he headlined the [[1,2,3 Soleils|1-2-3 Soleils]] concert in Paris (which led to the album of the same name), over [[Rachid Taha]] and [[Faudel]]. It was the biggest Algerian show ever staged in France and left no doubt that Khaled remained Raï's hottest attraction. 2000 brought [[Kenza (album)|Kenza]] and a change of producer, as former prog rocker [[Steve Hillage]] helped bring a more organic feel to the proceedings. A smash in Europe, once again it did little business in the U.S.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Cheb Khaled {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/cheb-khaled-mn0000089264/biography|access-date=2020-12-29|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref>

=== International impact ===
[[File:Khaled en Concert pour la Tolérance (2012).jpg|thumb|Cheb Khaled performing at the 7th annual Concert for Tolerance in [[Agadir]] (2012).]]
<blockquote>One of the Pioneers of world music, Rai musician Khaled has gone beyond all geo-political boundaries to become one of the world's most popular performers, mixing traditional [[Music of Algeria|Algerian music]] with western rhythms and styles such as [[Soul music|soul]], [[Rock music|rock]] and [[reggae]] to achieve his distinctive sound and voice unlike anyone ever.
Khaled has been celebrated not only for his music, but for his role as a builder of bridges between cultures. selling more than 80 millions copies around the world makes him a nationwide celebrity and unofficial ambassador for the country's Arab minority.
—[[Food and Agriculture Organization|Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations]], Rome, 2008 <ref>{{Cite web|title=تفاصيل {{!}} FAO {{!}} منظمة الأغذية والزراعة للأمم المتحدة|url=http://www.fao.org/publications/card/ar/c/cd23c480-c43c-56cf-bc1d-cc91cb2d6d61/|access-date=2021-05-03|website=www.fao.org}}</ref></blockquote>

Khaled's signature song, the 1993 hit "[[Didi (song)|Didi]]", became extremely popular in the Arabic-speaking countries and also in several other continents, including Europe, where it entered top charts in France, Belgium and Spain, and in Asia, including India and Pakistan. The song was also used in a [[Bollywood]] films ''Shreeman Aashiq'' and ''[[Airlift (film)|Airlift]]'' and a [[Malayalam cinema|Mollywood]] film titled ''[[Highway (2014 Hindi film)|Highway]]''. Khaled and producer [[Don Was]] appeared on ''[[The Tonight Show]]'' on 4 February 1993.<ref>{{cite news |title= Today's TV Tips |work= [[Atlanta Journal-Constitution]] |date= 4 February 1993 }}</ref> <ref name="Thompson2014">{{cite book|author=William Forde Thompson|title=Music in the Social and Behavioral Sciences: An Encyclopedia|date=18 July 2014|publisher=SAGE Publications|isbn=978-1-4522-8302-9|page=37}}</ref> 

In the wake of the 9/11 tragedy, Khaled became the first Arab artist to headline a U.S. tour, breaking attendance records throughout the U.S. in February 2002. In May of last year 2004 the two performed together for the first time since recording Khaled's hit single Didi, in Rome, Italy. Together [[Don Was]] & Khaled performed live a new rendition for [[Quincy Jones]]'s historic concert "We Are the Future". The song was part of the compilation CD and a Live DVD to be released throughout the world all the proceeds, to support children of war torn areas.

On 16 October 2003, Khaled was nominated [[FAO Goodwill Ambassador|Goodwill Ambassador]] of the [[Food and Agriculture Organization]] of the United Nations (FAO).

Khaled returned to the America in December of 2004 for a special guest performance at the [[Grammy Award|GRAMMY]] JAM 2004 in Los Angeles, Ca. He joined a cast of celebrity artists honoring the great musical legends Earth Wind and Fire, performing Brazilian Rhymes into [[Didi (song)|DIDI]] showing how there music crossed the world, fusing with his North African style.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Cheb Khaled for Citizens of the World {{!}} Equus World|url=http://www.equus-world.com/en/citizens-of-the-world/the-artists/cheb-khaled.html|access-date=2021-01-11|website=www.equus-world.com}}</ref>

In 2010 he performed ''Didi'' at the opening ceremony of the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]] in South Africa.

In 2012 Khaled's album ''[[C'est la vie (Khaled song)|C’est la vie]]'' sold more than one million copies in the European market, 1.8 million copies in the [[Middle East]] and North Africa, and over 4 million copies worldwide.<ref name="Sullivan2017">{{cite book|author=Steve Sullivan|title=Encyclopedia of Great Popular Song Recordings|date=17 May 2017|publisher=Rowman & Littlefield Publishers|isbn=978-1-4422-5449-7|page=693}}</ref><ref name="Hammond2017">{{cite book|author=Andrew Hammond|title=Pop Culture in North Africa and the Middle East: Entertainment and Society around the World|date=22 May 2017|publisher=ABC-CLIO|isbn=978-1-4408-3384-7|page=40}}</ref> The album reached number 5 on [[SNEP]], the official French Albums Chart.<ref>{{cite web | author= emiratesvoice | title=Cheb Khaled tops best French song list | website=emiratesvoice | url=https://www.emiratesvoice.com/en/274/cheb-khaled-tops-best-french-song-list}}</ref>

On 3 April 2015, Khaled was convicted for plagiarism of Didi, from ''Angui ou Selmi'', a musical composition recorded by Cheb Rabah (born Rabah Zerradine) in 1988.<ref>{{cite web|title=à propos de sa chanson à succès Didi : Khaled condamné pour plagiat |url=http://www.elwatan.com/culture/khaled-condamne-pour-plagiat-08-04-2015-291849_113.php |website=El Watan |access-date=5 October 2015 |url-status=unfit |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150412110057/http://www.elwatan.com/culture/khaled-condamne-pour-plagiat-08-04-2015-291849_113.php |archive-date=12 April 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Raï : Khaled condamné pour plagiat pour son tube " Didi "|url=http://www.lemonde.fr/culture/article/2015/04/07/rai-khaled-condamne-pour-plagiat-pour-son-tube-didi_4611196_3246.html|website=Le Monde|access-date=5 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title="Didi" : comparez la version de Khaled et l'originale de Rabah|url=http://tempsreel.nouvelobs.com/culture/20150408.OBS6694/didi-comparez-la-version-de-khaled-et-l-originale-de-rabah.html|website=Nouvel Obs|access-date=5 October 2015}}</ref> But on 13 May 2016, Court of Cassation removed the charges against Khaled, when a 1982 audio tape with the song was shown. This tape was recorded by Khaled and given to a producer located in Oran, 6 years before Cheb Rabah's record. In the end, Rabah had to compensate Khaled for the fees during this case.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/musique/2016/05/17/03006-20160517ARTFIG00183--didi-la-justice-donne-raison-a-khaled.php|title=Didi : la justice donne raison à Khaled|date=2016-05-17|work=FIGARO|access-date=2017-11-01|language=fr-FR}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
On 12 January 1995, Khaled married Samira Diabi, when she was 27. With Diabi he has four daughters and one son.

In 1997, his wife filed a complaint against him for domestic violence, before retracting the complaint.<ref name="JDD 2009">{{cite web | last=JDD | first=Le | title=Khaled proclame sa Liberté | website=lejdd.fr | date=30 March 2009 | url=https://www.lejdd.fr/Culture/Musique/Khaled-proclame-sa-Liberte-75494-3256346 | language=fr | access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="Libération.fr 1997">{{cite web | title=Khaled traîné devant le juge par sa femme | website=Libération.fr | date=14 June 1997 | url=https://www.liberation.fr/france-archive/1997/06/14/khaled-traine-devant-le-juge-par-sa-femme_207001 | language=fr | access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref>

In 1998, the biography ''Khaled: Derrière le sourire'' ([[French language|French]] for "Khaled: Behind the Smile) was published, which recounted his life.

Khaled has an illegitimate son with whom he has no contact. Before a court in 2001, he denied being the father of the child, continuing to claim that he had been "deceived".<ref name="leparisien.fr 2013">{{cite web | title=VIDEO. Le fils caché de Khaled : "Je suis né d'un père trop connu" | website=leparisien.fr | date=13 November 2013 | url=http://www.leparisien.fr/laparisienne/people/video-le-fils-cache-de-khaled-je-suis-ne-d-un-pere-trop-connu-13-11-2013-3311077.php | language=fr | access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="Algerie Focus 2013">{{cite web | title=VIDEO. Le fils caché de Cheb Khaled : "Je voudrais juste qu'il vienne me parler cinq minutes" :Algerie Focus France | website=Algerie Focus | date=13 November 2013 | url=https://www.algerie-focus.com/2013/11/video-le-fils-cache-de-cheb-khaled-je-voudrais-juste-quil-vienne-me-parler-cinq-minutes/ | language=fr | access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref> On 7 May 2001, Khaled was sentenced by the Nanterre criminal court to a two-month suspended prison sentence for "family desertion".<ref name="leparisien.fr 2001">{{cite web | title=Deux mois de prison avec sursis pour Khaled | website=leparisien.fr | date=7 May 2001 | url=http://www.leparisien.fr/faits-divers/deux-mois-de-prison-avec-sursis-pour-khaled-08-05-2001-2002152994.php | language=fr | access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref> His move to Luxembourg in 2008, where he's been residing ever since, has reportedly been motivated by these charges.<ref name="AkyeampongGates2012" />

He was awarded Moroccan citizenship in August 2013,<ref>{{cite web|title=Decret royal sur le bulletin officiel |page=2321 |publisher=Sgg.gov.ma |url=http://www.sgg.gov.ma/Portals/0/BO/bulletin/FR/2013/BO_6184_Fr.pdf |access-date=29 June 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140201234448/http://www.sgg.gov.ma/Portals/0/BO/bulletin/FR/2013/BO_6184_Fr.pdf |archive-date= 1 February 2014 }}</ref> which he didn't ask for but accepted because he felt he could not refuse.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.liberation.fr/culture/2013/11/10/khaled-remis-sur-ses-rai_945975 |title=Khaled, remis sur ses raï – Libération |publisher=Liberation.fr |access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref>
