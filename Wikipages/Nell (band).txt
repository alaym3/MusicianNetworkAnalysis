'''Nell''' ([[Hangul]]: 넬) is a South Korean [[alternative rock]] band formed in 2001. The band consists of lead vocalist, keyboardist and guitarist Kim Jong-wan, lead guitarist Lee Jae-kyung, bass guitarist Lee Jung-hoon and drummer Jung Jae-won. The group was named after the film, ''[[Nell (film)|Nell]]'', that starred [[Jodie Foster]]. The band is known for their gloomy and psychedelic sound, and has achieved fame with hits such as "Stay" from ''{{ill|Let It Rain (Nell album)|lt=Let It Rain|ko|Let It Rain|vertical-align=sup}}'', "Thank You" from ''{{ill|Walk Through Me|ko|Walk Through Me|vertical-align=sup}}'', "Good Night" and "마음을 잃다" (Losing Heart) from ''{{ill|Healing Process (album)|lt=Healing Process|ko|Healing Process|vertical-align=sup}}'' and  "기억을 걷는 시간" (Time Walking On Memory) from ''{{ill|Separation Anxiety (Nell album)|lt=Separation Anxiety|ko|Separation Anxiety|vertical-align=sup}}''. Their third studio album ''Healing Process'' was chosen as one of five best recordings of the year<ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.naver.com/main/read.nhn?mode=LSD&mid=sec&sid1=106&oid=111&aid=0000054732 |title=넬 공연, 예매 시작 2분만에 매진 |last=Park |first=Jae-deok |publisher=Joy News 24 |date=2007-01-17 }}</ref> by South Korean critics. Nell's fourth studio album ''Separation Anxiety'' was a hit in South Korea, ranking number one in various album charts.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://news.naver.com/main/read.nhn?mode=LSD&mid=sec&sid1=106&oid=117&aid=0001947452 |title=넬, 앨범판매 1위-콘서트 매진 '겹경사' |last=Kang |first=Ji-hoon |publisher=My Daily |date=2008-04-01 }}</ref>

==Musical career==

===Early years and turning point===
The group performed at clubs around the [[Sinchon-dong, Seoul|Sinchon-dong]] area and released two [[indie rock]] albums, ''{{ill|Reflection of|ko|Reflection of|vertical-align=sup}}'' and ''{{ill|Speechless (Nell album)|lt=Speechless|ko|Speechless (넬의 음반)|vertical-align=sup}}'', in 2001.

While performing at live clubs in [[Hongdae area|Hongdae]], they were picked up by [[Seo Taiji]], and soon became the first band to promote under the [[record label]] [http://www.goesoo.com/ Goesoo Indigene], a subsidiary of [[Seo Taiji Company]] in 2002. In 2006, the band decided to finish their contract with Goesoo Indigene. They signed with [[Woollim Entertainment]].

The band is heavily influenced by numerous [[British bands]] such as [[Radiohead]], [[Placebo (band)|Placebo]], [[Travis (band)|Travis]], and [[Muse (band)|Muse]]. They also performed a number of covers of Muse, [[Coldplay]], [[Sting (musician)|Sting]], and [[Bob Dylan]] during their gigs.

Nell has participated in both the 2006 and 2007 [[Pentaport Rock Festival]] of South Korea, where [[Placebo (band)|Placebo]] and [[Muse (band)|Muse]] also performed each year.

===Hiatus and comeback===
After a long hiatus due to [[Conscription in South Korea|compulsory military service]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2012/01/nell-to-break-four-year-hiatus-with-a-february-comeback |title=Nell to break four year hiatus with a February comeback |publisher=Allkpop |date=2012-01-19 |access-date=2013-07-04}}</ref> they released their fifth studio album titled ''{{ill|Slip Away (album)|lt=Slip Away|ko|Slip Away|vertical-align=sup}}'' on April 10, 2012, with the title track, "그리고 남겨진 것들" (The Day Before). Its music video starred [[Lee Min-ki]] and [[Song Jae-rim]], but none of the Nell members appeared. ''Slip Away'' was a hit in South Korea, placed top rank in various album charts, such as [[Mnet Media|Mnet]], [[Bugs Music|Bugs]], and Olleh Music.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allkpop.com/2012/04/nells-slip-away-sweeps-real-time-charts-tracks-from-the-album |title=NELL's 'Slip Away' sweeps real time charts + tracks from the album |publisher=Allkpop |date=2012-04-10 }}</ref> Kim Jong-wan featured in [[G-Dragon]]'s song "Today" in his 2012 album ''[[One of a Kind (G-Dragon EP)|One of a Kind]]''.

==='Gravity' mini-album series & Japanese debut===
On November 22, 2012, Nell's official homepage included a new picture signaling Nell's comeback. The picture showed a large moon that had a kind of explosion going on. Nell's new album, ''Holding onto Gravity'' was released on December 3.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://global.mnet.com/news/newsdetail.m?searchNewsVO.news_id=201211221040_21722&category=All&query=Nell&page=1 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130129003157/http://global.mnet.com/news/newsdetail.m?searchNewsVO.news_id=201211221040_21722&category=All&query=Nell&page=1 |url-status=dead |archive-date=2013-01-29 |title=Nell Signals Comeback with Teaser Photo |last=Hong |first=Grace Dan-bi |publisher=CJ E&M enewsWorld |date=2012-11-12 }}</ref> On December 2, 2012, Woollim's YouTube official channel released the music video for "White Night" starring [[Im Soo-jung (actress)|Im Soo-jung]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c2bH5s1WK0k |title=NELL'백야(White night_白夜)' Music video teaser (Drama ver.) |date=2012-11-28 }}</ref>

In January 2013, Nell made their Japanese debut with the Japanese edition of ''Slip Away'', released by [http://www.homegamerecords.com/ Home Game Records], a [[Watanabe Productions|Watanabe Music Publishing]] label dedicated to Korean rock.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2013/01/nell-make-their-japanese-debut-with-slip-away |title=Nell make their Japanese debut with 'Slip Away' |publisher=Allkpop |date=2013-01-23 |access-date=2013-07-04}}</ref> In May 2013, the official Woollim YouTube channel uploaded a teaser for the upcoming mini-album which was scheduled for release in June. Nell's official homepage was updated on June 3 confirming the release date for the second part of their three-part mini-album series of 'Gravity', ''{{ill|Escaping Gravity|ko|Escaping Gravity|vertical-align=sup}}''. The music video for the mini-album's title track, "Ocean of Light", was released June 9.
[[File:2014 K-Pop Night Out at SXSW, Nell.jpg|thumb|right|Nell at 2014 [[K-Pop Night Out at SXSW]]]]

===2014 participation in U.S. concert and Billboard recognition===

On March 11, 2014, the band performed at the second [[K-Pop Night Out at SXSW]] in [[Austin, Texas]] along with [[Hyuna]] and [[Jay Park]] and others.<ref>{{cite web|last=kpop1stop|title=HyunA, Jay Park, Nell, Idiotape and more to Perform at 2014 SXSW Music Festival|url=http://www.soompi.com/2014/03/06/hyuna-jay-park-nell-idiotape-and-more-to-perform-at-2014-sxsw-music-festival-in-austin-tx/|date=March 6, 2014|publisher=[[Soompi]]|access-date=December 13, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Minor|first=James|title=Don't Miss South Korean Showcases at SXSW!|url=http://sxsw.com/music/news/2014/dont-miss-south-korean-showcases-sxsw|date=March 5, 2014|publisher=[[South by Southwest|SXSW]]|access-date=December 13, 2014}}</ref> And on December 11, their album {{ill|Newton's Apple (Nell album)|ko|Newton's Apple|vertical-align=sup|lt=Newton's Apple}} was selected as number two on "Billboard's 10 Best K-Pop Albums of 2014" by [[Billboard K-Town]] with the write-up, "The strongest rock album of the year, Newton's Apple highlights Nell's vocalist/chief composer Kim Jong Wan's clever and honest songwriting skills, best heard on the single "Four Times Around the Sun" and "Grey Zone"....All the lyrics are wrapped in warm, lush arrangements and instrumentals that enchant the listener simply by themselves."<ref>{{cite web|last=Benjamin|first=Jeff, and Jessica Oak|title=The 10 Best K-Pop Albums of 2014|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/6397820/best-k-pop-albums-2014|date=December 11, 2014|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=December 12, 2014}}</ref>

=== 2016: Space Bohemian ===
On March 22, 2016, it was announced that Nell ended their contract with Woolim Entertainment and set up an independent record label named Space Bohemian.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.soompi.com/2016/03/21/nell-leaves-woollim-entertainment-to-set-up-independent-label/|title=Nell Leaves Woollim Entertainment to Set Up Independent Label|last=K|first=J.|date=2016-03-21|website=Soompi|access-date=2016-12-11}}</ref> On August 18, 2016, they released their first album with their new label, "C" with the title song Dream Catcher.
