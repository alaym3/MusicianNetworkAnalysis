'''CNCO''' is a Latin American [[boy band]] based in [[Miami|Miami, Florida]].<ref name="CNCO">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/6806078/cnco-la-banda-boy-band-finale|title=Hello CNCO! 'La Banda' Wraps Season With New Boy Band|last=Cobo|first=Leila|date=December 14, 2015|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref> The group consists of Richard Camacho, Erick Brian Colón, Christopher Vélez and Zabdiel De Jesús; former member Joel Pimentel departed the band in May 2021. They won a five-year recording contract with [[Sony Music Latin]] after becoming the winning competitors of the first season of ''[[La Banda (TV series)|La Banda]].'' The band toured with [[Ricky Martin]]. Their debut and second singles "Tan Fácil" and "Quisiera", charted well soon after their debut.<ref name="charthistory">{{cite web|title=CNCO – Billboard chart The group released their [[CNCO (album)|self-titled second studio album]], on April 6, 2018, which included the singles "[[Mamita (song)|Mamita]]" and "Se Vuelve Loca".<ref name="fresca">{{cite web|title=CNCO lanza álbum con música "más fresca y madura"|url=http://www.ecuavisa.com/articulo/entretenimiento/espectaculo/374381-cnco-lanza-album-musica-mas-fresca-madura|date=April 6, 2018|language=es|work=[[Ecuavisa]]|access-date=April 9, 2018}}</ref> Their debut EP ''Que Quiénes Somos,'' containing the singles "De Cero" and "Pegao" featuring [[Manuel Turizo]], was released on October 11, 2019. Both albums debuted at number one on the [[Billboard Top Latin Albums|''Billboard'' Top Latin Albums]] and the top 40 on th''e [[Billboard 200|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard 200|200]], while the EP debuted within the top 15 of the former chart.

In 2020, they began the era of their album ''Déjà Vu'', a cover album of Latin pop classics, with three singles released from November to December. On February 5, 2021, the band released the LP. The album was released preceded by the fourth single and accompanied by the fifth on its release day.

==History==

=== 2015: ''La Banda'' ===
Erick Brian Colón, Christopher Vélez, Zabdiel De Jesús, Richard Camacho and Joel Pimentel auditioned for the [[La Banda (U.S. season 1)|first season]] of ''La Banda'', a televised singing competition which started on [[Univision]] in September 2015, created by [[Simon Cowell]] and produced by Ricky Martin.<ref>{{cite web|last=Gardner|first=Elysa|title=Ricky Martin joins Simon Cowell's 'La Banda'|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2015/02/19/ricky-martin-to-be-first-judge-on-simon-cowells-la-banda|date=February 19, 2015|work=[[USA Today]]|access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref> They all received an 75% of votes from the public during auditions except Colón, though he was saved by Martin. The band's members competed individually with other contestants from [[Latin America]] and the United States before the show's judges, Martin, [[Alejandro Sanz]] and [[Laura Pausini]]. The boys had to go through several phases to become the winners. During the competition, they were put into groups, with Camacho and De Jesús in the same band, while Vélez, Colón and Pimentel were in another. The band was formed on the finale of the show, on December 13, 2015, when they received their name, which alludes to the Spanish word for five, "cinco", and is pronounced in English as it is spelled: "C-N-C-O."<ref name="handpicked">{{cite web|last=Flores|first=Griselda|title=Magneto, CNCO or Menudo? Vote for Your All-Time Favorite Latin Boy Band!|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7439041/magneto-cnco-menudo-latin-boy-band-poll|date=July 15, 2016|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref> They won a recording contract of 5 years with [[Sony Music Latin]],<ref name="album"/> and rapper [[Wisin]] was scheduled to produce their first album.<ref name="CNCO" /> Martin became their representative.<ref name="pre-sales"/><ref name="winners">{{cite web|last=Irizarry|first=Doris|title=First 'La Banda' winners form CNCO: the newest Latin boy band|url=http://www.axs.com/first-la-banda-winners-form-cnco-the-newest-latin-boy-band-72399|date=December 16, 2016|work=[[AXS TV|AXS]]|access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref>

They performed the song "Devuélveme Mi Corazón" on the finale, which included performances by [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]], [[Fifth Harmony]], Wisin, and a video congratulations from [[One Direction]].<ref name="CNCO" /><ref name="winners"/> They sang it again in [[Times Square]] at Univision's Feliz 2016 [[New Year]]'s celebration.<ref name="newyear">{{cite web|last=Benjamin|first=Jeff|title=Can CNCO Be the Next Big Boy Band? Listen to Debut Single 'Tan Fácil'|url=http://www..tv/2016/01/cnco-tan-facil-next-big-boy-band|date=January 29, 2016|work=[[Fuse (TV channel)|Fuse TV]]|access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Roiz|first=Jessica Lucia|title=Demi Lovato, Maluma, CNCO, Taylor Swift, Celebrate New Year's Eve In Times Square, Plus Full Performers List|url=http://www.latintimes.com/pulse/demi-lovato-maluma-cnco-taylor-swift-celebrate-new-years-eve-times-square-plus-full-362086|date=December 29, 2015|work=[[IBT Media|Latin Times]]|access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref>

On April 6, 2018, the boy band released their highly anticipated second self-titled album, ''[[CNCO (album)|CNCO]]''.<ref name="fresca"/> It debuted at number one on the [[Billboard Top Latin Albums|''Billboard'' Top Latin Albums]] and entered at number 33 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], succeeding their debut. The group released "Sólo Yo", along with its music video, to celebrate the album's release.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Burstein|first=Sergio|date=April 6, 2018|title=CNCO estrena video de 'Sólo yo' mientras lanza su nuevo disco|url=https://www.hoylosangeles.com/espectaculos/musica/hoyla-cnco-estrena-video-de-solo-yo-mientras-lanza-su-nuevo-disco-20180406-story.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 11, 2020|website=Hoy LA|language=Spanish}}</ref> On May 16, CNCO signed a new management deal with Walter Kolm Entertainment, after being managed by Ricky Martin for nearly three years.<ref>{{Cite web|title=CNCO Signs Management Deal With Walter Kolm Entertainment|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8456263/cnco-management-deal-walter-kolm-entertainment/|access-date=December 11, 2020|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> The music video for the fifth single "Se Vuelve Loca" was made available on July 19, with a Spanglish version being released for digital download and streaming several hours later.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=July 20, 2018|title=CNCO estrena hoy a nivel mundial el video musical de su nuevo sencillo "SE VUELVE LOCA"|url=https://sonymusiclatin.com/cnco-estrena-hoy-a-nivel-mundial-el-video-musical-de-su-nuevo-sencillo-se-vuelve-loca/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 11, 2020|website=Sony Music Entertainment Latin|language=}}</ref> It reached number one on the Latin Pop Songs Chart in October 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8480828/cnco-se-vuelve-loca-latin-pop-songs-chart|title=CNCO's 'Se Vuelve Loca' Hits No. 1 on Latin Pop Songs Chart|last=Bustios|first=Pamela|date=October 19, 2018|website=Billboard|access-date=November 8, 2018}}</ref> The band embarked on the CNCO World Tour, starting in Guatemala. They announced shows in the United States, thus becoming their first U.S. tour. The band released a collaboration with singer-songwriter [[Prince Royce]] on October 26, titled "Llegaste Tú".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Stivale|first=Shelby|date=October 26, 2018|title=CNCO Gives Off Tropical Vibes In Visual For New Hit 'Llegaste Tú'|url=https://tigerbeat.com/video/cnco-llegaste-tu/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 11, 2020|website=Tiger Beat|language=en-US}}</ref> On November 8, the group released a [[Hey DJ (CNCO song)#Meghan Trainor and Sean Paul remix|remix version]] of "Hey DJ", with American singer-songwriter [[Meghan Trainor]] and Jamaican artist [[Sean Paul]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Benjamin|first=Jeff|title=CNCO's Latest Pop Play Comes Via Spanglish 'Hey DJ' With Meghan Trainor & Sean Paul|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/jeffbenjamin/2018/11/09/cnco-hey-dj-meghan-trainor-sean-paul-spanglish-remix/|access-date=December 11, 2020|website=Forbes|language=en}}</ref>

===2019: ''Que Quiénes Somos'' ===
The band announced the single "Pretend", which was released on February 15, 2019. The song is their first official solo Spanglish release, and samples "[[Rhythm of the Night (song)|Rhythm of the Night]]" by [[DeBarge]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Benjamin|first=Jeff|date=February 21, 2019|title=CNCO Makes Biggest Spanglish Statement Yet With New 'Pretend' Single|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/jeffbenjamin/2019/02/21/cnco-makes-biggest-spanglish-statement-yet-with-new-pretend-single/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=Forbes|language=en}}</ref>

CNCO released the single "De Cero" on June 24 along with its music video.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fernandez|first=Suzette|date=June 24, 2019|title=CNCO Are More Romantic Than Ever in 'De Cero' Video: Watch|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8517219/cnco-de-cero-music-video|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> They performed a short version of "Pretend" at the [[2019 Teen Choice Awards|Teen Choice Awards of 2019]], where they won the Choice Latin Artist and were nominated for Choice Group. Spanish singer-songwriter Abraham Mateo launched his song "Me Vuelvo Loco" in collaboration with the boys in July.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Calvario|first=Liz|date=August 1, 2019|title=Abraham Mateo and CNCO Show Darker Side of Love In 'Loco' Music Video|url=https://www.etonline.com/abraham-mateo-and-cnco-show-darker-side-of-love-in-me-vuelvo-loco-music-video-exclusive-129643|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=Entertainment Tonight|language=en-US}}</ref> The group revealed their first extended play, ''Que Quiénes Somos'', in mid-August, along with its cover art.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Cordero|first=Rosy|date=August 20, 2019|title=CNCO flex their muscles as composers and writers on new EP 'Que Quienes Somos'|url=https://ew.com/music/2019/08/20/cnco-que-quienes-somos-ep-interview/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=EW.com|language=EN}}</ref> Its title references the line sung by Colón at the end in most of their songs and also alludes as to who the boys are as they co-wrote the EP themselves.<ref name=":0" /> They released the first promotional single, titled "Ya Tú Sabes" in the 23rd of the same month. It contains a sample of a song by Cuban salsa singer [[Celia Cruz]] and features Latin Trap in its production.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Exposito|first=Suzy|date=August 23, 2019|title=CNCO Pay Tribute to Celia Cruz in New Song 'Ya Tú Sabes'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/cnco-celia-cruz-new-song-ya-tu-sabes-875803/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> The band was announced as part of the line-up for [[2019 MTV Video Music Awards]] pre-show where they performed "De Cero" on August 26. The second promotional song, named "La Ley", was made free on all digital platforms on September 27. Both songs were released with audios on YouTube. The EP was released on October 11, 2019, and includes seven tracks in total. They also released the single "Pegao" with [[Manuel Turizo]] on the same day with a music video.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Martin|first=Annie|date=October 11, 2019|title=CNCO releases new EP, 'Pegao' music video|url=https://www.upi.com/Entertainment_News/Music/2019/10/11/CNCO-releases-new-EP-Pegao-music-video/7201570807852/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 11, 2020|website=UPI|language=en}}</ref> The EP ''Que Quiénes Somos'' debuted at No. 1 on the Latin Pop Albums ''Billboard'' chart. The group collaborated with fellow boy band [[PRETTYMUCH]] on the song "Me Necesita."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Roiz|first=Jessica|date=October 23, 2019|title=Watch Prettymuch & CNCO Team Up For an Epic Boy Band Battle in 'Me Necesita' Video|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8540076/prettymuch-cnco-me-necesita-video/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 11, 2020|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> CNCO performed "Ya Tú Sabes" and also performed "Me Vuelvo Loco" alongside [[Abraham Mateo]] at the 2019 Latin American Music Awards and also won 2 awards that night. On November 7 the group was featured on the song "[[Como Así]]" by Argentinian singer [[Lali Espósito]], which appears on her then-upcoming album, [[Libra (Lali album)|''Libra'']].<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=November 8, 2019|title=Lali lanza single y video junto a CNCO: Mirá "Como así"|url=https://billboard.com.ar/lali-lanza-single-y-video-junto-a-cnco-mira-como-asi/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=Billboard Argentina|language=es}}</ref> In the same month, CNCO announced a clothing campaign with [[Forever 21]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Roiz|first=Jessica|date=November 12, 2019|title=CNCO Teams Up With Forever 21 for First-Ever Clothing Line|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8543384/cnco-forever-21-capsule-collection-photos/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

On January 31, 2021, CNCO released their rendition of [[Franco De Vita]]'s 1986 ballad "Solo Importas Tú". ==Members==
===Christopher Vélez===
Christopher Bryant Vélez Muñoz was born on November 23, 1995, in [[New Jersey]]. He moved with his family to their home country, [[Ecuador]] when he was two years old. When he turned 18, Vélez returned to the U.S. in hopes of helping his family economically; he had several jobs, including shining shoes for a living. He originally wanted to go to school to study music but saw ''La Banda'' as a better opportunity. Vélez is the oldest member in CNCO.<ref name="winners42">{{cite web|last=Irizarry|first=Doris|date=December 16, 2015|title=First 'La Banda' winners form CNCO: the newest Latin boy band|url=http://www.axs.com/first-la-banda-winners-form-cnco-the-newest-latin-boy-band-72399|access-date=August 15, 2016|work=[[AXS TV|AXS]]}}</ref>

===Richard Camacho===
Richard Yashel Camacho Puello was born on January 22, 1997, in New York City but grew up in the [[Dominican Republic]]. His father is a musician and [[Camera operator|cameraman]] and his mother is a dancer, which is also a hobby of his. Camacho has been singing and dancing ever since he was three years old, and the first song he has ever listened to was "With You" by [[Chris Brown]]. He has three other siblings; Yashua Camacho, who also took the lifestyle of singing and is a solo artist, Dairan Camacho, and Yarliza Camacho. He had a daughter with Instagram model Yocelyn Alexander, named Aaliyah Camacho, after late R&B singer [[Aaliyah]].<ref name="winners42" />

===Zabdiel De Jesús===
Zabdiel De Jesús was born in [[Bayamón]], [[Puerto Rico]] on December 13, 1997. He developed an interest in singing at age twelve, singing with his church [[Hip hop music|hip-hop]] and [[Salsa music|salsa]] group, as well as dancing ballet at Ballet Concierto, a prestigious academy in [[Puerto Rico]]. As a young child, his father, a musician, showed him a love for the music that is now what keeps their family together. From a young age, De Jesús had the ability to play instruments. He has shown his skills at [[beatboxing]]. Interestingly, De Jesús' birthday is on the same day as the group's anniversary.<ref name="CNCO Music">{{cite web|title=CNCO Music|url=http://www.cncomusic.com/|access-date=August 15, 2016|work=CNCOMusic.com}}</ref>

=== Erick Brian Colón ===
Erick Brian Colón Arista was born on January 3, 2001, in [[Havana]], [[Cuba]]. He moved to [[Tampa]], [[Florida]] with his family at the age of 11 in 2012. There, Colón played soccer, danced, and participated in musical activities at school. He has a sister named Yanelis. Colón is the youngest member of the boyband and was in ''La Banda'' when he was only 14 years old.
