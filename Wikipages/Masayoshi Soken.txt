{{nihongo|'''Masayoshi Soken'''|祖堅正慶|Soken Masayoshi|born January 10, 1975}} is a Japanese [[video game music|video game composer]] and [[Sound editor (filmmaking)|sound editor]] who has worked for [[Square Enix]] since 2001. Soken is best known for being the lead composer and sound director of ''[[Final Fantasy XIV]]'' and its expansions. Throughout his musical career, Soken has also used the names "Sorbonne Soken" and "Louise Noma".

==Biography==
Born in [[La Paz, Baja California Sur]], [[Mexico]], Soken and his family later moved to [[Tokyo]], where he attended the [[Tokyo University of Science]], majoring in chemistry.<ref name="Biography">{{cite web|url=http://www.vgmonline.net/masayoshisoken|title=Masayoshi Soken Profile|publisher=Game Music Online|accessdate=September 21, 2014}}</ref><ref name="VGMO1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.vgmonline.net/masayoshisokeninterview/|title=Masayoshi Soken Interview: From Astral to Umbral|date=February 18, 2015|website=VGMO -Video Game Music Online-}}</ref> He was exposed to music at a young age—his father was a professional trumpet player and his mother taught piano.<ref name="VGMO1"/> Rather than pursue a career in science, he was hired as a sound editor and designer at [[Konami]].<ref name="VGMO1"/><ref name="Moukala">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-0fguLgOMwY|title=Talking with Masayoshi Soken - Final Fantasy XIV's Composer|via=www.youtube.com}}</ref> He joined [[Square (video game company)|Square]] in 1998 and his early work at the company primarily focused on sound effect design, rather than composition.<ref name="Moukala"/> He assisted [[Kenji Ito]] on [[sports video game]]s such as ''Gekikuukan Pro Baseball: The End of the Century 1999''.<ref name="VGMO1"/> His debut as a composer came with the Japan-exclusive [[sports game]]s ''Nichibeikan Pro Baseball: Final League'', in which he was the sole composer, and ''World Fantasista'' with [[synthesizer programmer]] [[Takeharu Ishimoto]].

In 2005, Soken worked on ''[[Drakengard 2]]'' and ''[[Front Mission 5: Scars of the War]]''.<ref name="Biography" /> The following year, Soken composed and arranged the score to ''[[Mario Hoops 3-on-3]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.square-enix.co.jp/music/sem/page/cm/profile/soken/index.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170609042425/http://www.square-enix.co.jp/music/sem/page/cm/profile/soken/index.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2017-06-09|title=SQUARE ENIX MUSIC|date=June 9, 2017}}</ref> Along with [[Kenji Ito]] and [[Tsuyoshi Sekito]], he created the soundtrack to ''[[Dawn of Mana]]'' (known as "''Seiken Densetsu 4''" in Japan), with [[Academy Award]]-winning composer [[Ryuichi Sakamoto]] providing the main theme.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.squareenixmusic.com/albums/s/seikendensetsu4.shtml |title=Seiken Densetsu 4 Original Soundtrack -Sanctuary- |publisher=Square Enix Music Online |accessdate=September 25, 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120417075536/http://www.squareenixmusic.com/albums/s/seikendensetsu4.shtml |archivedate=April 17, 2012 }}</ref> Soken also arranged a few tracks from previous ''[[Mana (series)|Mana]]'' games, and performed the [[electric guitar]] for his arrangements. In 2007, he scored the [[online game]] ''Elebest'' with Ai Yamashita.<ref name="Biography" />

Soken has also contributed to Square Enix advertisements; ''[[Front Mission 5: Scars of the War]]'' (2005) featured the sports commercial song "Blue Stream",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.squareenixmusic.com/reviews/harry/frontmission5.shtml|title=Front Mission 5 ~Scars of the War~ Original Soundtrack :: Review by Harry|publisher=Square Enix Music Online|author=Harry|accessdate=September 25, 2009}}</ref> Soken's only composition in the game.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.squareenixmusic.com/albums/f/frontmission5.shtml|title=Front Mission 5 ~Scars of the War~ Original Soundtrack|publisher=Square Enix Music Online|accessdate=September 25, 2009}}</ref> He also participated in a Square Enix advertisement for pencils where he got beaten up by two robots; the commercial featured music composed by him. Soken created the [[fanfare]] for Square Enix Music TV, a monthly video feature where new album releases are discussed and interviews with Square Enix composers are conducted.<ref name="Biography" /> For the [[iTunes]]-exclusive ''[[List of Square Enix compilation albums#Square Enix Music Official Bootleg|Square Enix Music Official Bootleg]]'' collection, Soken contributed the piece "Dog Street" for the first volume in 2006,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.squareenixmusic.com/reviews/don/squareenixbootleg1.shtml|title=Square Enix Music Official Bootleg Vol. 1 :: Review by Don|publisher=Square Enix Music Online|author=Don|accessdate=September 25, 2009}}</ref> and "Languid Afternoon" for the third volume in 2007; he went under the alias "Sorbonne Soken" on the third volume.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.squareenixmusic.com/reviews/don/squareenixbootleg3.shtml|title=Square Enix Music Official Bootleg Vol. 3 :: Review by Don|publisher=Square Enix Music Online|author=Don|accessdate=September 25, 2009}}</ref> In 2008, he composed the Japan-exclusive ''[[Nanashi no Game]]'', this time under the pseudonym "Louise Noma".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.squareenixmusic.com/musicnews2.php?subaction=showfull&id=1216843288&archive=&start_from=&ucat=4&|title=Nanashi no Game EP Released|publisher=Square Enix Music Online|author=Chris|date=July 23, 2008|accessdate=September 25, 2009|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120913233737/http://www.squareenixmusic.com/musicnews2.php?subaction=showfull&id=1216843288&archive=&start_from=&ucat=4&|archivedate=September 13, 2012}}</ref> In 2010, he composed another sports game for the Wii, ''[[Mario Sports Mix]]''.

Since the 2010 development team reshuffling, he has been sound director for ''[[Final Fantasy XIV]]''. Soken became primary composer for the title with the launch of ''A Realm Reborn'' and the expansions that followed. He formed a [[rock band]] called The Primals with members of the sound team to play at ''Final Fantasy XIV'' events such as Fan Festival.<ref name="Moukala"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://na.finalfantasyxiv.com/lodestone/topics/detail/416f6054a6618c4b7a0bc921821bb203e8237a77|title=Announcing the Fan Festival 2019 in Tokyo Stage Schedule|first=SQUARE ENIX|last=Inc|website=FINAL FANTASY XIV, The Lodestone}}</ref> The Primals have since released several albums starting with ''Final Fantasy XIV: From Astral to Umbral''.<ref name="VGMO1"/> Following [[Nobuo Uematsu]]'s illness in 2018,<ref>{{Citation|title=Final Fantasy Composer Nobuo Uematsu Stopping Work Due to Health Problems - IGN|url=https://www.ign.com/articles/2018/09/20/final-fantasy-composer-nobuo-uematsu-stopping-work-due-to-health-problems|language=en|access-date=September 6, 2019}}</ref> Soken composed the main theme for ''Shadowbringers''. With the previous expansions' main theme songs having previously been composed by Uematsu, this made ''Shadowbringers''{{'}} soundtrack the first in the ''Final Fantasy XIV'' series to be entirely written by Soken.

In May 2021, during the ''Final Fantasy XIV Digital Fan Festival'', Soken announced that he had been receiving [[chemotherapy]] for cancer treatment throughout most of 2020, adding that the cancer was in [[Remission (medicine)|remission]]. Soken kept the treatment hidden from most of the development team, doing some of his work for ''Final Fantasy XIV'' from hospital.<ref>{{Cite web|date=May 17, 2021|title='Final Fantasy XIV' composer Masayoshi Soken reveals cancer battle|url=https://www.nme.com/news/gaming-news/final-fantasy-xiv-composer-masayoshi-soken-reveals-cancer-diagnosis-2942462|access-date=May 20, 2021|website=[[NME]]|language=en-GB}}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
Soken primarily approaches his game composing with the player's experience in mind.<ref name="CB-Soken">{{Cite web|url=https://comicbook.com/gaming/news/final-fantasy-xiv-eorzean-symphony-masayoshi-soken/|title=FF XIV Composer Masayoshi Soken on the Eorzean Symphony, Rage Against the Machine, and Mercy|website=GAMING}}</ref> When tasked with arranging ''Final Fantasy'' music originally composed by [[Nobuo Uematsu]], he takes extra care to achieve this goal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usgamer.net/articles/final-fantasy-xiv-orchestra-interview|title=Final Fantasy XIV's Composer Was "Literally Shaking" Listening to an Orchestra Performing His Music|first=Mike|last=Williams|date=June 6, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.inverse.com/article/37691-final-fantasy-xiv-music-music-a-realm-reborn-masayoshi-soken-guinness-world-records|title=Why 'Final Fantasy XIV' Is in the Guinness Book of World Records|first=Corey|last=Plante|website=Inverse}}</ref> Soken credits his experience as a sound designer, sound editor, and voice editor for helping him handle the pressure of the ''Final Fantasy XIV'' remake.<ref name="VGMO1"/> He primarily composes using piano and keyboard but prefers playing guitar in live performances.<ref name="Moukala"/>

Soken's favorite bands are [[Rage Against the Machine]] and [[Pennywise (band)|Pennywise]].<ref name="CB-Soken"/><ref>{{cite web|last=Salbato|first=Mike|date=July 5, 2018|url=http://www.rpgfan.com/features/Masayoshi-Soken-E3-2018-Interview/|title=Masayoshi Soken E3 2018 Interview|website=RPGFan|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200702175230/https://www.rpgfan.com/features/Masayoshi-Soken-E3-2018-Interview/|archive-date=July 5, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>
