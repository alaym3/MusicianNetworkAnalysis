'''Las Ketchup''' is a Spanish [[girl group]] founded by [[flamenco]] record producer [[:es:Manuel Ruiz "Queco"|Manuel "Queco" Ruiz]]. The group, which consists of sisters Lucía, Lola, and Pilar Muñoz (later to be joined by a fourth sister Rocío), is best known for the 2002 hit single, "[[The Ketchup Song (Aserejé)]]", a single that sold over 7 million copies worldwide.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.esctoday.com/?p=5675|title=Las Ketchup to represent Spain!|last=Bakker|first=Sietse|publisher=European Broadcasting Union|date=27 February 2006|access-date=15 August 2012}}</ref> The group's debut studio album, ''[[Hijas del Tomate]]'', has sold over 2 million copies worldwide, but the second did not achieve the same success. 

They represented Spain at [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006]] in [[Athens]] with the song "[[Un Blodymary (song)|Un Blodymary]]". The group has not released any singles since 2006, but still performs together publicly around Spain and other countries.

==History==
===2001–02: Formation===
All members of the group are daughters of flamenco musician Juan Manuel Muñoz Expósito, best known by his stage name, El Tomate (Spanish for "The Tomato").

Las Ketchup were originally discovered by the flamenco record producer Manuel "Queco" Ruiz, who signed them to his newly formed, independent [[Córdoba (Spain)|Córdoba]] label Shaketown Music, that he ran together with Manuel Illán.<ref name="hit quarters">{{cite web |url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/opar/intrview_Portugu_sJ.html |title=Interview With Javier Portugués |publisher=[[HitQuarters]]|date=11 November 2002 |access-date=3 June 2011}}</ref>

===2002–05: ''Hijas del Tomate''===
In the middle of 2002, they had a major flamenco [[Europop]] fusion hit with "Aserejé" (released as "[[The Ketchup Song (Aserejé)|The Ketchup Song]]" in the United States, the United Kingdom and other countries), which reached the top of the charts in many countries, including: Spain, Portugal, Colombia, the United Kingdom, Italy, Japan, Albania, France, Finland, Turkey, Syria, Sweden, Norway, Belgium, the Netherlands, the Philippines, Switzerland, Germany, Romania, and Australia. However, it failed to hit the top 40 on the U.S. [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. The title and part of the lyrics were in Spanish. The hit also had an accompanying dance. The second single was "[[Kusha Las Payas]]" and garnered moderate success. The group re-released "The Ketchup Song" later in the same year, with a new video, as a Christmas song.

===2006: ''Un Blodymary'' and addition of Rocío===
The group took an extended absence from the public eye to write songs for a new album and, during the same period, Pilar had a baby. In 2006, they were joined by Rocío, a fourth sister, and came back as a four girl group to represent Spain in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006]]. The girls said themselves that Rocío had always been a member of "Las Ketchup", but that had not appeared in the promo material or performances because she was pregnant. They released their second studio album, titled ''Un Blodymary''. The album release was delayed until the beginning of 2006. They planned to release one million copies and their record producer Queco, who also produced and wrote their worldwide single, "Aserejé", indicated that the album displayed their original sound.{{Citation needed|date=January 2008}}  Their first single from the album was also titled "[[Un Blodymary (song)|Un Blodymary]]" for the Spanish entry in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006]]. The song finished twenty-first with only 18 points.

===2006–present===
In 2016, the group performed their hit "The Ketchup Song (Aserejé)" as an interval during the first semi final of the Swedish pre-selection to the [[Eurovision Song Contest]], [[Melodifestivalen 2016|Melodifestivalen]].<ref>{{cite web|title=No nos habían invitado a México: Las Ketchup|url=https://www.eluniversal.com.mx/articulo/espectaculos/2017/04/3/no-nos-habian-invitado-mexico-las-ketchup|publisher=[[El Universal (Mexico City)|El Universal]]}}</ref> In 2017, they performed at the [[Pal Norte]] in [[Monterrey]], Mexico.
