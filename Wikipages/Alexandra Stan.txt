'''Alexandra Ioana Stan''' (born 10 June 1989) is a Romanian singer-songwriter. Born in the city of [[Constanța]], she made her worldwide breakthrough with the 2010 single "[[Mr. Saxobeat]]", which was written and produced by Marcel Prodan and Andrei Nemirschi. They had previously discovered Stan at a karaoke bar in 2009 and signed her to their label, Maan Records. "Mr. Saxobeat" had followed the singer's debut single, "[[Lollipop (Param Pam Pam)]]" (2009), which brought her moderate fame in Romania. "Mr. Saxobeat" quickly achieved commercial success locally and abroad, reaching number one in several countries and gathering various [[Music recording certification|certification]]s. ''[[Saxobeats]]'', Stan's debut studio album, was released in August 2011 and features the follow-up singles "[[Get Back (ASAP)]]" (2011) and "[[Lemonade (Alexandra Stan song)|Lemonade]]" (2012), which had moderate success in Europe.

An alleged altercation with Prodan in 2013 and [[Alexandra Stan vs. Marcel Prodan|a lawsuit against him]] preceded the release of Stan's second album, ''[[Unlocked (Alexandra Stan album)|Unlocked]]'', which would eventually be issued in August 2014. Two of its singles, "[[Dance (Alexandra Stan song)|Dance]]" (2014) and "[[Cherry Pop]]" (2014), were successful in Japan. Stan's third studio album, ''[[Alesta (album)|Alesta]]'', was released in March 2016 and included a collaboration with [[Inna]] and [[Daddy Yankee]] on "[[We Wanna]]" (2015); the single went on to reach the top 60 in several countries. ''[[Mami (album)|Mami]]'', her fourth record, followed in 2018. Preceding the release of [[Mami (Alexandra Stan album)|its last single]] was Stan's feature on [[Manuel Riva]]'s "[[Miami (Manuel Riva song)|Miami]]" (2018), which peaked within the top ten in Romania and on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'s}} [[Dance Club Songs]] chart. Stan has received a number of awards and nominations, including the [[European Border Breakers Award]], a [[Japan Gold Disc Award]], an [[MTV Europe Music Awards|MTV Europe Music Award]] and two [[Romanian Music Awards]]. She is also referred to as one of the most successful Romanian artists alongside Inna.

She is brunette and... married|language=ro|newspaper=Click!|date=2 June 2014|access-date=28 August 2018}}</ref><ref name="intrebari">{{cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iol982JyAOE|title=40 de întrebări cu Denise Rifai (31.03.2021) - Alexandra Stan {{!}} Ediție completă|trans-title=40 Questions with Denise Rifai (31.03.2021) – Alexandra Stan {{!}} Complete Episode|language=Romanian|date=31 March 2021|access-date=1 April 2021|publisher=[[Kanal D (Romania)|Kanal D]]|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> Stan's father had used to work as a cook on a ship and was thus absent for a large part of her childhood in the [[:ro:Faleză Nord (cartier în Constanța)|Faleză Nord]] neighbourhood, which she stated had a large [[Father complex|emotional impact]] on her. In 2005, her family was confronted by financial problems due to the father's degrading health condition and moved to the [[Valu lui Traian]] commune, where Stan earned money as a waitress.<ref name="intrebari"/> She recalls having [[self-esteem]] issues and being bullied by classmates for being poor, for which she has eventually undergone [[post-traumatic stress disorder]] (PTSD) therapy.<ref name="intrebari"/><ref name="viva"/>

Showing an interest in music from a very young age, Stan studied at the Traian Secondary School and later at the Faculty of Management Andrei Șaguna, but dropped out of the latter in favour of pursuing a music career.<ref name="intrebari"/><ref name="showbiz" /> Her public singing debut was at a Romanian televised show when she was 15 years old.<ref>{{cite web|author=Scris de Edi |url=http://www.urban.ro/muzica/cum-arata-alexandra-stan-la-15-ani-video/ |title=Cum arata Alexandra Stan la 15 ani? |publisher=Urban.ro |date=4 July 2013 |access-date=12 February 2016 |language=ro |trans-title=How did Alexandra Stan look at the age of 15? |archive-date=7 July 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130707170050/http://www.urban.ro/muzica/cum-arata-alexandra-stan-la-15-ani-video/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> She eventually participated in various music contests, including the [[:ro:Festivalul național de muzică ușoară de la Mamaia|Mamaia Music Festival]] in 2009.<ref name="showbiz" /><ref name="debut"/> Romanian producers and songwriters Marcel Prodan and Andrei Nemirschi discovered Stan that year at a karaoke bar in Constanța and signed her to their record label, Maan Records.<ref name="debut">{{cite web|url=http://www.urban.ro/muzica/o-noua-voce-alexandra-stan/|title=O noua voce: Alexandra Stan|publisher=Urban.ro|language=ro|access-date=16 October 2015|date=25 November 2009|trans-title=A new singer: Alexandra Stan|archive-date=17 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151017144619/http://www.urban.ro/muzica/o-noua-voce-alexandra-stan/}}</ref> Retrospectively, in a 2021 interview, Stan claimed that the contract disrespected human rights and was written in a way so that Prodan and Nemirschi could have a widespread control over her. According to Stan, her reproaching this matter and the alleged underpayment she was facing to them resulted in several tensions.<ref name="intrebari"/> Stan rose to moderate fame and began being booked for concerts in Romania with the release of her debut single "[[Lollipop (Param Pam Pam)]]" (2009), which was given notable radio airplay.<ref name="showbiz" /><ref name="intrebari"/> Stan's father was involved in her management for two years early in her career.<ref name="intrebari"/>
