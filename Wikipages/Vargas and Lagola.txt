'''Vargas & Lagola''' are a Swedish [[songwriter|songwriting]] and [[record producer|production]] duo of [[Vincent Pontare]] and [[Salem Al Fakir]].

They have worked with many artists, including [[Avicii]], [[Axwell & Ingrosso]], [[David Guetta]], [[Galantis]], [[Madonna]] and [[Seinabo Sey]]. In addition to their songwriting and production work, they release alternative pop music.<ref name="The Line of Best Fit">{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/vargas-lagola-rolling-stone|title= Production Duo Vargas & Lagola launch themselves with party-house banger "Rolling Stone"|date=27 January 2017| via=www.thelineofbestfit.com}}</ref>

==Career==

[[Vincent Pontare]] and [[Salem Al Fakir]] have gone from successful solo artists to [[Grammis|Swedish Grammy Award]] winning songwriters and producers.<ref name="Grammis">{{cite web|url=http://grammis.se/om-grammis/grammisvinnarna-genom-aren/ |title= Grammisvinnarna genom åren|language=sv|via=www.grammis.se}}</ref>
Among the first co-writes together are [[Avicii]]’s 2013 [[Billboard Hot 100]] single "[[Hey Brother]]" and "[[Younger (Seinabo Sey song)|Younger]]" by [[Seinabo Sey]].<ref name="Avicii Billboard">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Avicii/chart-history/hot-100/song/797399|title=Avicii Chart History|via=www.billboard.com}}</ref>

At the 2014 [[Grammis|Swedish Grammy Awards]] Vargas & Lagola and [[Magnus Lidehäll]] were awarded Composer of the Year after writing for [[Veronica Maggio]]’s album ''[[Handen i fickan fast jag bryr mig]]'' and the [[Petter (rapper)|Petter]] album ''Början på allt'', and work with artists like [[Galantis]].<ref name="UMPG Scandinavia">{{cite web|url=http://www.umusicpub.com/se/News/2014/February/Grammis-2014.aspx|title= Grammis 2014|date=20 February 2014|via=www.umusicpub.com/se}}</ref>  In 2014 they wrote numerous songs on [[Mapei (musician)|Mapei]]’s album ''Hey Hey'' and [[Avicii]]’s "[[The Days (song)|The Days]]" and "[[Divine Sorrow]]" (with [[Wyclef Jean]]).<ref name="Mynewsdesk">{{cite web|url=http://www.mynewsdesk.com/dk/warner-music-denmark/pressreleases/wyclef-jean-and-avicii-team-up-for-divine-sorrow-1086405 |title=Wyclef Jean and Avicii team up for "Divine Sorrow"|date=19 November 2014|via=www.mynewsdesk.com/dk}}</ref>
Vargas & Lagola followed up with another successful year in 2015 co-writing most songs on Seinabo Sey’s highly acclaimed debut album ''[[Pretend (album)|Pretend]]'' and songs for [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]]’s ''[[Rebel Heart]]'' album.<ref name="Universal Music Publishing Scandinavia">{{cite web|url=http://www.umusicpub.com/se/News/2015/Oct/Seinabo-Sey-Pretend.aspx|title=Seinabo Sey releases debut album 'Pretend'|date=23 October 2015|via=www.umusicpub.com/se}}</ref><ref name="UMP Scand">{{cite web|url=http://www.umusicpub.com/se/News/2015/Mar/Madonna-releases-new-album-Rebel-Heart.aspx|title=Madonna releases new album 'Rebel Heart'|date=9 March 2015|via=www.umusicpub.com/se}}</ref>

Vargas & Lagola have co-written some of the biggest hits by [[Axwell and Ingrosso|Axwell Ʌ Ingrosso]] – "[[More Than You Know (Axwell and Ingrosso song)|More Than You Know]]", "[[Sun Is Shining (Axwell and Ingrosso song)|Sun Is Shining]]", "Dreamer" and more - taken from their 2017 album ''[[More Than You Know (Axwell & Ingrosso album)|More Than You Know]]''.<ref name="Discogs">{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Axwell-Ingrosso-More-Than-You-Know/release/10612112|title=Axwell Λ Ingrosso – More Than You Know|via=www.discogs.com}}</ref>

The duo have also contributed to two songs on Swedish rock band [[Ghost (Swedish band)|Ghost]]'s [[Billboard 200]]-charting and [[Grammy Award]]-nominated album ''[[Prequelle]]'', including the album's second single "[[Dance Macabre (song)|Dance Macabre]]" which topped [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'s [[Mainstream Rock (chart)|Mainstream Rock Chart]].<ref name="Ghost Billboard">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8460189/kanye-west-eighth-no-1-album-billboard-200-ye|title= Kanye West Earns Eight no. 1 album on Billboard 200 Chart with 'Ye' |date=6 October 2018|via=www.billboard.com}}</ref><ref name="Billboard">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8498544/ghost-mainstream-rock-songs-number-1-dance-macabre|title=Ghost Waltzes to Third Mainstream Rock Songs No. 1 With 'Dance Macabre'|date=14 February 2019|via=www.billboard.com}}</ref><ref name="Grammy">{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/ghost |title= GRAMMY Award Results for Ghost|via=www.grammy.com}}</ref>

In 2018, the same moment as Vargas & Lagola blazed onto the alternative pop scene with the hit single "Roads", they shared the no.1 spot as Sweden's most streamed songwriters for songs including [[Avicii]] – "[[Without You (Avicii song)|Without You]] (feat. [[Sandro Cavazza]])" and "[[Waiting for Love (Avicii song)|Waiting For Love]]".<ref name="Musikförläggarna">{{cite web|url=http://musikforlaggarna.se/nyhet/vargas-lagola-de-mest-populara-latskrivarna-2017/|title= Vargas & Lagola är de- mest populära låtskrivarna 2017|date=23 February 2018|via=www.musikforlaggarna.se}}</ref> 
After working closely with Avicii for several years, Al Fakir and Pontare played a key role in finishing up the 2019 posthumous [[Avicii]] album ''[[Tim (Avicii album)|TIM]]'' and are featured artists on three of the album songs.<ref name="The Guardian">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/jun/06/i-had-to-honour-him-the-friends-who-finished-avicii-final-album |title='I had to honour him': the friends who finished Avicii's final album|date=6 June 2019|via=www.theguardian.com}}</ref> On 5 December 2019, Vargas & Lagola performed at Avicii’s Tribute Concert in Stockholm.<ref name="Avicii Tribute Concert">{{cite web|url=https://www.aviciitributeconcert.com/ |title= Avicii Tribute Concert|via=www.aviciitributeconcert.com}}</ref>

Vargas & Lagola's debut album ''The Butterfly Effect'' was released in January 2020.<ref name="Scandipop">{{cite web|url=https://www.scandipop.co.uk/album-vargas-lagola-butterfly-effect/ |title= ALBUM: Vargas & Lagola - 'The Butterfly Effect'|via=www.scandipop.co.uk}}</ref>
