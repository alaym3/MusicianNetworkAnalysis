'''Vivian Girls''' is an American band based in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. The only consistent members have been [[Cassie Ramone]] and [[La Sera|Katy Goodman]], on guitar and bass, respectively, whereas the group had several drummers throughout its history. They took their name from a book by outsider artist [[Henry Darger]].<ref name="The Agit Reader • Feature: Vivian Girls">{{cite web|url=http://agitreader.com/features/vivian_girls-8.18.html |title=The Agit Reader • Feature: Vivian Girls |publisher=Agitreader.com |access-date=2012-02-23}}</ref><ref name ="Pitchfork - Vivian Girls Unveil Album Reissue Details, Extend Tour">[http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/142872-vivian-girls-unveil-album-reissue-details-extend-tour ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080821020009/http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/142872-vivian-girls-unveil-album-reissue-details-extend-tour |date=August 21, 2008 }}</ref>

== History ==
=== 2007–2014 ===
Vivian Girls, named after [[Henry Darger]]'s [[Masterpiece|magnum opus]] (full title: ''The Story of the Vivian Girls, in What is Known as the Realms of the Unreal, of the Glandeco-Angelinian War Storm, Caused by the Child Slave Rebellion''), started in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|NY]] in March 2007, as the trio of [[Cassie Ramone]] (guitar/vocals), Katy "Kickball Katy" Goodman<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2010/02/01/sweaty-hands-brickbreaker/ |title=Sweaty Hands: Brickbreaker « The FADER |publisher=Thefader.com |date=2010-02-01 |access-date=2012-02-23}}</ref> (bass/drums/vocals), and [[Frankie Rose]] (drums/bass/vocals).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.myspace.com/viviangirlsnyc |title=Vivian Girls &#124; Gratis muziek, tourneedata, foto's, video's |publisher=Myspace.com |access-date=2012-02-23}}</ref> While only a band for a short while, they recorded a demo, which included five original songs and a [[Wipers]] cover, and began to play locally in [[Brooklyn]] and Katy and Cassie's home state of [[New Jersey]].<ref name="The Agit Reader • Feature: Vivian Girls" /> Within the first couple of months, they had already developed a strong local following, supporting acts like [[Jay Reatard]], [[Sonic Youth]], and [[the King Khan & BBQ Show]], as well as finding kindred spirits in other locals like [[Cause Co-Motion!]], [[Crystal Stilts]], and [[Woods (band)|Woods]], with whom they shared many bills and helped develop a local scene. The band is still very much connected to their home scene.<ref>Hughes, Josiah. [https://archive.today/20120713181034/http://exclaim.ca/articles/multiarticlesub.aspx?csid1=136&csid2=4&fid1=40686 "The Rise and Rise of Vivian Girls"], ''[[Exclaim!]]'', September 2009.</ref>

In March 2008, they released the "Wild Eyes" single on the Plays With Dolls label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://smashintransistors.blogspot.com/2008/06/vivian-girls-wild-eyes-and-tell-world.html |title=SMASHIN' TRANSISTORS: VIVIAN GIRLS "Wild Eyes" and "Tell the World" 7inches |publisher=Smashintransistors.blogspot.com |date=2008-06-27 |access-date=2012-02-23}}</ref> With very little promotion and distribution, the single became an underground indie hit, as it charted on many college radio playlists and garnered positive reviews on the internet.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://gorillavsbear.net/2008/06/vivian-girls.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120724122701/http://gorillavsbear.net/2008/06/vivian-girls.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-07-24 |title=Vivian Girls |publisher=Gorilla Vs. Bear |date=2008-06-03 |access-date=2012-02-23 }}</ref>
Recorded in the same session, their debut self-titled LP was released by the Mauled by Tigers label, whose initial pressing of the LP sold out in ten days.<ref name ="Pitchfork - Vivian Girls Unveil Album Reissue Details, Extend Tour" /> During this time, the group signed with [[In The Red Records|In the Red Records]], who released a new single in August 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gorillavsbear.blogspot.com/2008/07/new-vivian-girls-i-cant-stay.html |title=gorillavsbear.net: new vivian girls :: "i can't stay" |publisher=Gorillavsbear.blogspot.com |date=2008-07-14 |access-date=2012-02-23}}</ref> In the Red re-released ''Vivian Girls'' on LP and CD formats in October 2008.<ref>[http://tinymixtapes.com/Vivian-Girls ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080819032322/http://tinymixtapes.com/Vivian-Girls |date=August 19, 2008 }}</ref>

In July 2008, the band underwent a lineup change, with new drummer Ali Koehler replacing Frankie Rose.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/photos/vivian-girls--dan-friel-silent- |title=Vivian Girls at Silent Barn + Dan Friel at Dead Herring &#124; Scene and Heard Photos |publisher=Impose Magazine |date=2008-08-07 |access-date=2012-02-23}}</ref> The band then went on an extensive tour that saw them visit the U.S., UK, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, and Puerto Rico in two months.<ref>{{cite web|last=May |first=Hana |url=http://www.heartymagazine.com/features/vivian-girls-cool-dude-attitude |title=Vivian Girls: Cool Dude Attitude |publisher=hearty magazine |date=2009-05-04 |access-date=2012-02-23}}</ref>

Their second album, ''[[Everything Goes Wrong (album)|Everything Goes Wrong]]'', was released by In the Red in September 2009.
<ref>Amy Granzin, [http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/record_review/146145-vivian-girls-vivian-girls Vivian Girls: Vivian Girls] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081108072224/http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/record_review/146145-vivian-girls-vivian-girls |date=2008-11-08 }}, ''[[Pitchfork Media]]'', October 3, 2008. Retrieved November 10, 2008.</ref> In May 2010, Fiona Campbell of Coasting replaced Ali Koehler, becoming Vivian Girls' third drummer. With her, they toured their third album, ''Share the Joy'', to Europe, UK, Thailand, Japan, Hong Kong, and the US. Campbell is from New Zealand and previously played there in a band called The Coolies.

The band also featured on the track "Worse to Come" on noise pop band [[Male Bonding]]'s debut album ''[[Nothing Hurts]]''.

Vivian Girls' third album, ''[[Share the Joy]]'', was released in spring 2011 by [[Polyvinyl Record Co.]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/40082-vivian-girls-sign-to-polyvinyl/ |title=Vivian Girls Sign to Polyvinyl &#124; News |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2010-09-16 |access-date=2012-02-23}}</ref>

In January 2014, the band announced they were breaking up, with two last shows at [[The Church on York]] in [[Los Angeles]], and [[Death by Audio]] in [[Brooklyn]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/53676-vivian-girls-break-up/ |title=Vivian Girls Break Up|publisher=Pitchfork |date=2014-01-22 |access-date=2014-01-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2014/01/vivian-girls-breakup-farewell-shows/|title=Vivian Girls Announce Breakup and Farewell Shows|work=[[Spin (magazine)|SPIN]]|date=2014-01-22|access-date=November 25, 2014}}</ref> They reunited briefly in October 2015 to play two songs at bassist Katy Goodman's wedding.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.facebook.com/story.php?story_fbid=939325949460948&substory_index=0&id=101114383282113 |title=Photo |publisher=Facebook |access-date=2015-12-12}}</ref>

=== 2019–present ===
On July 17, 2019, Cassie Ramone, Katy Goodman, and Ali Koehler announced the band's reformation, alongside a new full-length album, ''Memory'', to be released in September.<ref name=RollingStone-Comeback-2019>{{cite news|last1=Exposito|first1=Suzy|title=The Vivian Girls Come Out Swinging|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/vivian-girls-in-the-studio-reunion-album-memory-860720/|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=23 July 2019}}</ref> It was recorded in 2018 with producer Rob Barbato.<ref name=Steregum-Reunion-2019>{{cite news|last1=Rettig|first1=James|title=Vivian Girls Announce Reunion Album Memory: Hear "Sick"|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2051300/vivian-girls-reunion-album-sick/music/|work=[[Stereogum]]|date=17 July 2019}}</ref> On August 14, 2019, a music video for the song "Something to Do", directed by Jason Lester, was released.<ref name=Something-To-Do-Video-2019>{{cite news|last1=Exposito|first1=Suzy|title=See Vivian Girls Run Wild in New "Something to Do" Video|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/vivian-girls-something-to-do-video-871392/|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=14 August 2019}}</ref> On September 10, 2019, a music video for the song "Sludge", directed by Alex Ross Perry, was released.

=== Side projects ===
In 2010, Katy Goodman formed All Saints Day, a side project with [[Cat Power]]'s Gregg Foreman; the band has digitally released one self-titled EP. Her solo project, [[La Sera]], also formed in 2010, has released four albums. In 2013, she collaborated with Springtime Carnivore's [[Greta Morgan]] as Books of Love, recording the science fiction-themed love song "Space Time". Goodman and Morgan collaborated again in 2016, releasing the punk covers album ''Take It, It's Yours''. 

Cassie Ramone has a side project, [[The Babies]], formed in 2009, with [[Woods (band)|Woods]] bassist [[Kevin Morby]]. The Babies released two studio albums in 2011 and 2012. During The Babies' hiatus, Ramone embarked on a solo career, releasing her debut album, ''The Time Has Come'', in 2014. She released an album of Christmas music, ''Christmas in Reno'', in 2015.

Fiona Campbell also plays in Coasting and Chain & The Gang, and is co-owner of the M'Lady's Records label.

Ali Koehler was the touring drummer for [[Best Coast]] from 2010 to 2011. In 2013, she formed the band [[Upset (band)|Upset]], which has released three albums.

Frankie Rose was formerly the drummer for [[Crystal Stilts]] and [[Dum Dum Girls]], and was an original member of the band [[Beverly (band)|Beverly]]. She released her first self-titled solo album as Frankie Rose and the Outs in 2010; under her own name, she has released four albums.
