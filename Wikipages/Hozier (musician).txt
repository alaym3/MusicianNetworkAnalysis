'''Andrew John Hozier-Byrne''' (born 17 March 1990), known [[Mononymous person|mononymously]] as '''Hozier''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|h|oʊ|z|i|ɚ}} {{respell|HOH|zee|ər}}),<ref>{{cite news|url=http://1079ishot.com/how-do-you-pronounce-hozier/|title=How Do You Pronounce Hozier?|publisher=HOT 107.9|access-date=30 September 2018|date=28 January 2015}}</ref> is an Irish musician, singer, and songwriter. His music primarily draws from [[Folk music|folk]], [[Soul music|soul]], and [[blues]], often using religious and literary themes. He had his international breakthrough after releasing his debut single "[[Take Me to Church]]", which has been certified multi-platinum in several countries.

Born and raised in [[County Wicklow]], Hozier released his debut EP in 2013, featuring "Take Me to Church", which became a rock radio hit in the U.S. and peaked at number two on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His [[Hozier (album)|debut studio album]] was released in March 2014 to critical acclaim. It has been certified 6× platinum in Ireland and multi-platinum in several countries. In September 2018, he released an EP titled ''Nina Cried Power'' and featured the title track as a single, reaching number one on the [[Adult Alternative Songs|''Billboard'' Adult Alternative Songs]] chart. He released his second album ''[[Wasteland, Baby!]]'' in March 2019, which debuted atop the [[Irish Albums Chart]] and the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], and has since been certified gold in the U.S.<ref name="riaa.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/|title=Gold & Platinum|website=Riaa.com|access-date=31 May 2021}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Andrew John Hozier-Byrne was born on 17 March 1990 in [[Bray, County Wicklow]];<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6059764/hozier/biography| title=Hozier – Biography |work=Billboard|access-date=3 June 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/meet-the-bray-singer-cracking-america-30089463.html | title=Meet the Bray singer cracking America |work=Irish Independent|date=13 March 2014|access-date=3 June 2014}}</ref> the son of Raine Hozier-Byrne, an artist, and John Byrne, a local [[blues]] drummer who had a day job working at a bank.<ref name="propelling">Smyth, David (6 June 2014). "[https://www.standard.co.uk/go/london/music/religious-abuse-homophobia-and-singing-corpses-are-the-unlikely-ingredients-propelling-hozier-to-9499292.html Religious abuse, homophobia and singing corpses are the unlikely ingredients propelling Hozier to rock stardom]". ''Go London''.</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/hozier-if-i-wanted-to-make-a-f-king-pop-song-i-would-1.3794161?mode=amp|title=Hozier: 'If I wanted to make a f**king pop song, I would'|last=Bruton|first=Louise|date=23 February 2019|website=The Irish Times|access-date=2019-03-27}}</ref> He grew up near [[Delgany|Delgany, County Wicklow]].<ref name="rollingstone_60949" /><ref name="propelling" /> As a child, Hozier self-identified as a "class clown" and an "unfocused student", calling school a "monotonous nightmare".<ref name="youtube_qtn-gAQX2z4">{{Cite web | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qtn-gAQX2z4&t=125s/ | title=Hozier shares thoughts on his Quaker upbringing | date=4 February 2019 |website=Youtube}}</ref> He once worked a job scrubbing toilets at a golf course, which he described as a "good experience of hard work".<ref name="gweb" /> He began writing songs at the age of 15.<ref name="adored">Byrne, Niall (23 March 2014). He was refused a year's deferral by the college after missing exams to record demos for a music label.<ref name="searing" /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://issuu.com/osnovina/docs/issuu_9-1|title=Go Rail 9-1: Featuring Hozier |last=Mullally |first=Una |date=14 October 2013<!--|website=issuu.com-->|website=The Irish Times}}</ref>

==Career==
===2008–2012: Beginnings===
While at Trinity, Hozier became involved with the Trinity Orchestra. He was a member of the choral ensemble [[Anúna]] from 2007 to 2012 and appears as a soloist on their 2014 release ''Illuminations'' singing "[[Courir de Mardi Gras#La Chanson de Mardi Gras|La Chanson de Mardi Gras]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.anuna.ie/timeline|title=Our Story|website=ANÚNA|access-date=2 March 2019}}</ref> He toured and sang with the group internationally including performances in Norway and the Netherlands.<ref>{{cite web|author=Anna Job |url=http://www.goldenplec.com/hozier-interview/ |title=Hozier &#124; Interview |website=Goldenplec.com |date=1 August 2013|access-date=3 June 2014}}</ref> Hozier played at [[Oxegen|Oxegen Festival]] in [[Oxegen 2009|2009]]<ref>"The Line-up". ''Irish Times'' (Dublin). 10 July 2009.</ref> and [[Oxegen 2010|2010]].{{cn|date=August 2021}} In 2011, he opened up for a performance for [[Alex Winston]] in [[Dublin]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Fischer |first1=Reed |title=Six Videos of Hozier Before He Was Famous |url=http://www.citypages.com/music/six-videos-of-hozier-before-he-was-famous-6646529 |access-date=2 November 2020 |publisher=Citypages |date=19 February 2015}}</ref> In 2012, Hozier appeared as a backup singer for [[Billy Ocean]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Billy Ocean – When The Going Get's Tough |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CuK0LC2sI4M&feature=youtu.be | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211103/CuK0LC2sI4M| archive-date=2021-11-03 | url-status=live|website=YouTube |access-date=2 November 2020}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

===2013–2017: Breakthrough===
[[File:Hozier at the Troubadour in West Hollywood (14238011522).jpg|thumb|Hozier performing at [[Troubadour (West Hollywood, California)|The Troubadour]] in 2014]]
Hozier wrote the song "[[Take Me to Church]]" in 2013 in between playing [[open mic]] nights in [[Dublin]]; the rough demo resulted in him signing with indie label [[Rubyworks Records]].<ref name="rollingstone_60949">{{cite magazine| author=Andy Greene| date =29 January 2015| title =Behind Hozier's Unlikely Rise| magazine =[[Rolling Stone]] | location =New York City | issn =0035-791X | url =https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/behind-hoziers-unlikely-rise-20150129| access-date =2015-02-08}}</ref> Hozier released his debut [[extended play]], also called ''Take Me to Church'', on 3 July 2013.<ref>{{cite web |title=Take Me To Church - EP Hozier |url=https://genius.com/albums/Hozier/Take-me-to-church-ep |website=Genius |access-date=8 January 2021}}</ref> It appeared on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite magazine |title=Hozier |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/hozier/chart-history/tlp/ |magazine=Billboard |access-date=7 January 2021}}</ref> He initially recorded track demos in his attic studio before working on the record with producer [[Rob Kirwan]].<ref>{{cite web |title=An interview with Hozier |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/blogs/poplife/2013/10/14/an-interview-with-hozier/ |website=The Irish Times |access-date=8 January 2021}}</ref> The titular single was released in September 2013.<ref name="rollingstone_60949"/> The music video, alluding to themes of [[homophobia]], was released that same month, having been created on a "shoestring budget" and filmed entirely in black-and-white.<ref name="rollingstone_60949" /> The video was subsequently shared by gay English actor [[Stephen Fry]], which helped it reach the front page of [[Reddit]] and subsequently go [[viral video|viral]].<ref name= propelling/><ref name="searing">Mullally, Una (12 October 2013). "Hozier's soaring voice and searing heart: From quiet beginnings, the 'Take Me to Church' singer's music, and its political nous, are growing in depth and stature". ''Irish Times'' (Dublin).</ref><ref>Flanagan, Andrew (7 October 2013). "[http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/5748114/birth-of-a-buzz-behind-the-scenes-as-hozier-goes-viral Birth of a Buzz: Behind the Scenes as Hozier Goes Viral]". ''Billboard''. Retrieved 3 June 2014.</ref> "Take Me to Church" saw Hozier's rise to prominence, with the song scoring top five positions around the world and gaining multi-platinum certifications; the song also garnered critical acclaim for its lyricism and messaging.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G0tZZZmRir8| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211103/G0tZZZmRir8| archive-date=2021-11-03 | url-status=live|title=Hozier thinks all musicians are egomaniacs|date=21 June 2019|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref name="rollingstone_60949" /> The EP's concluding track, "Cherry Wine", appeared in [[Zach Braff]]'s movie ''[[Wish I Was Here]]'', chosen for its "heartbreaking lyrics and poetry".<ref>Ward, James (12 July 2014). "Hozier hits right note in Zach Braff's new movie". ''The Daily Mirror'' (London). p. 27.</ref> It was later performed on the [[The Late Late Show (Irish talk show)|''Late Late Show'']].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0pYY7rHvvDo| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211103/0pYY7rHvvDo| archive-date=2021-11-03 | url-status=live|title=Hozier – Cherry Wine – The Late Late Show – RTÉ One|last=The Late Late Show|date=11 March 2016|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/music-news/watch-hozier-performs-cherry-wine-on-the-late-late-show-with-james-corden-34604716.html|title=WATCH: Hozier performs Cherry Wine on The Late Late Show with James Corden |website=Independent.ie}}</ref> In 2014, Hozier released his second EP, ''From Eden''.

Hozier released his eponymous album, ''[[Hozier (album)|Hozier]]'', on 19 September 2014, including tracks from his first two extended-plays. The album drew inspiration from folk, R&B, and blues music.<ref name="billboard">{{cite magazine |last1=Brown |first1=Harley |title=Album Review: Hozier, 'Hozier' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/6281404/album-review-hozier-hozier |magazine=Billboard}}</ref> ''Hozier'' met critical success;<ref name="Metacritic">{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/hozier/hozier |title=Reviews for Hozier by Hozier |website=[[Metacritic]] |access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> Helen Brown of ''[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]'' noted that it was "an intense, youthful lyrical tangling of religion and romantic obsession that regularly finds him poised 'between love and abuse'".<ref name="The Telegraph">{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/cdreviews/11143317/Hozier-Hozier-review-glorious-and-uplifting.html |title=Hozier, Hozier, review: 'glorious and uplifting' |first=Helen |last=Brown |work=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]] |date=6 October 2014 |access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> ''Hozier'' peaked at number one in Ireland and finished second on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. The album is certified 2x platinum in the UK and US. After the release of "Take Me to Church", the record released five singles released from 2014 to 2016: "[[From Eden]]", "[[Sedated (song)|Sedated]]", "[[Work Song (Hozier song)|Work Song]]", [[Someone New (Hozier song)|Someone New]]", "Jackie and Wilson", and "[[Cherry Wine (Hozier song)|Cherry Wine]]", which all appeared on the [[Irish Singles Chart]]. The music videos for "From Eden" "Someone New" and "Cherry Wine" featured actresses [[Katie McGrath]], [[Natalie Dormer]] and [[Saoirse Ronan]], respectively.<ref>{{cite web |title=Hozier releases new video for 'From Eden' featuring Katie McGrath |url=https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/music-news/hozier-releases-new-video-for-from-eden-featuring-katie-mcgrath-30756852.html |website=Independent.ie |access-date=8 January 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/saoirse-ronan-backs-hozier-charity-single-in-domestic-violence-campaign-34444706.html|title=Saoirse Ronan backs Hozier charity single in domestic violence campaign|date=11 February 2016|work=The Independent|access-date=25 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190425084532/https://www.independent.ie/entertainment/music/saoirse-ronan-backs-hozier-charity-single-in-domestic-violence-campaign-34444706.html|archive-date=25 April 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> He embarked on an American and European tour to support the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pacificsandiego.com/things-to-do/music/pac-hozier-announces-new-fall-concert-dates-story.html|title=Hozier announces new fall concert dates|last=Varga|first=George|date=20 April 2015|publisher=Pacific San Diego|access-date=13 March 2019}}</ref>

"Take Me to Church" was later nominated at the [[57th Annual Grammy Awards]] for [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] in 2015. At the awards show, he performed the song with [[Annie Lennox]],<ref>{{cite news| url= http://www.evoke.ie/showbiz/hozier-annie-lennox-duet-grammy-awards/| website= Evoke.ie| title= Superstar duet! Guess who Hozier is performing with at the Grammy Awards?| date= 29 January 2015| access-date= 9 February 2015| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170630182306/http://evoke.ie/showbiz/hozier-annie-lennox-duet-grammy-awards| archive-date= 30 June 2017| url-status= dead}}</ref> and later at the [[2015 Billboard Music Awards|2015 ''Billboard'' Music Awards]]. On 12 November 2015, he won the [[VH1]] Artist of the Year, a fan-voted award. At the ceremony, he performed "Take Me to Church" and [[The Beatles]]' "Blackbird" with singer [[Tori Kelly]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/news/223025/hozier-tori-kelly-blackbird-you-oughta-know-big-music/|title=We Need More Hozier + Tori Kelly Collabs Like This Joint Performance Of "Blackbird" At VH1's Big Music In 2015 Concert|website=Vh1.com}}</ref> In June 2016, Hozier released the song "Better Love" as part of the ''[[The Legend of Tarzan (film)|Legend of Tarzan]]'' soundtrack.<ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.facebook.com/hoziermusic/videos/1017072688346065/ | title= Hozier announced the song "Better Love"|publisher=[[Facebook]]| date= 15 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Wm4CrOfbHMI | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211103/Wm4CrOfbHMI| archive-date=2021-11-03 | url-status=live| title= Hozier – Better Love (From The Legend of Tarzan – Single Version)|publisher=[[YouTube]] | date= 16 June 2016}}{{cbignore}}</ref> Hozier subsequently took a one-year hiatus from his work, moving back to [[Ireland]] to "reconnect" after touring his debut album.<ref name="idolator">{{cite web |title=Interview: Hozier Talks 'Wasteland, Baby!' & New Collaborators |url=https://www.idolator.com/7738602/hozier-interview-wasteland-baby?chrome=1 |website=Idolator |access-date=6 January 2021}}</ref>

=== 2018–present: ''Nina Cried Power'' EP and ''Wasteland, Baby!'' ===
[[File:Hozier Nobody 1.png|thumb|left|Hozier performing at the [[Glasgow Royal Concert Hall]] in 2019]]

In September 2018, Hozier returned with the release of the extended-play ''[[Nina Cried Power]]''. The record features a collaboration with [[Mavis Staples]] on the titular track. He released his second studio album's lead single, "Movement", on 14 November 2018, alongside a music video. Hozier's second album, ''[[Wasteland, Baby!]]'', was released on 1 March 2019, including tracks from his previous EP.<ref>{{cite news |last=Skinner |first=Tom |title=Hozier has announced 'Wasteland, Baby' – his first album in more than four years |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/hozier-new-album-wasteland-baby-single2431741-2431741 |access-date=10 February 2019 |work=NME|date=16 January 2019}}</ref> The thematic elements of the album center around his interpretation of the [[apocalypse]] while looking for thematic elements of romance and redemption.<ref name="youtube_qtn-gAQX2z4"/> Reviews were largely positive;<ref>{{cite web |title=WASTELAND, BABY! |url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/wasteland-baby!/hozier |website=Metacritic.com}}</ref> Elisabeth Woronzoff of ''[[PopMatters]]'' stated it "light[s] the artist's skill and vision of his craft... [and] that it [..] delivers while edifying the artist as an impactful voice in the art and activism sphere."<ref name="PopMatters">{{cite web|url=https://www.popmatters.com/hozier-wasteland-baby-review-2630193793.html?rebelltitem=3#rebelltitem3 |title='Wasteland, Baby!' As a result of his countryside upbringing, many of Hozier's early music exposure came from his parents' blues, jazz, and soul record collections, incorporating artists like [[John Lee Hooker]], [[Muddy Waters]], [[Bukka White]], and [[Tom Waits]].<ref name="rollingstone_60949" /><ref name="guardian1">{{cite news |title=Hozier: The best vocalists I can think of are female |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/jul/31/hozier-best-vocalists-female-take-me-to-church |access-date=8 January 2021 |work=The Guardian}}</ref> His first musical memories were drawn from his father's career as a drummer playing music in [[Dublin]].<ref name="MTV1">{{cite web |title=SEX, BOG BODIES AND NINA SIMONE: HOZIER REVEALS HIS SURPRISING INFLUENCES |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2029578/hozier-interview-irish-bog-people-sex/ |website=MTV |access-date=7 January 2021}}</ref> He has stated that his musical education was "grounded" in [[Chicago blues]] artists such as [[John Lee Hooker]], [[Otis Redding]], and [[Nina Simone]], to whom he pays tribute in the track "[[Nina Cried Power]]".<ref name="imag">{{cite web |last1=Llana |first1=Kaplan |title=Interview Magazine |url=https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/hozier#:~:text=BYRNE%3A%20Biggest%20musical%20influences%20would,Lee%20Hooker%20and%20Otis%20Redding. |website=In the Middle with Hozier |access-date=7 January 2021}}</ref> As a child, he was a fan of the bands [[Stereophonics]] and [[Daft Punk]].<ref name="MTV1" /> His draws inspiration primarily from [[Music of Ireland|Irish]] and [[African-American culture#Music|African-American]] artistry; he has said that the "roots" of jazz, rock, soul, and R&B have been largely shaped by black culture and finds importance in "crediting the legacy you're crediting".<ref name="gweb" /> His guitar work draws from [[Celtic folk]] inspiration, as well as musicians [[Ali Farka Toure]] and [[Tinariwen]].<ref name="gweb" /> Hozier has stated that "the best vocalists I can think of are female".<ref name="guardian1" /> Musically, Hozier has listed [[Aretha Franklin]], [[Johnny Cash]], [[Woody Guthrie]], [[Van Morrison]], [[Ella Fitzgerald]], [[St. Vincent (musician)|St Vincent]], [[Feist (singer)|Feist]], [[Little Green Cars]], [[Paul Simon]], [[Willie Dixon]], and [[Lisa Hannigan]] as musical and vocal influences.<ref>{{cite web |title=Hozier on Van Morrison: "His influence on me was significant" |url=https://www.hotpress.com/music/hozier-on-van-morrison-his-influence-on-me-was-significant-22827860 |website=Hot Press |access-date=7 January 2021}}</ref><ref name="guardian1" /><ref name="MTV1" /><ref name="songwriter">{{cite web |title=Writer of the Week: Hozier |url=https://americansongwriter.com/writer-week-hozier/ |website=American Songwriter |access-date=8 January 2021}}</ref>

===Songwriting===
Hozier states that his writing differs based on the starting point; varying from a [[couplet]] and [[Lyrics|lyrical]] idea, or a musical hook that he "flesh[es] out from a fairly embryonic point".<ref name="gweb">{{cite news |title=Hozier on Seamus Heaney, Maltesers and why Nina Simone turns his brain inside out |url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/live/2019/feb/15/hozier-webchat-post-your-questions-now-take-me-to-church?page=with:block-5c6ab87de4b022557d2471aa#liveblog-navigation |access-date=7 January 2021 |work=The Guardian|date=18 February 2019}}</ref> Lyrics "by far take [him] the most time", describing a "slow process of repetition" while "not lean[ing] too much on verbose phrases" to maintain the integrity of the sentiment.<ref name="songwriter" /> His process is described as "slow, methodical work" and he is "meticulous" about wording; he has stated that he "can defend any idea by the time someone hears it, because [he has] put it through a strainer seven times".<ref name="guardian1" /> His lyrics often contain vivid literary references and draw imagery from nature and religion;<ref name="rollingstone_60949" /> they tend to focus on themes of romantic relationships, love, and politics.<ref name="guardian1" /><ref name="nylon33">{{cite web |title=HOZIER'S MOMENT HAS ARRIVED |url=https://www.nylon.com/articles/hozier-interview |website=Nylon |access-date=8 January 2021}}</ref> Hozier has dismissed comparisons of his work to poetry, stating that to consider it such "would be a disservice to poetry itself."<ref name="songwriter" /> The "subversion of social norms" plays a role in his music, which often discuss the defiance of [[organised religion]] and social convention.<ref name="gweb" /> Hozier references the Irish concept of the [[craic]], which he interprets as subverting social norms and self-respect, and has stated, "If the Irish are not taking the piss out of something, what's the point really?"<ref name="gweb" /> His songwriting has been influenced by Irish music and folklore, as well as poets [[Seamus Heaney]] and [[W. B. Yeats]]. He has said that his first record contained a "fairytale aspect" influenced by [[Oscar Wilde]].<ref name="forbes22">{{cite web |title=Q&A: Hozier on Tom Waits, Poetry, Mixing Pop And Politics |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/stevebaltin/2019/12/17/qa-hozier-on-tom-waits-poetry-mixing-pop-and-politics/?sh=60c1708d3d18 |website=Forbes |access-date=8 January 2021}}</ref>

Steve Baltin of ''[[Forbes]]'' has stated that briefly studying [[music theory]] in college has influenced Hozier's sound, and that he writes from a "socially conscious" perspective.<ref name="forbes22" /> Hozier has stated that he believes "the personal is the political"; much of his work holds direct references to topical events.<ref name="forbes22" /> The "[[Take Me to Church]]" music video features two men in a same-sex relationship, and highlights the injustices and violence perpetrated against members of the [[LGBT]] community. The video was inspired by videos of violent crimes against gay men in Russia.<ref name="phoenixnewtimes_10884076">{{Cite web|url=https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/music/hozier-ditches-church-gets-political-on-new-ep-10884076|title=Hozier Ditches 'Church,' Gets Political on New EP|last=Cabral|first=Angelica|date=2018-10-11|website=Phoenix New Times|access-date=2019-03-26}}</ref> The music video for the song "[[Cherry Wine (Hozier song)|Cherry Wine]]" was released to raise awareness of domestic violence.<ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SdSCCwtNEjA | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211103/SdSCCwtNEjA| archive-date=2021-11-03 | url-status=live| title= Hozier- Cherry Wine| date= 13 February 2016}}{{cbignore}}</ref> "[[Nina Cried Power]]" is a song that highlights artists such as [[Nina Simone]], [[Bob Dylan]], and [[Mavis Staples]] whose work takes a political or social justice stance.<ref name="phoenixnewtimes_10884076"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hozier.com/ninacriedpowercast/|title=Activists featured in the Nina Cried Power video|last=www.hozier.com|first=Hozier-|website=Hozier|language=en|access-date=2019-03-26}}</ref> The music video features Irish activists alongside protest footage.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irishexaminer.com/breakingnews/entertainment/hoziers-nina-cried-power-video-is-a-tribute-to-irish-activists-868801.html|title=Hozier's Nina Cried Power video is a tribute to Irish activists|date=2018-09-13|website=Irishexaminer.com|access-date=2019-03-26}}</ref> "Be" also contains many allusions to [[sea level rise]] and refugee crises, referencing President [[Donald Trump]] and ''[[The Apprentice (US)|The Apprentice]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/16612538|title=Genius lyrics – Hozier, Be – 'Are on TV givin' people the sack'|last=bluejaysjay|first=genuis lyrics|date=2019-03-01|website=Genius Lyrics|access-date=2019-10-14}}</ref> In November 2019, Hozier released a new song entitled "Jackboot Jump", following live performances of it on tour. This song, besides being a direct reference to George Orwell's ''1984'', alludes to social demonstrations in Hong Kong, Russia and in America.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/hozier-shared-new-live-song-jackboot-jump-listen-now-2577396|title=Listen to Hozier's politically charged new live song 'Jackboot Jump' {{!}} NME|date=2019-11-22|website=NME Music News, Reviews, Videos, Galleries, Tickets and Blogs {{!}} NME.COM|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-04-21}}</ref> In 2019, Hozier performed an unreleased song whilst on tour entitled "But the Wages" that refers to temperatures rising as well as riots all around the world, while wages remain the same.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=jPTrurPsBms| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211103/jPTrurPsBms| archive-date=2021-11-03 | url-status=live|title=But The Wages|last=Hozier|website=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

==Activism==
Hozier is part of Home Sweet Home, an organisation led by Irish celebrities including actress [[Saoirse Ronan]] and musician [[Glen Hansard]]. In 2016, the organisation illegally took over an office building in [[Dublin]] to house 31 homeless families.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.irishcentral.com/news/politics/celebs-hansen-hozier-ronan-lead-illegal-homeless-uprising-in-dublin|title=Celebs Hansard, Hozier, Ronan, lead "illegal" homeless uprising in Dublin|date=2016-12-19|website=IrishCentral.com|language=en|access-date=2019-03-05}}</ref> Hozier, who had a Protestant upbringing in the [[Quakers|Quaker]] faith but also attended a Catholic school, is an outspoken critic of the Catholic Church.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Funaro |first1=Vincent |title=Singer Hozier on Catholic Church: It's an Organization of Men, It's Not About Faith |url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/singer-hozier-on-catholic-church-its-an-organization-of-men-its-not-about-faith.html |website=The Christian Post |access-date=21 July 2019}}</ref> He showed support for the [[Abortion in the Republic of Ireland|Irish abortion referendum]], and stated he felt "pride" in his generation and the democratic process following the vote. On 5 June 2020, he announced that all royalties from his song "Jackboot Jump" would go to the [[NAACP]] and [[Black Lives Matter]] movement following the [[George Floyd protests]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Twitter|url=https://mobile.twitter.com/Hozier/status/1269078569696854016|access-date=2020-06-07|website=mobile.twitter.com}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hozier continues to reside in Ireland.<ref name=":0" /> He has the last words of Irish poet [[Seamus Heaney]] tattooed on his arm ("noli timere", Latin for "don't be afraid").<ref>{{cite web |title=Hozier reveals admiration for poet Seamus Heaney and why he has 'noli timere' tattooed on his arm |url=https://www.irishmirror.ie/showbiz/irish-showbiz/hozier-reveals-admiration-poet-seamus-14020316 |website=Irish Mirror |access-date=8 January 2021}}</ref> He identifies as an [[agnostic]].<ref name="rollingstone_60949"/> While he admitted in 2019 that he rarely attends church except for funerals and weddings, he admires the Quaker religion for its pacifism and its anti-war sentiment. He is especially interested in how people use religious institutions to claim "infallibility" in order to gain control and power over others.<ref name="youtube_qtn-gAQX2z4"/> He had stated that one positive lesson he learned from his religious upbringing was to "look for the spark of the divine in every individual" and treat everybody as if "you are looking into the mind and face of God".<ref name="youtube_qtn-gAQX2z4"/>
