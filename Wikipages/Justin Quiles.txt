'''Justin Rafael Quiles Rivera''' (born March 29, 1990), known professionally as '''Justin Quiles''' or '''J Quiles''', is an American [[reggaeton]] singer. Quiles has been nominated for [[Premios Juventud]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://elgenero.com/quiles-nominado-en-premios-juventud/|title=J Quiles nominado en los Premios Juventud|publisher=El Género|language=es|date=March 20, 2014}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{cite web|url=http://www.latintimes.com/justin-quiles-talks-hit-single-maria-and-his-celebrity-crush-jlo-exclusive-206066 |title=Justin Quiles Talks Hit Single ‘María’ And His Celebrity Crush J.Lo [EXCLUSIVE&#93; |website=Latintimes.com |date=2014-09-03 |access-date=2016-09-25}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://www.justinquiles.com/bio/ |title=BIOGRAPHY — Justin Quiles |website=Justinquiles.com |access-date=2016-09-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Jessica Lucia Roiz |url=http://www.pulsopop.com/justin-quiles-me-curare-interview/ |title=J Quiles: A Promising New Urban Artist With 'Me Curare' |website=Pulsopop.com |date=2015-08-27 |access-date=2016-09-25}}</ref>

Besides writing his own songs, Quiles has written for his colleagues, most notably "Pierde Los Modales", by [[J Balvin]] feat. [[Daddy Yankee]], "Take It Off", by [[Lil Jon]] feat. [[Yandel]] & [[Becky G]], "Recuérdame", by [[Maluma (singer)|Maluma]], "Loba" and "Mi Nena", by [[Yandel]]<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7469523/5-songs-you-didnt-know-written-justin-quiles|title=5 Songs You Didn't Know Were Written by Justin Quiles|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-07-31}}</ref> and "[[Bichota]]", by [[Karol G]]<ref>{{Citation|title=Bichota - Karol G {{!}} Credits {{!}} AllMusic|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/bichota-mw0003443214/credits|language=en-us|access-date=2020-12-10}}</ref> where he also did backing vocals.<ref>{{Citation|title=KAROL G – BICHOTA|url=https://genius.com/Karol-g-bichota-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2020-12-10}}</ref>

Since August 2013, Quiles has been signed to record label [[Rich Music]]. In February 2017, Rich Music and Quiles signed a distribution deal with Warner Music's Latin division.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7701319/rich-music-justin-quiles-warner-music-latina-record-deal|title=Rich Music & Justin Quiles Sign Record Deal With Warner Music Latina: Exclusive|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-07-31}}</ref>

== Early life and career beginnings ==
Quiles was born in Bridgeport, Connecticut, to a Puerto Rican family, and was raised in Puerto Rico but moved back to Connecticut to finish high school.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":0" /> He has stated in interviews that his father was abusive to his mother, which is why they had to move back to Puerto Rico<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://www.latina.com/entertainment/music/justin-quiles-maria-exclusive-interview|title=EXCLUSIVE: Justin Quiles Talks New Single "Maria" & Writing for Yandel's Album!|website=LATINA|access-date=2017-07-31}}</ref> He wrote his first song when he was 13<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=http://read.tidal.com/article/tidal-rising-justin-quiles|title=TIDAL Rising: Justin Quiles|website=read.tidal.com|access-date=2017-07-31}}</ref> and says he grew up influenced by the music his older brother listened to.,<ref name=":3" /> such as [[El General]], [[Salsa music|salsa]] and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]],<ref name=":2" /> as well as [[Wisin & Yandel|Wisin y Yandel]], [[Tego Calderón|Tego Calderon]] and [[Don Omar]].<ref name=":3" />

Quiles moved back to Puerto Rico to become a singer. He started collaborating with the duo Genio & Baby Johnny. A year later, he went back to [[Orlando, Florida|Orlando]], where he recorded his first single, "Algo Contigo" with producer Lelo.{{citation needed|date=August 2017}}

Quiles signed with the record label [[Rich Music]], based in [[Miami]], in August 2013. His first single with Rich Music was the song "Orgullo", and a remix featuring the Colombian singer [[J Balvin]], which became a Top 20 hit on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Latin Rhythm Airplay chart.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6388408/justin-quiles/chart?f=1225|title=Justin Quiles - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=www.billboard.com|language=en|access-date=2017-07-31}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2015: ''Carpe Diem'' and other singles ===
Quiles' first album, ''Carpe Diem'', was released in March 2015<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/carpe-diem/id1234615020|title=Carpe Diem by Justin Quiles on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=2017-07-31}}</ref> and featured the songs "No La Toques", "Nos Envidian". That same year, he also released the single "Me Curare", which gained traction with a remix featuring Colombiano singer [[Maluma (singer)|Maluma]] and peaked at number 7 on the Billboard Tropical Songs chart.<ref name=":5" />

=== 2016–2017: ''La Promesa'' and future ===
On July 29, 2016, Quiles released his second studio album, ''La Promesa'' (The Promise), which is how he is sometimes referred to.<ref name=":4" /> The album debuted on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] charts as the #1 Latin Rhythm Album and #2 Latin Album overall.<ref name=":5" /> The main hit song on that album is "Si Ella Quisiera", which also got a remix by [[Yandel]] & Gadiel. The song peaked at number 8 on the Billboard Tropical Songs chart and number 20 on the Billboard Latin Pop Songs chart<ref name=":5" /> and was certified [[Music recording sales certification|Platinum]] in [[Spain]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://elgenero.com/justin-quiles-recibe-disco-de-platino-en-espana/|title=Justin Quiles recibe disco de platino en España|date=2017-04-07|work=Elgenero.com|access-date=2017-07-31|language=es-ES}}</ref>

Prior to releasing "La Promesa", Quiles had released the mixtape Imperio Nazza: Justin Quiles Edition, produced by duo Musicólogo y Menes. Prior to that, he had been featured on their album "Orion", which produced his song, "Si El Mundo Se Acabara". That same year, he also released the EP "JQ Miliano",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/ella-baila/id1236106551?i=1236106846|title=JQMILIANO - EP by Justin Quiles on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=2017-07-31}}</ref> his [[alter ego]], which featured the songs "Culpable", "Ella Baila" (and a remix featuring Messiah).

In 2017, Quiles released the song "Crecia" ft. Bad Bunny & Almighty<ref>{{Citation|last=Justin Quiles|title=Justin Quiles - Crecia ft. Bad Bunny & Almighty [Official Video]|date=2017-01-27|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ysxfhUG5_VA|access-date=2017-07-31}}</ref> in the new genre of Latin [[Trap music (hip hop)|Trap]]. He also released the songs "Cuestion De Tiempo" ft. [[Jory Boy]] and "Tu Pollo" ft. Sech, and was featured along with [[Fuego (singer)|Fuego]] on "Baila Toma" by [[Osmani García|Osmani Garcia]] and "Todo Cambio", by [[Becky G]].{{citation needed|date=August 2017}} In July 2017, he opened [[Premios Juventud]] with a performance of the song "Egoista".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://elgenero.com/justin-quiles-abrira-por-primera-vez-la-alfombra-de-premios-juventud-con-su-tema-egoista/|title=Justin Quiles abrirá por primera vez la alfombra de Premios Juventud con su tema "Egoísta"|date=2017-07-06|work=Elgenero.com|access-date=2017-07-31|language=es-ES}}</ref> The song peaked at 18th on the Billboard Latin Pop Songs chart and 25th on the Billboard Latin Airplay chart.<ref name=":5" />
