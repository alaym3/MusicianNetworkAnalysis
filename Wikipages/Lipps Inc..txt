'''Lipps Inc.''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|l|ɪ|p|s|ɪ|ŋ|k}} {{respell|LIP|sink}}, like "[[lip sync]]") was an American [[disco]] and [[funk]] group from [[Minneapolis, Minnesota]]. The group was best known for the [[List of Hot 100 number-one singles of 1980 (U.S.)|chart-topping]] 1980 worldwide hit single "[[Funkytown]]" which hit No. 1 in 28 countries and was certified as double-[[music recording sales certification|platinum]] in sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cynthiajohnson.net/index.php/bio|title=Cynthia Johnson - Bio|website=Cynthiajohnson.net|access-date=16 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170916010929/http://cynthiajohnson.net/index.php/bio|archive-date=September 16, 2017}}</ref>

The group originally consisted of lead vocalist [[Cynthia Johnson]] and a changing lineup of [[session musician]]s including guitarist [[David Z (music producer)|David Rivkin]], guitarist Tom Riopelle and bassist Terry Grant. [[Steven Greenberg (musician)|Steven Greenberg]], the creator of the act, [[songwriter|wrote]] and [[record producer|produced]] most of the group's music.<ref name="funkytown.com">{{Cite web|url=http://www.funkytown.com/ |title=Archived copy|access-date=2019-01-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20051210213816/http://funkytown.com/|archive-date=2005-12-10 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

==History==
Lipps Inc. started as a project of [[Steven Greenberg (musician)|Steven Greenberg]], at the time a wedding DJ who wanted to try his hand at writing [[disco]] songs. He had intended to use the name "Lip Sync," but it was already in use by another group, so instead, he chose the [[homophone]] "Lipps, Inc." Greenberg was the sole member of the group until he met [[Cynthia Johnson]] while auditioning singers for his song "Rock It". Johnson subsequently joined the project and Lipps Inc. became a duo.<ref name="CurrentLipsinc">{{cite web|last1=Swensson|first1=Andrea|title=Steven Greenberg talks about creating Lipps, Inc. and writing 'Funkytown'|url=https://blog.thecurrent.org/2014/02/steven-greenberg-talks-about-creating-lipps-inc-and-writing-funkytown/|website=Minnesota Public Radio|access-date=18 November 2019}}</ref>

Lipps Inc.'s first release was a 1979 single, "Rock It", on Greenberg's own Flight imprint. The act released its debut album ''[[Mouth to Mouth (Lipps Inc. album)|Mouth to Mouth]]'' in late [[1979 in music|1979]].

The second single from this album, "[[Funkytown]]", spent four weeks at #1 on both the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and on the [[Hot Dance Music/Club Play]] charts in the United States. The 7" single sold over two million copies within a few months and was awarded a [[music recording sales certification|platinum record]] within the same year of its release.<ref name="Allmusic awards">[{{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p26653/charts-awards/billboard-singles|pure_url=yes}} Allmusic - Funkytown Charting]</ref> It reached #2 in the [[United Kingdom]]<ref>[http://www.everyhit.co.uk/ UK Charts] Search 'Funkytown'</ref> and was a hit throughout the world.

Lipps Inc.'s later singles failed to match the band's initial success (the only other Hot 100 entry was "Rock It" which peaked at #64). However, more dance hits were released throughout the 1980s including "[[How Long (Ace song)|How Long]]" in 1981 (originally a hit for [[Ace (band)|Ace]]) which reached #4 on the U.S. dance chart. After the third album ''[[Designer Music]]'', Johnson left the band and was replaced by [[Margie Cox]] and [http://www.melanierosales.com Melanie Rosales]. Lipps Inc. released its final album ''4'' in 1983 before disbanding two years later.

===Europe===
The 'face' of the group in the Netherlands was the British dancer Doris_D (Debbie Jenner), who playbacked the song in [https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/TopPop TopPop] with her dancers. Due to the success of this performance, they were also invited to perform as Lipps Inc. in other European countries, including Germany. Jenner and her dancers would have success as Doris D & The Pins in early 1981

==Awards and recognitions==
* Three Billboard Music Awards, 1980
* Platinum and Double-Platinum Status with Funkytown
* Inductee into Minnesota Music Hall of Fame
