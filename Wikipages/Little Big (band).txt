'''Little Big''' is a Russian [[rave music|rave]] band founded on 1st April 2013 in [[Saint Petersburg]].<ref name="cloud">[https://soundcloud.com/littlebigrussia Official page of the group Little Big] on [[SoundCloud]] "Little Big – Russian pop group, founded in 2013 in St. Petersburg"</ref> The band consists of [[Ilya Prusikin|Ilya "Ilich" Prusikin]], Sergey "Gokk" Makarov, Sonya Tayurskaya, and Anton "Boo" Lissov.<ref name="lis">[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qcRZUZ0yAuU LISSOV VLOG – Backstage LITTLE BIG April 2 STEREO HALL (MSCs)] '' «ANTHONY LISSOV (LISSOFF) Voice of JANE AIR, Clown from LITTLE BIG» ''</ref> Their first full-length album, ''With Russia from Love'', was released on 17 March 2014.<ref name="wbitw">{{cite web|url=https://weirdestbandintheworld.com/tag/russian-pop/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406215437/https://weirdestbandintheworld.com/tag/russian-pop/|url-status=dead|archive-date=2019-04-06|title=russian pop – The Weirdest Band in the World|website=Weirdestbandintheworld.com|access-date=15 March 2020|date=2014}}</ref> The group has released three albums and nine [[Single (music)|singles]].

== History ==
The band made its debut on 1 April [[2013 in music|2013]], releasing their first video "Every Day I'm Drinking".<ref name = "every">[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QrU1hZxSEXQ LITTLE BIG – Everyday I'm drinking]</ref> They made their first public appearance on 2 July 2013 at the club A2,<ref name="dogg">{{cite web|url=http://www.sobaka.ru/city/music/17254|title=Интервью с Little Big|website=Sobaka.ru|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref> opening for [[Die Antwoord]].<ref name="dog">{{cite web|url=http://www.sobaka.ru/city/music/33035|title=Die Antwoord vs Little Big|website=Sobaka.ru|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref><ref name="int" /><ref name="dogg" />

They have toured in [[Europe]], [[Russia]], and [[North America]].<ref name = "rc">[http://rockcult.ru/interview-ilya-prusikin-2016 Interview | Ilya Prusikin: «Little Big – a musical reflection of society»] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160727010557/http://rockcult.ru/interview-ilya-prusikin-2016 |date=2016-07-27 }} Rock Cult</ref><ref name="int" /><ref name="tour">{{Cite web |url=http://www.thelittlebig.com/tour/ |title=LITTLE BIG TOUR |access-date=2016-10-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160523145202/http://www.thelittlebig.com/tour/ |archive-date=2016-05-23 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="noi" /> "This music is really in demand. We have not invested a single penny, just shot video, and became known in Europe", [[Ilya Prusikin]] said in an interview with UTV.<ref name="UTV">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kGrwxdFkYk8|title=Настоящая Музыка – Интервью с группой Little Big|last=Настоящая Музыка на UTV|date=19 February 2016|access-date=2 December 2018|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref><ref>*[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=75oX6mszDMQ Little Big Interview] in Couvre Feu 2015 – Samedi 22 Aout by Radio Fest (a broadcast of Stefline radio).

On 19 December 2015, the band released their second album, ''Funeral Rave''. It was 8th on the Russian [[iTunes]] chart for the 52nd week of 2015.<ref>[https://lenta.ru/news/2015/12/29/elka/ Song Firs about autism hit the Russian charts iTunes] , [[Lenta.ru]]</ref>

On 21 May 2016, videos for the songs "Give Me Your Money" and "Big Dick" received a prize at the Berlin Music Video Awards 2016.<ref name="rc" /><ref>

"We just want to show people that they own their lives. Countries and governments are not as important as they think, a person can deal with what he wants", said frontman Ilya Prusikin on the purpose of the band in an interview with ''Noisey''.<ref name="noi">{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/i-found-the-russian-die-antwoord|title=I Found the Russian Die Antwoord|date=5 December 2013|website=Noisey.vice.com|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref>

The band has its own label "Little Big Family", which includes Little Big (2016–present), {{ill|The Hatters (band)|lt=The Hatters|ru|The Hatters}} (2016–present), [[Tatarka (musician)|Tatarka]] (2016–present), {{ill|Khleb|ru|Хлеб (группа)}} (2017–present), and {{ill|Lizer|ru|LIZER}} (2018–present).<ref>[https://vk.com/photo-150575394_456243311?rev=1 Текущий состав «Little Big Family»]</ref>

In April 2018 Olympia Ivleva decided to leave the band.<ref name = "olympia">{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BhCIJLBDbPV/|title=Olympiya_ on Instagram: "В этот прекрасный день я хочу поздравить команду #LittleBig с днём рождения и пожелать ей дальнейшего продвижения и успехов в следующих…"|website=Instagram|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref> Later that year she stated her "burnout after five years of doing the same job", combined with intention to start her own non-musical businesses (chocolate paste and lingerie), were the reasons behind her exit.<ref>{{cite web |title=Почему она УШЛА ИЗ LITTLE BIG? ОЛИМПИЯ|trans-title=Why did she left LITTLE BIG? OLYMPIA |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nQeUMiZ50PU&t=1675s |website=Nezyny Redaktor |publisher=YouTube |access-date=17 March 2020 |language=ru |date=June 21, 2018}}</ref>

Little Big achieved viral success with the release of the video for "[[Skibidi]]" on 6 October 2018. The video features a dance that became popular when others tried to imitate it as part of the "Skibidi Challenge".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailydot.com/unclick/skibidi-challenge/|title=The internet is going bonkers over the Macarena-like #skibidichallenge|date=15 October 2018|website=Dailydot.com|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref>

Little Big took the first prize in the Most Trashy category at the Berlin Music Video Awards 2018 for their music video <nowiki>''Lolly Bomb''</nowiki><ref>{{Cite web|last=Berlin Music Video Awards|date=16 April 2021|title=Winners of the Berlin Music Video Awards 2018|url=https://www.berlinmva.com/news/winners-2018/|url-status=live|website=berlinmva.com}}</ref> and their music video <nowiki>''Skibidi'' won the first place in the Best Concept category in the festival'</nowiki>s 2019 edition.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Berlin Music Video Awards|date=16 April 2021|title=Winners of the Berlin Music Video Awards 2019!|url=https://www.berlinmva.com/news/2019-winners/|url-status=live|website=berlinmva.com}}</ref>

In March 2020, Little Big were announced as [[Russia in the Eurovision Song Contest|Russia's entry]] for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2020]], where they would have performed "[[Uno (Little Big song)|Uno]]".<ref>{{cite web|title=Russia's Eurovision act Little Big drops epic video for their amazing entry song|url=https://metro.co.uk/2020/03/13/russias-eurovision-little-big-uno-video-12394385|publisher=[[Metro (British newspaper)|Metro]] |access-date=11 May 2020 |language=en}}</ref> The music video for the song, released at the same time, features Yuriy Muzychenko from The Hatters and Florida Chanturia from [[Leningrad (band)|Leningrad]], both of whom were to join the band on stage during the contest.<ref>{{cite web |trans-title=Little Big in expanded edition will perform at Eurovision 2020|title=Little Big выступит на "Евровидении-2020" в расширенном составе |url=https://iz.ru/986194/2020-03-12/little-big-vystupit-na-evrovidenii-2020-v-rasshirennom-sostave |publisher=[[Izvestia]] |access-date=17 March 2020 |language=ru}}</ref> However, on 18 March 2020, the event was cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].

In 2020, the band won the Most Trashy category with their video <nowiki>''Go Bananas''</nowiki> at the Berlin Music Video Awards. The same year, they also placed 3rd for the Best Music Video.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Berlin Music Video Awards|date=16 April 2021|title=Winners of the Berlin Music Video Awards 2020|url=https://www.berlinmva.com/news/winners-berlin-music-video/|url-status=live|website=berlinmva.com}}</ref>

In October 2020, Little Big's cover version of "[[Gonna Make You Sweat (Everybody Dance Now)]]" was used over both the trailer and the closing credits of ''[[Borat Subsequent Moviefilm]]''.<ref>https://www.instagram.com/p/CF4yUa7Dwf7/</ref><ref>https://www.gazeta.ru/culture/news/2020/10/05/n_15036103.shtml</ref>

On 1 March 2021 Little Big announced on Instagram that founding member Anna Kast had died.<ref>{{Cite web|author=Little Big|date=2021-03-01|title=Little Big history started almost 8 years ago with: Anya, Ilich and Olympia. (...)|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CL36MX7LKYH/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-01|website=Instagram}}</ref>

On 8 March 2021, Little Big released a new single, "Sex Machine," along with a video which referenced the video to "UNO" the year prior. However, they subsequently posted on Instagram, "We are not going to the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021|Eurovision-2021]]. We think that Russia has many talented and unique artists, each one of them deserves the chance to be seen out there."<ref>url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CMKmqJcLfX3</ref>

Little Big have been nominated for Best Art Director with their music video <nowiki>''</nowiki>S*ck My D*ck 2020<nowiki>''</nowiki> at the Berlin Music Video Awards 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Berlin Music Video Awards|date=16 April 2021|title=2021 Nominees - Set 3|url=https://www.berlinmva.com/news/nominees-set-3/|url-status=live|website=Berlinmva.com}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
[[File:Little Big YT Views in a Year by County.png|thumb|right|Less than a half of Little Big's 738 million yearly [[YouTube]] views were from Russia, as of March 13, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://charts.youtube.com/artist/%2Fm%2F010s05zb?date_end=2020-03-13T00%3A00%3A00Z|title=Music Charts and Insgiht – Little Big – Worldwide – 12 months|publisher=[[YouTube]] Charts|date=2020-03-13|access-date=2020-03-15}}</ref>]]
The team calls itself a satirical art collaboration,<ref name="dog" /> which relies on the music, visuals, and the show.<ref name="dog" /> Little Big mocks various [[national stereotype]]s<ref name="noi" /> about Russia.<ref name="int" /> All the videos are filmed by co-founder Alina Pasok.<ref name="dog" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm7291521/|title=Alina Pasok - IMDb|website=IMDb.com|date=2021-08-06|access-date=2021-08-06}}</ref> The band shoots and produces all of their music videos.<ref name="int">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SAozC_hdj7Q|title=Ильич и Гокк (LITTLE BIG) – Интервью (Тула 31 October 2014)|last=Роман Гогов|date=7 November 2014|access-date=2 December 2018|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>

Since the first concert took place as the opening act for [[Die Antwoord]], the band was dubbed "The Russian Die Antwoord",<ref name="dog" /><ref name="noi" /> and is often compared with this band.<ref name="met">{{cite web|url=http://www.metatroniks.net/2013/12/12/little-big-from-russia/|title=Little Big from Russia|last=Metatroniks|date=12 December 2013|website=Metatronicks.net|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref> [[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] also called Little Big "a Russian mental patient's answer to Die Antwoord".<ref name="noi" /> Other critics point out that while Little Big has an [[Identity (social science)|identity]] of its own, it also exposes the listener to [[Russian folk songs]] and [[Russian culture]].<ref name="bmm">{{cite web|url=http://www.beatmashmagazine.com/los-die-antwoord-rusos-little-big|title=Los "Die Antwoord" rusos: Little Big – Beatmash Magazine|website=Beatmashmagazine.com|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref>

The band was influenced by a variety of musicians from [[Cannibal Corpse]], [[NOFX]], [[Red Hot Chili Peppers]], [[Rammstein]], and [[The Prodigy]] to [[Mozart, Wolfgang Amadeus|Mozart]] and [[Antonio Vivaldi|Vivaldi]].<ref name="UTV" /><ref name="barsa">{{cite web|url=http://www.theconcertinconcert.com/little-big-desde-rusia-en-barcelona-rave-funeral/|title=LITTLE BIG (desde Rusia) en BARCELONA – Rave Funeral|website=Theconcertinconcert.com|access-date=2 December 2018}}</ref>
