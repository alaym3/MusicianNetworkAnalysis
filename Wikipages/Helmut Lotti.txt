'''Helmut Lotti''' (born '''Helmut Barthold Johannes Alma Lotigiers'''; 22 October 1969), is a Belgian [[tenor]]<ref name="Goldsmith">Goldsmith B (2009). [https://archive.today/20131201171326/http://new.wdez.com/news/articles/2009/sep/24/belgian-tenor-helmut-lotti-wants-to-find-his-own-s Belgian tenor Helmut Lotti wants to find his own style]. ''[[WDEZ]]''. Retrieved 23 April 2010.</ref> and singer-songwriter. Lotti performs in several styles and languages. Once an [[Elvis impersonator]], he has sung African and [[Latino (demonym)|Latino]] and [[Jewish music]] [[hit record]]s, and he [[Crossover (music)|crossed over]] into classical music in the 1990s.<ref name = "Goldsmith"/>

==Life and music==
The son of Luc and Rita (née Lagrou), Helmut Barthold Johannes Alma Lotigiers was born in [[Ghent]], [[Belgium]], and began his singing career with a visual and singing style in an obvious imitation of [[Elvis Presley]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Helmut Lotti {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/helmut-lotti-mn0000746485/biography|access-date=3 July 2020|website=AllMusic|language=en-us}}</ref> and was described as "De Nieuwe Elvis" (in Dutch) or "The New Elvis". His first two albums were ''Vlaamse Nachten'' ("Flemish Nights", 1990) and ''Alles Wat Ik Voel'' ("All That I Feel", 1992). After a few more albums, he changed direction in 1995 with the first of what became a long series of "Helmut Lotti Goes Classic" albums, which proved to increase his popularity. Since 2000 he has also made successful recordings in traditional [[Latino (demonym)|Latino]], [[African music|African]] and Russian-style music.

Lotti sings in his native language [[Dutch language|Dutch]], as well as [[Afrikaans]], English, French, German, [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]], Italian, [[Latin]], Russian, Spanish, [[Ukrainian language|Ukrainian]], [[Zulu language|Zulu]], [[Xhosa language|Xhosa]], [[Transvaal Ndebele language|Ndebele]] and others. His albums has sold over 13 million worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-lotti/belgian-tenor-helmut-lotti-wants-to-find-his-own-style-idUSTRE58N15N20090924|title=Belgian tenor Helmut Lotti wants to find his own style|agency=Reuters|first=Belinda|last=Goldsmith|date=24 September 2009|access-date=19 July 2019}}</ref>

Lotti does volunteer work as an ambassador for [[UNICEF]]. Lotti took part in the ''[[0110]]'' concerts against racism, organised by [[Tom Barman]].<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Downing|first1=John D. H.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=iwPX23VameIC&q=lotti+0110+concerts&pg=PA184|title=Encyclopedia of Social Movement Media|last2=Downing|first2=John Derek Hall|date=2011|publisher=SAGE|isbn=978-0-7619-2688-7|language=en}}</ref> 

He was married 3 times, divorced 3 times and has 1 daughter. Lotti was diagnosed with [[autism]] as an adult.<ref>{{cite web |title=Helmut Lotti over autisme: “Eigenlijk beweeg ik me al mijn hele leven voort als een bang hert in het bos” |url=https://www.nieuwsblad.be/cnt/dmf20191226_04782461 |website=[[Het Nieuwsblad]] |access-date=14 May 2021 |language=Dutch |date=26 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Helmut Lotti: Ich habe Autismus! |url=https://www.schlager.de/news/2018/06/19/helmut-lotti-ich-habe-autismus/ |website=Schlager |access-date=14 May 2021 |language=German}}</ref>
