'''Grace Petrie''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|p|i|t|r|i}}, born 1987) is an English [[folk music|folk]] singer-songwriter and guitarist from [[Leicester]], England. She was hailed in ''[[The Guardian]]'' as "a powerful new songwriting voice" in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2011/nov/10/protest-music-akala-grace-petrie|title=Protest music: steel in the hour of chaos|first=Dorian|last=Lynskey|date=10 November 2011|website=Theguardian.com}}</ref>

==Biography==
Grace Petrie began performing in 2006 as a solo vocalist and acoustic guitarist, and self-released an eponymous album that year, followed in 2007 by second album ''Feeling Better''. In 2010, the advent of the [[Con'-Dem' alliance|Conservative-led coalition]] government following the (UK) [[2010 United Kingdom general election|general election]] influenced Petrie, who is a socialist, feminist, and lesbian,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.advertiser.ie/Galway/article/103347/grace-petrie-im-a-socialist-feminist-lesbian-protest-singer |work=Galway Advertiser |date=2 October 2018 |title=Grace Petrie – 'I'm a socialist, feminist, lesbian, protest singer'}}</ref> towards an increasing emphasis on politically focused songwriting, from a left-wing perspective. She made her debut appearance on [[Glastonbury Festival|Glastonbury]]'s Leftfield stage at the invitation of [[Billy Bragg]] in summer 2010, and widely praised third album ''Tell Me A Story'' followed, including signature song "Farewell to Welfare".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.themonograph.co.uk/reviews/album-reviews/grace-petrie-tell-me-a-story |title=Archived copy |access-date=7 November 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141107191631/http://www.themonograph.co.uk/reviews/album-reviews/grace-petrie-tell-me-a-story |archive-date=7 November 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://peacenews.info/node/6211/grace-petrie-tell-me-story|title=Grace Petrie, Tell Me a Story – Peace News|website=peacenews.info}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://socialistworker.co.uk/art/22962/Tell%20Me%20A%20Story%20-%20Grace%20Petrie|title=Tell Me A Story – Grace Petrie|website=Socialist Worker (Britain)}}</ref>

In 2011, Petrie toured with comedian [[Josie Long]]. Fourth album ''Mark My Words'' followed, including the song "Emily Davison Blues"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.redpepper.org.uk/grace-petrie-singing-for-change/|title=Grace Petrie: Singing for change|website=Redpepper.org.uk}}</ref> – a comment on media reaction to the [[2011 England riots|2011 riots]]. A special film directed by [[Chris Shepherd]] for the song "Rise" from the same album was screened on [[Channel 4]] as part of the TV series ''Random Acts''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://gracepetrie.com/news/channel-4-tomorrow-night|title=CHANNEL 4 TOMORROW NIGHT!|date=25 June 2012|website=Gracepetrie.com}}</ref> In 2012, Petrie took part in the "Anti-Capitalist Roadshow"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/nov/29/anti-capitalist-roadshow-celebrating-review|title=The Anti-Capitalist Roadshow: Celebrating Subversion – review|first=Robin|last=Denselow|date=29 November 2012|website=Theguardian.com}}</ref> alongside [[Roy Bailey (folk singer)|Roy Bailey]], [[Robb Johnson]], [[Leon Rosselson]], [[Peggy Seeger]] and others. She has also performed gigs with other political folk artists such as [[Chris T-T]], [[David Rovics]] and [[Dick Gaughan]], as well as political indie/punk rock bands like [[Thee Faction]] and [[Colour Me Wednesday]].
[[file:Purbeck_Valley_Folk_Festival_2021_-_Grace_Petrie_(51407831513).jpg|thumb|left|Petrie at the Purbeck Valley Folk Festival in 2021]]
In 2013, Petrie released her fifth album ''Love is My Rebellion'' with new backing band The Benefits Culture, consisting of Jess Greengrass (percussion) and Caitlin Field (bass).<ref>{{cite news|url=https://morningstaronline.co.uk/a-21ae-music-grace-petrie-and-the-benefits-culture|title=Music: Grace Petrie & The Benefits Culture|date=18 November 2013|website=Morning Star|first=Ian|last=Sinclair|accessdate=16 July 2020}}</ref>

Petrie has made an annual appearance at Glastonbury since 2010<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/Grace-laugh-doing-best-job-world/story-22874918-detail/story.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=7 November 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141107191739/http://www.leicestermercury.co.uk/Grace-laugh-doing-best-job-world/story-22874918-detail/story.html |archive-date=7 November 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> as well as regularly playing festivals such as [[Towersey Village Festival|Towersey]], [[Greenbelt Festival]], [[Latitude Festival|Latitude]] and others. She has toured nationally with [[Emmy the Great]], [[Billy Bragg]] and comedian [[Robin Ince]], and has made several appearances on [[BBC Radio 4]]'s ''[[The Now Show]]''. In 2014, a live concert recorded at St. Pancras Old Church was released on CD and DVD.

In 2015, she released the album ''Whatever's Left''.

In 2016, Petrie along with numerous other celebrities, toured the UK to support [[Jeremy Corbyn]]'s bid to become [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]].<ref name="liverpoolecho">{{cite news |last=Wright|first=Jade|url=https://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/whats-on/music-nightlife-news/jeremy-corbyn-concert-see-famous-11433117|title=Jeremy Corbyn concert will see famous faces celebrating Labour leader|newspaper=[[Liverpool Echo]]|date=6 June 2016|accessdate=27 November 2019}}</ref>
