'''XVideos''', stylized as '''XVIDEOS''', is a [[pornography|pornographic]] [[video sharing]] and viewing website. XVideos was founded in Paris in 2007. The website is now registered to the Czech company WGCZ Holding.<ref name="Invisible" /><ref name="theblast" /> {{as of|2021|08|}}, it was the most visited porn website and the seventh most visited website in the world.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=April 2020 |title=xvideos.com Traffic Statistics |url=http://similarweb.com/website/xvideos.com |access-date=2021-08-08 |website=SimilarWeb}}</ref>

WGCZ Holding also owns [[Bang Bros]], DDF Network, [[Penthouse (magazine)|''Penthouse'' magazine]], [[Private Media Group]], and Erogames and has a controlling interest in the productions gathered under the Legal Porno brand.<ref name="theblast">{{Cite web |date=5 June 2018 |title='BangBros' Owner Buys Penthouse Biz For $11.2 Million |url=https://theblast.com/bangbros-buys-penthouse-auction-millions/ |access-date=6 June 2018 |website=[[The Blast (website)|The Blast]]}}</ref><ref name="avn">{{Cite web |last=Mark Kernes |date=5 June 2018 |title=Penthouse Bankruptcy Auction Results in New Ownership |url=https://avn.com/business/articles/legal/after-yesterdays-bankruptcy-auction-penthouse-has-a-new-owner-779541.html |access-date=6 June 2018 |website=[[avn.com]]}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web |last=Gustavo Turner |date=2 January 2020 |title=Private Media Group Acquired by XVideos Parent Company WGCZ |url=https://www.xbiz.com/news/249330/private-media-group-acquired-by-xvideos-parent-company-wgcz |access-date=5 January 2020 |website=[[XBIZ]]}}</ref>

== History ==
XVideos was founded in Paris in 2007 by the French owner Stephane Michael Pacaud. XVideos serves as a pornographic media aggregator, a type of website which gives access to adult content in a similar manner as YouTube does for general content.<ref name="Pink2.0">{{Cite book |last=Tsika |first=Noah |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0uBKDQAAQBAJ&q=xvideos&pg=PA61 |title=Pink 2.0: Encoding Queer on the Internet |date=October 3, 2016 |publisher=Indiana University Press |isbn=978-0253023230 |location=Bloomington, IN |pages=61–62}}</ref><ref name="Economist" /> Video clips from professional videos are mixed with amateur and other types of content.<ref name="Pink2.0" /><ref name="Economist" /> By 2012, XVideos was the largest porn website in the world, with over 100 billion page views per month.<ref name="XBIZ2">{{Cite web |last=Yagielowicz |first=Stephen |date=April 4, 2012 |title=Report: The Internet Really Is for Porn |url=https://www.xbiz.com/news/146703/report-the-internet-really-is-for-porn |access-date=August 31, 2017 |website=XBIZ}}</ref> [[Fabian Thylmann]], the owner of [[MindGeek]], attempted to purchase XVideos in 2012 in order to create a monopoly of pornographic tube sites. The French owner of XVideos turned down a reported offer of more than $120&nbsp;million by saying, "Sorry, I have to go and play ''[[Diablo II]]''."<ref name="Economist">{{Cite news |date=September 26, 2015 |title=Naked capitalism |work=The Economist |url=https://www.economist.com/news/international/21666114-internet-blew-porn-industrys-business-model-apart-its-response-holds-lessons |access-date=August 31, 2017}}</ref> In 2014, XVideos controversially attempted to force content providers to either pledge to renounce the right to delete videos from their accounts or to shut down their accounts immediately.<ref name="avn1">{{Cite magazine |date=August 13, 2014 |title=XVideos.com Tube Site Accused of Strong-Arming Uploaders |url=https://avn.com/business/articles/technology/xvideos-com-tube-site-accused-of-strong-arming-uploaders-569826.html |magazine=AVN |access-date=August 31, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Online porn websites promote 'sexually violent' videos |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-56640178 |website=BBC News |access-date=25 April 2021 |date=5 April 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/04/16/opinion/sunday/companies-online-rape-videos.html|title=Why Do We Let Corporations Profit From Rape Vidoes ?|work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref>

== Web traffic and ranking ==
{{as of|2021|08|}}, XVideos was the most visited porn website and the seventh most visited website in the world,  as ranked by [[SimilarWeb]].<ref name=":0"/>

XNXX, a [[mirror site]] of XVideos, is the tenth most visited website overall and the second most visited website in the adult category.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |date=August 2021 |title=xnxx.com Traffic Statistics |url=http://similarweb.com/website/xnxx.com |access-date=2021-08-08 |website=SimilarWeb}}</ref>

== Censorship ==
{{See also|Internet censorship}}

=== Algeria ===
In 2019, the [[Algerian Government|Algerian government]] banned XVideos for no full reason, the Algerian government has given no official announcement regarding this fact, but the website remains accessible to the public via [[IP address spoofing|IP address]].

=== Bangladesh ===
On February 19, 2019, [[Government of Bangladesh|Bangladeshi government]] blocked access to the site along with 20,000 other pornographic sites as a part of their anti-porn war.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bangladesh blocks 20,000 websites in anti-porn 'war' |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2019/02/bangladesh-blocks-20000-websites-anti-porn-war-190219155030486.html |website=www.aljazeera.com}}</ref>

=== India ===
{{Further|Internet censorship in India}}
In 2015, the company was censored by the [[Indian government]] on a list of 857 pornographic websites, which also included non-pornographic websites such as [[CollegeHumor]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=2015-08-04 |title=Xvideos.com has the most subtle but brilliant response to govt's #Pornban |language=en-US |work=Firstpost |url=http://www.firstpost.com/india/xvideos-com-subtle-brilliant-response-govts-pornban-2378200.html |access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Akolawala |first=Tasneem |title=Xvideos, College Humor lash out at Indian government after porn ban {{!}} Latest Tech News, Video & Photo Reviews at BGR India |language=en-US |work=BGR India |url=http://www.bgr.in/news/xvideos-college-humor-lash-out-at-indian-government-after-porn-ban/ |access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref> In 2018, major Indian [[internet service provider]]s also blocked access to XVideos and other porn sites.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jaitly |first=Rohan |date=2018-10-26 |title=Reliance Jio blocks porn websites in India after DOT orders closure of 827 adult websites: Report {{!}} Tech News |url=https://www.timesnownews.com/technology-science/article/reliance-jio-bans-adult-websites-in-india-after-dot-orders-to-block-827-adult-websites-report/304516 |access-date=2019-01-20 |website=TimesNowNews.com}}</ref>

=== Lebanon ===
In 2014, [[Lebanon]]'s Minister of Telecommunications ordered internet service providers to block six pornographic websites<ref>{{Cite web |date=2014-09-11 |title=Lebanon Blocks Six Porn Sites, Sparks Fears of Further Censorship |url=https://smex.org/lebanon-blocks-six-porn-sites-sparks-fears-of-further-censorship/ |access-date=2019-10-10 |website=SMEX: Channeling Advocacy |language=en-US}}</ref> including XVideos. Some internet providers did not oblige, while others like Mobi DSL did.

=== Malaysia ===
In 2015, the [[Malaysian Government|Malaysian government]] banned XVideos for violating the [[Communications and Multimedia Act 1998]], which bans "obscene content" from being digitally distributed.<ref>{{Cite news |last=IBTimes |title=Malaysia porn ban: xVideo, PornHub among websites blocked for 'obscene content' |language=en |work=International Business Times, India Edition |url=http://www.ibtimes.co.in/malaysia-porn-ban-xvideo-pornhub-among-websites-blocked-obscene-content-654254 |access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref>

=== Philippines ===
On January 14, 2017, President [[Rodrigo Duterte]] blocked XVideos as a part of Republic Act 9775 or the Anti-Child Pornography Law.<ref name="bbc">{{Cite news |date=2017-01-06 |title=Popular porn sites blocked in Philippines |work=BBC |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-38637003}}</ref>

=== Venezuela ===
On June 14, 2018, the [[Venezuela]]n telecommunications and internet services company [[CANTV]] blocked access to the website without giving any statement in this regard.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Cantv bloqueó acceso a tres páginas pornográficas en Venezuela |url=http://www.el-nacional.com/noticias/sociedad/cantv-bloqueo-acceso-tres-paginas-pornograficas-venezuela_240283 |access-date=17 June 2018 |website=el-nacional.com |language=Spanish}}</ref>

=== Vietnam ===
Since November of 2019, Vietnamese ISPs has silently blocked access to every major pornographic websites, including XVideos. Most ISPs declined to comment and denied blocking the sites. Most users assumed that this was a result of a secret Government mandate. Despite that, the sites are still accessible through the use of a [[Virtual private network|VPN]].<ref name="Trí 2019">{{cite web |last=Trí |first=Dân |title=Các nhà mạng Việt Nam "âm thầm" chặn web đen |trans-title=Vietnamese carriers "silently" block the dark web |website=Báo điện tử Dân Trí |date=2019-11-19 |url=https://dantri.com.vn/suc-manh-so/cac-nha-mang-viet-nam-am-tham-chan-web-den-20191119145942809.htm |language=vi |access-date=2021-10-03}}</ref>
