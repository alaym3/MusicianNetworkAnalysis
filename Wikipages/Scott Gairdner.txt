'''Scott Gairdner''' (born 1987) is an American comedy writer, director, and podcaster, known for having created the [[Comedy Central]] animated series ''[[Moonbeam City]]'', for promoting the cult phenomenon of ''[[The Room]]'', alongside filmmaker Michael Rousselet, and for co-creating and co-hosting the theme park podcast ''Podcast: The Ride'' since 2017.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://variety.com/2017/film/festivals/the-disaster-artist-review-james-franco-tommy-wiseau-the-room-1202007596/ |title=Film Review: 'The Disaster Artist' |last=Debruge |first=Peter |date=2017-03-13 |website=Variety |language=en-US |access-date=2017-12-26}}</ref> He created [[Tiny Fuppets]].<ref name="huffingtonpost">{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/08/07/tiny-fuppets-bizzare-muppet-babies-knockoff_n_920511.html|title=Tiny Fuppets: The Most Bizarre 'Muppet Babies' Knockoff Ever (VIDEO)|publisher=huffingtonpost.com|accessdate=2014-04-19}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Kramer |first1=Mark |title=Want to Try Podcast: The Ride? Start Here. |url=https://www.vulture.com/2020/02/podcast-the-ride-best-episodes-downtown-disney.html |website=Vulture |accessdate=6 April 2020}}</ref>

He is married to Erin Pade.

==Career==
After studying film production at [[Loyola Marymount University]], Gairdner began his career by uploading comedy videos onto his YouTube channel, which he started in 2006,<ref name="bio">{{cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Bio|url=http://scottgairdner.com/bio|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190401030759/http://scottgairdner.com/bio|archive-date=2019-04-01|accessdate=27 November 2015|work=Scottgairdner.com}}</ref> including the "Sex Offender Shuffle."<ref>[https://www.vulture.com/2013/12/conan-writer-scott-gairdner-responded-to-a-weird-reddit-question-about-one-of-his-videos-with-hilarious-lies.html Vulture: 'Conan' writer Scott Gairdner responded to a weird Reddit question about one of his videos with hilarious lies]</ref> For two years (2010â€“2012) he worked at [[Funny or Die]] as a staff writer and director.<ref name=bio/>

In 2012, he was hired to work for the [[TBS (U.S. TV channel)|TBS]] show ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]'' as a writer and director. During his time working for ''Conan'', he shot dozens of sketches for the show.<ref name="bio" /> 

He created and serves as the executive producer of ''[[Moonbeam City]]'', an animated series on Comedy Central that debuted on September 16, 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://splitsider.com/2015/09/scott-gairdner-welcomes-you-to-moonbeam-city/ | title=Scott Gairdner Welcomes You to 'Moonbeam City' | work=Splitsider | date=15 September 2015 | accessdate=27 November 2015 | author=Kurland, Daniel}}</ref> Gairdner credits the videos he posted on his YouTube channel for gaining the attention of Funny or Die, and later, Conan, which in turn served as the "springboard" that led to him being able to create ''Moonbeam City''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.indiewire.com/article/moonbeam-city-creator-scott-gairdner-reached-for-the-stars-and-got-rob-lowe-20150916 | title='Moonbeam City' Creator Scott Gairdner Reached For the Stars, and Got Rob Lowe | work=Indiewire | date=16 September 2015 | accessdate=27 November 2015 | author=Miller, Liz Shannon}}</ref>

In 2017, as part of promoting the latest installment in his ''Clip Cup'' series, Gairdner helped launch the fictitious [[Over-the-top media services|OTT platform]] Vioobu, which managed to dupe several publications, as well as viewers, due to its expansive library of content.<ref>{{cite web |title=Vioobu Launches |url=https://www.vulture.com/2017/05/love-dramedies-about-comedians-watch-totally-real-show-cuplicated.html |website=Vulture}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Vioobu Official website |url=http://www.vioobu.com/clip-cup |website=Vioobu}}</ref>

Since 2017, Gairdner has co-hosted a [[theme park]] podcast, ''Podcast: The Ride'', alongside fellow comedians and theme park fans Mike Carlson and Jason Sheridan.
