'''Tyler Garcia-Posey''' (born October 18, 1991)<ref name="MTV17">{{cite web|title=17 Facts About Teen Wolf's Tyler Posey You Need In Your Life {{!}} MTV UK|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/teen-wolf/news/17-facts-about-teen-wolfs-tyler-posey-you-need-in-your-life|website=MTV.com|access-date=January 4, 2018|language=en}}</ref> is an American actor and musician who is known for his role as [[List of Teen Wolf characters#Main characters|Scott McCall]] on the [[MTV]] series ''[[Teen Wolf (2011 TV series)|Teen Wolf]]'' (2011–2017).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://tvline.com/2016/07/21/teen-wolf-ending-season-6-series-finale-2017/|title=Teen Wolf Ending After Season 6|last=Swift|first=Andy|date=July 21, 2016|website=TVLine|access-date=August 29, 2017}}</ref>

==Early life==
Posey was born in [[Santa Monica, California]], to Cyndi Terese Garcia and actor-writer [[John Posey (actor)|John Posey]].<ref name="tvg">{{cite web|url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/tyler-posey/bio/275903|title=Tyler Posey Biography|work=[[TV Guide]]|access-date=August 9, 2014}}</ref> He grew up in [[Santa Clarita, California]].<ref name="HuffPo 2012-07-22">{{cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/06/25/teen-wolf-star-tyler-posey_n_1625161.html|title=''Teen Wolf'' star Tyler Posey on his character's ethnicity: 'I'm pretty sure that he's Latino'|work=[[The Huffington Post]]|date=July 22, 2012|access-date=August 23, 2014}}</ref> Posey has two brothers.<ref name="tvg"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Tyler Posey's Brother Jesse Posey Biography |url=https://wealthycelebrity.com/jesse-posey/ |website=Wealthy Celebrity |access-date=16 June 2021}}</ref> He is of Mexican descent on his mother's side.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/teen-wolf-star-tyler-posey_n_1625161|title='Teen Wolf' Star Says He Doesn't Mind Being Shirtless On Set (EXCLUSIVE)|date=June 25, 2012|website=HuffPost}}</ref>

==Career==
Posey has worked steadily in film and television. In February 2002, he appeared in the film ''[[Collateral Damage (2002 film)|Collateral Damage]]''; in December of that year, he played the main character's son in the romantic comedy ''[[Maid in Manhattan]]''.<ref name="MTV">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/videos/news/855052/tyler-posey-was-almost-a-different-kind-of-wolf.jhtml#id=1695311|title=Sneak a peek at ''This Is How I Made It''|publisher=[[MTV]]|date=November 8, 2012}}</ref> He auditioned for the role of [[Jacob Black]] in the [[The Twilight Saga (film series)|''Twilight'' film series]] in 2007 but lost the role to his friend [[Taylor Lautner]]. The two regularly auditioned for the same roles as child actors.<ref name="MTV"/>

That same year, Posey voiced a character in the pilot of the Disney original cartoon ''[[SheZow]]'', which aired on May 4 as a part of the [[Shorty McShorts' Shorts]] miniseries.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.autoevolution.com/news/teen-wolf-tyler-posey-rides-his-harley-davidson-82791.html|title=Teen Wolf Tyler Posey Rides His Harley Davidson|last = Ungureanu|first = Ionut|date = June 20, 2014|work = Autoevolution|access-date = September 25, 2017}}</ref> In 2012, he co-starred in the film ''[[White Frog]]''. In 2011, Posey was cast in his [[breakthrough role|breakout role]] as the lead in the [[MTV]] television series ''[[Teen Wolf (2011 TV series)|Teen Wolf]],'' which is based on the [[Teen Wolf|1985 film]]. Posey plays high school student Scott McCall, who is bitten by a [[werewolf]] and must keep this fact secret while protecting his loved ones from a host of supernatural threats and beings. In 2015, he also became a co-producer for the fifth season of ''Teen Wolf''.<ref name="boys in Pixels">{{cite web|url=http://www.boysinpixels.com/2012/06/tyler-posey-with-his-band-at-roxy-in-la.html|title=Boys in Pixels|date=June 6, 2012|access-date=August 23, 2014|archive-date=June 10, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120610013720/http://www.boysinpixels.com/2012/06/tyler-posey-with-his-band-at-roxy-in-la.html}}</ref>

On May 24, 2017, Posey was cast as Lucas Moreno in the [[Blumhouse]] supernatural [[Thriller (genre)|thriller]] film ''[[Truth or Dare (2018 film)|Truth or Dare]]''.<ref>{{cite news |title='Teen Wolf' Star Tyler Posey, Violett Beane & More Are Game For 'Truth Or Dare' From Blumhouse|url=https://deadline.com/2017/05/tyler-posey-violett-beane-truth-or-dare-blumhouse-1202101715/|work= Deadline |last= N'Duka|first= Amanda|date= May 24, 2017 |access-date= January 3, 2018}}</ref> The film was released in theaters on April 13, 2018.<ref>{{cite news |title='Blumhouse's Truth Or Dare' Moves Up Two Weeks To Friday The 13th – Update|url=https://deadline.com/2017/11/blumhouses-truth-or-dare-april-2018-release-date-1202205290/|work= Deadline |last= D'Alessandro|first= Anthony |date= January 11, 2017 |access-date= January 13, 2018}}</ref>

On September 25, 2017, it was announced that Posey would join the third season of the [[VH1]] slasher television series ''[[Scream (TV series)|Scream]]'', starring in the role of Shane.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/teen-wolfs-tyler-posey-joins-mtvs-scream-1042576|title='Teen Wolf' Star Tyler Posey Joins MTV's 'Scream' (Exclusive)|last = Goldberg|first = Lesley|date = September 25, 2017|work = Hollywood Reporter|access-date = September 25, 2017}}</ref> The season premiered on July 8, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://etcanada.com/news/467876/scream-reboot-gets-trailer-with-cameo-from-paris-jackson/|title='Scream' Reboot Gets Trailer With Cameo From Paris Jackson|last=Samhan|first=Jamie|work=Entertainment Weekly|date=June 24, 2019|access-date=June 25, 2019}}</ref>

On February 20, 2019, it was announced that Posey would star as [[Michael Emerson (character)|Michael Emerson]] in [[The CW]]'s television reboot series of ''[[The Lost Boys]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Andreeva |first1=Nellie |title=Lost Boys': Tyler Posey, Kiele Sanchez, Medalion Rahimi & Dakota Shapiro Star, Catherine Hardwicke To Direct CW Pilot |url=https://deadline.com/2019/02/the-lost-boys-tyler-posey-kiele-sanchez-medalion-rahimi-dakota-shapiro-cast-catherine-hardwicke-direct-the-cw-pilot-1202561404/ |access-date=November 22, 2019 |work=Deadline}}</ref> On July 29, 2019, it was announced The CW was unhappy with the pilot and that Posey, along with most of the rest of the cast, would be recast.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Andreeva |first1=Nellie |title='The Lost Boys': Rob Thomas Gives Update On the CW Pilot Retooling |url=https://deadline.com/2019/07/the-lost-boys-pilot-reboot-rob-thomas-the-cw-retool-1202655709/ |access-date=November 22, 2019 |work=Deadline}}</ref>

On November 18, 2019, it was announced that Posey would star in [[Netflix]]'s animated ''[[Fast & Furious]]'' television series ''[[Fast & Furious: Spy Racers]]'', which premiered on December 26, 2019.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lawrence |first1=Derek |title=Tyler Posey, Vin Diesel's daughter Similce Diesel to star in Netflix's Fast & Furious animated series |url=https://ew.com/tv/2019/11/18/netflix-fast-furious-spy-racers-cast-first-look/ |access-date=November 24, 2019 |work=Entertainment Weekly}}</ref>

===Other work===
Posey used to front and play guitar in the band Disappearing Jamie, formerly known as Lost in Kostko. Their first performance was at [[Roxy Theatre (West Hollywood)|The Roxy]] in [[Los Angeles]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.socialitelife.com/tyler-posey-jamming-with-his-band-lost-in-kostko-at-the-roxy-photos-06-2012|title=Tyler Posey jamming with his band "Lost in Kostko" at the Roxy [photos]|last = Prieve|first = Michael|date = June 4, 2012|work = Socialite Life|access-date = September 25, 2017}}</ref> Lost in Kostko released their eight-track EP ''You're Going to Need a Towel'' in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7416386/tyler-posey-teen-wolf-music-all-time-low-interview|title='Teen Wolf' Star Tyler Posey Teams Up With All Time Low's Jack Barakat, Ready for His Music to 'Take Off'|last = Bliss|first = Karen|date = June 23, 2016|work = Billboard|access-date = July 10, 2020}}</ref> Posey announced the band's breakup via Instagram on July 17, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/b39sDnuDga|title=tylerposey58 Instagram|date = July 17, 2013|work = Instagram|access-date = July 10, 2020}}</ref>

In December 2016, Posey was a guest DJ at Emo Nite LA's second anniversary at the Echoplex in Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Tolentino |first1=Jia |title=The Rise of Emo Nostalgia |url=https://www.newyorker.com/culture/jia-tolentino/the-rise-of-emo-nostalgia |access-date=June 20, 2018 |work=The New Yorker}}</ref>

Posey played guitar and contributed vocals in the pop punk band PVMNTS along with guitarist/bassist/vocalist Freddy Ramirez and drummer Nick Guzman. The trio released the song "Standing (On My Own Two Feet)" on June 14, 2018, on which Posey wrote about the passing of his mother in 2014. The band self-released their six-track EP ''Better Days'' on August 17, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/tyler-posey-pvmnts-standing-on-my-own-two-feet|title=Tyler Posey's Pop-Punk Band Pvmnts Debut First Track, "Standing (On My Own Two Feet)"|last = Pettigrew|first = Jason|date = June 14, 2018|work = Alternative Press|access-date = July 26, 2018}}</ref> In April 2019, it was announced that Posey had left the band and was pursuing another music venture, which became the band Five North.

Five North is a pop-punk rock band formed by childhood best friends Posey and Kyle Murphy. Posey and Murphy originate from just off "The 5" ([[Interstate 5 in California]]) in [[Santa Clarita, California]], hence the origin of the band's name Five North.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/1iFpt30BRqyRwTQKqxSCiY?si=-biiY2F5S9Sr_V-QEmWJjQ|title=Spotify Bio|work = Spotify|access-date = July 10, 2020}}</ref> The two recruited [[Makeout (band)|Makeout]] drummer Scott Eckel after being introduced by music producer and close friend John Feldmann.<ref name="White">{{cite web|url=https://substreammagazine.com/2020/03/interview-five-north-2020|title=INTERVIEW: Five North discuss 'Scumbag' and relationship with John Feldmann|last = White|first = Logan|date = March 5, 2020|work = Substream Magazine|access-date = July 10, 2020}}</ref> Five North's debut single "This Mess"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=scrv7b5hJVc|title=Five North - This Mess|date = October 4, 2019|work = YouTube|access-date = October 10, 2019}}</ref> was released on October 4, 2019, and their debut eight-track EP ''Scumbag'' was released on March 6, 2020, via Big Noise Music Group.<ref name="White"/>

In August 2021, Posey made a cameo appearance in the music video for "Broke",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qAdO71VwGyc|title=The Bottom Line - Broke|work = YouTube|access-date = August 21, 2021}}</ref> the song by UK pop-punk band the Bottom Line, who Posey met when touring the UK together in 2018 during his time in PVMNTS. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-pvmnts-announce-uk-tour-for-november-december-2018|title=Dead Press|work = Dead Press|access-date = August 21, 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Posey became engaged to his childhood sweetheart, Seana Gorlick, in 2013.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.glamour.com/story/teen-wolfs-tyler-posey-engaged|title=*Teen Wolf*'s Tyler Posey Engaged to Longtime Girlfriend Seana Gorlick|last=Fusaro|first=Kim|work=Glamour|access-date=January 3, 2018|language=en}}</ref> The couple broke off the engagement that same year, after a ten-year relationship.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://people.com/tv/teen-wolf-tyler-posey-on-dating-after-his-engagement-ended/|title=Teen Wolf's Tyler Posey on Why He's Not Ready to Date Again After Ending His Engagement|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|access-date=January 3, 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/tyler-posey-fiancee-seana-gorlick-split-couple-ends-engagement-20142310/|title=Tyler Posey, Fiancee Seana Gorlick Split: Couple Ends Engagement|date=October 23, 2014|work=Us Weekly|access-date=January 3, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

Posey's mother, Cyndi Terese Garcia, died of breast cancer in December 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=December 28, 2014|title=Cyndi Garcia-Posey Obituary (2014)|url=https://www.legacy.com/obituaries/the-signal/obituary.aspx?pid=173585760|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=[[Legacy.com]]|publisher=Santa Clarita Valley Signal}}</ref> The fifth season of ''Teen Wolf'' was dedicated to her memory.<ref name="HuffPo 2012-07-22"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ibtimes.com/teen-wolf-star-tyler-posey-talks-emotional-struggle-after-mothers-death-reveals-scott-1810598|title='Teen Wolf' Star Tyler Posey Talks Emotional Struggle After Mother’s Death; Reveals Scott McCall Quote He Lives By|first=Nicole|last=Massabrook|date=February 9, 2015|website=International Business Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tylergposey.com/bio.html|title=Tyler Posey|publisher=Tylergposey.com|access-date=July 7, 2011}}</ref>

In an October 2020 interview, Posey disclosed that he had begun smoking marijuana at the age of 12 and had developed an addiction. At that time, Posey stated that he had been sober for 71 days.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.etonline.com/tyler-posey-gets-candid-about-sexuality-addiction-and-finally-loving-himself-155025|title=Tyler Posey Gets Candid About Sexuality, Addiction and Finally Loving Himself &#124; Entertainment Tonight|website=www.etonline.com}}</ref> In March 2021, Posey stated that he was no longer sober, but added that he had learned a great deal from his experience of sobriety.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.eonline.com/news/1246648/tyler-posey-reveals-hes-no-longer-sober-and-speaks-out-about-his-sexuality|title=Teen Wolf’s Tyler Posey Explains Why He’s No Longer Sober|date=March 10, 2021|website=E! Online}}</ref>

Also in October 2020, Posey revealed that he has "hooked up" with men and does not like to label his sexuality.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://attitude.co.uk/article/tyler-posey-says-hes-hooked-up-with-guys-in-candid-video/23905/|title=Tyler Posey says he's "hooked up with guys" in candid video|date=October 19, 2020|work=Attitude|access-date=October 19, 2020|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.out.com/celebs/2020/10/19/teen-wolfs-tyler-posey-comes-out-pansexual-bottoming-blowing-dudes|title='Teen Wolf's Tyler Posey Talks 'Blowing' Dudes and Bottoming|date=October 19, 2020|work=Out|access-date=October 19, 2020|language=en-US}}</ref> In an interview on [[Sirius XM]], Posey said, "I was hit with wanting to [[coming out|come out]] myself and be honest about it. I know a lot of kids look up to me, and I want to get rid of that stigma."<ref>{{Citation|title=Why Tyler Posey Decided to Come Out|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y8_D3He_eeE|language=en|access-date=2021-06-25}}</ref> In July 2021, Posey [[came out]] as [[queer]] and [[sexually fluid]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://attitude.co.uk/article/tyler-posey-comes-out-as-queer-and-sexually-fluid-hits-back-at-gay-baiting-claims/25305/|title=TYLER POSEY COMES OUT AS QUEER AND SEXUALLY FLUID, HITS BACK AT 'GAY-BAITING' CLAIMS|date=July 5, 2021|work=Attitude|language=en-UK}}</ref>
