'''Afie Jurvanen''' (born April 28, 1981), known by his stage name '''Bahamas''', is a Canadian musician.

== Early life ==
Jurvanen was born in [[Toronto]] and raised in [[Barrie]], [[Ontario]].<ref>Eisner, Ken (March 25, 2010). [https://www.straight.com/article-299431/vancouver/afie-jurvanen-reinvents-himself-bahamas "Afie Jurvanen reinvents himself as Bahamas"]. ''[[The Georgia Straight]]''. Retrieved 2010-04-02.</ref> He is of [[Finland|Finnish]] ancestry.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bahamas - Brushfire Records|url=http://brushfirerecords.com/artists/bahamas/|access-date=2019-03-22|website=brushfirerecords.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Music artist Bahamas grows into his adopted name|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/music/music-artist-bahamas-grows-into-his-adopted-name/article20174883/|access-date=2019-03-22}}</ref>

==Career==
Jurvanen taught himself guitar. He worked with a variety of musicians that includes [[The Lumineers]], [[Dallas Green (musician)|City and Colour]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/city-colour-3/|title=City And Colour|website=Tonedeaf.thebrag.com|date=May 21, 2012|access-date=November 14, 2020}}</ref> [[Feist (singer)|Feist]], [[Howie Beck]], [[Jason Collett]], [[Jack Johnson (musician)|Jack Johnson]], [[The Weather Station]], and [[Zeus (band)|Zeus]].<ref>Semansky, Matt (February 25, 2010). [http://www.thecoast.ca/halifax/bahamass-island-life/Content?oid=1536309 "Bahamas's island life"]. ''[[The Coast (newspaper)|The Coast]]''. Retrieved 2010-04-02.</ref> [[Downtown Music Publishing]] represents Bahamas' songs. Jurvanen recorded his debut album, ''[[Pink Strat]]'', in a cabin in rural Ontario in 2008. He released the album under the name Bahamas in 2009 and was subsequently nominated for a [[Juno Awards of 2010|2010 Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for Roots & Traditional Album of the Year – Solo|Roots & Traditional Album of the Year – Solo]].<ref name="junos">{{cite web |url=http://junoawards.ca/awards/artist-summary/?artist_name=Bahamas&submit=Search |title=Artist Summary: Bahamas |date=2013 |access-date=July 8, 2013 |website=JunoAwards.ca |publisher=[[Canadian Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences]] }}</ref>

Bahamas' second album, ''[[Barchords]], was released'' on February 7, 2012.<ref name=anticipated>{{cite magazine|url=http://exclaim.ca/News/exclaims_20_most_anticipated_canadian_albums_of_2012|title=Exclaim!'s 20 Most Anticipated Canadian Albums of 2012|magazine=[[Exclaim!]]|access-date=September 19, 2014}}</ref> The album received nomination at the [[Juno Awards of 2013|2013 Juno Awards]] for the [[Juno Award for Adult Alternative Album of the Year|Adult Alternative Album of the Year]]. Jurvanen received a nomination for [[Juno Award for Songwriter of the Year|Songwriter of the Year]] for the tracks "Be My Witness", "Caught Me Thinking", and "Lost in the Light".<ref name="junos"/>

Jurvanen released his third album, ''[[Bahamas Is Afie]]'' on August 19, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2014/05/bahamas-all-the-time-stream-afie-jurvanen/|title=Hear Bahamas Rip Soulful Harmonies on Falsetto-Filled 'All the Time'|date=May 29, 2014|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|access-date=September 19, 2014}}</ref> The album received first place on ''[[Q (radio show)|Q]]'''s Top 20 Albums of 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/radio/q/schedule-for-thursday-january-1st-1.2926482/1-bahamas-q-s-top-20-albums-of-2014-1.2926489|title=#1 Bahamas - Q's Top 20 Albums of 2014|publisher=[[Q (radio show)|Q]]|date=January 1, 2015|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref> ''Bahamas Is Afie'' received a nomination for Adult Alternative Album of the Year At the [[Juno Awards of 2015]], and Jurvanen received nomination for Songwriter of the Year for "All the Time", "Bitter Memories" and "Stronger Than That".<ref>[http://www.junos.ctv.ca/2015-juno-award-nominees/ "2015 Juno Award Nominees: The complete list"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150311161956/http://www.junos.ctv.ca/2015-juno-award-nominees/ |date=March 11, 2015 }}. [[CTV Television Network|CTV]], January 27, 2015.</ref> He won the awards in both categories.

Jurvanen tours with drummer [[Jason Tait]] and backing vocalist Felicity Williams.<ref>[[Ben Rayner]], [https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2014/08/17/bahamas_latest_album_is_all_about_afie_jurvanen.html "Bahamas' latest album is all about Afie Jurvanen"]. ''[[Toronto Star]]'', August 17, 2014.</ref> Bahamas opened for Jack Johnson, [[Robert Plant]], [[Wilco]], [[The Lumineers]] and [[City and Colour]].<ref>[http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-bahamas-afie-20140821-story.html "Bahamas wants listeners to lean in to hear 'Bahamas Is Afie' album"]. ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'', August 21, 2014.</ref>

Jurvanen released his fourth album, ''[[Earthtones (Bahamas album)|Earthtones]]'' on January 19, 2018.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/articles/rock/8014745/bahamas-new-album-earthtones-no-wrong-interview "Bahamas Announce New Album 'Earthtones,' Share Charming New Track 'No Wrong': Exclusive"]. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', October 26, 2017.</ref> Bahamas began their Canadian tour for Earthtones on January 18, 2018, the [[Earthtones World Tour]] and recruited [[Zach Gill]] to open the shows.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://bahamasmusic.net/#|title=BAHAMAS • new album EARTHTONES out now • on tour|last=Bennett|first=Jud Haynes and Mark|website=bahamasmusic.net|access-date=2018-02-08}}</ref>

Jurvanen released his fifth studio album, ''[[Sad Hunk]]'', on October 9, 2020. The album won the Juno Award for Adult Alternative Album of the Year at the [[Juno Awards of 2021]].<ref>Holly Gordon and Andrea Warner, [https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/here-are-the-2021-juno-award-winners-1.6036001 "Here are the 2021 Juno Award winners"]. [[CBC Music]], June 4, 2021.</ref>

==Personal life==
