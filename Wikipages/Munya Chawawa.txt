'''Munyaradzi Chawawa''' (born 29 December 1992) is a British-Zimbabwean comedian.

==Early life==
Chawawa was born in [[Derby]], [[England]],<ref name="nytimes.com">{{Cite web|title=For a U.K. Satirist and His Online Fans, Comedy Is Catharsis|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/04/09/world/europe/munya-chawawa-british-comic-profile.html|first=Isabella|last=Kwai|date=9 April 2021|website=New York Times|language=en}}</ref> but spent his childhood in [[Zimbabwe]].<ref name="TheFace">{{cite web |title=Munya Chawawa: "I want to break the mould of being from a classicallyâ€¦ |url=https://theface.com/culture/munya-chawawa-comedy-instagram-lockdown |website=The Face |language=en-gb}}</ref><ref name="IndependentProfile">{{cite web |title=Munya Chawawa: 'Saying abracadabra was the equivalent of blurting out the C-word in my house' |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/comedy/features/munya-chawawa-interview-b1804150.html |website=The Independent |language=en |date=22 February 2021}}</ref> In his youth, his family returned from Zimbabwe to [[Framingham Pigot]], a village close to [[Norwich]] in [[Norfolk]].<ref name="guardianProfile"/> He studied at the [[University of Sheffield]], earning a [[BSc]] degree in [[psychology]] in 2014.<ref name="TheFace"/><ref name="SheffieldAlumni">{{cite web |title=Where are They Now? Munya Chawawa {{!}} Alumni {{!}} The University of Sheffield |url=https://www.sheffield.ac.uk/alumni/our-alumni/stories/munya-chawawa |website=www.sheffield.ac.uk |language=en |date=30 October 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
Chawawa is known for his development and portrayal of [[fictional character|characters]] such as a posh [[drill music|drill]] rapper called "Unknown P"<ref>{{cite web |title=Watch Munya Chawawa's hilarious parody of Stormzy and Chip beef |url=https://www.nme.com/news/munya-chawawa-gets-involved-in-stormzy-and-chip-beef-with-hilarious-parody-2779134 |website=NME |date=10 October 2020}}</ref> and the chef "Jonny Oliver".<ref name="BustleProfile">{{cite web |title=Munya Chawawa Wants To Help You Laugh, Think & Most Of All, Get It Right |url=https://www.bustle.com/entertainment/munya-chawawa-on-comedy |website=Bustle |language=en}}</ref> Chawawa has said that he "created Unknown P as a nod towards the appropriation and false ownership of black culture that often happens in western societies".<ref name="NMEPiers"/> His comedic influences are [[John Oliver]], [[Andy Zaltzman]] and [[Sacha Baron Cohen]].<ref name="nytimes.com"/>

His popularity grew during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] in 2020, with the main focus being his 2019 [[Charlie Sloth|Fire in the booth]] freestyle, for the [[parody]] news sketches he created.<ref name="ITV">{{cite web |title=Viral comedian talks parodying news and being black in Norfolk |url=https://www.itv.com/news/anglia/2020-06-10/viral-comedian-talks-parodying-news-and-being-black-in-norfolk |website=ITV News |language=en |date=10 June 2020}}</ref> In November 2020 [[Atlantic Records]] released his first single, "Piers Morgan". His second single, 'Pain Au Chocolat' was released in August 2021, as well as a Daily Duppy on [[GRM Daily]] in December 2020. He has collaborated with the likes of comedians [[Mo Gilligan]], [[Michael Dapaah]] and musicians [[Unknown T]], (of which he got the name Unknown P from) [[S1mba]], and [[KSI]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Piers Morgan responds to Munya Chawawa's hilarious single named after him |url=https://metro.co.uk/2020/11/28/piers-morgan-responds-to-munya-chawawas-hilarious-single-named-after-him-what-is-this-13671014/ |website=Metro |language=en |date=28 November 2020}}</ref><ref name="NMEPiers">{{cite web |title=Munya Chawawa's Unknown P shares debut single, 'Piers Morgan' |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/munya-chawawas-posh-drill-rapper-unknown-p-shares-debut-single-piers-morgan-2826084 |website=NME |date=26 November 2020}}</ref>
