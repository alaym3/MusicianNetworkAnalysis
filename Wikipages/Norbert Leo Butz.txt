'''Norbert Leo Butz''' (born January 30, 1967) is an American actor and singer. He is best known for his work in [[Broadway theatre]]. He is a two-time winner of the [[Tony Award for Best Actor in a Musical]], and is one of only nine actors ever to have won the award twice as lead actor.

==Personal life==
Butz was born in [[St. Louis, Missouri]], the son of Elaine (née Bourisaw) and Norbert Butz.<ref>{{cite web|title=Norbert Leo Butz Biography|website=filmreference|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/44/Norbert-Leo-Butz.html|year=2008|access-date=June 11, 2008}}</ref> He was raised in a middle-class family; his parents are devout Catholics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://stlouisreview.com/article/2012-05-03/eagle-hurst-ranch|title=Eagle Hurst Ranch provides a real family vacation {{!}} St. Louis Review|website=stlouisreview.com|language=en|access-date=2017-12-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/culture/norbert-leo-butz|title=Norbert Leo Butz and the Root of Evil - Interview Magazine|date=2012-11-27|work=Interview Magazine|access-date=2017-12-16|language=en-US}}</ref> He is the seventh of 11 children in his family and is named after his father.<ref>{{cite web|author=Nancy Rosati|title=Spotlight On Norbert Leo Butz|website=Talkin' Broadway|url=http://www.talkinbroadway.com/spot/nlb1.html|access-date=June 11, 2008}}</ref> Some of his first theatre roles included playing the male leads at local all-girl high schools, such as [[Cor Jesu Academy]] and [[Nerinx Hall High School|Nerinx Hall]]. He graduated from [[Bishop DuBourg High School]]. Butz earned a [[Bachelor of Fine Arts]] from The Conservatory of Theatre Arts at [[Webster University]] and a [[Master of Fine Arts]] from The [[University of Alabama]]/[[Alabama Shakespeare Festival]]'s Professional Actor Training Program.<ref>{{cite web|author=Laura Graesser|title=Norbert Leo Butz Q&A|website=Ladue News|url=http://www.laduenews.com/diversions/arts-entertainment/norbert-leo-butz-q-a/article_d38f9371-de28-52e8-8f5e-fba932299091.html|date=March 28, 2013|access-date=January 5, 2014}}</ref> He would later receive an honorary degree from Webster University in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://library.webster.edu/archives/historyfacts/honorarydegrees.html|title=Honorary Degrees at Webster University|access-date=October 15, 2018}}</ref>

The murder of his sister, Teresa Butz, made national news when an assailant stabbed both her and her partner in her Seattle-area home on July 19, 2009.<ref>{{cite web|last=Hetrick|first=Adam|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/131364-Catch_Me_If_You_Can_Previews_Cancelled_Through_July_26|title=Catch Me If You Can Previews Cancelled Through July 26|website=[[Playbill]]|date=July 22, 2009|access-date=July 26, 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090725121847/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/131364-Catch_Me_If_You_Can_Previews_Cancelled_Through_July_26|archive-date=July 25, 2009|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Green|first=Jesse|title=Big Fish Star Norbert Leo Butz on How Tragedy Changed His Life and Career|url=http://www.vulture.com/2013/09/norbert-leo-butz-on-big-fish.html|website=Vulture. New York Magazine|access-date=September 25, 2013|date=September 22, 2013}}</ref>

Butz's daughter Georgia Teresa was born on January 2, 2011. Butz has two older daughters, Clara and Maggie Davis, from a previous marriage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.broadway.com/shows/catch-me-if-you-can/buzz/154809/wicked-sweethearts-norbert-leo-butz-and-michelle-federer-welcome-baby-girl-georgia/|title='Wicked' Sweethearts Norbert Leo Butz and Michelle Federer Welcome Baby Girl Georgia|website=Broadway.com|date=January 3, 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
Butz made his Broadway debut as a replacement swing – a type of [[understudy]] – in ''[[Rent (musical)|Rent]]'' in 1996, after original swing David Driver was let go. Butz ultimately replaced [[Adam Pascal]] as Roger in 1997. Additional Broadway credits include ''[[Thou Shalt Not (musical)|Thou Shalt Not]]'' (Camille Raquin, 2001–2002), for which he received a [[Tony Award]] nomination; ''[[Wicked (musical)|Wicked]]'' (the original Fiyero, 2003); and ''[[Dirty Rotten Scoundrels (musical)|Dirty Rotten Scoundrels]]'' (Freddy) for which he received the [[Tony Award for Best Leading Actor in a Musical]], the [[Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Actor in a Musical]], a [[Drama League Award]], and an [[Outer Critics Circle Award]]. His [[Off-Broadway]] credits include ''[[The Last Five Years]]'' (Jamie), ''[[Songs for a New World]]'' (Lead Male 2), ''[[Saved (play)|Saved]]'' (Fred), and ''[[Juno and the Paycock]]'' (Jerry Devine), and he has toured as the Emcee in ''[[Cabaret (musical)|Cabaret]]'' and as Freddy in ''Dirty Rotten Scoundrels''.

Butz's film roles have included ''[[Went to Coney Island on a Mission from God... Be Back by Five]]'' (Pawnbroker), ''Noon Blue Apples'' (Howard Philips), and ''West of Here'' (Josiah Blackwell).

Butz's projects include the film ''[[Dan in Real Life]]'' (with [[Steve Carell]], [[Juliette Binoche]], and [[Dane Cook]]), released in October 2007, the world premiere of ''[[Is He Dead?]]'', a hitherto unproduced [[Mark Twain]] play that opened at Broadway's [[Lyceum Theatre (Broadway)|Lyceum Theatre]] on December 9, 2007, and ''Fifty Words'' Off-Broadway with Elizebeth Marvel at the Lucille Lortel Theatre (2008).<ref>[http://www.lortel.org/lla_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=5229 "'Fifty Words' Listing"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121023191802/http://www.lortel.org/lla_archive/index.cfm?search_by=show&id=5229 |date=2012-10-23 }} Internet Off-Broadway database, accessed November 20, 2011</ref> In January 2008, he appeared as Captain Richard King in the miniseries adaptation of the ''Lonesome Dove'' prequel, ''[[Comanche Moon (miniseries)|Comanche Moon]]''.

Starting December 23, 2008, Butz stepped in to replace [[Jeremy Piven]] in [[David Mamet]]'s ''[[Speed-the-Plow]]''; Piven suddenly and unexpectedly dropped out of the play after he experienced health problems. 
Butz took over the part until January 13, 2009, when [[William H. Macy]] assumed the role for the remainder of the play's run.<ref>[http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20247585,00.html "Jeremy Piven Abruptly Abandons Broadway Play"] ''People Magazine'', December 18, 2008</ref>

Butz taught at [[Drew University]], [[Madison, New Jersey]] for the spring semester in 2008 in the drama department.<ref>{{cite news | author=Robert Wnorowski | title=Broadway performer to teach at Drew | work=The Acorn | publisher=Drew University | url=http://media.www.drewacorn.com/media/storage/paper914/news/2007/11/30/ArtsAndLeisure/Broadway.Performer.To.Teach.At.Drew-3138918.shtml | date=30 November 2007 | access-date=2008-06-11 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081227095017/http://media.www.drewacorn.com/media/storage/paper914/news/2007/11/30/ArtsAndLeisure/Broadway.Performer.To.Teach.At.Drew-3138918.shtml | archive-date=27 December 2008 | url-status=dead | df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite news | author=Stacie MacLaughlin | title=Curtain rises for Broadway star's arrival | work=The Acorn | publisher=Drew University | url=http://media.www.drewacorn.com/media/storage/paper914/news/2008/02/22/ArtsAndLeisure/Curtain.Rises.For.Broadway.Stars.Arrival-3228777.shtml | date=22 February 2008 | access-date=2008-07-20 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081227095027/http://media.www.drewacorn.com/media/storage/paper914/news/2008/02/22/ArtsAndLeisure/Curtain.Rises.For.Broadway.Stars.Arrival-3228777.shtml | archive-date=27 December 2008 | url-status=dead | df=dmy-all }}</ref>

He starred as Rowdy Kaiser in the ABC show ''[[The Deep End (TV series)|The Deep End]]''.

From April to May 9, 2010, he returned to the Broadway stage in ''[[ENRON (play)|ENRON]]'' as [[Jeffrey Skilling]].<ref>[http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Norbert_Leo_Butz_to_Star_in_ENRON_on_Broadway_20100107 Tony-Winner Norbert Leo Butz to Star in ENRON on Broadway] broadwayworld.com, January 7, 2010</ref> Despite Tony nominations, the play struggled with ticket sales.

Butz starred in the 2011 drama indie film ''[[Higher Ground (film)|Higher Ground]]'' with Vera Farmiga, who also directed it.<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/news/ni2022854/ Listing] Internet MovieDatabase</ref>

Butz originated the role of Carl Hanratty in the musical ''[[Catch Me If You Can (musical)|Catch Me If You Can]]'' which played pre-Broadway tryouts at the [[5th Avenue Theatre]] in Seattle, Washington from July 28 through August 14, 2009.<ref>[http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Tveit_and_Butz_Reveal_CATCH_ME_Casting_Musical_Premieres_at_Seattles_5th_Ave_723814_20090310 "Tveit and Butz Reveal 'CATCH ME' Casting, Musical Premieres at Seattle's 5th Ave 7/23-8/14"]</ref> Butz played the role of Carl Hanratty in the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] production of ''Catch Me if You Can'', which opened on April 10, 2011<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/news/article/144808-Norbert-Leo-Butz-Aaron-Tveit-Kerry-Butler-and-Tom-Wopat-Set-for-Broadways-Catch-Me-If-You-Can "Norbert Leo Butz, Aaron Tveit, Kerry Butler and Tom Wopat Set for Broadway's 'Catch Me If You Can' "] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101112182229/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/144808-Norbert-Leo-Butz-Aaron-Tveit-Kerry-Butler-and-Tom-Wopat-Set-for-Broadways-Catch-Me-If-You-Can |date=2010-11-12 }} playbill.com</ref> and closed in September 2011. For this role he won his second [[Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Actor in a Musical]]<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/news/article/151111-Drama-Desk-Awards-Go-to-Book-of-Mormon-Normal-Heart-War-Horse-Sutton-Foster-Norbert-Leo-Butz Drama Desk Awards Go to Book of Mormon, Normal Heart, War Horse, Sutton Foster, Norbert Leo Butz] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110629120949/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/151111-Drama-Desk-Awards-Go-to-Book-of-Mormon-Normal-Heart-War-Horse-Sutton-Foster-Norbert-Leo-Butz |date=2011-06-29 }}</ref> and his second [[Tony Award for Best Actor in a Musical]].<ref>Jones, Kenneth [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/151725-War-Horse-Book-of-Mormon-Anything-Goes-Normal-Heart-Win-2011-Tony-Awards "War Horse, Book of Mormon, Anything Goes, Normal Heart Win 2011 Tony Awards"] playbill.com, June 12, 2011</ref>

In April 2012, Butz appeared as himself in one episode of the NBC musical drama [[Smash (U.S. TV series)|''Smash'']]. Butz played [[Hal Wilner]] in ''[[Greetings from Tim Buckley]]'', a film on [[Tim Buckley|Tim]] and [[Jeff Buckley]], which premiered at the [[2012 Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Tony Winners Norbert Leo Butz and Frank Wood Join Penn Badgley in Greetings from Tim Buckley|url=http://www.broadway.com/buzz/157416/tony-winners-norbert-leo-butz-and-frank-wood-join-penn-badgley-in-greetings-from-tim-buckley/|publisher=Broadway.com|access-date=October 2, 2012|date=August 22, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Kurchak|first=Sarah|title=TIFF 2012 Adds Greetings From Tim Buckley, Spike Lee's Bad 25 Michael Jackson Doc, Skrillex Soundtrack|url=http://www.spinner.com/2012/08/15/tiff-2012-greetings-from-tim-buckley-spike-lee/|work=[[Spinner (website)|Spinner]]|access-date=October 2, 2012|date=August 15, 2012}}</ref>

From November 29, 2012 to January 6, 2013, Butz appeared on Broadway in [[Theresa Rebeck]]'s ''[[Dead Accounts]]'', opposite [[Katie Holmes]], [[Jayne Houdyshell]], [[Josh Hamilton (actor)|Josh Hamilton]], and [[Judy Greer]].

In 2013 he starred in the new [[Andrew Lippa]] musical ''[[Big Fish (musical)|Big Fish]]'', which premiered in Chicago in the spring and opened on Broadway in the Neil Simon Theatre in October, directed by [[Susan Stroman]].<ref>[[Simonson, Robert]]. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/169801-PLAYBILL-THEATRE-WEEK-IN-REVIEW-Sept-1-7-Hal-David-Willy-Wonka-Flashdance-on-Broadway "PLAYBILL THEATRE WEEK IN REVIEW, Sept. 1-7: Hal David, Willy Wonka, Flashdance on Broadway"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120918045011/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/169801-PLAYBILL-THEATRE-WEEK-IN-REVIEW-Sept-1-7-Hal-David-Willy-Wonka-Flashdance-on-Broadway |date=2012-09-18 }} Playbill.com, September 7, 2012</ref>

In 2012, he played Uncle Peck in a limited engagement revival of [[Paula Vogel]]'s play ''[[How I Learned to Drive]]''. He also starred in the 2013 film ''[[Better Living Through Chemistry (film)|Better Living Through Chemistry]]''. He played Kevin Rayburn in the 2015 [[Netflix]] television show ''[[Bloodline (TV series)|Bloodline]]''.

He also starred in the 2018 Broadway revival of ''[[My Fair Lady]]'' as Alfred Doolittle, the father of the leading role, Eliza Doolittle. He earned a nomination for [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actor in a Musical]] for the production.

From July 9–28, 2019, he appeared in an original musical collaboration titled TWOHANDER at [[Feinstein's/54 Below]] alongside [[Sherie Rene Scott]], with musical director Todd Almond.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://54below.com/events/sherie-rene-scott-norbert-leo-butz-project/|title=Sherie Rene Scott & Norbert Leo Butz: TWOHANDER|website=Feinstein's/54 Below|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-04}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/07/08/theater/sherie-rene-scott-norbert-leo-butz-twohander.html|title=They Didn't Speak for Nearly a Decade. Then They Made a Show About It.|last=Soloski|first=Alexis|date=2019-07-08|work=The New York Times|access-date=2020-02-04|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Butz previously shared the stage with Scott in the original productions of the musicals ''[[Dirty Rotten Scoundrels (musical)|Dirty Rotten Scoundrels]]'' and ''[[The Last Five Years]]''.

In 2021, Butz plays the role of Craig Maddox in the [[NBC]] drama series ''[[Debris (TV series)|Debris]]'' which is written by [[J. H. Wyman]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2020/02/debris-norbert-leo-butz-cast-nbc-sci-fi-drama-pilot-1202862802/|title='Debris': Norbert Leo Butz To Co-Star In NBC Sci-Fi Drama Pilot|work=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|date=February 18, 2020|access-date=February 18, 2020|first=Nellie|last=Andreeva}}</ref>
