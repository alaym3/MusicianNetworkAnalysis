'''Kimiko Elizabeth Glenn''' (born June 27, 1989) is an American actress and Broadway performer known for portraying Brook Soso in the [[Netflix]] series ''[[Orange Is the New Black]]'', for which she received three ensemble [[Screen Actors Guild Award|Screen Actors Guild Awards]]. She also starred as Dawn Pinkett in the Broadway musical ''[[Waitress (musical)|Waitress]]'' and has provided the voices of Ezor in ''[[Voltron: Legendary Defender]]'', Lena Sabrewing in ''[[DuckTales (2017 TV series)|DuckTales]]'', [[Peni Parker]] in ''[[Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse]]'', Stefani Stilton in ''[[BoJack Horseman]]'', Bridgette Yoshida in ''[[Close Enough]]'', Horse in ''[[Centaurworld]]'' and Izzy Moonbow in ''[[My Little Pony: A New Generation]]''.

==Early life and education==
Glenn was born and raised in [[Phoenix, Arizona]], with her sister, Amanda. Her mother, Sumiko, is [[Japanese people|Japanese]], and her father, Mark, is of [[Scottish people|Scottish]], [[Irish people|Irish]], and [[Germany|German]] descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2014/06/18/orange-is-the-new-black-s-kimiko-glenn-on-hippie-brook-soso-and-chapel-sex-with-natasha-lyonne.html|title=Orange Is the New Black's Kimiko Glenn on Hippie Brook Soso and Chapel Sex with Natasha Lyonne|website=[[The Daily Beast]]|first=Marlow|last=Stern|date=June 18, 2014}}</ref> Glenn began acting at the [[Valley Youth Theatre]] in Phoenix and several other local theaters when she was in [[fifth grade]].<ref name="AZCentral">{{cite web|url=https://www.azcentral.com/story/entertainment/television/2014/06/18/arizona-actor-kimiko-glenn-orange-new-black/10648115/|title=Arizona actor Kimiko Glenn in 'Orange Is the New Black'|website=[[The Arizona Republic]]|first=Kerry|last=Langel|date=June 19, 2014}}</ref> She attended [[Desert Vista High School]] in Phoenix and the [[Interlochen Center for the Arts|Interlochen Arts Academy]] boarding school in [[Interlochen, Michigan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ahwatukee.com/arts_life/article_c34e37a8-834d-11e1-a545-001a4bcf887a.html|title=Ahwatukee native kicks off premiere of Valley Youth Theatre musical|website=[[Ahwatukee Foothills News]]|first=Arselia|last=Gales|date=April 11, 2012}}</ref> She attended the [[Boston Conservatory]] for a year as a musical theatre major, but dropped out when she was cast in the touring company of ''[[Spring Awakening (musical)|Spring Awakening]]''.<ref name="AZCentral" />

==Career==
During her freshman year of college, in 2008, Glenn was cast as Thea in the first [[U.S.]] national tour of [[Steven Sater]]'s and [[Duncan Sheik]]'s rock musical ''[[Spring Awakening (musical)|Spring Awakening]]''. In 2013, she booked her breakthrough role as Litchfield Penitentiary inmate Brook Soso in the [[Netflix]] comedy-drama series ''[[Orange Is the New Black]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2013/08/sleepy-hollow-we-are-men-tomorrow-people-orange-add-recurring-569179/|title='Sleepy Hollow', 'We Are Men', 'Tomorrow People' & 'Orange' Add Recurring|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|first=Nellie|last=Andreeva|date=August 21, 2013}}</ref> for which she received [[Screen Actors Guild Awards]] in 2014, 2015, and 2016 for [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series|Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series]].

In 2014, she appeared in the [[Lena Dunham]]-directed music video "[[I Wanna Get Better]]" for [[Jack Antonoff]]'s solo project [[Bleachers (band)|Bleachers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cosmopolitan.com/entertainment/celebs/news/a7186/orange-is-the-new-black-brook-soso-kimiko-glenn-interview/|title=Orange Is the New Black's Kimiko Glenn Talks Brook Soso|website=[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]]|first=Clover|last=Hope|date=June 13, 2014|access-date=February 13, 2016}}</ref>

Glenn played the supporting role of Liv Kurosawa in the drama-thriller film ''[[Nerve (2016 film)|Nerve]]'' (2016), directed by [[Henry Joost]] and [[Ariel Schulman]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2015/04/kimiko-glenn-nerve-married-kino-lorber-gueros-1201412035/|title='Married's Kimiko Glenn Joins 'Nerve'; Kino Lorber Acquires 'Gueros'|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|first1=Erik|last1=Pedersen|first2=Amanda|last2=N'Duka|date=April 17, 2015}}</ref> and based on the young adult [[Nerve (Ryan novel)|novel of the same name]]. In 2016, she was cast as Dawn Pinkett in the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] transfer production of [[Sara Bareilles]]'s and [[Jessie Nelson (filmmaker)|Jessie Nelson]]'s musical ''[[Waitress (musical)|Waitress]]''. Preview performances began on March 25, 2016, at the [[Brooks Atkinson Theatre]], and the show officially opened on April 24.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2015/legit/news/waitress-musical-kimiko-glenn-orange-is-the-new-black-1201634490/|title='Waitress' Musical Adds Kimiko Glenn of 'Orange is the New Black'|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|first=Gordon|last=Cox|date=January 12, 2016}}</ref>

In 2018, Glenn began starring as Harlow in the comedy Web television series ''[[Liza on Demand]]'', with Travis Coles and creator [[Liza Koshy]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Meskhi|first=Megi|date=19 September 2019|title=Who Is Harlow On 'Liza On Demand'? Kimiko Glenn's Character Is Perfection|url=https://www.elitedaily.com/p/who-is-harlow-on-liza-on-demand-kimiko-glenns-character-is-perfection-18784890|url-status=live|access-date=22 January 2021|website=Elite Daily}}</ref> In 2019, she was a guest presenter with [[Nev Schulman]] on the MTV show ''[[Catfish: The TV Show|Catfish]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Debler|first=Lance|date=24 July 2019|title=Matthew's Catfish May Have Been The Most 'Sophisticated, Convincing' Con Yet|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3125647/matthew-chance-sophisticated-catfish/|url-status=live|access-date=22 January 2021|website=MTV News}}</ref>

In 2021, Glenn provided the voice of Izzy Moonbow in the Netflix animated film ''[[My Little Pony: A New Generation]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=D'Alessandro |first1=Anthony |title=Netflix's Animated 'My Little Pony' Movie Gets A Title And A Release Date; Voice Cast Revealed – Update |url=https://deadline.com/2021/06/eone-sells-my-little-pony-feature-to-netflix-1234692956 |website=Deadline |access-date=July 1, 2021 |date=June 30, 2021}}</ref>

Glenn is a [[Pescetarianism|pescatarian]].<ref>{{cite web|date=June 15, 2015|title=OITNB returns: Attitude meets Laura Prepon & Kimiko Glenn|url=https://attitude.co.uk/article/oitnb-returns-attitude-meets-laura-prepon-kimiko-glenn/6944/|access-date=November 30, 2019|website=[[Attitude (website)|Attitude]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|last=Wei|first=Christine|date=October 7, 2014|title=Litchfield Lessons: OITNB's Kimiko Glenn On What She's Learned From Brook Soso|url=https://www.mochimag.com/mochi-magazine/orange-is-the-new-black-brook-soso-kimiko-glenn-actress-interview|magazine=[[Mochi (magazine)|Mochi]]|access-date=November 30, 2019}}</ref>
