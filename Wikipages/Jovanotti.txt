'''Lorenzo Cherubini''' ({{IPA-it|loˈrɛntso keruˈbiːni}}; born 27 September 1966), better known as '''Jovanotti''' ({{IPA-it|dʒovaˈnɔtti|}}), is an Italian singer-songwriter and [[rapper]].

The name Jovanotti derives from ''giovanotti'', the plural form of the Italian word ''giovanotto'' ("young man"). Cherubini initially chose "Joe Vanotti" as his stage name, but a promotional poster for a night club incorrectly billed him as "Jovanotti" and the name stuck. (The spelling ''Jovanotti'' is anglicized; the letter J is rarely used in Italian) Jovanotti is commonly known to his fans by the diminutive form "Jova", and often refers to himself that way in promotional items and on his Web TV channel Jova.TV, which launched 2 October 2014.

Jovanotti gradually departed from his early mix of [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], rap and [[disco]], taking in [[funk]], [[world music]] and even classical arrangements and [[ska]] influences. As his musical influences changed, so did his lyrics too, which over time began to increasingly address philosophical, religious and political issues, which are more typical of the Italian [[cantautore]] tradition. His social and political commitment increased as well. Some of his earlier work is also closer to keyboard-heavy 1980s pop.

Most of his songs are sung in Italian; he also released a Spanish-language greatest hits album. His live album includes a short version of the [[Sugarhill Gang]]'s "[[Rapper's Delight]]."  Jovanotti appears on several international compilations, most notably  ''Red Hot + Rhapsody'', a 1998 tribute to [[George Gershwin]], on which he performed "I Got Rhythm". Jovanotti also appeared in one of [[Luciano Pavarotti]]'s charity concerts in 1996.

== Biography ==
=== Early life ===
Cherubini was born on 27 September 1966 in [[Rome]] to [[Tuscany|Tuscan]] parents, Mario and Viola Cardinali, but he spent part of his childhood in his parents' hometown, [[Cortona]], in the [[province of Arezzo]] ([[Tuscany]]).<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://firenze.repubblica.it/multimedia/home/429270/45/1 | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it| access-date =7 October 2011 | title = Buon compleanno Jovanotti! – Fotogalleria – Foto 1 di 11 | date = 27 September 2006}}</ref>

After completing his [[high-school]] studies, he started working as a disc jockey in several [[discothèques]] and radio stations in the province of Rome.<ref name=Wuz>{{Cite web | url = http://www.wuz.it/biografia/1321/jovanotti.html | publisher = Wuz.it | language = it | access-date = 7 October 2011 | title = Jovanotti – La biografia di Jovanotti}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url = http://firenze.repubblica.it/multimedia/home/429270/45/2 | work = [[la Repubblica]] | access-date = 7 October 2011 | title = Buon compleanno Jovanotti! – Fotogalleria – Foto 1 di 11 | date = 27 September 2006 | language = it}}</ref> 
In 1985 he moved to [[Milan]].<ref name=Wuz/> Two years later, during the summer of 1987, while he was playing a [[DJ set]] in [[Palinuro]], he met the record producer and [[talent scout]] [[Claudio Cecchetto]], who proposed they begin working together.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://musica.it.msn.com/fotogallery/foto.aspx?cp-documentid=151149264&page=2 | publisher = [[MSN]] | language = it | access-date = 7 October 2011 | title = L'incontro con Cecchetto | date = 2 February 2011 | archive-url = https://www.webcitation.org/6B1zGWrWZ?url=http://musica.it.msn.com/fotogallery/foto.aspx?cp-documentid=151149264&page=2 | archive-date = 29 September 2012 | url-status = dead }}</ref> That same year Jovanotti released his first single, "Walking," and started working as a DJ for one of the most prominent national radio stations, [[Radio DeeJay]],<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://firenze.repubblica.it/multimedia/home/429270/45/3 | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it| date = 27 September 2006 | title = Buon compleanno Jovanotti! – Fotogalleria – Foto 3 di 11}}</ref> which was founded by Cecchetto five years earlier.

=== Career breakthrough: 1980s and 1990s ===
His first studio album, ''[[Jovanotti for President]]'', was released in 1988 and became the symbol of the Italian youth of the eighties, without any interest in [[Ideology|ideologies]] and politics.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2002/04/21/jovanotti-la-musica-impegno.html | work = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it | date = 21 April 2002 | author = Tummino Titti | title = Jovanotti, la musica e l'impegno}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2005/dicembre/27/Temevo_altro_flop_futuro_Potrei_co_9_051227112.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | date = 27 December 2005 | title = Temevo un altro flop. Il futuro? Potrei fare tv | author = Andrea Laffranchi}}</ref> Despite receiving strongly negative criticism,<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.eventiverona.it/scaricapdf.asp?FileName=buon_sangue_tour_2005.pdf | publisher = www.eventiverona.it | format = PDF | language = it | access-date = 7 October 2011 | title = Buon sangue tour 2005 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20120425052031/http://www.eventiverona.it/scaricapdf.asp?FileName=buon_sangue_tour_2005.pdf | archive-date = 25 April 2012 }}</ref> the album became a big commercial success, reaching the third position on the Italian ''[[Musica e dischi]]'' Albums Chart<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.hitparadeitalia.it/hp_yenda/lpe1988.htm| publisher = www.hitpadadeitalia.it | language = it | access-date = 7 October 2011 | title = Gli album più venduti del 1988}}</ref> and selling over 400,000 copies in Italy.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/1994/aprile/16/Jovanotti_sposta_Palaeur_co_10_940416615.shtml | work = [[Corriere della Sera]] | date = 16 April 1994 | title = Jovanotti si sposta al Palaeur | access-date = 7 October 2011}}</ref> Moreover, the album spawned five singles, including the number-one hits, "Gimme Five" (also the name of his British TV show for Janet Street Porter's [[DEF II]] youth TV block on [[BBC Two]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=TVARK Media Player|url=https://www.tvark.org/?page=media&mediaid=50325|access-date=2021-03-08|website=www.tvark.org}}</ref>) and "Gimme Five (Rasta Five)" and the songs "Go Jovanotti Go" and "Mix, Remix & The singers," which entered the Italian top 5.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.hitparadeitalia.it/indici/per_interprete/aj.htm | publisher = hitparadeitalia.it | language = it | access-date = 7 October 2011 | title = Indice per artista: J}}</ref>

In 1998, Jovanotti played a brief role in the movie I Giardini dell'Eden, directed by Alessandro D'Alatri.

The song "Piove" was released for the second season of ''[[The Sopranos]]'' as an ending credits track.

[[File:Jova MTVday 2007 - DSC 6874-5 c.jpg|thumb|Jovanotti, 2007]]

=== 2000s ===
His 2005 CD, ''Buon Sangue'', was influenced by both rock and old school hip hop. It is one of his most innovative works to date. [[Edoardo Bennato]] collaborated on one track, while bass player [[Saturnino (musician)|Saturnino]] co-wrote two of the songs.

In 2007 he was featured at the end of [[Negramaro]]'s song, "Cade la Pioggia," of their CD, ''La Finestra'', and in 2008 he released the new album ''Safari''.

In 2008 he performed as guest artist on the track "Lugar Comum" from the album ''[[Encanto (Sérgio Mendes album)|Encanto]]''. At the end of 2009, he collaborated with [[Claudio Baglioni]] and Fabrizio Bosso, along with 70 other artists, in the song "Con tutto il mio cuore (with All My Heart)." On 1 January 2010 the group released "Baciami Ancora (Kiss Me Again)" for the soundtrack of a film by [[Gabriele Muccino]].

=== 2010s ===
In December 2010 he achieved a number one hit on the Italian Top 20 charts with "Tutto l'amore che ho." On 25 January 2011, he released his eighteenth album ''[[Ora (Jovanotti album)|Ora]]''. Later in 2011 he played at the [[Bonnaroo Music Festival]].

== Commitment ==
Along with the evolution of his sound, his social and political commitment for mainstream causes has increased since the 1990s. As a convinced pacifist, he frequently worked with organizations such as [[Make Poverty History]], [[Amnesty International]], and he has contributed to events dedicated to debt relief ([[Global Call to Action Against Poverty]]).

Jovanotti has a friendship with [[Bono]] due to their mutual quest for the cancellation of foreign debt in poor African countries. He also collaborated with [[Michael Franti]] of Spearhead on his ''Capo Horn'' and ''[[Ora (Jovanotti album)|Ora]]'' albums. Both Michael Franti and [[U2]] misspelled his name as Giovanotti in the liner note credits.

== Television ==
Even though Jovanotti has never had a UK chart hit under his own name, he was briefly known in the United Kingdom as the presenter of the [[DEF II]] programme ''Gimme 5''. The show, which featured a brief introduction each week by Jovanotti, showcased youth television programmes from around the world.

In 2020 he released exclusively for [[RaiPlay]] the documentary in sixteen episodes "Non voglio cambiare Pianeta" ''(I don't want to change Planet)'' which he filmed whilst travelling from Chile to Argentina on his bicycle, between January and February 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ragone|first=Martina|date=2020-05-25|title=Non voglio cambiare pianeta|url=https://www.ilmessaggeroitaliano.it/news/non-voglio-cambiare-pianeta/|access-date=2020-11-13|website=Il Messaggero Italiano|language=it-IT}}</ref> 

== Gino Latino ==
Jovanotti was behind one of two rival Gino Latino [[italo house]] tracks in the early 1990s. His Gino Latino single, "Welcome", reached number 17 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].

== Personal life ==
On 6 September 2008, Jovanotti married Francesca Valiani at Cortona, in the Church of Santa Maria Nuova. Their daughter Teresa was born in 1998. He dedicated the lullaby "Per te" to Teresa, one of the hits of the album ''Capo Horn'' (1999), and the song "Libera" from the album ''Lorenzo 2015 cc''.<ref>[http://www.gossip.it/news/jovanotti-figlia-teresa-news.html ''Jovanotti insieme alla figlia Teresa, 'tali e quali', cinguetta mamma Francesca Valiani''] (Italian)</ref>
