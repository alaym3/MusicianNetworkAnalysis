'''Josephine Eusebio Constantino-Asuncion''' (born December 4, 1988) is a [[Filipinos|Filipina]] [[pop rock]] singer-songwriter, occasional actress <!--Yeng only acts in films, not on television--> and host. She is referred to as the ''Philippines Pop Rock Royalty''<ref>{{Cite web|last=PH|first=One Music|title=10 Most Remarkable Things About Pop Rock Royalty Yeng Constantino|url=https://www.onemusic.ph/news/yeng-10-895|access-date=2021-07-09|website=One Music PH|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Kalaw|first=Bea|title=Lie Reposposa soars in exceptional run as 'Pinoy Big Belter' in Your Face Sounds Familiar 3 {{!}} ABS-CBN Entertainment|url=https://ent.abs-cbn.com/yourfaces3/articles-news/lie-reposposa-soars-in-exceptional-run-as-pinoy-big-belter-in-your-face-sounds-familiar-3-14911|access-date=2021-07-09|website=ent.abs-cbn.com|language=EN}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=ABS-CBN.com|title=The voice teens collab with Pop Rock royalty Yeng Constantino {{!}} ABS-CBN Entertainment|url=https://ent.abs-cbn.com/asap/videos/16091017-the-voice-teens-collab-with-pop-rock-royalty-yeng-constantino-139105|access-date=2021-07-09|website=ent.abs-cbn.com|language=en}}</ref> and is regarded as one of the biggest hitmakers in Philippine music industry. In 2006, she won the title "Grand Star Dreamer" in the [[Pinoy Dream Academy (season 1)|inaugural season]] of ''[[Pinoy Dream Academy]]'', the Philippine edition of [[Endemol]]'s reality TV show ''[[Star Academy]]''. Her debut album titled ''Salamat'' became the biggest selling album of her career being 3× Platinum in the Philippines and produced five #1 hits and six top 10 hits, the most by any female artist.<ref>{{Cite web|last=PH|first=One Music|title=Yeng Constantino Albums, Ranked!|url=https://www.onemusic.ph/news/yeng-constantino-albums-ranked-935|access-date=2021-07-08|website=One Music PH|language=en}}</ref> Constantino has sold an estimated of over 400,000 copies of her albums in the Philippines. ''Metro Society'' listed her as one of the most influential in social media.<ref>{{Cite web|last=News|first=ABS-CBN|date=2018-03-04|title=Anne, Vice, Angel lead Metro Society's most influential list|url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/03/04/18/anne-vice-angel-lead-metro-societys-most-influential-list|access-date=2021-07-10|website=ABS-CBN News|language=en}}</ref>

''Metro Style'' listed her as one of the most influential social media personalities in the Philippines.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Times|first=The Manila|date=2018-03-04|title=Anne Curtis, Marian Rivera lead most influential PH celebrities on social media, says magazine|url=https://www.manilatimes.net/2018/03/04/latest-stories/breakingnews/anne-curtis-marian-rivera-lead-influential-ph-showbiz-personalities-social-media-says-magazine/384031|access-date=2021-07-08|website=The Manila Times|language=en}}</ref> She is also one of the most followed female Filipino celebrities on Instagram as of 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=25 Most-followed Pinay Celebrities on Instagram in 2020|url=https://www.pep.ph/lifestyle/lifestyle/142575/most-followed-pinay-celebrities-on-instagram-2019-edition-a722-20190421-lfrm|access-date=2021-07-08|website=PEP.ph|language=en}}</ref> [[CNN Philippines]] listed the iconic hit [[Hawak Kamay]] at number 12 as one of the Best Filipino Love Songs of the last 25 years.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The 25 best Filipino love songs of the last 25 years|url=https://cnnphilippines.com/life/entertainment/Music/2019/2/7/filipino-love-songs.html|access-date=2021-07-08|website=cnn|language=en}}</ref> Yeng also starred in a film [[Write About Love (film)|Write About Love]] and took home the Best Supporting Actress Award in [[Metro Manila Film Festival|MMFF]] 2019, an affirmation of her acting prowess.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yeng Constantino: Pop Rock Princess|url=https://www.pep.ph/peptionary/148840/yeng-constantino-a738-20200123-lfrm|access-date=2021-07-08|website=PEP.ph|language=en}}</ref> As of July 2021, Yeng has over 292 million career streams on Spotify, becoming one of the Most Streamed Filipino artists of all time and has over 32 million accumulated views on her official YouTube channel with over a million subscribers as well. Her timeless hit ''Ikaw'' is also listed as the second most viewed OPM music video by a Female Filipino solo artist and 6th overall as of May 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=TEAM|first=PUSH|title=Yeng Constantino's 'Ikaw' reaches 100 million views on YouTube|url=https://push.abs-cbn.com/2020/6/6/fresh-scoops/yeng-constantinos-ikaw-reaches-100-million-view-27627|access-date=2021-07-08|website=Push|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==

===2006–07: ''Pinoy Dream Academy'', career beginnings and ''Hawak Kamay''===
On August 27, 2006, Yeng Constantino auditioned for the [[Pinoy Dream Academy (season 1)|inaugural season]] of the singing competition ''[[Pinoy Dream Academy]]''. In December, Constantino won the competition after garnering 697,648 votes (37.32%), becoming the first "Grand Star Dreamer", followed by [[Jay-R Siaboc]] as the first runner-up.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

After winning, Star Music launched Constantino's debut album entitled ''[[Salamat (album)|Salamat]]'', containing 10 songs, seven of which were original compositions by Constantino. Her album reached Gold Record status two weeks after its release and then later went Platinum after a month, followed by a "3x Platinum Record" certification several months later.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

After the success of her multi-platinum debut album, Star Music produced Constantino's second album. On February 28, 2008, her second album, ''[[Journey (Yeng Constantino album)|Journey]]'' was launched. The carrier single is "Ikaw Lang Talaga". The album contains 12 tracks, some of which were written by Yeng herself. The album reached Gold status after several months.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

===2009–12: ''Lapit'' and ''Yeng Versions Live''===
''[[Lapit]]'', her third studio album, was released digitally via Star Records' official website on October 9, 2009, then later released in physical format in music stores in the Philippines; a week later, the album was pulled due to some technical errors in the album tracklisting and was re-released before its grand album launch on November 1, 2009. ''Lapit'' went on to receive Platinum status.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

In March 2010, Constantino began her first regular television hosting stint as co-host of ''[[Music Uplate Live]]'', a late-night musical variety show, along with Tutti Caringal, Gee Canlas, and Martin Concio.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Franco|first1=Bernie|title=Yeng Constantino is ready to host Music Uplate Live|url=http://www2.abs-cbn.com/Feature/Article/6876/Yeng-Constantino-is-ready-to-host-Music-Uplate-Live.aspx|access-date=December 29, 2015|publisher=[[ABS-CBN Corporation]]|date=March 11, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304205749/http://www2.abs-cbn.com/Feature/Article/6876/Yeng-Constantino-is-ready-to-host-Music-Uplate-Live.aspx|archive-date=March 4, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> The show aired on [[ABS-CBN]] until September 2011.

On October 5, 2011, Star Records released her first live album, entitled ''[[Yeng Versions Live]]'', on which she sings 80's and 90's OPM hits. It was commercially successful and achieved Platinum status shortly after its release.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

===2013: Transformation and ''Metamorphosis''===
She released her fourth studio album entitled ''[[Metamorphosis (Yeng Constantino album)|Metamorphosis]]'' on January 28, 2013. Its first single, "B.A.B.A.Y", was released to radio stations on January 9. The music video for the second single, "Chinito", premiered on May 24 on [[Myx|Myx Channel]]. It features Filipino actor [[Enchong Dee]] as Constantino's love interest. The third single, "Sandata", was released on July 3, 2013.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

In November 2013, Yeng released her international single "Better Than Yesterday," a pop song featuring prominent drums and rap courtesy of Singaporean rapper, actor and radio personality Sheikh Haikel. The track was produced by Shorya Sharma.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

The album reached Gold in December 2014.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

===2014–15: ''All About Love'' and ''The Voice Kids''===
She released her fifth album, ''[[All About Love (Yeng Constantino album)|All About Love]]'', which was her attempt to tackle new genres beyond her pop-rock image.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://myxph.com/features/21122/yeng-constantino-launches-her-6th-album-all-about-love/|title=Yeng Constantino launches her 6th album "All About Love"|publisher=myx.ph|date=November 28, 2014}}</ref> It was released on iTunes on October 30, 2014, and on CD on November 27, 2014.<ref name="abs-cbnnews">{{cite news|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/11/27/14/bride-be-yeng-constantino-releases-love-album|title=Bride-to-be Yeng Constantino releases 'love' album|work=ABS-CBN News|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> The music video for the first single, "Ikaw", premiered on August 30 on [[Myx|Myx Channel]], which also served as a prenuptial with her then-fiancé Yan Asuncion.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://rp1.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/09/04/14/story-behind-yengs-emotional-music-video|title=The story behind Yeng's emotional music video|work=ABS-CBN News|date=September 5, 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150220215231/http://rp1.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/09/04/14/story-behind-yengs-emotional-music-video|archive-date=February 20, 2015}}</ref>{{ clarify | date = October 2015 | reason = To a lot of international English readers, a prenuptial suggests a prenuptial agreement, which I suspect is not what was intended here.}} On December 13, 2015, the album was awarded with Gold and Platinum awards on ASAP for reaching 15,000 sold copies.

In May 2015, Constantino announced that she would co-host the [[The Voice Kids (Philippines season 2)|second season]] of ''[[The Voice Kids (Philippine TV series)|The Voice Kids]]'', alongside [[Luis Manzano]] and her cousin [[Robi Domingo]], replacing [[Alex Gonzaga]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Yeng joins Voice Kids as host|url=http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2015/05/21/1456911/yeng-joins-voice-kids-host|access-date=December 29, 2015|work=The Philippine Star|date=May 21, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160118184258/http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2015/05/21/1456911/yeng-joins-voice-kids-host|archive-date=January 18, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="musical">{{cite news|last1=Jimenez|first1=Joyce|title=Yeng Constantino denies 'upstaging' Toni, Alex|url=http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2015/06/06/1462887/yeng-constantino-denies-upstaging-toni-alex|access-date=December 29, 2015|work=The Philippine Star|date=June 6, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160105164244/http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2015/06/06/1462887/yeng-constantino-denies-upstaging-toni-alex|archive-date=January 5, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> The season aired on ABS-CBN from June 6 to August 30, 2015.

===2016: Tenth year celebrations===
In 2016, Constantino was announced to be one of the mentors of the reality show [[We Love OPM]]: The Celebrity Sing-Offs, which ran from May 14 – July 17, 2016. Her team is named Oh My Girls, which was composed of [[Ylona Garcia]], Krissha Viaje and [[Alexa Ilacad]]. Oh My Girls placed second overall throughout the entire show's run.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

In May 2015, Constantino announced plans to release a musical in 2016 that would feature her discography from the past years to celebrate her tenth year in the music industry.<ref name="musical"/> It was titled Ako Si Josephine.

Later in 2016, Constantino joined the judging panel of [[ABS-CBN Corporation|ABS-CBN]]'s reality singing competition show [[Pinoy Boyband Superstar]], along with [[Vice Ganda]], [[Aga Muhlach]] and [[Sandara Park]].{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

===DIVAS===
On April 27, 2016, Constantino was announced to be part of the girl group DIVAS, along with [[Kyla (Filipino singer)|Kyla]], [[KZ Tandingan]] and [[Angeline Quinto]] ([[Rachelle Ann Go]] was originally going to also be part of the group, but left due to international commitments).{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

On November 11, 2016, the group staged their first concert, entitled DIVAS Live in Manila, at the [[Smart Araneta Coliseum]].{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

In 2018, the group held their second concert at the [[Smart Araneta Coliseum]] on December 15, 2018, with [[Boyz II Men]], entitled Boyz II Men with DIVAS.{{Cn|date=October 2021}}

===2018 - ''Synesthesia''===
In 2018, Constantino released her sixth studio album titled Synesthesia. The album spawned four singles "Pinipigil" and the hit single "Ako Muna" the song is a pop ballad and an emotional and self-empowering anthem. Constantino performed the song live on Wish 107.5 bus and it earned 7.2 million views on YouTube and she also performed the song in It's Showtime,<ref>{{Citation|title=Yeng Constantino performs "Ako Muna" LIVE on Wish 107.5 Bus|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eB8N5ugzHw8|language=en|access-date=2021-10-09}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=It's Showtime: Yeng Constantino performs "Ako Muna" and "Chinito"|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wwcs9CSRvLM|language=en|access-date=2021-10-09}}</ref> the song became a radio hit in the Philippines and peaked at number six on the Myx Daily Top Ten music videos chart, and number ten on the MOR Pinoy Biga10 radio chart, the song also played in the radio in the United Kingdom with six spin and in the Sweden, the music video of "Ako Muna" was released on March 2018 on YouTube the music video was directed by Miguel Alomajan, it earned 9.8 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ako Muna|url=https://www.top-charts.com/s/ako-muna-yeng-constantino|access-date=2021-10-09|website=www.top-charts.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Ako Muna - Yeng Constantino (Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1-UfxPh7Cqw|language=en|access-date=2021-10-09}}</ref> Two more singles on the album released:"Tahimik" and "Dasal."

===Influences===
Her early influences include Canadian superstars [[Alanis Morissette]] and [[Avril Lavigne]]; country music singers [[LeAnn Rimes]], [[Martina McBride]] and [[Trisha Yearwood]]; pop punk/rock bands [[the Ataris]], [[Blink 182]], [[Sum 41]], [[The Starting Line]], [[Coheed and Cambria]], [[Yellowcard]] and [[Sandwich (band)|Sandwich]]; and Filipina rock singers [[Acel Bisa]], [[Kitchie Nadal]] and [[Barbie Almalbis]].<ref name="facebook">{{cite web|url=https://m.facebook.com/yengunplugged/about|title=Yeng Constantino|publisher=m.facebook.com|access-date=November 1, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160118184258/https://m.facebook.com/yengunplugged/about|archive-date=January 18, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In November 2014, she admitted on her website that she was a fan of contemporary stars [[Demi Lovato]], [[Taylor Swift]], [[Hayley Williams]] and Avril Lavigne.<ref name="yengconstantino">{{cite web|url=http://www.yengconstantino.ph/well-hello-haters/|title=Well, Hello Haters. – Yeng Constantino|publisher=yengconstantino.ph|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref>

In 2018, Yeng started her own YouTube vlog, she often shows her admiration to [[Mariah Carey]].
