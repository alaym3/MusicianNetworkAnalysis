'''Isobel Bizu Beardshaw'''<ref name="guardianinterview">{{cite web |last1=Gibsone |first1=Harriet |title=Izzy Bizu: 'It's OK to be a space chicken' |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/jul/21/izzy-bizu-its-ok-to-be-a-space-chicken |website=The Guardian |access-date=4 January 2021}}</ref> (born 28 April 1994), better known as '''Izzy Bizu''', is an English-Ethiopian singer-songwriter<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/34313422/introducing-izzy-bizu|title=Introducing... Izzy Bizu|work=bbc.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tadias.com/03/09/2018/how-ethiopia-influenced-british-ethiopian-singer-izzy-bizus-music/|title=How Ethiopia Influenced British-Ethiopian Singer Izzy Bizu’s Music at Tadias Magazine|website=Tadias.com|access-date=22 October 2020}}</ref> signed to [[Epic Records]].<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|url=http://www.bimm.co.uk/news/izzys-busy-2014-capped-by-epic-record-deal/|title=Izzy Bizu signs to Epic Records|work=bimm.co.uk}}</ref> Bizu has garnered mainstream radio support from [[BBC Radio 1]]'s [[Annie Mac]] and [[Phil Taggart]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/izzy-bizu-unveils-new-single-give-me-love-143|title=Izzy Bizu unveils new single 'Give Me Love'|work=thefourohfive.com}}</ref> and [[BBC Radio 1Xtra]]'s [[Trevor Nelson]].<ref name="auto2">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p030brd0|title=Izzy Bizu in the Live Lounge|work=bbc.co.uk}}</ref> She has supported [[Coldplay]], [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]],<ref name="auto"/> [[Rudimental]],<ref name="auto3">{{cite web|url=http://rudimental.co.uk/tour|title=Rudimental live at Alexandra Palace|work=rudimental.co.uk|access-date=12 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160706064613/http://www.rudimental.co.uk/tour/|archive-date=6 July 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> and [[Foxes (singer)|Foxes]] on their latest UK tours.<ref name="auto4">{{cite web|url=http://www.livenation.co.uk/artist/foxes-tickets|title=Foxes UK Tour 2015 with Izzy Bizu|work=livenation.co.uk}}</ref> In November 2015, Bizu was shortlisted for a [[Brit Awards|Brit]] Critics' Choice award<ref name="auto5">{{Cite web|title = Izzy Bizu, Jack Garratt and Frances: meet the Brits critics' choice shortlist|url = and longlisted for [[BBC]]'s [[Sound of...]] 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/4ztqVqsPP6Mgzt2yxLwHWNs/the-longlist|title=BBC Sound Poll 2016|work=bbc.co.uk}}</ref> She won the [[BBC Music Awards|BBC Music Introducing Award]] at the 2016 ceremony. On 25 June 2016, Bizu performed on the Park Stage at the [[Glastonbury Festival]].

==Biography==
Izzy Bizu is from southwest [[London]],<ref name="auto6">{{cite web|url=http://news.livenation.co.uk/news/introducing-south-london-based-izzy-bizu/|title=Introducing: South London based Izzy Bizu|work=livenation.co.uk|access-date=12 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160711182202/http://news.livenation.co.uk/news/introducing-south-london-based-izzy-bizu|archive-date=11 July 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> and attended [[Stamford High School, Lincolnshire]].<ref>{{cite web | url =https://gsa.uk.com/2016/10/izzy-bizu-first-started/ | title =Izzy Bizu- ‘It first started here’ | publisher =Girls' Schools Association | access-date =2020-02-22 }}</ref> Her mother is [[People of Ethiopia|Ethiopian]] and her father is [[English people|English]]. She grew up between Ethiopia and Bahrain, where her father worked, before moving to London as a teen.<ref name="guardianinterview" /> She studied music tech in college, but dropped out shortly afterwards.<ref name="guardianinterview" />

==Career==
[[File:Izzy Bizu at the Victorious Festival 2016.jpg|thumb|Izzy Bizu performing at the Victorious Festival, 2016.]]

===2013: ''Coolbeanz''===
After the girl group SoundGirl split, Bizu won an open mic night competition in February 2013 winning over the crowd, which included [[Emeli Sande]] and [[Naughty Boy]], who were in attendance.<ref name="auto"/> She released her first independent EP, ''Coolbeanz'', in September 2013, which debuted at number 3 on the [[iTunes Store|iTunes]] Soul and RnB Chart and has sold 50,000 copies.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dmpgroup.com/artist/izzy-bizu/|title=Izzy Bizu – Coolbeanz EP|work=dmpgroup.com}}</ref> The EP’s lead single, "White Tiger", was also chosen as [[Zane Lowe]]’s ‘Next Hype’.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/izzybizu/bbc-radio-1-zane-lowes-next|title= BBC Radio 1 Zane Lowe's Next Hype (27.08.13)|work=soundcloud.com}}</ref> In October 2013, she supported [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]] on his UK tour,<ref name="auto7">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/errnc8/acts/apnxn3|title=Izzy Bizu at Glastonbury 2014|work=bbc.co.uk}}</ref> and in November [[Jamie Cullum]] handpicked her to open for him at [[The Roundhouse]] in London.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p021rl06/p021tp0h|title= Izzy Bizu and Jamie Cullum at Glastonbury 2014|work=bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

===2014–16: ''A Moment of Madness''===
In January 2014, Bizu entered [[Glastonbury Festival]]'s Emerging Talent Competition and made it to the final 8 of the competition,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/2014-emerging-talent-competition-finalists-announced/|title=Glastonbury Festival - 2014 Emerging Talent Competition finalists announced|website=Glastonburyfestivals.co.uk}}</ref> performing at the live finals of the event at Pilton Working Men's Club in April 2014. Bizu was named runner-up, winning £2,500 from the [[PRS for Music|PRS]] Foundation and a slot at that year's Glastonbury Festival.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/ma-win-2014-emerging-talent-competition/|title=Glastonbury Festival - M+A win 2014 Emerging Talent Competition|website=Glastonburyfestivals.co.uk}}</ref>

In June 2014, Bizu was also selected by [[BBC Introducing]]<ref name="auto7"/> to perform at the [[Glastonbury Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/the-wrap-up/blog/watch-izzy-bizu-perform-at-glastonbury-2014|title=Izzy Bizu performs at Glastonbury 2014|work=mtv.co.uk|access-date=12 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151126104039/http://www.mtv.co.uk/the-wrap-up/blog/watch-izzy-bizu-perform-at-glastonbury-2014|archive-date=26 November 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> In July 2014, she signed to Epic Records UK.<ref name="auto1"/> A year later, she released the first singles from her debut album, "Adam & Eve" and "Diamond", which enjoyed support from [[BBC Radio 1]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b05xqp3l|title=The Radio 1 Breakfast Show with Nick Grimshaw 15/06/2015|work=bbc.co.uk}}</ref> [[BBC Radio 2|Radio 2]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b05y3cqj|title=BBC Radio 2 – Jo Whiley, 16/06/2015|work=bbc.co.uk}}</ref> and [[BBC Radio 1Xtra|1Xtra]],<ref name="auto2"/> and performed at [[Glastonbury Festival|Glastonbury]] for the second time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/read/glastonbury-2015-by-jillian-edelstein |title=The Noisey Glastonbury Portfolio by Jillian Edelstein |work=noisey.vice.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924225633/http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/read/glastonbury-2015-by-jillian-edelstein |archive-date=24 September 2015 }}</ref>

In September 2015, following the release of her single "Give Me Love", she also made her TV debut on ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b06ds08q|title=Later... with Jools Holland – Series 47, Episode 2|work=bbc.co.uk}}</ref> Bizu supported both [[Rudimental]]<ref name="auto3"/> and [[Foxes (singer)|Foxes]]<ref name="auto4"/> on their UK headline tours.  In November 2015, Bizu was shortlisted for a [[Brit Awards|Brit]] Critics' Choice award.<ref name="auto5"/> In January 2016 Bizu's song 'White Tiger' featured on the advert for [[National Citizen Service|National Citizen Service (NCS)]] and in March 2016 she performed at NCS Yes Live alongside Tinie Tempah and Jess Glynne. Her debut album, ''[[A Moment of Madness]]'', was released on 2 September 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/a-moment-of-madness-deluxe/id1045652105|title=Izzy Bizu – A Moment of Madness|work=itunes.apple.com}}</ref> In April 2016, Bizu recorded (with the [[BBC Concert Orchestra]]) an uptempo, updated version of the [[Edith Piaf]] 1957 hit "[[La Foule]]" as the theme music for the [[BBC]]'s coverage of the [[Euro 2016]] Football Tournament.

Izzy Bizu was featured as a vocalist on [[Milky Chance]]'s track "Bad Things" on their 2017 album ''[[Blossom (Milky Chance album)|Blossom]]''.

==Artistry==
Bizu has described herself as a fan of jazz-fusion mixes and a lover of squat parties and warehouse raves. She grew up listening to [[Whitney Houston]], [[Ella Fitzgerald]], [[Bettye Swann]] and [[James Brown]]<ref name="auto6"/> and draws on these influences alongside newer influences such as [[Amy Winehouse]] and [[Adele]] to create a fusion of soul, funk, jazz and pop.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wiredevent.co.uk/izzy-bizu|title=Izzy Bizu – Singer-Songwriter|work=WIRED|access-date=12 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150920063700/http://www.wiredevent.co.uk/izzy-bizu|archive-date=20 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> She lists [[The Black Keys]], [[Diana Ross]], [[Grammatics]] and [[Sam Cooke]] among her other influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.codaagency.com/artist/967-izzy-bizu_121|title=About Izzy Bizu|work=codaagency.com|access-date=12 October 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305080454/http://www.codaagency.com/artist/967-izzy-bizu_121|archive-date=5 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>
