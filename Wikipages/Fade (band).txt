'''Fade''' (stylized as '''fade''') was a Japanese-American [[rock music|rock]] band formed in 1991. To date, Fade has released 5 [[Mini-LP|mini-albums]], 3 full-length studio albums, and 2 singles. Fade is currently signed with record label [[Universal Music Japan]].<ref>{{cite web|title=fade - Universal Music Japan|url=http://www.universal-music.co.jp/fade|work=|publisher=|accessdate=1 December 2013}}</ref> On April 9, 2014, fade announced an indefinite hiatus, in effect after the completion of their June tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=fade official > 【fadeから皆様へ 重大なお知らせ】|url=http://fadeonline.com/news/1831|accessdate=24 April 2014}}</ref>

==History==

{{Empty section|date=December 2012}}

===Pre-Fade and Formation===
The band was founded by drummer and songwriter Rui Watanabe and guitarist Kansei Miyaji, both of whom attended the same Japanese secondary school in [[New York City, New York]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Frieman|first=Richie|title=fade|url=http://www.penseyeview.com/content/fade|work=Pens Eye View: Music & Lifestyle|publisher=Penseyeview.com|accessdate=1 December 2012}}</ref> In 1994 bassist Noriyuki Hashimoto joined the group and in 1998 a second guitarist, Shingo Terasawa, (alias 5°, pronounced ''Godo'') came on board. In 1999 the members relocated to [[Tokyo, Japan]] and in 2001, Jon Underdown, who at the time was an [[exchange student]] hailing from [[Seattle, Washington]], joined the band solidifying the line-up. The following year, 2002, the group officially decided on the band name “Fade”.<ref name="JaME WORLD © 2004-2012 Japanese Music Entertainment">{{cite web|last=Gin|title=fade {{!}} JaME U.S.A.|url=http://www.jame-world.com/us/artists-biography-1410-fade.html|work=JaME - Artists|publisher=JaME-WORLD.com|accessdate=1 December 2012}}</ref> Jon, Rui and Kansei were born in the [[United States]] but only Jon doesn't have any Japanese ancestry while Noriyuki and Godo were born in [[Japan]].<ref name="JaME WORLD © 2004-2012 Japanese Music Entertainment"/>

===''Fade'' and ''A Moment of Truth''===
The band's self-titled debut mini-album, ''fade'', was released nationwide in Japan in 2003. It ranked 3rd on [[Japan Countdown]], a weekly countdown chart of album sales nationwide in Japan. Following their debut mini-album, while working with Markku Lappalainen (ex. [[Hoobastank]]), Erik Gregory (programming for [[Linkin Park]]’s 1st remix album ''[[Reanimation (Linkin Park album)|Reanimation]]''), and [[Jason C. Miller]] ([[Godhead (band)|gODHEAD]]) in 2005, Fade released their second mini-album entitled ''A Moment of Truth''. “Beautiful”, the album's 3rd track, was featured in the 2006 [[Shunji Iwai]] film, [[Rainbow Song]].<ref>{{cite web|title=fade - rock band - jrock|url=http://www.jpopasia.com/group/fade/|work=JpopAsia - groups|publisher=Jpopasia.com|accessdate=1 December 2012}}</ref>

===''Under the Sun'' and ''To Find A Better Tomorrow''===
''A Moment of Truth'' was shortly followed by the release of ''Under the Sun'', the band's 3rd mini-album, a few months later. The band's 4th mini-album release, ''To Find A Better Tomorrow'', in which they worked with [[Ted Jensen]], was released in 2008 ranking 9th on the Western Music chart of [[USEN]] Japan.<ref name="fade {{!}} Hard Rock">{{cite web|title=fade {{!}} Hard Rock|url=http://www.sputnikmusic.com/bands/fade/33208/|work=Reviews, Music, News {{!}} Sputnikmusic|publisher=Sputnikmusic.com|accessdate=1 December 2012}}</ref> The album's 6th track, “Slitting Regret”, features additional vocals from Toyo of Japanese [[industrial metal]] band [[Newbreed]].

===''Age of Innocence''===
2009 marked Fade's 5th release and first full-length studio album, ''Age of Innocence''.<ref>{{cite web|last=fade|title=Interview by Kathia Krüss|url=http://www.animepro.de/action/music-interviews/6163_e-mail-interview-mit-fade-interview|work=Anime Pro - Music Interview|publisher=AnimePro.de|accessdate=1 December 2012}}</ref> It included numerous songs that were featured on numerous [[marketing campaign]]s including [[ABC-Mart]],<ref name="fade {{!}} Hard Rock"/> and popular video games series' [[Drum Mania]] and [[Guitar Freaks]].<ref>{{cite web|title=【ギタドラV7】Reality Lost / fade|url=http://www.nicovideo.jp/watch/sm10439234|work=【ギタドラV7】|publisher=Niwango, Inc|accessdate=1 December 2012}}</ref>

===''Kings of Dawn''===
Fade's 6th release, ''Kings of Dawn'', arrived in 2011, which featured [[Koie Kenta]], of Japanese [[industrial metal]] band [[Crossfaith]], on the intro track “Born Ready”. This was also the first album featuring Jon singing in Japanese in addition to being the first album which the band hired producer, [[Hajime Okano]] ([[L'Arc en Ciel]]). An [[ITunes]] version of ''Kings of Dawn'' was released which included a cover of [[Bon Jovi]]'s, [[Livin' on a Prayer]](1986). In addition, a European edition of ''Kings of Dawn'' was also released in December 2011 through [[Gan-Shin|Gan-shin Records]].<ref>{{cite web|title=GANSHIN {{!}} fade|url=http://www.gan-shin.com/83-1-fade.html?129,82|work=Gan-shin - Artists|publisher=Gan-shin.com|accessdate=1 December 2012}}</ref>

===''One Reason'' and ''Cosmicalism''===
Concurrently, the singles "One Reason" and "Cosmicalism" (コズミカリズム Kozumikarizumu) were also released in April 2011. The song “[[Deadman Wonderland#Music|One Reason]]” by Fade featuring Deadman Wonder Band (DWB), was the opening theme song to the Japanese [[anime]] [[Deadman Wonderland]]. A [[Special edition|limited edition]] of “One Reason” was also released in 2011, which included a bonus DVD of the anime movie [[Deadman Wonderland]]. The song "One Reason" ranked 4th on the Independent Label music chart.<ref name="fade {{!}} Hard Rock"/>

===''Ten''===
Ten (天), the band's 7th album was released in July 2012. The band once again collaborated with producer [[Hajime Okano]], and featured Toyo from [[Newbreed]] on the album's 2nd track, "Chase for Daylight", Jon once described the concept of ''TEN'' as “ROCK with pop sensibility” and commented “I think we went to the poppyest we’ve ever been on one side, and we went to the heaviest we’ve ever been, too”.<ref>{{cite web|last=Underdown|first=Jonathan|title=Interview by Melissa Castor|url=http://www.thedivareview.com/fade_Exclusive_Interview.htm|work=The Diva Review - Listen and Hear (Music)|publisher=The Diva Review.com|accessdate=1 December 2012}}</ref> A limited-edition of ''TEN'' was also released which included a bonus DVD featuring selected live concert footage and music videos.

===''Crossroad ~History of fade~''===
The band's 8th album, released on 26 February 2014, features 17 tracks of which 3 are new. The band worked with Akira Yamaoka on new track "One Shot Dealer". Mixing for the album was done in LA by Jay Baumgardner.
