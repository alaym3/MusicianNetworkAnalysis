'''Ex Deo''' is a Canadian [[death metal]] band formed in [[Montreal]], [[Quebec]] in 2008. The band is a [[side project]] of [[Kataklysm]] frontman Maurizio Iacono,<ref name="AMG Review">{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=album|id=r1585374/review|pure_url=yes}}|title=Romulus – Review|last=Henderson|first=Alex|website=[[AllMusic]]|publisher=[[Macrovision]]|accessdate=17 June 2009}}</ref> and is based on the [[history of the Roman Empire]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bravewords.com/news/102243|title=Kataklysm Frontman's Ex Deo Project Inks Deal With Nuclear Blast|date=5 December 2008|work=[[Brave Words & Bloody Knuckles]]|accessdate=17 June 2009}}</ref>

==History==
[[File:Ex Deo - Paganfest 2013 Wuerzburg - 01-03-2013 04.jpg|thumb|Stéphane Barbe]]
[[File:Ex Deo - Paganfest 2013 Wuerzburg - 01-03-2013 03.jpg|thumb|Dano Apekian]]
Ex Deo was formed in 2008 as an artistic project of Maurizio Iacono, who is of Italian heritage. It became the first Roman Legion Melodic Death Metal band; in performance, band members wear modified Roman Legion uniforms.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Mike |title=Interview Ex Deo, Kataklysm Frontman Maurizio Iacono |url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=79470 |website=metalunderground.com |publisher=Metal Underground |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref>

===''Romulus'' (2009)===
Ex Deo's debut album ''[[Romulus (album)|Romulus]]'' was released in Europe and North America in June 2009, on [[Nuclear Blast]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Ex Deo - Romulus (Full Album) 2009 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g7fyjGbKNQc |website=youtube.com |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> The album was recorded with producer and Kataklysm guitarist [[Jean-François Dagenais]], featuring guest appearances by [[Karl Sanders]] ([[Nile (band)|Nile]]), Obsidian C. ([[Keep of Kalessin]]) and [[Adam Darski|Nergal]] ([[Behemoth (band)|Behemoth]]).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Falzon |first1=Denise |title=Ex Deo Romulus, June 2009 |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/ex_deo-romulus |website=exclaim.ca |publisher=Exclaim! |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> It was generally well-received.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Porter |first1=Christopher |title=Came, Saw, Conquered: Ex Deo, 'Romulus', Nov 2009 |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/express/wp/2009/11/04/ex-deo-romulus-maurizio-iacono-interview/ |website=washingtonpost.com |publisher=Washington Post |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Transilvanian |first1=MaD |title=A death metal appraisal of the Rome’s history, March 2010 |url=https://www.metal-archives.com/reviews/Ex_Deo/Romulus/239946/ |website=metal-archives.com |publisher=Encyclopaedia Metallum |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Ogle |first1=Ryan |title=EX DEO "Romulus" |url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/cdreviews/romulus/ |website=blabbermouth.net |publisher=Blabbermouth |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> The band toured in support of it, playing [[Hellfest (French music festival)|Hellfest]] in France, FortaRock Festival in Holland, Paganfest in Germany<ref>{{cite web |title=Paganfest 2009 |url=https://www.loudmemories.com/festivals/paganfest-2009-20044 |website=loudmemories.com |publisher=Loud Memories |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> and a group concert in New York.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ex Deo's Conert & Tour History |url=https://www.concertarchives.org/bands/ex-deo |website=concertarchives.org |publisher=Concert Archives |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> 

In December 14, 2010, the band parted with Nuclear Blast and signed a new record deal with [[Napalm Records]].<ref>{{cite web | title = The past is alive :... | url = https://www.facebook.com/exdeo/posts/170151553025116|website=Facebook.com | date = 2010-12-14 | accessdate = 2012-08-14 }}</ref>

===''Caligvla'' (2012-2014)===
Ex Deo's second album ''Caligvla'', was released on August 31, 2012, Caligula's 2000th anniversary. The album features guest appearances by Seth Siro ([[Spiros Antoniou]]) ([[Septicflesh]]), [[Mariangela]] Demurtas ([[Tristania (band)|Tristania]]), Stefano Fiori ([[Graveworm]]) and Francesco Artusato ([[All Shall Perish]])<ref>{{cite web |title=Ex Deo – Caligvla |url=https://www.discogs.com/master/654919-Ex-Deo-Caligvla |website=discogs.com |publisher=Discogs |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> and was well-received by critics.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Druhm |first1=Steel |title=Ex Deo – Caligvla Review, August 2012 |url=https://www.angrymetalguy.com/ex-deo-caligvla-review/ |website=angrymetalguy.com |publisher=Angry Metal Guy |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Ervin |first1=Cameron |title=Ex Deo Caligvla Review |url=http://www.metal-temple.com/site/catalogues/entry/reviews/cd_3/e_2/ex-deo-caligvla.htm |website=metal-temple.com |publisher=Metal Temple |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Metal |first1=D.T. |title=Ex Deo - Caligvla review, Oct 2012 |url=https://metalstorm.net/pub/review.php?review_id=11240 |website=metalstorm.net |publisher=Metal Storm |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Ex Deo Caligvla Album Review |url=https://www.nocleansinging.com/2012/09/21/ex-deo-caligvla/ |website=nocleansinging.com |publisher=No Clean Singing |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> 

In June 2012, the band released the video for the song ''I, Caligvla'' (featuring Maurizio's girlfriend and [[Colombia]]n model Suzzy),<ref>{{cite web |title=EX DEO - I, Caligvla |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NSW01sWSPQY |website=youtube.com |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref>and went on tour with [[Septicflesh]], which Iacono was then managing.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Falzon |first1=Denise |title=Ex Deo - Interview with Maurizio Iacono |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/ex_deo |website=exclaim.ca |publisher=Exclaim! |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Neilstein |first1=Vince |title=THE MOST BR00TALEST METAL TOUR...August 2012 |url=https://www.metalsucks.net/2012/08/07/the-most-br00talest-metal-tour-of-fall-2012-inquisition-septicflesh-krisiun-melechesh-and-ex-deo/ |website=metalsucks.net |publisher=Metal Sucks |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref>In addition to numerous North American dates, they played [[Bloodstock Open Air]] 2013,<ref>{{cite web |title=Bloodstock Band Line-Up 2013 |url=https://www.bloodstock.uk.com/events/boa-2013/stages |website=bloodstock.uk.com |publisher=Bloodstock |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> and were the special guests at the [[Cardiff]] show of the “At The Gate Of Sethu 2013 European Tour Part II”<ref>{{cite web |last1=Music |first1=Massive |title=“At The Gate Of Sethu 2013 European Tour Part II” |url=https://metalshockfinland.com/tag/at-the-gate-of-sethu-2013-european-tour-part-ii/ |website=metalshockfinland.com |publisher=Metal Shock Finland |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> in September 2013.

''Caligvla'' was nominated for Metal/Hard Music Album of the Year at the [[Juno Awards of 2013]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Past Nominees & Winners - Ex Deo |url=https://junoawards.ca/awards/past-winners-nominees/?search_year_val1=1970&search_year_val2=2021&search_category_val=&search_wins_val=no&tag_search_val=Ex+Deo&submit=submit |website=junoawards.ca |publisher=Juno Awards |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref>

===''The Immortal Wars'' (2015–2018)===
In February 2017, Ex Deo released their third album, ''The Immortal Wars'', which is about the [[Punic Wars]]. It also received excellent reviews.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tovey |first1=Steve |title=Ex Deo – The Immortal Wars, Feb 2017 |url=https://www.ghostcultmag.com/album-review-ex-deo-the-immortal-wars-napalm-records/ |website=ghostcultmag.com |publisher=Ghost Cult Magazine |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=X |first1=Madam |title=Ex Deo – The Immortal Wars Review, Feb 2017 |url=https://www.angrymetalguy.com/ex-deo-immortal-wars-review/ |website=angrymetalguy.com |publisher=Angry Metal Guy |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Kemp |first1=Donnie |title=Review: Ex Deo – The Immortal Wars (2017), Feb 2017 |url=https://maximumvolumemusic.com/review-ex-deo-immortal-wars-2017/ |website=maximumvolumemusic.com |publisher=Maximum Volume Music |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref> In 2018, the band went on a European tour with [[Ensiferum]], playing dates in France, the Netherlands and Denmark, including Royal Metal Fest.<ref>{{cite web |title=Royal Metal Fest 2018 |url=https://en.concerts-metal.com/concert_-_Royal_Metal_Fest_2018-42212.html |website=en.concerts-metal.com |publisher=Concerts-Metal |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Ex Deo's Concert Tour & History |url=https://www.concertarchives.org/bands/ex-deo |website=concertarchives.org |publisher=Concert Archives |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref>

===''The Thirteen Years of Nero'' (2021–)===
On June 14, 2021, Ex Deo released its fourth album, ''The Thirteen Years of Nero'', about the Roman Emperor [[Nero]].<ref>{{cite web |title=EX DEO - The Thirteen Years Of Nero (2021) FULL ALBUM |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ItR-PhS7HvE |website=youtube.com |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://bravewords.com/news/ex-deo-to-release-the-thirteen-years-of-nero-album-in-august-imperator-music-video-streaming|title=EX DEO To Release The Thirteen Years Of Nero Album In August; "Imperator" Music Video Streaming|publisher=[[Brave Words & Bloody Knuckles]]|access-date=June 14, 2021|date=June 14, 2021}}</ref> Once again, the band received rave reviews.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bowar |first1=Chad |title=Album Review: EX DEO The Thirteen Years Of Nero, August 2021 |url=https://metalinjection.net/reviews/ex-deo-the-thirteen-years-of-nero |website=metalinjection.net |publisher=Metal Injection |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Jonathan |title=Ex Deo – The Thirteen Years Of Nero (Album Review), August 2021 |url=https://www.sonicperspectives.com/album-reviews/ex-deo-the-thirteen-years-of-nero/ |website=sonicperspectives.com |publisher=Sonic Perspectives |access-date=2021-10-14}}</ref>
