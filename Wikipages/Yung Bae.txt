'''Dallas Cotton''', also known as '''Yung Bae''', is a [[future funk]] producer from Portland, Oregon, known for his album ''Bae'' which sampled 1980s Japanese [[city pop]].<ref>[https://www.amazon.com/Bae-Explicit-Yung/dp/B01LY3M5QV Bae (Explicit) by Yung Bae on Amazon Music]</ref>

==Early life==
Born in 1994 as Dallas Cotton,<ref name=Week>{{cite web|url=https://www.wweek.com/music/2018/07/11/meet-the-portland-producer-who-has-amassed-millions-of-streams-on-spotify-and-a-following-in-korea-without-ever-playing-a-hometown-show/|title=Meet the Portland Producer Who Has Amassed Millions of Streams on Spotify and a Following in Korea Without Ever Playing a Hometown Show|author=Shannon Gormley |publisher=Willamette Week|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref> Yung Bae began as a music producer based out of [[Portland, Oregon]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://liveforlivemusic.com/features/griz-st-augustine-2019/|title=GRiZ Set To Bring His Funky, Horn-Heavy Beats To St. Augustine With Yung Bae & Dwilly|date=12 April 2019|website=L4LM|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref> He attended the [[Oregon State University]] for four months before turning to music full time. His first full album was titled ''Bae''.<ref name=Week /> He has since moved and begun working out of [[Los Angeles]].<ref name=Wagon>{{cite web|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/a-sticky-sweaty-night-of-future-funk-with-yung-bae|title=Sweating it out future funk style with Yung Bae – gig report|date=23 April 2018|website=Bandwagon.asia|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref>

==Music career==
Yung Bae works in the future funk and [[vaporwave]] subgenres, releasing tracks online through sites like [[Bandcamp]]—he released eight in total on that site. These received several million streams on [[Spotify]].<ref name=Week /> His early release albums included three volumes of his album series ''Japanese Disco Edits''. In 2016 his releases included ''Skyscraper Anonymous'', ''BAE 2'', ''BA3'', and ''Bae: Side B''.<ref name=Times>{{cite web|url=https://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/music/5-vaporwave-tracks-to-prepare-you-for-yung-bae-at-valley-bar-10913771|title=5 Vaporwave and Future Funk Tracks to Get You Ready for YUNG BAE|first=Douglas|last=Markowitz|date=10 October 2018|website=Phoenix New Times|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref> In 2018 he released ''B4E''.<ref name=Wagon /> He has collaborated with acts such as Flamingosis, Brasstracks, and [[Method Man]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://edmidentity.com/2018/12/21/five-undercard-sets-snowglobe-2018/|title=Five Undercard Sets You'll Want to Catch at SnowGlobe 2018|first=Zachary|last=Lefevre|date=21 December 2018|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref>  In 2019 he released the single “Must Be Love”<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.edmsauce.com/2019/06/05/new-edm-release-radar-june-7th-wtf-is-coming-out-friday/|title=New EDM Release Radar - June 7th - WTF Is Coming Out Friday|first=Nick|last=White|date=5 June 2019|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref> and “Bad Boy”<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.laweekly.com/track-of-the-week-yung-baes-bad-boy-ft-billy-marchiafava-and-bbno/|title=Track of The Week: Yung Bae’s “Bad Boy” FT. Billy Marchiafava and bbno$|first=Shirley|last=Ju|date=1 July 2019|access-date=12 September 2019}}</ref> which were included in his album ''Bae 5''.<ref name=Disc>{{cite web |url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/4015361-Yung-Bae|publisher=Discogs|title=Yung Bae Discography}}</ref> “Bad Boy” was also his first single release after signing to major label [[Arista Records]].

In 2019 Yung Bae was named to the [[Billboard Dance Chart|Billboard Dance Ones to Watch]] list.<ref name=Bill /><ref name=Board>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8494631/dance-artists-to-watch-january-2019|title=Billboard Dance's Ones to Watch: January 2019|website=Billboard|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref> In 2019 he went on a 21-date North American tour<ref name=Week /> in support of his album ''Bae 5''.<ref name=Scene>{{cite web|url=https://www.clevescene.com/scene-and-heard/archives/2019/06/07/yung-bae-coming-to-the-grog-shop-in-october|title=Yung Bae Coming to the Grog Shop in October|first=Jeff|last=Niesel|website=Cleveland Scene|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref> He has also toured internationally through Asia<ref name=Return>{{cite web|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/yung-bae-talks-mixtapes-the-return-of-funk-and-motown-upcoming-collaborations-and-more|title=Yung Bae talks mixtapes, the return of funk and Motown, upcoming collaborations and more|date=17 April 2018|website=Banwagon.asia|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref> at locations such as the [[National Gallery Singapore|National Gallery of Singapore]],<ref name=Wagon /> and performed international music festivals<ref name=Bill>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8513611/crssd-fall-2019-lineup|title=Portugal. The Man, Hot Chip, Richie Hawtin & More Set For Fall 2019 Edition Of CRSSD|website=Billboard|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.edmsauce.com/2019/06/05/firefly-music-festival-featuring-epic-dance-music-lineup-in-2019/|title=Firefly Music Festival Featuring Epic Dance Music Lineup in 2019|last=Selbe|date=6 June 2019|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bandwagon.asia/articles/five-highlights-of-garden-beatsfestival-2019-gig-report|title=Five highlights of Garden Beats Festival 2019 – gig report|date=20 March 2019|website=Bandwagon.asia|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref> such as [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8503574/do-lab-coachella-2019-lineup|title=Pete Tong, Justin Jay, Mr. Carmack & More at Do LaB Coachella 2019|website=Billboard|access-date=24 June 2019}}</ref>
