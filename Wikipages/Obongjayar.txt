'''Steven Umoh''', known by his stage name '''Obongjayar''', is a [[Nigerians|Nigerian]] musical artist based in [[London]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.highsnobiety.com/p/obongjayar-interview/|title=Nigerian-Born Obongjayar Represents the Changing Face of London’s Sound|last=Nembhard|first=Candice|date=July 11, 2018|website=High Snobiety|access-date=December 1, 2018}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Steven Umoh grew up in [[Calabar]], [[Nigeria]]. He was raised by his grandmother; his mother had moved to the UK to escape Umoh's father, who was abusive. Early in his life, Umoh primarily listened to bootleg rap, particularly [[Eminem]], [[Usher (musician)|Usher]], [[Nelly]], [[Snoop Dogg]] and [[Ciara]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/get-to-know-obongjayar-who-makes-otherworldly-spirituals-for-the-modern-soul/|title=Get to Know Obongjayar, Who Makes Otherworldly Spirituals for the Modern Soul|last=Ravens|first=Chal|date=January 20, 2018|website=Pitchfork|access-date=December 1, 2018}}</ref>

Umoh moved to live with his mother in [[England]] at the age of 17, but stayed active in the Nigerian music scene. He grew up a devoutly religious [[Christians|Christian]], but became less so after he attended university in [[Norwich]].<ref name=":0" /> In college he sang primarily in an [[American Accent|American accent]], influenced by his childhood spent listening to American [[hip hop music]], but he transitioned to singing in his natural Nigerian-British accent before embarking on his professional career.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/kz7qnn/obongjayar-interview-2017-bassey-ep|title=Obongjayar Will Make You See Beyond Your Ego|last=Bassil|first=Ryan|date=September 14, 2017|website=Noisey|access-date=December 1, 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
Obongjayar started his career publishing his music on [[SoundCloud]]. His work caught the notice of [[XL Recordings]] executive [[Richard Russell (XL Recordings)|Richard Russell]], who then asked Obongjayar to contribute to his Everything Is Recorded project.<ref name=":1" /> In 2016, Obongjayar released his first [[extended play]], ''Home'', described by ''Noisey'' as "a unique voice: one that navigates darkness with nocturnal, near-spiritual hymns."<ref name=":2" />

He followed ''Home'' with a second EP, ''Bassey'', which was noted for its sparse production and [[afrobeat]] rhythms,<ref name=":0" /> as well as its themes of spirituality, politics, and his experiences as a Black person.<ref name=":2" /> He also contributed to Richard Russell's 2017 EP, ''Close But Not Quite.<ref name=":0" />''

In 2019, Obongjayar featured on [[Detroit]] rapper [[Danny Brown]]'s fifth studio album ''[[U Know What I'm Sayin?]]'' on the tracks "Belly of the Best" and "uknowhatimsayin¿".<ref>{{Citation|title=uknowhatimsayin¿ by Danny Brown|url=https://music.apple.com/tr/album/uknowhatimsayin/1476829920|language=en-gb|access-date=December 14, 2019}}</ref>

== Style ==
Obongjayar's music has been called "hard to describe", incorporating elements of [[afrobeat]], [[spoken word]], [[Soul music|soul]], and [[electronic music]]. His lyrics incorporate [[Spirituality|spiritual]] overtones, although they are not readily identifiable with any specific religious tradition.''<ref name=":0" />'' A unifying feature of all of Obongjayar's music is his very unique voice, which slides between [[rapping]], singing, and spoken word.<ref name=":1" />
