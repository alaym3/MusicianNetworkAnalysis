'''Joshua Mellody''' (born 1 June 1989),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://zomboyofficial.com/masterclass/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=September 1, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130911211323/http://zomboyofficial.com/masterclass/ |archive-date=September 11, 2013 }}</ref><ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/zomboy-mn0002842126
|title=Zomboy|publisher=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref> professionally known as '''Zomboy''', is an English [[dubstep music]] [[Record producer|producer]], [[songwriter]], and [[DJ]] whose mascot is a [[zombie]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Zomboy is coming!|url=http://www.djmag.com/node/18705|work=DJ Mag|access-date=4 May 2013}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Zomboy debuted in 2011 with the track "Organ Donor", which was released on Never Say Die Records ''Game Time EP''.<ref name="itunesgt">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/game-time-ep/id459440650|title=iTunes - Zomboy - Game Time EP|publisher=[[iTunes]]}}</ref> His debut EP was in top 5 of the [[Beatport]] dubstep charts for over eight weeks.<ref name="beatport">{{cite web|url=http://www.beatport.com/artist/zomboy/199919|title=Zomboy|publisher=[[Beatport]]}}</ref> At the end of the year, his music and remixes were licensed to compilations on labels like [[Warner Music Group|Warner]] and [[Ministry of Sound]].<ref name="beatport"/>

In 2012, he released his second EP, ''The Dead Symphonic EP''.<ref name="itunesds">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/the-dead-symphonic-ep/id557265669|title=iTunes - Zomboy - The Dead Symphonic EP|publisher=[[iTunes]]}}</ref> In March 2013, he released the single "Here to Stay"<ref name="itunesnts">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/here-to-stay-feat.-lady-chann/id609354899|title=iTunes - Zomboy - Here to Stay|publisher=[[iTunes]]}}</ref> featuring Lady Chann on No Tomorrow Records. In September 2013, his ''Reanimated EP'' was released in two parts, of which Pt. 1 was released on Never Say Die Records & Pt. 2 on No Tomorrow Recordings. His debut album entitled ''The Outbreak'' on Never Say Die Records was released in August 2014.
2016 saw the release of the ''Neon Grave EP'', featuring four tracks; remixes of these tracks were released in an EP in early 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.beatport.com/release/neon-grave/1719665 |title=Neon Grave from Never Say Die Records on Beatport |website=Beatport.com |access-date=2020-05-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/neon-grave-remixed-ep/1197870589 |title=Apple Music |website=Itunes.apple.com |access-date=2020-05-02}}</ref>

2017 also saw the release of an EP entitled ''Rott N' Roll Part 1'' coinciding with a US tour; the remixes of this EP were released that November.<ref>{{cite web|author=Dave Jenkins |url=http://ukf.com/words/exclusive-zomboy-unveils-wtf-first-single-long-awaited-debut-album/ |title=EXCLUSIVE: Sample a cheeky snippet of Zomboy's WTF!? |website=Ukf.com |date=2014-04-22 |access-date=2020-05-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/rott-n-roll-pt-1-remixed/1309036703 |title=Apple Music |website=Itunes.apple.com |access-date=2020-05-02}}</ref>

Zomboy cites influences such as [[Skrillex]], Reso, [[Rusko (musician)|Rusko]] and Bare Noize. He studied Music Production at the [[Academy of Contemporary Music]] in [[Guildford]].
