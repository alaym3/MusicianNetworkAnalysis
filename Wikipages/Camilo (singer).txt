'''Camilo Echeverry''' (born 16 March 1994), known [[mononymously]] as '''Camilo''', is a Colombian singer, musician and songwriter. He is known for his singles "Favorito", "[[Tutu (Camilo and Pedro Capó song)|Tutu]]", alongside [[Pedro Capó]] and [[Shakira]], and "Desconocidos", with [[Mau y Ricky]] and [[Manuel Turizo]]. His music is generally categorized as Latin pop with a mix of urbano and is noted for his romantic lyrics and [[soprano]] voice.

Debuted in 2008, after winning the talent show ''[[:es:Factor Xs|Factor XS]]'' in 2007. He is also known for writing and producing hits for other artists including [[Becky G]] and [[Natti Natasha]]'s "[[Sin Pijama]]" and [[Bad Bunny]]'s "[[Si Estuviésemos Juntos]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8527750/latin-artist-on-the-rise-camilo-echeverry|title=Meet This Week's Latin Artist on the Rise: Camilo|first=Suzette|last=Fernandez|date=19 August 2019|access-date=5 October 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eluniversal.com.co/farandula/irreconocible-asi-luce-hoy-camilo-echeverry-ganador-de-factor-xs-en-2007-277601-ABEU393113|title=¡Irreconocible! Así luce hoy Camilo Echeverry, ganador de Factor XS en 2007|date=4 May 2018|work=[[El Universal (Cartagena)|El Universal]]|language=es|trans-title=Unrecognizable! This is what Camilo Echeverry, 2007 winner of Factor XS, looks like today|access-date=5 October 2019}}</ref>

His accolades include five [[Latin Grammy Awards]] and two [[Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] nominations.

== Biography ==
=== 1994–2017: Early life and career beginnings ===
Camilo Echeverry was born in Medellín. His family home did not have a radio, but housed a collection of records by artists such as [[The Beatles]], [[Charly Garcia]], [[Facundo Cabral]], [[Mercedes Sosa]], and [[Pink Floyd]].<ref name=sunpost>{{cite web|url=https://thesunpost.com/2019/05/02/camilo-echeverry-declares-himself-a-thief-of-the-great-singer-songwriters/|title= Camilo Echeverry declares himself a "thief" of the great singer-songwriters|date=2 May 2019|access-date=17 April 2020|work=The SunPost}}</ref> He recalled, "That was my first seed, since I was little, my dream was to inherit that."<ref name=sunpost /> He formed a musical duo with his sister, Manuela, and auditioned for ''XS Factor'' with her, but the pair did not reach final round of the competition.<ref name=allmusicbio>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/camilo-mn0000838393/biography|title=Camilo – Artist Biography|work=Allmusic|last=Jurek|first=Thom|access-date=17 April 2020}}</ref> After winning the show as a solo artist the following year in 2007, Camilo began his career with the release of the single, "Regálame Tu Corazón".<ref name=gettoknowmtv>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3115547/camilo-echeverry-interview-sin-pijama-desconocidos/|title=Get to Know Camilo Echeverry, Reggaeton's Secret Pop Weapon|last=Villa|first=Lucas|work=MTV News|date=5 March 2019|access-date=17 April 2020}}</ref>

In 2008, Camilo made appearances on Colombian telenovelas ''Super Pá'' and ''[[En los tacones de Eva]]'', as well as the children's program ''Bichos''.<ref name=allmusicbio /> He released the 2010 mixtape ''Tráfico de Sentimientos'' and shortly after its release, Camilo decided to take a break from releasing his own music.<ref name=gettoknowmtv /> In 2015, he moved to [[Miami]], Florida, where he then furthered his career by writing songs for other artists, including "Sin Pijama" by [[Becky G]] and [[Natti Natasha]]’s, “Veneno” by [[Anitta (singer)|Anitta]], and "Ya No Tiene Novio" by [[Sebastian Yatra]] with [[Mau y Ricky]].<ref name=sunpost /><ref name=artistontherise>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8527750/latin-artist-on-the-rise-camilo-echeverry|title=Meet This Week's Latin Artist on the Rise: Camilo|last=Fernandez|first=Suzette|work=Billboard|date=19 August 2019|access-date=17 April 2020}}</ref> "Ya No Tiene Novio", released in August 2018, garnered over 517 million views on Youtube by March of the next year.<ref name=gettoknowmtv />

=== 2018–present: Success as a solo artist ===
[[File:Camilo Echeverry - Hombre de hojalata 2018.jpg|thumb|left|Camilo in 2018, pictured with his trademark [[handlebar moustache]]]]
Upon re-launching his solo career, Camilo made changes to his image. He eschewed his previously clean-cut style in favor of a more bohemian look, complete with a [[handlebar moustache]].<ref name=gettoknowmtv /> In 2018, he released the song "Desconocidos" with [[Manuel Turizo]] and Mau & Ricky. The ukulele-tinged reggaeton song peaked at number 23 on the ''Billboard'' Latin Pop Airplay chart.<ref name=latinpopchart>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/camilo/chart-history/LPO|title=Camilo – Billboard Latin Pop Airplay Chart History|work=Billboard|access-date=17 April 2020}}</ref> Following the success of "Desconocidos", Camilo revealed in February 2019 that he had signed with [[Sony Music Latin]].<ref name=gettoknowmtv /> On 22 February 2019, Camilo performed at the Venezuela Live Aid concert in [[Cúcuta]], Colombia, a town located on the border with Venezuela.<ref name=gettoknowmtv /> He reflected on the experience by explaining "It was a concert that moved me a lot. It was not easy, but so many people are suffering. I felt happy to be a part of raising global awareness to show what is happening in Venezuela is a tremendous injustice."<ref name=gettoknowmtv />

He released the video and single for "No Te Vayas" as his mainstream debut on 28 March 2019, and by August of that year, the video had reached more than 59 million views on Youtube.<ref name=artistontherise /> His 2019 song "La Boca" with Mau & Ricky was certified triple-platinum.<ref name=allmusicbio /> After the release of the song, he made his US television debut, performing "La Boca" and "No Te Vayas" at the [[2019 Premios Juventud]] in Miami.<ref name=artistontherise /> He released the song "Tutu" in collaboration with Puerto Rican singer [[Pedro Capó]], which reached the number two position on the Latin Pop Airplay chart.<ref name=latinpopchart /> Describing the lyrical content of the song, Camilo explained, “This song is who I am, what I feel every time I get on a stage or when I see my girlfriend.”<ref name=artistontherise /> Camilo released his first studio album ''Por Primera Vez'' on 17 April 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Panamá|first=LOS40|date=17 April 2020|title=Camilo lanza su primer álbum "Por Primera Vez"|url=http://los40.com.pa/los40/2020/04/17/formula40/1587138049_051697.html|access-date=19 January 2021|website=LOS40PA|language=es}}</ref> which debuted at number one on the ''Billboard'' Latin Pop Albums chart and number five on the Top Latin Albums chart.<ref name=first>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9368127/camilo-por-primera-vez-top-latin-albums-chart|title=A First for Camilo: 'Por Primera Vez' Scores Top 5 Debut on Latin Albums Chart|work=Billboard|date=29 April 2020|access-date=8 May 2020|last=Bustios|first=Pamela}}</ref> The album contains the singles "Tutu" and "Favorito".<ref name=first />

In March 2021, he released his album "Mis Manos" which included some collaborations with artists such as [[Evaluna Montaner]], [[Mau y Ricky]], [[El Alfa]], and Los Dos Carnales. His album is a candidate for Album Of The Year and Best Pop Vocal Album at the [[Latin Grammy Awards]].

At the [[21st Annual Latin Grammy Awards]], Camilo received six nominations, winning the award for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Pop Song|Best Pop Song]] for "Tutu".<ref name="EW" />
Recently Selena Gomez and Camilo released a new song called "999".

== Musical style ==
Camilo describes himself as "an honest singer who uses love as a reason for his songs."<ref name=artistontherise /> His music is generally characterized as Latin pop and reggaeton.<ref name=allmusicbio /> Suzette Fernandez of ''Billboard'' labeled his music as "romantic pop songs fused with urban beats".<ref name=artistontherise /> His vocals have been described as "distinctly delicate".<ref name=gettoknowmtv /> He cites Uruguayan singer [[Jorge Drexler]] as a major influence on his work.<ref name=sunpost /> He is also noted for his positivity on social media. Lucas Villa of [[MTV]] described his Instagram feed as "a Latinx Hallmark card filled with positive messages of hope", exemplified by his bio section consisting of "AMAR ES MI REVOLUCIÓN" ("Love is my revolution").<ref name=gettoknowmtv />

== Personal life ==
In February 2020, he married [[Evaluna Montaner]], daughter of the Argentine-Venezuelan singer and songwriter [[Ricardo Montaner]] and sister of the Venezuelan duo [[Mau y Ricky]]. In October 2021, the couple announced that they were expecting their first child in their latest single "Índigo". According to sources, that is the name they have chosen for their unborn child.
