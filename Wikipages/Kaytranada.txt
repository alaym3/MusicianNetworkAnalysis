'''Louis Kevin Celestin''' (born August 25, 1992), known professionally as '''Kaytranada''' (stylized as '''KAYTRANADA''', shortened as '''KAYTRA'''), is a Haitian-Canadian record producer and DJ.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtviggy.com/articles/stream-king-kaytranada-finds-success-irl/|title=Stream King Kaytranada Finds Success IRL|publisher=MTV October 22, 2013|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/15981-at-all/|title=Kaytranada - At All|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] September 5, 2013|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref><ref name="complex.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/05/kaytranada-happy-remix|title=Listen to Kaytranada's Remix of Pharrell's "Happy"|publisher=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] May 12, 2014|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.redbull.com/us/en/music/stories/1331641662373/photo-diary-kaytranada-x-mobb-deep-in-the-studio|title=Photo Diary: Kaytranada x Mobb Deep in the Studio|publisher=[[Red Bull]] March 31, 2014|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref> Celestin rose to prominence after releasing a series of mixtapes, remixes, and original music projects beginning in 2010 under the alias '''Kaytradamus'''. By 2013, and under the moniker Kaytranada, he began gaining wider recognition and, the following year, signed a deal with [[XL Recordings]], with whom he would release his critically acclaimed debut studio album ''[[99.9%]]'' in 2016. In 2019, he released its follow-up, ''[[Bubba (album)|Bubba]]'', for which he won two [[Grammy Award]]s including [[Best Dance/Electronic Album]]. Celestin is one half of the [[hip hop]] duo [[The Celestics]], along with his brother [[Lou Phelps]].

==Early life==
Kaytranada was born Louis Kevin Celestin on August 25, 1992 in [[Port-au-Prince]], [[Haiti]]. Shortly after he was born, his family relocated to [[Montreal]] where he was raised. Kaytranada began to DJ at the age of 14. The following year, at 15, Kaytranada began to produce after his brother introduced him to [[FL Studio]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hypetrak.com/2014/01/kaytranada-discusses-drake-production-influence-and-haitian-music/|title=Kaytranada Discusses Drake Production Influence and Haitian Music|publisher=Hypetrak|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/05/13/interview-kaytranada-xl/#ixzz34ABwAAO7|title=Interview: Kaytranada on His Funky XL Debut: "It's definitely going to surprise you"|publisher=[[The Fader]] May 12, 2014|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
Celestin began his career under the name Kaytradamus in 2010. He released two projects as Kaytradamus before changing his name to Kaytranada in 2012. Kaytranada was on the roster of HW&W Recordings, an independent label based in Los Angeles and Toronto.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kaytranada|url=http://huhwhatandwhere.com/kaytranada/|access-date=December 9, 2017|website=Huhwhatandwhere.com}}</ref> Kaytranada has released 13 projects and 41 remixes. He has toured more than 50 Canadian, American, European and Australian cities.{{As of?|date=November 2020}}<ref>{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/blog/kaytranada-not-missing-a-beat|title=Kaytranada: Not Missing A Beat|publisher=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] February 5, 2014|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.earmilk.com/tag/kaytranada|title=Kaytranada|publisher=EARMILK|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/tags/kaytranada|title=Kaytranada (Vibe)|publisher=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2014/06/best-new-artist-profile-kaytranda/|title=Kaytranada Channels Dilla, Embraces Up-Tempo Neo-Soul|date=3 June 2014|publisher=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] June 3, 2014|access-date=June 9, 2014}}</ref>

In December 2014, he signed an exclusive recording agreement with [[XL Recordings]]. During 2015, Kaytranada opened for two nights of [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]]'s [[Rebel Heart Tour]], one in Canada and one in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|title=Madonna's Rebel Heart Tour Confirms Kaytranada Will Open Tonight's Show at Joe Louis Arena|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Madonnas-Rebel-Heart-Tour-Confirms-Kaytranada-Will-Open-Tonights-Show-at-Joe-Louis-Arena-20151001|website=Broadwayworld.com}}</ref> Speculation arose that Kaytranada and producer [[Rick Rubin]] had started working together when a photo of them surfaced online around February 2015. It was later confirmed that they had formed a partnership backed by Pulse Recordings.{{Citation needed|date=November 2020}}

His debut album, ''[[99.9%]]'', was released on May 6, 2016.<ref>[http://www.cbcmusic.ca/posts/17548/cohen-gould-buffy-25-best-canadian-debut-albums "25 best Canadian debut albums ever"]. ''CBC Music'', June 16, 2017.</ref> The album includes features from [[GoldLink]], [[AlunaGeorge]], [[Syd (singer)|Syd]], [[Anderson .Paak]], and [[Vic Mensa]], among others. He also collaborated with [[Craig David]] on the song "Got It Good", which is on David's sixth studio album, ''[[Following My Intuition]]''. Kaytranada won the [[2016 Polaris Music Prize]] for his album ''[[99.9%]]''.<ref name="PMZ" /> The album received a score of 8.0 from Pitchfork.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/21899-999/|title=Kaytranada: 99.9% Album Review - Pitchfork|website=Pitchfork.com}}</ref>

On December 13, 2019, his second album, ''[[Bubba (album)|Bubba]]'', was released on RCA Records. It received a score of 8.1 from Pitchfork.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/kaytranada-bubba/|title=Kaytranada: Bubba Album Review - Pitchfork|website=Pitchfork.com}}</ref>

On March 14, 2021 Kaytranada won a Grammy for Best Dance Recording for '10%' and Best Dance/Electronic Album for 'Bubba'.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/kaytranada-wins-best-danceelectronic-album-bubba-2021-grammy-awards-show|title=KAYTRANADA Wins Best Dance/Electronic Album|date=14 March 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In an April 2016 article with ''[[The Fader]]'', Kaytranada disclosed that he is gay.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/04/05/kaytranada-album-interview-99-percent| title=Kaytranada Is Reaching 100%| work=[[The Fader]] | first=Alex| last=Frank | date= April 5, 2016|access-date= May 5, 2016 | archive-date= April 6, 2016 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160406064538/http://www.thefader.com/2016/04/05/kaytranada-album-interview-99-percent | url-status=live}}</ref>

==Discography==
{{Main|Kaytranada discography}}
Since 2010, Kaytranada has released two studio albums, two studio EPs, numerous singles, and at least a dozen [[Mixtape|beat tapes]]. He is also a prolific remix artist and has released a series of collaborative projects with [[The Celestics]] and [[Robert Glasper]], among others.
