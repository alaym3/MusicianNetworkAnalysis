'''Panshak Henry Zamani''' (born 30 October 1984), better known as '''Ice Prince''', is a Nigerian rapper and actor. He rose to fame after releasing "[[Oleku (Ice Prince song)|Oleku]]", one of Nigeria's most remixed songs of all time.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ice Prince's 'Oleku': Official video for one of Nigeria's most remixed hits|url=http://www.thisisafrica.me/new-releases/detail/1307/ice-prince-s-oleku-official-video-for-one-of-nigeria-s-most-remixed-hits|publisher=thisisafrica|access-date=23 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131202234326/http://www.thisisafrica.me/new-releases/detail/1307/ice-prince-s-oleku-official-video-for-one-of-nigeria-s-most-remixed-hits|archive-date=2 December 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> He won the 2009 Hennessy Artistry Club Tour.<ref name=Hennessyartistry>{{cite web|title=Hennessy Artistry|url=http://www.hennessyartistry.com.ng/hennessyartistry.html#/hennessyartistry/|publisher=Hennessyartistry|access-date=26 October 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The Ice Prince was crowned: Photos and all the scoop from the Hennessy Artistry 2009 Finale|date=27 September 2009|url=http://www.bellanaija.com/2009/09/27/the-ice-prince-was-crowned-photos-and-all-the-scoop-from-the-hennessy-artistry-2009-finale/|publisher=Bellanaija|access-date=26 October 2013}}</ref> His debut studio album ''[[Everybody Loves Ice Prince]]'' was released in 2011. It was supported by three singles: "Oleku", "[[Superstar (Ice Prince song)|Superstar]]" and "Juju". In 2013, Ice Prince released ''[[Fire of Zamani]]'' as his second studio album. The album contained the singles "[[Aboki (song)|Aboki]]", "More", "Gimme Dat" and "[[I Swear (Ice Prince song)|I Swear]]". On 1 July 2015, Ice Prince was announced as the vice president of [[Chocolate City (record label)|Chocolate City]]. He held the position until he left the label in 2016.<ref>{{cite web |title= M I Emerges As The New Chocolate City CEO |url= https://www.naij.com/475836-m-i-emerges-as-the-new-chocolate-city-chairman.html/ |publisher= Naij.com |access-date= 17 September 2015 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20160529080651/https://www.naij.com/475836-m-i-emerges-as-the-new-chocolate-city-chairman.html |archive-date= 29 May 2016 |url-status= dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=MI BECOMES CHOCOLATE CITY CEO |url=http://dailytimes.com.ng/m-i-becomes-chocolate-city-ceo/ |publisher=Daily Times |access-date=17 September 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151015211958/http://dailytimes.com.ng/m-i-becomes-chocolate-city-ceo/ |archive-date=15 October 2015  }}</ref>

==Early life==
Ice Prince was born Panshak Henri Zamani<ref>https://mdundo.com/news/19146</ref>  in the city of [[Minna]]. At age two, he moved with his family to the [[mining community|mining city]] of [[Jos]], [[Plateau State]]. He is of [[Angas people|Angas]] ethnicity.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Olq4gI4clA |title=Iceprince on the NdaniTGIFShow |date=24 May 2019 |access-date=5 April 2020|publisher = Ndani TV}}</ref>

== Education ==
While residing in Jos, he attended St. Murumba College. He also attended the Science Tutorial Niger State College in Jos.<ref name="MTV Base" />

== Career ==

In 1999, he began writing rap songs and started performing them at school; in 2001 he began recording them. In 2002, he and some friends formed the "[[Economic Community of West African States Monitoring Group|ECOMOG]] Squad" but split after a year. In 2004, he connected with [[M.I Abaga|M.I]], [[Jesse Jagz]], Ruby, Lindsey, Eve, Taz, and together they became what is now known today as the "Loopy Crew".<ref name="MTV Base" /> He attended the [[University of Jos]] to study zoology, but had to stop because of a "financial constraint".<ref name=Bellanaija>{{cite web|title=BN Saturday Celebrity Interview: Let's Tell You Why Everybody Loves Ice Prince – He's Talented, He's Smooth & He's Taking his Music to the World|date=28 July 2012|url=http://www.bellanaija.com/2012/07/28/bn-saturday-celebrity-interview-lets-tell-you-why-everybody-loves-ice-prince-hes-talented-hes-smooth-hes-taking-his-music-to-the-world/|publisher=Bellanaija|access-date=6 November 2013}}</ref>

===2010–2011: ''Everybody Loves Ice Prince''===
During the aforementioned interview, Ice Prince opened up about why he chose to name his debut album, ''Everybody Loves Ice Prince''. He said "What made me give my album that name was when I lost my Mum, really. There was so much love, so much care from all around the world. People really showed me love and it was after that incident that I came back to record my album and I thought what better name to call my album than Everybody Loves Ice Prince because I was shown love".<ref name=Bellanaija/>

"[[Oleku (Ice Prince song)|Oleku]]", Ice Prince's first single off his debut album was met with great reception throughout Nigeria.{{Citation needed|date=January 2016}} The song was released under [[Chocolate City (music label)|Chocolate City]]. ===2012–2013 :''Fire of Zamani''===
In 2012, he began frequent collaborations with Grip Boiz City producer Chopstix, releasing "[[Aboki (song)|Aboki]]" in the summer of 2012. An accompanying music video was released alongside it. "Aboki" peaked at number 92 on Afribiz's Top 100 chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Afribiz – Top 100|url=http://www.afribizcharts.com/top100.php?start=91|publisher=Afribiz|access-date=23 October 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029195616/http://www.afribizcharts.com/top100.php?start=91|archive-date=29 October 2013}}</ref>

On 28 August 2012, Ice Prince released "More" as the second single from the album. The song was also produced by Chopstix. The music video for "More" was shot and directed in London by Moe Musa. It stars [[Lola Rae]], a [[British Nigerian]] recording artist known for her single "Watch My Ting Go". The video premiered at the 2012 [[Channel O Music Video Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ice Prince – More (Official Video) |url=http://www.everythingmoa.com/content/ice-prince-more-official-video |publisher=Music of Africa |access-date=25 October 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029201423/http://www.everythingmoa.com/content/ice-prince-more-official-video |archive-date=29 October 2013  }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=VIDEO: Ice Prince – More – (Prod By Chopstix)|url=http://notjustok.com/2012/11/20/video-ice-prince-more-prod-by-chopstix/|publisher=Notjustok|access-date=25 October 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=VIDEO: Ice Prince – More|url=http://tooxclusive.com/2012/11/20/video-ice-prince-more/|publisher=tooxclusive|access-date=25 October 2013}}</ref>

On 25 January 2013, Ice Prince released "[[Aboki (song)#Aboki (Remix)|Aboki (Remix)]]"; he described the song as the "biggest African collision ever".<ref name = YouTube>{{cite web|title=The Juice – Ice Prince|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xVyx7E8DWEM|publisher=YouTube|access-date=23 October 2013}}</ref> The song features [[Sarkodie (rapper)|Sarkodie]], [[Mercy Johnson]], [[Wizkid (musician)|Wizkid]], [[M.I Abaga|M.I]] and [[Khuli Chana]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Listen to Ice Prince's MEGA Remix for "Aboki" featuring Sarkodie, Wizkid, M.I, Khuli Chana & Mercy Johnson|date=25 January 2013|url=http://www.bellanaija.com/2013/01/25/listen-to-ice-princes-mega-remix-for-aboki-featuring-sarkodie-wizkid-m-i-khuli-chana-mercy-johnson/|publisher=Bellanaija|access-date=23 October 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Ice Prince – Aboki remix featuring Sarkodie, Mercy Johnson, Wizkid, M.I & Khuli Chana (Produced by Chopstix)|url=http://www.ghanamotion.com/music/featured-artists/ice-prince-aboki-remix-featuring-sarkodie-mercy-johnson-wizkid-m-i-khuli-chana-produced-by-chopstix|publisher=Ghanamotion|access-date=23 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029190848/http://www.ghanamotion.com/music/featured-artists/ice-prince-aboki-remix-featuring-sarkodie-mercy-johnson-wizkid-m-i-khuli-chana-produced-by-chopstix|archive-date=29 October 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> On 24 April 2013, Ice Prince and Chocolate City released "Gimme Dat" and "[[V.I.P (Ice Prince song)|V.I.P]]" simultaneously. The former made the album's final track-listing, while the latter didn't.<ref>{{cite web|title=360Recommends: Ice Prince Zamani – VIP + Gimme Dat ft Burna Boy, Yung L and Olamide|url=http://www.360nobs.com/2013/04/360recommends-ice-prince-zamani-vip-gimme-dat-ft-burna-boy-yung-l-and-olamide/|publisher=360nobs|access-date=23 October 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The Fire of Zamani! ===2015–2016 :''Jos To The World''===
In 2015, Ice Prince began making available singles from his third studio album. The eventual release of the album signified his first independent album outside Chocolate City record label. He revealed to Pulse he had Universal Studio in the US mix and master the album.<ref>{{cite web|title= Ice Prince Rapper's JOS TO THE WORLD album is out now|url= http://www.pulse.ng/buzz/ice-prince-rappers-jos-to-the-world-album-is-out-now-id5670594.html|publisher=Pulse|access-date=6 February 2018}}</ref>
