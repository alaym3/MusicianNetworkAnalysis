'''Pretty Ricky''' is an [[United States|American]] [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]]/[[Hip hop music|hip hop]] [[musical ensemble|group]] originating from [[Miami]], [[Florida]]. The group originally consisted of brothers Ala Diamond (Baby Blue) Smith and [[Spectacular Smith]], plus Corey (Slick 'Em) Mathis and Marcus ([[Pleasure P]]) Cooper. Pleasure P was the group's main singer while the other three members performed as [[Rapping|rappers]]. After he departed in 2007 to embark on a solo career, the group shuffled through several replacement vocalists.

The name "Pretty Ricky" was taken from a character in the [[sitcom]] ''[[Martin (TV series)|Martin]]''. The group enjoyed its biggest success in the mid-2000s with their gold-selling albums ''[[Bluestars (album)|Bluestars]]'' and ''[[Late Night Special]]'', and the platinum singles "[[Grind with Me|Grind With Me]]" and "[[On the Hotline|On The Hotline]]".

== History ==

=== 1997-2003: Beginnings ===

In 1997, while performing together in [[Miami, Florida|Miami]], Pretty Ricky was signed to Bluestar Entertainment by CEO Joseph "Blue" Smith. Supported by their father, Joseph Smith, Sr., who also served as the band's manager and who is of [[Jamaicans | Jamaican]] descent,<ref>{{cite magazine |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Boys to Men - Pretty Ricky |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=9CYEAAAAMBAJ&q=Oct%202008%20vibe&pg=PP4 |magazine=Vibe Magazine |location=Google Books |page=108 |quote= Born to a Jamaican mother, Smith's father died... | publisher=Vibe Media Group |date=1 October 2008 |access-date=10 February 2020}}</ref> the group developed their music under the name of "Pretty Rickie and The Maverix" before changing it to "Pretty Ricky". With the help from producer Jim Jonsin of the Unusual Suspects production team, Pretty Ricky began to gain desirable attention through their local performances. However, it was not until 2002 that the group encountered their initial success as their song "Flossin'" became a huge hit on Miami radio station [[Power 96]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bmi.com/news/entry/20060310pretty_ricky_pretty_ricky |title=Pretty Ricky &#124; MusicWorld |publisher=BMI.com |date=2006-03-09 |access-date=2019-10-12}}</ref>

=== 2004–05: ''Bluestars'' ===

In 2004, Pretty Ricky released their first official single "[[Grind With Me]]". It reached #7 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], and was [[platinum certification|certified Platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/?s=Pretty+Ricky+grind+on+me |title=You searched for Pretty Ricky grind on me |publisher=RIAA |access-date=2019-10-12}}</ref>

The single made its way to popularity on WPOW-Power 96, and station Program Director Ira Wolf A/K/A/Tony the Tiger sent the single to [[Craig Kallman]] of [[Atlantic Records]]. After auditioning with Kallman, Pretty Ricky was offered a record deal on the spot.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.teenhollywood.com/2005/05/23/pretty-ricky-shines-on-bluestars|title=teenhollywood.com}}</ref>

On May 17, 2005, Pretty Ricky released their debut album titled ''[[Bluestars (Pretty Ricky album)|Bluestars]]''. It debuted at #16 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, selling 56,000 copies in the first week, and within several months, was [[Gold certification|certified Gold]] by RIAA.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/62729/a-welcome-exile-audioslave-opens-at-no-1|title=A Welcome 'Exile': Audioslave Opens At No. 1|work=Billboard|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref> On June 15, 2005, Pretty Ricky released their second single from the album, "[[Your Body (Pretty Ricky song)|Your Body]]", which charted on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 at #12.

In the summer of 2005 the group toured with [[Omarion]], [[Bow Wow (rapper)|Bow Wow]], [[Chris Brown (American singer)|Chris Brown]], [[Bobby V]], and other acts in the [[Scream Tour IV]] concert series.<ref>{{cite web|author=archive-Chris-Harris |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1504350/bow-wow-omarion-roll-out-scream-iv-summer-dates/ |title=Bow Wow, Omarion Roll Out Scream IV Summer Dates |publisher=MTV |date=2005-06-20 |access-date=2019-10-12}}</ref>

=== 2006–09: ''Late Night Special'' and Pleasure P's departure ===

On November 9, 2006, Pretty Ricky released the first single off their second album, ''[[Late Night Special]]'', titled "[[On the Hotline]]". It peaked on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 at #97 and was certified platinum by the RIAA.<ref name="RIAA"/> In January 2007, ''Late Night Special'' topped the ''Billboard'' 200 album charts with over 132,000 copies sold in the first week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1055522/pretty-ricky-shins-grab-top-album-chart-spots|title=Pretty Ricky, Shins Grab Top Album Chart Spots|work=Billboard|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref> The second single from the album, "[[Push It Baby]]", featured [[Sean Paul]] but failed to crack the Hot 100, reaching only #109. Pleasure P was later left the group after, and was replaced by Christopher "4Play" Myers in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|author=Vibe |url=https://www.vibe.com/2018/01/interview-pleasure-p-love-hip-hop-miami-pretty-ricky-reunion |title=Pleasure P Talks 'Love & Hip-Hop: Miami,' Pretty Ricky Reunion And More |publisher=Vibe |date=2018-01-08 |access-date=2019-10-12}}</ref>

In 2008, Pretty Ricky began work on a new album, ''Eighties Babies'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/eighties-babies-unreleased-mw0000796530|title=Eighties Babies [Unreleased]|work=AllMusic|access-date=February 17, 2016}}</ref> with an intended release date of October 14, 2008, but the album was shelved due to [[News leak|leaking]] and creative differences. 4Play quit the group in 2009 and was replaced by Emanuel "Lingerie" DeAnda.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://zhiphopcleveland.com/3194202/pretty-rickys-newest-member-hes-mean-he-sings-hes-lingerie/|title=Pretty Ricky's Newest Member...he's mean, he sings, he's Lingerie!!! – Z 107.9|work=Z 107.9|access-date=February 17, 2016}}</ref> Pretty Ricky then split from Atlantic and signed with independent Atlanta label [[Big Cat Records (U.S. record label)|Big Cat Records]], who released their official third album, ''[[Pretty Ricky (album)|Pretty Ricky]]'', in November 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/Pretty/Ricky/prweb2788484.htm|title=Big Cat Records to Distribute Pretty Ricky's Self Titled New LP November 17th|date=26 August 2009|work=PRWeb|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref>{{better source needed|reason=press release|date=September 2019}} It charted for only one week on the ''Billboard'' 200, at #97.

=== 2010–present ===

In 2010, Pretty Ricky released the singles "Personal Freak" and "Cookie Cutter", neither of which charted.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/cookie-cutter-single/id384311385|title=Cookie Cutter – Single|work=iTunes|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref> On August 10, 2010, they released an [[extended play]] titled ''Topless''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/topless-ep/id386112307|title=Topless – EP|work=iTunes|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref> In December 2010, the group announced their fourth album, ''Bluestars 2'', was in the works,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bet.com/video/106andpark/106guestrewind/pretty-ricky-12-28-2010-2315841.html|title=Pretty Ricky "Blue Stars 2"|date=27 December 2010|work=BET.com|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref> but the album was never produced.

On August 12, 2011, Pretty Ricky released a mixtape titled ''Sex Music Vol. 1: Streets N The Sheets''.<ref name="datpiff.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Pretty-Ricky-Sex-Music-Vol-1-Streets-N-The-Sheets-mixtape.261193.html|title=Pretty Ricky – Sex Music Vol. 1: Streets N The Sheets|work=DatPiff|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref>

After a three-year hiatus, Pretty Ricky released the non-charting single "Puddles" on February 16, 2015. This marked the return of Pleasure P.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/puddles-feat.-baby-blue-spectacular/id968823724|title=Puddles (feat. Baby Blue, Spectacular, Slickem & Pleasure P) – Single|work=iTunes|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thisisrnb.com/2015/02/listen-pleasure-p-reunites-with-pretty-ricky-on-new-single-puddles/|title=Listen: Pleasure P Reunites with Pretty Ricky on New Single "Puddles" – ThisisRnB.com – Hot New R&B Music, Videos, News|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref> 

In 2019, Pretty Ricky joined The Millennium Tour along with B2K, Mario, Chingy, Ying Yang Twins, Lloyd, and Bobby Valentino.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2018/12/21/b2k-reunion-tour/|title=OMARION ANNOUNCES B2K REUNION TOUR WITH PRETTY RICKY, MARIO, LLOYD, CHINGY & YING YANG TWINS|last=Miss2Bees|date=December 21, 2018|website=[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]|access-date=January 25, 2020}}</ref> on February 21, 2020, Pretty Ricky returned with a new single titled "Body", which is set to appear on the group's reunion album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://youknowigotsoul.com/pretty-ricky-returns-with-lsg-sampled-single-body|title=Pretty Ricky Returns With LSG Sampled Single "Body"|website=YouKnowIGotSoul|access-date=January 25, 2020}}</ref>
