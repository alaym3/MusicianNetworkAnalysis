'''Barrington DeVaughn Hendricks'''<ref name=":2">{{cite web|url=http://www.papermag.com/jpegmafia-1957629741.html|title=Radical Contrarian Rapper JPEGMafia On Gun Ownership, Trump And Flipping Rhetoric On Its Head|last=Gillespie|first=Blake|date=August 3, 2016|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200105132347/http://www.papermag.com/jpegmafia-1957629741.html|archive-date=January 5, 2020|access-date=April 21, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2018/03/who-is-jpegmafia|title=Who Is JPEGMAFIA?|last=Corrigan|first=Graham|date=March 29, 2018|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Pigeons & Planes]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180421234330/https://pigeonsandplanes.com/in-depth/2018/03/who-is-jpegmafia|archive-date=April 21, 2018|access-date=April 21, 2018}}</ref> (born October 22, 1989), known professionally as '''JPEGMafia''' (stylized in [[all caps]]), is an American rapper, singer, and record producer from [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. His 2018 album ''[[Veteran (JPEGMafia album)|Veteran]]'', released through [[Deathbomb Arc]], received widespread critical acclaim and was featured on many year-end lists. It was followed by 2019's ''[[All My Heroes Are Cornballs]]'', released to further critical acclaim.

==Career==
Hendricks developed an interest in music production at the age of 15, and he began producing after he learned how to [[Sampling (music)|sample]].<ref name="tg1">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/oct/04/jpegmafia-i-want-to-create-a-space-for-invisible-black-people|title=Radical rapper Jpegmafia: 'Black People have things to be mad about'|last=Pearce|first=Sheldon|date=October 4, 2019|work=The Guardian|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200301212518/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/oct/04/jpegmafia-i-want-to-create-a-space-for-invisible-black-people|archive-date=March 1, 2020|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref> Describing his production, Hendricks says "When I was first making beats, no one liked the beats. To this day I give people beats and they're just confused." He says "I started rapping because no one liked my beats." Noting that he started producing before he started rapping, Hendricks said "I'm a producer first kind of, and then a rapper second but I take both of them seriously."<ref>{{Citation|title=JPEGMAFIA {{!}} Q&A {{!}} Cambridge Union (2/2)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jsv8XwyA6cI|language=en|access-date=2021-11-10}}</ref>

During his military stay in Japan, he was producing and writing music under the name '''Devon Hendryx'''.<ref name="tg1"/> In 2015, he moved to [[Baltimore]], where he began to make music under his moniker JPEGMafia,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/jpegmafia-all-my-heroes-are-cornballs-2640309333.html|title=JPEGMAFIA Is Doing It All Wrong|author=Michael|date=September 13, 2019|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190915205015/https://www.papermag.com/jpegmafia-all-my-heroes-are-cornballs-2640309333.html|archive-date=September 15, 2019|access-date=September 24, 2019}}</ref>  under which he released his [[mixtape]] ''Communist Slow Jams'' in April 2015. Only a month later, he released his mixtape ''Darkskin Manson'' which was inspired by the [[2015 Baltimore protests|Freddie Gray protests]] in Baltimore that were happening right as he had moved there.<ref name="tl"/>
Following a number of mixtapes, Hendricks released his debut [[studio album]], ''[[Black Ben Carson]]'', in February 2016 via [[Deathbomb Arc]] featuring a much harsher, distorted sound than his other projects.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/6wqy8w/jpegmafia-black-ben-carson-premiere|title=JPEGMAFIA's New Album 'Black Ben Carson' Is Going to Heal, Inspire, and Revive America|last=Kramer|first=Kyle|date=February 15, 2016|website=Vice|language=en-US|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190610015832/https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/6wqy8w/jpegmafia-black-ben-carson-premiere|archive-date=June 10, 2019|access-date=June 8, 2019}}</ref> Four months later, he released a collaborative [[Extended play|EP]] with fellow Baltimore-based rapper Freaky titled ''The 2nd Amendment''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tinymixtapes.com/music-review/jpegmafia-x-freaky-2nd-amendment|title=JPEGMAFIA x Freaky – The 2nd Amendment|website=[[Tiny Mix Tapes]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190608023241/https://www.tinymixtapes.com/music-review/jpegmafia-x-freaky-2nd-amendment|archive-date=June 8, 2019|access-date=June 7, 2019}}</ref>
After less than 3 years living there, Hendricks moved from Baltimore to [[Los Angeles]] for his next studio album.<ref name="fader2018-a">{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/04/18/jpegmafia-veteran-interview|title=JPEGMAFIA is the out-of-pocket rap rebel the world needs right now|last1=Younger|first1=Briana|website=The Fader|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190910095130/https://www.thefader.com/2018/04/18/jpegmafia-veteran-interview|archive-date=September 10, 2019|access-date=April 18, 2018}}</ref> In January 2018, he released his second studio album [[Veteran (JPEGMafia album)|''Veteran'']].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/12/jpegmafia-veterans-day-merch|title=JPEGMAFIA dropped dog tags and PS2-themed merch for Veterans Day|last=Renshaw|first=David|date=November 12, 2018|website=[[The Fader]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190715154520/https://www.thefader.com/2018/11/12/jpegmafia-veterans-day-merch|archive-date=July 15, 2019|access-date=June 7, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jpegmafia.net/albums|title=JPEGMAFIA – Albums|website=jpegmafia.net|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200307162537/http://www.jpegmafia.net/albums|archive-date=March 7, 2020|access-date=June 7, 2019}}</ref> In an article on Bandcamp,<ref name="bndarticle">{{cite web|url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/2018/01/31/jpegmafia-feature/|title=On "Veteran," Rapper JPEGMAFIA Revels in Creative Freedom|last1=Gillespie|first1=Blake|website=Bandcamp Daily|date=January 31, 2018|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190526171954/https://daily.bandcamp.com/2018/01/31/jpegmafia-feature/|archive-date=May 26, 2019|access-date=January 31, 2018}}</ref> he said "I wanted to show I'm not just a one-trick pony. I always do weird shit. I usually just keep it to myself. This time, I just let the filter go." At the time of its release, ''Veteran'' was considered JPEGMafia's most experimental album to date, receiving widespread critical acclaim.<ref name="Breiham"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.orlandoweekly.com/orlando/rapper-jpegmafia-laughs-while-the-world-burns/Content?oid=10778234|title=Rapper JPEGMAFIA laughs while the world burns|last=Moyer|first=Matthew|date=February 14, 2018|website=[[Orlando Weekly]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180410030134/https://www.orlandoweekly.com/orlando/rapper-jpegmafia-laughs-while-the-world-burns/Content?oid=10778234|archive-date=April 10, 2018|access-date=June 7, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/jpegmafia-veteran/|title=JPEGMAFIA: Veteran Album Review|last=Thompson|first=Paul A.|date=January 31, 2018|website=Pitchfork|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131080014/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/jpegmafia-veteran/|archive-date=January 31, 2018|access-date=March 13, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/02/22/jpegmafia-rules-and-heres-proof|title=JPEGMAFIA rules, and here's proof|last=Darville|first=Jordan|date=February 22, 2018|website=The Fader|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180226041109/http://www.thefader.com/2018/02/22/jpegmafia-rules-and-heres-proof|archive-date=February 26, 2018|access-date=March 13, 2018}}</ref>

After the release of ''Veteran'', Hendricks started working on his next album. He recorded 93 songs, and whittled it down to 18 tracks.<ref name="gn93">{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/jpegmafia-says-he-recorded-93-songs-for-his-new-album|title=JPEGMAFIA Says He Recorded 93 Songs For His New Album - Genius|last1=Fu|first1=Eddie|website=Genius|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190914042803/https://genius.com/a/jpegmafia-says-he-recorded-93-songs-for-his-new-album|archive-date=September 14, 2019|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref> He mixed and mastered it at the end of [[Vince Staples]]' tour,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.factmag.com/2019/09/13/listen-to-jpegmafias-new-album-all-my-heroes-are-cornballs/|title=Listen to JPEGMAFIA's new album, All My Heroes Are Cornballs|last1=Jones|first1=Henry-Bruce|website=Fact Magazine|date=September 13, 2019|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190913203207/https://www.factmag.com/2019/09/13/listen-to-jpegmafias-new-album-all-my-heroes-are-cornballs/|archive-date=September 13, 2019|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref><ref name="vtour">{{cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/12/vince-staples-2019-tour-dates-north-america/|title=Vince Staples announces 2019 North American tour|last1=Schatz|first1=Lake|website=Consequence Of Sound|date=December 11, 2018|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190507133923/https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/12/vince-staples-2019-tour-dates-north-america/|archive-date=May 7, 2019|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref> posting percentage updates frequently on his [[Instagram]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/long-reads/time-is-ticking-for-jpegmafia/|title=Time is ticking for JPEGMAFIA|last1=Herwees|first1=Tasbeeh|website=Crack Magazine|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190608023246/https://crackmagazine.net/article/long-reads/time-is-ticking-for-jpegmafia/|archive-date=June 8, 2019|access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref> Prior to the release, he would label the project as a "disappointment" in interviews and his [[social media]]. The first single from the album, "Jesus Forgive Me, I Am a Thot", was released on August 13, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/08/13/jpegmafia-jesus-forgive-me-i-am-a-thot|title=JPEGMAFIA shares new song "Jesus Forgive Me, I Am a Thot"|last1=Renshaw|first1=David|website=The Fader|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190913225657/https://www.thefader.com/2019/08/13/jpegmafia-jesus-forgive-me-i-am-a-thot|archive-date=September 13, 2019|access-date=March 31, 2020}}</ref> He promoted the album by uploading a series of listening sessions to his [[YouTube]] channel where friends and artists such as [[Denzel Curry]], [[Jeff Tweedy]] (of [[Wilco]]) and [[Hannibal Buress]] discussed and reacted to cuts off of the album. ''[[All My Heroes Are Cornballs]]'' was released on September 13, 2019, to further critical acclaim,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/all-my-heroes-are-cornballs/jpegmafia|title=All My Heroes Are Cornballs by JPEGMAFIA Reviews and Tracks|date=September 13, 2019|website=Metacritic|publisher=[[Metacritic]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190921080251/https://www.metacritic.com/music/all-my-heroes-are-cornballs/jpegmafia|archive-date=September 21, 2019|access-date=September 13, 2019}}</ref><ref name="lobf">{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/reviews/albums/jpegmafia-all-my-heroes-are-cornballs-album-review|title=Get comfortable being uncomfortable with JPEGMAFIA's new album|last=Higgins|first=Sam|date=September 12, 2019|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191024001438/https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/reviews/albums/jpegmafia-all-my-heroes-are-cornballs-album-review|archive-date=October 24, 2019|access-date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> and was his first album to chart. In October 2019, he embarked on the JPEGMafia Type Tour to support his new album.<ref name="typetourannouncement">{{cite web |title=JPEGMAFIA on Twitter |url=https://twitter.com/darkskinmanson/status/1161751149730336768 |website=Twitter |access-date=November 17, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tinymixtapes.com/news/jpegmafia-shares-new-single-announces-tour-dates|title=JPEGMAFIA hikes five hours to film video for "Jesus Forgive Me, I Am A Thot," shares tour dates|last=Klein|first=Jeremy|date=August 14, 2019|work=[[Tiny Mix Tapes]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190814223237/https://www.tinymixtapes.com/news/jpegmafia-shares-new-single-announces-tour-dates|archive-date=August 14, 2019|access-date=September 15, 2019}}</ref>

In 2020, Hendricks released several singles across the span of the year, and compiled them into an EP, aptly titled ''[[EP!]]''. It was released on Hendricks' Bandcamp page on November 6,<ref>{{cite web| url=https://jpegmafia.bandcamp.com/album/ep|title=EP! - JPEGMAFIA|website=Bandcamp|date=November 6, 2020|access-date=December 10, 2020}}</ref> and on streaming services with the addition of one extra single on December 10.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/12/10/jpegmafia-ep-denzel-curry-tommy-genesis-super-tuesday|title=Listen to JPEGMAFIA's new collection EP!|website=The Fader|date=December 10, 2020|access-date=December 10, 2020}}</ref> On February 12, 2021, Hendricks released his second extended play, ''[[EP2!]]''. On October 2, Hendricks announced the album ''[[LP!]]'' , which was released on October 22, 2021, his birthday.

==In other media ==
In March 2020, Hendricks started a [[vlog]] series on his [[YouTube]] channel called HTBAR (''How To Build A Relationship'').<ref>{{cite web |title=A lot of things are bad, but this video of JPEGMAFIA and Orville Peck playing Smash Bros is good |url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/03/17/jpegmafia-orville-peck-interview-htbar-danny-brown-lykke-li |website=The Fader |access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> The series feature JPEGMafia talking to other artists and friends about various topics, mostly about music, life and politics. Each episode's soundtrack consist of unreleased songs, demos and instrumentals produced by JPEGMafia.<ref>{{cite web |title=JPEGMAFIA Shares More New Music With 'How To Build A Relationship' Part 5 |url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/4/jpegmafia-how-to-build-a-relationship-part-5-documentary |website=Hypebeast |date=April 6, 2020 |access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> Artists that have featured on the vlog series include [[Danny Brown]], [[Kenny Beats]], [[Lykke Li]], [[Orville Peck]], [[Saba (rapper)|Saba]] and more.<ref>{{cite web |title=JPEGMAFIA Talks with Kenny Beats on 'HTBAR' Episode 6 |url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/4/jpegmafia-htbar-episode-6-documentary-kenny-beats |website=Hypebeast |date=April 13, 2020 |access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> Hendricks has also appeared on The Cave, a YouTube series created by hip hop producer Kenny Beats.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.nme.com/blogs/kenny-beats-collaborations-idles-rico-nasty-jpegmafia-school-boy-q-2644214|title=A beginner's guide to Kenny Beats – the megastar producer's best collaborations so far|work=[[NME]]|first=Georgia|last=Evans|date=April 11, 2020|access-date=October 26, 2020}}</ref>

Hendricks is a fan of [[professional wrestling]], and has incorporated several references to it in his songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://baltimorebeat.com/2018/02/14/jpegmafia-returns-veteran-kaleidoscopic-look-life-trump/|title=JPEGMAFIA returns with "Veteran," a kaleidoscopic look at life under Trump|work=Baltimore Beat|first=Dominic|last=Griffin|date=February 14, 2018|access-date=October 26, 2020}}</ref> He made an appearance for wrestling promotion [[All Elite Wrestling]] (AEW) on an episode of their television show ''[[AEW Dynamite]]'' in September 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pwtorch.com/site/2020/09/30/9-30-aew-dynamite-tv-report-kellers-report-on-jericho-vs-kassidy-moxley-vs-mystery-opponent-starks-vs-darby-cody-answers-challenge/|title=9/30 AEW DYNAMITE TV REPORT: Keller's report on Jericho vs. Kassidy, Moxley vs. Mystery Opponent, Starks vs. Darby, Cody answers challenge|work=Pro Wrestling Torch|first=Wade|last=Keller|author-link=Wade Keller|date=September 30, 2020|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref>

Hendricks appeared on the 7th episode of season 5 of ''[[The Eric Andre Show]]'', "Named After My Dad's Penis", and competed in the third ''Rapper Warrior Ninja'' sketch - a [[parody]] of the TV show ''[[Sasuke (TV series)|Sasuke]]'' (which aired in the United States of America under the title ''Ninja Warrior'') and its American spin-off ''American Ninja Warrior''. Hendricks competed along with [[Lil Yachty]], [[Murs (rapper)|Murs]], [[Trippie Redd]], and [[Zack Fox]] in a challenge to cross a platform while freestyling and dodging various attacks. Hendricks fell off the platform while freestyling and failed.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://uproxx.com/music/jpegmafia-lil-yachty-trippie-redd-eric-andre-rapper-warrior-ninja/|title=UPROXX Jpegmafia, Lil Yachty, And Trippie Redd Compete On 'Rapper Warrior Ninja' For 'The Eric Andre Show'|date=November 16, 2020}}</ref>
