'''Brett Charles Young''' (born March 23, 1981) is an American [[country pop]] singer, songwriter and guitarist from [[Orange County, California]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Mastrogiannis|first1=Nicole|title=INTERVIEW: Five Things Brett Young Does When He 'Can't Sleep Without You'|url=http://wdsd.iheart.com/articles/entertainment-news-104651/interview-five-things-brett-young-does-14600267/|access-date=May 5, 2016|work=[[WDSD|94.7 WDSD]]|date=April 13, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160806013658/http://wdsd.iheart.com/articles/entertainment-news-104651/interview-five-things-brett-young-does-14600267/|archive-date=August 6, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> He was a college [[baseball]] [[pitcher]] but took up songwriting after an elbow injury.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Littman|first1=Margaret|title=10 New Country Artists You Need to Know: March 2016|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-country-artists-you-need-to-know-march-2016-20160301|access-date=May 5, 2016|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=March 1, 2016}}</ref> His [[Brett Young (EP)|self-titled debut EP]], produced by [[Dann Huff]], was released by [[Republic Nashville]] on February 12, 2016.<ref name="sounds">{{cite news|last1=Laffer|first1=Lauren|title=Newcomer Brett Young Debuts Self-Titled EP|url=http://www.soundslikenashville.com/music/newcomer-brett-young-debuts-self-titled-ep/|access-date=May 5, 2016|work=Sounds Like Nashville|date=February 12, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Dukes|first1=Billy|title=Brett Young Is Nothing But Honest on New 'Brett Young' EP|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/brett-young-interview-ep/|access-date=May 5, 2016|work=Taste of Country|date=March 23, 2016}}</ref> The lead single, "[[Sleep Without You]]", was released on April 11, and he had a major success with his following single, "[[In Case You Didn't Know (Brett Young song)|In Case You Didn't Know]]".  He released his second major label album  ''[[Ticket to L.A.]]'' in 2018.

==Early life==
Young was born in [[Anaheim, California|Anaheim]], Orange County.<ref name="baseballcube"/> He attended Calvary Chapel High School in [[Costa Mesa, California]], and then [[Ole Miss]], [[Irvine Valley College]], and [[Fresno State]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ivclasers.com/sports/bsb/alumni |title=Alumni |work=Irvine Valley College}}</ref><ref name="OC register"/> He started singing in the late 1990s when he stepped in to replace an absent leader of the band during a Christian worship meeting at high school.<ref name="OC register">{{cite news |url=http://www.ocregister.com/articles/young-79310-one-songs.html|title=Orange Pop: Former pitcher Brett Young is an ace as a singer |date=August 13, 2008|first=Robert |last=Kinsler |work=Orange County Register }}</ref>

Young was a pitcher on his high school baseball team and led the squad to a [[California Interscholastic Federation|CIF]] championship. He went to [[Ole Miss]] on a baseball scholarship in 1999 after turning down pre-draft selection by both [[Tampa Bay Rays|Tampa Bay Devil Rays]] and [[Minnesota Twins]].<ref>{{cite news |url= http://articles.latimes.com/1999/jun/04/sports/sp-44241 |title=Calvary Chapel Has Rebels With a Cause - Baseball: Mossberg, Sterbens and Young, all headed to Mississippi, hope to lead Eagles to Division V title tonight |date=June 4, 1999|first=Mike |last=Terry |work=The Los Angeles Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.soundslikenashville.com/news/introducing-brett-young-part-1/|title=Introducing… Brett Young: Part 1 |first= Lauren|last= Laffer |date=April 6, 2016|work=Sounds Like Nashville }}</ref> In his one season on the Rebel baseball team in 2000, he earned a varsity letter and posted two wins and two saves. He then [[Letterman (sports)|lettered]] for one season at Irvine Valley before moving to Fresno.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://grfx.cstv.com/photos/schools/fres/sports/m-basebl/auto_pdf/Stanford.pdf |title=Baseball Bulldog |date= February 10, 2003 |work=Fresno State University}}</ref> However, his pursuit of a professional baseball career was cut short by an elbow injury while at Fresno in 2003.<ref name=kfrog /><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nashcountrydaily.com/2016/05/12/whos-new-brett-young/ |title=Who's New: Brett Young |first=Chris |last=Parton |date=May 12, 2016 |work=Nash Country Daily |access-date=December 1, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170810011605/http://www.nashcountrydaily.com/2016/05/12/whos-new-brett-young/ |archive-date=August 10, 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Music career==
According to Young, he was inspired by [[Gavin DeGraw]] after he heard his album ''[[Chariot (album)|Chariot]]'' as well as singer/songwriter [[Jeffrey Steele]] and decided to return to music.<ref name="OC register"/> Young independently released a self-titled four-song EP in 2007,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://myspace.com/brettyoungsmusic/music/album/-b-ep-2007-b-6525511 |title=Brett Young Music |work=Myspace }}</ref> then ''Make Believe'' in 2011,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://play.spotify.com/album/25NSP90jHNeT0hotJY8NcN |title=Make Believe |work=Spotify}}</ref> followed by the albums ''Brett Young'',<ref>{{cite web |url=https://myspace.com/brettyoungsmusic/music/album/brett-young-19177818 |title=Brett Young |work=Myspace }}</ref> ''On Fire'',<ref>{{cite web |url=https://myspace.com/brettyoungsmusic/music/album/on-fire-18957794 |title=On Fire |work=Myspace }}</ref> and ''Broken Down'' in 2012–13.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://soundcloud.com/brettyoungmusic/sets/broken-down-acoustic-album |title=Broken Down |work=SoundCloud }}</ref>

After eight years based in Los Angeles, Young moved to Nashville.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboot.com/brett-young-ep-2016/ |title=Country Stardom Wasn't Brett Young's First Career Choice |first= Gayle |last=Thompson |date=July 3, 2016 |work=The Boot}}</ref> He was soon signed by the [[Big Machine Label Group]] in August 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bigmachinelabelgroup.com/big-machine-label-group-signs-brett-young/ |title=Big Machine Label Group Signs Brett Young |work=Big Machine Label Group |date=August 21, 2015 |access-date=November 30, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160603192710/http://www.bigmachinelabelgroup.com/big-machine-label-group-signs-brett-young/ |archive-date=June 3, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===2016–present: ''Brett Young'' EP and album===
[[File:Brett Young in concert in July 2017.jpg|thumb|upright=1.2|Brett Young performing in 2017]]
In February 2016, Young released a six-song [[Brett Young (EP)|self-titled EP]].<ref name=kfrog>{{cite web |url=http://kfrog.cbslocal.com/2016/04/29/stagecoach-behind-the-artist-brett-young/ |title=Stagecoach Behind the Artist Brett Young |date=April 29, 2016 |first=Monique |last=Parra |work=K-FROG |access-date=November 30, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161201020106/http://kfrog.cbslocal.com/2016/04/29/stagecoach-behind-the-artist-brett-young/ |archive-date=December 1, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> "[[Sleep Without You]]" was released as the first single from the EP in April 2016. Young wrote the song with Justin Ebach and Kelly Archer.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Thompson|first1=Gayle|title=Story Behind the Song: Brett Young, 'Sleep Without You'|url=http://theboot.com/brett-young-sleep-without-you-lyrics/|access-date=May 5, 2016|work=The Boot|date=April 15, 2016}}</ref> The second single to be released from the album "[[In Case You Didn't Know (Brett Young song)|In Case You Didn't Know]]", was released January 9, 2017.  The song reached number one on the [[Country Airplay]] chart,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7816571/brett-young-first-country-airplay-no-1-chris-stapleton |title='Case' Closed: Brett Young Earns First Country Airplay No. 1 |date=May 31, 2017 |first= Jim |last=Asker |work=Billboard }}</ref> and was certified triple Platinum by RIAA.<ref name=RIAA /> The album's third single, "Like I Loved You" released to country radio on July 17, 2017.

On February 10, 2017, Young released his self-titled debut studio album ''[[Brett Young (album)|Brett Young]]''. The album was produced by [[Dann Huff]], and released through [[Big Machine Label Group]]. It debuted at number two on the [[Top Country Albums]] chart.  Brett is currently touring with his band which includes lead guitarist Keaton Simons, drummer Billy Hawn, Keys/guitarist Matt Ferranti, and bass guitarist Noah Needleman.

===2018–2020: ''Ticket to L.A.''===
In December 2018, Young released his sophomore album ''[[Ticket to L.A.]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/brett-young-interview-new-album-ticket-to-la-gavin-degraw-766084/ |title=Brett Young Talks New Album 'Ticket to L.A.,' Gavin DeGraw Influence|first= Jon |last= Freeman |work=Rolling Stone |date= December 7, 2018}}</ref> The lead single for the album, "Here Tonight", which he co-wrote with Ben Caver, Justin Ebach and [[Charles Kelley]], was released in September 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/brett-young-here-tonight-today-show-768106/ |title=See Brett Young's Driving 'Here Tonight' on 'Today' |first=Jon |last=Freeman |work=Rolling Stone |date=December 12, 2018 }}</ref> It reached at number one on the Country Airplay in April 2019. The album's second single, "[[Catch (Brett Young song)|Catch]]" released to country radio on June 3, 2019.

===2020–present: ''Weekends Look a Little Different These Days''===
In April 2020, Young released the single "[[Lady (Brett Young song)|Lady]]", which he wrote about his wife and daughter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/brett-young-lady/|title=Brett Young's 'Lady' Is a Song for His Daughter, and Her Mother|last=Stefano|first=Angela|date=May 1, 2020|accessdate=June 8, 2021|work=[[Taste of Country]]}}</ref> It serves as the lead-off single to his third studio album ''[[Weekends Look a Little Different These Days]]'', which was released on June 4, 2021.<ref name="SLN">{{cite web|url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/news/brett-young-announces-weekends-look-a-little-different-these-days/|title=Brett Young Announces 'Weekends Look a Little Different These Days'|last=Parton|first=Chris|date=April 27, 2021|accessdate=June 4, 2021|work=Sounds Like Nashville}}</ref> "Lady" reached number one on the Country Airplay chart in April 2021. The album's second single "Not Yet" was released the same month.

==Personal life==
In February 2018, Young got engaged to his girlfriend Taylor Mills.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://people.com/music/brett-young-engaged-taylor-mills-proposal-details/ |title=Brett Young Is Engaged to Taylor Mills: All the Details on His Romantic Proposal |first=Sarah|last=Michaud|work=People|date=February 26, 2018|access-date=November 12, 2018}}</ref> The couple wed in [[Palm Desert, California]] on November 3, 2018. On April 6, 2019, they announced they are expecting their first child in the fall 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/brett-young-expecting-first-child-wife-taylor-pregnant/|title = Brett Young is Going to be a Dad! Country Star Expecting First Child with Wife Taylor}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://people.com/country/brett-young-marries-taylor-mills-first-photo/ |title=Brett Young Marries Longtime Love Taylor Mills in Stunning California Wedding — All the Details |first1=Melody|last1=Chiu|first2=Sarah|last2=Michaud|work=People|date=November 3, 2018|access-date=November 12, 2018}}</ref> Their daughter Presley was born on October 21, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/brett-young-welcomes-daughter-presley-elizabeth-first-photo/|title=Brett Young and Wife Taylor Welcome Daughter Presley Elizabeth: See Her First Photo|last1=Akoulitchev|first1=Octavia|last2=Michaud|first2=Sarah|date=October 24, 2019|access-date=October 24, 2019|work=People}}</ref> On January 27, 2021, the announced they are expecting their second child in the summer 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/brett-young-wife-pregnant-expecting-second-child-exclusive/|title=Brett Young and Wife Taylor Expecting Second Child: 'We Feel Very Blessed'|last1=VanHoose|first1=Benjamin|last2=Michaud|first2=Sarah|date=January 27, 2021|access-date=January 27, 2021|work=People}}</ref> Their second daughter Rowan was born on July 21, 2021.
