'''Millie Bobby Brown''' (born 19 February 2004) is a British actress and model.<ref>{{Citation|title=Millie Bobby Brown, Finn Wolfhard & Noah Schnapp Answer the Web's Most Searched Questions {{!}} WIRED|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PqGehRgKTLo|language=en|access-date=7 April 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=PqGehRgKTLo|title=Millie Bobby Brown, Finn Wolfhard & Noah Schnapp Answer the Web's Most Searched Questions|website=YouTube|access-date=21 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Portfolio|url=https://www.imgmodels.com/milliebobbybrown|access-date=27 March 2021|website=IMG Models}}</ref> In 2018, Brown was featured in the [[Time 100|''Time'' 100]] list of the world's most influential people,<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Millie Bobby Brown: The World's 100 Most Influential People|url=https://time.com/collection/most-influential-people-2018/5238181/millie-bobby-brown/|url-status=live|magazine=Time|language=en-us|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200510221443/https://time.com/collection/most-influential-people-2018/5238181/millie-bobby-brown/|archive-date=10 May 2020|access-date=4 May 2020}}</ref> and was appointed as a [[List of UNICEF Goodwill Ambassadors|UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador]], the then youngest person selected for this position.<ref name="National">{{cite web|date=15 May 2019|title=National ambassadors|url=http://www.unicef.org/people/people_nationalambassadors.html|access-date=19 February 2020|publisher=UNICEF}}</ref>

Following a series of guest roles on television, Brown achieved acclaim for her role as [[Eleven (Stranger Things)|Eleven]] in the [[Netflix]] sci-fi drama series ''[[Stranger Things]]'' (2016–present), which earned her two [[Primetime Emmy Award]] nominations for [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series|Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series]], and two [[Screen Actors Guild Awards|Screen Actors Guild Award]] nominations for [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Drama Series|Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Drama Series]]. She is one of the youngest nominees for the award.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.indiewire.com/2016/07/stranger-things-millie-bobby-brown-interview-eleven-11-netflix-season-2-1201707207/|title=Meet 'Stranger Things' Breakout Millie Bobby Brown, aka Lucky Number Eleven|last=Travers|first=Ben|date=19 July 2016|website=[[IndieWire]]|access-date=22 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160722135117/http://www.indiewire.com/2016/07/stranger-things-millie-bobby-brown-interview-eleven-11-netflix-season-2-1201707207/|archive-date=22 July 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

Brown made her feature debut with a lead role in the monster film [[Godzilla: King of the Monsters (2019 film)|''Godzilla: King of the Monsters'']] (2019), a character she reprised in the sequel ''[[Godzilla vs. Kong]]'' (2021). She also starred in and produced the Netflix mystery film [[Enola Holmes (film)|''Enola Holmes'']] (2020).
