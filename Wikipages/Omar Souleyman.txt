'''Omar Souleyman''' ({{lang-ar|عمر سليمان}}) is a Syrian singer and farmer from the village of [[Tell Tamer]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thequietus.com/articles/04413-omar-souleyman-jazeera-nights-album-review|title=Review: Omar Souleyman, Jazeera Nights|last=Barry|first=Robert|date=9 June 2010|publisher=The Quietus|access-date=12 March 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2010/10/31/omar_souleyman_to_bring_dabke_20_to_paramount_theatre/|title=Souleyman: Dabke 2.0|last=Krukowski|first=Damon|date=31 October 2010|newspaper=boston.com|access-date=12 March 2015}}</ref> near the city of [[Ras al-Ayn, al-Hasakah|Ra's al-'Ayn]] on the [[Syria–Turkey border]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://omarsouleyman.virb.com/bio |title=omarsouleyman.com (official web site) |publisher=Omarsouleyman.virb.com |access-date=2014-08-02}}</ref> He started his career as a part-time wedding singer in his native [[al-Hasakah Governorate]], and while he is a Sunni Arab, he emphasises the influence its culturally diverse milieu has had on his style: "My music is from the community I come from – the Kurdish, the [[Assyrian people|Ashuri]], the Arabic, they're all in this community. Even Turkish because it's so near, it's just across the border."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/oct/18/omar-souleyman-interview|title=Omar Souleyman: from wedding singer to the world|last=Beaumont-Thomas|first=Ben|date=18 October 2013|access-date=1 March 2015}}</ref>

==Career==

Omar Souleyman began his career in 1994, working with a number of musicians with whom he still performs. He has released an estimated five hundred studio and live albums under his name.{{citation needed|date=May 2020}} Eighty percent of those releases are recordings made at weddings and presented to the married couple, which are later copied and sold at local kiosks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.andymorganwrites.com/omar-souleyman-love-him-or-hate-him/ |title=OMAR SOULEYMAN – Love him or hate him |publisher=Andy Morgan Writes |access-date=2014-08-02}}</ref> The originality of his music is due to the fact that it mixes sounds of keyboards, electronic beats and vocals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/aug/20/omar-souleyman-syrian-wedding-singer-wooing-the-west|title = Omar Souleyman: The Syrian wedding singer wooing the west|date = 20 August 2015}}</ref>

In October 2013 a newly recorded album ''Wenu Wenu'' was released by UK label [[Domino Recording Company|Ribbon Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/51580-omar-souleyman-announces-four-tet-produced-lp-wenu-wenu/ |title=Omar Souleyman Announces Four Tet-Produced LP Wenu Wenu &#124; News |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2013-07-18 |access-date=2014-08-02}}</ref>

==Collaborations and notable appearances==
He appeared at the [[Glastonbury Festival]] in 2011, and performed during Chaos in Tejas in Austin, Texas in June 2011.<ref>{{cite web|last=Levy|first=A|title=Chaos in Tejas Preview: Omar Souleyman, The King of Syrian Techno|url=http://austinist.com/2011/05/18/omar_souleyman_the_king_of_syrian_t.php|work=The Austinist|access-date=4 September 2011}}</ref> In August 2011, Omar Souleyman appeared at [[Paredes de Coura Festival|Paredes de Coura]] in [[Portugal]]. Souleyman was chosen by [[Caribou (musician)|Caribou]] to perform at the [[All Tomorrow's Parties (music festival)|ATP Nightmare Before Christmas]] festival that they co-curated in December 2011 in Minehead, England.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atpfestival.com/events/nightmare2011.php |title=ATP Nightmare Before Christmas |publisher=Atpfestival.com |access-date=2014-08-02}}</ref>

In 2011 he recorded three remixes for [[Björk]]'s ''[[Biophilia (album)|Biophilia]]'', all found on the second disc of her "[[Crystalline (song)|Crystalline]] Series".

In 2013 he worked with British producer [[Four Tet]] on his album ''Wenu Wenu''. He collaborated with Four Tet again on his 2015 release ''Bahdeni Nami''.

In August 2013, before his performance at the ''Way Out West'' festival in [[Gothenburg]], [[Sweden]], Swedish authorities initially denied his artist visa out of concern that he would request asylum. His visa was granted two days before the festival.<ref>[https://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2054&artikel=5611154 Omar Souleyman granted a visa], Radio Sweden 7 August 2012</ref> In December 2013, he performed at the [[Nobel Peace Prize Concert]] in [[Oslo, Norway]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nobelpeaceprizeconcert.org/artists/omar/ |title=Omar Souleyman |publisher=[[Nobel Peace Prize Concert]] |access-date=December 12, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131216170258/http://www.nobelpeaceprizeconcert.org/artists/omar/ |archive-date=December 16, 2013 }}</ref>

In June 2014 he performed at The Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival in [[Manchester, Tennessee]]. In July he performed at [[Roskilde Festival]], Denmark and at the Mostly Jazz, Funk and Soul Festival, in [[Birmingham]], UK. He then performed at One Love Festival in [[Istanbul]] on 16 June 2014.<ref>http://www.redbull.com/tr/tr/music/stories/1331657182214/omar-souleyman-one-love-festival-istanbul</ref> In August 2014 he performed at [[Pukkelpop]] Festival in Kiewit, [[Hasselt]] in Belgium.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pukkelpop.be/nl/history/2014/|title=Pukkelpop 2014|website=pukkelpop.be|access-date=20 June 2017}}</ref> Later that summer on 31 August he played the Electric Picnic festival in [[Ireland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/2.2117/electric-picnic-omar-souleyman-acid-house-via-the-levant-1.1913322|title=Electric Picnic: Omar Souleyman - Acid House via the Levant|website=irishtimes.com|access-date=20 June 2017}}</ref> He also played at Treefort Music Festival in [[Boise, Idaho]] in March 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://boisestatepublicradio.org/post/syrian-wedding-singer-omar-souleyman-among-new-treefort-music-fest-performers|title=Syrian Wedding Singer Omar Souleyman Among New Treefort Music Fest Performers|first=Frankie|last=Barnhill|website=boisestatepublicradio.org|date=16 January 2015|access-date=20 June 2017}}</ref> In October 2017 he performed at [[Bristol]]'s SimpleThings festival.

== Personal life ==
Souleyman has been living in [[Turkey]] since the start of the [[Syrian Civil War]] in 2011, which heavily impacted his home region.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/oct/18/omar-souleyman-interview|title=Omar Souleyman: from wedding singer to the world|last=Beaumont-Thomas|first=Ben|date=18 October 2013|access-date=21 March 2018}}</ref> While in Turkey, Souleyman established a free bakery in the border town of [[Akçakale]], in order to serve poor families from Turkey and Syria refugees. In November 2021, he was arrested on charges of terrorism, with the Turkish authorities alleging that he was a member of the [[Kurdistan Workers' Party]] (PKK).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2021/nov/17/syrian-musician-omar-souleyman-held-on-terrorism-charges-in-turkey|title=Syrian musician Omar Souleyman held on terrorism charges in Turkey|first=Bethan|last=McKernan|location=[[Istanbul]]|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=17 November 2021|access-date=17 November 2021}}</ref> Souleyman was released two days later.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Monroe|first=Jazz|date=19 November 2021|title=Omar Souleyman Released After Questioning in Turkey|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/omar-souleyman-released-after-questioning-in-turkey/|access-date=19 November 2021|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|location=[[New York City|New York]]|publisher=[[Condé Nast]]|language=en-US}}</ref>
