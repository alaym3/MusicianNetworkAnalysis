'''Icehouse''' is a rock band from Sydney, formed in 1977.<!-- NB: Australian English treats a band name as plural as per British English.  -->
Initially known in Australia for their [[Pub rock (Australia)|pub-rock]] style, the band later achieved mainstream success playing [[New wave music|new-wave]] and [[synthpop]] music and attained Top 10 singles chart success locally and in both Europe and the U.S.<ref name="McF">{{cite book |title= [[Encyclopedia of Australian Rock and Pop]] |last= McFarlane |first= Ian | author-link= Ian McFarlane |publisher= [[Allen & Unwin]] |year= 1999 |chapter= Encyclopedia entry for 'Icehouse' |chapter-url= http://www.whammo.com.au/encyclopedia.asp?articleid=937 |isbn= 1-86448-768-2 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20040917195007/http://www.whammo.com.au/encyclopedia.asp?articleid=937|access-date= 12 January 2009 |archive-date= 17 September 2004}}</ref> The mainstay of both Flowers and Icehouse has been [[Iva Davies]] (singer-songwriter, record producer, guitar, bass, keyboards, [[oboe]])<ref name="ARDb">{{cite web | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130929120725/http://hem.passagen.se/honga/database/i/icehouse.html | url = http://hem.passagen.se/honga/database/i/icehouse.html | title = The Flowers / Icehouse | last1 = Holmgren | first1 = Magnus | publisher = [[Australian Rock Database]]. Passagen.se (Magnus Holmgren) | archive-date = 29 September 2013 | access-date = 21 March 2014 }}</ref> supplying additional musicians as required. The name "Icehouse", adopted in 1981, comes from an old, cold flat Davies lived in and the strange building across the road populated by itinerant people.<ref name="Count">{{cite web |url= http://www.countdownmemories.com/exclusive_interviews/iva_davies_interview.html |title= An interview with Iva Davies |last= Grech |first= Jason |date= 23 July 2004 |publisher= Countdown Memories |access-date= 16 June 2008 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20071108014704/http://www.countdownmemories.com/exclusive_interviews/iva_davies_interview.html | archive-date = 8 November 2007}}</ref>

Davies and Icehouse extended the use of synthesizers particularly the [[Sequential Circuits Prophet-5]] ("[[Love in Motion (Icehouse song)|Love in Motion]]", 1981), [[Linn LM-1|Linn drum machine]] ("[[Hey Little Girl]]", 1982) and [[Fairlight CMI]] (''[[Razorback (film)|Razorback]]'' trailer, 1983) in Australian popular music.<ref name="McF"/><ref name="Count"/><ref name="Warner">{{cite web|url= http://www.warnermusic.com.au/artist,w_artist,49247 |title= Artist: Icehouse |publisher= Warner Music Australia |access-date= 16 June 2008 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20080611054259/http://www.warnermusic.com.au/artist%2Cw_artist%2C49247 |archive-date= 11 June 2008 |url-status= dead |df= dmy-all }}</ref><ref name="NFSA">{{cite web|url= http://colsearch.nfsa.afc.gov.au/nfsa/search/display/display.w3p;adv=no;group=;groupequals=;holdingType=;page=6;parentid=;query=razorback;querytype=;rec=8;resCount=10 |archive-url= https://archive.today/20121127131218/http://colsearch.nfsa.afc.gov.au/nfsa/search/display/display.w3p;adv=no;group=;groupequals=;holdingType=;page=6;parentid=;query=razorback;querytype=;rec=8;resCount=10 |url-status= dead |archive-date= 27 November 2012 |title= ''Razorback'' trailer details |publisher= National Film & Sound Archive (NFSA) |access-date= 16 June 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0087981/fullcredits#cast |title= ''Razorback'' (1984) full cast & crew |publisher= [[Internet Movie Database]] (IMDb) |access-date= 16 June 2008 }}</ref> Their best-known singles on the Australian charts included "[[Great Southern Land]]", "Hey Little Girl", "[[Crazy (Icehouse song)|Crazy]]", "[[Electric Blue (song)|Electric Blue]]" and "[[My Obsession (Icehouse song)|My Obsession]]"; with Top Three albums being ''[[Icehouse (album)|Icehouse]]'' (1980, as Flowers), ''[[Primitive Man (album)|Primitive Man]]'' (1982) and ''[[Man of Colours]]'' (1987).<ref name="McF"/><ref name="Kent">{{cite book|title= [[Kent Music Report|Australian Chart Book 1970–1992]]|last= Kent|first= David |author-link= David Kent (historian)|publisher= Australian Chart Book|location= [[St Ives, New South Wales|St Ives]], N.S.W.|year= 1993|isbn= 0-646-11917-6}}</ref>

Icehouse was inducted into the [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA) [[ARIA Hall of Fame|Hall of Fame]] on 16 August 2006.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.ariahalloffame.com.au/inductees_listing.htm |title= ARIA 2008 Hall of Fame inductees listing |publisher= [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA)|access-date= 11 June 2008 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080615011737/http://www.ariahalloffame.com.au/inductees_listing.htm | archive-date = 15 June 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.ariaawards.com.au/history-by-award.php?awardID=36 |title= Winners by Award: Hall of Fame |publisher= [[Australian Recording Industry Association|ARIA]] |access-date= 11 January 2008 |url-status= dead |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20090202052952/http://www.ariaawards.com.au/history-by-award.php?awardID=36 |archive-date= 2 February 2009 }}

ARIA described Icehouse as "one of the most successful Australian bands of the eighties and nineties... With an uncompromising approach to music production they created songs that ranged from pure pop escapism to edgy, lavish synthesised pieces..."<ref name="ARIA2006"/> Icehouse has produced eight top-ten albums and twenty top-forty singles in Australia, multiple top-ten hits in Europe and North America and album sales of over 28-times platinum in [[Australasia]] alone.<ref name="ARIA2006"/>

==1977–1980: Flowers==
{{Main|Iva Davies}}
Flowers were formed in Sydney in 1977 by Iva Davies (vocals, guitar, bass guitar, keyboards, oboe), a classically trained musician, and their main creative force; with bass player Keith Welsh.<ref name="McF"/><ref name="ARIA2006"/> Davies was working as a part-time cleaner at a squash court managed by Welsh's mother, they lived nearby and were both interested in forming a band.<ref name="Kruger">{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/music/songlines/2005/11/01/1130720527778.html|title=City Songlines|last=Kruger|first=Debbie|author-link=Debbie Kruger|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|publisher=[[Fairfax Media]]|date=2 November 2005|access-date=21 June 2008}}</ref> Additional musicians used by Flowers in 1978 were Michael Hoste on keyboards and Don Brown on drums.<ref name="ARDb"/> The band built up a strong following as a live act around the [[pub rock (Australia)|pub circuit]], providing distinctive [[cover version]]s of songs by [[Roxy Music]], [[David Bowie]], [[Lou Reed]], [[T.Rex (band)|T-Rex]], [[Ultravox]] and [[Brian Eno]].<ref name="McF"/> By the middle of 1979 John Lloyd (ex-[[Paul Kelly (Australian musician)|Paul Kelly and the Dots]]) replaced Don Brown on drums, with Anthony Smith (who was sometimes called Adam Hall), on keyboards, replacing Michael Hoste who remained associated with the band and later rejoined.<ref name="ARDb"/> After signing to the independent Regular Records label, distributed by [[Festival Records (Australia)|Festival]], Flowers released their debut single in May 1980, "[[Can't Help Myself (Flowers song)|Can't Help Myself]]" (written by Davies),<ref name="APRA">{{cite web|url=http://www.apra.com.au/cms/worksearch/worksearch.srvlt?action=workSearch&switchDet=Y |title=Australasian Performing Right Association (APRA) |publisher=[[Australasian Performing Right Association|APRA]] |access-date=21 June 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080505235657/http://www.apra.com.au/cms/worksearch/worksearch.srvlt?action=workSearch&switchDet=Y |archive-date=5 May 2008  }} Note: requires user to input song title e.g. CANT HELP MYSELF</ref> which hit the Australian Top 10 in June 1980.<ref name="Kent"/> This was followed by their debut album ''[[Icehouse (album)|Icehouse]]'', which reached No.&nbsp;4 on the National albums chart<ref name="Kent"/> and became one of the year's biggest selling albums in Australia.<ref name="McF"/> The album, co-produced by Cameron Allan ([[Mental As Anything]]'s producer)<ref>{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p147009|pure_url=yes}} |title=Cameron Allan credits |website=[[allmusic]] |access-date=12 June 2008 }}</ref> and Davies,<ref name="ARDb"/> made use of [[synthesizer|synthesisers]], including the [[Minimoog]], [[ARP String Synthesizer|Solina Strings]] and [[Oberheim OB-1]].<ref name="Warner"/> Hoste co-wrote four tracks with Davies and played additional keyboards,<ref name="APRA"/> with Smith continuing to provide the main keyboards.<ref name="McF"/> Further singles "[[We Can Get Together]]" and "[[Walls (Icehouse song)|Walls]]" from ''Icehouse'' also hit the Top 20.<ref name="Kent"/>

Flowers' popularity was recognised when they were awarded the 1980 ''[[TV Week]]'' / ''[[Countdown (Australian TV series)|Countdown]]'' Rock Awards '[[Johnny O'Keefe]] New Talent Award' ahead of [[The Dugites]], [[INXS]] and [[Karen Knowles]].<ref name="TVWeek">{{cite web |url=http://baseportal.com/cgi-bin/baseportal.pl?htx=/webgirl/main&cmd=list&range=64,8&cmd=all&Id=154 |title=''Countdown'' Archives show No.&nbsp;241 |publisher=baseportal.com |access-date=4 July 2008 }}</ref> They were also nominated for 'Best Album' and 'Best Album Cover' for ''Icehouse'' but lost on both to [[Cold Chisel]]'s ''[[East (Cold Chisel album)|East]]'', Iva Davies was nominated as 'Best Songwriter' but lost to Cold Chisel's [[Don Walker (musician)|Don Walker]].<ref name="TVWeek"/> At the award ceremony, Flowers performed "[[Icehouse (song)|Icehouse]]" with Davies framed within a cube of white neon tubing.<ref name="Count"/> In early 1981, Flowers signed to [[Chrysalis Records]] for European, Japanese, UK and U.S. releases;<ref name="ARDb"/> they had to change their name due to legal restrictions and to prevent confusion with a Scottish group The Flowers.<ref name="McF"/><ref name="Bits">{{cite web |url=http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/storybitsandpieces.html |title=The Icehouse story – bits and pieces |publisher=Spellbound: a fanzine for Icehouse |editor=Kristin Lones and Cheryl Krueger |access-date=11 June 2008 }}</ref> Their last performance under the name Flowers was on 27 June 1981 at the Capitol Theatre in Sydney,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/storynew1.html |title=The Icehouse story – early years |last=Davies |first=Neville |publisher=Spellbound fanzine |access-date=24 June 2008 }}</ref> they chose the name of their album to become known as Icehouse.<ref name="McF"/>

==1981–1989: Icehouse==
As Icehouse, the band spent the second half of 1981 touring the UK, Canada and the U.S., whilst Chrysalis released most of their Flowers material under the name Icehouse.<ref name="McF"/> The single "Icehouse" was released in Europe and created some interest in the UK, partly because of a video directed by [[Russell Mulcahy]],<ref name="Count"/><ref name="Warner"/><ref name="Video">{{cite web |url=http://www.mvdbase.com/artist.php?last=Icehouse&first= |title=Icehouse artist videography |publisher=mvdbase.com |editor=Alex S. Garcia |year=2008 |access-date=19 June 2008 }}</ref> while in the U.S. the song peaked at No.&nbsp;28 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Mainstream Rock Tracks|Top Tracks]] chart in 1981.<ref name="BillS">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p4538|pure_url=yes}} |title=''Billboard'' singles charts |website=[[allmusic]] |access-date=11 June 2008 }}</ref> "Icehouse" had been written by Davies<ref name="APRA"/> when he lived at 18 Tryon Road, [[Lindfield, New South Wales|Lindfield]] in an old, cold flat of a two-storey mansion – across the street was a dishevelled house which had its lights on all night peopled by short-term residents.<ref name="Kruger"/> Davies later learned it was a half-way house for psychiatric and drug rehab patients.<ref name="Kruger"/> The first U.S. single "We Can Get Together" peaked at No.&nbsp;62 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="BillS"/> A single-only release, "[[Love in Motion (Icehouse song)|Love in Motion]]", was recorded by Davies using the [[Sequential Circuits Prophet-5|Sequential Circuits Prophet 5]],<ref name="Warner"/> and became the first new recording credited to Icehouse;<ref name="McF"/> it hit the Australian Top 10 in November 1981.<ref name="Kent"/> Icehouse split up late in 1981, Keith Welsh later becoming manager of Australian bands [[Do-Re-Mi (band)|Do-Ré-Mi]] and [[Boom Crash Opera]].<ref name="McF"/><ref name="ARDb"/>

===1981–1983: ''Primitive Man'', ''Fresco EP''===
In January 1982 Davies recorded ''[[Primitive Man (album)|Primitive Man]]'' essentially as a solo project,<ref name="McF"/> it was co-produced with [[Keith Forsey]]<ref name="ARDb"/> who had worked with [[Giorgio Moroder]] and later worked with [[Simple Minds]].<ref name="McF"/> Forsey supplied additional percussion; Davies supplied vocals, guitars, keyboards (Prophet 5), bass and programmed the Linn drum machine.<ref>{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r622259/credits|pure_url=yes}} |title=''Primitive Man'' (bonus tracks) credits |website=[[Allmusic]] guide |access-date=12 July 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.discogs.com/release/936473 |title=Icehouse: ''Masterfile'' |publisher=Discogs |access-date=24 June 2008 }}</ref> Released in August 1982 as an Icehouse album, ''Primitive Man'' reached No.&nbsp;3 on the National album charts<ref name="Kent"/> and provided their international breakthrough single, "[[Hey Little Girl]]" which peaked at No.&nbsp;7 in Australia,<ref name="Kent"/> No.&nbsp;2 in Switzerland,<ref name="Swiss singles">{{cite web|title=Search results for:Icehouse |publisher=hitparade.ch UK,<ref name="UKCharts">{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artists/ |title=Icehouse on UK charts |publisher=[[Official Charts Company]] |access-date=19 June 2008  }}</ref> Sweden and tour, Davies re-assembled Icehouse with Hoste and Lloyd, and new members: Robert Kretschmer (guitar, backing vocals), [[Guy Pratt]] (bass guitar, backing vocals) and Andy Qunta (keyboards, backing ===1984–1986: ''Sidewalk'', ''Boxes'', and ''Measure for Measure''===
Icehouse's next album ''[[Sidewalk (album)|Sidewalk]]'' was released in 1984 and peaked at No.&nbsp;8 on the National albums chart with singles "[[Taking the Town]]" peaked at No.&nbsp;29 in May, "Don't Believe Anymore" peaked at No.&nbsp;31 August and "Dusty Pages" peaked at No.&nbsp;82 in November.<ref name="Kent"/> Davies used the [[Fairlight CMI]] digital [[Sampler (musical instrument)|sampling]] synthesizer exclusively on this more sombre and reflective album.<ref name="McF"/> Davies was commissioned to compose and record the score for the [[Sydney Dance Company]] production of [[Graeme Murphy]]'s work ''[[Boxes (Icehouse album)|Boxes]]''; from Icehouse, Davies only used Kretschmer, with percussion being supplied by Masaki Tanazawa.  ''Boxes'' was first performed live at the [[Sydney Opera House]] on 7 November 1985.<ref name="McF"/> After the ''Boxes'' project, Icehouse was Davies, Kretschmer, Pratt and Qunta with former [[The Members|Members]] member Simon Lloyd (sax, trumpet, keyboards) and [[Steve Jansen]] (drums, percussion).<ref name="ARDb"/>

Their 1986 release ''[[Measure for Measure (album)|Measure for Measure]]'' featured [[Brian Eno]] as an additional performer; it provided the Australian singles of "[[No Promises (Icehouse song)|No Promises]]" No.&nbsp;19, "Baby, You're So Strange" No.&nbsp;12, "Mr. Big" No.&nbsp;14 and "Cross the Border".<ref name="Kent"/> Further inroads into the U.S. market occurred with "No Promises" peaking at No.&nbsp;9 on ''Billboard'' Mainstream Rock chart and "Cross the Border" reaching No.&nbsp;19.<ref name="BillS"/> In Canada, "No Promises" reached No.61.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.collectionscanada.gc.ca/obj/028020/f2/nlc008388.0719.pdf| title=RPM Top 100 Singles – August 30, 1986}}</ref> On tour for ''Measure for Measure'', Icehouse were Davies, Kretschmer, Lloyd and Qunta with Glenn Krawczyk on bass (replacing new member Vito Portolesi after nine shows) and Paul Wheeler on drums.<ref name="McF"/><ref name="ARDb"/> On 14 August 1986 their performance at New York City's [[The Ritz (rock club)|The Ritz]] was recorded as ''Icehouse: Live at the Ritz'', directed by [[John Jopson]] and broadcast on U.S. TV on 9 September 1987 and on Australian TV on 15 November 1987;<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0893528/ |title=''Icehouse: Live at the Ritz'' |website=[[Internet Movie Database|IMDb]] |date=9 September 1987 |access-date=23 July 2008 }}</ref> it was released on VHS and NTSC formats.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.discogs.com/release/857944 |title=Icehouse – ''Live at the Ritz'' |publisher=Discogs |access-date=25 June 2008 }}</ref> Jopson was subsequently used to direct four music videos for Icehouse.<ref name="Video"/> Stephen Morgan replaced Krawczyk on bass during 1987.<ref name="ARDb"/>

===1987–1989: ''Man of Colours'' and ''Great Southern Land''===

Their best-selling album is 1987's ''[[Man of Colours]]'',<ref name="McF"/> which contained the Australian hit singles "Crazy" which peaked at No.&nbsp;4 in July, "[[Electric Blue (song)|Electric Blue]]", co-written by Davies and [[John Oates]] of U.S. band [[Hall & Oates]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.apra.com.au/site/public/searchworksresult.stm?worktitle=ELECTRIC%20BLUE&switchdet=Y 1988 and "Nothing Too Serious" No.&nbsp;29 in May 1988.<ref name="Kent"/> It was the first Australian album to have five singles charting in the top 30, it remained at No.&nbsp;1 on the Australian album charts for eleven weeks<ref name="Kent"/> and has sold over 700,000 copies.<ref name="Warner"/> In Canada, "Crazy" reached No. 22,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.collectionscanada.gc.ca/obj/028020/f2/nlc008388.0932.pdf| title=RPM Top 100 Singles – February 6, 1988}}</ref> "Electric Blue" reached No. 10,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.collectionscanada.gc.ca/obj/028020/f2/nlc008388.8580.pdf| title=RPM Top 100 Singles – July 30, 1988}}</ref> With U.S. chart success for "Crazy", which reached No.&nbsp;14 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and No.&nbsp;10 on its [[Hot Mainstream Rock Tracks|Mainstream Rock]] chart, and "Electric Blue" peaked at No.&nbsp;7 Hot 100 and No.&nbsp;10 Mainstream, the band had reached their zenith of popularity, the album ''Man of Colours'' reached No.&nbsp;43 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="BillS"/><ref name="BillA">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p4538|pure_url=yes}} |title=''Billboard'' albums charts |website=[[allmusic]] |access-date=13 June 2008 }}</ref> ''Man of Colours'' was lauded in Australia during 1988, it won two [[ARIA Music Awards|ARIA Awards]], 'Album of the Year' and 'Highest-Selling Album'.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ariaawards.com.au/history-by-artist.php?letter=I&artist=Icehouse The only Icehouse releases for 1989 were a single "Touch the Fire" which reached No.&nbsp;13 in Australia in November and a compilation double album / video collection ''[[Great Southern Land (album)|Great Southern Land]]''.<ref name="ARDb"/><ref name="Kent"/><ref name="AusCharts">{{cite web|url=http://australian-charts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Icehouse |title=Discography Icehouse |publisher=australian-charts.com |access-date=13 June 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081202120317/http://australian-charts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Icehouse |archive-date=2 December 2008  }}</ref> December 1989 saw the release of "Jimmy Dean" as a single which reached No.&nbsp;47 on the ARIA singles charts.<ref name="Kent"/><ref name="AusCharts"/>

==1990–present: Iva Davies and Icehouse==
===1990–1992: ''Code Blue'', ''Masterfile''===
Qunta left at about this time with Icehouse recording their next album, ''[[Code Blue (Icehouse album)|Code Blue]]'' released in October, it was more ambitious and resulted in a brilliant album.<ref name="McF"/> Singles released from the album were "Big Fun" which peaked at No.&nbsp;47 on the ARIA singles charts in August, "Miss Divine" at No.&nbsp;16 in October and "Anything is Possible" at No.&nbsp;49 in January 1991.<ref name="AusCharts"/> For ''Code Blue'' recording, Icehouse were Davies, Lloyd, Morgan and Wheeler, for touring they added guitarist [[Paul Gildea]] and keyboardist Roger Mason (ex-[[Models (band)|Models]]).<ref name="McF"/><ref name="ARDb"/> In 1992, [[EMI]] released a compilation, ''[[Masterfile (Icehouse album)|Masterfile]]'' containing a new version of "Love in Motion" also released as a single featuring Davies in a duet with [[Christina Amphlett]] of [[Divinyls]].<ref name="ARDb"/>

===1993–1995: ''Full Circle'', ''Big Wheel'', and ''Berlin''===
''[[Full Circle (Icehouse album)|Full Circle]]'', an entire album of remixes was recorded during 1993, it included the track "Shakin' the Cage" which was released on the ''[[Spin One]]'' [[Extended play|EP]] in early 1993 and was followed by the album ''[[Big Wheel (Icehouse album)|Big Wheel]]'' in November, which contained the singles "Satellite" in October and "Big Wheel" in March 1994, while the ''Full Circle'' album itself was not released until December 1994; neither albums nor singles had any Top 40 chart success.<ref name="McF"/><ref name="AusCharts"/>

By 1995, Davies was again involved with the [[Sydney Dance Company]], this time with their production of ''Berlin''. The musical score was a collection of cover versions of songs by [[David Bowie]], [[Brian Eno]], Simple Minds, [[The Psychedelic Furs]], [[Frank Sinatra]], [[Lou Reed]], [[Roxy Music]], [[XTC]], [[Talking Heads]], [[The Velvet Underground]], [[Public Image Ltd.|PiL]], [[The Cure]] and [[Killing Joke]],<ref name="McF"/> which saw Davies collaborating with pianist [[Max Lambert]] in the development of the music.<ref name="EMI">{{cite web |url=http://www.emimusicpub.com/worldwide/artist_profile/iva-davies-icehouse_profile.html |publisher=[[EMI]] |title=Artist Profile – Iva Davies (Icehouse) |last=Hamblion |first=Maree |access-date=12 July 2008 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080708041231/http://www.emimusicpub.com/worldwide/artist_profile/iva-davies-icehouse_profile.html |archive-date = 8 July 2008}}</ref> Sessions were released as ''[[The Berlin Tapes (album)|The Berlin Tapes]]'' under the name Iva Davies and Icehouse in 1995 on DIVA (Iva Davies own label) / Massive Records for Australian release and [[Warner Music Australia|Warner Records]] for international release in 2002.<ref name="ARDb"/> As well as recording the ballet score, Davies and Icehouse performed live at each performance, ''Berlin'' ran for two seasons.<ref name="EMI"/> During 1999 Davies, together with Richard Tognetti and Christopher Gordon, composed ''The Ghost of Time'' as an expansion of "Great Southern Land" into a forty-minute performance for [[Sydney]]'s part of the worldwide millennial celebrations.<ref name="EMI"/><ref name="SydMill">{{cite web|url=http://www.cityofsydney.nsw.gov.au/nye/1999/press2.asp |title=Sydney Millennium – Iva Davies |publisher=City of Sydney |editor=Veronica Ozolins |access-date=3 July 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20041103062943/http://www.cityofsydney.nsw.gov.au/nye/1999/press2.asp |archive-date=3 November 2004 }}</ref> On New Year's Eve in 1999, Davies (vocals, guitar), Tognetti (violin), [[Guy Pratt]] (bass), Krawczyk (drums) ROM=Pari ([[Taiko]] – Japanese drums) and [[Sydney Symphony]] Orchestra performed ''The Ghost of Time'' on the northern forecourt of [[Sydney Opera House]] alongside [[Port Jackson|Sydney Harbour]] and was televised around the world on [[CNN]] and other news networks.<ref name="EMI"/><ref name="SydMill"/> Davies had released the solo album, ''The Ghost of Time'' earlier in December 1999.

===2001–2010: ''Bipolar Poems'', remasters===
Davies has been working since 2001 on a proposed Icehouse album to be titled ''[[Bi-polar Poems]]''<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/latest1201.html |title=Inside the Icehouse |editor=Kristin Lones & Cheryl Krueger |date=17 December 2001 |publisher=Spellbound fanzine |access-date=26 June 2008 |archive-date=5 July 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080705181428/http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/latest1201.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> with certain tracks available between 2004 and 2008 from the Official Iva Davies – Icehouse website<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/latest0304.html |title=Inside the Icehouse |editor=Kristin Lones & Cheryl Krueger |date=29 March 2004 |publisher=Spellbound fanzine |access-date=26 June 2008 |archive-date=5 July 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080705180245/http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/latest0304.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> including "Your God Not Mine". As of 2020, the album is still unreleased.

In 2002, [[The Whitlams]] covered "Don't Believe Anymore" from ''Sidewalk'' for their album ''[[Torch The Moon]]'', which enjoyed moderate radio success. During 2002, Davies digitally remastered all of Flowers' / Icehouse's studio albums adding bonus tracks to each—they were released by [[Warner Music Australia]]. In 2003 Iva Davies contributed "Ghost of Time" and other music to the film score of the [[Russell Crowe]] epic ''[[Master and Commander: The Far Side of the World]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0311113/soundtrack |title=''Master and Commander: The Far Side of the World'' (2003) – soundtrack listing |website=[[Internet Movie Database|IMDb]] |access-date=13 June 2008 }}</ref> In 2004, ''[[Heroes (Icehouse album)|Heroes]]'', which was ''The Berlin Tapes'' repackaged from the Australian and Japanese versions, was released.<ref name="ARDb"/><ref>{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r949656|pure_url=yes}} |title=''Heroes'' review |website=[[allmusic]] |last=Viglione |first=Joe |access-date=13 June 2008 }}</ref> On 16 August 2006, Icehouse were inducted into the [[ARIA Hall of Fame]] alongside [[Midnight Oil]], [[Divinyls]] and [[Rose Tattoo]].<ref name="ARIA2006"/> Icehouse performed on 21 September 2007 with the line-up: Davies, Paul Gildea (guitars), Steve Morgan (bass), Peter Maslen (drums) and Glen Reither (keyboards, saxophone).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/insidetheicehouse.html |title=Inside the Icehouse |editor=Kristin Lones & Cheryl Krueger |date=26 May 2008 |publisher=Spellbound fanzine |access-date=26 June 2008 |archive-date=9 May 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080509194048/http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/insidetheicehouse.html |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Icehouse / Flowers featured on [[Triple M]]'s 2007 Essential Countdown with 14 songs including: "Great Southern Land" at No.&nbsp;13, "Don't Believe Any More" No.&nbsp;78, "Street Cafe" No.&nbsp;187, "Love in Motion" No.&nbsp;327, "We Can Get Together" No.&nbsp;428, and "Electric Blue" No.&nbsp;454.<ref name="TripleM"/>

From 15 June 2008, Davies was a judge on [[Seven Network]] TV series ''[[Battle of the Choirs]]''.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.theaustralian.news.com.au/story/0,25197,23841589-5016626,00.html |last=Blundell |first=Graeme |newspaper=[[The Australian]] |title=Choir wars |date=14 June 2008 |access-date=9 July 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080801232810/http://www.theaustralian.news.com.au/story/0%2C25197%2C23841589-5016626%2C00.html |archive-date=1 August 2008 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> Davies commented on the status of Icehouse:
{{Quote|"Last year and January this year we did a couple of private, corporate shows... but as far as live touring in front of the public, we haven't done that for a very long time... It's an extremely difficult thing to have to sing for two hours and in sheer practical terms I'd have to be a lot fitter than I am now... I wrote and recorded an album a while ago. That song ["Your God Not Mine"] was written in 1998... The album [''[[Bi-polar Poems]]''] is in various states of unfinished, but I keep getting projects which interrupt me working on it..."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.news.com.au/heraldsun/story/0,21985,23924934-2902,00.html |title=Iva Davies goes into Battle |newspaper=[[Herald Sun|The Herald Sun]] |last=Adams |first=Cameron |date=26 June 2008 |access-date=9 July 2008 }}</ref>|Iva Davies|26 June 2008}}

4 June 2009: Icehouse performed at the Sacred Heart Mission's "Heart of St Kilda Concert". ===2011–present: ''Icehouse'' 30th anniversary, ''White Heat: 30 Hits'' and ''DubHouse Live''===
On 6 April 2011, Icehouse and Universal Music Australia publicly announced a new partnership for sales and distribution of the band's material. Accompanying this were details of the planned issue of a 30th-anniversary edition of the band's debut album ''Icehouse''. This release marks the first time any of the band's catalogue has been available for commercial download.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://maytherockbewithyou.com/May_The_Rock_Be_With_You/News/Entries/2011/4/6_ICEHOUSE_-_30th_anniversary_of_Flowers_album.html |title=Icehouse – 30th anniversary of Flowers album |publisher=May The Rock Be With You}}</ref> The album was released on 20 May 2011 digitally and as a multi-disc set.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.triplem.com.au/melbourne/music/news/blog/exclusive-flowers-icehouse-30th-anniversary-album-quite-a-package-says-iva-davies/20110520-cdab.html |title=Flowers Icehouse 30th Anniversary Album Quite A Package, Says Iva Davies |publisher=[[Triple M]] |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110709205457/http://www.triplem.com.au/melbourne/music/news/blog/exclusive-flowers-icehouse-30th-anniversary-album-quite-a-package-says-iva-davies/20110520-cdab.html |archive-date=9 July 2011 }}</ref>

On 7 July 2011, the band issued a press release about the forthcoming release of a new greatest hits compilation, titled ''White Heat: 30 Hits''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.spellbound-icehouse.org/mediareleaseicehousewhiteheat.pdf|title=Media Release: Icehouse – White Heat|website=Spellbound-icehouse.org|access-date=5 November 2019}}</ref> The album was released on 26 August 2011.

Icehouse have started to play live gigs around Australia.  One of the most recent was at 3 December [[Homebake#2011|Homebake 2011]] concert, performing under the moniker of "Icehouse Plays Flowers", where they played songs from their first two albums (both released singles and unreleased songs) — "Icehouse", "We Can Get Together", "Skin", "Boulevard", "Great Southern Land" and "Sister." [[Keith Welsh]] joined them on stage, playing bass-guitar. Black-and-white original PR photos from the early 1980s were flashed as a backdrop behind the band as they played.

On 12 July 2012, the entire Icehouse studio catalogue was reissued. ''Primitive Man'' and ''Man of Colours'' also received a bonus DVD to celebrate their 30th and 25th anniversaries, respectively. The band will be promoting the two anniversary albums with the "Primitive Colours" tour.<ref>{{cite web|author=Paul Cashmere|url=http://www.noise11.com/news/iva-davies-talks-about-new-icehouse-reissues-20120713 |title=Iva Davies Talks About New Icehouse Reissues &#124; New Music &#124; Music News |publisher=Noise11 |date=13 July 2012 |access-date=13 December 2012}}</ref>

On 4 and 7 December, Icehouse performed two shows at the [[Esplanade Hotel (Melbourne)|Esplanade Hotel]] in Melbourne and the Oxford Art Factory in Sydney, respectively, under the moniker of "DubHouse performing reggae-styled versions of classic Icehouse songs and old reggae favourites".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://themusicnetwork.com/icehouse-goes-dubhouse/|title=Icehouse goes DubHOUSE|website=Themusicnetwork.com|date=27 October 2015}}</ref> These concerts spawned the release of a live album released in January 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=629756833756497&set=a.166662916732560.39883.143105565754962 |title=Icehouse – Tijdlijnfoto's |website=Facebook.com|access-date=29 June 2014}}</ref>

They also performed a 40th Anniversary concert at the Roche Estate, in the NSW Hunter Valley in March 2017. The full concert is available for free on YouTube, and is highly recommended for any fans to watch.

On 9 February 2020, Icehouse played the St. Kilda Festival in Melbourne, Flowers had played the 1st festival in 1980, so for the 40th anniversary Icehouse played the majority of the Flowers album plus a few covers that they would have played back then, a recording of the concert was released in October 2020 as ''Icehouse Plays Flowers''.

In 2021, a remix of "Hey Little Girl" was released by Australian duo Mark Vick and Danny Muller, with the record credited to The Antipodeans Vs Icehouse.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://superdeluxeedition.com/news/icehouse-single-gets-remix-treatment/|title = Icehouse single gets remix treatment – SuperDeluxeEdition}}</ref>

===TV Week / Countdown Awards===
''[[Countdown (Australian TV series)|Countdown]]'' was an Australian pop music TV series on national broadcaster [[ABC Television (Australian TV network)|ABC-TV]] from 1974–1987, it presented music awards from 1979–1987, initially in conjunction with magazine ''[[TV Week]]''. The TV Week / Countdown Awards were a combination of popular-voted and peer-voted awards.<ref name="CountdownMarch1987">{{Cite web | url = http://www.countdownmemories.com/magazines/pdfs/1987_03.pdf | title = Countdown to the Awards | work = Countdown Magazine |date=March 1987 | publisher = [[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]] (ABC) | format = [[Portable document format]] (PDF) | access-date = 16 December 2010 }}</ref><ref name="Count"/><ref name="TVWeek"/><ref name="OzAwards">{{cite web |url=http://users.ncable.net.au/~ronjeff/top40/oz_king.htm |title=Australian Music Awards |publisher=Neighbourhood Cable |editor=Ron Jeff |date=8 January 2008 |access-date=4 July 2008 }}</ref>
