'''In the Valley Below''' is an American band from [[Grand Rapids, Michigan]] that formed in [[Echo Park, California]]  in 2011. The band is led by Jeffrey Jacob Mendel (vocals, guitar) and Angela Gail Mattson (vocals, keyboard).<ref>[http://www.independent.com/news/2013/feb/14/getting-know-valley-below/ Getting to Know In the Valley Below]; Santa Barbara Independent; February 14, 2013</ref> The band's first studio album, ''[[The Belt (album)|The Belt]]'', was released in 2014, featuring the single "[[Peaches (In the Valley Below song)|Peaches]]", which peaked at number 18 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Alternative Songs]] chart in 2016. Their sophomore album and feature film ''The Pink Chateau'' was released on April 26, 2019 via [[Bright Antenna|Bright Antenna Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/elephant-ep/id1249462109|title=Elephant - EP by In The Valley Below on Apple Music|website=iTunes|access-date=2017-08-04}}</ref>

==History==
===Formation===
Mendel and Mattson are from [[Memphis, Tennessee]], and [[Muskegon, Michigan]], respectively. They met in the [[Los Angeles]] music scene, and played in several local bands before starting In The Valley Below. After a trip to [[SXSW]] in [[Austin, Texas]], they formed a new bond and decided they needed to write music together. In the Valley Below began in 2011<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/in-the-valley-below-mn0003128960/biography|title=In the Valley Below &#124; Biography & History|website=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=20 November 2020}}</ref> as a studio project, and they self released a five-song [[Extended play|EP]] before the end of that year.<ref>[http://www.buzzbands.la/2011/11/15/ears-wide-open-in-the-valley-below/ Ears Wide Open: In The Valley Below]; Buzzbands.LA; November 15, 2011</ref><ref>[http://www.deftune.com/2012/09/valley-concert-review/ A Concert Review]; DefTune; September 17, 2012</ref><ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/oct/21/in-the-valley-below-new-band In the Valley Below (New band of the day No 1,622)]; ''The Guardian''; October 21, 2013</ref>

===''The Belt'' (2014)===
Their debut album, ''The Belt'', was released in different territories on different record labels around the world throughout 2013 and 2014. Written, produced and engineered by Jeffrey and Angela, the [[LA Weekly]] listed their debut album among the 10 Best Albums of 2014 by L.A. Artists.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6545712/tomorrows-hits-in-the-valley-below-michael-ray-omi Tomorrow's Hits: In the Valley Below]; ''Billboard Magazine''; April 24, 2015</ref><ref>[http://www.laweekly.com/music/the-10-best-albums-of-2014-by-la-artists-5287070 The 10 Best Albums of 2014 by L.A. Artists]; ''LA Weekly''; December 16, 2014</ref><ref>[http://antennamag.com/in-the-valley-below-video-neverminders/ Premiere: In The Valley Below’s (Slightly NSFW) “Neverminders” Video]; ''Antenna Magazine''; September 17, 2014</ref> [[The Huffington Post]] named "[[Peaches (In the Valley Below song)|Peaches]]" the #5 song of 2014,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/a-sides-with-jon-chattman_b_6329000|title=A-Sides with Jon Chattman Top 54 Songs of 2014|date=16 December 2014|website=HuffPost.com|accessdate=20 November 2020}}</ref>  while [[Noisey]] named it their #17 track of the year 2013.<ref>[http://noisey.vice.com/de/blog/die-noisey-de-jahrescharts-platz-30-bis-10 Die Noisey DE Jahrescharts: Platz 30 bis 11]; Noisey; December 18, 2013</ref>

The Peaches Remix EP containing remixes by [[Passion Pit]], [[Kele Okereke]] of [[Bloc Party]], Dirtcaps, We Love Machines, Death by Misadventure, Omniflux and Owlle was released on June 3, 2014.<ref>[https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/peaches-remixes/id881535232 Peaches - Remixes]; iTunes</ref>

=== ''Elephant'' EP (2017) ===
After three years on the road, the band moved from California to Grand Rapids, Michigan<ref name="auto1">{{Cite news|url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/itvb-elephant-interview-valley-below/|title=Elephant in the Room: An Interview with In The Valley Below - Atwood Magazine|date=2017-07-25|access-date=2017-08-05|language=en-US}}</ref> where they have been living and recording in a 93-year-old house.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://buzzbands.la/2017/07/05/video-valley-bloodhands-oh-fever/|title=Video: In the Valley Below, 'Bloodhands (Oh My Fever)'|last=5th|first=Kevin Bronson on July|last2=2017|date=2017-07-05|website=buzzbands.la|access-date=2017-08-05}}</ref> Their ''Elephant'' EP, was released on July 14, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://inthevalleybelow.com/new-music/|title=NEW MUSIC!|website=Inthevalleybelow.com|date=16 June 2017|accessdate=20 November 2020}}</ref> ''Elephant'' features the lead single "Bloodhands (Oh My Fever)," which is inspired by the [[Ferguson, Missouri|Ferguson, MO]] shooting of Michael Brown.<ref name="auto1"/>  When asked what Angela hopes people listening to ''Elephant'' will take away, she said "I’d like for someone to find a part of themselves in it; maybe we can get down into their emotions and make them feel a certain way – not necessarily have them think about who ''we'' are while listening to it.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/itvb-elephant-interview-valley-below/|title=Elephant in the Room: An Interview with In The Valley Below - Atwood Magazine|website=[[Atwood Magazine]]|date=2017-07-25|access-date=2017-08-05|language=en-US}}</ref>"

===''The Pink Chateau'' (2019)===
The band's sophomore album and movie, ''The Pink Chateau'' was released on April 26, 2019 via [[Bright Antenna]] Records.  Like their debut, all songs were written, produced and recorded by the duo except the album's single "Rise" which was produced and mixed by [[TV on the Radio]] band member and producer [[Dave Sitek]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.killbeatmusic.com/media-releases/2019/3/13/in-the-valley-below-announces-new-lp-feature-film|title=IN THE VALLEY BELOW ANNOUNCES NEW LP + FEATURE FILM|website=Killbeat Music|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref>

On March 28, 2019, the band hosted 2 private events in Los Angeles which they called The Pink Chateau, an audio [[Immersive theater|immersive]], multi-sensory music experience.  Guests were guided and wandered through a mansion wearing wireless headphones exploring different rooms where actors lived out erotic fantasies depicted from the movie.  The new album was broadcast through the headphones along with spoken word directions and erotic tails from Angela.  The end of the experience crescendoed into the band being revealed and performing live over the headphones in a hidden ballroom.<ref>{{Citation|last=Inc|first=Film Dreams|title=In The Valley Below at The Pink Chateau|date=2019-03-30|url=https://vimeo.com/327414984|access-date=2019-04-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://photos.feinphoto.com/Clients/Flux/n-FbdTk7/Mar-28-2019-The-Pink-Chateau|title=Mar. 28, 2019 The Pink Chateau - Fein Image|website=photos.feinphoto.com|access-date=2019-04-30}}</ref>

To promote the movie and album, the band screened the movie around the United States primarily at [[Alamo Drafthouse Cinema|Alamo Draft House Cinemas]]<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/in-the-valley-below-touring-new-film-pink-chateau-watch-nfsw-blue-sky-drugs-video/|title=In the Valley Below touring new film 'Pink Chateau': watch NFSW "Blue Sky Drugs" video|website=BrooklynVegan|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref> performing the score live over wireless headphones.  The movie premiered at the [[Newport Beach Film Festival]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newportbeachfilmfest.com/event/the-pink-chateau/|title=The Pink Chateau|website=Newport Beach Film Festival {{!}} April 25-May 2|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref> on April 26, 2019.

[[BrooklynVegan|Brooklyn Vegan]] calls the movie "A dreamlike diorama of interconnected musical vignettes inspired by vintage French erotica and the faded colors of 1970s films.  Each song on ''The Pink Chateau'' serves as a portal through which the film's enigmatic female protagonist confronts her most deep-seated desires."<ref name="auto"/> While [[PopMatters]] wrote "Perhaps the most intriguing project of the year thus far that melds alternative rock-pop music with risk-taking movie making"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.popmatters.com/in-the-valley-below-interview-2635572432.html|title=Go Inside 'The Pink Chateau': An Interview with In the Valley Below|date=2019-04-26|website=PopMatters|language=en|access-date=2019-04-29}}</ref> and Art Matters called it "a visual album of special magnificence.  An Alice in Wonderland of queer female please and beauty."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/HelloArtMatters/status/1122495108878938114|title=@bookofITVB has released a visual album of special magnificence. An Alice in the Wonderland of queer female pleasure and beauty. Hats off to @BrightAntennapic.twitter.com/SHncXa9Xbq|last=🅰🆁🆃 🅼 🅰🆃🆃🅴🆁🆂|date=2019-04-28|website=@HelloArtMatters|language=en|access-date=2019-04-30}}</ref>

The album cover depicts a simple drawing of a flower, while the back cover reveals the flower as the genitalia of a woman. The band released limited edition "[[Merkin]]" vinyl covers containing different colored furry triangle patches.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://inthevalleybelow.com/store/|title=Store|website=In The Valley Below|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-30}}</ref>
