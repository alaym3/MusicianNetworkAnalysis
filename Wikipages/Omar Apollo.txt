'''Omar Apolonio Velasco''' (born May 20, 1997), known professionally as '''Omar Apollo''', is a Mexican-American singer-songwriter.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_us/article/wjvezq/omar-apollo-friends-ep-interview-shaking-up-randb-with-his-soulful-love-songs |author=Nicole DeMarco |title=Omar Apollo is shaking up R&B with his soulful love songs |website=[[i-D]] |access-date=Apr 10, 2019}}</ref> Apollo sings in both English and Spanish.<ref name="shanks" />

== Early life ==
Born to Mexican parents, Apollo grew up in [[Hobart, Indiana]] with his three older siblings.<ref name="spotify" /> Apollo's parents both worked multiple jobs to support the family.<ref name="RS">{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/omar-apollo-interview-866248/|title=Omar Apollo Wants to Inspire Young Latinx Musicians to 'Go Crazy'|first1=Jon|last1=Blistein|date=August 6, 2019}}</ref> His family is from the Mexican city of Guadalajara, Jalisco.<ref>{{Cite web|last=  |first=  |date=2020-10-16|title=Meet Omar Apollo, the blue-haired, gender-rebellious, Mexican American Prince|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2020-10-16/omar-apollo-mexican-american-prince|access-date=2020-10-21|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US}}</ref> Growing up, he listened to his parents' favorite musicians such as [[Pedro Infante]], [[Los Panchos]] and [[The Beatles]].<ref name="spotify" />  His own influences include diverse musicians such as [[Neil Young]], [[Paul Simon]], [[John Mayer]], [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[Bootsy Collins]], [[Rick James]], and [[The Internet (band)|The Internet]].<ref name="shanks" /> He danced [[ballet folklorico]] when he was a child. He was also a part of his Catholic church choir.<ref name="spotify" />  At his request, Apollo's parents gifted him a guitar when he was 12.<ref name="Remezcla" />  Unfortunately, it was an electric guitar without an amp. Apollo traded the guitar at a pawn shop for an acoustic guitar and started learning how to play by watching and mimicking [[YouTube]] cover videos.<ref name="Remezcla" />  Apollo was also taught by his uncle and practiced playing at church.<ref name="Remezcla" />  He would go on to form a short-lived band with a friend at church.<ref name="shanks" />

== Career ==
Apollo started his career by creating and uploading his songs to the DIY streaming platform [[SoundCloud]].<ref name="spotify" />  During this time, he worked at [[Jimmy John]]'s and [[Guitar Center]] and lived in an attic.<ref>{{cite web|url =https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/get-to-know-omar-apollo-and-his-first-generation-love-songs/ | title = Get to Know Omar Apollo and His First-Generation Love Songs| author=Erika Ramirez | access-date = February 6, 2019 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref>  In 2017, using $30 borrowed from a friend, he uploaded his song "Ugotme" to [[Spotify]], where it was soon playlisted on the platform's ''Fresh Finds'' and racked up 20,000 streams in a day.<ref name=spotify>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/06/11/omar-apollo-friends-interview|title=Omar Apollo’s homegrown funk|website=The FADER}}</ref> A year later, the song would cross more than 15 million streams.<ref name="RS" />  He released his first EP, ''Stereo'' in 2018, which was similarly well received.<ref name="AWAL" /> In 2018 and 2019 he went on two tours, the "Want Tour" and the "Voyager Tour". During the 32-date "Voyager Tour" he released his second EP, ''Friends'' in April 2019.<ref name="shanks" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/omar-apollo-announces-new-friends-ep-shares-new-song-listen/ |title=Omar Apollo Announces New Friends EP, Shares New Song: Listen |author=Evan Minsker|access-date=April 3, 2019|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref>  The EP featured production by rock producer [[John Shanks]], who had previously worked with artists such as [[Michelle Branch]] and [[Melissa Etheridge]].<ref name="shanks">{{Cite web|url=https://lyricallemonade.com/p/a-conversation-with-omar-apollo-the-soul-funk-rock-and-hip-hop-showman-whos-one-of-the-most-refreshingly-real-rising-stars-around|title=Lyrical Lemonade|website=lyricallemonade.com}}</ref> Apollo is managed by Shanks' son, Dylan, who he met over [[Twitter]] in July 2017 when the latter booked Apollo for a university show at [[NYU]].<ref name="shanks" /><ref name="Remezcla" /> Apollo signed with [[AWAL|Artists Without A Label]] earlier that year and performed at [[SXSW]] as part of a showcase with the group.<ref name="AWAL" />  He went on his first European tour in 2019 as well, and played at [[Lollapalooza]] in August.<ref name="shanks" /> In late 2019, Apollo released the singles, "Frío" and "Hit Me Up", both collaborations with producer [[Kenny Beats]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Omar Apollo – Frío|url=https://genius.com/Omar-apollo-frio-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2020-07-17}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Omar Apollo, Dominic Fike & Kenny Beats – Hit Me Up|url=https://genius.com/Omar-apollo-dominic-fike-and-kenny-beats-hit-me-up-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2020-07-17}}</ref>

In April 2020, Apollo released the single "Imagine U", another collaboration with [[Kenny Beats]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Omar Apollo – Imagine U|url=https://genius.com/Omar-apollo-imagine-u-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2020-07-17}}</ref> On August 7, Apollo released the lead single "Stayback" from his upcoming debut studio album.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-07|title=Omar Apollo drops first single, 'Stayback', from his forthcoming debut album {{!}} NME|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/omar-apollo-announces-debut-album-first-single-stayback-released-this-week-2720790|access-date=2020-10-26|website=NME Music News, Reviews, Videos, Galleries, Tickets and Blogs {{!}} NME.COM|language=en-GB}}</ref> On September 10, he released the second single "Kamikaze".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ruiz|first=Matthew Ismael|title=Omar Apollo Shares New Song "Kamikaze"|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/omar-apollo-shares-new-song-kamikaze-listen/|access-date=2020-10-26|website=Pitchfork|language=en-us}}</ref> On September 25, Apollo appeared on [[alternative R&B]] Japanese singer [[Joji (musician)|Joji]]’s second album ''[[Nectar (Joji album)|Nectar]]'' on the track “High Hopes".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Joji Announces 'Nectar' Album Tracklist Alongside New Merch Collection|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/9/joji-nectar-new-album-tracklist-merch-collection|access-date=2020-10-26|website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref> Apollo released "Dos Uno Nueve (219)" and “Want U Around" (featuring [[Ruel (singer)|Ruel]]) before releasing his debut studio album ''Apolonio'' on October 16.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-07|title=Omar Apollo announces new project, 'Apolonio'|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/omar-apollo-shares-details-of-upcoming-debut-album-apolonio-2774470|access-date=2020-10-26|website=[[NME]]|language=en-GB}}</ref>

In 2021, Apollo released the single "Go Away", including a live performance on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon|The Tonight Show]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Omar Apollo, 'Go Away' |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/now-playing/2021/07/13/1014283975/omar-apollo-go-away |website=NPR |access-date=10 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Watch Omar Apollo’s Dramatic Performance of ‘Go Away’ on ‘Fallon’ |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/omar-apollo-go-away-fallon-1200003/ |website=Rolling Stone |access-date=10 November 2021}}</ref> He later followed up with the single "Bad Life", featuring [[Kali Uchis]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Omar Apollo (feat. Kali Uchis), 'Bad Life' |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/now-playing/2021/11/08/1053024800/omar-apollo-ft-kali-uchis-bad-life |website=NPR |access-date=10 November 2021}}</ref> Apollo was also nominated for two [[22nd_Annual_Latin_Grammy_Awards|Latin Grammy]] awards - Record of the Year and Best Alternative Song, both for "Te olvidaste", his collaboration with Spanish artist [[C. Tangana]].
