'''Nadine Alexis Paguia Lustre''' ({{IPA-tl|naˈdin ˈlustrɛ}} {{IPAc-en|lang|l|uː|s|t|r|ə}} {{Respell|LOOS-trə}}; born October 31, 1993) is a Filipina actress and singer. She rose to fame after playing the lead role of Eya Rodriguez in the film ''[[Diary ng Panget (film)|Diary ng Panget]]'' (2014).<ref>{{cite news|last=Gil|first=Baby|date=March 31, 2014|title= SOUNDS FAMILIAR: From Pop Girl to Diary ng Panget|url= http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2014/03/31/1306910/pop-girl-panget|work=The Philippine Star|location= Philippines|publisher= Philstar Daily Inc.|access-date= May 7, 2014}}</ref> Lustre has since starred in the television series ''[[On the Wings of Love (TV series)|On the Wings of Love]]'' (2015) and the films ''[[Never Not Love You]]'' (2018), ''[[Ulan (film)|Ulan]]'' (2019), and ''[[Indak (film)|Indak]]'' (2019).

In 2018, her performance as Joanne Candelaria in the romantic drama film ''[[Never Not Love You]]'' received critical acclaim and garnered her several awards such as the [[Young Critics Circle]] Best Performer award, the [[FAMAS Award for Best Actress]], and the [[Gawad Urian for Best Actress]].

==Early life and education==
Lustre was born on October 31, 1993 in [[Quezon City]], [[Philippines]].{{citation needed|date=July 2017}} She is the eldest of four children to Myraquel Paguia-Lustre and Ulysses Lustre, a mechanical engineer.<ref>{{cite news|work=ABS-CBN News|title=Nadine Lustre's mom hospitalized|url=http://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/01/19/16/nadine-lustres-mom-hospitalized|access-date=July 15, 2017|agency=[[ABS-CBN News]]|date=January 20, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Yanoria|first1=Luigene|title=Nadine Lustre's dad enters spotlight: Is he the next stage father?|url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/nadine-lustre-s-dad-enters-spotlight--is-he-the-next-stage-father-050122609.html|access-date=July 15, 2017|agency=[[Yahoo!|Yahoo]] Southeast Asia Newsroom|date=October 26, 2014}}</ref>

She attended Diliman Preparatory School and took Communication Arts Major in Films at [[Colegio de San Lorenzo]] in Congressional Avenue, Quezon City.<ref name="popgirl">{{cite news|url=http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/685912/bringing-back-glory-days-bagets|title=Bringing back the glory days of Bagets|last=Glorioso|first=Bot|date=May 15, 2011|work=The Philippine Star|access-date=May 7, 2014|publisher=Philstar Daily Inc.|location=Philippines}}</ref><ref>{{cite news

==Career==
Lustre, at the age of 8, started as a TV host in [[Radio Philippines Network|RPN]]'s (then a government-sequestered network, now privatized by the late [[Antonio Cabangon Chua]] as [[CNN Philippines]]) children oriented show entitled ''Storyland''. She was also featured a child host to [[Disney Channel (Southeast Asia)|Disney Channel Asia]]. After signing with [[Viva Entertainment]], she became one of the members of the cast in TV5's youth-oriented program ''Bagets'' and appeared in several [[GMA Network]] shows, including performances in ''[[SOP (variety show)|SOP Rules]].'' Lustre also appeared in the film ''[[Petrang Kabayo]]'' with [[Vice Ganda]] and starred in ''[[P. S. I Love You (TV series)|P. S. I Love You]],''<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.pep.ph/news/32001/nadine-lustre-and-aj-muhlach-loveteam-topbills-tv5-|title=Nadine Lustre and AJ Muhlach loveteam topbills TV5 teleserye|first=Glen|last=Sibonga|date=May 2, 2013|work=Philippine Entertainment Portal|access-date=November 20, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029195608/http://www.pep.ph/news/32001/nadine-lustre-and-aj-muhlach-loveteam-topbills-tv5-|archive-date=October 29, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> drama series in [[TV5 (Philippines)|TV5]].

===Music===
====2009–2013: Pop Girls====
{{main|Pop Girls}}
Lustre was one of the original members of girl group, ''[[Pop Girls]]'' which was launched by [[VIVA Entertainment Group|Viva Entertainment]] in 2009. Also in the group with Lustre are twins Lailah and Mariam Bustria, Diana Pama Aller, Rose Ginkel, and [[Shy Carlos]]. She eventually left the group to start a solo music career.

==== 2014–2016: Solo career with Viva and collaborations with James Reid ====
In 2014, Lustre released her [[Nadine Lustre (EP)|self-titled EP]] under [[Viva Records (Philippines)|Viva Records]]. It had hit singles such as ''Para-paraan, Paligoy-ligoy, No Erase, Bahala Na, and Hanap-hanap''; the last three songs mentioned she collaborated with [[James Reid (actor)|James Reid]], who later on became Lustre's onscreen and real life partner couple.

In 2015, her song "Me and You", from her self-titled album, was given the Titanium Award by Australian digital radio station ''[[SBS PopAsia]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sbs.com.au/popasia/blog/2014/04/30/hashtag-hits-sbs-popasias-live-chart-show|title=#HASHTAG HITS: SBS PopAsia's live chart show|publisher=SBS.com.au|access-date=July 20, 2015}}</ref> Lustre is among other artists like [[K-pop]] groups [[Super Junior]], [[U-KISS]] and [[Exo (band)|EXO]] who had songs that achieved Titanium status.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://starcinema.abs-cbn.com/latest-news/june/june3/nadine-lustres-song-reaches-titanium-status-in-australian-music-show|title=Nadine Lustre's song reaches Titanium status in Australian music show|date=June 3, 2015|publisher=ABS-CBN.com|access-date=July 20, 2015}}</ref>

Lustre and [[Callalily]] vocalist [[Kean Cipriano]] interpreted the song written by Mark Villar (not the [[Las Piñas]] [[Legislative district of Las Piñas|Congressman]]) entitled "Sa Ibang Mundo" for the 2015 [[Philippine Popular Music Festival|PhilPop]] songwriting competition. They earned the People's Choice award and the song was the second runner-up during the 2015 PhilPop's finals night held on July 25, 2015 at the [[Meralco Theater]].<ref name="pep">{{cite news|url=http://www.pep.ph/guide/music/18987/philpop-2015-winners-revealed-nadine-lustre-and-kean-cipriano-earned-peoples-choice-award|title=PhilPop 2015 winners revealed; Nadine Lustre and Kean Cipriano earned People's Choice Award|date=July 25, 2015|work=Philippine Entertainment Portal|access-date=July 26, 2015}}</ref>

Also in 2015, Lustre collaborated with [[Yassi Pressman]]'s song, ''Hush'', where Lustre showed off her rap skills. Lustre and Reid also released their own version of the song "On The Wings of Love" for their [[On the Wings of Love (TV series)|''teleserye'' with the same name]].

In the [[Myx Music Awards 2016|2016 Philippines Myx Music Awards]], Lustre won Favorite Song and Favorite Music Video for her song "Me and You". She also won Favorite Artist and Favorite Female Artist. Lustre also bagged the Favorite Collaboration award for her song "Hanap-Hanap" with Reid.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2016/03/16/1563508/nadine-lustre-wins-big-myx-music-awards-2016|title=Nadine Lustre wins big at MYX Music Awards 2016|work=The Philippine Star|access-date=May 13, 2016}}</ref>

Lustre and Reid in 2016 also released their own version of the song "This Time" under Viva which was used for their [[This Time (film)|movie with the same name]].

==== 2017–present: Solo career with Careless ====
In 2017, Lustre started collaborating with James Reid's newly founded record label, [[Careless (record label)|Careless Music Manila]].  She was featured in the song IL2LU, which was included in Reid's LP, ''[[Palm Dreams]]''. In the same year, Lustre also collaborated with Bret Jackson in a song called "Nowhere I Wouldn't Go", which was included in the latter's album, Island City Poems.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://insideshowbiz.onemega.com/bret-jackson-island-city-poems-already-chart-topper-day-release/|title=RET JACKSON'S ISLAND CITY POEMS ALREADY A CHART-TOPPER IN ITS DAY OF RELEASE!|date=December 1, 2017}}</ref>

In January 2018, Lustre released her first single under [[Careless (record label)|Careless Music Manila]]. The song is called "St4y Up". Lustre worked with Reid and music producer CRWN for the song's production. In the same month, she released a music video directed by [[Petersen Vargas]] for the single.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.scoutmag.ph/music/nadine-lustre-just-dropped-new-single-total-bop-scout-20180119|title=Nadine Lustre just dropped her new single and it's a total bop|date=January 19, 2018}}</ref>

In March 2018, Lustre and Reid released a cover of Sugarfree's "Prom" under [[Viva Records (Philippines)|Viva Records]], as part of the official soundtrack of their romance movie ''[[Never Not Love You]].''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Constantino|first=Brianne|date=May 20, 2019|title=HERE'S WHY EBE DANCEL CONGRATULATED JADINE ON MYX MUSIC AWARDS 2019 WIN|url=https://myx.abs-cbn.com/features/18786/ebe-dancel-congratulates-jadine-on-mmas-2019-win|website=Myx}}</ref>

In October 2018, Lustre was featured in three tracks from the ''Careless Mixtape'', which consists of songs from various artists of the record label. The tracks were ''I Like It'', a song she penned herself; ''Summer'', which was a duet with Reid; and ''Nobody'', which also featured Sofia Romualdez.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://insideshowbiz.onemega.com/nadine-lustre-careless-mixtape/|title=NADINE LUSTRE'S "I LIKE IT" TOPS ITUNES CHART HOURS AFTER RELEASE|date=October 12, 2018}}</ref>

In August 2019, Lustre once again recorded songs under [[Viva Records (Philippines)|Viva Records]] for the soundtrack of her dance musical film, [[Indak (film)|Indak]]. She was featured in the track ''Sumayaw sa Indak'' and in the cover of Thyro and Yumi's hit song ''Triangulo''. She also had a duet called ''Swerte Sa Palad Mo'' with her co-star [[Sam Concepcion]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Indak (Official Movie Soundtrack)|url=https://music.apple.com/ph/album/indak-official-movie-soundtrack/1469150699|website=Apple Music}}</ref>

In November 2019, Careless released another collaborative mixtape called ''The Island City Playlist''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=September 6, 2019|title=An Inside Peek: Welcome to Island City|url=https://wonder.ph/popculture/careless-music-manila-island-city/|website=Wonder Mag}}</ref> Lustre was featured in the track ''Headspace'' along with Curtismith, Massiah, and Billy Davis. She also collaborated in a track called ''No 32'' with rapper Ruby Ibarra. In December 2019, Lustre officially signed a recording contract with Careless Music.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lago|first=Amanda|date=December 13, 2019|title=James Reid's Careless is anything but|url=https://www.rappler.com/entertainment/music/james-reid-music-label-careless|website=Rappler}}</ref>

In October 2020, Lustre announced her first full-length album under [[Careless (record label)|Careless Music]], entitled ''[[Wildest Dreams (Nadine Lustre album)|Wildest Dreams]]''.<ref name="Rappler">{{Cite news|date=October 14, 2020|title=Nadine Lustre is releasing a new album on October 31|work=Rappler|url=https://www.rappler.com/entertainment/music/nadine-lustre-new-album-wildest-dreams-october-31}}</ref> The visual album, the first of its kind in the Philippines, garnered 1 million views in 2 days.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lariosa|first=Saab|date=November 3, 2020|title=Nadine Lustre's 'Wildest Dreams' reached 1M views in 2 days. ==== 2015–2016: ''Love team'' with James Reid ====
With the success of ''Diary ng Panget'' and her tandem with Reid, the pair moved on to star in the film adaptation of another Wattpad novel, ''[[Talk Back and You're Dead]]'', which was written by Alesana Marie.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.pep.ph/guide/movies/13899/james-reid-and-nadine-lustre-paired-anew-for-movie-version-of-talk-back-and-youre-dead |title=James Reid and Nadine Lustre paired anew for the big screen |work=Philippine Entertainment Portal|access-date=April 16, 2015}}</ref> The film also featured [[Joseph Marco]] and Pressman. It was released on August 20, 2014 in theaters nationwide. Lustre played the role of Miracle Samantha Perez, a rich girl who fell in love with a teenage gang leader who was also her childhood sweetheart.

In 2015, Lustre starred in her third film opposite of Reid<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/05/12/15/look-fans-flock-para-sa-hopeless-romantic-premiere|title=LOOK: Fans Flock to "Para Sa Hopeless Romantic" Premiere|work=ABS-CBN News}}</ref>'', [[Para sa Hopeless Romantic]]'', which is a film adaptation of the novel with the same name written by Marcelo Santos III. Lustre played the role of Rebecca del Mundo, a college student who writes love stories with bitter endings as a way of expressing her heartbreak. The movie received negative reviews. However, Oggs Cruz of ''Rappler'' wrote of Lustre's performance, "Lustre makes her character's seemingly unimportant struggle moving."

She also had a special role in Adolf Alix Jr.'s horror film ''[[Chain Mail (film)|Chain Mail]]'' as Anne Nuñez, which premiered on August 22, 2015. In December 2015, Lustre alongside Reid starred in an action comedy drama movie with [[Vice Ganda]] and [[Coco Martin]] in ''[[Beauty and the Bestie]]'' for the 2015 Metro Manila Film Festival directed by [[Wenn V. Deramas]]. As of January 25, 2016, the movie earned 526 million pesos in local and domestic screenings making it the [[List of highest-grossing films in the Philippines|highest-grossing Filipino film]] at that time.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://starcinema.abs-cbn.com/movies/beauty-and-the-bestie/latest-news/beauty-and-the-bestie-is-now-the-highest-grossing-film-of-all-time|title='Beauty and the Bestie' is now the highest-grossing film of all time!|website=Star Cinema|access-date=May 14, 2016}}</ref> The movie received lukewarm reviews from critics. Philbert Dy of [[ClickTheCity.com|Click The City]], however, praised the cast for being "so game" and "unafraid to look goofy". He remarks that Lustre is "pretty funny."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dy|first=Philbert|date=December 26, 2015|title='Beauty and the Bestie' Thrives on Absurdity|url=https://www.clickthecity.com/movies/article/18453/movie-review-beauty-and-the-bestie-thrives-on-absurdity/|website=Click The City}}</ref>

Lustre and Reid starred on their fourth movie called [[This Time (film)|''This Time'']] in May 2016 which earned 15 million pesos on its opening day.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://philnews.ph/2016/05/05/jadine-time-movie-15-million-opening-day/|title="This Time" Starring JaDine Earns 15 Million on Opening Day|last=Remus|date=May 5, 2016|website=Philippine News|access-date=May 14, 2016}}</ref> The movie was directed by Nuel Naval and was loosely based on the Wattpad novel ''That One Summer'' by Aly Almario.

==== 2018–present: Critical acclaim and subsequent projects ====

In 2018, Lustre and Reid made a comeback to the big screen with ''Never Not Love You'', which was directed by [[Antoinette Jadaone]]. Lustre's performance in the film earned her major awards from three of the most prestigious award-giving bodies in the Philippines, namely, the [[Young Critics Circle|Young Critics' Circle]], [[Gawad Urian Award|Gawad Urian]], and [[FAMAS Award|FAMAS]]. Wanngo Gallaga of ''Click The City'' called Lustre a "powerhouse" and the "real star" of the film while praising her performance by saying that "There is a whole world of emotions within her that she carries with every movement".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Gallaga|first=Wanggo|date=April 1, 2018|title='Never Not Love You' is unafraid to get quiet, intense, and very real|url=https://www.clickthecity.com/movies/article/9867/movie-review-never-not-love-you/}}</ref> ''[[Philippine Daily Inquirer|The Philippine Daily Inquirer's]]'' Cake Evangelista praised Lustre for being someone who "evokes a wealth of meaning and emotion"<ref>{{Cite web|last=Evangelista|first=Cake|date=April 8, 2018|title=Super Review: 'Never Not Love You' is JaDine's best work so far|url=https://lifestyle.inquirer.net/291080/super-review-never-not-love-jadines-best-work-far/}}</ref>

In 2019, she starred in Irene Villamor's [[Ulan (film)|Ulan]], her first major film project without Reid. She played the role of romance novel editor turned children's book writer Maya Landicho in the film, which uses elements of [[magic realism]]. Praising Lustre's performance in the film, Tito Valiente of ''[[BusinessMirror|Business Mirror]]'' wrote, "It has been such a long time when the camera of a local film has ever been in love with an actress. Nadine Lustre, with the face that can act, deserves that love."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Valiente|first=Tito|date=March 21, 2019|title='Ulan': A lovely, singular film about loving and not loving|url=https://businessmirror.com.ph/2019/03/21/ulan-a-lovely-singular-film-about-loving-and-not-loving/}}</ref> The film, which was co-produced by [[HOOQ]], got recognized by the Asian Academy Creative Awards. It won several awards in the regional category of the awards procedure, including one for Best Actress. Lustre competed with other regional winners for the Grand Prize, but lost to India's Shefali Shah.<ref>{{Cite web|date=December 7, 2019|title=2019 Final Winners List|url=https://www.asianacademycreativeawards.com/2019-final-winners-list/|website=Asian Academy Creative Awards}}</ref>

Lustre also starred in the dance musical film Paul Basinilio's [[Indak (film)|''Indak'']] opposite [[Sam Concepcion]]. She played the role of Jen, an island girl who joined a dance group after a video of her dancing went viral on social media. Lustre and Concepcion, along with other dancers in the film, went through weeks of dance training before filming began.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nadine Lustre and Sam Concepcion spill the toughest part about INDAK|url=http://www.vivaentertainment.ph/nadine-lustre-and-sam-concepcion-spill-the-toughest-part-about-indak/|website=Viva Entertainment}}</ref>

====Television====
On August 26, 2014, Lustre and Reid signed a two-year exclusive contract with [[ABS-CBN Corporation|ABS-CBN]] at an event attended by some of the prominent figures of the station.<ref>{{cite web|author=Star Cinema |url=http://starcinema.abs-cbn.com/latest-news/snapshots-jadine-signs-contract-with-abs-cbn |title=JaDine signs contract with ABS-CBN |publisher=Star Cinema |date=August 26, 2014 |access-date=July 19, 2015}}</ref> The contract signed Lustre and Reid as ABS-CBN's new loveteam. Any music, films or television shows headlined by them will be released under ABS-CBN and Viva.

Their television project with ABS-CBN was ''[[Wansapanataym]]''{{'}}s month-long special entitled ''My App Boyfie'' in October 2014,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/09/27/14/my-app-boyfie-pilot-trends-worldwide |title='My App Boyfie' pilot trends worldwide |work=ABS-CBN News|date=September 27, 2014 |access-date=July 21, 2015}}</ref> where Lustre portrayed the lead female role Anika Andres.

Lustre's breakthrough and most prominent role to date is Leah Olivar on the romantic comedy series ''[[On the Wings of Love (TV Series)|On The Wings Of Love]]'', which she starred alongside Reid as her [[leading man]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Buccat|first1=Rhys|title=Leah asks Clark to marry her in 'On the Wings of Love'|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/08/17/15/lea-asks-clark-marry-her-wings-love|access-date=August 22, 2015|work=ABS-CBN News|date=August 18, 2015}}</ref> The [[Philippine television drama|''teleserye'']] was originally supposed to run for 10 weeks but was extended for another three months. ===Concerts===
On February 20, 2016, Lustre and Reid had their first major concert titled "JaDine in Love" at the [[Smart Araneta Coliseum]]. Within seven hours after the concert announcement, there was already a total of ₱9 million in ticket revenues. It was announced during the presscon that tickets for general admission is sold out, and [[Standing-room only|SRO (Standing Room Only)]] tickets were made available for purchase by producers due to high demand.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.philstar.com/entertainment/2016/02/18/1554483/jadine-fever-will-never-end|title=JaDine fever will never end|work=The Philippine Star}}</ref>

=== Other ventures ===
==== ''Lustrous'' ====
In April 2018, Lustre launched her first ever make-up collection called ''Lustrous'' in collaboration with the international Australian brand, BYS. Lustre is the first ever Filipina celebrity to collaborate with the international brand. In a private launch held on March 23, she mentioned that she shares the same advocacy with BYS which stands for "be your self" and said that ''"It's nice to be able to work with a brand who inspires all the girls to be themselves."''  The event was hosted in Flossom Kitchen and Cafe by one of her closest friends Kiana Valenciano, daughter of singer-songwriter, musician and actor [[Gary Valenciano]].

The make-up line consists of a contour & highlight kit, lip & cheek tints in the shade Raw and [[Vermilion|Vermillion]], eye shadow palettes in the shades Luna and Calypso, eyeliners in the shades [[Turquoise]], [[Moonstone (gemstone)|Moonstone]] and [[Sapphire]], mascaras in the shades Void and Lazuli and an eyebrow pencil  & eyebrow gel in the shade Natural Brown. Each item is contained in a [[gold]] packaging and comes with different [[glitter]]y, [[red]] and brownish shades. The make-up line also offers a gift box that contains one shade of each item and is placed in a pink box with a tropical design. Lustrous is available in selected [[SM Department Store|SM Beauty]] and [[Watsons]] stores nationwide. Customers can also pre-order through their [[Instagram]] page lustrousph.

====''Luster''====

In December 2018, Lustre released her signature fragrance called ''Luster''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.preview.ph/beauty/luster-by-nadine-lustre-perfume-a00193-20190109|title=Nadine Lustre's Perfume Is Now Available Online|last=Arcano|first=Nicole|date=January 9, 2019|website=Preview}}</ref> The fragrance was first teased by Lustre in 2017 when she posted several boudoir-themed photos from the campaign. She revealed the project on Valentine's night.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Moran|first=Romeo|title=Nadine Lustre's New Luster Perfume Is A Sign Of Things to Come|url=https://www.scoutmag.ph/news/nadine-lustres-new-luster-perfume-sign-things-come-pepi-20170215}}</ref> In an interview with ''Preview'', Lustre revealed details about how she personally and independently produced and conceptualized the product.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|last=Rodriguez|first=Maura|date=February 22, 2017|title=Preview Exclusive: Nadine Lustre Reveals Everything You Need to Know About Her New Fragrance Line|url=https://www.preview.ph/beauty/exclusive-everything-you-need-to-know-about-luster-by-nadine-lustre-a73-20170222-lfrm}}</ref> The idea came from her mother who knew a chemist who makes perfumes.<ref name="auto1"/> She then started conceptualizing the campaign, which was inspired by "boudoir photos, 18th-century fashion photos, and vintage ''Vogue"''<ref name="auto1"/> She revealed that she chose such themes because she wanted to show everyone that she is "slowly changing and growing into a woman."<ref name="auto1"/> The perfume bottle was made to look like a "love potion or an 18th century perfume bottle"<ref name="auto1"/> For the campaign, Lustre worked with photographer BJ Pascual. The packaging was designed by artist Soleil Ignacio.<ref name="auto1"/> When asked about what it means to her to have her own brand of perfume, Lustre said, ""What does it mean for me? For me, this perfume, ''Luster'', showcases my creativity as a person. It showcases my character because it's the scent that I like. I'm selling you myself. I'm selling you a piece of me. This is as personal as it gets. This is me showing everyone ''na ito ako''. This is what I can do. This is the real me. When you buy the perfume, it's like literally taking me with you wherever you are."<ref name="auto1"/>

After some delay, Luster was officially launched on December 22, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|date=December 24, 2018|title=Nadine Lustre releases new fragrance line|work=ABS-CBN PUSH|url=https://push.abs-cbn.com/2018/12/24/fresh-scoops/nadine-lustre-releases-new-fragrance-line-198520}}</ref> The fragrance was described as having a "signature powdery fragrance with a luscious vanilla scent".<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|last=Yoingco|first=Faye|date=February 26, 2019|title=Nadine Lustre Finally Releases Her Brand New Scent|url=https://mega.onemega.com/nadine-lustre-perfume-lustrous/}}</ref> Lustre released brand new promotional photos, which were "Thumbelina-inspired" and featured life-sized peonies. For this campaign, Lustre collaborated with photographer Xander Angeles and her make up artist, Jelly Eugenio.<ref name="auto"/>

In February 2019, Lustre held an official launch event for the fragrance line in the form of a pop up store. The event was held at Vetro, an art gallery in Quezon City.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Meneses|first=Dani|date=February 25, 2019|title=Luster by Nadine Lustre Pop-up Store|url=https://www.themegamorphosisinc.com/single-post/2019/11/14/Luster-Pop-up-Store}}</ref>

==Musical influences==
Lustre is a fan of American popstar [[Lady Gaga]], whom she once called her "ultimate idol".<ref>{{Cite web|date=December 11, 2018|title=Nadine Lustre on who she would court: "That's a funny question…"|url=https://www.vivaentertainment.ph/nadine-lustre-on-who-she-would-court-thats-a-funny-question/|website=Viva Entertainment}}</ref> Discussing the popstar, Lustre has said, "She's very creative, she's out of this world. It's not the usual.
