'''Mamamoo''' ({{Korean|마마무}}, stylized in [[all caps]])<ref>{{Cite web|title = MAMAMOO {{!}} RBW|url = http://rbbridge.com/pages/artist1/|website = rbbridge.com|access-date = January 26, 2016|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170402040727/http://rbbridge.com/pages/artist1/|archive-date = April 2, 2017|url-status = dead}}</ref> is a South Korean [[girl group]] formed by [[RBW (company)|RBW]] (formerly WA Entertainment) in 2014, composed of four members: [[Solar (singer)|Solar]], [[Moonbyul]], [[Wheein]], and [[Hwasa]]. The group officially debuted with their single "Mr. Ambiguous" on June 18, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last=Choi|first=Jin-sil|date=June 3, 2014|title=|script-title=ko:마마무, 18일 정식 데뷔 확정 '4인 4색 개성 공개'|url=http://tenasia.hankyung.com/archives/264318|url-status=live|access-date=September 13, 2021|website=Ten Asia|language=ko}}</ref> Their debut was considered by some critics as one of the best [[K-pop]] debuts of 2014.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Lee|first=Ji-hyun|date=December 17, 2014|title=|script-title=ko:가요전문가 28인 선정, 올 최고 신인은(연말설문)|trans-title=WINNER (1st), MAMAMOO (2nd), AKMU & EXID (3rd) are 2014's Best Rookies According to Expert Panel|url=http://news.naver.com/main/read.nhn?mode=LSD&mid=sec&sid1=106&oid=108&aid=0002368714|url-status=live|access-date=December 18, 2014|website=Star News|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|last=Benjamin|first=Jeff|date=January 28, 2015|title=Top 5 K-Pop Artists to Watch in 2015|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/6451004/k-pop-breakout-artists-to-watch-2015|url-status=live|access-date=January 31, 2015|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> They are recognized for their retro, jazz, R&B concepts and their strong vocal performances.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://seoulbeats.com/2016/02/a-modern-spin-on-retro-mamamoos-winning-niche/|title=A Modern Spin on Retro: Mamamoo's Winning Niche|website=seoulbeats|language=en-US|access-date=March 17, 2016|date=2016-02-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Ranking K-Pop's best 15 retro concepts|url=https://www.allkpop.com/article/2016/10/ranking-k-pops-best-15-retro-concepts|access-date=2021-11-17|website=allkpop|language=en}}</ref>

== Name ==
The group name "Mamamoo" is symbolic of the first time a baby babbles. In addition, it has the meaning of approaching instinctively and primitively like a baby.<ref>{{Cite web|date=February 11, 2016|title=마마무, 이름은 무슨의미?…"아기가 처음에 옹알이하는 소리"|url=https://www.donga.com/news/article/all/20160211/76399646/1#|url-status=live|access-date=September 13, 2021|website=Donga|language=ko}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2014–2015: Formation, debut with ''Hello'' and rising popularity ===
Prior to their official debut, Mamamoo collaborated with several artists. On November 21, 2014, Mamamoo released their second EP ''[[Piano Man (EP)|Piano Man]]'' with the title track of the same name.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mamamoo Delays Release Of 'Piano Man' By One Day|url=http://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/134343/20141111/mamamoo-delays-release-of-piano-man-by-one-day.htm|website=KpopStarz|access-date=November 19, 2014|date=2014-11-11}}</ref> The title song peaked at 41 on [[Gaon Digital Chart|Gaon's Digital chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/online.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=ALL&targetTime=49&hitYear=2014&termGbn=week|title=2014년 49주차 Digital Chart|language=ko|publisher=Gaon|access-date=October 12, 2015}}</ref> By the end of 2014, Mamamoo ranked tenth highest among idol girl groups for digital sales, 19th in album sales, and 11th in overall sales according to Gaon's year-end rankings.<ref>{{cite news|author1=Kim Jinwoo|script-title=ko:2014결산 걸그룹 순위|trans-title=2014 Year-end girl group rankings|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/theme/theme_view.php?f_seq=239&theme=opinion&gubun=opinion|access-date=December 29, 2014|work=Gaon Chart|date=December 24, 2014|language=ko|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141231133304/http://gaonchart.co.kr/theme/theme_view.php?f_seq=239&theme=opinion&gubun=opinion|archive-date=December 31, 2014}}</ref> On January 10, 2015, Mamamoo performed a rendition of [[Joo Hyun-mi]]'s "Wait a Minute" at the singing show ''[[Immortal Songs 2]]'', reaching the final round before losing to [[Kim Kyung-ho]].<ref>{{cite web|title=[TV줌인] '불후' 마마무, 선배들 기죽이는 무서운 신인 'Immortal Songs 2' Mamamoo, a scary rookie that even intimidates seniors|url=http://news.naver.com/main/ranking/read.nhn?mid=etc&sid1=111&rankingType=popular_day&oid=213&aid=0000614321&date=20150111&type=1&rankingSectionId=106&rankingSeq=21|website=Naver|publisher=TV Report|access-date=January 11, 2015}}</ref>

[[File:마마무(MAMAMOO) 이상미의뮤직닷컴 공개방송 화성시 우정읍.jpg|thumb|220px|Mamamoo performing in September 2015.]]
On April 2, 2015, Mamamoo released "Ahh Oop!", the first single of their third EP titled ''[[Pink Funky]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://moonrok.com/news/mamamoo-make-april-2-comeback|title=Mamamoo to Make April 2 Comeback|publisher=Moonrok|access-date=October 12, 2015|date=2015-03-25}}</ref> "Ahh Oop!" marks the group's second collaboration with label mate [[Esna (singer)|Esna]], after she was featured in "Gentleman" on their second EP ''Piano Man''. On June 13, 2015, the group traveled to [[Ulaanbaatar]], Mongolia, to perform at an event sponsored by the South Korean Embassy with [[Crayon Pop]] and [[K-Much]]. The event was a commemorative concert held in honor of the 25th anniversary of diplomatic ties between South Korea and Mongolia.<ref>{{cite web|title=Crayon Pop, K-Much And Mamamoo Bring K-Pop To Mongolia For South Korean Embassy Event|url=http://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/210483/20150613/kpop-crayon-pop-k-much-mamamoo-mongolia.htm|website=KpopStarz|access-date=June 14, 2015|date=2015-06-13}}</ref>

On June 19, 2015, Mamamoo released their third EP ''Pink Funky'' and lead single "Um Oh Ah Yeh". The song was a commercial success, peaking at number three on the [[Gaon Music Chart|Gaon Chart]], becoming their first top-three single.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mamamoo Releases Comeback Teasers For 'Pink Funky'|url=http://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/210231/20150612/mamamoo-comes-back-with-pink-funky.htm|website=KpopStarz|access-date=June 14, 2015|date=2015-06-12}}</ref> On August 23, 2015, after promotions concluded, Mamamoo held their first fan meeting, entitled "1st Moo Party", for a total of 1,200 fans at the [[Olympic Park, Seoul|Olympic Park]] in Seoul. Tickets for the fan meeting sold out within one minute, so the group added an additional meeting for 1,200 more fans the same night.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mamamoo Entertain 2,400 Fans During Group's Double Feature Fan Meeting In Seoul|url=http://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/234102/20150825/mamamoo-fan-meeting.htm|website=KpopStarz|access-date=August 26, 2015|date=2015-08-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|author=James Buhain|title=Mamamoo's 1st fan meeting sell out in just 1 minutes|url=http://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/228393/20150807/mamamoo-fan-meeting.htm|work=kpopstar}}</ref> They also held another "Moo Party" in Los Angeles, which took place on October 4, 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fox|first=Kevin|date=2015-10-01|title=MAMAMOO To Perform And Meet Fans At 1st US 'MOO Party' Event In LA|url=https://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/244984/20151001/mamamoo-fan-meeting-los-angeles-moo-party.htm|access-date=2021-11-17|website=KpopStarz|language=en}}</ref> Mamamoo also collaborated and performed with label mate Basick on the Korean rap survival show ''[[Show Me the Money 4|Show Me the Money]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=MAMAMOO 'stand up' with Basick on 'Show Me The Money 4' semi-finals|url=https://www.allkpop.com/article/2015/08/mamamoo-stand-up-with-basik-on-show-me-the-money-4-semi-finals|access-date=2021-11-17|website=allkpop|language=en}}</ref>

On August 29, 2015, Mamamoo returned to ''[[Immortal Song 2]]'' with a rendition of [[Jo Young-nam]]'s "Delilah".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2015/08/mamamoo-return-to-the-immortal-song-stage-after-5-months|title=Mamamoo return to the 'Immortal Song' stage after 5 months|publisher=Allkpop|access-date=October 12, 2015}}</ref> On October 31, 2015, Mamamoo returned to the ''Immortal Song 2'', singing a rendition of Korean trot singer [[Bae Ho]]'s song "Backwood's Mountain" (두메산골). Their performance earned their first overall win on ''Immortal Song'' with 404 points.<ref>{{Cite web | script-title=ko:'불후의 명곡' 배호 특집 마마무 1부 우승 '시원시원한 가창력과 완벽한 호흡' | trans-title = Mamamoo wins 'Immortal Songs' Bae Ho Special – Strong Vocals and Perfect Breath | work = [[Busan Ilbo]] | date = October 31, 2015 | access-date = June 8, 2016 | url = http://news20.busan.com/controller/newsController.jsp?newsId=20151031000164 | language = ko }}</ref>

=== 2016–2017: Breakthrough with ''Melting'' and subsequent EPs===
[[File:Mamamoo in 2016 Korea Sale Festa Opening Ceremony.jpg|thumb|260px|Mamamoo at the  Korea Sale Festa Opening Ceremony on September 30, 2016.|left]]
On January 10, 2016, RBW announced Mamamoo's first solo concert since their debut in 2014. The concert, titled ''2016 Mamamoo Concert-Moosical'', was held on August 13–14, 2016, at the [[Olympic Hall]] in Seoul.<ref name=1stconcert>{{cite news|title=마마무, 8월 첫 단독 콘서트 '2016 MAMAMOO CONCERT-moosical' 개최|url=http://m.breaknews.com/a.html?uid=447098&section=sc4#cb |website=Break News|date=June 13, 2016|access-date=July 25, 2018|language=ko}}</ref> 7,000 tickets for the concert were sold out in one minute.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ko:마마무, 첫 콘서트 1분 만에 2회 전석 매진 ‘믿듣맘무’|url=https://m.entertain.naver.com/read?oid=382&aid=0000482860 |website=Break News|date=June 23, 2016|access-date=July 25, 2018|language=ko}}</ref>

On January 26, 2016, Mamamoo pre-released an R&B ballad, "I Miss You", from their first full-length album ''[[Melting (album)|Melting]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kim|first=Eunae|date=January 26, 2016|title='I miss you' 마마무, 또 일냈다..음원차트 상위권 싹쓸이|url=http://stoo.asiae.co.kr/article.php?aid=24617576339|url-status=live|access-date=May 20, 2021|website=Sports Today|language=ko}}</ref> On February 12, 2016, another track, "1cm/Taller than You" was pre-released with a music video. The full album was released on February 26, 2016, debuting at number 3 on the [[Gaon Music Chart|Gaon chart]]. The title track "You're the Best (넌 is 뭔들)" also debuted at number three but peaked at number one the following week, becoming their first number-one single.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/album.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=&targetTime=09&hitYear=2016&termGbn=week|title=Gaon Album Weekly chart for 2/21/2016 – 2/27/2016|website=Gaon Music Chart}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/online.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=ALL&targetTime=09&hitYear=2016&termGbn=week|title=Gaon Digital Weekly chart for 2/21/2016 – 2/27/2016|website=Gaon Chart}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/online.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=ALL&targetTime=10&hitYear=2016&termGbn=week|title=Gaon Digital Weekly chart for 2/28/2016 – 3/05/2016|website=Gaon Music Chart}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2016/02/mamamoo-get-down-to-earth-in-making-of-for-upcoming-full-album-melting|title=NMAMAMOO get down to earth in making-of for upcoming full album 'Melting'!|publisher=Allkpop.com|access-date=February 27, 2016}}</ref>

On March 16, 2016, Mamamoo performed in Austin, Texas at [[South By Southwest]]'s K-Pop Night Out.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/7256352/sxsw-2016-k-pop-calendar|title=SXSW 2016: Complete Guide to K-Pop at the Austin Festival|magazine=Billboard|date=March 14, 2016|access-date=August 19, 2016}}</ref>

– allkpop.com}}</ref> The lead single for ''Memory'' was announced to be "Décalcomanie".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2016/10/mamamoo-announce-a-release-date-for-their-upcoming-mini-album|title=Mamamoo announce a release date for their upcoming mini album – allkpop.com}}</ref> Shortly afterwards, Mamamoo participated in several year-end award shows, while also featuring in the OST titled "Love" for hit TV show ''[[Goblin (TV series)|Goblin]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=Dec 7, 2016|title=MAMAMOO To Sing OST For "Goblin"|url=https://www.soompi.com/article/922745wpp/mamamoo-sing-ost-goblin|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-17|website=Soompi|language=en}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: Focus on members' solo activities, ''Travel'', virtual concerts, and WAW Project ===
In January 2020, an OSEN interview with Kim Do-hoon, the CEO and executive producer of Mamamoo's record label [[RBW (company)|RBW]], was published, in which Kim detailed the trajectory of Mamamoo's career and activities in 2020. He reflected on the successes of Mamamoo's participation on ''Queendom'' and their second Korean studio album ''Reality in Black'' and confirmed that the members would begin to focus on solo projects, with each member hoping to showcase their individualities that they had previously shown in the "Four Seasons, Four Colors" project series.<ref>{{Cite web|date=January 21, 2020|title=김도훈이 말한 마마무의 2020년 "개인 역량 집중..원래 꿈 밀어주고픈"[인터뷰➀]|trans-title=Kim Do-hoon told Mamamoo in 2020, "Let's focus on individual capabilities.
