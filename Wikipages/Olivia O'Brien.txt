'''Olivia Gail O'Brien'''<ref name="ISWC"/> (born November 26, 1999) is an American singer-songwriter. She rose to fame in 2016 after collaborating with [[Gnash (musician)|Gnash]] on the single "[[I Hate U, I Love U]]", where she wrote and featured on the single,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kawashima |first1=Dale |title=Olivia O'Brien Co-Writes Her Duet Hit With Gnash, "i hate u, i love u," And Signs With Island Records |url=https://www.songwriteruniverse.com/olivia-obrien-interview-2016.htm |website=SongwriterUniverse |date=October 31, 2016 |publisher=SongwriterUniverse |access-date=6 January 2021}}</ref> it also peaked at number 10 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart in the United States and number one in Australia, resulting in a recording contract with [[Island Records]]. Her debut studio album, ''[[Was It Even Real?]]'', was released in April 2019. Her single "Josslyn" apart of her EP, ''The Results of My Poor Judgement'', was popular on the media-sharing app [[TikTok]] and is another one of her well-known works.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-05-09|title=Olivia O'Brien's 'Josslyn' Scores Midwest Pop Radio Airplay Amid Spotify, TikTok Gains|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2020/05/09/olivia-obriens-josslyn-scores-midwest-pop-radio-airplay-amid-spotify-tiktok-gains/|access-date=2021-01-06|website=Headline Planet|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Early life==
Olivia Gail O'Brien<ref name="ISWC"/> was born on November 26, 1999, in [[Thousand Oaks]], [[California]].<ref name="OTW">{{Cite web|url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/qa-olivia-obrien-opens-up-about-childhood|title=Q&A: Olivia O'Brien Opens Up About Childhood Struggles, Jack & Jack Tour, & Debut EP|work=[[Ones to Watch]]|access-date=April 23, 2020}}</ref> She has been singing since she was seven years old, and taught herself to play guitar and piano.<ref name="OTW" /> She attended [[Justin-Siena High School]] in [[Napa, California]].<ref name="Press Party">{{cite web|title=OLIVIA O'BRIEN RELEASES NEW SINGLE "TRUST ISSUES"|url=http://www.pressparty.com/pg/newsdesk/Universalmusicuk/view/156948/?isworld=y|website=Press Party|access-date=9 September 2016}}</ref> She also skipped a grade (3rd), and attended monastery and catholic schools in her youth.<ref name="youtube.com">{{Citation|title=#13 Olivia O'Brien {{!}} Music, Boys, Therapy, Embarrassment|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EzRceNwYx2Y|language=en|access-date=2021-03-03}}</ref> O'Brien also was bullied as a teenager, some of it stemming due to her socioeconomic class and the fact she was creating music.<ref name="youtube.com"/>

==Career==
===2013–2019: Breakthrough and ''Was It Even Real?''===
[[File:Olivia O'Brien @ El Rey 04 24 2019 (48498638806).jpg|thumb|right|160px|O'Brien performing in [[Los Angeles]] in 2019.]]
O'Brien initially gained the attention of [[Gnash (musician)|Gnash]] after posting a cover of one of his songs on [[SoundCloud]], after beginning to create music at the age of 13.<ref>{{Citation|title=#13 Olivia O'Brien {{!}} Music, Boys, Therapy, Embarrassment|url=https://youtube.com/watch?v=EzRceNwYx2Y?t=244|language=en|access-date=2021-03-03}}</ref> Gnash reached out to O'Brien, expressing interest in her material, and asked for her to send him any original songs that she had. O'Brien then sent a voice note of her original song, "I Hate U, Love U", and he invited her to record the song with him in [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="Press Party" /> "I Hate U, I Love U" (stylized in [[lowercase]]) was released in March 2016, and appears on Gnash's third EP titled ''Us''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/us/id1094931816|title=us by gnash on Apple Music|website=iTunes|date=March 25, 2016|access-date=September 1, 2016}}</ref> The single peaked at number 10 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and reached number one in [[Australia]].<ref name="hot100gnash">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6833103/gnash/chart?f=379|title=Gnash - Chart history - Billboard Hot 100|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=September 1, 2016}}</ref> Thereafter, O'Brien signed with [[Island Records]] and released her debut solo single, "Trust Issues", in August 2016, which premiered through ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' magazine.<ref name="Complex">{{cite web|last1=Ortiz|first1=Edwin|title=Premiere: Listen to Olivia O'Brien's New Song "Trust Issues"|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2016/08/olivia-obrien-trust-issues-premiere|website=Complex|access-date=September 9, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Island Records - Olivia O'Brien|url=http://www.islandrecords.com/artists/olivia-obrien/|website=Island Records|access-date=September 13, 2016}}</ref> Her second single, "Root Beer Float" featuring [[Blackbear (musician)|Blackbear]], premiered on September 15, 2016.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Kress|first1=Bryan|title=Olivia O'Brien Releases Sugary 'Root Beer Float,' Feat. blackbear: Exclusive Premiere|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7511035/olivia-obrien-root-beer-float-blackbear-premiere|magazine=Billboard|date=September 15, 2016|access-date=September 16, 2016}}</ref> O'Brien released a third single, "Find What You're Looking For", in October 2016.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Ramsay|first1=Kate|title=Premiere: Olivia O'Brien's New Single "Find What You're Looking For"|work=V Magazine|url=http://vmagazine.com/article/listen-olivia-obriens-new-single-find-youre-looking/|access-date=21 October 2016}}</ref> O'Brien released "Hate U, Love U" as a single in December, the original solo version of "I Hate U, I Love U."<ref name="hate u love u itunes">{{cite web|title=Hate U Love U - Single by Olivia O'Brien on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/hate-u-love-u-single/id1181789606|website=iTunes|date=December 9, 2016|access-date=12 December 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Pawlowski|first1=Sophie|title=We Can't Help But Love "Hate U Love You" - Nasty Galaxy|url=http://blog.nastygal.com/music/2016/12/cant-help-love-hate-u-love/|website=Nasty Gal|access-date=16 December 2016|date=15 December 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170510080309/http://blog.nastygal.com/music/2016/12/cant-help-love-hate-u-love/|archive-date=May 10, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> O'Brien also released an acoustic video for the song on December 20, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Briones|first1=Isis|title=You've Never Heard "Hate U Love U" Performed Like This Before|url=http://www.teenvogue.com/story/olivia-obrien-i-hate-u-i-love-u-acoustic-video|website=Teen Vogue|date=December 20, 2016|access-date=20 December 2016}}</ref> O'Brien made her television debut in July 2016 with Gnash performing "I Hate U, I Love U" on ''[[Late Night with Seth Meyers]]''.<ref name="Vogue">{{cite web|last1=Horowitz|first1=Steven J.|title=Singer-Songwriter gnash on Becoming the Year's Most Unlikely Music Sensation|url=http://www.vogue.com/13460204/gnash-i-hate-u-i-love-u-breakout-song-music-sensation/|website=Vogue|date=July 22, 2016|access-date=September 9, 2016}}</ref> O'Brien and Gnash made their second television performance on September 19, 2016, performing "i hate u, i love u" on ''[[Today (American TV program)|Today]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=gnash and Olivia O'Brien perform their hit 'I hate u, I love u' on TODAY|url=http://www.today.com/video/gnash-and-olivia-o-brien-perform-their-hit-i-hate-u-i-love-u-on-today-768472131750|website=TODAY|access-date=September 19, 2016}}</ref>

In February 2017, O'Brien released "Empty" as the lead single from her debut extended play.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gore|first1=Sydney|title=Olivia O'Brien Will Make You Feel Less "Empty"|url=http://www.nylon.com/articles/olivia-obrien-empty-video-premiere|website=NYLON|access-date=21 February 2017|language=en}}</ref> In March 2017, O'Brien was nominated for an [[IHeartRadio Music Awards|iHeartRadio Music Award]] for Best Solo Breakout, but lost to [[Zayn Malik]].<ref name="iHeartRadio awards">{{cite web|last1=Thorpe|first1=Isha|title=Here's The Complete List Of #iHeartAwards Winners {{!}} iHeartRadio Music Awards {{!}} iHeartRadio|url=http://news.iheart.com/features/iheartradio-music-awards-15/articles/15/490103/heres-the-complete-list-of-iheartradio-15616268/|website=iHeartRadio|access-date=7 March 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170306212021/http://news.iheart.com/features/iheartradio-music-awards-15/articles/15/490103/heres-the-complete-list-of-iheartradio-15616268/|archive-date=6 March 2017}}</ref> She released the next single from the EP in July, "RIP", with accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web|title=RIP - Single by Olivia O'Brien on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/rip/id1256601187?i=1256601665|website=iTunes|access-date=14 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Olivia O'Brien - RIP|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Th0btDcWHqQ|access-date=14 July 2017|date=13 July 2017}}</ref> The third single, "No Love", followed in November along with her debut extended play, ''It's Not That Deep''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Olivia O'Brien Releases Debut EP 'It's Not That Deep' via Island Records|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Olivia-OBrien-Releases-Debut-EP-Its-Not-That-Deep-via-Island-Records-20171117|website=Broadway World|access-date=21 November 2017|language=en}}</ref> O'Brien featured in the acoustic version of [[Jack & Jack|Jack & Jack's]] single "Beg", released in January 2018.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gore|first1=Sydney|title=Olivia O'Brien Hops On Jack & Jack's Acoustic Version Of "Beg"|url=http://trl.mtv.com/news/3060979/jack-and-jack-olivia-obrien-beg/|website=MTV News|access-date=9 February 2018|language=en}}</ref> In June 2018, a remix of "RIP" was released with the collaboration of [[G-Eazy]] and [[THEY.|Drew Love]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/rip-feat-g-eazy-drew-love-single/1402200179|title=RIP (feat. G-Eazy & Drew Love) - Single by Olivia O'Brien|work=iTunes (US), Apple Music|date=June 29, 2018|access-date=June 29, 2018}}</ref> In August 2018, "UDK" was released as the first promotional single from O'Brien's debut studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/art-books-music/a22635553/olivia-obrien-udk-song/|title=Olivia O'Brien Puts Haters in Their Place with "UDK"|work=[[Harper's Bazaar]]|date=August 3, 2018}}</ref> O'Brien released the lead single, "I Don't Exist", in September, followed by "Care Less More" in November, both with accompanying music videos.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wass |first1=Mike |title=Olivia O'Brien's Quirky "Care Less More" Video: Premiere |url=https://www.idolator.com/7688348/olivia-obrien-care-less-more-video-premiere |website=idolator |access-date=5 December 2018 |date=16 November 2018}}</ref> The third single, "Love Myself", was released on February 1, 2019.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://celebmix.com/olivia-obrien-releases-new-single-love-myself/|title=Olivia O'Brien Releases New Single 'Love Myself'|date=2019-02-06|website=CelebMix|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-02-06}}</ref> In March, O'Brien announced that her debut studio album, ''[[Was It Even Real?]]'', was scheduled for release on April 26, 2019. She then released "Just Friends" as the second promotional single, and "Just a Boy", as the final single before the release of the album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Multi-Platinum-Olivia-OBrien-Releases-Track-JUST-A-BOY-Ahead-Of-Debut-Album-20190419|title=Multi-Platinum Olivia O'Brien Releases Track JUST A BOY Ahead Of Debut Album|author=BWW News Desk|website=BroadwayWorld.com|language=en|access-date=2019-04-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ventsmagazine.com/2019/03/29/multi-platinum-olivia-obrien-releases-track-just-friends-alongside-album-was-it-even-real-announcement%E2%80%80/|title=Multi-Platinum Olivia O'Brien Releases Track "Just Friends" Alongside Album "Was It Even Real" Announcement -|website=ventsmagazine.com|access-date=2019-04-06}}</ref> In May 2019, O'Brien collaborated with the Norwegian group [[Seeb (music producers)|Seeb]] on "[[Fade Out (Seeb, Olivia O'Brien and Space Primates song)|Fade Out]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/olivia-obrien-opts-to-fade-out-in-playful|title=Olivia O'Brien Opts to "Fade Out" in Playful Collaboration with Seeb & Space Primates|website=[[Ones to Watch]]|access-date=2019-07-16}}</ref> The single was accompanied by a music video.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Olivia-OBrien-Teams-Up-With-Seeb-For-Summer-Anthem-FADE-OUT-20190515|title=Olivia O'Brien Teams Up With Seeb For Summer Anthem FADE OUT|author=BWW News Desk|website=BroadwayWorld.com|language=en|access-date=2019-07-16}}</ref>

===2019–2020: Micromixtapes and ''Josslyn''===
[[File:Olivia O'Brien 3 7 2020 (50774079761).jpg|thumb|right|160px|O'Brien performing in [[Orlando]] in 2020.]]
On November 15, 2019, O'Brien released her second EP, ''It Was a Sad Fucking Summer''.<ref name="micromixtape">{{Cite news|url=https://nylon.com/olivia-obrien-sad-fucking-summer|title=Olivia O'Brien Ends Her "Sad Fucking Summer" With A Micro-Mixtape|last=Stubblebine|first=Allison|date=November 15, 2019|work=[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]]|language=en|access-date=November 16, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url= https://vmagazine.com/article/it-was-a-sad-fking-summer-for-olivia-obrien/|title= It Was A Sad F**king Summer for Olivia O'Brien|work=[[V (American magazine)|V]]|last=Kelly|first=Dylan|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The EP forms part of a series O'Brien describes as "micromixtapes".<ref name="micromixtape" /> With this, she also announced a tour, It Was a Sad F**king Tour. The tour took place from February 2020 to March 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B45Oiu_Hrjw/|title=olivia o'brien on Instagram: "IM GOING BACK ON TOUR!!!!! It Was a Sad F**cking Tour starts this february! i'm so excited to see u all, and i want to make sure you guys…"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-02-15}}</ref> On February 6, 2020, O'Brien released her second micromixtape, ''The Results Of My Poor Judgement''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://soundigest.com/2020/02/13/olivia-obrien-releases-micro-mixtape-the-results-of-my-poor-judgement/amp/|title=Olivia O'Brien Releases Micro-Mixtape The Results of My Poor Judgement|work=Soundigest|access-date=April 23, 2020}}</ref> In April 2020, O'Brien was set to perform at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella Festival]], which was later canceled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.pmnewsnigeria.com/2020/01/03/coachella-announces-2020-lineup-see-full-list/|title= Coachella announces 2020 lineup, See Full list|work=[[P.M. News]]|last=Okanlawon|first=Taiwo|access-date=January 4, 2020}}</ref> The second track on ''The Results Of My Poor Judgement'', "Josslyn", was made a single in early May 2020 due to a dance challenge on [[TikTok]] with users dancing to the song garnering the song success.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Olivia O'Brien's "Josslyn" Scores Midwest Pop Radio Airplay Amid Spotify, TikTok Gains|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2020/05/09/olivia-obriens-josslyn-scores-midwest-pop-radio-airplay-amid-spotify-tiktok-gains/|date=2020-05-09|website=Headline Planet|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref> A "quarantine" music video shot at O'Brien's house in isolation during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] was released on May 8.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Olivia O'Brien on her start in music, micromixtapes and the story behind "Josslyn" [Interview]|url=https://earmilk.com/2020/05/12/olivia-obrien-on-her-start-in-music-micromixtapes-and-the-story-behind-josslyn-interview/|date=2020-05-12|website=EARMILK|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref> The single would be re-released on the ''Josslyn'' EP which included the original version of the song, a remix, a [[Demo (music)|demo]] of the song and a [[radio edit]] on May 15.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Josslyn - EP by Olivia O'Brien|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/josslyn-ep/1512464892|website=Apple Music|date=May 15, 2020|language=en-us|access-date=2020-05-27}}</ref> A remix with rapper [[24kGoldn]] was released on May 22,<ref>{{Citation|title=Josslyn (with 24kGoldn)|date=May 22, 2020|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/3Q7NAVhf5g8rhjkXehYJ6E|language=en|access-date=2020-05-27}}</ref> whilst the original song was serviced to top 40 radio formats on June 2.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Top 40/M Future Releases {{!}} Mainstream Hit Songs Being Released and Their Release Dates ...|url=https://www.allaccess.com/top40-mainstream/future-releases|date=2020-05-27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200527080736/https://www.allaccess.com/top40-mainstream/future-releases|access-date=2020-05-27|archive-date=May 27, 2020}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''Episodes'' ===
On September 14, 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|title=olivia o'brien on Instagram: "now 9/18"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CFFg6yHnQIq/|access-date=2020-09-18|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref> she announced the release of a new single, "Now", which was later released that Friday, September 18.<ref>{{Cite web|title=NOW - Single by Olivia O'Brien|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/now-single/1531320880|access-date=2020-09-18|website=Apple Music|date=September 18, 2020|language=en-us}}</ref> "Now" was teased in various posts leading up to the official announcement.<ref>{{Cite web|title=olivia o'brien's Instagram photo: "u probably think i'll never find out who ur fucking with but i know who ur fucking right now ;)"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CE7zfSOnsrl/|access-date=2021-01-16|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref> O'Brien then released the single, "Better Than Feeling Lonely" on January 1, 2021,<ref name=":1" /> after previously teasing the song on her Instagram and TikTok accounts.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.pmstudio.com/music/music.html?page=20210101-12836|title=Olivia O'Brien Releases New Song "Better Than Feeling Lonely"|work=PM Studio|date=January 1, 2021|access-date=January 1, 2021}}</ref> On February 15, 2021, O'Brien uploaded two TikTok videos featuring songs "Sociopath" and "Bitch Back". She told her followers that the video with the most virtual interaction would be released as a single.<ref>{{Citation|title=HELP ME PICK MY NEXT SINGLE !!|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@oliviagobrien/video/6929595325820751110|language=en|access-date=2021-02-16}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=HELP ME PICK MY NEXT SINGLE !!|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@oliviagobrien/video/6929595407035141382|language=en|access-date=2021-02-16}}</ref> She then clarified that both are featured on her upcoming second studio album.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2021-02-15|title=Screenshot of Comment from "Bitch Back" Announcement TikTok|url=https://i.imgur.com/vPWPLl1.png|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref> "Sociopath", after receiving the most virtual interaction, was released on April 9, 2021, as the first single from her upcoming album.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cantor |first1=Brian |title=Olivia O'Brien Releases Horror Music Video For New Single "Sociopath," Featuring Kelsey Calemine, Stassie Karanikolaou, Claudia Sulewski, More |url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2021/04/09/olivia-obrien-releases-horror-music-video-for-new-single-sociopath-featuring-kelsey-calemine-stassie-karanikolaou-claudia-sulewski-more/ |access-date=April 9, 2021 |work=Headline Planet |date=April 9, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Vilenne |first1=Louise |title=Nieuwe single Olivia O'Brien – "Sociopath" |url=https://www.dansendeberen.be/2021/04/09/nieuwe-single-olivia-obrien-sociopath/ |access-date=April 9, 2021 |work=Dansen Deberen |date=April 9, 2021}}</ref> Later on April 9, 2021, she announced in an Instagram post that her album would be titled ''Episodes'',<ref>{{Cite web|title=olivia o'brien on Instagram: "sociopath is out now! this is the first single off of my new album EPISODES. this album will be split into two parts, with the first part..."|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CNdFaq5HXx5/|access-date=April 10, 2021|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref> and that it would be released in two parts. Part one, titled ''Episodes: Season 1'' was released on June 11, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-11|title=Olivia O'Brien Tells The Story Behind "I Hate U, I Love U," Talks Part One Of New Record 'Episodes'|url=https://americansongwriter.com/olivia-obrien-tells-the-story-behind-i-hate-u-i-love-u-talks-part-one-of-new-record-episodes/|access-date=2021-06-11|website=American Songwriter|language=en-US}}</ref>
