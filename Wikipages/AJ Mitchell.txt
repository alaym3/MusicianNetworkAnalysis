'''Aaron Fredrick''' "'''AJ'''" '''Mitchell Jr.''' (born May 17, 2001<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url= https://www.allmusic.com/artist/aj-mitchell-mn0003779965/biography|title= AJ Mitchell {{!}} Biography|publisher=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref>), is an American singer-songwriter and musician who is currently signed to [[Epic Records]]. He gained a following in 2017 after posting clips of himself singing and performing covers on [[YouTube]] and [[Instagram]]. His first independent single, "Used To Be", was written by Mitchell at age 13 and has over 100 million streams.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.bnd.com/living/magazine/article229946864.html|title=Is a former Belleville teen headed for singing stardom? His L.A. manager says ‘yes’|date=May 3, 2019|website=Belleville News Democrat|last=Maddox|first=Teri}}</ref>

Mitchell has four songs that have reached the Top 40 on the US radio charts, the July 2018 release "Girls", which reached No. 39,<ref name="headlineplanet.com">{{cite web|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2018/11/25/aj-mitchells-girls-enters-top-40-pop-radio/|title=AJ Mitchell's "Girls" Enters Top 40 at Pop Radio|date=November 25, 2018|website=Headline Planet}}</ref> the March 2019 release "All My Friends", which reached No. 24,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2019/05/12/billie-eilish-bryce-vine-aj-mitchell-songs-make-top-25-at-pop-radio/|title=Billie Eilish, Bryce Vine, AJ Mitchell Songs Make Top 25 at Pop Radio|date=May 12, 2019|website=Headline Planet}}</ref>  "Slow Dance" (featuring [[Ava Max]]), which reached No. 28,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2019/09/22/songs-by-ariana-miley-lana-maroon-5-aj-mitchell-ava-max-reach-top-40-at-pop-radio/|title=Songs By Ariana, Miley & Lana, Maroon 5, AJ Mitchell & Ava Max Reach Top 40 At Pop Radio|date=2019-09-22|website=Headline Planet|access-date=2019-11-01}}</ref> and "Stop" from his upcoming debut studio album ''Skyview'', which reached No. 33.<ref>{{Cite web|title=AJ Mitchell|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/aj-mitchell/chart-history/TFM/song/1257950|access-date=2021-06-15|website=Billboard}}</ref>

== Early life ==
AJ Mitchell was born in [[Belleville, Illinois]] on May 17, 2001. His mother, Allison Mitchell, is a small business owner and his father, Aaron Fredrick Mitchell Sr, is a traveling nurse. AJ is the couple's only son and youngest child, after their daughters Andrea and Addison (Addy).

Mitchell began piano lessons at 4, inspired by his father who was also learning to play piano and write songs. Mitchell attended Belleville West High School, where he played on the football team and sang in the school choir.<ref name="auto"/>

== Career ==
=== 2015–2016: Career beginnings ===
By 13, Mitchell began performing at local open-mic nights in his hometown and posting covers on YouTube and [[Instagram]], which helped him gain a significant following.<ref name="auto"/> Mitchell's social media fan base helped him capture the attention of the LA-based artist manager Mike Spitz whom Mitchell has worked with since 2015.

Mitchell's videos on social media granted him an opportunity to relocate to Los Angeles in 2015 to collaborate with internet marketing agency Team 10.  After a brief period together, Mitchell left the group to focus his energy entirely on music.<ref name="billboard.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/podcasts/8512667/aj-mitchell-interview-all-my-friends-pop-shop-podcast|title=AJ Mitchell Talks Breakout Single 'All My Friends' & Plans for His 'More Mature' Debut Album: 'It's So Personal'|website=Billboard}}</ref>

=== 2017–2018: ''Hopeful'' ===
In 2017, Mitchell released his first independent single "Used to Be", a song he wrote at age 13. The song was produced by hip-hop producer and engineer [[Mike Dean (record producer)|Mike Dean]]. "Used to Be" has now gained over 150 million streams.

After departing the social media group, Mitchell received interest from many different record labels but eventually decided to sign with [[Epic Records]]. He inked his first major deal with the label in February 2018.

Mitchell's first studio EP with Epic, ''Hopeful'', was released in July 2018. Its lead single "Girls", co-written by Mitchell and [[Romans (musician)|Romans]], reached No. 39 on the US [[Mediabase]] pop radio chart.<ref name="headlineplanet.com"/> In November 2018, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' and Vevo named AJ one of the top 19 artists to watch in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8485169/vevo-2019-dsvr-artists-watch-list-videos-billboard|title=Billboard Reteams With Vevo to Announce 2019 DSCVR Artists To Watch List: Exclusive|date=November 15, 2018|website=Billboard}}</ref>

[[Epic Records]] CEO [[Sylvia Rhone]] referred to Mitchell's music as "exciting things to come", when discussing the growing talent roster at the record label.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/epic-records-sylvia-rhone-chairman-promotion-interview-1203203095/|title=Epic Records Chairman Sylvia Rhone: 'It's a Watershed Moment for Black Culture'|first1=Shirley|last1=Halperin|date=May 2, 2019|work=Variety}}</ref>

In 2018, Mitchell collaborated with [[Bay Area]] R&B singer-songwriter [[Marteen]] for the song "No Plans". Marteen later joined Mitchell in a support slot on the following year's 2019 Hopeful Tour.

=== 2019–present: ''Slow Dance'' and ''Skyview''===
In May 2019, Mitchell collaborated with songwriting duo Teamwork and Scottish singer-songwriter [[Nina Nesbitt]] on the song "After Hours".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.epicrecords.com/aj-mitchell-nina-nesbitt-light-up-summer-with-joint-single-after-hours-produced-by-teamwork/|title=AJ MITCHELL & NINA NESBITT LIGHT UP SUMMER WITH JOINT SINGLE "AFTER HOURS" PRODUCED BY TEAMWORK.|date=May 10, 2019|website=Epic Records}}</ref><ref name="apple.com">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/afterhours-single/1462303072|title=Afterhours – Single by teamwork, Nina Nesbitt & AJ Mitchell|via=music.apple.com}}</ref>

Later that month, in an interview with ''Billboard'', Mitchell announced that his forthcoming debut full-length album with [[Epic Records]] was nearly finished and would be released "very, very soon".<ref name="billboard.com"/>

On August 23, 2019, Mitchell released his second EP ''Slow Dance'', which includes his singles "Talk So Much", "Move On", and the title track featuring American singer [[Ava Max]].<ref name="sd"/>

Mitchell's debut studio album ''Skyview'' was released in October 2021 and includes the singles "Used to Be", "Slow Dance", "Cameras On", and "Stop".<ref name=renamed_from_2021_on_20210618204053>{{cite web|url=https://www.sonymusic.ca/press_release/aj-mitchell-continues-to-cement-his-rise-with-electric-new-single-stop|title=AJ Mitchell continues to cement his rise with electric new single "Stop"|website=[[Sony Music]]|date=April 9, 2021|access-date=June 14, 2021}}</ref>

== Artistry ==

=== Musician ===
Mitchell cites a wide variety of music as influential to his songwriting style, regularly noting his admiration of artists like [[Bruno Mars]] and [[Lil Wayne]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/aj-mitchell-mn0003779965/biography|title=AJ Mitchell {{!}} Biography & History|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=May 23, 2019}}</ref>
