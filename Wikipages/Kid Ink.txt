'''Brian Todd Collins''' (born April 1, 1986),<ref name="auto">{{cite web|last=Kellman |first=Andy |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/kid-ink-mn0002603333/biography |title=Kid Ink &#124; Biography |website=AllMusic |access-date=March 20, 2014}}</ref> known professionally as '''Kid Ink''', is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref name="MTV - RCA Deal"/><ref name=mtvuk>{{cite web|title=Kid Ink - MTV UK |url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/artists/kid-ink |access-date=May 22, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120531102435/http://www.mtv.co.uk/artists/kid-ink |archive-date=May 31, 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Gustafson|first=Kyle|title=Kid Ink at Howard Theatre|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/kid-ink-at-howard-theatre/2012/05/07/gIQAF2Jo8T_story.html|access-date=May 8, 2012|newspaper=Washington Post|date=May 7, 2012}}</ref> Collins released his debut independent album ''[[Up & Away (Kid Ink album)|Up & Away]]'' in 2012, following an EP titled ''[[Almost Home (Kid Ink EP)|Almost Home]]'', and his first major studio album, ''[[My Own Lane]]'' in 2014. The latter spawned the singles "[[Show Me (Kid Ink song)|Show Me]]" (featuring [[Chris Brown]]), "[[Iz U Down]]" (featuring [[Tyga]]), and "[[Main Chick]]" (featuring Chris Brown). On February 3, 2015, he released his third studio album ''[[Full Speed (album)|Full Speed]]'', which spawned the singles "[[Body Language (Kid Ink song)|Body Language]]" (featuring [[Usher (musician)|Usher]] and [[Tinashe]]), "[[Hotel (Kid Ink song)|Hotel]]" (featuring Chris Brown), and "[[Be Real (Kid Ink song)|Be Real]]" (featuring [[Dej Loaf]]).

On December 25, 2015, he released a surprise commercial mixtape ''[[Summer in the Winter]]'' with the supporting single "[[Promise (Kid Ink song)|Promise]]" (featuring [[Fetty Wap]]). In 2016, Kid Ink released a mixtape ''RSS2'', and an EP ''[[7 Series (Kid Ink album)|7 Series]]'' with lead single "[[F with U]]" (featuring [[Ty Dolla Sign]]) in 2017. In December 2018, he dropped the EP ''Missed Calls'' supported by the single "YUSO" (featuring [[Lil Wayne]] and [[Saweetie]]).

== Career ==

=== 2005–2012: Career beginnings, Tha Alumni Music Group and ''Up & Away'' ===
During his teenage years, Brian Todd Collins attended after-school music programs, where he produced hip-hop beats and tracks. He cites  [[Pharrell Williams]] and [[Swizz Beatz]] as part of his inspiration for its work ethic with the production and songwriting, when he said: "These were artists and producers who were writing for other people in all types of genres. It just gave me an open ear for all different types of music and that's how I approached it". He landed his first genuine production credit for a [[Nipsey Hussle]] single in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/international/8070671/kid-ink-clockenflap-festival-interview|title=Kid Ink interview, "In the middle of it all"|publisher=Billboard|date=2017}}</ref> At 22, Collins began [[rapping]] full-time, soon after<ref name="usatoday1">{{cite news|author=Patrick Ryan, USA TODAY 7 a.m. EST February 23, 2014 |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2014/02/23/on-the-verge-kid-ink-show-me/5467599/ |title=On the Verge: Kid Ink embraces radio-friendly rap |publisher=Usatoday.com |date=February 23, 2014 |access-date=March 20, 2014}}</ref> adopting the temporary [[stage name]] Rockstar. On February 1, 2010, Collins released his first official mixtape, titled ''World Tour''. The mixtape gained popularity that grabbed some of the attention towards a [[disc jockey|radio disc jockey]], DJ Ill Will, the founder of HotNewHipHop. He later signed to his record label, Tha Alumni Group label, and its joint label, 88 Classic. Collins also changed his stage name to Kid Ink, in respect for a label-mate, DJ Rockstar.<ref>Kid Ink interview</ref>

In 2011, Collins released his second mixtape, called ''Crash Landing''. The mixtape features [[guest appearance]]s from [[Roscoe Dash]], [[Ty Dolla Sign]], and [[Meek Mill]], among others. During the summer of 2011, Ink released his third mixtape, called ''Daydreamer'' that made available for digital download. The mixtape features guest appearances from [[Cory Gunz]], [[Maejor Ali]], [[Ray J]], [[Sean Kingston]] and [[Bow Wow (rapper)|Bow Wow]], among others. In September 2011, Collins released his fourth mixtape, called ''Wheels Up''. The mixtape features guest appearances from [[2 Chainz]], [[Nipsey Hussle]] and [[Tyga]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2012/02/13/introducing-kid-ink-rising-l-a-artist-talks-transition-from-producing-quick-notoriety/ |title=Introducing Kid Ink: Rising L.A. Artist Talks Transition From Producing & Quick Notoriety |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=February 13, 2012 |access-date=May 8, 2012}}</ref>

In February 2012, Collins was revealed to be a part of the ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'''s yearly Freshman class alongside fellow emerging artists at the time, such as [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Iggy Azalea]] and [[French Montana]], among others. On June 12, 2012, Ink released his debut [[studio album]], called ''[[Up & Away (Kid Ink album)|Up & Away]]''; including the [[Record production|production]] that was provided from [[Ned Cameron]], Capsvl and [[Jahlil Beats]], among others. The album debuted at number 20 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. Supporting this album was the release of his single, called "Time of Your Life", followed by a release of his second single, called "Lost in the Sauce".<ref>{{cite web|title=Kid Ink "Up & Away" Cover Art, Release Date |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.19519/title.kid-ink-up-away-cover-art-release-date/ |website=HipHopDX |access-date=May 8, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140310142417/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.19519/title.kid-ink-up-away-cover-art-release-date//|archive-date=March 10, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Kid Ink Reveals 'Up & Away' Album Cover Art x Confirms Release Date |url=http://thisbeatgoes.com/hip-hop-news/kid-ink-reveals-up-away-album-cover-art-x-confirms-release-date/ |access-date=May 8, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120513231952/http://thisbeatgoes.com/hip-hop-news/kid-ink-reveals-up-away-album-cover-art-x-confirms-release-date/ |archive-date=May 13, 2012}}</ref> In November of that year, Ink released his fifth mixtape, ''Rocketshipshawty''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.21910/title.mixtape-release-dates-joe-budden-wale-pusha-t-young-jeezy |title=Mixtape Release Dates: Joe Budden, Wale, Pusha T, Young Jeezy |website=HipHopDX |access-date=July 1, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150207101745/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.21910/title.mixtape-release-dates-joe-budden-wale-pusha-t-young-jeezy|archive-date=February 7, 2015 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

===2013–2014: Signing with RCA, ''Almost Home'' and ''My Own Lane''===
{{see also|Almost Home (Kid Ink EP)|My Own Lane}}
On January 4, 2013, he announced that he had signed a deal with [[RCA Records]] and premiered his first major label single, titled "[[Bad Ass (song)|Bad Ass]]" featuring [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]] and [[Meek Mill]] and produced by Devin Cruise.<ref name="MTV - RCA Deal"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22428/title.kid-ink-signs-to-rca-records|title=Kid Ink Signs To RCA Records |website=HipHopDX |access-date=July 1, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402170318/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22428/title.kid-ink-signs-to-rca-records|archive-date=April 2, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> The song would be released for digital download on January 22, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/bad-ass-feat.-meek-mill-wale/id592907153 |title=iTunes - Music - Bad Ass (feat. Meek Mill & Wale) - Single by Kid Ink |publisher=iTunes|date=January 22, 2013 |access-date=June 3, 2013}}</ref> The song was released to Rhythm Crossover radio in the United States on February 26, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fmqb.com/article.asp?id=16691 |title=Radio Industry News, Music Industry Updates, Arbitron Ratings, Music News and more! |publisher=FMQB |access-date=June 3, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160411072813/http://www.fmqb.com/Article.asp?id=16691 |archive-date=April 11, 2016 }}</ref> It has since peaked at number 90 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and number 27 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart. He released an [[extended play|EP]] titled ''[[Almost Home (Kid Ink album)|Almost Home]]'' on May 28, 2013. It featured other artists including [[French Montana]], Wale, Meek Mill, [[ASAP Ferg]] and [[Rico Love]].<ref name="hiphopdx1">{{cite web |last=Paine |first=Jake |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23942/title.kid-ink-almost-home-ep-release-date-cover-art |title=Kid Ink "Almost Home EP" Release Date & Cover Art |website=HipHopDX |date=May 14, 2013 |access-date=June 3, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130607230404/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.23942/title.kid-ink-almost-home-ep-release-date-cover-art |archive-date=June 7, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Upon its release, the EP debuted at number 27 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. It also produced the single "[[Money and the Power]]" which was featured in HBO's ''[[Hard Knocks (2001 TV series)|Hard Knocks]]'' and in the sports video game ''[[NBA Live 14]]'' as well as being one of the official theme songs of [[WrestleMania 31]]. On September 17, 2013, he released the first single from his second studio album ''[[My Own Lane]]'', entitled "[[Show Me (Kid Ink song)|Show Me]]" featuring [[Chris Brown]] and produced by [[DJ Mustard]].<ref>{{cite web |author=Nigel D. |url=http://realtalkny.uproxx.com/2013/09/topic/topic/music/kid-ink-ft-chris-brown-show-me-prod-by-dj-mustard/ |title=Kid Ink ft. ===2015–2016: ''Full Speed'' and ''Summer in the Winter''===
{{see also|Full Speed (album)}}
On September 9, 2014, "[[Body Language (Kid Ink song)|Body Language]]", which features [[Usher (entertainer)|Usher]] and [[Tinashe]], was released as the first single from his third studio album, ''[[Full Speed (album)|Full Speed]]''. The album was released on February 3, 2015, and has since peaked at number 14 on the US ''[[Billboard 200]]'' album chart.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kid-ink-announces-new-single-body-language-news.11898.html|title = Kid Ink Announces New Single "Vegas Strip"|date = September 2, 2014}}</ref> On December 25, 2015, Kid Ink released a commercial mixtape, ''[[Summer in the Winter]]'', with songs featuring [[Fetty Wap]], [[Akon]], [[Omarion]], [[Starrah]] and [[Bïa Krieger]].

===2016–present: ''RSS2'' and ''7 Series''===
On September 19, 2016, Kid Ink released the mixtape ''RSS2'' on Soundcloud. Kid Ink wanted to get back into his old mixtape sound. ''RSS2'' features underground artists including his younger brother, Juliann Alexander. ''RSS2'' spawned the "One Day" single from the project, with a music video. ''RSS2'' was released on Spotify and every other streaming service on March 30, 2017. On April 7, Kid Ink released the single "F With U" featuring [[Ty Dolla Sign]], with DJ Mustard producing. His second [[Extended Play|EP]] ''[[7 Series (Kid Ink album)|7 Series]]'' was released May 5, 2017.

== Personal life ==
Kid Ink has over 100 tattoos on his face, back, arms, hands, and torso.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.axs.com/five-things-you-didn-t-know-about-kid-ink-48206|title=Five things you didn't know about Kid Ink |work=AXS |access-date=March 5, 2019|language=en-US}}</ref> He got his first tattoo when he was 16 and has been "addicted" to getting tattoos since. His favorites include portraits of his mother and grandfather on his chest and a tiger on the front of his neck, which represents his Chinese birth sign. He showed off his tattoos for one of [[People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals|PETA]]'s "Ink Not Mink" anti-fur ads in 2013.<ref>Latifa Muhamed,"Kid Ink Joins PETA's "Ink Not Mink" Campaign," [http://hiphopwired.com/2013/03/12/kid-ink-joins-petas-ink-not-mink-campaign-videophotos/ HipHopWired.com], March 12, 2013.</ref>

Kid Ink has been in a relationship with model Asiah Azante for over a decade. The couple became engaged in June 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2015/07/01/kid-ink-gets-engaged-to-longtime-girlfriend-asiah-azante/ |title=Kid Ink Gets Engaged To Longtime Girlfriend Asiah Azante |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=July 1, 2015}}</ref> Azante gave birth to a girl in February 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kid-ink-and-fiance-asiah-azante-welcome-first-child-together-news.20110.html|title=Kid Ink & Fiance Asiah Azante Welcome First Child Together|website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref>
