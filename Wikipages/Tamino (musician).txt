'''Tamino-Amir Moharam Fouad''' (born 24 October 1996), known [[mononym]]ously as '''Tamino''', is a Belgian singer, musician and model.<ref>{{cite web |title=Tamino |url=https://www.imgmodels.com/taminomoharamfouad |website=IMG Models |accessdate=August 31, 2020}}</ref> He is of Belgian, Egyptian and Lebanese origin.<ref>{{YouTube | id=7HwXq7HSauU | title=Exclusive Interview With Tamino}}</ref> He is the grandson of renowned Egyptian singer and movie star [[Muharram Fouad]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/the-405-exchange-tamino-connects-with-his-egyptian-roots-on-amir-156 |title=The 405 Exchange: Tamino connects with his Egyptian roots on Amir |quote= It's this prevailing feeling that makes us grateful for an artist like Tamino – the Belgian/Egyptian musician who's truly doing his own thing. His debut album, Amir, is a brave release in a time of Spotify playlists and background coffee shop albums. }}</ref>

== Career ==
Before commercial success, at age 17 Tamino trained vocally at the Amsterdam Royal Conservatory. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/culture/article/tamino-interview|title = Belgian singer Tamino merges Arabic sounds with western sensibilities|date = 12 July 2020}}</ref>

In November 2016, Tamino was invited by Belgian band ''Het Zesde Metaal'' to play a [[Radio 1 (Belgium)|Radio 1 session]]. His first single, ''Habibi'', made an impression and he was immediately picked up by Radio 1.<ref> [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1jDqGvr_u9c '' Habibi '' on Radio 1 session in November 2016]</ref>

In 2017, Tamino won a new musical talent competition at [[Studio Brussels]].<ref>[https://stubru.be/denieuwelichting/denieuwelichting2017tamino0 De Nieuwe Lichting 2017 at Studio Brussels]</ref> On 11 October 2017 he played in the [[Ancienne Belgique]] (Brussels) where he was nicknamed 'the Belgian [[Jeff Buckley]]'.<ref> [https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/tamino-interview-album-amir-release-date-tracklist-radiohead-colin-greenwood-a8580426.html The Independent – "Is Tamino the heir to Jeff Buckley?" ]</ref> He has also played at [[Palais 12]] (Brussels) and during the [[Music Industry Awards|
 Flemish Music Industry Awards ]] and [[Melkweg|the Melkweg]] in Amsterdam. During the summer of 2017, he was featured in festivals across Belgium and the Netherlands such as [[Rock Werchter]] and [[Pukkelpop]].  

In the summer of 2018 ''Tamino'' performed in festivals across Europe including [[Rock en Seine]] (Paris).<ref>[https://www.bruzz.be/music/met-prins-tamino-naar-parijs-2018-11-29 Bruzz Media – "Met prins Tamino naar Parijs"]</ref> 
On 19 October 2018 Tamino released his first full-length album ''Amir'' ([[Arts_%26_Crafts_Productions|Arts & Crafts]]/[[Communion_Music|Communion]]). At the end of October, he played three sold-out shows at the Ancienne Belgique as part of a European headline tour. On 8 November 2018 he performed in Iceland at [[Iceland Airwaves]]. 

''Tamino'' was featured in [[Live Nation]]'s Ones To Watch,<ref>[https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/meet-tamino-the-mesmerizing-up-and-comer-with One's To Watch – "Meet Tamino, The Mesmerizing Up and Comer With Middle Eastern Influence"]</ref> which spoke of Tamino's voice saying his "falsetto surpasses 'face melting' and rises to a space of pureness that can only be described as divine."  He recently announced his first headline stadium show in November 2019 at the [[Lotto Arena]].
 
In 2019, ''Tamino'' played four shows at [[South_by_Southwest|SXSW]], his first ever North American shows where he shared the live version of his single "Indigo Night", with [[Radiohead]] bassist [[Colin Greenwood]]. On 10 May, Tamino released his live EP titled Live at Ancienne Belgique. Following SXSW he returned to Europe for a tour across the continent, including France, Great Britain, Germany, and the [[Sziget Festival]] in Budapest. 

Tamino has also been featured in the fashion world and alongside supermodel [[Gisele Bündchen]] in Missoni's SS19 Collection. During Paris Fashion week he was featured "in conversation" with Maison Valentino and performed a solo version of "Indigo Night".<ref>[https://www.instagram.com/p/BsqAyhzBddb/ Indigo Night]</ref> He has been featured in [[Vogue Hommes]]' March 2019 14-page spread which was photographed by critically lauded photographer [[Paolo Roversi]].

=== ''Amir'' ===
On 19 October 2018 Tamino released his first full-length album ''Amir''. Throughout the album, Tamino is joined by a collective of Arabic musicians based in Brussels called Nagham Zikrayat, an orchestra predominantly made up of professional musicians from the Middle East, most of whom are refugees that fled Iraq and Syria.

The BBC hailed Amir as "The New Sound Of [[Nile river|The Nile]]<ref>[https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-46315558 BBC News – "Tamino: The new 'Sound of the Nile'?"]</ref>" an ode to Tamino's grandfather [[Muharram Fouad]]. The Independent included Amir in their top 10 albums of 2018.<ref>[https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/albums-of-the-year-2018-arctic-monkeys-christine-queens-the-1975-janelle-monae-a8661091.html The Independent – "The 40 best albums of 2018"]</ref> Tamino won the Anchor Award at Germany's 2018 Reeperbahn festival and has been nominated for 5 MIA Award's in Belgium.
