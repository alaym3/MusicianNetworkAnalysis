'''''JackBoys''''' (stylized as '''JACKBOYS''') is a [[compilation album]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/listen-to-travis-scotts-new-jackboys-compilation/|title=Listen to Travis Scott's New ''JACKBOYS'' Compilation|last=Hussey|first=Allison|date=December 27, 2019|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|access-date=December 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/12/travis-scott-jackboys-stream|title=Travis Scott Drops Cactus Jack Compilation Project 'JACKBOYS'|last=Espinoza|first=Joshua|date=December 27, 2019|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|access-date=December 27, 2019}}</ref> by [[Cactus Jack Records#Roster|JackBoys]] (American record label, [[Cactus Jack Records]]) and American rapper [[Travis Scott]] (the leader). JackBoys is a collective and group of American rappers signed to Scott's Cactus Jack Records, which is made up of Scott himself, [[Sheck Wes]], [[Don Toliver]], Luxury Tax, and Scott's [[Disc jockey|DJ]] Chase B. The album was released on December 27, 2019. It features [[guest appearance]]s from [[Rosalía (singer)|Rosalía]], [[Lil Baby]], [[Quavo]] and [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]] from the hip hop trio [[Migos]], [[Young Thug]], and [[Pop Smoke]]. Upon debut, ''JackBoys'' opened atop the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] on January 11, 2020, becoming the first number one album of the 2020s. This is Scott's third album to debut atop the chart since his third studio album, [[Astroworld (album)|''Astroworld'']] (2018).

==Background and release==
In March 2017, Scott announced he would be launching his own [[Imprint label|imprint]], under the name of Cactus Jack Records.<ref name="cjr">{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/travis-scott-announces-new-label-cactus-jack-records-news.29716.html|title=Travis Scott Announces New Label "Cactus Jack Records"|website=HotNewHipHop.com|access-date=6 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2017/03/travis-scott-label-cactus-jack-records/|title=Travis Scott Plans To Lead New Label, Cactus Jack Records|website=vibe.com|access-date=8 March 2017}}</ref> During an interview, Scott said, "I'm not doing it to have financial control over my music. I want first and foremost to help other artists, launch new names, to provide opportunities. I want to do for them what happened to me, but better."<ref>{{cite web |title=Travis Scott Has Launched His Own Label, Cactus Jack Records |url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/03/06/travis-scott-launched-hiw-own-label-cactus-jack-records |website=The Fader |access-date=29 December 2019}}</ref> In September 2017, [[Smokepurpp]] signed to the label, but left sometime later in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/smokepurpp-clears-up-whether-travis-scott-dropped-him-from-cactus-jack-records-news.99804.html?_amp|title=Smokepurpp Clears Up Whether Travis Scott Dropped Him From Cactus Jack Records|last=Zidel|first=Alex|website=Hotnewhiphop|date=January 2, 2019|access-date=January 13, 2020}}</ref>

In February 2018, the label signed [[Sheck Wes]] in a joint deal with [[Interscope Records]] and [[GOOD Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pigeonsandplanes.com/music/2018/03/sheck-wes-do-that-wyoming-kanye-west|last=Cowen|first=Trace William|title=Sheck Wes Drops "Do That" and Reveals He Was Supposed to Be in Wyoming With Kanye|work=[[Pigeons & Planes]]|date=March 20, 2018|access-date=June 3, 2018}}</ref>
In August, [[Don Toliver]] was signed to the label.<ref name="DXCactus">{{cite news | last=Harling| first=Danielle| title =Don Toliver Signs To Travis Scott's Cactus Jack "Family"| website = HipHopDX | date =August 6, 2018 | url =https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.47923/title.don-toliver-joins-travis-scotts-cactus-jack-family#| access-date =September 7, 2018}}</ref> After the release of Scott's third studio album, ''[[Astroworld (album)|Astroworld]]'' (2018), artists [[Sheck Wes]] and [[Don Toliver]] began to rise to popularity after they appeared on the album. Wes's June 2017 song "[[Mo Bamba (song)|Mo Bamba]]" went viral in 2018, while Toliver's May 2019 song "[[No Idea (song)|No Idea]]" went viral on the app [[TikTok]] in the same year. Wes and Toliver both helped Scott on ''Astroworld''. Wes was featured alongside American rapper [[Juice Wrld]] on "No Bystanders" and Toliver was featured on "Can't Say".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/08/03/travis-scott-astroworld-credits|title=Here are the full album credits for Travis Scott's ASTROWORLD|last=Renshaw|first=David|website=[[The Fader]]|date=August 3, 2018|access-date=February 27, 2020}}</ref>

On November 29, 2019, Scott released JackBoys merchandise on his website, including a digital album preorder for $10.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2019/12/travis-scott-cactus-jack-jackboys-merch-release|title=Travis Scott and Cactus Jack Unveil Graphic-Heavy 'JACKBOYS' Merch|website=Hypebeast|date=December 27, 2019|access-date=February 27, 2020}}</ref> On December 24, Scott revealed on [[Twitter]] that the album would be released within the week.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/trvisXX/status/1209611008081154049|title=WHOLE GANG IS DELIRIOUS JACKBOYS PACK THIS WEEK? SHALL THE FANS EAT !!!!!! UNBUCKLE UR SEAT BELTS pic.twitter.com/TCJDYcgWs1|last=WEBSTER|first=JACQUES|date=2019-12-24|website=@trvisXX|language=en|access-date=2019-12-28}}</ref> On December 26, a day before the album was released, the cover art and release date were revealed and the song titles were leaked on Shazam.<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=STRAPPEDUS|number=1210316772458393600|title=Tracklisting for Travis Scott's Cactus Jack project "JACKBOYS" is making {{sic|it|'s|nolink=y}} way into Shazam's system pic.twitter.com/MYFYs56feU|date=2019-12-26|access-date=2019-12-28}}</ref>

==Commercial performance==
''JackBoys'' debuted at number one on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with 154,000 [[album-equivalent unit]]s, of which 79,000 were pure album sales.<ref name="BB200">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8547319/travis-scott-led-jackboys-debuts-at-no-1-on-billboard-200-as|title=Travis Scott-led 'Jackboys' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200, As Christmas Albums Depart Chart|work=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=January 5, 2020|access-date=January 6, 2020}}</ref> In its second week, the album dropped to number four on the chart, earning 47,000 album-equivalent units.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8547862/roddy-ricch-billboard-200-number-one-two-weeks|title=Roddy Ricch Returns to No. 1 on Billboard 200 Albums Chart With 'Please Excuse Me For Being Antisocial'|magazine=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=January 13, 2020|access-date=April 15, 2020}}</ref> In its third week, the album dropped to number seven on the chart, earning 38,000 more units that week.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8548614/selena-gomez-rare-number-one-billboard-200-albums-chart|title=Selena Gomez Earns Third No. 1 Album on Billboard 200 Chart With 'Rare|magazine=Billboard|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=January 21, 2020|access-date=April 15, 2020}}</ref>

All the songs on the album debuted on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]] with the exception of the titular track.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/search?artist=JackBoys&chart_name=Hot+100|title=Chart History (Hot 100) - JackBoys|magazine=Billboard|access-date=January 8, 2020}}</ref>
