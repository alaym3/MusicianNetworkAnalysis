'''Chauncey Alexander Hollis Jr.'''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://repertoire.bmi.com/|title=BMI &#124; Songview Search|website=repertoire.bmi.com}}{{Dead link|date=August 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> (born May 21, 1987), better known by his stage name '''Hit-Boy''', is an American record producer, rapper, singer, and songwriter. In May 2011, he had signed a two-year deal with [[Kanye West]]'s [[GOOD Music]] production branch. With this stimulus, he produced singles such as "[[Niggas in Paris]]" by [[Jay-Z]] and [[Kanye West]], "[[Trophies (song)|Trophies]]" by [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], "[[Clique (song)|Clique]]" by [[GOOD Music]], and "[[Racks in the Middle]]" by [[Nipsey Hussle]] featuring [[Roddy Ricch]]. "Racks in the Middle" won [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance|Best Rap Performance]] at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|2020 Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/nipsey-hussle-roddy-ricch-hit-boy-win-best-rap-performance-racks-middle-2020-grammys|title=Nipsey Hussle Wins Best Rap Performance|date=January 26, 2020|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en|access-date=January 27, 2020|archive-date=January 27, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200127171144/https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/nipsey-hussle-roddy-ricch-hit-boy-win-best-rap-performance-racks-middle-2020-grammys|url-status=live}}</ref>

In December 2012, Hit-Boy signed a recording contract with [[Interscope Records]] and subsequently founded his own record label imprint, Hits Since '87.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hit-Boy Confirms Signing with Kanye West|date=May 12, 2011|url=http://www.rapbasement.com/kanye-west/051211-producer-hit-boy-talks-about-being-signed-to-kanye-wests-good-music-label.html|access-date=May 12, 2011|archive-date=May 15, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110515211530/http://www.rapbasement.com/kanye-west/051211-producer-hit-boy-talks-about-being-signed-to-kanye-wests-good-music-label.html|url-status=live}}</ref> He has since expanded his production catalog with singles such as "[[Drop the World]]" by [[Lil Wayne]], "[[Sorry (Beyoncé song)|Sorry]]" by [[Beyoncé]], and "[[Sicko Mode]]" by [[Travis Scott]], all of which have been [[RIAA certification|certified platinum]] or higher by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).

==Career==
Hollis got his start on [[Myspace]] when he received a message from [[Polow Da Don]] stating simply "Let's get this paper, pimp", and reached out to say he wanted to meet Hollis and discuss signing him professionally.<ref>{{cite web |last=Gordon |first=Jeremy |url=http://bullettmedia.com/article/how-producer-hit-boy-got-from-myspace-to-kanye-wests-speed-dial/ |title=How Hit-Boy Went From Myspace to Kanye West's Speed-Dial |publisher=Bullettmedia.com |access-date=August 10, 2014 |archive-date=November 1, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141101185457/http://bullettmedia.com/article/how-producer-hit-boy-got-from-myspace-to-kanye-wests-speed-dial/ |url-status=live }}</ref> He has produced for big-name artists such as [[Big Sean]], [[Kid Cudi]], [[Kanye West]], [[Jay-Z]], [[Beyoncé]], [[Lil Wayne]], [[Eminem]], [[50 Cent]], [[Mary J. Blige]], [[Nas]], [[Chris Brown (American entertainer)|Chris Brown]], [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Justin Bieber]], [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Selena Gomez]] and several others. The California native is one of the founding members of the collective brand Surf Club (along with fellow beat makers Chase N.Cashe, BCarr, and Chili Chill). Surf Club has become a team to host a number of artists as well as songwriters and other producers. Surf Club originated in southern California. They got their start in the industry in 2007, after linking with [[Polow Da Don]]'s [[Zone 4 (record label)|Zone 4]], an imprint of [[Interscope Records]]. He began his work with Kanye West when he produced his GOOD Friday track, "Christmas in Harlem",<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/content/v-exclusive-hit-boy|title=Hit-Boy talks signing to G.O.O.D Music}}</ref> [[Big Sean]], [[Pusha T]], [[Musiq Soulchild]] and [[Cyhi Da Prynce]]. On May 2, 2011,<ref>{{cite web|title=Producer Hit-Boy Joins Kanye West's G.O.O.D Music Label|url=http://www.theboombox.com/2011/05/09/producer-hit-boy-joins-kanye-wests-g-o-o-d-music/|access-date=May 9, 2011|archive-date=May 16, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130516045907/http://www.theboombox.com/2011/05/09/producer-hit-boy-joins-kanye-wests-g-o-o-d-music/|url-status=live}}</ref> he signed to [[Kanye West]]'s [[GOOD Music]] Label, produced the single "[[Niggas in Paris]]", on the collaborative "[[Watch the Throne]]" album created by [[Kanye West]] and [[Jay-Z]], and produced the singles "[[Goldie (song)|Goldie]]" and "1 Train" for [[ASAP Rocky]].

On June 7, 2012, Hollis released his first official song in which he raps, the promotional single "Jay-Z Interview", which was produced by [[Bink!]]. Later in July 2012, Hollis was featured rapping on [[CyHi The Prynce]]'s mixtape ''Ivy League Club'', on the song "Entourage". Hollis then released the self-produced track "Old School Caddy", which features fellow GOOD Music member [[Kid Cudi]]. These two tracks appeared on his first full-length project, a mixtape titled ''HITstory'', which was released for free download on his official website. On December 23, 2012, Hollis secured a recording contract with [[Interscope Records]], under the record label Blueprint Group, along with the L.E.P. Bogus Boys. Soon after, he tweeted: "2012 has been great to me and my fam. Feeling more blessed than ever. Excited to see what God has planned for 2013. TU."<ref>{{cite web |last=Donaldson |first=Erich |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/12/hit-boy-and-lep-bogus-boys-sign-to-interscope |title=Hit-Boy and L.E.P. Bogus Boys Sign to Interscope |publisher=Complex |date=December 21, 2012 |access-date=August 10, 2014 |archive-date=November 1, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141101202437/http://www.complex.com/music/2012/12/hit-boy-and-lep-bogus-boys-sign-to-interscope |url-status=live }}</ref> On January 23, 2013, it was announced that his record label Hits Since '87, is now an imprint of [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Horowitz |first=Steven J. |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22645/title.hit-boy-announces-signing-to-interscope-records |title=Hit-Boy Announces Signing To Interscope Records &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=January 23, 2013 |access-date=August 10, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141101211159/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22645/title.hit-boy-announces-signing-to-interscope-records |archive-date=November 1, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

On June 29, 2013, he announced that he is no longer signed to GOOD Music, but still on good terms with everybody in the label.<ref>{{cite web |last=MTV News Staff |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2501446/hit-boy-parts-ways-with-kanye-wests-g-o-o-d-music/ |title=HIT-BOY PARTS WAYS WITH KANYE WEST'S G.O.O.D. MUSIC |publisher=MTV |date=January 7, 2013 |access-date=July 26, 2016 |archive-date=August 9, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160809031825/http://www.mtv.com/news/2501446/hit-boy-parts-ways-with-kanye-wests-g-o-o-d-music/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

In 2016, Hit-Boy and Local Los Angeles rapper [[Dom Kennedy]] came together to work on a joint mixtape named ''Half-A-Mil'' EP, subsequently adopting the name (dropping the EP) as an alias for the duo. ''Half-A-Mil'' EP was released on December 11, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Stream Dom Kennedy And Hit-Boy's Joint EP, Half-A-Mil|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/12/11/dom-kennedy-hit-boy-half-a-mil-ep|access-date=April 12, 2021|website=The FADER|language=en|archive-date=April 12, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210412023920/https://www.thefader.com/2016/12/11/dom-kennedy-hit-boy-half-a-mil-ep|url-status=live}}</ref> The mixtape consisted of five songs, with "100 Rounds" being the lead single off the mixtape. Following the release of their first mixtape, the pair decided to put out another mixtape a few months after their previous mixtape. ''Half-A-Mil 2'' EP released on February 24, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Half-A-Mil 2 EP Mixtape by Dom Kennedy x Hit-Boy|url=https://www.datpiff.com/Dom-Kennedy-x-Hit-Boy-Half-A-Mil-2-EP-mixtape.831065.html|access-date=April 12, 2021|website=www.datpiff.com|archive-date=April 12, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210412023923/https://www.datpiff.com/Dom-Kennedy-x-Hit-Boy-Half-A-Mil-2-EP-mixtape.831065.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The mixtape contained six songs with one guest feature from B.Carr. The duo would take a break and would not release music under the [[Half-A-Mil]] name. After some time, the frequent collaborators released ''Half-A-Mil 3'' EP, continuing the mixtape series and being the last one in the saga. ''Half-A-Mil'' 3 EP was August 8, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=https://hiphopdx.com|first=HipHopDX-|date=August 7, 2017|title=Dom Kennedy & Hit-Boy Join Forces For "Half-A-Mil 3" EP|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.44299/title.dom-kennedy-hit-boy-join-forces-for-half-a-mil-3-ep|access-date=April 12, 2021|website=HipHopDX|archive-date=April 12, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210412023922/https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.44299/title.dom-kennedy-hit-boy-join-forces-for-half-a-mil-3-ep|url-status=live}}</ref> The mixtape only had two feature from [[24hrs (rapper)|24hrs]] on "Don't run" and [[Quentin Miller]] on the tracks "Might as Well" and "In The Hills". Also, the duo released a music video for both songs on September 7, 2017.<ref>{{Citation|title=Half-A-Mil - In The Hills/Might As Well|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RIt7EP5UmLA|language=en|access-date=April 12, 2021|archive-date=August 11, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210811140943/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RIt7EP5UmLA|url-status=live}}</ref> After releasing all three mixtapes, the pair finally released their first commercial album, [[Courtesy of Half-a-Mil]], on November 29, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=November 30|first1=Lindsey IndiaPublished|last2=2017|title=Stream Dom Kennedy and Hit-Boy's 'Courtesy of Half-A-Mil' Album - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/dom-kennedy-hit-boy-courtesy-of-half-a-mil-album/|access-date=April 12, 2021|website=XXL Mag|language=en|archive-date=April 12, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210412023922/https://www.xxlmag.com/dom-kennedy-hit-boy-courtesy-of-half-a-mil-album/|url-status=live}}</ref> The album contains 14 tracks and numerous features such as [[Larry June]], TYuS, B.Carr, Jay 305, and [[Ty Dolla Sign]]. The Tandem would go on to take a hiatus and not release any new music. Nearly two and half years later, the group would release their second studio. "Also Known As" was released on July 31, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 5, 2020|title=Hit-Boy & Dom Kennedy Return As Half-A-Mil For "Also Known As" » Hype Off Life|url=https://hypeofflife.com/hit-boy-dom-kennedy-also-known-as/|access-date=April 12, 2021|website=Hype Off Life|language=en-US|archive-date=April 12, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210412023926/https://hypeofflife.com/hit-boy-dom-kennedy-also-known-as/|url-status=live}}</ref> The album consists of 11 tracks and features from [[03 Greedo]], [[24hrs (rapper)|24hrs]], and [[Nas]].

===Production style===
Hit-Boy uses [[FL Studio]] and custom music plug-ins to make his beats.<ref name="Hiphopmakers">{{cite web | url=http://hiphopmakers.com/fl-studio-music-producers | title=9 Popular Music Producers Who Use FL Studio | publisher=Hiphopmakers | access-date=July 26, 2014 | archive-date=July 11, 2014 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140711203356/http://hiphopmakers.com/fl-studio-music-producers | url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Concrete">{{cite web | url=http://www.concretemag615.com/2014/02/25/fruity-loops-kids-toy-producer-choice/ | title=Fruity Loops- From kids toy to Producer Choice | publisher=Concrete | access-date=July 26, 2014 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140809040038/http://www.concretemag615.com/2014/02/25/fruity-loops-kids-toy-producer-choice/ | archive-date=August 9, 2014 | url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hit-Boy is the nephew of Rodney Benford, from the [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] group [[Troop (band)|Troop]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Frydenlund|first=Zach|title=Hit-Boy's 25 Favorite Albums|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/11/hit-boys-25-favorite-albums/troop-em-attitudeem-1989|work=article|publisher=complex.com|access-date=February 17, 2013|archive-date=January 7, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130107000828/http://www.complex.com/music/2012/11/hit-boys-25-favorite-albums/troop-em-attitudeem-1989|url-status=live}}</ref> He grew up in [[Fontana, California]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://for/%20Hit-Boy,%20Quality%20is%20Everything|title=For Hit-Boy, Quality is Everything|website=Complex}}</ref>
