'''Purple Mountains''' was an American indie rock project formed by musician and poet [[David Berman (musician)|David Berman]]. The project debuted in May 2019, over a decade after the dissolution of Berman's previous group [[Silver Jews]]. An [[Purple Mountains (album)|eponymous album]] was released in July 2019. The album was recorded primarily in [[Chicago]] and produced by [[Jarvis Taveniere]] and [[Jeremy Earl]] of the band [[Woods (band)|Woods]].<ref name="American Songwriter">{{Cite web |url=https://americansongwriter.com/2019/07/purple-mountains-any-way-you-hear-it/ |title=Purple Mountains: Any Way You Hear It |last=Rutledge |first=Chris |date=July 10, 2019 |website=[[American Songwriter]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190710154954/https://americansongwriter.com/2019/07/purple-mountains-any-way-you-hear-it/ |archive-date=July 10, 2019 |access-date=July 11, 2019}}</ref>

The project's name is a [[mondegreen]] of the lyric "Purple mountain majesties" from "[[America the Beautiful]]".<ref name="American Songwriter" /> ''[[Nashville Scene]]''<nowiki/>'s Sean L. Maloney described it as "striking, heavy and maybe a little foreboding, yet it implies a majesty that is completely missing from the often-self-deprecating Silver Jews".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Maloney|first=Sean L.|date=August 16, 2019|title=Exploring the Majesty of Purple Mountains|url=https://www.nashvillescene.com/music/features/exploring-the-majesty-of-purple-mountains/article_3546b263-6935-5c8c-8188-213ae441ca9b.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-19|website=[[Nashville Scene]]|language=en}}</ref> The project was presumably dissolved upon Berman's death on August 7, 2019.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/david-berman-silver-jews-purple-mountains-dead-at-52/|title=David Berman (Silver Jews, Purple Mountains) Dead at 52|website=Pitchfork.com|access-date=8 August 2019}}</ref>

==Background==
Berman returned to music in 2018, co-producing [[Yonatan Gat]]'s second studio album ''[[Universalists (album)|Universalists]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.popmatters.com/yonatan-gat-universalists-review-2570056228.html|title=Yonatan Gat Universalists Review on PopMatters|website=Popmatters.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://relix.com/articles/detail/global-beat-yonatan-gat/|title=Yonatan Gat Interview for Relix|website=Relix.com}}</ref> On December 12, 2018, former [[Pavement (band)|Pavement]] and [[Silver Jews]] member [[Bob Nastanovich]] revealed on his podcast ''Three Songs'' that David Berman would release new music in 2019 under the name Purple Mountains, which was also the name of Berman's blog.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2026033/silver-jews-david-berman-to-launch-new-band-in-2019-says-bob-nastanovich/news/|title=Silver Jews' David Berman To Launch New Band In 2019, Says Bob Nastanovich|website=Stereogum.com|date=December 12, 2018}}</ref> His blog was also titled Menthol Mountains at times.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://americansongwriter.com/2019/05/david-berman-of-silver-jews-returns-with-new-project-purple-mountains/|title=David Berman of Silver Jews Returns with New Project Purple Mountains « American Songwriter|date=May 10, 2019}}</ref>

On May 10, 2019, Berman released his first music in over a decade, a single entitled "All My Happiness Is Gone" on vinyl by [[Drag City (record label)|Drag City]], using the moniker Purple Mountains. The single included two remixes of the song, "All My Happiness Is Wrong" by [[Noah Landis|Noah Count]] and "All My Happiness Is Long" by Mark Nevers featuring clipped recordings of [[Dave Cloud]], and also indicated that a full-length album was a "couple months away." It also credited Berman, Jeremy Earl and Jarvis Taveniere of [[Woods (band)|Woods]], Aaron Neveu and songwriter Anna St. Louis.<ref name="happiness">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/silver-jews-david-berman-returns-with-first-new-music-in-11-years/|title=Silver Jews' David Berman Returns With First New Music in 11 Years|website=Pitchfork.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Purple-Mountains-All-My-Happiness-Is-Gone/release/13613935|title=Purple Mountains - All My Happiness Is Gone|website=Discogs.com}}</ref> The single was released digitally on May 17, 2019, with the announcement of a full-length album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2019/05/david-berman-purple-mountains-album-tour-dates/|title=David Berman announces new album as Purple Mountains, shares "All My Happiness is Gone": Stream|website=Consequenceofsound.net|date=May 17, 2019}}</ref> The [[Purple Mountains (album)|eponymous debut album]], accompanied by a US tour, was released on July 12, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/silver-jews-david-berman-to-release-new-album-in-2019-says-bob-nastanovich/|title=Silver Jews' David Berman to Release New Album in 2019 says Bob Nastanovich - Pitchfork|date=12 December 2018|website=Pitchfork.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Minsker |first1=Evan |title=Silver Jews' David Berman Announces First New Album in 11 Years, Shares Song: Listen |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/silver-jews-david-berman-announces-first-new-album-in-11-years-shares-song-listen/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=May 21, 2019 |date=May 17, 2019}}</ref> "Darkness and Cold" was released as the album's second single on June 11, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2019/06/david-berman-purple-mountains-darkness-and-cold/|title=Hear "Darkness and Cold," a New Song From David Berman's Purple Mountains|website=Spin.com|date=June 11, 2019}}</ref> "Margaritas at the Mall" was released as the album's third and final single on June 28, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2019/06/purple-mountains-margaritas-at-the-mall/|title=Purple Mountains – "Margaritas at the Mall"|website=Spin.com|date=June 28, 2019}}</ref>

A remix of "All My Happiness Is Gone" by Australian electronic music group [[The Avalanches]], who Berman has collaborated with in the past, was commissioned by him, but a licensing issue prevented their version from being released.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2050767/the-avalanches-remix-purple-mountains-single/news/|title=The Avalanches' Remix Of New Purple Mountains Single Held Up By Licensing Issue|website=Stereogum.com|date=July 10, 2019}}</ref>

Berman died by [[suicide]] on August 7, 2019.<ref name="auto"/>
