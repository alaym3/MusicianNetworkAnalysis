'''Integrity''' is a [[hardcore punk]] band originally from [[Cleveland, Ohio]] but based in Belgium since 2003.<ref name="mtv">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2699254/integritys-dwid-hellion-on-suicide-black-snake-humanity-and-charles-manson/|title=Integrity's Dwid Hellion on 'Suicide Black Snake,' Humanity and Charles Manson |website=mtv.com|access-date=December 16, 2020}}</ref><ref name="cleveland.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.cleveland.com/music/2012/10/hardcore_metal_band_integrity.html|title=Hardcore metal band Integrity returns to Cleveland for first gig since 2005|website=cleveland.com|date=October 31, 2012|access-date=December 16, 2020}}</ref> It was formed in 1988 by lead vocalist Dwid Hellion.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=August 27, 2018|first=Bruce|last=Hardt-Valenzuela|title=30 years of Integrity: an interview with Dwid Hellion on his vast career|url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/30-years-of-integrity-an-interview-with-dwid-hellion-on-his-vast-career/|access-date=December 30, 2020|website=BrooklynVegan}}</ref>

The band plays [[Punk rock|punk]], hardcore, and [[Heavy metal music|metal]]-related music, and has released nearly 50{{Quantify|date=December 2020}} recordings since 1988 as well as playing at hundreds{{Quantify|date=December 2020}} of shows including numerous global festival appearances. Their sound is a mix of [[Noise music|noise]], punk, [[blues]], hardcore and heavy metal, with influences including [[Industrial music|industrial]], [[Noise music|noise]] and [[Experimental music|experimental]] music. Lyrical themes include religion, the supernatural, art, philosophy, horror, as well as mental illness and the occult.

== Musical style and legacy ==
According to its bio on [[Relapse Records]] website, Integrity was formed in Cleveland in 1988 and is considered to be one of the first bands to mix [[Heavy metal music|metal]] and [[hardcore punk|hardcore]] in a way that would later become popular. 

Hellion has also had numerous side projects, including his electronic project, ''Psywarfare''<ref name="vice">{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/psywarfare-dwid-hellion-interview|title=Sound as a Weapon: Integrity's Dwid Hellion on Psywarfare's Legacy|website=Noisey|access-date=June 19, 2017}}</ref> and horror-themed, ''Vermapyre''. He has also worked with the band ''Enforced''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=June 4, 2019|title=Hear Integrity's Dwid Hellion Rage on Enforced's New Ripper "Skinned Alive"|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/hear-integritys-dwid-hellion-rage-enforceds-new-ripper-skinned-alive|access-date=December 30, 2020|website=Revolver|language=en}}</ref>

In July 2017, Hellion was featured on the cover of [[Decibel (magazine)|''Decibel'' magazine]], No.&nbsp;153. He is quoted as saying "I do not listen to much new music. When I need a new album to listen to, I make one."<ref name="decibelmagazine 1">{{Cite news|url=http://decibelmagazine.com/blog/2017/5/30/hardcore-legends-integrity-grace-the-cover-of-the-latest-decibel|title=Hardcore Legends Integrity Grace the Cover of the Latest Decibel|work=Decibel Magazine|access-date=June 21, 2017}}</ref> Previously, in 2013, Integrity's ''Systems Overload'' (1995, Victory Records) was featured in the ''Decibel'' "Hall of Fame". In feature, the album is referred to as "the landmark work in a grand and heretical oeuvre".<ref name="decibelmagazine 2">{{Cite news|url=http://decibelmagazine.com/hall-of-fame/2015/3/12/integrity-systems-overload|title=Integrity - "Systems Overload"|work=Decibel Magazine|access-date=June 21, 2017}}</ref>
