'''Simisola Bolatito Kosoko'''  ([[née]] '''Ogunleye'''; born April 19, 1988), better known by her stage name '''Simi''', is a Nigerian singer, songwriter and actress.<ref name="P.M News">{{cite web |title=Adekunle Gold pens romantic message to mark Simi's birthday |url=https://www.pmnewsnigeria.com/2019/04/19/adekunle-gold-pens-romantic-message-to-mark-simis-birthday/ |publisher=P.M News |access-date=19 April 2019 |date=April 19, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://nationaldailyng.com/2015/12531-2/|title=I started with gospel music – Simi|work=National Daily|last=Kingsley|first=Ediale|date=April 18, 2016|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> She started her career as a gospel singer, releasing her debut studio album in 2008, titled ''Ogaju''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/201512282415.html|title=Nigeria: Even the Silliest Things Inspire Me – Simi|work=[[Media Trust|Daily Trust]]|publisher=allAfrica|last=Offiong|first=Adie Vanessa|date=December 25, 2015|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> She gained prominence in 2014 after releasing "[[Tiff (song)|Tiff]]", a song that was nominated for Best Alternative Song at [[The Headies 2015]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://pulse.ng/buzz/headies-2015-check-out-the-complete-nomination-list-id4215323.html|title=Headies 2015: Check out the complete nomination list|work=Pulse Nigeria|last=Akan|first=Joey|date=September 30, 2015|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> Simi signed a record deal with X3M Music in 2014, but left the label in May 2019 following the expiration of her contract.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nigeria's Simi leaves record label |url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/nigerias-simi-leaves-record-label |publisher=Music in Africa |access-date=3 May 2019 |date=2 May 2019}}</ref> She released her second studio album ''[[Simisola (album)|Simisola]]'' on September 8, 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.pmnewsnigeria.com/2014/04/02/simi-sammy-join-x3m-music/|title=Simi, Sammy Join X3M Music|work=[[P.M. News]]|last=Arogundade|first=Funsho|date=April 2, 2014|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thenet.ng/2017/09/its-here-simi-releases-her-debut-album-titled-simisola/|title=It's here! Simi releases her debut album, 'Simisola'|date=September 8, 2017|website=thenet.ng|access-date=September 13, 2017}}</ref> Her third studio album ''[[Omo Charlie Champagne, Vol. 1]]'' was released to coincide with her thirty-first birthday on April 19, 2019.<ref name="P.M News"/> She launched her record label Studio Brat in June 2019.<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 1212">{{cite web |first1=Motolani |last1=Alake |title=Simi launches her record company, Studio Brat |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/simi-launches-her-record-company-studio-brat/d70pneq |publisher=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=30 June 2019 |date=June 20, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life ==
Simi was born on 19 April 1988 in [[Ojuelegba, Lagos|Ojuelegba]], a suburb of [[Surulere]], Lagos State, as the last of four children.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.punchng.com/i-fantasise-about-working-with-ebenezer-obey-simi/|title=I fantasise about working with Ebenezer Obey –Simi|work=[[The Punch]]|last=Olonilua|first=Ademola|date=April 2, 2016|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> In an interview with Juliet Ebirim of ''[[Vanguard (Nigeria)|Vanguard]]'' newspaper, Simi revealed that her parents separated when she was 9 years old. She also revealed that she grew up as a [[tomboy]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.vanguardngr.com/2016/03/why-i-do-love-songs-simi/|title=Why I do love songs – Simi|work=[[Vanguard (Nigeria)|Vanguard Newspaper]]|last=Ebirim|first=Juliet|date=March 17, 2016|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref>

== Education ==
Simi attended Stars International College, a secondary school in Ikorodu, Lagos State.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7fbczH4KCYQ</ref> She is an alumna of [[Covenant University]], where she studied [[mass communication]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thenationonlineng.net/simii-dont-feel-threatened-anybody/|title=SIMI:I DON'T FEEL THREATENED BY ANYBODY|work=[[The Nation (Nigeria)|The Nation Newspaper]]|last=Medeme|first=Ovwe|date=March 27, 2016|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
===2006–13: Career beginnings and ''Ogaju''===
Simi grew up dancing and singing as a member of her local church's choir. She wrote her first song at age 10.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.goldmyne.tv/2015/08/why-i-sang-ma-bi-mi-ni-jamb-question-song-afro-pop-singer-simi/|title=WHY I SANG ''MA BI MI NI JAMB QUESTION SONG''- AFRO POP SINGER, SIMI|work=Goldmyne Entertainment|last=Odigie|first=McDonald|date=August 12, 2015|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> Her professional music career started in 2008 following the release of her debut studio album ''Ogaju'', which consisted of a variety of songs such as "Iya Temi" and "Ara Ile". The album was produced entirely by Samklef.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://theyesng.com/jamb-question-crooner-simi-shares-her-story/|title=JAMB QUESTION CROONER, SIMI SHARES HER STORY|work=YES INTERNATIONAL! Magazine|last=Ikeokwu|first=Juliet|date=June 12, 2015|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref>

===2014–2018: ''Restless'', ''Chemistry'' and ''Simisola''===
In January 2014, Simi released the 5 track [[Extended play|EP]] ''Restless'', which eventually earned her a record deal with X3M Music.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.newsflash.com.ng/2016/04/19/simi-5-fun-facts-about-the-gifted-female-vocalist/|title=Simi: 5 fun facts about the gifted female vocalist|work=News Flash Nigeria|date=April 19, 2016|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> The EP is composed mostly of covers recorded by popular acts, including [[Rihanna]]'s "[[Man Down (song)|Man Down]]" and [[Adele]]'s "[[Set Fire to the Rain]]".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thenet.ng/2014/01/netpod-simi-drops-new-ep-restless-free-download/|title=NETPod: Simi drops new EP 'Restless'|work=[[Nigeria Entertainment Today]]|date=January 10, 2014|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref>

On January 9, 2014, Simi released two singles "Tiff" and "E No Go Funny". Both songs received frequent airplay and were met with generally positive reviews from critics. The video for "Tiff" was directed by Josh Clarke and released on August 30, 2015.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://tooxclusive.com/audio/video-simi-tiff/|title=VIDEO: Simi – Tiff|work=[[tooXclusive]]|date=August 30, 2015|access-date=April 22, 2016}}</ref> The song was nominated for Best Alternative Song at the [[The Headies 2015|2015 edition]] of [[The Headies]].

Later in 2015, Simi won the Most Promising Act to Watch category at the [[2015 Nigeria Entertainment Awards]]. In an interview with ''[[Leadership (newspaper)|Leadership]]'' newspaper, Simi revealed that she started working on her second studio album, which was slated for release in 2016.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://leadership.ng/entertainment/512746/relationship-falz-professional-simi|title=My Relationship With Falz Is Professional – Simi|work=[[Leadership (newspaper)|Leadership Newspaper]]|last=Abulude|first=Samuel|date=March 27, 2016|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> Upon releasing "Jamb Question", Simi was listed as one of the artistes to watch out for in 2016 by [[NotJustOk]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://notjustok.com/2016/02/22/10-artists-to-watch-in-2016/|title=10 Artists To Watch in 2016|work=[[NotJustOk]]|first=Don|last=Boye|date=February 22, 2016|access-date=April 21, 2016}}</ref> The remix of "Jamb Question" features Nigerian rapper [[Falz]].

On February 14, 2016, Simi released the Oscar Heman Ackah-produced love [[ballad]] "Love Don't Care". It received frequent radio airplay and was met with positive reviews.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://dailytimes.ng/love-meets-art-simis-love-dont-care-video/|title=LOVE MEETS ART IN SIMI'S 'LOVE DON'T CARE' VIDEO|work=The Daily Times|first=Mutiat|last=Alli|date=May 19, 2016|access-date=January 1, 2017}}</ref> "Love Don't Care" effectively addresses tribalism and discrimination in Nigeria.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vexscheworld.com/2016/02/love-dont-care-simi-the-vexscheworld-review/|title=LOVE DON'T CARE- Simi (The Vexscheworld Review)|website=vexscheworld.com|date=February 24, 2016 |access-date=January 1, 2017}}</ref> The song's music video was directed by [[Clarence Peters]]. In October 2016, Simi was nominated for Best Breakthrough Act at the [[MTV Africa Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://pulse.ng/buzz/mama-2016-tekno-wins-best-breakthrough-act-id5647812.html|title=MAMA 2016: Tekno wins Best Breakthrough Act|work=Pulse Nigeria|first=Abiola|last=Solanke|date=October 23, 2016|access-date=January 1, 2017}}</ref> On October 27, Simi collaborated with Falz to release the extended play ''[[Chemistry (Falz and Simi EP)|Chemistry]]''. Oghene Michael of 360 Nobs described the EP as an "experiment of the word art". In December 2016, Simi was nominated in three categories at [[The Headies 2016]], winning one.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://punchng.com/headies-2016-winners/|title=Headies 2016 Awards : The winners are…|work=The Punch|date=December 23, 2016|access-date=January 1, 2017}}</ref>

In anticipation of her 12-track second studio album ''[[Simisola (album)|Simisola]]'', Simi released two singles; "Smile for Me" and "Joromi". Both songs were accompanied by music videos directed by [[Clarence Peters]] and Aje Films, respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.channelstv.com/2017/09/09/fans-go-emotional-simi-launches-album-simisola/|title=Fans Go Emotional As Simi Launches Album 'Simisola' • Channels Television|date=September 9, 2017|website=channelstv.com|access-date=September 13, 2017}}</ref> Music videos for "O Wa Nbe", "Complete Me", "Gone for Good" and "Aimasiko" were also released to further promote the album. Simi unveiled the album's track listing on September 1, 2017. ''Simisola'' was released seven days later and debuted at number 5 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' World Albums chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/world-albums/2017-09-30|title=World Music: Top World Albums Chart|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://guardian.ng/life/whatsnew/simi-unveils-official-tracklist-for-new-album/|title=Simi Unveils Official Tracklist For New Album|website=guardian.ng|date=September 2017|access-date=September 13, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/simi-bets-and-wins-simisola|title=Simi bets and wins on Simisola|date=September 12, 2017|website=musicinafrica.net|access-date=September 13, 2017}}</ref>

===2019: ''Omo Charlie Champagne, Vol. 1'' and Studio Brat===
In March 2019, Simi disclosed that she would be releasing her third studio album ''[[Omo Charlie Champagne, Vol. 1]]'' to coincide with her birthday on April 19, 2019. She disclosed this information in a series of social media messages.<ref>{{cite web |title=Simi Announces Title, Date For New Album |url=https://guardian.ng/life/music/simi-announces-title-date-for-new-album/ |publisher=Guardian Life |access-date=19 April 2019 |date=23 March 2019}}</ref> The album is a slight departure from the relatively afro-centric feel of ''[[Simisola (album)|Simisola]]'' (2017).<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 1" /> It is a mixture of [[sentimental ballad]], [[African popular music|Afropop]], Afro-fusion, [[Afro-soul]], [[R&B]], [[Electronic dance music|EDM]] and [[moombahton]].<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 1" /> The 13-track album features [[guest vocals]] from [[Patoranking]], [[Maleek Berry]], [[Falz]], and her husband [[Adekunle Gold]]. Its production was primarily handled by Oscar, with additional production from Vtek, [[Legendury Beatz]], and Sess.<ref>{{cite web |first1=Kam |last1=Tambini |title=Listen to Simi's New Album 'Omo Charlie Champagne Vol.1' |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/simi-omo-charlie-champagne-album-listen-download/ |publisher=OkayAfrica |access-date=19 April 2019 |date=April 19, 2019}}</ref> Simi dedicated the album to her father Charles Oladele Ogunleye, who died in 2014.<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 1">{{cite web |first1=Motolani |last1=Alake |title=Simi releases 13-track third studio album, 'Omo Charlie Champagne Vol. I' |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/new-album-simi-releases-brand-new-13-track-album-omo-charlie-champagne-vol-i/wgfeyn5 |publisher=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=19 April 2019 |date=April 19, 2019}}</ref> ''Omo Charlie Champagne, Vol. 1'' was preceded by three singles: "I Dun Care", "Lovin" and "Ayo".

In May 2019, X3M Music announced Simi's departure following the expiration of her recording contract. Both parties did not renew the contract and agreed to part ways.<ref>{{cite web |first1=Bayo |last1=Wahab |title=Simi leaves X3M Music as record contract expires |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/simi-leaves-x3m-music-as-record-contract-expires/pee842r |publisher=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=30 June 2019 |date=May 1, 2019}}</ref> In June 2019, Simi announced the launch of her independent record label, Studio Brat.<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 1212"/>

She released a song tittled '[[Duduke]]' in the year 2020 made her unborn baby, a symbol of love and hope.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-05-10|title=Simi's 'Duduke' makes her unborn baby a symbol of love and hope|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2020/05/simis-duduke-makes-her-unborn-baby-a-symbol-of-love-and-hope/|access-date=2021-02-27|website=Vanguard News|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== 2020: Restless II EP ===
In 2020, Simi released another EP titled Restless II which is part two of the initial Restless EP released in 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-05|title=Simi excellently documents the emotional travails of a 'Restless II,' emotionally entwined by 'friends with benefits' [EP Review]|url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/simi-restless-ii-ep-review-adekunle-gold-and-wurld-feature/1dss42s|access-date=2021-05-28|website=Pulse Nigeria|language=en}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Apart from being a singer and songwriter, Simi is also a [[sound engineer]]. She is credited with mixing and mastering [[Adekunle Gold]]'s debut studio album ''[[Gold (Adekunle Gold album)|Gold]]'', which was released in July 2016.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thenet.ng/2016/07/adekunle-gold-has-his-boo-simi-as-the-only-featured-act-on-his-gold-album/|title=Adekunle Gold has his 'boo' SIMI as the only featured act on his GOLD album|work=Nigerian Entertainment Today|last=Showemimo|first=Adedayo|date=July 19, 2016|access-date=July 28, 2016}}</ref> Her music style is basically hinged in the rhythm and blues, [[Soul music|soul]] and [[hip hop music]] genres.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://punchng.com/quit-gospel-music-simi/|title=Why I quit gospel music – Simi|work=[[The Punch]]|last=Yinus|first=Qismat|date=July 17, 2016|access-date=July 17, 2016}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Simi married a singer named [[Adekunle Gold]] in a private wedding ceremony on January 9, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.legit.ng/1214599-simi-adekunle-golds-wedding-was.html|title=Simi and Adekunle Gold's wedding: how it was|date=January 10, 2019}}</ref> It was later revealed that they had been dating for five years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dailypost.ng/2019/01/17/dated-5-years-adekunle-gold-confirms-getting-married-simi/|title=We dated for 5 years – Adekunle Gold confirms getting married to Simi|date=January 17, 2019}}</ref> Simi became a mother for the first time on the 30th of May 2020 when she gave birth to her daughter Adejare.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Emmanuel |first1=Stanley |title=Simi And Husband, Adekunle Gold Welcomes Baby Girl |url=https://candytv.ng/simi-and-husband-adekunle-gold-welcomes-baby-girl/ |website=Candy Tv |access-date=8 June 2020 |date=June 7, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Simi Welcomes A Baby Girl With Her Husband, Adekunle Gold|url=https://sellbeta.com/blog/simi-welcomes-a-baby-girl-with-her-husband-adekunle-gold/|access-date=June 8, 2020|website=Sellbeta Blog|date=June 8, 2020}}</ref>
