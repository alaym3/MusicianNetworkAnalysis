'''Michael Stafford''' (born 12 July 1990),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/b0311291-19f9-4288-96be-e8a5da0a104a |title=Music – Maverick Sabre |publisher=BBC |access-date=1 January 2012}}</ref> better known as '''Maverick Sabre''', is an English-born Irish singer, songwriter, and rapper.<ref name="Clash"/>

==Early life==
Sabre was born in [[London Borough of Hackney|Hackney, London]].<ref name="Clash"/> At age four, he moved with his family to [[New Ross|New Ross, County Wexford]], where he grew up. Following advice from English musician [[Plan B (musician)|Plan B]], he moved back to London to pursue a musical career.<ref name="Clash">{{citation|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/feature/ones-to-watch-2011-maverick-sabre/ |title=Ones to watch: 2011 – Maverick Sabre |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160316153608/http://www.clashmusic.com/feature/ones-to-watch-2011-maverick-sabre |archive-date=16 March 2016 |access-date=2016-12-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.bluesandsoul.com/feature/672/travelling_at_the_speed_of_light/ |title=Maverick Sabre: Travelling at the Speed of Sound |author= Pete Lewis |magazine=Blues & Soul|date= May 2011|access-date=20 August 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
===2008–2010: Early career and breakthrough===
Sabre's career began in 2008 when he appeared on the tracks "Lucky" and "Used to Blame" from the album ''Sense the Terror'' by Irish rappers Terawrizt and Nu-Centz. He also featured on rappers Johnnyboy and Intox's ''Propaganda'' EP and released his own tracks, such as "Lonely Side of Life" and "Fade Away". Sabre furthered his career in 2009 when he featured on Jermicide & Danny Digg's album ''Middle Class'', collaborating on three tracks: "Afraid", "Where We Go to", and "Still With Me".<ref>[https://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/B003K6BKQY Jermiside & Danny Diggs' 'Middle Class'] [[Amazon.com]], Retrieved 31 January 2011.</ref> Maverick Sabre also performed on SB.TV with the song "They Found Him a Gun"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F56-qvgvoJE&ob=av3n |title=Maverick Sabre – "They Found Him A Gun" – A64 |publisher=YouTube |date=1 November 2009 |access-date=20 August 2012}}</ref> and featured in BBC 1Xtra 'Jail Tales' programme, performing "These Walls".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L6nGmJUYeyU |title=Jail Tales – Maverick Sabre: These Walls – BBC Three |publisher=YouTube |date=15 February 2012 |access-date=20 August 2012}}</ref> Sabre then returned to working with his earlier cohorts, featuring on Terawrizt's album ''Written in Stone'' and Terawrizt & Nu-Centz' album ''Sense The Terror 2'', appearing on tracks such as "Life Fly By". On 25 November 2010, Sabre released his debut mixtape, ''The Travelling Man'', in the United Kingdom. The release was a free [[music download|digital download]] and featured the track "Sometimes" along with a promotional video to accompany the song.

On 6 January 2011, "Sometimes" was released as a single by Impala Records. Sabre guested on [[drum and bass]] duo [[Chase & Status]]'s second studio album ''[[No More Idols]]'', on "Fire in Your Eyes".  He also features on "Sleepless", a song regularly performed live by Chase & Status, however no studio version has been released to date. Sabre featured on [[Professor Green]]'s fourth single "[[Jungle (Professor Green song)|Jungle]]", released on 1 January 2010, which was featured on the album ''[[Alive Till I'm Dead]]''.<ref>[https://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/B004GR467G Professor Green featuring Maverick Sabre 'Jungle'] Amazon.com, Retrieved 16 January 2011.</ref> The single debuted at number 34 on the UK Singles Chart, peaking at 31 the following week.<ref name="Jungle">[https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/20110116/7501/ UK Singles Chart – Week Ending: 22 January 2011]  [[The Official Charts Company]], Retrieved 6 February 2011.</ref> On 28 February 2011, Maverick released his debut single "Look What I Done", taken from the [[extended play]] ''The Lost Words'', released on 7 March 2011.<ref name="TheLostWords">[http://mavericksabre.com/2011/02/03/live-from-maida-vale-with-radio-1/ Maverick Sabre performs at Maida Vale Studios] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110313041704/http://mavericksabre.com/2011/02/03/live-from-maida-vale-with-radio-1/ |date=13 March 2011 }} mavericksabre.com, Retrieved 3 February 2011.</ref> On 19 April 2011, Sabre performed with a live band on [[BBC Two]]'s ''[[Later… with Jools Holland]]''.<ref name="Later w/Jools">{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b010p0cn |title=Two Programmes – Later Live... with Jools Holland, Series 38, Episode 3 |publisher=BBC |date=19 April 2011 |access-date=1 January 2012}}</ref> After his performance of [[Oasis (band)|Oasis]]'s "[[Wonderwall (song)|Wonderwall]]" on [[Chris Moyles]]' [[BBC Radio 1]] Comic Relief show, Maverick's extended play ''The Lost Words'' reached number 2 in the iTunes Store Top Ten Songs chart. In 2011, he also appeared on [[The Script]]'s Science & Faith Tour supporting the Irish band, and performed his song "[[Let Me Go (Maverick Sabre song)|Let Me Go]]", which entered the UK Singles Chart at number 16 in August 2011.

===2011–2013: ''Lonely Are the Brave''===
On 25 July 2011, Sabre's first single "[[Let Me Go (Maverick Sabre song)|Let Me Go]]" was released and entered the UK chart at number 16. He released his debut album, ''[[Lonely Are the Brave (Maverick Sabre album)|Lonely Are The Brave]]'', on 6 February 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hmv.com/hmvweb/displayProductDetails.do?sku=8881 |title=Maverick Sabre: Lonely Are The Brave (2012): CD |publisher=hmv.com |access-date=1 January 2012}}</ref> He then released singles "[[I Need (Maverick Sabre song)|I Need]]", "What Have I Done To You" and "[[No One (Maverick Sabre song)|No One]]". Sabre featured on a remix of his song "I Need" with rappers [[Chipmunk (rapper)|Chipmunk]] and Benny Banks. He then featured on the track "We'll Never Know" alongside rappers Akala and Logic. On 29 January 2012, Sabre appeared on the Sunday Night Sessions segment of ''[[The Late Show with Joanne Good]]'' on [[BBC London 94.9]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00n6vwq |title=Maverick Sabre on Jo Good's Sunday Night Sessions |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date=29 January 2012 |access-date=20 August 2012}}</ref> Sabre appeared on the 2012 remake album ''[[Jeff Wayne's Musical Version of The War of the Worlds - The New Generation]]'' in the role of Parson Nathaniel. The role was performed by [[Jason Donovan]] in the 2012–2014 live shows. Around this time, Sabre started working with a teenage [[Jorja Smith]] and she became his protégé, who he ultimately introduced to the UK.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=htSq5_qkGMo |title=Maverick Sabre: I've been working with Jorja Smith since she was 15.}}</ref>

===2014–2017: ''Innerstanding''===
On 9 April 2014, Sabre released a video for the single "Breathe".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a563778/maverick-sabre-unveils-new-song-breathe-listen.html#~oB8VOEMeK6KXXr|title=Maverick Sabre unveils new song 'Breathe' – listen :  |publisher=Lewis Corner |access-date=10 April 2014}}</ref>
On 24 July 2014, Sabre released a video for the remix from "Emotion (Ain't Nobody)", featuring Chip, Devlin and George the Poet.
The album was released on 31 October 2015. Sabre was also featured on the track "Carry Me Home" from Jorja Smith's album Project 11, released on 17 November 2016.<ref>{{Citation|last=BBC Radio 1|title=Jorja Smith - Carry Me Home (feat. Maverick Sabre) - Radio 1's Piano Sessions|date=2016-11-23|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sGVojLwda_A|access-date=2018-02-21}}</ref>

===2018–present: ''When I Wake Up''===
On 22 March 2019, Sabre released his third studio album titled ''[[When I Wake Up]]'' on his own [[imprint label|imprint]] FAMM. The record was promoted by the singles "Drifting", "Her Grace" (featuring Chronixx), "Slow Down"(featuring Jorja Smith) and "Glory".
On 26 March 2020, "Glory" was featured on American medical drama ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]'' episode "[[Grey's Anatomy (season 16)|Love of My Life]]".<ref>{{Citation|last=TuneFind|title=Season 16E19 Love of My Life|date=2020-03-26|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/greys-anatomy/season-16/96778}}</ref>

In June 2020, Sabre announced a new EP titled ''You Know How It Feels'', scheduled to be released on 24 July 2020 via FAMM.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/maverick-sabre-announces-new-ep-with-lead-track-signs |title=Maverick Sabre announces new EP with lead track "Signs" |first=Cerys |last=Kenneally |work=[[The Line of Best Fit]] |date=26 June 2020 |access-date=1 July 2020}}</ref> The EP is promoted by lead single "Signs" released on 26 June.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/signs-single/1516987900 |title=Signs - Single by Maverick Sabre on Apple Music |publisher=[[Apple Music]] |access-date=1 July 2020}}</ref>

==Political views==
In November 2019, Sabre endorsed the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] in the [[2019 United Kingdom general election|2019 UK general election]].<ref name="dazeddigital">{{cite news |last=Dawson|first=Brit|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/politics/article/46937/1/jeremy-corbyn-lily-allen-mia-labour-arts-for-all-policy-uk-general-election|title=Jeremy Corbyn, Lily Allen, and M.I.A. launch Labour’s Arts for All policy|work=[[Dazed]]|date=25 November 2019|access-date=27 November 2019}}</ref>
