'''Lovelytheband''' (stylized in all lowercase) is an American [[indie pop]] band that formed in 2016 in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]], United States. The band consists of vocalist Mitchy Collins, guitarist Jordan Greenwald, and drummer Sam Price. The band is most known for their single "[[Broken (Lovelytheband song)|Broken]]" which charted on several US ''Billboard'' charts.

The band debuted in April 2017 with their single "Broken" which quickly earned over three million streams on [[Spotify]]. The single charted on several ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts. It topped the ''Billboard'' [[Alternative Songs]] chart and ''Billboard'' Rock Airplay chart for several weeks. The single also became the band's first top 10 on the ''Billboard'' Radio Songs chart and first top 40 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.

In September 2017, the band released their first EP, ''Everything I Could Never Say...''. They released the single "[[These Are My Friends]]" in May 2018. On August 3, 2018, they released their debut album ''[[Finding It Hard to Smile]]''.

==Career==
===2016–2018: "Broken" and ''Everything I Could Never Say...''===
Mitchy Collins, Jordan Greenwald, and Sam Price met and formed the band while at a nightclub in [[West Hollywood]].<ref>{{cite web |first=Mitch |last=Mosk |date=April 20, 2017 |url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/broken-lovelytheband-review/ |title=True Connection and Perfect Imperfection in lovelytheband's Debut "Broken" |website=Atwood Magazine |accessdate=January 8, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180109180910/http://atwoodmagazine.com/broken-lovelytheband-review/ |archivedate=January 9, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Apple">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/lovelytheband/1220215284 |title=Apple Music Preview |website=Apple Music |accessdate=January 8, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180109181104/https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/lovelytheband/1220215284 |archivedate=January 9, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=October 5, 2017 |url=https://www.bmi.com/news/entry/indie-spotlight-lovelytheband |title=Indie Spotlight: lovelytheband |website=BMI |accessdate=January 8, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180109121952/https://www.bmi.com/news/entry/indie-spotlight-lovelytheband |archivedate=January 9, 2018}}</ref> The band then released their debut single, "[[Broken (Lovelytheband song)|Broken]]", in April 2017.<ref>{{cite magazine |first=Rania |last=Aniftos |date=April 13, 2017 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7759741/lovelytheband-debut-single-broken-premiere |title=lovely.the.band Finds Love That Heals in Debut Single 'Broken': Exclusive Premiere |magazine=Billboard |accessdate=January 8, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170929121457/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7759741/lovelytheband-debut-single-broken-premiere |archivedate=September 29, 2017}}</ref>

"Broken" charted on several ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts. It topped the ''Billboard'' [[Alternative Songs]] chart, dated April 21, 2018, and stayed on top for seven consecutive weeks.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8347407/lovelytheband-alternative-songs-chart-number-one|title=Lovelytheband Leaps to No. 1 on Alternative Songs Chart With 'Broken'|last=Rutherford|first=Kevin|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=August 8, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180808011346/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8347407/lovelytheband-alternative-songs-chart-number-one|archive-date=August 8, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> It topped the ''Billboard'' Rock Airplay chart, dated June 9, 2018, and remained on top for nine consecutive weeks.<ref name=airplay>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8459907/lovelytheband-broken-rock-airplay-chart|title=Lovelytheband's 'Broken' Rules Rock Airplay Chart|last=Rutherford|first=Kevin|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=August 8, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180807220419/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8459907/lovelytheband-broken-rock-airplay-chart|archive-date=August 7, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> It peaked at number 10 on the ''Billboard'' Radio Songs chart (airplay chart across all genres), number 14 on the [[Mainstream Top 40]] chart (airplay chart for pop songs),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/radio-songs|title=Radio Songs|work=Billboard|accessdate=October 5, 2018}}</ref> and number 29 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="Hot 100 Nov 10"/> The single became the band's first top 10 on the ''Billboard'' Radio Songs chart and first top 40 on the Hot 100. It was also one of the four songs in 2018 that managed to chart in both the Alternative Songs chart and the Top 10 of the Radio Songs chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8482931/lovelytheband-broken-climbs-hot-100-chart-moves|title=Lovelytheband's 'Broken' Continues Alternative's Steady Radio Success and More Hot 100 Chart Moves|work=Billboard|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=November 2, 2018|accessdate=November 3, 2018}}</ref>

After signing with [[Another Century Records]], they released their debut EP, ''Everything I Could Never Say...'', in September 2017.<ref name="Apple" /><ref name="Genius">{{cite web |date=September 22, 2017 |url=https://genius.com/albums/Lovelytheband/Everything-i-could-never-say |title=Album ''everything i could never say..'' |website=Genius |accessdate=January 8, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180109181149/https://genius.com/albums/Lovelytheband/Everything-i-could-never-say |archivedate=January 9, 2018}}</ref> The band then toured with The Wrecks and La Bouquet.{{citation needed|date=March 2019}}

===2018–present: ''Finding It Hard to Smile'' and ''Conversations with Myself About You''===
On May 11, 2018, the band released the single "[[These Are My Friends]]" from their upcoming debut album. The album, ''[[Finding It Hard to Smile]]'', was then released on August 3, 2018.<ref name="AppleAlbum">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/finding-it-hard-to-smile/1396231359|title="Finding It Hard to Smile" by lovelytheband|website=Apple Music|accessdate=July 24, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180724062900/https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/finding-it-hard-to-smile/1396231359|archive-date=July 24, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=abcnradio>{{cite news|title=Lovelytheband Announces Debut Album 'Finding It Hard to Smile'  |url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2018/6/29/lovelytheband-announces-debut-album-finding-it-hard-to-smile.html |work=[[ABC News Radio]] |date=June 29, 2018 |accessdate=November 27, 2018 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20181227213731/http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2018/6/29/lovelytheband-announces-debut-album-finding-it-hard-to-smile.html |archivedate=December 27, 2018 |url-status=live}}</ref>

The band went on their first headlining tour, the Broken Like Me Tour, which started on August 15, 2018. The tour visited Canada and the United States. They also toured as the opening act for the European leg of [[5 Seconds of Summer]]'s [[Meet You There Tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8472066/lovelytheband-interview-broken-breakthrough-alt-hit|title=How Three Strangers Became Lovelytheband, With 2018's Breakthrough Alt Hit|work=Billboard|accessdate=March 15, 2019}}</ref> The band then embarked on their second headlining tour, Finding It Hard to Smile Tour, to support their debut album. The tour visited cities across the US and Canada, beginning in March 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2019-01-16-lovelytheband-announce-finding-it-hard-to-smile-tour-dates/|title=lovelytheband Announce 'Finding It Hard to Smile' Tour Dates|publisher=iHeartRadio|accessdate=March 15, 2019}}</ref>

On February 24, 2020, it was announced that Lovelytheband would be opening acts for Australian band, [[5 Seconds of Summer]] on the Latin America arena concert dates on their [[No Shame Tour (5 Seconds of Summer)|No Shame Tour]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=5 Seconds of Summer Announce North American No Shame Tour|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/live/8549774/5-seconds-summer-north-american-no-shame-tour-dates|date=2020-01-31|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=5 Seconds of Summer Add 2020 Tour Dates: Ticket Presale & On-Sale Info {{!}} Zumic {{!}} Music News, Tour Dates, Ticket Presale Info, and More|url=https://zumic.com/5-seconds-of-summer-add-2020-tour-dates-ticket-presale-and-on-sale-info|date=2020-01-31|website=Zumic|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref> Lovelytheband is set to be opening acts on three dates, starting on May 19, 2021 at the [[Palacio de los Deportes|Palacio de los Deportes Arena]] in [[Mexico City]], Mexico.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Live|url=https://www.5sos.com/tour|website=5 Seconds of Summer|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref>

On July 2, 2020, the band announced their second album, ''Conversations with Myself About You'', which was released on August 28, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-02|title=lovelytheband Announces Sophomore LP, Drops "idwgtyp"|url=https://www.idolator.com/7912219/lovelytheband-announces-sophomore-lp-drops-idwgtyp|access-date=2020-07-03|work=idolator}}</ref> It includes the singles "Loneliness for Love", "Waste", "I Should Be Happy", "idwgtyp", and "Buzz Cut".

On October 30, 2020 the band released the single "heartbreak of america" which highlighted problems in America such as gun violence, the 2020 presidential election, drug abuse, etc. The song was co-written by [[Foster the People]] frontman [[Mark Foster (singer)|Mark Foster]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-31|title=lovelytheband Shares Important Message With 'Heartbreak of America'|url=https://thehoneypop.com/2020/10/30/lovelytheband-shares-important-message-with-heartbreak-of-america/|access-date=2020-11-04|website=The Honey POP}}</ref>
