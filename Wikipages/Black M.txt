'''Alpha Diallo''',<ref>{{Cite web|last=sexion-dassaut-rap|date=22 July 2011|title=interview black m|url=https://sexion-dassaut-rap.skyrock.com/3018202137-interview-black-m.html|access-date=16 August 2021|website=Skyrock|language=fr}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Black Mesrimes''' or '''Black M''' (born 27 December 1984), is a French rapper and singer-songwriter and member of the [[Sexion d'Assaut]] group.

In 2014, he released his first solo album, entitled [[Les yeux plus gros que le monde|''Les yeux plus gros que le monde'']], which shortly after was certified diamond. It was due to this album that he was launched into the French scene. 

== Early life ==
Alpha Diallo's parents are of [[Guinea|Guinean]] origin, precisely from the [[Donghol-Sigon]] region in [[Fouta Djallon|Fouta Djalon]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bah|first=Aïs|title=Black M à Doghel Sigon, sur la terre de ses ancêtres !|url=https://www.gnakrylive.com/guinee/people/2692-black-m-a-doghel-sigon-sur-la-terre-de-ses-ancetres|access-date=16 August 2021|website=www.gnakrylive.com|language=fr-fr}}</ref> He grew up in the [[13th arrondissement of Paris]], a district in which he still lives today, coming from a modest family.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sur la route de Black M: un itinéraire tumultueux vers le succès|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/musique/2016/05/11/03006-20160511ARTFIG00242-sur-la-route-de-black-m-un-itineraire-tumultueux-vers-le-succes.php|access-date=16 August 2021|website=LEFIGARO|language=fr}}</ref>

==Career==

=== Sexion d'Assaut (2002–2013) ===
[[File:La Sexion D'Assaut.jpg|thumb|242x242px|Black M (far left) With La [[Sexion d'Assaut]] in 2012.]]
The pseudonym Black Mesrimes is a play on words, a contraction between the name of the former public enemy number one, [[Jacques Mesrine]], and the words “rhymes” and “black” evoking his skin color. Since 2014, he is mainly referred to as Black M. During his appearances in [[Sexion d'Assaut]] music videos, he uses certain facial expressions, such as making big eyes, an expression he made on the cover of his first album. He is considered one of the more technical members of Sexion d'Assaut, along with [[Gims]] and [[Lefa (rapper)|Lefa]].

With the group [[Sexion d'Assaut]], Black M released two studio albums: ''[[L'École des points vitaux]]'' (2010)<ref>{{Cite web|title=lescharts.com - Les charts français - L'École des points vitau|url=https://lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Sexion+d%27Assaut&titel=L%27%E9cole+des+points+vitaux&cat=a|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-19|website=lescharts.com}}</ref> and [[L'Apogée|''L'Apogée'']] (2012),<ref>{{Cite web|title=lescharts.com - Les charts français - L'Apogée|url=https://lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Sexion+d%27Assaut&titel=L%27apog%E9e&cat=a|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-19|website=lescharts.com}}</ref> two mixtapes: ''[[Les Chroniques du 75]]'' (2009) and ''[[En attendant L'Apogée: les Chroniques du 75]]'' (2011),<ref>{{Cite web|title=lescharts.com - Les charts français - En attendant L'Apogée: les Chroniques du 75|url=https://lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Sexion+d%27Assaut&titel=En+attendant+l%27apog%E9e+%2D+Les+chroniques+du+75&cat=a|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-19|website=lescharts.com}}</ref> two street albums: ''Le Renouveau'' (2008) and ''[[L'Écrasement de tête]]'' (2009),<ref>{{Cite web|title=lescharts.com - Les charts français - L'Écrasement de tête|url=https://lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Sexion+d%27Assaut&titel=L%27%E9crasement+de+t%EAte&cat=a|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-19|website=lescharts.com}}</ref> a mixtape: ''La Terre Du Milieu'' (2006), a reissue (2012), a live album: ''[[L'Apogée#Track list: L'Apog%C3%A9e %C3%A0 Bercy|L'Apogée à Bercy]]'' (2012),<ref>{{Cite web|title=lescharts.com - Sexion d'Assaut - Concert Bercy Live|url=https://lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Sexion+d%27Assaut&titel=Concert+Bercy+Live&cat=a|access-date=2021-08-19|website=lescharts.com}}</ref> three DVDs and a compilation: ''Best Of'' (2013).<ref>{{Cite web|title=lescharts.com - === ''Les yeux plus gros que le monde'' (2013–2015) ===
On 31 March 2014, Black M released his first solo album entitled ''[[Les yeux plus gros que le monde]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|title="Les yeux plus gros que le monde" de Black M est l'album du week-end|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Black-M/news-92419.html|access-date=16 August 2021|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> relatively well received by the specialized press,<ref>{{Cite web|date=25 April 2014|title=[Chronique] Black M : Les yeux plus gros que le monde|url=http://lerapenfrance.fr/chronique-black-m-les-yeux-plus-gros-que-le-monde/|access-date=16 August 2021|website=LREF|language=fr-FR}}</ref> sold 530,000 copies in December 2014 and certified diamond disc.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Forum Ados|url=https://www.public.fr/Forum-Ados|access-date=16 August 2021|website=Public.fr|language=fr}}</ref> In May 2014, his single "[[Sur ma route]]" ranked number 1 in sales in [[France]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kopfhörer Test und Vergleich 2021 auf kopfhoerer.com|url=https://www.kopfhoerer.com/|access-date=16 August 2021|website=Kopfhoerer.com|language=de}}</ref> and was certified platinum.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Booska-p|title=Black M certifié disque de platine !|url=https://www.booska-p.com/new-black-m-certifie-disque-de-platine-n31811.html|access-date=16 August 2021|website=www.booska-p.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Black M : les ventes de son premier album solo|url=https://musique.jeuxactu.com/news-les-ventes-de-les-yeux-plus-gros-que-le-monde-16568.htm|access-date=16 August 2021|website=MusiqueMag|language=fr}}</ref>

On 8 September he released the music video for another single with [[L'Institut|Dr Beriz]], "La légende Black". Black M announces the re-release of its first album under the name ''Le Monde plus gros que mes yeux'' (initially scheduled for release on 17 November 2014), the first single of which is "Je garde le sourire".<ref>[http://www.metronews.fr/culture/video-sexion-d-assaut-black-m-se-lance-en-solo-avec-le-clip-de-ailleurs/mmie!ATz1wwvL5T2A/ Paris ''Metro'': Black M se lance en solo avec le clip de "Ailleurs"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130908091920/http://www.metronews.fr/culture/video-sexion-d-assaut-black-m-se-lance-en-solo-avec-le-clip-de-ailleurs/mmie%21ATz1wwvL5T2A/|date=8 September 2013}} {{in lang|fr}}</ref> At the same time, Black M is investing in other projects. He thus participates in the soundtrack of the film ''[[The New Adventures of Aladdin]]'' featuring [[Kev Adams]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=AlloCine|title=Kev Adams et Black M en duo pour Les Nouvelles Aventures d’Aladin|url=https://www.allocine.fr/article/fichearticle_gen_carticle=18641941.html|access-date=16 August 2021|website=AlloCiné|language=fr}}</ref> or on the compilation Touche pas à ma zik by Tefa and [[Cyril Hanouna]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Deezer Premium|url=https://m.boutique.orange.fr/options/orange-deezer-premium|access-date=16 August 2021|website=m.boutique.orange.fr}}</ref>

=== ''Éternel Insatisfait'' (2015–2018) ===
At the end of 2014, Black M announced the preparation of his second solo album, titled ''[[Éternel insatisfait]]''. In 2016, he joined the troupe [[Les Enfoirés]]. On 28 October 2016, the album was released, followed by a tour, the “Éternel Big Black Tour”, which took place between April and December 2017. On 29 September 2017, he released the title "Death Note" and, a week more late, "Dress Code". These two extracts are found in the reissue of ''Éternel insatisfait'', released on 17 November 2017.

In November 2017, he announced that he was embarking on cinema with an autobiography which should begin filming at the beginning of 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black M se lance dans le cinéma avec un film autobiographique|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/cinema/2017/11/21/03002-20171121ARTFIG00166-black-m-se-lance-dans-le-cinema-avec-un-film-autobiographique.php|access-date=16 August 2021|website=LEFIGARO|language=fr}}</ref> On 18 April 2018, he did a private concert for one of his biggest fans of his initials "C.L", it was the best birthday present this old lady could have had!

=== ''Il était une fois...'' (2019–2020) ===
In September 2018, Black M announced on social networks that he was preparing his third album, which would be released in September 2019 and accompanied by a tour.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Booska-p|title=Black M : date, cover et tracklist de son prochain album !|url=https://www.booska-p.com/new-black-m-date-cover-et-tracklist-de-son-prochain-album-n110780.html|access-date=16 August 2021|website=www.booska-p.com|language=en}}</ref> He released the first single from this future album, titled Bon (Prologue), on 3 June 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black M prépare son troisième album avec Vianney|url=https://www.bfmtv.com/people/musique/black-m-prepare-son-troisieme-album-avec-vianney_AN-201810230098.html|access-date=16 August 2021|website=BFMTV|language=fr}}</ref> On 13 September 2019, three years after the release of his previous album, Black M released his third studio album, ''[[Il était une fois...]]''.

During its first week of operation, the disc sold only 5,600 copies, reaching 9,000 sales at the end of the third week. A disappointing performance for the artist in light of the sales of his previous albums. Despite good promotion and several appearances on television, the record was a real commercial failure.<ref>{{Cite web|date=20 September 2019|title=PLK, Black M : leurs chiffres de ventes révélés • RAPRNB|url=https://www.raprnb.com/2019/09/20/plk-black-m-leurs-chiffres-de-ventes-reveles/|access-date=16 August 2021|website=RAPRNB|language=fr-FR}}</ref>

=== ''Alpha...'' (since 2020) ===
At the end of 2019, Black M announced the great return of the [[Sexion d'Assaut]] for 2020, with both a major tour and another album promised to follow.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Booska-p|title=Pour Black M, la Sexion D'Assaut sera de retour en 2020 !|url=https://www.booska-p.com/new-pour-black-m-la-sexion-d-assaut-sera-de-retour-en-2020-n117175.html|access-date=16 August 2021|website=www.booska-p.com|language=en}}</ref> 

On 27 December 2020, the day of his birthday, Black M insulte, among others, [[Gims]] and [[Kev Adams]], a clash that seems quite strange,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Média|first=Prisma|title=Black M insulte Gims sur Twitter : les internautes sont choqués - Voici|url=https://www.voici.fr/news-people/actu-people/black-m-insulte-gims-sur-twitter-les-internautes-sont-choques-693808|access-date=16 August 2021|website=Voici.fr|language=fr}}</ref> and shocked the web (especially on [[Twitter]], where the insults were published). He also criticized the Sexion d'Assaut, whose return is planned in concert the same year. In fact, we later learn that he was posting these posts as "Black Shady", his hateful alter ego, to announce his return. On the same day, 27 December 2020, he released the sound "Black Shady Pt. 4", accompanied by a clip, which therefore follows "Black Shady Pt. 1 and 2", published as solo sounds in Sexion d'Assaut albums and "Black Shady Pt.3", published in his first album ''[[Les yeux plus gros que le monde]]'' in 2014. At the end of the clip, he announces the release of an [[Extended play]], ''[[Alpha...]] part 1'', for March 2021. The release is then postponed to 21 May 2021. In the meantime, he released other extracts from his album, such as "N.S.E.G." and "À la tienne".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black M dévoile sa vie dans une mini-série intitulée "AL'FAMILY"|url=https://www.mouv.fr/musique/rap-fr/black-m-devoile-sa-vie-dans-une-mini-serie-intitulee-al-family-369054|access-date=16 August 2021|website=Mouv|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Booska-p|title=Black M va tout dévoiler dans sa mini-série « Al'Family »|url=https://www.booska-p.com/new-black-m-va-tout-devoiler-dans-sa-mini-serie-al-family-n155146.html|access-date=16 August 2021|website=www.booska-p.com|language=en}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Since 2009, Black M has shared his life with Léa Djadja, image consultant and professional makeup artist whom he met on the set of the first "Wati bon son" clip of [[Sexion d'Assaut]]. Together they have a son, Isaac, who was born on 13 March 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black M et Léa Djadja : Un couple sur la route du succès !|url=https://www.purepeople.com/article/black-m-et-lea-djadja-un-couple-sur-la-route-du-succes_a371546/1|access-date=16 August 2021|website=www.purepeople.com|language=fr}}</ref> On 28 July 2020, the couple announced that they were expecting their second child.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black M et Léa Djadja attendent un 2e enfant : ils officialisent la grossesse|url=https://www.purepeople.com/article/black-m-et-lea-djadja-attendent-un-2e-enfant-ils-officialisent-la-grossesse_a398468/1|access-date=16 August 2021|website=www.purepeople.com|language=fr}}</ref> Léa gave birth to a daughter on 20 September 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black M et Léa Djadja parents pour la 2e fois : le sexe du bébé révélé !|url=https://www.purepeople.com/article/black-m-et-lea-djadja-parents-pour-la-2e-fois-le-sexe-et-le-prenom-reveles_a404405/1|access-date=16 August 2021|website=www.purepeople.com|language=fr}}</ref>

== Controversies ==

=== Homophobia accusation ===
The [[Sexion d'Assaut]] group, of which Black M is a part, was accused in 2010 of [[homophobia]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=à 07h00|first=Par Le 25 septembre 2010|date=25 September 2010|title=Le dérapage homophobe de Sexion d'Assaut|url=https://www.leparisien.fr/culture-loisirs/le-derapage-homophobe-de-sexion-d-assaut-25-09-2010-1081919.php|access-date=16 August 2021|website=leparisien.fr|language=fr-FR}}</ref> which led to bans on the air by Fun Radio and NRJ, as well as requests for the cancellation of concerts and its participation in [[2010 MTV Europe Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|title=MTV retire Sexion d'Assaut de ses Europe Music Awards|url=http://www.jeanmarcmorandini.com/article-45001-mtv-retire-sexion-d-assaut-de-ses-europe-music-awards.html|access-date=8 October 2010|publisher=Jeanmarcmorandini.com|language=fr}}</ref> “I believe it is high time that queers perish. Cut off their penises, leave them dead, found on the ring road, " chanted [[Gims]] of [[Sexion d'Assaut]] on the title "On t’a humilié".

=== Resumption of Dans ma rue ===
Black M is accused of anti-Semitism by the Belgian League against Anti-Semitism (LBCA), which calls for a boycott by the Belgian radios of his resumption of the title Dans ma rue by [[Doc Gynéco]], because of the use of the word "youpin" , a word which is a “racist insult to designate the Jew”.<ref>{{Cite web|title=TLFi|url=http://atilf.atilf.fr/|access-date=16 August 2021|website=atilf.atilf.fr}}</ref>

=== Centenary of the Battle of Verdun ===
In May 2016, Black M was invited to sing at the celebration of the centenary of the [[Battle of Verdun]] (300,000 dead, 400,000 wounded) by the socialist municipality, but he was the subject of criticism for the indecency of the arrival of this rapper at the commemoration of this historic battle, asking for the cancellation of the concert first on the part of the right, the [[National Rally]] and other personalities.<ref>{{Cite news|date=13 May 2016|title=Comment la « fachosphère » a fait annuler le concert de Black M à Verdun|language=fr|work=Le Monde.fr|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/les-decodeurs/article/2016/05/13/comment-la-fachosphere-a-fait-annuler-le-concert-de-black-m-a-verdun_4919477_4355770.html|access-date=17 August 2021}}</ref> He is criticized for lyrics to songs from [[Sexion d'Assaut]] anti-French ("conne de France", "moi aussi je baiserai la France" in 2010, and "ce pays de kouffar"), or homophobic ("il est grand temps que les pédés périssent”in 2006), even if for the latter, the [[LGBT community centre]] considers the controversy closed in 2011 after [[Sexion d'Assaut]] amended by collaborating with associations fighting against homophobia. [[Le Figaro]] criticizes the very relevance of a festive event of any kind to commemorate the death of 300,000 people.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Concert de rap aux cérémonies de Verdun : pourquoi cela choque|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/vox/politique/2016/05/11/31001-20160511ARTFIG00111-concert-de-rap-aux-ceremonies-de-verdun-la-france-humiliee.php|access-date=17 August 2021|website=LEFIGARO|language=fr}}</ref> On 9 May 2016, the rapper invited his detractors to come and see him: "Whether they like my music or not: we're going to have fun".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Interview . Black M se confie avant son concert à Verdun le 29 mai|url=https://www.estrepublicain.fr/edition-de-verdun/2016/05/09/black-m-se-confie-avant-son-concert-a-verdun-le-29-mai|access-date=17 August 2021|website=www.estrepublicain.fr|language=FR-fr}}</ref> The President of the Republic, [[François Hollande]] declared on 17 May 2016: "This initiative was after the ceremony, so that young people can have a festive moment".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Impôt, loi Travail, 2017... Revivez l'interview de François Hollande|url=https://www.europe1.fr/politique/francois-hollande-une-heure-pour-convaincre-2747047|access-date=17 August 2021|website=Europe 1|language=fr}}</ref>

The town hall canceled the concert on the grounds of the risk of disturbing public order following the controversy and a "surge of hatred and racism", the mayor having been physically threatened by appeals and considering himself let down by the government.<ref>{{Cite news|date=13 May 2016|title=Black M et la bataille de Verdun : retour sur une polémique|language=fr|work=Le Monde.fr|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/musiques/article/2016/05/13/le-rappeur-black-m-et-la-bataille-de-verdun-retour-sur-une-polemique_4919120_1654986.html|access-date=17 August 2021}}</ref> The Secretary of State for Veterans Affairs, [[Jean-Marc Todeschini]], declares on television that the cancellation of the Black M concert is "a first step towards totalitarianism, towards [[fascism]]".<ref>{{Cite web|date=13 May 2016|title=L'annulation de Black M est "un premier pas vers le fascisme"|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.fr/2016/05/13/concert-black-m-premier-pas-vers-fascisme-ministre-todeschini_n_9955780.html|access-date=17 August 2021|website=Le HuffPost|language=fr}}</ref> In reaction, Black M unexpectedly evokes his grandfather, a [[Senegalese Tirailleurs|Senegalese Tirailleur]], who fought during the [[World War II|Second World War]], he deplores the “hate speech”. He evokes the pride he had to participate in a concert linked to the commemoration of the Battle of Verdun.
