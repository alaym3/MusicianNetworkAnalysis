'''Donald James  Moen''' (born June 29, 1950) is an American singer, and a songwriter of [[contemporary worship music|Christian worship music]].

== Biography ==

Moen grew up in [[Two Harbors, Minnesota]] where he attended high school in 1968.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=The Biography of Don Moen|url=https://auntyfaith.com/2019/07/17/the-biography-of-don-moen/|last=auntyfaith|date=July 17, 2019|website=Auntyfaith.com|access-date=May 4, 2020|archive-date=February 26, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210226130440/https://auntyfaith.com/2019/07/17/the-biography-of-don-moen/|url-status=live}}</ref> Moen attended [[Oral Roberts University]], a Christian liberal arts school.

Moen married Laura Lee Szuchna in 1973.<ref name=":0" />

== Career ==
[[File:Don Moen in Qatar.jpg|thumb|Don Moen in 2018]]
He became a Living Sound musician for Terry Law Ministries and traveled with Terry Law for ten years.<ref>[http://www.donmoen.com/gen/pt.aspx?sn=1046&p1=1081 ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100116195245/http://www.donmoen.com/gen/pt.aspx?sn=1046&p1=1081 |date=January 16, 2010 }}</ref> After, he worked for Integrity Media for over 20 years, serving as creative director and president of Integrity Music, president of Integrity Label Group, and an executive producer of Integrity Music albums.<ref name="CCM_29_8">{{cite journal |last=Swartzendruber |first=Jay |date=February 2007 |title=Industrybeat: Moen of Many Hats |journal=[[CCM Magazine]] |volume=29 |issue=8 |page=22 |issn=1524-7848 }}</ref> He left Integrity Media in December 2007 to start a new initiative, The Don Moen Company.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/articles/news/Moens_Move/32921/p1/ |title=Moen's Move: Don Moen to leave Integrity Media to form new companies |publisher=Crossrhythms.co.uk |date=August 1, 2008 |access-date=August 22, 2013 |archive-date=October 15, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131015213913/http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/articles/news/Moens_Move/32921/p1/ |url-status=live }}</ref> The Don Moen Company acquired MediaComplete, the church software company that created MediaShout. Moen became a radio host for ''Don Moen & Friends'' in 2009. Moen received a [[Dove Award]] for his work on the musical ''God with Us'' in addition to nine nominations for his songs.

Moen also worked with Claire Cloninger, [[Paul Overstreet]], Martin J. Nystrom, Randy Rothwell, [[Ron Kenoly]], Bob Fitts, Debbye Graafsma, [[Paul Baloche]], [[Tom Brooks (music producer)|Tom Brooks]], among many others. He worked with musicians, Justo Almario, Carl Albrecht, [[Abraham Laboriel]], [[Alex Acuña]], [[Paul Jackson, Jr.]], [[Lenny LeBlanc]] and Chris Graham.{{Citation needed|date=January 2014}} He was a catalyst in launching the careers of [[Paul Baloche]], [[Darlene Zschech]], [[Israel Houghton]], and [[Hillsong United]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.davidsantistevan.com/50/ |title=Don Moen On Worship in Action, Authenticity, & Keeping It Simple |publisher=davidsantistevan.com |date=August 7, 2014 |access-date=August 11, 2014 |archive-date=August 12, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140812210644/http://www.davidsantistevan.com/50/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

He produced 11 volumes for the ''[[Hosanna! Music]]'' series of worship albums. His first album under his own name, ''Worship with Don Moen'', was released in 1992. His music has total global sales of over five million units.<ref name="CCM_29_8"/><ref>{{Cite web|title=Legendary Songwriter Don Moen Reveals Heartbreaking Story Behind 'God Will Make a Way'|url=https://www.christianpost.com/news/legendary-songwriter-don-moen-reveals-heartbreaking-story-behind-god-will-make-a-way.html|website=The Christian Post|access-date=May 4, 2020|archive-date=August 6, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190806170149/https://www.christianpost.com/news/legendary-songwriter-don-moen-reveals-heartbreaking-story-behind-god-will-make-a-way.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Notable recordings==
[[File:Don Meon and Lenny LeBlanc 2.jpg|thumb|Moen performing with [[Lenny LeBlanc]]]]
Moen's first album for [[Hosanna! Music]], ''Give Thanks'', became the label's bestseller. Produced by Tom Brooks, ''Give Thanks'' went on to be certified Gold by the RIAA. Moen followed this with a number of albums of his own including two, ''En Tu Presencia'' and ''Trono De Gracia'', in Spanish. ''God with Us'' won the Dove Award for Best Musical. On an Asian tour in 1999 he recorded ''The Mercy Seat'' at [[Singapore Indoor Stadium]] and ''Heal Our Land'' at Yoido Park in South Korea, which was released in 2000. One of Moen's albums, ''[[I Will Sing]]'', was recorded at [[Christian Broadcasting Network]].

''God Will Make a Way: The Best of Don Moen'' was released in 2003 and features 19 greatest hits. The title song was written for his sister-in-law and her husband, whose oldest son died in an auto accident while their other three children survived but were seriously injured. Moen's ''Hiding Place'' became his first studio album which was recorded at Paragon Studios in [[Franklin, Tennessee]], and was released in the autumn of 2006. ''I Believe There Is More'' released in late 2008. His third studio recording titled ''Uncharted Territory'' (funded successfully through [[Kickstarter]]) released on March 27, 2012.<ref name="JesusFreakHideout.com">{{cite web |author=TKO Marketing |url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/news/2012/01/06.inciite%20media%20Signs%20Renowned%20Worship%20Leader,%20Singer-Songwriter,%20Producer,%20Don%20Moen.asp |title=in:ciite Media Signs Renowned Worship Leader, Singer-Songwriter, Producer, Don Moen |publisher=JesusFreakHideout.com |date=January 6, 2012 |access-date=February 10, 2012 |archive-date=March 7, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120307163654/http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/news/2012/01/06.inciite%20media%20Signs%20Renowned%20Worship%20Leader,%20Singer-Songwriter,%20Producer,%20Don%20Moen.asp |url-status=live }}</ref> His Christmas album, ''Christmas: A Season of Hope'', was released on October 22, 2012, and some songs were recorded at a studio in Czech Republic.{{Citation needed|date=December 2011}} Moen released ''Hymnbook'' as a celebration of reaching over 1 million likes on his Facebook page.<ref name="DonMoen.com">[http://donmoen.com/hymnbook-giveaway/ ]{{dead link|date=August 2013}}</ref><ref name="The Christian Post">{{cite web |url=http://www.christianpost.com/news/don-moen-to-celebrate-1m-likes-on-facebook-with-album-giveaway-and-live-chat-75311/ |title=Don Moen to Celebrate 1M Likes on Facebook With Album Giveaway and Live Chat |work=The Christian Post |date=May 21, 2012 |access-date=August 22, 2013 |archive-date=July 14, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140714150032/http://www.christianpost.com/news/don-moen-to-celebrate-1m-likes-on-facebook-with-album-giveaway-and-live-chat-75311/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
