'''Adedamola Adefolahan''' (born 5 February 1996),<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/listen-watch-music-video-for-fireboy-dml-new-song-need-you-laughter-tears-goosebumps-album/|title=Watch Fireboy DML's New Music Video for 'Need You'|date=14 January 2020|website=[[OkayAfrica]]|language=en|access-date=17 January 2020|quote=The 23-year-old artist...}}</ref>  known professionally as '''Fireboy DML''', is a Nigerian singer. He is signed to [[YBNL Nation]], a record label founded by Nigerian rapper [[Olamide]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|date=29 November 2019|title=Fireboy DML album make pipo do wow!|work=[[BBC News Pidgin]]|url=https://www.bbc.com/pidgin/tori-50597826|access-date=17 January 2020}}</ref> His debut studio album ''[[Laughter, Tears and Goosebumps]]'' was released in 2019.<ref name=":0" /> He won Listener's Choice and was nominated for Song of the Year for "Jealous" at the 2020 [[Soundcity MVP Awards Festival]].<ref name=":1" />

==Early life==
Fireboy DML grew up in [[Abeokuta]], Ogun state and was a member of his local church choir.<ref name="The New York Times">{{cite web |first1=Joey |last1=Akan |title=The New Guard of Nigerian Musicians |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/08/29/t-magazine/nigerian-musicians-fireboy-dml-odunsi-the-engine.html |website=The New York Times |access-date=1 February 2020|date=29 August 2019}}</ref> He developed interest in music while studying at [[Obafemi Awolowo University]].

==Career==
===Breakthrough===
His breakthrough single "Jealous" first appeared on YBNL Nation's collaborative album ''YBNL Mafia Family'' (2018), before being re-released on 25 March 2019.<ref name="Native 3">{{cite web |first1=Debola |last1=Abimbolu |title= Watch the Cinematic Music Video for Fireboy DML's "Jealous"|url=https://thenativemag.com/music/watch-cinematic-music-video-fireboy-dmls/ |website=Native |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=26 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Jealous - Single by Fireboy DML |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/jealous-single/1457591530 |website=Apple Music |access-date=9 February 2020 |date=25 March 2019}}</ref> The song is composed of [[Riff|guitar riffs]], traditional drums and percussion; it combines African harmonies with elements of [[country music|country]] and [[soul music|soul]] music.<ref name="Native 3"/><ref name="The New York Times"/> "Jealous" was produced by Cracker Mallo and is centered around love and the complicated feelings that go along with it.<ref name="The New York Times" /> The visuals for "Jealous" was directed by Director K.<ref name="Native 3" />

On 14 June 2019, Fireboy DML released the romantic single "What If I Say".<ref>{{cite web |title=What If I Say - Single by Fireboy DML |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/what-if-i-say-single/1465686049 |website=Apple Music |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=14 June 2019}}</ref> It was produced by Pheelz, who incorporated a mix of percussion, ambient synth harmonies, and a drum riff into the production.<ref name="Native 4">{{cite web |first1=Debola |last1=Abimbolu |title=Fireboy DML shares music video for romantic new single, "What If I Say" |url=https://thenativemag.com/music/fireboy-dml-shares-music-video-romantic-new-single-say/ |website=Native |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=14 June 2019}}</ref> The visuals for "What If I Say" was directed by [[TG Omori]].<ref name="Native 4" /> On 1 August 2019, Fireboy DML released another single "King", in the song, he declares his worth to his love interest.<ref>{{cite web |title=King - Single by Fireboy DML |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/king-single/1475174591 |website=Apple Music |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=1 August 2019}}</ref><ref name="Native 5">{{cite web |first1=Debola |last1=Abimbolu |title=Fireboy DML brings Lover Boy feels to new video, "King"|url=https://thenativemag.com/music/watch-fireboy-dml-declare-worth-enthralling-music-video-king/ |website=Native |access-date=1 February 2020 |date=17 September 2019}}</ref> The accompanying music video for "King" was directed by [[TG Omori]]; it contains images of Fireboy and his love interest in a rose petal embellished Rolls Royce, as well as images of him singing at a fashion show where models walk on a runway.<ref name="Native 5" />

===''Laughter, Tears and Goosebumps''===
Fireboy DML released his debut studio album ''[[Laughter, Tears and Goosebumps]]'' on 29 November 2019. It was initially scheduled for release on 25 November.<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 78">{{cite web |first1=Motolani |last1=Alake |title='Laughter, Tears and Goosebumps' is a commendable debut by Fireboy |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/fireboy-laughter-tears-and-goosebumps-album-review/w6devcw |website=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=9 February 2020 |date=1 December 2019}}</ref> The album's music is a brand of R&B that combines love and social issues with empirical songwriting.<ref name="Pulse Nigeria 78" /> ''LTG'' comprises 13 tracks and doesn't feature any guest artist; it amassed over 6 million streams on [[Spotify]] three days after its release.<ref>{{cite web |first1=Tami |last1=Makinde |title= Fireboy DML Announces his debut album 'Laughter, Tears and Goosebumps'|url=https://thenativemag.com/music/fireboy-dml-laughter-tears-goosebumps/ |website=Native |access-date=9 February 2020 |date=6 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |first1=Nonni |last1=Wyldwolf |title=News:- Fireboy DML's Album "Laughter, Tears & Goosebumps" Hits 6Million Spotify Streams In 3days |url=https://madthisloaded.ml/ |website=Nigerian Sounds |access-date=9 February 2020 |date=2 December 2019}}</ref> It received generally positive reviews from [[music critics]], but was criticized for being labeled an "Afro-Life" record instead of an R&B album. ''LTG'' was supported by the previously released singles "Jealous", "What If I Say" and "King".<ref>{{cite web |first1=François |last1=Renoncourt |title=Fireboy DML, an urban romance |url=https://pan-african-music.com/en/fireboy-dml-laughter-tears-goosebumps/ |website=Pan African Music |access-date=9 February 2020 |date=21 November 2019}}</ref> The single "Vibration" was a fan favourite.

===''Apollo'' and continued success===
On 17 August 2020, Fireboy released the tracklist to his sophomore album, ''Apollo''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 August 2020|title=Fireboy releases tracklist for 17-track new album, 'Apollo'|url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/olamide-and-wande-coal-to-feature-on-fireboys-new-album-apollo/qztp75h|access-date=20 August 2020|website=Pulse Nigeria|language=en-US}}</ref>

In a review, ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' gave ''Apollo'' an 8.3 rating out of 10 and called it "the peak of his vision so far, a melodious, detailed, and effortless album of feel-good pop and R&B."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ani |first1=Ivie |title=APOLLO |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/fireboy-dml-apollo/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=November 21, 2021}}</ref> ''[[NotJustOk]]'' wrote: "At the base of his writing is a deep-seated knack for introspection, the desire to be himself and a whole others like himself, spinning personal and borrowed accounts of existential crisis, ambition, love, lust and depression."

In 2021, Fireboy DML released the single "Peru," which critics wrote "affirms [his] Afrobeats intelligence."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hansen |first1=Gabrielle Myers |title=Peru affirms Fireboy DML's Afrobeats intelligence |url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/peru-affirms-fireboy-dmls-afrobeats-intelligence |website=Music in Africa |access-date=November 21, 2021}}</ref> He was featured on [[Ladipoe]]'s hit "Running"<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bartee |first1=Richardine |title=Hear Nigerian artist Ladipoe’s dark new track featuring Afrobeats star Fireboy DML |url=https://grungecake.com/ladipoe-running-fireboy-dml/articles/83642 |website=GrungeCake.com |access-date=November 21, 2021}}</ref> and [[WSTRN]]'s single "Be My Guest",<ref>{{cite web |title=New Video: WSTRN feat. Fireboy DML – Be My Guest |url=https://www.bellanaija.com/2021/11/wstrn-fireboy-dml-be-my-guest-2/ |website=Bella Naija |access-date=November 21, 2021}}</ref> and [[Cheque]]'s single "History."

He made a musical appearance on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' and performed a medley of the songs "Vibration," from ''Tears, Laughter and Goosebumps'', and "Champion," from ''Apollo.''<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bloom |first1=Madison |title=Watch Fireboy DML Perform "Champion/Vibration" Medley on Fallon |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/watch-fireboy-dml-perform-championvibration-medley-on-fallon/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=November 21, 2021}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Fireboy DML describes his sound as "Afro-Life" and said he writes songs that audiences can relate to.<ref name="The New York Times" /> 
Alphonse Pierre of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' characterized his sound as "slow-groove pop" and said it "tells stories about love with the formulaic structure of a Hallmark Christmas romance".<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/fireboy-dml-like-i-do-new-song-listen/|title=The Ones: Fireboy DML's "Like I Do"|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|date=4 December 2019|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|language=en|access-date=21 May 2020}}</ref>

He has cited [[Jon Bellion]], [[Passenger (singer)|Passenger]] and [[Wande Coal]] as his key contemporary influences.<ref name="The New York Times" />

==In popular culture==
In 2020, Fireboy DML gained media coverage in Nigeria when his hit "Champion" was used by [[FC Bayern Munich]] for their [[UEFA Super Cup]] celebrations.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Alake |first1=Motolani |title=European champions, Bayern Munich uses Fireboy's 'Champion' to celebrate European Super Cup win |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/download-champ-by-fireboy-dml-ft-d-smoke/wfmr4vm |website=Pulse Nigeria |access-date=November 21, 2021}}</ref>

His songs were included in the ''[[FIFA 21]]'' game soundtrack.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Alake |first1=Motolani |title=Fireboy, Burna Boy and Rema feature on FIFA 21 soundtrack |url=https://www.pulse.ng/entertainment/music/fireboy-burna-boy-and-rema-feature-on-fifa-21-soundtrack-alongside-dua-lipa-aitch-and/djsshjy |website=Pulse Nigeria |date=28 September 2020 |access-date=29 September 2020}}</ref>
