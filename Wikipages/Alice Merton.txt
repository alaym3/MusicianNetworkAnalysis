'''Alice Merton''' (born 13 September 1993) is a German-born Irish-Canadian singer and songwriter.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nrz.de/region/niederrhein/aus-den-niederlanden/ueber-300-gigs-beim-eurosonic-in-groningen-id212998311.html|title=Über 300 Gigs beim Eurosonic in Groningen|last=Buschmann|first=Heiko|access-date=2018-01-03|language=de-DE}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://teamrock.com/review/2017-10-06/eloy-the-vision-the-sword-the-pyre-album-review|title=Eloy - The Vision, The Sword & The Pyre album review|date=2017-10-06|website=Teamrock.com|access-date=2018-01-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/track-by-track/alice-merton-no-roots-ep|title=Track By Track: Alice Merton on her debut EP|work=The Line of Best Fit|access-date=2018-01-03}}</ref> Merton achieved mainstream success with her debut single, "[[No Roots (song)|No Roots]]".<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7905180/alice-merton-interview-no-roots|title=Alice Merton's Wanderlust Anthem 'No Roots' Heads to U.S. After Blowing Up in Europe|last=Cirisano|first=Tatiana|date=2017-08-22|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-11-03}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Merton was born on 13 September 1993 in [[Frankfurt]], Germany to a German mother and an Irish-born father. Merton's family moved frequently due to her father's job as a mining consultant.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/alice-merton-no-roots-songs-tour-w517798|title=Artist You Need to Know: Alice Merton|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2018-04-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Lambert|first=Kirsten|date=2019-04-18|title=On Mint, global citizen Alice Merton unpacks her nomadic lifestyle|work=Chicago Reader|url=https://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/on-mint-global-citizen-alice-merton-unpacks-her-nomadic-lifestyle/Content?oid=69708927}}</ref>  When she was three months old, Merton moved to [[Connecticut]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite news|url=http://www.lemonademagazine.net/interviews/alice-merton-always-move/|title=Alice Merton: Always On The Move|date=2017-10-03|work=Lemonade Magazine|access-date=2018-01-03}}</ref> Shortly thereafter{{specify|date=February 2018}}, they relocated to [[Oakville, Ontario]], Canada where she was raised until the age of thirteen, learning classical piano and singing.<ref>{{cite web | last1=Yeung| first1=Neil| url=https://itunes.apple.com/sk/album/why-so-serious-single/1435090175| date=7 September 2018| access-date=11 November 2018|title=Why So Serious - Single}}</ref> Her family then relocated to [[Munich]].<ref name=":3" /><ref name="puls" /> Merton's return to Germany prompted her to learn German which enabled her to speak with her German grandmother whom she had only seen once annually growing up.<ref name=":4">{{Cite news|url=http://www.hr3.de/musik/wir-stellen-vor-alice-merton,alice-merton-106.html|title=Wir stellen vor: Alice Merton {{!}} hr3.de {{!}} Musik|last=Germany|first=hr3.de, Frankfurt|date=2017-09-07|work=hr3.de|access-date=2018-01-03|language=de-DE|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171024210004/http://www.hr3.de/musik/wir-stellen-vor-alice-merton,alice-merton-106.html|archive-date=2017-10-24|url-status=dead}}</ref> While in Germany, Merton wrote her first song and attended a German-language high school before graduating and moving to England.<ref name=":4" />

== Career ==
===2015–2017: ''The Book of Nature'', ''No Roots'', and mainstream success===

Merton first drew major attention in 2015  {{citation needed|date=December 2017}} as a songwriter and singer on [[Fahrenhaidt]]'s album ''The Book of Nature''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/de/Fahrenhaidt-The-Book-Of-Nature/release/6830597|title=Fahrenhaidt – The Book Of Nature|website=Discogs.com|access-date=2017-12-09}}</ref> On 14 November 2016 while in [[Hamburg]] she won the annual award for the promotion of talented newcomers in the category ''Acoustic Pop''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.swr3.de/musik/poplexikon/-/id=927882/did=4252588/1u20jz8/index.html|title=Biografie: Merton, Alice|date=2016-12-14|publisher=[[Südwestrundfunk]]|language=de|access-date=2017-12-09}}</ref>

After moving to Berlin, Alice and her manager Paul Grauwinkel founded the record label Paper Plane Records International, and released the song "[[No Roots (Alice Merton song)|No Roots]]" at the end of 2016. The song's lyrics were inspired by her feeling of "There's no one place where my home is."<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/alice-merton-no-roots|title=Singer-songwriter Alice Merton explores her nomadic past on "No Roots"|last=Kent|first=Matthew|date=2016-12-01|work=The Line of Best Fit|access-date=2017-12-03}}</ref> On both [[Hype Machine]] and [[Spotify]]'s ''Global Viral 50'' charts the song reached number one, and was soon included on the "Hit Play" lists of several radio stations. The song was also featured on episode 5x09 of the NBC TV series, ''[[The Blacklist (TV series)|The Blacklist]]''.
[[File:Alice Merton 12 11 2017 -23 (39345441732).jpg|thumb|Alice performing live in Los Angeles 2017]]
On the basis of this success, Merton officially released her first EP, ''No Roots'', on 3 February 2017. ''No Roots'' reached number one on the French charts, number two on the German charts, number three in Austria, number 25 in Switzerland and number one in US alternative radio charts. [[Vodafone Germany]] even included the song in one of its commercials.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musikradar.de/vodafone-song-aus-der-gigadepot-werbung/|title=Vodafone: Song aus der GigaDepot Werbung|date=2017-04-21|website=Musikradar.de|language=de|access-date=2017-12-09}}</ref> Alice signed with [[Mom + Pop Music]] in August 2017 for marketing within the United States,<ref name=":0" />  and on 2 August 2017 she released her second single ''Hit the Ground Running'' with her new record label.

She performed at the [[Life Ball]] in [[Vienna]] on 10 June 2017, and ended the year touring throughout Germany, appearing as opening act for [[Bosse (musician)|Bosse]] and [[Philipp Poisel]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vienna.at/live-vom-life-ball-2017-das-wiener-rathaus-feiert-schrill-durch-die-nacht/5315486|title=LIVE vom Life Ball 2017: Das Wiener Rathaus feiert schrill durch die Nacht|last=Beganovic|first=Amina|date=2017-06-10|website=Vienna.at|language=de|access-date=2017-12-09}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sputnik.de/news/musiknews/bosse_nimmt_alice_merton_mit-100.html|title=Bosse nimmt Alice Merton mit|website=Sputnik.de|language=de|access-date=2017-12-09}}</ref> In late 2017 she won the 2018 [[European Border Breakers Award]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.europeanborderbreakersawards.eu/en/the-winners/alice-merton/|title=The winners - Germany - Alice Merton|website=Europeanborderbreakersawards.eu|access-date=2017-12-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171210015758/https://www.europeanborderbreakersawards.eu/en/the-winners/alice-merton/|archive-date=2017-12-10|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===2018–2020: ''Mint''===

On 7 September 2018, Merton released "[[Why So Serious (song)|Why So Serious]]", the lead single from her debut album, ''[[Mint (Alice Merton album)|Mint]]''. She released two other singles since then. The album was released on 18 January 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/podcasts/8473922/alice-merton-interview-alternativefacts-podcast|title=Alice Merton Previews Just-Announced Debut LP: 'Every Song Sounds Like the Emotion It's Portraying'|last=Payne|first=Chris|date=2018-09-07|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-09-11}}</ref> The second official single Funny Business was released 30 November. The video for the single was premiered 9 January. Also, this day she released her brand new song from album "Homesick".

Merton dueted with British singer-songwriter [[Tom Odell]] in the single "Half As Good As You" that was released on 28 September 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-24|title=From making the Billboard charts with No Roots to singing with Tom Odell: interview with Alice Merton|url=https://the-shortlisted.co.uk/alice-merton-interview/|access-date=2021-07-23|website=The Shortlisted|language=en-GB}}</ref>

On 1 February 2019, Merton released the first part of the Making of Mint documentary and announced an April 2019 USA concert tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicnewsnet.com/2019/02/music-news-alice-merton-has-released-part-1-of-a-new-documentary-centered-around-the-making-of-her-lp-mintthe-lp-was.html|title=ALICE MERTON RELEASES 'MAKING OF MINT' DOCUMENTARY PT. 1|website=Musicnewsnet.com}}</ref>
[[file:Alice Merton - Southside Festival 2019 5404.jpg|thumb|Alice Merton live at [[Southside Festival]] 2019]]
In 2019, Merton was a coach on ninth series of ''[[The Voice of Germany]]'', and became the first female coach to win a series with [[Claudia Emmanuela Santoso]].

On 18 October 2019, Merton released her album ''[[Mint (Alice Merton album)|Mint]]'' again with four additional songs (Back To Berlin, PCH, Easy and Keeps Me Awake). Later, in the beginning of 2020, she toured across [[Germany]], [[Austria]], [[Czech Republic]] and [[Denmark]] singing these new songs, as well as the ones from her previous album.

===2021–present: upcoming studio album===

On 8 April 2021, Merton released "Vertigo". On 9 September, she released "Hero", both of which are from her untitled upcoming second studio album.

== Personal life ==
Merton currently resides in [[Berlin]].<ref name="puls">{{cite document|url=http://www.br.de/puls/musik/aktuell/alice-merton-interview-100.html|title=Kein Land der Welt ist meine Heimat|last=Mittelstraß|first=Ann-Kathrin|date=2017-07-18|publisher=[[Bayerischer Rundfunk]]|language=de|access-date=2017-12-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://undercoverrocklife.com/2016/12/12/no-roots-by-alice-merton/|title=No Roots by Alice Merton|date=2016-12-12|work=Undercover RockLife|access-date=2018-01-03}}</ref>
