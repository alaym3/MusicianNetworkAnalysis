'''Ossian''' are a [[Scotland|Scottish]] [[traditional music]] group, formed in 1976.<ref name="LarkinGE">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Encyclopedia of Popular Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1992|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-939-0|page=1878}}</ref>

The initial line-up brought together Billy Ross and former members of the group Contraband, [[William Jackson (Scottish composer)|Billy Jackson]], John Martin, and George Jackson.<ref name="LarkinGE"/> One of their earliest gigs was at the 1976 [[Kinross]] Folk Festival.

Each of the members was a multi-instrumentalist and singer. Their arrangements of songs, slow airs and dance tunes were meticulous, almost a chamber music approach to Scottish music. They sang in [[Scottish English|English]], [[Scots language|Scots]], and [[Scottish Gaelic|Gaelic]].

Billy Jackson's wire-strung harp, the [[cl√†rsach]], featured in most pieces, but he also played [[uilleann]] (Irish) pipes and whistles.<ref name="LarkinGE"/>  John Martin, who played fiddle and cello,<ref name="LarkinGE"/>  went on to become a member of [[The Tannahill Weavers]]. George Jackson (brother of Billy) played guitar, [[cittern]], mandolin, fiddle, whistle and flute.<ref name="LarkinGE"/>  Billy Ross was the main singer who played guitar, dulcimer and whistle.<ref name="LarkinGE"/> 

Their first two LPs were ''Ossian'' (1977) and ''[[St. Kilda Wedding]]'' (1978).<ref name="LarkinGE"/>  Billy Ross left the band in 1980, and was replaced by Tony Cuffe as lead vocalist, also playing guitar and whistle.<ref name="LarkinGE"/>  In 1981, [[MacDonald brothers|Iain MacDonald]] joined, playing highland pipes, flute, and whistle.<ref name="LarkinGE"/>  They broke up in 1989 when Tony Cuffe and William Jackson moved to the U.S.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ossian-mn0000625561|title=Ossian Songs, Albums, Reviews, Bio & More|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=2 October 2021}}</ref> 

In 1997, Billy Jackson reformed the band with the original singer, Billy Ross, and the group made a CD and performed some gigs for a time.
