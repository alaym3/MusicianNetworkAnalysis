'''Jeffrey Ernest Rosenstock'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/1570913-Jeff-Rosenstock|title=Jeff Rosenstock {{!}} Discography|access-date=October 21, 2021|via=[[Discogs]]}}</ref> (born September 7, 1982) is an American musician, multi-instrumentalist, singer and songwriter from [[Long Island]], United States. He was the lead singer of the [[ska punk]] band [[The Arrogant Sons of Bitches]], the [[Punk rock|punk]] musical collective [[Bomb the Music Industry!]] and the [[indie rock]]/[[noise pop]] band [[Kudrow (band)|Kudrow]]. After the breakup of [[Bomb the Music Industry!]], he began a solo career.

==Early life==

Jeff Rosenstock was raised in [[Baldwin, Nassau County, New York|Baldwin]] on New York's [[Long Island]].<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Essner |first1=Dean |title=Jeff Rosenstock on his poppy, self-reflective solo album 'We Cool?' |work=[[Washington Post]] |date=2015-04-23 |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/express/wp/2015/04/23/jeff-rosenstock-on-his-poppy-self-reflective-solo-album-we-cool/ |language=en-US |issn=0190-8286 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> He was born to a Jewish mother who worked as an art teacher and a German Catholic father who worked as a lawyer. Rosenstock identifies as Jewish.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Reiss |first1=Jon |title=Pwning The Music Industry: An Interview with Jeff Rosenstock AKA Bomb The Music Industry |work=[[Jewcy]] |date=2010-05-26 |url=https://jewcy.com/jewish-arts-and-culture/pwning_music_industry_interview_jeff_rosenstock_aka_bomb_music_industry }}</ref>

==Career==

Rosenstock began his solo career in 2012 by releasing his debut solo "mixtape"<ref name="ills">{{cite web|url=http://bombthemusicindustry.tumblr.com/post/34226054584/jeff-rosenstock-solo-mixtape|title=JEFF ROSENSTOCK SOLO "MIXTAPE (?)"|website=Bomb the Music Industry!}}</ref> ''I Look Like Shit'' two months after Bomb the Music Industry! had announced their intention to break up.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bombthemusicindustry.tumblr.com/post/28910528585/here-come-the-waterworks|title=HERE COME THE WATERWORKS...|website=Bomb the Music Industry!}}</ref> This was followed by the ''Summer Seven Club'' project in 2013, which granted purchasers of Rosenstock's 7-inch single "Summer" exclusive access to new songs periodically released over the following months. All the songs from ''Summer Seven Club'' were later collected and made available as the ''Summer +'' compilation album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://jeffrosenstock.bandcamp.com/album/summer|title=Summer +, by Jeff Rosenstock|website=Jeff Rosenstock}}</ref> Rosenstock's first<ref>{{cite web|url=http://businesscasual.limitedrun.com/products/543410-jeff-rosenstock-we-cool-cassette|title=Jeff Rosenstock "We Cool?" Cassette|website=Business Casual}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.moshcam.com/articles/6887/the-best-punk-albums-of-2015|title=The Best Punk Albums of 2015 - Moshcam|date=27 November 2015|website=Moshcam.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_au/article/6azg8g/rank-your-records-jeff-rosenstock-bomb-the-music-industry|title=Rank Your Records: Jeff Rosenstock Rates Every Bomb the Music Industry! Album|date=26 February 2015|website=Noisey.vice.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://restless-press.com/post/111981615372/jeff-rosenstock-streams-new-album-we-cool|title=Jeff Rosenstock Streams New Album 'We Cool?'|website=Restless Press}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.getalternative.com/201539jeff-rosenstock-we-cool-review/|title=Jeff Rosenstock - "We Cool" Review - The Alternative|date=10 March 2015|website=Getalternative.com}}</ref> studio album as a solo artist, ''[[We Cool?]]'', was released in 2015. His second solo album, entitled ''[[Worry. (album)|Worry]]'', was released on October 14, 2016. On [[New Year's Day]] 2018,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://jeffrosenstock.bandcamp.com/|title=POST-, by Jeff Rosenstock|website=Jeff Rosenstock}}</ref> Rosenstock's third studio album ''[[POST-]]'' with 10 tracks was released in January 2018. His fourth studio album, [[No Dream (album)|NO DREAM]], was released on May 20, 2020. A [[ska]] version of NO DREAM, titled SKA DREAM was released on April 20, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.mxdwn.com/2021/04/20/news/jeff-rosenstock-surprise-releases-ska-reimagining-of-no-dream-called-ska-dream/|title = Jeff Rosenstock Surprise-Releases Ska-Reimagining of No Dream Called Ska Dream -|date = 20 April 2021}}</ref>

Rosenstock has performed with many other ska and punk rock bands, including [[Mustard Plug]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.mustardplug.com/articles/punknews.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-11-05 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20131105070233/http://www.mustardplug.com/articles/punknews.html |archive-date=2013-11-05 |url-status=dead }}</ref> [[The Bruce Lee Band]], and [[AJJ (band)|AJJ]]. He has also performed with his [[Bomb the Music Industry!]] band-mates [[Laura Stevenson]] and Lee Hartney.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/jeffrosenstockmusic/info|title=Jeff Rosenstock|website=Facebook.com}}</ref> Some of his musical influences include [[Tom Waits]], [[Pulp (band)|Pulp]] and [[The Beach Boys]].

Rosenstock also founded the independent record label [[Quote Unquote Records]], which puts out all of its records for free (in keeping with Rosenstock's long time [[DIY_ethic#Music|DIY]] ideology). 

He has worked as a producer for other artists such as [[Mikey Erg]], [[The Smith Street Band]], [[Laura Stevenson]], and [[Dan Andriano]]. He is also a member of [[Antarctigo Vespucci]], a collaborative project between himself and [[Fake Problems]] front man [[Chris Farren (punk musician)|Chris Farren]].

Rosenstock composes the music for the [[Cartoon Network]] series ''[[Craig of the Creek]]''.
