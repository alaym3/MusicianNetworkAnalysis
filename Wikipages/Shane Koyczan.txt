'''Shane L. Koyczan''' {{IPAc-en|ˈ|k|ɔɪ|ˌ|z|æ|n}}<ref>{{Cite web|title=Shane Koyczan on Twitter: "“@simoneengel: How do you pronounce your last name? @koyczan” Smith. Just kidding. It's coy and zan (rhymes with pan) the c is silent." / Twitter|url=https://twitter.com/koyczan/status/229818377424756737|access-date=2021-11-30|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>, born 22 May 1976, is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[spoken word]] poet, writer, and member of the group [[Tons of Fun University]]. He is known for writing about issues like bullying, cancer, death, and eating disorders. He is most famous for the anti-[[bullying]] poem “[[To This Day]]” which has over 24 million views on YouTube.

== Background ==
Koyczan was born in [[Yellowknife]], [[Northwest Territories]], to a Canadian Indigenous father and a French mother. He grew up in [[Penticton]], [[British Columbia]]. In 2000, he became the first Canadian to win the Individual Championship title at the beach [[National Poetry Slam]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.cbc.ca/arts/story/2002/08/01/slam010802.html |title=Vancouver poets to compete at National Poetry Slam |first=Marsha |last=Lederman |publisher=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]] |date=1 August 2002 |accessdate=13 February 2010}}</ref> Together with American [[slam poet]] [[Mighty Mike McGee]] and fellow Canadian [[Tons of Fun University|C. R. Avery]], he is a co-founder of Vancouver, British Columbia [[spoken word]], "talk rock" trio, [[Tons of Fun University]] (T.O.F.U.).  In August 2007 Shane Koyczan and his work were the subject of an episode of the television documentary series ''[[Heart of a Poet]]'', produced by Canadian filmmaker [[Maureen Judge]] for broadcaster [[Bravo!]].<ref>[http://www.heartofapoet.ca/episodes.html Heart of a Poet] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120702071332/http://www.heartofapoet.ca/episodes.html |date=2 July 2012 }}, [[Bravo!]], August 2007</ref>

Koyczan has published several books, including poetry collection ''Visiting Hours'', ''Stickboy'', a novel in verse, ''Our Deathbeds will be Thirsty'', ''To This Day: For the Bullied and Beautiful'' and ''A Bruise on Light''.  ''Visiting Hours'' was selected by both the ''Guardian'' and'' Globe and Mail'' for their 2005 Best Books of the Year lists.

Koyczan's "We Are More" and Ivan Bielinski's "La première fois", commissioned by the [[Canadian Tourism Commission]], were unveiled at [[Canada Day]] festivities on [[Parliament Hill]] in [[Ottawa]] on 1 July 2007.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.backbonemag.com/Press_Release/Items/press_release_07040701.asp |title=CTC pioneers new-school tourism marketing, leads Canada Brand with new visual ID and celebrates Canada's 140th with slam poets on stage |date=4 July 2007 |publisher=[[Backbone Magazine]] |accessdate=13 February 2010}}</ref> Koyczan performed a variation on his piece at the [[2010 Winter Olympics opening ceremony|Opening Ceremony of the 2010 Winter Olympics]] in [[Vancouver]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.winnipegfreepress.com/sports/olympics/poet-from-yellowknife-becomes-overnight-sensation-after-olympics-monologue-84314537.html |title=Poet from Yellowknife becomes overnight sensation after Olympics monologue |first=Nick |last=Patch |publisher=[[Winnipeg Free Press]], [[The Canadian Press]] |date=13 February 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100216133048/http://www.winnipegfreepress.com/sports/olympics/poet-from-yellowknife-becomes-overnight-sensation-after-olympics-monologue-84314537.html |archivedate=16 February 2010 |accessdate=7 November 2013}}</ref>

Koyczan also collaborated on Vancouver-based musician [[Dan Mangan]]'s ''Roboteering'' EP on the track ''Tragic Turn of Events&nbsp;– Move Pen Move''.

In 2012, Shane Koyczan released a full-length digital album entitled "Remembrance Year", which "tackles abuse with courage and displacement with inspiration",<ref>{{cite web |last=Khaikin |first=Lital |title=Spoken Word Hits the Bedroom with Shane Koyczan and the Short Story Long: 2012 Album "Remembrance Year" |url=http://areyouthereason.com/spoken-word-hits-the-bedroom-with-shane-koyczan-and-the-short-story-long-2012-album-remembrance-year/ |publisher=Why Blue Matters |accessdate=30 December 2012}}</ref> in collaboration with the folk instrumentation of his band, The Short Story Long.

In February 2013 Koyczan released an animated video of his [[spoken word]] poem "[[To This Day]]" on [[YouTube]] and at the [[TED (conference)|TED Conference]].<ref name="YouTube">{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ltun92DfnPY |title=To This Day Project&nbsp;– Shane Koyczan |author=Shane Koyczan |date=19 February 2013 |publisher=[[YouTube]] |accessdate=28 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Shane Koyczan: "To This Day" … for the bullied and beautiful |url=http://www.ted.com/talks/shane_koyczan_to_this_day_for_the_bullied_and_beautiful.html? |publisher=TED |accessdate=22 March 2013}}</ref> The poem focused on the subject of bullying that Koyczan and others had received during their life and its lasting effects.<ref name="Quill & Quire">{{cite web |url=http://www.quillandquire.com/blog/index.php/quillblog/shane-koyczans-anti-bullying-poem-goes-viral/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130226120604/http://www.quillandquire.com/blog/index.php/quillblog/shane-koyczans-anti-bullying-poem-goes-viral/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=26 February 2013 |title=Shane Koyczan's anti-bullying poem goes viral |author=Natalie Sequeira |date=22 February 2013 |publisher=[[Quill & Quire]] |accessdate=17 March 2013 }}</ref>  "To This Day" went viral shortly after its release,<ref name="Viral1">{{cite web |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/02/25/shane-koyczan-to-this-day-anti-bullying-poem_n_2759849.html |title=Shane Koyczan's 'To This Day', Anti Bullying Poem, Goes Viral |author=Dominique Mosbergen |date=25 February 2013 |work=[[The Huffington Post]] |accessdate=15 March 2013}}</ref> with the video receiving over 20 million views as of January 2017, prompting many viewers to send Koyczan letters thanking him for publishing the poem.<ref name="CBS News">{{cite web |url=http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-504784_162-57570310-10391705/powerful-animated-version-of-a-poem-about-bullying |title=Powerful animated version of a poem about bullying |author=Casey Glynn |date=20 February 2013 |publisher=[[CBS News]] |accessdate=17 March 2013}}</ref><ref name="HLN">{{cite web |url=http://www.hlntv.com/video/2013/02/22/shane-koyczan-day-bullying |title=Bullied poet's slamming video goes viral |author=bbamsey |date=22 February 2013 |publisher=HLNtv.com |accessdate=28 February 2013}}</ref>

He has described himself as an 'okay atheist'.<ref>{{Citation|last=Shane Koyczan|title=Shane Koyczan 'Heaven, or Whatever'|date=8 December 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qjWqUxPFqSA&t=325s|accessdate=30 June 2017}}</ref>
