'''Amy Louise Billings''' (born 14 May 1986), known professionally as '''Amy Shark''', is an Australian [[indie pop]] singer-songwriter-guitarist and producer from the [[Gold Coast, Queensland]]. During 2008 to 2012, her early solo material was released and performed under the name '''Amy Cushway'''. Her 2016 single "[[Adore (Amy Shark song)|Adore]]" peaked at number&nbsp;3 on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Singles Chart]] and was also listed at number&nbsp;2 on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2016]]. Her album ''[[Love Monster (album)|Love Monster]]'' (July 2018) debuted at number&nbsp;1 on the ARIA Albums Chart. Shark has won 8 [[ARIA Music Awards]] from 22 nominations, including winning [[ARIA Award for Best Pop Release|Best Pop Release]] three&nbsp;times: in [[ARIA Music Awards of 2017|2017]] for her extended play, ''[[Night Thinker]]'', [[ARIA Music Awards of 2018|2018]] for ''Love Monster'' and [[2020 ARIA Music Awards|2020]] for "[[Everybody Rise]]".

== Career==
===2008–2013: Amy Cushway===
Amy Shark recorded and performed as Amy Cushway from 2008 to 2012.<ref name="APRA Cool"/> In June 2008 Cushway supported a gig by [[Australian Idol (season 5)#Brianna Carpenter|Brianna Carpenter]] at The Globe, Brisbane,<ref name="4ZZZ June 2008">{{cite web | url=http://www.4zzzfm.org.au/events/index.cfm?action=dsp_event&eventID=887&from=dsp_archive&startrow=296 | title=Amy Cushway Supporting Brianna Carpenter (''Australian Idol'' Top 12) @ The Globe | publisher=[[4ZZZ]] | date=6 June 2008 | via=National Library of Australia | archive-url=https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20091004072714/http://www.4zzzfm.org.au/events/index.cfm?action=dsp_event&eventID=887&from=dsp_archive&startrow=296 | archive-date=4 October 2009 | access-date=6 November 2021 }}</ref> and in November the singer backed Rhiannon Hart at Island Vibe Festival, South Brisbane.<ref name="NLA Island Vibe">{{cite web | title=Island Vibe Festival of Soul, Roots, Reggae & Island Culture | year=2008 | publisher=The Joynt | url=https://trove.nla.gov.au/work/36421435 | access-date=6 November 2021}}</ref> Cushway released two [[extended plays]], ''I Thought of You Out Loud'' (2008) and ''Love's Not Anorexic'' (2009),<ref name="Nov 2009"/> and toured nationally in July 2009 to promote the second EP.<ref name="Nov 2009"/> In November of that year Cushway described her early material, "As much as I enjoy writing intricate acoustic ballads, there's nothing better than adding thunderous beats and raw energy to your set."<ref name="Nov 2009">{{cite web | url = http://www.accessallareas.net.au/music_news/EkVApkplVEVWeIsgcB.php | title=Amy Cushway finds pop beats! | website = Access All Areas | date = 24 November 2009 | via = [[National Library of Australia]] | archive-url = https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20100325214341/http://www.accessallareas.net.au/music_news/EkVApkplVEVWeIsgcB.php | archive-date = 25 March 2010 | access-date = 8 March 2020 }}</ref> She was living in [[Varsity Lakes, Queensland|Varsity Lakes]] in April 2010 and explained how, "[record companies] did not ask for demos now but just asked what her MySpace page was."<ref name="Bedos">{{cite news | url = http://www.goldcoast.com.au/article/2010/04/09/206215_print_friendly.html | title = New MySpace for Musical Generation | last1 = Bedos | first1 = Stephanie | last2 = Godfrey | first2 = Miles | work = Gold Coast Bulletin | date = 9 April 2010 | via = National Library of Australia | archive-url = https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20100414014536/http://www.goldcoast.com.au/article/2010/04/09/206215_print_friendly.html | archive-date = 14 April 2010 | access-date = 16 March 2020 }}</ref> As Amy Cushway, the artist also issued two albums ''Broadway Gossip'' (2010)<ref>{{cite web | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20190730085428/https://music.yandex.ru/artist/1413893 | url = https://music.yandex.ru/artist/1413893 | title = Amy Cushway Artist | work = music.yandex.ru | archive-date = 30 July 2019 | access-date = 6 November 2021 | url-status = dead }}</ref> and ''It's a Happy City'' (2012).<ref name="Sputnik Dec 2016">{{cite web | url = https://www.sputnikmusic.com/review/72397/Amy-Cushway-Its-A-Happy-City/ | title = Amy Cushway – ''Its a Happy City'' | last = freedominspring | date = 5 December 2016 | access-date = 9 March 2020 }}</ref> By December 2016, Shark was no longer using Cushway and material under that name had been deleted from her accounts.<ref name="Sputnik Dec 2016"/>

===2014–2017: New name and ''Night Thinker''===

By the end of 2013, the artist started using the stage name, Amy Shark, because ''[[Jaws (film)|Jaws]]'' was her favourite film.<ref name="Yates">{{cite news | url = http://rollingstoneaus.com/music/post/amy-shark-overnight-success-a-decade-in-the-making/6155.html | title = Amy Shark: Overnight Success, a Decade in the Making | author = Yates, Rod | work = [[Rolling Stone Australia|Rolling Stone]] | date = 12 May 2017 | via = National Library of Australia | archive-url = http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/159038/20171105-0053/rollingstoneaus.com/music/post/amy-shark-overnight-success-a-decade-in-the-making/6155.html | url-status = dead | archive-date = 5 November 2017 | access-date = 8 March 2020 }}</ref> In February 2014 she independently released a five-track extended play, ''Nelson''.<ref name="couriermail"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicbrainz.org/release/43ab5d24-a261-4bd3-97de-a85f481152ae|title=Release "Nelson" by Amy Shark - MusicBrainz|website=musicbrainz.org}}</ref> She activated a new YouTube account, as Amy Shark Music, in 2014 and issued a single, "Spits on Girls", in July.<ref name="iTunes Spits"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/AmySharkMusic|title=Amy Shark – YouTube|via=YouTube|author=Amy Shark Music|access-date=26 January 2017}}</ref>

===2018–2019: ''Love Monster''===
{{main|Love Monster (album)}}
[[File:Amy Shark 02 20 2018 -27 (39007366310).jpg|thumb|left|Shark performing in Los Angeles, February 2018]]
In March 2018, Shark provided, "Sink In", for the film soundtrack of ''[[Love, Simon]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/love-simon-original-motion-picture-soundtrack/1334779517|title=Love, Simon (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack) by Various Artists on Apple Music|work=iTunes Store (US)|access-date=16 January 2018}}</ref> The related album, by various artists, debuted at No.&nbsp;37 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite web | url  =https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2018-03-31/ | title = ''Billboard'' 200: March 31, 2018 | work = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | publisher = Billboard Music | access-date = 14 March 2020 }}</ref> The following month, she performed at the [[Commonwealth Games]] [[2018 Commonwealth Games closing ceremony|closing ceremony]] on the Gold Coast, in a duet with [[Archie Roach]] and an [[Indigenous Australians|Indigenous]] youth choir for a rendition of Roach's "[[Let Love Rule (Archie Roach album)|Let Love Rule]]".<ref name="Lutton">{{cite news | url = https://www.smh.com.au/sport/commonwealth-games-2018/in-closing-why-you-should-expand-your-thinking-on-commonwealth-games-20180415-p4z9s5.html | title = In Closing, Why You Should Expand Your Thinking on Commonwealth Games | last = Lutton | first = Phil | work = The Sydney Morning Herald | date = 15 April 2018 | access-date = 14 March 2020 }}</ref><ref name="Eliezer">{{ cite news | url = https://themusicnetwork.com/tv-tune-ins-the-voice-returns-with-lowest-premiere-commonwealth-games-train-wreck-draws-1-3-million-for-aussie-music/ | title = TV Tune-ins: ''The Voice'' Returns with Lowest Premiere, Commonwealth Games Train Wreck Draws 1.3 Million | last = Eliezer | first = Christie | work = The Music Network | date = 16 April 2018 | access-date = 14 March 2020 }}</ref>

On 11 April 2018, Shark premiered a single, "[[I Said Hi]]", on Triple J before releasing it the following day.<ref name="I Said Hi">{{Cite news|url=http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/music/adore-star-amy-shark-calls-out-the-men-who-rejected-her-music-in-surprise-new-song/news-story/9ba4de936fa7fa2ed12f570a165439e7|title=Amy Shark, the artist everyone rejected, bites back with single|work=NewsComAu|access-date=2018-04-11}}</ref> It received unpaid promotion by comedians, and digital radio show hosts, Luke and Lewis using posters, megaphone and a large home-made sign hung up next to the Fox FM Melbourne station logo.<ref name="Radio promotion unpaid">{{cite web | url = https://instagram.com/p/Bj1rN2LHlD-/ | title = Instagram campaign Amy Shark says Hi | publisher = Instagram | access-date = 11 June 2018 }}</ref> That promotion captured the attention of the singer-songwriter who displayed it on her own Instagram account.<ref name="Amy Shark response">{{cite web | url = https://instagram.com/p/Bj181yAn8us/ | title = Amy Shark post | publisher = Instagram | access-date = 11 June 2018 }}</ref><ref name="Amy Shark post">{{cite web | url = https://instagram.com/p/Bj165TTjvVA/ | title = Amy Shark response | publisher = Instagram | access-date = 11 June 2018 }}</ref> "I Said Hi" peaked at number&nbsp;6 on the ARIA charts.

Shark's album, ''[[Love Monster (album)|Love Monster]]'', was released on 13 July 2018<ref name="Love Monster">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/love-monster/1368525652|title=Love Monster by Amy Shark|work=[[iTunes Store]] (AU)|access-date=13 April 2018}}</ref> and it debuted at number&nbsp;1 on the ARIA Charts. The album provided four additional singles, including "[[Mess Her Up]]" (March 2019). At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2018]], the artist won four ARIA Music Awards, with [[ARIA Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]], Best Pop Release and [[ARIA Award for Best Female Artist|Best Female Artist]] for ''Love Monster'', and [[ARIA Award for Producer of the Year|Producer of the Year]] for [[Dann Hume]] and M-Phazes work on "I Said Hi".<ref name="ARIA List"/> She was the most nominated artist and equal highest winner in that year.<ref name="ARIA List"/>

Shark promoted ''Love Monster'' during 2019 on her Regional Australia Tour. In December 2019, [[the Chainsmokers]]' single, "[[The Reaper (The Chainsmokers song)|The Reaper]]", featured Shark on vocals.<ref name="Brandle">{{cite web | url = https://theindustryobserver.thebrag.com/shane-billings-amy-shark-new-music/ | title = Shane Billings Talks New Amy Shark Music, Management: 'She's the Best Boss You Could Ask For' | last = Brandle | first = Lars | work = The Industry Observer | date = 25 February 2020 | access-date = 15 March 2020 }}</ref> At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2019]], she received four more nominations.<ref name="ARIA List"/>

===2020–present: ''Cry Forever''===
{{Main|Cry Forever}}
For her latest album, Amy Shark worked with [[Ed Sheeran]],<ref name="Cashmere">{{cite web |url=http://www.noise11.com/news/ed-sheeran-is-helping-out-amy-shark-for-her-next-album-20200124/ |title=Ed Sheeran is helping out amy shark for her next album |work=Noise11 |date=24 January 2020 |access-date=15 March 2020 |author=Cashmere, Paul |author-link=Paul Cashmere}}</ref> [[Diplo]],<ref name="Moran">{{cite web |url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/tayla-harris-controversy-inspires-amy-shark-to-play-aflw-grand-final-20190327-p51850.html |title='I was shocked': Amy Shark's Grand Final gig inspired by Tayla Harris controversy |work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]] |date=27 March 2019 |access-date=16 March 2013 |author=Moran, Robert |quote=She's been recording music with the likes of [[Diplo]] and [[Billy Corgan]]."}}</ref> [[Billy Corgan]] of Smashing Pumpkins,<ref name="Moran"/> and the Chainsmokers. On 16 February 2020, Shark performed at the [[Fire Fight Australia]] fund-raising relief concert in Sydney for the effects of the [[2019–20 Australian bushfire season]].<ref name="Elvish">{{cite web |url=https://happymag.tv/watch-queen-recreate-their-iconic-1985-live-aid-set-at-fire-fight-australia/ |title=Watch Queen recreate their iconic 1985 Live Aid set at Fire Fight Australia  |work=Happy Mag |date=17 February 2020 |access-date=18 March 2020 |author=Elvish, Emily}}</ref> Her live performance of "I Said Hi" appeared on the related album by various artists, ''Artists Unite for Fire Fight: Concert for National Bushfire Relief'' (12 March 2020).<ref name="Bleach">{{cite web |url=https://thomasbleach.com/2020/03/17/album-review-artists-unite-for-fire-fight/ |title=Album Review: ''Artists Unite For Fire Fight'' |work=ThomasBleach |date=17 March 2020 |access-date=18 March 2020 |author=Bleach, Thomas}}</ref> Thomas Bleach observed that she was, "euphorically telling the world that Australia says hi and thank you for all the support that has been given."<ref name="Bleach"/>

On 6 March 2020, Shark announced a management deal with Redlight's Will Botwin for international markets while husband, Shane Billings continued to manage her for the local market.<ref name="Brandle 2">{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9329401/amy-shark-signs-with-red-light-management/ |title=Amy Shark Signs with Red Light Management |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=6 March 2020 |access-date=15 March 2020 |author=Brandle, Lars}}</ref> On 23 October 2020, Shark released a single "[[C'mon (Amy Shark song)|C'mon]]" featuring [[blink-182]] drummer [[Travis Barker]].<ref name="Pan">{{cite web |url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/amy-shark-blink-182-travis-barker-collab-cmon/ |title=Amy Shark and Blink-182's Travis Barker drop banger collab with 'C'mon' |work=Tone Deaf |date=23 October 2020 |access-date=24 October 2020 |author=Pan, Alexander}}</ref> Alexander Pan of ''Tone Deaf'' observed, "[it's] a thumping power-pop ballad that's got a considerable amount of weight behind it."<ref name="Pan"/> Two days later she performed at the [[2020 NRL Grand Final]] at [[Stadium Australia]], Sydney.<ref>{{cite web
|url=https://www.triplem.com.au/story/amy-shark-to-headline-the-2020-nrl-grand-final-entertainment-168061/ |title=Amy Shark to headline the 2020 NRL Grand Final entertainment |publisher=[[Triple M]] |date=16 October 2020 |access-date=19 March 2021}}</ref> At the [[2020 ARIA Music Awards]], she won Best Pop Release for her single, "[[Everybody Rise]]" (June 2020)—marking the third time she'd received the award.<ref name="ARIA 2020 Wins"/> Shark performed the track live at the ceremony.<ref name="SMH Every">{{cite web |url=https://www.smh.com.au/culture/music/aria-awards-2020-amy-shark-gives-first-performance-of-the-night-20201127-p56ipe.html |title=Video: ARIA Awards 2020: Amy Shark gives first performance of the night |work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]] |date=27 November 2020 |access-date=1 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201129233633/https://www.smh.com.au/culture/music/aria-awards-2020-amy-shark-gives-first-performance-of-the-night-20201127-p56ipe.html |archive-date=29 November 2020 |url-status=dead |url-access=subscription}}</ref> She also performed as part of an all-female ensemble, singing "[[I Am Woman]]", in honour of [[Helen Reddy]] (1941–2020).<ref name="Gallagher">{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/helen-reddy-tribute-tones-and-i-amy-shark-aria-2020-2824980/ |title=Watch Tones and I, Amy Shark and more Australian female artists' Helen Reddy tribute at 2020 ARIA Awards |work=[[NME Australia]] |date=25 November 2020 |access-date=1 December 2020 |author=Gallagher, Alex |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201125123400/https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/helen-reddy-tribute-tones-and-i-amy-shark-aria-2020-2824980 |archive-date=25 November 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref> At the ceremony, she also won the public-voted category, [[ARIA Award for Best Australian Live Act|Best Australian Live Act]] for her Regional Tour during 2019.<ref name="ARIA 2020 Wins"/>

On 4 December 2020, Shark announced her second studio album, ''[[Cry Forever]]'', which was released on 30 April 2021.<ref name="Newstead">{{cite web |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/amy-shark-cry-forever-album-tour-announce-2021-new-song/12947406/ |title=Amy Shark announces new album, 2021 tour, shares 'All the Lies About Me' |work=[[Triple J]] |date=4 December 2020 |access-date=4 December 2020 |author=Newstead, Al}}</ref> Shark released the single "All the Lies About Me", alongside the album's announcement and additionally announced a national headline tour for mid-2021.<ref name="Newstead"/> Shark revealed the album's track listing in an interview with ''[[Rolling Stone Australia]]''on the same day.<ref name="Jenke 2">{{cite web |url=https://au.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/amy-shark-cry-forever-interview-20235/ |title=Amy Shark announces highly-anticipated new album, 'Cry Forever' |work=[[Rolling Stone Australia]] |date=4 December 2020 |access-date=4 December 2020 |author=Jenke, Tyler}}</ref>

On 19 March 2021, Shark appeared on [[Triple J]]'s ''[[Like a Version]]'' segment, performing a cover of [[Fall Out Boy]]'s "[[Sugar, We're Going Down]]" alongside a performance of her original track "Baby Steps" (marking her debut live performance of the song).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/watch-amy-sharks-mellow-cover-of-fall-out-boys-sugar-were-goin-down-2903574/ |title=Watch Amy Shark's mellow cover of Fall Out Boy's "Sugar, We're Goin Down" |work=[[NME Australia]] |date=19 March 2021 |access-date=19 March 2021 |author=Martin, Josh}}</ref> In September 2021, Shark featured on Gold Coast-based artist Indyanna Baby's debut single "I'm Not Giving Up".<ref name="INGU"/>

== Personal life ==

Amy Shark's birth father<!-- not named in reliable sources --> separated from her mother, Robyn, when Shark was "just a baby, one or two."<ref name=smh/> Upon the success of "Adore", Shark replied to her birth father's attempt to re-establish contact, "Haven't heard from my father in 15&nbsp;years until now. It must suck knowing ur daughter got all this way on her very own! Now beat it! I'm busy."<ref name=smh/> Of her stepfather, David Cushway, she recalled "I used to call him Dave and I remember the day I said to Mum, 'Do you think I could call Dave "Dad"?' She cried. She said, 'Why don't you go and ask him?' So I went and asked him, and he cried. I was only young but I remember it really well."<ref name=smh/> Early recordings were credited to Amy Cushway (2008–2012).<ref name="APRA Cool"/><ref name="Nov 2009"/>

===ARIA Music Awards===
The [[ARIA Music Awards]] are a set of annual ceremonies presented by [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA), which recognise excellence, innovation, and achievement across all genres of the [[music of Australia]].
