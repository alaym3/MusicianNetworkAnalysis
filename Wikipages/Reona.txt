'''Reona''' ({{Lang-ja|レオナ}} {{IPA-ja|ɾe.o̞.na̠|}}; stylized as '''ReoNa''', born October 20, 1998)<ref name="kaiyou-19">{{cite web |title=SAO GGOの神崎エルザ歌手が判明！ 19歳の新人「ReoNa」が劇中歌アーティストに抜擢 |url=http://kai-you.net/article/53128 |publisher=Kai-You Inc. |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |language=Japanese |date=May 6, 2018}}</ref> is a Japanese singer who is signed to [[Sacra Music]]. Having been active as a cosplayer and independent musician, she made her major debut in 2018 as the singing voice of the character Elza Kanzaki in the anime series ''[[Sword Art Online Alternative: Gun Gale Online]]''. Her first single under her own name was released in August 2018.

==Career==



Reona was born in Kagoshima on October 20, 1998.<ref name="kaiyou-19"/> She became interested in becoming a cosplayer during her elementary school years, as she had been recommended to become one by a classmate.<ref name="icem"/><ref name="marog">{{cite web |title=ReoNaはコスプレ界のれおぺこ！？画像や楽曲もwiki調査！ |url=https://marog01.com/reona-kanzaki-eruza-singer/ |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |language=Japanese}}</ref> Her interest in cosplay was boosted after ordering a costume of the [[Vocaloid]] [[Hatsune Miku]].<ref name="icem">{{cite web |title=1.48m級巨人の6年目コスプレイヤーReoNa*の超高校級コスプレ道と人生 |url=https://www.ism.life/contents/119 |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |language=Japanese}}</ref> She became active as a cosplayer while in junior high school, and went under the names {{nihongo|Reopeko|れおぺこ}} and ReoNa* while attending events such as [[Comiket]] 91 in 2016.<ref>{{cite web |title=【写真】金髪JKコスプレイヤーReoNa* コミケで会った可愛い子 Vol.1 |url=http://kai-you.net/article/36952 |publisher=Kai-You Inc. |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |language=Japanese |date=December 29, 2016}}</ref> She had also dreamed of becoming a singer for anime, and had released covers of songs on [[YouTube]].<ref name="icem"/><ref>{{cite web |title=ReoNa YouTube channel |url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCDGKZdgRlP__KOCgC58WeWA |publisher=YouTube |accessdate=August 2, 2018}}</ref>

Reona had her break as a singer in 2017 when she became a finalist in an audition held by the music label [[Sacra Music]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Sacra Music Hosts Anisong Newcomer Showcase! |url=https://otakumode.com/news/5b58237d460672db7b78d66f/Sacra-Music-Hosts-Anisong-Newcomer-Showcase! |publisher=Tokyo Otaku Mode |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |date=July 27, 2018}}</ref> She made her major debut as a singer the following year as the singing voice of the character Elza Kanzaki in the anime series ''[[Sword Art Online Alternative: Gun Gale Online]]''.<ref name="kaiyou-19"/> She released the song {{nihongo|"Pilgrim"|ピルグリム}} on April 15, 2018; the song topped iTunes Japan's anime singles chart as well as Amazon Japan's digital music rankings.<ref name="kaiyou-hsl">{{cite web |title=SAO GGOの神崎エルザ歌唱担当「ReoNa」が8月デビュー 『ハッピーシュガーライフ』ED曲に抜擢 |url=http://kai-you.net/article/53636 |publisher=Kai-You Inc. |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |language=Japanese |date=May 20, 2018}}</ref> This was followed by the release of the digital singles "Step, Step" and "Independence".<ref name="kaiyou-hsl"/> She released the mini-album ''Elza'' on July 4, 2018; the mini-album peaked at No. 8 on [[Oricon]]'s weekly charts.<ref name="elza">{{cite web |title=ELZA |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/723020/products/1280339/1/ |publisher=[[Oricon]] |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |language=Japanese}}</ref> Her first single under her own name, "Sweet Hurt", was released on August 29, 2018;<ref name="sweet">{{Cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/723020/products/1288348/1/|title=SWEET HURT|publisher=[[Oricon]]|language=Japanese|access-date=August 2, 2018}}</ref> the title track is used as the ending theme to the anime series ''[[Happy Sugar Life]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=ReoNa Performs Ending Theme for Happy Sugar Life Anime |url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2018-05-20/reona-performs-ending-theme-for-happy-sugar-life-anime/.131778 |publisher=[[Anime News Network]] |accessdate=August 2, 2018 |date=May 20, 2018}}</ref> Her second single "forget-me-not" was released digitally on January 13, 2019<ref>{{cite web|title=forget-me-not - EP|url=https://itunes.apple.com/jp/album/forget-me-not-ep/1447938409|publisher=[[iTunes]]|accessdate=January 13, 2019|language=Japanese|date=December 21, 2016}}</ref> and received a physical release on February 6, 2019; the title track is used as the second ending theme to the anime series ''[[Sword Art Online|Sword Art Online: Alicization]]'', and the single also includes the song {{nihongo|"Niji no Kanata ni"|虹の彼方に|Beyond the Rainbow}}, which is used as the ending theme for episode 19 of anime series ''Sword Art Online: Alicization''.<ref>{{cite web|title=ASCA, ReoNa Perform New Theme Songs for Sword Art Online: Alicization Anime|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2018-12-08/asca-reona-perform-new-theme-songs-for-sword-art-online-alicization-anime/.140568|publisher=[[Anime News Network]]|date=December 8, 2018|accessdate=December 8, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181209094436/https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2018-12-08/asca-reona-perform-new-theme-songs-for-sword-art-online-alicization-anime/.140568|archive-date=December 9, 2018|url-status=live|df=mdy-all}}</ref> Her feature single "Prologue" was released on June 26, 2019 under the Elza Kanzaki stage name.<ref>{{cite web |title=Prologue |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/723020/products/1322674/1/ |publisher=[[Oricon]] |accessdate=June 24, 2019 |language=Japanese}}</ref> Her third single "Null" was released on August 28, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Null |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/723020/products/1327463/1/ |publisher=[[Oricon]] |accessdate=November 5, 2019 |language=Japanese}}</ref> She released her fourth single "Anima" digitally on July 13, 2020, and received a physical release on July 22, 2020; the title song is being used as the opening theme of television series anime ''Sword Art Online: Alicization - War of Underworld: Part 2'' .<ref>{{cite web |title=ReoNA Sings Sword Art Online: Alicization - War of Underworld Anime's New Opening |url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2020-02-10/reona-sings-sword-art-online-alicization-war-of-underworld-anime-new-opening/.156278 |accessdate=February 13, 2020 |date=February 10, 2020}}</ref><ref name=Anima>{{cite web |title=ANIMA|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/723020/products/1354806/1/ |publisher=[[Oricon]] |accessdate=May 27, 2020 |language=Japanese}}</ref>{{efn|group=upper-alpha|This single was originally scheduled to be released on May 20, 2020, but was delayed due to the postponement of the anime. The anime is getting delayed due to [[COVID-19 pandemic]].}} She released her first album "unknown" on October 7, 2020.<ref name=Unknown>{{cite web |title=unknown|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/723020/products/1366703/1/ |publisher=[[Oricon]] |accessdate=September 17, 2020 |language=Japanese}}</ref> Reona collaborated with [[Hiroyuki Sawano]] on the song "time"; the song was used as  the ending theme to the anime series ''[[The Seven Deadly Sins (manga)|The Seven Deadly Sins: Dragon's Judgement]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/406048|title=「七つの大罪」新シリーズOPはポルノグラフィティ岡野昭仁、EDは澤野弘之×ReoNaが担当|publisher=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=November 25, 2020|accessdate=December 4, 2020|language=Japanese}}</ref>
