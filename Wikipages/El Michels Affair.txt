'''El Michels Affair''' is an American "[[cinematic soul]]" group led by New York-based musician [[Leon Michels]]. The group released its debut album ''Sounding Out The City'' in 2005. After touring behind [[Raekwon]] and other [[Wu-Tang Clan]] members, the group recorded the popular cover album ''Enter The 37th Chamber'' in 2009. After releasing other cover albums during the following decade, El Michels Affair released the original albums ''Adult Themes'' in 2020 and ''Yeti Season'' in 2021.

== History ==
Michels played with [[Soul Fire Records]] house band [[The Mighty Imperials]] before forming his own ensemble in 2002.<ref name="amg">[https://www.allmusic.com/artist/el-michels-affair-mn0000387097/biography El Michels Affair] at [[Allmusic]]</ref> This group released singles for Soul Fire and for Misty, a sublabel of [[Daptone Records]], as well as a full-length album which was released on the Michels-cofounded label [[Truth & Soul Records]] in 2005.<ref name="amg" /> Titled ''Sounding Out the City'', the album drew from [[library music]] and [[afrobeat]] influences.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2021-03-24|title=El Michels Affair, “Yeti Season”|url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/album-of-the-day/el-michels-affair-yeti-season-review|access-date=2021-03-26|website=Bandcamp Daily}}</ref> After performing with [[Raekwon]] at a concert, the group began working with other members of the [[Wu-Tang Clan]] and [[cover version|covered]] several of their songs for vinyl single releases.<ref name="amg" /> By 2009, this had yielded an album's worth of material, which was released in 2009 as ''Enter the 37th Chamber'', the name is a play on the album ''[[Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)]]''.<ref>[https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/13011-enter-the-37th-chamber/ Review of ''Enter the 37th Chamber'']. [[Pitchfork Media]], May 11, 2009.</ref> El Michels Affair followed this with a second album of arrangements of songs by Wu-Tang Clan and from its members' solo releases, titled ''Return to the 37th Chamber''.<ref>[http://www.clashmusic.com/reviews/el-michels-affair-return-to-the-37th-chamber Review of ''Enter the 37th Chamber'']. [[Consequence of Sound]], April 25, 2017.</ref><ref>[https://www.allmusic.com/album/return-to-the-37th-chamber-mw0003030370 Review of ''Enter the 37th Chamber'']. Allmusic.</ref> 

In 2019, they accompanied [[MadGibbs|Freddie Gibbs and Madlib]] at their popular [[Tiny Desk Concerts|NPR Tiny Desk]] performance and later recorded ''The Diamond Mine Sessions'' with them.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Freddie Gibbs And Madlib: Tiny Desk Concert|url=https://www.npr.org/2019/12/06/784794979/freddie-gibbs-and-madlib-tiny-desk-concert|access-date=2021-03-06|website=NPR.org|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Freddie Gibbs Says He’s Making More Music With Madlib and Shares ‘The Diamond Mine Sessions’|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/05/freddie-gibbs-interview-the-diamond-mine-sessions|access-date=2021-03-12|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>

El Michels Affair began the 2020s with new original material; they released their album ''Adult Themes'' in 2020 and followed it less than a year later with the [[Turkish pop music|Turkish pop]] and [[Progressive rock|prog]]-inspired ''Yeti Season'' in March 2021;<ref>{{Cite web|title=Adult Themes, by El Michels Affair|url=https://elmichelsaffair.bandcamp.com/album/adult-themes|access-date=2021-03-06|website=El Michels Affair}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Yeti Season, by El Michels Affair|url=https://elmichelsaffair.bandcamp.com/album/yeti-season|access-date=2021-03-06|website=El Michels Affair}}</ref> the album features vocals from [[Hindi]] language singer [[Piya Malik]] and The Shacks.<ref name=":0" />
