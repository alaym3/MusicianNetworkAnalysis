'''Omar Samuel Pasley''' (born 3 September 1986), better known by his stage name '''OMI''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|oʊ|m|iː}} {{Respell|OH|mee}}),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6605740/omi-pronunciation-poll|title=How Do You Pronounce OMI?|date=23 June 2015|work=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|access-date=12 September 2015}}</ref> is a Jamaican singer. He is best known for "[[Cheerleader (song)|Cheerleader]]", a worldwide hit for him in a remixed version by German DJ [[Felix Jaehn]]. He is currently signed to [[Ultra Music]], a part of [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony Music]],<ref name="Jamaica Observer">{{cite web|title=Omi's remix hits platinum|url=http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Omi-s-remix-hits-platinum_18309836|website=Jamaica Observer|access-date=29 April 2015}}</ref> and released his debut album ''[[Me 4 U]]'' on 16 October 2015.

==Career==
===Beginnings===
Omar Samuel Pasley was born in the parish of [[Clarendon Parish, Jamaica|Clarendon]]. OMI established in Kingston, after being discovered by the dancehall impresario Clifton Dillon (known as Specialist) and signed to Oufah, an independent Jamaican label.<ref name=nyt>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/07/18/arts/music/omi-soars-with-cheerleader-remix-and-aims-to-keep-riding-high.html?_r=1|author=Joe Coscarelli|title=MI Soars With 'Cheerleader' Remix and Aims to Keep Riding High|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=July 17, 2015|access-date=July 22, 2015}}</ref> His first recorded single was "Standing On All Threes" released with a music video. OMI had composed his future hit song "[[Cheerleader (song)|Cheerleader]]" in 2008. He eventually recorded it and performed and released in 2012. It was released in Jamaica along with a low-budget, high-concept video shot in [[Oregon]] during OMI's first trip to the United States, becoming a modest hit in Jamaica and also popular in Hawaii and in Dubai. Never releasing an album, OMI had various local hits in Jamaica like "Take It Easy", "Fireworks" (accompanied by a music video) and "Color of My Lips", the latter featuring [[Busy Signal]], a well-known Jamaican dancehall, ska, and reggae artist.

===2014–present: ''Me 4 U''===
In 2014, "Cheerleader" got a second life when Patrick Moxey, the president of [[Ultra Music]], a dance label partly owned by Sony Music heard the song and liked it offering to remix the song and relaunch it as a dance track. In early 2014, Ultra Music commissioned two remixes for the song, one by Ricky Blaze and the other by [[Felix Jaehn]], a 20-year-old German producer. Eventually Ultra Music opted for the airy tropical house version of "Cheerleader" as remixed by Jaehn. The remix initially became a huge hit in Sweden, eventually certified 5 times platinum. Soon the song spread through other European charts, notably France, Italy and Germany. It topped the charts in several European countries and in Australia. In the United Kingdom, OMI made history on May 24, 2015, when "Cheerleader" topped the [[UK Singles Chart]] for a fourth week in a row, marking the longest consecutive period any Jamaican artist has held that position on the chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=OMI Makes UK History|url=http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/OMI-MAKES-UK-HISTORY|website=Jamaica Observer|access-date=30 May 2015}}</ref> In the United States, the song entered the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in May 2015;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2015-05-09|title=Billboard Hot 100 – May 9, 2015|access-date=21 May 2015}}</ref> it later made the top spot of the Hot 100 after 12 weeks in the chart. Claiming the top spot, OMI has achieved recognition worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2015-06-06|title=Billboard Hot 100 – June 6, 2015|access-date=30 May 2015}}</ref> On August 27, 2015 he released the single "[[Hula Hoop (song)|Hula Hoop]]". He released his debut studio album ''Me 4 U'' on 16 October 2015. The album includes the singles "[[Cheerleader (song)|Cheerleader]]", "[[Hula Hoop (song)|Hula Hoop]]", "Stir It", and "Drop in the Ocean".
