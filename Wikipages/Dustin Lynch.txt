'''Dustin Charles Lynch''' (born May 14, 1985)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1684816/dustin-lynch-rides-high-with-cowboys-and-angels.jhtml |title=Dustin Lynch Rides High With "Cowboys and Angels" |publisher=CMT.com |access-date=2015-04-30}}</ref> is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter, signed to [[Broken Bow Records]]. Lynch has released four albums and one EP for the label: a [[Dustin Lynch (album)|self-titled]] album in 2012, ''[[Where It's At (Dustin Lynch album)|Where It's At]]'' in 2014, ''[[Current Mood]]'' in 2017, and ''Ridin' Roads'' in 2019. A fourth album, ''[[Tullahoma (album)|Tullahoma]]'', was released in January 2020. He has also released seventeen singles, of which seven have reached the No. 1 position on [[Country Airplay]].

==Early life and education==
Dustin Charles Lynch was born in [[Tullahoma, Tennessee]], on May 14, 1985.<ref name="soundfactory">{{cite web|url=http://songfactorymusic.com/artists_dustin.html |title=Biography|access-date=April 18, 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120409220138/http://songfactorymusic.com/artists_dustin.html |archive-date=April 9, 2012 }}</ref>

In 2003, Lynch graduated from [[Tullahoma High School]]. In 2007, Lynch graduated from [[Lipscomb University]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]] with a [[Bachelor of Science|B.S.]] degree in biology. He said he chose Lipscomb because it is located near the [[Bluebird Café]] where he would play and learn the craft of songwriting while attending college.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1694589/dustin-lynchs-college-years-paved-the-way-for-country-career/ |title=Dustin Lynch's College Years Paved the Way for Country Career |first =Craig |last=Shelburne |date= September 27, 2012 |work=CMT News}}</ref><ref name="duke">{{cite web |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/dustin-lynch-cowboys-and-angels-interview/ |title= Dustin Lynch Interview: 'Cowboys and Angels' Singer's Journey Begins and Ends at Home |first= Billy |last= Dukes |date=August 31, 2012|work=Taste of Country }}</ref> Although Lynch said he was interested in going to medical school, he decided to pursue a career in music.<ref name="lipscomb">{{cite web |url=http://www.lipscombsports.com/wherearetheynow/news/2012-13/7470/where-are-they-now--dustin-lynch/ |title=Where are they now? Dustin Lynch|date= September 12, 2012 |first= Mark |last=McGee |publisher=Lipscomb University Athletic }}</ref>

==Music career==
Lynch said he started playing guitar when he was around 8 or 9 but soon abandoned it, and then picked it up again at 15. When he was 16, he performed at the [[Bluebird Café]] in Nashville on an open mic night and was well received by the audience, so he decided to start a band.<ref name="lipscomb"/>

===2011–2013: ''Dustin Lynch''===
[[File:Dustin_Lynch.jpg|thumb|right|upright=1.2|Dustin Lynch in 2013]]
Lynch was first signed to [[Valory Music Group]], and when one of its executives left to join [[Broken Bow Records]], Lynch followed and signed with [[Broken Bow Records]] in late 2011.<ref name="duke"/> 
He released his debut single, "[[Cowboys and Angels (Dustin Lynch song)|Cowboys and Angels]]" in January 2012. Lynch wrote the song with [[Josh Leo]] and [[Tim Nichols]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Broken Bow Records Introduces Dustin Lynch|url=http://www.brokenbowrecords.com/news/broken-bow-records-introduces-%E2%80%A8dustin-lynch|access-date=January 25, 2012|newspaper=[[Broken Bow Records]]|date=January 17, 2012}}</ref> That same month, ''[[Country Weekly]]'' began streaming his songs on its website.<ref>{{cite news|title=Meet Dustin Lynch|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/news/meet-dustin-lynch|access-date=January 25, 2012|newspaper=[[Country Weekly]]|date=January 18, 2012}}</ref>

The self-titled debut album, ''[[Dustin Lynch (album)|Dustin Lynch]]'', was released in August 2012 and reached number one on the Top Country Albums chart.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://theboot.com/dustin-lynch-album-sales/ |title=Dustin Lynch Scores No. 1 Debut Album! |first= Stephen L. |last=Betts |date=August 30, 2012|work=The Boot }}</ref>  The album's second single, "[[She Cranks My Tractor]]", became his second top 20 hit in early 2013. It was followed by "[[Wild in Your Smile]]", which made the top 30.

Lynch co-wrote the song "[[Somebody Somewhere (Dallas Smith song)|Somebody Somewhere]]" by [[Dallas Smith]], as well as [[James Wesley]]'s 2013 single "Thank a Farmer".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/1564955/james-wesley-sets-up-debut-album-with-thank-a-farmer|title=James Wesley Sets Up Debut Album With 'Thank a Farmer'|last=Dauphin|first=Chuck|date=27 May 2013|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=23 February 2014}}</ref>

===2014–2015: ''Where It's At''===
In early 2014, Lynch released his fourth single and the lead single from his second album, "[[Where It's At (Yep, Yep)]]". It became his first number one single on the [[Country Airplay]] chart in September 2014, and [[Where It's At (Dustin Lynch album)|the album of the same name]] was released on September 9, 2014. "[[Hell of a Night (Dustin Lynch song)|Hell of a Night]]" and "[[Mind Reader (Dustin Lynch song)|Mind Reader]]" were released to country radio as the album's second and third singles, both of which also reached number one on the Country Airplay charts.

===2016–present: ''Current Mood'' and ''Tullahoma''===
"[[Seein' Red]]" was released on July 11, 2016 as the lead single from Lynch's third studio album ''[[Current Mood]]''.<ref name="Current Mood">{{cite web|url=http://www.nashcountrydaily.com/2017/08/09/dustin-lynch-announces-new-album-current-mood-and-headlining-tour/|title=Dustin Lynch Announces New Album, 'Current Mood,' and Headlining Tour|work=[[Nash Country Weekly|Nash Country Daily]]|last=Casey|first=Jim|date=August 9, 2017|access-date=August 10, 2017}}</ref> It hit number one on the Country Airplay chart for the week dated February 25, 2017. "[[Small Town Boy (song)|Small Town Boy]]" was released on March 20, 2017 as the second single. Following the album's release on September 8, 2017, "I'd Be Jealous Too" was released on October 23, 2017 as the album's third single.

In November 2017, Lynch made an appearance at the [[Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade]], performing his hit single "Small Town Boy". On September 18, 2018 he was inducted into the [[Grand Ole Opry]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2018-09-19-dustin-lynch-is-the-grand-ole-oprys-newest-member/|title=Dustin Lynch Is The Grand Ole Opry's Newest Member|work=iHeart Radio|last=Snapp|first=Lauryn|date=September 19, 2018|access-date=March 8, 2019}}</ref> He co-wrote [[Brett Kissel]]'s 2018 hit "Guitars and Gasoline".<ref>{{cite web |title=We Were That Song - Brett Kissel {{!}} Releases |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/we-were-that-song-mw0003125431/releases |website=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=January 9, 2020}}</ref>

In May 2018, Lynch released the single "[[Good Girl (Dustin Lynch song)|Good Girl]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theboot.com/dustin-lynch-good-girl-listen//|title=Dustin Lynch Drops Surprise New Single, 'Good Girl' [LISTEN]|work=The Boot|last=Carr|first=Courtney|access-date=March 8, 2019}}</ref> An EP entitled ''Ridin' Roads'' was released on March 8, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.radio.com/blogs/bob-diehl/dustin-lynch-changes-course-new-ridin-roads-ep|title=Dustin Lynch Changes Course on New 'Ridin' Roads' EP|work=Radio.com|last=Diehl|first=Bob|date=11 March 2019|access-date=March 13, 2019}}</ref> Its title track was released to country radio, and reached number one on the Country Airplay in 2020. It would also become the second single from his fourth studio album, ''[[Tullahoma (album)|Tullahoma]]'', which was released on January 17, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theboot.com/dustin-lynch-tullahoma-album/|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20191106222303/https://theboot.com/dustin-lynch-tullahoma-album/|archive-date = 2019-11-06|title = Everything We Know About Dustin Lynch's New Album, 'Tullahoma'}}</ref> The album's third single "[[Momma's House]]" was released to country radio on February 3, 2020.<ref name="NashCountry">{{cite web |last=Casey|first=Jim|title=Listen to Dustin Lynch's Fiery New Single, "Momma's House"|url=http://www.nashcountrydaily.com/2020/01/15/listen-to-dustin-lynchs-fiery-new-single-mommas-house/|work=Nash Country Daily|date=January 15, 2020}}</ref> "Red Dirt, Blue Eyes" was released to country radio in Australia as the third single on March 30, 2020.<ref name="MPE"/> A re-recorded version of "Thinking 'Bout You" featuring [[MacKenzie Porter]] was released to country radio in May 2021.<ref>{{cite news|title= Dustin Lynch Teams With MacKenzie Porter for a Conversational New Version of 'Thinking 'Bout You' [Listen]|first=Carena|last=Liptak|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/dustin-lynch-mackenzie-porter-thinking-bout-you/|work=Taste of Country|date=March 20, 2021}}</ref>

Ahead of his fifth studio album, Lynch released the promotional single "Tequila on a Boat" featuring [[Chris Lane]], on May 14, 2021.<ref>[https://youtube.com/watch?v=ONd1XO_AWiA Dustin Lynch - Tequila On A Boat (feat. Chris Lane) — Official Music Video]</ref>
