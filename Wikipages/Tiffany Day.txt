'''Tiffany Day''' was an Australian [[judoka]], who played for the extra-lightweight category.<ref>{{cite sports-reference|title = Tiffany Day|url = https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/da/tiffany-day-1.html|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20200418104816/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/da/tiffany-day-1.html|url-status = dead|archive-date = 18 April 2020|access-date = 29 January 2013}}</ref> She was a 2007 Australian judo champion for her weight division, and a member of Pittsworth Judo Club, under her personal coach Patrick Mahoon.

Day represented [[Australia]] at the [[2008 Summer Olympics]] in [[Beijing]], where she competed for the [[Judo at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's 48 kg|women's extra-lightweight]] class (48&nbsp;kg). She received a bye for the second preliminary round match, before losing out to Argentina's [[Paula Pareto]], who successfully scored an [[ippon]] (full point) with a [[yoko shiho gatame]] (side four quarter hold), at one minute and twenty seconds.<ref>{{cite web|title=Women's Extra Lightweight (48kg/106 lbs) Preliminaries|url=http://www.2008.nbcolympics.com/judo/resultsandschedules/rsc=JUW048402/index.html|publisher=[[NBC Olympics]]|accessdate=29 January 2013}}</ref>

Tiffany Day died unexpectedly on 9 April 2019 at the age of 29.<ref>{{cite web|title=Family’s tribute to Pittsworth Olympian after sudden death|url=https://m.qt.com.au/news/familys-tribute-to-pittsworths-olympian-after-sudd/3698569/|publisher=[[The Queensland Times]]|accessdate=16 May 2021}}</ref>
