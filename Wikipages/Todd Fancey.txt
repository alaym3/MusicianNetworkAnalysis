'''Todd George Fancey''' is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[guitarist]], [[keyboardist]], and solo artist. He is the guitarist for [[Vancouver]]-based [[indie rock]] band [[The New Pornographers]] and the bassist for the band [[Limblifter]]. Fancey is originally from [[Nova Scotia]].<ref name="amgOverview">{{cite web|last=Sendra|first=Tim|title=Fancey|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p653065|pure_url=yes}}|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=26 August 2010}}</ref>

==Early life==
Fancey was born in [[Dartmouth, Nova Scotia]], Canada.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sonicbids.com/band/fancey/ |title=About Fancey |website=Sonicbids.com |date= |accessdate=2017-04-12}}</ref>

==Solo career==
Fancey released his first solo album, ''Fancey'' on March Records in 2004. His second solo album, ''Schmancey,'' was released November 13, 2007 on [[What Are Records?]]. Fancey released his third solo album ''Love Mirage'' on January 27, 2017. His fourth album release is "County Fair" a collection of cover songs on January 26, 2018 . His third and fourth albums were self released on Stoner Disco Records.

==Television==
In 2008, Fancey participated off screen in an episode of ''[[The Office (U.S. TV series)|The Office]]'' entitled "[[Dinner Party (The Office)|Dinner Party]]". He portrayed Hunter (usually portrayed by [[Nicholas D'Agosto]]), Jan's administrative assistant who may have lost his virginity to her and wrote a song about it, although the storyline never revealed any evidence of such. The song, called 'That One Night," was co-written by Fancey, [[Gene Stupnitsky]] and [[Lee Eisenberg]]. The episode was nominated for an Emmy Award.

In April 2018 Todd was interviewed by Rolling Stone's Andy Greene Article about The Office Dinner Party episode.  <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/tv/features/the-office-john-krasinski-ed-helms-jenna-fischer-oral-history-dinner-party-episode-w518726|title = The Oral History of 'The Dinner Party,' the Best 'Office' Episode Ever|date = 7 April 2018}}</ref>

In late 2010, NBC hired Todd to write a song that was featured on NBC's Prime Time Special.  Fancey was also hired to create music for ''[[Caprica (TV series)|Caprica]]'', the ''[[Battlestar Galactica (2004 TV series)|Battlestar Galactica]]'' TV show prequel.{{Citation needed|date=March 2011}}
