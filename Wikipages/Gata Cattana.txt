'''Gata Cattana''', stage name of '''Ana Isabel García Llorente''' (11 May 1991 – 2 March 2017), was a Spanish rapper, poet and feminist.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://elpais.com/cultura/2019/03/22/actualidad/1553255193_198064.html|title=El último aliento de Gata Cattana|date=22 March 2020|publisher=[[El País]]|access-date=30 April 2020|language=es}}</ref>

In her career she managed to combine [[hip hop]] and [[electronic music]] with various themes and styles such as [[feminism]] and politics. Her lyrics referenced to [[Andalusia]]n culture, to the mythological ''[[Literary topos|topoi]]'' of the [[ancient age]] – especially Greek, Roman and [[ancient Egyptian]] literature – as well as poetry, philosophy and mass culture; her recurring themes included [[introspection]], [[dialectical materialism]], [[political andalusism]], [[anti-globalization]], [[existentialism]] and [[socialist feminism]].<ref name=pais>{{cite news|url=http://elpais.com/elpais/2017/01/16/tentaciones/1484544289_651436.html|title=La última gran esperanza de las mujeres en el rap: Gata Cattana|date=16 January 2017|publisher=El País|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://i-d.vice.com/es_es/article/gata-cattana-entrevista |title=Conoce a Gata Cattana, la poetisa que hace rap para la eternidad|date=14 February 2017|website=Vice|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.eldiario.es/cultura/musica/rapera-Gata-Cattana-muere-anos_0_618038921.html|title=La rapera Gata Cattana muere a los 26 años|date=2 March 2017|website=eldiario.es|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite video|title=Hablamos con GATA CATTANA ¿Rapera, trapera o poeta? #WAG1MEETS|publisher=WAG1 MAGAZINE|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NNIPnAkrSkk|date=6 February 2017|language=es}}</ref>
