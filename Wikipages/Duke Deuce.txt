'''Patavious Lashun Isom''' (born May 1, 1992), better known by his stage name '''Duke Deuce''', is an American rapper from [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]], [[Tennessee]]. Currently signed to [[Quality Control Music]], he is best known for his song "Crunk Ain't Dead", which received a remix with fellow Memphis rappers [[Juicy J]] (whom also produced the song) and [[Project Pat]], as well as [[Lil Jon]]. He released his debut studio album, ''Duke Nukem'', on February 26, 2021, which debuted at number three on the [[Top Heatseekers|''Billboard'' Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/duke-deuce-interview-the-break/|title=The Break Presents: Duke Deuce}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Duke Deuce Releases 'Duke Nukem' Album f/ Offset, ASAP Ferg, Lil Keed, and More|url=https://www.complex.com/music/duke-deuce-nukem-album|url-status=live|access-date=April 30, 2021|website=Complex}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Duke Deuce Announces New Album Duke Nukem, Shares New Song "Spin": Listen|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/duke-deuce-announces-new-album-duke-nukem-shares-new-song-spin-listen/|url-status=live|access-date=April 30, 2021|website=Pitchfork|date=22 February 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
Isom rose to fame with his single "Yeh", but gained more recognition with his track "Crunk Ain't Dead", which received a remix featuring [[Lil Jon]], [[Juicy J]] and [[Project Pat]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Here's Duke Deuce's "Crunk Ain't Dead" Remix f/ Juicy J, Project Pat, and Lil Jon|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/02/duke-deuce-juicy-j-lil-jon-project-pat-crunk-aint-dead-remix|url-status=live|access-date=April 30, 2021|website=Complex}}</ref> The track later served as the lead single from his third mixtape ''Memphis Massacre 2'', which was released on February 19, 2020.

Isom's debut studio album, ''Duke Nukem'', was released on February 26, 2021, and debuted at number three on the [[Top Heatseekers|''Billboard'' Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite magazine|title=Heatseekers Albums Chart: Week of March 12, 2021|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/heatseekers-albums/2021-03-12|url-status=live|access-date=April 30, 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>
