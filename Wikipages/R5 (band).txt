'''R5''' was an American [[pop rock]] band formed in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]], in 2009.<ref name=Billboard>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5719236/r5/biography|title=R5 - Biography|publisher=Billboard}}</ref> 
The band consisted of [[Ross Lynch]] (lead vocals/rhythm guitar), [[Riker Lynch]] (bass guitar/vocals), Rocky Lynch (lead guitar/vocals), [[Rydel Lynch]] (keyboard/vocals), and [[Ellington Ratliff]] (drums/vocals). They released their first EP ''Ready Set Rock'' on March 9th, 2010, but it was removed once Ross got his role on the Disney Channel Show ''[[Austin & Ally|Austin and Ally]].'' They then released the EP ''Loud'' on February 19th, 2013 [https://www.amazon.com/Loud-Ep-R5/dp/B00CLZ4JJK] Since releasing their debut album, ''[[Louder (R5 album)|Louder]]'', on September 24, 2013, which peaked at 24 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], followed by their world tour. Their second album, ''[[Sometime Last Night]]'', was released on July 10, 2015, and debuted at 6 on the US ''Billboard 200'', and reached the top 20 in five other countries. They have also released five EPs, eleven singles, and a documentary called ''R5: All Day, All Night'' that was first released in theaters April 15, 2015.<ref name="IMDB">{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4621790/|title=R5: All Day, All Night Documentary|date=June 2, 2015|access-date=7 December 2019|website=IMDB}}</ref> 

In 2018, it was announced that Ross and Rocky would be pursuing a different project under the name [[The Driver Era]]. All of the band's social media accounts under the handle "R5" were changed to "The Driver Era." Only Ross and Rocky are in The Driver Era.

==History==

===2009–2011: Formation and ''Ready Set Rock''===
[[File:Paparazzo Presents R5.jpg|thumb|230px|left|Rocky, Ross and Riker at R5's first East Coast show in 2012 (New Jersey)|alt=]]
R5 is made up of brothers Riker Lynch, Rocky Lynch, Ross Lynch, and their sister Rydel Lynch, all born and raised in [[Littleton, Colorado]], and their family friend Ellington Ratliff, whom they met at a California dance studio called the Rage.<ref name="teeninkfeb2012">[http://www.teenink.com/nonfiction/celebrity_interviews/article/388321/SingersActors--Riker-and-Rydel-Lynch-of-R5/ "Singers/Actors – Riker and Rydel Lynch of R5,"] Teen Ink, February 1, 2012.</ref> About a year after meeting, they asked Ratliff to join the band.<ref>Jeff Niesel, [http://www.clevescene.com/scene-and-heard/archives/2016/02/29/r5-opts-for-a-more-mature-sound-on-its-latest-album-sometime-last-night "R5 Opts for a More Mature Sound on its Latest Album, 'Sometime Last Night',"] ''[[Cleveland Scene]]'', February 29, 2016.</ref> While living in Colorado, the Lynch siblings went to a performing arts school. They used to put on shows for their family and charged them at least a dollar to watch them perform in their basement.<ref name="teeninkfeb2012"/> At age 16, Riker decided he wanted to move to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career. Their parents, Mark and Stormie, moved the entire family there in order to stay together. Shortly after the move, the siblings began appearing in commercials, while Rocky began teaching himself, Ross and Riker how to play guitar.<ref name="sweetyhigh.com">[https://www.sweetyhigh.com/read/happy-birthday-ross-lynch "Happy Birthday Ross Lynch + A Guide To R5,"] sweetyhigh.com, December 29, 2013.</ref>

In 2009, R5 officially started the band and debuted their [[YouTube]] web series, ''R5 TV'', documenting the band's day-to-day life and career beginning.<ref>Brittany Goldfield Rodrigues, [http://www.andpop.com/2016/06/15/r5-covers/ "10 Covers By R5 That Give Us A Lot Of Feelings,"] andpop.com, June 15, 2016.</ref> On September 5, they released their first music video, "Without You", directed by Riker.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mi3LRta-Z8g|title=R5 - Without You (Official video)|publisher=Riker Lynch Channel|access-date= January 12, 2013}}</ref> The band self-released a five-track EP entitled ''Ready Set Rock'' on March 9, 2010, consisting of songs written primarily by Riker, Rocky, Rydel, vocal coach Mauli B, and band coach E-Vega, who produced the EP.<ref name="R5sitioweb">{{cite web|url=http://r5rocks.com/band |title=About |publisher=R5Rocks.com |access-date= May 7, 2012}}</ref> No official single was released from EP.<ref>Rachel Ho, [http://www.starpulse.com/ready-set-r5-1848319070.html "Ready, Set… R5,"] StarPulse, January 8, 2013.</ref> On September 4 they released a music video for "Can't Get Enough of You" and, in the next month, for "Never".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qVU5hV8py-E|title=R5 - Can't Get Enough of You (Official video)|publisher=Riker Lynch Channel|access-date= January 12, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8QI-zVRsYCk|title=R5 - Never (Official video)|publisher=Riker Lynch Channel|access-date= January 12, 2013}}</ref> On July 26, 2011, the band released their debut promotional single, "Say You'll Stay".<ref>{{cite web| url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/say-youll-stay-acoustic-version/id453034405|title=Say You'll Stay (Acoustic Version) – Single – R5| publisher = [[iTunes Store]]|access-date= January 12, 2013}}</ref> The music video was released on August 3.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GG0raOJCWX0|title=R5 - Say You'll Stay (Official video)|publisher=Riker Lynch Channel|access-date= January 12, 2013}}</ref> During these early years they played sets throughout Southern California at venues, including the [[Orange County Fair (California)|Orange County Fair]], the [[San Diego County Fair]], the San Diego IndieFest, the [[Knitting Factory]], and [[Six Flags Magic Mountain]].

===2014–2016: ''Heart Made Up On You'' and ''Sometime Last Night''===
[[File:R5 Kicks Back with Walmart Soundcheck (1).jpg|thumb|left|270px|R5 members in 2014]]

Some of the bonus tracks from the deluxe edition of ''Sometime Last Night'' were tracks intended for the original album.<ref name="augusta"/><ref name="ggraff"/><ref name="kmiyasato"/> On November 15, 2014, the band released the album's lead single, "[[Smile (R5 song)|Smile]]", and performed the song at the [[American Music Awards]] pre-show.

===2017–2018: ''New Addictions'' and hiatus===
[[File:R5 New Addictions Tour Monterrey 10.jpg|thumb|right|200px|R5 performing in 2017]]
On March 16, 2017, Riker announced that the band was recording new songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://popmania.com/riker-lynch-r5-new-album-2017/|title= A Chat with Riker Lynch of R5 |publisher=Pop Mania|access-date=24 October 2016}}</ref> On April 5, they announced their fourth EP, ''[[New Addictions]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://r5rocks.com/store/12532/new-addictions-pre-release-listening-party|title=New Addictions Pre-release Listening Party|publisher=R5 Rocks|access-date=24 October 2016}}</ref> which was released on May 12, 2017.<ref>"R5's 'New Addictions' Officially Has a Release Date -- Fans React," [[Just Jared Jr.]], April 24, 2017.</ref><ref>[http://glittermagrocks.com/connect/2017/04/20/new-addictions/ New Addictions], ''Glitter'', April 20, 2017.</ref> The five-song EP includes the single "If", the original songs "Red Velvet", "Lay Your Head Down" and "Trading Time", and "[[Need You Tonight]]", a cover of the [[INXS]] single from their 1987 album ''[[Kick (INXS album)|Kick]]''. Short after the release of New Addictions, the new single Hurts Good was released<ref>Miruna Radu, [http://www.theyorker.co.uk/arts-and-culture/music/features/weekly-playlist-8th-may-14th-may/ "The Weekly Playlist (8th May – 14th May),"] ''The Yorker'', May 14, 2017.</ref><ref>Markos Papadatos, [http://www.digitaljournal.com/entertainment/music/review-r5-delivers-on-new-ep-new-addictions/article/492527 "Review: R5 delivers on new EP 'New Addictions',"] Digital Journal, May 13, 2017.</ref> The band also announced their [[List of R5 concert tours#New Addictions Tour|New Addictions Tour]], a US tour which begins on June 17, 2017 and toured to Mexico, Europe, South America, and other countries.

On February 1, 2018, R5 posted on their YouTube a video called The Last Show. Then on March 1, 2018, R5's Instagram and Twitter handles were changed to [[The Driver Era]] and all of R5's previous posts were deleted.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BfzIzrPHV4G |title=thedriverera
 |access-date=March 25, 2020}}}}</ref> On March 2, 2018, they announced that the band was going on a temporary hiatus and that [[The Driver Era]] would be a band with only Ross and Rocky.<ref>{{cite web|first=Kevin|last=Rutherford|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8265892/driver-era-r5-billboard-chart-debut|title=The Driver Era, Featuring Ex-Members of R5, Makes Chart Debut With 'Preacher Man'|publisher=Billboard|date=March 28, 2018|access-date=April 1, 2018}}</ref>

==Musical influences==
The band has cited [[The Beatles]], [[Fall Out Boy]], [[The Rolling Stones]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Bruce Springsteen]], [[Elvis Presley]], [[The Beach Boys]], [[INXS]], [[Jimi Hendrix]], [[Led Zeppelin]], [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] and [[Queen (band)|Queen]] as big musical influences. They have also mentioned more recent artists, including [[McFly]], [[The Killers]], [[Neon Trees]], [[Walk the Moon]], [[Maroon 5]], [[Jack White]], [[Bruno Mars]] and [[The Script]].<ref name="kmiyasato">Kiko Miyasato, [http://lasvegasmagazine.com/entertainment/2015/dec/25/r5-the-venetian/#/0 "R5: Young and the Restless,"] ''Las Vegas Magazine'', December 25, 2015.</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/lifestyle/family/exclusive-interview-band-r5-including-3278144|title=Exclusive: Interview with the band R5 including Disney Channel's Austin & Ally star Ross Lynch|work=Mirror|access-date=April 14, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.popstaronline.com/25-facts-on-ross-lynch/|title=25 Facts On Ross Lynch|work=Popstar Magazine|access-date=April 14, 2014}}</ref><ref>Jonathon Moran, [http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/sydney-confidential/r5-channel-inxs-on-tour-and-ross-lynchreveals-how-much-he-loves-australia/news-story/72c3a908198ee60fd0ae0224054ecda1 "R5 channel INXS on tour and Ross Lynch reveals how much he loves Australia,"] ''[[The Daily Telegraph (Sydney)|The Daily Telegraph]]'', January 18, 2016.</ref>
