'''Olivia Hastings Holt'''<ref name=video>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m4g2Ds8wkDg&feature=autoplay&list=UUg9wsCh4F0i4wt54BTAtVGw&playnext=5|title=Olivia Holt ~ Child Like Faith|access-date=October 5, 2014}}</ref> (born August 5, 1997<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hollywood.com/celebrities/olivia-holt-58287475/ |title=Olivia Holt |website=Hollywood.com |access-date=January 31, 2017}}</ref><ref name=medianet/>) is an American actress and singer. She starred in the [[Disney XD]] series ''[[Kickin' It]]'', [[Disney Channel Original Movie]] ''[[Girl vs. Monster]]'', and the [[Disney Channel Original Series]] ''[[I Didn't Do It (TV series)|I Didn't Do It]]''. From 2018 to 2019, she portrayed the titular role [[Cloak and Dagger (comics)|Tandy Bowen / Dagger]] in the [[Freeform (TV channel)|Freeform]] series ''[[Cloak & Dagger (TV series)|Cloak & Dagger]]''. In 2021, she portrayed Kate Wallis in ''[[Cruel Summer (TV series)|Cruel Summer]]''.

Holt had voice roles in the films ''[[Tinker Bell and the Legend of the NeverBeast]]'' (2014) and ''[[Gnome Alone]]'' (2017), and starred in the feature films ''[[Class Rank (film)|Class Rank]]'' (2017), ''[[Same Kind of Different as Me (film)|Same Kind of Different as Me]]'' (2017), and ''[[Status Update]]'' (2018). Her debut EP, ''[[Olivia (EP)|Olivia]]'', was released on [[Hollywood Records]] on July 15, 2016.

==Early life==
Holt was born in [[Germantown, Tennessee]], to Mark and Kim Holt. She has two siblings.<ref name="jbeifuss"/> At age three, her family moved to [[Nesbit, Mississippi]], where she grew up after briefly living in [[Memphis, Tennessee]].<ref name="jbeifuss">{{cite web|author=John Beifuss |url=http://www.commercialappeal.com/go-memphis/got-kicks |title=DeSoto County's Olivia Holt is the breakout star on Disney's martial-arts 'tween' comedy |publisher=[[The Commercial Appeal]] |access-date=February 26, 2012}}</ref> Growing up, she took gymnastics classes for seven years.<ref>[http://www.seventeen.com/celebrity/a17758/olivia-holt-kickin-it-interview/ "Get to Know Kickin' It's Olivia Holt,"] ''[[Seventeen (American magazine)|Seventeen]]'', March 6, 2012.</ref> In 2011, she moved to [[Los Angeles]] with her family.<ref name=medianet>{{cite web |url=http://www.disneyxdmedianet.com/web/showpage/showpage.aspx?program_id=900078&type=holt |title=Disney XD Medianet |publisher=Disney XD Medianet |year=2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140924114420/http://www.disneyxdmedianet.com/web/showpage/showpage.aspx?program_id=900078&type=holt |archive-date=September 24, 2014 |access-date=February 26, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="jbeifuss"/> She graduated from [[Oak Park High School (California)|Oak Park High School]] in 2015.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.beautifulballad.org/2015/06/12/186624/|title=Zendaya, Olivia Holt and Austin North Graduate High School|date=June 12, 2015|newspaper=Beautifulballad}}</ref>

===Television===
Holt began her acting career in local theater productions.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kidzworld.com/article/26115-olivia-holt-qanda |title=Olivia Holt Q&A |website=Kidzworld.com |access-date=February 26, 2012}}</ref> She started appearing in television commercials at the age of 10, for products including [[Hasbro]], ''[[Kidz Bop 14]]'', [[Mattel]], [[Bratz]] dolls, [[Littlest Pet Shop]], and Girl Gourmet.<ref>{{cite web |author=John Beifuss |url=http://www.scrippsnationalnews.com/web/mca/news/local-news/olivias-big-break |title=Role on Disney TV show a shot at stardom for DeSoto girl |publisher=[[The Commercial Appeal]] |date=December 24, 2010 |access-date=November 8, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150925095052/http://www.scrippsnationalnews.com/web/mca/news/local-news/olivias-big-break |archive-date=September 25, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>[http://www.disney.co.uk/disney-channel/access-all-areas/olivia-holt/interview/ "Teen Queen!"] Disney Channel. Accessed July 14, 2016.</ref>

Possessing skills in gymnastics, Holt was cast in [[Disney XD]]'s martial arts comedy ''[[Kickin' It]]'', which premiered on June 13, 2011.<ref name="ppicardi">Phillip Picardi (March 2, 2016), [http://www.teenvogue.com/story/olivia-holt-neutrogena-interview-new-album "You're About to See Olivia Holt Like You've NEVER Seen Her Before,"] ''[[Teen Vogue]]''.</ref> She played the role of Kim in the series, as a main character in seasons 1–3, and as a special guest on three episodes in season 4.<ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/video/exclusive-kim-comes-back-on-disney-xds-kickin-it/ "Exclusive: Kim Comes Back On Disney XD's Kickin' It!"] ''[[Tiger Beat]]'', August 4, 2014.</ref> She starred in the 2012 [[Disney Channel Original Movie]] ''[[Girl vs. Monster]]'', playing the lead character Skylar Lewis, a teenager who discovers that her family have long worked as monster hunters, and she is next in line.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.deadline.com/2012/03/disney-channel-sets-its-next-original-movie/ |title=Disney Channel Sets Its Next Original Movie |author=Nellie Andreeva |work=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |date=March 12, 2012}}</ref> She left ''Kickin' It'' as a regular after season 3 to star in the [[Disney Channel]] half-hour comedy series ''[[I Didn't Do It (TV series)|I Didn't Do It]]'', which premiered on January 17, 2014. ===Film===
In November 2014, Holt joined the cast of the drama ''[[Same Kind of Different as Me (film)|Same Kind of Different as Me]]'', directed by Michael Carney and also starring [[Greg Kinnear]] and [[Renée Zellweger]],<ref name="rford">Rebecca Ford (November 7, 2014), [https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/disney-channel-star-olivia-holt-747400 "Disney Channel Star Olivia Holt Joins 'Same Kind of Different as Me',"] ''The Hollywood Reporter''.</ref> who play her character's parents in the film.<ref name="edenton">Elizabeth Denton, [http://www.seventeen.com/beauty/celeb-beauty/news/a38610/olivia-holt-on-dealing-with-acne-confidence-and-ignoring-the-haters/ "Olivia Holt on Dealing with Acne, Ignoring the Haters and Staying True to Yourself,"] ''Seventeen'', March 9, 2016.</ref> The film was released in October 2017.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://variety.com/2017/film/reviews/same-kind-of-different-as-me-review-1202595639/ |title=Film Review: 'Same Kind of Different as Me' |last=Nicholson |first=Amy |date=October 20, 2017 |website=variety.com |access-date=April 6, 2019}}</ref> In November 2015, it was announced that Holt had been cast in a lead role in the indie comedy ''[[Class Rank (film)|Class Rank]]'', directed by [[Eric Stoltz]].<ref>Ross A. Lincoln (November 20, 2015), [https://deadline.com/2015/11/eric-stoltz-directed-indie-class-rank-casts-olivia-holt-in-lead-role-1201632260/ "Olivia Holt To Star In Indie 'Class Rank' Directed By Eric Stoltz,"] ''[[Deadline Hollywood]]''.</ref> In May 2016, it was announced that she would star alongside [[Ross Lynch]] in ''[[Status Update]]'', directed by [[Scott Speer]], with filming to commence the following month in Vancouver, Canada.<ref>Pamela McClintock (May 2, 2016), [https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/cannes-disney-channel-stars-ross-889445 "Cannes: Disney Channel Stars Ross Lynch, Olivia Holt Cast in 'Status Update',"] ''The Hollywood Reporter''.</ref><ref name="jmaldonado">Jennifer Maldonado (June 24, 2016), [http://www.m-magazine.com/posts/watch-olivia-holt-s-music-video-for-phoenix-106254 "Watch Olivia Holt's Music Video for 'Phoenix',"] ''[[M Magazine]]''.</ref>

===Music===
Holt recorded three songs for the 2012 film ''Girl vs. Monster'', in which she also starred. The songs were featured on the compilation ''[[Make Your Mark: Ultimate Playlist]]''. She won a [[2013 Radio Disney Music Awards|2013 Radio Disney Music Award]] for Best Crush Song for "Had Me @ Hello", which was featured in the film.<ref name="png"/> She also recorded a cover version of "[[Winter Wonderland]]" for the 2012 ''[[Disney Channel Holiday Playlist]]'' album.<ref>[http://abc7chicago.com/archive/8887937/ "Disney XD's 'Kickin' It' Star Olivia Holt on WCL,"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180706174701/http://abc7chicago.com/archive/8887937/ |date=July 6, 2018 }} Windy City Live. Accessed July 14, 2016.</ref> Her song "Carry On" was the theme song for the 2014 [[Disneynature]] film ''[[Bears (film)|Bears]]''.<ref name="rford"/>

In October 2014, Holt signed a record deal with [[Hollywood Records]].<ref name=Popstar>{{cite web|url=http://www.popstaronline.com/olivia-holt-signs-to-hollywood-records/|title=Olivia Holt Signs to Hollywood Records!|publisher=[[Popstar!|Popstar! Magazine]]|date=October 7, 2014|access-date=October 9, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141011000810/http://www.popstaronline.com/olivia-holt-signs-to-hollywood-records/|archive-date=October 11, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> Her debut single, "Phoenix", came out on May 13, 2016. The music video was released on June 23, 2016.<ref>Samantha Simon (May 20, 2016), [http://www.instyle.com/reviews-coverage/music/olivia-holt-facebook-live-phoenix-single "Olivia Holt Talks Music, Mascara, Pizza Toppings, and More,"] ''[[InStyle]]''.</ref><ref>Mariah Haas (May 13, 2016), [http://www.people.com/article/olivia-holt-phoenix-song "Olivia Holt Is 'Super Stoked for Everyone to Hear' Her New Single 'Phoenix',"] ''[[People (magazine)|People]]''.</ref> ''[[Olivia (EP)|Olivia]]'', her debut EP, was released on July 15, 2016.<ref name="jmaldonado"/><ref name="khkowalski">Kristine Hope Kowalski (June 24, 2016), [http://www.twistmagazine.com/posts/olivia-holt-reveals-her-debut-ep-s-release-date-fierce-cover-art-106282 "Olivia Holt Reveals Her Debut EP's Release Date + Cover Art,"] ''[[Twist (magazine)|Twist]]''.</ref>

In 2016, Holt went on her first tour, the Rise of a Phoenix Tour, with Ryland Lynch, [[Isac Elliot]] and [[Forever in Your Mind]].<ref>Sarah Lane (November 16, 2016), [https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/going-out-guide-for-northern-virginia-nov-17-23-2016/2016/11/15/680907a0-a608-11e6-ba46-53db57f0e351_story.html "Going Out Guide for Northern Virginia, Nov. 17–23, 2016,"] ''[[The Washington Post]]''.</ref> She and Forever in Your Mind also performed at the 2016 [[T.J. Martell Foundation|TJ Martell]] Family Foundation Day in Los Angeles on October 9, 2016.<ref>"Olivia Holt & Forever In Your Mind To Perform at TJ Martell's Family Day 2016," Just Jared Jr, September 23, 2016.</ref>

In April 2017, she was picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] appearing on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'' show with [[Hoda Kotb]] and [[Kathie Lee Gifford]] performing her hit "History". Her single "Generous", which was written by [[Fransisca Hall]] and [[MoZella]], was released on September 22, 2017. The song's music video was directed by Chris Applebaum.<ref>Mike Wass, [https://www.idolator.com/7668605/olivia-holt-new-single-generous "Olivia Holt Follows Up 'History' With New Single 'Generous',"] Idolator, September 12, 2017.</ref> "Generous" reached number 1 on the Billboard [[Dance Club Songs]] chart.<ref name="charthistory"/> In 2018, she collaborated with [[Martin Jensen (DJ)|Martin Jensen]] on "16 Steps"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8465834/martin-jensen-olivia-holt-16-steps-video|title=Martin Jensen & Olivia Holt Find Mood In The Desert In '16 Steps' Video: Watch|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Kat|last=Bein|date=July 17, 2018}}</ref> and with [[Nicky Romero]] on "Distance".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8494821/nicky-romero-distance-olivia-holt-hakkasan-group-residency|first=Kat|last=Bein|title=Nicky Romero Drops 'Distance' With Olivia Holt, Announces Hakkasan Group Residency: Exclusive |website=Billboard|access-date=January 25, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190125120636/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8494821/nicky-romero-distance-olivia-holt-hakkasan-group-residency|archive-date=January 25, 2019}}</ref> The same year, she released the single "Bad Girlfriend".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.idolator.com/7888879/olivia-holt-is-back-with-a-catchy-banger-called-bad-girlfriend|title=Olivia Holt Is Back With A Catchy Banger Called "Bad Girlfriend"|website=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|first=Mike|last=Wassdate=September 27, 2019|date=September 27, 2019}}</ref>

Her 2020 single "Love U Again" featuring [[R3hab]] reached number 40 on the Billboard [[Hot Dance/Electronic Songs|Hot Dance / Electronic Songs]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Wass |first=Mike |date=August 28, 2020 |title=Olivia Holt & R3HAB drop new single 'Love u again' |url=https://www.idolator.com/7913584/olivia-holt-r3hab-tease-new-single-love-u-again?adblock=1&firefox=1 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200914182921/https://www.idolator.com/7913584/olivia-holt-r3hab-tease-new-single-love-u-again |archive-date=September 15, 2020 |access-date=September 15, 2020 |website=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]}}</ref><ref name="charthistory">{{cite web |title=Olivia Holt Chart History |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/olivia-holt |website=Billboard |access-date=6 May 2021}}</ref> It was followed by "Take Me Out of It" later that year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/talk-me-out-of-it-mw0003466216|title=Talk Me Out of It - Olivia Holt|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=June 23, 2021}}</ref> Through early 2021 she released the singles "Do You Miss Me"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ntdiario.com/nota/2520/olivia-holt-estrena-do-you-miss-me|title=Olivia Holt estrena Do You Miss Me|work=NT Diario|date=February 26, 2021|language=es}}</ref> and "Love On You".<ref name="ET">{{cite web|url=https://www.etonline.com/olivia-holt-on-her-next-musical-era-and-cruel-summer-season-2-exclusive-167646|title=Olivia Holt on Her 'Next' Musical Era and ''Cruel Summer'' Season 2 (Exclusive) |website=[[ET Online]]|first=Philiana|last=Ng|date=June 21, 2021}}</ref> She also covered [[The Smashing Pumpkins]] song "[[Today (The Smashing Pumpkins song)|Today]]" for the TV series in which she stars ''[[Cruel Summer (TV series)|Cruel Summer]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Knight |first1=Rosie |title=There's Meaning In Every '90s Song You've Heard On Cruel Summer |url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2021/04/10445104/cruel-summer-season-1-soundtrack-90s-songs#slide-37 |access-date=8 June 2021 |publisher=Refinery 29 |date=2 June 2021}}</ref> On June 11, 2021, a compilation EP called "In My Feelings" was released, containing her previous released singles. On June 25, 2021, she released the single "Next", co-written with [[Meghan Trainor]].<ref name="ET"/>

===Endorsements===
In 2013, Holt partnered with mobile gaming system maker PlayMG as a spokesperson and part owner, to help the company develop new versions of its app-based games.<ref>Marc Graser (July 17, 2013), [https://variety.com/2013/digital/news/playmg-turns-to-disney-star-olivia-holt-to-promote-mobile-gaming-to-kids-1200564008/ "PlayMG Turns to Disney Star Olivia Holt to Promote Mobile Gaming to Kids,"] ''Variety''.</ref> She was announced as a brand ambassador for [[Neutrogena]] in March 2016.<ref name="ppicardi"/><ref name="edenton"/>
