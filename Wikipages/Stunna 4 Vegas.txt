'''Khalick<ref name="bmi"/> Antonio Caldwell''' (born January 1, 1996), better known as '''Stunna 4 Vegas''' (formerly stylized as '''$tunna 4 Vegas'''), is an American rapper. He is currently signed to fellow North Carolina rapper [[DaBaby]]'s label Billion Dollar Baby Entertainment. His most popular singles are "Animal", which featured DaBaby, and "Up The Smoke" featuring [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]].

Other hit singles include "Ashley", also featuring DaBaby, "Tomorrow" featuring [[Moneybagg Yo]], and "Do Dat" featuring DaBaby and [[Lil Baby]]. His debut album, ''[[Big 4x|BIG 4X]]'', was released on May 10, 2019. It peaked at number 50 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. His second studio album, ''[[Rich Youngin]]'', was released on January 17, 2020. It debuted at number 29 on the ''Billboard'' 200.

He is cousins with [[Philadelphia Eagles]] defensive tackle [[Javon Hargrave]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Alper|first=Josh|date=March 16, 2020|title=Stunna 4 Vegas Reveals Which Cousin Plays In NFL|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ySR_mv5ZzK0|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>

==Career==
===2018: "Animal" and signing to DaBaby===
In late September 2018, Caldwell released the track "Animal", which featured fellow North Carolina rapper [[DaBaby]]. The track gained popularity after it was posted on DaBaby's channel. Caldwell signed to DaBaby's record label Billion Dollar Baby Entertainment, and was featured on his track "4X" produced by Producer 20 (Igot20onmybeat) from his November 2018 mixtape ''Blank Blank''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/who-is-23-year-old-rapper-stunna-4-vegas-signed-to-dababy|title=In Search Of: North Carolina Rapper Stunna 4 Vegas Has Proven Himself Beyond DaBaby's Cosign|last=|first=|date=|website=Genius|access-date=}}</ref>

===2019: Further success and debut album===
Caldwell was featured on DaBaby's track "Joggers" from his debut album ''[[Baby on Baby]]'' in March 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ballerstatus.com/2019/04/01/dababy-baby-on-baby-review/|title=DaBaby – "Baby On Baby" Review|last=|first=|date=April 2019|website=BallerStatus.com|access-date=}}</ref> He was then [[Guest appearance|featured]] on [[Asian Da Brat]]'s 2019 mixtape ''[[Unfuccwitable]]'', on the track "I Love It".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/asian-da-brat-drops-off-her-eighth-mixtape-unfuccwitable-new-mixtape.119097.html|title=Asian Da Brat Drops Off Her Eighth Mixtape "UNFUCCWITABLE"|last=|first=|date=|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=}}</ref>
  
In May 2019, Caldwell signed with [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://jetlagtv.com/2019/05/08/stunna-4-vegas-signs-with-interscope-records/|title=Stunna 4 Vegas signs with Interscope Records|last=|first=|date=May 8, 2019|website=Jetlag TV|access-date=}}</ref> He then released his debut album under the label, ''BIG 4X'', on May 10, 2019. The album contained guest features from [[DaBaby]], [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]], [[NLE Choppa]], [[Young Nudy]], & [[Lil Durk]]. The album peaked at number 50 on the [[Billboard 200]], and received positive reviews.

In the following months, he released singles such as "Tomorrow" featuring [[Moneybagg Yo]], "Flintstones" featuring BannUpPrince, "Boat 4 Vegas" featuring [[Lil Yachty]], "Up The Smoke" with [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]], and "Long." He revealed the album's title during an interview with music website Groovy Tracks. In September 2019, he was featured on the track "Really" from DaBaby's second studio album ''[[Kirk (album)|Kirk]]''. The song debuted and peaked at number 63 on the [[Billboard Hot 100]], making it Caldwell's first entry on the Hot 100.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2019/10/dababy-kirk-tracks-chart-on-billboard-hot-100|title=All Songs From DaBaby's 'KIRK' Charts on Billboard Hot 100|last=|first=|date=|website=Hypebeast|access-date=}}</ref>

===2020: ''Rich Youngin''===
On January 17, 2020, Caldwell released his second studio album, ''Rich Youngin''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.54167/title.stunna-4-vegas-delivers-rich-youngin-album|title=Stunna 4 Vegas Delivers 'Rich Youngin' Album|date=January 16, 2020|website=HipHopDX}}</ref> It included features from [[DaBaby]], [[Lil Baby]], [[Blac Youngsta]], and [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]]. It peaked at number 29 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="BB200 RY">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/billboardcharts/status/1221980528175349760|title=Billboard Charts on Twitter: "Debuts on this week's #Billboard200 (1/2)"|access-date=January 28, 2020}}</ref> The album received lukewarm reviews from critics.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.albumoftheyear.org/album/207654-stunna-4-vegas-rich-youngin.php|title=Stunna 4 Vegas RICH YOUNGIN|work=Album of the Year|access-date=March 24, 2020}}</ref>

In February 2020, Caldwell earned his first ''Billboard''-charting single as a lead artist and his second charting single overall, with "[[Go Stupid]]" debuting and peaking at number 60 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/billboardcharts/status/1232041978319249409|title=Billboard Charts on Twitter: ".@Polo_Capalot's "Go Stupid," featuring @Nlechoppa1 & @Stunna4Vegas_, debuts at No. 60 on this week's #Hot100." / Twitter}}</ref>
On July 27, 2020, Stunna 4 Vegas released a collaboration with [[DaBaby]] titled "No Dribble".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/07/dababy-stunna-4-vegas-no-dribble-video|title=DaBaby and Stunna 4 Vegas Release Latest Video and Single "No Dribble|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|author=FNR TIGG|date=July 27, 2020|access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref> Later on, in November 2020, Caldwell released his third studio album, ''Welcome to 4 Vegas'', which included features from DaBaby, [[Murda Beatz]], Toosii, and Ola Runt and did not chart.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Stunna 4 Vegas drops off new album 'Welcome To 4 Vegas'|url=https://www.revolt.tv/new-music/2020/11/27/21722015/stunna-4-vegas-welcome-to-4-vegas-video|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 23, 2020|website=Respect}}</ref>
