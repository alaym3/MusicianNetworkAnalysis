'''Don Broco''' is a British [[rock music|rock]] band formed in [[Bedford]], England, in 2008. The band consists of Rob Damiani (lead vocals), Simon Delaney (guitar), Tom Doyle (bass and programing) and Matt Donnelly (drums, lead and backing vocals). The band have released four studio albums: ''[[Priorities (album)|Priorities]]'' (2012), ''[[Automatic (Don Broco album)|Automatic]]'' (2015), ''[[Technology (Don Broco album)|Technology]]'' (2018) and ''[[Amazing Things (Don Broco album)|Amazing Things]]'' (2021).

==History==

===Formation (2008–2010)===
The band's origins go back to before their university years during secondary school, attending [[Bedford Modern School]], where they played their first gigs, but it was not until after studying at [[University of Nottingham|Nottingham University]] that they decided to become a band.<ref name="Interview with Burmingham Live 2013">{{cite web|title=Interview – Don Broco|url=http://www.brumlive.com/interview-don-broco/|work=Brumlive.com|publisher=Burmingham Live|access-date=10 January 2014|author=Lucy Pryor|quote=Once we went to University we weren’t a band then...}}</ref>

The band initially took on many different names, including "Summer fall" and "Club Sex".<ref name="Don Broco 2013 interview with Contact Music">{{cite web|title=Don Broco - Interview|url=http://www.contactmusic.com/interview/don-broco-interview-2013-oct|work=Contactmusic.com|publisher=Contact Music|access-date=10 January 2014|author=Harry Hawcroft|quote=...'Club Sex Mix' - an old name of our band that didn't stick...}}</ref> One of the group's ideas was "Don Loco", which was changed to Don Broco following guitarist Simon Delaney breaking his wrist in a football accident.<ref name="Red Bull Interview 2013">{{cite web|title=Don Broco interview: What do You Wanna Know?|url=http://www.redbull.com/uk/en/music/stories/1331615190263/don-broco-twitter-interview|work=Redbull.com|publisher=Red Bull|access-date=10 January 2014|author=Glen Ferris|quote=@liam_fennessy: Why are you called Don Broco?}}</ref>

They first toured England in November 2008, playing gigs in places such as Leeds, Birmingham, Manchester, Swindon, Watford and County Durham amongst others.<ref name="lastfm">{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Don+Broco |title=Don Broco |publisher=Last.fm |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref>

They appeared at Camden Crawl and [[Download Festival]] in 2009, as well as supporting [[Enter Shikari]] on a short run of shows in May 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fyeahdonbroco.co.uk/gigography |title=Gigography |publisher=F Yeah Don Broco |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref>

Don Broco also played [[Underage Festival]], at Victoria Park in [[London]], UK, in both 2009 and 2010. They played [[Sonisphere]] festival 2010 on the [[Red Bull]] Bedroom Jam stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.efestivals.co.uk/festivals/bands.php?BandID=24542 |title=Don Broco (performer's page) |publisher=eFestivals |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref> The band then went on to support [[Enter Shikari]] at both their Christmas parties at Hatfield Forum in December 2010. In September 2010 Don Broco also played in a local festival called [[Amersham]] Summer Festival.

===''Big Fat Smile'' (2011)===
In January 2011, they released the video to "Beautiful Morning", directed by Lawrence Hardy. The following month they released "Beautiful Morning" as a single.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.nme.com/nme-video/youtube/id/iOzJvLpEzPQ/search/Don%20Broco |title=Don Broco - Beautiful Morning video |magazine=NME |access-date=2013-08-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130606091904/http://www.nme.com/nme-video/youtube/id/iOzJvLpEzPQ/search/Don%20Broco |archive-date=6 June 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> They released their [[Extended play|EP]] ''Big Fat Smile'' on 14 February 2011.<ref name="lastfm" /> It was produced and engineered by Matt O'Grady and mixed by [[John Mitchell (musician)|John Mitchell]].

Don Broco, alongside [[Lower than Atlantis]] and Veara, supported [[We Are The Ocean]] on tour in April/May 2011.  The band completed a third UK headline tour in May 2011 with support from Burn The Fleet. This run of shows included a show at the Alternative Escape Festival in Brighton, with [[Deaf Havana]] and at Hub Festival in Liverpool, as well as performances at [[Slam Dunk]] North and South.

Over the summer 2011, Don Broco played a number of UK Festivals including [[Download Festival]], [[Sonisphere Festival]],<ref name="Sonisphere lineup including Don Broco">{{cite web|title=SONISPHERE 2011 LINE UP COMPLETED|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/news/article/sonisphere-2011-line-up-completed|work=Rocksound.tv|publisher=RockSound|access-date=11 January 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140111033337/http://www.rocksound.tv/news/article/sonisphere-2011-line-up-completed|archive-date=11 January 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>  [[Hevy Festival]], Liverpool Sound City, [[Slam Dunk Festival]], The Great Escape Festival. and at the [[Reading and Leeds festivals]] on the BBC Introducing stage which they were put forward for by the BBC Introducing programme which covers the Bedford area where the band is originally from.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/festivals/readingandleeds/2011/artists/donbroco/#p00k2hhx |title=Reading and Leeds - 2011 - Don Broco |publisher=BBC |date=2011-08-28 |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref>

An accompanying video to the band's B-Side "We Are On Holiday" off their "Dreamboy" single was released in August 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://localsceneuk.blogspot.com/2011/08/don-broco-release-new-song-and-video.html |title=Don Broco release new song and video online |publisher=LocalScene UK |date=2011-08-13 |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref> The single was made available to download on [[iTunes]] on 22 August 2011.

===''Priorities'' and Rayner and Playford's departure (2012–2013)===
{{Main articles|Priorities (album)}}
Don Broco supported [[Four Year Strong]] on their UK tour in January and February 2012.<ref>{{cite web |author=Bhamra, Satvir |url=http://amplified.tv/2011/10/11/don-broco-to-support-four-year-strong-on-uk-tour/ |title=Don Broco to support Four Year Strong on UK Tour |publisher=Amplified |date=2011-10-11 |access-date=2013-08-21 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111117042813/http://amplified.tv/2011/10/11/don-broco-to-support-four-year-strong-on-uk-tour/ |archive-date=17 November 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> During this time, before the release of the debut album, Darren Playford and Luke Rayner left the band due to commitment issues and different interests, however they are still on good terms.<ref name="Luke Rayner Leave Don Broco">{{cite web|title=Luke Rayner Leave Don Broco|url=http://www.hitthefloor.com/music/rock-metal/luke-raynor-leaves-don-broco|work=Hitthefloor.com|publisher=Hit The Floor Magazine|access-date=11 January 2014}}</ref>  The band played Hit the Deck festival in Bristol and [[Nottingham]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fyeahdonbroco.co.uk/2012/03/07/hitthedeck2012/ |title=Don Broco at Hit The Deck Festival 2012 |publisher=F Yeah Don Broco |date=2012-03-07 |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref> the Great Escape Festival in [[Brighton]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fyeahdonbroco.co.uk/2012/02/29/greatescapefestival2012/ |title=Don Broco at the Great Escape Festival 2012 |publisher=F Yeah Don Broco |date=2012-02-29 |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref> Slam Dunk festivals in [[Leeds]], [[Hertfordshire]] and [[Cardiff]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://fyeahdonbroco.co.uk/2012/02/29/newsslamdunk2012/ |title=Don Broco at Slam Dunk 2012! |publisher=F Yeah Don Broco |date=2012-02-29 |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref> and Redfest 2012. Don Broco were going to support [[Futures (band)|Futures]] in April 2012, but their tour was postponed to July 2012.

On 23 March 2012, the band announced that they had signed a deal with Search and Destroy Records, a new venture between Raw Power Management and [[Sony Music]]. The band's first single "Priorities" from their debut album of the same name was released on 20 May, with the album released on 13 August. The band replaced original bassist with new bassist Tom Doyle. In April, the band supported [[You Me At Six]] in Dublin and Belfast on their Irish leg of their UK tour. The band replaced the support bill originally consisting of [[Kids In Glass Houses]], [[The Skints]] and [[Mayday Parade]], however the Irish leg was rescheduled after [[You Me At Six|Josh Francheschi]] contracted [[tonsillitis]]. The band supported [[The Used]] on their April/May UK tour, alongside [[Marmozets]].

The band released the second single from ''Priorities'', titled "Actors" it was released on the same day as Don Broco's debut album, 13 August 2012. The video sees the boys performing and a storyline where the boys don't get on and putting all the chemistry between them on for the cameras.

On 20 September, "Hold On" was revealed to be the third single from the album and shortly after they supported Lower Than Atlantis along with The Dangerous Summer and Gnarwolves in Autumn 2012, followed by their first European tour, supporting [[Young Guns (band)|Young Guns]] accompanied with [[Your Demise]] on their European leg in October 2012. It was announced that Don Broco will be the final support on the Rock Sound Riot Tour 2012 in November 2012 along with [[Billy Talent]] and [[Awolnation]].

In February 2013, they embarked on their first headline tour of the United Kingdom with dates spanning from February to April. For the February to March dates they brought [[Mallory Knox]] and Hey Vanity along as support. On 3 February it was announced that all dates for the February leg of the tour had sold out. For the April dates [[Pure Love (band)|Pure Love]] and Decade were the supporting acts.

On 30 March 2013 Don Broco performed a headlining set at the Radstock Festival, [[Liverpool]], at the [[O2 Academy Liverpool]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://entertaimnet.wordpress.com/2012/11/20/don-broco-definitely-recommended/ |title=DON BROCO / Definitely recommended!In addition, they played at Takedown festival on the 11th of May 2013.  |publisher=Entertaimnet.wordpress.com |date=2012-11-20 |access-date=2013-08-21}}</ref> On the weekend of 23–25 August 2013, Don Broco performed on the main stage at the [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]]. The band concluded 2013 by releasing their new song "You Wanna Know" as a standalone single which also came as a bundled EP featuring various remixed versions of the song.

===''Automatic'' and signing to SharpTone Records (2014–2016)===
{{Main articles|Automatic (Don Broco album)}}
Don Broco stated that the majority of 2014 would be focused around writing material for a new album and that they will headline a tour after its release.<ref name="Don Broco talks about future plans for 2014">{{cite journal|last=Sayce|first=Rob|title=DON BrOCO: For Bedford's finest, anything could happen in the next 12 months...|date=3 January 2013|work=Rocksound Magazine|issue=183|page=58|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/news/article/rock-sound-issue-183-is-out-now|access-date=19 January 2014}}</ref> It was announced that the band will be supporting British band [[You Me at Six]] during their UK headline tour during March and April 2014, along with support act [[Young Kato]].<ref name="Don Broco to support You Me at Six in 2014">{{cite web|title=You me At Six Reveal UK Tour Supports|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/news/article/you-me-at-six-reveal-uk-tour-supports|work=Rocksound.com|publisher=Rocksound|access-date=4 February 2014}}</ref>

In late August 2014 Don Broco were announced to headline 2015's [[Kerrang!]] tour in February, when they toured the UK with support from [[pop rock]] band [[We Are the In Crowd]] and [[metalcore]] bands [[Bury Tomorrow]] and [[Beartooth (band)|Beartooth]].<ref name="2015 Kerrang tour">{{cite web|last1=Carter|first1=Emily|title=First two bands on Kerrang! tour 2015 revealed|url=http://www.kerrang.com/22011/first-two-bands-kerrang-tour-2015-revealed/|website=Kerrang.com|publisher=Kerrang!|access-date=27 August 2014}}</ref>

On 23 November 2014, the first single from the new album, titled "Money Power Fame", premiered on [[BBC Radio 1]]'s "Rock Show" programme.<ref name="Money Power Fame release">{{cite web|title=Don Broco return with explosive new single ‘Money Power Fame’|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/don-broco-return-with-explosive-new-single-money-power-fame|website=Rocksound.tv|publisher=Rocksound|access-date=26 January 2015}}</ref> A second single, "Fire", was streamed online on 26 January 2015.<ref name="Fire release">{{cite web|last1=Carter|first1=Emily|title=Don Broco unleash brand new track, Fire|url=http://www.kerrang.com/27474/don-broco-unleash-brand-new-track-fire/|website=Kerrang.com|publisher=Kerrang!|access-date=26 January 2015}}</ref> On 7 April 2015, the band released the third song off the new album titled "What You Do to Me", followed by music videos for the tracks "Automatic" and "Superlove" respectively.

''[[Automatic (Don Broco album)|Automatic]]'' was released on 7 August 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/donbroco/photos/a.105709183476.91446.8137423476/10152913075243477/?type=1|title=DON BROCO - Timeline Photos - Facebook|work=facebook.com}}</ref> It is available in standard and deluxe versions; the deluxe version includes the standard ten tracks as well as four bonus tracks, two of these being previously released songs "Money Power Fame" and "You Wanna Know".

In April 2016, they supported [[Bring Me The Horizon]] on the European leg of their tour and also supported [[5 Seconds of Summer]] between 15 April 2016 and 8 June 2016 on their [[Sounds Live Feels Live World Tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/donbroco/photos/a.105709183476.91446.8137423476/10153616976333477/?type=3&theater|title=DON BROCO - Joining 5 Seconds of Summer for all these... - Facebook}}</ref>

On 24 June 2016, the band officially signed with [[SharpTone Records]], a label co-founded by Shawn Keith and [[Nuclear Blast Records|Nuclear Blast]] CEO Markus Staiger.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7415658/sharptone-records-attila-miss-may-i-we-came-as-romans|title=SharpTone Records Opens With Attila, Miss May I & We Came as Romans on Roster}}</ref> 

===''Technology'' and "Action" (2017–2019)===
{{Main articles|Technology (Don Broco album)}}
[[File:Don Broco - Rock am Ring 2018-3958.jpg|thumb|right|Don Broco (Rob Damiani) - [[Rock am Ring]] 2018]]
Don Broco released the single "Everybody" on 16 July 2016 along with an accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W6JZ6IgCKKo|title=Don Broco - Everybody (Explicit)|last=DonBrocoVEVO|date=16 July 2016|via=YouTube}}</ref>

On 21 October 2016, SharpTone Records uploaded Don Broco's 2013 music video for "You Wanna Know" on their YouTube channel. It was also announced that Don Broco will rerelease ''Automatic'' in the United States on 11 November 2016 through SharpTone Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VhWAutsQVY8|title=DON BROCO - You Wanna Know (OFFICIAL MUSIC VIDEO)|last=SharpTone Records|date=21 October 2016|via=YouTube}}</ref> The label uploaded the videos for "Money Power Fame", "Superlove", and "Automatic" to their YouTube channel prior to the release.

Don Broco released five singles for their third album ''[[Technology (Don Broco album)|Technology]]'': "Pretty", "Technology", "Stay Ignorant", "T-Shirt Song" and "Come Out to LA". The band went on to play to a sold-out crowd at London's [[Alexandra Palace]] in November, 2017, their biggest show to date. <ref>{{Cite web|last=Redrup|first=Zach|date=2017-11-20|title=LIVE: Don Broco @ Alexandra Palace, London (11/11/2017)|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/live-don-broco-alexandra-palace-london-11-11-2017/|access-date=2021-07-19|website=DEAD PRESS! {{!}} It's more than "just music" to us|language=en-GB}}</ref> ''Technology'' was released on 2 February 2018 via SharpTone Records.

In October to November 2016, Don Broco appeared as a support act for rock-metal band [[Bring Me The Horizon]] on their UK tour, playing arenas such as [[The O2 Arena]]. In 2018, Don Broco was one of the support bands for [[Our Last Night]]'s North American and European tours. They also toured with [[Mike Shinoda]] of [[Linkin Park]] in support of his album ''[[Post Traumatic]]''. 

The band were nominated for 'Best British Live Act' at the [[Kerrang! Awards 2019|2019 Kerrang! Awards]].<ref>''Kerrang!'', issue 1774, 25 May 2019, page 8.</ref>

On 6 September 2019, Don Broco released the standalone single "Action". Two versions of this single were released, one solely featuring Don Broco vocalists Rob Damiani and Matt Donnelly while the second features [[Caleb Shomo]] ([[Beartooth (band)|Beartooth]]), [[Tyler Carter]] ([[Issues (band)|Issues]]), [[Taka Moriuchi]] ([[One Ok Rock]]) and [[Tilian Pearson]] ([[Dance Gavin Dance]]). The music video for "Action" won the award for Best Music Video by Heavy Music Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|date=September 4, 2020|first=Joe|last=DiVita|title=Complete Winners List: 2020 Heavy Music Awards|url=https://loudwire.com/2020-heavy-music-awards-winners-list/|access-date=2020-09-30|website=Loudwire|language=en}}</ref>

===''Amazing Things'' (2020–present)===
{{Main articles|Amazing Things (Don Broco album)}}
Don Broco began writing new material for their fourth studio album in early 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Don Broco’s Rob Damiani Talks Writing Their New Album In Lockdown - Video Call - Videos|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/videos/watch/don-broco-rob-damiani-talks-writing-their-new-album-in-lockdown-video-call|access-date=2021-02-15|website=Rock Sound Magazine|language=en}}</ref> and began recording in December 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Redrup|first=Zach|date=2020-12-04|title=NEWS: Don Broco begin recording fourth album!|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-don-broco-begin-recording-fourth-album/|access-date=2021-02-22|website=Dead Press!|language=en-GB}}</ref> They released a series of studio updates via YouTube before the release of the first single "Manchester Super Reds No.1 Fan" on [[Annie Mac]]’s Future Sounds, and its accompanying music video on 13 May 2021. Announcement of the album ''[[Amazing Things (Don Broco album)|Amazing Things]]'' came the following day, along with a UK tour in October-November 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Don Broco Are Premiering A New Song Called ‘Manchester Super Reds No.01 Fan’ TONIGHT - News|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/don-broco-are-releasing-a-new-song-called-manchester-super-reds-no.01-fan-t|access-date=2021-05-13|website=Rock Sound Magazine|language=en}}</ref> The second single "Gumshield" was released on 8 July 2021, with a music video involving boxer [[Dave Allen (boxer)|Dave Allen]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-08|title=See Don Broco Get Mind-Altering Beatdown From Pro Boxer in New "Gumshield" Video|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/see-don-broco-get-mind-altering-beatdown-pro-boxer-new-gumshield-video|access-date=2021-07-10|website=Revolver|language=en}}</ref>

They are set to headline [[Slam Dunk Records|Slam Dunk Festival]], in [[Leeds]] and [[Hatfield, Hertfordshire|Hatfield]] in September 2021, and also released a small set of warm-up gigs to precede the festival.

==Musical style==
Primarily categorised as [[alternative rock]],<ref name=AllMusic/> the band's style has also been described as [[pop rock]] and [[post-hardcore]] by reviewers; AltSounds reviewed their debut album [[Priorities (album)|''Priorities'']] to have elements of all three genres.<ref name="Review of Priorities by AltSounds">{{cite web|title=Review: Don Broco – Priorities [Album]|url=http://hangout.altsounds.com/reviews/151233-review-don-broco-priorities-album.html|work=Hangout.altsounds.com/|publisher=AltSounds|access-date=10 January 2014|author=Candi H|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110234117/http://hangout.altsounds.com/reviews/151233-review-don-broco-priorities-album.html|archive-date=10 January 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref> AlreadyHeard expressed that the album was mostly alternative rock, yet praised the album's range of different moods, going from energetic to "cheeky" songs.<ref name="Priorities review by AlreadyHeard">{{cite web|title=Album Review: Don Broco - Priorities|url=http://alreadyheard.com/post/29352697715/album-review-don-broco-priorities#.UtBksJ6qnJY|work=Alreadyheard.com|publisher=AlreadyHeard|access-date=10 January 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170116083621/http://alreadyheard.com/post/29352697715/album-review-don-broco-priorities#.UtBksJ6qnJY|archive-date=16 January 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> A live review from ''[[Virtual Festivals]]'' reviewed the band's performance at the [[Leeds Festival]] in 2013 and proclaimed that the changes in the band's musical style compared to their earlier music from the likes of the ''Living the Dream'' and ''Thug Workout'' EPs were substantial. They claimed that due to this some members of their audience were unsatisfied and that the new material was dulled by a slower tempo and a "love-letter" lyrical style.<ref name="Review of Don Broco at Leeds Festival 2013">{{cite web|title=Don Broco at Leeds Festival 2013 review|url=http://www.virtualfestivals.com/latest/features/14210|work=Virtualfestivals.com|publisher=Virtual Festivals|access-date=10 January 2014|author=Ali Ryland|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110235108/http://www.virtualfestivals.com/latest/features/14210|archive-date=10 January 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

''RocksFreaks.Net'' expressed the ''Thug Workout'' EP to be a half mixture of [[nu metal]] and post-hardcore due to the mixture of half-rap verses and [[Screaming (music)|screams]] and related their sound to that of [[A Day to Remember]].<ref name="Don Broco Thug Workout EP Review by RockFreaks.Net">{{cite web|title=Don Broco Thug Workout EP|url=http://www.rockfreaks.net/albums/1843|work=Rockfreaks.net|publisher=RockFreaks.Net|access-date=10 January 2014}}</ref> The reviews for the third EP ''Big Fat Smile'' primarily called it a general [[Rock music|rock]] album with elements of rock pop. ''[[Rocksound]]'' called the album a "good mix of Brit-rock riffs, sarcastic lyrical content and jovial pop sensibilities".<ref name="Big Fat Smile review by Rocksound">{{cite web|title=DON BROCO - BIG FAT SMILE|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/reviews/article/don-broco-big-fat-smile|work=Rocksound.tv|publisher=RockSound|access-date=10 January 2014|author=Andy Ritchie}}</ref> with ''Alter the Press!'' also backing this statement up, placing them with the likes of many other "British rock hopefuls for 2011".<ref name="Big Fat Smile review by Alter The Press!">{{cite web|title=Album Review: Don Broco - Big Fat Smile|url=http://www.alterthepress.com/2011/02/album-review-don-broco-big-fat-smile.html|work=Alterthepress.com|publisher=Alter The Press!|access-date=10 January 2014|author=Sean Reid}}</ref>

Their second album ''Automatic'' saw the band change to a poppier sound with "funk and new wave elements".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-08-20|title=Review: Don Broco – Automatic|url=https://www.theedgesusu.co.uk/records/2015/08/20/review-don-broco-automatic-2/|access-date=2021-07-23|website=The Edge|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-08-07|title=ALBUM REVIEW: ‘Automatic’ by Don Broco|url=https://thesoundboardreviews.com/2015/08/07/album-review-automatic-by-don-broco/|access-date=2021-07-23|website=The Soundboard|language=en}}</ref> Third album ''Technology'' saw yet another change with a "far rockier sound",<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-02-07|title=ALBUM REVIEW: Don Broco - Technology|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/album-review-don-broco-technology/|access-date=2021-07-23|website=DEAD PRESS! {{!}} It's more than "just music" to us|language=en-GB}}</ref> but still with some synth elements.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Album Review: Don Broco - Technology|url=http://drownedinsound.com/releases/20201/reviews/4151594|access-date=2021-07-23|website=DrownedInSound|language=en}}</ref> Reviews of their fourth album ''Amazing Things'' show a change to a mix of nu metal, rap metal and post-hardcore, more similar to earlier EPs.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-11|title=Bruce Willis, Rap Metal, Troll Hate: Don Broco Talk Rule-Breaking New Album|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/bruce-willis-rap-metal-troll-hate-don-broco-talk-rule-breaking-new-album|access-date=2021-07-23|website=Revolver|language=en}}</ref>
