'''Lauren Isenberg''' (born May 7, 2002), known professionally as '''Renforshort''' (stylized as '''renforshort'''<ref name="Socan"/>) is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] singer and songwriter from [[Toronto]]. Debuting independently in 2019 with the singles "Waves" and "Mind Games", Isenberg released her debut [[extended play]], the ''Teenage Angst EP'', in March 2020.

== Life and career ==

=== Early life ===

Lauren Isenberg was born in [[Toronto]]. Her parents' taste of music influenced her at an early age, and she was exposed to musicians such as [[Billy Joel]], [[The Velvet Underground]] and [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]].<ref name="MusicExistence"/> As a child, she won a Chinese language competition for performing the folk song "[[Mo Li Hua]]".<ref name="Socan"/> Isenberg wrote her first song at 13 years old called "Hopeless Town", which was produced by Nathan Ferraro of the Canadian band [[The Midway State]], and worked on several songs in Los Angeles with producer Justin Gray, however decided that none of these songs showed off her full personality.<ref name="Socan"/> She began posting covers of songs to YouTube and Soundcloud in 2015,<ref name="Socan"/> starting with a cover of [[Labrinth]]'s "[[Jealous (Labrinth song)|Jealous]]". At 14, Isenberg decided that singing was her passion after performing at an [[open mic]] night.<ref name="Spindle">{{Cite web|url=https://spindlemagazine.com/meet-the-toronto-alt-pop-artist-renforshort |title=Meet The Toronto Alt-Pop Artist renforshort |date=March 13, 2020 |website=Spindle Magazine |access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref>

Isenberg met Canadian producer Jeff Hazin in 2016, who became her long-time collaborator (Isenberg has continued to work with the same co-writers since 2016).<ref name="Socan"/> In the same year, she collaborated with German drummer and [[YouTube]]r Sina Doering on her album ''Chi Might''.<ref name="GirlsGotGroove"/>

=== Debut ===

Under the moniker Ren,<ref name="VMagazine">{{Cite web|url=https://vmagazine.com/article/ren-new-song-idc/ |title=REN DROPS HER NEW SONG "IDC" |first=Ryan Killian |last=Krause |date=November 13, 2019 |website=V Magazine |access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref> Isenberg released her debut single "Waves" in February 2019.<ref name="OtO">{{Cite web|url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/ren-details-her-complicated-love-life-in |title=Ren Details Her Complicated Love Life In Vulnerable Music Video for "Waves" [PREMIERE] |first=Kelly |last=Itatani |date=February 22, 2019 |website=Ones to Watch |access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref> Music executives from [[Geffen Records]] took notice of her due to the song's popularity, and organized a meeting with her only a month after its release.<ref name="The Star">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2019/11/22/the-new-billie-eilish-or-the-next-nirvana-meet-torontos-new-17-year-old-pop-star-who-adds-the-90s-to-the-mix.html |title=The new Billie Eilish or the next Nirvana? Meet Toronto's new 17-year-old pop star who adds the '90s to the mix |first=Ryan |last=Porter |date=November 22, 2019 |website=The Star |access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref> Isenberg's second single "Mind Games" became a viral hit,<ref name="Spindle"/> and by November the two tracks had amassed five million streams across platforms.<ref name="Socan"/> "Mind Games" was later nominated for the 2019 [[SOCAN Songwriting Prize]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/socan_announces_2020_songwriting_prize_finalists |title=SOCAN Announces 2020 Songwriting Prize Finalists |first=Allie |last=Gregory |date=July 16, 2020 |website=Exclaim |access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref>

Isenberg's first single with Geffen Records was released in November, entitled "IDC".<ref name="VMagazine"/> On March 13, 2020, Isenberg released her debut [[extended play]], the ''Teenage Angst EP'',<ref name="MusicExistence"/> and changed her stage name to Renforshort.<ref name="Socan"/> The leading single from the release, "I Drive Me Mad", was inspired by Isenberg's experiences with anxiety and panic attacks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/music/ren-i-drive-me-mad-music-video-anxiety/ |title=Rising Pop Singer Ren, 17, Releases Video for 'I Drive Me Mad' Inspired by Her Battle with Anxiety |first=Melody |last=Chiu |date=February 28, 2020 |website=People |access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref> Promotion for the EP was difficult, as scheduled showcases in Toronto, New York and Los Angeles were delayed in an effort to [[flatten the curve]] against the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="Socan"/> She is a support act on YUNGBLUD’s Life on Mars tour.

=== Later Works ===
In 2021 Ren released another EP titled ''off saint dominique EP''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.renforshort.com/music/saint-dominique-ep|access-date=2021-10-19|website=www.renforshort.com}}</ref> which was released through Interscope Records on June 4. It was met with positive reviews from critics with [[NME]] describing it as " A celebration of ever changing emotion"<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-04|title=Renforshort – ‘Off Saint Dominique’ EP: pop-rock riser is unafraid to speak her mind|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/renforshort-off-saint-dominique-ep-radar-2954364|access-date=2021-10-19|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref> and publication Dork called the EP as having a "striking take on lyricism that continues to impress with a refreshing level of self-reflection"<ref>{{Cite web|last=Freyaldenhoven|first=Laura|date=2021-06-03|title=renforshort - Off Saint Dominique EP {{!}} Dork|url=https://readdork.com/albums/renforshort-off-saint-dominique-ep/|access-date=2021-10-19|website=readdork.com|language=en-US}}</ref> As of October 2021 the EP has surpassed 12 million streams on Spotify<ref>{{Citation|title=off saint dominique EP|date=2021-06-04|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/5BxlFXGHkjDUMCRAGpVyHD|language=en|access-date=2021-10-19}}</ref>

== Artistry ==

Renforshort's music is inspired by acts such as [[Bob Dylan]] and [[Amy Winehouse]], and from films such as ''[[Coraline (film)|Coraline]]'' and ''[[Call Me by Your Name (film)|Call Me by Your Name]]''.<ref name="BMI">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bmi.com/news/entry/bmi-indie-spotlight-ren |title=BMI Indie Spotlight: ren |date=March 30, 2020 |publisher=Broadcast Music, Inc. |access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==

Isenberg's mother is a real-estate agent. She has three brothers, who are all musicians.<ref name="The Star"/>
