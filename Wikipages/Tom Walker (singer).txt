'''Thomas Alexander Walker''' (born 17 December 1991) is a Scottish singer-songwriter.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.iamtomwalker.com/about/ | title=About Tom Walker | accessdate=12 March 2021 | work=iamtomwalker.com}}</ref> He was trained at the stage school [[London College of Creative Media]], and was given a contract soon after. He rose to fame after the release of his single "[[Leave a Light On (Tom Walker song)|Leave a Light On]]", which peaked at number 7 on the [[UK Singles Chart]] in June 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/52746/tom-walker/|title=Tom Walker {{!}}full Official Chart History {{!}}Official Charts Company|website=officialcharts.com|access-date=20 February 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190221054719/https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/52746/tom-walker/|archive-date=21 February 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Early life==
Walker was born in [[Kilsyth]], near [[Glasgow]], but at the age of three his family moved to the [[Cheshire]] town of [[Knutsford]]. He described his upbringing as being in a 'Glaswegian house' as his parents were [[Glaswegian dialect|Glaswegian sounding]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.itv.com/news/granada/update/2019-05-03/tom-walker-i-wrote-120-songs-for-this-album/|title=Tom Walker: "I wrote 120 songs for this album"|website=ITV News|access-date=24 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190511105425/https://www.itv.com/news/granada/update/2019-05-03/tom-walker-i-wrote-120-songs-for-this-album/|archive-date=11 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-47129598|title=Tom Walker: Scots gran is my role model|date=6 February 2019|access-date=24 May 2019|via=bbc.co.uk|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190510013851/https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-47129598|archive-date=10 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> He speaks with a Scottish accent when with family or in Scotland.<ref name="homecoming">{{cite web|url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/entertainment/music/music-news/rising-star-tom-walker-cant-10410909|title=Rising star Tom Walker heads for homecoming gig as he headlines King Tut's in Glasgow|first=Rick|last=Fulton|date=12 May 2017|website=Daily Record|access-date=24 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190723020554/https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/entertainment/music/music-news/rising-star-tom-walker-cant-10410909|archive-date=23 July 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=Boogie & Arlene|title=Tom Walker tells us how he once made a toastie on an iron - Boogie & Arlene interview|date=6 October 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QWu64Sp6F00&t=138s|access-date=31 May 2019}}</ref> His favourite artists while growing up included [[Ray Charles]], [[Muddy Waters]] and [[Paolo Nutini]].<ref name="homecoming"/>

==Career==
In 2014, prior to fame, Walker graduated from [[London College of Creative Media]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lccm.org.uk/people/alumni/tom-walker-2014-songwriter-artist/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160817020609/http://lccm.org.uk/people/alumni/tom-walker-2014-songwriter-artist/|url-status=dead|archive-date=17 August 2016|title=Tom Walker 2014 - Songwriter and Artist - LCCM - London College of Creative Media|date=17 August 2016|access-date=24 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gigslutz.co.uk/brand-new-state-art-music-venue-open-central-london/|title=Brand new, state of the art, music venue to open in Central London|date=7 March 2017|access-date=24 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190330070732/http://www.gigslutz.co.uk/brand-new-state-art-music-venue-open-central-london/|archive-date=30 March 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2017–2019: ''What a Time to Be Alive'' and collaborations===
In 2017, Walker released "[[Just You and I (Tom Walker song)|Just You and I]]", which was picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] appearing on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'' show with [[Kathie Lee Gifford]] where he performed the song.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.today.com/video/meet-scottish-singer-songwriter-tom-walker-elvis-duran-s-artist-of-the-month-902038083757|title=Meet Scottish singer-songwriter Tom Walker, Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|website=TODAY.com|access-date=20 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170522231526/http://www.today.com/video/meet-scottish-singer-songwriter-tom-walker-elvis-duran-s-artist-of-the-month-902038083757|archive-date=22 May 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> His follow-up single was "Blessings", the title track from his debut EP ''Blessings''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.epicrecords.com/tom-walker-releases-new-single-blessings-today/|title=TOM WALKER RELEASES NEW SINGLE "BLESSINGS" TODAY! – Epic Records|publisher=Epic Records|date=28 April 2017|access-date=20 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170720181944/http://www.epicrecords.com/tom-walker-releases-new-single-blessings-today/|archive-date=20 July 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> On 13 June 2017, Walker released the single "Heartland", which was co-written and produced by [[Naughty Boy]]. On 22 August 2017, the single was added to the BBC Radio 1 playlist.<ref name="coverttalent.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.coverttalent.com/news|title=News|website=Covert Talent Management|access-date=27 October 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171027182404/http://www.coverttalent.com/news|archive-date=27 October 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> Later in 2017, Walker was named as one of the new additions to the [[BBC Radio 1]] Brit List.<ref>{{cite web |first=Ben |last=Homewood |url=http://www.musicweek.com/talent/read/stefflon-don-j-hus-and-tom-walker-make-radio-1-s-brit-list-station-boss-hails-stellar-year-for-uk-talent/069221 |title=Stefflon Don, J Hus and Tom Walker make Radio 1's Brit List, station boss hails 'stellar year' for UK talent |work=MusicWeek |date=21 July 2017 |access-date=25 October 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171025190103/http://www.musicweek.com/talent/read/stefflon-don-j-hus-and-tom-walker-make-radio-1-s-brit-list-station-boss-hails-stellar-year-for-uk-talent/069221 |archive-date=25 October 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref> Walker has played support slots for artists such as [[George Ezra]], [[Gallant (singer)|Gallant]] and [[Jake Bugg]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.m-magazine.co.uk/genres/electronic-genres/featured-artist-tom-walker/|title=Featured artist: Tom Walker – M Magazine|date=4 September 2017|access-date=27 October 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171027203015/https://www.m-magazine.co.uk/genres/electronic-genres/featured-artist-tom-walker/|archive-date=27 October 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> On 28 September, Walker began his US tour, in Connecticut, supporting [[The Script]].<ref name="coverttalent.com"/>

On 13 October 2017, Walker released his single "[[Leave a Light On (Tom Walker song)|Leave a Light On]]" on [[Relentless Records]]. The single was co-written and produced by [[Steve Mac]].<ref name="indiecentralmusic.com">{{Cite web |url=http://indiecentralmusic.com/2017/10/23/tom-walker-releases-leave-a-light-on/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=25 October 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180702065354/http://indiecentralmusic.com/2017/10/23/tom-walker-releases-leave-a-light-on/ |archive-date=2 July 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The music video for "Leave a Light On" was produced by Charles Mehling, and filmed in Croatia.<ref name="indiecentralmusic.com" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/IamTomWalker/photos/a.512027015613926.1073741828.445716478911647/1084062561743699/?type=3&theater|title=Tom Walker|website=facebook.com}}</ref> It impacted numerous international charts and was included in his debut studio album ''[[What a Time to Be Alive (Tom Walker album)|What a Time to Be Alive]]''. In 2019, Walker won the [[Brit Award for British Breakthrough Act|Best Breakthrough Act]] at the [[2019 Brit Awards]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-47312709|title=Tom Walker dedicates Brit award to gran|date=20 February 2019|access-date=20 February 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190221063213/https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-scotland-47312709|archive-date=21 February 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> His song "Leave the Light On" was also nominated for "Best British Single".

===2020–present: Second studio album===
In November 2019, Walker confirmed he had begun working on his second studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/2019/1105/1088820-tom-walker-i-get-really-excited-about-irish-gigs/|title=Tom Walker: 'I get really excited about Irish gigs'|website=[[RTÉ]]|date=5 November 2019|access-date=19 April 2020}}</ref> Following this, in early 2020, ''What a Time to Be Alive'' achieved [[List of music recording certifications|platinum]] accreditation by the [[British Phonographic Industry]] for sales of over 300,000 units in the UK.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicweek.com/talent/read/tom-walker-s-debut-album-goes-platinum/078872|title= Tom Walker's debut album goes platinum|website=[[Music Week]]|date=12 February 2020|access-date=19 April 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Walker is a supporter of football club [[Manchester United F.C.]]<ref>{{cite news|last=Ashby|first=Ben|url=https://www.manutd.com/en/news/detail/my-united-with-tom-walker-from-inside-united-magazine|title=My United: Tom Walker on his love of the Reds|work=Manchester United F.C.|date=24 December 2018|access-date=4 March 2021}}</ref>
