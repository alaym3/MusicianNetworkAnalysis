'''Cimorelli''' {{IPAc-en|s|ɪ|m|ə|ˈ|ɹ|ɛ|l|i}} ({{IPA-it|tʃiːmoːrɛlˈli}}) is a singing group from [[El Dorado Hills, California]]<ref>[http://www.pledgemusic.com/blog/cimorelli-journey-guest-post Cimorelli Documents Meaningful Journey | PledgeMusic] Retrieved 2016-11-22.</ref> that first gained popularity on [[YouTube]] singing [[Cover version|cover music]]. They were subsequently signed to [[Universal Music]]'s [[Island Records|Island]] label. Now composing and writing their own songs, Cimorelli is made up of five sisters: Christina, Katherine, Lisa, Amy, and Lauren;<ref name=AM>[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/cimorelli-mn0002028658 Cimorelli], [[Allmusic]]</ref><ref name="Sacmag">{{cite web|url=http://www.sacmag.com/Sacramento-Magazine/November-2009/2009-Best-of-Sacramento/Entertainment/EDITORS-PICK-Best-Brother/Sister-Act/|title=EDITORS' PICK—Best Brother/Sister Act – Sacramento, California|publisher=Sacmag.com|access-date=2011-10-18}}</ref> their older brother, Mike Jr., was a member of the group from 2007 to 2008, while youngest sister Dani was a member from 2010 to 2020.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N8Z3xhR_QE4 Dani is leaving the band.], Cimorelli (posted to YouTube on January 4, 2020)</ref> The group's music is mostly made up of [[a cappella]] singing with occasional instruments.

{{As of|2018|04|post=,}} the group has released seven [[extended play]]s, a mixtape called ''Hearts on Fire'', and three full-length albums titled ''[[Up at Night]]'', ''Alive'', and ''Sad Girls Club''.

==Early life==
Growing up, the Cimorelli sisters were raised [[Catholic Church|Catholic]] and home schooled along with their five brothers (Mike Jr., Alex, Christian, Nick, and Joey) by their mom, Lynne Cimorelli. Lynne, a classical pianist, taught the children to sing and play the piano at young ages.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Music|first=Cecilia|title=It’s a family affair: These 6 Catholic sisters have over 3 million subscribers on YouTube|url=https://aleteia.org/2018/04/27/its-a-family-affair-these-6-catholic-sisters-have-over-3-million-subscribers-on-youtube/|access-date=2020-07-19|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Career==

===2007–2009: Formation and beginnings===
In 2007, Mike Jr., Christina, Katherine, Lisa, Amy, and Lauren launched Cimorelli as a vocal group. The group sang at small shows around their hometown and the [[Sacramento, California|Sacramento]] area.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Interview: Ryan Higa, David Choi, Ki Hong Lee, Cimorelli on building careers on YouTube|url=https://rappler.com/hustle/work/ryan-higa-david-choi-ki-hong-lee-cimorelli-youtube-careers-interview|access-date=2020-07-19|website=Rappler|language=en}}</ref> On December 12, 2008, Cimorelli released their debut EP, ''Hello There'' (also known as ''Cimorelli'').<ref>{{Cite web|title=Cimorelli by Cimorelli|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/cimorelli/302311613|access-date=2020-07-19|website=Apple Music|language=en-us}}</ref>

After the release of ''Hello There'', Mike Jr. left the band.

On August 10 2009, the group (then consisting of Christina, Katherine, Lisa, Amy, and Lauren) uploaded their cover of "[[Party in the U.S.A.]]". Following this release Cimorelli began releasing weekly covers. 

Cimorelli was signed by Island Records in 2009. The family then moved from [[El Dorado Hills, California]] to [[Malibu, California]].<ref>{{Cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-TSVRD1rQmU&t=70s|title=Draw My Life: 11 Siblings, Homeschooled, and Living in LA}}</ref>

===2010–2015: Island Records===
In 2010, their youngest sister, Dani, joined the band.

In 2011, Cimorelli released their second EP, ''CimFam''. This marked their first release under the Island label. The EP peaked at number 9 on the US [[Top Heatseekers]] chart.<ref name="Heatseekers"/> ''CimFam'' features an original song written by all of the girls and five covers.

In July 2012, Cimorelli was nominated for Choice Web Star at the [[2012 Teen Choice Awards]].<ref name="crushable">{{cite web|last=Henderson|first=Garnet|date=2012-07-18|title=Interview: Cimorelli Are Giving the Jonas Brothers a Run for Their Money|url=http://www.crushable.com/2012/07/18/entertainment/cimorelli-interview-teen-choice-awards-393/|access-date=2012-12-10|work=Crushable.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121214075638/http://www.crushable.com/2012/07/18/entertainment/cimorelli-interview-teen-choice-awards-393/|archive-date=2012-12-14|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Later that year, the group released their third EP, ''Believe It'', on December 11, 2012. The EP peaked at number 7 on the US Top Heatseekers chart.<ref name="Heatseekers"/>

On June 18, 2013, Cimorelli released their fourth EP, ''Made in America''. It features four original songs. The album peaked at number 179 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart and at number 4 on the US Top Heatseekers chart.<ref name="BB200"/><ref name="Heatseekers"/>

At the [[2013 Teen Choice Awards]], the group won the award of Choice Web Star.<ref name=":0" />

During the months of May and June 2014, Cimorelli performed at [[DigiTour Media|DigiFest]] in [[London]], [[New York City]], and [[Toronto]]. Later in June 2014 Cimorelli launched their web series titled ''Summer with Cimorelli''. 

On October 27, 2014, Cimorelli released their fifth EP, ''Renegade'', which peaked at number 10 on the US Heatseekers chart.<ref name="Heatseekers"/> In November 2014, Cimorelli released their first Christmas EP, ''Christmas Magic''.

In 2015, Cimorelli departed from Island Records, moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] and became independent artists. 

===2015–present: Independent artists===
Around May 2015, Cimorelli released a mixtape called ''Hearts on Fire'', which included nine acoustic tracks. This was their first release after departing from Island Records.

On May 17, 2016, the group released their debut full-length album, ''[[Up at Night]]''.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=Cimorelliband|number=732605590971789313|title=New Album: 'Up at Night' update: 'Up at Night' is HERE!|date=May 17, 2016|access-date=May 28, 2016}}</ref> It debuted at number 24 on the US [[Top Country Albums]] chart and peaked at number 7 on the US Heatseekers chart.<ref name="Heatseekers" /> The album sold 1,500 copies in the first week in the US.<ref>{{cite news|last=Bjorke|first=Matt|date=May 23, 2016|title=Country Album Sales Chart: May 23, 2016|work=Roughstock|url=http://roughstock.com/news/2016/05/40327-country-album-sales-chart-may-23-2016}}</ref>

Later that year on December 20, Cimorelli released their second full-length album, ''Alive''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Clarks|first=Jessie|title=Cimorelli To Release Second Album 'Alive'|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/2823-cimorelli-to-release-second-album-alive|access-date=2017-01-15|website=www.thechristianbeat.org}}</ref> It peaked at number 11 on the US Heatseekers and at number 42 on the [[Top Christian Albums]] chart.<ref name="Heatseekers"/><ref name="Christian"/>

On October 27, 2017, Cimorelli released their third album ''Sad Girls Club'', featuring a solo song from each sister, a first for the band. ''Sad Girls Club'' peaked at number 17 on the US Heatseekers chart.<ref name="Heatseekers"/>

In the summer of 2018, the group released their sixth EP, ''I Love You, or Whatever''. Also in summer 2018, Lisa, Amy, and Dani appeared in the film ''[[Hope Springs Eternal (film)|Hope Springs Eternal]]''.

In late 2018, Lisa and Lauren announced solo projects. In November, Lisa released "Goodbye" and in December, Lauren released "Running".

Lisa and Lauren have since released further solo projects. Lisa's include "Africa", "Italy", "I Don't Deserve It", "Unloved", "Florida" and "The Last Time" with Hudson Henry. Lauren's include "Flames", "Brown Eyed Boys" and "Pressure".{{citation needed|date=August 2021}}

==Health==
Seven out of eleven Cimorelli siblings including, Katherine, Amy, Lauren and Dani, suffer from [[brachydactyly]], a disease which causes their fingers to be abnormally short and crooked.<ref>Q&A - All About Our Crooked Fingers https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-Wn84ADnxd4</ref> 

Amy was diagnosed with [[Turner syndrome]] at the age of nine.<ref>I HAVE A RARE DISEASE AND IT ALMOST KILLED ME (My Story) https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=excu2Bb6DT0</ref>
