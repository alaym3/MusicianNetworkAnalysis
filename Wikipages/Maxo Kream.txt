'''Emekwanem Ogugua Biosah Jr.''' (born March 29, 1990),<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/maxo-kream/profile/| title=Maxo Kream Profile|publisher=HotNewHipHop}}</ref> known professionally as '''Maxo Kream''', is an American rapper from [[Houston, Texas]]. His debut album ''[[Punken]]'' was released in 2018, and the follow-up and major label debut, ''[[Brandon Banks (album)|Brandon Banks]]'', was released in 2019.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8521754/maxo-kream-brandon-banks-album-travis-scott-schoolboy-q-megan-thee-stallion| title=Maxo Kream Releases 'Brandon Banks' Album Feat. Travis Scott, ScHoolboy Q & Megan Thee Stallion: Listen|publisher=Billboard}}</ref>

== Career ==
Kream is of Nigerian descent through his father, who is a Nigerian immigrant to the U.S.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chron.com/entertainment/article/Rapper-Maxo-Kream-opens-up-on-Punken-12770960.php|title=Rapper Maxo Kream opens up on "Punken"|last=Andrew Dansby|date=March 21, 2018|website=Houston Chronicle|access-date=March 21, 2020}}</ref> Kream began rapping while still in high school, as part of the group Kream Clicc. In 2012, he started to gain popularity after releasing a remix of [[Kendrick Lamar]]'s "[[Rigamortus]]" on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OKZXvNQXuV8|title=Maxo Kream – Rigamortis (Official Video)|accessdate=September 14, 2016}}</ref> His early mixtapes ''Retro Card'' and ''Quicc Strikes'' pushed him into the limelight, hitting over 85,000 hits on LiveMixtapes. In 2014, he supported [[Chief Keef]] on the Texas leg of his tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/watching-the-morph-houston-rapper-maxo-kream-and-his-beautiful-terrifying-and-amazing-video|title=Watching the Morph: Houston Rapper Maxo Kream and His Beautiful, Terrifying, and Amazing Video|accessdate=September 14, 2016|date=July 22, 2013}}</ref>

His 2015 mixtape ''[[Maxo 187|#Maxo187]]'' received attention from [[XXL Magazine]] and [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2014/05/the-break-presents-maxo-kream/|title=The Break Presents: Maxo Kream – XXL|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/20356-maxo-187/|title=Maxo Kream" Maxo 187|website=Pitchfork|last=Pearce|first=Sheldon|date=March 19, 2015|accessdate=January 23, 2017}}</ref> He followed it up in 2016 with ''The Persona Tape''.

In 2018, he released his debut album ''[[Punken]]''.{{citation needed|date=August 2021}}

On June 27, 2019, he signed a deal with [[Roc Nation]].{{citation needed|date=August 2021}}

On July 19, 2019, he released his major label debut, ''[[Brandon Banks (album)|Brandon Banks]]'' via Big Persona/88 Classic/[[RCA Records]].<ref name="bb">{{cite web |last1=Bloom |first1=Madison |title=Maxo Kream Announces New Album Brandon Banks |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/maxo-kream-announces-new-album-brandon-banks/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |accessdate=June 18, 2019 |date=June 17, 2019}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
On October 22, 2016, Maxo Kream was arrested for engaging in an "organized crime" act and was held at Fort Bend County Jail in [[Richmond, Texas]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/maxo-kream-arrested-for-organized-criminal-activity-news.24927.html? |title=Maxo Kream arrested for organized crime |last=Walker |first=Angus |date=October 22, 2016 |website=Hotnewhiphop |access-date=October 22, 2016}}</ref>
