'''Hippie Sabotage''' are an [[electronic dance music]] duo from [[Sacramento, California]], consisted of brothers Kevin and Jeff Saurer.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Murphy|first1=Tom|title=The Sun Doesn't Set on EDM: Hippie Sabotage's Selfie Extravaganza and Gramatik's Expansion of the Genre|url=http://www.westword.com/music/the-sun-doesnt-set-on-edm-hippie-sabotages-selfie-extravaganza-and-gramatiks-expansion-of-the-genre-8018918|work=[[Westword]]|accessdate=27 July 2016}}</ref> They are well known for their remix of "[[Habits (Stay High)]]", which has surpassed 900 million views on [[YouTube]].  

== History ==
The brothers began working on music at the age of 12 where their focus was hip-hop where they created beats for local artists such as [[Yukmouth]]. The name Hippie Sabotage was inspired by their fathers' initials H.S.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.laweekly.com/hippie-sabotage-are-all-about-love-though-not-necessarily-peace/|title=Hippie Sabotage Are All About Love, Though Not Necessarily Peace|last=Callwood|first=Bret|date=February 1, 2018|website=LA Weekly|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> They initially started in the [[Independent music|indie]] genre in 2005 and gradually entered the [[electronica]] genre. They continued to venture into creating hip-hop musical beats for Chicago artists like Alex Wiley and C Plus. They progressed into making [[electronic music]], appearing at several music festivals such as [[Lollapalooza]], [[Bonnaroo Music Festival]] and [[Electric Zoo]]. 

The brothers released their own material in 2013 with their EP ''Vacants'' followed by ''The Sunny Album'' in 2014. [[Ellie Goulding]] posted the remix "[[Habits (Stay High)]]" with the brothers and Swedish singer [[Tove Lo]], which resulted in world-wide attention, hitting number one that year on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s Next Big Sound chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hippie Sabotage|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5938378/hippie-sabotage/chart?f=1167|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=27 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160924060437/http://www.billboard.com/artist/5938378/hippie-sabotage/chart?f=1167#|archive-date=2016-09-24|url-status=dead}}</ref> They later released their third EP ''Providence'' in 2016 followed by their EP ''Options'' That year, the brothers refused to exit the stage and assaulted a security guard after experiencing technical difficulties at What the Festival in [[Portland, Oregon]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2016/0/hippie-sabotage-caught-assaulting-security-festival-video/|title=Hippie Sabotage assault security at What The Festival|date=2016-06-21|website=Dancing Astronaut|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-25}}</ref>

In the year of 2018, they hit one-billion streams and announced their national tour, Legends of Fall.
