'''PTAF''' ([[acronym]] of ''Pretty and Taking All Fades'') was a [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] [[crunk]]  [[girl group]] from [[Los Angeles]] consisting of Kandi, K'Duce, and Alizé. They are most known for their viral song "Boss Ass Bitch", which garnered the attention of the public and scored them a record deal with [[Capitol Records]] in January 2014. In mid 2015 PTAF was dropped from Capitol Records after failing to find success with a second single.<ref name = "Fader">Zeichner, Naomi.[https://www.thefader.com/2014/01/31/nicki-minaj-boss-ass-bitch-remix-ptaf-interview/ "What Happens When Nicki Minaj Borrows Your Beat Without Asking?"]. ''The Fader''. January 31, 2014. Accessed July 26, 2016.</ref> In 2017 the group announced on social media that they had split up and that the individual members were pursuing solo careers but still remain friends. In October 2020, the group came back together to be featured on a song with Ayce Nyce, which has been streamed over 1 million times worldwide.

PTAF came together at Crenshaw Charter.<ref>Jeffries, David. [http://www.allmusic.com/artist/ptaf-mn0003217392/biography PTAF Biography], ''AllMusic''. Accessed July 26, 2016.</ref> The song was released May 9, 2012 accompanied by a music video. The music video was filmed in front of a silver gate at the school SEA Crenshaw.{{citation needed|date=October 2016}} On 29 March 2014, PTAF joined [[Vevo]] and released a more updated music video. Originally the girls were going to say, "I don't like that bitch" repetitively but used "boss" to promote female empowerment.<ref name = "Fader"/> The song gained popularity when used [[viral video|virally]] by users on the video site [[Vine (website)|Vine]].<ref name=bet>[http://www.bet.com/news/music/2014/04/25/ptaf-describe-what-it-means-to-be-a-boss.html#! PTAF Describes What It Means To Be A "Boss"]. [[BET]], April 25, 2014.</ref><ref>[http://thesource.com/2014/04/02/capitol-records-sign-vine-phenomenon-ptaf-the-first-female-rap-group-out-of-south-central-la/ Capitol Records Sign Vine Phenomenon PTAF, The First Female Rap Group Out Of South Central LA]. ''[[The Source]]'', April 2, 2014.</ref>

PTAF signed with [[Capitol Records]] around the time that [[Nicki Minaj]] released an unauthorized remix of "Boss Ass Bitch".<ref>Ramirez, Erika. [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5855088/nicki-minaj-releases-remix-to-ptafs-boss-ass-bitch-listen "Nicki Minaj Releases Remix to PTAF's 'Boss Ass Bitch': Listen"] ''Billboard.com''. December 30, 2013. Accessed July 26, 2016.</ref> Some press outlets reported that Minaj's mix drew Capitol's attention to the group,<ref>[http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/01/a-nicki-minaj-song-gets-three-girls-a-record-deal/ A Nicki Minaj Song Gets Three Girls A Record Deal]. ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', January 31, 2014.</ref> while others related that Capitol was already interested in the group at the time Minaj's remix was released.<ref name=bet/> After "Boss Ass Bitch" was placed in the movie ''[[The Bronze (film)|The Bronze]]'', the group was contacted by actress [[Melissa Rauch]] to collaborate an original song for the film, which yielded the track "Fuck That".<ref>[http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/bronze-rap-song-melissa-rauch-876709 Hear Melissa Rauch Drop F-Bombs With PTAF in 'The Bronze' Rap Song]. ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'', March 18, 2016.</ref> On May 14, 2021, Nicki Minaj officially released her "Beam Me Up Scotty" mixtape to streaming services, which included the "Boss Ass Bitch" remix. The remix debuted and peaked at number 16 on the Billboard [[Bubbling Under Hot 100]] Chart on the chart dated May 29, 2021.
