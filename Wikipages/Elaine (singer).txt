'''Ndivhuwo Elaine Mukheli''' (born 2 April 1999), known mononymously as '''Elaine''', is a South African [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]] singer and songwriter from [[Pretoria]]. While studying law at the [[University of the Witwatersrand]], she [[Self-publishing|self-released]] her debut EP ''[[Elements (Elaine album)|Elements]]'' in September 2019.<ref name="M&G">{{Cite news|last=Hlalethwa|first=Zaza|date=22 November 2019|title=Elaine is in her element|work=[[Mail & Guardian]]|url=https://mg.co.za/article/2019-11-22-00-elaine-is-in-her-element/|url-status=live|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> The EP reached No. 1 on both the national [[iTunes]] and [[Apple Music]] album charts, making her the first independent female artist to do so.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Nxumalo|first=Lee|date=8 April 2020|title=Elaine is South Africa’s new R&B It Girl|work=[[Red Bull]]|url=https://www.redbull.com/za-en/elaine-is-south-africas-new-rb-it-girl|url-status=live|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://947.co.za/articles/2020/05/05/zweli-chats-to-golden-girl-elaine-on-the-947top40|title="Every moment we have is a big moment. I'm grateful for their support." – Elaine|last=Mbhele|first=Zweli|publisher=[[947 (radio station)|947]]|date=8 May 2020|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref>''Elements'' was certified Platinum by [[Recording Industry of South Africa|RISA]] a year after its initial release and was nominated for Best R&B/Soul Album at the [[26th South African Music Awards]]. Elaine was subsequently [[Recording contract|signed]] to [[Columbia Records]] in December 2020. <ref name="M&G" />

Ndivhuwo Elaine Mukheli was born in [[Pretoria]] on 2 April 1999. She started singing at age six and participated in the school choir and various talent competitions while growing up.<ref name="10and5" /> In 2014 she received a gold medal at the [[World Choir Games|8th World Choir Games]] in [[Riga]], Latvia.<ref>{{Cite web|title=5 Things You May Not Know About Soulstress, Elaine|url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/triviawednesday-5-things-you-may-not-know-about-soulstress-elaine/|last=Ntsinde|first=Mandisa|date=15 January 2020|website=Zkhiphani|language=en-US|url-status=live|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Apple Music’s New Artist Spotlight, Elaine|url=https://leafrinique.co.za/apple-musics-new-artist-spotlight-elaine/|date=4 November 2019|website=Le'Afrinique|language=en-US|url-status=live|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> While attending high school, Mukheli began writing her own music.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=5 minutes with singer Elaine|url=http://www.bona.co.za/5-minutes-with-singer-elaine/|last=Mathebula|first=Kwanele|date=22 November 2019|website=Bona Magazine|language=en-GB|url-status=live|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> She began studying law at [[University of the Witwatersrand]] in approximately 2018,<ref name="M&G" /> and stated in November 2019 that she was part of the university's [[Golden Key International Honour Society|Golden Key Honour Society]].<ref name=":2" />

== Career ==

=== 2019–present: ''Elements'' ===
Elaine uploaded her debut single, "Slip Away" onto [[SoundCloud]] in February 2019, before officially self-releasing it in April 2019. Her follow-up single, "I Just Wanna Know" was released in August 2019. == Artistry ==
Elaine's musical style regarding [[Music genre|genre]] has been described as trap-soul (a blend of [[Trap music|trap]] and [[Soul music|soul]] music),<ref name="M&G" /><ref name="10and5" /><ref name="Kaunda" /> [[alternative R&B]],<ref name="AM">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/elaine-mn0002329876/biography|title=Elaine – Biography by Marcy Donelson|last=Donelson|first=Marcy|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref><ref name="Makhubo" /> [[contemporary R&B]],<ref name="AM" /><ref name="M&G" /> and [[neo-soul]].<ref name="Makhubo" /> She has cited R&B singers such as [[Lauryn Hill]] and [[Beyoncé]] as her biggest inspirations.<ref>{{Cite web|title=HYPE Magazine Interviews Elaine|url=https://hypemagazine.co.za/2019/10/02/hype-magazine-interviews-elaine/|date=2 October 2019|website=[[Hype (magazine)|Hype]]|language=en-GB|url-status=live|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> Elaine's musical style has been compared to that of [[Tsakani Mhinga]] and [[H.E.R.]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Elaine Mukheli, one SA talent you need to hear right now|url=https://www.glamour.co.za/lifestyle/glamour-guides/listen-elaine-mukheli-one-sa-talent-you-need-to-hear-right-now-44801936|last=Mkhabela|first=Gugulethu|date=13 March 2020|website=[[Glamour (magazine)|Glamour]]|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref> as well as [[Jhené Aiko]] and [[Summer Walker]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=For the Girls: Elaine is the South African singer you want to get familiar with|url=https://thenativemag.com/music/for-the-girls-south-africa-elaine/|last=Makinde|first=Tami|date=10 March 2020|website=The Native|language=en-US|url-status=live|access-date=29 May 2020}}</ref>
