'''Sara Jovanović''' ({{lang-sr-cyr|Сара Јовановић}}; {{IPA-sh|ˈsǎː.ra jo.ˈʋǎː.no.ʋit͡ɕ|pron}}; born 29 October 1993), also known under stage name '''Sara Jo''', is an [[Italians|Italian]]-born Serbian singer-songwriter and dancer. She rose to prominence as the second runner-up on the singing competition ''[[Prvi glas Srbije (series 2)|Prvi glas Srbije]]'' in 2012. As a member of [[Moje 3]], Jovanović then [[Serbia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2013|represented Serbia]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2013]] with "[[Ljubav je svuda]]".

She was also nominated for the ''Best Adria Act'' at the [[2017 MTV Europe Music Awards]].

==Life and career==
===Early life===
Born on 29 October 1993 in [[Rome]], Italy, she was raised bilingually in [[Serbian language|Serbian]] and [[Italian language|Italian]] as a single child to kindergarten teacher, Jasna Jovanović and the [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Serbia)|Ministry of Foreign Affairs]] official, Saša Jovanović.<ref name="blic">{{cite news|work=[[Blic (newspaper)|Blic]] |url=http://www.blic.rs/Zabava/Vesti/356545/Blic-sa-porodicama-takmicara-Prvog-glasa-Zaplacemo-dok-ih-gledamo |date=7 December 2012 |title="Blic" sa porodicama takmičara "Prvog glasa": Zaplačemo dok ih gledamo |language=sr |access-date=31 July 2013 |last=Krutenjuk |first=O.}}</ref><ref name="Balkanskom ulicom">{{cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yN6p9f76MJc|title=Balkanskom ulicom: Sara Jo|website=[[YouTube]]|publisher=[[RTS]]|author=Dedić, V.|language=sr|date= October 6, 2019|access-date=December 2, 2021}}</ref> She displayed interest in music and performance from an early age, practicing [[ballet]] and [[hip-hop dance]]. The family eventually permanently relocated to [[Belgrade]] in 2009, where Sara graduated from the Third Grammar School.<ref name="Balkanskom ulicom"/> She subsequently studied Italian language at the [[University of Belgrade Faculty of Philology]].<ref name="Balkanskom ulicom"/>

===2010-15: ''Prvi glas Srbije'' and Eurovision===
In early 2011, Jovanović won a singing contest organized by Serbian magazine ''Story'' with the covers she was posting on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.story.rs/video/15117-pogledajte-pobednicki-nastup-story-zvezde-sare-jovanovic.html |title=Pogledajte pobednički nastup Story Zvezde Sare Jovanović |date=18 April 2011 |work=Story |access-date=31 July 2013 |language=sr}}</ref> As a reward, she recorded her debut song "Zauvek" (''Forever''), which she performed on the popular [[late-night talk show]] ''[[Veče sa Ivanom Ivanovićem]]'', broadcast on [[Prva Srpska Televizija|TV Prva]].<ref name="story5">{{cite news |url=http://www.story.rs/vesti/svet-poznatih/18426-story-zvezda-sara-jovanovic-u-gostima-kod-ivana-ivanovica.html |date=13 October 2011 |title=Story zvezda Sara Jovanović u gostima kod Ivana Ivanovića |access-date=31 July 2013 |language=sr |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131003062655/http://www.story.rs/vesti/svet-poznatih/18426-story-zvezda-sara-jovanovic-u-gostima-kod-ivana-ivanovica.html |archive-date=3 October 2013 }}</ref> The following year Jovanović was featured on four songs released by rapper MCN for his self-titled debut album. Jovanović received wider media exposure by appearing on the [[Prvi glas Srbije (series 2)|second season]] of Serbian [[talent show|televised singing competition]] ''[[Prvi glas Srbije]]'', aired on TV Prva in 2012, where she placed third overall. Serbian edition of ''[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]]'' cited her as the "new energy on the music scene" because of her showmanship during the live show performances.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cosmopolitan.rs/celebrity/cosmo-ekskluziva/11251-sara-jovanovic-nova-energija-na-domacoj-sceni.html |date=January 2013 |access-date=31 July 2013 |language=sr |work=[[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]] |title=Sara Jovanović: Nova energija na domaćoj sceni}}</ref>

[[File:ESC2013 - Serbia 10 (crop 2).jpg|thumb|[[Moje 3]] performing at the first dress rehearsal of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2013]]; left to right: [[Mirna Radulović]], [[Nevena Božović]] and Sara Jovanović]]
In February the following year, it was announced that Jovanović, alongside two other show finalists [[Nevena Božović]] and [[Mirna Radulović]], would form a girl-group [[Moje 3]] to compete in the national selection competition to represent Serbia on the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2013]] in [[Malmö]], Sweden. They subsequently performed "[[Ljubav je svuda]]" (''Love Is Everywhere'') at the [[Serbia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2013|Beosong]] music festival, winning the contest with 42,5% of the public votes.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.rts.rs/upload/storyBoxFileData/2013/03/03/3069874/FinaleBeosong.pdf |title=Finale Beosonga – rezultati |date=3 March 2013 |access-date=13 July 2013 |language=sr |work=[[Radio Television of Serbia]]}}</ref> On 14 May, Moje 3 performed last at the Eurovision's first semi-final, placing 11th with 46 points and thus failed to qualify for the final.<ref>{{cite web|title=We have our first ten finalists! |url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=we_have_our_first_ten_finalists |work=[[Eurovision Song Contest]] |last=Leon |first=Jakov |date=14 May 2013 |access-date=31 July 2013}}</ref><ref name=semi1results>{{cite web|title=Eurovision Song Contest 2013 Semi-Final (1) |url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/history/by-year/contest?event=1783 |work=[[Eurovision Song Contest]] |date=18 May 2013 |access-date=31 July 2013}}</ref> Furthermore, they also received [[Barbara Dex]] Award for the worst dressed act.<ref name="barbaradex">{{cite news|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=moje_3_wins_the_barbara_dex_award |title=Moje 3 wins the Barbara Dex Award |work=[[Eurovision Song Contest]] |date=26 May 2013 |access-date=31 July 2013 |first=Gordon |last=Roxburgh}}</ref> Later that year Sara launched her own t-shirt line.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=moje_3_-_busier_than_ever |title=Moje 3 – busier than ever! |last=Leon |first=Jakov |work=[[Eurovision Song Contest]] |date=July 2012 |access-date=31 July 2013}}</ref> Between September and December, she also participated in the [[Tvoje lice zvuči poznato (Serbian season 1)|first Serbian series]] of ''[[Your Face Sounds Familiar]]'', where she finished as the runner-up to singer [[Ana Kokić]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.prva.rs/sr/program/emisija/story/23442/Tvoje+lice+zvu%C4%8Di+poznato.html |title=Tvoje lice zvuči poznato |work=[[Prva Srpska Televizija]] |date=21 October 2013 |language=sr}}</ref>

In June 2014, Jovanović released her first solo single, titled "Ko je ovde ko" (''Who Is Who Here'').<ref>{{cite news|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/ko-je-ovde-ko-single/id890413383 |work=[[iTunes]] |date=19 June 2014 |access-date=8 January 2015 |title=Ko je ovde ko - Single by Sara Jo}}</ref> Same year, she covered "Provokacija" (''Provocation'') by [[Montenegro|Montenegrin]] singer [[Boban Rajović]] for the movie ''[[Mali Budo]]'' and [[Moby Dick (band)|Moby Dick]]'s "Troje" (''The Three'') for the purposes of the film ''[[The Samurai in Autumn|Jesen samuraja]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://tracara.com/sara-jovanovic-prepevala-bobana-rajovica-u-rnb-verziji/ |title=Sara Jovanović prepevala Bobana Rajovića u r'n'b verziji |date=26 September 2014 |access-date=8 January 2015 |language=sr |work=Tračara.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sv0XSBjLq18|title=Sara Jo & Marko Mandić - Troje - Muzika iz filma 'Jesen samuraja'|publisher=Taramount|date=21 October 2016|access-date=17 June 2017}}</ref>

===2016-present===
In 2017, Sara Jo signed to Belgrade-based [[hip-hop]] record label Bassivity Music and released her [[electropop]] single "Nemam vremena za to" (''[I] Don't Have Time for That'') produced by [[Slobodan Veljković Coby|Coby]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.rs/video/4564/sara-jo-nemam-vremena-za-to|title=Sara Jo – ''Nemam vremena za to''|publisher=MTV Serbia|date=3 June 2017|access-date=17 June 2017}}</ref> In June, she released a [[dancehall]] promotional single "Samo ti" (''Only You'') for [[Tuborg GreenFest]] under [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Serbia]], which [[Sampling (music)|sample]]s [[Major Lazer]]'s "Tuborg Beat" courtesy of [[Mad Decent]].<ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=peQc2-m3MsI|title=#TBRGOPEN Sara Jo – Samo Ti|publisher=Tuborg Open|date=22 June 2017|access-date=27 June 2017}}</ref>

During the summer of 2019 she performed at the [[Exit (festival)|Exit]] festival and at the [[Pride parade#Serbia|Belgrade Pride]] in September.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mondo.rs/a1187834/Zabava/Muzika/EXIT-Gang-nastupaju.html|title=Exit Gang nastupaju|date=May 2019|publisher=Mondo|language=sr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://tracara.com/sara-jo-kuma-prajda-nastupaju-bojana-vunturisevic-i-zo/|title=Sara Jo kuma prajda, nastupaju Bojana Vunturišević i Zo|website=Tracara|language=sr}}</ref> Also that year, Sara Jo served as a mentor in the singing contest ''Discovered by Coke'' organised by [[Coca-Cola]]. Her candidate, Predrag Simić, eventually won the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mondo.rs/Zabava/Muzika/a1249920/Discovered-by-Coke-Predrag-Simic.html|title=Discovered by Coke - imamo pobednika!|website=mondo.rs|language=sr}}</ref>

In January 2020, Jovanović performed a medley of her hits at the [[2020 Music Awards Ceremony]].

In July the following year, she collaborated with Serbian singer [[Edita Aradinović|Edita]] on the single "Varalica" (''Cheater'').<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nova.rs/zabava/showbiz/video-preslusajte-magicni-duet-sare-jo-i-edite-aradinovic/|title=Poslušajte magični duet Sare Jo i Edite Aradinović|date=July 18, 2021|publisher=Nova.rs|author=Jovović, P.|language=sr}}</ref> In late August, Sara Jo performed at the closing ceremony of the [[WorldPride]] event in [[Copenhagen]] alongside Serbian drag queens Alex Electra and Dita Von Bill.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nova.rs/zabava/showbiz/uzivo-nastup-sare-jo-na-na-world-prajdu-u-kopenhagenu/|title=Nastup Sare Jo na World Prajdu u Kopenhagenu|date=August 21, 2021|publisher=Nova.rs|author=Stoisavljević, L.|language=sr}}</ref>
