'''André Robin De Shields''' (born January 12, 1946) is an American actor, singer, dancer, director, and choreographer.

De Shields originated the role of [[Hermes]] on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] in the musical ''[[Hadestown]]'', winning the [[73rd Tony Awards|2019]] [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actor in a Musical|Tony Award for Best Actor in a Featured Role in a Musical]] and the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards|2020]] [[Grammy Award for Best Musical Theater Album]] for his performance. He has also appeared on television, and won an [[Primetime Emmy Awards|Primetime Emmy Award]] for Outstanding Individual Achievement for his performance in the 1982 [[NBC]] broadcast of ''[[Ain't Misbehavin' (musical)|Ain't Misbehavin'<nowiki/>]]''.

==Early life and education==
André Robin De Shields was born on January 12, 1946 in [[Dundalk, Maryland]], to Mary Gunther and John De Shields. He was raised in [[Baltimore|Baltimore, Maryland]], the ninth of eleven children. De Shields obtained his high school diploma at [[Baltimore City College]] in 1964, then attended [[Wilmington College (Ohio)|Wilmington College]], where he starred in a production of [[Lorraine Hansberry]]'s ''[[A Raisin in the Sun]]''. He then transferred colleges and earned his [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in [[English literature]] from the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison|University of Wisconsin, Madison]] in 1970. In 1991, De Shields received his [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in [[African-American studies|African American studies]] from the [[Gallatin School of Individualized Study]] of [[New York University]].

He currently serves as an [[adjunct professor]] at the [[Gallatin School of Individualized Study]] of [[New York University]].

==Career==
De Shields began his professional career in the 1969 Chicago production of ''[[Hair (musical)|Hair]]'', which led to a role in ''[[The Me Nobody Knows]]'' and participation in Chicago's [[Organic Theater Company]]. He performed in a number of [[Off-Off-Broadway|off-off-Broadway]] productions at [[La MaMa Experimental Theatre Club]] in the [[East Village, Manhattan|East Village of Manhattan]] during the 1970s, 1980s, and early 1990s. These included Ken Rubenstein's ''Sacred Guard'' (1973),<ref>La MaMa Archives Digital Collections. [http://catalog.lamama.org/index.php/Detail/Occurrence/Show/occurrence_id/1235 "Production: ''Sacred Guard'' (1973)". Accessed June 20, 2018.]</ref> Lamar Alford's ''Thoughts'' (1974),<ref>La MaMa Archives Digital Collections. [http://catalog.lamama.org/index.php/Detail/Occurrence/Show/occurrence_id/1433 "Production: ''Thoughts'' (1974)". Accessed June 20, 2018.]</ref> and the ''Cotton Club Gala'' with music by [[Aaron Bell]] and directed by [[Ellen Stewart]] (1985).<ref>La MaMa Archives Digital Collections. [http://catalog.lamama.org/index.php/Detail/Occurrence/Show/occurrence_id/884 "Production: ''Cotton Club Gala'' (1985)". Accessed June 20, 2018.]</ref> He co-wrote (with Judith Cohen) and directed an evening of songs called ''Judith and the Cohen Sisters in Midnight in Manhattan'' at La MaMa in September/October 1984.<ref>La MaMa Archives Digital Collections. [http://catalog.lamama.org/index.php/Detail/Occurrence/Show/occurrence_id/2854 "Production: ''Judith and the Cohen Sisters in Midnight in Manhattan'' (1984)". Accessed June 20, 2018.]</ref> He directed a production of Chico Kasinoir's ''The Adventures of Rhubarb: The Rock and Roll Rabbit'' in 1985<ref>La MaMa Archives Digital Collections. [http://catalog.lamama.org/index.php/Detail/Occurrence/Show/occurrence_id/2865 "Production: ''Adventures of Rhubarb: The Rock and Roll Rabbit, The'' (1985)". Accessed June 20, 2018.]</ref> and a production of his own work, ''Saint Tous'', to celebrate [[Black History Month]] at La MaMa in February 1991.<ref>La MaMa Archives Digital Collections. [http://catalog.lamama.org/index.php/Detail/Occurrence/Show/occurrence_id/4519 "Production: ''Saint Tous'' (1991)". Accessed June 20, 2018.]</ref>

He made his [[Broadway debut]] as Xander in [[Stuart Gordon]]'s 1973 ''[[Warp!]]'' and next appeared in [[Paul Jabara]]'s 1973 ''[[Rachael Lily Rosenbloom (And Don't You Ever Forget It)]]'', which closed during previews.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ibdb.com/broadway-production/warp-3173|title=Warp – Broadway Play – Original |website=www.ibdb.com|access-date=June 20, 2018}}</ref> He then appeared in the title role of ''[[The Wiz]]'', [[Charlie Smalls]] and [[William F. Brown (writer)|William F. Brown]]'s 1975 musical directed by [[Geoffrey Holder]].<ref name="americantheatre.org">{{cite news|url=http://americantheatre.org/2014/10/giants-in-the-earth-a-tribute-to-geoffrey-holder/|title=Giants in the Earth: A Tribute to Geoffrey Holder|date=October 17, 2014|work=AmericanTheatre.org}}</ref>

After choreographing two [[Bette Midler]] musicals, De Shields returned to Broadway to perform in the musical revue ''[[Ain't Misbehavin' (musical)|Ain't Misbehavin'<nowiki/>]]'' in 1978.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F3071EFF3E5413728DDDA90A94DA405B888BF1D3&scp=2&sq=Ain%27t+Misbehavin%27+&st=p|title='Here 'Tis' - A Musical Bow to Fats Waller; The Cast|last=Wilson|first=John S.|date=February 20, 1978|newspaper=The New York Times|access-date=January 17, 2012|pages=C13}}</ref> The original production ran for over 1,600 shows and De Shields earned a 1978 [[Drama Desk Award|Drama Desk]] nomination for his performance. Three years later, he returned to Broadway to perform in ''Stardust: The Mitchell Parrish Musical'', a musical revue featuring the lyricist's work with [[Hoagy Carmichael]], [[Benny Goodman]], [[Duke Ellington]], and [[Leroy Anderson]].

In 1984, De Shields wrote, choreographed, directed, and starred in ''André De Shields' Haarlem Nocturne'', a Broadway musical revue featuring standards from the [[Great American Songbook|American songbook]], [[Pop music|pop hits]] from the early 1960s, and De Shields' own songs.<ref name="New York Times">{{cite news|url= https://www.nytimes.com/1984/01/31/arts/cabaret-de-shields-s-harlem-nocturne.html|work=New York Times| first=John S.| last=Wilson| title= Cabaret: De Shields's 'Harlem Nocturne'| date=January 31, 1984}}</ref> The revue was produced at the [[Latin Quarter (nightclub)|Latin Quarter]] and at La MaMa (with music by [[Marc Shaiman]]).<ref>La MaMa Archives Digital Collections. [http://catalog.lamama.org/index.php/Detail/Occurrence/Show/occurrence_id/2843 "Production: ''Haarlem Nocturne'' (1984)". Accessed June 20, 2018.]</ref> He appeared in a revival of ''[[Ain't Misbehavin' (musical)|Ain't Misbehavin'<nowiki/>]]'' in 1988, and next appeared on Broadway in 1997 as the Jester in ''[[Play On!]]'', a musical based on Ellington's songs. De Shields earned [[Tony Award|Tony]] and Drama Desk nominations for his performance.<ref name="Playbill.com">{{cite news|url=  http://www.playbill.com/news/article/black-fest-97-andre-de-shields-back-after-play-on-71225|work=Playbill.com| first=Linda| last=Armstrong| title= Black Fest '97: Andre De Shields Back After Play On!| date=August 12, 1997}}</ref>

In 2000, De Shields originated the role of Noah "Horse" T. Simmons in the [[Terrence McNally]] / [[David Yazbek]] musical adaptation of the [[The Full Monty|film]] ''[[The Full Monty (musical)|The Full Monty]]''. As with ''Play On!'', De Shields earned both Tony and Drama Desk nominations for this performance.<ref name="play1">{{cite news|url=https://playbill.com/features/article/playbill-on-lines-brief-encounter-with-andre-de-shields-101514/print |work=Playbill.com |first=Christine |last=Ehren |title=Playbill On-Line's Brief Encounter with Andre de Shields |date=May 29, 2001 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141021100225/https://playbill.com/features/article/playbill-on-lines-brief-encounter-with-andre-de-shields-101514/print |archive-date=October 21, 2014 }}</ref> In 2004, he appeared in the Broadway production of [[Mark Medoff]]'s ''Prymate'' at the [[Longacre Theatre]].<ref name="Talkin' Broadway">{{cite news|url=  http://www.talkinbroadway.com/world/Prymate.html| work=Talkin' Broadway| first=Mathew| last=Murray| title= Broadway Reviews: Prymate| date=May 5, 2004}}</ref> In 2008, he received a Drama Desk nomination for his performance in an [[off-Broadway]] production of [[Langston Hughes]]' ''[[Black Nativity]]''. In 2009, he appeared on Broadway opposite [[Joan Allen]] and [[Jeremy Irons]] in ''[[Impressionism (play)|Impressionism]]''.<ref name="New York Magazine">{{cite news|url= https://nymag.com/arts/theater/features/55479/|work=New York Magazine| first=Boris| last=Kachka|  title= The Impressionist: André De Shields| date=March 22, 2009}}</ref> The play ran through May 2009 at the [[Gerald Schoenfeld Theatre|Gerald Schoenfeld Theater]].

De Shields' [[Regional theatre in the United States|regional theatre]] credits include ''[[Play On (musical)|Play On!]]'', ''[[The Full Monty]]'', ''[[Waiting For Godot]]'', ''[[The Man Who Came to Dinner]]'', ''[[Death of a Salesman]]'', ''[[Dushyanta|Dusyanta]]: A Tale of [[Kālidāsa|Kalidasa]]'', ''The Gospel According to James'', and ''[[Camino Real (play)|Camino Real]]''. In 2013, he portrayed [[Akela (The Jungle Book)|Akela]] and [[King Louie]] in the world premiere of [[Mary Zimmerman]]'s adaptation of [[Rudyard Kipling]]'s ''[[The Jungle Book]]'', a co-production of the [[Goodman Theatre]] and [[Huntington Theatre Company]]. De Shields received his 3rd [[Jeff Award]] (Outstanding Achievement in the category of Actor in a Supporting Role – Musical) for his role as King Louie, and garnered an [[Elliot Norton Awards]] nomination for Outstanding Musical Performance by an Actor as well as an [[IRNE Awards]] nomination for Best Supporting Actor – Musical.

De Shields has portrayed Barrett Rude Sr. in ''[[The Fortress of Solitude (musical)|The Fortress of Solitude]]'', the musical based on [[Jonathan Lethem]]'s novel ''[[The Fortress of Solitude (novel)|The Fortress of Solitude]]'', since its inception in 2012 at [[Vassar College]]. ''The Fortress of Solitude'' premiered at the [[Dallas Theater Center]] in spring 2014,<ref name="The Dallas Morning News">{{cite news|url=http://www.dallasnews.com/entertainment/arts/headlines/20140309-hopes-are-high-and-sights-quietly-broadway-set-for-the-fortress-of-solitude-premiering-in-dallas.ece|work=Dallas Morning News| title= Hopes are high and sights quietly Broadway-set for 'The Fortress of Solitude,' premiering in Dallas| date=March 9, 2014}}</ref> and the off-Broadway production of ''The Fortress of Solitude'', co-produced with [[The Public Theater]], ran through November 2014. De Shields, [[Lillias White]], [[Stefanie Powers]], and [[Georgia Engel]] starred in the new musical ''[[Gotta Dance]]'', directed and choreographed by [[Jerry Mitchell]]. The musical began performances on December 13, 2015, at Chicago's [[Bank of America Theatre]] and ran through January 17, 2016.<ref>Cox, Gordon. [https://www.variety.com/2015/legit/.../gotta-dance-cast-broadway-120153044 "Stefanie Powers, Georgia Engel Join Cast of Broadway-Bound 'Gotta Dance'"] ''Variety'', June 29, 2015</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Gans|first=Andrew| url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/gotta-dance-helmed-by-jerry-mitchell-extends-chicago-run-broadway-debut-set-for-fall-375512#| title=Gotta Dance, Helmed by Jerry Mitchell, Extends Chicago Run; Broadway Debut Set for Fall| work=Playbill| date=December 14, 2015}}</ref>

He played [[Hermes]] in the Broadway musical ''[[Hadestown]]'', which began previews on March 22, 2019, at the [[Walter Kerr Theatre]]. He received the [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actor in a Musical]] for ''Hadestown'' on his third nomination.<ref>{{cite news|last=Hetrick|first=Adam|date=March 12, 2019|title=Hadestown Begins on Broadway March 22|work=Playbill|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/hadestown-begins-on-broadway-march-22}}</ref> On January 26, 2021, it was announced that De Shields would reprise his role as Hermes in ''Live From Mount Olympus,'' a narrative podcast for tweens directed by [[Rachel Chavkin]] and Zhailon Levingston.<ref>{{Cite web|last=White|first=Peter|date=January 26, 2021|title='This American Life' Distributor PRX, Onassis Foundation & Theater Company TEAM Launch Tween Podcast Series 'Live from Mount Olympus'|url=https://deadline.com/2021/01/prx-team-tween-podcast-series-live-from-mount-olympus-1234680393/|url-status=live|access-date=March 17, 2021|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref> The series was produced by [[Onassis Foundation|The Onassis Foundation]] and TRAX from [[PRX (radio)|PRX]]. De Shields' co-stars include fellow ''Hadestown'' cast member [[Amber Gray]], Divine Garland, [[Vinie Burrows]], Kristen Sieh, and more.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Harms|first=Talaura|date=January 26, 2021|title=In the News: André De Shields Voices Hermes in New Podcast With Rachel Chavkin, More|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/in-the-news-andre-de-shields-voices-hermes-in-new-podcast-with-rachel-chavkin-more|access-date=March 17, 2021|website=Playbill|language=en}}</ref>

De Shields has appeared on television on ''[[Another World (TV series)|Another World]]'', ''[[Cosby (TV series)|Cosby]]'', ''[[Sex and the City]]'', ''[[Great Performances]]'', ''[[Lipstick Jungle (TV series)|Lipstick Jungle]]'', ''[[Law & Order]],'' and ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]''. He won an [[Primetime Emmy Award]] for Outstanding Individual Achievement for his performance in the 1982 [[NBC]] broadcast of ''[[Ain't Misbehavin' (musical)|Ain't Misbehavin'<nowiki/>]]'', and played [[Tweedledum and Tweedledee|Tweedledum]] in a [[Alice in Wonderland (1983 film)|1983 televised production]] of ''[[Alice's Adventures in Wonderland|Alice in Wonderland]]'' that also featured [[Eve Arden]], [[Richard Burton]], [[Colleen Dewhurst]], [[James Coco]], [[Kaye Ballard]], and [[Nathan Lane]]. Most recently, De Shields appeared in [[John Mulaney]]'s ''[[John Mulaney & the Sack Lunch Bunch]]'' in which he sang "Algebra Song!" The comedy/musical/variety special was released on [[Netflix]] on December 24, 2019. In 2020, he played the role of Chubby, a recurring character, in the television series ''[[Katy Keene (TV series)|Katy Keene]]'' on [[The CW]].

On December 28, 2020, it was announced that De Shields will star as Anton Ego in a benefit concert presentation of ''[[Ratatouille the Musical]]'', an [[internet meme]] that originated on [[TikTok]], inspired by the 2007 [[Walt Disney Pictures|Disney]]/[[Pixar]] [[Ratatouille (film)|film]]. The concert streamed exclusively on [[TodayTix]] on January 1, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2020/12/ratatouille-the-tiktok-musical-cast-wayne-brady-tituss-burgess-adam-lambert-1234661967/|title = 'Ratatouille: The TikTok Musical' All-Star Cast to Include Wayne Brady, Tituss Burgess & Adam Lambert|date = December 28, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
De Shields is openly gay.<ref>{{cite news|last=Teeman|first=Tim|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/broadway-star-andre-de-shields-on-hadestown-tony-awards-racism-sexuality-and-fulfilling-his-parents-dreams|title=Broadway Star André De Shields on 'Hadestown,' Tony Awards, Racism, Sexuality, and Fulfilling His Parents' Dreams|work=The Daily Beast|date=April 30, 2019}}</ref> He has been living with [[HIV]] for over 30 years.<ref>{{Cite web|date=February 3, 2020|first=Juan Michael|last=Porter|title=Broadway Legend André De Shields Is Thriving at His Own Pace|url=https://www.thebody.com/article/broadway-legend-andre-de-shields|access-date=December 6, 2020|website=www.thebody.com|language=en-US}}</ref>
