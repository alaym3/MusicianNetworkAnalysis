'''Charlotte Mary O'Connor''',<ref>[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/charlotte-oc-mn0003212530/biography Artist Biography] by Andy Kellman, AllMusic.</ref> better known by her stage name '''Charlotte OC''', is a British singer-songwriter.

==Biography==
Charlotte O'Connor is the daughter of a half-[[Malawi|Malawian]], half-Indian mother and Irish father.<ref>[http://primarytalent.com/charlotte-oc/ "Charlotte OC"] at Primary Talent International.</ref> She grew up in [[Blackburn]], Lancashire, and attended [[Westholme School]]. She began playing the guitar at 15 years of age and attended weekend classes at the [[Liverpool Institute for Performing Arts]].<ref name=LT-2008>Sophie Wyllie,[http://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/news/3988173.Diana_Vickers__best_friend_has_X_Factor_after_signing_record_deal/ "Diana Vickers' best friend has X Factor after signing record deal"], ''[[Lancashire Telegraph]]'', 18 December 2008.</ref> When she was 16 years old, she became a global ambassador for surfwear brand [[Quiksilver]].<ref>Philip Javens, [http://soulculture.com/features/interviews/charlotte-oconnor-introducing-blackburns-acoustic-hip-pop-interview/ "Charlotte O'Connor: Introducing Blackburn's 'Acoustic Hip Pop' | Interview"], ''SoulCulture'', 4 October 2011.</ref><ref>[http://www.glassbutterbeach.com/charlotte-oconnor-confirmed-for-saturday/ "Charlotte O'Connor (Confirmed)"], ''Glassbutterbeach.com'', 3 April 2012.</ref>

O'Connor's music attracted the attention of agent Sam Bush, who signed her to a contract and arranged gigs for her in the United States. She signed a four-album recording deal with [[Columbia Records]] in late 2008, when she was 18 years old.<ref name=LT-2008/> Her debut single, "Treasure Island", was released in September 2011 and her debut album, ''For Kenny'' (produced by [[Mario Caldato Jr.]]), followed in November.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/leisure/9129966.charlotte-oconnor-blackburn-september-22/|title=What's on: Charlotte O'Connor, Blackburn, September 22|newspaper=[[Lancashire Telegraph]]|accessdate=2020-03-22}}</ref> O'Connor was dropped by Columbia and subsequently took work in her mother's hairdressing salon.<ref name="BBC - 24Nov2013 - Charlotte OC: tipped for success in 2014"/>

O'Connor signed with Stranger Records in 2013.<ref>[http://strangerrecords.com/artist/charlotte-oc/ "Charlotte OC"] at Stranger Records.</ref> She released her first EP, ''Colour My Heart'', in November 2013 under the stage name '''Charlotte OC'''; the same month, the [[BBC]] classed her as "tipped for success in 2014"<ref name="BBC - 24Nov2013 - Charlotte OC: tipped for success in 2014"/> and [[Digital Spy]] named her "one to watch".<ref name="Digital Spy - 06Nov2013 - Ones to watch: Watch Charlotte OC perform 'Cut The Rope'">{{cite news|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a529130/ones-to-watch-watch-charlotte-oc-perform-cut-the-rope.html|title=Ones to watch: Watch Charlotte OC perform 'Cut The Rope'|last=Copsey|first=Robert|date=6 November 2013|work=[[Digital Spy]]}}</ref> Her second EP, ''Strange'', was released by [[Polydor Records]] in September 2014,<ref name="The Line Of Best Fit - 14Aug2014 - Watch the video for Charlotte OC’s mysterious suburban love-ritual Strange">{{cite news|url=http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/discovery/charlotte-oc-strange|title=Watch the video for Charlotte OC's mysterious suburban love-ritual 'Strange'|last=Battolla|first=Melanie|date=14 August 2014|work=The Line of Best Fit}}</ref> and its track "Hangover" was playlisted on [[BBC Radio 1]].<ref name=BBCIntroducing>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/introducing/posts/On-the-playlist-Charlotte-OC-Hangover " On the playlist: Charlotte OC – Hangover"], ''Introducing...'', BBC Music, 30 July 2014.</ref> ''Colour My Heart'' and ''Strange'' were recorded with producer [[Tim Anderson (musician)|Tim Anderson]] in Los Angeles.<ref>[http://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2014/09/new-noise-charlotte-oc/ "New Noise: Charlotte OC"], ''Wonderland''.</ref> In March 2015, O'Connor released her third EP, ''Burning'', which features the single "If My House Was Burning".<ref>Andrew Trendell, [http://www.gigwise.com/features/98927/charlotte-oc-interview-burning-ep-berlin-and-blackburn "Charlotte OC: 'I Love to mix the ugly with the beautiful'"], ''Gigwise'', 6 March 2015.</ref>

Her first album, the Anderson-produced ''Careless People'', was released on 31 March 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.universalmusic.fr/4055-careless-people-le-premier-album-de-charlotte-oc.html|title="Careless People", le premier album de Charlotte OC|website=Universalmusic.fr|accessdate=2020-03-22}}</ref> It spawned four singles, "Darkest Hour", "Blackout", "Shell", and "Medicine Man".<ref>Myles Tanzer, [http://www.thefader.com/2016/09/29/charlotte-oc-is-trapped-in-her-darkest-hour-video "Charlotte OC Is Trapped In Her 'Darkest Hour' Video"], ''Fader'', 29 September 2016.</ref>

On 15 May 2020, O'Connor released the EP ''Oh the Agony, Oh the Ecstasy''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Mosk|first=Mitch|date=15 May 2020|title=Feature: Charlotte OC's 'Oh the Agony, oh the Ecstasy' EP is a passionate, soulful surrender|url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/oaoe-charlotte-oc-oh-the-agony-oh-the-ecstasy-ep-interview-feature/|url-status=live|website=Atwood Magazine}}</ref> featuring the three previously published songs "This Pain", "Freedom" and "Falling for You" as well as two new songs.

Since April 2021, the singer-songwriter released several new singles and EP's of her upcoming second studio album, "Here comes Trouble", whose O'Connor released on 15th of October 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=21 September 2021|title=Charlotte OC on Instagram|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CUF9vnqDxZ1/?utm_medium=share_sheet|url-status=live|website=Instagram}}</ref>

==Artistry==
O'Connor described her music as being influenced by [[gospel music|gospel]], [[soul music|soul]], and [[house music|house]] musics, as well as by the [[electronic music]] she heard at the German nightclub [[Berghain]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/ryg8d5/youneedtohearthis-sleeve-notes-charlotte-oc|title=Sleeve Notes: Charlotte OC|website=Vice.com|date=3 September 2014}}</ref><ref>Emily McDermott, [http://www.papermag.com/2014/09/charlotte_oc.php ""UK Singer Charlotte OC Is About To Blow Up"], ''Paper'', 16 September 2014.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/next-wave-class-of-2014-charlotte-oc|title=Features|website=Clashmusic.com|accessdate=2020-03-22}}</ref> As her parents surrounded her with folk and soul music, she lists [[Alicia Keys]], [[Leonard Cohen]], [[Lou Reed]], [[Joni Mitchell]], [[Aretha Franklin]], [[Billie Holiday]] and [[Marvin Gaye]] among her major influences.<ref name="BBC - 24Nov2013 - Charlotte OC: tipped for success in 2014">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-25031405|title= Charlotte OC: tipped for success in 2014 |last= Geoghegan|first= Kev|date=24 November 2013|publisher=BBC}}</ref> [[Noisey]] wrote that O'Connor "blends [[Bat for Lashes]]' cosmic mystic pop, [[Stevie Nicks]]' breathless vulnerability, and possesses the vocal chops to keep up with [[Adele]]",<ref>Kim Taylor Bennett, [http://noisey.vice.com/blog/listen-to-justin-martins-rad-remix-of-charlotte-ocs-strange-rules "Listen to Justin Martin's Rad Remix of Charlotte OC's 'Strange'"], ''Noisey'', 5 September 2014.</ref> while ''Hunger'' magazine wrote that her output "feels spiritual and almost occultist".<ref>[http://www.hungertv.com/feature/premiere-charlotte-oc-colour-heart/ "Premiere: Charlotte OC – Colour My Heart"], ''Hungertv.com'', 30 September 2013.</ref>
