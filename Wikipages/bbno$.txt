'''Alexander Leon Gumuchian''' ({{IPAc-en|g|ÊŠ|'|m|u:|tÊƒ|i:|É›|n}}; born June 30, 1995), better known as '''bbno$''' (pronounced as "baby no money"),<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://undergroundunderdogs.com/2018/11/07/bbnos-interview-profile-yung-gravy-china/|title=Meet bbno$: The Definition of Fun Rap|last=Lyazov|first=Valentin|date=November 7, 2018|website=Underground Underdogs|language=en-US|access-date=September 7, 2019}}</ref> is a Canadian rapper, singer, and songwriter from [[Vancouver]], [[British Columbia]]. He is best known for his collaborations with rapper [[Yung Gravy]] as well as his 2019 single, "[[Lalala (song)|Lalala]]" with producer [[Y2K (record producer)|Y2K]], which has reached over 700 million plays on the streaming service [[Spotify]].

== Early life ==
Alexander Leon Gumuchian is a Canadian-Armenian rapper, he was born in [[Vancouver]]<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodjuicebox.com/this-is-how-bbnos-got-big/|title=This is how bbno$ got big in China â€“ and why he's never going back to school|last=Fox|first=Isaac Nikolai|date=February 27, 2018|website=Good Juice Box|language=en-US|access-date=September 7, 2019}}</ref> in 1995. He was [[Homeschooling|home schooled]] before he began high school.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thisfunktional.com/2019/08/y2k-bbno-release-official-video-for.html|title=Y2K, BBNO$ release official video for LALALA|website=Thisfunktional|access-date=September 10, 2019}}</ref> While growing up, his mother had encouraged him to learn piano but he had always struggled with [[music theory]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://lyricallemonade.com/p/from-memes-to-the-masses-the-wild-rise-of-bbno|title=FROM MEMES TO THE MASSES: THE WILD RISE OF BBNO$|last=Harvey|first=Brodie|date=September 3, 2019|website=[[Lyrical Lemonade]]|access-date=September 10, 2019}}</ref> Gumuchian claims that he was good with rhythm and that he enjoyed playing [[Djembe]] drums, but did not listen to music for recreation until the age of 15.<ref name=":2" />

In 2017, Gumuchian released his first EP, ''Baby Gravy'', as a collaboration with [[Yung Gravy]],<ref name=":6">{{Citation|title=Baby Gravy â€“ EP by Yung Gravy & bbno$|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/baby-gravy-ep/1292505805|work=[[Apple Music]]|language=en-us}}</ref> shortly before releasing his debut studio album, ''Bb Steps'', and his second collaborative EP, ''Whatever'', with [[So Loki]] in 2018.<ref name=":4">{{Citation|title=Bb Steps by bbno$|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/bb-steps/1410016493|work=[[Apple Music]]|language=en-us}}</ref><ref name=":7">{{Citation|title=Whatever by bbno$ & So Loki|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/whatever/1439962893|work=[[Apple Music]]|language=en-us}}</ref>

=== 2019: ''Recess'', "Lalala" and ''I Don't Care at All'' ===
In early 2019, Gumuchian released his second studio album, ''Recess'', which draws inspiration from the original [[Disney Channel|Disney]] show of the [[Recess (TV series)|same name]], and includes features from [[Y2K (record producer)|Y2K]] and Trippy Tha Kid.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/recess/1447703608|title=Recess by bbno$|website=[[Apple Music]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dailychiefers.com/bbno-unleashes-some-heat-with-new-project-recess/|title=Bbno$ Unleashes Some Heat With New Project, "Recess"|last=Mike|date=January 28, 2019|website=Daily Chiefers|language=en-US|access-date=September 12, 2019}}</ref> Many tracks off of ''Recess'' gained millions of streams on [[Spotify]].<ref name=":5" /> Gumuchian and [[Y2K (record producer)|Y2K]] have been recognized for marketing their single "[[Lalala (song)|Lalala]]" online by using various websites and online apps, namely [[Tinder (app)|Tinder]], [[Instagram]] [[Meme|meme accounts]], [[TikTok]] and [[Craigslist]].<ref name=":9">{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/y2k-bbno-lalala-tiktok-tinder-craigslist-861097/|title=How Y2k and bbno$ Turned â€˜Lalalaâ€™ Into a Hit, One Tinder Match at a Time|last=Leight|first=Elias|date=July 24, 2019|website=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref><ref name=":5" /> The song was able to peak on over 20 charts around the world and gain over 400 million [[Streaming media|streams]] and 500,000 sales in the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://acharts.co/song/122878|title=Lalala by Y2K and Bbnos â€“ Music Charts|website=ACharts}}</ref><ref name=":9" /><ref name=":10" />

Later in 2019, Gumuchian would go on to release another album, ''I Don't Care At All''. The album featured various pre-released singles, "Slop", "Pouch", and "Shining On My Ex", the latter of which featured frequent collaborator Yung Gravy. The album was solely produced by Y2K.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/bbnos-releases-i-dont-care-at-all-album-new-mixtape.119762.html|title=bbno$ Releases "I Don't Care At All" Album|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=December 15, 2019}}</ref>

== Artistry ==

=== Musical style ===
Gumuchian has described his own music as "oxymoronic rap" that is "ignorant but melodic".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://beatroute.ca/2019/01/25/bbno-is-having-more-fun-than-you/|title=Bbno$ Is Having More Fun Than You|last=Nazarian|first=Andrea|date=January 25, 2019|website=[[BeatRoute Magazine]]|language=en-US|url-status=dead|access-date=September 12, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191007024701/https://beatroute.ca/2019/01/25/bbno-is-having-more-fun-than-you/|archive-date=October 7, 2019}}</ref>

=== Influences ===
Gumuchian grew up listening to [[Dubstep|straight bass dubstep]] and house music from names like [[Datsik (musician)|Datsik]] and [[Excision (musician)|Excision]], before listening to hip-hop artists, namely [[Tupac Shakur|Tupac]], [[Gucci Mane]] and [[Chief Keef]].<ref name=":2" /> He has cited [[Yung Lean]] and [[Pouya (rapper)|Pouya]] as his main inspirations as a rapper.<ref name=":11" /><ref name="THHGURU Exclusive Interview: BBNO">{{Cite web|url=http://www.thhguru.com/newmusic/2017/7/28/thhguru-exclusive-interview-bbno|title=THHGURU Exclusive Interview: BBNO$|date=July 28, 2017|website=TheHipHopGuru|language=en-US|access-date=September 12, 2019|archive-date=October 7, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191007024702/http://www.thhguru.com/newmusic/2017/7/28/thhguru-exclusive-interview-bbno|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Gumuchian lives in [[Vancouver]] but has previously lived in [[Kelowna]], where he received a degree in [[Kinesiology|human kinesiology]] at [[University of British Columbia (Okanagan Campus)|University of British Columbia Okanagan]] in 2019.<ref name="THHGURU Exclusive Interview: BBNO"/><ref name=":2" />
