'''King & Prince''' is a five-member Japanese [[Japanese idol|idol group]] under [[Johnny & Associates]] that debuted in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=ジャニーズ事務所『King & Prince』が今春CDデビュー|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2104159/full/|website=[[ORICON NEWS]]|access-date=26 February 2018|language=ja}}</ref> The group was originally a [[Johnny's Jr.]] unit, ''Mr. King vs Mr. Prince'' that was composed of 2 separate units: Mr. King and Prince.<ref>{{cite web|title=King & Prince to be making their CD debut this spring!|url=http://www.johnnys-net.jp/page?id=text&dataId=1421&lang=en_us|website=Johnny's net|access-date=1 March 2018}}</ref> The units later performed as one group for [[TV Asahi]]'s ''Roppongi Hills Natsu Matsuri Summer Station'' in June 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Johnny & Associates to debut new group "King & Prince" in spring!|url=http://aramajapan.com/news/johnny-associates-debut-new-group-king-prince-spring/84272/|website=ARAMA! JAPAN|access-date=26 February 2018}}</ref> Their debut was announced at a press conference on 17 January 2018 along with the establishment of Johnny & Associates' new record label under [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music]], ''Johnny's Universe'', with King & Prince becoming the first artists to sign under the label.<ref>{{cite web|title=ジャニーズJr．：新ユニット「Mr．King　VS　Mr．Prince」誕生　名前は「2時間前にジャニーさんが決めw」|url=https://mantan-web.jp/article/20150605dog00m200022000c.html|website=MANTANWEB|access-date=26 February 2018|language=ja}}</ref> They have sold 7.3 million physical copies in Japan.

The group used to have six members: Sho Hirano, Ren Nagase, Kaito Takahashi, Yuta Kishi, Yuta Jinguji and Genki Iwahashi. However, Genki Iwahashi withdrew from King & Prince and Johnny & Associates on 31 March 2021 due to [[anxiety disorder]].

==History==
===2015–2017: Beginnings===
[[Johnny & Associates]] announced the group's formation in a press conference on 5 June 2015. The group was formed to be special supporters for [[TV Asahi]]'s ''Summer Station'' event.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nikkansports.com/entertainment/news/1488074.html |script-title=ja:夏限定ジャニーズＪｒ選抜「キンプリ」誕生 |date=6 June 2015 |access-date=5 December 2018 |publisher=[[Nikkan Sports]] |language=ja }}</ref> Mr. King vs Mr. Prince was divided into two subgroups: "Mr. King", consisting of [[Sho Hirano]], Ren Nagase, and Kaito Takahashi and "Mr. Prince", consisting of [[Yuta Kishi]], Yuta Jinguji, and Genki Iwahashi.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2053852/full/ |script-title=ja:ジャニーズJr.平野紫耀、永瀬廉ら新ユニット『Mr. King vs Mr. Prince』結成 |date=6 June 2015 |access-date=5 December 2018 |publisher=Oricon |language=ja }}</ref> Producer [[Johnny Kitagawa]] chose their name in hopes that each member would "spark off each other" and "improve themselves through friendly rivalry".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://mantan-web.jp/article/20150605dog00m200022000c.html |script-title=ja:ジャニーズJr．：新ユニット「Mr．King　VS　Mr．Prince」誕生　名前は「2時間前にジャニーさんが決めた」 |date=5 June 2015 |access-date=5 December 2018 |publisher=Mantan Web |language=ja }}</ref> The group's first original song, "Summer Station" was used as the official theme song for the ''Summer Station'' event, which ran from July to August 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://thetv.jp/news/detail/61152/ |script-title=ja:キラキラ輝くキンプリが"球宴"で特別なステージを！ |date=4 July 2015 |accessdate=12 April 2021 |publisher=The Television |language=ja}}</ref> The group was meant to disband after the event. However, in August 2015, the members announced that they would continue activities as a group.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://top.tsite.jp/entertainment/johnnys/i/25085457/ |script-title=ja:活動継続決定のキンプリが『Mステ』出演！ アクロバットに生歌でファンを魅了 |date=22 August 2015 |access-date=5 December 2018 |publisher=T-Site News |language=ja |archive-url=https://archive.today/20151224124340/http://top.tsite.jp/entertainment/johnnys/i/25085457/ |archive-date=24 December 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

By 2016, Mr. Prince had changed their name to "Prince" and the six members predominately worked within their respective units.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/719750/profile/ |script-title=ja:King & Princeのプロフィール |accessdate=12 April 2021 |publisher=[[Oricon]] }}</ref> Mr. King released a photo book and were special supporters for ''Summer Station 2016'',<ref>{{cite web |url=https://mdpr.jp/news/detail/1610704 |script-title=ja:ジャニーズJr."Mr.KING"の勢いが止まらない！「ベストジーニスト」有力候補メンバーも？「狙っていきたい」 |date=31 August 2016 |access-date=5 December 2018 |publisher=Model Press |language=ja }}</ref> while Prince performed in the ''Johnny's Ginza'' musical and started to host their own [[Japanese variety show|variety show]], ''Mayonaka no Prince''. In September 2017, the six members performed in the Johnny's Jr. concert ''Summer Sta (Kimi ga) King's Treasure''. After the concert, they asked Johnny Kitagawa to let them debut together.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nikkansports.com/entertainment/news/201801180000012.html |script-title=ja:ジャニーズ久々新グループ「キンプリ」ＣＤデビュー |date=18 January 2018 |access-date=5 December 2018 |publisher=Nikkan Sports |language=ja }}</ref>

===2018–present: Debut and success===
On 17 January 2018, their debut was announced in a press conference, along with the establishment of Johnny & Associates' new record label under Universal Music, Johnny's Universe, with King & Prince becoming the first artists to sign under the label. Their debut single "Cinderella Girl" was released on 23 May 2018. It was used as the theme song for the drama ''[[Boys Over Flowers Season 2]]'' which stars member [[Sho Hirano]].<ref>{{cite web|title=デビュー曲「シンデレラガール」がTBS系 火曜ドラマ「花のち晴れ～花男 Next Season～」主題歌に決定！|url=https://www.universal-music.co.jp/king-and-prince/news/2018-03-21/|website=King & Prince|access-date=21 March 2018|language=ja}}</ref> "Cinderella Girl" was a commercial success; it sold over 577,000 copies in its first week, making it the first debut single in over 12 years to exceed initial sales of 500,000 copies. This also made it the most commercially successful debut of the decade.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2112370/full/ |title=【オリコン】King & Princeデビュー曲で栄冠 2010年代最高の初週57.7万枚 |date=29 May 2018 |access-date=5 December 2018 |publisher=Oricon |language=ja }}</ref> By late June King & Prince had become the highest earning new artist, with a total gross of ¥870,000,000.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2114576/full/ |script-title=ja:【オリコン】キンプリ、上半期新人王に 平野紫耀「本当にうれしくてびっくり」 |date=29 June 2018 |access-date=9 December 2018 |publisher=Oricon |language=ja }}</ref> In September 2018, "Cinderella Girl" was certified 3x Platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of Japan]] (RIAJ) for shipments of 750,000 units.<ref name="tr706">{{cite web|url=https://www.riaj.or.jp/riaj/open/open-record!file?fid=1655|title=The Record Vol. 706|date=September 2018|publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of Japan]]|page=14|language=ja|format=PDF|access-date=5 December 2018}}</ref>

Following the success of "Cinderella Girl", King & Prince embarked on a nationwide arena tour titled ''King & Prince First Concert Tour 2018''. The tour began in August at the [[Yokohama Arena]] with a sold-out show to 15,000 guests. When the tour concluded, the audience totaled about 260,000 fans.<ref>{{cite web |title=キンプリ、10・10に2ndシングル発売 初ツアー初日に1万5000人熱狂|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2117321/full/|publisher=[[Oricon]] |date=11 August 2018 |access-date=11 August 2018 |language=ja }}</ref> On 10 October 2018, King & Prince released their second single titled "Memorial". The single was used as the theme song for the television drama ''Bukatsu, Suki Janakya Dame Desu ka?'' starring members Kaito Takahashi, Yuta Jinguji, and Genki Iwahashi.<ref>{{cite web |title=Bukatsu, Suki Janakya Dame Desu ka? Manga Gets Live-Action TV Show |url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2018-08-31/bukatsu-suki-janakya-dame-desu-ka-manga-gets-live-action-tv-show/.136174 |website=[[Anime News Network]] |access-date=5 October 2018 |last=Sherman |first=Jennifer }}</ref> Another song included in the single, "High On Love", was used as the theme song for the film ''You, I Love'' which stars member Hirano.<ref>{{cite web |title=Uirabu. -Uiuishii Koi no Ohanashi- Live-Action Film's Trailer Streamed |url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2018-07-20/uirabu-uiuishii-koi-no-ohanashi-live-action-film-trailer-streamed/.134500 |website=[[Anime News Network]] |access-date=7 November 2018 |last=Hodgkins |first=Crystalyn }}</ref> "Memorial" sold more than 400,000 copies in its first week, and topped the [[Oricon Singles Chart]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2121558/full/ |script-title=ja:King & Prince、シングル2作連続初週売上40万超え KAT-TUN以来12年3ヶ月ぶり史上2組目の快挙 |date=16 October 2018 |access-date=9 December 2018 |publisher=Oricon |language=ja }}</ref> In the same month of release, it was certified 2x Platinum by the RIAJ for shipments of 500,000 units.<ref name="memorialriaj">{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2123281/full/ |title=King&Prince、Mr.Children、米津玄師がダブル・プラチナ認定 レコ協10月度ゴールドディスク認定 |date=12 November 2018 |access-date=9 December 2018 |publisher=Oricon |language=ja }}</ref>

In November 2018, Genki Iwahashi went on hiatus indefinitely to concentrate on treatment for his panic disorder.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2122295/full/ |script-title=ja:キンプリ・岩橋玄樹、11月初旬から活動休止へ パニック障害の治療専念「必ず戻ってきます」 |date=26 October 2018 |access-date=9 December 2018 |publisher=Oricon |language=ja }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.asahi.com/ajw/articles/AJ201812030040.html |title=Two young idols take sick leave, reveal the cause is panic disorder |date=3 December 2018 |access-date=9 December 2018 |publisher=[[Asahi Shimbun]] }}</ref> The group's management announced in February 2019 that he would rejoin the group and participate on their third single, "Kimi wo Matteru", but in March it was noted that he was to continue receiving treatment.<ref>{{cite web |title=King & Prince's Iwahashi Genki will not be resuming his activities yet, single release postponed |url=https://www.tokyohive.com/article/2019/03/king-princes-iwahashi-genki-will-not-be-resuming-his-activities-yet-single-release-postponed |website=tokyohive |access-date=10 March 2019}}</ref>
On March 31 2021, Genki Iwahashi officially left King & Prince and retired from the entertainment industry.
