'''Joshua Radin''' (born June 14, 1974) is an American singer-songwriter. He has recorded seven studio albums, and his songs have been used in a number of films and TV series. His most successful album, ''[[Simple Times]]'', was released in 2008.

==Beginnings==
Joshua Radin was born and raised in [[Shaker Heights, Ohio]], United States, to a Jewish family of [[Poles|Polish]], German, Austrian, and Russian background.<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/8569022.stm Talking Shop: Joshua Radin]. BBC News (March 29, 2010). Retrieved on January 5, 2019.</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Paul |last=Enright |url=http://waycoolmusic.net/musician/josh_radin/josh_radin.html |title=Musician Interviews – NAME |publisher=Way Cool Music |access-date=September 17, 2012}}</ref> He studied drawing and painting at [[Northwestern University]], following his college years with stints as an art teacher, screenwriter, and art gallery employee.

Radin turned to music when he moved to New York City, his father bought him a guitar, and he taught himself to play and write music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hillel.org/about/news/2008/sep/joshua_radin_17september2008.htm |title=Joshua Radin: New Album Hits #1 |publisher=Hillel.org |date=September 17, 2008 |access-date=September 17, 2012}}</ref>

In 2004, American actor [[Zach Braff]], a friend of Radin since their days at Northwestern, introduced Radin's first composition, "Winter", to ''[[Scrubs (TV series)|Scrubs]]'' show creator Bill Lawrence, who ultimately used several of Radin's songs in various scenes of the television series.<ref name=Joshua_Radin:_A_Musician_For_the_Fans>[http://www.socal.com/6067/204/joshua+radin++a+musician+for+the+fans.html Joshua Radin: A Musician For the Fans]. Socal.com. Retrieved on January 5, 2019.</ref><ref name=Josh_Radin_Biography>[https://web.archive.org/web/20120307233135/http://youmix.co.uk/bio/13888 Josh Radin Biography]. Youmix.co.uk</ref><ref>[https://www.iheart.com/podcast/1119-fake-doctors-real-friends-60367049/episode/314-my-screw-up-with-joshua-radin-75279356?cmp=ios_share&sc=ios_social_share&pr=false Fake Doctors, Real Friends: A Scrubs Rewatch Show with Zach & Donald]. iHeart.com. Retrieved on December 17, 2020.</ref>

==Musical career==
[[File:Joshua Radin.jpg|thumb|left|Radin in 2006]]
According to Radin his musical career started in 2004, and he had learned to play the guitar only two years before that. His first song, "[[We Were Here (Joshua Radin album)|Winter]]", appeared on his debut album ''[[We Were Here (Joshua Radin album)|We Were Here]]''.<ref name=sheknows/> Since that time, Radin has been touring the United States, as well as much of the United Kingdom and Europe. Over the course of his career, Radin has shared stages with artists such as [[Sheryl Crow]], [[Tori Amos]], [[Imogen Heap]], [[Meiko (American singer)|Meiko]], [[Missy Higgins]], [[Maria Taylor (musician)|Maria Taylor]], [[Gary Jules]], [[Amber Rubarth]], [[Schuyler Fisk]], [[The Script]] and many more. In addition to his American following, Radin has enjoyed success with his 2008 release, ''[[Simple Times]]'' in the United Kingdom. His song "[[I'd Rather Be With You]]" reached No. 11 on the UK Singles Chart and number two on the US Top Digital Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/_/joshua%20radin/ |title=Joshua Radin &#124; Artist |publisher=Official Charts |access-date=September 17, 2012}}</ref><ref>(September 27, 2008), "TOP DIGITAL". ''Billboard''. '''120''' (39):52</ref>

=== ''We Were Here'' (2006) ===
{{quote box|width=250px|quote=It's just the greatest music. He's a really talented guy. He's like the new [[Paul Simon]].|source= —Zach Braff, 2006<ref>Arnold, Chuck (December 25, 2006), "TOP 10 MUSIC".'' People''. '''66''' (26):45</ref>}}

Radin has said that much of the album was inspired by a bad break-up.<ref name=gitlin>Gitlin, Lauren (August 10, 2006), "Joshua Radin: We Were Here".'' Rolling Stone''. (1006):102</ref> It was released exclusively to iTunes for a month, before attracting attention from major labels.<ref name=newman>Newman, Melinda (April 1, 2006), "YOUNG READY TO ROCK".''Billboard''. '''118''' (13):38</ref> Released on Columbia Records in May 2006,<ref name=newman/> ''[[We Were Here (Joshua Radin album)|We Were Here]]'' presented the first batch of songs Radin ever wrote. The album drew critical acclaim and received a four-star review from ''Rolling Stone'', who praised it for Radin's "whispery, silver-bell voice" and its themes, calling them "poignant and refreshingly frank." The record hit number one on the iTunes Folk Album Chart and included a number of well known musicians, including [[Ryan Adams]] who played guitar on the song "Lovely Tonight".<ref>Esposito, Rose; Martin, Jeffrey Stuart; Lampert, David; Billings, Andrew C.; Budiansky, Sandra; Kazan, Tina; Albrand, Kurt; Lazev, Scott (March 28, 2008), "Feedback".'' Entertainment Weekly''. (984):4</ref> [[Zach Braff]] hand-picked one of the tracks to be featured on the soundtrack for ''[[The Last Kiss (2006 film)|The Last Kiss]]''.<ref name=gitlin/><ref>Greenblatt, Leah (August 11, 2006), "Track Stars".'' Entertainment Weekly''. (890):66</ref>

Radin played the song "Today" from the album, along with five other songs, for [[Ellen DeGeneres]] and [[Portia de Rossi]] at their August 16, 2008, wedding.<ref name=sheknows>{{cite web|first=Christina |last=Chomut |url=http://www.sheknows.com/entertainment/articles/807971/joshua-radin-finding-simple-times |title=Joshua Radin finding Simple Times |publisher=Sheknows.com |date=March 4, 2009 |access-date=September 17, 2012}}</ref><ref>Jordan, Julie; Tauber, Michelle (September 1, 2008), "'I'm the LUCKIEST Girl in the WORLD'". ''People''. '''70''' (9)"50–57</ref><ref>Arnold, Chuck (November 24, 2008), "QUICK CUTS".'' People''. '''70''' (21):47</ref>

=== ''Simple Times'' (2008) ===
Radin's second album, ''Simple Times'' went straight to No. 1 on the iTunes overall chart upon its release. Radin enlisted highly regarded producer [[Rob Schnapf]] (Elliott Smith, Beck). The two spent seven weeks recording at L.A.'s legendary Sunset Sound Studio working with a cast of musicians that included guitarist [[Greg Leisz]], bassist Johnny Flaugher, keyboardist Jason Borger, drummer [[Victor Indrizzo]] and percussionist Lenny Castro. Among the album's many highlights are "You Got Growin' Up To Do", featuring guest vocalist [[Patty Griffin]], lead single "[[I'd Rather Be with You]]" and "Brand New Day".<ref>Caulfield, Keith (April 4, 2009), "'Twilight' Bites Back; Decemberists' Digital Debut". ''Billboard''. '''121''' (13):37</ref> Tracks from the album were featured on TV shows such as ''[[Bones (TV series)|Bones]]'', ''[[Scrubs (TV series)|Scrubs]]'', ''[[House (TV series)|House]]'', ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]'', ''[[One Tree Hill (TV series)|One Tree Hill]]'', ''[[Life Unexpected]]'', ''[[90210 (TV series)|90210]]'', ''[[Brothers & Sisters (2006 TV series)|Brothers and Sisters]]'', ''[[American Idol]]'' and Australian hit series ''[[Packed to the Rafters]]'' among others.<ref name=sheknows/> Radin's "[[Simple Times]]" was released on [[Mom + Pop Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=574202 |title=Joshua Radin – Simple Times – Album Review |publisher=AbsolutePunk.net |date=September 9, 2008 |access-date=September 17, 2012}}</ref>

=== ''The Rock and The Tide'' (2010) ===
Radin's third studio album, ''[[The Rock and The Tide]]'' was released on October 12, 2010 on [[Mom + Pop Music]], his second release with the label. The album reached number five on the iTunes albums chart upon its release. ''The Rock and The Tide'' was produced by [[Martin Terefe]] (Cat Stevens, Ron Sexmith).<ref name=indierock/><ref>Arnold, Chuck (November 1, 2010), "QUICK CUTS".'' People''. '''74''' (16):45</ref> A later EP released in 2011 used 6 of the album's tacks recorded in an acoustic session.

=== ''Underwater'' (2012) ===
On September 4, 2012, Radin released his fourth studio album, ''Underwater''. The album was Radin's third release with [[Mom + Pop Music]].

Radin wrote songs with [[Janet Devlin]] in the Autumn of 2012 for her debut album.<ref>[http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/interviews/a432000/janet-devlin-interview-i-felt-lost-after-the-x-factor.html Janet Devlin: 'I felt lost after X Factor']. Digitalspy.co.uk (October 19, 2012). Retrieved on January 5, 2019.</ref>

=== ''Wax Wings'' (2013) ===
Joshua Radin's fifth studio album ''Wax Wings'' was released May 7, 2013. ''Wax Wings'', a self-released album, has eleven tracks. Included on the album is the song "Lovely Tonight", which had been previously released on iTunes as a single.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/news.php?f=165&pp=30&picon=1181&page=1 |title=Archived copy |access-date=March 23, 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130413162003/http://www.absolutepunk.net/news.php?f=165&pp=30&picon=1181&page=1 |archive-date=April 13, 2013 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> The tracks "In Her Eyes" and "Stay" were produced by [[Matt Noveskey]].

=== ''Onward and Sideways'' (2015) ===
Joshua Radin's released a sixth studio album on January 6, 2015. The album consisted of 13 tracks, one of which was a re-release of the ''Wax Wings'' track "Beautiful Day" recorded with [[Sheryl Crow]]. {{citation needed|date=May 2017}}

=== ''The Fall'' (2017) ===
[[File:Måns Zelmerlöw & Joshua Radin.jpg|thumb|upright=1.2|Radin (left) with [[Måns Zelmerlöw]] in 2016]]
Radin released a self-produced album titled ''The Fall'' on January 27, 2017 through Cadence Music.

=== ''Here, Right Now'' (2019) ===
Radin's eighth studio album, ''Here, Right Now'', was released on October 4, 2019 through [[Nettwerk|Nettwerk Music Group]]. It was his first album with Nettwerk. ''Here, Right Now'' features a cover of [[Tom Petty]]'s [[I Won't Back Down]].

==Personal life==
Joshua Radin became a favorite singer of the American TV host [[Ellen DeGeneres]] and he has appeared on several of her shows. DeGeneres invited Radin to sing his composition "Today", a favorite song of hers, at her wedding with [[Portia de Rossi]].

Radin also wrote the song "In Her Eyes" in celebration of his own sister's wedding.{{citation needed|date=May 2017}}

==Philanthropy==
Radin has been involved with Little Kids Rock, a national nonprofit that provides music education to schools without music programs. Radin visited schools in six cities during his 2011 tour in support of ''The Rock and the Tide'' to give guitar lessons on the song "Brand New Day", and brought some of the students on stage at his concerts to perform the song with him.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.littlekidsrock.org/news-events/press-releases/joshua-radin-partners-little-kids-rock-support-music-education/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=June 8, 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150908083557/http://www.littlekidsrock.org/news-events/press-releases/joshua-radin-partners-little-kids-rock-support-music-education/ |archive-date=September 8, 2015 }} Retrieved January 13, 2014</ref> He did so again in 2015 for his song "Beautiful Day".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.littlekidsrock.org/friends/our-big-fans/joshua-radin/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=January 13, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140114092456/http://www.littlekidsrock.org/friends/our-big-fans/joshua-radin/ |archive-date=January 14, 2014 }} Retrieved January 13, 2014</ref>

As of September 2010, he had amassed more than 75 film and television placements of his songs.<ref name=indierock>{{cite web|url=http://www.indierockreviews.com/news/2010/09/joshua-radins-the-rock-tide-october-12-tour-dates/ |title=JOSHUA RADIN'S 'THE ROCK AND THE TIDE' OUT OCTOBER 12 and Tour Dates &#124; Indie Rock News |publisher=Indierockreviews.com |access-date=September 17, 2012}}</ref><ref name=artistopia>[http://www.artistopia.com/josh-radin Joshua Radin at Artistopia] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707165740/http://www.artistopia.com/josh-radin |date=July 7, 2011 }}</ref>
