'''Vian Izak''' is a South African American singer-songwriter, record producer, and [[audio engineer]]. He created an animated alias to maintain his anonymity online.<ref name=Tonspion>{{cite web
|url= http://www.tonspion.de/news/hype-machine-heisser-scheiss-aus-dem-netz-kw-372016

|date= September 12, 2016
|publisher= Tonspion
|accessdate= February 14, 2017
}}</ref>  Izak's artist project is part of the [[virtual band]] movement popularized by acts such as the [[Gorillaz]] and [[The Archies]] in where the members of the group are portrayed as animations.<ref name=Newsarama>{{cite web
|url= https://www.newsarama.com/35714-vian-izak-mix-comics-indie-pop-with-starlit-summer-s-eve.html

==2015–2019: Early career and music==
Vian is a South African immigrant who moved to the United States in 2000. His love of songwriting started out of a need to communicate as English was not his native language.<ref name=HappyMag>{{cite web
|url= https://happymag.tv/premiere-vian-izak-breaks-ground-with-new-audiovisual-project/
|title= PREMIERE: A Life’s Work In Equilibrium: Vian Izak breaks ground with new audiovisual project
|author= James Christie-Murray
|date= March 15, 2018
|publisher= Happy Magazine
|accessdate= October 13, 2020
}}</ref> He cites [[U2]], [[Coldplay]], and [[Death Cab for Cutie]] as early influences.<ref name=RabbitRoom>{{cite web
|url= https://rabbitroom.com/2019/12/navigating-music-and-comics-with-vian-izak/
|title= Navigating Music and Comics with Vian Izak
|author= Jonny Jimison
|date= December 11, 2019
|publisher= The Rabbit Room
|accessdate= October 13, 2020
}}</ref> As a young man he moved to [[Nashville]] and opened a recording studio on the historic [[Music Row]].<ref name=RabbitRoom /> Izak brought an international sound to the Nashville music scene which made him stand out.<ref name=RabbitRoom /><ref name="HappyMag" /> This is exemplified in his collaborations with Grammy winning South African group [[Ladysmith Black Mambazo]] on "Brink of Love" and with Italian composer [[Davide Rossi]] on "Sangre de Cristo".<ref name=HappyMag /><ref name=HypeMag>{{cite web
|url= https://www.thehypemagazine.com/2020/08/vian-izak-sangre-de-cristo/
|title= Vian Izak – Sangre de Cristo
|author= Jerry Doby
|date= August 5, 2020
|publisher= The Hype Magazine
|accessdate= October 13, 2020
}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Vian Izak Weaves A Tapestry Of Sound And Nostalgia On Sangre de Cristo – Neon Music|url=https://neonmusic.co.uk/vian-izak-weaves-a-tapestry-of-sound-and-nostalgia-on-sangre-de-cristo|access-date=2020-10-13|language=en-US}}</ref>

He combines his music with the illustrations of Hein Zaayman to create multimedia comic stories. His first multimedia release, Northern Anthems (2018), was nominated for numerous awards including two Independent Music Awards.<ref name=IMA2>{{cite web
|url= http://independentmusicawards.com/17th-independent-music-awards-nominees/

==2020: Music and COVID aid==
In March 2020 Vian worked with [[U.S. Bancorp]] to release a [[COVID-19]] television advertisement using his song "Things Will Get Better" encouraging people to stay hopeful in hard times.<ref name=USBank>{{cite AV media |people=USBank |date=Apr 13, 2020 |title= Spirit Strong |medium= Youtube |language= English |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=topgjNDfB2o&feature=youtu.be |access-date= October 13, 2020 |archive-url= https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=topgjNDfB2o&feature=youtu.be |archive-date=Apr 13, 2020 |format=Youtube |publisher=US Bankcorp}}</ref> The advertisement was played nationally in the United States. Additionally, Izak released a music video for his song "The Navigator" thanking healthcare workers for their bravery during the pandemic which received over fifty thousand views on [[Facebook]] and [[YouTube]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Izak|first=Vian|date=May 1, 2020|title=The Navigator|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CPeSuz4hhQE|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=October 13, 2020|website=Youtube}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Vian Izak - The Navigator (Official Music Video)|url=https://www.facebook.com/vianizak/videos/vian-izak-the-navigator-official-music-video/226029388839952/|language=en|access-date=2020-10-13}}</ref>
