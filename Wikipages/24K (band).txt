'''24K''' ({{lang-ko|투포케이}}) is a South Korean male group formed by Choeun Entertainment. It currently consists of five members: Changsun, Kiyong, Imchan, Xiwoo and Youngwoong. The group's debut took place on September 6, 2012 with their debut mini album ''Hurry Up''.<ref name="Naver">{{cite web|url=http://people.search.naver.com/search.naver?where=nexearch&sm=tab_ppn&query=%ED%88%AC%ED%8F%AC%EC%BC%80%EC%9D%B4&os=483654&ie=utf8&key=PeopleService|title=투포케이 프로필|publisher=Naver|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://sports.donga.com/3/02/20120906/49161940/1|title='무서운 신예' 투포케이, 7일 방송 데뷔로 본격 활동|publisher=Sports Donga|language=Korean}}</ref>

==History==
===2012–2013: 4K, ''Hurry Up'', and ''U R So Cute''===
Prior to the debut of the group, the [[acoustic music|acoustic]] [[pop music|pop]] sub-unit 4K debuted.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Go|first1=Gyeong-min|script-title=ko:'어쿠스틱 팝' 포케이, 강렬한 댄스 예고 '반전티저' 공개|url=http://www.mydaily.co.kr/new_yk/html/read.php?newsid=201208171827411134|website=MyDaily|accessdate=May 7, 2016|language=Korean|date=August 17, 2012}}</ref> Consisting of vocalists Cory, Seokjune, Kisu, and Sungoh, they released ''Rocking Girl'' in June 2012.<ref name=Newsen>{{cite web|last1=Heo|first1=Seol-hui|script-title=ko:투포케이 첫미니앨범 판매순위 1위 '강력 신인 나타났다'|url=http://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201209131106542210|website=Newsen|accessdate=May 8, 2016|language=Korean|date=September 13, 2012}}</ref> With the addition of Daeil and Byungho, 24K released their first mini album ''Hurry Up'' and the title song on September 6.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Park|first1=A-reum|script-title=ko:하반기 가요계 다크호스 투포케이(24K) 마침내 세상에 나온다|url=http://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201209061020452510|website=Newsen|accessdate=May 8, 2016|language=Korean|date=September 6, 2012}}</ref> On January 16 of the following year, 24K began an additional promotion cycle for the record by performing "Secret Love."<ref>{{cite web|script-title=ko:투포케이, 후속곡 인기와 더불어 '하트춤' 히트 예감|url=http://www.segye.com/content/html/2013/01/23/20130123021353.html|website=Segye Ilbo|publisher=[[News World Communications]]|accessdate=May 9, 2016|language=Korean|date=January 23, 2013}}</ref>

24K released their second mini album ''U R So Cute'' on August 1, 2013.<ref name=MD>{{cite web|last1=Nam|first1=An-woo|script-title=ko:'순금돌' 투포케이, 1년만에 컴백…'귀여워 죽겠어'|url=http://www.mydaily.co.kr/new_yk/html/read.php?newsid=201307311548131133|website=MyDaily|accessdate=May 9, 2016|language=Korean|date=July 31, 2013}}</ref> A "cute" concept, the title track features a "[[hard rock]] guitar sound" mixed with a "European-style [[house music|house]] beat."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Choi|first1=Ji-ye|script-title=ko:'순금돌' 투포케이, 1년만에 '귀여워 죽겠어'로 컴백|url=http://www.mydaily.co.kr/new_yk/html/read.php?newsid=201308011614141136|website=MyDaily|accessdate=May 9, 2016|language=Korean|date=August 1, 2013}}</ref>

===2014–2015: Lineup changes, "Hey You" and "Super Fly"===
Member Dae-il participated in Mnet's TV show, Dancing 9 Season 2.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mnet Dancing 9 S2 Daeil Cut|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2BPHu-SJSBs}}</ref>

24K returned after a 2-year hiatus with the digital single, "Hey You," along with two new members, Hui and Jinhong.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=382&aid=0000360414|title=투포케이, '마스크맨' 두 멤버 전격 공개|language=Korean|publisher=Naver}}</ref>

On October 1, 2015, 24K made a comeback with their third mini album ''Super Fly'' and the title track of the same name.<ref>{{cite web|title=24K Super Fly MV|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pZyr7OLFvVA}}</ref>

===2016–2017: Further lineup changes, "Still 24K", ''The Real One'' and ''Addiction''===
[[File:24K in Budapest, Hungary, 2017.jpg|thumb|220px|24K in Budapest, Hungary in August 2017]]
24K had their first European concert in Warsaw, Poland on January 23, 2016 at Progresja Music Zone Main Stage, without Sung-oh. They held a showcase in Malaysia on April 22–24 which was presented by New Pro Star, with Sungoh and Daeil being absent for unknown reasons. On August 1, Choeun Entertainment made an official statement saying that members Daeil and Sungoh would be on hiatus; Sungoh was receiving medical treatment for his dislocated shoulder and Daeil had chosen to take a personal leave, although it was later discovered that Daeil had left the group to debut as a solo artist under the name "Big One".<ref>{{cite web|title=Choeun Entertainment's statement about Daeil|url=https://pbs.twimg.com/media/C-_BTd1XkAEClAk.jpg:large}}</ref> The statement also revealed that two new members, Changsun and Hongseob, would be added to continue as a seven-member group. 24K released the digital single "Still 24K" on August 11.

On October 21, 24K returned with their first full-length album titled ''The Real One'' containing previously released singles "Secret Love," "Hey You," "Super Fly," "Still 24K" and the title track "Bingo."

24K kicked off their first World tour on December 7, 2016 in 4 Brazilian cities and continued with European tour on January 3, 2017 in Milan, Italy and continued to tour to Helsinki FI, London UK, Warsaw PL, Lisbon PT, Cologne DE, Bucharest RO and finished on January 15, 2017 in Paris, France. Furthermore, in late March 2017 it was announced that 24K would have an encore tour to visit 4 more highly requested countries. The encore tour started on April 2, 2017 in Moscow, Russia and toured to Stockholm Sweden, Madrid Spain and finished on April 9, 2017 in Amsterdam, Netherlands.

24K released their comeback single and MV "Only You" from their single album ''Addiction'' on May 26, 2017.<ref name="bonnienclyde"/>

On November 2, Choeun Entertainment made a statement confirming that as of July 6, 2017, Hui had officially left the group, it was also confirmed that Daeil was no longer a member, and that Sungoh's future with the company is to be discussed after he completes his [[conscription in South Korea|mandatory military service]].{{citation needed|date=April 2018}} The remaining six members participated in the [[JTBC]]'s Survival show ''Mix Nine''.<ref>http://cafe.daum.net/official24K/5Pgo/314</ref>{{better source|date=April 2018}}

===2018–present: Blacklist, Kisu's enlistment and new members ===
On May 9, an interview with [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] revealed that the group’s upcoming EP was set to be released on May 25, with the title track titled Bonnie N Clyde.<ref name="bonnienclyde"/>

Also on May 9, it was revealed that 24K was the only idol group that was put on the blacklist by former president Park Geun-hye. The impeached president put together a blacklist of celebrities to exclude them from receiving any support from the state. The blacklist's existence was made known in October 2016 before the president's impeachment, the full list being uncovered in April. A representative of the blacklist declared that the blacklist consisted of those who expressed any sort of support against governmental commands about the Sewol accident and artists who opposed presidential candidates Park Won-soon and Moon Jae-in.<ref name="bonnienclyde">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8455839/24k-interview-political-blacklist|title=K-Pop Boy Band 24K Talk Unwittingly Being Blacklisted For Political Purposes|language=ko|work=Billboard|date=May 14, 2018|accessdate=May 14, 2018}}</ref><ref name=blacklist>{{cite web| url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=144&aid=0000551641| title='블랙리스트' 아이돌 투포케이, 5월말 새앨범 들고 귀환| language=Korean| publisher=Naver}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8455535/k-pop-boy-band-24k-blacklisted-presidents|title=K-Pop Boy Band 24K Were Blacklisted by Former South Korean Presidents: Report|work=Billboard|date=May 11, 2018|accessdate=May 14, 2018}}</ref> The blacklist includes their former manager and members Seokjune, Byungho, Cory, Kisu, and Daeil.<ref name=blacklist/>

On May 11, the company announced that member Kisu would be enlisting in the military to carry out his military service while the group makes another change to the lineup and add a new member named Kiyong. On May 25, 24K released their fourth EP ''Bonnie N Clyde''.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.soompi.com/2018/05/25/watch-24k-makes-anticipated-comeback-new-mv-bonnie-n-clyde/| title= Release of Bonnie N Clyde}}</ref>

On January 25, 2019, the company announced that member and leader Cory would be leaving 24K since his exclusive contract with the company has expired and will pursue his career as a producer with his new stage name "Corbyn". Cory has also released his solo song and music video "Million Dollar Dream", aka. "M$D" on February 27, 2019, on his official 28Laboratory youtube channel.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.soompi.com/article/1298191wpp/24k-issues-official-statement-regarding-corys-departure-sungohs-possible-return| title=24K Issues Official Statement Regarding Cory's Departure + Sungoh's Possible Return}}</ref> It was also stated that Sungoh would not continue as a member of the group and instead will work under Choeun Entertainment as music producer for 24K.

On June 26, 2019, the company announced that Jinhong was not renewing his contract, Hongseob would not be able to continue due to health reasons, and Jeonguk has chosen to concentrate on his solo work. Additionally, Kisu announced on his Twitter that he would not return with the group either. The company shared that they will add new members and described it as 24K season 2 coming soon.

On April 2, 2020, the company announced that the new member named Dojun joined the group.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://allaccessasia.com.au/24k-drops-their-new-members-profile-images/| title=24K drops their new member's profile images| publisher=All Access Asia}}</ref> On May 26, 2020, the four remaining members of 24K had announced that the hidden member, #5 and Dojun had left citing personal differences. Member #5's name and face were revealed after he left and became an A+ Entertainment trainee and the leader of their upcoming boy group A+ Boys.

On December 23, 2020, photos of a new member were published, his name is Youngwoong.

On June 12, 2021, 24K released the digital single "Welcome to the Mainstreet".

==Musical style and influences==
Upon debut, 24K employed various genres into its music. On the EP ''Hurry Up'', the record incorporated [[pop music|pop]], [[trance music|trance]], [[dubstep]], and [[industrial music|industrial]] into its sound.<ref name=musicians>{{cite web|last1=Moon|first1=Ji-yeon|script-title=ko:투포케이 "또 아이돌이라고? 뮤지션이란 느낌 주고싶다"(인터뷰)|url=http://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201210181344343010|website=Newsen|accessdate=May 8, 2016|language=Korean|date=October 18, 2012}}</ref> On the second EP ''U R So Cute'', the group incorporated elements of [[hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[electronic dance music]] into its sound.<ref name=MD/>

Cory has expressed his admiration for hip hop producers [[Dr. Dre]] and [[Teddy Park]]. Kisu was influenced by [[Urban Zakapa]], which led to his fascination with [[contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[soul music]].<ref name=musicians/>
