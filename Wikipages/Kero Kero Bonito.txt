'''Kero Kero Bonito''' ('''KKB''') are a British [[indie pop]] band formed in [[South London]] in 2011. The band consists of vocalist Sarah Midori Perry and producers and multi-instrumentalists Gus Lobban and Jamie Bulled.

Their musical style consists of [[indie pop]],<ref>{{cite news |last=Sauers |first=Camille |title=Kero Kero Bonito To Bring Weird British Bubblegum To San Antonio |url=https://www.sacurrent.com/sa-sound/archives/2018/07/19/kero-kero-bonito-to-bring-weird-british-bubblegum-to-san-antonio |newspaper=[[San Antonio Current]] |access-date=8 September 2018 |date=19 July 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180908164423/https://www.sacurrent.com/sa-sound/archives/2018/07/19/kero-kero-bonito-to-bring-weird-british-bubblegum-to-san-antonio |archive-date=8 September 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> [[electropop]],<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Gaca |first=Anna |title=New Music: Kero Kero Bonito – Bonito Generation |url=https://www.spin.com/2016/10/new-music-kero-kero-bonito-bonito-generation/ |magazine=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |access-date=8 September 2018 |date=14 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180828120004/https://www.spin.com/2016/10/new-music-kero-kero-bonito-bonito-generation/ |archive-date=28 August 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> [[dance-rock]],<ref>{{cite magazine |title = KERO KERO BONITO – SURPRISE! IT'S AN ALBUM. |url = http://www.flaunt.com/content/kero-kero-bonito-surprise-its-an-album |magazine = [[Flaunt Magazine|Flaunt]] |access-date = 8 October 2018 |date = 1 October 2018 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20181009013146/http://www.flaunt.com/content/kero-kero-bonito-surprise-its-an-album |archive-date = 9 October 2018 |url-status = live }}</ref> [[hyperpop]],<ref>{{cite book|last=Raymer|first=Miles|title=Hyperpop geniuses Kero Kero Bonito made us a playlist|url=https://ew.com/article/2014/11/25/kero-kero-bonito-playlist/|publisher=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|access-date=22 June 2021|date=25 November 2014|url-status=live }}</ref> [[Bubblegum music|bubblegum pop]],<ref>{{cite news|title=UK Bubble Gum Pop Rockers Kero Kero Bonito Gear Up for November Show in San Antonio|last=Conde|first=Chris|url=https://www.sacurrent.com/sa-sound/archives/2019/05/09/uk-bubble-gum-pop-rockers-kero-kero-bonito-gear-up-for-november-show-in-san-antonio|newspaper=[[San Antonio Current]]|date=9 May 2019|access-date=17 July 2021}}</ref> and [[tweelectro]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Day|first=Laurence|title=Kero Kero Bonito share springy new synthpop number "Trampoline"|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/kero-kero-bonito-share-springy-new-synthpop-number-trampoline|magazine=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|access-date=26 June 2021|date=26 September 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>
The band's earlier work was influenced by [[J-pop]] such as [[Kyary Pamyu Pamyu]], as well as [[dancehall]], and [[video game music]];<ref name="mack-dis">{{cite web |url=http://drownedinsound.com/in_depth/4148090-discover--kero-kero-bonito |title=DiScover: Kero Kero Bonito |last=Mack |first=Tommy |date=28 August 2014 |website=[[Drowned in Sound]] |access-date=1 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150414012613/http://drownedinsound.com/in_depth/4148090-discover--kero-kero-bonito |archive-date=14 April 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref> however, following their 2018 EP ''[[TOTEP]]'' their sound and influences diversified, with their second studio album, ''[[Time 'n' Place]]'', being influenced by [[indie rock]] contemporaries such as [[Mount Eerie]] and [[My Bloody Valentine (band)|My Bloody Valentine]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/10/10/kero-kero-bonito-interview-time-n-place |title=Kero Kero Bonito are smiling through it all |work=[[The FADER]] |access-date=12 October 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200601080834/https://www.thefader.com/2018/10/10/kero-kero-bonito-interview-time-n-place |archive-date=1 June 2020 |url-status=live }},</ref><ref>{{cite web|url = https://i-d.vice.com/en_us/article/598v3x/kero-kero-bonito-time-n-place-interview/|title = kero kero bonito finds solace in chaotic sounds|work = [[i-D]]|date = 23 October 2018|access-date = 15 November 2018|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20181128075533/https://i-d.vice.com/en_us/article/598v3x/kero-kero-bonito-time-n-place-interview|archive-date = 28 November 2018|url-status = live}}</ref> Perry, who is half-Japanese, sings and raps in both Japanese and English.

==History==
[[File:Kero Kero Bonito (12113716266).jpg|thumb|left|Jamie Bulled (left) and Gus Lobban (right)]]
[[File:Sarah Bonito Live.jpg|150px|thumb|Sarah Midori Perry performing the song "Break" from the album ''[[Bonito Generation]]'', live in Dublin (2018)]]
Lobban and Bulled grew up in [[Bromley]] in the suburbs south of London and met in their teens.<ref name="song-impose">{{cite web |url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/features/going-global-with-kero-kero-bonito |title=Going Global with Kero Kero Bonito |last=Song |first=Sandra |date=16 September 2014 |website=Impose |access-date=1 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150510105558/http://www.imposemagazine.com/features/going-global-with-kero-kero-bonito |archive-date=10 May 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref> In search for a new band member, they posted advertisements, including on MixB, an online bulletin board for Japanese expatriates, largely due to their interest in "[[Japanese rap]]".<ref name="song-impose"/><ref name="bulut-dummy">{{cite web |url=http://www.dummymag.com/features/next-kero-kero-bonito-interview |title=Next: Kero Kero Bonito |last=Bulut |first=Selim |date=29 August 2014 |website=Dummy |archive-date=1 September 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140901040817/https://www.dummymag.com/features/next-kero-kero-bonito-interview}}</ref> Perry was one of the first respondents. They selected her based on her background in art, and their interest in her bilingual ability.<ref name="bulut-dummy"/> Although she had collaborated with a Japanese girl group previously,<ref name="bulut-dummy"/> Perry did not have a background in professional singing; instead, she was interested in collaborating with Lobban and Bulled simply because she "wanted to try it".<ref>{{cite news |last=Aroseti |first=Rachel |title=Kero Kero Bonito: 'It's just a bassline, vocals, super clear message and that's it' |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/apr/07/kero-kero-bonito-interview |access-date=9 October 2018 |work=[[The Guardian]] |date=7 April 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181009211215/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/apr/07/kero-kero-bonito-interview |archive-date=9 October 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref>

The trio called themselves ''Kero Kero Bonito''; its meaning is intentionally ambiguous, with one derivation from the Japanese onomatopoeic words for frog croaks and a [[bonito|type of fish]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/nov/28/new-band-of-the-week-kero-kero-bonito-no-33 |title=New band of the week: Kero Kero Bonito (No 33) |last=Lester |first=Paul |date=28 November 2014 |website=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=1 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402135224/http://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/nov/28/new-band-of-the-week-kero-kero-bonito-no-33 |archive-date=2 April 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref> Other meanings include the Brazilian [[Southern lapwing|''quero-quero'' bird]] while "bonito" means "pretty" in both Portuguese and Spanish, thus "Pretty Quero-quero" – or even "I want, I want pretty" in a rough translation.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.vice.com/en_id/article/gvp8b4/kero-kero-bonito |title=Kero Kero Bonito Tell Us Why Positivity Is Punk AF |last=Lawrence |first=Iyas |date=18 November 2016 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |access-date=27 July 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171206135855/https://www.vice.com/en_id/article/gvp8b4/kero-kero-bonito |archive-date=6 December 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref>

The group released their debut mixtape ''Intro Bonito'' through [[Double Denim Records]] in August 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ew.com/article/2014/08/13/get-hooked-on-kero-kero-bonitos-sick-beat |title=Get hooked on Kero Kero Bonito's 'Sick Beat' |last=Raymer |first=Miles |date=13 August 2014 |website=[[Entertainment Weekly]] |access-date=1 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402095131/http://www.ew.com/article/2014/08/13/get-hooked-on-kero-kero-bonitos-sick-beat |archive-date=2 April 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/09/02/kero-kero-bonito-my-party-bo-en-remix|title=Hear London Producer bo en's Cutely Delirious Remix of Kero Kero Bonito|website=[[The FADER]]|first=Patrick D.|last=McDermott|date=2 September 2014|access-date=5 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170206022501/http://www.thefader.com/2014/09/02/kero-kero-bonito-my-party-bo-en-remix|archive-date=6 February 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> They wrote many of their songs featured on the mixtape using a [[Casio]] SA-45 mini-keyboard.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://diymag.com/2014/12/24/dear-santa-love-kero-kero-bonito |title=Dear Santa... Love Kero Kero Bonito |last=Hunt |first=El |date=24 December 2014 |website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |access-date=14 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141230043046/http://diymag.com/2014/12/24/dear-santa-love-kero-kero-bonito |archive-date=30 December 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref> The band contributed "Flamingo" to [[Ryan Hemsworth]]'s compilation EP ''shh#ffb6c1''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://diymag.com/2014/09/25/kero-kero-bonito-share-new-free-download-on-ryan-hemsworth-compilation |title=Kero Kero Bonito share new free download on Ryan Hemsworth compilation |date=25 September 2014 |website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |access-date=1 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402184939/http://diymag.com/2014/09/25/kero-kero-bonito-share-new-free-download-on-ryan-hemsworth-compilation |archive-date=2 April 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref> 

In September 2014, Kero Kero Bonito released ''Bonito Recycling'', a compilation of ''Intro Bonito'' remixes by artists including [[Danny L Harle]] and [[Spazzkid]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/22006/1/stream-kero-kero-bonitos-6-track-remix-recycling-ep |title=Stream Kero Kero Bonito's 6-track remix 'Recycling' EP |last=Cliff |first=Aimee |date=1 October 2014 |website=[[Dazed]] |access-date=1 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150319053812/http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/22006/1/stream-kero-kero-bonitos-6-track-remix-recycling-ep |archive-date=19 March 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref> The band released the single "Build It Up", on which Perry sings in a call and response pattern, in November 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2014/11/listen-to-kero-kero-bonitos-new-track-built-it-up.html |title=Listen to Kero Kero Bonito's New Track 'Built it Up' |last=Carrasco |first=Alexa |date=17 November 2014 |website=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |access-date=1 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402160847/http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2014/11/listen-to-kero-kero-bonitos-new-track-built-it-up.html |archive-date=2 April 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref> 

On 10 August 2015, Kero Kero Bonito announced their first North American tour, which took place during October 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/KeroKeroBonito/status/630747014268092416|title=We announced a US tour and released a new song (s/o @gorillavsbear)|first=Kero Kero|last=Bonito|date=10 August 2015|access-date=11 August 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190216231526/https://twitter.com/KeroKeroBonito/status/630747014268092416|website=Twitter.com|archive-date=16 February 2019|url-status=live}}{{Primary source inline|date=April 2020}}</ref> 

On 21 October 2016, the group released their first album ''[[Bonito Generation]]'' through Double Denim.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2016/10/19/kero-kero-bonito-generation-interview-2016|title=Interview: My Generation: Kero Kero Bonito|website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]|first=Will|last=Richards|date=19 October 2016|access-date=5 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170206103536/http://diymag.com/2016/10/19/kero-kero-bonito-generation-interview-2016|archive-date=6 February 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 2 February 2017, the [[YouTuber]] Berd published a parody animation of the band's single "Flamingo".<ref name="Flam">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JhDIILjlhBQ&ab_channel=Berd|title=Flamingo|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=13 October 2020}}</ref> The video went [[Viral video|viral]], and became an [[internet meme]], surpassing 21 million views as of February 2021. 

On 12 February 2018, the band released the single "Only Acting". It departs from the band's previous electronic sound with a darker, rock-influenced style.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/02/12/kero-kero-bonito-drops-new-single-and-video-only-acting|title=Kero Kero Bonito drops new single and video, "Only Acting"|website=The FADER|access-date=1 August 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190801155324/https://www.thefader.com/2018/02/12/kero-kero-bonito-drops-new-single-and-video-only-acting|archive-date=1 August 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> Later, on 20 February 2018, the band released the EP ''TOTEP''. The EP included "Only Acting", and other songs following the rock-influenced style.

[[File:Sarah Midori Perry and Jennifer Walton performing in Hoboken, NJ.png|alt=Sarah Midori Perry looking at an animatronic bird|thumb|220px|Sarah Midori Perry and Jennifer Walton performing in Hoboken, NJ in 2018]]
On 13 April 2018, the band performed their first 'full band' show. This involved two extra members, James Rowland on electric guitar and Jennifer Walton on drums and sampler, as well as Lobban on drums and keyboards and Bulled on bass guitar. Rowland previously played guitar on ''TOTEP''. This change in live lineup reflected their change in sound following the EP.{{fact|date=February 2021}}

On 8 May 2018, the band released the single, "Time Today", and announced their second studio album, ''[[Time 'n' Place]]'', which was released on 1 October 2018. The album was sonically a significant departure from their previous studio album, and was inspired by radical changes in the band's personal lives following their tour cycle for their debut album.{{fact|date=February 2021}}

On 8 July 2019, the band uploaded "KKB Life" to [[YouTube]], a vlog video recorded during the ''Time `n` Place'' era, announcing that a new era is starting. They announced a North America and Europe tour shortly after and released a new single "[[When the Fires Come]]" in September 2019. Later on in the month, before the tour started, they also released their fourth EP ''[[Civilisation I]]''.<ref>{{Citation|title=KKB Life|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7r30rZvuu3Y|access-date=2 October 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200131211506/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7r30rZvuu3Y|archive-date=31 January 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2020, the band wrote and performed "It's Bugsnax!", the theme song for the video game ''[[Bugsnax]]'', which was featured in its announcement trailer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bugsnax Song Writer Explains How Band Kero Kero Bonito Crafted The PS5 Game's Catchy Theme Song|url=https://www.gamespot.com/articles/bugsnax-song-writer-explains-how-band-kero-kero-bo/1100-6479071/|date=28 June 2020|access-date=20 July 2020|website=GameSpot|language=en-US|archive-date=20 July 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200720215331/https://www.gamespot.com/articles/bugsnax-song-writer-explains-how-band-kero-kero-bo/1100-6479071/|url-status=live}}</ref>

In April 2021, Kero Kero Bonito released their fifth EP, ''[[Civilisation II]]''.{{fact|date=October 2021}}

In September 2021, Kero Kero Bonito released "rom com 2021", a remix of [[Soccer Mommy]]'s single, "rom com 2004."  The song was released under the 23rd installment of [[Adult Swim]]'s Singles Series.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kero Kero Bonito and Soccer Mommy share "rom com 2021"|url=https://www.thefader.com/2021/09/27/kero-kero-bonito-and-soccer-mommy-share-rom-com-2021|date=27 September 2021|access-date=5 October 2021|website=The FADER|language=en-US|archive-date=20 July 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200720215331/https://www.gamespot.com/articles/bugsnax-song-writer-explains-how-band-kero-kero-bo/1100-6479071/|url-status=live}}</ref>

In addition to these two projects, Lobban co-wrote and produced two tracks on [[Saint Etienne (band)|Saint Etienne]]'s 2017 album ''[[Home Counties (album)|Home Counties]]''.

Prior to Kero Kero Bonito, Bulled was a member of the band Kabogaeries. Since forming Kero Kero Bonito, Bulled now records music under the name Wharfwhit,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://thump.vice.com/en_uk/article/ypjw37/wharfwhit-interview |title=WHARFWHIT Makes Awkward, Double-Jointed Bubblegum Electro-Pop and You Need to Listen to Him Now |date=10 November 2016 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Thump (Vice)]] |access-date=1 March 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171022033645/https://thump.vice.com/en_uk/article/ypjw37/wharfwhit-interview |archive-date=22 October 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref> with the 2016 single 'Elbows' and 2017 albums 'Evidently' and 'WHARFWHAT!?'<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wharfwhit.bandcamp.com/releases|title=WHARFWHAT!? by WHARFWHIT|date=14 November 2017|access-date=14 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171114202427/https://wharfwhit.bandcamp.com/releases|archive-date=14 November 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> released under this name.

==Band members==
* Sarah Midori Perry (Sarah Bonito) – lead vocals
* Douglas "Gus" Lobban – drums, keyboards, [[Sampler (musical instrument)|sampler]], backing vocals, production
* Jamie Bulled – keyboards, electric guitar, bass guitar, sampler, production

===Live band members===
The band perform live as both a traditional band, and as a trio using a sequencer and samplers. The full live band consists of these members:

* Sarah Bonito – lead vocals, keyboards
* Gus Lobban – backing vocals, keyboards, drums
* Jamie Bulled – bass guitar
* Jennifer Walton – drums, sampler
* James Rowland – guitar
