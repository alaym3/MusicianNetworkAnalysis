'''Tegan Joshua Anthony Chambers''' (born December 20, 1999), known professionally as '''Sleepy Hallow''', is an American rapper and singer. Originally from Jamaica, Chambers grew up in the [[Flatbush, Brooklyn|Flatbush]] section of [[Brooklyn]] in New York City alongside frequent collaborator [[Sheff G]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sleepy Hallow {{!}} HipHopMyWay|url=https://www.hiphopmyway.com/artists/underground/sleepy-hallow/|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=www.hiphopmyway.com|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=October 31, 2019|title=Sleepy Hallow Redefines the Sound of Brooklyn With His Debut Mixtape Don't Sleep|url=https://audibletreats.com/sleepy-hallow_pr2/|access-date=August 15, 2020|website=Audible Treats}}</ref>

In 2018, he had his first breakthrough viral single with "Flows", which also featured Sheff G.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Reneau|first=Steven|date=August 1, 2019|title=Sheff G & Sleepy Hallow Break Down The Meaning Of "Flows"|url=https://genius.com/videos/Sheff-g-sleepy-hallow-break-down-the-meaning-of-flows|url-status=live|access-date=September 14, 2020|website=Genius}}</ref> In August 2020, his single "[[Deep End Freestyle]]" featuring Fousheé was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref name="riaa.com">{{Cite web|title=SLEEPY HALLOW – DEEP END FREESTYLE (FEAT. FOUSHEÉ)|url=http://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?share=21656&t=t|access-date=August 15, 2020|website=RIAA}}</ref> In August 2021, his single "[[2055 (song)|2055]]" peaked at number 53 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], becoming his highest-charting song. He is currently signed to Winners Circle Entertainment and [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|date=August 12, 2020|title=Winner Circle Entertainment's Sleepy Hallow & Sheff G Announce Partnership With RCA Records: Exclusive|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9433088/sleepy-hallow-sheff-g-rca-records-partnership|access-date=August 15, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

== Early life ==
As a child, Chambers immigrated from Jamaica to the [[Flatbush, Brooklyn|Flatbush]] section of [[Brooklyn]], New York City. Around the age of 12, he started to rap. He later picked the name "Sleepy Hallow" as his rap name as that was his name in the streets. Chambers dropped out of school in the ninth grade.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lee|first=Cydney|date=2021-09-21|title=Sleepy Hallow: September’s R&B/Hip-Hop Rookie of the Month|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/sleepy-hallow-september-rb-hip-hop-rookie-9632769/|access-date=2021-11-30|website=Billboard|language=en-US}}</ref> He first met friend and collaborator Sheff G when the two were preparing to fight, but stopped when Sheff G was impressed with how Chambers carried himself.<ref>{{Citation|title=Sleepy Hallow & Sheff G Exclusive Interview How did they meet & more|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EGcjVLwiXko|language=en|access-date=2021-11-30}}</ref>

== Career ==
He and Sheff G were signed to former [[National Football League|NFL]] player, [[Junior Galette]]'s music record label, Nula Entertainment from 2017 to 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Romaker|first=Ian|date=May 4, 2018|title=Shimmy Shimmy Ya – Sheff G climbs from the New York underground|url=https://lemonwire.com/2018/05/04/shimmy-shimmy-ya-sheff-g-climbs-from-the-new-york-underground/|access-date=August 15, 2020|website=LemonWire}}</ref>

In 2019, Sleepy Hallow released his debut mixtape, ''Don't Sleep''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Brooklyn's Sleepy Hallow Drops Off Debut Project "Don't Sleep" Ft. Sheff G|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/brooklyns-sleepy-hallow-drops-off-debut-project-dont-sleep-ft-sheff-g-new-mixtape.119724.html|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=HotNewHipHop|date=November 2019}}</ref> In the same year, Winners Circle Entertainment was founded, with Sleepy Hallow as one of the first signees.<ref>{{Cite web|title=WINNERS CIRCLE ENTERTAINMENT ANNOUNCES PARTNERSHIP WITH RCA RECORDS SHEFF G & SLEEPY HALLOW RELEASE NEW RECORD “TIP TOE” – Sony Music Canada|url=https://www.sonymusic.ca/press_release/winners-circle-entertainment-announces-partnership-with-rca-records-sheff-g-sleepy-hallow-release-new-record-tip-toe|access-date=2021-11-30|website=www.sonymusic.ca}}</ref>

In 2020, he released his second mixtape, ''Sleepy for President''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sleepy Hallow: Sleepy For President|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/sleepy-hallow-sleepy-for-president/|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=Pitchfork}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Listen to Sleepy Hallow's Project 'Sleepy for President'|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/06/sleepy-hallow-sleepy-for-president|access-date=August 13, 2020|website=Complex}}</ref> The mixtape featured the hit single "[[Deep End Freestyle]]" featuring Fousheé which peaked on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 chart at number 80 on June 20, 2020.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=The Billboard Hot 100 Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/the-billboard-hot-100/2020-06-20|access-date=August 13, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> The single has over 85 million streams and was [[Music recording certification|certified gold]] by [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] in August 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=https://hiphopdx.com|first=HipHopDX-|date=August 12, 2020|title=NY Drill Stars Sheff G & Sleepy Hallow Ink RCA Deal|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.57299/title.ny-drill-stars-sheff-g-sleepy-hallow-ink-rca-deal|access-date=August 15, 2020|website=HipHopDX}}</ref><ref name="riaa.com" />

In August 2020, Winners Circle Entertainment started a partnership with [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|date=August 12, 2020|title=Winner Circle Entertainment's Sleepy Hallow & Sheff G Announce Partnership With RCA Records: Exclusive|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9433088/sleepy-hallow-sheff-g-rca-records-partnership|access-date=August 13, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> Sleepy Hallow and Sheff G released the single "Tip Toe", produced by Great John.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sheff G and Sleepy Hallow Announce RCA Records Partnership, Share New Song "Tip Toe"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/08/sheff-g-sleepy-hallow-rca-records-partnership-tip-toe|access-date=August 23, 2020|website=Complex}}</ref>

In June 2021, he released his first studio album, ''Still Sleep?''<ref name="Revolt">{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2021/4/16/22388046/sleepy-hallow-2055-video|title=Sleepy Hallow ventures into the year "2055" in new visual|work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|last=Cho|first=Regina|date=April 16, 2021|access-date=July 16, 2021}}</ref> The album includes the single, "[[2055 (song)|2055]]", which was released alongside a video that sees an animated Sleepy Hallow in an alternate, futuristic universe.<ref name="Revolt" /> The single would be certified platinum in October 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gold & Platinum|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Sleepy+Hallow&ti=2055&format=Single&type=#search_section|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-30|website=RIAA|language=en-US}}</ref>
