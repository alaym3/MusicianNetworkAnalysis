'''Yael Naim''' ({{lang-he|יעל נעים}}, born 6 February 1978) is a French-born Israeli singer and actress. She rose to fame in 2008 in the US after her hit single "[[New Soul]]" was used by [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] in an advertising campaign for its [[MacBook Air]]. The song peaked at No. 7 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. In 2013, the French government made her a knight of the {{lang|fr|[[Ordre des Arts et des Lettres]]}}.<ref name=har/>

==Life and career==
Yael Naim was born in [[Paris]], [[France]] to [[Sephardi Jews|Sephardic Jewish]] immigrants from [[Tunisia]]. At the age of four, she moved with her family to [[Ramat HaSharon]], Israel, where she spent the rest of her childhood. She served in the [[Israel Defense Forces]] as a soloist in the [[Israeli Air Force]] Orchestra.<ref>{{Cite news

She began her singing career with a part in the musical ''{{lang|fr|Les Dix Commandements}}''<ref name="artistnews">{{cite web|url=http://artists.letssingit.com/yael-naim-jv79n/news/ |title=Yael Naïm &#124; LetsSingIt Lyrics |website=Artists.letssingit.com |access-date=2020-04-11}}</ref> and her first solo album, ''[[In a Man's Womb]]'' (recorded in Los Angeles with [[Kamil Rustam]]), was released in 2001. She also sang the song "You Disappear" by [[Bruno Coulais]] for the film ''[[Harrison's Flowers]]''. In her early work, she was credited simply as Yael. She also performed a duet with [[Din Din Aviv]] titled "Mashmauyot".<ref>{{YouTube|EZe9UeKmKIM|Din Din Aviv and Yael Naim – Mashmauyot}}</ref> Her version of the [[Britney Spears]] song "[[Toxic (song)|Toxic]]" also garnered significant attention.<ref>[https://www.haaretz.com/1.5007776 Israeli Singer Yael Naim Embraces Apple, Britney Spears for Success] Haaretz. 23 March 2008</ref>

Naim joined with percussionist {{ill|David Donatien|fr}} and, over a period of two years, they arranged and recorded thirteen of Naim's songs in a studio in her apartment in Paris.<ref name="myspace">{{cite web|url=https://www.myspace.com/yaelnaim |title=YAEL NAIM & david donatien &#124; Listen and Stream Free Music, Albums, New Releases, Photos, Videos |website=Myspace.com |access-date=2020-04-11}}</ref> These were released as her second album, ''[[Yael Naim (album)|Yael Naim]]'', on 22 October 2007, on the {{lang|fr|[[Tôt ou tard]]}} label. The songs are in French, English, and [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]] and received critical acclaim.<ref name="artistnews" /> The album entered the French album chart at No. 11 the week after its release. Her style has been described as having a touch of folk and a touch of jazz, with mysterious and evocative words sung with a delicate and intentionally husky voice.<ref name="telerama">[http://www.telerama.fr/musique/20994-yael_naim.php ]{{dead link|date=April 2020}}</ref>

In January 2008, [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] featured her song "[[New Soul]]" in its debut commercial for the [[MacBook Air]] laptop. [[Steve Jobs]] himself picked the song for the launch of the laptop line.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=88415853 |title=Mac Ad Raises Yael Naim's Profile |website=Npr.org |access-date=2020-04-11}}</ref><ref name="song_fame">[http://idolator.com/354270/hey-lets-write-a-song-called-steve-jobs-is-dreamy ''Hey, Let's Write a Song Called "Steve Jobs Is Dreamy"''] 100 And Single, Idolator. Accessed 12 February 2008</ref><ref name="Daily News">Farber, Jim. [http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music/2008/03/11/2008-03-11_apple_ad_creates_recognition_for_yael_na.html Apple ad creates recognition for Yael Naim], ''[[New York Daily News]]'', 11 March 2008.</ref> Owing to high U.S. digital sales, the song debuted on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] for the chart week of 16 February 2008, at No. 9, becoming Naim's first U.S. top ten single, and making her the first [[Israelis|Israeli]] solo artist to ever have a top ten hit in the United States. "New Soul" moved up to No. 7 the following week. The song was also featured on the soundtrack of the movies ''[[The House Bunny]]'' and ''[[Wild Target]]''. Her third album was released in November 2010. The first single from this new record was "Go to the River".

In 2016, she won her second Female Artist of the Year award at {{lang|fr|[[Victoires de la Musique]]}}, the French equivalent of the Grammys.<ref name="nyt">[https://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/17/arts/international/a-french-israeli-singer-takes-an-unlikely-path-to-success.html A French-Israeli Singer Takes an Unlikely Path to Success], ''[[The New York Times]]''. 16 March 2016</ref> She previously won the award in 2011.<ref>[https://www.haaretz.com/1.5130723 French-Israeli Singer Claims Top Award in Paris] Haaretz. 3 March 2011</ref>

==Personal life==
She is married to {{ill|David Donatien|fr}}, a [[Martinique|Martiniquais]] musician, with whom she has two children.<ref name=nyt/>

==Television==
Naim's voice was used in a Season 21 episode of ''[[The Simpsons]]'', entitled "[[The Greatest Story Ever D'ohed]]". She voiced Dorit, the niece of an Israeli tour guide named Jakob (voiced by [[Sacha Baron Cohen]]).<ref name=har>[https://www.haaretz.com/israel-news/culture/.premium-yael-naim-still-feels-like-a-new-soul-1.5352849 Older and Wiser, Singer Yael Naim Still Feels Like a New Soul] Haaretz. 20 April 2015</ref>

Her single "Come Home" was in an episode of Season 7 of ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]'' and in episode 6 of the [[ABC Family]] series ''[[Switched at Birth (TV series)|Switched at Birth]]''. Also, her cover of [[Irene Cara]]'s "[[Flashdance (What a Feeling)]]" was used in the promotion video of ''[[The Voice UK]]'' in 2014.

== Philanthropy ==
On 27 November 2015, she participated together with [[Nolwenn Leroy]] and [[Camélia Jordana]] at the national memorial day for the victims of the [[November 2015 Paris attacks]] singing the song "{{lang|fr|Quand on n'a que l'amour}}" from [[Jacques Brel]].<ref name=lomondex1 >{{cite news |url = http://www.lemonde.fr/attaques-a-paris/article/2015/11/26/hommage-aux-invalides-pour-les-victimes-des-attentats_4818546_4809495.html |title = Hommage aux morts des attentats aux invalides |author1=Emeline Cazi |author2=David Revault d'Allonnes | access-date = 29 November 2015 |work = {{lang|fr|[[Le Monde]]}} |date = 26 November 2015 |language=fr}}</ref>
