'''Vulgaires Machins''' is a [[French Canadian]] [[punk rock]] group from [[Granby, Quebec|Granby]], [[Quebec]].  The group consists of Guillaume Beauregard (vocals, guitars),  Marie-Ève Roy (vocals, guitars), Maxime Beauregard (bass) and Patrick Landry (drums).  Their lyrics denounce consumerism a modern decadence.<ref>[http://blogues.lapresse.ca/brunet/2010/03/01/vulgaires-machins-libres-citoyens/ "Vulgaires Machins, libres citoyens"]. ''La Presse'', 1 March 2010</ref>

==History==
Vulgaires Machins was formed in 1995.<ref name=voir>Olivier Boisvert-Magnen, [https://voir.ca/musique/2016/08/02/il-y-a-10-ans-vulgaires-machins-compter-les-corps/ "Il y a 10 ans : Vulgaires Machins – Compter les corps"]. ''[[Voir]]'', August 2, 2016</ref> The next year they released their first album, ''La vie est belle''.<ref>James Christopher Monger, [http://www.allmusic.com/artist/vulgaires-machins-mn0001382512/biography "Vulgaires Machins"]. ''[[AllMusic]]''.</ref>

Their album ''Compter les corps'' was nominated for the [[Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for Francophone Album of the Year|Francophone Album of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2007]].<ref>"2007 Nominees". ''[[Saskatoon Star-Phoenix]]'', March 30, 2007.</ref>

They were also nominated for French Video of the Year at the 2007 [[MuchMusic Video Awards]] ("Compter les corps"), and in 2010 their song "Parasites" won the French ECHO songwriting prize.<ref>[http://www.chartattack.com/news/2010/10/07/brasstronaut-vulgaires-machins-win-echo-songwriting-prizes/ "Brasstronaut, Vulgaires Machins Win ECHO Songwriting Prizes"]. ''Chart Attack'' - Oct 7, 2010</ref> In 2012 they were featured on CBC Television's ''Studio 12''.<ref>[http://ici.radio-canada.ca/emissions/studio_12/2011-2012/document.asp?idDoc=196789 "Vulgaires Machins"]. ''Studio 12'', CBC Television, 12 février 2012</ref>

Guillaume Beauregard has also released music as a solo artist, receiving a [[SOCAN Songwriting Prize]] nomination in 2015 for his solo single "De pluie et de cendre",<ref>[https://voir.ca/nouvelles/actualite-musicale/2015/07/09/antoine-corriveau-remporte-le-10e-prix-de-la-chanson-socan/ "Antoine Corriveau remporte le 10e Prix de la chanson SOCAN"]. ''[[Voir]]'', July 9, 2015.</ref> and has had acting roles including in [[Maxime Giroux]]'s 2008 film ''[[Tomorrow (2008 film)|Tomorrow]] (Demain)''.<ref>Michel Tassé, [https://www.lavoixdelest.ca/archives/guillaume-beauregard-fait-son-cinema-6e573f7da1ab33b5198921a3bcacf701 "Guillaume Beauregard fait son cinéma"]. ''La Voix de l'Est'', January 29, 2009.</ref>
