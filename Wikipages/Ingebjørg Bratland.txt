'''Ingebjørg Harman Bratland''' (born 8 May 1990 in [[Edland]], [[Vinje]], [[Telemark]])<ref name=snl>{{cite web | url=https://snl.no/Ingebj%C3%B8rg_Harman_Bratland | title=Ingebjørg Bratland | language=Norwegian | last=Bitustøyl | first=Kjell | publisher=[[Store Norske Leksikon]] | date=14 December 2015 | accessdate=13 February 2016}}</ref> is a [[Norwegians|Norwegian]] [[folk singer]], [[kveder]] and artist. In 2008–2009, she attended [[Foss Upper Secondary School]] in Oslo.

== Early life ==
Bratland began singing as a [[kveder]] when she was five years old,<ref name=snl/> and was notable for her ability to perform folk songs, [[stev]] and ballads. She started taking singing lessons at an early age, primarily from Ellen Nordstoga. In 2004, at age 14, she received the 'Eckbo legaters folkemusikkpris'.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.folkedans.com/folkemusikk/Eckbo_prisvinnarar.htm | title=Vinnarar av "Eckbo legaters Folkemusikkpris for unge utøvere" | language=Norwegian | publisher= |website=Folkedans.com | accessdate=13 February 2016}}</ref> As a kveder, Bratland won the C-class at Landskappleiken traditional music competition four times. In 2009, she won the vocal class A during the Norwegian championship in traditional folk music at Geilo. She is the Nordic Champion in traditional folk music and, in 2009, received the [[Vinje]] Municipality Culture Scholarship.<ref name=vl>{{cite web | url=http://www.vl.no/reportasjer/mintro/tr%C3%B8yster%C3%B8ysta-1.48489 | title=Trøysterøysta | language=Norwegian | last=Kvamme | first=Kjell | publisher= |work=[[Vårt Land (Norwegian newspaper)|Vårt Land]] | date=15 November 2015 | accessdate=13 February 2016}}</ref> In 2010, Bratland was awarded the Fureprisen, which included NKR 50 000.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ballade.no/sak/fureprisen-til-ung-kveder | title=Fureprisen til ung kveder | language=Norwegian | first=Espen A. | last=Eik | publisher= |website=Ballade.no | date=26 June 2010 | accessdate=13 February 2016}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2013, Bratland released the album ''Heimafrå'' together with [[Odd Nordstoga]]. She was awarded the 2013 [[Spellemannprisen]] in the category "traditional folk music" for this album. She was nominated for the 2014 [[Spellemannprisen]] in the category "folk music" for her first solo album ''Berre Meg''. Her second solo album, ''Månesinn'', was released in 2015. The album contains tunes Bratland wrote herself, with the producers [[Espen Lind]] and Geir Hvidsten. Following her performance at the 2016 Spellemannsprisen, a remix of the single "Stjernene" with the rapper [[Lars Vaular]] as guest artist was released.

She contributed the song "Mitt hjerte alltid vanker" on H. K. H. Kronprinsesse Mette-Marits ''Sorgen Og Gleden'' and participated in other concerts and performances, including ''Den fyste song'' for H. M. Kong Harald in connection with his 70th birthday (2007).<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HKV27yS3ZwM& | title=King Harald unveiling a statue of his late mother | publisher= |website=YouTube | accessdate=13 February 2016}}</ref>

Bratland performed the title track of the children series ''Jul i svingen'' on [[NRK1]]. The series' music was written by [[Odd Nordstoga]] and was awarded the 2006 [[Spellemannprisen]] in the category Best music album for children. Bratland and Odd Nordstoga give a duet on the tune "Julevise". She participated in television broadcasts ''Kjempesjansen'' and ''Beat for Beat'' at [[NRK1]], and ''Det store korslaget'' at [[TV2 Norway]]. She performed [[Nordahl Grieg]]'s "Til Ungdommen" [[a cappella]] at the memorial concert in Oslo Cathedral 30 July 2011.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d18fHlaV3UA | title=Sang: 'Til ungdommen' – Ingebjørg Bratland | publisher= |website=YouTube | accessdate=13 February 2016}}</ref>
