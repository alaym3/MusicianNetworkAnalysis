'''Willemijn Verkaik''' ({{IPA-nl|ˈʋɪləmɛiɱ vərˈkɑik}}; born 16 June 1975) is a Dutch singer and actress. She is best known for her stage roles in ''[[Wicked (musical)|Wicked]]'' and ''[[Elisabeth (musical)|Elisabeth]]'', and for providing the singing voice for Elsa in both the German and Dutch versions of Disney's ''[[Frozen (2013 film)|Frozen]]''. She was the longest running Elphaba in the musical ''[[Wicked (musical)|Wicked]]'', having played the role over 2,000 times, and is the only person to have played the role in three languages.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.wickedthemusical.co.uk/london/about/cast-creative/willemijn-verkaik|title=Wicked The Musical|last=http://feastcreative.com|first=Feast Creative |website=wickedthemusical.co.uk|access-date=24 March 2017}}</ref> Her first performance as Elphaba was on 31 October 2007, in [[Stuttgart]], Germany, and her final performance was on 22 July 2017 in the [[West End of London]].

==Early life==
Verkaik was born in [[Son en Breugel]], Netherlands, on 16 June 1975, and grew up in [[Nuenen]] near [[Eindhoven]]. She graduated from the [[Rotterdam Conservatory]].<ref name="Nieuws" /><ref name="Zibb">[https://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=auto&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.rbb-online.de%2Fzibb%2Fvip%2Fbeitraege%2Fwillemijn_verkaik.html "Willemijn Verkaik and Mark Seibert"], Zibb {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140817065625/http://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=auto&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.rbb-online.de%2Fzibb%2Fvip%2Fbeitraege%2Fwillemijn_verkaik.html |date=17 August 2014 }}</ref>

==Career==
Before her acting career began, Verkaik sang in various pop bands for over ten years.<ref name="Nieuws">[http://musicalreports.nl/?p=10408 "Willemijn Verkaik maakt debuut in New York"], ''Musical Reports'', 2 August 2011 {{in lang|nl}}</ref> Her first professional theatre role was that of an ensemble member in the Dutch production of ''[[Elisabeth (musical)|Elisabeth]]''.<ref name="Playbill">[http://www.playbill.com/news/article/176808-DIVA-TALK-A-Chat-with-Dutch-Actress-Willemijn-Verkaik-Star-of-Broadways-Wicked "Diva Talk: A Chat with Dutch Actress Willemijn Verkaik, Star of Broadway's ''Wicked''"], ''[[Playbill]]''</ref>

In 2004, Verkaik recorded the singing voice of Erica in the Dutch version of the animated movie ''[[Barbie as the Princess and the Pauper]]''. In 2008, she was the Dutch voice of Lydia in ''[[Barbie & the Diamond Castle]]''.

Since then, her musical theatre credits have included ''Elisabeth'' (in Holland and Thun, Switzerland),<ref name="Musical">[http://www.musicalsite.be/news.php?id=3131 "Willemijn: alweer Nederlandse Elisabeth"], Musical Site  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110520042809/http://www.musicalsite.be/news.php?id=3131 |date=20 May 2011 }}</ref> ''[[The Three Musketeers (musical)|The Three Musketeers]]'', ''[[We Will Rock You (musical)|We Will Rock You]]'',<ref name="Nieuws" /><ref name="Zibb" /> and most notably, ''[[Wicked (musical)|Wicked]]'', in which she was chosen as the "Wicked Personality of the Year" in 2010, out of all the actresses who had played the role, in an online poll with over 16,000 voters.<ref>http://broadwayiswicked.com/2010/11/11/willemijn-verkaik-favourite-elphaba/  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101122170857/http://broadwayiswicked.com/2010/11/11/willemijn-verkaik-favourite-elphaba/ |date=22 November 2010 }}</ref> Verkaik is the only actress in the world to play Elphaba in three languages: she has performed in the German, Dutch, Broadway, and West End productions.

Verkaik originated the role of [[Elphaba]] in the original German-language production of ''[[Wicked (musical)|Wicked]]'', which was entitled "Wicked: Die Hexen Von Oz", and began performances in [[Stuttgart]] on 1 November 2007, prior to an official opening night on November 15. Sabrina Weckerlin was her original alternate, appearing in the two shows a week in which Verkaik was not contracted to perform.<ref>[https://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=auto&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fboulevard.nieuws.nl%2F477316%2FNederlandse_bemachtigt_hoofdrol_in_Duitse_musical "Dutch obtains in leading German musical"], Nieuws {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150723024436/http://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&sl=auto&tl=en&u=http%3A%2F%2Fboulevard.nieuws.nl%2F477316%2FNederlandse_bemachtigt_hoofdrol_in_Duitse_musical |date=23 July 2015 }}</ref><ref>[http://www.playbill.com/news/article/110856-Verkaik-and-Schere-to-Star-in-Germanys-Wicked "Verkaik and Schere to Star in Germany's Wicked"], ''[[Playbill]]''</ref><ref>[http://www.citynews-koeln.de/verkaik-willemijn-musical-hexen-elphaba-wicked-_id430.html "Willemijn Verkaik ist die grüne Hexe von 'Ozerhausen'"], staff writer, ''City News'', 5 December 2009 {{in lang|de}}</ref>

[[File:Willemijn Verkaik.png|thumb|Verkaik, 2012]]
Verkaik soon repeated her performance in the Dutch-language production, which premiered in [[Scheveningen]] on 6 November 2011, after previews from 26 October. This required her to learn the role in her native language. The run ended on 11 January 2013 and Verkaik then transferred to North America, making her Broadway debut in the English-speaking New York company of ''Wicked'' and marking her third language production. Verkaik played a limited 15-week engagement at the [[Gershwin Theatre]] from 12 February 2013 to 26 May 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/willemijn-verkaik-flies-into-broadways-wicked-feb.-12-202415|title=Willemijn Verkaik Flies into Broadway's ''Wicked'' Feb. 12|author=Andrew Gans|work=Playbill|date=12 February 2013}}</ref>

Verkaik subsequently played the role of Donna in the Stuttgart production of ''[[Mamma Mia! (musical)|Mamma Mia!]]'' for a limited run starting 26 July 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stage-entertainment.de/musicals-shows/mamma-mia-stuttgart/news/willemijn-verkaik-bei-mamma-mia.html|title=Vom Broadway zurück nach Stuttgart: Willemijn Verkaik spielt Hauptrolle bei Mamma Mia!|trans-title=From Broadway back to Stuttgart: Willemijn Verkaik to play the main role in Mamma Mia!|language=de|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140729000410/http://www.stage-entertainment.de/musicals-shows/mamma-mia-stuttgart/news/willemijn-verkaik-bei-mamma-mia.html|archivedate=29 July 2014}}</ref> before playing the role of Elphaba again. Beginning on 18 November 2013, she made her West End debut in ''Wicked'' at the [[Apollo Victoria Theatre]], London.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/westend/article/Willemijn-Verkaik-Confirmed-As-Londons-New-Elphaba-In-WICKED-From-November-20130603|title=Willemijn Verkaik Confirmed as London's New Elphaba in ''Wicked'' From November!|author=BWW News Desk|work=Broadway World}}</ref> Due to health reasons, Verkaik had to depart early from the West End production of ''Wicked'' on 19 July 2014, and was replaced by original cast member [[Kerry Ellis]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Kerry-Ellis-to-Return-to-West-Ends-WICKED-Willemijn-Verkaik-Will-Play-Final-Performance-this-Saturday-20140714|title=Kerry Ellis to Return to West End's ''Wicked''; Willemijn Verkaik Will Play Final Performance this Saturday|author=BWW News Desk}}</ref>

She can be heard on ''Wicked'''s German-language [[cast recording]], as well as Scott Alan's album ''What I Wanna Be When I Grow Up''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://broadwayworld.com/article/Photo_Coverage_The_Songs_of_Scott_Alan_at_Barnes_Noble_20101204|title=Photo Coverage: The Songs of Scott Alan at Barnes & Noble|author=Genevieve Rafter Keddy|work=BroadwayWorld.com}}</ref> and on the Scott Alan album ''LIVE'', which was recorded at the [[Birdland (New York jazz club)|Birdland]] jazz club in New York City on 30 April 2012. The CD was released on 26 June 2012.

Verkaik also provided the Dutch speaking and singing voice and the German singing voice of Elsa, the Snow Queen in Disney's 2013 animated movie ''[[Frozen (2013 film)|Frozen]]'' (the German speaking voice was provided by Dina Kürten). She also performed the song "[[Let It Go (Disney song)|Let It Go]]" in English at the UK premiere of the movie.

It was announced on 24 October 2014 that Verkaik would be joining the cast of the musical, ''[[Women on the Verge of a Nervous Breakdown (musical)|Women on the Verge of a Nervous Breakdown]]'', alongside Tasmin Greig, Ricardo Afonso, Hadyn Gwynne, Jérôme Pradon and Anna Skellern, in the role of Paulina.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/westend/article/WICKEDs-Willemijn-Verkaik-and-More-Join-Tamsin-Greig-in-West-Ends-WOMEN-ON-THE-VERGE-OF-A-NERVOUS-BREAKDOWN-20141023#.VEnaE1znHKI.twitter|title=''Wicked''{{'}}s Willemijn Verkaik and More Join Tamsin Greig in West End's ''Women on the Verge of a Nervous Breakdown''|author=BWW News Desk|work=BroadwayWorld.com}}</ref> The show's previews started on 16 December 2014 at the West End's [[Playhouse Theatre]] with an opening night on 12 January 2015. The show ran until 23 May 2015. This was Verkaik's first role after her surgery in 2014.

It was announced on 14 July 2015 that Verkaik would be returning to Stuttgart as Kala in ''[[Tarzan (musical)|Tarzan]]'' in August 2015, at the Stage Apollo Theater.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/former-broadway-elphaba-willemijn-verkaik-joins-the-german-cast-of-disneys-tarzan-357727|title=Former Broadway Elphaba Willemijn Verkaik Joins the German Cast of Disney's ''Tarzan''|author=Joe Gambino|work=[[Playbill]]|date=14 August 2015}}</ref>

On 14 September 2016, it was revealed that Verkaik would return to her starring role of Elphaba in the West End production of ''Wicked'' beginning 30 January 2017 as part of the production's 10th anniversary celebrations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/westend/article/-Willemijn-Verkaik-Returns-to-WICKED-this-January-20160914|title=Willemijn Verkaik Returns to ''Wicked'' this January!|author=BWW News Desk|work=Broadway World}}</ref> She played her final performance on 22 July 2017 and was succeeded by Alice Fearn.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.whatsonstage.com/west-end-theatre/news/new-cast-wicked-alice-fearn-sophie-evans_43683.html|title=New cast announced for Wicked|author=Ben Hewis|work=Whats on Stage}}</ref> Verkaik confirmed in her speech at the end of her final performance that, after 10 years, it was her last ever performance as Elphaba.

On 10 September 2017 it was announced that Verkaik would star in ''[[Ghost the Musical|Ghost – Das Musical]]'' in Berlin from 7 December 2017 to 7 October 2018, in the role of Molly.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nu.nl/cultuur-overig/4915769/willemijn-verkaik-speelt-hoofdrol-in-musical-ghost-in-berlijn.html|title=Willemijn Verkaik plays the lead in musical Ghost in Berlin |work=nu.nl|date=10 September 2017 }}</ref>

She next appeared as Sloane in the [[Metronom Theater]] production of ''[[Bat Out of Hell The Musical|Bat Out of Hell]]'' from 8 November 2018 to 30 March 2019, with previews starting from 2 November.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bellmedia.ca/pr/press/german-premier-in-oberhausen-bat-out-of-hell-the-musical-featuring-global-hits-by-jim-steinman-and-originally-performed-by-the-worldwide-legend-meat-loaf/|title=German premier in Oberhausen: BAT OUT OF HELL – The Musical featuring global hits by Jim Steinman and originally performed by the worldwide legend Meat Loaf – Bell Media|website=bellmedia.ca|access-date=5 November 2018}}</ref>

She then played Pepa in the Amsterdam production of ''Women on the Verge of a Nervous Breakdown'' from 4 July 2019 to 14 July 2019.<ref>https://vrouwenopderandvaneenzenuwinzinking.nl/</ref><ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=http://willemijnverkaik.nl/schedule-of-events/|title=Schedule of Events – Willemijn Verkaik|accessdate=14 December 2020}}</ref>

She toured the Netherlands playing Miss Hannigan in ''[[Annie (musical)|Annie]]'' from 26 November 2019 to 24 May 2020, with previews starting 5 November in Hoorn.<ref name="auto"/>

On 14 November 2019, it was announced that she will play the lead role of Jenna in ''[[Waitress (musical)|Waitress]]'' in the Netherlands in 2020–2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicaljournaal.nl/musical-waitress-met-willemijn-verkaik-in-de-hoofdrol-naar-nederlandse-theaters/|title=Musical 'Waitress' met Willemijn Verkaik in de hoofdrol naar Nederlandse theaters • musicaljournaal|date=14 November 2019|accessdate=14 December 2020}}</ref>

On 9 February 2020, Verkaik was called in to join American actress and singer [[Idina Menzel]], [[Aurora (singer)|Aurora]] and eight more of Elsa's international dubbers to perform the song “[[Into the Unknown (Disney song)|Into the Unknown]]” during the [[92nd Academy Awards]]. Every international performer sang one line of the song in a different language: [[Maria Lucia Rosenberg|Maria Lucia Heiberg Rosenberg]] in [[Danish language|Danish]], Verkaik in [[German language|German]], [[Takako Matsu]] in [[Japanese language|Japanese]], [[Carmen Sarahí]] in [[Spanish language in the Americas|Latin American Spanish]], [[Lisa Stokke]] in [[Norwegian language|Norwegian]], [[Katarzyna Łaska|Kasia Łaska]] in [[Polish language|Polish]], [[Anna Buturlina]] in [[Russian language|Russian]], [[Gisela (singer)|Gisela]] in [[Peninsular Spanish|European Spanish]] and [[Wichayanee Pearklin|Gam Wichayanee]] in [[Thai language|Thai]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/watch-idina-menzel-and-9-fellow-elsas-sing-frozen-2s-into-the-unknown-at-the-oscars|title=Watch Idina Menzel and 9 Fellow Elsas Sing Frozen 2's 'Into the Unknown' at the Oscars|last=McPhee|first=Ryan|date=2020-02-10|website=Playbill|language=en|access-date=2020-02-10}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Verkaik is multilingual, speaking Dutch, German and English. She is married to musician Bart van Hoof, a [[saxophone]] player.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gettyimages.com/detail/news-photo/bart-van-hoof-and-wife-willemijn-verkaik-pose-at-the-news-photo/161612956|title=Bart van Hoof and wife Willemijn Verkaik pose at the stagedoor as...|author=Bruce Glikas|work=Getty Images}}</ref>

== Awards ==

*In 2008 and 2010 Verkaik was elected best actress by readers of the German music magazine ''Da Capo'' for her portrayal of Elphaba in the German production of ''Wicked''.
*In 2009 she was elected best actress by readers of the German music magazine ''Da Capo'' for her portrayal of Amneris in the German production of ''Aïda''.
*In 2009 and 2010 she won the audience prize from the German magazine ''Musicals'' for best musical actress.
*In 2010 Verkaik won the voting organised by the site ''BroadwayisWicked.com'' where people could vote for the best Elphaba that played in a production of ''Wicked'' worldwide.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20101122170857/http://broadwayiswicked.com/2010/11/11/willemijn-verkaik-favourite-elphaba/ Willemijn Verkaik Favourite Elphaba]</ref> She received 49.9% of a total of 16,705 cast votes.
*On 9 January 2012 it was announced that Verkaik again, in a voting organised by ''BroadwayisWicked.com'', was titled ''Wicked Personality of the Year 2011''.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20160310124839/http://broadwayiswicked.com/2012/01/08/willemijn-verkaik-is-wicked-personality-of-the-year-2011/ Willemijn Verkaik is Wicked personality of the year 2011]</ref> She received 55% of a total of 590,000 cast votes.
*In May 2012 Verkaik won the ''Musicalworld Award'' for "best female lead in a big musical" for the portrayal of Elphaba in the Dutch production of Wicked.
