'''Tavoris Javon Hollins Jr.''' (born August 17, 1997), known professionally as '''Vory''' (formerly '''King Vory'''), is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He won a [[Grammy]] for his work on [[The Carters]]' ''[[Everything Is Love]]'' and has released several solo projects including the EP ''Lucky Me'' in 2018 and the single "You Got It" in 2019. He is currently signed to Electric Feel Entertainment and [[Dream Chasers Records|Dream Chasers]].<ref>[https://hiphopdx.com/singles/id.38547/title.vory-tells-his-side-in-cc-interlude name="Vory Tells His Side in "cc interlude]</ref>

==Early life==
Tavoris Javon Hollins Jr. was born and raised in [[Houston]], [[Texas]], He later moved to [[Louisville, Kentucky]] at age 16 to live with his father. While there, he met and began collaborating with singer and Louisville native, [[Bryson Tiller]]. He was formerly signed to Louisville-based record label, FPR Music Group, Derrick Washington, Sean Bowens and Latasha Harrison.<ref name="Maxx">{{cite news |last=Harrison |first=Brad |title=Following His Feature On Bryson Tiller's "Don't", Louisville Artist 'King Vory' Signs BMG Deal|publisher=Urban Maxx |date=4 April 2016| url=https://urbanmaxx.com/2016/04/04/following-his-feature-on-bryson-tillers-dont-louisville-artist-king-vory-signs-bmg-deal/ |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2015, Vory first received attention from the music industry after he was featured on Bryson Tiller's song, "Break Bread", under his former stage name King Vory.<ref name="DXBreak">{{cite magazine |title=Bryson Tiller f. King Vory – "Break Bread"|magazine=HipHopDX |date=12 March 2015| url=https://hiphopdx.com/singles/id.30096/title.bryson-tiller-f-king-vory-break-bread |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> He also co-wrote Tiller's, "[[Don't (Bryson Tiller song)|Don't]]",<ref name="HNHHGot">{{cite news |last=P |first=Milca |title=Vory Applies More Pressure On "You Got It" |publisher=HotNewHipHop |date=23 March 2019 |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/vory-applies-more-pressure-on-you-got-it-new-song.1982387.html |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> which reached number 13 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]<ref name="Tiller100">{{cite magazine |title=Bryson Tiller - Chart History - Hot 100|magazine=Billboard | url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Bryson-Tiller/chart-history/hot-100 |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> and appeared on Tiller's debut studio album ''[[Trapsoul]]''.<ref name="Maxx"/> In 2016, Vory began independently releasing songs, including "Overdose" <ref name="SourceDose">{{cite magazine |title=King Vory Releases First Single After Working With Bryson Tiller|magazine=The Source |date=9 February 2016| url=http://thesource.com/2016/02/09/king-vory-releases-first-single-after-working-with-bryson-tiller/ |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> and "My Life A Movie" featuring [[Blu (rapper)|Blu]] and produced by [[Dun Deal]].<ref name="HNHHMovie">{{cite news |last=Schwartz |first=Danny |title=Vory - My Life A Movie Feat. Blu (Prod. By Dun Deal) |publisher=HotNewHipHop |date=24 March 2016| url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/king-vory-my-life-a-movie-feat-blu-prod-by-dun-deal-new-song.1969072.html |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> Later in July 2016, FPR Music Group released his debut mixtape, ''Overdose''.<ref name="HNHHHold">{{cite news |last=Goddard |first=Kevin |title=Vory Drops Off New Single "Hold Of Me" |publisher=HotNewHipHop |date=7 November 2017| url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/vory-drops-off-new-single-hold-of-me-new-song.1976185.html |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref>

Later that year he moved to [[Los Angeles]], where he started working on developing his current sound. 
He met Canadian record producer [[Boi-1da]], with whom he would later collaborate on a variety of tracks.<ref name="LAW">{{cite news |last=Ju |first=Shirley |title=Track of the Week: Vory's "You Got It" |newspaper=LA Weekly |date=8 April 2019|url=https://www.laweekly.com/music/track-of-the-week-vorys-you-got-it-10145519 |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> In 2017, Vory continued releasing singles, including "Try",<ref name="ComplexTry">{{cite magazine |last=Ortiz |first=Edwin |title=Premiere: Vory Drops New Song "Try"|magazine=Complex |date=7 July 2017| url=https://www.complex.com/music/2017/07/vory-try |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> "Do That Shit",<ref name="Mass">{{cite news |last=Kenner |first=Rob |title=PREMIERE: Vory "Do That Shit"|publisher=MASS APPEAL |date=8 September 2017| url=http://archive.massappeal.com/vory-do-that-shit-premiere/ |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> and "Hold of Me". The latter song was premiered by [[Zane Lowe]] on [[Beats 1]] Radio.<ref name="HNHHHold"/> In 2018, he earned writing credits on numerous songs including Canadian rapper [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s "[[Mob Ties]]" from his fifth studio album ''[[Scorpion (Drake album)|Scorpion]]'' and American duo [[The Carters]]' "Friends" from their collaborate studio album ''[[Everything Is Love]]'',<ref name="LAW"/> which would go on to win a Grammy for his work on the album.<ref name="HNHHGot"/>

In August 2018, he signed to [[Capitol Records]] and Electric Feel Management and released his debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''Lucky Me''.<ref name="Respect">{{cite news |last=Rosario |first=Ricky |title=Taking Streaming Services by Surprise is Vory with 'Lucky Me'|publisher=Respect |date=23 August 2018| url=https://respect-mag.com/2018/08/taking-streaming-services-by-surprise-is-vory-with-lucky-me/ |access-date =9 April 2019}}</ref> He co-wrote and was featured in [[Rich the Kid]]'s song "Ring Ring" from his second studio album ''[[The World Is Yours 2]]'' and in the same day, Vory released a new single, "You Got It".<ref name="HNHHGot"/>

In June 2020, he signed to [[Dream Chasers Records|DreamChasers]] and released a self titled EP "Vory".<ref name="Stream Vory’s Self-Titled Album f/ Meek Mill, Starrah, and More"> {{Cite web|title=Stream Vory's Self-Titled Album f/ Meek Mill, Starrah, and More|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/12/vorys-new-self-titled-album-listen|access-date=2021-01-04|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>

Vory appeared on [[Kanye West]]'s tenth studio album, ''[[Donda]]'', on tracks God Breathed, Jonah, and No Child Left Behind.
