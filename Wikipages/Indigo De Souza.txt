'''Indigo De Souza''' is an American singer-songwriter from [[Asheville, North Carolina]]. Her most recent album ''[[Any Shape You Take]]'' was released in August 2021. She has been noted for creating "intimate, anxious [[indie rock]] songs [that] wrangle with disappointment and relationship challenges,"<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Donelson|first=Marcy|title=Indigo De Souza Biography, Songs, & Albums|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/indigo-de-souza-mn0003536018/biography|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-04|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> with personal and confessional lyrics.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lopez|first=Julyssa|title=Indigo De Souza|url=https://www.newyorker.com/goings-on-about-town/night-life/indigo-de-souza|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-04|website=The New Yorker|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Freedman|first=Max|date=25 August 2021|title=Indigo De Souza Is Just Being Honest|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3180185/indigo-de-souza-interview-any-shape-you-take/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-04|website=MTV News|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
De Souza grew up in the conservative small town of [[Spruce Pine, North Carolina]], where she and her artist mother faced difficulty fitting in.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Moreland|first=Quinn|date=2021-08-12|title=Indigo De Souza’s Ultimate Indie Rock Glow Up|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/indigo-de-souza-any-shape-you-take-interview/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-04|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref> Her family moved to Asheville during her teen years.<ref name=":2" />

With encouragement from her mother, De Souza started making music at age 9,<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Indigo De Souza|url=http://www.masqueradeatlanta.com/attraction/indigo-de-souza/|access-date=2021-09-04|website=The Masquerade|language=en-US}}</ref> and began self-producing her own recordings in 2016. Her first release was an EP titled ''Boys'' that was recorded in a friend's garage, followed by another EP titled ''Don't Cry Just Do'' in 2017.<ref name=":0" /> She self-released her first full-length album, ''[[I Love My Mom]]'', in June 2018. [[Saddle Creek Records]] discovered the album and gave it a proper release in 2021.<ref name=":0" /> The album gained notice from journalists for De Souza's lyrics on taboo topics.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Russell|first=Scott|date=2021-08-23|title=Indigo De Souza Is a Light on Any Shape You Take|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/music/indigo-de-souza/any-shape-you-take-album-review/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-04|website=pastemagazine.com|language=en}}</ref>

De Souza co-produced her second album, ''[[Any Shape You Take]]'', with Brad Cook who has also produced for [[Bon Iver]] and [[Waxahatchee]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chelosky|first=Danielle|date=2021-07-14|title=Artist To Watch: Indigo De Souza|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2153851/indigo-de-souza-artist-to-watch/interviews/band-to-watch/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-04|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref> The album was released in August 2021,<ref name=":0" /> and was noted for De Souza's emotional singing and her mastery of several different musical styles.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Indigo De Souza: Any Shape You Take|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/indigo-de-souza-any-shape-you-take/|access-date=2021-09-04|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref> De Souza began to tour with a full band featuring Dexter Webb (guitar), Zack Kardon (bass), and Avery Sullivan (drums). She also formed a [[neo soul]] side project called Icky Bricketts with Ethan Baechtold.<ref name=":1" />
