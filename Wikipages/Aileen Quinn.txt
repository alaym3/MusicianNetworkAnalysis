'''Aileen Quinn''' (born June 28, 1971) is an American actress, singer and dancer. She is best known for her role as Annie Warbucks in the 1982 film ''[[Annie (1982 film)|Annie]]''.

==Early life, family and education==
Quinn was born and raised in [[Yardley, Pennsylvania|Yardley]], [[Pennsylvania]],<ref name= speaks>{{cite news| url= http://www.nytimes.com/1982/05/16/movies/the-film-annie-speaks-her-mind.html |title= The Film Annie Speaks Her Mind | date= May 16, 1982| work= The New York Times| page= B1| first= Anna | last= Quindlen| access-date= December 4, 2020}}</ref> on June 28, 1971. The oldest child of Helenann Quinn and Andrew Quinn Sr., she is the older sister of Andrew Quinn Jr.<ref name= freckles>{{cite news| url= http://people.com/archive/cover-story-freckles-and-charm-vol-18-no-2/ |title= Freckles and Charm | website= [[People (magazine)|People]]| first= Richard K. |date= July 12, 1982 | last= Rein }}</ref> She began dance lessons when she was 4 at Knecht [[Ballet]] Academy in [[Levittown, Pennsylvania]], learning ballet and [[tap dance]].<ref name= speaks />

When she was young, Quinn was introduced to [[show business]] by her mother, who was a TV, radio, and stage singer/actress then elementary school teacher.<ref name= speaks /> Quinn begged to audition for a part in a local [[community theater]] production of ''[[Annie Get Your Gun (musical)|Annie Get Your Gun]]'' and won a role.<ref name= speaks /> She performed in additional community productions.

She attended Grey Nun Academy during elementary school<ref name=blu-ray>{{cite news| url= http://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/blogs/this-week-dvd-blu-ray/annie-30th-anniversary-dvd-star-aileen-quinn-recalls-175011097.html |title= 'Annie' 30th anniversary Blu-ray: Star Aileen Quinn recalls little-known facts | website= Yahoo.com| first= Meriah |last= Doty |date= October 4, 2012| access-date= December 4, 2020}}</ref> and graduated from [[Oak Knoll School of the Holy Child]] in [[Summit, New Jersey|Summit]], [[New Jersey]].

Taking a break between her [[child actor|child acting]] career, at the age of 18, Quinn decided to focus on school. She graduated from [[Drew University]] in 1994.<ref name= catching>{{cite news| url= http://ew.com/article/2012/10/02/aileen-quinn-30-year-anniversary-bluray-annie/ |title= 'Annie' Blu-ray: Catching up with Aileen Quinn | date= October 2, 2012| work= [[Entertainment Weekly]]| first=  Jeff | last= Labrecque | access-date= December 4, 2020}}</ref> She was a language major with a minor in [[political science]], and is a member of [[Sigma Delta Pi]], a Spanish honor society.

==Career==
After obtaining an agent, Quinn won a small role in the film ''[[Paternity (film)|Paternity]]'' and commercial work in New York City. She began appearing in television commercials including [[Planters]] [[Cheese puffs|Cheez Balls]], [[Shake ‘n Bake]]<ref name=blu-ray /> and Northern [[toilet paper|Bathroom Tissue]]. At age 8,<ref name=blu-ray /> she landed the role in New York City of the "swing orphan"<ref name= speaks /> ([[understudy]] to all of the orphans except Molly and Annie) in the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] production of ''[[Annie (musical)|Annie]]''.<ref name=blu-ray />

After eight auditions over the course of a year, and up against over 8,000 other competitors,<ref name= freckles /> Quinn received the title role in the 1982 movie ''[[Annie (1982 film)|Annie]]'', directed by [[John Huston]].<ref name=blu-ray /> ''Annie'' earned Quinn two [[Golden Globe Award|Golden Globe]] nominations, a win for "Best Actress" from the [[Young Artist Award|Youth in Film Awards]], and a [[3rd Golden Raspberry Awards|Razzie]] for "Worst New Star". Aileen was 9 years old when she was cast as Annie.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://www.upi.com/Archives/1981/01/14/pennsylvania-girl-will-play-Americas-favorite-orphan/6229348296400/ |title= Pennsylvania girl will play America's favorite orphan | date= January 14, 1981| website= UPI.com| agency= UPI}}</ref>

Aileen Quinn was under contract for several years with [[Columbia Pictures]] to make other ''Annie'' sequels which never materialized.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://nypost.com/2014/07/16/32-years-later-annie-still-singing-annie-songs/ |title= 32 years later, Annie still singing 'Annie' songs | date= July 16, 2014| work= New York Post}}</ref> During this time, she continued performing in lead roles in [[regional theater]] in such shows as ''[[The Wizard of Oz (1982 film)|The Wizard of Oz]]'' ([[Dorothy Gale|Dorothy]]), ''[[Bye Bye Birdie (musical)|Bye, Bye Birdie]]'' (Kim), ''Annie'' (Annie), ''[[Shenandoah (musical)|Shenandoah]]'' (Jenny), and ''[[A Day in Hollywood/A Night in the Ukraine]]'' (Harpo).  She also lent her voice to two animated cartoon specials which aired on national television, ''[[The Charmkins]]'' and ''[[The Wizard of Oz (1982 film)|The Wizard of Oz]]''.  The ''Annie'' film soundtrack album went [[double platinum]], and subsequently Quinn released her own album, ''Bobby's Girl'', in 1982. Soon after that release, she starred as Princess Zora in the classic fairy tale ''[[The Frog Prince (1986 film)|The Frog Prince]]'', which was released on videocassette and aired on the [[Disney Channel]] several times.

After graduation from college, Quinn once again took to the stage.  In 1994, she appeared as Bette in ''[[Oliver!]]'' at [[Paper Mill Playhouse]].  She toured the US for more than five years with three Broadway national tours: ''[[Fiddler on the Roof]]'' (Chava), ''[[Peter Pan]]'' (Tootles/Jane) and ''[[Saturday Night Fever (musical)|Saturday Night Fever]]'' (Annette). She studied [[Shakespeare's works|Shakespeare]] in London, where she appeared in ''[[As You Like It]]'' and ''[[Twelfth Night (play)|Twelfth Night]]'' at [[London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art|LAMDA]]. Off-Broadway productions include ''Dreamstuff'' (Princess), ''Creature'' (Elizabeth) and ''Yiddle With a Fiddle'' (Yiddle). Regional theater credits up to the present include ''[[The Unsinkable Molly Brown (musical)|The Unsinkable Molly Brown]]'' (Molly), ''[[That Was Then]]'' (World Premiere Play-April Gregory), and ''Funny, You Don't Look Like a Grandmother'' (her Daughter).

Quinn appeared in a few small roles in independent films between 2006 and 2010, most notably portraying Lily in ''Annie'' at Theatre Aspen. She hosted ''[[Generation Gap (TV series)|Generation Gap]]'', an interactive sitcom used to teach kids how to resolve conflicts. It was written and directed by filmmaker Edna Harris and aired on [[PBS]] in 2004. Quinn played a minor role in the 2009 film ''[[Multiple Sarcasms]]'' which stars [[Timothy Hutton]] and [[Mira Sorvino]].

Quinn is an adjunct theater professor at [[Monmouth University]] in [[West Long Branch, New Jersey]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://movietimes.com/celebrities/aileen-quinn|title=Aileen Quinn biography, photos, news, movies |website= MovieTimes.com |access-date=March 17, 2019}}</ref> Some of the school's campus was the site of the film version of ''Annie''.<ref name= catching /><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.app.com/story/news/history/erik-larsen/2014/12/16/daddy-warbucks-moved-monmouth/20506665/|title=When 'Daddy' Warbucks moved into Monmouth U|work=Asbury Park Press| access-date=October 17, 2018}}</ref> She was awarded an honorary degree from Monmouth University in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.monmouth.edu/news/|title=News |publisher= Monmouth University | first=Monmouth University 400 Cedar AvenueWest Long|last=BranchNJ07764732.571.3400| website= monmouth.edu |access-date=March 17, 2019}}</ref> Quinn was also a Spanish, drama, and dance teacher at [[Hudson Catholic Regional High School]] in [[Jersey City, New Jersey|Jersey City]], New Jersey.

She has her own band, Aileen Quinn and The Leapin' Lizards.<ref name= catching /><ref name= band>{{cite news| url= http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2016/sep/13/annie-star-aileen-quinn-new-band-leapin-lizards/ |title= 'Annie' star Aileen Quinn on new band The Leapin' Lizards | website= washingtontimes.com| date= September 13, 2016}}</ref> Quinn met her bandmates after a neighbor heard her singing in her apartment, then introduced Quinn to the other musicians.<ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.nevadaappeal.com/news/entertainment/aileen-quinn-and-the-leapin-lizards-bring-rockabilly-to-carson/ |title= Aileen Quinn and The Leapin' Lizards bring rockabilly to Carson | website= NevadaAppeal.com}}</ref> They released their first album, ''Spin Me'', in 2015.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://www.laweekly.com/music/red-aunts-say-goodbye-to-bassist-debi-martini-10139754 |title= Red Aunts Say Goodbye to Bassist Debi Martini | work= LAWeekly.com}}</ref> They released their second album, ''Lightning and Thunder'', in November 2019.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://knvc.org/news/aileen-quinn-the-leapin-lizards-and-a-serious-giant-jenga-match-a-photo-essay/ |title= Aileen Quinn & The Leapin' Lizards and a serious giant Jenga match – a photo essay | website= [[KNVC]].org}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Quinn is fluent in Spanish. She spent six months with a family in [[Chile]] as part of an [[exchange program]], and considers it one of her life's most rewarding experiences.{{citation needed|date= December 2020}} As of 2012, she had no children and was an aunt and [[Godparent|godmother]].<ref name= catching />
