'''D-Block Europe''', often abbreviated to '''DBE''', is a British [[hip hop collective]] consisting of Adam Nathaniel "Young Adz" Williams, Ricky Earl "Dirtbike LB" Banton and Lil Pino from [[Lewisham]], London.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gigsandtours.com/tour/d-block-europe|title=D-Block Europe|date=24 January 2019|website=Gigs and Tours|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190604165641/https://www.gigsandtours.com/tour/d-block-europe/|archive-date=4 June 2019|access-date=4 April 2019}}</ref> Their name is a reference to [[The Lox]] (also known as D-Block). They started to put out tracks on their own label in the beginning of 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/articles/4yhn1JLNWGX2QnTCv4b4RKR/d-block-europe|title=BBC - D Block Europe|website=BBC|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406024937/https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/articles/4yhn1JLNWGX2QnTCv4b4RKR/d-block-europe|archive-date=6 April 2019|access-date=4 April 2019}}</ref> The duo rose to fame in 2017 after releasing numerous singles including "Large Amounts". Their style encompasses elements of [[British hip hop]] and [[trap music|trap]], as well as use of [[Auto-Tune]].

D-Block Europe's debut mixtape ''Any Minute Now'', a collaborative project with British rapper from London [[Yxng Bane]], was released in 2018 and charted at number 14 on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. Their debut solo mixtape, ''Home Alone'', was released on 15 February 2019 and peaked at number 6 on the UK Albums Chart followed by their second mixtape of 2019, ''PTSD'', which debuted at number 4 on the UK Albums Chart. Their debut album ''[[The Blue Print: Us vs. Them]]'' reached number two on the UK Albums Chart on 16 October 2020. Before their Central Cee collaboration<ref>https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/61514/d-block-europe-ft-central-cee/</ref> debuted inside the Top 10 on 26 November 2021,<ref>https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/</ref> D-Block Europe were the act with the [[UK Singles Chart records and statistics|most hits without a Top 10]] managing to chart 29 singles
<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/54297/d-block-europe/|title = D-Block Europe &#124; full Official Chart History &#124; Official Charts Company|website = [[OfficialCharts.com]]}}</ref> into the UK Top 75 with none of them reaching the Top 10.
