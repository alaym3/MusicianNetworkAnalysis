'''Asan''' ({{IPA-ko|a.san}}) is a [[Administrative divisions of South Korea|city]] in [[South Chungcheong]] Province, [[South Korea]]. It borders the [[Seoul Capital Area]] to the north. Asan has a population of approximately 300,000.

Asan is known for its many hot springs and is a city of spas.

Asan has grown into the neighboring village, [[Onyang-dong]], which is also known for its hot springs.

==Transportation==

The city of Asan shares a station for the [[KTX]] high speed trains with the directly adjacent city of [[Cheonan]], which is thusly named [[Cheonan-Asan Station]]. It takes about 30&nbsp;minutes to travel from Asan to Seoul by the KTX train. It can be reached within 2 hours from [[Incheon International Airport]] by car. [[Seoul Metropolitan Subway]] extended one of its lines to service Asan on 15 December 2008. Two major highways, the [[Gyeongbu Expressway|Seoul-Busan]] and [[Seohaean Expressway|West Coast expressways]], also pass through Asan city.

==Industry and commerce==

Companies like [[Hyundai Motor Company|Hyundai Motor]], [[Samsung LED|Samsung LCD]], and [[Samsung Electronics|Samsung Electronic]] have factories in Asan. A total of 14 industrial complexes are currently occupied by auto parts, electronic parts and other factories.

The [[Port of Pyeongtaek-Dangjin|Port of Pyeongtaek]], closest to the [[east China]] coast among Korean ports, is nearby.

== Place worth visiting ==
'''Blue Crystal Village''': The Mediterranean village is located in ''Tangjeong-myeon, Asan-si, Chungcheongnam-do.'' This place, which was first a vineyard, was created as a new village with the construction of an industrial complex. ==Modern history==
* In 1983, an alteration of townships (ri) and towns (myeon) was made.
* In 1986, [[Onyang-eup|Onyang eup]] (town) of Asan was separated and the independent city of Onyang was created.<ref>Provisional changes including Guri city and 11 cities and counties and permission of modification naming Naju.(1985.12.28.) from [[Constitution of the Republic of Korea]]</ref>
* In 1995, the City of Onyang and Asan County were combined into the City of Asan.

==Notable people==
Famous people associated with Asan include the late [[Yun Posun]], former [[President of South Korea]].

Admiral [[Yi Sun-sin]], who defeated the [[Naval history of Japan|Japanese navy]] in the 16th century, lived for some time in Asan.{{Citation needed|date=October 2008}} There is a miniature replica of a [[turtle ship]] made famous by Admiral Yi on the outskirts of town. The [[Yi Sun-sin Stadium|local football stadium]] is named after him.

[[Virginia Tech shooting|Mass murderer]] [[Seung-Hui Cho]] was born in Asan.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cnews041.com/sub_read.html?uid=728 |title=Archived copy |access-date=9 April 2020 |archive-date=15 August 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140815102415/http://www.cnews041.com/sub_read.html?uid=728 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

[[North Korean defectors|North Korean defector]] [[Yeonmi Park]] lived in Asan after fleeing [[North Korea]] and [[Human trafficking in China|Chinese human traffickers]] through [[Mongolia]].<ref>{{Cite book|last=Park|first=Yeonmi|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/921419691|title=In order to live : a North Korean girl's journey to freedom|date=2015|others=Maryanne Vollers|isbn=978-1-59420-679-5|location=New York|oclc=921419691}}</ref>
