'''Webster Gradney, Jr.''' (born September 6, 1985)<ref name="hiphopdx.com">{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/interviews/id.428/title.webbie-louisiana-savagery|title=Webbie: Louisiana Savagery|date=June 20, 2005|access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref> who performs under the [[mononym]] '''Webbie''', is an American rapper from [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana]]. He's been signed to the independent [[Trill Entertainment]] label since 2003.<ref name="all music">{{cite web|last=Jeffries|first=David|title=Webbie – Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p724158/biography|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=Allmusic|year=2005|access-date=April 19, 2008}}</ref> In 2005 he came into the hip hop scene with "Gimme That" featuring [[Bun B]]. His songs "Bad Bitch" and "Swerve" were featured on ''[[Gangsta Musik]]'', his 2003 group album with Lil Boosie, and in the 2005 movie ''[[Hustle & Flow]]''. Webbie's second album, ''[[Savage Life 2]]'', was released in early 2008 with the hit single "[[Independent (Webbie song)|Independent]]" featuring [[Lil Boosie]] and [[Lil Phat]].

== Early life ==
Webbie was born in [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana]]. His mother died when he was nine years old so his parental care was split between his father and grandmother.<ref name="yahoo">{{cite web|title=Webbie – Biography|url=http://music.yahoo.com/ar-17973444-bio--Webbie|work=Yahoo! Music|publisher=Atlantic Records|year=2005|access-date=April 19, 2008}}</ref> Ever since he was five years old, he had been writing rhymes, and throughout his childhood he had become an avid fan of [[hardcore rap]] artists including Southern hip hop performers [[Master P]], [[Eightball & MJG]], the [[Geto Boys]], [[UGK]] Along With DjTrillReed (Second Cousin).
.

== Music career ==
Webbie first appeared on Lil Boosie's album ''For My Thugz ''on the track "Gotta Get It" in 2001. He and [[Lil Boosie]] released ''[[Ghetto Stories (Lil Boosie and Webbie album)|Ghetto Stories]]'' in 2003 and ''[[Gangsta Musik]]'' in 2004. As Webbie's solo track "[[Give Me That]]" featuring [[Bun B]] was frequently featured in mixtapes, as a label [[Trill Entertainment]] was growing in popularity & was signed for a distribution deal at [[Asylum Records]] along with (Webbie).
After he was signed there, his major-label debut ''[[Savage Life]]'' was released in 2005,<ref name="all music" /> debuting at number eight on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref>{{cite news|last=Harris|first=Chris|title=Janet Jackson Teaches Jack Johnson Discipline, Claims Sixth #1 Debut|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1582799/20080305/jackson_janet.jhtml|work=MTV News|date=March 5, 2008|access-date=April 19, 2008}}</ref>

Trill began a deal to sign Webbie to the subsidiary label [[Atlantic Records]] & was successful. Singles from his next album, ''[[Savage Life 2]]'', include "[[Independent (song)|Independent]]" featuring Boosie and [[Lil Phat]]. As of 2020, it is Webbie's most successful song to date, peaking at number nine on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and reaching the top of the [[Rap Songs]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/webbie/chart-history/RMS/song/536938|title = Webbie}}</ref>
Webbie released his third studio album ''[[Savage Life 3]]'' on November 15, 2011, under Trill Ent. The first single from the album was "What's Happenin'". The album has currently sold over 50,000 copies.

Webbie's fourth album ''Savage Life 4'' is the fourth in the ''Savage Life'' album series. The first single from the album "What I Do" was released on April 16, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/What-I-Do-Explicit/dp/B00CFK6TRQ/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1383229772&sr=8-1&keywords=webbie+what+i+do |title=What I Do [Explicit&#93;: Webbie: MP3 Downloads |access-date=October 31, 2013}}</ref> The album was released on November 19, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/savage-life-4-deluxe-edition/id728711796 |title=iTunes – Music – Savage Life 4 (Deluxe Edition) by Webbie |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=September 6, 1985 |access-date=October 31, 2013}}</ref>

== Acting career ==
He has made an independent biographical movie, ''[[Ghetto Stories (film)|Ghetto Stories]]'' in October 2010, with Lil Boosie, based on their lives and lyrical themes.<ref>{{cite web|title=Trill Fam – Survival of the Fittest|url=http://www.asylumrecords.com/new/bio.php?bio=30|publisher=Asylum Records|year=2007|access-date=April 19, 2008}} {{dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> He also appeared in the music film "Video Girl" which featured actress [[Meagan Good]].

== Controversy ==
On the October 14, 2011, episode of BET's ''[[106 & Park]]'', Webbie was banned from further appearances on the countdown show. Webbie appeared that day on the show as a guest judge for Freestyle Friday. The reason behind his ban was tied to allegations that he made sexually inappropriate comments to the show's hostess, Rocsi.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rocsi Speaks Out About Webbie Ban|url=http://theurbandaily.com/gossip-news/shamika-sanders/rocsi-finally-explains-why-lil-webbie-is-banned-from-106-park|date=November 29, 2011}}</ref>

On September 13, 2012, Webbie was arrested in Baton Rouge on suspicion of robbery and battery. He was accused of kicking and pushing a woman down the stairs before stealing $340 from her purse.<ref>{{cite web|title=Baton Rouge Rapper Charges Arrested on Suspicion of Robbery and Battery|url=http://www.411mania.com/music/news/254884/Baton-Rouge-Rapper-Charges-Arrested-on-Suspicion-of-Robbery-and-Battery.htm|access-date=September 18, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131111070656/http://www.411mania.com/music/news/254884/Baton-Rouge-Rapper-Charges-Arrested-on-Suspicion-of-Robbery-and-Battery.htm|archive-date=November 11, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>
