'''Paul Kalkbrenner''' ({{IPA-de|paʊ̯l ˈkalkbrɛnɐ}}) (born 11 June 1977<ref name=BerlinerMorgen>{{cite news|title=Paul Kalkbrenner, der Live-Act|url=http://www.morgenpost.de/kultur/article1284114/Paul-Kalkbrenner-der-Live-Act.html|newspaper=Berliner Morgenpost|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref>) is a German [[musician]], [[record producer|producer]] of [[electronic music]] and actor. Because he breaks down his tracks into elements that are reassembled onstage, Kalkbrenner is considered a live act, as opposed to a [[Disc jockey|DJ]].<ref name=Partysan /> He is most known for his single "[[Sky and Sand]]", which sold over 200,000 copies, went platinum, and was highly charted in countries such as Belgium and Germany. He is also known for portraying the main character Ickarus in the movie ''[[Berlin Calling]]'', written and directed by [[Hannes Stöhr]], which ran for several years at Kino Central in Berlin.

==Biography==
Paul Kalkbrenner grew up in Lichtenberg, Berlin,<ref name=Partysan>{{cite web|title=Interview with Paul Kalkbrenner|url=http://int.partysan.net/clublife/interview-with-paul-kalkbrenner/|website=Int.partysan.net|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> which was deep in the eastern sector of the city before the [[Berlin Wall]] came down. At the time of the [[Reunification of Germany]], Kalkbrenner was only thirteen years old. It was then that he began to play records to his peers at the youth clubs of Berlin, despite his 11 p.m. curfew set by his mother. He played together with his good friend Sascha Funke, who he met at his school and discovered that they both shared a common interest in [[techno]].<ref name=Equinox /> Greatly admiring the [[Underground Resistance (band)|Underground Resistance]] from [[Detroit]], both Kalkbrenner and Funke spent the years between 1992 and 1997 delving into record bins and familiarizing themselves with the best techno releases of the day.<ref name="Resident Advisor">{{cite magazine|last=Burns|first=Todd|title=Paul Kalkbrenner: The World Is Calling|url=http://www.residentadvisor.net/feature.aspx?1283|magazine=Resident Advisor|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> It was this and a radio show they listened to every Saturday (Rave Satellite) that led them to the techno scene. Meanwhile, at school, he learned to be a trumpet player and studied music theory.<ref name=Bowery>{{cite web|title=Paul Kalkbrenner|url=http://www.bowerypresents.com/event/385335-paul-kalkbrenner-new-york/|website=Bowerypresents.com|access-date=29 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203063501/http://www.bowerypresents.com/event/385335-paul-kalkbrenner-new-york/|archive-date=3 December 2013|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Kalkbrenner is a keen [[Association football|football]] fan and an avid supporter of [[FC Bayern Munich]].<ref name=Bayern>{{cite web|title=Kalkbrenner: 'I have a very good feeling' |url=https://fcbayern.com/de/news/2016/05/kalkbrenner-ich-habe-ein-sehr-gutes-gefuhl|website=fcbayern.com|access-date=23 January 2021}}</ref> After spending many long and sleepless nights on the dancefloors of [[E-Werk (Berlin)|E-Werk]], Planet, and Walfisch, the dream was born to make music himself. In 1995, at the age of eighteen, Kalkbrenner worked for two years as a cutter in the television industry to fund the equipment he bought for production.<ref name=PKBio>{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://paulkalkbrenner.net/press/|website=Paulkalkbrenner.net|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> During that time, Kalkbrenner and Funke lived together and began producing music on machines they both bought and borrowed. After getting into music production, it is then that Kalkbrenner decided he would like to only perform live with his own music.

==Career==
===BPitch Control===
Before [[Ellen Allien]] established [[BPitch Control]] as a [[record label]], she was throwing BPitch parties all around Berlin. It was at one of her parties that Kalkbrenner and Funke met Allien.<ref name="Resident Advisor" /> She came over to hear what they were working on, gave a few recommendations, and in 1999, Kalkbrenner released his first tracks as the Friedrichshain EP on BPitch Control under the name "Paul dB+." After releasing two albums, ''Superimpose'' (2001) and ''Zeit'' (2001), Kalkbrenner began to see album releases as his preferred format. His melodic sound, with its affinity for grand emotional gestures, obeyed the logic of the dancefloor, but his third album, ''Self'' (2004), introduced a narrative aesthetic. The track "Gebrünn Gebrünn", released one year later, became a crossover hit. Kalkbrenner released six albums and ten EPs with BPitch Control, until his amicable departure in 2008.

===''Berlin Calling''===
{{main|Berlin Calling}}
After his release ''Self'' (2004), Kalkbrenner was approached by Berlin-based film director and long time supporter of his work, [[Hannes Stöhr]], with the proposition of creating the soundtrack for Stöhr's movie about a Berlin DJ during the 2000s. In the course of their exchange, Stöhr had the realization that Kalkbrenner would be the perfect choice as the film's star. Kalkbrenner accepted the offer and they decided to title the film ''Berlin Calling''.<ref name="spiegel">{{cite web|last=Cadenbach|first=Christoph|title=Schizo in der Disko|work=[[Der Spiegel]]|date=2008-10-05|url=http://www.spiegel.de/kultur/kino/0,1518,581896,00.html|access-date=2009-07-24}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.screendaily.com/berlin-calling/4040187.article|title=Berlin Calling|website=Screendaily.com}}</ref> In late 2006, he moved to [[Aix-en-Provence]], South of France with Sascha Funke for six months to work on the soundtrack. The hit track off of the album, "Sky and Sand" was produced with the vocals of his own brother, [[Fritz Kalkbrenner]]. 2007 saw Kalkbrenner's return to Berlin, in order to film the movie. Although the film is fictitious, Kalkbrenner was able to relate to the film's main character, Ickarus who is mentally destroyed by the scene but still holds many amazing musical ideas inside his head. It is perhaps because of that correlation, that Kalkbrenner's first venture into acting was well executed and successful. Ickarus is also a representation of who Kalkbrenner did not want to become.<ref name=BC>{{cite web|title=Interview with Paul Kalkbrenner|url=http://www.berlin-calling.de/en/interview-paul-kalkbrenner|website=Berlin-calling.de|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> After the movie's release in October 2008, the album went platinum with over 200,000 and the song "Sky and Sand" topped charts for weeks.<ref name=PKBio /> The movie premiered at the [[Locarno International Film Festival]] in Switzerland and was well received, turning in a cult classic in Europe. It broke the record of longest run film in Germany, with over 145 weeks of continuous screenings at Kino Central in Berlin.<ref name=IbizaVoice /> In 2009, the movie was released on DVD.

===Paul Kalkbrenner Music===
[[File:Paul Kalkbrenner.tif|thumb|right|Paul Kalkbrenner in the midst of playing]]
In 2009, after receiving widespread acclaim for ''Berlin Calling – The Soundtrack'' (2008) album released on BPitch, Kalkbrenner decided that after ten years, it was time for him and the label to split ways. He believed that the label did not have what it took to support his artist growth, to propel him forward. Wanting to do his own thing, Kalkbrenner created [[Paul Kalkbrenner Musik]] as a platform for his own music.<ref name=Equinox>{{cite web|last=Keller|first=William|title=Interview Paul Kalkbrenner|url=http://www.equinoxmagazine.fr/interview-paul-kalkbrenner/|work=Equinox Magazine|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> Kalkbrenner set out on a sold-out, intensive, twelve-city tour in 2010 that was filmed with the help of the team that created ''Berlin Calling'', and released as ''Paul Kalkbrenner 2010 – A Live Documentary''. Since the label was begun, it has seen the release of two albums. ''Icke Wieder'' (3 June 2011) which was heavily supported by DJs like [[Sasha (DJ)|Sasha]], ranked number two in German music charts,<ref name=IbizaVoice /> and ultimately reached gold status with over 100,000 CDs sold.<ref name="trndmusik">{{cite web|title=Paul Kalkbrenner bringt neues Album "Icke Wieder" raus |website=Trndmusik.de |date=2011-04-12 |url=http://trndmusik.de/2011/04/paul-kalkbrenner-bringt-neues-album-icke-wieder-raus/ |access-date=2011-04-12 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110415072218/http://trndmusik.de/2011/04/paul-kalkbrenner-bringt-neues-album-icke-wieder-raus/ |archive-date=2011-04-15 }}</ref> The tour for the album brought together over 125,000 fans. That same summer, Kalkbrenner came to Ibiza for the first time to speak at the [[International Music Summit]] and play at one of Ibiza's renowned clubs, Amnesia.<ref name=IbizaVoice /> On 25 August 2012, Kalkbrenner married Romanian DJ/producer [[Simina Grigoriu]]. On 30 November 2012, he has released the album ''Guten Tag''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Paul Kalkbrenner|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/Paul%20Kalkbrenner|website=Discogs.com}}</ref> In February 2013, he began to tour to support the new album in Europe and the U.S.<ref name=vogue>{{cite magazine|last=Sagansky|first=Gillian|title=Berlin Powerhouse Producer Paul Kalkbrenner on His First Solo U.S. Tour and His New Video for "Since 77"|url=http://www.vogue.com/culture/article/berlin-powerhouse-producer-paul-kalkbrenner-on-his-first-solo-us-tour-and-his-new-video-for-since-77-music/#1|magazine=Vogue|access-date=5 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131209115822/http://www.vogue.com/culture/article/berlin-powerhouse-producer-paul-kalkbrenner-on-his-first-solo-us-tour-and-his-new-video-for-since-77-music/#1|archive-date=9 December 2013|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> ''Guten Tag'' has topped the charts in Switzerland.

====Florian Trilogy====
In 2015, he announced the release of the "Florian Trilogy" of three related works as a warmup for his planned album titled ''7'', to be released on August 7, 2015.<ref name="ew.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2015/07/17/paul-kalkbrenner-florian-feed-your-head|title=Paul Kalkbrenner chats about being the first to sample Jefferson Airplane|date=17 July 2015|website=Ew.com|access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref> The music video first episode "Cloud Rider" was released on May 18, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6j5t6WjtSfk|title=Paul Kalkbrenner – Cloud Rider (Official Video)|date=18 May 2015|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> The lyrics and vocals are performed by D Train with You're the One for Me.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QWmteg9guoo|title=D Train – You're the One for Me|date=2 September 2011|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> Episode 2, called "Mothertrucker", was released on Jun 12, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9B4HPRAIArU|title=Paul Kalkbrenner – Mothertrucker (Official Video)|date=12 June 2015|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> The third and final episode of the trilogy, "Feed your Head", was released on July 17, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rH9AriEezeI|title=Paul Kalkbrenner – Feed Your Head (Official Music Video)|date=17 July 2015|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> The lyrics for this song are from Jefferson Airplane with White Rabbit.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WANNqr-vcx0|title=Jefferson Airplane -White Rabbit-|date=9 November 2008|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> Paul explains in this <ref name="ew.com"/> article how the Florian episodes came to be. The episodes are set in America where techno was born. But it has been excluded for quite some time. The episodes explain how Florian eventually gets techno back to America, where it all started.

====''Back to the Future''====
In July 2016 Paul Kalkbrenner dropped a three-volume mix series that chronicled the arrival of techno in Berlin in the early 1990s. Entitled ''Back To the Future'' the project was part personal odyssey, part social history, documenting the birth of a musical moment that would forever change the landscape of Berlin, and contemporary dance music.<br/>
In producing the trilogy Kalkbrenner compiled a list of more than 5000 tunes from 1987–1993 on YouTube, taking the tracks off line and gradually reducing the list, then cutting and editing the chosen tracks, sometimes just snippets of a song, to fit the mixtapes. With over 65 tracks and three volumes, Kalkbrenner takes listeners back to the tunes he heard as a young boy listening to the East Berlin radio station [[DT64]].<br/>
Those tracks were made available for free on social media and topped over 1,5 Mio downloads, gaining him some renowned press echo.
''Rolling Stone'' wrote „Viral, techno history lesson",<ref>{{citation|periodical=Rolling Stone|title=Paul Kalkbrenner Talks His Viral Techno History Lesson|language=de|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/paul-kalkbrenner-talks-his-viral-techno-history-lesson-w432809}}</ref> Billboard magazin described ''BACK TO THE FUTURE'' as "work of historical excavation".<ref>{{citation|periodical=Billboard|title=Paul Kalkbrenner Embraces Nostalgia On ‘Back To The Future’ Mixtape|language=de|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7423989/paul-kalkbrenner-back-to-the-future-mixtape-dance}}</ref>

Starting in April 2017 he will perform a series of BACK TO THE FUTURE shows in the spring in rather smaller venues throughout Europe. These events will see Kalkbrenner return to DJing, which, though he has only played live since 1998, was how he performed as a teenager in Berlin youth clubs and in the early part of his career. The European tour was sold out within a week.

====''Parts of Life''====
On 18 May 2018 Kalkbrenner released a new album called ''Parts of Life'', that is his latest and 8th album overall. ''Parts of Life'' features 15 tracks named with the file numbers Kalkbrenner used during production. Its cover art is a painting from his uncle, Paul Eisel, depicting a collection of "unique personal objects". He revealed three tracks as a special premiere for launching his album (Part Three, Part Six and Part Eight).

===Production and live set-up===
Kalkbrenner uses [[software synthesizers]], sequencer ([[Ableton Live]]) in combination with [[MIDI controllers]], hardware [[synthesizers]] and [[drum machines]] for his live shows. It is a set up he feels comfortable with, and does not foresee changing.<ref name=soundwall>{{cite web|last=Ivic|first=Damir|title=PAUL KALKBRENNER: PERCHÉ DOVREI COMPLICARMI LA VITA DA SOLO?|url=http://www.soundwall.it/paul-kalkbrenner-perche-dovrei-complicarmi-la-vita-da-solo/|website=Soundwall.it|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> In the early days, for production, Kalkbrenner used a Roland sampler, [[Amiga 500]], [[Amiga 4000T|Amiga 4000 Turbo]], DAT, Roland S-750, and EFX to name a few.<ref name="Resident Advisor" /><ref name=IbizaVoice>{{cite web|last=Lavin|first=Polly|title=Catch up with Paul Kalkbrenner|url=http://www.ibiza-voice.com/story/news/3305|website=Ibiza-voice.com|access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> Since 2001, however, he sold all his equipment and only uses Ableton Live for music production.<ref name=IbizaVoice />

==Family connection==

In ihrer Musik zeigen sich die beiden Brüder auch als künstlerische Nachfahren ihres Großvaters. |author=Karim Saab |website=Maz-online.de|date=8 December 2014|access-date=15 March 2015}}</ref> and older brother of music producer [[Fritz Kalkbrenner]].
