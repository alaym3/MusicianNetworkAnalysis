'''Idris Vicuña''' (born May 28, 1990), known professionally as '''Eyedress''', is a Filipino singer-songwriter, rapper, and record producer.

== Early life ==
Eyedress was raised in an area in the Philippines which he described as "ghetto", and began playing the guitar at a young age. He and his family moved to [[Phoenix, Arizona|Phoenix]], [[Arizona]], where he was introduced to [[punk music]] through the "gangsters" and "anarchist types" in his neighborhood.<ref name="dazed">{{cite web |last1=Dabbay |first1=Kristine |title=24 hours with Eyedress |url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/19165/1/24-hours-with-eyedress |website=[[Dazed]] |access-date=February 19, 2021 |language=en |date=April 9, 2014}}</ref> At age 13, he and his family moved to [[California]] after his father, an animator, started working with [[20th Century Fox]]. While living in [[Orange County, California|Orange County]], California, he joined the [[crust punk]] band Liberal Underground as a bassist.<ref name="amsong">{{cite web |last1=Grunenberg |first1=Kira |title=Eyedress Uses Music to Bring His Unique Artistic Journey into View |url=https://americansongwriter.com/lets-skip-to-the-wedding-eyedress-album-interview/ |website=[[American Songwriter]] |access-date=February 19, 2021 |language=en |date=September 17, 2020}}</ref> He moved back to the Philippines at age 15 with his family. Before starting his career as a musician, he designed clothing for a brief period of time and, at age 20, he started composing music by making beats on his laptop and releasing them for free online.<ref name="guardian">{{cite web |last1=Lamont |first1=Tom |title=One to watch: Eyedress |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/jan/27/eyedress-one-to-watch-filipino-manila |website=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=February 17, 2021 |date=January 27, 2014}}</ref>

==Life and career==
After gaining a small following from making beats, Eyedress was introduced to Filipino singer Skint Eastwood through a promoter from Manila, and the two collaborated on the song "Biolumine". The song earned him attention from a French label which pushed for him to make an album, which then earned him a record deal with Abeano Records, a subsidiary of [[XL Recordings]].<ref name="guardian" /> On December 2, 2013, he released his debut [[extended play]], ''Supernatural'', which he recorded in his bedroom using [[FL Studio]].<ref name="guardiannewband">{{cite web |last1=Lester |first1=Paul |title=Eyedress (New band of the day No 1,632) |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/nov/04/eyedress-new-band?CMP=twt_gu |website=The Guardian |access-date=February 19, 2021 |language=en |date=November 4, 2013}}</ref><ref name="nme" />

Eyedress released his debut mixtape, ''Hearing Colors'', as a free [[Music download|digital download]] on April 11, 2014.<ref name="nmecolors" /> He released his second EP, ''Egyptian Night Club'', on [[SoundCloud]] on July 27, 2014.<ref name="factep">{{cite web |title=Stream a new EP by Eyedress, Egyptian Night Club |url=https://www.factmag.com/2014/07/27/stream-a-new-ep-by-eyedress-egyptian-night-club/ |website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]] |access-date=February 19, 2021 |language=en |date=July 27, 2014}}</ref> In September 2014, he released the single "Polo Tee" as a tribute to American record producer [[DJ Rashad]]. He released his first mixtape after leaving XL Recordings, ''Shapeshifter'', in 2015.

His four studio albums were released on [[Lex Records]]: ''Manila Ice'', on May 12, 2017;<ref name="fader">{{cite web|last=Seshadri |first=Pooja |url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/03/30/eyedress-manila-ice-interview |title=Eyedress Made An Album About Love And Survival In Manila |publisher=[[The Fader]] |date=March 30, 2017 |accessdate=March 6, 2020}}</ref> ''Sensitive G'', on November 16, 2018;<ref name="fadernofun">{{cite web |last1=Kochhar |first1=Nazuk |title=Eyedress is bleeding and fed up in "No Fun" |url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/10/26/eyedress-no-fun-video-tour-album-announce |website=The Fader |access-date=February 19, 2021 |language=en |date=October 26, 2018}}</ref> ''[[Let's Skip to the Wedding]]'' on August 7, 2020 and ''[[Mulholland Drive (album)| Mulholland Drive]]'' on August 27, 2021.

Two singles from ''Let's Skip to the Wedding'', "[[Jealous (Eyedress song)|Jealous]]" and "Romantic Lover", became viral hits on [[TikTok]] and "Jealous" was [[Music recording certification|certified]] Gold in Poland and the USA in August 2021.<ref>{{Cite web| url= https://www.thefader.com/2021/06/03/eyedress-spit-on-your-grave-something-about-you-featuring-dent-may| publisher= [[The Fader]] | title= Eyedress shares two new songs "Spit on Your Grave" and "Something About You" featuring Dent May | date=3 June 2021}}</ref><ref name="FIMI-singles">{{Cite certification| region=Poland|artist=Eyedress |title=Jealous|award=Gold|type=single|certyear=2021|access-date=16 August 2021}}</ref><ref name="RIAA"/>
