'''Anaal Nathrakh''' are a British [[extreme metal]] band formed in 1999 in [[Birmingham]], England by multi-instrumentalist [[Mick Kenney]] and vocalist [[Dave Hunt (musician)|Dave Hunt]].<ref name="vice.com" /> They are currently signed to [[Metal Blade Records]]. The band's name is [[Irish language|Irish]] for ''snake's breath'' ([[wikt:anál|anál]] [[wikt:nathair|nathrach]]) and was taken from [[Merlin]]'s Charm of Making in [[John Boorman]]'s 1981 film ''[[Excalibur (film)|Excalibur]]''.<ref name="auto"/> The band recorded two demos in 1999, which were later released as an album.<ref name="auto1"/>

The band has released eleven studio albums, one EP, and one compilation album. The band has been praised for mixing elements of [[grindcore]], [[black metal]], [[death metal]], [[industrial music|industrial]], and other genres. 

==History==
[[File:Anaal Nathrakh, Mick „Irrumator“ Kenney at Party.San Metal Open Air 2013.jpg|thumb|left|[[Mick Kenney]] playing for the band in 2013.]]

The band took their founding cues from Norwegian black metal bands such as [[Mayhem (band)|Mayhem]], [[Burzum]], and [[Darkthrone]]. The duo recorded two demos before delivering their first full album, ''[[The Codex Necro]]'', in 2001,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/anaal-nathrakh-mn0000750857/biography|title=Anaal Nathrakh - Biography & History - AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=18 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180819010905/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/anaal-nathrakh-mn0000750857/biography|archive-date=19 August 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> that, according to ''[[The A.V. Club]]'', "set a standard of [[blackened death metal|blackened death]] that still hasn’t been equaled".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.avclub.com/the-decade-s-best-metal-1798220832|title=The decade's best metal|first=Leonard|last=Pierce|access-date=19 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180819051246/https://music.avclub.com/the-decade-s-best-metal-1798220832|archive-date=19 August 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> 
In 2004, Anaal Nathrakh released their second full-length album, ''[[Domine Non Es Dignus]]'', on the French label [[Season of Mist]].<ref name="auto2">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/domine-non-es-dignus-mw0000262073|title=Domine Non Es Dignus - Anaal Nathrakh - Songs, Reviews, Credits - AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=18 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180819011210/https://www.allmusic.com/album/domine-non-es-dignus-mw0000262073|archive-date=19 August 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2006, the group's third album, ''[[Eschaton (album)|Eschaton]]'' (commonly referred to as the "[[end of the world (civilization)|end of the world]]" or "[[end time]]s"),<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/religion/religions/christianity/beliefs/endtimes_1.shtml |title=BBC - Religions - Christianity: End Times |publisher=[[BBC Online]] |date=2011-07-19 |access-date=2017-11-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180216195359/http://www.bbc.co.uk/religion/religions/christianity/beliefs/endtimes_1.shtml |archive-date=16 February 2018 |url-status=live}}</ref> was released.<ref name="auto3">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/eschaton-mw0000736714|title=Eschaton - Anaal Nathrakh - Songs, Reviews, Credits - AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=18 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180819011217/https://www.allmusic.com/album/eschaton-mw0000736714|archive-date=19 August 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> The album featured guest appearances by [[Shane Embury]] of [[Napalm Death]]<ref name="auto3"/> and [[Attila Csihar]] of Mayhem.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/anaal-nathrakh-hard-at-work-on-new-material/|title=ANAAL NATHRAKH Hard at Work on New Material|date=18 June 2007|access-date=19 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180819182323/http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/anaal-nathrakh-hard-at-work-on-new-material/|archive-date=19 August 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> The album ''[[Hell Is Empty and All the Devils Are Here]]'' was released through the band's new record label, FETO Records, in October 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/anaal-nathrakh-new-song-posted-online/|title=ANAAL NATHRAKH: New Song Posted Online|date=14 September 2007|access-date=19 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180819182327/https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/anaal-nathrakh-new-song-posted-online/|archive-date=19 August 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> The album features guest appearances from Joe Horvath of [[Circle of Dead Children]].<ref name="Allmusic">{{cite web |last=Freeman |first=Phil |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/hell-is-empty-and-all-the-devils-are-here-mw0000582701 |title=Hell Is Empty, and All the Devils Are Here |publisher=Allmusic |access-date=19 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180819011214/https://www.allmusic.com/album/hell-is-empty-and-all-the-devils-are-here-mw0000582701 |archive-date=19 August 2018 |url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2009, Anaal Nathrakh announced that they signed to record label [[Candlelight Records]] for their release of ''[[In the Constellation of the Black Widow]]''.<ref name="archive.org">{{cite web|url=http://heavymetal.about.com/od/a/fr/anaalnathrakh-intheconstellationoftheblackwidow.htm|title=Anaal Nathrakh in the Constellation of the Black Widow Review - Review of in the Constellation of the Black Widow by Anaal Nathrakh|date=14 March 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120314035130/http://heavymetal.about.com/od/a/fr/anaalnathrakh-intheconstellationoftheblackwidow.htm|archive-date=14 March 2012}}</ref> Commenting on the signing, V.I.T.R.I.O.L. stated, "Candlelight have shown a strong belief in Anaal Nathrakh and looking at some of the acts on their roster both past and present it seems they are capable of backing up their good intentions. With their help we hope we can finally get the planet cracked in half", and "''Constellation'' will be faster, more insidious, more ominous, more musically dexterous and wilder than ever before. The album will feature a curve ball guest appearance from Zeitgeist Memento of Mexican extremists Repvblika."<ref>[http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=115097 "Anaal Nathrakh Signs With Candlelight Records"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090614222648/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=115097 |date=14 June 2009 }}. Blabbermouth.net. 2009-02-25. Retrieved 2011-10-13.</ref>

Anaal Nathrakh released their sixth full-length album, ''[[Passion (Anaal Nathrakh album)|Passion]]'', in 2011,<ref>[http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=154418 "Anaal Nathrakh: New Song Available For Streaming"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110304223144/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=154418 |date=4 March 2011 }}. Blabbermouth.net. 2011-02-23. Retrieved 2011-10-13.</ref> and their seventh, ''[[Vanitas (Anaal Nathrakh album)|Vanitas]]'', in 2012. The band finished recording for their eighth full-length album, ''[[Desideratum (Anaal Nathrakh album)|Desideratum]]'', in February 2014, and released the album on 28 October 2014.<ref name=mstorm>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalstorm.net/pub/review.php?review_id=12949|title=Anaal Nathrakh - Desideratum (staff review) - Metal Storm|publisher=[[Metal Storm (webzine)|Metal Storm]]|date=24 October 2014|access-date=1 September 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170708152848/http://metalstorm.net/pub/review.php?review_id=12949|archive-date=8 July 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> ''[[Desideratum (Anaal Nathrakh album)|Desideratum]]'' was the band's first album release since signing to [[Metal Blade Records]] in June of the same year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/anaal-nathrakh-signs-to-metal-blade-records/|title=Anaal Nathrakh Signs To Metal Blade Records|date=24 June 2014|access-date=24 June 2014|publisher=[[Blabbermouth.net]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140702055741/http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/anaal-nathrakh-signs-to-metal-blade-records/|archive-date=2 July 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

The band's ninth album, ''[[The Whole of the Law]]'', was released on 28 October 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/anaal-nathrakh-to-release-the-whole-of-the-law-album-in-october/|title=ANAAL NATHRAKH To Release 'The Whole of the Law' Album in October|date=18 August 2016}}</ref> On 20 May 2018, it was revealed that they were going to release their tenth studio album, titled ''[[A New Kind of Horror]]''. The album was released on 28 September 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2018/07/18/anaal-nathrakhs-new-track-takes-the-band-forward/|title=Anaal Nathrakh's New Track Takes the Band "Forward!" - MetalSucks|date=18 July 2018|access-date=18 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180721014459/http://www.metalsucks.net/2018/07/18/anaal-nathrakhs-new-track-takes-the-band-forward/|archive-date=21 July 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

In early 2020, the band recorded their eleventh album, ''Endarkenment''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mickkenney.com/discography|title=Mick Kenney's official homepage - discography|date=9 June 2020|access-date=9 June 2020}}</ref> The album was released on 2 October 2020.<ref name="Metal Blade - Anaal Nathrakh">{{cite web|url=https://www.metalblade.com/anaalnathrakh/|title=Anaal Nathrakh|website=Metal Blade Records|access-date=16 August 2020}}</ref><ref name="Dead Press Aug 2020">{{cite web |url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-anaal-nathrakh-detail-eleventh-album-endarkenment/ |title=NEWS: Anaal Nathrakh detail eleventh album, 'Endarkenment'! |last=Morgan |first=Tom |date=2020-08-08 |website=DEAD PRESS! |access-date=2020-08-16 |language=en-UK}}</ref> ''[[Metal Hammer]]'' named it as the 31st best metal album of 2020.<ref name="MHtop5020">{{cite web |title=The 50 best metal albums of 2020 |url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/the-50-best-metal-albums-of-2020/2 |website=[[Metal Hammer]] |publisher=[[Future plc]] |access-date=March 12, 2021 |date=January 8, 2020}}</ref>
