'''Brendan Patrick Rice'''<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/writer/808309342/RICE%20BRENDAN%20PATRICK |title=ASCAP Ace Search |website=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP]] |access-date=March 15, 2020 |author=Rice, Brendan Patrick}}</ref> (born March 11, 1997), better known by his stage name '''Gus Dapperton''', is an American singer and songwriter from [[Warwick, New York]].

==History==
Dapperton has received particular attention for his fashion style, consisting at some points of a green [[bowl cut]], noticeable [[jewelry]], [[eyeliner]], brightly colored clothing, and thick-rimmed glasses.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/gus-dapperton-up-and-coming-artist-interview-music-videos-personal-style|title=Up-and-Coming Artist Gus Dapperton On Music Videos, His Personal Style, and His New EP}}</ref><ref name="vogue">{{cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/gus-dapperton-style-tour-photo-diary-global-100-yellow-and-such-you-think-youre-a-comic|title=Indie Pop Phenomenon Gus Dapperton Breaks Down His Quirky, Fashion-Filled Tour Wardrobe|publisher=Vogue}}</ref>

Dapperton's song "Of Lacking Spectacle" was featured on the Netflix series ''[[13 Reasons Why]]'' and appears on the soundtrack album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07CXQZF4S/ref=dm_ws_tlw_trk18|title=Of Lacking Spectacle|via=Amazon}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=November 2021}}

==Career==
{{expand section|date=July 2020}}

===2016–17: "Moodna, Once With Grace" and ''Yellow and Such''===
Gus Dapperton released his debut single, "Moodna, Once With Grace", in 2016. In 2017, Dapperton released his debut EP, ''Yellow and Such.''

===2018: ''You Think You're A Comic!''===
In 2018, Dapperton released his second EP, ''You Think You're A Comic!''.<ref name="MOWD"/>

===2019: ''Where Polly People Go to Read''===
In 2019, Dapperton released his debut album, ''[[Where Polly People Go to Read]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/gus-dapperton-where-polly-people-go-to-read/ |title=Gus Dapperton: Where Polly People Go to Read Album Review |work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |date=April 18, 2019 |access-date=July 2, 2020 |author=Cooper, Duncan}}</ref> The album received positive reviews from critics.

===2020&ndash;present: ''Orca''===
Dapperton's second studio album, ''Orca'', was released on September 18, 2020. He released four singles, "First Aid", "Post Humorous", "Medicine", and "Bluebird", prior to the album's release. This album features only one artist other than Dapperton, Filipino Australian artist [[Chela (singer)|Chela]] on the track "My Say So", during the bridge and background vocals.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Staff|first=Ethan Waters {{!}} Senior|date=2020-09-28|title=Gus Dapperton opens up on ‘Orca’|url=https://www.dailycal.org/2020/09/28/gus-dapperton-opens-up-on-orca/|access-date=2021-03-27|website=The Daily Californian|language=en-US}}</ref>

On April 7, 2021, Dapperton released a remix version of "Palms" as the album fifth single, featuring American rapper [[Channel Tres]]. The remix was included on the deluxe version of the album which was released on June 11, 2021. The deluxe has three new songs: "Sober Up", "Flatline" and "Steady", and the former was released as a single.<ref>{{Cite web|last=White|first=Caitlin|date=2021-06-11|title=Gus Dapperton Released A Deluxe Version Of ‘Orca’ With Several New Tracks And Shared His 2021 Tour Schedule|url=https://uproxx.com/indie/gus-dapperton-sober-up-orca-deluxe-2021-tour-dates/|access-date=2021-06-18|website=[[Uproxx]]}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Dapperton attended [[Drexel University]], but took a semester off to go on tour in support of his 2018 EP ''You Think You're a Comic!''.<ref name="Chic">{{cite web |url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-ott-gus-dapperton-0105-story.html |title=Gus Dapperton is more than a bowl cut and a few tracks |work=[[Chicago Tribune]] |date=January 4, 2018 |access-date=July 2, 2020 |author=Stewart, Allison}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/10/05/style/gus-dapperton-music.html |title=Indie pop artist Gus Dapperton taps into '90s style and sound |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=October 5, 2020 |access-date=July 2, 2020 |author=Dwyer, Kate}}</ref>
