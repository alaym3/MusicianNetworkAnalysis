'''Danielle Balbuena'''<ref name=Kellman>{{cite web|last=Kellman|first=Andy|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/070-shake-mn0003499511/biography|title=070 Shake {{pipe}} Biography & History|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> (born June 13, 1997<ref name="NJ" />), better known by her stage name '''070 Shake''', is an American [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] recording artist.<ref name=Machin2018>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/events/sxsw/8238823/sxsw-lineup-full-artists-conference-2018-festival|title=SXSW Announces Full 2018 Artist Lineup, Conference Details & More|last=Machin|first=Jennifer|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 8, 2018|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> 070 Shake is a part of the musical collective 070, who have released a mixtape together titled ''The 070 Project: Chapter 1'' (2016). Shake released her debut [[extended play]], ''[[Glitter (070 Shake album)|Glitter]]'', in March 2018.

In 2018, 070 Shake gained attention for her guest appearances and involvement with [[Kanye West]]'s production work in [[Wyoming]], featuring on songs "[[Ghost Town (Kanye West song)|Ghost Town]]" and "[[Violent Crimes (song)|Violent Crimes]]" from West's album ''[[Ye (album)|Ye]]'', as well as appearing on [[Pusha T]]'s ''[[Daytona (album)|Daytona]]'' and [[Nas]]' ''[[Nasir (album)|Nasir]]''. Shake released her debut studio album ''[[Modus Vivendi (070 Shake album)|Modus Vivendi]]'' on January 17, 2020.

==Career==
===2015–2016: Early years===
070 Shake was born in [[North Bergen, New Jersey]] and is of [[List of people from the Dominican Republic|Dominican]] descent. Balbuena attended [[North Bergen High School]], where she played on the school's [[basketball]] team.<ref name="NJ">Kuperinsky, Amy. [https://www.nj.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2018/06/070_shake_north_bergen_nj_kanye_west.html "070 Shake, breakout star from Kanye West album, comes home to N.J. for victory lap"], NJ Advance Media for [[NJ.com]], June 20, 2018, updated January 30, 2019. Accessed January 15, 2020. "Shake attended North Bergen High School not long before she was signed to Kanye West's record label, G.O.O.D. (Getting Out Our Dreams) Music, in 2016. In 2015, she was still a shooting guard on the Bruins girls basketball team."</ref> Prior to her music career, Shake wrote poetry.<ref name=Street2018/> Shake began her music career in late 2015, recording the songs "Proud" and "Swervin".<ref name=Street2018>{{cite web|last=Street|first=Mikelle|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8094126/rapper-070-shake-talks-kompetition-collective-ep-glitter-debuts-song-inspired-lil-peep|title=Rapper 070 Shake Talks Kompetition Collective EP 'Glitter' & Debuts Song Inspired by Lil Peep's Death|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 13, 2018|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> Shake's stage name is derived from her affiliation with the 070 music collective; 070 coming from [[New Jersey]]'s 070 [[ZIP Code]]s. By 2018, the 070 crew was noted to have 11 members including artists and producers.<ref name=Street2018/>

By 2016, Shake was earning hundreds of thousands of streams on [[SoundCloud]].<ref name=Isom2016>{{cite web|last=Isom|first=Eric|url=https://pigeonsandplanes.com/news/2016/07/070-shake-good-music|title=Is 070 Shake The Newest G.O.O.D. Music Signee?|work=[[Complex (magazine)#Complex|Pigeons & Planes]]|date=July 1, 2016|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> Soon after posting several songs on SoundCloud, Shake attracted the attention of [[Miami]]-based promoter and social media personality [[YesJulz]].<ref name=Burford2018/> After hearing "Proud" specifically, [[YesJulz]] reached out to Shake on [[Twitter]], quickly signing on to be her manager.<ref name=Burford2018/> Shake broke through with "Trust Nobody", released early in that year.<ref name=Kellman/> In August, Shake collaborated with fellow 070 members Ralphy River, Hack, and Tree on the single "Honey".<ref name=Ortiz2016>{{cite web|last=Ortiz|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2016/08/shake-070-crew-honey-premiere|title=Premiere: Shake and Her 070 Crew Drop "Honey"|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=August 15, 2016|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref><ref name=Braboy2016>{{cite web|last=Braboy|first=Mark|url=https://www.vibe.com/2016/08/shake-honey-070-song/|title=Shake & 070 Crew Dropped Some Organic "Honey"|work=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]|date=August 16, 2016|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> The song featured production from frequent collaborators The Kompetition,<ref name=Braboy2016/> a three-person production group.<ref name=Street2018/> "Honey" simultaneously premiered on ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' and [[Dash Radio#Channels|1 AM Radio]] on [[Dash Radio|Dash]].<ref name=Ortiz2016/> ''[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]'' commented that the track "[sounded] tailor made to rock on dance floors everywhere."<ref name=Braboy2016/>

Also in 2016, Shake signed with [[Kanye West]]'s [[GOOD Music]] label.<ref name=Street2018/> GOOD Music found out about Shake through Julz, as she played some of Shake's music at a [[Adidas Yeezy|Yeezy]] fitting, interesting the label's president, [[Pusha T]].<ref name=Burford2018/> After signing Shake, the label picked up her "Trust Nobody" track and re-released it in September.<ref name=Kellman/> Shake spent October and November opening up for English rock band [[The 1975]] during the American leg of their 2016 tour.<ref name=Kellman/> On December 8, 070 released their first mixtape, ''The 070 Project: Chapter 1''.<ref name=Burford2018/><ref>{{cite web|last=Mench|first=Chris|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2016/12/shake-070-new-mixtape-stream-the-070-project-chapter-1|title=Stream Shake and Her 070 Crew's 'The 070 Project: Chapter 1'|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=December 8, 2016|access-date=June 5, 2018}}</ref>

===2017–present: ''Glitter'', collaborations with Kanye West and ''Modus Vivendi''===
Shake modeled for Gypsy Sport's Fall 2017 runway show.<ref name=Bernard2017/> She also went on her first headlining tour in April.<ref>{{cite web|last=Madden|first=Sidney|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/02/070-shake-route-13-tour/|title=070 Shake Is Going on Tour|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|date=February 27, 2017|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> In a 2017 interview with [[Paper (magazine)|''Paper'']], Shake mentioned her ''Yellow Girl'' EP would release later that year.<ref name=Hazlehurst2017>{{cite web|last=Hazlehurst|first=Beatrice|url=http://www.papermag.com/beautiful-people-070-shake-is-the-change-she-wants-to-see-2487655696.html|title=Beautiful People: 070 Shake is the Change She Wants to See|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|date=September 21, 2017|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> However, the project was moved back to release after ''[[Glitter (070 Shake EP)|Glitter]]'', another collaboration with The Kompetition.<ref name=Street2018/>

Her solo debut, the 6-track ''Glitter'' EP was released on March 23, 2018,<ref name=Ch2018/> although it was originally slated for a January 26 release and to include 12 tracks.<ref name=Street2018/> At the time of ''Glitter''{{'}}s release, media outlets noted that her lyrical content concentrated on her self-esteem issues, drug use, and sexuality.<ref name=Fresh2018>{{cite web|last=Fresh|first=Mikey|url=https://www.vibe.com/2018/03/070-shake-glitter-ep-good-music/|title=G.O.O.D. Music's 070 Shake Debuts 'Glitter' EP|work=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]|date=March 26, 2018|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref><ref name=Ch2018>{{cite web|last=Ch|first=Devin|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/good-musics-070-shake-glitter-ep-hits-on-a-personal-level-new-mixtape.117926.html|title=G.O.O.D. Music's 070 Shake "Glitter" EP Hits On A Personal Level|work=HotNewHipHop|date=March 23, 2018|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> Shake described ''Glitter'' as "about being in a dark place and finding yourself and figuring it out. It's about being in the lowest of lows type of shit."<ref name=Street2018/> Shortly prior to ''Glitter''{{'}}s release, Shake performed at the [[South by Southwest|SXSW]] music festival.<ref name=Machin2018/>

In May, Shake was featured on "Santeria", a track from [[Pusha T]]'s album ''[[Daytona (album)|Daytona]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lamarre|first=Carl|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8457860/pusha-t-interview-daytona-album-kanye-west|title=Pusha T on How 'Daytona' Was 'Therapy' For Kanye & the Importance of Writing Your Own Raps|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=May 25, 2018|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> Shake also had featured vocals on "[[Ghost Town (Kanye West song)|Ghost Town]]" and "[[Violent Crimes (song)|Violent Crimes]]" from [[Kanye West]]'s album ''[[Ye (album)|Ye]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Renshaw|first=David|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/06/01/kanye-west-ye-album-credits|title=Here are the full album credits for Kanye West's ''ye''|work=[[The Fader]]|date=June 1, 2018|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref>

Michael Sapanora praised the two tracks as "standout cuts" on the album,<ref name=Sapanora2018b>{{cite web|last=Sapanora|first=Michael|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8459291/070-shake-kanye-west-ghost-town-ye|title=070 Shake Says Kanye West Finished 'Ghost Town' on 'Ye' Release Day|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=June 4, 2018|access-date=June 5, 2018}}</ref> and ranked "Ghost Town" specifically as the album's best song.<ref name=Sapanora2018a>{{cite web|last=Saponara|first=Michael|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8458820/kanye-west-ye-every-song-ranked|title=Ranking Every Song From Kanye West's 'Ye' Album: Critic's Picks|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=June 1, 2018|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> ''HotNewHipHop'' praised Shake's performance on "Ghost Town" as the highlight of the song, and called the track the climax of the album.<ref>{{cite web|last=Parizot|first=Matthew|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kanye-west-grabs-kid-cudi-john-legend-and-070-shake-for-the-massive-ghost-town-new-song.1978873.html|title=Kanye West Grabs Kid Cudi, John Legend, & 070 Shake For The Massive "Ghost Town"|work=HotNewHipHop|date=June 2, 2018|access-date=June 5, 2018}}</ref> Shake told ''[[Complex (magazine)#Complex|Pigeons & Planes]]'' that "Ghost Town" was nearly left off ''Ye'', as the track was finished on the same day of the album's release.<ref name=Corrigan2018>{{cite web|last=Corrigan|first=Graham|url=https://pigeonsandplanes.com/interviews/2018/06/070-shake-interview-kanye-west-ye-ghost-town|title=An Interview With 070 Shake, the Breakout Star of Kanye West's 'ye'|work=[[Complex (magazine)#Complex|Pigeons & Planes]]|date=June 4, 2018|access-date=June 5, 2018}}</ref> "Ghost Town" also featured vocals from [[Kid Cudi]], another inspiration of Shake's; she shared with ''Pigeons & Planes'', "All my life I grew up listening to Kanye West and Kid Cudi. I've cried to their music. They've definitely changed my life, and saved me from a lot of stuff."<ref name=Corrigan2018/> Shake's recording experience with West in [[Wyoming]] for his ''Ye'' album impacted her thoughts about ''Yellow Girl''; she stated "I don't know if it's going to be called ''Yellow Girl''. I know the songs are definitely changing. The experience I had in Wyoming makes me want to make music on a different level."<ref name=Corrigan2018/>

In 2019, she featured on DJ Khaled's album ''Father of Asahd'' alongside Buju Banton, Sizzla and Mavado on the song "Holy Mountain".

Shake released her debut album, ''[[Modus Vivendi (070 Shake album)|Modus Vivendi]],'' on January 17, 2020.<ref>{{Citation|title=Modus Vivendi|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/76R1YWLU02Q79Xa6Ri8eEA|language=en|access-date=January 30, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Prominently referenced in her music, Shake uses female pronouns when describing lovers.<ref>{{cite web|last=Monroe|first=Ian David|url=https://vmagazine.com/article/10-best-queer-songs-queer-artists-2016/|title=2016 Was a Strong Year for Queer Representation in Music|work=[[V (American magazine)|V]]|date=December 15, 2016|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> A 2017 [[Vogue (magazine)|''Vogue'']] essay on rising queer stars in rap music mentioned Shake being in a relationship with Sophia Diana Lodato.<ref name=Bernard2017>{{cite web|last=Bernard|first=Katherine|url=https://www.vogue.com/projects/13532951/pride-2017-rising-lesbian-rap-stars-essay/|title=Rapper's Delight|work=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]|date=June 25, 2017|access-date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> In a 2018 ''[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]'' article, Shake said she doesn't like to put labels on her sexuality. "I don't really identify myself as queer or gay or anything. I just like girls."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/get-to-know-070-shake-good-musics-shape-shifting-secret-weapon/|title=Get to Know 070 Shake, G.O.O.D. Music's Shape-Shifting Secret Weapon|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=January 30, 2020}}</ref>
