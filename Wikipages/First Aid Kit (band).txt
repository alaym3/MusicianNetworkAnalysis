'''First Aid Kit''' is a Swedish [[Folk music|folk]] duo consisting of the sisters Johanna and Klara Söderberg, born in 1990 and 1993, respectively.<ref name="bookofriends"/> When performing live, they are accompanied by a drummer, a [[Pedal steel guitar|pedal steel]] guitarist, and a keyboard player. In 2008, they gained some Internet popularity for their cover of the [[Fleet Foxes]]' song "Tiger Mountain Peasant Song".<ref name="Dolan">{{cite web|work=Rolling Stone|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/the-10-most-killer-cover-songs-of-the-past-decade-20130903|title=The 10 Most Killer Cover Songs of the Past Decade|last=Dolan|first=Jon|date=3 September 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.seattleglobalist.com/2012/03/13/stockholm-sisters-tribute-to-fleet-foxes-arrives-in-seattle/1559|title=Stockholm sisters' tribute to Fleet Foxes arrives in Seattle|last=Joko-Fujimoto|first=Claire|date=2012-03-13|website=The Seattle Globalist|access-date=26 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2008/dec/10/new-band-first-aid-kit|title=New band of the day – No 447: First Aid Kit|last=Lester|first=Paul|date=10 December 2008|website=the Guardian|access-date=2016-05-26}}</ref> ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine chose their song "Emmylou" as the number 10 "Single of the Year" in 2012. They have released four albums, four EPs and several singles. In 2015, they were nominated for a [[Brit Awards|Brit Award]] as one of the five [[Brit Award for International Group|best international groups]].<ref name="Brit2015">{{cite web |url=http://www.brits.co.uk/winners/ |title=The Brit Awards winners 2015 (archived) |date=25 February 2015 |access-date=22 September 2017 |url-status=bot: unknown |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150227214946/http://www.brits.co.uk/winners/ |archive-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> They were nominated again in 2019.<ref name="Brit2019">{{cite web|url = https://www.brits.co.uk/nominees|title= Brit Awards 2019 Vote for your favourite |work=brits.co.uk|access-date=12 January 2019}}</ref>

==Early history==
Sisters Johanna and Klara Söderberg are from [[Enskede-Årsta-Vantör|Enskede]], in [[Stockholm]]. Johanna was born on 31 October 1990 and Klara on 8 January 1993.<ref name="bookofriends">{{cite web|title=Book of friends, Swedish Radio P3, 9 July 2013 |url=http://static-cdn.sr.se/sida/images/3849/2909375_1200_674.jpg |publisher=Sverigesradio.se |access-date=22 September 2017}}</ref> Their father was a member of the Swedish rock pop band [[Lolita Pop]]<ref name="SommarP1">{{cite web|url=http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2071&artikel=5127784|title=Klara och Johanna Söderberg - Sommar & Vinter i P1, 8 July 2012|publisher=Sveriges Radio|date=2012-07-08|access-date=2015-04-05}}</ref>{{rp|at=3:20–4:03}} but quit before Johanna was born and became a teacher of history and religion.<ref name="Radar" /> Their mother teaches cinematography.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/rockandpopfeatures/9678475/First-Aid-Kit-interview-musical-pain-relief.html|title=First Aid Kit interview: musical pain relief|date=20 November 2012|publisher=telegraph.co.uk|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref>

From childhood, Klara and Johanna were eager singers, giving concerts using a [[jump rope]] as a pretend microphone.<ref name="SommarP1" />{{rp|at=0:40–2:40}} Klara's first favorite songs were [[Judy Garland]]'s songs from ''[[The Wizard of Oz (1939 film)|The Wizard of Oz]]'' and [[Billie Holiday]]'s version of "[[Gloomy Sunday]]", which she sang without much understanding of the English lyrics.<ref name="Sonic2011">{{cite web|url = http://www.sonicmagazine.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=4788&Itemid=117&mosmsg=Item+saved+successfully|title= Tvärsemot tiden (originally published in Sonic #60 in December 2011)|date=18 July 2015|publisher=sonicmagazine.com|access-date=22 July 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160306053316/http://www.sonicmagazine.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=4788&Itemid=117&mosmsg=Item+saved+successfully |archive-date=6 March 2016}}</ref> Klara wrote her first song "Femton mil i min Barbiebil" [Fifteen miles in my Barbie car] when she was six.<ref name="gulliga">{{cite web|url = http://www.svd.se/first-aid-kit-vill-inte-vara-gulliga_1143199|title= First Aid Kit vill inte vara gulliga|date=17 April 2008|publisher=svd.se|access-date=6 December 2017 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150723131112/http://www.svd.se/first-aid-kit-vill-inte-vara-gulliga_1143199|archive-date=23 July 2015}}</ref>

Klara and Johanna both attended the [[Internationella Engelska Skolan]] of Enskede.<ref name="School">{{cite web|title=First Aid Kit tillbaka där allt började, 27 March 2012|url=http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/musik/first-aid-kit-tillbaka-dar-allt-borjade/|publisher=dn.se|access-date=12 April 2015}}</ref> Klara applied for admission to a music school, but she was not accepted.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/musik/succesystrarna-fran-svedmyra/|title=Succésystrarna från Svedmyradate=19 January 2010|publisher=dn.se|access-date=7 December 2017 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160412162900/http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/musik/succesystrarna-fran-svedmyra/|archive-date=12 April 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Klara Söderberg: "Jag kom inte in på musikskolan", 14 October 2014|url=http://www.svt.se/skavlan/first-aid-kit-och-lena-endre-i-skavlan/|publisher=Sveriges Television|access-date=12 April 2015}}</ref>

In 2005, when Klara was 12, a friend introduced her to the band [[Bright Eyes (band)|Bright Eyes]].<ref name="Sonic2011" /> This led her to country and folk music stars such as [[Johnny Cash]], [[Bob Dylan]], [[Leonard Cohen]], [[Carter family]], [[Louvin Brothers]], [[Townes Van Zandt]], [[Gram Parsons]] and [[Emmylou Harris]]. The same year, she received a guitar as a Christmas present<ref name="Radar">{{cite web|title=Interview in Under The Radar, 18 October 2010|url=http://www.undertheradarmag.com/interviews/first_aid_kit/|publisher=Undertheradarmag.com|access-date=13 May 2014}}</ref> and quickly learned to play it.

Johanna enjoyed a wide range of music, from [[Britney Spears]] to [[Techno|German techno]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Interview in It's Nice That, 16 April 2015|url=http://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/first-aid-kit|publisher=itsnicethat.com|access-date=19 April 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Interview in Mashable, 16 October 2014|url=http://mashable.com/2014/10/16/first-aid-kit-band/|publisher=Mashable.com|access-date=23 October 2014}}</ref> When she saw the film ''[[O Brother, Where Art Thou?]]'' and listened to its soundtrack, she was inspired to sing "[[Down in the River to Pray]]" with Klara. Fascinated by the result, the two began to sing together, first at home and then as street singers in the [[Stockholm metro]] and in front of liquor stores.<ref name="SommarP1" />{{rp|at=16:10–17:55}}<ref name="WorldCafe2015">{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2015/07/31/428150148/world-cafe-sense-of-place-stockholm-first-aid-kit|title=Interview (audio): World Cafe Sense Of Place — Stockholm: First Aid Kit, 31 July 2015|publisher=WXPN & npr.org|access-date=2015-08-02}}</ref>{{rp|at=2:55-4:35}} Klara came up with the name for their band at age 13 by looking through a dictionary. She found the term "first aid kit" and thought it best described what she wanted her music to be.<ref name="Radar" />

Klara and Johanna also started to write and compose their own country-folk songs inspired by [[Devendra Banhart]] and [[CocoRosie]], among others,<ref name="Sonic2011" /> without much influence from their parents, who were fonder of [[Patti Smith]], [[Velvet Underground]] and the [[Pixies (band)|Pixies]].<ref name="Radar" /> Their father has said that he was astonished and actually a little jealous of the ease his daughters had in producing their music.<ref>{{cite web|title=Så är det att ha en popstjärna som barn, 21 March 2011|url=http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=4067&artikel=4413437|access-date=4 April 2015}}</ref>{{rp|at=8:20–8:55}} The most important advice their father gave them was to sing so loud that even somebody behind a wall could hear it.<ref name="Sonic2011" />

==Career==

===2007–2009: Beginnings and ''Drunken Trees'' EP===
In April 2007 the sisters began to upload their self-made songs to the social networking site [[MySpace]].<ref name="gulliga" /> They also sent a demo of their song "Tangerine" to  Swedish radio.<ref>{{cite web|title=First Aid Kit at Wichita website in 2009 (archived) |url=http://www.wichita-recordings.com/index.php/artists/detail/first_aid_kit/ |access-date=21 September 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090118014829/http://www.wichita-recordings.com/index.php/artists/detail/first_aid_kit/ |archive-date=January 18, 2009 }}</ref> It immediately went on air and was listed as one of the best songs of the summer 2007,<ref>{{cite web|title=Bäst juli 2007 (archived)|url=http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2945&artikel=1473208|publisher=sverigesradio.se|access-date=19 April 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304000935/http://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2945&artikel=1473208 |archive-date=4 March 2016}}</ref> which led to requests for live performances and even some offers from record companies. The first official performance as First Aid Kit was at PUNKTmedis library in Stockholm on 6 October.<ref name="School" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Stor publik på PUNKTdemo 6 oktober|url=http://punktmedis.blogspot.fi/2007/10/|publisher=PUNKTmedis|access-date=4 September 2017}}</ref> More performances followed, but as Klara was 14 and still at school, they could only play during weekends.

Coincidentally, their younger brother attended the same kindergarten as the daughter of [[Karin Dreijer]], half of Swedish electronic duo [[The Knife]], also known as [[Fever Ray]].<ref name="gulliga" /> Their mother asked Dreijer to check out her daughters' songs on MySpace.<ref name="Sonic2011" /> The band subsequently signed with [[Rabid Records]], a label co-owned by The Knife, with conditions that allowed the duo to have full control over their music and album art.<ref name="Radar" />

In April 2008, the band released their debut EP ''[[Drunken Trees]]'' in Sweden, and made their first appearance on Swedish TV.<ref name="gulliga" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rabidrecords.com:80/firstaidkit/default.html|title=First Aid Kit|publisher=rabidrecords.com|access-date=7 December 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080328230710/http://www.rabidrecords.com/firstaidkit/default.html|archive-date=28 March 2008}}</ref> The EP was produced by their father, and was a re-recorded collection of songs from their MySpace, originally entitled "Cross Oceans".

The duo was relatively well-known in Sweden by August 2008, when they uploaded a cover version of "Tiger Mountain Peasant Song" by [[Fleet Foxes]] to YouTube. [[Robin Pecknold]], frontman and lead guitarist for Fleet Foxes, praised the cover on his band's webpage, causing a surge in popularity for the video.<ref name="Radar" /><ref name="SommarP1" />{{rp|at=32:40-34:30}} This exposure led to international recognition, and by the end of 2008, the duo had signed a record deal with [[Wichita Records]].

[[File:First Aid Kit Wiesbaden 2009 double.jpg|thumb|right|Johanna (left) and Klara Söderberg in [[Wiesbaden]] in 2009]]
Due to international touring, Johanna quit high school in the autumn of 2008 and Klara never started it.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DtE9P57vBAA |title=LADYGUNN TV / First Aid Kit Interview |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2014-06-06}}</ref> Their father took a leave from his job as a teacher and accompanied them on tour, also helping with soundchecks. First Aid Kit's first show outside [[Scandinavia]] took place on 22 November 2008 at the [[Crossing Border Festival]] in Netherlands. Fleet Foxes performed the day before, and when Pecknold spotted the duo standing at the edge of the stage, they were unexpectedly asked to join to sing "Tiger Mountain Peasant Song".<ref name="SommarP1" />{{rp|at=34:30–35:10}}<ref name="WorldCafe2015" />{{rp|at=6:15–7:35}}

Wichita re-released ''Drunken Trees'' on 23 February 2009 as an enhanced EP, with "Tiger Mountain Peasant Song" added as a bonus track along with several videos.

===2010–2011: ''The Big Black and the Blue''===
The duo released their first album, ''[[The Big Black and the Blue]]'' on 1 February 2010.<ref>{{cite web |title=The Big Black And The Blue (Limited Edition CD) |url=http://www.wichita-recordings.com:80/shop/music/the-big-black-and-the-blue-2.html |access-date=6 December 2017 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100410162919/http://www.wichita-recordings.com/shop/music/the-big-black-and-the-blue-2.html |archive-date=10 April 2010}}</ref> As with the ''Drunken Trees'' EP, the album was again produced by their father. The release was followed by an extensive tour throughout the United States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand and parts of Europe, including [[Primavera Sound Festival]] in [[Barcelona]].<ref>{{cite web|title=First Aid Kit redo för stora genomslaget|url=http://www.helagotland.se/noje/first-aid-kit-redo-for-stora-genomslaget-7370083.aspx|publisher=Helagotland.se|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=First Aid Kit ready to save America this fall|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2010/07/first-aid-kit-ready-to-save-america-this-fall/|publisher=consequenceofsound.net|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=First Aid Kit Sunday 05 September - Saturday 11 September 2010|url=http://www.pennydrop.com.au/tour/first-aid-kit-5/|publisher=pennydrop.com.au|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=First Aid Kit September 16, 2010 The Wine Cellar, Auckland|url=http://www.einsteinmusicjournal.co.nz/first-aid-kit-live-photos/|publisher=einsteinmusicjournal.co.nz|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=San Miguel Primavera Sound 2010 Tickets|url=http://www.primaverasound.es/noticiaSingle?id=238/|publisher=primaverasound.es|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref>

On 19 November 2009, Klara and Johanna met [[Conor Oberst]] after the [[Monsters of Folk]]'s concert in Stockholm, and gave him a demo of their coming album.<ref>{{cite web|title="Vi är inblandade i allt tillsammans", 28 December 2011|url=https://www.expressen.se/noje/vi-ar-inblandade-i-allt-tillsammans/|publisher=expressen.se|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref> Oberst watched the duo perform at the [[Austin City Limits Music Festival]] pre-party in Texas on 7 October 2010. At this concert, he was especially impressed by the duo's acoustic performance of Ghost Town, during which the audience listened in complete silence. After meeting the duo, Oberst called [[Mike Mogis]] and asked him to see the duo's performance at ACL festival two days later, on October 9.<ref name="Sonic2011" /> After watching this performance, Mogis offered to produce the duo's next album.<ref name="SommarP1" />{{rp|at=29:20–31:20}}<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.svd.se/allt-stammer-for-first-aid-kit|title=Allt stammer för First Aid Kit|date=27 January 2012|publisher=svd.se|access-date=7 December 2017 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160312104906/http://www.svd.se/allt-stammer-for-first-aid-kit|archive-date=12 March 2016}}</ref>

Two days later, on 11 October 2010, after playing a concert in [[Nashville]], the duo received a telephone call from [[Jack White (musician)|Jack White]], who asked the duo to visit his studio the next day.<ref name="Sonic2011" /> The result was a vinyl single containing cover versions of [[Buffy Sainte-Marie]]'s "[[Universal Soldier (song)|Universal Soldier]]" and [[Mel London]]'s and [[Tampa Red]]'s "[[It Hurts Me Too]]". The single was released on White's [[Third Man Records]] label.<ref name="SommarP1" />{{rp|at=35:30–36:55}}<ref>{{cite web|title=14 May 2014 First Aid Kit at Jo Whiley BBC Radio 2, (at 2:30 of the interview)|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p01z4wq7/|publisher=Bbc.co.uk|access-date=25 May 2014}}</ref>

First Aid Kit performed at the [[Polar Music Prize]] gala on 30 August 2011 in Stockholm, where they performed the song "[[Dancing Barefoot]]" for the winner [[Patti Smith]]. Smith was moved to tears at the intense ending of the song.<ref name="SommarP1" />{{rp|at=4:03–6:40}}

===2012–2013: ''The Lion's Roar''===
[[File:First Aid Kit (Frankfurt am Main, 18.02.2012).jpg|thumb|left|First Aid Kit (Frankfurt am Main, 18 February 2012)]]
In January 2012, the band released their second album, ''[[The Lion's Roar (album)|The Lion's Roar]]'', produced by [[Mike Mogis]]. The album was critically acclaimed upon release, and debuted at number one in Sweden and number 35 in the UK. The duo toured extensively in support of the album, including performances at several major festivals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2517105/lollapalooza-2012-friday/|title=First Aid Kit talks festival essentials and more at Lollapalooza 2012|date=4 August 2012|publisher=mtv.com|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://diymag.com/archive/bestival-2012-first-aid-kit|title=Bestival 2012: First Aid Kit|date=12 September 2012|publisher=diymag.com|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/live/berlin-festival-2012|title=Berlin Festival 2012|date=19 September 2012|publisher=clashmusic.com|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.austinchronicle.com/music/2012-10-19/acl-live-shot-first-aid-kit/|title=ACL Live Shot: First Aid Kit|date=19 October 2012|publisher=austinchronicle.com|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref> The duo performed the song "Emmylou" on ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]'' in April 2012<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 April 2012|title=Watch First Aid Kit Play "Emmylou" On Conan|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2012/04/watch-first-aid-kit-play-emmylou-on-conan.html|access-date=6 December 2017|publisher=pastemagazine.com}}</ref>, and on [[Late Show with David Letterman|''The Late Show with David Letterman'']] in October 2012<ref>{{cite web|title=IMDb|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3323384/|access-date=18 January 2014|publisher=IMDb.com}}</ref>. 

On 28 August 2012, they performed "[[America (Simon & Garfunkel song)|America]]" for the Polar Music Prize winner [[Paul Simon]], who gave the duo a standing ovation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://time.com/3591929/first-aid-kit-america-video/|title=Watch First Aid Kit Travel the Country in 'America' Video|date=18 November 2014|publisher=time.com|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref>

[[File:First Aid Kit på Grammisgalan 2013.jpg|thumb|upright=.8|Johanna and Klara Söderberg at the [[Grammis|Swedish Grammis Awards]] in 2013]]

The song "The Lion's Roar" was featured in episode 2 of series 4 of ''[[Misfits (TV series)|Misfits]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.misfitsmusic.co.uk/php/season4/misfits_season4_episode_2.php|title=Misfits Series 4: Episode 2 Playlist |publisher=misfitsmusic.co.uk|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref> "In the Hearts of Men" was featured in episode 21 of season 8 of ''[[Bones (TV series)|Bones]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/bones/season-8/14102|title=Bones S8, E21 The Maiden in the Mushrooms|publisher=tunefind.com|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref>

In November 2012, the duo received awards for "Composer of the year" and "Breakthrough of the Year" from the Swedish Music Publishers Association.<ref>{{cite web|title=Musikförläggarnas pris - Resultat & bilder 2012|url=http://musikforlaggarna.se/musikforlaggarnas-pris/resultat-bilder/?yearly=2012|publisher=Musikförläggarna|access-date=12 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141218025532/http://musikforlaggarna.se/musikforlaggarnas-pris/resultat-bilder/?yearly=2012|archive-date=18 December 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> The song "Emmylou" was chosen by ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine as the #10 "Single of the Year" in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rolling Stone Music: 50 Best Songs of 2012: First Aid Kit, 'Emmylou'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/50-best-songs-of-2012-20121205/first-aid-kit-emmylou-19691231|publisher=Rollingstone.com|access-date=6 March 2014}}</ref> In February 2013, First Aid Kit was awarded the [[Nordic Music Prize]] for Best Nordic 2012 Album.<ref>{{cite web|title=First Aid Kit wins the third annual Nordic Music Prize! (archived) |url=http://nordicmusicprize.com/ |publisher=Nordicmusicprize.com |access-date=12 May 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140518112144/http://nordicmusicprize.com/ |archive-date=18 May 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Søstrene vant nordisk albumpris|url=http://www.aftenposten.no/incoming/Sostrene-vant-nordisk-albumpris-7121987.html|publisher=Aftenposten.no|access-date=15 February 2013}}</ref> They were also awarded four Swedish [[Grammis]] awards for 2012 "Artist of the Year", "Songwriter of the Year", "Best Pop of the Year" and "Album of the Year".<ref>{{cite web|title=First Aid Kit Win 4 Grammis! |url=http://www.wichita-recordings.com/news/first-aid-kit-win-4-grammis |access-date=4 April 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140201133837/http://www.wichita-recordings.com/news/first-aid-kit-win-4-grammis |archive-date=1 February 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Grammisvinnarna genom åren|url=http://grammis.se/om-grammis/grammisvinnarna-genom-aren/|publisher=Grammis.se|access-date=22 September 2017}}</ref>

===2014–2016: ''Stay Gold''===
[[File:First Aid Kit - Ruisrock 2014-07-06.jpg|thumb|left|First Aid Kit at [[Ruisrock]] 6 July 2014]]

Their third studio album, ''[[Stay Gold (First Aid Kit album)|Stay Gold]]'', was released on 10 June 2014 through [[Columbia Records]].<ref>{{cite tweet|user=ColumbiaRecords|author=Columbia Records|number=450643939285286912|date=31 March 2014|title=.@FirstAidKitBand's "Stay Gold" hits stores June 10th. Pre-order now &amp; get an instant download of "My Silver Lining":}}</ref> The band released two singles from the album: "[[My Silver Lining (First Aid Kit song)|My Silver Lining]]" and "Cedar Lane".<ref>{{cite web|title=My Silver Lining Single|url=http://www.indieshuffle.com/first-aid-kit-my-silver-lining/|publisher=Indieshuffle.com|access-date=31 March 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.scandipop.co.uk/first-aid-kit-cedar-lane/|title=First Aid Kit: 'Cedar Lane'|date=6 May 2014|publisher=scandipop.co.uk|access-date=6 December 2017}}</ref> As a result, the duo again received the "Composer of the year" prize from the Swedish Music Publishers Association.<ref>{{cite web|title=Musikförläggarnas pris - Resultat & bilder 2014|url=http://musikforlaggarna.se/musikforlaggarnas-pris/resultat-bilder/?yearly=2014|publisher=Musikförläggarna|access-date=12 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160311085652/http://musikforlaggarna.se/musikforlaggarnas-pris/resultat-bilder/?yearly=2014|archive-date=11 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Moreover, in January 2015 First Aid Kit was nominated for the [[2015 Brit Awards]] in the Best International Group category as one of the five nominees<ref name="Brit2015" /> and won the "Best group of the year" prize in the [[Sveriges Radio P3|Swedish Radio P3]] Gold Gala.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://sverigesradio.se/sida/gruppsida.aspx?programid=3964&grupp=21828&artikel=6070322/|title= Årets grupp: First Aid Kit|date=17 January 2015|language=sv|access-date=18 January 2015}}</ref> Furthermore, in the same month, a set of Swedish pop music related postage stamps was released in Sweden, one motif being First Aid Kit.<ref>{{cite web|title=Swedish pop exports on postage stamps|url=http://www.postnord.com/en/media/press-releases/postnord/2014/swedish-pop-exports-avicii-and-robyn-on-postage-stamps/|publisher=Postnord|access-date=9 November 2014}}</ref>

First Aid Kit began touring again in May 2014 in North America, Europe, Japan and Australia.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sony Press Release 25 February 2014: Swedish Duo First Aid Kit Unveils North American On 8 June Columbia released an EP ''Live from the Rebel Hearts Club'' in digital format that contains live versions of five songs from the [[Ruins (First Aid Kit album)|''Ruins'']] album in addition to a live version of ''You are the problem here''.<ref name="Rebel Hearts EP">{{cite web|url = http://ventsmagazine.com/2018/06/08/first-aid-kit-shares-live-from-the-rebel-hearts-club/|title= First Aid Kit shares 'Live from the Rebel Hearts Club'|work=ventsmagazine.com|date=8 June 2018|access-date=5 August 2018}}</ref> On 11 July [[Spotify]] released a stripped-down version of "Fireworks" and a cover of [[Kate Bush]]'s song "Running Up That Hill" as singles that were recorded in the new Spotify studio in Stockholm.<ref name="Spotify2019">{{cite web|url = https://newsroom.spotify.com/2018-07-12/the-otherworldly-magic-behind-first-aid-kits-new-spotify-singles/|title= The 'Otherworldly Magic' Behind First Aid Kit's New Spotify Singles|work=spotify.com|date=12 July 2018|access-date=13 July 2018}}</ref> On 14 September vinyl EP ''Tender Offerings'' was released containing four previously unreleased tracks.<ref name="Tender Offerings EP">{{cite web|url = https://www.amazon.com/Tender-Offerings-First-Aid-Kit/dp/B07F7R8KZC|title= Tender Offerings|work=amazon.com|access-date=25 August 2018}}</ref> It went number one in UK vinyl charts.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.officialcharts.com/charts/vinyl-singles-chart/|title=  Official Vinyl Singles Chart Top 40, 21 September 2018 - 27 September 2018 |website= [[OfficialCharts.com]] |access-date=22 September 2018}}</ref>

First Aid Kit sings backing vocals on several 2018 releases: on [[Alela Diane]]'s song ''Ether & Wood'' on her album Cusp,<ref name="Cusp"/> on [[George Ezra]]'s song ''Saviour'' on his album ''[[Staying at Tamara's]]''<ref name="Ezra Saviour">{{cite web|url = http://diymag.com/2018/03/13/george-ezra-first-aid-kit-saviour-staying-at-tamaras-watch|title= George Ezra recruits First Aid Kit for new track 'Saviour' |work=diymag.com|date=13 March 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref> and on [[Little Jinder]]'s song ''Goldwing'' on her album ''Hejdå''.<ref name="Jinder Goldwing">{{cite web|url = https://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/musik/a/VR8mn1/little-jinder-blandar-stark-elektronisk-haxgrogg|title= Little Jinder blandar stark elektronisk häxgrogg |work=aftonbladet.se|date=5 October 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref>

In November 2018, the duo's album ''Ruins'' was nominated for International Album of the Year at the 2019 UK Americana Awards.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.entertainment-focus.com/music-section/ef-country/uk-americana-awards-2019-nominations-revealed/|title=  UK Americana Awards 2019 nominations revealed |work=entertainment-focus.com|access-date=15 December 2018}}</ref> The band also received two Swedish Grammis award nominations in 2019, one for song of the year for ''"''Fireworks," and another for alternative pop album of the year for ''Ruins''.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://grammis.se/nominated_year/2019/|title=  Grammis nominated 2019 |work=grammis.se|access-date=15 December 2018}}</ref> In January 2019 they were again nominated for the International Group Brit Award.<ref name="Brit2019"/>

The second episode of the [[Dontnod Entertainment]]'s 2019 video game ''[[Life Is Strange 2]]'', released on 24 January 2019, features the song "I Found a Way" from the 2012 album [[The Lion's Roar (album)|The Lion's Roar]].

On 25 January 2019 it was announced that First Aid Kit perform their song ''Home Again'' in the new Moominvalley TV series soundtrack.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.moomin.com/en/blog/the-star-studded-lineup-for-the-moominvalley-tv-series-soundtrack-finally-revealed-tom-odell-mo-first-aid-kit-and-alma-featured/|title= The star-studded lineup for the Moominvalley TV series soundtrack finally revealed – Tom Odell, MØ, First Aid Kit and Alma featured |work=moomin.com|date=25 January 2019|access-date=27 January 2019}}</ref> The soundtrack album was released on 19 April.<ref name="Moominvalley">{{cite web|url = https://www.gutsy.fi/post/columbia-records-in-partnership-with-gutsy-animations-to-release-moominvalley-soundtrack |title= Columbia Records in partnership with Gutsy Animations to release Moominvalley soundtrack |work=gutsy.fi|date=22 February 2019|access-date=12 May 2019}}</ref>

On 16 October 2019 the duo received the [[Ulla Billquist]] scholarship that is awarded each year to female artists who through their artistry enriche the Swedish music scene.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.folketshusochparker.se/first-aid-kit-ar-2019-ars-ulla-billquist-stipendiat/ |title= First aid kit är 2019 års Ulla Billquist stipendiat |work= Folkets Hus och Parker |date=16 October 2019|access-date=17 October 2019}}</ref>

===2020–present: Other appearances===

On 13 January 2020 Johanna announced that she is expecting a baby in summer<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/a/8m1ew1/first-aid-kit-sangerskan-ar-gravid |title= First aid kit-sångerskan är gravid |work=aftonbladet.se|date=13 January 2020|access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref> A baby girl was born on 18 June.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/a/LAJ5Kp/first-aid-kit-stjarnan-har-fatt-barn |title= First aid kit-stjärnan har fått barn |work=Aftonbladet|date=23 June 2020|access-date=23 June 2020}}</ref> On 16 January Klara announced that she will start a country club in Stockholm with a house band.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.cafe.se/first-aid-kit-klara-och-sverrir-gudnason-startar-countryklubb-i-stockholm/ |title= First Aid Kit-Klara och Sverrir Gudnason startar countryklubb i Stockholm |work=cafe.se|date=16 January 2020|access-date=20 January 2020}}</ref> The first event was on 21 February.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/lyckad-start-for-klara-soderbergs-egna-countryklubb/ |title= Lyckad start för Klara Söderbergs countryklubb |work=Dagens Nyheter|date=22 February 2020|access-date=25 February 2020}}</ref> During spring 2020 First Aid Kit streamed live concerts from Johanna's home as well.
