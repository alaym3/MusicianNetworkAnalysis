"'''Beat Box'''" is the debut single by American rapper [[SpotemGottem]]. It was released on April 20, 2020, while the first remix, titled "'''Beat Box 2'''", featuring SpotemGottem linking up with American rapper [[Pooh Shiesty]], was released on December 18, 2020, alongside a video, and as part of Spotem's debut mixtape, ''Final Destination''. The first remix pushed the song to viral status on the video-sharing app [[TikTok]], spawning the "#JunebugChallenge", and later became SpotemGottem's first chart entry. 

American rapper [[DaBaby]] released a freestyle version of the song, which was worked into a second remix, titled "'''Beat Box 3'''", released February 18, 2021. A third remix of the song with vocals from American rapper [[Mulatto (rapper)|Mulatto]], accordingly dubbed the "Big Latto Mix", was released on March 13, 2021. A fourth remix followed on March 26, 2021, with Jamaican [[dancehall]] artist [[Shenseea]],<ref>{{Cite web|date=March 28, 2021|title=Shenseea Jumps On Official 'Beat Box' Remix With Florida Rapper SpotemGottem|url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2021/03/28/music/shenseea-jumps-on-official-beat-box-remix-with-florida-rapper-spotemgottem.html|access-date=March 28, 2021|website=DancehallMag}}</ref> following her freestyle version.<ref>{{cite web|last=Baugh|first=Claudine|date=January 25, 2021|title=Shenseea Swaps Dancing The 'Junebug Challenge' For Another Fire Freestyle|url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2021/01/25/news/shenseea-swaps-dancing-the-junebug-challenge-for-another-fire-freestyle.html|accessdate=March 8, 2021|work=Dancehall Mag}}</ref> A fifth remix, titled "'''Beat Box 4'''", was released April 16, 2021<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5V6kqd-jWKg</ref> featuring rapper [[NLE Choppa]]. A remix with BigKayBeezy<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.deezer.com/us/album/226847142|title = Beat Box (Freestyle)|date = April 22, 2021}}</ref> was released on April 22, 2021. On April 23, 2021, another remix entitled "'''Beat Box 5'''" featuring [[Polo G]] was released.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bm9X97ATDVs</ref> On May 3, 2021, another remix was released featuring [[Young M.A.]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.deezer.com/en/album/227107882|title = Beat Box (Freestyle)|date = May 3, 2021}}</ref> 

Lyrically, "Beat Box" is about how SpotemGottem's pistol is so loud it can "make a [[Beatboxing|beat box]]". The song contains violent rhymes, with a heavy bass production by Damn E.

==Background==
"Beat Box" was released in April 2020 as a standalone single.<ref name="Paper">{{cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/spotemgottem-beat-box-junebug-challenge-2650429854.html?rebelltitem=4#rebelltitem4|title=The #JunebugChallenge Is Launching This Florida Rapper's Career|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|last=Alston|first=Trey|date=February 10, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> Prior to the release of the Pooh Shiesty remix, the original version of "Beat Box" had already accumulated over six million views on [[YouTube]]. By January 2021, the remix had garnered over 2.8 million streams on [[Spotify]].<ref name="Genius">{{cite web|url=https://www.genius.com/amp/a/who-is-spotem-gottem-beatbox-2-lyrics|title=Florida Rapper Spotem Gottem Is Blowing Up With "BeatBox 2"|work=[[Genius (website)|Genius]]|last=Ahmed|first=Insanul|date=January 8, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> It was aided by [[viral video|viral]] [[TikTok]] videos of its accompanying dance, the #JunebugChallenge, named after its creator, Junebug, a social media personality and digital creator. He posted his first #JunebugChallenge video to TikTok and Instagram on December 24, and continued posting a new video until it started going viral.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2021/01/13/junebug-challenge/|title=TikTok: What is the Junebug Challenge? Latest dance trend explained!|work=HITC|last=Bain|first=Ellissa|date=January 13, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> The dance, as noted by ''[[HipHopDX]]''{{'}}s Marisa Mendez, involves "a spastic movement of your entire body in every and any random location you can find";<ref name="DX">{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.60237/title.beat-box-rapper-spotemgottem-proves-tiktoks-power-by-crashing-billboard-hot-100|title='Beat Box' Rapper SpotEmGottEm Proves TikTok's Power By Crashing Billboard Hot 100|work=[[HipHopDX]]|last=Mendez|first=Marisa|date=January 25, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> it includes flailing both arms, while leaning backwards and slightly limping.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/junebug-challenge-explainer-how-to-viral-tiktok/|title=The Junebug Challenge Is Everywhere. Here's What You Need to Know.|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=McKinney|first=Jessica|date=February 9, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> The dancing was described as "erratic".<ref name="AM">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/final-destination/1543302822|title=Final Destination by SpotemGottem on Apple Music|date=December 18, 2020|via=[[Apple Music]]|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref><ref name="Noisey">{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/y3gx4y/6-artists-who-could-be-bigger-than-tiktok-this-year|title=6 Artists Who Could Be Bigger Than TikTok This Year|work=[[Noisey]]|last=Corry|first=Kristin|date=January 22, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> SpotemGottem embraced the dance, and met Junebug in January 2021.<ref name="DX" />

==Composition and lyrics==
The production of "Beat Box" was described as containing "bass drums that taser your skin", with SpotemGottem delivering violent narratives about a shoot-out and how his [[Pistol Mitralieră model 1963/1965#Other civilian versions|draco]] is so loud, it "make a beat box".<ref name="Paper" /><ref name="Noisey" /> The songs's refrain, "Ready to get it started/Bitch, I got no sensе", was popularized in video clips on TikTok.<ref name="AM" /> The line, "Oh, he the plug, riding round with 4 bricks / Call me [[Kobe Bryant|Kobe]], 24 Kobe", sees SpotemGottem distorting his voice towards the end of the bar, similar to [[Playboi Carti]]'s "baby" voice.<ref name="Genius" /> "Beat Box 2" is essentially the same song, apart from the addition of Pooh Shiesty's verse.<ref name="Genius" />

==Critical reception==
[[Noisey]]'s Kristin Corry compared SpotemGottem's vocals to [[Kodak Black]] and ''[[No Ceilings]]''-era [[Lil Wayne]], stating that "At 18, he has got the energy and charisma to make you forget that 'Beat Box' is actually about a shootout".<ref name="Noisey" /> ''[[HipHopDX]]''{{'}}s Mark Elibert called the song an undeniable hit and a "banger".<ref name="DaBaby">{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.60797/title.dababys-new-freestyle-officially-becomes-spotemgottems-beat-box-3|title=DaBaby's New Freestyle Officially Becomes SpotemGottem's 'Beat Box 3'|work=[[HipHopDX]]|last=Elibert|first=Mark|date=February 26, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref>

==Music video==
The official video was released along with the song on April 20, 2020. It consists of SpotemGottem breaking out of a [[straitjacket]] and showing off a range of weapons.<ref name="Paper" /> The dance challenge is not featured in the video, as it only originated on TikTok in December 2020. By January 2021, the visual amassed nearly 14 million views on YouTube.<ref name="DX" />

==Chart performance==
Thanks to its viral success, the song debuted at number 84 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], on the chart dated January 30, 2021, becoming SpotemGottem's first entry on the chart.<ref name="DX" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/video/spotemgottems-beat-box-watch-now|title=SpotemGottem's "Beat Box"|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 8, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> Following the release of remixes "Beat Box 3" and "Beat Box 4", the song reached number 12 on the Hot 100, and at number 6 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|Hot R&B/Hip-Hop]] charts both dated April 24, 2021.<ref name="Hot 100"/><ref name="R&B/HH"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.60982/title.spotemgottems-beat-box-impacts-billboard-hot-100-thanks-to-dababy-pooh-shiesty-infinite-challenges|title=SpotemGottem's 'Beat Box' Impacts Billboard Hot 100 Thanks To DaBaby, Pooh Shiesty & Infinite Challenges|last=Saponara|first=Michael|date=March 9, 2021|accessdate=March 10, 2021|work=[[HipHopDX]]}}</ref> On top of becoming SpotemGottem's first entry to the component chart's top 10, the song received a joint credit on the website due to both Beat Box 2 and Beat Box 3 having significant impact on the song's performance. As such, Pooh Shiesty and DaBaby are both credited on the song, meaning the 2nd and 9th entry in the top 10 for Pooh Shiesty (following "[[Back in Blood (song)|Back in Blood]]") and DaBaby respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/dababy/chart-history/BSI/|title=DaBaby Chart History|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=March 22, 2021}}</ref>

[[DaBaby]] released his own freestyle version of the song on February 18, 2021, alongside a video in which he performs the song's dance challenge.<ref name="Complex">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/dababy-beatbox-remix-video|title=Watch DaBaby Rap Over SpotemGottem's "BeatBox" in New Video|work=[[Complex (magazine)| Complex]]|last=Mahadevan|first=Tara C.|date=February 19, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> His freestyle was worked into a remix and officially released as "Beat Box 3" on February 26.<ref name="DaBaby" />

===Composition===
In his freestyle, DaBaby raps about his "carefree approach to life, money, and interactions with women",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/music/dababy-remix-spotemgottem-beat-box/|title=DaBaby Remixes SpotEmGottem's Viral 'Beat Box' Track With A Boastful Video|work=[[Uproxx]]|last=Okon|first=Wongo|date=February 20, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> while giving a shout-out to SpotemGottem.<ref name="Complex" /> He also references YouTuber, [[Jojo Siwa]]: "Turn me up, niggas gon' see why/Nigga, you a bitch, JoJo Siwa (Bitch)". This line caused controversy as some perceived DaBaby to be dissing Siwa, however DaBaby explained that it was a play on words: "I 'Siwa' [see why] I'm not like the rest of you niggas". This is paired with DaBaby's nickname, JoJo, short for his real name Jonathan.<ref name="DaBaby" />

===Critical reception===
Mark Elibert and Michael Saponara of ''HipHopDX'' both called the freestyle "blazing",<ref name="DaBaby" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.60660/title.dababy-reignites-best-rapper-alive-debate-with-beat-box-freestyle|title=DaBaby Reignites Best Rapper Alive Debate With 'Beat Box' Freestyle|work=[[HipHopDX]]|last=Saponara|first=Michael|date=February 16, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> while Wongo Okon of [[Uproxx]] called DaBaby's contribution "braggadocios".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/music/dababy-jojo-siwa-freestyle-confused/|title=DaBaby Name-Dropped Jojo Siwa In A Recent Freestyle And People Have No Idea Why|work=[[Uproxx]]|last=Okon|first=Wongo|date=February 21, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> Paul Duong of ''[[Rap Radar]]'' said DaBaby's verse adds "more fuel to the phenomenon".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rapradar.com/2021/02/26/spotemgottem-ft-dababy-beat-box-3/|title=SpotEmGottem Ft. DaBaby "Beat Box 3"|work=[[Rap Radar]]|last=Diong|first=Paul|date=February 26, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref> Jade Dadalica of [[GRM Daily]] said DaBaby delivers "some quick-paced and witty bars over the catchy instrumental".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://grmdaily.com/dababy-beatbox/|title=DaBaby Remixes SpotemGottem's Viral "Beatbox" On Latest Freestyle|work=[[GRM Daily]]|last=Dadalica|first=Jade|date=February 20, 2021|accessdate=March 10, 2021}}</ref>
