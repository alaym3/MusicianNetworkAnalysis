'''Martrel Reeves'''<ref name=wsp>{{cite web|title=Fat Trel's Real D.C.|url=http://www.washingtoncitypaper.com/articles/42369/fat-trels-real-dc-for-the-prankster-prince-of-dmv/|publisher=Washington City Paper|access-date=June 16, 2014}}</ref> (born June 26, 1990), better known by his stage name '''Fat Trel''', is an American rapper. He is currently signed to DTLA Records with distribution via [[Beryl Media]], and was signed [[Rick Ross]]'s label [[Maybach Music Group]] and [[Atlantic Records]]. From 2009 to 2016, Fat Trel released a solo mixtape every year.

== Musical career ==
In 2011, Fat Trel released "Respect With The [[Tec-9|Tec]]" produced by [[Lex Luger (musician)|Lex Luger]]. He was selected to perform with [[Juicy J]], [[Smoke DZA]] and [[Joey Badass]].

In 2012, Fat Trel released the [[mixtape]] ''Nightmare on E Street'' featuring [[Kirko Bangz]], Cuz Quette, [[Red Café]], [[Rick Ross]] and [[Big K.R.I.T.]] and also worked with [[Chief Keef]] on  "Russian Roulette".<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XGMVd4kRacQ Chief Keef & Fat Trel – Russian Roulette (Official Video) – YouTube]. M.youtube.com (July 4, 2012). Retrieved on 2014-06-13.</ref> Fat Trel then joined Master P's Louie V Mob along with [[Alley Boy]]. Master P, Fat Trel and Alley Boy released a free mixtape as Louie V Mob on February 12, 2013, titled ''New World Order''.<ref>[http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/20347/louie-v-mob-new-world-order.html Louie V Mob – New World Order – No Limit Forever]. Livemixtapes.com. Retrieved on June 13, 2014.</ref> In addition to Master P, Trel and Alley, the tape also features guest appearances by [[Gucci Mane]], [[E-40]] and other artists. On August 19, 2013, Fat Trel released ''SDMG'' (which stands for "Sex, Drugs, Money, Guns").<ref>[http://www.datpiff.com/Fat-Trel-SDMG-mixtape.523092.html Fat Trel – SDMG // Free Mixtape @]. Datpiff.com (August 19, 2013). Retrieved on 2014-06-13.</ref> The mixtape features guest appearances by [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], [[Black Cobain]], Magazeen, Smoke DZA, [[Danny Brown (rapper)|Danny Brown]], [[YG (rapper)|YG]], [[Red Café]] and others. The production was handled by [[Young Chop]], [[Cardo]], [[Harry Fraud]], [[DJ Mustard]] and others.<ref>[http://www.complex.com/music/2013/08/fat-trel-sdmg Fat Trel "SDMG"]. Complex (August 19, 2013). Retrieved on 2014-06-13.</ref>

On November 6, 2013, [[Maybach Music Group|MMG]] announced that they had signed Fat Trel. Fat Trel released his first project on MMG, ''Gleesh'' on April 1, 2014. ''Gleesh'' is a 15-track mixtape with guest appearances from [[Rick Ross]], [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], [[Rockie Fresh]], Tracy T, [[Stalley]] and others, with production from All Star, Harry Fraud, Young Chop and others.<ref name="datpiff1">[http://www.datpiff.com/Fat-Trel-Gleesh-mixtape.597023.html Fat Trel – Gleesh Hosted by Maybach Music Group // Free Mixtape @]. Datpiff.com. Retrieved on June 13, 2014.</ref>

Ultimately, Gleesh would be the only project Fat Trel released with MMG. 

On June 26, 2020, Fat Trel released "Big Homie" via his Slutty Boyz Entertainment imprint in partnership with DTLA Records. The album is distributed by [[Beryl Media]].<ref name="datpiff2">[https://www.datpiff.com/Fat-Trel-Big-Homie-mixtape.982345.html Fat Trel – Big Homie Hosted by Slutty Boyz / DTLA Records / [[Beryl Media]] // Free Mixtape @]. Datpiff.com. Retrieved on June 26, 2020.</ref>

== Personal life ==
Fat Trel has a son and a daughter.<ref name=XXL2>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/fat-trel-finally-free-mixtape-interview/|title=Fat Trel's 'Finally Free' is the First Mixtape That He Has Ever Recorded Sober|first=Kairi|last=Coe|work=XXL|date=July 26, 2018|access-date=November 16, 2021}}</ref> He is a [[Christian]].<ref name=XXL3>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/fat-trel-interview-finally-free-mixtape/|title=Fat Trel Comes Clean About His Sobriety, Preps New Mixtape 'Finally Free'|work=XXL|first=Preezy|last=.|date=October 16, 2017|access-date=November 16, 2021}}</ref>

In 2016, Fat Trel was arrested twice in unrelated incidents. In March, he was arrested in [[Anne Arundel County]] for using [[counterfeit currency]] at a Maryland casino. Fat Trel claimed he got the money from a D.C. convenience store [[Automated teller machine|ATM]].<ref name=WaPo1>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/public-safety/dc-rapper-fat-trel-charged-with-possession-of-counterfeit-currency/2016/03/30/97b98576-f6a2-11e5-9804-537defcc3cf6_story.html|title=D.C. rapper Fat Trel charged with possession of counterfeit currency|first=Victoria|last=St. Martin|work=[[The Washington Post]]|date=March 30, 2016|access-date=November 16, 2021}}</ref> In  April, he was arrested in [[Arlington County, Virginia]] for driving while intoxicated, narcotics distribution, speeding, and other charges.<ref name=WaPo2>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/public-safety/dc-rapper-fat-trel-got-arrested-again/2016/04/08/115d63d4-fda1-11e5-886f-a037dba38301_story.html|title=D.C. rapper Fat Trel got arrested, again|first=Dana|last=Hedgpeth|work=[[The Washington Post]]|date=April 8, 2016|access-date=November 16, 2021}}</ref> He spent over a year in [[Arlington County, Virginia|Arlington County Jail]], before being released on September 22, 2017.<ref name=XXL1>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/fat-trel-released-from-jail/|title=Fat Trel Released From Jail|first=Max|last=Weinstein|work=XXL|date=September 24, 2017|access-date=November 16, 2021}}</ref> While in prison, he recorded nearly 250 songs.<ref name=XXL2 /> 

In 2018, after being released from prison, Fat Trel told ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' that he was sober and his mixtape ''Finally Free'' was the first mixtape he produced sober. He described the challenges of quitting [[Lean (drug)|lean]] and cited the death of [[Fredo Santana]] as a wake up call.<ref name=XXL2 />

On July 8, 2018, Fat Trel was pulled over in [[Prince George's County, Maryland]] for traffic violations and had his vehicle searched. Police found a .40 caliber [[ghost gun]] pistol with ten rounds of ammunition in the car. Fat Trel told police the weapon was his and he was arrested. He faced a maximum of 10 years in federal prison and plead guilty on September 22, 2020.<ref name=Justice1>{{cite web|url=https://www.justice.gov/usao-md/pr/dc-felon-pleads-guilty-federal-court-maryland-illegal-possession-ghost-gun-firearm-and|title=D.C. Felon Pleads Guilty in Federal Court in Maryland to Illegal Possession of a “Ghost Gun” Firearm and Ammunition|work=U.S. Department of Justice United States Attorney's Office District of Maryland|date=September 22, 2020|access-date=November 16, 2021}}</ref> On September 24, 2021, [[U.S. District Judge]] [[Paula Xinis]] sentenced Fat Trel to two years and six months in federal prison, followed by three years of supervised release.<ref name=Justice2>{{cite web|url=https://www.justice.gov/usao-md/pr/dc-felon-sentenced-30-months-federal-prison-illegal-possession-40-caliber-ghost-gun|title=D.C. Felon Sentenced to 30 Months In Federal Prison For Illegal Possession Of A .40 Caliber "Ghost Gun" Firearm And 10 Rounds Of Ammunition|work=U.S. Department of Justice United States Attorney's Office District of Maryland|date=September 24, 2021|access-date=November 16, 2021}}</ref> On October 15, 2021, Fat Trel was released from prison due to time served. He quickly reconnected with Rick Ross, who remains close with Fat Trel despite him no longer being part of the MMG label.<ref name=hiphopdx>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.65409/title.rick-ross-celebrates-fat-trel-prison-release-following-2018-weapons-arrest|title=Rick Ross celebrates Fat Trel's prison release following 2018 weapons arrest|first=Kyle|last=Eustice|date=October 25, 2021|access-date=November 16, 2021}}</ref>
