'''Ten Years After''' are a British [[blues rock]] band, most popular in the late 1960s and early 1970s. Between 1968 and 1973, Ten Years After scored eight [[Top 40]] albums on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums"/> In addition they had twelve albums enter the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/ten-years-after-mn0000020050/awards |title=Ten Years After &#124; Awards |website=AllMusic |access-date=29 January 2014}}</ref>

They are best known for tracks such as "I'm Going Home", "Hear Me Calling", "[[I'd Love to Change the World]]" and "[[Love Like a Man]]". Their musical style consisted of [[blues rock]]<ref>{{cite book|author=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|title=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1Q8EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA24|date=19 January 2002|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|page=24|issn=0006-2510}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|first=David|last=Dicaire|title=More Blues Singers: Biographies of 50 Artists from the Later 20th Century|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3E3pGRLc1L8C&pg=PA59|date=19 December 2001|publisher=McFarland|isbn=978-0-7864-1035-4|page=59}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|first=Robert|last=Santelli|title=The Big Book of Blues: A Biographical Encyclopedia|url=https://archive.org/details/bigbookofbluesbi00sant|url-access=registration|year=2001|publisher=Penguin Books|isbn=978-0-14-100145-6|page=[https://archive.org/details/bigbookofbluesbi00sant/page/455 455]}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|first=James E.|last=Perone|title=The Album: A Guide to Pop Music's Most Provocative, Influential, and Important Creations|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=M5Dh_Owq0agC&pg=PA262|year=2012|publisher=ABC-CLIO|isbn=978-0-313-37906-2|page=262}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last= Prown|first= Pete|date= 1997|title= Legends of Rock Guitar: The Essential Reference of Rock's Greatest Guitarists|publisher= Hal Leonard Corporation|page= 73|isbn= 978-0793540426}}</ref> and [[hard rock]].<ref>{{cite book |last= Chappell|first= Jon|date= 2006|title= Blues Guitar For Dummies|publisher= John Wiley & Sons|page= 241|isbn= 978-0470049204}}</ref>

==History==
===Formation: 1962–1966===
[[File:Blues Festival Suwałki 2009 - Ten Years After 02.jpg|250px|thumb|right|[[Leo Lyons]] and [[Joe Gooch]] of Ten Years After at [[Suwałki]] Blues Festival, 2009]]
The band's core formed in late 1960 as Ivan Jay and the Jaycats. After several years of local success in the [[Nottingham]]/[[Mansfield]] area, they changed their name to the Jaybirds in 1962 and later to Ivan Jay and the Jaymen. Ivan Jay (born Ivan Joseph Harrison, 1939, [[Nottingham]], [[Nottinghamshire]]; died April 2009, USA) sang lead vocals from late 1960 to 1962 and was joined by [[Ric Lee]] in August 1965, replacing drummer Dave Quickmire (born David Quickmire, 1940, [[Mansfield]], [[Nottinghamshire]]), who had replaced Pete Evans (born Peter Evans, 1940, [[Mansfield]], [[Nottinghamshire]]) in 1962. Roy Cooper (born 11 November 1943, [[Huthwaite]], [[Nottinghamshire]]) played rhythm guitar, plus did vocals from 1960 to 1962. The Jaybirds moved to London to back [[The Ivy League (band)|the Ivy League]] in 1966.<ref name="1966tya">{{cite web | url=http://alvinlee.com/biography/earlyyears.htm | title=Alvin Lee biography | publisher=Alvinlee.com | access-date=25 October 2013}}</ref> In the same year, [[Chick Churchill]] joined the group as keyboard player. That November, the quartet signed a manager, Chris Wright, and changed their name to Blues Trip. Using the name Blues Yard they played one show at the [[Marquee Club]] supporting the [[Bonzo Dog Doo-Dah Band]]. [[Alvin Lee]] and [[Leo Lyons]] again changed their name in 1966 to Ten Years After – in honour of [[Elvis Presley]],<ref name=originalnae>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/03/07/arts/music/alvin-lee-guitarist-of-ten-years-after-dies-at-68.html|title=Alvin Lee, British Blues-Rock Guitarist, Dies at 68|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|access-date=16 February 2016}}</ref> one of Lee's idols.<ref name=originalnae /> (This was ten years after Presley's successful year, 1956).<ref name=1966tya/><ref name="Guinness Rockopedia 3"/> Some sources<ref>{{cite web |last1=unknown |title=Pre-Ten Years After |url=http://www.alvinlee.de/pre_tya.htm |website=The History of Ten Years After |publisher=unknown |access-date=2 October 2019}}</ref> claim that the name was pulled by Leo Lyons from a magazine, advertising a book, ''Suez Ten Years After'' (referring to the [[Suez Crisis]]).

===Classic Ten Years After: 1967–1974===
The group was the first act booked by the soon-to-be Chrysalis Agency. It secured a residency at the Marquee, and was invited to play at the Windsor Jazz Festival in 1967. That performance led to a contract with [[Deram Records|Deram]], a subsidiary of [[Decca Records|Decca]] — the first band Deram signed without a [[hit record|hit]] single. In October 1967 they released the self-titled debut album, ''[[Ten Years After (Ten Years After album)|Ten Years After]]''.<ref name="AMG"/> In 1968, after touring Scandinavia and the United States, Ten Years After released a second album, the live ''[[Undead (Ten Years After)|Undead]]'', with a first version of the noteworthy song, "I'm Going Home".<ref name="AMG"/> They followed this in February 1969 by the studio issue ''[[Stonedhenge]]'', a British hit that included another well-known track, "Hear Me Calling", which was also released as a single. (In 1972, this song was [[cover version|covered]] by the British [[glam rock]] rising stars, [[Slade]].) In July 1969, the group appeared at the [[Newport Jazz Festival]], in the first event rock bands were invited to. Between 26 and 27 July 1969, they appeared at the [[Seattle Pop Festival]] held at Gold Creek Park. On 17 August, the band performed a breakthrough American appearance at [[Woodstock Festival|Woodstock]]; their rendition of "I'm Going Home" featuring Alvin Lee as lead singer/lead guitar, was featured in both the subsequent film and soundtrack album and catapulted them to star status.<ref name="AMG"/>  In 1970, Ten Years After released "[[Love Like a Man]]", the group's only hit in the [[UK Singles Chart]], where it peaked at #10.<ref name="British Hit Singles & Albums">{{Cite book

In 1971, the band switched labels to [[Columbia Records]] (US) and [[Chrysalis Records|Chrysalis]] (UK) and released the hit album ''[[A Space in Time]],'' which marked a move toward more commercial material.<ref name="AMG"/> It featured the group's biggest hit, "I'd Love to Change the World".<ref name="AMG"/> In late 1972, the group issued their second Columbia album ''[[Rock & Roll Music to the World]]'' and, in 1973, the live double album ''Ten Years After Recorded Live''. The band subsequently broke up after their final 1974 Columbia album, ''[[Positive Vibrations]]''.<ref name="AMG"/>

===Break up and reformation===
In the second half of the 1970s and early 1980s, Alvin actively toured with a new band he called Ten Years Later. The original Ten Years After reunited in 1983 to play the [[Reading and Leeds Festivals|Reading Festival]],<ref name="Guinness Rockopedia 2">{{Cite book

===Reunion, split with Alvin Lee and Leo Lyons: 1988–present===

In 1988, the members reunited for a few concerts and recorded the album ''[[About Time (Ten Years After album)|About Time]]'' (1989) with producer [[Terry Manning]] in [[Memphis, Tennessee|Memphis]].<ref name="Guinness Rockopedia 3"/><ref name="AMG"/> They stayed together for their longest continuous period, until 2003, however without releasing new studio effort. In 1994, they participated in the [[Sziget Festival|Eurowoodstock]] festival in [[Budapest]]. In 2003, the other band members replaced Alvin Lee with [[Joe Gooch]], and recorded the album, ''[[Now (Ten Years After album)|Now]]''.<ref name="AMG">{{cite web|first=William |last=Ruhlmann |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/ten-years-after-mn0000020050/biography |title=Ten Years After &#124; Biography |website=AllMusic |access-date=29 January 2014}}</ref> Material from the following tour was used for the 2005 double album, ''Roadworks''.<ref name="AMG"/> Alvin Lee mostly played and recorded under his own name following his split from the band. He died from complications during a routine medical procedure on 6 March 2013.<ref>{{cite web|first=Rob |last=Power |url=http://www.musicradar.com/news/guitars/ten-years-afters-alvin-lee-dies-571954 |title=Ten Years After's Alvin Lee dies |date=6 March 2013 |publisher=MusicRadar |access-date=15 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stereoboard.com/content/view/177754/9 |title=Ten Years After Singer And Guitarist Alvin Lee Dies Aged 68 |publisher=Stereoboard.Com |date=6 March 2013 |access-date=15 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mediabiz.de/musik/news/alvin-lee-von-ten-years-after-verstorben/330348 |title=MusikWoche &#124; News &#124; Alvin Lee von Ten Years After verstorben |publisher=Mediabiz.de |access-date=15 June 2013}}</ref> Ric Lee is also currently{{when|date=December 2020}} in a band called Ric Lee's Natural Born Swingers, along with Bob Hall. In January 2014, it was announced that both Gooch and Lyons had left Ten Years After.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.classicrockmagazine.com/news/ten-years-after-lose-frontman-and-bassist/ |title=Ten Years After lose frontman and bassist |work=Classic Rock Magazine |date=13 January 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140113192228/http://www.classicrockmagazine.com/news/ten-years-after-lose-frontman-and-bassist/ |archive-date=13 January 2014 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> Two months later, veteran bass player [[Colin Hodgkinson]] and singer/guitarist [[Marcus Bonfanti]] were announced as their replacements.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.classicrockmagazine.com/news/ten-years-after-reveal-new-lineup/ |title=Ten Years After reveal new line-up |work=Classic Rock Magazine |date=21 March 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140322043249/http://www.classicrockmagazine.com/news/ten-years-after-reveal-new-lineup/ |archive-date=22 March 2014 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> In October 2017, the band released its most recent studio album, ''A Sting in the Tale''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Ten-Years-After-A-Sting-In-The-Tale/release/10416460|title=Ten Years After - A Sting In The Tale|website=Discogs|access-date=1 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/a-sting-in-the-tale-mw0003049335/credits|title=A Sting in the Tale - Ten Years After - Credits - AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=1 August 2017}}</ref>
