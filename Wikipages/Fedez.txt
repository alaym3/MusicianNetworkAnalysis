'''Federico Leonardo Lucia''' (born 15 October 1989), better known by his stage name '''Fedez''' ({{IPA-it|ˈfɛːdets|lang}}), is an Italian rapper, influencer, singer and songwriter. In 2011, he released the albums ''Penisola che non c'è'' and ''Il mio primo disco da venduto'', released as free digital downloads. His first studio album, ''Sig. Brainwash – L'arte di accontentare'', was released in 2013, and it debuted at number one on the [[Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana|Italian Albums Chart]]. The album, which spawned three singles, including the top 10 hit "Cigno nero" featuring [[Francesca Michielin]], was later certified 3× Platinum by the [[Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana|Federation of the Italian Music Industry]]. As of [[September]] 2021, Fedez has 4 #1 studio albums and 23 top ten singles, 10 of which peaked at #1.

==Biography==
Federico Leonardo Lucia was born in [[Milan]] on 15 October 1989.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.corriere.it/spettacoli/13_marzo_16/miracolo_fedez_rapper_db875d38-8e03-11e2-8e0e-c5b76e411d4a.shtml|work=[[Corriere della Sera]]|language=it|access-date=7 July 2013|title=Il miracolo di Fedez rapper fai da te e re della classifica|author=Andrea Laffranchi|date=16 March 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.comingsoon.it/Musica/Artisti/?key=218&n=Fedez|publisher=Comingsoon.it|language=it|access-date=7 July 2013|title=Fedez. Biografia e Discografia}}</ref> Before moving to [[Rozzano]], he grew up between [[Buccinasco]] and [[Corsico]], in the [[province of Milan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rollingstonemagazine.it/musica/interviste-musica/intervista-a-fedez-rap-italiano/|work=[[Rolling Stone|Rolling Stone Italy]]|language=it|title=Il rap italiano ha bisogno di… Fedez! Ecco l'intervista|author=Paolo Madeddu|access-date=7 July 2013|date=14 March 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140202150151/http://www.rollingstonemagazine.it/musica/interviste-musica/intervista-a-fedez-rap-italiano/|archive-date=2 February 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>
He studied at the artistic [[lyceum]], but he dropped out in his junior year.

===Career===
In 2011, Fedez recorded his first self-produced album with a budget of only €500, ''Penisola che non c'è''.<ref name=RepubblicaPenisola>{{Cite web|url=http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2011/10/13/impegno-rap.html|work=[[la Repubblica]]|language=it|access-date=13 July 2013|title=Impegno Rap|author=Gloria Riva|date=13 October 2011}}</ref> The album, released as a free digital download, was entirely composed of songs about political and social issues.<ref name=RepubblicaPenisola/><ref name=RockitPenisola>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rockit.it/recensione/16400/fedez-penisola-che-non-ce|publisher=Rockit.it|language=it|access-date=29 June 2013|title=Fedez - Recensione - Penisola Che Non C'è | date=26 May 2011|author=Filippo Papetti}}</ref> 
Later in 2011, Fedez signed with the label Tanta Roba, founded by [[Guè Pequeno]] and DJ Harsh. The label produced his second free album, ''Il mio primo disco da venduto'', released as a digital download in November of the same year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rapburger.com/2011/11/30/fedez-fuori-il-mio-primo-disco-da-venduto-in-freedownload/|publisher=RapBurger.com|title=Fedez, fuori Il mio primo disco da venduto in freedownload|date=30 November 2011|language=it}}</ref>

In June 2012, Fedez appeared in a new version of [[883 (band)|883]]'s song "Jolly Blue", included on the album ''Hanno ucciso l'Uomo Ragno 2012'', recorded by the band's frontman [[Max Pezzali]] to celebrate the twentieth anniversary since the release of their first full-length album, ''Hanno ucciso l'Uomo Ragno''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rockol.it/recensione-4926/max-pezzali-hanno-ucciso-l-uomo-ragno-2012|publisher=Rockol.it|language=it|access-date=13 July 2013|title=Max Pezzali - Hanno ucciso l'Uomo Ragno 2012 - Recensione|author=Paola De Simone|date=12 June 2012}}</ref>

In January 2013, Fedez released his first single for a major label, "Si scrive schiavitù si legge libertà".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.earone.it/news/fedez_si_scrive_schiavitu_ma_si_legge_liberta_il_singolo_on_air_da_venerdi_11_gennaio_il_nuovo_album_a_marzo_5828913/|publisher=Earone.it|language=it|access-date=13 July 2013|title=Fedez - Si scrive schiavitù ma si legge libertà. Il singolo on air da venerdì 11 gennaio. Il nuovo album a marzo|author = Valentina Spada|date=9 January 2013}}</ref>
The song was later included in the album ''Sig. Brainwash - L'arte di accontentare'', released by [[Sony Music]] on 5 March 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www3.varesenews.it/musica/fedez-sceglie-varese-per-presentare-il-suo-nuovo-album-256875.html|work=Varese News|language=it|access-date=13 July 2013|title=Fedez sceglie Varese per presentare il suo nuovo album|author=Adelia Brigo|date=3 March 2013}}</ref> The album was also preceded by the top ten single "Cigno nero", performed with [[Francesca Michielin]], which was also certified platinum by the [[Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana|Federation of the Italian Music Industry]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ilmattino.it/napolismart/musica/il_cigno_nero_di_fedez/notizie/274185.shtml|work=[[Il Mattino]]|language=it|access-date=13 July 2013|title=Il Cigno nero di Fedez}}</ref><ref name=SinglesCertifications>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fimi.it/archivio_certificazioni.php |publisher=[[Federation of the Italian Music Industry]] |language=it |access-date=13 July 2013 |title=Archivio Certificazioni |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006100124/http://www.fimi.it/archivio_certificazioni.php |archive-date=October 6, 2014 }} ''Select ''Online'' certifications, Week ''--'', Year ''----'', and Artist ''Fedez'', then press ''Avvia la ricerca''.''</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://italiancharts.com/search.asp?search=Fedez&cat=s|publisher=Italiancharts.com. Hung Medien|title=Italian Charts - Fedez - Singles |access-date=13 July 2013}}</ref> The album debuted at number one on the [[Italian Albums Chart]], and it was later certified triple platinum denoting sales exceeding 180,000 units.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Fedez&titel=Fedez+presenta+Sig%2E+Brainwash+l%27arte+di+accontentare&cat=a|publisher=Italiancharts.com. Hung Medien|access-date=13 July 2013|title=Italian Charts - Fedez - Sig. Brainwash - l'arte di accontentare (album)}}</ref><ref name=AlbumsCertifications>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fimi.it/archivio_certificazioni.php |publisher=[[Federation of the Italian Music Industry]] |language=it |access-date=13 July 2013 |title=Archivio Certificazioni |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006100124/http://www.fimi.it/archivio_certificazioni.php |archive-date=October 6, 2014 }} ''Select certifications for ''Album e Compilation'', Week ''--'', Year ''----'', Artist ''Fedez'', then press ''Avvia la ricerca''.''</ref> To promote the album, Fedez embarked on a tour starting from 12 April 2013 in [[Turin]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cultura.panorama.it/musica/fedez-concerto-2013-brainwash-torino-scaletta-12-aprile|work=[[Panorama (magazine)|Panorama]]|access-date=13 July 2013|title=Fedez: il primo concerto del 2013|date=13 April 2013|author=Alessandro Alicandri|language=it}}</ref>
The album also spawned the single "Alfonso Signorini (eroe nazionale)", whose music video features Italian [[TV personality|TV personalities]] [[Alfonso Signorini]] and [[Raffaella Fico]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sorrisi.com/2013/06/14/fedez-e-alfonso-signorini-eroe-nazionale-il-video-del-nuovo-singolo-dallalbum-sig-brainwash/|work=[[TV Sorrisi e Canzoni]]|date=14 June 2013|title=Fedez e Alfonso Signorini (Eroe nazionale), il video del nuovo singlo da Sig. Brainwash|language=it|access-date=13 July 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130720044858/http://www.sorrisi.com/2013/06/14/fedez-e-alfonso-signorini-eroe-nazionale-il-video-del-nuovo-singolo-dallalbum-sig-brainwash/|archive-date=20 July 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In June 2013, Fedez was also a guest judge during the auditions of the [[X Factor (Italy series 7)|seventh season]] of the Italian talent show ''[[X Factor (Italy)|X Factor]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://xfactor.sky.it/2013/06/21/intervista-a-fedez/|publisher=[[Sky Italia]]|language=it|access-date=13 June 2013|date=21 June 2013|title=Intervista a Fedez}}</ref> In May 2013, it was announced that Fedez and [[Victoria Cabello]] would replace [[Elio]] and [[Simona Ventura]] as judges on the eighth season of the Italian version of ''[[X Factor (Italy)|X Factor]]'' alongside [[Morgan (singer)|Morgan]] and [[Mika (singer)|Mika]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sorrisi.com/2014/10/22/x-factor-intervista-fedez/|work=[[TV Sorrisi e Canzoni]]|language=it|date=22 October 2014|title=Fedez giudice di X Factor 8: l'intervista integrale|author=Francesco Chignola|access-date=25 October 2014|archive-date=25 October 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141025214120/http://www.sorrisi.com/2014/10/22/x-factor-intervista-fedez/}}</ref> His fourth studio album, ''Pop-Hoolista'', was released on 30 September 2014. Predeced by the single "Generazione boh", the record also includes duets with [[Malika Ayane]], [[Francesca Michielin]], [[Noemi (singer)|Noemi]], [[Elisa (Italian singer)|Elisa]] and [[J-Ax]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.panorama.it/musica/fedez-pop-hoolista-populista/|work=[[Panorama (Italian magazine)|Panorama]]|language=it|access-date=25 October 2014|title=Fedez è Pop-Hoolista: tutto sul nuovo album|author=Alessandro Alicandri|date=10 September 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141023174538/http://www.panorama.it/musica/fedez-pop-hoolista-populista/|archive-date=23 October 2014}}</ref> The album debuted atop the Italian Albums Chart, and it was certified gold one week after its release.<ref name=AlbumsCertifications/><ref name=ItaAlbumsPeakPos/>

In 2021, Fedez was one of the acts competing in the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2021|Sanremo Music Festival]]; dueting with long-time collaborator [[Francesca Michielin]], the pair performed their song "[[Chiamami per nome]]" (Call me by my name) and finished second.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-02|title=Sanremo 2021: la commozione di Fedez scioglie il pubblico social|url=https://www.vanityfair.it/music/concerti-eventi/2021/03/02/sanremo-2021-la-commozione-di-fedez-scioglie-il-pubblico-michielin-foto|access-date=2021-05-04|website=VanityFair.it|language=it}}</ref> In the same year, Fedez also conducted the program [[LOL - Chi ride è fuori]] toghether with [[Mara Maionchi]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-19|title=LOL: Chi ride è fuori - Uscito il trailer dello show comico di Prime Video|url=https://www.mam-e.it/spettacolo/lol-chi-ride-e-fuori-uscito-il-trailer-dello-show-comico-di-prime-video/|access-date=2021-04-07|website=MAM-e|language=it-IT}}</ref>

In June of the same year he released "''MILLE''", a collaboration with [[Achille Lauro]] and [[Orietta Berti]]; the song peaked at #1 in Italy, giving Fedez his tenth number one song.<ref>https://charts.billboard.it/hot-100-italia/WeekSelectForm</ref>

==Personal life==
Fedez proposed to [[Chiara Ferragni]], an Italian fashion blogger and entrepreneur, on 6 May 2017 during his concert in [[Verona]]. The concert and proposal were broadcast live on the Italian radio and TV channel RTL 102.5.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tF_3ch4GW8Q|title=Fedez dedicated a song to Chiara Ferragni to propose the blogger May 6, 2017 Arena di Verona Verona, Italy}}</ref> They have a son, Leone (born 19 March 2018), and a daughter, Vittoria (born 23 March 2021).<ref>https://www.harpersbazaar.com.au/celebrity/chiara-ferragni-welcomes-first-child-16040</ref> Fedez and Ferragni married in [[Noto]], Sicily on 1 September 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/chiara-ferragni-fedez-wedding-photos|title=The Blonde Salad's Wedding Was As Glam As Expected|last=Logan|first=Elizabeth|work=W Magazine|access-date=2018-09-01|language=en}}</ref>

==Activism==
In 2020, Fedez raised €3 million in 24 hours through a fundraiser with [[Chiara Ferragni]] to support the San Raffaele hospital in Milan during the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Italy]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Coronavirus, la raccolta fondi lanciata da Chiara Ferragni e Fedez raggiunge i 3 milioni in 24 ore: tutti i vip che hanno fatto donazioni |url=https://www.ilfattoquotidiano.it/2020/03/10/coronavirus-la-raccolta-fondi-lanciata-da-chiara-ferragni-e-fedez-raggiunge-i-3-milioni-in-24-ore-tutti-i-vip-che-hanno-fatto-donazioni/5731433/ |agency=Il fatto quotidiano |date=10 March 2020}}</ref>

Fedez is an avid supporter of [[LGBT]] rights. In 2021, right after a performance with [[Francesca Michielin]] at the First May Concert, the rapper interrupted the concert by reading a speech in defense of Ddl Zan, a proposed act to protect fragile groups from abuse (such as women, the disabled, and the LGBTQ+ community), reading homophobic quotes said by members of the Lega Nord faction, including [[Matteo Salvini]], {{ill|Simone Pillon|it|Simone Pillon}}, Giovanni De Paoli, {{ill|Andrea Ostellari|it|Andrea Ostellari}} and Alessandro Rinaldi.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2021-05-02|title=Italian rapper Fedez accuses state TV of censorship attempt|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-56966359|access-date=2021-05-04}}</ref> Fedez also spoke about freedom of expression, accusing the apex of [[Rai3]], Ilaria Capitani and Franco Di Mare, of having asked him to stop his speech because it was seen as "inappropriate in that context";<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-05-03|title=Fedez sparks RAI 'censorship' storm - English|url=https://www.ansa.it/english/news/politics/2021/05/03/fedez-sparks-rai-censorship-storm_5ecb6031-c4dd-445e-8eae-2e5a358bb313.html|access-date=2021-05-04|website=ANSA.it|language=en}}</ref> Rai3 denied the accusation of attempted censorship and Fedez published a phone call which, he states, shows the attempts of censorship by the network.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-05-03|title=State broadcaster in Italy under fire after 'censoring' rapper|url=http://www.theguardian.com/world/2021/may/03/state-broadcaster-in-italy-under-fire-after-censoring-rapper-fedez-rai|access-date=2021-05-04|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref>

==Endorsements==
Throughout his career, Fedez has endorsed multiple brands, including [[Puma AG|Puma]], [[Samsung]], [[Bershka]], [[Intimissimi]], [[Sisley]], [[Swarovski]] and [[Yamamay]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iodonna.it/moda/news/2021/05/17/fedez-yamamay-testimonial-costumi-da-bagno/|title=Fedez è il nuovo testimonial di Yamamay|date=17 May 2021}}</ref>
