'''Ricky Rydell Dillard''' (born February 25, 1965) is an American [[gospel music]]ian. He started his music career, in 1987, as a [[Chicago house]] musician. His first Christian music album was ''Promise'', by Muscle Shoals Records. His next album, ''A Holy Ghost Take-Over'', was released in 1993 by [[Malaco Records]]. The subsequent album, ''Hallelujah'', was released by them in 1995. Crystal Spring Records released, 1996's ''Work It Out'', 2000's ''No Limit'', and ''Unplugged'' in 2004. With [[Capitol Christian Music Group|EMI Gospel]], he released, ''7th Episode'', that came out in 2007. He released, ''Keep Living'', with [[Light Records]] in 2011. His next release, ''Amazing'', came out in 2014 with [[Entertainment One Music]].

All of his Christian albums charted on the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] [[Top Gospel Albums|Gospel Albums]] chart. His ''No Limit'' album charted on the [[Christian Albums]] chart, and the album ''Unplugged'' charted on the [[Independent Albums]] chart along with the releases ''Keep Living'', and ''Amazing'' that also charted on [[Billboard 200|The ''Billboard'' 200]]. He was nominated at the [[Grammy Award]]s twice, the first time in the [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary R&B Gospel Album|Best Contemporary R&B Gospel Album]] category at the [[34th Annual Grammy Awards]] for ''Promise'', and the second time would come at the [[57th Annual Grammy Awards]] in the category [[Grammy Award for Best Gospel Album|Best Gospel Album]] for ''Amazing''.

International Records, 1987) and ''As Always'' ([[Trax Records]], 1988).<ref name="discogs">[https://www.discogs.com/artist/27628-Ricky-Dillard Discography of Ricky Dillard] on [[Discogs]].</ref> He also worked with [[deep house]] pioneer [[Larry Heard]], who was the main producer of Dillard's first album, ''Let The Music Use You'', which was released on Jack Trax in 1989.<ref name="discogs" />

==Success==
Since his first gospel album ''Promise'' in 1991, all of his albums charted on the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] [[Top Gospel Albums|Gospel Albums]] chart. His ''No Limit'' album charted on the [[Christian Albums]] chart, and the album ''Unplugged'' charted on the [[Independent Albums]] chart along with the releases ''Keep Living'', and ''Amazing'' that also charted on [[Billboard 200|The ''Billboard'' 200]].

Ricky Dillard was nominated at the [[Grammy Award]]s twice, the first time in the [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary R&B Gospel Album|Best Contemporary R&B Gospel Album]] category at the [[34th Annual Grammy Awards]] in 1992 for ''Promise'', and the second time at the [[57th Annual Grammy Awards]] in 2015 in the category [[Grammy Award for Best Gospel Album|Best Gospel Album]] for ''Amazing''.

Also in 2015, he was nominated at the [[Stellar Awards|30th Stellar Awards]] in the following categories: Artist of the Year, Song of the Year, Male Vocalist of the Year, CD of the Year, Choir of the Year, Producer of the Year, Traditional Male Vocalist of the Year, Traditional CD of the Year, and Traditional Choir of the Year.<ref name="STELLAR">{{cite web|title=2015 N0MINEES: 30th Stellar Awards|url=http://www.thestellarawards.com/nominees.html|website=Thestellarawards.com|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref>
