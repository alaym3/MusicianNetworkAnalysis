'''Chelsea Emily'''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Production of "this is how you fall in love" // 30:40|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ADmTAi7L0UM|url-status=live}}</ref> '''Cutler''' (born February 11, 1997)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/chelseacutler/status/1227235603525316610|title=i'm 23!|last=cutler|first=chelsea|date=2020-02-11|website=@chelseacutler|access-date=2020-02-15}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter, and [[Record producer|producer]] from [[Westport, Connecticut]]. She released her debut studio album, ''How to Be Human'', in January 2020 through [[Republic Records]]. It peaked at number 23 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.

== Early life and education ==
Chelsea Cutler was born<ref name="R29">{{cite news | last=Rollings| first=Michaela| title =Meet The Next Generation Of Music Superstars| work=Refinery29| date =25 October 2017 | url =https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2017/10/175193/new-female-artists-to-watch| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> and raised in Westport, Connecticut.<ref name="Globe">{{cite news | last=Pennington| first=Juliet| title =Rosary beads in hand, this singer-songwriter is ready to hit the road| newspaper=Boston Globe| date =20 February 2020 | url =https://www.bostonglobe.com/2020/02/20/lifestyle/rosary-beads-hand-this-singer-songwriter-is-ready-hit-road/| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> She played acoustic instruments as a child and moved into electronic music production in high school.<ref name="OC">{{cite news | last=Amaral| first=Sarah| title =CHELSEA CUTLER'S RISE FROM SOCCER PLAYER TO SOUNDCLOUD STAR WAS ONLY THE BEGINNING| newspaper=OC Weekly| date =13 April 2018 | url =https://www.ocweekly.com/chelsea-cutler-went-from-a-soccer-player-to-a-soundcloud-star-2/| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> While still in high school in 2014, Cutler began posting covers and original songs on her SoundCloud.<ref name="Maine">{{cite news | last=Semmel| first=Sierra| title =Chelsea Cutler: the rise of an indie-pop icon| newspaper=Maine Campus| date =29 October 2018 | url =https://mainecampus.com/2018/10/chelsea-cutler-the-rise-of-an-indie-pop-icon/| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> She continued posting music to her SoundCloud while attending [[Amherst College]] in [[Massachusetts]], where she also played soccer with Amherst soccer legend Meredith Manley, who set the school record for red cards during her playing career.<ref name="OC"/> Cutler later decided to drop out of college to pursue music full-time after she was offered a support role on [[Quinn XCII]]'s national tour in the spring of 2018.<ref name="Bill2018">{{cite news | last=Engelman| first=Nicole| title =Chelsea Cutler On Her New Mixtape & Staying Grounded: 'Who You Are Isn't What You're Posting' Online| work=Billboard| date =13 November 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8484665/chelsea-cutler-sleeping-with-roses-ii-interview| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==
By 2017, several of Cutler's songs (including "Your Shirt") garnered numerous streams<ref name="OC"/> and led to collaborations that year with acts like [[Louis the Child (DJs)|Louis the Child]] ("Slow Down Love"), [[ayokay]] ("The Shine"),<ref name="Bill2018"/> Kidswaste ("Tonight"),<ref name="RTT">{{cite news | last=Farley| first=Jordan| title =Kidswaste And Manila Killa Team Up With Chelsea Cutler For Sparkling New Single "Tonight"| work=Run The Trap| date =8 November 2017 | url =https://runthetrap.com/2017/11/08/kidswaste-manila-killa-chelsea-cutler-tonight/| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> and [[Kasbo]] ("Found You").<ref name="BillKasbo">{{cite news | title =Kasbo & Chelsea Cutler Are Adorably Radiant on 'Found You': Premiere| work=Billboard| date =4 April 2017 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7751388/kasbo-chelsea-cutler-found-you| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> She signed a record deal with [[Ultra Records]] in late 2017 and released her first EP, ''Snow In October''.<ref name="OC"/> After touring with Quinn XCII in early 2018, Cutler released two independent mixtapes, ''Sleeping With Roses'' and ''Sleeping With Roses II''.<ref name="MTSU">{{cite news | title =REVIEW: CHELSEA CUTLER ACHIEVES BALANCE ON 'SLEEPING WITH ROSES II'| work=MTSU Sidelines| date =11 November 2018 | url =http://mtsusidelines.com/2018/11/review-chelsea-cutler-achieves-balance-on-sleeping-with-roses-ii/| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> She also went on her first two nationwide headlining tours in support of each mixtape, both of which sold out in advance.<ref name="Officiel">{{cite news | last=Cain| first=Felicity| title =Chelsea Cutler is Navigating Her Twenties Through Music| work=L'Officiel| date =17 January 2020 | url =https://www.lofficielusa.com/music/chelsea-cutler-new-album-how-to-be-human| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref>

In March 2019, she signed to Republic Records<ref name="Bill2020">{{cite news | title =Chelsea Cutler Breaks Down Her Vulnerable Debut Album 'How to Be Human': Exclusive| work=Billboard| date =17 January 2020 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8548247/chelsea-cutler-how-to-be-human-album-breakdown| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> and released a joint EP, ''brent'', with friend and label-mate [[Jeremy Zucker]] shortly after.<ref name="OTW">{{cite news | last=Arunarsirakul| first=Alissa| title =Jeremy Zucker & Chelsea Cutler Reflect on Fluctuating Relationships in Melancholic 'brent' EP| work=Ones to Watch| date =6 May 2019 | url =https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/jeremy-zucker-chelsea-cutler-reflect-on| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> The EP features the single "you were good to me", which they performed live on [[Today (American TV program)|''The Today Show'']].<ref name="OfficielZucker">{{cite news | last=Cain| first=Felicity| title =Jeremy Zucker is the 2020 Tumblr Boy That You Should Be Listening to Now| work=L'Officiel| date =16 April 2020 | url =https://www.lofficielusa.com/music/jeremy-zucker-debut-album-love-is-not-dying| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> In May 2019, Cutler collaborated with [[Kygo]] on the single, "[[Not OK (Kygo and Chelsea Cutler song)|Not OK]]".<ref name="BillKygo">{{cite news | title =Kygo & Chelsea Cutler Dance Away The Blues On 'Not Ok': Listen| work=Billboard| date =23 May 2019 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8512896/kygo-chelsea-cutler-not-ok| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> It became her third collaboration (after "Slow Down Love" and "The Shine") to appear on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart, peaking at number 9.<ref name="BillDance">{{cite news | title =Chelsea Cutler - Chart History - Hot Dance/Electronic Songs| work=Billboard| url =https://www.billboard.com/music/chelsea-cutler/chart-history/DAN| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref>

In late 2019, Cutler began releasing new singles, including "How To Be Human"<ref name="Bill2019">{{cite news | last=Aniftos| first=Rania| title =Chelsea Cutler Unveils New Single 'How to Be Human': Listen| work=Billboard| date =16 August 2019 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8527645/chelsea-cutler-how-to-be-human-listen| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> and "Lucky" (featuring Alexander 23).<ref name="StreamLucky">{{cite news | last=Casteel| first=Beth| title =Chelsea Cutler shares hopeful new track "Lucky"| work=Substream Magazine| date =1 November 2019 | url =https://substreammagazine.com/2019/11/chelsea-cutler-lucky/| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> Along with "Sad Tonight", the songs served as the primary singles for her debut studio album, ''How To Be Human'', released on January 17, 2020 by Republic Records.<ref name="Bill2020"/> On January 23, 2020 she performed the lead single, "Sad Tonight", on ''[[Late Night With Seth Meyers]]''.<ref name="BWW">{{cite news | title =VIDEO: Chelsea Cutler Performs 'Sad Tonight' on LATE NIGHT WITH SETH MEYERS| work=Broadway World| date =24 January 2020 | url =https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwtv/article/VIDEO-Chelsea-Cutler-Performs-Sad-Tonight-on-LATE-NIGHT-WITH-SETH-MEYERS-20200124| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> That song became Cutler's first entry on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Mainstream Top 40]] airplay chart, peaking at number 32 in March 2020.<ref name="Bill40">{{cite news | title =Chelsea Cutler - Chart History - Mainstream Top 40| work=Billboard| url =https://www.billboard.com/music/chelsea-cutler/chart-history/TFM| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref> In April 2020, she was featured alongside Quinn XCII on the Louis the Child track, "Little Things".<ref name="Sick">{{cite news | last=Thomas| first=Langston| title =Louis The Child Enlist Quinn XCII & Chelsea Cutler To Appreciate The "Little Things"| work=This Song is Sick| date =3 April 2020 | url =https://thissongissick.com/post/louis-the-child-little-things/| accessdate =23 July 2020}}</ref>

Cutler was also slated to perform at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] 2020,<ref name="coachella">{{cite web |title=Coachella Lineup 2020 |url=https://www.coachella.com/lineup#/artists/alphabetical |website=Coachella |accessdate=March 11, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170320040746/https://www.coachella.com/lineup/#/artists/alphabetical |archive-date=March 20, 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref> which was cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|author=Madeline Holcombe|title=Coachella and Stagecoach festivals canceled over concerns about coronavirus spread|url=https://www.cnn.com/2020/06/11/entertainment/coachella-stagecoach-cancelled-coronavirus/index.html|access-date=2020-07-22|website=CNN}}</ref> In June 2020, she released a version of her song "Crazier Things" which features [[Noah Kahan]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-29|title=Chelsea Cutler Taps Noah Kahan For "Crazier Things" Update|url=https://www.idolator.com/7912086/chelsea-cutler-taps-noah-kahan-for-crazier-things-update|access-date=2020-07-22|website=idolator}}</ref>
