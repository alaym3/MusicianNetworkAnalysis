'''Irving Ampofo Adjei'''<ref>https://www.umusicpub.com/us/Digital-Music-Library/album/104796/new-album</ref> (born 6 October 1994), known professionally as '''Headie One''', is a British rapper and singer. In 2018, he released the mixtape ''The One'', which included the single "Know Better", featuring rapper [[RV (rapper)|RV]], which became an "underground hit".<ref name="Clash">{{cite web|title=Slay In Their Lane: Nine Artists Who Will Dominate 2019|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/slay-in-their-lane-nine-artists-who-will-dominate-2019|last1=Brydon|first1=Grant|date=7 January 2019|website=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119122538/https://www.clashmusic.com/features/slay-in-their-lane-nine-artists-who-will-dominate-2019|archive-date=19 January 2019|access-date=13 January 2019}}</ref> He is a member of the [[UK drill]] group [[OFB (group)|OFB]].

Headie One is best known for his track "18Hunna" featuring rapper [[Dave (rapper)|Dave]], which peaked at number 6 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. He has released seven mixtapes, four solo mixtapes and three mixtapes with fellow OFB rapper [[RV (rapper)|RV]]. In 2019, Headie One released his seventh and first mainstream mixtape titled ''[[Music x Road]]'', which peaked at number 5 on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. He has since released ''[[Edna (album)|Edna]]'' which peaked at number one on the UK Albums Chart, an album which pays homage to his mother, Edna Adjei.<ref name="Headie One" /><ref name="Horner" />

== Early life ==
Adjei grew up in the [[Broadwater Farm]] [[Housing estate#United Kingdom and Ireland|housing estate]] in [[Tottenham]], London.<ref name="GQ" /><ref name="fact">{{cite web|last1=Thapar|first1=Ciaran|date=2 August 2018|title=Making the banned: MK The Plug and M1 On The Beat are UK drill's brightest hope|url=https://www.factmag.com/2018/08/02/mk-the-plug-and-m1-on-the-beat-are-uk-drills-brightest-hope/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190713230018/https://www.factmag.com/2018/08/02/mk-the-plug-and-m1-on-the-beat-are-uk-drills-brightest-hope/|archive-date=13 July 2019|access-date=18 January 2019|website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]]}}</ref> He is of [[Ghana]]ian origin and can speak [[Twi]].<ref>{{cite AV media  |title =Headie One Talks Uni Incident, New Music, Labels plus more [NFTR] |publisher=Not For The Radio |date=26 March 2018 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pg3BwISi9mM |access-date=18 January 2019 |medium=YouTube Video |time-caption=11:17}}</ref>

In his youth, he was "obsessed" with [[Association football|football]] and played for local team [[Haringey Borough F.C.]]<ref name="Davey">{{cite web |last1=Davey |first1=Jacob |title=VERSUS Jerseys: Headie One |url=https://versus.uk.com/2018/09/versus-jerseys-headie-one/ |website=Versus |access-date=25 August 2019 |date=17 September 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190825225841/https://versus.uk.com/2018/09/versus-jerseys-headie-one/ |archive-date=25 August 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> He was due to have a trial with [[Stevenage F.C.]] before an [[ankle fracture]] led to his exit from the sport.<ref name="Davey" /> Adjei is an avid supporter of [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Smyth |first1=David |title=Headie One: 'If I just stuck to drill, I'd be holding back my talents' |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/go/london/music/headie-one-interview-o2-academy-brixton-a4276211.html |website=Evening Standard |access-date=17 October 2020 |date=1 November 2019}}</ref>

He first began rapping in his early teens, though only began focusing on it after his first release from prison.<ref name=words>{{cite web |last1=Patterson |first1=Joseph JP |title=Headie One, Man Of Few Words, Lets His Bars Do All The Talking |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/11/headie-one-interview |website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |access-date=13 January 2019 |date=15 November 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119121104/https://www.complex.com/music/2018/11/headie-one-interview |archive-date=19 January 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> His moniker derives from a childhood nickname due to his head's resemblance to a [[Fifty pence (British coin)|50p coin]].<ref name="GQ" />

== Career ==
Adjei began releasing music in the early 2010s under the name Headz, including as part of a group named [[Star Gang]].<ref name="AllMusic" /><ref>{{cite AV media |title=Star Gang – Youngers From Farm Ft. Young RV and Instagram as 247_tt
|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eIpATMs-dHU |publisher=ColdCashMG |date=15 January 2011 |access-date=18 January 2018 |medium=YouTube Video |time-caption=0:00 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190428191015/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eIpATMs-dHU |archive-date=28 April 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> In 2014, he released his only [[mixtape]] under that alias, titled ''Headz or Tails''.<ref>{{cite tweet |author=Headie One |user=headieone |number=450333678640857089 |date=30 March 2014 |title=Download my debut mixtape 'Headz or Tails' !!! http://mixtapemadness.co.uk/Headz/HeadzOrTails/1794}}</ref> Also part of Star Gang was fellow Tottenham rapper [[RV (rapper)|RV]] – he and Headie One became frequent collaborators, including co-releasing the mixtapes ''Sticks & Stones'' (2016) and ''Drillers x Trappers'' (2017).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Daly |first1=Rhian |title=Watch RV and Headie One's football-themed video for 'Match Day' |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/rv-headie-one-football-themed-video-match-day-2465089 |website=[[NME]] |access-date=9 April 2019 |date=21 March 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190324030654/https://www.nme.com/news/music/rv-headie-one-football-themed-video-match-day-2465089 |archive-date=24 March 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> Together they formed part of [[OFB (group)|OFB]], a [[UK drill]] group based in the [[Broadwater Farm estate]].<ref name="fact" />

In February 2018, he released his next solo mixtape as Headie One, titled ''The One''. This included "Know Better" with [[RV (rapper)|RV]], which became an "underground hit".<ref name="Clash" /> This was his first release that was signed to [[Relentless Records]], a [[subsidiary]] of [[Sony Music]].<ref name="big read">{{cite web |last1=Bassett |first1=Jordan |title=The Big Read – Headie One: "If you make a mistake, you've got to move quickly" |url=https://www.nme.com/big-read-headie-one-make-mistake-youve-got-move-quickly |website=[[NME]] |date=18 April 2019 |access-date=7 September 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190809071518/https://www.nme.com/big-read-headie-one-make-mistake-youve-got-move-quickly |archive-date=9 August 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>
In May, he was featured on "Missing", a Belly Squad track that peaked at number 84 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Belly Squad ft Headie One |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/53499/belly-squad-ft-headie-one/ |website=[[Official Charts Company]] |access-date=13 January 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119121012/https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/53499/belly-squad-ft-headie-one/ |archive-date=19 January 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> This was followed by another solo mixtape, ''The One Two'', in June 2018, which entered the [[UK Albums Chart]] at number 32.<ref>{{cite web |title=The One Two |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/search/albums/the-one-two/ |website=[[Official Charts Company]] |access-date=18 January 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119182750/https://www.officialcharts.com/search/albums/the-one-two/ |archive-date=19 January 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> In November, he performed on his first national UK tour.<ref name="feelgood">{{cite web |last1=Simpson |first1=Dave |title=Headie One review – a feelgood celebration despite the bleak lyrics |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/nov/05/headie-one-review-gorilla-manchester-drill-mc |website=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=7 September 2019 |date=5 November 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190825225900/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/nov/05/headie-one-review-gorilla-manchester-drill-mc |archive-date=25 August 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In January 2019, his single "18Hunna", featuring Dave, entered the UK chart at number 6 – the highest a drill artist ever charted prior to [[Body (Russ Millions and Tion Wayne song)|"Body"]] by [[Russ Millions]] and [[Tion Wayne]].<ref name="Headie One">{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/52982/headie-one/|title=Headie One|website=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=30 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119121614/https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/52982/headie-one/|archive-date=19 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="Top40">{{cite web |title=Who is Headie One and what is the '18 Hunna' challenge? |url=https://www.bigtop40.com/features/headie-one-18-hunna-challenge-age-instagram/ |website=[[The Official Big Top 40]] |access-date=18 January 2019 |date=15 January 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119121104/https://www.bigtop40.com/features/headie-one-18-hunna-challenge-age-instagram/ |archive-date=19 January 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> In March, Adjei released his sixth mixtape, and third with RV, ''Drillers x Trappers II''.<ref>{{cite web |last=Oke |first=Tobi |title=Headie One And RV Return With 'Drillers x Trappers II' Mixtape |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/03/headie-one-rv-dxt-ii |website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |date=22 March 2019 |access-date=26 March 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190326125431/https://www.complex.com/music/2019/03/headie-one-rv-dxt-ii |archive-date=26 March 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> It entered the [[UK Albums Chart]] at number 21.<ref>{{cite web |title=DRILLERS AND TRAPPERS 2 |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/search/albums/drillers-and-trappers-2/ |website=[[Official Charts Company]] |access-date=30 March 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190330143441/https://www.officialcharts.com/search/albums/drillers-and-trappers-2/ |archive-date=30 March 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> "Match Day", the first single from the mixtape, entered the Singles Chart at number 86.<ref name="Headie One" /> In July, he featured on the "I Spy" single by [[Krept and Konan]], which peaked at number 18.<ref>{{cite web |title=KREPT/KONAN/HEADIE ONE/K-TRAP |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/55960/krept-konan-headie-one-k-trap/ |website=[[Official Charts Company]] |access-date=25 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190825225839/https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/55960/krept-konan-headie-one-k-trap/ |archive-date=25 August 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In August 2019, Adjei released his seventh mixtape in five years, ''[[Music x Road]]''.<ref name="Horner">{{cite web |last1=Horner |first1=Al |title=Headie One: Music X Road review – UK drill kingpin gets introspective |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/aug/23/headie-one-music-x-road-review |website=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=25 August 2019 |date=23 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190825032038/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/aug/23/headie-one-music-x-road-review |archive-date=25 August 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> The release was praised by critics,<ref name="Horner" /><ref name="Bassett">{{cite web |last1=Bassett |first1=Jordan |title=Headie One – 'Music x Road' review |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/headie-one-music-x-road-review |website=[[NME]] |date=22 August 2019 |access-date=25 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190823004717/https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/headie-one-music-x-road-review |archive-date=23 August 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Fletcher |first1=Harry |title=Headie One: Music X Road review – Drill meets dancehall, and Headie's ahead of the game |url=https://www.standard.co.uk/go/london/music/headie-one-music-x-road-album-review-a4219901.html |website=[[Evening Standard]] |access-date=25 August 2019 |date=23 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190825225839/https://www.standard.co.uk/go/london/music/headie-one-music-x-road-album-review-a4219901.html |archive-date=25 August 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> and was noted for being a move away from his usual sound – with ''[[NME]]'' describing it as being "often far from drill".<ref name="Bassett" /> It entered the UK Albums Chart at number 5 – his highest chart placing to date.<ref name="Headie One" /> Other than the previously-released "18Hunna", several tracks from the mixtape also charted, such as "Back to Basics" with [[Skepta]], "Rubbery Bandz" and "Both".<ref name="Headie One" />

In December 2019, he featured on [[Stormzy]]'s new album ''[[Heavy Is the Head (album)|Heavy Is the Head]]'' on the song "Audacity".

In July 2020, he released a single featuring [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] called "[[Only You Freestyle]]" produced by M1onthebeat.

In October 2020, his debut album ''Edna'' topped the UK charts.<ref>{{Cite news|date=16 October 2020|title=Headie One tops album chart with debut album, Edna|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-54575190|access-date=16 October 2020}}</ref> The album was dedicated to his late mother of the same name. It has garnered plenty of praise within the music world. ''[[NME]]'' praised the "melancholy, minor-key trap beats requisite of in-vogue modern hip-hop",<ref>{{Cite web|date=8 October 2020|title=Headie One – 'Edna' album review|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/headie-one-edna-review-2776222|access-date=16 October 2020|website=NME Music News, Reviews, Videos, Galleries, Tickets and Blogs {{!}} NME.COM|language=en-GB}}</ref> while ''[[The Times]]'' described Adjei as the "breakout star of drill".<ref>{{Cite news|last=Hodgkinson|first=Will|title=Headie One: Edna review — authentic vision of life on the margins|newspaper=[[The Times]]|language=en|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/headie-one-edna-review-authentic-vision-of-life-on-the-margins-9kz6fv09c|access-date=16 October 2020|issn=0140-0460}}</ref>

== Controversies ==
As a teenager, Adjei had been imprisoned three times,<ref name="GQ">{{cite web |last1=Halls |first1=Eleanor |title=Is drill the most controversial genre of music? |url=https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/article/drill-music-controversy-headie-one-harlem-spartans |website=[[GQ]] |access-date=13 January 2019 |date=14 July 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191104111045/https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/article/drill-music-controversy-headie-one-harlem-spartans |archive-date=4 November 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name=words /> including for dealing [[crack cocaine]] and [[heroin]], most recently in 2014.<ref name="big read" /><ref name="times">{{cite news|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/sports-brands-linked-to-crack-den-party-5rc8r69xz|title=Puma and JD Sports linked to 'crack den' party|last1=Simpson|first1=John|last2=McStay|first2=Josh|date=12 April 2018|website=[[The Times]]|access-date=9 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190331192800/https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/sports-brands-linked-to-crack-den-party-5rc8r69xz|archive-date=31 March 2019|url-status=live}}{{subscription required|s}}</ref>

In January 2018, he was reportedly attacked by rivals at the [[University of Bedfordshire]].<ref name="GQ" /><ref name="feelgood" /> A video of the assault was posted on [[Snapchat]] and then [[YouTube]], resulting in an "all-out gang war".<ref name="times" /> Headie responded with a [[Diss (music)|diss track]] "Know Better".<ref name="GQ" />

In March of that year, his concert at the [[Barbican Centre]] was shut down by the [[Metropolitan Police]].<ref name="GQ" /> The police had previously "singled out" his music in connection with rising knife crime in London.<ref name="Sky">{{cite web |last1=Cotter |first1=Lucy |title=Drill star says 'only a fool would link music to wave of violent crime' |url=https://news.sky.com/story/drill-star-says-only-a-fool-would-link-music-to-wave-of-violent-crime-11331911 |website=[[Sky News]] |access-date=13 January 2019 |date=15 April 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119174213/https://news.sky.com/story/drill-star-says-only-a-fool-would-link-music-to-wave-of-violent-crime-11331911 |archive-date=19 January 2019 |url-status=live }}</ref> In an interview in April, Headie claimed that "Only a fool would say that drill music is the root of the problem of violence in the capital."<ref name="Sky" /> Associations between London gang culture ("the war between [[Tottenham]] and [[Wood Green]]"), drill music and social media were examined in a [[BBC Radio Four]] programme ''File on Four'' 8 March 2020, focused on three violent murders.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/m000fvw2 |title=BBC Radio 4 – File on 4, Taking the Rap |website=Bbc.co.uk |access-date=21 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=3 March 2020|title=The rapper's track that sparked a wave of killings|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/stories-51722066|access-date=28 July 2020}}</ref>

On 8 January 2020, Adjei was sentenced to six months for possession of a knife and imprisoned for a fourth time.<ref name="knife" /> He was released early in April 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/headie-one-has-been-released-from-prison-2640432 |title=Headie One has been released from prison |date=3 April 2020 |first=Nick |last=Reilly |work=[[NME]] |access-date=21 July 2020}}</ref>

On 17 November 2020, Adjei was involved in an altercation with rapper [[Tion Wayne]] on board a flight from Dubai to London shortly after boarding.<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 November 2020|title=Headie One and Tion Wayne in 'altercation' on Dubai flight – reports say|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/headie-one-tion-wayne-fight-dubai-video-b1724333.html|access-date=18 November 2020|website=The Independent|language=en}}</ref>
