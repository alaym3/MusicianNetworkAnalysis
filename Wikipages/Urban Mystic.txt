'''Brandon Oneal Williams''',<ref>https://www.tiktok.com/@urbanmystic1</ref> (born November 30, 1984), better known by his stage name '''Urban Mystic''', is an [[United States|American]] recording artist from [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida]], United States. He is best known for his hit singles, "Where Were You" and "I Refuse". Mystic inked a record deal with indie label [[SoBe Entertainment]] and released his debut album ''Ghetto Revelations'' in 2004.

His second album ''Ghetto Revelations II'' was released in 2006. His third album ''GRIII: Old School 2 Nu Skool'' was released in 2009. His fourth album ''Ghetto Revelations IV: Love Intervention'' was released in 2013. His fifth album ''Soulful Classics'' was released in 2015.

== Early life ==
Williams was born the son of a church minister in 1984 in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, where he was also raised. In his early years, he developed an interest for music at his father's church.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/urban-mystic|title=Urban Mystic|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> He attended [[Dillard High School]] in [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida|Fort Lauderdale]] where he sang at every pep rally, show and event that was held in order to get noticed. He even performed at the local "Sistrunk Historical Festival" held every year in honor of Dr. James Sistrunk, one of the first African-American Physicians in [[Broward County, Florida]]. He was looking forward to pursuing a musical career with support from his brother Christopher. He grew more familiar with the music industry and became active as songwriter and producer.

== Career ==
In 2003, Williams gave himself the moniker ''Urban Mystic'' which reflects both his street mentality and his singing talent. In 2004, Williams entered into a record deal with [[SoBe Entertainment]]. His debut album, ''Ghetto Revelations'',<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/urban-mystic|title=Urban Mystic Chart History|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> released on November 30, 2004, included the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[R&B]] hit single "Where Were You", produced by [[KayGee]]. He also had worked with [[Red Spyda]] and [[El DeBarge]] on the album. The first single from his second album, "It's You", was released in 2005 and featured [[Paul Wall]]. In the same year, Williams joined the newfangled ''Spitkicker'' collective.

His second studio album, ''Ghetto Revelations II'' was released on March 21, 2006 and produced by [[Scott Storch]], [[KayGee]] and Terence "Tramp-Baby" Abney. It reached #10 in the [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers albums]] chart.<ref name="auto" /> The second single, "I Refuse", produced by [[Scott Storch]] and released in 2006, reached #36 in the Billboard [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart.

His third studio album, titled ''GRIII: Old School 2 Nu Skool'',<ref name="auto" /> was released in April 2009. The album featured a genre mix of [[Neo soul]] and [[Contemporary R&B]]. His fourth studio album entitled ''Love Intervention'', was released on February 26, 2013 via digital outlets.<ref name="release date">{{Cite web |url=https://www.amazon.com/Love-Intervention-Explicit/dp/B00B115RYQ |title=Archived copy |access-date=September 17, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130318043858/http://www.amazon.com/Love-Intervention-Explicit/dp/B00B115RYQ |archive-date=March 18, 2013 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref> The first single off the album is called "In the Morning" featuring producer MDMA, it was released for a [[music download|digital download]] on February 23, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/in-the-morning-feat-mdma-single/630351449|title=In the Morning (feat. MDMA) - Single by Urban Mystic|access-date=February 3, 2019|website=Itunes.apple.com}}</ref> The second single "Name On It", it was released on April 3, 2012 via [[iTunes Store|iTunes]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/name-on-it-single/id512033625|title=Itunes - Urban Mystic - Name On It - Single| publisher=[[Apple Inc.]] |date=2012-04-03}}</ref> The third single "I Promise" released on October 9, 2012 on [[iTunes Store|iTunes]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/i-promise-single/569157576|title=I Promise - Single by Urban Mystic|access-date=February 3, 2019|website=Itunes.apple.com}}</ref> ''Love Intervention'' was released for physical retailers on April 16, 2013<ref name="release date"/><ref name="PR">{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Love-Intervention-Urban-Mystic/dp/B00BKBCEA2/ref=sr_1_7?ie=UTF8&qid=1364182402&sr=8-7&keywords=Urban+Mystic|title=Urban Mystic - Love Intervention [Explicit] - Amazon.com Music|website=Amazon.com|access-date=February 3, 2019}}</ref>

Mystic released his fifth album ''Soulful Classics'' on July 31, 2015. It was preceded by the release of the singles "No Matter How High" and "Feel Good".

Mystic parted ways with [[SoBe Entertainment]] in early 2017.{{citation needed|date=July 2017}} and is currently signed to his own label Urban Life Music Group while planning on the release of his upcoming sixth album ''Underrated''.
