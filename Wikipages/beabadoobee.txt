From 2018 to 2019, she released five extended plays under the independent label [[Dirty Hit]]: ''Lice'' (2018), ''Patched Up'' (2018), ''Loveworm'' (2019), [[Space Cadet (EP)|''Space Cadet'']] (2019) and ''Our Extended Play'' (2021). Her debut studio album, ''[[Fake It Flowers]]'', was released in October 2020 and received critical acclaim.

Beabadoobee served as a supporting act for labelmates [[the 1975]] during several legs of their [[Music for Cars Tour]], as well as American singer [[Clairo]] during her [[Immunity Tour|''Immunity'' Tour]]. She was nominated for the [[Rising Star (Brit Award)|Rising Star Award]] at the [[2020 Brit Awards]], and was presented with the Radar Award at the 2020 [[NME Awards]]. Beabadoobee was also predicted as a breakthrough act for 2020 in an annual [[BBC]] poll of music critics, [[Sound of 2020]].

== Early life ==
Laus was born in [[Iloilo City]], in the Philippines on 3 June 2000 and moved to [[London]] with her parents at the age of 3.<ref name="NME1" /><ref name="Vice1" /> She grew up in [[West London]] listening to [[Music of the Philippines#Popular music (original Filipino music)|original Pinoy music]] as well as [[Pop music|pop]] and [[Rock music|rock]] music from the 1980s. While she was a teenager, she listened to [[indie rock]] including [[Karen O]], [[Yeah Yeah Yeahs]], [[Florist (band)|Florist]] and [[Alex G]].<ref name="AllMusic" /> She was expelled from [[Sacred Heart High School, Hammersmith|Sacred Heart High School]] before completing her [[Year Thirteen|thirteenth year]] at [[Hammersmith Academy]].<ref name="Smyth" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Cliff|first=Aimee|date=17 March 2020|title=Beabadoobee wants her songs to hit you like a rock|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/48382/1/beabadoobee-interview|access-date=13 July 2020|website=[[Dazed]]|language=en|issn=0961-9704}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Rising Star, Beabadoobee, Films Music Video At Hammersmith Academy|url=https://www.hammersmithacademy.org/news/rising-star-beabadoobee-films-music-video-at-hammersmith-academy/|access-date=13 July 2020|website=[[Hammersmith Academy]]|language=en-GB}}</ref> Laus spent seven years learning to play the violin, before getting her first guitar second-hand at the age of 17.<ref name="Guardian" /> She taught herself how to play the instrument using [[YouTube]] tutorials.<ref name="AllMusic" /> She was inspired by [[Kimya Dawson]] and the [[Juno (soundtrack)|''Juno'' soundtrack]] to start making music.<ref name="Vice1" />

=== 2020–present: "Death Bed", ''Fake It Flowers'' and ''Our Extended Play'' ===
In February 2020, Beabadoobee performed at the 2020 [[NME Awards]] after winning the Radar Award.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Morgan Britton|first=Luke|date=12 February 2020|title=Beabadoobee wins NME Radar Award at NME Awards 2020|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/beabadoobee-wins-nme-radar-award-nme-awards-2020-2608039|access-date=10 September 2020|website=[[NME]]}}</ref> She supported labelmates [[The 1975]] on their [[Music for Cars Tour]] for both the UK leg in February 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/beabadoobee-support-1975-uk-arena-tour-2569750|title=Beabadoobee to support The 1975 on UK arena tour|last=Reilly|first=Nick|date=2019-11-20|website=NME|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-24}}</ref> She was scheduled to also support the band during the North American leg of this tour in April 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/the-1975-announce-massive-run-of-north-american-shows-with-phoebe-bridgers-and-beabadoobee-2597730|title=The 1975 announce massive run of North American shows with Phoebe Bridgers and Beabadoobee|last=Clarke|first=Patrick|date=16 January 2020|website=[[NME]]|language=en-GB|access-date=18 January 2020}}</ref> however it was postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Greenwood|first=Koltan|date=18 March 2020|title=The 1975 postpone upcoming North American tour amid coronavirus|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/the-1975-postpone-upcoming-tour-due-to-coronavirus/|access-date=15 July 2020|website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]|language=en-US}}</ref>

A sample of Beabadoobee's 2017 debut single "Coffee" was used on Canadian rapper [[Powfu]]'s 2019 single, "[[Death Bed (Coffee for Your Head)]]". The song became a massively successful [[sleeper hit]] after going viral on the app [[TikTok]] in early 2020,<ref>{{Cite news|last=Kinos-Goodin|first=Jesse|date=11 June 2020|title=How a song goes viral: Powfu on 'Death Bed' and its steady rise to the top|work=[[CBC News]]|url=https://www.cbc.ca/music/how-a-song-goes-viral-powfu-on-death-bed-and-its-steady-rise-to-the-top-1.5607847|access-date=15 July 2020}}</ref> becoming Beabadoobee's first official chart entry in her career, both locally and internationally.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Zellner|first=Xander|date=24 March 2020|title=Powfu & Beabadoobee Each Earn First Hot 100 Entry With 'Death Bed'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9341476/death-bed-no-71-hot-100|access-date=15 July 2020|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> By April 2020, it had entered the Top 5 in several countries including the UK, Australia and New Zealand. It earned gold [[Music recording certification|certification]] status in Belgium, Canada, France, Mexico and New Zealand, as well as Platinum or higher in the US and the UK among several other countries. Speaking about her reaction to the popularization of "Death Bed", Beabadoobee said, "I'm not going to lie, it was overwhelming… I kinda hated it. I hated more people knowing about the first song I'd ever written and not my others. I was so stubborn but I grew into it and accepted that's just how life works. I was extremely grateful for its existence and it's only given me more opportunities."<ref>{{Cite web|date=19 October 2020|title=Beabadboobee: Far From the Middle|url=https://basementapproved.com/music/beabadboobee-far-from-the-middle/|access-date=20 October 2020|website=The Basement}}</ref>

release|url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/news/music/beabadoobee-shares-new-single-together-ahead-of-fridays-debut-album-release-2783446|access-date=14 October 2020|website=[[NME]]}}</ref> The album was released on 16 October 2020 to critical acclaim and debuted at number 8 on the [[UK Albums Chart]] in addition to peaking in the United States, Australia, Ireland, Japan and Scotland. According to sales in the United States, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' ranked Beabadoobee as the Top New Rock Artist of 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020|title=Top New Rock Artists - Year-End|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/year-end/2020/top-new-rock-artists|access-date=December 7, 2020|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> In 2021, Beabadoobee embarked on a headlining tour of the United Kingdom and Ireland to support the album.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Moore|first=Sam|date=7 September 2020|title=Beabadoobee announces 2021 UK and Ireland tour|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/beabadoobee-announces-2021-uk-and-ireland-tour-tickets-2746896|access-date=8 September 2020|website=[[NME]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Yeung|first=Vivian|date=7 September 2020|title=Beabadoobee announces UK and Ireland tour for 2021|url=https://crackmagazine.net/2020/09/beabadoobee-uk-ireland-tour/|access-date=8 September 2020|website=[[Crack (magazine)|Crack]]}}</ref>

Beabadoobee released her new single "Last Day On Earth" on the 24th of March, produced and co-written by Matty Healy and George Daniel of The 1975.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/beabadoobee-new-song-ep-the-1975-last-day-on-earth-listen-2907211|title=Beabadoobee shares new track ‘Last Day On Earth’ and announces ‘Our Extended Play’ EP}}</ref> The artist announced that the single is taken from her new EP ''[[Our Extended Play]]'', which she claimed that wrote it with her labelmates, "on the countryside". ''"I wanted to experiment on the sounds and sonics even more and the EP to me has a feeling of togetherness to it… how we’re all in this joined as one."'' <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/beabadoobee-announces-1975-produced-ep-shares-new-song-last-day-on-earth-listen/|title=beabadoobee Announces 1975-Produced EP, Shares New Song “Last Day on Earth"}}</ref>

== Artistry and reception ==
Beabadoobee has cited [[Elliott Smith]], [[The Moldy Peaches]], [[Pavement (band)|Pavement]], [[Mazzy Star]], [[The Beatles]], [[Simon & Garfunkel|Simon and Garfunkel]] and [[Daniel Johnston]] as her musical influences.<ref name="i-D1" /><ref name="Vice1" /> She has told ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]'' that she plans on making film soundtracks in the future, as they heavily inspired her to make music.<ref name="Vice1" /> On her background and using [[YouTube]] to find success, she said:<ref>{{Cite web|title=5 Questions With Beabadoobee|url=https://headlinermagazine.net/headliners/5-questions-with-beabadoobee.html|date=12 May 2020|website=Headliner|access-date=18 May 2020}}{{Dead link|date=March 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

{{Quote|text="My very traditional Asian family had the classic way of thinking: ‘play an orchestra instrument’ or ‘be a doctor’. Today, people start off making beats on a laptop, but hopefully I encourage young people to pick up the guitar and rock out! YouTube tutorials are a great way to develop your own style, and go at your own pace."}}

[[Taylor Swift]], [[Harry Styles]], [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]], [[Jaden Smith]] and [[Matty Healy]] of [[The 1975]] have each expressed their admiration for Beabadoobee.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Daly|first=Rhian|date=16 October 2020|title=On The Cover – Beabadoobee: "I wear my heart on my sleeve with this album"|url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/big-reads/beabadoobee-cover-interview-2020-fake-it-flowers-2787264|access-date=16 October 2020|website=[[NME]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Welsh|first=April Clare|date=19 June 2020|title=Cover story: The age of Beabadoobee|url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/long-reads/the-age-of-beabadoobee/|access-date=15 July 2020|website=[[Crack Magazine]]}}</ref><ref name="Khalid">{{Cite tweet|number=1129512170784530434|author=[[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] [confirmed account]|user=thegreatkhalid|title=if you wanna look out the window and create your own music video listen to this :(|date=18 May 2019|access-date=21 November 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191121144013/https://twitter.com/thegreatkhalid/status/1129512170784530434|archive-date=21 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-50098938|title=Beabadoobee: Meet Matty Healy's favourite singer|last=[[Newsbeat]]|date=24 October 2019|work=[[BBC News]]|access-date=19 November 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191024115209/https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-50098938|archive-date=24 October 2019|language=en-GB}}</ref>
