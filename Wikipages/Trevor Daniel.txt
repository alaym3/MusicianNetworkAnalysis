'''Trevor  Lyn Daniel''' (born December 8, 1994) is an [[American football]] [[Punter (football)|punter]] who is a free agent. He played [[college football]] at [[Tennessee Volunteers football|Tennessee]].

==Early years==
Daniel was raised in [[Dickson, Tennessee]].<ref name="sess" /> He  watched Tennessee Volunteer football games with his grandfather leading to his desire as a child to play for them.<ref name="sess" />

==College career==
Daniel was a punter for the [[Tennessee Volunteers football|Tennessee]] Volunteers in college.<ref name="knxnw" /> In the 2015 season, Daniel executed a punt late in the rivalry game against [[2015 Georgia Bulldogs football|Georgia]] that helped set up the Vols' first victory over the Bulldogs in several years. The punt went out of bounds on the Bulldogs' half-yard line.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/sports/2015/10/10/vols-down-2-offensive-linemen-during-game-vs-georgia/73728474/ |title=Vols' punter pins Georgia on 1-yard line late |work=The Tennessean |access-date=October 5, 2018 |language=en}}</ref> In his four years with the Volunteers, Daniel's average punting average was fifth in the nation.<ref name="sess" />

Daniel was the Ray Guy Punter of the Week in early September 2017.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/sports/high-school/2017/09/06/dickson-county-native-trevor-daniel-named-ray-guy-punter-week-tennessee/636862001/ |title=Dickson County native Trevor Daniel named Ray Guy punter of the week for Tennessee |website=The Tennessean |publisher=The Tennessean |access-date=September 1, 2018}}</ref>
He finished the 2017 season ranked 2nd nationally with a 95.99 GPR Punt Rating.

==Professional career==
===Houston Texans===
Daniel was signed by the [[Houston Texans]] as an undrafted free agent in 2018.<ref name="knxnw" /> Daniel won the punting job in 2018, beating out 18-year NFL veteran [[Shane Lechler]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.houstonchronicle.com/sports/texas-sports-nation/texans/article/McClain-Shane-Lechler-didn-t-lose-his-job-13196546.php |title=McClain: Shane Lechler didn't lose his job - Trevor Daniel won it |date=August 31, 2018 |work=HoustonChronicle.com |access-date=October 5, 2018 |language=en-US}}</ref> He made his NFL debut in the Texans' season opener against the [[2018 New England Patriots|New England Patriots]]. He had six punts for 230 net yards in the 27–20 loss.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201809090nwe.htm |title=Houston Texans at New England Patriots - September 9th, 2018 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en |access-date=October 5, 2018}}</ref> In the 2018 season, he had 74 punts for 3,237 net yards for a 43.74 average.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/D/DaniTr00/gamelog/2018/ |title=Trevor Daniel 2018 Game Log |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en |access-date=December 1, 2019}}</ref>

On September 17, 2019, Daniel was waived by the Texans.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.chron.com/sports/texans/article/Texans-sign-Bryan-Anger-cut-Trevor-Daniel-14447585.php |title=Texans switch punters: sign Bryan Anger, cut Trevor Daniel |last=Wilson |first=Aaron |date=September 17, 2019 |work=HoustonChronicle.com |access-date=September 17, 2019 |language=en-US}}</ref>

===Denver Broncos===
On December 31, 2019, Daniel signed a reserve/future contract with the [[Denver Broncos]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Broncos sign 15 players to future contracts, claim NT Joel Heath off waivers|url=https://www.denverbroncos.com/news/broncos-sign-15-players-to-future-contracts-claim-nt-joel-heath-off-waivers|website=DenverBroncos.com|date=December 31, 2019}}</ref> He was waived on April 23, 2020.<ref>{{cite news |author=DiLalla, Aric |title=Broncos waive punters Colby Wadman, Trevor Daniel |url=https://www.denverbroncos.com/news/broncos-waive-punters-colby-wadman-trevor-daniel |website=DenverBroncos.com |date=April 23, 2020 |accessdate=April 23, 2020}}</ref>
