'''Ahmad Balshe''' ({{lang-ar|أحمد بلوشي}}) (born 7 April 1984), known professionally as '''Belly''', is a Palestinian-Canadian rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kalouti|first=Noor|date=15 July 2017|title=6 Arabs who are quietly dominating the music industry|url=https://stepfeed.com/6-arabs-who-are-quietly-dominating-the-music-industry-7036|access-date=14 July 2020|website=StepFeed|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://ottawacitizen.com/entertainment/local-arts/the-weeknds-success-has-a-strong-ottawa-connection|title=The Weeknd's success has a strong Ottawa connection|date=16 February 2016|website=Ottawa Citizen}}</ref> Born in [[Jenin]] in the [[State of Palestine|Palestinian]] [[West Bank]], and raised in [[Ottawa]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ctvnews.ca/entertainment/absolutely-raunchy-rapper-s-lyrics-anger-some-at-toronto-s-canada-150-show-1.3488713|title=Rapper's lyrics anger some at Toronto's Canada 150 show|date=4 July 2017|website=CTV News}}</ref> Balshe embarked on a career as a musician when he was sixteen years old by playing drums in various Ottawa punk bands. At twenty-one years old, he moved on to his main love hip-hop with the release of his debut mixtape, ''Death Before Dishonor: Vol. 1''. He then released eight additional projects in a span of six years, including his debut studio album ''[[The Revolution (Belly album)|The Revolution]]'' (2007), as well as two collaborative mixtapes, ''The Lost Tapes'' and ''The Greatest Dream I Never Had'', with [[Kurupt]] and [[DJ Drama]], respectively.

After a hiatus from music, Belly secured a recording contract with [[XO (record label)|XO]] in 2015, and later an additional contract with [[Roc Nation]], after co-writing a variety of songs for [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] artists [[the Weeknd]] and [[Beyoncé]]. Belly received a total of eleven credits across the former's latest studio albums, while also providing backing vocals and co-writing the song "[[6 Inch]]" from the latter's album, ''[[Lemonade (Beyoncé album)|Lemonade]]''. Belly then went on to release four mixtapes, and his second studio album ''[[Immigrant (album)|Immigrant]]'' in 2018.

Belly was honoured as Songwriter of the Year at the 2016 SOCAN (Society of Composers, Authors and Music Publishers of Canada) Awards for his "significant and outstanding contributions to popular music over the past year".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.socan.ca/news/socan-awards-2016-celebrate-great-canadian-songwriters-composers-film-scorers-music-publishers|title=SOCAN Awards 2016 celebrate great Canadian songwriters, composers, film scorers, music publishers|access-date=21 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160627105444/http://www.socan.ca/news/socan-awards-2016-celebrate-great-canadian-songwriters-composers-film-scorers-music-publishers|archive-date=27 June 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> Alongside his SOCAN award, Belly has won a [[Juno Award]], three [[MuchMusic Video Awards]], and has twice attained a top chart position in the national [[Much Music Countdown]].

== Early life ==
Ahmad Balshe was born on 7 April 1984 in [[Jenin]], [[State of Palestine|Palestine]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=K|first=Disha|date=2021-07-22|title=Who is Belly? Meet the rapper behind song Better Believe feat. The Weeknd|url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2021/07/22/belly-rapper/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-25|website=HITC|language=en-GB}}</ref> He moved to [[Ottawa]], [[Ontario]], Canada with his family, through [[Saudi Arabia]], [[Lebanon]], and [[Jordan]], when he was seven years old to escape violence and poverty.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Belly: "Music is my therapy, my situation would be much worse without it"|url=https://www.joe.co.uk/music/belly-music-is-my-therapy-my-situation-would-be-much-worse-without-it-182619|access-date=2021-08-25|website=JOE.co.uk|language=en}}</ref> 

==Career==

===2005–2007: Early career and ''The Revolution''===
{{Main|The Revolution (Belly album)}}
Belly's first single, "[[Pressure (Belly song)|Pressure]]" featuring [[Ginuwine]], peaked at number 10 on the Canadian CHR radio charts, and number one on MuchMusic's Video chart. In April 2007, he released the video for his second single "[[Don't Be Shy (Belly song)|Don't Be Shy]]", featuring [[Nina Sky]], as well as the video for "History of Violence" directed by Martin Verigin and Jeffrey Hagerman. The latter remains his most controversial record to date, as he explains his views on the [[Israeli–Palestinian conflict]] and the [[War in Iraq]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://music.aol.ca/article/belly/42/|title=Belly shoots from the gut|access-date=10 June 2007|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070610152009/http://music.aol.ca/article/belly/42/|archive-date=10 June 2007|url-status=live}}</ref>

During the [[2007 NHL Playoffs]], as the [[Ottawa Senators]] were competing, Belly released the single "Bandwagon", produced by Hussain. When the Senators made the Stanley Cup Finals, he released a second version of the song which featured goaltender [[Ray Emery]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://slam.canoe.ca/Slam/Hockey/NHL/Playoffs/Ottawa/2007/05/18/4190720-sun.html|title=Champions, baby|access-date=18 May 2007}}</ref>

His debut album, ''[[The Revolution (Belly album)|The Revolution]]'', was released on 5 June 2007. This double disc album was divided into two sections: ''The People'' and ''The System''. ''The People'' contained more in depth songs, such as "History of Violence", "Follow Me", and "Revolutionary", where he challenges his listeners to discover the facts regarding the Middle East situation. Other songs on this disc include "People Change" and "Leave Me Alone", where he talks about his longtime friends and the memories that he kept from them. ''The System'' contained his hit singles "Ridin'", and "Pleasure".

''The Revolution'' achieved Gold sales in Canada and won the [[Juno Awards of 2008|2008]] [[Juno Award]] for Rap Recording of the Year.<ref>[http://www.fontananorth.com/pdfs/news/bellykeshia_20080415.pdf CP Records Releases New Belly and Keshia Chante single "Get To Know You"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080706022603/http://www.fontananorth.com/pdfs/news/bellykeshia_20080415.pdf |date=6 July 2008 }}</ref> The singles from ''The Revolution'' won two [[MuchMusic Video Awards]] for Best Rap Video: "[[Pressure (Belly song)|Pressure]]" in [[2007 MuchMusic Video Awards|2007]] and "Ridin'" in [[2008 MuchMusic Video Awards|2008]].

=== 2007–2011: Additional mixtapes and hiatus===
In 2011, Belly released his fifth mixtape ''Sleepless Nights'', hosted by DJ ill Will.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Belly - Sleepless Nights (Hosted by DJ ill Will)|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/belly-sleepless-nights-hosted-by-dj-ill-will-mixtape.9853.html|access-date=2021-08-21|website=HotNewHipHop|date=4 March 2011|language=en}}</ref> He also released his sixth mixtape in collaboration with rapper [[Kurupt]] entitled ''Belly & Kurupt The Lost Tapes 2008''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Belly & Kurupt - The Lost Tapes (Hosted by DJ ill Will)|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/belly-and-kurupt-the-lost-tapes-hosted-by-dj-ill-will-mixtape.14159.html|access-date=2021-08-21|website=HotNewHipHop|date=19 May 2011|language=en}}</ref> The mixtape, also hosted by DJ ill Will, contained a compilation of songs the two had worked on some time after Belly's debut studio album was released. Some of the songs featured on the mixtape were previously unreleased. Belly then released his seventh mixtape ''The Greatest Dream I Never Had'', hosted by [[DJ Drama]]''.''<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Greatest Dream I Never Had Mixtape by Belly Hosted by DJ Drama|url=https://www.datpiff.com/Belly-The-Greatest-Dream-I-Never-Had-mixtape.282276.html|access-date=2021-08-21|website=www.datpiff.com}}</ref>

Belly's next project ''Sleepless Nights 1.5'' was released in April 2012. The first single, "[[Hot Girl (Belly song)|Hot Girl]]" featuring [[Snoop Dogg]], was released on 8 September 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QBTuIMBY_wo|title=YouTube|website=[[YouTube]]|access-date=19 September 2016}}</ref> Its second single, "To The Top" featuring Ava, was released in 2010. Belly's first single of 2011 was "Back Against the Wall", featuring singer [[Kobe (singer)|Kobe Honeycutt]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EadKYHeowrI|title=YouTube|website=[[YouTube]]|access-date=19 September 2016}}</ref>

===2015–2016: Signing to XO and Roc Nation===
{{Main|Up for Days|Another Day in Paradise (mixtape)|Inzombia (mixtape)}}
In May 2015, after a five-year hiatus, Belly released his eighth mixtape ''[[Up for Days]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/34118/belly-up-for-days.html|title=Belly – Up For Days – NoDJ|access-date=19 September 2016}}</ref> It was his first project to be released under the record labels [[XO (record label)|XO]] and [[Roc Nation]] and featured guest appearances by [[Travis Scott]], [[Juelz Santana]], [[French Montana]] and [[the Weeknd]]. Belly was also prominently featured on the Weeknd's sophomore album ''[[Beauty Behind the Madness]]'' in August 2015, earning writing credits on six of the fourteen songs: "[[Often]]''"'', "[[The Hills (song)|The Hills]]''"'', "Shameless''"'', "[[Earned It]]''"'', "[[In the Night (song)|In the Night]]''"'' and "As You Are''"''. On 24 November 2015, Belly formally signed to Roc Nation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/belly_signs_with_roc_nation_for_new_lp|title=Belly Signs with Roc Nation for New LP|access-date=19 September 2016}}</ref>

In February 2016, Belly released the single "Zanzibar", featuring [[Juicy J]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/bellyisdead/zanzibar-feat-juicy-j|title=Zanzibar|access-date=19 September 2016}}</ref> On 27 May, he released his ninth mixtape [[Another Day in Paradise (mixtape)|''Another Day in Paradise'']], which featured guest appearances by Travis Scott, [[Lil Wayne]], [[Kehlani]], [[Starrah]], [[Waka Flocka]], Juicy J and [[B-Real]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rap-up.com/2016/05/27/belly-another-day-in-paradise-mixtape-stream|title=Belly releases follow up to Up for Days|date=10 June 2019 |website=Rapup |access-date=10 June 2019
}}</ref> On 11 November, he released his tenth mixtape [[Inzombia (Belly album)|''Inzombia'']]. It featured guest appearances from [[Young Thug]], [[Coke Boys Records|Zack]], [[Nav (rapper)|Nav]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Ty Dolla $ign]], [[Jadakiss]] and [[Ashanti (singer)|Ashanti]]. The mixtape was supported by two singles; "Consuela" and "The Day I Met You".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/stream-bellys-new-project-inzombia-news.25387.html? |title=Belly releases follow up to Another Day In Paradise |last=Goddard |first=Kevin |date=11 November 2016 |website=Hotnewhiphop |access-date=11 November 2016}}</ref> Later that month, the Weeknd released his third studio album [[Starboy (album)|''Starboy'']], with Belly earning writing credits on nine of the eighteen songs: "[[Party Monster (song)|Party Monster]]", "[[False Alarm (The Weeknd song)|False Alarm]]", "[[Rockin' (song)|Rockin']]", "[[Six Feet Under (The Weeknd song)|Six Feet Under]]", "Love to Lay", "A Lonely Night", "Ordinary Life", "Nothing Without You" and "All I Know".

===2017–2018: ''Mumble Rap'' and ''Immigrant''===
{{Main|Mumble Rap (mixtape)|Immigrant (album)}}
On 23 June 2017, Belly was featured on [[DJ Khaled]]'s tenth studio album [[Grateful (DJ Khaled album)|''Grateful'']] on the song "Interlude". On 6 October, he released his eleventh mixtape [[Mumble Rap (mixtape)|''Mumble Rap'']], which was executive produced by [[Boi-1da]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/stream-bellys-new-album-mumble-rap-new-mixtape.117518.html|title=Stream Belly's New Album "Mumble Rap"|website=HotNewHipHop|date=6 October 2017|access-date=8 February 2019}}</ref> On 8 December, he was featured on [[Juicy J]]'s fourth studio album [[Rubba Band Business|''Rubba Band Business'']] on the song "On & On", which also featured [[Tory Lanez]].

On 23 March 2018, Belly released the single "4 Days", featuring rapper [[YG (rapper)|YG]] and producer [[Mustard (record producer)|DJ Mustard]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Belly Talks Crafting '4 Days' With YG & DJ Mustard, JAY-Z Studio Sessions & Friendship With Kevin Durant|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8258249/belly-interview-midnight-zone-4-days-jay-z|access-date=2021-08-21|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> The song's music video was released three days later.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Saponara|first=Michael|date=March 27, 2018|title=Belly Turns Up With YG and DJ Mustard For New ''4 Days'' Video - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/belly-yg-dj-mustard-4-days-video/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-21|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> He then released the single "[[Maintain (song)|Maintain]]" featuring [[Nav (rapper)|Nav]] on 6 April, followed by the single "[[What You Want (song)|What You Want]]" featuring the Weeknd, which was released on 24 May. Both singles appeared on his second studio album, ''[[Immigrant (album)|Immigrant]]'', which was released on 12 October. The album also features guest appearances from [[Coke Boys Records|Zack]], [[French Montana]], [[Meek Mill]], [[Yo Gotti]], and [[M.I.A. (rapper)|M.I.A.]].

===2021: ''See You Next Wednesday''===
{{Main|See You Next Wednesday (album)}}
On 7 April 2021, Belly released the first single from his third studio album ''[[See You Next Wednesday (album)|See You Next Wednesday]]'', "Money on the Table" featuring [[Benny the Butcher]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Belly Drops "Money on the Table" Video f/ Benny The Butcher and 'IYKYK'|url=https://www.complex.com/music/belly-new-songs-money-on-the-table-featuring-benny-the-butcher-iykyk|access-date=2021-08-21|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref> He then released the second single, "Zero Love" featuring [[Moneybagg Yo]], on 3 June.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-03|title=Belly on Taking a Stand and Teaming With Moneybagg Yo|url=https://www.spin.com/2021/06/belly-zero-stand-after-hours-interview/|access-date=2021-08-21|website=SPIN}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Belly Recruits Moneybagg Yo for New Single 'Zero Love'|url=https://www.complex.com/music/belly-moneybagg-yo-zero-love|access-date=2021-08-21|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref> On 22 July, he released the third single "[[Better Believe]]" featuring the Weeknd and [[Young Thug]]. The song debuted on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart at number 88.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/belly/chart-history/HSI|title=Belly - Chart History|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=August 19, 2021}}</ref> On 18 August, Belly revealed the album artwork and tracklist, and announced the release date of 27 August.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Belly Details 'See You Next Wednesday' with Nas, the Weeknd, Young Thug {{!}} Exclaim!|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/belly_details_see_you_next_wednesday_with_nas_the_weeknd_young_thug|access-date=2021-08-21|website=exclaim.ca|language=en-ca}}</ref> The album also features guest appearances by [[Nas]], Nav, [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]], [[Big Sean]], [[Lil Uzi Vert]], and [[PnB Rock]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Belly Shares Release Date, Tracklist, and Features for New Album 'See You Next Wednesday'|url=https://www.complex.com/music/belly-release-date-tracklist-features-see-you-next-wednesday-album|access-date=2021-08-21|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>

== Personal life ==

On 30 July 2019, Balshe filed a lawsuit against Coachella Music LLC, Goldenvoice LLC, and IPS Security Incorporated alleging assault and battery, negligence, and emotional distress in response to an incident which occurred during the 2018 [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella Music Festival]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Blueskye |first1=Brian |title=Rapper Belly sues Coachella producer, security company alleging assault at 2018 festival |url=https://www.desertsun.com/story/life/2019/07/30/rapper-belly-sues-coachella-producer-assault-2018-festival/1870766001/ |website=desertsun.com |publisher=Palm Springs Desert Sun |access-date=7 October 2019 |date=30 July 2019}}</ref> A video obtained by TMZ shows festival security staff pushing Balshe up against a security barrier and repeatedly punching him.<ref>{{cite web |title=Rapper Belly Punched Repeatedly by Coachella Security During The Weeknd's Set |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AnfAj9_nW4I |website=YouTube |publisher=TMZ |access-date=7 October 2019 |location=Indio, California, USA |date=21 April 2018}}</ref>

On 31 May 2021, Balshe announced his engagement to resident doctor Dina Rabadi.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rapper Belly Proposed to Dina Rabadi in Big Sur, California|url=https://www.insideweddings.com/news/celebrity-style/rapper-belly-proposed-to-dina-rabadi-in-big-sur/48399/|url-status=live|access-date=2 June 2021|website=www.insideweddings.com}}</ref>
