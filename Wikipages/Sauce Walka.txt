'''Albert Walker Mondane''' (born June 29, 1990), known professionally as '''Sauce Walka''', is a [[Afro-Hondurans|Honduran]]-American rapper and songwriter from [[Houston, Texas]].<ref name="Sauce Walka on Trapworld Hip Hop" />

==Career==

Albert Mondane started his rapping career in 2007 under the name A-Walk, and was part of a Houston rap group called Mostheard.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HA6yd-eN-c4|title=Mostheard performance @ sharpstown mall|website=YouTube|accessdate=January 8, 2021}}</ref>
Mondane began releasing mixtapes as Sauce Walka in 2014, in the same year forming the duo Sauce Twinz with fellow rapper Sancho Saucy.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/sauce-twinz-mn0003492702/biography?1585435564283|title=Sauce Twinz|last=Yeung|first=Neil|publisher=allmusic|accessdate=March 28, 2020}}</ref> He has been credited with popularizing the modern usage of the hip-hop slang term "drip", a word he uses extensively throughout his lyrics.<ref name="Genius">{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/what-does-drip-mean|title=What Does "Drip" Mean?|publisher=Genius|accessdate=March 28, 2020}}</ref> In 2015, Mondane gained attention after releasing a [[diss song]] titled "Wack 2 Wack" targeted at [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], accusing him of appropriating Houston hip-hop culture.<ref name="Stereogum">{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2060836/underground-rap-will-never-die/franchises/status-aint-hood/|title=Underground Rap Will Never Die|last=Breihan|first=Tom|year=2019|publisher=Stereogum|accessdate=March 28, 2020}}</ref>

Sauce Walka has collaborated with various notable hip-hop acts such as [[A$AP Rocky]], [[Bun B]], [[Chief Keef]], [[Lil' Keke]], [[Maxo Kream]], [[Migos]], [[Slim Thug]], [[Travis Scott]], [[Trinidad James]], and [[XXXTentacion]].<ref name="Sauce Walka Enlists A$AP Rocky On ASAP Sauce">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/sauce-walka-enlists-asap-rocky-on-asap-sauce-new-song.1990948.html|title=Sauce Walka Enlists A$AP Rocky On "ASAP Sauce"|publisher=HotNewHipHop|accessdate=April 27, 2021}}</ref><ref name="Drip God on HotNewHipHop">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/stream-sauce-walkas-drip-god-project-new-mixtape.118372.html|title=Stream Sauce Walka's "Drip God" Project|publisher=HotNewHipHop|accessdate=March 28, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Sauce Ghetto Gospel 2 on HotNewHipHop">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/sauce-walka-drops-off-sauce-ghetto-gospel-2-ft-travis-scott-maxo-kream-and-more-new-mixtape.119866.html|title=Sauce Walka Drops Off "Sauce Ghetto Gospel 2" Ft. ===The Sauce Factory===
Sauce Walka founded the independent record label [[The Sauce Factory]] in 2014. The label name is often abbreviated as TSF.<ref name="TSF">{{cite web|url=https://www.houstonchronicle.com/entertainment/music/article/Texas-City-rapper-Peso-Peso-puts-troubled-past-14483067.php|title=Texas City rapper Peso Peso puts troubled past behind him|publisher=Houston Chronicle|first=Camilo|last=Hannibal Smith|date=October 2, 2019|accessdate=March 30, 2020}}</ref>

==Legal issues==
Mondane was involved in a gun-related incident in summer of 2009.<ref name="Gun Incident">{{cite web|url=https://www.houstonpress.com/music/holy-sauce-sauce-walkas-war-against-biters-8380863|title='Holy Sauce': Sauce Walka's War Against Biters|publisher=Houston Press|first=Brandon|last=Caldwell|date=May 6, 2016|accessdate=March 30, 2020}}</ref> Court records show that Mondane pleaded guilty to deadly conduct after being charged with shooting and wounding a person during a concert at Texas Southern University, which resulted in him serving community supervision. In 2018, Houston Police told [[KPRC-TV|KPRC]] that Mondane was involved in a criminal street gang called "Mash Mode" in 2009, at the time going by the moniker "A-Walk".<ref name="C2H">{{cite web|url=https://www.click2houston.com/news/2018/08/09/leader-of-popular-houston-rap-label-the-sauce-factory-responds-to-gang-accusations/|title=Leader of popular Houston rap label, The Sauce Factory, responds to 'gang' accusations|publisher=Click 2 Houston|first=Robert|last=Arnold|date=August 8, 2018|accessdate=March 30, 2020}}</ref>

In 2018, Texas state court documents described The Sauce Factory as a "documented gang" and "known for criminal activity". In response to the accusations, Mondane stated:<ref name="C2H"/>

{{blockquote|“I'm going to keep rapping because I know I'm not a gang. I know we're not a gang, I know that I'm a record label, I know that I'm an artist [...] I'm not into gang activity or none of that stuff that's going on, I'm not worried about that, it's not what's going on.”}}
