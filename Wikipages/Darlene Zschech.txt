'''Darlene Joyce Zschech''' ({{IPAc-en|d|ɑr|ˈ|l|iː|n|_|ˈ|tʃ|ɛ|k}}; née '''Steinhardt''' on 8 September 1965) is an Australian [[Pentecostal]] Christian worship leader and singer-songwriter who primarily writes [[Contemporary Christian music|praise and worship songs]]. Described as a pioneer of the modern worship movement,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.charismanews.com/culture/38616-darlene-zschech-reveals-jesus-in-latest-album|title=Darlene Zschech Reveals Jesus in Latest Album|last=Hamby|first=DeWayne|work=Charisma News|access-date=18 June 2018}}</ref> she is the former worship pastor of [[Hillsong Church]] and currently a member of [[Compassionart]], a charity founded by [[Martin Smith (songwriter)|Martin Smith]].<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xwI5AQAAIAAJ&focus|title=Encyclopedia of Contemporary Christian Music|last=Powell|first=Mark Allan|publisher=Hendrickson Publishers|year=2002|isbn=1-56563-679-1|edition=First printing|location=[[Peabody, Massachusetts|Peabody]], Massachusetts|page=1067}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Zschech starred on television at the age of 10, as part of an Australian children's show, ''Happy Go Round''.<ref>{{Citation|last=Terry Stewart|title=Happy Go Round Team (QTQ 9 Archive Footage)|date=9 February 2008|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sliIUu8cyfk|access-date=18 June 2018}}</ref> When she was 13, her parents divorced and the emotional stress of being on television and her parents' divorce resulted in her having [[Bulimia nervosa|bulimia]] for about four years.<ref>{{cite web |title=Darlene Zschech Interview |url=http://www.swcs.com.au/darlene%20zschech.htm|access-date=7 December 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Camerin Courtney |url=http://www.todayschristianwoman.com/articles/2001/march/3.36.html?start=4 |title=The Power of Praising God &#124; Today's Christian Woman |publisher=Todayschristianwoman.com |access-date=3 February 2014}}</ref>

In 1980, when Zschech was 15, her father rededicated his life to Christ and began taking her to church, where she also became a committed Christian and met her future husband, Mark Zschech.<ref name="todayschristianwoman2001">{{cite web|author=Camerin Courtney |url=http://www.todayschristianwoman.com/articles/2001/march/3.36.html?start=2 |title=The Power of Praising God &#124; Today's Christian Woman |publisher=Todayschristianwoman.com |access-date=3 February 2014}}</ref> When they married, her husband suggested that they move from Brisbane to Sydney, which was confirmed at a small church they were visiting one Sunday when the guest speaker said in the middle of his sermon, "This doesn't happen to me very often, but whatever it is you two prayed last night, God says do it now."<ref>{{cite web|title=Darlene Zschech Interview|url=http://www.swcs.com.au/darlene%20zschech.htm|access-date=7 December 2013}}</ref> They began attending Hills Christian Life Centre, which would become [[Hillsong Church]].

Zschech sang [[jingle]]s for a number of international companies including [[McDonald's]], [[KFC]] and [[Coca-Cola]], until she eventually joined the staff of Hillsong Church after penning "[[Shout to the Lord]]".<ref name="todayschristianwoman2001" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YDY_4bahXi0 |title=Revealing Jesus |via=YouTube |date=9 June 2013 |access-date=3 February 2014}}</ref> She released a vinyl album in 1987 called ''Make the Choice'' and a cassette album in 1993 called ''Pearls & Gold''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/artists/DarleneZschech.asp|title=Darlene Zschech Discography, Darlene Zschech Artist Database {{!}} Jesusfreakhideout.com|website=www.jesusfreakhideout.com|access-date=18 June 2018}}</ref>

Zschech and her husband have three daughters.

''[[Shout to the Lord (album)|Shout to the Lord]]'' was nominated as Album of the Year for the 1997 [[GMA Dove Award|Dove Awards]] and Song of the Year for the 1998 Dove Awards. In 2000, Zschech received a Dove Award nomination for Songwriter of the Year and received the International Award for influence in praise and worship.

In 2003, Zschech released her first official solo album, ''Kiss of Heaven''. ''Change Your World'' followed in 2005. She has since released two albums, in 2011, called ''Simply Darlene'' and ''You Are Love''. In addition to writing songs, she has written five books: ''Worship'' (1996), ''Extravagant Worship'' (2002), ''The Kiss of Heaven'' (2003), ''The Great Generational Transition'' (2009) and ''Revealing Jesus: A 365-Day Devotional'' (2013).

On 11 December 2013, Zschech announced that she had been diagnosed with breast cancer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.darlenezschech.com/blog/2013/12/29/important-update-from-darlene-and-mark/|title=Important Update from Darlene and Mark|last=Zschech|first=Darlene|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150226094850/http://www.darlenezschech.com/blog/2013/12/29/important-update-from-darlene-and-mark/|archive-date=26 February 2015|access-date=30 December 2013}}</ref> In a blog dated 30 November 2014, she wrote that she has been healed of cancer, which her doctor called miraculous.<ref>{{cite web|title=Thanksgiving|url=http://www.darlenezschech.com/blog/2014/11/30/thanksgiving/|access-date=30 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141202204454/http://www.darlenezschech.com/blog/2014/11/30/thanksgiving/|archive-date=2 December 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Hillsong Church===
Zschech was the worship pastor of Hillsong Church from 1996 to 2007, during which time she served as producer, vocal producer or executive producer for more than 20 albums under the Hillsong Music label and wrote more than 80 published worship songs.<ref>{{cite web|title=KLOVE: Darlene Zschech|url=http://www.klove.com/music/artists/darlene-zschech/|access-date=7 December 2013}}</ref> Under Zschech's leadership, the album ''[[People Just Like Us]]'' (1994) was the first Christian album in Australia to be certified gold and platinum,<ref name="socialphy1">{{cite web|title=Socialphy: Hillsong Music Australia|url=http://www.socialphy.com/posts/news-politics/21260/Hillsong-Music-Australia.html|access-date=7 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131212033542/http://www.socialphy.com/posts/news-politics/21260/Hillsong-Music-Australia.html|archive-date=12 December 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> and the album ''[[For All You've Done]]'' (2004) debuted at No. 1 on the Australian Record Industry Association album charts.<ref>{{cite news|title="For All You've Done" Hillsong Music Australia – Yahoo! 7 News}}</ref>

===="Shout to the Lord"====
Zschech composed "[[Shout to the Lord]]" in 1993 and it has become one of the most well-known modern worship songs, being sung by an estimated 25–30&nbsp;million churchgoers every Sunday since the song's release.<ref>{{cite web|title=Songfacts: Darlene Zschech's Shout to the Lord|url=http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=11164|access-date=7 December 2013}}</ref> She wrote the song during a time of personal struggle.<ref name="YouTubeShout">{{cite web |title=Hillsong's Darlene Zschech: "How I Wrote Shout to the Lord" |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=20kP79XJox4|access-date=7 December 2013}}</ref> When she presented it to Hillsong's then-worship pastor, [[Geoff Bullock]], she was so embarrassed that she requested he face away from her as she played and sang.<ref name="YouTubeShout" />

"Shout to the Lord" was first recorded on ''[[People Just Like Us]]'' and has appeared on more than 200 different albums by numerous artists in multiple languages. It is also the title track for the first live album co-produced with [[Integrity Music]] featuring Zschech as a worship leader. It was Integrity Music's first album to feature a female worship leader. The album was nominated for Praise & Worship Album of the Year at the 1997 Dove Awards. The song was nominated in the 1998 Dove Awards' Song of the Year category. It has been performed for the Pope at the Vatican and for the President of the United States.<ref>{{cite web|author=Camerin Courtney |url=http://www.todayschristianwoman.com/articles/2001/march/3.36.html |title=The Power of Praising God &#124; Today's Christian Woman |publisher=Todayschristianwoman.com |access-date=3 February 2014}}</ref>
