'''Rachel Bay Jones''' (born November 8, 1969){{citation needed|date = April 2019}} is an American actress and singer. She has played the roles of Catherine in the 2013 Broadway revival of ''[[Pippin (musical)|Pippin]]'' and Evan Hansen's mother, Heidi Hansen, in ''[[Dear Evan Hansen]]''.  The latter earned her the 2017 [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical]].

==  Early life ==
Jones was born in New York City and grew up in [[Boca Raton, Florida]]. Her parents were both Shakespearean actors. She dropped out of high school to perform in a local play, then moved to New York City to pursue acting at 19.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/tony-awards-rachel-bay-jones-dear-evan-hansen-nomination-caps-three-decade-quest-1002098|title=Tony Awards: Rachel Bay Jones' 'Dear Evan Hansen' Nomination Caps Three-Decade Quest|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=May 26, 2017|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
Jones's first Broadway role was as an understudy for the lead role of Esther in ''[[Meet Me in St. Louis (musical)|Meet Me in St. Louis]]'' in 1989.<ref name=":0" /><ref>[http://www.playbill.com/production/meet-me-in-st-louis-george-gershwin-theatre-vault-0000011042 ''Meet Me in St. Louis''] playbill (vault), accessed March 29, 2019</ref>

In the early 1990s, she was in the touring company of ''Grand Hotel'', as well as other national and international tours, performing ''Rent'' in German and ''Evita'' in Spanish.<ref name=":0" /> She performed in the 2009 Broadway revival of ''Hair'' as the replacement for Mother, Buddahdalirama, and Member Of The Tribe.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/production/hair-al-hirschfeld-theatre-vault-0000008201 ''Hair''] playbill (vault), accessed March 29, 2019</ref> She was one of the original cast members starring in the revival of ''[[Pippin (musical)|Pippin]]'',<ref name=":0" /> playing Catherine, Pippin's lover, during the entire run.<ref>Champion, Lindsay. [http://www.broadway.com/buzz/170095/rachel-bay-jones-on-being-scared-of-clowns-her-terrifying-evita-story-joining-the-pippin-circus/ "Rachel Bay Jones on Being Scared of Clowns, Her 'Terrifying' Evita Story & Joining the Pippin Circus"] broadway.com, June 13, 2013</ref><ref>Blank, Matthew. [http://www.playbill.com/article/playbillcoms-cue-a-pippin-star-rachel-bay-jones-com-204240 "CUE & A: Pippin Star Rachel Bay Jones"] playbill, April 9, 2013</ref>

[[File:Ben Platt & Rachel Bay Jones with their Tony Awards.jpg|thumb|Jones and fellow ''Dear Evan Hansen'' cast member [[Ben Platt (actor)|Ben Platt]] with their Tony Awards]]

Jones portrayed Evan's mother Heidi in the musical ''[[Dear Evan Hansen]]'', which premiered at the [[Arena Stage]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] in July 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/pasek-and-pauls-dear-evan-hansen-about-the-high-school-struggle-to-fit-in-premieres-in-d.c-355138#|title=Pasek and Paul's Dear Evan Hansen, About the High School Struggle to Fit In, Premieres in D.C. - Playbill|website=Playbill|date=July 30, 2015|access-date=July 12, 2017}}</ref> She reprised the role in the [[Off-Broadway]] production at the [[Second Stage Theatre]] in May 2016,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/dear-evan-hansen-announces-broadway-theatre-date-and-cast#|title=Tickets Go on Sale Today for Broadway's Dear Evan Hansen - Playbill|website=Playbill|date=June 19, 2016|access-date=July 12, 2017}}</ref> and then on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] starting in November 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/dear-evan-hansen-opens-on-broadway-december-4#|title=Dear Evan Hansen Opens on Broadway December 4 - Playbill|website=Playbill|date=December 4, 2016|access-date=July 12, 2017}}</ref> At the [[71st Tony Awards]], she won for [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical|Best Featured Actress in a Musical]] for her portrayal of Heidi Hansen.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.broadway.com/buzz/188851/good-for-you-dear-evan-hansens-rachel-bay-jones-wins-her-first-tony-award/|title=Good&nbsp;for&nbsp;You!&nbsp;Dear&nbsp;Evan&nbsp;Hansen's&nbsp;Rachel&nbsp;Bay&nbsp;Jones&nbsp;Wins&nbsp;Her&nbsp;First&nbsp;Tony&nbsp;Award|access-date=November 29, 2018}}</ref>

After leaving ''Dear Evan Hansen'' on August 5, 2018, Jones performed at several small venues in ''Something Beautiful'', a one-woman cabaret show that draws music from her expansive career and bringing meaning from her life into the lyrics.<ref>[https://www.theatermania.com/shows/florida-theater/rachel-bay-jones-something-beautiful_326787 "Rachel Bay Jones: Something Beautiful"] theatermania.com, accessed March 29, 2019</ref><ref name=sanfran>[https://www.broadwayworld.com/cabaret/article/Rachel-Bay-Jones-Brings-SOMETHING-BEAUTIFUL-to-Feinsteins-at-the-Nikko-20180719 "Rachel Bay Jones Brings 'Something Beautiful' to Feinsteins at the Nikko"] broadwayworld.com, July 19, 2018</ref>

In 2018, Jones appeared in the CBS television drama series ''[[God Friended Me]]'', playing the role of Susan.<ref>Fierberg, Ruthie. [http://www.playbill.com/article/dear-evan-hansens-rachel-bay-jones-lands-cbs-comedy-pilot# " Dear Evan Hansen's Rachel Bay Jones Lands CBS Comedy Pilot"] Playbill, March 26, 2018</ref><ref>[http://www.thefutoncritic.com/listings/20190311cbs07/ "Listings 'God Friended Me', Air Date March 31, 2019"] thefutoncritic.com, retrieved May 17, 2019</ref> She has also had guest roles on ''[[Modern Family]]'' and ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]''.

She appeared in the concert of ''[[Sweet Charity]]'' at the Merkin Concert Hall at the Kaufman Music Center on June 17, 2019, presented by the Transport Group.<ref>Gans, Andrew. [http://www.playbill.com/article/rachel-bay-jones-santino-fontana-more-set-for-sweet-charity-in-concert# "Rachel Bay Jones, Santino Fontana, More Set for 'Sweet Charity in Concert'"] Playbill, May 17, 2019</ref>

From January 29 to February 3, 2020 she starred as Diana in ''[[Next to Normal]]'' at the [[Kennedy Center]] Broadway Center Stage series.<ref>McPhee, Ryan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/rachel-bay-jones-to-star-in-next-to-normal-at-washington-dcs-kennedy-center-new-season-also-includes-to-kill-a-mockingbird-tour-premiere# "Rachel Bay Jones to Star in 'Next to Normal' at Washington, D.C.’s Kennedy Center; New Season Also Includes 'To Kill a Mockingbird' Tour Premiere"] playbill, March 28, 2019</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kennedy-center.org/whats-on/explore-by-genre/theater/2019-2020/bcs-Next-to-Normal-68827/|title=Broadway Center Stage: Next to Normal|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref>

In 2021, Jones joined the cast of ''[[The Good Doctor (TV series)|The Good Doctor]]'' as Salen Morrison.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Good Doctor's 'Internal Nemesis' Revealed in Game-Changing Season 5 Premiere — Grade the Episode!|url=https://www.yahoo.com/now/good-doctors-internal-nemesis-revealed-025930216.html|access-date=2021-10-08|website=www.yahoo.com|language=en-US}}</ref> This reunited her with former ''[[Dear Evan Hansen]]'' co-star [[Noah Galvin]] (who played the title role, the son of Jones's character, Heidi).

==Personal life==
She has a daughter, Miranda, and is partner to actor Benim Foster.<ref name=sanfran/> Jones has one sibling, her brother Darren.<ref name=":0" />
