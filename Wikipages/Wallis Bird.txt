'''Wallis Bird''' (born 29 January 1982) is an Irish musician, living in Berlin since 2012. As of 2021, she has released six studio albums, including ''[[Architect (album)|Architect]]'' in 2014 and ''[[Home (Wallis Bird album)|Home]]'' in 2016.

==Early life==
Bird came into touch with music at age six months old and she when received her first guitar from her father. Born left-handed, she lost five fingers of her left hand in a lawnmower accident,{{when|date=February 2021}} and having four sewn back on again. She became used to playing a right-handed guitar upside-down, which contributes to her unconventional style of playing. The Irish Times wrote: “The sheer visceral energy could kick-start an entire economy…”<ref name="wallisbird1">{{cite web|url=http://wallisbird.com/2012/bio.htm |title=the all new wallisbird.com |publisher=Wallis Bird |access-date=28 March 2012}}</ref>{{dead link|date=February 2021}}

After school{{when|date=February 2021}} Bird moved to [[Dublin]] to study songwriting at [[Ballyfermot]] Rock School. Here she met fellow band member Aoife O'Sullivan. During that time she kept on playing in small clubs or pubs as well as on major festivals. In 2005, she moved to Germany, met her management Karakter Worldwide and other musicians and formed a new supporting band, including brothers Christian and Michael Vinne. After first tourings [[Berlin]], London, Dublin, [[Cork (city)|Cork]], [[Stuttgart]], Frankfurt and [[Mannheim]] she decided to stay in Mannheim, where she lived for a year and a half.{{Citation needed|date=April 2009}} From 2006 to 2012, Bird lived in London.<ref name="wallisbird1"/> In 2008 the band was joined by Irish/Belgian  musician Aidan. Bird has recorded her material in Ireland, Germany and in her apartment in [[Brixton]], London.<ref name="wallisbird1"/> Since 2012, she has resided in [[Berlin]], Germany.

==Career==
In April 2006, her debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''Branches Untangle'' was independently released in Germany by her own label, Bird Records.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

In October 2006, Bird signed a worldwide recording contract with [[Island Records]] Group UK. The "Moodsets EP" was released for download and on vinyl in July 2007, with her debut album ''[[Spoons (album)|Spoons]]'' following on 18 September 2007 in Germany. Her song "Counting to Sleep" was chosen by iTunes as their 'Single of the Week' and the digital release of her album ''Spoons'' subsequently reached the Top 10 in the UK Album Download Chart. In the UK, her single, "Blossoms in the Street" was released in October 2007, with the physical release of ''Spoons'' following 22 October 2007.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

In February 2008, Bird toured the UK as support act for soul star [[Gabrielle (singer)|Gabrielle]], and later that year with [[Billy Bragg]].{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

In 2009 (Ireland/UK) and 2010 (Rest of Europe), Bird released her second album ''New Boots'', which entered the Irish album charts on No. 11, played over 80 headlining shows through Europe and supported over 20 shows in Europe of [[Rodrigo y Gabriella]].{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}} During the festival summer she played over 15 festivals in Europe, including Montreux Jazz Festival, Oxegen Festival, Frequency Festival, Rock En Seine, Lowlands and Pukkelpop.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

In January 2011, Bird toured with [[Anaïs Mitchell]]'s performance of her album ''[[Hadestown (album)|Hadestown]]'', providing opening support whilst also taking part in the main production of the show.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}} In November 2011, Bird released a special limited edition sole acoustic album (limited to 1000 copies) called ''The Mistakes are Intentional''. Bird recorded the solo versions in her home in London during the summer of 2011. The CD is packaged in a wallet which has been hand-stitched by Bird herself.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

Bird performed at the [[Eurosonic Festival]] in 2012 when Ireland was the Spotlight Country.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.rte.ie/ten/2012/0105/2fm.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121002174213/http://www.rte.ie/ten/2012/0105/2fm.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2 October 2012 |title=RTÉ 2fm gearing up for EuroSonic |date=5 January 2012 |access-date=5 January 2012 |work=RTÉ Ten |publisher=Raidió Teilifís Éireann }}</ref>

Bird released her third studio album in March 2012 in Ireland,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.towerrecords.ie/product/Bird%2Cwallis_Wallis_Bird/541400 |title=Bird,wallis – Wallis Bird – CD – Tower Records Dublin Ireland |publisher=Towerrecords.ie |date=9 March 2012 |access-date=28 March 2012}}</ref> Germany, Austria and Switzerland.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.de/Wallis-Bird-Vinyl-LP/dp/B006RD9KSM/ref=sr_1_5?ie=UTF8&qid=1330552974&sr=8-5 |title=Wallis Bird [Vinyl LP&#93;: Wallis Bird: Amazon.de: Musik |publisher=Amazon.de |date=9 September 2009 |access-date=28 March 2012}}</ref> The album was recorded in [[Berlin]], Germany. The first single, "Encore", was released on 2 March. The music video debuted on 1 February,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ch6-rqOrq5o |title=Wallis Bird – ENCORE – Official Video |publisher=YouTube |access-date=28 March 2012}}</ref> and had been shot in Berlin.

''[[Architect (album)|Architect]]'' is the fourth studio album, released in April 2014. The first single from the album was "Hardly Hardly", released across Europe on 28 March and 4 April 2014 respectively.

A live album featuring recordings from her European tours from her debut album to her 2014 release ''Architect'', ''Yeah!'' was released in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.love-your-artist.com/en/wallis-bird/music/wallis-bird-yeah-double-vinyl-preorder/|title=LYA - Love Your Artist - fair ticketing and merch |website=www.love-your-artist.com}}</ref>

''Home'' is her fifth studio album, released worldwide in September 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.love-your-artist.com/en/wallis-bird/music/wallis-bird-home-cd/|title=LYA - Love Your Artist - fair ticketing and merch|website=www.love-your-artist.com}}</ref>

''Woman'' is Birds's sixth studio album.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

==Discography==
Bird has been compared to singer-songwriters such as [[Ani DiFranco]], [[Fiona Apple]] or the young [[Janis Joplin]].{{citation needed|date=October 2016}} In her songs she combines elements of traditional folk music, blues and funk with a rock sound. Her style ranges from soft folk to power ballads.{{Citation needed|date=March 2012}}

==In popular culture==
In October 2008, Bird recorded a cover of [[Depeche Mode]]'s 1981 hit "[[Just Can't Get Enough (Depeche Mode song)|Just Can't Get Enough]]" for ''[[The Sun (United Kingdom)|The Sun]]'' newspaper's advertisement campaign. Following repeated requests from viewers, the song was digitally released in Europe as a double A-side single, together with Bird's own song "The Sunshine Song". 

In March 2010, Bird appeared on GOTV to present her favourite music videos and musical influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gotv.at/hostedby_detail.php?id=1527 |title=gotv – hosted by Wallis Bird |publisher=Gotv.at |access-date=12 March 2011}}</ref> Her song "To My Bones" from the album [[New Boots (album)|New Boots]] featured on an advert for Wexford tourism in 2011.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q-V9w1ZTKC8 | work=YouTube | title=Visit Wexford – Fun, Festive & Unforgettable }}</ref>
