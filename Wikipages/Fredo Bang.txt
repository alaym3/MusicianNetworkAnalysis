'''Fredrick Dewon Thomas Givens II''' (born March 29, 1996), known professionally as '''Fredo Bang''', is an American rapper from [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana]]. He is best known for his tracks "Oouuh" and "Top" (featuring [[Lil Durk]]). In April 2020, he released his debut studio album, ''[[Most Hated]]'', through Se Lavi Productions and [[Def Jam Recordings]].

== Early life ==
Givens was born on March 29, 1996, in [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=The Break Presents: Fredo Bang|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2020/04/fredo-bang-interview-the-break/|website=XXL|accessdate=May 22, 2020}}</ref> Givens grew up close to rappers Krazy Trey and Da Real Gee Money, both of whom were eventual victims of homicide in 2014 and 2017 respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/baton-rouge-rapper-gee-money-shot-and-killed/|title= Baton Rouge Rapper Da Real Gee Money Shot and Killed|website=XXL}}</ref>

== Career ==
Givens gained recognition after the release of his July 2018 single "Oouuh".<ref name=":0" /> His highest viewed videos are "Top", "Father", Oouuh" and "Trust Issues" which all have reached over 20 million views on [[YouTube]].

In January 2020, he was featured on [[Moneybagg Yo]]'s track "Spin on Em", which received a music video in May 2020. The record was produced by Fredo Bang's go-to producer and close affiliate, Hardbody B-Eazy.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Moneybagg Yo & Fredo Bang Take Prisoners in 'Spin On Em' Music Video: Watch|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9372245/moneybagg-yo-fredo-bang-spin-on-em-music-video|accessdate=May 22, 2020}}</ref> In April 2020, he released his debut studio album ''[[Most Hated]]'', which peaked at number 110 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fredo Bang declares himself 'Most Hated' with new album|url=https://www.revolt.tv/new-music/2020/4/17/21214475/fredo-bang-most-hated-album|website=REVOLT|accessdate=May 22, 2020}}</ref>
On September 25, 2020, he released a new project titled ''In the Name of Gee'', which peaked at number 40 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref name="Gee">{{cite tweet|url=https://twitter.com/FredoBang/status/1308486304976351232?s=19|title="In The Name Of Gee" 9/25...|number=1308486304976351232|user=FredoBang|author=Fredo Bang(confirmed account)|date=September 22, 2020|accessdate=September 25, 2020}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
In January 2016, Givens was arrested on charges of attempted second degree murder. He served two years in jail for the charge before being released in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Louisiana Rapper Fredo Bang Arrested for Attempted Second Degree Murder|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/01/fredo-bang-arrested-for-attempted-second-degree-murder/|website=XXL|accessdate=May 22, 2020}}</ref> He gets off parole in 2023.

On July 22, 2021, Givens was arrested again on a fugitive warrant out of Louisiana. He was arrested a day after his fellow Top Boy Gorilla artist Lit Yoshi, who was arrested on attempted murder charges.

== Personal life ==
Givens identifies as [[spiritual but not religious]], although he believes in a [[God]]. He does not have any children, but looks after Gee Money's children after Gee was murdered in Late 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.listennotes.com/podcasts/the-bootleg-kev/19-fredo-bang-x-hIvC3Y9Vs/|title=#19 – Fredo Bang – The Bootleg Kev Podcast|website=Listen Notes}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/new-music/2020/9/29/21493834/fredo-bang-in-gee-we-trust-video|title=Fredo Bang hangs with his son in new visual for "In Gee We Trust"|first=Jon|last=Powell|date=September 29, 2020|website=REVOLT}}</ref>

In 2021, Givens tested positive for [[COVID-19]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.63869/title.fredo-bang-contracts-covid-19-in-miami-jail|title= FREDO BANG CONTRACTS COVID-19 IN MIAMI JAIL}}</ref>
