'''Oakley Neil H T Caesar-Su''' (born 4 June 1998), known professionally as '''Central Cee''', is a British rapper and songwriter from [[Shepherd's Bush]], [[London]].<ref name="NMEpp"/> His first mixtape ''[[Wild West (mixtape)|Wild West]]'' was released in March 2021, and debuted at two on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref name="OCC" />

==Early life== 
Oakley Neil H T Caesar-Su was born on 4 June 1998 in Shepherd's Bush, London. Growing up, his father would play hip-hop, reggae, and dancehall music around him.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2021-03-17|title=Central Cee: Big Sean-endorsed West London rapper whose career finally thrived during lockdown|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref> Caesar-Su has stated that he kept to himself in school, but would occasionally misbehave and lose his temper. He went to school with fellow rapper [[Digga D]], being a couple of school years higher.<ref>{{Citation|title=Digga D went School with Central Cee and says he’s Cappin/Lying!!|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nq1kdTvX1W0|language=en|access-date=2021-05-24}}</ref> Ceasar-Su worked at a shoe store for three weeks before finding out his wage and quitting.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Central Cee: The Party-Starting Rapper Injecting Fun into Drill|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/4ad973/central-cee-the-party-starting-rapper-injecting-fun-into-drill|access-date=2021-05-24|website=www.vice.com|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
Caesar-Su first appeared on the "Ain't On Nuttin Remix" alongside [[J Hus]], Bonkaz, and more in January 2015, where he adopted the rapper name "Central Cee" (first listed as Central C).<ref>{{Citation|title=Ain't On Nuttin Remix - Bonkaz, J Hus, Reeko, Yung Reeks, Wholagun, Bully, Central C & Shower Malik|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIi6xrK3b6M|language=en|access-date=2021-06-08}}</ref> He released his "StreetHeat Freestyle" in February of the same year.<ref>{{Citation|title=Central Cee - #StreetHeat Freestyle [@Central_Cee] {{!}} Link Up TV|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7BcfYfAgy4k|language=en|access-date=2021-06-08}}</ref> Central Cee released his first project, the album ''17'', in 2017.<ref name="Stereoboard" /> His "Next Up?" freestyle was released in October 2019.<ref>{{Citation|title=Central Cee - Next Up? [S2.E39] {{!}} @MixtapeMadness|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9bSCrxy0DMU|language=en|access-date=2021-06-08}}</ref> 

After switching from [[auto-tuned]] hip hop to a style similar to [[UK drill]], Central Cee released his breakout single "Day in the Life" on 14 June 2020. He followed it up with "Loading", released on 22 October 2020. The music videos for both tracks were released by [[GRM Daily]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Wynter|first=Courtney|date=|title=Premiere: Central Cee Unleashes New Banger "Loading"|url=http://grmdaily.com/video/central-cee-loading|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201110194239/http://grmdaily.com/umbraco/surface/error/servererror?route=http%3A%2F%2Fgrmdaily.com%2Fumbraco%2Fapi%2Fposts%2Fresolve%2Fcentral-cee-loading&msg=server%20error(ajax)|archive-date=2020-11-10|access-date=2020-11-10|website=GRM Daily - Grime, Rap music and Culture}}</ref> He is well known for his singles "Loading", "6 for 6" and "Day in the Life", which combined have over 80 million views on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rahman|first=Aishah|date=2020-10-22|title=Central Cee is 'Loading' in clean new visuals|url=https://www.thelinkup.com/content/2785/central-cee-is-loading-in-clean-new-visuals|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201110195454/https://thelinkup.com/content/2785/central-cee-is-loading-in-clean-new-visuals|archive-date=2020-11-10|access-date=2020-11-10|website=Link Up Tv|language=en}}</ref>  "Loading" and "Commitment Issues" both reached the top 20 of the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2021/mar/12/central-cee-wild-west-review|title=Central Cee: Wild West review – gritty street-level drill from magnetic talent|work=[[The Guardian]]|last=Pritchard|first=Will|date=12 March 2021|access-date=19 March 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=loading {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/search/singles/loading/|access-date=2021-01-16|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|language=en}}</ref> Central Cee [[self-released]] his debut mixtape ''[[Wild West (mixtape)|Wild West]]'' in March 2021, which debuted at number two on the [[UK Albums Chart]] and number one on the [[UK R&B Album Chart|UK R&B Albums Chart]].<ref name="OCC" /> The single "Obsessed With You" reached number 5 on the UK Singles Chart. His second mixtape, ''23'', was announced in November 2021.<ref>{{Citation|title=Central Cee - Obsessed With You (Official Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oP7KHC129RQ|language=en|access-date=2021-11-17}}</ref>
