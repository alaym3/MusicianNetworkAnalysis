'''Paul Robert Wilbur''' (born January 18, 1951) is an American [[Christian music]]ian, [[worship leader]], and guitarist, who primarily plays a [[Messianic Judaism|messianic worship]] style of music. He has released albums with [[Integrity Media|Integrity Music]], [[Hosanna! Music]], [[Epic Records]], and Venture3Media during his career. His first known musical work, ''Up to Zion'', a [[live album]], was released in November 1991.

== Music career ==

Paul Wilbur's recording career began in a group called Harvest with Ed Kerr and Jerry Williams. The trio recorded two albums, the self-titled first album ''Harvest'' (1979) and their second album in 1981 called ''Morning Sun''.{{citation needed|date=January 2016}} Wilbur left after that leaving Kerr and Williams to continue with the band. He joined the group Israel's Hope. They released ''Introducing Israel's Hope'' in 1987.{{citation needed|date=January 2016}}

Wilbur's solo career began in 1991, with the release, ''Up to Zion'', a live album, released by [[Hosanna! Music]]. He has received reviews for nine of his albums from [[Cross Rhythms]]. Those reviewed were ''Shalom Jerusalem'' in 1995, ''Holy Fire'' in 1997, ''Jerusalem Arise!'' in 1999, ''Pray for the Peace of Jerusalem'' in 2002, ''The Watchman'' in 2005, ''Worship from the Heart of Israel'' in 2006, ''Praise Adonai'' in 2007, ''Live: A Night of Extravagant Worship'' in 2008, and ''Desert Rain'' in 2010,<ref name="CR Profile">{{cite web|author=[[Cross Rhythms]]|title=Paul Wilbur Artist Profile |url=http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/artists/Paul_Wilbur/8639/ |publisher=[[Cross Rhythms]]|accessdate=August 13, 2015}}</ref> which was also reviewed by [[Worship Leader (magazine)|''Worship Leader'']].<ref name="WL Review One">{{Cite journal|first=Randy |last=Cross |title=Paul Wilbur: ''Desert Rain'' |url=http://media.worshipleader.com/lib/2011jan/files/html5/index.html?page=70 |journal=[[Worship Leader (magazine)|Worship Leader]] |accessdate=August 14, 2015 |issue=January/February 2011 |page=70}}</ref> The 2013 album, ''Your Great Name'', was reviewed by ''[[CCM Magazine]]''.<ref name="CCM Review">{{Cite journal|first=Matt |last=Conner |title=Paul Wilbur: ''Your Great Name'' |url=http://www.ccmmagazine.com/article/review-your-great-name/ |journal=[[CCM Magazine]] |accessdate=August 14, 2015 |date=May 2, 2013}}</ref>

==Personal life==
At one time Wilbur resided in Jacksonville, Florida. He is married to Luanne Wilbur. They have two Godly sons, Nathan and Joel.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www1.cbn.com/700club/paul-wilbur-extravagant-worship|title=Paul Wilbur: Extravagant Worship|date=August 19, 2009|website=CBN.com - The Christian Broadcasting Network|accessdate=August 17, 2021}}</ref>
