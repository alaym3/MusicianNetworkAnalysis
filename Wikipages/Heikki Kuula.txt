'''Heikki Kuula''' (born 27 January 1983) is a Finnish rapper, record producer and graphic designer. He is known for his frequent collaborations with the fellow rapper [[Pyhimys]] who has also served as an executive producer on Kuula's albums.<ref>{{cite book|last=Paleface|title=Rappiotaidetta – Suomiräpin tekijät|year=2011|publisher=Like Kustannus Oy|location=Helsinki|isbn=978-952-01-0655-3|pages=79}}</ref>

==Career beginnings==

Heikki Kuula released his first album ''Hoodipihvii'' with [[Voli (rapper)|Voli]] in 2004, and two years later they teamed up again for ''Wordcup''. That year Kuula also released his first solo album ''Vihreä salmiakki''. His second solo album ''PLLP'' arrived in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|title=Heikki Kuula Discography|url=http://www.discogs.com/artist/Heikki+Kuula|publisher=Discogs|accessdate=7 February 2013}}</ref>

==Commercial success==

Kuula's third solo album ''Blacksuami'' was released in May 2010 and was the first to appear on the [[The Official Finnish Charts|Official Finnish Album Chart]] where it reached the position 41.<ref>{{cite web|title=Heikki Kuula: Blacksuami|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/heikki+kuula/blacksuami|publisher=Musiikkituottajat ry|accessdate=7 February 2013}}</ref> Heikki Kuula started to gain more recognition later that year after he appeared as a featured artist on the song "Epoo", a debut single by the rap duo [[JVG (band)|Jare & VilleGalle]]. Kuula also appeared on their next single "Nelisilmä" which reached number 17 on the [[The Official Finnish Charts|Official Finnish Singles Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Jare & VilleGalle Feat. Heikki Kuula: Nelisilmä|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/jare+%26+villegalle+feat.+heikki+kuula/nelisilmä|publisher=Musiikkituottajat ry|accessdate=7 February 2013}}</ref>

In February 2012, Heikki Kuula and Pyhimys released an album ''Katuvisioita'', using the pseudonyms Perhosveitsi-Heikki and Lika-Aki.<ref>{{cite web|title=Katuvisioita|url=http://www.pyhimys.com/levyt/katuvisioita/|publisher=Pyhimys / Yellowmic.|accessdate=6 February 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131215111207/http://www.pyhimys.com/levyt/katuvisioita/|archive-date=15 December 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album peaked at number nine on the Official Finnish Album Chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Perhosveitsi-Heikki Ja Lika-Aki: Katuvisioita|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/perhosveitsi-heikki+ja+lika-aki/katuvisioita|publisher=Musiikkituottajat ry|accessdate=7 February 2013}}</ref>

==Teflon Brothers==

Heikki Kuula is also a part of the hip hop and rap group [[Teflon Brothers]] with Pyhimys and Voli. They have released three albums so far; ''T'' (2009), ''©'' (2010) and ''Valkoisten dyynien ratsastajat'' (2013).<ref>{{cite web|title=Levyt|url=http://www.pyhimys.com/levyt/|publisher=Pyhimys / Yellowmic.|accessdate=7 February 2013}}</ref> The latter has become their most successful release to date, peaking at number six on the Official Finnish Album Chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Teflon Brothers: Valkoisten Dyynien Ratsastajat|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/teflon+brothers/valkoisten+dyynien+ratsastajat|publisher=Musiikkituottajat ry|accessdate=7 February 2013}}</ref> The album has also spawned a single "Seksikkäin jäbä", featuring [[Stig (singer)|Stig]] and [[Meiju Suvas]], which reached number three on the Official Finnish Singles Chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Teflon Brothers: Seksikkäin Jäbä (Feat. Stig & Meiju Suvas)|url=http://www.ifpi.fi/tilastot/virallinen-lista/artistit/teflon+brothers/seksikkäin+jäbä+(feat.+stig+%26+meiju+suvas)|publisher=Musiikkituottajat ry|accessdate=6 February 2013}}</ref>
