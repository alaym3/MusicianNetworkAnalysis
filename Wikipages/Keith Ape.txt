'''Dongheon Lee''' (이동헌; born December 25, 1993), known professionally as '''Keith Ape''' ({{Korean|키스 에이프|links=no}}) and previously known as '''Kid Ash''', is a [[South Korea]]n rapper from [[Seoul]].<ref name="complex">{{cite web|last=Kwak|first=Donnie|title=Keith Ape Is Ready to Be the World’s Next Trap Star |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2015/07/keith-ape-trap-star-interview|date=July 29, 2015|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|access-date=January 2, 2015}}</ref> He is a member of a crew called 'The Cohort'. Ape's breakout single "잊지마" ("It G Ma") was released on January 1, 2015,<ref name="complex"/><ref>{{Cite web|title = KEITH APE'15 ⊕|url = https://soundcloud.com/keithape|website = SoundCloud|access-date = 2015-12-14}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DPC9erC5WqU|access-date = 2015-12-14|title = 잊지마 (It G Ma)}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Kameir |first=Rawiya |title=This Guy In South Korea Is Taking His Obsession With American Rap To The Next Level |url= http://www.thefader.com/2015/01/23/keith-ape-is-the-south-korean-king-of-rap-covers |date=January 23, 2015 |work=[[The Fader]] |access-date=January 2, 2015}}</ref> and was ranked by [[Billboard K-Town|''Billboard'' K-Town]] at number five on their list of the Best K-Pop Songs of 2015.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Jeff Benjamin |author2=Jessica Oak |title=The 20 Best K-Pop Songs of 2015 |url= http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/6828140/best-k-pop-songs-2015 |date=December 30, 2015 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=January 3, 2016}}</ref>

==Musical style and reception==
Keith Ape has been called the "Korean [[OG Maco]]" and noted for an exciting concert performance at 2015's [[South by Southwest]] [[Rapping|rap]] showcase, among other performances which included [[Young Thug]], [[Desiigner]], [[Waka Flocka Flame]], [[XXXTentacion]], and more.<ref>{{cite web |last=Kramer |first=Kyle |title=The Craziest Rap Show at SXSW Was a Korean Import |url= http://noisey.vice.com/blog/sxsw-2015-keith-ape-review-recap |date=March 22, 2015 |work=[[Vice (magazine)|Noisey]] |access-date=January 2, 2016}}</ref> His concert at [[SOB's]] in 2015 was listed as one of ''[[The New York Times]]'' top 40 picks, "unrestrained mayhem", and "a clear inheritor of [[Southern hip hop|Southern rap]] rowdiness".<ref>{{cite web |author1=Jon Pareles |author2=Ben Ratliff |author3=Jon Caramanica |author4=Nate Chinendec |title=All the Best Concerts, of What the Critics Have Seen |url= https://www.nytimes.com/2015/12/30/arts/music/all-the-best-shows-of-what-the-critics-have-seen.html?_r=1 |date=December 29, 2015 |access-date=January 2, 2016}}</ref>

==Controversy==
On February 4, 2015, he was accused by American rapper [[OG Maco]] of [[cultural appropriation]].<ref name="cultural">{{cite web|title=American rapper OG Maco accuses Korean rapper Keith Ape of cultural appropriation by copying 'U Guessed It'|url=http://www.allkpop.com/buzz/2015/02/american-rapper-og-maco-accuses-korean-rapper-keith-ape-of-cultural-appropriation-by-copying-u-guessed-it|date=February 7, 2015|work=[[Allkpop]]|access-date=February 23, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160301192243/http://www.allkpop.com/buzz/2015/02/american-rapper-og-maco-accuses-korean-rapper-keith-ape-of-cultural-appropriation-by-copying-u-guessed-it|archive-date=March 1, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> OG Maco claimed Keith Ape and friends had mocked him by using black stereotypes to sell music in their video of "It G Ma". He also claimed that they had [[plagiarism|plagiarized]] the basis for their track from his debut single "[[U Guessed It]]".<ref name="cultural"/> As of August 13, 2015, OG Maco collects royalties from "It G Ma", and has since deleted his tweets regarding his accusations of cultural appropriation.<ref name="rowdy">{{cite web |last=Caramanica, Jon |title=Getting Rowdy: Keith Ape and Real Rap in Korea|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/08/16/arts/music/getting-rowdy-keith-ape-and-real-rap-in-korea.html?_r=1|date=August 13, 2015|work=The New York Times|access-date=February 23, 2016}}</ref> Nonetheless, he declined an invitation by Keith Ape to be part of a later remix rendition of "It G Ma".<ref name="rowdy"/>
