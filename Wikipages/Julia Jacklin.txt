'''Julia Jacklin''' (born 30 August 1990) is an Australian singer-songwriter from Sydney, Australia. Jacklin's musical style has been described as [[indie pop]], [[indie folk]], and [[alternative country]]. She has released two studio albums, ''[[Don't Let the Kids Win]]'' (2016) and ''[[Crushing (album)|Crushing]]'' (2019). Jacklin has also performed with the band Phantastic Ferniture, with whom she released the debut single "Fuckin 'n' Rollin" and a self-titled album in 2018, followed by subsequent singles.

== Career ==
=== 2014-2015: Phantastic Ferniture ===
In 2014, Jacklin formed the indie garage group Phantastic Ferniture along with members Elizabeth Hughes (guitar) and Ryan K Brennan (drums).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://phantasticferniture.com/bio|title=ALBUM OUT NOW – BIO|website=phantasticferniture.com|access-date=12 July 2019}}</ref>

In May 2018, the band released their debut single "Fuckin 'n' Rollin", accompanied by a music video directed by Nick McKinlay. In July 2018 the band released its self-titled album, produced by drummer Ryan K Brennan. The album featured subsequent singles "Gap Year", "Bad Timing" and "Dark Corner Dance Floor".

===2016: ''Don't Let the Kids Win''===
She gained an audience and significant critical acclaim through her first two singles "Pool Party" and "Coming of Age" which both received radio airplay on [[BBC Radio 6 Music]].<ref name="Compare My Radio">{{Cite web |url=http://comparemyradio.com/stations/BBC_6_Music |title=Archived copy |access-date=27 October 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190212011207/http://comparemyradio.com/stations/BBC_6_Music |archive-date=12 February 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She has been touring extensively since March 2016 in the US, UK, Europe and Australia, appearing at various festivals (most notably [[End of the Road Festival]], [[Electric Picnic]] and [[South by Southwest]]). She has played headline gigs and has also supported artists such as [[First Aid Kit (band)|First Aid Kit]], [[Whitney (band)|Whitney]], [[Marlon Williams (New Zealand musician)|Marlon Williams]] and [[Okkervil River]].<ref name="Song Kick">{{cite web|url=https://www.songkick.com/artists/8048068-julia-jacklin/gigography|title=Julia Jacklin|website=SongKick.com|access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref> In 2016, ''[[Rolling Stone Australia]]'' tipped Jacklin as one of their "Future Is Now" artists,<ref name="Rolling Stone Australia">{{cite web | last=Shanahan | first=Lucy | url=http://rollingstoneaus.com/music/post/future-is-now-julia-jacklin-ceres-nao-terry-tash-sultana-australia/5029 | title=Rolling Stone Australia | publisher=Rolling Stone Australia | date=25 October 2016 | access-date=26 October 2016 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161026232626/http://rollingstoneaus.com/music/post/future-is-now-julia-jacklin-ceres-nao-terry-tash-sultana-australia/5029 | archive-date=26 October 2016 | url-status=dead }}</ref> while [[Triple J]] nominated her for a [[J Award]] for Unearthed Artist of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/jawards/16/nominations/|title=J Awards 2016: Nominations for 2016|publisher=Triple J|date=2 November 2016|access-date=2 November 2016}}</ref> Jacklin did not consider herself a full-time musician until August 2016, when she really started touring and figured she could not manage her regular job anymore.<ref name="PTA2017">{{Cite web|url=http://www.passtheaux.co/julia-jacklin-interview/|title=Interview: Julia Jacklin on L.A., becoming a full-time musician, In N Out Burger|work=Pass The Aux|access-date=13 April 2017}}</ref>

Her debut studio album, [[Don't Let The Kids Win (album)|''Don't Let The Kids Win'']], was released in October 2016 on [[Transgressive Records]].<ref name=":0" /> ''[[The Guardian]]'' described it as "one of those albums that will slowly creep into the affections of a large number of people" while ''Rolling Stone Australia'' found her songs "simple and unadorned".<ref>''Don't Let The Kids Win'' reviews:

===2017: "Eastwick" / "Cold Caller"===
In September 2017, Julia Jacklin released a 7" single featuring two songs, once again recorded with producer Ben Edwards in Lyttelton, New Zealand. Touring in support of the single, Jacklin headlined [[Shepherd's Bush Empire]] in London<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://afterdark.co/events/london/o2-shepherds-bush-empire/julia-jacklin--o2-shepherds-bush-empire|title=Julia Jacklin / O2 Shepherds Bush Empire|website=AfterDark.co|access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref> on top of selling out shows in Los Angeles, New York City, [[Toronto]], [[Melbourne]], [[Sydney]] and cities throughout the United Kingdom.

=== 2018-present: ''Crushing'' ===
In March 2018, Jacklin confirmed, via social media, that she had completed her second album. The album was recorded with producer [[Burke Reid]] ([[Courtney Barnett]], [[The Drones (Australian band)|The Drones]]) and features Blain Cunneen (guitar), Dominic Rizzo (piano), Clayton Allen (drums), Harry Fuller (bass) and Georgia Mulligan (backing vocals). Singles released prior to the album's release included "Body", "Head Alone", "Pressure To Party" and "Comfort". [[Crushing (album)|''Crushing'']] was released in February 2019, and was warmly received by critics with an average score of 85 on [[Metacritic]], based on 26 reviews from mainstream publications.<ref>{{cite web|title=Crushing by Julia Jacklin|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/crushing/julia-jacklin|publisher=Metacritic|access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref> In light of the release of ''Crushing,'' Jacklin came at number 4 in ''Happy Mag''<nowiki/>'s list of "The 15 Australian female artists changing the game right now".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://happymag.tv/the-15-australian-female-artists-changing-the-game-right-now/|title=The 15 Australian female artists changing the game right now|language=en-US|access-date=30 October 2019}}</ref>

Jacklin has stated that the song, "Head Alone" was written about personal boundaries, contrasting with the encroachment of her personal and physical space during album tours and relationships.<ref name=":2" />

The tour in support of [[Crushing (album)|''Crushing'']] has included festival dates at [[Shaky Knees Music Festival]], [[Latitude Festival]] and [[Forecastle Festival]], as well as sold out headline shows for the majority of her world tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.songkick.com/artists/8048068-julia-jacklin|title=Julia Jacklin|website=Songkick.com|access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref>

In October 2020 Julia Jacklin released a 7" single for the fifth volume of the [[Sub Pop Singles Club]] containing two new songs, "to Perth, before the border closes" and "CRY".<ref>{{cite web |title=Julia Jacklin shares new songs 'to Perth, before the border closes' and 'CRY' |url=https://diymag.com/2020/10/12/listen-julia-jacklin-to-perth-before-the-border-closes-cry-sub-pop-singles-club-vol-5 |website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |access-date=24 July 2021 |date=12 October 2020}}</ref>

==Musical and visual style==
Allmusic described her music as a "meld of dreamy indie pop and confessional alt-country",<ref name=":0" /> while she cites as her influences [[Doris Day]], [[The Andrews Sisters]], [[Björk]] and [[Billy Bragg]].<ref name=":1" /><ref name="Australian Musician">{{cite web | last=Phillips | first=Greg | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CBGFs1efBCY | title= Australian Musician | access-date=28 October 2016}}</ref> She has also mentioned [[Fiona Apple]] and [[Leonard Cohen]] as influences.<ref name=PTA2017/>

In an interview with ''Sound of Boston'', Jacklin notes her music video style is inspired by [[Lars Tunbjörk]], a Swedish photographer who captured the mundane and absurd moments of modern life.<ref name="Sound of Boston"/> Jacklin has co-directing credits on all of her music videos to date. In February 2019, [[Stella Donnelly]] released a music video for her single ''"Tricks"'', in which Jacklin co-directed with longtime collaborator Nick McKinlay.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2019/02/27/stella-donnelly-tricks-video-directed-julia-jacklin-beware-of-the-dogs-watch|title=Stella Donnelly shares 'Tricks' video, directed by Julia Jacklin|last=Subscribe|website=DIY|access-date=9 April 2019}}</ref>
