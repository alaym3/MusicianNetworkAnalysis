'''Los Cuates de Sinaloa''' are a [[Norteño (music)|Norteño]] band led by cousins Gabriel and Nano Berrelleza, originally from [[La Vainilla]], [[Sinaloa]], but currently based in [[Phoenix, Arizona]].<ref>Billboard – 11 October 2008 – Page 40  Los Cuates de Sinaloa are cousins Gabriel and Nano Berrelleza, who perform corridos in the guitar-based sierreno style.</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.holaciudad.com/Cuates-Sinaloa-atentado-sufrieron-Mexico_0_449955015.html|title=Este domingo: Los Cuates de Sinaloa revelan todo sobre el terrible atentado que sufrieron en México|publisher=}}</ref> The band signed to [[Sony BMG]] in 2006. Their 2006 album, ''Puro Sierreno Bravo'', was number 13 in the Top Latin Albums.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/307995/los-cuates-de-sinaloa/biography|title=Los Cuates de Sinaloa – Biography|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jornada.unam.mx/2011/05/28/espectaculos/a12n1esp|title=La Jornada: Mientras más prohíban los narcocorridos, más los pedirán: Los cuates de Sinaloa|first=DEMOS, Desarrollo de Medios, S. A. de|last=C.V|date=28 May 2011|publisher=}}</ref> Their song "Negro y Azul" provided the opening music for the ''[[Breaking Bad]]'' episode "[[Negro y Azul]]", the song was an [[narcocorrido]] homage and performed as a [[New Mexico music]] style [[ranchera]], referencing the show's [[Neo-Western]] and [[New Mexico]] setting.<ref>David P. Pierson ''Breaking Bad: Critical Essays on the Contexts, Politics, Style, and Reception of the Television Series'' 2013  073917925X "Los Cuates de Sinaloa ... At the end of 2009, the band published the song for the first time in the record ''En Vivo con la Plebada'' (Sony Music) and it has recently been republished in the compilation record ''Mis favoritas'' (Sony Music, 2012)."</ref>
