'''Benjamin Evans Rector''' (born November 6, 1986)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ben-rector-mn0002536650/biography|title=Ben Rector &#124; Biography & History|website=AllMusic}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter and record producer based in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. He has released seven studio albums, including ''[[Brand New (Ben Rector album)|Brand New]]'' (2015), which peaked at number 9 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]],<ref name=billboard200/> and ''[[Magic (Ben Rector album)|Magic]]'' (2018), which debuted at number 1 on the ''Billboard'' [[Americana/Folk Albums]] chart.<ref name="shorefire" />

== Early life ==
Rector was born in [[Tulsa, Oklahoma|Tulsa]], [[Oklahoma]]. He took piano lessons as a child, but he did not pursue music until he picked up guitar in high school, which later led him to begin songwriting. He graduated from Tulsa's Metro Christian Academy in 2005 and from the [[University of Arkansas]] in 2009, with a degree in business and marketing.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Chancellor |first=Jennifer |title=Tulsa native Ben Rector shoots to stardom on his own steam |work=Tulsa World |url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/scene/tulsa-native-ben-rector-shoots-to-stardom-on-his-own/article_6cef9f85-d0f0-57d4-98dd-532425fdac07.html |access-date=December 20, 2017}}</ref>

== Music career ==

During his first year at the University of Arkansas, Rector released a self-titled [[extended play|EP]] that featured the song "Conversation", which went on to win the Grand Prize in the Pop category of the John Lennon Songwriting Contest in 2006.<ref name="JLSC">{{Cite web |title=2006 Grand Prize Winners – Session I |url=http://www.jlsc.com/winners/2006a/winners.php |access-date=September 5, 2011 |website=JLSC.com |publisher=The John Lennon Songwriting Contest}}</ref> He is the youngest person ever to win the award for the pop category.<ref>{{Cite web |title=For Ben Rector, It's All About the Music {{!}} Arkansas Alumni Association's Blog |url=https://blog.arkansasalumni.org/for-ben-rector-its-all-about-the-music/ |access-date=August 14, 2018 |website=blog.arkansasalumni.org}}</ref> The EP grew popular in surrounding states, and Rector began touring frequently on weekends and during breaks in the school year.<ref name="sltbillboard" />

Rector released his first full-length album ''Twenty Tomorrow'' in 2007 and its follow-up ''Songs That Duke Wrote'' in 2008.<ref name="ccp">{{Cite web |last=Parker |first=Chris |date=April 3, 2013 |title=Ben Rector isn't in a hurry to release his new album |url=http://www.charlestoncitypaper.com/charleston/ben-rector-isnt-in-a-hurry-to-release-his-new-album/Content?oid=4599771 |access-date=September 5, 2015 |website=[[Charleston City Paper]]}}</ref>

In April 2009 he was given the Northwest Arkansas Music Award (NAMA) for best male singer-songwriter in Northwest Arkansas.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Bartholomew |first=Dustin |date=April 24, 2009 |title=The 2009 NAMA Winners |url=http://www.fayettevilleflyer.com/2009/04/24/the-2009-nama-winners/ |access-date=September 5, 2015 |website=[[Fayetteville Flyer]]}}</ref> Later that year, he moved to Nashville to pursue songwriting and to continue his career as an artist.<ref name="ccp" />

Rector's third studio album, ''[[Into the Morning]]'', was released on February 16, 2010, and reached No.&nbsp;11 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Top Heatseekers]] chart.<ref name="sltbillboard">[http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/467318/ben-rector-is-a-gimmick-free-pop-nobody Billboard: Ben Rector Is a Gimmick Free Pop 'Nobody']. ''Billboard''. Posted: September 25, 2011. Retrieved February 23, 2012.</ref><ref name="go">[http://blog.lp33.tv/festival/ben-rector-family-friendly Ben Rector: Family Friendly] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100616031231/http://blog.lp33.tv/festival/ben-rector-family-friendly |date=June 16, 2010 }}. Blog.lp33.tv. April 9, 2010. Retrieved September 5, 2011.</ref> Following the release, he co-headlined the Three Amigos Tour with fellow Nashvillians Steve Moakler and [[Andrew Ripp]]. The rest of 2010 brought support slots with [[Dave Barnes]] and [[Five for Fighting]], The Beat Lives Forever co-headline tour with [[Drew Holcomb]] and The Neighbors, and the Feels Like Home tour in Germany, organized by Johannes Strate of the German band [[Revolverheld]].<ref name="sltbillboard" />

In early 2011 Rector headlined a tour in the Midwest, supported fellow Nashville artist [[Matt Wertz]]'s Weights & Wings national release tour, and performed on the [[VH1]] Best Cruise Ever with artists including [[Train (band)|Train]], [[The Script]], [[Colbie Caillat]] and [[Lifehouse (band)|Lifehouse]].<ref name="BCE">[http://archive.sltrib.com/article.php?id=14421165&itype=storyID The Salt Lake Tribune: VH1 Best Cruise Ever, featuring Train, Lifehouse, Colbie Caillat, The Script, setting sail soon]. ''The Salt Lake Tribune''</ref>

Rector's fourth studio album ''[[Something Like This]]'' was released on September 13, 2011, reaching number 41 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref name="sltbillboard" /> In mid-2011, Rector co-produced Steve Moakler's ''Watching Time Run''. Following the release of ''Something Like This'' Rector headlined the Good Time Tour which spanned 25 cities with Andrew Belle and Graham Colton as support. Rector supported [[Needtobreathe]] during their The Reckoning tour in early 2012, which continued until May 2013.<ref name="NTBTRT">[http://www.needtobreathe.net/blog/fe66d7-the-reckoning-spring-tour-2012/ NEEDTOBREATHE: The Reckoning Spring Tour 2012] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111224001542/http://www.needtobreathe.net/blog/fe66d7-the-reckoning-spring-tour-2012/ |date=December 24, 2011 }}. needtobreathe.net</ref>

''[[The Walking in Between]]'' was released on August 20, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ben Rector album review: 'The Walking In Between' |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/goingoutguide/ben-rector-album-review-the-walking-in-between/2013/10/23/5eefbc1e-36a7-11e3-8a0e-4e2cf80831fc_story.html |access-date=August 1, 2018 |website=The Washington Post}}</ref> Produced by Rector, Jamie Kenney and Charlie Peacock, it was the first release on Rector's own Aptly Named Recordings label. The album debuted at No.&nbsp;16 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ben Rector Album Debuts at 16 on Billboard 200 |url=http://www.alternativeaddiction.com/musicnews/article/3386/Ben-Rector-Album-Debuts-at-16-on-the-Billboard-200 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130926192853/http://www.alternativeaddiction.com/musicnews/article/3386/Ben-Rector-Album-Debuts-at-16-on-the-Billboard-200 |archive-date=September 26, 2013 |access-date=September 22, 2013}}</ref>

In 2014, Rector briefly took a break from songwriting to front the [[Huey Lewis and the News]] cover band Newy Lewis and the Hues.<ref name="tfp">{{Cite news |date=March 20, 2019 |title=Ben Rector brings 'Magic' to Memorial Auditorium |publisher=Chattanooga Times Free Press |url=https://www.timesfreepress.com/news/chattanooganow/music/story/2019/mar/20/ben-rector-brings-magic-town/490919/ |access-date=January 23, 2020}}</ref>

Rector toured with [[Needtobreathe]], [[Drew Holcomb]] and the Neighbors, and [[Colony House (band)|Colony House]] for the first leg of the "Tour De Compadres" tour in early 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tour Dates |url=http://www.needtobreathe.com/tour |access-date=February 8, 2016 |website=needtobreathe Official Website}}</ref> He co-headlined [[The Rock Boat]] in 2017,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Past Lineups |url=https://www.therockboat.com/history/lineup/ |access-date=January 23, 2020 |website=therockboat.com}}</ref> and supported [[Tim McGraw]] and [[Faith Hill]] on their 2017 [[Soul2Soul Tour]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Nicholson |first=Jessica |title=Tim McGraw, Faith Hill Reveal Opening Acts For 2017 Soul2Soul Tour |url=https://musicrow.com/2017/01/tim-mcgraw-faith-hill-reveal-opening-acts-for-2017-soul2soul-tour/ |access-date=January 23, 2020 |website=Music Row}}</ref>

Rector released his sixth studio album, ''[[Brand New (Ben Rector album)|Brand New]]'', on August 28, 2015, via Aptly Named Recordings. The album debuted No. 9 on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart, marking his first Top 10 album.<ref>{{Cite web |title=On the Charts: The Weeknd Finds 'Beauty' at Number One |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/on-the-charts-the-weeknd-finds-beauty-at-number-one-20150906 |access-date=October 13, 2015 |website=Rolling Stone}}</ref> It also debuted at No. 2 on the ''Billboard'' Top Current Rock Albums chart, No. 2 on the ''Billboard'' Album Core Genre Rock chart, No. 6 on the ''Billboard'' Album Sales chart, No. 3 on the ''Billboard'' Top Current Digital Album Sales chart, and No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Top Folk Albums chart.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The Weeknd's New Album Debuts at Number One with an Impressive Sales Week |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2015/09/06/the-weeknds-new-album-debuts-at-number-one-with-big-sales-week/ |access-date=October 13, 2015 |website=Forbes}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=The Week in Pop: Halsey's New Americana Might Actually Be A Real Demographic |url=http://www.stereogum.com/1829157/the-week-in-pop-halseys-new-americana-might-actually-be-a-real-demographic/franchises/the-week-in-pop/ |access-date=October 13, 2015 |website=Stereogum}}</ref>
