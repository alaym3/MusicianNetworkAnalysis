'''Oceans Ate Alaska''' are a British [[metalcore]] band hailing from [[Birmingham]] formed in 2010. The band are currently signed to [[Fearless Records]] and are known for "uniting boundaries between multiple genres in modern metal" and for "fusing unpredictable polyrhythms and sporadic partnering of melodic and dissonant passages".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://fearlessrecords.com/artists/oceans-ate-alaska/|title=Oceans Ate Alaska|website=Fearlessrecords.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> Original vocalist, James Harrison came up with the band name; naming it after the 1950 [[Lituya Bay]] tsunami that swallowed one of Alaska’s gulfs.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.pressreader.com/uk/kerrang-uk/20170419/281560880668624 |title=Archived copy |access-date=25 October 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191025140655/https://www.pressreader.com/uk/kerrang-uk/20170419/281560880668624 |archive-date=25 October 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==History==
Oceans Ate Alaska was founded in 2010 by [[University of Birmingham|Birmingham University's]] students James Harrison, James 'Jibs' Kennedy, Josh Salthouse, Alex Hurdley and Chris Turner. The band would go on to release their first single, "Taming Lions" on 4 August followed by their second single "Clocks" on the 26th both accompanied by a music video and being released through Great Escape Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5WQN2Ecz_ME |title=Oceans Ate Alaska - Clocks [Official Video |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2011-08-26 |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YquENum0u8Q |title=Oceans Ate Alaska - Taming Lions [Debut Single |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2011-08-04 |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> On 7 May 2012 the band released their debut EP, "Into the Deep" through Vagrant Records they would go onto rerelease the EP later that year on Density Records.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Kraus|first1=Brian|title=Oceans Ate Alaska sign to Density Records|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/oceans_ate_alaska_sign_to_density_records|magazine=Altpress.com|access-date=16 December 2014}}</ref> On 2 April the band released the music video for their song, "To Catch a Flame" from their new EP.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZWFJXyZY5sY |title=Oceans Ate Alaska - To Catch a Flame [Official Video |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2012-04-02 |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> The band released a physical edition of their "Into the Deep" EP on 13 November thanks to their new label, Density.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rockmusicreport.com/englands-oceans-ate-alaska-sign-to-density-records/|title=England's Oceans Ate Alaska sign to Density Records|website=Rockmusicreport.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

On 7 October 2014, the band announced that they have signed to [[Fearless Records]] and will be releasing their forthcoming debut album in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Fearless Records signs Oceans Ate Alaska|url=http://lambgoat.com/news/23415/Fearless-Records-signs-Oceans-Ate-Alaska|website=Lambgoat.com|access-date=16 December 2014}}</ref> Along with the announcement the band also released their first song from their new album, "Blood Brothers".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/oceans_ate_alaska_sign_to_fearless_records_stream_new_single/|title=Oceans Ate Alaska sign to Fearless Records; stream new single|date=8 October 2014}}</ref>

On 12 November, the band announced that they would be featured on Fearless Records' compilation album [[Punk Goes Pop 6]], covering [[Beyoncé]]'s "[[Drunk in Love]]".<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Sharp|first1=Tyler|title=Listen to parts of Oceans Ate Alaska's 'Punk Goes Pop Vol. 6' cover|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/listen_to_parts_of_oceans_ate_alaskas_punk_goes_pop_vol._6_cover|magazine=Altpress.com|access-date=16 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Video: Oceans Ate Alaska In-Studio Recording Cover of Beyonce's "Drunk In Love"|url=http://www.revolvermag.com/news/video-oceans-ate-alaska-in-the-studio-recording-cover-of-beyonces-drunk-in-love.html|website=Revolvermag.com|access-date=16 December 2014}}</ref>

On 6 January 2015, the second single and lead track from their new album, "Floorboards" was released and three days later the band released a guitar play through of the track.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.guitarworld.com/artists/oceans-ate-alaska-premiere-floorboards-playthrough-video-exclusive|title=Oceans Ate Alaska Premiere "Floorboards" Playthrough Video — Exclusive|first=Guitar World Staff 09|last=June 2015|website=Guitarworld.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/oceans_ate_alaska_release_lead_single_from_debut_album_lost_isles/|title=Oceans Ate Alaska release lead single from debut album, 'Lost Isles'|website=Altpress.com|date=6 January 2015|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> On the 19th the band released a video detailing the story behind their new album, ''Lost Isles''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/oceans_ate_alaska_tell_the_story_behind_their_debut_album_in_premiere_video/|title=Watch Oceans Ate Alaska tell the story behind their debut album (exclusive)|first=Sarah|last=Force|website=Altpress.com|date=19 January 2016}}</ref>

On 27 January 2015, the band released their music video for the third single from their new album, "Vultures and Sharks".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vMZ2mAkDBH0 |title=Oceans Ate Alaska – Vultures And Sharks (Official Music Video) |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2015-01-26 |access-date=2016-07-19}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/oceans_ate_alaska_rock_out_in_the_rain_for_their_vultures_and_sharks_music/|title=Oceans Ate Alaska rock out in the rain for "Vultures And Sharks" music video|website=Altpress.com|date=27 January 2015}}</ref>

The band would go on to release their debut album ''[[Lost Isles]]'' on 24 February 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wb35YFNgPtk |title=Oceans Ate Alaska – Floorboards (Lyric Video) |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2015-01-06 |access-date=2016-07-19}}</ref>

On 11 May, the band released a music video for their song "High Horse".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PywY6OpmOhI |title=Oceans Ate Alaska - High Horse (Official Music Video) |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2016-05-11 |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> Three days later ''Oceans Ate Alaska'' were announced to be playing their first ever United States tour in the Summer of 2015 as part of [[Northlane]]'s Node Tour along with [[Like Moths to Flames]] and [[In Hearts Wake]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thecirclepit.com/2015/05/northlane-like-moths-to-flames-in-hearts-wake-oceans-ate-alaska-tour|title=Northlane, Like Moths To Flames, In Hearts Wake, Oceans Ate Alaska Tour &#124; The Circle Pit|website=Thecirclepit.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

[[file:Oceans ate 2018 (2).png|thumb|Oceans Ate Alaska in 2018.]]

The band would go on to play the entire 2016 Vans Warped Tour in the United States alongside bands like [[Atreyu]], [[Sum 41]], [[Wage War]], [[Whitechapel (band)|Whitechapel]], [[New Found Glory]], [[The Word Alive]] and many more groups.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bringthenoise.com/blog/take-a-behind-the-scenes-look-at-oceans-ate|title=Take A Behind-The-Scenes Look At Oceans Ate Alaska's Recent Run On The Vans Warped Tour / the NOISE|website=Bringthenoise.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://diffuser.fm/warped-tour-2016-lineup/|title=Warped Tour Announces Full 2016 Lineup With New Found Glory, Good Charlotte + More|first=Beth|last=Kellmurray|website=Diffuser.fm|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

On 16 December 2016, the band announced that they had parted ways with vocalist James Harrison, but would remain on good terms. They added that they would reveal their new vocalist in the coming weeks. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/oceansatealaska/posts/1250652484991268|title=Oceans Ate Alaska|website=Facebook.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

On 3 February 2017, the band officially revealed that Jake Noakes would replace Harrison as the band's lead vocalist. The band released the following statement in regards to the replacement: " Our new vocalist is our very close personal friend and incredibly talented singer/screamer Jake Noakes from the UK! It's our honour to be performing alongside of him from here on out, he's an absolute monster!"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/amp/oceans-ate-alaska-have-a-new-vocalist|title=Oceans Ate Alaska Have A New Vocalist|website=Rocksound.tv}}</ref>

On 25 May 2017, the band released "Covert", the first single with Noakes performing vocals.<ref>{{cite web|last = Pryor| first = Terrance|url=https://www.axs.com/oceans-ate-alaska-release-new-song-covert-119061 |title=Oceans Ate Alaska release new song 'Covert' |website=Axs.com |access-date= 26 May 2017}}</ref>

On 9 June 2017, the band officially announced their sophomore album, "Hikari", would be released on 28 July and would feature a blend of Japanese instruments with drummer Chris Turner, stating that "We have a huge amount of respect for Japanese culture, especially their music" and "These unique and beautiful instruments have inspired us to create something brand new, and we are beyond proud of the end result!".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/oceans_ate_alaska_announce_new_album_hikari/|title=Oceans Ate Alaska announce new album and other news you might have missed today|first=Jordan|last=Toney|website=Altpress.com|date=9 June 2017}}</ref> The following day, Fearless Records released a video introducing fans of Oceans Ate Alaska to the band's new vocalist, Jake Noakes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/fearlessrecords/videos/an-introduction-to-jake-noakes-oceans-ate-alaska-vocalist/1449488371774344/|title=An introduction to Jake Noakes - Oceans Ate Alaska vocalist.|website=Facebook.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

On 28 July 2017, the band released their album ''[[Hikari (album)|Hikari]]'' as well as a music video for their song "Hansha".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bringthenoise.com/blog/oceans-ate-alaska-debut-thought-provoking-music|title=Oceans Ate Alaska Debut Thought Provoking Music Video for "Hansha" / the NOISE|website=Bringthenoise.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

On 14 September 2017, the band was announced to be participating as support for [[Wage War]]'s massively heavy five band "Deadweight Tour Part Two" alongside [[Gideon (band)|Gideon]], [[Varials]] and Loathe beginning on 24 November.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bringthenoise.com/blog/wage-war-announce-deadweight-tour-part-two-with|title=Wage War Announce Deadweight Tour Part Two With Oceans Ate Alaska, Gideon, Varials And More / the NOISE|website=Bringthenoise.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

On 5 January 2018, the band revealed that they will be participating on [[We Came As Romans]]' "Cold Like War Tour" with [[The Plot in You]], Currents and Tempting Fate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nextmosh.com/2018/01/05/we-came-as-romans-cold-like-war-tour/ |title=WE CAME AS ROMANS, THE PLOT IN YOU, OCEANS ATE ALASKA + MORE TO TOUR TOGETHER |website=Nextmosh.com |date=2015-01-05 |access-date=2018-04-08}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/we_came_as_romans_2018_tour_dates/|title=We Came As Romans announce Cold Like War Tour with the Plot In You, Oceans Ate Alaska, more|website=Altpress.com|date=5 January 2018}}</ref>

On 15 August 2018, the band was announced to opening for [[Like Moths to Flames]] on their fall "Dark Divine" tour alongside [[Phinehas (band)|Phinehas]] and Novelists beginning on 8 November.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/like-moths-to-flames-headlining-fall-tour-2018/|title=Like Moths To Flames announce fall headlining tour dates|first=Beth|last=Casteel|website=Altpress.com|date=15 August 2018}}</ref>

On 12 November, the band released their cover of [[Sting (musician)|Sting]]'s "[[Shape of My Heart (Sting song)|Shape of My Heart]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/oceansatealaska/videos/shape-of-my-heart/2288559531218693/|title=SHAPE OF MY HEART|website=Facebook.com|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> The song was then followed by drummer Chris Turner speaking on the songs that saved his life.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4kib9_V1-1s |title=Songs That Saved My Life: Chris Turner of Oceans Ate Alaska |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2018-11-13 |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

On 10 June 2019, the band announced on their Facebook page that they are currently in the studio working on their upcoming third album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.facebook.com/login.php?next=https%3A%2F%2Fm.facebook.com%2Fhome.php%3Fconfirmed_account%26refsrc%3Dhttps%253A%252F%252Flm.facebook.com%252F&refsrc=https%3A%2F%2Flm.facebook.com%2F&confirmed_account&_rdr|title=Log into Facebook |website=M.facebook.com}}</ref>

On 27 August 2019, the band was announced as part of the tenth anniversary "Macmillan Fest" along with 60 other musical acts. The event will host the "Great British Grind-Off" an epic battle of the bands and will take place on 7 September in Nottingham.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://totalrock.com/macmillan-fest-2019-full-lineup/|title=MacMillan Fest 2019: Full Lineup|website=Totalrock.com|date=10 June 2019}}</ref>

On 11 November 2020, the band released a new song and music video entitled "Metamorph", revealing the return of founding lead vocalist James Harrison to the band, while former vocalist Jake Noakes officially announcing his departure on his personal Instagram account due to his dream of prolonging his tattoo business.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CHdr9syghwP/ |title=Jake Noakes departs from Oceans Ate Alaska |publisher=[[Instagram]] |date=2020-11-11 |access-date=2020-11-11}}</ref>

==Members==
Before coming to the current line up, the band went through various member changes. The release of 'Clocks' music video feature James Harrison on vocals, Jibs on rhythm guitar, Josh Salthouse on lead guitar, Alex Hudrey on bass, and Chris Turner on drums. In 2012, the original bassist, Alex Hurdley left the band and was replaced by George Arvanitis for a short amount of time. After the release of 'To Catch a Flame' music video, Josh Salthouse and George Arvanitis left the band and were soon replaced by Adam Zytkiewicz and Mike Stanton respectively. In late 2016, frontman James Harrison parted ways with the band due to personal differences. On 1 February 2017, via their official facebook page, the band announced that they have found a new vocalist, Jake Noakes, in addition of Jake being a long time friend with Chris.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/oceansatealaska/posts/1292595870796929 |title=OUR NEW VOCALIST is our very close... - Oceans Ate Alaska |website=Facebook.com |access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref> On 11 November 2020, original vocalist James Harrison returned to the line-up while Jake Noakes stepped down from his position. Jake stated on his Instagram account that he was happy to see James back.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CHdr9syghwP/ |title=I’m happy to see James back as part of the Band - Jake Noakes |website=Instagram.com |access-date=2020-11-11}}</ref>
