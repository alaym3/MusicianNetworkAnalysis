'''Sabrina Benaim''' (born November 30, 1987 in [[Toronto]], Canada) is a writer, performance artist, and slam poet.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/artists/Sabrina-benaim|title=Sabrina Benaim|website=Genius|language=en|access-date=2018-12-12}}</ref> Benaim was a winner of the 2014 Toronto Poetry Slam.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://buttonpoetry.com/tag/sabrina-benaim/|title=sabrina benaim Archives|website=Button Poetry}}</ref> She is best known for her poem "Explaining My Depression To My Mother."<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/Sabrina-benaim-explaining-my-depression-to-my-mother-annotated|title=Sabrina Benaim – Explaining My Depression to My Mother|website=Genius|language=en|access-date=2018-12-12}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Sabrina Benaim was born November 30, 1987 in Toronto, Canada. She was a member of Canadian championship-winning 2014 Toronto Poetry Slam Team. She is currently a coach of the 2016 Toronto Poetry Slam (TPS) team.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://silvertigermedia.com.au/2018/04/interview-with-sabrina-benaim/|title=Interview with Sabrina Benaim|website=Silver Tiger Media|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-12}}</ref> At age 23, Benaim was found to have a benign tumor in her throat. She took to performance poetry as a way to cope with her health complications, and to raise awareness for issues like anxiety and depression.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/radio/q/thursday-feb-15-2018-pete-holmes-sabrina-benaim-and-more-1.4535580/how-a-tumour-in-her-throat-helped-spoken-word-poet-sabrina-benaim-find-her-voice-1.4535656|title=How a tumour in her throat helped spoken word poet Sabrina Benaim find her voice {{!}} CBC Radio|last=July 10|first=CBC Radio ·|website=CBC|language=en|access-date=2018-12-12}}</ref> She also represented [[Toronto]] at the [[Women of the World Poetry Slam|Women Of The World Poetry Slam.]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/sabrinabenaim.sb/|title=Sabrina Benaim|website=Facebook.com|language=en|access-date=2018-12-12}}</ref>

== Career ==
She took off on social media through her poem "Explaining My Depression To My Mother."<ref name=":0" /> From there she did a tour of The UK, Australia, USA & Canada connecting with many of her fans through her poetry, books, and storytelling which furthered the take off of her career.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.destroyalllines.com/tour/sabrina-benaim/|title=SABRINA BENAIM 2018 {{!}} Destroy All Lines|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-12}}</ref> Her poetry tackles issues that are relevant in our society like mental health, family, and love.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mimpmag.com/single-post/CINNAMON-LILAC-AND-POETRY|title=Sabrina Benaim Interview|website=Mimpmag.com|accessdate=12 December 2018}}</ref> Benaim has written poetry for ESPNW, the Government of Canada, and she made her television debut on Sport Chek. Benaim is well known for performing on the [[Button Poetry]] YouTube series.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hysteriamag.com/sabrina-benaim-not-alone/|title=SABRINA BENAIM // We're Not Alone|date=2018-05-03|website=Hysteria Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-12}}</ref>
