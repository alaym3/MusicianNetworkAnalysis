'''Natasha Tameika "Tasha" Cobbs Leonard''' (born July 7, 1981) is an American [[Gospel music|gospel]] musician and songwriter. She released the [[extended play]] ''[[Grace (Tasha Cobbs album)|Grace]]'' in 2013 with [[Capitol Christian Music Group|EMI Gospel]] (now [[Capitol Christian Music Group|Motown Gospel]]). The EP reached No. 61 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts. At the [[56th Annual Grammy Awards]], Cobbs won the Grammy for [[Best Gospel/Contemporary Christian Music Performance]]. She has also won 3 [[Stellar Awards]] and 3 [[Dove Awards]].<ref name="About Tasha Cobbs">{{Cite web|title=About Tasha Cobbs|url=http://tashacobbs.org/about/|access-date=2020-06-30|website=TashaCobbs.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Natasha Tameika Cobbs<ref>{{cite web |author=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP]] |title=COBBS NATASHA TAMEIKA Work ID #885043268 and IPI #440123901 |url=https://www.ascap.com/Home/ace-title-search/index.aspx |publisher=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP]] |access-date=February 25, 2015}}</ref> was born on July 7, 1981, in [[Jesup, Georgia]],<ref>Christine Thomasos, [https://www.christianpost.com/news/tasha-cobbs-honored-with-key-to-jessup-georgia.html Tasha Cobbs Honored With Key to Her Hometown], christianpost.com, USA, February 21, 2017</ref><ref name="AMB">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tasha-cobbs-mn0002086835/biography|title=Tasha Cobbs Awards|first=David|last=Jeffries|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 25, 2015}}</ref> to Bishop Fritz Cobbs (1954–2014) and Lady Bertha Cobbs.<ref>Miami time, [https://www.miamitimesonline.com/lifestyles/a-night-of-gospel-with-tasha-cobbs-leonard/article_432f1976-f5a4-11e9-8d5d-07191d70e4c4.html A night of gospel with Tasha Cobbs-Leonard], miamitimesonline.com, USA, October 23, 2019</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Gospel Singer Tasha Cobbs' Father Died Just Days After Winning Stellar Award for 'New Artist of The Year' |url=http://www.breathecast.com/articles/tasha-cobbs-father-died-just-days-after-winning-stellar-award-new-artist-of-the-year-13602/|publisher=Breathcast|first=Jeannie|last=Law|date=January 20, 2014|access-date=February 25, 2015}}</ref> She started leading worship at Jesup New Life Ministries, founded by her father.<ref name="About Tasha Cobbs"/>

In 2016, Cobbs-Leonard revealed that she had been diagnosed with depression in 2007.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gospel Singer Tasha Cobbs Opens Up About Living with Depression|url=https://www.essence.com/celebrity/gospel-singer-tasha-cobbs-opens-about-living-depression/|access-date=2020-06-30|website=Essence|language=en-US}}</ref>

On March 3, 2017, in a private ceremony attended by family and friends, she married Kenneth Leonard, a music producer and her longtime love.<ref>Christine Thomasos, [https://www.christianpost.com/news/tasha-cobbs-leonard-producing-pastor-william-murphys-next-album.html Tasha Cobbs Leonard Producing Pastor William Murphy's Next Album], christianpost.com, USA, February 24, 2018</ref><ref name="allmusic.com">{{Cite web|title=Tasha Cobbs Leonard {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/tasha-cobbs-leonard-mn0003642077/biography|access-date=2020-06-30|website=AllMusic|language=en-us}}</ref>  In July 2021 she adopted a son.<ref>https://gospelmusic.org/tasha-cobbs-leonard-and-husband-kenneth-announce-adoption-of-baby-boy-social-post/</ref>

==Ministry==
Starting in 2006, Cobbs-Leonard toured America with Pastor William H. Murphy III (of the dReam Center Church in [[Atlanta, Georgia]]) for three years.<ref name="allmusic.com"/> Since then, she has been leading worship at the dReam Center and managing the Worship and Arts department.<ref name="About Tasha Cobbs"/> In 2018, she appeared as herself in the film ''Sinners Wanted''.<ref>Anita Bennett, [https://urbanhollywood411.com/2020/03/30/tv-one-sinners-wanted-sets-premiere-date/ TV One Movie ‘Sinners Wanted’ Looks at Drama and Forgiveness in the Church], urbanhollywood411.com, USA, March 30, 2020</ref>

In 2019, Cobbs and her husband, Kenneth, founded The Purpose Place Church in South Carolina,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Our Pastors|url=https://www.thepurposeplace.net/our-pastors.html|access-date=2020-08-29|website=The Purpose Place|language=en}}</ref> where they serve as co-pastors.

==Music career==
She started her solo music career in 2010, with the independently self-released album ''Smile''.<ref name="AMB"/> The album got the attention of [[Capitol Christian Music Group|EMI Gospel]] (now [[Capitol Christian Music Group|Motown Gospel]]), and they released her EP, ''Grace'', on February 5, 2013. This was listed on two ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts: the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] at No. 61, and at No. 2 on the [[Top Gospel Albums]] chart.<ref name="AMA">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tasha-cobbs-mn0002086835/awards|title=Tasha Cobbs Awards|author=[[AllMusic]]|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 25, 2015}}</ref>  The EP later reached No. 1 on the Top Gospel Albums chart.<ref name="AMA"/> The singles "[[Break Every Chain]]" and "For Your Glory" also reached No. 1 on the Hot Gospel Songs charts.<ref name="AMA"/> At the 56th Annual Grammy Awards in 2014, Tasha Cobbs took home Best Gospel/Contemporary Christian Music Performance, winning her first Grammy. Cobbs was awarded the Gospel Artist of the Year, at the 2015 [[GMA Dove Award]]s.<ref>Iol,[https://www.iol.co.za/entertainment/whats-on/joburg/tasha-cobbs-leonard-excited-to-be-performing-in-sa-40977610 Tasha Cobbs Leonard 'excited' to be performing in SA], iol.co.za, South Africa, January 21, 2020</ref>
