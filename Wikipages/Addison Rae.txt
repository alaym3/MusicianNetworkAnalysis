'''Addison Rae Easterling''' (born October 6, 2000) is an American [[internet celebrity|social media personality]], dancer, actress and singer. In July 2019, she started actively posting content on [[TikTok]], where her dancing videos rose in fame. As of July 3, 2021, she has amassed over 81 million followers on TikTok, ranking as the [[List of most-followed TikTok accounts|third most-followed]] individual on the platform behind [[Charli D'Amelio]] and [[Khaby Lame]]. In August 2020, Rae was named as the highest-earning TikTok personality by ''[[Forbes]]''. She released her debut single, "[[Obsessed (Addison Rae song)|Obsessed]]" in March 2021, and made her acting debut in ''[[He's All That]]'', a gender swapped remake of the [[She's All That|1999 film]] released by [[Netflix]] on August 27, 2021.

== Early life ==
Addison Rae Easterling{{efn|Although she was given her mother's maiden name at birth, Monty Lopez is her bioloigical father.}} was born on October 6, 2000, to couple Monty Lopez and Sheri Easterling. She was born and raised in [[Lafayette, Louisiana]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Craighead|first=Olivia|date=June 11, 2020|title=Why TikTok's Addison Rae Is More Than Just a "Pouty Face"|language=en-US|work=The Wall Street Journal|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/addison-rae-tiktok-interview-hype-house-charli-11591878894|access-date=January 22, 2021|issn=0099-9660|archive-date=January 5, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210105095804/https://www.wsj.com/articles/addison-rae-tiktok-interview-hype-house-charli-11591878894|url-status=live}}</ref> Rae has two younger brothers, Enzo Lopez and Lucas Lopez. Addison's parents got divorced when she was younger and they were on and off often throughout her childhood; they got remarried in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Twersky|first=Carolyn|date=July 20, 2020|title=Addison Rae Just Told the Story About When She Decided to Leave College and Move to LA|url=https://www.seventeen.com/celebrity/a33368722/addison-rae-new-podcast-mom-sheri-leaving-college-moving-to-la/|access-date=January 26, 2021|website=Seventeen|language=en-US|archive-date=January 30, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210130213039/https://www.seventeen.com/celebrity/a33368722/addison-rae-new-podcast-mom-sheri-leaving-college-moving-to-la/|url-status=live}}</ref> Both parents also have their own presence on TikTok; Monty has 5 million followers and Sheri has 14 million followers.<ref name="people.com">{{Cite web|title=TikTok Star Addison Rae Launches New Podcast with Her Mom Sheri Nicole|url=https://people.com/tv/addison-rae-podast-mom-sheri-nicole/|access-date=January 26, 2021|website=People|language=EN|archive-date=January 31, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210131151443/https://people.com/tv/addison-rae-podast-mom-sheri-nicole/|url-status=live}}</ref>

Addison started dancing competitively at the age of 6 where she attended competitions across the country.<ref name="WSJ2">{{cite news|last1=Craighead|first1=Olivia|title=Why TikTok's Addison Rae Is More Than Just a "Pouty Face"|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/addison-rae-tiktok-interview-hype-house-charli-11591878894|access-date=June 27, 2020|website=The Wall Street Journal|date=June 11, 2020|archive-date=June 26, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200626175851/https://www.wsj.com/articles/addison-rae-tiktok-interview-hype-house-charli-11591878894|url-status=live}}</ref> Before moving to Los Angeles to pursue her TikTok career, the dancer briefly attended [[Louisiana State University]] (LSU) where she studied sports broadcasting in the fall but then dropped out when she began to gain popularity on TikTok.<ref name="studies2">{{cite web|last1=Kozma|first1=Leila|title=Addison Rae Shares New Details About Her Studies at LSU|url=https://www.distractify.com/p/addison-rae-lsu|access-date=June 27, 2020|website=distractify|archive-date=June 27, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200627113544/https://www.distractify.com/p/addison-rae-lsu|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
Rae first joined TikTok in July 2019, uploading dance videos to trending songs on the platform.<ref name="forbes2">{{Cite web|last=Talbot|first=Kate|date=April 7, 2020|title=Meet Addison Rae, One Of TikTok's Biggest Stars|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/katetalbot/2020/04/07/meet-addison-rae-one-of-tiktoks-biggest-stars/|access-date=April 8, 2020|website=[[Forbes]]|archive-date=April 7, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200407193114/https://www.forbes.com/sites/katetalbot/2020/04/07/meet-addison-rae-one-of-tiktoks-biggest-stars/#4f515fa671ed|url-status=live}}</ref> She has been part of [[the Hype House]], a TikTok collaborative group, since its formation in December 2019.<ref name="nyt2">{{Cite web|last=Lorenz|first=Taylor|author-link=Taylor Lorenz|date=January 3, 2020|title=Hype House and the Los Angeles TikTok Mansion Gold Rush|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/01/03/style/hype-house-los-angeles-tik-tok.html|access-date=March 29, 2020|website=[[The New York Times]]|archive-date=January 3, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200103101229/https://www.nytimes.com/2020/01/03/style/hype-house-los-angeles-tik-tok.html|url-status=live}}</ref> In just a few months, Rae gained over one million followers on TikTok and decided to leave LSU in November. "I remember that's when it changed for me," Rae told Business Insider in April 2020. “I knew I wanted to take it more seriously and expand it to other platforms. I uploaded a video to YouTube and got really active on Instagram."<ref name="BI earnings3">{{cite web|last1=Bradley|first1=Sydney|title=Addison Rae Easterling is the top-earning TikTok star with $5 million in the last year, according to Forbes. But Charli D'Amelio is close behind.|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/addison-rae-is-top-earning-tiktok-star-highest-income-forbes-2020-8?r=US&IR=T|access-date=August 8, 2020|website=Business Insider|archive-date=November 1, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201101073227/https://www.businessinsider.com/addison-rae-is-top-earning-tiktok-star-highest-income-forbes-2020-8?r=US&IR=T|url-status=live}}</ref> Rae's rapid success led her to signing with talent agency [[Endeavor (company)|WME]] in January 2020, along with her parents.<ref name="WME2">{{cite web|last1=Perelli|first1=Amanda|title=How talent agency WME plans to boost the careers of TikTok stars like Chase Hudson and Addison Rae, as the 'frenzy' to sign the platform's top creators continues|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/wme-plan-for-tiktok-stars-chase-hudson-addison-rae-sponsorships-2020-3?r=US&IR=T|access-date=August 8, 2020|website=Business Insider|archive-date=May 22, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210522103508/https://www.businessinsider.com/wme-plan-for-tiktok-stars-chase-hudson-addison-rae-sponsorships-2020-3?r=US&IR=T|url-status=live}}</ref>

In July, Rae partnered with [[American Eagle Outfitters|American Eagle]] on the brand's campaign of ''#AExME Back to School '20'' ads as the brand did their first virtual photoshoot that encompasses Addison shots in her bedroom due to the pandemic.<ref>{{Cite web|title=TikTok Star Addison Rae Styles and Photographs Herself in New American Eagle Campaign|url=https://people.com/style/addison-rae-fronts-american-eagle-campaign/|access-date=January 26, 2021|website=People|language=EN|archive-date=February 2, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210202134242/https://people.com/style/addison-rae-fronts-american-eagle-campaign/|url-status=live}}</ref> In that same month, Addison released a weekly [[podcast]] exclusive to [[Spotify]] with her mother called “Mama Knows Best” in July 2020 entailing topics covering their personal and career life.<ref name="people.com"/> Addison has also launched her own cosmetics line, Item Beauty with her as the role of ''Chief Innovation Officer'' which she co-founded with beauty startup Madeby Collective where she'll be overseeing most of the products in the brand.<ref name="BI earnings3"/><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Walsh|first=Savannah|date=August 3, 2020|title=Here's The One Piece of Advice Kourtney Kardashian Gave Addison Rae Before Her Beauty Launch|url=https://www.elle.com/beauty/makeup-skin-care/a33499173/addison-rae-item-beauty-launch-interview/|access-date=January 26, 2021|website=ELLE|language=en-US|archive-date=January 26, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210126161709/https://www.elle.com/beauty/makeup-skin-care/a33499173/addison-rae-item-beauty-launch-interview/|url-status=live}}</ref>

Rae's role was inspired by [[Freddie Prinze Jr.]]’s character Zachary Siler from the original.<ref name="He's all that2">{{cite web|last1=Rubin|first1=Rebecca|title=TikTok Star Addison Rae Cast in 'She's All That' Remake|url=https://variety.com/2020/film/news/tiktok-addison-rae-shes-all-that-remake-1234733549/|access-date=September 12, 2020|website=Variety|date=September 11, 2020|archive-date=September 11, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200911214645/https://variety.com/2020/film/news/tiktok-addison-rae-shes-all-that-remake-1234733549/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":2" /> More recently, she signed a deal with Netflix<ref>{{Cite web|author=Marianne Garvey|title=TikTok star Addison Rae signs deal with Netflix|url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/09/09/entertainment/addison-rae-netflix/index.html|access-date=2021-09-10|website=CNN|archive-date=September 10, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210910001517/https://www.cnn.com/2021/09/09/entertainment/addison-rae-netflix/index.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Politics===
During a UFC fight in July of 2021, Addison Rae got up from her seat to greet former President [[Donald Trump]], leading to speculation that she supported him.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.insider.com/tiktok-addison-rae-exposed-talking-donald-trump-ufc-264-2021-7|title=Addison Rae appeared to introduce herself to Donald Trump at a UFC fight|last=Colombo|first=Charlotte|date=13 July 2021|access-date=July 29, 2021|archive-date=September 24, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210924042606/https://www.insider.com/tiktok-addison-rae-exposed-talking-donald-trump-ufc-264-2021-7|url-status=live}}</ref> Further fueling the speculation, she did not answer questions regarding her support for the former President. An investigation done by [[BuzzFeed]] into her liked tweets found that she liked several tweets supporting Trump in 2016, such as one asking users to "like" the tweet if they supported Trump. However, a representative for Addison Rae claimed that the liked tweets were a bug.<ref>{{cite web|last=Durney|first=Ellen|url=https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/ellendurney/addison-rae-refused-comment-trump-supporter-liked-tweets|title=Addison Rae Stayed Silent When Paparazzi Asked Her If She’s A Trump Supporter After Their Controversial Interaction, And Now A Resurfaced Liked Tweet Suggests She Endorsed Him In 2016|date=22 July 2021|access-date=29 July 2021|archive-date=July 29, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210729163005/https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/ellendurney/addison-rae-refused-comment-trump-supporter-liked-tweets|url-status=live}}</ref>
