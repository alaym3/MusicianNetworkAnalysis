'''Maja "Maya" Berović''' ({{Lang-sr-cyr|Маја Беровић}}; born 8 July 1987) is a Bosnian singer. Debuted in 2007, she has acquired enormous popularity since of 2014, becoming one of the leading regional performers, mainly due to her collaboration with Bosnian rappers [[Jala Brat]] and [[Buba Corelli]].

==Early life==
Maja Berović was born 8 July 1987 in the village of [[Malešići (Ilijaš)|Malešići]], part of the [[Ilijaš]] municipality near [[Sarajevo]], [[Bosnia and Herzegovina]], to [[Serbs of Bosnia and Herzegovina|Serb]] parents. Her birth name was often falsely reported as "[[Majda|Maida]]" and her ethnicity was disputed.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.telegraf.rs/jetset/vesti-jetset/3092558-ja-sam-srpkinja-i-ponosim-se-time-maja-berovic-otkrila-koje-je-njeno-pravo-ime|title="Ja sam Srpkinja i ponosim se time": Maja Berović otkrila koje je njeno pravo ime|website=www.telegraf.rs|access-date=2019-11-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nezavisne.com/magazin/zuta-hronika/Maya-Berovic-o-porijeklu-Ja-sam-Srpkinja-i-ponosim-se-time/553638|title=Maya Berović o porijeklu: Ja sam Srpkinja i ponosim se time|website=nezavisne.com|date=15 August 2019|language=sr|access-date=2019-10-21}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mondo.rs/Zabava/Zvezde-i-tracevi/a1213517/Maya-Berovic-pravo-ime-kako-se-zove-Maja-Berovic.html|title=Ne znam zašto misle da se stvarno zovem Maida|website=mondo.rs|language=sr|access-date=2019-10-21}}</ref> She lost her father when she was three years old.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pulsonline.rs/puls-poznatih/njene-pesme-znaju-svi-ali-ne-i-zivotnu-pricu-maya-berovic-prvi-put-o-odrastanju-bez/g2lg8c5|title = Njene pesme znaju svi, ali ne i životnu priču: Maya Berović prvi put o ODRASTANJU BEZ OCA, POČETKU KARIJERE I POROCIMA!}}</ref> Berović lived in Malešići until the end of the [[Bosnian War]] in 1995, alongside her mother and older brother; she relocated to [[Bratunac]] where her mother is originally from. In a 2016 interview, Berović described her childhood during the Bosnian War: {{quote|"I had the bad fortune to belong to the generation that grew up during the war in Bosnia. I carry many ugly, and beautiful memories from that period of my life. The ugly memories include my family's relocation in search of a peaceful life, even though, at the time, I was not fully aware of the horrors of the situation. I spent most of my days playing, like any child."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svet.rs/nas-svet/estrada/maja-berovic-o-odrastanju-nisam-bila-ni-svesna-uzasa-kakav-je-rat|title=Maja Berović o odrastanju: Nisam bila ni svesna užasa kakav je rat|publisher=Svet|date=29 May 2016|access-date=20 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.srbijadanas.com/clanak/potresna-secanja-maya-berovic-nisam-bila-svesna-uzasa-kakav-je-bio-rat-u-bosni-27-05-2016|title=POTRESNA SEĆANJA|publisher=Srbija Danas|date=29 May 2016|access-date=20 June 2016}}</ref>|author=|title=|source=}}
In Bratunac, she attended school and first began practising music. From age 12 she used to sing in local [[Discothèque|discothèques]] every weekend until she turned 18. Berović grew up listening to artists such as [[Dragana Mirković]], [[Lepa Brena]] and [[Ceca (singer)|Ceca]] and lists them as her musical influences.

==Career==
Still a teenager, Maya was signed to the [[Belgrade]]-based record label [[Grand Production]] and released her debut studio album ''[[Život uživo]]'' (''Life, Live'') in August 2007,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Maya-%C5%BDivot-U%C5%BEivo/release/3848813|title=Maya-Život uživo|publisher=Discogs|year=2007}}</ref> achieving initial success with the song "Džin i limunada" (''Gin and Lemonade''). She soon parted ways with Grand Production and over a year later, in December 2008, she released her second studio album ''[[Crno zlato]]'' (''Black Gold'') under the short-lived label IN Music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Maya-Crno-Zlato/release/3855051|title=Maya-Crno zlato|publisher=Discogs|year=2008}}</ref> Two months after the release of ''Crno zlato'', Maya debuted a self-released non-album single "Koliko te ludo volim ja" (''How Madly I'm in Love with You'') in February 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Maya-Koliko-Te-Ludo-Volim-Ja/release/5477487|title=Maya-Koliko te ludo volim ja|publisher=Discogs|date=28 February 2009}}</ref>

Her big breakthrough came in February 2011 upon the release of her third and eponymous album, ''[[Maya (Maya Berović album)|Maya]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Maya-2011/release/3819205|title=Maya - 2011|publisher=Discogs|year=2011}}</ref> and the success of the [[Feminism|feminist]] ballad "Djevojačko prezime" (''Maiden Surname''), which was the recipient of the "Hit of the Year" award in [[Sarajevo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vesti.rs/Estrada/Maya-Berovic-dobila-nagradu-za-hit-godine.html|title=Maya Berović "[[Leti ptico slobodno]]" (''Fly, Bird, Freely'') in June 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smedia.rs/zabava/vest/28363/a.html?id=28363|title="Vruće" fotke i letnji hit: Maya Berović "dirnuće" vas u srce|publisher=S Media|date=26 June 2012|access-date=12 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Maya-Leti-Prico-Slobodno/release/5477467|title=Maya-Leti ptico [[Serbia]] and 20 October in neighboring countries.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Maya-Berovi%C4%87-Djevojka-Sa-Juga/release/3966541|title=Djevojka sa juga|publisher=Discogs|date=17 October 2014}}</ref>

In April 2014, Berović participated in [[Pink Music Festival 2014|Pink Music Festival]] with a club-oriented song called "Alkohol" (''Alcohol''), which was the most-listened to of the 26 entries.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svet.rs/najnovije-vesti/pesma-maye-berovic-najslusanija-ko-je-vas-favorit-pink-festivala|title=Pesma Maye Berović najslušanija: Ko je vaš favorit na Pink festivalu?|publisher=Svet|date=17 October 2014|access-date=18 October 2014}}</ref> The music video was done by the video production company IDJVideos and premiered on 9 May 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ekskluziva.ba/Ekskluzivno-Maya-Berovic-uradila-spot-za-pjesmu-Alkohol-/50073.html|title=Maya Berović uradila spot za pjesmu Alkohol!|publisher=Ekskluziva|date=9 May 2014|access-date=13 October 2014}}</ref> ''[[Opasne vode]]'' (''Dangerous Waters''), her fifth album and second consecutive project with City Records, was released 27 October 2014, and<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Maya-Berovi%C4%87-Opasne-Vode/release/6200046|title=Opasne vode|publisher=Discogs|date=27 October 2014|access-date=12 November 2014}}</ref> "Alkohol" served as the lead single. In addition to an updated and slightly different sound, a new fashion style also accompanied the new record.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svet.rs/najnovije-vesti/znate-li-ko-je-ovo-pevacica-uz-nove-pesme-promovise-i-novi-stajling|title=Znate li ko je ovo? Pevačica uz nove pesme promoviše i novi stajling!|publisher=Svet|date=17 September 2014|access-date=12 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.folk-estrada.hr/premijera-poslusajte-novi-album-maye-berovic-video/|title=Poslušajte novi album Maye Berović!|publisher=Folk estrada|date=18 October 2014|access-date=19 October 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141021210414/http://www.folk-estrada.hr/premijera-poslusajte-novi-album-maye-berovic-video/|archive-date=21 October 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In March 2016, Berović released "Pauza" (''Pause''), a non-album single. The single sparked controversy as it featured Berović singing in [[Ekavian|Ekavian dialect]], spoken in Serbia, instead of [[Ijekavian|Ijekavian dialect]], spoken in Bosnia and Herzegovina, that she sang in until that point. Maya claimed she had done that because her Serbian songwriters wrote in their dialect and the lyrics didn't always sound well and often couldn't rhyme in Ijekavian.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.opera17.rs/music/folk-music/odgovorila-maja-berovic-poludela-objavila-opasan-status|title=ODGOVORILA // Pevačica je POLUDELA zbog negativnih komentara: Rešila je svima da ODGOVORI i da im ZAPUŠI USTA!|publisher=Opera17|date=26 March 2016|access-date=2 November 2019}}</ref> However, she proceeded to sing in Ekavian even when working with Bosnian songwriters. On 8 July 2016, Berović's 29th birthday, she released "To me radi" (''That Makes Me'') featuring Bosnian rappers [[Jala Brat]] and [[Buba Corelli]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pulsonline.rs/puls-poznatih/54865/pogledajte-sta-najbolje-radi-plavusu-maju-berovic-video|title=Pogledajte šta najbolje radi plavušu Maju Berović|publisher=Puls|date=8 July 2016|access-date=16 July 2016}}</ref> It was the first collaborative song in her career and would eventually become one of her greatest hits.

Maya continued collaborating with Jala and Buba on her sixth studio album ''[[Viktorijina tajna (album)|Viktorijina tajna]]'' (''[[Victoria's Secret]]''). It was released on 2 July 2017 through City Records, produced by the Sarajevo-based Imperia production. This album has more urban sound than her previous [[Turbo-folk|pop-folk]] releases. ''Viktorijina tajna'' has reached enormous success, reaching more than 100 million views on [[YouTube]] and dominating charts on streaming service [[Deezer]]. All songs from the album became instant hits and, thanks to its success, Maya had all the venues that she had performed in sold out; she even went on a U.S. tour with Jala and Buba which also went very successfully. This album is arguably the most successful in Maya's ten-year-long career. On 9 July 2018, she released her seventh album ''[[7 (Maya Berović album)|7]]'', again with Jala and Buba.

On 1 November 2019, she released two new songs, "Zmaj" (''Dragon'') and "Uloga" (''Role'').

On 6 August 2020, Maya released her eight studio album ''[[Intime (Maya Berović album)|Intime]]'' (''Intimacies''). The songs are available on YouTube alongside the accompanying music videos.

==Personal life==
Berović married Alen Dragosav on 31 July 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alo.rs/v-i-p/estrada/da-joj-nije-bilo-verenika-njena-karijera-ne-bi-bila-ovakva-on-me-bezuslovno-podrzava-veliki-mi-je-oslonac-u-zivotu/78659|title=Da joj nije bilo verenika, njena karijera ne bi bila ovakva: On me bezuslovno podržava, veliki mi je oslonac u životu!|publisher=Alo!|date=21 December 2014|access-date=30 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141230175704/http://www.alo.rs/v-i-p/estrada/da-joj-nije-bilo-verenika-njena-karijera-ne-bi-bila-ovakva-on-me-bezuslovno-podrzava-veliki-mi-je-oslonac-u-zivotu/78659|archive-date=30 December 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> They currently reside in [[Graz]], [[Austria]], and [[Belgrade]], [[Serbia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.story.rs/vesti/svet-poznatih/43212-maja-berovic-zbog-ljubavi-sam-napustila-bosnu.html|title=Maja Berović: Zbog ljubavi sam napustila Bosnu|publisher=Story|date=10 August 2014|access-date=30 December 2014}}</ref> Berović is an [[Eastern Orthodoxy in Bosnia and Herzegovina|Eastern Orthodox Christian]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 April 2020|title=LJUDI, JA SAM PRAVOSLAVKA! ŠTO JE TO ČUDNO? Maja Berović udala se u crkvi u Grčkoj, a ovako će proslaviti VASKRS!|url=https://www.kurir.rs/stars/3449085/ljudi-ja-sam-pravoslavka-sto-je-to-cudno-maja-berovic-udala-se-u-crkvi-u-grckoj-a-ovako-ce-proslaviti-vaskrs|url-status=live|access-date=26 July 2021|website=Kurir|language=sr}}</ref>

In July 2019, Berović invited the citizens of Bosnia and Herzegovina to attend the first ever [[Sarajevo Pride]], held on 8 September 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pandurević|first=Dragana|date=23 July 2019|title=Naklon do poda za Mayu Berović i video kojim je podržala Paradu ponosa|url=https://noizz.rs/kontra/maya-berovic-gej-parada-sarajevo/f38erpy|url-status=live|access-date=26 July 2021|website=Noizz.rs|language=sr}}</ref>
