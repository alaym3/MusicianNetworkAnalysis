'''Saint Motel''' (stylized as '''SAINT MOTEL''') is an American [[indie pop]] band from [[Los Angeles]], whose music has been described as everything from "[[dream pop]]" to "indie prog."<ref>{{cite news|last=Fusilli |first=Jim |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052748703574604574501704063089802 |title=Warpaint, Free Energy, Fool's Gold and Others at CMJ Talk to Jim Fusilli About the Music-Making Business |work=The Wall Street Journal |date=2009-10-31 |access-date=2010-05-16}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/01/06/arts/music/06pink.html |title=The Circus Tumbles In, to the Sound of the '70s |work=The New York Times|date=2011-01-06 |first=Jon |last=Pareles}}</ref> The band consists of [[A/J Jackson]] (lead vocals, guitar, piano), Aaron Sharp (lead guitar), Dak Lerdamornpong (bass), and Greg Erwin (drums).<ref>{{cite web |last=Sankaran |first=Jasmine |url=http://www.asianweek.com/2012/09/06/music-qa-dak-lerdamornpong-from-saint-motel/ |title=Music Q&A: Dak Lerdamornpong from Saint Motel |publisher=AsianWeek |access-date=2014-08-24 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140722232120/http://www.asianweek.com/2012/09/06/music-qa-dak-lerdamornpong-from-saint-motel/ |archive-date=July 22, 2014 |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2343601/soundtrack|title = Knuckleball! (2012) - IMDb|website = [[IMDb]]}}</ref>

==Career==

===2007–2011: ''ForPlay''===
The group initially came together while Jackson and Sharp were attending film school in Southern California.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/discovery-saint-motel |title=Saint Motel |date=September 16, 2011 |publisher=Interview Magazine |access-date=September 16, 2011}}</ref> The duo later met Lerdamornpong at a sushi restaurant where he worked near campus and Greg who went to a school nearby. The band emphasizes visuals in addition to its music; their 2009 debut EP, ''[[ForPlay (album)|ForPlay]]'', included a video for each of the six songs.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/holiday-gift-guide/2009/12/music-gift-saint-motels-forplay-ep.html |title=Music gift: Saint Motel's 'ForPlay' EP &#124; Holiday Gift Guide &#124; Los Angeles Times |publisher=Latimesblogs.latimes.com |date=2009-12-10 |access-date=2010-05-16}}</ref>

===2012–2013: ''Voyeur''===
The band's first full-length album, ''[[Voyeur (Saint Motel album)|Voyeur]]'', debuted at No. 18 on the alt specialty radio charts and six of its eleven songs charted on the [[Hype Machine]] Charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fmqb.com/article.asp?id=40076 |title=Radio Industry News, Music Industry Updates, Nielsen Ratings, Music News and more! |publisher=FMQB |access-date=2014-08-24}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2012/05/check-out-saint-motel-1997-cos-premiere/ |title=Check Out: Saint Motel – "1997" (CoS Premiere) « Consequence of Sound |publisher=Consequenceofsound.net |date=2012-05-16 |access-date=2012-09-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kcrw.com/music/programs/ap/ap120723saint_motel_voyeur |title=Saint Motel: Voyeur - Album Preview on KCRW |publisher=Kcrw.com |access-date=2012-09-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicmuso.com/news/2112/los-angeles-quartet-039-saint-motel-039-release-debut-album-039-voyeur-039-on-july-10th-news-article.html |title=Los Angeles quartet 'Saint Motel' release debut album 'Voyeur' on July 10th |access-date=May 1, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120625010830/http://www.musicmuso.com/news/2112/los-angeles-quartet-039-saint-motel-039-release-debut-album-039-voyeur-039-on-july-10th-news-article.html |archive-date=June 25, 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://theaudioperv.com/2011/05/09/saint-motel-presents-judgment-day-on-may-21st-at-the-roxy/ |title=Saint Motel Presents Judgment Day On May 21st At The Roxy |publisher=The Audio Perv |access-date=2014-08-24}}</ref> The album was praised by journals ranging from ''[[The Washington Post]]'' to [[Daytrotter]]. [[KCRW]] Music Librarian and DJ Eric J. Lawrence wrote, "They have attacked [the debut album] with gusto on ''Voyeur'', adding dynamic touches to their rock-solid core of songwriting," and, "Overall the album is a satisfying package from start to finish."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.kcrw.com/music/shows/album-preview/saint-motel-voyeur|title=Critic Review for Saint Motel|last=Fusilli|first=Jim|date=2012-07-27|work=KCRW|access-date=2016-09-13}}</ref>

===2014–2015: ''My Type''===
The band released the EP ''My Type'' with [[Parlophone]] on August 17 in the UK.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bambridge |first=Fred |url=http://www.itsallindie.com/2014/06/saint-motel-sign-to-parlophone-records.html |title=It's All Indie: Saint Motel sign to Parlophone Records |publisher=Itsallindie.com |access-date=2014-08-24}}</ref><ref>[http://www.dailycal.org/2015/02/23/saint-motel-deliver-exuberant-live-performance/ "Saint Motel deliver exuberant live performance"]. ''Daily Californian'', February 23, 2015. Sasha Chebil</ref> "[[My Type]]" climbed the Top 40 charts in many European countries including Italy where it was certified platinum.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Barnabi|first1=Maria|title=La Nostra Canzone Del Giorno: My Type Dei Saint Motel|url=http://www.cosmopolitan.it/lifestyle/musica/news/a104724/my-type-dei-saint-motel/|website=Cosmopolitan Italia|date=September 30, 2014|publisher=Hearst Magazines|access-date=15 October 2014}}</ref> The band embarked on two European and one US tour in support of the EP and were expected to release the full-length album later in 2015. In December 2014 Saint Motel announced that they had joined the [[Elektra Records]] roster.<ref>{{cite web|last=Thompson |first=Gus |url=http://www.auburnjournal.com/article/12/11/14/media-life-ex-auburn-drummers-saint-motel-signs-elektra-records |title=Media Life: Ex-Auburn drummer's Saint Motel signs with Elektra Records |publisher=AuburnJournal.com |access-date=2015-04-23}}</ref> Saint Motel appeared as the musical guest on [[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]] on [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] performing the songs "My Type" and "Cold Cold Man."<ref>{{cite web|title=Tom Arnold, Maisie Williams, Music from Saint Motel|url=http://abc.go.com/shows/jimmy-kimmel-live/episode-guide/2015-03/24-tom-arnold-maisie-williams-music-from-saint-motel|website=abc.go.com|publisher=ABC|access-date=2015-04-23|format=Video upload|date=2015-03-24}}</ref> In April 2015, Saint Motel performed during both weekends on the main stage at the 2015 [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Miller |first=Jeff |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/music-festivals/6531556/coachella-saint-motel-my-type |title=Coachella 2015: Saint Motel Talks U.S. Breakout Moment |publisher=Billboard.com |access-date=2015-04-23}}</ref> They also performed at [[Piqniq]] and the 2015 [[KROQ Weenie Roast]].{{citation needed|date=August 2016}}

===2016–2018: ''saintmotelevision''===
On July 11, 2016, Saint Motel announced ''[[saintmotelevision]]'', their second album, would be released on October 21, 2016. The band embarked on a North American tour in support of the album, with [[Hippo Campus]] and [[JR JR]] as supporting acts.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2016/07/saint-motel-announces-new-album-saintmotelevision.html|title=Saint Motel Announce New Album, saintmotelevision|newspaper=pastemagazine.com|access-date=2017-01-05}}</ref> On August 12, Saint Motel released the album's first single, "Move," with an accompanying 360-degree video.<ref name="When The Gramophone Rings">{{cite web|last1=Green|first1=Jake|title=LISTEN: SAINT MOTEL // MOVE|url=http://whenthegramophonerings.com/2016/08/22/listen-saint-motel-move/|website=When The Gramophone Rings|publisher=When The Gramophone Rings|access-date=23 August 2016}}</ref> The song was featured in the [[EA Sports]] video game [[FIFA 17]]. In September, in an exclusive with ''Billboard'', they released another song from the album, "You Can Be You."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7511017/saint-motel-you-can-be-you-video-premiere|title=Saint Motel's 'You Can Be You' Song & Video: Exclusive Premiere|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Havens|first=Lyndsey|date=September 15, 2016|access-date=October 21, 2016}}</ref> Saint Motel has released 10 virtual reality components, one for each song on the album.<ref name="Fast Company - first full VR album">{{cite web |last1=Locker |first1=Melissa |title=This L.A. rock band just released the first full VR album |url=https://www.fastcompany.com/4037508/this-l-a-rock-band-just-released-the-first-full-vr-album |website=Fast Company |date=May 12, 2017 |access-date=26 June 2018}}</ref> This makes Saint Motel the first band to release an entire album with a virtual reality VR experience to accompany every track.<ref name="Billboard - First-Ever Virtual Reality Album">{{cite magazine |last1=Shouneyia |first1=Alexa |title=Saint Motel Is Turning 'saintmotelevision' Into First-Ever Virtual Reality Album |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7792133/saint-motel-saintmotelevision-virtual-reality-album-destroyer-watch |magazine=billboard |access-date=26 June 2018}}</ref> Saint Motel has continued to experiment with new technologies, releasing the first-ever augmented reality version of ''saintmotelevision'' as well as offering the option to purchase the albums ''ForPlay'' and ''Voyeur'' with Bitcoin.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ventsmagazine.com/2018/06/12/saint-motel-unveils-first-ever-augmented-reality-album/|title=SAINT MOTEL UNVEILS FIRST-EVER AUGMENTED REALITY ALBUM|last=Frometa|first=RJ|website=Vents Magazine|date=June 12, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.saintmotel.com/bitcoin|title=SAINT MOTEL BITCOIN|website=Saintmotel.com}}</ref>

=== 2019–present: ''The Original Motion Picture Soundtrack'' ===
On October 16, 2019, the band released the EP ''The Original Motion Picture Soundtrack: Part 1'', the first of two extended plays consisting of songs from their third album, ''The Original Motion Picture Soundtrack''.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8533032/saint-motel-original-motion-picture-soundtrack-part-1-breakdown|title=Saint Motel Break Down 'The Original Motion Picture Soundtrack: Part 1': Exclusive|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2019-10-30}}</ref> On March 16, 2020, the band released the single "A Good Song Never Dies". This was part of the EP ''The Original Motion Picture Soundtrack: Part 2'', the second part of their third album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thepreludepress.com/news/2020/3/16/saint-motel-share-new-track-a-good-song-never-dies|title=Saint Motel Share New Track, "A Good Song Never Dies"|website=The Prelude Press|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-01}}</ref> In 2020, "A Good Song Never Dies" was featured on the soundtrack to the video game ''[[MLB The Show 20]]''. On June 25, 2021, the full ''The Original Motion Picture Soundtrack'' album was released, containing every song from both EPs, the previously-released single "It's All Happening", and four new songs.
