'''Wax Fang''' is an American rock band formed in [[Louisville, Kentucky]], United States, that combines elements of classic, psychedelic, progressive, and experimental rock music, as well as electronic and folk. The band consists of Scott Carney, Corey McAfee, Zach Driscoll, and Dave Chale.

==History==

===''Black And Endless Night''===
The debut LP, ''Black & Endless Night'', was originally released as a solo album by Scott Carney in December 2005. After Jacob Heustis (bass) and Kevin Ratterman (drums) began performing with Carney, the band began playing under the moniker ''Wax Fang'' and ''Black & Endless Night'' was subsequently re-released as a Wax Fang album on their own label, ''Don't Panic Records.''<ref>{{cite web |url=http://performermag.com/Archives/sep.feature.0706.php |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2013-04-01 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130411160339/http://performermag.com/Archives/sep.feature.0706.php |archivedate=2013-04-11 }}</ref>

===''La La Land''===
After touring with [[My Morning Jacket]] in the fall of 2006, the band entered [[Ardent Studios]] with engineer ''Doug Easley'' ([[Easley McCain Recording]]) ([[Pavement (band)|Pavement]], [[Sonic Youth]], [[Guided by Voices]]) to begin recording their next album, [[La La Land (Wax Fang album)|La La Land]]. The band then opened for ''My Morning Jacket'' on New Year's Eve 2006 at
The [[Fillmore West|Fillmore]] in [[San Francisco]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Embry |first=Hunter |url=http://www.louisvillemusicnews.net/webmanager/index.php?WEB_CAT_ID=49&storyid=5739 |title=Wax Fang |publisher=Louisvillemusicnews.net |date= |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref>

In 2007, ''Wax Fang'' opened for [[Spoon (band)|Spoon]], [[Black Mountain (band)|Black Mountain]], [[Man Man]], [[The Whigs (band)|The Whigs]] and [[The Features]]. ''La La Land'' was released in November 2007.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://yewknee.com/blog/wax_fang_la_la |title=Wax Fang - La La Land |publisher=yewknee |date= |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Stabert |first=Lee |url=http://www.nashvillescene.com/nashvillecream/archives/2007/04/09/best-weeks-ever |title=Best Week(s) Ever &#124; Nashville Cream |publisher=Nashville Scene |date=2007-04-09 |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://bagofsongs.com/2007/10/04/black-mountain-new-album-info/ |title=Black Mountain : New Album Info |publisher=Bag Of Songs |date=2007-10-04 |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://backseatsandbar.com/2007/10/17/man-man-the-dame-oct-13/ |title=Man Man @ The Dame – Oct. 13 |publisher=Backseat Sandbar |date=2007-10-17 |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref>

In 2009, the band played [[SXSW]], [[Summerfest]], and the [[Wakarusa Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/festival/683807+Summerfest+2009,+June+28th |title=Summerfest 2009, June 28th at Summerfest Grounds (Milwaukee) on 28 Jun 2009 – |publisher=Last.fm |date= |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wakarusa.com/artist-lineup/2009/wax-fang |title=Wax Fang - Wakarusa Music Festival |publisher=Wakarusa.com |accessdate=2015-02-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141009001831/http://www.wakarusa.com/artist-lineup/2009/wax-fang/ |archive-date=2014-10-09 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Moore |first=Tracy |url=http://www.nashvillescene.com/nashvillecream/archives/2009/03/20/sxsw-thursday-night-wax-fang-no-age-and-next-big-nashville |title=SXSW Thursday Night: Wax Fang, No Age and Next Big Nashville &#124; Nashville Cream |publisher=Nashville Scene |date=2009-03-20 |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref>

In February 2010, the band signed with ''Absolutely Kosher Records'' for the European release of ''La La Land''. ''Wax Fang'' toured the UK in May 2010 playing such festivals as ''All Tomorrow's Parties (curated by Pavement)'', ''The Dot To Dot Festival'', [[The Great Escape Festival]], and "the Stag & Dagger Festival". The tour also included a BBC Session on [[BBC Radio 1]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Slater |first=Luke |url=http://drownedinsound.com/news/4139648-wax-fang-plot-uk-tour-give-away-free-track |title=Wax Fang plot UK tour, give away free track / Music News |publisher=Drownedinsound.com |date=2010-04-08 |accessdate=2015-02-23 |archive-date=2014-10-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141007063823/http://drownedinsound.com/news/4139648-wax-fang-plot-uk-tour-give-away-free-track |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2013, Scott Carney scored episode 151 of the television series ''[[American Dad!|American Dad]]'', and the song "Majestic" was featured prominently in the storyline.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/popcandy/2013/05/01/american-dad-wax-fang/2126243/ |title=Exclusive: Rockers Wax Fang inspire 'American Dad' |publisher=Usatoday.com |date=2013-05-01 |accessdate=2013-10-13}}</ref>

===''The Astronaut (Part 1)''===
In October 2010, ''Wax Fang'' released a 17-minute digital-only single on [[Removador Recordings & Solutions]] called ''The Astronaut (Part 1).'' The band opened for My Morning Jacket at the [[KFC Yum! Center]] to commemorate the release.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.songkick.com/concerts/6132666-my-morning-jacket-at-kfc-yum-center|title=My Morning Jacket and Wax Fang|publisher=Songkick.com|accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://removador.com/waxfang |title=Removador : Wax Fang |publisher=Removador.com |date= |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref>

In November 2011, ''Wax Fang'' toured with [[Dr. Dog]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.songkick.com/concerts/10064813-dr-dog-at-bluebird-nightclub |title=Dr. Dog Bloomington, Bluebird Nightclub, 04 Nov 2011 |publisher=Songkick.com |date=2011-11-04 |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.songkick.com/concerts/10063928-dr-dog-at-beachland-ballroom-and-tavern |title=Dr. Dog Cleveland, Beachland Ballroom and Tavern, 02 Nov 2011 |publisher=Songkick.com |date=2011-11-02 |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.songkick.com/venues/342-beachland-ballroom-and-tavern |title=Beachland Ballroom and Tavern Cleveland, Tickets for Concerts & Music Events 2015 |publisher=Songkick.com |date= |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref>

===''Mirror, Mirror EP''===
In August 2012, ''[[Spin (magazine)|SPIN]]'' announced the release of the ''[[Mirror, Mirror EP]]''. ''SPIN'' later premiered the video for the title track, "Mirror, Mirror". The video was self- produced by Scott Carney and Jacob Heustis.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.karatebodyrecords.com/artists/wax-fang/ |accessdate=April 1, 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130225040622/http://www.karatebodyrecords.com/artists/wax-fang/ |archivedate=February 25, 2013 |title=Karate school }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2012/08/hear-wax-fangs-ferocious-new-mirror-mirror/ |title=Hear Wax Fang's Ferocious New 'Mirror Mirror' &#124; SPIN &#124; Premieres |publisher=SPIN |date=2012-08-27 |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2012/10/see-wax-fangs-sinister-mirror-mirror-video/ |title=See Wax Fang's Sinister 'Mirror Mirror' Video &#124; SPIN &#124; Premieres |publisher=SPIN |date=2012-10-17 |accessdate=2015-02-23}}</ref>

In April 2013, ''SPIN'' announced the release of Wax Fang's digital-only single,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2013/04/wax-fang-the-blonde-leading-the-blonde-stream/|work=SPIN|accessdate=19 April 2013|author=Marc Hogan|title=Hear Wax Fang's Glammy, Sardonic 'The Blonde Leading the Blonde'|date=2013-04-19}}</ref> "The Blonde Leading The Blonde". The following month, on May 22, 2013, the video for "The Blonde Leading The Blonde" was premiered<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teamcoco.com/video/wax-fang-blonde-leading-the-blonde-premiere |title=Wax Fang "Blonde Leading the Blonde" - Exclusive Video Premiere @ |publisher=Teamcoco.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-13}}</ref> on [[Conan O'Brien]]'s online site [[Team Coco]].

On August 30, 2013, MTV Hive<ref>{{cite web|author=Brenna Ehrlich |url=http://www.mtvhive.com/2013/08/30/wax-fang-takes-on-god-and-man-in-king-of-the-kingdom-of-man/ |title=Wax Fang Takes On God And Man In 'King Of The Kingdom Of Man' |publisher=MTV Hive |date=2013-08-28 |accessdate=2013-10-13}}</ref> premiered the standalone track "King of the Kingdom of Man".

===''Victory Laps''===

On May 5, 2017, Wax Fang released their fourth studio album titled ''Victory Laps''.<ref>[http://www.leoweekly.com/2017/05/wax-fang-goes-new-direction-victory-laps/ Wax Fang goes in a new direction on ‘Victory Laps’]</ref>

==Touring==
The band has toured alongside acts such as [[My Morning Jacket]], [[Spoon (band)|Spoon]], [[Man Man]], [[The Whigs (band)|The Whigs]], [[The Features]], and [[Dr. Dog]].

Festivals include [[SXSW]], [[Summerfest]], [[Wakarusa Festival]], [[All Tomorrow's Parties]] (curated by Pavement), [[The Dot To Dot Festival]], [[The Great Escape Festival]] and the [[Stag and Dagger]] Festival.
