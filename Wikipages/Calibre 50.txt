'''Calibre 50''' is a [[Regional Mexican]] band founded in the city of [[Mazatlán]], [[Sinaloa]] in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/calibre-50-mn0002572930/biography|title=Calibre 50 - Biography & History - AllMusic|website=AllMusic|accessdate=13 April 2018}}</ref> Their style is [[Norteño (music)#Regional variations|Norteño-Banda]], a hybrid of [[Norteño music|Norteño]], which uses an [[Bass guitar|electric bass]] or [[tololoche]] for the low notes and replaces it with a [[sousaphone]] (tuba), which is typically used in [[Banda music|Banda]] for the bass notes.

==History==
Before founding Calibre 50, [[Edén Muñoz]] had been the accordionist and vocalist in the Norteño-banda group, [[Colmillo Norteño]]. With Muñoz, the group scored the hit songs "'''Sueño guajiro'''" and "'''Hotel El Cid'''" in 2009, among others. Nevertheless, conflicts with the other members of the band led Muñoz to quit Colmillo Norteño in December 2009, and he founded his own band in 2010, recruiting Armando Ramos as guitarist, Augusto Guido as drummer and Martín López as tuba player. The name of this new band was "Puro Colmillo Norteño", and they recorded their first album, "''Renovar o Morir''", under that name, as well as their first single ("'''El infiernito'''"). However, the original Colmillo Norteño sued them over the name, and after a legal dispute Muñoz' band was forced to change their name later in 2010. They chose the name by which they are known today: '''Calibre 50'''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Ochoa|first1=Anilú|title=Colmillo Norteño – Biografía|url=http://www.saps.com.mx/biografias/colmillo-norteno.html|publisher=SAPS Grupero|accessdate=29 May 2018|date=2 July 2014}}</ref> The name of the band comes from the comparison "with an element that will symbolize the strength and impact that the project has on the lives of the members, as well as those who like the [[Regional Mexican]] genre."

Calibre 50 first achieved notoriety for their controversial songs, among them [[corridos]]. Their first nationwide hit was "'''El tierno se fue'''" ("The nice guy is gone") in 2011, a song written by Lalo Ayala which contains many [[innuendo]]s and describes a sexual act in detail. In later years, they turned to release romantic songs as singles, however their albums still consist mostly of corridos and party songs.

In January 2014, drummer Augusto Guido left the band to work on his own group, "Los de Sinaloa"; he was replaced by Erick García.<ref>{{cite web |title=DEJA A CALIBRE 50 POR SU NUEVA BANDA |url=https://www.soygrupero.com.mx/contenido/las_rapiditas/deja-calibre-50-por-su-nueva-banda/2014/01/ |website=Soy Grupero |accessdate=16 July 2018|date=15 January 2014 }}</ref> Two months later, Tuba player Martín López also left the band to work on another project, "La Iniciativa". He was subsequently replaced by Alejandro Gaxiola.<ref>{{cite web |title=MARTÍN LÓPEZ DEJA CALIBRE 50 |url=https://www.soygrupero.com.mx/contenido/las_rapiditas/martin-lopez-deja-calibre-50/2014/03/ |publisher=Soy Grupero |accessdate=16 July 2018|date=5 March 2014 }}</ref>

In March 2017, Calibre 50 made history when the band became the first Regional Mexican act to perform on the [[Conan (talk show)|Conan]] show on an episode that took place in [[Mexico City]].

Calibre 50 made their [[Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo|Rodeo Houston]] debut on 11 March 2018 to a sold out crowd.

On 26 April 2018, Calibre 50, along with Colombian [[Urbano music|Urban]] singer [[J Balvin]], received an award from [[Pandora Radio]] for being the first artists to surpass a billion streams on that platform.<ref>{{cite web|title=J Balvin & Calibre 50 Receive Pandora's First-Ever 'Billions' Awards|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8390735/j-balvin-calibre-50-receive-pandora-billions-awards|website=Billboard|publisher=Billboard|accessdate=6 May 2018}}</ref>

In October 2020, the group broke the record for the most number-one songs on the ''Billboard'' [[Regional Mexican Airplay]] chart with 17 songs.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bustios |first1=Pamela |title=Calibre 50 Breaks No. 1 Record on Regional Mexican Airplay Chart With 'Decepciones' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9470903/calibre-50-deceptiones-breaks-record-on-regional-mexican-airplay-chart |website=Billboard |accessdate=25 October 2020 |date=22 October 2020|url-access=subscription}}</ref>
