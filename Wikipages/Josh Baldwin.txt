'''Josh Baldwin''' is an American [[Contemporary Christian music|Christian]] singer, songwriter and [[worship pastor]]. A member of the [[Bethel Music|Bethel Music collective]],<ref name="New artist Baldwin">{{cite web |url= https://www.christiantoday.com/article/bethel-music-introduces-new-artist-josh-baldwin/51158.htm |title= Bethel Music introduces new artist Josh Baldwin {{!}} Christian News on Christian Today |last= Ong |first= Czarina |date= April 21, 2015 |website= [[Christian Today]] |access-date= July 30, 2018}}</ref> Baldwin also serves as a worship leader at [[Bethel Church (Redding, California)|Bethel Church]] in [[Redding, California]].<ref name="Bethel Music Bio">{{cite web |url= https://bethelmusic.com/artists/josh-baldwin/ |title= Josh Baldwin - Bethel Music |website= [[Bethel Music]] |access-date= July 30, 2018}}</ref>

Josh Baldwin starting serving as a worship leader at MorningStar Fellowship Church after attending the ministry school there. He independently released ''Changing'', his first album, in 2008. In 2010, Baldwin then joined Queen City Church in [[Charlotte, North Carolina]], serving as worship pastor. Baldwin released ''Rivers'' in 2014, earning him his first [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] chart appearance with a placement on the US [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Albums]].

Josh Baldwin signed with [[Bethel Music]] in 2014, and moved to Redding, California to join Bethel Church. Baldwin made his first feature on a Bethel Music collective release, [[Have It All (Bethel Music album)|''Have It All'']] (2016), with the song "Praises (Be Lifted Up)". Baldwin then released [[The War Is Over (Josh Baldwin album)|''The War Is Over'']], his first solo effort with the label in mid-2017 to critical acclaim and the album debuted in the top-three sector of the American and British Christian music charts.

Bethel Music and Josh Baldwin released the song "[[Stand in Your Love]]" in 2018, as the lead single to [[Victory (Bethel Music album)|''Victory'']] (2019). The song reached the top ten of the US [[Christian Songs]] chart, Baldwin's first entry on the chart. It also garnered Baldwin's first nominations for the 2019 [[K-Love]] Fan Awards in the Breakout Single of the Year and Worship Song of the Year categories.<ref name="TCB K-Love">{{cite web |url= http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/6035-casting-crowns-danny-gokey-for-king-country-lauren-daigle-lead-k-love-fan-awards-nominations |title= Casting Crowns, Danny Gokey, for KING & COUNTRY & Lauren Daigle Lead K-LOVE Fan Awards Nominations |last= Longs |first= Herb |date= May 13, 2019 |website= The Christian Beat |access-date= May 23, 2019}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Joshua Aaron Baldwin was born on July 8, 1979, to William Baldwin, a pastor, and Kathy Baldwin in [[Albemarle, North Carolina]]. Baldwin shared that his father was also a [[worship leader]] and is "very much into music," and grew up playing drums for him and other worship leaders.<ref name="CCM Moving Forward">{{cite web |url= https://www.ccmmagazine.com/features/josh-baldwin-moving-forward/ |title= Josh Baldwin – Moving Forward – CCM Magazine |last= Conner |first= Matt |date= June 15, 2017 |website= [[CCM Magazine]] |publisher= Salem Communications |access-date= July 30, 2018}}</ref> He started leading worship and playing guitar during his high school years,<ref name="CCM Moving Forward" /> learning at Albemarle High School. Baldwin, at nineteen-twenty years, was studying at [[Lee University]] when he first realized that he wanted to be a worship leader after leading at a youth camp in North Carolina, thus dropping out of Lee University and enrolled at MorningStar Ministry School, attending together with [[John Mark McMillan]] and [[Jonathan David & Melissa Helser|Jonathan David Helser]].<ref name="CCM Moving Forward" />

== Career ==
Josh Baldwin started his career with MorningStar in early 2008, featuring on two albums: ''Worship and Warfare, Vol. 3'' with the song "After You"; and ''Jesus Broken'' with "Love You Only".<ref>{{cite web |url= https://leonardjonesmusic.com/album/worship-warfare-iii |title= Worship & Warfare III {{!}} Leonard Jones |website= [[Bandcamp]] |access-date= August 2, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= https://store.morningstarministries.org/products/jesus-broken |title= Jesus [Broken] {{!}} MorningStar Ministries |website= MorningStar Ministries |access-date= August 2, 2018}}</ref> He was a part of MorningStar Fellowship Church for nine years,<ref name="CCM Moving Forward" /><ref name="MorningStar Bio">{{cite web |url=  https://www.morningstarministries.org/biographies/josh-baldwin |title= Josh Baldwin Biography {{!}} MorningStar Ministries |website= MorningStar Ministries |access-date= August 2, 2018}}</ref> and he also travelled with artists [[Rita Springer]] and Suzy Yaraei working as a drummer.<ref name="AllMusic Bio">{{cite web |url= https://www.allmusic.com/artist/josh-baldwin-mn0003498068/biography |title= Josh Baldwin {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic  |last= Monger |first= Timothy |website= [[AllMusic]] |publisher= [[All Media Network]] |access-date= July 30, 2018}}</ref> Baldwin served as the worship minister at MorningStar in [[Wilkesboro, North Carolina]].<ref name="MorningStar Bio" /> On September 3, 2008, Josh Baldwin independently released his debut album, ''Changing''.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B001H9DVAY |title= Changing by Josh Baldwin on Amazon Music - Amazon.com |date= September 3, 2008 |website= [[Amazon.com]] |access-date= August 2, 2018}}</ref><ref name="MorningStar Bio" /> Baldwin then moved to [[Charlotte, North Carolina]], to serve as worship pastor of Queen City Church.<ref name="CCM Moving Forward" />

On September 17, 2013, Josh Baldwin launched a Kickstarter funding campaign to raise [[United States dollar|$]]12,000 for a new worship album's mixing, mastering, printing, and artwork to be completed.<ref name="Kickstarter">{{cite web |url= https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/402454470/josh-baldwins-new-worship-album |title= JOSH BALDWIN'S NEW WORSHIP ALBUM! by Josh Baldwin — Kickstarter |last= Baldwin |first= Josh |date= September 17, 2013 |website= [[Kickstarter]] |access-date= July 30, 2018}}</ref> The campaign was a success, with 202 backers pledging $12,635, with a twenty two day period that ended on October 10, 2013.<ref name="Kickstarter" /> Baldwin released ''Rivers'', his sophomore album on January 28, 2014, in partnership with Watershed Music.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.amazon.com/Rivers-Josh-Baldwin/dp/B00GQP9MAC |title= Rivers by Josh Baldwin on Amazon Music - Amazon.com |date= January 28, 2014 |website= [[Amazon.com]] |access-date= August 2, 2018}}</ref> ''Rivers'' peaked at No. 44 on the [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Albums]] chart issued by [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']],<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/heatseekers-albums/2014-02-15 |title= Heatseekers Albums Chart {{!}} Billboard |date= February 15, 2014 |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= July 31, 2018}}</ref> his first entry on a ''Billboard'''s chart. In 2014, Baldwin went on to sign a record deal with [[Bethel Music]] during a week-long visit with his wife to [[Redding, California]] after discussions with [[Brian Johnson (American musician)|Brian Johnson]], whom he knew since meeting in 2007.<ref name="CCM Moving Forward" /><ref name="AllMusic Bio" /> On March 11, 2016, Bethel Music then released its eighth live album dubbed ''[[Have It All (Bethel Music album)|Have It All]]'',<ref name="Have It All Highest Top Albums Debut">{{cite web |url= http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/1810-bethel-music-s-have-it-all-becomes-worship-ministry-s-highest-billboard-top-albums-debut |title= Bethel Music's "Have It All" Becomes Worship Ministry's Highest Billboard Top Albums Debut |last= Longs |first= Herb |date= March 21, 2016 |website= The Christian Beat |access-date= August 1, 2018}}</ref> with Baldwin performing a live cover of his song, "Praises (Be Lifted Up)".<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/1690-bethel-music-have-it-all-feature-story |title= Bethel Music "Have It All" Feature Story |last= Williams |first= Lindsay |date= February 22, 2016 |website= The Christian Beat |access-date= August 1, 2018}}</ref>

Josh Baldwin released his first album with Bethel Music, [[The War Is Over (Josh Baldwin album)|''The War Is Over'']], on May 26, 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.louderthanthemusic.com/product.php?id=2427 |title= Louder Than The Music - Josh Baldwin - The War is Over |last= Wright |first= Jay |website= Louder Than The Music |access-date= August 2, 2018}}</ref> ''The War Is Over'' earned sales of 3,000 [[album-equivalent units|equivalent album units]] in its opening week, and thus debuted at No. 2 on the US [[Christian Albums]] chart dated June 17, 2017, issued by '' Billboard''.<ref name="TWIO Billboard article">{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7825549/charlie-wilson-im-blessed-hot-gospel-songs-chart |title= Charlie Wilson 'Blessed' With a Hot Gospel Songs Chart-Topper {{!}} Billboard |last= Asker |first= Jim |date= June 8, 2017 |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= August 2, 2018}}</ref> The album also debuted at No. 3 on the [[Official Christian & Gospel Albums Chart]] in the United Kingdom.<ref name="OCC C&G Chart" /> "Get Your Hopes Up" was released as the first single from the album.<ref name="Daily Play MPE">{{cite web |url= http://daily.plaympe.com/josh-baldwin-get-your-hopes-up-album-version/ |title= Josh Baldwin - Get Your Hopes Up (album version) - Daily Play MPE®Daily Play MPE® |date= June 1, 2017 |website= Daily Play MPE® |publisher= Destiny Media Technologies Inc. |access-date= August 1, 2019}}</ref>

On July 19, 2018, Bethel Music and Josh Baldwin published the song "[[Stand in Your Love]]" on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oFizRY8w0-I |title= NEW SONG: Stand In Your Love - Josh Baldwin {{!}} Live From Heaven Come 2018 |date= July 19, 2018 |website= [[YouTube]] |publisher= YouTube, LLC |access-date= August 1, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.hallels.com/articles/19744/20180722/bethel-musics-josh-baldwin-releases-new-song-stand-in-your-love-listen-here.htm |title= Bethel Music's Josh Baldwin Releases New Song "Stand In Your Love" (Listen Here) : News : Hallels |last= Yap |first= Timothy |date= July 21, 2018 |website= Hallels |access-date= August 1, 2019}}</ref> They subsequently released "Stand in Your Love" on August 10, 2018,<ref>{{cite web |url=  http://www.breathecast.com/articles/singer-songwriter-josh-baldwin-releases-new-single-stand-in-your-love-38001/ |title= Singer-Songwriter Josh Baldwin Releases New Single "Stand In Your Love" : Music : BREATHEcast |last= Lundin |first= Marcus |date= August 11, 2018 |website= BREATHEcast |access-date= August 1, 2019}}</ref> as the first single from the Bethel Music's album, [[Victory (Bethel Music album)|''Victory'']] (2019).<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.hallels.com/articles/20707/20190125/bethel-music-delivers-victory-today.htm |title= Bethel Music Delivers "VICTORY" Today : News : Hallels |last= Yap |first= Timothy |date= January 25, 2019 |website= Hallels |access-date= August 1, 2019 |quote= "Stand In Your Love" from Josh Baldwin is proving to be a progressing radio hit. The track found on VICTORY is currently No. 5 at AC Indicator, No. 7 at AC Monitored and No. 7 at Christian Airplay.}}</ref> The single was a hit as it became his first entry on the US [[Christian Songs]] chart,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8494645/lauren-daigle-ties-carrie-underwood-record |title= Lauren Daigle Ties Carrie Underwood For No. 1 Record On Hot Christian Songs Chart {{!}} Billboard |last= Asker |first= Jim |date= January 23, 2019 |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher= Billboard-Hollywood Reporter Media Group |access-date= January 24, 2019 |quote= NEW TOP 10s: "Stand in Your Love" by Bethel Music's Josh Baldwin becomes his first top 10 on Hot Christian Songs (11-10). It hikes by 7 percent hike to 7.9 million impressions and holds at No. 7 on Christian Airplay.}}</ref> and peaked at No. 7. It garnered nominations for 2019 [[K-Love]] Fan Awards in the Breakout Single of the Year and Worship Song of the Year categories.<ref name="TCB K-Love" /><ref>{{cite web |url= https://freeccm.com/2019/05/16/news-k-love-fan-awards-nominations-announced-voting-now-open/ |title= News: K-LOVE Fan Awards Nominations Announced; Voting Now Open {{!}} Freeccm.com |last= Young |first= Abby |date= May 16, 2019 |website= Freeccm.com |access-date= August 1, 2019}}</ref> On July 19, 2019, Josh Baldwin released "[[Let the Redeemed]]" as a single.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.newreleasetoday.com/news_detail.php?newsid=4059 |title= Bethel Music's Josh Baldwin Returns with 'Let the Redeemed' |date= July 19, 2019 |website= NewReleaseToday |publisher= NRT Media, Inc |access-date= July 21, 2019}}</ref>
