'''John Itiola''', also known as '''''Wordsplayed''''', is a [[Christian hip hop|Christian rapper]] based in [[San Diego]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Wordsplayed {{!}} Album Discography|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/wordsplayed-mn0003502859/discography|access-date=2020-07-02|website=AllMusic|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=John Itiola|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm9275478/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=IMDb}}</ref> His style is influenced by [[trap music]]. He has made album appearances for artists like [[Andy Mineo|Andy Mineo (C-Lite)]], [[Gawvi|GAWVI]] and [[Social Club Misfits]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=John Itiola - MusicBrainz|url=https://musicbrainz.org/artist/dabf261a-9e88-474c-b543-aecda1735037|access-date=2020-07-04|website=musicbrainz.org}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-27|title=GAWVI - Climate Change ft. Aklesso & Wordsplayed|url=https://rapzilla.com/2020-03-gawvi-climate-change-aklesso-wordsplayed/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-03-31|title=Gawvi - Look At You Ft. wordsplayed|url=https://jamthehype.com/gawvi-look-ft-wordsplayed/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Jam the Hype|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The Misadventures of Fern & Marty by Social Club Misfits {{!}} CD Reviews And Information {{!}} NewReleaseToday|url=https://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=17184|access-date=2020-07-02|website=www.newreleasetoday.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=REVIEW :: Marty – Marty For President – Rap Remnant|url=https://rapremnant.com/music/reviews/review-deathbymartymar-marty-for-president/|access-date=2020-07-02|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Wordsplayed was born to Nigerian immigrants who moved to the [[United States]] in the early 1980s. He grew up in [[Long Island]], [[New York City|New York]].

In 2008, he became friends with members of the evangelical hip-hop ministry [[Nicky Cruz|T.R.U.C.E.]] (To Reach Urban Communities Everywhere), which included the rapper [[Andy Mineo]], who gave Itiola a guest spot (credited as John "Word Up" Itiola) on his 2009 Sin Is Wack mixtape.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2009-12-11|title=C-Lite "Jesus Over Hip Hop" ft. John Itiola|url=https://jamthehype.com/c-lite-qjesus-over-hip-hopq-ft-john-aka-word-up/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Jam the Hype|language=en-US}}</ref> He has also previously worked with Beleaf and Ruslan of ''the Dream Junkies'' and formerly of ''theBreax'', on the songs “Drag ‘Em” and “That’s Me” respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-07-07|title=Album Review : Beleaf - Red Pills + Black Sugar|url=https://www.indievisionmusic.com/reviews/beleaf-red-pills-black-sugar/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Indie Vision Music|language=en-US}}</ref>

Itiola also joined Mineo's artist collective Miner League.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-09-19|title=Andy Mineo reveals new Miner League artist on SMCT finale|url=https://rapzilla.com/2015-09-andy-mineo-reveals-new-miner-league-artist-on-smct-finale/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref> Itiola released his debut single as Wordsplayed, "Martinelli's (ft. [[Andy Mineo]])," on December 29, 2014<ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-12-31|title=Video: wordsplayed - Martinelli's ft. Andy Mineo|url=https://rapzilla.com/2014-12-wordsplayed-martinellis-ft-andy-mineo/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref> and "Sammy Sosa" on April 11, 2015. Itiola collaborated on the [[The Martyr (album)|Marty's]] album ([[Social Club Misfits|Social Club]]) in the song "The One with my Friends" alongside [[NF (rapper)|NF]], [[John Givez]], [[Fern (rapper)|Fern]] and Kaleb Mitchell.

In 2016, he debuted with the mixtape ''Clowntown'', which was released by Miner League and hit number 35 on Billboard's Top Christian Albums chart.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-03-25|title=wordsplayed Says 'Clowntown' is 'a Beautiful Mess'|url=https://rapzilla.com/2016-03-wordsplayed-says-clowntown-is-a-beautiful-mess/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-06-20|title=Music Video: wordsplayed - Clowntown|url=https://rapzilla.com/2017-06-wordsplayed-clowntown/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref> Also, he was a special guest with [[Social Club Misfits]] in ''"Friends & Family Tour"'' of [[Andy Mineo]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-06-19|title=Andy Mineo Announces Friends & Family Tour with Social Club Misfits and Wordsplayed|url=https://rapzilla.com/2017-06-andy-mineo-announces-friends-family-tour-with-social-club-misfits-and-wordsplayed/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2017, Itiola and Mineo teamed up for ''Andy Mineo & Wordsplayed Present Magic & Bird,'' on August 4, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=choover@wacotrib.com|first=CARL HOOVER|title=Rapper Andy Mineo brings back '90s in new 'Magic and Bird' project|url=https://www.wacotrib.com/entertainment/accesswaco/rapper-andy-mineo-brings-back-90s-in-new-magic-and-bird-project/article_0df5bd78-e712-56de-b102-3cfdbe1e0a71.html|access-date=2020-07-02|website=WacoTrib.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-07-03|title='Andy Mineo and Wordsplayed Present Magic & Bird' Mixtape Cover, Tracklist & Release Date Revealed|url=https://rapzilla.com/2017-07-andy-mineo-and-wordsplayed-present-magic-bird-mixtape-cover-tracklist-release-date-revealed/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref> The full-length collaboration debuted at number 49 on the all-genre Billboard 200.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-08-09|title=Andy Mineo & Wordsplayed Interview on New Mixtape|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7896845/andy-mineo-wordsplayed-interview-new-mixtape-magic-and-bird|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Billboard}}</ref> This album had 5 songs on the Billboard's "Hot Christian Songs" chart.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Wordsplayed|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/wordsplayed/chart-history/ICO|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Billboard}}</ref>

His latest releases, Lo-Fi Love 1 (LFL1) and Lo-Fi Love 2 (LFL2), have been paired with his stellar “Lo-Fidelity Hour” and "Lo-Fidelity Nights Uncut" direct to public access television series', respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-05-31|title=wordsplayed to Release New EP LFL1 at Midnight|url=https://rapzilla.com/2018-05-wordsplayed-to-release-new-ep-lfl1-at-midnight/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-10-02|title=Wordsplayed announces new album with release date|url=https://rapzilla.com/2018-10-wordsplayed-announces-new-album-with-release-date/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2019, was a guest on [[NBC Sports|NBC’s]] ‘Off The Dribble’ on ‘NBA Hot Takes’ segment.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-12-10|title=NBC's 'Off The Dribble' features Wordsplayed on 'NBA Hot Takes' Segment|url=https://rapzilla.com/2019-12-nbc-off-the-dribble-wordsplayed-nba-hot-takes/|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Rapzilla|language=en-US}}</ref>

In April 2020, he launched a [[podcast]] called "Circle of Trust", with the intention of "building a global network of people with positive, successful and like-minded ideas."<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Circle of Trust Podcast|url=https://radiopublic.com/the-circle-of-trust-podcast-GyyLM4|access-date=2020-07-02|website=radiopublic.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The Circle of Trust Podcast on Apple Podcasts|url=https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/the-circle-of-trust-podcast/id1511191185|access-date=2020-07-02|website=Apple Podcasts|language=en-us}}</ref>
