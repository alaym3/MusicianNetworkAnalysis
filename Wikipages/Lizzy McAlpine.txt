== Early life and education ==
Lizzy McAlpine grew up in the suburbs of [[Philadelphia|Philadelphia, Pennsylvania]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-02|title=Lizzy McAlpine & Ben Kessler Are Finally in Sync|url=https://blog.levelmusic.com/lizzy-mcalpine-ben-kessler-are-finally-in-sync/|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Level|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-07|title=Five to Discover: Acts You Need To Know|url=https://americansongwriter.com/maya-marie-the-kathy-super-power-trio-lizzy-mcalpine-tommy-sielgel-luke-schneider-five-to-discover/|access-date=2021-04-20|website=American Songwriter|language=en-US}}</ref> She has written music since she was in 6th grade.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Contributors|first=Author PTM|date=2020-08-18|title=TikTokTues: Lizzy McAlpine Shares Her Latest Album 'Give Me A Minute'|url=https://playtoomuch.com/tiktoktues-lizzy-mcalpine-shares-her-latest-album-give-me-a-minute/|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Play Too Much|language=en-US}}</ref> In high school, she sang in her school's co-ed a cappella group and did theater.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Log into Facebook|url=https://www.facebook.com/login/?next=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.facebook.com%2FThatsSoPhilly%2Fposts%2Fthis-week-we-are-featuring-singer-songwriter-lizzy-mcalpine-with-her-original-so%2F637325253040311%2F|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Facebook|language=en}}</ref>

McAlpine studied songwriting at [[Berklee College of Music|Berklee College of Music]] before leaving in her junior year to pursue music full-time.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Berg|first=Matt|date=March 3, 2021|title=With another album in the works, singer Lizzy McAlpine has left Berklee behind|work=The Boston Globe|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/2021/03/03/arts/with-another-album-works-singer-lizzy-mcalpine-has-left-berklee-behind/}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Berklee Popular Music Institute Launches 'Berklee at Home' Concert Series {{!}} Berklee|url=https://college.berklee.edu/news/berklee-now/berklee-popular-music-institute-launches-berkleeathome-concert-series|access-date=2021-04-20|website=college.berklee.edu|language=en}}</ref> In April 2020 at the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, she started the Instagram #BerkleeAtHome streaming concert series.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-27|title=Revealed: Billboard's 2020 Top Music Business Schools|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/9365285/billboard-top-music-business-schools-2020-list|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Billboard}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2018, McAlpine released an 8-song album, ''Indigo.''<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Sacks & Co. /// Lizzy McAlpine|url=http://sacksco.com/notes/lizzy_mcalpine.html|access-date=2021-04-20|website=sacksco.com}}</ref> She studied in [[Spain]] in fall 2019, where she wrote her first full album, ''Give Me A Minute,''<ref name=":0" /> and she released the 13-song album in August 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Give Me A Minute, by Lizzy McAlpine has garnered supporters from [[TikTok]] and [[Instagram]], with several publications calling her a "TikTok star."<ref>{{Cite web|title=TikTok Star Lizzy McAlpine Teams up with Ben Kessler on ‘FALSE ART’|url=https://www.exclusiveaudio.net/blog/falseart|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Exclusive Audio|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Scherner|first=Hannah|title=TikTok star Lizzy McAlpine releases folk debut 'Give Me A Minute' {{!}} Nü Sound|url=https://www.nusoundonline.com/lizzy-mcalpine-give-me-a-minute/|access-date=2021-04-20|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Is TikTok creating a musical meritocracy?|url=https://www.34st.com/article/2020/07/tiktok-music-streaming-spotify-record-label-penelope-scott-yoza-lizzy-mcalpine|access-date=2021-04-20|website=www.34st.com}}</ref> Her unreleased song "You Ruined the 1975", posted in June 2020, has over 8 million views on TikTok as of April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lizzy McAlpine (@lizzymcalpine) Official TikTok {{!}} Watch Lizzy McAlpine's Newest TikTok Videos|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@lizzymcalpine?referer_url=http://www.nusoundonline.com/&referer_video_id=6842001283667266822&refer=embed|access-date=2021-04-20|website=TikTok|language=en}}</ref>

Her musical style has been described as "folk-focused,"<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-18|title=The Hottest Independent Artists In The World: Justice for the Damned, Mstf, Lizzy McAlpine, Showtime Damion and Beren Olivia|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/the-hottest-independent-artists-in-the-world-justice-for-the-damned-mstf-lizzy-mcalpine-showtime-damion-and-beren-olivia/|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Music Business Worldwide|language=en-US}}</ref> "folk-pop,"<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2" /> and a blend of "jazz, pop, and R&B."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lizzy McAlpine|url=https://www.passim.org/artists/lizzy-mcalpine/|access-date=2021-04-20|website=Passim|language=en-US}}</ref> Her collaborators include [[Jacob Collier]], [[Dodie (singer)|Dodie]], [[Tom Rosenthal (musician)|Tom Rosenthal]], Ben Kessler, and Thomas Headon.<ref>{{Citation|title=Fireside Singsongs by Jacob Collier & Dodie & Lizzy McAlpine|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-BM3-OGzOgc|language=en|access-date=2021-04-20}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Tom Rosenthal feat.
