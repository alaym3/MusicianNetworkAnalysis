'''Olga Merediz'''<ref name="theatreworld">{{cite book|last1=Willis|first1=John|title=Theatre World 1992-1993|date=1995|isbn=9781557832030|page=207}}</ref><ref name="hollywood">{{cite web|title=Olga Merediz {{!}} Biography and Filmography {{!}} 1956|url=http://www.hollywood.com/celebrities/olga-merediz-57486177/|publisher=Hollywood.com|access-date=12 August 2017|date=6 February 2015}}</ref> (born February 15, 1956) is an American actress and singer. Merediz originated the role of [[In the Heights#Characters|Abuela Claudia]] in the [[Broadway musical]] ''[[In the Heights]]'', for which she received a [[Tony Award]] nomination for [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical|Best Performance by a Featured Actress in a Musical]]. She played the role for the show's entire [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] run, and reprised the role in the [[In the Heights (film)|film adaptation]].<ref>Gans, Andrew. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/144382-In-the-Heights-to-Close-on-Broadway-in-January-Miranda-to-Return-to-Cast "''In the Heights'' to Close on Broadway in January; Miranda to Return to Cast"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101201060620/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/144382-In-the-Heights-to-Close-on-Broadway-in-January-Miranda-to-Return-to-Cast |date=2010-12-01 }}. ''Playbill''.</ref><ref>Jones, Kenneth. [http://www.playbill.com/news/article/146256-Lights-Out-In-the-Heights-Ends-Broadway-Run-Jan-9-Tour-Continues-and-Film-Is-in-the-Wings "''In the Heights'' Ends Broadway Run Jan. 9; Tour Continues and Film Is in the Wings"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110111022641/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/146256-Lights-Out-In-the-Heights-Ends-Broadway-Run-Jan-9-Tour-Continues-and-Film-Is-in-the-Wings |date=2011-01-11 }}.playbill.com, January 9, 2011.</ref> Her television credits include NBC's 2016 series [[Shades of Blue (TV series)|''Shades of Blue'']] and [[Netflix]]'s ''[[Orange Is the New Black]]''.

==Early life==
Merediz was born in [[Guantánamo|Guantánamo, Cuba]] on February 15, 1956.<ref name="theatreworld" /><ref name="hollywood" /> She left Cuba with her family when she was five years old to [[Miami, Florida]].<ref name="tulane">{{cite web|last1=Freshley|first1=Catherine|title=Tulane University - Balance and Ambition|url=http://www2.tulane.edu/news/newwave/032613_merediz.cfm|publisher=Tulane University|access-date=12 August 2017|language=en}}</ref> Thereafter, she relocated with her family to [[Puerto Rico]] where she grew up.<ref name="tulane" /> Merediz graduated with a bachelor's degree from [[H. Sophie Newcomb Memorial College|Newcomb College]], the former female counterpart to [[Tulane University]] in [[New Orleans]], Louisiana.<ref name="tulane" />

==Career==

===Film and television===
After making her film debut in 1984's ''[[The Brother from Another Planet]]'', Merediz has appeared in a number of films, including ''[[The Milagro Beanfield War]]'' (1988), ''[[City of Hope (film)|City of Hope]]'' (1991), ''[[Evita (1996 film)|Evita]]'' (1996), ''[[Music of the Heart]]'' (1999), ''[[K-Pax]]'' (2001), 
''[[Remember Me (2010 film)|Remember Me]]'' (2010), ''[[Custody (2016 film)|Custody]]'' (2016), ''[[Humor Me (film)|Humor Me]]'' (2017), and the reprise of her 2008 [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical|Tony Award nominated]] Broadway role as Abuela Claudia, in the film adaptation ''[[In the Heights (film)|In the Heights]]'' (2021).

Her television credits, in both dramas and comedies, include recurring and guest roles in ''[[Diary of a Future President]]'', ''[[New Amsterdam (2018 TV series)|New Amsterdam]]'', ''[[Brooklyn Nine-Nine]]'', ''[[Orange Is the New Black]]'' and ''[[Madam Secretary (TV series)|Madam Secretary]]''. She has been a series regular in the UK television series ''[[Bounty Hunters (British TV series)|Bounty Hunters]]'' and the comedy ''[[Saint George (TV series)|Saint George]]'', has appeared in episodes of both ''[[Law & Order (TV series)|Law & Order]]'' and ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit|Law & Order: SVU]]'', and appeared as herself as a judge on ''[[Throwdown with Bobby Flay]]''.

===Theatre and stage===
In addition to ''In the Heights'', her [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] credits include ''Reckless'' at the Manhattan Theatre Club, ''[[Man of La Mancha]]'', and ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]''. She also appeared as Rosie in ''[[Mamma Mia! (musical)|Mamma Mia!]]''.

Before they went to Broadway, Merediz starred in the Off-Broadway versions of ''In the Heights'', and ''The Human Comedy''. Some of her other Off-Broadway credits include ''Women without Men'', ''The Haggadah'', ''Lullabye and Goodnight'', and ''The Blessing''.

Additionally, Merediz was directed by [[John Cassavetes]] in a play called ''Thornhill''.

===Voice-over credits===
Merediz has an extensive voice-over and narration portfolio, including the [[PBS]] Nature documentaries: ''Bears'',<ref>[https://www.pbs.org/wnet/nature/bears-i3rkxv/20457/ Bears]</ref> ''Hotel Armadillo'',<ref>[https://www.pbs.org/wnet/nature/hotel-armadillo-about/15186/ Hotel Armadillo]</ref> ''Jungle Animal Hospital'',<ref>[https://www.pbs.org/wnet/nature/jungle-animal-hospital/14219/ Jungle Animal Hospital]</ref> ''Nature's Miracle Orphans'',<ref>[https://www.pbs.org/wnet/nature/natures-miracle-orphans/12712/ Nature's Miracle Orphans]</ref> and ''Cuba: The Accidental Eden''.<ref>[https://www.pbs.org/wnet/nature/cuba-the-accidental-eden-introduction/5728/ Cuba: The Accidental Eden]</ref> Her voice can be heard in numerous TV/radio commercials, ''[[Dora the Explorer]]'', ''[[Go, Diego, Go!]]'', ''[[Elena of Avalor]]'', and ''[[Encanto (film)|Encanto]]''.

She served as spokesperson for the [[Metropolitan Transportation Authority|MTA]], New York City Transit Authority.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Steinhauer|first1=Jennifer|title=F.Y.I.|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1994/03/20/nyregion/fyi-506591.html|work=The New York Times|access-date=12 August 2017|date=20 March 1994}}</ref>

==Awards and nominations==
At the [[2008 Tony Awards]], she received a nomination for [[Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Musical|Best Performance by a Featured Actress in a Musical]] for the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] incarnation of ''In the Heights''.<ref>Soltes, John. [http://leadernewspapers.net/modules.php?name=News&file=article&sid=7431&new_topic=18 "Growing old with Olga Merediz in the new musical, ‘''In the Heights''’"]. ''The Leader''. June 19, 2008.</ref> In addition to her Tony Award nomination, Merediz won a 2007 [[Drama Desk Award]] for Outstanding Ensemble Performance for her role in the off-Broadway run of ''In the Heights'', as well as a 2003 HOLA Tespis Award from the [[Hispanic Organization of Latin Actors]].<ref>The Broadway League. [http://www.ibdb.com/awardperson.asp?id=82206 "Olga Merediz | Awards and Nominations"]. IBDB.</ref>

In 2013, ''Latina'' magazine named her as one of the 50 Best Latin Singers and Pop Stars of All Time. According to the magazine, "The Cuban Broadway star knows how to dominate a live stage and owns it!"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latina.com/entertainment/music/best-latin-singers-pop-stars-all-time |title=Music |publisher=LATINA |access-date=2017-08-12}}</ref>
