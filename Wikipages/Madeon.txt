'''Hugo Pierre Leclercq''' ({{IPA-fr|yɡo pjɛʁ ləklɛʁ}}; born 30 May 1994), better known by his stage name '''Madeon''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|m|æ||d|i|ən}}), is a French musician, DJ, record producer, singer and songwriter from [[Nantes]], currently based in [[Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://edm.com/interviews/madeon-good-faith-interview|title= MADEON ON GOOD FAITH, MOVING TO LOS ANGELES AND MORE [INTERVIEW]|publisher=EDM.com}}</ref> He initially came to widespread public attention through a YouTube video, "[[Pop Culture (song)|Pop Culture]]", where he performed a mash-up of 39 different popular songs in real-time using a [[Novation Digital Music Systems#Key products|Novation Launchpad]]. It received millions of hits in its first few days of release.<ref name="liberation"/> Leclercq has cited [[The Beatles]] and [[Daft Punk]] as his greatest musical influences.<ref name="liberation"/>  

Leclercq's first EP, ''[[The City (EP)|The City]]'', was released in 2012. His debut studio album, ''[[Adventure (Madeon album)|Adventure]]'', was released on 27 March 2015, supported by a 22-stop North American tour.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.musictimes.com/articles/28106/20150210/madeon-spring-north-american-headlining-tour-adventure-coachella.htm|title=Madeon Announces Spring North American Headlining Tour Supporting 'Adventure'|first=Ryan|last=Middleton|date=10 February 2015|website=Musictimes.com|access-date=25 August 2020}}</ref> On 15 November 2019, Leclercq released his second album, ''[[Good Faith (Madeon album)|Good Faith]]'', as part of the [[Good Faith Live]] tour.

He was ranked at number 105 on ''[[DJ Mag]]''{{'}}s top DJs for 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://djmag.com/news/heres-next-50-acts-who-just-missed-out-top-100-djs-2017|title=Here&#039;s the next 50 acts who just missed out on Top 100 DJs 2017|date=2017-10-25|work=DJMag.com|access-date=2017-11-15|language=en}}</ref>

In 2017, he was nominated for two [[Electronic Music Awards]] with [[Porter Robinson]] for Single of the Year and Live Act of the Year.

''Good Faith'' was nominated for a 2021 [[Grammy Award for Best Dance/Electronic Album|Best Electronic Album]] [[Grammy Award]].<ref name="Grammy">{{Cite web|date=2020-11-24|title=2021 GRAMMYs: Complete Nominees List|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/2021-grammys-complete-nominees-list|access-date=2020-12-03|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref>

==Career beginnings ==
Leclercq started composing music at the age of 11,<ref name=liberation>{{cite news |title=Madeon, l'électro kid |first=François-Luc |last=Doyez |url=http://next.liberation.fr/musique/01012369420-madeon-l-electro-kid |newspaper=Libération Next |date=5 November 2011 |access-date=13 April 2012|language=fr}}</ref> producing [[Eurodance|hands-up]] under the name of "Daemon" and Wayne Mont until 2010 when he began producing house music under his current name, Madeon, which is an [[anagram]] of his previous nickname. He won a remix competition for "[[The Island (Pendulum song)|The Island]]" by Pendulum the same year.<ref>{{cite web|title='The Island' Remix Competition Winners! |url=http://www.pendulum.com/news%2Cthe-island-remix-competition-winners_146.htm |url-status=bot: unknown |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141113154938/http://www.pendulum.com/news%2Cthe-island-remix-competition-winners_146.htm |archive-date=13 November 2014 }}</ref>

He went on to remix tracks by other electronic artists the following year. A clip of Leclercq performing his mash-up "[[Pop Culture (song)|Pop Culture]]", uploaded to YouTube on 11 July 2011, propelled him to international fame. It featured samples from thirty-nine songs mixed together on-the-fly using a [[Novation Digital Music Systems#Key products|Novation Launchpad]]. The clip went viral and reached over six million views in just a few days.<ref name="liberation"/> According to a Reddit AMA he hosted in November 2019, he had the idea, purchased the Launchpad, and recorded the song all on the same day.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.reddit.com/r/electronicmusic/comments/dy6ae9/hello_my_name_is_madeon_and_i_just_released_my/f80picb|title=r/electronicmusic - Comment by u/OfficialMadeon on "Hello ! My name is Madeon and I just released my new album Good Faith. AMA!"|website=Reddit.com|date=18 November 2019|access-date=25 August 2020}}</ref>

During live performances, apart from the Launchpads, he also uses Allen & Heath's Xone:K2, Novation's Launch Control XL and [[Ableton Live]].<ref name=novation>{{cite web|title=Madeon performs London debut with Launchpads|url=http://www.novationmusic.com/uk/community/news/madeon_performs_london_debut_with_launchpads/|work=Novation|access-date=13 April 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120625091609/http://www.novationmusic.com/uk/community/news/madeon_performs_london_debut_with_launchpads/|archive-date=25 June 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> His first live performance was in Paris in April 2011 where he was a support act to [[Yelle]],<ref name="liberation"/> and his UK debut was at The Nest in [[Dalston]], London where he performed alongside the likes of [[Stuart Price|Jacques Lu Cont]].<ref name="novation"/>

Leclercq has been featured several times on "15 Minutes of Fame", a segment of [[Pete Tong]]'s radio show on [[BBC Radio 1]], with both his debut single "Icarus" and his remix for [[deadmau5]]'s "[[Raise Your Weapon]]" given their first full play on the show.{{citation needed|date=April 2012}} His live recorded performance debut was for Pete Tong's BBC Radio 1 gala event in Hull on 27 January 2012, where he played a 20-minute-long set.<ref>{{cite news|title=Madeon – BBC Radio 1 and 1Xtra Live in Hull|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p00np6g0 |newspaper=bbc.co.uk |access-date=13 April 2012}}</ref>

A debut [[Extended play|EP]] was scheduled to be released at the end of 2011,<ref name="liberation"/> but then cancelled in favour of multiple singles.<ref>{{cite web|title=Any news when your EP is out?|url=https://twitter.com/itsmadeon/status/163681944746467328|work=Twitter|access-date=13 April 2012}}</ref> The first single, "[[Icarus (Madeon song)|Icarus]]", was released on 24 February 2012 on his independent label popcultur.{{fact|date=October 2021}}

Madeon toured with [[Lady Gaga]] as an opening act during her [[Born This Way Ball]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/news/madeon/377827-madeon-lady-gaga-collaboration|title=Madeon: 'I've Been Jamming With Lady Gaga' |website=Mtv.co.uk}}</ref> He later worked with Gaga on three tracks, "[[Gypsy (Lady Gaga song)|Gypsy]]", "Mary Jane Holland", and "[[Venus (Lady Gaga song)|Venus]]" on her third studio album, ''[[Artpop]]''.{{fact|date=October 2021}}

In 2012, he was invited to play four major American festivals, the [[Ultra Music Festival]] in Miami,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/code/1552434/ultra-music-festival-weekend-1-day-2-11-things-seen-and-heard|title=Ultra Music Festival Weekend 1, Day 2: 11 Things Seen and Heard}}</ref> [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] in California, [[Lollapalooza]] in Chicago and [[Electric Daisy Carnival#2012|Electric Daisy Carnival in New York]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Coachella – set times|url=http://www.coachella.com/lineup/set-times|work=Coachella.com|access-date=13 April 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120413122318/http://www.coachella.com/lineup/set-times|archive-date=13 April 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> as well as several UK dance music festivals, including the Dance Arena at the [[BBC Radio 1's Big Weekend#Line-up|BBC Radio 1's Big Weekend]]; he also provided support for [[Swedish House Mafia]]'s headlining set at the [[National Bowl|Milton Keynes Bowl]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dancingastronaut.com/2011/11/calvin-harris-and-madeon-added-to-swedish-house-mafia-milton-keynes-lineup/|title=Calvin Harris and Madeon added to Swedish House Mafia Milton Keynes lineup - Dancing Astronaut|date=17 November 2011}}</ref> Leclercq was listed at number 54 on ''[[DJ Mag]]'' in 2012.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.djmag.com/node/34744 | title = Top 100 DJs / 54. Madeon | access-date = 3 February 2013 | work = DJ Mag}}</ref> Madeon released a new single on 3 August 2013 titled "Technicolor."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/madeon/technicolor-original|title=Madeon - Technicolor (Original)}}</ref> On 25 February 2014, Leclercq released a track available for a free download on his website called "[[Cut the Kid]]", stating that it was created circa 2011 or 2012.

Many of his songs have been used in media franchises: "Icarus" was featured in the US show ''[[Dancing with the Stars]]'' in May 2012 and 21 May 2013 (finals with Aly Raisman) and on the in-game soundtrack of ''[[Forza Horizon]]''; "[[Finale (song)|Finale]]" and "[[Imperium (Madeon song)|Imperium]]" have been featured in EA Sports' ''[[FIFA 13]]'' and ''[[FIFA 15]]'' respectively. "You're On" was also featured in ''[[Pro Evolution Soccer 2016]]''. "Finale" has also been included in the opening cinematic of ''[[PlayStation All-Stars Battle Royale]]''; "The City" appears in EA Sports' ''[[NHL 15]]'', ''[[Need for Speed: Most Wanted (2012 video game)|Need for Speed: Most Wanted]]'' and "Technicolor (Club Extended)" in ''[[Need for Speed Rivals]]''.{{citation needed|date=November 2012}} "Finale" and Leclercq's remix of "The Night Out" by [[Martin Solveig]] were featured on the official soundtrack of [[ESPN]]'s [[Winter X Games XVII]] event in Tignes, France and both received several plays on air.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://xgames.espn.go.com/scene/ | title = Scene – X Games|website=Xgames.espn.go.com | access-date = 23 March 2013}}</ref> "Finale" continues to be used as the bumper music for X Games broadcasts.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=http://www.thefuture.fm/blog/news/madeon-announces-adventure-tour-2015/|title=Madeon Announces "Adventure" Tour 2015|date=2015-02-17|website=The Future FM Blog|access-date=2016-05-25}}</ref>

==2015: ''Adventure''==
[[File:Madeon (17090713011).jpg|250px|thumb|right|Madeon live in [[Brussels]], 2015]]
{{Main|Adventure (Madeon album)}}
In September 2014, Leclercq released "[[Imperium (Madeon song)|Imperium]]" as the lead single from his debut studio album, ''[[Adventure (Madeon album)|Adventure]]''. On 8 December 2014, Leclercq released the second single from the album, titled "You're On", featuring Kyan. On 9 February 2015, Leclercq released the album's third single, "Pay No Mind," featuring [[Passion Pit]]. The album's fourth single, "[[Home (Madeon song)|Home]]," was released on 10 March 2015. On 24 March 2015, the final single of ''Adventure'', "Nonsense," was released, featuring [[Mark Foster (singer)|Mark Foster]] of [[Foster the People]]. On 27 March 2015, ''Adventure'' was released.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.facebook.com/itsmadeon/posts/838042512918889 | title = Madeon |website=Facebook.com| access-date = 20 January 2015}}</ref> The album peaked at number 43 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6305752/Madeon/chart?f=305|title=Madeon - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=Billboard.com|access-date=2016-04-27}}</ref> and at number one on the US [[Dance/Electronic Albums]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6305752/Madeon/chart?f=322|title=Madeon - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=Billboard.com|access-date=2016-04-27}}</ref>

In mid-2015, Leclercq embarked on his first headlining tour, Adventure Live, consisting of 22 stops in North America.<ref name=":0" /><ref name="auto"/> The tour ended on 9 May 2015. Leclercq further promoted his debut studio album on his Pixel Empire US Tour in early 2016, which ended on 21 February 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/54889/20151117/madeon-mark-foster-nonsense-video-2016-pixel-empire-us-tour.htm|title=Madeon Shares 'Nonsense' Video Ahead Of 2016 Pixel Empire US Tour|date=2015-11-17|website=Music Times|access-date=2016-05-25}}</ref>

==2016–2017: ''Shelter''==
{{Main|Shelter (Porter Robinson and Madeon song)}}
On 11 August 2016, Leclercq released with his close friend [[Porter Robinson]] a new track called "[[Shelter (Porter Robinson and Madeon song)|Shelter]]". Throughout 2016–2017, he toured the US and Europe alongside [[Porter Robinson]] on a live show they called the [[Shelter Live Tour]]. This back to back set contained tracks made by both artists, often mixed together between themselves. Throughout the tour both Madeon and Porter also would sing their own songs. Their last performance was on 23 April 2017 at the [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youredm.com/2017/01/03/porter-robinson-confirms-coachella-final-shelter-live-show/|title=Porter Robinson & Madeon's Last Ever "Shelter" Performance Has Been Revealed|website=Youredm.com|date=3 January 2017|access-date=25 August 2020}}</ref>

In August 2016, the new gameplay trailer of EA Sports' ''[[FIFA 17]]'' was released, featuring Madeon's remix of "[[Song 2]]" by [[Blur (band)|Blur]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://youtube.com/watch?v=yYjD78X1d9Q/|title=Football, Powered by Frostbite - FIFA 17 Official Gameplay Trailer|date=2016-08-16|website=Youtube|access-date=2016-08-16}}</ref> which was also featured in a BBC Radio 1's Essential Mix in 2012. Leclercq's collaboration with [[Porter Robinson]], "[[Shelter (Porter Robinson and Madeon song)|Shelter]]" was included in the ''[[FIFA 17]]'' soundtrack.

==2018–present: ''Good Faith''==
{{Main|Good Faith (Madeon album)}}
On 13 November 2017, Leclercq announced that he would be releasing his next project in 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://djmag.com/news/madeon-offers-update-next-era-career|title=Madeon offers update on "next era" of career|date=2017-11-14|work=DJMag.com|access-date=2017-11-15}}</ref> Further hints about the project were not officially revealed until 20 August 2018, when Leclercq's creative director was interviewed via a Madeon-inspired podcast called the Pixel Empire Podcast.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.edmtunes.com/2018/08/madeon-will-reveal-details-of-2nd-album-via-pixel-empire-podcast/|title=Madeon Teases Clues of 2nd Album via Pixel Empire Podcast|date=2018-08-20|website=EDMTunes|access-date=2018-09-06}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BmuH7RL2hGg|title=Pixel Empire Podcast Episode 6: The Architects|date=2018-08-20|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=2018-09-06}}</ref>

On 30 January 2018, Leclercq stated on his Instagram and Twitter that he had moved to the United States to finish his upcoming album. On 20 March 2019, Leclercq announced his new live show would debut on Lollapalooza 2019. He stated this would not be a DJ set but rather a new live show; fans will be expecting new music.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20190620090832/https://twitter.com/madeon/status/1108431988254400512] </ref>

Later that year, on 28 May 2019, Leclercq announced that he would be releasing a new song on 29 May 2019 through a new website, goodfaith.world. On 29 May 2019, the goodfaith.world website was updated with a video<ref>{{cite web|author=Madeon|title=FOUR CITIES|date=2019-05-29|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TRL98Urky2w|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=2019-05-29}}</ref> previewing the new song, titled "[[All My Friends (Madeon song)|All My Friends]]", along with locations of record stores in four cities: [[Los Angeles]], [[New York City|New York]], [[Ålesund]], and [[Nantes]]. Each record store was issued two copies of the new single on clear 7" vinyl. On 30 May 2019, the full official audio was released on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web|author=Madeon|title= Madeon - All My Friends (Official Audio)|date=2019-05-30|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_qYyU019PbI|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=2019-05-30}}</ref>

On 10 July 2019, Leclercq premiered the first episode of Good Faith Radio (Beats Radio on Apple Music), where he officially played and released his next single, "Dream Dream Dream".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://weraveyou.com/2019/07/madeon-debuts-good-faith-radio-beats1radio/|title=Madeon debuts Good Faith Radio show midweek on Beats 1 Radio|date=2019-07-14|website=We Rave You|access-date=2019-07-15}}</ref> Along with the single, he played tracks by other artists, including some sent by his own fans accompanied by fan-made art.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/gabbyshreve/status/1149029192585175047?s=20|title=GFR Episode 1 - Art Feature by Gabrielle Shreve|date=2019-07-10|website=Twitter|access-date=2020-02-12}}</ref> He described the radio show as "...a space I want us to share, I want to introduce you to my favorite things and showcase some of the amazing music and art you create."<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=madeon|number=1148263705848602630|title=It's a space I want us to share, I want to introduce you to my favorite things and showcase some of the amazing music and art you create. The first episode airs THIS Wednesday at 11am PT. I'll be playing my new single Dream Dream Dream.|date=2019-07-08|access-date=2019-07-10}}</ref> Along with the radio premiere, it was released on music streaming platforms and a visualizer video was released on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web|author=MadeonVEVO|title=Madeon - Dream Dream Dream (Visualizer)|date=2019-07-10|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qQWAicHiVhk|access-date=2019-07-10}}</ref>

The [[Good Faith Live]] tour was premiered at Lollapalooza 2019,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/madeon/status/1158434428445757441?s=20|title=been waiting for this moment for years i will truly never forget this. Good Faith Live premiere at Lollapalooza. pic.twitter.com/x2rJUAuqfP|last=Madeon|date=2019-08-05|website=@madeon|language=en|access-date=2019-09-26}}</ref> followed by headlining shows in [[London]], [[Amsterdam]], and [[Paris]]. A longer North American leg started on 30 October in [[Vancouver]] and ended on 14 December in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], followed by an Australian tour as part of FOMO Festival in January 2020.

On 29 October 2019, Leclercq revealed that [[Good Faith (Madeon album)|''Good Faith'']] would be released on 15 November 2019. In an interview with [[NPR Music]], Leclercq said the album was based on his relationship to joy and his mental health: "I wanted to make music that was celebratory but that hinted at the fact that that joy had to have been reclaimed and fought for a little bit."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2019/12/07/785804884/french-dj-madeon-on-good-faith|title=French Entertainer Madeon On 'Good Faith'|website=NPR.org|language=en|access-date=2019-12-11}}</ref>

Madeon worked again with Lady Gaga on the track "[[911 (Lady Gaga song)|911]]" from her 2020 studio album, ''[[Chromatica]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Spano|first=Brittany|date=30 May 2020|title=Welcome to 'Chromatica': Inside Lady Gaga's Triumphant Dance Floor Return|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/lady-gaga-chromatica-making-of-bloodpop-axwell-1007139/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://archive.today/20200617192833/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/lady-gaga-chromatica-making-of-bloodpop-axwell-1007139/|archive-date=17 June 2020|access-date=16 June 2020|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref>

On 9 May 2020, Madeon played a DJ set for the virtual Secret Sky music festival. During the set, he previewed a song called "The Prince".<ref>Madeon (9 May 2020) [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PSRdKi2GscM "MADEON DJ SET (Secret Sky Festival)"]. [[YouTube]]. Retrieved 4 September 2020.</ref> The song was released on 14 August that year. Along with the release, Madeon launched an [[alternate reality game]]. When it was solved, a 6-track EP named ''12122017''  was found, reportedly named for the date it was made.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-01|title=Madeon Fans Finally Solve Riddle To Accessing Secret 2017 EP|url=https://www.youredm.com/2020/09/01/madeon-fans-finally-solve-riddle-to-accessing-secret-2017-ep/|access-date=2020-09-20|website=Your EDM|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=2020-09-02|title=The cryptic maze to uncover Madeon's secret EP has been solved [Listen]|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2020/09/the-cryptic-maze-to-uncover-madeons-secret-ep-has-been-solved-listen/|access-date=2020-09-20|website=Dancing Astronaut|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-02|title=Madeon Fans Solve Riddle To Unlock Secret 2017 'Celine 12122017' EP|url=https://www.cultr.com/news/madeon-fans-solve-riddle-to-unlock-secret-2017-celine-12122017-ep/|access-date=2020-09-20|website=CULTR|language=en-US}}</ref> However, after an email correspondence with Leclercq, he revealed that the EP was produced on 6 December 2017.<ref>https://cdn.discordapp.com/attachments/250710137671516161/792507169592967168/unknown.png</ref> The creation of the EP is attributed to Madeon's own anger due to "not feeling anything from music",<ref name=":1" /> and has referred to the EP as a document that serves as context to the Good Faith album instead of an EP.

On 18 September 2021, Madeon premiered the Good Faith Forever Live set at Second Sky music festival in Oakland, California.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bein|first=Kat|title=The 10 Best Moments of Porter Robinson's Second Sky 2021 Festival|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/9632815/porter-robinson-second-sky-festival-2021-best-moments|date=2020-09-20|access-date=2021-09-20|website=Billboard}}</ref>
