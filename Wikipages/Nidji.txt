'''Nidji''' is an Indonesian rock band based in [[Jakarta]]. Formed on February 2002, Nidji's name comes from the [[Japanese language|Japanese word]] ''niji'' (虹), meaning "rainbow". The band's music can be classified under the [[alternative pop]] genre, and the band has cited [[Coldplay]] and [[The Killers]] as major influences.<ref name="Bisnis">{{cite web|url=http://lifestyle.bisnis.com/read/20150510/225/431759/nidji-akan-tampil-di-otobursa-tumplek-blek-malam-ini|title=Nidji Akan Tampil di Otobursa Tumplek Blek Malam Ini|trans-title=Nidji will perform at Otobursa Tumplek Blek tonight|website=[[Bisnis Indonesia]]|language=id|first=Puput Ady|last=Sukarno|date=May 10, 2015|access-date=December 24, 2017}}</ref> The band currently consists of Yusuf Ubay (vocals/saxophone), Andro Regantoro (bass), Randy Danistha (keyboards/synthesizers), Ramadhista Akbar and Andi Ariel Harsya (guitars), and Adri Prakarsa (drums).<ref name="Giring left">{{cite web|url=https://www.cnnindonesia.com/hiburan/20180103175100-227-266502/ditinggal-giring-jadi-caleg-nidji-resmi-vakum|title=Ditinggal Giring Jadi Caleg, Nidji Resmi Vakum|trans-title=Giring left the band for joining politics, Nidji officially on hiatus|website=[[CNN Indonesia]]|language=id|first=Agniya|last=Khoiri|date=January 3, 2018|access-date=January 9, 2018}}</ref>
 
The band has released six [[Single (music)|single]]s: "Child", "Sudah", "Hapus Aku", "Kau dan Aku", "Disco Lazy Time" and "Biarlah". Two of the singles, "Heaven" from the ''[[Breakthru' (Nidji album)|Breakthru']]'' album and  "Shadows", taken from the ''Top Up'' album, were used as background music in the Southeast Asian third season promos for the television series ''[[Heroes (American TV series)|Heroes]]'',<ref>{{cite web |date =November 9, 2008|url=http://quirkyindonesia.blogspot.com/2008/11/heroes-season-3-shadows-by-nidji.html |title=~ Life in Indonesia ~: Heroes Season 3 – Shadows by Nidji|website=Quirky Indonesia|access-date=June 21, 2013}}</ref> and they provided soundtrack music for the Indonesian film ''[[Laskar Pelangi (film)|Laskar Pelangi]]''.

The album ''Breakthru''' was released in 2005 with an [[English language|English-language]] version the following year. The second album, ''Top Up'', was released in 2007.

== History ==
Nidji was formed in February 2002. The beginnings of Nidji came about through the friendship between Rama and Andro. They were joined by Arieland together they wrote a song entitled "Maria". However, "Maria" was not completed until Giring met them and added some vocals.

Afterwards, Andro recommended Adri who had often done jam sessions with him to fill the position of drummer. The four of them then invited Rama to join. At the beginning of April 2005, Nidji added another member, Randy, a keyboard player who was a close friend of Giring.<ref name="Musica Bio">{{cite web|url=https://www.musica.id/detail-artist/27-NIDJI?id=3|title=Artist: Nidji|website=[[Musica Studio's]]|language=id|access-date=December 24, 2017}}</ref>

In 2012, the "Liberty Victoria" song was used by [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] as a new anthem.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thejakartapost.com/news/2012/04/04/nidji-s-song-becomes-mu-s-next-anthem.html |title=Nidji’s song becomes MU’s next anthem |work=[[The Jakarta Post]] |first=Arief |last=Suhardiman |date=April 4, 2012 |access-date=April 5, 2012}}</ref>

Five years later, on April 5, 2017, the band released a new album entitled ''Love, Fake & Friendship''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hot.detik.com/music/3465655/nidji-buka-puasa-5-tahun-tanpa-album-lewat-love-fake--friendship/228|title=Nidji Buka Puasa 5 Tahun Tanpa Album Lewat 'Love, Fake & Friendship'|trans-title=Nidji releases 'Love, Fake & Friendship' after 5 years without album|website=[[DetikCom|Detik.com]]|language=id|first=Dicky|last=Ardian|date=April 5, 2017|access-date=January 9, 2018}}</ref> It is the last album that featured the original vocalist Giring before he left for his political career. From December 2018 to January 2019, Nidji and Musica Studios opened auditions to find Giring's successor. From around 2,000 participants, 10 chosen candidates had the opportunity to perform with Giring and the band. At Giring's final concert "Mimpi adalah Kunci" on February 2, 2019, Yusuf Ubay, who was also a ''[[Indonesian Idol (season 8)|2014 Indonesian Idol]]'' contestant, was chosen as Nidji's new vocalist.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.pikiran-rakyat.com/hidup-gaya/2019/02/03/konser-mimpi-adalah-kunci-detik-detik-ubay-yusuf-gantikan-giring-nidji-sebagai-vokalis|title=Konser 'Mimpi adalah Kunci', Detik-detik Yusuf Ubay Gantikan Giring Nidji|trans-title=The 'Mimpi adalah Kunci' Concert, Yusuf Ubay Moment to Replace Nidji's Giring|newspaper=[[Pikiran Rakyat]]|language=id|first=Windy Eka|last=Pramudya|date=February 3, 2019|access-date=February 11, 2019}}</ref>

==Side projects==
===NEV===
In October 2013, Nidji formed a [[side project]] called NEV (Nidji Electronic Version), which they used as an avenue to explore their [[Electronic music|electronic]] side. In this project, they rearranged their songs to the electronic version. However, the remix version will not be released to be an album. They will play it in live performance only.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hai.grid.id/Feature/Music/Nev-Sisi-Elektronik-Dari-Nidji|title=NEV, Sisi Elektronik Dari Nidji|trans-title=NEV, the electronic side of Nidji|website=[[Kompas Gramedia Group#Magazines|Hai Grid]]|language=id|date=October 31, 2013|access-date=January 9, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://hot.detik.com/music/2399938/nidji-bentuk-alter-ego-nidji-electronic-version|title=Nidji Bentuk Alter Ego 'Nidji Electronic Version'|trans-title=Nidji forms alter ego 'Nidji Electronic Version'|website=[[DetikCom|Detik.com]]|language=id|date=October 30, 2013|access-date=January 9, 2018}}</ref>

===NEV+===
Following the departure of the vocalist Giring and the hiatus of Nidji, the other five members formed a side project called NEV+ (Nidji Electronic Version Plus) in 2017. Its concept is the same with NEV. Without Giring participation, NEV name was changed to NEV+. 'Plus' means the collaboration of Nidji with a number of other singers, to fill in the gaps left by Giring.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.viva.co.id/showbiz/musik/954941-nidji-vakum-lahir-nev-plus|title=Nidji Vakum, Lahir NEV Plus|trans-title=Nidji on hiatus, NEV Plus formed|website=[[Visi Media Asia|Viva]]|language=id|date=September 9, 2017|access-date=January 9, 2018}}</ref>

In 2018, NEV+ alongside [[Noah (band)|NOAH]] vocalist [[Nazril Irham|Nazril "Ariel" Irham]] and [[:id:HIVI!|Hivi!]] former vocalist [[:id:Dalila Azkadiputri|Dalila "Dea" Azkadiputri]] performed "[[Janger Persahabatan (Asian Games 2018 Official Song)|Janger Persahabatan]]", a song written by Guruh Soekarnoputra. This song was made as one of the official songs for the [[2018 Asian Games]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://m.liputan6.com/showbiz/read/3523541/bareng-nev-plus-dan-dea-ariel-noah-berpartisipasi-di-asian-games-2018|title=Bareng Nev Plus dan Dea, Ariel NOAH Berpartisipasi di Asian Games 2018|trans-title=With Nev Plus and Dea, Ariel NOAH performing the 2018 Asian Games song|website=[[Liputan 6]]|language=id|first=Ruly|last=Riantrisnanto|date=May 13, 2018|access-date=June 4, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://entertainment.kompas.com/read/2018/05/02/182401810/guruh-soekarnoputra-janger-persahabatan-cocok-dengan-suara-ariel-noah|title=Guruh Soekarnoputra: "Janger Persahabatan" Cocok dengan Suara Ariel NOAH|trans-title=Guruh Soekarnoputra: "Janger Persahabatan" fits Ariel NOAH's vocals|newspaper=[[Kompas]]|language=id|first=Andi Muttya Keteng|last=Pangerang|date=May 2, 2018|access-date=June 4, 2018}}</ref>
