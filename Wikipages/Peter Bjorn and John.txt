'''Peter Bjorn and John''' are a Swedish [[indie pop]]/[[Indie rock|rock]] band formed in [[Stockholm]] in 1999 and named after the first names of the band's members: [[Peter Morén]] (vocals, guitar and harmonica), [[Björn Yttling]] (bass guitar, keyboards and vocals) and [[John Eriksson (musician)|John Eriksson]], known in his solo work as Hortlax Cobra (drums, percussion and vocals). Yttling also worked as producer for the band's first four albums.

They are best known for the 2006 single "[[Young Folks]]", which featured [[Victoria Bergsman]], formerly of [[the Concretes]], and was a top 20 hit in the [[UK Singles Chart]], as well as being featured in the football video game ''[[FIFA 08]]'', the baseball video game ''[[MLB 2K13]]'', the singing game ''[[Lips (video game)|Lips]]'', as the school project in the film ''[[Bandslam]]'', as part of the soundtrack of hit sitcom ''[[How I Met Your Mother]]'', as the opening track in the [[Pilot (Gossip Girl)|pilot]] episode of ''[[Gossip Girl]]'' and the theme tune to British Television adverts for Homebase store and [[Air New Zealand]] "Safety Safari" surfing video. It was also named ''[[NME]]'''s second-best track of 2006, beaten by "[[Over and Over (Hot Chip song)|Over and Over]]" by [[Hot Chip]].<ref>"[http://www.nme.com/news/nme/25390 NME 2006 Poll]". NME.com (2006). Retrieved 20 January 2007.</ref> In [[Australia]] it was voted number 16 in the annual [[Triple J]] [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2006|Hottest 100 for 2006]]. In recent years, Lars Skoglund of the band [[Laakso (band)|Laakso]] has filled in for Eriksson on drums and played bongos during some live shows since the latter developed tinnitus, and a shoulder injury on the tour for 2011's ''Gimme Some''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://indiespect.ch/2019/03/interview-peter-bjorn-and-john-en/|title=Interview with Peter Bjorn and John: Hidden dogmas behind their records|website=indiespect.ch|date=16 March 2019|access-date=2019-11-12}}</ref>

==History==
===Formation: 1997–2000===
Morén and Yttling began playing music with each other while in school. They shared an interest in bands such as [[The Stone Roses]] and [[Ride (band)|Ride]],{{CN|date=May 2021}} and would draw inspiration from acts they heard on the radio while growing up, including [[Depeche Mode]], [[Fleetwood Mac]] and [[Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark]] (OMD).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/feature/peter-bjorn-and-john|title=Peter Bjorn And John - Clash Q&A|date=18 March 2009|website=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]|access-date=24 May 2021|url-status=bot: unknown|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090322175250/http://www.clashmusic.com/feature/peter-bjorn-and-john|archive-date=22 March 2009|df=dmy-all}}</ref> They are also influenced by, but not interested in replicating, classic '60s baroque pop, power pop and new wave.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/peter-bjorn-and-john-mn0000843995/biography | title=Peter, Bjorn and John: Biography | author=Sendra, Tim | publisher=[[AllMusic]] | access-date=24 May 2021}}</ref> Their first band '''Piggy In The Middle''' released the album ''Images & Distorted Facts'' in 1997<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blaskoteket.se/artiklar/sound-affects/30/piggy-in-the-middle-fusionmusiker-pa-popjakt/|title=[SOUND AFFECTS] Piggy In The Middle: Fusionmusiker på popjakt – BLASKOTEKET|language=sv-SE|access-date=2019-11-12}}</ref> before disbanding after Morén and Yttling moved to [[Stockholm]], whereupon they met Eriksson in 1999. Their first gig as Peter Bjorn and John was an unsuccessful one aboard a boat in Stockholm; their expectations were not high from the start, as they "just wanted to make good music for [their] own amusement".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pbsfm.org.au/Documents.asp?ID=5379&Title=Peter+Bjorn+And+John+Q&A|title=PBS|website=Pbsfm.org.au|access-date=19 October 2018}}</ref>

===First years: 2001–2005===
After releasing the ''Forbidden Chords'' EP and a pair of singles ("Failing and Passing" and "I Don't Know What I Want Us to Do"), the band released its self-titled first album in 2002 on the tiny Beat That! label. After more shows, more EPs (''People They Know'' and ''100m of Hurdles''), and another single ("See Through"), the group jumped to the Planekonomi label in 2004 and released the ''Beats, Traps and Backgrounds'' EP. It was soon followed by the 2004 album ''[[Falling Out (Peter Bjorn and John album)|Falling Out]]'', which was picked up for American release by Hidden Adenda in late 2005.<ref name="Music Aol">{{cite web|url=http://music.aol.com/artist/peter-bjorn-and-john/biography|title=AOL - News, Sports, Weather, Entertainment, Local & Lifestyle|work=AOL.com|access-date=30 January 2015}}</ref>

===Mainstream success: 2006–2011===
The band's third album, ''[[Writer's Block (Peter Bjorn and John album)|Writer's Block]]'' (2006), followed a year later and became a minor international hit. The video to their song "Young Folks" was animated and directed by [[Ted Malmros]] from [[Shout Out Louds]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.peterbjornandjohn.com/|title=Peter Björn and John official website|website=Peterbjornandjohn.com|date=2007-02-08}} Last accessed April 21, 2007.</ref>

After completing various tours in support of ''Writer's Block'', the band focused on other projects before coming together to record and release a largely instrumental album, ''[[Seaside Rock]]'', in the autumn of 2008. ''[[Living Thing]]'', the band's darker, more experimental fourth full-length album, was released several months later in early 2009. Peter Bjorn and John played [[Lollapalooza]] 2009 in Chicago.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roxwel.com/player/lolla09peterbjornandjohn.html/|title=Lollapalooza interview|date=August 2009|website=Roxwel.com|access-date=25 August 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100201132429/http://www.roxwel.com/player/lolla09peterbjornandjohn.html|archive-date=1 February 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2011, Peter Bjorn and John returned to their melodic pop roots with the decidedly more accessible ''[[Gimme Some]]''. The band also opened up for [[Depeche Mode]] during their [[Tour of the Universe (tour)|Tour of the Universe]].<ref name="Music Aol"/> ''Gimme Some''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s second single "Second Chance" is heard prominently in advertising for the American [[light beer]] [[Bud Light]] and as the [[theme song]] for the [[CBS]] sitcom ''[[2 Broke Girls]]''.

===Recent years: 2012–present===
The three band members are also founding members of the Swedish artist collective and record label [[Ingrid (record label)|INGRID]]. The band soon revealed that work had commenced on their seventh album, but were hit by creative setbacks.<ref>{{cite web|last=Studarus |first=Laura |url=http://www.undertheradarmag.com/interviews/in_the_studio_peter_bjorn_and_john_on_their_next_album/ |title=In the Studio: Peter Bjorn and John on Their Next Album {{pipe}} Under the Radar - Music Magazine |website=Undertheradarmag.com |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref> In April 2015, Peter Bjorn and John released their first new music in four years with one-off single "High Up (Take Me to the Top)" on ''INGRID Volym 2'', a [[Record Store Day]] exclusive double-LP of which there are only 500 copies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.transversomedia.com/articles//peter-bjorn-and-john-high-up-take-me-to-the-top?rq=peter%20bjorn |title=Hear Peter Bjorn and John's First Single in Four Years "High Up (Take Me To The Top)" — TRANSVERSO |website=Transversomedia.com |date=2015-04-25 |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref> In July of the same year, the band debuted two new songs on [[NPR]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.transversomedia.com/articles//peter-bjorn-and-john-debut-new-song-do-si-do-on-nprs-world-cafe?rq=peter%20bjorn%20john |title=Peter Bjorn and John Debut Two New Songs, Hint at New Album on NPR's World Cafe — TRANSVERSO |website=Transversomedia.com |date=2015-07-27 |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref> before officially announcing forthcoming LP ''[[Breakin' Point]]'' much later on 11 March 2016, along with promotional single "What You Talking About."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.transversomedia.com/articles//what-you-talking-about-peter-bjorn-and-john-finally-return-with-new-single-album-announcement |title="What You Talking About?" Peter Bjorn and John Finally Return With New Single, Album Announcement — TRANSVERSO |website=Transversomedia.com |date=2016-03-10 |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref> [[Rolling Stone]] said of the single: "The band also unveiled the album's first single "What You Talking About?," an infectious slice of synth-heavy indie pop." The album was released on June 10, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.esquire.com/entertainment/music/a45618/peter-bjorn-and-john-profile-breakin-point/ |title=Peter, Bjorn and John Profile – The Trio Reclaimed Their Sound on 'Breakin' Point' |website=Esquire.com |date=2016-06-08 |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref> The album's first lead single (bearing the same name as the album), "[[Breakin' Point (song)|Breakin' Point]]" was released on 24 March 2016, following being premiered by Zane Lowe on Apple's [[Apple Music|Beats 1 Radio]] as the World Record.

In August 2018, the band announced the upcoming release of their eighth album, [[Darker Days (Peter Bjorn and John album)|''Darker Days'']], which was released on October 19, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|author=Sam Sodomsky |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/peter-bjorn-and-john-announce-new-album-and-tour/ |title=Peter Bjorn and John Announce New Album and Tour |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2018-08-08 |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref>

On February 22, 2019, the band released a follow up EP to ''Darker Days'' entitled ''EPBJ'' and featuring three new songs that were recorded during the same time as Darker Days, and, as the press release says, "showcase a softer and more introspective side of the band."<ref>{{cite web|author=Ana Yglesias |url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/peter-bjorn-and-john-announce-round-two-north-american-tour-new-ep |title=Peter Bjorn And John Announce Round Two North American Tour, New EP |publisher=Grammy.com |date=2019-01-15 |access-date=2019-05-02}}</ref> The band also released audio of a live studio session recorded at INGRID Studios in Stockholm, consisting of three tracks.

On October 18, 2019, the band announced their ninth studio album ''Endless Dream'' and released the single "Rusty Nail". The album was released on March 13, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|author=Peter Bjorn and John |url=https://twitter.com/PeterBjornJohn/status/1185103363375091712 |title=Peter Bjorn and John on Twitter: We celebrate our 20-year anniversary in 2020 with the release of our ninth full-length studio album – Endless Dream – due out March 13th via INGRID. The first single "Rusty Nail" is out now! #rustynail #endlessdream |website=Twitter.com |date=2019-10-18 |access-date=2020-03-14}}</ref>

==Collaborations and solo work==
Peter, Bjorn and John also collaborated with Canadian-born actor and hip-hop star [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]] on his 2009 mixtape, [[So Far Gone (mixtape)|So Far Gone]], on the song "Let's Call It Off." Many rappers like [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]], [[GZA]] and [[88 Keys]] collaborated on ''Re-Living Thing'', a remix of ''[[Living Thing]]'', released on 8 September 2009 by [[Mick Boogie]].

Morén has released four solo albums and one as a duo. On 8 April 2008 he released a solo album in English, ''[[The Last Tycoon (album)|The Last Tycoon]]'' on the [[Wichita Recordings]] and [[Quarterstick Records]] labels.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.avclub.com/articles/peter-moren-the-last-tycoon,7167/ | title=Social Competence by Peter Moren | author=Marc Hawthorne | website=Avclub.com | date=April 7, 2008 | access-date=October 10, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/the-last-tycoon-mw0000753105|title=The Last Tycoon - Peter Morén - Songs, Reviews, Credits - AllMusic|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=19 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/48381-peter-morens-ithe-last-tycooni-tracklist-revealed|title=Pitchfork: Peter Moren's "The Last Tycoon" Tracklist Revealed<!-- Bot generated title -->|website=Pitchforkmedia.com|access-date=19 October 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090114023514/http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/news/48381-peter-morens-ithe-last-tycooni-tracklist-revealed|archive-date=14 January 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2010, he released a solo album in Swedish, ''[[I spåren av tåren|I Spåren Av Tåren]]''. In 2012 he released the more political [[Pyramiden (album)|Pyramiden]], influenced by modern indie, new wave, and the formation of the artistic collective [[Ingrid (record label)|INGRID]], of which he is a founding member. He released his fourth solo album ''40'' in 2017 and in 2019 released a collaborative album with Swedish pop songwriter David Shutrick called ''En Åldrande Befolkning''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ng.se/recensioner/musik/peter-moren-och-david-shutrick-en-aldrande-befolkning|title=Peter Morén och David Shutrick - En åldrande befolkning|last1=Bat For Lashes - The Boys of Summer (Live at EartH|first1=London|last2=Aloha|first2=Son Little-|website=NÖJESGUIDEN|language=sv|access-date=2020-02-08|last3=Hotspot|first3=Pet Shop Boys-}}</ref> which was released first as an EP, then later expanded to an album and released in a second edition as an LP later that year.<ref>[https://shop.startracks.se/en/vinyl/peter-moren-david-shutrick-en-aldrande-befolkning-vinyl-lp-preor.html] {{dead link|date=March 2020}}</ref>

Björn Yttling has previously worked on jazz music and as a member of [[Ingrid (record label)|INGRID]] has collaborated in bands such as 'Smile' as well as pursuing his solo work as 'Yttling Jazz'. John Eriksson, having previously released three EPs under the name, has continued as a member of [[Ingrid (record label)|Ingrid]] to work under the name Hortlax Cobra releasing experimental electronic music. His first album, 'Night Shift', was released on the collective's record label in 2012. His second album '1984' was released in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|last=Camp |first=Zoe |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2012/12/album-review-hortlax-cobra-1984/ |title=Hortlax Cobra – 1984 {{pipe}} Album Reviews |publisher=[[Consequence of Sound]] |date=2012-12-14 |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref>  In 2017 he released his third album "Lightworks" on INGRID.<ref>{{cite web|last=Zimmerman |first=Lee |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2017/05/hortlax-cobra-lightworks-review.html |title=Hortlax Cobra: Lightworks :: Music :: Reviews :: Hortlax Cobra :: Paste |website=Pastemagazine.com |date=8 May 2017 |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref>  He is also a member of the Ingrid project Starlight Serenaders.

The group collaborated with producer [[Claptone]], providing vocals on the track "Puppet Theatre". The song is the first single from the album ''Charmer'', released in October 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/6648299/claptone-announces-debut-album-charmer-premieres-first-track-puppet |title=Claptone Announces Debut Album 'Charmer,' Premieres First Track 'Puppet Theatre' (Exclusive) |website=Billboard.com |date=2015-07-28 |access-date=2018-09-13}}</ref>
