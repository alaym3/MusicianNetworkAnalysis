'''Spacey Jane''' are an [[indie rock]] garage band from [[Perth]], Western Australia, who formed in 2016. The band consists of Caleb Harper (lead vocals, guitar, songwriting),  Kieran Lama (drummer and co-manager), Ashton Hardman-Le Cornu (guitar) and Peppa Lane (bass guitar); the latter of which replaced Amelia Murray following her departure from the band in July 2019.

The band's debut studio album ''[[Sunlight (Spacey Jane album)|Sunlight]]'' debuted at number 2 on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Albums Chart]] in June 2020. ''Sunlight'' won the annual [[Triple J]] Album poll in December 2020, as voted by listeners.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/spacey-jane-tops-the-2020-triple-j-album-poll/12972128|title=Spacey Jane tops the 2020 triple j Album Poll|first=Declan|last=Byrne|date=13 December 2020|website=triple j}}</ref> The band also held four positions in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2020|2020 Triple J Hottest 100]], with "Skin" reaching number 15 and "[[Booster Seat]]" reaching number 2.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://index.html/|title=Hottest 100 2020 &#124; triple j|website=index.html}}</ref>

==History==
===2016–2018: Formation and ''No Way to Treat an Animal''===
[[File:Spacey Jane at the Low302 in 2018.jpg|thumb|Spacey Jane performing at the Low302 in Sydney, Australia in September 2018.]]
Spacey Jane formed in 2016, amidst the isolated nature of Perth's music scene<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Stratton |first1=Jon |title=The difference of Perth music: A scene in cultural and historical context |journal=Continuum |date=6 August 2010 |volume=22 |issue=5 |pages=613–622 |doi=10.1080/10304310802311634 }}</ref> by Caleb Harper and Kieran Lama who grew up in [[Geraldton]], Western Australia, and played together in a band during high school, called Sicchino.<ref name=":3" /> Ashton Hardman-Le Cornu and Amelia Murrary met through a university French class. Lama then met Hardman-Le Cornu through a mutual relationship. The band name, Spacey Jane, came to be during a night out in Perth with one of the band members' mutual friends, Jane, as stated by the band.<ref name=":1" />

In August 2017, the band released their debut single "Still Running", which was followed by the EP ''No Way to Treat an Animal'' in November 2017.

===2018–2019: ''In the Meantime'' and ''In the Slight''===
[[File:Spacey Jane live at Miama Marketta in November, 2019.jpg|thumb|Spacey Jane performing at Miama Marketta in November 2019.]]

The band has performed at several festivals, including [[St Jerome's Laneway Festival|Laneway]] and were the winners of the [[Triple J Unearthed]] competition in 2018, resulting in their inclusion in the 2018 [[Falls Festival]] line up.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Spacey Jane|url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/artist/spacey-jane|website=triple j Unearthed|language=en|access-date=19 May 2020}}</ref> Three of the four founding members decided to commit to a career in music while attending Splendour in the Grass music festival in 2016. Bass player Amelia Murray left the band in 2019 to pursue a career in medicine, and was replaced by Peppa Lane. Lane's second show with the band was at the Splendour at the Grass music festival.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newcastleherald.com.au/story/6780098/spacey-jane-ready-to-bask-in-sunlight-of-their-album-debut/|title=Spacey Jane ready to bask in Sunlight of their album debut|first=Lisa|last=Rockman|date=4 June 2020|website=Newcastle Herald}}</ref> Spacey Jane's "Good for You" polled at number 80 in [[Triple J]]'s [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2019|Hottest 100 of 2019]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Search {{!}} Hottest 100 Archive {{!}} triple j|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/archive/search/|date=11 November 2017|website=ABC|language=en-AU|access-date=19 May 2020}}</ref><ref name=":0" /> The band has supported the likes of [[the Jungle Giants]] and [[Alex Lahey]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Melbourne » Laneway Festival '20|url=https://lanewayfestival.com/melbourne/line-up/spacey-jane|website=Laneway Festival '20|access-date=19 May 2020}}</ref>

===2019–2021: ''Sunlight''===
{{Main|Sunlight (Spacey Jane album)}}
On 6 January 2020, Artists Without A Label, abbreviated as [[AWAL]], announced a global deal signing with Spacey Jane.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|title=Welcoming Australian Indie Band, Spacey Jane|url=https://www.awal.com/blog/welcoming-spacey-jane|access-date=19 May 2020|website=www.awal.com|language=en-us}}</ref> The deal with AWAL offers services to the band such as "global marketing, campaign coordination, sync licensing, and global distribution".<ref name=":5" />

Spacey Jane participated in the first round of [[Isol-Aid]], a stay-at-home festival initiative aiming to assist the Australian Music industry during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Keith|first1=Sarah|title=The State of the music industry|last2=Hughes|first2=Diane|last3=Crowdy|first3=Denis|last4=Morrow|first4=Guy|last5=Evans|first5=Mark|isbn=978-2-36170-087-4|editor1-last=Sarafian|editor1-first=Victor|pages=221–241|chapter=Offline and online : liveness in the Australian music industries|hdl=1959.14/336715|oclc=896253467|editor2-last=Findlay|editor2-first=Rosie|s2cid=65034042}}</ref> The band performed a 20 minute live set through a [[live streaming|livestream]] on Instagram on 21 March 2020.<ref name=":7">Michael, L. (2020). ISOL-AID is on the way | PBA. Retrieved 23 April 2020, from <nowiki>https://probonoaustralia.com.au/news/2020/04/isol-aid-is-on-the-way/</nowiki></ref>

On 12 June 2020, the band released their debut studio album, ''[[Sunlight (Spacey Jane album)|Sunlight]]''.<ref name=":4">{{cite web |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/spacey-jane-skin-single-tour-debut-album-sunlight-interview/11999144 |title=Spacey Jane announce debut album 'Sunlight' with new single 'Skin' |work=[[Triple J]] |date=26 February 2020 |accessdate=8 April 2020 |author=Newstead, Al}}</ref> In January 2021, "[[Booster Seat]]" placed second in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2020]].

==Artistry==
===Musical style and influences===
Spacey Jane cite [[Wilco]], early [[Kings of Leon]], [[Courtney Barnett]], [[the Strokes]] and [[Pixies (band)|Pixies]] as musical influences.<ref name=":1"/>

===Songwriting and lyrical themes===
Frontman and lead songwriter Caleb Harper has stated his inspiration for songwriting comes from "processing emotions and life experiences" and "music as a means of catharsis".<ref name=":1"/> The band as a whole have said that they "aim to make music which resonates with people", and "focus on the sense of connection that music and the lyricality of music brings".<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Coso |first1=Denise |title='Music Connection's' Annual Directory of College/Indie Radio |magazine=Music Connection |volume=38 |issue=10 |date=October 2014 |pages=72–75 |id={{ProQuest|1622632236}} }}</ref>
