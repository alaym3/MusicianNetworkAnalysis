'''Vanessa Mahi''' (born 17 December 1997), known professionally as '''Ivorian Doll''', is an English rapper and Internet personality of [[Côte d'Ivoire|Ivorian]] descent.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|date=12 November 2020|title=Ivorian Doll: From YouTube drama to Queen of Drill|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/newsbeat-54650090|access-date=12 November 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=On The Radar: Ivorian Doll|url=http://www.musicweek.com/radar/read/on-the-radar-ivorian-doll/081623|access-date=12 November 2020|website=www.musicweek.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=YouTube Star Turned Rapper Ivorian Doll Is Making Drill 'Fun and Sexy'|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/g5p7q4/youtube-star-turned-rapper-ivorian-doll-is-making-drill-fun-and-sexy|access-date=12 November 2020|website=www.vice.com|language=en}}</ref> She made her debut in collaboration with Abigail Asante, releasing singles under the name '''Abigail x Ivorian Doll'''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rockson|first=Annie|date=16 April 2019|title=Abigail x Ivorian Doll Release Visuals for 'Spare Me'|url=https://amormagazine.co.uk/abigail-x-ivorian-doll-release-visuals-for-spare-me/|access-date=12 November 2020|website=Amor Magazine|language=en-GB}}</ref> She has since gone on to release several singles as a solo artist, such as "Rumours".<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2021/jan/01/im-not-wearing-tracksuits-im-sexy-ivorian-doll-drills-first-female-star|title='I'm not wearing tracksuits, I'm sexy!' Ivorian Doll, drill's first female star|journal=The Guardian|first=Christine|last=Ochefu|date=1 January 2021|accessdate=18 January 2021}}</ref>

==Early life==
Mahi was born in [[Flensburg]], Germany to parents from the [[Ivory Coast]] and moved to East London when she was three years old with her father.<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.ivoriandoll.com/full-bio|title=Bio|website=Ivorian Doll|accessdate=12 November 2020}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite journal|url= https://www.complex.com/music/2020/07/ivorian-doll-interview|title="I'm Just Trying To Compete With The Boys": An Interview With Ivorian Doll|journal=Complex|first=Minou|last=Itseli|date=31 July 2020|accessdate=12 November 2020}}</ref> Her mother initially stayed in Germany, later joining the family when Mahi was a teenager and her parents reunited.<ref name=":1">{{Cite journal|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/k7ap4x/ivorian-doll-noisey-cover-january-2021|title=Everybody's Talking About Ivorian Doll|journal=Vice|first=Nana|last=Baah|date=25 January 2021|accessdate=5 February 2021}}</ref> Her pseudonym refers to her [[Côte d'Ivoire|Ivorian]] heritage. Mahi's father is a pastor.<ref name=":1" /> He took her to acting classes from the age of 11.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title=Cover story: The gospel according to Ivorian Doll|url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/profiles/ivorian-doll/|access-date=2021-06-29|website=Crack Magazine}}</ref> She attended [[Haggerston School]].<ref name=":3" />

==Career==
Before starting her music career, Mahi was a YouTuber.<ref name=":0" /> Mahi describes herself as having gone into a rap career "accidentally".<ref name=":2" />

After making her musical debut collaborating with Abigail Asante, Mahi began releasing music as a solo performer in late 2019.<ref name=":2" /> Her debut single was "Queen of Drill".<ref name=":2" />

She released her breakout track, "Rumours", in April 2020.<ref name=":1" /> The music video for the track on YouTube reached a million views within a month.<ref name=":3" />

Mahi was nominated for Best Newcomer in the 2020 MOBO Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-24|title=Lianne La Havas, Aitch and Nines lead 2020 MOBO Awards nominations|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/nines-lianne-la-havas-and-aitch-lead-2020-mobo-awards-nominations-2823990|access-date=2021-06-29|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref>

In December 2020, Birmingham rapper [[Lady Leshurr]] released a [[Diss (music)|diss track]] about Mahi, "DIV".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lady Leshurr Smacks Down Ivorian Doll|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/lady-leshurr-smacks-down-ivorian-doll|access-date=2021-06-27|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> Mahi was announced as part of the [[Wireless Festival]] 2021 lineup in April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-28|title=Skepta, Future and Migos to Headline London's Wireless Festival 2021|url=https://hypebeast.com/2021/4/wireless-festival-2021-lineup-announcement-future-skepta-migos|access-date=2021-06-29|website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref> She also performed at [[Reading and Leeds Festivals|Reading Festival 2021]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-08-28|title=Ivorian Doll at Reading Festival 2021: "I want to be a big international artist"|url=https://www.nme.com/features/music-interviews/ivorian-doll-interview-reading-festival-2021-nicki-minaj-drill-courtney-love-tiffany-calver-3031654|access-date=2021-11-08|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref>

In 2021, she collaborated with Birmingham's [[Duran Duran]] on the track "Hammerhead" for their fortieth-anniversary album ''[[Future Past (Duran Duran album)|Future Past]]'', released in October 2021.

== Artistry ==
Mahi has described [[Nicki Minaj]],<ref name=":2" /> [[Lil' Kim]]<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|title=Meet Ivorian Doll, the Reigning Queen of Drill|url=https://www.hungertv.com/editorial/bow-down-meet-ivorian-doll-the-queen-of-drill/|access-date=2021-06-29|website=www.hungertv.com}}</ref> and [[Foxy Brown (rapper)|Foxy Brown]]<ref name=":4" /> as musical influences.
