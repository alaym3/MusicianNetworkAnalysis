'''Kristina Esfandiari''' (born 4 March 1988) is an American singer-songwriter and producer who writes music and performs under the monikers Dalmatian,<ref name=":0">{{cite news |last1=Jones |first1=Cat |title=HEAR DALMATIAN, THE TERRIFYING NEW RAP PROJECT FROM KING WOMAN’S KRIS ESFANDIARI |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/hear-dalmatian-the-new-terrifying-new-rap-project-from-king-womans-kris-esfandiari/ |accessdate=1 February 2020 |publisher=KERRANG}}</ref> Miserable, and NGHTCRWLR,<ref name=":1">{{cite news |last1=Moen |first1=Matt |title=Christian Brainwashing Videos Inspired NGHTCRWLR's Album |url=https://www.papermag.com/nghtcrwlr-album-premiere-2644936143.html |accessdate=1 February 2020 |publisher=Paper Magazine}}</ref> and with the bands King Woman and Sugar High.<ref name=":2">{{cite news |last1=LeSuer |first1=Mike |title=PREMIERE: Sugar High Announce Debut LP "Love Addict," Share Single "Losing" |url=http://floodmagazine.com/75454/premiere-sugar-high-announce-debut-lp-love-addict-share-single-losing/ |publisher=FLOOD}}</ref>

==Early life==
Kristina Esfandiari was born to immigrant parents, an Iranian father and a Serbian mother.<ref name=":1" /> They raised her in a [[Charismatic Christianity|charismatic Christian]] church.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mejia |first1=Paula |title=Metal Singer Kristina Esfandiari on Using Dark Sounds to Heal |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/metal-singer-kristina-esfandiari-on-using-dark-sounds-to-heal-203344/ |website=Rolling Stone |publisher=Penske Business Media |accessdate=2018-07-26}}</ref><ref name="Cvlt Nation">{{cite web |last1=Reveron |first1=Sean |title=CVLT NATION INTERVIEWS KRISTINA ESFANDIARI |url=http://www.cvltnation.com/cvlt-nation-interviews-kristina-esfandiari/ |website=Cvlt Nation |publisher=Cvlt Nation |accessdate=2018-07-26}}</ref> She was raised in Sacramento.<ref>{{Citation|title=Interview with King Woman Episode 9 of Dante & Leanne In the Mourning|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LnUMd4EKdIc|language=en|access-date=2021-09-17}}</ref> At 22, she moved to the [[Bay Area]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lefebvre |first1=Sam |title=King Woman Vanquishes the Holy Spirit |url=https://www.eastbayexpress.com/oakland/king-woman-vanquishes-the-holy-spirit/Content?oid=4216746 |website=East Bay Express |publisher=East Bay Express |accessdate=2018-07-26}}</ref>

== Career ==
Esfandiari began her musical career as a vocalist for shoegaze band [[Whirr (band)|Whirr]], before moving onto her own projects.<ref name=":3" />

In her early years as a performer, Esfandiari became well known for her heavy and dramatic vocals during live shows.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} As of June 2021, Esfandiari releases music under a multitude of personas: Miserable, KRIS, Dalmatian, Sugar High, and NGHTCRWLR.

Esfandiari is best known as the lead vocalist for the band King Woman. Esfandiari began King Woman as a solo project in 2009.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Esfandiari |first1=Kristina |title=A Jonestown Survivor and King Woman's Kristina Esfandiari Discuss Healing from Trauma |url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/7x4axy/a-jonestown-survivor-and-king-womans-kristina-esfandiari-discuss-healing-from-trauma |website=Vice |publisher=Vice Media |accessdate=2018-07-26}}</ref> The project later became a band, after she was joined by her childhood friend, Joey Raygoza.<ref name=":3">{{cite news |last1=Ruskin |first1=Zack |title=Kristina Esfandiari’s path to King Woman was a painful one |url=https://www.sfchronicle.com/music/article/Kristina-Esfandiari-s-path-to-King-Woman-was-a-12788211.php |accessdate=July 26, 2018 |publisher=San Francisco Chronicle |date=March 28, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Cvlt Nation"/>

=== Dalmatian ===
Esfandiari debuted her Dalmatian persona via her social media channels. Dalmatian is a rap project, inspired by artists including Elkk and JRAL.<ref name=":0" /> Dalmatian's first release was single "Pain Thresold", followed by "Friday the 13th", released October 2020.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|date=2020-10-31|title=See King Woman Singer Wield Sword to Slay Monsters in Dalmatian's Eerie New Video|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/see-king-woman-singer-wield-sword-slay-monsters-dalmatians-eerie-new-video|access-date=2021-06-29|website=Revolver|language=en}}</ref> Speaking to ''[[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]]'' in October 2020, Esfandiari said that she was working on Dalmatian's first album, featuring JRAL and Elkk.<ref name=":5" />

=== King Woman ===
King Woman released their first successful EP, ''Doubt'', with [[The Flenser]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://theflenser.com/king-woman-doubt/|title=King Woman – Doubt  {{!}}  The Flenser|website=theflenser.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref> in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/17287-king-woman-burn/|title="Burn" by King Woman Review {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref> The EP was inspired by Esfandiari's experiences of trying to leave her religious upbringing.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Noblé|first=Summer|title=Darkness Disguised As Light: An Interview With King Woman|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/darkness-disguised-as-lig_b_7133918|url-status=live|website=Huffington Post}}</ref>

King Woman signed to [[Relapse Records]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://label.relapse.com/artist/king-woman/|title=King Woman - Relapse Records|website=Relapse Records Official Website}}</ref> and released their debut LP, ''Created in the Image of Suffering'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://clrvynt.com/kristina-esfandiari-feature/|title=How Kristina Esfandiari Balances King Woman and Miserable|website=CLRVYNT|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref> which was labelled by ''Pitchfork'' as one of the best rock albums of 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/1429-the-month-in-metal-the-hopelessness-of-slayer-king-woman-and-shaarimoth/|title=The Month in Metal: The Hopelessness of Slayer, King Woman, and Shaarimoth {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/22902-created-in-the-image-of-suffering/|title=King Woman: Created in the Image of Suffering Album Review {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref> Writing for ''Beats Per Minute''’s “Darkest Albums” feature, John Amen called the album “a disturbing journey through consternation, despair, and heretical inquiry.”<ref>{{Cite web|last=Wohlmacher|first=John|last2=Amen|first2=John|last3=Kenny|first3=Brody|last4=Finlayson|first4=Ray|last5=Dedman|first5=Todd|last6=Smirnov|first6=Aleksandr|last7=Sentz|first7=Tim|last8=Barton|first8=Matthew|last9=WIllems|first9=Jasper|date=2021-05-31|title=BPM’s Darkest Albums {{!}} Beats Per Minute - Page 2|url=https://beatsperminute.com/bpms-darkest-albums-of-all-time/2/|access-date=2021-07-17|website=beatsperminute.com|language=en-US}}</ref> King Woman's second album, ''[[Celestial Blues]]'', was announced in June 2021, as was its July 2021 oncoming release by Relapse Records. The first single from the album, "Morning Star", was released ahead of the LP.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-02|title=King Woman announce new album Celestial Blues, unveil first single "Morning Star": Stream|url=https://consequence.net/2021/06/king-woman-celestial-blues-morning-star/|access-date=2021-06-24|website=Consequence|language=en-US}}</ref> This was followed by a second single, "Psychic Wound",<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-30|title=King Woman – "Psychic Wound"|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2152860/king-woman-psychic-wound/music/|access-date=2021-07-07|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref> and a third single, “Boghz”.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-14|title=King Woman's "Boghz" is Another Stunner|url=https://www.metalsucks.net/2021/07/14/king-womans-boghz-is-another-stunner/|access-date=2021-07-16|website=MetalSucks|language=en}}</ref>
The album was released to positive reviews.<ref>{{Cite web|title=King Woman: Celestial Blues|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/king-woman-celestial-blues/|access-date=2021-08-11|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Album review: King Woman – Celestial Blues|url=https://www.kerrang.com/reviews/album-review-king-woman-celestial-blues/|access-date=2021-08-11|website=Kerrang!}}</ref>

=== Miserable ===
Miserable is a shoegaze project debuted by Esfandiari in 2014 with EPs ''Halloween Dream'' and ''Dog Days''.<ref name=":4" /> ''Uncontrollable'', Miserable's debut album, was released via [[The Native Sound]] in April 2016.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2016/04/miserable-uncontrollable-premiere-stream/|title=Miserable Finds Grace in the Profane on ‘Uncontrollable’ LP|date=April 28, 2016|website=SPIN}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thenativesound.com/artists/miserable|title=The Native Sound|website=The Native Sound|language=en|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.baltimoresun.com/entertainment/music/midnight-sun-blog/bal-miserable-kristina-esfandiari-interview-uncontrollable-story.html|title=Miserable's Kristina Esfandiari on the relief of finishing her debut album|last=Kelley|first=Quinn|work=baltimoresun.com|access-date=2017-09-10|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://miserablegrl.bandcamp.com/|title=Uncontrollable, by Miserable|website=Miserable|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref>  Written and recorded over the course of a year, the LP is the most emotional release in Esfandiari's history as an artist. The album quickly became a fan favourite and her breakthrough record,<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/03/15/miserable-violet-premiere|title=Miserable’s Shoegazey "Violet" Will Destroy Your Heart|work=The FADER|access-date=2017-09-10|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7334020/best-songs-of-the-week-april-15|title=9 Best Songs of the Week: Billboard.com Editors' Picks -- April 15|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref> according to most mainstream media. [[Pitchfork (website)|''Pitchfork'']]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/18122-miserable-violet/|title="Violet" by Miserable Review {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref> and [[Spin (magazine)|''Spin'']]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/6wqy4q/miserable-oven-premiere|title=Miserable's Kristina Esfandiari Makes Death Sound Like a Dream with "Oven"|website=Noisey|language=en-us|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref> praised her work. She later toured extensively in the US.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/miserable-touring-behind-uncontrollable-dates-lp-stream/|title=Miserable touring behind ‘Uncontrollable’ (dates, LP stream)|website=BrooklynVegan|language=en-US|access-date=2017-09-10}}</ref>

=== NIGHTCRWLR ===
NIGHTCRWLR has been described as a "mixture of [[Drone music|drone]], harsh noise and [[Industrial music|industrial]] sounds".<ref name=":1" /> Her influences for the project include [[Yves Tumor]] and [[The Prodigy]].<ref name=":1" /> NIGHTCRWLR's debut album, ''Let the Children Scream'', was released via Amniote Editions in January 2020.<ref name=":1" />

=== Sugar High ===
Sugar High is a collaboration with producer and singer-songwriter Darcy Baylis, who Esfandiari met over [[Instagram]] in 2018.<ref name=":2" /> The duo released their debut album, ''Love Addict'', in 2020 on Dero Arcade.<ref name=":2" /> "Losing" and "Ugly" were released as singles from the album.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-02|title=King Woman Singer's New Project Sugar High: Hear Brooding New Song "Ugly"|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/king-woman-sugar-high-new-song-ugly|access-date=2021-06-29|website=Revolver|language=en}}</ref>
