'''Stand Atlantic''' are an Australian [[pop punk]] band from [[Sydney]], formed in 2012. The band consists of vocalist/guitarist Bonnie Fraser, guitarist David Potter, bassist Miki Rich, and drummer Jonno Panichi.

Stand Atlantic gained prominence in the Australian music scene with the release of their demo [[Extended play|EPs]], ''Catalyst'' (2013) and ''A Place Apart'' (2015).<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.killyourstereo.com/reviews/1053511/stand-atlantic-a-place-apart|title=Stand Atlantic - A Place Apart {{!}} Reviews {{!}} Killyourstereo.com|work=Killyourstereo.com|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> The band signed with [[Rude Records]] in 2017 and released the single "Coffee at Midnight", which landed them positive reviews.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.hysteriamag.com/stand-atlantic/|title=STAND ATLANTIC // Sign to Rude Records Over 'Coffee at Midnight'|date=29 June 2017|work=Hysteria Magazine|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/features/read/watch-the-new-video-from-one-of-australias-most-exciting-new-bands|title=Watch The New Video From One Of Australia's Most Exciting New Bands – Features|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> They released an EP, ''Sidewinder'', in September 2017. Their debut album, ''Skinny Dipping'', was released in October 2018. Their second album, ''Pink Elephant'', was released on 7 August 2020.

==History==

===Formation and early EPs (2012–2017)===
In 2012, friends Bonnie Fraser (lead vocals/rhythm guitar) and Arthur Ng (lead guitar), joined by David Potter (bass) and Jordan Jansons (drums) formed a band under the name What It's Worth.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.emergenza.net/default.aspx?pag=425&nav=AU&lng=en-au&cnt=18854|title=What It's Worth is bound to make it worth your while … seen live or simply heard on the radio.|date=1 July 2013|website=emergenza.net|access-date=3 April 2020}}</ref> The four-piece pop punk band began recording material, and released their debut single, "Bulletproof Vest" in December 2012. In May 2013, they released their debut demo EP, ''Catalyst'' via physical and online platforms, followed by the release of their next single, "Romeo". It was accompanied by two national tours of Australia in support of their debut EP.

In early 2014, the group changed their name from What It's Worth to Stand Atlantic.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://killyourstereo.com/interviews/1026802/stand-atlantic/|title=Stand Atlantic|date=14 June 2014|work=Singular Music|access-date=3 April 2020}}</ref> The band released their first single under the new name, "Breakaway", featuring the band's new sound, in March 2014.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140203085926/https://singularmusic.net/2014/02/03/stand-atlantic-formerly-what-its-worth/|title=Stand Atlantic (formerly What it’s Worth) prepare to launch new single|date=3 February 2014|work=Singular Music|access-date=3 April 2020}}</ref> It was accompanied by a fan-made lyric video.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140909195829/https://singularmusic.net/2014/06/09/stand-atlantic-breakaway-fan-lyric-video/|title=Stand Atlantic – ‘BREAKAWAY’ fan lyric video|date=9 June 2014|work=Singular Music|access-date=3 April 2020}}</ref>

Stand Atlantic returned to the studio during November 2014, working on their next demo EP, ''A Place Apart''. During this time Jansons left the band and was replaced by Ethan Mestroni. The first single, "Wasteland", was released in April 2015 with the full EP later the same month.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.killyourstereo.com/news/1053351/stand-atlantic-drop-new-single|title=Stand Atlantic drop new single {{!}} News {{!}} Killyourstereo.com|work=Killyourstereo.com|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> The EP was recorded by Rohan Kumar and mixed by Dave Petrovic.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://nagasakisound.com/stand-atlantic-wasteland-video/|title=STAND ATLANTIC Take Us To Their "Wasteland" - Nagasaki Sound|website=nagasakisound.com|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> The band toured with As It Is,<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bluntmag.com.au/interviews/as-it-is-the-british-are-coming/|title=As It Is: The British Are Coming {{!}} Blunt Mag|date=23 September 2015|work=Blunt Mag – Alternative Music News, Reviews, and Interviews|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> State Champs<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://singularmusic.net/2015/06/17/stand-atlantic-supporting-state-champs-usa/|title=Stand Atlantic supporting State Champs (USA)|date=17 June 2015|work=Singular Music|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> and Young Lions during 2015.

In 2016, the band toured with [[With Confidence]] before joining [[Cute Is What We Aim For]] on their tenth anniversary tour of ''[[The Same Old Blood Rush with a New Touch]]'' album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/cute_is_what_we_aim_for_announce_same_old_blood_rush_10_year_anniversary_to|title=Cute Is What We Aim For announce ‘Same Old Blood Rush’ 10-year anniversary tour - News - Alternative Press|website=Alternative Press|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> It was also announced that the band were to play at Bondi-Blitz.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bluntmag.com.au/tour-news/stories-polaris-lead-epic-and-free-bondi-blitz-2016-lineup/|title=Stories & Polaris Lead Epic (And Free!) Bondi Blitz 2016 Lineup {{!}} Blunt Mag|date=22 February 2016|work=Blunt Mag – Alternative Music News, Reviews, and Interviews|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> They released a cover of [[Ariana Grande]]'s "[[Break Free (song)|Break Free]]" on Ghost Killer Entertainment in June.<ref>{{Citation|last=Ghost Killer Entertainment|title=Ariana Grande - Break Free [Band: Stand Atlantic] Punk Goes Pop Style Cover (Alternative Rock)|date=7 June 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4Bg3RSUdeQQ|accessdate=14 November 2017}}</ref> In September 2016, the band announced via a Facebook post, that Ng had chosen to leave the band. The post also included news that they were working on new music with Stevie Knight.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://amnplify.com.au/portfolio-items/as-it-is-undercast-between-you-and-me-no-not-the-bees-stand-atlantic-the-evelyn-hotel-100217/|title=As It Is + Undercast + Between You And Me + No! Not The Bees + Stand Atlantic @ The Evelyn Hotel 10/02/17|work=Amnplify|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref>

On 21 June 2017, Stand Atlantic announced they had signed to [[Rude Records]], becoming the first Australian band to do so.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://music.theaureview.com/interviews/interview-sydneys-stand-atlantic-on-signing-to-rude-records-and-new-ep-sidewinder/|title=Interview: Sydney’s Stand Atlantic on signing to Rude Records and new EP, Sidewinder – the AU review|website=the AU review|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> The band announced later that day that their first single from the new EP, ''Coffee at Midnight'', would be premiered on [[Triple J|Triple J's]] ''[[short.fast.loud]]'' that night.<ref>{{Citation|title=Short.Fast.Loud|date=24 August 2017|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/programs/short-fast-loud/short.fast.loud/8838308|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|accessdate=14 November 2017}}</ref>

Stand Atlantic announced the ''Sidewinder'' EP the following week, with a release date set for 15 September 2017. On 23 July 2017, Stand Atlantic released the second single, "Mess I Made",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/listen-to-the-new-song-from-stand-atlantic|title=Listen To The New Song From Stand Atlantic – News|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> through ''Hysteria Mag''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.hysteriamag.com/stand-atlantic-premiere-second-single-mess-made/|title=STAND ATLANTIC // PREMIERE—Second Single 'Mess I Made'|last=Simmons|first=Jonty|date=23 July 2017|work=Hysteria Magazine|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref>

On 27 July 2017, they announced they would be supporting [[New Found Glory]] on their 20 Years of Pop/Punk tour, playing at sold-out shows across Australia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/stand-atlantic-have-been-announced-for-an-awesome-tour|title=Stand Atlantic Have Been Announced For An Awesome Tour - News - Rock Sound Magazine|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref>

The band released one more single before the release of the EP, the title track "Sidewinder" in August.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/entry/stand_atlantic_sidewinder_music_video|title=Pop rockers Stand Atlantic debut heartfelt music video for new song, "Sidewinder"—watch - Features - Alternative Press|website=Alternative Press|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> Stand Atlantic were featured in the September issue of ''[[Rock Sound]]'' magazine.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/new-magazine-neck-deep-open-up-about-the-peace-and-the-panic|title=NEW MAGAZINE: Neck Deep Open Up About 'The Peace And The Panic' - News - Rock Sound Magazine|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref>

The EP peaked at number 31 on the iTunes charts during release day.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/aus/artists/music/stand-atlantic/|title=iTunesCharts.net: Stand Atlantic (Australian iTunes Music Chart Performance)|website=www.itunescharts.net|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> In October 2017, Stand Atlantic announced they would be supporting [[Roam (band)|ROAM]] on their European and UK tours,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/roam-have-announced-a-brand-new-tour...-and-theyre-bringing-stand-atlantic|title=ROAM Have Announced A Brand New Tour... And They're Bringing Stand Atlantic With Them - News - Rock Sound Magazine|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.punktastic.com/news/wstr-and-stand-atlantic-to-support-roam-on-uk-tour/|title=WSTR and Stand Atlantic to support Roam on UK tour|website=Punktastic|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> starting in November. It was announced in November that Stand Atlantic would be supporting [[Knuckle Puck]] on their Australian tour in January 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.destroyalllines.com/tour/knuckle-puck/|title=KNUCKLE PUCK {{!}} Destroy All Lines|website=www.destroyalllines.com|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> Stand Atlantic's EP, ''Sidewinder'', made 10th place on Rock Sound's Top 50 Albums of 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/features/read/the-rock-sound-top-50-releases-of-the-year-10-1|title=Meet The Winners Of The First Ever Rock Sound Awards! - News - Rock Sound Magazine|last=|first=|date=|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=21 December 2017}}</ref>

Stand Atlantic are featured in ''[[Kerrang!]]''{{'}}s hottest bands of 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kerrang.com/the-news/k-1706-knocked-loose-and-the-hottest-bands-of-2018/|title=K!1706: Knocked Loose And The Hottest Bands Of 2018 — Kerrang!|website=Kerrang!|access-date=23 January 2018}}</ref>

It was announced late February that Stand Atlantic would be playing [[Slam dunk Festival|Slam Dunk Festival]] later in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/meet-the-bands-playing-the-rock-sound-breakout-stage-at-slam-dunk-fest|title=Meet The Bands Playing The Rock Sound Breakout Stage At Slam Dunk Festival 2018 - News - Rock Sound Magazine|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=28 February 2018}}</ref>

===Debut album ''Skinny Dipping'' (2018–2019)===
On 4 September 2018, Stand Atlantic announced their single "Lavender Bones" would premiere on [[Triple J]] later that night.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/standatlantic/status/1036766088648908800|title=stand atlantic on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=4 September 2018}}</ref> A debut record was teased and Triple J appeared to post the album's name, ''Skinny Dipping''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/villanvlle/status/1036893568357597184|title=lau on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=4 September 2018}}</ref> Stand Atlantic announced that they had signed to [[Hopeless Records]] on 5 September 2018 and that their debut album, ''Skinny Dipping'', would be released in October 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hopelessrecords.com/stand-atlantic|title=Stand Atlantic|website=Hopeless Records|access-date=4 September 2018}}</ref> On 2 October Stand Atlantic released "Lost My Cool", the second single from their debut album.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://dontboreus.thebrag.com/stand-atlantic-release-lost-my-cool/|title=Stand Atlantic release absolute beast of a song 'Lost My Cool'|date=2 October 2018|work=Don't Bore Us|access-date=2 October 2018}}</ref> Stand Atlantic released "Skinny Dipping", the title track and third single on 23 October. It premiered on [[BBC Radio 1]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/hopelessrecords/status/1054492595613827077|title=Hopeless Records on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=23 October 2018}}</ref> On 5 July 2019, Stand Atlantic officially announced Miki Rich as a permanent member of the band, after he had been touring with them since 2017.

===''Pink Elephant'' (2019–present)===
During Stand Atlantic's UK/EU tour in August 2019, through new merchandise, they announced a single that would be coming out in September titled "Hate Me (Sometimes)". They announced the single would debut on Australian radio station [[Triple J]] on 16 September.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B2aCOVIAa5o/|title=Stand Atlantic on Instagram: "ok hello we have a new song premiering on @triplej monday sep 16th at 6pm beep beep xx"|via=Instagram|access-date=16 September 2019}}</ref> It was officially released on 17 September, accompanied by a music video.<ref>{{cite web|title=Stand Atlantic - Hate Me (Sometimes) [Official Music Video]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9fZSD3tNv0|via=YouTube|access-date=17 September 2019}}</ref> Stand Atlantic announced on 24 October that they had started work on their second album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/it-looks-like-stand-atlantic-have-started-work-on-album-two|title=It Looks Like Stand Atlantic Have Started Work On Album Two – News|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=28 October 2019}}</ref> On 11 February 2020 Stand Atlantic premiered the single "Shh!" on Triple J. They followed this with the single "Drink to Drown" on 2 April 2020, accompanied by a video featuring their fans from over the world. On 13 May 2020 Stand Atlantic unveiled the single "Wavelength".<ref>{{Citation|title="We Wanted to Push Boundaries": Stand Atlantic Release New Song, Wavelength|url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/we-wanted-to-push-boundaries-stand-atlantic-release-new-song-wavelength/|access-date=22 May 2020}}</ref>

On 25 June 2020, Stand Atlantic released the single "Jurassic Park", accompanied by a music video. It was also announced that their second full-length album, ''Pink Elephant'', would release on 7 August 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Stand Atlantic Have Released A New Single AND Announced Their New Album – News|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/stand-atlantic-have-announced-the-details-of-their-new-album|access-date=24 June 2020|website=Rock Sound Magazine}}</ref> ''Pink Elephant'' debuted at number 23 on the [[ARIA Albums Chart]].<ref name="ARIA"/>

On April 29, 2021, the band released "Deathwish", a track which featured [[nothing,nowhere.]], along with an accompanying music video.<ref>{{Cite web|title=STAND ATLANTIC AND NOTHING,NOWHERE. DEBUT HYPNOTIZING "DEATHWISH"—WATCH|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/stand-atlantic-nothing-nowhere-deathwish/|access-date=6 Nov 2021|website=Alternative Press Magazine}}</ref> On November 4 they released "Molotov (OK)", with an accompanying music video. Fraser said of the song's themes that "I went to a Christian school for 3 years of my life and when a pastor says ‘all gays will burn in hell’ during an assembly, you’re gonna remember it, and you’re gonna write a song about it."<ref>{{Cite web|title=STAND ATLANTIC HAVE RELEASED A BRILLIANTLY CORROSIVE NEW TRACK ‘MOLOTOV [OK]’|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/stand-atlantic-have-released-a-corrosive-new-track-molotov-ok|access-date=6 Nov 2021|website=Rock Sound Magazine}}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
Will Cross of ''[[Rock Sound]]'', reviewing Stand Atlantic's EP ''Sidewinder'', wrote: "the trio have created an EP that's assured, accessible and properly brilliant."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/reviews/read/stand-atlantic-sidewinder|title=Stand Atlantic - 'Sidewinder' - Reviews - Rock Sound Magazine|website=Rock Sound Magazine|access-date=14 November 2017}}</ref> Caitlin Olsen of {{proper name|amnplify}}, reviewing the same EP, wrote: "the EP throws back to mid-2000s pop punk with a contemporary alternative twist. It's tight, it's soulful, and it's loaded with grit."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://amnplify.com.au/portfolio-items/stand-atlantic-sidewinder-ep-review-150917/|title=STAND ATLANTIC – "Sidewinder" (EP Review) - 15/09/17|work=Amnplify|access-date=8 November 2017}}</ref>

The band has stated influences from bands and artists such as [[Blink-182]], [[The Story So Far (band)|The Story So Far]], [[Justin Bieber]], [[Silverchair]], [[The 1975]], and [[Moose Blood]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://amnplify.com.au/portfolio-items/interview-with-bonnie-fraser-vocalist-from-stand-atlantic/|title=Interview with BONNIE FRASER - vocalist from STAND ATLANTIC|work=Amnplify|accessdate=8 November 2017}}</ref>
