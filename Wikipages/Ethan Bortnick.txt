'''Ethan Jordan Bortnick''' (born December 24, 2000)<ref name="IMDB">{{cite web|url=https://pro.imdb.com/name/nm2645125|title=Ethan Bortnick – IMDbPro|publisher=IMDb}}</ref> is an American pianist, singer, songwriter, record producer and musician. At three years old, he discovered a set of music skills, including [[perfect pitch]], on a toy keyboard that eventually led him to appearances on ''The Tonight Show'', ''Oprah'', Disney, Nickelodeon, and his award-winning concert specials on PBS. Ethan has had over 4 national television concert specials, airing at record amounts.<ref name="Simple History Ethan">{{cite web|url=https://jammerzine.com/first-listen-ethan-bortnick-leave-me-alone/ |title=First Listen: Ethan Bortnick – Leave Me Alone}}</ref> On October 3, 2010 (age 9), Bortnick was listed in the [[Guinness World Records]] for "The World's Youngest Solo Musician to Headline His Own Concert Tour"''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/youngest-musician-to-headline-a-solo-concert-tour|title=Youngest Musician to Headline a Solo Concert Tour|work=Guinness World Records|access-date=December 24, 2017}}</ref>''

Bortnick has been featured on national and international television programs.<ref name="oprah">{{cite web|url=http://www.oprah.com/slideshow/oprahshow/20080512_tows_smartestkids|title=Oprah.com – World's Smartest Kids}}</ref> He has helped raise over $50 million for charities.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ethanbortnick.com/bio|title=BIO|website=ethanbortnick.com|access-date=December 24, 2017}}</ref>  In 2011, AEG Network LIVE's broadcast of Ethan Bortnick's Musical Time Machine received two bronze Telly awards and Bortnick received three bronze Telly Awards in 2014 for The Power of Music.<ref name="Bronze Telly Award #1">{{cite web|url=https://www.tellyawards.com/winners/2011/peoples-telly/tv-programs-segments-or-promotional-pieces-tv-programssegments/ethan-bortnicks-time-machine/145137|title=tellyawards.com – Telly Awards}}</ref><ref name="Bronze Telly Award #2">{{cite web|url=https://www.tellyawards.com/winners/2014/filmvideo/tv-programs-segments-or-promotional-pieces-use-of-music/the-power-of-music/119272|title=tellyawards.com – Telly Awards}}</ref><ref name="Bronze Telly Award #3">{{cite web|url=https://www.tellyawards.com/winners/2014/filmvideo/tv-programs-segments-or-promotional-pieces-live-events/the-power-of-music/119271|title=tellyawards.com – Telly Awards}}</ref><ref name="Bronze Telly Award #4">{{cite web|url=https://www.tellyawards.com/winners/2014/peoples-telly/tv-programs-segments-or-promotional-pieces-tv-programssegments/the-power-of-music/119273|title=tellyawards.com – Telly Awards}}</ref><ref name="Bronze Telly Award #5">{{cite web|url=https://www.tellyawards.com/?s=ethan+bortnick|title=tellyawards.com – Telly Awards}}</ref>

Bortnick has headlined over 1,000 concerts across the globe.<ref name="Simple History Ethan"/>

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Bortnick began offering daily live performances on YouTube.<ref name="YouTube Channel">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/c/EthanBortnick/live |title=Live- Music Hangs with eTHAn}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Bortnick was born in [[Pembroke Pines, Florida]]. His parents, Hannah and Gene Bortnick, are Jewish immigrants from [[Ukraine]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://princetoninfo.com/|title=Princeton Info &#124; Princeton's Entertainment and Business Newspaper}}</ref> He attended a Montessori pre-school.<ref name="Lower Bucks Times">{{cite web|url=https://lowerbuckstimes.com/2017/03/20/the-kids-grown-up/|title=The kid's grown up - By A.D. Amorosi|website=Lower Bucks Times}}</ref>

==Career==

===PBS specials===
At 9 years old, Bortnick became the youngest artist to have his own National PBS Concert Special ''The Power of Music''.<ref name="usatoday.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/music/news/2010-08-02-ethanbortnick02_ST_N.htm|title=Piano prodigy Ethan Bortnick tickles his fans |website=USA Today}}</ref> ''The Power of Music'' was rated the number-one concert pledge show on public television for 2014–2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://fortitudepr.com/ethan-bortnick-1/|title=Ethan Bortnick|website=Fortitude PR|access-date=December 26, 2017}}</ref>&nbsp;

In 2017, Bortnick presented the third National PBS television concert "Generations of Music," with a guest appearance by [[Peter Yarrow]] (of [[Peter, Paul and Mary]]), Bethany Yarrow and [[Rufus Cappadocia]] and guided by musical director [[David Rosenthal (musician)|David Rosenthal]], and directed by Leon Knoles.<ref>16-year-old-musician-ethan-bortnick-presents-third-national-pbs-television-concert-generations-of-music-supporting-critical-funding-for-pbs-stations-and-the-arts-300499050.html</ref>

Bortnick started his own nationwide talent search called "Celebration of Music" in 2017. The show travels to various cities across the United States and allows contestants between the ages of 4 to 24 a chance to compete to win the opportunity to perform in a national television show.<ref name="WLTV.org">{{cite web |url=https://www.wlvt.org/news/celebration-of-music/#// |title=TV listing |website=wlvt.org|access-date=September 12, 2019}}</ref>

===Concert in Las Vegas===
On July 22 and 23, 2011, at the [[Las Vegas Hilton]] Bortnick became the youngest headliner ever in Las Vegas, at ten years of age.<ref name="lasvegassun.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.lasvegassun.com/news/2011/jul/24/photos-10-year-old-ethan-bortnick-headlines-las-ve/|title=Photos: 10-year-old Ethan Bortnick headlines at Las Vegas Hilton|date=July 24, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ktnv.com/news/actionnewsmidday/125830408.html|title=10-year-old Ethan Bortnick Headlining Show at Las Vegas Hilton – www.ktnv.com|access-date=August 3, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927185434/http://www.ktnv.com/news/actionnewsmidday/125830408.html|archive-date=September 27, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Recorded album===
In August 2011, Bortnick recorded his first album in [[Muscle Shoals, Alabama]]. The album was produced by [[Gary Baker (songwriter)|Gary Baker]], and his collaborators Matthew Craig and Rob Collier. The album is the soundtrack to the film ''[[Anything Is Possible (film)|Anything is Possible]]'', in which Bortnick plays the leading role.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.visionfilms.net/Details.aspx?id=28973a45-4089-e311-8492-d4ae527c3b65|title=Vision Films - Anything is Possible|website=visionfilms.net}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XLxB713o3Tc|title=MovieCentral - Anything is Possible - under license from Vision Films Inc.|website=youtube.com}}</ref>

===''Melody Street''===
Bortnick was the host of ''Melody Street'', a 22-minute musical-variety-show for preschoolers. Bortnick and his five best friends, a band of animated musical instruments; Val Violin, Febe Flute, Timmy Trumpet, Heidi Horn and Sammy Snare, entertained kids with humor, songs, skits, interactive games, backstage dramas, and celebrity musical guests.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://renderosity.com/melody-street-creates-broadcast-magic-for-kids-with-studiogpu-machstudio-pro-cms-15172|title=Melody Street Creates Broadcast Magic for Kids with StudioGPU | website=Renderosity.com}}</ref>
