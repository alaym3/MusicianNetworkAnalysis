'''Vincint Cannady''' (born 1991), known by the [[mononym]] '''Vincint''' (stylized as '''VINCINT'''), is an American [[Singing|singer]] and [[songwriter]]. He is best known as a finalist on the [[The Four: Battle for Stardom (season 1)|first season]] of the singing competition ''[[The Four: Battle for Stardom|The Four]].'' He released his debut EP, ''The Feeling'' on February 14, 2020. Cannady later released his debut studio album, ''There Will Be Tears'' on June 11, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dixon|first=Denton Davidson,Marcus James|last2=Davidson|first2=Denton|last3=Dixon|first3=Marcus James|date=2018-02-09|title=‘The Four’ finale recap: Did Evvie McKinney, Vincint Cannady, Candice Boyd or Zhavia win Season 1?|url=https://www.goldderby.com/article/2018/the-four-finale-recap-evvie-mckinney-vincint-cannady-candice-boyd-zhavia-win-season-1-news/|access-date=2021-06-11|website=GoldDerby|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life and education  ==
Vincint Cannady was born and raised in [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania]]. His father was a gospel singer in a group called the Christ United Gospel Singers, and Vincint began singing at the age of five.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Parker|first=Lynsey|title='Queer Eye' singer Vincint on smashing stereotypes and coming out at 'probably age 5 or 6'|url=https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/queer-eye-singer-vincint-on-smashing-stereotypes-and-coming-out-at-probably-age-5-or-6-074015486.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-02-28|website=www.yahoo.com|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title=Vincint Sang In Secret, Then In Choir — Now He's Belting Out The Feeling|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3156631/vincint-the-feeling-interview/|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|website=MTV News|language=en|access-date=2020-05-05}}</ref> He was shy about singing as a child, but his father took him to audition for an all-boys choir audition when he was seven to nurture his interest. Vincint was raised [[Baptists|Baptist]] but attended Catholic school growing up.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Pop Newcomer VINCINT Shares Spiritual 'Marrow' Video, Talks Going To Catholic School & Performing on 'The Four' Finale|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8098692/vincint-marrow-video-interview|access-date=2019-05-07|website=Billboard}}</ref> Vincint wrote his first song when he was twelve.<ref name=":3" /> He named [[Beyoncé]], [[Whitney Houston]], [[Céline Dion]], [[Madonna]], [[Britney Spears]], [[Björk|Bjork]], and [[Regina Spektor]] as early inspirations.<ref name=":1" />

He attended college at [[Berklee College of Music]] and graduated in 2013.<ref name="mtv-2020">{{cite web |last1=Head |first1=Daniel |title=Pop Vincint Sang In Secret, Then In Choir — Now He’s Belting Out The Feeling |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3156631/vincint-the-feeling-interview/ |website=MTV |access-date=2 July 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2018–2020: ''The Four'' & ''The Feeling'' ===
Vincint appeared on the first season of ''The Four'' one week before the finale, where he received unanimous "yes" votes from every judge after performing "[[Creep (Radiohead song)|Creep]]" by [[Radiohead]].<ref name="thefour">{{Cite web|last=Swift|first=Andy|date=2018-02-09|title=The Four Finale: And the Winner Is...|url=https://tvline.com/2018/02/08/the-four-finale-evvie-mckinney-wins-season-1-winner/|access-date=2019-05-07|website=TVLine|language=en}}</ref> He stated in an interview that producers attempted to persuade him to sing [[Gospel music|gospel]] and [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]] music due to his race, and some male producers refused to work with him.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammy-museum/news/vincint-brandon-stansell-linda-perry-more-lgbtq-artists-share-their-journeys-self|title=LGBTQ+ Artists Share Stories At The GRAMMY Museum|date=2019-02-06|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en|access-date=2019-05-07}}</ref> Vincint was eliminated in the first round of the finale competition along with contestant [[Zhavia Ward]].<ref name="thefour" />

He released three singles in 2018: "''Remember Me''", "''Mine''", and "''Marrow''", which was his debut single.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last=Moran|first=Justin|date=2018-06-01|title=Say That You'll Remember VINCINT|url=https://www.papermag.com/vincint-remember-me-2574289795.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-17|website=PAPER|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":2" /><ref name=":0" /> He released a music video for "Marrow" on February 8, 2018.<ref name=":0" /> The video was directed by Jake Wilson and shot in a church in North Hollywood, in an homage to Cannady's upbringing in Catholic school.<ref name=":0" /> The latter two singles were produced by [[Pretty Sister]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|date=2018-11-21|title=VINCINT Just Might Be Our Generation's Greatest Vocalist|url=http://www.papermag.com/vincint-mine-2621125081.html?rebelltitem=2|access-date=2019-05-07|website=PAPER|language=en}}</ref> His singing style ranges from [[Belting (music)|belting]] to whispery [[falsetto]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.out.com/popnography/2018/11/20/vincint-mine-single-ex-the-four|title=VINCINT Wrote a Song With Everything You're Too Scared to Tell Your Ex|date=2018-11-20|website=www.out.com|language=en|access-date=2019-05-07}}</ref> He characterizes his genre of music as [[Pop music|pop]].<ref name=":3" />

On April 5, 2019, Cannady premiered a music video for a single called, "''Please Don't Fall in Love''", directed by [[Jasper Soloff]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.papermag.com/vincint-please-dont-fall-in-love-video-2633792074.html?rebelltitem=8|title=This Is the 'Most Selfish' Song VINCINT's Ever Written|date=2019-04-05|website=PAPER|language=en|access-date=2019-05-07}}</ref> He released his debut EP, ''The Feeling'', on February 14, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-02-14|title=Happy V-Day From VINCINT|url=https://www.papermag.com/vincint-the-feeling-2645130641.html|access-date=2021-06-11|website=PAPER|language=en}}</ref> Vincint's song, "''Be Me''", ''w''as selected as the anthem of the fifth season of ''[[Queer Eye (2018 TV series)|Queer Eye]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Arnold|first=Chuck|date=2020-06-04|title=New ‘Queer Eye’ song singer Vincint has made over straight pals, too|url=https://nypost.com/2020/06/04/new-queer-eye-theme-song-singer-vincint-made-over-straight-pals-too/|access-date=2021-06-11|website=New York Post|language=en-US}}</ref> He also released a lead single for his debut album, called "''Hard 2 Forget''".

=== 2021–present: ''There Will Be Tears'' & ''The Getaway Tour'' ===
He released his debut album ''There Will Be Tears'' on June 11, 2021. [[Ari Shapiro]] of ''[[All Things Considered]]'' referred to the album as "vulnerable and danceable."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Yohannes|first=Alamin|date=2021-06-08|title=Vincint on his love of pop, making people dance, and debut album 'There Will Be Tears'|url=https://ew.com/music/vincint-there-will-be-tears-interview/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-09|website=EW.com|language=en}}</ref> ''The Getaway Tour'' is Cannady's debut concert tour, in support of his debut studio album, ''There Will Be Tears''. It will ran from January 12, 2022 to January 30, 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=VINCINT to Headline the Fonda Theatre|url=https://thescenestar.typepad.com/ss/2021/11/vincint-to-headline-the-fonda-theatre.html|access-date=2021-11-23|website=The Scenestar}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Cannady is openly gay, and officially came out when he was sixteen.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Arnold|first=Chuck|date=2020-06-04|title=New ‘Queer Eye’ song singer Vincint has made over straight pals, too|url=https://nypost.com/2020/06/04/new-queer-eye-theme-song-singer-vincint-made-over-straight-pals-too/|access-date=2021-02-28|website=New York Post|language=en-US}}</ref>
