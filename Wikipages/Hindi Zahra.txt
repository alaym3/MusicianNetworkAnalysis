'''Hindi Zahra''' ([[Tamazight]]: ⵀⵉⵏⴷⵉ ⵣⴰⵀⵕⴰ, [[Arabic]]: هندي زهرة, born 20 January 1979<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.djazairess.com/fr/lemidi/1504270801|title=La douceur de l'Orient aux inflexions des touaregs et de la langue berbère: Homeland, le nouvel album de Hindi Zahra|last=Ammour|first=Idi|date=27 April 2015|publisher=djazairess.com|location=Algiers|language=French|access-date=28 April 2015}}</ref> in [[Khouribga]], Morocco) is a Moroccan singer<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.efi-news.com/2011/09/hindi-zahra-music-for-soul.html |title=Hindi Zahra - Music for the Soul |publisher=EFI-News.com |date=21 September 2011 |access-date=25 October 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111213041747/http://www.efi-news.com/2011/09/hindi-zahra-music-for-soul.html |archivedate=13 December 2011}}</ref> and actress. When coming up with a stage name, she simply inverted her birth name.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thedailynewsegypt.com/music/hindi-zahra-if-music-be-a-tree.html |title=Hindi Zahra: If music be a tree |publisher=Thedailynewsegypt.com |date=24 June 2010 |access-date=1 May 2011}}</ref> Her songs are mostly in English but some lyrics as in the song "Imik Si Mik" are in the [[Berber language]].

Influenced by singers like [[Cheikha Rimitti]],<ref name="Hindi Zahra biography">{{cite web |url=http://www.hindi-zahra.com/en/biographie.php |title=Hindi Zahra biography |publisher=Hindi-zahra.com |date=23 March 2011 |access-date=1 May 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110711170357/http://www.hindi-zahra.com/en/biographie.php |archivedate=11 July 2011}}</ref> Hindi Zahra has drawn comparisons with [[Beth Gibbons]] of [[Portishead (band)|Portishead]], [[Billie Holiday]], [[Patti Smith]], and [[Norah Jones]].

==Career==
Zahra was raised by her Moroccan mother, a dancer and actress, in [[Khouribga]], Morocco.  At the age of fifteen, she left school and moved to Paris to live with her father, who had been a soldier.  At age 18 she worked at the [[Louvre]].<ref name="Hindi Zahra biography"/>

Zahra wrote her first lyrics and melodies. She is a self-taught [[multi-instrumentalist]]. By 2005 she had written about 50 songs of which ''Beautiful Tango'', ''Oursoul'', ''Try'', and ''Stand Up'' were first released on the EP ''Hindi Zahra'' in 2009 and eleven songs were recorded on Hindi Zahra's first album which was released in January 2010 at the Jazz label [[Blue Note Records]]. The video to the opening song ''Beautiful Tango'' was made by French director [[Tony Gatlif]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leparisien.fr/flash-actualite-culture/hindi-zahra-une-voix-nomade-entre-orient-et-occident-22-05-2010-932684.php |title=Hindi Zahra, une voix nomade entre Orient et Occident |work=Le Parisien|date=22 May 2010 |access-date=1 May 2011}}</ref> The song ''Stand Up'' was chosen for a commercial campaign by [[Western Union]]. In June 2010 she collaborated with French musician Blundetto on his debut album ''Bad Bad Things''. In November 2010, Hindi Zahra won the [[Prix Constantin]] for Best Album.<ref>{{cite web|author=Par PHILIPPE BROCHEN|url=http://next.liberation.fr/musique/01012301895-prix-constantin-qui-va-succeder-a-emily-loiseau |title=Hindi Zahra, prix Constantin 2010 |publisher=Next.liberation.fr |access-date=1 May 2011}}</ref> In February 2011, she won the [[Victoires de la Musique]] award for the best [[World music]] album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.transparent.com/french/les-victoires-de-la-musique-de-2011-the-2011-musics-victories/ |title=Les Victoires de la musique de 2011 (The 2011 Music’s Victories) |publisher=Transparent.com |date=11 February 2011 |access-date=1 May 2011}}</ref>

In 2014 she had roles in the films ''[[The Narrow Frame of Midnight]]'' by [[Tala Hadid]] and in [[The Cut (2014 drama film)|The Cut]] by [[Fatih Akin]].

In April 2015 her second studio album Homeland was released.
