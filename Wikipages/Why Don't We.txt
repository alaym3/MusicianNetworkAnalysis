'''Why Don't We''', shortened to '''WDW''', are an American [[boy band]] consisting of Jonah Marais, Corbyn Besson, Daniel Seavey, Jack Avery, and Zach Herron. They were formed in 2016 and have released two [[studio album]]s and six [[Extended play|extended plays]].

==History==
===2016–17: Career beginnings===
The band originally formed on September 27, 2016,<ref name=first-day/> after having all were forced to meet in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]], a year prior.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Falcone|first=Dana Rose|date=2017-12-15|title=Why Don't We: What to Know About the Hot New Boy Band|url=https://people.com/music/why-dont-we-band-people-explains/|website=PEOPLE}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Booth|first=Ali|date=2018-08-16|title=Why Don’t We Shares the Story Behind Their Decision to Form a Band|url=https://tigerbeat.com/video/why-dont-we-band/|website=Tiger Beat}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Locke|first=Ashely|date=2016-10-26|title=Boy Band Why Don't We Shares the Story Behind How They Formed Their Group|url=https://www.sweetyhigh.com/read/why-dont-we-interview-music-102616|website=Sweety High}}</ref> On October 20, 2016, the group released their debut single "Taking You", a track from their debut EP, ''Only the Beginning'', which was released on November 25 of the same year.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Singh|first=Olivia|date=December 4, 2016|title=Why Don't We Drops 'Only the Beginning' ;) EP|url=https://tigerbeat.com/2016/12/why-dont-we-only-the-beginning-ep/|website=TigerBeat}}</ref> They embarked on their first headlining tour, the "Taking You Tour", the next year. Their second EP, ''Something Different'', was released on April 21, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Campbell|first=Emily|date=2017-04-21|title=Why Don't We Releases "Something Different" EP|url=https://onairwithryan.iheart.com/featured/ryan-seacrest/content/2017-04-21-why-dont-we-releases-something-different-ep/|website=On Air with Ryan Seacrest}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Lee|first=Julia|date=April 21, 2017|title=Why Don't We Drops New 'Something Different' EP|url=https://tigerbeat.com/2017/04/why-dont-we-ep-something-different/|website=TigerBeat}}</ref> Following the release of their second EP, they embarked on the Something Different Tour, their second headlining tour.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Weatherby|first=Taylor|date=May 8, 2017|title=Why Don't We Announce Headlining Summer Tour: Exclusive|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7785050/why-dont-we-summer-tour-dates|website=Billboard}}</ref> The group's third EP, ''Why Don't We Just'', was released on June 2, 2017.<ref name="wdwj-ep">{{cite web |title=Why Don't We Just - ===2020–present: ''The Good Times and the Bad Ones''===
On September 29, 2020, the band released "[[Fallin' (Adrenaline)]]", the lead single from their second album, ''[[The Good Times and the Bad Ones]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Matozzo|first=Marissa|date=2020-09-29|title=On Set of Why Don't We's Comeback Video|url=https://www.papermag.com/why-dont-we-fallin-2647859484.html?rebelltitem=9#rebelltitem9?rebelltitem=9|access-date=2020-11-18|website=[[Paper (magazine)|PAPER]]|language=en}}</ref> It debuted at number 37 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart, their first ever entry.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cantor|first=Brian|date=2020-10-12|title=Why Don't We Celebrates Self-Written, Self-Produced "Fallin'" Making Top 40 On Billboard Hot 100 Chart|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2020/10/12/why-dont-we-celebrates-self-written-self-produced-fallin-making-top-40-on-billboard-hot-100-chart/|access-date=2020-11-18|website=Headline Planet|language=en-US}}</ref> Their second single from the album, "[[Lotus Inn]]", was released on December 4, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Phillips|first=Hedy|date=2020-12-07|title=Why Don't We — Lotus Inn|url=https://www.euphoriazine.com/blog/2020/12/music/videos-why-dont-we-lotus-inn/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-30|website=EUPHORIA. Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> They starred in a [[YouTube]] documentary titled ''30 Days With'', which showed the last stages of development and behind the scenes content of their then-upcoming album for 30 days.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Aniftos|first=Rania|date=2020-12-09|title=Why Don't We Put Their 'Heart Out on the Table' in Emotional '30 Days With' Docuseries: Exclusive Trailer|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9496419/why-dont-we-youtube-originals-30-days-trailer/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-30|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> Their third single from the album, "Slow Down", was released on December 17, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Deville|first=Chris|date=2020-12-18|title=Boy Band Why Don't We Sample Smashing Pumpkins' On New Single|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2111333/why-dont-we-slow-down-smashing-pumpkins-1979/music/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-30|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref> On January 15, 2021, ''The Good Times and The Bad Ones'' was released. The album is largely self-produced, although it also features the productions of [[Travis Barker]], [[Skrillex]], and [[Timbaland]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Whyte|first=Woodrow|date=14 January 2021|title=Why Don’t We breakdown their transformative new album featuring Heath Ledger and Percy Jackson|url=https://www.popbuzz.com/exclusives/interviews/why-dont-we-the-good-times-lyrics-meaning/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-30|website=PopBuzz|language=en}}</ref> The album debuted at number 3 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] albums chart, making this their highest entry on the chart.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=2021-01-24|title=Morgan Wallen's 'Dangerous: The Double Album' No. 1 for Second Week on Billboard 200|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9515712/morgan-wallen-dangerous-the-double-album-tops-billboard-200-week-two|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-30|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

In 2021, the band and their manager [[Randy Phillips (music producer)|Randy Phillips]] filed a lawsuit against their other band manager David Loeffler. The band then petitioned the California Labor Commission to throw out its contract with Loeffler and management company Signature Entertainment.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Boy Band Why Don't We Asks Labor Commission for Help Dropping Manager|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/legal-and-management/9623042/why-dont-we-petition-drop-manager|access-date=2021-10-26|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

== Band members ==
===Jack Robert Avery===
Jack Robert Avery (born July 1, 1999) was born in [[Burbank, California]], but raised in [[Susquehanna, Pennsylvania]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cassi|first=Sarah|date=February 22, 2019|title=Boy band Why Don’t We coming to the Great Allentown Fair|url=https://www.lehighvalleylive.com/allentown/2019/02/boy-band-why-dont-we-coming-to-the-great-allentown-fair.html|website=lehighvalleylive}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Carter|first=Brooke|date=February 7, 2018|title=Jack Avery Net Worth 2018|url=https://gazettereview.com/2018/02/jack-avery-net-worth/|website=Gazette Review}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=March 5, 2015|title=Teen Hit Sensation: Jack Avery &#124; Raider Reader Online News|url=http://www.raiderreader.org/teen-hit-sensation-jack-avery/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170924193252/http://www.raiderreader.org/teen-hit-sensation-jack-avery|archive-date=2017-09-24|website=Raider Reader}}</ref> Avery released a solo single called "Liar" in 2016 and was part of the meet-and-greet tour "Impact", which Zach Herron and Corbyn Besson was also a part of.<ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/2018/07/jack-avery-fun-facts/ "10 Things You Didn't Know About Jack Avery"] Tiger Beat</ref> He also starred in a short film called ''Fearless Five''.<ref>[https://www.j-14.com/posts/why-don-t-we-boys-149405/ "Why Don't We Boys: A Guide to the Band Members"] J-14</ref> On April 22, 2019, Avery's former girlfriend, Gabriela Gonzalez, gave birth to their daughter, Lavender May Avery.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Swaim|first=Stephanie|date=2019-05-12|title=Why Don't We Singer Jack Avery Welcomes First Child With Gabriela Gonzalez|url=https://accessonlinefrontprod.ao.apps.nbcuni.com/articles/why-dont-we-singer-jack-avery-welcomes-first-child-with-gabriela-gonzalez|access-date=2020-11-18|website=Access|language=en}}</ref> 

===Corbyn Matthew Besson ===
Corbyn Matthew Besson (born November 25, 1998) was born in [[Dallas]], [[Texas]],<ref name="dallasobserver.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.dallasobserver.com/music/review-why-dont-we-at-the-theatre-at-grand-prairie-11630708|title=Why Don’t We Proves That Boy Bands Aren’t Such a Bad Thing, After All|first=Garrett|last=Gravley|date=April 1, 2019|website=Dallas Observer}}</ref> but raised in [[Virginia]]. Besson went to high school in Centreville, VA.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/why-dont-we-interview-2571403336.html|title=Why Don't We Is the Next Generation's All-American Boy Band|date=May 23, 2018|website=PAPER}}</ref> Besson's cousins are Dutch girl group [[O'G3NE]].<ref>[https://www.instagram.com/p/BjqGSfTnBxB/ "Corbyn • WHY DON’T WE on Instagram: "met my cousins @og3ne for the first time tonight! they are the most talented girls on this planet. my whole dutch fam came out to the show..."] Instagram</ref> Before Why Don't We, he had a following on [[YouNow]] and released a solo single called "The Only One" to [[iTunes]] in 2014. His other acoustic single, Marathon, was also released on the platform. <ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/2018/07/corbyn-besson-fun-facts/ "10 Things You Didn't Know About Corbyn Besson"] Tiger Beat| iHeartRadio"] iHeartRadio</ref> Besson was also a part of the Impact tour along with Zach Herron and Jack Avery.

===Zachary Dean Herron===
Zachary Dean Herron (born May 27, 2001), the youngest member of the group, was raised in [[Dallas]], [[Texas]].<ref name="dallasobserver.com"/> Growing up, he sang in the choir. Before Why Don't We, Herron covered songs on YouTube. His cover of "[[Stitches (Shawn Mendes song)|Stitches]]" by [[Shawn Mendes]] went viral. Zach also produced two singles himself, Timelapse and Why, in 2016.<ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/2018/07/zach-herron-fun-facts/ "10 Things You Didn't Know About Ryan Shan"] Tiger Beat</ref>

===Jonah Marais Roth Frantzich===
{{Anchor|Jonah Marais}}
Jonah Marais Roth Frantzich (born June 16, 1998), the oldest member of the group, grew up in [[Stillwater, Minnesota]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.twincities.com/2017/11/29/stillwater-native-jonah-marais-and-why-dont-we-to-play-kdwb-jingle-ball/|title=From fan to boy band: Stillwater native Jonah Marais to play KDWB Jingle Ball|date=November 30, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.startribune.com/stillwater-teen-is-online-star-in-the-making/286900921/|title=Stillwater teen is an online star in the making|website=Star Tribune}}</ref> Before Why Don't We, Marais had a following on YouNow. He also released an album, ''When the Daylight's Gone'', in 2016 and went on the 2014 [[DigiTour Media|DigiTour]].<ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/2018/07/jonah-marais-fun-facts/ "10 Things You Didn't Know About Jonah Marais"] Tiger Beat</ref>

===Daniel James Seavey===
Daniel James Seavey (born April 2, 1999), grew up in [[Portland, Oregon]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oregonlive.com/movies/2015/04/vancouvers_daniel_seavey_talks.html|title=Vancouver's Daniel Seavey talks about leaving 'American Idol' -- and his 16th birthday|first=Kristi Turnquist &#124; The|last=Oregonian/OregonLive|date=April 3, 2015|website=oregonlive}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oregonlive.com/movies/2015/01/american_idol_daniel_seavey_15.html|title='American Idol': Daniel Seavey, 15-year-old from Vancouver, talks about making it to Hollywood Week|first=Kristi Turnquist &#124; The|last=Oregonian/OregonLive|date=January 31, 2015|website=oregonlive}}</ref> A main producer of the band, he can play over 20 instruments by ear.<ref>[https://people.com/music/why-dont-we-band-people-explains/ "Why Don't We: What to Know About the Hot New Boy Band"] People</ref> As a child, his father would take him to do street performances on the city's art walks.<ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/2018/07/daniel-seavey-fun-facts/ "10 Things You Didn't Know About Daniel Seavey"] Tiger Beat</ref> In 2015, Seavey competed on [[American Idol (season 14)|season 14]] of ''[[American Idol]]'' and finished in ninth place, getting voted off on his birthday.<ref>[https://www.capitalfm.com/features/why-dont-we-age-net-worth-names/ "Who Are "Why Don't We"?
