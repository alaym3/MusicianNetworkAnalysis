'''Neeti Mohan''' (born 18 November 1979) is an Indian singer. She sings mainly in [[Hindi language|Hindi]] films, but has also done songs in [[Marathi language|Marathi]], [[Tamil language|Tamil]], [[Telugu language|Telugu]], [[Kannada language|Kannada]], [[Bengali language|Bengali]], [[Punjabi language|Punjabi]], and [[English language|English]]. Born in [[Delhi]], she was one of the winners of the [[Channel V]]'s reality show ''[[Popstars]]'', subsequently being chosen for [[Aasma]], with other winners of the show. She rose to prominence after recording "Ishq Wala Love" from ''[[Student of the Year]]'' (2012), ultimately winning the [[Filmfare RD Burman Award for New Music Talent]] and was nominated for the [[Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer|Best Female Playback Singer]] for "Jiya Re" from ''[[Jab Tak Hai Jaan]]'' (2012).

Her rendition of six [[jazz]] tracks in the soundtrack album of [[Amit Trivedi]]'s ''[[Bombay Velvet]]'' (2015) received positive response from the music critics. The following year, she received her second Filmfare nomination with the song "Sau Aasmaan" from ''[[Baar Baar Dekho]]'' (2016). In that year, Mohan appeared as a coach in the 1st season of ''[[The Voice India Kids]]'' and the second season of ''[[The Voice (TV series)|The Voice India]]''. Besides her career in music, she has also been involved in various charities and social causes.

==Early life and background==
Neeti Mohan was born in Delhi.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/tv/news/hindi/Sisters-cross-swords-for-TRPs/articleshow/5373443.cms |title=Sisters cross swords for TRPs |work=The Times of India|date=25 December 2009 |access-date=15 October 2015}}</ref> Her father, Brij Mohan Sharma, is a government officer and her mother, Kusum, is a homemaker.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mumbaimirror.com/others/sunday-read/Relative-Value-Fourtified-fame/articleshow/45242614.cms |title=Relative value: Fourtified Fame |work=Mumbai Mirror|last==Guha|first=Kunal|date=23 November 2014 |access-date=15 October 2015}}</ref> Neeti Mohan is the eldest of four sisters; [[Shakti Mohan]], [[Mukti Mohan]] and Kriti Mohan.<ref name=song>{{cite news|url=http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/celebrities/features/type/view/id/6664/ |title="I have to become the song" - Neeti Mohan |work=[[Bollywood Hungama]] |last=Vijayakar|first=Rajiv|date=3 June 2014 |access-date=14 October 2015}}</ref> Neeti Mohan initiated in learning music at the [[Gandharva Mahavidyalaya]].<ref name=bar>{{cite web|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/bollywood/neeti-mohan-raising-the-bar/ |title=Neeti Mohan: Raising the Bar|work=The Indian Express|last==Khurana|first=Suanshu|date=6 May 2015 |access-date=14 October 2015}}</ref> During the time, she was involved in dramatics and dancing; considering singing as a "serious hobby".<ref name=song/> 

During her school days, Mohan was part of the school band, and participated in [[Delhi Republic Day parade]] for five years in a row.<ref name=song/> Convinced by her father, Mohan participated in many extra-curricular activities during her school; singing, dancing, performing in the band and theatre.<ref name=toshi/> She was adjudged best cadet in the [[National Cadet Corps (India)|National Cadet Corps of India]] and represented India as Youth Exchange Programme cadet.<ref name=toshi>{{cite web|url=http://www.daijiworld.com/news/news_disp.asp?n_id=100142 |title=Kuwait: Singers Toshi-Sharib, Neeti Mohan to Rock Indian Carnival 2011 |publisher=[[Daijiworld Media]]|date=24 April 2011 |access-date=14 October 2015}}</ref> Later, she learnt music formally at the [[Bhatkhande Music Institute]] and continued training in Mumbai with Rajashree Pathak, for five years.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/energy-of-sound/ |title=The story of musician and singer Vinod Kumar |work=The Indian Express|date=1 February 2015 |access-date=15 October 2015}}</ref><ref name=song/> Apart from training in singing, Mohan studied dancing with her two sisters.<ref name=song/> She trained in [[Bharata Natyam]] and [[Kathak]] under the supervision of [[Ashley Lobo]].<ref name=song/> Besides training kids to dance, Mohan worked as an instructor there.<ref name=song/> Mohan graduated in philosophy from [[Miranda House, University of Delhi]].<ref name=bar/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/music/news/Sisters-Shakti-and-Neeti-Mohan-to-return-to-alma-mater-Miranda-House-to-perform/articleshow/51012415.cms |title=Sisters Shakti and Neeti Mohan to return to alma mater Miranda House to perform |work=The Times of India|last=Bhatia|first=Saloni |date=17 February 2016 |access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref>

In a candid interview with TV host [[Amin Dhillon]] that has gone viral with over 400K views, Mohan shared the untold story on how AR Rahman discovered her. It was 2006 and Mohan's sister Shakti was performing as a dancer at a AR Rahman show for New Year's Eve. Shakti had called Mohan right before the show and Mohan had wished to speak to Rahman but didn't get a chance. Instead she told her sister that one day she would be performing with Rahman and six months later, Mohan was singing with him. Mohan had previously met Rahman's manager while she was in Aasma and in 2007 Mohan bumped into him again when he was coming out of the cinema. It was at this chance meeting Mohan learned Rahman and his team were auditioning new voices for his USA tour. She recorded a vocal audition, singing Maya Maya from the film Guru among other songs. After 3 weeks, Mohan got a call from Rahman's manager informing her that Rahman liked her voice and she was picked for the tour. <ref>{{Citation|title=How Neeti Mohan Got Discovered by AR Rahman (Inspirational) {{!}} Exclusive Interview!|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s_KaMBGUG_s|language=en|access-date=2021-04-08}}</ref>

==Career==
===2012–14: ''Student of the Year'' and into Bollywood===
Mohan made her [[Bollywood]] debut with [[Vishal–Shekhar]]-composed ''[[Student of the Year]]'' (2012), where she performed the soft-ballad "Ishq Wala Love", along with [[Salim Merchant]] and [[Vishal–Shekhar|Shekhar Ravjiani]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/music/all-genres-welcome-but-melodies-are-special-for-neeti-mohan/story-D6U8TreuwPolKBAYZKzdfN.html |title=All genres welcome, but melodies are special for Neeti Mohan |work=Hindustan Times|last=Sen|first=Torsha |date=7 October 2015 |access-date=15 October 2015}}</ref> She was next heard in "Jiya Re" from ''[[Jab Tak Hai Jaan]]'' which was composed by [[A. R. Rahman]]. For her work in both the songs, Mohan won the [[Filmfare RD Burman Award for New Music Talent|RD Burman Award for New Music Talent]] at the Annual [[Filmfare awards]], and won the [[Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer|Best Female Playback Singer]] for the song "Jiya Re".<ref name=first/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/movies/features/type/view/id/4404 |title=Nominations for 58th Idea Filmfare Awards 2012 |work=Bollywood Hungama |date=13 January 2013|access-date=7 November 2014}}</ref> Besides, Mohan provided the chorus along with [[Natalie Di Luccio]] in [[Raghav Sachar]]-composed "Bittoo Sab Ki Lega" from ''[[Bittoo Boss]]'', released the same year.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://indiatoday.intoday.in/story/bittoo-boss-raghav-sachar-bittoo-sab-ki-lega/1/179865.html |title=Bittoo Boss packs right punch with desi tadka, global sound |work=[[India Today]] |date=29 March 2012 |access-date=15 October 2015}}</ref> During the year, she released her first single, titled "Ja Ja"; composed and written by Mohan, assisted by her father.<ref name=acting/>

===2015–present: ''Bombay Velvet'', ''Baar Baar Dekho'' and beyond===
Mohan's biggest release of 2015 came with [[Amit Trivedi]]'s ''[[Bombay Velvet]]'', where she performed six [[jazz]]y tracks for the album.<ref name=Jazz>{{cite news|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/jazz-by-the-bay/|title=Jazz by the Bay |work=The Indian Express|date=2 May 2015 |access-date=15 October 2015}}</ref> Trivedi was in search of a voice with an "[[Asha Bhosle]] meets [[Nina Simone]]" tonal quality for [[Anushka Sharma]]'s Jazz singer character Rosie in the film, when he considered Mohan as the choice.<ref name=bar/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dnaindia.com/entertainment/report-ranbir-kapoor-and-anushka-sharma-jazz-up-bombay-velvet-promotions-2083423 |title=Ranbir Kapoor and Anushka Sharma jazz up 'Bombay Velvet' promotions |work=Daily News and Analysis|date=5 May 2015 |access-date=15 October 2015}}</ref> According to ''The Indian Express'', "what has worked" in the song "Mohabbat Buri Bimari" is Mohan's extemporisation in the middle of the track—with all the hiccups, laughs and squeals in place.<ref name=bar/> The film marks the first time she read a script before recording a track to the respective film.<ref name=high/> "I was asked to sing just like how I would perform live".<ref name=high/> She recorded the songs at the studio dressed like a jazz singer, with people around her to cheer her.<ref name=high/> For another track "Dhadaam Dhadaam", Mohan was locked up in a room and was asked to "feel the pain of someone who's going through a break-up". ==Artistry==
===Voice and musical style===
Regarding Mohan's [[voice type]], ''[[The Indian Express]]'' wrote that she has a "high pitched", "raw" vocal structure.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/play/middling-in-the-air/ |title=Middling in the Air |work=The Indian Express |last=Khurana|first= Suanshu |date=23 January 2015 |access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref> According to Amitabh Bhattacharya, Mohan's voice has the "right kind of jazz sensibilities with a certain Hindustaniyat".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/know-all-about-the-making-of-bombay-velvets-music/ |title=Know all about the making of Bombay Velvet's music |work=The Indian Express|last=Ghosh|first= Sankhayan |date=14 May 2015 |access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref> Mohan mentioned that her classical training taught her how to approach a song in "different octaves". She explored more about texture of voice and harmony through her time at Aasma.<ref name=song/> Complimenting her Jazz vocals in ''Bombay Velvet'', ''The Indian Express'' stated "If "Ka Kha Gha" shows Mohan's range, "Dhadam Dhadam" gets melancholy a voice".<ref name=Jazz/> During her career, she performed various genres of songs, though she loves "singing melodies the most".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/music/all-genres-welcome-but-melodies-are-special-for-neeti-mohan/story-D6U8TreuwPolKBAYZKzdfN.html |title=All genres welcome, but melodies are special for Neeti Mohan |work=Hindustan Times|last=Sen|first= Torsha |date=7 October 2015 |access-date=18 October 2015}}</ref> In an interview, Mohan expressed her keen to explore bhajans.<ref name=bhajan/>

===Influence===
Mohan has said that from childhood she has been influenced by [[Lata Mangeshkar]], [[Asha Bhosle]] and [[Kishore Kumar]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/music/bappi-lahiri-shaan-and-neeti-mohan-tell-us-why-retro-music-is-so-popular/story-ZZDb9FsQ8CilgNOiH2vRrO.html |title=Bappi Lahiri, Shaan and Neeti Mohan tell us why retro music is so popular |work=Hindustan Times|date=27 April 2016 |access-date=18 October 2015}}</ref> She considered Mangeshkar as her "greatest teacher for playback",<ref name=song/> while she credited [[A. R. Rahman]] for making her sustain her standard; "He is a task master but he gets the best out of you. His standards are very high, be it a recording or live performance".<ref name=connect/> Mohan has also been influenced by many artists outside the country. She considered herself as a fan of [[John Mayer]] for his inspiring style of writing lyrics.<ref name=acting/> Mohan credits the 1971 released song "[[Imagine (John Lennon song)|Imagine]]" by [[John Lennon]] for inspiring on achieving her dreams with the "right kind of dedication".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dnaindia.com/entertainment/report-songs-that-changed-the-world-and-me-2264455 |title=Songs that changed the world... and me |work=Daily News and Analysis |last=Silva|first= Melissa D'Costa |date=16 October 2016 |access-date=26 November 2017}}</ref>
