'''Diego Luna Alexander''' ({{IPA-es|ˈdjeɣo ˈluna aleksanˈdeɾ}}; born 29 December 1979) is a Mexican actor, singer, director, and producer. Following a career in Mexican [[telenovela]]s, he has appeared in films including ''[[Y tu mamá también]]'', ''[[Open Range (2003 film)|Open Range]]'', ''[[Milk (2008 American film)|Milk]]'', ''[[Rudo y Cursi]]'', ''[[Dirty Dancing: Havana Nights]]'', ''[[The Terminal]]'', ''[[Berlin, I Love You]]'' and ''[[Rogue One|Rogue One: A Star Wars Story]]''. He played drug trafficker [[Miguel Ángel Félix Gallardo]] in the first two seasons of ''[[Narcos: Mexico]].''

==Early life==
Luna was born in [[Toluca]] and raised in [[Mexico City]]. His mother, Fiona Alexander, was a British costume designer of Scottish and English descent.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://coolhunting.com/culture/interview-diego-luna/|title=Interview: Diego Luna|date=2013-02-18|website=COOL HUNTING|language=en-US|access-date=2019-07-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.signonsandiego.com/uniontrib/20040815/news_1a15diego.html|title=On a roll: Diego Luna's movie career is smokin' hot|work=The San Diego Union-Tribune|date=2014-08-15|access-date=2016-12-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081011075253/http://www.signonsandiego.com/uniontrib/20040815/news_1a15diego.html|archive-date=2008-10-11}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/latinoimagemaker0000beru|url-access=registration|title=Latino Image Makers in Hollywood|first1=Frank|last1=Javier|first2=Garcia|last2=Beruman|publisher=McFarland|year=2014}}</ref> She died in a car accident when Luna was two years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2007/10/28/PK96SS6Q1.DTL&type=movies|title=LATINO FESTIVAL LAUDS LUNA}}</ref> His father, [[Alejandro Luna]], is a theater, film, and opera [[scenic design|set designer]] who reinforced the importance of theater and the arts in his life. Alejandro would bring Diego to the sets and mentor him in different aspects of art, cultivating a desire to become an actor and uphold the family tradition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/28/Diego-Luna.html|title=Diego Luna Biography (1979-)}}</ref> Luna and his frequent co-star [[Gael García Bernal]]  were friends from infancy. Their mothers were close friends.<ref name="independent.co.uk">{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/profiles/how-we-met-gael-garc-237-a-bernal-diego-luna-1799842.html|accessdate=2021-03-25|title=How We Met: Gael Garcia Bernal & Diego Luna}}</ref>

==Career==

===Acting===
[[File:International_Jury_Berlinale_2017_3.jpg|thumb|right|250px|Diego Luna, [[Maggie Gyllenhaal]] and [[Julia Jentsch]] at Berlinale 2017]]
Luna acted in his first play at age seven. As a child actor, he appeared in several telenovelas.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/world/mexico-americas/la-fg-mexico-diego-luna-20180328-story.html|title=Actor Diego Luna was a Hollywood golden boy. So why did he go back to Mexico?|date=March 28, 2018|website=Los Angeles Times}}</ref>

Luna starred as Tenoch Iturbide alongside [[Gael García Bernal]] in ''[[Y Tu Mamá También]]'' (2001), a Mexican road trip movie.<ref name="auto"/> A critical and box office success, the role brought him international stardom.<ref name="auto"/> The film also was the beginning series of high profile collaborations with childhood friend García Bernal,<ref name="independent.co.uk"/> with whom he would costar in several films and go on to establish [[Canana Films]].

In 2002, Luna starred in ''[[Vampires: Los Muertos]]'' and the Oscar-winning ''[[Frida]]''. He also appeared in ''[[Open Range (2003 film)|Open Range]] ''(2003), ''[[The Terminal]]'' (2004) alongside Tom Hanks, ''[[Dirty Dancing: Havana Nights]]'' (2004) (a Cuban-based sequel to ''[[Dirty Dancing]]''), ''[[Criminal (2004 film)|Criminal]]'' (2004), ''[[Only God Knows]]'' (2006), ''[[Fade to Black (2006 film)|Fade to Black]]'' (2006), ''[[Mister Lonely]]'' (2007), ''[[Rudo y Cursi]]'' (2008), ''[[Just Walking]]'' (2008), ''[[Milk (2008 American film)|Milk]]'' (2008), and ''[[Elysium (film)|Elysium]]''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Diego Luna on Star Wars Spin-Off, His Father Hating Christmas & Recreating The Princess Bride - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CjApD1utCIk&feature=youtu.be|access-date=2020-12-15|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref> (2013).{{citation needed|date=September 2019}}

In March 2015, Luna joined the cast of [[Ana Lily Amirpour]]'s film ''[[The Bad Batch (film)|The Bad Batch]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/news/3336919/ana-lily-amirpours-cannibal-film-gets-interesting-cast/|title=Ana Lily Amirpour's Cannibal Film Gets Interesting Cast - Bloody Disgusting!|date=20 March 2015}}</ref>

'' ''In ''[[Rogue One: A Star Wars Story]]'' (2016), he portrayed [[Cassian Andor]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://remezcla.com/film/diego-luna-rogue-one-series-disney-star-wars-d23/|title=Diego Luna Says Being in Novelas Prepared Him for Upcoming 'Rogue One' Series|date=August 27, 2019|website=Remezcla}}</ref> a captain and intelligence officer of the Rebel Alliance, starring alongside [[Felicity Jones]]. He will reprise the role in an upcoming [[Disney+]] series centered around the character. In December 2020, he confirmed that the production of the series has begun in London on [[Jimmy Kimmel Live!|Jimmy Kimmel Live.]]

Luna starred as Mexican drug cartel leader [[Miguel Ángel Félix Gallardo|Miguel Angel Felix Gallardo]] in the [[Netflix]] series ''[[Narcos: Mexico]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/narcos-mexico-season-2-diego-luna-scoot-mcnairy-return-star-1168955|title='Narcos: Mexico' Sets Diego Luna, Scoot McNairy as Season 2 Stars|website=The Hollywood Reporter}}</ref> which premiered in November 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/11/16/arts/television/narcos-mexico-recap-premiere.html|title='Narcos: Mexico' Premiere: 'It Doesn't Have a Happy Ending' - The New York Times}}</ref>

===Other work===
[[File:Rogue_One-_A_Star_Wars_Story_Japan_Premiere_Red_Carpet-_Diego_Luna_&_Felicity_Jones_(34989492253).jpg|thumb|right|250px|Diego and [[Felicity Jones]] at the premiere of ''Rogue One: A Star Wars Story'' in Japan.]]
After starring in many [[telenovela]]s,{{citation needed|date=September 2019}} Luna and [[Gael García Bernal]] created [[Canana Films]] in 2005.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Fernandez|first1=Jay A.|title=Gael Garcia Bernal and Diego Luna's Canana Films to Start Filming 'La Ultima Pelicula' in Mexico Dec. 16 |url=http://www.indiewire.com/2012/12/gael-garcia-bernal-and-diego-lunas-canana-films-to-start-filming-la-ultima-pelicula-in-mexico-dec-16-42506/|work=www.indiewire.com}}</ref>

In 2012, Luna directed his first film, the [[biopic]] ''[[Cesar Chavez (film)|Cesar Chávez]]'', about the [[Cesar Chávez|eponymous]] founder of the [[United Farm Workers]].<ref name="Wilkinson">[http://articles.latimes.com/2012/jul/01/entertainment/la-ca-cesar-chavez-film-20120701 Wilkinson, Tracy. "Diego Luna's Cesar Chavez Movie Marches in Mexico."] ''[[Los Angeles Times]].'' July 1, 2012. Accessed 2012-10-14.</ref>

Luna became a member of the jury for the [[Un Certain Regard]] section of the [[Cannes Film Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/article/62147.html|title=Un Certain Regard Jury 2016|date=28 April 2016|access-date=28 April 2016|work=Cannes Film Festival|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160501164908/http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/article/62147.html|archive-date=2016-05-01}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=September 2019}}

Luna and García Bernal co-founded Ambulante A.C., an organization and film festival working to bring documentary films to recommended places.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ambulante.com.mx/|title=Ambulante A.C.|date=1 June 2012|publisher=Ambulante A.C.|access-date=1 June 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120531185030/http://www.ambulante.com.mx/|archive-date=31 May 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> The organization received the prestigious [[Washington Office on Latin America]]'s Human Rights Award in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wola.org/wola_human_rights_award|title=WOLA's Human Rights Awards|date=20 September 2011|publisher=Washington Office on Latin America|access-date=1 June 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120406160241/http://www.wola.org/wola_human_rights_award|archive-date=6 April 2012}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=September 2019}} In January 2015, Luna narrated a video about the obstacles faced by children migrating from their home countries and seeking refuge in the US.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wola.org/about/board|title=Board of Directors|date=21 September 2011|publisher=Washington Office on Latin America|access-date=1 January 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wola.org/es/noticias/nuevo_video_de_wola_ninos_migrantes_mexicanos_olvidados_en_la_frontera|title=Nuevo video de WOLA narrado por Diego Luna y reporte: Niños migrantes mexicanos olvidados en la frontera|date=22 January 2015|publisher=Washington Office on Latin America|access-date=22 January 2015}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=September 2019}}

In October 2019 Luna and García Bernal announced they were joining the Creative Advisory Board for TV and Film development company EXILE Content along with [[Adam Grant]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ramos |first1=Dino-Ray |title=Diego Luna, Gael Garcia Bernal Join Exile's Creative Advisory Board With Chair Adam Grant |url=https://deadline.com/2019/10/diego-luna-gael-garcia-bernal-exile-creative-advisory-board-adam-grant-elizabeth-chai-vasarhelyi-serena-mccalla-1202750778/ |website=Deadline |publisher=PMC |access-date=19 February 2020}}</ref>

In 2021, he received a [[Daytime Emmy Awards|Daytime Emmy Award]] for Outstanding Daytime Talent in a Spanish Language Program for his [[Amazon Prime Video]] talk show ''[[Pan y Circo]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Cordero|first=Rosy|url=https://deadline.com/2021/07/daytime-emmy-award-winners-alex-trebek-zac-efron-karrueche-tran-1234795492/|title=Alex Trebek, Zac Efron, Karrueche Tran, More, Announced As Daytime Emmy Fiction & Lifestyle Winners—Complete Winners List|website=[[Deadline Hollywood|Deadline]]|date=18 July 2021|access-date=19 July 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Luna married [[Camila Sodi]] in February 2008; they were divorced in March 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://latino.foxnews.com/latino/entertainment/2013/03/25/diego-luna-and-wife-announce-split/|title=Diego Luna and Wife Announce Split|work=[[Fox News Latino]]|date=25 March 2013|access-date=23 June 2013}}</ref> They have two children: Jerónimo (b. August 12, 2008),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2008/08/15/diego-luna-and/|title=Diego Luna and Camila Sodi Welcome Son Jerónimo|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=15 August 2008|access-date=19 April 2011}}</ref> and Fiona (b. July 1, 2010), named after Luna's mother.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2010/07/02/diego-luna-camila-sodi-welcome-daughter-fiona/|title=Diego Luna, Camila Sodi Welcome Daughter Fiona|last=Leon|first=Anya|date=2 July 2010|publisher=[[People (magazine)|People]]|access-date=19 April 2011}}</ref>
