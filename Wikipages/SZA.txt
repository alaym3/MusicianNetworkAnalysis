'''Solána Imani Rowe''' (born November 8, 1990<ref>https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lizzo-shares-adorable-throwback-photo-with-sza-news.142514.html</ref><ref>https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2021/11/09/lizzo-sza/</ref>), is an American singer and songwriter. Born in [[St. Louis]], [[Missouri]], she began making music in the early 2010s, releasing two [[extended play]]s—''[[See.SZA.Run]]'' where she pays respect to the year she was born in the cover, and ''[[S (EP)|S]]''—before signing with the [[hip hop music|hip hop]] record label [[Top Dawg Entertainment]], through which she released ''[[Z (EP)|Z]]'', her third EP and first retail release.<ref name="Billboard-Z"/>

SZA's debut studio album, ''[[Ctrl (SZA album)|Ctrl]]'' (2017), was released to universal acclaim from music critics.<ref>{{Cite web |title=CTRL by SZA |url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/ctrl/sza |work=Metacritic |access-date=December 1, 2017 |archive-date=October 8, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171008072915/http://www.metacritic.com/music/ctrl/sza |url-status=live }}</ref> It debuted at number three on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and was certified double [[Platinum certification|platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA). At the [[60th Annual Grammy Awards]], the album earned her four nominations, including [[Best New Artist]]. In 2020, it was placed on ''[[Rolling Stone]]''{{'}}s [[Rolling Stone's 500 Greatest Albums of All Time|500 Greatest Albums of All Time]] list. In August 2017, she was featured on [[Maroon 5]]'s Top 10 hit single "[[What Lovers Do]]". The next year, she collaborated with [[Kendrick Lamar]] to create the top 10 single "[[All the Stars]]" for the ''[[Black Panther (soundtrack)|Black Panther]]'' soundtrack; for which she was nominated for the [[Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song|Golden Globe Award]] and the [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.essence.com/entertainment/sza-kendrick-lamar-all-the-stars-oscar-nomination/ |title=All The Stars: SZA And Kendrick Lamar Are Now Oscar-Nominated |first=Rachaell |last=Davis |date=January 22, 2019 |website=Essence |language=en-US |access-date=May 21, 2019 |archive-date=July 9, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190709034914/https://www.essence.com/entertainment/sza-kendrick-lamar-all-the-stars-oscar-nomination/ |url-status=live }}</ref> In 2021, she released the single "[[Good Days]]" and featured on [[Doja Cat]]'s "[[Kiss Me More]]", both of which reached the top 10 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]],<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Morgan Wallen, SZA Hit Hot 100's Top 10, Chris Brown & Young Thug Rule Radio Songs|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9512991/morgan-wallen-sza-chris-brown-young-thug-hot-100-top-10/|access-date=March 10, 2021|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=SZA reacts to landing her first solo 'Billboard' Hot 100 hit, "Good Days" - Music News - ABC News Radio|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2021/1/20/sza-reacts-to-landing-her-first-solo-billboard-hot-100-hit-g.html|access-date=March 10, 2021|website=abcnewsradioonline.com|language=en}}</ref> with the latter breaking the record for the longest-running all-female top 10 hit.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Doja Cat & SZA's "Kiss Me More" Breaks Record Held By Brandy & Monica|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/doja-cat-and-szas-kiss-me-more-breaks-record-previously-held-by-brandy-and-monica-news.138033.html|access-date=2021-08-25|website=www.hotnewhiphop.com|date=August 24, 2021}}</ref>

SZA is a [[neo soul]] singer whose music has been described as [[alternative R&B]] with elements of [[soul music|soul]], [[hip hop music|hip hop]], [[minimal music|minimalist]] [[contemporary R&B|R&B]], [[indie rock]], [[cloud rap]], [[witch house (music genre)|witch house]], and [[chillwave]].<ref name="Guardian"/> SZA's lyrics are described as "unravelling", and her songs often revolve around themes of [[Human sexuality|sexuality]], [[nostalgia]], and [[Abandonment (emotional)|abandonment]].<ref name="neontommy.com"/> She has received 14 [[Grammy Awards|Grammy]] nominations, making her [[Grammy Award records#Most%20nominations%20without%20winning|one of the artists with the most Grammy nominations without winning]].

==Early life==
Solána Imani Rowe was born on November 8, 1990, in [[St. Louis]], Missouri, and raised in [[Maplewood, New Jersey]].<ref name="Complex">{{cite web |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/who-is-sza/ |first=Insanul |last=Ahmed |title=Who is SZA? |work=Complex Music |date=September 8, 2013 |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=April 29, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190429221725/http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/who-is-sza/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Her father was an executive producer at [[CNN]], while her mother was an executive at [[AT&T]].<ref name="Morris">{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/how-sza-beat-depression-and-cracked-the-top-10-with-ctrl-128463/ |title=How SZA Beat Depression and Cracked the Top 10 With 'Ctrl' |last=Morris |first=Alex |date=August 30, 2017 |magazine=Rolling Stone |language=en-US |access-date=April 14, 2019 |archive-date=April 14, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190414181021/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/how-sza-beat-depression-and-cracked-the-top-10-with-ctrl-128463/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Rowe was born to a [[Christians|Christian]] mother and a [[Muslim]] father.<ref name="Morris"/> She has an older half-sister named Tiffany Daniels and an older brother, Daniel, a rapper named Manhattan.<ref name="Complex"/><ref name="Morris"/> She was raised as an orthodox Muslim.<ref name="Complex"/>
<blockquote>It's like the belief in one God, all the pillars of Islam et cetera, and I think those are ideas that will never leave me, those make sense in my spirit. It's the way that I connect with God; it has always made sense to me. I think I would love to wear my [[hijab]] but I feel like I don't wanna wear my hijab and talk crazy on stage and be in videos with [[Travis Scott]]. Like I don't wanna be disrespectful because I have too much love and respect for the religion, for my father, and for myself.<ref name="HOT97">{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XnN7p1lZU2k |title=Sza On CTRL, Relating to Rihanna, Standing for Pharrell & Her Faith |publisher=HOT 97 |date=6 June 2017 |via=YouTube |access-date=15 April 2020}}</ref></blockquote>
She attended a [[Muslim]] prep school every day after her regular schooling. However, due to the [[September 11 attacks]], Rowe was subjected to bullying in 7th grade, leading her to stop wearing her [[hijab]].<ref name="Complex"/> Rowe attended [[Columbia High School (New Jersey)|Columbia High School]], where she was very active in sports, including [[gymnastics]] and [[cheerleading]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/everything-you-need-to-know-about-sza-new-video.41999.html |title=Everything You Need To Know About SZA |first=Danny |last=Schwartz |date=June 14, 2017 |work=HotNewHipHop |access-date=October 17, 2017 |archive-date=October 18, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171018074412/https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/everything-you-need-to-know-about-sza-new-video.41999.html |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="HOT97"/>
After graduating from high school in 2008,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/06/sza-columbia-high-school-the-weekend/ |title=SZA gives motivational speech at old high school, performs "The Weekend": Watch |first=Lake |last=Schatz |date=June 5, 2018 |website=Consequence of Sound |language=en-US |access-date=May 1, 2019}}</ref> Rowe later went to three separate colleges, finally settling at [[Delaware State University]] to study marine biology.<ref name="Complex"/><ref name="Morris"/> She eventually dropped out in her last semester; however, she immediately started taking on random jobs in order to make money.<ref name="Morris"/><ref name="BillboardMay2013">{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1563547/sza-talks-quitting-her-day-job-working-with-other-emile-haynie |first=Reggie |last=Ugwu |title=SZA Talks Quitting Her Day Job, Working With Holy Other & Emile Haynie |work=Billboard |date=May 23, 2013 |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=May 11, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190511112454/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/1563547/sza-talks-quitting-her-day-job-working-with-other-emile-haynie |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.theringer.com/2017/6/9/16039410/sza-ctrl-30bc177676ef |title=SZA Takes Back Her 'Ctrl' |last=Peters |first=Micah |date=June 9, 2017 |website=The Ringer |access-date=April 14, 2019 |archive-date=April 14, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190414183145/https://www.theringer.com/2017/6/9/16039410/sza-ctrl-30bc177676ef |url-status=live }}</ref> Rowe formed her stage name from the [[Supreme Alphabet]], taking influence from rapper [[RZA]] of the [[Wu-Tang Clan]]. The last two letters in her name stand for Zig-Zag and [[Allah]], while the first letter S can mean either savior or sovereign.<ref name="Complex"/>

==Career==
===2011–2014: Career beginnings and EPs===
{{Main|See.SZA.Run|S (EP)|l2=S|Z (EP)|l3=Z}}
[[File:SZA, August 2013.jpg|thumb|left|upright=0.85|SZA performing in 2013]]

SZA first met members of [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] during the CMJ New Music Report in 2011, when her boyfriend's clothing company sponsored a show in which [[Kendrick Lamar]] was performing. Her early music was given to TDE president [[Punch (rapper)|Terrence "Punch" Henderson]], who was surprised with the quality of the material.<ref name="Billboard-Z">{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6041313/sza-talks-z-album-being-the-only-girl-in-top-dawg-entertainment |title=SZA Talks 'Z' Album & Being the Only Girl In Top Dawg Entertainment |first=Reggie |last=Ugwu |date=April 7, 2014 |magazine=Billboard |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=June 14, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180614224345/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6041313/sza-talks-z-album-being-the-only-girl-in-top-dawg-entertainment |url-status=live }}</ref> The two stayed in touch, and after SZA began generating buzz with the release of her two EPs, TDE stepped in to sign her in 2013, making her the label's first female artist. SZA's early music was recorded with her friends and neighbors in which they "stole a bunch of beats off the Internet".<ref name="BillboardMay2013"/> On October 29, 2012, SZA [[self-released]] her debut [[extended play|EP]] ''[[See.SZA.Run]]''.<ref name="Complex-S-EP">{{cite web |url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/ep-premiere-sza-see-sza-run |title=EP Premiere: SZA "See SZA Run" |first=Zach |last=Frydenlund |date=October 29, 2012 |website=Complex Music |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=August 1, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180801004224/http://www.complex.com/music/2012/10/ep-premiere-sza-see-sza-run |url-status=live }}</ref>

===2015–2018: ''Ctrl'' and breakthrough===
{{Main|Ctrl (SZA album)|l1=Ctrl}}

Muller of ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine, Rowe's vocals alternate between a "vapory husk and a sky-high falsetto".<ref name="MeetSZA">{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/meet-sza-kendrick-lamars-newest-labelmate-54484/ |title=Meet SZA, Kendrick Lamar's Newest Labelmate |first=Marissa G. |last=Muller |date=November 4, 2013 |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |access-date=May 11, 2018 |quote=For the record, her name is pronounced S(źź)áuh, like RZA and GZA. |archive-date=March 13, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200313233142/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/meet-sza-kendrick-lamars-newest-labelmate-54484/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Jordan Sargent of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' magazine labelled Rowe's vocals as being "chillwave" and "ethereal".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19278-sza-z/ |title=SZA "Z" |first=Jordan |last=Sargent |date=April 18, 2014 |work=Pitchfork |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=April 11, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190411211108/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19278-sza-z/ |url-status=live }}</ref> SZA disputes her music being labelled as hip-hop, R&B and pop, stating she often listens to a variety of music including [[Stevie Nicks]], classical jazz, folk, and rap. Further, she said: "when you try to label it [her music], you remove the option for it to be limitless. It diminishes the music."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rap-up.com/2017/10/21/sza-reveals-album-with-tame-impala-mark-ronson/ |title=SZA Reveals Album with Tame Impala and Mark Ronson |date=October 21, 2017 |work=Rap-Up |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=July 31, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180731161900/http://www.rap-up.com/2017/10/21/sza-reveals-album-with-tame-impala-mark-ronson/ |url-status=live }}</ref> SZA began writing songs due to being "passionate" about writing, and enjoyed poetry; when writing lyrics, SZA "freestyles" them in order to express whatever comes to her "mind", noting that it does not always make sense to herself.<ref name="BillboardMay2013"/> Thematically, SZA's work contains "unravelling lyrics", that touch upon themes of [[Human sexuality|sexuality]], [[nostalgia]], and [[Abandonment (emotional)|abandonment]].<ref name="neontommy.com"/>

SZA's musical style has been described as "alt R&B".<ref name="Guardian">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/mar/08/sza |title=New Band Of The Day: SZA |first=Paul |last=Lester |date=March 8, 2013 |newspaper=The Guardian |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=November 17, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181117081536/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/mar/08/sza |url-status=live }}</ref> SZA's songs are built over "layers of sliced, delayed, and reversed vocals" and contains "twists and mutates".<ref name="neontommy.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.neontommy.com/news/2014/04/sza-z-album-review |title=SZA: 'Z' Album Review |first=Ben |last=Benjamin |date=April 10, 2014 |website=Neon Tommy |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180731213458/http://www.neontommy.com/news/2014/04/sza-z-album-review |archive-date=July 31, 2018 |access-date=April 15, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Complex-S-EP"/> Reggie Ugwu from [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] magazine finds her musical style to feature an "agnostic utopia dripping with mood", that straddles the "line between minimalist R&B, '80s synth pop and soul".<ref name="Billboard-Z"/> Rowe's music is primarily [[PBR&B]] and [[neo-soul]], but has been noted for taking influences from a broad variety of genres including [[soul music|soul]], [[hip hop music|hip hop]], minimalist R&B, [[indie rock]], cloud rap, ethereal R&B, [[witch house (music genre)|witch house]] and [[chillwave]] elements.<ref name="Guardian"/> Michael Madden described SZA's musical genre as being "agnosticism corresponds", noting that her work is not just one style of music and is versatile, noting the musical style is not just "R&B, pop, soul, or one thing at all".<ref name="consequenceofsound1">{{cite magazine |url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2014/04/album-review-sza-z/ |title=SZA – Z &#124; Album Reviews |last=Madden |first=Michael |date=April 8, 2014 |magazine=Consequence of Sound |access-date=April 12, 2014 |archive-date=April 13, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140413131104/http://consequenceofsound.net/2014/04/album-review-sza-z/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

SZA listens to [[Ella Fitzgerald]] for vocal influence,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.redbull.com/en/music/stories/1331643712041/interview-with-up-and-coming-rnb-star-sza |title=Five things you should know about SZA |first=Aimee |last=Cliff |date=April 7, 2014 |website=Redbull.com |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=May 18, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160518173347/http://www.redbull.com/en/music/stories/1331643712041/interview-with-up-and-coming-rnb-star-sza |url-status=live }}</ref> and has said that [[Lauryn Hill]] is one of her personal influences.<ref name="Vogue"/> SZA also cites a wide range of musical artists as influences,<ref name="BillboardMay2013"/> including [[Kameelah Williams|Meelah]], the [[Red Hot Chili Peppers]], [[LFO (American band)|LFO]], [[Macy Gray]], [[Common (rapper)|Common]], [[Björk]], [[Jamiroquai]], and "a lot of [[Wu-Tang Clan|Wu]], [[Nas]], [[Mos Def]], [[Jay Z|Hov]]".<ref name="Complex"/> Speaking on her influences, SZA said: "[My] personal influences came from dancing with [[American Ballet Theatre]] and doing pieces to Björk [music]. That's the only time I had really any outside influence to music. So, the people that I fell in love with on a musical level were always much older. [[Jamiroquai]] is just, like, the shit for me."<ref name="refinery29.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.refinery29.com/sza-interview |title=SZA's Unlikely Path To Pop Stardom |first=Nathan |last=Reese |date=October 9, 2013 |website=Refinery29 |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=August 9, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180809090838/http://www.refinery29.com/sza-interview |url-status=live }}</ref> In an interview for [[Live Nation Entertainment]], SZA described the meeting she had with [[Beyoncé]] for the writing of the song "[[Feeling Myself (Nicki Minaj song)|Feeling Myself]]", affirming "Beyoncé might be the most perfect, beautiful being I've ever met in my whole life. She's the most inspirational woman on earth, next to my mother". SZA also spoke about [[Rihanna]], admiring her strong and confident attitude, of someone who does only the music she wants to do, and that if there's anybody that she could imagine singing her words, it would be Rihanna.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sza Talks Beyoncé, Rihanna, and the Importance of Staying Present|url=http://www.lntvglobal.com/en-fr/article/sza-talks-beyonce-rihanna-and-the-importance-of-staying-present/|access-date=August 30, 2020|website=LNTV}}</ref>

During an interview, SZA said she is less inspired by strictly music, and more inspired by creating [[art]] in general; she has looked up to people who were not "typical artists" including her "favorite gymnast, ice-skater, saxophonist, painter, or movie director," continuing to say she was particularly inspired by film director [[Spike Lee]].<ref name="refinery29.com"/> During an interview with ''[[W (magazine)|W]]'', SZA spoke on her style influences, saying a large amount of her style inspiration comes from movies, including [[Wes Anderson]] films, praising his use of "pantone color palette" and that she "would love to dress like a character from ''[[Moonrise Kingdom]]''. Or perhaps [[Bill Murray]] in ''[[The Life Aquatic]]''."<ref name="WMag">{{cite web |url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/sza-hair-style/ |title=She's Just SZA |first=Sarah |last=Leon |date=April 9, 2014 |website=W Magazine |access-date=April 15, 2020}}</ref> Along with her music, SZA's image has been compared to neo-soul artists Lauryn Hill and [[Erykah Badu]].<ref name="huffingtonpost.com"/> SZA's hair became a point of interest during the early stages of her career and she discussed it in interviews with [[Vogue (magazine)|''Vogue'']] and ''[[Harper's Bazaar]]''.<ref name="Vogue">{{cite web |url=http://www.vogue.com/vogue-daily/article/why-sza-huge-natural-hair-is-our-new-obsession/#1 |title=Why SZA's Huge Natural Hair is Our New Obsession |first=Chioma |last=Nnadi |date=March 27, 2014 |website=Vogue |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140801075903/http://www.vogue.com/vogue-daily/article/why-sza-huge-natural-hair-is-our-new-obsession/#1 |archive-date=August 1, 2014 |access-date=April 15, 2020}}</ref> During her performances, SZA tends to wear "free-flowing" clothes that are easy to move around in and has also been known to wear pajamas or baggy clothing onstage.<ref name="nytimes.com">{{cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/21/fashion/sza-pitchfork-festival-style.html |title=SZA's Pitchfork Festival Style |first=Elizabeth |last=Bristow |date=July 21, 2014 |work=The New York Times |access-date=April 15, 2020 |archive-date=August 9, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180809073817/https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/21/fashion/sza-pitchfork-festival-style.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Awards and nominations ==
{{Main|List of awards and nominations received by SZA}}
Throughout her career, SZA has earned fourteen [[Grammy]] nominations, a [[Golden Globe]] nomination, and an [[Academy Award]] nomination. She has earned one [[American Music Award]], one [[Billboard Music Award]], two [[MTV Video Music Award]]s, two [[BET Awards]], including [[BET Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] in 2018, and received the "Rulebreaker Award" at the [[Billboard Women in Music]] event in 2018. SZA also won the [[Soul Train Music Award for Best New Artist]] in 2017.
