A '''very important person''' or '''personage'''<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=vip {{!}} Origin and meaning of the name vip by Online Etymology Dictionary|url=https://www.etymonline.com/word/vip|access-date=2020-07-07|website=www.etymonline.com|language=en}}</ref> ('''VIP''' or '''V.I.P.''') is a person who is accorded special [[Privilege (legal ethics)|privilege]]s due to their high social [[Social status|status]], [[Social influence|influence]] or importance.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://wordnetweb.princeton.edu/perl/webwn?s=very%20important%20person | title = Very Important Person | access-date = 2011-05-23 | publisher = The Trustees of Princeton University}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=VIP definition and meaning {{!}} Collins English Dictionary|url=https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/vip|access-date=2020-07-07|website=www.collinsdictionary.com|language=en}}</ref> The term was not common until sometime after World War 2 by [[Royal Air Force|RAF]] pilots.<ref name=":1" />{{Additional citation needed|date=July 2020}}

Examples include [[celebrities]], [[head of state|heads of state]] or [[head of government|heads of government]], other [[politicians]], major [[employer]]s, [[high roller]]s, high-level [[corporate officer]]s, [[bankers]], [[economists]], [[clergy]], [[military personnel]], [[High-net-worth individual|wealthy individuals]], or any other socially notable person who receives special treatment for any reason.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Synonyms of VIP {{!}} Thesaurus.com|url=https://www.thesaurus.com/browse/vip|access-date=2020-07-07|website=www.thesaurus.com|language=en}}</ref>{{Additional citation needed|date=July 2020}} The special treatment usually involves separation from common people, and a higher level of comfort or service.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Waida|first=Maria|title=20 Impressive VIP Event Ideas|url=https://blog.bizzabo.com/vip-event-ideas|access-date=2020-07-07|website=blog.bizzabo.com|date=3 January 2019|language=en-us}}</ref>

In some cases, such as with [[Ticket (admission)|ticket]]s, VIP may be used as a title in a similar way to ''premium'' or ''exclusive.'' Usually, VIP tickets can be purchased by anyone, but still meaning separation from other customers, own security checks etc.{{Citation needed|date=July 2020}}

Sometimes, the term ''very very important person'' (''VVIP'' or ''V.V.I.P.'') is also used,<ref>[http://acronyms.thefreedictionary.com/VVIP Free Dictionary definition], accessed 15 June 2016</ref> especially with reference to VIPs with very high rank or [[spending]] power.<ref>[https://blogs.wsj.com/scene/2010/12/23/meet-the-vvip-very-very-important-people/ Meet the VVIP (Very Very Important People)], ''Wall Street Journal'' 23 December 2010. Requires subscription. Retrieved 15 June 2016</ref> It is used especially when anyone can buy VIP treatment, to distinguish people with especially high requirements.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Vritika|date=2013-09-27|title=Difference between VIP and VVIP|url=http://www.differencebetween.info/difference-between-vip-and-vvip|access-date=2020-07-07|website=www.differencebetween.info}}</ref>
