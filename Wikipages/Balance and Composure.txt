'''Balance and Composure''' was an American [[alternative rock]] band from [[Doylestown, Pennsylvania]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Artist Biography by Steve Leggett |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/balance-composure-mn0002462726/biography |title=Balance & Composure &#124; Biography |website=AllMusic |accessdate=January 11, 2014}}</ref> They formed in the winter of 2007, after the breakup of two local Doylestown bands.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/bands/balanceandcomposure |title=Balance and Composure |publisher=Punknews.org |date= |accessdate=January 11, 2014}}</ref>

The band's music was often suggested having similarities to [[Title Fight]], [[Brand New (band)|Brand New]], and [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nosleeprecords.com/artist/balance-and-composure |title=Balance And Composure |publisher=No Sleep Records |accessdate=January 11, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131103182119/http://www.nosleeprecords.com/artist/balance-and-composure |archive-date=November 3, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> They released three studio albums and two EPs over the course of their career. Their second album ''[[The Things We Think We're Missing]]'' reached number 51 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], number 10 on the [[Independent Albums]], number 13 on the Modern Rock/[[Alternative Albums]] and number 16 on the [[Rock Albums]] charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/the-things-we-think-were-missing-mw0002563652/awards |title=The Things We Think We're Missing - Balance & Composure &#124; Awards |website=AllMusic |date=September 9, 2013 |accessdate=January 11, 2014}}</ref>

On December 14, 2017, on Taylor Madison's ''Strange Nerve'' podcast, vocalist Jon Simmons announced that their upcoming anniversary tour would be their last.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/balance_and_composure_announce_split|title=Did Balance And Composure split up? - News - Alternative Press|last=Dickman|first=Maggie|date=December 14, 2017|website=Alternative Press|language=en|access-date=2018-05-21}}</ref> On January 14, 2019 a press release went out on the band's [[Twitter]] account announcing a farewell tour. Six dates were confirmed, saying that "These shows are the only shows we have planned in our future, we would love if you joined us one last time."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/balance-and-composure-announce-final-tour/|title=Balance and Composure Announce Final Tour|last=Blais-Billie|first=Braudie|date=January 15, 2019|website=Pitchfork|access-date=2020-02-14}}</ref> The band later added extra dates due to high demand.
