'''Noah Gale''', professionally known as '''Ro Ransom''', is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer from [[Manhattan]], [[New York City|New York]]. He is currently signed to Same Plate Entertainment and [[Sony Music]]. He had a breakthrough hit with his 2016 single "See Me Fall". His most recent project, an EP entitled ''Possessed'', was released in September 2018.

==Early life==
Noah Gale<ref name="HNHHShoot">{{cite news | last=Lilah| first=Rose| title =Shooting The Shit With Ro Ransom | work=HotNewHipHop| date =12 November 2014 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/shooting-the-shit-with-ro-ransom-news.12831.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> was born in [[Queens]], New York and grew up in the [[Harlem]] neighborhood of Manhattan.<ref name="Chiefers">{{cite news | title =Exclusive: Meet Ro Ransom The New York Artist Who Wants All The Smoke| work=Daily Chiefers| date =17 December 2018 | url =https://dailychiefers.com/meet-ro-ransom/| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref>

He wrote his first rap in sixth grade<ref name="Fest">{{cite news | title =Meet Ro Ransom, The Harlem Rapper Defying Hip-Hop’s Norms| work=Rapfest| date =15 October 2018 | url =http://therapfest.com/meet-ro-ransom-harlem-rapper-defying-hip-hops-norms/| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> and began releasing music at age 15 in 2008 using the stage name Nero.

==Career==
In 2011, Gale began using the stage name Ro Ransom to avoid confusion with an EDM group also named Nero.<ref name="Hype2011">{{cite news|last=Morris|first=Matt|date=30 October 2011|title=Ro Ransom - Even The Devil May Cry|work=HypeBeast|url=https://hypebeast.com/2011/10/ro-ransom-even-the-devil-may-cry|access-date=4 September 2019}}</ref> He had also considered "Young Coyote" as his pseudonym.<ref name="HNHHShoot"/> In 2012, he released a remix of the [[Purity Ring (band)|Purity Ring]] song "Odebear". He followed that with his first mixtape using the Ro Ransom moniker, ''Ransomnia''. That collection featured the single "Deadman Wonderland"<ref name="ComplexRansomnia">{{cite news | last=Isenberg| first=Daniel| title =Mixtape: Ro Ransom "Ransomnia"| work=Complex| date =2 July 2012 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2012/07/mixtape-ro-ransom-ransomnia| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> and guest appearances from [[Trae tha Truth]], Casey Veggies, 10ille, Chuck Inglish, AraabMuzik and Ricky Hil.<ref name="HNHHRansomnia">{{cite news | last=Lilah| first=Rose| title =Ro Ransom - Ransomnia | work=HotNewHipHop| date =3 July 2012 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-ransomnia-mixtape.57951.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> In 2013, Ransom released several songs, including "No Fucks Given" (using an [[Eminem]] sample),<ref name="HNHHNFG">{{cite news | last=Lilah| first=Rose| title =Ro Ransom - No Fucks Given | work=HotNewHipHop| date =7 January 2013 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-no-fucks-given-song.1165695.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> "Forgiveness",<ref name="ComplexForgive">{{cite news | last=Josephs| first=Brian| title =Listen: Ro Ransom "Forgiveness"| work=Complex| date =24 May 2013 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2013/05/listen-ro-ransom-forgiveness| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> "Lesbian Girl" (using an [[Aaliyah]] sample),<ref name="ComplexLesbian">{{cite news | last=Josephs| first=Brian| title =Listen: Ro Ransom "Lesbian Girl"| work=Complex| date =26 May 2013 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2013/05/listen-ro-ransom-lesbian-girl| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> and "Ebony & Ivory".<ref name="ComplexEI">{{cite news | last=X| first=Dharmic| title =Video: Ro Ransom "Ebony And Ivory"| work=Complex| date =30 May 2013 | url =https://www.complex.com/music/2013/05/video-ro-ransom-ebony-and-ivory| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref>

In October 2014, Ransom released his second mixtape, ''Ro Ransom is the Future''. The collection featured the single "Anaconda Vise". It had two bonus discs, one with extra songs and a third disc containing a collection a freestyles, only released to his fanclub.<ref name="HNHHRRITF">{{cite news | last=Lilah| first=Rose| title =Ro Ransom - Ro Ransom Is The Future| work=HotNewHipHop| date =30 October 2014 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-ro-ransom-is-the-future-new-mixtape.115504.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> In August 2015, he released the single "See Me Fall" (featuring Kensei Abbot)<ref name="HNHHSee">{{cite news | last=Goddard| first=Kevin| title =Ro Ransom - See Me Fall Feat. Kensei Abbot| work=HotNewHipHop| date =17 August 2015 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-see-me-fall-feat-kensei-abbot-new-song.1965919.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> which would go on to become a breakthrough hit for Ransom.<ref name="WSUM">{{cite news | last=Adams| first=Zach| title =Ro Ransom Interview| publisher=WSUM| date =19 July 2018 | url =https://wsum.org/2018/07/19/ro-ransom-interview/| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> He released a music video for the song that December,<ref name="FaderSee">{{cite news|last=Yoo|first=Noah|date=8 December 2015|title=Ro Ransom Shares The Video For "See Me Fall" ft. Kensei Abbot|work=The Fader|url=https://www.thefader.com/2015/12/08/ro-ransom-see-you-fall|access-date=4 September 2019}}</ref> and a remixed version by Y2K came out in March 2016.<ref name="HNHHY2K">{{cite news | last=Goddard| first=Kevin| title =Ro Ransom - See Me Fall (Y2K Remix) | work=HotNewHipHop| date =10 March 2016 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-see-me-fall-y2k-remix-new-song.1968891.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> Together, both versions of the song have accumulated over 100 million streams across multiple platforms.<ref name="Chiefers"/>

Throughout 2016, Ro also released several other tracks including "Talk Too Much",<ref name="FaderTalk">{{cite news | last=Yoo| first=Noah| title =Listen To Ro Ransom’s "Talk Too Much"| work=The Fader| date =10 January 2016 | url =https://www.thefader.com/2016/01/10/listen-to-ro-ransoms-talk-too-much| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> "Donuts" (featuring Kensei Abbot),<ref name="FaderDonuts">{{cite news | last=Darville| first=Jordan| title =Ro Ransom And Kensei Abbot Call Out Imposters On "Donuts"| work=The Fader| date =10 January 2016 | url =https://www.thefader.com/2016/01/10/listen-to-ro-ransoms-talk-too-much| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> "Invented Swag" (featuring Kensei Abbot and Nessly),<ref name="HNHHSwag">{{cite news | last=Lilah| first=Rose| title =Ro Ransom - Invented Swag Feat. Kensei Abbot & Nessly (Prod. By Misogi) | work=HotNewHipHop| date =26 September 2016 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-invented-swag-feat-kensei-abbot-and-nessly-prod-by-misogi-new-song.1971408.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> and "Doppelgänger".<ref name="HNHHDopp">{{cite news | last=Smith| first=Trevor| title =Ro Ransom - Doppelgänger | work=HotNewHipHop| date =28 November 2016 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-doppelg-nger-new-song.1972073.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> Early in 2017, he opened for [[Dua Lipa]] on her first North American tour.<ref name="Bay">{{cite news | last=Alleyne| first=Robert| title =Photos: Dua Lipa at Great American Music Hall| work=The Bay Bridged| date =21 March 2017 | url =http://thebaybridged.com/2017/03/21/photos-dua-lipa-great-american-music-hall/| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref><ref name="Alt">{{cite news | last=Sattar| first=Ramisha| title =Dua Lipa Concert Review| work=Alt Philanthropy| date =11 April 2017 | url =https://www.altphilanthropy.com/music-reviews/dua-lipa-concert-review| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> He secured a writing placement on [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]]'s ''[[American Teen (album)|American Teen]]'' album, co-writing the song "Let's Go".<ref name="Impose">{{cite news | last=G| first=Andre| title =Ro Ransom, "Stop The World"| work=Impose| date =21 August 2017 | url =https://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/ro-ransom-stop-the-world| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> In October 2017, he released a mixtape called ''Momentum'' while on tour with Connecticut rapper Witt Lowry. It featured the aforementioned "See Me Fall", "Prettiest", and "Doppelgänger" as well as the single "Prettiest" featuring Jazz Cartier.<ref name="HNHHMom">{{cite news | last=Smith| first=Trevor| title =Ro Ransom Is Still the Future On His "Momentum" EP | work=HotNewHipHop| date =29 October 2017 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-is-still-the-future-on-his-momentum-ep-new-mixtape.117570.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref>

In April 2018, Ransom released the song "Floetry" (featuring Kensei Abbot),<ref name="ImposeFlo">{{cite news | last=G| first=Andre| title =Ro Ransom, "Floetry"| work=Impose| date =30 April 2018 | url =https://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/ro-ransom-floetry| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> which was his first single to be released on Same Plate Entertainment, a joint venture label with Sony Music. In June of that year, he released the single "Wraith",<ref name="EMWraith">{{cite news | last=Steindorf| first=Jack| title =Ro Ransom releases another impressive single with "Wraith"| work=Earmilk| date =11 June 2018 | url =https://earmilk.com/2018/06/11/ro-ransom-releases-another-impressive-single-with-wraith/| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> a song influenced by 1990s [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] acts like [[TLC (group)|TLC]] and [[Destiny's Child]].<ref name="WSUM"/> The song would get an official remix from Y2K in January 2019.<ref name="HNHHWraithY2K">{{cite news | last=A.| first=Aron| title =Ro Ransom Taps Y2K For "Wraith (Remix)" | work=HotNewHipHop| date =21 January 2019 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/ro-ransom-taps-y2k-for-wraith-remix-new-song.1981747.html| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> In September 2018, Ransom released his first and only project with Same Plate/Sony, the EP ''Possessed''.<ref name="RevoltPossessed">{{cite news | last=Ichiki| first=Chase| title =Ro Ransom releases "Possessed" his most personal project yet| work=Revolt| date =14 September 2018 | url =https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/09/14/ro-ransom-releases-possessed-personal-project-07006a77ed| access-date =4 September 2019}}</ref> The collection featured eight tracks with "Floetry" and "Wraith" as singles.
