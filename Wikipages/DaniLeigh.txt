'''Danielle Leigh Curiel''' (born December 20, 1994), known professionally as '''DaniLeigh''', is an <!--Do not add "Dominican", as she was not born there, per [[MOS:ETHNICITY]]--> American singer, songwriter, rapper, dancer, and choreographer. Initially appearing as a backup dancer, Curiel began her career by posting covers of popular songs on YouTube before moving to Los Angeles, where she began choreographing for other artists and dancing. She made a career breakthrough after working with [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] on the music video for his single "[[Breakfast Can Wait]]". DaniLeigh was signed to [[Def Jam Records]] in 2017, releasing mixtapes and singles starting that year.<ref name = "Def Jam bio">{{Cite web |url=https://www.defjam.com/artists/danileigh |title=DaniLeigh |date=2019-01-03 |website=Def Jam |language=en |access-date=2019-06-30}}</ref>

==Early life==
Curiel was born on December 20, 1994, in [[Miami, Florida]], to  [[People of the Dominican Republic|Dominican]] parents.<ref name=AllMusic/> She was deeply interested in music during her youth, and cites [[Missy Elliott]], [[Aaliyah]], [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], and [[Rihanna]] as influences. She started dancing at the age of 12 and singing at the age of 14, though she was initially too shy to sing in front of others until she received positive feedback.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.axs.com/5-things-you-didn-t-know-about-danileigh-129994 |title=5 things you didn't know about DaniLeigh |date=2019-05-01 |website=AXS |language=en-US |access-date=2019-07-01}}</ref> Though she started posting vocal covers of songs such as [[Musiq Soulchild]]'s "So Beautiful" on [[YouTube]], her career did not officially start until she moved to Los Angeles, where she made connections and started off as a backup dancer to artists such as [[Pharrell Williams]] and [[Nelly Furtado]]. She also formed part of the pop duo Curly Fryz with her sister. DaniLeigh has a daughter with rapper DaBaby. <ref name=AllMusic>{{Cite web |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/danileigh-mn0003652279/biography |title=DaniLeigh — Biography & History |website=AllMusic |language=en-us |access-date=2019-07-01}}</ref>

==Career==
Curiel was asked by [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] to direct the video for his single "[[Breakfast Can Wait]]" when she was 18 years old.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ferreiro|first=Laura|title=Prince Enlists Unknown Teenager to Direct "Breakfast Can Wait" Video|url=http://music.yahoo.com/blogs/stop-the-presses/prince-enlists-unknown-teenager-direct-breakfast-wait-video-205700055.html|publisher=Yahoo Music|access-date=31 December 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Lewis|first=Randy|title= Watch Prince's new video 'Breakfast Can Wait'|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2013/oct/11/entertainment/la-et-ms-watch-prince-video-breakfast-can-wait-premiere-20131011|work=Los Angeles Times|access-date=31 December 2013|date=11 October 2013}}</ref> Following the video's success, Prince continued mentoring DaniLeigh until his death in 2016. She released her first single "D.O.S.E" in 2015, but her first successful single was "Play", released in 2017. The single is an empowerment song for women and [[Latino (demonym)|Latino]]s, and features Mexican-American rapper Kap G.<ref name = "Def Jam bio" />

After signing with [[Def Jam Records]], she released her first EP, ''Summer with Friends'', in 2017, followed up by ''The Plan'' in 2018. The latter features appearances by rappers [[YG (rapper)|YG]], [[Lil Yachty]], [[Lil Baby]], and [[YBN Nahmir]]. She later released a few more singles such as "Lil Bebe", which was remixed featuring vocals from Lil Baby, and "Easy", which was remixed featuring vocals from singer [[Chris Brown]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.bet.com/video/bet-jams/2019/music-videos/danileigh-featuring-chris-brown-easyremix.html?cid=facebook |title=DaniLeigh featuring Chris Brown - "Easy" (Remix) |website=BET.com |access-date=2019-07-01}}</ref> She has also made a cameo appearance in [[Megan Thee Stallion]]'s "[[Hot Girl Summer]]" music video. In late 2019, she choreographed for [[DaBaby]]’s music video “BOP on Broadway”.

In January 2021, the singer issued an apology after posting part of a song called "Yellow Bone" on social media, which was criticized for promoting [[colorism]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gray |first1=Arielle |title=DaniLeigh's Apology for the 'Yellow Bone' Controversy Only Makes It Worse |url=https://www.glamour.com/story/danileigh-apology-for-the-yellow-bone-controversy |website=Glamour |date=January 26, 2021}}</ref>

==Musical style==
DaniLeigh incorporates elements of [[hip hop]] and [[Music of Latin America|Latin music]] into her primarily [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]]-style music.<ref name = "Def Jam bio" /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://remezcla.com/features/music/danileigh-profile/ |title=Meet DaniLeigh, the Dominican Rapper & Singer With a Co-Sign From Prince |date=2018-08-08 |website=Remezcla |language=en-US |access-date=2019-07-10}}</ref>
