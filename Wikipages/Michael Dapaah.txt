'''Michael Dapaah''' (born 10 August 1991)<ref name="Michael Dapaah">{{cite web|title=Michael Dapaah.|work=[[Twitter]].|url=https://twitter.com/michaeldapaah/status/366241243753218049?lang=en|accessdate=10 August 2013}}</ref> is a British actor, rapper, and comedian best known for portraying the fictional rapper '''Big Shaq''' (also known as '''Roadman Shaq'''). He is also known for his [[mockumentary]] ''SWIL'' (Somewhere in London), which focuses on four characters and their journey to success.

== Early life ==
Michael Dapaah was born in 1991 in [[Croydon]], south [[London]], to first-generation [[Ghana]]ian immigrants.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.channel4.com/news/michael-dapaah-on-big-shaq-politics-growing-up-in-croydonand-what-prison-taught-him|title=Michael Dapaah on Big Shaq, politics, growing up in Croydon, and what prison taught him|last=Dotiwala|first=Jasmine|website=Channel 4 News|date=3 May 2018|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-01-18|archive-date=19 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119121444/https://www.channel4.com/news/michael-dapaah-on-big-shaq-politics-growing-up-in-croydonand-what-prison-taught-him|url-status=live}}</ref> While he was growing up, his parents wanted him to study science in order to become a doctor, but he was never interested in the subject, instead being more interested in acting and comedy. He also spent time in South Africa as a child. For a time he was in prison.<ref name=":0" /> He studied film, acting and theatre at [[Brunel University London|Brunel University]] and took a course from the National Youth Theatre.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/ikrd/here-is-how-michael-dapaah-made-mans-not-hot-and-the-ting|title=The Most Viral Man Of The Year Isn't A One-Hit Wonder|last=Dahir|first=Ikran|website=BuzzFeed|language=en|access-date=2019-01-18|archive-date=19 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119121036/https://www.buzzfeed.com/ikrd/here-is-how-michael-dapaah-made-mans-not-hot-and-the-ting|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
Dapaah began his career by garnering a social following among the British-African community for his brief comedic parts in shows like ''Meet the Adebanjos'', which deals with a Nigerian family in London.

In May 2017 along with friend and fellow Brunel graduate Marv Brown, he launched a [[mockumentary]] series on [[YouTube]] titled ''SWIL'' (Somewhere in London) which amassed over 1 million views in its first series.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/michael-dapaah-big-shaq-lyrics-mans-not-hot-rap-freestyle-radio1-bbc-1xtra-mc-quakez-shakez-story-2017-11?IR=T|title=The man behind 'Big Shaq' tells the story of his viral freestyle 'Man's Not Hot'|work=Business Insider|access-date=20 November 2017|language=en|archive-date=26 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171126045821/http://www.businessinsider.com/michael-dapaah-big-shaq-lyrics-mans-not-hot-rap-freestyle-radio1-bbc-1xtra-mc-quakez-shakez-story-2017-11?IR=T|url-status=live}}</ref> In this series he created the characters MC Quakez and Big Shaq.<ref name=":1" />

In 2020, Michael Dapaah started making the move to a fitness show called "Belly Must Go".<ref>[https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/lifestyle/article/michael-dapaah-belly-must-go-fitness-routine Michael Dapaah on his obsession with early gym starts]</ref>
