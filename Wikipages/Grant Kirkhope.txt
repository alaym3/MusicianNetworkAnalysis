'''Grant Kirkhope''' is a British video game composer, film composer and voice actor, who is best known for working on games such as ''[[GoldenEye 007 (1997 video game)|GoldenEye 007]]'', ''[[Banjo-Kazooie (video game)|Banjo-Kazooie]]'', ''[[Donkey Kong 64]]'', and ''[[Perfect Dark]]'', among many others. Kirkhope has been nominated for various [[BAFTA]], [[ASCAP]], and [[IFMCA]] awards for his contributions to video game music. He was also involved in casting the voice actors for the ''[[Viva Piñata (TV series)|Viva Piñata]]'' cartoon.<ref name="Interview with Grant Kirkhope">{{cite web|url=http://www.rare-extreme.com/?section=editorials&page=vpinatainterview.html |title=Interview with Grant Kirkhope |publisher=rare-extreme.com |access-date=31 May 2016 |url-status=unfit |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071019001948/http://www.rare-extreme.com/?section=editorials&page=vpinatainterview.html |archive-date=19 October 2007 }}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Kirkhope was born in [[Edinburgh, Scotland]], where his mother worked as a music hall dancer. Kirkhope's father was an avid music fan and exposed him to early influences such as [[Frank Sinatra]] and [[Glenn Miller]]. Kirkhope taught himself to play guitar at age 11 and is classically trained in the trumpet.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Wojnar|first=Zak|date=2017-04-11|title=From GoldenEye To Yooka-Laylee: Grant Kirkhope Reflects On His Career|url=https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2017/04/11/from-goldeneye-to-yooka-laylee--composer-grant-kirkhope-reflects-on-his-storied-career.aspx|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-14|website=Game Informer}}</ref><ref name="squareenix">{{cite web |url=https://www.squareenixmusic.com/features/interviews/grantkirkhope.shtml |title=Game Music :: Interview with Grant Kirkhope (May 2010) |publisher=Square Enix Music Online |author=Chris Greening |access-date=20 March 2018}}</ref> Kirkhope was brought up in [[Knaresborough]], North Yorkshire from the age of five, and attended [[King James's School, Knaresborough|King James's School]]. Kirkhope played in various bands after leaving the [[Royal Northern College of Music]], including [[Zoot and the Roots]] along with the saxophonist [[Snake Davis]]. Kirkhope also spent many years as part of "the Big Bad Horns" which were part of UK rock band [[Little Angels]].<ref name=":2" /> Kirkhope joined [[Rare (company)|Rare]] in October 1995,<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{cite tweet| user=grantkirkhope| number=1184110014455148544| title=Today’s the day I started at Rare in 1995| author=Grant Kirkhope| date=2019-10-15| url-status=live|}}</ref> he played for two bands called Syar and Maineeaxe where he played the guitar and already knew [[Robin Beanland]], another Rare composer.

==Career==
Kirkhope is a [[BAFTA]], [[ASCAP]] and [[IFMCA]]-nominated composer who has created the soundtrack for various video games.<ref name="auto">[https://www.linkedin.com/in/grantkirkhope] {{dead link|date=July 2020}}</ref> Kirkhope's score for ''[[Viva Piñata (video game)|Viva Piñata]]'' was nominated by BAFTA in the Original Score category in its 2007 awards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://awards.bafta.org/award/2007/games/original-score|title=2007 Games Original Score {{!}} BAFTA Awards|website=awards.bafta.org|language=en|access-date=2017-09-06}}</ref>

''[[Kingdoms of Amalur: Reckoning]]'' was nominated for Best Score for a Video Game or Interactive Media by the International Film Music Critics Association<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://filmmusiccritics.org/2015/03/knorr-cohen-curran-kirkhope-receive-ifmca-award-for-civilization-beyond-earth/|title=Knorr, Cohen, Curran, Kirkhope receive IFMCA Award for Civilization: Beyond Earth {{!}} IFMCA: International Film Music Critics Association|website=filmmusiccritics.org|language=en-US|access-date=2017-09-06}}</ref> and in the Cue Awards 2012 for Best Overall Score, Best Video Game Score and Grant was also nominated for Best Break Out Composer.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://cueawards.com/post/42880947347/2012-cue-award-nominees|title=2012 Cue Award Nominees|work=THE CUE AWARDS|access-date=2017-09-07|language=en}}</ref>

Kirkhope was nominated in the 2015 ASCAP Composer’s Choice Awards for his work on “Civilization: Beyond Earth”<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ascap.com/playback/2015/02/action/vote-2015-composers-choice.aspx|title=Vote for the 2015 ASCAP Composers’ Choice Awards|work=www.ascap.com|access-date=2018-05-26}}</ref> and also won the Best Original Score for a Video Game or Interactive Media 2015 from the International Film Music Critics Association for the same score.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://filmmusiccritics.org/awards-archive/ifmca-awards-2014/|title=2014 IFMCA Awards|date=2015-02-20|work=IFMCA: International Film Music Critics Association|access-date=2018-05-26|language=en-US}}</ref> ''[[Civilization: Beyond Earth]]'' also won Best Score: Video Game at the Cue Awards 2015.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://cueawards.com/|title=THE CUE AWARDS|website=THE CUE AWARDS|language=en|access-date=2017-09-06}}</ref> He was nominated in the 2016 ASCAP Composer’s Choice Awards for his work on ''Civilization: Beyond Earth – Rising Tide'' and was also nominated for Best Original Score for a Video Game or Interactive Media 2016 from the International Film Music Critics Association for the same score.<ref name=":0"/> ''Civilization: Beyond Earth – Rising Tide'' was also nominated for Best Score: Video Game at the Cue Awards 2016.<ref name=":1"/>
