'''Pale Waves''' are an English [[indie rock]] band from [[Manchester]], formed in 2014. It was founded originally as Creek when lead singer and guitarist Heather Baron-Gracie met drummer Ciara Doran while attending university in Manchester. The band was joined by guitarist Hugo Silvani and bassist Charlie Wood, completing the lineup.<ref>{{cite web |last=Prudham |first=Matthew |url=http://www.palatinate.org.uk/words-pale-waves/ |title=A few words with: Pale Waves |work=[[Palatinate (newspaper)|Palatinate]] |date=26 October 2017 |access-date=16 November 2017}}</ref>

After signing a record deal with [[Dirty Hit]] in 2017, Pale Waves released their debut single, "[[There's a Honey]]", followed by "[[Television Romance]]". In 2018, the band were ranked fifth in the [[Sound of...|BBC Sound of 2018]] poll and won the ''NME'' Under the Radar Award at the [[NME Awards]]. Pale Waves' debut EP, ''[[All the Things I Never Said (Pale Waves EP)|All the Things I Never Said]]'', was released in February 2018, followed by their debut studio album, ''[[My Mind Makes Noises]]'', on 14 September 2018. The band's second album, ''[[Who Am I? (Pale Waves album)|Who Am I?]]'', was released on 12 February 2021, and peaked at number three on the UK Albums Chart; the record also hit number one on the [[UK Independent Singles and Albums Charts|UK Independent Albums]] Chart.

==History==

===2014–2017: Formation and early career===

[[File:Pale Waves 12 06 2018 -6 (31483558177).jpg|thumb|Pale Waves are fronted by Heather Baron-Gracie.|alt=A woman performs at a concert; she is holding a guitar in one hand and a microphone in the other]]

Originally named Creek,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://music.mxdwn.com/2018/01/22/news/pale-waves-announce-new-all-the-things-i-never-said-ep-for-march-2018-release/ |title=Pale Waves Announce New All The Things I Never Said EP for March 2018 Release |website=mxdwn |date=22 January 2018 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> Pale Waves formed in 2014 when drummer Ciara Doran met Heather Baron-Gracie while attending [[British and Irish Modern Music Institute|BIMM Manchester]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Pollard |first=Alexandra |url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/03/06/pale-waves-band-all-the-things-i-never-said-new-years-eve-interview |title=Pale Waves is the goth pop hybrid you didn't know you needed |work=[[The Fader]] |date=6 March 2018 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bimm.co.uk/news/pale-waves-from-bimm-to-madison-square-working-with-the-1975/ |title=Pale Waves: From BIMM To Madison Square & Working With the 1975 |website=[[British and Irish Modern Music Institute|BIMM]] |date=1 November 2017 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> The two enlisted second guitarist Ben Bateman and bassist Ryan Marsden to complete the lineup, but they departed from the band shortly thereafter, with current bassist Charlie Wood and second guitarist Hugo Silvani replacing them.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |last=Thomas |first=Fred |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/pale-waves-mn0003604120/biography |title=Pale Waves {{!}} Biography |website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=16 November 2017}}</ref> The band recorded early demos with production team Sugar House that were released in 2015, catching the attention of the independent record label [[Dirty Hit]], to which they were signed in 2017.<ref name="allmusic"/>

The band's first single under Dirty Hit, "[[There's a Honey]]", was released on 21 April 2017 to widespread acclaim.<ref>{{cite web |last=Smith |first=Thomas |url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/pale-waves-1975-pop-theres-a-honey-1982705 |title=Pale Waves drop their 1975-produced pop banger 'There's A Honey' |work=[[NME]] |date=20 February 2017 |access-date=16 November 2017}}</ref> On 1 June 2017, the band played a sold-out show at [[Madison Square Garden]] supporting [[The 1975]] on their North American tour.<ref>{{cite web |last=Daly |first=Rhian |url=https://www.nme.com/photos/music-photos/1975-madison-square-garden-2083488 |title=The 1975 continue their world-conquering run with iconic Madison Square Garden show |work=NME |date=3 June 2017 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> The band's second single, "[[Television Romance]]", was released on 16 August 2017,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/Television-Romance/dp/B074N2GMXV |title=Television Romance by Pale Waves |website=[[Amazon (company)|Amazon]] (UK) |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> with its music video directed by The 1975 lead singer [[Matty Healy]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Stubbs |first=Dan |url=https://www.nme.com/features/pale-waves-1975-matty-healy-interview-2151614 |title=The 1975 and Pale Waves: Matty Healy introduces your favourite new pop band |work=NME |date=20 October 2017 |access-date=16 November 2017}}</ref> Healy later appeared on the cover of ''[[NME]]'' with Baron-Gracie for the 20 October 2017 issue of the magazine.<ref>{{cite web |last=Graves |first=Shahlin |url=http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/pale-waves/13423 |title=Heather Baron-Gracie & Matty Healy on the cover of NME, October 2017. |website=Coup De Main Magazine |date=20 October 2017 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> Pale Waves embarked on their first North American headlining tour in November and December 2017, playing 21 shows over four weeks.<ref name="altpress">{{cite web |last=Dickman |first=Maggie |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/pale_waves_new_years_eve |title=Pale Waves debut dreamy, dance-ready new song, "New Year's Eve"—listen |work=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |date=7 November 2017 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref>

The band headlined ''[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]'' magazine's Class of 2018 shortlist for its December/January issue.<ref name="DIY Class of 2018">{{cite web |url=https://diymag.com/2017/12/01/diy-class-of-2018-pale-waves-sigrid-shame-matt-maltese-out-now |title=The Class Of 2018 issue of DIY – featuring Pale Waves, Sigrid, Shame and more – is out now! |work=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |date=1 December 2017 |access-date=10 January 2018}}</ref> On 7 November 2017, the band released the single "New Year's Eve",<ref name="altpress"/><ref name="New Year's Eve">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/new-years-eve-single/1299123535 |title=New Year's Eve – Single by Pale Waves |website=[[iTunes Store]] (UK) |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> followed by "My Obsession" on 13 December 2017.<ref name="My Obsession">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/my-obsession-single/1318081381 |title=My Obsession – Single by Pale Waves |website=iTunes Store (UK) |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref>

===2018–2019: ''All the Things I Never Said'' and ''My Mind Makes Noises''===
Baron-Gracie announced on 4 January 2018 via [[Twitter]] that the band had started recording their debut album.<ref>{{cite web |last=Ackroyd |first=Stephen |url=https://www.readdork.com/news/pale-waves-have-started-recording-their-debut-album/ |title=Pale Waves have started recording their debut album |work=Dork |date=4 January 2018 |access-date=9 January 2018}}</ref> On 8 January, it was announced that the band had been placed fifth in the [[Sound of...|BBC Sound of 2018]] poll.<ref name="BBC Sound of 2018">{{cite web |last=Youngs |first=Ian |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-42455531 |title=BBC Sound of 2018: Pale Waves interview |website=[[BBC News]] |date=8 January 2018 |access-date=10 January 2018}}</ref> Speaking to the [[BBC]], Baron-Gracie talked about how the band's debut album is shaping up and its darker tone: "The songs we have out now are very influenced by romance. The album is me talking about a lot of my darker issues. I talk about a lot of things that go on in my mind rather than in my heart".<ref name="BBC Sound of 2018"/> "The Tide", the first song written by the band, was released on 1 February 2018 as the third single from the band's then-upcoming debut [[extended play]] (EP), ''[[All the Things I Never Said (Pale Waves EP)|All the Things I Never Said]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last=Graves |first=Shahlin |url=http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/pale-waves/13823 |title=Listen: Pale Waves re-release 'The Tide'. |website=Coup De Main Magazine |date=1 February 2018 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref><ref name="The Tide">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/the-tide-single/1337717280 |title=The Tide – Single by Pale Waves |website=iTunes Store (UK) |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref>

Pale Waves won the NME Under the Radar Award at the [[NME Awards]] 2018, and performed "There's a Honey" at the ceremony at London's [[O2 Brixton Academy|O<sub>2</sub> Academy Brixton]] on 14 February 2018.<ref name="NME Under the Radar">{{cite web |last=Trendell |first=Andrew |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/pale-waves-nme-radar-award-supported-hmv-vo5-nme-awards-2018-2241589 |title=Pale Waves win NME Under The Radar Award supported by HMV at the VO5 NME Awards 2018 |work=NME |date=14 February 2018 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> The band also received a nomination for Best Video for "Television Romance",<ref name="NME Best Video">{{cite web |last=Daly |first=Rhian |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/vo5-nme-awards-2018-nominations-vote-2220352 |title=Lorde, Kasabian, and Dua Lipa lead VO5 NME Awards 2018 nominations |work=NME |date=17 January 2018 |access-date=17 January 2018}}</ref> but lost out to [[The Big Moon]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Daly |first=Rhian |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/the-big-moon-best-music-video-supported-by-princess-yachts-vo5-nme-awards-2018-2241591 |title=The Big Moon win Best Music Video supported by Princess Yachts at the VO5 NME Awards 2018 |work=NME |date=14 February 2018 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref>

Pale Waves' debut EP, ''All the Things I Never Said'', was released digitally on 20 February 2018,<ref>{{cite web |last=Darville |first=Jordan |url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/02/20/pale-waves-all-the-things-i-never-said |title=Pale Waves share debut EP ''ALL THE THINGS I NEVER SAID'' |work=The Fader |date=20 February 2018 |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/all-the-things-i-never-said-ep/1341837407 |title=ALL THE THINGS I NEVER SAID – EP by Pale Waves |website=iTunes Store (UK) |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> followed by a 12-inch vinyl release on 16 March 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://store.dirtyhit.co.uk/*/*/ALL-THE-THINGS-I-NEVER-SAID-EP/5PV40000000 |title=All the Things I Never Said EP |website=[[Dirty Hit]] |access-date=22 April 2018}}</ref> On 6 April 2018, it was announced that the band had signed to [[Interscope Records]] in a joint venture with Dirty Hit to release music in the United States.<ref>{{cite web |last=Homewood |first=Ben |url=http://www.musicweek.com/labels/read/pale-waves-seal-joint-venture-with-interscope/071974 |title=Pale Waves seal joint venture with Interscope |work=[[Music Week]] |date=6 April 2018 |access-date=10 April 2018}}</ref> The third single from the band's debut album, "[[Kiss (Pale Waves song)|Kiss]]", following "There's a Honey" and "Television Romance", was released on 15 May 2018. "Noises" was released as the fourth single on 28 June 2018.

In an interview with ''NME'' magazine in early September 2018 the band revealed they are working on a new EP following the debut album. Baron-Gracie discussed how the new EP will lean towards "pop punk and rock'n'roll" and will touch on themes such as politics, acceptance and sexuality.<ref name="NMEIN">{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/nme-big-read-pale-waves-2375204-2 |title=Pale Waves – American gothic |website=NME |date=7 September 2018 |access-date=7 September 2018}}</ref> The band's debut album, ''My Mind Makes Noises'', was released on 14 September 2018 and reached number eight on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref name="vmag">{{cite web |last=Bell |first=Sadie |url=https://vmagazine.com/article/music-discovery-british-indie-pop-band-pale-waves-makes-cinematic-music-can-dance-cry/ |title=British Indie Pop Band Pale Waves Makes Cinematic Music You Can Dance & Cry To |work=[[V (American magazine)|V]] |date=12 October 2017 |access-date=16 November 2017}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Who Am I?''===
Speaking to ''[[NME]]'' while at [[Radio 1's Big Weekend]] in May 2019, Baron-Gracie updated on the progress of the EP, saying the band "[had] so many songs" and that they would cut the amount to "five or six tracks" with the remainder that "will probably go on the album".<ref name="NMEupdate">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/pale-waves-tell-us-about-going-pop-punk-on-their-new-ep-and-penning-hits-for-taylor-swift-2493012|title=Pale Waves on channeling Taylor Swift on their "pop punk"-inspired new EP: "We're aiming to take over the world" |website=NME|date=28 May 2019 | access-date=6 June 2019}}</ref> However, in September 2019 the band reported they will no longer be doing an EP, rather "diving deep into the second album".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.readdork.com/features/pale-waves-interview-sept19|title=Pale Waves talk "diving deep into the second album"|website=Readdork.com|date=3 September 2019|access-date=23 July 2020}}</ref> On 17 October 2019 the band won the Q Best Breakthrough Act at the [[Q Awards|Q Awards 2019]].<ref name="Q Awards"/>

In January 2020 Pale Waves released the song "SkinDeepSkyHighHeartWide" in collaboration with [[Lawrence Rothman]] for [[The Turning (2020 film)|The Turning]] official motion picture soundtrack.<ref name="Skin Deep"/> The band were involved in a road accident in March 2020 on their way to support [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]] in [[Berlin]]. The group's tour bus rolled off the road, leaving them trapped in the bus. Minor injuries were sustained in the accident and the tour bus burnt out. Ciara Doran on [[Instagram]] described the incident, "We honestly thought we were going to die. The injuries will heal but this will stay with me and everyone involved for forever."<ref name="BBC">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-51766817|title=Pale Waves 'grateful to be alive' after bus crash |work=BBC News |date=6 March 2020 | access-date=16 March 2020}}</ref>

The band's second album, ''[[Who Am I? (Pale Waves album)|Who Am I?]]'', was announced on 10 November 2020 along with its lead single "Change", which was named as [[Annie Mac]]'s [[Hottest Record in the World]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/pale-waves-announce-second-album-who-am-i-and-share-nostalgic-first-single-change-2813603|title=Pale Waves announce second album 'Who Am I?' and share nostalgic first single 'Change'|date=10 November 2020}}</ref> With both Baron-Gracie and Doran being members of the LQBTQ+ community, they both aimed for the album to reflect that, with Baron-Gracie stating in an interview with [[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] that she wanted to be "a voice for LGBTQ+ people". The album was partially recorded in Los Angeles with [[Rich Costey]] though [[COVID-19]] restrictions forced the band to return to the U.K. and finish the remainder of the album remotely. Of making an album during the pandemic, Baron-Gracie confessed that "for me, music and art is for people not to feel so alone and isolated. I want to be that person my fans look up to and find comfort in."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/11/11/pale-waves-change-who-m-i-release-date|title = Pale Waves announce second album details, share "Change"}}</ref> ''Who Am I?'' was released on 12 February 2021 and reached number 3 on UK Albums Chart.<ref name=ukalbumchart/>

==Musical style==
Pale Waves have been described as [[indie pop]],<ref name="vmag"/><ref>{{cite web |last=Dickman |first=Maggie |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/pale_waves_the_tide |title=Hear Pale Waves' new indie-pop anthem, "The Tide" |work=Alternative Press |date=31 January 2018 |access-date=11 April 2018}}</ref> [[indie rock]],<ref>{{cite web |last=Smith II |first=Geoffrey |url=http://www.sfweekly.com/slideshows/pale-waves-and-the-candescents-at-rickshaw-stop-wednesday-november-29th-2017/ |title=Pale Waves and The Candescents at Rickshaw Stop, Wednesday, November 29th, 2017 |work=[[SF Weekly]] |date=30 November 2017 |access-date=11 April 2018}}</ref> [[synth-pop]],<ref>{{cite web |last=Darville |first=Jordan |url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/03/19/pale-waves-heavenly-music-video |title=Pale Waves make puppets stylish in the "Heavenly" video |work=The Fader |date=19 March 2018 |access-date=11 April 2018}}</ref> [[dream pop]],<ref>{{cite web |last=Thompson |first=Stephen |url=https://www.npr.org/2018/03/01/588364556/the-austin-100-pale-waves |title=The Austin 100: Pale Waves |website=[[NPR Music]] |date=1 March 2018 |access-date=11 April 2018}}</ref> [[pop punk]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Kinnett |first=Tristan |url=https://music.mxdwn.com/2021/01/14/news/pale-waves-channel-pop-punk-in-new-video-for-easy/ |title=Pale Waves Channel Pop Punk in New Video for "Easy" |website=[[mxdwn.com]] |date=14 Jan 2021 |access-date=4 Feb 2021}}</ref> The band have cited artists such as [[The Blue Nile]], [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[The Cranberries]], [[Cocteau Twins]], [[The 1975]], [[Alanis Morissette]] and [[Avril Lavigne]] as influences.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/big-reads/pale-waves-cover-interview-2020-change-who-am-i-2821221|title = Pale Waves: "I've been hiding who I am for so long now. Baron-Gracie geeks}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Phoenix |first1=Stormy |title=Album Review: All the Things I Never Said by Pale Waves |url=https://www.kalx.berkeley.edu/news/album-review-all-things-i-never-said-pale-waves |access-date=13 November 2020 |quote=Heavily influenced by The Cure, The Cranberries, Cocteau Twins, and Avril Lavigne, Pale Waves are the next goth pop band}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Caminiti |first1=Kasey |title=Pale Waves Are Gothic Gems |date=15 May 2018 |url=https://dujour.com/culture/pale-waves-interview-new-song-release-kiss/ |access-date=13 November 2020 |quote=Drawing inspiration from 80’s punk bands like The Cure and stylistic aspirations from early punk rock princess Avril Lavigne, Pale Waves are creating their own musical identity}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/get-to-know/news/get-to-know-pale-waves |title=Get to Know: Pale Waves |publisher=[[MTV UK]] |date=19 October 2017 |access-date=16 November 2017}}</ref> Baron-Gracie has stated, "I love a lot of '80s artists like Prince and [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]]. They were amazing. I definitely looked up to Dolores O’Riordan. She has one of my favourite voices of all time. She gave off that attitude – she was totally herself. I loved her fashion sense, she was such a cool person".<ref>{{cite news |last=Power |first=Ed |date=17 September 2018 |title='I looked up to Dolores O'Riordan.
