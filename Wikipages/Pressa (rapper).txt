'''Quinton Armani Gardner''' (born May 10, 1996), professionally known as '''Pressa''', is a rapper and singer from [[Toronto]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_ca/article/vb3ny4/pressa-has-lived-a-hard-life-now-he-wants-peace|title=Pressa Has Lived a Hard Life struggling with becoming a boy she now he, Wants Peace|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Quinton Armani Gardner was born on May 10, 1996, in [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]] to a Filipino mother and a Jamaican father. He was five weeks old when his father was sentenced to 15 years in prison for [[second-degree murder]], which left his mother to care for him and his older brother.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_uk/article/vb3ny4/pressa-has-lived-a-hard-life-now-he-wants-peace|title=Pressa Has Lived a Hard Life, Now He Wants Peace|first=Sharine|last=Taylor|date=November 21, 2017}}</ref> He grew up in an area known as Driftwood in the [[Jane and Finch]] neighbourhood of Toronto.<ref name="TorontoSun">{{Cite web|url=https://torontosun.com/2016/04/29/up-and-coming-rappers-wanted-in-kidnapping/wcm/aa564b81-47e8-4624-bfb2-7fe57cbecb40|title=Kidnapping accused up-and-coming rapper &#124; Toronto Sun|date=April 30, 2016}}</ref>

== Career ==

Gardner rose to prominence after the release of his hit single "Novacane", which was produced by frequent collaborator [[Murda Beatz]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/pressa-and-murda-beatz-reunite-on-who-did-it-new-song.1984037.html|title=Pressa & Murda Beatz Reunite On "Who Did It"|website=HotNewHipHop|date=August 7, 2019|access-date=September 10, 2019}}</ref> In 2017, he opened for [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] on his [[Boy Meets World Tour]] despite being on bail for kidnapping allegations, and was featured in ''[[6ix Rising]]'', a [[Canadian]] [[documentary film]] about the [[Canadian hip hop|hip hop culture in Canada]]. Later that year, he gained significant attention for his unique flow on his single "Canada Goose" featuring [[Tory Lanez]], which was released in December.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pressa Roams the === 2019–present ===
In 2019, Gardner released ''Prestige'', an album named after his father, which debuted at the number 1 spot on the [[iTunes]] Canada [[hip hop music]] charts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hiphopcanada.com/pressa-prestige-debut-no-1/|title=Pressa debuts at No. 1 with Prestige album|date=April 16, 2019|website=HipHopCanada}}</ref> It featured appearances from Burna Bandz as well as Lil Richie. Pressa collaborated with British rapper [[Russ (UK rapper)|Russ]] and Taze on the single "Vida Loca", released in July 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hiphopcanada.com/russ-splash-pressa-taze-vida-loca/|title=Russ Splash enlists Pressa & Taze for "Vida Loca"|date=July 28, 2019|website=HipHopCanada}}</ref> Pressa's latest single, "O.T.M. (Out The Mud)", was released on February 7, 2020, via [[WorldStarHipHop]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Out The Mud: WSHH|url=https://www.worldstarhiphop.com/videos/video.php?v=wshhsBBWO5780qwQk4MX}}</ref>

In May 2020, he signed with [[Sony Music]] Canada, in conjunction with Blue Feathers Records, coinciding with his release of his single "96 Freestyle".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/05/pressa-96-freestyle|title=Premiere: Pressa Does Vegas in '96 Freestyle' Video|website=Complex}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
As of 2019, Gardner is currently living in [[Los Angeles]], California, having been relocated from Toronto.<ref name="TorontoSun" /> In April 2021, he began a relationship with [[Coi Leray]] another rising artist known as the daughter of Hip Hop mogul & former CEO of The Source Magazine [[Benzino]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/blueface-seemingly-disses-coi-lerays-new-boyfriend-pressa-news.133688.html|title = Blueface Seemingly Disses Coi Leray's New Boyfriend Pressa|website = HNHH|date = June 11, 2021}}</ref>
