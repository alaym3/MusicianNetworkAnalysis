'''Fenne Lily''' is a [[Dorset]]-born, [[Bristol]]-based [[Folk music|folk]] singer-songwriter. Her debut album, ''On Hold'', was released in 2018.

== Early life ==

Fenne Lily was born in [[Dorset]], United Kingdom. She has a brother,  to whom her song ''Brother'' is dedicated. She attended Beaminster School until the age of 16 and began playing concerts in Bristol at around the age of 15.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.ticketweb.uk/event/julia-jacklin-gorilla-tickets/9785715?REFERRAL_ID=tmfeed.htm | title = Fenne Lily biography | access-date = 7 October 2019 | year = 2019| publisher = Ticketweb| quote = "Though Fenne had been gigging in Bristol since she was 15, with her dad driving her up for shows two or three times a month, she finally moved to the city when she was 18, embarking on an art foundation course and discovering a love of photography"}}</ref>

== Career ==

===2016-2017===
In 2016, Lily began to play at music festivals around the UK. At [[T in the Park]] Festival, she was asked to play a session on the [[BBC Introducing]] stage.<ref>{{Cite web| url = https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p041l45g | title = BBC Introducing: Fenne Lily | access-date = 7 October 2019 | year = 2019| publisher = British Broadcasting Corporation}}</ref> That year, Fenne Lily also played at [[Larmer Tree Festival]], [[Latitude Festival]], [[Bluedot Festival]], [[Farmfestival]], [[Port Eliot]] Festival, Seven Layers,<ref>{{Cite web| url = https://7layersmusic.com/ | title = Seven Layers Festival, Amsterdam| access-date = 7 October 2019 | year = 2017| publisher = 7 Layers Music}}</ref> [[Sŵn]] Festival and at High and Lonesome Festival.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.highandlonesome.co.uk/ | title = High & Lonesome| access-date = 7 October 2019 | year = 2016| publisher = High & Lonesome}}</ref>

In 2017, Lily was awarded a Momentum grant by the Performing Rights Society.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://prsfoundation.com/grantees/momentum-fenne-lily | title = Momentum: Fenne Lily | access-date = 7 October 2019 | year = 2017| publisher = Performing Rights Society}}</ref> In late 2017, Lily supported Sivu, Siv Jakobsen and Paul Thomas Saunders on a UK tour.

===2018: ''On Hold''===
Her debut album, ''On Hold'', was self-released in early 2018. During this time, she also supported [[Dodie (singer)|dodie]] during her 2018 UK tour.

In August 2018, Lily played on the Rising stage at the [[Green Man Festival]] in Wales. A support slot with [[Lucy Dacus]] followed in the autumn,<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.theklabristol.co.uk/live/1048/lucy-dacus | title = THE THEKLA ARCHIVES: 2018 | access-date = 7 October 2019}}</ref> and the pair later toured the U.S. together. Lily was warmly received in the U.S.<ref>{{cite magazine | last = Marandola | first = Angela | date = 21 March 2019 | title = Concert Review: Lucy Dacus at Columbus Theatre | url = https://whatsuprhodeisland.com/2019/03/concert-review-lucy-dacus-at-columbus-theatre/ | magazine = What'sUpRhodeIsland| location = Rhode Island | publisher = What’sUpNetworks | access-date= 7 October 2019}}</ref> She returned to the UK in mid-2019.

===2020-present: ''BREACH''===
In 2020, Fenne Lily released "Hypochondriac," her first single on [[Dead Oceans]], followed by her second single "To Be A Woman Pt. 2" which was released in April 2020.<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.nme.com/news/music/fenne-lily-new-single-to-be-a-woman-pt-2-2656559 | title = Fenne Lily shares new single 'To Be a Woman Pt.2' | access-date = 6 February 2021}}</ref> In June 2020, she released her lead single titled "Alapathy" from her upcoming album as well as a video to go along with it and launched details for her album, ''BREACH''.<ref>{{Cite web| url= https://www.nme.com/news/music/fenne-lily-shares-new-single-and-music-video-alapathy-2694248|title=Fenne Lily shares new single and music video 'Alapathy'| website= NME| access-date= 15 February 2021}}</ref> On August 26, 2020, Fenne Lily released the single "Solipsism" with an accompanying video.<ref>{{Cite web| url= https://www.nme.com/en_asia/news/music/fenne-lily-drops-new-single-and-music-video-solipsism-2737919| title= Fenne Lily drops new single and music video 'Solipsism'| website= NME| access-date= 22 February 2021}}</ref>

On September 18, 2020, she released her second full-length album, ''BREACH''.
