'''Mikko Kuoppala''' (born 28 October 1981), professionally known as '''Pyhimys''' (in English ''The Saint''), is a Finnish rapper, songwriter and founder of the record company Yellowmic Records. He has gained success both as a solo artist and as a member of groups such as [[Teflon Brothers]] and [[Ruger Hauer]]. He is also working as a songwriter.

==Solo career==

Pyhimys was signed to [[Monsp Records]] in early 2006. Previously he had released independently some material, such as the ''Tapaus'' trilogy in 2004 (''Poikkeustapaus'', ''Ongelmatapaus'' and ''Rajatapaus''). His first official release was ''Ai, tähän väliin?'' EP in May 2006.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tapaus-trilogia|url=http://www.pyhimys.com/levyt/tapaus-trilogia/|publisher=Pyhimys / Yellowmic.|accessdate=6 February 2013}}</ref>

In September 2007, Pyhimys released his first official album ''Salainen Maailma''. The album received generally positive reviews, although some critics blamed it for being too long and for exhausting listeners with too much lyrical content.<ref>{{cite web|last=Tolonen|first=Arttu|title=PYHIMYS: Salainen Maailma|url=http://www.soundi.fi/levyarviot/pyhimys-salainen-maailma|publisher=Soundi}}</ref> His second album ''Tulva'' followed just six months later in March 2008, and in August 2008, Pyhimys collaborated with a fellow rapper [[Timo Pieni Huijaus]] on an album ''Arvoitus koko ihminen''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Arvoitus koko ihminen|url=http://www.pyhimys.com/levyt/arvoitus-koko-ihminen/|publisher=Pyhimys / Yellowmic.|accessdate=6 February 2013}}</ref>

In May 2011, Pyhimys returned with his third album ''Medium'', and in October 2011, he released ''Paranoid'', a 13-track album with each song named "Paranoid" and produced by [[Steen1]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Paranoid|url=http://www.pyhimys.com/levyt/paranoid/|publisher=Pyhimys / Yellowmic.|accessdate=6 February 2013}}</ref> In an interview later that year, Pyhimys said that he would be quitting his solo career, although he plans to continue performing with his two groups.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pyhimys lopettaa soolouransa|url=http://www.basso.fi/keskustelu/lehti/pyhimys-lopettaa-soolouransa/|publisher=Basso.fi|accessdate=6 February 2013|archive-url=https://archive.is/20120723014314/http://www.basso.fi/keskustelu/lehti/pyhimys-lopettaa-soolouransa/|archive-date=23 July 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Pyhimys teamed up with [[Heikki Kuula]] in February 2012 on an album ''Katuvisioita'' which they released under pseudonyms Perhosveitsi-Heikki and Lika-Aki.<ref>{{cite web|title=Katuvisioita|url=http://www.pyhimys.com/levyt/katuvisioita/|publisher=Pyhimys / Yellowmic.|accessdate=6 February 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131215111207/http://www.pyhimys.com/levyt/katuvisioita/|archivedate=15 December 2013}}</ref>

==Teflon Brothers and Ruger Hauer==

Pyhimys formed a group [[Teflon Brothers]] with Heikki Kuula and [[Voli (rapper)|Voli]] in 2006, and their first album ''T'' was released in July 2009. Teflon Brothers released a free mixtape ''Iso hätä'' in April 2010, and their second album ''©'' followed in August 2010. Their third album, ''Valkoisten dyynien ratsastajat'', was released in January 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lindfors|first=Sami|title=Veljekset trendintappajat|url=http://fifi.voima.fi/artikkeli/2012/kesakuu/veljekset-trendintappajat|publisher=Voima|accessdate=6 February 2013}}</ref>

Pyhimys is also a part of the group [[Ruger Hauer]], with Tommishock and [[Paperi T]]. Their first album ''Se syvenee syksyllä'' came out in November 2010, and in October 2012, they released a follow-up album, ''Erectus''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ruger Hauerin pojat: "Naiset etsivät tekosyitä antaa!"|url=http://www.como.fi/uutiset/ruger-hauerin-pojat-naiset-etsivat-tekosyita-antaa/|publisher=Como|accessdate=6 February 2013}}</ref>

==Songwriting==

Mikko Kuoppala is signed as a writer to [[Elements Music]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Topliners|url=http://www.elementsmusic.fi/writers/|publisher=Elements Music Oy|accessdate=18 February 2015}}</ref> He has written songs for artists such as [[Kasmir (singer)|Kasmir]], [[Arttu Wiskari]], [[Mikael Gabriel]] and [[Diandra (Finnish singer)|Diandra]].
