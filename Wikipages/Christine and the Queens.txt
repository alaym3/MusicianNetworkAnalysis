'''Héloïse Adélaïde Letissier''' ({{IPA-fr|elɔiz adelaid lətisje}}; born 1 June 1988), known professionally as '''Christine and the Queens'''<ref name="Konbini Queens">{{cite web|last1=Narduzzi|first1=Guillaume|language=fr|title=Chris est redevenue Christine and the Queens avec son EP surprise, La Vita Nuova|trans-title=Chris has become Christine and the Queens again with her surprise EP La Vita Nuova|url=https://www.konbini.com/fr/musique/ep-surprise-la-vita-nuova-chris-christine-and-the-queens/|newspaper=Konbini|date=28 February 2020|access-date=10 October 2020}}</ref> or occasionally simply '''Chris''',<ref name="Guardian_Cragg">{{cite web|last1=Cragg |first1=Michael|title=Christine and the Queens: 'I've just discovered sex, I can't stop yet!'|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/sep/22/christine-and-the-queens-ive-just-discovered-sex-i-cant-stop-yet|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=22 September 2018|access-date=12 September 2020}}</ref> is a French singer and songwriter. Born and raised in [[Nantes]], she started learning piano at the age of four and found inspiration in one of London's clubs while studying. Christine released a series of [[Extended play|extended plays]] throughout 2011–2013.

Her debut album, ''[[Chaleur humaine]]'' (2014), received critical acclaim, reached number 2 on both French and UK charts, and was certified diamond in France; it was also a best selling debut record in the United Kingdom. In 2018, she released her second album, ''[[Chris (album)|Chris]]'', to further critical acclaim. It was ranked album of the year by ''[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]'', ''[[The Guardian]]'', and ''[[The Independent]]'', and placed in top-ten of nine other year-end lists. "[[Damn, dis-moi|Girlfriend]]" was recognized by [[Time (magazine)|''Time'']] as song of the year. In reaction to her mother's death, Christine released in 2020 an EP [[La vita nuova (EP)|''La Vita Nuova'']], with some critics calling it her strongest work up to that point. ''Time'' again named her song, "[[People, I've Been Sad]]", the song of the year.

In 2016, Christine was ranked number 1 in ''[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]''{{'s}} list of most powerful and influential French people who "promote French genius", ahead of the country's president.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Van Renterghem|first=Marion|date=23 November 2016|title=Les confessions de Christine and the Queens|url=https://www.vanityfair.fr/culture/voir-lire/articles/aux-origines-de-christine-and-the-queens/48063|access-date=18 April 2021|website=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]|language=fr}}</ref> The next year, ''[[Forbes]]'' placed her on its list of 30 most influential and talented people under 30, and ''Time'' included her on its list of next generation leaders twice – in 2016 and 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Robehmed|first=Natalie|date=15 January 2017|title=30 Under 30 Europe: Kygo, Dev Patel, Michaela Coel And The Entertainment Class Of 2017|url=https://www.forbes.com/30-under-30-europe-2017/entertainment/#fa372be20308|access-date=17 April 2021|website=[[Forbes]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|last=|first=|date=|title=Next Generation Leaders|url=https://time.com/collection/next-generation-leaders/|access-date=18 April 2021|magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]}}</ref> Her accolades include four [[Victoires de la Musique|Victories of Music]] awarded by French [[Ministry of Culture (France)|Ministry of Culture]]. Christine and the Queens has been signed to the independent record label [[Because Music]] since 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.music-story.com/christine-and-the-queens/biographie |title=Christine and the Queens biographie |website=Music-story.com |language=fr |first=François |last=Alvarez |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141028215043/http://www.music-story.com/christine-and-the-queens/biographie |archive-date=28 October 2014}}</ref>

==Early life==
Héloïse Adélaïde Letissier was born on 1 June 1988 in [[Nantes]], France.<ref>{{cite news|last=Kourles|first=Gia|date=7 October 2016|title=Christine, a Pop Star Who Sings With Her Muscles|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/10/06/arts/dance/christine-and-the-queens-an-alter-ego-combining-music-and-dance.html}}</ref> Her father, Georges Letissier, taught English literature at the [[University of Nantes]] and specialises in [[Victorian era]] literature.<ref>{{cite web|title=Personnel de l'université – Letissier Georges|url=http://www.flce.univ-nantes.fr/letissier-g/0/fiche___annuaireksup/|access-date=8 December 2016|website=Université de Nantes – UFR Langue et Cultures Étrangères|language=fr}}</ref> Her mother, Martine Letissier, taught both French and Latin at a local middle school.<ref name=":02">{{Cite news|last=Snapes|first=Laura|date=4 September 2016|title=Christine and the Queens: 'I just want to shatter everything'|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|url=https://www.theguardian.com/global/2016/sep/04/christine-and-the-queens-heloise-letissier-interview|access-date=8 December 2016}}</ref> She began learning to play the [[piano]] at the age of four, learned [[Ballet|classical dance]] at five, and then [[Jazz|modern-jazz]].<ref name=":62">{{Cite web|last=Chabert|first=Chrystel|date=2 October 2014|title=Christine and The Queens : "Je ne veux pas choisir entre chanson française et pop-music anglaise"|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/culture/musique/pop/christine-and-the-queens-quot-je-ne-veux-pas-choisir-entre-chanson-francaise-et-pop-music-anglaisequot_3315833.html|access-date=21 February 2021|website=[[France Info|Franceinfo]] (Radio France)|language=fr}}</ref> Her parents recommended to her writers such as [[Sarah Waters]] and [[Judith Butler]], whose works served both as inspiration and reference in Letissier's youth.<ref name=":02" /> She attended [[Lycée Georges Clemenceau (Nantes)|Lycée Clemenceau]] learning theatre and then [[Lycée Fénelon, Paris|Lycée Fénelon]] secondary school in Paris learning literature.<ref name=":12">{{Cite web|date=5 September 2014|title=Christine and the Queens, nouvelle reine de la scène|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/musique/2014/09/05/03006-20140905ARTFIG00287-christine-and-the-queens-la-reine-de-la-scene.php|access-date=21 February 2021|website=[[Le Figaro]]|language=fr}}</ref> She later studied at the Department of Arts of [[École normale supérieure de Lyon]] (ENS Lyon) and also studied [[drama]] at the Lyon Regional Conservatory ([[:fr:Conservatoire à rayonnement régional de Lyon|fr]]).<ref name=":12" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Arrêté du 5 février 2009 portant nomination d'élèves à l'Ecole normale supérieure de Fontenay - Saint-Cloud (session 2008)|url=https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/jorf/id/JORFTEXT000020287892|access-date=21 February 2021|website=[[Légifrance]]|language=fr}}</ref>

In 2010, Letissier had to leave the theater conservatory, as she was depressed after a romantic break-up.<ref name=":52">{{Cite web|last=Van Renterghem|first=Marion|date=23 November 2016|title=Les confessions de Christine and the Queens|url=https://www.vanityfair.fr/culture/voir-lire/articles/aux-origines-de-christine-and-the-queens/48063|access-date=17 April 2021|website=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair France]]|language=fr}}</ref> She made a trip to London and was inspired by the work of local [[drag queen]] musicians, including [[Russella]], at the [[Soho]] nightclub Madame Jojo's.<ref name="Express20152">{{Cite news|last=Medioni|first=Gilles|date=13 February 2015|title=Videos: La voix royale de Christine and the Queens|language=fr|work=[[L'Express]]|url=http://www.lexpress.fr/culture/musique/videos-la-voix-royale-de-christine-and-the-queens_1650188.html}}</ref> The [[Queen (slang)|queens]] then became 'the Queens' in her stage name as a tribute.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Redon|first=Sarah|date=23 March 2015|title=Christine and the Queens : "Je suis bisexuelle"|url=https://www.terrafemina.com/article/christine-and-the-queens-je-suis-bisexuelle_a266643/1|access-date=21 February 2021|website=Terrafemina.com|language=fr}}</ref> As soon as she returned to France, she left the ENSL [[grande école]] in the middle of the second year to devote herself fully to the musical project now called "Christine and the Queens".<ref name="lemonde.fr2">{{cite news|last=Lecolant|first=Mathilde|date=20 July 2013|title=Christine & the Queens: "amener un peu de fantaisie ou de bizarre" dans le genre populaire|language=fr|newspaper=[[Le Monde]]|url=http://www.lemonde.fr/culture/article/2013/07/20/christine-the-queens-amener-un-peu-de-fantaisie-ou-de-bizarre-dans-le-genre-populaire_3450619_3246.html}}</ref> She dedicated many of her creations to them, and to all [[transgender]] individuals, describing her genre as "freakpop".<ref name="lemonde.fr2" />

==Career==
===2010–2013: EP releases and early live appearances===
Christine and the Queens gave her first recital at a small Lyon club in 2010. She released her debut [[extended play]], ''Miséricorde'', in 2011, with Marc Lumbroso ([[Jean-Jacques Goldman]]'s producer).<ref name=":52" /> Her second EP, titled ''Mac Abbey'', followed in 2012, with minor hits "Narcissus Is Back" and "[[Christine (Christine and the Queens song)|Cripple]]". The same year, she was the opening act for [[Lykke Li]], [[The Dø]], and [[Woodkid]]. Christine won the Best Discovered Act award, known as "Découverte", at the [[Printemps de Bourges]] music festival and also won the "Première Francos" award at the [[Les Francofolies de La Rochelle]] festival. Then she signed with the independent label [[Because Music]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Biographie et actualités de Christine & The Queens France Inter|url=https://www.franceinter.fr/personnes/christine-queens|access-date=21 February 2021|website=www.franceinter.fr|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Reynolds|first=Lisa|date=July 2017|title=Chaleur humaine by Christine and the Queens|url=https://issuu.com/eilemagazine/docs/july2017_final|magazine=EILE Magazine|language=en|volume=4|issue=12|pages=22–25|access-date=13 April 2020|via=[[Issuu]] Issuu.com}}</ref><ref name=":52" />

In 2013, Christine was the opening act for [[Lilly Wood and the Prick]] and [[Gaëtan Roussel]]. On 3 June, she released the single, and also an [[Extended play|EP]] of the same name, titled "[[Nuit 17 à 52]]", which garnered her first charting on the official French [[SNEP]] albums chart. The song was the first single of her forthcoming studio album.

===2014–2017: ''Christine and the Queens''===
{{Main|Chaleur humaine}}
[[File:Festival des Vieilles Charrues 2014 - Christine and the Queens - 001.jpg|thumb|right|Christine at the [[Vieilles Charrues Festival]] in 2014|upright=0.9]]
Christine and the Queens' debut album, ''[[Chaleur humaine]]'', was first released in [[France]] on 2 June 2014.<ref name=":02" /> She was almost the sole writer and producer; the track "[[Les Paradis perdus|Paradis perdus]]" is a cover of a 1973 French song. Besides "Nuit 17 à 52", three singles were released: "[[Saint Claude (song)|Saint Claude]]", "[[Christine (Christine and the Queens song)|Christine]]", and "Paradis Perdus". "Christine" is the French version of "Cripple" earlier released in 2012. After the album reached number 2 on the French chart and was certified diamond there,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Les certifications|url=https://snepmusique.com/les-certifications/|access-date=21 February 2021|website=SNEP|language=fr|quote='Type "Christine and the Queens" in the search field and then press <Enter>'}}</ref> Christine toured France.<ref name=":62" />

For the [[United States|American]] market, the single "[[Christine (Christine and the Queens song)|Tilted]]", English version of "Cripple"/"Christine", was released on 3 March 2015 under the [[Neon Gold Records]] label. Christine then toured in the US for promotion with [[Marina and the Diamonds]]. Exclusively for this market, [[Saint Claude (song)|''Saint Claude'']] EP was released on 14 April, including five songs from ''Chaleur humaine'' in English versions. On 16 October, the full album, entitled ''Christine and the Queens'', was released via [[Because Music]].<ref name=":22">{{Cite web|title=CHRISTINE AND THE QUEENS by Christine and the Queens|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/christine-and-the-queens/christine-and-the-queens|access-date=21 February 2021|website=[[Metacritic]]}}</ref> Many tracks were reworked with English lyrics and revamped beats by producer Ash Workman.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Williams|first=Nick|date=16 October 2015|title=Christine & The Queens' Heloise Letissier Reimagines Her French Debut for Stateside Appeal: Album Review|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/review/6730790/christine-the-queens-self-titled-album-review|access-date=21 February 2021|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|language=en}}</ref> Two tracks were replaced with three new songs, and two of these new songs were collaborations – "[[Jonathan (song)|Jonathan]]" with [[Perfume Genius]], and "[[No Harm Is Done]]" with rapper Tunji Ige. These two new collaborations were released as additional singles, the latter on 11 September, and the former on 16 October.<ref>{{Cite web|date=9 March 2018|title=Prepare to Fall for Christine And The Queens: Here's Her Single "Tilted" - Noisey|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/6wq5gm/christine-and-the-queens-tilted|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180309100236/https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/6wq5gm/christine-and-the-queens-tilted|url-status=dead|archive-date=9 March 2018|access-date=21 February 2021}}</ref> On 11 November, Christine was warmly received in her first performance in large venue, the [[Webster Hall]], New York; the next day, she appeared on ''[[The Daily Show]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=12 November 2015|title=À la conquête de l'Amérique, Christine and The Queens triomphe à New York|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/musique/2015/11/12/03006-20151112ARTFIG00110--la-conquete-de-l-amerique-christine-and-the-queens-triomphe-a-new-york.php|access-date=24 April 2021|website=[[Le Figaro]]|publisher=[[Agence France-Presse|AFP]]|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The Daily Show All Seasons|url=https://www.thetvdb.com/series/the-daily-show/seasons/all|access-date=18 December 2018|website=the tvdb}}</ref>'' In 2015 top-ten lists by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]],'' "Tilted" was included as one of best songs of the year.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last1=Feeney|first1=Nolan|last2=Johnston|first2=Maura|date=1 December 2015|title=Top 10 Everything of 2015 - Top 10 Best Songs|url=https://time.com/collection-post/4132832/top-10-best-songs-2/|access-date=21 February 2021|magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]}}</ref> [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] listed the song in its 2010 best-of, "defining tracks of the decade" list at number 106.<ref name=":42">{{cite web|title=The 200 Best Songs of the 2010s|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/the-200-best-songs-of-the-2010s/|access-date=7 October 2019|work=Pitchfork}}</ref> At the end of the year, she was back home, and, on 10 December, [[Madonna]] invited Christine to dance with her on stage during her concert at the [[AccorHotels Arena|Bercy Arena]], Paris.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2 March 2016|title=Madonna donne une fessée à Christine and The Queens sur scène|url=https://video.lefigaro.fr/figaro/video/madonna-donne-une-fessee-a-christine-and-the-queens-sur-scene/4656648580001/|access-date=24 April 2021|website=[[Le Figaro|Figaro Live]]|language=fr}}</ref>

[[File:Music - Christine and the Queens - Webster Hall (22560420068).jpg|thumb|left|Christine and the Queens at the [[Webster Hall]] in 2015|upright=0.9]]

===2018–2019: ''Chris''===
{{Main|Chris (album)}}
For her second studio album, Christine and the Queens had early sessions with both [[Mark Ronson]] and [[Damon Albarn]], but eventually decided to work alone, with [[Cole M.G.N.]] as co-producer on some tracks.<ref name="Guardian_Cragg" /> "Confident in her decision to go it alone, she cut her hair short and started again as Chris. 'I wanted to risk it all,' she said, smiling."<ref name="Guardian_Cragg" /> In April 2018, she announced a tour across North America and Europe, planned for the autumn.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Daramola|first=Israel|date=23 April 2018|title=Christine and the Queens Announce Fall Tour|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8372408/christine-and-the-queens-announce-fall-tour|access-date=22 May 2018|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> In July, Christine also announced details of an upcoming album titled ''[[Chris (album)|Chris]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=5 July 2018|title=Christine And The Queens explains why she had to become 'Chris' on new album|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/christine-queens-explains-become-chris-new-album-2350632|access-date=21 February 2021|website=[[NME]] - Music, Film, TV, Gaming & Pop Culture News|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref name="pitchfork2">{{cite web|last=Blais-Billie|first=Braudie|date=5 July 2018|title=Christine and the Queens Announces New Album ''Chris'', Shares Song and Video|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/christine-and-the-queens-announces-new-album-chris-shares-song-and-video/|access-date=5 July 2018|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref> It was preceded by the release of two singles, each in an English and French version: [[Damn, dis-moi|"Girlfriend"/"Damn, dis-moi"]] on 17 May, and [[Doesn't Matter (Voleur de Soleil)|"Doesn't Matter"/"Doesn't Matter (Voleur de soleil)"]] on 5 July. Later, she also released the singles: English-language "[[5 dols|5 Dollars]]" on 16 August, and French-language "La Marcheuse" on 23 August.

''Chris'' was released on 21 September 2018 and received universal critical acclaim.<ref>{{Citation|title=Chris by Christine and the Queens|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/chris/christine-and-the-queens|language=en|access-date=21 February 2021}}</ref> ''[[AllMusic|Allmusic]]'' editors summarized: "the singer/songwriter's triumphant second album borrows from '80s R&B and questions gender roles, engaging minds, hearts, and bodies along the way."<ref>{{Cite web|title=AllMusic 2018 Year in Review {{!}} Best of 2018|url=https://www.allmusic.com/year-in-review/2018|access-date=17 April 2021|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref> Robert Steiner of ''[[The Boston Globe]]'' called the album "a refreshing, empowering record" and complimented its "stellar production and contagiously danceable jams", as well as Letissier's "engrossing lyricism". Although he felt that the album "loses steam" in its second half, Steiner named "The Walker" as a highlight for its "poignant" portrayal of a victim of [[domestic violence]].<ref name=":32">{{Cite web|last=Steiner|first=Robert|date=19 September 2018|title=On Christine and the Queens' 'Chris,' revelations you can dance to|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/2018/09/19/christine-and-queens-chris-revelations-you-can-dance/42Ipej4AL4W8MDtwDEfiUI/story.html|access-date=21 February 2021|website=[[The Boston Globe]]|language=en}}</ref> ''Chris'' peaked at number 2 on the [[Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique|French]] and at #3 on the [[UK Albums Chart|UK]] chart. The record featured at the top of many year-end best-of lists, ranked number one by ''[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]'', ''The Guardian'', and ''The Independent'', and placed in the top-five of five other lists, for a total of 12 positions in top-ten. "Girlfriend" was named song of the year by ''Time'' .<ref>{{Cite magazine|date=17 November 2018|title=These Are the Best Songs of 2018|url=http://time.com/5455058/best-songs-2018/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181117003403/http://time.com/5455058/best-songs-2018/|archive-date=17 November 2018|magazine=TIME}}</ref> In the 'top pop albums of the 2010s' listings, ''Chris'' was ranked #15 by ''[[Consequence (publication)|Consequence]]'', ahead of [[Rihanna]]'s ''[[Anti (album)|Anti]]'' and [[Ariana Grande]]'s ''[[Thank U, Next]]'', and #24 by ''[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]'', ahead of [[Lady Gaga]]'s ''[[Born This Way (album)|Born This Way]]'' and [[Adele]]'s ''[[25 (Adele album)|25]]''.

===2020–present: ''La vita nuova''===
{{Main|La vita nuova (EP)}}
On 5 February 2020, Christine released "[[People, I've Been Sad]]", the first single from her forthcoming extended play. ''[[La vita nuova (EP)|La vita nuova]]'' ('The New Life'), containing five tracks and one bonus track, was released on 27 February. Again, she was the sole writer, with Ash Workman as co-producer.<ref name=":72">{{cite web|date=27 February 2020|title=Christine and the Queens Drops Surprise ''La Vita Nuova'' EP Feat. Caroline Polachek|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2074987/christine-and-the-queens-surprise-la-vita-nuova-ep-caroline-polachek/music/album-stream/|access-date=17 March 2020|work=[[Stereogum]]}}</ref> The singer simultaneously released a conceptual short film directed by her longtime collaborator Colin Solal Cardo. It takes place at the famed historical Paris landmark [[Palais Garnier]], features Christine and a group of dancers dancing to the songs from the EP, and concludes with a guest appearance from featured artist [[Caroline Polachek]].<ref name=":72" /> The EP was met with widespread critical acclaim upon its release.<ref>{{Citation|title=La vita nouva [EP] by Christine and the Queens|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/la-vita-nouva-ep/christine-and-the-queens|work=[[Metacritic]]|access-date=18 April 2021}}</ref> ''NME'' 's El Hunt gave a rave review, summarizing; "conceptually, 'La Vita Nuova' is an astonishing feat – but even better than that, it also oozes an intensity of feeling that punches right in the gut."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hunt|first=El|date=28 February 2020|title=Christine and The Queens' – 'La Vita Nuova' review: surprise EP is an astonishing and emotional sucker-punch|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/christine-and-the-queens-ep-la-vita-nuova-caroline-polacheck-review-2615947|access-date=18 April 2021|website=[[NME]]}}</ref> ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]'' called the EP "arguably [her] best work to date".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Aswad|first=Jem|date=27 February 2020|title=Christine and the Queens' 'La Vita Nuova': EP Review|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/reviews/christine-and-the-queens-la-vita-nuova-ep-review-1203518306/|access-date=21 February 2021|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|language=en-US}}</ref> "People, I've Been Sad" was recognized by ''Time'' as song of the year and also placed in the top-three of year-end critics' lists by ''[[NPR]]'', ''Pitchfork'', and ''The Guardian''.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last1=Bruner|first1=Raisa|last2=R. Chow|first2=Andrew|date=30 November 2020|title=The 10 Best Songs of 2020|url=https://time.com/5914984/best-songs-2020/|access-date=20 April 2021|magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]}}</ref> The short film was included in ''Pitchfork''<nowiki/>'s list of "The 20 Best Music Videos of 2020"; Ryan Dombal wrote: "this is art-pop cinema that's both steeped in history and gloriously unafraid to blaze its own way."<ref>{{Cite web|date=17 December 2020|title=The 20 Best Music Videos of 2020|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/lists-and-guides/best-music-videos-2020/|access-date=20 April 2021|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref> Two more singles were released from ''La vita nuova'': "I Disappear In Your Arms" on 8 June and [[La vita nuova (song)|the title track]] featuring Caroline Polachek on 14 August; an EP with remixes of the latter was released on 28 August.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Triscari|first=Caleb|date=11 August 2020|title=A.G. Cook to feature on Christine and the Queens' 'La Vita Nuova' remix collection|url=https://www.nme.com/en_au/news/music/a-g-cook-to-feature-on-christine-and-the-queens-la-vita-nuova-remix-collection-2724998|access-date=20 April 2021|website=[[NME]]}}</ref>

==Artistry==
===Lyrical and musical style===
Christine and the Queens prefers lyrics that are not immediately understandable. She explained that she enjoys "authors with difficult lyrics", such as [[Alain Bashung]].<ref name="Clique2">{{Cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rpU0HQVEi_M|title=Clique x Chris(-tine and the Queens)|date=20 September 2018|language=fr|quote=J'aime les auteurs de textes rugueux, il faut venir vers le texte. Quand on pense à Alain Bashung, ses textes sont une matière poétique qui se travaille, qui se mâche et qui se réfléchit.}}</ref> Her music has been labelled by music critics as [[Pop music|pop]],<ref name="AllMusic2">{{cite web|last=Collar|first=Matt|title=Christine and the Queens Bio|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/christine-and-the-queens-mn0003106289/discography|access-date=25 June 2017|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref> [[synthpop]],<ref name="pitchfork2" /> [[electropop]],<ref name="AllMusic2" /> [[indie pop]], [[experimental pop]],<ref name="lemonde.fr2" /> and [[art pop]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Snapes|first=Laura|title=Christine and the Queens Can't Stop Googling Weird Diseases|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/1231-christine-and-the-queens-cant-stop-googling-weird-diseases/|access-date=22 August 2016|website=Pitchfork|date=19 July 2016}}</ref> while she described her own work as "freakpop".<ref name="lemonde.fr2" />

===Influences===
Letissier emphasized the importance of her experience with Madame Jojo's [[Drag (clothing)|drag]] club in London: "These drag artists have become my friends, they've inspired my creativity. They gave me the idea of creating a character, inventing another silhouette, another way of being in this world. Before it was a musical project, Christine was for me the answer to how to live properly. The ''Queens'' in ''Christine and the Queens'' is my tribute to them. Without the queens, I wouldn't be here".<ref name="Terrafemina2">{{cite web|last=Redon|first=Sarah|date=23 March 2015|title=Christine and the Queens : "Je suis bisexuelle"|url=http://www.terrafemina.com/article/christine-and-the-queens-je-suis-bisexuelle_a266643/1|access-date=11 February 2017|website=[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]]|language=fr}}</ref>

She also said, "[she] does not want to choose between French music and English pop music" and takes influence from both.<ref>{{cite web|date=26 June 2014|title=Christine and the Queens : "Je ne veux pas choisir entre chanson française et pop-music anglaise"|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/culture/musique/pop/christine-and-the-queens-quot-je-ne-veux-pas-choisir-entre-chanson-francaise-et-pop-music-anglaisequot_3315833.html|access-date=23 July 2019|website=France Info|language=fr}}</ref> In a November 2013 interview with ''{{ill|Brain Magazine|fr}}'', she cited artists such as [[Christophe (singer)|Christophe]], [[David Bowie]] (especially his [[The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars|Ziggy Stardust]] [[Ziggy Stardust (character)|character]]<ref name=":05">{{Cite web|last=Brochen|first=Philippe|date=29 May 2014|title=Christine and The Queens, la vie scène {{!}} portrait|url=https://www.liberation.fr/musique/2014/05/29/christine-and-the-queens-la-vie-scene_1029715/|access-date=20 April 2021|website=[[Libération]]|language=fr}}</ref>), [[Kanye West]], [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Philip Glass]], [[T. Rex (band)|T. Rex]] as well as the ''[[Soul Train]]'' soundtracks as her musical influences. She also named [[Michael Jackson]] as her favourite male singer and "either [[Patti Smith]] or [[Kate Bush]]" as her favourite female singer.<ref>{{cite web|date=5 November 2013|title=Le Sojeans questionnaire avec Christine and the Queens|url=http://www.brain-magazine.fr/article/leche-vitrines/16318-Le-Sojeans-questionnaire-avec-Christine-and-the-Queens|website=Brain-magazine|language=fr|quote=Quelles sont tes influences musicales ? ''Les BO de Soul Train, Bowie et T-Rex, toute la discographie de Christophe, Kanye West et Kendrick Lamar, du contemporain (Philip Glass)... j'écoute beaucoup sans hiérarchiser''. Ton chanteur préféré ? ''Michael Jackson.'' Ta chanteuse préférée ? ''Patti Smith ou Kate Bush.''}}</ref> Other influences include [[Björk]],<ref name="Express20152" /> [[Beyoncé]], [[Daniel Balavoine]], [[Karin Dreijer|Fever Ray]], [[Frank Ocean]],<ref name=":05" /> [[Janet Jackson]], [[Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis]],<ref>{{cite web|date=4 August 2019|title=Christine and the Queens: 'I decided to stop apologising'|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-49206530|access-date=21 August 2019|website=BBC News}}</ref> [[Mylène Farmer]],<ref>{{cite web|date=22 August 2018|title=Chris(tine) évoque son admiration pour Mylène Farmer, "un personnage fascinant"|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Christine-and-the-Queens/news-107796.html#b37q5rhOW63LMeVF.99|access-date=23 July 2019|website=Charts in France|language=fr}}</ref> [[Joe Jackson (musician)|Joe Jackson]], [[Lou Reed]], and [[Serge Gainsbourg]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Tang|first=Estelle|date=8 June 2016|title=The 5 Albums that Changed Christine and the Queens' Life|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/music/a36934/christine-and-the-queens-5-favorite-albums/|access-date=23 July 2019|website=[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]]}}</ref>
