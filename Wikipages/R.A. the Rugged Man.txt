'''R.A. Thorburn''', better known by his stage name '''R.A. the Rugged Man''', is an American [[rapper]] and producer.<ref>[http://nature-sounds.net/artists/r-a-the-rugged-man/ R.A. the Rugged Man]. Nature-sounds.net. Retrieved on May 30, 2011.</ref> He began his music career at age 12, building a reputation locally for his lyrical skills. Thorburn signed to major label [[Jive Records]] at age 18, but his debut album, ''Night of the Bloody Apes'', was never released.<ref name="villagevoice">{{cite web|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/2009-11-10/music/r-a-the-rugged-man-abides//|title=R.A. the Rugged Man Abides|author=Ben Westhoff|date=November 10, 2009|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref>

Since then, he has worked with the likes of [[Mobb Deep]], [[Wu-Tang Clan]], [[Kool G Rap]], and [[Notorious B.I.G.]], as well as producers [[Erick Sermon]], [[Trackmasters]], [[DJ Quik]], [[The Alchemist (musician)|The Alchemist]], [[J-Zone]], and [[Ayatollah (record producer)|Ayatollah]].{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} He was featured on all three of [[Rawkus]]'s Soundbombing albums, as well as the platinum-selling [[WWF Aggression]] album. After a brief stint on [[Capitol Records]] (during which he recorded another unreleased album, ''American Lowlife''), Thorburn signed with independent label [[Nature Sounds]] and released his official debut, ''[[Die, Rugged Man, Die]]''.<ref name="aboveground">[http://abovegroundmagazine.com/news/culture-news/03/03/r-a-the-rugged-man-releases-video-based-on-experience-with-jive-records/ Above Ground Magazine: ''R.A. The Rugged Man Releases Video Based On Experience With Jive Records'']</ref> In 2013, Thorburn released his second album, ''[[Legends Never Die (R.A. the Rugged Man album)|Legends Never Die]]'', via Nature Sounds.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kevinnottingham.com/2013/02/20/r-a-the-rugged-man-the-peoples-champ-prod-by-apathy/|title=R.A. The Rugged Man: The People's Champ [prod by Apathy] - KevinNottingham.com|author=Jeremiah|work=KevinNottingham.com|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref>

In addition to his hip hop career, Thorburn has been active as boxing commentator and film critic.{{Citation needed|date=August 2020}} He has contributed to [[Vibe (magazine)|''Vibe'']], [[King (magazine)|''King'']], [[Complex (magazine)|''Complex'']], ''Rides'', ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'',<ref name="xxlmag.com">[http://www.xxlmag.com/bloggers/2009/10/it%E2%80%99s-a-beautiful-thing-being-ra-the-rugged-man/ It's A Beautiful Thing Being R.A. the Rugged Man] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110114091456/http://www.xxlmag.com/bloggers/2009/10/it%E2%80%99s-a-beautiful-thing-being-ra-the-rugged-man/ |date=January 14, 2011 }}. Xxlmag.Com. Retrieved on May 30, 2011.</ref> ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]'', ''[[The Ring (magazine)|The Ring]]'', among others.  He wrote and produced the cult film ''[[Bad Biology]]'', and also hosts the web series "Film School" on [[MTV]]'s Film.com.

== Career ==

=== 1992–2003: Beginnings ===
In 1992, Thorburn signed with [[Jive Records]], then in the mid-1990s signed with [[Priority Records]]/[[EMI]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abovegroundmagazine.com/news/culture-news/03/03/r-a-the-rugged-man-releases-video-based-on-experience-with-jive-records/|title=R.A. The Rugged Man Releases Video Based On Experience With Jive Records|last=Hakes|first=Tyler|date=March 3, 2011|publisher=aboveGround Media|access-date=July 24, 2013}}</ref> His contract was later absorbed by [[Capitol Records]] but he began recording independently in the early 2000s. He has worked with [[Master of ceremonies|MC]]s such as [[The Notorious B.I.G.]], [[Mobb Deep]], and [[Chuck D]].{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}  He was featured on all three of [[Rawkus]]'s Soundbombing albums, as well as the platinum-selling ''[[WWF Aggression]]'' album, performing the theme song for [[Chris Jericho]]. In [[Ego Trip (magazine)|''Ego Trip'']] Vol. 1, Issue 3, The Notorious B.I.G. was quoted as saying, "And I thought I was the illest," when referring to Thorburn.<ref>[http://www.villagevoice.com/2009-11-10/music/r-a-the-rugged-man-abides/ R.A. the Rugged Man Abides – Page 1 – Music – New York]. ''Village Voice'' (November 10, 2009). Retrieved on 2011-05-30.</ref>

=== 2004–2012: ''Die, Rugged Man, Die'', film cameos & magazine writing ===
In 2004, Thorburn released his first album ''[[Die, Rugged Man, Die]]''<ref>[http://365aay.com/y1d171/ 365 Albums a Year » Year 1, Day 171: R.A. The Rugged Man – Die, Rugged Man, Die (2004)] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707062822/http://365aay.com/y1d171/|date=July 7, 2011}}. (April 6, 2010). Retrieved on 2011-05-30.</ref> through Brooklyn-based label [[Nature Sounds]]. He has cameos in several music videos including [[Havoc (musician)|Havoc]] from [[Mobb Deep]]'s ''I'm the Boss'', [[Sadat X]]'s ''Throw the Ball'' and [[Masta Killa]]'s ''Old Man'' featuring [[RZA]] and [[Ol' Dirty Bastard]].

The October 2006 issue of ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]'' featured Thorburn's verse on [[Uncommon Valor: A Vietnam Story]] as its "Hip-Hop Quotable" of the month{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} and HipHopDX named it as the "Verse of the Year".<ref>Hale, Andreas. (January 1, 2007) [http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/interviews/id.693/title.2006-hiphopdx-awards 2006 HipHopDX Awards | Rappers Talk Hip Hop Beef & Old School Hip Hop]. HipHop DX. Retrieved on 2011-05-30.</ref> AllHipHop.com states that "This record will be remembered most for R.A.'s robotic flow recounting his own father's story of war while absolutely murdering the beat."{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} When reviewing his song "On the Block", [[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe Magazine]] said, "I love this song. It give me goosebumps. It's very inventive."{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}

In addition to his hip hop career, Thorburn wrote a monthly movie column for ''[[Mass Appeal Magazine]]'', was a contributor to The Ego Trip Book of Rap Lists (St. Martin's Press) and Ego Trip's Big Book of Racism (HarperCollins), and has written numerous articles for other magazines including [[Vibe (magazine)|''Vibe'']], [[King (magazine)|''King'']], [[Complex (magazine)|''Complex'']], ''Rides'', [[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]<ref name="xxlmag.com" /> and ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]''.{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}

A [[horror film]] fan, Thorburn has written three screenplays with cult film director [[Frank Henenlotter]] and is writer-producer of the Henenlotter film ''[[Bad Biology]]''. The film has an original score by Josh Glazer (J. Glaze) with additional production by [[Prince Paul (producer)|Prince Paul]], and cameos by Playboy model [[Jelena Jensen]] and Penthouse Pet Krista Ayne. Thorburn is working on his directorial debut, a film based on his family, ''God Take, God Give''.<ref>[http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.9728/title.r-a-the-rugged-man-talks-new-film-and-music-projects R.A. The Rugged Man Talks New Film And Music Projects]. (August 31, 2009). Retrieved on 2013-04-17.</ref>

=== 2013–present: ''Legends Never Die'' and ''All My Heroes Are Dead'' ===
[[File:R.A the Rugged Man at Hip Hop Kemp 2013.jpg|thumb|Performing in 2013]]
Thorburn's second studio album ''[[Legends Never Die (R.A. the Rugged Man album)|Legends Never Die]]'' was released on April 30, 2013.<ref>"OK-Tho.com's Top 20 Albums Of 2013" http://www.ok-tho.com/2014/01/top-of-year-albums-of-2013.html</ref>{{better source needed|date=October 2017}} Guest appearances include [[Brother Ali]], [[Masta Ace]], [[Tech N9ne]], [[Talib Kweli]], [[Hopsin]], [[Eamon (singer)|Eamon]], and [[Krizz Kaliko]]. His first single of the album was "The Peoples Champ", the second was "Learn Truth" feat. Talib Kweli. Upon release the album debuted at number 1 on the US Billboard [[Top Heatseekers|Heatseekers Albums]] chart, number 17 on the Billboard [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart, and at number 131 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/1561262/ll-cool-j-scores-13th-top-10-on-top-rbhip-hop-albums|title=LL Cool J Scores 13th Top 10 on Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums|work=Billboard |access-date=May 30, 2013}}</ref> In 2016, Thorburn was featured on German rapper [[Kool Savas]] studio album [[:de:Essahdamus|''Essahdamus'']] on the song "Wahre Liebe".

The third studio album by Thorbun, ''[[All My Heroes Are Dead (R.A. the Rugged Man album)|All My Heroes Are Dead]]'', was released on April 17, 2020. It features artists like [[Ghostface Killah]], [[Eamon (singer)|Eamon]], [[Atmosphere (music group)|Atmosphere]], [[Ice-T]], [[Vinnie Paz]], [[Brand Nubian]], [[Chino XL]], [[M.O.P.]] and [[Onyx (hip hop group)|Onyx]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicmeter.nl/album/781004|work=musicmeter |title=R.A. the Rugged Man - All My Heroes Are Dead |access-date=April 25, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Thorburn's father, Staff Sgt. John A. Thorburn, was a [[Vietnam veteran]] affected by [[Agent Orange]].{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} His brother Maxx was born handicapped and blind, eventually dying at the age of 10. Thorburn's sister, Dee Ann, died in 2007 at the age of 26. Thorburn tells his father's story in "[[Uncommon Valor: A Vietnam Story]]," from the [[Jedi Mind Tricks]] album ''[[Servants in Heaven, Kings in Hell]]''. John A. Thorburn died January 7, 2010, from cancer.<ref>[http://www.hiphopug.com/ra-the-rugged-man.html Hip Hop Underground] bio on R.A. the Rugged Man describing the death of is family members</ref> Thorburn is of German, Scottish and Sicilian descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.25867/title.r-a-the-rugged-man-says-justin-timberlake-is-not-the-epitome-of-talent-|title=R.A. The Rugged Man Says Justin Timberlake Is "Not The Epitome Of Talent"|author=HipHopDX|date=October 18, 2013|work=HipHopDX|access-date=March 9, 2015}}</ref>

Thorburn has two children: Ella (born in 2016) and John A. Thorburn (born in 2017).
