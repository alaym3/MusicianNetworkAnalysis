'''Emily Savitri Haines''' (born 25 January 1974) is an American-Canadian singer and songwriter. She is the lead singer, keyboardist and songwriter of the rock band [[Metric (band)|Metric]] and a member of the [[musical collective]] [[Broken Social Scene]]. As a solo artist, she has performed under her own name and as '''Emily Haines & The Soft Skeleton'''. Haines possesses the [[vocal range]] of a [[mezzo-soprano]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Emily Haines |url=https://therangeplanet.proboards.com/thread/719/emily-haines |website=therangeplanet.proboards.com |publisher=The Range Planet |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url = http://www.collegiatetimes.com/stories/14574/metrics-latest-acoustic-ep-plug-in-plug-out-softens-its-usually-energetic-sound| title = Metric's latest acoustic EP 'Plug In, Plug Out' softens its usually energetic sound| work=Collegiate Times| date=2 November 2009| access-date=14 January 2013| first=Ryan| last=Arnold}}</ref>

==Early life==
Born in [[New Delhi]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Singh |first1=Sonya and Puneet |title=Metric’s Emily Haines Thinks...Feb 2016 |url=https://www.lamag.com/culturefiles/metrics-emily-haines-thinks-the-bands-new-album-sounds-like-a-total-freaky-art-record-and-shes-cool-with-it/ |website=lamag.com |publisher=Los Angeles Magazine |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref>and raised in [[Fenelon Falls]], [[Ontario]], Haines is a dual citizen of Canada and the United States (her parents were both born in the US).<ref>{{cite web |title=Taking Measurements: An Interview with Metric – PopMatters |url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/feature/metric-041013</ref> She is the daughter of Canadian poet [[Paul Haines (poet)|Paul Haines]]; her mother founded a school for under-privileged children in India.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.emilyhaines.com/#bio|title=Home|website=Emily Haines|access-date=4 October 2017}}</ref> Her middle name, Savitri, is from ''[[Savitri: A Legend and a Symbol]]'', an epic poem by [[Sri Aurobindo]].<ref name="auto"/> Her sister is the Canadian television journalist [[Avery Haines]] and her brother is Tim Haines, owner of Bluestreak Records in [[Peterborough, Ontario]], Canada.

Haines grew up in a house rich with experimental art and musical expression and her early influences included [[Carla Bley]], [[Robert Wyatt]], and [[PJ Harvey]]. She studied drama at the [[Etobicoke School of the Arts]] (ESA), where she met [[Amy Millan]] and [[Kevin Drew]], with whom she would later collaborate in Broken Social Scene (co-founded by Drew). During their time at ESA, Haines and Millan formed their first band together.<ref>Berman, Stuart. ''This Book Is Broken.'' 2009</ref>

Haines attended the [[University of British Columbia]] in Vancouver between 1992 and 1993, and Montreal's [[Concordia University]] between 1995 and 1996. In 1996, she distributed a limited-edition album entitled ''[[Cut in Half and Also Double]]'' that included songs written and recorded during her student years.<ref>{{Citation | last =Mullin | first =Kyle | title =Sisters of music: Metric's Emily Haines' lifelong friendship with Stars' Amy Millan | newspaper =Monday Magazine | date =7 November 2012 | url =https://www.mondaymag.com/entertainment/sisters-of-music/ | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20190118202630/https://www.mondaymag.com/entertainment/sisters-of-music/ | archive-date =18 January 2019 | access-date =18 January 2019}}</ref>

==Professional music==
[[File:Emily-Haines.jpg|thumb|Haines in 2006]]

Haines met [[James Shaw (musician)|James Shaw]] in Toronto in 1997 and began performing as a duo called "Mainstream".<ref name=spinmag>{{cite web|title=Why They're Called... Metric – SPIN – Profiles – Spotlight|url=https://www.spin.com/2010/07/why-theyre-called-metric/|access-date=21 April 2013}}</ref> After releasing an [[Extended play|EP]] entitled ''[[Mainstream EP]]'', they changed the band's name to "Metric", after a sound programmed by Shaw on his keyboard.<ref name=spinmag /> [[Joshua Winstead]] and Joules Scott-Key joined the band in 2001.<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web|title=Metric – Music Biography, Credits and Discography : AllMusic|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/metric-mn0000380651|access-date=25 April 2013}}</ref> 
As of 2021, Metric has released seven studio albums: ''[[Old World Underground, Where Are You Now?]]'' (2003), ''[[Live It Out]]'' (2005), ''[[Grow Up and Blow Away]]'' (2007, but recorded in 2001), ''[[Fantasies (album)|Fantasies]]'' (2009), ''[[Synthetica]]'' (2012), ''[[Pagans in Vegas]]'' (2015), and ''[[Art of Doubt]]'' (2018).

Haines also contributed vocals or backing vocals to songs by Broken Social Scene, [[Jason Collett]], [[Stars (Canadian band)|Stars]], [[Delerium]], [[K-Os]], [[KC Accidental]], [[The Stills]], [[Tiësto]], [[The Crystal Method]] and [[Todor Kobakov]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Credits: Emily Haines |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/emily-haines-mn0000186972/credits |website=allmusic.com |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref>

In 2004, Metric appeared in the 2004 drama film ''[[Clean (movie)|Clean]]''. The band, appearing as themselves, performed their song "Dead Disco" and had minor speaking roles in a backstage scene. "Dead Disco" also featured on ''[[Clean (movie)|Clean Original Soundtrack]]''. In 2010, she appeared as part of Broken Social Scene, on the soundtrack for the film ''[[This Movie is Broken]]''.

In 2006, as Emily Haines & The Soft Skeleton, Haines released the studio album ''[[Knives Don't Have Your Back]]''. The songs "Our Hell" and "Doctor Blind" were issued as singles and corresponding music videos were produced. ''Knives Don't Have Your Back'' was followed in 2007 by the EP ''[[What Is Free to a Good Home?]]'', which was inspired by the death of her father.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Amorosi |first1=A.D. |title=Emily Haines dedicates second solo album to late father , Nov 2017 |url=https://www.phillyvoice.com/emily-haines-dedicates-second-solo-album-to-late-father/ |website=phillyvoice.com |publisher=Philly Voice |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref>  Her solo work is typically more subdued and piano-based than her work with Metric.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Schlanger |first1=Talia |title=Emily Haines On World Cafe, Dec 2017 |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2017/12/14/570811533/emily-haines-on-world-cafe |website=npr.org |publisher=NPR |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref> She often appears with Amy Millan as her opening act.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mullin |first1=Kyle |title=Sisters of Music |url=https://www.mondaymag.com/entertainment/sisters-of-music/ |website=mondaymag.com |publisher=Monday Magazine |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref>

Prior to the fourth Metric album ''Fantasies'', Haines traveled to Argentina to write.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fortune |first1=Drew |title=Happiness Is a Warm Band |url=https://www.popmatters.com/109599-happiness-is-a-warm-band-an-interview-with-emily-haines-of-metric-2496068161.html |website=popmatters.com |publisher=Pop Matters |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref> <ref>{{cite web|title=Metric's Emily Haines Makes Style Poetic|url=http://www.refinery29.com/emily-haines#slide-11|publisher=refinery29|access-date=29 August 2013|date=August 2013}}</ref> She became good friends with [[Lou Reed]] and [[Laurie Anderson]] before Reed's death in 2013.<ref name="rollingstone.com">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/metrics-emily-haines-pays-tribute-to-lou-reeds-integrity-and-humor-20131028|title=Metric's Emily Haines on Lou Reed's Integrity and Humor|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|access-date=31 October 2017}}</ref>

Haines contributed to Broken Social Scene's 2017 album [[Hug of Thunder]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/07/broken-social-scene-share-first-album-in-seven-years-hug-of-thunder-stream/|title=Broken Social Scene share first album in seven years, Hug of Thunder: Stream|date=3 July 2017|work=Consequence of Sound|access-date=31 July 2017}}</ref> and performed with Broken Social Scene on [[The Late Show with Stephen Colbert]] 30 March 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=Broken Social Scene - Halfway Home (Stephen Colbert) |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QCD9U1yf3lU |website=youtube.com |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/63964-broken-social-scene-return-with-halfway-home-first-new-song-in-7-years-listen/|title=Broken Social Scene Return With "Halfway Home," First New Song in 7 Years: Listen |website=Pitchfork |access-date=23 April 2017}}</ref> In May 2017, Haines was with Broken Social Scene in Manchester, England, the night after the [[Manchester Arena bombing|terror attack at the Ariana Grande concert]]; she attributes her ability to carry on and play after the attack to the support of former [[The Smiths]] guitarist [[Johnny Marr]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/metric_singer_emily_haines_says_johnny_marr_helped_her_through_broken_social_scenes_post-attack_manchester_gig|title=Emily Haines Explains How Johnny Marr Helped Her Through Broken Social Scene's Post-Attack Manchester Gig|website=exclaim.ca|access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref>

On September 15, 2017, Emily Haines & The Soft Skeleton released the album ''[[Choir of the Mind]]''; it was recorded a year earlier in Toronto and released by [[Last Gang Records]] and eOne ([[Entertainment One]]).<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/metrics-emily-haines-details-new-solo-album-choir-of-the-mind-w486804|title=Metric's Emily Haines Details New Solo Album 'Choir of the Mind'|work=[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]|access-date=17 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Thompson |first1=Stephen |title=Review: Emily Haines, 'Choir Of The Mind', Sept 2017 |url=https://www.npr.org/2017/09/07/548881327/first-listen-emily-haines-choir-of-the-mind |website=npr.org |publisher=NPR |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref> The album's lead single, "Fatal Gift", was released on 9 June 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/73983-emily-haines-metric-broken-social-scene-announces-first-new-album-in-11-years/|title=Emily Haines (Metric, Broken Social Scene) Announces First New Album in 11 Years |website=Pitchfork|access-date=9 June 2017}}</ref> In support of the album, Haines toured North America in late 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.emilyhaines.com/#tourvideo|title=Home|publisher=Emily Haines|access-date=12 August 2017}}</ref>

==Instruments==
When performing with Metric, Haines plays two synthesizers. Her primary synthesizer is a [[Sequential Circuits]] Pro-One, which she has played since the band's early years.<ref name=gearwire>{{cite web|title=Sequential Circuits Pro-One Is Always Onstage With Emily Haines of Metric – YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OXa0_Cfcf9Q|access-date=29 August 2013}}</ref> As secondary synthesizer, she played a [[Kawai]] MP9000,<ref>{{cite web|title=Metric – Interview in an east village bar (NY) 2001 – YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FMr6lXUSsyo|access-date=29 August 2013}}</ref> which was replaced by [[E-mu]]'s PK-6 Proteus Keys in 2003, and was followed by the [[Clavia Nord Wave]] in 2012. She also plays the [[tambourine]], electric guitar, piano and harmonica.<ref>{{cite web|title=Review: Canadian indie band Metric rocks the Fillmore Miami Beach |publisher=miami.com |url=http://www.miami.com/review-canadian-indie-band-metric-rocks-fillmore-miami-beach-article}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Emily Haines & The Soft Skeleton – Live Review on The Red Alert|url=http://www.theredalert.com/reviews/haines.php}}</ref>

==Other projects==
In August 2013, Haines became involved with FLEET4HEARme, a collaboration with Fleet Jewelry and HearMe, an organization that aims to increase children's access to music programs in public schools.<ref>{{cite web|title=Metric's Emily Haines Makes Style Poetic|url=http://www.refinery29.com/emily-haines#slide-1|publisher=refinery29|access-date=29 August 2013|date=August 2013}}</ref> In September 2015, the Canadian outerwear brand Rudsak unveiled a leather jacket customized by Haines.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Metric's Emily Haines teams with Montreal brand Rudsak on design collaboration|url = http://www.torontosun.com/2015/08/25/metrics-emily-haines-teams-with-montreal-brand-rudsak-on-design-collaboration|newspaper = Toronto Sun |agency = The Canadian Press |access-date = 26 September 2015}}</ref> The jacket is a classic black motor-cycle style with lyrics from Metric song "The Governess" on the back.

Metric is active in the international advocacy organization [[Global Citizen (organization)|Global Citizen]], and Haines is involved with its girls and women issues campaign #SheDecides.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bliss |first1=Karen |title=Metric's Emily Haines on Why...Aug 2017 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7905127/metric-emily-haines-global-citizen-she-decides-manifesto |website=billboard.com |publisher=Billboard |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref> In 2017, Haines partnered with House of Matriarch High Perfumery to create her own fragrance, "Siren".<ref>{{cite web |title=Metric's Emily Haines Unveils New Fragrance, Nov 2017 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8039727/metrics-emily-haines-unveils-new-fragrance-siren |website=billboard.com |publisher=Billboard |access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref>
