'''Jeremy Zucker''' (born March 3, 1996) is an American singer-songwriter, best known for his songs ''comethru,'' ''you were good to me'' and ''all the kids are depressed''. Since his first published music in 2015, Zucker has created a vast discography including multiple EP's and two full length albums, ''love is not dying'' (2020) and ''CRUSHER'' (2021).

==Early life==
Originally from [[Franklin Lakes, New Jersey]], Zucker was raised in a musical household with his parents and two older brothers. While a student at [[Ramapo High School (New Jersey)|Ramapo High School]], he began making music in his bedroom and later became part of a band called the “Foreshadows”. After graduating high school, he attended [[Colorado College]] where he graduated in 2018 with a degree in [[molecular biology]].<ref>Gerace, Joseph M. [https://patch.com/new-jersey/wyckoff/franklin-lakes-teen-inspires-giving-to-paterson-middl2c9ec96666 "Ramapo Student Inspires Giving at Paterson School; Jeremy Zucker not only gave, but inspired others to open up their hearts"], Wyckoff, NJ Patch, January 7, 2013. Accessed November 25, 2019. "Jeremy Zucker, then a sophomore at Ramapo High School, had been interested in completing a community service project."</ref><ref>Mercuri, Monica. [https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8459852/jeremy-zucker-interview-tour-all-the-kids-are-depressed-video "Jeremy Zucker Talks 'all the kids are depressed' Video & Preparing for First Headlining Tour"], ''[[Billboard (magazine)]]'', June 8, 2018. Accessed November 25, 2019. "A few weeks ago, 22-year-old singer-songwriter-producer Jeremy Zucker was graduating from Colorado College with a degree in molecular biology. And now, he's embarking on a new chapter in his life, recently releasing his fifth EP glisten and preparing for his first U.S. headlining tour in the fall.... Originally from Franklin Lakes, N.J., Zucker was raised in a musical household with his parents and two older brothers."</ref> Before producing his own music, he told ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' in an interview that his first job was a snowboard instructor at a ski mountain (later becoming a skiing instructor).<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Get to Know 'All the Kids Are Depressed' Singer Jeremy Zucker: Watch|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8478357/jeremy-zucker-video-interview/|access-date=2021-01-22|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
{{Expand section|date=April 2020|information prior to the release of his third EP}}
In 2015, Zucker released "''Beach Island"''. This EP is the oldest musical record Zucker has up on his platforms. He released his second studio EP'' "Breathe"'',<ref>{{Citation|title=Breathe|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/25yx8uHQ8wwR5xWeDFxAlU|language=en|access-date=2020-10-21}}</ref> containing breakthrough hit "''‘Bout It"'' in December 2016. In 2017, Zucker released his EP "''Motions"'', featuring the song "''Heavy''", which later the recording artist [[Blackbear (musician)|blackbear]] remixed into "''Make Daddy Proud''", and included on his own LP ''[[Digital Druglord]]'' five months later.<ref>{{Citation|title=digital druglord|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/30QoUTsSdpQuZunPNFsLnm|language=en|access-date=2021-01-22}}</ref> Zucker and blackbear later collaborated on the single "talk is overrated" on Zucker's EP "''idle"'', released in 2017. Zucker released the EP "stripped." in February 2018, then the EP titled "glisten", in May 2018. In September 2018, Zucker released the EP "summer", which contains Zucker's well known song "comethru". "comethru" is a response to the way Zucker felt after graduating college in May 2018 and moving back home to [[New Jersey]] into the same house where Zucker grew up. This song has since gone on to be streamed over 800 million times across all platforms.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/09/jeremy-zucker-comethru|title=Premiere: Jeremy Zucker's "comethru" Is an Ode to His Transitional Summer|website=Complex}}</ref> Also featured on the EP is “''thinking 2 much''”, a collaboration with singer [[Eden (musician)|Eden]]. In 2019, Zucker collaborated with friend and fellow singer-songwriter, Chelsea Cutler, to release a song called, "you were good to me." This song was the leading single prior to the release of their first collaborative EP titled "brent" which released on April 19th, 2019. Prior to this collaboration, Zucker had worked with Cutler once before on a song titled "better off" which was released with his EP "glisten" and was met with some success. 

On July 26th, 2019, Zucker released "''oh, mexico''", which would later be the lead single for his debut album "''love is not dying". The second single, "''always, i'll care''", was released on February 7, 2020. A music video for the song was released the same day.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://radiofacts.com/jeremy-zucker-returns-with-new-song-always-ill-care/|title=JEREMY ZUCKER RETURNS WITH NEW SONG "always, i'll care" {{!}} RadioFacts|last=August|first=Jimmy|date=February 27, 2020|website=Radiofacts|language=en-US|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=March 9, 2020}}</ref> The third single off the album, "''not ur friend''", was released on February 28, 2020. The fourth single, "''julia''" was released March 24, 2020. "love is not dying" was released on April 17, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B-XK3yRg6Bt/|title=love is not dying, april 17th|website=Instagram}}</ref> The album is an autobiographical collection of songs that were recorded in Brooklyn during the second half of 2019. Zucker had a tour for this album planned under the same name "love is not dying tour" but it was sadly canceled due to the COVID-19 pandemic. <ref>{{Cite web|title=Jeremy Zucker on the Pre-Apocalyptic Beauty of 'love is not dying' [Q&A]|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/jeremy-zucker-love-is-not-dying-interview|last=Polo|first=Maxamillion|date=April 17, 2020|website=[[Ones to Watch]]|url-status=live|access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref> On July 24, 2020, Zucker released his single "supercuts". Before 2021, he released with Claire Rosinkranz a new version of her song "''Backyard Boy''",<ref>{{cite AV media |date=November 10, 2020 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WmnnvfP-j0k |title=Claire Rosinkranz - Backyard Boy (with Jeremy Zucker) [Official Video] |publisher=ClaireRosinkranzVEVO |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=October 5, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210902140523/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WmnnvfP-j0k |archive-date=September 2, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> and "''Nothing's The Same''"<ref>{{cite AV media |date=December 16, 2020 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG3mfZp6llo |title=Alexander 23, Jeremy Zucker - Nothing's The Same |publisher=Alexander23VEVO |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=October 5, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210211041910/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mG3mfZp6llo |archive-date=February 11, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> with [[Alexander 23]].

On January 15, 2021, Zucker and [[Chelsea Cutler]] released their song "''this is how you fall in love''"<ref>{{cite AV media |date=January 15, 2021 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qkgVgjN188 |title=Jeremy Zucker & Chelsea Cutler - this is how you fall in love (Official Music Video) |publisher=JeremyZuckerVEVO |via=[[YouTube]] |access-date=October 5, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210902233838/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qkgVgjN188 |archive-date=September 2, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> from their second EP. Zucker and Cutler performed on a livestream show called "''brent: live on the internet''",<ref name="merged1">{{cite tweet |last=Zucker |first=Jeremy |author-link=Jeremy Zucker |user=jeremyzucker |number=1351579634916331522 |date=January 19, 2021 |title=chels and i are so incredibly proud to announce “brent: live on the internet”, a livestream moment taking place on february 10 ! general on-sale tomorrow at noon est. brent forever &lt;3 https://t.co/OfNxTPij04 https://t.co/43oYc58wuS |language=en |access-date=October 5, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210119171919/https://twitter.com/jeremyzucker/status/1351579634916331522 |archive-date=January 19, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> where they will introduce "''brent ii''" and unite again for some songs of "''brent''". ==Influences==
Zucker has cited [[Blink-182]], [[Jon Bellion]], [[Blackbear (musician)|blackbear]], [[Eden (musician)|Eden]], [[Bon Iver]], [[Mac Miller]], and [[Wet (band)|Wet]] as some of his musical influences.<ref name="about">{{cite web|url=http://www.republicrecords.com/artists/jeremy-zucker|title=Jeremy Zucker <nowiki>|</nowiki> Republic Records|work=[[Republic Records]]|date=16 October 2017|accessdate=September 8, 2019}}</ref>

His music is described as "a fusion of organic airy beats, lush soundtrack-style soundscapes, and biting tumblr-worthy lyricism". Zucker describes himself as a "social introvert”.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-08-21|title=Jeremy Zucker's new single is a spellbinding emotional masterpiece|url=https://happymag.tv/jeremy-zuckers-new-single-is-a-spellbinding-emotional-masterpiece/|access-date=2021-01-22|website=Happy Mag|language=en-US}}</ref>
