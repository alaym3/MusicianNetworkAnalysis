'''Finn Askew''' (born May 21, 2001)<ref name=":1" /> is a British musician from [[Wellington, Somerset|Wellington]] in south-west England.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Teen Taunton musician Finn Askew hitting the right notes|url=https://www.somersetcountygazette.co.uk/leisure/music_and_gigs/17345150.finn-askew-creating-new-fusion-music/|access-date=2021-02-03|website=Somerset County Gazette|language=en}}</ref>

Finn cites US hip-hop acts such as [[Juice Wrld|Juice WRLD]] and [[Lil Peep]] as music influences of his sound<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Get To Know: Finn Askew {{!}} MTV UK|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/mtv-push/news/get-to-know-finn-askew|access-date=2021-02-03|website=www.mtv.co.uk|language=en}}</ref> though he fell in love with music after uncovering his parent's CDs, a mixture of [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]], [[the Smiths]], and [[the Beach Boys]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Finn Askew's Debut Single 'Roses' Is Remarkably Potent|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/finn-askews-debut-single-roses-is-remarkably-potent|access-date=2021-02-03|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> Finn's music is described as an "indie sound, washed one moment in a rosy hue of pop and the next polished to a high, RnB sheen."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Finn Askew: Confidence Man|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/longread/finn-askew-confidence-man-interview|access-date=2021-02-03|website=The Line of Best Fit|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
Finn Askew's debut single "Roses" was created and recorded when he was 16.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-04|title=Polydor 2021: Finn Askew|url=https://notion.online/polydor-2021-finn-askew/|access-date=2021-02-03|website=Notion|language=en-GB}}</ref> He got the beat from [[YouTube]] and used [[GarageBand]] and his £20 microphone to create it.<ref name=":0" /> "Roses" featured in a live stream on [[V Live]] from [[Lee Tae-yong|Taeyong]] (member of K-pop band [[NCT (group)|NCT]]).<ref>{{Citation|title=Taeyong singing Finn Askew's roses|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_R23EbphkRY|language=en|access-date=2021-02-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=คุยกับ Finn Askew ถึงเพลง "Roses" จากเด็กแต่งเพลงในโรงรถสู่การเป็นศิลปินที่ไม่เหมือนใคร|url=https://www.sanook.com/music/2428529/|access-date=2021-02-03|website=www.sanook.com/music|language=th}}</ref> As a result the track was included in the Spotify viral charts in a number of southeast Asian countries and has over 12 million streams on Spotify alone.

Finn released his debut EP ''Peach'' which combines lo-fi hip-hop, piano jazz and indie rock in January 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=SIT IN ON FRIDAY - Finn Askew|url=https://www.title-mag.com/post/sit-in-on-friday-finn-askew|access-date=2021-02-03|website=www.title-mag.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=10 Cool New Pop Songs to Get You Through The Week: Jon Batiste, 3OH!3, Griff & More|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9515911/jon-batiste-new-pop-songs-playlist-january-25/|access-date=2021-02-03|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

Finn Askew was listed as one of [[Vevo]] Dscvr's Artists To Watch for 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-16|title=VEVO reveals DSCVR Artists To Watch 2021 list, teams with MBW to showcase acts|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/vevo-reveals-dscvr-artists-to-watch-2021-list-teams-with-mbw-to-showcase-acts/|access-date=2021-02-03|website=Music Business Worldwide|language=en-US}}</ref>
