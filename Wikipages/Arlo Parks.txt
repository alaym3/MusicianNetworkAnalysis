'''Anaïs Oluwatoyin Estelle Marinho'''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Veitch|first=Mara|date=19 August 2020|title=Arlo Parks's Music Captures the Angst and Agony of Growing Up|url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/arlo-parks-hurt-new-album-interview/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823110513/https://www.wmagazine.com/story/arlo-parks-hurt-new-album-interview/|archive-date=23 August 2020|access-date=23 August 2020|website=[[W (magazine)|W]]|issn=0162-9115}}</ref> (born 9 August 2000),<ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1292485861028831235|user=arloparks|title=I turned 20! years! old! today! Thank u 2 everyone for the messages of luv n light - my heart is full.|author=Arlo Parks}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Anais Oluwatoyin Estelle MARINHO (born August 2000)|url=https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/officers/2ug9XJYgNulNpBDz7pNwqbPRP0w/appointments|access-date=5 June 2020|website=[[Companies House]]|language=en}}</ref> known professionally as '''Arlo Parks''', is a British singer-songwriter and poet. Her debut studio album, ''[[Collapsed in Sunbeams]]'', was released in 2021 to critical acclaim and peaked at number three on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. It earned her nominations for [[Brit Award for British Album of the Year|Album of the Year]], [[Brit Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] and [[Brit Award for British Female Solo Artist|Best British Female Solo Artist]] at the [[2021 Brit Awards]]. It won the 2021 Hyundai [[Mercury Prize]] for best album.

== Career ==

=== 2018–2019: ''Super Sad Generation'' and ''Sophie'' ===
Parks chose her stage name as a distinctive personal pseudonym, inspired by [[King Krule]] and [[Frank Ocean]].<ref name=":5" /><ref name=":8" /> In 2018, she began uploading demos to [[BBC Music Introducing]]. This caught the attention of radio presenters across the UK who distributed these demos to Ali Raymond of Beatnik Creative, who soon began managing Parks.<ref name=":22">{{Cite magazine|last=Cirisano|first=Tatiana|date=December 7, 2020|title=One To Watch: Arlo Parks Talks Mining 'Traumatic Places' to Create Her Debut Album|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9493250/arlo-parks-collapsed-in-sunbeams-one-to-watch-interview|access-date=16 January 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> She made her solo debut when she released the song "Cola" through Beatnik Records in November 2018,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/song-of-the-day/arlo-parks-cola|title=Arlo Parks introduces soulful melancholy with debut offering "Cola"|last=Swash|first=Olivia|date=23 November 2018|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|language=en|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref> and announced the release of her debut EP, ''Super Sad Generation''.<ref name="Complex1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/03/premiere-arlo-parks-romantic-garbage|title=Premiere: Soul Singer Arlo Parks Deals With An Intense Early Crush On "Romantic Garbage"|last=Keith|first=James|date=19 March 2019|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|language=en|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref> She told ''Line of Best Fit'' that the song is "a reminder that betrayal is inevitable when it comes to pretty people that think flowers fix everything." Olivia Swash wrote that the vocals on the song "flourish thanks to [Parks'] creative writing background, with her delicate tone taking centre stage against the gently plodding guitars and soft crackle of vinyl."<ref name=":0" /> By November 2019, the song had amassed over three million streams on Spotify.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url=https://theface.com/style/arlo-parks-dr-martens-tough-as-you-poetry-literature|title=Storytelling with Arlo Parks|last=Thomas|first=Katie|date=9 November 2019|website=[[The Face (magazine)|The Face]]|language=en-gb|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''Collapsed in Sunbeams'' ===
Parks embarked on her first headlining tour of Europe in February and March 2020,<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last=Hewitson|first=Rosie|date=16 December 2019|title=Class of 2020: Arlo Parks|url=http://diymag.com/2019/12/16/class-of-2020-arlo-parks|access-date=25 December 2019|website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]|language=en}}</ref> but could not complete it due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. In May 2020, Parks released the singles "Eugene" and "[[Black Dog (Arlo Parks song)|Black Dog]]", which were well received during the [[COVID-19 lockdowns|COVID-19 lockdown]],<ref name=":02">{{Cite web|last=Richards|first=Will|date=20 May 2020|title=Arlo Parks: Creator of the year's most devastating song|url=https://www.nme.com/features/nme-radar-arlo-parks-black-dog-is-the-years-most-devastating-song-2671623|access-date=5 June 2020|website=[[NME]]|language=en-GB}}</ref> the latter of which became [[BBC Radio 1]]'s [[Annie Mac (radio show)|Tune of the Week]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ahmed|first=Narzra|date=13 May 2020|title=Singer-songwriter and poet Arlo Parks' confessional style seeps into her powerful new single 'Black Dog'|url=https://whenthehornblows.com/content/2020/5/10/arlo-parks-black-dog|access-date=5 June 2020|website=When The Horn Blows|language=en-US}}</ref> Parks made [[List of NME covers|the front cover of ''NME'']] in late July 2020.<ref name=":8" /> She won the [[AIM Independent Music Awards|AIM Independent Music Award]] for One to Watch in 2020 in August 2020, after losing the same award to [[Georgia (musician)|Georgia]] a year before.<ref name=":7">{{Cite news|last=Beaumont-Thomas|first=Ben|date=12 August 2020|title=Sarathy Korwar, Flying Lotus and FKA twigs among 2020 AIM awards winners|work=The Guardian]|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/aug/12/sarathy-korwar-flying-lotus-fka-twigs-2020-aim-awards|access-date=19 August 2020|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Parks and [[Moses Boyd]] made the front cover of ''[[Music Week]]'' for the publication's indie takeover special following the AIM Awards ceremony.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Homewood|first=Ben|date=10 August 2020|title=The shape of indie to come: Meet Moses Boyd & Arlo Parks|url=https://www.musicweek.com/interviews/read/the-shape-of-indie-to-come-meet-moses-boyd-arlo-parks/080727|access-date=16 September 2020|website=Music Week}}</ref> Parks released her debut album, ''[[Collapsed in Sunbeams]]'', on 29 January 2021.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|date=20 October 2020|title=Arlo Parks has announced her debut album, 'Collapsed In Sunbeams'|url=https://www.readdork.com/news/arlo-parks-debut-album-collapsed-in-sunbeams|access-date=20 October 2020|website=Dork|publisher=}}</ref>

On 12 February 2021, Parks was the music act on The Graham Norton Show singing '[[Caroline (Arlo Parks song)|Caroline]]'. On 19 February 2021, Parks was the main guest of [[Jools Holland]] on his BBC programme ''[[Later...]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/watch-arlo-parks-sleaford-mods-and-more-christen-new-season-of-later-with-jools-holland-2884889|title = Watch Arlo Parks, Sleaford Mods and more christen new season of 'Later... With Jools Holland'|website = [[NME]]|date = 20 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicweek.com/media/read/how-later-with-jools-holland-became-a-lifeline-for-artists-in-lockdown/082638|title = How Later... With Jools Holland became a lifeline for artists in lockdown}}</ref>

On 11 May, she won the Breakthrough Artist Award at the [[2021 Brit Awards]].

On 9 September, the album Collapsed in Sunbeams won the Hyundai Mercury Prize for album of the year. Presenting the award, judge Annie MacManus said: "We chose an artist with a singular voice who uses lyrics of remarkable beauty … and connects deeply with her generation as she does so".

== Personal life ==
Parks is openly bisexual and is based in London.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/interviews/arlo-parks-on-the-rise|title=On The Rise: Arlo Parks|last=Williams|first=Pip|date=28 January 2019|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|language=en|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="BBC1" /> She was educated at [[Latymer Upper School]] in Hammersmith and completed her [[A Level]]s in early 2019 at [[Ashbourne College]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.musicweek.com/waves/read/making-waves-arlo-parks/076960|title=Making Waves: Arlo Parks|last=Homewood|first=Ben|date=31 July 2019|website=Music Week|language=en|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-05|title=Arlo Parks interview: 'People like to write that I was confused about my sexuality – but I never felt that'|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/arlo-parks-interview-collapsed-in-sunbeams-b1780530.html|access-date=2021-03-30|website=The Independent|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=19 October 2020|title=Ashbourne College Facebook post|website=[[Facebook]]|url=https://www.facebook.com/ashbournecollege/posts/anais-marinho-known-as-arlo-parks-has-achieved-incredible-success-in-her-music-c/4503629539678188/|url-status=live|access-date=27 April 2021}}</ref> In the autobiographical text on her [[Spotify]] profile, Parks claimed that she spent most of secondary school "feeling like that black kid who couldn't dance for shit, listening to too much emo music and crushing on some girl in her Spanish class."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/4kIwETcbpuFgRukE8o7Opx|title=About - Arlo Parks|last=|first=|date=|website=[[Spotify]]|language=en|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref>

Parks has named [[Sylvia Plath]] and [[Joni Mitchell]] as among her influences.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/arlo-parks-interview-collapsed-in-sunbeams-b1780530.html|title=Arlo Parks interview: 'People like to write that I was confused about my sexuality – but I never felt that'|date=5 January 2021|website=The Independent}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/alpk-arlo-parks-interview-2020-hurt-music/|title=Interview with Arlo Parks: On Turning Poems into Songs|date=16 September 2020}}</ref>
