'''Forth Wanderers''' is an American [[Indie rock]] band from [[Montclair, New Jersey]], formed in 2013. The band consists of vocalist, Ava Trilling, guitarist and songwriter Ben Guterl, guitarist Duke Greene, bassist Noah Schifrin and drummer Zach Lorelli. Formed while the members attended [[Montclair High School (New Jersey)|Montclair High School]], Forth Wanderers self-released their first EP, ''Mahogany'', in 2013,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mahogany, by Forth Wanderers|url=https://forthwanderers.bandcamp.com/album/mahogany|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Forth Wanderers}}</ref> followed by the mini-album ''Tough Love'' in 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tough Love, by Forth Wanderers|url=https://forthwanderers.bandcamp.com/album/tough-love|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Forth Wanderers}}</ref> Following moderate success in the [[Independent music|DIY scene]], including a Twitter shout-out from pop singer [[Lorde]],<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-04-23|title=How Forth Wanderers Made An Indie Rock Record For The Ages|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1991600/how-forth-wanderers-made-an-indie-rock-record-for-the-ages/franchises/interview/|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Stereogum}}</ref> the group released their second EP, ''Slop'', in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Slop, by Forth Wanderers|url=https://forthwanderers.bandcamp.com/album/slop|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Forth Wanderers}}</ref> The EP was co-released between [[Father/Daughter Records]] in the United States and [[Marathon Artists]] in the United Kingdom.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Forth Wanderers - Slop EP|url=http://www.fatherdaughterrecords.com/products/575813-forth-wanderers-slop-ep|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Father/Daughter Records|language=en}}</ref> In March 2017, the band played [[South by Southwest]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Forth Wanderers|url=https://schedule.sxsw.com/2017/events/MS40061|access-date=2020-10-20|website=SXSW 2020 Schedule|language=en}}</ref>

In 2018, Forth Wanderers signed to indie label [[Sub Pop]] and on April 27, 2018, released their debut studio album, ''Forth Wanderers''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Forth Wanderers, by Forth Wanderers|url=https://forthwanderers.bandcamp.com/album/forth-wanderers|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Forth Wanderers}}</ref> The album was produced and recorded in [[Philadelphia]] over 5 days in the summer of 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Records|first=Sub Pop|title=Forth Wanderers share official video for "Taste" from their acclaimed self-titled debut - Headline US Tour begins this Wednesday, June 27th|url=https://www.subpop.com/news/2018/06/25/forth_wanderers_share_official_video_for_taste_from_their_acclaimed_self_titled_debut_headline_us_tour_begins_this_wednesday_june_27th|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Sub Pop Records|language=en}}</ref> It received a 7.7 rating from [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Forth Wanderers: Forth Wanderers|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/forth-wanderers-forth-wanderers/|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref> and placed at #20 on [[Stereogum]]'s "50 Best Albums of 2018."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-06-06|title=The 50 Best Albums Of 2018 So Far|url=https://www.stereogum.com/featured/the-50-best-albums-of-2018-so-far/|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Stereogum}}</ref> 

In the summer of 2018, the band was scheduled to go on a U.S. tour to support the album, during which they were slated to play [[Jack Antonoff]]'s Shadow of the City Festival and [[Jay-Z]]'s [[Made in America Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Records|first=Sub Pop|title=Forth Wanderers share official video for "Taste" from their acclaimed self-titled debut - Headline US Tour begins this Wednesday, June 27th|url=https://www.subpop.com/news/2018/06/25/forth_wanderers_share_official_video_for_taste_from_their_acclaimed_self_titled_debut_headline_us_tour_begins_this_wednesday_june_27th|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Sub Pop Records|language=en}}</ref> The day before the tour was set to begin, on June 26, 2018, the band published a statement written by Trilling to their Facebook and Twitter pages announcing they would be cancelling the tour due to a "recently diagnosed mental health issue."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-06-26|title=Forth Wanderers Cancel Tour|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2003542/forth-wanderers-cancel-tour-to-address-mental-health-diagnosis/news/|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Stereogum}}</ref> The following month, they published another statement announcing they would be taking a break for the "immediate future."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Forth Wanderers|url=https://www.facebook.com/forthwanderers/posts/1085595984923618|access-date=2020-10-20|website=www.facebook.com|language=en}}</ref>

In May 2019, Trilling wrote an article for [[Vice.com|Vice]] where she detailed her experience dealing with a panic disorder while being in a DIY band and further explained her reasoning for cancelling the tour.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Forth Wanderers' Ava Trilling on Touring with Panic Disorder|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/wjmkpx/forth-wanderers-ava-trilling-on-touring-with-panic-disorder|access-date=2020-10-20|website=www.vice.com|language=en}}</ref> The band has not released any new music since ''Forth Wanderers'', although songwriter and guitarist Ben Guterl released an EP under the moniker "Ben Special" in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ben Special|url=https://benspecial.bandcamp.com/|access-date=2020-10-20|website=Ben Special}}</ref>
