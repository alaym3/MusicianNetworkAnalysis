'''Nicole Blonsky'''<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/movies/a34397/zac-efron-prepares-for-hairspray.html |title=Zac Efron prepares for 'Hairspray'|work=[[Digital Spy]]|date=November 7, 2008 |access-date= November 7, 2008|archive-date=May 30, 2019|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20190530193624/https://www.digitalspy.com/movies/a34397/zac-efron-prepares-for-hairspray/|url-status=live}}</ref> (born November 9, 1988)<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.upi.com/Top_News/2018/11/09/UPI-Almanac-for-Friday-Nov-9-2018/7831541607233/|title=UPI Almanac for Friday, Nov. 9, 2018|work=[[United Press International]]|date=November 9, 2018|quote=actor Nikki Blonsky in 1988 (age 30)}}</ref> is an American actress, singer, dancer, and internet personality who is known for her breakthrough role as Tracy Turnblad in the film ''[[Hairspray (2007 film)|Hairspray]]'' (2007), for which she won two [[Critics' Choice Movie Awards|Critics' Choice Awards]] and received nominations for a [[Golden Globe Awards|Golden Globe Award]] and a [[Screen Actors Guild Awards|SAG Award]]. She has since starred as Willamena Rader in the [[ABC Family]] series ''[[Huge (TV series)|Huge]]'' (2010), for which she received a [[Teen Choice Awards|Teen Choice Award]] nomination, had a recurring role as Margot on the series [[Smash (TV series)|''Smash'']] (2013), and had roles in the films [[Geography Club (film)|''Geography Club'']] (2013) as Therese and ''[[The Last Movie Star]]'' (2017) as Faith.

==Career==
[[File:2008 Voice Awards Nikki Blonsky (20163852830).jpg|220x220px|thumb|Blonsky at the 2008 Voice awards]]
Blonsky began acting in 2007, first portraying Tracy Turnblad in the musical film [[Hairspray (2007 film)|''Hairspray'']], an adaptation of the [[Hairspray (musical)|musical of the same name]].<ref name="ylihti" /><ref name="Fox">{{cite news |last=Fox |first=Michael |title=ARTS: Welcome To The '60s |newspaper=The Detroit Jewish News |date=July 19, 2007 |url=http://jnonline.us/main.asp?SectionID=3&SubSectionID=7&ArticleID=3398 |access-date=July 21, 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928083415/http://jnonline.us/main.asp?SectionID=3&SubSectionID=7&ArticleID=3398 <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archive-date=September 28, 2007}}</ref> The film went on to become a commercial and critical success, and became Blonsky's breakthrough role.<ref>{{Cite web|title='Chuck & Larry' Can't Stop 'Hairspray' Sheen|url=https://www.boxofficemojo.com/article/ed3564700676/|access-date=2020-06-25|website=Box Office Mojo}}</ref> She received praise for her performance, with [[Roger Ebert]] of The ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]'' writing "Without somebody like Nikki Blonsky at the heart of the movie, it might fall flat, but everybody works at her level of happiness..."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ebert|first=Roger|title=Hairspray movie review & film summary (2007) {{!}} Roger Ebert|url=https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/hairspray-2007|access-date=2020-10-09|website=rogerebert.com/|language=en}}</ref> She received several awards and nominations, including winning a [[Critic's Choice Awards]] for Best Actress and Best Acting Ensemble,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|author=BWW News Desk|title=Blonsky, Coen, 'Enchanted' & 'Hairspray' Win Critics' Choice Awards|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Blonsky-Coen-Enchanted-Hairspray-Win-Critics-Choice-Awards-20080108|access-date=2020-06-25|website=BroadwayWorld.com|language=en}}</ref> and being nominated a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Motion Picture Comedy or Musical|Golden Globe Award for Best Actress]] and the [[Screen Actors Guild Awards]] for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=WarnerBros.com {{!}} Home of WB Movies, TV, Games, and more!|url=https://www.warnerbros.com/|access-date=2020-06-25|website=warnerbros.com}}</ref><ref name=":3" />

On June 22, 2008, she sang the [[American National Anthem]] in the pre-race ceremony at the 2008 Toyota/Save Mart 350 at [[Infineon Raceway]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/pictures/celebrity-circuit-23-6-08/6/| title=Celebrity Circuit: At the Races|publisher=[[CBS News]]|date= June 2008|access-date= June 3, 2019|archive-date= June 3, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190603164358/https://www.cbsnews.com/pictures/celebrity-circuit-23-6-08/6/|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2009, Blonsky also co-starred in the [[MTV]] mystery series ''[[Valemont]]'' in 2009. In 2010, Blonsky co-starred with [[Raven Goodwin]] in the short lived [[ABC Family]] series ''[[Huge (TV series)|Huge]]'', which premiered in June 2010,<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/television/news/e3ib6547f261e43d0f6628afca3fe2c9d75|title=Nikki Blonsky to star in ABC Family's 'Huge'|last=Andreeva|first=Nellie|date=March 22, 2010|magazine=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|access-date=March 23, 2010|archive-date=January 3, 2013|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130103104028/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/nikki-blonsky-star-abc-familys-21880|url-status=live}}</ref> and ran for one season. For the role, Blonsky received a nomination for a [[Teen Choice Awards|Teen Choice Award]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|date=2011-03-29|url=http://www.teenchoiceawards.com/pdf/TC10-Winners.pdf|access-date=2020-06-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110329170414/http://www.teenchoiceawards.com/pdf/TC10-Winners.pdf|archive-date=March 29, 2011}}</ref>

In 2011, Blonsky earned a [[cosmetology]] license, and began working part-time as a hairstylist and make-up artist in her hometown of [[Great Neck, New York]], between acting auditions.<ref>{{cite web|last=Lovece|first=Frank|date=December 12, 2011|title=Nikki Blonsky: No beauty school dropout|url=http://www.newsday.com/entertainment/nikki-blonsky-no-beauty-school-dropout-1.3384825|url-status=live|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120910170748/http://www.newsday.com/entertainment/nikki-blonsky-no-beauty-school-dropout-1.3384825|archive-date=September 10, 2012|work=[[Newsday]]|location=New York City / Long Island}}</ref>

In 2013, Blonsky appeared in two episodes of the NBC Broadway drama series ''[[Smash (U.S. TV series)|Smash]]''.<ref name="latimes1">{{cite news |last=Barton |first=Chris |url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/arts/culture/la-et-cm-smash-nicki-blonsky-jesse-l-martin-20120917,0,2094184.story |title='Smash' adds Broadway vets Nikki Blonsky, Jesse L. Martin |newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]] |date=September 17, 2012 |access-date=February 8, 2013 |archive-date=January 26, 2020 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20200126194942/https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/arts/la-xpm-2012-sep-17-la-et-cm-smash-nicki-blonsky-jesse-l-martin-20120917-story.html |url-status=live }}</ref> Later that year, she appeared in [[Geography Club (film)|the film adaptation]] of the book ''[[Geography Club]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/geography_club_2013/|title=Geography Club (2013)|publisher=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|access-date=June 3, 2019}}</ref> The film received mixed reviews.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Lopez|first1=John|last2=Angeles|first2=ContributorWriting/filmmaker living in Los|date=2013-04-30|title=Newport Film Festival's 'The Geography Club' Depicts the New Normal of Gay Relationships|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/newport-film-festivals-th_b_3188384|access-date=2020-06-25|website=HuffPost|language=en}}</ref> She and the cast were nominated for an award at the [[ShoWest Convention]] in 2014.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|title=ShoWest Convention, USA (2014)|url=http://www.imdb.com/event/ev0000608/2014/1/|access-date=2020-10-09|website=IMDb}}</ref>

Blonsky appeared [[Off-Broadway]] in the play ''Stuffed'' by [[Lisa Lampanelli]] at the Westside Theatre in October 2017 to November 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://iobdb.com/Production/6428 |title=''Stuffed''|publisher=[[Internet Off-Broadway Database]]|access-date= June 3, 2019}}</ref> In 2020, Blonsky launched her video blogging series on her [[Instagram]] account, in which she chats with other celebrities.

==Personal life==
In July 2008, Blonsky and her parents were involved in a violent confrontation with the family of [[Bianca Golden]], a former contestant on ''[[America's Next Top Model]]'', at the [[Providenciales International Airport]] in [[Turks and Caicos]].<ref name="Michelle Tauber">{{cite news|url= http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20231966,00.html|title= Nikki Blonsky: Airport Brawl Left Me 'Destroyed'|first=Michelle|last=Tauber|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=October 9, 2008|access-date=March 22, 2014}}</ref> Golden, Blonsky, and Blonsky's father were all charged with assault.<ref>{{cite magazine|url = http://people.com/crime/nikki-blonskys-father-denied-bail-in-airport-brawl-case/| title=Nikki Blonsky's Father Denied Bail in Airport Brawl Case|first=Steve|last=Helling| magazine=People| date=August 8, 2008 |access-date= February 10, 2017}}</ref> That December, the charges against Blonsky and Golden were dropped.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/77088/nikki-blonsky-next-top-modeler-cleared-in-airport-smackdown | title=Nikki Blonsky, Next Top Modeler Cleared in Airport Smackdown|first=Gina|last=Serpe| publisher=[[E! News]]| date=December 31, 2008 |access-date= February 10, 2017}}</ref>

In June 2020, Blonsky [[coming out|came out]] as a [[lesbian]] in a video posted to the social media site [[TikTok]] set to the song "[[I'm Coming Out]]" by [[Diana Ross]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nikki Blonsky on TikTok|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@therealnikkiblonsky/video/6843486113852591365?request_from=server|access-date=2020-06-28|publisher=Nikki Blonsky [[TikTok]] account|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://people.com/movies/hairspray-star-nikki-blonsky-comes-out-as-gay/|title=Hairspray Star Nikki Blonsky Comes Out as Gay While Lip-Syncing Diana Ross' 'I'm Coming Out'|first=Benjamin |last=VanHoose |date=June 29, 2020|access-date=June 29, 2020|magazine=[[People (magazine)|People]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/rambling-reporter/nikki-blonsky-coming-as-gay-it-was-a-long-time-coming-1301051|title = Nikki Blonsky on Coming Out as Gay: "It Was a Long Time Coming"|date = June 30, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.today.com/popculture/hairspray-star-nikki-blonsky-comes-out-gay-tiktok-video-t185505|title='Hairspray' star Nikki Blonsky comes out as gay to Diana Ross song}}</ref> In August 2020, Blonsky confirmed that she currently resides on Long Island.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Nikki Blonsky Interview|url=http://famadillo.com/nikki-blonsky-exclusive-interview-tiktok-long-island-and-podcasts/|publisher=Famadillo.com|language=en}}</ref>
