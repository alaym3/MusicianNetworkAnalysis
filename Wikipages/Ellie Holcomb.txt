'''Elizabeth Asher Holcomb'''<ref name=ascap>ASCAP IPI #584603533</ref> (née '''Bannister'''; born September 12, 1982) is an American [[Contemporary Christian music|CCM]]-[[folk music|folk]] singer-songwriter raised in Nashville, Tennessee.<ref name=allaccess /> Her father is noted music producer [[Brown Bannister]], and she was a member of [[Drew Holcomb and the Neighbors]], whose frontman is her husband Drew. They met while in school at the [[University of Tennessee]] together.

==Early life and education==
Elizabeth Asher Bannister was born in [[Nashville, Tennessee]] on September 12, 1982 to parents [[Brown Bannister]] and Debra Bannister. She graduated from [[Christ Presbyterian Academy]]<ref>https://www.allaccess.com/christian/10-questions/archive/25790/10-questions-with-ellie-holcomb</ref> and the [[University of Tennessee]], in 2005, with her bachelor's degree in English and in 2006 with her Masters of Science in Teacher Education.<ref name=degree>{{cite web|url=http://www.utk.edu/commencement/program/2006.pdf |title=Annual Commencement of The University of Tennessee |date=May 12, 2006 |author=University of Tennessee |publisher=University of Tennessee |accessdate=February 16, 2014 |pages=27–28 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120917084221/http://www.utk.edu/commencement/program/2006.pdf |archivedate=September 17, 2012  }}</ref> In 2006, Bannister married Drew Holcomb, and they have three children.<ref name=knoxnews>{{cite web|url=http://www.knoxnews.com/news/2008/aug/29/holcomb-finds-perfect-person-to-occupy-passenger/?print=1|title=Holcomb finds perfect person to occupy 'Passenger Seat'|last=Bledsoe|first=Wayne|date=August 29, 2008|work=[[Knoxville News Sentinel]]|accessdate=February 16, 2014}}</ref><ref name=crossrhythms>{{cite web|title=Drew Holcomb & The Neighbors: Folk roots, alternative rock or Americana|last=Cummings|first=Tony|url=http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/articles/music/Drew_Holcomb__The_Neighbors_Folk_roots_alternative_rock_or_Americana/52663/p1/|date=November 24, 2013|authorlink=Tony Cummings|publisher=[[Cross Rhythms]]|accessdate=February 16, 2014}}</ref>{{failed verification|date=March 2019}} They reside together in Nashville, Tennessee.{{citation needed|date=March 2019}}

== Music career ==

Holcomb began her musical career with [[Drew Holcomb and the Neighbors]] in 2005, and she started her individual career pursuit in 2011. In 2011, she released the ''[[Magnolia EP]]'', which charted on numerous ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts on September 10, 2011. Her second EP, ''[[With You Now]]'' did not come out until 2013, and this album peaked at No. 7 on the [[Christian Albums]] chart, which happened on September 7, 2013. Holcomb released her debut studio album on the Full Heart Music label on February 18, 2014, ''[[As Sure as the Sun]]'', which has garnered critical praise. She was awarded Best New Artist at the 2014 GMA Dove Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|last=GMA|date=2014-10-16|title=NEWS: 2014 Dove Awards Recap + Winners|url=https://gospelmusic.org/news-2014-dove-awards-recap-winners/|access-date=2021-03-31|website=The Gospel Music Association|language=en-US}}</ref>

Following the success of ''As Sure as the Sun'', Ellie Holcomb released her second full-length album, ''Red Sea Road'' on January 27, 2017. ''Red Sea Road'' was produced by her father, Brown Bannister, who was battling cancer while Ellie recorded the album. The family banded together and this album was often an outlet for Ellie. The album peaked at No. 2 on the Christian Chart. Three songs have charted in the Top Christian Singles: "Find You Here" peaked at No. 21, "Red Sea Road" peaked at No. 13 and "Wonderfully Made" reached No. 143.{{citation needed|date=March 2019}}

In 2018, Holcomb released two children's works: ''Sing: Creation Songs'' an album and ''Who Sang the First Song?'' a book.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=20289|title=Sing: Creation Songs EP by Ellie Holcomb – CD Reviews And Information – NewReleaseToday|website=www.newreleasetoday.com|accessdate=March 17, 2019}}</ref>
