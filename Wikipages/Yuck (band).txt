'''Yuck''' were a [[Rock music|rock]]<ref name=Soundof/><ref name=yuck-for-liverpool-sound-city/> band that originated in London, England in 2009. The band's final lineup was drummer Jonny Rogoff, guitarist Ed Hayes, bassist Mariko Doi, and lead vocalist/guitarist Max Bloom, who formerly played in the band [[Cajun Dance Party]] along with former Yuck member [[Daniel Blumberg]].<ref>[http://allmusic.com/artist/yuck-p2114799/biography/ Yuck – Biography] at [[Allmusic]], Retrieved 18 February 2011.</ref> The band's [[Yuck (album)|self-titled debut album]] was released through [[Fat Possum]] on 21 February 2011 in the United Kingdom. Critics have likened the band to bands such as [[Dinosaur Jr.]], [[The Smashing Pumpkins]], [[Pavement (band)|Pavement]], [[My Bloody Valentine (band)|My Bloody Valentine]] and [[Sonic Youth]].<ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/reviews/4v9p Yuck – Review] [[BBC]], Retrieved 20 February 2011.</ref>

==History==
Daniel Blumberg and Max Bloom left their previous group [[Cajun Dance Party]] in 2008. They formed Yuck in 2009 in London, England. The group began recording its debut studio album in the summer of the 2010 in London in Max Bloom's parents' house. The band toured with [[Modest Mouse]], [[Tame Impala]], and [[Unknown Mortal Orchestra]]

On 12 April 2012, the band released the non-album track, "Chew" via their official [[SoundCloud]] page.<ref>[https://soundcloud.com/yuck/chew Chew by Yuck on SoundCloud - Hear the world's sounds]</ref>

On 15 April 2013, Yuck revealed on their official [[Facebook]] page that they would be recording their second album in late April. They also revealed that Daniel Blumberg had left the band to concentrate on releasing his own music.<ref>[https://www.facebook.com/yuckband/posts/633921409967025 Facebook update] Retrieved 15 April 2013.</ref>

On 18 July 2013, Yuck released a new song, "Rebirth" and it was made available for free download via their official website.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q23oLtRfzCU Yuck - Rebirth - YouTube (Published on 18 Jul 2013)] Retrieved 15 April 2013.</ref>

On 13 August 2013, [[Huw Stephens]] debuted Yuck's new single, "Middle Sea" on Radio 1. The new album was revealed to be titled ''[[Glow & Behold]]'' during an interview with lead singer Max Bloom. It was released on 30 September 2013.<ref>[http://umusic.co.uk/artists/yuck/discography/glow-and-behold Yuck - Glow And Behold Previews, trackist, reviews and artwork from Universal UK Umusic.co.uk] Retrieved 15 April 2013.</ref>

On 5 September 2013, Yuck posted a video of a live recording of [[New Order (band)|New Order]]'s "[[Age of Consent (song)|Age of Consent]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7b5QmYxa20g&feature=player_embedded#t=0 |title=Yuck - Age of Consent (New Order cover) - Live at RAK |publisher=YouTube |date=2013-09-05 |access-date=2018-02-14}}</ref> The video was recorded 30 August 2013 by [[Michael Lawrence (filmmaker)|Michael Lawrence]] in [[RAK Studios]], along with some other songs.  This track would become the [[A-side and B-side|B-side]] to the "Middle Sea" single. New member and guitarist Ed Hayes plays on the track.

On 23 January 2014, at a performance in Portland, Oregon, Yuck announced an EP. They played a new song from the EP after the announcement. The ''Southern Skies'' EP was released in April 2014.

On 8 July 2015, Yuck released their first single since the release of the ''Southern Skies'' EP entitled "Hold Me Closer".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Coughlan|first1=Jamie|title=Yuck Share Euphoric New Single 'Hold Me Closer'|url=http://overblown.co.uk/yuck-share-euphoric-new-single-hold-me-closer/|website=Overblown|access-date=14 July 2015}}</ref> On 12 January 2016 the band released a new single, "Hearts in Motion", taken from their third studio album ''[[Stranger Things (Yuck album)|Stranger Things]]'', released on 26 February 2016.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Coughlan|first1=Jamie|title=Yuck Share 'Hearts in Motion', Announce New Album|url=http://overblown.co.uk/yuck-share-hearts-in-motion-announce-new-abum/|website=Overblown Webzine|access-date=12 January 2016}}</ref>

On 15 February 2021 the band posted a message to their Facebook page to announce that they would no longer be touring or making new music together.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2116334/yuck-announce-breakup-on-10th-anniversary-of-debut-album/news/|title=Yuck Announce Breakup On 10th Anniversary Of Debut Album|first=James|last=Rettig|publisher=Stereogum|date=15 February 2021|access-date=15 February 2021}}</ref>
