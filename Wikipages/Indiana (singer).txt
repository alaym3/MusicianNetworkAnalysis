'''Lauren Henson''', better known by her stage name '''Indiana''', is a British singer-songwriter from [[Loughborough]]. Her 2015 debut album, ''[[No Romeo]]'', includes the UK top 20 single "[[Solo Dancing]]" (2014).

==Biography==
Henson grew up in Loughborough, Leicestershire.<ref name=Guardian>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/sep/14/lauren-henson-indiana-singer-interview|title=Indiana: 'After gigs you'll find me changing nappies'|author=Nadia Khomami|newspaper=The Guardian|date=2014-09-13}}</ref> She moved to [[Nottingham]] to live with her partner, with whom she has two children.<ref name=Guardian/><ref name=NP-2013/> She worked as a printer in a T-shirt boutique and runs a fashion label called Some Kind of Nature, which specialises in [[streetwear]], with her partner and a mutual friend.<ref name=Guardian/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundspheremag.com/spotlight/band/spotlight-indiana/|title=Spotlight: Indiana|work=Soundsphere magazine}}</ref> The family had moved to [[Long Eaton]] in Derbyshire by 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.derbytelegraph.co.uk/time-heard-crowd-singing/story-26554947-detail/story.html|title=Indiana: Check out the Derbyshire pop star who played Glastonbury and has a top-20 single and album &#124; Derby Telegraph|date=24 May 2015|website=www.derbytelegraph.co.uk|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150524153903/http://www.derbytelegraph.co.uk/time-heard-crowd-singing/story-26554947-detail/story.html|access-date=23 July 2020|archive-date=24 May 2015}}</ref>

Henson began writing songs in 2010, after she taught herself to play piano using one that she was storing temporarily for her sister.<ref name=NP/> She was approached by musician [[John Beck (songwriter)|John Beck]] after she uploaded a cover of one of his compositions, "[[Gabriel (Joe Goddard song)|Gabriel]]", to the video sharing website [[YouTube]].<ref name=tlobf>{{cite web|url=http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/introducing/indiana-125371|title=Introducing: Indiana - The Line Of Best Fit|author=Andriana Albert|work=The Line of Best Fit}}</ref> They wrote their first song together, "Blind as I Am", which she performed at her first gig in April 2012, at the semi-finals of the live music competition Future Sound of Nottingham.<ref name=NP>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nottinghampost.com/Indiana-s-come-long-way/story-15933985-detail/story.html|title=Indiana's already come a long way &#124; Nottingham Post|date=26 July 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150726170054/http://www.nottinghampost.com/Indiana-s-come-long-way/story-15933985-detail/story.html|archive-date=26 July 2015}}</ref> Henson took her stage name, '''Indiana''', in memory of her late father, who was a fan of the [[Indiana Jones (franchise)|''Indiana Jones'' film series]].<ref name=tlobf/><ref name=Billboard>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6143201/indiana-solo-dancing-interview-no-romeo|title=Indiana Interview: Your New Favorite Pop Artist On 'Solo Dancing,' Stardom and Her 'Dark, Twisted' Pop. - Billboard|work=Billboard}}</ref>

"Blind as I Am" was released as Indiana's debut single in November 2012, followed by "Bound" in February 2013, "Smoking Gun" in June 2013, and "Mess Around" in October 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/indianathegirl/posts/164252600384008|title=Indiana - Blind as I am is now available on iTunes, I... - Facebook|work=facebook.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/smoking-gun-single/id720735845|title=Smoking Gun - Single|work=iTunes}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/mess-around-ep/id690085077|title=Mess Around - EP|work=iTunes}}</ref> Also in 2013, Indiana signed a record deal with [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony Music]] and played at the [[Splendour in Nottingham]] and [[Glastonbury Festival|Glastonbury]] music festivals, the latter on the [[BBC Introducing]] stage.<ref name=NP-2013/> She performed for Queen [[Elizabeth II]] during the monarch's visit to [[BBC Radio 1]], singing a cover of [[David Bowie]]'s "[[Heroes (David Bowie song)|Heroes]]" with Irish band [[The Script]].<ref name=NP-2013>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nottinghampost.com/m-weirdo-Indiana/story-20052759-detail/story.html|title=Notts music star Indiana on The Queen, Radio 1 and motherhood &#124; Nottingham Post|date=10 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131110035015/http://www.nottinghampost.com/m-weirdo-Indiana/story-20052759-detail/story.html|archive-date=10 November 2013}}</ref>

Indiana's fifth single, "[[Solo Dancing]]", became her breakthrough release, amassing substantial attention from music blogs and inclusion on ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine's mid-year "Singles of 2014" list.<ref name=Billboard/> Released commercially in April 2014, it reached number 14 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref name=officialcharts>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/12373/indiana/|title=INDIANA|work=officialcharts.com}}</ref> "[[Heart on Fire (Indiana song)|Heart on Fire]]", its follow-up (in August 2014), was less successful, peaking at number 89 in the UK.<ref name=officialcharts/> On 4 September 2014, Indiana appeared on BBC Radio 1's Innuendo Bingo.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MzhUjjre0Hk |title=Indiana does Innuendo Bingo |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2014-09-04 |access-date=2020-04-14}}</ref> and appeared again on 6 January 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_2IG5oC3tbs |title=Innuendo Bingo with Indiana |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=2015-01-06 |access-date=2020-04-14}}</ref>  Indiana's debut album, ''[[No Romeo]]'', was released in February 2015 and reached number 17 on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref name=officialcharts/> John Beck co-wrote and produced the majority of ''No Romeo'', which also features contributions from [[Joel Pott]] and [[Jesse Shatkin]]. Indiana embarked on a UK tour in May 2015 and performed for the second time at the Glastonbury Festival the following month.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gigwise.com/news/97578/indiana-announces-uk-headline-tour-dates-for-may---tickets|title=Indiana announces UK headline tour for May 2015 - tickets|author=Alexandra Pollard|work=gigwise.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.derbytelegraph.co.uk/8203-Indiana-Returns-Glastonbury/story-26722704-detail/story.html|title=Derbyshire artist Indiana returns to Glastonbury &#124; Derby Telegraph|date=24 June 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150624175654/http://www.derbytelegraph.co.uk/8203-Indiana-Returns-Glastonbury/story-26722704-detail/story.html|archive-date=24 June 2015}}</ref>

In July 2015, Indiana released an EP entitled ''State of Play'', which features her track "Blind as I Am" alongside cover versions of [[George Michael]]'s "[[Careless Whisper]]", [[Dolly Parton]]'s "[[Jolene (song)|Jolene]]", and [[Blu Cantrell]]'s "[[Breathe (Blu Cantrell song)|Breathe]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.josepvinaixa.com/blog/indiana-state-of-play-ep-available-on-itunes/|title=Ultimate Music - Indiana "State of Play – EP" - Available on iTunes!|work=Ultimate Music}}</ref> In November, she was featured on the single "Dance on My Heart" by producers Mark Knight and Adrian Hour.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.youredm.com/2015/11/09/mark-knight-adrian-hour-team-up-with-indiana-for-dance-on-my-heart-video/ |title = Mark Knight & Adrian Hour Team up with Indiana for "Dance on My Heart" &#91;Video&#93;|date = 2015-11-09}}</ref>

In 2018, Indiana was featured on the title track for [[Gunship (band)|Gunship]]'s "[[Dark All Day]]" album, also featuring prolific 80's saxophone sensation [[Tim Cappello]].

===Topless photo controversy===
In September 2018, Indiana sparked controversy when she released topless photos of herself, her hands over her breasts, taken in front of a [[World War I]] memorial plaque in a London church.<ref>{{cite news |last=Petter |first=Olivia |title=Singer sparks controversy by posing topless in front of WW1 memorial |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/singer-topless-photo-world-war-one-memorial-ww1-instagram-indiana-lauren-henson-a8525456.html |work=[[The Independent]] |access-date=25 October 2018 |date=6 September 2018}}</ref> She later explained that she did not realise it was a war memorial when the photos were taken, and defended her actions by saying, "Pretty sure those boys that gave their lives for this country would enjoy a casual bit of side-boob."<ref>{{cite web |last=Gorman |first=Rachel |title=Photo of topless Nottingham singer at church war memorial causes controversy |url=https://www.nottinghampost.com/whats-on/music-nightlife/photo-topless-nottingham-singer-church-1968275 |work=[[Nottingham Post]] |access-date=25 October 2018 |date=7 September 2018}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Indiana describes her music as "menacing and emotional [[electronica]] with 80s-inspired synths and haunting vocals".<ref name=Glamour/> She cites [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]], [[Gary Numan]], [[Frank Ocean]], [[Portishead (band)|Portishead]], [[The Weeknd]], and [[the xx]] as her biggest musical influences.<ref name=tlobf/><ref name=Glamour>{{cite web|url=http://www.glamourmagazine.co.uk/celebrity/entertainment/monitor/2013/10/indiana-singer-interview-mess-around|title=Indiana Singer Interview Mess Around – Music News (Glamour.com UK)|work=glamourmagazine.co.uk}}</ref> She has mentioned the soundtrack of the 2011 film ''[[Drive (2011 film)|Drive]]'' as being a major inspiration for her work.<ref name=DS>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/interviews/a593371/indiana-interview-i-have-to-think-about-selling-records-now.html#~paSQOfT3Wof8fE|title=Indiana interview: 'I have to think about selling records now'|work=Digital Spy|date=2014-08-29}}</ref>
