== Early life ==
Black Atlass was born in [[Montreal|Montreal, Quebec]]. After his father got a new job his family settled in Canada, Ontario, where he grew up.<ref name=":3" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Leijon|first=Erik|title=Black Atlass navigates his way to the stars|url=http://www.montrealgazette.com/entertainment/Black+Atlass+navigates+stars/7997324/story.html|access-date=2020-11-27|website=www.montrealgazette.com|language=en-ca}}</ref><ref name=":4" />

== Career ==
Black Atlass began his career in the music industry while he was still in high school.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|title=Black Atlass: The anti-Justin Bieber - Macleans.ca|url=https://www.macleans.ca/culture/hes-the-anti-justin-bieber/|access-date=2020-11-27|website=www.macleans.ca}}</ref>  He created an eponymous EP, ''The Black Atlass'' EP, in his parent’s basement in 2012. The EP’s single, Paris, was used by the luxury retailer, Louis Vuitton, to score a film celebrating their exhibit at the [[Musée des Arts Décoratifs, Paris|Musée Des Arts Décoratifs]].<ref name=":9">{{Cite web|last=Brown|first=Jacob|title=Artist of the Week: Black Atlass|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/artist-of-the-week-black-atlass|access-date=2020-11-27|website=Vogue|language=en-us}}</ref> The track was also featured in a Saint Laurent fragrance ad in 2013. The same year, Black Atlass was invited to the front row and dressed by Christian Dior’s menswear designer [[Kris Van Assche]] for their spring/summer 2014 show in Paris.<ref name=":8" /><ref name=":10">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=One to watch: Black Atlass|url=https://www.buro247.me/culture/music/one-to-watch-black-atlass.html|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref><ref name=":2" />

He released his second EP, ''Young Bloods'', in 2014, on Fool’s Gold Records.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black Atlass|url=http://first-avenue.morseko.de/performer/black-atlass/|access-date=2020-11-27|website=First Avenue|language=en-US}}</ref> To promote the EP, toured in support of the French artist Woodkid, with dates in Toronto, Montreal and across Europe. Woodkid also directed the music video for one of the EP’s songs, "Jewels", which starred the model Anja Rubik. 

In the fall of 2016, [[Alexander Wang (designer)|Alexander Wang]] featured Black Atlass in his fashion campaign alongside Kylie Jenner, Vince Staples, Lexi Boling, Zoe Kravitz, A$AP Ferg, Big Sean, [[Metro Boomin]], [[MØ]], [[Skrillex]], [[Tinashe]], [[Tyga]] and others.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title=How Black Atlass Became Fashion's Latest It-Boy: Exclusive|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/lifestyle/7728808/how-black-atlass-became-fashions-latest-it-boy|access-date=2020-11-27|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":4">{{Cite web|date=2018-07-13|title=MEET: Black Atlass - The Fresh New Face of Fashion And R&B|url=http://www.umusic.ca/2018/07/13/meet-black-atlass/|access-date=2020-11-27|website=Umusic|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2018, he was signed to The Weeknd’s record label XO, and released his debut studio album, ''Pain & Pleasure''. In the same year, he collaborated with Jessie Reyez on the song "Sacrifice", which was featured on the [[Fifty Shades Freed]] soundtrack.<ref name=":7" /><ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4" /><ref name=":0" />

Black Atlass’ second studio album, ''Dream Awake'', was released on April 3, 2020. The album consists of 12 tracks.<ref name=":5" /><ref name=":6" /><ref name=":7" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Black Atlass on Joining XO and Weaving Dreams {{!}} Northern Clutch|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/08/black-atlass-on-joining-xo-and-weaving-dreams|access-date=2020-12-07|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>

== Recognitions and nominations ==
In April 2013, Black Atlass was recognized as ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue's]]'' "Artist of the Week".<ref name=":9" /> His album ''Pain & Pleasure'' was nominated for a JUNO Award for "R&B/Soul Recording Of The Year" in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Juno Awards: Full List of Winners {{!}} Hollywood Reporter|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/lists/juno-awards-2019-winners-list-full-1180913/item/r-b-soul-recording-year-1180878|access-date=2020-11-27|website=www.hollywoodreporter.com}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Black Atlass is interested in photography and graphic design, and originally intended to carry both as a profession after high school. <ref name=":3" />
