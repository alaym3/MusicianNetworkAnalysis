'''Air Traffic Controller''' is an [[indie pop]] band that started in [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]].

The lead singer, singer/songwriter Dave Munro, had previously served in the [[United States Navy]] as an [[air traffic controller]], inspiring the name for the band.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/music/2012/09/20/boston-band-air-traffic-controller-cleared-for-take-off/XSVGfdrOyHwTzLDfMpiaLK/story.html|title=Boston band Air Traffic Controller is cleared for take-off - The Boston Globe|website=BostonGlobe.com|access-date=2016-04-08}}</ref> While he was serving in the navy, he sent home demos from his music and later met singer/songwriter Casey Sullivan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.airtrafficcontrollermusic.com/bio/|title=Bio|website=Air Traffic Controller|access-date=2016-03-31}}</ref>

== Reception ==
The band was a 2015 ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot 100 Fest performer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/air-traffic-controller/biography/|title=Air Traffic Controller Bio {{!}} Air Traffic Controller Career|website=MTV Artists|access-date=2016-04-08}}</ref> Additionally, they have over 15 million [[Spotify]] plays. They were selected as a Showcase Artist for [[SXSW]] 2016 and made the [[NPR]] Hot 100 List for [[SXSW]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://schedule.sxsw.com/2016/events/event_MS36545|title=Air Traffic Controller  |website= SXSW 2016 Event Schedule|access-date=2016-04-08}}</ref> They have also been featured a 'New Band of the Week' in ''[[The Guardian]]''.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/nov/25/air-traffic-controller|title=Air Traffic Controller (New band of the day No 1,647)|last=Lester|first=Paul|date=2013-11-25|newspaper=The Guardian|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077|access-date=2016-03-31}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.vanyaland.com/2016/03/11/song-premiere-air-traffic-controller-throw-down-the-alt-pop-gauntlet-with-warrior/|title=Song Premiere: Air Traffic Controller throw down the alt-pop gauntlet with 'Warrior'|website=Vanyaland|access-date=2016-03-31}}</ref> They have also been featured in multiple ''Guardian'' and ''Boston Globe'' articles.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/music/2012/06/14/noisy-neighbors-air-traffic-controller-nordo/A8c21duLGcU5wyxgZgSrAN/story.html|title=NOISY NEIGHBORS: Air Traffic Controller, ‘Nordo’ - The Boston Globe|website=BostonGlobe.com|access-date=2016-04-08}}</ref><ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> Their first album, ''The One'', was nominated for [[MTV]]'s "Best Break Out Artist Music Video" award in 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ifc.com/2012/06/premiere-air-traffic-controller-hurry-hurry-acoustic|title=Premiere: Air Traffic Controller "Hurry Hurry" (acoustic)|website=IFC|access-date=2016-04-08}}</ref> Their song, "People Watching," is featured in EA SPORTS [[NHL 17]]. In 2016, they won the Independent Music Award in the categories of Best Indie/Alt. Rock Album (''Black Box''), Best Music Producer (Bleu, also on ''Black Box'') and Best Indie/Alt. Rock song ("The House").<ref>{{cite web|title="The 15th Annual Independent Music Awards Nominees"|url=http://independentmusicawards.com/the-15th-independent-music-awards-nominees/|website=Independent Music Awards|publisher=Music Resource Group LLC|accessdate=26 June 2017}}</ref>
