'''Young Ejecta''' (formerly known as simply '''Ejecta''') is an American [[synthpop]] duo, consisting of [[Neon Indian]]'s Leanne Macomber and producer Joel Ford of [[Ford & Lopatin]]. The band name was changed to Young Ejecta in 2014 due to the group getting a copyright notice from the closely named DJ Ejeca.<ref name = "minialbum"/> Macomber appears nude on all official album covers and promotional material, and in most of their music videos.

==History==
In November 2013, Ejecta released their debut album, ''[[Dominae]]''. In 2015 issued their mini-album and second studio LP ''[[The Planet (album)|The Planet]]''.  The album received an indifferent review from PopMatters<ref>[http://www.popmatters.com/review/190095-young-ejecta-the-planet/ "Young Ejecta The Planet"]. ''PopMatters'', Colin Fitzgerald, 2 February 2015</ref> and a fairly positive one from ''Paste'' magazine.<ref>[https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2015/01/young-ejecta-your-planet-ep-review.html "Young Ejecta: Your Planet EP Review"]. ''Paste'', Adrian Spinelli, January 27, 2015</ref>  Heather Phares of [[Allmusic]] describes the band's synthpop sound as "gauzy".<ref>{{cite web|last=Phares|first=Heather|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/young-ejecta-mn0003343048/biography|title=Young Ejecta Biography|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=December 25, 2014}}</ref>

In August 2019, their song “Welcome to Love” was chosen as the outro audio of Netflix’s “Orange is the New Black” on the 10th episode of the 7th and final season.

==Members==
*[[Leanne Macomber]] – vocals, synthesizers
*[[Ford & Lopatin|Joel Ford]] – synthesizers, drum programming, producer<ref>[http://www.sfgate.com/music/article/A-rebirth-of-sorts-for-artist-in-Young-Ejecta-6514040.php "A rebirth, of sorts, for artist in Young Ejecta"]. ''SSF Gate'', By Ryan Kost September 18, 2015. </ref>
