'''Simbiatu''' "'''Simbi'''" '''Abisola Abiola Ajikawo'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/Catalog.aspx?detail=writerid&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&keyid=1543368&subid=0|title=BMI – Repertoire Search|website=repertoire.bmi.com}}</ref> (born 23 February 1994), better known by her stage name '''Little Simz''', is a [[British-Nigerian]] rapper, singer and actress of [[Yoruba people|Yoruba]] heritage. Following the release of four mixtapes and five EPs, she released her debut album ''A Curious Tale of Trials + Persons'' on 18 September 2015 through her independent label, AGE: 101 Music. The album entered the [[UK R&B Albums Chart]] at number 20<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/charts/r-and-b-albums-chart/20150925/115/|title=Official R&B Albums Chart Top 40 – Official Charts Company}}</ref> and the [[UK Independent Albums Chart]] at number 43.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/charts/independent-albums-chart/20150925/131/|title=Official Independent Albums Chart Top 50 – Official Charts Company}}</ref> She released her second studio album ''Stillness in Wonderland'' in 2016. Her third studio album ''[[Grey Area (album)|Grey Area]]'' (2019) won Best Album at both the [[Ivor Novello Awards]] and the [[NME Awards]] as well as being nominated for the [[Mercury Prize]].<ref name=mercury>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/little-simz-announces-new-album-grey-area-shares-new-song-listen/|title=Little Simz Announces New Album ''GREY Area'', Shares New Song: Listen|last=Blais-Billie|first=Braudie|date=16 January 2019|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|access-date=3 March 2019}}</ref> Her fourth album, ''[[Sometimes I Might Be Introvert]]'' (2021) received critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/sometimes-i-might-be-introvert/little-simz/|title=Little Simz – 'Sometimes I Might Be Introvert' review: stunning introspection from a true great|access-date=5 September 2021}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Ajikawo was born in [[Islington]], London to [[Nigerian]] parents. She was raised with two older sisters.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-08|title=Little Simz|url=https://notion.online/little-simz/|access-date=2020-12-23|website=Notion|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref name="Red Bull">{{cite web|last1=Ekanayake|first1=Manu|title=Who is… Little Simz?|url=http://www.redbull.com/gb-en/little-simz-need-to-know-interview|website=Red Bull|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> She is ethnically [[Yoruba people|Yoruba]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qqmv4daiBZg|title=Little Simz – 101 FM (Official Video)|last=Little Simz|date=12 June 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> She studied at [[Highbury Fields School]] in London. She went on to attend [[St Mary's, Islington|St Mary]]'s Youth Club in Upper Street, Islington, which pop stars [[Leona Lewis]] and [[Alexandra Burke]] also attended.<ref name="islingtongazette1">{{cite web |url=http://www.islingtongazette.co.uk/what-s-on/music/islington_rapper_lil_simz_destined_for_big_things_1_1416604 |title=Islington rapper Lil Simz destined for big things – Music |work=Islington Gazette |date=20 June 2012 |access-date=22 April 2013 }}{{dead link|date=February 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
Ajikawo later studied at [[Westminster Kingsway College]] and pursued her music career from there.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.westking.ac.uk/news/double-mobo-nominations-former-westminster-kingsway-college-student/|title=Double MOBO nominations  – Westminster Kingsway College|access-date=17 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170318091912/http://www.westking.ac.uk/news/double-mobo-nominations-former-westminster-kingsway-college-student/|archive-date=18 March 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Career ==

===Acting===
Simz appeared as Vicky in the BBC children's series [[Spirit Warriors (TV series)|''Spirit Warriors'']], originally broadcast in 2010,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://screenterrier.blogspot.com/2009/05/bo-and-spirit-world-casting-news.html |title=Bo and the Spirit World casting news |access-date= 1 December 2010|date=3 June 2009 |publisher=ScreenTerrier |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http%3A%2F%2Fscreenterrier.blogspot.com%2F2009%2F05%2Fbo-and-spirit-world-casting-news.html&date=2010-12-01 |archive-date=1 December 2010 }}</ref> and as Meleka in the [[E4 (TV channel)|E4]] television series, ''[[Youngers]]''.<ref name="leave2remainlil">{{cite web |url=http://www.leave2remain.org/2012/11/production-blog-2-lil-simz/ |title=Production Blog 2: Lil Simz |publisher=Leave2remain.org |date=4 December 2012 |access-date=22 April 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130415061741/http://www.leave2remain.org/2012/11/production-blog-2-lil-simz/ |archive-date=15 April 2013 }}</ref>

[[File:Little Simz, Seat Village stage 5.jpg|thumb|Little Simz at Primavera Sound 2019]]

Simz was also the narrator for the television series, ''Afrofuturism.''<ref>{{Citation|title=Afrofuturism|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt8806308/|access-date=6 May 2019}}</ref>

She appeared in [[Netflix]]'s revival and third series of ''[[Top Boy]]'' which premiered in Autumn 2019.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/newsbeat-47784979|title=Top Boy series three trailer: Dave and Little Simz join cast|date=2 April 2019|work=BBC News|access-date=20 August 2019}}</ref>

She also appeared in the Sony/Marvel movie "Venom - Let There Be Carnage", singing her song "Venom" in a nightclub.

===Music===
She describes her music as rap and experimental.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wikifestivals.com/content/bbc-introducing-and-aif-presentslil-simz |title=BBC Introducing and AIF presents...Lil Simz |publisher=WikiFestivals |date=n.d.|access-date=22 April 2013}}</ref>

Simz has performed alongside [[Estelle (musician)|Estelle]], [[Tinie Tempah]], [[Ms. Dynamite]] and [[Kano (rapper)|Kano]].<ref name="islingtongazette1"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/TheRealKano/status/753893891288526848?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw|title=Kano on Twitter}}{{Primary source inline|date=March 2020}}</ref>

Simz has performed at [[Rising Tide UK|Rising Tide]], [[iluvlive]], Industry Takeover (Urban Development) [[Hackney Empire]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/e9wmxj/acts/a336v2 |title=Radio 1's Hackney Weekend 2012 – Acts – Lil Simz |publisher=BBC |date=n.d.|access-date=22 April 2013}}</ref> [[Somerset House]] and the [[House of Lords]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://mobo.com/news-blogs/single-of-the-week-lil-simz-eastern-jam-freestyle |title=Single of The Week: Lil Simz – Eastern Jam (Freestyle) &#124; MOBO Awards |publisher=Mobo.com |date=n.d.|access-date=22 April 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151002103057/http://mobo.com/news-blogs/single-of-the-week-lil-simz-eastern-jam-freestyle |archive-date=2 October 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She also performed at the BBC [[BBC Radio 1Xtra|1Xtra]] Prom 2015 at the [[Royal Albert Hall]], alongside a full orchestra led by [[Jules Buckley]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b065yp93/credits|title=BBC Radio 3 – BBC Proms, 2015 Season, 1Xtra Prom – Credits|work=BBC}}</ref>

Simz can be heard on the ''Leave to Remain'' film soundtrack, performing the song "Leave It As That".<ref name="leave2remainlil"/> In early 2013, she appeared on [[BBC Radio 1 Xtra]] to discuss her performance at the [[Hackney Weekend]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b01rft58|title=BBC Radio 1Xtra – Adele Roberts, Little Simz comes by for a Brew 4 Two|work=BBC}}</ref>

Simz then went on to do a Maida Vale session for [[Huw Stephens]], and has since gone on to receive critical acclaim from the likes of [[Zane Lowe]], [[Dizzee Rascal]] and Revolt TV.{{Citation needed|date=March 2014}} She also has received praise from [[Kendrick Lamar]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eZsK8mw4VzM|title=MistaJam Speaks To Kendrick Lamar|last=BBC Radio 1Xtra|date=9 June 2015|via=YouTube}}</ref>

On 16 December 2016 she released her second studio album ''Stillness in Wonderland''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/12/12/little-simz-stillness-in-wonderland|title=Little Simz Is Releasing New Album Stillness in Wonderland On Friday}}</ref> It was inspired by [[Alice's Adventures in Wonderland]] and supported by a comic book, art exhibition and festival.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://clashmusic.com/features/through-the-rabbit-hole-clash-meets-little-simz|title=Through The Rabbit Hole: Clash Meets Little Simz|newspaper=Clash Magazine|access-date=12 February 2017}}</ref>

In 2017, she performed as the opening act for [[Gorillaz]] during their [[Humanz Tour]], and was the vocalist for the song "Garage Palace", featured on the Super Deluxe edition of their album ''[[Humanz]]''.

On 6 September 2018, Simz and her label AGE 101 signed a worldwide deal with [[AWAL|AWAL Recordings]],<ref>{{Cite web|first=|title=AWAL Announces Worldwide Partnership Deal With Little Simz|url=https://www.awal.com/press/announces-worldwide-partnership-little-simz|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-23|website=AWAL|language=en}}</ref> after AWAL had distributed her debut album "A Curious Tale of Trials + Persons" in 2015. This deal was renewed on 18 June 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-18|title=AWAL renews worldwide partnership with British rapper Little Simz|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/awal-renews-worldwide-partnership-with-british-rapper-little-simz/|access-date=2021-07-23|website=Music Business Worldwide|language=en-US}}</ref>

On 1 March 2019 she released her third studio album ''[[Grey Area (album)|Grey Area]]'' to widespread critical acclaim and was nominated for the ''[[Mercury Prize]]<ref name="mercury" />'' and [[Independent Music Companies Association|IMPALA]]'s European Independent Album of the Year Award (2019).<ref name="IMPALA19">{{Cite web|url=https://www.impalamusic.org/22-nominees-shortlisted-for-impalas-european-independent-album-of-the-year-award/|title=22 NOMINEES SHORTLISTED FOR IMPALA'S EUROPEAN INDEPENDENT ALBUM OF THE YEAR AWARD|website=www.impalamusic.org|date=January 30, 2020|access-date=September 17, 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Ajikawo lives in London.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-12-15|title=Sunday with Little Simz: 'I want to be inside and cosy'|url=http://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2019/dec/15/sunday-with-little-simz-rapper-i-want-to-be-inside-and-cosy|access-date=2020-12-23|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref> She is a childhood friend of actor [[Fady Elsayed]].

In 2018 her friend, model Harry Uzoka, was murdered, inspiring her to write her song "Wounds".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-09-21|title=Model jailed for murdering fashion rival in row over girlfriend|url=http://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2018/sep/21/model-george-koh-jailed-for-murdering-fashion-rival-harry-uzoka-in-row-over-girlfriend|access-date=2020-12-23|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref>

Ajikawo is a supporter of [[Arsenal F.C.]]<ref>{{Cite tweet |number=1128248599370981377 |user=LittleSimz |title=ARSENAL @Arsenal |date=14 May 2019 |access-date=28 February 2021}}</ref>
