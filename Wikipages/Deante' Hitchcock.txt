'''Deante' Van Hitchcock''' (born March 10, 1993), is an American rapper from [[Atlanta, Georgia]]. He initially gained recognition as a rapper following the releases of three mixtapes: ''19 Summers'' (2012), ''Wishful Thinking'' (2015) and ''Good'' (2016). He received widespread attention on the internet in 2016 after a weekly series of freestyles called New Atlanta Tuesday's.<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web |title=Deante' Hitchcock |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/deante-hitchcock-mn0003679309/biography |website=AllMusic}}</ref> In 2017, he signed to [[ByStorm Entertainment|ByStorm]] and [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Deante' Hitchcock |url=https://www.rcarecords.com/artist/deante-hitchcock/ |website=RCA Records}}</ref> His debut album ''[[Better (Deante' Hitchcock album)|Better]]'' was released on May 13, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Zidel |first1=Alex |title=Deante' Hitchcock Releases Debut Album "Better" With 6LACK, J.I.D, Young Nudy, & More |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/deante-hitchcock-releases-debut-album-better-with-6lack-jid-young-nudy-and-more-new-mixtape.120363.html |website=HotNewHipHop |accessdate=May 13, 2020}}</ref>

==Early life==
From Riverdale, a small suburb of Atlanta, Georgia, Deante' Hitchcock started rapping at age 12 where he was in a group. His uncle, who co-wrote [[TLC (group)|TLC]]'s "[[No Scrubs]]", introduced him to music.<ref>{{cite web |title=JUST A SAMPLE 2: AN INTERVIEW WITH DEANTE' HITCHCOCK |url=http://www.intheroughstyle.com/lifesgoods/2019/3/23/just-a-sample-2-an-interview-with-deante-hitchcock |website=In The Rough Style}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Cruz |first1=Ricky |title=Deante' Hitchcock: An Abnormally Talented MC To Look Out For |url=http://culture.affinitymagazine.us/deante-hitchcock-an-abnormally-talented-mc-to-look-out-for/ |website=Affinity Magazine |accessdate=11 May 2019}}</ref> He attended [[Georgia Southern University]], and dropped out in his junior year in college to pursue his music career.<ref>{{cite web |title=KINDA NEAT EPISODE 122: DEANTE' HITCHCOCK |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2tlhhiXMVRc |website=YouTube}}</ref>

==Musical career==
=== 2012–18: Career beginnings and various projects ===
Deante' Hitchcock's musical career began in college, when he released his first mixtape, ''19 Summers'', on September 17, 2012.<ref name="AllMusic" /> His first official mixtape titled ''Wishful Thinking'' was released on February 24, 2015.<ref>{{cite web |title=Wishful Thinking |url=https://soundcloud.com/deante-hitchcock/1-wishful-thinking-deante-hitchcock |website=Soundcloud}}</ref> During 2016, Hitchcock gained more recognition after releasing more weekly freestyles, which went viral and reached the attention of [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]]. He released his second mixtape titled ''Good'' on July 28, 2016. The mixtape was also available to purchase and on streaming services, and is the first of a trilogy.<ref>{{cite web |title=Good |url=https://open.spotify.com/album/0GkkYjnPcOSjKKojH8ChIS |website=Spotify}}</ref> The in-concert recording live album of this mixtape was released on March 9, 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=Good: Live |url=https://open.spotify.com/album/1FWNtScncbmmjQzrD7rYgu |website=Spotify}}</ref> A month prior, Hitchcock released his first EP called ''Just a Sample'' on Valentine's Day in 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=DEANTE' HITCHCOCK DROPS 'JUST A SAMPLE' OF DOPE MUSIC |url=http://2dopeboyz.com/2017/02/14/deante-hitchcock-just-a-sample-ep-stream/ |website=2dopeboyz}}</ref> On July 9, 2017, Hitchcock released an EP with fellow Atlanta rapper Michael Aristotle titled ''No Pressure''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Deante Hitchcock & Michael Aristotle - No Pressure [EP Stream] |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/deante-hitchcock-and-michael-aristotle-no-pressure-ep-stream-new-mixtape.117334.html |website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref>

Hitchcock's career received a boost when he signed with the [[RCA Records|RCA]]-affiliated [[ByStorm Entertainment|ByStorm]] label, where he released the six-track EP ''So Much for Good Luck'' on October 13, 2017.<ref name="AllMusic" /> The EP included the single "Wide Open" featuring [[GoldLink]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Wide Open By Deante' Hitchcock Feat. GoldLink Is As Real As It Gets |url=http://stereochampions.com/wide-open-deante-hitchcock-feat-goldlink-real-gets/ |website=Stereo Champions}}</ref> On June 18, 2018, he released his fourth EP called ''Thank You For Your Patience'' after the release of his album experienced delays due to sample clearances.<ref name="hotnewhiphop_118161">{{cite web |last1=P. |first1=Milca |title=Deante' Hitchcock Quenches The Thirst With "THANK YOU FOR YOUR PATIENCE" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/deante-hitchcock-quenches-the-thirst-with-thank-you-for-your-patience-new-mixtape.118161.html |website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref> In late 2018, he was on tour with [[6LACK]] for the [[East Atlanta Love Letter|From East Atlanta With Love]] tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/6lack-announces-world-tour-featuring-tierra-whack-boogie-and-more-news.57301.html |title=6LACK Announces World Tour Featuring Tierra Whack, Boogie & More |work=HotNewHipHop |date=14 August 2018|accessdate=September 3, 2018}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Just a Sample 2'' and ''Better'' ===
On January 6, 2019, Deante' Hitchcock was invited to the [[Dreamville Records|Dreamville]] recording sessions for the compilation album ''[[Revenge of the Dreamers III]]'', appearing on the song "PTSD".<ref>{{cite web |title=These 106 Artists Are Working on Dreamville's New Compilation LP |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/01/artists-invited-record-dreamville-revenge-dreamers-iii-album/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |accessdate=January 8, 2019 |date=January 8, 2019}}</ref> Hitchcock spoke with ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' about the sessions saying "The first day, everybody was surprised as hell when the shit first started popping up. Everybody started seeing folks posting golden tickets and invites. I peeped the location, like, 'Yo, it's in Atlanta? Right here in our backyard.' I ain’t get an invite on the first day. I was feeling some type of way. But I was like, 'That just mean I’ve got to work harder.' That next morning, I got my invite from Mark Pitts. He sent me and [Young] Nudy. I was there every day I could be."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kennedy |first1=John |title=AN ORAL HISTORY OF DREAMVILLE'S 'REVENGE OF THE DREAMERS III' RECORDING SESSIONS |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/04/oral-history-dreamville-revenge-of-the-dreamers-iii-recording-sessions/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |date=April 2, 2019}}</ref> He was also a supporting act during the [[DiCaprio 2|Catch Me If You Can Tour]] with [[JID]] in 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Findlay |first1=Mitch |title=J.I.D. Announces New String Of "Catch Me If You Can" Tour Dates |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/jid-announces-new-string-of-catch-me-if-you-can-tour-dates-news.71784.html |website=HotNewHipHop |accessdate=February 13, 2019 |date=February 12, 2019}}</ref>

On March 15, he released the EP ''Just a Sample 2'', with guest appearances from [[Childish Major]], [[H.E.R.]] and [[Kilo Ali]]. Prior to release, the EP was led by singles "7:45" and "Side Nigga Anthem".<ref name="hotnewhiphop_1982111">{{cite web |last1=P |first1=Milca |title=Deante' Hitchcock Returns With "7:45" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/deante-hitchcock-returns-with-7-45-new-song.1982111.html |website=HotNewHipHop }}</ref><ref name="hotnewhiphop_1982257">{{cite web |last1=P |first1=Milca |title=Deante' Hitchcock Issues The Official "Side N*gga Anthem" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/deante-hitchcock-issues-the-official-side-n-gga-anthem-new-song.1982257.html |website=HotNewHipHop }}</ref> He also released music videos for "Changed For You" and "Never (Let You Go)".<ref>{{cite web |last1=P |first1=Milca |title=Deante' Hitchcock Visits Couples' Therapy In "Changed For You" Clip |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/deante-hitchcock-visits-couples-therapy-in-changed-for-you-clip-new-video.45164.html |website=HotNewHipHop }}</ref> In November 2019, he released the single "How TF" featuring [[6lack|6LACK]], supported by a music video in March 2020.<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Lamarre |first1=Carl |title=Watch Deante Hitchcock Lose His Mind Over Love in 'How TF' Video With 6lack: Exclusive |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9343447/deante-hitchcock-how-tf-video |magazine=Billboard}}</ref> On April 15, he released the second single "I Got Money Now" featuring [[JID]]. His debut album ''Better'' was released on May 13, 2020, including guest appearances from [[Young Nudy]], [[Miguel (singer)|Miguel]], and [[St. Beauty]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rashed |first1=Ayana |title=Deante' Hitchcock Set To Release Debut Album 'Better' On May 13 via RCA |url=https://respect-mag.com/2020/04/deante-hitchcock-set-to-release-debut-album-better-on-may-13-via-rca/ |website=Respect Mag |date=15 April 2020 |accessdate=15 April 2020}}</ref>

==Artistry==
===Influences===
Deante' Hitchcock has been favorably compared to the likes of [[T.I.]] and [[J. Cole]]. Hitchcock also cited [[Lil Wayne]], [[Jay-Z]], [[André 3000]], and [[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]] as his favorite rappers.<ref name="AllMusic" /><ref>{{cite web |title=Atlanta's Best Kept Secret w/ Deante Hitchcock Day1Radio Live Podcast |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Gmi06Jgah4Q |website=YouTube}}</ref>
