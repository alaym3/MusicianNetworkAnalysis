'''Colony House''' is an American [[indie rock]] quartet from Franklin, Tennessee.<ref name=Allmusic/> Their debut album, ''When I Was Younger'', reached No. 154 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and No. 3 on the [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Colony House - Chart history |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6196029/colony-house/chart |accessdate=October 20, 2014}}</ref>

== Background ==

Natives of [[Franklin, Tennessee]], the band is composed of brothers Caleb Chapman (born October 2, 1989)<ref>{{cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/choosingtoseejou00chap_0|url-access=registration|page=[https://archive.org/details/choosingtoseejou00chap_0/page/54 54]|quote=Caleb Chapman October 2, 1989.|title=Choosing to SEE: A Journey of Struggle and Hope|first1=Mary Beth|last1=Chapman|first2=Ellen|last2=Vaughn|date=September 1, 2010|publisher=Revell|via=Internet Archive}}</ref> (vocals) and Will Chapman (born February 6, 1991)<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://marybethchapman.com/will-franklin-was-born-21-years-ago-today/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=November 18, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151119084020/http://marybethchapman.com/will-franklin-was-born-21-years-ago-today/ |archive-date=November 19, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref> (drums), guitarist Scott Mills, and bassist Parke Cottrell. Caleb and Will are the sons of [[Contemporary Christian music|contemporary Christian]] musician, [[Steven Curtis Chapman]]. The band began working together while in high school in 2009.<ref>{{Cite news |first=Bob |last=Boilen |date=May 22, 2014 |title=Colony House, 'Silhouettes' |publisher=[[NPR]] |url=https://www.npr.org/event/music/314625502/colony-house-silhouettes |accessdate=October 20, 2014}}</ref> They have described their sound as "rock n' roll" with "stripped-down instruments",<ref>{{Cite news |first=Liana |last=Weston |title=Sound Scout: Colony House Is Not Just Your Ordinary Rock Trio |magazine=[[Teen Vogue]] |url=http://www.teenvogue.com/entertainment/music/2014-10/colony-house-interview |accessdate=October 20, 2014}}</ref> influenced by [[U2]] and [[Cold War Kids]]. Originally named Caleb, they changed the name to Colony House in 2013, after an apartment complex where they had lived. They self-released three EPs, before releasing their debut album, ''When I Was Younger'', on July 22, 2014.<ref name=Allmusic>{{Cite web |first=Fred |last=Thomas |title=Colony House &#124; Biography |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/colony-house-mn0003268468/biography |accessdate=October 20, 2014}}</ref> The lead single from the album, "Silhouettes", received [[alternative rock]] radio airplay, and was the most downloaded song on [[SiriusXM]] [[Alt Nation]].<ref>{{Cite web |first=Jon |last=Chattman |title=''A-Sides with Jon Chattman'': Seeing "Silhouettes" of Colony House; "Delve" Into Unappreciated Songs By, Em, Overappreciated Artists |date=October 18, 2014 |work=[[HuffPost]] |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/jon-chattman/a-sides-with-jon-chattman_b_5688548.html |accessdate=October 20, 2014}}</ref> The band made their television debut on September 29, 2014 on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Late Night with Seth Meyers]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |title= Colony House Performance: "Silhouettes" |date=September 30, 2014 |publisher=[[NBC]] |url=http://www.nbc.com/late-night-with-seth-meyers/video/colony-house-performance-silhouettes/2815620 |accessdate=October 20, 2014}}</ref>

Colony House's second studio album, ''Only the Lonely'', released on January 13, 2017. Singles released off the album "You Know It", "Lonely" and "You & I" and "This Beautiful Life". The album charted at No. 76 on the ''Billboard'' 200 charts. The band then went on a headlining tour in support of the album with guest Deep Sea Diver and Knox Hamilton. In addition, they opened for Switchfoot's Native Tongue tour in 2019, along with [[Tyson Motsenbocker]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fantasyrecordings.com/switchfoot-announces-new-album-native-tongue-and-north-american-tour/|title=SWITCHFOOT ANNOUNCES NEW ALBUM 'NATIVE TONGUE' & NORTH AMERICAN TOUR|date=October 19, 2018|website=Fantasy Records|access-date=September 12, 2019}}</ref>

On September 9, 2019, the group announced the title of their third album, ''Leave What's Lost Behind''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/ColonyHouse/status/1171135093903114242|title=Leave What's Lost Behind // LP3pic.twitter.com/X4m2nGycl6|date=September 9, 2019|via=[[Twitter]]|access-date=September 12, 2019}}</ref> Three singles were released in advance of the album: "Looking for Some Light," "Original Material," and "Where I'm From."<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://thepreludepress.com/news/2019/11/15/colony-house-release-new-single-original-material|title=Colony House Release New Single, "Original Material"|website=The Prelude Press|access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref> ''Leave What's Lost Behind'' was released on January 24, 2020. An acoustic version of the albums leading track "Looking for Some Light" was released a few months later on April 1, 2020. Shortly after the release of the album, Colony House announced a nationwide "Leave What's Lost Behind Tour," with Tyson Motsenbocker, scheduled to run through the end of March 2020. The tour was postponed halfway through due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], and later completely cancelled.

Over the course of 2020, Colony House released a number of music videos and acoustic performances all filmed in the midwest.{{citation needed|date=April 2021}} "When the Walls Come Crashing Down" that featured [[Jon Foreman]] and [[Jillian Edwards]] was released on June 12, 2020.<ref name=":1" /> On October 10 and 11, 2020, they performed two back-to-back socially distanced drive-in concerts in their hometown of Franklin, Tennessee.{{citation needed|date=April 2021}} At the drive-in concert, Colony House premiered their film ''Everybody is Looking for Some Light''.{{citation needed|date=April 2021}} The film was part live concert, part documentary, and part music video, was made available as a streaming event on March 20, 2021.{{citation needed|date=April 2021}} A live album, ''Colony House Live, Vol. 1'', was released on January 29, 2021.{{citation needed|date=April 2021}} Colony House's film "Everybody is Looking For Some Light" was made available as a streaming event on March 20, 2021.{{citation needed|date=April 2021}}

On April 20, 2021, Colony House released the single "O YA". A couple months later on June 1, 2021, Colony House Announced the "Back Before You Know It Tour" with special guest [[Fleurie (musician)|Fleurie]], scheduled to run from September 10 to November 27, 2021 and cover almost the entirety of North America. The band released their long-anticipated single "Lights On" on June 18, 2021. On July 23, 2020, the band released "Automatic" featuring [[Fleurie (musician)|Fleurie]] which was their final single from ''Rotten Tomatoes'', the bands first EP under the name Colony house.<ref name=":2" /> ''Rotten Tomatoes'' was released on September 10, 2020.<ref name=":3" /> The "Back Before You Know it Tour" began on September 10, 2021 in Knoxville, Tennessee.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Contributor|first=Allison Padovani|title=Colony House is 'Back Before You Know It' with first show of their tour|url=https://www.utdailybeacon.com/city_news/entertainment/colony-house-is-back-before-you-know-it-with-first-show-of-their-tour/article_f47fba1c-13f9-11ec-8b10-0bfa8d4bd6f3.html|access-date=2021-09-22|website=The Daily Beacon|language=en}}</ref>
