'''Day Wave''' is an American [[indie rock]] project from [[Oakland, California]], formed in 2015. The band, based around musician Jackson Phillips, with accompanying musicians for live performances, released their debut EP ''Headcase'' in 2015. Day Wave has been featured in ''[[Los Angeles Times| LA Mag]]'' and ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]].'' They opened for [[Blonde Redhead]] during their fall 2016 tour.<ref>{{cite web |last=Hampp|first=Andrew |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/6634359/day-wave-headcase-ep-interview |title=Day Wave Talks DIY 'Headcase' EP, Ditching Synth-Pop & More|publisher=Billboard |date=July 16, 2015 |accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=White|first=Caitlin |url=http://www.stereogum.com/1807620/day-wave-we-try-but-we-dont-fit-in/mp3s/ |title=Day Wave – "We Try But We Don’t Fit In" |publisher=StereoGum |date=June 10, 2015 |accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Lester|first=Paul |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/may/25/the-playlist-new-bands-day-wave-boycrush-rangleklods-and-more |title=The playlist: new bands – Day Wave, Boycrush, Rangleklods and more |publisher=The Guardian |date=May 25, 2015 |accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Bennett|first=Kim Taylor |url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/premiere-day-wave-we-try-but-we-dont-fit-in |title= DAY WAVE'S "WE TRY BUT WE DON'T FIT IN" IS AN OUTSIDER ANTHEM FOR DREAMERS|publisher=Vice |date= |accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Lawrence|first=Eric J. |url=http://blogs.kcrw.com/musicnews/2015/04/day-wave-artist-you-should-know-song-premiere/ |title=Day Wave: Artist You Should Know (Song Premiere) |publisher=KCRW Music Blog |date=April 8, 2015 |accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.laweekly.com/event/blonde-redhead-day-wave-5733621 |title=Blonde Redhead, Day Wave |publisher=LA Weekly |accessdate=July 28, 2015}}</ref>

In November 2016, Day Wave signed onto [[Harvest Records]] and released the single "Wasting Time".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thebaybridged.com/2016/11/09/day-wave-joins-harvest-records-releases-new-song/|title=Day Wave joins Harvest Records, releases new song|last=9|first=Rob Goodman{{!}}November|last2=Records|first2=2016{{!}}Comments Off on Day Wave joins Harvest|date=2016-11-09|website=The Bay Bridged - San Francisco Bay Area Indie Music|access-date=2017-03-18|last3=Song|first3=Releases New}}</ref> The song "Hard to Read" appears in 2016 video game ''[[Watch Dogs 2]]''. In February 2017, Day Wave announced its debut album, ''The Days We Had'', which was released on May 5, 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2017/02/day-wave-jackson-phillips-releases-new-single-some.html|title=Day Wave Announces Debut Album, Releases New Single "Something Here"|work=pastemagazine.com|access-date=2017-03-18|language=en}}</ref> Phillips relocated to the Echo Park neighborhood of Los Angeles in early 2017.<ref name=PTA2017>{{Cite web|url=http://www.passtheaux.co/interview-day-wave-el-rey-theatre/|title=Interview: Day Wave maintains lo-fi charm on debut album|work=Pass The Aux|access-date=2017-05-25|language=en}}</ref> Like on his earlier EPs, Phillips recorded straight to tape on his full-length debut.<ref name=PTA2017 /> Phillips's recordings with Day Wave included some of the earliest recorded work of Australian-American musician [[Hazel English]], and they co-released a cover of [[Interpol (band)|Interpol]]'s "[[PDA (Interpol song)|PDA]]."<ref name= "English">{{cite news |last=Offenberger |first=Bre |url= https://www.thepostathens.com/article/2020/04/hazel-english-wake-up-review-day-wave |title=Album Review: Hazel English molds dream pop perfection on ‘Wake UP!’ |work=[[The Post (Ohio student newspaper)|The Post]] |date=April 26, 2020 |access-date=January 7, 2020}}</ref><ref name= "Interpol">{{cite news |last=Murray |first=Robin |url= https://www.clashmusic.com/news/day-wave-hazel-english-tackle-an-interpol-classic |title=Day Wave, Hazel English Tackle An Interpol Classic |work=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]] |date=November 17, 2017 |access-date=January 7, 2020}}</ref> On April 24, 2020, Day Wave released its third EP, ''Crush''.

The touring members of Day Wave include brothers Henry Moser (bass) and Jack Moser (keyboard/synth) as well as Nick de Ryss (drums), and Alex Lasner (guitar). After Phillips co-produced [[Pete Yorn|Pete Yorn's]] album ''Caretakers'', Phillips, de Ryss, Henry Moser joined Yorn's 2019 tour as his backing band.<ref> Chareunsy, Don, [https://www.spokesman.com/stories/2019/oct/17/days-of-yorn-singer-songwriter-pete-yorn-gets-inti/ "Days of Yorn: Singer-songwriter Pete Yorn gets intimate on solo acoustic tour"]</ref>
