'''Jackson Wang''' ({{zh|t=王嘉爾}}; {{lang-ko|왕잭슨}}; born 28 March 1994) is a [[Hong Kong]] rapper, singer, fashion designer and dancer based in China. He is the founder of record label [[Team Wang]], and is the creative director and lead designer for fashion brand Team Wang Design.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Jackson Wang Talks Solo Career, Working in GOT7 & Musical Goals|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/k-town/8506965/jackson-wang-interview-solo-career-got7/|access-date=2021-01-27|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Okwodu|first=Janelle|title=Team Wang is the Pop-Fueled Fashion Line to Know Now|url=https://www.vogue.com/slideshow/jackson-wang-team-wang-collection-interview|access-date=2021-01-27|website=Vogue|date=6 July 2020|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Anyanwu|first=Obi|date=2020-07-07|title=Jackson Wang Launches 'Team Wang' Apparel Line|url=https://wwd.com/fashion-news/fashion-scoops/jackson-wang-launches-team-wang-apparel-line-1203667494/|access-date=2021-01-27|website=WWD|language=en-US}}</ref> He is active in [[mainland China]] as a solo artist and television host. He is a member of the South Korean [[boy group]] [[Got7]], and also a member of the Chinese hip hop group Panthepack.

Born and raised in Hong Kong, Wang was a [[Sabre (fencing)|sabre]] fencer for Hong Kong's fencing team. He was ranked eleventh in the [[2010 Summer Youth Olympics]],<ref name="FC">[http://www.asianfencing.com/2011ajcfcresults.asp Asian Junior and Cadet Fencing Championship] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140907234915/http://www.asianfencing.com/2011ajcfcresults.asp |date=7 September 2014 }} Retrieved 7 September 2014.</ref> and won first place at the Asian Junior and Cadet Fencing Championships in 2011. After passing a music audition, he moved to [[Seoul]], South Korea in July 2011 to pursue a career in [[K-pop]]. In January 2014, after over two years of training, Wang debuted as a member of Got7 with the single "Girls Girls Girls".

== Life and career ==
=== 1994–2016: Early life and debut with Got7 ===
{{Main|Got7}}
Wang was born in [[Kowloon Tong]], [[British Hong Kong]], on 28 March 1994, and grew up in [[Sha Tin District]], [[New Territories]]. His father, [[Wang Ruiji]], is a former member of China's national fencing team and an [[Asian Games]] gold medalist. His mother, Sophia Chow, is a former acrobatics gymnast from [[Shanghai]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://top.todayonhistory.com/a/201601/28142.html|title=王嘉尔个人资料 王嘉尔妈妈周平|date=7 January 2016|website=Todayonhistory.com|access-date=29 June 2018}}</ref> His maternal grandfather, [[Zhou Yongchang]], was a pioneer of ultrasound diagnostic medicine of mainland China.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://hz.edushi.com/bang/info/4-21-n4138494.html|title=王嘉尔外公周永昌去世 医学界泰斗周永昌竟是王嘉尔外公真的假的？|date=25 October 2017|website=Edushi|language=zh|access-date=26 October 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171026170308/http://hz.edushi.com/bang/info/4-21-n4138494.html|archive-date=26 October 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xportsnews.com/jenter/?ac=article_view&entry_id=907331|title=갓세븐 측 "잭슨, 외조부상…스케줄 불참 양해 부탁" (공식입장)|date=25 October 2017|website=Xports News|language=ko|access-date=26 October 2017}}</ref> Under the guidance of his father and other professional coaches, Wang started his fencing training at the age of ten. He went on to win multiple awards as part of the Hong Kong national fencing team,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://the-sun.on.cc/cnt/sport/20100505/00514_009.html|title=型男王嘉爾劍指Top4 - 太陽報|date=5 May 2010|website=the-sun.on.cc|language=zh-hk|access-date=29 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://the-sun.on.cc/cnt/sport/20110706/|title=王嘉爾佩劍俊男咩都要第一 - 太陽報|date=6 July 2011|work=太陽報|access-date=29 June 2018|language=zh-hk}}</ref> including first place at the Asian Junior and Cadet Fencing Championship in 2011.<ref name="FC" /> He attended the [[American International School Hong Kong]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://got7.jype.com/profile.asp|title=Got7 Profile|publisher=JYPE|access-date=11 September 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150511141939/http://got7.jype.com/profile.asp|archive-date=11 May 2015}}</ref>

In 2010, while playing basketball at his school,<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|date=2020-08-07|title=포즈부터가 남달라, 패션화보도 접수한 JYP 신예 갓세븐|url=https://www.chosun.com/site/data/html_dir/2014/01/22/2014012200762.html|access-date=2021-07-04|website=www.chosun.com|language=ko}}</ref> Wang was noted by a representative of South Korean talent agency [[JYP Entertainment]] and invited to participate in the global auditions in [[Kowloon]], which he passed in December 2010, five or six months later, ranking first place among 2,000 applicants.<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":0">{{Citation|last=BigBoyTV|title=Jackson Wang On His Solo Music, Upcoming Album, Attention in The U.S. + More!|date=15 February 2019|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Sfw0IQGCbrI|access-date=27 February 2019}}</ref> He was offered a scholarship to [[Stanford University]] for fencing, but turned it down after passing his audition.<ref>{{cite AV media|people=Jackson Wang|date=2 June 2017|title=Got7 1st concert "Fly In Seoul" final DVD – Interview with Got7|medium=DVD|language=ko|time=31:38|publisher=JYP Entertainment|quote=When I was in Hong Kong, I was invited by Stanford. I was asked to be on their fencing team. I could have gone to a prominent university and win a global medal. It was a huge challenge to give all that up and come to Korea to be a singer.}}</ref> In July 2011, Wang moved to Seoul, South Korea for his [[K-pop]] training. He made an appearance on the reality survival program ''Win: Who Is Next'' two years later, which aired on [[Mnet (TV channel)|Mnet]] on 6 September 2013. The program was a competition between [[YG Entertainment]] trainees (who later debuted as members of [[Winner (band)|Winner]] and [[iKon (South Korean band)|iKon]]), and JYP trainees. Wang appeared alongside fellow trainees Mark, Yugyeom, and [[BamBam (singer)|BamBam]], who were then selected as members of [[Got7]].

[[File:Jackson Wang at Got7's second album 'GOT♡' showcase, 18 June 2014 03.png|thumb|250x250px|Wang at Got7's showcase in June 2014|left]]
After two and a half years of training,<ref name=":0" /> Wang was selected as one of the final members of JYP Entertainment's new boy group Got7 and debuted with the single "Girls Girls Girls", released on 16 January 2014, from the group's first [[Extended play|EP]] ''[[Got It?]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/79270/video-got7s-girls-girls-girls-mv-breaks-through-10-million-views-on-youtube|title=KPOP NEWS - [Video] GOT7's 'Girls Girls Girls' MV Breaks through 10 Million Views on YouTube {{!}} Mwave|website=mwave.interest.me|access-date=24 May 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160624031144/http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/79270/video-got7s-girls-girls-girls-mv-breaks-through-10-million-views-on-youtube|archive-date=24 June 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2014, Wang joined his first variety show, [[Seoul Broadcasting System|SBS]]' ''[[Roommate (TV series)|Roommate]]'', as a member in its second season. Wang's individual popularity rose after he appeared on the series, and he was later awarded the Newcomer Award at the 2014 [[SBS Entertainment Awards]].<ref name="newcomer">{{Cite web|title=[bnt photo] Lee Kyung Gyoo Won The Biggest Award at SBS|last=Khelil|first=Nawael|work=BNT News|date=31 December 2014|access-date=23 April 2016|url=http://www.bntnews.co.uk/app/news.php?nid=16704}}</ref> He subsequently appeared on several other Korean variety shows such as ''[[Star King (TV series)|Star King]]'', ''[[Law of the Jungle (TV series)|Law of the Jungle]]'', ''[[Happy Together (2001 TV series)|Happy Together]]'', ''[[Radio Star (TV series)|Radio Star]]'', ''[[Problematic Men]]'', ''[[Our Neighborhood Arts and Physical Education]]'', ''[[Saturday Night Live Korea]]'', ''A Look At Myself'', and others. On 12 May 2015, Wang was appointed as a new [[Master of ceremonies|MC]] for SBS' music show ''[[Inkigayo]]'', following the departure of [[ZE:A]]'s [[Hwang Kwanghee|Kwanghee]].

In December 2015, Wang made his Chinese television debut as one of the presenters (alongside [[He Jiong]]) on the Chinese version of the show ''[[Please Take Care of My Refrigerator]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=213&aid=0000807058|title="도니형, 잘할게요" 잭슨, 中 '냉장고' 메인 MC 도전기|website=Naver|access-date=17 April 2016}}</ref> called ''[[Go Fridge]]'', which was well received. He also wrote the lyrics, composed and arranged the theme song for the show in seasons 2 and 3.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ent.qq.com/a/20160108/050663.htm|title=王嘉尔在键盘上被敲击了35万次 他到底弄啥嘞|publisher=Entertainment QQ}}</ref> In March 2016, Wang was appointed as MC for ''Fresh Sunday'', a show on [[Hunan TV]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://isplus.live.joins.com/news/article/article.asp?total_id=19809787&cloc=|title=[피플is] 中 예능 샛별..GOT7 잭슨 "연이어 고정MC"|website=Ilgan Sports|date=30 March 2016|access-date=17 April 2016}}</ref> Later in 2016, he starred in the show ''Fighting Man'' alongside [[Jam Hsiao]], [[Wang Kai (actor)|Wang Kai]], [[Jing Boran]], [[Bai Jingting]], and Yang Shuo.{{citation needed|date=July 2018}}

On 29 April 2016, Got7 held their first concert in Seoul, where Wang performed his self-composed songs "I Love It" and "WOLO (We Only Live Once)" with his group members Yugyeom and BamBam.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.asiatoday.co.kr/view.php?key=20160501001552068|title=[공연 어땠어?] 갓세븐, 섹시·달콤·파워풀한 무대로 다시 한번 성장|date=2 May 2016}}</ref> In November 2017, prior to the release of Got7 second Japanese extended play "Turn Up", Wang dropped out of all group activities in Japan due to health concerns and conflicting schedules.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.got7japan.com/turnup/|title=GOT7「TURN UP」SPECIAL SITE|website=www.got7japan.com|language=ja|access-date=16 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://entertain.naver.com/now/read?oid=112&aid=0002954849|title=갓세븐 측 "잭슨, 日 활동 중단..6인 체제 활동"(공식)|access-date=16 November 2017|date=5 September 2017|language=ko}}</ref> In December 2016 Wang's first solo commercial for [[Midea Group|Midea]] was released in China.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://3g.china.com/act/economy/11179727/20160916/23562562.html|title=我有"嘉"宾，"美"不胜收——美的携手王嘉尔开启智能新时代|website=3g.china.com|access-date=4 March 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170305035020/http://3g.china.com/act/economy/11179727/20160916/23562562.html|archive-date=5 March 2017}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Because of his work in the entertainment industry in China, Korea and the rest of the world, Jackson is proficient in a number of languages; he can fluently speak and understand Mandarin, Cantonese, Korean and English.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chan|first=Tim|date=June 29, 2018|title=Glass exclusive – interview with K-Pop star Jackson Wang of GOT7 – The Glass Magazine|url=https://www.theglassmagazine.com/interview-with-jackson-wang-of-got7/|url-status=live|access-date=September 3, 2021|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-19|title=K-Pop Sensation Jackson Wang is Taking Over the World - Got7 China Rapper Interview|url=https://www.lofficielusa.com/[category]/[slug]|access-date=2021-09-03|website=www.lofficielusa.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

In August 2019, Wang uploaded a photo of the Chinese flag and declared himself as "one of 1.4 billion guardians of the Chinese flag" on his official [[Sina Weibo|Weibo]] account, after a Chinese flag was removed and tossed into the Victoria Harbour during the [[2019-20 Hong Kong protests]].<ref>[https://apnews.com/c4eba17f96b94b6882302cf066200c73 Chinese pop stars publicly back Beijing on Hong Kong]. ''Associated Press.'' 19 August 2019.</ref> In a January 2020 interview with ''[[Forbes]]'', Wang introduced himself as "Jackson Wang from China" and stated: "My goal is just to put my name out on the table just to let everybody to get to know me. I want to ... let people know that Chinese kids are working on good music too."<ref name=snakeortherabbit/>

In March 2021, Wang terminated his working relationship with [[Adidas]], after their previously made public statement regarding alleged forced labour in the cotton-producing region of [[Xinjiang]] resurfaced.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Zhang|first=Tianwei|date=2021-03-25|title=Xinjiang Cotton: Li Ning, Anta Shares Surge While Nike, Adidas, Burberry Hit With Backlash|url=https://wwd.com/business-news/retail/xinjiang-cotton-ban-china-nike-uniqlo-hm-1234787090/|access-date=2021-04-26|website=WWD|language=en}}</ref>
