'''Norma Monserrat Bustamante Laferte''' (born 2 May 1983), known professionally as '''Mon Laferte''', is a Chilean singer, songwriter and musician. She is known for her musical versatility, having composed songs in a wide variety of genres such as pop, rock, [[bolero]], [[cumbia]] and [[Salsa music|salsa]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Agarzábal |first1=Nicolás |title=Mon Laferte, la chilena que quiere desbancar a Shakira |url=https://www.clarin.com/viva/mon-laferte-chilena-quiere-desbancar-shakira_0_SJzj7o_SQ.html |website=Clarín.com |access-date=16 May 2021}}</ref> She is also currently the Chilean artist with the most listeners on [[Spotify]] worldwide,<ref>{{cite web|date=25 September 2019|title=Latin Pop Primer: The 15 Female Artists You Need to Know Now|url=https://www.eonline.com/ap/news/1076572/latin-pop-primer-the-15-female-artists-you-need-to-know-now|access-date=2020-10-13|website=E! Online}}</ref> as well as being the Chilean artist with the most nominations in a single edition of the [[Latin Grammy Awards]] (5 in 2017).<ref>{{cite news |last1=Marti |first1=Diana |title=2017 Latin Grammy Awards: Complete List of Nominations |url=https://www.eonline.com/news/881397/2017-latin-grammy-awards-complete-list-of-nominations |access-date=16 May 2019 |work=E! Online |publisher=E! Entertainment Television |date=9 September 2017 |quote=Leading the list this year is Residente with nine nominations, Maluma with seven, Shakira with six, and Kevin Jiménez ADG, Juanes, and Mon Laferte with five each.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Mon Laferte |url=https://historia-biografia.com/mon-laferte/ |website=Historia y Biografía |publisher=Historia-biografia.com |access-date=16 May 2019 |language=es |date=25 July 2018 |quote=En el año 2017 ... Su canción “Amárrame” ganó en ese año el premio Grammy a mejor canción alternativa y fue nominada en las categorías de: canción del año, mejor álbum de música alternativa, álbum del año y registro del año.}}</ref>

To date she has sold in [[Latin America]] 1,500,000 digital copies between albums and singles, making her the best-selling Chilean artist in the digital era. Some of her successes include "Tormento," "Amor completo," "Si tú me quisieras," "[[Tu falta de querer]]," "[[Amárrame]]", and "Mi buen amor." She received a Latin Grammy in 2017 for Best Alternative Song for "[[Amárrame]]."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-11-16|title=Mon Laferte Wins Best Alternative Song|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/mon-laferte-wins-best-alternative-song-18th-latin-grammys|access-date=2020-10-13|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-11-16|title=See The Full List Of 2017 Latin GRAMMY Winners|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/2017-latin-grammy-awards-full-winners-list|access-date=2020-10-13|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Mon Laferte was born Norma Monserrat Bustamante Laferte in [[Viña del Mar]], Chile. She cites her grandmother who sang [[bolero]]s as one of her first influences, and also recalls following her at young age to [[tango]] clubs in [[Valparaíso]].<ref>Interview in Dulce Patria. May 13, 2016. [[Radio Cooperativa]].</ref> She started her career at the age of 9, after winning a guitar in a singing contest.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vogue.mx/vip/perfiles/articulos/mon-laferte/7412|title=El ritmo latino de Mon Laferte|date=9 June 2017}}</ref> She received a scholarship to attend a musical conservatory in Viña del Mar at the age of 13, and soon after began playing in bars in [[Valparaíso]] and [[Santiago]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.monlaferte.com/#!bio/ct0n|title=Mon Laferte|website=Mon Laferte|access-date=14 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190601190747/https://www.monlaferte.com/#!bio/ct0n|archive-date=1 June 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> 

In October 2013, Laferte became a vegetarian for ethical reasons. As of 2017, she is a [[vegan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/tiramillas/encuentros/mon-laferte/2017/09/12/index.html|access-date=18 November 2017|language=es|title=Los lectores entrevistan a Mon Laferte|publication-date=12 September 2017|website=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20171118090045/http://www.marca.com/tiramillas/encuentros/mon-laferte/2017/09/12/index.html|archive-date=18 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Mon Laferte y su lado vegetariano: "No utilizo nada de animales, me parecía súper falso comérmelos"|url=http://www.elciudadano.cl/chile/mon-laferte-y-su-lado-vegetariano-no-utilizo-nada-de-animales-me-parecia-super-falso-comermelos/03/28/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://archive.today/20170512165855/http://www.elciudadano.cl/chile/mon-laferte-y-su-lado-vegetariano-no-utilizo-nada-de-animales-me-parecia-super-falso-comermelos/03/28/|archive-date=12 May 2017|access-date=18 November 2017|website=[[El Ciudadano]]|language=es|publication-date=28 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Laferte|first=Mon|title=Laferte's official Twitter|url=https://twitter.com/monlaferte|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20171118093556/https://twitter.com/monlaferte|archive-date=18 November 2017|access-date=18 November 2017|website=Twitter}}{{Primary source inline|date=December 2018}}{{Primary source inline|date=April 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2003, Laferte, then known as Monserrat Bustamante, entered in the Chilean reality competition series ''[[Rojo (TV series)|Rojo]]''. The same year she released her first [[Album|studio album]], ''[[La Chica de Rojo]]''. The album had great success in Chile, receiving Gold and Platinum certifications. She became part of the ''Clan Rojo'' and was on the television series for four seasons.

In 2007, Laferte decided to start a new chapter in her musical career by moving from Chile to [[Mexico City]], where she began singing in nightclubs and recording cover songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.indierocks.mx/musica/entrevistas/entrevista-con-mon-laferte/|title=Entrevista con Mon Laferte|website=Indie Rocks!|language=es-ES|access-date=26 January 2019}}</ref> In 2009, she released a single titled "Lo mismo que yo," which was to be the lead single from an upcoming album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.emol.com/noticias/magazine/2009/05/18/358686/con-look-lily-allen-ex-estrella-de-rojo-adelanta-nuevo-disco.html|title=Con look Lily Allen, ex estrella de "Rojo" adelanta nuevo disco {{!}} Emol.com|last=S.A.P|first=El Mercurio|date=18 May 2009|website=Emol|language=es|access-date=26 January 2019}}</ref> The same year, Laferte was diagnosed with [[thyroid cancer]], effectively putting a halt to her album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lasegunda.com/Noticias/CulturaEspectaculos/2013/01/812286/el-radical-giro-de-mon-laferte-ya-no-me-reconocen|title=El radical giro de Mon Laferte: "Ya no me reconocen"|first=El Mercurio|last=S.A.P.|website=lasegunda.com|publisher=[[La Segunda]]|access-date=1 July 2016}}</ref>

Around the time of her battle with cancer, she abandoned her original stage name and introduced the world to Mon Laferte, expressing that the name represented a new beginning for her:<blockquote>"Mi cambio de nombre no es por querer ser otra persona, es que las circunstancias de la vida me han llevado a cambiar mucho y sentí que tenía que empezar de cero."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.latundra.com/monlaferte/|title=La Tundra Revista {{!}} Mon Laferte en concierto en Londres|language=es-ES|access-date=2019-01-26}}</ref></blockquote>Laferte decided to scrap the album she was recording in 2009, and returned two years later with her second studio album, titled ''[[Desechable]]''. The next year, in 2012, she was invited to judge the second season of the Chilean version of [[The X Factor]], called [[Factor X (Chile)|Factor X]], along with [[Karen Doggenweiler]], [[Tito Beltrán]] and [[José Luis Rodríguez (singer)|José Luis Rodríguez]].

It is also at this time her presentation at the beginning of 2012 as a [[Singing|vocalist]] of the Mexican heavy metal female band Mystica Girls, with whom in February 2014 she recorded the album titled ''Gates of Hell''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/mx/luisdelvalle/metal-laferte|title=Tenemos que hablar del no tan oscuro pasado metalero de Mon Laferte|last=Valle|first=Luis Del|website=BuzzFeed|language=es|access-date=2020-02-08}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/mon-laferte-decade-recap-921666/|title=So, How Was Your Decade, Mon Laferte?|last=Exposito|first=Suzy|date=2019-12-10|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-08}}</ref>

In 2013, she released her third album, ''[[Tornasol]]''. She received media attention in 2015 with her single "Tu falta de querer" from the album ''[[Mon Laferte Vol.1|Mon Laferte, Vol.1]]''. In 2016, she won a [[MTV Millennial Awards|MTV Millennial Award]] for the "Latin Video of the Year"<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtvla.com/cr/lo-ultimo-en-mtv/noticias/ganadores-de-los-premios-mtvmiaw|title=Todos los ganadores de los premios #MTVMIAW 2016 – MTV America Latina|access-date=9 April 2021}}</ref> and receiving two nominations on the [[Latin Grammy Awards of 2016]] for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] and [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album|Best Alternative Music Album]].

Laferte released her fifth album ''[[La Trenza]],'' her most acclaimed album to date, in 2017. Her single with Colombian rock star [[Juanes]], "Amárrame," won the Best Alternative Song award at the [[18th Annual Latin Grammy Awards|18th Latin GRAMMYs]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/mon-laferte-wins-best-alternative-song-18th-latin-grammys|title=Mon Laferte Wins Best Alternative Song|date=16 November 2017}}</ref> in which she was also nominated for Best Alternative Music Album, Song of the Year, Album of the Year, and Record of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latingrammy.com/en/nominees|title=18a Entrega Anual del Latin GRAMMY|website=Latin GRAMMYs}}</ref> She also wins the category "Best North Latin Artist" in the [[2017 MTV Europe Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-11-12|title=Here's the full list of MTV EMA 2017 winners {{!}} NME|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/heres-full-list-mtv-ema-2017-winners-2158725|access-date=2020-10-13|website=NME Music News, Reviews, Videos, Galleries, Tickets and Blogs {{!}} NME.COM|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Newman|first=Vicki|date=2017-11-14|title=A full list of winners from the MTV EMA Awards 2017|url=http://www.mirror.co.uk/3am/celebrity-news/who-won-mtv-ema-awards-11511005|access-date=2020-10-13|website=mirror}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=THESE ARE THE BIG WINNERS AT THE 2019 MTV EMA {{!}} Noticias {{!}} MTV EMA|url=https://www.mtvema.com/es/es/noticias/31m1fd/these-are-the-big-winners-at-the-2019-mtv-ema|access-date=2020-10-13|website=www.mtvema.com|language=es}}</ref>

In February 2018 she released the single "Antes de Ti" that was nominated to the [[19th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]] as "Song of the Year". The video for the single was also Mon Laferte's directorial debut.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/altlatino/2018/02/27/588087392/mon-laferte-after-a-stellar-year-a-directorial-debut|title=Mon Laferte: After A Stellar Year, A Directorial Debut|website=NPR.org|language=en|access-date=2019-06-28}}</ref> In June 2018, she co-hosted the 2018 [[MTV Millennial Awards]] in Mexico City at the [[Mexico City Arena]] along with the Venezuelan [[YouTuber|YouTube personality]] [[La Divaza]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Mon Laferte co-host 2018 MTV Millenial Awards |url=https://www.24horas.cl/tendencias/espectaculosycultura/mon-laferte-sera-la-animadora-de-los-premios-mtv-miaw-2018-2730574 |website=24Horas.cl |publisher=24 Horas |access-date=6 June 2018}}</ref>

During the year 2018, Mon worked on her sixth studio album, '' [[Norma (album)|Norma]] '', which was recorded in a single session in studio A of [[Capitol Studios]] of Los Angeles, California, United States, the recording was made in one shot, without using the [[overdubbing]]  technique of audio layers, but all the instruments playing simultaneously to give the material the feeling of live recording. In this recording 13 musicians participated. The production of this album was in charge of [[Omar Rodríguez-López]], the recording engineer was [[Bruce Botnick]]. The album was released on 9 November 2018.

On 26 October, a collaboration was published for the new Christmas album of the American singer [[Gwen Stefani]], in a version of the song [[Feliz Navidad (song)|"Feliz Navidad"]] by the Puerto Rican singer-songwriter [[José Feliciano]]. At the end of 2018, the international television network [[HTV (Latin America)|HTV]] nominated Mon Laferte in the "Best Southern Artist" category of the [[Heat Latin Music Awards]] 2019.

In January 2019, she was announced to participate in the American Music Festival [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]]. In February 2019, Mon Laferte received her first nomination at the [[Billboard Latin Music Awards]] in the Top Latin Albums Artist of the Year category, Female. Also the Album [[Norma (album)|''Norma'']] received a Gold record in Mexico for 30,000 copies sold.

In May 2019, the singer announced her U.S. tour, under the headline "La Gira de Norma". The first tour date is set for 10 August 2019,  in Seattle's [[Neptune Theatre (Seattle)|Neptune Theatre]], following a European tour that same Summer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-latin/mon-laferte-announces-u-s-headlining-tour-839737/|title=Mon Laferte Announces U.S. Headlining Tour|last=Exposito|first=Suzy|date=2019-05-28|website=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-28}}</ref> On 14 November 2019, Mon Laferte made headlines with her act of political protest, exposing her breasts at the Latin Grammys to display the message "En Chile Torturan Violan y Matan" (English translation: In Chile they torture, rape and kill) on her bare chest.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-latin-america-50430477|title=Chile protests: Government bows to demands for referendum|date=15 November 2019|access-date=9 April 2021|website=Bbc.com}}</ref>

On 19 November 2019, Laferte was interviewed by Patricia Janiot from Univision. In that interview, Mon Laferte justified the looting and burning of numerous supermarkets and other buildings by saying that "they were only material goods". When asked if she condemned violence, she said, "I do not approve of any kind of violence. Now, if you ask me personally, if I have to go burn down a supermarket that has robbed from me my entire life to demand the basic rights I feel I deserve, I will do it!" She accused the police and armed forces of burning the dozens of subway stations that were burnt by rioters on 18 Oct 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aARKYwHlia0|title=Mon Laferte habla con Patria Janiot de la crisis política que se vive en Chile|access-date=9 April 2021|via=www.youtube.com}}</ref>
[[File:Mon Laferte en el Museo de la Ciudad de México.jpg|thumb|Mon Laferte at her solo exhibition in Mexico City.]]
On 30 November 2019, Laferte was subpoenaed by Chilean prosecutors to give a statement regarding the evidence she may or may not have to support her accusation that Chilean police and armed forces actively participated in the burning of subway stations. Chilean police announced that, depending upon her statement, they might press criminal charges against her.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latercera.com/nacional/noticia/carabineros-pide-que-mon-laferte-declare/921356/?outputType=amp|title=Carabineros anuncia "acciones civiles y penales" contra Mon Laferte y pide a Fiscalía que cite a cantante tras dichos sobre quema de estaciones de Metro - La Tercera|website=Latercera.com|access-date=9 April 2021}}</ref> The charges were later dropped.<ref>{{cite web |last1=McGowan |first1=Charis |title=Mon Laferte: The Chilean pop sensation challenging repression |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-latin-america-56899171 |website=BBC |access-date=31 October 2021}}</ref>

In 2021, Laferte contributed a cover of the Metallica song "[[Nothing Else Matters]]" to the charity tribute album ''[[The Metallica Blacklist]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=He|first=Richard S.|date=2021-09-10|title=Every Metallica Blacklist cover ranked from worst to best|url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/every-metallica-blacklist-cover-ranked-from-worst-to-best|access-date=2021-10-24|website=loudersound|language=en}}</ref>

=== Visual arts ===
On 11 March 2020, she debuted as a visual artist in the solo exhibit entitled ''Gestures'' at the [[Museum of the City of Mexico]], containing 76 art pieces. Laferte commented she has painted for ten years as a self-taught artist with some lessons from her father, a painter himself.<ref>{{Cite web|last=MacMasters|first=Merry|date=11 March 2020|title=Mon Laferte, la pintora, expone en el Museo de la Ciudad de México|url=https://www.jornada.com.mx/ultimas/cultura/2020/03/11/soy-201cchilanga-por-derecho-propia201d-mon-laferte-3547.html|url-status=live|access-date=26 May 2021|website=La Jornada|language=Spanish}}</ref>
