'''The Regrettes''' is an American [[punk rock]] band from [[Los Angeles]]. The band is led by frontwoman [[Lydia Night]].<ref name="LAW">{{cite news | last=Ohanesian| first=Liz| title =Teen Rockers The Regrettes Embrace Imperfection on Their Whirlwind Debut Album| newspaper =[[LA Weekly]]| date =January 9, 2017 | url =http://www.laweekly.com/music/teen-rockers-the-regrettes-embrace-imperfection-on-their-whirlwind-debut-album-7784408| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Vogue">{{cite news | last=Rosenzweig| first=Mathias| title =The Regrettes Is the Teen Band You Need—No Matter Your Age| publisher =[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]| date =October 10, 2016 | url =http://www.vogue.com/article/the-regrettes-teen-band-you-need-to-know| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref> They released their debut studio album, ''[[Feel Your Feelings Fool!]]'', on [[Warner Bros. Records]] in January 2017.<ref name="Trib">{{cite news | last=Kot| first=Greg| title =Regrettes channel rage, redemption in grrrl power songs| newspaper =[[Chicago Tribune]]| date =March 30, 2017 | url =http://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-regrettes-ott-0331-20170324-column.html| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref><ref name="SLO">{{cite news | last=Dobstaff| first=Chris| title =For the Regrettes, fighting insecurities is the pinnacle of punk| newspaper =[[The Tribune (San Luis Obispo)|The Tribune]]| date =February 14, 2017 | url =http://www.sanluisobispo.com/entertainment/article132703334.html| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Observer">{{cite news | last=Abrams| first=Margaret| title =The Regrettes' Lead Singer Wants Girls to Feel Their Feelings| newspaper =[[New York Observer]]| date =January 18, 2017 | url =http://observer.com/2017/01/the-regrettes-lead-singer-wants-girls-to-feel-their-feelings/| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref><ref name="AP">{{cite news | last=Ralph| first=Caitlyn| title =The Regrettes plot headlining tour and other news you might have missed today| publisher =[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]| date =May 3, 2017 | url =http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/the_regrettes_plot_headlining_tour_and_other_news_you_might_have_missed_tod| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref>

==History==
Prior to the formation of the band, lead vocalist Lydia Night met guitarist Genessa Gariano, bassist Sage Chavis, and drummer Maxx Morando in music school.<ref name="Vogue"/><ref name="Trib"/> At the time, Gariano, Chavis and Morando were in a band together,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Beau |first1=Auxin |title=Interview - The Regrettes (26/09/2017) |url=http://wewillpunkyou.com/theregrettesitwen|website=Wewillpunkyou.com |access-date=June 14, 2018}}</ref> called "Genessa"<ref>{{cite web |title=Music |url=https://degeneratenews.com/music/|website=Degeneratenews.com |access-date=June 14, 2018}}</ref> and Night was in the two-piece band [[Pretty Little Demons]] (who changed their name to ''The Regrettes'' in the last few months of life) with drummer Marlhy Murphy. Both of the bands played a concert together, but not soon after<ref>{{cite web |last1=Lanhan |first1=Tom |title=THE PV Q&A: Lydia Night of The Regrettes on Songwriting: "I've been writing songs since I was six, and that's how I learned to cope with certain things" |url=http://www.purevolume.com/news/THE-PV-QA-Lydia-Night-The-Regrettes|website=Purevolume.com |access-date=June 14, 2018}}</ref> Murphy departed from the band after the release of their EP "''Hey!''" on October 16, 2015,<ref name="Hey Now" /> leading to Night asking Gariano, Chavis, and Morando to join. The "''Hey!''" EP was eventually discovered by a representative at Warner Brothers, which would eventually led to them signing the band.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Fichtner|first1=Abbie|title=Best New Artist 2017: An Interview With Lydia Night of The Regrettes|url=https://music.mxdwn.com/2017/12/20/features/best-new-artist-2017-an-interview-with-lydia-night-of-the-regrettes/|access-date=May 12, 2018}}</ref> In 2016, the band toured with both [[Tacocat]] and [[Sleigh Bells (band)|Sleigh Bells]], opened for [[Kate Nash]], and performed at [[South by Southwest]].<ref name="Vogue"/><ref name="SFW">{{cite news | last=Carroll| first=Elle| title =Out to Lunch With the Regrettes| newspaper =[[SF Weekly]]| date =February 23, 2017 | url =http://www.sfweekly.com/music/music-music/out-to-lunch-with-the-regrettes/| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Noisey"/>

[[File:The Regrettes 05 28 2016 -16 (27090351580).jpg|thumb|The Regrettes performing in 2016. From left to right: Gariano, Night, Chavis]]
In 2015, the band's independent release led to a record deal with Warner Bros. Records.<ref name="LAT2016"/> Their first album was produced by [[Mike Elizondo]] and their first single, "A Living Human Girl", was released in June 2016.<ref name="Rookie">{{cite news | last=Lovitt| first=Bryn| title =The Regrettes: A Living Human Girl| publisher =[[Rookie (magazine)|Rookie]]| date =June 20, 2016 | url =http://www.rookiemag.com/2016/06/the-regrettes-living-human-girl/| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref>
Other singles followed including "Hey Now", "Hot", and "Seashore."<ref name="Stereo">{{cite news | last=Robinson| first=Collin| title =The Regrettes – "Hey Now"| publisher =[[Stereogum]]| date =August 2, 2016 | url =http://www.stereogum.com/1891699/the-regrettes-hey-now/mp3s/| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Clash">{{cite news | last=Murray| first=Robin| title =Premiere: The Regrettes - 'Hot' (Live)| publisher =[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]| date =October 31, 2016 | url =http://www.clashmusic.com/videos/premiere-the-regrettes-hot-live| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref> On January 13, 2017, the band released its debut studio album, ''Feel Your Feelings Fool!''.<ref name="LAW"/> In May 2017, the band announced a summer headlining tour which includes stops at [[Summerfest]] and [[Riot Fest]].<ref name="AP"/> On February 9, 2018, the band released the single "Come Through," from their EP ''Attention Seeker'', released on February 23, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/attention-seeker-ep/1341748314|title=Attention Seeker - EP by The Regrettes|website=Music.apple.com}}</ref> It was accompanied by a music video. On May 9, 2018, the band announced Morando's departure.<ref name='The Regrettes Release Powerful New Track, "Poor Boy"' /> On May 22, 2018, the tour bus containing all their belongings was stolen from outside the Sunflower Lounge, Birmingham, UK where they later played a sold-out gig.
<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.indiemidlands.com/2018/05/23/live-review-the-regrettes-the-sunflower-lounge/|title=Live Review – The Regrettes @ The Sunflower Lounge – Indie Midlands|date=October 27, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181027041722/http://www.indiemidlands.com/2018/05/23/live-review-the-regrettes-the-sunflower-lounge/|access-date=September 21, 2020|archive-date=October 27, 2018}}</ref> On May 31, 2018, the band contributed the sixth [[Hamilton (musical)#Hamildrops (2017–2018)|Hamildrop]], a cover of the song "Helpless". Lin-Manuel Miranda credited producer [[Mike Elizondo]] as having suggested the idea.<ref>{{cite tweet |last= Miranda |first= Lin-Manuel |user=Lin_Manuel |number=1002164065723211776 |date=May 31, 2018 |link=https://twitter.com/Lin_Manuel/status/1002164065723211776 |access-date=May 31, 2018 |title=All credit to our friend Mike Elizondo, who works w The Regrettes. He told us they had an amazing take on Helpless and I said, “Yup” SO FAST }}</ref> On September 3, 2018, the band announced Sage Chavis' departure,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Casteel |first1=Beth |title=THE REGRETTES' ANNOUNCE DEPARTURE OF BASSIST, NEW ALBUM COMING IN 2019 |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/the-regrettes-bassist-leaves-band-new-album-2019/ |website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)]] |access-date=May 19, 2019}}</ref> being replaced by [[Violet Mayugba]].<ref name='The Regrettes Release Powerful New Track, "Poor Boy"'>{{cite web |last1=Butera |first1=Ava |title=The Regrettes Release Powerful New Track, "Poor Boy" |url=https://hearteyesmag.com/record-reviews/2018/11/17/the-regrettes-release-powerful-new-track-poor-boy|website=Hearteyesmag.com |access-date=May 19, 2019}}</ref> On November 28, 2018, the band announced the departure of Mayugba and recruitment of Brooke Dickson as their new bass player.<ref name="Ladies and Gentlemen, the Remarkable Regrettes Are Coming for You, Too (premiere)">{{cite web |last1=BIALAS |first1=MICHAEL |title=Ladies and Gentlemen, the Remarkable Regrettes Are Coming for You, Too (premiere) |url=https://www.popmatters.com/regrettes-poor-boy-2622401167.html|website=Popmatters.com |access-date=May 19, 2019}}</ref> From February 6 to March 17, 2019, The Regrettes were the opening act of [[Twenty One Pilots]]' European leg of [[The Bandito Tour]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.twentyonepilots.com/trench-v3|title=twenty one pilots &#124; Trench - available now|website=Twentyonepilots.com}}</ref> On June 18, 2019, The Regrettes announced their second studio album, ''How Do You Love?''. The album was released on August 9, 2019.<ref name="hdyl">{{cite web |last1=TV News Desk |title=The Regrettes Announce New Album 'How Do You Love?' |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/The-Regrettes-Announce-New-Album-How-Do-You-Love-20190618 |website=BroadwayWorld.com |access-date=June 18, 2019 |date=June 18, 2019}}</ref>

==Musical style==
The Regrettes' musical style has been labeled as [[punk rock]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Barquist |first1=Robben |title=The Regrettes Resurrect Feminist Punk With Their New EP "Attention Seeker" |url=https://culturecollide.com/regrettes-resurrect-feminist-punk-new-ep-attention-seeker/|website=Culturecollide.com|access-date=June 9, 2018}}</ref><ref name="LAT2016">{{cite news | last=Martens| first=Todd| title =The Regrettes may be high-school age, but the band's worldview is all grown up| newspaper =[[Los Angeles Times]]| date =August 17, 2016 | url =http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-regrettes-20160814-snap-story.html| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref> [[riot grrrl]],<ref name="With Guitars - Feelings">{{Cite web|url=https://www.withguitars.com/regrettes-feel-feelings-fool-riot-grrrl-resurgence-like-bikini-kill-taco-cat/|title=The Regrettes: "Feel Your Feelings, Fool" &#124; riot grrrl resurgence – like Bikini Kill, Taco Cat|date=January 13, 2017|website=WithGuitars.com}}</ref><ref name="louder than war">{{cite web|url=http://louderthanwar.com/regrettes-share-uk-tour-dates-supporting-swmrs-autumn/|title=The Regrettes Share UK Tour Dates Supporting SWMRS|date=September 10, 2017|website=Louderthanwar.com|access-date=January 18, 2018}}</ref> [[garage pop]],<ref name="SFW" /><ref name="Noisey">{{cite news | last=Stone| first=Avery| title =Teen Band The Regrettes Tell It Like It Is: Here's "A Living Human Girl" Video| publisher =[[Noisey]]| date =July 22, 2016 | url =https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/the-regrettes-a-living-human-girl-video-premiere| access-date =July 12, 2017}}</ref> and [[garage punk (fusion genre)|garage punk]],<ref name="all music">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-regrettes-mn0003523295/biography|title=The Regrettes - Biography & History - AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=January 18, 2018}}</ref><ref name="The Regrettes Resurrect Feminist Punk With Their New EP “Attention Seeker”">{{cite web |last1=BARQUIST |first1=ROBBEN |title=The Regrettes Resurrect Feminist Punk With Their New EP "Attention Seeker" |url=https://culturecollide.com/regrettes-resurrect-feminist-punk-new-ep-attention-seeker/ |access-date=May 19, 2019}}</ref> featuring elements of [[garage rock]], '60s [[doo-wop]] and [[surf music]],<ref name="louder than war" /> [[rockabilly]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pullen |first1=Larissa |title=Review: Feel Your Feelings Fool! – The Regrettes |url=https://www.oxfordstudent.com/2017/01/30/review-feel-feelings-fool-regrettes/ |website=Oxfordstudent.com|access-date=December 27, 2018}}</ref> and [[pop music]].<ref name="all music" /> Michael Bialas, a writer at [[PopMatters]] described their sound as "girl-group [[power pop|power]]-[[pop punk]]"<ref name="Ladies and Gentlemen, the Remarkable Regrettes Are Coming for You, Too (premiere)" /> In an article by Culture Collide, their style was described as "Channeling classic doo-wop through a catchy garage-punk filter" and as bringing "a level of impassioned aggression to tried-and-true pop structures, creating a compelling product with significant crossover appeal."<ref name="The Regrettes Resurrect Feminist Punk With Their New EP “Attention Seeker”" />

Lyrically, their music has been described as following themes such as [[women's empowerment]],<ref name="Ladies and Gentlemen, the Remarkable Regrettes Are Coming for You, Too (premiere)" /> [[feminism]],<ref name="The Regrettes Resurrect Feminist Punk With Their New EP “Attention Seeker”" /> [[politics]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Engelman |first1=Nicole |title=The Regrettes Release Outspoken Anti-Brett Kavanaugh Anthem 'Poor Boy': Listen |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8485455/the-regrettes-poor-boy-anti-brett-kavanaugh-anthem |website=[[Billboard (magazine)]] |access-date=May 19, 2019}}</ref> and love.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ovies |first1=Marco |title=The Regrettes release new single for lead singers "Pumpkin" |url=https://earmilk.com/2019/02/19/the-regrettes-release-new-single-for-lead-singers-pumpkin/ |access-date=May 19, 2019}}</ref> In an article by [[The Verge]], their lyrics were described as "brash and unapologetic".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Deahl |first1=Dani |last2=Amelia |first2=Holowaty Krales |title=WHAT'S IN YOUR BAG, THE REGRETTES? |url=https://www.theverge.com/2018/11/5/17936648/the-regrettes-whats-in-your-bag-pop-punk |website=[[The Verge]] |access-date=May 19, 2019}}</ref>

They also cite musical influences including [[Bikini Kill]], [[L7 (band)|L7]], [[7 Year Bitch]],<ref>{{cite web|last1=Adams|first1=Margaret|title=The Regrettes' Lead Singer Wants Girls to Feel Their Feelings|url=http://observer.com/2017/01/the-regrettes-lead-singer-wants-girls-to-feel-their-feelings|access-date=May 24, 2018}}</ref> [[Elvis Presley]], [[Buddy Holly]], [[The Ronettes]], [[Hole (band)|Hole]],<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bennett|first1=Lauren|title=The Regrettes: The Body Positive Band You Never Knew You Needed|url=https://theculturetrip.com/north-america/articles/the-regrettes-the-body-positive-band-you-never-knew-you-needed/|website=Culturetrip.com|access-date=May 21, 2018}}</ref> [[The Crystals]], [[Lesley Gore]] and [[Patsy Cline]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Ferla|first1=Lis|title=HIT ME WITH YOUR BEST SHOT: THE REGRETTES INTERVIEW;|url=https://lastyearsgirl.pixlet.net/the-regrettes-interview/|website=Lastyearsgirl.pixlet.net|access-date=May 21, 2018}}</ref>
