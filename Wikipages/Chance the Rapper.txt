'''Chancelor Johnathan Bennett''' (born April 16, 1993), known professionally as '''Chance the Rapper''', is an American rapper, singer, and record producer.

Born in [[Chicago]], Chance the Rapper released his debut mixtape ''[[10 Day]]'' in 2012. He began to gain mainstream recognition in 2013 after releasing his second mixtape, ''[[Acid Rap]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/06/28/entertainment/chance-the-rapper-acid-rap-10day-trnd/index.html|title=Chance the Rapper blesses us by releasing mixtapes '10Day' and 'Acid Rap' on all streaming platforms|author1=Erin Davis|author2=Brian Ries|website=CNN|date=June 28, 2019|access-date=2020-02-24|archive-date=2019-12-31|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191231213114/https://www.cnn.com/2019/06/28/entertainment/chance-the-rapper-acid-rap-10day-trnd/index.html|url-status=live}}</ref> He then released his third mixtape, ''[[Coloring Book (mixtape)|Coloring Book]]'' in 2016, which garnered further critical acclaim and attention. It earned him three [[Grammy Award]]s, including the award for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]]; upon winning, it became the first [[Streaming media|streaming]]-only album to win a Grammy Award, and peaked at number eight on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/71555-grammys-2017-chance-the-rapper-wins-best-rap-album/|title=Grammys 2017: Chance the Rapper Wins Best Rap Album|website=Pitchfork|date=February 13, 2017|language=en|access-date=2020-02-24|archive-date=2020-05-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200506044510/https://pitchfork.com/news/71555-grammys-2017-chance-the-rapper-wins-best-rap-album/|url-status=live}}</ref> His debut studio album ''[[The Big Day (album)|The Big Day]]'' was released in 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2019/07/26/742508980/the-big-day-is-finally-here-stream-chance-the-rapper-s-debut-album|title='The Big Day' Is Finally Here: Stream Chance The Rapper's Debut Album|newspaper=NPR|date=July 26, 2019|language=en|access-date=2020-02-24|archive-date=2020-03-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200304180346/https://www.npr.org/2019/07/26/742508980/the-big-day-is-finally-here-stream-chance-the-rapper-s-debut-album|url-status=live|last1=Madden|first1=Sidney}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ew.com/music/2019/07/26/chance-the-rapper-releases-debut-album-the-big-day/|title=It's 'The Big Day': Chance the Rapper drops official debut album|website=EW.com|language=en|access-date=2020-02-24|archive-date=2020-04-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200414225745/https://ew.com/music/2019/07/26/chance-the-rapper-releases-debut-album-the-big-day/|url-status=live}}</ref>

Alongside his solo career, Chance the Rapper is a member of the Chicago collective [[Savemoney]], and is the vocalist for the band the Social Experiment; they released the album ''[[Surf (Donnie Trumpet & The Social Experiment album)|Surf]]'' in 2015, led by trumpeter [[Nico Segal]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/05/13/chance-the-rapper-social-experiment-coloring-book|title=How Chance The Rapper & The Social Experiment Could Change The Grammys Forever|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=2020-02-24|archive-date=2020-02-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200225182913/http://www.thefader.com/2016/05/13/chance-the-rapper-social-experiment-coloring-book|url-status=live}}</ref> He is also involved in social activism in the Chicago community where he resides.<ref name="CTactivism">{{cite news |last1=Fragassi |first1=Selena |title=Chance the Rapper talks music, Chicago and activism during MCA chat |url=https://chicago.suntimes.com/entertainment/chance-the-rapper-talks-music-chicago-and-activism-during-mca-chat/ |newspaper=[[Chicago Sun-Times]] |date=March 5, 2018 |language=en |access-date=March 26, 2019 |archive-date=March 26, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190326233822/https://chicago.suntimes.com/entertainment/chance-the-rapper-talks-music-chicago-and-activism-during-mca-chat/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== 2012–2015: ''Acid Rap'' and The Social Experiment ===

=== Christianity ===
Bennett is a [[Christians|Christian]] and refers to Jesus Christ in many of his songs.<ref>Dark, David, [http://www.mtv.com/news/2882185/i-speak-to-god-in-public-chance-the-rappers-faith/ "I Speak To God In Public: Chance The Rapper's Faith" za] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161006145115/http://www.mtv.com/news/2882185/i-speak-to-god-in-public-chance-the-rappers-faith/ |date=2016-10-06 }}, MTV, May 18, 2016.</ref> Bennett was raised as a Christian by his grandmother but later fell out of the faith.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.christiantoday.com/article/chance.the.rapper.says.his.grandmas.turn.to.dust.prayer.helped.him.find.his.way.back.to.god/104132.htm|title=Chance The Rapper Says His Grandma's 'Turn to Dust' Prayer Helped Him Find His Way Back to God {{!}} Christian News on Christian Today|website=www.christiantoday.com|language=en|access-date=August 23, 2017|archive-date=August 24, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170824051851/https://www.christiantoday.com/article/chance.the.rapper.says.his.grandmas.turn.to.dust.prayer.helped.him.find.his.way.back.to.god/104132.htm|url-status=live}}</ref> He rediscovered his faith when his daughter was born with [[atrial flutter]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2016/8/26/chance-the-rapper-says-daughters-health-scare-brought-my-fai.html|title=Chance the Rapper Says Daughter's Health Scare "Brought My Faith Back" – Music News|website=ABC News Radio|language=en|access-date=August 23, 2017|archive-date=August 24, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170824054754/http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2016/8/26/chance-the-rapper-says-daughters-health-scare-brought-my-fai.html|url-status=live}}</ref> Speaking about the situation, Bennett said "[I just] pray a whole lot, you know, and need a lot of angels and just see shit in a very, like, direct way. And…you know, God bless everything, it worked out well."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://blog.godreports.com/2017/01/chance-the-rappers-grandmother-helped-him-find-his-way-back-to-god/|title=Chance the Rapper's grandmother helped him find his way back to God {{!}} God Reports|website=blog.godreports.com|language=en-US|access-date=August 23, 2017|archive-date=August 24, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170824092325/http://blog.godreports.com/2017/01/chance-the-rappers-grandmother-helped-him-find-his-way-back-to-god/|url-status=live}}</ref> Bennett wrote on Twitter following the situation on January 31, 2016, "Today's the last day my old life, last day smoking cigs. Headed to church for help. All things are possible through Christ who strengthens me."<ref>{{Cite tweet|Cite tweet|user=chancetherapper|number=693833227987980290|title=Today's the last day my old life, last day smoking cigs. Headed to church for help. All things are possible thru Christ who strengthens me.|date=January 31, 2016|access-date=August 23, 2017}}</ref>

Bennett has labelled himself as a Christian rapper following the release of his mixtape ''[[Coloring Book (mixtape)|Coloring Book]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7800145/chance-the-rapper-interview-teen-vogue-kanye-west|title=Chance the Rapper Says Working With Kanye West is 'Life-Affirming'|work=Billboard|access-date=August 23, 2017|archive-date=September 7, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170907054926/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7800145/chance-the-rapper-interview-teen-vogue-kanye-west|url-status=live}}</ref> Bennett believes that God is to thank for his blessings and his success.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/opinion/2017/05/30/kendrick-lamar-chance-the-rapper-right-about-god-christian-schneider-column/102160680/|title=Kendrick Lamar and Chance the Rapper are right about God|work=USA TODAY|access-date=August 23, 2017|language=en|archive-date=August 24, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170824052636/https://www.usatoday.com/story/opinion/2017/05/30/kendrick-lamar-chance-the-rapper-right-about-god-christian-schneider-column/102160680/|url-status=live}}</ref> He has attended multiple dates for Kanye West's [[Sunday Service (group)|Sunday Service]], most notably performing his verse on "Ultralight Beam" at [[Sunday Service Coachella performance|Coachella 2019]] and in his hometown at Chicago's [[Huntington Bank Pavilion]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.spin.com/2019/09/kanye-west-brings-out-chance-the-rapper-at-chicago-sunday-service-watch/ |title=Kanye West and Chance the Rapper Perform "Ultralight Beam" at Chicago "Sunday Service": Watch |last=Arcand |first=Rob |date=2019-09-08 |website=Spin |access-date=2019-09-16 |archive-date=2019-09-11 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190911171405/https://www.spin.com/2019/09/kanye-west-brings-out-chance-the-rapper-at-chicago-sunday-service-watch/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
