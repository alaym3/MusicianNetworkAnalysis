'''Michael Samuel Kiwanuka''' (born 3 May 1987) is a British singer-songwriter and record producer who is signed with [[Polydor Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.polydor.co.uk/artist/michael-kiwanuka/|title=Artists – Polydor Records|date=12 February 2018|publisher=Polydor Records|access-date=12 January 2018}}</ref> His 2012 debut album, ''[[Home Again (Michael Kiwanuka album)|Home Again]]'', went gold in the United Kingdom and his second album, ''[[Love & Hate (Michael Kiwanuka album)|Love & Hate]]'', debuted in 2016 at number one.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/michael-kiwanuka/|title=Michael Kiwanuka|website=Forbes.com|access-date=3 February 2019}}</ref> He has been nominated for numerous honours, including the [[Brit Awards]], [[MTV Europe Music Awards]], [[BBC Music Awards]], and [[The Grammy Awards]]. In January 2012, he won the [[BBC]]'s [[Sound of...|Sound of 2012]], and in September 2020, he won the [[Mercury Prize]].

==Early life==
Born and raised in [[Muswell Hill]], London, Kiwanuka is the son of Michael and Deborah, [[Uganda]]n parents who escaped the [[Idi Amin|Amin regime]].<ref name="Doyle">Doyle, Tom (2012) "The Soul Man", ''[[Mojo (magazine)|Mojo]]'', February 2012, p. 68-9</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2011/may/05/first-sight-michael-kawanuka|title=First sight: Michael Kiwanuka|access-date=20 June 2011|date=5 May 2011|author=Charlotte Richardson Andrews|website=Theguardian.co.uk}}</ref> He attended [[Fortismere School]], leaving there in 2005 after completing his [[A-Levels]].<ref name="Doyle" /> He went on to study at the School of Media, Arts and Design at the [[University of Westminster]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Alumnus tops BBC Sound of 2012 poll|url=http://www.westminster.ac.uk/news-and-events/news/media/2012/alumnus-tops-bbc-sound-of-2012-poll|access-date=12 January 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120123204915/http://www.westminster.ac.uk/news-and-events/news/media/2012/alumnus-tops-bbc-sound-of-2012-poll|archive-date=23 January 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Michael Kiwanuka.jpg|thumb|Performing at the 2012 [[Montreux Jazz Festival]]]]

===Early years===
Kiwanuka worked as a session guitarist, playing with [[Chipmunk (rapper)|Chipmunk]] and [[Bashy]] before working as a solo artist.<ref name="Doyle" /> His first proper gig was at The Oxford in [[Kentish Town]] at age 22.<ref>{{Cite web|publisher=[[YouTube]]|title=Michael Kiwanuka – My Community – #SupportMusic – Sponsored By Levi's® Music Project|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VUoWB8V3rfI|access-date=3 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kentishtowner.co.uk/2014/04/29/ich-bin-kentishtowner-michael-kiwanuka-singer-songwriter/|title=Ich Bin Kentishtowner: Michael Kiwanuka, singer-songwriter|last1=29 April|first1=Kentishtowner|last2=2014|date=29 April 2014|website=Kentishtowner.co.uk|access-date=3 February 2019}}</ref> He came to the attention of Communion Records, which released his first two [[Extended play|EP]]s,<ref name="Doyle" /> including his debut ''Tell Me A Tale'' on 13 June 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.twentyfourbit.com/post/4737310239/introducing-michael-kiwanuka-at-first-listen|title=Introducing: Michael Kiwanuka|access-date=20 June 2011|date=18 April 2011|website=TwentyFourBit.com}}</ref>

===2011–2016: ''Home Again'' and ''Love & Hate''===
Kiwanuka supported [[Adele]] on her [[Adele Live]] 2011 tour, as well as at her [[iTunes Festival]] 2011 gig,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/fashion/features/agenda-kat-maconie-michael-kiwanuka-limanation-pan-am-transform-festival-2288856.html|title=Agenda: Kat Maconie; Michael Kiwanuka; Limanation; Pan Am; Transform festival|access-date=20 June 2011|date=29 May 2011|work=The Independent}}</ref> and played at the 2011 [[Hard Rock Calling]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/frankie-and-the-heartstrings/57071|title=Frankie And The Heartstrings, Michael Kiwanuka and Lissie added to Hard Rock Calling bill|access-date=20 June 2011|date=3 June 2011|website=Nme.com|publisher=IPC Media}}</ref>

In 2011, he signed a deal with [[Polydor Records]].<ref name="Doyle" /> He was included in the BBC's [[Sound of...|Sound of 2012]] poll and was named as the winner on 6 January 2012.<ref name=so12>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/soundof/2012/artists/michaelkiwanuka/ |title=Sound of – 2012 – Michael Kiwanuka |publisher=BBC Music|date=22 November 2011 |access-date=6 January 2012}}</ref> In June 2012, he was illustrated in a BBC article regarding Spain vis-à-vis Uganda during the [[Eurozone crisis]]; his putative counterpart was actress [[Penélope Cruz]].

Kiwanuka released his debut studio album, ''[[Home Again (Michael Kiwanuka album)|Home Again]]'', in 2012 to positive reviews from critics. The album peaked at number 4 in the UK and, as of 2012, has sold over 70,000 copies in the UK.<ref name="May2012">{{cite web|url=http://content.yudu.com/A1wtc5/MW180512/resources/index.htm? |title=Charts EU Airplay Week 18 (p.32)|publisher=[[Music Week]] |date=17 May 2012 |access-date=19 May 2012 |url-access=subscription }}</ref>

After scrapping an entire second album (due to be called Night Songs) after he was discouraged by an A&R man,<ref name="theguardian.com">{{Cite web|date=2019-10-06|title=Michael Kiwanuka: 'I'm living my dream. And I was wasting it with thoughts of inferiority'|url=http://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/oct/06/michael-kiwanuka-interview-love-hate-cold-little-heart|access-date=2021-06-27|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref> in 2016, Kiwanuka released his follow-up to ''Home Again'', ''[[Love & Hate (Michael Kiwanuka album)|Love & Hate]]''. The album was a critical and commercial success, reaching number 1 on the UK albums chart. The album was produced by [[Danger Mouse (musician)|Danger Mouse]] and spawned several singles.

=== 2018-2019: Collaborations and ''Kiwanuka'' ===
Michael recorded a track with [[Unkle|UNKLE]] for the 2018 [[Roma (2018 film)|Roma]] companion album ('On My Knees'). After another collaborative song and video, this time with Tom Misch, was released in June 2019 ('Money'), his single, You Ain't the Problem preceded his third album [[Kiwanuka (album)|Kiwanuka]] which was released in November 2019, again to further critical and commercial success. The album placed at number 2 in the UK charts, has been gold-certified for over 100,000 sales and would go on to win the UK Mercury Music Prize for 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Michael Kiwanuka wins the 2020 Hyundai Mercury Prize|url=https://www.mercuryprize.com/news/michael-kiwanuka-wins-the-2020-hyundai-mercury-prize#:~:text=Michael%20Kiwanuka%20has%20been%20announced,One%20Show%20on%20BBC%20One.|access-date=2021-06-27|website=www.mercuryprize.com}}</ref> his first win after his first two albums which were nominated. [[Danger Mouse (musician)|Danger Mouse]] was involved with the album, along with producer [[Inflo]]. Michael has gone on record that the choice of album name reflected his increasing confidence in being himself<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-09-24|title=Mercury Prize-winner Michael Kiwanuka: 'I am not an imposter'|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-50260987|access-date=2021-06-27}}</ref>

===Musical style===
Kiwanuka has acknowledged influences from musicians such as [[Jimi Hendrix]], [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]],<ref>{{cite news| url= https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/michael-kiwanuka-add-to-queue-nirvana-isaac-hayes-hey-arnold-prince-rihanna | work=Interview Magazine | title= Michael Kiwanuka is Listening to Nirvana, Isaac Hayes, and Hey Arnold! | date=30 October 2019}}</ref> [[Ray Charles]], [[Bill Withers]],<ref name="Doyle" /> [[Otis Redding]],<ref name="Doyle" /> [[Jack Johnson (musician)|Jack Johnson]], [[Pops Staples]], [[The Band]], [[Joni Mitchell]], [[Bob Dylan]], [[Eric Bibb]], [[Tommy Sims]], [[Wham!]], [[Richie Havens]] and [[Funkadelic]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.port-magazine.com/music/soundtrack-michael-kiwanuka-2/|title=Soundtrack: Michael Kiwanuka|website=Port-magazine.com|date=15 July 2016|access-date=21 April 2019}}</ref> He has played with [[James Gadson]], who drummed for Bill Withers.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-13694904 | work=BBC News | title=Michael Kiwanuka: Singing with his soul on his sleeve | date=16 June 2011}}</ref>

Among others, Kiwanuka’s musical style has been compared to [[Bill Withers]] and [[Terry Callier]].<ref name="ReferenceA">{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/oct/06/michael-kiwanuka-interview-love-hate-cold-little-heart|title = Michael Kiwanuka: 'I'm living my dream. And I was wasting it with thoughts of inferiority'|website = [[TheGuardian.com]]|date = 6 October 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Kiwanuka is a lifelong fan of [[Tottenham Hotspur F.C.]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tottenhamhotspur.com/news/2020/october/michael-kiwanuka-on-n17-live-on-sunday/|title=Michael Kiwanuka on N17 Live on Sunday |date=30 October 2020|website=Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|access-date=18 March 2021}}</ref> He moved out of London to Southampton shortly before the release and promotion of his third album.<ref name="theguardian.com"/>

Kiwanuka has openly stated he has struggled with [[anxiety]] and other mental health issues.<ref name="ReferenceA"/>
