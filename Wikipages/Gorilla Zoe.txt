'''Alonzo Keith Mathis Jr.''' (born January 27, 1982), better known by his stage name '''Gorilla Zoe''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|z|oʊ}}), is an American rapper best known for being a member of the rap group [[Boyz n da Hood (rap group)|Boyz N Da Hood]]. His solo debut album ''Welcome to the Zoo'' came out in 2007. His next two albums, ''[[Don't Feed Da Animals]]'' and ''[[King Kong (Gorilla Zoe album)|King Kong]]'', were released in 2009 and 2011. In 2015, he released two mixtapes: ''Recovery'' and ''Raised in the Jungle''.<ref>{{cite web| url= http://www.mtv.com/artists/gorilla-zoe/ |title= Gorilla Zoe| website= [[MTV]].com| access-date= January 30, 2019}}</ref> He has been an independent artist since 2018.

==Career==
In 2006, Mathis replaced [[Young Jeezy]] as a member of [[Boyz n da Hood (rap group)|Boyz N Da Hood]]. He first saw success in collaborations with [[Yung Joc]]'s "[[Coffee Shop (Yung Joc song)|Coffee Shop]]" and "[[Bottle Poppin']]," which charted on several ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts.<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p972562|pure_url=yes}}|title=Gorilla Zoe > Biography|last=Birchmeier|first=Jason|publisher=allmusic|access-date=January 30, 2019}}</ref> He was then signed as a solo artist for [[Block Entertainment]] and [[Bad Boy South]].

On June 19, 2007, Gorilla Zoe released his first single, "[[Hood Figga]]", which was a success, peaking at #38 on the [[Billboard 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His debut solo album, ''[[Welcome to the Zoo]]'', was released in October 2007, peaking at #18 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], #8 on [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]], and #3 on [[Top Rap Albums]]. The same year, he met Mrs. Lika Joon, with whom he had a child 12 years later.<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p972562|pure_url=yes}}|title=Gorilla Zoe > Charts & Albums > Billboard Albums|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=January 30, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Top Rap Albums">{{cite web| url= http://www.billboard.com/artist/303028/gorilla-zoe/chart?sort=date&f=335|title=Gorilla Zoe - Chart History - Rap Albums |work= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=August 6, 2009| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20140411045136/http://www.billboard.com/artist/303028/gorilla-zoe/chart?sort=date&f=335|archive-date=April 11, 2014| url-status=dead| df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url= https://www.britishhiphop.co.uk/features/interviews/gorilla_zoe.html| title=Gorilla Zoe |last= Fear| first= Danielle| date= July 3, 2008 |work= BritishHipHop.co.uk| access-date=January 30, 2019}}</ref> In 2007, Gorilla Zoe was chosen for the 2008 [[XXL (magazine)|''XXL'' Freshmen]] cover along with rappers [[Saigon (rapper)|Saigon]], [[Plies (rapper)|Plies]], [[Rich Boy]], [[Joell Ortiz]], [[Lupe Fiasco]], [[Lil Boosie]], [[Crooked I]], [[Papoose (rapper)|Papoose]], and [[Young Dro]].<ref>{{cite magazine|magazine=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL Freshmen]] |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2012/03/where-are-they-now-2007-freshman-recap/|title=Where Are They Now?: 2007 Freshman Recap |date=March 27, 2012|access-date=January 30, 2019}}</ref>

On October 28, 2008, "[[Lost (Gorilla Zoe song)|Lost]]", Gorilla Zoe's first single from his second album, was released.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://www.mtv.com/news/1607159/gorilla-zoes-lost-video-targets-your-deepest-depression/|title=Gorilla Zoe's 'Lost' Video Targets Your 'Deepest Depression' |last= Reid| first= Shaheem| date=March 17, 2009|publisher=MTV News|access-date=January 30, 2019}}</ref> The full album, ''[[Don't Feed da Animals]]'', was released on March 17, 2009, topping the ''Billboard'' Top Rap Albums chart.<ref name="Top Rap Albums"/> It sold 29,000 copies in its first week, peaking on the ''Billboard'' 200 at #8. A second single from the album, "What It Is", featuring [[Rick Ross (rapper)|Rick Ross]] and Kollosus, was later released, and a third single, "Echo", followed.

During February 2010, Gorilla Zoe released a mixtape every day on the mixtape website [[DatPiff.com]].<ref name="28 Mixtapes 28 Days">{{cite web| url= http://www.datpiff.com/gorillazoehard| title= Gorilla Zoe 28 Mixtapes 28 Days |website= datpiff.com|access-date= January 30, 2019}}</ref> He released an EP, ''I Am Atlanta 3''. He also worked on a mixtape with Die-Verse City member Qu1k.

On June 14, 2011, Gorilla Zoe released his third solo album, ''[[King Kong (Gorilla Zoe album)|King Kong]]''. Although not as successful as his previous albums, it charted on the ''Billboard'' 200 at #56, selling 10,300 copies in its first week out.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.sohh.com/2011/06/eminem_royce_da_59_raise_hell_gorilla_zo.html#more |title=Eminem & Royce Da 5'9 Raise Hell, Gorilla Zoe Climbs High, Random Axe Slices Into The Chart| last= Langhorne| first= Cyrus| work= SOHH.com |access-date=February 27, 2012| date=June 22, 2011 |archive-url= https://archive.today/20140808115950/http://www.sohh.com/2011/06/eminem_royce_da_59_raise_hell_gorilla_zo.html#more|archive-date=August 8, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[Allmusic]] rated the album three-and-a-half stars out of five.<ref name="Allmusic">{{cite web |url= http://www.allmusic.com/album/king-kong-r1967599|title=King Kong - Review| work= Allmusic.com}}</ref> A single from the album, "What's Goin' On", was released on December 14, 2010,<ref>{{cite web| url= https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/whats-goin-on-single/id4083267500 |title= What's Goin On - Single by Gorilla Zoe |website= iTunes.com| access-date= January 30, 2019}}</ref> and peaked at #99 at the Billboard [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] Chart.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.billboard.com/artist/303028/Gorilla+Zoe/chart?f=367|title=Gorilla Joe - Chart History| work=Billboard |access-date= August 7, 2014 |url-status= dead| archive-date= August 25, 2014| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20140825114757/https://www.billboard.com/artist/303028/Gorilla+Zoe/chart?f=367}}</ref>

On May 6, 2014, after a two-year hiatus, Gorilla Zoe released a new mixtape, ''Recovery'', and it was revealed that he had signed to rapper [[Flo Rida]]'s label International Music Group.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Gorilla-Zoe-Recovery-mixtape.618603.html| title= Recovery Mixtape by Gorilla Zoe| work= datpiff.com| access-date=January 30, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| website= livemixtapes.com| url= https://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/28181/gorilla-zoe-recovery.html| title=Gorilla Zoe - Recovery| access-date= January 30, 2019}}</ref>

In July 2018, Gorilla Zoe released "Fat Jesus", his first single as an independent artist.
