'''Lloyd Polite Jr.''' (born January 3, 1986),<ref>{{cite web|last=Jeffries|first=David|title=Lloyd – Biography|work=[[Allmusic]]|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p661069/biography|pure_url=yes}}|access-date=December 26, 2008}}</ref> better known by his [[Mononymous person|mononym]] '''Lloyd''', is an American singer. He began his career in music as a member of the [[preteen]]-[[boy band]] [[N-Toon]]. The group disbanded in 2001 and Lloyd embarked on a solo career in 2003. He subsequently signed a recording contract with [[Murder Inc. Records]] (formerly The Inc.) and [[Def Jam Recordings]]. In 2004, Lloyd released his solo debut single "[[Southside (Lloyd song)|Southside]]" (featuring [[Ashanti (singer)|Ashanti]]), the title track of his debut album, ''[[Southside (Lloyd album)|Southside]]'' (2004). The single quickly charted on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], and became a [[Top 40]] hit. His second album ''[[Street Love]]'', was released on March 13, 2007. It featured the top-twenty hits "[[You (Lloyd song)|You]]" and "[[Get It Shawty]]".

Lloyd's third album ''[[Lessons in Love (Lloyd album)|Lessons in Love]]'' (2008), gave the singer his second top ten ''Billboard'' 200 album. Lloyd's career received an enormous boost in 2009–10 after being featured on the second single of [[Young Money Entertainment|Young Money]]'s hit "[[BedRock]]", gaining publicity from his presence on its guest list. Lloyd also released a 2009 EP entitled ''Like Me: The Young Goldie EP''. On March 13, 2010, Lloyd signed to [[Interscope Records|Interscope]] and [[Zone 4 (record label)|Zone 4]]. His fourth album ''[[King of Hearts (Lloyd album)|King of Hearts]]'', was released July 5, 2011. The first single, "[[Lay It Down (Lloyd song)|Lay It Down]]", became a top-ten hit on ''Billboard''{{'}}s [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]].

In 2016, Lloyd released "[[Tru (song)|Tru]]", his first single in five years, and an EP of the same name that same year. On August 31, 2018, he released his fifth studio album ''[[Tru (Lloyd album)|Tru LP]]'', which included the single "Caramel". The remix, released in February 2019 via an official music video, featured [[City Girls]]. In March 2019, Lloyd joined an all-star lineup of early-2000s artists for The Millennium Tour, which quickly became one of the year's most popular shows.

Lloyd had a television acting role in 2005 on the [[United Paramount Network|UPN]] show ''[[One on One (TV series)|One on One]]'', playing the role of A-Train, boyfriend to main cast member Spirit (played by [[Sicily (actress)|Sicily]]). In 2019, he debuted his film acting skills as Gregory Williams, founding member of [[Switch (band)|Switch]], in [[TV One (U.S. TV network)|TV One]]'s original biopic, ''The Bobby Debarge Story''.

== Early life ==
Lloyd Polite Jr. was born on January 3, 1986, in [[New Orleans]], [[Louisiana]], to Lloyd Polite Sr. and Robin Lewis Polite. He grew up in the [[Calliope Projects]] in [[Central City, New Orleans]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hotspotatl.com/playlist/get-caught-up-with-lloyd-gallery/item/3539828/|title=Get Caught Up With Lloyd [GALLERY]|date=May 12, 2016|website=hotspotatl.com|access-date=March 18, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lloydladies.com/1536939540.html|title=lloydladies.com|website=www.lloydladies.com|access-date=March 18, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170322024702/http://www.lloydladies.com/1536939540.html|archive-date=March 22, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> and then moved to [[Decatur, Georgia]]. In Decatur, Lloyd found his desire to sing and later relocated to New York City where he met [[Irv Gotti]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1613850/lloyd-irv-gotti-talk-about-parting-ways/|title=Lloyd, Irv Gotti Talk About Parting Ways|website=mtv.com|access-date=March 18, 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2003–2007: ''Southside'' and ''Street Love'' ===
In 2003, Lloyd was discovered and signed to [[Irv Gotti]]'s [[The Inc. Records|The Inc.]] as a solo artist and released his debut album, ''[[Southside (Lloyd album)|Southside]]'', on July 20, 2004. It debuted at number 11 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and number three on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums charts, selling over the R&B singer [[Ashanti (entertainer)|Ashanti]], managed to peak at number 24 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 and number 13 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs charts. The second single, "[[Hey Young Girl]]" was released in August 2004 and peaked at number 61 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart. The third and final single, "[[Southside (Lloyd song)|Southside Remix]]" featured Ashanti and rapper [[Scarface (rapper)|Scarface]]. Later that year he featured on [[8Ball & MJG]]'s "Forever" and Tango Redd's "Let's Cheat". In 2004, Lloyd was featured on label-mate [[Ja Rule]]'s "[[Caught Up (Ja Rule song)|Caught Up]]"; the song saw minor success in the United States, reaching number 65 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart. However, on the [[UK Singles Chart]] the song peaked at number 20.

The singer reentered the recording studios to begin work with producers [[Bryan-Michael Cox]], James Lackey, [[Jazze Pha]], [[Big Reese]] and Jasper Cameron and The Inc.'s [[7 Aurelius]]. Released on March 13, 2007, ''[[Street Love]]'' debuted at number two on the US ''[[Billboard magazine|Billboard]]'' 200 and Top R&B/Hip-Hop albums charts, with sales of 145,000 copies emerging as Lloyd's highest debut and biggest first week.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1053680/musiq-lloyd-usher-six-big-debuts-onto-album-chart |title=Musiq, Lloyd Usher Six Big Debuts Onto Album Chart|access-date=June 23, 2007 | work=USA Today}}</ref> It eventually received a [[gold certification]], and has sold over half a million copies domestically.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://blogs.usatoday.com/listenup/2007/05/riaa_gold_plati.html |title=RIAA gold & platinum ... and how they're really selling/April 2007 edition |access-date=June 23, 2007 |work=USA Today |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070517190811/http://blogs.usatoday.com/listenup/2007/05/riaa_gold_plati.html |archive-date=May 17, 2007 }}</ref> The album's lead single, Jasper Cameron-penned "[[You (Lloyd song)|You]]", featured rapper [[Lil Wayne]], was a big commercial success, becoming his first top ten single on the Hot 100. In addition, it reached the top position on the ''Billboard'' Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart. The single reached number 45 in the [[UK Single Charts|United Kingdom]] and number 25 in [[New Zealand Singles Chart|New Zealand]]. The second single "[[Get It Shawty]]" reached number 16 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 and number four on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs charts. It also reached number 37 in the United Kingdom. The third and final single, "[[Player's Prayer]]", was released and peaked at number 74 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart. The song was not promoted and didn't receive a music video. Later in 2007, he was featured on [[Huey (rapper)|Huey]]'s "[[When I Hustle]]" and [[Dem Franchize Boyz]]'s "Turn Heads", both songs saw minor success in the United States, reaching number 80 and 75 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart.

Accessed August 21, 2008.</ref> "[[How We Do It (Around My Way)]]", featured rapper [[Ludacris]], was released as the first single, peaking at number 77 on the US ''Billboard'' Top R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart and number 75 on the [[UK Singles Chart|UK]] charts. Due to the low success of the song, it only appeared on select versions of the album as a bonus track. The second single, "[[Girls Around the World]]", featured [[Lil Wayne]] and was a moderate hit. On March 13 it was confirmed that Lloyd signed to [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1633804/20100312/lloyd.jhtml|title=Exclusive: Lloyd's New Song, 'Set Me Free' -- Check It Out Here!|website=[[MTV]]|access-date=June 18, 2016}}</ref>

=== 2010–2013: ''King of Hearts'' ===
In May 2010, Lloyd teamed up with fellow New Orleans native [[Mystikal]] and recorded "Set Me Free". The video was shot primarily in the [[Calliope Projects]] of New Orleans. The song featured heavy brass sounds and a sample of "Unchain My Heart" by [[Ray Charles]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|title=Lloyd Drops 'Set Me Free' Video, Featuring Mystikal|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1639336/lloyd-drops-set-me-free-video-featuring-mystikal.jhtml|publisher=MTV|access-date=August 11, 2012}}</ref> On July 25, 2010, Lloyd announced via [[Twitter]] that his new album will be entitled ''[[King of Hearts (Lloyd album)|King of Hearts]]''. It was released on July 5, 2011, and on August 16, 2010, Lloyd released the first single entitled "[[Lay It Down (Lloyd song)|Lay It Down]]". The second single was "[[Cupid (Lloyd song)|Cupid]]" and the third single released was "[[Dedication to My Ex (Miss That)]]"; to date the single is Lloyd's biggest international hit peaking at number three on the Australian and UK charts. The song features [[André 3000]] of [[OutKast]] and is narrated by [[Lil Wayne]]. The fourth single released from the album is "Be the One" featuring [[Trey Songz]] and [[Young Jeezy]]. The single was released on December 18 and the music video premiered as the Jam Of The Week on MTV Jams.

On October 29, 2012, Lloyd released his first mixtape ''The Playboy Diaries Vol. 1''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.livemixtapes.com/mixtapes/19266/lloyd-the-playboy-diaries.html|title=Lloyd – The Playboy Diaries – DJ Scream|access-date=June 18, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Lloyd-The-Playboy-Diaries-Vol-1-mixtape.411883.html|title=Lloyd – The Playboy Diaries Vol. 1 Hosted by DJ Scream|access-date=June 18, 2016}}</ref> It features [[Lil Wayne]], [[August Alsina]], [[Trae Tha Truth]] among others. In January 2013 Lloyd released a song called "Twerk Off" featuring rapper [[Juicy J]] and was produced by [[Drumma Boy]].

=== 2016–present: ''Tru'' ===
On May 6, 2016, he released his first single in five years titled "Tru", with the audio for the song released on YouTube 12 days later.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/lloyd-tru-prod-by-justice-league-new-song.1969677.html|title=Lloyd – Tru (Prod. By J.U.S.T.I.C.E. League) – Stream [New Song]|website=HNHH|date=May 6, 2016|access-date=June 18, 2016}}</ref> On June 23, 2016, during an interview, Lloyd revealed that he had signed a new distribution deal with [[Empire Distribution|Empire]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PyEhidnB-RM|title=YouTube|website=[[YouTube]]|access-date=August 30, 2016}}</ref> Lloyd announced the release of his fifth studio album titled ''Out My Window'' on October 25, 2016.<ref name="rap-up.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2016/10/26/lloyd-announces-new-album-out-my-window/|title=Lloyd Announces New Album 'Out My Window' – Rap-Up|website=www.rap-up.com|access-date=March 18, 2018}}</ref> On November 7 Lloyd announced via his Instagram that he would be releasing a new EP before his album on December 9, 2016, titled ''Tru''.

On July 9, 2018, Lloyd released "Caramel" as the first single from his album ''[[Tru (Lloyd album)|Tru]]''. The album was released on August 31.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ratedrnb.com/2018/07/lloyd-releases-new-single-caramel/|title=Lloyd Releases New Single 'Caramel'|website=ratedrnb.com|date=July 10, 2018|access-date=July 20, 2018}}</ref>

On September 3, 2010, Lloyd cut off his signature long hair and donated it to charity.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopwired.com/2010/09/03/lloyd-hair-cut-pictures-99999/|title=Lloyd Cuts Hair, Donates It To Charity [Photos]|first1=Danielle Canada Sep 3|last1=2010|first2=11:50am Share This Tweet This Email|last2=This|date=September 3, 2010|access-date=June 18, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://straightfromthea.com/2010/09/07/lloyd-haircut-locks-for-love-photos-video/|title=Lloyd Sheds His Locks for Love… [PHOTOS + VIDEO]|last=ATLien|date=September 7, 2010|access-date=June 18, 2016}}</ref> On July 6, 2011, Lloyd revealed a new tattoo of "Guns & Roses" that was tattooed on the back of his head.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2011/07/06/lloyd-debuts-guns-roses-tattoo-on-his-head/|title=Lloyd Debuts 'Guns & Roses' Tattoo on His Head|access-date=June 18, 2016}}</ref> On March 14, 2016, it was announced and revealed that Lloyd had earned his [[General Educational Development|GED certificate]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2016/05/lloyd-high-school-diploma/|title=Lloyd Suits Up In Cap And Gown After Receiving GED|date=May 14, 2016|access-date=August 30, 2016}}</ref> In an August 2016 interview with [[Sway Calloway]], Lloyd explains his hiatus through the lyrics of his song "Tru", expressing how he lost an unborn child to an abortion and that it "left a big hole", while also detailing other family issues. In September 2017, Lloyd's wishes of having a child came true when his girlfriend gave birth to their son River. The two welcomed their daughter in late 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lloyd and His Girlfriend Are Expecting a Baby|url=https://www.rap-up.com/2017/08/14/lloyd-dehea-abraham-expecting-baby/|access-date=February 2, 2021|website=Rap-Up|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Lloyd Speaks on His Relationship with Murder Inc, Losing a Child, Miguel Beef + New Life and New Music|url=http://swaysuniverse.com/lloyd-speaks-on-his-relationship-with-murder-inc-losing-a-child-miguel-beef-new-life-and-new-music|website=swaysuniverse.com|publisher=Sways Universe|access-date=December 10, 2016}}</ref>
