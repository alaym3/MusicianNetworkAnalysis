'''Oscar Lang Jr.''' was an [[American football]], [[basketball]], and [[baseball]] player as well as a coach.<ref name=consh>{{cite news|url=http://contentdm1.accesspa.org/cdm/ref/collection/pcofl-creco/id/353|title=[No title]|date=February 7, 1900|work=The Conshohocken Recorder|accessdate=April 16, 2015}}</ref>

==University of Virginia==
He was a prominent [[Fullback (American football)|fullback]] for the [[Virginia Cavaliers football]] team of the [[University of Virginia]].

===1895===
He was selected [[College Football All-Southern Team|All-Southern]] in [[1895 Virginia Cavaliers football team|1895]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/1986569//|work=The Daily Tar Heel|title=[No title]|via=[[Newspapers.com]]|date=April 18, 1896|page=4|accessdate=March 14, 2015}} {{Open access}}</ref>

==Pro ball==
He played with the Philadelphia All-Pro team.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://archive.org/stream/lagenda190300unse#page/180/mode/2up/search/Lang|title=L'Agenda|year=1903|page=180}}</ref>

==Bucknell==
In 1897 he played as a [[Halfback (American football)|halfback]] for the [[Bucknell Bison football|Bucknell Bison]].<ref name=consh/>

==Coaching career==
He coached the [[Susquehanna University]] football eleven.<ref>{{cite news|work=The Times|title=Forward Passes|date=November 7, 1901|page=12|via=[[Newspapers.com]]}} {{Open access}}</ref>
