'''Freedom Fry''' is an American [[indie pop]] band from [[Los Angeles]], California, United States, composed of [[Paris]]ian-born [[Marie Seyrat]] and American [[Bruce Driscoll]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blog.viva-radio.com/Post.aspx?p=2126 |title=Viva Radio Blog |publisher=Blog.viva-radio.com |date=2012-04-04 |accessdate=2015-11-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151122115126/http://blog.viva-radio.com/Post.aspx?p=2126 |archive-date=2015-11-22 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Career==
===''Let the Games Begin'' EP===
[[File:FreedomFry-SXSW.jpg|thumb|Freedom Fry performing for KUTX at SXSW]]
The duo first met in April 2011 when Seyrat was the stylist on a video for Driscoll's other band [[Blondfire]]. Impressed by Seyrat's whispery voice, Driscoll asked her if she would be interested in writing some songs together.<ref>{{cite web|title=Song + Video : Freedom Fry : "Summer In The City" « My Old Kentucky Blog |url=http://www.myoldkentuckyblog.com/?p=32936|publisher=Myoldkentuckyblog.com|date=2012-07-17 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref> In August they recorded and released the Let The Games Begin EP.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.themahoganyblog.com/2012/02/introducing-freedom-fry/ |title=Introducing… Freedom Fry |accessdate=January 25, 2017 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120205163704/http://www.themahoganyblog.com/2012/02/introducing-freedom-fry/ |archivedate=February 5, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/let-the-games-begin-ep/id459132886|title=Let the Games Begin EP by Freedom Fry on iTunes |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2011-08-20 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

The video for the first single, Tallest Dreams, starred [[Gia Mantegna]], daughter of actor [[Joe Mantegna]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Freedom Fry - "Tallest Dreams"|url=http://www.earmilk.com/2012/01/06/freedom-fry-tallest-dreams-videotrack/|publisher=Earmilk |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

The last song on the EP, Rolling Down, was featured on the season 7 premiere of [[Bones (TV series)|Bones]], during the birth of the main character's baby, Christine.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wetpaint.com/bones/articles/bones-music-what-song-was-playing-when-brennan-gave-booth-to-her-baby |title=Bones Music: What Song Was Playing When Brennan Gave Birth to Her Baby? |accessdate=January 25, 2017 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120605034105/http://www.wetpaint.com/bones/articles/bones-music-what-song-was-playing-when-brennan-gave-booth-to-her-baby |archivedate=June 5, 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wetpaint.com/bones/video/listen-official-music-video-for-rolling-downs-freedom-fry-the-song-playing-when-brennan-gives-birth-on-bones-season-7|title=Listen! Official Music Video for Rolling Down's "Freedom Fry" — The Song Playing When Brennan Gives Birth on Bones Season 7 &#124; Wetpaint, Inc. &#124; Wetpaint, Inc|publisher=Wetpaint.com|date=2012-04-03|accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

==="Earthquake"===
Their follow up single and video, "Earthquake", was released on [[Valentine's Day]] 2012. It was named single-of-the-week by French music magazine [[Les Inrockuptibles]].<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://popstereo.blogspot.com/2012/07/freedom-fry-are-tasty.html |title=The POP! Stereo: Freedom Fry Are Tasty |publisher=Popstereo.blogspot.com |date=2012-07-20 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://newonmyplaylist.com/freedom-fry-earthquake-video/ |title=[new&#93; Freedom Fry: Earthquake (video) |publisher=Newonmyplaylist.com |date=2012-02-08 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Broden Terry |url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2635232 |title=The Freedom Of An Earthquake - News Article |publisher=AbsolutePunk.net |date= |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/earthquake-single/id499891560 |title=Earthquake - Single by Freedom Fry on iTunes |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2012-02-14 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

===''Outlaws'' EP===
Following a visit to the grave of [[Billy the Kid]] in [[Fort Sumner, New Mexico|Fort Sumner, NM]], the duo began to conceptualize a wild west themed EP.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.therecordstache.com/2012/04/freedom-fry-outlaws-ep.html |title=Freedom Fry - Outlaws EP |publisher=The Record Stache |date=2012-04-17 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref> In April 2012, they released the Outlaws EP, featuring two original songs and a cover of [[Serge Gainsbourg]] and [[Brigitte Bardot]]'s [[Bonnie and Clyde (Serge Gainsbourg and Brigitte Bardot song)|Bonnie and Clyde]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thewoundedjukebox.com/2012/04/14/freedom-fry-bonnie-clyde/ |title=Freedom Fry: "Bonnie & Clyde" |publisher=The Wounded Jukebox |date=2012-04-14 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/outlaws-single/id516640322 |title=Outlaws - Single by Freedom Fry on iTunes |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2012-04-10 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

==="Summer in the City"===
The song and video for "Summer in the City", was released in July 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nylonmag.com/nylonblogs/blog/2012/07/17/music-video-freedom-fry/ |title=music video: freedom fry |accessdate=January 25, 2017 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130407063820/http://nylonmag.com/nylonblogs/blog/2012/07/17/music-video-freedom-fry/ |archivedate=April 7, 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/summer-in-the-city-single/id543627469 |title=Summer in the City - Single by Freedom Fry on iTunes |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2012-07-10 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cougarmicrobes.com/2012/07/weekend-videos-freedom-fry-summer-city/ |title=Weekend Videos: Freedom Fry - Summer In The City |publisher=Cougar Microbes |date=2012-07-16 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bitcandy.com/newFinds/newFindDedicated/newfindsfreedomfry/ |title=New Finds: Freedom Fry &#124; Top Indie |publisher=BitCandy |date=2012-08-06 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

===''The Wilder Mile''===
''The Wilder Mile'' EP and video were released on 4 August 2014.<ref>{{cite web|author=Kevin Bronson |url=http://www.buzzbands.la/2014/08/01/video-freedom-fry-wilder-mile/ |title=Video: Freedom Fry, 'The Wilder Mile' – |publisher=Buzzbands.la |date=2014-08-01 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.indieshuffle.com/freedom-fry-the-wilder-mile/ |title=[LISTEN&#93; Freedom Fry - The Wilder Mile |publisher=Indie Shuffle |date= |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.therecordstache.com/2014/08/freedom-fry-the-wilder-mile/ |title=Freedom Fry - The Wilder Mile |publisher=The Record Stache |date=2014-10-20 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

==="Shaky Ground (Hey Na Na Na)"===
At the end of 2014 the band began working with The Agency Group for European booking<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theagencygroup.com/artist/freedom-fry/ |title=Freedom Fry - United Talent Agency |publisher=Theagencygroup.com |date= |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref> and manager Jeff Klein of A-Side Worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.asideworldwide.com/clients/freedom-fry/ |title=Freedom Fry &#124; A-Side Worldwide, LLC |publisher=Asideworldwide.com |date= |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref> Their first musical offering of 2015 was the single, "Shaky Ground (Hey Na Na Na)".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kickkicksnare.com/2015/01/22/freedom-fry-shaky-ground-hey-na-na-na/ |title=Freedom Fry: Shaky Ground (Hey Na Na Na) |publisher=Kick Kick Snare |date=2015-01-22 |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref> The song reached number one on [[Hype Machine]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freedomfrymusic.com/news/2015/02/shaky-ground-1-hype-machine/ |title=Shaky Ground |publisher=Freedomfrymusic.com |date= |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

In 2016 the band signed with [[Polydor Records]] for Europe and officially released "Shaky Ground" with an accompanying two-part music video featuring both the English version and a new French version of the song.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://buzzbands.la/2016/06/10/video-freedom-fry-shaky-ground/ |title=Video: Freedom Fry, ‘Shaky Ground’ |publisher=buzzbands.la |accessdate=2016-07-11}}</ref>

“Shaky Ground” was used in the official trailer for [[Locked Down (film)|Locked Down]], the 2021 [[Doug Liman]] film starring [[Anne Hathaway]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/movie/locked-down-2021 |title=Songs and music featured in Locked Down Soundtrack
 |publisher=Tunefind.com |date=2021-01-14 |accessdate=2021-01-13}}</ref>

==="21"===
In May, 2015, Freedom Fry released the single, "21". The handclaps, group vocals, and scream featured in the song were performed by the audiences at their Echo residency as part of a crowd recording experiment.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.earmilk.com/2015/05/27/freedom-fry-release-21-premiere/ |title=Freedom Fry release "21" [Premiere |publisher=Earmilk |date= |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref> The song reached number one on [[Hype Machine]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freedomfrymusic.com/news/2015/06/21-hits-1-on-hype-machine |title=21 Hits #1 on Hype Machine - Freedom Fry |publisher=Freedomfrymusic.com |date= |accessdate=2015-11-21}}</ref>

==="Awake"===
In January 2017, Freedom Fry followed up their single 'Shaky Ground' with a new single titled "Awake." Prior to release, "Awake" had already been heard in several advertisements, television shows, and films, including the original [[Netflix]] series [[Love (TV series)|Love]] and the film "Baby Baby Baby" starring [[Adrianne Palicki]] with [[Jessica Alba]], and [[William Shatner]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eastportlandblog.com/2017/01/14/freedom-fry-awake |title=Freedom Fry - Awake |publisher=Eastportlandblog.com |date=2017-01-15 |accessdate=2017-01-24}}</ref>

===“Girl On Fire”===

Freedom Fry’s cover of “Man on Fire” by [[Edward Sharpe and the Magnetic Zeros]] was featured in the [[Super Bowl]] ad “A Giant Story” by [[Intuit]]. The reworked version, titled “Girl on Fire,” was released in February 2018. The short film from [[Intuit]] has amassed over 20 million views on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://intuitblog.com/powering-prosperity/girlonfire-the-song-behind-a-giant-story/ |title=#GirlOnFire: The Song Behind "A Giant Story" |publisher=IntuitBlog.com |date=2018-02-04 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref><ref name="GirlBoss.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.girlboss.com/life/2018-2-23-freedom-fry-interview |title=Meet The Duo Behind That Feminist Super Bowl Ad Track You Loved |publisher=GirlBoss.com |date=2018-02-26 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ktafrbsKeZw |title=Intuit A Giant Story |publisher=Intuit  |date=2018-02-01 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref>

===''Classic'' LP===
[[File:Freedom-Fry-Billboard-Melrose.jpg|thumb|Billboard for Freedom Fry's Classic LP on Melrose Ave.]]
In February 2018 Freedom Fry announced their forthcoming album, ‘Classic.’ On June 1, 2018, the album was released. It included two previously released songs, “Awake” and “Wild Child” and ten new songs. The song “Cold Blooded Heart” premiered on Culture Collide. The track “Die Tryin” premiered on [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://buzzbands.la/2018/02/24/stream-freedom-fry-classic/ |title=Stream: Freedom Fry, ‘Classic’ |publisher=Buzzbands.la |date=2018-02-24 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://culturecollide.com/song-premiere-freedom-fry-goes-western-cold-blooded-heart/ |title=Song Premiere: Freedom Fry Goes Western for "Cold Blooded Heart" |publisher=CultureCollide.com |date=2018-05-03 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8458138/freedom-fry-die-tryin |title=Stream: Freedom Fry’s ‘Die Tryin’ Is Their Anti-Apathy Love Song’: Premiere |publisher=Billboard.com |date=2018-05-29 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref>

The album features notable performances from Stewart Cole of [[Edward Sharpe and the Magnetic Zeros]] and string arranger, Phillip Peterson.

The song “Classic” went to number one on the [[Hype Machine]].<ref name="BroadwayWorld.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Freedom-Frys-Debut-Album-CLASSIC-Out-Today-Release-Shows-In-LA-San-Francisco-20180601 |title=Freedom Fry’s Debut Album CLASSIC Out Today + Release Shows In L.A. & San Francisco |publisher=BroadwayWorld.com |date=2018-06-01 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref>

The band’s marketing efforts for ‘Classic’ included a [[billboard]] on Melrose Ave, flying an airplane banner above [[South by Southwest]], and surprising the audience with Mariachis during their official showcase and at their album release show in Los Angeles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://therevue.ca/2018/04/10/sxsw-2018-memories-freedom-fry/ |title=SXSW 2018 Memories - Freedom Fry |publisher=therevue.ca |date=2018-04-10 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref>

An album trailer starring [[Shameless (U.S. TV series)|Shameless]] actress, [[Emma Kenney]] was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/ffc-freedom-fry-classic-interview-2018/ |title=Past, Present, Future, and Classics: A Conversation With Freedom Fry |publisher=AtwoodMagazine.com |date=2018-06-01 |accessdate=2018-06-02}}</ref>

===2019 EPs===

During 2019 Freedom Fry released a new three-song EP every month. Each EP consisted of two original songs and a cover. The covers included songs from [[Elton John]], [[The Strokes]], [[The Smiths]], [[Styx (band)|Styx]], [[Carly Simon]], and others.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://buzzbands.la/2019/11/26/freedom-fry-have-released-11-eps-this-year-with-one-to-come-here-are-the-highlights/ |title=Freedom Fry have released 11 EPs this year (with one to come): Here are the highlights |publisher=Buzzbands.la |date=2019-11-26 |accessdate=2019-12-22}}</ref> Their cover of [[Clint Eastwood (song)|Clint Eastwood]] by [[Gorillaz]] was used as the end credits song during the season one finale of [[The Morning Show (American TV series)|The Morning Show]] starring [[Jennifer Aniston]] and [[Reese Witherspoon]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://leosigh.com/listen-to-freedom-frys-clint-eastwood-from-the-morning-show-gorillaz-cover/ |title=Listen to Freedom Fry’s ‘Clint Eastwood’ from The Morning Show - quirky Gorillaz cover is awesome |publisher=Leo Sigh |date=2019-12-21 |accessdate=2019-12-22}}</ref> In June of 2019 the duo released a remix of [[Bad Guy (Billie Eilish song)|Bad Guy]] by [[Billie Eilish]] on their [[SoundCloud]] page.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://floodmagazine.com/62455/listen-billie-eilishs-bad-guy-gets-remixed-by-freedom-fry/ |title=LISTEN: Billie Eilish’s “bad guy” Gets Remixed by Freedom Fry |publisher=Flood Magazine |date=2019-06-06 |accessdate=2019-12-22}}</ref>
