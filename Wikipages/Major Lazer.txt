'''Major Lazer''' is an [[electronic dance music]] DJ trio, which includes [[record producer]] [[Diplo]], and DJs [[Walshy Fire]] and [[Ape Drums]]. It originates from [[Kingston, Jamaica]] and was founded in 2008 by Diplo and [[Switch (songwriter)|Switch]], with Switch leaving after three years in 2011. He was then replaced by both [[Jillionaire]] and Walshy Fire. In June 2019, Jillionaire left the group and was replaced by Ape Drums.<ref name="AD"/> Its music spans numerous [[Music genre|genres]], mixing [[reggae]] with [[dancehall]], [[reggaeton]], [[Soca music|soca]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7326599/jillionaire-bunji-garlin-television-major-lazer-dance|title=Listen to Major Lazer's Jillionaire Join Forces With Soca Star Bunji Garlin on 'Television' (Exclusive)|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-06-24}}</ref> [[House music|house]] and [[moombahton]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.indoclubbing.com/magazine/308/edm-band-interview-major-lazer-usa|title=Interview – EDM Band Interview – Major Lazer (USA)|work=indoclubbing.com}}</ref><ref>{{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0002836946|label=''Peace Is the Mission''|first=David|last=Jeffries|access-date=19 July 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Dolan|first=Jon|date=June 2, 2015|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/major-lazer-peace-is-the-mission-20150602|title=Major Lazer's New Album: Peace Is the Mission|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|publisher=[[Jann Wenner|Wenner Media]]|access-date=June 6, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://ca.complex.com/music/2013/09/people-keeping-moombahton-alive/diplo-major-lazer|title=Diplo & Major Lazer – Meet the People Keeping Moombahton Alive – Complex CA|author=Ma-less|work=Complex CA}}</ref>

Major Lazer has released four full-length albums: ''[[Guns Don't Kill People... Lazers Do]]'' in 2009, ''[[Free the Universe]]'' in 2013, ''[[Peace Is the Mission]]'' in 2015, and ''[[Music Is the Weapon]]'' in 2020. They have also released six EPs, including ''Apocalypse Soon'' in March 2014, which featured vocal contributions from [[Pharrell Williams]] and [[Sean Paul]], and ''[[Know No Better]]'' in 2017, which featured appearances from Camila Cabello, Quavo, Travis Scott, and J Balvin, among others. Major Lazer has also notably produced ''[[Reincarnated (album)|Reincarnated]]'', the debut reggae album by [[Snoop Dogg]] under his "Snoop Lion" persona.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Blistein|first1=Jon|title=Snoop Lion and Major Lazer Host Raucous Bash in Austin|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/snoop-lion-and-major-lazer-host-raucous-bash-in-austin-20130315|website=Rolling Stone|access-date=19 September 2016}}</ref> In March 2018, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' ranked Major Lazer at number five on their 2018 ''Billboard'' Dance 100 ranking of dance musicians.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8257429/billboard-dance-100-2018 |title=Billboard Dance 100 Artists of 2018: The Complete List |website=Billboard |access-date=2018-03-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.wjbdradio.com/music-news/2018/03/22/the-chainsmokers-named-top-djs-in-the-world-by-billboard |title=The Chainsmokers named top DJs in the world by Billboard |publisher=WJBD |access-date=2018-03-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180322221157/http://www.wjbdradio.com/music-news/2018/03/22/the-chainsmokers-named-top-djs-in-the-world-by-billboard |archive-date=22 March 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==History==
===2009–11: ''Guns Don't Kill People... Lazers Do'' and ''Lazers Never Die'' EP===
[[Image:MAJOR LAZER - COACHELLA 2010.jpg|thumb|left|Skerrit Bwoy with Major Lazer at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella Festival]], 2010.|alt=]]
Originally made up of DJs [[Diplo]] and [[Switch (songwriter)|Switch]], the two were introduced while working with [[M.I.A. (rapper)|M.I.A.]]. The duo's first album ''[[Guns Don't Kill People... Lazers Do]]'' was released on 16 June 2009, on [[Downtown Records]]. It was recorded in [[Jamaica]] at [[Tuff Gong]] Studios.<ref>{{cite web |last=Dacks |first=David |url=http://exclaim.ca/articles/multiarticlesub.aspx?csid1=134&csid2=4&fid1=39444 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120714225848/http://exclaim.ca/articles/multiarticlesub.aspx?csid1=134&csid2=4&fid1=39444 |url-status=dead |archive-date=14 July 2012 |title=Major Lazer Shoots To Thrill • Interviews • |publisher=Exclaim.ca |date=6 May 2012 |access-date=16 April 2013 }}</ref> Vocalists such as [[Santigold]], [[Vybz Kartel]], [[Ward 21]], [[Busy Signal]], [[Nina Sky]], [[Amanda Blank]], [[Mr. Vegas]], [[Turbulence (Musician)|Turbulence]], [[Mapei (musician)|Mapei]], [[T.O.K]], Prince Zimboo, Leftside and others contribute guest vocals to the album, as well as additional production from [[Afrojack]] , [[Nebat Drums]] and [[Crookers]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/34831-major-lazer-diplo-switch-to-release-digital-reggae-lp/ |title=Major Lazer (Diplo + Switch) to Release "Digital Reggae" LP &#124; News |website=Pitchfork |date=13 March 2009 |access-date=16 April 2013}}</ref> This is the first release to come out of the label partnership between Downtown Records and Diplo's label [[Mad Decent]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Diplo takes Mad Decent Downtown|url = http://soundbleed.com/2009/03/diplo-takes-mad-decent-downtown/|website = SoundBleed|access-date = 2016-01-27|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160128073844/http://soundbleed.com/2009/03/diplo-takes-mad-decent-downtown/|archive-date = 28 January 2016|url-status = dead}}</ref> Major Lazer ended a US tour summer of 2009.<ref>Deluca, Dan [http://www.philly.com/inquirer/weekend/20090605_Malian_duo_s_music_reflects_wedded_bliss.html "The songs of summer"], ''Philadelphia Inquirer''</ref>

The album's first single was "Hold the Line", the video for which, directed by [[Eric Wareheim]], was nominated for a [[MTV Video Music Awards|MTV Video Award]] in the [[MTV Video Music Award – Breakthrough Video|Breakthrough Video]] category. "Hold the Line" was also featured in the video game [[FIFA 10]]. The track "Zumbie" has a music video that features comedian [[Andy Milonakis]].<ref>[http://www.filter-mag.com/index.php?id=19087&c=6 "Major Lazer, Zumbie Video"], ''Filter'' magazine</ref> Music videos for the songs "[[Pon de Floor]]", "Keep It Going Louder", and "[[Bubble Butt]]" were directed by [[Eric Wareheim]]. In June 2009, Major Lazer were featured for the first time on the cover of the publication ''[[The Fader]]'', in its 62nd issue.<ref>{{cite magazine
  | title = We Part (and Make Magazines)
  | magazine = The Fader
  | date = 16 June 2009
  | url = http://www.thefader.com/2009/06/16/we-party-and-make-magazines/
  | access-date = 13 November 2013}}</ref>
Major Lazer performed at the Falls Music & Arts Festival (Lorne, Victoria, Australia) on 30 December 2009 and the [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella Music Festival]] in April 2010. They also performed at the [[Pitchfork Music Festival]] in July 2010 and the Sasquatch Music Festival on Monday 30 May 2011. Live visuals were created by Pomp&Clout. Major Lazer performed at the 2011 Outside Lands festival in San Francisco.<ref>{{cite web|title=Outside Lands 2011 lineup|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/outside-lands-2-5/|website=Brooklyn Vegan |access-date=20 October 2016}}</ref>

A mixtape with [[La Roux]] titled "Lazerproof" was made available for free download on 26 May.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/38907-major-lazer-la-roux-team-for-mixtape/ |title=Major Lazer, La Roux Team for Mixtape &#124; News |website=Pitchfork |date=24 May 2010 |access-date=16 April 2013}}</ref> [[Diplo]] posted cover art on his Twitpic. It is the character Major Lazer in War Machine's silver suit, [[Elly Jackson]] of La Roux in the signature Iron Man suit, and Whiplash, the villain in the ''Iron Man 2'' movie. On 20 June 2010, the group released an EP titled ''[[Lazers Never Die]]''. It contained two new songs and three remixes, by [[Thom Yorke]] and others. Switch provided production for [[Beyoncé]]'s "[[Run the World (Girls)]]", which sampled "Pon de Floor". Major Lazer also features in the track, "Look at Where We Are", from the extended version of Hot Chip's album ''[[In Our Heads]]'', released June 2011.

===2012–15: ''Free the Universe'', ''Apocalypse Soon'' EP, and ''Peace Is the Mission''===
[[Image:Major Lazer 2013.jpg|thumb|Diplo and Jillionaire performing at the [[Congress Theater]], [[Chicago]], in 2013.]]
Major Lazer's live shows previously included [[hype man]] Skerrit Bwoy,<ref>{{cite web|title=Major Lazer Bio|url=http://top40-charts.com/artist.php?aid=16684|website=Top 40 Charts|access-date=20 October 2016}}</ref> although he left the group in 2012 to pursue religion. In 2012, Major Lazer began working with [[Snoop Dogg]] to produce some tracks that were to be included on his new reggae project. Along with the project came his new alter ego persona known as "Snoop Lion". Switch left the collaborative effort due to "creative differences" as claimed by Diplo. He departed in late 2011 and was replaced with DJ/Producers Jillionaire and Walshy Fire. Also, [[Boaz van de Beatz]], artist from [[Mad Decent]] (record label of [[Diplo]]), has collaborated with most of the group albums and is considered a member of the group.{{Citation needed|date=June 2017}}
In September 2012, Major Lazer announced that its upcoming album, ''[[Free the Universe]]'', would be released on 12 March 2013 on Mad Decent. They also announced Fall 2012 tour dates.<ref name="NYMN September 2012">{{cite web |title=UPDATE: Major Lazer's Free The Universe Release |url=http://nymn.com/update-major-lazers-free-the-universe-release/|publisher=Nymn.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120915102540/http://nymn.com/update-major-lazers-free-the-universe-release/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-09-15 | date=11 September 2012 |access-date=23 September 2012}}</ref>

Major Lazer partly produced UK singer [[Rita Ora]]'s debut album, ''[[Ora (Rita Ora album)|Ora]]''. Major Lazer worked with [[No Doubt]] for their sixth studio album, ''[[Push and Shove (album)|Push and Shove]]''. The album's title track features producers Major Lazer and Jamaican reggae artist [[Busy Signal]], and was released as a promotional single in August 2012.
Major Lazer launched their Fall Tour 2012 in October, with shows in Oakland, Washington, D.C., Boston, and New York City, among others.

In February 2013, Major Lazer signed with indie record label [[Secretly Canadian]]. At the same time, they announced the new release date, 16 April, for their album ''[[Free the Universe]]''. They also announced the Free the Universe 2013 World Tour, which ran through the spring of 2013.

[[File:Walshy Fire of Major Lazer @ Flow 2015.jpg|thumb|left|Walshy Fire of Major Lazer performing at [[Flow Festival]] in Helsinki, Finland, August 2015]]
On 8 February 2015, during the [[Grammy Award]] ceremony, Diplo revealed details of the third Major Lazer album. He confirmed that the album would incorporate artists such as [[Ariana Grande]], [[Ellie Goulding]] and [[Pusha T]], and would be titled ''[[Peace Is the Mission|Peace is the Mission]]''.

The first single, "[[Lean On]]", was a collaboration with French producer [[DJ Snake]] and featured vocals from Danish recording artist [[MØ]]. It was released 2 March 2015. The song was first offered to [[Rihanna]] and then [[Nicki Minaj]], who both turned it down; however Major Lazer later expressed that no one could have done a better job than MØ in the recording.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/34001810/diplo-wanted-rihanna-or-nicki-minaj-to-sing-on-lean-on|title=Diplo wanted Rihanna or Nicki Minaj to sing on Lean On|access-date=2016-08-04}}</ref> The song reached number 4 on the [[US Billboard charts|US Billboard]]'s Hot 100 chart and number 2 in the UK charts. It also became Spotify's most streamed song of all time,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/6760813/major-lazer-lean-on-spotify-most-streamed-song-all-time|title=Major Lazer & DJ Snake's 'Lean On' Becomes Spotify's Most-Streamed Song of All Time|access-date=2016-08-04}}</ref> receiving universally positive reviews while reaching over 2 billion views on YouTube, making it the twelfth most viewed video on the site.<ref>{{cite AV media |publisher=majorlazer |title=Major Lazer & DJ Snake – Lean On (feat. MØ) (Official Music Video) |date=2015-03-22 |via=YouTube |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YqeW9_5kURI| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211107/YqeW9_5kURI| archive-date=2021-11-07 | url-status=live|access-date=2016-08-04}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

Four promotional singles were released; "Roll the Bass" 23 March 2015, "Night Riders", a collaboration with [[Travis Scott|Travi$ Scott]], [[2 Chainz]], [[Pusha T]] and [[Mad Cobra]], released 20 April 2015, and "Too Original", a collaboration with [[Elliphant]] and Jovi Rockwell, released on 11 May 2015. On June 2, 2015, a lyric video was released for "[[Be Together (Major Lazer song)|Be Together]]", a collaboration with [[Wild Belle]], making it the fourth promotional single.

The second single from the album, titled "[[Powerful (song)|Powerful]]", featured English singer-songwriter [[Ellie Goulding]] and Jamaican singer [[Tarrus Riley]]. It was released simultaneously with the album, and was added to [[BBC Radio 1]]'s playlist on June 21, 2015. It reached the Top 10 in Australia and Poland.

In an interview with ''[[inthemix]]'' on 22 May 2015, Walshy Fire announced that Major Lazer's fourth studio album would be titled ''Music Is the Weapon'' and that it would be released late 2015 or early 2016.<ref name="album">{{cite web|url=http://www.inthemix.com.au/news/60601/Were_getting_two_Major_Lazer_albums_this_year|title=Major Lazer are "almost sure" they'll be in Australia this summer|access-date=27 May 2015}}</ref> The album never arrived in 2015 or 2016, though Diplo did reveal on 27 April 2015 during an interview with Belgian radio station [[Studio Brussel]] that Major Lazer was working on a new track with Belgian singer [[Selah Sue]], and that it might appear on their next album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://stubru.be/popnieuws/majorlazerneemtnummeropmetselahsue|title=Major Lazer neemt nummer op met Selah Sue|access-date=1 June 2015}}</ref> Selah Sue herself had confirmed this during an earlier interview with the same radio station on 4 May 2015.

''[[Peace Is The Mission]]'' was released on June 1, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/58726-diplos-major-lazer-announce-new-album-peace-is-the-mission-featuring-collaborations-with-pusha-t-ellie-goulding-2-chainz-ariana-grande-more/|title=Diplo's Major Lazer Announce New Album Peace Is The Mission Featuring Collaborations with Pusha T, Ellie Goulding, 2 Chainz, Ariana Grande, More |access-date=13 May 2015}}</ref> It featured artists such as [[Machel Montano]], [[Wild Belle]], [[Pusha T]], [[Ellie Goulding]], and [[Ariana Grande]]. It peaked at number 12 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart and received general critical success.

On 27 November 2015, Major Lazer released a re-issue of their third studio album ''Peace is the Mission'', re-titled as ''Peace is the Mission: Extended''. It featured five additional songs, including a collaboration with Dutch electro house DJ [[MOTi]] (featuring [[Ty Dolla Sign|Ty Dolla $ign]], [[Wizkid (musician)|Wizkid]], and [[Kranium]]), a remix of their previous track "Light It Up" featuring Nyla and Fuse ODG, and a cover of [[Frank Ocean]]'s "Lost" featuring previous collaborator and Danish recording artist MØ.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/62168-major-lazer-announce-expanded-edition-of-peace-is-the-mission/|title=Major Lazer Announce Expanded Edition of Peace Is the Mission|work=Pitchfork}}</ref>

On 5 November 2015, Major Lazer featured on [[Katy B]]<nowiki>'s single ''</nowiki>[[Who Am I (Katy B song)|Who Am I]]<nowiki>''</nowiki> alongside [[Craig David]].

Exactly one year after ''Peace is the Mission'' was released, Major Lazer announced a new remix album titled ''Peace Is the Mission: Remixes''. It featured remixes of selected tracks from their third studio album, including "Lean On", "Powerful", "Be Together", "Night Riders", and "Roll the Bass". Major Lazer had over 27 million tags in music identification service [[Shazam (service)|Shazam]] in 2015. This, along with [[Diplo]]'s other collaboration [[Jack Ü]], gave Diplo the greatest number of Shazams in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dancingastronaut.com/2015/12/diplo-shazamed-artist-2015/|title=Diplo is the most Shazamed artist of 2015|access-date=9 December 2015}}</ref>

=== 2016–19: ''Know No Better'' EP, ''Essentials'' and ''Africa Is the Future'' EP ===
On 22 July 2016, a new track titled "[[Cold Water (song)|Cold Water]]", featuring vocals from [[MØ]] and [[Justin Bieber]], was released by Major Lazer. It debuted at No. 2 on the Billboard Hot 100, and number 1 in several other countries including on the [[The Official Chart|UK Official Charts]], making it their most popular single yet.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.inquisitr.com/3361144/major-lazer-justin-bieber-mo-end-drakes-uk-no-1-reign-cold-water-elvis/|title=Major Lazer, Justin Bieber & MØ Throw 'Cold Water' On Drake, Top Charts In Australia & More|access-date=2016-08-04}}</ref> On 30 September 2016, "[[Believer (Major Lazer and Showtek song)|Believer]]", a collaboration with Dutch production duo [[Showtek]], was released as the second single from Major Lazer's upcoming fourth album. The album's title, ''Music Is the Weapon'', had been announced in May 2015, and was set to have "a few more club songs, a few more festival songs" than ''Peace is the Mission'', but to be "in the same spectrum."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/major_lazer_name_new_album_music_is_weapon|title=Major Lazer Name New Album 'Music Is the Weapon' |language=en |access-date=2017-10-08}}</ref>

On 6 December 2016, Major Lazer released a promotional single for ''Music Is the Weapon'', titled "My Number". The song is a collaboration with [[Bad Royale]] that samples "[[54-46 That's My Number]]", which Pitchfork describes as "a genre-defining classic from legendary ska/reggae group [[Toots and the Maytals]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/70266-major-lazer-sample-toots-and-the-maytals-on-new-song-my-number-listen/|title=Major Lazer Sample Toots and the Maytals on New Song "My Number": Listen - Pitchfork|website=Pitchfork|access-date=12 August 2017}}</ref> The song contains newly recorded vocals from frontman [[Toots Hibbert]] specifically designed for Major Lazer, changing the original lyrics to incorporate the group into the song while keeping the original melody.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WX5i81l2LTE| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211107/WX5i81l2LTE| archive-date=2021-11-07 | url-status=live|title=Major Lazer & Bad Royale - My Number|date=5 December 2016|via=YouTube |access-date=12 August 2017}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/my-number-feat.-toots-single/id1180814535?app=itunes|title="My Number - Single" by Major Lazer on iTunes|website=iTunes|access-date=12 August 2017}}</ref>

Following the success of singles "[[Cold Water (song)|Cold Water]]" and "Believer", Major Lazer released their third single titled "[[Run Up]]" 26 January 2017. The song features Canadian musician [[PartyNextDoor]] and rapper [[Nicki Minaj]], serving as a follow-up to the tropical, laid-back sound of "Cold Water". ''Music Is the Weapon'', however, still failed to arrive, prompting rumors that Major Lazer would not be releasing an album after all. However, in 2018, Diplo stated that the album will be released in 2019 and that it will be their last.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/09/diplo-says-the-next-major-lazer-album-might-be-their-last|title=Diplo Says the Next Major Lazer Album Might Be Their Last|website=Complex}}</ref>

On 1 June 2017, Major Lazer released a six-song extended play titled [[Know No Better (EP)|''Know No Better'']], with the title track featuring [[Camila Cabello]], [[Travis Scott]], and [[Quavo]]. The EP's second single, "[[Sua Cara]]", features Brazilian musicians [[Anitta (singer)|Anitta]] and [[Pabllo Vittar]]. Several remixes of the EP's title track have since been released.

On 19 October 2018, Major Lazer released a compilation album titled ''[[Major Lazer Essentials]]'', a 10-year anniversary record that includes all of Major Lazer's greatest hits, and new songs including "[[Blow That Smoke]]", featuring [[Tove Lo]], which was released 17 October 2018. 

On 20 March 2019,  Major Lazer released an extended play titled ''Africa Is the Future''.

=== 2019–present: Jillionaire's departure and ''Music Is the Weapon'' === 

On 10 May 2019, Major Lazer released the song "Can't Take It from Me", featuring [[Skip Marley]], as the new lead single for their fourth studio album.<ref>{{citation|url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2019/05/major-lazer-share-their-first-single-cant-take-it-from-me-from-final-upcoming-studio-album/|title=Major Lazer share their first single 'Can't Take It From Me' from final upcoming studio album|website=[[Dancing Astronaut]]|last=Mao|first=Jessica|date=10 May 2019|access-date=15 May 2019}}</ref> In June 2019, [[Jillionaire]] left the group to focus on solo projects, and was replaced by American DJ and record producer [[Ape Drums]].<ref name="AD">{{citation|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2046237/jillionaire-ape-drums-major-lazer/news/|title=Jillionaire Quits Major Lazer, Replaced By Ape Drums|website=[[Stereogum]]|last=DeVille|first=Chris|date=3 June 2019|access-date=30 July 2019}}</ref><ref>{{citation|url=https://djmag.com/news/jillionaire-has-left-major-lazer|title=JILLIONAIRE HAS LEFT MAJOR LAZER|website=[[DJ Mag]]|last=Coney|first=Brian|date=4 June 2019|access-date=30 July 2019}}</ref> The album's second single, "[[Que Calor]]", featuring [[J Balvin]] and [[El Alfa]], was released 11 September 2019. The album's fifth single, "[[Lay Your Head on Me]]", featuring [[Marcus Mumford]] of the band [[Mumford & Sons]], was released on 26 March 2020.

In a 2019 interview with ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex Magazine]]'', Diplo revealed that the album would be titled ''Lazerism'', but the name was later changed back to the original title, ''[[Music Is the Weapon]]'', and was released on October 23, 2020.<ref>{{citation|url=http://dancemusicnw.com/a-new-era-of-major-lazer-with-ape-drums/|title=Ape Drums ushers in a new era for Major Lazer|website=Dance Music Northwest|last=Lutz|first=Quinn|date=21 June 2019|access-date=21 September 2019}}</ref><ref name="ml4">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/MAJORLAZER/status/1311335554236076034|title=MAJOR LAZER on Twitter: "MUSIC IS THE WEAPON. OCTOBER 23 #ML4"|publisher=Twitter|date=2020-09-30|access-date=2020-09-30}}</ref>
