'''John Derek Yancey'''<ref name=about>{{cite web|title=Illa J John Yancey|url=https://www.facebook.com/illajmusic/info|work=[[Facebook.com]]|access-date=November 20, 2013}}</ref> (born October 13, 1986),<ref name=about/> better known by his stage name '''Illa J''', is a rapper, singer, producer and songwriter<ref name=about/> from Detroit, Michigan who has released two albums on [[Delicious Vinyl Records]].<ref name=about/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/illa-j-mn0001005124/biography|title=Illa J – Biography & History – AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=December 14, 2016}}</ref> He is the younger brother of the late legendary [[hip hop]] producer, and rapper [[J Dilla]], and a former member of hip hop group [[Slum Village]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_ca/read/it-takes-a-village-the-long-evolving-saga-of-detroits-slum-village-456|title=It Takes a Village: The Tragedy and Triumph of Detroit's Slum Village}}</ref> He also released a collaborative album as Yancey Boys along with [[Frank Nitt]].<ref name=about/> Illa J's second solo album ''[[ILLA J LP]]'' will come out via Brooklyn based record label Bastard Jazz.<ref>http://www.axs.com/news/interview-with-illa-j-emcee-on-his-new-music-slum-village-and-more-50440</ref>

==Early life==
Illa J grew up in a musical family. He is the younger brother of hip hop legend [[J Dilla]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://artistwiki.com/illa-j|title = Illa J Wiki, biography, pictures, Illa J songs & albums}}</ref> and is the son of Maureen Yancey, a former Opera singer, and a former [[Jazz]] bassist. John Yancey was the youngest of four children including a sister (Martha) and two brothers (Earl and James). The family lived in a house near McDougall and Nevada, on the east side of Detroit.<ref>[http://www.metrotimes.com/editorial/story.asp?id=9544 Let it roll (Metro Times Detroit)]</ref> According to Slum Village founding member T3, before getting into music Illa J's main focus was basketball.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://soundofboston.com/interview-slum-village/|title=Interview: Slum Village|date=March 9, 2015}}</ref> In the year of 2006, after the passing of his brother, he decided to drop out of [[Central Michigan University]], and continue the family's involvement with music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/ca/artist/illa-j/id286925141#fullText|title = Illa J on Apple Music}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2008, he released his debut album, ''Yancey Boys'' on [[Delicious Vinyl Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/yancey-boys-mw0000803367|title=Yancey Boys – Illa J – Songs, Reviews, Credits – AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=December 14, 2016}}</ref> It is produced entirely by previously unused beats that were made by J Dilla and were left sitting at the offices of Delicious Vinyl over several years.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.hiphopgalaxy.com/Illa-J-hip-hop-8107.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=November 7, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131219003108/http://www.hiphopgalaxy.com/Illa-J-hip-hop-8107.html |archive-date=December 19, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> [[Stones Throw Records]] released a digital instrumental version of the album in 2009.<ref>[http://stonesthrow.com/store/album/jdilla/yancey-boys-instrumentals Stones Throw Store]</ref> In the following year (2010), he quietly released a second EP entitled, ''4 Past Midnite''.<ref>http://hiphopisdream.com/illa-j-4-past-midnight/#.Un0zj1_naM8</ref> In the year 2013, he followed with the album [[Evolution (Slum Village album)|''Evolution'']] as [[Slum Village]] along with rapper T3 and producer Young RJ,<ref name=about/> and a collaborative album with [[Frank Nitt]], entitled [[Sunset Blvd (album)|''Sunset Blvd'']].<ref>http://www.urb.com/2013/11/06/carrying-on-a-legacy-yancey-boys-interview/</ref><ref name="HHDX">{{cite web|last=Baker|first=Soren|title=J Dilla Beats Featured On Yancey Boys' "Sunset Blvd" LP|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.24728/title.j-dilla-beats-featured-on-yancey-boys-sunset-blvd-lp|publisher=HipHopDX|access-date=October 28, 2013|date=July 18, 2013}}</ref> In 2015 he released ILLA J LP on Bastard Jazz Records.
