'''Michael David Rosenberg''' (born 17 May 1984), better known by his stage name '''Passenger''', is an English singer-songwriter and musician. Previously the main vocalist and songwriter of [[Passenger (British band)|Passenger]], Rosenberg opted to keep the band's name for his solo work after the band dissolved in 2009. In 2012, he released the song "[[Let Her Go]]" which topped the charts in 16 countries.<ref name="LightTouch">{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/goingoutguide/music/passengers-mike-rosenberg-tackles-dark-subjects-with-a-light-touch/2013/08/21/486bca48-0518-11e3-88d6-d5795fab4637_story.html|title=Passenger's Mike Rosenberg tackles dark subjects with a light touch|work=Washington Post|access-date=28 September 2014}}</ref> In 2014, the song was nominated for the [[Brit Award]] for [[2014 BRIT Awards#Nominees and winners|British Single of the Year]], and he received the British Academy's [[Ivor Novello Award]] for Most Performed Work.<ref name="Ivor">{{cite journal |first=Mike |last=Pakinkis |title=Ivor Novello Awards 2014: All the winners |journal=[[Music Week]] |publisher=Intent Media |location=London, England |url=http://www.musicweek.com/news/read/ivor-novello-awards-2014-all-the-winners/058529 |date=22 May 2014 |access-date=24 June 2014}}</ref>

==Early life==
Rosenberg was born on 17 May 1984<ref name="Star"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/passengermusic/status/203139368632852480|title=Twitter / passengermusic: Thank you all so much for my birthday messages xxx im having a lovely day thanks!! Manchester – you were|publisher=Twitter|date=17 May 2012|access-date=26 April 2013}}</ref> in [[Brighton]] to Quaker parents, English mother Jane and American Jewish father, Gerard Rosenberg, originally from [[Vineland, New Jersey]].<ref name="LightTouch"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://articles.philly.com/2012-09-19/entertainment/33926857_1_michael-kiwanuka-emeli-sande-ed-sheeran|title=Brits Are Here|work=Philly.com|access-date=28 September 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pro.khop-fm.tritonflex.com/common/more.php?m=58&ts=1387209628&article=E5B37011666B11E3B51EFEFDADE6840A&mode=2|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140201183026/http://pro.khop-fm.tritonflex.com/common/more.php?m=58&ts=1387209628&article=E5B37011666B11E3B51EFEFDADE6840A&mode=2|url-status=dead|archive-date=1 February 2014|title=KHOP @ 95.1 - ALL THE HITS - Passenger Says Stage Name's More "Mysterious" and "Interesting" than His Actual Name [From ABC News]|date=1 February 2014|access-date=31 March 2018}}</ref> Rosenberg learned classical guitar at a young age and at around 14–15 started to write songs.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} He did not apply himself at school in Brighton, spending his time making music.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Rosenberg left school at the age of 16 to pursue a career in the music industry and spent the next few years as a [[busker]] in England and Australia.<ref>Doyle, Patrick. "Q&A: Passenger." ''[[Rolling Stone]]''; 13 February 2014: 26. ProQuest. Web. 16 April 2014</ref>

==Music career==
===Early years===
Rosenberg did his first performance when he was 16.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Rosenberg's father, a film production worker, introduced him to former member of the band [[Faithless]], [[Jamie Catto]], in 2001; this led to Rosenberg getting a two-song spot at the Free Burma Campaign benefit gig at the Royal Court in London in 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://burmacampaign.org.uk/an-evening-of-music-and-comedy-for-burma/|title=An evening of music and comedy for Burma|work=Burma Campaign UK|access-date=11 November 2014}}</ref> On the night, Rosenberg met his future writing partner Andrew Phillips and established contact with the IE Music label.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Back in Brighton, Phillips and Rosenberg pooled their musical influences (from [[Simon & Garfunkel]] to [[DJ Shadow]]), and started to write songs at Phillips’ in-house studio in [[Hove]].{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} In 2003, they formed the Mike Rosenberg Band, engaging Marcus O’Dair (bass), Alon Cohen (drums) and Richard Brinklow (keyboards) through connections within the Brighton music scene.<ref>{{cite journal|title=Passenger|journal=Encyclopedia of Popular Music, 4th Ed.|date=2014}}</ref>

===2003–2009: Passenger===
{{Main|Passenger (British band)}}
Rosenberg founded the band Passenger with Andrew Phillips in 2003 in Brighton and Hove.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} The name of the band was stylised as '''/Passenger.''' (with a slash at the beginning and a dot at the end).{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} The five-person band's only album, ''Wicked Man's Rest'', was released in 2007, on Chalkmark.<ref>Aston, Martin. "Passenger. Wicked Man's Rest", ''[[The Times]]'', p. 24. (15 September 2007)</ref> Rosenberg wrote the majority of the album's tracks, with the exception of "Four Horses", which was written by Phillips.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} The band broke up in 2009.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}}

===2009–2010: Solo career and ''Wide Eyes Blind Love'', ''Divers and Submarines'' and ''Flight of the Crow''===
After the break-up of Passenger, Rosenberg kept the band's name as his personal stage name, and took to [[busking]] for a solo music career. In October 2009 he went to Australia, where he supported acts such as [[Lior]] and Sydneysiders Elana Stone and Brian Campeau.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} He then played at One Movement, a major music industry-focused festival in [[Perth]].{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} This earned him a following in Australia and he was selling out 500-seater venues across Australia.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} His debut solo album, ''Wide Eyes Blind Love'', was released in 2009.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} It was produced and mixed by former bandmate Andrew Phillips, who also provided backing vocals, and played guitar and other instruments, and featured vocals by Isobel Anderson.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Rosenberg also played various shows in the United Kingdom during this time,{{when|date=November 2020}} including a support slot for [[Turin Brakes]]' tenth anniversary show at [[Theatre Royal, Drury Lane]] in London.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}}

Rosenberg produced a fans-only limited release ''Divers and Submarines'', again supported by former bandmate Andrew Phillips and vocalist Isobel Anderson.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} His third studio album, ''Flight of the Crow,'' was recorded in Australia<ref name="Chronicle">. "Double date – troubadour brings travelling tales to city", ''[[Bath Chronicle]]'', p. 46. (18 August 2011)</ref> and saw him joined in the studio by various Australian independent musical talents including [[Lior]], [[Kate Miller-Heidke]], [[Boy & Bear]], [[Josh Pyke]] and [[Katie Noonan]].{{citation needed|date=November 2020}}

===2011–2013: ''All The Little Lights''===
[[Image:Passenger-1983.jpg|thumb|right|200px|Passenger in September 2013]]
{{Main|All the Little Lights}}
Rosenberg's fourth album, ''[[All the Little Lights]]'', was released in the summer of 2012 in North America on [[Nettwerk|Nettwerk Records]].<ref>{{cite web|title=All The Little Lights North America Release Date|url=http://nettskinny.com/story/20120612/passenger-joins-nettwerk-release-all-little-lights-aug-28-fall-tour-ed-sheeran-confir|access-date=28 January 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130805230544/http://nettskinny.com/story/20120612/passenger-joins-nettwerk-release-all-little-lights-aug-28-fall-tour-ed-sheeran-confir|archive-date=5 August 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> Recorded at [[Linear Recording]] in [[Sydney]], Rosenberg worked with a core Australian band that included [[Boy & Bear]] drummer Tim Hart, jazz bassist Cameron Undy, and keyboards player [[Stu Hunter]], from Katie Noonan and The Captains.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/PassengerOfficial/info |title=Passenger – About |publisher=Facebook |access-date=5 April 2013}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=November 2020}} During the summer and autumn of 2012, Rosenberg toured the UK, opening for [[Jools Holland]] and [[Ed Sheeran]], the latter of whom he had known since Sheeran was about 15 while living in [[Cambridge]].<ref name="Star"/> He joined Australian acts the John Butler Trio, and Josh Pyke with a co-headlined UK tour.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} He also opened for Ed Sheeran's 2012 North American tour<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vancouverweekly.com/who-is-mike-rosenberg/|title=''Vancouver Weekly'': Interview by Maria Perez with Passenger (Mike Roesenberg)|work=Vancouver Weekly|date=11 October 2012|access-date=28 September 2014}}</ref> and also in Paris. He also opened for Ed Sheeran on four of his five sell-out dates in Ireland in January 2013 and in Australia and New Zealand in early 2013,<ref>{{cite web|title=Ed Sheeran – UK (Belfast) and Ireland (Dublin) Tour Diary|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jd0mjsuXhOw|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> and supported Sheeran in his Brighton dates and in Reading.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ents24.com/brighton-events/brighton-centre/ed-sheeran/2796496:|title=10/19/12|work=Ents24|access-date=28 September 2014}}</ref> Rosenberg performed "All the Little Lights" at the [[Children in Need]] Rocks concert at the [[Hammersmith Apollo]] in London, broadcast on BBC One on 14 November 2013..<ref>{{cite web|title=Line up|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/ejfbj5/performances|work=Children in Need Rocks|publisher=BBC|access-date=9 November 2013}}</ref>

===2014–2015: ''Whispers'' and ''Whispers II''===
{{main|Whispers (Passenger album)|l1=Whispers|Whispers II}}
On 24 March 2014, Rosenberg unveiled "Whispers", the title track of his new album, as part of his set at the [[Teenage Cancer Trust]] charity concert at the [[Royal Albert Hall]] in London where he also performed alongside [[Ed Sheeran]].<ref>{{cite news|title=British singer/songwriters get Teenage Cancer Trust benefit shows off to a storming start at London's Royal Albert Hall|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/ed-sheeran-and-passenger-unveil-new-songs-at-teenage-cancer-trust-show__3893/?Day=&Month=&Year=2007|publisher=Official Charts Company}}</ref> On 26 March 2014 Rosenberg announced details of his fifth studio album.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} He released ''[[Whispers (Passenger album)|Whispers]]'' on 9 June 2014.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Talking to [[Digital Spy]] about the album, he said: "This is easily the most 'up' album I've ever made, it's quite cinematic. There are lots of big stories and big ideas. There are also some sombre moments about loneliness and death but hey, it wouldn't be a Passenger album without those". He released "Hearts on Fire" as the album's lead single on 14 April 2014.<ref name="DSWhispers">{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a560247/passenger-announces-new-album-whispers.html|title=Passenger announces new album Whispers – Music News -|work=Digital Spy|date=26 March 2014|access-date=28 September 2014}}</ref>

Rosenberg recorded ''[[Whispers II]]'' at the same time as recording ''Whispers''.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} ''Whispers II'' was released on 20 April 2015.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} All profits from the album go to the [[UNICEF UK]] initiative to help children in [[Liberia]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=English|first1=Joe|title=Passenger's new album release will help children in danger in Liberia|url=http://blogs.unicef.org.uk/2015/03/24/passengers-new-album-children-in-danger-in-liberia-whispers-ii/|publisher=Unicef|date=24 March 2015|access-date=27 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518105301/http://blogs.unicef.org.uk/2015/03/24/passengers-new-album-children-in-danger-in-liberia-whispers-ii/|archive-date=18 May 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===2016–2017: ''Young as the Morning, Old as the Sea'' and ''The Boy Who Cried Wolf''===
{{main|Young as the Morning, Old as the Sea|The Boy Who Cried Wolf (Passenger album)|l2=The Boy Who Cried Wolf}}
On 16 June 2016, the music video for "Somebody's Love" was uploaded to his YouTube channel, thereby announcing his seventh album, ''[[Young as the Morning, Old as the Sea]]''.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} A second track from the album, "[[Anywhere (Passenger song)|Anywhere]]" was released on 19 August 2016.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} ''Young as the Morning, Old as the Sea'' was released on 23 September 2016, and became Passenger's first UK number one album.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/passenger-pips-bruce-springsteen-to-score-his-first-number-1-album-this-is-so-unbelievably-exciting-__16499/ |title=Passenger pips Bruce Springsteen to score his first Number 1 album: "This is so unbelievably exciting!!" |publisher=Official Charts |date=30 September 2016 |access-date=27 July 2017}}</ref>

On 25 July 2017, at the end of his ''Young as the Morning, Old as the Sea'' tour, Rosenberg announced via the Passenger Facebook page that his performance on Sunday 23 July would be "my/our last gig for a while".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/PassengerOfficial/posts/10155309442278964|title=Passenger|website=www.facebook.com|access-date=31 March 2018}}</ref> The following day, he announced that his eighth album, ''[[The Boy Who Cried Wolf (Passenger album)|The Boy Who Cried Wolf]]'', would be released two days later on 28 July 2017.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} The album was announced with a livestream of the album performed live in its entirety, broadcast from Passenger's studio simultaneously to YouTube and Facebook.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} The album charted at number 5 in the Official UK Charts.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://passengermusic.com/news/new-album-boy-cried-wolf-order-now/ |title=New Album 'The Boy Who Cried Wolf' |publisher=Passengermusic.com |date=26 July 2017 |access-date=27 July 2017}}</ref>

===2018: ''Runaway''===
{{Main|Runaway (Passenger album)|l1=Runaway}}
On 18 May 2018, Passenger released the song "Hell or High Water".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zgDbp5C74sU|title= Hell or High Water |publisher=Youtube.com |date=18 May 2018 |access-date=18 May 2018}}</ref> It was recorded in a variety of different national parks across [[Utah]], [[Nevada]], [[Arizona]] and [[California]]<ref name=newalbum>{{cite web |url=https://passengermusic.com/runaway/new-album-runaway-european-tour-on-sale-now/ |title=NEW ALBUM – RUNAWAY & EUROPEAN TOUR ON SALE NOW |access-date=25 May 2018}}</ref> In the following days, he also announced an autumn European tour <ref>{{cite tweet |user=passengermusic |number=999203763335389184 |date=22 May 2018 |title=I'll be embarking on a EUROPEAN TOUR this autumn :-) if you're on the mailing list you should have been sent a password and link to preorder tickets now. If you're not on the mailing list you can sign up by using this link - https://Passenger.lnk.to/PreSaleSignUp . Can’t wait to see you all! }}</ref> and teased fans through social media with a cover for a new album.<ref>{{cite tweet |user=passengermusic |number=998926544788774912 |date=22 May 2018 |title=Runaway}}</ref>
[[File:Passenger.jpg|thumb|Passenger during his [[Runaway Tour (Passenger)|Runaway Tour]] performing [[Let Her Go]] in [[Bogota]], [[Colombia]]]]
On 25 May 2018, he formally announced his ninth studio album, ''[[Runaway (Passenger album)|Runaway]]'', and released a live acoustic version of "Hell or High Water" recorded in [[Venice, Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VLNIvEZDa8o|title= Hell or High Water (Acoustic Live)|website=YouTube |date=25 May 2018 |access-date=25 May 2018}}</ref> Passenger described ''Runaway'' as a concept album, as he realised many of the songs had a strong sense of [[Americana (music)|Americana]].{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} He also believed the songs would work well accompanied by American visuals and thus, alongside long-time collaborators Jarrad Seng, Stu Larsen and Chris Vallejo, embarked on a three-week-long road trip through the United States to film videos for each track.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Additionally, they recorded acoustic videos for each track, filmed in a variety of different locations.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Rosenberg plans to release a song every three weeks, sharing both the official and acoustic videos, plus behind-the-scenes footage.<ref name=newalbum/> ''Runaway'' was released on 31 August 2018.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}}

===2019–2020: ''Sometimes It's Something, Sometimes It's Nothing at All'', ''Patchwork'' and world tour===
On 18 March 2019, Passenger released the song "Restless Wind" on YouTube.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Two more songs, "Helplessly Lost" and "Paper Cut, Chinese Burn," were subsequently released a month later, as well as a cover of [[Fleetwood Mac]]'s "[[Landslide (Fleetwood Mac song)|Landslide]]".{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} On 2 May 2019, Passenger both announced and released his tenth album, ''[[Sometimes It's Something, Sometimes It's Nothing at All]]''.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} Unlike more recent Passenger albums, the record is arranged entirely for vocals, acoustic guitar and a string quartet.{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} All the profits from sales will be donated to Shelter, a UK-based homeless charity.<ref>{{cite web |title=Passenger - News - Sometimes It's Something, Sometimes It's Nothing at All |url=https://passengermusic.com/news/sometimes-its-something-sometimes-its-nothing-at-all-new-album/ |access-date=8 May 2019}}</ref>

At the end of January, Passenger announced a world tour including dates in the UK, Europe, Australia, North America, and New Zealand.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://passengermusic.com/news/world-tour-announcement/|title=World Tour Announcement|date=2020-01-27|website=Passenger|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-12}}</ref> On 20 March 2020, he announced and released his new single "The Way That I Love You" with a video.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://passengermusic.com/news/new-single-the-way-that-i-love-you/|title=New single - The Way That I Love You|date=2020-03-20|website=Passenger|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-20}}</ref> On 1 May 2020, he released his new single "London In The Spring".<ref>{{Cite web|title=New Single - London In The Spring|url=https://passengermusic.com/news/new-single-london-in-the-spring/|date=2020-05-01|website=Passenger|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-04}}</ref> On 10 July 2020, Passenger released his eleventh album ''[[Patchwork (Passenger album)|Patchwork]]''. The album was written and recorded during the [[COVID-19 lockdowns|coronavirus lockdown]] alongside producer Chris Vallejo and guest musicians [[Andrew Philips (musician)|Andrew Phillips]] (a collaborator of Rosenberg's and formerly of the band Passenger) and Richard Brinklow.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Passenger-Patchwork/release/15617881|title=Passenger – Patchwork (2020, 234 kbps (VBR), File)|accessdate=12 February 2021|website=Discogs.com}}</ref> The record was released as a funding project for [[The Trussell Trust]], an NGO and charity that works to end the need for food banks in the UK.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Baines|first=Huw|title=Passenger Releases New Album 'Patchwork' - Stereoboard|url=https://www.stereoboard.com/content/view/228688/9|access-date=2020-07-12|website=Stereoboard.com|language=en}}</ref>

=== 2020–2021: ''Songs for the Drunk and Broken Hearted'' ===
On 8 January 2021, Passenger released ''[[Songs for the Drunk and Broken Hearted]]''. The record was supposed to be released by early 2020, but later delayed due to the coronavirus lockdown. The record's release supports both Ecologi and [[Eden Reforestation Projects]], non-profit organisations aiming to rebuild natural landscapes destroyed by [[deforestation]]. A tree will be planted for every physical copy of the album sold via the Passenger store.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-30|title=Interview: Passenger on getting drunk, being vulnerable and playing a sad clown|url=https://www.theaureview.com/music/interview-passenger-on-getting-drunk-being-vulnerable-and-playing-a-sad-clown/|access-date=2021-01-08|website=The AU Review|language=en-AU}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Rosenberg currently lives in Brighton.<ref name="Star">{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2012/12/07/passenger_aka_mike_rosenberg_grateful_for_ed_sheerans_support.html|title=Passenger, a.k.a. Mike Rosenberg, grateful for Ed Sheeran's support|work=thestar.com|date=7 December 2012|access-date=28 September 2014}}</ref> He is a fan of English football club [[Arsenal F.C.]] In a 2021 interview, he said he became an Arsenal supporter despite being from Brighton after watching Arsenal beating [[Sheffield Wednesday F.C.]] in the [[FA Cup Final]] in 1993.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-07|title=Let Her Go... again: interview with Mike Rosenberg, aka Passenger|url=https://the-shortlisted.co.uk/passenger-interview-mike-rosenberg/|access-date=2021-07-22|website=The Shortlisted|language=en-GB}}</ref> In May 2015, Rosenberg appeared at Arsenal's 'A Night to Inspire' event and played a version of the '49 Undefeated' fan chant.<ref name="Soundcloud">{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/officialarsenal/passengers-version-of-49-undefeated?in=officialarsenal%2Fsets%2Fplayer-exclusives|title=Passenger's version of '49 Undefeated'!|work=soundcloud.com|access-date=22 May 2015}}</ref>
