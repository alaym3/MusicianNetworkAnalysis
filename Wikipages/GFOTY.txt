'''Polly-Louisa Salmon''' (born 19 July 1990), better known by her stage name '''GFOTY''', an abbreviation of '''Girlfriend of the Year''', is a British [[pop music|pop]] singer. From 2013 to 2018, she released music through the [[PC Music]] label. She is currently signed to Pretty Wavvy.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Pretty Wavvy - Roster|url=https://www.prettywavvy.com/artists|access-date=|website=}}</ref> Much of her work has been in the form of mini-mixes.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.factmag.com/2015/01/27/pc-music-regular-gfoty-shares-cake-mix-featuring-an-apropos-blink-182-cover/ |title=PC Music regular GFOTY shares Cake Mix, featuring an apropos Blink-182 cover |date=27 January 2015 |website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref>

==Biography==
GFOTY is the daughter of art dealer [[Jeff Salmon]].<ref name="cliff-fader">{{cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/06/15/popping-off-gfoty-parody-lotic |title=PC Music And The Limitations Of Parody |last=Cliff |first=Aimee |date=15 June 2015 |website=[[The Fader]] |access-date=17 June 2015}}</ref> She grew up in [[London]], England and ran a blog.<ref name="id-weinstock"/><ref name="vice-bassil">{{cite web |url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/blog/the-weird-and-wonderful-world-of-pc-music |title=Trying to Make Sense of Hannah Diamond and Post-Ringtone Music |last=Bassil |first=Ryan |date= |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |access-date=28 February 2015}}</ref> Her earliest songs include "Take a Picture", which includes rap group Serious Thugs, and "Friday Night", which is a darkly-comical take on pop clichés about partying teenagers.<ref name="spin-weiss">{{cite web |url=https://www.spin.com/2014/12/pc-music-sophie-qt-ag-cook-trend-of-the-year-best-of-2014/ |title=Trend of the Year: How PC Music Chewed Up Pop Conventions |last=Weiss |first=Dan |date=17 December 2014 |website=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref><ref name="cmu-barchi">{{cite web |url=http://www.completemusicupdate.com/article/cmu-artists-of-the-year-2014-pc-music/ |title=CMU Artists Of The Year 2014: PC Music |last=Barchi |first=Aly |date=12 December 2014 |website=[[Complete Music Update]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> A remix of the latter has since been incorporated into producer [[Sophie (musician)|Sophie]]'s sets.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dummymag.com/photos/simple-things-festival-2014-review-and-photos |title=Festival review: Simple Things 2014 |last=Mitchell |first=Aurora |date=29 October 2014 |website=Dummy |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref>

Her debut single, "Bobby", was released in 2013 via the [[PC Music]] record label and collective. It describes feelings of futility after a breakup.<ref name="vice-bassil"/><ref name="fader-kretowicz">{{cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/06/26/sophie-kyary-pamyu-pamyu-pc-music-cute-music |title=You're Too Cute: Kyary Pamyu Pamyu, Sophie, PC Music and the Aesthetic of Excess |last=Kretowicz |first=Steph |date=26 June 2014 |website=[[The Fader]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> GFOTY uses a spoken word delivery over a tense, synthesised wall of sound.<ref name="fader-kretowicz"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/staff-picks-and-good-shit-for-the-week-of-september-19 |title=Staff Picks and Good Shit for the Week of September 19, Featuring Dylan Walker of Full of Hell |last=Millard |first=Drew |date=19 September 2014 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |access-date=28 February 2015}}</ref> She released ''Secret Mix'' in March 2014. It includes cover versions of songs by [[Celine Dion]], [[Toni Braxton]], and [[Carly Simon]]. [[Tiny Mix Tapes]] named ''Secret Mix'' the third best music release of 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tinymixtapes.com/features/2014-favorite-50-music-releases-of-2014?page=4 |title=2014: Favorite 50 Music Releases of 2014 |last=Zetina |first=Ariel |date=December 2014 |publisher=[[Tiny Mix Tapes]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> She also appeared on [[PC Music]]'s mix for ''[[DIS Magazine]]''{{'s}} ''DISown''. Focusing on the relationship between art and commerce, her contribution includes conspicuous product placement for [[Red Bull]].<ref name="fader-kretowicz"/> One of the songs from her mix became her second single "Don't Wanna / Let's Do It".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.stereogum.com/1691514/gfoty-dont-wanna-lets-do-it/ |title=GFOTY – 'Don't Wanna / Let's Do It' |last=Bowe |first=Miles |date=9 July 2014 |publisher=[[Stereogum]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> It creates an uneasy mood by taking two conflicting phrases and frantically sampling them.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/07/09/gfoty-battles-indecision-on-dont-wanna-lets-do-it |title=GFOTY Battles Indecision on 'Don't Wanna / Let's Do It' |last=Joyce |first=Colin |date=9 July 2014 |website=[[The Fader]] |access-date=28 February 2015}}</ref>

[[File:Ffb6c1.png|thumb|right|150px|GFOTY's "My Song" was inspired by the [[hex triplet]] FFB6C1.<ref name="pitchfork-joyce"/>]]
GFOTY contributed vocals to "Hard", the B-side to Sophie's "Lemonade" single.<ref name="mass-appeal-liu">{{cite web |url=http://massappeal.com/maxo-not-that-bad-ft-gfoty/ |title=Maxo 'Not That Bad' ft. GFOTY |last=Liu |first=Nelson |date=2 October 2014 |website=[[Mass Appeal (magazine)|Mass Appeal]] |access-date=27 February 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303234511/http://massappeal.com/maxo-not-that-bad-ft-gfoty/ |archive-date=3 March 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In September 2014, producer [[Ryan Hemsworth]] released the compilation EP ''shh#ffb6c1'' on his pseudo-label Secret Songs, which included a GFOTY-featured track named "My Song".<ref name="pitchfork-joyce">{{cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/17137-gfoty-my-song/ |title=GFOTY: 'My Song' |last=Joyce |first=Colin |date=30 September 2014 |publisher=[[Pitchfork Media]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> Shortly after, she collaborated with [[PC Music]] artist Maxo on "Not That Bad".<ref name="mass-appeal-liu"/>

For Halloween in 2014, GFOTY appeared alongside fellow label artists including [[A. G. Cook]] and [[Danny L Harle]] for a single, special live Halloween webcast named ''Dead or Alive''. All of the [[PC Music]] artists appeared in the webcast, portraying spooky alter egos of themselves. GFOTY's persona was "Ghoulfriend of the Year". The show was broadcast in the UK, USA, Denmark and Japan.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tinymixtapes.com/news/pc-music-wants-you-to-spend-your-halloween-alone-with-them |title=PC Music Wants You To Spend Your Halloween Alone With Them |author=Dream Baby |date=27 October 2014 |publisher=[[Tiny Mix Tapes]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref>

In 2015 GFOTY released a mix titled ''Cake Mix''. It is composed of bright, saccharine songs interspersed with buzzing, glitchy breakdowns.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/01/27/all-the-gfoty-things |title=Download GFOTY's Cover Of Blink-182's 'All The Small Things' |last=McDermott |first=Patrick D. |date=27 January 2015 |website=[[The Fader]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.stereogum.com/1732111/gfoty-all-the-small-things-blink-182-cover/ |title=GFOTY – 'All The Small Things' (Blink-182 Cover) |last=Rettig |first=James |date=27 January 2015 |publisher=[[Stereogum]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> The mix includes an adaptation of [[Peter Andre]]'s 1996 single "[[Mysterious Girl]]" featuring [[Bubbler Ranx]] and a cover of [[Blink-182]]'s 2000 single "[[All the Small Things]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dummymag.com/new-music/gfoty-cake-mix-stream-mixtape-pc-music |title=GFOTY drops 'Cake Mix' |date=27 January 2015 |website=Dummy |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> In March 2015,  a mix titled ''Dog Food Mix 1'' with [[PC Music]] artist [[Spinee]] was released, as a reworking of GFOTY's earlier songs.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.spin.com/2015/03/pc-music-gfoty-spinee-dog-food-mix-1-stream/ |title=PC Music's GFOTY and Spinee Unleash Their 'Dog Food Mix 1' |last=Brodsky |first=Rachel |date=5 March 2015 |website=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |access-date=5 March 2015}}</ref> A second mix, ''Dog Food Mix 2'', was released in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.spin.com/2015/05/pc-music-gfoty-spinee-dog-food-mix-2-stream-london-show/ |title=GFOTY and Spinee Get Pointedly Weird on Their 'Dog Food Mix 2' |last=Carley |first=Brennan |date=26 May 2015 |website=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |access-date=26 May 2015}}</ref>

March 2015 saw GFOTY head to the USA to play the [[PC Music]] showcase at the Empire Garage in Austin, Texas as part of [[SXSW]]. Her performance was well received, with [[Exclaim|''Exclaim!'']] writing that it was a "fascinating set"<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/gfoty-empire_garage_austin_tx_march_19|title=GFOTY Empire Garage, Austin TX, March 19|website=exclaim.ca}}</ref>  and [[Noisey]] saying "Here's what I did when I saw GFOTY, which was probably the best thing I've seen all week: I fucking lost my mind. I loved it. I was ecstatic."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/rgpgej/pc-music-sxsw-showcase-review-gfoty-hannah-diamond-qt-ag-cook|title=Just Let Go and Enjoy PC Music, OK?|first=Kyle|last=Kramer|date=20 March 2015}}</ref> On 8 May 2015, GFOTY performed as part of a [[PC Music]] show at [[BRIC House]] in Brooklyn, New York as part of the [[Red Bull Music Academy]] Festival. The show was billed as the premiere of Pop Cube, "a multimedia reality network".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/05/11/arts/music/review-pc-music-and-sophie-in-a-high-concept-extravaganza-at-bric-house.html|title=Review: PC Music and Sophie in a High-Concept Extravaganza at BRIC House|first=Jon|last=Pareles|date=10 May 2015|website=Nytimes.com}}</ref>

On 25 October 2016, GFOTY released a [[glitch pop]]-influenced [[pop rock]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1907361/stream-gfoty-call-him-a-doctor/music/album-stream/|title=Stream GFOTY Call Him A Doctor|last=Rettig|first=James|date=25 October 2016|website=[[Stereogum]]|publisher=SpinMedia|access-date=20 November 2016}}</ref> EP titled ''Call Him A Doctor'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/pcmus/chad|title=GFOTY - Call Him A Doctor (pc-r7)|website=Soundcloud.com}}</ref> which is presumed to be a continuation of her previous EP, ''VIPOTY''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/pcmus/status/790659153610039296|title=What ever happened to Chad?|first=P. C.|last=Music|date=24 October 2016}}</ref>

On 9 November 2017, the single "Tongue" was released with an accompanying joint music video for "Tongue" and the ''VIPOTY'' track "Poison".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1971109/gfoty-poisontongue-video/video/|title=GFOTY – "Poison/Tongue" Video|date=9 November 2017}}</ref> "Tongue" is a single from her "greatest hits" compilation called ''GFOTYBUCKS'' - which contains 19 tracks from ''VIPOTY'', ''Call Him A Doctor'', past mixes, and various singles - was released on 17 November 2017. On the same date, a "32 page magazine created by GFOTY with exclusive GFOTYBUCKS recipes, lyrics and artwork by various GFOTY approved artists" is available to buy as a bundle with the ''GFOTYBUCKS'' CD.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://merch.pcmusic.info/index.php/pc-music/gfotybucks-lyric-recipe-magazine-and-cd|title=GFOTYBUCKS Zine + CD - PC Music (HomePage)|date=10 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171110005421/http://merch.pcmusic.info/index.php/pc-music/gfotybucks-lyric-recipe-magazine-and-cd|accessdate=21 July 2020|archive-date=10 November 2017}}</ref>

On 14 December 2018, GFOTY confirmed that she had left the [[PC Music]] label to become an indie artist. She released her single "Boy Next Door" under her own label "GIRLFRIEND RECORDS."<ref>{{Citation|title=Boy Next Door - Single by GFOTY|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/boy-next-door-single/1445625847|language=en-us|access-date=2019-02-27}}</ref> Via Twitter,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/GFOTY/status/1091060997685723138|title=If you thought BOY NEXT DOOR was a banger.. just you wait for my Whole Entire EP #ifyouthinkimabitchyoushouldmeetGFOTY !!!|last=GFOTY|date=2019-01-31|website=Twitter.com|language=en|access-date=2019-02-26}}</ref> she announced that she will be releasing her new EP ''If You Think I'm A Bitch You Should Meet GFOTY'' in March 2019.

GFOTY's music favours tense, bouncy beats with pounding drums.<ref name="pitchfork-sherburne">{{cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/485-pc-music/ |title=PC Music's Twisted Electronic Pop: A User's Manual |last=Sherburne |first=Philip |date=17 September 2014 |publisher=[[Pitchfork Media]] |access-date=28 February 2015}}</ref> Her style of sing-speaking gives her a more coarse, human quality compared to the rest of the PC Music roster.<ref name="stereogum-bowe">{{cite web |url=https://www.stereogum.com/1709363/maxo-not-that-bad-feat-gfoty/ |title=Maxo – 'Not That Bad' (Feat. GFOTY) |last=Bowe |first=Miles |date=2 October 2014 |publisher=[[Stereogum]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> Describing her singing as "child like", GFOTY often manipulates her words by twisting an individual syllable.<ref name="id-weinstock"/><ref name="stereogum-bowe"/> Her spoken word vocals are supported by distorted, high-pitched backing vocals.<ref name="diy-hunt"/><ref name="stereogum-bowe"/> GFOTY's more sentimental songs are offshoots of bittersweet [[power ballad]]s by singers like [[Celine Dion]] and [[Toni Braxton]].<ref name="fader-kretowicz"/> She has cited [[R. Kelly]] as an influence, calling him "either the most clever guy in the universe or a complete moron, and I love that I can't figure it out."<ref name="id-weinstock"/><ref name="fader-kretowicz"/>

===Public image===
GFOTY has been characterised as "the most obviously political" act on the PC Music label.<ref name="spin-weiss"/> Her persona is animated and loudmouthed,<ref name="cmu-barchi"/><ref name="fader-raise">{{cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/02/03/gfoty-how-i-live |title=How I Live: GFOTY |last=Raiss |first=Liz |date=3 February 2015 |website=[[The Fader]] |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> as a lampoon of [[club culture]].<ref name="vice-kramer">{{cite web |url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/blog/pc-music-sxsw-showcase-review-gfoty-hannah-diamond-qt-ag-cook |title=Just Let Go and Enjoy PC Music, OK? |last=Kramer |first=Kyle |date=20 March 2015 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |access-date=20 March 2015}}</ref> GFOTY's comments regarding race have been criticised, with [[Noisey]] removing some of her remarks from its site.<ref name="cliff-fader"/> She has likened herself to a female version of ''[[The Game: Penetrating the Secret Society of Pickup Artists]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/may/02/pc-music-dance-music-collective |title=PC Music: the future of pop or 'contemptuous parody'? |last=Wolfson |first=Sam |date=2 May 2015 |website=[[The Guardian]] |access-date=2 May 2015}}</ref> GFOTY's lyrics detail nights of drinking and casual sex.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/blog/pc-music-conceptual-art-project-end-of-year-2014 |title=Even If They're an Elaborate Joke, PC Music Dominated 2014 |last=Bassil |first=Ryan |date=16 December 2014 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |access-date=3 March 2015}}</ref> Sporting a sweatsuit for her live performances, she describes her fashion as "a pink onesie covered in [[Swarovski]] crystals with loads of money coming out the pockets and [[Ugg boots]]".<ref name="fader-kretowicz"/><ref name="vice-kramer"/> GFOTY uses an exaggeratedly feminine style similar to the cute aesthetic of her contemporaries. However, this is often used to conceal a more sinister element, and she has commented that she doesn't "particularly think gfoty {{sic}} is cute".<ref name="fader-kretowicz"/>

With low-quality [[selfie]]s, photo manipulation, and the use of wigs, GFOTY shrouds her image and identity.<ref>{{cite journal |last=Jones |first=Charlie Robin |url=http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/21588/1/pc-musics-digital-dreams |date=11 September 2014 |title=PC Music's digital dreams |journal=[[Dazed & Confused (magazine)|Dazed & Confused]] |volume=4 |pages=178–183 |access-date=28 February 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vogue.com/1539827/what-is-pc-music-sophie-qt-ag-book/ |title=A Visual Primer on PC Music, London's Weirdest New Subculture |last=Frank |first=Alex |date=19 September 2014 |website=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]] |accessdate=28 February 2015}}</ref> Her facetious interviews with press often blur the line between her real life and the GFOTY character.<ref name="fader-raise"/> She characterised GFOTY as "a state of mind which can only be achieved by the deepest level of meditation on a beach in Barbados surrounded by cute jetski instructors."<ref name="vice-bassil"/> GFOTY often discusses having trained as a chef and baking desserts.<ref name="id-weinstock"/><ref name="fader-raise"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://blog.logomagazine.com/post/98066949560/gfoty-ryan-hemsworths-shhsecret-date |title=GFOTY & Ryan Hemsworth's ShhSecret date |last=Hemsworth |first=Ryan |authorlink=Ryan Hemsworth |date=22 September 2014 |website=Logo Magazine |access-date=27 February 2015}}</ref> She maintains an [[Instagram]] profile of inspirational quotes.<ref name="pitchfork-sherburne"/>
