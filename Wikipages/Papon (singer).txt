'''Angaraag Mahanta''' (born 24 November 1975), known by his [[stagename]] Papon, is an [[Indian people|Indian]] [[playback singer]] and composer from [[Assam]]. Papon has also sung in many languages apart from [[Assamese language|Assamese]] like [[Hindi]], [[Bengali language|Bengali]], [[Tamil language|Tamil]] and [[Marathi language|Marathi]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Sarmah |first=Chandan |date=29 January 2010 |title=Tunes with a rare freshness |url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1100129/jsp/northeast/story_12041480.jsp |newspaper=The Telegraph |location=Calcutta, India |access-date=22 November 2011}}</ref> He is the lead singer and founder of the folk-fusion band Papon and The East India Company.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sentinelassam.com/north-east-india-news/assam-news/popular-assam-singer-angarag-papon-mahanta-turned-45-today-513091|title=Popular Assam singer Angarag 'Papon' Mahanta turned 45 today - Sentinelassam|first=Sentinel Digital|last=Desk|date=24 November 2020|website=www.sentinelassam.com}}</ref>

==Career==
Papon's initial training was in [[Indian classical music|Indian Classical]] and [[Indian folk music|folk music]]. He learnt vocals primarily but also plays the [[guitar]] and [[Pump organ|harmonium]]. His music covers genres such as [[Ambient music|ambient electronic]], [[acoustic folk]] and Indian classical music and it has been identified as having traces of [[ghazals]].{{citation needed|date=May 2021}}

Papon started his musical career in 1998 with the Assamese song "Nasaba Sokule" from the album ''Snigdha Junak''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Boruah|first=Pallab Saikia, Kulendra Bora, Tinkumoni|title=Assamese Songs with Lyrics : Collection of Old and New Assamese Songs|url=https://geetsankalan.com/lyrics/Nasaba_Sokule_Najaba_Kaxole|access-date=2021-09-23|website=geetsankalan.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=NASABA SOKULE {{!}} ZUBEEN GARG {{!}} ANGARAG MAHANTA {{!}} ASSAMESE LYRICAL VIDEO SONG {{!}} SNIGDHA JUNAK|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8suZO_DfxAc|language=en|access-date=2021-09-23}}</ref> In 2004 he released his first album, ''Junaki Raati''

The second album he produced was Sinaaki Osinaaki, which was released in 2009. His first folk music album was Rangphul a Bihu album, after which he produced Gomseng and subsequently, Phagunor Gaan an album representing the typical holi songs of Barpeta in West Assam. He sang on a devotional Borgeet album called Chandamukhi and is working on a traditional folk album of Tokari songs.{{citation needed|date=December 2020}} 

Papon made his debut in Bollywood with the song "Om mantra" from the film ''Strings - bound by faith'' in 2006.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chakrabarty|first=Shreoshree|date=2020-12-24|title=Papon voices the latest single of Saregama featuring Erica and Rohman|url=https://www.mediainfoline.com/music/papon-voices-the-latest-single-of-saregama-featuring-erica-and-rohman|access-date=2021-09-23|website=Media Infoline|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=[EXCLUSIVE] Papon on recreation of old classic songs: You want to have your own version as a cover|url=https://www.timesnownews.com/entertainment-news/article/exclusive-papon-on-recreation-of-old-classic-songs-you-want-to-have-your-own-version-as-a-cover/675330|access-date=2021-09-23|website=www.timesnownews.com|language=en}}</ref> In 2011, his song was "Jiyein Kyun" from the movie ''[[Dum Maro Dum (film)|Dum Maro Dum]]'' which was his first big hit''.'' In 2014, he sang "Lakeerein", which is a poem by [[Gulzar]], for the film ''[[Kya Dilli Kya Lahore]]'' and "Sun RI Bavli" for [[Nagesh Kukunoor]]'s film ''[[Lakshmi (2014 film)|Lakshmi]]''. In 2015, he sang "Moh moh ke dhage" for the film ''[[Dum Laga Ke Haisha]]'' and "Humnava" in ''[[Hamari Adhuri Kahani]]'' (2015). In 2016, he sang "Bulleya" for the film ''[[Sultan (2016 film)|Sultan]].'' In August 2016, [[Red Bull]] released "Hometown Heroes," a seven-part documentary web series that revisits the story of Papon's youth and rising career.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.firstpost.com/living/papons-hometown-heroes-story-of-singer-who-revived-folk-music-is-now-a-web-series-2937312.html|title=Papon's Hometown Heroes: Story of singer who revived folk music is now a web series - Living News , Firstpost|date=13 August 2016|website=Firstpost}}</ref><ref>{{cite AV media |title=Papon : Red Bull Hometown Heroes : Episode 1 - Where It All Began |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eGsY_OxTCgY |date=31 October 2016 |medium=YouTube video |access-date=5 May 2021}}</ref> Papon's 2018 song "Baba Bolta Hain Bas Ho Gaya", from the movie ''[[Sanju]]'', comments on differences between the real Sanju and the "reel" Sanju.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/news/sanju-new-song-baba-bolta-hain-bas-ho-gaya-is-a-funny-take-on-how-celebs-feel-about-gossip/articleshow/64816069.cms|title='Sanju' new song: 'Baba Bolta Hain Bas Ho Gaya' is a funny take on how celebs feel about gossip - Times of India|work=The Times of India|access-date=2018-07-06}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ndtv.com/entertainment/sanju-song-baba-bolta-hain-bas-ho-gaya-a-treat-to-ranbir-kapoor-and-sanjay-dutt-fans-1876053|title=Sanju Song Baba Bolta Hain Bas Ho Gaya: A Treat To Ranbir Kapoor And Sanjay Dutt Fans|work=NDTV.com|access-date=2018-07-06}}</ref>

===Television===
In 2013, Coke Studio @ MTV and for his episode on this season of the show, Papon produced 6 songs – 3 brand new compositions and 3 cross-border folk songs. In the first season of Coke Studio @ MTV, Papon sang the upbeat song "Pak Pak (Bihu Naam)", produced by Leslie Lewis, and in its second season, he produced his own track, the Assamese folk song "Tokari" and also featured as a vocalist on celebrated musician-composer Nitin Sawhney's episode singing the beautiful, folk song "Sahil Tak".{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}}

Papon's work on this season of Coke Studio @ MTV has been critically lauded of the 3 new compositions, "Tauba", with Benny Dayal, while the Papon solo "Khumaar", "Benaam Khwahishein" has received good response from the people. Papon also reinvented 3 folk songs for his episode of Coke Studio @ MTV. "Baisara Beera", "Dinae Dinae" and "Jhumor".{{Citation needed|date=December 2020}}

In 2013, Papon was also responsible for the theme song for [[MTV Roadies (season 10)|MTV Roadies X]]: Battle for Glory – "Jajabor" (Jajabor being an Assemese word for yayavar - "traveler").<ref>{{Cite news|date=2013-02-02|title=Roadies X: Papon composes new theme song– Jajabor|newspaper=[[The Hindustan Times]]|url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/tv/roadies-x-papon-composes-new-theme-song-jajabor/story-cbQUSLGPfRijqaBkWCGGBM.html|access-date=9 December 2016}}</ref>

In 2017, he was one of the judge in [[The Voice India Kids]].

==Band==
[[File:Papon in NIT Silchar.jpg|thumb|Papon performing in Incandescence in [[NIT Silchar]], 10 February 2014]]
In 2007, Papon formed an electronic folk-fusion band called Papon and The East India Company. The band consists of Papon on vocals, Brin Desai and Hameem Kader on live electronics, Jinti on lead guitars, Kirti on percussion and Dipu on bass and Tanmay on Drums<ref>{{cite web |url=http://nh7.in/paponandtheeastindiacompany/ |title=Papon & The East India Company |website=nh7 |access-date=23 November 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://goodtimes.ndtv.com/News_Details.aspx?SubcatDetails=News,526 |title=Almost Famous |website=[[NDTV Good Times]] |access-date=23 November 2011}}</ref> The six members came from backgrounds varying from Assamese folk to metal and blues to [[trip hop]].

The band has performed twice at the SAARC Music Band Festival in New Delhi, the Eastwind Festival, and the Kovalam Literary Festival and has performed overseas with Sutasi in [[Singapore]]. The band took a two-year break in the wait for a solo album which was released internationally at the end of 2011.<ref name="Papon goes national" /> The band is back in action in November with 4 big festivals in the same month. They also played at the October Fest in [[Bangalore]], NH7 Weekender fest in [[Pune]] and the Rock for Rights concert in [[New Delhi]].<ref>{{cite news |date=21 November 2011 |title=Jamming for Rights |url=http://epaper.hindustantimes.com/PUBLICATIONS/HT/HD/2011/11/21/ArticleHtmls/JAMMING-FOR-RIGHTS-21112011108004.shtml?Mode=1 |work=[[Hindustan Times]] |access-date=23 November 2011}}{{dead link|date=August 2021|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>

Earlier their track "OMO" was taken on by Saregama HMV for release on the compilation album, ''The Underground'' along with a song called "Jiya Baittho Jai" in an album called ''Sahibol''. In 2008, their track called "Khuda Hota" was released by Dada Music an extension of Music Today.{{Citation needed|date=March 2018}}

In 2015, Papon and The East India Company performed at Oasis in [[Birla Institute of Technology and Science|BITS Pilani Pilani Campus]], Dhaka International Folk Fest and in 2016, he performed at [[Spree]] in [[BITS Pilani Goa Campus]]. {{citation needed|date=September 2016}}{{Citation needed|date=March 2018}}

==Personal life and education==
Papon was born in Guwahati, Assam to the [[Assamese music|Assamese folk singer]]s [[Khagen Mahanta]] and Archana Mahanta. He has spent most of his childhood in Assam.<ref>{{cite press release|url=http://www.afaqs.com/news/company_briefs/index.html?id=50421_Watch+out+for+the+multi-talented+Papon+on+Coke+Studio@MTV|title=Watch out for Papon on Coke Studio|date=6 July 2011|work=afaqs!|agency=Avian Media|access-date=23 November 2011}}</ref> His father, Khagen Mahanta, died on 12 June 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last=Staff|date=13 June 2014|title=Legendary Assamese Singer Khagen Mahanta dies at 72|newspaper=Times of Assam|url=http://www.timesofassam.com/headlines/legendary-assamese-singer-khagen-mahanta-dies-72/|access-date=1 June 2015}}</ref>

He now lives in [[Mumbai]] with his wife Shweta Mishra Mahanta. They have two children.

He initially went there for Architecture but late enrolled himself to a degree in English Literature in Delhi University. His spent some time in Ramjas College before he shifted to Moti Lal Nehru College. He eventually dropped out on the 3rd year of college.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-10-09|title=Papon: In Motilal Nehru, I would sit with a guitar and girls would flock around me|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/bollywood/papon-in-motilal-nehru-i-would-sit-with-a-guitar-and-girls-would-flock-around-me/story-8XWsUiYJogCq5iPJNHkdBJ.html|access-date=2020-11-19|website=Hindustan Times|language=en}}</ref>

==Collaborations==
Papon has performed with the [[MIDIval Punditz]] and [[Karsh Kale]] at various festivals around the world including the [[Paléo Festival|Paléo Music Festival]] in [[Switzerland]], [[Jakarta International Java Jazz Festival]] in [[Jakarta]], [[Mosaic Music Festival]] in Singapore and [[Oslo#Music and events|Oslo World Music Festival]] in Norway. Aside from performing with them, Papon has co-written a couple of songs with the MIDIval Punditz that featured on their third album ''Hello Hello'' which ranked among the top three songs on [[Amazon.com|Amazon]], worldwide. He collaborated with the Welling Jazz Band, in [[Wellington]], [[New Zealand]], for a project playing some of his music during the Crossings festival held in Wellington, New Zealand in 2007. Also in the project was [[Rhian Sheehan]], a popular electronic artist and producer from Wellington. He also wrote a song with Karsh Kale for his new album ''Cinema''. He is working on two international collaborations which were to be released in 2012.{{citation needed|date=April 2015}}

Papon has featured in albums which carry the labels of MDR Records, in a collaboration with Times Music, and has also featured in the soundtrack of [[Bollywood]] films ''[[Dum Maaro Dum (film)|Dum Maaro Dum]]'', ''[[I Am Kalam]]'', ''[[Soundtrack (film)|Soundtrack]]'', ''[[Patang]]'', and most recently ''Barfi!''. He also featured on the Indian leg of the show [[Coke Studio (India)|Coke Studio @ MTV]]. He performed on the show [[Global Indian Film Awards]] with MIDIval Punditz, [[Kailash Kher]] and [[Shubha Mudgal]]. He also appeared on [[STAR World India|Star world India]], Episode six of [[The Dewarists]] with [[Rabbi Shergill]] and recorded a song "Khule Da Rabb". Papon has also worked with Shantanu Moitra in the 4th season of The Dewarists for 'O Megh' which is inspired by Rabindranath Tagore's poem of same name.<ref name="Papon goes national">{{cite news |last=Sinha |first=Dipanjan |date=18 November 2011 |title=Papon goes national on musical movement |url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1111118/jsp/northeast/story_14764470.jsp |work=The Telegraph |location=Calcutta, India |access-date=23 November 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R61PVcZxGMw |title=O Megh - Music Video ft.
