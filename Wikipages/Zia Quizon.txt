'''Zia Denise Marie Padilla Quizon''' (born September 23, 1991), known by her stage name '''Zia Quizon''', is a [[Filipino people|Filipino]] singer-songwriter and recording artist. She began her career after being signed to [[PolyEast Records]] and releasing her self-titled debut album in 2011.

==Biography==
Zia Denise Marie Padilla Quizon was born in September 23, 1991. She is the daughter of actor and comedian [[Dolphy Quizon]] and actress and singer [[Zsa Zsa Padilla]]. She was raised with her older [[half-sister]] [[Karylle]] and younger sister, Nicole.<ref>{{cite news|title=How Nicole came into Dolphy's life|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/07/17/12/how-nicole-came-dolphys-life|date=July 17, 2012|work=ABS-CBN News|access-date=March 5, 2013}}</ref> She has seventeen half-siblings through her father's previous relationships.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zia Quizon|url=http://asap.abs-cbn.com/artists/ZIA|work=[[ASAP (variety show)|ASAP]]|access-date=October 4, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Belen|first=W.M.|title=Zia Quizon–another beauty and brains enters showbiz|url=http://www.manilatimes.net/index.php/life-and-times/showtime/7868-zia-quizonanother-beauty-and-brains-enters-showbiz|work=The Manila Times|access-date=October 4, 2012|date=September 23, 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
Quizon made her debut in October 2011 with an original song called "Ako Na Lang", which was written and produced by Jungee Marcelo. It was the first single from her self-titled debut album. The song was received well commercially and its music video peaked at No. 1 on [[Myx|Myx chart]]. She then released a second single called "Dear Lonely", written by Kiko Salazar.<ref>http://www.ziaquizon.com/</ref> Her self-titled debut album, ''Zia'', consists of six tracks with three being [[Original Pilipino Music|original composition]] and the other being covers. She later received many awards for the work from the album including a "Favorite New Artist" award from Myx and a Gold Record Award from [[ASAP (variety show)|ASAP]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Awards|url=http://ziaquizon.com/highlights/|date=December 28, 2012|access-date=March 10, 2013}}</ref>

On July 7, 2013, she announced that her second studio album be released and will contain mainly original composition than her previous album.<ref>{{cite news|title=Zia unbound|url=http://lifestyle.inquirer.net/112279/zia-unbound|date=July 7, 2013|work=Philippine Daily Inquirer|access-date=July 26, 2013}}</ref> The first single from the album premiered on July 8 at [[My Only Radio]] FM radio station titled, "[[Bakit Hindi Ka Crush Ng Crush Mo?]]", the official theme song for the film of the same name. The song was written by Jungee Marcelo who also wrote her debut single.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zia Quizon's new single #BakitHindikaCrushngCrushMo on Monday sa MOR 101.9!|url=https://twitter.com/ziniansofficial/statuses/353355301296160772|date=July 6, 2013|publisher=Zinians Official Twitter page|access-date=July 26, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Kim, Xian in 'Bakit Hindi Ka Crush ng Crush Mo?' trailer|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/07/12/13/kim-xian-bakit-hindi-ka-crush-ng-crush-mo-trailer|date=July 12, 2013|work=ABS-CBN News|access-date=July 23, 2013}}</ref> The music video for the song was released on ABSstarcinema's YouTube channel on July 23. The video's plot features Quizon at a photo shoot session while being pursued by a man who works on set, played by [[Ramon Bautista]] who is also the author of the book which the film was based on.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bakit Hindi Ka Crush Ng Crush Mo by Zia Quizon|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D_rUR454dRE|date=July 23, 2013|publisher=ABSstarcinema's YouTube channel|access-date=July 24, 2013}}</ref> The title of her second studio album was later announced to be "''A Little Bit Of Lovin'''" and was released digitally on July 18.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hey guys the carrier single of @ziaquizon's A LITTLE OF LOVIN' will be out anytime this week.
