'''Noah Oliver Smith''', known professionally as '''Yeat''', is an American rapper from [[Portland, Oregon]]. He rose to popularity in 2021 after the release of his song "Sorry Bout That" and the subsequent release of "Gët Busy." Following those releases, he received co-signs from musicians such as [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]] and [[Lil Yachty]]. Interscope Records signed the latest emerging sensation to a record deal.

==Career==
===2021–present: Viral success, "Gët Busy", and ''Up 2 Më''===
Yeat achieved viral success online through such platforms like [[TikTok]].<ref name=Brake2021>{{cite web|last=Brake|first=David Aaron|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.64436/title.new-music-friday-new-albums-from-baby-keem-common-tommy-genesis-paul-wall-az-more#|title=New Music Friday - New Albums From Baby Keem, Common, Tommy Genesis, Paul Wall, AZ + More|work=[[HipHopDX]]|date=September 9, 2021|accessdate=September 29, 2021}}</ref><ref name=Myers2021>{{cite web|last=Myers|first=Owen|url=http://www.theonlinerocket.com/campus-life/2021/09/03/who-are-they-yeat/|title=Who are they: YEAT|work=[[The Rocket (Slippery Rock)|The Rocket]]|date=September 3, 2021|accessdate=September 29, 2021}}</ref> Yeat initially emerged following his ''4L'' mixtape, which released July 11, 2021.<ref name=Pierre2021b>{{cite web|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/yeat-up-2-me/|title=Yeat: Up 2 Më Album Review|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=September 17, 2021|accessdate=September 29, 2021}}</ref><ref name=Myers2021/> The ''4L'' project notably included "Sorry Bout That" and "Money Twërk".<ref name=Pierre2021b/>

Later, he released the mixtape ''Trëndi'' which had mainstream success with “Mad bout that” and “Fukit” .<ref name=Myers2021/>

A snippet of his song "Gët Busy" went viral online and attracted considerable media and fan attention upon its release.<ref name=Pierre2021a>{{cite web|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/yeat-get-busy-new-song-listen/|title=Listen to Yeat's "Gët Busy":The Ones|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=August 30, 2021|accessdate=September 29, 2021}}</ref> The song was particularly cited by media outlets for its line: "this song already was turnt but here's a bell,", which was immediately followed by the ringing of church bells.<ref name=Pierre2021a/><ref name=Richards2021>{{cite web|last=Richards|first=Chris|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/music/yeat-review-up-2-me/2021/09/13/76a05aea-14a1-11ec-9589-31ac3173c2e5_story.html|title=Yeat redefines what it means for a rapper to rock the bells|work=[[The Washington Post]]|date=September 13, 2021|accessdate=September 29, 2021}}</ref> Fellow rappers [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]] and [[Lil Yachty]] also referenced the line.<ref name=Pierre2021b/>

On September 10, 2021, Yeat released his album ''Up 2 Më''.<ref name=Cole2021>{{cite web|last=Cole|first=Samantha|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/yeat-follows-up-his-viral-success-with-new-project-up-2-me-new-mixtape.121938.html|title= Yeat Follows Up His Viral Success With New Project "Up 2 Me" |work=HotNewHipHop|date=September 12, 2021|accessdate=September 29, 2021}}</ref> The album received generally positive reception from music reviewers. ''[[HipHopDX]]'' wrote that "Yeat's irreverence on ''Up 2 Më'' is intoxicating. His rapping style is so effortless it feels that he floats across the digicore-inspired beats. His latest will prove to be his biggest look yet, but Yeat's looking comfortable in the driver's seat."<ref name=Brake2021/> Alphonse Pierre of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' gave the album a 6.7/10 rating, writing that Yeat "continues to go beyond the usual rage rap expectations."<ref name=Pierre2021b/> Pierre also expressed that Yeat's vocal performance was refreshing, although the production was "stuck in the same box" for much of the album.<ref name=Pierre2021b/> Chris Richards of ''[[The Washington Post]]'' also commented on Yeat's vocal performance, writing that the rapper "visits nearly every corner of his lungs, throat, mouth and sinuses," on the song "Turban".<ref name=Richards2021/> Following the release of ''Up 2 Më'', Yeat made his debut on the [[Rolling Stone charts#Rolling Stone Artists 500|''Rolling Stone'' Artists 500]] list at number 296.<ref name=RScharts>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/drake-artists-500-chart-kacey-musgraves-baby-keem-1230206/|title= Drake Cruises to Another Week At Number One on the Artists 500 Chart |work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=September 21, 2021|accessdate=September 29, 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Yeat stated in an interview that he is of [[Mexican people|Mexican]] and [[Romanian people|Romanian]] ancestry.<ref name=Myers2021/><ref>{{cite AV media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b1Fu_nvUhXQ|title=YEAT on Up 2 Me, Twizzy Rich, KanKan, 4L, Sorry Bout That, T-Pain, & More|work=Our Generation Music|date=July 31, 2021|accessdate=September 29, 2021|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>
