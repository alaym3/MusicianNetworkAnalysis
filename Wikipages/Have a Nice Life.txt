'''Have a Nice Life''' is an American [[post-punk]] band founded in [[Connecticut]] in 2000 by Dan Barrett and Tim Macuga.

==History==
Have a Nice Life was formed in 2000 by Dan Barrett and Tim Macuga of [[Connecticut]]. They have mentioned the likes of [[My Bloody Valentine (band)|My Bloody Valentine]], [[Joy Division]], [[New Order (band)|New Order]], [[Earth (American band)|Earth]], [[Sunn O)))]], [[Xasthur]], [[Leviathan (musical project)|Lurker of Chalice]], [[The Smiths]], [[Nine Inch Nails]], [[Swans (band)|Swans]], [[Sisters of Mercy (band)|Sisters of Mercy]], [[Kraftwerk]], and the philosophy of [[Friedrich Nietzsche]] as influences on their music.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2008-05-30|title=Have A Nice Life - Middletown, US - Gothic / Industrial / Shoegaze|url=http://www.myspace.com/haveanicelife|access-date=2020-10-19|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080530043559/http://www.myspace.com/haveanicelife|archive-date=May 30, 2008}}</ref>

Their debut album, ''[[Deathconsciousness]]'', has received critical acclaim since its release in 2008.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/have-a-nice-life-deathconsciousness-retrospective | title=Have a Nice Life's Deathconsciousness is the Next Greatest Album of All Time | work=Vice | date=24 November 2014 | access-date=19 April 2015 | author=Hill, John}}</ref> It initially did not receive much recognition but gained traction through discussions on online communities and [[word of mouth]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/r7pz8z/have-a-nice-life-deathconsciousness-retrospective|title=Have a Nice Life's 'Deathconsciousness' Is the Next Greatest Album of All Time|website=Vice.com|access-date=November 21, 2020}}</ref> The band have since developed a cult audience.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rosean|first=Samuel|date=January 31, 2020|title=The Beginner's Guide To: Slowcore|url=http://drownedinsound.com/in_depth/4152207-the-beginners-guide-to--slowcore|access-date=2021-01-11|website=[[Drowned In Sound]]|language=en}}</ref>

The band followed up with the [[extended play]] ''[[Time of Land]]'', released as a free [[Digital distribution|download]] in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.enemieslist.net/timeofland/ |title=About Time of Land by Have a Nice Life |publisher=Enemies List Home Recordings |access-date=February 25, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120626000308/http://www.enemieslist.net/timeofland/ |archive-date=June 26, 2012 }}</ref>

On January 27, 2014,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theflenser.bandcamp.com/album/the-unnatural-world|title=The Unnatural World, by Have a Nice Life|website=Theflenser.bandcamp.com|access-date=November 21, 2020}}</ref> Have a Nice Life released their second studio album ''[[The Unnatural World]]''. According to [[Metacritic]], the album has received generally favorable reviews.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/the-unnatural-world/have-a-nice-life | title=The Unnatural World | work=Metacritic | access-date=19 April 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2014/01/have_a_nice_lif.html|title = Have A Nice Life and Woods of Desolation streaming new LPs|access-date = October 12, 2015}}</ref>

On November 8, 2019, the band released their third studio album ''[[Sea of Worry]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Have a Nice Life - Sea of Worry |url=https://theflenser.com/sea-of-worry/ |website=The Flenser |date=26 August 2019 |access-date=3 November 2019}}</ref>
