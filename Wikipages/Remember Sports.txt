'''Remember Sports''' is an American [[indie rock]] band from [[Philadelphia]], [[Pennsylvania]], United States. Formed in [[Gambier, Ohio]], and originally just named '''Sports''', they have released four albums on [[Father/Daughter Records]].

==History==
Remember Sports formed in 2012 when all members were attending [[Kenyon College]] in Gambier, Ohio.<ref name="npr">{{cite web|last1=Katzif|first1=Mike|title=Songs We Love: Sports, 'Get Bummed Out'|url=https://www.npr.org/2015/09/23/441152919/songs-we-love-sports-get-bummed-out|publisher=[[NPR]]|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/02/20/586018799/songs-we-love-remember-sports-up-from-below|title=Songs We Love: Remember Sports, 'Up From Below'|last=Katzif|first=Mike|date=2018-02-20|work=[[NPR]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180222224316/https://www.npr.org/2018/02/20/586018799/songs-we-love-remember-sports-up-from-below|archive-date=2018-02-22|language=en}}</ref> They released their first album in 2014 on Bandcamp titled ''Sunchokes''.<ref name="stereogum">{{cite web|last1=Claymore|first1=Gabriela|title=Band To Watch: Sports|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1813975/band-to-watch-sports-saturday-stereogum-premiere/franchises/band-to-watch/|publisher=[[Stereogum]]|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref><ref name="imposemagazine">{{cite web|last1=Porcello|first1=Niccolo|title=Sports, Sunchokes|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/sports-sunchokes-stream|publisher=Impose Magazine|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref> In 2015, Sports signed to [[Father/Daughter Records]], where they released their second album titled ''All Of Something''.<ref name="stereogum"/><ref name="stereogum1">{{cite web|last1=Rettig|first1=James|title=Stream Sports All Of Something|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1839164/stream-sports-all-of-something/mp3s/|publisher=Stereogum|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref><ref name="spin">{{cite web|last1=Joyce|first1=Colin|title=Sports Double Down on Misty-Eyed Alt-Rock on 'All of Something' LP|url=http://www.spin.com/2015/10/sports-all-of-something-album-lp-stream-premiere/|publisher=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref> The album was produced by Kyle Gilbride ([[Waxahatchee]], [[Upset (band)|Upset]], [[Field Mouse (band)|Field Mouse]]).<ref name="brooklynvegan1">{{cite web|last1=Sacher|first1=Andrew|title=Sports streaming "Saturday" from new LP 'All Of Something'|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/sports-streamin/|publisher=[[BrooklynVegan]]|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref> All Of Something was recorded primarily while most of the members were still in college.<ref name="rollingstone">{{cite web|title=Sports|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-november-2015-20151125/sports-20151124|publisher=[[Rolling Stone]]|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref> The album received four stars out of five by ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine.<ref name="rollingstone1">{{cite web|last1=Dolan|first1=Jon|title=Sports: All of Something|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/sports-all-of-something-20151102|publisher=Rolling Stone|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref>  The album was listed at number ten on [[PopMatters]] list titled "The Best Indie Pop of 2015".<ref name="popmatters1">{{cite web|last1=Heaton|first1=Dave|title=The Best Indie Pop of 2015|url=http://www.popmatters.com/feature/the-best-indie-pop-of-2015/|publisher=[[PopMatters]]|access-date=2 April 2017}}</ref>

In August 2016, Remember Sports released a demo of their song "Makin It Right", which was featured on the ''Clearmountain Compilation Vol. 1''.<ref name="stereogumcite">{{cite web|last1=Rettig|first1=James|title=Sports – "Making It Right" (Demo)|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1893052/sports-making-it-right-demo/mp3s/|publisher=Stereogum|access-date=2 April 2017}}</ref>

In November 2017, the band announced their name change from Sports to Remember Sports to avoid confusion with other bands, including the Oklahoma-based band, Sports.<ref name=":0" /><ref name="stereogumcit21">{{cite web|last1=Claymore|first1=Gabriela Tully|title=Sports Change Their Name|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1971579/sports-change-their-name/news/|publisher=Stereogum|access-date=16 November 2017}}</ref> In 2018, the band released their third album titled ''Slow Buzz''.<ref name="stereogumdotcom">{{cite web |last1=Rettig |first1=James |title=Stream Remember Sports Slow Buzz |url=https://www.stereogum.com/1996221/stream-remember-sports-slow-buzz/music/ |publisher=Stereogum |access-date=11 February 2021}}</ref> In 2020, the group contributed a cover of the song "[[Just the Girl]]" by [[the Click Five]] for a tribute compilation celebrating the life of musician [[Adam Schlesinger]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9403070/adam-schlesinger-tribute-album-benefit-covid-19-relief|title=All-Star Adam Schlesinger Tribute Album 'Saving For a Custom Van' Benefits COVID-19 Relief|first=Gil|last=Kaufman|date=June 16, 2020|work=Billboard|access-date=June 16, 2020}}</ref> In 2021, the band announced their fourth LP, ''Like a Stone'', released on April 23, 2021.<ref name="brooklynvegan">{{cite web |last1=Sacher |first1=Andrew |title=Remember Sports announce new album 'Like A Stone,' share "Pinky Ring" |url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/remember-sports-announce-new-album-like-a-stone-share-pinky-ring/ |publisher=[[BrooklynVegan]] |access-date=11 February 2021}}</ref>
