'''Jeffery Lamar Williams'''<ref name=BMIDATA>{{cite web|url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/writer.asp?blnWriter=True&blnPublisher=True&blnArtist=True&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&querytype=WriterID&keyid=550231296&keyname=WILLIAMS+JEFFERY+LAMAR&CAE=664487309&Affiliation=BMI|title=Songwriter/Composer: WILLIAMS JEFFERY LAMAR|last=Williams|first=Jeffery Lamar|publisher=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]] (BMI)|access-date=July 24, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160204185253/http://repertoire.bmi.com/writer.asp?blnWriter=True&blnPublisher=True&blnArtist=True&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&querytype=WriterID&keyid=550231296&keyname=WILLIAMS+JEFFERY+LAMAR&CAE=664487309&Affiliation=BMI|archive-date=February 4, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> (born August 16, 1991), known professionally as '''Young Thug''',<ref name="SEX"/> is an American rapper. He is considered to be an influential figure of his generation, with his music impacting the modern sound of [[hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[trap music]].<ref name="Weiss">{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.com/culture/story/20191021-why-young-thug-is-the-21st-centurys-most-influential-rapper|title=Why Young Thug is the 21st Century's Most Influential Rapper|date=October 21, 2019|access-date=October 22, 2019|publisher=[[BBC]]|last=Weiss|first=Jeff|archive-date=October 24, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191024074609/http://www.bbc.com/culture/story/20191021-why-young-thug-is-the-21st-centurys-most-influential-rapper|url-status=live}}</ref> Known for his [[eccentricity (behavior)|eccentric]] vocal style and fashion,<ref name="Allmusic">{{cite web |last1=Jeffries |first1=David |title=Biography - Young Thug |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/mn0002068342 |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=August 11, 2019 |archive-date=August 26, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190826111423/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/mn0002068342 |url-status=live }}</ref> Thug initially released a series of independent [[mixtape]]s beginning in 2011 with ''I Came from Nothing''. In early 2013, he signed with [[Gucci Mane]]'s [[1017 Records]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5930256/young-thug-is-still-signed-to-1017-brick-squadatlantic-says|title=Young Thug|work=Billboard|access-date=March 9, 2015|archive-date=March 10, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140310171752/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5930256/young-thug-is-still-signed-to-1017-brick-squadatlantic-says|url-status=live}}</ref> and later that year he released his label debut mixtape ''1017 Thug'' to critical praise.<ref name="fad1">{{cite magazine|title=GEN F: Young Thug|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/05/08/gen-f-young-thug/#/0|magazine=The Fader|access-date=January 12, 2014|archive-date=January 13, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140113215055/http://www.thefader.com/2013/05/08/gen-f-young-thug/#/0|url-status=live}}</ref>

Young Thug received mainstream recognition in 2014 with the singles "[[Stoner (song)|Stoner]]" and "[[Danny Glover (song)|Danny Glover]]" in addition to appearances on several singles, including [[T.I. (rapper)|T.I.]]'s "[[About the Money]]", [[Tyga]]'s "[[Hookah (song)|Hookah]]", and [[Rich Gang]]'s "[[Lifestyle (Rich Gang song)|Lifestyle]]". That year, he also signed to [[Lyor Cohen]]'s [[300 Entertainment]] and collaborated on the mixtape ''[[Rich Gang: Tha Tour Pt. 1]]''. In 2015, he amassed a number of mixtapes, including ''[[Barter 6]]'' and two installments of his ''[[Slime Season]]'' series. Subsequently in 2016, Thug issued commercial mixtapes ''[[I'm Up (Young Thug mixtape)|I'm Up]]'', ''[[Slime Season 3]]'', and ''[[Jeffery (mixtape)|Jeffery]]''.<ref name="spin">{{cite web|last1=Unterberger|first1=Andrew|title=Stream and Download Young Thug's 'Slime Season 3′ Mixtape |url=http://assets.rollingstone.com/assets/2013/list/10-best-mixtapes-of-2013-20131209/items/young-thug-1017-thug-19691231/139672/_original/1035x1035-120313-1017-thug-1800-1386093794.jpg |website=[[Spin Magazine]] |access-date=March 28, 2016|archive-date=October 24, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141024143311/http://assets.rollingstone.com/assets/2013/list/10-best-mixtapes-of-2013-20131209/items/young-thug-1017-thug-19691231/139672/_original/1035x1035-120313-1017-thug-1800-1386093794.jpg|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2017, Thug featured on the hit song "[[Havana (Camila Cabello song)|Havana]]" by Cuban pop singer [[Camila Cabello]], which became his first number-one single on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. In 2018, he released a compilation album ''[[Slime Language]]'' with his label [[YSL Records]].<ref name="Noisey">{{cite web |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/listen-to-young-thugs-new-album-beautiful-thugger-girls |title=Listen To Young Thug's New Album, 'Beautiful Thugger Girls' |publisher=Noisey |date=June 16, 2017 |access-date=June 16, 2017 |archive-date=June 3, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200603081045/https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/8qpebv/listen-to-young-thugs-new-album-beautiful-thugger-girls |url-status=live }}</ref> In 2019, he won the [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year]] for his songwriting contribution on "[[This Is America (song)|This Is America]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/02/young-thug-wins-first-ever-grammy-feature-childish-gambino-this-is-america/|title=Young Thug Wins Grammy for Childish Gambino's "This Is America" - XXL|first=Marisa|last=Mendez|website=XXL Mag}}</ref>

Young Thug's debut studio album, ''[[So Much Fun]]'' (2019), debuted at number one on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and included the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 top-twenty singles "[[The London (song)|The London]]" and "[[Hot (Young Thug song)|Hot]]". The following year, his single "[[Go Crazy (Chris Brown and Young Thug song)|Go Crazy]]" with [[Chris Brown]] from the collaborative mixtape ''[[Slime & B]]'' (2020) peaked at number three on the Hot 100, becoming his first top-ten single as a lead artist. Additionally, he reached number one on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 for the second time in his career with his feature on Travis Scott's single "[[Franchise (song)|Franchise]]". In 2021, Thug released the compilation album ''[[Slime Language 2]]'' which debuted at number one on the ''Billboard'' 200. His second studio album, ''[[Punk (Young Thug album)|Punk]]'', was released on October 15, 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hypebeast.com/2019/12/young-thug-new-album-february-release-date-reveal |title=Young Thug reveals new album is dropping in February |work=Hypebeast |date=December 18, 2019 |access-date=December 26, 2019 |author=Ingvaldsen, Torsten |archive-date=December 18, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191218070848/https://hypebeast.com/2019/12/young-thug-new-album-february-release-date-reveal |url-status=live }}</ref> It debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, becoming his third number one album, as well as his second chart topping project of 2021.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Caulfield|first=Keith|date=October 24, 2021|title=Young Thug's 'Punk' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Albums Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9650236/young-thugs-punk-number-one-billboard-200-chart/|url-status=live|access-date=October 24, 2021|magazine=Billboard|language=}}</ref>

==Early life==
Jeffery Lamar Williams was born in [[Atlanta]], the tenth of eleven children. He is from [[Sylvan Hills, Atlanta|Sylvan Hills]], a neighborhood in Atlanta (Zone 3),<ref name=ae>{{cite web|title=Archive Entertainment {{!}} Young Thug |url=http://www.archiventertainment.com/artist/young-thug/ |publisher=Archive Entertainment |access-date=January 12, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130305064219/http://www.archiventertainment.com/artist/young-thug/ |archive-date=March 5, 2013 }}</ref> and grew up in the Jonesboro South [[public housing|projects]].<ref name="fad1" /> Other rappers from the neighborhood include [[Waka Flocka Flame]], [[2 Chainz]], [[Ludacris]] and Williams's childhood friend [[PeeWee Longway]], from whom Williams lived four doors down.<ref name="seattle">{{cite web|title=5 things you should know about Young Thug|url=http://www.seattletimes.com/entertainment/music/5-things-you-should-know-about-young-thug/|website=Seattle Times|date=March 26, 2015|access-date=February 25, 2017}}</ref><ref name="xxl">{{cite web|last1=Weinstein|first1=Max|title=Peewee Longway Is One of Hip-Hop's Secret Weapons Read More: Peewee Longway Is One of Hip-Hop's Secret Weapons – XXL | url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/01/peewee-longway-hip-hop-secret-weapon/?trackback=tsmclip|website=XXL Mag|access-date=February 25, 2017}}</ref>
Young Thug has told interviewers that he was expelled in the sixth grade for breaking a teacher's arm. He was sent to juvenile prison for four years.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Vozick-Levinson|first1=Simon|date=December 4, 2014|title=Perma-Stoned Oddball Young Thug Is the Hottest Voice in Rap|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/perma-stoned-oddball-young-thug-is-the-hottest-voice-in-rap-66515/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180829072520/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/perma-stoned-oddball-young-thug-is-the-hottest-voice-in-rap-66515/|archive-date=August 29, 2018|access-date=August 28, 2018|magazine=Rolling Stone}}</ref>

Thug subsequently released his first project on the label, his fourth mixtape, ''1017 Thug''. The mixtape was met with positive reviews from music critics, which noted it for its original style. ===2014: Rise to popularity and label issues===
On January 18, 2014, Young Thug revealed that he had been offered $8.5 million to sign to [[Future (rapper)|Future]]'s [[Freebandz]] record label.<ref>{{cite web|author=C. Vernon Coleman II |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/01/young-thug-signing-futures-freebandz-imprint/ |title=Young Thug Signing To Future's Freebandz Imprint – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=January 18, 2014 |access-date=August 27, 2015}}</ref> In March 2014, Young Thug's affiliation with [[Cash Money Records]] and its [[chief executive officer]] (CEO) [[Birdman (rapper)|Birdman]], resulted in much speculation in the media about him signing to the label. However, the label's publicist later stated this was untrue.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Young Thug Is Still Signed to 1017 Brick Squad/Atlantic, Says Source|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5930256/young-thug-is-still-signed-to-1017-brick-squadatlantic-says|magazine=Billboard|access-date=March 27, 2014|archive-date=March 10, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140310171752/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5930256/young-thug-is-still-signed-to-1017-brick-squadatlantic-says|url-status=live}}</ref> On March 28, 2014, Ronald "Caveman" Rosario, director of Urban Music at 1017 Distribution, cleared the situation, stating that Young Thug signed a management deal with Birdman's [[Rich Gang]], not a record deal, and is still signed to 1017 Brick Squad.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Gucci Mane Made $1.3 Million Dollars In 2013|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/03/gucci-mane-made-1-3-million-dollars-2013/|magazine=XXL|access-date=March 28, 2014}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Young Thug has six children by four women (three sons and three daughters). He became a father at the age of 17.<ref name="GQ">{{cite web|url=https://www.gq.com/story/young-thug-best-rapper-alive-interview|title=What Planet Is Young Thug From?|first=Devin Friedman, Ben|last=Watts|date=February 16, 2016|access-date=September 19, 2017|archive-date=October 13, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171013142130/https://www.gq.com/story/young-thug-best-rapper-alive-interview|url-status=live}}</ref> In April 2015, he became engaged to Jerrika Karlae, who runs a swimsuit line and whose mother manages [[Young Dolph]].<ref name="xxl 3">{{cite web|last1=J|first1=Miranda |title=Young Thug Is Head Over Heels For Jerrika Karlae Read More: Young Thug Is Head Over Heels For Jerrika Karlae |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/03/young-thug-is-head-over-heels-for-jerrika-karlae/|website=[[XXL Magazine]]|access-date=September 2, 2016}}</ref> Thug bought his first home in September 2016 after the release of his mixtape Jeffery.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/09/16/young-thug-just-bought-his-first-house|title=Young Thug Just Bought His First House|website=The FADER|access-date=August 19, 2017|archive-date=August 19, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170819063506/http://www.thefader.com/2016/09/16/young-thug-just-bought-his-first-house|url-status=live}}</ref> The home, based in Buckhead, Atlanta, is more than 11,000 square-feet, has six bedrooms, 11 bathrooms, a full bar, a theater room and a four-car garage.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ajc.com/news/local/lawsuit-young-thug-owes-million-his-atlanta-home/stfUwh2D19AUii1Ph3q6tO/|title=Lawsuit: Young Thug owes $2.2 million on his Atlanta home|last=Northam|first=Mitchell|website=ajc|access-date=August 19, 2017|archive-date=August 19, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170819061339/http://www.ajc.com/news/local/lawsuit-young-thug-owes-million-his-atlanta-home/stfUwh2D19AUii1Ph3q6tO/|url-status=live}}</ref>

During his childhood, one of Thug's older brothers was shot and killed in front of the family home.<ref name="Ihaza">{{Cite magazine|last=Ihaza|first=Jeff|date=2021-07-28|title=The Abundant Heart and Revolutionary Vision of Young Thug|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/young-thug-punk-ysl-1202951/|access-date=2021-07-28|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref>

In April 2020, during a concert livestream, Thug revealed a recent brush with death, stating: {{quote|I kinda just stayed in the bed and I was like, 'Yo, call the ambulance. I can't move my body, then later, when the ambulance came, I couldn't get out of bed. They had to get me out of the bed, basically. I felt like my whole body was numb and I couldn't move. I went to the hospital and I had found out that I had liver and kidney failure. And I kinda had sorta passed away, like I kinda died.<ref name="Ihaza">{{Cite magazine|last=Ihaza|first=Jeff|date=2021-07-28|title=The Abundant Heart and Revolutionary Vision of Young Thug|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/young-thug-punk-ysl-1202951/|access-date=2021-07-28|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Murphy|first=Chris|date=2020-04-30|title=Young Thug Reveals That He Almost Died of Kidney and Liver Failure|url=https://www.vulture.com/2020/04/young-thug-reveals-he-suffered-from-kidney-and-liver-failure.html|access-date=2021-07-28|website=Vulture|language=en-us}}</ref>}}

He's also charged with possession of amphetamine, Alprazolam, codeine (2 counts) and a firearm.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.tmz.com/2018/09/06/young-thug-charged-drug-gun-possession-felony/|title=Young Thug Charged With 8 Felonies For Drug And Gun Possession|work=TMZ|access-date=September 7, 2018|archive-date=September 7, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180907005017/http://www.tmz.com/2018/09/06/young-thug-charged-drug-gun-possession-felony/|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== Philanthropy ===
In December 2016, Thug joined the #fightpovertyagain campaign.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Renshaw|first1=David|title=Young Thug On The Fight Against Poverty: "I Always Want To Stay Involved."|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/12/13/young-thug-poverty-interview|website=The FADER|access-date=July 25, 2017|archive-date=November 8, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171108034857/http://www.thefader.com/2016/12/13/young-thug-poverty-interview|url-status=live}}</ref> On June 29, 2017, Thug donated all proceeds of a sold-out concert to [[Planned Parenthood]], stating on [[Twitter]] that "I was a teenage parent. Planned+unplanned parenthood is beautiful."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sodomsky|first1=Sam|title=Young Thug Donates New York Concert Proceeds to Planned Parenthood {{!}} Pitchfork|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/young-thug-donates-new-york-concert-proceeds-to-planned-parenthood/|website=pitchfork.com|date=June 30, 2017|access-date=July 25, 2017|language=en|archive-date=July 8, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170708215238/http://pitchfork.com/news/young-thug-donates-new-york-concert-proceeds-to-planned-parenthood/|url-status=live}}</ref>
