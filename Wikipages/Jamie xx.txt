'''James Thomas Smith'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mobile.ascap.com/aceclient/AceWeb/#ace/writer/598347296/SMITH%20JAMES%20THOMAS|title=ASCAP Ace Search - James Thomas Smith|publisher=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] (ASCAP)|access-date=20 November 2016}}</ref> (born 28 October 1988), known professionally as '''Jamie xx''', is an English musician, DJ, record producer and remixer. He is known for both his solo work and as a member of the English [[indie pop]] band [[The xx]].

He has been described as a "driving force" behind The xx,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/oct/16/jamie-xx-review-joyous-dj-set-club-alchemist-o2-academy-brixton |title= Jamie xx review – unremittingly joyous DJ set from a club alchemist |publisher=The Guardian |date=2015-10-16 |author=Ian Gittins |access-date=2017-10-11}}</ref> contributing to the group's significant commercial and critical success. As a solo artist, he has been recognised with a 2015 [[Mercury Prize]] nomination, and a 2016 [[Grammy Award for Best Dance/Electronic Album]] nomination for his debut album ''[[In Colour (Jamie xx album)|In Colour]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/6785984/grammy-2016-nominees-dance-electronic-jack-u-skrillex-diplo-jamiexx|title= Grammy Nominations 2016: Dance Nominees Are Deserving, But Can't Catch a Major Category Break|last1= Medved|first1= Matt|date= 2015-12-07|website= Billboard|access-date= 2015-12-15}}</ref>

==Career==
===2005–09===
Smith's musical career began in 2005 when he joined [[The xx]] accompanying old schoolmates Romy Madley Croft, Oliver Sim and Baria Qureshi of the [[Elliott School (London)|Elliott School]] in London, notable for alumni including [[Hot Chip]], [[Burial (musician)|Burial]] and [[Four Tet]].<ref name=Joke>{{cite web|url=http://www.dummymag.com/features/2009/07/14/the-xx-it-started-out-as-a-joke-/ |title=The xx" "It started as a joke"|last=Saxelby|first=Ruth|work=Dummy|access-date=23 August 2009|date=14 July 2009}}</ref> He first used the stage name Jamie xx in July 2009 in a promotional mix for the band's debut album ''[[xx (album)|xx]]'' released on the ''[[Fact (UK magazine)|FACT]]'' magazine mix series.<ref>[http://www.factmag.com/2009/07/31/fact-mix-70-the-xx/ FACT mix 70: The xx] FACT Magazine</ref> The mixtape was compiled by Jamie Smith and featured, amongst others, four tracks credited to Jamie xx – one self-production and three remixes. The ''xx'' album went on to become [[British Phonographic Industry#Certifications|platinum]] in the UK.<ref>[http://www.bpi.co.uk/certifiedawards/Search.aspx Certified Awards Search] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090924015932/http://www.bpi.co.uk/certifiedawards/search.aspx |date=2009-09-24 }} British Recorded Music Industry</ref> Afterward, Jamie Smith went on to do more remix work for artists like [[Florence and the Machine|Florence + The Machine]], [[Adele]], [[Jack Peñate]] and [[Glasser (musician)|Glasser]].<ref>[http://www.discogs.com/artist/Jamie+xx Jamie xx] discogs.com</ref>

===2010–present===
In late 2010, a Jamie xx remix of the song "NY Is Killing Me" from [[Gil Scott-Heron]]'s last album ''[[I'm New Here]]'' aired on radio stations across the UK and Europe. The remix of "I'll Take Care of U" followed in January 2011. Both singles drew the attention of the general public and the critics. They set the way for a 13-track remix album entitled ''[[We're New Here]]'', produced entirely by Jamie xx and credited to "Gil Scott-Heron and Jamie xx". The album was released on 21 February 2011 on the [[XL Recordings]] label, but a full-album preview could be streamed on the website of ''[[The Guardian]]'' as early as 14 February.<ref>{{Cite news|last=The Guardian Music Blog |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/musicblog/2011/feb/14/gil-scott-heron-jamie-xx |title=New album stream: Gil Scott-Heron and Jamie xx – We're New Here |work=The Guardian|series=Music Blog |date=14 February 2011 |access-date=18 February 2011}}</ref> ''We're New Here'' received critical acclaim and was named "a cohesive, considered masterpiece in its own right" by [[BBC]]'s Ele Beattie.<ref name=Review>{{Cite news|last=Beattie |first=Ele |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/reviews/v46m |title=Gil Scott-Heron and Jamie xx We're New Here Review: A cohesive, considered masterpiece in its own right. |publisher=BBC |work=BBC Music |date=17 February 2011 |access-date=23 February 2011}}</ref>

On 6 June 2011 the two-track self-produced single "[[Far Nearer / Beat For]]" was released. The song "Far Nearer" was selected Best New Track by ''[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/12215-far-nearer/|title=Jamie xx|work=Pitchfork}}</ref> and the [[A-side and B-side|double A-side]] single charted at number 128 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.zobbel.de/cluk/110618cluk.txt |format=TXT |title=Chart statistics |website=Zobbel.de |access-date=2020-03-20}}</ref> Later the same year, Smith produced the title track off [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]]'s second album, ''[[Take Care (album)|Take Care]]'', which features [[Rihanna]]. In addition, Smith helped create several reworks for [[Radiohead]]'s song "Bloom" which were released on a remix album entitled ''[[TKOL RMX 1234567]]''. He also co-produced the song "When It's All Over" with [[Alicia Keys]] on her ''[[Girl on Fire (album)|Girl on Fire]]'' album. In 2014, he announced an upcoming collaboration with [[John Talabot]] on Tim Sweeney's Beats in Space online radio show. In 2014 he released the singles "Girl/Sleep Sound" and "All Under One Roof Raving".

On 27 March 2015 he released two singles. "Loud Places", featuring [[the xx]]'s Romy Madley Croft, and "Gosh". On the same day he also announced that his debut album, ''[[In Colour (Jamie xx album)|In Colour]]'', would be out on 1 June 2015.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://pitchfork.com/news/58948-jamie-xx-details-in-colour-shares-loud-places-video/ |title=Jamie xx Details In Colour, Shares "Loud Places" Video |publisher=Pitchfork |date=27 March 2015 |access-date=27 March 2015}}</ref> Ahead of its release, ''In Colour'' was streamed on iTunes as a preview with album-spanning visuals that 'reacted' to each sound.<ref>{{cite news|title=See Jamie xx's visual album preview for 'In Colour'|url=http://www.neverenoughnotes.co.uk/2015/05/see-jamie-xxs-visual-album-preview-for-in-colour/|access-date=30 May 2015|work=Never Enough Notes|publisher=Never Enough Notes|date=28 May 2015|ref=www.neverenoughnotes.co.uk}}</ref>

Jamie xx composed the score for a ballet, ''Tree of Codes'', commissioned by [[Manchester International Festival]] for its 2015 edition. Based on a [[Tree of Codes|novel of the same name]] by [[Jonathan Safran Foer]], the ballet was choreographed by [[Wayne McGregor]] and had a set created by [[Olafur Eliasson]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://pitchfork.com/news/59879-jamie-xx-previews-score-to-ballet-tree-of-codes/ |title=Jamie xx Previews Score to Ballet Tree of Codes |publisher=Pitchfork |author= Jeremy Gordon |date=2015-06-09 |access-date=2015-07-03}}</ref>

In 2020, he released his first single in 5 years, "Idontknow", which received positive to lukewarm reception from critics.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/jamie-xx-new-song-idontknow-984278/|title=Jamie xx Returns With Bustling New Single ‘Idontknow’|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|author=Daniel Kreps|date=15 April 2020|accessdate=29 August 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/jamie-xx-idontknow/|title=“Idontknow” by Jamie xx Review|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|author=Hubert Adjei-Kontoh|date=15 April 2020|accessdate=29 August 2021}}</ref> He was credited on [[Tyler, The Creator]]'s 2021 album [[Call Me If You Get Lost]] as a co-producer on the track "RISE!".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/tyler-the-creator-releases-new-album-call-me-if-you-get-lost-listen/|title=Tyler, the Creator Releases New Album ''Call Me If You Get Lost''|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|author=Madison Bloom|date=25 June 2021|accessdate=29 August 2021}}</ref>
