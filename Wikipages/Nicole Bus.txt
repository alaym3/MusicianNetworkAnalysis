'''Nicole Bus''' is a Dutch singer who is currently signed to [[Roc Nation]]. Her 2018 single, "You", is tied for the fastest debut single to reach the top of the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Adult R&B Songs]] chart. She released her American debut studio album, ''Kairos'', in October 2019. Prior to signing to Roc Nation, Bus had released a variety of music in the Netherlands and won the [[Grote Prijs van Nederland]] ("Grand Prize of the Netherlands") in the singer-songwriter category in 2010. She also appeared on the Dutch singing competition programs, ''[[X Factor (Dutch TV series)|X Factor]]'' and ''[[The Voice of Holland]]''.

==Early life and education==

Nicole Bus was born in [[Nieuwegein]], Netherlands<ref name="DeBrug">{{cite news | last=Mertens| first=Dieuwertje| title =In de jas: Nicole Bus| language =Dutch| newspaper =de Brug Krant| date =24 November 2011 | url =https://debrugkrant.nl/in-de-jas-nicole-bus/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> and grew up partially in the municipality of [[Houten]]. Her father is [[Dutch people|Dutch]] and her mother is [[Afro-Curaçaoan]]. Bus was raised in church where she sang and learned how to play a variety of instruments, including guitar, piano, drums, bass, and synthesizers. She began writing songs at age 11.<ref name="Volks">{{cite news | last=van Gijssel| first=Robert| title =Ik schreef Zonnestralen omdat ik wil dat mensen stoppen met zeiken| language =Dutch| newspaper =de Volkskrant| date =7 August 2016 | url =https://www.volkskrant.nl/cultuur-media/ik-schreef-zonnestralen-omdat-ik-wil-dat-mensen-stoppen-met-zeiken~bf535f04/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> When she was 16, Bus was briefly homeless after her parents separated.<ref name="CJP">{{cite news | last=van Veluw| first=Rick| title =In de tent met... Nicole Bus| language =Dutch| work =CJP| date =22 July 2016 | url =https://www.cjp.nl/blog/2016/07/in-de-tent-met-nicole-bus/78047/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> In 2006, however, she was a supporting act for American [[Gospel music|gospel]] musician, [[Canton Jones]]. She also toured as a gospel singer to locations like Israel, [[Palestinian territories|Palestine]], Nicaragua,<ref name="Roots">{{cite news | last=Kemp| first=Ivan| title =Nicole Bus timmert hard aan de weg| language =Dutch| work =Love My Roots| date =13 January 2019 | url =http://lovemyroots.nl/2019/01/13/nicole-bus-timmert-hard-aan-de-weg/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> and Indonesia in 2008. She would later attend the Pop Academie in [[Utrecht]] and the [[Conservatorium van Amsterdam]] where she studied music and vocal work and received her diploma in 2016.<ref name="Volks"/>

==Career==

In 2009, Bus won a gospel music competition. She followed that by being awarded the Grote Prijs van Nederland ("Grand Prize of the Netherlands") in the singer-songwriter category in 2010.<ref name="Volks"/> The following year, she released the studio album, ''The Heart of the Matter'' on Bladehammer through [[EMI]] Netherlands.<ref name="AM">{{cite news | last=Kellman| first=Andy| title =Nicole Bus – Biography| publisher =AllMusic| date =July 2019 | url =https://www.allmusic.com/artist/nicole-bus-mn0003503941/biography| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref><ref name="MENO">{{cite news | title =Nicole Bus werkt samen met Bladehammer Music| language=Dutch| work =The Men-O| date =12 April 2011| url =https://themeno.nl/nicole-bus-werkt-samen-met-bladehammer-music/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> In 2013, she auditioned for the fifth season of the Dutch singing competition series, ''X Factor'', but did advance beyond the judges.<ref name="Script">{{cite news | title =X Factor 2013: aflevering 4 – De laatste auditie| language=Dutch| work =Script Girl| date =10 May 2013 | url =https://www.scriptgirl.nl/2013/05/x-factor-aflevering-4/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> Later that year, she appeared on the [[The Voice of Holland (season 4)|fourth season]] of the singing competition series, ''The Voice of Holland'', where she advanced to the quarterfinals.<ref name="DX">{{cite news | last=Moorwood| first=Victoria| title =Nicole Bus Talks Upcoming Album, Neo Soul & Signing To Roc Nation| work =HipHopDX| date =23 April 2019 | url =https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.51144/title.nicole-bus-talks-upcoming-album-neo-soul-signing-to-roc-nation| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref>

In May 2016, she released an EP of Dutch-language tracks called ''Nederlandse Bodem''<ref name="RO">{{cite news | last=Schavemaker| first=Peter| title =Nicole Bus: "Mijn teksten bestaan uit Oud Hollands, de straattaal, dialecten, spreekwoorden en Algemeen Beschaafd Nederlands"| language=Dutch| work =Reporters Online| date =13 June 2016 | url =https://reportersonline.nl/nicole-bus-mijn-teksten-bestaan-uit-oud-hollands-de-straattaal-dialecten-spreekwoorden-en-algemeen-beschaafd-nederlands/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> with the singles, "Zonnestralen"<ref name="Volks"/> and "Lopen".<ref name="CJP"/> The project was released by the Dutch label, [[TopNotch]].<ref name="Volks"/> She later opened for [[Sandra van Nieuwland]] on several dates in November and December 2016.<ref name="Billin">{{cite news | title =Nicole Bus support-act Sandra van Nieuwland| language=Dutch| work =Top Billin| date =25 October 2016 | url =https://www.topbillin.nl/news/archive/nicole-bus-support-act-sandra-van-nieuwland/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> In March 2017, she released a Dutch-language album entitled, ''Magnolia'', also through TopNotch. She also taught music lessons at various schools during that time.<ref name="Vice">{{cite news | last=Selvi| first=Berkan| title =nicole bus bloeit op met haar nieuwe album 'magnolia'| language=Dutch| work =i-D| date =17 March 2017 | url =https://i-d.vice.com/nl/article/5934jk/nicole-bus-bloeit-op-met-haar-nieuwe-album-magnolia| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref>

Later in 2017, [[Atlanta]]-based producer, [[Needlz]], posted an [[Instagram]] video featuring Bus' vocals. The Instagram video caught the attention of Roc Nation executives<ref name="SourceNeedlz">{{cite news | title =Producer Needlz gives Insider Perspective to the staying Power of R&B Artist Nicole Bus| work =The Source| date =15 July 2019 | url =https://thesource.com/2019/07/15/producer-needlz-gives-insider-perspective-to-the-staying-power-of-rb-artist-nicole-bus/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> and prompted them to contact Needlz and Bus.<ref name="BET">{{cite news | title =How Roc Nation Singer Nicole Bus Got Discovered From Instagram| publisher =BET| date =14 June 2019 | url =https://www.bet.com/music/2019/06/14/how-roc-nation-singer-nicole-bus-got-discovered-from-instagram.html| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> In the meantime, she released an independent English-language EP entitled, ''T.O.T.A'', in late 2017.<ref name="Forbes">{{cite news | last=Alvarez| first=Lauren| title =How The Soulful Emerging Artist Nicole Bus Plans On Bringing Back Music Samples| work =Forbes| date =26 April 2019 | url =https://www.forbes.com/sites/laurenalvarez/2019/04/29/how-the-soulful-emerging-artist-nicole-bus-plans-on-bringing-back-music-samples| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> She was ultimately signed to Roc Nation in 2018.<ref name="BillMarch">{{cite news | last=Zellner| first=Xander| title =R&B Singers Nicole Bus, The Bonfyre & Kiana Ledé Debut on Emerging Artists Chart| work =Billboard| date =20 March 2019 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8503374/nicole-bus-bonfyre-kiana-lede-emerging-artists-chart| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref>

In December of that year,<ref name="Bounce">{{cite news | title =Nicole Bus Presents Her Ode To Black Men In ‘You’| work =Soul Bounce| date =12 December 2018 | url =http://www.soulbounce.com/2018/12/nicole-bus-presents-you-audio/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> she released her first Roc Nation single, "You", which was produced by Needlz. The single features quoted lyrics from The Charmels' "As Long as I've Got You" and a sample of the [[Wu Tang Clan]]'s "[[C.R.E.A.M.]]". It also became a radio hit in the US, reaching 5.5&nbsp;million listeners by January 2019 and<ref name="RSMain">{{cite news | last=Leight| first=Elias| title =See Nicole Bus’ Glittering Video for Breakout Single ‘You’ | work =Rolling Stone| date =29 January 2019 | url =https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/nicole-bus-you-video-785489/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> ultimately topping the ''Billboard'' Adult R&B Songs chart in a record-tying amount of time for a debut single (8 weeks).<ref name="BillRecord">{{cite news | last=Zellner| first=Xander| title =Nicole Bus Ties Record for Fastest-Rising Adult R&B No. 1 for a Debut Hit| work =Billboard| date =15 May 2019 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8511646/nicole-bus-ties-record-fastest-rising-adult-rb-number-1-you| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> "You" also received several remixes, including those from [[Ghostface Killah]]<ref name="RSKillah">{{cite news | last=Kreps| first=Daniel| title =Hear Ghostface Killah Add Fresh Verses to ‘C.R.E.A.M.’ Beat on Nicole Bus’ ‘You’| work =Rolling Stone| date =25 January 2019 | url =https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/ghostface-killah-cream-you-784751/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> and [[Rick Ross]].<ref name="DX"/> In the ensuing months, Bus performed at a number of events, including [[Summer Jam (festival)|Summer Jam]],<ref name="Hot97">{{cite news | last=Cole| first=Vernessa| title =Nicole Bus...A New Voice That is Here to Stay | work =Hot 97| date =9 April 2019 | url =https://www.hot97.com/news/news/summer-jam/nicole-busa-new-voice-here-stay-photovideo| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> Femme It Forward,<ref name="VarietyFemme">{{cite news | title =Cardi B, Brandy, City Girls to Headline ‘Femme It Forward’ Concerts and Events| work =Variety| date =16 April 2019 | url =https://variety.com/2019/music/news/cardi-b-brandy-city-girls-headline-femme-it-forward-concerts-events-1203190600/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> and the [[Essence Music Festival]]. She also opened for [[Common (rapper)|Common]] on his "Let Love Tour" in July 2019.<ref name="Rythmic">{{cite news | last=Mitchell| first=Michael| title =Nicole Bus Announce ‘Let Love’ Tour Dates with Common| work =Rhythmic| date =9 July 2019 | url =https://rhythmic.fm/nicole-bus-announce-let-love-tour-dates-with-common/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref>

That month, she released her second single, "Mr. Big Shot",<ref name="Respect">{{cite news | last=Trapp| first=Malcolm| title =R&B Singer Nicole Bus Delivers "Mr. Big Shot" Ahead Of Forthcoming Debut Album| work =Respect Magazine| date =19 July 2019 | url =https://respect-mag.com/2019/07/rb-singer-nicole-bus-delivers-mr-big-shot-ahead-of-forthcoming-debut-album/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> which would also reach the Adult R&B Songs chart, peaking at number 19.<ref name="BSAdult">{{cite news | title =Adult R&B Songs| work =Billboard| url =https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-adult-r-and-b-airplay/2019-10-12| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> In August of that year, she performed a [[Tiny Desk Concerts|Tiny Desk Concert]] for [[NPR Music]].<ref name="NPR">{{cite news | last=O'Neill| first=Abby| title =Nicole Bus: Tiny Desk Concert| publisher =NPR| date =21 August 2019 | url =https://www.npr.org/2019/08/21/752808217/nicole-bus-tiny-desk-concert| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> On 18 October 2019, she released her American debut studio album, ''Kairos'', on Roc Nation.<ref name="EssenceKairos">{{cite news | last=Scott| first=Sydney| title =Roc Nation's New Artist Nicole Bus Drops Debut Album 'KAIROS'| work =Essence| date =18 October 2019 | url =https://www.essence.com/entertainment/playlist/roc-nation-nicole-bus-kairos/| accessdate =29 October 2019}}</ref> "Kairos" is the Greek word for "appointed time". The album is co-produced by Bus herself, Needlz,<ref name="RF">{{cite news | title =BREAKOUT DUTCH R&B/SOUL ARTIST NICOLE BUS ANNOUNCES NEW ALBUM KAIROS OUT OCTOBER 18TH| work =Radio Facts| date =6 October 2019 | url =https://radiofacts.com/breakout-dutch-rb-soul-artist-nicole-bus-announces-new-album-kairos-out-october-18th/| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref> and TL Cross.<ref name="YKIGS">{{cite news | title =Nicole Bus Interview: "You" Success, Signing To Roc Nation, New Album (Exclusive)| work =You Know I Got Soul| date =20 July 2019 | url =http://youknowigotsoul.com/nicole-bus-interview-you-success-signing-to-roc-nation-new-album-exclusive| accessdate =18 October 2019}}</ref>
