'''Capstan''' is an American [[post-hardcore]] band from [[Orlando, Florida]]. To date, the band has released six EPs and two studio albums. The band was formerly signed to Adventure Cat Records,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Dickman|first=Maggie|date=September 5, 2017|title=Neck Deep, Tonight Alive manager launches Adventure Cat Records, signs Capstan and Summer Wars|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/adventure_cat_records_sign_capstan_summer_wars/|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Alternative Press|language=en-US}}</ref> and is currently signed to [[Fearless Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=November 9, 2018|title=Capstan sign with Fearless Records + release "Stars Before the Sun"|url=https://substreammagazine.com/2018/11/capstan-fearless-stars-sun-2018/|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Substream Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

== History ==

=== Early years and EPs (2012–2016) ===
Capstan formed in March 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ferguson|first=Jason|title=Band of the Week: Capstan|url=https://www.orlandoweekly.com/Blogs/archives/2017/08/07/band-of-the-week-capstan|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Orlando Weekly|language=en}}</ref> On April 20, 2013, the band self-released ''Growing Deaf'', a split EP with the band Priorities.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Growing Deaf Split EP, by Capstan|url=https://capstan.bandcamp.com/album/growing-deaf-split-ep|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Capstan}}</ref> Capstan recorded their half of the EP in their house, and had it mastered by John Naclerio at NADA Studios.<ref name=":1" /> On December 2, 2014, Capstan self-released their second EP, ''Seasonal Depression''.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Seasonal Depression, by Capstan|url=https://capstan.bandcamp.com/album/seasonal-depression|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Capstan}}</ref> On November 15, 2015, the band self-released an acoustic EP, ''Parting Gifts''.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title=Parting Gifts, by Capstan|url=https://capstan.bandcamp.com/album/parting-gifts|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Capstan}}</ref> ''Parting Gifts'' was recorded and mixed at Torch & Star Studios, and was mastered at NADA Studios. 

On July 19, 2016, Capstan self-released their fourth EP, ''Cultural Divide''. The EP was well-received and got the band noticed by Michael Kaminsky of KMGMT, manager of [[Neck Deep]], [[As It Is (band)|As It Is]], [[Tonight Alive]], and more.<ref name=":0" /> At the time, Kaminsky was planning a new project, Adventure Cat Records, and signed Capstan as one of the label's first two artists.<ref name=":0" /> Kaminsky said about the band in an interview with [[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] that "Sometimes you hear an act that inspires you to do more. In this case, it was two acts, and we started a record label just to work with them."<ref name=":0" />

On January 12, 2018, Capstan released their fifth EP, ''In The Wake of Our Discord'' via Adventure Cat Records.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|title=In The Wake Of Our Discord, by Capstan|url=https://capstan.bandcamp.com/album/in-the-wake-of-our-discord|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Capstan}}</ref> The EP was produced, engineered, and mixed by Seth Henderson at Always Be Genius Studios in Chicago.<ref name=":5" /> The EP was mastered by Kris Crummett at Interlace Audio in [[Portland, Oregon]].<ref name=":5" /> In March 2018, it was announced that Capstan would be playing the Owly.FM stage at [[Vans]]' 2018 [[Warped Tour]]. Later that year, Capstan self-released ''Haunted'' on June 15, composed of two reimagined recordings of previously released songs.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|title=Haunted, by Capstan|url=https://capstan.bandcamp.com/album/haunted|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Capstan}}</ref> The EP was recorded and mixed by James Paul Wisner at Wisner Productions and mastered by Andy VanDette at Engine Room Audio.<ref name=":6" /> Soon after, in July 2018, [[Silverstein (band)|Silverstein]] announced their 15th anniversary tour for ''[[When Broken Is Easily Fixed]]'' with Capstan as one of the three opening acts.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Casteel|first=Beth|date=July 31, 2018|title=Silverstein announce anniversary tour with a surprising support act|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/silverstein-15-anniversary-tour-dates-announced-fall-2018/|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Alternative Press|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== ''Restless Heart, Keep Running'' (2019) ===
On September 20, 2019, Capstan released their debut studio album, ''Restless Heart, Keep Running'' via [[Fearless Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=September 24, 2019|title=ALBUM REVIEW: Capstan – Restless Heart, Keep Running|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/album-review-capstan-restless-heart-keep-running/|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=DEAD PRESS! {{!}} It's more than "just music" to us|language=en-GB}}</ref> The album was applauded for its blend of elements from [[post-hardcore]], [[pop punk]], and [[rock music|rock]].<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|date=September 24, 2019|title=ALBUM REVIEW: Capstan – Restless Heart, Keep Running|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/album-review-capstan-restless-heart-keep-running/|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=DEAD PRESS! {{!}} It's more than "just music" to us|language=en-GB}}</ref> Cryptic Rock reviewed the album saying, "Overall, Capstan’s debut album works to cement their place in the scene and showcases why they are here to stay. An honest piece of work, they can only continue to grow from here. Emotional and revealing, Cryptic Rock gives ''Restless Heart, Keep Runnin''g 4 out of 5 stars."<ref name=":7" /> In October 2019, Capstan embarked on a headlinining North America tour, traveling through the [[East Coast of the United States|East Coast]], [[Midwestern United States|Midwest]], and [[Toronto]].

=== ''Separate'' (2021) ===
On July 23, 2021, Capstan released their second studio album, ''Separate'', via [[Fearless Records]].<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|title=Capstan Have Announced The Details of A New Album ‘Separate’ – News|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/capstan-have-announced-the-details-of-a-new-album-separate|access-date=July 12, 2021|website=Rock Sound Magazine|language=en}}</ref>
