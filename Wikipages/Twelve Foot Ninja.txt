'''Twelve Foot Ninja''' is an Australian [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band from [[Melbourne]], Victoria. They released their debut album ''Silent Machine'' in 2012. The band consists of Kin (vocals), Russ (drums), Stevic (lead guitar), and Rohan (rhythm guitar). They won Best New Talent at the 2014 Revolver Golden Gods Awards (America's only hard rock music awards)<ref>{{cite web|title=Metal Insider - 2014 Winners of Golden Gods Awards|url=http://www.metalinsider.net/awards/and-the-winners-of-the-2014-revolver-golden-gods-awards-are|website=Metal Insider|accessdate=7 March 2016}}</ref> as well as two listener-voted awards from SiriusXM's Liquid Metal the year before. The band broke a world record for the most amount crowdfunded for a music video.<ref>[https://themusic.com.au/news/all/2013/05/03/twelve-foot-ninja-smash-crowdfunding-records/] {{dead link|date=October 2020}}</ref> Their first headline tour of Europe in April 2017 sold out five of the seven UK shows.

==History==
=== 2008–2011: Early years and early EPs===
The band formed in 2008 and released their debut EP ''New Dawn'' in 2008, resulted in the band earning a [[Triple J Unearthed]] number 1 with the track "Dark Passenger", various support slots, and an appearance at Sydney's Come Together Festival.

Their second EP, ''Smoke Bomb'', was released in November 2010. Produced by the band’s guitarist Steve "Stevic" Mackay in his Melbourne home studio, the EP was mixed by local producer/engineer Hadyn Buxton ([[Trial Kennedy]], [[Jonesez]], [[Blueline Medic]], [[Dukes of Windsor]], [[Felix Riebl]]) and mastered by [[Howie Weinberg]] ([[Deftones]], [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]], [[Jeff Buckley]]) at [[Masterdisk]], [[New York City]]. Guest musicians on the release include [[Clinton McKinnon (musician)|Bär McKinnon]] ([[Mr. Bungle]]) on Saxophone and [[Ollie McGill]] ([[Cat Empire]]) on Keyboards.

===2012–2015: ''Silent Machine'' ===
Their debut studio album, ''[[Silent Machine]]'', was released in November 2012.<ref name=Killyourstereo>{{cite web|last=H.|first=Kane|title=Journalist|url=http://www.killyourstereo.com/news/14340/twelve-foot-ninja-unleash-project-12/|publisher=Kill Your Stereo|accessdate=24 August 2012}}</ref> The album's first official single "Mother Sky" was released on 24 August and the video was premiered on Triple J's Home & Hosed blog.<ref name=Dom>{{cite web|last=Alessio|first=Dom|title=DJ|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/homeandhosed/|website=Abc.net.au|accessdate=24 August 2012}}</ref> The second single "Coming for You" was released on 2 November 2012. Each song from the debut album came with a comic, created by Keith Draws (UK), inspired by the lyrics and based on the original fable of ''Twelve Foot Ninja'' (co-written by Steve "Stevic" Mackay).<ref name=Mather>{{cite web|last=Rockbewithyou|first=Maythe|title=Author|url=http://maytherockbewithyou.com/mtrbwy/2012/08/project-12-12-tracks-12-comics-12-weeks-by-twelve-foot-ninja/|publisher=May The Rock Be With You|accessdate=24 August 2012}}</ref> The story follows a sole ninja's mission to restore balance in a world headed toward a self-inflicted apocalypse <ref name=Claire>{{cite web|last=Lothian|first=Claire|title=Owner|url=http://theaftergigguide.wordpress.com/bands/twelve-foot-ninja/|website=Aftergigguide.wordpress.com|accessdate=24 August 2012}}</ref> All the individual comics were later combined into a 72-page bound comic book.

In early 2013, Twelve Foot Ninja embarked on their first headline tour of Australia and sold out every capital city.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thebrag.com/twelve-foot-ninja-play-manning-bar/|title=Twelve Foot Ninja to Play Manning Bar|website=Thebrag.com|date=19 July 2013|accessdate=6 October 2020}}</ref> The band successfully completed a [[crowdfunding]] campaign on Australian site [[Pozible]] in May 2013 for a music video "Ain't That a Bitch" <ref name=Charles>{{cite web|last=Luzar|first=Harles|title=Twelve Foot Ninja’s Pozible-Crowdfunded, Over the Top & NSFW Music Video|url=http://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2014/02/31364-twelve-foot-ninjas-pozible-crowdfunded-top-nsfw-music-video/|publisher=Croufun Insider|accessdate=6 June 2016}}</ref> about a fantastic revenge story on an internet troll.

In October–November 2013, Twelve Foot Ninja toured Europe and North America for the first time. Their first show in London at the Camden Barfly sold out 3 weeks in advance and they also played Euroblast in Cologne, selling the most merchandise after the headliners Meshuggah. Their US/Canada tour in 2013 was opening for progressive metal band Periphery. In May 2014 Twelve Foot Ninja returned to North America for their first headline tour. They also played AEG festivals [[Rock on the Range]], [[Carolina Rebellion]], Welcome To Rockville, [[Rocklahoma]]. They were also invited to showcase at [[Michele Clark]]'s revered radio conference in San Diego [[Sunset Sessions]] Rock.

In July 2014, the single "Coming for You" was serviced to US Active Rock radio and reached number 45 on the Active Rock chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hardrockdaddy.com/tag/twelve-foot-ninja/|title=Twelve Foot Ninja Archives|website=Hardrockdaddy.com|accessdate=6 October 2020}}</ref> The video of "Coming for You" has reached over 4.1 million views on YouTube and their total channel views reached over 10 million.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/twelvefootninja|title=Twelve Foot Ninja - YouTube|website=[[YouTube]]|accessdate=6 October 2020}}</ref> The band toured the US again from October to December 2014 in support of [[In This Moment]].

===2016–2019: ''Outlier'' ===
In November 2015, Twelve Foot Ninja announced they were working on their second album for release late 2016. At that time they leaked an early track from the studio called "One Hand Killing".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/video-twelve-foot-ninja-one-hand-killing/|title=VIDEO: Twelve Foot Ninja - One Hand Killing|website=Deadpress.com|date=19 November 2015|accessdate=6 October 2020}}</ref> A few weeks later they released a video clip for the same track. The band also started a PledgeMusic campaign to help raise funds for the making of the new album and to start advance pre-orders.<ref>[http://www.pledgemusic.com/twelvefootninja] {{dead link|date=October 2020}}</ref> When talking to [[Loudwire]] vocalist Kin said "It has its roots in the ‘Silent Machine’ album but we’re kind of experimenting with a few different things. There’s more emphasis on songs and the actual songwriting as opposed to just riffs".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://loudwire.com/twelve-foot-ninja-upcoming-album-heavier-and-softer/|title=Twelve Foot Ninja Reveal Influences for Upcoming Album|website=Loudwire.com|accessdate=6 October 2020}}</ref> 

On 19 July 2016, Twelve Foot Ninja announced the title and release date of their second album, ''[[Outlier (Twelve Foot Ninja album)|Outlier]]'', released on 26 August 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicinjection.com.au/2016/07/19/twelve-foot-ninja-add-10-invincibility-with-single-and-outlier-album-release-26-august-2016/|title=TWELVE FOOT NINJA ADD +10 INVINCIBILITY WITH SINGLE AND ‘OUTLIER’ ALBUM RELEASE – 26 AUGUST 2016 |website=Musicinjection.com.au|date=19 July 2016|accessdate=6 October 2020}}</ref> ''Outlier'' debuted at #1 on the Australian 100% Independent Album chart,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.air.org.au/feature|title=Features|website=Air.org.au|accessdate=6 October 2020}}</ref> #2 on the ARIA Australian Artist Chart, and #6 on the overall ARIA chart. In the US, Outlier debuted at #15 on Billboard's Alternative Rock chart and #3 on Billboard's Heatseeker chart. The band were personally selected by [[Disturbed (band)|Disturbed]] to be their support act on the 2016 Australian tour.

In April-May 2017, Twelve Foot Ninja toured Europe for the first time as a headliner. They sold out 5 of their 7 shows in the UK with strong numbers and some sold out shows in Holland, Germany, Switzerland, Czech Republic, Spain, and Italy.{{cn|date=October 2021}} In July 2017, Twelve Foot Ninja announced their Monsoon Tour of Europe, India, and Nepal, book-ending the tour with headlining shows at Euroblast Festival in Cologne and Silence Festival in Kathmandu.{{cn|date=October 2021}}

In 2019, long-serving bassist and founding member Damon McKinnon had departed from the band.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2019/06/05/news/bassist-damon-mckinnon-exits-twelve-foot-ninja/|title=Bassist Damon McKinnon Exits Twelve Foot Ninja}}</ref>

===2020–present: ''Vengeance''===
On 27 May 2021, the band released a music video for the song "Long Way Home". It was announced that the song would feature on an upcoming album slated to be released in 2021. The second single, "Start the Fire", was released on 9 July 2021. The third single "Over and Out" was released on 9 September 2021.

In October 2021, the album ''Vengeance'' was released and debuted at number 20 on the ARIA charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.aria.com.au/charts/news/music-of-the-spheres-hands-coldplay-seventh-aria-1-album|title=Music Of The Spheres hands Coldplay seventh ARIA #1 album|website=[[ARIA Charts]]|date=22 October 2021|access-date=23 October 2021}}</ref>

==Awards and nominations==
===Revolver Golden Gods Awards===

The Revolver Golden Gods Awards was an American annual music awards ceremony that presented awards from 2009 through to 2014 for solo musicians and bands from the rock, punk and metal sectors. It was organized by the US music magazine Revolver.

Twelve Foot Ninja was *Best New Talent* Winners for Silent Machine. 2014.
