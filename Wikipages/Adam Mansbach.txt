'''Adam Mansbach''' (born July 1, 1976) is an American author. He has previously been a visiting professor of literature at [[Rutgers University-Camden]], with their New Voices Visiting Writers program (2009-2011).

== Biography ==
Mansbach graduated from [[Columbia College (New York)|Columbia College]] in 1998 and received a MFA from [[Columbia University School of the Arts]] in 2000.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=“The Youngest in the Room,” by Adam Mansbach ’98|url=https://www.college.columbia.edu/cct/latest/lions-den/-youngest-room-mansbach-98|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=July 4, 2020|website=Columbia College Today}}</ref>

Mansbach wrote the "[[Children's literature|children's book]] for adults" ''[[Go the Fuck to Sleep]]''.<ref name=harmanci>{{cite news|last=Harmanci|first=Reyhan|title='Go the F--- to Sleep': The Case of the Viral PDF|url=http://www.baycitizen.org/books/story/go-f-sleep-case-viral-pdf/|newspaper=The Bay Citizen|date=12 May 2011|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141020084505/https://www.baycitizen.org/news/books/go-f-sleep-case-viral-pdf/|archivedate=20 October 2014}}</ref>  Other books Mansbach has written include ''[[Angry Black White Boy]]'', a [[San Francisco Chronicle]] Best Book of 2005,<ref name="borntousemics">{{cite book|author1=Dyson, Michael Eric|author2=Daulatzai, Sohail|title=Born to Use Mics|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xPLbK6ts2e4C&pg=PT170|accessdate=July 8, 2013|year=2010|publisher=Basic Civitas Books|ISBN=978-0-465-00211-5|page=292}}</ref> and ''[[The End of the Jews]]''<ref>{{cite book | last = Mansbach | first = Adam | title = The end of the Jews : a novel | publisher = Spiegel & Grau | location = New York | year = 2008 | isbn = 0-385-52044-1 }}</ref> (for which he won the [[California Book Award#California Book Awards|California Book Award]] for fiction in 2008).<ref name=nyt>{{cite news|last=Harmanci|first=Reyhan|title=A Whim, A Book, And, Wow!|accessdate=13 May 2011|newspaper=New York Times|date=28 April 2011|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/04/29/us/29bcbart.html}}</ref><ref name="california club">{{cite web|title=The California Book Award Winners 1931-2006 |url=http://www.commonwealthclub.org/sites/default/files/CABookAwardWinners_ByLastName.pdf |publisher=[[Commonwealth Club of California]] |accessdate=13 May 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110926235116/http://www.commonwealthclub.org/sites/default/files/CABookAwardWinners_ByLastName.pdf |archivedate=26 September 2011 }}</ref> Mansbach was the founding editor of the 1990s hip-hop journal ''Elementary''.<ref name=borntousemics/>

His book ''[[Stay the Fuck at Home]]'' (2020), was written to support awareness of [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United States|coronavirus self-isolation measures]]; it has yet to be formally published. The book was read on ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live]]'' by actor [[Samuel L. Jackson]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Parker |first1=Ryan |title=Samuel L. Jackson Reads 'Stay the F---' at Home' to Stress Isolation Amid Pandemic |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/samuel-l-jackson-reads-stay-fk-at-home-stress-isolation-pandemic-1287856 |accessdate=1 April 2020 |work=The Hollywood Reporter |date=1 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Kirkland |first1=Justin |title=Samuel L. Jackson Reading Stay the F**k at Home Is a Gentle Reminder to Stay the F**k At Home |url=https://www.esquire.com/entertainment/tv/a32005295/samuel-jackson-stay-the-fuck-at-home-video-jimmy-kimmel/ |accessdate=1 April 2020 |work=Esquire |publisher=Hearst Digital Media |date=1 April 2020}}</ref>

He lives in [[Berkeley, California]] and co-hosts a radio show, "Father Figures".<ref name="about">{{cite web|title=Adam Mansbach's biography page|url=http://www.adammansbach.com/about.html |accessdate=26 January 2013| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110707082723/http://adammansbach.com/about.html| archivedate= 7 July 2011 | url-status= live}}</ref><ref name="Father Figures">{{cite web| title= Father Figures KPFA Berkeley| url= http://www.kpfa.org/short-run-series/father-figures| accessdate= 26 January 2013| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20120517183655/http://www.kpfa.org/short-run-series/father-figures| archivedate= 2012-05-17| url-status= dead}}</ref>
