'''Rosalind Barber'''<ref name="auto">{{Cite web |title=The Calvin & Rose G Hoffman Prize winners |url=https://www.kings-school.co.uk/news/the-calvin-rose-g-hoffman-prize-winners/ |website=[[The King's School, Canterbury]] |accessdate=6 January 2020 |language=en |date=13 December 2018}}</ref> (born 1964) is an English novelist and poet.<ref>[http://www.forwardartsfoundation.org/poet/ros-barber/ Forward Arts. Retrieved 27 October 2019.]</ref> She is also a university lecturer in English, who supports the view that [[Marlovian theory of Shakespeare authorship|Christopher Marlowe wrote Shakespeare]].

==Academia==
As of 2021, Barber lectures in the Department of English and Comparative Literature at [[Goldsmiths, University of London]].<ref>[https://www.gold.ac.uk/ecl/staff/barber-ros/ Goldsmiths page. Retrieved 27 October 2019.]</ref> She has a BSc in Biology, an MA in creative writing, the arts and education, and a PhD in English literature, all from the [[University of Sussex]]. She also has an Open University BA in English literature and philosophy.<ref>[https://www.gold.ac.uk/ecl/staff/barber-ros/ Goldsmiths College site. Retrieved 19 September 2019.]</ref>

Barber has worked as a computer programmer.<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Masters |first1=Tim |title=Author faced 'hostility' over book |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-23100252 |accessdate=2 January 2020 |work=BBC |date=28 June 2013}}</ref> She won the [[Hoffman Prize]] in 2011, 2014 and 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |title=THE MARLOWE PAPERS by Ros Barber {{!}} Kirkus Reviews |url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/ros-barber/marlowe-papers/ |publisher=[[Kirkus Reviews]] |accessdate=6 January 2020 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Hoffman Prize Winners |url=http://www.marlowe-society.org/hoffman-prize-winners/ |website=The Marlowe Society |accessdate=6 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="auto"/>

==Novels==
Barber's first novel, ''[[The Marlowe Papers]]'' (2012), is written in [[blank verse]]. She subscribes to the [[Marlovian theory of Shakespeare authorship]].<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Nicholl |first1=Charles |authorlink1=Charles Nicholl (author) |title=Exiting the Stage |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/01/27/books/review/the-marlowe-papers-by-ros-barber.html |accessdate=30 October 2019 |work=The New York Times |date=25 January 2013 |quote=Dr. Barber is a "Marlovian" not only in the generic and beneficial sense of being an admirer of Marlowe, but in the more specific and, some will say, more tiresome sense of being a believer in the theory that Marlowe wrote the plays of Shakespeare.}}</ref> In the book, Marlowe's death is a ruse and he writes plays in Shakespeare's name. The book won the [[Desmond Elliott Prize]]<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-23080170 |title=The Marlowe Papers wins Desmond Elliott Prize |last=Masters |first=Tim |date=2013-06-27 |work=BBC News |access-date=2017-03-11 |language=en-GB}}</ref> and the [[Authors' Club First Novel Award]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://poetrysociety.org.uk/poets/ros-barber/ |title=Ros Barber – The Poetry Society |website=poetrysociety.org.uk |language=en-GB |access-date=2017-03-11}}</ref> Her second novel, ''Devotion'' (2015),<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Feay |first1=Suzi |title=Devotion by Ros Barber review – the conflict between religion and science |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2015/aug/19/devotion-ros-barber-review |accessdate=6 January 2020 |work=The Guardian |date=19 August 2015}}</ref> was shortlisted for the [[Encore Award]].<ref name="auto1">{{Cite book |last1=McLoughlin |first1=Nigel |title=The Portable Poetry Workshop |date=2016 |publisher=[[Macmillan Publishers]] |isbn=978-1-137-60596-2 |pages=viii |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=pyOHDQAAQBAJ&q=barber+devotion+%22Encore+Award%22&pg=PR8 |accessdate=6 January 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

Barber made an appearance at the [[Brighton Fringe]] in 2012.<ref>[http://rosbarber.com/wordjam-at-brighton-fringe/ Author's page. Retrieved 19 September 2019.]</ref><ref>[https://www.rlf.org.uk/fellowships/ros-barber/ Royal Literary Fund. Retrieved 19 September 2019.]</ref> She and [[Nicola Haydn]] wrote a one-man stage adaptation of ''The Marlowe Papers'' performed in 2016.<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Hall |first1=Duncan |title=The Marlowe Papers, Otherplace At The Basement, Kensington Street, Brighton, until Saturday, January 29, call 01273 987516 |url=https://www.theargus.co.uk/leisure/critic/14238788.the-marlowe-papers-otherplace-at-the-basement-kensington-street-brighton-until-saturday-january-29-call-01273-987516/ |access-date=30 September 2021 |work=[[The Argus (Brighton)]] |date=29 January 2016 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last1=Barber |first1=Ros |title=Further Developments with The Marlowe Papers |url=https://rosbarber.com/further-developments-with-the-marlowe-papers/ |website=Ros Barber |access-date=30 September 2021 |date=12 February 2016}}</ref>

==Poetry==
Of Barber's three volumes of poetry, ''Material'' (2008) was a [[Poetry Book Society]] recommendation.<ref name="auto1"/> Its title poem, which also appears in the [[Faber and Faber|Faber]] anthology ''Poems of the Decade'' (2015), was in England's school sixth-form syllabus as of 2017.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://qualifications.pearson.com/content/dam/pdf/News/A%20level%20news/Amendment-letter-a-level-english-literature-poem-of-decade.pdf |title=Amendment to GCE AS and A level English Literature, Prescribed texts – Poems of the Decade |date=2017-01-04 |website=pearson.com |publisher=Pearson |access-date=2017-11-13}}</ref>
