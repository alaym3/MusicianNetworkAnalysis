'''Monali Thakur''' (born 3 November 1985) is an Indian singer and actress. She is the recipient of a [[National Film Awards|National Film Award]], [[Filmfare Award]] and many more awards. Thakur won the [[National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] for the song "Moh Moh Ke Dhaage" from the film ''[[Dum Laga Ke Haisha]]'' (2015) and the [[Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] for the song "Sawaar Loon" from the Film "[[Lootera]]" (2013). She was a judge on Zee TV's ''[[Sa Re Ga Ma Pa L'il Champs 2014]]''. She was also an "expert" on Colors TV's ''Rising Star'' for two consecutive seasons.<ref name="national">{{cite web|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/monali-thakur-realised-actual-gravity-of-national-award-through-others-reaction-2781027/|title=Monali thank realised actual gravity of national award through others reaction|access-date=7 July 2017|date=2 May 2016}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Thakur was born on 3 November 1985 in a [[Bengalis|Bengali]] musical family in [[Kolkata]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Tiwari |first1=Akanksha |title=फेमस सिंगर मोनाली ठाकुर को इस गाने ने बनाया था फेमस |url=https://www.newsnationtv.com/entertainment/bollywood/singer-monali-thakur-birthday-film-race-song-changed-monali-thakur-life-164489.html |access-date=3 November 2020 |work=News Nation |date=3 November 2020 |language=hi}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Happy Birthday Monali Thakur: Playlist of Her Best Songs |url=https://www.news18.com/news/lifestyle/happy-birthday-monali-thakur-playlist-of-her-best-songs-3034754.html |access-date=3 November 2020 |work=News18 |date=3 November 2020 |language=en}}</ref> Her father [[Shakti Thakur]] was a professional singer and actor in Bengali film industry and her sister Mehuli Thakur is a playback singer in Bengal.<ref name=pavithran>Pavithran, Eva. "[http://www.verveonline.com/61/people/monali.shtml Oomph In Her Voice]", Verve magazine, Volume 16, Issue 5, May 2008.</ref><ref name=telly_chakkar>"[http://www.tellychakkar.com/y2k6/feb/7feb/news_idol.php Monali Thakur out of Idol race!] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080620075057/http://www.tellychakkar.com/y2k6/feb/7feb/news_idol.php |date=20 June 2008 }}", Telly Chakkar, 7 February 2006.</ref> She has learnt [[hip-hop dance|hip-hop]] and [[Bharatanatyam]] and is also a trained [[Salsa (dance)|salsa]] dancer.<ref name="indiatimes1">{{cite web|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2013-07-12/news-and-interviews/40535357_1_monali-thakur-amit-trivedi-lootera |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203051824/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2013-07-12/news-and-interviews/40535357_1_monali-thakur-amit-trivedi-lootera |url-status=dead |archive-date=3 December 2013 |title=Singing is like acting: Monali Thakur |date=12 July 2013 |work=[[The Times of India]] |access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/dance-helps-me-rid-my-inhibitions-monali-thakur-2793273/ |title=Dance helps me rid my inhibitions: Monali Thakur |publisher=The Indian Express |date=10 May 2016 |access-date=11 August 2020}}</ref>

An alumnus of [[St. Xavier's College, Kolkata]],<ref>{{cite news |title=All about alumni |url=https://www.telegraphindia.com/entertainment/all-about-alumni/cid/506645 |access-date=14 January 2021 |work=The Telegraph |date=28 January 2009}}</ref> Thakur started singing in school and college competitions and performing at local functions,<ref name=shruti>Shruti, I.L. "[http://www.deccanherald.com/CONTENT/Aug262008/metro-tue2008082586512.asp Katrina liked my voice] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080924032505/http://www.deccanherald.com/Content/Aug262008/metro-tue2008082586512.asp |date=24 September 2008 }}", The Deccan Herald, 26 August 2008.</ref> and received the Best Playback Singer award at the [[Anandalok Awards]] for the serial ''Sri Ramkrishna'' when she was 14.<ref name="telegraphindia1">{{cite web|author=Das|first=Abhijit|date=4 January 2007|title=Monali Thakur---Biography---erasedwords.com|url=https://www.erasedwords.com/2020/06/monali-thakur-biography.html|access-date=14 June 2020|publisher=erasedwords.com}}</ref> She rose to popularity after placing ninth in ''[[Indian Idol (season 2)|Indian Idol 2]]''.<ref name="dnasyndication1">{{cite web |url=http://dnasyndication.com/dna/article/DNPUN18807 |title=Language no bar – DNA – English News & Features – Lifestyle & Leisure |publisher=dnasyndication.com< |date=15 September 2009 |access-date=29 November 2013 |archive-date=24 December 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171224214056/http://dnasyndication.com/dna/article/DNPUN18807 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

She had to struggle even after ''[[Indian Idol]]'' to get a strong foothold in the music industry. She got an offer from music director [[Pritam Chakraborty]] to sing two songs for the Bollywood film ''[[Race (2008 film)|Race]]'' in 2008 – "Khwab Dekhe" (Sexy Lady) and "Zara Zara Touch Me".<ref name="dnasyndication1"/> She was originally scheduled to sing only one song, but her first recording impressed the film's directors [[Abbas–Mustan]] enough for them to book her a second.<ref name=pavithran /> "Zara Zara Touch Me" was enormously successful, becoming the fourth-most-played song on Indian radio during the first half of 2008.<ref name=aircheck>"[http://www.exchange4media.com/E4M/RADIO/radionews.asp?section_id=7&news_id=31630&tag=26543 And the most popular songs playing on radio are...; AirCheck India takes stock] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110720205013/http://www.exchange4media.com/E4M/RADIO/radionews.asp?section_id=7&news_id=31630&tag=26543 |date=20 July 2011 }}", exchange4media News Service, 5 July 2008</ref> She also got nominated for an [[IIFA Award for Best Female Playback]] and an [[Apsara Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] for the song "Zara Zara Touch Me". Monali's first large format live concert was at Powai Sarvajanin Durgotsav in 2011.{{citation needed|date=July 2018}}

She was a judge in the Bengali music reality show ''Bengali Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Li'l Champs'' for two years.<ref name="indiatimes1"/><ref name="indianexpress1">{{cite web|url=http://www.indianexpress.com/news/pep---fizz/675895/0 |title=Pep & Fizz |publisher=Indian Express |date=3 September 2010 |access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref> She also appeared in the Bengali show ''Key Hobe Biggest Fan''<ref name="indianexpress1"/> and was part of ''[[Coke Studio (India)|Coke Studio]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cokestudioindia.com/season2/monali.html?WT.cl=1&WT.mn=Monali%20Thakur |title=Monali Thakur |publisher=Coke Studio India |access-date=29 November 2013}}</ref>

In 2014, Thakur won the [[Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] for the song "Sawaar Loon" from the film ''[[Lootera]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=59th Idea Filmfare Awards: Winners|url=http://photogallery.indiatimes.com/awards/filmfare-awards/59th-idea-filmfare-awards/59th-idea-filmfare-awards-winners/articleshow/29339572.cms|website=The Times of India|access-date=11 June 2016|date=24 January 2014}}</ref> In March 2016, she received the [[National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] for the song "Moh Moh Ke Dhaage" from the film ''[[Dum Laga Ke Haisha]]''<ref>{{cite web|title=Monali Thakur realised 'actual gravity' of National award through others' reaction|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/monali-thakur-realised-actual-gravity-of-national-award-through-others-reaction-2781027/|website=The Indian Express|access-date=11 June 2016|date=2 May 2016}}</ref> and earned a nomination at the Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer for the same.<ref>{{cite web|title=Nominations for the 61st Britannia Filmfare Awards|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/news/Nominations-for-the-61st-Britannia-Filmfare-Awards/articleshow/50542166.cms|website=The Times of India|access-date=11 June 2016|date=12 January 2016}}</ref> In 2018, Thakur appeared in the [[Zee5]] show ''Lockdown'' along with [[Mickey Singh]] which was produced by [[Badshah (rapper)]]'s production house Afterhours. They recreated two tracks, "Jiya Jale" and "Gur Nalon Ishq Meetha".<ref>{{cite news |title=Badshah nervous about stepping into production |url=https://www.indiatvnews.com/entertainment/celebrities-badshah-nervous-about-stepping-into-production-446701 |access-date=7 November 2020 |work=www.indiatvnews.com |date=11 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=ZEE5 launches Lockdown with Badshah, Kailash Kher, Raftaar, Jonita Gandhi and many more |url=https://zeenews.india.com/television/zee5-launches-lockdown-with-badshah-kailash-kher-raftaar-jonita-gandhi-and-many-more-2133845.html |access-date=7 November 2020 |work=Zee News |date=17 August 2018 |language=en}}</ref> Apart from these Monali has also sung several hits like "Cham Cham" song from 2016 film ''[[Baaghi (2016 film)|Baaghi]]'' which has garnered more than 800 Million views on YouTube as of October 2020 and The Title Track of 2018 film "[[Badrinath Ki Dulhania]]" which she sung along with "[[Neha Kakkar]]", "[[Dev Negi]]" and "[[Ikka Singh]], has been watched more than 700 Million times on Youtube as of October 2020. Thakur has also released her singles, "Tamanna" (2018), "Shy Mora Saiyaan" (2018), "O Re Naseeba" (2019), "Pani Pani Re" (2019), "Dugga Elo" (2019) and her latest Single is "Dil Ka Fitoor" (2020). In 2020 Thakur has sung 2 more singles: "Aaina" along with Ranajoy Bhattacharjee and "Elo Maa Dugga Thakur" along with ''[[Sonu Nigam]]''.

==Discography==

===Early years (2000-2012)===

Monali started her career in 2000 with the song 'Sundar Kato Sundar' from the Bengali movie [[Sajoni Aamar Sohag]], sung along with [[Kavita Krishnamurti]], [[Goutam Ghose]] and Pratik Choudhary. But this didn't gave her recognition. She become known after participating in second season of Singing Reality show [[Indian Idol]] in 2005, She was eliminated very early and stood at 9th position. About her experience with Indian Idol, which ran for up to six months, Thakur said that she was mostly nervous the entire time. "I would get severe anxiety attacks while singing on stage and I wish I had more fun at the time".However, she opened up about the impact of participating in and winning these shows: "You win good money and it gives you face value but you can only capitalise it for no more than a couple of months. The real war starts afterwards once you start working bit by bit".<ref name="auto2">{{cite web|url=https://scroll.in/reel/879152/monali-thakur-interview-reality-shows-gives-you-face-value-but-the-real-war-starts-later|title=Monali Thakur interview: 'Reality shows gives you face value but the real war starts later'|first=Devarsi|last=Ghosh|website=Scroll.in}}</ref>

After this, She got her Bollywood Debut song 'Kubool Kar le' in the 2006 film [[Jaan-E-Mann]], though this was first song as a playback singer, composed by Indian Idol judge [[Anu Malik]] in 2006, but she considers her real breakthrough to be the superhit Zara Zara Touch Me, by music director [[Pritam]] in the 2008 film [[Race (2008 film)|Race]].<ref name="auto2"/> she sang two songs, 'Khwab Dekhe-Sexy Lady' and 'Zara Zara Touch Me', which was enormously successful. She was originally scheduled to sing only 'Zara Zara Touch Me', but later sung both. According to her: "Ramesh ji and Kumar ji [the producers of Race] had signed me up for an album for Tips at a time independent albums had become extinct, but they liked my voice, and Pritam got me to sing for a scratch recording. Abbas-Mustan [the directors] liked it, and that is how I got the other song, Khwab Dekhe, too".<ref name="auto2"/> Joginder Tuteja of [[Bollywood Hungama]] picked it along with 'pehli Nazar Mein' as the biggest hits of the album and praised the song calling it fresh, naughty and seductive.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.filmibeat.com/music/reviews/2008/race-music-review-290108.html|title=Race Music Review|first=By : Joginder Tuteja|last=Indiafm|date=29 January 2008|work=Filmi Beat|access-date=15 April 2021}}</ref> Zara Zara Touch Me became one of the year's biggest chartbusters<ref name="auto2"/> That year, She also sung the song 'Meow' with [[Suzanne D'Mello]] composed by again [[Pritam]]. Also, she performed the title track of Bengali Series [[Ekhane Aakash Neel]] which is an Official Remake of [[StarPlus]] [[Sanjivani (2002 TV series)]]. In 2009, her first song was 'Khudaya khair' from [[Billu]] which she co-sung with [[Soham Chakrabarty]]. The song received mixed reviews from critics but Monali was praised for her sweet vocals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://planetbollywood.com/displayReview.php?id=m010709110436|title=Billu Barber - music review by Atta Khan - Planet Bollywood|website=planetbollywood.com}}</ref> Her other songs that year included 'Do Dil Hai Janwa', 'Hai Junoon' and some Bengali tracks like 2 songs, 'Prithibi Onek Boro' and 'Kalke Jilam Jajaber' in [[Love (2008 Bengali film)]] and 3 songs in [[Dujone]].

In 2010, she sang the song 'Dilrubaon ke Jalwe' in the film [[Dulha Mil Gaya]] with [[Amit Kumar]].  Shot on [[Shah Rukh Khan]] and [[Sushmita Sen]], the song met with positive reviews where [[Bollywood Hungama]] praised her completely changed vocals compared to her previous songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bollywoodhungama.com/movie/dulha-mil-gaya/songs/music-critic-review/|title=Dulha Mil Gaya Music Review - Bollywood Hungama|first=Bollywood|last=Hungama}}</ref> Next, she sang a solo track 'Ishq Mein' in [[Prince (2010 film)]]. The song was praised where critics called it upbeat and her vocals seductive.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bollywoodhungama.com/movie/prince-2/songs/music-critic-review/|title=Prince Music Review - Bollywood Hungama|first=Bollywood|last=Hungama}}</ref> That year, she also sang 'Anjaana Anjaani ki Kahani' from [[Anjaana Anjaani]]. The refreshing song composed by [[Vishal & Shekhar]] along with the whole album received huge critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/music/music-review-robot-anjaana-anjaani/story-aOKS7u2Z99peGMWFJXEHtJ.html|title=Music review: Robot, Anjaana Anjaani|date=15 September 2010|website=Hindustan Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bollywoodhungama.com/movie/anjaana-anjaani/songs/music-critic-review/#:~:text=Anjaana%20Anjaani%20is%20a%20fantastic,show%20in%20a%20big%20way |title=Anjaana Anjaani Music Review |publisher=Bollywood Hungama |date=2010-10-01 |access-date=2021-03-17}}</ref> After a series of Bengali songs, like 'It's Only Pyaar', 'O Yaara Ve', 'Bol Na Aar' from [[Dui Prithibi (2010 film)]], 3 Tracks in [[Kellafate]], and many more os such, in 2011, she sang songs like 'Naina', 'Ayo Manaye Jashn','Tik Tuk' from [[Rascals (2011 film)|Rascals]], 'Give it up' from [[United Six]], 'Haal-e-dil' from [[Bbuddah... Hoga Terra Baap]] composed by [[Vishal & Shekhar]], co-sung with [[Amitabh Bachchan]]. She also lend her voice to 3 songs, 'Ke Se', 'O Shona Eshona' and 'Mon Banjara' to 2011 Bengali film [[Fighter (2011 film)|Fighter]]. This was followed by some more Bengali tracks.

In 2012, she sang 'Tu Mohabbat Hai' from [[Tere Naal Love Ho Gaya]] with [[Atif Aslam]], composed by [[Sachin-Jigar]], the song received critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bollywoodhungama.com/movie/tere-naal-love-ho-gaya/songs/music-critic-review/|title=Tere Naal Love Ho Gaya Music Review - Bollywood Hungama|first=Bollywood|last=Hungama}}</ref> Apart from other songs that year, she sang 'Aga Bai' along with [[Shalmali Kholgade]] from the movie [[Aiyyaa]]. The song composed by [[Amit Trivedi]] was a hit and had favourable reviews where [[Filmfare]] called it a 'Fast-paced Disco Number'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.filmfare.com/reviews/music-review-aiyyaa-1431.html|title=Music Review: Aiyyaa|website=filmfare.com}}</ref> About the song she said: "I was feeling incomplete at the time as I had so much to offer, So, I would complain to Amit [Trivedi], saying what was the point of doing riyaaz [practice] for 10-12 hours if I cannot use it. Then, he asked me to sing a thumri in Aga Bai, parts of which had already been sung by Shalmali Kholgade".<ref name="auto2"/> Among others, She also Sang a Bengali Track 'Shadow Tales' in the film [[Aparajita Tumi]].Composed by [[Shantanu Moitra]], the song was appreciated by critics.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicaloud.com/2012/01/18/aparajita-tumi-music-review-bengali-movie-soundtrack/|title=Aparajita Tumi – Music Review (Bengali Movie Soundtrack)|date=19 January 2012}}</ref>

==='Sawaar Loon' and Bollywood film debut as an actress (2013-2014)===

In 2013, Thakur sang 'Sawaar Loon', a melodious track composed by [[Amit Trivedi]] from the film [[Lootera]]. The song met with highly positive reviews, [[Filmfare]] wrote, "Monali Thakur instills sugar in our ears, the '50s orchestration, coupled with the perfect pitch of the singer manages to take you back to a bygone era",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.filmfare.com/features/music-review-lootera-3569.html|title=Music Review: Lootera|website=filmfare.com}}</ref> while Mohar Basu of [[Koimoi]] wrote, "A romantic ballad out of the 50s, the song sung by Monali Thakur is astonishingly wonderful. Thakur's heart melting voice is gripping and Amit Trivedi's tunes and [[Amitabh Bhattacharya]]'s heart wrenching lyrics skillfully weaves a wonderful tapestry of music that we can call sheer finesse. The playful drift of Thakur's voice, is abundant in naivety making the song soulfully innocent".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.koimoi.com/bollywood-movies/music-reviews/lootera-music-review-2/|title=Lootera Music Review|date=30 May 2013}}</ref> The song won her first [[Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://photogallery.indiatimes.com/awards/filmfare-awards/59th-idea-filmfare-awards/59th-idea-filmfare-awards-winners/articleshow/29339572.cms|title=Monali Thakur won the Best Playback Singer (Female) Award for her song Sawaar loon (Lootera) from Usha Uthup and Mohit Chauhan at the 59th Idea Filmfare Awards 2013, held at the Yash Raj Studios in Mumbai, on January 24, 2014.|website=photogallery.indiatimes.com}}</ref> Her next releases that year were 'Hip Hop Pammi' from [[Ramaiya Vastavaiya]] sung with [[Mika Singh]], 'Muh Meetha Kara De' from [[Rabba Main Kya Karoon]], 'Laila' from [[Nasha (film)|Nasha]]. Also, she sang the title track of [[I Don't Luv U]] along with Neuman Pinto, the singer's voice was praised, though the song was not.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.koimoi.com/most-popular/i-dont-luv-u-music-review/|title=I Don't Luv U Music Review|date=25 April 2013}}</ref> 'Raghupati Raghav' from [[Krrish 3]] was her next release, co-sung with [[Neeraj Shridhar]] and composed by [[Rajesh Roshan]], the song received praise where singers were applauded for energetic vocals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.koimoi.com/bollywood-movies/music-reviews/krrish-3-music-review/|title=Krrish 3 Music Review|date=19 September 2013}}</ref> Her Next releases that year were 2 tracks from the movie [[I, Me Aur Main]], 'Darbadar' in which she collaborated again with [[Sachin-Jigar]] and 'Meri Jaaniye' performed alongside [[Shaan (singer)|Shaan]]. while the former, a classical touch song, the latter is a Romantic Number with a Western Touch. Both of them were well appreciated by critics where, Suparna Thombare of 'Bollywood Life' stated about 'Darbadar' that "The ektara, clap sounds and Monali Thakur's silken voice lead you into this Sufi number that slowly zooms into a high energy song. Monali gets the chance to sing a semi-classical number for the first time and she truly shines,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bollywoodlife.com/news-gossip/i-me-aur-main-music-review-a-mix-of-instant-hits-and-mushy-numbers-193369/|title=I, Me Aur Main music review: A mix of instant hits and mushy numbers!|date=25 February 2013|website=Bollywood Life}}</ref> Joginder Tuteja of [[Bollywood Hungama]] stated about 'Meri Jaaniye' "It is good to see Monali wear a different hat for this one and step into a Western avtar".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bollywoodhungama.com/movie/i-me-aur-main/songs/music-critic-review/|title=I, Me Aur Main Music Review - Bollywood Hungama|first=Bollywood|last=Hungama}}</ref> She performed a female solo 'Laila' in [[Nasha (film)|Nasha]], composed by musical duo [[Sangeet-Siddharth]], the song was well received and called a sweet one.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ndtv.com/entertainment/music-review-nasha-634062|title=Music review: Nasha|website=NDTV.com}}</ref> That year also, she sang some Bengali tracks.

In 2014, Thakur made her film debut with [[Lakshmi (2014 film)|Lakshmi]] dealing with the harsh realities of human trafficking and child prostitution, which continues behind closed curtains in rural areas of India. Directed by [[Nagesh Kukunoor]], the film did not perform well and received mixed reviews by critics but Thakur's performance was praised.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/movie-reviews/movie-review-by-anupama-chopra-lakshmi-is-effective-but-brutally-disturbing/story-AZM3O3g1wGeZfUzVz6XpSJ.html|title=Movie review by Anupama Chopra: Lakshmi is effective but brutally disturbing|date=22 March 2014|website=Hindustan Times}}</ref> She won Best Actress Award at the South-Asian International Film Festival in Washington DC, making her the first Bollywood singer to have won.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rediff.com/movies/report/lakshmi-star-monali-thakur-wins-best-actress-award-in-washington/20140919.htm|title=Lakshmi star Monali Thakur wins Best Actress award in Washington|first=Subhash K.|last=Jha|website=Rediff}}</ref> That year, She also Collaborated with [[Bappi Lahiri]] for the Bangla version of the song 'Tune Mari Entriyan' from the film [[Gunday]]. The song was a chartbuster in both Hindi and Bangla. Mitesh Saraf of 'Plannet Bollywood' appreciated the song "Bappi Lahiri and Monali Thakur takes centre stage and both does really well in maintaining the entertainment quotient"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://planetbollywood.com/displayReview.php?id=m011614063234|title=Gunday - music review by Mitesh Saraf - Planet Bollywood|website=planetbollywood.com}}</ref> while, [[Rediff.com]] stated "Monali Thakur goes all out to make the best use of the platform provided to her. A chartbuster all the way".<ref>{{cite web | last=Tuteja | first=Joginder | title=Music review: Gunday has entertaining songs | website=Rediff | date=2014-01-26 | url=https://www.rediff.com/movies/review/music-review-gunday-has-entertaining-songs/20140126.htm | access-date=2021-03-17}}</ref> She provided English Vocals in the song 'Love Me Thoda Aur' sung by [[Arijit Singh]] for the film [[Yaariyan (2014 film)]]. During the year, She voiced for some other songs, 'Sweety' from [[Bobby Jasoos]] performed alongside [[Aishwarya Nigam]] and composed by [[Shantanu Moitra]], the song received mixed reviews,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.koimoi.com/bollywood-movies/music-reviews/bobby-jasoos-music-review/|title=Bobby Jasoos Music Review|date=18 June 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bollywoodlife.com/news-gossip/bobby-jasoos-music-review-shantanu-moitras-compositions-for-vidya-balans-detective-film-fail-to-impress-359039/|title=Bobby Jasoos music review: Shantanu Moitra's compositions for Vidya Balan's detective film fail to impress!|date=14 June 2014|website=Bollywood Life}}</ref> the Title Track of [[Karle Pyaar Karle]] co-sung with [[Benny Dayal]], the reviews for the songs were fair.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/music/music-reviews/karle-pyaar-karles-music-review/articleshow/28938981.cms|title=Music review: Karle Pyaar Karle - Times of India|website=The Times of India}}</ref>

==='Moh Moh Ke Dhaage' and further hits (2015-2017)===
She began the new year with a song 'Turram Khan' in [[Hawaizaada]], co-sung by [[Papon (singer)|Papon]] and [[Ayushmann Khurrana]]. 'Karthik' of Milliblog stated, "superbly led by Papon, till Ayushmann Khurrana and Monali Thakur interject later in the song with a lively melodic distraction".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://milliblog.com/2015/01/19/hawaizaada-music-review-hindi-rochak-kohli-mangesh-dhakde-ayushmann-khurrana-vishal-bhardwaj/|title=Hawaizaada (Music review), Hindi – Rochak Kohli, Mangesh Dhakde, Ayushmann Khurrana & Vishal Bhardwaj by Milliblog!}}</ref> After it, She sung 3 Bengali songs of which 2, 'Ure Geche' and 'Tumi Aashe Paashe' composed by [[Indraadip Dasgupta]] from the film [[Parbona Ami Chartey Tokey]], 1 song 'Kono Ek Nilchee Pari' from  [[Black (2015 Bengali film)|Black]].

This year turned out to be again very successful for her when she sung a semi-classical melody 'Moh Moh Ke Dhaage' from the film [[Dum Laga Ke Haisha]] starring [[Ayushmann Khurrana]], [[Bhumi Pednekar]] and Composed by [[Anu Malik]]. A separate male version of the song is sung by [[Papon (singer)|Papon]]. According to her, "It doesn’t happen often that in the world of EDMs and Hiphop Bollywood numbers one gets a chance to sing evergreen rich Indian melody, hence I can’t thank Anu Ji enough to trust me as a singer with this beautiful song "Moh Moh Ke Dhaage" , thus keeping a promise he made years back to me in a reality show where I was just a contestant".<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://www.india.com/entertainment/moh-moh-ke-dhaage-singer-monali-thakur-discusses-her-success-story-316460/|title=Moh Moh Ke Dhaage singer Monali Thakur discusses her success story!|first=India com Entertainment|last=Desk|date=14 March 2015|website=India News, Breaking News &#124; India.com}}</ref> The Song became a Chartbuster and was Highly appreciated by Audience as well as Critics.<ref name="auto"/> Prateek Sur of 'Bollywood Life' commented, "Monali impresses with her melodic vocals".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bollywoodlife.com/news-gossip/dum-laga-ke-haisha-music-review-anu-malik-brings-back-the-romance-of-the-1990s-for-this-ayushmann-khurrana-starrer-475966/|title=Dum Laga Ke Haisha music review: Anu Malik brings back the romance of the 1990s for this Ayushmann Khurrana starrer|date=25 February 2015|website=Bollywood Life}}</ref> The song won her several awards including [[National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] at the [[63rd National Film Awards]],<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/monali-thakur-realised-actual-gravity-of-national-award-through-others-reaction-2781027/|title=Monali Thakur realised 'actual gravity' of National award through others' reaction|date=2 May 2016}}</ref> [[IIFA Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] at [[17th IIFA Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/topic/Monali-Thakur/awards|title=Monali Thakur Awards: List of awards and nominations received by Monali Thakur &#124; Times of India Entertainment|website=timesofindia.indiatimes.com}}</ref> Also, she was nominated for [[Filmfare Award for Best Female Playback Singer]] for the same. The composer Anu Malik as well as lyricist of the song [[Varun Grover (writer)|Varun Grover]] won several awards. Varun won [[National Film Award for Best Lyrics]] for it.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.news18.com/news/movies/bahut-achcha-hai-par-slow-hai-national-award-winning-lyricist-varun-grover-recalls-initial-reactions-to-moh-moh-ke-dhaage-1222145.html|title='Bahut achcha hai, par slow hai': National Award winning lyricist Varun Grover recalls initial reactions to 'Moh Moh Ke Dhaage'|date=28 March 2016|website=News18}}</ref> As of 2021, the lyrical video of the song has garnered more than 130 Million views on YouTube. On winning prestigious National Award, Thakur says, "This gives a lot of happiness when you know that you have won an award or for that matter that you have been nominated for your work. I realised the actual gravity of this award when I saw the reactions of family and friends".<ref name="auto1"/>
