{{nihongo|'''Nogizaka46'''|乃木坂46|extra=Nogizaka Forty-six}} is a [[Japanese idol|Japanese female idol]] group produced by [[Yasushi Akimoto]], created as the {{nihongo|official rival|公式ライバル|kōshiki raibaru}} of the group [[AKB48]]. They are the first group from the [[Sakamichi Series]], which also includes sister groups [[Sakurazaka46]] (formerly Keyakizaka46), [[Yoshimotozaka46]], and [[Hinatazaka46]].

The group's musical catalogue includes twenty-eight singles and five compilation albums. Starting with their third single, each of Nogizaka46's single releases have reached the top position on the weekly Oricon chart, and each single since "[[Influencer (song)|Influencer]]" has sold at least one million copies. The group has also created radio shows, theater productions, television programs, and films, including ''[[NogiBingo!]]'', ''[[Hatsumori Bemars]]'', ''Nogizaka Under Construction'', ''[[Asahinagu (film)|Asahinagu]]'', and a series of documentary films about the group.

Nogizaka46 has twice won the Grand Prix at the annual [[Japan Record Awards]], with consecutive wins for "Influencer" in 2017 and "[[Synchronicity (song)|Synchronicity]]" in 2018. The group has sold nearly 18 million CDs in Japan.

== History ==

=== 2011–2012: Formation and early chart success ===
The formation of Nogizaka46 was first announced on June 29, 2011, when it became the first group to be labelled as the "official rival" to the group [[AKB48]], rather than a sister group like [[NMB48]] or [[SKE48]]. The group was named after the location of the [[Sony Music Japan]] offices near [[Nogizaka Station]]. Producer Yasushi Akimoto said that the number "46" was chosen as a direct challenge to AKB48, implying that Nogizaka46 would succeed with fewer members.<ref name="oricon20110629">{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/news/confidence/89336/full/|title="AKB48公式ライバル"乃木坂46結成 一般公募でメンバー決定|work=ORICON STYLE|publisher=Oricon ME|access-date=August 22, 2011| language=ja|date=June 29, 2011}}</ref> In contrast to AKB48 and its sister groups, the image of Nogizaka46 is based on the Japanese people's perception of conservatively-dressed private all-girls high school students in France, which carries the connotations of elegance and refinement.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Ueda|first=Yutaka|date=March 2016|title=Image Strategy of Nogizaka46: How Do Young People Perceive It?|url=http://repository.seikei.ac.jp/dspace/bitstream/10928/865/1/ippankenkyuhoukoku-49-5_1-18.pdf|journal=Bulletin of Seikei University|volume=49|pages=2}}</ref>

Overall, 38,934 people applied to join Nogizaka46. Final auditions for the group were held from August 20–21, 2011, with 56 finalists competing for 36 available places, and 16 members joining as "senbatsu" members specially selected for performances and media appearances.<ref name="oricon20110822">{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2000936/full/|script-title=ja:AKB48"公式"ライバル乃木坂46、36人決定 毎日が総選挙?!|trans-title=AKB48 Official rival Nogizaka46, 36 members were selected|access-date=March 5, 2016|date=August 22, 2011|publisher=oricon ME inc.|language=ja}}</ref> On October 2, 2011, the group launched its first television variety show, titled {{nihongo|''Nogizakatte, Doko?''|乃木坂って、どこ?| Lit: Where is Nogizaka?}} and hosted by the comedy duo [[Bananaman (comedy duo)|Bananaman]].<ref name="oricon20110822" /><ref name="tower20110912">{{cite web|url=http://tower.jp/article/news/2011/09/12/n13|title=乃木坂46のTVレギュラー番組「乃木坂って、どこ?」が10月にスタート|publisher=TOWER RECORDS ONLINE|access-date=September 13, 2011|language=ja|date=September 12, 2011}}</ref>

On February 22, 2012, Nogizaka46 released their debut single "[[Guruguru Curtain]]". It ranked second on the [[Oricon]] chart and sold 136,309 copies in the first week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/js/w/2012-03-05/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120408135015/http://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/js/w/2012-03-05/|script-title=ja:2012年02月20日～2012年02月26日のCDシングル週間ランキング|date=March 5, 2012|archive-date=April 8, 2012|access-date=September 25, 2016|language=ja}}</ref> Their second single, "[[Oide Shampoo]]", was released on May 2 of the same year, and became their first number one song on the Oricon weekly chart, with sales of 156,000 copies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2011204/full/|script-title=ja:【オリコン】乃木坂46、AKB指原との同門対決制す 今年初の新人首位|date=May 8, 2012|access-date=September 25, 2016|publisher=oricon ME inc.|language=ja}}</ref> The choreography for "Oide Shampoo" caused a brief controversy, as critics disapproved of the members lifting their skirts over their faces during one part of the performance.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sanspo.com/geino/news/20120421/ido12042105030000-n1.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120423192852/http://www.sanspo.com/geino/news/20120421/ido12042105030000-n1.html|script-title=ja:乃木坂46、めくり"封印"ひらひら"解禁"|archive-date=April 23, 2012|date=April 21, 2012|access-date=November 7, 2015|publisher=[[Sankei Sports|Sankei Digital Inc.]]|language=ja}}</ref>

In June 2012, Nogizaka46 took part in Yubi Matsuri, an idol festival produced by [[Rino Sashihara]], performing before 8,000 people at [[Nippon Budokan]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/71786|script-title=ja:指原莉乃「ゆび祭り」で豪華コラボ版「ヘビロテ」実現|publisher=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=June 26, 2012|access-date=November 20, 2012|language=ja}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.mynavi.jp/news/2012/11/02/182/|title=指原莉乃プロデュース「第一回ゆび祭り」、DVD&Blu-rayでの発売が決定!|publisher=My Navi|date=November 2, 2012|access-date=November 20, 2012|language=ja}}</ref> They debuted their first musical theater production, titled ''16 nin no Principal'', at Shibuya Parco Theater in September 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/75143|script-title=ja:乃木坂46「16人のプリンシパル」公演、本日から予約開始|date=August 22, 2012|access-date=September 25, 2016|publisher=Natasha,Inc|language=ja}}</ref> By the end of 2012, Nogizaka46 reached number one on the Oricon yearly chart in the newcomers sales category, with annual sales of 870 million yen.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2019912/full/|script-title=ja:【オリコン年間】乃木坂46、新人セールス1位 AKB関連グループ初|date=December 21, 2012|access-date=October 20, 2016|publisher=oricon ME inc.|language=ja}}</ref>

=== 2016–2017: Third generation and Japan Record Award ===
From February 20 through February 22, 2016, Nogizaka46 broadcast an Internet TV program titled ''Nogizaka46 4th Anniversary Nogizaka 46 hours TV'', in which the members produced and performed their own material for forty-six consecutive hours, on six websites simultaneously.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2067212/full/|script-title=ja:乃木坂46、6サイトで「46時間TV」配信 アンダーライブ東北シリーズ発表|date=February 20, 2016|access-date=May 25, 2016|publisher=oricon ME inc.|language=ja}}</ref> In the week following the performance, the group's twelfth single "[[Taiyō Nokku]]" was selected for the 30th [[Japan Gold Disc Award]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/177777|script-title=ja:ゴールドディスク大賞は嵐が2年連続4回目、アレキ・AKB48らも各賞受賞|date=February 27, 2016|access-date=September 25, 2016|publisher=Natasha,Inc|language=ja}}</ref> On May 25, 2016, they released their second album ''[[Sorezore no Isu]]'', which reached number one on the Oricon weekly albums chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2070814/full/|script-title=ja:乃木坂46、新ビジュアルは国立新美術館で撮影|date=April 27, 2016|access-date=May 25, 2016|publisher=oricon ME inc.|language=ja}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/ja/w/2016-06-06/|script-title=ja:週間 CDアルバムランキング 2016年06月06日付|date=June 6, 2016|access-date=September 25, 2016|publisher=oricon ME inc.|language=ja}}</ref> The group promoted the second album with a second broadcast of ''Nogizaka 46 hours TV''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/188010|script-title=ja:乃木坂46、2ndアルバム発売記念し2度目の「乃木坂46時間TV」OA|date=May 20, 2016|access-date=June 17, 2016|publisher=Natasha,Inc|language=ja}}</ref> On August 5, they released their second photobook, titled ''1 Jikan Okure no I Love You'', which sold 41,000 copies in the first week and ranked 1st on Oricon weekly book ranking.<ref name="oricon20160811" />

The group's 2017 national tour held performances in Tokyo, Sendai, Osaka, Nagoya, and Niigata, and concluded on November 7 and 8 at Tokyo Dome, where they drew in over 55,000 fans each day.<ref name="natasha20170702">{{cite web|script-title=ja:乃木坂46、初の東京ドームワンマン決定「やっと来たね」 - 音楽ナタリー|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/239164|website=音楽ナタリー|publisher=Natasha Inc|language=ja}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=「真夏の全国ツアー2017 Final 東京ドーム公演」開催決定のお知らせ...｜ニュース｜乃木坂46公式サイト|url=http://www.nogizaka46.com/news/2017/07/2017-final.php|website=www.nogizaka46.com|language=ja}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|script-title=ja:乃木坂46、初の東京ドームワンマン決定「やっと来たね」 - 音楽ナタリー|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/239164|website=音楽ナタリー|publisher=natasha inc|language=ja}}</ref> Three weeks later they performed overseas for the second time, with a selection of group members performing live at C3AFA Singapore.<ref>{{cite web|title=乃木坂46初のアジア進出！11月にシンガポールでイベント出演が決定！海外向けにFacebook、Weiboアカウントも開設...｜ニュース｜乃木坂46公式サイト|url=http://www.nogizaka46.com/news/2017/10/11-1.php|website=www.nogizaka46.com|language=ja}}</ref> The group ended the year by winning the grand prize at the 59th Japan Record Awards for their seventeenth single, titled "[[Influencer (song)|Influencer]]",<ref>{{cite web|script-title=ja:『レコ大』乃木坂46が初の大賞 キャプテン桜井「関わってくれたすべての皆さんのおかげ」|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2103390/full/|website=ORICON NEWS|publisher=oricon ME|language=ja-JP}}</ref> and by making their third consecutive appearance on NHK's ''Kōhaku Uta Gassen''.<ref>{{cite web|title=「紅白」初出場はSHISHAMO、リトグリ、エレカシ、JUMP、大知、WANIMAら10組 - 音楽ナタリー|url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/256968|website=音楽ナタリー|publisher=natasha inc|language=ja}}</ref>

A series of first generation member graduations started in 2018, when Rina Ikoma, who had centered six of the group's singles, graduated from the group in April.<ref name=ikomagrad>{{cite news|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/279198|language=ja|title=生駒里奈卒業コンサート、"猫背の少女"が胸を張って乃木坂を上り切る|work=Oricon News|date=April 23, 2018|access-date=June 22, 2018}}</ref> The final day of the 7th Birthday Live in February 2019 was a graduation concert for first generation member Nanase Nishino, who had centered or co-centered seven singles.<ref name=nishinograd>{{cite news|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2130094/full/|language=ja|title=西野七瀬、卒コンで白石麻衣と涙の抱擁30秒|trans-title=Nanase Nishino and Mai Shiraishi in tearful hug at graduation concert|work=Oricon News|date=February 25, 2019|access-date=March 27, 2019}}</ref> In March 2019, [[Misa Etō]] held a solo concert as her final public appearance with the group.<ref name=misasenpaisolo>{{cite news|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2131842/full/|language=ja|title=乃木坂46衛藤美彩、異例の卒業ソロ公演|trans-title=Misa Etō in exceptional graduation solo performance|work=Oricon News|date=March 20, 2019|access-date=March 27, 2019}}</ref> Nogizaka46's first captain, [[Reika Sakurai]], graduated from the group on the final day of the 2019 national summer tour, and was succeeded as captain by [[Manatsu Akimoto]].<ref name=sakuraisotsugyou>{{cite news|url=https://www.nikkansports.com/entertainment/news/201909010001178.html|language=ja|title=秋元真夏「誰よりも玲香の応援団になる」手紙全文|work=Nikkan Sports|date=September 2, 2019|access-date=January 30, 2020}}</ref>

On January 7, 2020, [[Mai Shiraishi]] announced her plans to leave the group after the release of its twenty-fifth single.<ref name=shiraishisotsugyou>{{cite news|url=https://www.sponichi.co.jp/entertainment/news/2020/01/07/kiji/20200107s00041000025000c.html|title=白石麻衣に聞く 乃木坂卒業を相談したのは「唯一の同い年の1期生」|work=[[Sports Nippon|Sponichi Annex]]|date=January 7, 2020|access-date=January 7, 2020}}</ref> On April 28, via her official blog, Mai Shiraishi announced that, due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], her graduation would be delayed until it is safe to hold a graduation concert.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.nogizaka46.com/mai.shiraishi/2020/04/056035.php|script-title=ja:皆さん。|date=April 28, 2020|access-date=May 13, 2020|publisher=Nogizaka46LLC|language=ja}}</ref>

In the summer of 2018, a joint audition was held with Keyakizaka46 and Hinatazaka46. After the auditions, 39 members were accepted with 11 being assigned to Nogizaka46, forming the group's fourth generation. On February 16, 2020, five [[Sakamichi Series]] trainees were assigned to Nogizaka46 as fourth generation members.<ref name=trainees2020>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/367475|title=乃木坂46、欅坂46、日向坂46「坂道研修生」配属先グループ発表|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=February 16, 2020|access-date=February 23, 2020}}</ref>

On July 19, 2021, the group announced an audition for the group's fifth generation with applications starting that day and screening taking place on August 10. The new generation of members will be unveiled in December 2021.<ref>{{cite web |title=乃木坂46 新メンバーオーディション開催決定！ |url=https://www.nogizaka46.com/news/2021/07/post-464345716.php |website=Nogizaka46 Official Website |publisher=Nogizaka46 LLC |access-date=October 10, 2021 |language=ja |date=July 19, 2021}}</ref> To celebrate 10th anniversary of the group, Nogizaka46 will release the [[greatest hits album]] on December 15, 2021,<ref>{{cite news |title=乃木坂46、10周年で初ベスト12・15発売 全国の"○○坂"駅にポスター掲出へ |url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/2209841/full/ |access-date=October 10, 2021 |work=Oricon |date=October 10, 2021 |language=ja}}</ref> titled ''[[Time Flies (Nogizaka46 album)|Time Flies]]'',<ref>{{cite news |last1=Nishibiro |first1=Tomokazu |title=乃木坂46 初のベストアルバム タイトルが「Time flies」に決定！ |url=https://www.excite.co.jp/news/article/AllNightNippon_321982/ |access-date=October 23, 2021 |work=Excite |date=October 23, 2021 |language=ja}}</ref> and hold the concert 10th Year Birthday Live at [[Nissan Stadium (Yokohama)|Nissan Stadium]] on May 14–15.<ref>{{cite web  |url=https://www.nogizaka46.com/news/2021/11/post-2110231.php |title=「10th Year Birthday Live」 2022年5月14.15日 日産スタジアムにて開催決定！|access-date=November 20, 2021 |work=Nogizaka46 official website|date=November 20, 2021 |language=ja}}</ref>

== Tours and concerts ==
Since their first "Birthday Live" concert in 2013, Nogizaka46 has held annual concerts during which the group performs all or most of the songs in their catalogue. At the first Birthday Live concert at [[Makuhari Messe]], the group performed all of the songs from their first four singles to an audience of 9,000 people.<ref name=1stbirthdaylive>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/85424|title=乃木坂46、デビュー1周年で全曲ライブ＆新曲も初披露|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=February 23, 2013|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref> In 2014 they performed for 13,000 fans at [[Yokohama Arena]],<ref name=2ndbirthdaylive>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/110449|title=サプライズ連発！乃木坂46デビュー2周年祝う5時間ライブ|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=February 23, 2014|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref> and the following year they performed 69 songs in a seven-and-a-half hour birthday concert for 38,000 fans at [[Seibu Dome]].<ref name=3rdbirthdaylive>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://mdpr.jp/music/detail/1469434|title=乃木坂46西野七瀬ら感涙 AKB48小嶋陽菜もサプライズ登場 7時間半・全69曲に3万8000人熱狂＜3周年ライブ／写真特集＞|work=Modelpress|date=February 22, 2015|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref> The group's growing catalogue and fan base led to longer concerts at larger venues, including three-day concerts at [[Meiji Jingu Stadium]] in 2016<ref name=4thbirthdaylive>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/199796|title=乃木坂46、明治神宮球場公演3DAYS開幕！桜井玲香も復帰|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=August 29, 2016|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref> and  [[Saitama Super Arena]] in 2017,<ref name=5thbirthdaylive>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/221529|title=「自分で選んだ道の先に正解があると信じてます」乃木坂46橋本奈々未、涙の卒業ライブ|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=February 21, 2017|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref> and a four-day Birthday Live concert in 2019 at [[Kyocera Dome]], at which the group performed all 177 songs in their catalogue.<ref name=7thbirthdaylive>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/321324|title=乃木坂46西野七瀬が地元大阪でラストステージ、7年間のアイドル人生に感謝|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=February 15, 2019|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref>

Nogizaka46 has also held a national summer concert tour each year at venues across Japan, concluding with a show in Tokyo. In 2013, the tour included ten performances in five cities, with a final three-hour concert at [[Yoyogi National Gymnasium]].<ref name=1stsummertour>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/100850|title=乃木坂46、圧巻の代々木競技場ライブに計2万4000人動員|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=October 7, 2013|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref> Four years later, the national tour concluded with a two-day performance for 100,000 fans at [[Tokyo Dome]].<ref name=5thsummertour>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/255978|title=上り続ける乃木坂46、初東京ドームで見据えたさらなる高み「もっとすごい所へ」|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=November 9, 2017|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref> In addition to the regular summer concert tour, the group has occasionally held concerts to commemorate holidays such as [[Christmas]],<ref name=1stxmas>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/106234|title=乃木坂46、初武道館でファン1万2000人とXmasパーティ|date=October 20, 2013|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref> or to highlight members of the group who have not been chosen to perform on the main singles.<ref name=underlive2018>{{cite news|language=ja|url=https://natalie.mu/music/news/313126|title=乃木坂46アンダーライブで川後陽菜が涙の卒業「7年間で本当に大切なものができた」|work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]|date=December 21, 2018|access-date=February 10, 2020}}</ref>
