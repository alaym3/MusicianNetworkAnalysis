'''8Ball & MJG''' is an American [[hip hop music|hip hop]] duo from [[Memphis, Tennessee]]. They met at [[Ridgeway Middle School (Memphis, Tennessee)]] in 1984. In 1993, the duo released their debut album ''[[Comin' Out Hard]]''. They went on to release ''[[On the Outside Looking In]]'' (1994), ''[[On Top of the World (album)|On Top of the World]]'' (1995), ''[[In Our Lifetime (Eightball & MJG album)|In Our Lifetime]]'' (1999), ''[[Space Age 4 Eva]]'' (2000), ''[[Living Legends (album)|Living Legends]]'' (2004), ''[[Ridin' High (8Ball & MJG album)|Ridin' High]]'' (2007) and ''[[Ten Toes Down]]'' (2010).

==Career==
8Ball & MJG first appeared on the rap scene with their underground 1991 album ''Listen to the Lyrics''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/Eightball-And-MJG-Listen-To-The-Lyrics/release/322679 |title=Eight Ball And MJG* - Listen To The Lyrics (Cassette) at Discogs |publisher=Discogs.com |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> In 1993, they released the successful album ''[[Comin' Out Hard]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B000001ZDK |title=Comin' Out Hard: 8ball & Mjg: Music |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> Their subsequent albums in the 1990s, including 1994's ''[[On the Outside Looking In]]'', and 1995's ''[[On Top of the World (8Ball & MJG album)|On Top of the World]]'' cemented their status as some of the South's best rappers.<ref name="apple1">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/on-top-of-the-world/id333891795 |title=iTunes - Music - On Top Of The World by 8Ball & MJG |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=October 31, 1995 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> ''On Top of the World'' was particularly successful, peaking at #8 on the [[Billboard Hot 200]] and being certified Gold.<ref name="apple1"/> It also contained the song "Space Age Pimpin'", which was 8Ball & MJG's first single to chart, reaching #58 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Singles and Tracks]] chart and #22 on the [[Hot Rap Singles]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00198J8QE |title=Space Age Pimpin' [Explicit&#93;: 8 Ball & Mjg: MP3 Downloads |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> After those albums both 8Ball & MJG released solo albums, first MJG's ''[[No More Glory]]'' in 1997 and then 8Ball's ''[[Lost (Eightball album)|Lost]]'' in 1998.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/no-more-glory/id333991132 |title=iTunes - Music - No More Glory by MJG |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=November 18, 1997 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/lost/id333893357 |title=iTunes - Music - Lost by Eightball |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=May 19, 1998 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> They reunited in 1999 to release their fourth album as a group, titled ''[[In Our Lifetime (Eightball & MJG album)|In Our Lifetime]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/in-our-lifetime/id333988630 |title=iTunes - Music - In Our Lifetime by 8Ball & MJG |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=May 18, 1999 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> One year later in 2000, they released their fifth group album entitled ''[[Space Age 4 Eva]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Birchmeier |first=Jason |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/space-age-4-eva-mw0000106779 |title=Space Age 4 Eva - Eightball & MJG : Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |publisher=AllMusic |date=November 21, 2000 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref>

In 1996, they appeared on the [[Red Hot Organization]]'s compilation CD, [[America is Dying Slowly]], alongside [[Biz Markie]], [[Wu-Tang Clan]], and [[Fat Joe]], among many other prominent hip hop artists.<ref name="apple2">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/america-is-dying-slowly/id321978086 |title=iTunes - Music - America Is Dying Slowly by Various Artists |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> The CD, meant to raise awareness of the AIDS epidemic among African American men, was heralded as "a masterpiece" by [[The Source (magazine)|The Source]] magazine.<ref name="apple2"/> In the early 2000s they would sign with [[Sean Combs]]' [[Bad Boy Records]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Langhorne |first=Cyrus |url=http://www.sohh.com/2009/06/8ball_speaks_life_after_d.html |title=8Ball Explains Bad Boy Break, Rewards Eminem W/ Rap Legend Status |publisher=Sohh.Com |date=June 8, 2009 |access-date=2013-06-05 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131219013530/http://www.sohh.com/2009/06/8ball_speaks_life_after_d.html |archive-date=December 19, 2013 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> They already had some experience with the label, being featured on the song "The Player Way" from Bad Boy rapper [[Mase]]'s 1997 album ''[[Harlem World]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Stanley |first=Leo |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/harlem-world-mw0000596053 |title=Harlem World - Mase : Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |publisher=AllMusic |date=October 28, 1997 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> Their first album for [[Bad Boy Records]], ''[[Living Legends (album)|Living Legends]]'', came out in 2004 and was certified [[RIAA certification|Gold]] by the [[RIAA]].<ref name="allmusic1">{{cite web|last=Jeffries |first=David |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/living-legends-mw0000473986 |title=Living Legends - Eightball & MJG : Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |publisher=AllMusic |date=May 11, 2004 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> Their second album on [[Bad Boy Records]] was titled ''[[Ridin' High (8Ball & MJG album)|Ridin' High]]'' and was released in March 2007.<ref name="allmusic1"/>

Commercially one of the high points of 8Ball & MJG's career was their being featured on [[Three 6 Mafia]]'s hit song "[[Stay Fly]]" in 2005.<ref name="apple3">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/stay-fly-feat.-young-buck/id159353156 |title=iTunes - Music - Stay Fly (feat. Young Buck & 8Ball & MJG) - EP by Three 6 Mafia |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=July 5, 2005 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> That song peaked at #13 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], which is the biggest hit of Three 6 Mafia's career and the biggest hit for 8Ball & MJG.<ref name="apple3"/> The song was a collaboration between two of the most successful rap groups from the state of [[Tennessee]], whence Three 6 Mafia also hail.<ref name="apple3"/> Today 8Ball and MJG also head their own record labels. 8Ball heads 8 Ways Entertainment (distributed by [[Koch Entertainment]]), while MJG heads MJG Muzik.<ref name="rapreviews1">{{cite web|url=http://www.rapreviews.com/archive/2006_11_lightup.html |title=8 Ball & 8 Ways Entertainment :: Light Up the Bomb :: 8 Ways Entertainment |publisher=Rapreviews.com |date=November 21, 2006 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> On their label are the young, up and coming Memphis duo, Da Volunteers, who are widely known throughout the [[Southern United States]] for their 2006 single, "What's Yo Favorite Color?", which glorifies their neighborhood of [[Orange Mound]].<ref name="rapreviews1"/>

In September 2007, 8Ball and MJG signed deals in Sacramento, CA with [[Real Talk Entertainment]] 8Ball released a group album with E.D.I of the Outlawz entitled ''[[Doin' It Big]]'' on April 1, 2008 and MJG released a solo album entitled ''[[Pimp Tight]]'' on April 29, 2008.<ref name="rapreviews1"/><ref>{{cite web|last=Rinaldi |first=Matt |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/mjg-pimp-tight-mw0000783597 |title=MJG: Pimp Tight - 8Ball : Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |publisher=AllMusic |date=April 29, 2008 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> In June 2008 the group announced that they officially signed onto [[T.I.]]'s record label [[Grand Hustle]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Burgess |first=Omar |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.7128/title.t-i-signs-8ball-mjg-while-beef-continues |title=T.I. Signs 8Ball & MJG While Beef Continues &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=June 16, 2008 |access-date=2013-06-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080619215253/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.7128/title.t-i-signs-8ball-mjg-while-beef-continues |archive-date=June 19, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Their eighth album as a group and their first on Grand Hustle, titled ''[[Ten Toes Down]]'', was released in May 2010.<ref name="allmusic2">{{cite web|last=Jeffries |first=David |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/ten-toes-down-mw0001968250 |title=Ten Toes Down - Eightball & MJG : Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |publisher=AllMusic |date=May 4, 2010 |access-date=2013-06-05}}</ref> It reached #36 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] in its first week.<ref name="allmusic2"/>
