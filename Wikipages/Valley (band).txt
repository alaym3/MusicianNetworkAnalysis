'''Valley''' is a [[Canada|Canadian]] [[indie pop]] band based in [[Toronto]]. 

The band members are lead vocalist Rob Laska, guitarist Michael (Mickey) Brandolino, bassist Alex Dimauro, and drummer Karah James.<ref name="Dingee">Alexandra Johnson Dingee, [https://www.queensjournal.ca/story/2018-11-29/music/seeing-yellow-with-indie-rockers-valley/ "Seeing yellow with indie rockers VALLEY"]. ''[[Queen's Journal]]'', November 29, 2018.</ref> Valley received a [[Juno Award]] nomination for [[Juno Award for Breakthrough Group of the Year|Breakthrough Group of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2020|2020 Juno Awards]]<ref>Melody Lau, [https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/alessia-cara-and-tory-lanez-lead-the-2020-juno-nominations-1.5436386 "Alessia Cara and Tory Lanez lead the 2020 Juno nominations"]. [[CBC Music]], January 28, 2019.</ref> for supporting The Band Camino<ref>Katie Arenas, [https://www.musicinminnesota.com/the-band-camino-no-need-to-tryhard-for-a-great-show/ "The Band CAMINO: No Need to "try hard" for a Great Show"]</ref> and [[Lennon Stella]] on their tours.<ref>Joshua Hanson, [https://thereviewsarein.com/2019/03/21/lennon-stella-opens-love-me-tour-in-toronto/ "Lennon Stella Opens Love, me Tour in Toronto"]</ref>

== History ==
The band formed in 2014 after the members of two other bands accidentally got double-booked for the same time slot at a local recording studio.<ref>John Bkila, [https://www.insidehalton.com/whatson-story/5628988-oakville-s-valley-releases-debut-album/ "Oakville’s Valley releases debut album"]. ''[[Metroland Media Group|Inside Halton]]'', May 14, 2015.</ref> They released their debut EP, ''Car Test'', in 2015, and followed up with ''This Room Is White'' in 2016.<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/valley-this_room_is_white_ep_stream "Valley, 'This Room Is White"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', June 1, 2016.</ref> In 2018 they released ''Maybe Side A'', the first of two EPs featuring tracks planned for their full-length debut album;<ref name="Dingee" /> the second EP, ''Maybe Side B'', followed in spring 2019, and the full album ''Maybe'' was released in September.<ref>Gina Scarpino, [https://www.beyondthestagemagazine.com/get-to-know-valley/ "Get To Know: Valley"]. ''Beyond the Stage'', September 18, 2019.</ref>

Valley released their single "nevermind"<ref>Logan White, [https://substreammagazine.com/2020/07/premiere-valley-nevermind-single/ "PREMIERE: Valley will be in your head all day with "nevermind"]</ref> in July 2020, which was then followed by "hiccup"<ref>Abhigyaan Bavaria, [https://www.beyondthestagemagazine.com/valley-release/ VALLEY releases another upbeat pop banger in "hiccup"]</ref> in September. On October 15, Valley announced the release of their new EP, ''sucks to see you doing better'' which featured both "nevermind" and "hiccup", as well as four new tracks, including an acoustic version of "hiccup".<ref>Jarod, [https://volatileweekly.com/2020/10/valley-announce-new-ep-release-hiccup-acoustic/ VALLEY Announce New EP + Release "hiccup" (acoustic)]</ref>

In February 2021, Valley released their single "Like 1999"<ref>{{Cite web|last=melodicmag|date=2021-04-01|title=Valley release music video for TikTok song "Like 1999"|url=https://www.melodicmag.com/2021/04/01/valley-release-music-video-for-tiktok-song-like-1999/|access-date=2021-04-28|website=// MELODIC Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> and announced they would tour with [[Coin (band)|Coin]] in over 30+ cities across North America, beginning in October.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-27|title=Alt-Pop Trio COIN To Perform At Fillmore On December 2nd|url=https://www.wccbcharlotte.com/2021/04/27/alt-pop-trio-coin-to-perform-at-fillmore-on-december-2nd/|access-date=2021-04-28|website=WCCB Charlotte's CW|language=en-US}}</ref>   

Midway through 2021, they released a new single, "SOCIETY" (styled in all caps), which the band says came from their previous experiences of balancing an accessible narrative with their artistic pursuits.<ref>https://www.altpress.com/news/valley-society-coin-tour-rainbow-dreamland/</ref> On July 23, 2021, they released a new single, "Tempo".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nguyen|first=Austin|title=Valley Set the|url=https://www.popmatters.com/valley-tempo-song|access-date=2021-09-20|website=PopMatters|language=en-US}}</ref>

The single "Homebody" was a nominee for the 2021 [[SOCAN Songwriting Prize]].<ref>Megan LaPierre, [https://exclaim.ca/music/article/socan_songwriting_prize_announces_2021_finalists "SOCAN Songwriting Prize Announces 2021 Finalists"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', June 17, 2021.</ref> "Oh sh*t... are we in love?" was released on September 1st, 2021.
