'''Sofiane Youssef Samir Boussaadia''' (born 28 February 1993), known professionally as '''Boef''' ({{IPA-nl|buf|lang}}; "thief"), is a French-Dutch rapper and vlogger of Algerian origin.

Within a year of his start in 2015, he became one of the most popular rappers in the Netherlands after [[Lil' Kleine]] and [[Ronnie Flex]].<ref name="qoutenet">{{cite web |url=https://www.quotenet.nl/entertainment/a184610/dit-is-het-succes-van-rapper-boef-kun-je-er-niet-tegen-dat-ik-meer-verdien-video-184610/ |title=Dit is het succes van rapper Boef: 'Kun je er niet tegen dat ik meer verdien?' (video) |website=Quote |author=Mark Koster |accessdate=10 May 2019}}</ref> He released an album and various singles. His vlog and rap videos were viewed many millions of times on the internet.

His first EP ''Gewoon Boef'' was released in February 2016 and his first album ''Slaaptekort'' came out in March 2017. ''Slaaptekort'' (Dutch for 'sleep deprivation') is the album with the most streams on release date in the Netherlands, a record that was previously held by [[Ed Sheeran]]'s ''[[÷ (album)|÷]]''. Many of his songs have made it on the charts in the Netherlands and Belgian Flanders.<ref>{{cite news|language=nl |url=http://www.metronieuws.nl/nieuws/showbizz/2017/03/boef-domineert-van-sommige-dingen-heb-ik-spijt |first=Daisy |last=Heyer |title=Boef domineert: 'Van sommige dingen heb ik spijt' |newspaper=[[Metro (Dutch newspaper)|Metro]] |date=17 March 2017 |accessdate=20 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|language=nl |url=http://www.dutchcharts.nl/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Boef |title=Boef in Dutch charts |website=dutchcharts.nl |accessdate=20 March 2017}}</ref>

==Early life==
Boef was born in Aubervilliers in [[Île-de-France]] (Paris conurbation). He has Algerian parents and a French nationality. At the age of four he came to his uncle in [[Eindhoven]]. He also lived with him for two years in [[Houston]], [[Texas]], and at the age of thirteen he moved to Alkmaar. He told newspaper [[De Volkskrant]] that he was in juvenile detention for six months at the age of sixteen and one and a half years from the age of eighteen for a criminal offense and then six months for refusing to cooperate with the [[probation]] service.<ref name="101barz">{{cite web |url=https://101barz.bnnvara.nl/video/1419/boef-toen-ik-vrij-kwam-had-ik-niet-verwacht-dat-dit-zou-gebeuren |website=BNN |access-date=10 May 2019|title=101Barz - BOEF - 'Toen ik vrij kwam, had ik niet verwacht dat dit zou gebeuren' }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Mijn leven is lijp. |publisher=de Volkskrant}}</ref>

==Career==
Since his release from prison, he started focussing on [[rap music]]. He contacted Zonamo Underground through his friend and rapper Otie, after contacting Zonamo Underground they invited Boef for a session in February 2015.<ref name="101barz" /> This was very well received and gained over a million views on [[YouTube]] in a short period of time.

[[File:Boef2017.jpg|thumb|200px|right|Boef in 2017.]]
After his first zonamo session in 2015, Boef signed a contract with Zonamo Underground. His success grew beyond expectations of both himself and his management.<ref name="101barz" /> [[BNN (Dutch broadcaster)|BNN]] presenter Rotjoch said about his breakthrough: "In 2015 Boef suddenly arrived; nobody knew him. After his Zonamo session he is indispensable in the scene."<ref name="101barz" />

He released several songs that reached the Single Top 100. His [[Extended play|EP]] Gewoon BOEF came in at number 4 in February 2016 in the Dutch Album Top 100, in which it is recorded around thirty weeks. The album was also in the [[Flemish]] Album Top 200 for a few weeks.<ref name="ned"/>

In April 2016 Boef released his breakthrough hit "Lauw" which gained millions of views on [[YouTube]]. He recorded the whole video clip in [[Dubai]] together with the producer Harun B. He also has a vlogging channel on YouTube, where he uploads videos of his daily life and trips.<ref name="qoutenet" />

From 2021, Boussaadia offers entrepreneurial courses together with [[Lil' Kleine]].<ref>https://www.parool.nl/kunst-media/nu-te-koop-via-een-prijzige-cursus-het-succes-van-boef-en-lil-kleine~bc9a9919/</ref>

== Controversy ==
During the New Year's night of 2017–2018, Boef had a flat tire after a performance. After three women gave him a lift when coming back from a party, he called them "hookers" on social media. The following day, he defended himself by saying that girls who are drunk, wear "slutty" clothes and are in clubs until eight in the morning, are "hookers" compared to girls who listen to their parents and study and sit at home. As a result, Dutch and Belgian radio stations boycotted his music for a time. He apologized for his statements the following day (2 January) and also on 20 January 2018.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.rtlnieuws.nl/rtlboulevard/toch-excuses-boef-na-hoerenopmerking |title = Toch excuses Boef na 'hoerenopmerking'|date = 2018-01-02}}</ref><ref>https://www.parool.nl/kunst-en-media/rapper-boef-biedt-excuses-aan-na-vrouwen-hoeren-te-noemen~a4552351/</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.rtvnoord.nl/nieuws/189166/Rapper-Boef-biedt-opnieuw-excuses-aan-zaal-juicht | title=Rapper Boef biedt opnieuw excuses aan; zaal juicht}}</ref>
