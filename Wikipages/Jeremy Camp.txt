'''Jeremy Thomas Camp''' (born January 12, 1978) is an American [[contemporary Christian music]] singer and songwriter from [[Lafayette, Indiana]]. Camp has released eleven albums, four of them [[RIAA certification|RIAA]]-certified as [[Music recording certification|Gold]], and two live albums. His original music is a mixture of ballads and up-tempo songs with rock influence. Camp has won five [[GMA Dove Award]]s, has been nominated for three [[American Music Awards]], and was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best Pop/Contemporary Gospel Album]] in 2010 for his album, ''[[Speaking Louder Than Before]]''. ''[[I Still Believe (film)|I Still Believe]]'', a film based on Camp's first marriage, was released on March 13, 2020.

==Early life==
[[File:Jeremy Camp Wonderland.jpg|thumb|upright|Jeremy Camp performing in June 2006]]
Jeremy Camp was born in [[Lafayette, Indiana]].<ref name="allmusic">{{Cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p542070/biography|pure_url=yes}} |title=Jeremy Camp Biography |last=Mark Deming |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=October 13, 2008}}</ref> His father Tom, the pastor at Harvest Chapel (associated with [[Calvary Chapel Costa Mesa]]), taught him how to play [[guitar]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.pe.ag.org/Conversations2009/4963_Camp.cfm |title=Pentecostal Evangel |publisher=Pe.ag.org |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140314142743/http://www.pe.ag.org/Conversations2009/4963_Camp.cfm |archive-date=March 14, 2014 |access-date=March 7, 2013}}</ref> After completing high school, Camp attended [[Calvary Chapel Bible College]] in [[Murrieta, California]], for two years and earned an associate of theology degree.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.todayschristianmusic.com/artists/jeremy-camp/features/breaking-camp/ |title=Jeremy Camp &#124; Breaking Camp |last=David Jenison |date=February 5, 2001 |publisher=TodaysChristianMusic.com |access-date=March 7, 2013}}</ref> After one of the worship leaders heard him play in the kitchen of the school, he urged Camp to become a part of the worship team. Soon, he led worship and played all over Southern California.

==Musical career==
Camp has scored 32 No. 1 hits on Christian Radio across all formats ([[Contemporary Hit Radio]], [[Hot AC]], Rock, and Inspirational Charts). His album ''[[Beyond Measure (Jeremy Camp album)|Beyond Measure]]'' contributed six back-to-back No. 1 hits, including the track "[[Let It Fade]]" which held the No 1. spot for 10 weeks on AC Radio.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cmspin.com/newsmanager/anmviewer.asp?a=5287&z=26 |title=Jeremy Camp Continues To Dominate Radio |date=December 16, 2008 |publisher=CM Spin Network |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110193143/http://www.cmspin.com/newsmanager/anmviewer.asp?a=5287&z=26 |archive-date=January 10, 2014 |access-date=January 10, 2014}}</ref> His first single was "Understand". He has made ten music videos: "Understand", "[[Walk by Faith]]", "[[Take You Back]]", "[[Tonight (Jeremy Camp song)|Tonight]]", "Give You Glory", "Let It Fade", "I Am Willing" (a song that was not included on any of his albums), "Speaking Louder Than Before", "[[The Way (Jeremy Camp song)|The Way]]", and "[[Reckless (Jeremy Camp song)|Reckless]]".

Camp scored Christian music top spots for "Take You Back" and "[[Lay Down My Pride]]". He also was voted the Best Male Artist in the 2005 Reader's Choice Music Awards in the ''[[Christianity Today]]'' magazine.<ref name="t40 Jan06" /> Camp was also ASCAP 2005 Songwriter of the Year and won the Dove Award for the Male Vocalist of the Year for the second consecutive year. In 2005, he recorded the song "Open Up Your Eyes" for the compilation release ''[[Music Inspired by the Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe]]'', a September 2005 album which contained original songs written by various Christian artists for the 2005 film ''[[The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe]]''. Camp closed the year with both ''[[Stay (Jeremy Camp album)|Stay]]'' and ''[[Carried Me|Carried Me: the Worship Project]]'' being [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]]-certified as Gold.<ref name="t40 Jan06">{{Cite web |url=http://www.top40-charts.com/news.php?nid=19691&string=K%203 |title=Jeremy Camp Closes '05 With Double Gold / Song Of The Year |date=January 6, 2006 |publisher=Top40-Charts.com |access-date=October 22, 2008}}</ref>

Camp earned the top honors at the Twenty-Eighth Annual [[ASCAP]] Music Awards, winning the Songwriter Of The Year award ("Take You Back" and "This Man"). "Take You Back" also earned him the Song Of The Year award. Camp won two 2006 ''CCM'' Reader's Choice Awards, for Favorite Artist and Favorite Male Artist. He took part in the [[GMA Music Awards|Dove award]] for Special Event Album of the Year, which went to ''Music Inspired by The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe''. He released a special edition of his hit album, ''[[Restored]]''. Four new songs were included on ''Restored: The Deluxe Gold Edition'' as well as a letter to his fans.

Camp went back into the studio to record ''[[Beyond Measure (Jeremy Camp album)|Beyond Measure]]'' in early 2006 and was released on October 31, 2006. Camp is also credited as producer for his wife's album, ''Don't Wait'', which was released in September 2006. "Tonight", the first single from the album ''Beyond Measure'', was released in mid-August, and eventually hit No. 1 on Christian contemporary hit radio. It was the 11th most played single on HR stations in 2007.<ref name="R&R 2007">{{Cite web |url=http://www.radioandrecords.com/Formats/Charts/YearEnd/07/Christian_CHR_songs.asp |title=2007 Year End Charts |work=[[R&R (magazine)|R&R]] |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090525095330/http://www.radioandrecords.com/Formats/Charts/YearEnd/07/Christian_CHR_songs.asp |archive-date=May 25, 2009 |access-date=January 6, 2008}}</ref> "Give Me Jesus," the third single off "Beyond Measure," made it into the top 5 on Inspirational radio. The fourth single, "Give You Glory," debuted on Jewish AJ radio at No. 16. His song "Let It Fade" was the seventh most played song on HR radio in 2007. That year he also released a two-disc (CD and DVD) for his concert tour "[[Live Unplugged (Jeremy Camp album)|Live Unplugged]]" recorded in Franklin, Tennessee.<ref name="R&R 2007" />

His song "No Matter What" was the 4th most-played song on [[R&R (magazine)|''R&R'']] magazine's [[Contemporary Christian Music|Christian CHR]] chart for 2008.<ref name="RR2008CHR">[http://www.radioandrecords.com/Formats/Charts/YearEnd/08/Christian_CHR_songs.asp 2008 Year End Charts/Top Christian CHR songs] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090525080129/http://www.radioandrecords.com/Formats/Charts/YearEnd/08/Christian_CHR_songs.asp |date=May 25, 2009 }}, [[R&R (magazine)|R&R magazine]]. Retrieved January 6, 2009</ref>
He also came out with his new studio album ''[[Speaking Louder Than Before]]'', containing the hit lead single "[[There Will Be a Day]]". On November 17, 2009, Camp released another live album: ''[[Jeremy Camp Live]]''. This album featured songs from all albums to date. His ''Speaking Louder Than Before'' received his first [[Grammy Awards|Grammy]] nomination.

''Beyond Measure'' was certified as Gold by the RIAA. The worship album ''[[We Cry Out: The Worship Project|We Cry Out]]'' was released on August 24, 2010. Camp was also a judge for the 10th annual [[Independent Music Awards (IMAs)|Independent Music Awards]] to support independent artists' careers.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.independentmusicawards.com/ima_new/pastjudges.asp |title=Past Judges |publisher=Independent Music Awards |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713024722/http://www.independentmusicawards.com/ima_new/pastjudges.asp |archive-date=July 13, 2011 |access-date=February 16, 2012}}</ref>

On May 20, 2011, Camp released his first book, ''I Still Believe''. The book is about Camp's life, illuminating his childhood, the death of his first wife, and where he believes God has brought him.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.todayschristianmusic.com/artists/jeremy-camp/news/jeremy-camp-pens-his-personal-story-i-still-believe/ |title=Jeremy Camp Pens His Personal Story, I Still Believe |date=July 14, 2011 |publisher=Today's Christian Music |access-date=January 20, 2012}}</ref>

''[[Reckless (Jeremy Camp album)|Reckless]]'' was released on February 12, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://musicfaith.com/music-news/jeremy-camp-to-release-new-album-and-book-next-week/ |title=JEREMY CAMP TO RELEASE NEW ALBUM AND BOOK NEXT WEEK |date=February 6, 2013 |publisher=MusicFaith.com |access-date=February 6, 2013}}</ref> This is Camp's first studio album since 2008, when he released ''[[Speaking Louder Than Before]]''. The first single released from the album was the title track, "Reckless". A video accompanied the single in February 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/news/2013/02/15.JEREMY%20CAMP%20RELEASES%20NEW%20VIDEO%20FOR%20RECKLESS.asp |title=JEREMY CAMP RELEASES NEW VIDEO FOR "RECKLESS" |date=February 15, 2013 |publisher=jesusfreakhideout.com |access-date=January 9, 2014}}</ref> "Reckless" was followed by his second single "My God", which reached number 5 on the [[Christian Songs]] Chart. He also released an acoustic video of "My God" to his [[Vevo]] Channel in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.vevo.com/watch/jeremy-camp/my-god-acoustic-performance/USUV71300867 |title=My God (Acoustic Performance) - Jeremy Camp |date=June 18, 2013 |publisher=VEVO.com |access-date=January 9, 2014}}</ref>

Camp released his tenth studio album, ''[[I Will Follow (album)|I Will Follow]]'', on February 3, 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/jeremycamp/photos/a.197290231974.139058.7045321974/10152397794176975/?type=1 |title=Jeremy Camp on Facebook |last=Jeremy Camp |date=November 6, 2014 |publisher=Jeremy Camp}}</ref> The album's lead single, "He Knows", was released in September 2014.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/jeremycamp/photos/a.197290231974.139058.7045321974/10152281517521975/?type=1 |title=Jeremy Camp on Facebook |last=Jeremy Camp |date=September 15, 2014 |publisher=Jeremy Camp}}</ref>

Camp released his eleventh studio album, ''The Answer'', on October 6, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/the-answer/id12709711421 |title=Jeremy Camp on Facebook |last=Jeremy Camp |date=October 6, 2017 |publisher=iTunes Store}}</ref> The album's lead single, "Word of Life", was released in June 2017.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.vevo.com/watch/jeremy-camp/word-of-life/USUMV1700392 |title=Jeremy Camp on Facebook |last=Jeremy Camp |date=June 2017 |publisher=Jeremy Camp}}</ref>

==Personal life==

Camp and his first wife, Melissa Lynn Henning-Camp (born October 7, 1979), were married on October 21, 2000.<ref name="melissacamp">{{Cite web |url=http://www.melissacamp.com/main.php |title=Melissa Lynn Camp |publisher=Melissacamp.com |access-date=October 13, 2008}}</ref> She was diagnosed with [[ovarian cancer]] and died on February 5, 2001, when he was 23 and she was 21.<ref name="melissacamp" /> Some of his early songs reflect the emotional ordeal of her illness. "[[I Still Believe (Jeremy Camp song)|I Still Believe]]" was the first song he wrote. "[[Walk by Faith]]" was written during their honeymoon.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.interlinc-online.com/artists/index.html?p=2&id=69&PHPSESSID=5e87a2a33c2586c2632f15e4d9fb6b13 |title=Jeremy Camp - First Marriage |publisher=Interlinc-online.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120318001717/http://www.interlinc-online.com/artists/index.html?p=2&id=69&PHPSESSID=5e87a2a33c2586c2632f15e4d9fb6b13 |archive-date=March 18, 2012 |access-date=February 16, 2012}}</ref> A film based on Camp's romance with Melissa, ''[[I Still Believe (film)|I Still Believe]]'', was released in March 2020, with Campâ€™s role being played by [[KJ Apa]] and the role of Melissa being played by [[Britt Robertson]].

On December 15, 2003, he married [[Adrienne Camp|Adrienne Liesching]], former frontwoman for [[The Benjamin Gate]]. They have two daughters and one son: Isabella "Bella" Rose Camp (born September 25, 2004), Arianne "Arie" Mae Camp (born April 5, 2006),<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://christianbookpreviews.com/christian-book-author.php?isbn=0615468667 |title=Jeremy Camp: Author of I Still Believe |publisher=Christian Book Previews |access-date=January 21, 2012}}</ref> and Egan Thomas Camp (born August 17, 2011).<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://breathecast.christianpost.com/Christian.Music.Artist-Francesca.Battistelli/Article-764_5453.htm |title=Jeremy Camp welcomes new son |date=August 24, 2011 |publisher=BreatheCast |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120713121807/http://breathecast.christianpost.com/Christian.Music.Artist-Francesca.Battistelli/Article-764_5453.htm |archive-date=July 13, 2012 |access-date=January 21, 2012}}</ref> In 2009, the couple announced that they were expecting a child, but the pregnancy ended due to a miscarriage after three months.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.air1.com/BLOG/post/2009/08/22/Sad-Jeremy-Camp-News.aspx |title=Sad Jeremy Camp News |date=August 22, 2009 |publisher=Air1.com |access-date=February 16, 2012}}</ref> Camp is also an ordained [[minister (Christianity)|minister]].<ref name="CCM_29_8">{{Cite journal |last=Cowart |first=Jeremy |date=February 2007 |title=Love Beyond Measure |journal=[[CCM Magazine]] |volume=29 |issue=8 |pages=32â€“35}}</ref>

Camp is not related to fellow Christian musician [[Steve Camp]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.themonitor.com/entertainment/camp-28002-tickets-christian.html |title=WIN TICKETS: Christian singer Jeremy Camp + an exclusive interview &#124; camp, tickets, christian - Entertainment |last=Sandra Gonzalez |date=June 26, 2009 |publisher=TheMonitor.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120912093410/http://www.themonitor.com/entertainment/camp-28002-tickets-christian.html |archive-date=September 12, 2012 |access-date=February 16, 2012 }}</ref>
