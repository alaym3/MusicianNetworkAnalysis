'''Joelle Joanie''' "'''JoJo'''" '''Siwa''' (born May 19, 2003) is an American dancer, singer, and [[YouTube]] personality. She is known for appearing for two seasons on ''[[Dance Moms]]'' along with her mother, Jessalynn Siwa, and for her singles "[[Boomerang (JoJo Siwa song)|Boomerang]]" and "Kid in a Candy Store". Siwa posts daily videos of her day-to-day life on her YouTube channel, "Its JoJo Siwa".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/whats-on/family-kids-news/jojo-siwa-meets-fans-birmingham-13389826|title=This is what happened when JoJo Bows star came to Birmingham|last=Bentley|first=David|date=2017-07-26|work=birminghammail|access-date=2018-04-09}}</ref> She was included on ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'}}s annual list of the [[Time 100|100 most influential people in the world]] in 2020.

==Early life and career==
Joelle Joanie Siwa was born in [[Omaha, Nebraska]], on May 19, 2003, to Jessalynn (née Lombardi), a professional dance instructor from [[Iowa]], and Tom Siwa, a [[Chiropractic|chiropractor]] from [[Nebraska]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://nwcomaha.wixsite.com/nwcomaha/about-us |title="back pain, neck pain", carpal tunnel, sciatica, disc herniation|access-date=April 16, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=October 15, 2013 |url=http://www.telegraphherald.com/news/article_73c7f931-0f40-5984-bd57-cfacb7544e08.html |title=Mother, daughter step into spotlight |last=Nevans-Pederson |first=Mary |work=Telegraph Herald |access-date=April 16, 2018}}</ref> She has one sibling, an older brother named Jayden Siwa, who is also a vlogger.<ref>{{Cite web|author=Its JoJo Siwa|title=YOU HAVE TO SEE THIS TO BELIEVE THIS!!!|website=[[YouTube]]|date=September 13, 2017 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?t=8s&v=bKMVpcbG-Uc=36s |access-date=April 16, 2018}}</ref>

Siwa started her career as a top-5 finalist and the youngest contestant on the second season of ''[[Abby's Ultimate Dance Competition]]'', produced by [[Abby Lee Miller]] of ''[[Dance Moms]]'' fame.<ref>Siwa, JoJo. [https://itsjojosiwa.com/meet-jojo "Meet JoJo"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161011135501/https://itsjojosiwa.com/meet-jojo/ |date=2016-10-11 }}, ItsJoJoSiwa.com, accessed July 19, 2016</ref> She appeared on the show with her mother and was eliminated in week 9.<ref>[http://glamorosi.blogspot.com/2013/11/762-Abbys-Ultimate-Dance-S2-E10-Divas-JoJo-Eliminated.html "''Abby's Ultimate Dance Competition'' Divas in the House: JoJo Eliminated"], November 6, 2013, July 19, 2016</ref>  Siwa soon began to appear on ''Dance Moms'', beginning with auditioning for Miller's "ALDC" dance competition team in 2014 and being selected for the team in early 2015.<ref name="Billboard1">{{cite magazine |last1=Angermiller |first1=Michele Amabile |title='Dance Moms' Star JoJo Siwa Takes on Bullying in 'Boomerang': Exclusive First Listen |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7356825/dance-moms-jojo-siwa-boomerang-bullying-exclusive-stream |magazine=Billboard |access-date=March 9, 2019 |date=May 5, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.gamenguide.com/articles/59367/20161026/dance-moms-season-6-spoilers-news-update-jojo-siwa-kicked-out-from-aldc-debbie-allen-to-replace-abbie-miller.htm|title='Dance Moms' Season 6 Spoilers, News & Update: JoJo Siwa Kicked Out From ALDC? Meet The Newest 'Dance Moms' Season 5 Star |url=http://www.ibtimes.com/who-jojo-siwa-meet-newest-dance-moms-season-5-star-1783862 |website=International Business Times |access-date=March 9, 2019 |date=January 14, 2015}}</ref>

In May 2016, Siwa released "[[Boomerang (JoJo Siwa song)|Boomerang]]" for download and a previous single, "I Can Make U Dance." "Boomerang" addresses the subject of online bullying.<ref name=Billboard1/><ref>[http://www.bsckids.com/2016/05/jojo-siwa-confronts-bullying-new-single-boomerang JoJo Siwa Confronts Bullying In New Single "Boomerang"]. Bsckids.com (May 10, 2016). Retrieved on 2016-11-27.</ref> Its video has been viewed more than 900 million times and received over 2.5 million likes. In 2018, Siwa was named the Breakout Artist of the Year by [[Vivid Seats]].<ref>{{cite web|title=2018 Year In Review: The Year in Live Events|url=https://www.vividseats.com/blog/2018-top-live-events|access-date=2019-01-04|date=2018-12-18|publisher=Vivid Seats}}</ref>

In 2018, Siwa announced that she would be going on her first major concert tour in 2019, entitled [[D.R.E.A.M. The Tour]]. She was scheduled to visit a mixture of theaters, outdoor amphitheaters and stadiums, as well as indoor arenas in a total of 52 cities across the United States and Canada. Additionally, other performances would take place in the United Kingdom and Australia. The tour was scheduled to begin in [[Phoenix, Arizona]] on May 17. The first leg of the tour was scheduled to conclude on June 26 in [[Austin, Texas]], before resuming for leg two on July 10 in [[Orlando, Florida]]. The final date of the second leg is scheduled to be on August 20 in [[Vancouver]], Canada.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/-Nickelodeons-JoJo-Siwa-DREAM-The-Tour-Adds-17-New-Dates-20190405|title=Nickelodeon's 'JoJo Siwa D.R.E.A.M. ==Other ventures==
In 2016, Siwa released a line of hair bows in a collaboration with fashion accessory and jewelry company [[Claire's]]. The line of hair bows, which were inspired by the hairbows Siwa is known herself for wearing, experienced particular success among primary school pupils in the [[United Kingdom]], where some schools took to banning them for a variety of reasons, including peer pressure, causing distraction from work, the impairment of pupils' concentration, and violation of school dress policies.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2017/03/06/mums-angered-jojo-bows-banned-schools-across-country/|title=Parents angered as 'Jojo Bows' banned in schools across the country|last=Horton|first=Helena|date=2017-03-06|work=The Telegraph|access-date=2018-04-17|issn=0307-1235}}</ref> Due to the bows' popularity, some stores also began selling similar bows.<ref name="Palmer">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-39140210|title=Why schools are banning 'JoJo bows'|last=Palmer|first=Kate|date=March 2, 2017|work=BBC News|access-date=April 17, 2018}}</ref> In 2017, Siwa commented that her bows were a "symbol of power, confidence, believing-ness."<ref name="Palmer"/>

Siwa subsequently partnered with the American department store [[J. C. Penney]] to release a line of accessories, bedroom decor, and a doll made in her likeness, and has released several books aimed at young children.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.christiantoday.com/article/jojo-siwa-inspires-a-new-clothing-line-by-jcpenney/126726.htm|title=JoJo Siwa inspires a new clothing line by JCPenney|website=www.christiantoday.com|language=en|access-date=2018-04-09}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.kqed.org/pop/96314|title=The Wisdom of JoJo Siwa, the 14-Year-Old Megastar You Didn't Know Was a Megastar|date=October 16, 2017|work=KQED|access-date=April 9, 2018}}</ref>

In June 2019, the [[FDA]] issued a warning about dangerous levels of [[asbestos]] in a JoJo Siwa-branded cosmetics kit, prompting Claire's to recall the product.<ref name=FDA>{{cite tweet|number=1136758750256271365|title=FDA Safety Alert|author=Food and Drug Administration|author-link=Food and Drug Administration|user=US_FDA|date=June 6, 2019|access-date=June 15, 2019}}</ref><ref name=USAToday>{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/allthemoms/2019/06/09/jojo-siwa-claires-makeup-kit-recalled-after-fda-finds-asbestos/1402366001/|title=Claire's recalls JoJo Siwa's makeup kit for tweens after finding asbestos in it|first=Sonja|last=Haller|newspaper=[[USA Today]]|date=June 9, 2019|access-date=June 15, 2019}}</ref>

Siwa signed with [[Nickelodeon]] in 2017, appearing in the film ''[[Blurt!]]'' with [[Jace Norman]] and the show ''[[Lip Sync Battle Shorties]]'' with [[Nick Cannon]]. Siwa later appeared in [[The Masked Singer (American season 3)|season 3]] of ''[[The Masked Singer (American TV series)|The Masked Singer]]'' as "T-Rex". At the age of 16, she was the youngest contestant to appear on the show until [[Shannon family|Honey Boo Boo]] performed as one half of "Beach Ball" in [[The Masked Singer (American season 6)|season six]]. Siwa appeared as a playable character in the 2020 video game ''[[Nickelodeon Kart Racers 2: Grand Prix]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Scullion|first=Chris|date=October 9, 2020|title=Review: Nickelodeon Kart Racers 2: Grand Prix - A Massive Improvement On The Terrible Original|url=https://www.nintendolife.com/reviews/nintendo-switch/nickelodeon_kart_racers_2_grand_prix|access-date=July 26, 2021|website=Nintendo Life}}</ref>

In January 2021, [[Spin Master]] created a card game called "JoJo's Juice", which contained questions about nudity and theft, despite being marketed toward children ages 6 and older.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Tenbarge|first=Kat|title=A JoJo Siwa card game for kids was pulled after parents criticized 'inappropriate' questions about kissing, being naked, twerking, and stealing|url=https://www.insider.com/jojo-siwa-jojos-juice-game-grooming-inappropriate-questions-2021-1|access-date=2021-01-06|website=Insider}}</ref> Siwa later apologized and claimed she was not involved in the game's content development; the game was recalled from all toy store shelves.<ref>{{Cite web|author=Marika Gerken|title=JoJo Siwa responds to board game controversy saying she 'had no idea' about the 'inappropriate' content|url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/01/05/us/jojo-siwa-game-trnd/index.html|access-date=2021-01-06|website=CNN}}</ref>

Siwa competed on the [[Dancing with the Stars (American season 30)|thirtieth season]] of ''[[Dancing with the Stars (American TV series)|Dancing with the Stars]]'', in which she was part of the first same-sex dancing partnership in the show's history.<ref>{{cite news|url = https://www.cnn.com/2021/09/08/entertainment/dancing-with-stars-new-cast/index.html|title = 'Dancing With the Stars' Season 30 cast revealed|work = [[CNN]]|last = Respers|first = Lisa France|date = September 8, 2021|accessdate = September 8, 2021}}</ref> She and her partner, [[Jenna Johnson (dancer)|Jenna Johnson]], ultimately placed 2nd.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Thomas|first=Megan|title='Dancing with the Stars' Season 30 ends with historic win|url=https://www.cnn.com/2021/11/23/entertainment/dwts-season-30-finale-recap/index.html|access-date=2021-11-23|website=CNN}}</ref>

She is one of [[TIME Magazine's 100 most influential people of 2004|TIME Magazine's 100 Most Influential People]].<ref>{{cite news|url = https://www.usatoday.com/picture-gallery/entertainment/tv/2021/09/28/dancing-with-the-stars-meet-the-celebrity-dancers-contestants/8376030002/|title = 'Dancing With the Stars' Season 30: Which celebrity dancers have been eliminated?|work = [[USA Today]]|date = October 27, 2021|accessdate = October 29, 2021}}</ref>
