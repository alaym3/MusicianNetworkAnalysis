'''Eric Aldwin Bellinger, Jr.''' (born March 26, 1984)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://digitaldjpool.com/artists/eric-bellinger|title = Eric Bellinger Music at Digital DJ Pool}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter, and vocal producer from [[Los Angeles, California]]. Bellinger began songwriting in 2010, and his co-writing credits including  "[[Lemme See]]" by [[Usher (singer)|Usher]], "[[New Flame]]" by [[Chris Brown]], "You Don't Know" by [[Tank (American singer)|Tank]], "[[Right Here (Justin Bieber song)|Right Here]]" by [[Justin Bieber]], and "Disrespectful" by [[Trey Songz]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ericbellinger.com/discography/|title=Eric Bellinger|website=Eric Bellinger|access-date=2017-03-03}}</ref> In 2011, Bellinger won a [[Grammy]] in the category of [[Best R&B Album]] for contribution to Chris Brown's album ''[[F.A.M.E. (Chris Brown album)|F.A.M.E.]]''. In 2014, Bellinger renewed focus on his recording career and released his debut album, ''The Rebirth'' in February of that year. He then released two [[extended play]]s with [[300 Entertainment]], ''Eric B for President: Term 1'' in 2016, and ''Eric B for President: Term 2'' in 2017.

==Early life==
Bellinger grew up in [[Los Angeles, California]] but attended schools in the [[Norwalk, California|Norwalk]]/[[Santa Fe Springs]] area, where he met his future manager Nieman Johnson, the grandson of [[The Jackson 5|Jackson 5]] songwriter [[Bobby Day]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/interview-eric-bellinger-talks-rebirth-album-usher-chris-brown-influence-positive-vibe-music|title=Interview: Eric Bellinger Talks The Rebirth Album, Usher & Chris Brown Influence, Positive Vibe in his Music - YouKnowIGotSoul.com|date=2014-10-03|access-date=2017-03-03|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Career==
===''The Writing Camp'' and early career===
Bellinger had earned a [[football scholarship]] to [[University of Southern California]], but rejected the offer to record and tour with [[R&B]] group AKNU. The group was signed to [[Epic Records]] by executives Max Gousse and [[Tommy Mottola]], and in 2010 he was invited to work with songwriting collective [[The Writing Camp]] by childhood friend and mentor Erika Nuri.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/interview-eric-bellinger-talks-rebirth-album-usher-chris-brown-influence-positive-vibe-music|title=Interview: Eric Bellinger Talks The Rebirth Album, Usher & Chris Brown Influence, Positive Vibe in his Music - YouKnowIGotSoul.com|date=2014-10-03|access-date=2017-04-10|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name="theboombox.com">{{Cite web|url=http://theboombox.com/eric-bellinger-cuffing-season-album-interview/|title=Eric Bellinger Talks 'Cuffing Season' Album, Working With Game|website=The Boombox|language=en-US|access-date=2017-04-10}}</ref> Bellinger first co-wrote for [[Pop music|pop]] acts such as [[Selena Gomez & the Scene|Selena Gomez and the Scene]], [[Jacob Latimore]], and [[Big Time Rush (band)|Big Time Rush]], until 2012 when he contributed to the song “Death Penalty” on Los Angeles [[rapper]] [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]]'s mixtape ''[[California Republic (mixtape)|California Republic]]''.<ref name="theboombox.com"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.vibe.com/2012/04/new-mixtape-game-california-republic/|title=New Mixtape: Game 'California Republic'|date=2012-04-05|work=Vibe|access-date=2017-04-10}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ericbellinger.com/discography/|title=Eric Bellinger|website=Eric Bellinger|access-date=2017-04-10}}</ref> Bellinger's connection with The Game led to him writing and featuring on several songs off The Game's album ''[[The Documentary 2]]'', for which he received greater recognition and signed a publishing deal with [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing|Sony ATV]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/eric-bellinger/profile/|title=Eric Bellinger|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=2017-04-10}}</ref> However, complications with Bellinger's songwriting catalogue caused him to leave Sony ATV to sign with publisher [[Broadcast Music, Inc.|BMI]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://singersroom.com/content/2013-01-15/publisher-bmi-signs-singersongwriter-eric-bellinger/|title=BMI Signs Singer/Songwriter Eric Bellinger - Singersroom.com|date=2013-01-15|work=Singersroom.com|access-date=2017-04-10|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Solo career and ''The Rebirth''===
In 2013, Bellinger released a three-part series of mixtapes: ''Born II Sing Vol. 1'',<ref name=":1" /> ''Born II Sing Vol. 2'',<ref name=":2" /> and ''Born II Sing Vol. 3''.<ref name=":3" /> Bellinger released his debut [[studio album]], ''The Rebirth'', on May 17, 2014, as a [[double album]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/full-album-stream-for-eric-bellinger-s-rebirth-news.9288.html|title=Full Album Stream For Eric Bellinger's "ReBirth"|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=2017-04-10}}</ref> ''The Rebirth'' peaked at 23 on the [[U.S. R&B/Hip-Hop|US R&B/Hip Hop]] charts, and 15 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|US R&B chart]], and also entered the [[Heatseekers Albums|Heatseakers Albums]] chart.<ref name=":4" /> Bellinger followed ''The Rebirth'' with the studio albums, ''Cuffing Season'' and ''Cuffing Season, Part 2'', which released in July 2015 and December 2015, respectively. ''Cuffing Season'' peaked at 6 on the US R&B chart, while ''Cuffing Season, Part 2'' peaked at 23.<ref name=":5" /><ref name=":6" /> Bellinger released two mixtapes in the spring of 2016, ''Choose Up Season'' and ''Eventually''.<ref name=":7" /> ''Choose Up Season'' featured the song "Valet" featuring [[Fetty Wap]] and [[2 Chainz]].<ref name=":8" /> Between 2016 and 2017, Bellinger released three [[extended plays]] as part of the ''Eric B. for President'' series, the title referencing the [[Eric B. & Rakim|Eric B & Rakim]] song "[[Eric B. Is President]]". The ''Eric B. for President'' series featured two full-instrumental extended plays: ''Eric B for President: Term 1'', released on September 9, 2016, an [[Acoustic music|acoustic]] remake of the EP titled ''Term 1, titled Eric B for President: Term 1 (Acoustic)'', released on January 20, 2017, and ''Eric B for President: Term 2'', released on March 10, 2017.<ref name=":9" /><ref name=":10" /> G.O.A.T. (combiner two version) and Drive By Certifications Gold from [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=ERIC+BELLINGER#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum|website=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]}}</ref>

=== Awards ===
In 2011, Bellinger won a [[Grammy Award|Grammy]] in the category of [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Album|Best R&B Album]] for his work on ''[[F.A.M.E. (Chris Brown album)|F.A.M.E.]]'' by Chris Brown.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.grammy.com/artist/chris-brown|title=Chris Brown|date=2013-04-05|work=GRAMMY.com|access-date=2017-04-10|language=en}}</ref> In 2013, Bellinger co-wrote "[[Lemme See]]" by [[Usher (singer)|Usher]], which was named an [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP]] Award Winning R&B/Hip-Hop Song.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ascap.com/eventsawards/awards/rsawards/2013/r-and-b-hip-hop-songs|title=R&B/Hip-Hop Songs|work=www.ascap.com|access-date=2017-04-10}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

==Personal life==
Bellinger married [[La'Myia Good|La’Myia Good]] on January 1, 2015.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://singersroom.com/content/2015-01-28/eric-bellinger-and-megan-goods-sister-lamyia-good-are-married-and-expecting/|title=Eric Bellinger and Meagan Good's Sister La'Myia Good Are Married? And Expecting! - Singersroom.com|date=2015-01-28|work=Singersroom.com|access-date=2017-04-11|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2021/1/6/eric-bellinger-shares-his-romantic-love-story-with-lamiyah-g.html/|title= Eric Bellinger shares his romantic love story with La'Miyah Good-Bellinger|website=abcnewsradioonline.com}}</ref> Bellinger and Good met in the early 2000s in Los Angeles, where both were working on music with the same manager before losing contact for a few years, they re-connected later.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.essence.com/2015/01/30/baby-board-lamyia-good-eric-bellinger-tying-knot-maternity-pictures|title=Baby On Board: La'Myia Good and Eric Bellinger on Tying the Knot, and That Maternity Shoot!|last=Ade-Brown|first=Lathleen|date=2015-01-30|website=Essence.com|access-date=2017-04-11}}</ref> Bellinger and Good have 2 sons, Elysha and Eazy Bellinger.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0ynH8tho3No/|title=Eric Bellinger Talks Going From Producer To Artist, His Wife La'Myia & More|website=Youtube}}</ref> Bellinger is the brother-in-law of [[Meagan Good]].
