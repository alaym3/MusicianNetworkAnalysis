'''Lucian Piane''' (born October 4, 1980),<ref>{{cite web  | title =Lucian Piane Theatre Credits on BroadwayWorld.com  | work =Broadway World  | publisher =BroadwayWorld.com  | date =2009  | url =http://broadwayworld.com/people/?personid=8683  | accessdate = 2009-02-04 }}</ref> also known by the [[Internet]] nickname '''RevoLucian''', is an [[United States of America|American]] [[composer]] and [[record producer|music producer]]. He has composed music for several films, television shows, theater productions and singers, and received a [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] [[Music recording sales certification|Platinum Album Award]] for his production work on ''[[Hairspray (2007 soundtrack)|Hairspray: Soundtrack to the Motion Picture]]''. Piane has also achieved [[List of Internet phenomena|Internet fame]] under the name RevoLucian for his satirical [[techno]] [[remix]]es, most notably "[[Bale Out]]", which featured profanity-laced tirades from actor [[Christian Bale]] made on the set of ''[[Terminator Salvation]]''.

==Career==
Piane graduated from [[New York University]] in 2003 with a [[Bachelor of Music]] in [[Musical composition|Composition]].<ref name="Bio" />  He has worked closely with composer and songwriter [[Marc Shaiman]]; the two have collaborated on such films as ''[[Team America: World Police]]'', ''[[Rumor Has It...]]'' and ''[[Hairspray (2007 film)|Hairspray]]'', as well as the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] show ''[[Martin Short: Fame Becomes Me]]'',<ref>{{cite book  | last =Viagas  | first =Robert   |author2=Aubrey Reuben |author3=Ben Strothmann   | title =The Playbill Broadway Yearbook: June 2006 &ndash; May 2007  | publisher =Hal Leonard Corporation  | year =2007  | page =270  | isbn =978-1-55783-732-5}}</ref> and the [[76th Academy Awards]].<ref name="Bio" /> Piane received a [[Recording Industry Association of America]] [[Music recording sales certification|Platinum Album Award]] for his production work on [[New Line Records]]' ''[[Hairspray (2007 soundtrack)|Hairspray: Soundtrack to the Motion Picture]]'', which was also nominated for a [[Grammy Award]].<ref name="Bio">{{cite web  | last =Piane  | first =Lucian  | title =Biography  | work =Lucian Piane &mdash; Composer • Songwriter • Producer  | publisher =www.revolucian.com  | url =http://www.revolucian.com/bio.html  | accessdate =2009-02-04  | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20081121161319/http://www.revolucian.com/bio.html  | archive-date =2008-11-21  | url-status =dead  }}</ref> Piane scored the music for the 2006 musical ''[[In Your Dreams (musical)|In Your Dreams]]'', which was written by Zeke Farrow.<ref>{{cite news   | last =BWW News Desk   | title ='In Your Dreams' Penned By Shaiman Protege Gets Showcase  | work =Broadway World  | publisher =BroadwayWorld.com  | date =April 27, 2006  | url =http://broadwayworld.com/article/In_You_Dreams_Penned_By_Shaiman_Protege_Gets_Showcase_20060427  | accessdate = 2009-02-04 }}</ref>

Piane has composed scores for the [[HBO]] documentary ''An Omar Broadway Film'' and the film ''[[Pretty Ugly People]]''. He wrote the song "Year End Rap" which blogger [[Perez Hilton]] performed on his [[VH1]] special, ''What Perez Sez''. Piane also wrote the song "Stunning" for [[Calpernia Addams]] for her reality television show, ''[[Transamerican Love Story]]''. Piane lives in [[Los Angeles, California]], and in 2009 worked on the album ''[[Champion (RuPaul album)|Champion]]'' by [[RuPaul]].<ref name="Bio" /><ref name="rantremixed" /> Piane composed the music for the [[2009 in film|2009]] [[horror film]], ''[[Sorority Row]]'',<ref>{{cite news| title =Sorority Row Review  | work = [[Variety magazine]] | publisher =www.variety.com | date = September 9, 2009| url = https://www.variety.com/review/VE1117940992.html?categoryid=31&cs=1 | accessdate = 2010-02-02 | first=Russell | last=Edwards}}</ref><ref name="grierson" /> and arranged music for a song and video called "The Clap" by [[Perez Hilton]], recorded for the motion picture soundtrack of the film ''[[Another Gay Sequel: Gays Gone Wild]]''. In 2011 he teamed up with RuPaul again producing the album ''[[Glamazon (album)|Glamazon]]''.<ref>{{cite news   | last =Potton   | first =Ed   | title =No joke: Perez Hilton takes up singing  | work =[[The Times]]  | publisher =Times Newspapers Ltd.  | date =February 6, 2009  | url =http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/music/article5669824.ece  | accessdate = 2009-02-06 | location=London}}</ref> On February 24, 2014
his third album with RuPaul, ''[[Born Naked]]'', was released.

==Controversies==

On November 3, 2016, Piane generated controversy when he sent out a series of [[Twitter|tweets]] and Instagram posts that implied the Clintons had murdered people, that [[Michelle Obama]] was an "idiot" and various other conspiracies. His tweets were criticized for being racist, often using [[ethnic slurs]]. This led to a major backlash against his longstanding ties with [[RuPaul]], who came to Piane's defense, attributing the incidents to mental illness.<ref name="Taylor">{{cite news|last1=Taylor|first1=Jeff|title='Drag Race' judge Lucian Piane Twitter meltdown targets Obama, Clinton, Ellen|url=http://www.lgbtqnation.com/2016/11/drag-race-judge-lucian-piane-twitter-meltdown-targets-obama-clinton-ellen/|accessdate=5 November 2016|publisher=LGBTQ Nation|date=4 Nov 2016}}</ref><ref name="Broverman">{{cite news|last1=Broverman|first1=Neal|title=Drag Race Judge Lucian Piane Sinks Low in Epic Twitter Meltdown|url=http://www.advocate.com/people/2016/11/03/drag-race-judge-lucian-piane-sinks-low-epic-twitter-meltdown|accessdate=4 November 2016|publisher=Advocate|date=3 Nov 2016}}</ref><ref name="Queerty">{{cite news|title=Gay "Drag Race" Judge Goes On Crazed Twitter Rant, Slams The Clintons And Obamas|url=https://www.queerty.com/gay-drag-race-judge-goes-crazed-twitter-rant-slams-clintons-obamas-20161103|accessdate=4 November 2016|publisher=Queerty|date=3 Nov 2016}}</ref> Piane later claimed he made the posts while in a state of “marijuana psychosis”.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.washingtonblade.com/2017/02/01/lucian-piane-apologizes-racist-twitter-meltdown/|title=Lucian Piane apologizes for Twitter meltdown, blames ‘marijuana psychosis’|last=Cooper|first=Mariah|date=February 1, 2017|website=Washington Blade}}</ref>

In August 2019, Piane was arrested multiple times, most notably on August 27th after refusing to leave a [[Chase Bank|Chase bank]]. After the arrests became public knowledge, his family revealed that he was undergoing mental health treatment.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://metro.co.uk/2019/08/30/former-rupauls-drag-race-judge-lucian-piane-arrested-gunpoint-trespassing-10658761/|title=Former RuPaul’s Drag Race judge Lucian Piane ‘arrested at gunpoint for trespassing’|last=Griffin|first=Louise|date=August 30, 2019|website=Metro}}</ref>
