'''Bastille''' (stylised as '''BΔSTILLE''') are <!-- Do not change "are" to "is". Groups that originate from Great Britain are treated plural. See WP:ENGVAR for more info. --> an English [[pop rock]] band formed in 2010. The group began as a solo project by lead vocalist [[Dan Smith (singer)|Dan Smith]], but later expanded to include keyboardist Kyle Simmons, bassist and guitarist Will Farquarson and drummer Chris "Woody" Wood.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/features/bastille-nme-interview|title=Bastille: The Band The World Fell Head Over Heels For|last=Stubbs|first=Dan|date=7 July 2016|website=nme.com|access-date=8 July 2016}}</ref><ref name="auto">{{Cite news|title=Bastille takes a cinematic journey on stage and across the US|work=Charlotte Observer|url=https://www.charlotteobserver.com/entertainment/music-news-reviews/article148954174.html}}</ref>

After an independently released debut single and a self-released EP, the band signed to [[Virgin Records]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title = EMI signs Bastille and Eric Prydz to Virgin Records|url = http://www.musicweek.com/news/read/emi-signs-bastille-and-eric-prydz-to-virgin-records/047494|access-date = 18 June 2015|date = 1 December 2011|website = musicweek.com|publisher = NewBay Media|others = Newsdesk}}</ref> Their first studio album, ''Bad Blood'', was released in March 2013 and entered the [[UK Albums Chart]] at number one and included the hit single "[[Pompeii (song)|Pompeii]]" which peaked at number two on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. The band was nominated for four [[Brit Awards]] at the [[2014 Brit Awards|2014 ceremony]], winning the [[British Breakthrough Act]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Brits 2014: Winners in full|url = https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-25674995|website = bbc.co.uk|access-date = 18 June 2015|date = 19 February 2014|publisher = BBC|others = 7newsdesk}}</ref> As of August 2021, Bastille have sold over eleven million records worldwide.<ref name="Myers">{{cite web|url = http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-brit-awards-2014-biggest-selling-nominees-revealed-real-winners-2298/|title = The BRIT Awards 2014: The biggest selling nominees revealed|date = 19 February 2014|publisher = [[Official Charts Company]]|last = Myers|first = Justin|website = officialcharts.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.https://chuffmedia.com/artists/bastille|title=Bastille - Chuffmedia|website=Chuffmedia|language=en|access-date=21 August 2021}}</ref>

==History==

===Formation and early releases (2010–2011)===

[[File:Bastille logo.svg|thumb|Bastille logo]]Bastille debuted in July 2010 with their limited edition 7" single that featured the two tracks "Flaws" and "Icarus". Released through London-based independent record label Young & Lost Club, only 300 copies were pressed.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Bastille (4) – Flaws|url = http://www.discogs.com/Bastille-Flaws/release/3697903|website = Discogs|access-date = 24 June 2015}}</ref> One of the copies found its way into the hands of Alex Baker, presenter of the unsigned/independent artists radio show on Kerrang! Radio. Baker started to support Bastille on air, and the band began to find an audience. They released the EP ''[[Laura Palmer EP|Laura Palmer]]'' themselves later in 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Bastille (4) – Laura Palmer EP|url = http://www.discogs.com/Bastille-Laura-Palmer-EP/release/5534706|website = Discogs|access-date = 24 June 2015}}</ref> After debuting further tracks online through sites such as YouTube and MySpace, the band's audience started to grow.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title = Bastille: 'We've had a year of proving people wrong'|url = https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/jan/23/bastille-weve-enjoyed-proving-people-wrong|website = the Guardian|access-date = 24 June 2015|first = Peter|last = Robinson|date = 23 January 2014}}</ref> In December 2011 Smith signed a contract with Virgin Records.<ref name=":0" /> The group subsequently secured a few support slots and later performed at major UK festivals including [[Glastonbury Festival|Glastonbury]], the [[Isle of Wight Festival|Isle of Wight]], [[Redfest]], Reading and Leeds Festival and [[Blissfields]]. The band also recorded and released two parts of their mix-tape titled ''Other People's Heartache'' for free.<ref name=":1" />

===''Bad Blood'' and ''All This Bad Blood'' (2012–2015)===
[[File:Bastille at Queens' College May Ball 2013.JPG|thumb|right|Bastille in concert at [[Queens' College, Cambridge|Queens' College]] [[May Ball]], 2013]]
On 27 April 2012, their first official single release, "[[Overjoyed (Bastille song)|Overjoyed]]", was released through [[Virgin Records]], as confirmed by EMI on 1 May 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.emimusic.com/blog/2012/overjoyed-bastille-release-debut-virgin-single/ |title=Overjoyed Bastille release debut Virgin single |publisher=[[EMI]] |access-date=4 April 2013 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6HBIJ2WfD?url=http://www.emimusic.com/blog/2012/overjoyed-bastille-release-debut-virgin-single/ |archive-date=6 June 2013 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Following its video premiere in November 2011, popular music publication, [[Q (magazine)|''Q'' Magazine]] introduced the track as its 'Track of the Day' for 7 March 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.qthemusic.com/2012/03/bastille_-_overjoyed.html|title=Q Magazine – Music news & reviews, music videos, band pictures & interviews Magazine|access-date=11 March 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140218193348/http://news.qthemusic.com/2012/03/bastille_-_overjoyed.html|archive-date=18 February 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> The second single to be released from the upcoming debut studio album, ''[[Bad Blood (Bastille album)|Bad Blood]]'', was the eponymous track, "[[Bad Blood (Bastille song)|Bad Blood]]". An accompanying video for the single was released on the band's official VEVO channel on 29 June 2012, and digitally through Virgin Records on 20 August 2012. Bastille was also named 'New Band of the Day' by British newspaper ''[[The Guardian]]'' in July 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/jul/25/new-band-bastille|title=New band of the day – No 1,316: Bastille|author=Paul Lester|work=the Guardian|access-date=11 March 2015}}</ref> The song charted moderately at number 90 in the United Kingdom and saw the group's first entry into the chart. In August 2012 the band performed at UK festivals like [[Reading Festival|Reading]] and [[Reading Festival|Leeds]], as well as supporting British singer-songwriter [[Emeli Sandé]]. They later played a small headlining tour, titled 'Flaws Tour' in October 2012, with [[Swiss Lips]] as their support act. in November 2012, the band performed at Hong Kong's Clockenflap Music and Arts Festival, their first outside of Europe. Their third single, a re-release of their first 7" non-album single "[[Flaws (song)|Flaws]]", was a commercial success, earning the group their first position in the UK Top 40, debuting at number-twenty-one.

[[File:Provinssirock 20130614 - Bastille - 01.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Dan Smith at [[Provinssirock]] festival 2013 in [[Seinäjoki]], Finland]]
In February 2013, Bastille confirmed the release of the fourth single to be taken from ''Bad Blood'', "[[Pompeii (Bastille song)|Pompeii]]". The song was positively reviewed by critics, reaching number two in the United Kingdom and number five in the US. Their debut album ''Bad Blood'' was released on 4 March 2013, and placed at number one in the [[UK Albums Chart]]. On 11 March, Bastille was announced to be playing at the Reading and Leeds Festival in August 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://metro.co.uk/2013/03/11/green-day-aap-rocky-bastille-and-brand-new-all-added-to-reading-and-leeds-festivals-2013-3536896/|title=Green Day, Phoenix, Bastille & more for Reading/Leeds festivals 2013 – Metro News|work=Metro|access-date=11 March 2015}}</ref> In an interview with [[Digital Spy]] in March 2013, "[[Laura Palmer (Bastille song)|Laura Palmer]]" was revealed to be the fifth single to be released from the album, by lead vocalist, Dan Smith.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a464088/bastille-to-release-laura-palmer-as-new-single-listen.html|title=Bastille to release 'Laura Palmer' as new single – listen|work=Digital Spy|access-date=11 March 2015}}</ref> It was released on 3 June 2013, and an accompanying video for the track premiered through the band's official VEVO page on 12 April 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JQnSc0bczg0&list=LLAio3U50D3cfxHStvYG68Ew|title=Bastille – Laura Palmer|date=11 April 2013|work=YouTube|access-date=11 March 2015}}</ref> The group joined English rock band [[Muse (band)|Muse]] as a supporting act on their [[The 2nd Law Tour]] in May 2013 and did so again in June 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bastille added to the line up in the UK|url=http://muse.mu/news,bastille-added-to-the-line-up-in-the-uk_1534.htm|access-date=12 April 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170620055057/http://muse.mu/news,bastille-added-to-the-line-up-in-the-uk_1534.htm|archive-date=20 June 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> In May 2013, Bastille released their ''Haunt'' EP in the US via iTunes. On Saturday 6 July the band headlined their first UK summer music festival at [[Blissfields]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Bastille added to the line up in the UK|url=http://www.efestivals.co.uk/news/13/130204d.shtml|access-date=4 February 2013}}</ref> Smith was quoted as saying "We're massively excited about our first ever headline slot at a festival. Particularly because we love Blissfields so much, and because they've been so supportive to us over the last couple of years." The performance coincided with the band appearing on the cover of a national music publication, [[Notion magazine]], for the first time. On 24 August 2013, "[[Things We Lost in the Fire (song)|Things We Lost in the Fire]]" was released as the sixth single from the band's debut album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/_/bastille/|title=Bastille|access-date=11 March 2015}}</ref> A music video for the song was shot in [[Vilnius]] and [[Kėdainiai]], Lithuania.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gigwise.com/news/82948/watch-bastilles-new-video-for-things-we-lost-in-the-fire|title=Watch: Bastille's new video for 'Things We Lost In The Fire'|author=Ben Lawrence|work=GIGWISE.com}}</ref> On 3 September 2013, Bastille released their ''Bad Blood'' album in the US via iTunes.{{Citation needed|date=August 2017}}

On 9 October 2013 Bastille released a new single, "[[Of the Night]]", a mash-up of the songs "[[The Rhythm of the Night]]" by [[Corona (group)|Corona]] and "[[Rhythm Is a Dancer]]" by [[Snap!]]. The single debuted at number two on UK Singles Chart. The song was promoting ''[[All This Bad Blood]]'', a reissue of the band's debut album, released on 25 November 2013. In January 2014 Will Farquarson said in an interview with ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine that the band had already started to work on its second album in between tour dates.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/5876951/bastille-working-on-sophomore-album-in-between-tour-dates |title=Bastille Working on Sophomore Album in Between Tour Dates |first=Gary |last=Graff |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=20 January 2014 |access-date=26 January 2014}}</ref> The band performed on ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' on 25 January 2014. On 4 February 2014 Bastille won the [[BRIT Awards|BRIT Award]] for Best Breakthrough Act and performed a remixed version of "Pompeii" with [[Rudimental]] and their song "[[Waiting All Night]]" at the [[2014 BRIT Awards|ceremony]]. Two hours after their appearance, sales of ''Bad Blood'' were up by 132% to give them a second week at number one in the UK album chart and "Pompeii" had climbed 29 places in the singles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/brits-2014-stars-see-hefty-sales-uplift-overnight-2303/|title=BRITs 2014 stars see hefty sales uplift overnight|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|date=20 February 2014|last=Myers|first=Justin}}</ref> On 1 April 2014 the Official Charts Company announced that Bad Blood was the biggest selling digital album of 2013, and Number 11 in the over-all Official Artist Albums Top 40 of 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-official-top-40-biggest-selling-artist-albums-of-2014-so-far-2874/|title=The Official Top 40 Biggest Selling Artist Albums of 2014 so far!|website=Officialcharts.com|access-date=2 December 2017}}</ref> Also in April 2014, the band performed at the [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival]] at the Empire Polo Club in [[Indio, California]]. On 31 May 2014, the band took part in the Annual [[KROQ Weenie Roast]] in [[Irvine, California]], performing among other bands including [[The Neighbourhood]] and [[Fall Out Boy]]. On 13 July 2014, Bastille played at [[T in the Park]]. In September 2014 they released the last single from ''Bad Blood'', "[[Oblivion (Bastille song)|Oblivion]]". The Bad Blood Tour finished in 2014.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.axs.com/bastille-announces-fall-2014-north-american-tour-dates-21243|title=Bastille announces fall 2014 North American tour dates|work=AXS|access-date=28 February 2019|language=en-US}}</ref> They were part of the supergroup [[Band Aid 30]] whose version of "[[Do They Know It's Christmas?]]" was released in November and featured other artists such as [[One Direction]] and [[Ed Sheeran]]. All proceeds of the song went towards helping the [[2014 Ebola crisis]].<ref name="bandaid30">{{Cite news |title=Elbow and Ed Sheeran among Band-Aid 30 artists |work=[[BBC News Online]] |date=10 November 2014 |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-29986704}}</ref> It became the band's first UK #1.

===''VS.'', ''Wild World'' (2014–2017)===
[[File:Bastille 2015-7.jpg|thumbnail|right|Dan Smith (2015)]]
In late 2013 Bastille began to play new songs called "Blame" and "Campus". Another song called "Oil On Water" was played during sound-check and leaked online.{{Citation needed|date=August 2017}} They began proper work on the album in 2014,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/bastilledan/status/440166635132170241|title=BASTILLE on Twitter|work=Twitter}}</ref> and planned to start recording it in September.<ref name="Xpress">{{cite web|url=http://xpressmag.com.au/bastille/|title=Bastille|work=X-press Magazine – Entertainment in Perth}}</ref> Farquarson said that they had "16 or 17 tracks" demoed already for the album. He also said “We’re going into the studio in September to record; hopefully by then we’ll have 20 or maybe more. I think it’s always better to have more material and whittle it down. Our producer has gone on tour with us, so we’ve been doing things on our days off and during sound-checks."<ref name="Xpress"/><ref name="Gigwise">{{cite web|url=http://www.gigwise.com/news/82716/Bastille-'Our-second-album-could-be-quite-guitar-heavy'|title=Bastille: 'Our second album could be quite guitar-heavy'|author=Andrew Trendell|work=GIGWISE.com}}</ref> Dan Smith said the album will include more guitars saying "we didn't have much of that on the first album and it may not end up on it but it would be quite interesting to try it out. We feel quite free. If we want to go quite electronic then we can and if we want to go rockier and indie then we can."<ref name="Gigwise"/> On 21 October 2014 Bastille announced their third mixtape, ''VS. (Other People's Heartache, Pt.III)'' with the release of their song "Torn Apart", featuring the artist's Grades and [[Lizzo]], on [[Zane Lowe]]'s BBC Radio 1 show. They also wrote and recorded the song "The Driver" for the re-score of the film ''[[Drive (2011 film)|Drive]]'' which was released a week later and also featured on the mixtape. ''VS. (Other People's Heartache, Pt.III)'' was released on 8 December 2014 and featured the Gemma Sharples Quartet, [[Haim (band)|Haim]], [[MNEK]], Tyde, Rationale, [[Lizzo]], Grades, [[Angel Haze]], F*U*G*Z, Braque, [[Rag'n'Bone Man]], and [[Skunk Anansie]]. In November 2014, Bastille was featured on the [[Band Aid 30]] version of "[[Do They Know It's Christmas?]]".<ref name="bandaid30"/> They were nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] at the [[57th Annual Grammy Awards]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.boom-asia.com/en/2015/01/07/interview-with-kyle-simmons-bastille|title=Interview with Bastille – Kyle Simmons {{!}} BOOM MAGAZINE|date=7 January 2015|website=BOOM MAGAZINE|language=en-US|access-date=8 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170829075712/https://www.boom-asia.com/en/2015/01/07/interview-with-kyle-simmons-bastille/|archive-date=29 August 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> but lost out to [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/videos/bastille-grammy-nomination-caps-an-amazing-year|title=Bastille: GRAMMY Nomination Caps An Amazing Year|date=6 February 2015|publisher=Grammy.com|access-date=4 January 2016}}</ref>

In 2015, Bastille recruited a new touring member, [[Charlie Barnes (musician)|Charlie Barnes]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/33284625/bastille-weve-been-experimenting-with-our-new-music|title=Bastille: 'We've been experimenting with our new music'|date=26 June 2015|website=BBC|access-date=7 July 2016}}</ref> On 22 September 2015, Bastille released an independent single called "Hangin{{'}}". It was announced via [[Facebook]] that their second album would be released in 2016. Also, during that summer, the band debuted two new songs live: "Grip" and "Snakes".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2015/06/17/bastille-debut-new-track-grip-during-current-festival-run|title=Bastille debut new track 'Grip' during current festival run|website=Diymag.com|access-date=2 December 2017}}</ref> In April 2016, the band debuted a new song entitled "The Currents".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/bastille/92821|title=Watch Bastille debut 'weird' new song 'The Currents' at Snowbombing festival|access-date=23 April 2016}}</ref> On 2 June 2016, the band uploaded a trailer including an excerpt from the then-unreleased song ''Two Evils'' announcing the name of the album to be [[Wild World (Bastille album)|''Wild World'']].<ref name="Wild World trailer">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/bastilleuk/videos/983608061674220/ |title=WWCOMMS. 001 // Hello |publisher=[[Facebook]] |date=2 June 2016 |access-date=15 June 2016}}</ref> On 9 June, it was announced that the band's new single, "Good Grief", would be released on 16 June 2016.{{citation needed|date=June 2016}} On 16 June 2016, [[Annie Mac]] debuted "[[Good Grief (Bastille song)|Good Grief]]" on her [[BBC Radio 1]] show as the "hottest record in the world." The track was immediately released to radio, streaming services, and for purchase following the debut.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/bastilledan/status/743509294851686400|title=BASTILLE on Twitter|website=Twitter|access-date=16 June 2016}}</ref> In an interview with Mac following the song's premiere, Dan Smith revealed that their second album would "go through a variety of sounds, but it is very much us [Bastille]." On 24 June 2016, Bastille performed the songs "Good Grief" and "Two Evils" live for the first time at [[Glastonbury Festival]] as part of their set on the Other Stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/ewcj5v/acts/adfqwh|title=Glastonbury 2016 – Bastille|website=Bbc.co.uk|access-date=2 December 2017}}</ref> On 30 June 2016, Bastille announced the release of the second LP ''Wild World'' and embarked on their Wild Wild World Tour in July.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7423976/bastille-announce-arena-tour-new-album-wild-world|title=Bastille Announce Arena Tour & New Album, 'Wild World'|publisher=Billboard}}</ref> Released on 9 September, the album reached the top of the mid-week sales chart,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/showbiz/celebrity-news/bastille-storming-charts-again-with-album-made-in-windowless-london-studio-a3344566.html|title=Bastille storming charts again with album made in windowless studio|last=Foster|first=Alistair|date=14 September 2016|website=evening standard|language=en-GB|access-date=15 September 2016}}</ref> while the band set up pop-up shops in London, New York and Los Angeles to promote the release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-blogs/bastille-dan-pop-up-shop|title=Dan Smith Invites Us Into Bastille's Dystopian Office At Their London Pop-Up Shop|last=Daily|first=Rhian|date=13 September 2016|website=NME|language=en-GB|access-date=15 September 2016}}</ref> On 16 September, it was confirmed that ''Wild World'' had reached #1 on both the U.K. and Scottish album charts.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bastille - Artist - Official Charts|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/_/Bastille/|website=UK Albums Chart|access-date=17 September 2016}}</ref> The album won "Best Album" at the [[NME Awards 2017|2017 NME Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/bastille-triumph-best-album-supported-hmv-category-vo5-nme-awards-2017-1976576|title=Bastille triumph in Best Album supported by HMV category at VO5 NME Awards 2017|website=[[NME]]|access-date=20 May 2020}}</ref> On [[Record Store Day|World Record Store Day]] 2017, the band released an original song titled "Comfort of Strangers."

The band was part of the '[[Artists for Grenfell]]' charity single. Including other artists such as [[Stormzy]] and [[Rita Ora]], the cover of the [[Simon & Garfunkel]] song "[[Bridge over Troubled Water (song)|Bridge Over Troubled Water]]" was released on 21 June 2017 to raise money for the victims of the [[Grenfell Tower fire]]. It became the band's second UK #1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/grenfell-tower-single-bridge-over-troubled-water-enters-at-number-1-less-than-48-hours-after-release__19495/|title=Grenfell Tower single Bridge Over Troubled Water enters at Number 1 less than 48 hours after release|website=officialcharts.com|access-date=23 June 2017}}</ref>

===''Doom Days'' and ''Other People's Heartache, Pt. IV'' (2018–2019)===
[[File:Bastille at IOW 2019.jpg|alt=Dan Smith of Bastille in the crowd at the 2019 Isle of Wight Festival|left|thumb|235x235px|Bastille performing at the 2019 [[Isle of Wight Festival]]]]The band announced their third studio album, which was released on 14 June 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8509713/bastille-tour-dates|title=Bastille Announce 'Doom Days' Album Release Date, North American Tour|last=Aniftos|first=Rania|website=Billboard|language=en|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref> Speaking to [[NME]], Smith said "We never really stop writing and recording, but I can confirm we spent a lot of this year writing our third album."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/bastille-reveal-third-album-details-nme-interview-2128760|title=Bastille reveal third album details in NME cover interview|date=25 August 2017|work=NME|access-date=25 August 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> On 1 September 2017, the band headlined the annual Pilton Party<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/information/pilton-party-2017/|title=Glastonbury Festival - Pilton Party|first=Pretty Good|last=Digital|website=Glastonbury Festival - 21–25 June 2017|access-date=27 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/2017-pilton-party-with-bastille-is-a-sell-out/|title=Glastonbury Festival - 2017 Pilton Party with Bastille is a sell out!|first=Pretty Good|last=Digital|website=Glastonbury Festival - 21–25 June 2017|access-date=27 March 2018}}</ref> Held at Pilton Worthy Farm, home of [[Glastonbury Festival]]. On 23 November 2017, [[Craig David]] and Bastille released "[[I Know You (Craig David song)|I Know You]],” the second single from David's album, ''[[The Time Is Now (album)|The Time Is Now]].'' The single peaked at number 5 in 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/ed-sheeran-claims-first-number-1-single-and-album-of-2018-on-the-official-chart__21377/|title=Ed Sheeran claims first Number 1 single and album of 2018|access-date=21 March 2018|language=en}}</ref>

On 9 May 2018, "[[Quarter Past Midnight]]", the first single from the group's third album ''[[Doom Days]]'' was aired on BBC Radio 1.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Trendell |first1=Andrew |title=Check out Bastille's euphoric new single "Quarter Past Midnight" |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/bastille-new-track-quarter-past-midnight-2306521 |access-date=19 June 2018 |publisher=NME |date=9 May 2018}}</ref>

==Name and musical style==

The name of the band derives from [[Bastille Day]], which is celebrated on 14 July, Smith's birthday.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Bastille's storming success |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/rockandpopfeatures/10400588/Bastilles-storming-success.html |access-date=18 June 2015 |date=25 October 2013 |work=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |last=McCormick |first=Neil}}</ref>

Bastille's musical style has been described as [[indie pop]]<ref name="thecrcconnection">{{cite web|url=https://www.thecrcconnection.com/features-arts-entertainment/2016/10/05/indie-pop-group-bastille-releases-sophomore-album/|title=Indie-pop group Bastille releases sophomore album|last=Smith|first=Stan|date=5 October 2016|access-date=4 September 2020}}</ref> [[alternative rock]],<ref name="auto"/> [[synth-pop]],<ref>{{cite news|author=John D. Buchanan|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/bastille-mn0002825814/biography|title=Bastille AllMusic|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=1 January 2021}}</ref> [[electropop]],<ref name="scotsman">{{cite news|first= David |last= Pollock |date= 1 March 2014 |title= Gig review: Bastille, Edinburgh |newspaper= The Scotsman |url= http://www.scotsman.com/lifestyle/culture/music/gig-review-bastille-edinburgh-1-3324332 |archive-date= 26 January 2016 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20160126114505/http://www.scotsman.com/lifestyle/culture/music/gig-review-bastille-edinburgh-1-3324332}}</ref> and [[pop rock]].<ref name="thecrcconnection2">{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/ontheverge/2013/09/28/on-the-verge-bastille-delivers-pop-rock-optimism/2805697/|title=On the Verge: Bastille delivers pop-rock optimism|last=Ryan|first=Patrick|date=28 September 2013|access-date=4 September 2020}}</ref>

The band performed "Bad Decisions" on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8531410/bastille-fallon-bad-decisions|title=Bastille storm 'Fallon' with 'Bad Decisions': Watch|website=Billboard|access-date=22 May 2020}}</ref> Their mashup of [[Joy Crookes]]' song "Anyone But Me" and [[Easy Life]]'s song "Nightmares", was shown on [[Channel 4]] as part of highlights from ''[[Barclaycard]] Share The Stage'' highlights (2020).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tvhighlights.bradfordzone.co.uk/television/tvprogrammes/barclaycard-share-this-stage-channel-4-31-august-1200am/|title=Barclaycard Share This Stage|website=tvhighlights.bradfordzone.com|access-date=31 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dAHcmDwwc3g|title='Anyone But Me x Nightmares' recorded in Abbey Road Studio 1 for Barclaycard Share The Stage|website=[[YouTube]]|access-date=31 August 2020}}</ref>

===Award ceremonies===
The band performed "[[Pompeii (song)|Pompeii]]" at the [[2014 Brit Awards|Brit Awards (2014)]] as part of a mashup with [[Ella Eyre]] and [[Rudimental]] with their song "[[Waiting All Night]]". They also performed the song at the [[2014 iHeartRadio Music Awards|iHeartRadio Music Awards (2014)]]. They performed "[[Happier (Marshmello and Bastille song)|Happier]]" at the [[2018 MTV Europe Music Awards|MTV EMAs (2018)]] as part of a collaboration with [[Marshmello]] and [[Anne-Marie (singer)|Anne-Marie]], who sang their song "[[Friends (Marshmello and Anne-Marie song)|Friends]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/ema/news/2018-ema-annemarie-bastille-and-marshmellos-explosive-medley-closes-the-show|title=2018 ema: Anne-marie, bastille and marshmello's explosive medley closes the show|website=MTV|access-date=21 May 2020}}</ref>

===Game of Thrones===
In 2017, all members of the band, and tour manager Dick Meredith, featured as extras in the final episode of [[Game of Thrones (season 7)|season 7]] of ''[[Game of Thrones]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/tv/news/game-of-thrones-bastille-cameo-season-7-finale-the-dragon-and-the-wold-a7917566.html|title=Game of Thrones: You probably missed Bastille's cameo in the season 7 finale|website=Independent|access-date=21 May 2020}}</ref> Simmons and Meredith made the cut, whereas Smith, Wood, and Farquarson were cut out.
