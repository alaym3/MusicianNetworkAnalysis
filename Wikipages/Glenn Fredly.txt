'''Glenn Fredly Deviano Latuihamallo''' (30 September 1975 – 8 April 2020) was an [[Indonesians|Indonesian]] [[R&B]] singer and songwriter as well as producer and actor. Entering the music industry after winning a singing contest in 1995, he released more than 10 albums. He produced new talented artists like female vocalist [[Yura (Indonesian singer)|Yura]] and male soloist Gilbert Pohan. He also produced ''[[Cahaya Dari Timur: Beta Maluku]]''. In 2013, he served as a coach for ''[[The Voice Indonesia]]''.

==Career==
In 1995, he won the [[Cipta Pesona Bintang]] singing contest.{{sfn|Kurniasari 2009-08-07, Get ready}} That same year, he joined the rock band Funk Section.{{sfn|IndonesiaSelebriti.com, Glenn Fredly}} The following year, he was a finalist in the [[Asia Song Festival]].{{sfn|Kurniasari 2009-08-07, Get ready}} In 1998, Glenn left Funk Section to be a solo R&B singer, feeling that it suited his vocal style better. He released his first album, ''GLENN'', that year. It was relatively well received, selling 50 thousand copies; R&B was generally not popular in Indonesia at the time.{{sfn|IndonesiaSelebriti.com, Glenn Fredly}}

In 2000, Glenn released his second solo album, ''Kembali'' (''Return''), with the single "Kasih Putih" ("White Love") receiving much airplay.{{sfn|IndonesiaSelebriti.com, Glenn Fredly}} In 2001, he placed third in the Russian Asia Dauzy International Song Festival.{{sfn|Kurniasari 2009-08-07, Get ready}}

In August 2002, he released one of his most successful album, titled ''Selamat Pagi, Dunia'' (Good Morning, World).

He married singer [[Dewi Sandra]] in [[Uluwatu]], [[Bali]] on 3 April 2006. Glenn filed for divorce in 2009.{{sfn|The Jakarta Post 2009-03-25, Glenn files}}

In March 2009, Glenn was one of the main performers in a tribute concert to deceased Indonesian singer [[Chrisye]] during the [[Java Jazz Festival]].{{sfn|The Jakarta Post 2009-03-07, Glenn to sing}} That same year, he performed tribute concerts to [[Michael Jackson]] in five cities throughout Indonesia, covering Jackson's hit songs.{{sfn|Kurniasari 2009-08-07, Get ready}}

In 2011, he joined Tompi and [[Sandhy Sondoro]] to perform a set of concerts under the name Trio Lestari (The Eternal Trio).{{sfn|Fawilia 2011-06-26, An evening}}

Glenn celebrated his 17th anniversary in the music business with a sold-out concert in front of 7,500 people at [[Istora Gelora Bung Karno]] on 2 September 2012.

In 2014, he entered film producing with the film ''[[Cahaya Dari Timur: Beta Maluku]]'', which received the best movie award from the [[Indonesian Film Festival]].

==Death==
Glenn died on 8 April 2020 in Setia Mitra Hospital in [[South Jakarta]] at the age of 44. It was reported that his cause of death was [[meningitis]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Glenn Fredly Meninggal karena Sakit Meningitis|url=https://kumparan.com/kumparanhits/glenn-fredly-meninggal-karena-sakit-meningitis-1tBV6Z3yxZD|language=id|website=Kumparan.com|date=8 April 2020}}</ref> He was married to his wife, Mutia Ayu, for 7.5 months (they were married on 19 August 2019). Their daughter, Gewa, was born on 28 February 2020.

==Influences==
Glenn credited [[Michael Jackson]] as being one of his influences, saying that Jackson inspired him "through his music, his style, his dancing, everything". He also credited Jackson's humanitarian work for inspiring his own charitable work.{{sfn|Kurniasari 2009-08-07, Get ready}} He also admired [[Marvin Gaye]] and [[Quincy Jones]],{{sfn|IndonesiaSelebriti.com, Glenn Fredly}} and was a fan of [[Chrisye]].{{sfn|The Jakarta Post 2009-03-07, Glenn to sing}}
