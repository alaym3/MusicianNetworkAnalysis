'''N.Flying''' ({{lang-ko|엔플라잉}}, [[Japanese language|Japanese]]: エヌフライング; short for New Flying which also means 'new wings' or 'new escape'<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cafe.daum.net/N.Flying/UDwb/3|title=N.Flying|publisher=Official Daum Cafe|access-date=2014-04-22|language=ko}}</ref>) is a South Korean [[rap rock]] band formed by [[FNC Entertainment]] in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sg.entertainment.yahoo.com/news/fnc-to-present-new-boy-band-n-flying-074044150.html|title=FNC to Present New Boy Band N Flying through 'Cheongdam-dong 111'|publisher=CJ E&M enewsWorld via Yahoo!|date=2014-03-12|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140313040218/http://sg.entertainment.yahoo.com/news/fnc-to-present-new-boy-band-n-flying-074044150.html|archive-date=March 13, 2014|df=mdy-all}}</ref> The group released its first digital indie single "Basket"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jpopasia.com/nflying/album/35437/basket/|title=JpopAsia|last=JpopAsia|website=JpopAsia}}</ref> in Japan on October 1, 2013. The group marked its debut in Korea on May 20, 2015, with its first EP, including lead single 'Awesome'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I4yiOSuk930|title=N.Flying (엔플라잉) - 기가 막혀 (Awesome) M/V|last=N.Flying (엔플라잉)|date=May 19, 2015|via=YouTube}}</ref> The band originally consisted of four members: Kwon Kwang-jin, Lee Seung-hyub, Cha Hun and Kim Jae-hyun. Yoo Hwe-seung was added to the band in June 2017.<ref name=yhs>{{Cite news|url=http://osen.mt.co.kr/article/G1110670211|title=[단독] '프듀2' FNC 연습생 유회승, 엔플라잉 신멤버 합류 확정|access-date=2017-06-19|language=ko}}</ref> Kwang-jin left the group on December 26, 2018, and his position was filled by Seo Dong-sung on January 1, 2020.

== History ==
=== Pre-debut ===
In 2009, Kwon Kwang-jin debuted as the bassist of labelmate [[CNBLUE]]. However, due to personal circumstances, Kwang-jin left the band in late September.<ref name="mwave.interest.me">{{cite web|url=http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/55017/nflying-kwangjin-was-originally-in-cnblue|title=One of N.Flying's Members was Originally a Member of CN Blue|date=January 3, 2014|publisher=CJ E&M enewsWorld|access-date=April 22, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140209122438/http://mwave.interest.me/enewsworld/en/article/55017/nflying-kwangjin-was-originally-in-cnblue|archive-date=February 9, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> In January 2011, Kwang-jin returned to train under FNC Entertainment meeting trainees Seung-hyub, Jae-hyun and Cha Hun. Prior to the debut of N.Flying, lead vocal Park Rai-On left the band in early 2013 due to mandatory military service in his native country of Singapore. FNC Entertainment subsequently confirmed that his contract was terminated to make way for the group's debut.<ref name="m.star.naver.com">{{cite web|url=https://m.star.naver.com/NFlying/news/end?id=11684050|title=FNC Trainee Park Rai-On officially removed from N.Flying|date=January 3, 2013|publisher=CJ E&M enewsWorld|access-date=January 3, 2013}}</ref>  The remaining members would go on to form N.Flying.

In 2013, Lee Seung-hyub appeared in [[Juniel]]'s music video titled ''Pretty Boy,'' playing her love interest she meets at a café.<ref name="youtube.com">{{youtube|lpwEghgpRxc|Juniel: Pretty Boy MV}}</ref>

=== 2013–2014: Japanese debut with ''Basket'', ''One and Only'' and delayed debut in Korea ===
N.Flying began their first performance at Shibuya CYCLONE on September 28.<ref name="facebook.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/officialnflying|title=NFlying|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref>
On October 1, they released their first indie single album titled ''Basket'' and performed as the opening act at [[F.T. Island]]'s 'Replay' Zepp tour.<ref name="facebook.com"/>
''BASKET'' peaked at number two on [[Tower Records]] weekly singles chart, number two on [[Oricon]] Indies weekly singles chart and number four on Oricon daily singles chart.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://topstarnews.net/detail.php?number=48508|title=엔플라잉(N.Flying), 일본 타워레코드 1위...괴물 신인|publisher= topstarnews.net}}</ref> 
The band continued doing live gigs at live clubs and also performed as opening act at CNBLUE's 'One More Time' Arena Tour.<ref name="facebook.com"/>
On November 14, N.Flying did their first one-man live at Live Space Halot.<ref name="facebook.com"/>

On January 1, N.Flying released their second Japanese indie single album titled ''One and Only''.<ref name="fncent.co.jp">http://fncent.co.jp/information/?category=1&infoid=55#info55 {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140103141456/http://fncent.co.jp/information/?category=1&infoid=55 |date=January 3, 2014 }}</ref> The album hit No.1 on [[Tower Records]] chart on its second day of release.<ref name="facebook.com"/>

They made their first television appearance on the 7th episode of FNC's reality drama, [[TVN (South Korea)|tvN]]'s ''[[Cheongdam-dong 111]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://topstarnews.net/detail.php?number=48374|title= 엔플라잉(N.Flying), 첫 선 'FT아일랜드-씨엔블루' 잇는다|publisher= TopStarNews|date= January 2, 2014|access-date= June 18, 2015|language=ko}}</ref> As a sequel of the show, N.Flying revealed their debut story in March 2014 which aired on the same channel.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://osen.mt.co.kr/article/G1109767216|title= tvN '청담동111' 속편 온다...엔플라잉 데뷔 드라마|publisher= OSEN|date= January 23, 2014|access-date= April 22, 2014|language=ko}}</ref>

On January 29, it was announced that N.Flying had been appointed as the new endorsement model of Buckaroo Jeans.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://enews24.interest.me/news/article.asp?nsID=336257|title= 'FNC 대형신인' 밴드 '엔플라잉'이 김우빈의 바통을 이어받는다|publisher= enews24|date= January 29, 2014|access-date= April 22, 2014|language=ko}}</ref> In the final episode of their reality show, 'Cheongdamdong111: N.Flying's Way of Becoming a Star', Seung-hyub was appointed as the leader of N.Flying.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fTjaMXeyzVg|title=청담동111 - N.Flying 엔플라잉 스타가 되는 길 5화(마지막회) 다시보기 Full ver.|last=N.Flying (엔플라잉)|date=April 11, 2014|via=YouTube}}</ref>

In March, they accompanied labelmate F.T. Island to be their opening act at the latter's Singapore concert.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://showbiz.omy.sg/Showbiz/Events/N-Flying-to-open-for-FTISLAND-Singapore-concert-244043|title=N-Flying to open for FTISLAND Singapore concert|date=2014-02-26|publisher=Singapore Press Holdings Ltd|access-date=2014-04-22|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150618152952/http://showbiz.omy.sg/Showbiz/Events/N-Flying-to-open-for-FTISLAND-Singapore-concert-244043|archive-date=June 18, 2015|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

N.Flying was supposed to make their Korean debut in 2014 but it was delayed after the leader of the band, Seunghyub, injured his knee in July.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2014/07/nflying-to-delay-their-domestic-debut-in-light-of-seunghyubs-injury|title=N.Flying to delay their domestic debut in light of Seunghyub's injury|date=2015-02-15|publisher=allkpop=2014-07-11}}</ref>

On July 23, 2017, FNC Entertainment opened N.Flying's teaser site for their August comeback.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.soompi.com/2017/07/23/n-flying-releases-comeback-schedule-2nd-mini-album-real-n-flying/|title=Update: N.Flying Reveals New Group Teaser Photo For Comeback With 5 Members|last1=23|first1=J. On May 16, 2018, they released their fourth EP ''How Are You?''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mstoo.asiae.co.kr/view.htm?no=2018051614312855030|title=엔플라잉 "섹시 콘셉트 위해 멤버 모두 4kg 이상 감량"|website=mstoo.asiae.co.kr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.xportsnews.com/jenter/?ac=article_view&entry_id=975453&_REFERER=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.xportsnews.com%2F%3Fac%3Darticle_search%26skey%3Dtc%26sword%3D%25EC%2597%2594%25ED%2594%258C%25EB%259D%25BC%25EC%259E%2589%26x%3D0%26y%3D0#_enliple|title=컴백' 엔플라잉 "서정 섹시 콘셉트, 멤버 전원 4kg 이상 감량|website=m.xportsnews.com}}</ref>

N.Flying announced a year-long project, Fly High Project with the aim of introducing more people to the band's potential and music. ===2019: Breakthrough, ''Spring Memories'', and ''Yaho'' ===

On January 2, N.Flying remained as a four-member band with their single "Rooftop" through ''Fly High Project #2''.<ref>https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/112/0003115641</ref> Following the release, their concert series "N.Flying Fly High Project Note 2. 2019" was held on January 19 at MUV Hall.<ref>https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/408/0000064847</ref> "Rooftop" first entered the lower ranks of Korean music charts and soon after there had concluded their promotions on music shows in early March, the song gained sudden popularity and went viral after an anonymous person shared it to an online community.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-03-15|title=엔플라잉 "'옥탑방' 역주행으로 1위, 최초 작성자 찾고 싶다"|url=https://www.dispatch.co.kr/2011299|access-date=2020-06-03|website=디스패치 {{!}} 뉴스는 팩트다!|language=ko-KR}}</ref> "Rooftop" later went on to #1 on all major Korean music charts and topped Gaon's weekly digital chart for two consecutive weeks and its monthly digital chart for the month of March.<ref>https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/109/0003957305</ref><ref>http://www.gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/online.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=ALL&targetTime=03&hitYear=2019&termGbn=month</ref> On March 5, N.Flying winning their first ever music show on ''The Show'',<ref>https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/109/0003963474</ref> following ''Inkigayo'' on March 17.<ref>https://www.soompi.com/article/1310898wpp/n-flying-takes-2nd-win-for-rooftop-on-inkigayo-performances-mamamoo-wooseok-kualin-txt-more</ref>

On April 24, N.Flying released their fifth EP ''Spring Memories'' with the title track through ''Fly High Project #3''. The EP includes six songs including their previous tracks, "Like a Flower" and "Rooftop". Following the conjunctions of the project release, a concert series was held entitled "N.Flying Fly High Project Note 3" on April 29 at MUV Hall.<ref>https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/109/0003989975</ref> The album debuted at #5 on Gaon.<ref>http://www.gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/album.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=&targetTime=17&hitYear=2019&termGbn=week</ref>

In June, N.Flying's first world tour 2019 Live Up All Night was announced to be held in Bangkok on June 20 and Hong Kong on August 10, and in Paris on September 22.<ref>https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/241/0002926987</ref>

N.Flying held their solo concert series "N.Flying Fly High Project Note 4" on July 20 at Yes24 Live Hall.<ref>https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/112/0003188285</ref>

On October 15, N.Flying released their sixth EP ''Yaho'' with the lead track "Good Bam".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hellokpop.com/featured/k-pop-comeback-spotlight-n-flyings-6th-mini-album-yaho/|title=K-Pop Comeback Spotlight: N.Flying- 6th Mini Album "YAHO"|website=hellopop.com}}</ref>
