'''Oje Ken Ollivierre''' (born 14 June 1981),<ref name="Last">{{Cite web|url=https://www.last.fm/music/Protoje/+wiki|title=Protoje age, hometown, biography|website=Last.fm|accessdate=30 October 2019}}</ref> popularly known as '''Protoje''', is a contemporary [[reggae]] singer and songwriter from [[Jamaica]]. His mother is Jamaican singer [[Lorna Bennett]], best known for her 1972 rendition of "Breakfast in Bed".<ref name="Johnson1503">Johnson, Richard (2015) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/-MY-BEST-WORK-YET-_18547817 'My best work yet': Protoje pours it all in latest set]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 11 March 2015. Retrieved 12 March 2015</ref> His father is a former [[Calypso music|calypso]] singer Mike Ollivierre from the island nation of [[Saint Vincent and the Grenadines]]. He has one daughter with his wife Lindsay.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/musicblog/2015/mar/12/protoje-i-wanted-to-capture-the-history-of-jamaican-music|title=Protoje: 'I wanted to capture the history of Jamaican music'|first=Erin|last=MacLeod|date=12 March 2015|website=Theguardian.com|accessdate=30 October 2019}}</ref>

==Music career==
He was born in [[Saint Elizabeth, Jamaica]].<ref name="Last"/> Protoje first gained notice for his 2005 mixtape ''Lyrical Overdose Volume 1'', featuring mostly hip hop-influenced songs. With a guest appearance by Busy Signal, however, his future career was leveled as a reggae and dancehall artist. In 2010, Protoje began working with Don Corleon Records, run by his cousin<ref>{{cite web|last=Henry|first=Krista|url=http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20110308/features/features1.html|title=All in the family – Cousins Protoje, Don Corleon share their love for music|publisher=Jamaica Star|date=28 March 2011}}</ref> and popular Jamaican producer Don Corleon.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bishop|first=Marlon|title=5 Dancehall Producers Dropping Fire|url=http://jamaica-star.com/thestar/20110308/features/features1.html|publisher=MTV Iggy|year=2013}}</ref> After releasing "Dread", "JA" and "Roll" in 2010, Protoje's debut album, entitled ''The Seven Year Itch'', was released on 25 January 2011. The first single off the album, dubbed "Arguments" (2009), was responsible for his early popularity in Jamaica. In 2011, he released another single off the album entitled "Rasta Love" featuring [[Kymani Marley|Ky-Mani Marley]].

His second album, ''The 8 Year Affair'', was released 12 February 2013. "Who Dem a Program" was the first single released from the album in January 2012, with "This Is Not a Marijuana Song" following mid-year. "Kingston Be Wise" was heavily promoted via traditional and social media. On the day of its release, 5 November 2012, the music video became the most viewed YouTube link among internet users in Jamaica.{{citation needed|date=July 2014}} The hashtag #KBW was used on Twitter. The song "Kingston Be Wise" (a remake of [[Ini Kamoze]]'s song "England be nice") was featured in the video game ''[[Grand Theft Auto V]]'' on the in-game "Blue Ark" radio station,<ref>{{Cite web|title = Rockstar reveals definitive GTA 5 radio station song list|url = http://metro.co.uk/2013/10/03/rockstar-reveals-definitive-gta-5-radio-station-song-list-4133849/|website = Metro|accessdate = 28 August 2015|first = Metro Music Reporter for|last = Metro.co.uk}}</ref> increasing its popularity further and making an international name for the reggae artist. Protoje cited reggae artist [[Ini Kamoze]] and group [[Black Uhuru]] as influences when recording the album.<ref>{{cite web|title=Protoje: Reviving Reggae in Jamaica|url=http://www.afropop.org/wp/10756/protoje-revives-reggae-jamaica/|publisher=Afropop Worldwide|author=Saxon Baird|date=7 January 2013}}</ref>

In December 2014 Protoje was included on [[BBC Radio 1Xtra]]'s 'Hot for 2015' list.<ref>"[http://jamaica-gleaner.com/gleaner/20141217/ent/ent1.html Protoje Makes BBC 1xtra's Hot For 2015 Artiste List]", ''[[Jamaica Gleaner]]'', 17 December 2014. Retrieved 21 December 2014</ref>

His third album ''Ancient Future'' was initially set for release in September 2014,<ref name="Dyer">Dyer, Deidre (2014) "[http://www.thefader.com/2014/06/26/interview-protoje-on-the-reggae-roots-revival-and-his-low-key-love-of-pop-music/ Interview: Protoje on the Reggae Roots Revival and His Low-Key Love of Pop Music]", ''[[The Fader]]'', 4 July 2014. Retrieved 4 July 2014</ref> but was put back until March 2015.<ref>"[http://jamaica-gleaner.com/article/entertainment/20150309/protoje-release-ancient-future-album Protoje To Release 'Ancient Future' Album]", ''[[Jamaica Gleaner]]'', 9 March 2015. Retrieved 9 March 2015</ref> Produced by Phillip "Winta" James, the album includes the song "Who Knows", a collaboration with [[Chronixx]], which was released as a single.<ref name="Johnson1503" /> Two tracks from the album, "Answer to Your Name" and "Criminal" were released ahead of the album as exclusives on the ''England Be Wise'' mixtape for [[BBC Radio 1Xtra|BBC 1xtra]] DJ [[Toddla T]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://soundcloud.com/i-d-online-1/i-dj-toddla-t|title = England Be Wise Mixtape|date = 4 February 2015|accessdate = 26 August 2015|website = soundcloud|publisher = |last = |first = }}</ref> Two weeks after its release it topped the ''Billboard'' Top Reggae Albums chart.<ref>Brooks, Sadeke (2015) "[http://jamaica-gleaner.com/article/entertainment/20150326/protoje-tops-reggae-billboard-chart Protoje Tops Reggae Billboard Chart]", ''[[Jamaica Gleaner]]'', 26 March 2015. Retrieved 26 March 2015</ref>

On 14 June 2016, Protoje released Side B of his fourth studio album, ''Royalty Free'', as a free download to the public on his website.

His 2018 album, ''A Matter of Time'', received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination in the Best Reggae Album category.<ref>Bonitto, Brian (2018) "[http://301-joweb.newscyclecloud.com/entertainment/it-s-protoje-s-time_151873?profile=1116 It's Protoje's time]", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', 12 December 2018. Retrieved 14 December 2018</ref>

On 27 March 2020, Protoje joined [[Chronixx]] on a remix of [[Alicia Keys]]' song ''Underdog''.<ref>Collins, Latoya (2020) "[https://www.dancehallmag.com/2020/03/27/music/alicia-keys-recruits-chronixx-protoje-for-underdog-remix-listen.html Alicia Keys Recruits Chronixx & Protoje For ‘Underdog (Remix)’: Listen]", ''DancehallMag'', 27 March 2020. Retrieved 28 March 2020</ref>

On 13 April 2020, Protoje announced a new label deal with [[RCA Records]].  The deal allowed him "a certain level of creative control" and included artists [[Lila Iké]] and [[Sevana]] who he had been mentoring under his own In.Digg.Nation label.<ref>Leight, Elias (2020) "[https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/protoje-rca-deal-sevana-lila-ike-982332/ Protoje Announces Label Deal With RCA"], ''Rolling Stone'', 13 April 2020. Retrieved 13 April 2020</ref><ref>Williams, Shirvan (2020) "[https://www.dancehallmag.com/2020/04/13/news/protoje-lila-ike-and-sevana-sign-major-deal-with-u-s-label-rca-records.html Protoje, Lila Iké And Sevana Sign Major Deal With U.S. Label RCA Records"], ''DancehallMag'', 13 April 2020. Retrieved 13 April 2020</ref>
