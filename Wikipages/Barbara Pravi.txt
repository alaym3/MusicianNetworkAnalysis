'''Barbara Piévic<ref name=":0" />''' ({{lang-sr-Cyrl|Барбара Пјевић|translit=Barbara Pjević}};<ref name=":0" /> born 10 April 1993), known professionally as '''Barbara Pravi''', is a French singer, songwriter and actress from Paris. After signing with [[Capitol Music Group|Capitol Music France]] in 2015, she released two extended plays with the label: ''Barbara Pravi'' (2018) and ''Reviens pour l'hiver'' (2020). As a songwriter, Pravi has written songs for a number of artists including [[Yannick Noah]], [[Julie Zenatti]], [[Chimène Badi]] and [[Jaden Smith]]. She additionally composed the song "[[J'imagine]]" by [[Valentina (singer)|Valentina]], which won the [[Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2020]]. 

Pravi [[France in the Eurovision Song Contest 2021|represented France]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021]] with the song "[[Voilà (Barbara Pravi song)|Voilà]]", securing 2nd place, the best result for France since 1991.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|last=Erdbrink|first=Thomas|date=23 May 2021|title=Eurovision, Celebrating the Sounds of a Postpandemic Continent|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/05/23/arts/music/eurovision-results.html|access-date=31 May 2021|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Her debut album, ''[[On N’enferme Pas Les Oiseaux]]'' was released in August 2021.

==Early life==
Pravi was born in [[Paris]] on 10 April 1993.<ref name="bio">{{Cite web|title=Eurovision-fr.net – Informations -Eurovision FRANCE 2021 : Entrevue exclusive avec Barbara Pravi|url=http://www.eurovision-fr.net/news/lire.php?id=6560|access-date=16 January 2021|website=Eurovision-fr.net|archive-date=30 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210130235647/http://www.eurovision-fr.net/news/lire.php?id=6560|url-status=live}}</ref> Her family consists primarily of artists and musicians. Pravi's father is of [[Serbians|Serbian]] and [[Algerian Jews|Algerian Jewish]] descent, while her mother is of [[Polish Jews|Polish Jewish]] and Iranian origin.<ref>[https://www.leparisien.fr/culture-loisirs/musique/il-y-a-trois-ans-jetais-a-bout-comment-barbara-pravi-est-devenue-la-favorite-de-leurovision-16-05-2021-XGRHPPJMBNFDBK7C4R3VLM63WQ.php «Il y a trois ans, j’étais à bout» : comment Barbara Pravi est devenue la favorite de l’Eurovision], ''Le Parisien''</ref> Pravi's maternal grandfather is Iranian painter [[Hossein Zenderoudi]].<ref name="bio"/><ref name=":1" /> 

Pravi's birth name is Barbara Pjević;<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.eurofansradio.com/eurovision-news/eurovision-news-2019/france-barbara-pravi-to-fly-the-flag-in-rotterdam-singing|title=France: Barbara Pravi to fly the flag in Rotterdam singing "Voila"|date=31 January 2021|work=Eurofans Radio}}</ref> she adopted the stage name Barbara Pravi from the [[Serbian language|Serbian]] word {{lang|sr-Latn|pravi}} ({{lang-sr-Cyrl|прави|lit=real', 'authentic}}, in the [[grammatical gender|masculine]]), as a homage to her Serbian grandfather.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.enroute-eurovision.fr/actualites/eurovision-france-2021-cest-vous-qui-decidez-barbara-pravi-voila/|title=Eurovision France 2021, c’est vous qui décidez : Barbara Pravi – Voilà|language=French|date=18 January 2021|work=En Route: Eurovision|access-date=30 January 2021|archive-date=30 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210130235651/https://www.enroute-eurovision.fr/actualites/eurovision-france-2021-cest-vous-qui-decidez-barbara-pravi-voila/|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
===2014–2018: Early career===
Pravi began her music career in 2014, after meeting French musician Jules Jaconelli. With Jaconelli, she began composing songs. The following year, Pravi signed a contract with [[Capitol Music Group|Capitol Music France]]. Upon beginning her professional recording career, Pravi performed on the soundtrack of the French version of the Swiss film ''[[Heidi (2015 film)|Heidi]]'', and afterwards was cast as Solange Duhamel in the musical show ''[[:fr:Un été 44|Un été 44]]'' in November 2016, performing songs written by [[Jean-Jacques Goldman]], [[Charles Aznavour]] and [[Maxime Le Forestier]].<ref>'' On m'appelle Heidi '', {{BNF | 450757102}}.</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Télématin On encadre – Barbara Pavi|url=https://www.france.tv/france-2/telematin/398655-on-encadre-barbara-pavi.html|language=fr|access-date=18 January 2021|archive-date=27 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210127193626/https://www.france.tv/france-2/telematin/398655-on-encadre-barbara-pavi.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2017, Pravi released her first official single "Pas grandir".<ref>{{Cite web|title="Pas grandir" : Barbara Pravi fait le grand saut avec un titre pétillant et féministe|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Barbara-Pravi/news-104722.html|access-date=19 January 2021|website=chartsinfrance.net|archive-date=28 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210128004437/http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Barbara-Pravi/news-104722.html|url-status=live}}</ref> The single was later included on her self-titled debut [[extended play]], which was released the following year. Also in 2017, Pravi starred in the television film ''La Sainte famille'', which was later broadcast on [[France 2]] in December 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BARBARA PRAVI|url=https://www.animae-talents.com/barbara-pravi|access-date=19 January 2021|website=Animae|language=fr|archive-date=28 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210128003715/https://www.animae-talents.com/barbara-pravi|url-status=live}}</ref> From 2017 until 2018, Pravi performed on the 55 Tour, supporting French singer [[Florent Pagny]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Un dernier verre avec Barbara Pravi|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/sortir-paris/2018/02/26/30004-20180226ARTFIG00190-un-dernier-verre-avec-barbara-pravi.php|access-date=13 January 2021|website=Le Figaro|language=fr|archive-date=9 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201109011826/https://www.lefigaro.fr/sortir-paris/2018/02/26/30004-20180226ARTFIG00190-un-dernier-verre-avec-barbara-pravi.php|url-status=live}}</ref> At the end of 2018, Pravi decided to alter her musical style by adopting a more traditional French ''[[chanson]]'' style, rather than the pop music that had dominated her early releases.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Février 2020|first=6|date=6 February 2020|title=Barbara Pravi en interview : "J’ai compris que j’avais besoin d’amour, de personnes bienveillantes"|url=https://www.aficia.info/interview/barbara-pravi-3/186435|access-date=13 January 2021|website=aficia.|language=fr-FR|archive-date=6 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200306012039/https://www.aficia.info/interview/barbara-pravi-3/186435|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2019–present: Eurovision success===
In 2019, Pravi first became associated with the [[Eurovision Song Contest]] after composing the song "[[Bim bam toi]]" for French teen singer [[Carla Lazzari|Carla]], composed with French musician Igit. The song [[France in the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2019|represented France]] at the [[Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2019]] in [[Gliwice]], where it placed fifth.<ref>{{cite Following the success of "Bim bam toi", Pravi later released her second extended play ''Reviens pour l'hiver'' in February 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Barbara In 2020, Pravi and Igit were confirmed to have written the French Junior Eurovision entry once again. Their song, "[[J'imagine]]" performed by the French child singer [[Valentina (singer)|Valentina]], later [[France in the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2020|represented France]] at the [[Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2020]] in [[Warsaw]]. "J'imagine" went on to win the competition, becoming the first French entry to win Junior Eurovision.<ref name=": 1">{{Cite web|title=Valentina – France – Poland 2020|url=https://junioreurovision.tv/participant/valentina-2020|access-date=13 January 2021|website=junioreurovision.tv|language=en|archive-date=12 December 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201212095137/https://junioreurovision.tv/participant/valentina-2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Cocorico ! Later in 2020, Pravi was announced as one of the contestants in ''[[France in the Eurovision Song Contest 2021|Eurovision France, c'est vous qui décidez!]]''. She competed in the final on 30 January 2021 with the song "[[Voilà (Barbara Pravi song)|Voilà]]", composed by herself along with Igit and Lili Poe.<ref>{{Cite web|date=9 December 2020|title=France has started their new selection process for Eurovision 2021|url=https://eurovision.tv/story/france-returns-to-national-final-to-select-their-entry-for-eurovision|access-date=13 January 2021|publisher=Eurovision Song Contest|language=en|archive-date=9 December 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201209090932/https://eurovision.tv/story/france-returns-to-national-final-to-select-their-entry-for-eurovision|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Média|first=Prisma|title=Eurovision France : qui est Barbara Pravi, la favorite des fans du concours ? She had reportedly been asked by [[France Télévisions]] to be a candidate for the Eurovision Song Contest for years prior, but had declined the opportunity due to feeling as if she was not ready.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Barké|first=Sébastien|date=1 January 2021|title=Eurovision France 2021 : qui est Barbara Pravi, l'interprète de "Voilà" ?|url=https://www.programme-tv.net/news/evenement/eurovision-eurovision-2021/267621-eurovision-france-2021-qui-est-barbara-pravi-linterprete-de-voila/|access-date=13 January 2021|website=programme-tv.net|language=fr|archive-date=13 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210113090945/https://www.programme-tv.net/news/evenement/eurovision-eurovision-2021/267621-eurovision-france-2021-qui-est-barbara-pravi-linterprete-de-voila/|url-status=live}}</ref>
