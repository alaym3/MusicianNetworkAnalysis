'''Mustafa Zahid''' ({{lang-ur|مصطفی زاہد}}; born 18 December 1984) is a Pakistani [[music composer]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sameenamer.blogspot.com/2006/05/roxen-shining-through.html|title=Roxen shining through|website=sameenamer.blogspot|date=2006-05-20|accessdate=2016-09-14}}</ref> who achieved success with his 2007 singles "Toh Phir Aao" and "Tera Mera Rishta". He is also the band leader and the lead vocalist of [[Roxen (band)|Roxen]], a rock band which was formed in 2004.<ref name="eglamsham.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.eglamsham.com/roxen.htm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2009-03-18 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081202020333/http://www.eglamsham.com/roxen.htm |archivedate=2 December 2008 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

==Early life==

Mustafa was born into a [[Muslim]] family in [[Lahore]], Punjab, Pakistan. He is the cousin of [[Ali Azmat]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/225008/the-family-connection/|title="The family connection"|newspaper=The Express Tribune}}</ref> who was the lead vocalist of the famous rock band [[Junoon (band)|Junoon]], and is also related to the late [[Sufi]] kalam singer, actor, director and producer [[Inayat Hussain Bhatti]].<ref>[https://runwaypakistan.com/lord-of-the-roxen-mustafa-zahid/ "Lord of the Roxen – Mustafa Zahid"], ''Runaway Pakistan''.</ref> From childhood he never thought that he would become a singer. He was daring from his young age and done anchoring for the college function. During that he was asked to sing some song by the professors. He hesitatingly sang bheegi bheegi raton me song for about 2 lines. From this he got lot of motivation to become a singer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefridaytimes.com/tft/bollywood-is-a-lot-of-hard-work/|title=Bollywood is a lot of hard work|date=31 July 2014|publisher=The Friday Times}}</ref>

==Mainstream and Bollywood==

In mid-2007, two of his songs were used in the [[Bollywood]] movie [[Awarapan]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.glamsham.com/movies/scoops/07/jun/27_pakistani_rock_band_roxen_petrified.asp|title=Pakistani rock band ROXEN petrified! - bollywood news : glamsham.com|publisher=}}</ref> and he has performed in outside of Pakistan, including Glasgow<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.isthismusic.com/mustafa-zahid|title=Mustafa Zahid - is this music?|work=is this music?|date=24 February 2008}}</ref> and Bahrain.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gulf-daily-news.com/newsdetails.aspx?srch=1&storyid=188100|title=Gulf Daily News " Local News " Bahrain date for Roxen|publisher=}}</ref>

==Roxen==
{{Main article|Roxen (band)}}
Their debut album titled '''Roxen-E-Dewaar''' was released in late 2006 and remained on top of all charts in Pakistan for almost six weeks. The Indian release of the album was scheduled in mid December 2008, but was put on hold by Indian label after the recent Mumbai Attacks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.radioandmusic.com/content/editorial/news/hom-records-puts-roxen-album-release|title=HOM Records puts off Roxen album release|publisher=}}</ref> [[Universal Music]] has signed Mustafa Zahid and [[Roxen (band)]] for a worldwide release of their Upcoming Album.<ref>{{cite web|title=Universal Music signs Roxen and Mustafa|date=7 August 2013|url=http://www.pakium.com/2013/08/07/universal-music-signs-mustafa-zahid-roxen-band}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/1022939/roxens-second-album-to-come-out-in-india/|title=Roxen's second album to come out in India|newspaper=The Express Tribune|date=2016-01-06|accessdate=2016-09-15}}</ref>
