'''Edward Joseph Mahoney''' (March 21, 1949&nbsp;– September 13, 2019), known professionally as '''Eddie Money''', was an American singer and songwriter who, in the 1970s and 1980s, had eleven [[Top 40]] songs, including "[[Baby Hold On]]", "[[Two Tickets to Paradise]]", "[[Think I'm in Love (Eddie Money song)|Think I'm in Love]]", "[[Shakin' (Eddie Money song)|Shakin']]", "[[Take Me Home Tonight (song)|Take Me Home Tonight]]", "[[I Wanna Go Back]]", "[[Walk on Water (Eddie Money song)|Walk on Water]]", and "[[The Love in Your Eyes (Eddie Money song)|The Love in Your Eyes]]". Critic [[Neil Genzlinger]] of ''[[The New York Times]]'' called him a working-class rocker<ref name=nytobit/> and Kristin Hall of the [[Associated Press]] stated he had a husky voice.<ref name=apdeath>{{Cite news | url=https://apnews.com/dd4e6aeded69408c9efc4566661d009e | title=Eddie Money, 'Two Tickets to Paradise' singer, dies at 70 | first=Kristin M. | last=Hall | publisher=[[Associated Press]] | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> In 1987, he was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best Male Rock Vocal Performance]] for "Take Me Home Tonight".<ref>{{cite magazine  |last1=Bertram |first1=Scot |date=September 23, 2019 |title=Eddie Money, the Slightly Undersold Rock Artist |url=https://www.nationalreview.com/2019/09/remembering-eddie-money-undersold-rock-artist/ |magazine=[[National Review]] |access-date=September 25, 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Edward Joseph Mahoney was born in Brooklyn, New York City on March 21, 1949, to a large family of Irish Catholics. His parents were Dorothy Elizabeth (''née'' Keller), a homemaker, and Daniel Patrick Mahoney, a police officer.<ref name=nytobit>{{Cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/09/13/arts/music/eddie-money-dead.html| title=Eddie Money, 'Two Tickets to Paradise' Singer, Is Dead at 70 | first=Neil | last=Genzlinger | work=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/eddie-money-talks-hit-songs-new-reality-show-real-money-w519120| title=Eddie Money Talks New Reality Show and Why He'll Never Retire| first=Jason | last=Newman | magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]| date=April 25, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=https://alibi.com/music/55841/Because-I-Cant-Get-Enough.html |title=Music Interview: Eddie Money | work=[[Alibi.com]]}}</ref> He grew up in Levittown, New York,<ref name=wpobit/><ref name=newsdaydead>{{Cite news | url=https://www.newsday.com/entertainment/music/eddie-money-dead-1.36255622 | title=Reports: LI-raised rocker Eddie Money has died | first=Rafer | last=Guzmán | work=[[Newsday]]| date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> but spent some teenage years in Woodhaven, Queens.<ref name=recharged/> Money was a street singer since the age of 11.<ref name=nytobit/> As a teenager, he played in rock bands, in part to get dates from cheerleaders.<ref name=wpobit/> He was thrown out of one high school for forging a report card.<ref name=apdeath/> In 1967, he graduated from [[Island Trees High School]].<ref name=nytobit/>

At the age of 18, he tried to follow in the footsteps of his grandfather, father, and brother as a New York City Police Department trainee.<ref name="nypd">{{Cite news | url=https://nypost.com/2019/09/13/nypd-clears-up-eddie-money-cop-mystery/ | title=NYPD clears up Eddie Money cop mystery | last1=Fitz-Gibbon | first1=Jorge | work=[[New York Post]] | last2=Italiano | first2=Laura | date=September 14, 2019}}</ref> However, after working as a clerk and typist, he left in 1968 to pursue a career in music,<ref name="apdeath" /> as the police did not allow him to grow his hair long.<ref name="wpobit">{{cite news | url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/obituaries/eddie-money-singer-behind-take-me-home-tonight-and-two-tickets-to-paradise-dies-at-70/2019/09/13/3fdc6b0c-c8e5-11e9-a1fe-ca46e8d573c0_story.html | title=Eddie Money, singer behind 'Take Me Home Tonight' and 'Two Tickets to Paradise,' dies at 70 | first=Harrison | last=Smith | work=[[The Washington Post]] | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> "I couldn't see myself in a police uniform for 20 years of my life, with short hair," he later said.<ref name="nypd" /> His bandmates also fired him because they did not want a police officer in the group.<ref name="nytobit" /> His father was not happy with the decision to play music and tore the [[Jimi Hendrix]] posters from his wall.<ref name="wpobit" />

In 1968, Money moved to Berkeley, California.<ref>{{Cite news | last=Beals| first=Melba| url=https://people.com/archive/ten-years-after-he-threw-the-book-at-him-a-besieged-judge-finds-he-can-bank-on-rocker-eddie-money-vol-13-no-23/ | title=Ten Years After He Threw the Book at Him, a Besieged Judge Finds He Can Bank on Rocker Eddie Money | work=[[People (magazine)|People]] | date=June 9, 1980}}</ref> There, he studied with vocal coach Judy Davis, and took on the stage name Eddie Money, dropping a few letters from his name and sarcastically referencing the fact that he was always broke.<ref name=wpobit/>

==Career==
===Music career===
[[File:DaveRajputWithhadoeStevensEddieMoneyAndrewStarrMarkusSchulz..jpg|thumb|upright=1.1|Money (center) in 1990]]
Money became a regular performer at clubs in the San Francisco Bay Area.<ref name=DeathVariety/> After gaining the attention of [[Bill Graham (promoter)|Bill Graham]],<ref name=apdeath/> he secured a recording contract with [[Columbia Records]], releasing his debut album in 1977. He charted with singles such as "[[Baby Hold On]]" and "[[Two Tickets to Paradise]]", about visiting his girlfriend despite not having money.<ref name=wpobit/><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.billboard.com/music/eddie-money/chart-history | title=Eddie Money Chart History | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref>

In 1978, Money opened for [[Santana (band)|Santana]] at Boston's [[Wang Theatre|Music Hall]].<ref name=nytobit/> The following year, he sang the bridge section on "[[I'm Alright (Kenny Loggins song)|I'm Alright]]", a song written and performed by [[Kenny Loggins]]. In 2014, Money claimed that Loggins never gave him credit for his contribution.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://herald-review.com/blogs/tim_cain/eddie-money-vs-kenny-loggins/article_ade806d4-b04e-11e3-babf-0019bb2963f4.html | title=Eddie Money vs. Kenny Loggins | first=Tim | last=Cain | work=[[Herald & Review]] | date=March 20, 2014}}</ref>

In 1982, Money took advantage of the [[MTV]] music video scene with his humorous narrative videos for "[[Think I'm in Love (Eddie Money song)|Think I'm in Love]]", performed at [[The Mission Inn Hotel & Spa]], and "[[Shakin' (Eddie Money song)|Shakin']]".<ref name=wpobit/> In the early 1980s, he appeared on ''[[The Midnight Special (TV series)|The Midnight Special]]'', ''[[Fridays (TV series)|Fridays]]'', and ''[[Solid Gold (TV series)|Solid Gold]]''.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.metv.com/stories/rip-eddie-money-hit-making-rocker-of-the-1970s-and-80s | title=R.I.P. Eddie Money, hit-making rocker of the 1970s and '80s | work=[[MeTV]] | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> In 1978 and 1984, he appeared on ''[[American Bandstand]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.tv.com/shows/american-bandstand/eddie-money-deborah-allen-255568/ | title=American Bandstand Season 27 Episode 19 | publisher=[[TV.com]]}}</ref>

Money's career began to decline following an unsuccessful 1983 album (''[[Where's the Party?]]'') and struggles with drug addiction.<ref name=rewinding>{{Cite news | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8475851/eddie-money-ronnie-spector-take-me-home-tonight-rewinding-the-charts-1986 | title=Rewinding the Charts: In 1986, Eddie Money & Ronnie Spector Staged a Comeback With 'Take Me Home Tonight' | first=Kevin | last=Rutherford | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> However, he made a comeback in 1986 with the album ''[[Can't Hold Back (Eddie Money album)|Can't Hold Back]]'', which received a [[music recording certification]] of platinum.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.claremoreprogress.com/news/eddie-money-starship-featuring-mickey-thomas-to-land-in-catoosa/article_b54b1216-9fd5-11e7-b9df-effd4bacae77.html | title=Eddie Money, Starship featuring Mickey Thomas to land in Catoosa | work=[[Claremore Daily Progress]] | date=September 22, 2017}}</ref> "[[Take Me Home Tonight (song)|Take Me Home Tonight]]", a single from the album, peaked at No. 4 on the [[Billboard Hot 100]] chart in the United States.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite web |last1=Caulfield |first1=Keith |title=Eddie Money's 'Take Me Home Tonight' Streamed Over 3 Million Times in U.S. Since His Death |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8530290/eddie-money-take-me-home-tonight-streamed-over-3-million-times-since-death |website=billboard.com |publisher=Billboard |access-date=September 19, 2019}}</ref> Money only agreed to perform the song—which included a line from "[[Be My Baby]]", a song [[Ronnie Spector]] performed as part of [[The Ronettes]]—after Spector agreed to sing the line herself.<ref name=recharged>{{cite news | url=https://www.mcall.com/news/mc-xpm-1987-02-20-2563076-story.html | title=With His Career Recharged, Eddie Money Can't Hold Back | first=Carrie | last=Stetler | work=[[The Morning Call]] | date=February 20, 1987}}</ref> In 1987, Money was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best Male Rock Vocal Performance]] for "Take Me Home Tonight".<ref name=apdeath/> "[[I Wanna Go Back]]" and "Endless Nights"—two other singles from the ''Can't Hold Back'' album—peaked at No. 14 and No. 21, respectively.<ref name=rewinding/>

In 1988, Money released ''[[Nothing to Lose (Eddie Money album)|Nothing to Lose]]'', which featured the Top 10 hit "[[Walk on Water (Eddie Money song)|Walk on Water]]" and the Top 40 hit "[[The Love in Your Eyes (Eddie Money song)|The Love in Your Eyes]]".<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.poconorecord.com/entertainmentlife/20170518/cant-hold-back-when-eddie-money-takes-stage-at-mount-airy | title='Can't Hold Back' when Eddie Money takes the stage at Mount Airy | first=Jim | last=Radenhausen| work=[[Pocono Record]] | date=May 18, 2017}}</ref>

Beginning in 1992, Money opened the summer concert season for [[DTE Energy Music Theatre]] in Clarkston, Michigan.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.detroitnews.com/story/obituaries/2019/09/13/eddie-money/40142395/ | title=Eddie Money, longtime fixture at Pine Knob/DTE, dies | first=Adam | last=Graham | work=[[The Detroit News]] | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> In 1996, he wrote the theme music to ''[[Quack Pack]]'', a Disney cartoon.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Correspondent|first=TOM LOUNGES Times|title=Money's still rockin' after all these years|url=https://www.nwitimes.com/entertainment/moneys-still-rockin-after-all-these-years/article_59949c62-d5af-5820-949f-457c23291d87.html|access-date=June 7, 2020|website=nwitimes.com|language=en}}</ref>

Money was inducted into the [[Long Island Music Hall of Fame]] in 2008.<ref name=newsdaydead/> In January 2010, he performed a medley of his hit singles during the halftime performance at the [[2010 Liberty Bowl (January)|Liberty Bowl]].<ref>{{Cite news | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2010/01/02/eddie-moneys-liberty-bowl_n_409501.html | title=Eddie Money's Liberty Bowl Halftime Show Performance VIDEO: 'Two Tickets To Paradise' | work=[[HuffPost]] | first=Whitney | last=Snyder | date=January 2, 2010}}</ref>
[[File:Eddie-Money-Gulfstream-Park-(2006).jpg|thumb|upright=1.1|Money performing in 2006]]
Money wrote and performed original songs for the films ''[[Americathon]]'' (1979), ''[[Over the Top (1987 film)|Over the Top]]'', ''[[Back to the Beach]]'' (both 1987), and ''[[Kuffs]]'' (1992), along with the television series ''[[Hardball (1989 TV series)|Hardball]]'' (1989–1990).<ref>{{Cite book | last=Terrace | first=Vincent | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AU6nCgAAQBAJ |title=The Television Crime Fighters Factbook: Over 9,800 Details from 301 Programs, 1937–2003 | edition=illustrated |publisher=McFarland  | page=60 | isbn=978-1476611433 | year=2015}}</ref>

On May 10, 2019, Money released the track "Brand New Day" as the lead single from his [[Brand New Day (Eddie Money album)|album of the same name]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://classicsdujour.com/hear-eddie-moneys-brand-new-day-from-his-first-new-studio-album-in-15-years/|title=Hear Eddie Money's "Brand New Day" from His First New Studio Album in 15 Years|date=May 10, 2019|website=Classics Du Jour|language=en-US|access-date=September 14, 2019}}</ref> It was originally set to be released on July 19, 2019,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.melodicrock.com/videos/eddie-money-returns-brand-new-day-july-19|title=EDDIE MONEY Returns With 'Brand New Day' July 19|date=May 13, 2019|website=MelodicRock.com|access-date=September 14, 2019}}</ref> but was postponed following the announcement of Money's illness and subsequent death. On April 17, 2020, ''Brand New Day'', was released as a 5-song EP. A second EP containing the remaining six songs from the intended album will be released at a later date according to Money's wife Laurie who said "These will be his last recordings. The other half is a little more vintage. There's a big difference so they needed to be separated."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newsday.com/entertainment/music/eddie-money-ep-brand-new-day-1.43909408|title=Posthumous EP from LI's Eddie Money released|date=April 16, 2020|website=MelodicRock.com|access-date=December 2, 2020}}</ref>

In the three days following Money's death, fans streamed "Take Me Home Tonight" more than 3.1 million times, which was an increase of 349% compared to the previous three-day period. Fans also streamed his other songs by 931% more than the three previous days.<ref name="Billboard"/>

===Television, film, and radio career===
Money made several screen appearances.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/eddie-money/credits/148451/ | title=Eddie Money | website=[[TV Guide]]}}</ref>

In 1997, he appeared in ''[[Wonderland (1997 film)|Wonderland]]'', a documentary film about Levittown, New York, where Money went to high school. In the film, he said if he had "two tickets to paradise, I'd probably get back to Levittown".<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kOgCAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA20 | title=A Suburban Safari | work=[[New York (magazine)|New York]] | date=October 27, 1997}}</ref>

Money played a fictionalized version of himself on a 1999 episode of [[List of The Drew Carey Show episodes#Season 5 (1999–2000)|season 5 of ''The Drew Carey Show'']]. In the episode, he had been Mimi's first husband early in his career and they never made their divorce official.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.eddiemoney.com/history | title=Eddie Money – History | work=EddieMoney.com | publisher=Eddie Money}}</ref> In May 2002, he played himself on an episode of the sitcom ''[[The King of Queens]]''.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.ctpost.com/local/article/Headliner-entertainment-announced-for-Milford-12903679.php | title=Rock headliner announced for Milford Oyster Festival | first=Jim | last=Shay | work=[[Connecticut Post]] | date=May 10, 2018}}</ref>

In October 2011, Money became the host of "Money in the Morning", a radio show on [[WSRV]]. The gig lasted about three months.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.ajc.com/blog/music/eddie-money-has-died/Uc2vQWqIlAuJIKJRdQi36I/ | title=Eddie Money has died at 70 | first=Melissa | last=Ruggieri | work=[[The Atlanta Journal-Constitution]] | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref> He appeared in a 2012 [[GEICO]] insurance commercial in which he is depicted as a travel agency owner who sings "[[Two Tickets to Paradise]]" to a family that wants tickets for a vacation.<ref name=wpobit/>

In January 2016, Money partnered with [[Howard Perl Entertainment]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.clevescene.com/scene-and-heard/archives/2015/09/24/eddie-money-to-headline-rock-and-roll-to-the-rescue-benefit-part-2|title=Eddie Money to Headline Rock & Roll to the Rescue Benefit (Part 2)|last=Niesel|first=Jeff|website=Cleveland Scene|language=en|access-date=January 23, 2020}}</ref> MTV VJ [[Nina Blackwood]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://coolcleveland.com/2015/11/ex-mtv-vj-ninablackwood-hosts-benefit-hrrocksinonp-for-geauga-county-rescue-village/|title=Ex-MTV VJ @NinaBlackwood Hosts Benefit @HRRocksinoNP for Geauga County Rescue Village|date=November 3, 2015|website=CoolCleveland|language=en-US|access-date=January 23, 2020}}</ref> and [[Hard Rock Rocksino Northfield Park|Hard Rock Rocksino]] to produce "Money for the Animals",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://coolcleveland.com/2015/11/ex-mtv-vj-ninablackwood-hosts-benefit-hrrocksinonp-for-geauga-county-rescue-village/|title=Eddie Money to Headline Rock & Roll to the Rescue Benefit (Part 2)|last=Niesel|first=Jeff|website=Cleveland Scene|language=en|access-date=January 23, 2020}}</ref> a show designed to raise funds and adopt rescue animals in need.

In 2018, Money appeared in episode 6 of ''[[The Kominsky Method]]'' as a fictionalized version of himself who is indebted to the [[Internal Revenue Service]] and portrays the character Freddie Money in an eponymous [[tribute act]] at a casino to avoid further tax problems.<ref>{{cite web |title="The Kominsky Method" Chapter 6: A Daughter Detoxes |url=https://m.imdb.com/title/tt8282440/ |website=IMDb.com |access-date=September 23, 2019}}</ref>

On April 8, 2018, ''Real Money'', a [[reality television]] series about Money and his family, debuted on [[AXS TV]].<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.mlive.com/news/jackson/2018/05/eddie_money_headlines_classic.html | title=Eddie Money headlines classic rock show at Jackson County Fair | first=Taylor | last=DesOrmeau | work=[[Booth Newspapers]] | date=May 14, 2018}}</ref> An episode sharing his cancer diagnosis aired on AXS TV the day before he died. The show's second season was expected to follow Money's "journey as he tells his family about the disease and undergoes treatment."<ref name=cause>{{cite news | url=https://www.newsweek.com/eddie-money-death-cancer-music-1459189 | title=What Was Eddie Money's Cause of Death? Musician Dead at 70 Years Old | last=Williams | first=Janice | work=[[Newsweek]] | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref>

In late April 2018, [[Weekly Alibi]]'s August March interviewed Money, who waxed poetic about his career, his family and his new television show.<ref>{{citation |url=https://alibi.com/music/55841/Because-I-Cant-Get-Enough.html |title=Music Interview: Because I Can't Get Enought |newspaper=[[Weekly Alibi]] |date=April 26, 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life and death==
In 1980, after drinking vodka, Money overdosed on a synthetic [[barbiturate]] that he mistook for cocaine. He suffered damage to the [[sciatic nerve]] on his left leg, was unable to walk for months, and had a permanent [[limp]] thereafter.<ref name=apdeath/><ref name=DeathVariety/><ref name=Clean_Sober/><ref name=nytobit/>

On Valentine's Day 1984 in Moraga, California, Money married Margo Lee Walker, a student from Los Angeles. He and his bride tried to keep the wedding private, "but a crowd of screaming teenage fans showed up."<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.upi.com/Archives/1984/02/15/WEDDING-NEWS/8588445669200/ | title=Wedding News | work=[[United Press International]] | date=February 15, 1984}}</ref>

Money married Laurie Harris in 1989.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://people.com/archive/eddie-money-vol-45-no-24/ |title=Eddie Money profile | work=[[People (magazine)|People]] | date=June 17, 1996}}</ref> Together, they had five children: Zachary, Jessica, Joseph, Julian, and Desmond.<ref name=Clean_Sober>{{cite news | url=http://www.cnn.com/2003/SHOWBIZ/Music/01/15/people.watn.money/ | title=Eddie Money now clean and sober | work=[[CNN]] | date=January 13, 2003 | last=Kappes | first=Serena | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111103203931/http://articles.cnn.com/2003-01-15/entertainment/people.watn.money_1_money-talks-rocker-eddie-money?_s=PM%3ASHOWBIZ | archive-date=November 3, 2011}}</ref> They were married for 30 years and had renewed their vows three months prior to his death.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kietty |first1=Martin |title=Eddie Money Renewed Marriage Vows Months Before He Died Read More: Eddie Money Renewed Marriage Vows Months Before He Died |url=https://ultimateclassicrock.com/eddie-money-marriage-vows/ |website=ultimateclassicrock.com |publisher=UCR |access-date=September 25, 2019}}</ref>

In March 2000, Money purchased a home in Westlake Village, California, where he lived with his family.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.vcstar.com/picture-gallery/news/local/communities/conejo-valley/2018/04/03/take-a-peek-at-eddie-moneys-family-westlake-home/33516049/ | title=Take a peek at Eddie Money's family, Westlake home | work=[[Ventura County Star]] | date=April 3, 2018}}</ref> At one point in the early 2000s, Money also had a home in Island Estates, a gated community in Palm Coast, Florida, which he called "my place to play golf, be creative, go fishing, go surfing and have fun".<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.news-journalonline.com/news/20190913/eddie-money-famed-rocker-and-former-flagler-county-resident--dies-at-70 | title=Eddie Money, famed rocker and former Flagler County resident, dies at 70 | first=Matt | last=Bruce | work=[[The Daytona Beach News-Journal]] | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref>

In 2001, Money joined a 12-step program to deal with his drinking and made a promise to his wife and children that he would change. In 2003, he reported that he was clean and sober.<ref name="Clean_Sober"/>

In July 2019, Money underwent heart valve surgery and contracted [[pneumonia]], causing him to cancel tour dates. He had been a cigarette smoker for years.<ref name=newsdaydead/> On August 24, 2019, he revealed that he had been diagnosed with stage 4 [[esophageal cancer]].<ref>{{cite news | url=https://edition.cnn.com/2019/08/24/entertainment/eddie-money-cancer-diagnosis-trnd/index.html | title=Rocker Eddie Money announces he has esophageal cancer | last=Vera | first=Amir| date=August 24, 2019| work=[[CNN]]}}</ref> Complications from the cancer resulted in his death at [[Keck Hospital of USC]] in Los Angeles on September 13, 2019, at age 70.<ref name=cause/><ref name=DeathVariety>{{cite news | url=https://variety.com/2019/music/news/eddie-money-dead-dies-1203334855/ | title=Eddie Money, 'Two Tickets to Paradise' Singer, Dies at 70 | work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] | last=Halperin | first=Shirley | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/eddie-money-dead-obit-884000/ | title=Eddie Money, 'Take Me Home Tonight' and 'Baby Hold On' Hitmaker, Dead at 70 | last=Kreps | first=Daniel | work=[[Rolling Stone]] | date=September 13, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=News|first=A. B. C.|date=December 17, 2020|title=The late Eddie Money's family files wrongful death lawsuit against hospital that treated him|url=https://www.kshe95.com/real-rock-news/the-late-eddie-moneys-family-files-wrongful-death-lawsuit-against-hospital-that-treated-him/|access-date=June 21, 2021|website=KSHE 95|language=en-US}}</ref> A year later, his family filed a [[Wrongful death claim|lawsuit alleging wrongful death]] against the hospital, with an additional allegation of medical malpractice.<ref name=":0" />
