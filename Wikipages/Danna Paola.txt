'''Danna Paola Rivera Munguía''' (born 23 June 1995) is a Mexican actress, singer and model. She gained popularity as a child actress and singer, starring in dozens of television projects throughout her early childhood and adolescence.

==Early life==
Danna Paola was born and raised in [[Mexico City]], Mexico. She is the daughter of Patricia Munguía and Juan José Rivera Arellano, the former singer of Grupo Ciclón and [[Los Caminantes]]. Her parents divorced during her childhood. She has an older sister, Vania.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.biosstars-mx.com/d/danna_paola.html|title=Danna Paola – Biografía|access-date=28 September 2013|publisher=biosstars-mx.com|language=es|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130927204035/http://www.biosstars-mx.com/d/danna_paola.html|archive-date=27 September 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.oprahdaily.com/entertainment/tv-movies/a31260925/who-is-danna-paola/|title=Who Plays Lu on "Élite"? Meet Danna Paola, Pop Star and Actress|date=13 March 2020|access-date=26 July 2021}}</ref>

==Acting career==

===1999–2003: Early career===
Danna Paola's acting career began in 1999 when at age 4 she and her sister attended [[Televisa]]'s casting call in Mexico City for ''[[Plaza Sésamo]]'', the Mexican version of ''[[Sesame Street]]''. Both were later cast on the show and appeared in several episodes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telenovelajuvenil.com/danna-paola-esta-en-su-salsa.html|title=Danna Paola está en su salsa|date=28 April 2009|access-date=28 September 2013|publisher=telenovelajuvenil.com|language=es|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130728145211/http://www.telenovelajuvenil.com/danna-paola-esta-en-su-salsa.html|archive-date=28 July 2013}}</ref> One year later at age 5, she was cast in the telenovela, ''Rayito de Luz'', in 2000. In 2001, she received her first lead role in the children's telenovela, ''[[María Belén]]''. The same year, she released her debut album, titled ''Mi Globo Azul''.{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}}

===2004–2012: Breakout success===
In 2004, she was chosen as the lead in the successful children's series, ''[[Amy, la niña de la mochila azul]]''. Her second studio album, ''Océano'', soon followed. A year later, she was the female lead of ''Pablo y Andrea'', the television adaptation of the classic novel, ''[[The Adventures of Tom Sawyer]]''. Over the next several years, she earned parts in multiple television series produced by [[Televisa]].{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}}

In 2009, she was cast as "Patito" in ''[[Atrévete a Soñar]]'', the Mexican remake of the popular Argentine children's telenovela, [[Patito Feo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vanguardia.com.mx/dan_una_probadita_de_atrevete_a_sonar_-271026.html/|title=Dan una "probadita" de Atrévete a soñar|date=11 December 2008|access-date=28 September 2013|work=Vanguardia|language=es}}</ref> Danna Paola filmed two seasons of the show and production was concluded in late 2009. ''Atrévete a Soñar'' marked Paola's transition from children's to teen telenovelas.  Paola and the cast of the telenovela recorded two soundtracks and toured across Mexico, in order to promote the show.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.informador.com.mx/entretenimiento/2009/144497/6/atrevete-a-sonar-presenta-su-nuevo-album.htm|title=Atrévete a soñar presenta su nuevo album|access-date=28 September 2013|work=El Informador|language=es}}</ref> The telenovela and its soundtrack were commercially successful throughout Mexico and Latin America. The soundtrack was certified double platinum in Mexico.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cronica.com.mx/nota.php?id_nota=454844/|title=Recibe "Atrévete a soñar" discos de Oro y Platino|access-date=28 September 2013|work=La Crónica de Hoy |language=es}}</ref>

In late 2010, she voiced "Rapunzel" for the Latin American Spanish-language dubbing of the Disney film ''[[Tangled]]''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/rapunzel.html|title=Rapunzel|website=Charguigou|access-date=17 May 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160520064959/http://disneyinternationaldubbings.weebly.com/rapunzel.html|archive-date=20 May 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> and participated in the film's Spanish-language soundtrack.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/mx/album/enredados-banda-sonora-la/id411744057|title=Enredados (Banda Sonora de la Película) de Various Artists en iTunes|website=iTunes|access-date=17 May 2016}}</ref>

===2013–2015: ''Wicked'' and other projects===
In May 2013, Danna Paola auditioned for the first [[Spanish-language]]  production of the successful [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] musical, ''[[Wicked (musical)|Wicked]]'', in Mexico City. She auditioned for both [[Glinda]] and [[Elphaba]], performing ''[[Popular (Wicked song)|Popular]]'' and ''[[Defying Gravity (song)|Defying Gravity]]''. Later that same month, the final cast for the Mexican production was announced, with Paola cast as "[[Elphaba]]". The production opened in [[Mexico City]] at Teatro Telcel on 17 October 2013, and closed on 18 January 2015. Critics in Mexico, as well as the play's original composer, [[Stephen Schwartz (composer)|Stephen Schwartz]], praised her performance.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.diarioimagen.net/?p=150582|title=Creadores de Wicked aplauden el musical mexicano que supera al de Broadway|access-date=19 October 2013|date=15 October 2013|work=Diario Imagen|first=Asael|last=Grande|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.provincia.com.mx/danna-paola-calla-prejuicios-y-sorprende-al-publico-en-wicked/|title=Danna Paola calla prejuicios y sorprende al público en "Wicked"|access-date=19 October 2013|date=17 October 2013|publisher=Provincia|author=Notimex|language=es|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131020113431/http://www.provincia.com.mx/danna-paola-calla-prejuicios-y-sorprende-al-publico-en-wicked/|archive-date=20 October 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> In addition to being well received by critics, the play was also a commercial success in Mexico City. During its first 10 weeks, "Wicked" sold over 100,000 tickets and had more than 100 performances; it broke a box office record making it one of the most successful theatre productions in Mexico's history.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tvnotas.com.mx/2014/01/18/C-58264-wicked-lanza-promocion-para-celebrar-sus-100-representaciones-en-cartelera.php|title='Wicked' lanza promoción para celebrar sus 100 representaciones en cartelera|access-date=8 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.televisa.com/telenovelas/noticias/677874/danna-paola-rechaza-dos-telenovelas-malquerida-sangre-guerreras/|title=Danna Paola rechaz dos telenovelas|access-date=8 October 2014}}</ref> At the age of 18, Danna Paola was the youngest person to play the role of "Elphaba" in a professional theatre production.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://innuendoandoutuendo.com/2013/09/wicked-to-open-in-mexico-city-in-october-complete-casting-announced/|title=Wicked to Open in Mexico City in October; Complete Casting Announced|access-date=10 December 2013|date=8 September 2013|publisher=innuendoandoutuendo.com}}</ref>

In January 2014, Danna Paola was cast in the movie, ''Saving Sara Cruz'',  a modern-day remake of the 1992 film, ''The Bodyguard''. Production on the film never commenced and the project was later shelved.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.younghollywood.com/videos/yhstudio/2014/february/mexican-wicked-starlet-danna-paola-sings-defying-gravity.html|title=Mexican 'Wicked' Starlet Danna Paola Sings "Defying Gravity"|work=Young Hollywood|access-date=16 October 2014}}</ref> The film was to be Danna Paola's English-language debut.{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}}

In March 2014, Danna, debuted as fashion designer collaborating with the popular Mexican clothing brand, "Sexy Jeans".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mx.hola.com/cine/201403066402/danna-paola-disenadora-moda/|title=Danna Paola da el salto a las pasarelas y debuta como diseñadora|work=Hola!|access-date=17 October 2014}}</ref> Her line, "Danna Paola, by SexyJns", was commercially successful with products selling out across Mexico.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://yucatan.com.mx/espectaculos/es-cantante-actriz-y-disenadora-de-modas|title=Es cantante, actriz y diseñadora de modas|work=Yucatan's Daily|access-date=17 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.elgrafico.mx/farandula/13-03-2014/danna-paola-nos-habla-sobre-su-sexy-linea-de-ropa|title=Danna Paola nos habla sobre su sexy línea de ropa|work=El Grafico|access-date=17 October 2014}}</ref>

In August 2014, Danna participated in the [[ABBA]] tribute album, "Dancing Queens", recording a cover of  "[[Take A Chance On Me]]". "[[Take A Chance On Me]]" was released as single on 2 September 2014 and debuted at number 1 on [[iTunes]] México.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.universalmusicmagazine.com/los-hits-de-abba-se-revisten-2/|title=Los hits de Abba se revisten|work=Universal Music Magazine|access-date=16 October 2014}}</ref>

On 29 October 2014, she performed "No hay bien", the Spanish-language version of "[[No Good Deed (song)|No Good Deed]]", to a sold-out crowd at the [[National Auditorium|Auditorio Nacional]] for the ''Lunas del Auditorio'' award show. That night, ''Wicked'' received an award in the  "Broadway Show" category.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://laprimeraplana.com.mx/2014/10/30/danna-paola-sorprende-cantando-en-las-lunas-del-auditorio-video/|title=Danna Paola sorprende cantando en Las Lunas del Auditorio|work=La Primera Plana|access-date=5 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141104052807/http://laprimeraplana.com.mx/2014/10/30/danna-paola-sorprende-cantando-en-las-lunas-del-auditorio-video/|archive-date=4 November 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Danna Paola appeared in over 300 performances of ''Wicked'' from 2013 to early 2015. Following the conclusion of "Wicked", she was invited by producer Alex Gou, to join the cast of the musical, [[Hoy No Me Puedo Levantar]], where she played the lead role of "Maria" and went on tour throughout Mexico.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Woodside|first1=Maxine|title=Arranca Danna Paola temporada de 'Hoy No Me Puedo Levantar'|url=http://www.radioformula.com.mx/notas.asp?Idn=518448&idFC=2015|website=Radioformula|publisher=Radioformula|access-date=17 July 2015}}</ref> In July 2015, she signed a contract with the U.S. based Spanish-language network, ''[[Telemundo]]'', marking her return to telenovelas after a nearly five-year absence.{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}}

In August 2015, she recorded the duet, "Mientras Me Enamoras" with Mexican singer, Lalo Brito. A music video was also filmed, and it premiered on Brito's YouTube channel in September 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latarde.com.mx/destilanquimica-81294.html|title= Destilan química Lalo Brito y Danna Paola|access-date=21 January 2015}}</ref>
During the summer of 2015, Paola moved to Miami to film the telenovela drama, ''[[¿Quién es quién?]]''. The project is her first telenovela role outside of [[Televisa]].  Filming for the program concluded in September 2015. ''[[¿Quién es quién?]]'' began airing weeknights in the U.S. on Telemundo on 9 February 2016.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Di Lauro|first1=Janet|title=Danna Paola Stars in New Telemundo Series ‘¿Quién es quién?’|url=http://www.soapoperanetwork.com/2016/02/47552|website=Soap Opera Net|publisher=Soap Opera Net|access-date=22 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=De la Luz Miranda|first1=María|title=Danna Paola concluye grabaciones de "Quién es quién"|url=http://www.radioformula.com.mx/notas.asp?Idn=555333&idFC=2015|website=Radio Formula|publisher=Grupo Formula|access-date=15 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=De la Luz Miranda|first1=María|title=Danna Paola, feliz con Telemundo|url=http://www.eluniversal.com.mx/articulo/espectaculos/television/2015/07/15/danna-paola-feliz-con-telemundo-y-hoy-no-me-puedo|website=El Universal|publisher=El Universal|access-date=17 July 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=De la Luz Miranda|first1=María|title=Confirmado: Danna Paola se va a Telemundo|url=http://www.laopinion.com/confirmado-danna-paola-se-va-a-telemundo|website=La opinion|publisher=Agencia Reforma|access-date=17 July 2015}}</ref>

===2016–present===
In early 2016, Paola filmed in the Mexican movie, ''¡Como va! Lo más sencillo es complicarlo todo'' (later renamed ''Lo Más Sencillo Es Complicarlo Todo''), on location in [[Mexico City]], [[Puerto Vallarta]], and [[Querétaro City|Querétaro]]. The movie was released in Mexican cinemas on 26 January 2018.<ref>{{cite web|author=Notimex|title=DANNA PAOLA LE TIRA LLEGAR A HOLLYWOOD Y HASTA PREPARA SERIE EN INGLÉS|url=http://www.lasestrellas.tv/espectaculos/cine-y-series/danna-paola-debut-pelicula-lo-mas-sencillo-es-complicado/|website=Las Estrellas|publisher=Televisa|access-date=30 January 2018}}</ref><ref name="film">{{cite web|url=http://yucatan.com.mx/espectaculos/cine/danna-paola-vuelve-al-cine|title=Danna Paola vuelve al cine'|author=Notimex|date=12 April 2016|website=yucatan.com.mx|access-date=25 April 2016|language=es}}{{Dead link|date=July 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
In mid-April 2016, she was confirmed as a cast member in the telenovela drama, ''La Doña'',  produced by [[Telemundo]].  The telenovela filmed in Mexico with [[Aracely Arámbula]] and [[David Chocarro]] and premiered in November 2016. The finale aired on Telemundo in the U.S. on 1 May 2017.<ref name="doña">{{cite web |url=http://www.latintimes.com/la-dona-telemundo-finale-live-stream-video-watch-aracely-arambula-danna-paola-last-416750|title='La Doña' Telemundo Finale Live Stream Video: Watch Aracely Arámbula, Danna Paola In Last Episode!|author=Latin Times|date=1 May 2017|website=latintimes.com|access-date=30 January 2018|language=es}}</ref>

In 2017, Paola was cast in the [[Telemundo]] bio-series, ''José José: El príncipe de la canción'', based on the life of Mexican singer [[José José]]. Paola played famed Mexican singer-songwriter, [[Lucero (entertainer)|Lucero]] and appears in 4 episodes of the show. The miniseries premiered on Telemundo on 15 January 2018.<ref name="series">{{cite web |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwtv/article/Telemundos-Original-Production-JOSE-JOSE-EL-PRINCIPE-DE-LA-CANCION-Premieres-115-20180103|title="Telemundo's Original Production JOSE JOSE, EL PRINCIPE DE LA CANCION Premieres 1/15"|author=Broadway World|date=3 January 2018|website=Broadwayworld.com|access-date=30 January 2018 |language=es}}</ref> In late January 2018, Paola was scheduled to begin filming a role in the bio series based on the life of Mexican actress, Silva Pinal. She later dropped out before filming began and instead revealed that she was cast in the [[Netflix]] original Spanish teen thriller television series, ''[[Élite (TV series)|Élite]]''. Production for the television show forced Paola to move to [[Madrid]] for six months starting in January 2018. She was cast in the program following a long audition process in 2017. The show was shot in [[4K resolution|4K]] and it premiered globally on Netflix in October 2018; Paola went on to star in 3 seasons of the series.<ref name="netflix">{{cite web |url=https://media.netflix.com/en/press-releases/élite-second-netflix-spanish-original-series-coming-to-netflix-in-2019|title="ÉLITE SECOND NETFLIX SPANISH ORIGINAL SERIES COMING TO NETFLIX IN 2019"|author=Alicia Deza|date=17 July 2017 |website=netflix.com|access-date=30 January 2018|language=en}}</ref> In 2019, Paola was cast as a judge on the singing-competition series [[La Academia]] for [[TV Azteca]].{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}} In 2021, she was cast as a mentor in Spanish singing competition series ''[[Top Star. ¿Cuánto vale tu voz?]]'' for [[Telecinco]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rodríguez |first1=Christian |title=Danna Paola será jueza de ‘Top Star’, un programa musical donde trabajará junto a Isabel Pantoja |url=https://www.cosmopolitan.com/es/famosos/peliculas-series/a35925431/danna-paola-top-star-isabel-pantoja/ |website=Cosmopolitan España |access-date=10 May 2021 |language=Spanish |date=24 March 2021}}</ref>

==Music career==
===Debut studio album as solo singer (2011–2013)===
In 2011, she began the production for her fourth solo studio album which also served as her debut album as a "grown-up" solo recording artist. Her previous discography included songs for telenovelas, children's music, and film soundtracks.  She revealed that she wanted her new album to reflect her growth as an artist and shed her image as a child actress.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cronica.com.mx/nota.php?id_nota=664381/|title=Contenta Danna Paola con disco homónimo|access-date=28 September 2013|work=La Crónica de Hoy |language=es}}</ref> "[[Ruleta (Danna Paola song)|Ruleta]]", written by Mexican pop singer, [[Paty Cantú]], was announced as the album's lead single. The official music video premiered on Paola's official VEVO channel on 14 March 2012. The single rose to number 32 on the Top Mexican Airplay charts in July 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1426608/latin-notas|title=Latin Notas|date=21 October 2014|work=Billboard|access-date=8 October 2014}}</ref>

===Upcoming second studio album (2016–present)===
In-mid February 2016, she announced that her upcoming single, "Baila hasta caer", would be released in March for radio and digital download. The song was recorded in Miami sometime during the filming of her telenovela, ''[[¿Quién es quién?]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gonzalez|first1=Moises|url=https://www.elsoldelcentro.com.mx/espectaculos/cine/tu-primer-amor-no-va-a-estar-contigo-toda-la-vida-danna-paola|title=Tu primer amor no va a estar contigo toda la vida: Danna Paola|website=People en español|publisher=People en Español|access-date=22 February 2016}}{{Dead link|date=November 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

As part of the 2018 film release for "Lo Más Sencillo Es Complicarlo Todo", she released the single, "¿Dónde estabas tú?" in November 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.elsoldelcentro.com.mx/espectaculos/cine/tu-primer-amor-no-va-a-estar-contigo-toda-la-vida-danna-paola|title=Tu primer amor no va a estar contigo toda la vida: Danna Paola|date=24 January 2018|work=El sol del centro|access-date=30 January 2018}}{{Dead link|date=November 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Her latest single, "Oye Pablo" was released on 29 August 2019.<ref>{{cite news |title="Es demasiado tarde": Danna Paola se sincera sobre lo que sucedió con el protagonista de "Oye Pablo"|trans-title="It's too late": Danna Paola opens up about what happened to the protagonist of "Oye Pablo" |url=https://www.infobae.com/america/entretenimiento/2020/10/17/es-demasiado-tarde-danna-paola-se-sincera-sobre-lo-que-sucedio-con-el-protagonista-de-oye-pablo/ |access-date=14 January 2021 |work=Infobae |date=17 October 2020}}</ref>
