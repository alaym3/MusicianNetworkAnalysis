==Early life==
Smith was born in Dallas, Texas. At 14, he became interested in music and decided to pursue it as a hobby, teaching himself guitar.<ref name=Lubbock>{{cite news|last=Johnson|first=Natalie|title=Granger Smith returns from Iraq, Kuwait, and White House|url=http://lubbockonline.com/stories/082808/fea_464018158.shtml|newspaper=Lubbock-Avalanche Journal|access-date=March 29, 2014}}</ref> After graduating from Lake Highlands High School in 1998, Smith attended Texas A&M University in College Station, Texas, and was a member of the Corps of Cadets. After his sophomore year, he moved from Texas to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] after signing a songwriting contract with EMI Music Publishing.Later, in 2005, he returned to Texas and re-enrolled at Texas A&M to complete his degree. == Musical career ==
Granger Smith was signed to a contract at the age of nineteen in Nashville. He has performed three times at the [[White House]], and in 2008 he traveled to perform for soldiers in [[Iraq]] and [[Kuwait]].<ref name="Lubbock"/> His 2013 album ''[[Dirt Road Driveway]]'' peaked at number 15 on US country charts and number 11 on US indie charts.

On August 12, 2015, Smith announced he had signed with [[Broken Bow Records]] imprint Wheelhouse Records. Smith's EP ''[[4x4 (Granger Smith album)|4x4]]'' was co-produced by Smith and [[Frank Rogers (record producer)|Frank Rogers]]. The album debuted on the Top Country Albums chart at number 6, and reached number 51 on ''Billboard'' 200. The lead single from the album was "Backroad Song", which sold over 32,000 downloads in its first week of release.

Some of Smith's recordings make use of an assistant named Earl Dibbles Jr.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.countrycountdownusa.com/2017/02/11/who-is-earl-dibbles-jr/|title=Who Is Earl Dibbles Jr.?|date=February 11, 2017|website=Country Countdown USA}}</ref>

In December 2018, Smith released a single from his soundtrack album called ''They Were There, A Hero's Documentary'', which came out on November 30, 2018.<ref name="Itunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/they-were-there-heros-documentary-original-motion-picture/1440136437|title=Granger Smith New Documentary called 'They Were There, A Hero's Documentary (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack)'|work=[[iTunes]]|date=November 30, 2018|access-date=November 30, 2018}}</ref> "They Were There" and its music video came out on the same day, December 7, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wG69eX4Rxwo|title=Granger Smith New Single Music Released from 'They Were There, A Hero's Documentary (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack)'|work=YouTube|date=December 7, 2018|access-date=December 7, 2018}}</ref>

On July 19, 2019, Smith performed for an audience of 86,000 as the opening act for the [[The Garth Brooks Stadium Tour|Garth Brooks Stadium Tour]] at the Albertsons Stadium in Boise.<ref>{{Citation|title=The Garth Brooks Stadium Tour|date=April 2, 2020|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=The_Garth_Brooks_Stadium_Tour&oldid=948716819|work=Wikipedia|language=en|access-date=April 17, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==

He married Amber Emily Bartlett on February 11, 2010. They met on the set of the music video for Smith's song "Don't Listen to the Radio", and she has been featured in several of his music videos since. The couple have one daughter and three sons.<ref name="river">{{cite web|last=|first=|title=Granger Smith's Real-Life Love Story Plays Out in 'Happens Like That' Music Video|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/granger-smith-wife-happens-like-that-video/|url-status=live|access-date=February 22, 2018|work=Taste of Country}}</ref>

On June 6, 2019, Smith announced that the couple's youngest son, River Kelly Smith, had died following a drowning accident at their home.<ref>{{cite news|last1=|first1=|date=June 6, 2019|title=Country singer Granger Smith announces death of 3-year-old son|publisher=USA Today|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2019/06/06/granger-smith-son-dead-tragic-accident/1370451001/|access-date=}}</ref> River had previously appeared in the video for "Happens Like That".<ref name="river"/>

On March 11, 2021, Granger Smith and his wife Amber, announced they were having their fourth child, a baby boy in August.<ref>{{Cite web|date=March 11, 2021|title=Granger Smith and Wife Amber Expecting Baby Boy 21 Months After the Death of Son River|url=https://people.com/parents/granger-smith-wife-amber-pregnant-expecting-baby-boy-after-death-of-son-river/|url-status=live|access-date=|website=People.com|language=en}}</ref> Their son, Maverick Beckham Smith was born August 20, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 20, 2021|title=Granger Smith and Wife Amber Welcome Son Maverick Beckham: 'So Many Emotions'|url=https://people.com/parents/granger-smith-wife-amber-welcome-son-maverick-beckham/|url-status=live|access-date=|website=People.com|language=en}}</ref>
