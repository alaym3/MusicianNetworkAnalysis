'''9ice''', born '''Alexander''' '''Abolore Adegbola Akande''' (born 17 January 1980) is a [[Nigerians|Nigerian]] musician, songwriter and dancer.He is known for his powerful use of the Yoruba language in his music as well as his proverbial lyrics and unique style of delivery.

== Early life ==
9ice was born into a [[Polygamy|polygamous]] home of five wives and nine children, in [[Ogbomosho]], [[Oyo State]] in Western [[Nigeria]]. He grew up in shomolu [[Bariga]] suburbs of [[Lagos]]. 9ice had also dreamed of being a musician.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2017/03/marriage-no-marriage-children-9ice/|title=Marriage or no marriage, I am with my children – 9ice|date=25 March 2017|website=Vanguard News|access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> His parents discovered his singing talent, and decided to allow him become a musician.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://buzznigeria.com/9ice/|title=9ice Biography And Everything You Need To Know About The Musician|last=Udeze|first=Chuka|date=25 June 2018|website=BuzzNigeria – Famous People, Celebrity Bios, Updates and Trendy News|access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref><ref name="Before Stardom With… 9ice">{{Cite web|url=https://punchng.com/before-stardom-with-9ice/|title=Before Stardom With… 9ice|website=Punch Newspapers|access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 1996, 9ice recorded his first demo titled '''''Risi de Alagbaja,''''' but it was not until 2000 that he released his first official [[Solo (music)|solo song]] ''Little Money''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thisdaylive.com/index.php/2020/04/19/youtube-music-harnessing-the-power-of-google-2/|title=YouTube Music: Harnessing the Power of Google |date=19 April 2020|website=THISDAYLIVE|access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref><ref name="Before Stardom With… 9ice"/>

In 2008, 9ice released the single ''Gongo Aso''. With the song gaining popularity, 9ice was asked to perform at the [[Nelson Mandela 90th Birthday Tribute]] concert in London, United Kingdom in June 2008.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/7475962.stm|title=Reporter's log: Mandela concert|date=27 June 2008|access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref> He went on to win the Best Hip Hop Artist of the Year at the [[MTV Africa Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pulse.ng/9ice-singer-releases-8th-studio-album-titled-id-cabasa/6rs88nn|title=Singer releases 8th studio album titled 'ID Cabasa'|date=22 November 2016|website=Pulse Nigeria|access-date=29 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/7744492.stm|title=Nigerians sweep MTV Africa awards|date=23 November 2008|work=BBC News|access-date=13 February 2016}}</ref>

''Gongo Aso'' won him four further awards at the 2009 edition of the [[The Headies|Hip Hop World Awards]] held at the International Conference Centre, [[Abuja]]: the Album of the Year, Artiste of the Year, Song of the Year and the Best Rap in Pop Album categories.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.modernghana.com/nollywood/3480/my-mother-left-me-when-i-was-8-months-old-9ice.html|title=My mother left me when I was 8 months old – 9ice|website=Modern Ghana|access-date=20 April 2020}}</ref>

In 2020, 9ice released another album entitled "'''''Tip of The Iceberg: Episode 1'''''"<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=[Album] 9ice – Tip Of The Iceberg: Episode 1|url=https://virginsound.com.ng/albums/9ice-tip-of-the-iceberg-episode-1/|date=2020-05-30|website=VirginSound|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-30}}</ref> to top his previously released albums. He is the Founder and CEO of his own music record label Alapomeji Ancestral Record.  [https://m.guardian.ng/tag/9ice]
