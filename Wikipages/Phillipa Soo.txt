'''Phillipa Anne Soo'''{{efn|Pronounced {{IPAc-en|ˈ|f|ɪ|l|ɪ|p|ə|_|s|uː}}.}} (born May 31, 1990) is an American actress. Soo is best known for originating the role of [[Eliza Hamilton]] in the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] musical ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]'', a performance which earned her a nomination for a [[70th Tony Awards|2016]] [[Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical|Tony Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role in a Musical]] and a [[Grammy Award for Best Musical Theater Album]] in the [[58th Annual Grammy Awards|same year]].<ref name=Windman>{{cite news|last1=Windman|first1=Matt|title=Phillipa Soo on starring in Broadway's sold-out smash 'Hamilton'|url=http://www.amny.com/entertainment/phillipa-soo-on-starring-in-broadway-s-sold-out-smash-hamilton-1.10715582|access-date=12 August 2015|publisher=amNewYork|date=6 August 2015}}</ref><ref name=Nordstrom>{{cite news|last1=Nordstrom|first1=Leigh|title=Phillipa Soo Soars in Broadway Hit 'Hamilton'|url=http://wwd.com/eye/people/phillipa-soo-stars-hamilton-broadway-10196850/|access-date=12 August 2015|publisher=WWD|date=5 August 2015}}</ref><ref name=Schulman>{{cite news|last1=Schulman|first1=Michael|title=The Women of "Hamilton"|url=http://www.newyorker.com/culture/cultural-comment/the-women-of-hamilton|access-date=12 August 2015|work=The New Yorker|date=6 August 2015}}</ref> She also originated  the role of [[Natasha Rostova]] in the off-Broadway production of ''[[Natasha, Pierre & The Great Comet of 1812]]'' in 2012 and the title role in the Broadway production of ''[[Amélie (musical)|Amélie]]'' in 2017. 

Soo appeared on Broadway in ''[[The Parisian Woman]]'', which ran from November 2017 to March 2018. She appeared in the film [[Over the Moon (2020 film)|''Over the Moon'']] playing the voice of [[Chang'e]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Michael|first=Ordona|date=26 January 2021|title=Guiding light to new myth; Late screenwriter Audrey Wells helped 'Over the Moon,' a Chinese legend-inspired film voiced by Asian Americans, find its way|work=Los Angeles Times|url=https://www.pressreader.com/usa/los-angeles-times/20210126/282497186329736|url-status=live}}</ref> In [[73rd Primetime Emmy Awards|2021]], she received a nomination for a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited or Anthology Series or Movie|Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Actress in a Supporting Role in a Limited Series or Movie]] for her performance in the [[Disney+]] [[Hamilton (2020 film)|live stage recording]] of ''Hamilton'' which was released in 2020.

== Early life and education ==
Soo was born in Libertyville, Illinois to a white American mother and a Chinese American father. In an interview with ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'', Soo spoke about her upbringing: "In terms of my own experience, my dad is first-generation, so his parents were from [[China]], and my mom was born and raised in [[southern Illinois]], and she was involved in the arts. My dad's a doctor. I was very much always encouraged [to pursue an acting career], but the mentality of 'get your education' was still there."<ref name="Hollywood Reporter"/>

Phillipa attended [[Libertyville High School]] in suburban [[Chicago]] from 2004 to 2008.<ref>{{Cite web|title = ePawPrints {{!}} District 128|url = http://d128.org/epawprints|website = d128.org|access-date = 2015-12-02}}</ref> Soo graduated from [[Juilliard School|The Juilliard School]]'s acting program in 2012.<ref name="Robbins">{{Cite web|title = BWW Interview - Debut of the Month: HAMILTON's Phillipa Soo|url = http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/BWW-Interview-Debut-of-the-Month-HAMILTONs-Phillipa-Soo-20150820|access-date = 2015-09-06|first = Caryn|last = Robbins}}</ref>

== Acting career ==

===Theater===
After graduating from Juilliard in 2012, Soo was cast as [[Natasha Rostova]] in the [[Ars Nova (theater)|Ars Nova]] production of [[Dave Malloy]]'s ''[[Natasha, Pierre & The Great Comet of 1812]]'', based on [[Leo Tolstoy]]'s ''[[War and Peace]]''. The show went on to transfer from Ars Nova to another Off-Broadway space, Kazino, a tent custom-built for the show.

[[File:Phillipa Soo Renée Elise Goldsberry and Jasmine Cephas Jones perform Hamilton at the White House.jpg|thumb|Soo (left) performs with ''Hamilton'' castmates at the White House]]
After seeing her performance in ''Great Comet'', director [[Thomas Kail]] and composer and writer [[Lin-Manuel Miranda]] asked her to take part in an early 2014 reading of the musical ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]'', where she read as the leading female role, "Eliza" ([[Elizabeth Schuyler Hamilton]]). Soo remained with the show through its Off-Broadway and Broadway debuts. She received a 2016 [[Tony Award]] nomination for Best Performance by an Actress in a Leading Role in a Musical, along with [[Laura Benanti]], [[Carmen Cusack]], [[Jessie Mueller]], and winner [[Cynthia Erivo]].<ref>{{cite web|title=See Full List of 2016 Tony Award Nominations {{!}} Playbill|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/2016-tony-nominations-are-being-revealed|website=Playbill|date=3 May 2016|language=en}}</ref> Soo played her final performance in ''Hamilton'' on July 9, 2016, and the role of Eliza was taken over by [[Lexi Lawson]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/theater-arts/hamilton-names-replacements-leslie-odom-jr-phillipa-article-1.2700988|title='Hamilton' names replacements for Leslie Odom Jr. & Phillipa Soo|last=Dziemianowicz|first=Joe|date=July 6, 2016|website=The Washington Post|access-date=July 9, 2016}}</ref>

Soo appeared in the title role in the pre-Broadway engagement of ''[[Amélie (musical)|Amélie]]'' at the [[Ahmanson Theatre]] in [[Los Angeles]] from December 2016 to January 2017. ''Amélie'' then began preview performances on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] at the [[Walter Kerr Theatre]] on March 9, 2017, opening officially on April 3.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Viagas|first1=Robert|title=Full Cast Announced for Broadway-Aimed Amélie Musical {{!}} Playbill|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/hamilton-39-s-phillipa-soo-will-star-in-amelie-musical-on-broadway#|website=Playbill|date=17 October 2016|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=New Musical Amélie Opens on Broadway April 3 {{!}} Playbill|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/broadways-amelie-begins-performances-march-9#|website=Playbill|date=3 April 2017|language=en}}</ref> The show closed on May 21, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Instagram post by Amélie on Broadway • May 4, 2017 at 11:02pm UTC|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BTsCpXbj7Ph/|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref>

Soo also appeared on Broadway as Rebecca in ''[[The Parisian Woman]]'', an original play by [[Beau Willimon]]. The production began preview performances at the [[Hudson Theatre]] on November 7, 2017, and opened on November 30 for a limited run through March 11, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/hamiltons-phillipa-soo-and-marton-csokas-join-the-parisian-woman-on-broadway|title=Hamilton's Phillipa Soo and Marton Csokas Join The Parisian Woman on Broadway {{!}} Playbill|website=Playbill|date=5 September 2017|language=en|access-date=2017-10-15}}</ref>

===Film and television===
In 2013 she was cast in a small recurring role in the NBC television series ''[[Smash (U.S. TV series)|Smash]]'' as the character Lexi. She appeared in five episodes in the second season before the show's cancellation.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Phillipa Soo|url = https://www.imdb.com/name/nm5623883/|website = IMDb|access-date = 2015-09-06}}</ref> She had a small supporting role as Nia in the 2014 television pilot ''Dangerous Liaisons'', but it did not get picked up to series.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Goldberg|first=Lesley|date=20 February 2018|title='Hamilton' Tony Nominee Boards CBS Drama Pilot|work=The Hollywood Reporter|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/hamilton-tony-nominee-boards-cbs-drama-pilot-1086793|url-status=live|access-date=26 March 2021}}</ref> 

In 2018 it was announced that Soo had been cast in the CBS series ''[[The Code (2019 TV series)|The Code]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://variety.com/2018/tv/news/hamilton-phillipa-soo-cbs-drama-pilot-the-code-1202706055/|title='Hamilton' Star Phillipa Soo Joins CBS Drama Pilot 'The Code'|last=Otterson|first=Joe|date=2018-02-21|work=Variety|access-date=2018-08-19|language=en-US}}</ref> The show aired for one season before its cancellation in July 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Petski|first1=Nellie Andreeva,Denise|last2=Andreeva|first2=Nellie|last3=Petski|first3=Denise|date=2019-07-23|title='The Code' Canceled By CBS After One Season|url=https://deadline.com/2019/07/the-code-canceled-cbs-one-season-1202607273/|access-date=2020-07-13|website=Deadline|language=en}}</ref> In 2021, she received a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited or Anthology Series or Movie|Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Limited Series or Movie]] nomination for her performance in the [[Disney+]] live stage recording of ''[[Hamilton (2020 film)|Hamilton]]'' which was released in 2020.

===Philanthropy===
Inspired by [[Elizabeth Schuyler Hamilton]], the character she played in ''[[Hamilton (musical)|Hamilton]]'', Soo started the Eliza Project initiative in partnership with [[Graham Windham]], the first private orphanage in New York City that is mentioned in the last song at the end of the show. Through the program, Soo plans to provide students at the Graham School with acting, dancing, and rap workshops. According to Soo, the core mission of "The Eliza Project" is "to use the arts as a means of expression, as an outlet for personal experience, and to uplift the creative spirit."<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/hamilton-cast-helps-children-in-need-1451442302 |title='Hamilton' Cast Helps Children in Need |first=Leslie |last=Brody |date=December 30, 2015 |newspaper=Wall Street Journal}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Soo became engaged to actor [[Steven Pasquale]] in February 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Broadways-Steven-Pasquale-and-Phillipa-Soo-Are-Engaged-20160216 |title=Broadway's Steven Pasquale and Phillipa Soo Are Engaged! |last=Khan |first=Jessica |date=16 February 2016 |website=Broadway World|access-date=17 February 2016}}</ref> They married on September 24, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/tony-nominee-phillipa-soo-and-broadway-star-steve-pasquale-are-married|title=Tony Nominee Phillipa Soo and Stage and Screen Star Steven Pasquale Marry {{!}} Playbill|website=Playbill|date=25 September 2017|language=en|access-date=2017-10-15}}</ref> In 2019, the two starred opposite each other in an episode of ''The Code''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Legit Bad Day|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt10026186/|access-date=2019-07-29}}</ref> She has a dog named Billie, which she adopted for her 30th birthday in 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Shattuck|first=Kathryn|date=27 October 2020|title=Phillipa Soo's Week: Fund-Raising Videos and 'Agents of Chaos'|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/27/movies/phillipa-soo-over-the-moon.html|url-status=live}}</ref>
