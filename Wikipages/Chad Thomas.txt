'''Chad Thomas''' (born October 12, 1995), also known as '''Major Nine''',<ref name="palmbeachpost.com">{{cite web|url=http://caneswatch.blog.palmbeachpost.com/2017/03/20/producer-of-sacks-and-beats-chad-thomas-makes-track-for-rick-ross/|title=Rick Ross calls on college football player, artist & music producer Chad Thomas - Canes Watch|publisher=}}</ref> is an [[American football]] [[defensive end]] who is currently a free agent. He is also a [[Rapping|rapper]] and music producer. He played [[college football]] at the [[University of Miami]] and was drafted by the Browns in the third round of the [[2018 NFL Draft]].

==High school career==
Thomas led [[Booker T. Washington High School (Miami)|Booker T. Washington High School]] to the Class 4A state title as junior in 2012 and class 4A state champs as senior in 2013. He recorded 57 tackles and six sacks in 2012. He was named 2013 second-team All-USA by ''[[USA Today]]'' and tallied 71 tackles and eight sacks as senior. After high school, Thomas chose Miami over [[Alabama Crimson Tide football|Alabama]], [[Florida State Seminoles football|Florida State]], [[Arkansas Razorbacks football|Arkansas]] and [[Duke Blue Devils football|Duke]].<ref name="hurricanesports.com">{{cite web|url=https://hurricanesports.com/roster.aspx?rp_id=5290|title=University of Miami Hurricanes Official Athletic Site|website=hurricanesports.com}}</ref>

==College career==
Thomas started every game as a senior, recording 41 tackles, 12.5 for a loss (leading the team) and 5.5 sacks.<ref name="hurricanesports.com"/>

==Professional career==
On December 27, 2017, it was announced that Thomas had accepted his invitation to play in the [[2018 East-West Shrine Game]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Chad Thomas Will Play in East-West Shrine Game |url=https://247sports.com/college/miami/Bolt/Chad-Thomas-Will-Play-in-East-West-Shrine-Game-112776631/ |access-date=August 26, 2018 |website=247sports.com |author=Ivins, Andrew |date=December 27, 2017 }}{{Dead link|date=November 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> He had a productive week of practice and separated himself as the top defensive end prospect playing in the [[East-West Shrine Game]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Big O Scouts Florida Players: Chad Thomas Shines At East-West Shrine |url=https://miami.cbslocal.com/2018/01/19/big-o-scouts-florida-players-chad-thomas-shines-at-east-west-shrine/ |access-date=August 26, 2018 |website=miami.cbslocal.com |date=January 19, 2018}}</ref> On January 20, 2017, Thomas made one tackle as part of the East who lost 14–10 to the West in the 2018 East-West Shrine Game.<ref>{{cite web |title=East-West Shrine Game stats |url=https://www.shrinegame.com/stats1 |access-date=August 26, 2018 |website=shrinegame.com}}</ref> His overall performance during the week garnered him an invitation to the [[2018 Senior Bowl]].<ref>{{cite web |title=2018 NFL Draft: Miami Hurricanes' Chad Thomas gets Senior Bowl spot |url=http://caneswatch.blog.palmbeachpost.com/2018/01/19/2018-nfl-draft-miami-hurricanes-chad-thomas-gets-senior-bowl-spot/ |access-date=August 26, 2018 |website=caneswatch.blog.palmbeachpost.com |author=Porter, Matt |date=January 19, 2018}}</ref> Thomas also impressed scouts and team representatives during practices for the [[Senior Bowl]], but was unable to play in the game due to an unspecified injury.<ref>{{cite web |title=Miami Hurricanes football: How did UM prospects fare at the Senior Bowl? The [[Cleveland Browns]] selected Thomas in the third round (67th overall) of the [[2018 NFL Draft]]. ==Music career==
Thomas is also a music producer and rapper, known as Major Nine.<ref name="palmbeachpost.com"/> Thomas has worked with [[Rick Ross]], [[Raphael Saadiq]], and has produced his own music.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/Major-nine-testimony-lyrics|title=Major Nine – Testimony|website=Genius.com}}</ref> In 2020, he appeared on [[Chris Brown]] and [[Young Thug]]'s mixtape ''[[Slime & B]]'', on the track "Trap Back".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://listen.tidal.com/album/140576140|title=Slime & B by Chris Brown|publisher=[[Tidal (service)|Tidal]]|access-date=May 8, 2020}}</ref> He released his popular album, "Soul Ties" on December 25th 2018, which led to a huge increase in his fanbase.
