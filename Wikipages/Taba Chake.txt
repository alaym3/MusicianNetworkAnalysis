'''Taba Chake''' (born 24 December 1993) is a [[Nyishi people|Nyishi]] finger-style guitarist and singer-songwriter based in [[Mumbai]]. His debut EP titled ''Bond with Nature'' was released in 2016, with a 10-track album ''Bombay Dreams'' being released in 2019. 

== Early life ==
Chake grew up in a village called Rono in [[Papum Pare district|Papum Pare]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=13 November 2019|title=Soundtrack of a City|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/lifestyle/art-and-culture/soundtrack-of-a-city-6116721/|access-date=25 March 2021|website=The Indian Express|language=en}}</ref> His parents never went to school and he describes his generation as "the first generation who grew up with buildings, telephones, roads and cars."<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Nehra|first=Devi|date=5 December 2020|title=Taba Chake brings Nyishi to the indie scene: Singer-songwriter on performing in an Arunachal dialect, his artistic evolution|url=https://www.firstpost.com/entertainment/taba-chake-brings-nyishi-to-the-indie-scene-singer-songwriter-on-performing-in-an-arunachal-dialect-his-artistic-evolution-9076791.html|url-status=live|access-date=25 March 2021|website=Firstpost}}</ref> He speaks and understands five languages: [[Nishi language|Nyishi]], [[Hindi]], English, [[Nepali language|Nepali]] and [[Assamese language|Assamese]], and uses all of these in his music, however in 2008 he decided to prioritise the use of Nyishi to keep the language alive and present it to a wider audience.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title=Multilingual singer-songwriter Taba Chake out with his latest single ‘Blush’|url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/magazine/2020/nov/08/multilingual-singer-songwriter-taba-chake-out-with-his-latest-singleblush-2220122.html|access-date=25 March 2021|website=The New Indian Express}}</ref> 

Chake started practicing music at the age of six and started writing songs at the age of 11.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|date=5 February 2021|title=Musical notes from the North East|url=https://www.newindianexpress.com/cities/bengaluru/2020/feb/05/musical-notes-from-the-north-east-2099015.html|url-status=live|access-date=25 March 2021|website=The New Indian Express}}</ref> He left Arunchal Pradesh to study music in [[Bangalore]] in around 2010.<ref name=":1" /> Chake experimented with many genres of music as he matured, including playing guitar and composing music for a [[Heavy metal music|metal]] band, before joining a Hindi band, which he performed in from 2009 to 2011. Following its breakup, Chake started performing solo.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|last=Datta|first=Sravasti|date=12 June 2019|title=Notes from Arunachal|language=en-IN|work=The Hindu|url=https://www.thehindu.com/entertainment/music/notes-from-arunachal/article27868543.ece|access-date=24 March 2021|issn=0971-751X}}</ref>

== Career ==
Chake's first album ''Bond with Nature'' was released in 2016.<ref name=":0" /> In around 2017, Chake won a scholarship to study music at the Swarnabhoomi Academy of Music in [[Chennai]], but dropped out before graduating due to disliking the theoretical nature of the course.<ref name=":3" />

Chake's second album ''Bombay Dreams'' was released in 2019. It features songs in English, Hindi and Nyishi, and revolves around themes of love, hope, unity and positivity.<ref name=":4" /> It is inspired by his move to [[Mumbai]] in 2018, and took him about a year and a half to record and release, with some songs featured (''Meri Dastan'' and ''This is the Day'') having taken eight years to develop.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":5">{{Cite web|last=Britto|first=David|title=Taba Chake Returns With Easygoing New Love Song ‘Blush’|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/arunachalese-fingerstyle-guitarist-taba-chake-kicks-off-lvng-tour-week/|url-status=live|website=Rolling Stone India}}</ref> ''Bombay Dreams'' received 12.2&nbsp;million streams across all platforms, and made it to No. 12 on [[Apple Music]]'s All Genre Charts.<ref name=":3" />

Chake released the single ''Blush'' in November 2020, conceived through listening to friends' stories about love and helped through his own emotions for a [[Long-distance relationship|long-distance]] lover who lived in Delhi while he was in Mumbai.<ref name=":3" /> ''Blush'' was produced by Ritwik De, of instrumental rock band Zokokva, who also worked on ''Bombay Dreams.''<ref name=":5" />

Chake was chosen by [[Spotify]] for their emerging artist program RADAR, which assists him in promotion of his music through their app.<ref name=":3" />
