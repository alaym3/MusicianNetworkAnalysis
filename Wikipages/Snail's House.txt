'''Keitaro Ujiie''' (born February 23, 1997), also known as '''Snail's House''' or '''Ujico*''',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://beta.otaquest.com/tokyo-underground-musical-movement/|title=The Sounds of Tokyo’s Hyper-Cute Underground Movement|date=March 19, 2017|author=Lachlan Johnston|website=otaquest.com|access-date=July 29, 2018}}</ref> is a Japanese [[Electronic music|electronic]] musician. He currently resides in [[Tokyo]], [[Japan]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://x.dmndr.com/introducing-ujico/|title=Tokyo Transmission: Introducing Ujico – DMNDR|website=x.dmndr.com|language=en-US|access-date=July 18, 2018}}</ref>

== History ==
He started publishing music on [[SoundCloud]] in 2012 under the name Ujico* (which he stated was a childhood nickname given to him by a friend).<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=http://beta.otaquest.com/discussions-001-ujico/|title=discussions.001 // Tokyo-Based Producer 'Ujico*/Snail's House' - OTAQUEST|date=May 17, 2017|work=OTAQUEST|access-date=July 18, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2013, Ujiie created his own [[record label]], Youth Composer Association, a Tokyo-based label specifically geared towards young musicians.<ref name=":0" /> In 2014, after listening to performances by [[Hiromi Uehara]] and various [[jazz fusion]] music,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/2019/04/03/snails-house-keitaro-ujiie-interview/|title=Coming Out Of His Shell: Chatting With Snail’s House Mastermind Keitaro Ujiie|last=Editorial|date=April 3, 2019|website=Bandcamp Daily|language=en|access-date=May 1, 2019}}</ref> Ujiie was inspired to start playing the [[piano]].<ref name=":1" /> 

From that point, his music career began to grow and he created a separate Snail's House outfit for his "kawaii" music, which officially began on August 30, 2014, with the release of "Nyan Nyan Angel!". His first two [[Extended play|EP]]s as Snail's House, ''Kirara'' and ''Kawaii Collective'', were released in mid-2015, containing high-pitched vocal chops, vibrant [[synthesizer]] sounds, and happy-sounding [[Melody|melodies]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/interest/2015-05-15/future-bass-music-gets-a-kawaii-makeover/.88173|title=Future Bass Music Gets a Kawaii Makeover|work=Anime News Network|access-date=September 17, 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://daily.bandcamp.com/2018/03/23/kawaii-japanese-electronic-feature/|title=Eight Kawaii Artists Using Grotesque Sounds to Redefine "Cute" Japanese Music|date=March 23, 2018|work=Bandcamp Daily|access-date=September 17, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=http://otaquest.com/snails-house-warp-star/|title=Snail's House Releases Infectious New Single 'Warp Star'|date=July 19, 2018|work=OTAQUEST|access-date=September 17, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

On October 10, 2017, the music video for the song 'Pixel Galaxy' was released onto Youtube, it quickly began to go viral and it gave Ujiie a large boost in followers, though the song has significantly less plays on all [[Streaming media|music streaming]] services, the music video now has more than 75 million views on Youtube.

On October 9, 2018, Ujiie announced that a small game was being developed for his upcoming two-track album ''Snailchan Adventure''.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/loudnessfete/status/1049762630422781952|title=Ujico*/Snail’s House on Twitter|website=Twitter|language=en|access-date=October 24, 2018}}</ref> The album was released on October 23 with an accompanying music video,<ref>{{Citation|last=Ujico*/Snail's House|title=Snail's House - Snailchan Adventure (Official MV)|date=October 23, 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=US3VTVDRbjE|access-date=October 24, 2018}}</ref> and Ujiie published a preview of the unfinished game.<ref name=":3" /> As of May 1, 2019, the game has not been released, with development presumed to be ongoing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.otaquest.com/snails-house-upcoming-game-snailchan-adventure-previewed/|title=Snail's House' Upcoming Game "Snailchan Adventure" Previewed|last=Johnston|first=Lachlan|date=January 25, 2019|website=OTAQUEST|language=en-US|access-date=May 1, 2019}}</ref>
