'''Jacob Banks''' (born 24 July 1991) is a Nigerian-born English singer and songwriter from [[Birmingham]].<ref name="Liverpool Sound City">{{cite web|url=http://www.liverpoolsoundcity.co.uk/profile.aspx/View/61|title=Jacob Banks |publisher=Liverpool Sound City|access-date=13 May 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131215235417/http://www.liverpoolsoundcity.co.uk/profile.aspx/View/61|archive-date=15 December 2013}}</ref> Banks won the 2012 [[MOBO]] UnSung regional competition in Birmingham<ref>{{cite web|last=Cofie|first=Bibi|title=MOBO UnSung 2012: Artist Catchup|url=http://www.mobo.com/news-blogs/mobo-unsung-2012-artists-catch|publisher=MOBO|access-date=9 May 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130808175205/http://www.mobo.com/news-blogs/mobo-unsung-2012-artists-catch|archive-date=8 August 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> and the [[Adidas]] "Are You In" music competition.<ref name="wonderlandmagazine">{{cite web|last=Hawkins|first=Shane|title=New Noise: Jacob Banks|url=http://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2013/03/new-noise-jacob-banks/|publisher=Wonderland|access-date=9 May 2013}}</ref> His music is influenced by a range of genres including [[Soul music|soul]], [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Morris|first=Matt|title=Jacob Banks – The Monologue (Full EP Stream)|url=http://hypetrak.com/2013/01/jacob-banks-the-monologue-full-ep-stream/|publisher=Hypetrak|access-date=9 May 2013}}</ref> Banks is currently signed with American label [[Interscope Records]].<ref name="Record Label">{{cite web|url=https://www.interscope.com/artists/jacob-banks |title=Jacob Banks |publisher=Interscope Records |date= n.d.|access-date=15 February 2019}}</ref>

==Music career==

===2012–present: Breakthrough===
<ref>{{cite web|last=Cochrane|first=Greg|title=BBC Newsbeat – Introducing – Jacob Banks|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/21954630|publisher=BBC|access-date=9 May 2013}}</ref> The second release from ''The Monologue'', titled "Worthy", was played by [[BBC Radio 1]] DJ [[Zane Lowe]] as his "Next Hype" record on his Radio 1 show and received additional plays on [[1Xtra]], [[XFM]], [[6Music]] and [[Annie Nightingale]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Collins|first=Hattie|title=i-N Session: Jacob Banks|url=http://i-donline.com/2013/02/i-n-session-jacob-banks/|publisher=i-D Magazine|access-date=9 May 2013|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130629115507/http://i-donline.com/2013/02/i-n-session-jacob-banks/|archive-date=29 June 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> In April 2013, Banks played his first headline show at the London [[St Pancras Old Church]].<ref name="MTV UK">{{cite web|last=Maz|first=Maz|title=Jacob Banks Announces Headline Show|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/music/urban/8887-jacob-banks-announces-headline-show|publisher=MTV The Wrap Up|access-date=9 May 2013}}</ref> He supported [[Emeli Sande]] on her April 2013 UK tour.<ref name="Hunger TV">{{cite web|last=Hunger TV|title=Jacob Banks: Worthy|url=http://www.hungertv.com/feature/jacob-banks-worthy/|work=Hunger Magazine|access-date=9 May 2013}}</ref> Banks features on [[Wretch 32]]'s song "[[Doing OK]]", the second single from Wretch's forthcoming third studio album. Banks features on [[Chase and Status]]' "Alive" from their album, ''Brand New Machine''. He also supported them, on their Brand New Machine 2013 UK tour. Banks announced he was to release a joint EP featuring himself, [[Wretch 32]] and George the Poet titled ''Gambling Man'' in 2013. However, this did not occur.<ref name="Live Magazine">{{cite web|last=Alya|first=Alya|title=Music: Jacob Banks Interview|url=http://www.live-magazine.co.uk/2013/01/music-jacob-banks-interview/|work=Live Magazine|access-date=9 May 2013}}</ref> On 1 May 2014, his collaboration with [[All About She]], "I Can Wait", was released for free download as part of their debut [[extended play]] ''Go Slow''. Banks has also worked with other artists such as [[Bondax]], [[Jake Gosling]], Knox Brown, [[Plan B (musician)|Plan B]] and [[Wretch 32]].<ref name="wonderlandmagazine" />

On 21 July 2015, Banks' second extended play entitled ''The Paradox'' was released. It features a guest appearance from [[Odd Child Recordings]] rapper Avelino on the track "Monster".

In 2016, Banks was featured on Norwegian production team [[Seeb (music producers)|Seeb]]'s release, "[[What Do You Love]]". The song reached number 2 on [[VG-lista]], the official Norwegian singles chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://norwegiancharts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Jacob+Banks|title=norwegiancharts.com – Discography Jacob Banks|first=Steffen|last=Hung|website=norwegiancharts.com}}</ref>

On 9 March 2017, Banks premiered his two-part narrative video, written and co-directed by himself, for his single, "Unholy War".<ref name="Vaunter">{{cite web|title=Jacob Banks shares his stunning two-part ‘Unholy War’ video|url=https://vaunter.co/music/urban/jacob-banks-unholy-war-video/0150510|publisher=Vaunter|access-date=9 March 2017}}</ref> The single is off his upcoming EP, 'The Boy Who Cried Freedom'.<ref name="Vaunter"/>
In November 2018, he released his new album, "Village", which made No. 1 on new and upcoming albums on Spotify.

==In popular culture==

Banks' song, "Monster 2.0" was featured in the [[Codemasters]] game, ''[[Dirt 4]]''.
[[File:Jacob Banks Piknik i Parken 2018 (195122).jpg|thumb|Jacob Banks at the Vigeland stage in the Vigeland Park, which took place on 17 June 2018 in Oslo.]]

"Move With You" was featured in the [[EA Sports]] game, ''[[FIFA 15]]''.

In 2013, "Worthy" was played on the American TV series ''Suits'' in season 3, episode 9.

"Unknown" was featured in the Season 3 finale of the Starz TV series ''[[Power (TV series)|Power]]'', and received viral attention for his tremendous voice. This attention led to the song "Monster" featuring in the first episode of season 4. In 2018, "Unknown" was featured during the TV Series ''[[Suits (U.S. TV series)|Suits]]'' in season 7, episode 13.

"Monster" was used during the TV series "Quantico" in Season 1 Episode 2 which aired in October 2015. "Monster" was also sampled to create rapper Flawless real talk's song of the same name.

In 2017, "Unholy War" was featured in the season finale of the TV series '' [[The Blacklist: Redemption|Redemption]]'', Season 3 Episode 3 of the TV Series ''[[Lucifer (TV series)|Lucifer]]'' and in the reveal trailer for the 2017 Need for Speed game, ''[[Need for Speed: Payback]].'' In 2018, "Unholy War" was featured during the season 1 finale of the TV series ''[[Black Lightning (TV series)|Black Lightning]]''. It was also featured on the Starz TV series ''Power''.

In 2018 Banks penned and performed the song "In The Name of Love" for the movie ''[[The Equalizer 2]]''.

"Love Ain't Enough" was used in the [[EA Sports]] game, ''[[FIFA 19]]''.

Banks' collaboration with [[X Ambassadors]], "Baptize Me" was featured in the '[[Game of Thrones]]' soundtrack.

In 2018, "Unholy War" was used in the TV series "Queen of the South" in season 3, episode 5.

In 2019, "Every Age" was featured in a commercial for the gum brand [[Extra (gum)|Extra]].

In 2020, "Slow Up" was used in the second season of the British series "Save Me".

In 2021, "Slow Up" was used in the Netflix film ''[[Fatherhood (film)|Fatherhood]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|last=Weston|first=Christopher|date=2021-06-18|title=Fatherhood soundtrack: What songs feature in the 2021 Netflix movie?|url=https://www.hitc.com/en-gb/2021/06/18/fatherhood-soundtrack/|access-date=2021-06-27|website=HITC|language=en-GB}}</ref> "Found " was featured in the final season of Lucifer, and "Something Beautiful" was featured in feature film, ''[[Tom_&_Jerry_(2021_film)|Tom & Jerry]]''.
