'''The Wytches''' are an English rock band formed in 2011 in [[Peterborough]]. Following their formation, the band were based in [[Brighton]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/may/01/the-wytches|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|title=New Band of the Day: The Wytches|date=1 May 2013|accessdate= 1 January 2014  }}</ref> The band consists of singer-songwriter Kristian Bell, bassist Daniel Rumsey and keyboardist/guitarist Mark Breed. Drummer Gianni Honey left the band in 2019.

== History ==
Kristian Bell and Gianni Honey had previously played together in the Crooked Canes in their hometown of Peterborough, whom the duo later dismissed as being "really adolescent and embarrassing".<ref name="nme_radar" /> Honey was taught by former Morrissey drummer Spike T. Smith and played in local bands, Through Fire This Fall,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://27_black.webs.com/theband.htm |title=Through Fire This Fall :: 5-piece ALTERNATIVE-ROCK band |date=2014-01-10 |access-date=2016-10-11 |url-status=bot: unknown |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110182243/http://27_black.webs.com/theband.htm |archivedate=10 January 2014 }}</ref> the [[Joy Division]] influenced the Proles<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/theprolesband/info|title=The Proles - About - Facebook|website=Facebook.com}}</ref> and [[metalcore]] band Khalo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.peterboroughtoday.co.uk/news/features/news-features/download-this-khalo-1-72918|title=Download This: Khalo|last=|first=|date=9 February 2009|website=Peterboroughtoday.co.uk|access-date=}}</ref>

Following the split of the Crooked Canes, the duo moved to Brighton to attend university and advertised around campus for a bassist. Daniel Rumsey was the only person to respond and joined the band. Rumsey was a singer-songwriter originally from [[Dorset]] who had fronted the "horror punk" band Fall Victim before moving to Brighton where he fronted Dan Rumsey & the Bitter End<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.reverbnation.com/playlist/view_playlist/-4?page_object=artist_1306173#!/danrumseythebitterend|title=Dan Rumsey & The Bitter End's Songs|website=Reverbnation.com}}</ref> and the Voyage Andromeda.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thevoyageandromeda.com/about-the-crew |title=About The Crew |date=2014-01-10 |access-date=2016-10-11 |url-status=bot: unknown |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110232039/http://www.thevoyageandromeda.com/about-the-crew |archivedate=10 January 2014 }}</ref> Initially called the Witches, the band changed the spelling to Wytches to make their band name more easily found on [[Google]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.7bitarcade.com/music/article/interview-the-wytches/ |title=Interview: The Wytches » 7Bit Arcade |last=Day |first=Natt |date=28 June 2013 |website=7bitarcade.com |access-date=2016-10-11 |url-status=bot: unknown |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140110181404/http://www.7bitarcade.com/music/article/interview-the-wytches/ |archivedate=10 January 2014 }}</ref> The band originally featured second guitarist Mark Breed, with whom Bell later started a side project with called the Mark and Kristian Band. The band released their debut single ''Beehive Queen'' in June 2013 via Hate Hate Hate Records<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rockfeedback.com/concerts/detail/the-wytches-sailor-jerry-presents-show|title=The Wytches (Sailor Jerry Presents show) - RFB|website=Rockfeedback.com}}</ref> and follow up single ''Robe For Juda'' in November 2013.<ref name="nme_radar">{{cite web|url= https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-blogs/radar-band-of-the-week-the-wytches|title=Radar Band Of The Week - The Wytches|publisher=[[New Musical Express]]|accessdate= 1 January 2014}}</ref> In September 2013 the band released a low key limited edition cassette ''Thunder Lizard Revisited''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.contactmusic.com/press/the-wytches-announce-the-release-of-thunder-lizard-revisited-ep-on-limited-cassette|title=The Wytches Announce The Release Of 'Thunder Lizard Revisited' Ep On Limited Cassette|date=20 September 2013|website=Contactmusic.com}}</ref> The band released their debut US single ''Crying Clown'' (originally the b-side to the band's debut UK single) via [[Fat Possum Records]] in November 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fatpossum.com/news/358|title=American Wrestlers Share "Hello, Dear", Now Premiering via DIY – Announce More Tour Dates|website=Fatpossum.com}}</ref>

On 4 February 2014, the band announced they had signed with [[Heavenly Recordings]] and released ''Gravedweller'' as a free digital single.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thewytches.com/post/75580372382/heavenly-recordings |title={{!}} Heavenly Recordings |date=2014-02-22 |access-date=2016-10-11 |url-status=bot: unknown |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140222043554/http://www.thewytches.com/post/75580372382/heavenly-recordings |archivedate=22 February 2014 }}</ref> The band also signed to [[Partisan Records]] in the US.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2014/02/uk_band_the_wyt.html|title=UK band The Wytches sign to Partisan, readying debut LP, playing SXSW (dates, streams)|website=Brooklynvegan.com}}</ref>

The band released their debut album ''[[Annabel Dream Reader]]'' in August 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19572-the-wytches-annabel-dream-reader/|title=The Wytches: Annabel Dream Reader Album Review|last=Heller|first=Jason|date=27 August 2014|website=Pitchfork.com|access-date=2016-10-11}}</ref> The album was recorded over two days at [[Toe Rag Studios]] in [[Hackney, London]] and co-produced by Bell and former [[The Coral|Coral]] guitarist [[Bill Ryder-Jones]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.partisanrecords.com/artists/the-wytches/|title=The Wytches – Partisan Records|access-date=4 May 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140505015853/http://www.partisanrecords.com/artists/the-wytches/|archive-date=5 May 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album was recorded and mixed by Luke Oldfield on an 8-track [[Studer]] A80.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/tilehousestudios/posts/10100765846165551|title=Tilehouse Studios - Timeline - Facebook|website=facebook.com}}</ref> It reached number 50 in the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/30139/wytches/|title=WYTCHES {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|website=Officialcharts.com|access-date=2016-10-11}}</ref> 

In August 2016, Mark Breed re-joined the band as a permanent member. In September 2016 they released their second album ''All Your Happy Life''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2016/09/30/the-wytches-all-your-happy-life-album-review|title=The Wytches - All Your Happy Life|website=Diymag.com|access-date=2016-10-11}}</ref> 

In 2018, the band parted ways with their UK record label Heavenly Recordings and US record label Partisan, leaving them unsigned.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://partisanrecords.com/artists|title=Artists ← Partisan Records|website=Partisanrecords.com}}</ref> In March 2019, the band pulled out of a planned UK support tour with [[Drenge (band)|Drenge]] plus several warm up dates, citing "unforeseen circumstances".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/ddrreennggee/videos/vb.131250840275995/636305213486018/?type=2&theater|title=STRANGE CREATURES UK TOUR|website=Facebook.com}}</ref> In October 2019, the band played 3 gigs as a 3-piece, with Gianni Honey replaced by Demelza Mather.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://counteract.co/news/wytches-actress-bishop-birmingham-tickets/|title=The Wytches announce return with intimate Birmingham gig|first=Richard|last=Franks|website=Counteract.co|date=13 September 2019}}</ref>

In May 2020 Daniel Rumsey released an EP called ''The Darkest Day'' from a new side project called Dan Rumsey & the Dark Days.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-05-15|title=Dan Rumsey & The Dark Days - The Darkest Day (Self-Released)|url=http://www.godisinthetvzine.co.uk/2020/05/15/dan-rumsey-the-dark-days-the-darkest-day-self-released/|access-date=2020-06-25|website=God Is In The TV|language=en-US}}</ref>

On 10 June 2020, the band released new single ''Cowboy'' via their own label ''Cable Code Records''. The band confirmed that Gianni Honey had left the band, revealing that the band "were disheartened by the whole thing. All the work that goes into getting a band off its feet and into the public felt like something we’d have to try all over again". Session drummer Demelza Mather had toured with the band, but hasn't been announced as an official member of the band.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/the-wytches-blaze-a-trail-on-new-single-cowboy|title=The Wytches Blaze a Trail on New Single 'Cowboy'}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bimm.ac.uk/stories/success/demelza-mather/|title=Demelza Mather - Alumni}}</ref>
