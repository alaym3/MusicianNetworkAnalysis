'''1814''' is a [[reggae]] band from [[New Zealand]].

==History==
The band began in 2004 as a three-piece act formed by Patu Colbert and his sons Shaun and Jimmy. Five other members joined the band over the next five years as the original members felt they needed more musicians to produce the sound they wanted.<ref name=niceup /> The band's lead singer is Darren Katene.<ref>[http://www.radionz.co.nz/national/programmes/teahikaa/20100516 Radio New Zealand National : Programmes A-Z : Te Ahi Kaa : 2010-05-16]</ref>

The name 1814 was taken from the year that missionary [[Samuel Marsden]] held the first sermon in the [[Bay of Islands]].<ref name=niceup /> The band have played alongside several Kiwi favourites including [[Ardijah]], [[Katchafire]], [[Cornerstone Roots]], [[Unity Pacific]], [[Che Fu]], and [[Moana and the Moahunters|Moana and the Tribe]] and many more<ref>[http://www.muzic.net.nz/artists/2421release28 11.html 1814 - New Zealand Musicians and Bands] ''muzic.nz.net''</ref>

Their first album, ''Jah Rydem'', was released in 2008.<ref name=niceup>[http://www.niceup.org.nz/news/1814 1814 - Reggae runnings in Aotearoa, New Zealand] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100521090646/https://www.niceup.org.nz/news/1814 |date=21 May 2010 }} ''niceup.org.nz''</ref> It was produced by Wiremu Karaitiana and recorded at the Muscle Music Studios in the north of New Zealand.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.amplifier.co.nz/release/40339/jah-rydem.html |title=Jah Rydem |website=Amplifier NZ Music |access-date=24 April 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100419164855/http://www.amplifier.co.nz/release/40339/jah-rydem.html |archive-date=19 April 2010 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> With positive reviews.<ref>[http://www.niceup.org.nz/reviews/1814-jah-rydem 1814 - Jah Rydem - Reggae runnings in Aotearoa, New Zealand] ''niceup.org.nz''</ref> In 2011 ''The Covers Album'' was released in New Zealand by producer Hayden Taylor of Yorkstreet Recording Studios and mastered by Chris Winchcombe.

The band's second album, ''Relax'', was released in 2012. A new feel and direction for the summer reggae sounds like "Sunshine", written by saxophonist Chris Pierce and "Unite" cowritten by female vocalist Kalani Marsters sung by Neihana Mackey Harrison. The Relax album was produced and mastered by band founder Patu Colbert and producer Hayden Taylor at Yorkstreet Recording Studios in Auckland.
