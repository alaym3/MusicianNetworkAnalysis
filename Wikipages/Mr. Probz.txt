'''Dennis Princewell Stehr''' (born 15 May 1984),<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.metronieuws.nl/showbizz/2014/10/mr-probz-ik-heb-niet-het-makkelijkste-leven-gehad|title=Mr. Probz: &#039;Ik heb niet het makkelijkste leven gehad&#039;|newspaper=Metronieuws|language=nl|access-date=2016-10-24}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Mr. Probz''', is a Dutch rapper, singer, songwriter, actor and record producer.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://www.statemagazine.nl/forum.php/article?data%5Barticleid%5D=1589|title=Mr. Probz zit vast|last=Kerkhof|first=Sander|date=29 September 2006|publisher=Statemagazine.nl|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110724170742/http://www.statemagazine.nl/forum.php/article?data%5Barticleid%5D=1589|archive-date=July 24, 2011|access-date=2010-11-04}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5895217/mr-probz/biography|title=Mr. Probz - Biography {{!}} Billboard|last=Jeffries|first=David|website=www.billboard.com|publisher=Rovi|access-date=2016-10-24}}</ref> In 2013, he released the song "[[Waves (Mr. Probz song)|Waves]]", which was remixed in 2014 by [[Robin Schulz]], becoming an international hit. He has released one album and featured in the film ''[[Bolletjes Blues]]''.<ref name=":0" />

==Personal life==
Mr. Probz was born to parents of [[Danish people|Danish]] and [[Curaçao]]an descent. His musical career started through graffiti, expanding to rap.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Parkin |first1=Chris |title=Need to know: Mr Probz |url=https://www.redbull.com/int-en/need-to-know-mr-probz |website=[[Red Bull]] Music |access-date=6 April 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
===2010–13: ''The Treatment''===
{{Main|The Treatment (Mr. Probz album)}}
In 2010, Mr. Probz was nominated for a BNN/State Award in the category "Best Artist". In 2011, Mr. Probz had a Dutch single, "Meisje luister", in collaboration with Kleine Viezerik. It reached #1 on the FunX Hip Hop Chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=FunX HipHop Top 20 Week 46|url=http://www.funx.nl/index.php/charts/hiphop-chart}}</ref> On 16 September 2013, Mr. Probz released his debut album ''The Treatment'' for free through SoundCloud.<ref>[https://soundcloud.com/mrprobz/sets/mr-probz-the-treatment "Mr. Probz - The Treatment by Mr. Probz on SoundCloud"], 16 September 2013. Retrieved on 16 September 2013.</ref> He also released the album on [[iTunes]].<ref>[https://itunes.apple.com/nl/album/the-treatment/id711930018 iTunes - Muziek - 'The Treatment' van Mr. Probz]</ref> The album peaked to number 12 on the [[Dutch Albums Chart]]. The album includes the singles "I'm Right Here", "Turning Tables" and "Gold Days".

===2013–present: "Waves" and success===
In November 2013, he released the single "[[Waves (Mr. Probz song)|Waves]]", becoming a top 10 hit in the Netherlands. The song also charted in [[Australia]], [[Belgium]] and [[New Zealand]]. The song was remixed by German DJ and producer [[Robin Schulz]] and was released on 4 February 2014 in North America and 7 February 2014 in Europe. The remix propelled the song to greater success, topping the charts in [[Austria]], [[Germany]], [[Norway]], [[Sweden]], [[Switzerland]] and the [[United Kingdom]], and peaking within the top 10 of the charts in [[Denmark]], [[Finland]], [[Hungary]], [[Ireland]], [[Italy]], [[France]] and the [[United States]]. He featured on [[50 Cent]]'s single "[[Twisted (50 Cent song)|Twisted]]", which was released in May 2014. 

His follow-up to "Waves", "[[Nothing Really Matters (Mr. Probz song)|Nothing Really Matters]]", was released in September 2014 to critical acclaim.<ref name=nothing>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/nothing-really-matters-single/id922865280|title=Nothing Really Matters - single|publisher=iTunes Australia|access-date=3 October 2014}}</ref> He featured on [[Professor Green]]'s single "[[Little Secrets (Professor Green song)|Little Secrets]]", which was released in December 2014. In 2014, Mr Probz collaborated with Dutch DJ [[Hardwell]] on a song titled "Birds Fly", which was included on Hardwell's debut album ''[[United We Are]]'', released in January 2015.

In August 2017, Mr. Probz released the single "Gone" featuring [[Anderson .Paak]]

On March 9, 2018, Mr. Probz released the single and music video called "Space for Two".
