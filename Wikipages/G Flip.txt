'''Georgia Claire Flipo''' (born 22 September 1994), known professionally as '''G Flip''', is an Australian singer, songwriter, producer, drummer, and musician from [[Melbourne]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/say-gday-to-g-flip/9462840/ |title=Meet G Flip, the drummer-turned-solo-star who blew up Unearthed |publisher=[[Triple J]] |date=20 February 2018 |access-date=1 October 2020 |author=Byrne, Declan}}</ref> They released their debut studio album, ''[[About Us (album)|About Us]]'', on 30 August 2019.

==Early life and career==
Flipo has been playing drums since the age of nine, and percussion still forms a big part of their songwriting process despite now being a multi-instrumentalist.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pedestrian.tv/music/g-flip-queer-fans/ |title=How G Flip's Real, Raw And Relatable Lyrics Ended Up Winning Her A Legion Of Queer Fans |work=[[Pedestrian.TV]] |date=20 December 2020 |access-date=10 January 2021 |author=Crellin, Zac}}</ref>

They said that they "spent most of 2017 holed up in their bedroom writing and recording" <ref name="junkee146704" /> having previously performed in various different Melbourne music acts as a session drummer and occasional backup vocalist,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/say-gday-to-g-flip/9462840|title=Say G'Day To G Flip |publisher=[[Triple J]] |date=20 February 2018 }}</ref> including for the Melbourne band Empra.<ref name="BrooklynVegan" />

In February 2018 they uploaded their debut single "About You" to [[Triple J Unearthed]] and that same day their track was premiered on [[Triple J]].<ref name="junkee146704">{{cite web|url=https://junkee.com/g-flip-about-you/146704|title=Let Us Introduce You To The Song Australia Is Currently Losing Its Shit Over |website=Junkee |date=14 February 2018 }}</ref> The song was officially released on 2 March 2018. [[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] named it "Best New Track" <ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/g-flip-about-you/|title= G Flip - About You Over |work=Pitchfork }}</ref> and the music video, filmed on their iPhone, was featured in [[YouTube]]'s New Music Playlist.<ref name="BrooklynVegan">{{cite web|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/who-is-g-flip-her-song-about-you-is-catchy-pop-and-shes-playing-sxsw/|title=Who is G Flip? Their Song 'About You' Is Catchy Pop And They're Playing SXSW  |website=Brooklyn Vegan }}</ref> Pining international interest in their project, Flipo was subsequently invited to play their first ever solo show at [[SXSW]] in [[Austin, Texas]] the following month.<ref name="BrooklynVegan" /> Flipo's follow up track "Killing My Time" was released in May 2018.

In November 2018, Flipo was nominated for a [[J Award]] as Unearthed Artist of the Year <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.triplejunearthed.com/article/meet-your-2018-j-award-nominees-unearthed-artist-year|title=Meet Your 2018 J Award Nominees  |website=Triple J }}</ref> and was the inaugural winner of the [[ARIA]] Emerging Artist Scholarship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.heraldsun.com.au/lifestyle/melbourne/muso-g-flip-wins-aria-emerging-artist-scholarship/news-story/2d038e3aabda3f0f041f3aa8ed073d50|access-date=21 May 2019 |title=G Flip Wins ARIA Emerging Artist Scholarship |work=Herald Sun |date=7 December 2018}}</ref>

In December 2018, Flipo performed "Proud Mary" and "[[Blame It on the Boogie]]" at the [[Australian Broadcasting Corporation|ABC]]'s "The Night is Yours" New Year's Eve Celebration 2018 at the [[Sydney Opera House]],<ref>{{Citation|last=ABC TV & iview|title=The Night is Yours: G Flip - Proud Mary|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PYBi3S9ht4c|access-date=2019-01-02}}</ref> and their song "About You" was featured in Season 2, Episode 10 of ''[[The Bold Type]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://freeform.go.com/shows/the-bold-type/news/the-bold-type-official-season-2-music-guide|title=The Bold Type Official Season 2 Music Guide |website=Freeform |access-date=2019-01-02}}</ref>

In January 2019, "About You" polled at number 38 and "Killing My Time" at number 62 in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2018]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/18/countdown/1-100/|title=Hottest 100 Countdown 2018  |publisher=[[Triple J]] |date=11 November 2018 }}</ref>

In July 2019, G Flip won Breakthrough Independent Artist of the Year at the [[Australian Independent Record Labels Association]] (AIR) Awards.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.noise11.com/news/courtney-barnett-and-gurrumul-share-best-independent-album-or-ep-air-award-20190726 |title=Courtney Barnett and Gurrumul share Best Independent Album or EP AIR Award |work=Noise11 |date=26 July 2019|access-date=26 July 2019}}</ref> Their debut studio album was released on 30 August 2019.

Flipo worked with [[Ariel Rechtshaid]] on their track "I Am Not Afraid". Rechtshaid noted to [[Rolling Stone]] that "G Flip is a phenomenal musician... [They're] a strong songwriter and [have] a very authentic voice, and [they] are a badass producer.” <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/g-flip-australia-artist-you-need-to-know-934412/|title= How G Flip Used Pop Music to Conquer Their Fears| website=[[Rolling Stone]] |date= 10 January 2020|access-date=20 January 2020 }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Flipo is [[coming out|openly]] [[lesbian]].<ref name="I">{{cite web |url=https://www.intomore.com/culture/g-flip-is-australias-latest-musical-export |title=G Flip is Australia's latest Musical export |work=Intomore |date=14 December 2018 |access-date=16 December 2019 }}</ref> They attended [[primary school]] at Our Lady of the Assumption Primary School (OLA) in [[Cheltenham, Victoria|Cheltenham]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]] and [[secondary school]] at [[Star of the Sea College]] in [[Brighton, Victoria|Brighton]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]], graduating in 2011.<ref name="I"/>

On 13 June 2021, Flipo came out as [[non-binary gender|non-binary]] on [[Instagram]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web |url=https://www.pedestrian.tv/entertainment/g-flip-non-binary/ |title=Aussie music legend G Flip has come out as non-binary & we're so flipping happy for them |work=[[Pedestrian.TV]] |date=13 June 2021 |access-date=14 June 2021 |author=Morrison-Thiagu, Saskia |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210614010211/https://www.pedestrian.tv/entertainment/g-flip-non-binary/ |archive-date=14 June 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> They use they/them pronouns.<ref name=":0" />
