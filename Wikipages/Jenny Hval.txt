'''Jenny Hval''' (born 11 July 1980) is a Norwegian singer-songwriter, record producer, musician, and novelist. She has released seven solo albums, two under the alias '''Rockettothesky''', and five under her own name.

In 2015, Hval released her fifth studio album, ''[[Apocalypse, Girl]]'', to widespread critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/20638-apocalypse-girl/ |title=Album Review |publisher=pitchfork.com |access-date=2021-02-16 }}</ref> The following year, she released ''[[Blood Bitch]]'', a [[concept album]] influenced by [[vampires]], [[menstruation]] and 1970s [[horror films]].

== Music career ==
Until 1999, Hval was the vocalist of a gothic metal band called Shellyz Raven.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://jennyhval.com/music/other-projects/old-stuff |title=Jenny Hval Website |publisher=JennyHval.com |access-date=2014-12-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141226164606/http://jennyhval.com/music/other-projects/old-stuff |archive-date=2014-12-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She studied at the [[University of Melbourne]], [[Australia]], specializing in creative writing and performance. While studying, she was vocalist in Australian bands ''iPanic'' and ''Folding For Air'' with Thomas McGowan releasing an EP "Are you afraid of heights?" in 2004.

Moving back to Norway, she released her debut EP ''Cigars'' in 2006, and was nominated for a [[Spellemannprisen]] (the Norwegian equivalent of [[Grammy awards]]) in the "best newcomer" category. Adopting the name ''Rockettothesky'', she was signed to Trust Me Records, releasing two studio albums – ''To Sing You Apple Trees'' in 2006, and ''Medea'' in 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://thequietus.com/articles/03523-jenny-and-the-jets-the-musical-universe-of-rockettothesky |title=Jenny And The Jets: The Musical Universe of Rockettothesky |first=Wyndham |last=Wallace |publisher=TheQuietus.com |date=2010-01-12 |access-date=2014-12-26}}</ref>

Following the release of ''Medea'', she went back to using her birth name. Signing on the label Rune Grammofon, she released the albums ''[[Viscera (Jenny Hval album)|Viscera]]'' (2011)<ref name="The Guardian – 18Apr2013 – Singer Jenny Hval: 'I'm so cruel'">{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/apr/18/singer-jenny-hval |title=Singer Jenny Hval: 'I'm so cruel' |last=Beaumont-Thomas |first=Ben |date=2013-04-18 |work=[[The Guardian]] |publisher=[[Guardian Media Group]] |access-date=2013-05-05}}</ref> and ''[[Innocence Is Kinky]]'' (2013). In 2015 she released her third album, ''[[Apocalypse, Girl]]'' on New York City's [[Sacred Bones Records]]. Her solo music has been described as [[avant-garde music|avant-garde]],<ref name="roll_Jenn">{{Cite web | title = Jenny Hval 'Apocalypse, girl' Album Review | last = Mapes | first = Jillian | work = Rolling Stone | date = 12 June 2015 | access-date = 2017-01-09 | url = https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/jenny-hval-apocalypse-girl-20150612 }}</ref> [[art-pop]],<ref name="theg_Jenn">{{Cite web | title = Jenny Hval: Apocalypse, Girl review – provocative, compelling art-pop | last = Hutchinson | first = Kate | work = the Guardian | date = 4 June 2015 | access-date = 2017-01-09 | url = https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/jun/04/jenny-hval-apocalypse-girl-review-provocative-compelling-art-pop }}</ref> and "a kind of [[experimental folk]] music,"<ref name="newy_Jenn">{{Cite web | title = Jenny Hval's Experimental, Feminist Folk Music | last = Crawford | first = Anwen | work = The New Yorker | date = 22 June 2015 | access-date = 2017-01-09 | url = http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2015/06/22/soft-apocalypse }}</ref> among other terms. She has also collaborated with Håvard Volden, as ''Nude on Sand'', releasing a self-titled album in 2012, and with [[Susanna Wallumrød]], which resulted in a 2014 album ''Meshes of Voice'' (released on Wallumrød's ''SusannaSonata'' label). She supported [[St. Vincent (musician)|St. Vincent]] on a tour.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2015-02-25|title=Jenny Hval Announces New Album Apocalypse, girl|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/58611-jenny-hval-announces-new-album-apocalypse-girl/|access-date=2021-10-27|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2016 she released the album ''[[Blood Bitch]]'' where she collaborates with [[Lasse Marhaug]].<ref name="pitc_Jenn">{{Cite web | title = Jenny Hval: Blood Bitch Album Review | last = Pelly | first = Jenn | work = pitchfork.com | date = 4 October 2016 | access-date = 2017-01-09 | url = http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/22314-blood-bitch/ }}</ref> The album was awarded the [[Nordic Music Prize|Phonofile Nordic Music Prize]] in 2017. The judges released a statement calling the album "engrossing, atmospheric, challenging and thought-provoking."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/72011-jenny-hval-wins-2017-nordic-music-prize/|title=Jenny Hval Wins 2017 Nordic Music Prize|website=Pitchfork|language=en|access-date=2019-03-11}}</ref>

In 2018 she collaborated with Håvard Volden again under the name Lost Girls, to release an EP entitled ''Feeling''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/lost-girls-feeling-ep/|title=Lost Girls: ''Feeling EP''|date=8 March 2018|access-date=28 December 2018|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Jenn|last=Pelly}}</ref> The duo's name was inspired by the eponymous [[Lost Girls (graphic novel)|graphic novel]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-02-05|title=Jenny Hval and Håvard Volden announce new collaboration Lost Girls|url=https://www.factmag.com/2018/02/05/jenny-hval-havard-volden-new-collaboration-lost-girls/|access-date=2021-10-26|website=Fact Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> 

In September 2019, Hval released her seventh album, ''[[The Practice of Love (album)|The Practice of Love]]'', featuring guest vocals from Vivian Wang, formerly of the psych rock band [[The Observatory (band)|The Observatory]], Australian singer-songwriter [[Laura Jean]], and French experimental musician [[Félicia Atkinson]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/features/moodboard/jenny-hval-the-practice-of-love-interview/ |title=How Dried Figs, Norwegian Coffee, and Rotting Porn Magazines Inspired Jenny Hval's ''The Practice of Love'' |last=Pelly |first=Jenn |date=16 September 2019 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=22 September 2019}}</ref> Hval had intended a tour to promote the album,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2019-10-28|title=Jenny Hval Announces 2020 Tour|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/jenny-hval-announces-2020-tour/|access-date=2021-10-17|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref> but cancelled the North American shows due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jenny Hval on Instagram: “Today I am postponing our upcoming North American tour. I’m really sorry to have to do this, but of course, there is no other way. The…”|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B9ruh0RJUQL/|access-date=2021-10-17|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref>

In 2021, Lost Girls released their first full-length LP, ''[[Menneskekollektivet]].'' The album was received to "universal acclaim," according to aggregate review website Metacritic.<ref>{{Citation|title=Menneskekollektivet by Lost Girls|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/menneskekollektivet/lost-girls|language=en|access-date=2021-10-17}}</ref>

== Style and influences ==
Hval was influenced by the androgyny in 1980s pop music. In particular, she was inspired by the music video for [[Kate Bush|Kate Bush's]] song ''[[Cloudbusting]],'' which features Bush as a young boy; Hval has expressed her admiration for the English musician's ability to write from many different perspectives.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-06-09|title=Confronting: Jenny Hval - 'Everyday Life Through A Pedalboard Of Effects'|url=https://nbhap.com/people/confronting-jenny-hval|access-date=2021-10-26|website=NBHAP|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2013-04-18|title=Singer Jenny Hval: 'I'm so cruel'|url=http://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/apr/18/singer-jenny-hval|access-date=2021-10-26|website=the Guardian|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=We ran willingly, horse-like, girl-like, boy-like. Her voice neighing in the back of her throat, and when I came closer we collided and kissed in the passing, on the mouth, like horses do. I said, her thin lips over enamel and steel. I felt the outline of her braces against my own, little silver arms reaching for each other|url=https://genius.com/6848959/Jenny-hval-sabbath/We-ran-willingly-horse-like-girl-like-boy-like-her-voice-neighing-in-the-back-of-her-throat-and-when-i-came-closer-we-collided-and-kissed-in-the-passing-on-the-mouth-like-horses-do-i-said-her-thin-lips-over-enamel-and-steel-i-felt-the-outline-of-her-braces-against-my-own-little-silver-arms-reaching-for-each-other|access-date=2021-10-26|website=Genius}}</ref> In her Master's thesis, "The Singing Voice as Literature," Hval explored the [[sound poetry]] of Kate Bush's music, analysing the [[lyrics]] and music together, instead of as separate parts of a song.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jenny Hval & Lawrence Kumpf {{!}} ISSUE Project Room|url=https://issueprojectroom.org/news/jenny-hval-lawrence-kumpf|access-date=2021-10-26|website=issueprojectroom.org}}</ref>

Hval's spoken-word delivery has been compared to [[Laurie Anderson|Laurie Anderson's]] music.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lost Girls Menneskekollektivet|url=https://www.newyorker.com/goings-on-about-town/night-life/lost-girls-menneskekollektivet|access-date=2021-10-27|website=The New Yorker|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Jenny Hval|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/9126-jenny-hval/|access-date=2021-10-27|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref>

== Writer ==
After studying literature and working as a freelance columnist and writer, Jenny Hval published her novel ''Perlebryggeriet'' (Pearl Brewery) in 2009. An English edition, titled ''Paradise Rot: A Novel'', was translated by Marjam Idriss and published by [[Verso Books]] in October 2018.<ref>{{Cite book|title=Paradise rot : a novella|last=1980–|first=Hval, Jenny|others=Idriss, Marjam|isbn=9781786633835|edition= English-language |location=London|oclc=1043955944}}</ref> Her second work, ''Inn i ansiktet'' (Into the Face), was published in Norway in October 2012.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Hval|first=Jenny|title=Inn i ansiktet|publisher=Forlaget Oktober|year=2012|isbn=9788249510559|location=Norway}}</ref> Hval published her third novel, ''Å hate Gud'' (To Hate God), in 2018, which also received a translation into English by Marjam Idriss and was published by Verso Books under the title ''Girls Against God'' in October 2020.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Hval|first=Jenny|title=Girls Against God|publisher=Verso Books|year=2020|isbn=9781788738958}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hval|first=Jenny|title=more work – Jenny Hval|url=http://jennyhval.com/more-work|access-date=6 October 2020|website=Jenny Hval}}</ref>
