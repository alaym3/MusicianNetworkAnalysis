'''Jordan Edward Benjamin'''<ref name="Collegian">{{cite news | last=Molloy| first=Alexandra| title =Let's talk about Grandson, and no, I'm not talking about your grandkid| newspaper =The Daily Collegian| date =October 15, 2018 | url =https://dailycollegian.com/2018/10/lets-talk-about-grandson-and-no-im-not-talking-about-your-grandkid/| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Weekend">{{cite news | last=Kernan| first=Patrick| title =Young the Giant, other alt rock acts to play show at Kirby Center in January| newspaper =The Weekender| date =October 23, 2018 | url =https://www.theweekender.com/insidestories/31222/young-the-giant-other-alt-rock-acts-to-play-show-at-kirby-center-in-january| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> (born October 25, 1993), known professionally as '''Grandson''' (stylized as '''grandson'''),<ref name="Lupi">{{cite news | title =The musician, the movement: grandson| publisher =Lupinore| date =August 4, 2017 | url =https://www.lupinore.com/city-life/2017/8/3/the-musician-the-movement-grandson| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> is a Canadian-American singer, songwriter, and musician currently signed to [[Fueled by Ramen]]. He released his major label debut EP, ''A Modern Tragedy Vol. 1'', on June 15, 2018, and released the follow-up  ''A Modern Tragedy Vol. 2'' on February 22, 2019. The initial EP featured the single "Blood // Water", which appeared on several ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard charts|charts]] in the United States and Canada. Benjamin's music focuses on modern-day issues that are less recognized by the media and public. On December 4, 2020, he released his debut studio album, ''[[Death of an Optimist]].''

==Early life and education==
Benjamin was born to a Jewish family<ref>{{Cite web|title=Generation X: Inside grandson’s fight for a new alternative|url=https://www.kerrang.com/features/generation-x-inside-grandsons-fight-for-a-new-alternative/|access-date=2021-07-23|website=Kerrang!}}</ref> in [[New Jersey]], [[United States]], but when he was 3 years old his family moved to [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]], where his maternal relatives lived<ref>{{Cite web|last=Taylor·Interviews·September 13|first=Jamie|last2=2018·|date=2018-09-13|title=Interviews {{!}} A Chat With grandson|url=https://www.concertaddicts.com/interviews/a-chat-with-grandson-by-bri-elizibeth|access-date=2021-07-23|website=Concert Addicts|language=en-US}}</ref> (he holds [[dual citizenship]] of both countries).<ref name="BillFuel"/><ref name="Charlatan"/> He has two sisters.<ref name="KROQ">{{cite news | last=Holiday| first=Megan| title =Grandson Talks Origins + How His Song "Overdose" Helped Solidify What He Does| publisher =KROQ| date = October 29, 2018| url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8285184/grandson-signs-with-fueled-by-ramen-following-his-powerful-post| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> Benjamin grew up largely in the [[Eglinton West]] area of [[Toronto]].<ref name="BillFuel">{{cite magazine | last=Newman| first=Melinda| title =Grandson Signs With Fueled By Ramen Following His Powerful Post-Parkland Theme, 'Thoughts & Prayers'| magazine =Billboard| date = April 4, 2018| url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8285184/grandson-signs-with-fueled-by-ramen-following-his-powerful-post| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Charlatan">{{cite news | last=Pelletier| first=Jeff| title =Grandson turns rage into inclusive blend of rock| newspaper =The Charlatan| date =February 14, 2019 | url =http://charlatan.ca/2019/02/grandson-turns-rage-into-inclusive-blend-of-rock/| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Varsity2017">{{cite news | last=Moshenski-Dubov| first=George| title =Grandson goes beyond the melody| newspaper =The Varsity| date = July 13, 2017| url =https://thevarsity.ca/2017/07/13/torontos-grandson-on-going-beyond-the-melody/| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> He attended [[Northern Secondary School (Toronto)|Northern Secondary School]] in Toronto and, after graduation, enrolled in [[McGill University]] in [[Montreal]]. There, he studied education with the initial goal of becoming a teacher.<ref name="Charlatan"/><ref name="Bull">{{cite news | last=Butz-Weidner| first=Benjamin| title =Meet Grandson: The McGill Drop-Out Making it in the Music Business, Involved with the Recent Naked Trump Statues| newspaper =The Bull & Bear| date =March 6, 2017 | url =http://bullandbearmcgill.com/meet-grandson-mcgill-drop-making-music-business-involved-recent-naked-trump-statues/| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> He also spent a great deal of time [[Disc jockey|DJing]] and playing shows in Montreal while at [[McGill University|McGill]].<ref name="Vice">{{cite magazine | last=Eff| first=Billy| title =grandson n'a pas besoin de vos prières ou de vos pensées| magazine =Vice|language=fr | date =May 18, 2018 | url =https://www.vice.com/fr_ca/article/xwmdg7/grandson-na-pas-besoin-de-vos-prieres-ou-de-vos-pensees| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> After two years, he transferred to [[Concordia University]], another Montreal institution. He studied [[communication]]s there briefly before dropping out and moving to [[Los Angeles]] in 2014 to pursue a music career.<ref name="Lupi"/><ref name="Charlatan"/>

==Career==
Benjamin began releasing music under the pseudonym Grandson, stylized as grandson, in late 2015.<ref name="Varsity2017"/> In 2016, he released several singles that accrued millions of streams, including "Bills", "Things Change", and "Bury Me Face Down".<ref name="BillFuel"/><ref name="Pop">{{cite news | last=Twardzik| first=Tom| title =New artist GRANDSON is at the front of rock and roll's evolution | publisher =Pop Dust| date =January 31, 2018 | url =https://www.popdust.com/new-artist-grandson-is-at-the-front-of-the-rock-and-roll-evolution-2528596956.html| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> In 2017, he was signed to [[RCA Records]], and continued releasing singles, including "Best Friends" and "Kiss Bang".<ref name="Lupi"/><ref name="Bull"/> He also performed at [[North by Northeast]] that year (among other festivals).<ref name="Varsity2017"/>

In April 2018, it was announced that grandson had signed to the [[Fueled By Ramen]] record label.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8285184/grandson-signs-with-fueled-by-ramen-following-his-powerful-post|title=Grandson Signs With Fueled By Ramen Following His Powerful Post-Parkland Theme, 'Thoughts & Prayers,'|date=April 4, 2018|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> He had also released two new singles, "[[Blood // Water]]" (a song about political corruption that would go on to appear on multiple [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' charts]]) and "Thoughts & Prayers", which offered a critique of [[Gun law in the United States|American gun laws]] in the wake of the [[Stoneman Douglas High School shooting|shooting]] at [[Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School]] in [[Parkland, Florida|Parkland]], [[Florida]].<ref name="BillFuel"/><ref name="Alt">{{cite magazine | last=Darus| first=Alex| title =Grandson announces politically charged EP, drops music video for "Blood // Water"| magazine =Alternative Press| date =June 4, 2018 | url =https://www.altpress.com/news/grandson-album-announcement-blood-water/| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> He released his first EP on [[Fueled by Ramen]], ''A Modern Tragedy Vol. 1'', in June 2018.<ref name="Alt"/><ref name="Sub">{{cite news | last=White| first=Logan| title =grandson announces new EP 'modern tragedy vol.1' and releases new song "Blood // Water"| publisher =Substream| date =June 5, 2018 | url =https://substreammagazine.com/2018/06/grandson-modern-tragedy-blood-water/| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> That EP also featured the song "6:00" which discussed [[Police brutality in the United States|police brutality]].<ref name="Bill6">{{cite magazine | last=Weatherby| first=Taylor| title =Grandson Tackles Police Brutality Controversy With Intense '6:00' Video: Premiere| magazine =Billboard| date =September 28, 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8477328/grandson-600-video| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> Also in June 2018, grandson was featured on [[Mike Shinoda]]'s song "Running from My Shadow", taken from his debut album ''[[Post Traumatic]]''; the track was released also with an accompanying music video in May 2018.<ref name="BillShinoda">{{cite magazine | last=Kaufman| first=Gil| title =Mike Shinoda Drops Gritty Video for 'Running From My Shadow' Featuring Grandson: Watch| magazine =Billboard| date =May 25, 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8457877/mike-shinoda-video-running-from-my-shadow-grandson| access-date =April 12, 2019}}</ref>

Throughout 2018, grandson toured with acts like [[Hobo Johnson]], [[Joywave]], [[Nothing But Thieves]],<ref name="Sub"/><ref name="Bill6"/><ref name="Varsity2018">{{cite news | last=Moshenski-Dubov| first=George| title =In conversation with grandson| newspaper =The Varsity| date =June 17, 2018 | url =https://thevarsity.ca/2018/06/17/in-conversation-with-grandson/| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> [[Smashing Pumpkins]],<ref name="SubSmashing">{{cite news | last=Scott| first=Kate| title =The Nights We Stole Christmas w/Muse, Smashing Pumpkins, and more!| work =Substream Magazine| date =December 26, 2018 | url =https://substreammagazine.com/2018/12/the-nights-we-stole-christmas-w-muse-smashing-pumpkins-and-more/| access-date =March 11, 2019}}</ref> and [[Young the Giant]].<ref name="Weekend"/><ref name="University">{{cite news | last=Kamaraj| first=Chandana| title =Young the Giant Hits Home with Audience, Produces More Mature Album| newspaper =The University Times| date =December 5, 2018 | url =https://unewsonline.com/2018/12/05/young-the-giant-hits-home-with-audience-produces-more-mature-album/| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> He also appeared on an episode of ''[[Late Night with Seth Meyers]]'' in August 2018, performing "[[Blood // Water]]".<ref name="BillSeth">{{cite magazine | last=Kaufman| first=Gil| title =Grandson Rages Against The Corporate Machine During Intense 'Blood//Water' Performance on 'Late Night': Watch | magazine =Billboard| date =August 16, 2018 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8470484/late-night-grandson-rages-bloodwater-performance-watch| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref>

In February 2019, he released a new single, "Apologize", and announced his second EP, ''A Modern Tragedy Vol. 2'', which was released on February 22, 2019.<ref name="Charlatan"/><ref name="Upset">{{cite news | last=Taylor| first=Sam| title =grandson has announced a new EP, 'a modern tragedy vol. 2'| publisher =Upset| date =February 5, 2019 | url =https://www.upsetmagazine.com/news/grandson-has-announced-a-new-ep-a-modern-tragedy-vol-2| access-date =March 1, 2019}}</ref> In late May, grandson released a cover of [[Rage Against the Machine]]'s "Maria", a song featured on the 1999 album ''[[The Battle of Los Angeles (album)|The Battle of Los Angeles]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|url = https://loudwire.com/grandson-cover-rage-against-the-machine-maria/|title = Grandson Unleashes Cover of Rage Against the Machine's 'Maria'|author = Chad Childers|magazine = [[Loudwire]]|date = May 31, 2019|access-date = September 14, 2019}}</ref>

In June 2019, he won the [[SOCAN Songwriting Prize]] for his song "[[Blood // Water]]",<ref name=bloodwater>[https://www.fyimusicnews.ca/articles/2019/06/24/politically-charged-blood-water-wins-socan-songwriting-prize "Politically Charged 'Blood // Water' Wins SOCAN Songwriting Prize"]. ''FYI Music News'', June 24, 2019.</ref> while in September 2019 the singer released ''A Modern Tragedy Vol. 3'', the final installment of the Modern Tragedy trilogy EPs;<ref>{{cite magazine|url = https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/grandson-has-announced-a-new-ep-a-modern-tragedy-vol-3/|title = grandson Has Announced A New EP, a modern tragedy vol. 3|magazine = [[Kerrang!]]|date = August 21, 2019|access-date = September 14, 2019}}</ref> the record features the lead single "Rock Bottom", which peaked at No. 38 on the Canadian Rock Chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url = https://www.billboard.com/music/grandson/chart-history/canada-rock|title = Grandson Chart History|magazine = Billboard|access-date = September 14, 2019}}</ref>

On September 23, 2020, grandson announced on his Facebook page that his debut full-length album ''[[Death of an Optimist]]'' would be released on December 4, 2020. ''[[Death of an Optimist]]'' is grandson's debut album, being his first record to have a heavier tone since the release of the last a modern tragedy EP, a modern tragedy vol. 3. The album gained a lot of publicity and recognition through his social media.
