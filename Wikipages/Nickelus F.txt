'''Nickelus F''' (formerly known as '''Nick Fury''') is a [[rapper]], [[songwriter]], and [[Record producer|producer]]<ref name="massappeal.com">{{cite web|url=https://massappeal.com/nickelus-f-on-working-with-drake-ghostwriting-and-triflin/|title=Nickelus F on working with Drake, ghostwriting, and Triflin|website=Mass Appeal|access-date=2017-10-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171007071608/https://massappeal.com/nickelus-f-on-working-with-drake-ghostwriting-and-triflin/|archive-date=2017-10-07|url-status=dead}}</ref> from [[Richmond, Virginia]]. He is best known for being the [[Black Entertainment Television]] ''[[106 & Park]]'' "[[Freestyle Friday]]" champion seven weeks in a row and being inducted into its Hall of Fame in 2007. Nickelus F is also known for his collaborations with rapper [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]]. On the Cut the Check [[mixtape]], Drake and Nickelus F released the song "AM 2 PM" by FTP Productions, which was also included on Drake's first mixtape ''[[Room for Improvement (mixtape)|Room for Improvement]]'', a project which features Nickelus F on several tracks including "Money," "A Scorpio's Mind," and "S.T.R.E.S.S.".

==Music career==

Nickelus F first began rapping in the 4th grade while at summer camp but he did not begin to take rap seriously until he was in the 6th grade. His first official song was recorded over [[LL Cool J]]'s song "[[Hey Lover]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hustlegrl.com/blog/2009/04/nickelus-f-interview-with-hustlegrl/|title=Nickelus F interview with hustleGRL – hustleGRL.com|website=hustlegrl.com}}</ref>

In 2000, Nickelus F got his first break into the [[hip hop]] industry when he competed against 300 contenders in the regional "Source Unsigned Hype Emcee Battle" where he placed second despite having no formal recording experience. He was also named "[[Unsigned Hype]]" in ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]'' in 2000 at the age of 17.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/dxnext/id.145/title.nickelus-f|title=Nickelus F|publisher=hiphopdx.com}}</ref>

Nickelus F then went on to participate in BET's 106 & Park Freestyle Fridays where he was crowned champion after winning seven weeks in a row in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hustlegrl.com/blog/2009/04/nickelus-f-interview-with-hustlegrl/|title=Nickelus F interview with hustleGRL|website=Hustle GRL |date=April 11, 2009|accessdate=July 6, 2010}}</ref>

In November 2009, Nickelus F was featured in ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'''s "Ten Most Underrated New Rappers".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/blogs/2009/11/24/complex-presents-the-10-most-underrated-new-rappers/|title=Complex Presents: The 10 Most Underrated New Rappers|date=November 24, 2009|accessdate=July 6, 2010|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]}}</ref>

In 2015, Nickelus F collaborated with rapper/producer [[Lil Ugly Mane]], under his production name Shawn Kemp, and released the album ''Trick Dice''. This album was released on cassette and according to Nickelus F, sold out in one hour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tinymixtapes.com/news/lil-ugly-mane-and-nickelus-fs-joint-tape-trick-dice-sells-out-one-hour|title=Lil Ugly Mane and Nickelus F’s joint tape Trick Dice sells out in one hour|publisher=Tiny Mixtapes|last=Diamond|first=Samuel}}</ref>

In 2016, Nickelus F made his return to battle rap in a league, "Southpaw Battle Coalition" against Danja Zone, and in 2017 against Bigjinya.

Nickelus F is currently part of a collective in Richmond, Virginia, known as the Association of Great Minds or AGM. It features fellow artists Radio B and [[Michael Millions]], producer Namebrand, and photographer/videographer Monsee.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://artisticmanifesto.com/2015/01/04/conversation-nickelus-f-part-3-richmond-music-scene-kanye-west-rants-drake/#.WdfKLEmouHs|title=A Conversation With Nickelus F Part 3: The Richmond Music Scene, Kanye West Rants & Drake|date=4 January 2015|publisher=}}</ref>

[[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]] has described Nickelus F as "one of the most gifted people I know at finding [[rapping#Flow|flows]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.complex.com/music/2015/07/drake-ghostwriting-collaborating|title = Surprise: Drake Has a History of Using "Ghostwriters" (If You Want to Call Them That)|website = Complex|access-date = 2016-04-20}}</ref>

As of 2018, the last released collaboration between Drake and Nickelus F was in 2013, titled "Number 15," which was released as a bonus track fifteen on Nickelus F's "Vices" album.

===Ghostwriting===

In a 2010 interview with ''[[Complex Magazine]]'', when asked if he had ever ghostwritten for Drake, Nickelus F responded  "Yeah, I've done that. I know the rumor going around. I don't want anything I say to be misconstrued. I helped out, you know."<ref name="complex.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2010/02/interview-nickelus-f-talks-exterminator-day-job-discovering-portishead-writing-for-drake|title=Interview: Nickelus F Talks Exterminator Day Job, Discovering Portishead, & Writing For Drake|website=Complex}}</ref>

===Influences===

Nickelus F considers himself to be a "true student"<ref name="auto"/> of [[hip-hop]] and [[hip-hop culture]]. He cites [[Project Pat]], [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Tha Dogg Pound]], [[C-Bo]], [[Spice 1]], [[Lord Finesse]], and high school friend J.R. The Great as a few of his hip-hop inspirations. However, he cites [[Kanye West]], [[Jay Z]], [[MF DOOM]], [[Raekwon]], and [[Future (rapper)|Future]] as his top 5 rappers.<ref name="massappeal.com"/>

==Personal life==

Born in Portland, Oregon, to a father who served in the military, Nickelus F moved frequently as a child.  He spent time in his childhood living in [[Berlin]], [[Germany]] and lived in various places across [[Virginia]], [[United States]] including [[Hampton, Virginia|Hampton]], [[Charlotte]], [[West Virginia]], and [[Richmond, Virginia|Richmond]].<ref name="complex.com"/>
While battle rapping on BET's 106 & Park Freestyle Fridays, Nickelus F was working as an exterminator for Terminex, a pest control service. He would head to New York from Richmond, Virginia after work on Thursdays to compete in [[Black Entertainment Television]] ''[[106 & Park]]'' "[[Freestyle Friday]]" every week.<ref name="complex.com"/>

After the birth of his two twin daughters, Nickelus F began his undergraduate degree at the [[Virginia Commonwealth University]] in Richmond, Virginia. He credits school for making him more efficient with his time and music as it provided him with structure in his life.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://artisticmanifesto.com/2014/12/10/conversation-nickelus-f-part-1-marvin-sapp-peteys-premium-du-rags-vcu/#.WdaoeKhyKHt|title=A Conversation With Nickelus F Part 1: Marvin Sapp, Petey's Premium Du Rags, & VCU|date=10 December 2014|publisher=Artistic Manifesto|last=Clark|first=Michell}}</ref>

In 2015, Richmond, Virginia street artist Nils Westergard painted a mural of Nickelus F on the side of a building in [[Oregon Hill]], [[Virginia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cruisingwithnickf.com/9/|title=Cruising around Richmond with Nickelus F — Barbershop|website=Cruising around Richmond with Nickelus F}}</ref> Nils Westergard created the mural because he believed that Nickelus F deserved recognition from his hometown of Richmond, Virginia and he was in a unique position to provide it via street art.<ref>{{cite web |title=Nickelus F Nils Westergard and Agm speak on Nick's mural in Oregon Hill |url=http://thecheatsmovement.com/2015/08/27/nickelus-f-niles-westergard-and-agm-speak-on-nicks-mural-in-oregon-hill/ |url-status=dead |date=August 27, 2015 |access-date=October 10, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160918121917/http://thecheatsmovement.com/2015/08/27/nickelus-f-niles-westergard-and-agm-speak-on-nicks-mural-in-oregon-hill/ |archive-date=September 18, 2016 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>
