'''Tiziano Ferro''' ({{IPA-it|titˈtsjaːno ˈfɛrro}}; born 21 February 1980)<ref name=allmusic/> is an Italian singer, [[Grammy Award]] winning, songwriter, producer and author.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tizianoferro.com/eng/#section_12|title=Tiziano Ferro biography |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |website=Tiziano Ferro Official Site |access-date= 25 October 2016}}</ref> He broke through in 2001 with his international hit single "[[Perdono]]" and has remained commercially successful since then, in several countries. Ferro has released a Spanish version of each of his albums and has also sung in English, Portuguese, and French. Known as the modern face of [[Italian pop music]], he frequently writes songs for other artists and has produced albums for [[Giusy Ferreri]], [[Alessandra Amoroso]] and [[Baby K (artist)|Baby K]].

Aside from his success as an artist, Ferro is well known for his personal struggles. Having been [[overweight]] as a teenager, he has been outspoken about his battles with [[food addiction]] and [[eating disorder]]s. In October 2010, at the height of his fame, Ferro came out as [[gay]], having himself struggled with depression related to self-acceptance about his homosexuality.

Ferro is currently one of the best-selling artists in Italy. Both his third album ''[[Nessuno è solo]]'' and fourth album ''[[Alla mia età (album)|Alla mia età]]'' were certified diamond. Despite his fears, his [[coming out]] did not negatively affect his career, as fifth album ''[[L'amore è una cosa semplice]]'' was the best-selling album of 2012 in Italy, and [[TZN - The Best of Tiziano Ferro|his first greatest hits album]] was supported by a [[Lo stadio Tour 2015|stadium tour]]. As of 2015, Ferro has sold over 15 million records worldwide.<ref>http://www.sanremonews.it/2015/02/09/leggi-notizia/argomenti/festival-di-sanremo/articolo/a-sanremo-arriva-il-maxi-cartiglio-lungo-200-metri-dei-mitici-baci-perugina.html</ref>

==Biography==

===Childhood and early beginnings===
Ferro's interest in music was born when he received a [[keyboard instrument|toy keyboard]] as a present, which was used to compose his first songs when he was seven.<ref name="RockolBio">{{Cite web | url = http://www.rockol.it/biografia/Tiziano-Ferro | publisher = Rockol.it | language = it | access-date =23 October 2011 | title = Tiziano Ferro – Biografia}}</ref> He later started taking guitar and piano classes at a local [[Music school|conservatory of music]]. When he was 16 years old, he joined a [[gospel choir]]<ref name=allmusic>{{Cite web | url = http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tiziano-ferro-p511734 | website = [[AllMusic]] | access-date =23 October 2011 | title = Tiziano Ferro – Biography}}</ref> and during the same years, he started performing in [[piano bar]]s and [[karaoke]] contests.<ref name="MTVBio">{{cite web | url = http://www.mtv.it/musica/artisti/archivio/artista.asp?idArt=13889#biografia | publisher = [[MTV Italy]] | language = it | access-date =24 October 2011 | title = Tiziano Ferro: La biografia}}</ref>

In 1997, Ferro participated in the "Accademia della Canzone di Sanremo", a music contest created with the purpose to choose the contestants of the [[Sanremo Music Festival]], but was eliminated during the first stage of the competition. The following year, Ferro participated again in the same contest, and despite being chosen as one of the 12 finalists, he did not make it to win the competition.<ref name="RockolBio"/> However, during the selections, Ferro met music producers [[Alberto Salerno]] and [[Mara Maionchi]], and started to collaborate with them.<ref name="RockolBio"/><ref name="MTVBio"/>

In 1999 Ferro also toured throughout Italy as a [[back vocalist]] with the Italian [[hip hop band]] Sottotono.<ref>{{Cite web|  url = http://roma.repubblica.it/dettaglio/le-vostre-domande-a-tiziano-ferro/1651015/2 | website = [[la Repubblica]] | language = it |access-date =23 October 2011 | title = Le vostre domande a Tiziano Ferro | date = 13 June 2009}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url = http://archiviostorico.corriere.it/2004/aprile/28/Ragazzi_sto_arrivando__vm_0_040428014.shtml | website = [[Corriere della Sera]] | language = it | access-date =23 October 2011 | title = Ragazzi, sto arrivando | author = Andrea Laffranchi | date = 28 April 2004}}</ref>

===Career breakthrough===
In 2001, Ferro signed a contract with [[EMI Music|EMI Music Italy]]. On 22 June 2001, he released his first single, "[[Perdono|Xdono]]".<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.fattitaliani.it/index.php?mact=News,cntnt01,detail,0&cntnt01articleid=3883&cntnt01origid=102&cntnt01returnid=102| publisher = fattitaliani.it | language = it | access-date =23 October 2011 | title = Tiziano Ferro, il 22 giugno 2001 "XDONO" arrivava nelle radio italiane… | date = 20 June 2011}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.rockol.it/news-259176/-Tiziano-Ferro--esce-in-autunno-il-nuovo-album | title = Tiziano Ferro: esce in autunno il nuovo album | language = it|  date = 20 June 2011}}</ref> During the first months after the release, the song received a poor reception, but it later became a hit and, after topping the [[FIMI|Italian Singles Chart]] in September 2001,<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Tiziano+Ferro&titel=Xdono&cat=s | publisher = italiancharts.com | access-date =23 October 2011 | title = Tiziano Ferro – Xdono}}</ref> it was certified double platinum by the [[Federation of the Italian Music Industry]] for sales exceeding 100,000 copies.<ref>{{Cite magazine | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=MQ8EAAAAMBAJ&q=tiziano+ferro+billboard+perdono&pg=PA24 | magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date =21 October 2011 | title = Latin Notas – Seeing double| author = Leila Cobo | date = 15 March 2003 | page = 24}}</ref>

The song was included in Ferro's debut album, ''[[Rosso Relativo]]'', released on 26 October 2001.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.emimusic.it/artist/tiziano-ferro/disco/album/rosso-relativo | title = Rosso relativo | language = it| publisher = [[EMI Music]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=ABIEAAAAMBAJ&q=rosso+relativo+tiziano+ferro&pg=PA59 | magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date =23 October 2011 | title = Retailers hope EMI 'Echoes' Int's Merchants Rate Major's Schedule | author = Gordon Masson | date = 10 November 2001 | page = 59}}</ref> Following the commercial success obtained in Italy, in 2002 the album was released in several European countries. A [[Spanish-language]] version of the record, titled ''Rojo Relativo'', was also released in Spain and Latin America.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://musica.fnac.es/a62085/Tiziano-Ferro-Rojo-Relativo-sin-especificar | title = Tiziano Ferro – Rojo Relativo | language = es | publisher = [[Fnac]] | access-date =23 October 2011}}</ref> ''Rosso Relativo'' obtained success in different markets throughout its various editions and had sold more than 1,000,000 copies worldwide by 2004, according to [[EMI|EMI Music]].<ref>{{Cite magazine | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=ShAEAAAAMBAJ&q=rosso+relativo+tiziano+ferro+copies&pg=PA64 | magazine = [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | access-date =23 October 2011 | title = Artists to Watch | author = Mark Worden | date = 10 September 2004 | page = 64}}</ref> The album also allowed Ferro to receive a nomination for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] at the [[Latin Grammy Awards of 2003]].<ref name="LatinGrammy"/>

In October 2011 Ferro released "[[La differenza tra me e te]]",<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://tg24.sky.it/tg24/spettacolo/2011/10/14/tiziano_ferro_nuovo_singolo_la_differenza_tra_me_e_te.html | publisher = [[Sky Italia]] | language = it | access-date =29 October 2011 | title = La differenza tra me e te: il nuovo singolo di Tiziano Ferro | date = 14 October 2011}}</ref> the lead single from his fifth studio album ''[[L'amore è una cosa semplice]]'' (titled ''[[L'amore è una cosa semplice|El amor es una cosa simple]]'' in Spanish). The album itself was certified eight times platinum and became the best-selling album of 2012.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.fimi.it/dett_ddmercato.php?id=77 | website = [[FIMI]] | language = it | access-date = 4 April 2013 | title = Classifiche annuali 2012 FIMI GfK: Domina il repertorio Italiano | date = 14 January 2013 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130117063538/http://www.fimi.it/dett_ddmercato.php?id=77 | archive-date = 17 January 2013 | df = dmy-all }}</ref>

During the spring and summer of 2012, Ferro embarked on a largely domestic concert tour, with additional stops in [[Switzerland]], [[Belgium]] and [[Monaco]].<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.teamworld.it/news/in-evidenza/tiziano-ferro-date-tour-estivo-2012/ | website = Team World | language = it | access-date = 4 April 2013 | title = Tiziano Ferro: date tour estivo 2012 | date = 27 April 2012}}</ref> The tour's highlight was Ferro's first full stadium concert at the [[Stadio Olimpico]] in Rome. This sold-out concert was attended by 50,000 fans.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.woonko.com/speciali/tiziano-ferro-allolimpico-lacrime-sudore-e-tanta-passione-70938.html | website = Woonko | language = it | access-date = 4 April 2013 | title = Tiziano Ferro all'Olimpico: lacrime, sudore e tanta passione | author = Chiara Colasanti | date = 22 July 2012}}</ref>

In 2012 Tiziano wrote the song Per Te,<ref>{{Cite web | url = https://www.facebook.com/tizianoferro/posts/10155874010942898/ | website = [[Facebook]] | language = it | title = Chris Botti | date = 30 August 2018 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> sung by [[Andrea Bocelli]] and contained in the album of the world-famous jazz player [[Chris Botti]], which will earn him a [[Grammy Award]] as Best Pop Instrumental Album.

===''TZN - The Best of Tiziano Ferro''===
Ferro released his first greatest hits album ''[[TZN - The Best of Tiziano Ferro]]'' in November 2014.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.radioitalia.it/news/nuova_uscita/6200_tiziano_ferro_pubblicher%C3%A0_una_raccolta_nel_2014.php | website = Radio Italia | language = it | access-date = 13 September 2013 | title = Tiziano Ferro pubblicherà una raccolta nel 2014 | date = 4 June 2013 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130930090513/http://www.radioitalia.it/news/nuova_uscita/6200_tiziano_ferro_pubblicher%C3%A0_una_raccolta_nel_2014.php | archive-date = 30 September 2013 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> Both the lead single "[[Senza scappare mai più]]" and the second single "[[Incanto (Tiziano Ferro song)|Incanto]]" reached the top five in Italy and were certified platinum. The album itself spent six non-consecutive weeks at number one, the last of which came after his acclaimed guest performance at the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2015|Sanremo Music Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Scarpone|first=Cristian|title=Classifica Italiana: I Dear Jack assaltano la top ten ma il re è Tiziano Ferro|url=http://www.allmusicitalia.it/news/classifica-italiana-dear-jack-assaltano-la-top-ten-ma-il-re-e-tiziano-ferro.html|language=it|access-date=9 October 2015|website=All Music Italia|date=19 February 2015}}</ref> It was eventually certified seven times platinum and spent almost a year in the top ten on the Italian album chart.<ref>{{cite web|last=Scarpone|first=Cristian|title=Tiziano Ferro e Vasco Rossi dei boom: 300 mila copie e 6 dischi di platino|url=http://www.allmusicitalia.it/news/tiziano-ferro-e-vasco-rossi-dei-boom-300-mila-copie-e-6-dischi-di-platino.html|language=it|access-date=9 October 2015|website=All Music Italia|date=5 October 2015}}</ref> In Spain, it was Ferro's first top ten album in more than ten years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://spanishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Tiziano+Ferro&titel=TZN+-+The+Best+Of+Tiziano+Ferro&cat=a|title=spanishcharts.com - Tiziano Ferro - TZN - The Best Of Tiziano Ferro|website=spanishcharts.com}}</ref>

In the summer of 2015 Ferro [[Lo stadio Tour 2015|toured the main Italian stadiums]], including two concerts at the [[San Siro]] stadium in Milan and two at the [[Stadio Olimpico]] in Rome.<ref name="contattonews">{{cite web|url=http://www.contattonews.it/2015/02/09/tiziano-ferro-raddoppia-a-roma-il-26-e-27-giugno/379976/|title=Tiziano Ferro tour Doppio appuntamento|publisher=contattonews.it|access-date=2015-05-07}}</ref> The Milan concerts were recorded and added to a new CD/DVD edition of ''TZN - The Best of Tiziano Ferro'', that was released in November 2015. That same month Ferro started a European arena tour to continue promoting the greatest hits compilation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.contattolab.it/tiziano-ferro-ecco-il-tanto-atteso-dvd-de-lo-stadio-tour/|title=Tiziano Ferro, arriva il tanto atteso Dvd de "Lo Stadio Tour"|publisher=contattolab|access-date=27 October 2015 | date = 26 October 2015}}</ref>

===''Il mestiere della vita''===
Ferro announced the release date of his sixth studio album a year in advance, at the last concert of his European arena tour. He told the crowd that it would be released on 2 December 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusicitalia.it/news/tiziano-ferro-annuncia-la-data-di-uscita-del-nuovo-album-2-dicembre-2016.html|title=Tiziano Ferro annuncia la data di uscita del nuovo album: 2 dicembre 2016|date=22 December 2015|last=Scarpone|first=Cristian|website=All Music Italia|access-date=26 November 2016}}</ref> In June the album's title was revealed to be [[Il mestiere della vita]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusicitalia.it/news/tiziano-ferro-fuori-a-dicembre-il-mestiere-della-vita.html|title=Tiziano Ferro: Fuori a dicembre il nuovo album "Il Mestiere Della Vita"|date=6 June 2016 |last=Meo|first=Oriana|website=All Music Italia|access-date=26 November 2016}}</ref> The album's lead single "[[Potremmo ritornare]]" was released in October and debuted on top of the Italian Singles Chart. It was his first number-one hit as lead artist since 2008's "[[Alla mia età (song)|Alla mia età]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.optimaitalia.com/blog/2016/11/04/classifica-fimi-guidata-da-tre-generazioni-con-benji-e-fede-giorgia-e-pooh-tiziano-ferro-svetta-tra-i-singoli/445028|title=Classifica Fimi guidata da tre generazioni con Benji e Fede, Giorgia e Pooh: Tiziano Ferro svetta tra i singoli|date=4 November 2016 |last=Dessi|first=Martina|website=Optima Italia|access-date=19 November 2016}}</ref>

Among the collaborators include [[Baby K (artist)|Baby K]], Raige, Emanuele Dabbono, Tormento and [[Carmen Consoli]], the latter collaboration Ferro and Consoli sang together in the second single Il conforto, extracted on January 13, 2017. The song reached the 4th position in the [[Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana|Italian Singles Chart]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Tiziano+Ferro&titel=Incanto&cat=s|title=italiancharts.com - Tiziano Ferro - Incanto|website=italiancharts.com}}</ref>

Composed of 13 tracks, the album features [[Pop music|pop]], [[Hip hop music|rap]] and [[Rhythm and blues|R&B sounds]], with themes ranging from forgiveness to fear and anger.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.correttainformazione.it/il-mestiere-della-vita-tiziano-ferro-recensione-migliori-testi-canzoni-tracklist-significato/81653408.html|title=Il Mestiere della Vita di Tiziano Ferro: Recensione e migliori testi delle canzoni in tracklist con significato|date=2 December 2016}}</ref>

From June to July 2017 the tour "Il mestiere della vita Tour 2017" was held, consisting of thirteen dates in Italian stadiums.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/cultura/musica/2016/10/06/tiziano-ferro-annuncia-date-tour-2017_38ebeee8-9e97-437d-82ac-9270b4963e54.html|title = Tiziano Ferro annuncia date tour 2017 - Musica|date = 6 October 2016}}</ref> In April 2018 the singer-songwriter won an Onstage Award for the best Italian tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.optimaitalia.com/blog/2018/04/04/tutti-i-vincitori-degli-onstage-awards-2018-tiziano-ferro-vasco-rossi-riki/1056793|title=Tutti i vincitori degli Onstage Awards 2018 da Tiziano Ferro a Vasco Rossi e Riki|date=4 April 2018}}</ref>

In 2018 he collaborates with [[Ed Sheeran]] and his brother Matthew on the composition of "Amo solo te" <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tg24.sky.it/spettacolo/musica/2019/02/25/andrea-bocelli-ed-sheeran-nuovo-singolo|title = Andrea Bocelli e ed Sheeran: Nuovo singolo "Amo soltanto te"}}</ref>("This Is the Only Time" in the English version): Tiziano Ferro is the author of the lyrics of the song interpreted by [[Andrea Bocelli]] and [[Ed Sheeran]] and included in the album ''[[Sì (Andrea Bocelli album)|Sì]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.andreabocelli.com/it/discografia/si-deluxe-edition/|title=Andrea Bocelli|date=29 March 2019}}</ref>

===''Accetto Miracoli''===
''Accetto miracoli'' was released in November 2019. The album, certified Double Platinum in Italy,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ansa.it/sito/notizie/cultura/musica/2020/07/20/tiziano-ferro-doppio-platino-per-lalbum-accetto-miracoli_38ce4ddb-e96a-44cd-a319-d8104de078dc.html|date=20 July 2020|website=ANSA.it|title= Tiziano Ferro doppio platino per l'album Accetto Miracoli |language=it}}</ref> was also released in Spanish language, as ''Acepto milagros'', one week after the Italian release.

Produced by the guru of American R&B sound [[Timbaland]] (9 of 12 tracks) and anticipated by two singles, "Buona (Cattiva) Sorte" and the title track "Accept Miracles", it is an album that its author has defined: "fresh, honest, energetic, the result of the need to deliver myself to new experiences".{{citation needed|date=October 2020}}

''Accetto Miracoli'' contains 12 tracks: "Vai ad Amarti", "Amici per errore", "Balla per Me" (a duet with Jovanotti), "In mezzo a questo inverno", "Come farebbe un uomo", "Seconda pelle", "Il destino di chi visse per amare", "Le 3 parole sono 2", "Casa a Natale", "Un Uomo Pop", "Buona (Cattiva) Sorte" and "Accetto Miracoli". Only in digital formats, there are 2 additional bonus tracks, "Accetto Miracoli" and "In mezzo a questo inverno", arranged by the producer Julio Reyes Copello.

In the Spanish version of the album, the tracklist includes a duet on the title track with [[Ana Guerra]]. The single went to number 1 on the digital charts in Spain, France, Benelux.{{citation needed|date=October 2020}}

===Personal life===
He was diagnosed with depression in 2008 and started taking [[antidepressant]]s. In late 2009, he came close to taking his own life, going as far as to write a [[suicide]] note.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.gaywave.it/articolo/tiziano-ferro-pensava-al-suicidio/23801/ | publisher = Gay Wave | language = it | access-date = 18 July 2013 | title = Tiziano Ferro pensava al suicidio | date = 25 October 2010}}</ref> In early 2010 his desperation led him to break down and [[coming out|came out]] to his father, an event that inspired the title of his first book, ''Trent'anni e una chiacchierata con papà'' (Thirty years old and a chat with dad). This book, published in October 2010, contains most of his personal diaries, dutifully kept since his teenage years.<ref name="la rep">{{Cite news |url=http://www.repubblica.it/persone/2010/10/05/news/tiziano_ferro-7752713/?ref=HRESS-1 |trans-title=Ferro: I'm gay and I have the freedom to say so |title=Ferro: Sono omosessuale e ho la libertà di poterlo dire |last=Castaldo |first=Gino |date=5 October 2010 |publisher=[[La Repubblica]] |language=it |access-date=6 October 2010}}</ref> Its release was preceded by an interview with [[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]] in which he declared his [[homosexuality]] to the public.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.style.it/vanitypeople/show/musica/2010/10/05/tiziano-ferro.aspx | publisher = Style.it | language = it | access-date = 18 July 2013 | title = Tiziano Ferro: "Mi voglio innamorare (di un uomo)" | date = 5 October 2010 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20150924121405/http://www.style.it/vanitypeople/show/musica/2010/10/05/tiziano-ferro.aspx | archive-date = 24 September 2015 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> The following year Ferro moved back to Italy to live closer to his friends and family.<ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.lastampa.it/2012/02/21/spettacoli/tiziano-ferro-alla-mia-etanon-ho-piu-paura-di-essere-felice-p0eXGHEdQ1YXw0zi1twIgM/pagina.html | publisher = La Stampa | language = it | access-date = 18 July 2013 | title = Tiziano Ferro: "Alla mia età non ho più paura di essere felice" | date = 21 February 2012}}</ref>

In 2019, Ferro announced his marriage to former Warner Bros. consultant Victor Allen. The two were married in [[Sabaudia]], a comune of the Latina province in Latium. In a statement made to the Corriere, he confirmed that he considers himself both "gay and Catholic".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.corriere.it/spettacoli/19_luglio_16/01-interni-rewrewrcorriere-web-sezioni-577fb010-a726-11e9-8722-90fee69fd06f.shtml?refresh_ce-cp |title=Tiziano Ferro: "Un Dio simpatico (e il mio matrimonio davanti al Circeo)" |trans-title=Tiziano Ferro: "a nice God (and my wedding in front of the Circeo)" |publisher=Corriere della Sera |author=Tiziano Ferro |date=16 July 2019 |language=it |access-date=16 July 2019}}</ref>
