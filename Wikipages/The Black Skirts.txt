'''Jo Hyu-il''' ({{Korean|hangul=조휴일|hanja=|rr=|mr=}}; born 1982),<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.khan.co.kr/kh_news/khan_art_view.html?artid=201706162209005&code=940100|title=[커버스토리 - 굿바이 둔촌주공]헌 집 주면 새집 준다지만 고향도 재건축이 될까요|date=2017-06-16|website=The Kyunghyang Shinmun|language=ko|access-date=2019-06-25}}</ref> better known as '''The Black Skirts''' ({{Korean|hangul=검정치마|hanja=|rr=|mr=}}), is an [[indie rock]] musician based in South Korea. In 2010, he released his debut album, ''201'', which won Best Modern Rock Album at the 2010 [[Korean Music Awards]].<ref name=":1" /> He has since released the albums, ''Don't You Worry Baby (I'm Only Swimming)'' (2011),<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.khan.co.kr/kh_news/khan_art_view.html?artid=201107212119165&code=960802|title=[음반리뷰]검정치마 ‘Don’t You Worry Baby(I’m Only Swimming)’|date=2011-07-21|website=The Kyunghyang Shinmun|language=ko|access-date=2019-06-25}}</ref> ''Team Baby'' (2017),<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://tenasia.hankyung.com/archives/1219516|title=검정치마, 정규 3집 'TEAM BABY' 발매…6년만의 귀환|date=2017-05-28|website=Ten Asia|language=ko-KR|access-date=2019-06-25}}</ref> and ''Thirsty'' (2019).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mk.co.kr/news/home/view/2019/02/75036/|title=검정치마, 1년 9개월 만에 신곡 발매…12일 ‘THIRSTY’ 공개|date=2019-02-07|website=Maeil Business Newspaper|language=ko|access-date=2019-06-25}}</ref>

== Background ==
Jo was born in [[Seoul|Seoul, South Korea]], on December 5, 1982. He moved to the United States when he was 12 years old and grew up in [[New Jersey]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://seoulbeats.com/2011/12/k-pop-indie-gem-the-black-skirts/|title=K-pop Indie Gem: The Black Skirts|date=2011-12-18|work=seoulbeats|access-date=2018-04-05|language=en-US}}</ref> His musical taste was influenced at an early age by what he would learn from American music media such as [[MTV]] and ''[[Rolling Stone]].''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.elle.co.kr/article/view.asp?MenuCode=en010302&intSno=19200|title=변한 것 없는 검정치마 {{!}} 엘르코리아(ELLE KOREA)|website=www.elle.co.kr|language=ko|access-date=2018-04-05}}</ref> He was also heavily influenced by [[Alanis Morissette]]'s ''[[Jagged Little Pill]]'' and the first album he ever purchased was [[Michael Jackson|Michael Jackson's]] ''[[Dangerous (Michael Jackson album)|Dangerous]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://whitequeen.tistory.com/1288|title=검정치마 조휴일 인터뷰_ 2집은 노골적으로 교외적, BPM은 쭉 떨어질 계획|date=2011-08-02|work=화이트퀸의 음악여행|access-date=2018-04-05|language=ko}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== Pre-The Black Skirts ===
In 2004, Jo formed a three-member [[punk rock]] band called Castel Prayon while living in [[New York City]]. The band eventually dissolved as members went on to pursue their individual endeavors, leaving Jo to become a one-man band.

=== The Black Skirts (2006–Present) ===
In 2006, Jo returned to South Korea and began making music as a [[one-man band]] under the name, The Black Skirts.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mnet.com/artist/156576/profile|title=검정치마 소개|website=Mnet|language=ko|trans-title=Black Skirts profile|access-date=2018-01-15}}</ref> In 2009, Jo released an unofficial album titled ''My Feet Don't Touch the Ground (And I Am So Winded I Can't Sing for You Today).''

In 2010, Jo gained notoriety in the Korean indie rock scene upon releasing ''201,'' his first album as The Black Skirts Produced by Kia Eshghi.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://tenasia.hankyung.com/archives/1219516|title=검정치마, 정규 3집 'Team Baby' 발매…6년만의 귀환|last=Kim|first=Soo-kyun|date=2017-05-29|work=10 Asia|access-date=2018-01-15|language=ko-KR|trans-title=The Black Skirts return after 6 years with 3rd album 'Team Baby'}}</ref> The album was recorded in his basement.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newsis.com/view/?id=NISX20110715_0008693318|title='검정치마' 조휴일, 나? 이런 가수야…반말 인터뷰|last=NEWSIS|website=newsis|language=ko|access-date=2018-04-05}}</ref> Later that year, he won Best Modern Rock Album at the [[Korean Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.koreatimes.co.kr/www/news/art/2010/03/201_62072.html|title=Korean Music Awards to Honor Quality|last=Han|first=Sang-hee|date=2010-03-09|work=The Korea Times|access-date=2018-01-15|language=en}}</ref> A special edition of ''201'' was released on March 18, 2010.

On July 13, 2011, Jo released his second album, ''Don't You Worry Baby (I'm Only Swimming).''

On January 7, 2016, Jo signed to HIGHGRND.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20171024000871|title=[Herald Interview] After 3 years, Epik High raps as if it’s their last|last=Hong|first=Dam-young|date=2017-10-24|work=The Korea Herald|access-date=2018-01-15|language=en}}</ref>

On May 30, 2017, Jo released his third album part 1, ''Team Baby,'' whose album cover features Jo's parents. He announced that this album is composed of three parts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sportsworldi.com/newsView/20170529000442?OutUrl=naver|title=검정치마, 30일 6년 만에 정규앨범 발매|date=2017-05-29|website=스포츠월드|language=kr|access-date=2019-03-21}}</ref>

In 2018, Jo left HIGHGRND and started working under his own company Doggy Rich.

On February 12, 2019, Jo released his third album part 2, ''THIRSTY,'' with his label partner BESPOK.

Two years after the release of their album, THIRSTY, Jo returns with a full English-language EP called ''Good Luck To You, Girl Scout!''. The EP was said to be “inspired by my friends who got dumped last summer during the height of Corona Virus.” <ref>{{Cite web|title=Facebook|url=https://www.facebook.com/theblackskirts/photos/pcb.4144147242271630/4144145738938447/|access-date=2021-05-30|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref>
