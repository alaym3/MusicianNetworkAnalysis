'''Frederick Lloyd''' (born 23 February 1991) is a British [[film director]] and [[composer]]. He attended [[University for the Creative Arts]] in [[Farnham]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ucreative.ac.uk/news/2012/april/pantheon#.UCFG7GF5rzg|title = UCA &#124; University for the Creative Arts}}</ref> graduated in 2012 with a first class degree in Film Production.

Lloyd's ''Pantheon'' was screened at the [[British Kinematograph, Sound and Television Society|BKSTS]] 2012 student showcase.<ref>https://www.bksts.com/secure/about.asp</ref> This was his third film to be shown at the BFI showcase, with ''The Constant'' being shown in 2011 and ''The Death of the Cinema Projectionist'' the same year. ''Pantheon'' went on to win Best Film, Best Score and Best Production Design at Screentest Film Festival 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.screentestfest.org.uk/bestof2013/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2015-12-19 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130703195902/http://www.screentestfest.org.uk/bestof2013/ |archivedate=2013-07-03 }}</ref> It also screened internationally at [[Plus Camerimage]] 2012 in Poland and [[National Film Festival for Talented Youth]] 2013 in Seattle, USA.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://ursinevulpine.co.uk/press/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-08-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130911081231/http://ursinevulpine.co.uk/press/ |archive-date=2013-09-11 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In July 2013, it was released online for public viewing a year after its completion.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/42293055|title = Pantheon|date = 16 May 2012}}</ref>

''The Constant'' was also selected for the 2011 [http://bbcmusicvideofestival.tumblr.com/ BBC Music Video Festival] and shown by the BBC on 20 screens around the UK.<ref>http://www.thenewcurrent.com/2011/09/21/13942/{{Dead link|date=July 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==Film & Trailer Scoring==
{{BLP sources section|date=April 2017}}

Lloyd composes under the moniker of Ursine Vulpine, and scored the award-winning soundtrack for ''Pantheon'' (2012).

In October 2012, the track "Decompression/Reborn: Death" was used on the 2nd trailer for [[Kathryn Bigelow]]'s film ''[[Zero Dark Thirty]]''. It originally featured on the soundtrack to ''[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2232746/ Pantheon]''.

In July 2013, Ark Ascending was used for the second theatrical trailer of ''[[The Hunger Games: Catching Fire]]''.

In August 2013, the track "Reborn: Death (Alternate)", an unused track from the soundtrack to [https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2232746/ Pantheon], was featured in the second trailer for [[Paul Greengrass]]'s film ''[[Captain Phillips (film)|Captain Phillips]]''.

In October 2015, Lloyd assisted in scoring a rearrangement of 'The Force' theme for the final trailer for ''[[Star Wars: The Force Awakens]]'' as well as its sequel ''[[Star Wars: The Last Jedi]].''

In June 2016, Lloyd's remake of [[Chris Isaak]]'s original ''[[Wicked Game]]'' was used in the E3 reveal trailer for [[Microsoft]]'s [[Forza Horizon 3]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Forza Horizon 3 - Trailer oficial E3 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RqkpT0qag5c |website=YouTube |publisher=XboxBR}}</ref>

In December 2016, Lloyd's remake of the [[Flaming Lips]]' original "[[Do You Realize??]]" was used for the first theatrical trailer of ''[[Transformers: The Last Knight]]''.

In April 2019, the song titled "I'm Going to Drive Until It Burns My Bones" from the Album Respire was used for the trailer to the HBO Original show ''[[Chernobyl (miniseries)|Chernobyl]]''.

In July 2020, Lloyd's orchestral remake of "Triage at Dawn" from the [[Half-Life 2]] [[Kelly Bailey (composer)|soundtrack]] was used for the trailer to the interactive storybook [[Half-Life: Alyx|The Final Hours of Half-Life: Alyx]].

In March 2021, the track "Renzokuken" was used on the 2nd trailer for [[Zack Snyder]]'s film ''[[Zack Snyder's Justice League]]''
