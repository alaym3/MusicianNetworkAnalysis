'''Rivers of Nihil''' is an American [[progressive metal]] band from [[Reading, Pennsylvania]]. They are currently signed to [[Metal Blade Records]]. To date the band has released two [[EP album|EPs]] and four [[studio albums]]. Their most recent release is 2021's ''The Work''. Their 2018's album ''[[Where Owls Know My Name]]'' <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalblade.com/us/releases/rivers-of-nihil-where-owls-know-my-name/|title=Rivers of Nihil "Where Owls Know My Name"|website=Metalblade.com}}</ref> debuted at number 61 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metal-temple.com/site/news/view/general_news/rivers-of-nihil-charts.htm|title=Metal-Temple.com :: RIVERS OF NIHIL: Charts with "Where Owls Know My Name." |website=Metal-temple.com}}</ref> selling 2,750 copies in its first week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalinsider.net/columns/metal-by-numbers/metal-by-numbers-3-28-stone-temple-pilots-soar-to-the-top-10-nihil-annihilates|title=Metal By Numbers 3/28: Stone Temple Pilots soar to the top 10; Nihil annihilatesMetal Insider|date=March 28, 2018|website=Metalinsider.net}}</ref>

==History==
Rivers of Nihil was formed in 2009. They recorded their first EP ''Hierarchy'' later that year with [[Century (American band)|Century]] vocalist Carson Slovak, who produced numerous works by other bands such as [[August Burns Red]], [[Texas in July]], and [[Black Crown Initiate]] (Slovak would later work with the bands on their second and third studio albums, adding writing credits to "Terrestria III: Wither" on ''Where Owls Know My Name'').<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/1895703-Carson-Slovak?page=2|title=Carson Slovak|website=Discogs.com}}</ref> During this period the band opened shows for bands such as [[Decapitated (band)|Decapitated]], [[Decrepit Birth]], [[Suffocation (band)|Suffocation]], [[Dying Fetus]], and [[Misery Index (band)|Misery Index]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/rivers-of-nihil-signs-with-metal-blade-records/|title=RIVERS OF NIHIL Signs With METAL BLADE RECORDS|date=December 10, 2012|website=Blabbermouth.net}}</ref>

In 2011, the band worked with producer Len Carmichael to record their second [[EP album|EP]] ''Temporality Unbound''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalblade.com/us/news/metal-blade-records-signs-rivers-of-nihil/|title=Metal Blade Records Signs Rivers Of Nihil|website=Metalblade.com}}</ref> The album would spawn a music video for the song “(sin)Chronos".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalinjection.net/video/rivers-of-nihil-sinchronos-official-video|title=Rivers of Nihil- (sin)Chronos Official Video|website=Metalinjection.net}}</ref>

In February 2012, [[MetalSucks]] referred to Rivers of Nihil as the "best new Death metal band you'll hear this year".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2012/02/16/rivers-of-nihil-are-the-best-new-death-metal-band-youll-hear-this-year/|title=RIVERS OF NIHIL ARE THE BEST NEW DEATH METAL BAND YOU'LL HEAR THIS YEAR - MetalSucks|date=February 16, 2012|website=Metalsucks.net}}</ref>

On December 10, 2012, [[Metal Blade Records]] announced that they'd signed Rivers of Nihil to a record deal, and released a demo for the song "Rain Eater". The label also announced that the band would be working with [[Erik Rutan]] of [[Hate Eternal]] and [[Morbid Angel]] to record their first [[studio album]] at Mana Recording Studios in [[Tampa, Florida]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2012/12/10/rivers-of-nihil-sign-with-metal-blade/|title=Rivers of Nihil Sign with Metal Blade - MetalSucks|date=December 10, 2012|website=Metalsucks.net}}</ref> The band released [[The Conscious Seed of Light|the album]] in the United States on October 15, 2013. The album artwork was created by [[Dan Seagrave]], who would handle the artwork on the band's next two albums as well.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/rivers-of-nihil-a-fertile-altar-lyric-video-released/|title=RIVERS OF NIHIL: 'A Fertile Altar' Lyric Video Released|date=November 22, 2013|website=Blabbermouth.net}}</ref>

Rivers of Nihil returned to Atrium studios to work again with Slovak on their second full-length studio album, ''[[Monarchy (Rivers of Nihil album)|Monarchy]]'', which was released on August 21, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/rivers-of-nihil-to-release-monarchy-in-august/|title=RIVERS OF NIHIL To Release 'Monarchy' In August|date=June 13, 2015|website=Blabbermouth.net}}</ref> The album sold 1,175 copies in its first week.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalinsider.net/columns/metal-by-numbers/metal-by-numbers-93-a-disturbing-amount-of-debuts|title=Metal By Numbers 9/3: A disturbing amount of debutsMetal Insider|date=September 3, 2015|website=Metalinsider.net}}</ref> On August 17, 2015, the band made ''Monarchy'' available for streaming in full.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2015/08/17/streamers-of-nihil-stream-the-new-rivers-of-nihil-album-monarchy-in-full/|title=Streamers of Nihil: Stream the New Rivers of Nihil Album Monarchy in Full - MetalSucks|date=August 17, 2015|website=Metalsucks.net}}</ref>

On September 7, 2017, the band announced via their Facebook account that they'd returned to the studio, once again working at Atrium Audio, and working again with audio engineers Carson Slovak and Grant McFarland.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2017/09/07/rivers-of-nihil-are-in-the-studio/|title=Rivers of Nihil are in the Studio - MetalSucks|date=September 7, 2017|website=Metalsucks.net}}</ref> The band recruited The Kennedy Veil's live drummer Jared Klein to track drums.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalinjection.net/latest-news/rivers-of-nihil-is-in-the-studio-working-on-a-new-album|title=RIVERS OF NIHIL Is In The Studio Working On A New Album|date=September 8, 2017|website=Metalinjection.net}}</ref> On March 16, 2018, the band released ''[[Where Owls Know My Name]]'' as well as a music video for the song "A Home".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2018/03/16/watch-rivers-of-nihils-new-video-for-a-home/|title=Watch Rivers of Nihil's New Video for "A Home" - MetalSucks|date=March 16, 2018|website=Metalsucks.net}}</ref> Metal Injection gave the album a perfect 10/10 score.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalinjection.net/reviews/rivers-nihil-where-owls-know-my-name|title=Album Review: RIVERS OF NIHIL Where Owls Know My Name|date=March 15, 2018|website=Metalinjection.net}}</ref>

On September 24, 2021, the band released their fourth album, titled "The Work".
