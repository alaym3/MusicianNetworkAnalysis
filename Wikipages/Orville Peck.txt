'''Orville Peck''' is a [[country music]]ian based in Canada.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|last=Lebar|first=Erin|url=https://www.winnipegfreepress.com/arts-and-life/entertainment/music/behind-the-mask-510377692.html|url-access=registration|title=Canadian country crooner a man of mystery|date=May 25, 2019|work=[[Winnipeg Free Press]]|access-date=June 19, 2019}}</ref> He wears a [[fringe (trim)|fringed]] [[domino mask|mask]] and has never shown his face publicly.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.orlandoweekly.com/Blogs/archives/2019/06/12/lonesome-drifter-orville-peck-to-ride-into-orlando-in-september|title=Lonesome drifter Orville Peck rides into Orlando this September|last=Moyer|first=Matthew|website=Orlando Weekly|language=en|access-date=June 20, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.lofficielusa.com/music/orville-peck-interview-2019|title=Get to Know Orville Peck, Country's Resident Mystery Music Man|date=March 21, 2019|website=[[L'Officiel]]|access-date=February 26, 2020}}</ref> He released his debut album ''[[Pony (Orville Peck album)|Pony]]'' in 2019,<ref name=":0" /> followed by the EP ''[[Show Pony]]'' the next year.

== Early life ==
Peck was born somewhere in the [[Southern Hemisphere]] and lived there most of his life.<ref name=":3" /> He is the son of a sound engineer, and he did [[voice-over]] work for cartoons and other media as a child.<ref name=":u">{{Cite web|url=https://foreverdogpodcasts.com/podcasts/sloppy-seconds/|title=Bamboozlement (w/ Orville Peck)|date=May 1, 2020|work=Sloppy Seconds with Big Dipper & Meatball|language=en|format=Podcast|via=foreverdogpodcasts.com|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref> Growing up, he trained in ballet for 12 years, and performed in musical theatre. By the time he was in his early 20s, Peck had been on national tours of musicals.<ref name=":u" />

In his mid-20s, he moved to London to study acting at the [[London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art]], and later starred in a play on the [[West End theatre|West End]].<ref name=":u" />

== Career ==
"Orville Peck" is a pseudonym;<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/zma7a3/orville-peck-pony-turn-to-hate-interview|title=Orville Peck Is a Lone Stranger Singing Country Songs From Behind a Mask|last1=Suarez|first1=Gary|last2=Joyce|first2=Colin|date=March 20, 2019|website=Vice|language=en-US|access-date=June 19, 2019}}</ref> he has been described as "presumably older than 20 and younger than 40".<ref name=":2" />

Peck self-produced his debut album, ''Pony'', and released it in 2019 through a collaboration with [[Sub Pop]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/vogueworld/article/orville-peck-masked-country-music-musician-born-to-die|title=Meet Orville Peck, Country Music's Anonymous Masked Musician|last=Satenstein|first=Liana|website=Vogue|date=June 13, 2019|language=en|access-date=June 19, 2019}}</ref> He has noted that he "wrote, produced and played every instrument he could" on the album while working in a coffee shop and living with his parents.<ref>{{Cite tweet|last=Peck|first=Orville|title= Speechless... thank you.|date=June 15, 2020|user=orvillepeck|number=1272573115998982144|access-date=June 19, 2020}}</ref> In June of that year, he performed his songs "Dead of Night" and "Take You Back" live on [[CBC Radio One]]'s ''[[Q (radio show)|Q]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbc.ca/radio/q/friday-june-14-2019-orville-peck-joe-namath-and-more-1.5173801/meet-orville-peck-the-masked-country-music-star-1.5173815|title=Meet Orville Peck, the masked country music star|publisher=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|Radio Canada]]|website=[[CBC Music]]|date=June 14, 2019|access-date=June 19, 2020}}</ref> ''Pony'' was named to the initial longlist for the [[2019 Polaris Music Prize]] in June 2019.<ref name=longlist /> The album also received a [[Juno Award]] nomination for [[Juno Award for Alternative Album of the Year|Alternative Album of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2020]].<ref name="Juno Awards 2020">{{Cite web|first=Melody|last=Lau|url=https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/alessia-cara-and-tory-lanez-lead-the-2020-juno-nominations-1.5436386|title=Alessia Cara and Tory Lanez lead the 2020 Juno nominations|publisher=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|Radio Canada]]|website=[[CBC Music]]|date=January 28, 2019|access-date=June 19, 2020}}</ref>

Peck performed "Dead of Night" on ''[[Jimmy Kimmel Live]]'' on January 29, 2020.<ref name=freeman>{{Cite magazine|first=Jon|last=Freeman|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/orville-peck-dead-of-night-kimmel-pony-945470|title=Orville Peck Performs 'Dead of Night' on 'Kimmel'"]|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=January 30, 2020|access-date=June 19, 2020}}</ref> He also announced a tour of selected cities in the [[United States]], including performances at the [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]] and [[Stagecoach Festival|Stagecoach]] festivals.<ref name=freeman/>

In May 2020, Peck announced the follow-up to his debut album, an [[Extended play|EP]] titled ''Show Pony'', with a release date of June 12, 2020. In July, 2020, Peck announced the delayed release of ''Show Pony'' until August 14, 2020, in recognition of the [[Black Lives Matter]] movement and the ongoing protests against [[police brutality]] in the United States.<ref name="NME Show Pony Delay" /><ref name="Rolling Stone: Show Pony" />

Peck recorded a cover of "[[Smalltown Boy]]" for the 2020 Pride edition of the [[Spotify Singles|Spotify Singles series]]. The song was released exclusively on [[Spotify]] on June 29, 2020, and everywhere else on July 31, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Listen to Orville Peck cover Bronski Beat's '80s anthem 'Smalltown Boy'|first=Michael|last=Marotta|url=https://vanyaland.com/2020/06/29/listen-to-orville-peck-cover-bronski-beats-80s-anthem-smalltown-boy/|website=Vanyaland|date=June 29, 2020|access-date=July 31, 2020}}</ref>

In April 2021, Peck was featured on American drag queen and singer [[Trixie Mattel]]'s EP, ''Full Coverage, Vol 1.'' He appeared on the song "Jackson", a duet cover of [[Johnny Cash]] and [[June Carter Cash]]'s "[[Jackson (song)|Jackson]]."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Curran|first=Sarah|date=2021-04-30|title=Orville Peck And Trixie Mattel Team Up For Foot Stomping Cover Of 'Jackson'|url=https://etcanada.com/news/775465/orville-peck-and-trixie-mattel-team-up-for-foot-stomping-cover-of-jackson/|access-date=2021-04-30|website=ET Canada|language=en-US}}</ref> In June, he was revealed to be the sixth artist on [[Lady Gaga]]'s upcoming album ''[[Born This Way The Tenth Anniversary]]'', with Peck reimagining the song "[[Born This Way (song)|Born This Way]]" in a country style.<ref>Allie Gregory, [https://exclaim.ca/music/article/orville_peck_turns_lady_gagas_born_this_way_into_a_country_anthem "Orville Peck Turns Lady Gaga's "Born This Way" into a Country Anthem"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', June 4, 2021.</ref>

== Identity speculations ==
Sources have speculated that Peck is a persona of Daniel Pitout, drummer of the Canadian punk band [[Nü Sensae]], based on the similarity of Peck's tattoos,<ref name="Hagwood2019">{{Cite news|last=Hagwood|first=Alex|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/10/12/style/orville-peck-a-masked-gay-country-music.html|title=Orville Peck, a Masked Gay Country Star, Rides Into Brooklyn|date=October 12, 2019|work=[[The New York Times]]|access-date=January 30, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Saxberg|first=Lynn|url=https://ottawacitizen.com/entertainment/local-arts/meet-orville-peck-the-mysterious-new-country-artist-who-plays-in-ottawa-thursday|title=Meet Orville Peck, the mysterious new country artist who plays in Ottawa Thursday|website=[[Ottawa Citizen]]|date=April 10, 2019|access-date=June 19, 2020}}</ref> as well as Peck mentioning that he was in a punk band. Pitout was born in [[Johannesburg]], [[South Africa]],<ref name=":y">{{Cite news|first=Eric|last=Torres|url=http://gayletter.com/eating-out-with-daniel-pitout/|title=Eating Out with Daniel Pitout|work=[[Gayletter]]|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref> and Peck has said he grew up in a well-known part of the [[Southern Hemisphere]].<ref name=":u" /> Pitout also starred as a replacement in ''[[Peter Pan Goes Wrong]]'' in the [[West End theatre|West End]] in 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/people/Daniel-Pitout/|title=Daniel Pitout Theatre Credits|website=[[BroadwayWorld]]|language=en|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref> which lines up with Peck mentioning he was in a West End play while he lived in London.<ref name=":u" /> Peck has not confirmed his identity on the record.<ref>{{Cite news|first=Leonie|last=Cooper|url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2019/nov/19/orville-peck-i-grew-up-feeling-alienated-so-i-became-a-lone-cowboy|title=Orville Peck: 'I grew up feeling alienated – so I became a cowboy'|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=November 19, 2019|access-date=June 19, 2020}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Peck is [[gay]].<ref name="Hagwood2019" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Masked country king Orville Peck would like his own version of The Bachelor|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/06/19/orville-peck-would-you-rather-video|access-date=June 19, 2019|website=[[The Fader]]|language=en}}</ref> Regarding his reasons for wearing a mask to perform, Peck has stated that "the only reason I don't talk about it in depth is not because I want to dodge any questions, but because I want people to have their own take on it. I don't want to lay it out and pin it down. I just don't think that's important."<ref>{{Cite web|first=Amy|last=Campbell|url=https://www.gq.com.au/entertainment/music/queer-cowboy-orville-peck-is-redefining-country-music-for-a-new-generation-of-fans/image-gallery/6d4cbba51f16a1bb89c45e5e399d8cd0|title=Queer Cowboy Orville Peck Is Redefining Country Music For A New Generation Of Fans|website=[[GQ Australia]]|date=January 16, 2020|access-date=September 29, 2020}}</ref>
