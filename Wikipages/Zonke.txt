==Early life and education==
Zonke was born in KwaZakhele, [[Eastern Cape]], South Africa. Her father, Vuyisile Dikana, was a drummer for a group called Black Slave and the Flamingo while her late stepmother Anneline Malebo was a singer for a musical band known as Joy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.capitalfm.co.ke/lifestyle/2012/11/21/zonke-africas-next-big-star/|title=ZONKE: Africa's next big star|work=Capital Lifestyle|date=21 November 2012|access-date=8 June 2016}}</ref> She is an alumna of the [[University of the Free State]], from where she graduated with a degree in human resource management in 1997.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://loudestgist.com/wiki/2016/05/luthando-shosha-biography/|title=Zonke Dikana Biography|website=LoudestGist|date=31 May 2016|access-date=8 June 2016}} {{dead link|date=October 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
===1996–2007: early beginnings, ''Soulitary'' and ''Life, Love 'n Music''===
During Zonke's early years as a singer, Anneline Malebo introduced her to several artists, including Ringo, Joe Nina and Deborah Fraser, who she worked with either in writing or vocal capabilities. Zonke had her big break when she wrote a song titled "Africa, My Motherland" for the band Jazzkantine, based in Germany.<ref name="tmp">{{cite web|url=http://www.tmperforming.com/?page_id=9|title=Zonke|publisher=TM Performing Arts Management|access-date=8 June 2016}}</ref> The band later became known as Culture Clan after Zonke and the rappers Mandla and Mzo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/201211130813.html|title=Kenya: Zonke Dikana for Tusker Malt Lager 100 Club|website=Capital FM|date=13 November 2012|access-date=8 June 2016}}</ref>

In 2003, Culture Clan released an album titled ''Africa''. It was released in Germany, winning awards from the [[Channel O Music Video Awards|Channel O]] and [[Metro FM Music Awards]].<ref name="tmp"/> During her time in Germany, Zonke's first studio album, ''[[Soulitary]]'', was released by Upper Street Records with distribution in Germany, Japan and Italy in 2004. In 2007, [[Kalawa Jazmee Records]] released her second album, ''[[Life, Love 'n Music]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://l.fienipa.com/Zonke|title=African Musician: Zonke|website=Museke|author=Zbee|date=9 October 2008|access-date=8 June 2016}} {{dead link|date=October 2020}}</ref>

===2008–13: ''Ina Ethe'' and ''Give and Take Live''===
In June 2011, Zonke left Kalawa Jamzee for TMP Records and started to record ''[[Ina Ethe]]'', her third studio album. Preceding the album's release were two singles, "Jik'Izinto" and "[[Feelings (Zonke song)|Feelings]]". ''Ina Ethe'' was self-produced and was released by TMP Records to critical reviews.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://channelo.dstv.com/2012/03/cd-review-ina-ethe/|title=Album review: Ina Ethe|work=[[Channel O]]|date=6 March 2012|access-date=9 June 2016}}<br>- {{cite news|url=http://www.sowetanlive.co.za/entertainment/2011/12/01/can-t-take-the-soul-out-of-her|title=Can't take the soul out of her|work=Sowetan Live|last=Tsumele|first=Edward|date=11 December 2011|access-date=9 June 2016}}</ref> The album did well commercially, going double platinum.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.showbizone.co.za/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=923:zonkes-double-platinum-strikeun-offer&catid=latest-news&Itemid=484|title=ZONKE'S DOUBLE PLATINUM STRIKE/UN OFFER|website=Showbizone|date=21 January 2013|access-date=8 June 2016}}</ref>

On 25 October 2013, Zonke recorded a live album at the Lyric Theatre in  Goldreef City, Johannesburg, released by [[Sony Music Entertainment]] as ''[[Give and Take Live]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.timeslive.co.za/entertainment/music/2013/07/06/zonke-more-than-delivers-at-give-and-take-livedvd-recording|title=Zonke more than delivers at Give and Take Live DVD recording|work=Times Live|last=Ndlovu|first=Andile|date=6 July 2013|access-date=9 June 2016}}</ref> The album achieved gold status within two months of its release.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldlive.co.za/golden-start-to-year/|title=Golden start to year|work=Herald Live|author=Thulani Gqirana|date=14 January 2014|access-date=8 June 2016}}</ref> ''Give and Take Live'' also won two category awards at the 20th South African Music Awards.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sundayworld.co.za/news/2014/04/29/mafikizolo-biggest-winners-of-samas|title=Mafikizolo biggest winners of SAMAs|work=Sunday World|date=29 April 2014|access-date=8 June 2016}}</ref>

===2018-2019: ''L.O.V.E'' ===
Her seventh  studio album, ''L.O.V.E'', was released on 15 June 15, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zonke Dikana: L.O.V.E, path to healing |url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/amp/sundayworld/lifestyle/up-close/2018-06-28-zonke-dikana-love-path-to-healing/|work=SowetanLIVE|first=Boitumelo |last=Kgobotlo|date=28 June 2018}}</ref> It is a 9-track album featuring [[Kwesta]] on a song titled "Soul to Keep". In April 2018, Zonke released the lead single "Tonight", which had platinum sales. The album was certified certified gold with sales of 25 000 units.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zonke's L.O.V.E album certified GOLD - ZAlebs|url=https://zalebs.com/top-of-the/zonke-dikana/zonkes-love-album-certified-gold|work=Zalebs|date=June 22, 2020}}</ref>

''L.O.V.E.'' was nominated  for Album of the Year, Female Artist of the Year and won Best R&B/Soul Album at the [[25th South African Music Awards|25th annually ceremony]]

==Tours and notable performances==
On 5 July 2013, Zonke performed and recorded her live album ''Give and Take Live'' at a sold-out concert at the Lyric Theatre in Johannesburg after it was postponed following the death of her sister Busisiwe Dikana.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sowetanlive.co.za/entertainment/2013/05/22/zonke-dikana-s-concert-is-postponed|title=Zonke Dikana's concert is postponed|work=[[Sowetan LIVE]]|last=Ngubane|first=Sandiso|date=22 May 2013|access-date=11 June 2016}}</ref> On 20 March 2016, Zonke performed at the Ticketpro Dome in Johannesburg alongside American soul singer [[Lionel Richie]], who was touring South Africa as part of his "All The Hits All Night Long" tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.ewn.co.za/2016/03/22/Lionel-Richie-seals-SA-tour-with-a-promise|title=Lionel Richie seals SA tour with a promise|work=Eyewitness News|last=Theletsane|first=Winnie|date=22 March 2016|access-date=2 July 2016}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Zonke Dikana is a versatile singer who sings in English and [[Xhosa language|Xhosa]] language. During her time as a Kalawa Jazmee Records artist, her genre of music was mostly [[house music]] before she switched to [[afrosoul]] and [[neo soul]] on signing to TMP Entertainment.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.sundayworld.co.za/lifestyle/2011/11/27/ready-to-reveal-the-real-zonke|title=Ready to reveal the real Zonke|work=Sunday World|last=Tlhoaele|first=Boitumelo|date=27 November 2011|access-date=9 June 2016}}</ref> Her musical influences include [[Busi Mhlongo]], [[Miriam Makeba]] and [[Brenda Fassie]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.powerfm.co.za/guests/zonke-dikana/|title=Zonke Dikana|publisher=Power FM|date=16 October 2015|access-date=2 July 2016}}</ref>
