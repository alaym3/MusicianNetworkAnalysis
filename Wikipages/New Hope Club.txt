'''New Hope Club''' is a British [[Pop rock music|pop]] trio formed in 2015, consisting of Reece Bibby, Blake Richardson, and George Smith. Their debut EP, ''Welcome to the Club'', was released through [[Steady Records]]/[[Hollywood Records]] on 5 May 2017; the band released their [[New Hope Club (album)|self-titled debut album]] in February 2020, which peaked at number five on the [[UK Album Charts]].

==History==
George Smith and Blake Richardson formed New Hope Club in October 2015.<ref name="ncraig">Nicola Craig, [http://celebmix.com/artist-101-new-hope-club/ "Artist 101: New Hope Club"], Celebmix, February 2017.</ref> As a member of the boy band [[Stereo Kicks]],<ref name="lcorner">Lewis Corner, [http://www.digitalspy.com/music/news/a776609/the-vamps-have-signed-new-band-new-hope-club-to-their-record-label/ "The Vamps have signed new band New Hope Club to their record label"], Digital Spy, 8 December 2015.</ref> Reece Bibby appeared on the eleventh season of ''[[The X Factor (UK series 11)|The X Factor]]'' in the UK in 2014, finishing fifth.{{cn|date=February 2020}} Stereo Kicks broke up in July 2015 after failing to land a record deal;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/StereoKicksOfficial/posts/776683005782203|title=Stereo Kicks|website=facebook.com|access-date=6 November 2017}}{{Primary source inline|date=February 2018}}</ref> in November 2015, Bibby joined New Hope Club.<ref>Kristine Hope Kowlaski, [http://www.twistmagazine.com/posts/reece-bibby-starts-a-new-band-after-stereo-kicks-breakup-77367 "Take a Listen: Reece Bibby Starts a New Band After Stereo Kicks' Breakup"], ''[[Twist (magazine)|Twist]]'', 13 November 2015.</ref><ref>Brie Hiramine, [http://www.j-14.com/posts/stereo-kicks-reece-bibby-starts-new-hope-club-band-77410 "'X Factor' Boy Band Member Starts Another Group After Leaving His Old Band"], ''[[J-14 (magazine)|J-14]]'', 13 November 2015.</ref> Bibby, Smith, and Richardson are all originally from [[Northern England]].<ref>"Meet Hollywood Record's British Band New Hope Club", ''[[Just Jared Jr.]]'', 7 May 2017.</ref><ref name="shudson">Shawna Hudson, [https://www.sweetyhigh.com/read/new-hope-club-mcm-050817 "Find Out All About British Boy Band New Hope Club"], Sweety High, 8 May 2017.</ref> New Hope Club's first recording was a cover of "[[Wake Up (The Vamps song)|Wake Up]]" by [[The Vamps (British band)|The Vamps]] in October 2015.<ref>[http://essentiallypop.com/epop/2017/04/six-quick-questions-for-new-hope-club/ "Six Quick Questions For New Hope Club"], essentiallypop.com, 25 April 2017.</ref> In December 2015, The Vamps signed the band to their label [[Steady Records]], a subsidiary of [[Virgin EMI Records]].<ref name="lcorner"/> They were the second band signed to the label, after [[The Tide (band)|The Tide]].<ref name="lcorner"/>

The band have supported The Vamps on three of their UK/Europe tours (2017, 2018, 2019) and performed a song each night ('Cecilia') on the Wake Up Tour (2016). They also accompanied The Vamps on their 2018 USA tour

New Hope Club's debut 4-song EP ''Welcome to the Club'' was released on [[Hollywood Records]] via [[Steady Records]] on 5 May 2017.<ref name="shudson"/><ref name="jammerzine">[https://jammerzine.com/new-hope-club-announce-debut-ep/ "New Hope Club Announce Debut EP"], Jammerzine, 3 May 2017.</ref> The video for "Perfume" was released in January 2017, and the video for "Fixed" was released with their EP.<ref name="jammerzine"/> The band spent 10 weeks on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Next Big Sound chart, peaking at number 5 on 16 September 2017.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/artist/7786268/new-hope-club/chart New Hope Club Chart History], ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. Retrieved 8 June 2017.</ref> At the [[2017 Teen Choice Awards]], they were nominated for Choice Next Big Thing.<ref>[http://perezhilton.com/2017-08-14-teen-choice-awards-2017-the-complete-winners-list "Teen Choice Awards 2017: The Complete Winners List!"] [[perezhilton.com]], 14 August 2017.</ref>

The band released the fourth and final music video of their EP, for the song "Water", on 6 October 2017.

A Christmas song called "Whoever He Is" was released along with a video on 1 December 2017.

The band played their first ever headlining shows<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://twitter.com/NewHopeClub/status/1003315008288317441|title=New Hope Club on Twitter|work=Twitter|access-date=2018-06-11|language=en}}</ref> at the [[Shepherd's Bush Empire|O2 Shepherd's Bush Empire]], in London, on the 1st and 2nd of June of 2018. These shows were sold out and supported by acts such as [[Max and Harvey|Max & Harvey]] and RoadTrip.
In early 2018, they released the music video for their single "Start Over Again" and two songs from the “Early Man” movie soundtrack ("Good Day" and "Tiger Feet”). In the summer of the same year, they also released the music video for their song, "Medicine." This was their fastest music video to reach 1 million views on YouTube. In October 2018, they released their second EP ''Welcome to the Club Pt. 2''.

They released the single "Permission" on 1 February 2019,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Stivale |first1=Shelby |title=NEW HOPE CLUB TO RELEASE NEW SINGLE ‘PERMISSION’ THIS FRIDAY |url=http://tigerbeat.com/2019/01/nhc-permission/ |website=TigerBeat.com |access-date=17 May 2019}}</ref> and the single "Love Again" on 3 May 2019.<ref name="loveagain">{{cite web |last1=Stivale |first1=Shelby |title=NEW HOPE CLUB TO RELEASE LATEST AND GREATEST SINGLE ‘LOVE AGAIN’ |url=http://tigerbeat.com/2019/04/nhc-love-again/ |website=TigerBeat.com |access-date=17 May 2019}}</ref>

==Touring==
New Hope Club opened for [[The Vamps (British band)|The Vamps]] on their 2016 UK tour,<ref name="lcorner"/> and for their tour of the UK and Ireland in the spring of 2017.<ref>Hayley Smith, [http://www.unitedbypop.com/interviews/new-hope-club-uk-ireland-tour/ "Interview: New Hope Club Hit The Road With The Vamps For UK Arena Tour"], United By Pop, 27 April 2017.</ref> They regularly performed "[[Oh Cecilia (Breaking My Heart)]]" with The Vamps during that tour.<ref name="ncraig"/> They also opened for [[Martina Stoessel|Tini Stoessel]] on her 2017 Got Me Started Tour in Germany.<ref>[http://www.juiceorcider.com/2017/03/17/new-hope-club-tour-in-germany-for-the-first-time/ "New Hope Club to Tour in Germany for the First Time"], Juice or Cider, 17 March 2017.</ref> They have also opened for [[Sabrina Carpenter]] on her North American summer 2017 De-Tour,<ref>[http://www.juiceorcider.com/2017/04/27/new-hope-club-will-tour-america-with-sabrina-carpenter-this-summer/ "New Hope Club Will Tour America With Sabrina Carpenter This Summer"], Juice or Cider, 27 April 2017.</ref> and opened yet again for The Vamps on their fall 2017 tour in Australia.<ref>[http://themusic.com.au/news/all/2017/06/02/the-vamps-are-bringing-their-huge-world-tour-to-aus-this-year/ "The Vamps Are Bringing Their Huge World Tour To Aus This Year"], themusic.com.au, 2 June 2017.</ref> In August 2017, they performed at [[Arthur Ashe Kids' Day]] at [[Arthur Ashe Stadium]] in New York City.<ref>Rachael Ellenbogen, [http://www.ibtimes.com/new-hope-club-dishes-new-music-songwriting-process-dream-collaborations-2584241 "New Hope Club Dishes On New Music, Songwriting Process And Dream Collaborations"], ''[[International Business Times]]'', 1 September 2017.</ref> In the beginning of 2019 they did a special mini tour in Europe, doing shows in Paris, Amsterdam, Antwerp and Cologne. They embarked their first ever world tour, the Love Again Tour last June 8, 2019 in Manila, Philippines at the New Frontier Theater, continuing to Bangkok, Thailand and Seoul, South Korea. In the summer of 2019 they did shows in the US, and the tour visited a few countries in Europe as well. In the autumn of 2019 the tour visited the UK and Ireland.<ref name="loveagain"/>

==Members==
* Blake Richardson, born on {{birth date and age|df=yes|1999|10|02}} – lead vocals, guitar, piano.
* George Smith, born on {{birth date and age|df=yes|1999|03|09}} – vocals, guitar, piano.
* Reece Bibby, born on {{birth date and age|df=yes|1998|08|13}} – vocals, bass, drum, cajon, guitar. He was a member of the ''[[X Factor]]'' group, [[Stereo Kicks]].
