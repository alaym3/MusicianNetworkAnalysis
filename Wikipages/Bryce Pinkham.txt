'''Bryce Allen Pinkham''' (born October 19, 1982) is an American actor and singer. He has appeared in the PBS period drama ''[[Mercy Street (TV series)|Mercy Street]]''. On [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]], he played Monty Navarro in ''[[A Gentleman's Guide to Love and Murder]]''. For the latter role, he received nominations for a [[Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actor in a Musical]] and a [[Grammy Award|Grammy]] (Best Musical Theater Album). He is a [[Leonore Annenberg]] Arts Fellow and co-founder of ''Zara Aina'', a not-for-profit children's theater company in Madagascar. He starred in the original [[YouTube]] animated web series ''[[Helluva Boss]]''.

==Background==
Pinkham was born in [[Redding, California]].<ref>[https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:VGGQ-CMW California Birth Index, 1905-1995] via FamilySearch database</ref> He was raised in the East San Francisco Bay Area.<ref>Blank, Matthew. He is a graduate of [[Boston College]] and the [[Yale School of Drama]].<ref name=bway>[http://www.playbill.com/person/bryce-pinkham-vault-0000114997 "Bryce Pinkham Broadway"] ''Playbill'' (vault), retrieved January 3, 2017</ref>

==Career==
Pinkham originated roles in ''[[Ghost (musical)|Ghost: The Musical]]'' and ''[[Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson]]'' on Broadway.<ref name=bway/> In 2013, he originated the role of Monty Navarro in the Broadway production of ''[[A Gentleman's Guide to Love and Murder]]'', for which he was nominated for a [[Tony Award for Best Performance by a Leading Actor in a Musical]] as well as a [[Grammy Award]]. He played the role on Broadway for over 700 performances. In 2014, he played Paul Revere in an introductory commercial titled ''Italian Invasion'' for the [[Fiat 500L]], created by the [[Doner Company]].<ref name="refitalian">{{cite web|title=Fiat's New Spot Shouts, 'The Italians Are Coming!'| publisher = Agency Spy|author=Ella Riley-Adams| date=June 26, 2013|url = http://www.adweek.com/agencyspy/fiats-new-spot-shouts-the-italians-are-coming/51124}}</ref><ref name="pinkham">{{cite web|title=FIAT® 500L - Italian Invasion / Revolution - (2013)|publisher=Adland.tv|author=|date=|url=https://adland.tv/commercials/fiat-500l-italian-invasion-revolution-2013-30-usa}}</ref>

He played Peter Patrone in the Broadway revival of ''[[The Heidi Chronicles]]'', and was nominated for an [[Outer Critics Circle Award]] and the [[Drama League Award]] for Distinguished Performance.<ref name=bway/> In 2016, he returned to Broadway leading the cast of [[Roundabout Theater Company]]'s musical, Irving Berlin's ''[[Holiday Inn (musical)|Holiday Inn]]''. His film and television appearances include as a series regular in the second season of the TV series ''[[Mercy Street (TV series)|Mercy Street]]'', playing Major Clayton McBurney III, head of the Union Army hospital.<ref>Vine, Hannah. [http://www.playbill.com/article/exclusive-patina-miller-brian-f-obyrne-bryce-pinkham-in-pbs-mercy-street# Exclusive: Patina Miller, Brian F. O’Byrne, Bryce Pinkham in PBS’ 'Mercy Street'"], ''Playbill'', November 9, 2016</ref> Other roles include performances in the comedy ''[[The Comedian (2016 film)|The Comedian]]'' (2016) which stars [[Robert De Niro]], and [[Baz Luhrmann]]'s Netflix drama ''[[The Get Down]]''. Previously, he has appeared on ''[[The Good Wife]]'' and ''[[Person of Interest (TV series)|Person of Interest]]''. He appeared in the [[PBS]] miniseries ''God in America'', "A Nation Reborn Drama" (2010).<ref>[https://www.pbs.org/godinamerica/etc/credits.html#2 ''God in America''], ''PBS'', retrieved January 4, 2017</ref><ref>[https://www.pbs.org/godinamerica/press/ " ''God in America'' Press Release"], ''PBS'', retrieved January 4, 2017</ref> In 2012, Pinkham was awarded the [[Leonore Annenberg]] Fellowship given to "a limited number of exceptionally talented young dancers, musicians, actors and visual artists as they complete their training and begin their professional life."<ref>http://www.leonoreannenbergscholarships.org/profile/pinkham-bryce/</ref>
