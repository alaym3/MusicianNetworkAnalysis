'''Frank Carter and the Rattlesnakes''' are an English [[punk rock]]<!-- please don't add ANY genres without providing a source --> band formed in 2015 by frontman [[Frank Carter (musician)|Frank Carter]] with guitarist Dean Richardson. Famed for their raucous live shows, the band released their debut album ''[[Blossom (Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes album)|Blossom]]'' in 2015, and the follow-ups ''[[Modern Ruin (Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes album)|Modern Ruin]]'' in 2017 and ''[[End of Suffering]]'' in 2019. Their fourth album, ''[[Sticky (Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes album)|Sticky]]'', is due for release in October 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-detail-fourth-album-sticky/|title=NEWS: Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes detail fourth album, 'Sticky'!|date=5 July 2021}}</ref>

==History==
===Formation, ''Rotten'' EP, and ''Blossom'' (2015)===
{{Main|Blossom (Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes album)}}
After leaving the band [[Gallows (band)|Gallows]] in 2011 due to artistic differences, Carter started [[Pure Love (band)|Pure Love]], a project with guitarist Jim Carroll, ending that band in 2015 to form Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes with guitarist Dean Richardson, formerly of [[Heights (band)|Heights]].<ref name="nme">{{cite news |last1=Cooper |first1=Leonie |title=Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes – 'Modern Ruin' Review |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/frank-carter-modern-ruin-review |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=NME |date=20 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Senior |first1=Nicholas |title=Album Review: Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes – 'Modern Ruin' |url=https://newnoisemagazine.com/review-frank-carter-modern-ruin/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=New Noise Magazine |date=30 January 2017}}</ref><ref name="clashmusic">{{cite news |last1=Gray |first1=Josh |title=Between Love And Hate: Frank Carter Interviewed |url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/between-love-and-hate-frank-carter-interviewed |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Clash Music |date=24 April 2019}}</ref> Carter and Richardson had met a few years earlier, when Carter asked Richardson, who was also a designer and coder, to make him a website.<ref name="blurt">{{cite news |last1=Clegg |first1=Jeff |title=READY TO STRIKE: Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes |url=https://blurtonline.com/feature/ready-strike-frank-carter-rattlesnakes/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Blurt |date=21 June 2017}}</ref> With The Rattlesnakes, Carter returned to his hardcore roots, while retaining some of the power-pop sensibilities of Pure Love.<ref name="blurt"/>

On 4 May 2015, Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes released the three-track EP ''Rotten''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/features/premiere-frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-rotten-ep|title=Premiere: Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes - 'Rotten' EP|work=Clash Magazine}}</ref> The three songs would also appear on the band's debut full-length album, ''[[Blossom (Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes album)|Blossom]]'', released on International Death Cult on 14 August 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/frank-carter/16190|title=NME Reviews - Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes - 'Blossom'|author=NME.COM|work=NME.COM|date=28 July 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Jamieson |first1=Sarah |title=FRANK CARTER & THE RATTLESNAKES - BLOSSOM |url=https://diymag.com/2015/08/14/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-blossom-album-review |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=DYI |date=14 August 2015}}</ref> It peaked at #18 on the UK Albums Chart.<ref name="ukcharts">{{cite web |title=Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes UK Albums |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes/chart-history/official-uk-albums |website=billboard.com |access-date=31 October 2019}}</ref> Carter has described ''Blossom'' as being "about sadness and not knowing what to do with it."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Shutler |first1=Ali |title=Frank Carter and the Rattlesnakes are on a mission: "A revolution starts with a big fucking change" |url=https://www.upsetmagazine.com/features/frank-carter-and-the-rattlesnakes-interview-mar19 |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Upset Magazine |date=3 May 2019}}</ref>

===''Modern Ruin'' and live album (2016–2018)===
{{Main|Modern Ruin (Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes album)}}
The band finished recording ''[[Modern Ruin (Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes album)|Modern Ruin]]'' in January 2016, just a few months after the launch their first album.<ref name="riots">{{cite news |title=Frank Carter And The Rattlesnakes: "Now I just don't care if I upset people. What I care about is the music that I'm making, and making sure it's heard a lot." |url=http://musicandriots.com/powerful-charismatic-still-very-effective-an-interview-with-frank-carter/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Music & Riots |date=13 May 2017}}</ref> ''Modern Ruin'' was released on International Death Cult on 20 January 2017.<ref name="nme"/> It peaked at number 7 on the UK Albums Chart,<ref name="ukcharts"/> and number 23 on the ''Billboard'' Heatseekers Albums chart.<ref>{{cite web |title=Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes: Heatseekers Albums |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes/chart-history/heatseekers-albums |website=billboard.com |access-date=31 October 2019}}</ref> The album has a moodier feel than ''Blossom''.<ref>{{cite news |title=frank carter & the rattlesnakes |url=https://www.punkrocktheory.com/interviews/frank-carter-rattlesnakes |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Punk Rock Theory |date=9 February 2017}}</ref> It was recorded by Thomas Mitchener, formerly the band's touring bassist, in his Broadfields studio in [[Watford, England]], which is also where ''Blossom'' was recorded.<ref name="riots"/><ref name="clash1">{{Cite news|url=http://clashmusic.com/features/stand-up-and-speak-out-frank-carter-and-the-rattlesnakes|title=Stand Up And Speak Out: Frank Carter And The Rattlesnakes|last=Browne|first=Lois|date=29 January 2017|newspaper=Clash Magazine|language=en|access-date=2017-01-29}}</ref>    

In November 2017 the band announced a live album.<ref name="kerrang">{{cite news |title=Frank Carter & the Ratlesnakes Announce Live Album |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-announce-live-album/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Kerrang! |date=16 November 2017}}</ref> ''23 Live At Brixton Academy'' was released on 9 March 2018, having been recorded at their 8 December 2017 headline show at the [[O2 Academy Brixton]] in London, where they played every song from their first two albums plus their new single "Spray Paint Love".<ref name="kerrang"/><ref>{{cite news |title=Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes Share New Live Album From Brixton Academy |url=https://diymag.com/2018/03/13/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-live-album-brixton-academy |access-date=31 October 2019 |work=DIY |date=13 March 2018}}</ref>

===''End of Suffering'' and ''Blossom'' reissue (2019–2020)===
{{Main|End of Suffering}}
The band released their third album, ''[[End of Suffering]]'', on International Death Cult on 3 May 2019.<ref name="clashmusic"/> The album peaked at #4 on the UK Albums Chart.<ref name="ukcharts"/> ''[[NME]]'' wrote that the album "resembles a firework display on this dazzling record" where Carter "continues to mine increasingly sensitive territory, with impressive results."<ref name="nmesuffering">{{cite news |last1=McMahon |first1=James |title=Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes – 'End Of Suffering' review |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-end-of-suffering-review |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=NME |date=30 April 2019}}</ref> Some of the tracks address Carter's mental-health battle.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Divita |first1=Joe |title=Frank Carter and the Rattlesnakes: Managing Anxiety + What Helps Us |url=https://loudwire.com/frank-carter-rattlesnakes-managing-anxiety-what-helps/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Loudwire |date=15 July 2019}}</ref> Three singles were released off the album: "Crowbar", "Anxiety", and "Kitty Sucker".<ref>{{cite news |title=LISTEN TO FRANK CARTER AND THE RATTLESNAKES' NEW SONG, KITTY SUCKER |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/listen-to-frank-carter-and-the-rattlesnakes-new-song-kitty-sucker/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Kerrang! |date=1 May 2019}}</ref> [[Rage Against the Machine]] and [[Audioslave]] guitarist [[Tom Morello]] guests on the song "Tyrant Lizard King". The collaboration came about after Carter ran into Morello in Spain at the festival [[Resurrection (festival)|Resurrection]], and performed "[[Killing in the Name]]" with Morello's band [[Prophets of Rage]].<ref>{{cite news |title=WHY TOM MORELLO IS ON THE NEW FRANK CARTER ALBUM |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/why-tom-morello-is-on-the-new-frank-carter-album/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Kerrang! |date=16 January 2019}}</ref> Following the album's release, the band toured in support of it, culminating in a sold-out show to 10,000 fans at London's [[Alexandra Palace]] in February 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ed |first1=Mason |title=In Pictures: Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes' Biggest-Ever Show |url=https://www.kerrang.com/features/in-pictures-frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-biggest-ever-show/ |website=Kerrang |access-date=7 July 2021}}</ref> However, any further tour plans throughout 2020 were cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], forcing a premature end to the ''End of Suffering'' album cycle. 

In light of this period of unanticipated inactivity, the band unearthed archive recordings from their debut album ''[[Blossom (Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes album)|Blossom]]'' and announced a reissue to celebrate the fifth anniversary of its release in June 2020. The reissue features live versions of the record's original ten tracks, in addition  to three previously unreleased studio tracks.<ref name="KBlossomReissueAnnounce">{{cite web |title=Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes Announce Deluxe Edition Of Debut Album Blossom |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/frank-carter-announces-deluxe-edition-of-the-the-rattlesnakes-debut-album/ |website=Kerrang! |access-date=20 July 2021}}</ref> One of the unreleased tracks, "Fire", was released ahead of the reissue as the lead single, with the rest of the album following in October 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes Blossom (Deluxe) |url=https://www.banquetrecords.com/frank-carter-%26-the-rattlesnakes/blossom-%28deluxe%29/FCATBLOSSOMDLX |website=Banquet Records |access-date=20 July 2021}}</ref>

===''Sticky'' (2021–present)===
{{Main|Sticky (album)}}
Following the cancellation of any further tour plans in support of ''End of Suffering'' due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the band began working on new music. This culminated in the release of the single "My Town", featuring [[Idles]] frontman [[Joe Talbot (musician)|Joe Talbot]] in April 2021.<ref name="NN">{{cite web |last1=Williams |first1=Keegan |title=News: Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes Release New Single "My Town" Feat. Joe Talbot |url=https://newnoisemagazine.com/news-frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-release-new-single-my-town-feat-joe-talbot/ |website=New Noise |date=29 April 2021 |access-date=7 July 2021}}</ref> The band then announced their fourth album, ''[[Sticky (album)|Sticky]]'', alongside its eponymous single in July 2021. The record, released on 15 October 2021, was produced by guitarist and primary band member Dean Richardson and features further guest appearances from Lynks, [[Cassyette]] and [[Bobby Gillespie]].<ref name="LS">{{Cite web |title=Frank Carter shares new single Sticky, announces album and UK/European tour |url=https://www.loudersound.com/news/frank-carter-shares-new-single-sticky-announces-album-and-ukeuropean-tour |access-date=7 July 2021 |website=Louder Sound|date=5 July 2021 }}</ref>

==Musical style==
The band's musical style has been associated with rock, punk rock and hardcore genres. According to Carter they "perfectly fill the gap between indie, punk and rock and roll."<ref name="clash1"/> Carter has described the band's songwriting style as a mixture of all the musical genres the band likes, "pouring it together in a melting pot that is real and new."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Morawitz |first1=Owen |title=FRANK CARTER & THE RATTLESNAKES |url=https://www.hysteriamag.com/frank-carter-rattlesnakes-death-genre/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Hysteria |date=29 April 2019}}</ref> He told the [[BBC]] in 2017, "Punk to me is not a sound, it's not a fashion, it's not a lifestyle. It's a mindset. You either have it or you don't."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Blake |first1=Jimmy |title=Frank Carter: Grime is more punk than punk at the moment |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/38582811/frank-carter-grime-is-more-punk-than-punk-at-the-moment |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=BBC |date=12 February 2017}}</ref>

==Performances==
Carter is known as a dynamic live performer, delivering high-energy sets.<ref name="nmesuffering"/><ref name="cos">{{cite news |last1=Kaufman |first1=Spencer |title=Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes to headline 2019 Monster Energy Outbreak Tour |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2019/07/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-2019-monster-energy-outbreak-tour/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Consequence of Sound |date=15 July 2019}}</ref> The band has also aimed to foster a safe, respectful, and inclusive environment at their live performances. They encourage equality and empowerment with their fans, with Carter calling for only female and non-binary fans to crowdsurf in a safe environment during certain songs.<ref name="nmeempower">{{cite news |last1=Trendell |first1=Andrew |title=Frank Carter: "Rock gigs have been dominated by men for years – all bands need to work to empower women" |url=https://www.nme.com/music-interviews/frank-carter-rock-gigs-dominated-by-men-for-years-all-bands-need-to-work-to-empower-women-2545552 |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=NME |date=6 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Murphy |first1=Sam |title=Frank Carter Delivers Stirring Speech Before Inviting A Girls-Only Stage Dive At Brisbane Show Read |url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/frank-carter-delivers-stirring-speech-inviting-girls-stage-dive-brisbane-show/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Music Feeds |date=12 December 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Ralph |first1=Caitlyn |title=Frank Carter Delivers Speech About Respecting Women at Concerts |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/frank_carter_delivers_speech_about_respecting_women_at_concertswatch/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Alt Press |date=2 May 2017}}</ref> In 2019 Carter was quoted as saying, "Rock gigs have been dominated by men for years - all bands need to work to empower women."<ref name="nmeempower"/>

The Rattlesnakes' first tour was the Rotten UK Tour from May to June 2015.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Redrup |first1=Zach |title=Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes add more dates to June 2015 UK tour! |url=http://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-frank-carter-and-the-rattlesnakes-add-more-dates-to-june-2015-uk-tour/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Deadpress |date=29 April 2015}}</ref> In March 2016 they toured the UK and Ireland.<ref>{{cite news |title=Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes Announce UK and Ireland Tour |url=https://diymag.com/2016/11/16/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-announce-uk-and-ireland-tour |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=DYI |date=16 November 2016}}</ref> They performed on the [[Kerrang! Tour]] 2016 in support of [[Sum 41]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Meet Your Final Band on the Kerrang! Tour 2016 |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/meet-your-final-band-on-the-kerrang-tour-2016/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Kerrang! |date=18 November 2015}}</ref> In February 2017, they supported [[Biffy Clyro]] on tour.<ref>{{cite news |title=Frank Carter documents Biffy Clyro support tour with new video for 'Vampires' |url=https://www.upsetmagazine.com/news/frank-carter-documents-biffy-clyro-support-tour-new-video-vampires |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Upset Magazine |date=8 March 2017}}</ref> In October 2017, the band cancelled their North American tour, following the cancellation of four weeks of touring in Europe, so that Carter could focus on his mental health and wellbeing.<ref>{{cite news |title=Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes Cancel U.S. Tour |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-cancel-u-s-tour/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |work=Kerrang! |date=4 October 2017}}</ref> The band had to pull out of some shows in the summer of 2018, including the [[INmusic Festival]] in Croatia, after they were involved in a bus crash.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bassett |first1=Jordan |title=Queens of the Stone Age shimmied and shook their way through the first night of Zagreb's INmusic Festival |url=https://www.nme.com/festivals/inmusic/queens-of-the-stone-age-shimmied-and-shook-their-way-through-the-first-night-of-zagrebs-inmusic-festival-2343817 |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=NME |date=26 June 2018}}</ref>

After playing a secret set at [[Reading Festival]] in 2018, the band played the festival's main stage in 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Frank Carter: 'We're Gonna Have Two of the Biggest Circle Pits the Festival Has Ever Seen' |url=https://www.readingfestival.com/news/frank-carter-interview-2019 |website=readingfestival.com |access-date=31 October 2019}}</ref> In the spring and summer of 2019, they toured the UK and Europe, and performed at the Hellendoorn Dauwpop Festival, [[Primavera Sound]], [[Deichbrand]], and [[Sziget Festival]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Frank Carter Drops New Single; Announces Third Album and Headline Tour |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/frank-carter-drops-new-single-announces-third-album-and-headline-tour/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Kerrang! |date=15 January 2019}}</ref> They supported [[Foo Fighters]] on their 2019 European Tour,<ref>{{cite news |last1=Krol |first1=Charlotte |title=Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes and Slaves lead Foo Fighters support acts at huge shows in Scotland and Ireland |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/foo-fighters-slaves-frank-carter-the-rattlesnakes-to-support-f-2457767 |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=NME |date=6 March 2019}}</ref> and supported [[Bring Me the Horizon]] on their 2019 Australia Tour.<ref>{{cite news |title=BRING ME THE HORIZON // 'FIRST LOVE' AUSTRALIA TOUR 2019 |url=https://www.hysteriamag.com/bring-horizon-first-love-australia-tour-2019/ |access-date=31 October 2019 |publisher=Hysteria |date=11 April 2019}}</ref> The band headlined the 2019 Monster Energy Outbreak Tour.<ref name="cos"/> Their 2019 North American tour was set to begin on 23 September 2019 in New York City, with [[The Beaches (band)|The Beaches]] as the opening band on most dates,<ref name="cos"/> but the tour was cancelled due to Carter being in a car crash several days before the first show.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Lavin |first1=Will |title=Frank Carter postpones U.S. tour dates following horrific car crash |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/frank-carter-postpones-u-s-tour-following-horrible-car-crash-2550377 |access-date=1 November 2019 |publisher=NME |date=23 September 2019}}</ref> The Rattlesnakes will headline a tour of Australia in 2020 and played three UK sold-out shows in February, at the Alexandra Palace, Glasgow's Barrowlands and the Manchester Academy.<ref>{{cite web |title=Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes announce 2020 Aussie headline tour |url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/frank-carter-rattlesnakes-2020-tour/ |website=tonedeaf.thebrag.com |date=30 July 2019 |access-date=31 October 2019}}</ref>
