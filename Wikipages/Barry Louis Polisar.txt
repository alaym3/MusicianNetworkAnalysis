'''Barry Louis Polisar''' is an author and singer-songwriter who writes [[children's music]] and numerous [[children's books]], poems, and stories.

==Works==
Polisar has traveled throughout the [[United States of America|United States]] and [[Europe]] as a visiting author in schools and libraries. He is a five-time [[Parents' Choice Award]] winner and has performed in The [[White House]], [[The Smithsonian]], and The [[Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts]]. He has written songs for [[Sesame Street]] and [[The Weekly Reader]], has starred in an [[Emmy Award]]-winning television show for children, and has been featured regularly on [[The Learning Channel]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://articles.baltimoresun.com/1991-03-28/news/9113003317_1_barry-louis-polisar-white-house-county-schools|title=Invitation From Bush For Polisar|work=tribunedigital-baltimoresun|access-date=2017-02-28|language=en}}</ref>

Polisar works with literacy groups, media specialists, reading teachers, and Title I programs in schools and has won a Special Library Recognition Award for his "ability to communicate with and excite children to read".

Polisar's song "All I Want Is You" was featured during the [[Juno (soundtrack)|opening credits]] of [[Jason Reitman]]'s film ''[[Juno (film)|Juno]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Sound track for Juno (2007) |publisher=Internet Movie Database |url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0467406/soundtrack |access-date=25 March 2010}}</ref> This song is featured on advertisements of the [[National Lottery (United Kingdom)]], the [[Honda Civic]] "Date With a Woodsman", and the [[Del Monte Foods]] "Bursting with Life".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://yannigroth.com/2015/04/04/10-ads-based-on-barry-louis-polisar-music/|title=10 Ads Based on Barry Louis Polisar Music|date=2015-04-04|website=Yannig Roth / This is my blog|access-date=2017-02-28}}</ref>

A two-disc [[tribute album]], compiled by Aaron Cohen of the [[Radioactive Chickenheads|Radioactive Chicken Heads]] entitled "We're Not Kidding! A Tribute to Barry Louis Polisar" was released in 2010. The album features covers of 60 of Polisar's songs by artists such as [[DeLeon (band)|DeLeon]], [[Rebecca Loebe]], [[Radioactive Chickenheads|The Radioactive Chicken Heads]], [[Tor Hyams]], and Barry Louis Polisar's son [[Evan Polisar]].<ref>{{cite web |last=McNamara |first=Caitlin |date=24 December 2009 |title=Children's Songwriter Paid Tribute by Fans Who Became Musicians |website=Brooklyn Daily Eagle |url=http://www.brooklyneagle.com/categories/category.php?category_id=27&id=32663 |access-date=18 December 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Polisar was born in Brooklyn, New York, and grew up in Maryland. He began his career when he moved to [[Montgomery County, Maryland]].<ref>"Md. musician featured in 'Juno' to perform, DC". ''Associated Press''. 30 January 2010.</ref>

== Awards ==
*Polisar's TV show Field Trip won two Emmy Awards in its first season.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2008/jan/30/entertainment/et-junosong30|title='Juno' gives rebirth to songwriter's career|last=Burris|first=Joe|date=2008-01-30|work=Los Angeles Times|access-date=2017-02-28|language=en-US|issn=0458-3035}}</ref>
*Polisar's recordings have won four (5) different Parents' Choice Awards.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.msac.org/touring-artists-roster/barry-louis-polisar|title=Maryland State Arts Council - Advancing the Arts Across Maryland|work=Maryland State Arts Council website|access-date=2017-02-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170301092858/https://www.msac.org/touring-artists-roster/barry-louis-polisar|archive-date=1 March 2017|language=en-US|url-status=dead}}</ref>
*In 2008, Barry won a ''Lifetime Achievement Award'' from the ''Children's Music Web'' for "three decades of being a distinctive voice for kids."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/2008/12/prweb1710354.htm|title=Grammy Nomination and Lifetime Award Reveal Barry Louis Polisar's Staying Power|work=PRWeb|access-date=2017-02-28}}</ref>
*Numerous Children's Music Web Awards for Best Children's Recording, Best New Song, and Best "Classic" recording.
*Polisar's book Insect Soup won a Parents' Guide Award for Outstanding Children's Books.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.childrenslibrary.org/icdl/BookPreview?bookid=polinse_01050001&route=text&lang=English&msg=&ilang=English|title=Insect soup - About This Book|website=www.childrenslibrary.org|access-date=2017-02-28}}</ref>
*Polisar's Scrapbook video won a National Media Gold Award and an award from the Coalition on Quality Children's Recordings.
*Two Maryland State Artist Awards.
*Polisar's recording of Old Dogs, New Tricks was named "one of the twenty best Children's Recordings of all time" in The American Library Association's Children's Jukebox and Award Hall of Fame.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://archive.org/details/isbn_9780838906507|url-access=registration|quote=polisar Children's Jukebox.|title=Children's Jukebox: A Subject Guide to Musical Recordings and Programming Ideas for Songsters Ages One to Twelve|last=Reid|first=Rob|date=1995-01-01|publisher=American Library Association|isbn=9780838906507|pages=[https://archive.org/details/isbn_9780838906507/page/12 12], 15|language=en}}</ref>
*Maryland Library Association's Special Recognition Award for his "contributions on behalf of children of all ages" for his "ability to communicate with children and excite them to read."
