'''Lenka Kripac''' (born 19 March 1978) is an Australian singer and actress best known for her song "[[The Show (Lenka song)|The Show]]", from her debut album, [[Lenka (album)|''Lenka'']]. "[[The Show (Lenka song)|The Show]]" has been used in numerous advertisements, most notably for [[Old Navy]], as well as the [[Nickelodeon]] film ''[[Angus, Thongs and Perfect Snogging]]'' and the 2011 film [[Moneyball (film)|''Moneyball'']].<ref>[https://www.skiddle.com/artists/lenka-123511354/] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180105070544/https://www.skiddle.com/artists/lenka-123511354/ |date=5 January 2018 }}, ''[[Biography]]''. Retrieved on 05 Jan 2018.</ref> Her song "[[Everything at Once]]" was used in a [[Windows 8]] television advertisement and in a Disney Movie Rewards commercial.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZZ2cftjyHys |title=Windows 8: Lenka – Everything At Once (TV Spot) &#124; Microsoft |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2013-07-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160602091145/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZZ2cftjyHys |archive-date=2 June 2016 |url-status=live |df=dmy-all }}</ref> Her fifth studio album, ''[[Attune]]'', was released in 2017.<ref name=":0" />

==Early life==
The daughter of [[Czech Republic|Czech]]-born jazz trumpet musician, Jiří Křipač<ref name="mtvasia1">{{cite web |url=http://www.mtvasia.com/Music/FeaturedArtist/Lenka/ |title=Lenka &#124; Artist |publisher=MTV Asia |access-date=11 July 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090813180042/http://www.mtvasia.com/Music/FeaturedArtist/Lenka/ |archive-date=13 August 2009 |url-status=live |df=dmy-all }}</ref> and Australian schoolteacher, Eden, Lenka was raised in the Australian coastal town of [[Bega, New South Wales|Bega]] until age seven, when her family moved to [[Sydney]], where she received her schooling, acting and music training, and started to work as a highly regarded theatre [[actress]] and later musician.<ref name="lenkamusic-bio">{{cite web |url=https://lenkamusic.com/biography |title=Bio |work=The Official Lenka Site |publisher=Sony Music Entertainment |access-date=18 May 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090830061521/http://www.lenkamusic.com/biography |archive-date=30 August 2009 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Lenka, Portland 2008.jpg|thumb|left|Lenka at the Hotel Cafe Tour at Doug Fir in [[Portland, Oregon|Portland]], [[Oregon]]]]

===2008–2012: Beginnings and self-titled debut album===
As a teenager, Lenka studied acting at the [[Australian Theatre for Young People]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ravemagazine.com.au/content/view/12128/30/ |title=Brisbane Street Press – Lenka |magazine=Rave Magazine |date=4 November 2008 |access-date=11 July 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110302063134/http://www.ravemagazine.com.au/content/view/12128/30/ |archive-date=2 March 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> where she trained with actress [[Cate Blanchett]].<ref name="Columbia.co.uk">{{cite web |url=http://www.columbia.co.uk/releases/950/ |title=Columbia Records UK " Releases " Lenka – Lenka |publisher=Columbia.co.uk |access-date=11 July 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090825002120/http://www.columbia.co.uk/releases/950/ |archive-date=25 August 2009 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> Lenka starred in the Australian ABC-TV drama series ''[[GP (TV series)|GP]]'' as Vesna Kapek in the 1990s. She also hosted ''[[Cheez TV]]'' and has guest starred in other Australian TV series, including ''[[Home and Away]]'', ''[[Wildside (U.S. TV series)|Wildside]]'', ''[[Head Start (TV series)|Head Start]]'', and ''[[Spellbinder (TV series)|Spellbinder]]''. She appeared in Australian feature films ''[[The Dish]]'' and ''[[Lost Things]]'', as well as in theatre productions.<ref name="Columbia.co.uk"/> Lenka provided the vocals for 2 tracks on [[Paul Mac]]'s 2005 album ''[[Panic Room (album)|Panic Room]]''. As Lenka Kripac, she was a member of the Australian [[electronic music|electronic]]-rock [[crossover (music)|crossover]] band [[Decoder Ring]] for two of their albums.  She then moved to [[California]] in 2007.<ref name="lenkamusic-bio"/>

After adopting her first name as her sole artistic name, Lenka released her eponymous debut album on 24 September 2008, with "[[The Show (Lenka song)|The Show]]" (produced by Stuart Brawley) chosen to be the first single release from the set.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=15986 |title=The Show by Lenka Songfacts |publisher=Songfacts.com |access-date=2015-07-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110614063348/http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=15986 |archive-date=14 June 2011 |url-status=live |df=dmy-all }}</ref> The album peaked at number 142 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. Her song "Everything at Once" was featured in a Windows 8 ad, becoming a worldwide success.<ref name="allmusic1">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1422145/charts-awards|pure_url=yes}} |title=Lenka – Charts & Awards – Billboard Albums|publisher=[[AllMusic]] |date=23 September 2008 |access-date=11 July 2010}}</ref> Lenka creates paper art type stop-motion animated music videos for each of her singles with her husband James Gulliver Hancock, a visual artist from Australia, for a deliberately childlike effect.<ref name="mtvasia1"/> She provided vocals on two tracks ("Addicted" and "Sunrise") on German artist [[Schiller (band)|Schiller]]'s album ''[[Atemlos (Schiller album)|Atemlos]]'', released in Germany on 12 March 2010.

In 2011 she released her second album ''[[Two (Lenka album)|Two]]'' which was inspired by her engagement and is full of romantic love songs. Despite a warm critical reception,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gantdaily.com/2011/01/13/australian-singer-lenka-returns-with-sophomore-album-two/|title=Australian singer Lenka returns with sophomore album "Two"|date=13 January 2011|publisher=GantDaily.com|access-date=12 March 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120313143243/http://gantdaily.com/2011/01/13/australian-singer-lenka-returns-with-sophomore-album-two/|archive-date=13 March 2012|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> the album failed to match the success of her debut album, with ''Two'' reaching peak chart positions of 69 and 88 on the Belgian and Swiss charts respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ultratop.be/fr/showitem.asp?interpret=Lenka&titel=Two&cat=a|title=Lenka - Two|author=Steffen Hung|publisher=ultratop.be|access-date=30 January 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hitparade.ch/showitem.asp?interpret=Lenka&titel=Two&cat=a|title=Lenka - Two|author=Steffen Hung|publisher=hitparade.ch|access-date=12 March 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121111204024/http://hitparade.ch/showitem.asp?interpret=Lenka&titel=Two&cat=a|archive-date=11 November 2012|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Her third album ''[[Shadows (Lenka album)|Shadows]]'' appeared in 2013 after the birth of her son.

===2015–present: Subsequent releases and ''Attune''===
Her fourth studio album ''[[The Bright Side (Lenka album)|The Bright Side]]'' was released in 2015 and the most featured single in this album became "[[Blue Skies (Lenka song)|Blue Skies]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.musicweek.com/news/read/sony-atv-to-jointly-release-new-lenka-album/061847|title=Sony/ATV to jointly release new Lenka album {{!}} News {{!}} Music Week|website=www.musicweek.com|language=en|access-date=2018-10-31}}</ref> A remix version of the song, released on the YouTube remix channel [[Trap Nation]], has over 30 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{Citation|last=Trap Nation|title=Lenka - Blue Skies (REVOKE Remix)|date=2015-05-29|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-mSRraXCY9w|access-date=2018-10-31|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180609174350/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-mSRraXCY9w|archive-date=9 June 2018|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Her latest album, ''[[Attune]],'' was released in 2017 and contains the track ''Heal'', which was created in cooperation with Australian singer and author [[Sally Seltmann]].<ref name=":0">[https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lenka-mn0001010609/biography/Artist Biography by Andrew Leahey] Retrieved on 16 Jan 2018</ref> Her most played songs on [[BBC]] are "All My Bells Are Ringing", "We Will Not Grow Old", "Everything at Once", "Don't Let Me Fall", and "Trouble Is a Friend".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/ae9ed5e2-4caf-4b3d-9cb3-2ad626b91714|title=Lenka - New Songs, Playlists & Latest News - BBC Music|website=BBC|language=en-GB|access-date=2018-10-31|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180920162818/https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/ae9ed5e2-4caf-4b3d-9cb3-2ad626b91714|archive-date=20 September 2018|url-status=live|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Lenka currently resides in regional [[New South Wales]], [[Australia]]. Lenka is married to visual [[artist]] James Gulliver Hancock.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.asiaone.com/News/Latest+News/Showbiz/Story/A1Story20110225-265251.html |title=Lenka's all grown up and married |publisher=Asiaone.com |date=2011-02-25 |access-date=2013-07-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120511100907/http://www.asiaone.com/News/Latest%2BNews/Showbiz/Story/A1Story20110225-265251.html |archive-date=11 May 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> She announced on 27 September 2011 that she was pregnant.<ref>[https://lenkamusic.com/ca/news/happy-news ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120104175225/http://www.lenkamusic.com/ca/news/happy-news |date=4 January 2012 }}</ref> In March 2012, she announced on Twitter that she had given birth to a boy named Quinn.
