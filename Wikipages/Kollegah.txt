'''Felix Martin Andreas Matthias Blume'''<ref>{{Cite web|date=7 November 2014|title=Das Rätsel um Kollegahs bürgerlichen Namen|url=https://kiloherz.info/2014/11/das-raetsel-um-kollegahs-buergerlichen-namen/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-12|website=kiloherz.info|language=de}}</ref> (born 3 August 1984), known professionally as '''Kollegah''', is a German [[Rapping|rapper]]. Born in [[Friedberg, Hesse]] and raised by a single mother in [[Simmern]], he started rapping in the Reimliga Battle Arena in December 2004. Intending to pursue a solo career as a rapper, Kollegah released his first [[mixtape]] ''Zuhältertape'' in July 2005 and was signed to ''[[Selfmade Records]]'' in December of the same year. He went on to pursue a variety of styles on subsequent albums ''[[Alphagene]]'' (2007), [[Kollegah (album)|Kollegah]] (2008), [[Bossaura]] (2011). His fourth studio album ''[[King (Kollegah album)|King]]'' was released in 2014 and became his best-selling album to date. His fifth album, ''Zuhältertape Vol. 4'' was released in 2015, ''Imperator'' in 2016 and ''[[Monument (Kollegah album)|Monument]]'' in 2018. Kollegah released four collaborative studio albums with German rapper [[Farid Bang]], the ''Jung brutal gutaussehend'' tetralogy between 2009 and 2018.{{efn|''[[Jung, brutal, gutaussehend]]'' (2009), ''[[Jung, brutal, gutaussehend 2]]'' (2013), and ''[[Jung, brutal, gutaussehend 3]]'' (2017), and ''Platin war gestern'' (2018)}}

He is often referred to listeners as the best German rapper.{{cn|date=March 2021}} He is famous for his lyrics (especially his wordplays, multisyllabic rhymes and punchlines) and also his double-time.{{cn|date=March 2021}}

== Biography ==
===1984–2005: Early life, ''Reimliga Battle Arena'', ''Zuhältertape'' and ''Alphagene''===
[[File:Kollegah 2008.jpg|thumb|Kollegah in 2008]]
Felix Blume was born on 3 August 1984 in [[Friedberg, Hesse]], to a Canadian father and a German mother. His parents divorced during his childhood. After the divorce, he moved with his mother to [[Simmern]] in [[Rhineland-Palatinate]]. He attended the Herzog-Johann-Gymnasium, where he passed his [[Abitur]] exam in 2005.<ref name="abitur">{{cite web |url=http://allgood.de/features/interviews/ich-habe-mir-astronomie-im-autodidaktischen-verfahren-beigebracht/ |title="Ich habe mir Astronomie im autodidaktischen Verfahren beigebracht." |trans-title=I taught myself astronomy in the autodidactic process. |date=29 April 2014 |access-date=6 April 2018 |first=Jan |last=Wehn |publisher=All Good |language=de |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180407115751/http://allgood.de/features/interviews/ich-habe-mir-astronomie-im-autodidaktischen-verfahren-beigebracht/ |archive-date=7 April 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="juice">{{cite web |url=https://juice.de/kollegah-titelstory/ |title=Kollegah {{bracket|Titelstory}} |first=Jakon |last=Paur |date=13 May 2014 |access-date=6 April 2018 |publisher=Juice |language=de |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180407053733/https://juice.de/kollegah-titelstory/ |archive-date=7 April 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> Through his Algerian stepfather he converted to [[Islam]]. From December 2004 until May 2005 Kollegah participated in rap battles hosted on online platform ''Reimliga Battle Arena'' (RBA) and won 11 out of 14 evaluated battles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.r-b-a.de/index.php?ID=4100&MEMBER=12303|title=RBA V4 – 0.03|publisher=RBA|access-date=20 August 2018|language=de|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171205160238/http://www.r-b-a.de/index.php?ID=4100&MEMBER=12303|archive-date=5 December 2017|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |title=Die Akte Kollegah |trans-title=The file Kollegah |publisher=Backspin |first=Niko |last=Hüls |year=2007 |pages=40–42 |language=de}}</ref>

Blume released his first Mixtape, the ''Zuhältertape'' (ZHT) for free on 20 July 2005 on his website. The 14-track long project was downloaded—according to Blume—over 5,000 times in one month.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.projecter.net:80/kollegah/index.html |title=Kollegah – News |publisher=Kollegah.de |date=20 July 2005 |access-date=21 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20051205023112/http://www.projecter.net/kollegah/index.html |archive-date=5 December 2005 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The project was re-released in December 2005 through [[Selfmade Records]]. In July 2006 Blume published his second Mixtape ''Boss der Bosse'' (ZHT 2). He was criticised for his live performance during the ''[[Splash! (festival)|Splash!]]'' in 2006.<ref name="Juice11/07">{{cite book|first=Davide |last=Bortot|work=Juice Magazin|title=Heisser Herbst – Kollegah |trans-title=Hot autumn – Kollegah |date=November 2007|language=de|pages=68–70}}</ref> Blume's first studio album ''Alphagene'' was set to release in September 2007, but was postponed to November, after he was found with [[cocaine]] and [[amphetamine]] in [[Bad Kreuznach]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.laut.de/News/Kollegah-Drogenhandel-verdirbt-Releasedate-31-08-2007-5191 |title=Drogenhandel verdirbt Releasedate |trans-title=Drug trafficking spoils releasedate |date=31 August 2007 |access-date=12 September 2017 |archive-date=11 September 2017 |publisher=[[Laut.de]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170911223512/http://www.laut.de/News/Kollegah-Drogenhandel-verdirbt-Releasedate-31-08-2007-5191 |language=de}}</ref> ''Alphagene'' became Kollegahs first project to enter the German charts on 30 November 2007. The album debuted on number 51 and left the charts next week.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/album-details-75986 |title=Kollegah – Alphagene |date=30 November 2007 |access-date=12 September 2017 |publisher=Offizielle Charts |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180821033123/https://www.offiziellecharts.de/album-details-75986 |archive-date=21 August 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> According to Selfmade Records, the album was sold over 20,000 times.<ref name="Sales">{{cite web |url=http://hiphop.de/magazin/news/selfmade-records-verraten-gesamtverkaufszahlen-labelgeschichte-285250#.VnGxfVKbKVF |title=Selfmade Records verraten Gesamtverkaufszahlen der Labelgeschichte |trans-title=Selfmade Records reveal total sales figures of the label history |publisher=Hiphop.de |first=Jonas |last=Lindemann |date=12 December 2015 |access-date=12 September 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170910222311/http://hiphop.de/magazin/news/selfmade-records-verraten-gesamtverkaufszahlen-labelgeschichte-285250 |archive-date=10 September 2017 |language=de}}</ref>

===2008–2010: ''Kollegah'', ''Chronik 2'', ''ZHT 3'', ''Jung, brutal, gutaussehend'' and feud with Aggro Berlin===
On 28 August 2008, Blume released his second studio album ''Kollegah'', which peaked at 17 in Germany,<ref>{{cite web|date=12 September 2008|title=Kollegah - Kollegah|url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/album-details-95159|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160806042804/https://www.offiziellecharts.de/album-details-95159|archive-date=6 August 2016|access-date=12 September 2017|work=Bundesverband Musikindustrie}}</ref> 48 in Switzerland<ref>{{cite web|title=KOLLEGAH - KOLLEGAH|url=http://hitparade.ch/album/Kollegah/Kollegah-95159|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161203014838/http://hitparade.ch/album/Kollegah/Kollegah-95159|archive-date=3 December 2016|access-date=12 September 2017}}</ref> and sold over 20,000 copies.<ref name="Sales" />

On 17 April 2009, ''Chronik 2'' was released, an album featuring the [[Selfmade Records]] artists Kollegah, Favorite, Casper and Shiml. The previously released song "Westdeutschlands Kings" performed by Kollegah, [[Favorite (rapper)|Favorite]] and [[Farid Bang]], targeted insults at [[Sido (rapper)|Sido]], [[Fler]] and [[Kitty Kat]] from [[Aggro Berlin]].<ref>[http://www.bassdraft.com/de/news/Aggro_Diss_Westdeutschlands_Kings/index.html 12.03.2009: Aggro Diss: "Westdeutschlands Kings" – News (Downloads, Beef)] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707213450/http://www.bassdraft.com/de/news/Aggro_Diss_Westdeutschlands_Kings/index.html |date=7 July 2011 }}. Bassdraft (31 December 2008). Retrieved on 2011-10-24.</ref> In March, a song titled "Früher wart ihr Fans" was released by [[Fler]], [[Kitty Kat]] and [[Godsilla]] as a reaction. In the song, they accuse Selfmade Records artists of having created the feud for the sole purpose of marketing their album; Kitty Kat also attacks Favorite by referring to the death of his parents, who died because of a fire while camping. Kollegah responded on 20 March with the song "Fanpost", calling Fler a "fat potato" and claims that he was penetrated with a carrot.<ref>[http://www.bassdraft.com/de/news/Kollegah_Fanpost_Fler_Diss/ 20.03.2009: Kollegah: "Fanpost" (Fler Diss) – News (Downloads, Beef)] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707213801/http://www.bassdraft.com/de/news/Kollegah_Fanpost_Fler_Diss/ |date=7 July 2011 }}. Bassdraft (20 March 2009). Retrieved on 2011-10-24.</ref> Fler responded with the song "Schrei nach Liebe", the chorus of which is heavily based on [[Die Ärzte]]'s 1993 hit single "[[Schrei nach Liebe]]".<ref>[http://www.bassdraft.com/de/news/Fler_Schrei_nach_Liebe_Kollegah_Diss/index.html 23.03.2009: Fler: "Schrei nach Liebe" (Kollegah Diss) – News (Beef)] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707213812/http://www.bassdraft.com/de/news/Fler_Schrei_nach_Liebe_Kollegah_Diss/index.html |date=7 July 2011 }}. Bassdraft (23 March 2009). Retrieved on 2011-10-24.</ref>

Three months after the release of ''Chronik 2'' Blume released his first collaborative album with [[Farid Bang]], called ''[[Jung, brutal, gutaussehend]]''. In December Blume released the third part of his ''Zuhältertape-Series''. A year later Selfmade released the a limited Box-Set with all three ''Zuhältertape's'' and the ''Hoodtape Volume 1,'' which was re-released on 18 December 2010 as ''Hoodtape Volume 1 X-Mas Edition'' with some additional and extended tracks.

===2011–2015: ''Bossaura'', ''Jung, brutal, gutaussehend 2'', ''King'' and ''Zuhältertape 4''===
[[File:Kollegah - 2014 - 1.jpg|thumb|upright|Kollegah in 2014]] 
In 2011, Kollegah released his album ''Bossaura'', which peaked at number 5 in German album charts. ''Jung, brutal, gutaussehend 2'', his collaboration album with Farid Bang, was released in 2013 and reached number 1 in Germany, Austria and Switzerland. It sold more than 80,000 copies in the first week. Kollegahs album ''King'' in 2014 sold more than 160,000 copies in the first week and was his second number 1 album. ''Zuhältertape 4'' (ZHT 4)was released in December 2015 and also reached number 1 in Germany, Austria and Switzerland.

== Controversies ==

=== Antisemitism and conspiracy theories ===
On his third mixtape ''[[Zuhältertape Volume 3]]'', Blume used the line "Endlösung der Rapperfrage", directly referring to the [[Final Solution]] during the [[Holocaust]]. Gerhard Strate called it mocking of Holocaust victims. His fourth studio album ''Zuhältertape Vol. 4'' contained statements like "Nutte, Zeit, dass du Putzlappen befeuchtest / Ich bring Schusswaffengeräusche wie die Schutzstaffel der Deutschen" ("Hooker, that time that you moisturize cloths / I bring firearms sounds like the [[Schutzstaffel]] of the Germans") or "Kid, es ist der Boss, der für 'ne Modezeitschrift Posen einnimmt wie die Wehrmacht, die in Polen einschritt" ("Kid, it is the boss, who poses for a fashion magazine like the [[Wehrmacht]], which [[Invasion of Poland|invaded Poland]]").<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cicero.de/kultur/echo-preis-kollegah-farid-bang-kunstfreiheit-menschenwuerde-antisemitismus |title=Kunstfreiheit gegen Menschenwürde |date=12 April 2018 |publisher=Cicero |first=Gerhard |last=Strate |access-date=26 April 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180422064552/https://www.cicero.de/kultur/echo-preis-kollegah-farid-bang-kunstfreiheit-menschenwuerde-antisemitismus |archive-date=22 April 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In 2016 Blume released the song "Apokalypse", in which he depicts human history as being the good against the bad. The final battle is set in [[East Jerusalem]]. The devil is sitting in a [[skyscraper]] and the climax of the video is a [[book burning]]. After the world is freed from the evil, Blume says that "[[Buddhism|Buddhist]], [[Islam|Muslims]] and [[Christianity|Christians]] will rebuild the world peacefully" and forgot to mention [[Jews]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www1.wdr.de/mediathek/video/sendungen/die-story/video-die-dunkle-seite-des-deutschen-rap-100.html |title=Die dunkle Seite des deutschen Rap |date=29 March 2018 |publisher=WDR |access-date=6 May 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180505015250/https://www1.wdr.de/mediathek/video/sendungen/die-story/video-die-dunkle-seite-des-deutschen-rap-100.html |archive-date=5 May 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fr.de/kultur/musik/echo-verleihung-2018-antisemitismus-als-aesthetischer-code-a-1484491,0 |title=Antisemitismus als ästhetischer Code |publisher=Frankfurter Rundschau |date=12 April 2018 |access-date=6 May 2018 |first=Daniel |last=Dillmann |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180418230818/http://www.fr.de/kultur/musik/echo-verleihung-2018-antisemitismus-als-aesthetischer-code-a-1484491,0 |archive-date=18 April 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Kollegah denies any accusations of antisemitism. In 2021, he published an album with Asche, a German rapper with Jewish roots. Furthermore, Kollegah and German-Jewish rapper Sun Diego fueled rumours that they plan a collaborative albums together. 

Kollegah criticized the American foreign policy several times. He also doubts the official version of [[9/11 conspiracy theories|9/11]], the [[Moon landing conspiracy theories|Moon landing]] and [[evolution theory]] and also spread rumours about [[Pizzagate conspiracy theory|Pizzagate]] and [[Chemtrail conspiracy theory|chemtrails]].{{citation needed|date=August 2021}}

In November 2016, Blume released the documentation ''Kollegah an Palästina (Eine StreetCinema Dokumentation)'' on his [[YouTube]] channel [https://www.youtube.com/user/Bosshaftchannel Alpha Music Empire.]. He travelled through the [[Palestinian territories]], visited the [[Israeli West Bank barrier|West Bank barrier]], the refugee camp [[Am'ari]] and supported a school.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AOqNrC_ILIM |title=KOLLEGAH IN PALÄSTINA (Eine StreetCinema Dokumentation) |date=29 November 2016 |access-date=10 September 2017 |via=YouTube |publisher=Bosshaft TV |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180421025839/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AOqNrC_ILIM |archive-date=21 April 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> The documentation started a controversy in the German rap scene.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.stern.de/kultur/musik/kollegah-erntet-kritik-fuer-seine-palaestina-dokumentation-7222176.html |title=Kollegah erntet heftige Kritik für Palästina-Doku |date=2 December 2016 |access-date=9 October 2017 |publisher=Stern |first=Carsten |last=Heidböhmer |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170910030019/http://www.stern.de/contentcontrol-7591798.html |archive-date=10 September 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.welt.de/kultur/article160698449/Wie-antisemitisch-ist-der-deutsche-Rap.html |title=Wie antisemitisch ist der deutsche Rap? |first=Konstantin |last=Nowotny |newspaper=Die Welt |publisher=Welt |date=30 December 2016 |access-date=9 October 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170910174943/https://www.welt.de/kultur/article160698449/Wie-antisemitisch-ist-der-deutsche-Rap.html |archive-date=10 September 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In 2018 months after release, Kollegah and Farid Bang were criticized for the lines ''commit another Holocaust and come with Molotov'' and ''My body is more defined than those of Auschwitz inmates'' on their album ''Jung, Brutal, Gutaussehend 3''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Eddy|first1=Melissa|last2=Curry|first2=Andrew|title=Fury in Germany as Rap Duo With Anti-Jewish Lyrics Gets Award|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/18/arts/music/anti-semitism-german-rap.html|work=The New York Times|date=18 April 2018|access-date=20 April 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180419220253/https://www.nytimes.com/2018/04/18/arts/music/anti-semitism-german-rap.html|archive-date=19 April 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> They received the coveted German [[Echo Music Prize|ECHO award]] for this album and also performed live, and in reaction to this, several artists gave their ECHOs back. Both first defend their lines as a form of stylistic means, exaggeration, provocation, artistic boundaries, but later apologized.

===Religious beliefs===
At the age of 15, Blume came in contact with [[Islam]] through his Algerian stepfather. He converted to the religion in the same year.

===Legal issues===
During a concert of his "Imperator" tour in [[Leipzig]], there was a violent incident onstage. As is often the case with rap concerts, a [[Battle rap|rap battle]] between two spectators should be performed on the stage. One of the participants tried to grab Blume's sunglasses. As a result, Blume shoved the man, kicked him and punched him in the face. A few hours later, videos of the attack were uploaded to [[YouTube]] and [[Facebook]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qeHiazK53vo |title={{bracket|Full}} Kollegah schlägt Fan - Imperator Tour Leipzig 18.03.17 |author=TheGsGClan |work=YouTube |date=18 March 2017 |access-date=11 September 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170820132627/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qeHiazK53vo |archive-date=20 August 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref> An uninvolved person reported Blume to the police.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.morgenpost.de/vermischtes/article209981097/Kollegah-verpruegelt-Fan-bei-einem-Konzert-in-Leipzig.html |title=Anzeige gegen Kollegah nach Faustschlag beim Konzert |publisher=Berliner Morgenpost |date=19 March 2017 |access-date=11 September 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170911213738/https://www.morgenpost.de/vermischtes/article209981097/Kollegah-verpruegelt-Fan-bei-einem-Konzert-in-Leipzig.html |archive-date=11 September 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.spiegel.de/panorama/leute/rapper-kollegah-schlaegt-fan-nieder-a-1139471.html |title=Rapper Kollegah verpasst Fan Faustschlag ins Gesicht |newspaper=Der Spiegel |date=19 March 2017 |access-date=11 September 2017 |publisher=Spiegel Online |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170911213939/http://www.spiegel.de/panorama/leute/rapper-kollegah-schlaegt-fan-nieder-a-1139471.html |archive-date=11 September 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref>
