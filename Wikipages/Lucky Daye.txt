'''David Debrandon Brown''' (born September 25, 1985), professionally known as '''Lucky Daye''', is an American [[Singing|singer]]-[[songwriter]] from [[New Orleans, Louisiana]]. He is signed to [[Keep Cool Records]] and [[RCA Records]]. He released his first EP, ''I'' on November 9, 2018, and his second EP, ''II'', on February 6, 2019. The EPs were part of a series leading up to his debut [[studio album]] ''[[Painted (Lucky Daye album)|Painted]]'', which was released on May 24, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lucky Daye Unveils I, {{sic|Comprised |hide=y|Of}} First Five Tracks From Forthcoming Debut Album |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Lucky-Daye-Unveils-I-Comprised-Of-First-Five-Tracks-From-Forthcoming-Debut-Album-20181109 |website=Broadwayworld |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Mahadeven |first1=Tara |title=Listen to Lucky Daye's New Project 'I' |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/11/lucky-daye-i |website=Complex |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref> Daye has earned a collective of six [[Grammy Award]] nominations for his musical work.

==Early life==
David Debrandon Brown was born on September 25, 1985 in [[New Orleans, Louisiana]]. He was raised in a [[cult]] that forbade secular music, but he discovered an innate knack for singing and taught himself melodies by turning children books into songs of his own. After his mother left the cult,&nbsp;Brown found himself going back to immerse himself in classic R&B and eventually fell hard for the likes of&nbsp;[[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[Rick James]], [[Lauryn Hill]], and [[Stevie Wonder]]. He soon relocated to [[Atlanta, Georgia]] to pursue music.<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web |last1=Kellman |first1=Andy |title=Lucky Daye - Biography |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lucky-daye-mn0003788963/biography |website=AllMusic |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref><ref name="DJBooth">{{cite web |last1=Chesman |first1=Donna-Claire |title=Meet Lucky Daye, the R&B Singer Who Ran out of Fucks |url=https://djbooth.net/features/2018-11-12-lucky-daye-meet-interview |website=DJBooth |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Lucky Daye Ignites the Start of an Impressive R&B Career With "Roll Some Mo" |url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/lucky-daye-ignites-the-start-of-an-impressive-rb |website=Ones To Watch |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref>

==Musical career==
Daye previously competed on [[American Idol (season 4)|American Idol]] (Season 4 in 2005), at age 19. He auditioned with "[[A Change Is Gonna Come]]" by [[Sam Cooke]], and was given four "yes" votes. He was eliminated shortly after reaching the Top 20.

Daye established himself as a songwriter and background vocalist and landed credits in 2008 on songs by [[Keith Sweat]] on his album ''[[Just Me (Keith Sweat album)|Just Me]]'' and [[Ne-Yo]] on "[[She Got Her Own]]" In 2014, he wrote for [[Boyz II Men]] on the song "Believe Us". During 2016 and 2017, he co-wrote songs released by [[Keke Palmer]] ("Enemiez"), [[Ella Mai]] ("10,000 Hours," "Down"), [[Trey Songz]] ("Song Goes Off"), and two tracks from the album ''[[Strength of a Woman (album)|Strength of a Woman]]'' by [[Mary J. Blige]], among other contemporary R&B artists.<ref name="AllMusic" />

In October 2018, Daye announced that he has signed to [[Keep Cool Records]] and [[RCA Records]], and released his first single called "Roll Some Mo".<ref>{{cite web |title=RCA / KEEP COOL's newest signee Lucky Daye shares his new song "Roll Some Mo" |url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/10/24/rca-cools-newest-signee-lucky-daye-shares-song-roll-mo-07006bb661 |website=Revolt |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref> On November 9, he released his first EP titled ''I'', which is part of a series leading up to his forthcoming debut studio album ''[[Painted (Lucky Daye album)|Painted]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=New Music: Lucky Daye – Roll Some Mo |url=http://youknowigotsoul.com/new-music-lucky-daye-roll-some-mo |website=You Know I Got Soul |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Leight |first1=Elias |title=Lucky Daye Doesn't Want to Fit In |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/lucky-daye-debut-ep-i-754161/ |website=Rolling Stone}}</ref>

On January 17, 2019, Daye released the first single from the second EP in the series called "Karma". On February 6, 2019, he released the second installment of the EP series, ''II''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ichiki |first1=Chase |title=Lucky Daye delivers his second installment "II" of his debut album |url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2019/02/06/lucky-daye-delivers-installment-ii-debut-album-07003d2177 |website=Revolt |access-date=6 February 2019}}</ref> He is slated to go on tour with [[Ella Mai]] beginning in February 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lucky Daye Releases New Single KARMA |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Lucky-Daye-Releases-New-Single-KARMA-20190117 |website=Broadwayworld |access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref> In November 2019, Daye received four nominations at the [[62nd Grammy Awards]] including one for [[Best R&B Album]] for his debut ''Painted''. On November 21, 2019, Daye opened for R&B singer Khalid on his Free Spirit tour in New Zealand.

On September 25, 2020, he co-wrote English singer [[Zayn Malik|Zayn]]'s single, "Better".

On February 12, 2021, Daye released his first EP since 2019, titled ''Table for Two''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Rose|first=Jordan|date=12 February 2021|title=Lucky Daye Unleashes New EP 'Table for Two' f/ Ari Lennox, Queen Naija, and More|url=https://www.complex.com/music/lucky-daye-table-for-two-ep-stream|url-status=live|access-date=2021-02-18|website=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]|language=en}}</ref>

On May 21, 2021, Daye released a cover of Marvin Gaye’s “[[Mercy Mercy Me (The Ecology)]]” to celebrate the 50th anniversary of [[What's Going On (Marvin Gaye album)|What’s Going On]].
