'''The Blue Stones''' are a Canadian [[blues rock]] duo based in [[Windsor, Ontario]].

== History ==
In August 2018, The Blue Stones signed a record contract with [[Entertainment One]], where they subsequently announced plans to re-release their debut LP ''Black Holes'' on October 26, 2018. They subsequently toured the US with their Be My Fire Tour.<ref>{{cite web |title=Entertainment One Signs The Blue Stones |url=http://cashboxcanada.ca/9629/entertainment-one-signs-blue-stones |website=[[Cashbox (magazine)|Cashbox Canada]] |accessdate=30 October 2018 |date=3 August 2018}}</ref> In early January 2020, The Blue Stones were nominated for the 2020 Juno Award in the category of Breakthrough Group of the Year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.intelligencer.ca/news/local-news/windsor-rock-duo-the-blue-stones-nominated-for-juno-award/wcm/c6fdcd00-5e6b-4cd2-afef-6e9bd988c3b9|title=Windsor rock duo The Blue Stones nominated for Juno Award|last=Chen|first=Dalson|date=29 January 2020|website=The Intelligencer|url-status=live|access-date=February 9, 2020}}</ref>

In early 2021, the band announced their sophomore LP "Hidden Gems," co-written and produced by [[Mutemath]] frontman [[Paul Meany]]. The ten song album was preceded by singles "Shakin' Off The Rust," "Grim," "Careless," "Let It Ride," and "Spirit." "Hidden Gems" was released on March 19, 2021.

== Members ==
* Tarek Jafar – vocals, guitar
* Justin Tessier – percussion, backing vocals
