'''Eidola''' is an American [[rock (music)|rock]] band from [[Salt Lake City]], [[Utah]], formed in 2011. The band consists of lead vocalist, composer and multi-instrumentalist Andrew Wells, lead guitarist Sergio Medina, bass guitarist James Johnson, rhythm guitarist and unclean vocalist Matthew Dommer, and drummer Matthew Hansen. They self-released their debut studio album, ''The Great Glass Elephant'', in 2012. The group signed to [[Blue Swan Records]] in 2014 and released their second and third albums, ''Degeneraterra'' (2015) and ''To Speak, To Listen'' (2017). In December 2017, lead guitarist Brandon Bascom left the band and was replaced by [[Royal Coda]] guitarist Sergio Medina.

The band released the single "Counterfeit Shrines" in June 2021, their first new song in over four years, and released their fourth studio album, ''[[The Architect (Eidola album)|The Architect]]'' in September on Blue Swan Records and [[Rise Records]].

==Career==

===2011–12: Formation and ''The Great Glass Elephant''===

Eidola first formed as a four-piece [[ambient music|ambient]] [[post-hardcore]] band from [[Salt Lake City]], [[Utah]] in 2011, with lead vocalist and guitarist Andrew Michael Wells, screamer and guitarist Matthew Dommer, bass guitarist James Johnson, and drummer Matthew Hansen.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.last.fm/music/Eidola/+wiki |title=Eidola hometown, biography - Last.fm |website=Last.fm}}</ref> The group became well known in the local [[hardcore punk]] scene in the Salt Lake City area, eventually garnering an online and local cult following. The band became known for their eclectic amalgamation of [[math rock]], [[post-rock]], [[metalcore]], post-hardcore, and ambient music and their eccentric live concert performances.<ref name="allmusic.com">{{Cite web |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/eidola-mn0003333198/biography |title=Eidola - Biography & History - AllMusic |website=AllMusic}}</ref>

The band recorded their debut full-length studio album with music engineer Randy Cordner, who produced, mixed and mastered the record.<ref name="bandcamp.com">{{Cite web |url=https://eidolamusic.bandcamp.com/album/the-great-glass-elephant |title=The Great Glass Elephant, by Eidola |website=Eidola}}</ref> The band self-released their debut studio album, ''The Great Glass Elephant'', on December 1, 2012.<ref name="bandcamp.com" /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/the-great-glass-elephant/584406364 |title=The Great Glass Elephant by Eidola |website=[[iTunes]] |date=1 December 2012}}</ref>

===2013–16: ''Degeneraterra''===

In late 2013, the band added guitarist Brandon Bascom to take on live guitar duties to allow Andrew to focus on vocals. Eidola toured briefly with American band I, Omega in the US from December 5 to December 12, 2014.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://lambgoat.com/news/23658/I-Omega-Eidola-tour-dates |title=I, Omega, Eidola tour dates}}</ref>

Eidola began writing and recording their second studio album with [[Dance Gavin Dance]] guitarist [[Will Swan (musician)|Will Swan]] and former Dance Gavin Dance guitarist Josh Benton, in 2014.<ref name="bandcamp.com1">{{cite web|url=https://eidolamusic.bandcamp.com/album/degeneraterra|title=Degeneraterra, by Eidola|website=Eidola}}</ref> The album was recorded and produced at Pus Cavern Recording Studios in [[Sacramento]], [[California]].<ref name="bandcamp.com1" /> Their second studio album, ''Degeneraterra'', was released on May 5, 2015, and was the group's first release on Swan's independent record label [[Blue Swan Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/degeneraterra/980795638|title=Degeneraterra by Eidola|website=[[iTunes]]|date=5 May 2015|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/BlueSwanRecords/posts/891466440876230|title=Blue Swan Records|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref>

In support of ''Degeneraterra'', the band embarked on a headlining tour from October 18 to November 8, 2015, with support from rock bands [[Makari (band)|Makari]] and Que Sera.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ryansrockshow.com/news/eidola-announce-fall-tour-dates|title=EIDOLA Announce Fall Tour Dates|date=12 October 2015|publisher=}}</ref> On May 31, 2016, the band announced that they were touring as support on [[Hail the Sun]]'s tour, with additional support from Belle Noire, from July 18 to July 25, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/eidola/status/737703627775565826|title=Eidola on Twitter|publisher=}}</ref>

===2017–2019: ''To Speak, To Listen''===

Eidola toured as support on [[Dance Gavin Dance]] and [[Chon (band)|CHON]]'s co-headlining tour in North America, with Vasudeva, from February 24 to March 19, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/eidolaUT/posts/886491481387307:0|title=Eidola|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref> On March 28, 2017, it was announced that Eidola would tour in support of [[Hail the Sun]]'s headlining U.S. tour with American rock bands Capsize and Limbs, taking place from May 21 to June 24, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bringthenoise.com/blog/hail-the-sun-announce-headlining-tour-with|title=Hail The Sun Announce Headlining Tour With Capsize, Eidola and LIMBS / the NOISE|publisher=}}</ref>

On April 28, 2017, Eidola announced their third studio album, entitled ''To Speak, To Listen'', with a scheduled release date of June 2, 2017, on [[Blue Swan Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://livingthevanlife.wordpress.com/2017/04/28/eidola-announce-new-album/|title=Eidola – Announce New Album|date=28 April 2017|publisher=}}</ref> The band revealed that they had recorded with producer Dryw Owens. The album featured collaborations with American musician [[Joe Lancaster (musician)|Joey Lancaster]], former [[A Lot Like Birds]] bass guitarist Matthew Coate, and musician and saxophonist Nicholas Pope.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eidolamusic.bandcamp.com/album/to-speak-to-listen|title=To Speak, To Listen, by Eidola|website=Eidola}}</ref>

In support of ''To Speak, To Listen'', the band headlined a North American tour with musical groups [[The Ongoing Concept]] and Save Us From The Archon, which took place from August 17 to September 19, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/eidola-announce-summer-tour-with-the-ongoing-concept-120678|title=Eidola announce summer tour with The Ongoing Concept|publisher=}}</ref>

On December 15, 2017, Eidola announced their headlining To Speak, To Listen Tour, with support from rock band Capstan and singer Andrés, which took place from February 8 to March 3, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/eidolaUT/photos/a.595820093787782.1073741828.155947704441692/1101830789853374/?type=1&theater|title=Eidola|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref> On the same day, the group released an instrumental version of their third studio album, ''To Speak, To Listen''.<ref name="bandcamp.com2">{{cite web|url=https://eidolamusic.bandcamp.com/album/to-speak-to-listen-instrumental|title=To Speak, To Listen (Instrumental), by Eidola|website=Eidola}}</ref>

On December 31, 2017, the band announced the departure of guitarist Brandon Bascom and revealed that [[Sianvar]] guitarist, also formerly of [[Stolas (band)|Stolas]], Sergio Medina would be joining the line-up.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/eidolaUT/photos/a.307010796002048/1113236925379427/?type=3|title=Eidola|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref>

On November 9, 2018, the band announced they would be finishing their new album in January 2019, in addition to participating in [[Blue Swan Records|Swanfest]], headlined by [[Dance Gavin Dance]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/eidolaUT/photos/a.595820093787782/1421583041211479/|title=Eidola|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''The Architect''===

On November 11, 2019, the band confirmed that their fourth studio album was completed and would be "ready in early 2020" with a tour announcement to follow in support of its release.<ref name="twitter.com">https://twitter.com/eidola/status/1193936849829412865 {{Bare URL inline|date=November 2021}}</ref> In December 2019, it was confirmed that Eidola will be performing at the second annual [[Blue Swan Records#SwanFest|SwanFest]] on April 25, 2020, in [[Sacramento, California]].<ref>https://twitter.com/eidola/status/1205550636659396608 {{Bare URL inline|date=November 2021}}</ref> Due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], SwanFest was postponed to April 2022 and the group withheld the announcement of their 2020 headlining tour.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://nextmosh.com/dance-gavin-dance-animals-as-leaders-issues-veil-of-maya-spring-tour-postponed/ |title = Dance Gavin Dance, Animals as Leaders, Issues & Veil of Maya spring tour postponed &#124; NextMosh: Rock and Metal News|date = 13 March 2020}}</ref> In April 2020, Andrew Wells confirmed the title of their upcoming fourth record would be ''[[The Architect (Eidola album)|The Architect]]'' but expressed uncertainty on the release date amidst the [[COVID-19]] outbreak and its effect on the music industry.

On June 29, 2021, Eidola released the single "Counterfeit Shrines", their first new song in over four years, as the lead single from their fourth studio album ''The Architect'', which was released on September 17, 2021.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvQV1ZKIaGI</ref> The band toured as support on Dance Gavin Dance's [[Afterburner (Dance Gavin Dance album)|Afterburner Tour]] in September and October 2021.

==Musical style==

Eidola has been labeled [[post-hardcore]], [[experimental rock]], [[progressive rock]], [[post-rock]], [[metalcore]], [[math rock]], and [[progressive metal]].<ref name="allmusic.com" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.allaccess.com/an-interview-with-the-progressive-post-hardcore-band-eidola/|title=An Interview With The Progressive Post-Hardcore Band, EIDOLA! - All Access Music|website=music.allaccess.com|date=23 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://api.sonicbids.com/band/eidola/?destination=/find-band-openings/new/?destination=/manage/musician/?destination=%252Ffind-band-openings%252Fnew%252F|title=About Eidola|website=Sonicbids}}</ref>
