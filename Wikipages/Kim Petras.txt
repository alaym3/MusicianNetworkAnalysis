'''Kim Petras''' (born 27 August 1992) is a German singer and songwriter based in [[Los Angeles]]. Between 2016 and 2020, she released music as an independent artist under her own imprint, BunHead Records, before signing with [[Republic Records]] in 2021 where she will release her upcoming third album and major-label debut.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Aswad|first=Jem|date=26 August 2021|title=Pop Singer-Songwriter Kim Petras Signs With Republic Records (EXCLUSIVE) |url=https://variety.com/2021/music/news/kim-petras-republic-records-1235049989/|access-date=27 August 2021|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]}}</ref>

Petras began recording music as a teenager, releasing her debut extended play ''One Piece of Tape'' in 2011. She independently released her debut single in 2017, "[[I Don't Want It at All]]", which went on to top several viral music charts on [[Spotify]].<ref name="Galore">{{cite web |url=https://galoremag.com/kim-petras-single-i-dont-want-it-all-bratty-banger/ |title=Kim Petras' new single is a bratty banger |date=16 January 2018 |access-date=3 March 2018 |archive-date=22 October 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191022164926/https://galoremag.com/kim-petras-single-i-dont-want-it-all-bratty-banger/}}</ref> The song was followed by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''-charting singles "[[Feeling of Falling]]" (with [[Cheat Codes (DJs)|Cheat Codes]]), "[[Heart to Break]]", and "[[1, 2, 3 Dayz Up]]" (featuring [[Sophie (musician)|Sophie]]).

Following her early success, Petras released a collection of digital singles which would later comprise her unofficial body of work known as ''Era 1''. Petras released her debut project, ''[[Clarity (Kim Petras album)|Clarity]]'', on 28 June 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ahlgrim|first=Callie|title=Kim Petras breaks down the 3-year creation of her new single 'Malibu' and teases her colorful debut album: 'I don't feel heartbroken anymore'|url=https://www.insider.com/kim-petras-malibu-lead-single-debut-album-interview-2020-5|access-date=10 August 2021|website=Insider}}</ref> The record was preceded with a nine-week-long promotional campaign, during which Petras released one single per week with an accompanying visual lyric video. ''Clarity'' received acclaim from music critics, reached number seven on the [[Heatseekers]] Chart and number 26 on the [[Independent Albums]] chart. Following this, Petras released a Halloween-themed album ''[[Turn Off the Light (album)|Turn Off the Light]]'' in October 2019. In 2020, Petras saw international chart success with her singles "[[Malibu (Kim Petras song)|Malibu]]" and "[[Broken Glass (Kygo and Kim Petras song)|Broken Glass]]", the latter being a collaboration with [[Kygo]].
Petras achieved more success with a snippet release and a MTV EMA performance of her upcoming song " Coconuts" which went viral on TikTok and brought her to more attention.

== Early life ==

Kim Petras was born in [[Cologne]], [[North Rhine-Westphalia]], Germany.<ref name="allmusic2">{{cite web|last=Donelson|first=Marcy|title=Kim Petras Biography|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kim-petras-mn0003130856/biography|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190528194140/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kim-petras-mn0003130856/biography|archive-date=28 May 2019|access-date=28 May 2019|publisher=allmusic}}</ref><ref name="aboutme">{{cite web|date=20 February 2012|title=About me « Kim Petras|url=http://kimpetrasonline.com/about-me/?lang=en|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120220144257/http://kimpetrasonline.com/about-me/?lang=en|archive-date=20 February 2012}}</ref> While she was [[Sex assignment#AMAB|assigned male at birth]], her parents said that from the age of two, Petras knew she was a girl.

In 2006, Petras, then aged 13, appeared on a German television current affairs show in which she discussed her medical [[gender transitioning|gender transition]]. At age 14, Petras appeared in a [[documentary]] and a [[talk show]],<ref>[http://www.stern.de/wissen/mensch/transsexualitaet-kims-kindheit-im-falschen-koerper-586766.html Kims Kindheit im falschen Körper] ("Kim's childhood in the wrong body") (German) – [[Stern (magazine)|Stern]] (2006)</ref><ref>[http://www.stern.de/tv/reportage/:Reportage-VOX-Mann-Frau-Menschen-K%F6rper-/624510.html "Mann oder Frau?"]{{dead link|date=December 2011}} – television documentary on [[VOX (German TV channel)]] show Stern TV-Reportage</ref> in a push to get permission for early [[sex reassignment surgery]] at age 16, before the minimum age of 18 in Germany. These appearances resulted in international media coverage of her transition, touting her as the "world's youngest transsexual".<ref>{{cite web |title=The Glow-Up of Kim Petras |date=26 July 2019 |url=https://www.papermag.com/kim-petras-clarity-project-2639362951.html |publisher=Paper |access-date=1 March 2020 |archive-date=1 March 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200301191447/https://www.papermag.com/kim-petras-clarity-project-2639362951.html}}</ref>

In September 2007, she was a model for a German chain of hair salons.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20120220141556/http://www.unisex-friseure.de/Events/Shootings/Default.aspx Fotoshooting mit Kim im Unisex Bonn 1 September 2007] – unisex-friseure.de</ref> Petras was evaluated by the head of the psychiatric unit at Frankfurt Hospital, Dr Bernd Meyenburg, and approved for gender confirmation surgery at 16 years old;<ref name="heavy">{{cite web|date=15 November 2019|title=Kim Petras 5 Fast Facts You Need to Know|url=https://heavy.com/entertainment/2019/11/kim-petras|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200301191504/https://heavy.com/entertainment/2019/11/kim-petras/|archive-date=1 March 2020|access-date=1 March 2020|publisher=heavy.com}}</ref> Petras announced, in November 2008, that the surgery was completed.<ref>[https://archive.today/20120718182523/http://kimperium.blogspot.com/2008/11/nur-mal-nebenbei.html Gender reassignment surgery blog post] (26 November 2008)</ref> ''[[The Daily Telegraph]]'' claimed Petras was the youngest person in the world to have had that surgery at the time. About her surgery, she stated "I was asked if I feel like a woman now – but the truth is I have always felt like a woman – I just ended up in the wrong body".<ref name="outprofile">{{cite web |title=Trans Pop Star Kim Petras Shares Debut Dr. Luke-Produced Single |date=2 August 2017 |url=https://www.out.com/music/2017/8/02/trans-pop-star-kim-petras-shares-debut-dr-luke-produced-single |publisher=out.com |access-date=1 March 2020 |archive-date=23 June 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190623173204/https://www.out.com/music/2017/8/02/trans-pop-star-kim-petras-shares-debut-dr-luke-produced-single}}</ref>

In an interview with [[BuzzFeed]], Petras stated that the first song she ever wrote was "about this dude in second grade who didn't like me back".<ref>{{cite web |title=Kim Petras Tells Us About Her First Times |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y9t09g79lSs |website=YouTube |publisher=BuzzFeed Celeb |access-date=10 July 2020 |archive-date=12 July 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200712091804/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y9t09g79lSs }}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2008–2014: Career beginnings ===
[[File:Kim Petras.jpg|thumb|Petras performing in 2012]]
Between 2008 and 2009, Petras released a string of singles under Joyce Records: "Fade Away" (2008), "Last Forever," "Die for You," and "Boomerang" (all 2009). In 2009, she was featured on "Taste" by Sobertruth and in 2011 was featured on "Magnetic". Later in 2011, Petras released her debut [[extended play]], ''One Piece of Tape'', under the imprint Bionic Ballroom.

In 2013, Petras was featured on two singles named "Flight to Paris" and "Hearbeat" by German DJ Klaas. Throughout the next few years, Petras worked with producers including [[The Stereotypes]], C.J. Abraham, Stephen Dresser, Johan "Jones" Wetterberg, Edward Ellis, and Aaron Joseph on a music career, releasing [[Demo (music)|demos]] on her [[SoundCloud]] page, including one titled "STFU".<ref name="NastyGal" /> For her contributions to [[social media]], Petras was ranked at number 19 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'s}} Artist Chart, listing developing artists, in July 2013.<ref name="history">{{cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?f[0]=ts_chart_artistname%3Akim%20petras&f[1]=itm_field_chart_id%3A-&f[2]=ss_bb_type%3Achart_item&type=2&artist=kim%20petras |title=Kim Petras – Chart history |publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=31 October 2018}}</ref> In 2015, Petras appeared on "You" by Isaac Phase for his album, ''Phase 00001''.

=== 2019–2020: ''Clarity'' and ''Turn Off the Light'' ===
[[File:Kim Petras Aaron Joseph Clarity Tour 2019.jpg|thumb|Petras and Aaron Joseph on The Clarity Tour in 2019]]

Throughout May and June, Petras released one song a week as a lead-up to the album's release.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/kim-petras-announces-new-collection-of-tracks-clarity|title=Kim Petras announces new collection of tracks Clarity|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|last=Kenneally|first=Cerys|date=13 June 2019|access-date=14 June 2019|archive-date=16 June 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190616063145/https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/kim-petras-announces-new-collection-of-tracks-clarity|url-status=dead}}</ref> In June, Petras embarked on the 24 date "Broken Tour" across North America and Europe.<ref>{{cite web |last=Robinson |first=Dominiq |title=Kim Petras announces 'Broken' Tour 2019 |url=https://www.axs.com/kim-petras-announces-broken-tour-2019-137958 |website=AXS |access-date=21 May 2019 |archive-date=1 May 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190501195750/https://www.axs.com/kim-petras-announces-broken-tour-2019-137958}}</ref> Petras was featured on the covers of ''Galore'' and ''Notion'' magazines that same month. In August, Petras announced that there would be a limited edition vinyl pressing of ''Turn Off the Light, Vol. 1'' through [[Urban Outfitters]]. Petras has announced that ''Turn Off the Light, Vol. 2'' will be released exactly a year after the first part. == Artistry and public image ==
Kim Petras is a [[Pop music|pop]] artist who also makes [[electronic dance music]] (EDM), [[dance-pop]], [[electropop]] and [[Bubblegum music|bubblegum pop]].<ref name="allmusic2"/><ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Lipshutz |first1=Jason |title=First Stream: New Music From Harry Styles, Camila Cabello, Sam Hunt & More |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8532772/first-stream-harry-styles-camila-cabello-sam-hunt |magazine=Billboard |access-date=13 October 2019 |archive-date=19 October 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191019050729/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8532772/first-stream-harry-styles-camila-cabello-sam-hunt}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Wetmore|first=Brendan|title=Charli XCX Dissects Her Album, Track by Track|url=https://www.papermag.com/charli-xcx-track-by-track-2640323881.html?rebelltitem=64#rebelltitem64|website=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|date=16 September 2019|accessdate=4 July 2021}}</ref> She credits the late 1990s and early 2000s pop scene,<ref name="Faded" /> as well as 1980s [[Italo disco]],<ref name="PAPER" /> as the primary basis for her sound. Petras' inspirations include [[Katy Perry]], [[Lady Gaga]], [[Beyoncé]], [[Britney Spears]], [[Christina Aguilera]], [[Kylie Minogue]], [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]] and the [[Spice Girls]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://nyunews.com/music/2019/06/26/pops-newest-princess-is-here-and-her-name-is-kim-petras/ |title=Pop's Newest Princess is Here, and Her Name is Kim Petras |date=26 June 2019 |access-date=17 May 2020 |archive-date=11 February 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200211122412/https://nyunews.com/music/2019/06/26/pops-newest-princess-is-here-and-her-name-is-kim-petras}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://junkee.com/kim-petras-interview/196546 |title=Kim Petras Wants It All, And She Doesn't Care If She Pisses People Off |last=Richards |first=Jared |date=15 March 2019 |access-date=16 May 2019 |archive-date=23 August 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190823222810/https://junkee.com/kim-petras-interview/196546}}</ref><ref name="Faded">{{cite web |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/a3np5e/kim-petras-faded-video-premiere-interview |title=Kim Petras Is a True, Unapologetic Poptimist |date=25 January 2018 |access-date=3 March 2018 |archive-date=17 June 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180617170614/https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/a3np5e/kim-petras-faded-video-premiere-interview}}</ref> as well as [[Boy George]], [[Debbie Harry]], [[Queen (band)|Queen]], [[Freddie Mercury]],<ref name="NastyGal" /> [[Judy Garland]], Baby E, Lil Aaron, and [[Liz Y2K]].<ref name="Galore" /> In an interview, Petras stated that "To me, pop music is an escape from my problems. I can put on my headphones and listen to it for three and a half minutes to forget about everything that's bothering me. It's always been that way for me. I feel like pop definitely saved my life in so many ways."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.idolator.com/7822785/kim-petras-is-a-glam-goddess-in-new-spread-for-notion?chrome=1 |title=Kim Petras is a Glam Goddess in New Spread for 'Notion' |date=5 July 2019}}</ref>

On her song writing, Petras told ''Noisey'' that "there's something about making a song that everybody can sing and remember, and when you listen to it the first time you already know the words by the second chorus, like you've always known the song. I'm obsessed with that idea."<ref name="Faded" /> She commented on themes, stating "I write about boys, heartbreak, sex, having fun and the things that I go through."<ref name="Galore" />
