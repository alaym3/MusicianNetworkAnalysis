'''The Bee Family''' (formerly the '''Eh Bee Family''') are Canadian internet personalities best known for their [[Vine (service)|Vine]], [[YouTube]], and [[Instagram]] channels, which have over 9 million combined subscribers and well over 2 billion total views.<ref name="today">{{cite news|last1=Kennedy|first1=Lesley|title=Move over, BatDad! Meet Vine's latest hilarious family| url=http://www.today.com/parents/move-over-batdad-meet-vine-s-latest-hilarious-family-t75201|accessdate=14 January 2012|publisher=Today|date=27 February 2014}}</ref> They create skits and parodies to entertain a family-centered audience. They are a family of four based in Thornhill, Ontario<ref name="gazette_review">{{cite web|last1=Carter|first1=Brooke|title=Andres B Net Worth 2018|url=http://gazettereview.com/2018/01/andres-b-net-worth/|website=Gazette Review|accessdate=21 January 2018|date=15 January 2018}}</ref> consisting of the father (Andrés Burgos, known as Papa Bee), the mother (Rossana Burgos, known as Mama Bee), their son (Roberto Burgos, known as Mr Monkey or Mr Bee), and their daughter (Gabriela Burgos, also known as Gabriela Bee, Miss Monkey, or Miss Bee).<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Luscombe |first=Belinda |date=18 May 2017 |title=The YouTube Parents Who are Turning Family Moments into Big Bucks |url=http://time.com/4783215/growing-up-in-public/ |access-date=12 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.yorkregion.com/news-story/1935371-people-we-love-rossana-burgos/ |title=PEOPLE WE LOVE: Rossana Burgos |last=Kelly |first=Tim |date=2013-01-23 |website=YorkRegion.com |language=en-CA |access-date=2018-01-12}}</ref>

== Career ==
The Eh Bee Family first started posting vines in January 2013. They became popular on Vine and made it their career. Their social media accounts include [[Vine (service)|Vine]], [[YouTube]], [[Instagram]], and [[Facebook]]. They have also starred in sponsored advertisements for such companies as [[Johnson & Johnson]], [[Toyota]], and [[Regal Cinemas]] and have also been featured in the ''[[New York Daily News]]'',<ref>Amos, Candace. [http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/5-hysterical-vine-dads-absolutely-article-1.2252183 "5 hysterical Vine dads you should absolutely be following"], ''The New York Daily News'', June 9, 2015</ref> on [[Buzzfeed]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Watch This Family Be Awesome And Hilarious On Vine|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/krystieyandoli/watch-this-family-be-awesome-and-hilarious-on-vine|website=BuzzFeed}}</ref> [[Good Morning America]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Video: Celebrating Superstar Dads That Use Social Media|url=https://abcnews.go.com/GMA/video/superstar-dads-gma-dads-social-media-24072968|website=ABC News}}</ref> [[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today.com]],<ref>Kennedy, Lesley. [http://www.today.com/parents/move-over-batdad-meet-vine-s-latest-hilarious-family-t75201 "Move over, BatDad! Meet Vine's latest hilarious family"], Today.com, February 27, 2014, accessed April 16, 2016</ref> and other media outlets. The family's social media was nominated for a [[Streamy Awards|Streamy Award]] in 2015<ref>Rosa, Christopher. [http://www.vh1.com/news/49160/streamy-award-nominees "And the Nominees for the 5th Annual Streamy Awards Are…"], VH1, August 12, 2015</ref> and a [[Shorty Awards|Shorty Award]] in 2016.<ref>Lee, Ashley. [http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/lists/shorty-awards-2016-nominations-full-871929/item/arts-entertainment-actor-shorty-awards-871903 "Shorty Awards: The Complete Nominations List"], ''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'', March 1, 2016</ref> On February 17, 2018, Gabriela Bee released her song "Sound in Color".<ref name="gabi apple music">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/gabriela-bee/1339907179|title=Gabriela Bee on Apple Music|website=Apple Music}}</ref> On September 1, 2018, Gabriela Bee released another song, "Something More".<ref name="gabi apple music" />

On April 2019, Gabriela posted a cover version of the Beatles’ 1968 hit song [[Ob-La-Di, Ob-La-Da]] on YouTube, appearing in the video as seven different musicians and performing all of the vocals by herself. By November 2021, the song had earned more than 29 million views.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vPukHebsQeQ]</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://seeitlive.co/girl-band-covers-beatles/|title=Teen girl covers all parts of Beatles song herself, gets more than 9 million views on YouTube}}</ref>

On July 15, 2020, the family changed their name from the "Eh Bee Family" to "The Bee Family".
