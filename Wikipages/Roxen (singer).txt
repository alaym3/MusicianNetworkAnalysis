'''Larisa Roxana Giurgiu''' ({{IPA-ro|laˈrisa rokˈsana ˈdʒjurdʒju}}; born 5 January 2000), known professionally as '''Roxen''' ({{IPA-ro|rokˈsen|pron}}), is a Romanian singer signed to [[Global Records]]. Roxen rose to prominence upon being featured on Romanian producer [[Alexandru Cotoi|Sickotoy]]'s "[[You Don't Love Me (Sickotoy song)|You Don't Love Me]]" (2019), which reached number three in [[Airplay 100 (Romania)|Romania]] and received radio airplay in several other countries. The singer's following singles, "[[Ce-ți cântă dragostea]]" and "Spune-mi", eventually peaked atop the national chart. Roxen was scheduled to [[Romania in the Eurovision Song Contest 2020|represent Romania]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2020]] with the song "[[Alcohol You]]" before the event's [[coronavirus disease 2019]] (COVID-19)-related cancellation. The singer was instead internally selected as [[Romania in the Eurovision Song Contest 2021|the country's representative]] for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021|2021 contest]] with the song "[[Amnesia (Roxen song)|Amnesia]]", but failed to progress from the first semi-final, finishing in 12th place.

==Life and career==
===2000–2019: Early life and career beginnings===
Born Larisa Roxana Giurgiu on 5 January 2000 in [[Cluj-Napoca]], Roxen discovered a passion for music at the age of seven and took singing and piano lessons.<ref name="dc"/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.zi-de-zi.ro/2019/10/20/o-noua-editie-a-festivalului-armonii-muzicale/|title=O noua editie a Festivalului "Armonii muzicale"|trans-title=A new edition of the "Musical Harmonies" festival|date=20 October 2019|author=Gondor, Andreea|access-date=11 February 2020|language=ro|publisher=Zi de zi}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://a1.ro/news/social/roxen-dua-lipa-de-romania-reprezentanta-noastra-la-eurovision-2020-id959370.html|title=Dua Lipa de România, reprezentanta noastră la Eurovision 2020|trans-title=The Romanian Dua Lipa is our Eurovision 2020 representative|language=ro|date=12 February 2020|access-date=12 February 2020|publisher=[[Antena 1 (Romania)|Antena 1]]}} ''Note: Play the video.''</ref> Signed by [[Global Records]],<ref name="artists">{{cite news|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2020/02/01/romania-tvr-confirms-partnership-with-global-records-for-internal-selection-of-2020-artist/248846/|title=Romania: TVR confirms partnership with Global Records for internal selection of 2020 artist|publisher=[[Wiwibloggs]]|date=1 February 2020|access-date=1 February 2020|author=Gallagher, Robyn}}</ref> the singer was featured on Romanian producer [[Alexandru Cotoi|Sickotoy]]'s "[[You Don't Love Me (Sickotoy song)|You Don't Love Me]]" in August 2019.<ref name="youdontloveme"/> The song attained commercial success in Romania, reaching number three on the country's [[Airplay 100 (Romania)|Airplay 100]] chart,<ref name="Romania"/> and being playlisted by radio stations in several territories including France, the United States, Russia and Spain.<ref name="dc"/> Roxen's debut solo single "[[Ce-ți cântă dragostea]]" released later that year in November peaked at number one in Romania.<ref name="Romania"/> [[Music journalism|Music critics]] have since regarded Roxen's primary genre as being [[deep house]],<ref name="dc">{{cite news|url=https://www.dcnews.ro/roxen-cucereste-topurile-muzicale-melodiile-ei-o-terapie_727411.html|title=Roxen cucerește topurile muzicale!|trans-title=Roxen attains success on music charts!|language=ro|date=3 February 2020|access-date=5 February 2020|publisher=DC News|author=Scutaru, Cristina}}</ref> and compared the singer's music style and appearance to that of [[Dua Lipa]] and [[Billie Eilish]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.unica.ro/video/exclusiv-roxen-ne-spune-povestea-vietii-ei-299682|title=Roxen ne spune povestea vieții ei|trans-title=Roxen tells us the story of her life|language=ro|work=Unica|date=7 January 2020|access-date=5 February 2020}}</ref> In a București FM interview, Roxen cited [[Beyoncé]] as a major inspiration.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yiTeZw93xTM|title=Roxen, reprezentantul Romaniei la Eurovision, in de 10X Romania alaturi de Razvan Petre|trans-title=Roxen, Romania's Eurovision representative, on 10X Romania with Razvan Petre|date=6 March 2020|access-date=7 March 2020|language=ro|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>

===2020–present: Eurovision and continued success===
In early February 2020, it was reported that Roxen was one of three artists shortlisted to [[Romania in the Eurovision Song Contest|represent Romania]] at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2020]]; broadcaster [[Romanian Television]] (TVR) had collaborated with Global Records for the selection.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://escbubble.com/2020/02/romania-will-reveal-the-representative-on-10th-february/|title=Romania will reveal the representative on 10th February|publisher=ESCBubble|date=4 February 2020|access-date=4 February 2020|author=Muntean, Marco}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tvr.ro/tvr-i-global-records-parteneri-pentru-eurovision-song-contest-2020_26414.html#view|title=TVR și Global Records, parteneri pentru Eurovision Song Contest 2020|trans-title=TVR and Global Records, partners for the 2020 Eurovision Song Contest|date=31 January 2020|access-date=31 January 2020|language=ro|publisher=[[Romanian Television]]}}</ref> On 11 February, the singer was revealed by TVR as the Romanian representative.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://eurovision.tv/video/roxen-will-represent-romania-at-the-eurovision-song-contest-2020|title=Roxen will represent Romania at the Eurovision Song Contest 2020|publisher=[[European Broadcasting Union]]|date=11 February 2020|access-date=11 February 2020}}</ref> The five songs for ''[[Romania in the Eurovision Song Contest 2020#Selecția Națională 2020|Selecția Națională]]''—"[[Alcohol You]]", "Beautiful Disaster", "Cherry Red", "Colors" and "Storm"—were released on 21 February.<ref name="songssn">{{cite news|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2020/02/21/romanias-roxen-releases-snippets-of-her-five-songs-up-for-contention-for-eurovision-2020/250352/|title=Romania's Roxen releases snippets of her five songs in contention for Eurovision 2020|publisher=[[Wiwibloggs]]|date=21 February 2020|access-date=21 February 2020|author=Percy, Lucy}}</ref> "Alcohol You" emerged as the winner of the national final on 1 March.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://escxtra.com/2020/03/01/roxen-to-sing-alcohol-you-in-rotterdam/|title=Roxen to sing "Alcohol You" in Rotterdam!|publisher=ESCXtra|author=Luukela, Sami|date=1 March 2020|access-date=1 March 2020}}</ref> However, on 18 March, the [[European Broadcasting Union]] (EBU) announced the cancellation of the Eurovision Song Contest 2020 due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic|pandemic]] of the [[coronavirus disease 2019]] (COVID-19).<ref name="cancellation">{{cite news|url=https://eurovision.tv/story/eurovision-2020-in-rotterdam-is-cancelled|title=Eurovision 2020 in Rotterdam is cancelled|publisher=[[European Broadcasting Union]]|date=18 March 2020|access-date=18 March 2020}}</ref> Although TVR had considered retaining "Alcohol You" as a song for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://escxtra.com/2020/03/18/roxen-and-tvr-release-statements-regarding-eurovision/|title=Roxen and TVR release statements regarding Eurovision|publisher=ESCXtra|author=Luukela, Sami|date=18 March 2020|access-date=18 March 2020}}</ref> EBU soon after announced that intended 2020 entries were not eligible for the following year.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://eurovision.tv/story/intention-to-honour-eurovision-2020-songs-artists|title=Intention to honour Eurovision 2020 songs and artists|publisher=[[European Broadcasting Union]]|date=20 March 2020|access-date=20 March 2020}}</ref> Roxen was nonetheless internally selected for the 2021 contest.<ref name="roxen 2021">{{cite news|url=http://eurovision.tvr.ro/2020/tvr-a-decis-roxen-va-reprezenta-romania-la-eurovision-song-contest-2021_26787.html|title=TVR a decis: Roxen va reprezenta Romania la Eurovision Song Contest 2021|language=ro|publisher=[[Romanian Television]]|date=31 March 2020|access-date=31 March 2020|trans-title=TVR has decided: Roxen will represent Romania at the Eurovision Song Contest 2021}}</ref>

In June 2020, the singer was hired as the brand ambassador for Romanian box-dye hair color product Loncolor Expert HEMPstyle, appearing on product packagings and in a commercial which prominently featured the ''Selecția Națională'' entry "Storm".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2020/06/07/romania-pretty-in-purple-roxen-turns-heads-as-brand-ambassador-for-hair-colour-range/255172/|title=Romania: Pretty in purple! Roxen turns heads as brand ambassador for hair colour range|publisher=[[Wiwibloggs]]|date=7 June 2020|access-date=23 June 2020|author=Gallagher, Robyn}}</ref> Starting with the release of "How to Break a Heart" in July, Roxen had been benefiting from a promotional campaign for which Global Records partnered with [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.musicbusinessworldwide.com/warner-music-names-global-records-as-licensee-in-romania/|title=Warner Music names Global Records as licensee in Romania|publisher=Music Business Worldwide|author=Stassen, Murray|date=4 August 2020|access-date=5 August 2020}}</ref> In October, Roxen scored a second number-one single on the Airplay 100 with "Spune-mi".<ref name="Romania"/> "[[Amnesia (Roxen song)|Amnesia]]", Roxen's Eurovision 2021 entry, was selected by an internal jury panel and released on 4 March.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://eurovision.tvr.ro/2021/tvr-prezinta-juriul-eurovision-romania-2021_31129.html|title=TVR prezinta juriul Eurovision Romania 2021|trans-title=TVR presents the Eurovision jury|language=Romanian|publisher=[[Romanian Television]]|access-date=3 March 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://escxtra.com/2021/03/02/roxen-eurovision-2021-4-march/|title=Roxen's Eurovision 2021 entry to be released on 4th March|language=Romanian|date=2 March 2021|access-date=3 March 2021|publisher=ESCXtra}}</ref> It was performed in the first semi-final of the contest on 18 May 2021, but failed to qualify for the final, finishing in 12th place with 85 points.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eurovision.tv/story/first-semi-final-qualifiers|title=First Semi-Final 2021: The ==Personal life==
At the age of 14, Roxen was diagnosed with [[lyme disease]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.profm.ro/stiri/diagnosticul-grav-primit-de-roxen-la-14-ani-sursa-de-inspiratie-pentru-amnesia-piesa-cu-care-ne-reprezinta-la-eurovision-2021-117591|title=Diagnosticul grav primit de Roxen la 14 ani, sursa de inspirație pentru "Amnesia", piesa cu care ne reprezintă la Eurovision 2021|trans-title=The serious disease Roxen which was diagnosed with at the age of 14 and which is the inspiration for her Eurovision 2021 entry "Amnesia"|language=Romanian|publisher=[[Pro FM]]|date=17 May 2021}}</ref> Roxen is [[Vegetarianism|vegetarian]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2021/04/08/roxen-10-facts-about-romanias-eurovision-2021-singer/264075/|title=Roxen: 10 facts about Romania's Eurovision 2021 singer|publisher=[[Wiwibloggs]]|date=8 April 2021|access-date=11 June 2021|author=Smith, David}}</ref> As of June 2020, the singer resides in [[Bucharest]]. At the beginning of the same month, Roxen was allegedly emotionally abused and [[blackmail]]ed by an ex-boyfriend and obtained a [[restraining order]] against him after having made a complaint to the local police.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.profm.ro/stiri/entertainment/celebri/roxen-ordin-de-restrictie-pentru-fostul-iubit-ce-a-declarat-artista-104693|title=Roxen, ordin de restrictie pentru fostul iubit|trans-title=Roxen, restraining order against ex-boyfriend|language=Romanian|publisher=[[Pro FM]]|access-date=23 June 2020}}</ref> In July 2021, the singer [[Coming out|came out]] as [[Non-binary gender|non-binary]] in a [[TikTok]] video,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Roxen on TikTok – Reply to @sotmarieic|url=https://m.tiktok.com/v/6988983656010353926.html?_r=1|date=26 July 2021|access-date=29 July 2021|publisher=[[TikTok]]}}</ref> and eventually clarified that they identify with all pronouns.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Roxen on TikTok – Reply to @kooltaurus.com|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@roxen/video/6990322064628436229|language=ro|date=29 July 2021|access-date=12 August 2021|publisher=[[TikTok]]}}</ref> Roxen furthermore stated: "Since I was little, I feel like a boy in a girl's body. In my previous life I was really a boy".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2021/08/03/romanian-singer-roxen-comes-out-as-non-binary/266235/|title='I always thought I was a boy in a girl's body' — Romania's Roxen comes out as non-binary|publisher=[[Wiwibloggs]]|date=3 August 2021|access-date=15 August 2021|author=Adams, Oliver}}</ref>
