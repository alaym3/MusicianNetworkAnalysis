'''Nsikayesizwe David Junior Ngcobo'''<ref name="name">{{cite web|url=https://www.forbesafrica.com/entertainment/2020/06/08/the-def-jam-star-and-shape-shifter-in-african-music-i-come-from-a-place-where-dreaming-is-not-a-thing/|title=The Def Jam Star And Trend-Setter In African Music: 'I Come From A Place Where Dreaming Is Not A Thing'|website=[[Forbes (magazine)|Forbes Africa]]|last=Methil|first=Renuka|date=June 8, 2020|access-date=8 June 2020}}</ref> (born 11 February 1997), known professionally as '''Nasty C''', is a [[South Africa]]n rapper, songwriter, and record producer.

After releasing three [[mixtapes]], one being "Undisputed" and two [[Extended play|EP]]s, Ngcobo released his debut album ''[[Bad Hair (album)|Bad Hair]]'' (2016), and ''[[Strings and Bling]]'' (2018) which was associated and supported by his first tour, ''The Ivyson Tour''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://trueafrica.co/article/nasty-cs-bad-hair-turns-out-good-the-review/|title=Nasty C's Bad Hair turns out good - the review|website=TRUE Africa}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://theplugmag.com/album-review-nasty-cs-strings-bling/|title=Album Review – Nasty C's "Strings & Bling"|website=The Plug}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.informamoz.site/2021/03/nasty-c-pede-desculpas-ao-eminem.html|title=Nasty C diz ao Eminem para não prestar atenção ao seu vídeo recente|website=INFORMA Moz}}</ref>

He was the youngest recipient of an award at the [[South African Hip Hop Awards 2015|2015 South African Hip Hop Awards]], namely the Best Freshman Award.<ref>{{cite web |title=2015 SOUTH AFRICAN HIP HOP AWARD WINNERS |url=http://www.mtvbase.com/news/2015-sa-hip-hop-awards-winners/z21iyq |website=MTV Base |access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Klein |first1=Alyssa |title=And The 2015 South African Hip-Hop Awards Winners Are... |url=http://www.okayafrica.com/south-african-hip-hop-awards-2015-winners/ |website=OkayAfrica |access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref>

In March 2020, Nasty C signed a joint venture deal with [[Def Jam Records]], and released his United States official single after joining Def Jam, "There They Go", which gained him global recognition, he then released his third album on 28 August 2020, and his first on the label which is titled ''[[Zulu Man With Some Power]]'' (2020).

==Life and career==
===1997–2015: Early life and initial mixtapes===
Nsikayesizwe David Junior Ngcobo was born on 11 February 1997 in [[Diepkloof]], a borough of [[Soweto, Johannesburg]]. After the passing of his mother who was involved in a fatal car crash while he was 11 months, he relocated to [[Durban, Kwa-Zulu Natal]] to be raised by his father, David Maviyo Ngcobo, who is a [[human resources]] manager.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.channel24.co.za/The-Juice/News/this-is-how-nasty-c-got-his-name-20180302|title=This is how Nasty C got his name|website=Channel24}}</ref> He has an older brother, Siyabonga Ngcobo, who influenced him to rap as he learnt the basics of producing music from him at the age of nine. Ngcobo attended Strelitzia Secondary School in Lotus Park, [[Durban]] where he completed his 12th grade.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://roodepoortnorthsider.co.za/253457/nasty-c-goes-big-at-20/|title=Nasty C goes big at 20|website=Roodepoort Northsider}}</ref>

At the age of fourteen, Ngcobo released his debut [[mixtape]] ''One Kid, a Thousand Coffins'' on 14 May 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/one-kid-a-thousand-coffins/651467364|title=One Kid a Thousand Coffins by Nasty_C on Apple Music|website=Apple Music}}</ref> He then released his debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''L.A.M.E'' (Levitating Above My Enemies) on 4 April 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Nasty_C, Nasty_C New Releases: C L.A.M.E EP (Extended Play Version) on Beatport|url = https://pro.beatport.com/release/c-l-a-m-e-ep-extended-play-version/1290065|website = pro.beatport.com|access-date = 2016-01-21}}</ref> The following year on 10 February, he released his second mixtape ''Price City'' which produced the successful single "Juice Back" that raised his prominence.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ecr.co.za/shows/damon-beard-/nasty-c-drops-hot-video/|title=Nasty C drops hot video|website=East Coast Radio}}</ref> "Juice Back" later spawned a [[Gemini Major]]-produced remix featuring singer [[Davido]] and rapper [[Cassper Nyovest]] on 20 November 2015.

===2016–2017: ''Bad Hair''===
{{Main|Bad Hair (album)}}

On 11 February 2016, Ngcobo released "[[Hell Naw]]" which was later revealed to be the lead single from his upcoming debut album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.peoplemagazine.co.za/entertainment/jam-day-nasty-c-hell-naw/|title=Jam of the Day: Nasty C – Hell Naw|website=People Magazine}}</ref> On 23 September, Ngcobo then released his debut album, ''[[Bad Hair (album)|Bad Hair]]'' under Mabala Noise Entertainment. For the album's [[Cover art|artwork]], he got his fans on [[Twitter]] to email him their portraits showing their bad hair periods, he then combined all the portraits in an artistic manner assembling his face.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sahiphopmag.co.za/2016/08/nasty-c-debuts-bad-hair-album-cover/|title=Nasty C Debuts The "Bad Hair" Album Cover|website=SA Hip Hop Mag}}</ref> ''Bad Hair'' reached number-one on the [[iTunes]] local charts and was the most streamed album in South Africa within its 24 hours.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://quenchsa.com/2016/09/27/nasty-c-bad-hair-itunes/|title=Nasty C's Bad Hair Tops iTunes Album Chart, Despite Being Free|website=Quench SA}}</ref> Ngcobo released a 14-minute film featuring three songs from ''Bad Hair'' directed by Kyle Lewis. The short-film which included tracks "Don't Do It'", "Good Girls and Snapchat Hoes" and "Phases" featuring Rowlene, was commercially featured in several countries including France, Germany, United Kingdom and United States, which was infrequent for an African video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ecr.co.za/news/entertainment/apple-music-praises-nasty-cs-epic-bad-hair-video/|title=Apple Music praises Nasty C's 'epic' Bad Hair video|website=EastCoastRadio}}</ref>

On 2 December 2016, Ngcobo released the extended version for ''Bad Hair'' named ''[[Bad Hair (album)#Bad Hair Extensions|Bad Hair Extensions]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Nasty-C-Bad-Hair-Extensions/release/12440593|title=Nasty C - Bad Hair Extensions (File, AAC, Album)|website=Discogs}}</ref> ''Bad Hair Extension'' featured new four songs including the lead single ''Allow'' featuring American rapper [[French Montana]]. In winning many accolades for both 2016 and 2017, Ngcobo received his biggest nomination which was for the [[BET Awards 2017|2017]] [[BET Award for Best International Act: Africa]] where he lost to [[Wizkid]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailysun.co.za/News/Entertainment/babes-aka-and-nasty-c-nominted-for-bet-awards-20170516|title=BABES, AKA AND NASTY C NOMINATED FOR BET AWARDS!|website=Daily Sun}}</ref> He was also nominated for the [[2017 MTV Europe Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dstv.com/news/babes-wodumo-nasty-c-nominated-for-mtvema-20171005|title=Babes Wodumo, Nasty C nominated for #MTVEMA|website=DStv}}</ref> Ngcobo was ranked number-three on the 2017 [[MTV Base: SA's Hottest MCs]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thesouthafrican.com/mtv-base-south-africas-hottestmcs-list-2018/|title=MTV Base drops off South Africa's #HottestMCs list|website=The South African}}</ref>

===2018: UMG deals and ''Strings and Bling''===
{{Main|Strings and Bling}}

On 16 February,  Ngcobo signed a distribution deal with Universal Music Africa<ref>{{cite web |title=Nasty C confirms exclusive deal with Universal Music |url=https://www.iol.co.za/entertainment/music/local/nasty-c-confirms-exclusive-deal-with-universal-music-13319750 |website=[[Independent Online (South Africa)|Independent Online]] |access-date=26 June 2020 |language=en}}</ref> in partnership with Universal Music South Africa and a talent booking deal with UMG Live Africa.<ref>{{cite web |title=rapper nasty c signs exclusive deal with universal music and umg live |url=https://umglive.africa/rapper-nasty-c-signs-exclusive-deal-with-universal-music-and-umg-live/ |website=UMG Live Africa |access-date=26 June 2020}}</ref> On 1 June, during his visit to [[Nigeria]], he signed a distribution deal with [[Universal Music Group Nigeria]],<ref name="Premium Times" /> before the pre-release of his singles, "Jungle" and "King" featuring American rapper [[A$AP Ferg]], from his second album, ''[[Strings and Bling]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://destinyman.com/2018/06/01/nasty-c-back-new-music-featuring-aap-ferg/|title=Nasty C is back with new music featuring A$AP Ferg|website=Destiny Man}}</ref> In June 2018, during a press release with [[Premium Times]],<ref name="Premium Times">{{cite web |title=Nasty C shares two new singles, 'King', 'Jungle' {{!}} Premium Times Nigeria |url=https://www.premiumtimesng.com/entertainment/music/270658-nasty-c-shares-two-new-singles-king-jungle.html |website=[[Premium Times]] |access-date=26 June 2020}}</ref> the general manager of [[Universal Music Group Nigeria]], Ezegozie Eze said "''Strings and Bling'' is a testament to skill, craft and passion that he brings to his game every single time, and we are thrilled to bring this excellent body of work to the market". ''Strings and Bling'' was then released on 6 July 2018 in both digital and physical formats.<ref>{{cite web |last1=KEKANA |first1=CHRIZELDA |title=Strings and Bling is here & we're obsessed! Here are 5 reasons why! |url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/tshisa-live/tshisa-live/2018-07-06-strings-and-bling-is-here--were-obsessed-here-are-5-reasons-why/ |website=TimesLive |access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Nasty C's 'Strings And Bling' Is Out And People Love It |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.co.za/2018/07/06/nasty-cs-strings-and-bling-is-out-and-people-love-it_a_23476219/ |website=TheHuffingtonPost |access-date=7 July 2018}}</ref> ''[[The Times (South Africa)|TimesLIVE]]'' named ''Strings and Bling'' one of the best hip hop albums in South Africa, and it was also included in the list of hip hop albums on ''Tina'' magazine.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/tshisa-live/tshisa-live/2018-07-06-strings-and-bling-is-here--were-obsessed-here-are-5-reasons-why/|title=Strings and Blings is here & we're obsessed! Here are 5 reasons why!|website=TimesLIVE}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://tinamagazine.com/2018/08/best-rapper-in-south-africa.html|title=Best Rapper In South Africa Now: Top 10 South African Rappers 2018|date=2018-08-26|work=TINA Magazine|access-date=2018-09-27|language=en-GB}}</ref> The album's other singles include "SMA" featuring Rowlene, and the promotional single "Gravy".

On 20 August 2018, Ngcobo established his own record label, Tall Racks Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypemagazine.co.za/2018/08/21/nasty-c-announces-his-new-tall-racks-records-label-first-artist-signed/|title=Nasty C Announces His New 'Tall Racks Records' Label & First Artist Signed|website=Hype Magazine}}</ref> His long-term associated singer Rowlene was the first act to sign under the label, alongside himself. Ngcobo began the Ivyson Tour on 6 October 2018, at the Melrose Arch in [[Johannesburg]]. The tour with five consecutive shows (four in South Africa and one in Namibia) was a success with filled up attendance.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.politicalanalysis.co.za/nasty-c-announces-ivyson-tour-dates/|title=Nasty C announces Ivyson Tour dates|website=Political Analysis South Africa}}</ref> As for 2019, Ngcobo was named the ambassador of the male grooming company, [[Axe (brand)|Axe]] South Africa.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.peoplemagazine.co.za/celebrity-news/local-celebrities/nasty-c-axe-ambassadorship/|title=Nasty C On His Axe Ambassadorship|website=People Magazine}}</ref>

At the 2019 Dstv Mzansi Viewers Choice Awards his single  "SMA (Vol. 1)" featuring  Rowlene  was nominated for Favourite Song Of the Year.<ref>{{cite news|title=Nominees for the 2020 DStv Mzansi Viewers’ Choice Awards announced {{!}} Channel|url=https://www.news24.com/amp/channel/tv/news/nominees-for-the-2020-dstv-mzansi-viewers-choice-awards-announced-20191129|date=29 November 2019|first=
Thinus |last=Ferreira|location= South Africa |work=News24}}</ref>

On November 24, 2019, he  took-home two [[All Africa Music Awards|AFRIMA]] awards includes  Best African Rapper/Lyricist and Best African Collaboration.<ref>{{cite web|title=Burna Boy, Nasty C, Stonebwoy, Nadia Nakai & More Win 2019 AFRIMA Awards - OkayAfrica |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/amp/full-list-of-afrima-awards-2019-winners-2641445187|first=Damola |last=Durosomo|access-date= 24 November 2019|work=OkayAfrica}}</ref>

===2019{{endash}}present: Def Jam, Def Jam Africa and ''Zulu Man with Some Power''===
Ngcobo made the third installment of his concert tour, The Ivyson Tour, which kicked-off on 20 September 2019, in [[Cape Town]], [[South Africa]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/nasty-c-announces-dates-for-third-installment-of-ivyson-tour/|title=Nasty C Announces Dates for Third Installment of Ivyson Tour|website=OkayAfrica}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.shelflife.co.za/blogdetail/Nasty-C-The-Ivyson-Tour-Cape-Town|title=Nasty C 'The Ivyson Tour' - Cape Town|website=Shelflife}}</ref> On 27 September 2019, he released "God Flow" featuring rapper crownedYung, the lead single of his upcoming third studio album, ''[[Zulu Man With Some Power]]''. It has been reported that there are international artists who are going to appear on the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/za/album/god-flow-feat-crownedyung-single/1481494954|title=God Flow featuring Crowned Yung by Nasty C|website=Apple Music}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://zalebs.com/whats-hot/everything-we-know-about-nasty-c-s-new-album|title=Everything We Know About Nasty C's New Album|website=ZAlebs}}</ref>

In March 2020, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' reported that Nasty C signed a joint venture deal with [[Def Jam Records]], with the release of single "There They Go" as his debut under the label.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9343413/south-africa-rapper-nasty-c-def-jam-recordings-exclusive|title=South Africa's Star Rapper Nasty C Signs with Def Jam Recordings: Exclusive|date=26 March 2020|website=Billboard|access-date=21 April 2020}}</ref>

In April 2020, Nasty C released the visual EP ''Lost Files'', with the songs presented in performance clips on YouTube. He explained the EP's concept stating: "They're songs that didn't make it onto certain projects, songs that ended up as just ideas". On 14 May, the EP was uploaded on [[Apple Music]].<ref name="Lost Files">{{Cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/amp/nasty-c-lost-files-2645996291|title=You Can Now Stream Nasty C's 'Lost Files' EP|website=[[Okay Africa]]|last=Mkhabela|first=Sabelo|date=14 May 2020|access-date=14 May 2020}}</ref>

On 20 May 2020, Nasty C made his acting debut with a cameo role in the six-part [[International expansion of Netflix#2015–2016: Further expansion into Asia and Africa, CES announcement|Netflix African]] original young adult series ''[[Blood & Water (South African TV series)|Blood & Water]]'', playing the role of Zhero.<ref name="Netflix">{{Cite web|url=https://www.channel24.co.za/TV/News/rapper-nasty-c-makes-his-acting-debut-in-netflixs-blood-water-20200514|title=Rapper Nasty C makes his acting debut in Netflix's 'Blood & Water'|website=[[News24 (website)|Channel 24]]|last=Ngwadla|first=Nkosazana|date=14 May 2020|access-date=14 May 2020}}</ref> On 28 May 2020, Nasty C signed with [[Universal Music Group]] flagship Def Jam Africa.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Adegoke |first1=Yinka |title=The legendary hip hop label Def Jam has been launched in Africa |url=https://qz.com/africa/1860785/universal-music-ups-def-jam-africa-label-in-nigeria-south-africa |website=[[Quartz (publication)|Quartz Africa]] |access-date=26 June 2020 |language=en}}</ref> On 5 June, Nasty C teamed up with American rapper [[T.I.]] for the protest song "They Don't". Proceeds from the song benefit the non-profit organizations Until Freedom and Solidarity Fund, with lyrics referencing the [[murder of George Floyd]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/ti-and-nasty-c-share-new-song-they-dont-listen/|title=T.I. and Nasty C Share New Song "They Don't": Listen|website=[[Pitchfork (magazine)|Pitchfork]]|last=Strauss|first=Matthew|date=8 June 2020|access-date=8 June 2020}}</ref> On 25 June, he released a collaborative mixtape with [[DJ Whoo Kid]], titled ''Zulu''.

After several postponements, on 6 August 2020, Nasty C took to his social media and announced that his much anticipated album ''Zulu Man with Some Power'' would be released within the month of August. He released the track list consisting of 20 songs featuring international and South African artists, including [[Lil Keed]], [[Ari Lennox]], [[Lil Gotit]], [[T.I]], [[Tellaman]] and Rowlene. The album was released 28 August 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2020/08/nasty-c-palm-trees-stream/amp/|title=Nasty C Drops New Single "Palm Trees": South African rapper is set to release his new album Zulu Man With Some Power on August 28th|work=[[Consequence of Sound]]|date=7 August 2020|access-date=27 August 2020|author=Young, Alex}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/tshisa-live/tshisa-live/2020-08-28-legendary-or-take-the-l-heres-what-the-streets-think-of-nasty-cs-new-album/|title='Legendary' or 'take the L'? Here's what the streets think of Nasty C's new album|website=TimesLIVE}}</ref> In November 2020, Nasty C won Best Male Rap Act award at African Muzik Magazine Awards 2020 ceremony.<ref>{{cite web|title="African Muzik Magazine Awards 2020: All the winners {{!}} Music In Africa |url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/african-muzik-magazine-awards-2020-all-winners|publisher =Music In Africa|first=Gabriel Myers|last= Hansen|date=16 November 2020}}</ref>

In June 2021, he released a single titled "Best I Ever Had".<ref>{{cite web|title=Listen to Nasty C’s New Song ‘Best I Ever Had’ - OkayAfrica |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/amp/nasty-c-best-i-ever-had-stream-2653235950|work=OkayAfrica|date=June 4, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Fans praise Nasty C for his range after he drops new song, ‘Best I Ever Had’|url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/amp/tshisa-live/tshisa-live/2021-06-04-fans-praise-nasty-c-for-his-range-after-he-drops-new-song-best-i-ever-had/|date=June 4, 2021|work=TshisaLIVE |first=Chrizelda|last= Kekana}}</ref> At 6th ceremony of [[All Africa Music Awards]], he received three nominations for Songwriter of the Year, Best Male Artist in Southern Africa and Best African Rapper/Lyricist.<ref>{{cite web|title=All Africa Music Awards 2021: All the nominees {{!}} Music In Africa|url= https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/all-africa-music-awards-2021-all-nominees|work=Music In Africa|first= Gabriel Myers |last=Hansen|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref>
