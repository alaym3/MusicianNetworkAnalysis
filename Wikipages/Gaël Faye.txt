'''Gaël Faye''' (born 6 August 1982) is a [[Rwanda]]n-[[France|French]] singer, rapper, and writer.

He was born in [[Bujumbura]], [[Burundi]] of a French father and Rwandan mother. He emigrated in France at the age of 13 escaping from the [[Burundian civil war]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Gael Faye ce qui lui plait |url=https://next.liberation.fr/musique/2013/02/13/gael-faye-ce-qui-lui-plait_881630 |accessdate=25 April 2020 |agency=Liberation|date=13 February 2013}}</ref>

He wrote a book about it, ''[[Small Country]]'' (''Petit Pays''), a semi-biographical book. It was first published in France in August 2016 by [[Éditions Grasset|Grasset]], and has since been translated into 36 languages.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cummins |first1=Anthony |title=Small Country by Gaël Faye – review |url=https://www.theguardian.com/books/2018/jun/10/small-country-gael-faye-rwanda-burundi |accessdate=25 April 2020 |agency=The Guardian |date=10 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/05/29/books/gael-faye-small-country.html|title=A French-Rwandan Rap Star Turned Novelist From Burundi|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=29 May 2018|first=Tobias|last=Grey}}</ref>
