'''Forth Wanderers Football Club''' are a Scottish [[football (soccer)|football]] club based in the village of [[Forth, Lanarkshire|Forth]], [[South Lanarkshire]]. Formed in 1904 they compete in the {{Scottish football updater|ForthWnd}} and play in red strips with a white trim with a second strip of all black.

==History==
Since 1934 they have played at Kingshill Park in the village,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.forth.themutual.net/wanderersofold.html|title=Themutual.net|website=Forth.themutual.net|access-date=1 June 2018}}</ref> however they have had to share with [[Carluke Rovers F.C.|Carluke Rovers]] for a spell and play on a public park in [[Carnwath]] when this ground proved unusable. The team's first games were played at Pleasure Park before the Wanderers moved to Hie Dyke in 1929. It was 5 years later until they moved to what is their current home, Kingshill Park in 1934.

Forth have supplied two Scottish international goalkeepers in [[George Wood (footballer)|George Wood]] and [[Robert Douglas (footballer)|Rab Douglas]].<ref>{{cite web|title=George Wood Receives Clydesdale Sports Hall of Fame Award|url=http://www.sportclydesdale.net/PlainText/PlainText.aspx?SectionId=e228e095-2e19-4765-86a4-15fe3935df8a|publisher=Clydesdale Sports Council|access-date=28 May 2013}}</ref> [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]] legend [[Willie Waddell]] came from the village and starred for the team in his pre-Ibrox days.<ref>{{cite news|last=Reynolds|first=Jim|title=A doddle for The Deedle|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/sport/spl/aberdeen/a-doddle-for-the-deedle-1.789339|access-date=28 May 2013|newspaper=[[The Herald (Glasgow)|The Herald]]|date=15 October 1992}}</ref>

Wanderers reached the semi-finals of the [[Scottish Junior Cup]] in 1981 and their home quarter-final tie versus [[East Kilbride Thistle F.C.|East Kilbride Thistle]] attracted a record crowd of 2,324.<ref>{{cite book| first=David| last=McGlone| first2=Bill| last2=McLure| title=The Juniors - 100 Years. A Centenary History of Scottish Junior Football| publisher=Mainstream| year=1987| isbn=1-85158-060-3| page=294}}</ref>

The team's co-managers are Graham Cairns and Thomas Devine.

==Honours==
* [[Lanarkshire Junior Football League|Lanarkshire League]] winners: 1965–66
* [[Central Junior Football League|Central League B Division]] winners: 1979–80
* Lanarkshire Junior Cup: 1959–60
* Lanarkshire Hozier Cup: 1913–14
* Central League Cup: 1984–85
* [[Scottish Junior Cup]] semi-finalists 1980–81
