'''Bryson Lashun Potts'''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/nle-choppa-announces-hes-officially-off-probation-news.92794.html|title=NLE Choppa Announces He's Officially Off Probation|last=Zidel|first=Alex|date=October 9, 2019|website=HotNewHipHop|access-date=December 20, 2019}}</ref> (born November 1, 2002), known professionally as '''NLE Choppa''' (formerly '''YNR Choppa'''), is an American rapper from [[Memphis, Tennessee]]. He rose to fame with his January 2019 single "[[Shotta Flow]]", which was [[Music recording certification|certified platinum]] by the [[RIAA]] and peaked at number 36 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. The single was featured on his debut [[extended play|EP]] ''[[Cottonwood (EP)|Cottonwood]]'', released in December of that year.

Potts released his debut studio album ''[[Top Shotta]]'' (2020) which peaked at number 10 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and contained the singles "[[Camelot (NLE Choppa song)|Camelot]]" and "[[Walk Em Down]]" (featuring [[Roddy Ricch]]), which peaked at number 38 on the Hot 100. His second mixtape ''[[From Dark to Light]]'' was released on November 1, 2020. Potts announced his second studio album "Me Vs. Me". He will release the album on December 17, 2021.

== Early life ==

Potts was born to a Nigerian father and [[Jamaicans|Jamaican]] mother.<ref name="Emerging">{{cite news |last=Richards |first=M.T |date=January 23, 2020 |title=NLE Choppa: Young Memphis Rapper Tops Emerging Artist Charts |url=https://www.memphisflyer.com/memphis/nle-choppa-young-memphis-rapper-tops-emerging-artist-charts/Content?oid=22227329 |work=Memphis Flyer Newspaper |location=Memphis,Tennessee,Japan |access-date=February 11, 2020}}</ref> He was raised in [[Memphis, Tennessee]] in the [[Parkway Village, Memphis|Parkway Village]] area, and also was enrolled in [[Houston High School (Germantown, Tennessee)|Houston High School]] where he played basketball.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.prephoops.com/2018/10/4-observations-from-cordovas-open-gym-workout/|title=4 observations from Cordova's open gym workout|first=Courtney|last=Smith|publisher=Prep Hoops|date=October 22, 2018|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref> He began [[Freestyle rap|freestyling]] with friends at the age of 14 and started taking music seriously at 15.<ref name="XXL">{{cite news|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/02/nle-choppa-interview-the-break/|title=THE BREAK PRESENTS: NLE CHOPPA|first=Robby|last=Seabrook III|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|date=February 20, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
===2018–2019: Beginnings, breakthrough, record deal, and ''Cottonwood''===
NLE Choppa's manager is his mother, Angela Potts, who has been managing him since he started expressing an interest in rap music.<ref name="Mother">{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/10/nle-choppa-everything-you-need-to-know|title=Everything You Need to Know About NLE Choppa|last=Jessica|first=McKinney|date=October 2, 2019|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|access-date=October 10, 2019}}</ref> He released his first song, "No Love Anthem", in March 2018 under the name YNR Choppa. On July 22, he released his debut [[mixtape]], ''No Love the Takeover''. In December, he appeared on "No Chorus Pt''.'' 3", a cypher-style song by his Shotta Fam collective. His opening verse and his dance moves in the accompanying music video made him stand out from the group, earning him an online buzz.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thedoublecup.com/nle-choppa|title=NLE Choppa: The Shotta Flow Rapper On The Come Up|publisher=The Double Cup|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://allhiphop.com/features/nle-choppa-the-16-year-old-viral-phenom-looks-to-take-the-game-over-LLsVrsqKTEiMPwt4uYgwuw/ |title=NLE Choppa: The 16 Year Old Viral Phenom Looks To Take The Game Over |date=12 February 2019 |work=AllHipHop |access-date=22 July 2021 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190216223907/https://allhiphop.com/features/nle-choppa-the-16-year-old-viral-phenom-looks-to-take-the-game-over-LLsVrsqKTEiMPwt4uYgwuw/ |archive-date=16 February 2019 }}</ref>

After the surge of popularity, he released his breakout single "[[Shotta Flow]]". The video reached 10 million views in a month.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.50303/title.nle-choppa-is-only-16-years-old-wants-to-make-millions-to-give-back#|title=Interview: NLE Choppa Is Only 16 Years Old & Wants To Make Millions To Give Back|first=Nunet|last=Clitandre|publisher=Hip-Hop DX|date=February 14, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref> A few days after its release, ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' highlighted the track as its Song of the Day, praising his energetic delivery and adding that "he hits a new dance move every time the camera cuts back to him".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/nle-choppa-shotta-flow-new-song-listen/|title=The Ones: NLE Choppa's 'Shotta Flow'|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|date=January 15, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref> The song entered the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in May 2019, debuting at number 96.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8509603/nle-choppa-hot-100-debut|title=NLE Choppa Scores First Billboard Hot 100 Entry With 'Shotta Flow'|first=Xander|last=Zellner|date=May 1, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref> It later peaked at number 36. The official remix, featuring [[Blueface]] and a [[Cole Bennett]]-directed music video, was released in June, shortly after the original song was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/blueface-assists-nle-choppa-on-the-shotta-flow-remix-new-song.1983454.html|title=Blueface Assists NLE Choppa On The 'Shotta Flow' Remix|work=HotNewHipHop|date=June 20, 2019|access-date=June 20, 2019}}</ref>

In February, he released the song's sequel, "Shotta Flow 2". Moves|first=Carl|last=Lamarre|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=April 23, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref> It was around this time that it was reported that Choppa had sparked a bidding Exclusive|first=Tatiana|last=Cirisano|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=February 13, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref> He turned these offers down, from Republic Records, Interscope Records, Caroline, however, to instead sign with the independent distribution company, [[UnitedMasters]], while keeping his master recordings and publishing.<ref>{{cite Capo|website=WORLDSTARHIPHOP|access-date=November 16, 2019}}</ref> Also in March, he was featured on [[Birdman (rapper)|Birdman]] and [[Juvenile (rapper)|Juvenile]]'s song "Dreams".<ref>{{cite "Dreams"|first=Alex|last=Zidel|publisher=HotNewHipHop|date=March 14, 2019|access-date=May 9, 2019}}</ref> In April 2019, NLE released another single called "Birdboy", produced by SGULL.

In May, Choppa released a new single, "Blocc Is Hot", produced by ATL Jacob.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/music/news/nle-choppa-26-atl-jacob-talk-new-single-blocc-is-hot-wanting-to-get-lil-wayne-on-the-remix/ar-AAARmTG|title=NLE Choppa & ATL Jacob Talk New Single 'Blocc Is Hot,' Wanting to Get Lil Wayne on the Remix|first=Michael|last=Saponara|publisher=MSN|date=May 3, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref> It was made as a tribute to his favorite childhood rapper, [[Lil Wayne]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/nle-choppa-returns-on-blocc-is-hot-new-song.1982854.html|title=NLE Choppa Returns On 'Blocc Is Hot'|first=Milca|last=Pierre|publisher=HotNewHipHop|date=May 4, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref> A few days later, he made his music festival debut by performing at the [[Beale Street Music Festival]] in Memphis. Bob Mehr of [[The Commercial Appeal]] praised his act, saying that he "did much to seize the opportunity on the big stage in his hometown" and that "his career is likely to see plenty more memorable Memphis moments."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.commercialappeal.com/story/entertainment/music/2019/05/05/nle-choppa-memphis-may-beale-street-music-festival/3655352002/|title=Memphis rapper NLE Choppa brings the party to Beale Street Music Festival|first=Bob|last=Mehr|publisher=[[The Commercial Appeal]]|date=May 5, 2019|access-date=May 9, 2019}}</ref> On June 14, 2019, he released the single, "Free YoungBoy", produced by [[CashMoneyAP]]. This song was the first release from his own label No Love Entertainment (NLE), which he launched in partnership with [[Warner Records]].<ref name="NoLoveLabel">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8519758/nle-choppa-launches-own-label-warner-records|title=NLE Choppa Launches Own Label in Partnership with Warner Records|last=Eggertsen|first=Chris|date=July 12, 2019|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 30, 2019}}</ref> According to [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] magazine, No Love Entertainment is slated to release new music from NLE Choppa.<ref name="NoLoveLabel" /> On September 13, 2019, NLE Choppa released a new single titled "[[Camelot (NLE Choppa song)|Camelot]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/09/nle-choppa-camelot-video|title=Watch NLE Choppa's Cole Bennett-Directed Video for "Camelot"|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|access-date=October 7, 2019}}</ref> The song peaked at number 37 in the US, becoming his second Top 40 hit, following "Shotta Flow".<ref name="Hot 100">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/nle-choppa/chart-history/HSI|title=NLE Choppa Chart History: Hot 100|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 31, 2020}}</ref>

The EP also consists of a collaboration with [[Meek Mill]]. To accompany the release of ''Cottonwood'', a short film of the same name was released on the same day.<ref name="Cottonwood" />

On June 12, 2020, he released "Shotta Flow 5", the fourth sequel to his 2019 single "[[Shotta Flow]]".<ref name="SF5">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/06/nle-choppa-shotta-flow-5|title=NLE Choppa Unleashes Video for New Track "Shotta Flow 5"|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=Rose|first=Jordan|date=June 12, 2020|access-date=June 13, 2020}}</ref> The single "Narrow Road" featuring [[Lil Baby]] released on July 30.<ref name="Narrow">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/Nlechoppa1/status/1288525204721766401?s=19|title=Narrow Road Ft @lilbaby4PF TOMORROW and ALBUM PRE ORDER 💜|via=@nlechoppa1 on [[Twitter]] (confirmed account)|date=July 29, 2020|access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref> One week later, on August 7, ''Top Shotta'' was released. On August 11, 2020, NLE Choppa was included on ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''{{'}}s 2020 [[XXL (magazine)#Annual Freshman Class List|Freshman Class]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/xxl-2020-freshman-class-revealed/|title=XXL 2020 FRESHMAN CLASS REVEALED|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=Cline|first=Georgette|date=August 11, 2020|access-date=August 11, 2020}}</ref> On September 5, Choppa vowed to stop rapping about violence, stating he has more to talk about and wants to "spread positivity and wake people up".<ref name="Violence">{{cite web|url=https://amp.www.complex.com/music/2020/09/nle-choppa-to-stop-rapping-about-violence|title=NLE Choppa Vows to Stop Rapping About Violence: 'I Got More To Talk About Now'|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=Tigg|first=FNR|date=September 6, 2020 |access-date=October 15, 2020}}</ref> On October 15, he announced a new project titled ''[[From Dark to Light]]'', which was released on November 1, his birthday. The mixtape's cover art sees him sitting with his legs crossed in an outside setting, with butterflies around him and a rainbow in the distance; a nod to his new spiritual journey.<ref name="Dark">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/nle-choppa-announces-new-project-from-dark-to-light-news.119641.html?_amp|title=NLE Choppa Announces New Project "From Dark To Light"|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Zidel|first=Alex|date=October 15, 2020|access-date=October 15, 2020}}</ref> On October 23, NLE released the music video for his song, "Narrow Road" featuring [[Lil Baby]]. The music video has over 28 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ade |title=NLE Choppa Biography Music Career |url=https://networthbaze.com/nle-choppa-net-worth-height/ |website=NetWorthBaze |date=January 3, 2021 |publisher=Ade |access-date=February 18, 2021}}</ref> In August 2020, NLE Choppa announced a planned release of "Shotta Flow 6", which will feature [[Lil Wayne]], and supposed to be releasing soon.<ref>{{cite news|date=August 27, 2020|title=NLE CHOPPA SAYS 'SHOTTA FLOW 6' WILL ONLY HAPPEN WITH A LIL WAYNE FEATURE|work=[[HipHopDX]]|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.57552/title.nle-choppa-says-shotta-flow-6-will-only-happen-with-a-lil-wayne-feature#}}</ref> In February 2021, Choppa teased a second part to "Walk Em Down" with [[Lil Durk]] through a tweet.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/Nlechoppa1/status/1361147876064120833|title=Walk Em Down 2 @lildurk|via=[[Twitter]]|date=February 25, 2021|access-date=May 23, 2021}}</ref> These songs are expected to be included on his second album, ''Me vs. Me'', due December 17, 2021.<ref name="MeVsMe">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/me-vs-me/1593435771|title=Me vs. Me by NLE Choppa on Apple Music|via=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=November 9, 2021}}</ref>

==Musical style==

NLE Choppa is noted for his "animated vocals" and energetic rapping, which he notably employed on his breakout single, "[[Shotta Flow]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/levels/nle-choppa-shotta-flow-new-song-listen/|title=New Rap Song of the Day: NLE Choppa's 'Shotta Flow|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|date=January 15, 2019|access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref> His sound has also been described as "melodic", albeit "heavy-hitting".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8498476/nle-choppa-turned-down-a-3-million-record-deal-partner-united-masters|title=Why 16-Year-Old Rapper NLE Choppa Turned Down a $3 Million Record Deal to Partner With UnitedMasters: Exclusive|last=Cirisano|first=Tatiana|date=February 15, 2019|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref> Jessica McKinney of [[Complex (magazine)|''Complex'']] magazine stated he usually has a "wild and rambunctious" delivery in his verses.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/best-new-music-this-week-the-weeknd-mac-miller-nle-choppa|title=Best New Music This Week: The Weeknd, Mac Miller, NLE Choppa, and More|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=McKinney|first=Jessica|date=March 20, 2020|access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref> As a child, he listened to UGK and [[Lil Wayne]], however he recalls listening "to a lot of unreleased music all around the city" of Memphis.<ref name="Golden">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/nle-choppa-memphis-rap-hero-with-the-golden-touch-2753578?amp|title=NLE Choppa: Memphis rap hero with the golden touch|work=[[NME]]|last=Williams|first=Kyann-Sian|date=September 16, 2020|access-date=October 15, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life ==
On June 20, 2020, NLE Choppa welcomed his first child, a daughter named Clover Brylie Potts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://rapcurrent.com/2020/06/nle-choppa-and-ex-girlfriend-mariah-welcome-baby-girl/|title=NLE Choppa & Ex-Girlfriend Mariah Welcome Baby Girl|date=June 21, 2020|website=RapCurrent}}</ref> He stated having a child on the way changed his mindset; "I knew I needed to change and be a better person for my daughter".<ref name="Golden"/>

In August 2020, NLE Choppa gravitated towards a "spiritual" awakening on social media, launching a&nbsp;YouTube channel called "Awakened Choppa", where he documents his [[holistic]] and healthier lifestyle which includes [[veganism]] and gardening.<ref name="Violence" />

He has said he struggled with depression, and often raps about this in his music. He meditates in order to deal with mental health issues and the pressures of being a young entertainer.<ref name="Stories">{{cite web|url=https://amp.www.complex.com/visual-stories/nle-choppa-debut-album-xxl-freshman-list|title=NLE Choppa On Debut Album, 'XXL' Freshman List & More Complex Stories|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=October 2020|access-date=October 15, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Golden" /> He was banned from Instagram in early 2020, something which he said "grounded" him.<ref name="Stories" />

During an unspecified time, NLE Choppa served time at a [[Youth detention center|juvenile detention center]].<ref name="Mother" /> He has never revealed what he was arrested for, but he said his time in detention motivated him to turn his life around. In an episode of his YouTube series ''The Rise of NLE Choppa'', he said that being in detention helped him a lot and was an "eye-opener".<ref name="Mother" />

On March 29, 2021, NLE Choppa was arrested for burglary, drug and weapon charges. He released a freestyle called First Day Out over the viral [[SpotemGottem]]'s [[Beat Box (SpotemGottem song)|Beat Box]] Beat.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.browardclerk.org/Web2/CaseSearchECA/CaseDetail/?caseid=MTExNTMzNzg%3d-FTa8QiIvG6Q%3d&caseNum=21002970CF10A&category=CR&accessLevelCodeOUT=ANONYMOUS&ato=D|title=Broward County Clerk of Court|website=browardclerk.org}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/nle-choppa-arrested-faces-burglary-gun-and-drug-posssion-charges|title=NLE Choppa Arrested, Faces Burglary, Gun, and Drug Possession Charges|website=Complex.com}}</ref>

On May 3, 2021, NLE Choppa and his crew were involved in a fight in [[Santa Monica, California]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Video Shows NLE Choppa and His Crew Get Into Fight|url=https://www.complex.com/music/nle-choppa-fight-beach|access-date=May 5, 2021|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Woods|first=Aleia|date=May 3, 2021|title=Fight Involving NLE Choppa Caught on Video|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/nle-choppa-fight/|url-status=live|access-date=May 5, 2021|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref>

On June 9, 2021, NLE Choppa claimed that he helped cure a girl’s cancer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=NLE Choppa Claims His Plant-Based Diet and Herbs 'Helped Cure' Someone's Cancer|url=https://www.complex.com/music/nle-choppa-claims-his-plant-based-diet-and-herbs-helped-cure-someones-cancer|access-date=2021-06-12|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-06-10|title=NLE Choppa Claims He Helped Cure Someone's Cancer|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.62607/title.nle-choppa-claims-he-helped-cure-someones-cancer|url-status=live|access-date=2021-06-12|website=HipHopDX}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Gunn|first=Tamantha|date=2021-06-10|title=NLE Choppa says he helped cure someone of cancer|url=https://www.revolt.tv/news/2021/6/10/22528252/nle-choppa-cure-cancer|access-date=2021-06-12|website=REVOLT|language=en}}</ref>
