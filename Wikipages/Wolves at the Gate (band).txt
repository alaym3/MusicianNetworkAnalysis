'''Wolves at the Gate''' is an American [[Christian metal]] and [[post-hardcore]] band from [[Cedarville, Ohio]], United States. Formed in 2008, they are currently signed to [[Solid State Records]], where the band released three EPs: ''We Are the Ones'' (2011), ''Back to School'' (2013), and ''Reprise'' (2015), four full-length albums: ''Captors'' (2012), ''VxV'' (2014), ''Types & Shadows'' (2016), ''Eclipse'' (2019), and a Christmas single entitled "The King" in 2012.

== Background ==

The band formed in 2008, and are from [[Cedarville, Ohio]].<ref name=fb /><ref name=altpress />

In September 2011, the band signed to label [[Solid State Records]].<ref name=altpress />

The band released their first EP on November 15, 2011 entitled ''We Are the Ones'',<ref name=jfhstudioepone>{{cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/WeAretheOnesEP.asp|title=Wolves at the Gate, "We Are the Ones EP" Review|last=Myatt|first=Wayne|date=December 23, 2011|publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout|access-date=April 18, 2014|archive-date=April 19, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140419020217/http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/WeAretheOnesEP.asp|url-status=live}}</ref> and their second on September 30, 2013 entitled ''Back to School''.<ref name=jfhstudioeptwo>{{cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/BackToSchoolEP.asp|title=Wolves at the Gate, "Back To School EP" Review|last=Lambert|first=Aaron|date=October 18, 2013|publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout|access-date=April 18, 2014|archive-date=April 4, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140404191719/http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/BackToSchoolEP.asp|url-status=live}}</ref>

The band released their debut studio album ''[[Captors (album)|Captors]]'' on July 3, 2012, with Solid State.<ref name=ivm /><ref name=jfhstudiolpone>{{cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/Captors.asp|title=Wolves at the Gate, "Captors" Review|last=Myatt|first=Wayne|date=July 3, 2012|publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout|access-date=April 18, 2014|archive-date=April 18, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140418220323/http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/Captors.asp|url-status=live}}</ref> For the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charting week of July 21, 2012, ''Captors'' charted at No. 7 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Christian Albums]] chart,<ref name=chr>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/2012-07-21/christian-albums|title=Christian Albums : July 21, 2012|author=''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''.biz|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=July 21, 2012|access-date=April 18, 2014}}</ref> and at No. 17 on the [[Top Hard Rock Albums|Hard Rock Albums]] chart.<ref name=hr>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/2012-07-21/hard-rock-albums|title=Hard Rock Albums : July 21, 2012|author=''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''.biz|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=July 21, 2012|access-date=April 18, 2014}}</ref>

Their next album, ''[[VxV]]'' (Pronounced ''Five by Five''), was released on June 10, 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.solidstaterecords.com/news/4562/Wolves_At_The_Gate_Release_Sophomore_Album_VxV// |title=Wolves at the Gate &#124; VxV |publisher=Solid State Records |access-date=May 30, 2014 |archive-date=July 27, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140727145456/http://www.solidstaterecords.com/news/4562/Wolves_At_The_Gate_Release_Sophomore_Album_VxV// |url-status=live }}</ref> The band released the song "Dust to Dust" on April 24, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/wolves_at_the_gate_stream_new_song_dust_to_dust|title=Wolves at the Gate stream new song, "Dust To Dust" - News - Alternative Press|website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]]|access-date=November 8, 2016|archive-date=November 8, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161108133655/http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/wolves_at_the_gate_stream_new_song_dust_to_dust|url-status=live}}</ref> On May 12, 2015, the band released their third EP, ''Reprise'', consisting of acoustic versions of songs from previous albums. The band's third album, ''Types and Shadows'', was released on November 4, 2016.

Their fourth album, ''Eclipse'', was announced on April 24, 2019, with a planned release date of July 26, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=wookubus |title=Wolves at the Gate's New Album "Eclipse" Due in July |url=https://www.theprp.com/2019/04/24/news/wolves-at-the-gates-new-album-eclipse-due-in-july/ |website=Theprp |access-date=April 25, 2019 |archive-date=April 25, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190425022215/https://www.theprp.com/2019/04/24/news/wolves-at-the-gates-new-album-eclipse-due-in-july/ |url-status=live }}</ref> At the same time, new band photos were released with a new member, Joey Alarcon coming in on guitars.<ref name="Drifter">{{cite web|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/hear-ohio-post-hardcore-act-wolves-gates-soaring-new-song-drifter|title=Heat Ohio Post-Hardcore Act Wolves at the Gate's Soaring New Song "Drifter"|website=[[Revolver Magazine]]|first=Kelsey|last=Chapstick|date=June 13, 2019|access-date=June 24, 2019|archive-date=June 25, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190625013642/https://www.revolvermag.com/music/hear-ohio-post-hardcore-act-wolves-gates-soaring-new-song-drifter|url-status=live}}</ref> They released its first single, "The Cure", on April 26, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=wookubus |title=Wolves at the Gate Reveal New Track "The Cure" |url=https://www.theprp.com/2019/04/26/news/wolves-at-the-gate-reveal-new-track-the-cure/ |website=Theprp |access-date=April 29, 2019 |archive-date=November 8, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201108001816/https://www.theprp.com/2019/04/26/news/wolves-at-the-gate-reveal-new-track-the-cure/ |url-status=live }}</ref> A second single, "A Voice in the Violence", was released on May 16, 2019.{{citation needed|date=May 2019}} A third single, "Drifter", was premiered via ''[[Revolver Magazine]]'' on June 13, 2019.<ref name="Drifter"/>

On September 4, 2020, the band announced their fourth EP, ''Dawn'', consisting of acoustic versions of songs off ''Eclipse'', would be releasing October 9, 2020.{{citation needed|date=September 2020}} That same day, the first single, an acoustic version of "A Voice in the Violence" was released and the second, "Counterfeit", was released on September 25, 2020.{{citation needed|date=September 2020}}

In March 9, 2021, it was revealed that [[Tim Lambesis]] was working on a new side project with the band's guitarist Joey Alarcon. Lambesis had previously co-produced ''Eclipse'' with Alarcon and Steve Cobucci.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.theprp.com/2020/08/30/news/as-i-lay-dyings-tim-lambesis-has-been-in-the-studio-for-his-new-side-project-lately/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=October 25, 2021 |archive-date=October 25, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211025160649/https://www.theprp.com/2020/08/30/news/as-i-lay-dyings-tim-lambesis-has-been-in-the-studio-for-his-new-side-project-lately/ |url-status=live }}</ref> In April 16, 2021, the band released a new single, "Stop The Bleeding",<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-wolves-at-the-gate-debut-new-song-stop-the-bleeding/ |title=NEWS: Wolves At The Gate debut new song, 'Stop The Bleeding'! {{!}} DEAD PRESS! {{!}} It's more than "just music" to us<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=October 25, 2021 |archive-date=October 25, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211025160512/https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-wolves-at-the-gate-debut-new-song-stop-the-bleeding/ |url-status=live }}</ref> which was followed by another single, "Shadows", in October 15, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://radiou.com/insider/wolves-at-the-gate-unveils-new-single-shadows/ |title=RadioU {{!}} Wolves At The Gate unveils new single Shadows<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=October 25, 2021 |archive-date=October 25, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211025160520/https://radiou.com/insider/wolves-at-the-gate-unveils-new-single-shadows/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
