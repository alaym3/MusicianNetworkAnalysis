'''Jasmine Ying Thompson''' (born 8 November 2000) is an English singer and songwriter. Thompson began her career at the age of ten by filming herself singing and uploading the videos to [[YouTube]].<ref name="national_profile" /> In 2014, she was featured on German [[deep house]] producer [[Robin Schulz]]'s song "[[Sun Goes Down (Robin Schulz song)|Sun Goes Down]]",<ref name="national_profile" /> which charted within the top 10 in multiple countries including Australia, Germany, Austria, and Switzerland.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Robin+Schulz+feat.+Jasmine+Thompson&titel=Sun+Goes+Down&cat=s|title=australian-charts.com - Robin Schulz feat. Jasmine Thompson - Sun Goes Down|website=Australian-charts.com|access-date=19 March 2019|archive-date=29 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190329235747/https://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Robin+Schulz+feat.+Jasmine+Thompson&titel=Sun+Goes+Down&cat=s|url-status=live}}</ref> As of March 2019, her YouTube channel has amassed over 3.3 million subscribers and over 570 million views.

Her acoustic cover version of [[Chaka Khan]]'s original "[[Ain't Nobody]]" reached number 32 on the [[UK Singles Chart]] in 2013;<ref name="UK"/> this success led to the song being featured in an advertisement by [[Sainsbury's]]. The remixed version from Thompson was then remixed again by DJ [[Felix Jaehn]]—as "[[Ain't Nobody#Felix Jaehn version|Ain't Nobody (Loves Me Better)]]"—in 2015 and became a huge hit, peaking at number two in the UK, and charting in several other countries.<ref name="tvadmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.tvadmusic.co.uk/2013/09/sainsburys-by-sainsburys-range-2|title=Sainsbury's - By Sainsbury's Range|website=Tvadmusic.co.uk|access-date=2 October 2013|archive-date=6 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131006083248/http://www.tvadmusic.co.uk/2013/09/sainsburys-by-sainsburys-range-2/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/counting-stars-earns-onerepublic-first-ever-uk-number-1-single-2528|title=Counting Stars earns OneRepublic first ever UK Number 1 single|publisher=[[The Official Charts Company]]|access-date=8 October 2013|archive-date=6 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131006191033/http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/counting-stars-earns-onerepublic-first-ever-uk-number-1-single-2528/|url-status=live}}</ref> Her voice has been described as "eerie", "soft", and "incandescent".<ref name="allmusic"/>

==Early life==
Thompson was born on 8 November 2000 in [[London]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/04e74624-554c-4689-a811-92e6145f40c0|title=Jasmine Thompson - New Songs, Playlists & Latest News - BBC Music|website=BBC|access-date=30 March 2017|archive-date=8 November 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191108221410/https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/04e74624-554c-4689-a811-92e6145f40c0|url-status=live}}</ref> to an English father and a Chinese mother.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jasmine Thompson profile |url=https://www.reverbnation.com/artist/questions/artist_2440700 |website=ReverbNation.com |access-date=30 October 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160308204151/https://www.reverbnation.com/artist/questions/artist_2440700 |archive-date= 8 March 2016 }}</ref> In 2017, Thompson opened up about the divorce of her parents, which has inspired her, at an interview about ''Wonderland'', her latest EP at that time.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jasmine Thompson Bio|website=Jasminethompson.com|url=http://www.jasminethompson.com/about|access-date=10 June 2018|archive-date=2 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171202010857/http://www.jasminethompson.com/about|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==

In September 2013, Thompson released a cover of [[Chaka Khan]]'s "[[Ain't Nobody]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bild.de/unterhaltung/musik/youtube/star-jasmine-thompson-im-bild-interview-50825324.bild.html |title=Musik-Star Jasmine Thompson (16) im BILD-Interview - »Das verbindet mich mit YouTube-Bibi |website=Bild.de |access-date=17 April 2017 |archive-date=22 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180822113409/https://www.bild.de/unterhaltung/musik/youtube/star-jasmine-thompson-im-bild-interview-50825324.bild.html |url-status=live }}</ref> The song featured in an advert for UK supermarket chain [[Sainsbury's]] for their ''by Sainsbury's'' range. The song has peaked at number thirty two on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. In October 2013, she released the extended play ''Under the Willow Tree''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dailymotion.com/video/x32a60p |title=Jasmine Thompson - Under The Willow Tree - EP Preview (Behind the Scene) |publisher=The Daily Motion}}</ref> One of the songs, named "Run", enjoyed moderate success in Europe and the United States.{{Citation needed|date=December 2017}}

===2014: ''Another Bundle of Tantrums''===
On 20 April 2014, Thompson released her second album, ''Another Bundle of Tantrums''. The album peaked at number 126 on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. In September 2014, Thompson's cover of "Everybody Hurts" was used in the [[BBC]] autumn trailer for ''[[EastEnders]]''. The trailer showed characters [[Kat Slater|Kat Moon]], [[Sharon Watts]], and [[Linda Carter]] repeating Thompson's lyrics.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.soundsblog.it/post/419494/jasmine-thompson-chi-e-scheda-info |title=Jasmine Thompson: curiosità e notizie sulla cantante inglese |website=Soundsblog.it |access-date=30 March 2017 |archive-date=20 October 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201020235848/https://www.soundsblog.it/post/419494/jasmine-thompson-chi-e-scheda-info |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://los40.com/los40/2015/10/01/lomas40/1443713274_097572.html |title=Conoce a Jasmine Thompson |first=Los 40 |last=Principales |date=1 October 2015 |website=Los40.com |access-date=30 March 2017 |archive-date=27 May 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170527182424/http://los40.com/los40/2015/10/01/lomas40/1443713274_097572.html |url-status=live }}</ref> Jasmine also released a cover of [[Clean Bandit]]'s song "[[Rather Be]]" on 13 April 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rather Be - Clean Bandit (Cover By Jasmine Thompson) - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_as_IxjD3Oo|access-date=2020-12-22|website=www.youtube.com|archive-date=25 July 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210725110040/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_as_IxjD3Oo|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2015–2016: ''Adore'' EP===
On 12 June 2015, Thompson released her first single on [[Atlantic Records]]'s label, entitled "[[Adore (Jasmine Thompson song)|Adore]]".<ref name="national_profile">{{cite web |url=http://www.thenational.ae/arts-life/music/teen-star-jasmine-thompson-talks-fame-and-her-debut-album-ahead-of-love-music-your-way-fest |title=Teen star Jasmine Thompson talks fame and her debut album ahead of Love Music Your Way fest - The National |website=Thenational.ae |access-date=30 March 2017 |archive-date=28 June 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170628212622/http://www.thenational.ae/arts-life/music/teen-star-jasmine-thompson-talks-fame-and-her-debut-album-ahead-of-love-music-your-way-fest |url-status=live }}</ref> Later that year, on 18 September, Thompson released her third extended play, ''Adore''. It included 5 songs: "Adore", "Do It Now", "Great Escape", "Let Myself Try", and "Crystal Heart".

In 2015, German music producer and DJ [[Felix Jaehn]] released a remix of "[[Ain't Nobody]]", originally released by Thompson in 2013 titled "[[Ain't Nobody#Felix Jaehn version|Ain't Nobody (Loves Me Better)]]". The song became a worldwide hit, topping the charts in Austria, Germany, Hungary, Israel, and the Netherlands, and peaking within the top ten of the charts in Belgium, Denmark, Finland, France, Romania, Sweden, and Switzerland.{{citation needed|date=February 2017}}

In 2016, her cover of "Mad World" was used in the trailer for Ewan McGregor's film ''[[American Pastoral (film)|American Pastoral]]''.<ref name="ewan_trailer"/><ref name="rfm.fr">{{cite web |url=http://www.rfm.fr/news/Jasmine-Thompson-decouvrez-son-nouveau-single-Mad-World-9996 |title=Jasmine Thompson : découvrez son nouveau single "Mad World" |website=Rfm.fr |access-date=30 March 2017 |archive-date=21 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180821223237/http://www.rfm.fr/news/Jasmine-Thompson-decouvrez-son-nouveau-single-Mad-World-9996 |url-status=live }}</ref> In the same year, her cover of ″Mad World″ also was used in the French movie, ''Ares''. It is played at the end of the movie leading to the credits.<ref name="rfm.fr"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.netflix.com/title/80165083 |title=Arès - Netflix |website=Netflix.com |access-date=10 June 2017 |archive-date=18 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210118184532/https://www.netflix.com/title/80165083 |url-status=live }}</ref>

===2017: ''Wonderland'' EP===
On 19 May 2017, Thompson released her fourth extended play, ''Wonderland''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Q&A: YouTube Stardom, ‘Wonderland,’ and the Teenage Years with Jasmine Thompson / Ones To Watch|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/qa-youtube-stardom-wonderland-and-the|access-date=2020-12-15|website=Ones To Watch|archive-date=23 September 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200923200000/https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/qa-youtube-stardom-wonderland-and-the|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Fuse — Watch Jasmine Thompson Speak in Emojis Describing ‘Wonderland' EP, Florence Welch Collab Dreams|url=https://www.fuse.tv/videos/2017/10/jasmine-thompson-i-speak-emoji-interview-wonderland-ep|language=en|access-date=2020-12-15|archive-date=10 August 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200810052314/https://fuse.tv/videos/2017/10/jasmine-thompson-i-speak-emoji-interview-wonderland-ep|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 29 March 2019, Thompson released her fifth extended play, ''Colour''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Knock Journal —Jasmine Thompson Talks Her New EP "Colour"|url=https://theknockturnal.com/exclusive-jasmine-thompson-talks-her-new-ep-colour/|access-date=2020-12-15|website=theknockturnal.com|archive-date=25 July 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210725110033/https://theknockturnal.com/exclusive-jasmine-thompson-talks-her-new-ep-colour/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Jasmine Thompson Breaks Down 'Colour' EP: Exclusive|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8505735/jasmine-thompson-colour-ep-breakdown|access-date=2020-12-15|website=Billboard|language=en|archive-date=28 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190428082333/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8505735/jasmine-thompson-colour-ep-breakdown|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2020: New music ===
On 16 July 2020, Thompson collaborated with German DJ [[Zedd]] on the song "[[Funny (Zedd and Jasmine Thompson song)|Funny]]".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-07-16|title=Jasmine Thompson and Zedd Get the Last Laugh in "Funny"|url=https://americansongwriter.com/funny-jasmine-thompson-zedd-song-interview/|access-date=2020-12-15|website=American Songwriter|language=en-US|archive-date=1 December 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201201214317/https://americansongwriter.com/funny-jasmine-thompson-zedd-song-interview/|url-status=live}}</ref>
