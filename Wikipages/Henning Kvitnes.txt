'''Henning Kvitnes''' (born 13 May 1958) is a singer/songwriter from [[Tistedal]], [[Halden]] in [[Norway]]. His debut came with The Ice Cream Band, but he later found bigger success as a solo artist.

==Biography==
Kvitnes first made a name for himself in 1978, with The Ice Cream Band, who warmed up for [[Elvis Costello]] at [[Chateau Neuf]] in [[Oslo]]. The band soon changed name to [[The Young Lords (band)|The Young Lords]]. As a member of The Young Lords he released his first album, Same Shit - New Wrapping in 1980. In 1991 he released his first album in Norwegian, titled [[Veien hjem]].

Kvitnes has during his career collaborated with several of Norway's most famous musicians, [[Åge Aleksandersen]], [[Arve Tellefsen]], [[Steinar Albrigtsen]] and [[Claudia Scott]], to mention a few.

In 2007 he won a Norwegian Grammy, [[Spellemannsprisen]], for his album Stemmer i gresset.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spellemann.no/index.php?option=com_winner&Itemid=29|title=Spellemann // Hjem|first=The|last=Fold|website=Spellemann}}</ref> He has also won [[Norsk artistsforbunds ærespris]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.norskartistforbund.no/article.asp?id=104 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2010-12-09 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120302072224/http://www.norskartistforbund.no/article.asp?id=104 |archive-date=2012-03-02 |url-status=dead }}</ref> an annual honorary title given out to stand out musicians.
