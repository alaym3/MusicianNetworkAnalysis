'''Scott Jablonski''' (known by his stage name '''SJ''') is an American singer-songwriter, performing artist, and attorney based in [[South Florida]]. As a recording artist, SJ has released two studio albums and one live album through his independent record label, Acoustic Soul Records. In 2011, the singer was nominated by [[Sirius XM]]'s [[The Coffee House (Sirius XM)|The Coffee House]] as "Discovery of the Year", and his song "I Like You" appeared on several [[airplay]] charts in the US in 2012. He has opened for acts like [[Ed Sheeran]] and [[Pentatonix]].

==Early life and education==
Jablonski was born in [[Cleveland]], Ohio. He graduated from [[Gannon University]] in 1999 with a [[bachelor's degree]] in political science and foreign language studies. He also played varsity soccer while at the school. In 2004, he earned a master's degree at the [[University of Pittsburgh]]'s [[University of Pittsburgh Graduate School of Public and International Affairs|Graduate School of Public and International Affairs]]. He also earned a [[Juris Doctor|JD]] degree from the school that year.<ref name="BC">{{cite news | last=Taylor| first=John| title =Music Review: SJ – Coffee: Strong Brew Edition | publisher =BlogCritics| date =February 26, 2013 | url =http://blogcritics.org:80/music-review-sj-coffee-strong-brew/| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518025042/http://blogcritics.org:80/music-review-sj-coffee-strong-brew/| archive-date=May 18, 2015| url-status=dead| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Pitt">{{cite news | title =Attorney and Singer-Songwriter Scott R. ==Career==
After graduating from the University of Pittsburgh, Jablonski embarked on a career in law. With Ronald A. Brand, he co-authored ''Forum Non Conveniens: Past, Present and Future'' which was published by [[Oxford University Press]] in 2008. His legal writings have also appeared in other publications like the ''Journal of International Law and Politics'' and the [[University of Miami]] ''Inter-American Law Review'', among others.<ref name="BC"/><ref name="Pitt"/><ref name="Legal"/>

In 2009, his friends and family heard a demo of a song he had made and urged him to pursue music as a career. He adopted the stage name, "SJ," for that endeavor.<ref name="BC"/> In 2010, he founded the Acoustic Soul Group, of which his Acoustic Soul Records is apart.<ref name="Pitt"/> He began releasing songs that year, including "I Like You" which was nominated for a Hollywood Music & Media Award. He was also named a grand finalist in the UK's Festival4TheStars for writing the song.<ref name="Pens">{{cite news | last=Frieman| first=Richie| title =SJ| publisher =PensEyeView| date =2011 | url =http://www.penseyeview.com/content/sj| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> A Spanish version of the song, "Lo Mejor De Mi," won first place in the International Songwriting Competition in the Latin category.<ref name="Noise2010">{{cite news | title =SJ Scores 1st Place (Latin) in the International Songwriting Competition of 2010| publisher =The Noise Group| date =December 19, 2010 | url =http://thenoisegroup.com/sj-scores-1st-place-for-latin-in-the-international-songwriting-competition-2010-with-lo-mejor-de-mi/| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref>

In March 2011, "I Like You" reached the top 10 of ''[[FMQB]]''{{'}}s [[Adult contemporary music|adult contemporary]] chart alongside acts like [[Katy Perry]], [[Bruno Mars]], and [[Train (band)|Train]].<ref name="Noise2011">{{cite news | title =SJ's "I Like You" Blazes Into Adult Contemporary Top 10 on FMQB.com| publisher =The Noise Group| date =March 30, 2011 | url =http://thenoisegroup.com/sjs-i-like-you-blazes-into-adult-contemporary-top-10-on-fmqb-com/| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> In May 2011, he released his first commercial studio-recorded [[Extended play|EP]], ''Coffee'', featuring the songs "I Like You," "Let Me Be," "Coffee," and others.<ref name="QRO">{{cite news | last=Chase| first=Ted| title =SJ : Coffee| publisher =QRO| date =September 22, 2011 | url =https://www.qromag.com/reviews/album_reviews/sj_coffee/| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> Later in 2011, Sirius XM's The Coffee House station nominated SJ for the "Discovery of the Year" award, and his song, "Coffee," won a Hollywood Music & Media Award.<ref name="BC"/><ref name="IMA">{{cite news | last=Folkman| first=Martin| title =Discover Artists Who Joined The 11th IMAs: SJ| publisher =Independent Music Awards| date =March 2, 2012 | url =http://independentmusicawards.com/blog/2012/03/02/discover-artists-who-joined-the-11th-imas-sj/| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> In July 2012, it was announced that [[Taylor Guitars]], [[Sennheiser]], [[Future Sonics]], Gator Cases, Hercules Stands, and [[Elixir strings]] had officially endorsed SJ.<ref name="Yahoo01">{{cite news | title =Breakout Acoustic Artist SJ Endorsed by Major Guitar and Music Companies | work =Yahoo! Finance| date =July 26, 2012 | url =https://finance.yahoo.com/news/breakout-acoustic-artist-sj-endorsed-141500426.html| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150424032542/https://finance.yahoo.com/news/breakout-acoustic-artist-sj-endorsed-141500426.html| archive-date=April 24, 2015| url-status=dead| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Songspace">{{cite news | title =SJ | publisher =American Songspace| url =http://www.americansongspace.com/sjacousticmusic/| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130402113931/http://www.americansongspace.com/sjacousticmusic/| archive-date=April 2, 2013| url-status=dead| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref>

In August 2012, he released his first full-length studio album entitled ''Coffee: Strong Brew Edition''. The album featured all the songs from the 2011 EP, ''Coffee'', with 7 new tracks. Other South Florida artists like Randy Singer, Erica Sommer, and Joshua Stedman were featured on some songs. The album was recorded at Outer Reach Music Studios in Miami.<ref name="BC"/><ref name="New">{{cite news | title =SJ – Coffee: Strong Brew Edition| publisher =New Releases Now| date =August 14, 2012 | url =http://www.newreleasesnow.com/album/sj--coffee-strong-brew-edition| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> SJ also released an accompanying live album and DVD entitled ''Coffee: Unfiltered''. The song, "Let Me Be," spent over 13 weeks on [[Mediabase]]'s adult contemporary charts, peaking at number 32.<ref name="BC"/><ref name="Pent">{{cite news | title =SJ Opens for NBC's "The Sing Off" Champions Pentatonix | publisher =MarketWired| date =October 24, 2012 | url =http://www.marketwired.com/press-release/sj-opens-for-nbcs-the-sing-off-champions-pentatonix-1717323.htm| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref>

In September 2012, he was one of 25 finalists nominated for the ''[[American Songwriter]]'' Coffeehouse Tour competition.<ref name="ASW">{{cite magazine | last=Ritchie| first=John| title =The American Songwriter Coffeehouse Tour: 25 Finalists| magazine =American Songwriter| date =September 10, 2012 | url =http://americansongwriter.com/2012/09/the-american-songwriter-coffeehouse-tour-25-finalists/| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> In October and November, SJ opened for Pentatonix on 6 dates, concluding his nationwide "Coffee Tour."<ref name="BC"/><ref name="Pent"/> Later in November, he went on a short Midwest tour.<ref name="Skope">{{cite news | title =SJ TO TOUR MIDWEST STARTING NOV 7 + VIDEOS| publisher =Skope| date =October 25, 2012 | url =https://skopemag.com/2012/10/25/sj-to-tour-midwest-starting-nov-7-videos| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> Throughout 2011 and 2012, SJ's songs were played on over 50 [[adult album alternative]] and [[Americana (music)|americana]] radio stations across the country.<ref name="BC"/><ref name="Songspace"/>

In May 2013, SJ opened for Ed Sheeran at [[SunFest]] in [[West Palm Beach]], Florida.<ref name="Sun">{{cite news | title =SunFest 2013 Friday Night: Local Acoustic Artist SJ Won Hearts| publisher =SunFest| date =May 4, 2013 | url =https://www.sunfest.com/blog/sunfest-2013-friday-night-local-acoustic-artist-sj-won-hearts| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> In September of that year, he opened for [[Robin Zander]] of [[Cheap Trick]].<ref name="Burg">{{cite news | title =Weekly-Weekend-What-To-Do (09.06–8.13)| publisher =ILoveTheBurg.com| date =September 6, 2013 | url =https://ilovetheburg.com/weekly-weekend-what-to-do-09-06-8-13/| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> In 2014, SJ was named one of 8 semi-finalists in 3 categories for the International Songwriting Competition.<ref name="T4C">{{cite news | title =Acoustic Singer/Songwriter SJ Earns Ranking In The 2013 International Songwriting Competition Semi-Finals| publisher =Top 40 Charts| date =February 26, 2014 | url =http://top40-charts.com/news.php?nid=98177| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref> Later in 2014, he launched the ''Covers & Others'' video series in collaboration with Day's Edge Productions. The goal of the series was to bring awareness to organizations with social, scientific, or environmental initiatives by playing [[Cover version|covers]] of songs over videos related to their work.<ref name="MI2N">{{cite news | title =Acoustic Singer-Songwriter SJ Launches New "Covers & Others" Video Series| publisher =Music Industry News Network| date =May 23, 2014 | url =http://mi2n.com/press.php3?press_nb=174271| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref><ref name="PRW">{{cite news | title =Acoustic Singer-Songwriter SJ Releases New Video That Shows A Century of Climate Change| publisher =PRWeb| date =October 1, 2014 | url =https://www.prweb.com/releases/2014/09/prweb12190483.htm| access-date =November 20, 2018}}</ref>
