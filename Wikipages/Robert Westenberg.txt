'''Robert Westenberg''' (born October 26, 1953) is an American [[musical theatre]] actor, acting teacher and professor. He appeared on [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] in ''[[Sunday in the Park with George]]'', ''[[The Secret Garden (musical)|The Secret Garden]]'' and ''[[Into the Woods]]'', as well as several other musicals. He is the musical theatre coordinator and associate professor in the theatre and dance department at [[Missouri State University]].

==Early life and education==
Born in [[Miami Beach, Florida]], but raised in [[Fresno, California]] with his six siblings. Westenberg graduated [[Bullard High School (Fresno, California)|Bullard High School]] in 1971, where he was active in sports, specifically football. He then attended [[California State University, Fresno]].<ref name=master/><ref>Rawson, Christopher. [http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/20010928medea0928fnp3.asp "Stage Preview: 'Medea' brings top-class, hard-working actor to the bad-guy role of Jason"] post-gazette.com, September 28, 2001</ref>

Westenberg received a master's degree from Denver’s [[National Theatre Conservatory]].<ref name=msu/>

==Career==
===Broadway===
He made his [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] debut in the 1983 revival of ''[[Zorba (musical)|Zorba]]'' as Nikos, for which he won the [[Theatre World Award]].<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/production/zorba-broadway-theatre-vault-0000012262# " ''Zorba'', Broadway 1983"] Playbill (vault), accessed July 27, 2016</ref> He played the dual role of Soldier/Alex in [[Stephen Sondheim]]'s ''[[Sunday in the Park with George]]'' in May 1984. He later took over [[Mandy Patinkin]]'s leading role of [[Georges Seurat|George Seurat]] where he played opposite [[Bernadette Peters]] in September of that year.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/personlistpage/person-list?production=00000150-aea2-d936-a7fd-eef6797b0000&type=op# " ''Sunday in the Park with George'' Broadway 1984"] Playbill (vault), accessed July 27, 2016</ref> He received a [[Tony Award]] nomination and won a [[Drama Desk Award]] for his performance as [[Prince Charming|Cinderella's Prince]] and the [[Big Bad Wolf|Wolf]] in Sondheim's ''[[Into the Woods]]'' (1987).<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/production/into-the-woods-martin-beck-theatre-vault-0000008219# " ''Into the Woods'' Broadway 1987"] Playbill (vault), accessed July 27, 2016</ref> It was during this show where he met his wife, [[Kim Crosby (singer)|Kim Crosby]], who played [[Cinderella]].<ref name=where>Culwell, Logan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/did-they-finish-the-hat-where-are-the-stars-of-sunday-in-the-park-with-george-now-com-348196# "Did They Finish the Hat? Where Are the Stars of 'Sunday in the Park with George' Now?"] Playbill, May 12, 2015</ref> 

In 1991, he starred with Patinkin again along with [[Daisy Eagan]], [[Alison Fraser]] and [[Rebecca Luker]] as Dr. Neville Craven in the original Broadway production of ''[[The Secret Garden (musical)|The Secret Garden]]''.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/production/the-secret-garden-st-james-theatre-vault-0000004178# " ''The Secret Garden'' Broadway 1991"] Playbill (vault), accessed July 27, 2016</ref> In 2020, Westenberg directed the same production at [[Missouri State University]], where he is head of the Musical Theatre department.<ref>{{cite web|last=Culwell-Block|first=Logan|date=September 16, 2020|title=Robert Westenberg Returns to the 'Prophetic' Secret Garden Amid Coronavirus Pandemic|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/robert-westenberg-returns-to-the-prophetic-secret-garden-amid-coronavirus-pandemic|access-date=2021-01-12|website=Playbill}}</ref>

===Other===
In 2002, Westenberg was in the US and Japan tour of ''[[The Full Monty (musical)|The Full Monty]]''.<ref>Ehren, Christine. [http://www.playbill.com/article/full-montys-westenberg-woods-bare-all-for-boston-dc-san-fran-japan-com-104628# " 'Full Monty' 's Westenberg, Woods Bare All for Boston, DC, San Fran, Japan"], ''Playbill'', March 22, 2002</ref> Westenberg has been in several [[The Muny|MUNY]] (St. Louis) productions. He played Henry Higgins in ''[[My Fair Lady]]'' in 2001 opposite his wife, [[Kim Crosby (singer)|Kim Crosby]], as Eliza Doolittle.<ref>[http://www.talkinbroadway.com/regional/seattle/se107.html "2002 Interview with Robert Westenberg"] talkinbroadway.com</ref>

He played the roles of both Mr. Darling and Captain Hook in the July 2007 St. Louis MUNY production of ''[[Peter Pan (1954 musical)|Peter Pan]]''.<ref name="peter">Gans, Andrew. [http://www.playbill.com/article/hes-flying-francis-jue-is-peter-pan-at-the-muny-beginning-july-16-com-142222# "He's Flying: Francis Jue Is 'Peter Pan' at the Muny Beginning July 16"] ''Playbill'', July 16, 2007</ref> He reprised these roles in a 2009 production in [[Branson, Missouri]], with [[Cathy Rigby]] as Peter Pan.<ref>Cantrell, Rachel. [http://broadcast.branson.com/2009/05/15/cathy-rigby-as-peter-pan-flies-onto-the-mansion-theatre-stage/ "Cathy Rigby as Peter Pan"] branson.com, May 15, 2009</ref> In 2012, he directed a production of ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]'' at [[Springfield Little Theatre]].

===Television and film credits===
Westenberg's film credits include ''[[Mr. and Mrs. Bridge]]'' and ''[[The Ice Storm (film)|The Ice Storm]]''. He has made guest appearances on ''[[Third Watch]]'', ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'', and the short-lived series ''[[Aliens in the Family]]''. He played Prince Raymond on the soap opera, ''[[One Life to Live]]'' from 1989-90.<ref name=master/>

== Personal life ==
He married actress and singer Kim Crosby on June 19, 1991, and the couple now have three children.

Westenberg lives with his wife, daughters Emily and Katherine, and son Joe in [[Springfield, Missouri]], where he is  musical theatre coordinator and associate professor in the theatre and dance department at Missouri State University. He was previously 
chair of the theatre program at [[Drury University]].<ref name=msu>[http://blogs.missouristate.edu/coalexpressions/?p=7075 "Award-winning actor to lead MSU’s musical theatre program"] missouristate.edu, February 1, 2016</ref><ref>[http://www.broadwayworld.com/kansas-city/article/INTO-THE-WOODS-Tony-Nominee-Robert-Westenberg-Joins-MSU-Faculty-20160202 " 'INTO THE WOODS' Tony Nominee Robert Westenberg Joins MSU Faculty"] broadwayworld.com, February 2, 2016</ref><ref>Gordon, David. [http://www.theatermania.com/broadway/news/5-facts-we-learned-into-the-woods-reunion_73309.html "5 Facts We Learned About 'Into the Woods' During the Original-Cast Reunion"] theatermania.com, June 22, 2015</ref><ref name=master>[http://www.masterworksbroadway.com/artist/robert-westenberg/ "Robert Westenberg Bio"] masterworksbroadway.com, accessed July 2016</ref>
