'''Theodore Joseph Jones III''' (September 19, 1984&nbsp;– October 29, 2018),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.davismortuaryservice.com/sitemaker/memsol.cgi?user_id=2147389|title=Theodore J. Jones, III September 19, 1984 – October 29, 2018|website=davismortuaryservice.com|access-date=January 25, 2019}}</ref> better known by his stage name '''Young Greatness''', was an American rapper best known for his 2015 single "[[Moolah (song)|Moolah]]",<ref name="Pitchfork">{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/rapper-young-greatness-shot-and-killed-report/|title=Rapper Young Greatness Shot and Killed|first=Matthew|last=Strauss|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=October 29, 2018|access-date=October 29, 2018}}</ref> which peaked at number 85 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart.<ref name="Billboard">{{Cite web|title=Young Greatness - Chart history|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6818842/young-greatness/chart|work =[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 13, 2016}}</ref>  He was shot and killed in 2018.<ref name="Pitchfork"/>

==Early life==
Jones was born on September 19, 1984, in New Orleans, but moved to [[Houston]] after [[Hurricane Katrina]].<ref name=amg>[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/young-greatness-mn0002919653/biography Young Greatness] at [[AllMusic]]</ref> He grew up listening to [[Juvenile (rapper)|Juvenile]], [[Jay-Z]] and [[The Notorious B.I.G.|Biggie Smalls]].<ref name="XXL">{{Cite web | url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2015/07/break-presents-young-greatness/|title =The Break Presents: Young Greatness|work =[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|date=July 31, 2015|access-date=July 13, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
Taking the name Young Greatness, he began attracting notice from Houston rappers such as [[Bun B]] and [[Mike Jones (rapper)|Mike Jones]], resulting in a deal with the record label [[Quality Control Music]] and [[Motown]] in 2015.<ref name="XXL" /> In November 2015, he released the single "[[Moolah (song)|Moolah]]", which peaked at number 85 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="Billboard"/> In March 2016, ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' included Greatness in their list of "10 New Artists You Need to Know".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/pictures/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-march-2016-20160324 |title=10 New Artists You Need to Know: March 2016 |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=March 24, 2016 |access-date=January 27, 2017}}</ref> In July 2016, Greatness performed "Moolah" on ''[[The Late Show with Stephen Colbert]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Young Greatness Performs "Moolah" on 'The Late Show with Stephen Colbert'|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/07/young-greatness-moolah-late-show-stephen-colbert/|work=XXL |date=July 13, 2016|access-date=September 13, 2016}}</ref>

==Death==
On October 29, 2018, Jones was shot and killed outside a [[Waffle House]] restaurant located at [[Elysian Fields Avenue]] in [[New Orleans]]. He was 34 years old.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rapper Young Greatness Killed in Shooting at New Orleans Waffle House: Report|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8482058/young-greatness-dead-rapper-shot-waffle-house/|work=Billboard |date=October 29, 2018|access-date=}}</ref>
