'''Trophy Eyes''' is an Australian [[punk rock]] band from [[City of Newcastle|Newcastle]], formed in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Tarala|first1=Kate|title=LIVE: Trophy Eyes getting upwardly mobile|url=http://www.theherald.com.au/story/2676331/live-trophy-eyes-getting-upwardly-mobile/|publisher=The Herald|access-date=29 December 2014}}</ref> They are currently signed to [[Hopeless Records]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bird|first1=Michele|title=Trophy Eyes sign to Hopeless Records|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/trophy_eyes_sign_to_hopeless_records|publisher=Alternative Press|access-date=29 December 2014}}</ref> The band currently consists of vocalist John Floreani, bassist and backing vocalist Jeremy Winchester, lead guitarist Andrew Hallett, and drummer Blake Caruso.  Their single "Hurt"  was ranked #135 on Triple J's Hottest 100. Other Hottest 100 appearances include #101 in 2016 with "Chlorine" and #93 in 2018 with "You Can Count On Me".<ref>{{cite web|title=Triple J's Hottest 100|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/17/countdown/101-200/|website=abc|publisher=abc|access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref> To date, they have released three studio albums: ''Mend, Move On'' (2014), ''[[Chemical Miracle]]'' (2016),  and ''[[The American Dream (Trophy Eyes album)|The American Dream]]'' (2018).

==History==
===Early years and ''Everything Goes Away'' (2013–2014)===
Trophy Eyes was formed in 2013 by Andrew Hallett on lead guitar, Kevin Cross on rhythm guitar, Jeremy Winchester on bass and backing vocals, Callum Cramp on drums, and John Floreani on vocals. They later released an EP, ''Demo 2013,'' on 8 April 2013, which consisted of two tracks, "Chacho" and "Personal Taste".<ref>{{cite web|title=Demo 2013|url=https://trophyeyes.bandcamp.com/album/demo-2013|website=bandcamp|publisher=bandcamp}}</ref> ''Everything Goes Away'' has five songs and it was written about a period of his life during which he lived in his high school town prior to moving to Newcastle. It was released on 29 April 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Everything Goes Away|url=https://trophyeyes.bandcamp.com/album/everything-goes-away|website=bandcamp|publisher=bandcamp|access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref> On the same day, a music video for their single "Hourglass" was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FplhzwZJ6BI|title=Trophy Eyes - Hourglass (Official Music Video)|author=Hopeless Records|date=29 April 2014|access-date=2 July 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref>

===''Mend, Move On'' and ''Chemical Miracle'' (2014–2016)===
On 17 September, their single "In Return" was released alongside an accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IKSQmmbHYt0|title=Trophy Eyes - In Return (Official Music Video)|author=Hopeless Records|date=17 September 2014|access-date=2 July 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> On 9 October, they released a second single "White Curtains" and another music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V83L4F3d9sk|title=Trophy Eyes - White Curtains (Lyric Video)|author=Hopeless Records|date=9 October 2014|access-date=2 July 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> On 24 October, their third single "Penfold State Forest" was released.

In just under a year after the release of ''Everything Goes Away'', they released their debut studio album, ''Mend, Move On'' was released on 4 November 2014 under Hopeless Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mend, Move On|url=https://trophyeyes.bandcamp.com/album/mend-move-on| website=bandcamp| publisher=bandcamp| access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref> The album was recorded at Karma Sound Studios in Thailand with Shane Edwards, as would their next two albums.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Williams|first1=Ryan|title=Trophy Eyes|url=https://www.newcastlemirage.com/stories/trophy-eyes|website=Newcastle Mirage|publisher=oasis social|access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref>

The band released a non-album single called "Tired Hearts" in September 2015.

Trophy Eyes performed at the 2015 Vans Warped Tour on the Hard Rock stage and Full Sail stage. They shared a stage with bands like Emarosa, Have Mercy, Knuckle Puck, and Palisades.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Crane|first1=Matt|title=Blessthefall, Hundredth, Alive Like Me, more announced for Warped Tour 2015|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/blessthefall_hundredth_alive_like_me_more_announced_for_warped_tour_2015|publisher=Alternative Press|access-date=29 December 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150104015326/http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/blessthefall_hundredth_alive_like_me_more_announced_for_warped_tour_2015|archive-date=4 January 2015|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

The band's sophomore studio album, ''Chemical Miracle'' released on 14 October 2016.<ref>{{cite web|title=Trophy Eyes release new album ‘Chemical Miracle’|url=http://pop.inquirer.net/2016/10/trophy-eyes-release-new-album-chemical-miracle/|website=Pop Inquirer|access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref> Like their other album, this one was also recorded in Thailand by Shane Edwards.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Underwood|first1=Alex|title=Chasing the Chemical Miracle with Trophy Eyes|url=http://pilerats.com/written/get-to-know/chasing-the-chemical-miracle-with-trophy-eyes/|website=Pilerats|access-date=10 February 2018}}</ref>

===Cramp's departure and ''The American Dream'' (2017–2019)===
At Warped Tour 2017, they played alongside bands such as [[Knocked Loose]], [[Boston Manor (band)|Boston Manor]], and [[Movements (band)|Movements]]. They have also toured the United Kingdom with Welsh band [[Neck Deep]], American band [[Knuckle Puck]] and Canadian band [[Seaway (band)|Seaway]] on the Intercontinental Championships Tour. This tour lasted from 26 January to 6 February.<ref>{{cite magazine |title=ND: The Intercontinental Championships Tour |department=Tour advertisement |magazine=[[Kerrang!]] |publication-date=17 January 2015 |issue= 1551 |page=37}}</ref> 

In October, long-time drummer Callum Cramp announced his departure from the band.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Newstead|first1=Al|title=Trophy Eyes wave goodbye to original drummer|url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/trophy-eyes-wave-goodbye-to-original-drummer/9139650|website=abc|publisher=abc|access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref> Personal tensions between Cramp and the band were cited as the reason he left.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.newcastleherald.com.au/story/5447522/trophy-eyes-on-their-new-sound-and-losing-former-drummer/|title=John Floreani talks Trophy Eyes' new pop direction and why Callum Cramp left the band|publisher=Newcastle Herald|first=Josh|last=Leeson|date=7 June 2018|access-date=2 July 2019}}</ref> "Hurt", the band's first single without Cramp, was released on 8 November 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hurt|url=https://trophyeyes.bandcamp.com/album/hurt|website=bandcamp|publisher=bandcamp|access-date=9 February 2018}}</ref>

From 24 November to 17 December, the band embarked on their first US headlining tour with support from Free Throw, Grayscale, and Head North. Blake Caruso filled in for drums on this tour before officially joining the band.

On 29 March 2018, they joined the line-up of Australia's first Download Festival in Melbourne.

On 29 May 2018, the single "You Can Count On Me" was released alongside an accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gs_7y0ToBcU|title=Trophy Eyes - You Can Count On Me (Official Music Video)|author=Hopeless Records|date=29 May 2018|access-date=2 July 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> On 31 July, the second single, "Friday Forever" was released alongside an accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UzHpkfu8koM|title=Trophy Eyes - Friday Forever (Official Music Video)|author=Hopeless Records|date=31 July 2018|access-date=2 July 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> Trophy Eyes' third studio album, ''[[The American Dream (Trophy Eyes album)|The American Dream]]'', was released on 3 August. A music video for their track "Lavender Bay", was released on 14 November.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9C1NAK1TPbc|title=Trophy Eyes - Lavender Bay (Official Music Video)|author=Hopeless Records|date=14 November 2018|access-date=2 July 2019}}</ref>

=== Cross' departure and "Figure Eight" (2019–Present) ===
In April 2019, Trophy Eyes performed as a support act for [[Bring Me the Horizon]] during the Australian leg of their First Love Tour alongside [[You Me At Six]] and [[Frank Carter & The Rattlesnakes]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/bring-me-the-horizon-announce-australian-tour-support-acts/|title=Bring Me The Horizon Announce Australian Tour Support Acts|last=Langford|first=Jackson|date=22 February 2019|publisher=Music Feeds|access-date=2 July 2019}}</ref> On 16 July, the band announced that founding guitarist Kevin Cross will leave the band after they perform at [[Splendour in the Grass]] that Saturday.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/trophyeyesmusic/status/1150976153449930752|title=Trophy Eyes on Twitter: "Our dear friend Kevin will play his last show with Trophy Eyes at Splendour In The Grass this Saturday|date=16 July 2019|website=Twitter|access-date=16 July 2019}}</ref>

On 15 January 2020 the band released the single, "Figure Eight", their first new single since ''The American Dream''. Unlike their other songs, "Figure Eight" features the addition of a saxophone, which is rumoured to be played by bassist Jeremy Winchester.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/trophy-eyes-have-released-a-saxxy-new-single|title=Trophy Eyes Have Released A Saxxy New Single - News|website=Rock Sound Magazine|language=en|access-date=2020-01-15}}</ref> Blake Caruso confirmed on [[Twitter]] that the single would not be on the upcoming album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/blake_caruso/status/1217265259762733056|title=New single drops tomorrow. It’s different to anything we’ve ever done before and we’re stepping over boundaries to create a piece of music we enjoy. This song is a stand alone to what we have in store next. We hope you enjoy it|last=Caruso|first=Blake|date=2020-01-14|website=@blake_caruso|language=en|access-date=2020-01-15}}</ref>
