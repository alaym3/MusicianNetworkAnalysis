'''Rodarius Marcell Green'''<ref name="Movie" /> (born August 27, 1998), known professionally as '''Rod Wave''', is an American rapper and singer. He is known for his strong voice and incorporation of [[R&B]] and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]].<ref name="Movie">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/news/9425659/rod-wave-interview|title=Why Rod Wave Might Be Writing Movie Scripts One Day|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Lamarre|first=Carl|date=July 28, 2020|access-date=July 30, 2020}}</ref> Rod rose to fame with the 2019 single "[[Heart on Ice]]", which went viral on [[YouTube]] and [[TikTok]] and peaked at number 25 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="ComplexTikTok">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/tiktok-songs-worth-listening-to/rod-wave-hear-on-ice|title=12 Songs Blowing Up on TikTok (That Are Actually Worth Listening to)|last=McKinney|first=Jessica|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=March 26, 2020|access-date=March 29, 2020}}</ref> Rod's debut album, ''[[Ghetto Gospel (album)|Ghetto Gospel]]'' (2019), peaked at number 10 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="BB200">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2019-11-18|title=Billboard 200 chart|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 11, 2020}}</ref> His second album, ''[[Pray 4 Love]]'' (2020), peaked at number 2 on the ''Billboard'' 200,<ref name="Lamarre">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/amp/articles/news/9425659/rod-wave-interview|title=Why Rod Wave Might Be Writing Movie Scripts One Day|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|last=Lamarre|first=Carl|date=July 28, 2020|access-date=August 7, 2020}}</ref> and included the song "[[Rags2Riches (song)|Rags2Riches]]", which peaked at number 12 on the Hot 100. His third album, ''[[SoulFly (album)|SoulFly]]'' (2021), debuted at number one on the ''Billboard'' 200, marking his first chart-topping album. The album features the tracks "[[Richer (song)|Richer]]" (featuring [[Polo G]]), "[[Street Runner]]", and "[[Tombstone (Rod Wave song)|Tombstone]]", the latter of which is his highest-charting song, peaking at number 11 on the Hot 100

== Career ==
Rod's career began in 2016 with the release of his mixtape ''Hunger Games Vol.1''. He released several mixtapes independently prior to signing with Alamo Records. On June 14, 2019, he released his mixtape ''PTSD'', which included the song "[[Heart on Ice]]". The song went viral on [[YouTube]] and [[TikTok]], peaking at number 25 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="ComplexTikTok" /> Rod's debut album, ''[[Ghetto Gospel (album)|Ghetto Gospel]]'', was released on November 1, 2019, and peaked at number 10 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="BB200" />

Rod's second album ''[[Pray 4 Love]]'' was released on April 3, 2020, debuting at number 2 on the ''Billboard'' 200, with a deluxe edition following on August 7.<ref name="Lamarre" /> Several songs from the album charted on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, including "[[Rags2Riches (song)|Rags2Riches]]", which peaked at number 12 on the Hot 100 and became his highest-charting song.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1295413518158757888|user=billboardcharts|title=.@rodwave's "Rags2Riches," featuring...|date=August 17, 2020}}</ref> On August 11, 2020, Rod Wave was included on ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]''{{'}}s 2020 [[XXL (magazine)#Annual Freshman Class List|Freshman Class]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/xxl-2020-freshman-class-revealed/|title=XXL 2020 FRESHMAN CLASS REVEALED|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=Cline|first=Georgette|date=August 11, 2020|access-date=August 11, 2020}}</ref> In July 2020, Rod Wave revealed during an interview that he was working on his third album; it was originally scheduled for August 27, 2020.<ref name="Movie" /> On March 6, 2021, he revealed the track list for the album, titled '''[[SoulFly (album)|SoulFly]]''. When ''[[SoulFly (album)|SoulFly]]'' came out it was the number one streaming album on YouTube. It was released on March 26, and includes a sole feature from [[Polo G]] on the single, "[[Richer (song)|Richer]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CMGFlbcMISV/|title=SoulFly‚úàÔ∏èüñ§üåç 3/26th The Return üåä|via=RodWave on Instagram|date=March 6, 2021|accessdate=March 7, 2021}}</ref><ref name="SoulFly">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/rod-wave-is-all-about-his-business-on-street-runner-single-new-song.1990557.html%3f|title=Rod Wave Is All About His Business On "Street Runner" Single|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Marie|first=Erika|date=January 8, 2021|accessdate=March 8, 2021}}</ref>

=== Influences ===
Rod grew up listening to [[E-40]] (who was featured in his song "Calabasas"), [[Chingy]],  [[Boosie Badazz]], [[Kanye West]], and [[Kevin Gates]] (who would later be featured in his song "Cuban Links").<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/03/rod-wave-interview-the-break/|title=The Break Presents: Rod Wave ‚Äì XXL|last=III|first=Robby Seabrook|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=December 13, 2019}}</ref> Kevin also helped produce Rod wave's album "Ghetto gospel". Rod revealed in an interview with [[The Breakfast Club (radio show)|The Breakfast Club]] that he listens to [[Ed Sheeran]]. 

There are also snippets of him singing covers of songs by artists including [[Adele]], [[Plain White T's]] and [[The Script]], often given his own twist to the artists individual songs.
