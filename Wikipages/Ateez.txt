'''Ateez''' ([[Korean language|Korean]]: 에이티즈; [[Japanese language|Japanese]]: エイティーズ; stylized as '''ATEEZ'''), is a South Korean [[Boy band|boy group]] formed by KQ Entertainment. The group consists of eight members: Hongjoong, Seonghwa, Yunho, Yeosang, San, Mingi, Wooyoung, and Jongho. They debuted on October 24, 2018, with the [[extended play]] (EP) ''[[Treasure EP.1: All to Zero]].''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/ateez-k-pop-group-interview-2631528496.html|title=ATEEZ on Tour Life, Getting Along and Listening to Their Fans|website=Paper Magazine|last=Travis|first=Emlyn|date=March 13, 2019|access-date=March 25, 2021|language=en}}</ref> As rookies, Ateez were recipients of the Next Generation Award at the 2019 Golden Disc Awards, as well as being named Worldwide Fans' Choice at both the 2019 and 2020 [[Mnet Asian Music Awards]] (MAMA). Ateez were also dubbed "4th Generation Leaders" by the [[Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism|Korean Ministry of Culture, Sports, and Tourism]], and are official global ambassadors for Korean culture and tourism, often referred to as "Global Performance Idols" by the Korean media.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/421/0004632271|script-title=ko:에이티즈, '2020 해외문화 홍보대사'→월드투어 성공…글로벌 대세|website=News1|via=Naver|last=Kim|first=Minji|date=May 12, 2020|access-date=March 26, 2021|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Lee|first=Woong|date=May 8, 2020|script-title=ko:에이티즈, 해외문화홍보대사로 대한민국 알린다|url=https://www.yna.co.kr/view/AKR20200508102500005?input=1195m|url-status=live|access-date=March 26, 2021|website=Yonhap News|language=ko}}</ref>

As of March 2021, Ateez has released six Korean-language EPs, one full-length album, and three Japanese albums. The releases ''[[Treasure EP.Fin: All to Action]], [[Treasure Epilogue: Action to Answer]],'' ''[[Zero: Fever Part.1]],'' and ''[[Zero: Fever Part.2]]'' have all topped South Korean album charts, with ''Zero: Fever Part. 1'' becoming the group's first album to be certified platinum on the Gaon Albums Chart. To date, Ateez has sold over one million physical copies of their releases in South Korea.<ref>{{Cite web|script-title=ko:가온 인증(Gaon Certification) - Album|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/album.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=&termGbn=month|url-status=live|access-date=September 15, 2020|website=Gaon Music Chart}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{cite web|last=Lee|first=Min-ji|date=September 10, 2020|script-title=ko:여전히 뜨거운 싹쓰리, 8월 가온차트 2관왕|url=https://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=202009100837392410|url-status=live|access-date=March 26, 2021|website=Newsen|language=ko}}</ref>

==History==
===2016–2018: Formation, Pre-debut, and ''Treasure EP.1''===
Plans for a new KQ Entertainment boy group began after a company staff member discovered a letter and sample [[mixtape]] from Ateez's future leader, Kim Hongjoong, which expressed his interest in becoming a [[Korean idol#Trainee system|trainee]] at KQ Entertainment due to his admiration of [[Block B]], a group also managed by the company.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Park|first=Seo-yeon|date=February 22, 2021|script-title=ko:'정희' 최강창민, 데뷔 18년만 성공적 첫 DJ..에이티즈, 동방신기♥→새앨범 스포[종합]|language=ko|work=[[Herald Corporation|Herald POP]]|url=http://pop.heraldcorp.com/view.php?ud=202102221201280145845_1|access-date=July 16, 2021}}</ref> Hongjoong then became the company's first official trainee, and would remain the only trainee until Yunho joined him six months later.<ref>{{Cite web|author=MTV News Staff|date=January 25, 2019|title=Meet ATEEZ, The Rookie K-pop Group Poised To Break Big In 2019|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3110486/ateez-rookie-k-pop-group-say-my-name/|url-status=live|access-date=September 10, 2020|website=MTV News|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Morin|first=Natalie|date=August 13, 2019|title=ATEEZ Are Finally Seeing Their Hard Work Pay Off|url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2019/08/239353/ateez-kpop-kcon-idol-trainee-interview|url-status=live|access-date=September 10, 2020|website=Refinery29|language=en}}</ref> Over the next year, they would be joined by Seonghwa, Yeosang, San, Mingi, Wooyoung, and Jongho. Multiple members had previously trained at other companies: Mingi at [[Maroo Entertainment]], Jongho at [[TOP Media]], and Yeosang and Wooyoung at [[Big Hit Entertainment]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=March 23, 2020|title=MINGI (ATEEZ) Profile, Facts & Pre-Debut|url=https://karchives.com/song-mingi/|access-date=September 10, 2020|website=karchives.com|language=en-US}}</ref>{{Unreliable source?|date=September 2021}}<ref>{{Cite web|last=Choi|first=Jongho|date=May 25, 2020|script-title=ko:[#종호] 에이티니 노래 추천이에여!! 수록곡까지 너무 좋네여! 저랑 오래전부터 같이 연습했던 형이에용ㅎㅎ 형 첫 솔로 앨범 낸 거 축하해여!! #ATEEZ #에이티즈|url=https://twitter.com/ATEEZofficial/status/1264866351362928640|url-status=live|website=Twitter|language=ko|quote=An older brother who I used to be a trainee with for a long time (Referencing [[Kim Woo-seok (singer)|Kim Woo-seok]], an artist signed under [[TOP Media]]).}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Choi|first=Hee-jae|date=November 5, 2020|script-title=ko:"빌보드 집중조명" 에이티즈, 전 세계가 'PICK'한 新 한류돌 [차세대 K팝루키②]|url=https://www.xportsnews.com/?ac=article_view&entry_id=1347167|url-status=live|access-date=April 29, 2021|website=X Sports News|language=ko}}</ref>

Prior to debuting, Ateez were called KQ Fellaz.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|date=November 7, 2018|title=Have You Heard Of: Ateez (KQ Fellaz)|url=https://www.sbs.com.au/popasia/blog/2018/11/07/have-you-heard-ateez-kq-fellaz|url-status=live|access-date=September 14, 2019|website=SBS PopAsia}}</ref> In late 2017, Hongjoong, Mingi, Wooyoung, and Jongho competed on the survival reality show [[Mix Nine]], placing 42nd, 63rd, 72nd, and 43rd, respectively, out of all male contestants.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kim|first=Young-rok|date=November 16, 2017|script-title=ko:'믹스나인' KQ김홍중X송민기X정우영X최종호, 당찬 포부|url=https://sports.chosun.com/news/ntype.htm?id=201711170100144430010307&servicedate=20171116|url-status=live|access-date=April 27, 2021|website=Sports Chosun|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=|script-title=ko:2차 평가 순위|url=https://tv.jtbc.joins.com/event/pr10010513/pm10046907|url-status=live|access-date=August 14, 2021|website=[[JTBC]]|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Song|first=Cho-rong|date=December 12, 2017|title=|script-title=ko:‘믹스나인’ 1차 개별 평가 소년X소녀 종합 순위…종합 1등 해피페이스 우진영|language=ko|work=News Inside|url=http://www.newsinside.kr/news/articleView.html?idxno=480823|access-date=August 14, 2021}}</ref> On May 18, 2018, KQ Entertainment released the [[YouTube]] video "KQ Fellaz Performance Video I", which featured all eight of the future Ateez members together for the first time, dancing to the song "[[Pick It Up (Famous Dex song)|Pick It Up]]" by rapper [[Famous Dex]]. Shortly after, their pre-debut web series, ''KQ Fellaz American Training'', premiered, showcasing the eight members traveling to [[Los Angeles|Los Angeles, California]], to train in dance at [[The Millennium Dance Complex|Millennium Dance Complex]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Warner|first=Solly|date=March 26, 2021|title=ATEEZ: The Machine Behind The Unstoppable K-Pop Force|url=https://notion.online/ateez-the-machine-behind-the-unstoppable-k-pop-force/|access-date=September 18, 2021|url-status=live|website=Notion}}</ref> During this period, a ninth trainee, Lee Junyoung, was promoted as a member of the KQ Fellaz, but ultimately was not added to the final group lineup.<ref name=":6"/> For the finale of the series, KQ Fellaz released the single "From", composed and produced by Hongjoong.<ref>{{Cite web|date=|title=From (CD Only)|url=https://www.melon.com/song/detail.htm?songId=31547766|url-status=live|website=[[Melon (online music service)|Melon]]|access-date=April 25, 2021}}</ref>

KQ Entertainment then announced a KQ Fellaz reality show with a set of three teasers. The second teaser, released on July 3, 2018, revealed the official name for the group: Ateez. The show, ''Code Name Is Ateez'', premiered on [[Mnet (TV channel)|Mnet]] on July 20.<ref name=":5" />

On October 24, 2018, Ateez released their debut EP, ''[[Treasure EP.1: All to Zero]]'', with music videos simultaneously released for the lead singles "Pirate King" (Korean: 해적왕; [[Revised Romanization of Korean|RR]]: ''Haejeogwang'') and "Treasure".<ref>{{cite web|last=Jeon|first=Hyo-jin|date=October 24, 2018|script-title=ko:[DA:투데이] 에이티즈, 오늘 데뷔…괴물 퍼포돌 출격 준비 끝|url=https://sports.donga.com/3/all/20181024/92558647/1|url-status=live|access-date=April 25, 2021|website=Sports Dong-a|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Kim|first=Na-yul|date=October 24, 2018|script-title=ko:[팝's신곡]"세상을 뒤집을 듯이"..에이티즈, 숨은 'Treasure' 찾는 당찬 포부|url=http://pop.heraldcorp.com/view.php?ud=201810241439550423602_1|url-status=live|access-date=April 25, 2021|website=[[Herald Corporation|Herald POP]]|language=ko}}</ref> The album peaked at number 7 on the weekly [[Gaon Album Chart|Gaon Albums Chart]], selling 23,644 copies in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|script-title=ko:2018년 43주차 Album Chart|url=http://www.gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/album.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=&targetTime=43&hitYear=2018&termGbn=week|url-status=live|access-date=July 10, 2019|website=Gaon Music Chart|language=ko}}</ref>
