'''Ama Serwah Genfi''' (born July 4, 1994), known professionally as '''Amaarae''', is a Ghanaian-American singer, songwriter, producer, and engineer known for her work around representation of gender and race in music.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gay sex is banned - so Ghanaians dance against homophobia|url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/news/africa/2019-12-01-gay-sex-is-banned-so-ghanaians-dance-against-homophobia/|access-date=2020-06-17|website=SowetanLIVE|language=en-ZA}}</ref><ref name="Ghana is now">{{Cite web|title=Ghana is now|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/12/04/ghana-scene-profile-darovibes-amaarae-joey-lit|access-date=2020-06-17|website=The FADER|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-01-03|title=The interview we forgot to document - CRTL+ ALT+DEL Amaarae|url=http://harmattanrain.com/amaarae-forgotten-interview/|access-date=2020-06-17|website=Harmattan Rain|language=en-US}}</ref> After collaborating with local artists and putting out a few non-album singles, she released her debut 6 track EP, ''Passionfruit Summers''<ref>{{Cite news|title=Passionfruit Summers|work=Genius|url=https://genius.com/Amaarae-passionfruit-summers-lyrics|access-date=November 30, 2017}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Amaarae|url=https://nataal.com/amaarae|access-date=2020-06-17|website=nataal.com|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name="okayafrica.com">{{Cite web|date=2018-06-12|title=Amaarae's New Music Video for 'Fluid' Is a Beautiful Masterpiece|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/amaarae-fluid-video-ghana/|access-date=2020-06-17|website=OkayAfrica|language=en}}</ref> in 2017. 

In 2020 Amaarae released the song "[[Sad Girlz Luv Money]]" featuring Moliy on her debut album ''The Angel You Don't Know''. In 2021 the song was remixed with a feature from [[Kali Uchis]] which then went on to become a viral  hit on social media and streaming platforms, charting globally.

== Early life ==
Genfi was born on July 4, 1994, in New York and raised between [[Atlanta]] and [[Accra]]<ref name=":0" /> to Ghanaian parents Ama Bawuah and Kwadwo Boateng Genfi. She is the eldest of two children. Amaarae began to experiment with music at the age of 13, when she wrote her first song.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.konbini.com/ng/entertainment/discover-amaarae-sex-positve-neo-soul-star/|title=Discover: Amaarae, The Sex-Positive Ghanaian Neo-Soul Star|work=Konbini|access-date=October 4, 2018}}</ref> In an interview with Unorthodox Reviews, she said "I wrote my first song when I was about 13 and it was a reimagining of [[R.Kelly]] and [[Usher (singer)|Usher]]’s "[[Same Girl (R. Kelly and Usher song)|Same Girl]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.unorthodoxreviews.com/10-questions-amaa-rae|title=10 Questions: Amaarae|work=Unorthodox Reviews|access-date=February 5, 2018}}</ref> During her teenage years, she also took a likening to watching [[Music video|music videos]]. She recalls one of her most vivid memories being watching the music video for the [[Kelis]] song [[Young, Fresh n' New]]. She recalls Kelis' unique expression being an inspiration for her own expression later.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-01|title=Ghanaian pop star Amaarae: 'I'm presenting black women as deities'|url=http://www.theguardian.com/music/2021/jan/01/ghanaian-pop-star-amaarae-im-presenting-black-women-as-deities|url-status=live|access-date=2021-07-20|website=[[The Guardian]]|language=en}}</ref>

During high school she started making mixtapes and by 17, she took up an internship at a music studio. At university, she undertook voice training and honed her songwriting skills while studying English literature and in June 2017 she returned to Ghana.<ref name=":0" />

== Career ==
Having collaborated with artists like AYLØ, Kay-Ara, Yaw P, Amaarae released her debut project ''Passionfruit Summers''<ref>{{Cite news|title=Passionfruit Summers|work=Genius|url=https://genius.com/Amaarae-passionfruit-summers-lyrics|access-date=November 30, 2017}}</ref><ref name=":02">{{Cite web|title=Amaarae|url=https://nataal.com/amaarae|access-date=2020-06-17|website=nataal.com|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name="okayafrica.com2">{{Cite web|date=2018-06-12|title=Amaarae's New Music Video for 'Fluid' Is a Beautiful Masterpiece|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/amaarae-fluid-video-ghana/|access-date=2020-06-17|website=OkayAfrica|language=en}}</ref> in 2017. The album features the standout song, "Fluid", which was complemented by a critically acclaimed music video.<ref>{{Cite news|title=I just want people to have sex with my music.|work=Globe Trotter Mag|url=https://www.globetrottermag.com/news-features/amaraee-fluid|access-date=July 9, 2019}}</ref>

Recognized both locally and internationally, she was named one of [[Apple Music]] Africa's Favourite New Artist in April 2018<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/entertainment/Amaa-Rae-releases-new-song-titled-Fluid-658954#|title=Amaa Rae releases new song titled 'Fluid'|work=Ghana Web|access-date=June 10, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://thebftonline.com/2018/lifestylesports/amaarae-a-refreshing-afro-fusion-voice/|title=Amaarae: a refreshing Afro Fusion voice|work=B&FT Online|access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.blankpaperz.com/amaarae/|title=AMAARAE|work=BlankPaperz|access-date=May 11, 2019}}</ref> and later that year became an [[Beats 1|Apple Music Beats 1]] featured artist<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://thenativemag.com/music/apple-musics-african-music-playlist-special-highlights|title=APPLE MUSIC'S "BEATS 1" STREAMS HOUR-LONG SPECIAL ON AFRICAN MUSIC|work=Native Mag|access-date=December 14, 2018}}</ref> for her debut project ''Passionfruit Summers'' which she released through her independent record label, Golden Child LLC on November 30, 2017.<ref name="okayafrica.com" /> She performed with [[Teni (singer)|Teni]], Boj of [[DRB LasGidi]] and Odunsi at [[ART X Lagos]], an art fair in Lagos, Nigeria on November 3, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://thenativemag.com/communities/artx-live|title=ODUNSI, TENI THE ENTERTAINER, AMAA RAE FOR ART X LIVE! 2018|work=Native mag|access-date=October 26, 2018}}</ref>

On March 23, 2019, Amaarae became one of the acts chosen to perform at the first-ever [[Boiler Room (music broadcaster)|Boiler Room]] event to happen in Accra, Ghana alongside La Meme Gang (A Collective comprising [[Nxwrth]], RJZ, KwakuBS, [[Darkovibes]], Kiddblack and [[$pacely]]) and Award Winning Rapper [[Kwesi Arthur]].<ref>{{Cite news|title=TRUE MUSIC AFRICA: GHANA|work=Boiler Room|url=https://boilerroom.tv/session/true-music-africa-ghana|access-date=March 3, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=DJ Mic SMith, Vision DJ, Kwesi Arthur, Amaarae to headline Boiler Room and Ballantine's TRUE MUSIC AFRICA|work=YFM Ghana|url=https://www.yfmghana.com/2019/02/20/dj-mic-smith-vision-dj-kwesi-arthur-amaarae-to-headline-boiler-room-and-ballantines-true-music-africa/|access-date=February 20, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Boiler Room is coming to Ghana|work=Music in Africa|url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/boiler-room-coming-ghana|access-date=February 21, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=DIASPORA DISTINS: ANTONIA ODUNLAMI WITH AMAARAE|work=Worldwide FM|url=https://worldwidefm.net/show/diaspora-distins-antonia-odunlami-with-amaarae/|access-date=June 28, 2019}}</ref>

Amaarae has since added [[Stonebwoy]], [[Kojey Radical]], [[M3NSA]], Santi, [[Blaqbonez]], Buju, Odunsi, B4bonah and very recently, in 2019, the Nigerian Singer-songwriter [[Wande Coal]]<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-05-22|title="Spend Some Time" Amaarae ft. Wande Coal|url=https://notion.online/spend-some-time-amaarae-ft-wande-coal/|access-date=2020-06-17|website=Notion|language=en-GB}}</ref> to the list of artists she has worked with.<ref name=":02" />

In October 2019, Amaarae penned and unprecedented op-ed for [[OkayAfrica]] consisting of short stories that contextualised her experience as a female musician in male-dominated landscape, entitled [https://www.okayafrica.com/amaarae-like-it-op-ed/ ''3 Moments That Defined My Journey As a Young Woman In Search of Herself & Her Purpose'']. These included "Thank God Herself", "Why Do You Hate Me?", and "A Conversation with Alexis" that would precede the latest of her non-album singles, "Like It".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-10-02|title=3 Moments That Defined My Journey As a Young Woman In Search of Herself & Her Purpose|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/amaarae-like-it-op-ed/|access-date=2020-06-17|website=OkayAfrica|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":1" />

On November 12, 2020, Amaarae released her debut studio album, ''[[The Angel You Don't Know]]''. Owen Myers of ''Pitchfork'' stated, "''The Angel You Don't Know'' crackles with innovation, a pacesetter at a time when industry bigwigs are waking up to the long-held truth that Africa is setting the global tempo for pop music."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Amaarae: The Angel You Don't Know|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/amaarae-the-angel-you-dont-know/|access-date=2020-11-23|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref>
