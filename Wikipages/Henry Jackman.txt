'''Henry Pryce Jackman''' (born 1974)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/henry-jackman-mn0001872328/biography|title=Henry Jackman|last=Donelson|first=Marcy|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 20, 2020}}</ref> is an English composer, conductor, arranger, pianist, musician, and songwriter. He composed music for films such as ''[[Kong: Skull Island]]'', ''[[X-Men: First Class]]'', ''[[Winnie the Pooh (2011 film)|Winnie the Pooh]]'', ''[[Wreck-It Ralph]]'', ''[[Puss in Boots (2011 film)|Puss in Boots]]'', ''[[Monsters vs. Aliens]]'', ''[[Captain Phillips (film)|Captain Phillips]]'', ''[[Captain America: The Winter Soldier]]'', ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]'', ''[[Kick-Ass (film)|Kick-Ass]]'', ''[[Kick-Ass 2 (film)|Kick-Ass 2]]'', ''[[Big Hero 6 (film)|Big Hero 6]]'', ''[[The Interview]]'', the theme from ''[[The Falcon and the Winter Soldier]]'' and ''[[Detective Pikachu (film)|Detective Pikachu]]'', as well as the video games ''[[Uncharted 4: A Thief's End]]'' and ''[[Disney Infinity 2.0]]''.

==Life and career==
Jackman was born in [[Hillingdon]], [[London]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Henry Jackman biography |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/1490249/henry-jackman/biography |website=Billboard.com |access-date=12 October 2016}}</ref> He studied classical music at [[St Paul's Cathedral School|St. Paul's Cathedral Choir School]], [[Eton College]], [[Framlingham College]] and [[University of Oxford]].

Jackman has done programming and production work with artists including [[Mike Oldfield]] (''[[Voyager (Mike Oldfield album)|Voyager]]''), [[Sally Oldfield]] (''Flaming Star''), [[Trevor Horn]]/[[Art of Noise]] (''[[The Seduction of Claude Debussy]]''), [[Elton John]] and [[Gary Barlow]]. He co-produced [[Seal (musician)|Seal]]'s unreleased 2001 album ''[[Togetherland]]''.<ref>{{cite book |title=The Greatest Music Never Sold |page=15 |publisher=Hal Leonard |date=2007 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=40FLKsz7NhcC|isbn=9780879309053 }}</ref> "[[This Could Be Heaven (Seal song)|This Could Be Heaven]]", released from the album, was also co-written by Jackman and used on the movie ''[[The Family Man]]''<ref>{{cite web |title=The Family Man soundtrack review |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/the-family-man-mw0000110697 |website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=12 October 2016}}</ref> and included on the deluxe edition of Seal's compilation album ''[[Hits (Seal album)|Hits]]''.

Jackman has released three albums, ''Utopia'' (2003), ''Transfiguration'' (2005), and ''Acoustica'' (2007; with Augustus Isadore).

Jackman had various minor roles on film scores since 2006, generally working with mentor [[Hans Zimmer]], including for ''[[The Da Vinci Code (film)|The Da Vinci Code]]'' (music programmer), ''[[The Dark Knight (film)|The Dark Knight]]'' (music arranger) and additional music for ''[[Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest]]'', ''[[Pirates of the Caribbean: At World's End (soundtrack)|At World's End]]'', ''[[The Simpsons Movie]]'', ''[[Kung Fu Panda]]'' and ''[[Hancock (film)|Hancock]]''. In 2009, Jackman, Zimmer and [[John Powell (composer)|John Powell]] won the [[36th Annie Awards#Music in an Animated Television Production or Short Form|2008 Annie Award for Music in an Animated Television Production or Short Form]] for their work on [[DreamWorks Animation]]'s ''[[Secrets of the Furious Five]]'' (a sequel to ''Kung Fu Panda'').<ref>{{cite web |title=36th Annual Annie Nominations and Awards Recipients |url=http://annieawards.org/36th-annie-awards|website=Annieawards.org |access-date=12 October 2016}}</ref> He has since composed soundtracks for ''[[Monsters vs. Aliens]]'', ''[[Henri IV]]'', ''[[Gulliver's Travels (2010 film)|Gulliver's Travels]]'', ''[[X-Men: First Class]]'', ''[[Winnie the Pooh (2011 film)|Winnie the Pooh]]'', ''[[Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter (film)|Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter]]'', ''[[Wreck-It Ralph]]'', ''[[Captain America: The Winter Soldier]]'', and ''[[Captain America: Civil War]]''. His first major video game score was ''[[Uncharted 4: A Thief's End]]''.

==Family==
Henry Pryce Jackman is the son of keyboardist and arranger [[Andrew Pryce Jackman]], who was a member of [[The Syn]] and worked for many years with [[Chris Squire]] of [[Yes (band)|Yes]]. His uncle Gregg Jackman is a sound engineer and producer who has worked with the [[King's Singers]] and [[Barclay James Harvest]];<ref>{{cite web |title=Gregg Jackman biography |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/gregg-jackman-mn0000191311 |website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=12 October 2016}}</ref> Henry and his uncle both worked on [[Moa (singer)|Moa]]'s 1999 album ''Universal''.<ref>{{cite web |title=''Universal'' album credits |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/universal-mw0000047337/credits |website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=12 October 2016}}</ref> His grandfather, Bill Jackman, played [[clarinet]] on "[[When I'm Sixty-Four]]" on [[The Beatles]]' ''[[Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Andrew Price Jackman biography |url=http://www.bjharvest.co.uk/apjack.htm|website=Bjharvest.co.uk |access-date=12 October 2016}}</ref>
