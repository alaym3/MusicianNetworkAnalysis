'''DeMario DeWayne White Jr.''' (born September 22, 1991), known professionally as '''Moneybagg Yo''', is an American rapper. He is signed to fellow Memphis rapper [[Yo Gotti]]'s record label, [[Yo Gotti#Collective Music Group|Collective Music Group]] (CMG),<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/10/yo-gotti-signs-moneybagg-yo/ |title=Yo Gotti Signs MoneyBagg Yo |publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |author=Simmons, Ted |date=October 28, 2016 |access-date=August 28, 2017}}</ref> in a joint venture deal with [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/7898197/moneybagg-yo-interscope-records-distribution-deal-yo-gotti-interview |title=Memphis Star Moneybagg Yo Talks Independent Success & New Distribution Deal: Exclusive |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |author=Lamarre, Carl |date=August 14, 2017 |access-date=August 28, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://massappeal.com/moneybagg-yo-yo-gotti-memphis-rap/ |title=Is Moneybagg Yo Memphis Rap's Next Big Thing? |publisher=[[Mass Appeal (media)|Mass Appeal]] |author=Robinson, Zandria |date=August 18, 2017 |access-date=August 28, 2017}}</ref> His first two projects to debut in the Top 20, ''[[Federal 3X]]'' and ''[[2 Heartless]]'', debuted at No. 5 and No. 16 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], respectively.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2017/08/21/rapper-moneybagg-yo-reaches-the-top-10-for-the-first-time-behind-keshas-latest-no-1/#207a67173c82 |title=Rapper Moneybagg Yo Reaches The Top 10 For The First Time Behind Kesha's Latest No. 1 |work=[[Forbes]] |author=McIntyre, Hugh |date=August 21, 2017 |access-date=August 28, 2017}}</ref> His third album, [[Time Served (album)|''Time Served'']], debuted at number 3 on the ''Billboard'' 200 in January 2020, while his fourth album, ''[[A Gangsta's Pain]]'', was released in April 2021 and became his first chart-topping project.

== Early life ==
DeMario DeWayne White Jr. was born on September 22, 1991, in [[South Memphis, Memphis, Tennessee|South Memphis, Tennessee]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite magazine |date=October 29, 2018 |title=Moneybagg Yo Talks Going Back to His Roots for Upcoming Album 'Reset' |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8482220/moneybagg-yo-album-interview |access-date=July 28, 2020 |magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

== Career ==
His early mixtapes were issued in 2012, including ''From Da Block 2 Da Booth'' and ''October 20th''. In 2016, he released three more, one of which won a Memphis Hip Hop Award for Mixtape of the Year. ''Federal Reloaded'', a revision of the first installment of the ''Federal'' series, featured guest appearances from Y Grizzle, [[Young Dolph]] and [[OG Boo Dirty]], among others. The follow-up, ''ELO (Everybody Lives On)'', included guest appearances from [[Yo Gotti]] and Migos' [[Quavo]], among others. His work with Yo Gotti continued on a joint effort titled ''[[2 Federal]]'', released in October 2016. ''[[Heartless (Moneybagg Yo mixtape)|Heartless]]'' arrived in February 2017 and charted at No. 177 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref name="BB200" /> The mixtape featured guest appearances from [[YFN Lucci]] and [[Lil Durk]]. In October 2017, Moneybagg released ''[[Federal 3X]]'', his debut with Interscope since signing a distribution deal. The mixtape contains a sole feature from [[YoungBoy Never Broke Again]].

In 2018, Moneybagg Yo delivered a compilation album: ''Moneybagg Yo Presents: NLess Ent x Bread Gang''. The project was released on January 1, 2018. On February 14, 2018, Moneybagg released his sequel mixtape to 2017's ''Heartless'', ''[[2 Heartless]]'' with features from Quavo, Yo Gotti, [[Lil Baby]], and [[BlocBoy JB]] and production from [[Zaytoven]] and [[Southside (record producer)|Southside]]. On July 27, 2018, he released a nine-track mixtape, ''Bet On Me'', with features from YoungBoy Never Broke Again, Lil Baby, and [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]] with the production handled from [[Tay Keith]], Drum God, Javar and others. Moneybagg Yo released his debut studio album ''[[Reset (Moneybagg Yo album)|Reset]]'' in November 2018.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/08/moneybagg-yo-federal-3x-project/ |title=Listen to Moneybagg Yo's New 'Federal 3x' Project |publisher=XXL |last=Weinstein |first=Max |date=August 10, 2017 |access-date=August 29, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/moneybagg-yo-mn0003604916/biography |title=MoneyBagg Yo &#124; Biography & History |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |author=Yeung, Neil Z. |access-date=August 29, 2017}}</ref> He released his second studio album, ''[[43va Heartless]]'', in May 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/moneybagg-yo-releases-43va-heartless-featuring-blac-youngsta-kevin-gates-offset-city-girls-new-mixtape.119169.html |title=Moneybagg Yo Releases "43va Heartless" Featuring Blac Youngsta, Kevin Gates, Offset, City Girls |website=HotNewHipHop|date=May 24, 2019 }}</ref> His third studio album, ''[[Time Served (album)|Time Served]]'', was released in January 2020. It includes "[[All Dat]]" and "[[U Played]]", his first [[Hot 100]]-charting singles.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/1/moneybagg-yo-time-served-album-stream |title=Moneybagg Yo Drops Roc Nation Debut 'Time Served' |website=Hypebeast|date=January 10, 2020 }}</ref> The album reached number three on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], with 66,000 units sold during its first week of release. It was followed up by a deluxe edition in May. On June 30, 2020, he released the single "[[Said Sum]]", his highest-charting song as a lead artist.<ref name="Sum" /> <br />
After Moneybagg Yo and fellow rapper [[Blac Youngsta]] both announced they would be releasing their respective solo albums on a shared day of September 18, 2020, the rappers took this to social media two days before to announce they would instead release a collaborative mixtape titled ''Code Red''. Moneybagg Yo also released a remix of "Said Sum", featuring American [[hip hop music|hip hop]] group [[City Girls]] and American rapper [[DaBaby]], which is included on the mixtape.<ref name="Code">{{cite web |url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2020/9/16/moneybagg-yo-and-blac-youngsta-announce-their-collaboration.html |title=Moneybagg Yo and Blac Youngsta announce their collaboration album, 'Code Red' |work=ABC News Radio |last=George |first=Rachel |date=September 16, 2020 |access-date=September 16, 2020}}</ref>

Moneybagg released his fourth studio album, ''[[A Gangsta's Pain]]'', on April 23, 2021. It features guest appearances from [[Kaash Paige]], [[Big30]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[Polo G]], [[Lil Durk]], [[Jhené Aiko]], and [[Pharrell Williams]]. The album became his first number-one project, debuting with 110,000 units on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], and returned to the top in its third week.<ref name="Rookie">{{cite web|url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/new-music/2021/6/1/22463136/moneybagg-yo-rookie-of-the-year-song|title=Moneybagg Yo is :Rookie of the Year" on new single|work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]]|last=Powell|first=Jon|date=June 1, 2021|access-date=June 12, 2021}}</ref> On June 1, 2021, Moneybagg released the standalone single, "Rookie of the Year", an ode to basketball player [[Ja Morant]].<ref name="Rookie" />

== Personal life ==
Moneybagg Yo has eight children, four sons and four daughters.<ref>{{Cite web |via=The Breakfast Club (radio show) |date=January 11, 2020 |title=Moneybagg Yo Talks Faithful Relationships, Quitting Lean, Signing To Yo Gotti + More |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bTApaz9dXyk |access-date=July 28, 2020 |website=YouTube}}</ref> He dated fellow rapper [[Megan Thee Stallion]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/11/moneybagg-yo-addresses-his-relationship-with-megan-thee-stallion |title=Moneybagg Yo Addresses His Relationship with Megan Thee Stallion: 'We Good' |work=Complex |author=Jose Martinez |date=November 26, 2019 |access-date=April 11, 2020}}</ref> however they broke up in 2019; she appeared on his single "[[All Dat]]". As of January 2020, he is dating club hostess Ariana "Ari" Fletcher.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.revolt.tv/platform/amp/news/2020/1/9/21058697/moneybagg-yo-confirms-relationship-ariana-fletcher |title=Moneybagg Yo confirms relationship with Ariana Fletcher |work=[[Revolt (TV network)|Revolt]] |last=Gunn |first=Tamantha Gunn |date=January 9, 2020 |access-date=July 13, 2020}}</ref>

In 2018, MoneyBagg Yo converted to Islam, taking his [[Shahada]] from rapper, [[Kevin Gates]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://myreligionisrap.net/blogs/news/moneybagg-yo-converts-to-islam-in-presence-of-kevin-gates-then-they-drop-new-track-federal-pressure|title=Moneybagg Yo Converts to Islam in Presence of Kevin Gates then They Drop New Track 'Federal Pressure'}}</ref> He prays five times a day and he fasts during Ramadan.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VYcEMA670JQ</ref>

Moneybagg Yo described Islam as what made him quit drinking lean, stating in an interview that, "I'm Muslim so I'm disciplined...I'm strong-minded."<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bTApaz9dXyk</ref>
