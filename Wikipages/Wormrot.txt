'''Wormrot''' is a Singaporean [[grindcore]] band formed in 2007, immediately after the founding members had completed their mandatory two years of [[National service in Singapore|national service]].<ref name="Hoare">{{cite journal|last=Hoare|first=James|date=June 2010|title=Wormrot feature|journal=[[Terrorizer (magazine)|Terrorizer]]}}</ref> The band have released three studio albums to date, as well as a number of EPs and split releases with other bands. Their latest album ''Voices'' was released on October 14, 2016 through [[Earache Records]]. They have been described as one of the top 10 grindcore bands by [[OC Weekly]], and have toured [[Europe]], and the [[United States]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.ocweekly.com/music/top-10-grindcore-bands-6597574|title=Top 10 Grindcore Bands|last=Distefano|first=Alex|date=2014-03-04|newspaper=OC Weekly|access-date=2016-10-14}}</ref> In 2017, they became the first Singaporean act to play at the [[Glastonbury Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/lifestyle/entertainment/singapore-band-wormrot-perform-in-a-train-at-glastonbury|title=Singapore band Wormrot perform in a 'train' at Glastonbury|website=Straitstimes.com|date=24 June 2017}}</ref>

==History==
The band was discovered in late December 2009 by [[Digby Pearson]], the owner of record label [[Earache Records|Earache]], in a [[mixtape]] posted on the ''[[Invisible Oranges]]'' blog.<ref name="Smith">{{cite journal|last=Smith|first=Zach|date=August 2010|title=Grind! in the name of love|journal=[[Decibel (magazine)|Decibel]]|issue=70|page=40|issn=1557-2137}}</ref> After hearing the song "Born Stupid" on the compilation, Pearson searched out and downloaded Wormrot's debut, ''Abuse'', from file-sharing site [[MediaFire]].<ref name="O'Hagar">{{cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2010/04/13/wormrot-strive-to-be-singapores-chief-export-on-abuse/|title=Wormrot strive to be Singapore's chief export on Abuse|last=O'Hagar|first=Sammy|date=13 April 2010|website=MetalSucks.net|accessdate=13 July 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.invisibleoranges.com/2010/02/earache-and-burzum-album-leak/|title=Earache and Burzum album leak|last=Lee|first=Cosmo|date=22 February 2010|website=[[Invisible Oranges]]|accessdate=23 July 2010}}</ref> Pearson was impressed with the album's quality and the band's "ambition, hard work ethic, and willingness to put [its home country] and Asian grindcore in the global map."<ref name="Pearson">{{cite news|url=http://askearache.blogspot.com/2010/01/how-did-earache-sign-wormrot.html|title=How did Earache sign Wormrot?|last=Pearson|first=Digby|date=31 January 2010|publisher=[[Earache Records]]|accessdate=11 July 2010}}</ref> Pearson approached the band on their [[Myspace]] page, asking if they were interested in joining Earache's roster.<ref>{{cite web |title=Metal Horde SG interview with WORMROT |url=https://metalhordesg.wordpress.com/2011/08/11/metal-horde-sg-interview-with-wormrot/|website=Metalhordesg.wordpress.com |accessdate=17 September 2018}}</ref> The band signed a [[recording contract]] with Earache in late January 2010.<ref name="Pearson" /><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bravewords.com/news/131192|title=Wormrot ink deal with Earache Records|date=27 January 2010|work=[[BW&BK]]|accessdate=11 July 2010}}</ref>

Earache reissued ''Abuse'' in Europe on 13 April 2010 and in the United States on 8 June 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bravewords.com/news/136616|title=Wormrot debut out now in Europe|date=13 April 2010|work=BW&BK|accessdate=11 July 2010}}</ref><ref name="Pacheco About.com">{{cite web|url=http://heavymetal.about.com/od/w/gr/wormrot-abuse.htm|title=Abuse review|last=Pacheco|first=George|website=[[About.com]]|accessdate=11 July 2010}}</ref> To promote the album's reissue, Wormrot toured Europe between late April and June 2010, and US between mid August and September 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bravewords.com/news/137412|title=Wormrot kick off European tour|date=23 April 2010|work=BW&BK|accessdate=11 July 2010}}</ref>

Their second studio album, ''[[Dirge (album)|Dirge]]'', was released on 3 May 2011 through [[Earache Records]]. The album received positive reviews from [[AllMusic]] writer Phil Freeman and other critics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/dirge-mw0002130434|title=Dirge – Wormrot {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits {{!}} AllMusic|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=2016-07-25}}</ref> The band released an EP titled 'Noise' digitally through [[Scion AV]] on 20 September 2011. The EP was distributed as a download for free.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.scionav.com/2011/09/20/wormrot-noise-ep-release/|title=Wormrot 'Noise' EP Release, Scion AV|date=2011-09-20|website=Scion Audio Visual Blog|access-date=2016-07-25}}</ref>

Wormrot went on an extended US tour in early March and April 2011 and a European tour in August–September 2011. In January 2012, they had toured Indonesia and their last European tour was in June–July 2012.<ref name="2012 European Tour Flyer">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151813700605640&set=a.246530065639.303402.237638635639&type=1&theater|title=2012 European Tour Flyer|website=Facebook.com}}</ref> In a summer 2012 interview, the band commented that they would be taking a three-to-four-year break, during which they would continue writing material for another album.<ref name="Cardiff Interview 2012">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YgrwPcFhVYU|title=Cardiff Interview 2012|publisher=[[YouTube]]|accessdate=17 June 2012}}</ref>

On April 6, 2015, the band announced that Fitri had been removed from the band by Arif and Rasyid. The current status of Fitri is unknown, with the band writing that "For the past couple of weeks, he has distanced himself from us and has remained incontactable [sic]. We fear that he has gotten himself into some shit too deep, he’s not comfortable to share with us. If any of his friends who are reading this have any news regarding Fitri, let him know that we’re waiting for any kind of reply. Any."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2015/04/06/wormrot-issue-wormupdate/|title=Wormrot Issue Wormupdate|date=2015-04-06|website=Metalsucks.net|access-date=2016-10-14}}</ref> In June 2015, the band announced that they had recruited a new drummer, Vijesh Ghariwala, and would be continuing work on recording their third album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.metalinjection.net/latest-news/yes/wormrot-found-a-new-drummer-were-getting-a-new-album|title=WORMROT Found A New Drummer! We're Getting A New Album! – Metal Injection|date=2015-06-12|website=Metalinjection.net|language=en-US|access-date=2016-10-14}}</ref>

On 20 July 2016, the band's third studio album, ''Voices'', was revealed with an album trailer featuring a new song titled "Fallen Into Disuse". The band later recorded a music video for this song in support of the album's release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/wormrot-fallen-into-disuse|title=Singaporean Grindcore Squad Wormrot Returns with "Fallen Into Disuse" – Noisey|website=Noisey.vice.com|language=en-us|access-date=2016-10-14}}</ref> According to Arif, the song is about "unconscious abandonment from reality. Desperately trying to be accepted into the norm. At the very least. Failed miserably." Other songs including were also premiered in advance of the album's release, and the album was made available to stream in full on Noisey on October 13.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2016/10/10/exclusive-track-premiere-gods-heaven-wormrot/|title=Exclusive Track Premiere: "God's in His Heaven" by Wormrot|date=2016-10-10|website=Metalsucks.net|access-date=2016-10-14}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/wormrot-returns-to-grind-blockheads-and-posers-into-dust-on-voices|title=Wormrot Returns to Grind Blockheads and Posers into Dust on 'Voices' – Noisey|website=Noisey.vice.com|language=en-us|access-date=2016-10-14}}</ref> ''Voices'' was released on October 14, 2016 through Earache Records.
