'''Raúl Alejandro Ocasio Ruiz''' (born January 10, 1993), known professionally as '''Rauw Alejandro''', is a Puerto Rican singer and rapper. He belongs to "the new generation" of Puerto Rican [[Urbano music|urban musicians]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|last=Fernandez|first=Suzette|title=Latin Artist On the Rise: Meet Rauw Alejandro|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8495732/rauw-alejandro-meet-latin-artist-on-the-rise|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 31, 2019|access-date=February 7, 2019}}</ref> His debut studio album, ''[[Afrodisíaco (album)|Afrodisíaco]]'', was released in November 2020. His second studio album, ''Vice Versa'', released in June 2021, featured the lead single "[[Todo de Ti]]". He has won one [[Latin Grammy Awards|Latin Grammy Award]] from four nominations.

==Life and career==
Raúl Alejandro Ocasio Ruiz was born on January 10, 1993, in [[San Juan, Puerto Rico]] and was raised in [[Canóvanas, Puerto Rico|Canóvanas]] and [[Carolina, Puerto Rico|Carolina]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Coca|first=Laura|date=April 14, 2020|title=40 cosas sobre Rauw Alejandro, por Rauw Alejandro|url=https://los40.com/los40/2020/02/19/los40urban/1582114461_610396.html|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=Los 40|language=es}}</ref> His father, guitarist Raúl Ocasio, and his mother, [[backing vocalist]] María Nelly Ruiz, introduced him to some of his musical influences like [[Elvis Presley]], [[Michael Jackson]], and [[Chris Brown]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Del Toro|first=Jackeline|title=Rauw Alejandro se lanza a la conquista "de las nenas" con su música|url=https://www.primerahora.com/horizonte/entretenimiento/musica/nota/rauwalejandroselanzaalaconquistadelasnenasconsumusica-1200938/|access-date=February 7, 2019|work=Primera Hora|date=January 19, 2017|language=es}}</ref> During many years, Alejandro and his father lived in the United States, mainly [[Miami]] and New York City, where he got inspiration from the [[R&B]] and [[dancehall]] genres.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rauw Alejandro junto a Nicky Jam son la nueva sensación del verano|url=https://www.pronto.com.ar/articulo/espectaculos/rauw-alejandro-junto-nicky-jam-son-nueva-sensacion-verano/20190125120025312600.html|work=Pronto|date=January 25, 2019|access-date=February 7, 2019|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Rivera|first=Jose|date=January 25, 2019|title=Entrevista Rauw Alejandro: "Siempre trato de mantener mi esencia en lo que hago"|url=https://umomag.com/revista/entrevistas/rauw-alejandro-siempre-trato-de-mantener-mi-esencia-en-lo-que-hago/|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=UMOMAG™|language=es}}</ref> Alejandro and fellow Puerto Rican rapper [[Anuel AA]] are childhood friends; they both went to the same school and had classes together with Federico A Benitez.<ref>{{Cite web|date=November 18, 2020|title=Interview: Rauw Alejandro Talks Debut Album 'Afrodisíaco'|url=https://www.idolator.com/7916145/interview-rauw-alejandro-talks-debut-album-afrodisiaco|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=idolator}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite magazine|last=Cobo|first=Leila|date=November 12, 2020|title=20 Questions With Rauw Alejandro: On Braids, Michael Jackson, His Mom & Debut Album 'Afrodisiaco'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/9483183/20-questions-with-rauw-alejandro|access-date=December 4, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

As a child, Alejandro competed in school [[talent shows]] because he has a passion for dancing.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Villa|first=Lucas|date=November 19, 2020|title=How Rauw Alejandro Blends R&B And Reggaetón As An Afrodisíaco|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3171868/rauw-alejandro-interview-afrodisiaco-latin-grammys/|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=MTV News|language=en}}</ref> After graduating high school, he enrolled at [[University of Puerto Rico]]. From the age of six until he was twenty, he also played [[association football]], but later quit because he "could not perform as he expected to" and suffered an injury at age 20. He moved to [[Orlando, Florida]] to attempt to get scouted to play in the [[Premier Development League|Premier Development League (PDL)]], but eventually was unsuccessful.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lopez|first=Julyssa|date=March 12, 2019|title=Rauw Alejandro is the Rising Artist Bringing Dance Showmanship to Urbano|url=https://remezcla.com/features/music/rauw-alejandro-profile-urbano/|access-date=December 3, 2020|website=Remezcla}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Alarcon|first=Jesus Triviño|date=April 19, 2019|title=Rauw Alejandro Brings Dance to Latin Urban|url=https://tidal.com/magazine/article/rauw-alejandro-interview-2/1-54908|access-date=December 4, 2020|website=[[Tidal (service)|Tidal Magazine]]}}</ref><ref name=":1" />

After quitting football, he was left in a light depression, so to improve his mood he decided to switch to a musical career and began to publish songs through [[SoundCloud]] in 2014.<ref name="Vocero">{{cite web|last=Rodríguez|first=Yomaris|title=Rauw se abre paso|url=https://www.elvocero.com/rauw-se-abre-paso/article_246f9548-808d-11e8-95ce-83e3f38c0470.html|access-date=February 7, 2019|work=[[El Vocero]]|date=July 6, 2018|language=es}}</ref> In November 2016, he released his debut mixtape titled ''Punto de Equilibrio''.<ref name="Vocero" /> In January 2017, he signed a music deal with Duars Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rauw Alejandro lanza vídeo de su nuevo sencillo|url=https://www.primerahora.com/entretenimiento/musica/nota/rauwalejandrolanzavideodesunuevosencillo-1313054/|access-date=February 7, 2019|work=Primera Hora|date=November 21, 2018|language=es}}</ref> In 2018, he was chosen by [[Sony Music Latin]] to be part of "Los Próximos", a musical project in which the label sought to enlist new musical talents.<ref>{{cite web|last=Escribano Carrasquillo|first=Rosa|title=Cepa de exponentes urbanos que promete|url=https://www.primerahora.com/entretenimiento/musica/nota/cepadeexponentesurbanosquepromete-1298284/|access-date=February 7, 2019|work=Primera Hora|date=August 21, 2018|language=es}}</ref> The musical project helped him gain fame, and as a result other notable artists noticed him and he was featured in multiple collaborations like [[Kevin Roldán]] and [[Khea]]'s "Pa' Tu Casa",<ref>{{cite web|date=October 17, 2018|title=Colaboración de Khea con Kevin Roldán|url=https://www.cmtv.com.ar/noticias/noticia_completa.php?bnid=1657&nid=21986&artista=CMTV&titulo=Colaboracion_de_Khea_con_Kevin_Roldan|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190210152633/https://www.cmtv.com.ar/noticias/noticia_completa.php?bnid=1657&nid=21986&artista=CMTV&titulo=Colaboracion_de_Khea_con_Kevin_Roldan|archive-date=February 10, 2019|access-date=February 10, 2019|work=CMTV Argentina|language=es}}</ref> and "Luz Apaga" by [[Ozuna (singer)|Ozuna]], [[Lunay (singer)|Lunay]] and [[Lyanno]] that year.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ozuna se une a nuevos exponentes del género en sencillo musical|url=https://www.metro.pr/pr/entretenimiento/2018/11/19/ozuna-se-une-a-nuevos-exponentes-del-genero-en-sencillo-musical.html|access-date=February 10, 2019|work=Diario Metro de Puerto Rico|date=November 19, 2018|language=es}}</ref> In December 2017, he released his first single as a lead artist, titled "Toda" with Alex Rose. It peaked at the 29th spot on the ''Billboard'' Hot Latin Songs charts in November 2018. A remix of "Toda" featuring [[Cazzu]], [[Lenny Tavárez]], and [[Lyanno]] was released in May 2018. The song proved successful, with the music video having over 1 billion views on [[YouTube]] as of November 2020. In January 2019, he released his single "Que le dé" with [[Nicky Jam]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Rauw Alejandro lanza "Que le dé" con la bendición de Nicky Jam|url=http://www.billboard.com.ar/noticia/6716/rauw-alejandro-lanza-que-le-d-con-la-bendicin-de-nicky-jam|work=[[Billboard Argentina]]|date=January 30, 2019|access-date=February 14, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190214233440/http://www.billboard.com.ar/noticia/6716/rauw-alejandro-lanza-que-le-d-con-la-bendicin-de-nicky-jam|archive-date=February 14, 2019|language=es}}</ref> In December 2019, his song "Fantasias" with [[Farruko]] peaked at the number 12 spot on the US Latin chart, which is also the highest position his songs have reached on the chart, along with "Tattoo". Both songs stayed for 20 weeks on the chart, the highest duration for one of Alejandro's songs to date and won the [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Urban Fusion/Performance]] in 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Flores |first1=Griselda |last2=Cobo |first2=Leila |title=Camilo Is Top Winner, Cuban Anthem ‘Patria y Vida’ Wins Song of the Year at 2021 Latin Grammys: Winners List |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/latin/2021-latin-grammys-winners-list-1234999583/ |website=Billboard |access-date=19 November 2021 |date=19 November 2021}}</ref>

Alejandro's debut album, ''[[Afrodisíaco (album)|Afrodisíaco]]'', was released on November 13, 2020. It was preceded by the release of the songs and music videos, "Enchule", the "Elegí" remix, and "Reloj", with [[Anuel AA]].<ref name="Reloj">{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/10/rauw-alejandro-anuel-aa-reloj-video|title=Rauw Alejandro Enlists Anuel AA for New Video "Reloj"|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=C. Mahadevan|first=Tara|date=October 23, 2020|accessdate=February 6, 2021}}</ref>

On May 20, 2021 he released "Todo de ti", that peaked at the number 2 spot in Top Songs Global on [[Spotify]]. On June 25, 2021 he released his album, ''Vice Versa'' through [[Sony Music Latin]]. ''Afrodisíaco'' earned him a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Música Urbana Album|Best Música Urbana Album]] at the [[64th Annual Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/2022-grammys-complete-winners-nominees-nominations-list|title=2022 GRAMMYs Awards Show: Complete Nominations List|website=Grammys.com|date=November 23, 2021|accessdate=November 23, 2021}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Rauw Alejandro's music style includes [[Latin R&B]], [[reggaetón]], rhythmic pop, and ballads. ''Billboard'' commented that it has helped "set him apart from a growing class of pop-leaning reggaetoneros".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/9630115/rauw-alejandro-latin-star-billboard-2021-interview/|title=Rauw Alejandro Was Determined To Break Out, But Never Expected 'Panties and Bras Thrown Onstage'|work=Billboard}}</ref>
