'''Sophie Frances Cooke''' (born 27 June 1993), known [[:wikt:mononym|mononymously]] as '''Frances''', is a British singer and songwriter from [[Newbury, Berkshire|Newbury]], Berkshire, England.

==Early life==
Born in [[Oxford]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/1edb0c81-d487-4002-8519-07cc01c0fc96|title=Frances – Songs, Playlists, Videos and Tours|publisher=BBC Music|accessdate=8 January 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.genesreunited.co.uk/search/results?sourcecategory=life%20events%20%28bmds%29&collection=births%20utf0026%20baptisms&datasetname=england%20utf0026%20wales%20births%201837-2006&firstname=sophie&lastname=cooke&birthyear=1993&birthyear_offset=0&keyword=frances|title=Sophie Cooke – England & Wales Births 1837–2006|publisher=Genes Reunited|accessdate=8 January 2016}}</ref> she grew up in Newbury and attended [[St Gabriel's School]] in nearby [[Sandleford]].<ref name="nt1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.newburytoday.co.uk/news/news/16646/Newbury-singer-songwriter-in-running-for.html|title=Newbury singer songwriter in running for BBC music award|date=4 December 2015|publisher=Newbury Today}}</ref> She later studied music at the [[Liverpool Institute for Performing Arts]].<ref name="nt1"/>

==Career==
In August 2014, she was the featured artist for the track "Fire May Save You" for the French music label [[Kitsuné]].<ref name="noisey1">{{Cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/check-kitsuns-new-additionfrances-and-her-debut-fire-may-save-you|date=27 August 2014|title=Check Kitsuné's New Addition—Frances and her Debut "Fire May Save You"|publisher=Noisey}}</ref> The single was released as part of an [[extended play|EP]], which contained remixes of the same track.<ref name="noisey2">{{Cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/blog/frances-interview-2015|title=Frances: From Country Life to Bright Lights|date=13 October 2015|publisher=Noisey}}</ref> In July 2015, she released the EP ''Grow'' for [[Communion Music|Communion Records]].<ref name="noisey2"/> In October 2015, she released the EP ''Let It Out''.<ref name="clash1">{{Cite news|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/listen-frances-shares-new-single-let-it-out|title=Listen: Frances Shares New Single 'Let It Out'|work=Clash|date=2 September 2015}}</ref>

In November 2015, she was shortlisted for the [[2016 BRIT Awards]]: Critics' Choice Award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/nov/24/izzy-bizu-jack-garratt-frances-brits-critics-choice-shortlist|title=Izzy Bizu, Jack Garratt and Frances: meet the Brits critics' choice shortlist|work=The Guardian|date=24 November 2015}}</ref> In December 2015, she was nominated for the [[Sound of...#Sound of 2016|BBC Sound of 2016]].<ref name="telegraph1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/artists/who-bbc-sound-of-2016/|title=Who will be the Sound of 2016? |work=The Telegraph|date=7 January 2016}}</ref>

In 2016, she portrayed Katter the Butterfly singing "[[In My Life]]" in ''[[Beat Bugs]]'' episode 10b.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-blogs/the-beatles-tastic-beat-bugs-might-be-the-coolest-kids-tv-show-ever-769397 |title = The Beatles-tastic 'Beat Bugs' Might be the Coolest Kids TV Show Ever|date = 28 July 2016}}</ref>
