'''Brian Mark Johnson''' (born March 17, 1978) is an American [[Contemporary worship music|contemporary worship]] musician and [[worship pastor]]. He is the president and co-founder of [[Bethel Music]],<ref name="Overcoming Anxiety">{{cite web |url= https://bethelmusic.com/blog/overcoming-anxiety/ |title= Overcoming Anxiety - Bethel Music |last= Johnson |first= Brian |website= [[Bethel Music]] |access-date= March 7, 2017}}</ref> as well as a member of its Artist Collective and is one-half of the husband-and-wife worship duo, '''Brian & Jenn Johnson'''.<ref name="Brian AllMusic">{{cite web |url= http://www.allmusic.com/artist/brian-johnson-mn0002037327 |title= Brian Johnson {{!}} Album Discography {{!}} AllMusic |access-date= February 26, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Brian ibethel.org">{{cite web |url= http://www.ibethel.org/leadership/brian-johnson/ |title= Brian Johnson |website= ibethel.org |publisher= [[Bethel Church (Redding, California)|Bethel Church]] |access-date= February 26, 2017}}</ref> He is also a senior worship pastor at [[Bethel Church (Redding, California)|Bethel Church]] in [[Redding, California|Redding]], [[California]], and a Senior Overseer of WorshipU,<ref name="Brian ibethel.org" /> an online school of worship under Bethel Music which he co-founded with Jenn Johnson.<ref name="Brian Bethel Music">{{cite web |url= https://bethelmusic.com/artists/brian-johnson/ |title= Brian Johnson - Bethel Music |website= [[Bethel Music]] |access-date= February 27, 2017}}</ref>

As a solo musician, Johnson has released one album, ''Love Came Down - Live Acoustic Worship in the Studio'' (2010). As a part of Brian & Jenn Johnson, three live albums have been released: ''[[Undone (Brian & Jenn Johnson album)|Undone]]'' (2001), ''[[We Believe (album)|We Believe]]'' (2006), the duo's first release to chart on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Christian Albums]] chart, and ''[[Where You Go I Go]]'' (2008). The duo has also released one studio album, ''[[After All These Years (Brian & Jenn Johnson album)|After All These Years]]'' (2017), which charted on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. As a founding member of [[Bethel Music]], Johnson has been featured on most Bethel Music releases. As a songwriter, Johnson has co-written [[CCLI]] Top 100 Chart ranking songs such as "Love Came Down", "[[No Longer Slaves]]", "Forever", and "One Thing Remains".<ref name="Brian Bethel Music" /><ref name="AATY press release">{{cite press release |url= http://www.themcollective.com/press-archive/2017/1/13/bethel-musics-brian-jenn-johnson-to-unveil-after-all-these-years-first-solo-album-together-in-more-than-a-decade |title= Bethel Music's Brian & Jenn Johnson To Unveil After All These Years, First Solo Album Together in More Than A Decade – the media collective |last1= Kelm |first1= Velvet |last2= Huppenthal |first2= Brooke |date= January 13, 2017 |website= The Media Collective |location= Nashville, Tennessee |access-date= February 27, 2017 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170204084933/http://www.themcollective.com/press-archive/2017/1/13/bethel-musics-brian-jenn-johnson-to-unveil-after-all-these-years-first-solo-album-together-in-more-than-a-decade |archive-date= February 4, 2017 |url-status= dead }}</ref> Johnson has also worked as a producer of multiple Bethel Music, Jesus Culture, and Brian & Jenn Johnson releases, alongside various producers such as [[Jeremy Edwardson]] and Ian McIntosh.

==Early life==
Johnson was born on March 17, 1978 to [[Bill Johnson (pastor)|Bill Johnson]] and Brenda "Beni" Johnson in [[Redding, California|Redding]], [[California]]. In the year of his birth, the Johnson family moved to [[Weaverville, California]], after Bethel Church, under the leadership of his grandfather, Earl Johnson, sent his father to pastor Mountain Chapel.<ref name="Bill Johnson History">{{cite news |url= http://www.charismanews.com/opinion/watchman-on-the-wall/59517-bill-johnson-burning-to-see-the-nations-on-fire-for-jesus |title= The Truth About Bethel's Bill Johnson – Charisma News |last= LeClaire |first= Jennifer |date= August 26, 2017 |website= Charisma News |publisher= Charisma Media |access-date= February 27, 2017}}</ref> He completed his high school education at [[Trinity High School (Weaverville, California)|Trinity High School]] in Weaverville. After seventeen years, the Johnsons moved back to Redding in 1996 when Bill and Beni Johnson assumed leadership of Bethel Church.<ref name="Bill Johnson History" />

Johnson has two siblings: an older brother, Eric Johnson, who is a pastor at Bethel Church, and a younger sister Leah Valenzuela (née Johnson), who is also a member of the Bethel Music collective and one of the worship leaders at Bethel Church.

===2000s===
In 2000, Johnson and his wife, Jenn Johnson, were appointed as senior worship pastors at Bethel Church. As Brian & Jenn Johnson, they released their debut album, ''[[Undone (Brian & Jenn Johnson album)|Undone]]'', in 2001, featuring Johnson's younger sister Leah Valenzuela.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://bethelmusic.com/albums/undone/ |title= Undone - Bethel Music |website= [[Bethel Music]] |access-date= February 27, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/products/Brian__Jenn_Johnson_with_Leah_Valenzuela/Undone/127999/ |title= Review: Undone - Brian & Jenn Johnson with Leah Valenzuela {{!}} Cross Rhythms |last= Coupe |first= Chris |date= January 30, 2013 |website= [[Cross Rhythms]] |access-date= March 23, 2017}}</ref> After a five-year hiatus, Brian & Jenn Johnson released their second live album, ''[[We Believe (album)|We Believe]]'' in 2006, which was co-produced by Johnson alongside [[Jeremy Edwardson]] and Steven Tracy. ''We Believe'' also charted at No. 48 on the ''Billboard'' Christian Albums chart, making the album the first by the duo to register on the chart.<ref name="B and J Billboard Search">{{cite web |url= http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?page=1&f[0]=ts_chart_artistname:Brian_%26_Jenn_Johnson&f[1]=ss_bb_type:chart_item&type=2&artist=Brian_%26_Jenn_Johnson |title= Chart Search {{!}} Billboard |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date= April 1, 2017 }}{{Dead link|date=June 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> On September 30, 2008, ''[[Where You Go I Go]]'', the duo's third album, was released via the label Ion Records.

===2010s===
In 2010, Bethel Music released ''[[Here Is Love]]'', its debut album on October 12, via its own label, Bethel Music alongside [[Integrity Media|Integrity Music]]. Johnson featured on four tracks: "My Soul Sings", "What Does It Sound Like", "Worthy Is the Lamb" and "Here Is Love", which he sang alongside his wife, Jenn Johnson. Johnson made an appearance on Dave Fitzgerald's debut album, ''Hope of Heaven: Live at Bethel Church'',<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.bethel.tv/speakers/show/57/david-fitzgerald |title= David Fitzgerald {{!}} Bethel.TV |website= Bethel.TV |access-date= April 13, 2017}}</ref> which was released on October 26,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.christiantoday.com/article/worship.leader.dave.fitzgerald.makes.debut.with.hope.of.heaven/26608.htm |title= Worship leader Dave Fitzgerald makes debut with Hope of Heaven {{!}} Christian News on Christian Today |date= August 31, 2010 |website= Christian Today |access-date= March 9, 2017}}</ref> featuring on the song "Shine".<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/products/Dave_Fitzgerald/Hope_Of_Heaven_Live_At_Bethel_Church/101959/ |title= Review: Hope Of Heaven: Live At Bethel Church - Dave Fitzgerald {{!}} Cross Rhythms |last= Luff |first= Stephen |date= April 29, 2011 |website= [[Cross Rhythms]] |access-date= March 9, 2017}}</ref> Soon after Fitzgerald's release, ''Love Came Down - Live Acoustic Worship in the Studio'', Brian Johnson's debut live album, was released by record labels Bethel and Kingsway Music on November 9. ''Love Came Down'' was produced by [[Jeremy Edwardson]],<ref name="Love Came Down AllMusic">{{cite web |url= http://www.allmusic.com/album/love-came-down-live-acoustic-worship-in-the-studio-mw0002034888/credits |title= Love Came Down: Live Acoustic Worship In The Studio - Brian Johnson {{!}} Credits {{!}} AllMusic  |website= [[AllMusic]] |publisher= [[All Media Network]] |access-date= February 13, 2017}}</ref> and recorded at The Soundhouse,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://bethelmusic.com/store/product/love-came-down-live-acoustic/ |title= Love Came Down (Live Acoustic) - Bethel Music Store |website= [[Bethel Music]] |access-date= March 3, 2017 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170214180942/https://bethelmusic.com/store/product/love-came-down-live-acoustic/ |archive-date= February 14, 2017 |url-status= dead }}</ref> a studio owned by Jeremy Edwardson in Redding, California.

In 2011, Brian Johnson featured on five tracks including "You Are Good", "One Thing Remains" and "What Would I Have Done" alongside Jenn Johnson on Bethel Music's second live album, ''[[Be Lifted High]]'', the first album by Bethel to register on ''Billboard'''s Christian Albums Chart, peaking at No. 7.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.billboard.com/artist/277740/Various+Artists/chart?f=310 |title= Various Artists – Chart history |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |version= Christian Albums |access-date= March 7, 2017 |archive-date= April 20, 2017 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170420163528/http://www.billboard.com/artist/277740/Various+Artists/chart?f=310 |url-status= dead }}</ref> 2012 saw Bethel Music release two albums that Brian Johnson featured in and co-produced: ''[[The Loft Sessions]]'' and ''[[For the Sake of the World]]''. The album peaked at No. 51 on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart for three weeks, concurrently ranking at No. 2 on the Christian Albums chart.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?page=64&f[0]=ts_chart_artistname:Bethel_Music&f[1]=ss_bb_type:chart_item&type=2&artist=Bethel_Music |title= Chart Search {{!}} Billboard |website= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date= April 1, 2017 }}{{Dead link|date=June 2021 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> The song "Love Came Down", co-written by Brian Johnson alongside Jeremy Riddle, Ian McIntosh and [[Jeremy Edwardson]], was featured on the [[American Idol (season 12)|twelfth season]] of ''[[American Idol]]'' as part of the "Songs I Wish I'd Written" night,<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.praisecharts.com/blog/bethel-music-releases-first-ever-solo-album/ |title= PraiseCharts {{!}} Bethel Music Releases First Ever Solo Album |date= August 9, 2013 |website= PraiseCharts |publisher= PraiseCharts Publishing, Inc. |access-date= April 12, 2017 |quote= A Bethel song, "Love Came Down," written by Johnson and Riddle, was even featured on American Idol last season as part of the "Songs I Wish I'd Written" night.}}</ref> with the performance of [[Kari Jobe]]'s rendition as on ''[[Where I Find You]]'' done by the contestant Angie Miller, a top 3 finalist.

On January 13, 2017, Brian & Jenn Johnson announced the release of ''[[After All These Years (Brian & Jenn Johnson album)|After All These Years]]'', the duo's first album containing new songs in over a decade since the live LP release ''We Believe'' in 2006, was slated for January 27.<ref name="AATY press release" /> ''After All These Years'' made its debut on ''Billboard'' [[Christian Albums]] chart at No. 1 with 16,000 equivalent album units sold as of February 8, 2017.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7685262/reba-mcentire-tim-mcgraw-country-christian |title= Country Goes Christian: Reba McEntire No. 1, Tim McGraw & Faith Hill Debut {{!}} Billboard |last= Asker |first= Jim |date= February 8, 2017 |website= Billboard.com |publisher= [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date= April 12, 2017 |quote= Another husband-and-wife team, Brian & Jenn Johnson, bows at No. 1 on Top Christian Albums with After All These Years (16,000 equivalent album units).}}</ref> The album also registered on the [[Billboard 200]] at No. 21 and at No. 32 on the [[Canadian Albums Chart|Canadian Albums]] chart.<ref name="B and J Billboard Search" /> Brian Johnson had an interview with Sharefaith Magazine about the album and his worship ministry.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://www.sharefaith.com/blog/2017/03/bethel-music-interview-brian-johnson/ |title= Bethel Music Interview: Brian Johnson - After All These Years |author= Sharefaith Team |date= March 20, 2017 |website= Sharefaith Magazine |access-date= April 12, 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Brian Johnson married Jennifer Louise Johnson (née Rock) in 2000. The couple has two daughters, Haley and Téa, as well as one biological son, Braden, and one adopted son, Ryder, who was adopted in mid-2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gospelmusic.org/bethel-musics-brian-jenn-johnson-adopt-a-baby-boy/|title=Bethel Music's Brian & Jenn Johnson Adopt a Baby Boy – The Gospel Music Association|date=June 9, 2017|website=Gospel Music Association|access-date=July 7, 2017}}</ref> The family resides on a farm in Redding, California.<ref name="Brian ibethel.org" /><ref name="Brian Bethel Music" />
