'''{{lang|la|Status quo|italic=Status quo}}''' is a [[Latin]] phrase meaning the existing [[state of affairs (sociology)|state of affairs]], particularly with regard to social, political, religious or military issues.<ref>{{cite Merriam-Webster|status quo}}</ref> In the [[Sociology|sociological]] sense, the ''status quo'' refers to the current state of social structure and/or values.<ref name=gloss>{{cite web|author1=Dr. C. Michael Botterweck|title=Glossary for Sociology 100|url=http://academics.triton.edu/faculty/mbotterweck/glossary.html|website=academics.triton.edu|access-date=8 February 2015}}</ref> With regard to policy debate, it means how conditions are, contrasted with a possible change. For example: "The countries are now trying to maintain the ''status quo'' with regard to their nuclear arsenals." To maintain the ''status quo'' is to keep things the way they presently are.

The related phrase ''[[wikt:status quo ante|status quo ante]]'', literally "the status before", refers to the state of affairs that existed previously.<ref>{{cite Merriam-Webster|status quo ante}}</ref>

== Political usage ==

Via [[social movement|social movements]] the status quo might be overhauled. These seek to alleviate or prevent a particular issue and often to shape social feeling and cultural expression of a [[society]] or [[nation]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.featherpicking.com/The%20Social%20Climate.htm|title=The Social Climate|access-date=2009-03-11|work=The Optimal Environment: Part Four|publisher=www.featherpicking.com|last=Clark|first=Pamela|year=2000}}</ref> The status quo is at least in part rejected by their protagonists &ndash; progressives &ndash; leading the movement.<ref name=vocab>{{cite web|title=Status Quo - Dictionary Definition|url=http://www.vocabulary.com/dictionary/status%20quo|website=vocabulary.com|publisher=vocabulary.com|access-date=8 February 2015}}</ref>

Advocating to improve the status quo is a persuasive [[rhetorical device]]. This is sometimes critiqued as a policy of deliberate ambiguity as not formalizing or defining the adverse situation. 

In democratic meetings, a [[casting vote]] will often be subject to a custom that is cast per the status quo, the heart of [[Speaker Denison's rule]]. [[Clark Kerr]] reportedly said: "The status quo is the only [[Problem solving|solution]] that cannot be [[veto]]ed".<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=l-w6CwAAQBAJ&q=clark+kerr+The+status+quo+is+the+only+solution+that+cannot+be+vetoe&pg=PA182|title=Momentum: The Responsibility Paradigm and Virtuous Cycles of Change in Colleges and Universities|last=Seymour|first=Daniel|date=2015-12-07|publisher=Rowman & Littlefield|isbn=9781475821048|language=en}}</ref>

[[Karl Marx]] viewed organized religion as a means for the [[bourgeoisie]] to keep the [[proletariat]] content with an unequal status quo.<ref name = "Boundless">Boundless. "Religion and Social Control." Boundless Sociology. Boundless, 27 Jun. 2014. Retrieved 08 Feb. 2015 [https://www.boundless.com/sociology/textbooks/boundless-sociology-textbook/religion-14/the-conflict-perspective-107/religion-and-social-control-596-2095/]</ref>
