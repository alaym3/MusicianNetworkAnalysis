'''Ulrika Spacek''' are an English alternative rock band, formed in 2014 and currently composed of singer-guitarist Rhys Edwards, guitarist Rhys Williams, guitarist-keyboardist Joseph Stone, bassist Syd Kemp and drummer Callum Brown.

Edwards, Stone and White were previously members of Reading band Tripwires, with Williams having previously toured with the band as an additional guitarist<ref>{{cite tweet|user=Tripwires|author=Tripwires|number=515875888811225088|date=27 September 2014|title=Our friend and sometimes live gtrist viscous liquid puts out his cassette EP 'songs for jealous lovers' today. Check it out @onenoteforever}}</ref> and releasing material under the Viscous Liquid moniker.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fat-cat.co.uk/publishing/artist/viscous-liquid|title=FATCAT RECORDS - PUBLISHING|website=Fat-cat.co.uk|accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref>

==History==
===Previous projects, 2002–2014===
School friends Rhys Edwards, Joseph Stone and Ben White have worked with each other in bands since 2002. The Enigma Project were formed in Reading, Berkshire in 2002 by Edwards, Sam Pilsbury and George Simpson with White joining soon after. Simpson was replaced in 2005 by Joseph Stone.<ref>[http://strangeglue.com/interviews/strange-glue-showcase-tripwires-interview-and-music/10002637 ]{{dead link|date=June 2020}}</ref> They signed to Automator Records and released their debut single "Astronaut/Microcosm" in July 2005, plus the follow up EP "Scenes of the 21st Century".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/berkshire/content/articles/2005/06/01/localband_enigmaproject_feature.shtml |title=Berkshire - Entertainment - The Enigma Project |publisher=[[BBC]] |date=2014-10-28 |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> 
 
In 2007, The Enigma Project changed their name to Tripwires and released the singles "Kings & Queens" and "Just So You Know" the following year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kudosrecords.co.uk/release/jsk009trdd/tripwires-just-so-you-know.html |title=Tripwires — Just So You Know &#124; Josaka |website=Kudosrecords.co.uk |date=2008-07-21 |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> The songs were picked up by national radio and championed by the likes of [[Huw Stephens]] and [[Steve Lamacq]]. The same year the band performed on the BBC Introducing stage at [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roomthirteen.com/features/632/Tripwires_Interview.html |title=Reading and Leeds Festival - Tripwires Interview - RoomThirteen - Online Rock Metal Alternative Music Magazine - Reviews Interviews News Tours |website=RoomThirteen.com |date= |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref><ref>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/introducing/artists/tripwires/] {{dead link|date=June 2020}}</ref> The band began work on their debut album 'Spacehopper' in August 2010, and was set for release in April 2011 via Club AC30.<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=http://tinfoilskin.blogspot.co.uk/2011/01/hallelujah.html |title=Tripwires: Hallelujah |website=Tinfoilskin.blogspot.co.uk |date=2011-01-21 |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> However, the album was scrapped and the band parted ways with the label. The band recorded a different version of the album in New York with producer [[Nicolas Vernhes]], and it was eventually released in June 2013 via French Kiss Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.atpfestival.com/artist/tripwires |title=Tripwires - All Tomorrow's Parties |website=Atpfestival.com |date= |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://flowerbooking.com/artists/tripwires/ |title=Flowerbooking |website=Flowerbooking.com |date= |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.frenchkissrecords.com/tripwires/ |title=Tripwires — Frenchkiss Records |website=Frenchkissrecords.com |date= |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> They self released their second album ''Watermelancholia'' via [[Bandcamp]] in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tripwires.bandcamp.com/album/watermelancholia-2 |title=Watermelancholia &#124; Tripwires |website=Tripwires.bandcamp.com |date= |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> The album was recorded in London and Berlin through 2013 and 2014.<ref>[http://tripwires.co.uk/] {{dead link|date=June 2020}}</ref>

===Current lineup and signing to Tough Love Records, 2014–present===
In Spring 2014, Edwards began working on new material in Berlin with Reading school friend and sometime Tripwires live guitarist Rhys Williams. On returning to England, they re-located to [[Homerton]], [[East London]] where they completed the album at a shared house named KEN<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thequietus.com/articles/19657-ulrika-spacek-interview |title=Features &#124; Escape Velocity &#124; Hypnotic Loops: An Interview With Ulrika Spacek |publisher=[[The Quietus]] |date=2016-02-04 |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> with drummer Callum Brown and fellow Tripwires members Joseph Stone and Ben White. The band made their live debut in London in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/events/728410450589557/ |title=Bad Vibrations Present: Doug Tuttle (Mmoss / Trouble In Mind), Ulrika Spacek, Tv Wonder & Truism |website=Facebook.com |date=2015-05-13 |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> Soon after saw the formation of 'Oysterland', a self-curated night that combined the band's earliest live performances with a series of art exhibitions.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/events/1144855358863429/ |title=Oysterland #3: Ulrika Spacek (Live) + JC Flowers (Live) + Mellah (Live) + Video Installation from Zoë Que |website=Facebook.com |date=2015-10-28 |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> They released ''The Album Paranoia'' in February 2016<ref>[http://www.lefserecords.com/news/2016/1/28/ulrika-spacek-the-album-paranoia-out-on-tough-love-lefse-records-feb-5] {{dead link|date=June 2020}}</ref> and embarked on a UK tour the same month.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2016/01/05/ulrika-spacek-enter-a-new-stratosphere-on-beta-male|title=Ulrika Spacek enter a new stratosphere on ‘Beta Male’|website=Diymag.comm|accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> In September 2016, the band released stand-alone single "Everything: All The Time".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1894856/ulrika-spacek-everything-all-the-time/mp3s/ |title=Ulrika Spacek – “Everything: All The Time” |website=Stereogum.com |date=2016-08-22 |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref>

The band released their second album ''Modern English Decoration'' in June 2017, which was preceded by the single "Mimi Pretend".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/ulrika-spacek-announce-new-album-share-new-single-mimi-pretend|title=Ulrika Spacek announce Modern English Decoration, share new single “Mimi Pretend”|website=Thelineofbestfit.com|accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> Initial copies of the album on vinyl came with a bonus 7", which neglected to include a tracklisting. One of the tracks was later confirmed to be a cover version of a song by Parsley Sounds entitled "Ease Yourself And Glide".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/song-of-the-day/ulrika-spacek-ease-yourself-and-glide|title=Ulrika Spacek pay homage to Parsley Sound on “Ease Yourself And Glide”|website=Thelineofbestfit.com|accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref> Commenting on plans for forthcoming releases, frontman Rhys Edwards stated that "We will certainly be putting music out, it won't be a full length though. We see our first two albums as brother and sister records and think it will be nice to leave them both out there for a little while before we think about putting out another record".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.drunkenwerewolf.com/interviews/ulrika-spacek-2017/ |title=Ulrika Spacek Interview, Tough Love Records |website=DrunkenWerewolf.com |date=2017-07-22 |accessdate=2020-06-03}}</ref>
