'''Amandla Stenberg''' (born October 23, 1998) is an American actress and singer. She{{efn|Stenberg uses [[she (pronoun)|she/her]] and [[singular they|they/them]] pronouns. This article uses she/her pronouns for consistency}} was included in ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]''{{'}}s list of Most Influential Teens in both 2015 and 2016,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|title=The 30 Most Influential Teens of 2015|url=http://time.com/4081618/most-influential-teens-2015/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170921001041/http://time.com/4081618/most-influential-teens-2015/|archive-date=September 21, 2017|access-date=September 21, 2017|website=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|title=The 30 Most Influential Teens of 2016|url=http://time.com/4532104/most-influential-teens-2016/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170923205943/http://time.com/4532104/most-influential-teens-2016/|archive-date=September 23, 2017|access-date=September 21, 2017|website=Time}}</ref> and has received several accolades, including a [[Teen Choice Awards|Teen Choice Award]], an [[NAACP Image Awards|NAACP Image Award]], and nominations for four [[Black Reel Awards]] and a [[Critics' Choice Movie Awards|Critics' Choice Award]].

Stenberg made her acting debut with the film ''[[Colombiana]]'' (2011), and had her breakthrough playing [[Hunger Games characters|Rue]] in the film ''[[The Hunger Games (film)|The Hunger Games]]'' (2012), for which she won the [[Teen Choice Awards|Teen Choice Award]] for Best Chemistry. She had the recurring role of Macey Irving in the series [[Sleepy Hollow (TV series)|''Sleepy Hollow'']] (2013–2014), voiced Bia in ''[[Rio 2]]'' (2014) and starred as Halle Foster in the sitcom [[Mr. Robinson (TV series)|''Mr. Robinson'']] (2015). She garnered acclaim for her portrayals of Maddy Whittier in ''[[Everything, Everything (film)|Everything, Everything]]'' (2017) and as Starr Carter in the film ''[[The Hate U Give (film)|The Hate U Give]]'' (2018), the latter of which won her the [[NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actress in a Motion Picture]] and earned her a nomination for the [[Critics' Choice Movie Awards|Critics' Choice Award for Best Young Actor/Actress]]. Stenberg also starred as Ruby in ''[[The Darkest Minds]]'' (2018), Leyna in ''[[Where Hands Touch]]'' (2018) and Julie in the [[Netflix]] miniseries ''[[The Eddy]]'' (2020).

Stenberg has been recognized for her outspoken views, particularly towards her activism for [[LGBT|LGBT youth]], which has received significant media coverage. In 2015, she made her musical debut as part of the folk-rock duo Honeywater. Her single, "Let My Baby Stay" was featured in the film ''Everything, Everything'' (2018).<!-- DO NOT CHANGE all pronouns to "they"/"them"/etc. See Stenberg's statement on this topic, specifically requesting that this NOT be done, at: http://www.teenvogue.com/story/amandla-stenberg-gender-pronouns-they-them & https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2018/lifestyle/amandla-stenberg-looks-a-lot-like-the-future-of-celebrity/ -->

== Career ==

She received a number of awards and nominations, including a [[Black Reel Awards|Black Reel Award]] nomination.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Black Reel Awards (2013)|url=http://www.imdb.com/event/ev0000957/2013/1/|access-date=2020-08-01|website=IMDb}}</ref> In 2013, she was cast in the short film ''Mercy'' playing the daughter of [[Robin Thicke]] and [[Paula Patton]]; Thicke directed the film.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Robin Thicke Directs and Stars in Short Film 'Mercy' (Exclusive)|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/robin-thicke-directs-stars-short-648218|access-date=2020-06-05|website=The Hollywood Reporter|date=October 16, 2013|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200605005129/https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/robin-thicke-directs-stars-short-648218|archive-date=June 5, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Michaels|first=Sean|date=2013-10-17|title=Robin Thicke trades a microphone for a movie camera|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/oct/17/robin-thicke-movie-blurred-lines-mercy|access-date=2020-06-05|issn=0261-3077|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200605005229/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/oct/17/robin-thicke-movie-blurred-lines-mercy|archive-date=June 5, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> Stenberg had a [[recurring role]] on season one of ''[[Sleepy Hollow (TV series)|Sleepy Hollow]]'' from 2013 to 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|title='Sleepy Hollow': 'Hunger Games' Star Makes Her Debut (Exclusive Video)|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/sleepy-hollow-amandla-stenberg-makes-659511|access-date=2020-06-05|website=The Hollywood Reporter|date=November 25, 2013|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200605003319/https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/sleepy-hollow-amandla-stenberg-makes-659511|archive-date=June 5, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>
