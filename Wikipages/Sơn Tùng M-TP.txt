'''Nguyễn Thanh Tùng''' (born 5 July 1994), known professionally as '''Sơn Tùng M-TP''' ({{IPA-vi|s̪əːn˧ tuŋ˨˩ ɛm˧ te˧ pe˧|}}), is a [[Vietnam]]ese singer-songwriter and actor. Born and raised in [[Thái Bình|Thái Bình City]], [[Thái Bình Province]], his family discovered his singing ability when he was two years old. His self-written 2012 and 2013 singles, "Cơn mưa ngang qua" and "Em của ngày hôm qua" launched his career. These were followed by successful singles such as "Chắc ai đó sẽ về", "[[Lạc trôi]]" and "Nơi này có anh". In 2017, Tùng released the compilation album ''[[m-tp M-TP]]'' and published his autobiography, ''Chạm tới giấc mơ.''

Tùng spent four years as part of Văn Production and WePro Entertainment until his record label, [[M-TP Entertainment]], was founded in late 2016. His other works include the M-TP Ambition - Chuyến bay đầu tiên concert tour (2015–2016), a starring role in the 2014 film ''[[Dandelion (2014 film)|Dandelion]]'' (which earned him a [[Golden Kite Prize]] for Young Prominent Actor) and an appearance as a contestant on the television series ''The Remix''. Called a "Prince of [[V-pop]]" for his popularity, Tùng has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Sơn Tùng M-TP|many accolades]] which include a [[MTV Europe Music Award]], a [[Dedication Music Award]], a [[2017 Mnet Asian Music Awards|Mnet Asian Music Award]], seven [[Green Wave (radio show)|''Green Wave'' Awards]] and an inclusion on ''[[Forbes|Forbes Vietnam]]''{{'s}} 2018 [[Forbes 30 Under 30|30 Under 30]] list.

Tùng recalled in his autobiography ''Chạm tới giấc mơ'' that he frequently listened to his grandparents singing [[Quan họ]] (a [[Music of Vietnam|Vietnamese]] [[folk music]] style originating in [[Bắc Ninh]]) as a child. Its signature vocal style eventually affected his way of singing [[Slur (music)|slur]] notes, and Vietnamese folk music in general has inspired his work.<ref name="chamtoigiacmo" /> The singer later grew up listening to artists such as [[Quang Vinh]], whom he idolized, [[Chris Brown]], [[Rihanna]] and [[Justin Bieber]].<ref name="part1" /> Tùng has also been influenced by the Vietnamese underground [[hip hop]] scene and K-pop acts, including [[Big Bang (South Korean band)|Big Bang]], [[Super Junior]] and [[TVXQ]].<ref name="part1" /> After the death of Thái Bình-born composer An Thuyên in 2015, he said that Thuyên was the biggest influence on his artistry, and expressed appreciation when the composer defended him during the controversy surrounding "Chắc ai đó sẽ về" in 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://news.zing.vn/son-tung-m-tp-khoc-nhu-mua-trong-tiec-sinh-nhat-post556109.html |title=Sơn Tùng M-TP khóc như mưa trong tiệc sinh nhật |trans-title=Sơn Tùng M-TP cries on his birthday stage |author=Kim Chi |date=July 6, 2015 |access-date=July 22, 2017 |work=Zing |language=vi }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://congly.vn/giai-tri/nhac/son-tung-m-tp-gui-tam-thu-cho-co-nhac-sy-an-thuyen-106828.html |title=Sơn Tùng M-TP gửi tâm thư cho cố nhạc sỹ An Thuyên |trans-title=Sơn Tùng M-TP writes a personal letter to composer An Thuyên |author=Hà Thu |date=July 9, 2015 |access-date=July 26, 2017 |work=Công Lý |language=vi }}</ref>

==Public image and achievements==
{{See also|List of awards and nominations received by Sơn Tùng M-TP}}
[[File:Sơn Tùng M-TP hát Chín bậc tình yêu tại đêm nhạc An Thuyên 27s.jpg|alt=Tùng performing on stage, with blonde hair and a white suit|thumb|upright|Tùng performing at An Thuyên's tribute concert in July 2016]]
Tùng, known as one of the most successful Vietnamese artists, has been called the "Prince of [[V-pop]]".<ref name="prince"/><ref name="bingomusic">{{cite web |url=https://e.vnexpress.net/news/travel-life/culture-arts/v-pop-star-signs-deal-with-top-korean-music-platform-praised-across-asia-3751557.html |title=V-pop star signs deal with top Korean music platform, praised across Asia |author=Van An |date=May 18, 2018 |access-date=May 20, 2018 |work=VnExpress }}</ref> ''[[Forbes|Forbes Vietnam]]'' and ''Thể thao & Văn hóa'' described the singer "a phenomenon of Vietnamese Pop".<ref name="forbes"/><ref name="cungtuy"/> According to ''Thể thao & Văn hóa''{{'s}} Cung Ly, Tùng's knack for "smoothly" blending originality with mainstream K-pop was the reason for his success.<ref name="cungtuy"/> However, public reception during his early career was polarized.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://thethaovanhoa.vn/van-hoa-giai-tri/son-tung-mtp-giua-tan-cung-yeu-ghet-n20160113142633668.htm |title=Sơn Tùng M-TP: Giữa tận cùng Yêu & Ghét |trans-title=Sơn Tùng M-TP: A thin line between love and hate |author=Hoàng Lê |date=January 15, 2016 |access-date=September 10, 2017 |work=Thể thao & Văn hóa |language=vi }}</ref> Due to Tùng's past copyright issues, his work was frequently compared to other foreign songs.<ref name="copycat" /><ref name="forbes" /> ''Forbes Vietnam''{{'s}} contributor Phan T. Trang wrote that although the public has been more "generous and open" towards the singer since the releases of "Lạc trôi" and "Nơi này có anh", the industry still doubts his creativity.<ref name="forbes" />

In 2015, the WeChoice Awards named Tùng one of its five Inspiration Ambassadors (the awards show's top honor).<ref name="Home page"/> It also voted him one of the ten most influential people in Vietnam in 2014 and 2017, and one of 2015's five most influential artists.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://wechoice.vn/ |title=Home page |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160911152445/http://wechoice.vn/ |archive-date=September 11, 2016 |access-date=September 10, 2017 |publisher=2015 WeChoice Awards |language=vi }}</ref><ref name="wechoice.vn">{{cite web |url=http://wechoice.vn/ |title=Home page |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170423045438/http://wechoice.vn/ |archive-date=April 23, 2017 |access-date=September 10, 2017 |publisher=2016 WeChoice Awards |language=vi }}</ref><ref name="wechoice2018"/> In May 2016, [[President of the United States|U.S. President]] [[Barack Obama]] mentioned Tùng during a speech examining the impact of [[social media]] on young Vietnamese. The speech was part of Obama's town-hall meeting with members of the Young Southeast Asian Leaders Initiative in Ho Chi Minh City.<ref>{{cite speech |last=Obama |first=Barack |author-link=Barack Obama |title=Remarks by President Obama at YSEALI Town Hall |event=Young Southeast Asian Leaders Initiative |date=May 25, 2016 |location=Ho Chi Minh City |publisher=U.S. Embassy & Consulate in Vietnam |url=https://vn.usembassy.gov/20160525obama-yseali-town-hall/ |access-date=May 2, 2018 |language=en |quote="You're streaming the latest Son Tung MTP hit."}}</ref> In 2018, ''Forbes Vietnam'' included Tùng in its annual [[Forbes 30 Under 30|30 Under 30]] list.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://english.vov.vn/economy/forbes-vietnam-announces-30-under-30-list-367942.vov |title=Forbes Vietnam announces 30 Under 30 list |author=''VOV'' staff(s) |date=January 2, 2018 |access-date=March 4, 2018 |work=[[Voice of Vietnam|VOV]] }}</ref> He has also been included on the [[Green Wave (radio show)|''Green Wave'' Awards]]' top-five Favorite Singers (Top Hit board) list three consecutive times since 2015.{{efn|The 18th ''Green Wave'' Awards ceremony was held on 18 December 2015, but the 19th edition was held on 3 January 2017.<ref name="greenwave2015"/><ref name="greenwave2017"/>}}<ref name="greenwave2015"/><ref name="greenwave2017">{{cite web |url=http://thethaovanhoa.vn/van-hoa-giai-tri/lan-song-xanh-2016-son-tung-mtp-noi-bat-hari-won-trang-tay-n20170104064825068.htm |title=Làn sóng xanh 2016: Sơn Tùng M-TP nổi bật, Hari Won trắng tay |trans-title=2016 Green Wave Awards: Sơn Tùng M-TP stands out, Hari Won gets snubbed |author=N.M |date=January 4, 2017 |access-date=September 10, 2017 |work=Thể thao & Văn hóa |language=vi }}</ref><ref name="greenwave2018"/>

Tùng has been described as a fashion icon, despite his fashion and music being repeatedly compared to South Korean musician [[G-Dragon]] and members of Big Bang.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://english.thesaigontimes.vn/52122/Son-Tung-M-TP-listed-among-top-Asian-fashion-icons.html |title=Son Tung M-TP listed among top Asian fashion icons |author=''The Saigon Times'' staff(s) |date=February 3, 2017 |work=[[Saigon Times|The Saigon Times]]|access-date=April 24, 2018}}</ref><ref name="chichi" /> In 2017, Vietnamese [[Elle (magazine)|''Elle'']] gave him a Style Award for Most Stylish Male Singer.<ref name="esa2017">{{cite web|url=http://www.elle.vn/thoi-trang/son-tung-mtp-thang-giai-nam-ca-si-phong-cach-nhat-cua-nam|title=Sơn Tùng MTP – Nam ca sĩ phong cách nhất tại ELLE Style Awards 2017|author=''Elle Vietnam'' staff(s)|date=June 28, 2017|work=Elle Vietnam|language=vi|trans-title=Sơn Tùng M-TP – Most Stylish Male Singer at 2017 ''Elle'' Style Awards|access-date=September 10, 2017}}</ref> His fan base, ''Sky'', has a large following in the country.<ref name="150million"/> Tùng has endorsed a number of brands and companies, including Oppo,<ref name="f3">{{cite web |url=https://sohoa.vnexpress.net/photo/san-pham/oppo-them-ban-f3-co-chu-ky-son-tung-m-tp-3606195.html |title=Oppo thêm bản F3 có chữ ký Sơn Tùng M-TP |author=Huy Đức |date=July 8, 2017 |work=VnExpress |language=vi |trans-title=Oppo releases a new edition of F3 with Sơn Tùng M-TP's signature |access-date=April 24, 2018}}</ref> [[Yamaha Motor Company|Yamaha]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://antt.vn/gia-xe-may-tay-ga-dong-xe-duoc-ca-si-son-tung-m-tp-lam-dai-su-gia-bao-nhieu-7894.htm |title=Giá xe máy tay ga: Dòng xe được ca sĩ Sơn Tùng M-TP làm đại sứ giá bao nhiêu?
