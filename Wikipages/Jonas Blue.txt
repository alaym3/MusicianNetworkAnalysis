'''Guy James Robin'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skiddle.com/news/all/Jonas-Blue-Interview-Life-in-the-fast-lane/30862/|title=Jonas Blue Interview: Life in the fast lane|website=Skiddle.com}}</ref> (born 2 August 1989), known professionally as '''Jonas Blue''', is an English [[Disc jockey|DJ]], record producer and songwriter based in London.<ref name="techden">{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicradar.com/news/tech/fast-car-hitmaker-jonas-blue-takes-us-into-his-tech-den-634622|title=Fast Car hitmaker Jonas Blue takes us into his tech den|first=Danny Turner10|last=February 2016|website=MusicRadar|date=10 February 2016}}</ref> He produces music that blends [[dance music|dance]] with [[pop music]]<ref name=":0" /> and features unestablished, talented pop artists on his studio projects.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nashville.com|url=https://www.nashville.com/|access-date=12 September 2021|website=Nashville.com}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2015–2017: Breakthrough and ''Jonas Blue: Electronic Nature – The Mix 2017'' ===
In 2015, Blue released a [[tropical house]] cover of [[Tracy Chapman]]'s 1988 single "[[Fast Car]]". It was his debut single and features the vocals from [[Dakota (singer)|Dakota]].<ref name="purplerevolver" /> The Jonas Blue version peaked at number two on the [[UK Singles Chart]], behind [[Zayn Malik]]'s "[[Pillowtalk (song)|Pillowtalk]]".<ref name="AMILK" /> Its UK peak meant it charted higher than Chapman's original, which peaked at number five on the chart in May 1988 and a position higher upon a re-release in April 2011.<ref name="rereleasea" /> It also remained in the top 10 for 11 weeks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/48918/jonas-blue-and-dakota/|title=JONAS BLUE & DAKOTA {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|publisher=Official Charts Company|access-date=13 June 2016}}</ref> The Jonas Blue version also reached number one in Germany, Sweden, Australia and New Zealand, as well as reaching the number one spot on the US viral chart on [[Spotify]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://www.jonasbluemusic.com/|title=Jonas Blue Biography|website=jonasbluemusic|access-date=13 June 2016}}</ref> The single has been certified platinum in Italy<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fimi.it/certificazioni#/category:singoli/year:2016/page:0/term:Jonas_Blue_feat._Dakota|title=Italian single certifications – Jonas Blue feat. Dakota – Fast Car|website=Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana}}</ref> and the UK,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bpi.co.uk/certified-awards/search.aspx|title=British single certifications – Jonas Blue feat. Dakota – Fast Car|website=Enter Fast Car in the field Keywords. Select Title in the field Search by. Select single in the field By Format. Select Platinum in the field By Award. Click Search.|publisher=British Phonographic Industry}}</ref> 2× platinum in New Zealand<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://nztop40.co.nz/chart/singles?chart=4212|title=The Official New Zealand Music Chart|publisher=[[Recorded Music NZ]]|access-date=13 June 2016}}</ref> and 3× platinum in Australia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.noise11.com/news/aria-singles-drake-one-dance-still-no-1-20160514|title=ARIA Singles: Drake One Dance Still No 1|date=14 May 2016|website=Australia's Music News Authority|access-date=13 June 2016}}</ref> To date, the track has been streamed almost 732 million times on Spotify,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/track/6OZh916QF8XNunWaP97WEZ|title=Fast Car – Radio Edit|date=4 December 2015|publisher=Spotify|access-date=13 June 2016}}</ref> and has achieved over 310 million views on [[Vevo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5yXQJBU8A28|title=Jonas Blue – Fast Car ft. Dakota|via=YouTube|access-date=13 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GjnadPBMJGs|title=Jonas Blue – Fast Car ft. Dakota|via=YouTube|access-date=13 June 2016}}</ref>

On 3 June 2016, he released a new single titled "[[Perfect Strangers (Jonas Blue song)|Perfect Strangers]]", featuring [[JP Cooper]] on [[Virgin EMI Records]].<ref name="bigpst">{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/fast-car-feat-dakota-radio-edit-single/1444631344|title=Fast Car (feat. Dakota) [Radio Edit] – Single by Jonas Blue|website=Apple Music|date=4 December 2015}}</ref> It peaked at No. 2 on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. To date, the song has been certified platinum in the UK,<ref name="BPI">{{cite web|url=https://www.bpi.co.uk/brit-certified/|title=Certified Awards Search|publisher=[[British Phonographic Industry]]|format=To access, enter the search parameter "Jonas Blue" and select "Search"}}</ref> double platinum in Australia<ref name="ARIA 7 November">{{cite web|url=http://www.ariacharts.com.au/charts/singles-chart|title=ARIA Australian Top 50 Singles|publisher=[[Australian Recording Industry Association]]|date=7 November 2016|access-date=5 November 2016}}</ref> and gold in New Zealand.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=http://nztop40.co.nz/chart/singles?chart=4212|title=New Zealand single certifications – Jonas Blue feat. Dakota – Fast Car|publisher=Nztop40.co.nz|location=New Zealand}}</ref> The track also led to Blue reaching one billion streams across his tracks and videos. October 2016 saw the release of his third single titled "[[By Your Side (Jonas Blue song)|By Your Side]]", featuring British singer [[Raye (singer)|Raye]], again on [[Virgin EMI Records]]. It peaked at No. 15 on the UK Singles Chart.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.digitalspy.com/music/news/a812380/jonas-blue-teams-up-with-raye-for-another-storming-dance-anthem-listen-to/|title=Listen to Jonas Blue's storming new dance anthem here|date=27 October 2016|work=Digital Spy|access-date=8 November 2016}}</ref>

On 5 May 2017, he released the song "[[Mama (Jonas Blue song)|Mama]]", featuring Australian singer [[William Singe]] which charted at No. 4 on the UK Singles Chart.<ref>{{cite web|date=11 May 2017|title=Jonas Blue, 'Perfect Strangers' hitmaker, unveils catchy new track|url=http://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/05/11/17/jonas-blue-perfect-strangers-hitmaker-unveils-catchy-new-track|work=[[ABS-CBN Corporation]]|access-date=24 February 2018 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170917213735/http://news.abs-cbn.com/entertainment/05/11/17/jonas-blue-perfect-strangers-hitmaker-unveils-catchy-new-track |archive-date=17 September 2017 }}</ref>

=== 2017–2019: ''Blue'' ===
On 14 September 2017, Jonas Blue produced the song "[[Heartline]]" sung by [[Craig David]] for his album ''[[The Time Is Now (album)|The Time is Now]]''. It managed to chart at No. 24 on the UK Singles Chart. On 13 October 2017, the single "[[We Could Go Back]]" was released, featuring Moelogo. It charted at No. 74 on the UK Singles Chart.<ref name="apple.com">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/we-could-go-back-feat-moelogo-single/id1291184156|title=We Could Go Back (feat. Moelogo) – Single by Jonas Blue|website=[[Apple Music]]|date=13 October 2017}}</ref>

On 5 January 2018, Blue released "[[Hearts Ain't Gonna Lie]]", with [[Arlissa]]. On 16 March 2018, he released the single "[[Alien (Sabrina Carpenter and Jonas Blue song)|Alien]]" with [[Sabrina Carpenter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/music/sabrina-carpenter-reveals-inspiration-behind-new-song-alien/|title=Watch Sabrina Carpenter and Jonas Blue's New Music Video for Their Emotional Single 'Alien'|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|last=Nicole|first=Sands|date=2 April 2018}}</ref> On 25 May 2018, he released "[[Rise (Jonas Blue song)|Rise]]" featuring [[Jack & Jack]].<ref name="auto">{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8457944/jonas-blue-jack-and-jack-rise-listen |title=Jonas Blue, Jack & Jack Bring Summer Heat on 'Rise': Listen |last=Bein |first=Kat |date=25 May 2018 |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180526052603/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8457944/jonas-blue-jack-and-jack-rise-listen |archive-date=26 May 2018 |access-date=27 May 2018}}</ref> It charted at No. 3 on the UK Singles Chart. He released "[[I See Love (Jonas Blue song)|I See Love]]" featuring [[Joe Jonas]] on 29 June 2018, which appears on the ''[[Hotel Transylvania 3]]'' soundtrack as well as Blue's debut album. American DJ [[Marc Kinchen|MK]] released a song titled "[[Back & Forth (MK, Jonas Blue and Becky Hill song)|Back & Forth]]" with Blue and English singer-songwriter [[Becky Hill]]. It charted at No. 12 in the UK. Bantu released "Roll with Me" with Blue featuring Shungudzo and ZieZie on 21 September 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/roll-with-me-feat-shungudzo-ziezie-single/1435779825|title=Roll With Me (feat. Shungudzo & ZieZie) – Single by Bantu & Jonas Blue|website=[[iTunes]] (UK)|date=21 September 2018|access-date=21 September 2018}}</ref> On 5 October 2018, Blue released "[[Polaroid (Jonas Blue, Liam Payne and Lennon Stella song)|Polaroid]]" with English singer [[Liam Payne]] and Canadian singer [[Lennon Stella]] as the eighth single from the debut studio album ''Blue''.<ref name="iTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/polaroid-single/1436923614|title=Polaroid – Single by Jonas Blue, Liam Payne & Lennon Stella|date=5 October 2018|publisher=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=22 October 2018}}</ref> The song charted at No. 12 on the UK Singles Chart. His debut album, ''[[Blue (Jonas Blue album)|Blue]]'', was released on 9 November 2018. It was also confirmed that Blue was to be the executive producer of British girl group [[Four of Diamonds]]' debut album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.femalefirst.co.uk/music/musicnews/four-diamonds-debut-album-executive-produced-jonas-blue-1149211.html|title=Four of Diamonds' debut album executive produced by Jonas Blue|last=June 2018|first=18|website=www.femalefirst.co.uk|language=en|access-date=17 January 2019}}</ref> On 30 November 2018, Jonas Blue produced the song 'Blind' sung by Four of Diamonds for their upcoming album.

"Desperate" featuring [[Nina Nesbitt]] was released as the ninth single from Blue's debut album on 23 January 2019. "Wild" featuring Chelcee Grimes, Tini and [[Jhay Cortez]] was released as the tenth and final single from the album on 27 February 2019.

=== 2019–2020: ''Est. 1989'' ===
On 9 March 2019, Jonas Blue re-released his popular summer anthem "[[Rise (Jonas Blue song)|Rise]]" but this time it featured the Korean-Japanese girl group [[Iz*One]]. On 22 March 2019, Blue released "[[What I Like About You (Jonas Blue song)|What I Like About You]]" featuring Theresa Rex. It peaked at No. 16 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/what-i-like-about-you-feat-theresa-rex-single/1455636535|title=What I like About You (feat. Theresa Rex) – Single by Jonas Blue on Apple Music|website=Apple Music|date=22 March 2019|access-date=22 March 2019}}</ref> On 31 May 2019, Dutch DJ [[Tiësto]] released the song "[[Ritual (Tiësto, Jonas Blue and Rita Ora song)|Ritual]]" with Blue and British singer [[Rita Ora]], which peaked at No. 24 on the UK Singles Chart. On 19 July 2019, Blue released the song "I Wanna Dance",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/au/album/i-wanna-dance-single/1471582312|title=I Wanna Dance – Single by Jonas Blue|website=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> followed by the song "[[Younger (Jonas Blue and Hrvy song)|Younger]]" with English singer [[Hrvy]] on 6 September 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.suruga-ya.com/en/product/221220757|title=Jonas Blue / Blue-Deluxe Edition}}</ref>

On 15 November 2019, Jonas Blue worked with RetroVision to release "All Night Long". On 20 November 2019, Blue was featured on the [[Got7]] song "Now or Never" from their EP ''[[Call My Name (EP)|Call My Name]]''. On 12 December 2019, Blue released the song "Billboard" with Chinese singer Tifa Chen. On 28 February 2020, Blue released the song "[[Mistakes (Jonas Blue and Paloma Faith song)|Mistakes]]" with English singer-songwriter [[Paloma Faith]]. On 26 June 2020, Blue released the song "Naked" with American singer [[Max Schneider|Max]]. These tracks, with the exception of "Now or Never", were later featured on Blue's 2020 compilation ''Est. 1989''. On 19 November 2020, Jonas Blue co-produced the song "[[My Head & My Heart]]" for [[Ava Max]] from her album ''[[Heaven & Hell (Ava Max album)|Heaven & Hell]]'', which reached the top 20 of the UK Singles Chart.

=== 2021-present: Upcoming album ===
On 5 February 2021, Jonas Blue released the song "Something Stupid" featuring Awa. On 28 May 2021, Jonas Blue released the song "[[Hear Me Say]]" with [[LÉON]]. The song 'Hear Me Say' debuted on the UK Singles Chart at No.65. On 10 September 2021, Jonas Blue collaborated with [[R3hab]] to release "Sad Boy" which featured the vocals of [[Ava Max]] and Kylie Cantrall. It is the second time that Jonas Blue has worked with Ava Max since Jonas Blue co-produced the hit single "[[My Head & My Heart]]" for her album. It is the second artist that Jonas Blue has worked with since working with Dark Heart for the album "Blue".
