'''Michael Lamar White II'''<ref name="listen.tidal.com">{{Cite web|url=https://listen.tidal.com/album/182794833/credits|title=Try the TIDAL Web Player}}</ref> (born June 18, 1999), known professionally as '''Trippie Redd''', is an American rapper. He is one of the most prominent members of the [[mumble rap|SoundCloud rap]] scene, which gained mainstream acclaim in the late 2010s. His debut mixtape, ''[[A Love Letter to You]]'' (2017), and its lead single, "Love Scars", propelled him to popularity. Trippie Redd's singles "[[Dark Knight Dummo]]", "[[Taking a Walk]]", and "[[Topanga (song)|Topanga]]" all reached the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]]. His debut studio album, ''[[Life's a Trip]]'' (2018),<ref name=":16">{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/07/trippie-redd-release-date-new-album-lifes-a-drip|title=Trippie Redd Shares Release Date For 'Life's A Trip' Album|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref> and sophomore album, ''[[! (Trippie Redd album)|!]]'' (2019),<ref name=":14">{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2019/07/trippie-redd-new-album-release-date/|title=Trippie Redd Announces New Album, Releases New Song|last=Mendez|first=Marisa|website=XXL Mag|access-date=August 10, 2019}}</ref> both reached the top five of the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], while his fourth mixtape, ''[[A Love Letter to You 4]]'' (2019), topped the chart.<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Caulfield |first=Keith |title=Trippie Redd Rules With First No. 1 Album on Billboard 200 Chart |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8545115/trippie-redd-no-1-album-billboard-200-chart |magazine=Billboard |access-date=December 1, 2019}}</ref> Trippie Redd's third studio album, ''[[Pegasus (Trippie Redd album)|Pegasus]]'' (2020), reached number two on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{cite magazine |last=Caufield |first=Keith |title=Ariana Grande Claims Fifth No. 1 Album on Billboard 200 Chart With 'Positions' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9480311/ariana-grande-positions-tops-billboard-200/ |magazine=Billboard |access-date=21 November 2020}}</ref>

==Early life==
White was born on June 18, 1999, in [[Canton, Ohio]]. He has stated he is of [[Irish people|Irish]], [[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|Native American]] and [[African American]] descent.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://beat.media/the-new-wave-who-is-trippie-redd|title=The New Wave: Who Is Trippie Redd?|date=October 8, 2017|access-date=September 8, 2018|publisher=Beat|first=Alí|last=González}}</ref> His father, Michael Lamar White, was in prison at the time of his birth, and his mother, Tonya White, was left to raise him as a single parent.<ref name="XXLBreak">{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/07/trippie-redd-interview-break/|title=The Break Presents: Trippie Redd|publisher=XXL Mag|access-date=August 9, 2017|first=Max|last=Weinstein}}</ref> White has a younger brother nicknamed "Hippie Redd" and had an older brother who made music under the name "Dirty Redd". Dirty Redd was killed in a car accident in 2014, and White talked to ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' about the time following the fatal accident.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/08/the-road-to-trippie-redd-lifes-a-trip/|title=The Road to Trippie Redd's Debut Album 'Life's a Trip'2014: Dirty Redd Dies, and Trippie's Music Career Begins|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=2019-09-25}}</ref> White grew up in Canton, though he moved to [[Columbus, Ohio]], several times.<ref name="MA">{{Citation|last=Mass Appeal|title=Open Space: Trippie Redd|date=June 30, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BnJn5n1Roc8|access-date=August 9, 2017|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> His interest in music began when his mother played [[Ashanti (singer)|Ashanti]], [[Beyoncé]], [[Tupac Shakur|Tupac]], and [[Nas]] while White was growing up. He later listened to artists such as [[T-Pain]], [[Kiss (band)|Kiss]], [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]], [[Gucci Mane]], [[Fountains of Wayne]], [[the Ataris]], [[Green Day]], [[the Offspring]], [[Cute Is What We Aim For]], [[Simple Plan]], [[the Summer Set]], [[Hit the Lights]], [[Marilyn Manson]] and [[Lil Wayne]].<ref name="MA" /><ref name="XXLBreak" /> White began rapping after being inspired by Taevion Williams, another rapper who went by the stage name Lil Tae.<ref name="MA" /><ref name="Noisey">{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/qvvp9v/trippie-redd-is-on-the-way-to-becoming-soundcloud-raps-frank-sinatra|title=Trippie Redd Is on the Way to Becoming Soundcloud Rap's Frank Sinatra|publisher=Noisey|date=August 11, 2017|access-date=September 8, 2018}}</ref> White began taking his music career seriously and began recording music, releasing "Sub-Zero" and "New Ferrari" in 2014 but soon deleting those songs.<ref name="XXLBreak" />

Following his high school graduation, White moved to [[Atlanta]], where he met rapper [[Lil Wop]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.drillking.org/meet-lil-wop-2017-trapper-of-the-year/|title=Meet Lil Wop, 2017 Trapper of the year|date=July 18, 2017|first=Shawn|last=Jackson|access-date=September 8, 2018}}</ref> and was eventually offered a deal with a record label.<ref name="PAP">{{Cite news|url=http://pigeonsandplanes.com/in-depth/2017/08/trippie-redd-interview|title=24 Hours in Miami With Trippie Redd|publisher=PigeonsandPlanes|access-date=September 8, 2018|date=August 1, 2017|first=Jacob|last=Moore}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Trippie Redd 2018 better crop.png|right|thumb|upright|Trippie Redd performing in February 2018]]

===2016–2018: Career beginnings and ''Life's a Trip''===
Lil Wop helped White get started with a professional recording studio. They began working with Kodie Shane and recorded three projects: ''Awakening My Inner Beast'', ''Beast Mode'' and ''Rock the World Trippie''.<ref name="XXLBreak" /><ref name="PAP" /> White eventually signed to the label Strainge Entertainment (now known as Elliot Grainge Entertainment) and relocated to [[Los Angeles]].<ref name="Noisey" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.strainge.com/|title=Welcome|website=Strainge Entertainment|access-date=September 12, 2017}}</ref>

On May 26, 2017, White released his debut [[mixtape]], ''[[A Love Letter to You]]'', with the lead single "Love Scars", which received more than 8 million views on [[YouTube]] within several months<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/trippie-redd-a-love-letter-to-you-new-mixtape.117163.html|title=Trippie Redd - A Love Letter To You|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=September 12, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|last=Pigeons & Planes|title=Trippie Redd - "Love Scars" Official Music Video {{!}} Pigeons & Planes Premiere|date=June 25, 2017|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NUNYcwDkBPc|access-date=September 12, 2017}}</ref> and more than 13 million on SoundCloud.<ref>{{Citation|title=Love Scars/You hurt me [Produced by: Elliott Trent] </3|url=https://soundcloud.com/trippie-hippie-2/love-scars|access-date=September 12, 2017}}</ref> White was included on [[XXXTentacion]]'s album ''[[17 (XXXTentacion album)|17]]'', on the song "[[Fuck Love]]", which peaked at number 28 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="XHot100">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/music/xxxtentacion/chart-history|title=XXXTentacion – Chart History: Billboard Hot 100|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=November 2, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/xxxtentacion-fuck-love-feat-trippie-redd-new-song.1975474.html|title=XXXTENTACION - Fuck Love Feat. Trippie Redd|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=September 12, 2017}}</ref>

On October 6, 2017, White released his second mixtape, ''[[A Love Letter to You 2]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/10/06/trippie-redd-a-love-letter-to-you-2-mixtape|title=Trippie Redd shares new mixtape A Love Letter To You 2|work=The FADER|access-date=October 17, 2017}}</ref> The album debuted at number 34 on the ''Billboard'' 200. Later that month, White released a collaboration EP with Lil Wop, ''Angels & Demons''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/10/trippie-redd-lil-wop-angels-and-demons-ep/|title=Trippie Redd and Lil Wop Release Collab EP 'Angels & Demons' - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=December 6, 2017}}</ref>

On December 6, 2017, White released the single "[[Dark Knight Dummo]]", featuring [[Travis Scott]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2017/12/travis-scott-trippie-redd-new-song-dark-knight-dummo/|title=Trippie Redd and Travis Scott Drop New Song ''Dark Knight Dummo'' - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=December 6, 2017}}</ref> The song peaked at 72 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, making it White's first entry on the chart as a lead artist.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/travis-scott/chart-history/hot-100/2|title=Travis Scott Chart History|work=Billboard|access-date=February 10, 2018}}</ref> On December 25, 2017, White released the song "TR666" on his SoundCloud account, which features [[Swae Lee]] and was produced by [[Scott Storch]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/swae-lee-and-trippie-redd-collab-on-tr66-new-song.1976774.html|title=Swae Lee & Trippie Redd Collab On "TR66"|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=December 27, 2017}}</ref> The track was previewed on November 30.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/trippie-redd-previews-new-song-with-swae-lee-news.40183.html|title=Trippie Redd Previews New Song With Swae Lee|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=December 26, 2017}}</ref> White released "18" alongside [[Baauer]], [[Kris Wu]], [[George Miller (entertainer)|Joji]] and [[Rich Brian]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2018/01/kris-wu-trippie-redd-rich-brian-joji-baauer-18-video/|title=Trippie Redd Joins Kris Wu, Rich Brian and More in ''18'' Video - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=March 15, 2018}}</ref>

In an interview with ''Billboard'' in March 2018, White said his debut studio album would feature collaborations with Lil Wayne and Erykah Badu.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8263173/trippie-redd-debut-album-lil-wayne-erykah-badu|title=Trippie Redd Says Debut Album Will Include Features From Lil Wayne & Erykah Badu|work=Billboard|access-date=April 5, 2018}}</ref> In July 2018, White announced that the album would be titled ''[[Life's a Trip]]'' and be 26 tracks long; he later trimmed it to 16 tracks.<ref name=":15">{{Cite news|title=Trippie Redd Reveals Tracklist & Artwork for 'Life's A Trip'|work=HYPEBEAST|url=https://hypebeast.com/2018/7/trippie-redd-reveals-lifes-a-trip-tracklist-draft|access-date=July 3, 2018}}</ref> White released the singles "Me Likey" and "How You Feel" on June 22, 2018,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/trippie-redd-releases-a-new-song-called-me-likey-new-song.1979137.html|title=Trippie Redd Releases A New Song Called "Me Likey"|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref> and "[[Taking a Walk]]" on August 6, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8469105/trippie-redd-lifes-a-trip-track-list-taking-a-walk-song|title=Trippie Redd Releases New Song 'Taking a Walk,' Reveals 'Life's a Trip' Track List|work=Billboard|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref> ''Life's a Trip'' was released August 10, 2018,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/07/trippie-redd-release-date-new-album-lifes-a-drip|title=Trippie Redd Shares Release Date For 'Life's A Trip' Album|website=Complex|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref><ref name=":17">{{Cite news|title=Trippie Redd Announces Release Date For Upcoming 'Life's A Trip' Album|work=HYPEBEAST|url=https://hypebeast.com/2018/7/trippie-redd-lifes-a-trip-album-release-date|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref> and debuted at number four on the ''Billboard'' 200 album chart,<ref name=":18">{{Cite news|title=Trippie Redd 'Life's a Trip' LP Debuts in Billboard 200 Top Five - XXL|language=en|work=XXL Mag|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/08/trippie-redd-lifes-a-trip-billboard-200-top-5/|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref> with the single "Taking a Walk" debuting at number 46 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 chart. Later in 2018, Trippie Redd released ''[[A Love Letter to You 3]]'', which reached number three on the ''Billboard'' 200.

===2019–2020: ''A Love Letter to You 4'' and ''Pegasus''===
On July 24, 2019, White released the single "[[Mac 10 (song)|Mac 10]]", featuring American rappers [[Lil Baby]] and Lil Duke, the lead single of his second studio album, ''[[! (Trippie Redd album)|!]]''. The album was released on August 9, 2019, and debuted at number three on the US ''Billboard'' 200 with 51,000 album-equivalent units.<ref name=":14" />  The album received lukewarm receives and, amid criticism, White removed the song "They Afraid Of You", featuring [[Playboi Carti]], from the album. Despite this, White went on to release ''[[A Love Letter to You 4]]'' that November. The mixtape debuted at number one on the ''Billboard'' 200, becoming his first chart-topping album.

Following this, White featured on several songs in the first half of 2020. He appeared on English [[YouTuber]], media personality, and rapper [[KSI (entertainer)|KSI]]'s single "[[Wake Up Call (KSI song)|Wake Up Call]]", which was released on January 31, 2020. The song serves as the second single to the latter's debut studio album, ''[[Dissimulation (KSI album)|Dissimulation]]''. The song reached number 11 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/uk/artists/music/ksi/songs/wake-up-call-feat-trippie-redd/|title=iTunesCharts.net: 'Wake Up Call (feat. Trippie Redd)' by Ksi (British Songs iTunes Chart)|website=www.itunescharts.net|access-date=2020-02-06}}</ref> He also released a collaboration with [[Juice Wrld]] titled "[[Tell Me U Luv Me]]", which reached the top 40 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. On May 15, 2020, White released the single "Excitement" with [[PartyNextDoor]], as the lead single for his third studio album, ''[[Pegasus (Trippie Redd album)|Pegasus]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/excitement-single/1511570944?l=en|title=Excitement - Single by Trippie Redd & PARTYNEXTDOOR on Apple Music|date=May 15, 2020|via=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=May 16, 2020}}</ref> On June 18, which was his 21st birthday, he released another single titled "Dreamer", a single for ''Neon Shark'', the deluxe edition of ''Pegasus'', which would be his "rock album".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/trippie-redd-reveals-dreamer-album-cover-and-release-date-news.112346.html|title=Trippie Redd Reveals "Dreamer" Album Cover & Release Date|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Findlay|first=Mitch|date=June 12, 2020|access-date=June 12, 2020}}</ref> On August 18, the entire album leaked after White had stated that he would delay the album even further if more of his music continued to leak online.<ref>{{cite web |last=Zidel |first=Alex |title=Trippie Redd's Entire "Pegasus" Album Leaks: Report |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/trippie-redds-entire-pegasus-album-leaks-report-news.116284.html?_amp |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=September 21, 2020}}</ref> On September 11, he released the single "I Got You", featuring [[Busta Rhymes]], as the second single from ''Pegasus''. On October 7, he released the third and final single, "Sleepy Hollow", from the album. The album was released on October 30, 2020, and debuted at number two on the ''Billboard'' 200.

===2021–present: ''Trip at Knight''===
White began the year by releasing the deluxe edition of ''Pegasus'', a rock reissue titled ''[[Neon Shark vs Pegasus]]''. He later announced his fourth studio album ''[[Trip at Knight]]'', which was expected to release later in the year. In the lead-up to the album's release, White released two high profile collaborations. The first, "[[Miss the Rage]]" featuring Playboi Carti, debuted at number 11 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, becoming the highest charting single of White's career. The second lead single was "[[Holy Smokes]]" featuring [[Lil Uzi Vert]], which peaked at number 50 on the Hot 100. On August 11, White announced the album's tracklist, as well as a tour set to begin on August 25.

On September 9, 2021, someone had opened fire on White's tour bus, striking the driver.<ref>{{cite web|title=Trippie Redd Concert Support Crew Bus Reportedly Shot at After Baltimore Show|url=https://www.complex.com/music/trippie-redd-concert-support-crew-bus-shot-at-baltimore-show}}</ref>

If we give niggas clout, we give niggas clout. It was an accident."<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://massappeal.com/trippie-redd-denounce-tekashi-6ix9ine/|title=Trippie Redd Denounces Tekashi 6ix9ine As A "Pedophile"|date=August 7, 2017|work=Mass Appeal|access-date=March 15, 2018}}</ref> On November 11, 2017, White was attacked in a New York hotel and claimed that a member of 6ix9ine's team ambushed him.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=http://www.worldstarhiphop.com/videos/video.php?v=wshhxN5aHdXMytN6m3tq|title=Trippie Redd Claims Tekashi 6ix9ine And Crew Jumped Him At His Hotel In New York, Bans Tekashi From LA!|work=WORLDSTARHIPHOP|access-date=March 15, 2018}}</ref> 6ix9ine later insinuated his involvement, saying "I don't know what's going on, but your chin is bruised up. I just want you to be my friend again," before singing a hook from "Poles1469".<ref name=":3">{{Cite news|url=http://www.worldstarhiphop.com/videos/video.php?v=wshh8O1taL47T3o6M2N4|title=Tekashi 6ix9ine Responds To Trippie Redd Calling Him Gay & Ranting About Him On Instagram! In May 2018, 6ix9ine started a feud with rapper Tadoe and [[Chief Keef]] over allegations that Tadoe had abused artist Cuban Doll for talking to 6ix9ine. White supported Tadoe in the feud and went on to release a diss track toward 6ix9ine, "I Kill People", featuring Tadoe and Chief Keef.<ref name=":6">{{Cite news|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.46764/title.trippie-redd-teases-tekashi-6ix9ine-diss-song-with-chief-keef-tadoe|title=Trippie Redd Teases Tekashi 6ix9ine Diss Song With Chief Keef & Tadoe|last=http://hiphopdx.com|first=HipHopDX -|work=HipHopDX|access-date=June 17, 2018}}</ref><ref name=":7">{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/trippie-redd-connects-with-chief-keef-and-tadoe-on-new-song-i-kill-people-new-song.1978870.html|title=Trippie Redd Connects With Chief Keef & Tadoe On New Song "I Kill People!"|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=June 17, 2018}}</ref>

===XXXTentacion===
In October 2017, a preview of Drake's song "[[God's Plan (song)|God's Plan]]" was shown on social media. The song originally featured White singing the hook and giving an additional verse.<ref name=":9">{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/10/drake-trippie-redd-collabing-new-music/|title=Are Drake and Trippie Redd Collabing on New Music? - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=March 15, 2018}}</ref> Florida rapper Jahseh Onfroy, known by his stage name [[XXXTentacion]], who had prior issues with Drake,<ref name=":10">{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/video/2017/04/xxxtentacion-drake-stop-speaking-patois/|title=XXXTentacion Tells Drake to Stop Speaking Patois - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=March 15, 2018}}</ref> began to associate with 6ix9ine, to White's chagrin.<ref name="xxlmag.com"/> In March 2018, Onfroy "banned" White from Florida, promising to assault him if he attempted to enter the state. Onfroy apologized to White during a performance later that month, and the two reconciled after White accepted his apology.<ref name=":11">{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/video/2018/03/trippie-redd-xxxtentacion-beef-banned-from-florida/|title=Trippie Redd Calls XXXTentacion ''P*!?y'' for Florida Ban - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=March 15, 2018}}</ref> White and Onfroy went on to collaborate on multiple occasions.

Following [[Murder of XXXTentacion|XXXTentacion's death]] on June 18, 2018, which was also White's 19th birthday, White dyed his hair in memory of the artist<ref name=":12">{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/06/trippie-redd-dyes-hair-like-xxxtentacion-in-memory-of-late-rapper/|title=Trippie Redd Dyes Hair Like XXXTentacion in Memory of Late Rapper - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=July 3, 2018}}</ref> and released "Ghost Busters", a collaboration with [[Quavo]], XXXTentacion and [[Ski Mask the Slump God]] in memoriam.<ref name=":13">{{Cite news|url=https://hypebeast.com/2018/6/trippie-redd-xxxtentacion-ski-mask-the-slump-god-quavo-ghost-busters-stream|title=Trippie Redd Taps XXXTENTACION, Ski Mask the Slump God & Quavo For "Ghost Busters"|work=HYPEBEAST|access-date=July 3, 2018}}</ref> In a 2021 interview, White said that he still thinks about XXXTentacion every day.<ref>{{cite web|title=TRIPPIE REDD TALKS NEW PROJECT INSPIRED BY LIL UZI VERT, MEMORIES W/JUICE WRLD & XXXTENTACION|url=https://m.youtube.com/watch?t=1270&v=YUhDutRaXnQ&feature=youtu.be}}</ref> On White's 2021 album, ''Trip At Knight'', a posthumous collaborative song with White and XXXTentacion titled "Danny Phantom" was released, which was a reworked version of "Ghost Busters".

==Personal life==
White said in March 2017 that he was worth $7 million and purchased his mother a $300,000 home.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/03/trippie-redd-bought-his-mom-a-house/|title=Trippie Redd Claims He Bought His Mom a New House|publisher=XXL Mag|date=March 1, 2018|first=Michael|last=Sapanora|access-date=March 15, 2018}}</ref>
 
He was briefly in a relationship with fellow rapper [[Coi Leray]].

==Legal issues==
White was arrested in [[Cobb County, Georgia]] following an assault on rapper FDM Grady in late May 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2018/05/30/trippie-redd-lil-wop-atlanta-arrest|title=Trippie Redd was reportedly arrested in Atlanta|publisher=The Fader|access-date=May 30, 2018 |date=May 30, 2018 |first=Lily |last=Puckett}}</ref> According to Grady, White and rapper Lil Wop insulted Grady's girlfriend, leading Grady to draw a firearm briefly before engaging in a fist fight with White. At that point, Grady was attacked by four men including White and Wop.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/05/trippie-redd-lil-wop-arrested-in-atlanta-for-fighting/|title=Trippie Redd and Lil Wop Arrested in Atlanta for Fighting|publisher=XXL Mag|date=May 30, 2018|first=Nick|last=Mojica|access-date=May 30, 2018}}</ref> White was arrested on charges of affray (public fighting), criminal trespass and simple battery.{{citation needed|date=August 2018}} A few weeks later in early June, White was again arrested in [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]] on an assault charge for allegedly pistol-whipping a woman.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.tmz.com/2018/06/12/rapper-trippie-redd-arrested-again-assault/|title=Rapper Trippie Redd Arrested Again for Alleged Assault|publisher=TMZ|date=June 12, 2018|access-date=June 12, 2018}}</ref>
