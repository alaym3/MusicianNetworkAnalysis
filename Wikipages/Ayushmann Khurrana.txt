'''Ayushmann Khurrana''' (born '''Nishant Khurrana''' on 14 September 1984) is an Indian actor, singer, writer and television host who works in [[Hindi]] films. Known for his portrayals of ordinary men often battling social norms,<ref name="mint">{{cite news|author=Sen, Raja|title=Ayushmann Khurrana, actor of the year |url=https://www.livemint.com/Leisure/j3Uz5v4brrUGeBNoi9SIiO/Ayushmann-Khurrana-actor-of-the-year.html |work=Mint |access-date=6 December 2018|date=6 July 2018}}</ref><ref name="open">{{cite news|author=Bamzai, Kaveree|title=Ayushmann Khurrana: Mr Everyman |url=http://www.openthemagazine.com/article/cinema/ayushmann-khurrana-mr-everyman |work=Open |access-date=7 July 2019|date=5 July 2019}}</ref> he is the recipient of [[List of awards and nominations received by Ayushmann Khurrana|several awards]], including a [[National Film Award]] and four [[Filmfare Awards]]. He is one of the Highest paid actors in India. He has appeared in ''[[Forbes India]]''{{'s}} [[Forbes Celebrity 100|Celebrity 100]] list of 2013 and 2019. ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine named him one of the [[Time 100|100 most influential people in the world]] in 2020.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ayushmann Khurrana: The 100 Most Influential People of 2020|url=https://time.com/collection/100-most-influential-people-2020/5888475/ayushmann-khurrana/|access-date=23 September 2020|work=Time}}</ref>

Khurrana won the second season of  reality television show [[MTV Roadies]] in 2004 and ventured into an anchoring career. He made his film debut in 2012 with the romantic comedy ''[[Vicky Donor]]'' opposite co-debutante [[Yami Gautam]], in which his performance as a sperm donor earned him the [[Filmfare Award for Best Male Debut]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mid-day.com/articles/entertainment-actor-ayushmann-khurrana-movie-shubh-mangal-savdhan-trailer-trolling-erecticle-dysfunction-bollywood/18516195|title=Ayushmann Khurrana reveals the most amusing comment he received for 'Shubh Mangal Savdhan'}}</ref><ref name="Vicky Donor HIT2">{{cite web|url=http://www.indicine.com/movies/bollywood/vicky-donor-is-a-hit/|title=Vicky Donor is a HIT|publisher=Indicine.com|access-date=10 March 2015}}</ref> Following a brief setback, he starred in the commercially and critically successful romance ''[[Dum Laga Ke Haisha]]'' (2015), and went on to establish himself with the comedies ''[[Bareilly Ki Barfi]]'' (2017), ''[[Shubh Mangal Saavdhan]]'' (2017), ''[[Badhaai Ho]]'' (2018), ''[[Dream Girl (2019 film)|Dream Girl]]'' (2019), ''[[Bala (2019 film)|Bala]]'' (2019) and ''[[Shubh Mangal Zyada Saavdhan]]'' (2020), the thriller ''[[Andhadhun]]'' (2018), and the crime drama ''[[Article 15 (film)|Article 15]]'' (2019).<ref name="star">{{cite web|title=After Back-To-Back Hits, Ayushmann Khurrana Knows He's Become A Star But He Doesn't Want To Believe It |url=https://www.indiatimes.com/entertainment/after-back-to-back-hits-ayushmann-khurrana-knows-he-s-become-a-star-but-he-doesn-t-want-to-believe-it-355144.html |website=Indiatimes |access-date=22 October 2018|date=21 October 2018}}</ref> Khurrana's performances as a blind pianist in ''Andhadhun'' and an honest cop in ''Article 15'' won him two consecutive [[Filmfare Critics Award for Best Actor]], and he also won the [[National Film Award for Best Actor]] for the former.

Additional to his acting roles, Khurrana has sung for all of his films with the exception of ''[[Dum Laga Ke Haisha]]'', ''[[Bala (2019 film)|Bala]]'' and the [[Amazon Prime Video]]-presented comedy-drama ''[[Gulabo Sitabo]]'' (2020); his debut "[[Pani Da Rang]]" earned him the [[Filmfare Award for Best Male Playback Singer]], and was also composed by him and composer friend [[Rochak Kohli]].

==Early life and education==
Khurrana was born on 14 September 1984 in [[Chandigarh]] to Poonam and P. Khurrana,<ref>{{cite web |title=Ayushmann turns 31; Bollywood wishes him |url=https://www.bollywoodmantra.com/news/ayushmann-turns-31-bollywood-wishes-him/19179/ |website=BollywoodMantra |access-date=14 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=I've evolved as an actor: Ayushmann Khurrana |url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/541722/ive-evolved-as-an-actor-ayushmann-khurrana/ |website=IANS |publisher=The Express Tribune |access-date=5 June 2016|date=28 April 2013}}</ref> as Nishant Khurrana. His parents changed his name to Ayushmann Khurrana when he was 3 years old. He was a part of Guru Nanak Khalsa College. He studied at [[St. John's High School, Chandigarh|St. John's High School]], [[Chandigarh]] and [[DAV College, Chandigarh|DAV College]], [[Chandigarh]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ayushmann's Portfolio Pics|url=http://photogallery.indiatimes.com/celebs/bollywood/ayushmann-khurrana/ayushmanns-portfolio-pics/Ayushmann-Khurrana/articleshow/22629606.cms|website=The Times of India|access-date=5 June 2016}}</ref> He majored in [[English Literature|English literature]] and has a master's degree in Mass Communication from the [[Panjab University|School of Communication Studies, Panjab University]].<ref name="toifacts">{{cite web|title=Lesser known facts about Ayushmann Khurrana|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/Lesser-known-facts-about-Ayushmann-Khurrana/Ayushmann-Khurrana-Lesser-known-facts/photostory/41511591.cms|website=The Times of India|access-date=5 June 2016|date=21 September 2015}}</ref> He did serious theatre for five years. He was also the founding member of DAV College's "Aaghaaz" and "Manchtantra", which are active theatre groups in Chandigarh.<ref name="toifacts"/> He conceived and acted in street plays and won prizes in national college festivals such as [[Mood Indigo (festival)|Mood Indigo]] ([[IIT Bombay]]), OASIS ([[Birla Institute of Technology and Science, Pilani]]) and St. Bedes Shimla.<ref name="toifacts"/> He also won a Best Actor award for playing [[Ashwatthama]] in [[Dharamvir Bharati]]'s [[Andha Yug]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ayushmann's Portfolio Pics |url=http://photogallery.maharashtratimes.indiatimes.com/articleshow/36495087.cms |website=Maharashtra Times|access-date=5 June 2016}}</ref>

=== 2004–2011: Television shows and early career ===
After completing his graduation and post-graduation in Journalism, his first job was as an [[radio personality]] at [[Big FM (Indian radio station)|BIG FM]], [[Delhi]]. He hosted the show ''Big Chai – Maan Na Maan'', ''Main Tera Ayushmann'' and also won the Young Achievers Award in 2007 for it.<ref name=" RJ Ayushmann Wins Young Achievers Award">{{cite web |url=http://www.indiaprwire.com/pressrelease/radio/200705012745.htm |title=RJ Ayushmann of BIG 92.7 FM creates history: Wins Young Achievers Award |publisher=India PRwire |date=1 May 2007 |access-date=11 October 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120407040153/http://www.indiaprwire.com/pressrelease/radio/200705012745.htm |archive-date=7 April 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He was the youngest recipient of the Bharat Nirman Award in New Delhi.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Ayushmann Khurrana – Ayushmann Khurrana Biography|url = http://www.koimoi.com/actor/ayushmann-khurrana/|website = www.koimoi.com|access-date = 4 January 2016}}</ref>

He also worked in many other [[MTV India|MTV]] shows such as ''MTV Fully Faltoo Movies'', ''Cheque De India'' and ''Jaadoo Ek Baar''.<ref name="MTV launches Three Films">{{cite web|url=http://www.tellychakkar.com/news/mtv-launches-three-films |title=MTV launches Three Films |publisher=Telly Chakkar |date=10 September 2008}}</ref> He then turned television host with a multiple-talent-based reality show [[India's Got Talent]] on [[Colors (TV channel)|Colors TV]], which he co-anchored with   [[Nikhil Chinapa]],<ref name="Ayushman and Nikhil">{{cite web |url=http://buzzg.com/entertainment/in-conversation-with-the-host-ayushman-and-nikhil.html |title=In conversation with the host- Ayushman and Nikhil |publisher=BuzzG.com |date=30 June 2009 |access-date=11 October 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110813000109/http://buzzg.com/entertainment/in-conversation-with-the-host-ayushman-and-nikhil.html |archive-date=13 August 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and ''Stripped'', which gave latest updates on the Indian TV industry with a comic tinge, again on [[MTV India|MTV]]. At the end of the year, he was also the anchor of the singing reality show ''[[Music Ka Maha Muqqabla]]'' on [[STAR Plus]].<ref name="Ayushmann to host Music Ka Mahamuqabla">{{cite web |url=http://www.indya.com/gallery/indyagallery.aspx?aid=1126 |title=Ayushmann to host Music Ka Mahamuqabla |publisher=Indya.com |date=30 June 2009 |access-date=11 October 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120601024216/http://www.indya.com/gallery/indyagallery.aspx?aid=1126 |archive-date=1 June 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Apart from hosting the second season of ''[[MTV Rock On]]'' and ''[[India's Got Talent]]'' on [[Colors (TV channel)|Colors]], he was also a part of the anchoring team of [[Indian Premier League|Extra Innings T20 for Indian Premier League Season 3]] on [[SET Max]] with Gaurav Kapur, [[Samir Kochhar]] , and Angad Bedi,<ref name="Max Unveils Extra Innings T20 for IPL 2010">{{cite web|url=http://www.zimbio.com/Bollywood+Movies/articles/0145UqBQrxk/Max+Unveils+Mega+Extra+Innings+T+20+IPL+2010 |title=Max Unveils Mega Extra Innings T20 for IPL 2010 |publisher=Zimbio.com |date=10 March 2010}}</ref><ref name="Starplus.in">{{cite web |url=http://starplus.startv.in/news/882/ayushmann-khurana-to-host-bollywood-superstar-buzz.aspx |title=Ayushmann Khurrana to host Superstar Buzz |publisher=Star Plus |date=30 October 2010 |access-date=11 October 2011 |archive-date=5 November 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111105235958/http://starplus.startv.in/news/882/ayushmann-khurana-to-host-bollywood-superstar-buzz.aspx |url-status=dead }}</ref> following which he took up the offer of anchoring the dance-based reality show ''[[Just Dance (Indian TV series)|Just Dance]]'' on [[STAR Plus]].

===2012–2015: Film debut and career struggles===
[[File:Ayushmann Khurrana 1.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Khurrana at an event for ''[[Vicky Donor]]'' (2012), his film debut]]
Khurrana made his acting debut in 2012 with [[Shoojit Sircar]]'s romantic comedy ''[[Vicky Donor]]'', co-starring [[Annu Kapoor]] and debutante [[Yami Gautam]]. Marking the production debut of actor [[John Abraham]], who cameoed in a promotional song, it starred Khurrana in the titular role of a [[sperm donor]]. [[Anupama Chopra]] found Khurrana to be "earnest" in it but felt that his comedic work was overshadowed by that of Roy Kapur.<ref>{{cite news |last=Chopra |first=Anupama |title=Anupama Chopra's review: Nautanki Saala!|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/movie-reviews/anupama-chopra-s-review-nautanki-saala/story-0xhcpsNynUYZ31AxqK6leP.html|work=Hindustan Times |date=20 April 2013 |access-date=6 July 2019}}</ref> He also recorded two songs for the film's soundtrack.<ref>{{cite news|author=Shah, Kunal M|title=Ayushmann records two songs for 'Nautanki Saala!'|url=http://www.mid-day.com/entertainment/2012/oct/171012-Ayushmann-records-two-songs-for-Nautanki-Saala.htm|work=Mid Day|date=17 October 2012|access-date=6 July 2019}}</ref> A year later, Khurrana teamed with [[Yash Raj Films]], as part of a three-film deal, in the romantic comedy ''[[Bewakoofiyaan]]'' (2014), co-starring [[Sonam Kapoor]] and [[Rishi Kapoor]], about a young man who has trouble convincing his girlfriend's father to approve of their marriage.<ref>{{cite news |last=Roy |first=Priyanka |title=Bewakoofiyaan and beyond|url=https://www.telegraphindia.com/entertainment/bewakoofiyaan-and-beyond/cid/200972|work=The Telegraph |date=16 March 2014| access-date=6 July 2019}}</ref> Writing for ''[[The New York Times]]'', Andy Webster disliked the film's "strained, contrived humor" but commended Khurrana for "holding his own opposite [Rishi] Kapoor".<ref>{{cite news |last=Webster |first=Andy |title=Boy Meets Girl; Khurrana's career prospects improved when he starred opposite newcomer [[Bhumi Pednekar]] in [[Sharat Katariya]]'s romance ''[[Dum Laga Ke Haisha]]'' (2015).<ref name="decode"/><ref>{{cite news |last=Jha |first=Lata |title=Ten hits that helped actors bounce back|url=https://www.livemint.com/Leisure/QMUFu4fxJ02I8tjwr8yCFN/Ten-hits-that-helped-actors-bounce-back.html|work=Mint |date=2 December 2015 |access-date=6 July 2019}}</ref> It tells the story of an underachieving man who is married against his will to an overweight woman, and marked his first film where he did not sing any of the songs.
