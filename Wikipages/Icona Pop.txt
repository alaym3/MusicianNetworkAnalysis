'''Icona Pop''' ({{IPAc-en|lang|aɪ|ˈ|k|ɒ|n|ə|_|p|ɒ|p}}) is a Swedish [[electropop]] duo that formed in 2009, with [[electro house]] and [[indie pop]] music influences.<ref name=svd>{{cite news|author=Tweet Stockholm TT Spektra |url=http://www.svd.se/kultur/icona-pop-gor-dansmusik-med-punkattityd_7280417.svd |title=Icona Pop gör dansmusik med punkattityd |newspaper=Svenska Dagbladet |date=15 June 2012 |language=sv |publisher=Svd.se |access-date= 24 February 2013}}</ref> Its two members, '''Caroline Hjelt''' and '''Aino Jawo''', grew up in [[Stockholm]] and create what the Swedish press has described as music which "you can both laugh and cry to at the same time".<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.svd.se/kultur/icona-pop-gor-dansmusik-med-punkattityd_7280417.svd |title=Icona Pop gör dansmusik med punkattityd |language=sv |agency=[[Tidningarnas Telegrambyrå]] |newspaper=[[Svenska Dagbladet]] |date=15 June 2012 |access-date=7 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mynewsdesk.com/se/universalmusic/pressreleases/svenska-icona-pop-hyllas-vaerlden-oever-771702 |title=Svenska Icona Pop hyllas världen över! |language=sv |publisher=[[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Sweden]] |date=13 June 2012 |access-date=24 February 2013}}</ref> They signed to [[TEN Music Group]] in 2009 and are currently also in a label deal with [[Ultra Music]].<ref name=":0" /> Their biggest hit to date has been "[[I Love It (Icona Pop song)|I Love It]]".

==Career==

===Beginning===
Caroline Hjelt (born 8 November 1987, of a Swedish mother and [[Sweden Finns|Sweden-Finnish]] father<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.is.fi/viihde/art-2000000782260.html |title=Suositulla ruotsalaisbändillä suomalaistausta: "Olen hyvin suomalainen" |last=Rantanen |first=Kimmo |date=15 July 2014 |website=[[Ilta-Sanomat]] |language=fi |access-date=9 August 2019 |quote=...&nbsp;Hjeltin isän puolen isovanhemmat ovat suomalaisia.}}</ref>) and Aino Jawo (born 7 July 1986, of a Finnish mother and [[The Gambia|Gambian]] father<ref>{{cite web|last=Nika|first=Colleen|title=Meet Icona Pop: Sweden's Latest Stylish Dance Invaders|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/blogs/thread-count/meet-icona-pop-swedens-latest-stylish-dance-invaders-20111013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111014062138/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/blogs/thread-count/meet-icona-pop-swedens-latest-stylish-dance-invaders-20111013|url-status=dead|archive-date=14 October 2011|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|access-date=14 November 2013}}</ref>) attended the same [[music school]] in Stockholm,<ref>{{cite web|last=Patel|first=Puja|title=Icona Pop Talk Shop: Swedish Duo on Romance and Revenge|url=https://www.spin.com/2012/06/icona-pop-talk-shop-swedish-duo-romance-and-revenge/|work=[[SPIN Magazine]]|date=25 June 2012|access-date=14 November 2013}}</ref> but met by chance at a party in February 2009 and formed the duo. Four weeks later, they had written songs for their very first performance. Two years later, the two described their music as "classical pop melodies with drums and [[synth]]s".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/kultur/musik/tillsammans-ar-vi-ododliga |title="Tillsammans är vi odödliga" – Kultur | language= sv | publisher=SVT.se |date= 13 November 2012|access-date=24 February 2013}}</ref>

The English-language press, including ''[[NME]]'', ''[[The Guardian]]'', ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' and [[Pitchfork Media]], have praised the duo.<ref>{{cite web|author=Sheffield, Rob |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/iconic-20121203 |title=Iconic | work = [[Rolling Stone]] |date= 3 December 2012|access-date= 24 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/icona-pop-know-whats-good-for-you-premiere-20121015 |title=Icona Pop Know What's 'Good for You' – Premiere | work = [[Rolling Stone]] |date= 15 October 2012 |access-date= 24 February 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/features/rising/8848-icona-pop/ |title=Rising: Icona Pop |publisher= [[Pitchfork Media]] |date= 25 May 2012 |access-date= 24 February 2013}}</ref> ''The Guardian'' described the debut single "Manners" as "effortlessly cool".<ref>{{cite web|last=Cragg |first=Michael |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/musicblog/2011/mar/07/icona-pop-top-rated |title=New music: Icona Pop – Top Rated | work = [[The Guardian]] |date= 7 March 2011 |access-date= 24 February 2013}}</ref> The duo have worked in the studio with producers including Style of Eye, Patrik Berger ([[Robyn]]), Elof Loelv ([[Niki & The Dove]]) and [[Starsmith]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://acetj.channel961.com/new2/featured/f/3748/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130407233753/http://acetj.channel961.com/new2/featured/f/3748/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=7 April 2013 |title=Icona Pop &#124; NEW! Artist – The Ace & TJ Show |publisher=Acetj.channel961.com |access-date=24 February 2013 }}</ref>

In fall 2012, they released an [[extended play]] ''[[Iconic (EP)|Iconic]]'' in the US and the album ''Icona Pop'' in Sweden. The duo have performed in those countries as well as, the [[UK]], [[Germany]], [[Italy]], Canada, and [[Hong Kong]] and have also been interviewed by members of the Swedish and worldwide press.{{cn|date=September 2020}}

===2012–13: Breakthrough with "I Love It" and ''This Is... Icona Pop''===
{{Main|Icona Pop (album)|This Is... Icona Pop}}
[[File:Icona Pop StyleNite.jpg|thumb|Icona Pop at [[Stylenite|Michalsky StyleNite]] of [[Berlin Fashion Week]], August 2013]]
In the summer of 2012, they took part in a number of festivals including electro music festival Summerburst in [[Gothenburg]] and also appeared at the [[Stockholm Stadium]], at the start of an international festival and broadcast via [[Sveriges Television]] (SVT).

"I Love It", a pre-release of their self-titled debut album ''[[Icona Pop (album)|Icona Pop]]'' charted on [[Sverigetopplistan]], the official Swedish singles chart. The song, which features vocals from British recording artist [[Charli XCX]], was also featured in the 2012 video game ''[[Need for Speed: Most Wanted (2012 video game)|Need for Speed: Most Wanted]]'', in a German television commercial for [[Coca-Cola Light]] in January 2013,<ref>''[http://newsroom.coca-cola-gmbh.de/2013/01/14/coca-cola-light-startet-jubilaumsjahr-mit-neuer-„night-boost“-kampagne/ Coca Cola Light startet Jubiläumsjahr mit neuer "Night-Boost"-Kampagne] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130121122559/http://newsroom.coca-cola-gmbh.de/2013/01/14/coca-cola-light-startet-jubilaumsjahr-mit-neuer-%E2%80%9Enight-boost%E2%80%9C-kampagne/ |date=21 January 2013 }}'' – Press release of Coca Cola Company on 14 January 2013.</ref> the theme song to the MTV [[reality television|reality-television]] series ''[[Snooki & JWoww]]'', and in an episode of the HBO [[comedy-drama]] television series ''[[Girls (TV series)|Girls]] ''and the popular vampire-teen drama television series ''[[The Vampire Diaries]]''. The second single "Ready for the Weekend" was released on 11 September 2012. Both tracks appear on their second EP, released on 16 October 2012. "I Love It" was also used in the commercial for the [[Samsung Galaxy S4]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=66_VutLUSoc|title=Samsung Galaxy S4 TV Advert - Group Play|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>

Their EP ''Iconic EP'' peaked at No. 21 on the U.S. [[iTunes]] dance chart. In 2013, they embarked on a tour of the United States with [[Passion Pit]] and [[Matt & Kim]]. In January 2013, "I Love It" was featured in the third episode of the second season of ''[[Girls (TV series)|Girls]]'', the comedy-drama series created by [[Lena Dunham]]. Two months later, the duo was named one of [[Fuse (TV channel)|Fuse]]'s 30 must-see artists at the [[South by Southwest]] festival.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.fuse.tv/galleries/2013/02/sxsw-2013-must-see-bands-south-by-southwest#21 |title=Archived copy |website=[[Fuse (TV channel)]] |access-date=1 March 2013 |archive-date=11 May 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130511134407/http://www.fuse.tv/galleries/2013/02/sxsw-2013-must-see-bands-south-by-southwest#21 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The same month, "I Love It" was featured in the 16th episode of the fourth season, "Bring It On", of ''[[The Vampire Diaries]]''. On 26 March 2013, "I Love It" was performed on the week 2 results episode of ''[[Dancing with the Stars]]''. On 3 May 2013, the duo performed "I Love It" on the NBC [[late-night talk show]] ''[[Late Night with Jimmy Fallon]]'' and on ABC's ''[[Good Morning America]]'' morning news/entertainment show. Three days later, "I Love It" was featured in the Fox [[musical-comedy]] series, ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]''. The song was performed by the "New Directions" in Glee's 22nd and final episode of season four, "[[All or Nothing (Glee)|All or Nothing]]". It was also featured in a 2013 [[ShoeDazzle]] commercial. 

On 19 May 2013 the duo performed "I Love It" on the [[Billboard Music Awards]], wearing custom lit outfits by designer [[Geoffrey Mac]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rollingout.com/2013/05/20/2013-billboard-music-awards-performance-photos/|title=2013 Billboard Music Awards performance photos|first=R.|last=Hawkins|website=Rollingout.com|date=20 May 2013}}</ref> The song also is featured in a commercial for the [[WNBA]]'s forthcoming season that was aired during the 4th quarter of Game 7 of the [[Miami Heat|Heat]]-Pacers [[Eastern Conference (NBA)|Eastern Conference]] Championship game on 3 June 2013. It was also featured in a commercial during the [[San Antonio Spurs|Spurs]]-Heat basketball game on 9 June 2013.

On 4 June 2013, the duo released their single "[[Girlfriend (Icona Pop song)|Girlfriend]]". Icona Pop revealed in an interview that they recorded their international album with all new songs. They said that "the new album isn't just going to be sixteen tracks like 'I Love It'" and that they are trying new genres and sounds. In July 2013, they received two [[Teen Choice]] awards for Choice Music Breakout Group and Choice Single: Group.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.teenchoiceawards.com/tcnews/ |title=Teen Choice Awards Nominations: Choice Music Breakout Group, Choice Single: Group |publisher=Teenchoice.com |access-date=4 July 2013 }}{{dead link|date=November 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

On 28 July 2013, "[[All Night (Icona Pop song)|All Night]]" was released as the official third single from the album. It gained moderate success on European charts. On 4 August 2013, the duo visited [[Montreal]], where they performed at the Osheaga Festival.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.osheaga.com/lineup/ |archive-url=http://webarchive.loc.gov/all/20130920224506/http://www.osheaga.com/lineup/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2013-09-20 |title=Line Up festival, musique et arts Osheaga |website=Webarchive.loc.gov |date=2013-09-20 |access-date=2020-04-10}}</ref> They also appeared on [[Ellen (TV series)|''Ellen'']] in December of that year.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ivhm_Wg0GLo</ref>

===2014&ndash;present===
On 23 June 2014 the duo released a new single called "[[Get Lost (Icona Pop song)|Get Lost]]" which gained less international attention.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://slate.com/culture/2014/06/icona-pop-s-get-lost-the-duos-new-song-is-a-great-summer-jam-audio.html|title=Icona Pop's New Song Is a Perfect Addition to Your Summer '14 Playlist|last=Harris|first=Aisha|date=2014-06-23|website=Slate Magazine|language=en|access-date=2019-09-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://time.com/2917370/icona-pop-get-lost-song-of-the-summer/|title=Icona Pop Still Shooting for Song of the Summer With 'Get Lost'|magazine=Time|language=en|access-date=2019-09-01}}</ref> In the same period, the duo also performed as the opening act for [[Katy Perry]] and [[Miley Cyrus]] tours. They also collaborated with [[Tiesto]] for the song "[[Let's Go (Tiësto song)|Let's Go]]" and with [[Cobra Starship]] for the single "[[Never Been In Love (song)|Never Been In Love]]"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1914039/cobra-starship-icona-pop-never-been-in-love/|title=Icona Pop And Cobra Starship May Restore Your Faith In Love -- Or At Least Love Songs|last=Ehrlich|first=Brenna|website=MTV News|language=en|access-date=2019-09-01}}</ref> which became a successful hit in Italy.{{Citation needed|reason=Cannot find source|date=September 2019}}
On 26 May 2015, the duo released the single "[[Emergency (Icona Pop song)|Emergency]]", a collaboration with [[Erik Hassle]]. The song peaked a few weeks later on the Billboard Dance Chart, making their second single to top that chart after ''All Night''. The song was also featured in the video game ''[[FIFA 16]]''.{{Citation needed|date=September 2019}} In July, the EP ''Emergency'' was released. It includes the critically acclaimed ''First Time'' and the smash-hit ''Clap Snap''.{{Citation needed|date=September 2019}}
The duo was announced as an opening act for the North American leg of [[One Direction]]'s "On the Road Again" tour in 2015. Also in 2015, their song "We Got The World" was featured in the movie ''[[Pitch Perfect 2]]''.{{Citation needed|reason=Add link to soundtrack|date=September 2019}} On 23 February 2016, Icona Pop performed a new single, "[[Someone Who Can Dance]]" at the [[Grammis]] in Stockholm.{{Citation needed|reason=Grammis homepage is not a valid source|date=September 2019}} On 20 October 2016, the duo released their single "[[Brightside (Icona Pop song)|Brightside]]". On 16 June 2017, Icona Pop released a new single "[[Girls Girls (song)|GIRLS GIRLS]]", co-written by Tove Lo.{{Citation needed|date=September 2019}} In October 2017, they released a song with Australian duo [[Peking Duk]] called "[[Let You Down (Peking Duk song)|Let You Down]]".<ref>{{Citation|title=Peking Duk (Ft. Icona Pop) – Let You Down|url=https://genius.com/Peking-duk-let-you-down-lyrics|language=en|website=Genius.com|access-date=2019-09-01}}</ref> In late December 2017, the duo released an EP called "Så mycket bättre 2017 – Tolkningarna" including 7 covered songs.{{Citation needed|date=September 2019}} In early March 2018, the duo collaborated with Avon on a music video called "All My Girls". In late April 2018, the duo was featured on a song called "Faded Away" by [[Sweater Beats]]. In late May 2018, Icona Pop said on their social media that they were currently working on their third studio album. In June 2019 the duo performed at St. Louis Pridefest. Icona Pop released a song with [[R3hab]] called "This Is How We Party" on 8 February 2019.<ref>{{cite web|title=iTunes - Music - This Is How We Party (with Icona Pop) - Single by R3HAB|date=8 February 2019|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/this-is-how-we-party-with-icona-pop-single/1450006112|publisher=[[iTunes Store]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=R3HAB (Ft. Icona Pop) – This Is How We Party|url=https://genius.com/R3hab-this-is-how-we-party-lyrics|website=Genius.com|language=en|access-date=2019-09-01}}</ref> On 26 July 2019, the duo returned with the first single from the upcoming third studio album – "Next Mistake". The Swedish duo have signed to [[Ultra Music]] as of 2019.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8523984/icona-pop-ultra-records-next-mistake-interview-2019|title=Icona Pop Sign to Ultra Music, Share 'Next Mistake' & Reflect on 10 Years: 'We've Become Boss Ladies' (Exclusive)|website=Billboard.com|date=26 July 2019|access-date=2019-09-01}}</ref>
