'''Pa Salieu Gaye''' (born 1 July 1997) is a British rapper and singer. In January 2020, he released his debut single "Frontline", which was the most-played track of 2020 on [[BBC Radio 1Xtra]]. He has since featured on songs with South London's [[SL (rapper)|SL]] for "Hit the Block", Meekz, [[M1llionz]] and Teeway for "Year of the Real" and joined Backroad Gee and [[Ambush (rapper)|Ambush]] for the remix to "Party Popper". He has collaborated with artists such as [[FKA Twigs]] and SL.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=8 October 2020|title=Why Pa Salieu is the rapper to have on your radar|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/esmagazine/why-pa-salieu-is-the-rapper-to-have-on-your-radar-a4566486.html|access-date=17 October 2020|website=Evening Standard|language=en}}</ref> His style is renowned for blending elements of Afro-beats, grime and drill.<ref>{{Cite web|date=14 October 2020|title=The evolving sound of UK drill|url=https://djmag.com/longreads/evolving-sound-uk-drill|access-date=17 October 2020|website=DJMag.com}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|date=6 July 2020|title=Pa Salieu: UK rap's genre-blurring rising star on his journey from the Frontline to greatness|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/pa-salieu-interview-uk-rap-genre-blurring-rising-star-frontline-radar-2701805|access-date=17 October 2020|website=NME Music News, Reviews, Videos, Galleries, Tickets and Blogs {{!}} NME.COM|language=en-GB}}</ref> In June 2020, he released "Betty" and "Bang Out".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/mtv-push/news/get-to-know-pa-salieu|title=Get To Know: Pa Salieu &#124; MTV UK|website=www.mtv.co.uk}}</ref> He released his debut [[mixtape]] titled ''[[Send Them to Coventry]]'' on 13 November 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/B08LYY587N |title=Amazon Music: Send Them to Coventry by PA Salieu |publisher=Amazon.co.uk |date= |accessdate=29 October 2020}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Pa Salieu was born in [[Slough]], where he spent the early stages of his life. His parents moved to the UK from [[The Gambia]]. He was named after his father's eldest brother, Pa Salieu, a police officer back home who died in his early twenties. His aunt is a Gambian folk singer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Get To Know: Pa Salieu {{!}} MTV UK|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/mtv-push/news/get-to-know-pa-salieu|access-date=17 October 2020|website=www.mtv.co.uk|language=en}}</ref>

Before his first birthday, his parents sent him back to The Gambia where he lived with his grandmother. There, Pa spent his formative years living in a household with his elder relatives. He describes his time there as the most important period in his life.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/05/21/pa-salieu-interview-frontline|title=Pa Salieu is Coventry's hopeful rap star|website=The FADER}}</ref> At the age of 8, his mother brought him back to England. They moved to [[Hillfields]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/7kzen4/pa-salieu-interview-icons-idols-issue-spring-2020|title=Pa Salieu: "There's blood, sweat and tears on the frontline"|first=Danil|last=Boparai|date=28 February 2020}}</ref> a suburb in the North of [[Coventry]]. His new home provided inspiration for his music career, becoming the focus of his breakout song "Frontline" released in January 2020, produced by Jevon. Through the song, he depicts life in Coventry and documents the neighbourhood that raised him.

Pa started pursuing a music career after his close friend was killed.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theface.com/music/pa-salieu-frontline-interview-coventry-rap-twitter-j-hus|title=100% Pa Salieu: the Coventry rapper leading the UK rap Frontline|website=The Face}}</ref> He recorded his first freestyle at a friend's small home studio setup, where he would regularly visit to write and record music. In October 2019, he was the victim of a shooting in Coventry where he was shot in the head but he survived and made a full recovery.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coventrytelegraph.net/news/you-cant-stop-greatness-coventry-17731524|title=Coventry rapper's defiant message after being shot in the head|first=Josh|last=Layton|date=12 February 2020|website=CoventryLive}}</ref>

== Music ==
Salieu is signed to [[Warner Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.warnerrecords.co.uk/artists/pa-salieu|title=Pa Salieu}}</ref> He wrote "Frontline" in 20 minutes, and the music video was released on YouTube via Mixtape Madness.<ref name=":1" /> Since its release, it has accumulated over two million views and over three million streams.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=2020 vision: Pa Salieu is ready to deliver his testimony|url=https://www.redbull.com/gb-en/get-to-know-pa-salieu-career-interview|access-date=17 October 2020|website=Red Bull|language=en}}</ref> It has also become the most played track of 2020 on [[BBC Radio 1Xtra|BBC 1Xtra]], Reprezent Radio and [[Rinse FM]]. Salieu's music has received support from [[Virgil Abloh]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/virgil-ablohs-lockdown-playlist|title=Virgil Abloh's Lockdown Playlist|first=Nicole|last=Phelps|website=Vogue|date=19 March 2020}}</ref> BBC Radio 1's [[Annie Mac]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/m000fwtc|title = BBC Radio 1 – Radio 1's Future Sounds with Clara Amfo, HAIM Hottest Record}}</ref> and Tiffany Calver<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/m000dz30|title = BBC Radio 1Xtra – 1Xtra's Rap Show with Tiffany Calver, Pa Salieu does a Tiffany Calver freesyle!}}</ref> as well as [[OVO Sound]] co-founder Oliver El Khatib.

The early momentum of "Frontline" has continued with a string of feature verses. He collaborated with South London rapper [[SL (rapper)|SL]] on the song "Hit the Block", as well as Meekz, M1llionz and Teeway on "Year of the Real". He also collaborated with Backroad Gee and Ambush for the remix to their track, "Party Popper". In June 2020, Salieu released two tracks – "Betty" and "Bang Out". He was premiered by Annie Mac and DJ Target on [[BBC Radio 1]]. He was described by the London ''[[Evening Standard]]'' as the rapper to watch in 2020, and has received strong support from [[J Hus]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2" /> In January 2021, he was announced as the winner of the ''[[BBC Sound of...]]'' 2021.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-55319957|title=Rapper Pa Salieu wins BBC Sound of 2021: 'I am the voice of the voiceless'|work=BBC News|date=6 January 2021}}</ref> On 22 January 2021, he did his debut US TV performance on [[Jimmy Fallon]] "[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon|The Tonight Show]]" and performed "Frontline".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pa Salieu: Frontline – YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eLdQI7KAYl8|access-date=15 February 2021|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref> Pa Salieu has been selected by [[GQ]] as one of 11 "new musicians who'll make 2021 better"<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pa Salieu|url=https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/culture/gallery/best-new-musicians-2021?image=5ff8205e48a749ea907ba51e|access-date=16 February 2021|website=British GQ|date = 11 January 2021|language=en-GB}}</ref> and he was also on the cover of [[NME]]'s first issue of 2021, NME 100 edition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/big-reads/pa-salieu-cover-interview-2021-send-them-to-coventry-2852072|title=Pa Salieu: "I've been cut from the system, but I will rise up like a phoenix"|website=[[NME]]|date=8 January 2021}}</ref>
