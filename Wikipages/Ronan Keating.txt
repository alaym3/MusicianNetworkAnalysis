'''Ronan Patrick John Keating'''<ref name="RonanBiography">{{cite web |url=http://www.sing365.com/music/lyric.nsf/Ronan-Keating-Biography/477B5AA424C51D2C48256A86001239F9 |title=Ronan Keating Biography |publisher=Sing365.com |access-date=13 April 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110109035229/http://www.sing365.com/music/lyric.nsf/Ronan-Keating-Biography/477B5AA424C51D2C48256A86001239F9 |archive-date=9 January 2011 }}</ref> (born 3 March 1977) is an Irish singer, songwriter, and television & radio presenter who currently hosts a breakfast show on Magic Radio. He debuted in 1994 alongside [[Keith Duffy]], [[Michael Graham (singer)|Michael Graham]], [[Shane Lynch]], and [[Stephen Gately]], as the co-lead singer (with Gately) of Irish group [[Boyzone]]. His solo career started in 1999 and he has recorded eleven albums. He gained worldwide attention when his single "[[When You Say Nothing at All]]" was featured in the film ''[[Notting Hill (film)|Notting Hill]]'' and reached number one in several countries.

As a solo artist, Keating has sold over 20 million records worldwide alongside the 25 million records with Boyzone,<ref>{{cite news|author=John Hall |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/when-you-sell-nothing-at-all-despite-a-previous-55-million-album-sales-ronan-keatings-latest-release-fires-sells-just-181-copies-8117290.html |title=When you sell nothing at all: Despite a previous 55 million album sales, Ronan Keating's latest release 'Fires' sells just 181 copies – News – Music |work=The Independent |date=7 September 2012 |access-date=14 December 2012 |location=London}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/finance/2012/0707/1224319600611.html|title=Ronan Keating entertainment firm continues to make profits | work=The Irish Times | date=7 July 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url =http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20100313/local/the-success-story-rolls-on |title  =The success story rolls on |work=The Times|location=Malta |date =13 March 2010 |access-date=14 January 2011}}</ref> and in Australia, he is best known as a judge on ''[[All Together Now (2018 Australian TV series)|All Together Now]]'' and ''[[The X Factor (Australian TV series)|The X Factor]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nowmagazine.co.uk/celebrity-news/tv-news/469216/ronan-keating-lands-aussie-x-factor-job/1/ |title=Ronan Keating lands Aussie X Factor job |work=Now Magazine |date=21 May 2010 |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref> from 2010 until 2014 and a coach on ''[[The Voice (Australian TV series)|The Voice]]'' in 2016. Keating is active in charity work and has been a charity campaigner for the [[Marie Keating Foundation]], which raises awareness for [[breast cancer]] and is named after his mother, who died from the disease in 1996.

==Early life==
Keating was born on 3 March 1977, the youngest of five children. He grew up in [[Bayside, Dublin]] and [[County Meath]] attending [[St. Fintan's High School]]. His father, Gerry Keating, was a [[Truck driver|lorry driver]]; his mother Marie was a mobile hairdresser. He has one sister: Linda, and three brothers: Ciarán, Gerard and Gary.<ref name="Ronan Keating Biography">{{cite web|last=Cheap|first=Buy|url=http://allartistbiography.blogspot.com/2008/06/ronan-keating.html|title=All Artist Biography: Ronan Keating|publisher=Allartistbiography.blogspot.com|date=3 June 2008|access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Fd_NI4mZOlMC&q=ronan+keating+st+fintans+high+school&pg=PT26 |title=Life Is A Rollercoaster - Ronan Keating - Google Books |date=31 October 2011|isbn=9781446491522 |last1=Keating |first1=Ronan }}</ref> He was a track and field athlete and represented Ireland in several tournaments, winning the All Ireland under-13 200 m title.

==Career==
===1993–1999: Boyzone===
[[File:Boyzone 2009.jpg|thumb|upright=1.2|left|Boyzone performing on tour with Ronan Keating as the lead singer]]

In 1993, the then 16-year-old Ronan Keating was the youngest member to join [[Boyzone]]. Keating, [[Keith Duffy]], Richard Rock, [[Shane Lynch]], Mark Walton and [[Stephen Gately]] were chosen as Boyzone members. Walton and Rock would later leave the group before being replaced by [[Michael Graham (singer)|Michael Graham]]. The group performed in various clubs and pubs before being signed by [[PolyGram]] in 1994 and released a cover version of "[[Working My Way Back to You]]" by [[The Four Seasons (band)|The Four Seasons]]. The group also released a cover version of "[[Love Me for a Reason]]" which would peak on several charts. In 1998, his mother died at the age of 51<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/1177815.stm |title=Baby girl for Ronan |work=BBC News |date=19 February 2001 |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref> from breast cancer,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/article1042279.ece?token=null&offset=24&page=3|title=Ireland: Interview: Ciaran Byrne meets Ronan Keating|work=The Times|access-date=27 September 2014}}</ref> resulting in the establishment of the Marie Keating Foundation.

At the age of 21 years, Keating married [[Yvonne Connolly]] in April 1998 and together, the couple have three children: Jack (born 15 March 1998), Marie (born 18 February 2001) and Ali (born 7 September 2005). The couple are now divorced after Keating had an affair.<ref>{{cite news|last=Malleta |first=Czarina |title=Ronan Keating Left 'Devastated' After Estranged Wife Yvonne Finds New Love |url=http://au.ibtimes.com/articles/372872/20120813/ronan-keating-devastated-yvonne-finds-new-love.htm |access-date=30 November 2013 |newspaper=International Business Times |date=13 August 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131204052059/http://au.ibtimes.com/articles/372872/20120813/ronan-keating-devastated-yvonne-finds-new-love.htm |archive-date=4 December 2013}}</ref>

From 1994 to 1999, Boyzone released 17 singles, 3 studio albums and 1 compilation album before disbanding in 2000 selling 27 million records and 3 million records from 4 singles, 1 compilation and studio album since their reformation.

=== 1999–2006: ''Ronan'', ''Destination'', ''Turn It On'' and ''Bring You Home''  ===
In 1999, while still a member of Boyzone, Keating recorded a version of "[[When You Say Nothing at All]]" for the motion picture ''[[Notting Hill (film)|Notting Hill]]''. The single peaked at number one in the UK charts, leading to a successful solo career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.timesofmalta.com/articles/view/20100205/local/keating |title=Ronan Keating live in Malta on April 3 |work=The Times|location=Malta |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref> Keating released his debut solo album, titled ''[[Ronan (album)|Ronan]]'', in 2000, which peaked at [[List of number-one albums (UK)|number one]] in the [[UK Albums Chart]]. The album was given several negative reviews, although it sold over 750,000 copies and became one of the top selling albums of the year in the United Kingdom. It debuted at number one on the [[UK Albums Chart]], and has been certified four-times platinum by the [[British Phonographic Industry]] for sales of 1.2 million copies. In the singer's native Ireland, the album debuted at number two. The album also became a commercial success in other European countries, where it charted within the top ten of eight countries. In 2001, ''Ronan'' was certified two-times platinum by the [[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry]] for shipments of two million copies inside Europe.<ref>{{cite web|title=IFPI Platinum Europe Awards – 2001 |url=http://www.ifpi.org/content/section_news/plat2001.html |publisher=[[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry]] |access-date=9 April 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090422231201/http://www.ifpi.org/content/section_news/plat2001.html |archive-date=22 April 2009}}</ref>
[[File:RonanKeating.jpg|thumb|upright=0.8|Keating at the 2002 MTVAsia Awards pre-party in Singapore]]
The album produced four UK and Irish top-ten singles: "[[When You Say Nothing at All#Ronan Keating|When You Say Nothing at All]]" (originally recorded for the soundtrack of the 1999 film ''[[Notting Hill (film)|Notting Hill]]''), "[[Life Is a Rollercoaster]]", "[[The Way You Make Me Feel (Ronan Keating song)|The Way You Make Me Feel]]", and "[[Lovin' Each Day]]", the latter of which was featured on the re-release edition of the album, and later on Keating's second album, ''[[Destination (Ronan Keating album)|Destination]]'' (2002). Also the song "Lovin each day" is from a movie "Summer catch".

After the successful debut album ''Ronan'' Keating continued on with his solo career and has since released four more studio albums: ''[[Destination (Ronan Keating album)|Destination]]'' (2002), ''[[Turn It On]]'' (2003), ''[[10 Years of Hits]]'' (2004) and ''[[Bring You Home]]'' (2006), four of which peaked at number one in the [[UK Albums Chart]]. Keating collaborated with several major stars, including [[Elton John]], [[Lulu (singer)|Lulu]], [[LeAnn Rimes]] and the [[Bee Gees]], for these albums.

Keating and [[Paul Brady]] co-wrote the 2001 hit single "[[The Long Goodbye (Brooks & Dunn song)|The Long Goodbye]]",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.polydor.co.uk/group_artists.php?id=40 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130505045423/http://www.polydor.co.uk/group_artists.php?id=40 |url-status=dead |archive-date=5 May 2013 |title=Ronan Keating – Artists – Polydor Records |publisher=Polydor.co.uk |access-date=12 April 2012 }}</ref> one of Keating's most loved hits amongst fans and a huge hit for [[Brooks & Dunn]] in the United States. Keating and Brady won the "BMI European song-writing award" for the single. In 2003, Keating was named [[Rear of the Year]] – an award given to celebrities with a notable [[buttocks|posterior]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rearoftheyearcompetition.com/history.html |title=Rear of the Year – History |publisher=Rearoftheyearcompetition.com |access-date=12 April 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121201020443/http://www.rearoftheyearcompetition.com/history.html |archive-date= 1 December 2012}}</ref> In October 2007, Keating was listed in the ''[[Guinness Book of Records]]'' for being the only artist ever to have 30 consecutive top 10 singles in the UK chart, beating the likes of [[Elvis Presley]]. Keating also embarked on two world tours, won the [[Ivor Novello]] and BMI songwriting awards, released an autobiography, had a role as ambassador for [[Christian Aid]] and had sales in excess of 22 million albums worldwide. In 2007 he released two fragrances, based on Sicilian lemon, lavender and Clary sage smells. All proceeds go to the Marie Keating foundation.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}

Keating performed at the [[2006 FIFA World Cup]] opening party at the [[Brandenburg Gate]] in Berlin, Germany, in front of an audience of nearly 250,000 people, and posed [[Nudity|nude]] for [[Cosmopolitan (magazine)|Cosmopolitan]] magazine's 10 Years of Naked Centrefolds. On 9 May 2007, Keating became the first international number-one selling foreign artist to perform a concert in [[Phnom Penh]], Cambodia. Keating also performed at the Olympic Stadium Indoor Arena. Keating has performed duets with Elton John at Madison Garden in New York and has sung for the Pope twice and performed for [[Charles, Prince of Wales|Prince Charles]] at the Prince's Trust 30th birthday, which took place at the Tower of London. He performed on stage for the Swedish Royal family at Crown Princess Victoria's birthday celebrations, and at the opening ceremony of the 19th European Athletics Championships in [[Gothenburg]].

===2007–2010: Boyzone reformation, ''Songs for My Mother'' and ''Winter Songs'' ===
On 5 November 2007, Keating confirmed that [[Boyzone]] would reunite for a special appearance on the BBC's annual fundraiser, [[Children in Need]], performing a medley of hits, although had not yet commented on the possibility of a new tour or album. Soon, they went on a 29-date tour, taking in cities such as Cardiff, Newcastle, Liverpool, London at the O2 Arena and Wembley, Manchester, Birmingham, Glasgow, Aberdeen, Sheffield, Newcastle, Nottingham, Edinburgh Castle and the RDS in Dublin. A greatest hits collection was released and two new singles in 2008 selling 700,000 copies.

[[Stephen Gately]], a close friend and bandmate of Keating, died on 10 October 2009 of a [[pulmonary edema]].

In 2009, Keating co-wrote the song "[[Believe Again (Niels Brinck song)|Believe Again]]" sung by [[Niels Brinck]], who won the [[Dansk Melodi Grand Prix]] and represented Denmark in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2009]] in Moscow where it qualified for the final from the second semi-final and finished in 13th place with 74 points.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dr.dk/melodigrandprix/solister/2009/brinck.htm |title=Brinck – dr.dk/Melodi Grand Prix |publisher=Dr.dk |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref> Following the success of the album ''[[Songs for My Mother]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicloversgroup.com/ronan-keating-to-release-fifth-solo-album-songs-for-my-mother/ |title=Ronan Keating To Release Fifth Solo Album 'Songs for My Mother' |publisher=Musicloversgroup.com |date=12 February 2009 |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref> he has recorded another entitled ''Winter Songs''. Teaming up again with ''Songs for My Mother'' producer Steve Lipson, Keating pulled together eleven songs that evoked memories of winter and Christmas for him. ''Winter Songs'' is a mix of traditional and modern festive classics, including tracks from artists such as [[Simon & Garfunkel]], [[Joni Mitchell]] and [[Sugarland]] along with perennials "[[Silent Night]]" and "[[Have Yourself a Merry Little Christmas]]". The album also includes two and new songs, "It's Only Christmas" and "Scars".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/reviews/3rgw |title=Music – Review of Ronan Keating – Winter Songs |publisher=BBC |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref>

In March 2010 the new [[Boyzone]] album "Brother" was released and went to number one in the UK and Ireland selling 500,000 copies with very little promotion to add to the singles which sold 175,000 copies.

On 12 November 2010, Keating released his seventh studio album, entitled ''[[Duet (Ronan Keating album)|Duet]]'', in Australia and New Zealand.<ref name="auspop">{{cite web|url=http://auspop.blogspot.com/2010/10/ronans-oz-exclusive.html |title=Auspop: Ronan'S Oz Exclusive |publisher=Auspop.blogspot.com |date=25 October 2010 |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref> "[[Believe Again (Niels Brinck song)|Believe Again]]" was released as the [[lead single]] from the album and features guest vocals from Australian singer, [[Paulini]]

==Charity work==
In October 2005 shortly after joining [[Christian Aid]] and the Trade Justice Campaign in 2004, Keating went to Rome where he was appointed as a UN Goodwill Ambassador via the Food and Agricultural Organisation.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.un.org/sg/mop/gwa.shtml |title=United Nations Messengers of Peace |publisher=United Nations |date=15 September 2007 |access-date=12 April 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120411151431/http://www.un.org/sg/mop/gwa.shtml |archive-date=11 April 2012}}</ref>

Keating ran in the [[London Marathon]] on 13 April 2008 to raise money for Cancer Research, with a personal best time of 3hr 59min.<ref>[http://info.cancerresearchuk.org/news/celebritynoticeboard/2008/index.htm ]{{dead link|date=April 2012}}</ref>{{dead link|date=January 2021}} He has also walked the length of Ireland twice, each time raising money for the Marie Keating Foundation, now linked to Cancer Research in the UK. Each year the foundation holds charity balls and various events, raising funds to keep their mobile units on the road.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}

In 2009, Keating climbed [[Mount Kilimanjaro]] to raise money for [[Comic Relief]]; joining him were [[Gary Barlow]], [[Chris Moyles]], [[Ben Shephard]], [[Kimberley Walsh]], [[Denise van Outen]], [[Fearne Cotton]], [[Alesha Dixon]] and [[Cheryl Cole]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/tvandradio/4904802/Celebrities-climbing-Mount-Kilimanjaro-finish-four-hour-trek.html |title=Celebrities climbing Mount Kilimanjaro finish four-hour trek |newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph]] |access-date=12 April 2012 |location=London |date=2 March 2009}}</ref>

In June 2009 Keating received a Special Achievement Award from Cancer Research UK – he was singled out for his work with them since 2006. He, along with the Marie Keating Charity, has helped to raise over [[pound sterling|£]]1.7 million for the UK charity. This money is used to fund three special mobile units which raise awareness throughout the UK.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ronankeating.com/news.php?item=37 |title=News |publisher=Ronan Keating |access-date=12 April 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426044233/http://www.ronankeating.com/news.php?item=37 |archive-date=26 April 2012}}</ref>

In October 2009 he pulled out of the [[Chicago Marathon]] due to the sudden death of his bandmate [[Stephen Gately]]; he had intended to run in memory of his mother.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://chicagomarathon.camony.com/ |title=Chicago Marathon |publisher=Chicagomarathon.camony.com |access-date=12 April 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426044233/http://chicagomarathon.camony.com/ |archive-date=26 April 2012 }}</ref>

In September 2011 he swam the Irish sea 90&nbsp;km stretch from [[Dublin]] to [[Holyhead]] with a team of celebrities to raise money for cancer charities and set a new Guinness World Record for open water swimming with an 11-member celebrity team. They raised £1 million for Cancer Research Britain and the Marie Keating Foundation.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-wales-north-west-wales-14738793 |title=Irish Sea Swim: Ronan Keating reaches Dublin |work=BBC News|date=1 September 2011 |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref>

==Other work==
===Presenting===
On 3 May 1997, Keating presented the [[Eurovision Song Contest 1997|Eurovision Song Contest]] along with [[Carrie Crowley]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/history/by-year/contest?event=312 |title=Eurovision Song Contest 1997 &#124; Eurovision Song Contest – Baku 2012 |publisher=Eurovision.tv |access-date=12 April 2012}}</ref> He presented the [[MTV Europe Music Awards]] in [[1997 MTV Europe Music Awards|1997]] and [[1999 MTV Europe Music Awards|1999]], and co-presented the [[MTV Asia Awards]] in 2002 with [[Mandy Moore]]. He also hosted [[Miss World 1998]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.altiusdirectory.com/Entertainment/miss-world-hosts.php |title=Miss World Hosts List, Miss World 2011 Host, List of Miss World Hosts |publisher=Altiusdirectory.com |access-date=12 April 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100828224148/http://www.altiusdirectory.com/Entertainment/miss-world-hosts.php |archive-date=28 August 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref> That same year, Keating co-hosted the [[Royal Variety Performance]] alongside [[Ulrika Jonsson]] at the [[Lyceum Theatre, London|Lyceum Theatre]] in London.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/230154.stm|title=Spicy Royal Variety Performance|work=BBC News|date=8 December 1998|access-date=25 November 2017}}</ref>

In 1999, Keating presented all nine episodes of the BBC1 talent show ''Get Your Act Together''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ukgameshows.com/ukgs/Get_Your_Act_Together_(1)|title=Get Your Act Together (1) - UKGameshows|website=www.ukgameshows.com|access-date=17 September 2020}}</ref>

On 20 April 2009, Keating guest co-hosted ''[[The Morning Show (TV program)|The Morning Show]]'' in Australia alongside [[Kylie Gillies]], while the show's regular male co-host [[Larry Emdur]] was on holiday.<ref>[http://www.accessallareas.net.au/music_news/EkFlFlEFllZhkkvzYu.php] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090404125830/http://www.accessallareas.net.au/music_news/EkFlFlEFllZhkkvzYu.php |date=4 April 2009 }}</ref>
In addition, he has stood in for [[Steve Wright (DJ)|Steve Wright]] on his ''Sunday Love Songs'' on [[BBC Radio 2]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ronankeating.forums.umusic.co.uk/t/4173.aspx |title=Radio 2 Sunday love songs sept 13th 9:05-11am – Ronan Keating Official Forum |publisher=Ronankeating.forums.umusic.co.uk |access-date=12 April 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426044234/http://ronankeating.forums.umusic.co.uk/t/4173.aspx |archive-date=26 April 2012}}</ref> He currently presents a Sunday request show on [[Magic 105.4 FM]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://planetradio.co.uk/magic/|title=Magic Radio - More of the Songs You Love|website=Magic Radio}}</ref>

In a section for the [[Jeremy Clarkson]] 2009 DVD ''Duel'', Keating was invited to take part in an off-road race to find "the fastest off-road celebrity", along with newsreader Kate Silverton, Rugby player Matt Dawson, and Clarkson.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://cars.uk.msn.com/features/jeremy-clarkson/articles.aspx?cp-documentid=150466722 |title=Jeremy Clarkson's new DVD: CLARKSON – DUEL – Jeremy Clarkson – Features – MSN Cars – MSN UK |publisher=MSN |date=16 November 2011 |access-date=12 April 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110723021216/http://cars.uk.msn.com/features/jeremy-clarkson/articles.aspx?cp-documentid=150466722 |archive-date=23 July 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He came third overall, after being forced to abandon the car with only 20 seconds left to complete the course to disarm the on-board time bomb.

In 2010, Keating became a judge and mentor on the Australian version of ''[[The X Factor (Australian TV series)|The X Factor]]'', joining [[Guy Sebastian]], [[Natalie Imbruglia]], and [[Kyle Sandilands]] for the [[The X Factor (Australia season 2)|second season]] of the show. During the second season, Keating mentored the Over 25s category and his fellow Boyzone bandmembers helped him in the judges' house selection of the contest. He eventually became the winning mentor with his contestant [[Altiyan Childs]]. Keating went on to mentor the Groups for two consecutive years, notably with [[The Collective (band)|The Collective]] finishing third in the [[The X Factor (Australia season 4)|fourth season]]. For the [[The X Factor (Australia season 5)|fifth season]], he was assigned the Boys category and made series history; both his acts, [[Taylor Henderson]] and [[Jai Waetford]], reached the grand-finals and finished runner-up and third place respectively. On the [[The X Factor (Australia season 6)|sixth season]], Keating became the winning mentor for a second time with [[Marlisa Punzalan]], the youngest contestant and first act from the Girls category to win. On 25 April 2015, it was announced that Keating would not be returning for the seventh season of ''The X Factor''.<ref>{{cite news|last=Moran|first=Jonathon|url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/sydney-confidential/no-more-ronan-for-x-factor/story-fni0cvc9-1227320554705|title=No more Ronan for X Factor|work=[[The Daily Telegraph (Sydney)|The Daily Telegraph]]|date=25 April 2015|access-date=26 April 2015}}</ref>

On Christmas Eve 2015, during the broadcast of [[Carols by Candlelight]] on [[Channel 9 Australia]], it was announced that Keating would be returning to Australian TV screens in 2016 as a coach on the fifth season of ''[[The Voice Australia]]'', joining other coaches [[Jessie J]], [[Delta Goodrem]] and [[The Madden Brothers]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.9jumpin.com.au/show/thevoice/latest/2016/january/2016-coaches/|title=Your 2016 Coaches Announced!|work=[[9Jumpin]]|date=24 December 2015|access-date=28 December 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151224232155/http://www.9jumpin.com.au/show/thevoice/latest/2016/january/2016-coaches/|archive-date=24 December 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In June 2015 he filled in as a guest anchor on the morning slot of UK radio station Magic FM.<ref>{{cite news | url= http://www.evoke.ie/showbiz/ronan-keating-radio-uk-presenter/ | website=Evoke.ie | title= Ronan turns DJ as he and fiancee Storm kick-off wedding planning | date= 16 June 2015 | access-date= 17 June 2015 | archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20150617143737/http://www.evoke.ie/showbiz/ronan-keating-radio-uk-presenter/ | archive-date= 17 June 2015 | url-status=dead | df= dmy-all }}</ref>

In September 2017, Keating was signed to co-host Magic Breakfast alongside Harriet Scott on [[Magic (UK radio station)|Magic Radio]] running Monday to Fridays, 6{{nbsp}}am till 10{{nbsp}}am. Keating also presents his own show on Saturday evenings 5pm till 7pm with Disney Plus.<ref>[http://www.bauermedia.co.uk/newsroom/press/magic-radio-sign-ronan-keating-to-breakfast Magic Radio Sign Ronan Keating to Breakfast.] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180704213549/http://www.bauermedia.co.uk/newsroom/press/magic-radio-sign-ronan-keating-to-breakfast |date=4 July 2018 }} ''magic.co.uk'' Retrieved 5 September 2017</ref>

From 2017 to 2021, Keating guest-presented episodes of ''[[The One Show]]'', before becoming a main presenter in May 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-56925454|title=Ronan Keating and Jermaine Jenas join The One Show permanently|website=bbc.co.uk/news|access-date=9 May 2021}}</ref>

On 19 November 2020, Keating was a guest panellist on the [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]] Daytime show, ''[[Loose Women]]'', and made history as being part of the first all male panel in the show's 21 year history.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Warner|first=Sam|date=19 November 2020|title=Loose Women welcomes all-male panel for first time in show's history|url=https://www.digitalspy.com/tv/reality-tv/a34725288/loose-women-men-all-male-panel/|access-date=19 November 2020|website=Digital Spy|language=en-GB}}</ref>

===Acting===
Keating unsuccessfully auditioned for a role in ''[[The Hobbit (film series)|The Hobbit]]''. The film is based on the [[J. R. R. Tolkien]] [[The Hobbit|novel of the same name]].<ref>[http://www.deccanchronicle.com/entertainment/boyzone-ronan-keating-wants-pursue-acting-917]  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100429092024/http://www.deccanchronicle.com/entertainment/boyzone-ronan-keating-wants-pursue-acting-917 |date=29 April 2010 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news | title = Keating starting to look the part after 'Hobbit' rejection | url = http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/film-cinema/keating-starting-to-look-the-part-after-hobbit-rejection-3117702.html | work = The Independent | author =  Louise Hogan | date = 24 May 2012 }}</ref>

In April 2011, Keating began working on ''[[Goddess (2013 film)|Goddess]]'', a 2013 romantic comedy film co-starring British actress [[Laura Michelle Kelly]]. The film was directed by [[Mark Lamprell]], who wrote ''[[Babe: Pig in the City]]''. In November 2014, he took over the part of 'Guy' in ''[[Once (musical)|Once]]'' from David Hunter, at the [[Phoenix Theatre (London)|Phoenix Theatre]] in London's West End, performing the role until March 2015. He was be the fourth principal 'Guy' in the West End production, and the first Irishman to have played the role since [[Glen Hansard]] in the [[Academy Award for Best Original Song|Oscar-winning]] original film, on which the musical is based.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-27730136|title= Ronan Keating to make West End debut in Once|date=6 June 2014|work=BBC News|access-date=6 June 2014}}</ref> His performances in ''Once'' received positive reviews from critics.<ref>{{cite news|url =https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-30205508|title=Ronan Keating: West End debut in Once 'liberating'|date=26 November 2014|work=BBC News|access-date=27 November 2014}}</ref>

In 2017, Keating starred alongside [[Jessica Marais]] in the fourth season of the Australian drama, ''[[Love Child (TV series)|Love Child]]''.
The same year, he also starred as Harold Le Druillenec, brother of [[Louisa Gould]] in the film ''[[Another Mother's Son]]'', based on a true story from the [[German_occupation_of_the_Channel_Islands|German occupation of the Channel Islands.]]

==Feuds==
===Louis Walsh===
When Keating announced he wanted to take a break from Boyzone, [[Louis Walsh]] continued to manage his career. Keating achieved a number one hit with "[[Life is a Rollercoaster]]" in 2000 while his album sold 4.4 million copies. Keating and Walsh later agreed to part company and then had a bitter falling out.<ref>{{cite news | url= http://www.rte.ie/arts/2006/0529/keatingr.html | title= RTÉ.ie Entertainment: Keating talks about feud with Walsh
| publisher= Raidió Teilifís Éireann | access-date= 4 October 2009 | date= 29 May 2006 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://showbiz.sky.com/Ronans-Feud-With-Louis-Walsh |title=Ronan's Feud With Louis Walsh |publisher=Sky Showbiz |access-date=4 October 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110807055812/http://showbiz.sky.com/Ronans-Feud-With-Louis-Walsh |archive-date=7 August 2011}}</ref>
Walsh told the press: "[Keating] wasn't the most talented one – he's not a great singer and he's got no personality."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebiographychannel.co.uk/biographies/louis-walsh/biography.html |title=Biography – Louis Walsh on Bio |publisher=Thebiographychannel.co.uk |access-date=12 April 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120104193404/http://www.thebiographychannel.co.uk/biographies/louis-walsh/biography.html |archive-date=4 January 2012}}</ref>
Keating later told ''Closer'' magazine: "That man absolutely tried to ruin me and if he thinks we can ever hug and make up he can forget it. I haven't heard from him in three years and I wouldn't have a problem if I never saw him again. He's not a nice character."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.startrip.tv/2006/05/ronan_keating_l.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426044233/http://www.startrip.tv/2006/05/ronan_keating_l.html|url-status=dead|title=Star Trip: Ronan Keating: "Louis Walsh tried to ruin me!"<!-- Bot generated title -->|archive-date=26 April 2012}}</ref> 

The feud between the two men had apparently ended by March 2008,<ref>{{cite web
| url= http://www.contactmusic.com/news.nsf/story/keating-ends-walsh-feud_1063152 |title= Boyzone – Keating Ends Walsh Feud – Contactmusic News |publisher= Contact Music
|access-date=4 October 2009 }}</ref> after which Keating helped in selecting the finalists in the groups category in ''[[The X Factor (UK series 6)|The X Factor]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thetalentzone.co.uk/xfactor2009/judgeshomes/groups.html |title=Louis Walsh 2009 X Factor Judges House: Louis House In Italy |publisher=Thetalentzone.co.uk |access-date=12 April 2012 |archive-date=8 November 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101108051758/http://thetalentzone.co.uk/xfactor2009/judgeshomes/groups.html |url-status=dead }}</ref> However, by the time Boyzone celebrated their 20th anniversary in 2013, Walsh had stopped managing the group, claiming his tenure as manager had ended "badly".{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}

===Thom Yorke===
Keating criticised [[Radiohead]] frontman [[Thom Yorke]] several times in the press throughout late 2008 and early 2009, calling him a "muppet" and an "idiot" due to Yorke allegedly ignoring Keating at a hotel.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.monstersandcritics.com/people/news/article_1466069.php/Ronan_Keating_blasts_Yorke |title=Monsters and Critics |access-date=23 May 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090526050942/http://www.monstersandcritics.com/people/news/article_1466069.php/Ronan_Keating_blasts_Yorke |archive-date=26 May 2009}}</ref> Shortly after Keating's remarks, [[Kanye West]] and [[Miley Cyrus]] also complained about Yorke's alleged rudeness. In response to the accusations, Yorke left a blog on Radiohead's official site for those who were not among "those people I have managed to offend by doing nothing."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.eonline.com/news/104279/radiohead-s-thom-yorke-schools-miley-cyrus-kanye-west-on-humility/ |title=Radiohead's Thom Yorke Schools Miley Cyrus & Kanye West on Humility |publisher=eonline.com |date=13 March 2009 |access-date=8 October 2015}}</ref>
