'''State Champs''' (stylized sometimes as '''STATE CHAMPS''') is an American rock band from [[Albany, New York]], formed in 2010. They are currently signed to [[Pure Noise Records]] and have released three EPs and three full-length albums. They released an acoustic EP titled ''The Acoustic Things'' in October 2014 and their second full-length album ''[[Around the World and Back]]'' one year later, in October 2015. In May 2017, they released a deluxe version of their album ''[[Around the World and Back]]'', along with a DVD of footage from their last few tours.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/state_champs_announce_around_the_world_and_back_deluxe_edition_with_live_dv |title=State Champs announce 'Around The World And Back' deluxe edition with DVD and new songs |date=March 30, 2017 |accessdate=March 31, 2017 |author=Ralph, Caitlyn|website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] }}</ref> Their 2013 full-length, ''[[The Finer Things (album)|The Finer Things]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3465041|title=State Champs - The Finer Things|work=AbsolutePunk.net|accessdate=July 16, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/reviews/entry/state_champs_the_finer_things|title=State Champs - The Finer Things|work=Alternative Press|accessdate=July 16, 2015|date=2013-10-04}}</ref><ref>Review of ''The Finer Things'', ''[[Kerrang!]]'', October 12, 2013, p. 52.</ref> debuted at number 2 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Heatseekers Albums chart.<ref name="auto">[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/state-champs-mn0003190264/awards Charts], Allmusic</ref> Their third album, ''[[Living Proof (State Champs album)|Living Proof]]'', was released on June 15, 2018.

==History==

===Formation and early releases (2010–2012)===
State Champs formed in the spring of 2010 in [[Albany, New York]]<ref name=AMbio/> by founding members lead guitarist Tyler Szalkowski and lead singer Derek DiScanio. Shortly after forming, they self-released ''EP 2010'' in August,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/ep-2010-ep/id388451689|title=Ep - 2010 - Ep by State Champs|work=iTunes|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref> which was followed by their second self-released EP, ''Apparently, I’m Nothing'', in January 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2130581|title=Featured Stream: State Champs|work=AbsolutePunk.net|author=Ilvonen, Keagan |date=January 18, 2011|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref> This EP contained several new songs and ''2010 EP'' in its entirety. In July, the band toured with The Tired and True and Call It Fiction.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2338822|title=State Champs Tour Dates|work=AbsolutePunk.net|author=Nassiff, Thomas |date=June 27, 2011|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref> In April 2012, interest in a demo of "Critical" helped the band gain a recording contract, a management company and a booking agency.<ref name=Walker45>Walker 2015, p. 45</ref> On April 19, it was announced that the band signed to [[Pure Noise Records|Pure Noise]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=2714802|title=State Champs Sign To Pure Noise Records|work=AbsolutePunk.net|author=Ilvonen, Keagan |date=April 19, 2012|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref> In mid-June, the group recorded their next EP with Jay Maas at Getaway Recording Studio.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/state_champs_pure_noise_records_recording_with_jay_maas_later_this_month|title=State Champs (Pure Noise Records) recording with Jay Maas later this month|work=Alternative Press|publisher=Alternative Press Magazine, Inc|author=Yancey, Bryne|date=June 4, 2012|accessdate=December 19, 2017}}</ref>

===''The Finer Things'' and ''The Acoustic Things'' (2013–2015)===
Originally, the band was supposed to support [[Chunk! No, Captain Chunk!]] on their Pardon My French Tour in March and April 2013;<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.punknews.org/article/50365/tours-chunk-no-captain-chunk-handguns-state-champs-city-lights|title=Tours: Chunk! No, Captain Chunk! / Handguns / State Champs / City Lights|work=Punknews.org|author=Paul, Aubin|date=January 20, 2013|accessdate=March 3, 2016}}</ref> instead Chunk! No, Captain Chunk! went on tour with [[A Day to Remember]], resulting in State Champs supporting Handguns on their Pardon My Angst tour in March.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3024262|title=Handguns Announce The "Pardon My Angst" Tour|work=AbsolutePunk.net|author=Ilvonen, Keagan |date=January 25, 2013|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref> The band finished recording their debut full-length with producer Sam Pura at The Panda Studios and co-producer and former [[New Found Glory]] guitarist [[Steve Klein (musician)|Steve Klein]] in [[Fremont, CA]] in May 2013.<ref>[http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3173701 State Champs finish recording full length], AbsolutePunk</ref> The group supported [[New Found Glory]] on their west coast tour in June,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/state_champs_added_to_new_found_glory_sticks_and_stones_west_coast_dates_wi|title=State Champs added to New Found Glory 'Sticks And Stones' west coast dates with Cartel|work=Alternative Press|publisher=Alternative Press Magazine, Inc|author=Kraus, Brian|date=March 20, 2013|accessdate=December 19, 2017}}</ref> before supporting [[Cartel (band)|Cartel]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/state_champs_added_to_cartel_tour_dates|title=State Champs added to Cartel tour dates|work=Alternative Press|publisher=Alternative Press Magazine, Inc|author=Kraus, Brian|date=March 20, 2013|accessdate=December 19, 2017}}</ref>

Their debut full-length album, ''The Finer Things'', was released on October 8, 2013.<ref>[http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3332291 The Finer Things release announcement], AbsolutePunk</ref> It debuted at #2 on the [[Billboard magazine|Billboard]] Heatseekers chart,<ref name="auto"/> selling a little over 3,100 copies in the first week of release. To support the album, the band supported [[Motion City Soundtrack]] and [[Bayside (band)|Bayside]] on a fall tour in North America. The band spent a majority of 2014 touring in support of ''The Finer Things''. The band started their busy 2014 schedule in March with their first full United States tour, opening for [[We Are The In Crowd]], [[William Beckett (singer)|William Becket]], [[Set It Off (band)|Set It Off]] and Candy Hearts.<ref>[http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3566631 We Are The In Crowd Tour Dates], AbsolutePunk</ref> Shortly after completing this tour, the band headed on tour overseas for the first time to support [[pop-punk]] band [[The Wonder Years (band)|The Wonder Years]] and [[A Loss for Words]]. The tour routed throughout the UK and mainland Europe.<ref>[http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3578631 State Champs UK tour with The Wonder Years], AbsolutePunk</ref>

The band performed on the ''Kevin Says'' stage for the 2014 installment of the [[Vans Warped Tour]] occasionally playing the main ''Kia Soul'' stage.<ref>[http://vanswarpedtour.com/post/2014-warped-stage-guide Warped Tour 2014 Stage Guide] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150823033927/http://vanswarpedtour.com/post/2014-warped-stage-guide |date=2015-08-23 }}, VansWarpedTour.com</ref> During the tour, it was announced that the band would also be co-headlining the 2014 [[Pure Noise Records|Pure Noise Records Tour]] with [[Handguns (band)|Handguns]], with support from [[Forever Came Calling]], [[Front Porch Step]], Heart to Heart and Brigades.<ref>[http://purenoise.net/news/state-champs-front-porch-step-announced-for-pure-noise-tour 2014 Pure Noise Tour Announcement], PureNoise.net</ref> On October 7, 2014, the band released an acoustic EP titled ''The Acoustic Things'' which featured acoustic versions of five tracks from ''The Finer Things'' as well as two new songs.<ref>[http://altpress.com/features/entry/state_champs_the_acoustic_things_ep_premiere State Champs ‘The Acoustic Things’ EP premiere], AltPress.com</ref>

===''Around the World and Back'' (2015–2017)===
The band opened for [[All Time Low]] on their ''Future Hearts'' tour in spring 2015, and then opened for [[5 Seconds of Summer]]'s [[Rock Out with Your Socks Out Tour|''Rock Out With Your Socks Out Tour'']] in June 2015 in Australia and New Zealand. On July 15, the band announced their second album, ''Around the World and Back'', would be released on October 16;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/state_champs_announce_new_album_with_secrets_single|title=State Champs announce new album with "Secrets" single - Alternative Press|work=Alternative Press|accessdate=July 16, 2015|date=2015-07-16}}</ref> by November, the album had reached number 30 on the US ''Billboard'' Top 200 albums chart.<ref name="Ciccone">{{Cite journal |last=Ciccone |first=Rachael |date=March 9, 2016 |title=An Interview with State Champs: They've Got More Secrets Than You'll Ever Know |journal=[[The Aquarian Weekly]] |url=http://www.theaquarian.com/2016/03/09/an-interview-with-state-champs-theyve-got-more-secrets-than-youll-ever-know/ |accessdate=March 12, 2016 }}</ref> The band began a co-headlining world tour in the UK with [[Neck Deep]] in February 2016, with support from [[Creeper (band)|Creeper]] and [[Light Years (band)|Light Years]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/neck-deep-state-champs-just-announced-an-amazing-tour|title=Neck Deep + State Champs Just Announced An Amazing Tour|work=Rock Sound Magazine|author=Biddulph, Andy |date=October 5, 2015|accessdate=November 24, 2015}}</ref>  The tour continued in the US and Canada in February and March with support from [[Knuckle Puck]] and [[Like Pacific]]. Official music videos were produced for two of the album's singles, "All You Are Is History" and "Secrets".<ref name="Ciccone"/>

While touring with [[Neck Deep]] in March, both bands also appeared on the cover of that month's issue of [[Alternative Press Magazine]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/magazine/issue/333_neck_deep_state_champs|website=Alternative Press|title=#333 Neck Deep & State Champs|date=2016-02-16}}</ref> After their co-headlining US tour, the band then supported [[A Day to Remember]] with [[Parkway Drive]] on the Just Some Shows tour in the US during the month of May 2016.<ref>{{cite web|author=Emily|title=A Day to Remember Announce U.S. Shows With Parkway Drive and State Champs|url=http://www.kerrang.com/40167/a-day-to-remember-announce-u-s-shows-with-parkway-drive-and-state-champs/|website=Kerrang}}</ref>

It was then announced that State Champs would once again be joining the 2016 [[Vans Warped Tour]] for the entire length of the tour, mainly playing on the Journeys Right Foot Stage.<ref>{{cite web|title=90 Days to Warped Feature Band: State Champs|url=http://vanswarpedtour.com/2016/90-days-to-warped-feature-band-state-champs/|website=vanswarpedtour.com}}</ref>  Post Warped Tour, State Champs made an appearance at an intimate acoustic show with [[Sleeping with Sirens]] on August 25.<ref>{{cite web|author=Jen|title=Sleeping with Sirens and State Champs to Play Acoustic Show|url=http://www.kerrang.com/41415/sleeping-with-sirens-announce-acoustic-show/|website=kerrang.com}}</ref> The show took place at London's Union Chapel as a warm-up show prior to a festival appearance.  That same weekend they then opened the main stage at [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]], in England.<ref>{{cite web|last1=McDaid|first1=Heather|title=State Champs Give Reading Festival The Business|url=http://www.upsetmagazine.com/live-reviews/state-champs-give-reading-festival-business/|website=upsetmagazine.com}}</ref> In addition to touring and festival appearances, the band also contributed to "Rock Sound Presents: The Black Parade", a compilation of [[My Chemical Romance]] covers released in September 2016.  Within the album, they contributed a cover of the song "The Sharpest Lives."<ref>{{cite web|title=Introducing...'Rock Sound Presents: The Black Parade'|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/introducing...-rock-sound-presents-the-black-parade|website=rocksound.tv}}</ref>

On March 21, 2017, the band released the single, "Slow Burn" and announced the release of a deluxe edition on ''Around the World and Back'' on May 5, with two new songs ("Slow Burn" and "Hurry Up and Wait"), two live songs ("All You Are Is History" and "Elevated") and two acoustic songs ("Secrets" and "Losing Myself"). Also announced was the release of a full-length DVD, directed by Elliot Ingham. Member Tony Diaz took a small break from the band the return to Viper City with Recon.

===''Living Proof'' (2017–present)===
Around late November 2017, Derek posted a picture on his Instagram with the caption "just finished recording vocals on the last song of the new record". Following this during early March 2018, State Champs posted what looked to be a set for a music video on their Instagram account. On April 11, 2018, the album artwork and track listing for ''Living Proof'' was leaked on various music websites. Later, on April 19, they released the lead single for the album, called "Dead and Gone", and announced that ''Living Proof'' would be released on June 15, 2018. On May 6, the band released the second single from the album, "Crystal Ball". On May 24, the group premiered the third single, "Mine Is Gold", on ''Billboard''{{'}}s website. On June 15, the album was released. On March 26th 2020, the band announced that Tony Diaz would be stepping away from State Champs. 

==Style and influences==
State Champs' style has been described as [[pop punk]].<ref name=AMbio>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/state-champs-mn0003190264/biography|title=State Champs {{!}} Biography & History |work=AllMusic|author=Thomas, Fred|accessdate=December 3, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Gardner|first1=Ryan|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3465041 |title=State Champs - The Finer Things|publisher=Absolute Punk|accessdate=October 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Kraus |first=Brian |url=http://www.altpress.com/reviews/entry/state_champs_the_finer_things |title=State Champs - The Finer Things |publisher=Altpress.com |date=October 4, 2013|accessdate=October 28, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punknews.org/review/12234/state-champs-the-finer-things|title=State Champs - The Finer Things|work=Punknews.org|author=RENALDO69|date=October 30, 2013|accessdate=October 14, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rocksound.tv/reviews/read/state-champs-the-finer-things|title=State Champs - The Finer Things - Reviews|work=Rock Sound|author=Ritchie, Andy |date=October 4, 2013|accessdate=October 14, 2015}}</ref> Their influences include [[The Starting Line]], [[Blink-182]], [[Sum 41]], [[Fall Out Boy]], [[Green Day]], [[My Chemical Romance]], [[Taking Back Sunday]], [[All Time Low]], [[New Found Glory]], [[The All-American Rejects]], and [[Eighteen Visions]].

==Accolades==
''Around the World and Back'' was ranked at number 4 in ''Alternative Press''{{'}}s "10 Essential Records of 2015" list.<ref name=Hall96>Hall 2015, p. 96</ref> Mackenzie Hall of ''Alternative Press'' wrote that the band brought "everything you love about old-school pop punk with new-school heart."<ref name=Hall96/> The album was included at number 8 on ''Rock Sound''{{'}}s top 50 releases of 2015 list.<ref>Bird, ed. 2016, p. 28</ref> "If I'm Lucky" was nominated for Best Music Video and "Secrets" was nominated for Song Of The Year at the 2016 [[Alternative Press Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.altpress.com/aptv/video/and_the_nominees_for_the_2016_apmas_are|title=Here are the nominees for the 2016 AP Music Awards!|work=Alternative Press|author=Whitt, Cassie|date=March 18, 2016|accessdate=May 1, 2016}}</ref> State Champs won Best Breakthrough Band at the 2016 Alternative Press Music Awards. "Losing Myself" won the award for Best Music Video at the 2017 Alternative Press Music Awards.
