'''Juan Luis Londoño Arias''' (born 28 January 1994), known professionally as '''Maluma''', is a Colombian singer.

Born and raised in [[Medellín]], he developed an interest in music at a young age, and began recording songs at the age of 16. He released his debut album, ''[[Magia (Maluma album)|Magia]]'', a year later in 2012. However, his breakthrough album was 2015's ''[[Pretty Boy, Dirty Boy]]'', which led to successful collaborations with several artists. He released ''[[F.A.M.E. (Maluma album)|F.A.M.E.]]'' in 2018, to further commercial success. He followed it up with ''[[11:11 (Maluma album)|11:11]]'' in 2019, and ''[[Papi Juancho]]'', released without further announcement in 2020. His single "[[Hawái (song)|Hawái]]" reached number three on the [[Billboard Global 200|''Billboard'' Global 200]], and became the first number one single on the [[Billboard Global Excl. U.S.|''Billboard'' Global Excl. U.S.]] chart. With sales of over 18 million records (albums and singles), Maluma is one of the best-selling Latin music artists.<ref name="Maluma">{{cite web|url=https://www.deejay.it/articoli/maluma-lintervista-esclusiva-a-radio-deejay-amo-litalia-le-donne-sono-cosi-hot/|title=Maluma, l'intervista esclusiva a Radio DEEJAY: "Amo l'Italia. Le donne sono così hot"|access-date=21 January 2021|date=2 December 2020|publisher=[[Radio DeeJay]]|archive-url=https://archive.today/20210121074008/https://www.deejay.it/articoli/maluma-lintervista-esclusiva-a-radio-deejay-amo-litalia-le-donne-sono-cosi-hot/|archive-date=21 January 2021|language=it|quote=Per i fan della musica latina Maluma non ha bisogno di presentazioni: con 18 milioni di copie vendute tra singoli e album e streaming da record su Spotify, è riconosciuto in tutto il mondo come uno degli artisti di maggior successo ed influenza della musica latinoamericana urban.}}</ref> Musically, Maluma's songs have been described as [[reggaeton]], [[Latin trap]], and [[Pop music|pop]].

Maluma has a number of singles that have charted within the top 10 on ''[[Billboard charts|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Latin Songs]], including "[[Felices los 4]]", "[[Borró Cassette]]", and "[[Corazón (Maluma song)|Corazón]]". His collaborative efforts "[[Chantaje]]" with [[Shakira]] and "[[Medellín (song)|Medellín]]" with [[Madonna]] have reached the top of the Hot Latin Songs and the [[Dance Club Songs]] chart, respectively. He has worked with other international artists, such as [[Ricky Martin]], [[J Balvin]], and [[The Weeknd]]. Maluma has won a [[Latin Grammy Award]], an [[MTV Video Music Award]], two [[Latin American Music Awards]], and been nominated for a [[Grammy Award]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop or Urban Album|Best Latin Pop Album]].

== Early life ==
Maluma was born to Marlli Arias and Luis Londoño and grew up with an older sister, Manuela. He took an early interest in football which he started playing as a toddler through middle school, competing in the lower divisions in the teams [[Atletico nacional|Atletico Nacional]] and Equidad Sports Club. Additionally, he developed a keen interest in music during his early teenage years and was famed as a good singer in his high school. He graduated from Hontanares School in El Retiro where he was encouraged by many of his friends to enter local singing competitions.<ref name="BIOGRAPHY {{!}} Maluma">{{Cite web|url=https://maluma.online/biografia/?lang=en|title=BIOGRAPHY {{!}} Maluma|website=maluma.online|language=en-US|access-date=28 February 2018}}</ref>

He got his first musical break when he was fifteen after he composed, together with a close friend, a song titled "No quiero"; a year later Juan Parra, his uncle, offered him the opportunity to record it in a studio as his birthday present.<ref name="BIOGRAPHY {{!}} Maluma" /> After impressing a group of music producers, he was asked to choose a name that would be easy to remember and be accepted by followers of the urban genre. He chose the stage name ''Maluma,'' which is a combination of the first syllable of the names of his mother Marlli, his father Luis and his sister Manuela.<ref name="BIOGRAPHY {{!}} Maluma" />

== Career ==
=== 2010–2013: Beginnings and ''Magia'' ===
Maluma began his musical career in 2010, recording singles. After "Farandulera" became a radio hit, [[Sony Music]] and its subsidiary label [[List of Sony Music Entertainment labels#Other Sony Music national companies|Sony Music Colombia]] decided to sign the singer to record his first studio album.<ref name="bbinterview" /> The label then launched the next single, "Loco", which was inspired by "rampant and uncontrolled love".

In 2012, Maluma released his first [[studio album]], titled ''[[Magia (Maluma album)|Magia]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.eluniversal.com.co/farandula/maluma-un-artista-que-sabe-lo-que-quiere-170305|title=Maluma, un artista que sabe lo que quiere|date=8 September 2014|work=El Universal|location=Colombia|language=es|trans-title=Maluma, an Artist Who Knows What He Wants|access-date=12 September 2016}}</ref> The video for the single "Obsesión" was filmed at the [[Antioquia Department]] Railway Station and featured Colombian model Lina Posada, who had previously appeared in the music video for the song "[[Taboo (Don Omar song)|Taboo]]" (2011) by [[Don Omar]]. So far the video has more than 185 million views on [[YouTube]]. Other singles released from the album include "Miss Independent" and "Pasarla Bien", with "Miss Independent" reaching number two on the Colombian [[National-Report]] songs chart, becoming his first top five song on the chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://national-report.com/forms/init100s.php |title=National-Report Top Nacional Semana 31 |date=5 August 2012 |access-date=15 August 2014 |work=National Report |url-status=bot: unknown |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6EskdOHgm?url=http://national-report.com/forms/init100s.php |archive-date=5 March 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://national-report.com/forms/init100s.php |title=National-Report Top Nacional Semana 9 |date=3 March 2013 |access-date=15 August 2014 |work=National Report |url-status=bot: unknown |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6EskdOHgm?url=http://national-report.com/forms/init100s.php |archive-date=5 March 2013 }}</ref> Maluma was also nominated for a 2012 Shock Award as "Best New Artist".<ref name="buenamusica">{{cite web|title=Biografía de Maluma|url=http://www.buenamusica.com/maluma/biografia|access-date=14 August 2014|publisher=Buena Música|language=tr}}</ref><ref name=shock2012>{{cite web|url=http://bogota.oxigeno.fm/noticia/maluma-supera-las-siete-mil-descargas-y-es-nominado-en-los-premios-shock/20120907/nota/1757627.aspx|title=Maluma supera las siete mil descargas y es nominado en los premios Shock|work=Oxígeno|publisher=[[Grupo PRISA]]|date=7 September 2011|access-date=13 August 2014|language=es|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140819083020/http://bogota.oxigeno.fm/noticia/maluma-supera-las-siete-mil-descargas-y-es-nominado-en-los-premios-shock/20120907/nota/1757627.aspx|archive-date=19 August 2014}}</ref> The final two singles from his debut album, "Primer Amor" and "Miss Independent"; the latter was accompanied with a music video, recorded in [[Medellín]], gathering 48 million views on YouTube.<ref>{{Youtube|RnCOOTZ_fwQ|"Miss Independent"}}</ref>

[[File:Maluma en vivo..JPG|thumb|200px|Maluma performing in [[Bogotá]], Colombia in 2014]]
In 2013, "[[La Temperatura]]", featuring Puerto Rican singer Eli Palacios, was released as the first single from his upcoming mixtape, ''[[PB.DB The Mixtape]]''; the song reached number seven on the [[National-Report]] charts. It also became his first single to enter the US charts for the Latin audience by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''; it reached number 25 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Tropical Songs]] chart, number 8 on the [[Latin Pop Airplay]] songs chart, number 24 on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Latin Songs]] chart and number 4 on the [[Latin Rhythm Airplay]] songs chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Maluma – Chart history|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?page=124&f[0]=ts_chart_artistname%3AMaluma|work=Billboard|access-date=17 April 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Maluma – Chart history|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6184364/maluma/chart|work=Billboard|access-date=17 April 2017}}</ref>

=== 2014–2018: ''Pretty Boy, Dirty Boy'' and ''F.A.M.E.'' ===
In 2014, he released the music videos for the singles "[[La Curiosidad]]", "[[Carnaval (Maluma song)|Carnaval]]" and "Addicted".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.entretengo.com/musica/maluma-la-curiosidad-video-oficial.html|title=Maluma presenta el video de su canción 'La Curiosidad'|author=María Mónica |date=5 May 2014 |publisher=Entretengo |access-date=14 August 2014|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://latintrends.com/malumas-carnaval|title=Maluma's 'Carnaval' What a Merry-Go-Round!|date=5 February 2015|access-date=27 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tuteve.tv/noticia/espectaculos/241037/2014/08/13/maluma-presenta-videoclip-de-su-tema-addicted|title=Maluma presenta videoclip de su tema "Addicted"|publisher=Tuteve |access-date=14 August 2014|language=es}}</ref> He also collaborated with singer [[Elvis Crespo]], on the theme song "Olé Brazil" recorded for the [[2014 FIFA World Cup]]. On 16 July 2014, Maluma appeared at the [[2014 Premios Juventud]], where he performed "La Temperatura". That same month, the singer was confirmed as a judge/coach on [[Caracol Television]]'s ''The Voice Kids'', alongside [[Fanny Lu]] and [[Andrés Cepeda]]. In August, he made his debut as a host at the inaugural awards [[Kids' Choice Awards Colombia]] of 2014.

[[File:Maluma - Sin contrato - Festival de Viña del Mar 2017.jpg|thumb|upright|left|Maluma performing during the Festival de Viña del Mar 2017 in Chile]]
In early 2015, Maluma released the mixtape ''[[PB.DB The Mixtape]]'', a compilation of various singles.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6184364/maluma/biography |title=Biography |work=Billboard |access-date=21 April 2017}}</ref> His second album ''[[Pretty Boy, Dirty Boy]]'' was released in late 2015, containing elements of reggaeton, [[pop music|pop]] and [[urban music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.efe.com/efe/usa/gente/maluma-lanza-su-segundo-disco-y-asegura-que-ha-sido-como-un-embarazo-eterno/50000102-2735381|title=Maluma lanza su segundo disco y asegura que "ha sido como un embarazo eterno"|language=es|publisher=[[EFE]]|date=11 October 2015|access-date=31 May 2016}}</ref> The singles "[[Borró Cassette]]", "[[El Perdedor (Maluma song)|El Perdedor]]" and "[[Sin Contrato]]" entered the top 10 on ''Billboard'' [[Hot Latin Songs]].<ref name="Hot Latin Songs">{{cite web|title=Maluma – Chart history|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6184364/maluma/chart?f=363|work=Billboard|access-date=17 April 2017}}</ref>

In April of that year he performed at the newly named [[Riccardo Silva Stadium]] for the inaugural match of the new NASL soccer team The [[Miami FC]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nbcmiami.com/news/local/Colombian-Music-Sensation-Maluma-Set-to-Perform-at-Miami-FCs-Historic-Home-Opener-375110211.html|title=Maluma Set to Perform at Miami FC's Home Opener|work=NBC 6 South Florida|access-date=1 May 2018|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.miamifc.com/news/2016/04/01/global-music-idol-maluma-joins-miami-fc-at-historic-home-opener|title=Global music idol Maluma joins Miami FC at historic home opener|access-date=1 May 2018}}</ref> He then embarked on The [[Pretty Boy, Dirty Boy World Tour]] in May 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sonymusic.com.co/news/maluma-tour/|title=El Tour De Maluma|publisher=Sony Music Colombia|language=es|access-date=31 May 2016}}</ref>

In 2017, his hit single "[[Felices los 4]]" became his fifth top 5 on the [[Hot Latin Songs]] chart,<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7800845/maluma-felices-los-4-top-5-hot-latin-songs-chart |title=Maluma's 'Felices Los 4' Climbs Into Top 5 on Hot Latin Songs Chart |date=18 May 2017 |work=Billboard |access-date=19 May 2017}}</ref> followed by "[[Corazón (Maluma song)|Corazón]]", which also reached the top 5 on the chart. The music videos for both singles have over 1 billion views on [[YouTube]]. He also released the short film ''X'', which featured his songs "[[GPS (Maluma song)|GPS]]" featuring [[French Montana]], "[[Vitamina]]" featuring [[Arcángel (singer)|Arcángel]] and "23".<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://caracol.com.co/programa/2017/11/25/top_caracol/1511648502_732993.html |title="X, the film", el cortometraje con el que Maluma presenta su nuevo proyecto musical |date=25 November 2017 |work=Caracol |access-date=7 April 2018 |language=es}}</ref>

Maluma recorded the Spanish version of "[[Colors (Jason Derulo and Maluma song)|Colors]]", one of the theme songs of the [[2018 FIFA World Cup]], and performed in English by [[Jason Derulo]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.elnuevoherald.com/entretenimiento/musica/article205232244.html |title=Maluma se une a Jason Derulo para uno de los temas del Mundial Rusia 2018 |date=14 March 2018 |work=[[El Nuevo Herald]] |access-date=6 April 2018 |language=es}}</ref> Maluma returned to the Riccardo Silva Stadium in Miami to record the video,<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.miamifc.com/news/2018/04/17/maluma-returns-to-riccardo-silva-stadium-to-shoot-new-music-video|title=Maluma Returns to Riccardo Silva Stadium to Shoot New Video for Coca Cola's 2018 FIFA World Cup Anthem|access-date=1 May 2018}}</ref> one of a growing number of artists using the city to shoot music videos.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.sun-sentinel.com/features/fl-reg-music-videos-filmed-in-miami-20180423-story.html|title=Drake, Taylor Swift, Zayn and others make Miami music videos a trend again|last=Diaz|first=Johnny|work=Sun-Sentinel.com|access-date=1 May 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> Maluma's third studio album ''[[F.A.M.E. (Maluma album)|F.A.M.E.]]'' was released on 18 May 2018. In an interview he expressed its name means "Faith, Alma [Soul in Spanish], Music & Essence". He worked with producers Jay Cash, Vinylz and collaborators [[Daddy Yankee]], [[Prince Royce]] and Jason Derulo on the album.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.vibe.com/2018/03/maluma-ebro-beats-1-interview/ |title=Maluma Opens Up About His Favorite Rapper, New Album & Working With Jason Derulo |date=14 March 2018 |work=Vibe |access-date=6 April 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/meet-maluma-latin-pops-next-crossover-star-w518454 |title=Meet Maluma, the Colombian Heartthrob Who Could Be Latin Pop's Next Crossover Star |last=Leight |first=Elias |date=4 April 2018 |work=Rolling Stone |access-date=6 April 2018}}</ref> He performed "Felices los 4" at the [[2018 MTV Video Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8471359/maluma-performs-felices-los-4-2018-vmas-video |title=Maluma Puts On Explosive Performance Of 'Felices Los 4' at the 2018 VMAs: Watch |date=21 August 2018 |work=Billboard |access-date=21 August 2018}}</ref> With "Mala Mia", he topped the Latin Airplay, [[Latin Pop Airplay|Latin Pop Songs]] and [[Latin Rhythm Airplay]] charts for the second time in the year, following "[[Clandestino (Shakira and Maluma song)|Clandestino]]".<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8483657/maluma-mala-mia-tops-latin-airplay-latin-pop-songs-latin-rhythm-airplay-charts |title=Maluma's 'Mala Mia' Hits No. 1 on Latin Airplay, Latin Pop Songs & Latin Rhythm Airplay Charts |last=Bustios |first=Pamela |date=6 November 2018 |work=Billboard |access-date=6 November 2018}}</ref>

=== 2019–present: ''11:11'' and ''Papi Juancho'' ===
[[File:Maluma and Madonna.PNG|thumb|Maluma during the world premiere of music video "[[Medellín (song)|Medellín]]" on MTV in April 2019]]
In 2019, Maluma collaborated with American singer-songwriter [[Madonna]], appearing on the songs "[[Medellín (song)|Medellín]]" and "Bitch I'm Loca" from her album ''[[Madame X (album)|Madame X]]''.<ref name="MaluMado">{{Cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8499720/madonna-maluma-instagram-photos |title=Madonna and Maluma Hit the Recording Studio: See the Photos |last=Fernandez |first=Suzette |date=24 February 2019 |work=Billboard |access-date=1 April 2019}}</ref> In April it was announced that Maluma will star in ''[[Marry Me (upcoming film)|Marry Me]]'' alongside [[Jennifer Lopez]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2019/07/jennifer-lopez-maluma-marry-me-universal-stx-1202645820/|title=Jennifer Lopez Rom-Com 'Marry Me' Jumps From STX To Universal; Maluma Joins Cast|website=Deadline Hollywood|first=Anthony|last=D'Alessandro|date=15 July 2019|access-date=26 September 2019}}</ref>He will star in Walt Disney Animation Studios 60th film [[Encanto (film)|Encanto]]

On 17 May 2019, Maluma released his fourth studio album ''[[11:11 (Maluma album)|11:11]]'', supported by the singles "[[HP (song)|HP]]", "[[11 PM (song)|11 PM]]", "Instinto Natural" with Sech and "[[No Se Me Quita]]" with [[Ricky Martin]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/jeffbenjamin/2019/02/15/maluma-1111-album-will-break-him-bigger-than-ever/#1f73ae5c5e5a | title=5 Reasons Maluma's '11:11' Album Will Break Him Bigger Than Ever | work=[[Forbes]] | date=15 February 2019 | access-date=1 May 2019 | author=Benjamin, Jeff}}</ref> Another collaboration with Madonna, "Soltera", appeared on the album.<ref name=MaluMado /> To promote the album, Maluma embarked on the [[11:11 World Tour]], with concerts around the world between May 2019 and September 2020.

On 26 September 2019, he collaborated with Colombian singer [[J Balvin]] on his single "Qué Pena".

Maluma surprise-released his fifth studio album, ''[[Papi Juancho]]'', on 21 August 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/maluma-releases-new-album-papi-juancho-listen/|title=Maluma Releases New Album ''PAPI JUANCHO'': Listen|author=Hussey, Allison|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|publisher=[[Condé Nast]]|date=21 August 2020|access-date=25 August 2020}}</ref> The second single from ''Papi Juancho'', "[[Hawái (song)|Hawái]]", became a commercial success. It reached number one in multiple countries and topped the [[Hot Latin Songs]] chart for nine consecutive weeks, becoming his second number-one hit on the chart. He also released a remix featuring [[The Weeknd]]. The remix peaked at number 12 on [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and became Maluma's highest peak on the chart.

On 29 January 2021, Maluma released an EP titled ''#7DJ (7 Días en Jamaica)'' featuring seven songs along with their music videos on his YouTube channel.<ref name="7DJ">{{cite news |last=''Billboard'' Staff |title=Every Song on Maluma's ''#7DJ (7 Días En Jamaica)'' Ranked: Critic's Picks |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/9517656/maluma-7dj-album-songs-ranked/ |access-date=29 January 2021 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=28 January 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Maluma is Catholic but has said he appreciates aspects of other religions.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m5ToLK1XRWc|title=Maluma breaks down his tattoos|work=E! Online|access-date=7 September 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

Maluma's romantic life has been the subject of much speculation which he has kept private for the most part. However, in an interview in January 2018 he admitted to being in a relationship with Cuban-Croatian model Natalia Barulich.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.oprahmag.com/life/relationships-love/a27319988/maluma-girlfriend/|title=This is How Maluma Met His Girlfriend Natalia Barulich|work=The Oprah Magazine|access-date=11 July 2019|language=en-US}}</ref> He said of the relationship: "We have a great time together, we have fun, we love each other, and we also support each other...I think those are the fundamentals. It's the most important thing. Right now, we're dating. We like what we have, I like her company, and she supports me and loves me, so you're all seeing me like never before."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/914244/inside-maluma-s-romance-with-natalia-barulich|title=Inside Maluma's Romance With zerta|work=E! Online|access-date=28 February 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> Maluma and Natalia Barulich broke up in 2019.

== Public image ==
[[File:Maluma in 2018.jpg|thumb|upright|right|Maluma during an interview at the [[2018 MTV Video Music Awards]].]]
In 2019, ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'' called Maluma "Menswear Icon in the Making", saying "Maluma has attitude, Maluma has style. He likes his clothes brights and bold, which goes quite nicely with his bright and bold music, personality, and overall demeanor."<ref>{{cite web|title=Why Maluma Is a Menswear Icon in the Making|url=https://www.vogue.com/vogueworld/article/maluma-in-dsquared2-menswear-milan|work=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]|date=17 June 2019|access-date=13 July 2021}}</ref>

Through his music, he has also carried out social activism, such as the music video for "[[El Perdedor (Maluma song)|El Perdedor]]", where showcased the racism and discrimination by the US police to people of Latin descent living in the country.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://varietylatino.com/2016/musica/noticias/nuevo-video-maluma-el-perdedor-236029/|title=Nuevo video de Maluma, 'El perdedor', muestra el racismo que viven los latinos en EE.UU.|language=Spanish|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety Latino]]|first=Karla|last=Figueroa|date=22 April 2016|accessdate=29 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.peopleenespanol.com/article/maluma-revela-detalles-del-video-de-el-perdedor-video|title=Maluma revela detalles del video de "El perdedor" (VIDEO)|work=[[People en Español]]|first=Alexander|last=Mabry-Paraiso|date=24 March 2016|accessdate=29 May 2016}}</ref> Maluma has publicly declared his support for the [[LGBT community]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Las claves del éxito de Maluma|url=https://los40.com.ar/los40/2016/05/27/musica/1464359395_011807.html|work=[[Los 40]]|date=27 May 2016|access-date=13 July 2021}}</ref>

In October 2016, the song "[[Cuatro Babys]]" featuring [[Noriel (rapper)|Noriel]], [[Bryant Myers]], and [[Juhn]] received controversy over its lyrics, as some considered them to objectify women. A petition was posted on [[Change.org]] demanding the removal of the song from digital platforms.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7603779/maluma-cuatro-babys-petition-response|title=Maluma Responds to Online Petition Against His Song 'Cuatro Babys'|work=Billboard|access-date=16 April 2018}}</ref> Despite this controversy, the popularity of "Cuatro Babys" earned it a 12× Latin Platinum certification by the [[RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=cuatro#search_section|title=RIAA's Gold & Platinum Program|work=RIAA}}</ref>
