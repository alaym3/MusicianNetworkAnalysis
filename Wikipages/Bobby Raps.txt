'''Robert John Richardson''' (born December 22, 1992), better known by his stage name '''Bobby Raps''', is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2017/08/bobby-raps-desensitized-video|title=Watch Bobby Raps' Cinematic "Desensitized" Video|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|first=Joe|last=Price|date=August 17, 2017|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> He has been a member of Thestand4rd,<ref name=startribune2017/> Audio Perm,<ref name=startribune2017>{{cite web|url=https://www.startribune.com/minneapolis-wiz-kid-bobby-raps-makes-his-major-label-mark-today/433153743/|title=Twin Cities wiz kid Bobby Raps makes his major-label 'Mark' today|work=[[Star Tribune]]|first=Chris|last=Riemenschneider|date=July 7, 2017|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> and Dequexatron X000.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.citypages.com/music/hamburger-helper-drops-watch-the-stove-mixtape-featuring-bobby-raps-dj-tiiiiiiiiiip-8171471|title=Hamburger Helper drops Watch the Stove mixtape featuring Bobby Raps, DJ Tiiiiiiiiiip|work=[[City Pages]]|first=Jay|last=Boller|date=April 1, 2016|access-date=April 24, 2020}}</ref>

==Early life==
Bobby Raps was born Robert John Richardson<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/music/robert-john-bobby-raps-richardson|title=Robert John "Bobby Raps" Richardson|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=July 2, 2020}}</ref> on December 22, 1992 in [[Saint Paul, Minnesota]].<ref name=allmusic>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/bobby-raps-mn0003605063/biography|title=Bobby Raps – Biography|work=[[AllMusic]]|first=Neil Z.|last=Yeung|access-date=April 19, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200820133558/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/bobby-raps-mn0003605063/biography|archive-date=August 20, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> While in high school, he started rapping and producing beats.<ref name=allmusic/> He graduated from [[Saint Paul Central High School]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.startribune.com/you-scream-who-screams-little-girls-scream-for-the-stand4rd/281261741/|title=You Scream? Who Screams? Little Girls Scream for the Stand4rd|work=[[Star Tribune]]|first=Maria|last=Isa|date=November 4, 2014|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2011, Bobby Raps released his debut mixtape, ''Gimme Daps''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.citypages.com/music/twin-cities-rap-mixtape-roundup-vol-1-6639480|title=Twin Cities rap mixtape roundup, Vol. 1|work=[[City Pages]]|first=Jack|last=Spencer|date=June 7, 2011|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> In 2015, he released a collaborative EP with [[Corbin (musician)|Corbin]], titled ''Couch Potato''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2015/06/01/download-corbin-spooky-black-and-bobby-raps-couch-potato|title=Download Corbin's New Couch Potato EP With Bobby Raps|work=[[The Fader]]|first=Duncan|last=Cooper|date=June 1, 2015|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> Entirely produced by Bobby Raps, it features a guest appearance from Izell Pyramid.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blog.thecurrent.org/2015/06/corbin-and-bobby-raps-rich-mattson-and-more-this-weeks-minnesota-record-releases/|title=Corbin and Bobby Raps, Rich Mattson, and more: This week's Minnesota record releases|work=The Current|publisher=[[Minnesota Public Radio]]|first=Aaron|last=Bolton|date=June 2, 2015|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> ''[[City Pages]]'' described it as "a thoroughly haunting dose of ethereal hip-hop that sounds like if [[James Blake (musician)|James Blake]] cut a record with [[Earl Sweatshirt]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.citypages.com/music/hello-fuck-heads-corbin-and-bobby-raps-drop-surprise-ep-7395391|title='Hello Fuck Heads': Corbin and Bobby Raps Drop Surprise EP|work=[[City Pages]]|first=Raghav|last=Mehta|date=June 2, 2015|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> The opening song of the EP, titled "Welcome to the Hell Zone", was included on ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]''{{'}}s "Best Songs of the Week" list.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2015/06/best-songs-of-the-week-80/bobby-raps-corbin-welcome-to-the-hell-zone|title=Best Songs of the Week: 5. Bobby Raps & Corbin – "Welcome to the Hell Zone"|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|first=Joe|last=Price|date=June 5, 2015|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> The duo's live performances have received favorable reviews from ''City Pages''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.citypages.com/music/review-7416136|title=Review: Corbin & Bobby Raps feed off Varsity crowd|work=[[City Pages]]|first=Jack|last=Spencer|date=June 18, 2015|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> and ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/review/6627325/corbin-bobby-raps-concert-review-new-york|title=Corbin & Bobby Raps Bring 'Couch Potato' to New York City: Live Review|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Elias|last=Leight|date=July 13, 2015|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> In 2017, he released his major-label debut, ''Mark'', on [[Republic Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.citypages.com/music/bobby-raps-greg-grease-and-more-in-this-months-10-song-twin-cities-rap-roundup/438583443|title=Bobby Raps, Greg Grease, and more in this month's 10-song Twin Cities rap roundup|work=[[City Pages]]|first=Michael|last=Madden|date=August 7, 2017|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> It includes production from [[Shlohmo]] and [[D33J]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/07/bobby-raps-mark-mixtape/|title=Bobby Raps Releases New 'Mark' Mixtape|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|first=Lindsey|last=India|date=July 7, 2017|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref> ''Complex'' called it "[Bobby Raps'] strongest body of work to date."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2017/07/bobby-raps-mark-stream|title=Listen to Bobby Raps' New Mixtape 'Mark'|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|first=Joe|last=Price|date=July 8, 2017|access-date=April 19, 2020}}</ref>
