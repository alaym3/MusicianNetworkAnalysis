'''"Domani"''' (Italian for "tomorrow") is a [[1955 in music|1955]] song written by [[Ulpio Minucci]] with lyrics by [[Tony Velona]]. The melody is based on the intermezzo from the [[comic opera]] [[I Quattro Rusteghi]] by [[Ermanno Wolf-Ferrari]].

The most popular version of the song was recorded in 1955 by [[Julius LaRosa]], released by [[Cadence Records]] as catalog number 1265.<ref>{{cite web|title=45cat.com|url=http://www.45cat.com/record/1265us|website=45cat.com|accessdate=December 3, 2017}}</ref> It first reached the [[Billboard magazine]] charts on July 13, 1955 and lasted 7 weeks on the chart, peaking at #13.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Whitburn|first1=Joel|title=The Billboard Book of Top 40 Hits|date=2004|publisher=Billboard Books|location=New York|isbn=0-8230-7499-4|page=357|edition=8th}}</ref>

The song was rendered in French - retaining the title "Domani" - by [[:fr:Renée Lebas|Renée Lebas]]; a Finnish rendering: "Huomenna", was recorded by [[:fi:Maynie Sirén|Maynie Sirén]].

Another song of the same name was released by the [[Twilight Singers]] on the "Black Is the Color of My True Love's Hair" [[Extended play|EP]] and on [[Greg Dulli]]'s 2005 album ''[[Amber Headlights]]''.
