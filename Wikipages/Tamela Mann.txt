'''Tamela Jean Mann''' ([[née]] '''Johnson''') is an American [[gospel music|gospel]] singer, and actress.

Tamela Mann began her career as a singer with the gospel group [[Kirk Franklin|Kirk Franklin and the Family]]. She was a featured soloist on several tracks while with Franklin's group, including "Don't Take Your Joy Away", "Now Behold the Lamb", and "Lean on Me", the latter of which also included [[Mary J. Blige]], [[Crystal Lewis]], [[R. Kelly]], and [[Bono]]. She released her debut studio album in 2005. Her third studio album, ''[[Best Days (Tamela Mann album)|Best Days]]'' was certified Gold by the RIAA, and lead single "[[Take Me to the King]]" 
was a major commercial success, as well as receiving a nomination for the [[Grammy Award for Best Gospel/Contemporary Christian Music Performance]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/tamela-mann|title=Tamela Mann|website=Billboard.com|access-date=January 19, 2018}}</ref>

Tamela Mann is known for her role as Cora Simmons in [[Tyler Perry]]'s plays, including ''[[I Can Do Bad All By Myself (play)|I Can Do Bad All By Myself]]'', ''[[Diary of a Mad Black Woman (play)|Diary of a Mad Black Woman]]'', ''[[Madea's Family Reunion (play)|Madea's Family Reunion]]'', ''[[Madea's Class Reunion]]'', ''[[Meet the Browns (play)|Meet the Browns]]'', and ''[[What's Done in the Dark]]''. Mann has also appeared in movies including ''[[Meet the Browns (film)|Meet the Browns]]'', ''[[Madea Goes to Jail]]'' and ''[[Madea's Big Happy Family]]''.

She recorded some songs on the soundtrack for ''Diary of a Mad Black Woman'', including participating in the song "Father (Can You Hear Me)". Mann is reprising her role as Cora Simmons in the new television comedy, ''[[Tyler Perry's Assisted Living]]'', which debuted on [[BET]] in September 2020.

Tamela Mann is married to [[David Mann (actor)|David Mann]]. They starred in the play ''[[I Can Do Bad All By Myself (play)|I Can Do Bad All By Myself]]'', then later on the TBS sitcom ''[[Meet the Browns (TV series)|Meet the Browns]]'' from 2009 to 2011. In 2015, they began starring on the Bounce TV sitcom ''[[Mann & Wife]]''.

Aside from her musical and acting pursuits, Mann launched a new line of clothing, the Tamela Mann Collection, in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://getuperica.com/117427/tamela-mann-launches-plus-size-active-apparel/|title = Tamela Mann Launches Plus Size Active Apparel|date = April 16, 2019}}</ref> The brand offers a variety of athleisure and workout wear for the curvy woman.

==Early life==
Mann was born in [[Fort Worth, Texas]], the youngest of 14 children. She and her siblings grew up in a very spiritual home grounded in the [[Church of God in Christ]] (COGIC). They were reared by their mother, "Mother Eppe," who believed in and exercised solid biblical principles.

By the age of 12, Mann was singing in the church's adult choir and was often selected to sing solos.  Although she was bashful, she blossomed in the local church and high school choirs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.Tamelamann.com|title=Homepage|website=Tamelamann.com|access-date=January 19, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
===Kirk Franklin and the Family===
Mann's musical career began when she joined [[Kirk Franklin and the Family]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tamelamann.com/website.htm |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050212200427/http://www.tamelamann.com/website.htm |url-status=dead |archive-date=February 12, 2005 |title=The Leading Tamela Mann Site on the Net |publisher=tamelamann.com |access-date=June 16, 2012 }}</ref>  She has also enjoyed collaborating and performing with artists such as [[Yolanda Adams]], [[Mary J. Blige]], [[Al Green]], [[Celine Dion]], [[Bono]], [[R. Kelly]] and [[Fred Hammond]].  "Local legends" that have collaborated with Mann include" Demarcus Williams, Myron Williams, Kermit Wells, Excell Amos, Myron Butler and a host of others. She went on to join the gospel musical cast of [[David E. Talbert]] as he directed the stage play ''He Say... She Say... But What Does God Say?''

===Acting career===
Mann began acting in 1999 when she was discovered by [[Tyler Perry]]. She debuted in the stage play ''[[I Can Do Bad All By Myself (play)|I Can Do Bad All By Myself]]''.

She went on to act in ''[[Meet the Browns (play)|Meet the Browns]]'', ''[[Madea's Family Reunion]]'', ''[[Madea's Class Reunion]]'', ''[[Diary of a Mad Black Woman (play)|Diary of a Mad Black Woman]]'' and ''[[What's Done in the Dark]]''. She has appeared on television shows including ''[[The Tonight Show]]'' with [[Jay Leno]], BET Soundstage, the 1999 [[Grammy Awards]], the [[Dove Awards]], the United States of America World Olympics, ''[[Tyler Perry's House of Payne]]'', and Tyler Perry's ''[[Meet the Browns (TV series)|Meet the Browns]]'', in which she portrays Mr. Brown's daughter.

She has appeared in movies, including the 2001 comedy ''[[Kingdom Come (2001 film)|Kingdom Come]]'', ''[[Diary of a Mad Black Woman]]'' and ''[[Meet the Browns (film)|Meet The Browns]]''. She also had a role in 2009's Tyler Perry movie ''[[Madea Goes to Jail]]''.  Mann appeared in the 2012 film [[Sparkle (2012 film)|Sparkle]], a remake of the [[Sparkle (1976 film)|1976 film of the same name]], alongside [[Whitney Houston]] and ''[[American Idol]]'' winner and R&B singer [[Jordin Sparks]], making her film debut.

She is currently filming a new cooking show with her husband David, ''Hanging with the Manns''. They also have a reality show on BET "It's a Mann's World"
[[File:Tamela Mann 18 2.jpg|thumb|Tamela Mann in 2017]]

===Solo musical career===
In 2005, Mann and her husband David created their own label, Tillymann Music Group, which they have released several projects through. In 2005 her solo album, ''Gotta Keep Movin{{'}}'', was released. In 2007 she released her first live album, ''The Live Experience''. Mann's songs "Father Can You hear Me" and "Take It To Jesus" can be heard in the movie and on the soundtrack album of ''[[Diary of a Mad Black Woman]]''.

In 2009 her second studio album, ''The Master Plan'', was released. It peaked at number 2 on the ''Billboard'' Gospel Album Chart, and at number 97 on the ''Billboard'' 200.

Mann's third studio album, ''[[Best Days (Tamela Mann album)|Best Days]]'', was released on August 14, 2012. It debuted at number 1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Top Gospel Albums chart, and number 14 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

Mann won Stellar Award's "Best Female Gospel Artist of the Year" in 2014, as well as Best Gospel Artist at the 2014 BET Awards.

In 2018, Mann released a duets album with her husband entitled, ''Us Against the World: The Love Project''.

In 2019, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' announced that ''[[Best Days (Tamela Mann album)|Best Days]]'' was the Gospel Album of the Decade.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/8543756/decade-in-charts-2010s-gospel|title = The Decade in Gospel Charts: Hezekiah Walker Deserves 'Praise' and Tamela Mann is 'Best'}}</ref>

On August 7, 2020, Mann released the single "Touch from You".<ref name="TouchFromYou">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/touch-from-you/1524138185?i=1524138426&uo=2&partnerId=11&at=10l4UI&app=itunes&ct=music-songs|title=Touch from You by Tamela Mann on Apple Music|via=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=August 7, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Tamela is the wife of actor [[David Mann (actor)|David Mann]],  known for his role as Deacon Leroy Brown in some of Tyler Perry's plays. In the Madea TV shows and movies, she plays Mr. Brown's daughter, Cora.<ref>Christian, Margena. "Husband and wife, David and Tamela Mann, keep Madea on her toes in ''Meet the Browns''." ''Jet Magazine''. 118.2. August 27, 2007: 34.</ref>
They have always worked together professionally.

David and Tamela renewed their 25th wedding vows in the spring of 2013. They raised five children together: David's daughter, Porcia, niece Sonya, David’s daughter Tiffany, and their children together, David Jr and Tia. They also have thirteen grandchildren.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://alwaysalist.com/tv/davidtamelamann/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=January 15, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150124064259/http://alwaysalist.com/tv/davidtamelamann/ |archive-date=January 24, 2015 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

In January 2015, a reality show about their family, ''It's A Mann's World'', premiered on [[BET]]. David and Tamela are devout Christians, and in this program are able to show how they have a fun-loving, balanced family, centered in godly principles and coping with real-life obstacles. In 2019, Tamela joined the [[WW International]] family as a weightloss ambassador.<ref>Ref: https://www.essence.com/feature/tamela-mann-weight-watchers-new-ambassador/</ref> On July 23, 2019, Tamela had double knee surgery just a couple of months after starting her weight loss journey. She recovered quickly and went on to lose 100 pounds of weight by January 1, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://starstudds.com/tamela-mann-weight-loss/|title = Everything You Need to Know about Tamela Mann Weight Loss! &#124; Star Studds}}</ref>
