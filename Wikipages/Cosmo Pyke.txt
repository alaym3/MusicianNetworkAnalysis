'''Cosmo Pyke''' is an English singer-songwriter from [[Peckham, London]].

== Biography ==
Pyke was born in [[Peckham]] in South east London. He grew up listening to [[Joni Mitchell]], [[Jimi Hendrix]], [[Bob Marley]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Joan Armatrading]] and [[The Beatles]]. His mother, Rix Pyke, used to be in a comedy trio [[Feminism|feminist]] group in the 1970s.<ref name=":0" /> Pyke attended [[BRIT School|The Brit School]] for Performing Arts to study music production and performance.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.fredperry.com/us/subculture/playlist-profile-cosmo-pyke|title=Playlists and interviews exploring British subculture: Cosmo Pyke|website=www.fredperry.com|language=en|access-date=2019-01-15}}</ref> He released his debut EP "Just Cosmo" in 2017.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://thechargeronline.com/5629/entertainment/2018-is-promising-year-for-peckham-artist-cosmo-pyke/|title=2018 is promising year for Peckham artist Cosmo Pyke|last=Carrington|first=William|date=2018-01-30|website=The Charger Online|access-date=2019-01-15}}</ref> He released his second EP ‘A Piper for Janet’ in January 2021.

== Artistry ==
Pyke has been compared to [[King Krule]] because of his eclectic musical influences. ''[[The Guardian]]'' described his music as, "a fusion of [[jazz]], [[Two-tone (music genre)|2-tone]], [[Tyler, the Creator]] and [[the Kooks]]."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/may/11/cosmo-pyke-im-this-peckham-guy-who-wants-to-take-up-all-opportunities|title=Cosmo Pyke: ‘You don't have to be some working-class rapper'|last=Gibsone|first=Harriet|date=2017-05-11|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-01-15|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Pyke describes his combination of soul, jazz, reggae and hip-hop as, "spacey, beautiful, and lazy."<ref name="auto"/>
