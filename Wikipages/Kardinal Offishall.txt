'''Jason Drew Harrow'''<!-- Don't edit the name without providing a citation, it will be removed. --><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/search/workID/313183150|title=ACE Repertory|website=Ascap.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/lyrics/offishall_kardinal/money_jane_remix/2289979/lyrics.jhtml |title=MTV |publisher=MTV |access-date=2011-03-02}}</ref> (born May 11, 1976<!-- Don't change the birth date, it will be removed. -->),<ref name=kardinal/> better known by his [[stage name]] '''Kardinal Offishall''' ({{IPAc-en|k|ɑːr|d|ᵻ|ˈ|n|æ|l|_|oʊ|f|ᵻ|ˈ|ʃ|æ|l}}),<ref name=kardinal2>{{cite web |url=http://web.rbmamedia.net/video-archive/transcript/kardinal_offishall__its_official/transcript |title=Red Bull Music Academy |publisher=Red Bull Music Academy |access-date=2011-03-02 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130222004243/http://web.rbmamedia.net/video-archive/transcript/kardinal_offishall__its_official/transcript |archive-date=2013-02-22 }}</ref> is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[rapping|rapper]], [[record producer]], [[Disc jockey|DJ]], and [[music executive|record executive]]. Often credited as Canada's "[[hip hop music|hip hop]] ambassador", he is regarded as one of the country's best hip hop producers, and is best known for his distinctive [[reggae]] and [[dancehall]]-influenced style of hip hop.<ref>{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=kardinal offishall|bio=true}} |title=Billboard |publisher=Billboard |access-date=2011-03-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=1477 |title=XXL Magazine |publisher=Xxlmag.com |access-date=2011-03-02 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091007150521/http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=1477 |archive-date=2009-10-07 }}</ref><ref>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r471440|pure_url=yes}} Husslin' > Overview] AllMusic. Accessed on October 24, 2012.</ref><ref name=kardinal>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p423368|pure_url=yes}} Kardinal Offishall > Biography] AllMusic. Accessed on October 24, 2012.</ref>

A native of [[Toronto]], Offishall began his career in the city's mid-1990s [[underground hip hop]] scene, as a member of The Circle.<ref name=canoel>[http://jam.canoe.ca/sceneMusic/Artists/O/Offishall_Kardinal/1998/05/26/748411.html Kardinal Offishall making waves] Jam! Music. Accessed on October 22, 2012.</ref> He released several independent [[12-inch single|12" singles]], a debut album (''[[Eye & I]]''), and an EP (''[[Husslin']]''), before signing with major label [[MCA Records]] in 2000. The following year, Offishall released his second album, ''[[Quest for Fire: Firestarter, Vol. 1]]'', spawning the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' hit "[[BaKardi Slang]]", which popularized Toronto's nickname "[[Name of Toronto#Nicknames|T-dot]]",<ref>[http://entertainment.ca.msn.com/music/photos/gallery.aspx?cp-documentid=24741538&page= "BaKardi Slang" by Kardinal Offishall] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100708164732/http://entertainment.ca.msn.com/music/photos/gallery.aspx?cp-documentid=24741538 |date=2010-07-08 }} MSN. Accessed on October 22, 2012.</ref> and the minor hit "[[Ol' Time Killin']]"; both singles became his [[signature song]]s. After failing to release the ''Firestarter Vol. 2'' album in 2003, on MCA, and later [[Geffen Records]] (which absorbed MCA),<ref name=kardinal2/> Offishall briefly returned to the underground scene, releasing several more independent 12" singles.

In 2005, Offishall's third album, ''[[Fire and Glory]]'', was released in Canada. That year, he collaborated with [[contemporary R&B|R&B]] singer [[Akon]] for the first time, starting a musical partnership that lasted until 2010.<ref name="okayplayer"/><ref name="comeupshow"/> In 2007, he signed with Akon's [[Kon Live Distribution]] label, through Geffen, and released his fourth album, ''[[Not 4 Sale (Kardinal Offishall album)|Not 4 Sale]]'', the following year. The first single, "[[Dangerous (Kardinal Offishall song)|Dangerous]]", became a top five hit on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], and was certified triple [[Music Canada#Certification awards|platinum]] in Canada.<ref name="certification">[http://www.musiccanada.com/GPSearchResult.aspx?st=&ica=False&sa=Kardinal+Offishall&sl=&smt=0&sat=-1&ssb=Artist Gold Platinum Database] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140222135407/http://www.musiccanada.com/GPSearchResult.aspx?st=&ica=False&sa=Kardinal+Offishall&sl=&smt=0&sat=-1&ssb=Artist |date=2014-02-22 }} Music Canada. Accessed on October 22, 2012.</ref> Offishall released two [[Music Canada#Certification awards|gold]]-certified singles, "[[The Tide Is High#Kardinal Offishall version|Numba 1 (Tide Is High)]]" and "[[Body Bounce]]", before leaving Kon Live in late 2010.<ref name="okayplayer">[http://largeup.okayplayer.com/2012/07/17/heds-and-dreds-talking-t-dot-with-kardinal-offishall/ Heds and Dreds: Talking T-Dot Roots and Culture with Kardinal Offishall] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120727084737/http://largeup.okayplayer.com/2012/07/17/heds-and-dreds-talking-t-dot-with-kardinal-offishall/ |date=2012-07-27 }} Okayplayer. Accessed on October 22, 2012</ref><ref name="comeupshow">[http://thecomeupshow.com/2012/07/05/interview-kardinal-offishall-talks-with-the-come-up-show-about-a-m-t-r-i-m-toronto-and-the-music-industry/ Kardinal Offishall talks with The Come Up Show about A.M.T.R.I.M, Toronto and the music industry] The Come Up Show. Accessed on October 22, 2012.</ref> Since then, his latest singles have been released on his independent label Black Stone Colleagues Inc. In 2012, Offishall released ''[[Allow Me to Re-Introduce Myself]]'', a collaborative mixtape with producer [[Nottz]].

In 2013, Offishall became the creative executive director of [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Canada]]'s [[artists and repertoire|A&R]] team.<ref name="executive">[http://exclaim.ca/News/kardinal_offishall_joins_universals_r_team_as_creative_executive_director Kardinal Offishall Joins Universal's A&R Team as Creative Executive Director] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140312054520/http://exclaim.ca/News/kardinal_offishall_joins_universals_r_team_as_creative_executive_director |date=2014-03-12 }} ''Exclaim!''. Accessed on February 17, 2014.</ref> Two years later, he released his fifth album ''[[Kardi Gras, Vol. 1: The Clash]]'' under the label. On April 8, 2021, Offishall was promoted to senior vice-president of A&R for Universal Music Canada.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/music/news/music-industry-moves-universal-music-canada-names-kardinal-offishall-senior-vp-of-a-r/ar-BB1fmGEP |title=Music Industry Moves: Universal Music Canada Names Kardinal Offishall Senior VP of A&R | work=[[MSN]] |access-date=2021-04-08 |date=2021-04-08 }}</ref>

==Biography==

===Early life and career beginnings===
Harrow was born in [[Scarborough, Toronto|Scarborough]], [[Ontario]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.whaddat.com/miccheck/kardi.html |title=Whaddat? |publisher=Whaddat.com |access-date=2011-03-02 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101201031736/http://www.whaddat.com/miccheck/kardi.html |archive-date=2010-12-01 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=McKinnon |first=Matthew |url=http://www.cbc.ca/arts/music/canadianhiphop.html |title=CBC |publisher=Cbc.ca |date=2005-03-22 |access-date=2011-03-02}}</ref> in the east end of Toronto, and raised by [[Jamaica]]n immigrant parents.<ref name="Jackson">Jackson, Kevin (2014) "[http://www.jamaicaobserver.com/entertainment/Making-it--Offishall-_16318818 Making It 'Offishall']", ''[[Jamaica Observer]]'', March 21, 2014. Accessed on March 23, 2014</ref> From the ages of 2 to 13, he lived in the city's [[Flemingdon Park]] neighbourhood. He moved back to Scarborough for two years, before finally settling in [[Oakwood–Vaughan]], in the city's west end.<ref>[http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2008/09/interview-kardinal-offishall/ Kardinal Offishall [Interview&#93;] HipHopCanada. Accessed on January 22, 2011.</ref><ref name=chartattack>[http://www.chartattack.com/news/64693/kardis-second-key-2-the-city Kardi's Second Key 2 The City] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110708141426/http://www.chartattack.com/news/64693/kardis-second-key-2-the-city |date=2011-07-08 }} CHARTattack. Accessed on January 22, 2011.</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Mike|last=Ross |url=http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/O/Offishall_Kardinal/2006/02/17/1447418.html |title=Jam! Showbiz |publisher=Jam.canoe.ca |date=2006-02-17 |access-date=2011-03-02}}</ref> While in high school, he would throw parties at the [[Alexandra Park, Toronto|Alexandra Park]] Community Centre.<ref name=chartattack/> He also is a former [[York University]] student but did not complete his degree.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://yorkisu.smugmug.com/2010-2011/Yorks-52nd-Birthday/17251714_GKr3Gs#!i=1309695455&k=Q62cMNZ&lb=1&s=A |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130202113004/http://yorkisu.smugmug.com/2010-2011/Yorks-52nd-Birthday/17251714_GKr3Gs%23!i=1309695455&k=Q62cMNZ&lb=1&s=A |url-status=dead |archive-date=2013-02-02 |title=York University's 52nd Birthday |publisher=York University |access-date=2012-02-16 }}</ref>

He started rapping at the age of eight and was winning competitions by the time he was 12.<ref name=kardinal/> At age 14, he performed live on stage for the first time, with [[Nelson Mandela]] in attendance during Mandela's first visit to Toronto following his release from prison earlier that year (1990).<ref name=montrealmirror>{{cite web|url=http://www.montrealmirror.com/ARCHIVES/1997/022797/music1.html |title=Montreal Mirror |publisher=Montreal Mirror |date=1997-02-27 |access-date=2011-03-02}}</ref> By 1993, he decided to change his alias "KoolAid", and went by the moniker "Kardinal Offishall" after being inspired by the great 17th century French politician [[Cardinal Richelieu]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.exclaim.ca/index.asp?layid=22&csid=1&csid1=4483 |title=Exclaim |publisher=Exclaim.ca |access-date=2011-03-02 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060619193455/http://www.exclaim.ca/index.asp?layid=22&csid=1&csid1=4483 |archive-date=2006-06-19 }}</ref> That year, Offishall co-founded The Circle, a collective of artists including [[Choclair]], [[Jully Black]], [[Solitair]], Tara Chase, and [[Saukrates]].<ref name=kardinal2/> In 1994, he made his first recorded appearance on Saukrates' single "Still Caught Up".<ref name=kardinal2/>

===1996–1999: ''Eye & I''===
Kardinal was signed to a publishing deal with [[Warner/Chappell Music|Warner/Chappell Music Canada]] at the age of 20.<ref name=kardinal/> In 1996, he released his first single "[[Naughty Dread]]", which was featured on the ''[[Rap Essentials Volume One]]'' compilation and earned him a [[Juno Award]] nomination for [[Juno Award for Rap Recording of the Year|Best Rap Recording]]. In 1997, Kardinal released his debut album ''[[Eye & I]]'' on [[Capitol Hill Music]]. The only single from the album, "[[On wit da Show]]", had considerable video play on [[MuchMusic]]. The album received rave reviews from music critics.<ref name=eyeandi>[http://www.octopusmediaink.com/CitizenHipHop.html Kinder, Gentler Rap, Eh?] Ottawa Citizen. Accessed on November 1, 2008.</ref><ref>[http://www.seemagazine.com/Issues/1998/0625/cover1.html Kardinal Offishall, with guests] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927150111/http://www.seemagazine.com/Issues/1998/0625/cover1.html |date=2011-09-27 }} SEE. Accessed on October 21, 2009.</ref> [[AllMusic]] stated that Kardinal "blended soul, dancehall, reggae, hip-hop, and a wholly inventive approach to beats on his 20-track debut album, ''Eye & I''."<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p423368|pure_url=yes}} Kardinal Offishall > Biography] Allmusic. Accessed on November 1, 2008.</ref> Unfortunately, the album was poorly distributed in Canada, and a lack of radio support resulted in the album receiving limited commercial attention.<ref name=eyeandi/><ref>[http://www.exclaim.ca/articles/coverstory.aspx?csid1=75 Kardinal Offishall - Man on Fire] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070626131337/http://www.exclaim.ca/articles/coverstory.aspx?csid1=75 |date=2007-06-26 }} Exclaim!. Accessed on November 1, 2008.</ref> Over 4,000 copies of the album were sold in its first three months of release.<ref name=kardinal/> In 1998, he was featured on the Juno-winning single "[[Northern Touch]]" with the [[Rascalz]], [[Choclair]], [[Checkmate (rapper)|Checkmate]] and [[Thrust (rapper)|Thrust]].<ref name=kardinal/> The following year, he produced Choclair's hit single "[[Let's Ride (Choclair song)|Let's Ride]]".<ref name=kardinal/>

===2000–2003: ''Husslin{{'}}'' and ''Quest for Fire: Firestarter, Vol. 1''===
''Husslin{{'}}'' was an [[extended play|EP]] released on April 11, 2000. It was released independently on [[Figure IV Entertainment]] and distributed by Fat Beats Records in the United States. The title track, "[[Husslin' (song)|Husslin']]", was one of the hottest [[12-inch single|12" singles]] of 2000.<ref name=husslin>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r471440|pure_url=yes}} Husslin' > Overview] AllMusic. Accessed on October 19, 2008.</ref> "And What?", featuring [[Saukrates]], was released as a single in 1999. "Husslin'" and "Mic T.H.U.G.S." also appear on Kardinal's second studio album, ''[[Quest for Fire: Firestarter, Vol. 1]]''. An updated version of "U R Ghetto When", known as "U R Ghetto 2002", is on the ''Quest for Fire'' album. In 2000, Kardinal signed with [[MCA Records]]. He released the album ''Quest for Fire: Firestarter, Vol. 1'' in 2001, which spawned the hits "[[BaKardi Slang]]" and "[[Ol' Time Killin']]".One year after its release, 25,000 copies of the album were sold in Canada.<ref name=hiphopcanada>[http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2002/07/has-canadian-hip-hop-truly-arrived-article/ Has Canadian hip-hop truly "arrived"?] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110821143454/http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2002/07/has-canadian-hip-hop-truly-arrived-article/ |date=2011-08-21 }} HipHopCanada. Accessed on October 18, 2009.</ref> It received generally favorable reviews from music critics. ''[[The Source (magazine)|The Source]]'' gave the album 3½ out of 5 mics.<ref name=hiphopcanada/> RapReviews.com gave it a 7/10 rating, calling it a "mixed bag," and stating "there are also some perfect 10's to be found here."<ref name=kardi>[http://www.rapreviews.com/archive/2002_02_firevol1.html Kardinal Offishall :: Firestarter Vol. 1 * Quest For Fire] RapReviews. Accessed on September 24, 2009.</ref> ''[[The A.V. Club]]'' gave the album a favorable review, praising its "impressive musical and lyrical consistency."<ref>[https://www.avclub.com/articles/kardinal-offishall-firestarter-volume-1-quest-for,18359/ Firestarter Volume 1: Quest For Fire] The A.V. Club. Accessed on October 18, 2009.</ref> AllMusic gave it 2½ out of 5 stars, noting that Kardinal "displays only flashes of promise here."<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r532969|pure_url=yes}} Quest for Fire: Firestarter, Vol. 1 > Overview] Allmusic. Accessed on September 24, 2009.</ref> The album was nominated for [[Juno Award for Rap Recording of the Year|Best Rap Recording]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2002|2002 Juno Awards]].

After MCA folded in 2003, Kardinal's highly anticipated follow up album ''Firestarter Vol. 2: The F-Word Theory'' was shelved along with the music video for the single "[[Belly Dancer (Kardinal Offishall song)|Belly Dancer]]" featuring [[Pharrell Williams|Pharrell]], and Kardinal eventually found himself without a label. Had the album been released, there would have been production from [[Timbaland]] and [[The Neptunes]], among others.<ref name=kardinal2/>

===2004–2008: ''Fire and Glory'' and ''Not 4 Sale''===
In 2004, Kardinal released an independent [[mixtape]] titled ''Kill Bloodclott Bill'' with his production company, [[Black Jays]], and he also released his second major-label album titled ''[[Fire and Glory]]'' on November 15, 2005, through [[Virgin Records]] in Canada only. Hits on ''Fire and Glory'' include "[[Everyday (Rudebwoy)]]" and "Heads Up". RapReviews.com gave the album an 8.5/10 rating, stating "''Fire and Glory'' is a better album than ''Firestarter Vol. 1''," and "aside from having better lyrics than most of his counterparts, Kardinal's unique style also sets him apart."<ref>[http://www.rapreviews.com/archive/2007_01_fireandglory.html Kardinal Offishall :: Fire and Glory] RapReviews. Accessed on September 28, 2009.</ref> The album was nominated for [[Juno Award for Rap Recording of the Year|Rap Recording of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2006|2006 Juno Awards]].

''Not 4 Sale'' is the fourth studio album by Canadian rapper Kardinal Offishall, released September 9, 2008 on Kon Live/Geffen Records. It is his second international major-label album, after 2001's ''Quest for Fire: Firestarter, Vol. 1''. It was a critical success, spawning the top 5 ''Billboard'' Hot 100 single "Dangerous", and the minor hit "Numba 1 (Tide Is High)". 11,869 copies of the album were sold in the United States, in its first week of release. It debuted on the ''Billboard'' 200 at number 40. As of February 15, 2009, the album has sold 34,822 copies. In Canada, it debuted at number 8 on the Canadian Albums Chart, with 4,247 copies sold in the first week. The album received generally favorable reviews from music critics. AllMusic gave it 4 out of 5 stars, calling it "an entirely solid album", also stating "this freedom fighting and socially conscious writing is tempered with hooky club tracks that never fail."[14] ''USA Today'' gave it 3 out of 4 stars, noting "his potent blend of hip-hop and dancehall gives him a flavor all his own."PopMatters gave the album a 6/10 rating, writing "although many of the tracks here are glossy pop productions, Kardinal has not really changed since he was first heard in the '90s."The album won the award for Rap Recording of the Year at the 2009 Juno Awards.

===2009–2018: Universal Music Canada partnership and ''Kardi Gras, Vol. 1: The Clash''===
In 2008, he was featured on the Just Dance (RedOne Remix) by Lady Gaga, part of the EP "Just Dance (Remixes, Pt. 2)". In 2010, he was included in the [[Young Artists for Haiti]]'s version of "[[Wavin' Flag]]" in an effort to raise money for disaster relief. He starred along with many Canadian stars. Also in 2010, he was featured in [[Raghav]]'s single "[[So Much (song)|So Much]]". In 2011, he collaborated with Canadian dance-pop act [[Audio Playground]] on their gold selling single "Famous", which reached #28 in Canada and #10 on the USA Billboard Dance charts. In 2011, he collaborated with singer [[Karl Wolf]] on his single "Ghetto Love".

In 2013, Kardinal Offishall, along with [[Madchild]] from [[Swollen Members]], were featured on Canadian hip hop artist Classified's [[Classified (Classified album)|self-titled album]] on the track "Look Up". He also appeared on the charity single "[[True Colors (Cyndi Lauper song)|True Colors]]" by [[Artists Against Bullying]]. On December 16, 2013, Offishall joined [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Canada]] as Creative Executive Director of [[artists and repertoire|A&R]].<ref name="executive"/> Aside from developing Canadian talent, he has also expressed interest in developing international talent, stating "I'm searching the world for superstars."<ref>[http://fyimusicnews.ca/post/75132719922/in-conversation-with-kardinal-offishall#.UuvA8pLElq8.twitter IN CONVERSATION WITH KARDINAL OFFISHALL] FYI Music News. Accessed on February 17, 2014.</ref> On October 30, 2015, Kardinal Offishall released his fifth studio album ''Kardi Gras, Vol. 1: The Clash'' which spawned the hit single "That Chick Right There", which reached number 68 on the Canadian Hot 100.

===2019–present: ''Pick Your Poison''===
On June 4, 2019, Kardinal Offishall released a new single called "Run" which was used as the [[Toronto Raptors]] theme song during their championship run in the [[2019 NBA Finals]] and also serves as the first single off his upcoming album ''Pick Your Poison''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/kardinal_offishall_shares_new_single_run|title=Kardinal Offishall Shares New Single "Run"|website=Exclaim.ca}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fyimusicnews.ca/articles/2019/06/14/kardinal-offishall-run|title=Kardinal Offishall - Run|date=June 14, 2019|website=FYIMusicNews.ca}}</ref>

In October 2021, it was announced that he will be a judge on the upcoming second season of ''[[Canada's Got Talent]]'', slated to air in 2022.<ref>[https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2021/10/26/howie-mandel-lilly-singh-among-judges-set-for-canadas-got-talent.html "Howie Mandel, Lilly Singh among judges set for ‘Canada’s Got Talent’"]. ''[[Toronto Star]]'', October 26, 2021.</ref>
