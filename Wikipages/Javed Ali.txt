'''Javed Ali''' is an Indian playback singer who predominantly sings in regional [[Hindi]] language [[Bollywood]] films. He has also sung in various regional languages like [[Bengali language|Bengali]], [[Kannada]], [[Malayalam]], [[Gujarati language|Gujarati]], [[Marathi language|Marathi]], [[Odia language|Odia]], [[Tamil language|Tamil]], [[Telugu language|Telugu]] and [[Urdu]].

==Early life==
Javed Ali was born Javed Hussain in Panchkuian Road, Delhi.<ref>{{cite news |date=19 August 2016 |title=I can never hurt anyone: Javed Ali |url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/kolkata/javed-ali-inetrview/articleshow/53907653.cms |work=The Times of India}}</ref><ref name="rediff"/> He studied in Ramjas School 4, [[Paharganj]].<ref name="pioneer"/> Javed Ali started singing at a very early age with his father Ustad Hamid Hussain, a popular [[qawwali]] singer. During an interview, he said that his father used to sing [[kirtans]]. Ghazal singer [[Ghulam Ali (singer)|Ghulam Ali]] heard Ali's voice and felt that he could become a great singer in future. Ghulam Ali not only guided Javed, but also gave him chance to sing in his live concerts. As a tribute and honour to his [[guru]] Ghulam Ali, Javed changed his name from Javed Hussain to Javed Ali.<ref name="rediff">{{cite web |title=Javed Ali: The voice of Amitabh, Hrithik |url=http://specials.rediff.com/movies/2008/feb/05sld1.htm |work=Rediff.com}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2007, Javed Ali came into limelight for his song "Ek Din Teri Raahon Mein" from the film [[Naqaab]] and thereafter he sang "Jashn-e-Bahaaran" from ''[[Jodhaa Akbar]]'', "Arziyan" from ''[[Delhi-6]]'', "Kun Faya Kun" from ''[[Rockstar (2011 film)|Rockstar]]'', "Guzarish" from ''[[Ghajini (2008 film)|Ghajini]]'', "Aa Jao Meri Tamanna" from ''[[Ajab Prem Ki Ghazab Kahani]]'', "Gale Lag Ja" from ''[[De Dana Dan]]'', "Tu Hi Haqeeqat" from ''[[Tum Mile]]'', "Tum Tak" from ''[[Raanjhanaa]]'', Jab Tak Hai Jaan title track from the film ''[[Jab Tak Hai Jaan]]'', "Deewana Kar Raha Hai" from ''[[Raaz 3]]'', "Ishaqzaade" title track from the film ''[[Ishaqzaade]]'', "Galat Baat Hai" from ''[[Main Tera Hero]]'', [[Daawat-e-Ishq]] film's title track, "Maula" from ''[[Wazir (film)|Wazir]]'', "Nagada Nagada" from ''[[Jab We Met]]'', "Tu Jo Mila" from ''[[Bajrangi Bhaijaan]]'', "Saanson Ke" from ''[[Raees (2017 film)|Raees]]'', "Kuch Nahi" from ''[[Tubelight (2017 Hindi film)|Tubelight]]'', and "Naina Lade" from ''[[Dabangg 3]]''. He is doing playback singing in various regional languages like Bengali, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Tamil, Telugu and Urdu. He judged reality shows like ''[[Sa Re Ga Ma Pa L'il Champs 2011]]'' on [[Zee TV]] Great Music Gurukul 2015 on [[Colors Bangla]], [[Sa Re Ga Ma Pa L'il Champs 2017]] on [[Zee TV]] and [[Indian Idol]] Season 10 in 2018 on [[Sony Entertainment Television]]. ==Awards and nominations==
===Awards won===
* [[International Indian Film Academy Awards|IIFA]] Awards 2009 in The Best Male Playback Singer category for the song ''Jashn-E-Bahara'' from the film [[Jodhaa Akbar]].<ref>{{cite news |date=18 May 2015 |title=Javed Ali, Nooran sisters to pay tribute to AR Rahman at IIFA 2015 |url=http://www.radioandmusic.com/entertainment/editorial/news/javed-ali-nooran-sisters-pay-tribute-ar-rahman-iifa-2015-150518 |work=radioandmusic.com}}</ref>
* [[Screen Awards]] 2012 (19th) in The Best Playback Singer Category for the Title Track of the film named [[Ishaqzaade]].
* Own Radio Mirchi's [[Mirchi Music Awards]] (4 Awards) in 2012 in the following categories: Best Song Representing the Sufi Tradition – ''Kun Faya Kun'' from the film [[Rockstar (2011 film)|Rockstar]], Best Indipop Song "Mera Kya Saheb Haitera" from "Dil Ki Baatein", Best Album of the year and Mirchi listener Award for [[Rockstar (2011 film)|Rockstar]].
* Uttar Pradesh Government has Honored Javed Ali with the highest prestigious award of the state – '''Yash Bharti Samman'''.
* [[India TV]] awarded Javed Ali with the Yuwa Award 2015.

==Social works==
Along with playback singing & live performing, Javed Ali is associated with various social works. He performs live for various social causes to raise funds. In 2016 Javed Ali performed in Mumbai at a fundraiser event for war widows.<ref>{{cite news |date=12 August 2016 |title=Singer Javed Ali to perform at fundraiser for war widows |url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/mumbai/Singer-Javed-Ali-to-perform-at-fundraiser-for-war-widows/articleshow/53669951.cms |work=The Times of India}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=11 August 2016 |title=Singer Javed Ali to perform at fundraiser for war widows |url=http://indiatoday.intoday.in/story/singer-javed-ali-to-perform-at-fundraiser-for-war-widows/1/738426.html |work=.intoday.in}}</ref>
