'''Måneskin''' ({{IPA-da|ˈmɔːnəˌske̝nˀ|lang}};<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ordnet.dk/ddo/ordbog?query=måneskin|title=måneskin|language=da|work=Den Danske Ordbog|access-date=January 10, 2019|archive-date=April 12, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210412052643/https://ordnet.dk/ddo/ordbog?query=m%C3%A5neskin|url-status=live}}</ref> {{langnf||Danish |moonlight}}) are an Italian [[Rock music|rock]] band formed in [[Rome]] in 2016. The band is composed of vocalist [[Damiano David]], bassist Victoria De Angelis, guitarist Thomas Raggi, and drummer Ethan Torchio.<ref name="RSBio">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.it/artista/maneskin/|work=[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]|language=Italian|title=Biografia Måneskin|access-date=5 October 2018|archive-date=5 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190305234338/https://www.rollingstone.it/artista/maneskin/|url-status=live}}</ref> Performing in the streets in their early days, they rose to prominence after finishing second in the [[X Factor (Italian season 11)|eleventh season]] of the Italian [[talent show]] ''[[X Factor (Italian TV series)|X Factor]]'' in 2017. Their international breakthrough ensued when the foursome won the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021]] for {{esccnty|Italy|y=2021}} with the song "[[Zitti e buoni]]".  

The band have released two studio albums, entitled ''[[Il ballo della vita]]'' (2018) and ''[[Teatro d'ira: Vol. I]]'' (2021). They have also released one extended-play and ten singles, which have topped the Italian and European music charts. They have collected 34 platinum and 7 gold certifications from [[Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana|FIMI]],<ref>{{Cite web |url = https://www.amica.it/2021/06/15/maneskin-spotify-ascoltati-mondo-dischi-doro-date-concerti/ |title = I Maneskin i più ascoltati al mondo! Dischi d’oro e concerti già esauriti |date = 15 June 2021 |access-date = 16 June 2021 |website=[[Amica (magazine)|Amica]]|author = Antonella Catena|language=it}}</ref> and have sold over one million records in Italy before their post-Eurovision breakthrough.<ref name="Vogue36">{{Cite web |url = https://www.vogue.co.uk/miss-vogue/article/maneskin-interview |title = "We Are Just Four Friends Doing What We Love": Eurovision Breakout Star Måneskin’s Damiano David Plans On Staying Humble |date = 3 June 2021 |access-date = 3 June 2021 |website=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]|author = Scarlett Conlon}}</ref> Up to that point in their career, their most successful Italian single was "[[Torna a casa]]". In 2021, Måneskin became the first Italian rock band to reach the top 10 on the [[UK Singles Chart]], with their songs "Zitti e buoni", "[[I Wanna Be Your Slave]]" and their cover of [[The Four Seasons (band)|The Four Seasons]]'s "[[Beggin'#Måneskin version|Beggin']]" reaching the top 10 on the [[Billboard Global 200#Billboard Global Excl. U.S.|''Billboard'' Global Excl. U.S.]] chart, collecting multiple international certifications for sales of over three million copies internationally, and four million in total.

== History ==

=== 2015–2016: Beginnings on the street ===
[[File:Måneskin_logo.svg|thumb|right|The logo of the band]]

Members [[Damiano David]], Victoria De Angelis, and Thomas Raggi first met each other as students during their [[Secondary education in Italy#Liceo|high school]] days in [[Rome]], with Ethan Torchio from nearby [[Frosinone]] soon joining them when they were inquiring for a drummer to complete the line-up.<ref name="Agenzia Italia">{{cite web|url=https://www.agi.it/spettacolo/musica/maneskinxfactordamiano_daviditunes-3158308/news/2017-11-24/|work=[[Agenzia Giornalistica Italia]]|language=it|access-date=16 June 2021|title=Tutto quello che c'è da sapere sui Maneskin|date=24 November 2017|author=Sonia Montrella}}</ref><ref name="Mamme2018">{{cite news |url = https://www.corriere.it/sette/18_marzo_29/maneskin-cea76b6c-31b4-11e8-a935-2154f7b1bc02.shtml |date = 29 March 2018 |title = Perché i Måneskin fanno ballare anche le mamme delle fan |access-date = 16 June 2021 |newspaper=[[Corriere della Sera]] |first=Luca |last=Mastrantonio |language=it}}</ref><ref name="NYTConquer">{{cite news|first=Elisabetta|last=Povoledo|access-date=16 June 2021|title=They Won Eurovision. The Pulse Contest also marked a turning point in their career, since they had to start writing their own songs. The competition led them to perform at the Felt Music Club & School in front of 200–300 people, and they later went on to win first prize.<ref name="La lettura"/><ref name="Corriere December 2017">{{cite web|url=https://www.corriere.it/la-lettura/cards/i-maneskin-l-altra-sorpresa-x-factor/gli-esordi.shtml|work=[[Corriere della Sera]]|language=Italian|title=Måneskin, gli altri vincitori di X Factor|author=Jessica Chia|date=31 December 2017|access-date=2 November 2018|archive-date=20 January 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210120171258/https://www.corriere.it/la-lettura/cards/i-maneskin-l-altra-sorpresa-x-factor/gli-esordi.shtml|url-status=live}}</ref>

They later performed as [[buskers]] in the streets of the Colli Portuensi district of Rome, as well as in the historical centre of Rome, including in [[Via del Corso]].<ref name="Repubblica November 2017"/> One of their first live concerts outside their native city took place in [[Faenza]], at the 2016 Meeting of Independent Record Labels. === 2017–2019: ''X Factor'' and debut breakthrough with ''Il ballo della vita'' ===

In 2017, they took part in the [[X Factor (Italian season 11)|eleventh season]] of the Italian [[talent show]] ''[[X Factor (Italian TV series)|X Factor]]'', under the guidance of mentor [[Manuel Agnelli]], finishing in second place behind [[Lorenzo Licitra]].<ref name="Bolognini 2017">{{cite web|url=http://www.repubblica.it/spettacoli/tv-radio/2017/12/15/news/x_factor_finale_licitra_maneskin-184165621/|title=TV X Factor, la vittoria di Lorenzo Licitra: la voce del tenore batte il glam rock dei Maneskin|work=[[la Repubblica]]|access-date=5 October 2018|author=Luigi Bolognini|date=15 December 2017|archive-date=5 October 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181005031219/http://www.repubblica.it/spettacoli/tv-radio/2017/12/15/news/x_factor_finale_licitra_maneskin-184165621/|url-status=live}}</ref> There they covered "[[Beggin'#Måneskin version|Beggin']]" by [[The Four Seasons (band)|the Four Seasons]], "[[Take Me Out (song)|Take Me Out]]" by [[Franz Ferdinand (band)|Franz Ferdinand]], and "[[Somebody Told Me]]" by [[the Killers]], among others. The original song they performed during the show, "[[Chosen (song)|Chosen]]", was released as a single on 24 November 2017 by Sony Music and charted at number two on the Italian [[FIMI]] Singles Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://radiodate.it/radio-date/maneskin-chosen-177082-24-11-2017-radiodate/|publisher=Radiodate.it|language=Italian|title=Chosen - Måneskin|access-date=2018-10-04|archive-date=2018-10-05|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181005030811/http://radiodate.it/radio-date/maneskin-chosen-177082-24-11-2017-radiodate/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://italiancharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=M%E5neskin&titel=Chosen&cat=s|publisher=Italiancharts.com. === 2020–present: ''Teatro d'ira'' and global breakthrough ===
From late 2019 until spring 2020, the band lived in [[London]] where they worked on their music style and new material.<ref name="WWD"/><ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.londraitalia.com/cultura/maneskin-a-londra-tra-live-e-cambi-di-vita/|title = Måneskin a Londra, tra live e cambi di vita|author = Alessandro Allocca|work = Londra Italia.com|date = 7 November 2019|access-date = 28 May 2021|language = it}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.revenews.it/interviste/2021/03/22/maneskin-teatro-dira-vol-1-intervista/|title = Måneskin: "Scriviamo e parliamo di noi, senza metafore"|author = Grazia Cicciotti|work = Revenews, [[Il Messaggero]] |date = 22 March 2021|access-date = 15 June 2021|language = it}}</ref> In October 2020, their seventh single "[[Vent'anni (Måneskin song)|Vent'anni]]" was released.<ref name="Ventanni">{{Cite web|url = https://www.rockol.it/news-717724/maneskin-vent-anni-nuovo-singolo-intervista-video|title = Ecco cosa hanno fatto i Maneskin a Londra|author = Mattia Marzi|work = Rockol|date = 30 October 2020|access-date = 25 May 2021|language = it|archive-date = 25 November 2020|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20201125084438/https://www.rockol.it/news-717724/maneskin-vent-anni-nuovo-singolo-intervista-video|url-status = live}}</ref> On 6 March 2021, the band won the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2021]] with the song "[[Zitti e buoni]]", ahead of runners-up [[Francesca Michielin]] and [[Fedez]], and [[Ermal Meta]]. Their win was viewed by many to be a surprise, as rock is considered an atypical contesting music genre in the history of the Festival.<ref name="NYTConquer"/><ref>{{cite web |date=7 March 2021 |url=https://www.billboard.it/news/festival-di-sanremo/maneskin-vincitori-sanremo-2021/2021/03/0758142/ |title=Måneskin: "Non ci aspettavamo una reazione così veloce dal pubblico, siamo pronti a restituire l'entusiasmo" |website=Billboard Italia |access-date=16 June 2021 |language=it}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |date=7 March 2021 |url=https://www.billboard.it/news/festival-di-sanremo/la-vittoria-dei-maneskin-non-e-trasgressione/2021/03/0758147/ |title=La vittoria dei Måneskin non è trasgressione |website=Billboard Italia |author=Silvia Danielli |access-date=16 June 2021 |language=it}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.iltempo.it/tv-news/2021/03/02/video/sanremo-i-maneskin-raccontano-il-loro-brano-zitti-e-buoni--26394985/|title=Sanremo, i Maneskin raccontano il loro brano "Zitti e buoni"|date=2 March 2021|work=[[Il Tempo]]|access-date=4 March 2021|archive-date=10 March 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210310080356/https://www.iltempo.it/tv-news/2021/03/02/video/sanremo-i-maneskin-raccontano-il-loro-brano-zitti-e-buoni--26394985/|url-status=live}}</ref> Prior to Sanremo, the band announced its new recording project entitled ''Teatro d'ira'', with the second studio album ''[[Teatro d'ira: Vol. I|Vol. I]]'' released on 19 March 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tg24.sky.it/spettacolo/musica/2021/02/11/maneskin-teatro-ira-album|title=I Maneskin annunciano il nuovo album 'Teatro d'ira vol. 1': info e data d'uscita|work=[[Sky TG24]]|date=11 February 2021|access-date=4 March 2021|language=it|archive-date=11 February 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210211122324/https://tg24.sky.it/spettacolo/musica/2021/02/11/maneskin-teatro-ira-album|url-status=live}}</ref>

==== Eurovision Song Contest ====
{{See also|Italy in the Eurovision Song Contest 2021}}[[File:ESC 2021 Rotterdam 1st Semi Jury Show Italy2.jpg|thumb|The band performing "[[Zitti e buoni]]" during a rehearsal for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021]]]]

As the winners of the Sanremo Music Festival 2021, Måneskin were designated as the [[Italy in the Eurovision Song Contest 2021|Italian representatives]] at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2021]], held in [[Rotterdam]], [[Netherlands]], with "Zitti e buoni".<ref name="sanremo">{{Cite web|last=|date=2021-03-07|title=Sanremo 2021, Maneskin: "Ora l'Eurovision"|url=https://www.adnkronos.com/sanremo-2021-maneskin-ora-leurovision_6HS7o0N42CgrhyMeKNjDTO|url-status=live|access-date=2021-06-12|website=Adnkronos}}</ref> Arriving as main favorites to win, on 22 May the band confirmed the predictions by going on to win the contest with an energetic performance, having received a total of 524 points, thus giving Italy its third Eurovision triumph after [[Gigliola Cinquetti]] in [[Eurovision Song Contest 1964|1964]] and [[Toto Cutugno]] in [[Eurovision Song Contest 1990|1990]]. Måneskin also became the first band and "Zitti e buoni" the first rock song to win the contest since [[Lordi]] with "[[Hard Rock Hallelujah]]" for [[Finland in the Eurovision Song Contest|Finland]] in [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006|2006]].<ref name="NYTEuro">{{cite news|first1=Alex|last1=Marshall|first2=Elisabeth|last2=Vincentelli|access-date=1 June 2021|title=Italy wins the 2021 Eurovision Song Contest.|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/05/22/world/europe/italy-maneskin-wins-2021-eurovision.html|newspaper=[[The New York Times]]|date=22 May 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=4 June 2021|title=Måneskin: The Eurovision winners storming the UK charts|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-57331991|access-date=16 June 2021|website=[[BBC]]|author=Steve Holden}}</ref><ref name="eurovision_2021_winner_wiwibloggs">{{Cite news|date=22 May 2021|title=Eurovision 2021 winner: Italy's Måneskin takes the victory with "Zitti e buoni"|work=[[Wiwibloggs]]|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2021/05/22/eurovision-2021-winner-italys-maneskin-takes-the-victory-with-zitti-e-buoni/265446/|access-date=22 May 2021|archive-date=22 May 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210522224945/https://wiwibloggs.com/2021/05/22/eurovision-2021-winner-italys-maneskin-takes-the-victory-with-zitti-e-buoni/265446/|url-status=live}}</ref> Upon accepting the winner's trophy, Damiano shouted into the microphone:<ref name="NYTEuro"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2021/may/22/eurovision-2021-maneskin-triumphs-for-italy-in-rotterdam|title=Eurovision 2021: Måneskin triumph for Italy in Rotterdam|date=23 May 2021|access-date=16 June 2021|website=[[The Guardian]]|author=Martin Belam}}</ref><ref name="BBCRock">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-57530407|title=Eurovision winners Måneskin enter UK top 10|date=19 June 2021|access-date=20 June 2021|website=[[BBC]]}}</ref>
