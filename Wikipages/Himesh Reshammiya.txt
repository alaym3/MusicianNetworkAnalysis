'''Himesh Reshammiya''' (born 23 July 1973) is an Indian playback singer, music director, songwriter, film & music producer and actor in [[Bollywood]]. He started his career as music director in the film ''[[Pyaar Kiya To Darna Kya (1998 film)|Pyaar Kiya To Darna Kya]]'' in 1998 and made his acting debut with the film ''[[Aap Kaa Surroor (film)|Aap Kaa Surroor]]'' in 2007.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Paul |first1=Ushnota |title=Himesh Reshammiya on Salman Khan, his survival mantra and making music |url=https://www.filmfare.com/features/himesh-reshammiya-on-salman-khan-his-survival-mantra-and-making-music-13176.html |access-date=27 April 2020 |work=filmfare.com |date=3 May 2016 |language=en |archive-date=9 July 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160709041158/http://www.filmfare.com/features/himesh-reshammiya-on-salman-khan-his-survival-mantra-and-making-music-13176.html |url-status=live}}</ref>

==Music and film career==
===Early career (1998–2002)===
Reshammiya began producing TV series such as ''Andaz'', ''Ahha'', ''Aman'', ''Aashiqui'', ''Amar Prem'' and ''[[Jaan (TV series)|Jaan]]'' on [[Zee TV]]. He also composed title tracks for some of these shows.<ref>{{cite news |title=Himesh Reshammiya was a Television producer – Times of India |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/did-you-know/Himesh-Reshammiya-was-a-Television-producer/articleshow/3534299.cms |access-date=25 April 2020 |work=The Times of India |date=28 September 2008 |language=en}}</ref>
Reshammiya's father was supposed to make a film with [[Salman Khan]] in the 1990s, but this did not take place; when Khan heard one of Reshammiya's compositions, he was impressed and promised to let him compose music for one of his films. In 1998, Reshammiya made his debut as a music composer with the Salman Khan film ''[[Pyaar Kiya To Darna Kya (1998 film)|Pyaar Kiya To Darna Kya]]'', composing two songs for the project: "Pyaar Kiya To Darna Kya" and "Tum Par Hum Hai Aatke". Both songs became hits.<ref>{{cite news |title=Himesh Reshammiya credits Salman for his success |url=https://gulfnews.com/entertainment/bollywood/himesh-reshammiya-credits-salman-for-his-success-1.1686371 |access-date=25 April 2020 |work=gulfnews.com |language=en |archive-date=11 October 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201011143953/https://gulfnews.com/entertainment/bollywood/himesh-reshammiya-credits-salman-for-his-success-1.1686371 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Salman has better sense of music than Bhushan Kumar: Himesh – Times of India |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/news/Salman-Khan-has-better-sense-of-music-than-Bhushan-Kumar-Himesh-Reshammiya/articleshow/34477514.cms |access-date=25 April 2020 |work=The Times of India |language=en |archive-date=2 October 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141002233753/http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/news/Salman-Khan-has-better-sense-of-music-than-Bhushan-Kumar-Himesh-Reshammiya/articleshow/34477514.cms |url-status=live}}</ref>

He went on to compose music for ''[[Bandhan (1998 film)|Bandhan]]'' and ''[[Hello Brother (1999 film)|Hello Brother]]'', together with other composers. His first movie as a solo composer was another Salman Khan production, ''[[Dulhan Hum Le Jayenge]]''.
Reshammiya gained some prominence when he composed music for ''[[Humraaz]]''. This earned him a nomination for [[Filmfare Award for Best Music Director|Filmfare Award for Best Music Direction]].

===2003–2010===
Reshammiya gained more prominence when he composed songs for another Salman Khan film, ''[[Tere Naam]]'' (2003), for which he won several accolades, including the [[Zee Cine Award for Best Music Director]] and [[Screen Award for Best Music Director]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Soulful melodies like 'Tere Naam' in Salman's Prem Ratan Dhan Payo |url=https://www.indicine.com/movies/bollywood/soulful-melodies-like-tere-naam-in-salmans-prem-ratan-dhan-payo/ |access-date=27 April 2020 |archive-date=8 November 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151108170336/http://www.indicine.com/movies/bollywood/soulful-melodies-like-tere-naam-in-salmans-prem-ratan-dhan-payo/ |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Raj |first1=Jem |title=Celebrating 11 Years of Tere Naam |url=https://bollyspice.com/celebrating-11-years-tere-naam/ |access-date=27 April 2020 |work=BollySpice.com – The latest movies, interviews in Bollywood |date=15 August 2014}}</ref>
In 2004, he wrote music for ten films, among them ''[[Aitraaz]]''. In 2005, he worked on ten more films, including ''[[Aashiq Banaya Apne]]'' and ''[[Maine Pyaar Kyun Kiya?]]''.

At this point, he also began working as a playback singer with the song "[[Aashiq Banaya Apne#Soundtrack|Aashiq Banaya Aapne]]", which earned him numerous awards, including [[Filmfare Award for Best Male Playback Singer]], [[IIFA Award for Best Male Playback]], and [[Zee Cine Award for Best Playback Singer – Male|Zee Cine Award for Best Male Playback Singer]].<ref name="Aashiq Banaya">{{cite news |title=Himesh Reshammiya reveals how he created Aashiq Banaya Aapne |url=https://www.indiatoday.in/movies/celebrities/story/himesh-reshammiya-reveals-how-he-created-aashiq-banaya-aapne-1604249-2019-09-28 |access-date=27 April 2020 |work=India Today |language=en |archive-date=14 February 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200214061530/https://www.indiatoday.in/movies/celebrities/story/himesh-reshammiya-reveals-how-he-created-aashiq-banaya-aapne-1604249-2019-09-28 |url-status=live}}</ref>

As a singer, Reshammiya has recorded multiple popular songs, such as "Aashiq Banaya Aapne", "Tera Suroor", "Zara Jhoom Jhoom", "[[Jhalak Dikhlaja]]", "Andaz Apna Apna", "Afsana", "Shakalaka Boom Boom", "Meri Awaargi", "Hookah Bar", "Chalao Na Naino Se", and "Tandoori Nights". His music has a style of composition based on a fusion of Western and [[Indian classical music]], and Western and Indian Rock music accompanied by [[techno]] beats.
Reshammiya's first studio album [[Aap Kaa Surroor]] sold 55 million copies,<ref>{{cite news |title=Himesh Reshammiya's Aap Kaa Surroor Rules With The '55 Million Copies' Record In India Coming Very Close Only To Michael Jackson's Thriller Worldwide? | url=https://www.koimoi.com/bollywood-news/himesh-reshammiyas-aap-kaa-surroor-rules-with-the-55-million-copies-record-in-india-coming-very-close-only-to-michael-jacksons-thriller-worldwidede/}}</ref> becoming one of the highest-selling albums in India, in comparison [[Michael Jackson]]'s [[Thriller (album)|Thriller]] sold 65 Million copies worldwide.

Reshammiya has also worked as an actor, beginning with the film ''[[Aap Kaa Surroor (film)|Aap Kaa Surroor]]''.<ref>{{cite news |title=Is Himesh Reshammiya worth Rs 5 crore? |url=http://articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com/2008-04-07/news/27696514_1_amar-prem-himesh-reshammiya-rat-race |access-date=22 May 2012 |newspaper=The Economic Times |date=7 April 2008 |author=Gaurav Malani |author2=Chuman Das|archive-date=21 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131021132031/http://articles.economictimes.indiatimes.com/2008-04-07/news/27696514_1_amar-prem-himesh-reshammiya-rat-race|url-status=live}}</ref>

He was the first Indian singer to perform at [[Wembley Arena]] in London and the Heineken music hall in Amsterdam.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://movies.indiatimes.com/news-gossip/news/Himesh-Reshammiyas-first-English-album/articleshow/6318206.cms |title=Himesh Reshammiya's first English album |date=16 August 2010 |access-date=16 August 2010 |work=The Times of India|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100823131922/http://movies.indiatimes.com/news-gossip/news/Himesh-Reshammiyas-first-English-album/articleshow/6318206.cms |archive-date=23 August 2010}}</ref>

Reshammiya was actively involved in mentoring and judging the singing competition ''[[Sa Re Ga Ma Pa|Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Challenge]]'' on [[Zee TV]]. His group was known as Jai Mata Di Let's Rock Gharana{{citation needed|date=December 2017}}. He was the winning mentor of the show in [[Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Challenge 2007|2007]]. In [[Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Challenge 2009|2009]], [[Vaishali Mhade]], a female contestant from his Jai Mata Di Let's Rock Gharana group, won the singing competition. He is also the team captain, judge, and mentor on [[Star Plus]]'s show ''[[Music Ka Maha Muqqabla]]'' for the team Himesh's Warriors. Reshammiya was also the judge of the musical reality show ''[[Sur Kshetra]]'', which was telecasted on [[Sahara One]]. He appeared as the special guest in the Grand Finale of ''Jo Jeeta Wohi Superstar 2'', where he also trained the top four contestants.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-06-19/tv/32316224_1_finale-grand-success-contestants |title=Himesh Reshammiya is back on television! |date=20 June 2012 | access-date=1 July 2013 | archive-date=21 October 2013 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131021143514/http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-06-19/tv/32316224_1_finale-grand-success-contestants |work=[[The Times of India]] | url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Controversies===
In his early singing career, when he was criticized for his nasal singing style, Reshammiya stated that [[R. D. Burman]] also sang through his nose. After this comment, [[Asha Bhosle]], legendary singer and wife of Burman, said, "If anyone says Burman sa'ab sang through his nose, he should be slapped". Reshammiya subsequently clarified that he never intended to insult R. D. Burman.<ref>{{cite news |title=Asha Bhosle wants to slap Himesh – Times of India |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/lucknow-times/Asha-Bhosle-wants-to-slap-Himesh/articleshow/531496.cms |access-date=25 April 2020 |work=The Times of India |date=22 November 2006}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Asha Bhosle wants to slap Himesh: The Indian Express |url=https://zeenews.india.com/home/asha-bhosle-wants-to-slap-himesh-the-indian-express_337356.html |access-date=25 April 2020 |work=Zee News |date=23 November 2006 |language=en |archive-date=11 October 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201011143956/https://zeenews.india.com/home/asha-bhosle-wants-to-slap-himesh-the-indian-express_337356.html |url-status=live }}</ref>

In his debut film as an actor, ''Aap Kaa Surroor'', Reshammiya added the song "Tanhaiyyan" from [[Boney Kapoor]]'s proposed film ''[[Milenge Milenge]]''.{{when|date=March 2017}}<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.glamsham.com/movies/scoops/06/nov/11_himesh_reshammiya_boney_kapoor.asp |title=Reshammiya, Boney Kapoor in a song row |access-date=26 November 2006|archive-date=14 March 2007|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070314030344/http://www.glamsham.com/movies/scoops/06/nov/11_himesh_reshammiya_boney_kapoor.asp|url-status=live}}</ref> Initially, Reshammiya had composed the song for Kapoor's film, but later decided to use it for his own movie. Tips, which held the audio rights of ''Milenge Milenge'', accused Reshammiya of copyright violation, as audio rights for "Aap Kaa Surroor – The Real Luv Story" were acquired by [[Super Cassettes Industries T-Series|T-Series]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.businessofcinema.com/index.php?file=story&id=1626 |title=Himesh Reshammiya violates copyright act |date=7 November 2006 |access-date=26 November 2006|archive-date=16 March 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120316073329/http://www.businessofcinema.com/index.php?file=story&id=1626|url-status=live}}</ref> However, Reshammiya defended himself, saying that he had made Kapoor listen to the song, but it was not included in ''Milenge Milenge''. He also claimed to have tried to get in touch with Kapoor for seven months, and since there was no progress on the film, he decided to use the song for his own movie.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/delhi-times/tanhaiyyan-is-mine/articleshow/373552.cms |title=Tanhai |date=8 November 2006 |access-date=26 November 2006 |work=The Times of India|archive-date=27 November 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171127124902/https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/delhi-times/tanhaiyyan-is-mine/articleshow/373552.cms|url-status=live}}</ref>
