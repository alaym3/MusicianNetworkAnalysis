'''Omar Souhaili''' (born in Casablanca, [[Morocco]]) better known by his stage name
'''Dizzy DROS''', is a [[Moroccans|Moroccan]] rapper, songwriter and producer.
The rapper became known in 2011 with his first single and music video, "Cazafonia".
His music being played on Moroccan radio stations like Hit radio, MedRadio.. allowed the rapper to gain popularity being one of the first Moroccan rap songs to reach over 1 million views on YouTube.
His authentic style has allowed him to become one of the leading urban music artists coming out of Morocco.
From generating over 150 million views on YouTube to selling out shows internationally (Paris, France / Istanbul, Turkey), he has become a rare asset to the urban music industry in Morocco.

==Early life and career==
Born in Casablanca, Morocco, Dizzy DROS was raised in Bine Lemdoune, a popular neighbourhood in Casablanca. DROS stands for 'Da Rhymes of Streets' although he is also known as Mr. Cazafonia. Dizzy DROS started to write songs and to develop his personal style in rap at the age of 17.<ref>{{cite web|title=DROS SUR HIT RADIO|url=http://www.hitradio.ma/musique/artistes/dros/|publisher=Hit Radio|accessdate=30 January 2014}}</ref>
 
For over four years the rapper worked on different demos singles before releasing "Cazafonia". It was at the beginning of his career that DROS met Khalid Douache (DJ Key), a member of the Moroccan hip hop scene who directed the video "Cazafonia" in 2011.

In September 2013, DROS performed in the music festival [[Boulevard des Jeunes Musiciens|L'Boulevard des Jeunes Musiciens]].
Later on, in December 2013, Dizzy DROS performed in the Centre Culturel Renaissance in Rabat as the invited artist of the project "Fábrica de Rimas".  Dizzy DROS shared stage with the Colombian rappers C15 and the Moroccan rap group [[H-Kayne]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Fábrica de Rimas en Rabat|url=http://www.aecid.es/ES/cultura/Paginas/Actividades/Actividades%202013/F%C3%A1brica-de-Rimas-en-Rabat-.aspx|publisher=AECID|accessdate=30 January 2014}}</ref>

His first album '3azzy 3ando Stylo' (33S) was released November 22, 2013. The official release took place at the [[Instituto Cervantes]] of Casablanca.  The album contains 21 tracks including an intro, an outro and two skits, as well as collaborations with Moroccan rappers such as Muslim, Shayfeen, M-Doc and Would Chaab. The songs of the album dwell on the life of Moroccan urban youth.<ref>{{cite web|title=Casablanca MC Dizzy DROS Is Taking Moroccan Rap By Storm|url=http://www.okayafrica.com/2013/11/17/dizzy-dros-3ando-stylo-morocco-rap/|publisher=Okayafrica|accessdate=6 February 2014}}</ref>

Dizzy DROS has participated in TV and Radio shows. DROS was invited in January 2013 to the talk show ''Génération News'' to participate in a debate on Moroccan artists and Internet. DROS also appeared in the programs ''Ni9ach 2.0'' and ''Ajial'', both in the Moroccan public TV channel [[2M TV|2M]]. The rapper was featured many times in the 'Momo Morning Show' show on Hit Radio and the program 'Arts Mag' in Medina FM. Dizzy DROS has also been interviewed by Moroccan written press such as the newspaper [[Libération (Morocco)|Libération]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Ouassat|first=Mehdi|title=Le rappeur Dizzy Dros : L'autoproduction me permet de préserver ma liberté|url=http://www.libe.ma/Le-rappeur-Dizzy-Dros-L-autoproduction-me-permet-de-preserver-ma-liberte_a44336.html|accessdate=30 January 2014|newspaper=Libération|date=November 27, 2013}}</ref>

On February 1, 2014, Dizzy DROS was invited to perform at the 7th Edition of the Maroc Web Awards that took place at the [https://web.archive.org/web/20140128080029/http://www.tnmv.ma/def.asp?codelangue=23 National Theatre Mohamed V] in [[Rabat]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Sakhi|first=Hafsa|title=Maroc Web Awards: 13 lauréats pour la 7ème édition|url=http://www.lematin.ma/exclusif/2014/maroc-web-awards_13-laureats-pour-la-7eme-edition/196074.html|accessdate=3 February 2014|newspaper=Le Matin|date=February 2, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Et les gagnants de la septième édition des Maroc Web Awards sont...|url=http://www.marocwebawards.com/et-les-gagnants-de-la-septieme-edition-des-maroc-web-awards-sont/|accessdate=5 February 2014|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140301190641/http://www.marocwebawards.com/et-les-gagnants-de-la-septieme-edition-des-maroc-web-awards-sont/|archivedate=1 March 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Roudaby|first=Youssef|title=Les Maroc Web Awards récompensent la culture geek|url=http://www.telquel-online.com/content/les-maroc-web-awards-r%C3%A9compensent-la-culture-geek|accessdate=6 February 2014|newspaper=TelQuel|date=February 5, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140209182218/http://telquel-online.com/content/les-maroc-web-awards-r%C3%A9compensent-la-culture-geek|archive-date=9 February 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2019, Dizzy Dros gained even bigger attention by releasing "Moutanabbi", a track in which he dissed Moroccan rapper Don Bigg.

Dizzy Dross voiced the character "Measurehead" in the 2019 video game, [[Disco Elysium]].

==Critical response==
''3azzy 3ando Stylo'' is considered by the magazine [[TelQuel]] as "one of the best hip hop albums of these last ten years" in Morocco.<ref>{{cite news|last=Saadi|first=Meryem|title=Dizzy Dros, le rappeur qui monte|url=http://telquel-online.com/content/dizzy-dros-le-rappeur-qui-monte|accessdate=30 January 2014|newspaper=TelQuel|date=December 8, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140202100007/http://telquel-online.com/content/dizzy-dros-le-rappeur-qui-monte|archive-date=2 February 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>  The magazine [[Aujourd'hui Le Maroc]] described Dizzy DROS' rap style as "an impressive flow, an improvisation that flirts with the mastery of words."<ref>{{cite news|last=Nigrou|first=Imane|title=Fnac Casablanca : Dizzy Dros et Nhass en show case|url=http://www.aujourdhui.ma/maroc-actualite/magazine/fnac-casablanca-dizzy-dros-et-nhass-en-show-case-106873.html|accessdate=30 January 2014|newspaper=Aujourd'hui Le Maroc|date=December 19, 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131225064154/http://www.aujourdhui.ma/maroc-actualite/magazine/fnac-casablanca-dizzy-dros-et-nhass-en-show-case-106873.html|archivedate=25 December 2013}}</ref>
