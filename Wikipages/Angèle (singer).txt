'''Angèle Van Laeken''' ({{IPA-nl|vɑn ˈlaːkə(n)}}; born 3 December 1995), better known by her stage [[mononym]] '''Angèle''' ({{IPA-fr|ɑ̃ʒɛl|lang}}), is a Belgian singer-songwriter, musician, pianist, record producer and actress.

She was one of 2018's biggest breakout acts in French and Belgian pop,<ref name=RTL>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rtl.fr/culture/arts-spectacles/rencontre-decouvrez-la-chanteuse-angele-dans-laissez-vous-tenter-7794012151|title=Angèle : la jeune chanteuse belge est la sensation musicale du moment|website=[[RTL (French radio)|RTL]]|access-date=20 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190713035410/https://www.rtl.fr/culture/arts-spectacles/rencontre-decouvrez-la-chanteuse-angele-dans-laissez-vous-tenter-7794012151|archive-date=13 July 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> breaking [[Stromae]]'s record for weeks at the top of the Belgian singles charts with her 2018 single "[[Tout oublier]]" which features her brother [[Roméo Elvis]].<ref name=Libre>{{Cite news|url=https://www.lalibre.be/culture/musique/angele-detrone-stromae-5c6a6069d8ad5878f0c8ec66|date=18 February 2019|title=Angèle détrône Stromae|newspaper=[[La Libre Belgique]]}}</ref>

== Early life==
Angèle van Laeken was born on 3 December 1995, in [[Uccle]], [[Brussels-Capital Region]]. Angèle is the daughter of the singer [[Marka (singer)|Marka]] and of the actress Laurence Bibot. Her brother is the rapper [[Roméo Elvis]].

Her father encouraged her from an early age to learn the piano, which inspired her musical career.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hbli1k1ruhA|title=Angèle : l'interview "première fois" {{!}} JACK|via=YouTube|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref>

Angèle spent a difficult adolescence in a strict Catholic school before attending the Decroly School. The artistic options at this high school helped develop her musical skills.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.greenroom.fr/120224-angele-romeo-elvis|title=Angèle, ou comment cesser d'être "la soeur de" Romeo Elvis|website=Greenroom|date=27 November 2017|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref> She then enrolled in the Jazz Studio Antwerp for a jazz education before joining her father's band.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tijd.be/sabato/algemeen/angele-klaar-voor-grote-doorbraak/9993849.html|title=Angèle klaar voor grote doorbraak|work=De Tijd|last=Bonne|first=Laura|date=21 March 2018|access-date=1 December 2020|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201201152605/https://www.tijd.be/sabato/algemeen/angele-klaar-voor-grote-doorbraak/9993849.html|archive-date=1 December 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==
===2018–present: Career beginnings and ''Brol''===
Angèle gave her first performances in cafés around [[Brussels]]. Notably at the restaurant Le Delecta, in [[Ixelles]], now closed.{{citation needed|date=August 2019}}

She was first noticed for her cover of [[Dick Annegarn]], "Bruxelles",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.franceinter.fr/emissions/dans-la-playlist-de-france-inter/dans-la-playlist-de-france-inter-05-janvier-2018|title=En une seule chanson, la jeune Angèle est déjà pressentie comme le phénomène de l'année 2018|website=[[France Inter]]|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref> and on [[Instagram]] videos where she mixed singing and comedy. In 2017, she was the support act for [[Ibeyi]] and the Belgian rapper [[Damso]], whom she also accompanied in his album Lithopédion.

Her first single, "La Loi de Murphy", was released in late 2017 and received millions of views on YouTube. A second single "Je veux tes yeux"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D0KrWq0i1QI|title=Angèle – Je veux tes yeux [CLIP OFFICIEL]|date=31 January 2018|via=YouTube|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref> was released in early 2018. The two video clips were directed by the Belgian photographer [[Charlotte Abramow]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lesinrocks.com/2018/02/07/style/la-photographe-charlotte-abramow-nous-parle-dangele-de-feminisme-et-de-spaghettis-111043326|title=La photographe Charlotte Abramow nous parle d'Angèle, de féminisme et de spaghettis|website=[[Les Inrockuptibles]]|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref>

In May 2018, she performed her first Parisian concert at the [[Le Trianon (theatre)|Trianon]] accompanied by [[MC Solaar]] on her cover of his song "Victime de la mode". She performed in several summer festivals<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://culturebox.francetvinfo.fr/musique/pop/on-y-etait-angele-ravit-son-public-parisien-au-trianon-273613|title=On y était : Angèle, la révélation pop, charme son public parisien au Trianon|website=[[France Info]]|date=23 May 2018|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref> in Belgium, France and Switzerland such as [[Les Ardentes]], [[Dour Festival|Dour]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dourfestival.eu/fr/soulwax-beth-ditto-diplo-big-boi-wizkid-and-24-other-new-artists/|title=Dour Festival|access-date=20 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180314104415/http://www.dourfestival.eu/fr/soulwax-beth-ditto-diplo-big-boi-wizkid-and-24-other-new-artists/|archive-date=14 March 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[Garorock]] and [[Rock Werchter]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rockwerchter.be/fr/blog/rock-werchter-presente-ses-belges|title=Rock Werchter 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180619214038/https://www.rockwerchter.be/fr/blog/rock-werchter-presente-ses-belges|archive-date=19 June 2018|url-status=dead|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref> In 2018, Angèle released her third single, "La Thune".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.moustique.be/21266/angele-se-depense-sur-la-thune|title=Angèle se dépense sur La Thune|website=[[Moustique]]|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref>

In August 2018, she announced the tracklist of her first album ''Brol'' on the Initial label.<ref name=RTL/> The album was released on 5 October 2018, the same day as her single duet with her brother, Roméo Elvis. This album was successful and resulted in Angèle invitation to perform on various French TV shows. The single, "Tout oublier", spent nine weeks at number 1 on the [[Ultratop]] charts, breaking the record for a Belgian artist formerly set by [[Stromae]].<ref name=Libre/>

In 2019, Angèle became one of the most sought for artists in France and [[Belgium]] and affirmed her feminism with the song "Balance ton quoi" in reference to the French #[[Me Too movement|metoo]] movement, [[BalanceTonPorc|Balance ton porc]]. [[Pierre Niney]] makes an appearance in the video alongside some of Angèle's friends to talk about struggles her female friends have encountered with men. The song is not about ridiculing men but about educating everyone to live in a more accepting society. Also in 2019 Angèle became an ambassador for [[Chanel]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vmagazine.com/article/angele-is-the-new-face-of-chanels-2021-eye-makeup-campaign/|title=Angèle Is The New Face of Chanel's 2021 Eye Makeup Campaign|website=V Magazine|access-date=13 July 2021}}</ref>

In April 2020, Angèle performed for [[Together at Home|One World: Together At Home]]-A global broadcast & digital special to support front line healthcare workers and the [[World Health Organization|WHO]] during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], hosted by [[Lady Gaga]].

In October 2020, Angèle was pictured alongside London singer [[Dua Lipa]] on the set of a music video. A collaboration with the latter was later announced and would feature on the reissue of Lipa's new album [[Future Nostalgia]]. "[[Fever (Dua Lipa and Angèle song)|Fever]]" was released on October 30, 2020. The music video was released a week later on November 6, 2020, on YouTube. Before the release of the video, Lipa announced on social media that Angèle will be appearing as a special guest on her live stream concert [[Studio 2054]] on 27 November 2020.<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=DUALIPA|author=Dua Lipa|number=1324398089562632193|date=5 November 2020|title=I’m so excited to announce @angele_vl as the first of many special guests joining me for Studio 2054! FEVER VIDEO OUT TOMORROW UK: 1PM  FR: 2PM // Get ur tickets now!|access-date=5 November 2020|url-status=live|archive-url=https://archive.today/EIDr2|archive-date=5 November 2020}}</ref>

In 2021 Angèle made her film debut in ''[[Annette (film)|Annette]]'', directed by [[Leos Carax]].

== Musical influences ==
Angèle is a fan of [[Ella Fitzgerald]] and [[Hélène Ségara]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.20minutes.fr/culture/2175275-20171124-angele-onde-pop-venue-plat-pays|title=Angèle, l'onde pop venue du plat pays|website=20 Minutes|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref> She is inspired by many musical genres from French songs to electronic music and rap, which she disliked as a teenager. She became influenced by rap particularly while performing with her brother [[Romeo Elvis]] and the rappers [[Caballero (rapper)|Caballero]] and [[JeanJass]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.konbini.com/fr/entertainment-2/romeo-elvis-angele/|title=Angèle, la sœur de Roméo Elvis, nous parle de "J'ai vu", leur featuring familial|website=Konbini|access-date=20 September 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
From 2017 to 2019, she dated French dancer and choreographer Léo Walk. She wrote the song "Perdus" which is about the end of their relationship, linked to her new notoriety.<ref name=Date1>{{Cite web|url=https://www.purepeople.com/article/angele-separee-de-leo-walk-elle-ne-regrette-pas-d-avoir-expose-leur-couple_a367823/1|title=Angèle séparée de Léo Walk : elle ne regrette pas d'avoir exposé leur couple|website=Pure People|date=January 3, 2020}}</ref>

In August 2020, Angèle announced on [[Instagram]] that she is dating French comedian Marie Papillon, while [[coming out]].<ref name=Date2>{{Cite web|url=https://www.20minutes.fr/arts-stars/people/2837463-20200810-chanteuse-angele-officialise-couple-marie-papillon|title=La chanteuse Angèle officialise son couple avec Marie Papillon|website=[[20 minutes (France)|20 minutes]]|date=August 10, 2020}}</ref><ref name=Date3>{{Cite web|url=https://tetu.com/2020/08/11/angele-fait-son-coming-out-sur-instagram/|title=Angèle fait son coming out sur Instagram|website=[[Têtu]]|date=August 10, 2020}}</ref> They separated the following year.<ref name=Documentary>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.fr/entry/angele-coming-out-vole-hanouna-documentaire-netflix_fr_618e35dfe4b0ab5f28481e8b|title=Angèle revient sur son coming out "volé" par Hanouna dans son documentaire Netflix|website=[[HuffPost]]|date=November 11, 2021}}</ref> In her 2021 self-titled [[Netflix]] documentary, Angèle confirms being [[Bisexuality|bisexual]].<ref name=Documentary/>

===Victoires de la Musique===
Victoires de la Musique is an annual French award ceremony where the Victoire accolade is delivered by the [[French Ministry of Culture]] to recognize outstanding achievement in the music industry that recognizes the best musical artists of the year.  The awards are the French equivalent to the [[Grammy Awards]] and the [[Brit Awards]] for music, and it is one of the major awards in France.
