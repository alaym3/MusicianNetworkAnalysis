'''Bears in Trees''' are an English band from [[Croydon]], [[South London]], England.

In October 2020, Bears in Trees announced that they had been signed by Boston label [[Counter Intuitive Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bears in Trees|first=|date=28 October 2020|title=Announcement Tweet|url=https://twitter.com/bears_in_trees/status/1321452217552035840|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 December 2020|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Counter Intuitive Records|url=https://www.counterintuitiverecords.com/|access-date=2021-01-03|website=Counter Intuitive Records|language=en}}</ref>

== History ==
Childhood friends Callum Litchfield and Iain Gillespie formed the band in 2014 after the dissolution of their previous project, The November Criminals,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Fleury|first=Thomas|date=18 June 2020|title=Ruthless X George Berry|url=https://www.ruthlessmagazine.co.uk/music/ruthless-x-george-berry-bears-in-trees-the-industry-and-subfrantic|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 December 2020|website=Ruthless Magazine}}</ref> which also included Bears in Trees guitarist Nick Peters. Litchfield met George Berry, originally brought on board to mix and record their songs, at college.<ref name=":0" /> Berry has since also become the band's drummer.

== Musical influences ==
The band cite [[Fall Out Boy]], [[The Front Bottoms]], [[Modern Baseball]], [[The Wombats]], [[Walk the Moon]], [[The 1975]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fresa|first=Carina|date=1 February 2020|title=Bears in Trees - A Hot Up and Coming Band in the UK|url=https://www.hercampus.com/school/suffolk/bears-trees-hot-and-coming-band-uk|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 December 2020|website=Her Campus|language=en}}</ref> [[Dodie Clark|Dodie]] and [[Cavetown (musician)|Cavetown]]<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Bears in Trees|first=|date=29 November 2020|title=Instagram post|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CIMOOQ8hvE9/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 December 2020|website=Instagram}}</ref> as their main influences, but have also made reference to many others across their social media.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Bears in Trees|first=|date=|title=Official TikTok|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@bearsintrees?lang=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 December 2020|website=TikTok|language=en}}</ref>

== Band members ==
*George Berry – [[Drum kit|drums, percussion]] and the band's [[Record producer|producer]]. Berry graduated from [[Academy of Contemporary Music|the Academy of Contemporary Music]] with a first class honours degree in Professional Music Production.
*Iain Gillespie – [[Singing|vocals]], [[bass guitar]]. 
*Callum Litchfield – [[Singing|vocals]], [[ukulele]], [[piano]]. Litchfield is a classically-trained flautist.
*Nick Peters – [[guitar]] and lyrics. Peters runs the majority of the band's social media.<ref name=":0" />

==== ''Just Five More Minutes'' (2017) ====
Bears in Trees released their debut album ''Just Five More Minutes'' on 14 August 2017.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Bears in Trees|first=|date=|title=Just Five More Minutes|url=https://bearsintreesofficial.bandcamp.com/album/just-five-more-minutes|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 December 2020|website=Bandcamp}}</ref> Tracks 1–11 were produced by Berry, while track 12 was produced by Absolex. Joe Matthews provided the trumpet for this album.<ref name=":2" />

Bears in Trees refer to their fans as "sandboxes" as a reference to the song 'The Sandbox One'. The song 'Good Rhymes for Bad Times' was the album's pre-release single.

==== ''I Want To Feel Chaotic'' (2020) ====
All the songs on the ''I Want To Feel Chaotic'' EP were released individually as singles prior to the EP's complete release on 5 June 2020. This EP is the band's first release under the Counter Intuitive label.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bears in Trees|first=|date=|title=I Want To Feel Chaotic|url=https://counterintuitiverecords.bandcamp.com/album/bears-in-trees-i-want-to-feel-chaotic|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 December 2020|website=Bandcamp}}</ref> The band refers to this period of single releases as the ''I Want To Feel Chaotic'' era.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Matuson|first=Lexi|date=2 March 2020|title=The Vulnerability, Chaos, and Intimacy of the New Bears In Trees Era|url=https://ascribemagazine.com/the-vulnerability-chaos-and-intimacy-of-the-new-bears-in-trees-era/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=13 December 2020|website=Ascribe Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> The band have said that this EP is about “about coming to terms with the worst parts of your personality – with the things that scare you, with the things that keep you awake at night… with the deaths of your friends, and the slow acceptance that you are deserving of love.”<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-28|title=Review: Bears In Trees - "Permanence"|url=https://ascribemagazine.com/review-bears-in-trees-permanence/|access-date=2021-05-14|website=Ascribe Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> The song 'Reverberate' also contains references to [[Catch-22]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Bears in Trees – Reverberate|url=https://genius.com/Bears-in-trees-reverberate-lyrics|access-date=2021-05-14}}</ref>

==== and everybody smiled back (2021) ====
Bears in Trees announced their first full-length studio album under Counter Intuitive Records, <b><i>and everybody else smiled back,</i></b> on 16 September 2021.  The album was released on 19 November <ref>{{Cite web|title=Bears in Trees on Twitter|url=https://twitter.com/bears_in_trees/status/1438549080649646083?s=20|access-date=2021-09-19|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>  The lead single, <i>Great Heights,</i> was also released on 16 September.  The following single, ''I'm Doing Push Ups,'' was released on 8 October. The third and final single of the album, ''Little Cellist,'' was released on 5 November. Accompanying music videos, also directed by Jaitly, were uploaded on 18 September and 9 October, respectively.  <ref>{{Cite web|title=Bears in Trees - Great Heights (Official Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HVO-Kf51hK8|access-date=2021-09-19|website=YouTube|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=I'm Doing Push Ups (Official Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3l26J-MESm4|language=en|access-date=2021-10-09}}</ref> The music video for ''Little Cellist'' was released on 6 November but unlike the band previous music videos was directed by Jamie Benyon. It is also the first music  video to not contain the band <ref>{{Citation|title=Bears in Trees - Little Cellist (Official Music Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oH18QkAdRBI|language=en|access-date=2021-11-09}}</ref>

== Tours ==
Bears in Trees had their debut UK tour in mid-2021. The tour was originally due to take place in December 2020, but had to be postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. Since then, Bears in Trees have also toured the UK with [[Noah Finnce|NOAHFINNCE.]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=NOAHFINNCE Tour 2021, Official Concert Tickets from MyTicket.co.uk|url=https://myticket.co.uk/artists/noahfinnce|access-date=2021-09-07|website=MyTicket}}</ref>
