'''Joshua Michael Garrels''' (born September 25, 1980) is an American singer-songwriter, producer, and composer from [[South Bend, Indiana]]. His music combines traditional folk music with other musical elements and the nontraditional exploration of Christian themes.

==Biography==
Garrels is originally from [[South Bend, Indiana]]. He currently lives in [[Muncie, Indiana]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://whatzup.com/music-comedy/josh-garrels-songwriter-back-home-in-indiana|title=Songwriter Back Home in Indiana'|website=www.whatzup.com|access-date=March 4, 2020}}</ref> with his wife and five children. In 2005, he co-founded Small Voice Records and released ''Over Oceans'', ''Jacaranda'', ''Lost Animals'' and ''Home''. Garrels released his sixth album in June 2011 titled ''Love & War & The Sea in Between''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cdbaby.com/Artist/JoshGarrels |title=Josh Garrels Biography  |accessdate=April 9, 2011}}</ref> ''[[Christianity Today]]'' called the album "prophetic, incisive, achingly human, and longingly spiritual", and rated it as their 2011 Album of The Year.<ref>[http://www.christianitytoday.com/ct/music/reviews/2011/lovewar.html Josh Garrels-- Love & War & The Sea In-Between (Small Voice Records).] ''Christianity Today''. accessed October 25, 2011.</ref><ref>[http://www.christianitytoday.com/ct/music/interviews/2011/fartheralong-december20.html?start=4 Christianity Today 2011 Album of The Year] "Christianity Today". accessed December 21, 2011.</ref> In 2012, Garrels collaborated with the music collective [[Mason Jar Music]] to film the music-documentary movie ''The Sea in Between,'' in which Mason Jar and Garrels traveled to the remote [[Mayne Island]] to perform music on several of Mayne's most beautiful locations.<ref>[http://www.theseainbetween.com The Sea In Between Official Website] accessed January 29, 2013.</ref> "Don't Wait For Me" was featured on ''[[American Idol]]'' on January 29, 2014.<ref>[https://twitter.com/JoshGarrels/status/428677978663288832 Twitter] accessed January 29, 2013.</ref> He released ''Home'' on April 7, 2015.

==Charity==
From March 14, 2013, to March 28, 2013, (14 days) Garrels had 161,245 album downloads on Noisetrade.com which raised $71,566 in "tips". All of the funds were given in full to [[World Relief]] to help with their work to bring peace and restoration to the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo]].<ref>[http://joshgarrels.com/news/ Josh Garrels' Official Website] accessed March 31, 2013.</ref>

== Continued work with Noisetrade ==
In the weeks leading up to the 2015 release of the album ''Home,'' Garrels gave away free copies of his previous albums on [[Noisetrade.com]]. When ''Home'' was released, listeners had a choice between buying it on [[ITunes]] or downloading it for free. Garrels gave away 42,000 copies of ''Home'' its first week.<ref>[http://joshgarrels.com/news/ Josh Garrels' Official Website] accessed April 15, 2015.</ref>
