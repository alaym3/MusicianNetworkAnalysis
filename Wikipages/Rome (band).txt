'''Rome''' is an experimental [[neofolk]] and [[martial industrial]] act founded in November 2005 as a main output for the songs of Jérôme Reuter (born August 2nd, 1981)<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Cristina|date=2010-04-26|title=Interviu: Jerome Reuter, Rome|url=http://muzica.metropotam.ro/interviuri-cu-artisti-romani-si-internationali/Interviu-Jerome-Reuter-Rome-art6915231146/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-17|website=Metropotam|language=ro, en}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=Muno|first=Claudine|date=2005-01-21|title=MUSIK: Du und deine Freunde|url=https://www.woxx.lu/820/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-17|website=[[Woxx]]|language=de-DE}}</ref> of [[Luxembourg]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/rome-mn0001529434|title=Rome {{!}} Biography, Albums, Streaming Links {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=2016-09-29}}</ref> Though Reuter is the main creative force in the band, he performs live with a range of musicians who also contribute performances on various studio recordings. In early 2006 Rome was signed to the Swedish record label [[Cold Meat Industry]]. Rome has since signed with the [[Trisol Music Group]] record label as of 2009. Rome is considered one of the most important acts within the neofolk genre.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.cvltnation.com/a-cvlt-nation-exclusive-interview-with-romeby-oliver-sheppard/|title=A CVLT Nation exclusive interview with ROMEby Oliver Sheppard -|date=2013-02-05|language=en-US|access-date=2016-09-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.idieyoudie.com/2013/06/an-interview-with-jerome-reuter-of-rome/|title=An Interview With Jerome Reuter of Rome|website=www.idieyoudie.com|access-date=2016-09-29}}</ref> 

==Background==
Reuter previously recorded music under the name Reggie Fain, which was influenced by [[Tom Waits]].<ref name=":3" /> He was also a member of an [[Oi!]] band<ref>{{Cite web|last=Murphy-Bates|first=Sebastian|date=2012-01-19|title=THE SKINFLICKS INTERVIEW with bassist Patrick Skinflick! 19/01/2012|url=http://ballbreakersmedia.blogspot.com/2012/01/skinflicks-interview-with-bassist.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-17|website=Ball Breakers Media}}</ref> called the Skinflicks,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Caregari|first=Luc|date=2013-08-16|title=NÉOFOLK: L’éternel incompris|url=https://www.woxx.lu/6699/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-17|website=[[Woxx]]|language=fr}}</ref> and a [[post-punk]] band called Mack Murphy and the Inmates,<ref name=":1" /> where he performed under the name Mack Murphy.<ref name=":3" />

Reuter's first recording as Rome was the EP ''Berlin'', which was recorded in Patrick Damiani's studio in Germany in December 2005.<ref>{{Cite web|date=|title=biography|url=http://romepage.eu/main/biography.htm|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100220130502/http://romepage.eu:80/main/biography.htm|archive-date=2010-02-20|access-date=2021-03-17|website=romepage.eu}}</ref> Damiani, a [[Sound Engineer|sound engineer]], has been considered a full-time member of Rome since 2008.<ref>{{Cite web|last=isis|date=2007-11-01|title=Rome Interview; This Twisted Crown|url=http://www.heathenharvest.com/article.php?story=20071027075624326|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121015081233/http://www.heathenharvest.com/article.php?story=20071027075624326|archive-date=2012-10-15|access-date=2021-03-17|website=[[Heathen Harvest]]}}</ref><ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last=Knepper|first=Erika|date=2015-01-08|title=Preview ROME (Jérôme Reuter Solo) - Munich 2015-01-29|url=https://www.reflectionsofdarkness.com/previews-news-154/15346-preview-rome-jerome-reuter-solo-munich-2015-01-29|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-17|website=Reflections of Darkness|language=en-gb}}</ref> In 2009, violinist Nikos Mavridis joined the group,<ref name=":4" /> although he left after being conscripted into the Greek military.<ref name=":2" />

Rome has had live performances featuring Die Weisse Rose on drums.<ref>{{Cite magazine|date=2008-12-29|title=ROME|number=10|pages=11-13|magazine=Kogaionon|url=https://issuu.com/kogaionon/docs/k10_web|access-date=2021-03-17}}</ref>

==Musical style==
According to Paul Simpson, the band's sound combines "military drumming and horns with atmospheric electronic textures and ethereal acoustic guitars, as well as samples and poetic lyrics often relating to war-related themes, as well as more universal topics such as love, pain, and death."<ref name=":0" /> Many of his album have historical themes, such as ''Flowers From Exile'' and ''A Passage to Rhodesia'', which focus on the [[Spanish Civil War|Spanish]] and [[Rhodesian Civil War|Rhodesian Civil Wars]], respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Della Pelle|first=Giulia|date=2020-11-04|title=Rome di Jerome Reuter: l'Europa in musica|url=https://www.shockwavemagazine.it/rubriche/singolarita-musicali/rome-di-jerome-reuter-leuropa-in-musica/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-03-18|website=Shockwave Magazine|language=it-IT}}</ref> Reuter has cited [[Jacques Brel]], [[Léo Ferré]], [[Albert Camus]], and [[Jean Genet]] as influences on his music and lyrics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://rome.lu/about/|title=About {{!}} ROME|website=rome.lu|access-date=2016-09-29}}</ref>
