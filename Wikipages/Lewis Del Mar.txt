'''Lewis Del Mar''' is an American [[experimental pop]]<ref name="consequenceofsound.net">{{Cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2015/07/lewis-del-mar-premieres-gripping-new-song-memories-listen/|title=Lewis Del Mar premieres gripping new song "Memories" — listen|date=2015-07-27|website=Consequence of Sound|language=en-US|access-date=2016-05-28}}</ref> duo from [[Rockaway Beach, Queens|Rockaway Beach]], New York City. Consisting of singer and guitarist Danny Miller, and drummer and producer Max Harwood,<ref name=":0" /> the group first received attention in early 2015 when their debut single ''Loud(y)'' was featured in the number one spot on [[Hype Machine]].<ref name="consequenceofsound.net"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/lewis-del-mar-premiere-malt-liquor-interview|title=Get to Know Rockaway Beach's Lewis Del Mar and Stream Their Newest Song "Malt Liquor" {{!}} NOISEY|website=NOISEY|access-date=2016-05-28}}</ref> They released their debut EP in January 2016,<ref name=":0">{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/6843717/columbia-lewis-del-mar-debut-ep-interview|title=New Columbia Signees Lewis Del Mar Release Hypnotic Debut EP & Talk Surfing With MGMT: Exclusive|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2016-05-28}}</ref> which peaked at number 7 on Billboard Emerging Artists.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6866172/lewis-del-mar/chart?f=2048|title=Lewis Del Mar - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=www.billboard.com|access-date=2016-05-28}}</ref> Their self-titled debut album was released on October 7, 2016, through [[Columbia Records]].

== Career ==
=== ''Lewis Del Mar'' (2016-2020) ===
The group's self titled debut album [[Lewis Del Mar (album)|''Lewis Del Mar'']] received support from [[NPR]], [[Vice (magazine)|''Vice'']], and ''[[The New York Times]]'', and led to late night performances on ''[[The Late Late Show with James Corden]]'' and ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]''. ''Rolling Stone'' called the lead single "Loud(y)" "A beachside dreamscape with acoustic guitars and electronic drums washing up on shore",<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-october-2016-110340/lewis-del-mar-110924/|title=10 New Artists You Need to Know Right Now|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=21 October 2016}}</ref> while Los Angeles radio station [[KCRW]] said it was "one of the best tunes to come out [in 2016]. The album also led to an international headlining tour from 2016-2017, including stops at globally renowned festivals such as [[Austin City Limits]], [[Outside Lands]], and [[Lollapalooza]].

=== ''AUGUST'' (2020-Present) ===
In May 2020, the group announced that their second album ''AUGUST'' would be released on August 21, 2020. The album's lead single, "The Ceiling" was premiered by [[Zane Lowe]] as a Beats 1 Radio "Worlds First", and received support from [[Annie Mac]] on BBC Radio 1's "Future Sounds". Culture Collide called the single "a triumphant return",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://culturecollide.com/lewis-del-mar-make-triumphant-return-with-moving-singles-the-ceiling-and-border-ch-iii/|title=Lewis del Mar make triumphant return with moving singles "The Ceiling" and "Border (CH. III)"|date=22 May 2020}}</ref> and ''[[TIME]]'' named it one of the best songs of May 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://time.com/5830566/best-new-songs-may-2020/|title = The Best New Songs of May 2020, from Lady Gaga to Anuel AA}}</ref> "The Ceiling" was followed by singles "Border (CH. III)" and "Rosalie (CH. II)", the latter of which Dujour referred to as "a declaration of the importance of human connections and relationships."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dujour.com/culture/sound-bite-ashe-michael-love-michael-niall-horan-lewis-del-mar-new-songs/|title = Sound Bite: Ashe, Michael Love Michael, and Lewis del Mar|date = 19 June 2020}}</ref>
