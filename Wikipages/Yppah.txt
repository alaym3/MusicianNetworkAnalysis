'''Joe Corrales Jr.''', also known as '''Yppah''' (pronounced "Yippah"), is an American electronic/rock musician.<ref>{{cite web |title=Yppah |url=http://yppah.bandcamp.com/|website=Yppah.bandcamp.com |access-date=21 August 2013}}</ref> He is currently signed to [[Future Archive Recordings]] and resides in [[Long Beach, California]], United States.

In 2020, Joe Corrales Jr. earned a [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]] '''Gold Certified Single''' for the use of the Yppah song, "Never Mess With Sunday" by [[Lil Peep]] on the song "Star Shopping".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gold & Platinum|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/|access-date=2021-07-23|website=RIAA|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last1=April 20|first1=C. Vernon Coleman IIPublished|last2=2019|title=Classic Lil Peep Song "Star Shopping" Officially Released - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/lil-peep-star-shopping/|access-date=2021-07-23|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref>

==History==
===Early years===
Corrales began playing [[guitar]] and [[bass guitar|bass]] in [[rock band]]s during high school, and later became more involved in electronic music and turntablism. He was involved in the early creation of [[Mashup (music)|mashups]], and was part of a [[Turntablism|turntablist]] group called ''The Truth''. Yppah is influenced by [[shoegaze]], various electronic music, [[psychedelic soul]], and rock music, evidenced by heavy use of reverb, effects, and electronics.

===You Are Beautiful At All Times===
His first album, titled ''[[You Are Beautiful at All Times]]'', was released in 2006 on [[Ninja Tune]] records.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ninjatune.net/ninja/release.php?id=1185|title=Ninja Tune|website=Ninjatune.net|access-date=2 April 2020}}</ref> The single "Again With The Subtitles" was released immediately prior to the album, and is featured in the film, ''[[21 (2008 film)|21]]''. "It's Not The Same," a song from the album, was used in a trailer for ''[[Alone in the Dark (2008 video game)|Alone in the Dark]]'', as well as the episode "Last Resort" from the series ''[[House (TV Series)|House]]''. Similarly, the song "In Two, the Weakly" is featured in the ''[[CSI: Crime Scene Investigation|CSI]]'' episode "Ending Happy".

===They Know What Ghost Know===
''They Know What Ghost Know'' released on May 18, 2009 in the UK, Japan and Australia. The U.S. release date was June 23, 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blogs.myspace.com/index.cfm?fuseaction=blog.view&friendID=5619302&blogID=462198370 |title=Myspace |website=Blogs.myspace.com |access-date=2013-06-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090207065936/http://blogs.myspace.com/index.cfm?fuseaction=blog.view |archive-date=2009-02-07 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Released to positive reviews, the album has a more pronounced shoegaze and psychedelic sound with "lush" instrumentation.<ref>{{cite web|last=Martin |first=Andrew |url=https://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/107443-yppah-they-know-what-ghost-know/ |title=Yppah: They Know What Ghost Know |website=PopMatters.com |access-date=2013-06-20}}</ref> The album crosses into numerous genres, with ''[[NME]]'' associating it with other DJ-based albums<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/yppah/10480 |title=NME Album Reviews - Album review: Yppah - 'They Know What Ghost Know' |website=Nme.Com |date=2009-05-19 |access-date=2013-06-20}}</ref> while other reviewers referring to it as "[[big beat]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cyclicdefrost.com/blog/2009/05/08/yppah-they-know-what-ghost-know-ninja-tuneinertia/ |title=Yppah – They Know What Ghost Know (Ninja Tune/Inertia) |website=Cyclicdefrost.com |date=2009-05-08 |access-date=2013-06-20}}</ref>

===Eighty One===
''Eighty One'' released on February 4, 2012 in Japan and April 2, 2012 worldwide to critical acclaim. ''Eighty One'' is Yppah's most widely received album to date, with the singles "Film Burn" (feat. [[Anomie Belle]]) and "D Song" (feat. [[Anomie Belle]]) charting on radio stations across North America and Japan.<ref name="yppaheightyone">{{cite web |author=Jason Kinnard |url=http://blog.kexp.org/2012/04/17/song-of-the-day-yppah-film-burn-feat-anomie-belle/|title=Song of the Day: Yppah – Film Burn (feat. Anomie Belle)|website=Blog.kexp.org|access-date=April 17, 2012}}</ref> ''Eighty One'' features several guest appearances by [[Seattle]] artist [[Anomie Belle]] who Corrales met when the two artists performed together on tour with [[Bonobo (musician)|Bonobo]]. [[Anomie Belle]] has also appeared with Yppah during live performances, performing on guitar, keys, violin, drum machine and vocals in support of the album.<ref>{{cite news | first=Bryce | last=Shoemaker | url=http://www.nadamucho.com/sasquatch-recap-tame-impala-steals-the-show-from-sigur-ros-saturday/ | title=Sasquatch Recap: Yppah feat Anomie Belle | date=May 27, 2013 | website=Nadamucho.com|access-date=2 April 2020 }}</ref>

===Tiny Pause===
On October 22, 2012, Corrales announced that the fourth album was almost complete, numbering 11 tracks so far.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/yppahmusic/posts/10151267919940129|title=Yppah|website=Facebook.com|access-date=2 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://yppah.bandcamp.com/album/tiny-pause|title=Tiny Pause, by Yppah|website=Yppah.bandcamp.com|access-date=2 April 2020}}</ref> The final album was cut down to nine tracks and was officially released October 16, 2015.

===Sunset in the Deep End===
The fifth album from Yppah was released on February 7, 2020 on [[Future Archive Recordings]].

'''Performed Live on [[KEXP-FM|KEXP]]''' in 2020 with Ali Coyle (vocals, bass) and Trent Moorman (drums).<ref>{{Citation|title=Yppah - Tree Ghost (Live on KEXP)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DBF14EQv3mU|language=en|access-date=2021-07-23}}</ref>
