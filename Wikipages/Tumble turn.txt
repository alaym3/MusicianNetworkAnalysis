{{short description|Turn in swimming}}
[[File:Swimming flip turns.jpg|thumb|300px|Swimmers performing tumble turns during a front crawl race]]
A '''tumble turn''' or '''flip turn''' is one of the [[turns in swimming]], used to reverse the direction in which the person is [[Human swimming|swimming]]. It is done when the swimmer reaches the end of the [[swimming pool]] but still has one or more lengths to swim.

The technique's development is credited, by the [[International Swimming Hall of Fame]], to [[Al Vande Weghe]] at the [[Amateur Athletic Union|AAU]] Nationals in 1934.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ishof.org/albert-vandeweghe-(usa).html| title=ALBERT VANDEWEGHE (USA), 1990 Honor Swimmer | accessdate = 2015-10-08}}</ref> Previously this entry erroneously credited the invention of the flip turn to [[University of Texas at Austin|University of Texas]] swim coach [[Tex Robertson]] while he was training [[Adolph Kiefer]] for the [[1936 Summer Olympics|1936 Olympics]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.texassports.com/index.php?s=&url_channel_id=20&url_article_id=7131&url_subchannel_id=&change_well_id=2 |title=Former Men's Swimming and Diving coach "Tex" Robertson passes away at 98 |publisher=Texassports.com |date=2007-08-27 |accessdate=2007-09-17 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080927225618/http://www.texassports.com/index.php?s=&url_channel_id=20&url_article_id=7131&url_subchannel_id=&change_well_id=2 |archivedate=September 27, 2008 }}</ref>

Flip turns are usually only used during [[Freestyle swimming|freestyle]] and [[backstroke]] races. In [[Butterfly stroke|butterfly]] and [[breaststroke]] races, regulations require swimmers to touch the end of the pool with both hands simultaneously before turning back for another length. While they legally can flip turn<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://swimswam.com/joe-gardner-executes-perfect-breaststroke-flip-turns-at-big-tens/|title = Joe Gardner Executes Perfect Breaststroke Flip Turns at Big Tens|date = 14 March 2018}}</ref> during butterfly and breaststroke races, it is more common to turn left or right to begin the next lap. This style of turning is called an open turn.

If the event is [[medley swimming]] turns vary based on the combination order of what stroke the swimmer is changing from and to. According to the USA Swimming Stroke and Turn Regulations, in order of the strokes the turns are to be:<ref>{{Cite web|title=New Medley Rule Change To Go Into Effect Sept. 21|url=http://www.usaswimming.org/news/2017/09/20/new-medley-rule-change-to-go-into-effect-sept.-21|access-date=2021-10-20|website=www.usaswimming.org|language=en}}</ref>
# [[Butterfly stroke|Butterfly]] to [[backstroke]] is a two-hand touch open turn. Once a legal touch has been made, the swimmer may turn in any manner, but the shoulders must be at or past vertical towards the back when the swimmer leaves the wall. 
# [[Backstroke]] to [[breaststroke]] the swimmer must first touch on their back. Once a legal touch has been made, the swimmer may turn in any manner.
# [[Breaststroke]] to [[Freestyle swimming|freestyle]] is two hand touch open turn. Once a legal touch has been made, the swimmer may turn in any manner.
