'''Chloe Moriondo''' (stylized in all lowercase as '''chloe moriondo''')<ref>{{Cite web |title=chloe moriondo |url=https://www.youtube.com/user/ChloeThingsDoes |language=en |access-date=2020-03-03}}</ref> (born September 29, 2002)<ref>{{Cite web |title=some stuff about me |url=https://bugkiiiid.tumblr.com/about |website=Tumblr |access-date=2020-02-29}}</ref><ref name="lesbian" /> is an American [[singer-songwriter]] and [[YouTuber]] born and based in Detroit. Moriondo's style blends elements of [[indie pop]] and [[bedroom pop]].<ref name="MENGenre" /> Their{{efn|Moriondo uses they/them, she/her and he/him pronouns.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/kidzwithbugz|title=chloe moriondo ✿ (@kidzwithbugz) / Twitter|access-date=August 31, 2021|via=[[Twitter]]|quote=18 and grody ♡ Listen to my album if u want !!!!!!!!!! (they/she/he)}}</ref> This article uses "they/them" pronouns for consistency.}} YouTube channel, which they began in January 2014, sits at over 3 million subscribers and over 132 million video views.<ref name="lesbian" /> Moriondo self-released their first studio album, ''Rabbit Hearted.'', on July 31, 2018,<ref name="RYM">{{Cite web |title=Rabbit Hearted. chloe moriondo |url=https://genius.com/albums/Chloe-moriondo/Rabbit-hearted |website=[[Genius (website)|Genius]] |access-date=February 26, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Anxiety, Innocence, and Vulnerability: An Interview with Chloe Moriondo |url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/chmr-chloe-moriondo-interview-2020-kindergarten-ghost-adventure-spirit-orb/ |last=Mosk |first=Mitch |date=2020-04-09 |website=Atwood Magazine |language=en-US |url-status=live |access-date=2020-04-28}}</ref> and their first major label [[Extended play|EP]], ''Spirit Orb'', on April 24, 2020.<ref name="SpiritOrb">{{Cite web |title=CHLOE MORIONDO ANNOUNCES SPIRIT ORB EP ARRIVING APRIL 24TH |url=https://ventsmagazine.com/2020/04/10/chloe-moriondo-announces-spirit-orb-ep-arriving-april-24th/ |last=Frometa |first=RJ |date=2020-04-10 |website=Vents Magazine |language=en-US |url-status=live |access-date=2020-04-28}}</ref> In August 2020, Moriondo was signed to [[Fueled by Ramen]] and released "I Want To Be With You".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thatsgoodenoughforme.com/chloe-moriondo-signs-with-fueled-by-ramen-premieres-new-video|title=Chloe Moriondo signs with Fueled by Ramen; premieres new video|date=August 25, 2020|accessdate=January 4, 2021|via=thatsgodenoughforme.com}}</ref> On May 7, 2021, Moriondo released their second studio album, ''Blood Bunny''.

==Personal life==
Moriondo supports the [[Black Lives Matter]] movement<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.readdork.com/features/chloe-moriondo-cover-interview-sept20|title=Chloe Moriondo: Bring it on|date=September 21, 2020|accessdate=25 September 2020}}</ref> and identifies as [[Non-binary gender|non-binary]] and unlabeled.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f2fO_0CjczE|title=a ramble about self identity, growth, and being a lesbian|accessdate=23 January 2021}}</ref> They use [[Singular they|they/them]], [[She (pronoun)|she/her]], and [[He (pronoun)|he/him]] pronouns.
