'''Kenneth La'ron'''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://primarytalent.com/kennyhoopla/#:~:text=Kenneth+La%27ron,+known+by,some+of+his+greatest+influences|title=Kennyhoopla &#124; Primary Talent International|website=primarytalent.com}}</ref> (born August 5, 1997<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.madeofmillions.com/articles/forward-motions-kennyhoopla-on-music-as-escapism|title=Forward Motions: KennyHoopla on Music as Escapism|website=Made of Millions Foundation}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/KennyHoopla/status/1290998747401527296 |title=Tweet |website=twitter.com |accessdate=2020-10-31}}</ref>), known by his stage name '''KennyHoopla''', is an American singer, songwriter, and rapper. He is primarily known from his singles "How Will I Rest in Peace if I'm Buried by a Highway?" and "Estella". He released his commercial debut EP ''How Will I Rest in Peace if I'm Buried by a Highway?'' on May 15, 2020.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/5/kennyhoopla-how-will-i-rest-in-peace-if-im-bured-by-a-highway-ep-stream|title=KennyHoopla Is Magnetic on New 'how will i rest in peace if i'm buried by a highway?//' EP|website=HYPEBEAST}}</ref>

== Early life ==
La'ron was born in [[Cleveland|Cleveland, Ohio]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.paradigmagency.com/music/kennyhoopla/#:~:text=Kenneth+La%27ron,+known+professionally,some+of+his+greatest+influences|title=KennyHoopla - Paradigm Talent Agency|website=www.paradigmagency.com}}</ref> When asked  about his earliest musical memory in an interview with ''[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]]'' magazine, La'ron recalled recording a "horrible" freestyle rap on a cassette toy his mother gave him when he was around 10 years old.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2020/05/19/get-to-know-kennyhoopla|title=Get To Know... KennyHoopla|website=diymag.com}}</ref>

== Music career ==

=== 2016–2019: ''Beneath the Willow Tree'' and other singles ===
KennyHoopla began posting songs to his SoundCloud page in 2016. On October 5, 2016, he released an EP titled ''Beneath the Willow Tree'' to SoundCloud, which consisted of some of the tracks he had posted throughout the year, as well as some new songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/kennyhoopla/sets/beneath-the-willow-tree-ep|title=Beneath The Willow Tree// (EP)|website=SoundCloud}}</ref> The EP received acclaim from several music blogs, who considered him an artist to watch.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dailychiefers.com/kenny-hoopla-beneath-the-willow-tree-ep/|title=Kenny Hoopla – Beneath The Willow Tree// EP|date=October 5, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://badgerherald.com/artsetc/2016/10/11/608-release-kenny-hoopla-dazes-amazes-on-debut-ep/|title=608 Release: Kenny Hoopla dazes, amazes on debut EP|first=Henry|last=Solotaroff-Webber}}</ref> On October 26, he uploaded the song "Cave" to SoundCloud, which became his most streamed song on the site at the time.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/kennyhoopla/cave-prod-taxpurposes|title=CAVE// (prod. taxpurposes)|via=soundcloud.com}}</ref>

On August 6, 2017, he released his commercial debut single "Waves", along with a music video. This was followed by "Sickness" in 2018. On February 1, 2019, he released the single "Lost Cause",<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/kennyhooplas-lost-cause-visuals-paint-him-as|title=KennyHoopla’s "Lost Cause//" Visuals Paint Him as an Alt-R&B Purveyor [PREMIERE] / Ones To Watch|website=Ones To Watch}}</ref> which became his most popular song at the time and gained him more exposure. Its music video was released on February 6.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RI4h-cs450o |title=Video |website=www.youtube.com |accessdate=2020-10-31}}</ref> This was followed by "Sore Loser" on September 18, which was also positively received.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://floodmagazine.com/68649/premiere-kennyhoopla-wins-with-new-nostalgia-on-sore-loser/|title=PREMIERE: KennyHoopla Wins with New Nostalgia on "Sore Loser//"|website=FLOOD}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2019/09/kennyhoopla-interview|title=Meeting Faith Halfway: KennyHoopla's Time is Now|website=Complex}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''How Will I Rest in Peace if I'm Buried by a Highway?'' and ''Survivors Guilt: The Mixtape'' ===
On February 4, 2020, KennyHoopla released the single "How Will I Rest in Peace if I'm Buried by a Highway?"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Gm_PPNbgMjg |title=Video |website=www.youtube.com |accessdate=2020-10-31}}</ref> This track quickly became his most popular song to date, becoming his first song to reach 1 million views on YouTube, and garnering praise for its unique [[post-punk]] and [[new wave music|new wave]] inspired sound.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/02/04/kenny-hoopla-how-will-i-rest-video|title=KennyHoopla is the life of the party in his "how will i rest in peace if i’m buried by a highway?//" video|website=The FADER}}</ref> The track also received radio play on alternative music stations, and peaked at number 8 on the [[Alternative Airplay|Billboard Alternative Airplay chart]]. On March 12, he released the follow-up single, "The World is Flat and This is the Edge".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2020/03/kennyhoopla-the-world-is-flat-and-this-is-the-edge/|title=KennyHoopla drops new song "the world is flat and this is the edge//": Stream|date=March 12, 2020}}</ref>

On May 13, 2020, he released the single "Plastic Door", and announced that his EP ''How Will I Rest in Peace if I'm Buried by a Highway?'' would be released that Friday, May 15.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.readdork.com/news/kennyhoopla-brings-the-feels-with-new-track-plastic-door|title=KennyHoopla brings the feels with new track ‘plastic door//’|website=Dork}}</ref> The EP received critical acclaim, being praised for its versatile mixture of sounds such as [[indie rock]], [[hip hop music|hip hop]], [[pop punk]], and [[drum and bass]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/kennyhoopla-how-will-i-rest-in-peace-if-im-buried-by-a-highway-ep-interview/|title=Here’s why the only artist KennyHoopla is trying to sound like is himself|first=Jack|last=Angell|date=May 14, 2020}}</ref><ref name="auto"/> On August 28, he released a reworked version of his 2019 single "Lost Cause", featuring guest vocals from [[Grandson (musician)|grandson]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2020/08/28/listen-kennyhoopla-grandson-lostcause|title=KennyHoopla teams up with Grandson for 'lostcause//' reworking|website=diymag.com}}</ref> On October 14, he released another version of the song, this time featuring guest vocals from [[Jesse Rutherford (singer)|Jesse Rutherford]] of [[The Neighbourhood]].

On October 12, 2020, he revealed that he is working on his debut album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/KennyHoopla/status/1315737554281394176 |title=Tweet |website=twitter.com |accessdate=2020-10-31}}</ref> Two days later, on October 14, he posted a picture to his Twitter account of him in the studio with [[Blink-182]] drummer [[Travis Barker]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/KennyHoopla/status/1316398473168650240 |title=Tweet |website=twitter.com |accessdate=2020-10-31}}</ref> On November 20, he released the song "Estella", which features Barker on drums. Its music video was released five days later, on November 25.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/watch-kennyhoopla-and-travis-barker-drive-around-town-in-new-estella-video-2826053|title=Watch KennyHoopla and Travis Barker drive around town in new ‘ESTELLA//’ video|date=November 26, 2020}}</ref>

On May 7, 2021, he released the single "Hollywood Sucks" featuring Travis Barker, and announced that he would be releasing the mixtape ''Survivors Guilt: The Mixtape'' on June 11.<ref>{{cite web |title=KennyHoopla shares ‘hollywood sucks//’, announces mixtape |url=https://www.bluntmag.com.au/music/kennyhoopla-shares-hollywood-sucks-announces-mixtape/ |access-date=7 May 2021}}</ref>

KennyHoopla is scheduled to support [[Yungblud]] in London on his Occupy the UK tour in 2021,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/KennyHoopla/status/1277650076681330689 |title=Tweet |website=twitter.com |accessdate=2020-10-31}}</ref> and [[Machine Gun Kelly (musician)|Machine Gun Kelly]] on his 2021 ''Tickets to My Downfall'' U.S. tour, alongside [[Jxdn]] and Carolesdaughter<ref>{{cite web |last1=Clarke |first1=Patrick |title=Machine Gun Kelly announces huge run of ‘Tickets To My Downfall’ tour dates |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/machine-gun-kelly-announces-huge-run-of-tickets-to-my-downfall-tour-dates-2928244 |website=[[NME]] |access-date=29 April 2021}}</ref>

==Musical style==
La'ron's music has been categorised as [[indie rock]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Angell |first1=Jack |title=HERE’S WHY THE ONLY ARTIST KENNYHOOPLA IS TRYING TO SOUND LIKE IS HIMSELF |url=https://www.altpress.com/features/kennyhoopla-how-will-i-rest-in-peace-if-im-buried-by-a-highway-ep-interview/ |website=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |access-date=29 April 2021}}</ref> and [[pop punk]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=SUNDBERG |first1=EMILY |title=NESSA BARRETT |url=https://www.nylon.com/entertainment/nessa-barrett-interview |website=[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]] |access-date=29 April 2021}}</ref> often incorporating elements of [[New wave music|new wave]] and [[dance-punk]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Polo |first1=Maxamillion |title=KennyHoopla’s "how will i rest in peace if i’m buried by a highway? //" Is Pure Catharsis |url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/kennyhoopla-how-will-i-rest-in-peace |access-date=9 May 2021}}</ref>
