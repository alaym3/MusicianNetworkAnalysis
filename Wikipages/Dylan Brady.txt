'''Dylan Marshall Brady''' (born November 27, 1993) is an American music producer and singer-songwriter<ref name="vice">{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/gy73b7/dylan-brady-noisey-mix-peace-love-interview|last=Jones|first=Colin|title=This Dylan Brady Mix Is Colorful Music for Couch-Sitting|work=Vice|date=December 14, 2018|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref> best known for being one half of [[experimental electronic music]] duo [[100 Gecs]]. He also owns and operates his own record label, Dog Show Records.

==Early life==
Dylan Marshall Brady<ref>{{Cite web|date=March 2, 2015|title=Anne Adams Marshall/Mama gec on Instagram: "Me and my son, Dylan Marshall Brady, taken by Suzy Gorman, 15 sec photo shoot. Greatest pic in such a long, long time:) so grateful!!!!!…"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/zvHz7MLFjd/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://archive.today/8Mr46|archive-date=July 21, 2020|access-date=July 21, 2020|website=Instagram|language=en}}</ref> grew up in suburban [[St. Louis, Missouri]] and took piano lessons in high school, where he first became interested in composing music. While attending college for audio engineering, he began putting music out on [[SoundCloud]], as an electronic hip hop producer.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.them.us/story/100-gecs-interview-dylan-brady-laura-les|title=Meet 100 gecs, the Absurdist Pop Duo Inspired By Everything on the Internet|website=Them|last=Kim|first=Michelle|date=January 16, 2020|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref> He is currently based in [[Los Angeles, California]], where he collaborates with musical partner [[Laura Les]] remotely from her home in [[Chicago]].<ref name="vice"/>

==Career==
Brady began his career releasing several demo albums on Soundcloud in 2013 and 2014. He self-released his debut studio album ''All I Ever Wanted'' in May 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2015/05/album-premiere-dylan-brady-all-i-ever-wanted|title=Album Premiere: Dylan Brady - 'All I Ever Wanted'|last=Price|first=Joe|work=Complex|date=May 1, 2015|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref> In 2016 he released the EP ''Choker'' and released a self-titled EP as part of 100 gecs. In 2017 Brady released two EPs, ''Sinses'' and ''Dog Show''. In October 2018, Brady released the EP ''Peace & Love,'' his first release on the [[Mad Decent]] label.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2018/12/dylan-drady-peace-and-love-interview|title=Dylan Brady is Making Vital Pop Music That's Both Accessible and Delightfully Weird|last=Price|first=Joe|work=Complex|date=December 13, 2018|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref> His collaborative studio album with producer Josh Pan, ''This Car Needs Some Wheels,'' was released in March 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/vbwp5y/josh-pan-dylan-brady-wheels-stream-video|title=Watch the Surreal Video for Josh Pan and Dylan Brady's Road Jam "Wheels"|last=Joyce|first=Colin|website=Vice|access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref>

Brady is also the leader of the St. Louis-based band [[Cake Pop (band)|Cake Pop]] with Aaron Cartier, Ravenna Golden, Lewis Grant, [[Pritty]], Robel Ketema, Kevin Bedford, and Adam Newcomer. They released their debut self-titled EP on [[SoundCloud]] in 2015. On March 30, 2021 the band released their single "Black Rum", followed by the single "Satin Bedsheets" on April 20. On the same day as the release of "Black Rum", the band announced that their debut studio album ''[[Cake Pop 2]]'' would be released on May 14, 2021 by [[Mad Decent]].<ref name="Cake Pop">{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/100-gecs-dylan-brady-and-cake-pop-share-new-song-black-rum-watch-the-video/|title=Cake Pop, Including 100 gecs’ Dylan Brady, Share New Song "Black Rum": Watch the Video|last=Yoo|first=Noah|website=Pitchfork|date=March 30, 2021|access-date=March 30, 2021}}</ref>
