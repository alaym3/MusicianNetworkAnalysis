'''Kashmir''' is a [[Denmark|Danish]] [[alternative rock]] band consisting of [[Kasper Eistrup]] ([[Singing|vocals]] and lead [[guitar]]); [[Mads Tunebjerg]] ([[Bass guitar|bass]]); [[Asger Techau]] ([[Drum kit|drums]]) and [[Henrik Lindstrand]] ([[Keyboard instrument|keyboards]] and [[guitar]]).

==History==
In the spring of 1991, Eistrup, Tunebjerg and Techau formed a heavy blues band under the name "Nirvana" at ''Kastanievej Efterskole'', a continuation school, in [[Frederiksberg]], [[Denmark]]. Shortly after they started performing Thursday nights at ''Ordrup Gymnasium'', a local high school.{{Citation needed|date=August 2014}}

When the [[United States|American]] band [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]] started to gain success, they changed their name to "Kashmir", after the [[Led Zeppelin]] [[Kashmir (song)|song]].{{Citation needed|date=August 2014}}

In 1993, they finished second in "DM i Rock", a national amateur concert contest, behind [[Dizzy Mizz Lizzy]] and subsequently became popular in Denmark.{{Citation needed|date=August 2014}}

In 2000, the band won six [[Danish Music Awards]]: 'Best Danish Band', 'Best Danish Album', 'Best Danish Songwriter', and 'Best Danish Rock Album' for ''[[The Good Life (Kashmir album)|The Good Life]]''; and 'Best Danish Producer' and 'Best Danish Music Video' for "Mom in Love, Daddy in Space".<ref name="DMA">{{cite web  | title = Danish Music Awards timeline  | publisher = DR  | url = http://www.dr.dk/dma/tidslinie/index.asp  | archive-url = https://archive.today/20070311051107/http://www.dr.dk/dma/tidslinie/index.asp  | url-status = dead  | archive-date = 2007-03-11  | access-date = 2005-10-26  }}</ref>

In 2001, Lindstrand joined the band's lineup.

In 2004, the band won four Danish Music Awards for 'Best Danish Band', 'Best Danish Rock Album' for ''Zitilites'', 'Best Danish Music Video' for "Rocket Brothers" and 'Best Album Cover' for ''Zitilites''.<ref name="DMA" />

Their album ''No Balance Palace'' (2005), features [[Lou Reed]] reciting a poem by Eistrup on "Black Building" and [[David Bowie]] who sings a duet with Eistrup on "The Cynic". Additionally, the album was produced by [[Tony Visconti]].

On November 13, 2009 Kashmir released the first single "Mouthful of Wasps" on their website. The band also announced via [[Twitter]] that ''[[Trespassers (album)|Trespassers]]'', their sixth full-length album, would see its release on February 1, 2010. ''Trespassers'' was produced by John O'Mahony and Andy Wallace, and recorded at [[Jimi Hendrix]]'s [[Electric Lady Studios]] in [[New York City]].

In 2017, the band went on indefinite hiatus.<ref name="https://gaffa.dk/nyhed/115328/kashmir-oploser-firmaet-bag-bandet">{{cite web |last1=Gaffa |title=Kashmir opløser firmaet bag bandet |url=https://gaffa.dk/nyhed/115328/kashmir-oploser-firmaet-bag-bandet |website=Gaffa.dk}}</ref>
