'''Chinsea Lee''' (born 1 October 1996), popularly known as '''Shenseea''', is a [[Jamaicans|Jamaican]] [[dancehall]] [[Musician|recording artist]], [[Singing|singer]], and [[songwriter]].

== Early life ==
Born in [[Mandeville, Jamaica|Mandeville]], Jamaica at Mandeville Regional Hospital, she resided with her family in Harmony Hall, [[St. Elizabeth Parish|St. Elizabeth]] before moving to [[Kingston, Jamaica]] at the age of 3. However, she was still back and forth between St. Elizabeth and Kingston during her primary years to spend time with her grandmother.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|title=Star of the Month: Shenseea open to meeting her father|url=http://jamaica-star.com/article/entertainment/20180711/star-month-shenseea-open-meeting-her-father|accessdate=3 October 2019|website=jamaica-star}}</ref>

== Career ==
Shenseea was a promo girl until she was signed by Lemuel Samuel's Rockstar. She gained recognition after remixing the well-known Vybz Kartel's song "Loodi" and performing on a side stage at [[Reggae Sumfest]] in 2015, she took the main stage in 2017. As her notoriety grew, she toured internationally.<ref name="july">{{cite web|url=http://jamaica-gleaner.com/article/entertainment/20170721/shenseea-graduates-sumfests-main-stage-loodi-tune-performer-gets-12|title=Shenseea graduates to Sumfest's main stage - Loodi tune performer gets 12 minutes tonight|work=Jamaica Gleaner|date=July 21, 2017 |accessdate=June 22, 2018}}</ref> Vybz Kartel and Shenseea then dropped a hot collab "Secret (Remix)" in October 2018. Alongside her singing career, she holds multiple corporate sponsorship deals with companies such as Telecom, Firm Flow, Boom and a Pepsi-Cola JA sponsorship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypelifemagazine.com/celebritynews/shenseea-pepsi-jamaica-deal-brand-ambassador/|title=Shenseea Inks Deal With Pepsi Jamaica As Brand Ambassador|work=Hype Life Magazine|date=June 29, 2017|accessdate=February 3, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://dancehallhiphop.com/2017/11/13/shenseea-and-her-son-featured-in-new-commercial/|title=Shenseea and Her Son Featured In New Commercial - Urban Islandz|date=November 13, 2017|work=Urban Islandz|accessdate=June 22, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2020/03/28/news/shenseea-keeps-on-trending-lands-endorsement-deal-with-boom-energy-drink.html|title=Shenseea Keeps On Trending, Lands Endorsement Deal With Boom Energy Drink|work=DancehallMag|date=March 28, 2020|accessdate=March 28, 2020}}</ref>

In 2016, she won "Young Hot and Hype Artiste" at the Youth View Awards; Break Out Celebrity of 2016, and Collaboration of The Year for her song "Loodi" with [[Vybz Kartel]] which has been her most successful track to date.<ref name="july" /> In addition to dancehall music she has shown skills in freestyling as a rapper.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dancehallhiphop.com/2017/12/06/is-shenseea-plotting-a-career-switch-to-hip-hop/|title=Is Shenseea Plotting A Career Switch To Hip-Hop?|date=December 6, 2017|work=Urban Islandz|accessdate=June 22, 2018}}</ref> She teamed up with [[Sean Paul]] for the 2017 single "Rolling"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jamaica-star.com/article/entertainment/20170922/sean-paul-shenseea-team-rolling|title=Sean Paul, Shenseea team up on 'Rolling'|work=Jamaica Star|date=September 22, 2017|accessdate=June 22, 2018}}</ref> and on the leg of his 2017 European tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.loopjamaica.com/content/whats-shenseea-new-music-sexual-harassment-and-more|title=What’s Up! Shenseea on new music, sexual harassment, and more|website=www.loopjamaica.com|date=November 13, 2017|accessdate=June 22, 2018}}</ref>

Since her rise to fame, she has done numerous tours around the world with her team Romeich Entertainment. She continues to work with other musicians both Jamaican and American.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}

She was featured on [[Christina Aguilera]]'s 2018 album ''[[Liberation (Christina Aguilera album)|Liberation]]'', on the song "Right Moves".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://hypelifemagazine.com/celebritynews/shenseea-keida-christina-aguilera-liberation-album-right-move-feature/|title=Shenseea And Keida Featured On Christina Aguilera's New Album 'Liberation'|date=May 11, 2018|work=Hype Life Magazine|accessdate=February 3, 2019}}</ref> After signing with [[Interscope Records|Interscope]] records in 2019 , she released a new 
hip-hop/dancehall track featuring American artist [[Tyga]], called "Blessed" which has clocked in 55 million views on [[YouTube]].{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}

In January 2020, she collaborated with Jamaican [[record producer]] [[Rvssian]] on a single titled "IDKW" which also featured American rappers [[Swae Lee]] and [[Young Thug]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dancehallmag.com/2020/01/17/music/watch-the-official-idkw-video-by-rvssian-with-shenseea-swae-lee-and-young-thug.html|title=Watch The Official ‘IDKW’ Video By Rvssian With Shenseea, Swae Lee and Young Thug|date=17 January 2020|website=DancehallMag|accessdate=13 February 2020}}</ref>

She also collaborated with Zum on a single titled "Rebel."{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}

She has also collaborated with [[Kanye West]] on his latest album, ''[[Donda]]''. She appeared in the song "Pure Souls", alongside [[Roddy Ricch]] and "Ok Ok Pt 2" with Rooga. Shenseea received an Album of the year nominated for her collaboration on Kanye West’s album [[Donda]]’’for 2022’s 64th Grammy Awards. This is the Dancehall artist’s first Grammy nomination.

==Personal life==
In 2015, at the age of 19, Shenseea gave birth to her son, named Rajeiro Lee, before her music career took off.<ref>[https://buzz-caribbean.com/life/shenseea-writes-touching-ig-note-to-son-on-his-birthday/ Shenseea writes touching IG note to son on his birthday]</ref> In 2020, her son signed an endorsement deal for Babybop Kids Store at the age of four.<ref>[http://jamaica-star.com/article/entertainment/20200221/shenseea%E2%80%99s-son-lands-endorsement-deal Shenseea’s son lands endorsement deal]</ref> Shenseea is biracial. Her mother was Jamaican and her father is Korean. 

In 2020, Shenseea lost her mother suddenly. The news sent shockwaves through the dancehall community, with many artists showing their support, such as Jada Kingdom, [[Popcaan]], and [[Spice (dancehall)|Spice]]. Jada and Shenseea ended their feud due to this.{{Citation needed|date=February 2021}}
