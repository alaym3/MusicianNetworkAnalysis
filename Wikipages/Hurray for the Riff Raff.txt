'''Hurray for the Riff Raff''' is an [[Americana (music)|Americana]] band from [[New Orleans]]. It was formed by Alynda Segarra, a singer-songwriter from [[the Bronx]], [[New York (state)|New York]], after she had moved to New Orleans in 2007.

As Segarra's project, the group originally performed different styles of [[folk music]] while releasing several albums [[independent music|independently]]. Since signing with [[ATO Records]], the band has released the critically acclaimed studio albums ''[[Small Town Heroes]]'' (2014) and ''[[The Navigator (Hurray for the Riff Raff album)|The Navigator]]'' (2017), featuring a more distinctive and comprehensive Americana sound.<ref name=AllMusic-bio>{{cite web|last=Monger|first=James Christopher|title=Hurray for the Riff Raff: Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/hurray-for-the-riff-raff-mn0002534755/biography|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=3 February 2014}}</ref>

== Background ==
=== Alynda Segarra ===
Alynda Mariposa Segarra (Formerly known as alynda lee) was raised by her aunt and uncle in the [[Bronx]] where she developed an early appreciation for doo-wop and Motown. She is an American rock musician of [[Puerto Rican people|Puerto Rican]] heritage.<ref name=OffBeat-2014>{{cite web|last=Swenson|first=John|title=Radio Zeitgeist: Hurray for the Riff Raff's Alynda Lee Segarra|url=http://www.offbeat.com/2014/02/01/radio-zeitgeist-hurray-riff-raff-alynda-lee-segarra/|work=[[OffBeat (music magazine)|OffBeat]] |access-date=3 February 2014|date=February 1, 2014}}</ref> Her mother is former New York City Deputy Mayor [[Ninfa Segarra]].<ref name=NYT-2017>{{cite news |last=Farber|first=Jim|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/03/08/arts/music/hurray-for-the-riff-traff-the-navigator-interview.html|title=Hurray for the Riff Raff’s Alynda Segarra Finds Herself in a Concept Album|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=March 8, 2017|access-date=June 8, 2017}}</ref>

Segarra became a regular attendee of [[hardcore punk]] shows at [[ABC No Rio]] when she was young.<ref name=eMusic-2010 /> She left her home in the Bronx at age 17, spending time crossing [[North America]], hopping [[freight trains]].<ref name=eMusic-2010>{{cite web|last=Keyes|first=J. Edward|title=eMusic Selects Q&A: Hurray For the Riff Raff |url=http://www.emusic.com/features/spotlight/20010_201002-qa-selects-riffraff.html|work=[[eMusic]]|access-date=3 February 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100315033742/http://www.emusic.com/features/spotlight/20010_201002-qa-selects-riffraff.html|archive-date=March 15, 2010}}</ref>

In late 2018, Segarra joined organizers of Newport Folk Festival to travel to Puerto Rico and deliver instruments to various public schools.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8491198/hurray-for-the-riff-raff-palante-interview-puerto-rico-resistance|title=Hurray For the Riff Raff On Her Energizing Visit to Puerto Rico: Musicians 'Keep the Resistance Alive'|website=Billboard}}</ref> During that visit, Segarra played her first concert on the island at La Respuesta in Santurce.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.latinorebels.com/2018/12/04/hftrrpuertorico/|title=Hurray for the Riff Raff Goes Home to Puerto Rico|date=December 4, 2018|website=Latino Rebels}}</ref>

=== Dead Man Street Orchestra ===
Around 2007, Segarra became a part of the [[Dead Man Street Orchestra]], a hobo band that was documented in a photo essay by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time Magazine]]'' in 2007.<ref name=Time-2007>{{cite news |last=Heil |first=James |url=http://www.time.com/time/photoessays/2007/hobo_band/ |title=Time Photo Essay: The Ballad of the Hobo |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |date=January 19, 2007 |access-date=3 March 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110219055017/http://www.time.com/time/photoessays/2007/hobo_band/ |archive-date=19 February 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Segarra traveled with the group for two years, releasing two independent albums and embarking on freight train tours across the country.

== Musical career ==
[[File:Hurray for the Riff Raff - Haldern Pop Festival 2017-12.jpg|thumb|306x306px|Hurray for the Riff Raff at [[Haldern Pop]] Festival 2017.]]
After two self-released albums, 2008's ''It Don't Mean I Don't Love You'' and 2010's ''Young Blood Blues,'' Hurray for the Riff Raff released a self-titled CD composed of Segarra's favorite songs from those records on [[Loose Music]] in Europe on March 21, 2011. Tracks from the band's debut release received airplay on [[BBC Radio 2]] and [[BBC 6 Music]].

In February 2011, the band were featured in an article in ''[[The Times]]'', based around the [[HBO]] TV series, ''[[Treme (TV series)|Treme]]'', with their track "Daniella" being listed in their selection of [[New Orleans]]' essential songs.<ref name=LooseMusic-2011>{{cite web|url=http://loosemusic.com/hurray-for-the-riff-raff/hurray-for-the-riff-raff-in-the-times |title=Hurray for the Riff Raff - The Times |work=[[Loose Music]] |date=February 1, 2011 |access-date= 3 March 2011}}</ref>

In May 2012, Hurray for the Riff Raff released ''Look Out Mama'' on their own label, Born to Win Records and on [[Loose Music]] in Europe. ''[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]'' said it "sounds like something The Band would’ve had playing on a Victorola while making ''Music From Big Pink'' in Woodstock.".<ref name=NoDep-2012>{{cite web|last=Gibson|first=Donald|url=http://www.nodepression.com/profiles/blogs/exclusive-premiere-hurray-for-the-riff-raff-look-out-mama |title= Hurray for the Riff Raff |work= [[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]] |date=April 16, 2012}}</ref> The album was recorded in [[Nashville, Tennessee]] at The Bomb Shelter Studios and produced by Andrija Tokic ([[Alabama Shakes]]).<ref name="AmericanSongwriter-2012">{{cite web|last=Schlansky |first=Evan |url=http://www.americansongwriter.com/2012/04/video-premiere-hurray-for-the-riff-raff-look-out-mama/ |title= Hurray for the Riff Raff |work= [[American Songwriter]] |date=April 23, 2012}}</ref> The record features "End of the Line,"''My Dearest Darkest Neighbor'' on July 1, 2013 through [[Mod Mobilian|Mod Mobilian Records]] and [[This Is American Music]]. The album was previously available only as a [[Kickstarter]] reward and in limited local release and featured handmade covers.<ref name=Kickstarter-LookOutMama-2012>{{cite web|last=Hurrayfortheriffraff|title=Help Hurray for the Riff Raff Release Our New Album|url=https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1667604631/help-hurray-for-the-riff-raff-release-our-new-albu|work=[[Kickstarter]]|access-date=3 February 2014}}</ref><ref name=TimesPicayune-2013>{{cite news|last=Fensterstock|first=Alison|title=A take on John Lennon's 'Jealous Guy' heralds Hurray for the Riff Raff's new cover CD, due July 1 |url=http://www.nola.com/music/index.ssf/2013/06/a_take_on_john_lennons_jealous.html|access-date=16 June 2013|work=[[Times-Picayune]] |date=June 12, 2013}}</ref> Tracks on the record include songs by Townes Van Zandt, Billie Holiday, Gillian Welch, Leadbelly, John Lennon, Lucinda Williams, Joni Mitchell, Hank Williams, and George Harrison—and features Segarra's interpretation of Gillian Welch's "Ruination Day."

''[[Wall Street Journal]]'' describes Segarra's singing: "She has a subtle, expressive voice that she wraps around songs that draw on the sounds and styles of the American South, and her lyrics often take unconventional tack on traditional subjects."<ref name=WSJ-2013>{{cite news|last=Danton|first=Eric R.|title=Hurray for the Riff Raff Streams ‘I Know It’s Wrong (But That’s Alright)’|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2013/11/26/hurray-for-the-riff-raff-streams-i-know-its-wrong-but-thats-alright/|access-date=3 February 2014|newspaper=[[Wall Street Journal]]|date=November 26, 2013}}</ref>

''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]'' previewed a video of their song "St. Roch Blues".<ref name=Spin-StRoch-2014>{{cite web|last=McGovern|first=Kyle|title=Watch Hurray for the Riff Raff Honor New Orleans in 'St. Roch Blues' Video|url=https://www.spin.com/2014/01/hurray-for-the-riff-raff-new-orleans-st-roch-blues-video/|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|access-date=3 February 2014|date=January 14, 2014}}</ref>

In February 2014, Hurray for the Riff Raff had their [[ATO Records]] debut, ''Small Town Heroes''.<ref name="ATO-signs-2013">{{cite web|title=ATO Welcomes Hurray for the Riff Raff|url=http://atorecords.com/featured/ato-welcomes-hurray-for-the-riff-raff/|work=ATO Records|access-date=3 February 2014|date=June 30, 2013}}</ref> The record featured “The Body Electric,” a song that NPR’s Ann Powers called “The Political Song of the Year” in 2014 and says the album, "sweeps across eras and genres with grace and grit."

Describing Segarra's music, ''[[NPR]]'' says "Segarra's morning-after alto might be the least showy great voice to hit the national scene this year."<ref name=NPR-NewPolFolk-2014>{{cite web|last=Powers|first=Ann|title=Hurray For The Riff Raff's New Political Folk|url=https://www.npr.org/blogs/therecord/2014/01/22/265039131/hurray-for-the-riff-raffs-new-political-folk|work=[[NPR]]|access-date=3 February 2014|date=January 23, 2014}}</ref>

In early July 2016, the band played the Danish festival of Roskilde, to an enthusiastic audience.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dagsavisen.no/kultur/musikk/slik-husker-vi-roskilde-2016-1.748517 |title=Slik husker vi Roskilde 2016 |website=Dagsavisen.no |access-date=2017-03-10}}</ref> In December 2016, they announced a new record, ''[[The Navigator (Hurray for the Riff Raff album)|The Navigator]]'' on [[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] with the first single "Rican Beach." The album was released on March 10, 2017 on [[ATO Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/70190-watch-hurray-for-the-riff-raffs-video-for-new-song-rican-beach/ |title=Watch Hurray for the Riff Raff’s Video for New Song "Rican Beach" |publisher=Pitchfork |date=2016-12-05 |access-date=2017-03-10}}</ref>

In 2019 'Pa'lante' won 'Best Music Video' at the SXSW Film Festival Jury and Specials Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sxsw.com/film/2019/announcing-the-2019-sxsw-film-festival-jury-and-special-awards/|title=Announcing the 2019 SXSW Film Festival Jury and Special Awards|date=2019-03-13|website=SXSW|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-03}}</ref>
