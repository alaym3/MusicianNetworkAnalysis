'''Vilma Palma e Vampiros''' is an Argentine [[Neofolk|neo]] [[folk rock]] band from [[Rosario, Santa Fe|Rosario]]. They came to prominence in 1992 after the release of the hits Bye, Bye and La Pachanga.<ref name=Billboard19950415>{{cite news |last=Fernandez Bitar |first=Marcelo |date=15 April 1995 |title=Barca's Vilma Palma Takes The Long Road To Buenos Aires |work=Billboard |page=30}}</ref>

==Origin of the name==
Translated as ''Vilma Palma and Vampires'', the name was taken from a graffiti<ref name=Billboard19950415/>  written over the metallic shutters of a closed furniture store by its former employees, calling out the manager: "Vilma Palma e Hijos Vampiros de los Obreros" (''Vilma Palma and Children, Vampires of the Workers''). With time, the graffiti vanished until the words "Vilma Palma e Vampiros" remained by 1991.<ref>{{cite web |title=VILMA PALMA E VAMPIROS - Un Poco de Historia |url=http://www.vilmapalma.com/about-us.html |website=www.vilmapalma.com |accessdate=20 July 2020}}</ref>

This explanation makes for the grammatical error in the name: ''e'' is used instead of ''y'' (as ''and'') only when followed by a word starting with ''i-'' or ''hi-''.
