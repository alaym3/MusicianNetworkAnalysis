'''Orla Joan Gartland''' (born 3 February 1995) is an Irish singer-songwriter and guitarist from Dublin who gained popularity from posting cover songs on her YouTube account. As of June 2021, her YouTube channel has received over 21 million total views.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCePJhYKT0qg7u3oxSw_laXw|title=Orla Gartland|website=YouTube|language=en|access-date=22 October 2019}}</ref>

Gartland describes her music as folk pop, most heavily influenced by [[Joni Mitchell]],<ref name="Post">{{cite web|url=http://www.businesspost.ie/#!story/Home/News/Top+Tips+for+the+Weekend/id/79976514-152c-6662-2ece-c4312685488|title=Top Tips for the Weekend|last=O'Regan|first=Nadine|date=3 January 2014|work=[[The Sunday Business Post]] |access-date=6 January 2014}}</ref> [[Regina Spektor]], [[Imogen Heap]].<ref name="Campus"/><ref name="Soundhall"/> She frequently refers to herself as a "music makin' ginger nutcase".<ref name="Hennessy">{{cite news |last=Hennessy |first=David |date=March 18, 2020 |title=Doing it her way |url=https://www.theirishworld.com/doing-it-her-way/ |work=[[The Irish World (London)|The Irish World]] |access-date=December 22, 2020}}</ref> 

In August 2011 Gartland released her first EP ''Laughing at My Own Jokes''. The album artwork was done by a fan who won a contest. However, this EP has been deleted from most platforms and now "Roots" is considered her first official EP.

In 2012, she released her debut single, "Devil on My Shoulder", which entered the singer-songwriter iTunes charts in Ireland.<ref name="Hennessy" />

On 11 November 2013, Gartland released her second EP, ''[[Roots (Orla Gartland album)|Roots]]''. On 24 November, the ''Irish Mirror'' featured Gartland in a full-page article and stated that on [[iTunes]], the EP had topped the main Irish albums chart while hitting number 15 on the main UK albums chart and number two on the US singer-songwriter albums chart.<ref name="Mirror">{{cite web|last=Kierans|first=Marie|url=http://www.irishmirror.ie/news/irish-news/internet-sensation-orla-gartland-lines-2844502 |title=Internet sensation Orla Gartland lines up Irish & UK tour |work=[[Irish Mirror]]|date=24 November 2013|access-date=24 November 2013}}</ref>

Gartland's second EP, ''Lonely People'', was released on 18 January 2015, while her third EP, ''Why Am I Like This?'', was released on 24 May 2019. Her fourth EP, ''Freckle Season'', was released on 21 February 2020 and features previous singles "Did It to Myself", "Figure It Out", and "Heavy".

== Life and career ==
In an interview by ''Campus.ie'', Gartland is quoted "I played [[violin]], [[fiddle]], and [[Irish traditional music|trad]] and Irish stuff from the age of about five years old...my parents got me into lessons and the [[guitar]] at about 12 years old and it went from there."<ref name="Campus">{{cite web|last=Doona|first=Miriam|url=http://campus.ie/surviving-college/music/orla-gartland-interview|title=Orla Gartland – Interview|publisher=Campus.ie|date=8 November 2013|access-date=30 November 2013}}</ref> She posted her first YouTube video at age 13<ref name="Campus"/> about which she stated "I had been playing guitar for just over a year and wailing alongside the chords. I hadn't a clue how to sing; the breathing or any of the technical stuff (still haven't the foggiest) – so just wanted some feedback on that really!".<ref name="Soundhall">{{cite web |url=http://soundhall.wordpress.com/2012/02/23/an-interview-with-orla-gartland/|title=An Interview With... Orla Gartland|date=23 February 2012|publisher=Soundhall |access-date=30 November 2013}}</ref>
In June 2021, Gartland publicly came out as [[Bisexuality|bisexual]] on Twitter.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1409916906656645125|user=orlagartland|title=hey. i’m bi ! on this tail end of pride month I feel like celebrating by screaming it from some virtual rooftop<!-- full text of tweet that Twitter returned to the bot (excluding links) added by TweetCiteBot. === 2009–2013: Early music ===
In 2009, Gartland launched her YouTube channel under the name "MusicMaaad." She posted covers and then started posting originals. The first original she posted was titled "Green Eyed Monster."

In 2011, [[Broken Wall Films]] made a video of Gartland's song "All The Little Details."  The video was shot in a coffee shop which remained open for business during the filming.<ref>{{cite AV media|people=Allain, Nick (Director)|date=14 November 2011|title=Orla Gartland: All The Little Details Behind the Scenes – Part 1|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ob7SugREYPc|access-date=9 December 2013|format=video|publisher=Podsmiths}}</ref><ref>{{cite AV media|people=Allain, Nick (Director)|date=14 November 2011|title=Orla Gartland: All The Little Details Behind the Scenes – Part 2|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-nwXVsOqZ8U|access-date=9 December 2013|format=video|publisher=Podsmiths }}</ref> "All The Little Details" was Gartland's first professionally recorded song. It was included along with two other songs on her "Laughing At My Own Jokes" EP.

In 2012, Gartland opened for ''[[Britain's Got Talent]]'' contestant [[Ryan O'Shaughnessy]] at [[The Academy (music venue)|The Academy]]<ref name="festivalandgigguide">{{cite web|last=Freeman|first=Pat|url=http://www.festivalandgigguide.ie/index.php/blog/440-ryan-o-shaughnessy-ryan-will-debut-his-new-band-at-the-academy-this-friday-october-5th-in-the-academy|title=Ryan O'Shaughnessy|publisher=Festival and Gig Guide|date=2 October 2012|access-date=30 November 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029195832/http://www.festivalandgigguide.ie/index.php/blog/440-ryan-o-shaughnessy-ryan-will-debut-his-new-band-at-the-academy-this-friday-october-5th-in-the-academy|archive-date=29 October 2013}}</ref> and for Scottish singer-songwriter [[Nina Nesbitt]] at [[Bewley's]] Cafe Theatre.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goldenplec.com/competition-nina-nesbitt-support-orla-gartland-at-bewleys-theatre-may-4th|title=Competition: Nina Nesbitt (support Orla Gartland) at Bewleys Theatre on 4 May|publisher=GoldenPlec|date=1 May 2012|access-date=30 November 2013}}</ref> She released her debut single "Devil on my Shoulder" on 17 June on iTunes where it reached No. 2 on the Ireland singer-songwriter chart.<ref name="Devil on my Shoulder">{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Orla+Gartland/Devil+On+My+Shoulder|title=Devil on my Shoulder|publisher=Last.fm|access-date=30 November 2013}}</ref> The single was launched at a sold-out show headlined by Gartland at The Academy in Dublin.<ref>{{cite web|last=Cuffe|first=Aidan|url=http://www.goldenplec.com/orla-gartland-announces-dublin-and-cork-dates|title=Competition: Orla Gartland at The Academy 2 – CLOSED|publisher=GoldenPlec|date=24 May 2012|access-date=30 November 2013}}</ref>

In March 2013 Gartland announced her first tour, headlining five shows in the UK and in Ireland during July 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fortitudemagazine.co.uk/music/acoustic/orla-gartland-announces-first-ever-uk-ireland-tour/4731|title=Orla Gartland Announces First Ever UK & Ireland Tour|publisher=Fortitude Magazine|date=5 March 2013|access-date=30 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goldenplec.com/orla-gartland-announces-dublin-and-cork-dates|title=Orla Gartland announces Dublin and Cork dates|publisher=GoldenPlec|date=6 March 2013|access-date=30 November 2013}}</ref>

=== 2013–2015: Debut EPs and collaborations ===
On 11 November 2013 Gartland released her four-song EP ''Roots'' on iTunes.<ref name="Fortitude">{{cite web|last=MacKay|first=Connor|url=http://www.fortitudemagazine.co.uk/music/ep-review-orla-gartland-roots/13333|title=EP Review: Orla Gartland- Roots|publisher=Fortitude Magazine|date=11 November 2013|access-date=23 November 2013}}</ref> The titular track "Roots" was named Apple iTunes "Single of the Week" in the UK and Ireland.<ref>{{cite web|last=Gayadien|first=Amrish|url=http://www.applespot.nl/55803/apples-single-van-de-week-roots-orla-gartland|title=Apple's Single of the Week: Roots – Orla Gartland|publisher=AppleSpot|date=17 November 2013|access-date=23 November 2013}}</ref> She toured in support of the album in February 2014 visiting ten cities in Ireland and in the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bandsintown.com/OrlaGartland|title=Orla Gartland on Tour|publisher=Bandsintown.com|date=23 November 2013|access-date=23 November 2013}}</ref>

Gartland contributed the song "Cast Your Stone" on the album ''[[Simple Things (CAS album)|Simple Things]]'', a compilation album released on 15 November 2013 by [[Niall Breslin]] in support of suicide prevention in Ireland. She participated in a group performance of "Simple Things" musicians on [[RTÉ]] programme, ''[[The Saturday Night Show]]''.<ref name="Mirror"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rte.ie/ten/news/2013/1117/487189-bressie|title=Bressie reaches out on Saturday Night Show|website=[[RTÉ]]|date=27 November 2013|access-date=28 November 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rte.ie/ten/news/2013/1118/487453-cycle-against-suicide-album-launched|title=Cycle Against Suicide album launched|website=[[RTÉ]]|date=18 November 2013|access-date=28 November 2013}}</ref>

The ''[[Irish Daily Star]]'' selected Gartland as one of "20 music acts to watch in 2014."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thestar.ie/20-music-acts-to-watch-in-2014|title=20 music acts to watch in 2014|publisher=[[Irish Daily Star]]|date=10 January 2014|access-date=9 January 2014}}</ref>
The ''[[Irish Independent]]'' selected Gartland as one of "Ireland's most influential teens."<ref>{{cite news|last=Clifford|first=Graham|url=http://www.independent.ie/lifestyle/irelands-most-influential-teens-29949942.html|title=Ireland's most influential teens|newspaper=[[Irish Independent]]|date=27 January 2014|access-date=27 January 2014}}</ref> GoldenPlec selected Gartland as their "Plec Pick" of 2014, chosen as one of the best solo artists in Ireland.

Gartland, along with Greta Isaac, performed the song "Have Yourself A Merry Little Christmas" which they contributed to the compilation album ''It's Coming on Christmas''. The album raised funds for the breast cancer charity, [[CoppaFeel!]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cargocollective.com/DaisyDigital/It-s-Coming-on-Christmas-2014-1|title=It's Coming on Christmas|publisher=Daisy Digital|access-date=21 December 2014}}</ref> Gartland has played alongside [[Dodie Clark]] in all three of her EP release tours,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nz.pinterest.com/pin/410109109806495073/ |title=1,437 Likes, 21 Comments – Orla Gartland (@orlagartland) on Instagram: "Newcastle tonight w @doddleoddle #intertwinedtour" &#124; dodie clark &#124; Pinterest &#124; Comment… |website=Nz.pinterest.com |access-date=27 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BaRjkY1Db3L/ |title = Orla Gartland on Instagram: "and so it begins. #YouTour"}}</ref> and acted as a supporting act in Clark's 2019 'Human' tour.<ref name="Refinery">{{cite news |last=Lindsay |first=Kathryn |date=March 2, 2020 |title=Orla Gartland's Stripped-Down "Figure It Out" Music Video Brings The Song Back To Its Core |url=https://www.refinery29.com/en-us/2020/03/9493280/orla-gartland-figure-it-out-music-video-freckle-season-sessions |work=[[Refinery29]] |access-date=December 22, 2020}}</ref>

Gartland's second EP, ''Lonely People'', debuted in 2015 and was described by ''[[Atwood Magazine]]'' as an "undeniable triumph" that "captivates" by combining [[funk]] and [[alt-pop]].<ref name="Pettican">{{cite news |last=Pettican |first=Luke |date=July 30, 2018 |title=Today's Song: Orla Gartland's Refreshingly Relatable Alt-Pop Anthem 'I Go Crazy' |url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/oggc-orla-gartland-i-go-crazy-review/ |work=[[Atwood Magazine]] |access-date=December 24, 2020}}</ref>

=== 2018–2020: Third and fourth EPs ===
On 2 May 2018, Gartland released her first single in three years, "I Go Crazy",<ref>{{Citation|last=Orla Gartland|title=Orla Gartland – I Go Crazy (official audio)|date=1 May 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=thqjlHkJcQw|access-date=27 May 2018}}</ref> and on 2 November 2018 she released a new single "Between My Teeth",<ref>{{Citation|last=Orla Gartland|title=Orla Gartland – Between My Teeth|date=2 November 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dPSmWFGkCw4|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> which she announced were both standalone singles.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1091456935189270533|user=orlagartland|title=@swiftdodie @stareyesdodie hey! IGC &amp; BMT are standalone singles :) WAILT &amp; more to come will appear together on a release<!-- full text of tweet that Twitter returned to the bot (excluding links) added by TweetCiteBot. This may be better truncated or may need expanding (TW limits responses to 140 characters) or case changes. --> |accessdate=18 February 2019|language=en|date=1 February 2019}}</ref> On 8 February 2019, she released a new single "Why Am I Like This?",<ref>{{Citation|last=OrlaGartlandVEVO|title=Orla Gartland – Why Am I Like This?|date=7 February 2019|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SJgnWnb7wG8|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> followed by "Flatline" on 5 April 2019, both of which formed a part of her EP ''Why Am I Like This?'', released on 24 May 2019.<ref name="Motley">{{cite news |last=Shelly |first=Laurie |date=November 4, 2019 |title=Interview: Orla Gartland |url=https://motley.ie/interview-orla-gartland/ |work=[[Motley Magazine]] |access-date=December 22, 2020}}</ref>

On 6 September 2019, Gartland released the single "Did It to Myself", followed by "Figure It Out" on 1 November 2019,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goldenplec.com/new-irish-music-you-need-to-hear-this-week/|title=New Irish Music You Need To Hear This Week|date=1 November 2019|website=GoldenPlec|language=en|access-date=5 December 2019}}</ref> and "Heavy" on 15 January 2020, all of which appear on her EP ''Freckle Season''. "Figure It Out" is described as being about a relationship between individuals where neither can escape from it and the idea of needing space from someone you still care about.<ref>{{cite news |last=Garcia |first=Erica Danielle |date=November 25, 2019 |title=Today's Song: Orla Gartland Stays Hopeful In Fiery Single 'Figure It Out' |url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/ogfo-orla-gartland-figure-it-out-song-review/ |work=[[Atwood Magazine]] |access-date=December 22, 2020}}</ref>

She was also a co-writer of K-pop boy band [[BTS]]'s song "134340" from the album ''[[Love Yourself: Tear]]''.<ref name="Motley"/>

"Heavy" featured in the [[BBC Three]] trailer for the 2020 adaptation of ''[[Normal People (TV series)|Normal People]]'',<ref name="Refinery"/> with "Did It To Myself" featuring directly in the tv series.<ref>{{cite news |last=Brayden |first=Kate |date=January 13, 2021 |title=Orla Gartland tackles envy on confessional new track 'More Like You' |url=https://www.hotpress.com/music/orla-gartland-tackles-envy-on-confessional-new-track-more-like-you-22838543 |work=[[Hot Press]] |access-date=January 13, 2021}}</ref>

In 2020, Gartland was part of an [[Irish people|Irish]] collective of female singers and musicians called "[[Irish Women in Harmony]]", that recorded a version of the song "[[Dreams (The Cranberries song)|Dreams]]" in aid of the charity Safe Ireland, which deals with [[domestic abuse]] which had reportedly risen significantly during the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the Republic of Ireland|Covid-19 lockdown]].<ref name="it-1">{{cite news |last=Boland |first=Rosita |date=19 June 2020|title=Irish Women in Harmony record Cranberries song in aid of Safe Ireland |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/irish-women-in-harmony-record-cranberries-song-in-aid-of-safe-ireland-1.4283348 |access-date=28 July 2020 |work=[[The Irish Times]] |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Smither |first=Tanis |date=June 23, 2020 |title=Irish Women in Harmony for Safe Ireland: Orla Gartland on growing up in the Irish music scene |url=https://www.hotpress.com/music/irish-women-in-harmony-for-safe-ireland-orla-gartland-on-growing-up-in-the-irish-music-scene-22820220 |work=[[Hot Press]] |access-date=December 22, 2020}}</ref>

Gartland also released her third and fourth EPs, ''Why Am I Like This?'' and ''Freckle Season'', as a vinyl in 2020 titled ''Why Is Freckle Season Like This?''. The vinyl included the tracklist of both EPs in addition to a currently unreleased song, "Don’t Fall In Love With A Musician" and an acoustic version of "Figure It Out".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.banquetrecords.com/orla-gartland/why-is-freckle-season-like-this%3F/OG02 |title=Orla Gartland: Why Is Freckle Season Like This? |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=July 17, 2020 |website=[[Banquet Records]] |access-date=April 24, 2021}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''Woman on the Internet'' ===
On 16 October 2020, Gartland released a single called "Pretending".<ref>{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Orla Gartland Unveils New Track 'Pretending' |url=https://diymag.com/2020/10/19/watch-orla-gartland-pretending-video |work=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |date=October 19, 2020 |access-date=December 22, 2020}}</ref> The song discusses the difficulty and trouble of pretending to be the person someone else wants, in order to be a "people pleaser".<ref>{{cite news |last=Brow |first=Jason |date=October 16, 2020 |title=Orla Gartland Captures The 'Exhausting' Toll Of Pleasing Others In New 'Pretending' Video |url=https://hollywoodlife.com/2020/10/16/orla-gartland-pretending-video-new-song/ |work=[[Hollywood Life]] |access-date=December 22, 2020}}</ref> This is to be the first single released from ''Woman on the Internet'', her debut album released on 20 August 2021.<ref>{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=Orla Gartland Releases New Single 'Pretending' From Upcoming Album |url=https://outnowmagazine.com/orla-gartland-pretending/ |work=Out Now Magazine |date=October 2020 |access-date=December 22, 2020}}</ref> <ref>{{Cite web|last=|date=June 9, 2021|title=Orla Gartland announces debut album 'Woman on the Internet' {{!}} News|url=http://diymag.com/2021/06/09/orla-gartland-debut-album-woman-on-the-internet|url-status=live|access-date=2021-06-09|website=diymag.com|language=en}}</ref>The second single, "More Like You", was released on 13 January 2021. According to Gartland herself, the song deals with identity, jealousy, and the desire to have the life of someone who seems to have everything easy.<ref>{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s)/no by-line.--> |date=January 13, 2021 |title=Orla Gartland Shares New Single "More Like You" |url=https://diymag.com/2021/01/13/listen-orla-gartland-more-like-you |work=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |access-date=January 13, 2021}}</ref> The third single, "Zombie!", was released on 13 April 2021. It deals with the topic of [[toxic masculinity]] and with Gartland entreating another individual to open up about their emotions and stop repressing their true feelings.<ref>{{cite news |last=Scolforo |first=Carli |date=April 13, 2021 |title=Orla Gartland Shares Peppy New Single, "Zombie!" |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/music/orla-gartland/orla-gartland-shares-zombie/ |work=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |access-date=April 13, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |author=<!--Staff writer(s)/no by-line.--> |title=Behind the music - Orla Gartland |url=https://www.rte.ie/entertainment/2021/0422/1211446-behind-the-music-orla-gartland/ |work=[[RTÉ.ie]] |date=April 22, 2021 |access-date=May 21, 2021}}</ref> The song was produced alongside Pete Robertson, a producer for [[beabadoobee]] and [[The Vaccines]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Murray |first=Robin |date=April 13, 2021 |title=Orla Gartland's 'Zombie!' Is A Potent Depiction Of Male Emotional Repression |url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/orla-gartlands-zombie-is-a-potent-depiction-of-male-emotional-repression |work=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]] |access-date=April 13, 2021}}</ref>
