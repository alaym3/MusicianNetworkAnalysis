'''Alessandro Mahmoud''' ({{IPA-it|alesˈsandro maˈmud}}, {{IPA-arz|mæħˈmuːd|lang}}; born 12 September 1992), known professionally as '''Mahmood''', is an Italian singer-songwriter. He first came to prominence after competing in the [[X Factor (Italian season 6)|sixth season]] of the [[X Factor (Italian TV series)|Italian version]] of ''[[The X Factor]]''. In 2019, he won the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2019|Sanremo Music Festival]] with the song "[[Soldi]]", and went on to represent {{esccnty|Italy|y=2019}} in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2019]],<ref>{{cite news|publisher=La Repubblica |title=Sanremo, il vincitore è Mahmood. Ultimo è secondo, terzo classificato Il Volo|url=https://www.repubblica.it/dossier/spettacoli/festival-di-sanremo-2019/2019/02/09/news/sanremo_2019_serata_finale_vincitore-218728164/|date=10 February 2019}}</ref><ref name="Castagneri">{{cite news |last1=Castagneri |first1=Alice |last2=Pavanello |first2=Roberto |title=Mahmood vince il Festival di Sanremo |url=https://www.lastampa.it/2019/02/09/spettacoli/festival-di-sanremo-la-diretta-della-finale-2TJdTHov8uMeB2HK82VCJL/pagina.html |access-date=11 February 2019 |work=[[La Stampa]] |date=10 February 2019 |language=it}}</ref> finishing in 2nd place. His debut album, ''[[Gioventù bruciata]]'', was released in February 2019 and debuted at number one on the Italian albums chart.

==Early life==
Born in [[Milan]] to an [[Egyptians|Egyptian]] father and an Italian mother from [[Sardinia]], he was raised around [[Gratosoglio]], a district of [[Milan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ilgiornale.it/news/spettacoli/mahmood-conquista-lariston-sono-ragazzo-italiano-1642959.html|title=Mahmood conquista l'Ariston: "Sono un ragazzo italiano"|last=Rio|first=Paolo Giordano Laura|website=Il Giornale|language=it|access-date=12 February 2019}}</ref> When he was five years old, his parents divorced and he was subsequently raised by his mother.<ref name="NYtimes">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/03/31/world/europe/italian-minister-pans-hit-song-by-an-immigrants-son.html|title=Italian Minister Pans Hit Song by an Immigrant's Son|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=31 March 2019|access-date=1 April 2019}}</ref><ref name="unionesarda">{{Cite web|url=https://www.unionesarda.it/articolo/spettacoli/2019/02/07/mahmood-a-sanremo-quel-padre-scomparso-e-la-sardegna-fonte-della-7-832376.html|title=Mahmood: "Quel padre scomparso e la Sardegna, fonte della mia ispirazione" IL VIDEO|website=unionesarda.it|language=it|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
===2012–2018: ''X Factor'' and beginnings===
In 2012, Mahmood auditioned for the [[X Factor (Italian season 6)|sixth season]] of the [[X Factor (Italian TV series)|Italian version]] of ''[[The X Factor]]''. He became part of the boys category mentored by [[Simona Ventura]]. He was originally eliminated at Judges Houses, then returned as a wildcard, before eventually being eliminated in the third episode.<ref name="esckaz">{{Cite web|url=http://www.esckaz.com/2019/ita.htm|title=Mahmood (Italy) / Махмуд (Италия)|website=esckaz.com|access-date=21 March 2019}}</ref> After this experience, he worked in a bar and attended a music school, studying piano, solfeggio and music theory, where he also started writing songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://italicsmag.com/2019/02/23/eurovision-song-contest-2019-mahmood/|title=Eurovision Song Contest 2019: Who Is Mahmood?|work=Italics Magazine|date=23 February 2019|access-date=28 March 2019}}</ref> In 2013, he released his debut single "Fallin' Rain".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.radiomusik.it/mahmoud-fallin-rain-audio-e-cover-singolo/|publisher=RadioMusik.it|language=it|date=24 July 2013|title=Mahmoud – Fallin' Rain (audio e cover singolo)|author=Alberto Muraro}}</ref>

In 2016, Mahmood participated in the Newcomers Section of [[Sanremo Music Festival 2016|Sanremo Music Festival]] with the song "Dimentica". In 2017, he released the single "Pesos", with which he participates in the fifth edition of the Wind Summer Festival, winning the third episode of the Youth section.<ref name="esckaz" /> In 2017, he was also featured on [[Fabri Fibra]]'s single "Luna".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/luna-feat-mahmood-single/1252021989|title=Luna (feat. Mahmood) - Single by Fabri Fibra|work=iTunes|access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref> In September 2018, he released his debut [[extended play]], ''[[Gioventù bruciata]]''. In November 2018, [[Marco Mengoni]] released his fifth album, ''Atlantico'', which features three tracks co-written by Mahmood, including the single "Hola (I Say)".<ref name="esckaz" />

===2018–2020: ''Gioventù bruciata'', Sanremo and Eurovision===
[[File:Mahmood rehearsal.jpg|thumb|200px|Mahmood during a Eurovision rehearsal in May 2019.]]
In December 2018, Mahmood was one of 24 acts selected to compete in ''Sanremo Giovani'', a televised competition aimed at selecting two newcomers as contestants of the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2019|69th Sanremo Music Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lastampa.it/2018/11/27/spettacoli/sanremo-giovani-ecco-i-cantanti-selezionati-per-la-finale-tanti-ex-dei-talent-pIDYZRPwvaq57oLx6zBUnI/pagina.html|work=[[La Stampa]]|date=27 November 2018|title=Sanremo Giovani 2019, ecco i 24 cantanti selezionati per la finale: tanti ex dei talent|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref> Mahmood was placed first in the second episode of the show, with his entry "Gioventù bruciata", also receiving the Critics' Award among acts performing in the second final.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rockol.it/news-699102/sanremo-giovani-vincitore-e-mahmood-classifica-finale|publisher=Rockol.it|language=it|access-date=16 February 2019|title=Sanremo Giovani, Mahmood vince la seconda finale e va in gara tra i big. A lui anche il Premio della Critica. La classifica|date=22 December 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ilfattoquotidiano.it/2018/12/22/sanremo-giovani-mahmood-e-il-vincitore-della-seconda-serata-ecco-il-cast-completo-di-sanremo-2019/4854466/|work=[[Il Fatto Quotidiano]]|date=22 December 2018|title=Sanremo giovani, Mahmood è il vincitore della seconda serata. Ecco il cast completo di Sanremo 2019|author=Giuseppe Candela|access-date=16 February 2019|language=it}}</ref> "[[Soldi]]" was later announced as his entry for the Sanremo Music Festival 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sorrisi.com/musica/sanremo/festival-di-sanremo-2019/|work=[[TV Sorrisi e Canzoni]]|language=it|access-date=16 February 2019|title=Sanremo 2019: duetti, ospiti e ultime notizie dal Festival|date=8 February 2019}}</ref>

Mahmood performed the song for the first time during the first live show of the 69th Sanremo Music Festival, which was held on 5 February 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.it/tv/news-tv/sanremo-2019-la-cronaca-della-prima-serata/444213/|work=[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]|language=it|access-date=16 February 2019|title=Sanremo 2019, la cronaca della prima serata|date=5 February 2019}}</ref> [[Dario Faini|Dario "Dardust" Faini]], co-writer of the song, directed the Sanremo Orchestra during his performance.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ilrestodelcarlino.it/ascoli/spettacoli/mahmood-canzone-faini-1.4436279|work=[[Il Resto Del Carlino]]|language=it|access-date=16 February 2019|title=Mahmood vince Sanremo 2019, l'ascolano Dario Faini ha scritto la canzone|date=10 February 2019}}</ref> During the third live show, "Soldi" was the first performance of the night.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rockol.it/news-700767/sanremo-2019-terza-serata-pagelle-commento-e-scaletta|publisher=Rockol.it|language=it|access-date=16 February 2019|title=Sanremo 2019, terza serata: date la conduzione del Festival alla Vanoni, gli Zen Circus spaccano|date=7 February 2019}}</ref> On 8 February 2019, Mahmood performed the song in a new version, featuring rapper [[Gué Pequeno]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.repubblica.it/dossier/spettacoli/festival-di-sanremo-2019/2019/02/08/news/sanremo_cronaca_quarta_serata-218647863/|work=[[la Repubblica]]|language=it|access-date=16 February 2019|title=Sanremo, la quarta serata è dei duetti. Con Ligabue l'Ariston è rock, Anastasio canta gli sdraiati|author=Alessandra Vitali|date=8 February 2019}}</ref> During the first round of the final, "Soldi" placed 7th in the televote, but was the most voted by the experts jury, and the second most voted by the press jury.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rai.it/dl/doc/2019/02/10/1549808790256_Risultati_Televoto_Sanremo_2019.pdf|publisher=[[RAI]]|title=Classifiche Sanremo 2019|language=it}}</ref> As a result, Mahmood gained a spot in the top three acts of the competition and after an additional performance, the song was declared the winner of the 69th Sanremo Music Festival.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lastampa.it/2019/02/10/spettacoli/sanremo-al-gran-finale-TQzvKWYSYixiL47tnN7AXO/pagina.html|work=[[La Stampa]]|language=it|access-date=16 February 2019|title=Mahmood vince il Festival di Sanremo, sul podio Ultimo e Il Volo|author=Alberto Mattioli|date=10 February 2019}}</ref> 
Mahmood also received the "Enzo Jannacci" Award for Best Performance.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rockol.it/news-700765/sanremo-2019-premio-enzo-jannacci-assegnato-nuovoimaie-a-mahmood|publisher=Rockol|language=it|access-date=16 February 2019|title=Sanremo 2019, il Premio Enzo Jannacci assegnato da Nuovoimaie a Mahmood|date=8 February 2019}}</ref> and the "Premio Baglioni d'oro" award for best song voted by the participating artists.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.avantionline.it/sanremo-2019-un-festival-che-vuole-descrivere-il-nostro-paese/|work=[[Avanti! (Italian newspaper)|Avanti! onLine]]|language=it|access-date=12 February 2019|title=Sanremo 2019: un Festival che vuole descrivere il nostro Paese|author=Barbara Conti|date=12 February 2019}}</ref>

On 22 February 2019, Mahmood reissued ''Gioventù bruciata'' as a full-length [[Album|studio album]], which topped the Italian albums chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://italiancharts.com/search.asp?search=Mahmood&cat=a|title=Discographie Mahmood (Albums)|website=italiancharts.com|access-date=7 May 2019}}</ref> In April 2019, Mahmood was featured on the single "[[Calipso (song)|Calipso]]" by [[Charlie Charles]] and Dardust, which topped the Italian singles chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fimi.it/top-of-the-music/classifiche.kl#/charts/3/2019/18|title=Top Singoli – Classifica settimanale WK 18|publisher=[[Federazione Industria Musicale Italiana]]|language=it|access-date=3 May 2019}}</ref> In May 2019, he represented [[Italy in the Eurovision Song Contest 2019|Italy]] at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2019]] in [[Tel Aviv]], [[Israel]]. He performed "Soldi" in the grand final of the contest on 18 May, placing second in the final ranking. The song topped the charts in Greece, Israel, Italy and Lithuania and reached the top 10 in five more countries, eventually becoming the most-streamed Eurovision song ever on [[Spotify]], a record held until late January 2021 when it was surpassed by the 2019 Eurovision winner, "[[Arcade (song)|Arcade]]" by [[Duncan Laurence]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2019/10/16/italy-mahmoods-soldi-is-now-the-most-streamed-eurovision-song-on-spotify/242939/|title=Italy: Mahmood's "Soldi" is now the most-streamed Eurovision song on Spotify|publisher=Wiwibloggs|access-date=14 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title="Arcade" passes "Soldi" as the most-streamed Eurovision entry on Spotify|url=https://escxtra.com/2021/01/29/arcade-passes-soldi-as-the-most-streamed-eurovision-entry-on-spotify/|access-date=14 February 2021|publisher=ESCXTRA}}</ref> In Israel in 2019, Mahmood performed together with Israeli singer [[Eden Ben Zaken|Eden Ben-Zaken]] and the two even planned to record a duet together, which ended up being cancelled due to the outbreak of the [[COVID-19 pandemic|covid-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-09-19|title=Mahmood and Eden Ben Zaken perform in Rishon LeZion, Israel. October 3, 2019|url=https://www.touristisrael.com/mahmood-eden-ben-zaken-rishon-lezion-israel/27565/|access-date=2021-11-20|website=Tourist Israel|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Mix • Mahmood - I Don't Care feat. Eden Ben Zaken + Uramaki + Sabbie Mobili|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dLIWVR7cNDk|language=en|access-date=2021-11-20}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Eden Ben Zaken vola in Italia per conoscere Mahmood (andato in onda in Israele il 12/9/2019)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LwMqOD0mveI|language=en|access-date=2021-11-20}}</ref> Mahmood performed at the closing ceremony of the [[2019 Summer Universiade]] in [[Naples]] with three of his songs "Soldi", "[[Gioventù bruciata (song)|Gioventù bruciata]]" and "Anni 90".

=== 2020–present: ''Ghettolimpo'' ===
On 11 June 2021, Mahmood released his sophomore studio album, ''[[Ghettolimpo]]''.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Graziola|first=Alberto|date=2021-04-19|title=Ghettolimpo, Mahmood, album, cover, significato, tracklist|url=https://www.soundsblog.it/post/ghettolimpo-mahmood-album-quando-esce-tracklist-cover|access-date=2021-06-11|website=Soundsblog|language=it}}</ref> The album's release was preceded by five singles: "[[Rapide (song)|Rapide]]", "[[Dorado (song)|Dorado]]" (in collaboration with fellow Italian rapper [[Sfera Ebbasta]] and Colombian singer [[Feid]]), "[[Inuyasha (song)|Inuyasha]]", "[[Zero (Mahmood song)|Zero]]" (also included in the soundtrack of the [[Zero (2021 TV series)|2021 Netflix series of the same name]]) and "[[Klan (song)|Klan]]".

==Public image==
Mahmood is a portmanteau of Alessandro's surname Mahmoud and the English expression "my mood".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://wiwibloggs.com/2019/04/17/mahmood-10-facts-about-italys-eurovision-2019-singer/236577/|title=Mahmood: 10 facts about Italy's Eurovision 2019 singer|work=[[Wiwibloggs]]|date=17 April 2019|access-date=1 May 2019}}</ref> Despite his father's origins, Mahmood does not speak Arabic but is fluent in [[Sardinian language|Sardinian]].<ref name="unionesarda" /> Following his Sanremo win, then Deputy Prime Minister [[Matteo Salvini]], who is known for his  anti-immigrant views, criticized him for winning over [[Ultimo (singer)|Ultimo]] who received the highest number of public votes.<ref name="euronews">{{cite web|url=https://www.euronews.com/2019/02/11/controversy-sparked-as-italian-egyptian-wins-italy-s-sanremo-song-contest|title=Winner of Italy's Sanremo song contest highlights political divides|work=[[Euronews]]|date=11 February 2019|access-date=26 April 2019}}</ref>

Mahmood has an aversion to being categorized by his nationality or sexuality.<ref name="NYtimes" /> He has stated that "declaring 'I'm gay' leads nowhere" and "If we continue with these distinctions, homosexuality will never be perceived as a normal thing, which it is".<ref name="vanityfair">{{cite web|url=https://www.vanityfair.it/music/storie-music/2019/02/01/mahmood-sanremo-2019-soldi-canzone-foto-gossip|title=Mahmood: "Vi piace cantautore Moroccan pop?"|work=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]]|date=1 February 2019|access-date=22 March 2019}}</ref> According to Italian press, while he has refused to answer the question of whether he's gay, he has stated that he's "happy and engaged."<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-05-22|title=Lorenzo Tobia Marcucci e la storia d'amore con Mahmood|url=https://metropolitanmagazine.it/lorenzo-tobia-marcucci-e-la-storia-damore-con-mahmood/|access-date=2021-11-20|website=Metropolitan Magazine|language=it-IT}}</ref>

In 2019, he was named to the Italian [[Forbes 30 Under 30|''Forbes'' 30 Under 30]], a list showcasing entrepreneurs, entertainers and celebrities who have made a name for themselves before reaching the age of 30.<ref>{{cite web|date=29 March 2019|title=Forbes 100 30 Under 30 Italia 2019|url=https://forbes.it/classifica/forbes-100-under-30-italia-2019/|access-date=26 April 2019|work=[[Forbes]]}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Mahmood has described his music as [[Moroccan pop]]. According to him "what sets me apart are the [[Middle Eastern music|Middle Eastern]] sounds that emerge here and there".<ref name="vanityfair" /> "Soldi", which is an autobiographic song about his "unconventional family", includes a sentence in Arabic.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://stream24.ilsole24ore.com/video/lifestyle/sanremo-mahmood-soldi-canzone-autobiografica-piena-rabbia/ABdj4kSB?refresh_ce=1|work=[[Il Sole 24 Ore]]|language=it|access-date=20 May 2019|title=Sanremo, Mahmood: Soldi canzone autobiografica piena di rabbia|date=11 February 2019}}</ref> One of his earliest musical memories is listening to Arabic music with his father, before he left the family.<ref name="blastingnews" /> Growing up he also listened to Italian artists like [[Lucio Battisti]], [[Lucio Dalla]] and [[Paolo Conte]].<ref name="blastingnews">{{cite web|url=https://it.blastingnews.com/tv-gossip/2018/12/sanremo-giovani-il-trionfo-di-mahmood-da-x-factor-con-la-ventura-ai-big-del-festival-002802511.html|title=Sanremo Giovani, il trionfo di Mahmood: da X Factor con la Ventura ai big del Festival|work=[[Blasting News]]|date=22 December 2018|access-date=20 May 2019}}</ref><ref name="NYtimes" /> He considers himself very linked to Sardinia's culture and folklore, which often acts as a source of inspiration for the composition of his songs.<ref name="unionesarda" />

Mahmood has cited American singer [[Frank Ocean]] as his biggest influence: "[His music] is very free and in which everyone can identify themselves, gay or straight, because the essential is the person and the energy that he transmits and not the sexual orientation".<ref name="rollingstone">{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.it/musica/interviste-musica/la-gioventu-bruciata-di-mahmood/430410/|title=La 'gioventù bruciata' di Mahmood|work=Rolling Stone|date=30 September 2018|access-date=13 May 2019}}</ref> His other favorite artists include [[Travis Scott]], [[Beyoncé]], [[Jazmine Sullivan]], [[Rosalía (singer)|Rosalía]] and [[SZA (singer)|SZA]].<ref name="rollingstone" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://los40.com/los40/2019/06/19/musica/1560955090_216018.html|title=40 cosas sobre Mahmood que (quizás) no sabías|work=[[Los 40]]|date=20 June 2019|access-date=20 June 2019}}</ref>

=== Author and songwriter for other artists ===
Mahmood during his career has written and composed songs with many Italian songwriters and producers, including Francesco Catitti, Dardust, [[Charlie Charles]], [[Alessandro Raina]], and artists such as [[Marracash]], [[Chiara (Italian singer)|Chiara]], [[Marco Mengoni]], [[Fabri Fibra]], [[Elodie (singer)|Elodie]] and [[Michele Bravi]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/artists/Mahmood|title=Mahmood|website=Genius|language=en|access-date=2020-03-24}}</ref>
In 2019, thanks to his victory at the Sanremo Music Festival, [[Mogol (lyricist)|Mogol]] offered the artist a scholarship to improve his skills as a songwriter at the European Centre in Toscolano for authors, composers and artists.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://it.blastingnews.com/cultura-spettacoli/2019/02/mahmood-invitato-alla-scuola-di-mogol-il-maestro-la-canzone-e-carina-ma-e-da-sgrezzare-002857207.html|title=Mahmood invitato alla scuola di Mogol, il maestro: 'La canzone è carina ma è da sgrezzare'|date=2019-02-27|website=Blasting News|language=it|access-date=2020-04-15}}</ref>
