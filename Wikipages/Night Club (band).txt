'''Night Club''' is an American [[Electronic music|electronic]] band, formed by [[Mark Brooks (director)|Mark Brooks]] (aka 3 Kord Scissor King)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenervousbreakdown.com/stow/2012/10/night-club-tackles-dark-pop |title=Stephen Tow &#124; Night Club Tackles Dark Pop |publisher=The Nervous Breakdown |date=2012-10-10 |accessdate=2016-02-15}}</ref> and Emily Kavanaugh in 2012. The band is from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].

==Career==
Night Club released their first single “Lovestruck” in July 2012 and their self-titled debut EP in September 2012. A video for "Lovestruck" was released in September 2012 in conjunction with the release of the EP. Night Club's radio debut occurred on [[KROQ-FM|KROQ]] DJ [[Rodney Bingenheimer]]'s ''Rodney on the Roq'' program on October 22, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kroq.cbslocal.com/2012/10/22/rodney-on-the-roq-playlist-102212-silversun-pickups-karen-o-the-art-and-more |title=Rodney On The Roq Playlist 10/22/12: Silversun Pickups, Karen O, The Art, And More « The World Famous KROQ |website=Kroq.cbslocal.com |date=2012-10-22 |accessdate=2016-02-15}}</ref> Night Club released a video for their second single "Control" in February 2013.<ref>{{cite web|author=Kevin Bronson |url=http://buzzbands.la/2013/02/15/video-night-club-control/ |title=Video: Night Club, 'Control' – |website=Buzzbands.la |date=2013-02-15 |accessdate=2016-02-15}}</ref>

Night Club released their second EP ''Love Casualty'' in June 2013.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/172512-night-club-love-casualty/ |title=Night Club: Love Casualty |website=PopMatters.com |date= |accessdate=2016-02-15}}</ref> In conjunction with the EP release, the band released three videos: "Poisonous", "Strobe Light" and "Give Yourself Up".

Night Club released their third EP ''Black Leather Heart'' in September 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.electricity-club.co.uk/night-club-black-leather-heart-ep/ |title=NIGHT CLUB Black Leather Heart EP |publisher=The Electricity Club |date=2014-09-02 |accessdate=2016-02-15}}</ref> In conjunction with the EP release, the band released three videos: "Need You Tonight", "She Wants To Play With Fire" and "Not In Love".

In 2013, Night Club composed the music for the [[Comedy Central]] television pilot ''[[Moonbeam City]]'' featuring [[Rob Lowe]]. The show was picked up for a first season in 2014 and the band composed the music for all ten episodes. "Moonbeam City" aired in late 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cartoonbrew.com/tv/comedy-central-teases-1980s-crime-parody-moonbeam-city-102291.html |accessdate=March 27, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150425145900/http://www.cartoonbrew.com/tv/comedy-central-teases-1980s-crime-parody-moonbeam-city-102291.html|title=Comedy Central Teases 1980s Crime Show Parody ‘Moonbeam City’ |archivedate=April 25, 2015 }}</ref> Milan Records released the official soundtrack for ''Moonbeam City'' in November 2015.

In 2015, Night Club composed the music for the feature film ''[[Nerdland]]'' which was directed by [[Chris Prynoski]] and written by [[Andrew Kevin Walker]].<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.bubbleblabber.com/night-club-tapped-to-do-music-for-nerdland/ |title='Night Club' tapped to do music for Nerdland |website=Bubbleblabber.com |date= |accessdate=2016-03-16}}</ref> The film features [[Paul Rudd]], [[Patton Oswalt]], [[Kate Micucci]], [[Riki Lindhome]], [[Hannibal Buress]], [[Mike Judge]] and [[Paul Scheer]]. The film was released theatrically on December 6, 2016. The official soundtrack was released by Gato Blanco on the same day.

The band released their first LP, ''[[Requiem for Romance]]'', in October 2016. The videos for "Bad Girl", "Dear Enemy" and "Show It 2 Me" were released in conjunction with the album. The LP reached #2 on the [[CMJ]] RPM chart and was voted best non-[[Heavy metal music|metal]] album by ''Metal Injection'' in 2016. <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalinjection.net/lists/best-of-2016/robs-top-10-albums-of-2016 |title=Rob's Top 10 Albums of 2016 |publisher=Metal Injection |date=2016-12-23}}</ref>

The band's second LP, ''[[Scary World]]'', was released in August 2018. The videos for "Candy Coated Suicide", "Schizophrenic" and "Your Addiction" were released in conjunction with the album. The LP debuted at #4 US, #8 UK and #11 Sweden on the [[iTunes]] electronic charts upon release.<ref>[https://twitter.com/vitriolpromo/status/1033076525766193158/photo/1] {{dead link|date=October 2020}}</ref> ''Electrozombies'' named "Scary World" #2 on their best albums of 2018. <ref>{{Cite web |url=http://electrozombies.com/article/best-of/best-albums-2018// |title=Electrozombies best albums of 2018 |website=Electrozombies.com |date=December 27, 2018}}</ref> The song "Scary World" was included in ''The Electricity Club''<nowiki/>'s 30 Songs of 2018. <ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.electricityclub.co.uk/the-electricity-clubs-30-songs-of-2018/|title=The Electricity Club’s 30 SONGS OF 2018|website=Electricityclub.co.uk|date=3 December 2018|accessdate=4 October 2020}}</ref> ''MXDWN'' named Night Club "Best New Artist of 2018".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://music.mxdwn.com/2019/01/22/features/best-new-artist-2018-an-interview-with-emily-kavanaugh-and-mark-brooks-of-night-club/ |title=MXDWN Best New Artist 2018 |website=MXDWN |date=January 22, 2019}}</ref>

The band's third LP, ''Die Die Lullaby'', was released in October 2020 and was co-mixed by [[Dave Ogilvie|Dave "Rave" Ogilvie]]. <ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.electricityclub.co.uk/night-club-die-die-lullaby/|title=Night Club Die Die Lullaby |website=electricityclub.co.uk |date=October 2, 2020}}</ref> The first single "Miss Negativity" was accompanied by a music video featuring Kavanaugh as a pageant performer, with references to the film [[Carrie (1976 film)|''Carrie'']]. <ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-28|title=mxdwn PREMIERE: Night Club's New Video for "Miss Negativity" is a Hellish Fever Dream of Pageantry -|url=https://music.mxdwn.com/2020/08/28/news/mxdwn-premiere-night-clubs-new-video-for-miss-negativity-is-a-hellish-fever-dream-of-pageantry/|access-date=2020-08-28|website=Music.mxdwn.com|language=en-US}}</ref> The video for "Gossip" was released in conjunction with the album. The LP debuted at #1 on the US iTunes electronic chart upon release.
