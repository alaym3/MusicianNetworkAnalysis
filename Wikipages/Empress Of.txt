'''Lorely Rodriguez''' (born October 19, 1989),  known professionally as '''Empress Of''', is an American singer, songwriter, musician and record producer based in Los Angeles. Her debut album, ''[[Me (Empress Of album)|Me]]'', was released to critical acclaim in 2015.

== Career ==
Rodriguez initially gained attention in 2012 for anonymously releasing a series of one minute-long demos (via [[YouTube]]) prefaced only by a solid color entitled "Colorminutes".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://prettymuchamazing.com/music/stream/check-out-empress-of-11-colorminutes|title=Empress Of's 11 "Colorminutes" « PMA - Pretty Much Amazing|website=Prettymuchamazing.com}}</ref> Her first 7" single, "Champagne" was released soon after on November 5, 2012, through a limited run via No Recordings.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1192151/empress-of-champagne-video/video/|title=Empress Of – "Champagne" Video|website=Stereogum.com|date=November 5, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/norecordings/empress-of-champagne-1|title=Empress Of // "Champagne"|website=SoundCloud.com}}</ref> Lorely's stage name was inspired by a [[tarot card reading]] she did with a friend: "The first card he pulled out was an Empress card and I was like, 'It's me, I am Empress.' [...] the Empress card is connected to fertility and mothering and strength. It's kind of nice to have those feelings."<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/empress-of-interview|title=Empress Of Takes the Cards She Was Dealt|website=Noisey.vice.com|access-date=August 22, 2016|date=September 2015}}</ref>

On April 2, 2013, her bilingual four-track EP ''Systems'' was released via [[Double Denim Records|Double Denim]] and [[Terrible Records]] in the UK and North America respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/15044-hat-trick/|title=Empress Of|website=Pitchfork.com}}</ref> Since the release of her EP, she's showcased at [[SXSW]], [[Iceland Airwaves]] and [[Pitchfork Music Festival|Pitchfork's Summer Music Festival]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sidewalkhustle.com/5-minutes-with-empress-of-at-pitchfork-music-festival-2013/|title=5 Minutes with Empress Of at Pitchfork Music Festival 2013|website=Sidewalkhustle.com|date=August 2, 2013}}</ref> and toured as a support act with the likes of [[Jamie Lidell]], [[Jungle (band)|Jungle]], [[Kimbra]] and [[Florence and the Machine]].

"Water Water", the first single off her debut full-length ''[[Me (Empress Of album)|Me]]'' was released on April 14, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://terriblerecords.com/2015/04/empress-of-debuts-new-song-water-water/|title=Empress Of debuts new song "Water Water"|website=Terriblerecords.com}}</ref> She has also released a Spanish-language version of the track, "Agua Agua" "so that [her] mother listens [to her] music." Rodriguez's debut album ''Me,'' which she self-produced and recorded in [[Mexico City|Mexico]] was released on September 11, 2015, via [[XL Recordings]] and Terrible.

During 2017, Rodriguez released a standalone single, "[[Go to Hell (Empress Of song)|Go to Hell]]" on July 12. 2017 via Terrible. She also featured on two singles for Los Angeles producers, [[DJDS]] with the ''[[Billboard charts|Billboard]]''-charting "Why Don't You Come On" additionally featuring [[El Paso]] R&B singer, [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] and a cover of [[Lana Del Rey]]'s ''[[Lust for Life (Lana Del Rey album)|Lust for Life]]'' single, "[[Love (Lana Del Rey song)|Love]]" released on August 29, 2017, and December 1, 2017, respectively through [[Loma Vista Recordings]] for their third studio album ''Big Wave More Fire''.

On April 11, 2018, [[double A-side|double single]] "[[Trust Me Baby / In Dreams]]" premiered on [[Zane Lowe]]'s Apple Music "[[Beats 1]]" radio show and released via Terrible. The former was co-produced by [[Cole M.G.N.]] In October 2018, she was featured on [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]]'s first extended play release, [[Suncity]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8505545/every-khalid-collaboration-ranked|title=All 27 of Khalid's Collaborations, Ranked: Critic's Picks|website=Billboard|access-date=April 24, 2019}}</ref>

Rodriguez's second album, ''[[Us (Empress Of album)|Us]]'', was released on October 19, 2018. She supported [[Maggie Rogers]] on her fall 2019 ''[[Heard It in a Past Life]]'' tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/03/20/maggie-rogers-fall-tour|title=Maggie Rogers unveils fall tour dates|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=April 24, 2019}}</ref>

Her song "When I’m With Him" is featured on the soundtrack of the video game [[eFootball Pro Evolution Soccer 2020]].

On April 12, 2020, Rodriguez's released her third studio album, [[I'm Your Empress Of]] via XL Recordings and Terrible. The album was preceded by the single, "Give Me Another Chance" released and premiered March 3. 2020 via Zane Lowe's Apple Music "Beats 1" radio.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/empress-of-announces-new-album-shares-video-for-new-song-watch/|title=Empress of Announces New Album, Shares Video for New Song|date=March 3, 2020}}</ref> Due to the 2020 COVID-19 crisis, no touring or live performances could be done to promote the album.

During 2020, Rodriguez founded and announced the start her own record label, Major Arcana. The announcement marked the fulfilment of her contracts with XL Recordings and Terrible Records respectively, thus allowing all future releases to be fully independent under her own brand.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vulture.com/2020/11/empress-of-interview-new-label-youve-got-to-feel.html|title = Empress of Didn't Start a Record Label Just for Herself (But It Helps)|date = November 11, 2020}}</ref>

On October 27, 2020, Rodriguez released her first single via Major Arcana, "You've Got To Feel" featuring [[Grammy nominated]] singer/songwriter, [[Amber Mark]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2020/10/empress-of-amber-mark-youve-got-to-feel/|title = Empress of enlists Amber Mark for new song "You've Got to Feel": Stream|date = October 27, 2020}}</ref> while subsequently releasing another single, "Broken" for the soundtrack of the [[Amazon Prime Video]] series, [[The Wilds (TV series)|The Wilds]] on Nov 20, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uproxx.com/indie/empress-of-broken-the-wilds/|title = Empress of Shares Her Dark New Song 'Broken' from Amazon's 'The Wilds'|date = November 20, 2020}}</ref>

During 2021, Rodriguez was featured on American singer/producer, [[MNDR]]'s single "Love in Reverse" released March 31, 2021, off her second studio album, "Hell To Be You Baby" via [[Peter Wade Keusch|Watersound Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2122732/mndr-love-in-reverse-feat-empress-of/music/|title = MNDR – "Love in Reverse" (Feat. Empress Of)|date = March 31, 2021}}</ref>

On April 30, 2021, Rodriguez announced partnering with an [[Ad Council]]-backed national campaign called "Sound It Out Together". The purpose of the campaign being, to highlight youth [[mental health]] and create an outlet for [[adolescent]] school children of color, to express their emotions and experiences through music.

With assistance from professional songwriters (Rodriguez, [[KAMAUU]], [[Lauren Jauregui]] and [[Tobe Nwigwe]] respectively), the subjects of discussion between the artists and the children featured were turned into fully produced songs and released as compilation EP for the campaign via [[Mass Appeal Records|Mass Appeal]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ourculturemag.com/2021/05/03/empress-of-releases-new-song-one-breath|title = Empress of Releases New Song 'One Breath'|date = May 3, 2021}}</ref>

Rodriguez's involvement included the single, "One Breath", a collaboration w/ a 14-year-old girl named Marianne on their shared experience of both being first-generation [[Latin Americans]] and balancing that duality or [[double consciousness]] through daily life.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1xj6aguVWIU&ab_channel=AdCouncil</ref>

== Personal life ==
Rodriguez is a first-generation Honduran-American.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/10/18/658556429/empress-of-pivots-from-me-to-us|title=Empress Of Pivots From 'Me' To 'Us'|work=NPR.org|access-date=October 31, 2018|language=en}}</ref> She was raised by her mother in the Los Angeles metropolitan area. Lorely's first experiences with music were listening to her father's cassette tapes of [[the Beatles]] and [[Pet Shop Boys]].<ref name="auto"/> Formerly a resident of Brooklyn, NY, much of her debut album, ''Me'' addressed the hardships she faced while living in Brooklyn: "I hate the rats, I hate the rent, I hate all these shitty guys, I hate capitalism. [...] I am getting cat-called in the street, just being a woman walking and I should just be able to walk down the street, you know."

Rodriguez has a degree from [[Berklee College of Music]] in Boston.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://galoremag.com/empress-of-releases-her-debut-record-me-with-a-newfound-confidence/|title=Empress Of Releases Her Debut Record 'Me' With A Newfound Confidence |website=Galoremag.com|date=September 10, 2015|language=en-US|access-date=August 22, 2016}}</ref> She cites [[Julee Cruise]], Elizabeth Fraser of the [[Cocteau Twins]], [[the Zombies]], and [[the Beach Boys]] as some of her musical inspirations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://allstonpudding.com/interview-getting-real-with-empress-of/|title=INTERVIEW: GETTING REAL WITH EMPRESS OF|website=Allstonpudding.com|date=April 10, 2013|language=en-US|access-date=August 22, 2016}}</ref>
