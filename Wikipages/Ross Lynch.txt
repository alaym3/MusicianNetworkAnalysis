'''Ross Shor Lynch''' (born December 29, 1995) is an American singer, songwriter, musician, and actor.<ref name="TVguide.com">{{Cite web |title=Ross Lynch |url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/ross-lynch/bio/3000703739/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210629095013/https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/ross-lynch/bio/3000703739/ |archive-date=2021-06-29 |access-date=2021-06-29 |website=TVGuide.com |language=en}}</ref> He was the lead vocalist of the [[pop rock]] band [[R5 (band)|R5]] and is one half of the band [[the Driver Era]], with his brother, [[Rocky Lynch]]. As an actor, he is known for his debut role as [[Austin & Ally#Main cast|Austin Moon]] on the [[Disney Channel]] original series  ''[[Austin & Ally]]'',<ref name="medianet-lynch">{{Cite web |url=http://www.disneychannelmedianet.com/web/showpage/showpage.aspx?program_id=900089&type=lynch |title=''Austin & Ally'' – Cast – Ross Lynch |work=Disney Channel Medianet |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/63pcvJJ41?url=http://www.disneychannelmedianet.com/web/showpage/showpage.aspx?program_id=900089&type=lynch |archive-date=December 10, 2011 |quote=Born December 29, 1995, in Littleton, Colorado }}</ref> and for his role as Brady in the ''[[Teen Beach Movie]]'' series.

In 2017, Lynch branched into film, starring in the biopic ''[[My Friend Dahmer (film)|My Friend Dahmer]],''<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/ross-lynch-play-young-jeffrey-910214 |title=Ross Lynch to Play Young Jeffrey Dahmer in 'My Friend Dahmer' (Exclusive) |last=Ford |first=Rebecca |date=July 14, 2016 |website=The Hollywood Reporter |access-date=February 5, 2017 |archive-date=March 7, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170307181519/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/ross-lynch-play-young-jeffrey-910214 |url-status=live }}</ref> where he played a teenage [[Jeffrey Dahmer]]. In 2018, Lynch also starred in ''Status Update'', playing the character of Kyle. Between 2018 and 2020, Lynch starred as Harvey Kinkle on the [[Netflix]] television series ''[[Chilling Adventures of Sabrina (TV series)|Chilling Adventures of Sabrina]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://variety.com/2018/tv/news/netflix-chilling-adventures-of-sabrina-series-ross-lynch-harvey-1202726794/ |title=Netflix's 'Sabrina' Series Casts Ross Lynch as Harvey Kinkle |last=Otterson |first=Joe |website=Variety |access-date=March 14, 2018 |archive-date=March 15, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180315021801/http://variety.com/2018/tv/news/netflix-chilling-adventures-of-sabrina-series-ross-lynch-harvey-1202726794/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Early life==
Lynch was born and raised in the [[Denver]] suburb of [[Littleton, Colorado]]. He has four siblings, sister [[Rydel Lynch|Rydel]], and brothers [[Riker Lynch|Riker]], Rocky, and Ryland. He was educated at home starting in the fourth grade when he learned to sing and play the guitar and piano.<ref name="denverpost">{{Cite web |url=http://www.denverpost.com/television/ci_19334824 |title=Ostrow: Ross Lynch of Littleton hopes he'll strike a chord with fans on Disney show |last=Ostrow |first=Joanne |date=November 14, 2011 |work=The Denver Post |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/63gn7gIVv?url=http://www.denverpost.com/television/ci_19334824 |archive-date=December 4, 2011 }}</ref> Lynch is a second cousin of entertainers [[Derek Hough]] and [[Julianne Hough]], their maternal grandmothers being sisters.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://instagram.com/p/nROuFnCf6b/# |title=On my way to the #rdmas to support my cousins @officialR5 good luck tonight! |last=Hough |first=Julianne |date=April 27, 2014 |work=Instagram |access-date=June 19, 2014 |archive-date=June 5, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140605074445/http://instagram.com/p/nROuFnCf6b |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="dancing">{{cite web |last=Bobbin |first=Jay |date=March 16, 2015 |url=http://zap2it.com/2015/03/dancing-with-the-stars-julianne-hough-wants-to-be-truthful-without-tearing-the-dancers-apart/ |title=''Dancing With the Stars''{{'s}} Julianne Hough wants to be truthful without tearing the dancers apart |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161006082722/http://zap2it.com/2015/03/dancing-with-the-stars-julianne-hough-wants-to-be-truthful-without-tearing-the-dancers-apart/ |archive-date=October 6, 2016 |work=Zap2it}}</ref> Lynch and his family moved to Los Angeles in 2007.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.popstaronline.com/25-facts-on-ross-lynch/ |title=25 Facts On Ross Lynch |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140426202249/http://www.popstaronline.com/25-facts-on-ross-lynch/ |archive-date=April 26, 2014 |website=popstaronline.com |date=March 18, 2016}}</ref>

===Television===
Lynch has appeared on the television shows ''[[So You Think You Can Dance]]'' and ''[[Moises Rules!]]'', and in the 2010 short film ''Grapple''.<ref name="billboardbio" /> He was in [[Kidz Bop]], appearing in a 2009 Kidz Bop video.<ref name="NYP-KidzBop">{{Cite news |url=https://nypost.com/2015/01/23/heres-why-kidz-bop-is-becoming-a-star-factory/ |title=Here's why Kidz Bop is becoming a star factory |last=Phull |first=Hardeep |date=January 23, 2015 |work=[[New York Post]] |access-date=February 2, 2016 |archive-date=February 3, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160203080359/http://nypost.com/2015/01/23/heres-why-kidz-bop-is-becoming-a-star-factory/ |url-status=live }}</ref> He was also in [[Cymphonique]]'s music video for "Lil' Miss Swagger".

==Music career==
[[File:Ross Lynch Loud.jpg|thumb|upright=0.7|left|Lynch performing in R5's Loud Tour in 2013]]

He has danced for the Rage Boyz Crew, a group founded by a dance company in [[Southern California]].<ref name="billboardbio">{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/369121/ross-lynch/biography |title=Ross Lynch – Biography |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=February 2, 2016 |archive-date=February 3, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160203080359/http://www.billboard.com/artist/369121/ross-lynch/biography |url-status=live }}</ref>

===With R5===
[[File:R5 2013.jpg|thumb|right|upright=1.1|R5 performing in [[Commerce, California]], in 2013]]
In March 2010, R5 self-released an [[extended play|EP]], ''[[Ready Set Rock]]'' and in April 2012, they signed with [[Hollywood Records]].<ref name="R5HollywoodRecords">{{Cite web |url=http://www.asweetbeat.com/celebrity-news/ross-lynch-signed-a-record-deal-with-r5/ |title=Ross Lynch Signed a Record Deal with R5! |date=April 26, 2012 |website=asweetbeat.com |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130102022219/http://www.asweetbeat.com/celebrity-news/ross-lynch-signed-a-record-deal-with-r5/ |archive-date=January 2, 2013 |access-date=January 12, 2013 }}</ref> Their second EP, ''[[Loud (EP)|Loud]]'', was released on February 19, 2013, which featured the lead single and title track "[[Loud (R5 song)|Loud]]". The band's first full-length album, ''[[Louder (R5 album)|Louder]]'', was released on September 24, 2013, and the album includes the four songs from ''[[Loud (EP)|Loud]]'' as well as seven new songs. The second single from the album, "[[Pass Me By (R5 song)|Pass Me By]]", premiered on [[Radio Disney]] on August 16, 2013. The music video premiered on August 29 on Disney Channel and is available for public viewing on the band's [[Vevo]] channel. The third single, "[[(I Can't) Forget About You]]", was released on December 25, 2013, and reached number 47 on the [[Mainstream Top 40 (Pop Songs)|''Billboard'' Digital Pop Songs]] chart, and the fourth single "[[One Last Dance (R5 song)|One Last Dance]]" was released on May 29, 2014. The third extended play, ''[[Heart Made Up on You]]'', was released on July 22, 2014, and the self-titled single was released on August 1, 2014.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/heart-made-up-on-you-ep/id895159559 |title=Heart Made Up On You – EP by R5 on iTunes |via=iTunes |access-date=April 28, 2016 |archive-date=June 11, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160611111718/https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/heart-made-up-on-you-ep/id895159559 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On November 16, 2014, the band released the first single from second album, "[[Smile (R5 song)|Smile]]". "[[Let's Not Be Alone Tonight]]", the second single, was released on February 13, 2015.<sup>[[R5 (band)|[22]]]</sup> "[[All Night (R5 song)|All Night]]" was released as the third single on June 2, 2015, along with the album's iTunes pre-order.<sup>[[R5 (band)|[23]]]</sup> The band released their second full-length album on July 10, 2015, titled ''[[Sometime Last Night]]'', and debuted at number 6 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], number 1 on the ''Billboard'' Top Pop Albums, number 3 on the ''Billboard'' Top Digital Albums, and number 4 on the ''Billboard'' Top Album Sales.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/sometime-last-night/id996627621 |title=Sometime Last Night by R5 on iTunes |via=iTunes |access-date=April 28, 2016 |archive-date=April 13, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160413124602/https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/sometime-last-night/id996627621 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On May 12, 2017, R5 released their fifth EP ''[[New Addictions]]''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/new-addictions-ep/id1232342771 |title=New Addictions – EP by R5 on Apple Music |website=iTunes |access-date=August 16, 2017 |archive-date=June 2, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170602081705/https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/new-addictions-ep/id1232342771 |url-status=live }}</ref>

On February 1, 2018, R5 posted on their YouTube a video called The Last Show. On March 1, R5's Instagram and Twitter handles were changed to "the Driver Era" and all of R5's previous posts were deleted.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BfzIzrPHV4G |title=The Driver Era Instagram}}</ref> On March 2, it was reported that the Driver Era would be a band consisting only of Ross and Rocky.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8265892/driver-era-r5-billboard-chart-debut |title=The Driver Era, Featuring Ex-Members of R5, Makes Chart Debut With 'Preacher Man' |last=Rutherford |first=Kevin |date=March 28, 2018 |website=Billboard |access-date=April 1, 2018 |archive-date=March 29, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180329043655/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8265892/driver-era-r5-billboard-chart-debut |url-status=live }}</ref>
