'''Greta Simone Kline''' (born March 21, 1994), formerly known by the stage name '''Frankie Cosmos''', is an American musician and singer-songwriter. She is known for her independent releases, inspired by [[Frank O'Hara]]'s poetry, [[Do it yourself|DIY ethics]] of [[K Records]] and the early 2000s New York City's [[anti-folk]] scene.<ref name="rising" /><ref name="all" /> She is the daughter of actors [[Kevin Kline]] and [[Phoebe Cates]]. Her former stage name "Frankie Cosmos" is now the name of her [[Band (rock and pop)|band]].

==Early life==
Greta Kline was born in New York City, the daughter of [[Academy Award]]-winning actor [[Kevin Kline]] and actress [[Phoebe Cates]].<ref name="rising" /> Her father is of [[German-Jewish]] and [[Irish people|Irish]] descent<ref name="parents">{{cite news | author=Dotson Rader | title=Kevin Kline Interview from Parade | work=Parade | url=http://dramafan.tripod.com/kevinkline/paradekk.html | date=October 16, 1994 | access-date=June 7, 2008 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080720173547/http://dramafan.tripod.com/kevinkline/paradekk.html | archive-date=July 20, 2008 | url-status=dead | author-link=Dotson Rader }}</ref> and her mother has [[History of the Jews in Russia|Russian-Jewish]] and [[Chinese Filipino|Chinese-Filipino]] ancestry.<ref>{{cite news |title=Pinoys who made it in Hollywood |first=Boy |last=Villasanta |url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/06/23/10/filipinos-also-shine-hollywood |newspaper=[[ABS-CBN Corporation]] |date=June 23, 2010 |access-date=May 28, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100626172621/http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/06/23/10/filipinos-also-shine-hollywood |archive-date=June 26, 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite book |title=Teen life in Asia |last=Slater |first=Judith J. |year=2004 |publisher=Greenwood Publishing Group |location=Westport, Connecticut |isbn=978-0-313-31532-9 |page=183 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7Oz5cvi3z3EC&q=%22Phoebe%20Cates%22%20Filipino&pg=PA183 |access-date=May 28, 2011 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Cohen |first1=Matthew Isaac |year=2009 |title=British performances of Java, 1811–1822 |journal=South East Asia Research |volume=17 |issue=1 |pages=87–109 |publisher=IP Publishing Ltd |doi=10.5367/000000009787586389 |s2cid=147291754 }}</ref> She has one older brother, Owen Kline.

Kline was introduced to music by her family. She settled on guitar as her primary instrument of choice at seventh grade, after playing the piano and briefly the drums.<ref name="rising">{{cite web | url=http://pitchfork.com/features/rising/9345-frankie-cosmos/ | title=Rising: Frankie Cosmos | publisher=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] | date=March 5, 2014 | access-date=November 15, 2014 | author=Pelly, Jean}}</ref> Home-schooled for most of high school, she spent time by attending underground rock shows in New York and getting involved in the [[Westchester County, New York|Westchester]] music scene.<ref name="rising"/><ref name="vice">{{cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/blog/frankie-cosmos-embody-premiere-video-interview|title=Frankie Cosmos Is Here To Show You Her Own Universe|publisher=[[Vice (magazine)|Noisey Vice]]|access-date=November 15, 2014|author=White, Caitlin}}</ref> Kline  attended [[New York University]]'s [[Gallatin School of Individualized Study]] for two years, and studied English and/or poetry.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newyorker.com/goings-on-about-town/night-life/porches-frankie-cosmos|title=Porches and Frankie Cosmos|website=Newyorker.com|access-date=April 6, 2018}}</ref> Kline found the school to be unaccommodating of her touring schedule, and dropped out.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.spin.com/2016/03/frankie-cosmos-new-album-next-thing-interview/|title=Frankie Cosmos Is Ready to Be Herself|website=Spin.com|access-date=April 6, 2018}}</ref>

Kline and her brother Owen both appeared in the films ''[[The Anniversary Party]]'' (2001) and ''[[The Squid and the Whale]]'' (2005).

==Career==
Kline started performing and recording [[Independent music|independently]] under the alias '''Ingrid Superstar'''. She released a number of [[Lo-fi music|lo-fi]] recordings mainly under her [[Bandcamp]] account. In early 2010s, she was also introduced to the music community of [[Purchase College]], which introduced her to [[Independent record label|independent]] [[Double Double Whammy]] record label.<ref name="rising" /><ref name="all">{{cite web | title=Frankie Cosmos | url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/frankie-cosmos-mn0003230225/biography | publisher=[[Allmusic]] | access-date=November 15, 2014 | author=Thomas, Fred}}</ref> In late 2011, she started to perform under the alias Frankie Cosmos, a name coined by her now ex-boyfriend Aaron Maine of the band [[Porches (band)|Porches]].<ref name="rising" /><ref name="vice" /> Maine played drums for Frankie Cosmos and Kline played bass for Porches at one point in time.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2014/09/porches_frankie.html | title=Porches & Frankie Cosmos playing 2 nights at Baby's All Right for the venue's 1 year anniversary (and other dates) | publisher=[[Brooklyn Vegan]] | access-date=November 15, 2014}}</ref>

In 2014, Kline released her debut studio album as Frankie Cosmos, ''Zentropy'', with her backing band.<ref name="p4k-review">{{cite web | url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/19130-frankie-cosmos-zentropy/ | title=Frankie Cosmos - Zentropy | publisher=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] | date=March 11, 2014 | access-date=November 15, 2014 | author=Powell, Mike}}</ref>

Kline released the EP ''[[Fit Me In]]'' on November 13, 2015 on Bayonet Records.
Kline released her second studio album, ''[[Next Thing (album)|Next Thing]]'', on April 1, 2016 on Bayonet Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/63172-frankie-cosmos-announces-new-album-next-thing-shares-sinister/ |title=Frankie Cosmos Announces New Album Next Thing, Shares "Sinister" |publisher=[[Pitchfork Media]] |first=Jazz |last=Monroe |date=January 27, 2016 |access-date=April 9, 2016}}</ref>

On April 12, 2017, Frankie Cosmos announced a slew of tour dates and that they recently had signed to Sub Pop records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blog.adafruit.com/2018/03/26/musicmakers009-frankie-cosmos-musicmakers-musicmonday-frankiecosmos-subpop/|title=MusicMakers009: Frankie Cosmos|date=March 26, 2018|website=Blog.adafruit.com|access-date=April 6, 2018}}</ref> They stated around this time that they are working on a new album.

On June 9, 2017, Kline opened for IAN SWEET and Girlpool at Warsaw in Brooklyn as part of her new project Lexie. Joined by Warehouse members Alex Bailey and Doug Bleichner, their first release is on Bandcamp and it is called "Record Time!".

In January 2018, Kline released the lead single "Jesse" from the album ''[[Vessel (Frankie Cosmos album)|Vessel]]''. The album was released on March 30, 2018 through Sub Pop records.

On March 13, 2019, Kline released "Haunted Items", featuring just herself on vocals and piano.

The band Frankie Cosmos released their fourth studio album, ''Close It Quietly'' on September 6, 2019.<ref name="ciq">{{cite web |last1=Strauss |first1=Matthew |title=Frankie Cosmos Announce New Album, Share Video for New Song "Windows": Watch |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/frankie-cosmos-announce-new-album-share-video-for-new-song-windows-watch/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=June 18, 2019 |date=June 18, 2019}}</ref>

Two of Kline's songs, "Fool" and "The End", were both showcased during two episodes on the [[Netflix]] drama series [[Hilda (TV series)]].

Throughout the first few months of quarantine, Kline did weekly Instagram livestream performances.
