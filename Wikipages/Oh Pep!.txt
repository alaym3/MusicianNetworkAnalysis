'''Oh Pep!''' are an Australian band formed in [[Melbourne, Australia]] by Olivia Hally (vocals, guitar) and [[Pepita Emmerichs]] (violin, mandolin). Their debut album, ''[[Stadium Cake]],'' was released on 24 June 2016. 

Emmerichs characterised her band's musical style as "[[folk music|folk]]/[[pop music|pop]]/[[country music|country]] with [[bluegrass music|bluegrass]] instrumentation".<ref name="MeltingPot" /> She has cited among the band's influences [[Paul Kelly (Australian musician)|Paul Kelly]], [[Ryan Adams]], [[Gillian Welch]], [[Andrew Bird]], [[Leonard Cohen]], [[Bob Dylan]], [[Johnny Cash]], [[Glen Hansard]], [[Elbow (band)|Elbow]], [[Bright Eyes (band)|Bright Eyes]] and [[Arcade Fire]].<ref name="Lip" /> Christine Campbell of ''Lip Magazine'' called their music "an original mix of different kinds of folk, pop, [[indie music|indie]] and more."<ref name="Lip" /> [[NPR Music|NPR Music's]] [[Bob Boilen]], after seeing Oh Pep! perform at the 2015 [[CMJ Music Marathon]], praised their songs as "charming ... with lyrical and structural twists and turns."<ref name="BradBoilen">[https://www.npr.org/sections/allsongs/2015/10/19/449184189/bob-boilens-top-10-discoveries-from-the-2015-cmj-music-marathon "Bob Boilen's Top 10 Discoveries From The 2015 CMJ Music Marathon"] ''All Songs Considered'', 19 October 2015. Retrieved 22 October 2015</ref>

==Career==
===2009-2015: Formation and early releases===
The duo met at the [[Victorian College of the Arts Secondary School]] in 2009, and began writing songs together.<ref name="Lip">Christine Campbell, [http://lipmag.com/arts/interview-oh-pep/ "Interview: Oh Pep!"] ''Lip Magazine'' (3 December 2012). Retrieved 10 April 2015</ref><ref name="MeltingPot">[http://www.meltingpotonline.com/artist/oh-pep/ "Q&A, with PEPI"] ''The Melting Pot''. Retrieved 10 April 2015</ref> In mid-2011, their musical duo, known as Oh Pep!, expanded into a five-piece lineup with Stuart West (double bass), Paddy Montgomery (mandolin, bouzouki) and Justin Olsson (drums, percussion).<ref name="Lip" /> The band's line-up has changed several times since, with Hally and Emmerichs the constant factor in all line-up configurations.

Pepita Emmerichs was also a child actress, who had appeared in [[Spike Jonze]]'s 2009 film ''[[Where the Wild Things Are (film)|Where the Wild Things Are]]'' as Claire, the protagonist's older sister.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theage.com.au/entertainment/movies/where-the-wild-things-are-20091203-ge87hu.html|title=Where the Wild Things Are|date=3 December 2009|website=The Age|accessdate=26 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/10/16/movies/16where.html|title=Monsters Make Everything Better Spike Jonze’s Film|first=Manohla|last=Dargis|website=Nytimes.com|accessdate=26 October 2018}}</ref>

The band's self-titled debut EP, which was entirely self-produced and funded, was released on 6 September 2012.<ref name="Lip" /><ref name="FirstEP">[http://ohpepmusic.bandcamp.com/album/oh-pep "Oh Pep!"] Oh Pep! official site. Retrieved 10 April 2015</ref> Their second EP, ''II'', was released on 10 October 2013.<ref name="II">[http://ohpepmusic.bandcamp.com/album/ii-2 "II"] Oh Pep! official site. Retrieved 10 April 2015</ref>

In January 2013, Oh Pep! covered [[Paul Simon]]'s ''[[Graceland (album)|Graceland]]'' in its entirety as part of the event "[[RocKwiz]] presents Pure Pop's Summer of Classic Albums".<ref name="Lip" /><ref name="TimeOut">[http://www.au.timeout.com/melbourne/music/events/5094/pure-pops-summer-of-classic-albums "Rockwiz presents Pure Pop's Summer of Classic Albums"] ''TimeOut Melbourne''. Retrieved 10 April 2015</ref> In November 2014, the band performed as a showcase act at the Australasian Worldwide Music Expo.<ref name="Q&A">[http://www.beat.com.au/music/awme-qa-oh-pep "AWME Q&A: Oh Pep!"] ''Beat''. Retrieved 10 April 2015</ref>

Oh Pep! released their third EP, ''Living'', on 21 August 2015, through Star House Collective.<ref>[https://ohpepmusic.bandcamp.com/album/living "Living"] Oh Pep! official site. Retrieved 22 October 2015.</ref> Oh Pep! performed at the 2015 [[Americana Music Festival & Conference]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], where [[Pop Matters]]' listed them as one of the event's "unquestionable standouts",<ref>Annie Galvin, [http://www.popmatters.com/post/10-americana-fest-bands-to-fall-in-love-with-right-now "10 Americana Fest Bands to Fall in Love With Right Now"] ''Pop Matters'', 8 October 2015. Retrieved 27 October 2010</ref> writing that "seeing Oh Pep! is one of those times when the crowd realizes they're seeing future stars for the first time."

Oh Pep! also performed at the 2015 [[CMJ Music Marathon]], where they received praise from [[NPR Music|NPR Music's]] [[Bob Boilen]],<ref name="BradBoilen"/> ''[[The New York Times]]''' [[Jon Pareles]],<ref>[https://twitter.com/JonPareles/status/655771306248511488 Tweet by Jon Pareles] @JonPareles, Twitter.com, 18 October 2015. Retrieved 28 October 2015.</ref> and the [[KCRW|KCRW Music Blog]].<ref>Nassir Nassirzadeh, [https://blogs.kcrw.com/musicnews/2015/10/cmj-music-marathon-2015-11-artists-that-ruled-the-fest/ "CMJ Music Marathon 2015: 11 Artists That Ruled The Fest"] KCRW Music Blog, 20 October 2015. Retrieved 27 October 2015.</ref> In October 2015, Hally and Emmerichs performed as Oh Pep! for [[NPR Music|NPR Music's]] Tiny Desk Concerts series.<ref>[https://www.npr.org/event/music/448983620/oh-pep-tiny-desk-concert "Oh Pep!: Tiny Desk Concert"] [[NPR Music]], 16 October 2015. Retrieved 22 October 2015.</ref> 

===2016-present: ''Stadium Cake'' & ''I Wasn't Only Thinking About You...''===
The band's debut album, ''[[Stadium Cake]],'' was released on 24 June 2016.<ref name=StadiumCake>[https://www.npr.org/sections/allsongs/2016/04/05/473090597/new-mix-weezer-the-jayhawks-colin-stetson-more "New Mix: Weezer, The Jayhawks, Colin Stetson, More"] ''[[All Songs Considered]]'', [[NPR Music]], 5 April 2016. Retrieved 8 April 2016.</ref>{{efn-ua|''Stadium Cake'' was released on [[Dualtone Records]] in North America,<ref name=StadiumCake/> Star House Collective in Europe,<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20170904134612/http://www.starhousecollective.com/index.php/artists/oh-pep "Oh Pep!"] Star House Collective official website. Archived 4 September 2017.</ref> and Barely Dressed Records in Australia and New Zealand.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20170904134614/http://www.barelydressedrecords.com/ohpep Oh Pep!] Barely Dressed Records official website. Archived 4 September 2017.</ref>}}

For ''Stadium Cake'', Oh Pep! sought to explore different musical styles. According to Hally, "We're more aware now of exactly what we want with our sound, and for that matter exploring sounds that we haven't used before. [...] There's more of a pop influence this time, some rock."<ref name="Brag">Adam Norris, [http://www.thebrag.com/music/oh-pep "Oh Pep!"] ''The Brag''. Retrieved 10 April 2015</ref> In their review of the band's debut album, ''[[The Village Voice]]'' described the Oh Pep! sound as "starting off slow and spare — all delicate strumming and hushed vocals — it soon grows into something fast, boisterous, and baroque [...] From the get-go it's clear that ''Stadium Cake'' is a pop album."<ref>Bryan Hood (2016) [https://web.archive.org/web/20170904134807/https://www.villagevoice.com/2016/06/29/oh-pep-aim-for-the-big-leagues-with-an-ambitious-folk-pop-debut/ "Oh Pep! Aim for the Big Leagues With an Ambitious Folk-Pop Debut"] ''The Village Voice'', 29 June 2016. Archived 4 September 2017.</ref>

The band's music featured in the 2017 short film ''Martha The Monster'', directed by Christopher Weekes.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20170904123038/https://www.psfilmfest.org/2017-shortfest/short-films/martha-the-monster ''Martha the Monster'' - Palm Springs International Film Festival] Archived 4 September 2017</ref> "Doctor Doctor" also appeared in season one of the television series ''[[The Bold Type]]''.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20170904123719/http://freeform.go.com/shows/the-bold-type/news/the-bold-type-official-music-guide-season-1 The Bold Type Official Music Guide Season 1] ''Freeform''. Archived 4 September 2017.</ref>
