'''India Ambré Perkins''', known mononymously as '''Ambré''', is an American singer and songwriter. She is currently signed to [[Roc Nation]]. She has released two full-length projects with ''Wanderlust'' (2015) and ''2090's'' (2016). Her debut major label EP, ''Pulp'', was released in November 2019, by Roc Nation. She first gained recognition after collaborating with [[Kehlani]] on a [[Cover version|cover]] of [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s "[[Preach (Drake song)|Preach]]" in 2015. She also co-wrote two tracks ("Changes" and "U") off of [[H.E.R.]]'s [[H.E.R. (album)|2017 self-titled album]], which was nominated for multiple [[Grammy Awards]].

==Early life==
India Ambré Perkins<ref name="Fader">{{cite news | last=Dandridge-Lemco| first=Ben| title =Ambré And Kehlani Stay Off The Grid On "No Service in the Hills"| work =The Fader| date =October 20, 2016 | url =https://www.thefader.com/2016/10/20/ambre-perkins-kehlani-no-service-in-the-hills-premiere| accessdate =October 25, 2019}}</ref> was born and raised in New Orleans, Louisiana.<ref name="Hype">{{cite news | title =Emerging R&B artist Ambré glides through smokey woods in "fubu"| work =The Hype Magazine| date =September 8, 2019 | url =https://www.thehypemagazine.com/2019/09/emerging-rb-artist-ambre-glides-through-smokey-woods-in-fubu/| accessdate =October 25, 2019}}</ref> At age 4, she entered into the [[foster care]] system and went in and out of multiple homes and schools as a child. As a teenager, she joined a church choir and also performed at various talent competitions.<ref name="Sheep">{{cite news | last=Jordan| first=Shea| title =Who Is Ambré Perkins? – A Breakdown of the TSNMI Mob's Second-In-Command| work =Black Sheep HTX| date =August 20, 2015 | url =http://blacksheephtx.com/music/2015/08/ambre-perkins-breakdown-tsnmi/| archiveurl=http://webcache.googleusercontent.com/search?q=cache:cdWd9OVc95gJ:blacksheephtx.com/music/2015/08/ambre-perkins-breakdown-tsnmi/+&cd=6&hl=en&ct=clnk&gl=us&client=firefox-b-1-d| archivedate=September 19, 2019| url-status=dead| accessdate =October 25, 2019}}</ref> At age 17 in 2014, she met producer Erick Bardales and began making music with him.<ref name="AP">{{cite news | title =PREMIERE: New Orleans alt. R&B singer Ambré Perkins drops a timeless feel-good anthem in "Must Be The Fall"| work =Afro Punk| date =August 18, 2017 | url =https://afropunk.com/2017/08/premiere-new-orleans-alt-rb-singer-ambre-perkins-drops-timeless-feel-good-anthem-must-fall/| accessdate =October 25, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==

In April 2015, Ambré released her first full-length mixtape, ''Wanderlust'',<ref name="KMWander">{{cite news | title =Ambré Perkins – wanderlust…| work =Kel & Mel Reviews| date =April 22, 2015 | url =https://kelandmelreviews.com/2015/04/22/ambre-perkins-wanderlust/| accessdate =October 25, 2019}}</ref> which was produced by Erick Bardales.<ref name="AP"/> The following month, she collaborated with Kehlani on a cover of Drake's "Preach".<ref name="BET">{{cite news | title =Hear Kehlani and Ambré Perkins's Cover of Drake's 'Preach'| publisher =BET| date =May 15, 2015 | url =https://www.bet.com/news/music/2015/05/15/kehlani-ambre-perkins-drake-preach-cover.html| accessdate =October 25, 2019}}</ref> This led to her opening for Kehlani on her "[[You Should Be Here (mixtape)|You Should Be Here]]" tour<ref name="AP"/> in the summer of 2015.<ref name="Sheep"/> She also appeared at Kehlani's "Tsunami Christmas" concert in December 2015.<ref name="SFW">{{cite news | last=Schiewe| first=Jesse| title =Kehlani's Tsunami Christmas was a Tsunami of Mishaps and Disappointments| work =SF Weekly| date = December 21, 2015| url =http://www.sfweekly.com/music/kehlanis-tsunami-christmas-was-a-tsunami-of-mishaps-and-disappointments/| accessdate =October 25, 2019}}</ref>
