'''Ntokozo Zakwe''' is a South African Rapper born in [[KwaMashu]], KwaZulu-Natal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/entertainment/2012-02-20-durban-born-star-is-making-waves-in-the-music-industry/&grqid=g35T-SgO&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Durban born star is making waves in the music industry|publisher=sowetanlive.co.za|date=20 February 2012|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Zakwe was born in the township of KwaMashu in KwaZulu-Natal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://radiodut.co.za/theres-no-formula-for-a-hit-its-a-gift-from-god-zakwe/&grqid=j4HXw2eb&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Zakwe theres no formula for a hit its a gift from God|publisher=radiodut.co.za|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iol.co.za/dailynews/news/rapper-zakwe-releases-album-cebisa-to-kick-off-vdj2018-15865998&grqid=cO4ZazC7&s=1&hl=en-ZA|access-date=13 February 2020|title=Rapper zakwe releases album cebisa to kick off vdj2018|publisher=iol.co.za}}</ref> He went to Brettonwood High School where he completed his matric and developed his passion for hip hop music. He then went to further his studies at the Durban University of Technology but due to financial difficulties he dropped out and chose to pursue his dream of becoming a musician.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ikindmedia.com/videos/new-zakwe-music-video-featuring-danger-and-zuluboy-html/&grqid=TZCwccZI&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Zakwe – humble beginnings|publisher=ikindmedia.com|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://radiodut.co.za/its-very-needed-to-represent-where-youre-from-zakwe/&grqid=TZCwccZI&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Its very needed to represent where youre from – Zakwe|publisher=radiodut.co.za|date=25 October 2019|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==
Zakwe started releasing music as an underground artist, at this stage he released a number of vernacular mixtapes which were titled ''Street Music''. The mixtapes were well received in the underground hip hop scene, this then led to two of his tracks within the tapes to eventually make it into Hype Sessions and thus received massive airplay.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nativerhythms.co.za/website/zakwe/&grqid=y0p6ub5l&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Zakwe—Native Rhythms|publisher=nativerhythms.co.za|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

After the success of his mixtapes, [[Zuluboy|Zulu Boy]] another [[durban]] based emcee, featured Zakwe on his track "Zobe Zisho" which led to Zakwe bagging a recording and artist management deal with Native Rhythms.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/53tX0k8tF3LaZYncnfpCtd&grqid=a6tCrs_B&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Zuluboy recruits Zakwe—Spotify|publisher=spotify.com|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtvbase.com/news/south-african-rapper-zakwe-has-left-his-label-heres-why/6qbyhq&grqid=Edvswx9x&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=South African rapper Zakwe has left his label heres why|publisher=mtvbase.com|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

In 2011 he released his self-titled debut album ''Zakwe'' which was a huge success and was certified gold status by the Recording Industry of South Africa (RISA), earning him five nominations at the South African Music Awards, he won the Metro FM Award for best Hip Hop artist and the first best Lyricist Award at the South African Hip Hop Awards (SAHHAs) the following year, thus taking his place amongst the best rappers in South Africa.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/entertainment/2019-08-13-sa-hip-hop-still-moving-millions/&grqid=sQfnkISi&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=SA hip hop still moving millions|publisher=sowetanlive.co.za|date=13 August 2019|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://onenationmusic.com/zakwe-is-dropping-a-new-album-this-year/&grqid=mMKtJSk4&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Lyrically he's one of the best in South African Hip Hop|publisher=onenationmusic.com|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://m.bizcommunity.com/Article/196/40/90152.html&grqid=HlEuqG9r&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Zakwe|publisher=bizcommunity.com|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://justnje.com/zakwe-shares-news-of-his-first-love-song-set-backs/|title=Zakwe shares news of his first love song set backs|publisher=justnje.com|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

Zakwe's success and talent earned him endorsement deals with SAB Miller and Hennessy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bands.co.za/zakwe_bands.htm&grqid=GoJ6_5HS&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Zakwe|publisher=bands.co.za|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref> He was one of the performing acts at the 2011 Metro FM awards in the province of [[Mpumalanga]], he also was one of the live acts in the 2012 and 2013 South African Music Awards.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.southafrica-newyork.net/consulate/Yearbook_2016/SAYB1516.pdf|title=Yearbook—2016|publisher=southafrica-newyork.net|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref> In February 2013 he was the opening act for American rapper [[Kanye West]] at the Dome in johannesburg, South Africa.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pressreader.com/south-africa/the-mercury-south-africa/20130222/282544425712238|title=Three times lucky for rapper Zakwe—Pressreader|publisher=pressreader.com|date=22 February 2013|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

In 2015 he released ''Impande'' an album which became an instant hit and earned him three nominations at the South African Hip Hop Awards for Best Album, Best Male Artist and Best Lyricist, He won the Best Vernacular Hip Hop at the 12th South African Traditional Music Awards (SATMA) for the song "Reverse" featuring Duncan.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/news/south-africa/2015-12-09-hip-hop-women-under-raps/&grqid=aOiqeimD&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Hip hop women under raps|publisher=timeslive.co.za|date=9 December 2015|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://superbalist.com/thewayofus/2017/12/04/mixtape-vol-137/12073&grqid=C4JlOotu&s=1&hl=en-ZA|publisher=superbalist.com|date=4 December 2017|title=Durban Rapper Zakwe wins SATMA|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

Zakwe released a hit single called "Sebentin" featuring Cassper Nyovest and Musiholiq in 2017 which received positive reviews. A remix of the song was released in the same year which featured Blaklez, Musiholiq, Cassper Nyovest, [[Kwesta]], [[Hip Hop Pantsula|HHP]] and PRO. The remixed version of the song won the Remix of the Year at the 25th South African Music Awards.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/universal-music-group-artists-win-big-samas|title=Universal Music Group Artists Win Big at Samas|publisher=musicinafrica.net|access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

On 6 July 2018, Zakwe released his third studio album titled ''Cebisa''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iol.co.za/dailynews/news/rapper-zakwe-releases-album-cebisa-to-kick-off-vdj2018-15865998&grqid=J0FwY6A4&s=1&hl=en-ZA|title=Rapper Zakwe releases album to kick off vdj2018 – Cebisa|date=6 July 2018|access-date=8 March 2020|publisher=iol.co.za}}</ref>

On July 2020, his album ''Cebisa'' was certified gold by the Recording industry of South Africa.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zakwe's Third Studio Album 'Cebisa' Has Finally Been Certified Gold! - SA Hip Hop Mag |url=https://sahiphopmag.co.za/2020/07/zakwes-third-studio-album-cebisa-has-finally-been-certified-gold/amp/|work=SA Hip Hop|date=24 July 2020}}</ref>
On December 4,  2020, Zakwe and Duncan  their  collaboration album titled ''Zakwe & Duncan'' was released.<ref>{{cite web|title=ZakweSA & DuncanSkuva Release Their Joint Album #ZakweAndDuncan|url=https://slikouronlife.co.za/blog/18971/at-zakwesa-at-duncanskuva-release-their-joint-album-zakweandduncan|work=Slikouronlife|first=Nokulunga|last=Maqubela|date=12 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Zakwe and Duncan release self-titled joint album |work= JustNje|date=4 December 2020 |url=https://justnje.com/zakwe-and-duncan-release-self-titled-joint-album/}}</ref>

The album features  [[Kwesta]], Assessa, Just Bheki, Kay Masta, Beast, Supreme, Lilly, Max Ov, [[K.O]], DJ Tira, the Q Twins, and Riot. 
<ref>{{cite web|title=Zakwe & Duncan Drop New #ZakweAndDuncan Joint Album {{!}} Hype Magazine|url= https://hypemagazine.co.za/2020/12/04/zakwe-duncan-drop-new-zakweandduncan-joint-album/|work=Hype Magazine|date=December 4, 2020}}</ref>

Zakwe received a nomination for Artist of the Decade at the 2021 South African Hip Hop Awards.<ref>{{cite web|title=Full list of 2021 SA Hip Hop Awards nominees {{!}} Fakaza News |url=https://fakazanews.com/2021/11/02/full-list-of-2021-sa-hip-hop-awards-nominees/|publisher=Fakaza News|last=Bukola|access-date= November 2, 2021}}</ref>
