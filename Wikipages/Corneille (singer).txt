'''Cornelius Nyungura''' (born 24 March 1977),<ref name="Official North American Corneille Website">{{cite web |url=http://www.corneille.ca |title=Official North American Corneille Website |accessdate=5 March 2009}}</ref> known by his stage name '''Corneille''',  is a Canadian singer and songwriter. He was born in West Germany to [[Rwanda]]n parents, spent most of his childhood in Rwanda, and eventually emigrated to [[Quebec]], Canada in 1997.<ref name="fa">{{cite web|last1=Djenaidi|first1=Oumelkheir|title=Corneille à la conquête du marché américain|url=http://www.france-amerique.com/articles/2008/12/19/print/corneille-je-ne-me-sens-plus-seul-au-monde.html|website=france-amerique.com|publisher=France-Amerique|accessdate=13 August 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924015134/http://www.france-amerique.com/articles/2008/12/19/print/corneille-je-ne-me-sens-plus-seul-au-monde.html|archivedate=24 September 2015}}</ref> He sings in French and English. His work is greatly influenced by American [[funk]] and [[soul music]]; he is inspired by [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[Marvin Gaye]] and [[Stevie Wonder]].

==Biography==
===Early life===
Corneille was born in [[Freiburg im Breisgau]], [[Germany]], although he spent much of his childhood in Rwanda. He discovered his passion for music in 1993, and so joined an [[R&B]] group, who won the Découverte 1993 competition.<ref name="fa"/> This also introduced him to songwriting and [[musical composition]]. However, his life changed after the [[Rwandan genocide]] in 1994. His father, Émile Nyungura, was a leader of the political party [[Social Democratic Party (Rwanda)|PSD]] thought to be opposed to the government and therefore a target; Corneille witnessed the assassination of his parents and his three brothers and sisters when he was 17 years old.<ref>{{cite web|title=Corneille a très envie de renouer avec le Rwanda|url=http://www.franceinfo.fr/emission/tout-et-son-contraire/2013-2014/corneille-tres-envie-de-renouer-avec-le-rwanda-11-18-2013-07-50|website=franceinfo.fr|publisher=Radio France|accessdate=13 August 2015}}</ref> He fled to Germany, where some of his parents' friends took him in.<ref>{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p648209|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography: Corneille|last=Ankeny|first=Jason|publisher=Allmusic|accessdate=4 May 2010}}</ref>

===Career===
In July 1997, Corneille decided to leave Germany in order to pursue studies in communication, and moved to [[Montreal]], Quebec, Canada to attend [[Concordia University (Quebec)|Concordia University]]. During this time, he founded '''O.N.E.''', an R&B band, with [[Pierre Gage]] and Gardy Martin, whom he had met shortly after arriving in Quebec.<ref name="fa"/> ''Zoukin'', one of the group's songs written by Corneille, rose to first place in Canadian [[airplay (song)|airplay]]. Then, at the beginning of 2001, Corneille left the group, wishing to pursue a solo career. He began to centre his songs on his past.<ref name="fa"/>

During 2002, Corneille worked on various projects. He wrote and composed the songs "Ce soir" for the ''Cocktail R&B 2'' compilation (Ghetto R&B, [[Sony Music]]) and "Si seulement on s'aimait" for ''Hip Hop Folies'' (Sony Music). Invited by [[David A. Stewart|Dave Stewart]], he performed at his first [[Variety show|showcase]] at the Réservoir in Paris, and had the chance to sing with [[Jimmy Cliff]]. In January 2003, he was invited to perform as an opening act for [[Cunnie Williams]] at the [[Paris Olympia|Olympia]].<ref name=rfi/>

Corneille's first album, the Canadian version of ''Parce qu'on vient de loin'', was released on 10 September 2002. The French version, which included numerous bonuses on the second disc, was released on 20 October 2003, and quickly became successful.<ref name=rfi>{{cite web|title=Corneille|url=http://www.rfimusique.com/artiste/chanson/corneille/biographie|website=rfimusique.com|publisher=Radio France Internationale|accessdate=13 August 2015}}</ref> In April 2003, Corneille sang with [[Craig David]], repeating a verse in French in the song ''Rise and Fall''. He undertook his first tour during May 2003, playing to full houses in Paris, [[Marseille]], [[Lyon]] and [[Bordeaux]]. In March 2005, the singer participated in the concert ''Africa Live'', which was organised to fight against (malaria) [[malaria|paludism]].<ref name=rfi/> This was the first time that he had returned to his home continent since the genocide. ''Les marchands de rêves'', Corneille's second album, was released in November 2005.<ref name=rfi/>

Corneille has worked with the [[Canadian Red Cross]] to raise awareness of children who are victims of war.<ref name=rfi/> He released his first English-language album, ''The Birth of Cornelius'', in July 2007.<ref name="fa"/> In late 2014 he became a contestant on the [[Danse avec les stars (France season 5)|fifth season]] of the French television channel [[TF1]]'s ''[[Danse avec les stars|Danse avec les Stars]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Danse avec les Stars 5 : Corneille est éliminé, revoir sa dernière danse|url=http://www.tf1.fr/tf1/danse-avec-les-stars/news/danse-stars-5-corneille-elimine-revoir-derniere-danse-video-4419719.html|website=tf1.fr|publisher=TF1|accessdate=13 August 2015}}</ref>

===Eurovision===
Corneille wrote the song "[[Il était temps]]" for [[Virginie Pouchain]] after he qualified to represent [[France]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006]]. The song finished 22nd out of 24 finalists.<ref>{{cite web|title=Eurofans 2012 : Virginie Pouchain au meeting de printemps|url=http://www.eurovision-fr.net/news/lire.php?id=1682|website=eurovision-fr.net|publisher=Eurovision-fr.net|accessdate=13 August 2015}}</ref>

===Personal life===
Corneille married Canadian model and actress Sofia de Medeiros in 2006; they have two children.<ref>{{cite web|title=Corneille et sa femme Sofia de Medeiros attendent leur premier enfant ! Cette fois, c'est officiel !|url=http://www.purepeople.com/article/corneille-et-sa-femme-sofia-de-medeiros-attendent-leur-premier-enfant-cette-fois-c-est-officiel_a39009/1|website=purepeople.com|publisher=Pure People/Webedia|accessdate=13 August 2015}}</ref>
