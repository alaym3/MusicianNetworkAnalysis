'''Jackman Thomas Harlow''' (born March 13, 1998) is an American rapper and songwriter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/02/05/jack-harlow-stays-true-to-his-roots|title=Jack Harlow Stays True to His Roots|work=The Fader|date=February 5, 2019|access-date=March 23, 2020}}</ref> He is signed to [[Don Cannon]] and [[DJ Drama]]'s record label Generation Now, an imprint of [[Atlantic Records]]. He is also the co-founder of his own musical collective, Private Garden. His first major breakthrough came with the release of his 2020 single "[[Whats Poppin]]", which peaked at number two on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His first studio album, ''[[Thats What They All Say]]'', was released on December 11, 2020, and debuted at number 5 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. In 2021, he was a guest on [[Lil Nas X]]’s "[[Industry Baby]]". It reached #1 on the Hot 100, becoming Harlow’s highest charting song overall and his first chart topper.

== Early life ==
Harlow was born on March 13, 1998, in [[Louisville, Kentucky]].<ref name="LouisMain">{{cite news |last=Moss |first=Josh |title=Kid with a Mic |work=Louisville |date=August 4, 2017 |url=https://www.louisville.com/content/kid-mic |access-date=December 13, 2018}}</ref><ref name="XXLBreak" /><ref name="BBOct">{{cite news |last=Elibert |first=Mark |title=Jack Harlow Talks 'Loose' Mixtape, Putting Louisville on the Map & Working With DJ Moose, Moose Cannon |publisher =Billboard |date=October 10, 2018 |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8478971/jack-harlow-loose-mixtape-dj-drama-interview |access-date=December 13, 2018}}</ref> He is the son of Maggie Harlow (née Payette), a businesswoman, and Brian Harlow, and was raised on a horse farm.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vman.com/article/jack-harlows-cinematic-approach-to-hip-hop/|title = Jack Harlow's Cinematic Approach to Hip-Hop}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nawbokentucky.org/news.php?printit=1&id=35|title = NAWBO Kentucky - Newsletter}}</ref> He is of French and Irish descent.<ref>{{cite web |date=November 25, 2020 |title=Jack Harlow Goes Undercover on Twitter, Instagram, and Wikipedia.|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G7XQyDOG69Q|url-status=live|access-date=March 4, 2021|website=GQ}}</ref> Harlow moved from Shelbyville to [[Louisville]] with his family as a child and began rapping at age 12. He and his friend Copeland used a ''[[Guitar Hero]]'' microphone and a laptop to record rhymes and songs. They made a CD entitled ''Rippin' and Rappin{{'}}'' and sold copies at their school, [[Highland Middle School (Louisville, Kentucky)|Highland Middle School]]. When he was in seventh grade, Harlow acquired a professional microphone and made his first mixtape, ''Extra Credit'', using the moniker "Mr. Harlow." He formed a collective called "Moose Gang" with various friends; during this time, he worked on two mixtapes that were ultimately never released, ''Moose Gang'' and ''Music for the Deaf''.

== Artistry ==

=== Style ===
Harlow's craft has been characterized by critics as blending playful confidence with emotional sincerity in both his music and lyrics,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Lyrical Lemonade|url=https://lyricallemonade.com/p/jack-harlows-confetti-validates-hes-a-jack-of-all-trades|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=lyricallemonade.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=November 7, 2019|title=Jack Harlow Is Here to Make Ear Candy|url=https://www.papermag.com/jack-harlow-confetti-2641248214.html?rebelltitem=12#rebelltitem12?rebelltitem=12|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=PAPER|language=en}}</ref> which often explore themes such as sexuality, partying, and drug use.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Chesman|first=Donna-Claire|title=Jack Harlow Is Moving Towards Feeling: Interview|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2020-03-13-jack-harlow-sweet-action-interview-new-music|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=DJBooth|language=en}}</ref> Kate Hutchinson of ''[[The Guardian]]'' opined that Harlow's most interesting songs are his more introspective ones, dealing with topics such as being uncomfortable with his acclaim, and his [[white privilege]].<ref name=Hutchinson2020>{{cite web|last=Hutchinson|first=Kate|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/dec/20/jack-harlow-thats-what-they-all-say-review-debut|title=Jack Harlow: That's What They All Say review – breezy confessionals|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=December 20, 2020|access-date=February 6, 2021}}</ref> Thomas Hobbs, another writer for ''The Guardian'', wrote that Harlow embraces "being a dorky outsider", while additionally having "enough charisma" to pull off "using language that is usually cringeworthy coming from suburban white people".<ref>{{cite web|last=Hobbs|first=Thomas|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/dec/30/audiences-dont-want-white-anger-how-white-rap-grew-a-conscience|title='Audiences don't want white anger': how white rap grew a conscience|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=December 30, 2020|access-date=February 6, 2021}}</ref> He has described his own music as emphasizing rhythm, and his lyricism as being "personal but fun",<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jack Harlow Releases 'Confetti' Mixtape Featuring Bryson Tiller: Listen|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8530638/jack-harlow-confetti-mixtape|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> and geared toward "connect[ing] with people".<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://warnermusicgroup.app.box.com/s/4ur16rf6w4m428xcd5ds4y43rxvya4ho|title=Box|website=warnermusicgroup.app.box.com}}</ref>

Harlow's music has been categorized broadly as rap and R&B. Publications have further tagged his music as blending [[trap music|trap]]<ref>{{cite web|last=Hobbs|first=Thomas|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/51492/1/jack-harlow-thats-what-they-all-say-2020-interview|title=Jack Harlow has the world at his feet|work=[[Dazed]]|date=December 23, 2020|access-date=February 6, 2021}}</ref> with instruments such as pianos and [[uilleann pipes]].<ref name=Hutchinson2020 /><ref>{{cite web|last=Blake|first=Emily|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/jack-harlow-pre-add-chart-1104175/|title= Jack Harlow Rises to Number One on Apple Music Pre-Add Chart |work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=December 15, 2020|access-date=February 6, 2021}}</ref>

Harlow has stated that, being white, he "can never truly know what [the experience of racism] is like", and that it is his responsibility as a white rapper to approach his work with that knowledge in mind.<ref>{{Cite web|date=September 16, 2020|first=Aleia|last=Woods|title=2020 XXL Freshman Class Discuss Police Brutality, Racism and Social Justice With Until Freedom: Part 1 – The Past|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/2020-xxl-freshman-roundtable-interview-until-freedom-the-past/|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref> Harlow has also described his position as a white rapper as like being "the guest inside a house of a culture that isn't mine".

=== Influences ===
Harlow cites a range of influences across multiple genres, including [[Eminem]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Lavin|first=Will|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/eminem-praises-jack-harlow-during-special-phone-call-3045734|title=Eminem praises Jack Harlow during "special" phone call|work=[[NME]]|date=September 14, 2021|accessdate=October 6, 2021}}</ref> [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Lil Wayne]], [[Outkast]], [[Paul Wall]], [[Willie Nelson]], [[Johnny Cash]], [[Hall & Oates|Hall and Oates]], and [[Jesse McCartney]], among others.<ref>{{Cite web|date=April 18, 2020|title=Jack Harlow's 'Great Stories Last Forever' Playlist|url=https://www.spin.com/2020/04/jack-harlow-playlist/|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=Spin}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=August 11, 2020|title=Jack Harlow – 2020 XXL Freshman|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/jack-harlow/|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Q&A {{!}} Jack Harlow|url=https://flaunt.com/content/jack-harlow|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=Flaunt Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref> Harlow has also identified cinema as an influence, aiming for his songs to be "like short films". His favorite filmmakers are [[Martin Scorsese]], [[Quentin Tarantino]], and [[Alfred Hitchcock]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jack Harlow's Cinematic Approach to Hip-Hop|url=https://vman.com/article/jack-harlows-cinematic-approach-to-hip-hop/|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=Vman}}</ref>

==Philanthropy and activism==
Harlow is an open supporter of the [[Black Lives Matter]] political movement, having attended a rally to protest the [[Shooting of Breonna Taylor|killing of Breonna Taylor]], which occurred in his hometown.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=July 15, 2020|title=Jack Harlow Is Going To Be A Star, Whether Or Not He Ever Becomes A Great Rapper|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2091477/jack-harlow-is-going-to-be-a-star-whether-or-not-he-ever-becomes-a-great-rapper/franchises/columns/status-aint-hood/|access-date=November 9, 2020|website=Stereogum}}</ref>

In October 2021, Harlow donated to five Louisville-based organizations: AMPED, the Center for Women and Families, the Grace M. James Academy of Excellence, Louisville Urban League and [[Metro United Way]].<ref name=Lee2021>{{cite web|last=Lee|first=Cydney|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9640041/jack-harlow-donations-louisville-charities/|title=Jack Harlow Announces Donations to Local Louisville Organizations: 'People Need Hope, They Need Love'|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=October 4, 2021|accessdate=October 6, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wlky.com/article/louisville-rapper-jack-harlow-donating-to-5-hometown-organizations-people-need-hope/37872559|title=Louisville rapper Jack Harlow donating to 5 hometown organizations: 'People need hope'|publisher=[[WLKY]]|date=October 5, 2021|accessdate=October 6, 2021}}</ref> Citing the spirit and pride he holds for his hometown, Harlow stated that "People need hope, they need love," when announcing his donation plans.<ref name=Lee2021/> [[KFC]] and [[Instagram]] pledged to match up to $50,000 of what Harlow raises through the social media platform's fundraising tool.<ref name=Lee2021/>
