'''Neo Jessica Joshua''' (born 20 December 1987), better known as '''Nao''' ({{IPAc-en|Àà|n|e…™|o ä}}<ref name="npr"/>), is an English singer-songwriter and record producer from [[East London]].<ref name=BBCSO>{{cite web|title=BBC Music Sound of...2016 ‚Äì NAO|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/21tBzg4nhXckl2qvjNj5gfg/nao|publisher=BBC|access-date=30 November 2015}}</ref><ref name="itunes"/> Her sound has been described as [[soul music|soul]] combined with [[electronic music]], [[funk]] and [[R&B]].<ref name=BBCSO/> Nao coined the term "[[Wonky (music)|wonky]] [[funk]]" to describe her style.<ref>{{cite web|title=The sound of Nao: from teenage choir teacher to creator of 'wonky funk'|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/jan/21/nao-bbc-sound-of-2016-interview|work=[[The Guardian]]|first=Tshepo|last=Mokoena|date=21 January 2016|access-date=28 July 2017}}</ref><ref name="ukmc">{{cite web|title=British Singer NAO brings 'Wonky Funk' to life|url=http://info.umkc.edu/womenc/2017/04/12/british-singer-nao-brings-wonky-funk-to-life|date=12 April 2017|access-date=28 July 2017|work=[[University of Missouri‚ÄìKansas City]]}}</ref> She released her debut album, ''[[For All We Know (Nao album)|For All We Know]]'', in 2016, her second album, ''[[Saturn (album)|Saturn]]'', in 2018, followed by her most recent album, ''And Then Life Was Beautiful'', released in 2021.

==Background and early life==
Nao was the youngest of five children in her Jamaican mother's house, but is the only child of her mother and father, who raised her ‚Äúas friends‚Äù between London and Nottingham, where he lives. Her single ‚ÄúAnother Lifetime‚Äù was dedicated to her mother. 

She studied vocal jazz at the [[Guildhall School of Music & Drama]] in London and went on to be a backing singer for artists such as [[Kwabs]] and [[Jarvis Cocker]].<ref name="BBCSO"/><ref name="BBCGlasto">{{cite web|title=Glastonbury 2015|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/e84mxj/acts/ajcwxj#p02vtv68|publisher=BBC Music|access-date=30 November 2015}}</ref> For six years up to 2014 she sang in an all-girl [[a cappella]] group called The Boxettes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theartsdesk.com/new-music/boxettes-kings-place|title=The Boxettes, Kings Place|website=The Arts Desk|date=13 January 2012}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/08/15/beatbox-group-rudimental-cover-boxettes_n_5682411.html|title=This All-Female Beatbox Group's Awesome Rudimental Cover Is Anything But Basic|date=15 August 2014|work=HuffPost}}</ref>

==Career==
In October 2014 she released her first EP, ''So Good'', it reached number 4 on the [[iTunes]] electronic chart and the title track made the [[BBC Radio 1]] and [[BBC 1Xtra]] national playlists in the United Kingdom.<ref name=BBCSO/><ref name="itunes">{{cite web|title=NAO on iTunes|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/artist/nao/id935698752#fullText|website=Apple iTunes|access-date=30 November 2015}}</ref><ref name="Guardian"/> This was followed by a second EP, ''15 February'' (''II MMXV''), in May 2015,<ref name="Guardian">{{cite web|title=New band of the week: Nao|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/aug/28/new-band-of-the-week-nao-no-68-february-15-soul|website=The Guardian|date=28 August 2015|access-date=30 November 2015}}</ref> receiving the "Best New Music" tag from ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]''. Nao made her hit ‚ÄúAnother Lifetime‚Äù for her mother.<ref name="BBCGlasto"/> In September 2015 Nao was named as a nominee for best newcomer at the 2015 [[MOBO Awards]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Grime and hip-hop artists JME and Krept And Konan dominate Mobo nominations|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/article/34400939/grime-and-hip-hop-artists-jme-and-krept-and-konan-dominate-mobo-nominations|work=BBC Newsbeat|date=30 September 2015|access-date=30 November 2015}}</ref> Nao was a featured vocalist and writer on [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]]'s UK number 1 album, ''[[Caracal (album)|Caracal]]'', on the track "Superego".<ref>{{cite web|title=Caracal by Disclosure|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/caracal/disclosure/details|website=Metacritic|access-date=30 November 2015}}</ref> She was featured in the long list for the [[BBC]] [[Sound of...]] 2016 prize in November 2015, ultimately being named in third place in January 2016.<ref name="SO16">{{cite web|title=BBC Music Sound of 2016 longlist ‚Äì listen to the nominees|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/nov/30/bbc-sound-of-2016-longlist-listen-nominees|website=The Guardian|date=30 November 2015|access-date=30 November 2015}}</ref> Nao also featured as a co-writer on the track "Velvet / Jenny Francis (Interlude)" on [[Stormzy]]'s debut album ''[[Gang Signs & Prayer]]''.

Her debut album, ''[[For All We Know (Nao album)|For All We Know]]'', was released on 29 July 2016, leading to a [[Brit Award]] Nomination for Best British Female Solo Artist.<ref name="ukmc"/>

In 2017, she released a buzz single, entitled "Nostalgia", as she tells fans that she was back in the studio recording material for her second album. During that time, she also appeared as a co-writer in [[Mura Masa]]'s [[Mura Masa (album)|self-titled debut album]] on the song "Nothing Else!", featuring [[Jamie Lidell]].

In mid-2018, she revealed that her next single was to be called "Another Lifetime" and co-written and produced by debut album affiliates [[Grades (producer)|Grades]] and [[Stint (producer)|Stint]]. It was eventually released in June 2018. She also performed live with [[Chic (band)|Chic]] and [[Mura Masa]] on the song "Boogie All Night", which is included on Chic's 2018 comeback album ''[[It's About Time (Chic album)|It's About Time]]''; she is a featured artist on the song alongside Mura Masa. She released her second album ''[[Saturn (album)|Saturn]]'' on 26 October 2018. ''Saturn'' respectively received nominations for Album of the Year at the [[Mercury Prize]] in 2019 and [[Best Urban Contemporary Album]] at the [[62nd Grammy Awards]] in 2020.

On 29 July 2021, Nao revealed details of her third album, ''And Then Life Was Beautiful'', which was released on September 24, 2021.<ref>{{cite news|date=29 July 2021|title=Nao Announces New Album And Then Life Was Beautiful, Shares Song|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/nao-announces-new-album-and-then-life-was-beautiful-shares-song-listen/|last=Monroe|first=Jazz|access-date=26 August 2021|website=Pitchfork|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Personal life==
On 15 January 2020, Nao announced via Instagram that she was expecting her first child.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Singer NAO Is Pregnant! Here's Four Reasons She'll Be The Coolest Mom Ever|url=https://essence.com/love/singer-nao-announces-pregnancy-instagram/|access-date=2021-08-26|website=Essence|language=en-US}}</ref> She gave birth to a girl on 11 June 2020.<ref>{{cite tweet|number=1271116658766221312|user=thisNAO|title=Welcome to Planet Earth baby girl üíõ I...|date=11 June 2020}}</ref> In 2021, Nao announced that she would not be touring for her third album because of her diagnosis of [[chronic fatigue syndrome]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-09-22|title=Nao interview: ‚ÄòI have Chronic Fatigue Syndrome ‚Äì I won‚Äôt tour this album‚Äô|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/nao-interview-chronic-fatigue-syndrome-b1923914.html|access-date=2021-11-11|website=The Independent|language=en}}</ref>
