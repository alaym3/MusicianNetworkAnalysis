'''Efterklang''' ({{IPA-da|ˈeftɐˌkʰlɑŋˀ}}) is a [[Denmark|Danish]] [[indie rock]] group from [[Copenhagen]], formed in December 2000. The band has recorded five studio albums and are currently signed to the [[4AD]] label, as well as their own record label Rumraket.

In 2012, they released their fourth album ''[[Piramida (album)|Piramida]]'' to good reviews.<ref name=mid/><ref name=pira/><ref name=fact/>

==History==
===2001–2011===
The name ''Efterklang'' comes from the [[Danish language|Danish]] word for "remembrance" or "reverberation." Formed in [[Copenhagen]], its three core members are Casper Clausen, Mads Brauer and Rasmus Stolberg.<ref name="Info">{{Cite web |url=http://efterklang.net/home/info/ |title=Efterklang >> Info |access-date=17 February 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110530102406/http://efterklang.net/home/info/ |archive-date=30 May 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The original lineup also included Rune Mølgaard, but he has taken a more secluded role since 2007.<ref name="Info"/> Drummer Thomas Husmer left the band 2011.<ref name="Info"/> When performing live, the core three-piece band is complemented with the addition of live band members.

Efterklang released their first album ''[[Tripper (Efterklang album)|Tripper]]'' to warm critical acclaim in autumn 2004.<ref>Keith Kawaii. ''themilkfactory.co.uk''. October 2004. Retrieved 15-9-12</ref> Their second album ''[[Parades (Efterklang album)|Parades]]'' saw the band working with British producer [[Darren Allison]] ([[Spiritualized]]),<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://efterklang.net/home/discography/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=12 March 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130111105258/http://efterklang.net/home/discography/ |archive-date=11 January 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and was released in October 2007, receiving widespread critical acclaim by the likes of ''[[Mojo (magazine)|Mojo]]'',<ref>"''Parades''-review". Mojo. Novembre 2007. p.106. "Every so often an album comes along that's so original it's difficult to accurately liken it to anything else—even Efterklang's last album, "Tripper," is left behind by Parades."</ref> ''[[Uncut (magazine)|Uncut]]'',<ref>"''Parades''-review". ''[[Uncut (magazine)|Uncut]]''. November 2007. p.98. "More organic than their 2004 debut, Parades is just as richly rewarding".</ref> ''[[Drowned in Sound]]''<ref>Mike Diver [http://drownedinsound.com/releases/11167/reviews/2470983?search "''Parades''-review" ] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140313031748/http://drownedinsound.com/releases/11167/reviews/2470983?search |date=13 March 2014 }}. ''Drowned in Sound''. Retrieved 15-9-12</ref> and ''[[Stylus magazine]]''.<ref>[http://www.stylusmagazine.com/reviews/efterklang/parades.htm "''Parades'' – review"] ''Stylusmagazine''. Retrieved 15-9-12</ref> ''Parades'' was named the 38th best album released between April 2005 to April 2009 by ''[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]'' in April 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/feature/clash-essential-50-38-35|title=Clash Essential 50 – 38-35|website=Clashmusic.com|access-date=20 September 2019}}</ref> In September 2008, Efterklang and [[Danish National Symphony Orchestra|The Danish National Chamber Orchestra]] performed ''Parades'' in its entirety at the [[Copenhagen Concert Hall]]; the performance was documented on a limited edition LP/DVD set entitled ''Performing Parades'', which was released in October 2009.

In September 2009, Efterklang signed a new record deal with the British independent label 4AD. Their third full-length album ''[[Magic Chairs]]'' was Efterklang's first on their new label, released in February 2010.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.4ad.com/releases/magic-chairs-0 |title=Archived copy |access-date=20 September 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190920111420/https://www.4ad.com/releases/magic-chairs-0 |archive-date=20 September 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> ''Magic Chairs'' won IMPALA's European Independent Album of the Year on 28 February.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.impalamusic.org/docum/04-press/2009/PR%20-%2020110228.htm |title=color |publisher=Impalamusic.org |date=2011-02-28 |access-date=2012-04-03}}</ref> The record was well received by ''[[Pitchforkmedia]]''.

In August 2010, French filmmaker [[Vincent Moon]] and Efterklang's 8-piece live band met up on an island off the Danish coast. The objective was to shoot a film with the same length as an album, and a film full of performances, experiments and collaborations. The result was called ''An Island''.

In 2011, drummer and trumpet player Thomas Husmer left the band and the other members announced that they would carry on as a trio.<ref>[http://efterklang.net/home/news/page/2/ "Thomas leaves Efterklang"] ''Efterklang.net''. Retrieved 15-09-12</ref>

===''Piramida'' (2012)===
In 2012, the band went on tour to present their fourth album ''[[Piramida (album)|Piramida]]'' with an orchestra and drummer [[Budgie (drummer)|Budgie]] of [[Siouxsie and the Banshees]] as special guest for the live shows. The premiere at the Sydney's "Opera House" in June was praised by ''[[Time Out (magazine)|Time Out]]''<ref>[http://www.au.timeout.com/sydney/music/features/10774/efterklang Efterklang The Danes make an art-music splash with the SSO] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120603203435/http://www.au.timeout.com/sydney/music/features/10774/efterklang |date=3 June 2012 }}. ''Time Out''. Retrieved 27 June 2012</ref> and ''[[Mojo (magazine)|Mojo]]''.<ref name=mojo>[http://efterklang.net/home/2012/08/06/updated-piramida-tour-plan-incl-mojo-approval/ "Mojo Approval"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120822112859/http://efterklang.net/home/2012/08/06/updated-piramida-tour-plan-incl-mojo-approval/ |date=22 August 2012 }}. ''Efterklang.net''. Retrieved 27 June 2012</ref> The latter wrote : "Piramida is the sound of Efterklang’s grasp meeting their reach, of their ambition evading the pretentious, the blankly grandiose, and rewarding them with a masterpiece.".<ref name=mojo/> A European leg coincided with the release of ''Piramida''.<ref name=dates>[http://efterklang.net/home/concerts/ Efterklang tour dates] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120910005454/http://efterklang.net/home/concerts/ |date=10 September 2012 }}. ''Efterklang.net''. Retrieved 27 June 2012</ref> The band performed with an orchestra and Budgie in Ireland, Scandinavia, UK, the Netherlands and Belgium.<ref name=dates/>

At its release in September, their fourth album ''Piramida'' received good reviews. ''Mojo'' rated it four stars and said: "Despite the elegant grey-sky thinking, deep beneath the emotional permafrost, Piramida isn't as cold as it seems".<ref name=pira>"''Piramida'' -review". ''Mojo''. October 2012, p.86</ref> ''[[BBC|BBC Music]]'' said : "This see-saw, between exquisite gloom and bruised hope, is part of what makes Piramida so powerful" before concluding by these words, "rarely have the Serious Young Man Blues been articulated with such grace, so affectingly".<ref name=mid>Stevie Chick. [https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/reviews/cfrj "Piramida -review"]. ''bbc.co.uk''. 2012-09-11. Retrieved 2012-09-15</ref> British ''[[FACT (United Kingdom magazine)|Fact]]'' magazine wrote: "Piramida is an abandoned mining town located deep within the Arctic Circle. It’s the setting for Efterklang’s fourth and finest album, an acutely musicianly affair employing lorry loads of classical instruments, brass, synths and what appears to be a choir of thousands broadcasting from the deep end of a fjord".<ref name=fact>Christian F. [http://www.factmag.com/2012/09/19/piramida/ "Piramida – review"] ''factmag.com''. Retrieved 2012-09-15</ref> ''[[Q (magazine)|Q magazine]]'' commented: "here’s a band on top of the world, and on top of their game… ''Piramida'' is a revelation."<ref>[http://thesagegateshead.org/event/ns-efterklang-23-10-12/ "''Piramida'' Q magazine review"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120918045222/http://thesagegateshead.org/event/ns-efterklang-23-10-12 |date=18 September 2012 }}. ''Thesagegateshead.org''. Retrieved 2012-09-15</ref>

From December Efterklang went on tour with their new 6-piece band. In 2013, the ''Piramida'' tour took the band around North America and Europe.<ref>[http://efterklang.net/home/concerts/ Tour] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120910005454/http://efterklang.net/home/concerts/ |date=10 September 2012 }}. ===''Leaf Label Re-issues'' (2015)===
In 2015, [[The Leaf Label]] released a number of re-issues as part of its 20th anniversary celebrations. A poll enabled fans to vote for which albums from the label's back catalogue they wished to see re-issued,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theleaflabel.com/en/news/view/454/Tony%20Morley|title=The Leaf Label: 20th Anniversary Reissues |publisher=Theleaflabel.com|access-date= 28 October 2015}}</ref> and Efterklang's ''[[Parades (Efterklang album)|Parades]]'' was selected. The album, released on double vinyl LP, was initially available to fans through the [[PledgeMusic]] service and was introduced to shops in early 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theleaflabel.com/en/news/view/490/DM|title=The Leaf Label: Leaf 20 |publisher=Theleaflabel.com|access-date= 28 October 2015}}</ref>
A double vinyl re-issue of ''[[Tripper (Efterklang album)|Tripper]]'' is also available from [[The Leaf Label]] after a successful [[Ninja Tune|BeatDelete]] campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theleaflabel.com/en/news/view/484/DM|title=The Leaf Label: Efterklang - Tripper Reissues |publisher=Theleaflabel.com|access-date= 28 October 2015}}</ref>

===Liima===
In 2016, the members of Efterklang, along with drummer Tatu Rönkkö, formed the band Liima. They released their debut album, ''ii'', on 18 March 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://4ad.com/|title=The official website for independent record label 4AD.|website=4ad.com|access-date=20 September 2019}}</ref> The album received positive reviews, earning a score of 70 on Metacritic, based on 8 reviews.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/ii/liima|title=ii by Liima|publisher=Metacritic|access-date=20 September 2019}}</ref>

Liima's second album, ''1982'', was released on 3 November 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cityslang.com/releases/liima-1982|title=Liima - 1982|first=Truth &|last=Co|date=20 September 2019|website=Cityslang.com|access-date=20 September 2019}}</ref>

==Rumraket==
Efterklang has released albums on the British/American record label [[The Leaf Label]], but have also released material on their own record label Rumraket (the Danish word for "space rocket"). So far, the Rumraket roster includes releases from [[Grizzly Bear (band)|Grizzly Bear]], [[Amiina]], Cacoy, Erik Levander, Kama Aina, [[Slaraffenland]], Taxi Taxi!, and Canon Blue.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/label/55893-Rumraket|title=Rumraket|publisher=Discogs|access-date=20 September 2019}}</ref>

Efterklang has a history of working with many guest musicians on their recordings. They have also produced a vast number of artistic music videos by filmmakers such as Karim Ghahwagi, Tobias Stretch, [[Anders Morgenthaler]], Carolina Melis, and Jeremiah Zagar. Nan Na Hvass from Hvass&Hannibal is the creator of the critically acclaimed artwork for Efterklang's ''One-Sided LP'', ''Under Giant Trees'', ''Parades'', and the "Mirador" music video.
