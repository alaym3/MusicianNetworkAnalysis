'''''NorthernSound''''' is the second [[compilation album]] by [[6ixBuzz#6ixBuzz Entertainment|6ixBuzz]]. It was [[Independent music|self-released]] on December 13, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/21NNXDB7eznCt2lMAEQBKQ|title=NorthernSound|date=13 December 2019|via=open.spotify.com}}</ref> A deluxe edition of the album with four more tracks was later released on February 25, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.traxsource.com/title/1294716/northernsound-deluxe|title=6ixbuzz - NorthernSound (Deluxe)|website=www.traxsource.com}}</ref> 

==Background==
NorthernSound is the second compilation album by Toronto-based entertainment and media company  Canadian entertainment company and media platform [[6ixBuzz]], under their division [[6ixBuzz#6ixBuzz Entertainment|6ixBuzz Entertainment]]. The album contains 13-tracks and features Toronto based artists including [[NorthSideBenji]], Houdini, Casper TNG, K Money, [[Pvrx]], LocoCity, Da Crook, [[Puffy L‚Äôz]], Ramriddlz, Roney, and more. It was released on December 13, 2019, via digital download including Apple Music and Spotify.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hiphopcanada.com/6ixbuzz-northernsound/|title=6ixBuzz features some of Toronto's best on NorthernSound|date=December 13, 2019|website=HipHopCanada}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/6ixbuzz|title=6ixbuzz|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> The album was executively produced by Canadian record producer Jmak.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://respect-mag.com/2020/01/hot-on-the-block-toronto-production-giant-jmak-speaks-on-latest-projects/|title=HOT ON THE BLOCK: Toronto Production Giant Jmak Speaks on Latest Projects|date=January 20, 2020}}</ref>

The lead single, "Too Soft" by Houdini and NorthSideBenji, was premiered on BBC 1xtra by [[Kenny Allstar]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rapondemand.com/6ixbuzz-features-some-of-torontos-best-on-new-project-northernsound/|title=6ixBuzz features some of Toronto's best on new project NorthernSound}}</ref> The album was the last appearance by Bvlly and Why-S before their deaths on Christmas Eve.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/12/25/americas/bvlly-christmas-eve-death-trnd/index.html|title=Toronto rapper Bvlly, 24, killed on Christmas Eve|author1=Leah Asmelash |author2=Artemis Moshtaghian|website=CNN|date=25 December 2019}}</ref>
