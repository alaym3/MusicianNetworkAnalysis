'''Rory Charles Graham''' (born 29 January 1985), better known as '''Rag'n'Bone Man''', is an English  singer and songwriter. He is known for his deep [[baritone]] voice. His first hit single, "[[Human (Rag'n'Bone Man song)|Human]]", was released in 2016, and his [[Human (Rag'n'Bone Man album)|debut album of the same name]] was released in 2017. At the [[2017 Brit Awards]], he was named [[Brit Award for British Breakthrough Act|British Breakthrough Act]] and received the [[Critics' Choice (Brit Award)|Critics' Choice Award]].

== Early life ==
Rory Charles Graham<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/ace/#ace/writer/732864331/GRAHAM%20RORY%20CHARLES|title=ASCAP Ace Search|last=Graham|first=Rory Charles|publisher=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] (ASCAP)|access-date=29 April 2017}}</ref> was born on 29 January 1985 in [[Uckfield|Uckfield, East Sussex]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/jan/01/little-punk-jazz-shoegaze-the-new-blues|title='A little punk, a little jazz, a little shoegaze': meet the new blues|first=Laura|last=Barton|date=1 January 2015|via=The Guardian}}</ref> He attended [[Ringmer Community College]] in [[Ringmer]], from which he was [[Expulsion (education)#United Kingdom|expel]]led, and then enrolled at [[Uckfield College]] in his hometown. At the age of 15,{{Citation needed|date=February 2019}} he began [[Rapping|MCing]] with a [[drum and bass]] crew using the handle Rag 'N' Bonez, inspired by watching repeats of the [[BBC]] sitcom ''[[Steptoe and Son]]''. While at school, he was part of a group of youths supported by [[The Prince's Trust]] who developed a community project which involved painting and gardening in [[Crowborough]].

== Career ==
When he moved to [[Brighton]], his friend Gi3mo formed the rap group Rum Committee and invited him to join them. He started performing at Slip-jam B, where he met people who helped him start his career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huckmagazine.com/art-and-culture/music-2/rag-n-bone-man-mc-building-refuge-former-loves/|title=Rag N' Bone Man: The MC building a refuge for his past loves|date=9 June 2016|website=Huckmagazine.com|access-date=23 December 2016}}</ref> Over the next few years, they supported hip hop artists [[Pharoahe Monch]]<ref>{{cite web|first1=About the Author Culture Vulture Emma Calway is Dead Curious' Culture|last1=Vulture|accessdate=2021-02-16|title=Pharoahe Monch Live at Concorde 2, Brighton|url=http://deadcurious.com/2014/03/06/pharoahe-monch-live-at-concorde-2-brighton/|date=6 March 2014|website=Dead Curious}}</ref> and [[KRS-One]] at Brighton's Concorde 2, and released their own album titled ''Boozetown'' (2012) through [[Bandcamp]] and other digital stores. Just weeks before the Rum Committee album release, Rory Graham was asked to support [[Joan Armatrading]] at [[Brighton Dome]]. With no solo releases to his name to distribute on the night, he worked with Rum Committee producers Gi3mo and Sherlock Bones to create his first official release, the eight-track ''Bluestown EP'' (2012), which included one feature from the rap artist Ceezlin.{{citation needed|date=January 2018}}

===''Wolves'' and ''Disfigured'' EPs (2011–2015)===
In 2011, Graham started working with [[British hip hop|UK hip hop]] label [[High Focus Records]], releasing a number of recordings with them such as  a collaboration with MC/producer Leaf Dog titled ''Dog 'n Bone EP'' (2013) and a project with MC/producer Dirty Dike titled ''Put That Soul on Me'' (2014). Shortly afterwards, he began to collaborate with record producer [[Mark Crew]], who at the time was working on [[Bastille (band)|Bastille]]'s debut album ''[[Bad Blood (Bastille album)|Bad Blood]]''. Graham signed a publishing deal with [[Warner/Chappell Music|Warner Chappell]] in 2013.<ref>{{Citation|title=Disfigured – EP by Rag'n'Bone Man on Apple Music|date=6 March 2015|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/disfigured-ep/id962160321|language=en-us|access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref>

In 2014, in collaboration with Mark Crew, Graham released the EP ''Wolves'' through Best Laid Plans Records, containing nine tracks with guests including rapper [[Vince Staples]], [[Stig of the Dump (rapper)|Stig of the Dump]], and [[Kae Tempest]]. Graham, along with [[Skunk Anansie]], also featured on Bastille's third mixtape, ''[[VS. (Other People's Heartache, Pt. III)|VS.]]'', on the song "Remains". His follow-up in 2015, the ''Disfigured'' EP, was also released through Best Laid Plans Records. The lead track "Bitter End" was playlisted on [[BBC Radio 1Xtra|BBC Radio 1 Xtra]], and made it onto [[BBC Radio 1]]'s "In New Music We Trust" playlist.{{citation needed|date=May 2020}}

===''Human'' (2016–2020)===
[[File:Rag'n'Bone Man-3882.jpg|thumb|Graham at the SWR3 New Pop Festival 2017]]
Graham's first hit single, "[[Human (Rag'n'Bone Man song)|Human]]", was released on [[Columbia Records]] in July 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hitsdailydouble.com/news&id=299318|title=HITS Daily Double : Rumor Mill – SONY MUSIC U.K.'S TIPS FOR 2016|website=Hitsdailydouble.com|access-date=23 December 2016}}</ref> It peaked at number one in the official singles charts in Austria, Belgium, Germany and Switzerland. It was certified Gold in Austria, Belgium, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Sweden and Switzerland. "Human" was used as the theme music to the [[Amazon Prime]] series ''[[Oasis (2017 TV series)|Oasis]]'', in the official launch trailer for the video game ''[[Mass Effect: Andromeda]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X6PJEmEHIaY|title=MASS EFFECT™: ANDROMEDA – Official Launch Trailer|website=YouTube|access-date=10 March 2017}}</ref> in the trailer for the 2017 film ''[[Thank You for Your Service (2017 film)|Thank You for Your Service]]'', in the television series ''[[Inhumans (TV series)|Inhumans]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=50LQGcb5knE|title=Thank You for Your Service Trailer No. 1 (2017)|website=YouTube|access-date=20 June 2017}}</ref> and in the season 2 intro for the TV show ''[[Into the Badlands (TV series)|Into the Badlands]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-An_emPkjv0|title=Rag'nBone Man's Human intro for Into the Badlands season 2|website=YouTube|access-date=1 September 2017}}</ref> The song was also used in the outro for ''[[Ash vs. Evil Dead]]'' Season 3 Episode 9, as well as the ''[[Tom Clancy's Jack Ryan]]'' television series.

Graham's debut album, also titled ''[[Human (Rag'n'Bone Man album)|Human]]'', was released on 10 February 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/ragnbone-man-human-review|title=Rag'n'Bone Man – 'Human' Review – NME|website=NME|access-date=10 February 2017}}</ref> The album opens with the song "Human", features the single "[[Skin (Rag'n'Bone Man song)|Skin]]", and has tracks produced by Mark Crew, [[Two Inch Punch]], and [[Jonny Coffer]]. The album won the [[BBC Music Awards|BBC Music Award]] for British Album of the Year in 2017 and he was nominated for Artist of the Year. Later in 2017, Graham collaborated with British [[virtual band]] [[Gorillaz]], appearing on the song "The Apprentice" from the deluxe edition of their fifth studio album ''[[Humanz]]''.{{Citation needed|date=October 2017}}

Graham also sang and co-wrote one of the songs, "Broken People", from [[Will Smith]]’s 2017 Netflix film ''[[Bright (film)|Bright]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Logic Links Up With Rag'N'Bone Man for New Track "Broken People"|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2017/11/logic-rag-n-bone-man-broken-people-collaborative-record|access-date=2021-05-09|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref>

In January 2019, Graham collaborated with Scottish DJ and record producer [[Calvin Harris]] to record the song "[[Giant (Calvin Harris and Rag'n'Bone Man song)|Giant]]". It peaked at number two on the [[UK Singles Chart]]. He also collaborated with the Italian singer [[Zucchero Fornaciari]] to write the song "Freedom", for the album ''[[D.O.C. (Zucchero album)|D.O.C.]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Zucchero Fornaciari: arriva in radio "Freedom", il nuovo singolo |url=https://www.insidemusic.it/zucchero-fornaciari-arriva-in-radio-freedom-il-nuovo-singolo/ |date=1 October 2019 |language=it |publisher=Inside Music |access-date=1 October 2019}}</ref>

===''Life by Misadventure'' (2021)===
In January 2021, Graham released "[[All You Ever Wanted (Rag'n'Bone Man song)|All You Ever Wanted]]", the lead single from his second studio album ''[[Life by Misadventure]]''. He then collaborated with [[Pink (singer)|Pink]] for the second single from the album, "[[Anywhere Away from Here]]". The album was originally going to be released on 23 April 2021,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/ragnbone-man-announces-new-album-life-by-misadventure|title=Rag'n'Bone Man Announces New Album 'Life By Misadventure'|work=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]|last=Murray|first=Robin|date=29 January 2021|access-date=22 August 2021}}</ref> but was postponed to 7 May 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/ragnbone-man-pink-anywhere-away-from-here-2910070 |title=Listen to Rag'n'Bone Man's new collaboration with Pink, 'Anywhere Away From Here' |work=[[NME]] |date=April 9, 2021 |access-date=April 29, 2021 |author=Moore, Sam}}</ref>
