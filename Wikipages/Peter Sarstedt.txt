'''Peter Eardley Sarstedt'''<ref name="allmusic"/> (10 December 1941 – 8 January 2017), briefly billed early in his career as '''Peter Lincoln''', was an English singer-songwriter and instrumentalist. He was the brother of musicians [[Eden Kane]] and [[Clive Sarstedt]]. 

Although his music was classified as pop, it generally encompassed ballads derived from traditional [[folk music]] rather than traditional [[rock and roll]]. He was best known for writing and performing the song "[[Where Do You Go To (My Lovely)?]]", which topped the [[UK Singles Chart]] in 1969. Set to a "faux European [[waltz]] tune"<ref name="allmusic"/> and described as "a romantic novel in song",<ref name=scotsman/> it won an [[Ivor Novello Awards|Ivor Novello Award]]. The record remained Sarstedt's biggest hit. He had 1 more hit single and 1 hit album but despite numerous releases never had chart success again.  

He continued to tour throughout the 1980s, 1990s and 2000s, mainly in 1960s revival-type shows, until his retirement in 2010 due to ill health.<ref name="bbc"/>

==Early life==
Sarstedt was born in [[New Delhi]] in 1941, into a family long resident in India. His ancestor Christian Ludwig Wilhelm Sarstedt, born in 1841 in Hanover, migrated to India and died there in 1893. His father Albert James Sarstedt, a civil servant in [[British India]], married secondly Coral Byrne.<ref name="45-rpm">{{cite web | publisher = 45-rpm.org.uk|url=http://www.45-rpm.org.uk/dirp/peters.htm|title=Peter Sarstedt|access-date=28 September 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | publisher = [[British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors]] | url = http://www.basca.org.uk/members-directory/petersarstedt/ | title = Peter Sarstedt | access-date = 14 May 2012 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140303233004/http://www.basca.org.uk/members-directory/petersarstedt/ | archive-date = 3 March 2014 | df = dmy-all }}</ref><ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Unterberger|first=Richie|title=Peter Sarstedt|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/peter-sarstedt-mn0000843106/biography|publisher=Allmusic|access-date=20 September 2020}}</ref> Both of his parents had trained as classical musicians.<ref name="theguardian"/>

After [[Indian independence movement|Indian independence from Britain]] in 1945, Sarstedt’s parents managed a [[tea#Cultivation and harvesting|tea plantation]] in the Darjeeling district of West Bengal, where Sarstedt attended Victoria Boys' School, a [[boarding school]] in [[Kurseong]].<ref name="allmusic"/><ref name="scroll"/> He later moved to [[Kolkata|Calcutta]]; in 1954, Sarstedt’s father died, and the family moved to England,<ref name="allmusic"/><ref name="scroll">{{Cite news |url=https://scroll.in/latest/826234/british-indian-singer-songwriter-peter-sarstedt-dead-at-75 |title=British-Indian singer-songwriter Peter Sarstedt dead at 75 |work=Scroll |date=9 January 2017 |access-date=9 January 2017}}</ref> settling in south London just before the rock 'n' roll boom,<ref name="allmusic"/><ref name="The Sarstedt Brothers"/> and the Sarstedt brothers started out performing [[skiffle]]<ref name="allmusic"/> in a group  known as the ''Fabulous Five''.<ref name="britannica">{{cite web|url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Peter-Sarstedt|title=Peter Sarstedt - British Singer-songwriter}}</ref>

Sarstedt was a younger brother of the 1960s pop star [[Eden Kane]], for whom he was backing singer and briefly played [[bass guitar|bass]], and an elder brother of pop singer [[Clive Sarstedt]].<ref name="biography">{{cite web|url=http://www.music.us/biography/artist/13905/peter_sarstedt.html|title=Peter Sarsedt Biography|publisher=Music Entertainment Network|access-date=2 October 2009 |url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110724135037/http://www.music.us/biography/artist/13905/peter_sarstedt.html|archive-date=24 July 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Career==
===Hit recordings===
Sarstedt was best known for his 1969 [[List of UK Singles Chart number ones of the 1960s|UK number one single]], released on the [[United Artists Records|United Artists]] label, "Where Do You Go To (My Lovely)?", a portrait of a poor-born girl Marie-Claire who becomes a member of the European [[jet set]].<ref name="bbc"/><ref name="mirror">{{Cite news |url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/news/world-news/singer-songwriter-peter-sarstedt-dies-9585161 |title=Singer-songwriter Peter Sarstedt dies aged 75 after a long battle with illness |work=Mirror |date=8 January 2017 |access-date=8 January 2017}}</ref> The song topped the chart in 14 countries.<ref name="allmusic"/> It was also awarded the 1970 [[Ivor Novello Awards|Ivor Novello Award]] for Best Song Musically and Lyrically.<ref name="theguardian"/><ref>{{cite web |url= http://theivors.com/archive/1970-1979/the-ivors-1970/ |title= The 15th Ivor Novello Awards |work= The Ivors |access-date= 19 August 2016 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170307082550/http://theivors.com/archive/1970-1979/the-ivors-1970/ |archive-date= 7 March 2017 |url-status= dead }}</ref> He later cited his first wife, Anita, as the inspiration for the song.<ref name=scotsman/>

His debut album in 1969 reached number eight on the British charts<ref name="britannica" /> and his follow-up single, "Frozen Orange Juice", reached number 10 in the [[UK Singles Chart|UK]] in 1969.<ref name="telegraph">{{Cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/music/news/peter-sarstedt-singer-do-go-lovely-dies-75/ |title=Peter Sarstedt, singer of Where Do You Go To My Lovely?, dies at 75 |work=The Telegraph |date=8 January 2017 |access-date=8 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lyricszoo.com/peter-sarstedt/frozen-orange-juice/|title=Lyricszoo.com|work=Lyricszoo|access-date=28 September 2014}}</ref> However, subsequent releases failed to chart. Sarstedt recorded songs include "Beirut", "Take Off Your Clothes", and "I Am a Cathedral".<ref name="theguardian"/>

Another selection from the album ''Where Do You Go To (My Lovely)?,'' from which his single of the same title had been released, was called "Overture." This instrumental, whose music Sarstedt had composed in collaboration with Ian Green and [[Ray Singer (record producer)|Ray Singer]], was later used as the theme music for [[Keep America Beautiful]]'s famous "Crying Indian" public service announcement, which starred [[Iron Eyes Cody]].{{CN|date=November 2020}}

===Touring===
In the 1980s and 1990s, Sarstedt frequently toured the southern UK as part of the "Solid Silver '60s" package tours,<ref name="Rock movers & shakers">{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=L2kGAQAAMAAJ&q=Solid+Silver+%2760s+tour |title=Rock movers & shakers |publisher=Billboard Publications |year=1989 |isbn=9780823076086 |page=445}}</ref> having returned to England after several years residing in Denmark.<ref name="45-rpm"/><ref name="ktvl">{{Cite news |url=http://ktvl.com/news/entertainment/where-do-you-go-to-my-lovely-singer-peter-sarstedt-dead |title='Where Do You Go to My Lovely?' Singer Peter Sarstedt dead |work=KTVL |date=8 January 2017 |access-date=9 January 2017}}</ref> In the 1990s and 2000s, he continued to release new albums and tour. In 1997 he released the album ''[[England's Lane (Peter Sarstedt album)|England's Lane]]'', which featured the follow-up to "Where Do You Go To", entitled  "The Last of the Breed"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Peter-Sarstedt-Englands-Lane/release/2175000 |title=Peter Sarstedt – England's Lane (CD) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=18 December 2016}}</ref> and in 2007 an album of new material called ''On Song.''<ref name="on song">{{cite web|title=On Song|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/on-song-mw0001123768|website=www.allmusic.com}}</ref> He sang harmonies on "[[Don't Think Twice, It's All Right|Don't Think Twice]]" and "[[The Last Thing on My Mind]]" on Canadian singer Peter Thompson's 2007 album, ''Taking a Dive (Heart First)''.
===Popular culture===
New interest was sparked in his music after "Where Do You Go To" was used in the 2007 [[Wes Anderson]] films ''[[Hotel Chevalier]]'' and ''[[The Darjeeling Limited]]''.<ref name="bbc"/><ref name="allmusic"/> In 2012 a compilation of songs called ''Highlights—the Demos'' was released.<ref name=demos>{{cite web|url=http://www.petersarstedt.com/highlights_demos_album.html|title=Highlights - Demos|access-date=12 January 2017}}</ref>
===Later recordings and touring===
Sarstedt's final album, released in 2013, was titled ''Restless Heart.''<ref name="telegraph"/> It was produced by [[Ray Singer (record producer)|Ray Singer]] and the single and accompanying video "Valentine," directed and produced by Lara Singer, was released on Singer Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pEeUCJaOQuc|title=Peter Sarstedt - Valentine (Official)|last=Singer Records|date=12 June 2013|access-date=12 January 2017|via=YouTube}}</ref> A third instalment of "Where Do You Go To", continuing the story of Marie-Claire, to be titled "Farewell Marie-Claire", did not materialize.<ref name="website">{{cite web|url=http://www.PeterSarstedt.com|title=Peter Sarstedt website|website=Petersarstedt.com|access-date=26 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060205113310/http://petersarstedt.com/|archive-date=5 February 2006|url-status=dead}}</ref> Sarstedt last performed live in 2010.<ref name="music-news.">{{Cite news |url=http://www.music-news.com/news/UK/102633/Peter-Sarstedt-Where-Do-You-Go-To-My-Lovely-writer-dies |title=Peter Sarstedt 'Where Do You Go To My Lovely' writer dies |work=Music News |date=9 January 2017 |access-date=9 January 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life and death==
Sarstedt married dentist Anita Atke in 1969 and moved to Denmark. The couple divorced five years later.<ref name=scotsman>{{cite web|url=http://www.scotsman.com/news/obituaries/obituary-peter-sarstedt-singer-songwriter-who-topped-charts-with-where-do-you-go-to-my-lovely-1-4335831|title=Obituary: Peter Sarstedt, singer-songwriter who topped charts with Where Do You Go To (My Lovely)|publisher=scotsman.com/|access-date=12 January 2017}}</ref> His second wife was Joanna Meill. From 2013 he lived in a retirement home in Sussex, England. He had [[progressive supranuclear palsy]] (PSP), diagnosed in 2015, but originally misdiagnosed as [[dementia]] in 2013.<ref name="music-news."/> He died on 8 January 2017 at the age of 75.<ref name="bbc">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-38548507 |title=Singer-songwriter Peter Sarstedt dies aged 75 |work=[[BBC News]] |date=8 January 2017 |access-date=9 January 2017}}</ref><ref name="thetimes">{{Cite news |url=http://www.thetimes.co.uk/edition/news/peter-sarstedt-dies-tdm9mrccg |title=Peter Sarstedt dies |work=The Times |date=9 January 2017 |access-date=9 January 2017}}</ref> He is survived by his wife and two adult children.<ref name=scotsman/>

==Siblings==
Sarstedt's brothers are
*"Rick" Sarstedt (born Richard Graham Sarstedt, 29 March 1940, in [[New Delhi]], India)<ref name="Eden Kane">{{cite web|author=Bruce Eder |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/eden-kane-mn0000178089 |title=Eden Kane &#124; Biography & History |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=2 October 2015}}</ref> who himself topped the [[UK Singles Chart]] in 1961 with "[[Well I Ask You]]," under the stage name [[Eden Kane]].<ref name="bbc"/>
*[[Clive Sarstedt|Robin Sarstedt]] (born Clive Robin Sarstedt, 21 January 1944, in [[Ajmer]], Rajasthan, India), the youngest brother,<ref>{{Allmusic |class = artist |id = p20360 |label = Clive Robin Sarstedt |access-date = 24 April 2012 }}</ref><ref name="The Sarstedt Brothers">{{cite web|title=The Sarstedt Brothers|url=http://www.petersarstedt.com/sarstedt_brothers.html|website=www.petersarstedt.com}}</ref> who made his recording debut as "Wes Sands" (recorded by [[Joe Meek]]), and then continued as "Clive Sands".<ref name="Clive Sarstedt">{{cite web|title=Clive Sarstedt|url=http://www.petersarstedt.com/generalinfo.html|website=www.petersarstedt.com}}</ref> He had a hit in 1976 (as "Robin Sarstedt") with the [[Hoagy Carmichael]] cover "[[My Resistance Is Low]]."<ref name="The Sarstedt Brothers"/><ref name="bbc"/>
