'''Sanelisiwe Twisha''' (born 20 November 1987), known professionally as '''Moonchild Sanelly'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dailysun.co.za/Celebs/moonchild-survives-malaria-scare-20191205|title=32-year-old Moonchild survives malaria scare|website=dailysun|access-date=17 April 2020}}</ref> is a South African musician and dancer. She is known for her signature blue-colored hair and her self-created music genre called "Future ghetto punk".<ref>{{cite news |last1=Marunya |first1=Kundai |title=SA act to headline Shoko Festival |url=https://www.herald.co.zw/sa-act-to-headline-shoko-festival/ |accessdate=5 February 2019 |work=The Herald |date=8 September 2018}}</ref> Originally from [[Port Elizabeth]], the singer first came into limelight in the [[Durban]] poetry and music scene before relocating to [[Johannesburg]], where she has continued her musical career.

Her music has reached audiences beyond South Africa's borders, playing in festivals such as SXSW in Austin, Texas<ref>{{cite news |last1=Darangwa |first1=Shingai |title=5 hot African artists to look forward to at SXSW in Texas this year |url=http://livemag.co.za/youth-culture-in-south-africa/music/4-hot-african-artists-sxsw-in-texas-this-year/ |accessdate=5 February 2019 |agency=Live SA |date=26 February 2016}}</ref> and Primavera Sound in Barcelona.<ref>{{cite news |last1=OkayAfrica |title=Soaking in the African Acts at Primavera Sound Festival |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/primavera-sound.amp |accessdate=5 February 2019 |agency=Okayafrica |date=8 June 2016}}</ref> In August 2018, she went on a European tour with South African [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] group [[Die Antwoord]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mathaba |first1=Nosipho |title=Moonchild heads for Europe |url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/entertainment/2018-07-13-moonchild-heads-for-europe/ |accessdate=5 February 2019 |agency=Sowetan Live |date=13 July 2018}}</ref> Her music fuses elements of experimental electronic, afro-punk and edgy-pop with hints of her strong kwaito, hip hop and jazz background.<ref name="in studio">{{cite news |last1=Ferreira |first1=Jonathan |title=In Studio: Moonchild Sanelly |url=https://www.redbull.com/za-en/in-studio-moonchild-sanelly |accessdate=5 February 2019 |agency=Red Bull |date=13 July 2017}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Moonchild Sanelly was born in [[Port Elizabeth]], South Africa. She was born into a musical family, with her brother being a [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] producer, her mother a jazz singer, and her cousins [[kwaito]] dancers.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mg.co.za/article/2014-12-04-singer-moonchild-reaches-for-the-stars/?amp|title=Singer Moonchild Sanelly reaches for the stars|work=Mail & Guardian|author=Staff Reporter|date=14 December 2014}}</ref>

She moved to [[Durban]] in 2005 to study fashion at the Linea Fashion Design Academy and stayed there for six years.<ref name="remember">{{cite news |last1=Nkoyana |first1=Ayanda |title=As I long as I can remember. I grew up around music – Moonchild Sanelly |url=https://www.w24.co.za/PopCulture/Entertainment/Celebrity-gossip/as-i-long-as-i-can-remember-i-grew-up-around-music-moonchild-sanelly-20180219 |accessdate=5 February 2019 |agency=W24 Celebs |date=19 February 2018}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2006, Sanelly started performing in shows at [[Durban University of Technology]] with a key focus in poetry and hip hop, although she didn't actually call herself a poet for a long time until seeing people react. She featured regularly on a weekend show on Gagasi FM, called ''Poetry with King Siso''.<ref name="in studio"/> In mid 2020, she signed a record deal with Transgressive Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=Moonchild Sanelly Signs To Transgressive Records, Releases “Bashiri”|url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/newmusicfridays-moonchild-sanelly-signs-to-transgressive-records-releases-bashiri/|publisher=Zkhiphani|first=Mandisa|last=Ntsinde}}</ref> In March 2015, she released her studio album ''Rabulapha!'' with Transgressive Records.<ref>{{cite web |title=Rabulapha! Moonchild Sanelly |work=Apple Music |accessdate=28 July 2021 |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/rabulapha/1529234953 }}</ref> ''Rabulapha!'' earned  her a nomination at the [[22nd South African Music Awards|22nd Annual  South African Music Awards]] for Best Alternative Album. Red Bull discovered her music through an [[Extended play|EP]] she was featured on with DJ Shimza, which was eventually a part of the ''Soul Candi'' album.<ref name="remember"/> In 2019, Sanelly was featured on [[Beyoncé]]'s [[The Lion King: The Gift]] album in the song "My Power" along with [[Busiswa]] and Nija. Towards the end of 2019, she dropped her 4 tracks mini album project titled  ''Nude'' EP.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://iminathi.net/moonchild-sanelly-nudes-ep/|title=Moonchild Sanelly Nude EP|work=iminathi|accessdate=6 December 2019}}</ref> She made a notable contribution on Our Culture, first track off Zingah's 2020 album.{{citation needed|date=August 2020}}

In 2020, she was also featured on [[JazziDisciples]] album, ''0303'' in the song "Askies"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://samusicmag.co.za/2020/04/moonchild-sanelly-inspires-body-positivity-while-promoting-new-single-ft-jazzidisciples-askies/|title=Moonchild Sanelly Inspires Body Positivity while Promoting New Single Ft JazziDisciples "Askies"|work=SA Music Mag|accessdate=13 August 2020}}</ref> and later in "With Love to an Ex", from Gorillaz' seventh studio album ''[[Song Machine, Season One: Strange Timez]]''.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}

On June 29, 2021, her single "Yebo Teacher" was released.<ref>{{cite web |title=Moonchild Sanelly releases new single, Yebo Teacher {{!}} JustNje|url=https://justnje.com/moonchild-sanelly-releases-new-single-yebo-teacher/|work=JustNje|date=June 30, 2021}}</ref>
She will host upcoming show ''Madness Method'' airs on BET.<ref>{{cite web|title=Moonchild Sanelly to host new show|url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/amp/s-mag/2021-07-28-moonchild-sanelly-to-host-new-show/|work=Sowetan LIVE|date=28 July 2021}}</ref>

She made an announcement  on [[twitter]] of her feature on FIFA 2022 soundtrack.<ref>{{cite news|title=Moonchild Sanelly is featured on the FIFA 2022 soundtrack {{!}} Fakaza News |url=https://fakazanews.com/2021/09/21/moonchild-sanelly-is-featured-on-the-fifa-2022-soundtrack/|publisher=Fakaza News|last=Adejoy|date=September 21, 2021|location =South Africa }}</ref>
On October 1, 2021, she announced her upcoming single "Demon" with Sadnightdynamite.<ref>{{cite news|title=Moonchild to drop new single, ‘Demon’ {{!}} Fakaza News|url=https://fakazanews.com/2021/10/01/moonchild-to-drop-new-single-demon/|publisher=Fakaza News|location=South Africa |last=Bukola|date=1 October 2021}}</ref> The song is set to be released on October 4, 2021.
