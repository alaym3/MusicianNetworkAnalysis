'''Natalia Beatriz Dora Peluso'''<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-08-07|title=Biografía: ¿Quién es Nathy Peluso? Esta es su historia|url=https://happyfm.es/musica/biografia-quien-es-nathy-peluso-su-historia-191657/|access-date=2021-11-07|website=Happyfm}}</ref> (born 12 January 1995<ref>{{Cite news|last=Mañana|first=Carmen|date=2020-10-17|title=Nathy Peluso, nuevo poderío|language=es|work=El País|url=https://elpais.com/elpais/2020/10/16/eps/1602842250_387646.html|access-date=2021-11-07|issn=1134-6582}}</ref>), known artistically as '''Nathy Peluso''', is an Argentine singer, songwriter, dancer and pedagogist. Born in Argentina, and raised in Spain, Peluso became interested in the [[performing arts]] at an early age, performing cover songs at musical bars in her teenage years.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Deguer|first=Catalina|date=2020-11-01|title=Nathy Peluso, de los bares a los Grammy: 'Las mujeres me hacen saber que soy una inspiración para ellas'|url=https://www.clarin.com/espectaculos/musica/nathy-peluso-bares-grammy-mujeres-hacen-saber-inspiracion-_0_4Jvt4vUBy.html|access-date=2021-11-07|website=Clarín|language=es}}</ref> After graduating [[King Juan Carlos University]], Peluso relocated to [[Barcelona]] to pursue a professional career in music, with her first releases, ''Esmeralda'' (2017) and ''[[La Sandunguera]]'' (2018), being confected independently.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fernández|first=Sara|date=2021-01-18|title=Nathy Peluso entre la innovación, la genialidad y la polémica|url=https://marieclaire.perfil.com/noticias/personajes/musica-cantante-nathy-peluso-polemica-apropiacion-cultural-disco.phtml|access-date=2021-11-07|website=Marie Claire|language=es}}</ref> Peluso slowly started to become recognized in Spain.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-15|title=Nathy Peluso: "A mí me gusta la E de explícito"|url=https://www.elmundo.es/cultura/musica/2020/10/16/5f882c75fc6c8374198b464f.html|access-date=2021-11-07|website=ELMUNDO|language=es}}</ref> After signing [[Sony Music]], Peluso started working on her breakthrough album ''[[Calambre]]'' (2020), for which she won the [[Latin Grammy Award]] for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Music Album|Best Alternative Music Album]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nathy Peluso, una máquina impactante {{!}} El Diario Vasco|url=https://www.diariovasco.com/culturas/musica/nathy-peluso-maquina-concierto-san-sebastian-20211012225446-nt.html|access-date=2021-11-07|website=www.diariovasco.com}}</ref>

Distinguisged for her theatrical personality onstage, and her fusion of [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], [[Soul music|soul]], and [[world music]], Peluso's popularity expanded after collaborating with [[Bizarrap]] on "[[Nathy Peluso: Bzrp Music Sessions, Vol. 36|Nasty Girl]]", achieving commercial success and social media virality in Latin America.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Latin Artist on the Rise: Meet Nathy Peluso, From The Underground Hip-Hop Scene to Latin Grammys|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/9462722/latin-artist-on-the-rise-nathy-peluso/|access-date=2021-11-07|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=El éxito global de la Bizarrap Music Session de Nathy Peluso|url=https://www.filo.news/musica/El-exito-global-de-la-Bizarrap-Music-Session-de-Nathy-Peluso-20210107-0054.html|access-date=2021-11-07|website=Filo News|language=es}}</ref> She has also collaborated with [[Christina Aguilera]], [[Karol G]], and [[C. Tangana]], with the latter one's duet "[[Ateo]]" debuting atop the [[PROMUSICAE|Spanish charts]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-10-19|title='Ateo' de C. Peluso's artisty has been awarded four [[Premios Gardel]] and a [[Latin Grammy Award]] out of six nominations, including [[Latin Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], among many others.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-24|title=Nathy Peluso se consagró en los Gardel 2021 con cuatro premios|url=https://www.perfil.com/noticias/protagonistas/nathy-peluso-se-consagro-en-los-gardel-2021-con-cuatro-premios.phtml|access-date=2021-11-07|website=Perfil|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-11-18|title=Artists Talk First-Time Latin GRAMMY Nominations|url=https://www.grammy.com/latin-grammys/news/2020-latin-grammys-cazzu-guaynaa-nathy-peluso-soy-emilia|access-date=2021-11-07|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Peluso was born in 1995 in [[Luján, Buenos Aires|Luján]], [[Argentina]], but was raised in the [[Saavedra, Buenos Aires|Saavedra]] neighbourhood in [[Buenos Aires]]. While growing up she listened to a variety of artists like [[Ella Fitzgerald]] and [[Ray Charles]], as well as folk artists such as [[João Gilberto]], [[Ray Barretto]] or [[Atahualpa Yupanqui]]. She has Italian ascendance.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2018-06-08|title=Nathy Peluso: "Soy consciente del talento que tengo y no me hace falta copiar a nadie"|url=https://www.revistavanityfair.es/poder/articulos/nathy-peluso-cantante-entrevista/31495|access-date=2021-11-07|website=Vanity Fair España|language=es-ES}}</ref>

In 2004, she emigrated to Spain with her family. She first resided in [[Alicante]]. At the age of 16, she began to perform at hotels and restaurants in [[Torrevieja]], mainly performing classic songs by Frank Sinatra, Etta James or Nina Simone. As a teenager she began uploading versions to her YouTube channel. She moved to [[Murcia]] shortly after, where she entered the city's local university to study a degree in Audiovisual Communication. After leaving the degree program, she moved to [[Madrid]] to study Fine Arts at the [[King Juan Carlos University]]. She specialized in Pedagogy of Visual Arts and Dance. While studying, she worked in the hospitality industry as a waitress at VIPS and Domino's Pizza, among others.

After finalizing her studies, she moved to [[Barcelona]], where she currently resides.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Zanellato|first=Romina|date=2021-04-08|title=Nathy Peluso: cómo construyó una carrera al borde de la fantasía|language=es-AR|work=La Nación|url=https://www.lanacion.com.ar/revista-rolling-stone/nathy-peluso-como-construyo-una-carrera-al-borde-de-la-fantasia-nid05042021/|access-date=2021-11-07|issn=0325-0946}}</ref> In 2017 she began to dedicate herself completely to music with the release of her first mixtape ''Esmeralda''.

== Career ==

=== 2017–2018: First releases ===
In October 2017, Peluso independently released a seven-track extended play titled ''Esmeralda'', which included the single "Corashe".<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 February 2020|title=Nathy Peluso, "la 'fuckin' jefa": "Soy un poco drama queen, me gustan la ironía, la pasión y la exageración"|url=https://www.elmundo.es/cultura/musica/2020/02/06/5e3af90ffc6c839d388b45fc.html|access-date=29 September 2020|website=ELMUNDO|language=es}}</ref> The track aroused the interest of many music critics and independent music magazines like ''Mondo Sonoro'' and ''[[Rockdelux]].''<ref>{{Cite web|date=1 September 2017|title=Nathy Peluso: "A mí se me etiqueta como trap y me la suda un poco"|url=https://www.mondosonoro.com/entrevistas/nathy-peluso/|access-date=29 September 2020|website=MondoSonoro|language=es}}</ref>

In April 2018, she released a second EP, ''La Sandunguera'', with Everlasting Records, which spawned the singles "La Sandunguera" and "Estoy Triste". She later embarked on a tour with her band Big Menu that had over a hundred concerts, including gigs at [[Bilbao|BBK Live]], [[Sónar]] and the [[Festival Internacional de Benicàssim]]. The tour visited Spain and selected parts of Europe and Latin America. She was nominated at the Independent Music Awards (MIN) for Song of the Year and Best Music Video for "La Sandunguera" She also received the Discovery Artist award at the [[Latin Alternative Music Conference]] in New York City.<ref>{{Cite web|date=1 July 2018|title=Mala Rodríguez|url=https://www.lavanguardia.com/vida/20180714/45886533174/mala-rodriguez-es-importantisimo-que-el-feminismo-no-sea-racista.html|access-date=29 September 2020|website=La Vanguardia|language=es}}</ref>

In 2019 she released her debut book "Deja Que te Combata", a compilation of her thoughts, reflections, stories and past and upcoming projects.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2 July 2019|title=Reseña del libro "Deja que te combata" de Nathy Peluso (2019)|url=https://www.mondosonoro.com/criticas/libros-novedades/nathy-peluso-deja-que-te-combata/|access-date=29 September 2020|website=MondoSonoro|language=es}}</ref> During that year she continued promoting herself through new releases like "Natikillah" and appearances at major festivals such as [[Primavera Sound]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=EFE|date=1 June 2019|title=Nathy Peluso llama al empoderamiento de las mujeres desde el Primavera Sound|url=https://www.eldiario.es/cultura/nathy-peluso-empoderamiento-primavera-sound_1_1524172.html|access-date=29 September 2020|website=ElDiario.es|language=es}}</ref> She also partnered with [[Samsung]] to be part of their Somos Smart Girl campaign alongside [[Blanca Suárez]], [[Sandra Barneda]] and [[Carolina Marín]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2 May 2019|title=Blanca Suárez, Sandra Barneda, Nathy Peluso y Carolina Marín son las nuevas SmartGirl de Samsung|url=https://prnoticias.com/2019/05/29/blanca-suarez-samsung-smartgirl/|access-date=29 September 2020|website=PR Noticias|language=es}}</ref>

=== 2019–present: ''Calambre'' ===
In December 2019, Peluso signed a record deal with [[Sony Music Spain]]. Her first release as a signed artist was the independent single "Copa Glasé".

In 2020 she reached the attention of the general public and the mainstream audience after ''[[Operación Triunfo (series 11)|Operación Triunfo]]'' contestant Anaju sang "La Sandunguera" on prime time to keep participating on the show.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Yotele|first=Redacción|date=1 February 2020|title=Nía cantará un tema de Beyoncé y Anajú se atreverá con Nathy Peluso en la gala 5 de 'OT 2020'|url=https://www.elperiodico.com/es/yotele/20200210/ot-2020-gala-5-temas-nia-beyonce-7843257|access-date=29 September 2020|website=elperiodico|language=es}}</ref> After that, Peluso was invited to the show on [[International Women's Day|Women's Day]] to promote her new single "Business Woman", defined as a feminist anthem.<ref>{{Cite web|last=vertele.eldiario.es|date=5 March 2020|title='OT 2020' recibe en su Gala 8 a Nathy Peluso y Dora como artistas invitadas|url=https://vertele.eldiario.es/noticias/OT-Gala-8-Nathy-Peluso-Dora-temas-operacion-triunfo_0_2210478963.html|access-date=29 September 2020|website=vertele|language=es-ES}}</ref> During the peak of the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Peluso released a musical collaboration with urbano artist [[Rels B]] titled "No Se Perdona", which had great commercial success. Another single "Buenos Aires" was released shortly after.<ref>{{Cite web|last=CMTV|title=Nathy Peluso lanza "Buenos Aires", canción y video que retrata el espejismo vivido durante el confinamiento.|url=https://www.cmtv.com.ar/noticias/noticia_completa.php?bnid=2594&nid=25046&artista=Nathy_Peluso&titulo=“Buenos_Aires”,_estreno_de_Nathy_Peluso_|access-date=29 September 2020|website=CMTV|language=es}}</ref> It received critical acclaim for its message and sound and scored a [[Latin Grammy Award|Latin Grammy]] nomination for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Alternative Song|Best Alternative Song]]. She also received a [[Premios Gardel|Premio Gardel]] and a [[The Latin Recording Academy|Latin Grammy]] nomination for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Jamele|first=Agustín|date=2 September 2020|title=El trap argentino se impone en los Latin Grammy|url=https://www.perfil.com/protagonistas/el-trap-argentino-se-impone-en-los-latin-grammys.phtml|access-date=29 September 2020|website=Perfil.com|language=es}}</ref> She was also covered in ''[[The Guardian]]'' for the first time.<ref name="Guardian">{{Cite news|last=Hutchinson|first=Kate|date=2 September 2020|title=One to watch: Nathy Peluso|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/sep/26/one-to-watch-nathy-peluso|access-date=29 September 2020|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>
