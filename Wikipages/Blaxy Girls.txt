'''Blaxy Girls''' was a [[Romania]]n [[All-female band|all-girl]] [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Constanta]], [[Romania]] formed in 2007. The band consisted of lead singer Rucsandra Iliescu, bassist Cristina Marinescu, guitarist Diana Ganea, pianist Ana Maria Nanu and drummer Gela Marinescu.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Blaxy Girls discography - RYM/Sonemic|url=https://rateyourmusic.com/artist/blaxy-girls|access-date=2021-11-06|website=Rate Your Music|language=en}}</ref>

The group's first single, "If You Feel My Love" quickly became very popular in Romania. Their second great hit was "[[Dear Mama (Blaxy Girls song)|Dear Mama]]". The clip was internationally released in the beginning of March, as a present for the women and mothers all around the world. The first album of the group was released in December 2008.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.telegrafonline.ro/1226700000/articol/70321/mihai_traistariu_si_8222blaxy_girls8221_in_concert_la_constanta.html|title=Michael Trăistariu and "Blaxy Girls" in concert in Constanta|last=Toader |first=Elena|date=15 November 2008|work=Telegraf|language=Romanian|accessdate=24 September 2009}}</ref> The album included a bonus track "If You Feel My Love (Chaow MIX)", a remixed version of the lead single which features a sample of a classic Beijing opera about [[Chen Shimei and Qin Xianglian]]. They appeared in the semi-finals of the 2008 [[Golden Stag Festival]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.antena3.ro/stiri/showbiz/manelistul-costi-ionita-responsabil-pentru-succesul-trupei-de-pop-rock-blaxy-girls_54815.html |title=Maneliste Costi Ionita, responsible for the success of pop-rock band Blaxy Girls |date=3 October 2009 |publisher=Antena3 |language=Romanian |accessdate=24 September 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090708051740/http://antena3.ro/stiri/showbiz/manelistul-costi-ionita-responsabil-pentru-succesul-trupei-de-pop-rock-blaxy-girls_54815.html |archivedate=July 8, 2009 }}</ref> and were among the finalists for the [[Romania in the Eurovision Song Contest 2009|2009 Romanian]] [[Eurovision Song Contest 2009|Eurovision Song Contest]], with the song [[Dear Mama (Blaxy Girls song)|Dear Mama]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telegrafonline.ro/1233180000/articol/76330/8222zero8221_8222blaxy_girls8221_si_dalma_printre_finalistii_eurovisionului_romanesc.html|title="Zero", "Blaxy Girls" and Dalma, including Romanian Eurovision finalists|date=29 January 2009|work=Telegraf|language=Romanian|accessdate=24 September 2009}}</ref> finishing in a tie for second place.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.esctoday.com/news/read/13118|title=Romania has decided for Eurovision!|last=Floras|first=Stella |date=31 January 2009|publisher=esctoday.com|accessdate=24 September 2009}}</ref>

They planned to compete in the national selection of [[Romania]] for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2010]] with the song ''"Save The World"'' but later withdrew.<ref>{{cite news|last=Busa|first=Alexandru|date=21 October 2009|title=The Blaxy Girls play their card again|work=esctoday.com|url=http://www.esctoday.com/news/read/14408|url-status=dead|accessdate=21 October 2009|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091024041457/http://www.esctoday.com/news/read/14408|archivedate=24 October 2009}}</ref>

In 2020, their track "If You Feel My Love (Chaow MIX)" became a widespread [[Internet meme|meme]], which was often depicted alongside a clip from [[Tom & Jerry]] set to the song. In the clip, [[Tom Cat]], as a [[caricature]] of a [[Chinese people|Chinese person]], spins on a record player while another vinyl record rests on his head.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Chin Cheng Hanji|url=https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/chin-cheng-hanji|access-date=2021-11-06|website=Know Your Meme}}</ref> The track was nicknamed "Chin Cheng Hanji" due to the English pronunciation of the Chinese lyrics. In 2021 the track was used in another meme parodying the proposed [[Social Credit System|Chinese Social Credit System]] in which one is asked questions about China.<ref>{{Cite web|title=China's Social Credit System / +15 Social Credit|url=https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/events/chinas-social-credit-system-15-social-credit|access-date=2021-11-06|website=Know Your Meme}}</ref> If they are answered in a way that opposes the official narrative provided by the [[Chinese Communist Party|Chinese government]] (e.g. acknowledging the [[1989 Tiananmen Square protests and massacre|Tiananmen Square Massacre]]) one is berated and/or punished. Similarly, if one answers "correctly" they are rewarded with more Social Credit Points.<ref>{{Cite web|title=People Don't Understand China's Social Credit, and These Memes Are Proof|url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/pkpe3z/china-social-credit-meme|access-date=2021-11-06|website=www.vice.com|language=en}}</ref>
