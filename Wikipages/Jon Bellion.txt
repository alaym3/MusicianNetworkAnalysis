'''Jonathan David Bellion'''<ref name=BMIDATA>{{cite web|url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/writer.asp?blnWriter=True&blnPublisher=True&blnArtist=True&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&querytype=WriterID&keyid=550236388&keyname=BELLION+JONATHAN+DAVID&CAE=667682299&Affiliation=BMI&term=|title=Songwriter/Composer: Bellion Jonathan David|publisher=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]] (BMI)|access-date=June 16, 2016}}{{dead link|date=November 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> (born December 26, 1990) is an American singer, rapper, songwriter, and record producer. He was born and raised in [[Lake Grove, New York]] on [[Long Island]].<ref name="elitedaily1">{{Cite news|url=http://elitedaily.com/entertainment/jon-bellion-interview/1530077/|title=Jon Bellion's Emotional, Inspiring Journey To The Top Of The Music Industry|date=2016-06-21|work=Elite Daily|access-date=2017-04-02}}</ref> He is best known for his song "[[All Time Low (Jon Bellion song)|All Time Low]]," along with his many big writing and producing credits. Bellion has released four [[mixtape]]s and two [[studio album]]s. His first, ''[[The Human Condition (Jon Bellion album)|The Human Condition]]'', was released on June 10, 2016, and it debuted at number five on the US ''Billboard'' 200.<ref name="US200">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/1539461/jon-bellion/chart?f=305|title=Jon Bellion - Chart History {{!}} Billboard 200|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 1, 2017}}</ref> He released his second studio album, ''[[Glory Sound Prep]]'', on November 9, 2018.

He is currently signed to [[Visionary Music Group]]<ref>{{cite web|last1=Guarino|first1=Nick|title=Jon Bellion – Life (Music Video) : Indie / R&B / Pop Single [TSIS PREMIERE]|url=http://thissongissick.com/blog/2012/jon-bellion-life-music-video-tsis-premiere/#sthash.G6sEBRhn.dpbs|website=This Song is Sick|publisher=This Song is Sick|access-date=September 12, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140912205248/http://thissongissick.com/blog/2012/jon-bellion-life-music-video-tsis-premiere/#sthash.G6sEBRhn.dpbs|archive-date=September 12, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> and [[Capitol Records]]. Bellion also opened the concerts from the third leg of [[Twenty One Pilots]]' [[Emotional Roadshow World Tour]], which also featured guest [[Judah and the Lion]].

==Early life==
Bellion was born and raised in [[Lake Grove, New York]]. He is of [[Italians|Italian]] descent and his family comes from [[Naples]].<ref name="elitedaily1"/> Torn between his love for basketball and music throughout high school, Bellion made the choice to pursue his music career in his junior year. After high school, he enrolled in the music program at the private [[Five Towns College]] in [[Dix Hills, New York]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.ftc.edu/five-towns-college-former-student-jon-bellion-wins-grammy-award/|title=Five Towns College Former Student Jon Bellion Wins A Grammy Award {{!}} Five Towns College|date=2015-02-09|work=Five Towns College|access-date=2017-04-02}}</ref> At the age of 19, he decided to drop out of college and focus on writing professionally. He worked under songwriter and Warner Brothers A&R executive [[Kara DioGuardi]] for a year and signed a deal through her personal publishing company. Through this experience, Bellion learned how to write songs for different artists, in genres such as rock and hip hop.<ref>{{cite web|author=Shawn Setaro |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/shawnsetaro/2016/06/22/jon-bellion-from-pop-songs-to-pixar/#6e0182071a72 |title=Jon Bellion: From Pop Songs To Pixar |website=Forbes.com |access-date=2020-04-12}}</ref>

== Career ==

===2011–2014: Early career and various mixtapes===
Bellion's first mixtape, ''Scattered Thoughts Vol. 1'' was released in early 2011 via his Facebook page and reached over 11,000 downloads. Bellion composes and produces all of his own music. Jon Bellion signed with [[Visionary Music Group]] in 2012 and released a cover of Drake's "The Motto" on VMG's YouTube page.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Guarino|first1=Nick|title=Jon Bellion – The Motto (Drake Cover) : Chill Original Indie / R&B Cover|url=http://thissongissick.com/2012/jon-bellion-the-motto-drake-cover/|website=This Song is Sick|publisher=This Song is Sick|access-date=September 12, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140912211320/http://thissongissick.com/2012/jon-bellion-the-motto-drake-cover/|archive-date=September 12, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2012, Jon Bellion wrote the chorus to the song "[[The Monster (song)|The Monster]]" by [[Eminem]] featuring [[Rihanna]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Markman|first1=Rob|title=Meet Eminem And Rihanna's 'Monster' Songwriter|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1716476/eminem-rihanna-jon-bellion-the-monster-writer/|website=MTV|publisher=MTV News|access-date=September 12, 2014}}</ref> This song won a [[Grammy Award]] in the 2015 Grammy Awards for Best Rap/Sung Collaboration. Bellion also co-wrote and produced the song "[[Trumpets (Jason Derulo song)|Trumpets]]" by [[Jason Derulo]] in 2012, which was not released until 2013 in the UK and 2014 in the US.

Jon Bellion released his second free project, titled ''Translations Through Speakers'' on February 20, 2013, his third, ''The Separation'' on December 10 of the same year and his fourth free project, ''The Definition'' on September 23, 2014, with the single "Luxury"<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Ramirez|first1=Erica|title=Jon Bellion, 'Simple and Sweet': Exclusive Video Premiere|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6214428/jon-bellion-simple-and-sweet-exclusive-video-premiere|magazine=Billboard|publisher=Billboard|access-date=September 12, 2014}}</ref> Bellion also headlined his first national tour called The Beautiful Mind Tour in October 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/08/jon-bellion-simple-and-sweet-video|title=Watch Jon Bellion's "Simple and Sweet" Video|last=Ortiz|first=Edwin|website=Complex|access-date=September 12, 2014}}</ref>In 2014, Bellion also released a final mixtape titled “The Definition”.

===2015–2017: Touring and ''The Human Condition''===
Bellion released several singles in early 2015, including "Woodstock (Psychedelic Fiction)", "All Time Low", and "Woke the F*ck Up"<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20140805121938/https://soundcloud.com/jonbellion ]</ref> through [[Capitol Records]]. On March 31, 2015, Bellion announced his second national tour, The Definition Tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=JON BELLION ANNOUNCES 2015 HEADLINING TOUR; RELEASES NEW SONG, "WOODSTOCK"|url=http://theearlyregistration.com/2015/03/31/jon-bellion-2015-headlining-tour/|website=The Early Registration|access-date=February 6, 2016}}</ref> The tour began on May 26 and concluded on July 2. Bellion was also featured on "[[Beautiful Now]]", a song on [[Zedd]]'s album ''[[True Colors (Zedd album)|True Colors]]'', which was released on May 15, 2015. Bellion was also featured on American rapper [[B.o.B]]'s album ''[[Psycadelik Thoughtz]]'' on the song "Violence".

Bellion released several acoustic versions of his songs, including one of "All Time Low" on February 26, 2016, and one of "Human" on March 4, 2016.

Bellion confirmed that his debut album ''[[The Human Condition (Jon Bellion album)|The Human Condition]]'' was set to be released on June 10, 2016. He released three promotional singles from the album: "Guillotine", on April 13, 2016, "80's Films", on May 27, 2016, and "Maybe IDK" on June 2, 2016. The lead single from the album, "[[All Time Low (Jon Bellion song)|All Time Low]]" was released on May 13, 2016<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/All-Time-Low-Explicit/dp/B01F9W9CZC/|title=All Time Low [Explicit] by Jon Bellion|website=[[Amazon (company)|Amazon]]|access-date=November 5, 2018}}</ref> and became his most successful single to date, reaching number 16 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|US Billboard Hot 100]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/hot-100/2017-01-14|title=Top 100 Songs - Billboard Hot 100 Chart|work=Billboard|access-date=November 5, 2018}}</ref> and was certified [[Double-platinum]] in the US and Australia.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2017/05/30/jon-bellions-time-low-certified-2x-platinum-united-states/|title=Jon Bellion's "All Time Low" Certified 2x Platinum In United States|date=May 30, 2017|work=Headline Planet|access-date=November 5, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> On June 10, ''The Human Condition'' was officially released. Bellion states that the album is simply about being human, explaining, "Us as humans all have the same problems. We struggle with pride and tons of different issues that nobody really wants to talk about. So I figured if I'm the honest one showing how human I am, it will make people feel better about themselves." In his music, Jon talks a lot about God. He doesn't just talk about a vague idea of faith; he makes sure to emphasize God specifically.<ref name="elitedaily1"/> Jon Bellion also says the sonics and album artwork for ''The Human Condition'' were created in order to get the attention of [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]]/[[Pixar]], saying, "It's like a giant business plan for Disney Pixar because it's always been my dream to score a movie for them. So basically if my debut album blows up, [[John Lasseter]] [of Disney Pixar] and these guys will hopefully see these images and be like 'Wait a second this is not one of our movies, what is this?'"<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lovaas|first1=Taylor|title=From Choirs to Animations, Jon Bellion's Debut Album "The Human Condition" is Full Artistry Both Sonically and Visually: SU Featured Interview|url=http://swaysuniverse.com/from-choirs-to-animations-jon-bellions-debut-album-the-human-condition-is-full-artistry-both-sonically-and-visually-su-featured-interview|website=Sway's Universe|access-date=June 14, 2016}}</ref> The artwork for the album was created by visual development artist David Ardinaryas Lojaya. During ''The Human Condition'' tour, Bellion announced that a representative from Pixar met him at one of his shows.<ref>{{Citation|last=baha moore|title=Jon Bellion Pixar Announcement|date=December 4, 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6Yeybd3YKjc|access-date=November 5, 2018}}</ref> He was invited to the Pixar Campus and said he might be involved with a Pixar film in the near future.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://berkeleybside.com/bellion-at-pixar/|title=Jon Bellion's dreams come true at Pixar Animation Studios|date=November 15, 2016|work=The B-Side|access-date=November 5, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

In June 2017, Bellion was picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] and was featured on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'' show hosted by [[Kathie Lee Gifford]] and [[Hoda Kotb]] and broadcast nationally where he performed "All Time Low" live. Jon Bellion was the opening act for the US leg of rock duo [[Twenty One Pilots]]' 2017 [[Emotional Roadshow World Tour]]. The 33-show tour began on January 17 in Providence, Rhode Island and ended on March 5 in Louisville, Kentucky.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.inquisitr.com/3502917/jon-bellion-joins-twenty-one-pilots-for-u-s-leg-of-emotional-roadshow-world-tour/|title=Jon Bellion Joins Twenty One Pilots For U.S. Leg Of 'Emotional Roadshow World Tour'|date=2016-09-12|work=The Inquisitr News|access-date=2017-04-02}}</ref>

On October 13, 2017, Bellion released his mixtapes ''Translations Through Speakers'', ''The Separation'' and ''The Definition'' on digital stores as well as releasing a collection with those three mixtapes entitled ''Growth''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Growth by Jon Bellion on iTunes|date=2017-10-13|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/growth/1294918458|language=en-US|access-date=November 5, 2018}}</ref>

===2018: ''Glory Sound Prep''===

On January 25, Bellion teased a snippet of an upcoming song on an Instagram video. On April 16, Bellion began teasing his next album with 4 pictures captioned with "GSP". He later changed the name of his Instagram to his band/group "Beautiful Mind". On June 23, Bellion released a behind the scenes video of his previous album's last track, "Hand of God".

On October 9, Bellion posted a photo to his Twitter account from "GSP Staff", stating: "Tomorrow, Headmaster [[Stormzy]] requests your presence for an orientation ceremony in the main atrium. Time: 3:00 pm EST." The following day he announced the studio album ''Glory Sound Prep'' on his Twitter account, which was set to be released on November 9. On October 17, he released a preview on his social media accounts of the first single from the album, "[[Conversations with My Wife]]", which was released on October 19.<ref>[https://twitter.com/jonbellion/status/1052651082524979200 ]{{dead link|date=April 2020}}</ref> The following week, he released a preview on his social media accounts of the second single, "JT", and released it on October 26.  "Stupid Deep", the third single off of the album, was released on November 2.

On November 9, Bellion released ''Glory Sound Prep''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gonzagabulletin.com/arts_and_entertainment/jon-bellion-s-sophomore-album-paints-the-story-of-his/article_82d37952-e905-11e8-af65-53b09597646a.html|title=Jon Bellion's sophomore album paints the story of his life for listeners|author=Mila Yoch|website=The Gonzaga Bulletin|language=en|access-date=2019-01-25}}</ref>

==== Beautiful Mind Records ====
Bellion officially started his own record label in July 2019 called Beautiful Mind Records and signed the pop/soul band, [[Lawrence (band)|Lawrence]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BzjcfoQAPIt/|title=Beautiful Mind's Instagram photo: "Beautiful Mind Records. @JonBellion is proud to launch a platform for artists where total freedom and infrastructure to support ideas comes..."|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-04-25}}</ref> Lawrence was the opener for most of his shows on the Glory Sound Prep Tour. He helped write and produce their first three singles "Casualty", "It's Not All About You", and "The Weather."<ref>{{Citation|title=Lawrence – Casualty|url=https://genius.com/Lawrence-casualty-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2020-04-25}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Lawrence – It's Not All About You|url=https://genius.com/Lawrence-its-not-all-about-you-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2020-04-25}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Lawrence – The Weather|url=https://genius.com/Lawrence-the-weather-lyrics|language=en|access-date=2020-04-25}}</ref>

==Influences==
Bellion has stated on multiple occasions that [[Kanye West]] is one of his biggest inspirations. "I loved everything but it was Kanye West who really changed everything for me." Bellion has also stated that he was inspired by [[Eminem]], [[Pharrell Williams]], [[Coldplay]], [[John Mayer]], [[André 3000]], and [[Paul Simon]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Khan|first1=Ahmad|title=No Genre: An Introduction to Jon Bellion|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/ahmad-khan/no-genre-an-introduction-_b_5565470.html|website=Huffington Post|access-date=September 12, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Artist Interview - Jon Bellion|url=http://songrevelation.com/2011/12/artists-bands/up-and-coming-artists/artist-interview-jon-bellion/|website=Song Revelation|publisher=Song Revelation|access-date=September 16, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006100439/http://songrevelation.com/2011/12/artists-bands/up-and-coming-artists/artist-interview-jon-bellion/|archive-date=October 6, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> Bellion has also made it known that he is a big fan of [[J Dilla]] and Disney Pixar, drawing inspiration from the musician and computer animation film studio for both ''The Definition'' and ''The Human Condition''. Bellion explains that he loves how Pixar movies are "simple yet presented in a way that is groundbreaking."<ref>{{cite web|title=jon bellion 'definition' album is a mix between dilla and pixar|url=http://www.mtv.com/videos/news/1085436/jon-bellion-definition-album-is-a-mix-between-dilla-and-pixar.jhtml|website=MTV|access-date=14 June 2016}}</ref> Bellion credits much of his musical development and success to his bandmates whom he met while in college, expressing his love for them in his "Beautiful Mind Documentary" stating, "everybody in my band has taught me something, and has gotten me to where I am because of them" continuing "I surround myself with people better than me at something, you will very rarely see somebody around me, especially musically, who I don't look up to".<ref>{{Citation|last=Visionary Music Group|title=Jon Bellion - Beautiful Mind Documentary|date=2014-02-19|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Lr3AvyhXQ3g|access-date=2017-04-02}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Bellion announced that he was married during the early months of 2018.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20200219235129/https://www.songmeaningsandfacts.com/conversations-with-my-wife-by-jon-bellion/ ]</ref> His song "[[Conversations with My Wife]]" from his 2018 album ''[[Glory Sound Prep]]'' alluded to his marriage and its attributes, leading his fanbase to question the relationship status that Bellion seldom discussed publicly.

Bellion identifies as a Christian.<ref>http://www.thesojourniwu.com/jon-bellions-glory-sound-prep-review/#:~:text=Jon%20Bellion%20is%20a%20self,anything%20he%20has%20previously%20released.</ref>
