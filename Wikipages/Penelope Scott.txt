'''Penelope Scott''' is an American musician, singer-songwriter, and producer. She has self-produced all of her own music. After releasing the [[compilation album]]s ''Junkyard'' (2020) and ''The Junkyard 2'' (2020), Scott released her debut album, ''Public Void'', in August 2020. Her viral song "Rät" peaked at 29 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Hot Rock & Alternative Songs|Hot Rock & Alternative Songs Chart]]. Scott's latest EP ''Hazards'' released in 2021.

==Background==
===Early life and career===
Scott began learning the piano at the age of eight.<ref name="Shitstorm"/> In middle and high school, she focused on writing lyrics and learning how to record.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Interview: Penelope Scott on "Big Overwhelming Feelings" in Music|url=https://ftpunks.com/interview-penelope-scott-on-big-overwhelming-feelings-in-music/|last=Marie|first=Olivia|date=January 21, 2021|website=For the Punks|access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref> Scott began uploading her songs online while taking a music production class in college. She released the compilation album ''Junkyard'' on February 27, 2020, followed shortly by the release of ''The Junkyard 2'' on May 13, 2020.<ref name="Meritocracy"/> The latter explores themes of [[emotional labor]], healthcare and insecurity.<ref name="Shitstorm"/> A song from the second album, "Sweet Hibiscus Tea", went viral that same month.<ref name="Meritocracy">{{Cite web|url=https://www.34st.com/article/2020/07/tiktok-music-streaming-spotify-record-label-penelope-scott-yoza-lizzy-mcalpine|title=Is TikTok creating a musical meritocracy?|website=[[34th Street Magazine|34th Street]]|last=Sagner|first=Denali|date=July 27, 2020|access-date=May 14, 2021|language=en}}</ref> Scott later expressed surprise at the success of ''The Junkyard 2'' given its lack of [[Audio mixing (recorded music)|mixing]]. Her college courses aided in her later mixing skills, though she also took courses in philosophy and computer science.<ref name="Shitstorm"/>

===2020-2021: ''Public Void''===
Her debut album, ''Public Void'', was released on [[Bandcamp]] on August 29, 2020, then on streaming services on September 25, 2020. In late 2020, Scott's music found a larger audience on the short-form video-sharing app [[TikTok]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.michigandaily.com/arts/screaming-void-tiktok-covid-and-penelope-scott/|title=Screaming into the void: TikTok, COVID and Penelope Scott|website=[[The Michigan Daily]]|last=Arp|first=Claire|date=November 4, 2020|access-date=April 7, 2021}}</ref> Her song "Rät" went viral in November 2020. The song is about disappointment with Silicon Valley and technology billionaires, including [[Elon Musk]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Penelope Scott Breaks Down The Meaning Of "Rät"|url=https://genius.com/a/penelope-scott-breaks-down-the-meaning-of-rat|last=Gebreyes|first=Rahel|date=Feb 5, 2021|website=[[Genius (website)|Genius]]|access-date=3 March 2021}}</ref> "Rät" peaked at 29 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Hot Rock & Alternative Songs|Hot Rock & Alternative Songs Chart]].<ref name="Rock" /> Music producer Jesse Cannon described its lyrics as "so extremely online".<ref name="Vox">{{cite web|url=https://www.vox.com/the-highlight/22538526/playlists-spotify-music-pop-stars-charts|title=The pop star versus the playlist|work=[[Vox (website)|Vox]]|last=Harding|first=Charlie|date=June 29, 2021|accessdate=July 15, 2021}}</ref> Tens of thousands of TikTok videos incorporate the song.<ref name="Shitstorm"/> On November 4, 2020, Scott released the single "Born2Run", which had gone viral prior to its official release.<ref name="Meritocracy" /> The song garnered attention after the [[2021 storming of the United States Capitol]], due to its lyrics describing a fictional storming of the U.S. capitol by politically-involved youth.<ref name="Shitstorm"/> On April 30, 2021, Scott was featured on the song "Brittle, Baby!" by Char Chris. She also appeared in its music video as an animated version of herself.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GBYewC8pjPU|title=Brittle, Baby! - Char Chris & Penelope Scott (Official Music Video)|website=[[YouTube]]|date=May 1, 2021|access-date=May 13, 2021|language=en}}</ref> 

===2021-present: ''Hazards'' EP===
In July 2021, Scott released the song "7 O'Clock" and announced an EP titled ''Hazards'', which released on August 27, 2021 through Many Hats Distribution.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nusoundonline.com/penelope-scott-releases-7-oclock-announces-hazards-ep/|title=Penelope Scott releases "7 O’Clock" announces ‘Hazards’ EP|website=Nu Sound|last=Hendricks|first=Nathaniel|date=27 July 2021|access-date=3 August 2021|language=en}}</ref> She released the song "Dead Girls" as a single a day before the release of the EP.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://americansongwriter.com/daily-discovery-penelope-scott-muses-on-youth-death-the-internet-age/|title=Daily Discovery: Penelope Scott Muses On Youth, Death, & The Internet Age|website=[[American Songwriter]]|last=Vitagliano|first=Joe|date=31 August 2021|access-date=5 September 2021|language=en}}</ref>

==Recognition==
In November 2020, she appeared on ''[[Rolling Stone]]''{{'}}s Breakthrough 25, which showcases artists with large gains in streaming numbers—Scott was fifth, with 5.4{{nbsp}}million streams that week.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/charts/breakthrough/2020-11-04/|title=Breakthrough 25|website=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=November 4, 2020|access-date=April 7, 2021}}</ref> Her song "Rät" appeared in the Top 40 of ''Billboard''{{'}}s Hot Rock & Alternative Songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/artist/penelope-scott/1513307072|title=About Penelope Scott|access-date=April 7, 2021|website=[[Apple Music]]}}</ref> ''Billboard''{{'}}s Danielle Chelosky said about her music, "Sonically, it's like being inside of a videogame; lyrically, it's like scrolling a [[Tumblr]] meme page of an edgy teenage girl."<ref name="Shitstorm">{{Cite web|title=Viral Phenomenon Penelope Scott's Songs Capture the S--tstorm of Internet Culture|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9542817/penelope-scott-interview-tiktok-rat/|last=Chelosky|first=Danielle|date=March 19, 2021|access-date=April 7, 2021|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|language=en}}</ref>

She has been described as an example of TikTok allowing for music artists to be successful without signing to a label; she reached 3{{nbsp}}million monthly listeners on [[Spotify]] without substantial media coverage.<ref name="Meritocracy" /><ref name="Vox"/> By March 2021, her music had been streamed an estimated 88{{nbsp}}million times in the United States.<ref name="Shitstorm"/>

==Musical style and influences==
Her music has been described as "baroque punk".<ref name="Vox"/> Danielle Chelosky of ''Billboard'' said that her music features aspects of [[hyperpop]]. Scott said that she has "been waiting for anyone to come up with an accurate description" of her genre of music.<ref name="Shitstorm"/>
