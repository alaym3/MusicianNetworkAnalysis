'''White Reaper''' is an American [[Garage punk (fusion genre)|garage punk]] band based in [[Louisville, Kentucky]], United States. The band is composed of Tony Esposito (guitar/vocals), Ryan Hater, Hunter Thompson, Nick Wilkerson, and Sam Wilkerson. The band has released two [[Extended play|EPs]] and three full-length albums, their first album in 2015 ''[[White Reaper Does It Again]]'',<ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web|last1=Heller|first1=Jason|title=White Reaper: White Reaper Does It Again|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/20794-white-reaper-does-it-again/|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|access-date=7 February 2017}}</ref><ref name="consequenceofsound">{{cite web|last1=Roffman|first1=Michael|title=White Reaper ‚Äì White Reaper Does It Again|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2015/07/album-review-white-reaper-white-reaper-does-it-again/|publisher=[[Consequence of Sound]]|access-date=7 February 2017}}</ref><ref name="nprmusic">{{cite web|last1=Mosurock|first1=Doug|title=Review: White Reaper, 'White Reaper Does It Again'|url=https://www.npr.org/2015/07/08/420827108/first-listen-white-reaper-white-reaper-does-it-again|publisher=[[NPR Music]]|access-date=7 February 2017}}</ref><ref name="pastemagazine">{{cite web|last1=Hayden|first1=Mack|title=White Reaper: White Reaper Does It Again Review|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2015/07/white-reaper-white-reaper-does-it-again-review.html|publisher=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]|access-date=7 February 2017}}</ref> and ''[[The World's Best American Band]]'' in 2017. White Reaper released their third studio album ''[[You Deserve Love]]'' on October 18, 2019, their major label debut.

== History ==

=== Early years (2012-2014) ===
White Reaper was formed by Tony Esposito and Nick Wilkerson while they were in high school. When they were 17 years old, they saw an entirely white [[Death (personification)|Grim Reaper]] prop in a Halloween Express, becoming known as White Reaper thereafter.<ref>{{Cite web|title=White Reaper Is the World‚Äôs Best American Band {{!}} Quick 6|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZcrbBvKs0cU|date=24 October 2019|website=YouTube|access-date=8 May 2020}}</ref> The duo then recorded two demos, DEMO 2012 and WHITE AURA in 2012. A [[Single (music)|7-inch]] featuring the songs "The Cut" and "Conspirator''"'' was released in 2013 on Earthbound Records.<ref>{{Cite web|title=White Reaper - Conspirator / The Cut 7" / Earthbound Records|url=http://earthboundrecords.bigcartel.com/product/white-reaper-conspirator-the-cut-7|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130813142403/http://earthboundrecords.bigcartel.com/product/white-reaper-conspirator-the-cut-7|url-status=dead|archive-date=2013-08-13|date=2013-08-13|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref> Wilkerson's twin brother Sam Wilkerson then joined the band as the bassist. Elementary school friend of Esposito and keyboardist Ryan Hater would also join the band.

=== Polyvinyl Records (2014-2018) ===
After signing to [[Polyvinyl Record Co.|Polyvinyl Records]] in early 2014, their label debut [[Extended play|EP]] White Reaper was released.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Polyvinyl Records|url=https://www.polyvinylrecords.com/artist/white_reaper|website=www.polyvinylrecords.com|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref> The band followed it with a tour with [[Young Widows]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=White Reaper {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/white-reaper-mn0003247637/biography|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref> In 2015, White Reaper's first studio album ''[[White Reaper Does It Again]]'' was released on [[Polyvinyl Record Co.|Polyvinyl Records]] and they opened for bands such as [[Twin Peaks (band)|Twin Peaks]] and [[Together Pangea]]. Guitarist Hunter Thompson joined the band in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=white reaper on Instagram: "This is Hunter. His last name is Thompson and he was born on the 4th of July. He's the newest member of White Reaper and you will treat him‚Ä¶"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BJtJkXjhiOM/|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref> Their second album ''[[The World's Best American Band]]'' was released in 2017. In 2018, White Reaper played at [[South by Southwest]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=white reaper on Instagram: "full sxsw schedule üå¥üòá what shows y'all coming to ??"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bf_XZy8hjo1/|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref> and toured as an opening act for [[Weezer]] and [[Billy Idol]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=white reaper on Instagram: "bringing our live show to cream city for this @weezer gig one night only Milwaukee, come thru #cheeseheadsunite üç¶üßÄüç¶"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/Bh681xWnWHY/|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=white reaper‚Äôs Instagram profile post: "üëÄ gigging w the OG @billyidol a wr favorite since the womb. is this real? did we die? alright guys guess it‚Äôs been fun"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BlDkylmnpdX/|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref>

=== Elektra years (2019-present) ===
In 2019, White Reaper would sign with [[Elektra Records]]. Three singles were released from their upcoming albums in Summer of 2019. The song "[[Might Be Right]]" became the band's highest charting single, peaking at number six on the [[Hot Rock Songs|US Hot Rock Songs]] chart and atop the [[Alternative Songs|US Alternative Songs]] chart.<ref>{{Citation|title=Might Be Right|date=2020-05-01|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Might_Be_Right&oldid=954312293|work=Wikipedia|language=en|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref> White Reaper would play at the music festival [[Riot Fest]] and with [[The Killers]]. In October 2019, [[You Deserve Love]] was released, their major label debut. White Reaper appeared on [[Jimmy Kimmel Live!]] in December 2019, playing "Might Be Right" and "Real Long Time".<ref>{{Cite web|title=White Reaper perform "Might Be Right" and "Real Long Time" on Kimmel: Watch|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2019/12/white-reaper-real-long-time-kimmel-watch/|date=2019-12-20|website=Consequence of Sound|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-09}}</ref> White Reaper contributed a cover of the Metallica song "[[Sad but True|Sad But True]]" to the charity tribute album ''[[The Metallica Blacklist]]'', released in September 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=He|first=Richard S.|date=2021-09-10|title=Every Metallica Blacklist cover ranked from worst to best|url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/every-metallica-blacklist-cover-ranked-from-worst-to-best|access-date=2021-10-22|website=loudersound|language=en}}</ref>
