'''Thomas Purcell''', known professionally as '''Wave Racer''', is an Australian musician, multi-instrumentalist and music producer.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.edmcanada.com/blog/2014/2/18/wave-racer-makes-his-triple-j-mix-available-as-a-free-download-for-only-24-hours|title=Wave Racer makes his Triple J mix available as a free download for only 24 hours|date=18 February 2014|work=EDM Canada|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lessthan3.com/news/electronic-music-genres-venturing-pigeon-hole|title=Genres Or Labels? Venturing Down The Pigeonhole|last=Kopp|first=Shea|date=29 April 2015|website=Lessthan3.com|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/listen-wave-racer-streamers/|title=LISTEN: Wave Racer – 'Streamers' – Music Feeds|last=Williams|first=Tom|date=11 February 2014|work=Music Feeds|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref> The name "Wave Racer" is adapted from the [[Nintendo 64]] game "[[Wave Race 64]]". His debut single, consisting of two songs, "Rock U Tonite" and "Stoopid", was released in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.insomniac.com/artist/Wave-Racer|title=Wave Racer|website=Insomniac.com|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nesthq.com/wave-racer-stoopid|title=Wave Racer – Stoopid – NEST HQ|date=20 July 2013|work=NEST HQ|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.youredm.com/2013/11/03/deep-bondax-wave-racer/|title=Into The Deep With Bondax & Wave Racer|last=Ward|first=Nick|date=3 November 2013|website=Your EDM|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref> The same year, he signed with Australian independent record label [[Future Classic]], who also signed other electronic artists including [[Cashmere Cat]] and [[Flume (musician)|Flume]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dancingastronaut.com/2016/03/future-classic-announces-one-million-label-compilation/|title=Future Classic announces 'One in a Million' label compilation|last=Hooks|first=Patrick|date=2 March 2016|website=dancingastronaut.com|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/51187/20151017/bass-house-producer-wave-racer-shares-debut-ep-flash-drive.htm|title=Australian Producer Wave Racer Shares Debut EP 'Flash Drive' [LISTEN]|last=Wojcik|first=Alexandria|date=17 October 2015|website=musictimes.com|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref>

His "Flash Drive" [[Extended play|EP]] was released in 2015, featuring a guest appearance by [[Lido (musician)|Lido]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/wave-racer-mn0003328159/biography|title=Wave Racer – Biography & History|last=Yeung|first=Neil|publisher=AllMusic|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/09/10-producers-that-should-be-scoring-video-games/wave-racer-2|title=10 Producers That Should Be Scoring Video GamesWave Racer|last=Brice|date=29 September 2014|website=Complex|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref> In 2021, he released his debut full-length album, ''To Stop From Falling Off The Earth''.<ref name=":1" />

== Early life and career ==
Purcell was born in Sydney, New South Wales on 26 April, 1992. He has cited his start in [[electronic dance music]] production as being part of a [[disco house]] duo with his friend called "Pablo J & The Lobsterettes".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://runthetrap.com/2015/07/21/rtt-exclusive-interview-basenji-talks-future-bass-upcoming-ep-and-touring/|title=RTT Exclusive Interview: Basenji talks Future Bass, Upcoming EP, and Touring|last=Chung|first=Max|date=21 July 2015|website=Run The Trap|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref> Speaking about the comparison of his music to the likes of [[Cashmere Cat]] and [[Hudson Mohawke]], Purcell quoted them as massive influences and said "...people often describe my music as [[future bass]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/03/wave-racer-interview|title=Wave Racer is More Than Just 'Future Bass'|last=Jake|date=28 March 2014|website=Complex|access-date=5 June 2017}}</ref>
