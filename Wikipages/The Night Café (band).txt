'''The Night Café''' is a British [[indie pop]]<ref name="iinag 2019">{{cite web |title=The Night Café – Indie pop upstarts from Liverpool |url=https://www.indieisnotagenre.com/artist/the-night-cafe/ |website=IINAG |accessdate=23 August 2019}}</ref> band from [[Liverpool]]. The band consists of Sean Martin (vocals/guitar), Josh Higgins (guitar), Arran O'Connell Whittle (bass) and Carl Dillon (drums). The band formed in 2013, released two EPs: ''Get Away from the Feeling'' and ''Bunkbed'' in 2017 and 2018, respectively, before releasing their debut studio album ''0151'' in 2019.

== History ==
The band's name comes from the Vincent van Gogh [[The Night Café|painting of the same name]] (originally known as ''Le Café de nuit'').<ref name="iinag 2019"/> The band formed in 2014 while the members were still in secondary school, and bonded over mutual musician interests such as [[Title Fight]] and [[Kings Of Leon]].<ref name="iinag 2019"/>

The quartet recorded their first singles, "Growing Up" and "Addicted" at the Whitewood Recording Studio in [[Liverpool]], which were both produced by Robert Whiteley. The band released their debut EP, ''Get Away From The Feeling'' in 2017, which was followed by opening for the band [[Sundara Karma]] during the summer 2017. The following year, the band released ''Bunkbed'', their second EP.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Freyaldenhoven |first1=Laura |title=It's album time for The Night Café: "We want to have a good time" |url=https://www.readdork.com/features/the-night-caf%C3%A9-interview-sept19 |website=Dork |accessdate=23 August 2019 |date=23 August 2019}}</ref>

On 8 May 2019, the band announced their debut studio album, ''0151'',<ref>{{cite web |title=The Night Café announce album '0151' |url=http://diymag.com/2019/05/08/the-night-cafe-announce-album-0151 |website=DIY |accessdate=23 August 2019 |date=8 May 2019}}</ref> which was released on 23 August 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Freyaldenhoven |first1=Laura |title=The Night Cafe - 0151 So infectious it should come with a doctor’s note |url=https://www.readdork.com/reviews/the-night-cafe-0151}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Mulhern |first1=Shaun |title=Album Review: The Night Café - '0151' |url=https://whenthehornblows.com/content/2019/8/21/album-review-the-night-cafe-0151 |website=When The Horn Blows |accessdate=23 August 2019 |date=21 August 2019}}</ref>
