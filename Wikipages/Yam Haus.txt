'''Yam Haus''' is an American [[Pop music|pop]] [[Band (rock and pop)|band]] formed by Lars Pruitt ([[Singing|vocals]]), Seth Blum ([[guitar]]), Jake Felstow ([[Drum kit|drums]]), and Zach Beinlich ([[Bass guitar|bass]]). Yam Haus has played alongside greats such as [[Adam Lambert]], [[Tori Kelly]], [[Kacey Musgraves]], and [[American Authors]]. Their 2018 debut album ''Stargazer'' has been streamed over 3.5 million times.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=Stokes·TRACKS·May 8|first1=Gem|last2=2020|date=2020-05-08|title=Introducing: Yam Haus|url=https://www.euphoriazine.com/blog/2020/05/music/tracks-introducing-yam-haus/|access-date=2021-05-23|website=EUPHORIA.|language=en-US}}</ref>

== History ==
Yam Haus was formed in 2017 in Minneapolis, Minnesota. Lars, Seth, and Zack all met at high school in Hudson, Wisconsin, and later met Jake in Minneapolis while he was attending college.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ballalatak|first=Katie|date=2019-04-03|title=Q&A with Hudson-based Yam Haus|url=https://www.minnesotamonthly.com/arts-entertainment/yam-haus-qa/|access-date=2021-05-24|website=Minnesota Monthly|language=en-US}}</ref> The band had started as a daily YouTube vlog to start building a fanbase.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Marsh|first=Steve|date=2020-05-17|title=Yam Haus on Their Uncertain Next Act|url=http://mspmag.com/api/content/6d9d345c-7e68-11ea-b52f-1244d5f7c7c6/|access-date=2021-05-24|website=Mpls.St.Paul Magazine|language=en-us}}</ref> Since their formation, Yam Haus have found themselves selling out shows across the Midwest, and they have been included on lineups such as First Avenue's "Best New Bands" of 2018; and the 2019 Basilica Block Party. Yam Haus also sold out their headlining show at First Avenue in December 2019.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Riley|first=Jill|title=Live Virtual Session: Yam Haus at Sounds Like Home|url=https://www.thecurrent.org/feature/2020/05/29/yam-haus-sounds-like-home-live-virtual-session|access-date=2021-05-23|website=www.thecurrent.org}}</ref>

Their first single, West Coast, was released April 13, 2018 as a precursor to their debut album, ''Stargazer.<ref name=":0" />'' In the winter of 2019 the quartet released their [[EP album|EP]] called ''Stargazer Sessions,'' which features acoustic versions of a number of songs from their debut album and "Spoke Too Soon".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yam Haus|url=https://www.exploreminnesota.com/event/yam-haus/23857|access-date=2021-05-23|website=Explore Minnesota|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-02-07|title=Yam Haus Trade Synths for Acoustic Guitars with Surprising Results|url=https://www.musicinminnesota.com/yam-haus-trade-synths-for-acoustic-guitars-with-surprising-results/|access-date=2021-05-24|website=Music In Minnesota|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Pop group Yam Haus challenging Mpls indie scene|url=https://www.kare11.com/article/news/pop-group-yam-haus-challenging-mpls-indie-scene/89-54fdf38e-879f-471e-b2be-9327f5823929|access-date=2021-05-24|website=kare11.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

In preparation for their 2020 summer headlining tour the group released their second EP, ''The Band Is Gonna Make It'', featuring their top track, "The Thrill" which was filmed at the popular theme park [[Valleyfair]]. Novocaine|url=https://www.thecurrent.org/feature/2021/04/09/yam-haus-novocaine|access-date=2021-05-24|website=www.thecurrent.org}}</ref> In the winter of 2020 Yam Haus released their first Christmas EP, ''A Very Yam Haus Christmas'', featuring the track "Auld Lang Syne".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yam Haus Releases Christmas EP|url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/music/basketball_nba/yam-haus-releases-christmas-ep/vp-BB1bS9E0|access-date=2021-05-24|website=www.msn.com}}</ref> The song gained recognition as the video featured footage, filmed by Jay Christiansen,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Writer|first=Loretta Harding/Contributing|title=Bird's-eye view to success|url=https://www.presspubs.com/white_bear/news/bird-s-eye-view-to-success/article_403e00e8-91d8-11eb-b0ad-27840479f883.html|access-date=2021-05-24|website=Press Publications|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-01|title=2020 wrap-up|url=https://kstp.com/news/2020-wrap-up-video/5965775/|access-date=2021-05-24|website=KSTP|language=en}}</ref> from the [[George Floyd protests in Minneapolis–Saint Paul|George Floyd protests]] in Minneapolis.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=December 31|first1=AbbeyPublished|last2=2020|title=Local Band Creates Emotional Minnesota Version of Auld Lang Syne|url=https://minnesotasnewcountry.com/local-band-creates-emotional-minnesota-version-of-auld-lang-syne/|access-date=2021-05-24|website=98.1 -
