'''Benjamin Daniele Wahlgren Ingrosso''' (born 14 September 1997) is a Swedish singer, songwriter and record producer. As a child, he appeared in the leading role in several musicals, and in 2006 he won [[Lilla Melodifestivalen]] with the song "Hej Sofia". He won the celebrity dancing TV show ''[[Let's Dance 2014]]'', and has competed twice in [[Melodifestivalen]] in [[Melodifestivalen 2017|2017]] and [[Melodifestivalen 2018|2018]] respectively. He won in 2018 and represented Sweden in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2018]] in [[Lisbon]], [[Portugal]], with the song "[[Dance You Off]]".

==Early life==
Ingrosso comes from a well known family in Sweden: his mother [[Pernilla Wahlgren]] is a singer known for her song "Piccadilly Circus" and his father [[Emilio Ingrosso]] is a restaurateur.<ref name="https://www.thelocal.se/20180509/five-things-you-need-to-know-about-eurovision-hopeful-benjamin-ingrosso-sweden-kardashians-wahlgren">{{cite web|url=https://www.thelocal.se/20180509/five-things-you-need-to-know-about-eurovision-hopeful-benjamin-ingrosso-sweden-kardashians-wahlgren|title=What you need to know (but are too cool to ask) about Eurovision hopeful Benjamin Ingrosso|date=9 May 2018|website=The Local|access-date=18 January 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119120920/https://www.thelocal.se/20180509/five-things-you-need-to-know-about-eurovision-hopeful-benjamin-ingrosso-sweden-kardashians-wahlgren|archive-date=19 January 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> He is a self-taught pianist and guitarist.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Benjamin Ingrosso - Sweden — Lisbon 2018|url=https://eurovision.tv/participant/benjamin-ingrosso|access-date=2020-10-25|website=Eurovision.tv|language=en|archive-date=25 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201125135901/https://eurovision.tv/participant/benjamin-ingrosso|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==

===2006–2013: Lilla Melodifestivalen 2006, singing and acting===
[[File:Markoolio & Benjamin Wahlgren 2009.jpg|170px|thumb|right|Ingrosso (right) performing on stage with [[Markoolio]] in 2009]]

Ingrosso has acted in several musicals and plays such as ''[[Nils Karlsson Pyssling]]''.<ref name="sydsvenskan.se">{{cite web|url=https://www.sydsvenskan.se/2017-02-08/ingrosso-skrev-av-sig-om-brustet-forhallande|title=Ingrosso skrev av sig om brustet förhållande|publisher=Sydsvenskan|access-date=7 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180308041718/https://www.sydsvenskan.se/2017-02-08/ingrosso-skrev-av-sig-om-brustet-forhallande|archive-date=8 March 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> He won the [[Lilla Melodifestivalen]] in 2006 with the song "Hej Sofia", and he represented Sweden in [[MGP Nordic 2006]] in [[Stockholm]], where he placed fourth.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://esctoday.com/161147/sweden-benjamin-ingrosso-reveals-video-clip-for-dance-you-off/|title=Eurovision Sweden: Benjamin Ingrosso reveals video clip for Dance you off! - ESCToday.com|date=12 March 2018|publisher=esctoday.com|access-date=24 April 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180425182845/http://esctoday.com/161147/sweden-benjamin-ingrosso-reveals-video-clip-for-dance-you-off/|archive-date=25 April 2018|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Benjamin Ingrosso|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/5748741-Benjamin-Ingrosso|access-date=2020-10-25|website=Discogs|language=en|archive-date=18 February 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210218205355/https://www.discogs.com/artist/5748741-Benjamin-Ingrosso|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2014–2017: ''Let's Dance 2014'' and Melodifestivalen===
[[File:Benjamin ingrosso.melodifestival2017.17d801.1100612.jpg|170px|thumb|right|Ingrosso performing at [[Melodifestivalen 2017]]]]

Ingrosso won the Swedish celebrity dancing TV-series ''[[Let's Dance 2014]]'', broadcast on [[TV4 (Sweden)|TV4]], alongside his professional dancing partner [[Sigrid Bernson]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = Benjamin Wahlgren vann Let's dance|url = http://www.gp.se/kulturnoje/1.2366123-benjamin-wahlgren-vann-let-s-dance|website = www.gp.se|access-date = 7 March 2017|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140511132010/http://www.gp.se/kulturnoje/1.2366123-benjamin-wahlgren-vann-let-s-dance|archive-date = 11 May 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> He has participated in his mother Pernilla Wahlgren's TV-series ''[[Wahlgrens värld]]'', which is broadcast on [[Kanal 5 (Sweden)|Kanal 5]], since 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2054&artikel=6908340|title=Swedish Eurovision star on celebrity families, bathrooms and writing hits - Radio Sweden|publisher=Sveriges Radio (Radio Sweden)|access-date=8 June 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180425120055/https://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2054&artikel=6908340|archive-date=25 April 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

Ingrosso participated in [[Melodifestivalen 2017]] with the song "[[Good Lovin' (Benjamin Ingrosso song)|Good Lovin']]", which he co-wrote along with [[Louis Schoorl]] and Matt Pardon.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/benjamin-ingrosso-i-topp-fem-i-melodifestivalen-2017-det-ar-helt-sjukt/|title=Benjamin Ingrosso i topp fem i Melodifestivalen 2017: "Det är helt sjukt!"|first=Moa Linette Lindahl, Gustav|last=Dahlander|access-date=7 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180308042336/https://www.svt.se/melodifestivalen/benjamin-ingrosso-i-topp-fem-i-melodifestivalen-2017-det-ar-helt-sjukt/|archive-date=8 March 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> He placed fifth in the final.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://esctoday.com/140884/sweden-mariette-benjamin-ingrosso-final-melodifestivalen-2017/|title=Eurovision Sweden: Mariette and Benjamin Ingrosso to the final of Melodifestivalen 2017! - ESCToday.com|date=11 February 2017|publisher=esctoday.com|access-date=7 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180308042457/http://esctoday.com/140884/sweden-mariette-benjamin-ingrosso-final-melodifestivalen-2017/|archive-date=8 March 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''En gång i tiden'' ===
Ingrosso took part in the eleventh series of Swedish [[reality television]] show, ''[[Så mycket bättre]],'' alongside multiple other Swedish artists including [[Loreen (singer)|Loreen]] and [[Tove Styrke]]. On 15 January 2021 he released his second studio album, ''[[En gång i tiden]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Benjamin Ingrosso låter unik på svenska|url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/a/0K95Ag|access-date=2021-01-15|website=Aftonbladet|language=sv|archive-date=17 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210417090832/https://www.aftonbladet.se/a/0K95Ag|url-status=live}}</ref> The album is entirely in Swedish and marks his first full album in the language. Speaking about the album on [[social media]], Ingrosso stated, "I love and miss singing in Swedish" and that the inspiration for the record came from his "love for Swedish pop and all of the great Swedish musicians that’s been my source of inspiration since [he] was a kid"<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Post by Benjamin Ingrosso|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CJod5amAAP7/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211029052913/https://www.instagram.com/static/bundles/es6/Consumer.css/c312b2da145f.css|archive-date=29 October 2021|access-date=2021-01-17|website=Instagram}}</ref> The second part of the album, ''[[En gång i tiden (del 2)]]'', was released on 16 April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ingrosso är glimrande och ojämn|url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/a/kRgMaQ|access-date=2021-04-17|website=Aftonbladet|language=sv|archive-date=17 April 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210417000620/https://www.aftonbladet.se/a/kRgMaQ|url-status=live}}</ref>
