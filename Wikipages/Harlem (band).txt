'''Harlem''' is an American [[garage rock]] band comprising [[vocalist]]/[[guitarist]]/[[drummer]] Michael Coomers, vocalist/guitarist/drummer Curtis O'Mara and bassist Jose Boyer, formerly of Chapel Hill-based [[The Gondoliers]] and The Kashmir.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.matadorrecords.com/harlem/biography.html |title=Harlem Biography |publisher=Matador Records |archivedate=2013-06-15 |url-status=bot: unknown |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130615163307/http://www.matadorrecords.com/harlem/biography.html }}</ref>

==History==
Harlem started in [[Tucson, AZ]] before relocating to [[Austin]] where they generated a mountain of attention, both with their live shows and their self-issued 2008 album ''Free Drugs ;-)'', mastered by Nathan Sabatino at Loveland Recording Studios.<ref>{{citation|last=Stevens|first=Andrew|title=New Upcoming Bands|work=Andrew Stevens}}</ref>

Matador signed the [[Austin, Texas]] trio to a multi-record, worldwide deal. Harlem recorded their 2nd album in the summer of 2009.<ref name="matadorrecords1">{{cite web|url=http://matablog.matadorrecords.com/2009/06/17/harlem-and-matador/ |title=Matablog |publisher=Matador Records |date=2009-06-17 |accessdate=2017-01-17}}</ref> ''[[Hippies (album)|Hippies]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/14093-hippies/|title=Harlem: Hippies Album Review {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> was released on April 6, 2010. It was recorded by Mike McHugh at "The Distillery" in Costa Mesa, California. The band supported [[The Dead Weather]] in July, 2010. As of April 2012, the band is on an indefinite hiatus as all members are busy with other projects.<ref name="matadorrecords1"/>

Coomers currently performs with Lace Curtains (also featuring [[Bloc Party|Bloc Party's]] [[Matt Tong]]), who have released the albums ''The Garden of Joy and the Well of Loneliness'' on 1 August 2009,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/17143-the-garden-of-joy-and-the-well-of-loneliness/|title=Lace Curtains: The Garden of Joy and the Well of Loneliness Album Review {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> and ''A Signed Piece of Paper'' on 28 October 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2014/10/album-review-lace-curtains-a-signed-piece-of-paper/|title=Album Review: Lace Curtains – A Signed Piece of Paper|date=2014-10-22|website=Consequence of Sound|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref>

O'Mara currently plays with Grape St., and Boyer is performing with Daytona<ref>{{cite web|url=http://austin.culturemap.com/news/entertainment/05-15-12-17-34-we-miss-harlem-but-we-love-their-side-projects/#slide=0|title=We miss rock band Harlem — but we love their side projects|website=CultureMap Austin|access-date=2016-06-21}}</ref> and Las Rosas.

Harlem released a new album ''[[Oh Boy (Harlem album)|Oh Boy]]'' in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gorillavsbear.net/premiere-harlem-cry-now-cry-later/ |title=premiere: Harlem – Cry Now Cry Later |website=Gorillavsbear.net |date= |accessdate=2020-03-07}}</ref>
