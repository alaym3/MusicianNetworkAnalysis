'''Elizabeth Mitchell''' (born '''Elizabeth Joanna Robertson''') is an American actress known for her lead role as [[Juliet Burke]] on the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] drama mystery series ''[[Lost (TV series)|Lost]]'' (2006–2010).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ew.com/article/2009/05/19/lost-scoop-juli/|title='Lost' scoop: Juliet mystery (sort of) solved|date=19 May 2009|website=Entertainment Weekly|first=Michael|last=Ausiello|access-date=December 6, 2019}}</ref> Mitchell also had lead roles on the television series ''[[V (2009 TV series)|V]]'' (2009–2010), ''[[Revolution (TV series)|Revolution]]'' (2012–2014), and ''[[Dead of Summer (TV series)|Dead of Summer]]'' (2016), as well as recurring roles as the [[The Snow Queen|Snow Queen]] on ''[[Once Upon a Time (TV series)|Once Upon a Time]]'' (2014) and Anna Volovodov on ''[[The Expanse (TV series)|The Expanse]]'' (2018).

Mitchell is also known for her roles in numerous films, including ''[[Gia]]'' (1998), ''[[Frequency (2000 film)|Frequency]]'' (2000), ''[[Nurse Betty]]'' (2000), ''[[The Santa Clause 2]]'' (2002), ''[[The Santa Clause 3: The Escape Clause]]'' (2006), ''[[Running Scared (2006 film)|Running Scared]]'' (2006), ''[[Answers to Nothing (film)|Answers to Nothing]]'' (2011), and ''[[The Purge: Election Year]]'' (2016).

==Early life==
Mitchell was born in [[Los Angeles]], California.{{Citation needed |date=July 2021}} Her stepfather, Joseph Day Mitchell, and mother, Josephine Marian Mitchell (née Jenkins), are lawyers based in Dallas.{{Citation needed |date=July 2021}} Mitchell and her mother moved to [[Dallas]], Texas in 1970, where her mother married Joseph Mitchell in 1975.<ref>Texas Department of State Health Services. Texas Marriage Index, 1966–2002. Texas Department of State Health Services, Texas.</ref> Mitchell graduated from [[Booker T. Washington High School for the Performing and Visual Arts]], a public [[magnet school]]. She is the eldest of three sisters, the others being Kristina Helen "Kristie" Mitchell and Katherine Day "Kate" Mitchell.<ref name=myHeritage>{{cite web | url=https://www.myheritage.com/research/record-10182-58122/elizabeth-mitchell-in-biographical-summaries-of-notable-people | title=Elizabeth Mitchell - Biographical Summaries of Notable People - MyHeritage | author=<!--General data sheet; no single author indicated.--> | website=MyHeritage.com | access-date= 24 December 2016 }}</ref>

She attended [[Stephens College]], graduating with a [[Bachelor of Fine Arts]] in acting, and then studied at the [[British American Drama Academy]].<ref name=SouthBeauty>{{cite web |url=http://www.sobeautymag.com/southern-beauties/elizabeth-mitchell/ |title=Southern Beauty Magazine " Elizabeth Mitchell |publisher=www.sobeautymag.com |access-date=2010-11-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101222043233/http://www.sobeautymag.com/southern-beauties/elizabeth-mitchell/ |archive-date=December 22, 2010}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Elizabeth Mitchell & Stephen Collins (13923732591).jpg|thumb|234x234px|Mitchell at a [[Revolution (TV series)|Revolution]] panel, at [[WonderCon]] in 2014, with actor [[Stephen Collins]]]]
Mitchell's theatre work includes six years at [[Dallas Theater Center]] and a season at Encore Theater.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-08-22|title=Empire|url=https://www.empireonline.com/people/elizabeth-mitchell/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=Empire|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Stephens College graduate Elizabeth Mitchell has taken one of TV’s juiciest roles on ‘Lost’ and run with it, setting the sky as the limit - Lifestyle - Columbia Daily Tribune - Columbia, MO|url=https://webcache.googleusercontent.com/search?q=cache:qPXhTRF-5W0J:https://www.columbiatribune.com/article/20080131/Lifestyle/301319832+&cd=17&hl=en&ct=clnk&gl=ie|access-date=2021-01-31|website=webcache.googleusercontent.com}}</ref><ref name="SouthBeauty" />

She had an early TV role as Dinah Lee on the soap opera ''[[Loving (TV series)|Loving]]'' from 1994 to 1995.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Mistretta|first=Amy|date=2016-02-24|title=Soap Opera Actors: Where are they now? Faith Ford returns for The Bridge: Part Two, as Kelly Ripa celebrates 15 year milestone|url=https://soaps.sheknows.com/soaps/news/46761/soap-opera-actors-where-are-they-now-faith-ford-returns-for-the-bridge-part-two-as-kelly-ripa-celebrates-15-year-milestone/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=Soaps.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

She co-starred in the 1998 movie ''[[Gia]]'' and the 2000 movie ''[[Frequency (2000 film)|Frequency]]''.

Other movie roles include ''[[The Santa Clause 2]]'' (2002), ''[[The Santa Clause 3: The Escape Clause]]'' (2006), ''[[Running Scared (2006 film)|Running Scared]]'' (2006), and ''[[Answers to Nothing (film)|Answers to Nothing]]'' (2011).

On TV, she played psychiatrist [[Kim Legaspi]], the first woman partner of [[Kerry Weaver]] during the 2000–2001<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Mitovich|first=Matt Webb|date=2020-10-23|title=Elizabeth Mitchell Joins Outer Banks|url=https://tvline.com/2020/10/22/elizabeth-mitchell-cast-outer-banks-season-2/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=TVLine|language=en-US}}</ref> season of TV series ''[[ER (TV series)|ER]],'' in a recurring role. She also had a one-episode role in ''[[House M.D.]]'' as a patient.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dr. Kim Legaspi, ER|url=https://lgbtfansdeservebetter.com/character/dr-kim-legaspi-er/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=LGBT Fans Deserve Better|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Newspapers|first=Gail Shister, Knight Ridder|title='ER' ACTRESS DIGS HER CHARACTER'S LESBIAN RELATIONSHIP|url=https://www.orlandosentinel.com/news/os-xpm-2001-05-05-0105040459-story.html|access-date=2021-01-31|website=OrlandoSentinel.com|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=House M.D.: The Complete First Season (Disc 01)|url=https://www.lowpro.net/phpdvdprofiler/index.php?mediaid=I879D0AB37241ECC9.4&action=show|access-date=2021-01-31|website=www.lowpro.net}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-23|title=40 Celebrities You Probably Forgot Were on ER|url=https://www.pulse.com.gh/lifestyle/mens-health/40-celebrities-you-probably-forgot-were-on-er/dbzk874|access-date=2021-01-31|website=Pulse Ghana|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2006, she played [[Juliet Burke]] on ''Lost'', beginning in [[A Tale of Two Cities (Lost)|the third season premiere]], a role she would play for four seasons, three as a main character and one in a recurring capacity.

In March 2009, Mitchell was cast in the ABC series ''[[V (2009 TV series)|V]]'', a remake of the [[V (1983 miniseries)|science-fiction television miniseries]]. Although ABC and [[Warner Bros.]] officials said she was only cast as a guest star, the announcement led to speculation and concern that Mitchell's character would be killed off at the end of [[Lost (season 5)|''Lost''<nowiki>'</nowiki>s fifth season]],<ref>{{Cite news|title=Exclusive: Elizabeth Mitchell trading "Lost" for "V"? |last=Ausiello |first=Michael |work=Entertainment Weekly |url=https://ew.com/article/2009/03/20/exclusive-eliza/|date=2009-03-20 |access-date=2019-12-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090322011320/http://ausiellofiles.ew.com/2009/03/exclusive-eliza.html |archive-date=2009-03-22 }}</ref> which ended on a cliff-hanger that left the fate of her character unknown. Mitchell was later named the lead actress on ''V''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abcmedianet.com/DNR/2009/abc_20092010_schedule.pdf |title=ABC Unveils 2009–2010 Primetime Schedule |publisher=[[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] Medianet |author=McPherson, Stephen |date=May 19, 2009 |access-date=May 19, 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090611203141/http://abcmedianet.com/DNR/2009/abc_20092010_schedule.pdf |archive-date=June 11, 2009 }}</ref> and her character was indeed written out in the premiere of the sixth season of ''Lost'', but returned for the two-part series finale.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Elizabeth Mitchell, 'Lost' producers talk about Juliet's fate|url=https://ew.com/article/2009/11/02/elizabeth-mitchell-lost-producers-talk-about-juliets-fate/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=EW.com|language=EN}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Elizabeth Mitchell reflects on fan-reaction to 'Lost' finale|url=https://ew.com/comic-con/2016/07/22/lost-elizabeth-mitchell-comic-con/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=EW.com|language=EN}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Elizabeth Mitchell on Juliet’s ‘Lost’ fate|url=http://www.today.com/popculture/elizabeth-mitchell-juliet-s-lost-fate-wbna33588639|access-date=2021-01-31|website=TODAY.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Harnick|first=Chris|date=2012-09-17|title=Elizabeth Mitchell Previews 'Revolution' & Looks Back At 'Lost' Finale|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/revolution-elizabeth-mitchell-lost-finale_n_1881998|access-date=2021-01-31|website=HuffPost|language=en}}</ref> ''V'' lasted for one more season, which premiered on January 4, 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|title='V' canceled. Elizabeth Mitchell free.|url=https://ew.com/article/2011/05/13/v-canceled-elizabeth-mitchell/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=EW.com|language=EN}}</ref>

Mitchell had a guest-starring role on ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'' in 2011, portraying June Frye.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tvguide.com/News/Kecks-Exclusives-Elizabeth-1030402.aspx|title=Keck's Exclusives: Elizabeth Mitchell Turns Evil on SVU|work=TV Guide|first=William|last=Keck|date=March 7, 2011|access-date=March 7, 2011}}</ref> In 2012, she joined the cast of the series ''[[Revolution (TV series)|Revolution]]<ref>{{cite web|title='Revolution': Elizabeth Mitchell on Sticking With Genre Fare and Her Biggest Surprise (Q&A)|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/revolution-elizabeth-mitchell-370773|website=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=23 September 2016}}</ref>'' as Rachel Matheson, replacing actress [[Andrea Roth]]. The series premiered on September 17, 2012<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/revolution-nbc-elizabeth-mitchell-lost-jj-abrams-343674.html |title='Revolution's' 'Lost' Reunion: Elizabeth Mitchell Joins J.J. Abrams Drama as Series Regular |last=Goldberg |first=Lesley |date=June 30, 2012|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|access-date=June 30, 2012}}</ref> and ended in May 2014. She then recurred as the [[The Snow Queen|Snow Queen]] on the TV fantasy series ''[[Once Upon a Time (TV series)|Once Upon a Time]]'' in late 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title='Once Upon a Time' star Elizabeth Mitchell spills scoop on the Snow Queen|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2014/10/13/once-upon-time-elizabeth-mitchell-snow-queen-spoilers|website=Entertainment Weekly|access-date=23 September 2016}}</ref>

[[File:Elizabeth Mitchell game.jpg|thumb|Mitchell in [[Seattle]] 2008 supporting the [[Boston Red Sox]]]]
In 2016, Mitchell starred as U.S. senator Charlene "Charlie" Roan in the science-fiction horror film ''[[The Purge: Election Year]]''. Also that year, it was announced Mitchell would join the main cast of the [[Freeform (TV channel)|Freeform]] supernatural horror series ''[[Dead of Summer (TV series)|Dead of Summer]]'', portraying Deb Carpenter. The series ended after one season.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Wagmeister|first1=Elizabeth|title=‘Once Upon a Time’ Alums Elizabeth Mitchell & Elizabeth Lail Cast in Freeform’s ‘Dead of Summer’|url=https://variety.com/2016/tv/news/dead-of-summer-casting-elizabeth-mitchell-elizabeth-lail-freeform-1201701827/|website=variety.com|access-date=23 September 2016|date=10 February 2016}}</ref>

In 2018, Mitchell played Anna Volovodov on season 3 of ''[[The Expanse (TV series)|The Expanse]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-22|title=Lost: What has the cast been up to since the Island?|url=https://www.flickeringmyth.com/2020/09/lost-what-has-the-cast-been-up-to-since-the-island/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=Flickering Myth|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=The Expanse 3x02 IFF mit Episodenkritik|url=https://www.serienjunkies.de/the-expanse/3x02-iff.html|language=de|access-date=2021-01-31}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Miller|first=Liz Shannon|date=2018-04-19|title=‘The Expanse’: The Syfy Drama Takes On TV Faith in a New Way, By Actually Using Real Religions|url=https://www.indiewire.com/2018/04/expanse-season-3-methodists-anna-volovodov-spoilers-1201954680/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=IndieWire|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=O'Neil|first=Shana|date=2017-07-22|title=Lost star Elizabeth Mitchell boarding The Expanse for Season 3|url=https://www.syfy.com/syfywire/elizabeth-mitchell-and-david-strathairn-will-join-the-expanse-in-season-3|access-date=2021-01-31|website=SYFY WIRE|language=en}}</ref>

She will be in the upcoming movie ''[[A Very Senior High]]''<ref>{{Cite web|last=McNary|first=Dave|date=2018-08-03|title=Film News Roundup: Elizabeth Mitchell Joins Ellen Burstyn’s ‘Welcome to Pine Grove!’|url=https://variety.com/2018/film/news/elizabeth-mitchell-ellen-burstyn-welcome-to-pine-grove-1202893394/|access-date=2021-01-31|website=Variety|language=en-US}}</ref> and has joined the second season of the [[Netflix]] series ''[[Outer Banks (TV series)|Outer Banks]].''<ref name=":0" />

==Personal life==
While filming ''[[The Linda McCartney Story]]'' in 2000, Mitchell and co-star [[Gary Bakewell]] began dating and later became engaged, but the relationship ended in 2002.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://elizabeth-mitchell.org/media/europe-intelligence-wire-lmcs-review/|title=Europe Intelligence Wire – Interview with Gary Bakewell|website=Elizabeth Mitchell Fan Club|access-date=2016-08-07}}</ref>

Mitchell married improvisation actor Chris Soldevilla in 2004 and the two lived together in [[Bainbridge Island, Washington]], with their son, who was born in 2005.<ref>{{cite magazine |url= https://people.com/archive/losts-mystery-woman-elizabeth-mitchell-vol-69-no-6/ |title=Lost's Mystery Woman Elizabeth Mitchell|magazine= [[People (magazine)|People]]|access-date=2019-12-06|date= 2008-02-18|first=Monica|last=Rizzo}}</ref> In 2013, Mitchell and Soldevilla divorced due to irreconcilable differences.{{citation needed|date=January 2020}} In 2014, Mitchell purchased a ranch in [[Bainbridge Island, Washington]].
