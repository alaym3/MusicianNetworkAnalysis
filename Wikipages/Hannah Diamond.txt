'''Hannah Amond''' (born 20 June 1991),<ref>{{cite tweet|user=Hannahdiamond_|number=745329268163551233|date=21 June 2016|title=Thank u everyone for all the birthday wishes yesterday!!}}</ref> known professionally as '''Hannah Diamond''', is an English singer, songwriter, photographer, and visual artist. She has recorded on [[A.G. Cook]]'s [[PC Music]] label since 2013, beginning with her debut single "[[Pink and Blue]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://giantmenmanagement.com/2014/11/hannah-diamond-is-the-1-most-blogged-artist-in-the-world-today/|title=Hannah Diamond is the number 1 most blogged artist in the world today |publisher=Giant Men Management |date=29 November 2014 |accessdate=29 November 2014}}</ref> Her music and visual art employs an aesthetic of [[hyperreality]], heavily produced [[cuteness]] in tension with [[sincerity]].<ref name="vice-bassil">{{cite web |url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/blog/the-weird-and-wonderful-world-of-pc-music |title=Trying to Make Sense of Hannah Diamond and Post-Ringtone Music |last=Bassil |first=Ryan |date=23 May 2014 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |accessdate=19 January 2015}}</ref> Her debut studio album, ''[[Reflections (Hannah Diamond album)|Reflections]],'' was released in 2019. Diamond has also produced visual artwork and [[promotional material]]s for other artists and publications.<ref name="dazed-and-confused"/>

==Music career==
===2012–2015: Beginnings===
Diamond met [[GFOTY]] through a friend, and GFOTY introduced her to [[A. G. Cook]].<ref name="bbc-stephens">{{cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p02mrwmt |title=PC Music Interview |last=Stephens |first=Huw |date=25 March 2015 |publisher=[[BBC Radio 1]] |access-date=25 March 2015}}</ref> After a vocalist failed to show up to a session with Cook, the two worked on their first musical collaboration. In 2012, they recorded Diamond's first solo song, titled "Attachment".<ref name="vice-weinstock">{{cite web |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_gb/article/hannah-diamond-and-gfotys-beginners-guide-to-pc-music |title=hannah diamond and gfoty's beginner's guide to pc music |last=Weinstock |first=Tish |date=12 February 2015 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |access-date=12 February 2015}}</ref> Diamond released her debut single "[[Pink and Blue]]" through PC Music in October 2013. The song resembles a lullaby, with a harshly synthetic quality.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://diymag.com/archive/the-neu-bulletin-20th-november-2013 |title=The Neu Bulletin (20th November 2013) |last=Milton |first=Jamie |date=20 November 2013 |website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref> The attention received by "Pink and Blue" helped to introduce the fledgling label,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.completemusicupdate.com/article/cmu-artists-of-the-year-2014-pc-music/ |title=CMU Artists Of The Year 2014: PC Music |last=Barchi |first=Aly |date=12 December 2014 |website=[[Complete Music Update]] |access-date=8 February 2015}}</ref> and it placed 5th on ''Fact''{{'}}s list of "The 100 best tracks of the decade so far".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.factmag.com/2014/11/13/the-100-best-tracks-of-the-decade-so-far/97/ |title=The 100 best tracks of the decade so far |date=13 November 2014 |website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref>

The following January, Diamond appeared on A. G. Cook's "Keri Baby". The song toyed with the idea of Diamond as an [[MP3]] file or a digital entity on a screen.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.factmag.com/2014/08/13/pc-music-the-10-best-tracks-so-far-from-2014s-most-love-or-hate-record-label/7/ |title=PC Music: the 10 best tracks so far from 2014's most divisive record label |last=Moynihan |first=Joe |date=13 August 2014 |website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]] |access-date=8 February 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.theawl.com/2014/11/pc-music-forever |title=PC Music Forever |last=Cliff |first=Aimee |date=21 November 2015 |website=[[The Awl]] |access-date=8 February 2015}}</ref> Diamond made her first live performance in April, at Basement in London.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://diymag.com/archive/hannah-diamond-confirms-debut-live-appearance |title=Hannah Diamond Confirms Debut Live Appearance |last=Milton |first=Jamie |date=25 March 2014 |website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |access-date=8 February 2015}}</ref> She released "Attachment" as her second single later that month. "Attachment" is a melancholy ballad about modern relationships, with a high-pitched melody backed by farcical harmonies.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.factmag.com/2014/04/24/download-hannah-diamond-attachment-pc-music/ |title=PC Music pop star Hannah Diamond drops digital ballad 'Attachment' |date=24 April 2014 |website=[[Fact (UK magazine)|Fact]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/16898-attachment/ |title=Hannah Diamond: 'Attachment' |last=Lau |first=Melody |date=29 April 2014 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref>

Diamond's third solo single "Every Night" was released in November 2014 and became PC Music's first single available through the [[iTunes Store]].<ref name="billboard-anderson">{{cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/real-time-charts/6363534/emerging-picks-of-the-week-12-05-14 |title=Will Butler, Against the Current & Hannah Diamond: Emerging Picks of the Week |last=Anderson |first=Trevor |date=4 December 2014 |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref> The song discusses feelings of desire and showcases a more bold personality than Diamond's earlier songs.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/hannah-diamond-new-song-every-night-2014 |title=Hannah Diamond Released a Cute Song Called 'Every Night' That Perfectly Captures the Feeling of Falling for Someone |last=Bassil |first=Ryan |date=24 November 2014 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref> Her vocals are childlike and staccato, supported by "oh-ooh-oh" harmonies.<ref name="billboard-anderson"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://exclaim.ca/Music/article/hannah_diamond-every_night |title=Hannah Diamond 'Every Night' |last=Murphy |first=Sarah |date=24 November 2014 |website=[[Exclaim!]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref> The production drew comparisons to [[La Bouche]] and "[[Call Me Maybe]]" by [[Carly Rae Jepsen]].<ref name="billboard-anderson"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/soundclouds-of-the-underground-holiday-party-edition/ |title=SoundClouds of the Underground: Holiday Party Edition |last=Chapman |first=Alex |year=2014 |website=[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref> "Every Night" received nearly 200,000 plays on [[SoundCloud]] within two weeks, and it became Diamond's first song to appear on a [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' chart]], reaching 28th on the [[Billboard Twitter Real-Time|Emerging Artists]] chart.<ref name="billboard-anderson"/>

March 2015 saw Diamond head to the US to play the [[PC Music]] showcase at the Empire Garage in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], Texas as part of [[SXSW]]. Her performance was very well received with [[The Guardian]] calling it a "well-crafted performance, as much about the aesthetic and choreography as the [[Camp (style)|camp]] songs"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/mar/20/pc-music-sxsw-good-taste-pop-makeover|title=PC Music at SXSW review – good taste goes out the window in pop makeover|last=Ellis-Petersen|first=Hannah|date=20 March 2015|publisher=[[Guardian Media Group]]|access-date=22 December 2016|work=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> and according to ''[[Flavorwire]]'' was the one "who impressed the most."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.flavorwire.com/510450/from-online-to-irl-inside-pc-musics-debut-live-show-at-sxsw|title=From Online to IRL: Inside PC Music's Debut Live Show at SXSW|website=Flavorwire}}</ref> On 8 May 2015, Diamond performed as part of a PC Music show at [[BRIC House]] in Brooklyn, New York as part of the [[Red Bull Music Academy]] Festival. The show was billed as the premiere of Pop Cube, "a multimedia reality network".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Pareles|first1=Jon|title=Review: PC Music and Sophie in a High-Concept Extravaganza at BRIC House|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/05/11/arts/music/review-pc-music-and-sophie-in-a-high-concept-extravaganza-at-bric-house.html?_r=0|website=The New York Times|accessdate=13 November 2016|date=10 May 2015}}</ref> In November 2015, Diamond released her song "Hi" with a music video made in conjunction with ''[[i-D]]'' magazine. After having the resources to produce her first music video, she selected "Hi" as a "way of tying up my older material, and a good way of introducing my next phase".<ref name="whitehouse-id">{{Cite web|title = hi, it's hannah diamond {{!}} read {{!}} i-D|url = https://i-d.vice.com/en_gb/article/hi-its-hannah-diamond |last=Whitehouse |first=Matthew |date=5 November 2015 |website = i-D|accessdate = 12 November 2015}}</ref> Diamond had begun recording a full-length album, originally planned for release in 2015.<ref name="whitehouse-id"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.stereogum.com/1739776/a-g-cook-drop-fm-feat-hannah-diamond/ |title=A. G. Cook – 'Drop FM' (Feat. Hannah Diamond) |last=Rettig |first=James |date=19 February 2015 |website=[[Stereogum]] |access-date=1 March 2015}}</ref>

===2016–present: ''Reflections''===
February 2016 saw Hannah Diamond collaborate with [[Charli XCX]] on a new track titled "Paradise", which featured on XCX's [[Vroom Vroom (EP)|''Vroom Vroom EP'']], the first release on XCX's label Vroom Vroom Recordings. According to the singer, the label will combine her "love for bubblegum pop with mystery and darkness."<ref>{{Cite web
| url = http://www.factmag.com/2016/02/26/charli-xcx-hannah-diamond-paradise-sophie-vroom-vroom/
| title = Charli XCX and Hannah Diamond unleash rave-ready 'Paradise'
| website = FACT Magazine: Music News, New Music.
| access-date = 29 February 2016
}}</ref> In October that year, Diamond released the new single "Fade Away" with a lyric video via PC Music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2016/10/09/hannah-diamond-fade-away|title=Hannah Diamond Shares Addictive And Glossy Track "Fade Away"|website=The FADER}}</ref> On 22 December 2016, Diamond released a free single, "Make Believe", which was produced by easyFun and A.G. Cook.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1917488/hannah-diamond-make-believe/music/|title=Hannah Diamond – "Make Believe"|last=Breihan|first=Tom|date=22 December 2016|website=[[Stereogum]]|publisher=[[SpinMedia|SpinMusic]]|access-date=22 December 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://makebelieve.pcmusic.info/#info|title=Make Believe|last=|first=|date=|website=[[PC Music]]|publisher=|access-date=22 December 2016}}</ref>

On 13 December 2017, she released a new mix, ''Soon I won't see you at all,'' which contains three new tracks, one of which is a cover of "Concrete Angel" by [[Gareth Emery]]. It was made available on YouTube for streaming with a link to a ZIP file of the mix in the description.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1975502/stream-hannah-diamonds-soon-i-wont-be-here-at-all-mix/music/album-stream/|title=Stream Hannah Diamond's Soon I Won't See You At All Mix|date=13 December 2017}}</ref> On 16 November 2018, Diamond released "True", which is the lead single from her debut album ''[[Reflections (Hannah Diamond album)|Reflections]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2023017/hannah-diamond-true/music/|title=Hannah Diamond - "True"|date=15 November 2018|work=Stereogum|access-date=16 November 2018}}</ref>

On 17 September 2019, Diamond released the song "Part of Me" with [[Danny L Harle]]. Just over a month later, on 30 October, Diamond released the single "Invisible" with its accompanying music video. With this release, she also announced the release date for ''[[Reflections (Hannah Diamond album)|Reflections]]'' and revealed the album artwork. On 13 November, Diamond released another single leading up to the release of her album, titled "Love Goes On"''.'' The song was released with an accompanying music video.

On 31 October 2019, she announced her first headlining tour "The Invisible Tour" and added new dates on 1 November 4 and 28 November 2019. The tour began on 4 December 2019. On 22 November 2019, her debut album [[Reflections (Hannah Diamond album)|''Reflections'']] was released, and the tracklist was revealed the day before. On 5 February 2020, she announced that she will be the opening act in 4 shows for [[Carly Rae Jepsen]]’s [[Dedicated Tour]]. The ''Reflections'' remix album was released in April 2020,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2020/04/22/listen-hannah-diamond-remixes-debut-album-reflections|title=Hannah Diamond releases remixes from debut album 'Reflections'|last=Subscribe|website=diymag.com|language=en|access-date=2020-04-22}}</ref> and the vinyl was released in early 2020. 

In 2020, Diamond featured on the remix for [[100 Gecs|100 gecs]]' "xXXi_wud_nvrstøp_ÜXXx" alongside Estonian rapper [[Tommy Cash (rapper)|Tommy Cash]], and appeared on A. G. Cook's two 2020 albums ''[[7G (album)|7G]]'' and ''[[Apple (A. G. Cook album)|Apple]]''. In 2021, Diamond appeared on the Danny L Harle single, "Boing Beat", from his debut album ''[[Harlecore]]'', and featured on the remix for A. G. Cook's "The Darkness", alongside [[Sarah Bonito]] of [[Kero Kero Bonito]]. She appeared on labelmate [[Namasenda]]'s debut ''[[Unlimited Ammo]]'' mixtape in October 2021, and designed much of the project's single and album artwork.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Namasenda: All guns blazing|url=https://crackmagazine.net/article/profiles/namasenda-all-guns-blazing/|access-date=2021-11-26|website=Crack Magazine}}</ref> 

==Visual artistry==
Growing up, Diamond followed the work of fashion photographers such as [[Mert and Marcus]], [[David LaChapelle]], and [[Nick Knight (photographer)|Nick Knight]].<ref name="vice-weinstock"/> She studied fashion communication and styling, and her early work focused on [[internet celebrities]].<ref name="diy-hunt">{{cite web |url=http://diymag.com/2014/06/11/inside-the-hard-drive-of-pc-music |title=Inside the hard drive of PC Music |last=Hunt |first=El |date=11 June 2014 |website=[[DIY (magazine)|DIY]] |accessdate=21 January 2015}}</ref> She is a member of Diamond Wright, which has made promotional images for [[QT (musician)|QT]], latex clothing brand Meat, and PC Music artist Princess Bambi.<ref name="pitchfork-sherburne"/> In 2015, she shot Charli XCX in global commercial campaigns for Boohoo and [[Lynx (deodorant)|Lynx]] Impulse.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Diamond Wright on Twitter|url = https://twitter.com/DiamondWright_/status/652606780380422144|website = Twitter|accessdate = 4 November 2015}}</ref> Before the founding of PC Music, Diamond worked with Cook on marketing material for makeup brand Illamasqua.<ref name="diy-hunt"/> She has also done work as co-editor and director of photography for ''LOGO Magazine''.<ref name="pitchfork-sherburne"/>

She cited the futuristic visuals for [[TLC (group)|TLC]]'s "[[Waterfalls (TLC song)|Waterfalls]]" music video as influential for their emphasis on technology.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://thump.vice.com/words/hannah-diamond-knows-meta-is-better |title=Hannah Diamond Knows Meta is Better |last=Harrison |first=Angus |date=3 February 2015 |website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |accessdate=3 February 2015}}</ref> Diamond's music is often inspired by high-definition imagery and fashion campaigns.<ref name="bbc-stephens"/> Cook describes her as an artist "in control of [her] own image", noting Diamond's involvement in producing the promotional material.<ref name="dazed-and-confused">{{cite journal|last=Jones |first=Charlie Robin|url=http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/21588/1/pc-musics-digital-dreams|title=PC Music's Digital Dream |website=[[Dazed & Confused (magazine)|Dazed & Confused]] |date=11 September 2014 |volume=4 |pages=178–183 |accessdate=13 October 2014}}</ref> Her cover artwork uses heavy photo retouching to produce unnatural, [[hyperreality (art)|hyperreal]] versions of herself.<ref name="diy-hunt"/><ref name="pitchfork-joyce">{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/17234-hannah-diamond-every-night/ |title=Hannah Diamond: 'Every Night' |last=Joyce |first=Colin |date=24 November 2014 |work=[[Pitchfork Media]] |accessdate=24 January 2015}}</ref> The covers show Diamond in front of empty, one-dimensional spaces.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pitchfork.com/thepitch/714-pc-musics-inverted-consumerism/ |title=PC Music's Inverted Consumerism |last=Geffen |first=Sasha |date=30 March 2015 |publisher=[[Pitchfork Media]] |access-date=30 March 2015}}</ref> Her outfits are influenced by London [[streetwear]], and she is known for wearing her trademark pastel pink puffa jacket.<ref name="dazed-and-confused"/><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vogue.com/1539827/what-is-pc-music-sophie-qt-ag-book/ |title=A Visual Primer on PC Music, London's Weirdest New Subculture |last=Frank |first=Alex |date=19 September 2014 |website=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]] |accessdate=24 January 2015}}</ref> January 2016 saw Diamond feature on the cover of OKgrl, a new online platform created by stylist Louby Mcloughlin & DVTK, the ex digital directors of fashion brand [[Kenzo (brand)|Kenzo]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = OKgrl is here! {{!}} Wonderland Magazine|url = http://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2016/01/okgrl/|website = Wonderland Magazine|access-date = 15 February 2016|language = en-US}}</ref> Diamond has also spent time designing typefaces, including a new font which was featured in the lyric video for her song, "True".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/hannah-diamond-announces-new-album-reflections-shares-new-song-true-listen/|title=Hannah Diamond Announces New Album Reflections, Shares New Song "True": Listen {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=18 November 2018}}</ref>

As a photographer, Diamond's work includes a 2018 cover feature for [[DIY (magazine)|DIY]], featuring [[Years & Years]]' [[Olly Alexander]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = LET IT BURN: YEARS & YEARS {{!}} DIY Magazine|url = http://diymag.com/2018/04/27/let-it-burn-years-and-years-palo-santo-cover-feature-olly-alexander|website = DIY mag|access-date = 14 August 2019|language = en-US}}</ref> So far in 2019, Diamond has shot for [[Sophia Webster]]'s Spring/Summer 2019 campaign,<ref>{{Cite web|title = Wedding Crushers for Sophia Webster {{!}} Hannah Diamond|url = http://photography.hannahdiamond.com/wedding-crushers-for-sophia-webster|website = Hannahdiamond.com|access-date = 14 August 2019|language = en-US}}</ref> the cover feature of the second issue of the French magazine Jalouse, which features [[Migos]] rapper [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]],<ref>{{Cite web|title = Offset for Jalouse {{!}} Hannah Diamond|url = http://photography.hannahdiamond.com/offset-for-jalouse|website = hannahdiamond.com|access-date = 14 August 2019|language = en-US}}</ref> and she additionally shot a [[L'Officiel]] editorial for Charli XCX promoting her new album ''[[Charli (album)|Charli]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lofficielusa.com/music/charli-xcx-editorial-2019|title=Charli XCX Has Leveled Up Through New Album 'Charli'|website=lofficielusa.com|access-date=29 October 2019}}</ref>

In 2020, Diamond took over a creative director position for UK alt-pop band [[Sundara Karma]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kennealy|first=Cerys Kennealy|date=2 October 2020|title=Sundara Karma unveil new single "Kill Me" with Hannah Diamond-directed video|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/sundara-karma-unveil-new-single-kill-me-with-hannah-diamond-directed-video|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=26 October 2020|website=[[The Line Of Best Fit]]}}</ref> Along with the band's frontman Oscar Pollock, she co-directed the music video for their single "Kill Me",<ref>{{Cite web|last=Staff|first=Crack|date=1 October 2020|title=Watch the Hannah Diamond-directed video for Sundara Karma's new single 'Kill Me'|url=https://crackmagazine.net/2020/10/watch-the-video-for-sundara-karmas-new-hannah-diamond-produced-single/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=26 October 2020|website=[[Crack Magazine]]}}</ref> as well as made a series of promotional graphics, including the single artwork.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Diamond|first=Hannah|date=2019|title=Hannah Diamond. Work.|url=http://photography.hannahdiamond.com/#/sundara-karma/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=26 October 2020|website=Hannah Diamond}}</ref> Shots from their visual collaboration were also featured on the DIY Magazine cover in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Wright|first=Lisa|date=8 February 2019|title=LET THERE BE LIGHT: SUNDARA KARMA|url=https://diymag.com/2019/02/08/let-there-be-light-sundara-karma-ulfilas-alphabet-cover-feature|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=26 October 2020|website=[[DIY Magazine]]}}</ref>
