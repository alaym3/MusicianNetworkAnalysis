'''Judy Kuhn''' (born May 20, 1958) is an American actress and singer, known for her work in musical theatre. A four-time [[Tony Award]] nominee, she has released four studio albums and sang the title role in the 1995 film ''[[Pocahontas (1995 film)|Pocahontas]]'', including her rendition of the song "[[Colors of the Wind]]", which won its composers the [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]].

Kuhn made her professional stage debut in 1981 and her [[Broadway (theatre)|Broadway]] debut in the 1985 original production of the musical ''[[The Mystery of Edwin Drood (musical)|The Mystery of Edwin Drood]]''. Subsequent Broadway roles include [[Cosette]] in ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]'' (1987), Florence Vassy in ''[[Chess (musical)|Chess]]'' (1988), and Amalia Balash in ''[[She Loves Me]]'' (1993). For all three, she received Tony Award nominations. She also received an [[Olivier Award]] nomination for her 1989 [[West End theatre|West End]] debut playing Maria/Futura in ''[[Metropolis (musical)|Metropolis]]''. Other musical roles include Betty Schaeffer in the 1993 US premiere production of ''[[Sunset Boulevard (musical)|Sunset Boulevard]]'' in Los Angeles and her [[Obie Award]] winning role as Emmie in the 2001 [[Off-Broadway]] production of ''Eli's Comin''. She received a fourth Tony nomination in 2015 for her role as Helen Bechdel in the original Broadway production of ''[[Fun Home (musical)|Fun Home]]'', and a second Olivier nomination in 2020 for her role as Golde in a London revival of ''[[Fiddler on the Roof]]''.

==Early life==
Kuhn (pronounced {{respell|kyoon}}) was born in New York City to Jewish parents and grew up in [[Bethesda, Maryland]]. She attended [[Georgetown Day School]] in Washington, D.C.<ref>Gilbert, Sophie. [https://www.washingtonian.com/2012/12/06/sondheim-meets-pocahontas/ "Sondheim Meets Pocahontas at Judy Kuhn Performance"] ''[[Washingtonian (magazine)|Washingtonian]]'', December 6, 2012</ref> She entered [[Oberlin College]].<ref name=lanes>Lanes, Elliot. [https://www.broadwayworld.com/washington-dc/article/BWW-Interview-Theatre-Life-with-Judy-Kuhn-20180502 "BWW Interview: Theatre Life with Judy Kuhn"] broadwayworld.com, May 2, 2018</ref> After taking voice lessons with Frank Farina,<ref name=Farina >[https://web.archive.org/web/20090304142749/http://www.laopera.com/artist/farina.franco.aspx Frank Farina]</ref> Kuhn transferred into the [[Oberlin Conservatory of Music]].<ref name=lanes/> Kuhn was also interested in musical theater and other types of music, in addition to classical music for which the Conservatory is best known. She trained as an "operatic [[soprano]]" at Oberlin,<ref>[http://www.masterworksbroadway.com/artist/judy-kuhn/ "Judy Kuhn"] masterworksbroadway.com, accessed April 15, 2016</ref> and graduated in 1981.<ref>[http://new.oberlin.edu/conservatory/departments/opera-theater/music-alumni.dot/conservatory/departments/opera-theater/music-alumni.dot "Alumni Opera Theater, Oberlin Conservatory of Music"]{{dead link|date=April 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} oberlin.edu, accessed April 15, 2016</ref>

After college, she moved to Boston, where she waited tables and studied acting. After appearing in summer stock, Kuhn moved back to New York.{{citation needed|date=April 2019}}

==Stage career==
===1985–1989===
Her Broadway debut was in ''[[Drood]]'', a [[Rupert Holmes]] musical based on the unfinished [[Charles Dickens]] novel, in 1985. She played the roles of "Alice / Miss Isabel Yearsley / Succubae" and understudied the title role played by [[Betty Buckley]].<ref>[http://www.ibdb.com/Production/View/4386#tabs-OpenNightCredit ''Drood''] ibdb.com, accessed April 15, 2016</ref> Her next appearance on Broadway was in the ill-fated ''[[Rags (musical)|Rags]]'', which opened on August 21, 1986 and closed after four performances.<ref>[http://www.ibdb.com/Production/View/4420 ''Rags''] ibdb.com, accessed April 15, 2016</ref>

Her next role of [[Cosette]] in the 1987 multiple award-winning Broadway production of ''[[Les Misérables (musical)|Les Misérables]]'' brought her the first [[Tony Award]] nomination, as Best Featured Actress in a Musical (1987),<ref>Blau, Eleanor. "2 Musicals Lead Tony Nominations", ''The New York Times'', May 12, 1987, Section C; Page 15, Column 4</ref> and the [[Drama Desk Award]] (1987) nomination as Outstanding Featured Actress in A Musical.<ref>(no author). "'Me and My Girl' and 'Les Miserables' Top Nominations for Drama Desk Awards", ''The Associated Press'', May 10, 1987, Domestic News</ref>

Kuhn appeared in the [[Trevor Nunn]]-directed ''[[Chess (musical)|Chess]]'', with music by [[Benny Andersson]] and [[Björn Ulvaeus]] and lyrics by [[Tim Rice]] in the 1988 Broadway transfer from the [[West End theatre|West End]], playing one of the main roles (Florence Vassy).<ref name=chess>[http://www.playbill.com/production/chess-imperial-theatre-vault-0000006035 ''Chess''] playbill.com, accessed April 15, 2016</ref> Despite the show's success in London, Trevor Nunn decided to rework it for Broadway from a pop/rock opera as staged in London into a more conventional musical theater piece with a new book by [[Richard Nelson (playwright)|Richard Nelson]]. As a result, the new show was greeted with mostly negative reviews and closed after less than a two-month run, on June 25, 1988.<ref name=chess/> Kuhn's performance in the musical received praise from the critics. "Her beautiful pop-soprano voice is the show's chief pleasure. She acts the sympathetic, gutsy role with spirit and heart", wrote ''[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]''.<ref>[https://http//squareone.org/Chess/review3.html%2BChess "Reviews for the Broadway Production. 'Chess'. See Humm, Variety, 29 April 1988"]{{dead link|date=April 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} quareone.org (webcache.googleusercontent.com), accessed January 2, 2014</ref><ref>[http://www.icethesite.com/home/chess/chess-on-broadway-the-reviews/ "'Chess' on Broadway the Reviews, see ''Variety'', 29 April 1988] icethesite.com, accessed December 27, 2015.</ref> The ''[[Village Voice]]'' noted that "she pours a river of feeling and lush vocal tone into...the role". {{citation needed|date=July 2012}} She garnered her second [[Tony Award]] nomination, this time as Best Actress in a Musical (1988), and a 1988 [[Drama Desk Award]] nomination as Outstanding Actress in a Musical.<ref name=chess/> In addition, The Original Broadway Cast recording of the musical was nominated for a [[Grammy Award]].

She reprised her role of Florence Vassy later in January 1989 in a [[Carnegie Hall]] concert performance with the rest of the Broadway cast, which was a benefit for the Emergency Shelter Inc.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/1989/01/03/theater/chess-cast-to-perform-in-carnegie-hall-benefit.html?pagewanted= "'Chess' Cast to Perform In Carnegie Hall Benefit"] ''The New York Times'', January 3, 1989</ref> She also performed in a ''Chess'' concert version in 1989 in [[Skellefteå]], Sweden, during a chess World Cup final tournament, where she joined with [[Tommy Körberg]] and [[Murray Head]], two principal actors from 1986 West End production of the musical.

Kuhn made her London debut in 1989, when she starred in the [[West End theatre|West End]] production of ''[[Metropolis (musical)|Metropolis]]'', with Jeremy Kingston, reviewing for ''The Times'' (London) writing "I greatly enjoyed Kuhn's edgy, angular performance."<ref>Kingston, Jeremy. "City of dreadful night; Review of 'Metropolis' at the Piccadilly Theatre", ''The Times'' (London), March 9, 1989, Issue 63337 (no page number)</ref> She received an [[Olivier Award]] nomination as [[Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress in a Musical|Best Actress in a Musical]].<ref name=":0">[http://www.olivierawards.com/about/previous-winners/view/item98524/olivier-winners-1989-90 "Olivier Winners 1989–90"] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20110717074504/http://www.olivierawards.com/about/previous-winners/view/item98524/Olivier-Winners-1989-90/ |date=July 17, 2011 }}, OlivierAwards.com, accessed July 3, 2012.</ref>

===1990–1996===
Kuhn's next major Broadway project, ''Two Shakespearean Actors'' (1992), despite a cast that included [[Brian Bedford]], [[Frances Conroy]], [[Hope Davis]], [[Victor Garber]], [[Laura Innes]] and [[Eric Stoltz]],<ref>[http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/Cast/3487/Two-Shakespearean-Actors-at-Cort-Theatre " ''Two Shakespearean Actors'': Opening Night Cast"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130708134828/http://www.playbillvault.com/Show/Detail/Cast/3487/Two-Shakespearean-Actors-at-Cort-Theatre |date=July 8, 2013 }}, PlaybillVault.com; accessed July 4, 2012.</ref> was commercially unsuccessful, closing after 29 regular performances.<ref>[http://www.ibdb.com/production.php?id=4656 "''Two Shakespearean Actors'' Listing"], Internet Broadway Database; accessed July 4, 2012.</ref>

In 1993, Kuhn played in the [[Roundabout Theater Company]] revival of ''[[She Loves Me]]'', portraying Amalia Balash, a young [[Budapest]] shopgirl who is unaware that the co-worker she despises is the young man with whom she's been sharing an anonymous correspondence. Her performance earned her a [[Tony Award]] nomination as Best Actress in a Musical.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/production/she-loves-me-criterion-center-stage-right-vault-0000003830 " ''She Loves Me'', 1993"] playbill.com, accessed April 15, 2016</ref> The 1993 Broadway recording of this revival does not feature Kuhn, who left the production before the album was produced.

In December 1993, Kuhn played the role of Betty Schaefer in the U.S premiere production of ''[[Sunset Boulevard (musical)|Sunset Boulevard]]'' at the [[Shubert Theatre (Los Angeles)|Shubert Theatre]] in Los Angeles.<ref>Gerard, Jeremy. [https://variety.com/1993/legit/reviews/sunset-boulevard-5-1200434846/ "Review: ‘Sunset Boulevard’"] ''Variety'', December 9, 1993</ref> The L.A production recorded a cast album, which is the only unabridged cast recording of the show with the original London recording being cut by thirty minutes.{{citation needed|date=April 2016}}

Regional theatre credits in the early 1990s include ''[[The Glass Menagerie]]'' at the [[McCarter Theatre]], [[Princeton, New Jersey]], in 1991 as "Laura"<ref>Gussow, Mel. [https://www.nytimes.com/1991/01/21/theater/review-theater-the-limits-of-love-and-little-glass-animals.html?pagewanted= "Review. The Limits of Love and Little Glass Animals"] ''The New York Times'', January 21, 1991</ref> and ''[[Martin Guerre]]'', at the Hartford Stage Company, [[Hartford]], Connecticut in 1993.<ref>Klein, Alvin. [https://www.nytimes.com/1993/01/10/nyregion/theater-martin-guerre-as-mystery-rather-than-history.html?pagewanted="Theater. Martin Guerre' as Mystery Rather Than History"] ''The New York Times'', January 10, 1993</ref> Kuhn reprised her role as Cosette in 1995, for the 10th anniversary concert performance at the [[Royal Albert Hall]] in London, which was released on DVD as ''[[Les Miserables: The Dream Cast in Concert]]''.<ref name=dream>{{cite web|url=http://broadwayworld.com/article/BBC-to-Release-Special-Edition-DVD-of-LES-MISERABLES-10th-Anniversary-Concert-1120-20121108 |title=BBC to Release Special Edition DVD of LES MISERABLES 10th Anniversary Concert, 11/20 |publisher=broadwayworld.com |access-date=2016-04-15}}</ref>

===2007–present===
On October 23, 2007, Kuhn returned to the Broadway production of ''Les Misérables'' after 20 years, this time assuming the role of [[Fantine]]. She succeeded [[Lea Salonga]] and remained with the show until the revival ended on January 6, 2008.<ref>[http://www.playbill.com/news/article/111107.html Playbill News: I Am My Own Mom: Judy Kuhn, Once a Cosette, Will Play Fantine in Les Miz] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080210154925/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/111107.html |date=February 10, 2008 }}</ref>

Kuhn portrayed Fosca in the Off-Broadway [[Classic Stage Company]] revival of the [[Stephen Sondheim]]-[[James Lapine]] musical ''[[Passion (musical)|Passion]]'' from its opening in February 2013 through its scheduled closing in April 2013.<ref>Piepenburg, Erik.  [http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/04/19/judy-kuhn-and-melissa-errico-cast-in-passion-revival "Judy Kuhn and Melissa Errico Cast in ''Passion'' Revival"], ''The New York Times'', April 2012.</ref> Kuhn had previously played Fosca in the Stephen Sondheim celebration production in 2002 at the [[Kennedy Center]].<ref>[http://www.sondheimguide.com/passion.html#KC "''Passion'' at the Kennedy Center"],. SondheimGuide.com, accessed July 3, 2012</ref>

In 2013, Kuhn originated the role of Helen Bechdel in the off-Broadway [[The Public Theater|Public Theater]] production of the musical ''[[Fun Home (musical)|Fun Home]]'', which began its run September 30, 2013 and opened officially on October 22, 2013. The run was extended multiple times and closed on January 12, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last=Hetrick |first=Adam |title=Jeanine Tesori-Lisa Kron Musical Fun Home Extends for Second Time at the Public |url=http://playbill.com/news/article/183745-Jeanine-Tesori-Lisa-Kron-Musical-Fun-Home-Extends-for-Second-Time-at-the-Public |work=Playbill |access-date=November 2, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131030192910/http://www.playbill.com/news/article/183745-Jeanine-Tesori-Lisa-Kron-Musical-Fun-Home-Extends-for-Second-Time-at-the-Public |archive-date=October 30, 2013 }}</ref> She played the same role in the Broadway production, which ran from April 2015 to September 10, 2016 at the [[Circle in the Square]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Hetrick|first=Adam|date=December 8, 2014|title=Fun Home Taps Original Off-Broadway Stars for Broadway Transfer|url=http://playbill.com/news/article/fun-home-taps-original-off-broadway-stars-for-broadway-transfer-336821|newspaper=Playbill.com|access-date=December 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Viagas|first=Robert|title=Tony-Winning Musical Fun Home Closes on Broadway Today|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/tony-winning-musical-fun-home-closes-on-broadway-today#|work=Playbill|date=September 10, 2016}}</ref>

Kuhn played the role of "Golde" in the Broadway revival of ''[[Fiddler on the Roof]]'', starting on November 22, 2016.<ref>Gans, Andrew. [http://www.playbill.com/article/broadways-fiddler-on-the-roof-will-welcome-judy-kuhn# "Broadway’s 'Fiddler on the Roof' Will Welcome Judy Kuhn"] Playbill, September 19, 2016</ref> She plays Golde in the [[Menier Chocolate Factory]] (London) production of ''Fiddler on the Roof'' which began on November 23, 2018 and ran to March 9, 2019.<ref>Culwell-Block, Logan. [http://www.playbill.com/article/a-look-at-judy-kuhn-and-andy-nyman-in-londons-fiddler-on-the-roof# "A Look at Judy Kuhn and Andy Nyman in London's 'Fiddler on the Roof'"] Playbill, December 7, 2018</ref>
