'''Be'lakor''' is an Australian [[melodic death metal]] band from Melbourne, Victoria. The band's name originates from the character ''Be'lakor the Dark Master'', in the [[Warhammer Fantasy (setting)|Warhammer Fantasy]] game setting.<ref name="interviewnl"/>

==History==
Be'lakor formed as a band in 2004, but didn't start playing live shows until 2005.<ref name ="interview">{{cite web|url=http://www.seaoftranquility.org/article.php?sid=1412 |title=Interviews: Australia's Be'lakor Avoid the Sophomore Jinx with Stone's Reach |date=25 October 2009 |last=Pardo |first=Pete |access-date=28 November 2009 |publisher=Sea of Tranquility}}</ref> In 2007, the band self-released<ref name ="interview" /> their debut album, ''[[The Frail Tide]]'', which received generally positive reviews.<ref name="interviewnl">{{cite web |url=http://www.lordsofmetal.nl/showinterview.php?id=2000 |title=Be'lakor  |author= Lords Of Metal - Issue 77: January 2008 |access-date=28 November 2009 |publisher=Lords of Metal |date=January 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themetalforge.com/modules.php?name=Reviews&rop=showcontent&id=1268|publisher=The Metal Forge |title=Belakor, ''The Frail Tide''|access-date=28 November 2009}}</ref> In 2008, Be'lakor signed with Descent Productions, and re-released ''The Frail Tide'' in digipack form.<ref name="primecuts">{{cite web |url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=36797 |title=Be'lakor Signs With Descent Productions |author=deathbringer |publisher=Metal Underground |date=22 May 2008 |access-date=28 November 2009}}</ref>  In 2009, Be'lakor embarked on a national tour, and signed with Prime Cuts Music.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=45026 |title=Be'lakor Announces Australian Tour |author=deathbringer |publisher=Metal Underground |date=1 June 2009 |access-date=28 November 2009}}</ref>  Their second album, ''[[Stone's Reach]]'', was released on 20 June 2009<ref name="primecuts" />
and received much praise by reviewers.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://masterful-magazine.com/recenzja_result.php?wynik=detail&rodzaj=recenzje&id=2636 |title=Be'lakor - "Stone's Reach" (Australia) |publisher=Masterful Magazine |access-date=28 November 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091031202456/http://www.masterful-magazine.com/recenzja_result.php?wynik=detail&rodzaj=recenzje&id=2636 |archive-date=31 October 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.seaoftranquility.org/reviews.php?op=showcontent&id=8183 |title=Review: "Be'lakor: Stone's Reach" - Sea of Tranquility |date=7 September 2009 |last=Pardo |first=Pete |access-date=28 November 2009 |publisher=Sea of Tranquility}}</ref>  In November 2009, Be'lakor signed with Kolony Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=50397 |title=Be'lakor Signs To Kolony Records |date=20 November 2009 |author=xFiruath |publisher=Metal Underground |access-date=28 November 2009| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20091123162040/http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=50397| archive-date= 23 November 2009 | url-status= live}}</ref>

In March 2010, Be'lakor's album ''Stone's Reach'' took first place in [[Metal Storm (webzine)|Metal Storm]]'s "Best Melodeath/Gothenburg Metal Album" for 2009.<ref>[http://www.metalstorm.ee/awards/categories.php?cat_id=4 The Best Melodeath/Gothenburg Metal Album]. ''MetalStorm.net''. Retrieved 16 September 2010.</ref> Be'lakor also supported Turisas on their national tour of Australia in May. They were the opening act for [[Alestorm]] in Melbourne for their "PLUNDER DOWNUNDER” tour in June 2010. The band also performed at the [[Summer Breeze Open Air|Summer Breeze Open Air Festival]] in Germany in August 2010.<ref>[http://www.summer-breeze.de/site.php?name=News News/Newsletter] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100504104856/http://www.summer-breeze.de/site.php?name=News |date=2010-05-04 }}. ''Summer-Breeze.de''. Retrieved 16 September 2010 and 2012.</ref>

Be'lakor released their third album ''[[Of Breath and Bone]]'' in June 2012. They began touring Europe, playing [[Brutal Assault]] in the Czech Republic. They also performed at the [[Summer Breeze Open Air|Summer Breeze Open Air Festival]] in Germany during August 2012. They supported [[Apocalyptica]] in Melbourne during their 2012 Australia tour in September, and [[At the Gates]] in Melbourne during their tour in November.

In August 2015, the band returned to Europe and played [[Brutal Assault]] in [[Czech Republic|Czechia]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/belakor/2015/vojenska-pevnost-josefov-jaromer-czech-republic-6bf646ea.html|title=Be'lakor Setlist at Brutal Assault XX|website=setlist.fm|access-date=2018-02-16}}</ref> Rockstadt Open Air in Romania<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/belakor/2015/cetatea-rasnov-rosenau-romania-13f789d9.html|title=Be'lakor Setlist at Rockstadt Extreme Festival 2015|website=setlist.fm|access-date=2018-02-16}}</ref> and [[Summer Breeze Open Air|Summer Breeze]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/belakor/2015/flugplatz-des-aeroclubs-dinkelsbuhl-dinkelsbuhl-germany-7bf782c0.html|title=Be'lakor Setlist at Summer Breeze 2015|website=setlist.fm|access-date=2018-02-16}}</ref> Matt Dodds of Arbrynth took over lead guitar duties for Shaun due to illness.

On 21 September 2015, the band announced that their fourth album would be released in March 2016. Later, on 29 December, drummer Jimmy Vanden Broeck left the band to pursue other musical ventures. He was replaced by Elliott Sansom, who also performs with Melbourne-based bands Dusk Cult & Okera.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metal-archives.com/artists/Elliott_Sansom/135702|title=Elliott Sansom - Encyclopaedia Metallum: The Metal Archives}}</ref> On 24 June 2016 Be'lakor released ''[[Vessels (Be'lakor album)|Vessels]]'', the band's fourth album.<ref>[http://belakorband.com/release/vessels-2016 Release details of ''Vessels'']. ''belakorband.com''. Retrieved 30 September 2016.</ref> "Coherence" followed five years later.
