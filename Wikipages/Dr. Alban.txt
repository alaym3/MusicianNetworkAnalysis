'''Alban Uzoma Nwapa''' (born 26 August 1957), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Dr. Alban''', is a [[Nigeria|Nigerian]]-[[Sweden|Swedish]] recording artist and producer with his own record label, Dr. Records.<ref name="Allmusic: Dr. Alban (Biography)">{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/dr-alban-mn0000203600/biography|title=Allmusic: Dr. Alban (Biography)|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=19 March 2011}}</ref> His music can best be described as [[Eurodance]]/[[Hip hop music|hip-hop]] [[reggae]] in a [[dancehall]] style.<ref name="Allmusic: Dr. Alban (Biography)"/> He has sold an estimated 16 million records worldwide<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dralban.net/index.php?option%3Dcom_content%26view%3Darticle%26id%3D55%26Itemid%3D118 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-09-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130824003939/http://dralban.net/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=55&Itemid=118 |archive-date=24 August 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.naijasmostincredible.com/dr-alban-50-greatest-nigerians-of-all-time/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-09-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120913075835/http://www.naijasmostincredible.com/dr-alban-50-greatest-nigerians-of-all-time/ |archive-date=13 September 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.calista.com.tr/news/newsread.asp?ID%3D114 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-09-06 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130618094053/http://www.calista.com.tr/news/newsread.asp?ID=114 |archive-date=18 June 2013 }}</ref> and is most famous for his worldwide 1992 hit [[It's My Life (Dr. Alban song)|"It's My Life"]], from the album ''[[One Love (Dr. Alban album)|One Love]]''.<ref name="Allmusic: Dr. Alban (Biography)"/>

==Life and career==

===Early life===
Alban was born Alban Uzoma Nwapa into a middle-class family of 10 children in [[Oguta]], [[Imo state]], Nigeria. He is [[Igbo people|Igbo]].<ref name="Allmusic: Dr. Alban (Biography)"/> 

===Education===
He got his secondary education at [[Christ the King College, Onitsha|Christ The King College]] and spent most of his youth in his hometown  of Oguta. At age 23, he traveled to [[Sweden]] to study [[dentistry]].<ref name="Allmusic: Dr. Alban (Biography)" /> To finance his studies, Dr. Alban became a DJ at the Stockholm club ''Alphabet Street''.<ref name="Allmusic: Dr. Alban (Biography)" /> Very quickly, his name became widely known, especially since he often sang to the records he put on turntables. DJ René Hedemyr discovered him. Alban finished his studies and even opened his own dentistry practice, keeping his DJ-ing as a lucrative sideline.

===Denniz Pop and achieving success: 1990s===
In 1990, he met [[Denniz Pop]] from the SweMix label and, together with Denniz and rap artist [[Leila K]], released his first record ''[[Hello Afrika]]''. At this point, he took the stage name Dr. Alban, a nod to his dental studies. His debut album included hits like "[[Hello Afrika (song)|Hello Afrika]]" and "[[No Coke]]", both of which ended up being million-selling singles.<ref name="Allmusic: Dr. Alban (Biography)"/> The album itself was quite successful and earned him gold certification awards in numerous markets including Germany (for sales of over 250,000 units), Austria (25,000), and Switzerland (25,000).<ref name="Dr. Alban's German Certification(s) for Hello Afrika">{{cite certification|region=Germany|artist=Dr. Alban|title=Hello Afrika|access-date=19 March 2011}}</ref><ref name="Austria's Certification-database">{{cite web|url=http://www.ifpi.at/?section=goldplatin |title=Gold & Platin |publisher=IFPI (Austria) |access-date=19 March 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110511092844/http://www.ifpi.at/?section=goldplatin |archive-date=11 May 2011 |df=dmy }}</ref><ref name="Swiss Certifications for 1991">{{cite web |url=http://www.swisscharts.com/awards.asp?year=1991|title=AWARDS 1991|work=IFPI (Switzerland)|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=19 March 2011}}</ref>

One year later, this success was surpassed by his second album ''[[One Love (Dr Alban album)|One Love]]''. The album included European hit singles such as "[[It's My Life (Dr. Alban song)|It's My Life]]" (which was used as background music for a [[Tampax]] advert) and "[[Sing Hallelujah (Dr. Alban song)|Sing Hallelujah]]". The single "It's My Life" reached No.1 in [[Israel]], [[Austria]], the [[Netherlands]], [[Sweden]], and Germany, and No.2 in Norway, Switzerland, and the United Kingdom.<ref name="European positions for Dr. Alban's It's My Life">{{cite web |url=http://www.swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Dr%2E+Alban&titel=It%27s+My+Life&cat=s|title=DR. positions for Dr. Alban">{{cite web |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artists/|title=The Official Charts Company: Dr. Alban |publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=19 March 2011}}</ref> Alban's third album ''[[Look Who's Talking!]]'', released in 1994, hit the top 10 in numerous markets including Germany, Switzerland, and Austria.<ref name="German positions for Dr. Alban's Albums"/><ref name="European positions for Dr. Alban's Look Who's Talking Now (Album)">{{cite web |url=http://www.swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Dr%2E+Alban&titel=Look+Who%27s+Talking+%2D+The+Album&cat=a|title=Dr. Alban: Look Who's Talking Now (Album)|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 March 2011}}</ref> The album was the first to earn Alban a gold certification award in his home country of Sweden for sales of over 50,000 units.<ref name="Swedish Certifications 1987-1998">{{cite web |url=http://www.ifpi.se/wp/wp-content/uploads/guld-platina-1987-1998.pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120521214300/http://www.ifpi.se/wp/wp-content/uploads/guld-platina-1987-1998.pdf|archive-date=21 May 2012|title=Guld & Platina 1987–1988|language=sv|publisher=[[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry|International Federation of the Phonographic Industry – Sweden]]|access-date=23 April 2013}}</ref>

Dr. Alban founded his own record label ''Dr. Records'', whereon he released his 1996 album ''[[Born in Africa (Dr. Alban album)|Born in Africa]]''. The album was unable to match the success of his previous releases. It only reached No.12 in Finland, while it peaked at No.37 in Switzerland and Sweden, No.52 in Germany, and No.41 in Austria.<ref name="German positions for Dr. Alban's Albums"/><ref name="European positions for Dr. Alban's Born in Africa (Album)">{{cite web |url=http://www.swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Dr%2E+Alban&titel=Born+In+Africa&cat=a|title=Dr. Alban: Born in Africa (Album)|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 March 2011}}</ref> The single "[[Born in Africa (song)|Born in Africa]]" went to number 1 in Finland.

In 1997, Alban released the compilation album ''The Very Best of 1990–1997'', which only charted in Austria, Sweden, and Germany. The same year, the artist also released the studio album ''Believe'', which peaked at No.27 in Sweden, No.30 in Finland, and No.41 in Austria.<ref name="European positions for Dr. Alban's Believe (Album)">{{cite web |url=http://www.swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Dr%2E+Alban&titel=I+Believe&cat=a|title=Dr. Alban: Believe (Album)|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 March 2011}}</ref> In late 1998, Dr. Alban released a single with German-based artist [[Sash!]], entitled "Colour the World", which experienced moderate chart success in Europe.<ref name="European positions for Dr. Alban's Colour the World (Single)">{{cite web |url=http://www.swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Sash%21+with+Dr%2E+Alban&titel=Colour+The+World&cat=s|title=Dr. Alban: Colour the World (Single)|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 March 2011}}</ref>

===Decline and return to music: 2000s===
In 2000, Alban released the single "What Do I Do", which charted only in Sweden at No.43; it spent only two weeks on the charts there.<ref name="European positions for Dr. Alban's What Do I Do (Single)">{{cite web |url=http://www.swedishcharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Dr%2E+Alban&titel=What+Do+I+Do&cat=s|title=Dr. Alban: What Do I Do (Single)|publisher=Hung Medien|access-date=20 March 2011}}</ref> The album ''Prescription'' was a flop as it failed to chart anywhere.

In 2007, after years of absence from the music scene, Dr. Alban released the studio album ''Back to Basics''. It was sold on the internet only through Dr. Alban's official website. In Russia however, both the physical CDs and [[Compact Cassette|cassettes]] were released.

===Later activities: 2010s–present===
In 2010, Alban collaborated once again with [[Sash!]] to produce a remake of "Hello Afrika", "Hello South Afrika", dedicated to the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]]. It was followed by a single consisting of 16 remixes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Dr-Alban-Vs-Sash-Hello-South-Africa/release/4157195|title=Dr. Alban Vs. Sash! – Hello South Africa (2010, File)|access-date=22 June 2021|website=Discogs.com}}</ref>

On 15 February 2014 at the [[Scandinavium]] arena in [[Gothenburg]], Dr. Alban teamed up with [[Jessica Folcker]] and arrived in fifth place in the third heat of [[Melodifestivalen 2014]], with the song "Around the World", to win the right to represent Sweden at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2014]] in Copenhagen, Denmark.

In 2015, he released the single "Hurricane", which failed to enter the European charts.

In May 2020, Alban released the song "Hello Sverige" (in Swedish) to encourage the population of Sweden to respect social distancing measures put in place to counteract the COVID-19 pandemic. The next month, he released an English version titled "Hello Nations". The song is another remake of his classic hit "Hello Afrika".

In June 2020, Alban released the single "Drama", also in Swedish, in collaboration with singer Folkhemmet. The song talks about Alban's long-running acrimonious relationship with Swedish [[Tabloid journalism|tabloid]] journalists.
