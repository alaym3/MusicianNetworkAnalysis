'''Holy Holy''' is a [[Supergroup (music)|supergroup]] which performs the musical works of [[David Bowie]]. The group features drummer [[Woody Woodmansey]], formerly of Bowie's backing band [[The Spiders from Mars]] and [[Tony Visconti]], Bowie's long time producer and occasional bass player, as well as a number of other notable musicians.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thechronopages.com/james-stevenson-woody-woodmanseys-holy-holy/|title=Holy Holy continues to play the music of David Bowie live|website=thechronopages.com|access-date=2019-07-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thestrangebrew.co.uk/articles/tony-visconti/|title=Tony Visconti – David Bowie and Holy Holy|date=2019-02-05|website=The Strange Brew|access-date=2019-07-03}}</ref>

==Tours==
The group undertook a short tour in the UK in September 2014 and a larger scale tour of the UK and Japan in the summer of 2015. In January 2016 they toured the east coast of the United States and followed this with a full tour of US and Canadian cities in the Spring of the same year. Their show consisted of a first half during which they performed David Bowie's classic ''[[The Man Who Sold the World (album)|The Man Who Sold the World]]'' and a second half during which early Bowie classics were performed. In March-April 2017 they performed another Bowie album in its entirety, ''[[The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars]]'' in [[Kingston upon Hull|Hull]] and other UK cities. They continue to tour.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6842958/holy-holy-david-bowie-concert-tony-visconti|title=Tony Visconti's Holy Holy Concert Becomes Strange, Mad Celebration After David Bowie's Death|website=Billboard|access-date=2019-07-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/jan/13/holy-holy-david-bowie-tribute-spider-from-mars-toronto-review|title=Holy Holy review – ex-Spider from Mars sees David Bowie off in style|last=Doherty|first=Mike|date=2016-01-13|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-07-03|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.classicpopmag.com/2014/09/tony-visconti-woody-woodmansey-glenn-gregory-steve-norman-and-holy-holy-to-perform-david-bowies-the-man-who-sold-the-world-album-with-special-guests/|title=Tony Visconti, Woody Woodmansey, Glenn Gregory, Steve Norman and Holy Holy to perform David Bowie’s ‘The Man Who Sold the World’ album with special guests|date=2014-09-10|website=Classic Pop Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-07-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.godisinthetvzine.co.uk/2019/02/09/holy-holy-the-barbican-york-08-02-2019/|title=Holy Holy – York Barbican, 08/02/2019|date=2019-02-09|website=God Is In The TV|language=en-US|access-date=2019-07-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.scotsman.com/arts-and-culture/music/music-review-holy-holy-old-fruitmarket-glasgow-1-4874050|title=Music review: Holy Holy, Old Fruitmarket, Glasgow|website=www.scotsman.com|language=en|access-date=2019-07-03}}</ref>

==Releases==
In September 2014, the band released a double A side single comprising the new song "We Are King", written and sung by Steve Norman of Spandau Ballet with a cover of David Bowie's "Holy Holy" sung by Heaven 17’s Glenn Gregory. In June 2015 they released a live recording of their The Man Who Sold the World show, recorded live in London and produced by Tony Visconti.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/fr/Tony-Visconti-And-Woody-Woodmanseys-Holy-Holy-With-Glenn-Gregory-And-Steve-Norman-The-Man-Who-Sold-T/release/7191836|title=Tony Visconti And Woody Woodmansey's Holy Holy* With Glenn Gregory And Steve Norman (2) - The Man Who Sold The World Live In London|website=Discogs|language=fr|access-date=2019-07-03}}</ref>
