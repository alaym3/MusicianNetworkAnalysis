'''Monty Are I''' (formerly known as '''Monty's Fan Club''' and '''Monty''')  is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Cranston, Rhode Island]]. The band is named after the groups’s former music instructor, Arthur Montanaro.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://johnstonsunrise.net/stories/named-for-their-cranston-music-teacher-monty-are-i-has-hit-the-road,12854 |title=Named for their Cranston music teacher... Monty Are I has hit the road |last=Kernan |first=Joe |date=22 June 2006 |website=Johnston Sunrise |access-date=9 October 2021}}</ref> The "Are I" portion of the name is a pun on "RI", Rhode Island's postal abbreviation.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.citybeat.com/music/article/13015560/monty-are-i-with-hawthorne-heights-just-surrender-and-more |title=Monty Are I with Hawthorne Heights, Just Surrender and more |last=Baker |first=Brian |date=16 December 2009 |website=CityBeat |access-date=9 October 2021}}</ref>

==History and achievements==
Monty Are I won [[Takeover Records (California)|Takeover Records]]' "Sign My Band" contest on [[MySpace]] in 2005.<ref>{{cite web|author=US |url=http://www.myspace.com/signmybandcontest |title=Sign My Band Contest &#124; Gratis muziek, tourneedata, foto's, video's |publisher=Myspace.com |date= |accessdate=2012-04-03}}</ref> They also won the [[WBRU]] "Rock Hunt" in 2002 and the [[Ernie Ball]] sponsored "Battle of the Bands" in 2003.<ref>Bob Gulla, [http://www.providencephoenix.com/music/top/documents/03365736.asp The Full Monty] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040909091712/http://www.providencephoenix.com/music/top/documents/03365736.asp |date=2004-09-09 }}, ''The Providence Phoenix'', November 28, 2003. Accessed July 7, 2007.</ref> The group signed to [[Stolen Transmission]], and were upstreamed to [[Island Records]].

Monty has many national tours under their belt, including five years on Vans [[Warped Tour]], and tours with [[Just Surrender]], [[Secondhand Serenade]], [[Amber Pacific]], [[Anberlin]], [[Powerspace]], [[Rx Bandits]], [[My Chemical Romance]], [[Story of the Year]], [[Sum41]], [[Taking Back Sunday]], [[Yellowcard]], [[Hawthorne Heights]] and [[The Red Jumpsuit Apparatus]].<ref>[{{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p818569/biography|pure_url=yes}} Monty Are I] at Allmusic.com</ref>

Monty Are I's song "In This Legacy" is featured in the [[Xbox (console)|Xbox]]/[[PS2]] games: [[ATV Offroad Fury 4]] and [[Tony Hawk's Project 8]].

Their first album exclusively under Island/DefJam is titled ''Break Through The Silence'' and was released on September 22, 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://johnstonsunrise.net/stories/monty-are-i-represent-roots-with-new-album,46951 |title=Monty Are I represent roots with new album |date=2 October 2009 |website=Johnston Sunrise|access-date=9 October 2021}}</ref> "One in a Million" was the first single, released on August 25, 2009.

As of 2017, new plans for the recording of any new Monty Are I material are put on hold as lead vocalist/guitarist Aiello is touring with [[Thirty Seconds to Mars]] in support of the 2013 album, [[Love, Lust, Faith and Dreams]], contributing bass and keyboards to their live performances.



'''2019 Reunion'''

On October 16, 2019  the band posted to their Instagram page the date "01.04.20" and announced their plans for a reunion show at The Met in Pawtucket for that date. Less than 48 hours later, the show sold out. The show was then moved to The Strand.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B3s5c99hY33/|title=Monty Are I (@montyareiofficial) • Instagram photos and videos|last=|first=|date=|website=www.instagram.com|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref>

On December 6th via the band's Instagram, a new song was announced called "In My Mind."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B5u7NutnYIC/|title=Monty Are I on Instagram: “***ANNOUNCEMENT 3 OF 3*** Our reunion show is only 4 weekends away! And if you were surprised that we're playing again, 10 years later...…”|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2019-12-12}}</ref>
