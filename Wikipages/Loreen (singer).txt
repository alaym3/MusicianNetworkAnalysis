'''Lorine Zineb Nora Talhaoui''', known professionally as '''Loreen''' ({{IPA-sv|lɔˈreːn|IPA}}; born 16 October 1983)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/loreen-mn0000276058/biography|title=Loreen {{!}} Biography & History|last=Clancy|first=Daniel|website=AllMusic|access-date=2016-09-20}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/1e234774-8e37-4378-b01d-b7dd6ced769c |title= Loreen |website= BBC |access-date= 24 October 2019}}</ref> is a Swedish pop [[singing|singer]], songwriter and [[record producer]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Loreen's Biography |url=http://www.loreen.se/#biography |website=Loreen.se |date=23 September 2012 |access-date=23 September 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Sweden tipped for Eurovision success |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-18115692 |newspaper=BBC News |date=18 May 2012 |access-date=25 May 2012}}</ref> [[Sweden in the Eurovision Song Contest 2012|Representing Sweden]], she won the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2012]] in [[Baku]], with her entry "[[Euphoria (Loreen song)|Euphoria]]". The song reached number one in many European countries, including Germany and Sweden.

Interested in becoming a musician, Loreen took part in the ''[[Idol 2004]]'' television competition, finishing fourth. The following year she released her first single, "The Snake", with the band [[Rob'n'Raz]] and became a television presenter on [[TV11 (Sweden)|TV400]]. While working as a segment producer and director for several Swedish reality TV shows, she entered [[Melodifestivalen 2011]] with the song "[[My Heart Is Refusing Me]]", which became a Top 10 hit in Sweden. She also had Top 20 hits in Sweden with the singles "[[Crying Out Your Name]]" (2012) and "[[Statements (song)|Statements]]" (2017).

==Early life==
Loreen was born in [[Stockholm]], Sweden, in 1983 to [[Moroccans in Sweden|Moroccan]] [[Berbers|Berber]] immigrant parents. She was raised in a liberal [[Islam in Sweden|Muslim]] household. Early in her life, Loreen moved to [[Västerås]]. She grew up there, and calls Västerås her hometown.<ref name="kpwebben">Auer, Josefin. [http://www.kpwebben.nu/artiklar/2012/ur-arkivet-artisterna-i-mellofinalen-2012/loreen-kp11-2011/index.xml KP: Loreen KP11 2011]{{dead link|date=February 2017}}. kpwebben.nu. Retrieved 10 March 2012.</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Musikläraren tror på seger |first=Nora Nergiz |last=Westerbergh |url=http://vlt.se/kulturnoje/1.1575759-musiklararen-tror-pa-seger |newspaper=Vestmanlands Läns Tidning |date=10 March 2012 |access-date=11 June 2012}}</ref> She spent most of her teenage years in Gryta, a residential area in Västerås.<ref name="vlt1">[http://vlt.se/kulturnoje/1.1575988-loreen-vinner-melodifestivalen Loreen vinner Melodifestivalen].</ref><ref name="Q&A">[http://svt.se/2.148833/artistkolumn_-_loreen Loreen Q&A från Melodifestivalen 2011] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120404130022/http://svt.se/2.148833/artistkolumn_-_loreen |date=4 April 2012 }}. ==Music career==

===2004–2010: ''Idol'' and hiatus===
Loreen became known to Swedish audiences after taking part in the Swedish ''[[Idol 2004]]'' competing as Lorén Talhaoui. During the qualification round, she placed third by the public vote. She would have been eliminated, but received a wildcard from the judges, allowing her to compete in the program.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.resume.se/sections/nyheter/helgintervjuer/2012/10/26/det-tar-x-antal-nitar-och-till-slut-ar-botten-nadd/ |publisher=Resume.se |language=sv |access-date=2 January 2013 |archive-date=2 January 2013 |title=Det tar x antal nitar och till slut är botten nådd |date=28 October 2012 |first=Linus |last=Fremin |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130102160111/http://www.resume.se/sections/nyheter/helgintervjuer/2012/10/26/det-tar-x-antal-nitar-och-till-slut-ar-botten-nadd/ |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> She finished fourth overall, and was eliminated during the eighth week.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://noje.se.msn.com/kandisnytt/photopage.aspx?cp-documentid=250336370&page=8 | publisher = [[Msn]] | language = sv | access-date = 2 January 2013 | title = Loreen – från Idolförlorare till superstjärna | date = 21 June 2012 | first = Quetzala | last = Blanco | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20131224105932/http://noje.se.msn.com/kandisnytt/photopage.aspx?cp-documentid=250336370&page=8 | archive-date = 24 December 2013 | url-status = dead }}</ref> After ''Idol'', in 2005, she released a promotional single, "The Snake", with the group [[Rob'n'Raz]]. That same year, she also presented the TV show ''Lyssna'', broadcast by the Swedish station [[TV11 (Sweden)|TV400]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.expressen.se/noje/idol-loren-far-eget-tv-program/ |work=[[Expressen]] |language=sv |title=Idol-Lorén får eget tv-program |access-date=2 January 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130102160729/http://www.expressen.se/noje/idol-loren-far-eget-tv-program/ |archive-date=2 January 2013 |date=18 December 2004 |first=Lina |last=Modin |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> After a year, she took a hiatus from more visible roles, and worked as a segment producer and director for reality TV shows such as [[TV3 (Sweden)|TV3]]'s ''Värsta pojkvänsakademin'', [[TV4 (Sweden)|TV4]]'s ''Matakuten'' and [[Sveriges Television|SVT]]'s ''Frufritt''.<ref>"[http://www.resume.se/nyheter/media/2012/04/17/loreen-gjorde-varsta-pojkvansakademin/ Loreen gjorde 'Värsta pojkvänsakademin'] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120418220307/http://www.resume.se/nyheter/media/2012/04/17/loreen-gjorde-varsta-pojkvansakademin/ |date=18 April 2012 }}", ''Resumé'', 17 April 2012. Retrieved 17 May 2012.</ref>

===2014–2015: ''Paperlight'' project and touring===
[[File:Loreen and her dancers at Art on Ice 2014-2.jpg|thumb|Loreen performing at ''Art on Ice 2014'' at the [[Ericsson Globe]] in Stockholm.]]

On 14 July 2017, Loreen released a new single called "Body".<ref>{{cite web|first=Stratos |last=Agadellis |url=http://esctoday.com/148809/sweden-loreen-releases-new-single-body/ |title=Sweden: Loreen releases new single Body |publisher=ESC Today |website=esctoday.com |date=14 July 2017 |access-date=10 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Nathan |last=Stella |url=http://escbubble.com/2017/07/loreen-releases-body-lead-single-album-nude/ |title=Loreen releases 'Body', the lead single for her album NUDE! |publisher=ESC Bubble |website=escbubble.com |date=14 July 2017 |access-date=10 August 2017}}</ref> She also revealed that "Body" was serving as lead single from her first EP ''[[Nude (EP)|Nude]]'', which was released on 25 August 2017.<ref>{{cite web|first=Bradley |last=Stern |url=http://popcrush.com/loreen-body-nude-era-single-album/ |title=With Her 'Body', Loreen Kicks Off The 'Nude' Era |publisher=[[PopCrush]] |date=14 July 2017 |access-date=10 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Rezo |last=Mamsikashvili |url=http://wiwibloggs.com/2017/07/14/serving-body-loreen-drops-lead-single-second-album-nude/193206/ |title=Seving "Body"! ===2020–present: Return to music, ''Så mycket bättre'' and acting debut ===

In order to celebrate the [[Melodifestivalen]] Hall of Fame, on 29 February 2020 Loreen performed "Fiction Feels Good", a reimagined medley of her hits including "Euphoria", "My Heart is Refusing Me" and "Statements", on the ''Andra Chansen'' show.<ref>{{cite web|first=Stratos |last=Agadellis |url=https://eurovoix.com/2020/02/21/sweden-loreen-melodifestivalen-2020/ |title=Sweden: Loreen to Perform at Melodifestivalen 2020 Andra Chansen |publisher=Eurovoix|website=eurovoix.com |date=12 September 2020 |access-date=5 December 2020}}</ref> In June 2020, she made a [[Cameo appearance|cameo]] on [[David Dobkin (director)|David Dobkin]]'s ''[[Eurovision Song Contest: The Story of Fire Saga]]'', along with other Eurovision winners, which premiered on [[Netflix]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=All the cameos to watch out for in Eurovision Song Contest: The Story of Fire Saga|url=https://metro.co.uk/2020/06/26/cameos-watch-eurovision-song-contest-story-fire-saga-12905657/|first=Emma|last=Kelly|date=June 26, 2020|website=Metro|access-date=June 27, 2020}}</ref> On 30 April 2020, it was announced Loreen was joining the Swedish TV show ''[[Så mycket bättre]]'' to be aired in Autumn. Songs released from the show were under her new label [[Universal Music Group]] with whom she signed with in 2020.<ref>{{cite web|first=Stratos |last=Agadellis |url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/a/70v61o/loreen-klar-for-sa-mycket-battre |title=Loreen klar för "Så mycket bättre" |publisher=Aftonbladet |website=aftonbladet.se |date=30 April 2020 |access-date=5 December 2020}}</ref> Later in the year, it was announced she would be part of the Netflix film ''JJ+E'' to be released 8 September 2021, portraying the main character's mother.<ref>{{cite web|first=Stratos |last=Agadellis |url=https://escxtra.com/2020/09/12/loreen-to-star-in-netflix-film-vinterviken-2021/ |title=Loreen to star in Netflix film "Vinterviken 2021" |publisher=ESCXtra|website=escxtra.com |date=12 September 2020 |access-date=5 December 2020}}</ref><ref name="JJ+E">{{cite web|url=https://www.netflix.com/gb/title/81248121 |title=JJ+E |publisher=Netflix|website=Netflix.com |date=11 August 2021 |access-date=11 August 2021}}</ref>

On 22 March 2021, Loreen's first self-written Swedish single "Sötvattentårar" was announced to be released that Friday.<ref>{{cite web|author=Loreen|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CMtzcMEJfLh/ |title=Instagram Post |date=22 March 2021 |access-date=22 March 2021}}</ref>

==Political activism==
During the Eurovision Song Contest 2012 in [[Baku]], [[Azerbaijan]], Loreen was the only entrant to meet [[Human rights in Azerbaijan|local human rights]] activists. She later told reporters that "human rights are violated in Azerbaijan every day. One should not be silent about such things."<ref name=Reut255>{{cite web |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/azerbaijan-eurovision-idUSL5E8GP85U20120525 |title=Swedish Eurovision star strays into Azeri rights row |first=Margarita |last=Antidze |date=25 May 2012 |publisher=Reuters |access-date=26 May 2012}}</ref> An Azerbaijan government spokesman responded critically, saying the contest should not "be politicised"<ref name=Reut255/> and requested that the [[European Broadcasting Union|EBU]] prevent such meetings.<ref>{{cite news|title=Azerbaidzhan syyttää Ruotsin viisuedustajaa politikoinnista |url=http://yle.fi/uutiset/azerbaidzhan_syyttaa_ruotsin_viisuedustajaa_politikoinnista/6109366 |publisher=Yle |language=fi |date=24 May 2012 |access-date=28 May 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120527034208/http://yle.fi/uutiset/azerbaidzhan_syyttaa_ruotsin_viisuedustajaa_politikoinnista/6109366 |archive-date=27 May 2012 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> Swedish diplomats replied that the EBU, Swedish TV and Loreen had not acted against the competition's rules.<ref>{{cite news |title=Loreen mitt i diplomatiskt drama |url=http://www.dn.se/kultur-noje/musik/loreen-mitt-i-diplomatiskt-drama |newspaper=Dagens Nyheter |language=sv |date=24 May 2012 |access-date=28 May 2012}}</ref>

In July 2012, Loreen performed at [[Slavianski Bazaar in Vitebsk|Slavianski Bazaar]] in Belarus, where Belarusian president [[Alexander Lukashenko]] was in attendance. During her visit to the country, she met with the wife of political prisoner [[Ales Bialiatski]], [[Viasna Human Rights Centre|Viasna]] representatives and independent journalists, and in a two-hour meeting expressed her support of political prisoners and signed the petition to ban the death penalty in the country.<ref name=CivilRights>{{cite web |url=http://www.civilrightsdefenders.org/news/loreen-meets-human-rights-defenders-in-belarus/ |title=Loreen meets human rights defenders in Belarus |author=Civil Rights Defenders |date=13 July 2012 |access-date=16 July 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120808123350/http://www.civilrightsdefenders.org/news/loreen-meets-human-rights-defenders-in-belarus/ |archive-date=8 August 2012 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref> Loreen later stated she was fully aware of the risks coming with "speaking out," including the possibility of being stopped or arrested at the airport when trying to return home.<ref>{{cite news |title=Loreen: "Tänkte att det fick bära eller brista"|url=http://www.expressen.se/noje/loreen-tankte-att-det-fick-bara-eller-brista/ |newspaper=Expressen |language=sv |date=15 July 2012 |access-date=16 July 2012}}</ref>

In August 2013, she was the ambassador of the [[Swedish Committee for Afghanistan]]<ref name=Afghanistan1>{{cite web |url=http://www.wadsam.com/famous-singer-loreen-ambassador-for-swedish-committee-for-afghanistan-232/ |title=Famous singer Loreen 'Ambassador' for Swedish Committee for Afghanistan |author=Wadsam |date=10 November 2013 |access-date=10 April 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140317055044/http://www.wadsam.com/famous-singer-loreen-ambassador-for-swedish-committee-for-afghanistan-232/ |archive-date=17 March 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and she visited [[Kabul]], [[Afghanistan]], and the village Yaskin Bala in Warsaj Valley with [[Carl Bildt]], [[Minister for Foreign Affairs (Sweden)|Minister for Foreign Affairs of Sweden]].<ref name=Afghanistan1/> The year after, the construction of a new primary school was due to start in the village and the plan was that Loreen would come back and follow the work of the new school until it was finished.<ref>{{cite news |title=Famous singer Loreen 'Ambassador' for SCA|url=http://www.swedishcommittee.org/news?post_id=2511 |newspaper=[[Swedish Committee for Afghanistan]] |date=5 November 2013 |access-date=16 July 2014}}</ref>{{Update inline|date=September 2017}} "It was a great experience to meet the people of Warsaj in Yaskin Bala, both men and women. I hope to come back soon and meet my friends again," says Loreen, "Education is the most important issue, when it comes to decrease poverty and to help people taking control over their own lives," says Loreen, "I am very impressed of what [[Swedish Committee for Afghanistan|SCA]] is doing in Afghanistan. To me, this is the right way of development efforts. I am happy to be committed in that work."<ref name=Afghanistan1/><ref name=Afghanistan2>{{cite web |url=http://eurovisionireland.net/2013/08/21/loreen-is-going-to-afghanistan/ |title=Loreen is going to Afghanistan? |publisher=Eurovision Ireland |date=21 August 2013 |access-date=10 April 2014}}</ref> For her work, Loreen was announced new patron of the [[World's Children's Prize for the Rights of the Child|World's Children's Prize]] in New York City, USA, and received the ''Crystal Globe''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://worldschildrensprize.org/singer-loreen-new-patron-of-the-worlds-childrens-prize.html |title=Singer Loreen new patron of the World's Children's Prize |author=worldschildrensprize.org |date=17 October 2013 |access-date=16 April 2014}}</ref> On 30 October 2014, she won the World's Children's Prize's ''Crystal Globe'' for the second time and met with [[Nobel Peace Prize]] winner [[Malala Yousafzai]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Malala Yousafzai nominated for Children's Nobel Prize 2014|url=http://news.biharprabha.com/2014/02/malala-yousafzai-nominated-for-childrens-nobel-prize-2014/|work=IANS|publisher=news.biharprabha.com|access-date=8 February 2014}}</ref> and [[Queen Silvia of Sweden]] in a ceremony at the [[Gripsholm Castle]] in [[Mariefred]], Sweden.<ref name=newpatron>{{cite web |url=http://worldschildrensprize.org/loreen |title=Loreen new Patron |author=worldschildrensprize.org |date=30 October 2014 |access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9bRSt4ly8zk |title=World's Children's Prize Patron Loreen accept her Crystal Globe at the WCP Ceremony 29 October |author=worldschildrensprize.org |date=30 October 2014 |publisher=YouTube |access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> She wants to support the WCP—the world's largest educational programs for children about children's rights.<ref name=newpatron/>
