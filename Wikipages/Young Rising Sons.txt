'''Young Rising Sons''' are an American [[indie pop]] band consisting of Andy Tongren (lead singer/guitar), Julian Dimagiba (bass), Max Iantorno (guitar) and Steve Patrick (drums) formed in 2010. With the release of their debut single [[High (Young Rising Sons song)|“High”]], Young Rising Sons signed with Dirty Canvas/[[Interscope Records]] in 2013.<ref name="allaccess1">{{cite web|url=http://music.allaccess.com/an-interview-with-the-talented-guys-of-young-rising-sons-lead-singer-andy-tongren-dishes-on-their-recent-signing-with-interscope-records-their-upcoming-tour-with-little-daylight-and-much-more/ |title=An Interview With The Talented Guys of Young Rising Sons! Lead Singer, Andy Tongren Dishes on Their Recent Signing with Interscope Records, Their Upcoming Tour With Little Daylight and Much More |publisher=Music.allaccess.com |access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref>

[[BBC Radio 1]] DJ, [[Zane Lowe]] named “High” a “Next Hype” track upon its release in the spring of 2014.<ref name="indiemusic1">{{cite web|url=http://indiemusic.co/2014/07/25/young-rising-sons-celebrate-interscope-signing-with-release/ |title=Young Rising Sons Celebrate Interscope Signing with Release |publisher=Indiemusic.co |date=2014-07-25 |access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref>

==Biography==

===2010–2012: Band formation===
Dimagiba and Patrick grew up playing music together in Red Bank, NJ. After watching Andy Tongren perform an acoustic set at a New York City bar, the other members approached him about taking lead vocals in their newly formed band. They wrote music together for several years under different aliases until eventually landing the name Young Rising Sons.

===2013–2016: ''The High'' and Interscope Signing===
Through mutual friends of Andy Tongren, Young Rising Sons were introduced to New York producers [[Shep Goodman]] and [[Aaron Accetta]]. After hearing Tongren's soulful voice and witnessing the band's incredible chemistry, they immediately signed Young Rising Sons to a production deal with their label Dirty Canvas Music.<ref name="allaccess1"/>

The band then went on to collaborate with Dirty Canvas [[Shep Goodman]] and [[Aaron Accetta]] as well as long time friends Christian Medice and James Adam Shelley to record new music which ultimately led to their debut single [[High (Young Rising Sons song)|“High”]].

The video for “High” premiered on Mostly Junk Food and Buzznet.<ref>{{cite web|last=Scarlata |first=Amy |url=http://www.buzznet.com/tags/premiere/video/ |title=Video |publisher=Buzznet |date=2011-06-23 |access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref> The response was explosive and quickly went viral hitting #1 on Hypem Twitter and landing on the main music page on YouTube.<ref name="indiemusic1"/> With the well received buzz, “High” instantly caught the attention of tastemakers across the globe including [[BBC Radio 1]] DJ, [[Zane Lowe]], who dubbed “High” his “Next Hype” track.

The band signed with [[Interscope Records]] in 2014. With this, Young Rising Sons released a self-titled four track EP through Dirty Canvas/[[Interscope Records]].<ref name="allaccess1"/>

Young Rising Sons spent 2014-2015 touring North America with such artists as [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://welistenhear.com/news/video-young-rising-sons-high/|title=Video: Young Rising Sons "High" - Listen Hear|date=2015-01-21|publisher=Welistenhear.com|access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref> [[Weezer]], Bleachers, [[Kongos (band)|Kongos]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.myoceanstyle.com/young-rising-sons-on-tour-in-support-of-debut-ep/ |title=OCEAN Style – Young Rising Sons – On Tour In Support Of Debut Ep |publisher=Myoceanstyle.com |date=2014-08-25 |access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref> [[The 1975]],<ref>{{cite web|author=Tehrene Firman |url=http://www.teenvogue.com/entertainment/music/2014-11/young-rising-sons-video-premiere |title=Young Rising Sons Exclusive Live Performance |publisher=Teen Vogue |date=2014-11-11 |access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref> and [[The Neighbourhood]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Andrew Evans |url=http://whenthegramophonerings.com/2014/09/13/win-tickets-the-neighbourhood-young-rising-sons-london-uk/ |title=Win Tickets // The Neighbourhood & Young Rising Sons // London (UK) |publisher=When The Gramophone Rings |access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref>

“High” was featured in the NHL Awards, MLB Network, Carson Daly, NBC Summer Image Campaign in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://music.allaccess.com/young-rising-sons-shine-bright-at-las-troubadour/ |title=Young Rising Sons Shine Bright at LA’s Troubadour! &#124; All Access Music |publisher=Music.allaccess.com |access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref> The single was also featured on [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]]’s hit television show ''Red Band Society'' and [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]’s ''Selfie'' in 2014 <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1vBWHXM5NdI |title=YouTube |publisher=YouTube |access-date=2015-11-05}}</ref>

On Monday, May 2, 2016, the band officially announced via their social media outlets that Dylan Scott would be leaving the band to pursue other interests.<ref>{{cite web|title=with love, the sons|url=https://www.facebook.com/youngrisingsons/photos/a.562767507075187.131414.521890777829527/1203458959672702/?type=3&theater|website=Young Rising Sons - Facebook|access-date=3 May 2016}}</ref>

=== 2017-present ===
In 2017 Young Rising Sons announced their departure from Interscope Records.  They went on to release singles, "Whiskey," and "Noise," followed by the "SAD / Scatterbrain" EP in 2018.  The band announced in late fall of 2018 they would be taking a hiatus. On October 16th 2020 the band ended the hiatus and released a new single called "Halloween". They released another new single on December 11th 2020 titled “Sadder”. And on January 29 2021, Young Rising Sons released the single “Sunday Sunshine”.

==Discography==
Young Rising Sons released their debut single [[High (Young Rising Sons song)|"High"]] on July 22, 2014.<ref name="allaccess1"/> The self-titled EP followed, officially released on July 22, 2014 by Dirty Canvas/[[Interscope Records]].<ref name="indiemusic1"/>

The band released their latest single "Skeletons" on March 5th 2021.
