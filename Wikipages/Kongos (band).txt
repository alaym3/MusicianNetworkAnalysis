'''Kongos''' (often styled as '''KONGOS''') is a [[South African Americans|South African American]] band, now based in [[Phoenix, Arizona]].<ref>{{cite web |author1=Lester, Paul |title=Kongos (No 1,738) Band of the Week |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/apr/09/kongos |work=The Guardian |access-date=11 December 2018 |date=9 April 2014 |quote=they've got Greek blood, grew up in South Africa and the UK and now live in the States}}</ref> The [[alternative rock]] group consists of four brothers: Dylan (vocals, bass guitar, guitar), Johnny (accordion, keyboards, vocals), Jesse (drums, percussion, vocals) and Daniel Kongos (guitar, vocals).

==Beginnings==
They spent their childhoods in the city of East London, South Africa. They compose, record and perform in [[Phoenix, Arizona|Phoenix]], Arizona. They graduated from [[Chaparral High School (Arizona)|Chaparral High School]] in Scottsdale, Arizona and attended [[Arizona State University]]. The four men are the sons of recording artist [[John Kongos]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jambase.com/Artists/83600/Kongos/Bio |title=Kongos Bio, History, Info on JamBase |publisher=Jambase.com |access-date=23 July 2014}}</ref> They are of [[Greeks|Greek]], [[Americans|American]] and [[Mexicans|Mexican]] origin and have attended the Greek Saheti<ref>{{cite web|title=Kongos comes home to Saheti|url=http://www.saheti.co.za/Kongos.htm|publisher=Saheti School, South Africa|access-date=12 March 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140312224811/http://www.saheti.co.za/Kongos.htm|archive-date=12 March 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref> school in [[Gauteng]], South Africa.

==History==

===''Lunatic'' (2011–2015)===
The band's second album ''[[Lunatic (Kongos album)|Lunatic]]'' was released in 2012.<ref name="ComeWithBillboard"/> The single "I'm Only Joking" topped various South African charts, including all three of [[Tuks FM]]'s charts, and received copious airtime on [[5FM]] and on many other stations in South Africa.<ref name="Charts">{{cite web|url=http://www.oppikoppi.co.za/article/view/kongos?facebook=0|title= KONGOS |access-date=15 August 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120627085416/http://www.oppikoppi.co.za/article/view/kongos?facebook=0 |archive-date=27 June 2012 }}</ref> The music video for the song debuted on TV 5 on [[SABC 3]] and was in rotation on [[MK (channel)|MKTV]]. Their second single "Come with Me Now" was in rotation on both 5FM and Tuks FM. The song appeared on the 5FM Top 40 and the Tuks FM Top 30 charts.<ref name="Charts" />

In late October 2013, the band self-released ''Lunatic'' in the United States.<ref name="ComeWithBillboard"/> In 2014, both "I'm Only Joking" and "[[Come with Me Now]]", the latter of which being their top song, began receiving noticeable exposure in the United States, gaining momentum in airplay on radio and being featured in television commercials there. Their song "Come with Me Now" was used as the official theme song for WWE's [[pay-per-view]] event ''[[Extreme Rules (2014)|Extreme Rules]]'' and in the motion picture ''The Expendables 3''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2333784/soundtrack|title=The Expendables 3 (2014) |website=IMDb.com}}</ref> As a result, the band was signed by [[Epic Records]] in late January 2014 and the band re-released ''Lunatic''. "Come with Me Now" had sold more than 70,000 copies as of March 2014, but has since been certified Platinum by the RIAA, signifying sales of more than a million copies.<ref name="ComeWithBillboard">{{cite web|title=Kongos Scores Fastest Top 10 Alternative Debut Single Since Lorde's 'Royals'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5937501/kongos-scores-fastest-top-10-alternative-debut-single-since-lordes|website=Billboard.com|access-date=15 March 2014}}</ref>

===''Egomaniac'' (2016)===
On 27 March 2016, Kongos announced via their newsletter that their third album, ''Egomaniac'', would be released on 10 June 2016 and that the first single, "Take It from Me", would premiere on 15 April. On 28 April 2016, the second instant grat track "I Don't Mind" was released to album pre-order customers. The official Egomaniac tour was announced in June 2016 commencing in September.

===''The Front Lounge, Bus Call, and 1929''===
On 16 November 2017, the band released the first installment of their new podcast, The Front Lounge, to be updated weekly. It was here they hinted towards the documentary of their lives on the road while touring for three years. The band officially announced the documentary, titled Bus Call, on 31 January 2018. On 5 February 2018, the band released the title of their fourth album, ''1929'', with no further information. They had been sharing photos and videos from their studio, hinting towards the album’s release on their social media pages.

''1929, Pt. 1'' was released on 18 January 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://crypticrock.com/kongos-1929-album-review/|title=KONGOS – 1929 (Album Review)|first=Lisa|last=Whealy|website=Crypticrock.com|date=14 January 2019}}</ref> ''1929, Pt. 2'' was released on 11 October 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kongos.com/|title=KONGOS|website=Kongos.com}}</ref>
