'''Zoe Wees''' is a German singer-songwriter. Her debut single "[[Control (Zoe Wees song)|Control]]" charted in several nations including number 1 in France ([[SNEP]] Radio)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://snepmusique.com/classement-radio/?semaine=40|title=Singles Radio (Week 40, 2020)|author=<!--Not stated--> |date=2 October 2020 |website=SNEP |access-date=16 October 2020}}</ref> and the top 10 in Belgium. Wees' music has been described as "raw, deeply emotional" that ranges "from raspy low to unbelievably powerful and sky high".<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Barbeito|first=Camila|date=2021-01-27|title=Everything to Know About Zoe Wees, the German Singer Behind Megahit "Control"|url=https://www.popsugar.com/node/48127211|access-date=2021-04-26|website=POPSUGAR Celebrity|language=en-US}}</ref> Wees can often be found playing the piano or guitar.<ref name=":0" />

==Background==
Wees grew up in [[Hamburg]], Germany.<ref name="Wonderland">{{cite web |title=Newcomer Zoe Wees Talks Breakout Single "Control" And New EP |url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2020/07/14/new-noise-zoe-wees/ |website=Wonderland |access-date=13 October 2020 |date=14 July 2020}}</ref> She has lived with [[benign rolandic epilepsy]] (BRE),<ref name="LondonInStereo" /> a syndrome that caused her to have feelings of exclusion from others, helplessness, and loss of control.<ref name="LondonInStereo" /> Wees stated, "The sickness was stronger than me, and left scars that have become part of my life. Accepting them has taken so much time, but they make me what I am today: a fighter."<ref name="Notion">{{cite web |title=Interview: Taking Control With Zoe Wees |url=https://notion.online/taking-control-with-zoe-wees/ |website=Notion |access-date=13 October 2020 |date=18 September 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
Wees has written music in English since childhood.<ref name="Wonderland" /> She began working on music as a teenager when her teacher came up to her after a school concert wanting to work with her on music. In early 2017, Wees took part in the fifth season of the music talent show ''[[The Voice Kids (German TV series)|The Voice Kids]]''. Wees joined pop singer [[Sasha (German singer)|Sasha]]'s team in the "Blind Auditions". In the "Sing-Offs", the third of five phases in the competition, Wees was eliminated.<ref name="Notion" /> Wees released the song "[[Control (Zoe Wees song)|Control]]" on 13 March 2020.<ref name="WizardRadio">{{cite web |title=Interview with Zoe Wees |url=https://www.wizardradio.co.uk/news/interview-with-zoe-wees |website=www.wizardradio.co.uk |access-date=13 October 2020}}</ref> The song is written about anxiety and not being in control, but more specifically, about her battle with benign rolandic epilepsy.<ref name="Spotify">{{cite web|date=6 July 2020|title=Germany's Zoe Wees Owns Her Journey on Powerful Debut Single|url=https://newsroom.spotify.com/2020-07-06/germanys-zoe-wees-owns-her-journey-on-powerful-debut-single/|access-date=13 October 2020|website=[[Spotify]]}}</ref><ref name=":0" /> Her mission in writing the song was to say thank you to her primary school teacher who pushed her along as a young girl into becoming who she is today.<ref name=":0" />

On 19 April, Wees released "Ghost", part of her debut EP ''Golden Wings,'' which was released on 21 May via [[Capitol Records]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Paul|first=Larisha|date=2021-04-19|title=Breakout Singer Zoe Wees Shares New Single ‘Ghost’ {{!}} uDiscover|url=https://www.udiscovermusic.com/news/zoe-wees-ghost-single/|access-date=2021-04-26|website=uDiscover Music|language=en-US}}</ref> Wees said that "Ghost" was written about letting someone get too close even if you know they can hurt you and about being vulnerable to that person in a way that could essentially destroy your life.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Zoe Wees dares to be emotionally vulnerable on new release "Ghost"|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/new-music/song-of-the-day/zoe-wees-ghost|access-date=2021-04-26|website=The Line of Best Fit|language=en}}</ref> Ghost was co-written by Ricardo Muñoz Repko, VVAVES, Nicolas Rebscher, and Patrick Salmy.<ref name=":1" />

==Musical style and influences==
Wees noted that she listened to [[Jessie J]] and [[Miley Cyrus]] as she grew up.<ref name="Wonderland" /> Artists that she cited as influences include Jessie J and [[Lewis Capaldi]].<ref name="LondonInStereo">{{cite web |title=Zoe Wees - Interview |url=https://www.londoninstereo.com/zoe-wees-interview/ |website=London In Stereo |access-date=13 October 2020 |date=27 July 2020}}</ref> She describes her genre as pop.<ref name="Wonderland" />
