'''Tyrese Darnell Gibson''' (born December 30, 1978)<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/tyrese_darnell_gibson_born_1978_13629289 |title=The Birth of Tyrese Gibson |website=californiabirthindex.org |access-date=August 31, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=tvg>{{cite web|url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/tyrese-gibson/bio/177762/| title=Tyrese Gibson Biography| publisher=[[TV Guide|TVGuide.com]]|archive-date=October 3, 2015|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20151003005943/http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/tyrese-gibson/bio/177762/|url-status=dead}}</ref> is an American singer, actor, and songwriter. He released his [[Tyrese (album)|self-titled]] debut studio album in 1998, which featured the single "[[Sweet Lady (Tyrese song)|Sweet Lady]]", peaking at number twelve on the U.S. [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His second and third albums, ''[[2000 Watts]]'' and ''[[I Wanna Go There]]'', were released in 2001 and 2002; respectively, the latter contained the lead single "[[How You Gonna Act Like That]]", which became Tyrese's highest-charting single, reaching number seven on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. His fourth album, ''[[Alter Ego (Tyrese album)|Alter Ego]]'', explored [[hip hop music|hip hop]], while he was nominated for the [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Album]] for his fifth album ''[[Open Invitation]]'' (2011). Tyrese's sixth album, ''[[Black Rose (Tyrese album)|Black Rose]]'' (2015), debuted at number one on the U.S. [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], becoming his highest-charting album. He has sold over 4 million records in the United States.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5679825/tgts-three-kings-debuts-at-no-1-on-top-rbhip-hop-albums-chart |title=TGT's 'Three Kings' Debuts at No. 1 on Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums Chart |last=Ramirez |first=Rauly |date=August 29, 2013 |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130902015844/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/5679825/tgts-three-kings-debuts-at-no-1-on-top-rbhip-hop-albums-chart |access-date=August 19, 2019|archive-date=September 2, 2013 }}</ref>

Tyrese had his first starring role in [[John Singleton]]'s [[coming-of-age]] [[hood film]] ''[[Baby Boy (film)|Baby Boy]]'' (2001), and then began appearing as [[Roman Pearce]] in the [[The Fast and the Furious|''Fast & Furious'' franchise]]. Tyrese reunited with Singleton for the [[action film]] ''[[Four Brothers (film)|Four Brothers]]'' (2005), and plays Robert Epps in the [[Transformers (film series)|''Transformers'' franchise]]. He appeared in the [[comedy film]] ''[[Ride Along 2]]'' (2016), and is set to appear in the [[superhero film]] ''[[Morbius (film)|Morbius]]'' (2022).

== Music career ==

=== Music beginnings ===
Gibson's career began when he auditioned for a Coca-Cola commercial at the suggestion of his high school music teacher. An appearance in a 1994 [[Coca-Cola]] advertisement, singing the phrase "Always Coca-Cola",<ref name="baller">{{cite web | title=BallerStatus | work=Tyrese: Waist Deep | url=http://www.ballerstatus.net/beyond/read/id/04270158/ | access-date=June 19, 2006 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070202120139/http://www.ballerstatus.net/beyond/read/id/04270158/| archive-date= February 2, 2007}}</ref> led to bigger fame. It also led him to other appearances, such as for [[Guess (clothing)|Guess]] and [[Tommy Hilfiger]].

=== 1998–1999: ''Tyrese'' ===

Gibson was signed as an artist to [[RCA Records]] in early 1998. Afterwards, he released his debut single "[[Nobody Else (Tyrese song)|Nobody Else]]". It quickly rose on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart, peaking at No. 36. On September 29, 1998, he released his self-titled album ''[[Tyrese (album)|Tyrese]]'' at the age of 19. It debuted on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts at No. 17. In late 1998, Gibson became the new host of the weekday music video show ''MTV Jams'' on [[MTV]] and a host and [[video jockey|VJ]] for the channel.<ref name=jam>{{Cite journal|last=Hay|first=Carla|title=RCA's Tyrese Finds 'Sweet' Success|journal=Billboard|date=January 23, 1999|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yg0EAAAAMBAJ}}</ref> Afterwards, he released the second single from the album "[[Lately (Tyrese song)|Lately]]". It made it to No. 56 on the ''Billboard'' charts. Then, the album's third single and highest-charting single "[[Sweet Lady (Tyrese song)|Sweet Lady]]" became the album's biggest hit, reaching No. 9 on the R&B charts. The single earned Gibson a Grammy nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Male R&B Vocal Performance|Best R&B Male Vocal Performance]]. The album eventually went on to be [[Platinum certification|certified Platinum]]. Gibson, along with singers [[Ginuwine]], [[RL (singer)|RL]] of [[Next (band)|Next]], and [[Case (singer)|Case]], were featured on the soundtrack of ''[[The Best Man (1999 film)#Soundtrack|The Best Man]]'' on the single "[[The Best Man I Can Be]]".

=== 2000–2001: ''2000 Watts'' ===

On May 22, 2001, Gibson released his second studio album ''[[2000 Watts]]''. The first single off the album was "[[I Like Them Girls]]," which reached No. 15 on the [[Billboard Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart. The album went on to be certified [[Music recording sales certification|Gold]], selling over 500,000 copies. The third single off the album, "Just a Baby Boy," with [[Snoop Dogg]] and Mr. Tan, was featured on the [[Baby Boy (soundtrack)|soundtrack]] to the film ''[[Baby Boy (film)|Baby Boy]]'', Gibson's first major acting role.

=== 2002–2004: ''I Wanna Go There'' ===

After [[RCA Records]] was disbanded Gibson went on to sign to [[J Records]]. There he released his third studio album ''[[I Wanna Go There]]'' on December 10, 2002. His first single from the album and arguably his most successful single to date "[[How You Gonna Act Like That]]" debuted on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart at No. 7.

=== 2005–2010: ''Alter Ego'' and hiatus ===
[[File:TyreseGibsonDec08.jpg|thumb|upright=0.85|Gibson in 2008]]
On December 12, 2006, Gibson released his fourth studio album, ''[[Alter Ego (Tyrese album)|Alter Ego]]'', his first double-disc album. It was also his first album in which he debuts his [[rapping]] persona. The first single off the album was "One" debuting on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart at No. 26. The album itself is considered Gibson's lowest selling album to date. In 2007, Gibson, [[Ginuwine]], and [[Tank (American singer)|Tank]] founded [[TGT (group)|TGT]].

=== 2011–2012: ''Open Invitation'' ===

After taking time from music to focus on his family and his highly successful acting career, Gibson decided it was time to return to music. In 2011, he signed himself and his newly founded label Voltron Recordz to [[EMI]] and announced he was working on a new album titled ''[[Open Invitation]]''. On August 16, 2011, Gibson released the lead single "Stay". The music video for the single featured fellow actor, friend and ''Baby Boy'' co-star [[Taraji P. Henson]]. It peaked on the US [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]] chart at No. 11.<ref>[{{BillboardURLbyName|artist=tyrese|chart=all}} The Basics: ''Tyrese'']. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. Retrieved on October 7, 2011.</ref> The second single "Too Easy" featured fellow actor, friend and rapper [[Ludacris]]. It peaked on the US Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart at No. 38. The third single "Nothing On You" peaked on the US Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart at #61. ''Open Invitation'' was released on November 1, 2011. It debuted on the US ''Billboard'' 200 albums chart at No. 9, sold 130,000 copies in its first week and has gone on to sell over 400,000 copies. In 2013, the album earned Gibson his third [[Grammy Award|Grammy]] nomination at the [[2013 Grammy Awards]] for Best R&B Album.

=== 2013–2017: TGT and ''Black Rose'' ===
In early 2013, it was announced and confirmed after much speculation that Gibson, [[Ginuwine]], and [[Tank (American singer)|Tank]] would be releasing their debut collaboration album, to be distributed by [[Atlantic Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/tgt-tyrese-ginuwine-and-tank-sign-atlantic-records|title=TGT (Tyrese, Ginuwine and Tank) Sign To Atlantic Records|work=Vibe|access-date=July 20, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/02/06/new-music-tgt-tyrese-ginuwine-tank-sex-never-felt-better/|title=New Music: TGT (Tyrese, Ginuwine, Tank) – 'Sex Never Felt Better'|work=Rap-Up|access-date=July 20, 2015}}</ref> In 2014, Gibson released the album ''[[Black Rose (Tyrese album)|Black Rose]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rapbasement.com/tyrese/041013-tyrese-documentary-a-black-rose-that-grew-through-concrete-full-stream-release.html|title=Tyrese Documentary "A Black Rose That Grew Through Concrete" Full Stream Release – Rap Basement|work=Rap Basement|date=April 10, 2013|access-date=July 20, 2015}}</ref> A double album set was released in 2015. On July 10, 2015, ''Black Rose'' was released and had debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200, with first-week sales of 77,000 copies, making it Gibson's first number one album of his career.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/6634623/tyrese-scores-his-first-no-1-album-on-billboard-200-chart-black-rose|title=Tyrese Scores His First No. 1 Album on Billboard 200 Chart|work=Billboard}}</ref>

=== 2017–present: ''Identity Theft'' ===
In October 2017, Gibson worked on a project titled ''Identity Theft'', and also he returned to the alter ego Black Ty, the album focuses more on his hip hop roots. He predicted that it will "change hip hop".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2017/10/tyrese-new-rap-album-inspired-by-jay-z|title=Tyrese Is Working on a Rap Album That He Says Will 'Change Hip-Hop'|website=Complex.com}}</ref>

When Gibson competed on [[The Masked Singer (American season 5)|season five]] of ''[[The Masked Singer (American TV series)|The Masked Singer]]'', interim host [[Niecy Nash]] introduced his character as "Porcupine" only for Gibson to tell her that his character preferred to be called "Robopine".
