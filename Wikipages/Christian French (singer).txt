'''Christian French''' (born March 17, 1997) is an American pop singer-songwriter based in Los Angeles. He grew up in [[Fishers, Indiana]]<ref name="Wallock">{{Cite web |url= https://americansongwriter.com/christian-french-drops-i-think-too-much-ahead-of-new-ep/|title= Christian French Drops "i think too much" Ahead of New EP |website=American Songwriter |last=Wallock |first=Matt |date=April 17, 2020 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> and learned to play piano by watching YouTube instructionals and in high school started posting song covers on SoundCloud.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/dive-head-first-into-christian-frenchs|title= Dive "head first" into Christian French’s Intoxicating Pop Melodies [Q&A] |website=Ones to Watch |last=Arunarsirakul |first=Alissa |date=May 21, 2019 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref>

== Biography ==
While a pre-med student<ref name="Bruner">{{Cite web |url= https://time.com/5797481/best-songs-of-the-week-dixie-chicks-bad-bunny/|title= The Best New Songs of the Week, From the Dixie Chicks to Bad Bunny |website=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |last=Bruner |first=Raisa |date=March 6, 2020 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> at [[Indiana University]], he started writing original music. After dropping out of college, he moved to Los Angeles and put out a few singles with producer Triegy, including their first track, "Fall for You", which charted on [[Spotify]]'s U.S. Viral 50.<ref name="The B-Side">{{Cite web |url= https://hitsdailydouble.com/the_bside&id=316136|title= The B-Side|website=Hits Daily Double |date=April 22, 2019 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> He followed that with "By Myself", also a collaboration with Triegy, that earned over 50 million Spotify listens.

In 2018 he worked with Grammy-nominated producer [[Dru Decaro]],<ref name="The B-Side"/> and released his debut EP, ''natural colors'' and the single "Sweet Home".<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.clashmusic.com/news/listen-christian-french-sweet-home|title= Listen: Christian French – 'Sweet Home' |website=Clash |last=Murray |first=Robin |date=August 22, 2018 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> That same year he left college to tour with [[Chelsea Cutler]] on her Sleeping with Roses Tour.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://atwoodmagazine.com/cf-christian-french-feature-2019/|title= A Dream Becoming a Reality: A Conversation With Christian French |website=[[Atwood Magazine]]|last=Fitzgerald |first=Kelsey |date=June 10, 2019 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> In 2019, French was a guest on [[Hoodie Allen]]'s single "Come Around"<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.allmusic.com/artist/christian-french-mn0003864255/biography|title= Artist Biography by Matt Collar |website=All Music|last=Collar |first=Matt |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> and toured with Quinn XCII on the From Tour With Love spring tour.<ref name="The B-Side"/>

That same year, Christian signed to [[Disruptor Records]]/[[Sony Music]]<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://socialitelife.com/singer-christian-french-answers-the-socialite-seven/|title= Singer Christian French Answers The Socialite Seven |website=Socialite Life |last=Fitzgerald |first=Christine |date=April 21, 2020 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> and released the 6-track EP ''bright side of the moon'', ruminating on mental health and tales of romance. The single "Head First", produced by Andrew Luce, amassed more than 32 million Spotify spins and 2&nbsp;million YouTube views. The accompanying video premiered on [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] on June 18, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8516211/christian-french-head-first-video|title= Christian French Dives 'Head First' in Mesmerizing New Video: Exclusive |website=Billboard |last=Spruch |first=Kirsten |date=June 18, 2019 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref>

On January 17, 2020, the video for his track "time of our lives" premiered on [[Ones to Watch]].<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/christian-french-reminds-us-that-this-is-the-time|title= Christian French Reminds Us That This Is the "time of our lives" [Premiere] |website=Ones to Watch |last=Polo |first=Maxamillion|date=January 17, 2020 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> In March 2020, ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine named his single, "crowded room", one of its Best New Songs of the Week<ref name="Bruner"/> and it appeared on Spotify's New Music Friday playlist.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://breakingandentering.net/2020/03/23/newmusicmonday-christian-french-releases-his-latest-single/|title= #newmusicmonday: Christian French Releases His Latest Single|website=Breaking and Entering|last=Wellen |first=Lily |date=March 23, 2020 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref> His next EP, ''Good Things Take Time'',<ref name="Wallock"/> is scheduled for release in summer 2020. The first track from that release, "i think too much", was released in spring 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://hollywoodlife.com/2020/04/22/christian-french-i-think-too-much-new-song-interview/|title= Christian French’s New Song Is All About‘Checking Myself’ When His Thoughts Are‘Too Much’ |website=Hollywood Life |last=Brow |first=Jason |date=April 22, 2020 |accessdate=July 4, 2020}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Growing up, French was an avid hockey player. French played for local Indianapolis travel teams for much of his youth. During the 2013–2014 season, French was the captain of the Indiana Jr. Ice U16 AAA team that featured [[Logan Brown]] who was selected in the [[2016 NHL Entry Draft]] 11th overall by the [[Ottawa Senators]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Indiana Jr. Ice 16U AAA |url=https://www.eliteprospects.com/team/12049/indiana-jr.-ice-16u-aaa/2013-2014#players |website=Elite Prospects |publisher=Elite Prospects |accessdate=October 1, 2020}}</ref> In his three years at [[Indiana University Bloomington|Indiana University]], French would play for the [[Indiana University Bloomington|Indiana University]] hockey team each year.<ref>{{cite web |title=Christian French |url=https://www.eliteprospects.com/player/311953/christian-french |website=Elite Prospects |publisher=Elite Prospects |accessdate=October 1, 2020}}</ref>
