'''Lorne Balfe''' is a Scottish [[composer]] and [[Record producer|producer]] of [[film]], [[Television program|television]], and [[video game]] scores. A veteran of [[Hans Zimmer]]'s [[Remote Control Productions (American company)|Remote Control Productions]], Balfe is known for his composing music for big-budget films like ''[[13 Hours: The Secret Soldiers of Benghazi]],'' ''[[Terminator Genisys]]'', ''[[Mission: Impossible – Fallout]]'', and the [[DreamWorks Animation|DreamWorks animated films]] ''[[Home (2015 animated film)|Home]]'' and ''[[Penguins of Madagascar]]'', as well as the video games ''[[Assassin's Creed: Revelations]]'', ''[[Assassin's Creed III]]'', ''[[Crysis 2]]'', ''[[Skylanders]]'' and the ''[[Call of Duty]]'' franchise. He has also scored the television series ''[[The Bible (miniseries)|The Bible]]'', ''[[Marcella (TV series)|Marcella]]'', ''[[The Crown (TV series)|The Crown]]'', and ''[[Genius (U.S. TV series)|Genius]]'', the latter for which he was nominated for a [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Original Main Title Theme Music]].

Balfe went to [[Fettes College]] in Edinburgh, where he had a music scholarship.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.scotsman.com/arts-and-culture/music/composing-a-career-of-note-in-la-1-846507|title=Composing a career of note in LA|website=[[The Scotsman]]|date=9 July 2002}}</ref>
