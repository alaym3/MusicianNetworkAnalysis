He has received [[List of awards and nominations received by Timothée Chalamet|several accolades]] throughout his career, including nominations for an [[Academy Awards|Academy Award]], three [[BAFTA Film Awards]], two [[Golden Globe Awards]], four [[Screen Actors Guild Awards]], and four [[Critics' Choice Movie Awards]]. Chalamet began his acting career in short films and commercials, before appearing in the television drama series ''[[Homeland (TV series)|Homeland]]'' in 2012. Two years later, he made his feature film debut in the comedy-drama ''[[Men, Women & Children (film)|Men, Women & Children]]'' and subsequently appeared in the science-fiction film ''[[Interstellar (film)|Interstellar]]''.

Chalamet's [[Breakthrough role|breakthrough]] came in 2017 with his role as [[Elio Perlman]] in [[Luca Guadagnino]]'s coming-of-age romantic drama ''[[Call Me by Your Name (film)|Call Me by Your Name]]'', after which he appeared in the coming-of-age films ''[[Hot Summer Nights (film)|Hot Summer Nights]]'' and ''[[Lady Bird (film)|Lady Bird]]'' as well as the western film ''[[Hostiles (film)|Hostiles]]''. His performance in ''Call Me by Your Name'' earned him an Academy Award nomination for [[Academy Award for Best Actor|Best Actor]]; at 22 years old, it made him the [[List of oldest and youngest Academy Award winners and nominees#Youngest nominees 2|third-youngest]] nominee in the category. He then played [[Nic Sheff]] in the autobiographical drama ''[[Beautiful Boy (2018 film)|Beautiful Boy]]'' (2018), for which he was nominated for a [[Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Motion Picture|Golden Globe Award]], a [[Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Male Actor in a Supporting Role|Screen Actors Guild Award]], and a [[BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role|BAFTA Film Award]]. In 2019, Chalamet starred as [[Henry V of England]] and [[Theodore "Laurie" Laurence]] in the period dramas ''[[The King (2019 film)|The King]]'' and ''[[Little Women (2019 film)|Little Women]]'', respectively. In 2021, he starred as the lead role of [[Paul Atreides]] in [[Denis Villeneuve]]'s epic science fiction film ''[[Dune (2021 film)|Dune]]'' and in the [[ensemble cast]] of [[Wes Anderson]]'s ''[[The French Dispatch]]''.

On stage, Chalamet starred in [[John Patrick Shanley]]'s autobiographical play ''Prodigal Son'' in 2016, for which he won a [[Lucille Lortel Award]] and garnered a nomination for a [[Drama League Award]].

==Career==
===Early roles and acting background (2008–2016)===
As a child, Chalamet appeared in several commercials and acted in two horror [[short film]]s called ''Sweet Tooth'' and ''Clown'' before making his television debut on an episode of the long-running [[police procedural]] series ''[[Law & Order]]'' (2009), playing a murder victim.<ref name="Verge" /> He followed this with a minor role in the [[television film]] ''[[Loving Leah]]'' (2009). In 2011, he made his stage debut in the [[Off-Broadway]] play ''The Talls'', a [[Coming-of-age story|coming-of-age]] comedy set in the 1970s, in which he played a sexually curious 12-year-old Nicholas. The chief theatre critic of ''[[New York Daily News]]'' wrote "Chalamet hilariously captures a tween's awakening curiosities about sex."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/08/17/theater/reviews/the-talls-by-anna-kerrigan-review.html|title=A 17-Year-Old Beanstalk From a Wholesome '70s Family Is Eager to Go Far Out|work=[[The New York Times]]|url-access=limited|first=Charles|last=Isherwood|date=August 16, 2011|access-date=August 9, 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180919105109/https://www.nytimes.com/2011/08/17/theater/reviews/the-talls-by-anna-kerrigan-review.html|archive-date=September 19, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music-arts/talls-review-family-drama-familiar-story-excellent-acting-play-extra-boost-article-1.950067|title='The Talls' review: Family drama is familiar story, but excellent acting gives play extra boost|work=[[New York Daily News]]|first=Joe|last=Dziemianowicz|date=August 16, 2011|access-date=August 9, 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180919105809/https://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/music-arts/talls-review-family-drama-familiar-story-excellent-acting-play-extra-boost-article-1.950067|archive-date=September 19, 2018}}</ref> In 2012, he had recurring roles in the drama series ''[[Royal Pains]]'' and the [[spy fiction|spy-thriller]] series ''[[Homeland (TV series)|Homeland]]'', in which he played [[Finn Walden]], the rebellious son of the Vice President.
