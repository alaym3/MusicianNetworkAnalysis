'''The Irrepressibles''' is the creative guise of British musician Jamie Irrepressible.<ref name="Advocate">{{ cite news | first=Christopher | last=Rudolph | url=http://www.advocate.com/arts-entertainment/music/2013/02/18/irrepressibles-lead-singer-talks-about-being-gay-and-men-hes | title=The Irrepressibles Lead Singer Talks About Being Gay and the Men He's Loved | work=[[The Advocate (LGBT magazine)|The Advocate]] | date=18 February 2013 | accessdate=1 October 2016}}</ref>

==History==
Created in 2002, the band have released three full studio albums to date and six EPs. All songs are written by Jamie Irrepressible. The name ''The Irrepressibles'' is "about breaking boundaries in music and being honest about being gay in music."<ref name="Advocate"/> Based in London, England, for many years, Jamie is currently based in [[Berlin]], Germany. Jamie changed his name from "Jamie McDermott" to "Jamie Irrepressible" in 2013 on his collaboration with Norwegian electronic duo [[Röyksopp]].

===''2009: From the Circus to the Sea''===
Released in January 2009 as the soundtrack to Shelly Love's ''The Forgotten Circus'', ''[[The Independent]]'' described The Irrepressibles as one of Britain's best kept secrets.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/album-the-irrepressibles-from-the-circus-to-the-sea-of-naked-design-1515573.html|title=Album: The Irrepressibles, From the Circus to the Sea, (Of Naked|work=The Independent|access-date=2018-06-13|language=en-GB}}</ref>

===''2010: Mirror Mirror''===
On 11 January 2010, The Irrepressibles released their debut studio album, ''Mirror Mirror'', a collection of 12 baroque pop songs produced by [[Dimitri Tikovoi]] and William Turner Duffin, and written by Jamie McDermott. The album features the single "In This Shirt".

''Mirror Mirror'' received critical acclaim. ''[[The Guardian]]'' called it "theatrical and very different, a ripe, colourful riposte to all that is [[Simon Cowell|Cowell]]"<ref>{{ cite news | url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2010/jan/10/irrepressibles-mirror-mirror | title=The Irrepressibles: Mirror, Mirror | work=The Guardian | accessdate=13 February 2014}}</ref> and ''[[The Independent]]'' described it as "a dramatic soundscape dripping with echoes of the [[Weimar Republic]] and ''[[Belle Époque|la belle époque]]''."<ref>{{ cite news | url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/album-the-irrepressibles-mirror-mirror-v2-1863668.html | title=Album: The Irrepressibles, Mirror Mirror (V2) | work=The Independent | accessdate=13 February 2014}}</ref>

==="In This Shirt"===
"In This Shirt" garnered critical and public acclaim after it was used as the soundtrack for the short film ''The Lady Is Dead'' by Israeli production company PAG Films, described by [[Sundance Channel (United States)|Sundance Channel]] as "fantastic".<ref>{{ cite news | url=http://www.sundancechannel.com/blog/2010/08/pag-the-lady-is-dead | title=Pag: The Lady Is Dead | publisher=Sundance Channel | accessdate=13 February 2014}}</ref> It was remixed by various artists including [[Röyksopp]], [[Hercules & Love Affair]] and [[Zero 7]].

The song received renewed attention in 2018 when it was used by French figure skater [[Kévin Aymoz]] as his free skating program.<ref name=interview18>{{cite web |url=https://www.inthelopodcast.com/news/2018/12/04/interview-kevin-aymoz |title=Interview: Kévin Aymoz at Internationaux de France 2018 |publisher=in the loop |website=inthelopodcast.com |accessdate=5 December 2020|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20201205194811/https://www.inthelopodcast.com/news/2018/12/04/interview-kevin-aymoz |archivedate=5 December 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref> It also received renewed attention 2020 through its widespread use in videos on [[TikTok]] during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name=tiktok>{{cite web |url=https://www.tiktok.com/music/In-This-Shirt-6836350448765651717 |title=TikTok In This Shirt |publisher=TikTok |website=tiktok.com |accessdate=5 December 2020|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20201205194430/https://www.tiktok.com/music/In-This-Shirt-6836350448765651717 |archivedate=5 December 2020 |url-status=live}}</ref>

===2012: ''Nude''===
In November 2012, The Irrepressibles released their second album, ''Nude''. Self-produced by Jamie McDermott, the album similarly received critical acclaim. ''[[The Quietus]]'' described it as a "remarkably varied and unpredictable album" stating that "the results border on the spectacular".<ref>{{ cite news | url=http://thequietus.com/articles/10694-the-irrepressibles-nude-review | title=The Irrepressibles NUDE | publisher=The Quietus | accessdate=13 February 2014}}</ref> ''The Independent'' gave it 4 out of 5 stars, calling it "an act of bravery in a cowardly world" where McDermott "heralds homosexual love as a heroic, romantic and redemptive force".<ref>{{ cite news | url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/album-the-irrepressibles-nude-of-naked-design-8219374.html | title=Album: The Irrepressibles, Nude (Of Naked Design) | work=The Independent | accessdate=13 February 2014}}</ref>

===''Nude EP''s===
2013 saw the release of ''Nude: Landscapes'', the first of three EPs inspired by – and expanding on – the band's second album. This first EP saw the band take a "minimal symphonic" and "acoustic" direction, as described by McDermott in an interview with [[QX (British magazine)|QX Magazine]].<ref>{{ cite news | url=http://www.qxmagazine.com/blog-event/the-irrepressibles | title=The Irrepressibles | publisher=QX | accessdate=13 February 2014 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140227192739/http://www.qxmagazine.com/blog-event/the-irrepressibles/ | archivedate=27 February 2014 | df=dmy-all }}</ref> The second EP, ''Nude: Viscera'', released on 14 February 2014, "brings together influences from New Wave, Grunge, and Exotica".<ref>{{ cite news | url=http://brightondome.org/event/4599/the_irrepressibles | title=The Irrepressibles | publisher=Brighton Dome | accessdate=13 February 2014}}</ref> The third EP, ''Nude: Forbidden'', was released on 6 April 2014.

=== 2018: Third album: Superheroes ===
On 31 May 2018, The Irrepressibles released a new single, "Submission", from their forthcoming third album. The video, directed by [[Cyprus|Cypriot]] filmmaker [https://www.savvaspictures.com/ Savvas Stavrou], was featured by [[Nowness]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nowness.com/picks/the-irrepressibles-submission-savvas-stavrou|title=The Irrepressibles – Submission – Savvas Stavrou|last=|first=|date=|website=www.nowness.com|access-date=2018-06-23}}</ref> A second single, "Dominance", was released on 22 November 2018. The visuals, a collaboration between Italian photographer Paride Mirabilio and Turkish director Mertcan Mertbilek, were featured by Pornceptual.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://pornceptual.com/the-irrepressibles/|title=The Irrepressibles|date=2018-12-11|website=PORNCEPTUAL|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-19}}</ref> A third single, "Anxiety", was released on 29 November 2018. A fourth single, "International", was released on 22 March 2019. The video was directed by Jamie Irrepressible in collaboration with Ukrainian director Alexey Romanowski and was featured by New Noise Magazine.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newnoisemagazine.com/stream-irrepressibles-international/|title=Song Premiere: The Irrepressibles - "International"|date=2019-03-22|website=New Noise Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-19}}</ref> A fifth single,"Let Go (Everybody Move Your Body)", was released on March 27, 2020, it was described by [[Clash (magazine)|CLASH Magazine]] as "an absolute bulldozer - a stunning pop missile that explodes staid sexual norms.."<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Irrepressibles New Single Is An Absolute Bulldozer|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/the-irrepressibles-new-single-is-an-absolute-bulldozer|access-date=2020-11-12|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> A video was directed by Savvas Stavrou and was featured by [https://www.kaltblut-magazine.com KALTBLUT] Magazine. A sixth single, The Most Beautiful Boy (Strong Outside A Man But Inside a Boy), was released on July 23, 2020 alongside the album release.  The album was acclaimed by the media with [[Loud and Quiet]] describing the album as "cleverly inflecting the central romance withi ncisive commentary on masculinity and mental health.",<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Irrepressibles - Superheroes - Album Review|url=https://www.loudandquiet.com/reviews/the-irrepressibles-superheroes/|access-date=2020-10-29|website=Loud And Quiet|language=en-US}}</ref> whilst The Quietus describing it as "hard not to fall in love with this album" and a "beautiful open book of his journey uniting with his mental health, masculinity and homosexuality".<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Quietus {{!}} Reviews {{!}} The Irrepressibles|url=https://thequietus.com/articles/28533-the-irrepressibles-superheroes-review|access-date=2020-10-29|website=The Quietus|language=en-us}}</ref>

===Collaborations===
The Irrepressibles had Jamie McDermott providing guest vocals for [[Jake Williams|Rex the Dog]]'s single "Do You Feel What I Feel" and in 2013 on Röyksopp's singles "Something in My Heart" and "Twenty Thirteen". In 2012, Jamie collaborated with Hotel Pro Forma and the [[Latvian Radio Choir]] on ''WAR SUM UP''. Jamie composed the music for ''WAR SUM UP'', which toured extensively worldwide, receiving acclaim from Danish outlet ''[[Gaffa (magazine)|Gaffa]]'',<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://gaffa.dk/anmeldelse/54025/hotel-pro-forma-war-sum-up-odense-teater|title=Hotel Pro Forma: War Sum Up, Odense Teater|work=gaffa.dk|access-date=2018-06-13|language=da}}</ref> as well as ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/11/04/arts/music/war-sum-up-at-the-howard-gilman-opera-house-at-bam.html|title=‘War Sum Up’ at the Howard Gilman Opera House at BAM|last=Smith|first=Steve|access-date=2018-06-13|language=en}}</ref>

In 2014, he also featured on Röyksopp's album ''[[The Inevitable End]]'' on the tracks "You Know I Have to Go", "I Had This Thing", "Here She Comes Again", and "Compulsion", in addition to the previously released "Something in My Heart" on which he was credited for the first time as Jamie Irrepressible. In 2015, having relocated to [[Berlin, Germany]], Jamie began working with American [[alternative country]] artist Jon Campbell and produced his debut EP, released in 2016.<ref>{{cite news | first=Josh | last=Withey | url=http://www.gaytimes.co.uk/culture/29391/introducing-jon-campbells-unique-brand-of-alternative-country/ | title=Introducing Jon Campbell's unique brand of alternative-country | work=[[Gay Times]] | date=29 February 2016 | accessdate=1 October 2016 }}</ref>
