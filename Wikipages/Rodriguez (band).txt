'''Rodriguez''' was a [[Rock music|rock]] band active in the mid to late 1990s based in [[San Luis Obispo]], California.

The band is notable as an early collaboration between Matt Ward (who later came to prominence as [[M. Ward]]) and [[Kyle Field (musician)|Kyle Field]] (aka [[Little Wings]]), along with a series of drummers: Jake Hockel, Sanjeev Srinivas, and Mike Funk.<ref>"[http://media.www.mustangdaily.net/media/storage/paper860/news/2007/02/13/Columns/Little.Wings.Takes.Flight.In.Slo-2715349.shtml Little Wings takes flight in SLO]", ''Mustang Daily'', 13 February 2007</ref><ref name="lw">"[http://www.dreamgeo.com/LittleWings.html Little Wings] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060410075305/http://www.dreamgeo.com/LittleWings.html |date=2006-04-10 }}" (Kyle Field interview), dreamgeo.com</ref>  The band was described by Field as "the band in which I learned how to play music".<ref name="lw" />

They completed one full-length album, 2000's ''Swing Like A Metronome'', which was produced by [[Jason Lytle]] of [[Grandaddy]] and released by independent label Devil in the Woods.  The band also released a couple of songs recorded by Adam Selzer from the [[M. Ward]] band and [[Norfolk & Western (band)|Norfolk & Western]].  Co-dependent Records released the "Weren't a Problem" EP as a 7" in 1996 and a cassette entitled "Cash Crops and Box Plots" in 1995.
