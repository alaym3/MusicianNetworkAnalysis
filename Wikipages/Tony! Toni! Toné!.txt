'''Tony! Toni! Toné!''' was an [[United States|American]] [[Soul music|soul]]/[[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] [[band (music)|group]] from [[Oakland, California]], popular during the late 1980s and early to mid-1990s. During the band's heyday, it was composed of [[D'wayne Wiggins]] on lead [[vocals]] and [[guitar]], his brother [[Raphael Saadiq]] (born Charles Ray Wiggins) on lead vocals and [[bass (instrument)|bass]], and their cousin Timothy Christian Riley on [[drum]]s and [[Keyboard instrument|keyboards]]. Originally, the band went by "Tony, Toni, Toné" as a joke, until they realized it "had a nice ring to it".<ref>{{Cite web |url= http://watch.muchmusic.com/specials/ota-live/ |title=Specials : OTA Live : Raphael Saadiq (Live Interview) 1/4 |access-date= 2008-12-26 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20081218040919/http://watch.muchmusic.com/specials/ota-live/#clip123632 |archive-date= 2008-12-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

After their debut album ''[[Who? (album)|Who?]]''<ref name="Wynn">{{cite web|last=Wynn|first=Ron|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/tony!-toni!-ton%C3%A9!-mn0000790667|title=Tony! Toni! Toné! – Music Biography, Credits and Discography|publisher=[[Allmusic]]. [[Rovi Corporation]]|access-date=2012-08-19}}</ref> in 1988 followed by ''[[The Revival (Tony! Toni! Toné! album)|The Revival]]'' in 1990, the group achieved their greatest commercial success with the [[RIAA certification|double platinum]] certified ''[[Sons of Soul]]'' in 1993.<ref>{{cite web|last=Erlewine|first=Stephen Thomas|author-link=Stephen Thomas Erlewine|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/sons-of-soul-mw0000619408|title=Sons of Soul – Tony! Toni! Toné!|publisher=Allmusic. Rovi Corporation|access-date=2012-08-19}}</ref><ref name="riaa">{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?table=SEARCH_RESULTS&artist=%22Tony!%20Toni!%20Tone!%22|title=US Certifications > Tony! Toni! Toné!|publisher=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]|access-date=2013-06-10}}</ref> Tony! Toni! Toné! disbanded after the release of their fourth album ''[[House of Music]]'' (1996), which critics cite as their best work.<ref>{{cite web|last=Christgau|first=Robert|url=http://music.msn.com/music/consumerguide/2008/10/|title=Inside Music: Consumer Guide|publisher=[[MSN Music]]. [[Microsoft]]|date=October 2008|access-date=2012-03-30|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111209061421/http://music.msn.com/music/consumerguide/2008/10/|archive-date=2011-12-09|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|author=Schruers, Fred|editor1-first=Nathan|editor1-last=Brackett|editor1-link=Nathan Brackett|editor2-first=Christian|editor2-last=Hoard|title=[[The Rolling Stone Album Guide|The New Rolling Stone Album Guide]]|others=Completely Revised and Updated 4th Edition|publisher=[[Simon & Schuster]]|date=November 1, 2004|isbn=0-7432-0169-8|page=[https://archive.org/details/newrollingstonea00brac/page/818 818]|display-authors=etal}}</ref>

==History==
[[File:Amir Khalil of "Tony! Toni! Toné!".jpg|thumb|left|275px|Amir Khalil of Tony! Toni! Toné! performing in [[Louisville, Kentucky]], 2012]]
===1988&ndash;1992: ''Who?'' and ''The Revival''===

Their first album, ''[[Who? (album)|Who?]]'', produced and co-written by Denzil Foster and Thomas McElroy, was released in 1988. The album went [[Music recording sales certification|gold]] and had several hit singles. The first of these, "[[Little Walter (song)|Little Walter]]" went to #1 on the R&B charts. The next three singles, "[[Born Not to Know]]", "Baby Doll" and "For the Love of You" were all Top 10 R&B singles. ''Who?'' was a modest success.<ref name="SacBee">{{cite news|date=November 5, 1993 |title=From Tonies to Townies - Hot Soul Stars Tony! Toni! Tone! Warm Up for a National Tour in Their Hometown |newspaper=[[The Sacramento Bee]] |page=TK14 |publisher=[[The McClatchy Company]] |location=Sacramento|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=SB&p_theme=sb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EB0DA7AA655A00D&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |access-date= 2011-07-24}}</ref> It charted for 44 weeks on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard 200|Top Pop Albums]], peaking at number 69,<ref name="bbWho">[{{BillboardURLbyName|artist=tony! toni! toné!|chart=all}} Who? - Tony! Toni! Tone! | Billboard.com]. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. Retrieved on 2011-06-17.{{dead link|date=March 2018}}</ref> and produced four [[single (music)|single]]s, including the R&B hit "[[Little Walter (song)|Little Walter]]". On December 5, 1989, the album was certified [[RIAA certification|gold]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), for shipments of 500,000 copies in the United States.<ref name="RIAAwho">[http://riaa.org/goldandplatinumdata.php?resultpage=resultpage=1&table=SEARCH_RESULTS&action=&title=&artist=Tony!%20Toni!%20Tone!&format=&debutLP=&category=&sex=&releaseDate=&requestNo=&type=&level=&label=&company=&certificationDate=&awardDescription=&catalogNo=&aSex=&rec_id=&charField=&gold=&platinum=&multiPlat=&level2=&certDate=&album=&id=&after=&before=&startMonth=1&endMonth=1&startYear=1958&endYear=2008&sort=Artist&perPage=25# RIAA - Recording Industry Association of America]{{Dead link|date=September 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}. [[Recording Industry Association of America]]. Retrieved on 2011-06-17.</ref> As of August 1993, it has sold over 700,000 copies in the US.<ref>{{cite news|date=August 10, 1993|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=ST&s_site=dfw&p_multi=ST&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EAF8F158A1A2A0A&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=13 CLASS ACTS|newspaper=[[Fort Worth Star-Telegram]]|publisher=Star-Telegram Operating|location=Fort Worth|access-date=2012-05-05}}</ref>

Inspired by live instrumentation, [[turntablism]], and classic [[soul music]], Tony! Toni! Toné! recorded and produced their second album, ''[[The Revival (Tony! Toni! Toné! album)|The Revival]]'', mostly themselves and released it in 1990 to commercial success.{{sfn|Hildebrand|1994|p=235}}<ref name="Gonzales">{{cite journal|last=Gonzales|first=Michael A.|date=February 1997|title=Family Ties|journal=[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]|volume=5|issue=1|page=76|location=New York|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qCwEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA74|access-date=March 27, 2012}}</ref> Released on May 8, 1990, by [[Wing Records]],<ref name="RIAA"/> ''The Revival'' charted for 64 weeks on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Billboard 200|Top Pop Albums]], peaking at number 34 on the chart.<ref name="bbRevival">"[{{BillboardURLbyName|artist=tony! toni! toné!|chart=all}} The Revival - Tony! Toni! Toné!]". ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. Retrieved on June 17, 2011.</ref> The group's second album ''[[The Revival (Tony! Toni! Toné! album)|The Revival]]'' was released in 1990 and reached [[RIAA certification|platinum]] status. The album spawned several #1 R&B hits with "[[It Never Rains (In Southern California)]]", "[[Feels Good]]", "[[The Blues (song)|The Blues]]", and "[[Whatever You Want (Tony! Toni! Tone! song)|Whatever You Want]]" all topping the R&B charts. "Feels Good" was the group's first single to breach the Top 10 of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and went gold. The album's second [[single (music)|single]] "Feels Good" was released on June 19 and certified [[RIAA certification|gold]] on November 13 after it had shipped 500,000 copies.<ref name="RIAA">"[http://riaa.org/goldandplatinumdata.php?resultpage=resultpage=1&table=SEARCH_RESULTS&action=&title=&artist=Tony!%20Toni!%20Tone!&format=&debutLP=&category=&sex=&releaseDate=&requestNo=&type=&level=&label=&company=&certificationDate=&awardDescription=&catalogNo=&aSex=&rec_id=&charField=&gold=&platinum=&multiPlat=&level2=&certDate=&album=&id=&after=&before=&startMonth=1&endMonth=1&startYear=1958&endYear=2008&sort=Artist&perPage=25# RIAA Gold & Platinum Searchable Database - Tony! Retrieved on June 17, 2011.</ref> The single topped the R&B chart for two weeks and reached number nine on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot 100]] in the fall of 1990, going on to sell over one million copies.<ref name="Hogan1">{{cite web|last=Hogan|first=Ed|url=https://www.allmusic.com/song/feels-good-mt0007403822|title=Feels Good - Tony! ''The Revival'' broadened the group's exposure to fans beyond their initial R&B audience.{{sfn|Bourgoin|LaBlanc|1994|p=249}} However, they became ambivalent about their newfound mainstream success and their music being labeled "retro" by critics.<ref name="Gonzales"/> In an interview for ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' magazine, lead singer and bassist [[Raphael Saadiq|Raphael Wiggins]] expressed his dissatisfaction with the music industry, saying that "every record company wants to get a group and put 'em in a [[Mercedes-Benz|Benz]] with a car phone and a beeper, show them dressing in three different outfits, put them in a video shot on a beach with lots of swinging bikinis. You won't ever see us on a beach. We're just down-to-earth, funky, like-to-play guys."<ref name="People">{{cite journal |last1=Linden |first1=Amy |last2=Givens |first2=Ron |last3=Tomashoff |first3=Craig |url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20110733,00.html |title=Picks and Pans Main: Song |journal=[[People (magazine)|People]] |date=July 5, 1993 |volume=40 |issue=1 |access-date=March 27, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120512024422/http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20110733,00.html |archive-date=May 12, 2012 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Griffin">{{cite journal |last=Griffin |first=Gil |date=June 23, 1993 |title=Recordings; 2 Hip-Hop Trios, Back With Brio |journal=[[The Washington Post]] |pages=C.07  |url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/washingtonpost/access/72163275.html?dids=72163275:72163275&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Jun+23%2C+1993&author=Gil+Griffin&pub=The+Washington+Post+(pre-1997+Fulltext)&desc=Recordings%3B+2+Hip-Hop+Trios%2C+Back+With+Brio&pqatl=google |access-date=March 27, 2012}}</ref> Before considering a follow-up album, the band recorded several songs for film soundtracks, including "Me and You" for ''[[Boyz n the Hood (soundtrack)|Boyz n the Hood]]'' (1991), "House Party (I Don't Know What You Come to Do)" for ''[[House Party 2 (soundtrack)|House Party 2]]'' (1991), and "Waiting on You" for ''[[Poetic Justice (soundtrack)|Poetic Justice]]'' (1993).{{sfn|Bourgoin|LaBlanc|1994|p=249}}

===1992&ndash;1995: ''Sons of Soul''===
Having fulfilled their creative intentions with ''The Revival'', Tony! Toni! Toné! wanted to pay homage to their musical influences with ''Sons of Soul''.<ref name="Rule">{{cite news|last=Rule |first=Sheila |date=September 29, 1993 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1993/09/29/arts/the-pop-life-381193.html?pagewanted=2 |title=The Pop Life |newspaper=[[The New York Times]] |access-date=March 27, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140108133517/http://www.nytimes.com/1993/09/29/arts/the-pop-life-381193.html?pagewanted=2 |archive-date=January 8, 2014 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="Sculley">{{cite news|last=Sculley |first=Alan |date=January 14, 1994 |url=http://articles.dailypress.com/1994-01-14/features/9401140125_1_raphael-wiggins-soul-wilson-pickett |title=Tony! Toni! Tone! Revives Sounds of Soul |newspaper=[[Daily Press (Virginia)|Daily Press]] |location=Virginia |access-date=March 27, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518102313/http://articles.dailypress.com/1994-01-14/features/9401140125_1_raphael-wiggins-soul-wilson-pickett |archive-date=May 18, 2015 |url-status=live }}</ref> In a 1993 interview for ''[[The New York Times]]'', Wiggins elaborated on their direction for the album, stating "We're paying homage to a lot of older artists who paved the way for us artists like [[the Temptations]], [[Sly and the Family Stone]], [[Earth, Wind and Fire]]. They're the people who inspired us when we were growing up, people like [[Aretha Franklin]], [[James Brown]]. We feel we're the sons of everything and all those people who came before us."<ref name="Rule"/> He also explained the album's title as a declaration of them being descendants of those artists, "not in a grandiose sense, but from the standpoint that we really are the musical offspring of all that's come before us&nbsp;... paying homage to our past, but creating in a contemporary environment."<ref name="Keyboard">{{cite journal|title=Tony! Toni! Tone!|journal=[[Keyboard (magazine)|Keyboard]]|year=1993|volume=19|issue=7|page=16}}</ref>

Tony! Toni! Toné! took a hiatus as a group after the commercial and critical success of ''Sons of Soul''. According to vocalist and bassist [[Raphael Saadiq|Raphael Wiggins]], each member had pursued individual music projects, and "the group was trying to figure out where everybody's time, space and head was at."<ref>{{cite news|ref={{SfnRef|Anon. (a)|1996|p=12}}|author=Anon. [a]|date=November 21, 1996|title=Tony Toni Toné Hits Comeback Trail|newspaper=The Muncie Times|page = 12}}</ref> He, [[D'wayne Wiggins]], and Timothy Christian Riley worked on songwriting and production for other recording artists during the band's hiatus, including [[D'Angelo]], [[En Vogue]], [[Karyn White]], [[Tevin Campbell]], and [[A Tribe Called Quest]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Smith|first=Shawnee|date=October 5, 1996|title=Tony Toni Toné Rebuild Their 'House'&nbsp;– Mercury Set Finds Trio in Cohesive Style|magazine=Billboard |volume=108|issue=40|publisher=Nielsen Business Media|id=0QkEAAAAMBAJ|issn=0006-2510|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0QkEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA16|access-date=November 20, 2012|via=Google Books|page = 16}}</ref><ref name = jones>{{cite news|ref={{SfnRef|Jones (a)|1996|p=6.D}} |last=Jones|first= Steve [a]|date=November 19, 1996|title=A Retro-Active Dwelling Tony Toni Tone Returns with 'House of Music'|newspaper=[[USA Today]]|publisher=[[Gannett Company]] |access-date=November 20, 2012|page = 6.D|url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/USAToday/access/16332155.html?dids=16332155:16332155&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Nov+19%2C+1996&author=Steve+Jones&pub=USA+TODAY+(pre-1997+Fulltext)&desc=A+retro-active+dwelling+Tony+Toni+Tone+returns+with+'House+of+Music'&pqatl=google |url-access=subscription }}</ref> Raphael Wiggins adopted the surname "Saadiq" for his professional name in 1994—"man of his word" in Arabic—and released his solo [[single (music)|single]] "[[Ask of You]]" in 1995.<ref>{{cite web|last=Coker|first=Cheo Hodari|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1997-01-12/entertainment/ca-17719_1_tony-toni-tone|title=Time to Jam&nbsp;– or Jam?|work=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=January 12, 1997|access-date=November 20, 2012}}</ref><ref name = jones/> Their work outside the band led to rumors of a break-up during the time between albums.<ref name="jones" /> Tony! Toni! Toné! eventually regrouped and began recording ''House of Music'' in September 1995.<ref>{{cite AV media notes|ref={{SfnRef|Anon. (b)|1996}}|author=Anon. [b]|year=1996|title=House of Music|others=Tony! Toni! Toné!|type=CD booklet|publisher=Mercury Records|id=P 2–34250|location=United States}}</ref>

===1996&ndash;1998: ''House of Music'' and breakup===
In 1996, the group released their final studio album to date, ''[[House of Music]]''. The album lacked the strong singles of earlier entries, only getting "Thinking Of You" & "Let's Get Down" into the top 10 on the R&B charts, with "Thinking Of You" hitting #22 on the Hot 100, though it eventually reached platinum status.

''[[House of Music]]'' expanded on Tony! Toni! Toné!'s previous traditional R&B-influenced work by emphasizing live instrumentation and ballads.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Peitier|first=Sidney|year=1997|title=Tony Toni Toné, House of Music|magazine=Upscale: The Successful Black Magazine|publisher=Upscale Communications|volume=10|id=VY8OAQAAMAAJ|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VY8OAQAAMAAJ|access-date=November 20, 2012|via=Google Books}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Brown|first=David W.|url=http://www.thecrimson.com/article/1996/12/6/tony-toni-tone-brings-back-unprocessed/|title=Tony Toni Tone Brings Back Unprocessed Spirit of Soul|work=[[The Harvard Crimson]]|date=December 6, 1996|access-date=November 20, 2012}}</ref> In the opinion of ''[[Daily Herald (Arlington Heights)|Daily Herald]]'' writer Dan Kening, the album continued the band's mix of contemporary R&B and old-fashioned soul, deeming it "half a tribute to their '60s and '70s soul music roots and half a masterful blend of modern smooth balladeering and danceable funk."<ref>{{cite news|last= Kening|first=Dan|date=December 20, 1996|title=Tony Toni Tone Finds Right Groove in 'House' Album Reviews|newspaper=[[Daily Herald (Arlington Heights)|Daily Herald]]|publisher=Paddock Publications|page = 6|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=ADHB&p_theme=adhb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0ED027B37708C135&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |access-date=November 20, 2012 |url-access=subscription }}</ref> Released on November 19, 1996, ''House of Music'' reached number 32 on the [[Billboard 200 |''Billboard'' 200]] and spent 31 weeks on the chart.{{sfn|Anon. (a)|n.d.}} In its first eight weeks, the album sold 318,502 copies in the US.{{sfn |Goldberg|1997}} Tony! Toni! Toné! inaugurated its release with a [[virtual press conference|satellite press conference]] and in-store performance at a small retail outlet in the [[San Francisco Bay Area]]. They also embarked on a tour of [[historically black colleges and universities|historically black college]]s and Black Independent Coalition record shops after "[[Let's Get Down (Tony! Toni! Toné! song)|Let's Get Down]]" had been sent to R&B and [[crossover (music)|crossover]] radio on October 28 as the album's [[lead single]]; its [[music video]] was released to outlets such as [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]], [[The Box (US TV channel)|The Box]], and [[MTV]].{{sfn|Smith|1996|p=20}} Tony! Toni! Toné! performed the song on the sketch comedy show ''[[All That]]''; on the music variety program ''[[Soul Train]]'', they performed "Let's Get Down" and "Annie May".<ref>{{harvnb|Anon. (b)|n.d.}}; {{harvnb |Anon. (c)|n.d.}}.</ref> "Thinking of You" was released as the second single on March 11, 1997, by which time ''House of Music'' had sold 514,000 copies, according to [[Nielsen SoundScan]].{{sfn |Reynolds|1997|p=21}} On August 6, the album was certified [[RIAA certification|Platinum]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).{{sfn |Anon. (d)|n.d.}}

The single "Me & You" appears on the [[Boyz n the Hood (soundtrack)|soundtrack]] to the motion picture ''[[Boyz n the Hood]]''. Following the release of ''[[Sons of Soul]]'', the group was a part of the R&B supergroup [[Black Men United]], along with [[Silk (group)|Silk]] and [[H-Town (band)|H-Town]]. The song "U Will Know" appeared on the soundtrack for the movie ''[[Jason's Lyric]]''.

==Other endeavors==
Raphael Saadiq released his first solo effort, the Top 20 ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' hit "Ask of You" for the ''[[Higher Learning]]'' soundtrack, in 1995. Around the same time, Saadiq became a much-sought-after R&B [[record producer|producer]], scoring hits for [[D'Angelo]], [[Total (band)|Total]], [[The Roots]], and others. Later in the 2000s, he started a solo career, releasing two albums:  ''Instant Vintage'' (2002) and ''Ray Ray'' (2004). Regarding changing his surname to 'Saadiq' for a solo career, in February 2009 Raphael stated to writer Pete Lewis of [[Blues & Soul]]: "I just wanted to have my own identity".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bluesandsoul.com/feature/409/raphael_saadiq_this_years_vintage/|title=Raphael Saadiq: This year's vintage|website=Bluesandsoul.com}}</ref>

[[Lucy Pearl]] was an R&B [[supergroup (music)|supergroup]] formed in 1999 as the brainchild of Raphael Saadiq. The other members of Lucy Pearl were [[Dawn Robinson]] ([[En Vogue]]) and [[Ali Shaheed Muhammad]] ([[A Tribe Called Quest]]). They released their self-titled debut album in 2000. After two singles, "Dance Tonight" and "Don't Mess with My Man", Dawn Robinson left and was replaced by Joi. The new line-up released the track "Without You". The group split up shortly after, releasing no other material.

In 2005, [[D'wayne Wiggins]] became the bandleader for the ''[[Weekends at the D.L.]]'' television show hosted by comedian [[D. L. Hughley]], which aired on the [[Comedy Central]] cable network until 2006. [[D'wayne Wiggins]] solo album, ''Eyes Never Lie'', sold approximately 150,000 units.

==Reunions==
In 2003, members of Tony! Toni! Toné!, except for Saadiq, were invited by [[Alicia Keys]] to be guest artists on her album ''[[The Diary of Alicia Keys]]''. The song that resulted from that session was called "[[Diary (Alicia Keys song)|Diary]]." Released as a single in the fall of 2004, it gave them their first Top 10 US hit in eleven years and a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals|Best R&B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals]] at the [[47th Grammy Awards|2005 Grammy Awards]].
