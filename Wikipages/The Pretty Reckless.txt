'''The Pretty Reckless''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[New York City]], formed in 2009. The band consists of [[Taylor Momsen]] (lead vocals, rhythm guitar), Ben Phillips (lead guitar, backing vocals), Mark Damon (bass), and Jamie Perkins (drums).

The band released their debut studio album, ''[[Light Me Up]]'', in August 2010. The album spawned three moderately successful singles, most notably "[[Make Me Wanna Die]]". The band released the ''[[Hit Me Like a Man EP]]'' in early 2012. In March 2014, the band released their second studio album, ''[[Going to Hell]]'', which included the singles "[[Heaven Knows (The Pretty Reckless song)|Heaven Knows]]" and "[[Messed Up World]]" which topped the US and UK rock charts.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/pretty-reckless-becomes-first-female-fronted-band-to-reach-no-1-with-first-two-hits |title=Pretty Reckless Becomes First Female Fronted Band To Reach No. 1 With First Two Hits |date=September 5, 2014 |publisher=Blabbermouth.net |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160529125226/http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/pretty-reckless-becomes-first-female-fronted-band-to-reach-no-1-with-first-two-hits/ |archive-date=May 29, 2016  }}</ref> The band's third studio album, ''[[Who You Selling For]]'', was released on October 21, 2016, by [[Razor & Tie]]. The album spawned the single "[[Take Me Down (The Pretty Reckless song)|Take Me Down]]", which earned the band their fourth number one on the US rock chart. The band's fourth studio album, ''[[Death by Rock and Roll]]'', was released on February 12, 2021.

"[[Miss Nothing]]" was the second single to be released from ''[[Light Me Up]]''. ===2012–2014: ''Hit Me Like a Man EP'', commercial success with ''Going to Hell''===

[[The Medicine Tour]] was the second headlining concert tour by The Pretty Reckless in support of their 2010 debut album ''[[Light Me Up]]'' and their 2012 extended play ''[[Hit Me Like a Man EP]]''. The tour dates were announced on January 23, 2012 via the band's official website. The band performed at several of the same venues they had visited the previous year, with 52 shows in North America, 5 shows in South America and 2 shows in Asia. Their second [[extended play]] ''[[Hit Me Like a Man EP]]'' was released on March 6, 2012. It featured three new songs, as well as live performances of two songs from ''[[Light Me Up]]''. ===2016–2019: ''Who You Selling For''===
In the beginning of September 2015, Momsen confirmed that the band was working on new material in the studio.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Taylor Momsen on Instagram: "Returning to our home, plus that great Neve #newTPRmusic #artisticfertilization"|url = https://instagram.com/p/7L-CKmuRT0/|website = Instagram|access-date = September 17, 2015|url-status = live|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20151218044539/https://www.instagram.com/p/7L-CKmuRT0/|archive-date = December 18, 2015|df = mdy-all}}</ref> Writing for the album began shortly after the completion of two years of touring in support of the band's second studio album, ''[[Going to Hell]]'' (2014). <blockquote>"We had so much we wanted to say, it was like shaking a can of soda on tour, and then when we started writing we cracked the seal", Momsen said. "The touring life is very isolating. You look at the world through a bus or airplane window. But music is the healing factor. It's the one thing that is grounding and a true companion through the forest. It saved us—again."<ref name="blabbermouth.net"/> </blockquote>On April 14, 2016, Momsen confirmed via Twitter that the new album was almost finished. The lead single from the band's third studio album, "[[Take Me Down (The Pretty Reckless song)|Take Me Down]]", had its world premiere on [[iHeartRadio]] stations on July 14, 2016<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.iheart.com/news/listen-to-the-pretty-reckless-take-14905227/ |title=Listen to The Pretty Reckless – 'Take Me Down' {{!}} World Premiere |publisher=[[iHeartRadio]] |date=July 14, 2016 |access-date=July 14, 2016 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160717182752/http://www.iheart.com/news/listen-to-the-pretty-reckless-take-14905227/ |archive-date=July 17, 2016}}</ref> and became the band's fourth single to top the Mainstream Rock chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7556883/the-pretty-reckless-rock-songs-record-take-me-down|title=The Pretty Reckless Sets Mainstream Rock Songs Record With New No. 1|date=October 26, 2016 |last=Rutherford |first=Kevin |work=Billboard |access-date=October 6, 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161027125445/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7556883/the-pretty-reckless-rock-songs-record-take-me-down |archive-date=October 27, 2016}}</ref> The single was released digitally the next day,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/take-me-down-single/id1132434657 |title=Take Me Down – Single by The Pretty Reckless |publisher=[[iTunes Store]] (US) |access-date=July 21, 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160721181124/https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/take-me-down-single/id1132434657 |archive-date=July 21, 2016}}</ref> and was serviced to US [[active rock]] radio on July 19.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allaccess.com/rock/future-releases |title=Rock Future Releases |publisher=All Access Music Group |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160710052709/http://www.allaccess.com/rock/future-releases |archive-date=July 10, 2016 |access-date=July 16, 2016}}</ref> The band's third studio album, ''[[Who You Selling For]]'', was released on October 21, 2016 by [[Razor & Tie]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/the-pretty-reckless-to-release-who-you-selling-for-album-in-october/ |title=THE PRETTY RECKLESS To Release 'Who You Selling For' Album in October |publisher=[[Blabbermouth.net]] |date=August 9, 2016 |access-date=August 9, 2016 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160810162613/http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/the-pretty-reckless-to-release-who-you-selling-for-album-in-october/ |archive-date=August 10, 2016}}</ref> 

In support of the album, the band embarked on the [[Who You Selling For Tour]], which kicked off on October 20, 2016 in [[Tulsa, Oklahoma]].<ref name="blabbermouth.net">{{Cite news|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/the-pretty-reckless-to-release-who-you-selling-for-album-in-october/|title=THE PRETTY RECKLESS To Release 'Who You Selling For' Album in October|date=August 9, 2016|publisher=Blabbermouth.net|access-date=November 17, 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161116184239/http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/the-pretty-reckless-to-release-who-you-selling-for-album-in-october|archive-date=November 16, 2016}}</ref> The official music video for the album's second single "Oh My God" was released on February 8, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundlinkmagazine.com/pretty-reckless-premier-new-video-oh-god/|title=The Pretty Reckless premier new video for Oh My God - Soundlink Magazine|date=February 9, 2017|access-date=October 6, 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170921095522/http://www.soundlinkmagazine.com/pretty-reckless-premier-new-video-oh-god/|archive-date=September 21, 2017}}</ref> During the tour, on May 17, 2017, the band was the opening act for [[Soundgarden]] during lead vocalist [[Chris Cornell]]'s last performance, at the [[Fox Theatre (Detroit)|Fox Theater]] in [[Detroit]], [[Michigan]], before he committed suicide. The Pretty Reckless paid tribute to Cornell by performing a cover version of [[Audioslave]]'s "[[Like a Stone]]" on May 20 at the Philadelphia radio station [[WMMR]]'s annual MMR-B-Q at [[BB&T Pavilion]] in [[Camden, New Jersey]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/the-pretty-reckless-pays-tribute-to-chris-cornell-with-like-a-stone-performance-in-camden-video/ |title=THE PRETTY RECKLESS Pays Tribute To CHRIS CORNELL With 'Like A Stone' Performance in Camden (Video) |publisher=Blabbermouth.net |date=May 21, 2017 |access-date=May 21, 2017 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170524223737/http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/the-pretty-reckless-pays-tribute-to-chris-cornell-with-like-a-stone-performance-in-camden-video |archive-date=May 24, 2017}}</ref> A third single, "[[Back to the River (song)|Back to the River]]", featuring [[Warren Haynes]] of the [[Allman Brothers Band]] was released on June 13, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allaccess.com/rock/future-releases|title=Rock Future Releases - Rock Music Radio Songs' Release Dates - AllAccess.com|date=June 5, 2017|access-date=October 6, 2017|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170605175353/http://www.allaccess.com/rock/future-releases|archive-date=June 5, 2017}}</ref>

===2020–present: ''Death by Rock and Roll''===
In November 2019, Momsen revealed on [[Instagram]] that the band was working on their fourth studio album with [[Matt Cameron]] of [[Pearl Jam]] and [[Soundgarden]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Diehl |first1=Bob |title=The Pretty Reckless Working on New Album With Matt Cameron |url=https://www.radio.com/music/rock/the-pretty-reckless-working-on-new-album-with-matt-cameron |website=www.radio.com |access-date=November 21, 2019 |language=en |date=November 7, 2019}}</ref> In February 2020, Momsen confirmed the album's completion and revealed its title, ''[[Death by Rock and Roll]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/the-pretty-reckless-spring-tour-2020/ |title=The Pretty Reckless announce first tour since 2017–UPDATE |date=February 17, 2020 |work=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |last=Greenwood |first=Koltan |access-date=June 20, 2020}}</ref> The album was released on February 12, 2021.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://loudwire.com/the-pretty-reckless-new-album-release-date-artwork-track-listing/ |title=The Pretty Reckless Confirm Release Date for ‘Death By Rock and Roll’ Album |date=November 11, 2020 |last=Schaffner |first=Lauryn |publisher=Loudwire |access-date=November 11, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/the-pretty-recklesss-taylor-momsen-explains-decision-to-delay-death-by-rock-and-roll-album-until-2021/ |title=THE PRETTY RECKLESS's TAYLOR MOMSEN Explains Decision To Delay 'Death By Rock And Roll' Album Until 2021 |date=August 18, 2020 |publisher=Blabbermouth.net |access-date=August 19, 2020}}</ref> In May 2020, the band signed with [[Fearless Records]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/the-pretty-reckless-signs-u-s-deal-with-fearless-records/#comments |title=THE PRETTY RECKLESS Signs U.S. Deal With FEARLESS RECORDS |date=May 5, 2020 |publisher=Blabbermouth.net |access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref> The lead single, which is the [[Death by Rock and Roll (song)|title track]] of the album, was released on May 15, 2020.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/the-pretty-reckless-death-by-rock-and-roll-new-song/ |title=Hear the Pretty Reckless return with anthemic "Death By Rock And Roll" |date=May 15, 2020 |last=Greenwood |first=Koltan |work=Alternative Press |access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref> The band's tour was set to begin in September 2020, having been delayed from May 2020 due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/the-pretty-reckless-rescheduled-tour-dates-coronavirus/ |title=The Pretty Reckless reschedule tour dates in response to coronavirus |last=Greenwood |first=Koltan |date=April 3, 2020 |work=Alternative Press |access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref> 

The Pretty Reckless became the first female-fronted band to have five number one singles when "[[Death by Rock and Roll (song)|Death by Rock and Roll]]" rose to the top of the Mainstream Rock Chart.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9427389/the-pretty-reckless-sets-new-mark-for-women-atop-mainstream-rock-songs-chart-with-death-by-rock-and-roll |title=The Pretty Reckless Sets New Mark for Women Atop Mainstream Rock Songs Chart With 'Death by Rock and Roll' |date=July 30, 2020 |last=Rutherford |first=Kevin |magazine=Billboard |access-date=July 31, 2020}}</ref> The Halloween-themed "Broomsticks" was released in late October,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/the-pretty-reckless-celebrate-halloween-early-with-new-track-broomsticks/ |title=The Pretty Reckless Celebrate Halloween Early With New Track, Broomsticks |date=October 23, 2020 |last=Carter |first=Emily |work=[[Kerrang!]] |access-date=November 13, 2020}}</ref> and "25" was released on November 13.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://loudwire.com/the-pretty-reckless-new-song-25-lyrics/ |title=The Pretty Reckless Drop Retrospective New Song '25' |last=Schaffner |first=Lauryn |date=November 13, 2020 |website=Loudwire |access-date=November 13, 2020}}</ref> In January 2021, the band released "And So It Went" featuring [[Tom Morello]] as the next single for ''Death by Rock and Roll''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/the-pretty-reckless-recruit-tom-morello-for-explosive-new-single-and-so-it-went/ |title=The Pretty Reckless recruit Tom Morello for explosive new single, And So It Went |last=Carter |first=Emily |date=January 8, 2021 |access-date=January 9, 2021 |work=Kerrang!}}</ref> Momsen stated that the song comments on the world's current "civil unrest": "The world has been in such a state of civil unrest. 'And So It Went' basically comes from that vision. As a songwriter, I feel like I'm not here to preach. I use music to observe and communicate what I see around me."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Colothan|first=Scott|date=January 8, 2021|title=The Pretty Reckless premiere Tom Morello collaboration 'And So It Went'|url=https://planetradio.co.uk/planet-rock/news/rock-news/the-pretty-reckless-tom-morello/|access-date=January 11, 2020|website=[[Planet Rock (radio station)|Planet Rock]]}}</ref>

==Musical style==
The Pretty Reckless has been described as [[hard rock]]<ref name="AMG Bio"/> [[post-grunge]],<ref name="AMG Bio"/> and [[alternative rock]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/light-me-up-mw0002032905 | title=The Pretty Reckless - Light Me Up | work=[[AllMusic]] | access-date=July 1, 2015 | author=Eremenko, Alexey | url-status=live | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150813032732/http://www.allmusic.com/album/light-me-up-mw0002032905 | archive-date=August 13, 2015 | df=mdy-all }}</ref> 

==Live performances==
The band opened the [[V Festival]] in the UK in 2010.  The band embarked on a four-date UK tour starting in [[Glasgow]], Scotland at the [[Barrowland Ballroom]] on December 13, 2010, supported by [[Francesqa]].

In 2011, the band announced a tour running from early February to late March. The band appeared at the [[Download Festival]] in June.<ref>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/newsbeat/12619268 "Taylor Momsen's The Pretty Reckless to play Download"], bbc.co.uk, 2/3/11, retrieved 15/3/11</ref> A planned performance at the [[Soundwave (Australian music festival)#Soundwave Revolution|Soundwave Revolution]], an Australian music festival, was cancelled. During the summer, the band performed in several European festivals such as [[Rock Am Ring]] in June, [[Optimus Alive!]] on July 8 in Portugal, [[Rock Werchter]] on July 2 in Belgium, [[Wireless Festival]] on July 3, [[Montreux Jazz Festival]] in Montreux, Switzerland on July 4 and [[T in the Park]] on July 10 in Scotland. On August 6, 2011, the band performed at the 2011 [[Lollapalooza]] festival in [[Chicago, Illinois]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://lineup.lollapalooza.com/events/2011/08/06/ |title=Lollapalooza 2011 |publisher=Lineup.lollapalooza.com |date=July 19, 2011 |access-date=September 13, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713225051/http://lineup.lollapalooza.com/events/2011/08/06/ |archive-date=July 13, 2011 }}</ref> The Pretty Reckless opened several [[Evanescence]] shows in October and November with two gigs at the HMV [[Hammersmith Apollo]] in London among others.<ref>[http://www.allheadlinenews.com/articles/90055738?Taylor%20Momsen%20announces%20tour%20with%20Evanescence "Taylor Momsen announces tour with Evanescence]{{dead link|date=November 2017 |bot=Rfl0216 |fix-attempted=yes }}, AHN, 27/7/11, retrieved 31/7/11</ref> On October 8, 2011, the band performed at the 2011 [[Tulsa State Fair]] in [[Tulsa, Oklahoma]]. On October 26, 2011, the band performed at Ritual nightclub in Ottawa, Ontario, with guests Static Revolt and The Escape Mode.<ref>{{cite web|last=Robinson|first=Joe|title=Guns N' Roses Tap 'Gossip Girl' Taylor Momsen As Tour Opener|url=http://ultimateclassicrock.com/guns-n-roses-taylor-momsen/|publisher=Ultimate Classic Rock|access-date=December 8, 2011|date=November 30, 2011|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111209084102/http://ultimateclassicrock.com/guns-n-roses-taylor-momsen/|archive-date=December 9, 2011}}</ref>

In January 2012, the band released tour dates for its second headlining tour, [[The Medicine Tour]]. Directly following those tour dates, the Pretty Reckless supported [[Marilyn Manson]] on their current tour, [[Hey Cruel World... Tour]]. In July 2012, The Pretty Reckless arrived in Argentina for the first time. The band's show was on July 29 in [[Buenos Aires]]. In September 2012, The Pretty Reckless headlined the Bazooka Rocks Festival in [[Metro Manila|Manila]], Philippines, ending their The Medicine Tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=SMX Convention Center|url=http://www.theprettyreckless.com/events/detail.aspx?eid=76299&aid=1135|publisher=The Pretty Reckless|access-date=June 13, 2012|date=June 8, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120827092922/http://www.theprettyreckless.com/events/detail.aspx?eid=76299|archive-date=August 27, 2012}}</ref> On September 20, 2013 the band kicked off the first leg of their third tour, the [[Going to Hell Tour]], in North America.
