== Background ==
J Hus was born in [[London]] and grew up in the city's [[Stratford, London|Stratford]] district. He is of [[The Gambia|Gambian]] descent and was raised by his mother, who emigrated to England when she was 25 years old.<ref name="fader2017">{{Cite news |url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/04/25/j-hus-fader-cover-story-interview-afrobeats |title=J Hus Is The Sound Of Diaspora's Boomerang |publisher=[[The Fader]] |date=25 April 2017 |access-date=7 December 2017 |language=en |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171206072740/http://www.thefader.com/2017/04/25/j-hus-fader-cover-story-interview-afrobeats |archive-date=6 December 2017 |url-status=live }}</ref> Jallow was raised [[Muslim]] and is of ethnic [[Fula people|Fula]] and [[Wolof people|Wolof]] heritage.<ref name="TheGuardian" /> Growing up he had wanted to become an actor "but then around Year 10 or Year 11, it was whatever really", and he was expelled from school as he had, in his words, "started getting into a bit of trouble."<ref>{{cite web |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/53ndxk/j-hus-interview-2017-common-sense |title=No One Is Like J Hus |publisher=[[Noisey]] |date=12 May 2017 |access-date=7 November 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181107104241/https://noisey.vice.com/en_uk/article/53ndxk/j-hus-interview-2017-common-sense |archive-date=7 November 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref> In September 2014, distraught following two separate prison stints, he was advised to focus on pursuing a music career by his childhood friend Moe who also agreed to be his manager. Moe and his older brother would go on to form their own management company 2K Management.<ref name="fader2017" /> J Hus is a supporter of [[Arsenal F.C.]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.premierleague.com/news/618728|title=J Hus: Aubameyang will crush Spurs!|date=9 February 2018|work=[[Premier League]]|access-date=16 February 2021}}</ref>

J Hus got his stage name from the word "hustler", explaining that he would buy a pack of doughnuts and sell them individually at a profit when he was in [[secondary school]].<ref name="TheGuardian">{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/may/25/rapper-j-hus-i-was-doughnut-hustler-common-sense|title=Rapper J Hus: 'I was a doughnut hustler!'|last=Yates|first=Kieran|date=25 May 2017|website=the Guardian|language=en|access-date=16 February 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180218024023/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/may/25/rapper-j-hus-i-was-doughnut-hustler-common-sense|archive-date=18 February 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Music career ==
=== 2015–present: ''Common Sense'' and ''Big Conspiracy'' ===
J Hus began his career by recording several freestyles,<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|title=J Hus {{!}} Biography, Albums, Streaming Links|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/j-hus-mn0003446628|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170322015646/http://www.allmusic.com/artist/j-hus-mn0003446628|archive-date=22 March 2017|access-date=21 March 2017|publisher=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref> including #StreetHeat, Bl@CKBOX and [[GRM Daily]]<ref name="thelinkup">{{cite web|url=http://thelinkup.com/why-jhus-is-a-rare-breed-of-sick-rapper-and-anthem-factory/|title=Why J Hus Is A Rare Breed Of Sick Rapper and Anthem Factory|publisher=Thelinkup.com|access-date=21 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170322015919/http://thelinkup.com/why-jhus-is-a-rare-breed-of-sick-rapper-and-anthem-factory/|archive-date=22 March 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> and publishing them online.<ref name="allmusic" /> He followed this with "#Rated", which samples the beat from [[French Montana]]'s "Don't Panic", and "Want From Me" Remix of [[Kojo Funds]]' song, which gained a lot of coverage, helping his breakthrough. His next release was "Dem Boy Paigon", which was described by Ajay Rose of The Link Up as having "brought together an Afro-beat sound with lyrical rap ... capable of turning any dance upside down". He followed this with "No Lie" and his [[SB.TV|SB:TV]] "Warm Up Session".<ref name="thelinkup" />

Around the end of March 2015, he and [[MoStack]] released their "Westwood Crib Session". In late May 2015, he released "Lean & Bop",<ref name="thelinkup" /> which was streamed more than 10 million times in total on music platforms.<ref name="sound of">{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-34636509|title=BBC Sound Of 2016 longlist revealed|date=30 November 2015|publisher=BBC News|access-date=22 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180924204418/https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-34636509|archive-date=24 September 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Around this time, he also released a "Daily Duppy" for GRM Daily, which he followed with his first mixtape, ''The 15th Day''.<ref name="thelinkup" />

Jallow released the single "Friendly" in 2016, which received a nomination at the 2016 MOBO Awards, as well as "Playing Sports", "Liar Liar" (remix) and "Solo One", the last of which appeared on the ''[[Brotherhood (2016 film)|Brotherhood]]'' soundtrack.<ref name="thelinkup" />

In 2017, he featured on [[Nines (rapper)|Nines]]' "High Roller", which featured on his album ''[[One Foot Out]]''. He also featured on [[Stormzy]]'s "Bad Boys" from his album ''[[Gang Signs & Prayer]]'', which peaked at number 22 on the [[UK Singles Chart]],<ref name="bad boys">{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/51166/stormzy-ft-ghetts-and-j-hus/|title=STORMZY FT GHETTS & J HUS|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=21 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170322022822/http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/51166/stormzy-ft-ghetts-and-j-hus/|archive-date=22 March 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> on [[Dave (rapper)|Dave]]'s "Samantha", which peaked at number 63,<ref name="samantha">{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/50839/dave-and-j-hus/|title=DAVE AND J HUS|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]|access-date=21 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170322021309/http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/50839/dave-and-j-hus/|archive-date=22 March 2017|url-status=live}}</ref> and charted at number 9 with his solo composition "Did You See".<ref name="did you see">{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/48523/j-hus/|title=J HUS {{!}} full Official Chart History|publisher=Official Charts Company|access-date=20 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170530131252/http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/48523/j-hus/|archive-date=30 May 2017}}</ref> The song served as the lead single off his debut album, ''[[Common Sense (J Hus album)|Common Sense]]'' (2017). Upon release, the album was positively received by fans, and includes features from Mo Stack, MIST, Tiggs da Author and [[Burna Boy]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/04/03/j-hus-common-sense/ |title=J Hus Announced Details Of His Debut Album, Common Sense |publisher=Fader |date=4 April 2017 |access-date=4 April 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170404024620/http://www.thefader.com/2017/04/03/j-hus-common-sense |archive-date=4 April 2017}}</ref> In May 2018, he released his [[Extended play|EP]], ''[[Big Spang]]'' (2018).

Following his arrest for carrying a bladed article in public, Jallow was sentenced to eight months in prison.<ref name=":0" /> Jallow's label stopped releasing music, with his only verse whilst incarcerated coming from the song "Disaster" which was released on collaborator [[Dave (rapper)|Dave]]'s debut album, ''[[Psychodrama (album)|Psychodrama]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/43688/1/dave-rapper-psychodrama-debut-album-therapy-mental-health|title=The radical healing sound of Dave's debut album Psychodrama|last=Dazed|date=13 March 2019|website=Dazed|language=en|access-date=20 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406015839/https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/43688/1/dave-rapper-psychodrama-debut-album-therapy-mental-health|archive-date=6 April 2019}}</ref> "Disaster" debuted at number eight on the UK Singles Chart on 15 March 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/dave-s-psychodrama-debuts-at-number-1-in-incredibly-close-official-albums-chart-battle-against-foals__25810/|title=Dave's Psychodrama album debuts at Number 1|website=officialcharts.com|access-date=20 March 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190405225324/https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/dave-s-psychodrama-debuts-at-number-1-in-incredibly-close-official-albums-chart-battle-against-foals__25810/|archive-date=5 April 2019}}</ref>

Hours after his release from prison in April 2019 he made a surprise appearance on stage at [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s concert at the [[The O2 Arena|O2 Arena]] in London.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8505915/j-hus-drake-london-concert-prison-release|title=J Hus Hits the Stage at Drake's London Concert Just Hours After Release From Prison|website=billboard.com|access-date=6 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190406115239/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8505915/j-hus-drake-london-concert-prison-release|archive-date=6 April 2019}}</ref>

In December 2019, J Hus announced he will no longer tour for "the next 3/4 years, maybe more". He also stated that following his next album, he would have no more features but that he wanted to work with [[Burna Boy]] and [[21 Savage]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/3am/celebrity-news/j-hus-bombshell-rapper-announces-21031818|title=J Hus bombshell as rapper announces retirement – 'I'll never tour again'|last=Domachowski|first=Lucy|date=5 December 2019|website=mirror|access-date=8 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191208162729/https://www.mirror.co.uk/3am/celebrity-news/j-hus-bombshell-rapper-announces-21031818|archive-date=8 December 2019}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
In 2011, J Hus was arrested outside Westfield following a "mass attack" on four people, which ended in one of them being stabbed. He'd already received a referral order that year after being caught with a knife in public.<ref>{{cite web |title=J Hus: Why he felt he had to take a knife to Westfield |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/newsbeat-46578742 |website=[[The BBC]] |publisher=[[BBC]] |access-date=16 December 2018 |date=15 December 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181216050114/https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/newsbeat-46578742 |archive-date=16 December 2018 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In 2014 and 2015, he was arrested and served stints in Her Majesty's [[Feltham Prison]].<ref name="TheGuardian" /> He accrued six convictions for ten offences between 2011 and 2016, including for carrying a knife and for violent disorder. He has also been given an [[ASBO]].<ref name="bbc2018" />
