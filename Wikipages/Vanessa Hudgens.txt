'''Vanessa Anne Hudgens''' ({{IPAc-en|'|h|ʌ|dʒ|ə|n|s}}; born December 14, 1988) is an American actress and singer. After making her [[feature film]] debut in ''[[Thirteen (2003 film)|Thirteen]]'' (2003), Hudgens rose to fame portraying [[Gabriella Montez]] in the [[High School Musical (franchise)|''High School Musical'' film series]] (2006–2008), which brought her significant mainstream success.<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p761737|pure_url=yes}} Vanessa Hudgens Biography] Allmusic</ref> The success of the first film led Hudgens to acquire a recording contract with [[Hollywood Records]], with whom she released two studio albums, ''[[V (Vanessa Hudgens album)|V]]'' (2006) and ''[[Identified]]'' (2008).

Since the release of her studio albums and the ''High School Musical'' franchise, Hudgens has focused on her acting career. She has appeared in the films ''[[Bandslam]]'' (2009), ''[[Beastly (film)|Beastly]],'' ''[[Sucker Punch (2011 film)|Sucker Punch]]'' (both 2011), ''[[Journey 2: The Mysterious Island]]'', ''[[Spring Breakers]]'' (both 2012), ''[[Second Act (film)|Second Act]]'' (2018), ''[[Bad Boys for Life]]'' (2020), and the film adaptation of ''[[Tick, Tick... Boom! (film)|Tick, Tick...Boom!]]'' (2021). Hudgens played the role of Emily Locke in the [[NBC]] series [[Powerless (TV series)|''Powerless'']] (2017). She also played the title role in the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] musical ''[[Gigi (musical)#Productions|Gigi]]'' (2015) and had roles in two of [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]]’s live musical productions: Rizzo in ''[[Grease: Live]]'' (2016) and Maureen Johnson in ''[[Rent: Live]]'' (2019). .

==Career==

===Early roles===
Starting in 1998, Hudgens performed in musical theater as a singer, and appeared in local productions of ''[[Carousel (musical)|Carousel]]'', ''[[Adaptations of The Wizard of Oz|The Wizard of Oz]]'', ''[[The King and I]]'', ''[[The Music Man]]'', and ''[[Cinderella]]'', among others.<ref name="vanbio">[http://www.sing365.com/music/lyric.nsf/Vanessa-Hudgens-Biography/1D3E3038159B6760482571F50005D332 Vanessa Hudgens Biography] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090211112100/http://sing365.com/music/lyric.nsf/Vanessa-Hudgens-Biography/1D3E3038159B6760482571F50005D332 |date=February 11, 2009 }}. sing365.com. Accessed June 12, 2009.</ref> Two years after her career in stage plays and musicals, she began auditioning for commercials and television shows, and her family moved to Los Angeles after she won a role in a television commercial.<ref name="kidzworld">[http://www.kidzworld.com/article/8777-vanessa-hudgens-biography Vanessa Hudgens Biography] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100210175211/http://www.kidzworld.com/article/8777-vanessa-hudgens-biography |date=February 10, 2010 }} KidzWorld.com</ref><ref name="parade">{{cite web|last=Kaplan|first=James|title=Vanessa Hudgens, High School Sweetheart|url=http://www.parade.com/celebrity/2009/07/vanessa-hudgens-high-school-sweetheart.html|date=July 26, 2009|work=[[Parade (magazine)|Parade]]|access-date=September 20, 2009|archive-date=January 14, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100114005806/http://www.parade.com/celebrity/2009/07/vanessa-hudgens-high-school-sweetheart.html|url-status=live}}</ref> She began her acting career at age 15, and she briefly attended [[Orange County High School of the Arts]], followed by homeschooling with tutors.<ref>{{cite web|author=Jocelyn Vena|title=High School Musical' Was Vanessa Hudgens' Only High School Experience|url=http://www.mtv.com/movies/news/articles/1597627/20081021/story.jhtml|date=October 22, 2008|work=MTV|access-date=September 20, 2009|archive-date=October 25, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081025045723/http://www.mtv.com/movies/news/articles/1597627/20081021/story.jhtml|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>Heather Phares. Hudgens had her first acting role in an episode of the sitcom ''[[Still Standing (American TV series)|Still Standing]]'', airing on [[CBS]] in 2002,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0710388/|title=Still Rocking|date=October 21, 2002|via=IMDb}}</ref> in which she portrayed Tiffany.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tv.yahoo.com/vanessa-hudgens/contributor/1136720 |title=Vanessa Hudgens &#124; TV shows, credits and biography – Yahoo TV |publisher=Tv.yahoo.com |access-date=January 24, 2014}}</ref> Later that year, she guest starred in an episode of the series ''[[Robbery Homicide Division]]'', airing on [[CBS]]. Hudgens made her theatrical debut in the 2003 drama film ''[[Thirteen (2003 film)|Thirteen]]'', in the supporting role of Noel. The film, which revolved around teenager Tracy trying to fit in and getting caught up in the wrong crowd, was somewhat of a commercial success, grossing $10&nbsp;million worldwide. Despite its minor commercial success, the film received positive critical reviews.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/thirteen/ |title=Thirteen |website=Rotten Tomatoes |access-date=January 24, 2014 |archive-date=November 17, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201117025532/https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/thirteen |url-status=live }}</ref> ''[[USA Today]]'' called ''Thirteen'' the most "powerful of all recent wayward-youth sagas".<ref>{{cite web|last=Clark|first=Mike|title='Thirteen' hits you – and keeps you|url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/movies/reviews/2003-08-19-thirteen_x.htm|date=August 19, 2003|work=USA Today|access-date=May 5, 2012|archive-date=August 28, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120828162115/http://www.usatoday.com/life/movies/reviews/2003-08-19-thirteen_x.htm|url-status=live}}</ref> Hudgens subsequently landed a role in the 2004 science fiction-adventure film ''[[Thunderbirds (2004 film)|Thunderbirds]]'', based on the [[Thunderbirds (TV series)|1960s television series]]. Hudgens portrayed the character of Tin-tin. The film was a commercial failure and was strongly criticized for "abandoning the original concepts".<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2009/feb/07/gerry-anderson-thunderbirds-auction | work=[[The Guardian]] | location=London | title=Gerry Anderson auctions Thunderbirds treasures | first=Caitlin | last=Fitzsimmons | date=February 7, 2009 | access-date=May 5, 2012}}</ref>

===2005–2008: ''High School Musical'' and music===
[[File:Vanessa Anne Hudgens, HSM2.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Hudgens performing at the [[High School Musical: The Concert]] in 2007]]
In January 2006, Hudgens played [[Gabriella Montez]], one of the lead roles in the [[Disney Channel Original Movie]] ''[[High School Musical]]''. The film saw Hudgens portraying the new girl at high school who falls for the captain of the basketball team. The two later reveal a passion for singing, and audition for the school play together. Hudgens starred alongside [[Zac Efron]] and [[Ashley Tisdale]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Christopher|first=Rocchio|title=Vanessa Hudgens' original plan was to audition for 'American Idol'|url=http://www.realitytvworld.com/news/vanessa-hudgens-original-plan-was-audition-for-american-idol-6189.php|date=November 29, 2007|work=Reality TV World|access-date=September 21, 2009|archive-date=January 16, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100116231628/http://realitytvworld.com/news/vanessa-hudgens-original-plan-was-audition-for-american-idol-6189.php|url-status=live}}</ref> the former of which she was partnered up with during the auditioning process due to their "chemistry".<ref>{{cite web|last=Nudd|first=Tim|title=Zac Efron: It Always Clicked With Vanessa|url=https://people.com/celebrity/zac-efron-it-always-clicked-with-vanessa/|date=July 17, 2007|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|access-date=March 5, 2019}}</ref> It was [[Disney Channel]]'s most-watched film that year with 7.7&nbsp;million viewers in its premiere broadcast in the US, until August's premiere of ''[[The Cheetah Girls 2]]'', which achieved 8.1&nbsp;million viewers.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/television/news/2007-08-09-high-school-musical2_N.htm | work=USA Today | title=Can 'High School Musical' do it again? | first=Bill | last=Keveney | date=August 13, 2007 | access-date=May 4, 2010 | archive-date=October 16, 2011 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111016214333/http://www.usatoday.com/life/television/news/2007-08-09-high-school-musical2_N.htm | url-status=live }}</ref> For the film, Hudgens recorded numerous songs which had commercial success. The song "[[Breaking Free]]", a duet with Zac Efron, became Hudgens' highest peak on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] at number 4, and number 9 in the UK.<ref name="Hot 100">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/vanessa-hudgens/chart-history/hsi/|title=Vanessa Hudgens Hot 100 Chart History|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=November 16, 2019}}</ref> Following the success of the film, Hudgens released her debut studio album, ''[[V (Vanessa Hudgens album)|V]]'', on September 26, 2006. It sold 34,000 copies in its first week, and debuted at number 24 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref name="Billboard.com">{{cite news |first=Katie |last=Hasty |title=Ludacris Scores Third No. 1 With 'Release Therapy' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/57060/ludacris-scores-third-no-1-with-release-therapy |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Nielsen N.V.|Nielsen Business Media, Inc.]] |date=October 4, 2006 |access-date=July 24, 2008 |archive-date=September 8, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140908154422/http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/57060/ludacris-scores-third-no-1-with-release-therapy |url-status=live }}</ref> In February 2007, the album was [[certified gold]] by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]].<ref name="RIAA">{{cite certification|artist=Vanessa Hudgens|region=United States|access-date=November 16, 2019}}</ref> As of August 2009, the album has sold 570,000 copies in the US.<ref>{{cite news |first=Susan |last=Visakowitz |title=Interest in Cyrus' Music Continues Amid Photo Scandal |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1045696/interest-in-cyrus-music-continues-amid-photo-scandal |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]  |date=April 30, 2008 |access-date=July 24, 2008}}</ref> It was preceded by two singles, "[[Come Back to Me (Vanessa Hudgens song)|Come Back to Me]]" and "[[Say OK]]", both of which were moderately successful in several countries. Both singles received promotion on Disney Channel, with the music videos for both premiering on the channel.

Brian Schall sued Hudgens in 2007 for an alleged "breach of contract"; according to the suit, Schall claims he advanced costs and expenses on Hudgens' behalf for her songwriting and recording career.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lawyer sues Vanessa Hudgens for $150,000|work=[[NBC]]|date=September 8, 2007|url=https://www.today.com/popculture/lawyer-sues-vanessa-hudgens-150-000-wbna20853530|access-date=June 3, 2009|archive-date=November 17, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201117025556/https://i1.wp.com/opposition24.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/cropped-Element-31.png?fit=32%2C32&ssl=1|url-status=live}}</ref> Schall claims Hudgens owed him $150,000 after helping her earn more than $5&nbsp;million for her music career. Hudgens argued that she was a 16-year-old [[Minor (law)|minor]] when she signed the contract in October 2005, and therefore too young to do so. She subsequently disaffirmed it on October 9, 2008. Papers filed in court by her lawyer say California's Family Code "provides that the contract of a minor is voidable and may be disaffirmed before (age&nbsp;18) or within a reasonable time afterward."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1573241/20071101/winehouse_amy.jhtml|title=Amy Winehouse Stumbles Through EMA Performance; Plus Justin Timberlake, Avril Lavigne, Britney Spears, Lindsay Lohan & More, In for the Record|date=November 1, 2008|work=MTV|access-date=September 20, 2009|archive-date=March 25, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100325213452/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1573241/20071101/winehouse_amy.jhtml|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2008, Hudgens was sued by Johnny Vieira, who claims he was owed a share of Hudgens' advances, royalties and merchandising revenue in exchange for his management services. Vieira accuses Hudgens of abandoning her talent team as soon as she became a commercial name.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.dose.ca/story.html?id=3d340fa5-deeb-4cd7-8f25-09f61218707c |title=Vanessa Hudgens Gets Sued...
