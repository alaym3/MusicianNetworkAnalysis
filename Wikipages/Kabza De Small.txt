'''Kabelo Petrus Motha''' better known by his stage name '''Kabza De Small''', is a South African [[Disc jockey|DJ]] and record producer. He was born in [[Mpumalanga]] and raised in [[Pretoria]]. He was born on the 27th of November 1992.{{citation needed|date=November 2021}} He has been referred to as the king of the [[Amapiano]] genre.<ref name="dailysun">{{cite web|url=https://www.dailysun.co.za/News/Entertainment/kabza-de-small-is-king-of-amapiano-20181107|title=Kabza De Small is king of Amapiano |website=Daily Sun}}</ref> Motha has collaborated extensively with record producer [[DJ Maphorisa]].

Forbes ranked Kabza 18th in the Forbes Under 30 list in 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mihlali Ndamase , Kabza De Small and Master KG make Forbes Africa ’30 under 30′ list {{!}} YOMZANSI |url=https://www.yomzansi.com/2021/06/02/forbes-africa-30-under-30-list-2021/|access-date =June 2, 2021|publisher=YoMzansi}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Kabelo Motha was born in [[Mpumalanga]], South Africa, and was raised in [[Pretoria]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bimba.co.za/kabza-de-small|title=Kabza De Small Bio and Lyrics|website=Bimba}}</ref> Motha began his career as a DJ in 2009, venturing in the genres: Afro-fused [[House music|house]] and [[Electronic dance music|EDM]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://onenationmusic.com/video-kabza-de-small-has-been-creative-from-the-day-get-go/|title=Kabza De Small|website=One Nation Music}}</ref> In 2016, he made his production credits by releasing his debut album, ''Avenue Sounds'' under the label, House Afrika Records. In November 2018, he gained attention by releasing songs, "Amabele Shaya" and "Umshove", respectively, featuring guest vocals by the amapiano counterpart, Leehleza. Following both songs, he continued his collaboration with Leehleza as he released the song "Bamba La", on 3 January 2019.

''Scorpion Kings'', Motha's collaborative studio album with record producer [[DJ Maphorisa]], was released on 5 July 2019, supported by the lead single, "Koko". The album was certified diamond in South Africa, with over 100, 000 units sold.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://zalebs.com/whats-hot/kabza-de-small-says-he-will-receive-some-good-rest-during-festive-season|title=Kabza Says He Will Receive Some Good Rest During Festive Season|website=ZAlebs}}</ref> On 1 October 2019, they released their sophomore studio album, ''Piano Hub'', which was supported by the single, "Nana Thula", featuring guest vocals by Njelic.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/icymi-kabza-de-small-x-dj-maphorisa-bless-fans-with-piano-hub/|title=ICYMI: Kabza De Small x DJ Maphorisa Bless Fans With Piano hub|website=Zkhipani.com}}</ref> On 18 October 2019, Motha released his second studio album, ''Pretty Girls Love Amapiano''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/listen-kabza-de-small-new-ep-pretty-girls-love-amapiano/|title=Listen to Kabza De Small's New EP 'Pretty Girls Love Amapiano'|website=OkayAfrica}}</ref> In continuing his collaboration with DJ Maphorisa, they released their third studio album, ''The Return of Scorpion Kings'', on 29 November 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://justnje.com/dj-maphorisa-and-kabza-de-small-release-much-anticipated-album-the-return-of-the-scorpion-kings/|title=DJ Maphorisa and Kabza De Small release much-anticipated album, The Return Of The Scorpion Kings. His latest offering is that of co-producing Blaqboy music present Made With Love EP single Lerato Fela by King Monada |website=JustNje}}</ref>

On 4 April 2020, during the [[COVID-19 pandemic in South Africa|COVID-19 national lockdown period]], he released an EP titled ''Pretty Girls Love Amapiano 2'', it features Tyler ICU, DJ Maphorisa, Xolani, King Deetoy.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kabza De Small - 'Pretty Girls Love Amapiano' {{!}} YOMZANSI|url=https://www.yomzansi.com/2020/04/17/kabza-de-small-pretty-girls-love-amapiano-vol2/|work=YoMzansi}}</ref>

On 26 June 2020, he released his next solo studio album, ''I Am The King Of Amapiano: Sweet & Dust'', featured both South African & international acts such as [[Burna Boy]], [[Wizkid]], [[Cassper Nyovest]], Dali Wonga, [[DJ Maphorisa]], [[Samthing Soweto]], [[Sha Sha (singer)|Sha sha]], [[Mlindo the Vocalist|Mlindo]], Bontle Smith.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.okayafrica.com/amapiano-music-albums-eps-stream-right-now/?rebelltitem=6 |title= 7 Amapiano Albums & EPs to Stream Right Now |website=Okay Africa}}</ref>

In October 2020, he produced [[Oscar Mbo]]’s single titled ''Genesis'' from the album, ''For The Groovists''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/south-african-artists-to-watch-2021/?rebelltitem=12
|title=13 South African Artists To Look Out For In 2021|work=Okay Africa|accessdate=10 April 2021}}</ref> Motha took-home Music Producer of the Year award  at the 2020 African Muzik Magazine Awards.<ref>{{cite web|title="African Muzik Magazine Awards 2020: All the winners {{!}} Music In Africa|url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/african-muzik-magazine-awards-2020-all-winners|publisher=Music In Africa|access-date=15 October 2021|first=Gabriel Myers |last=Hansen}}</ref>

On 9 April 2021, Kabza released  ''[[Rumble in the Jungle (DJ Maphorisa, Kabza De Small and Tresor album)|Rumble in the Jungle]]'', a collaborative album with record producer [[DJ Maphorisa]] and singer [[Tresor (singer)|TRESOR]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.okayafrica.com/kabza-de-small-dj-maphorisa-tresor/|title= DJ Maphorisa and Kabza De Small Enlist Tresor on Their New Amapiano Album |work=Okay Africa |accessdate=16 June 2021}}</ref>

At the [[27th South African Music Awards]] he received three nominations  includes; Album of the Year, Male Artist  of the Year and Best AmaPiano Album, which made him the most nominated artist, for his album ''I Am The King Of AmaPiano: Sweet & Dust''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://iminathi.net/kabza-de-small-i-am-the-king-of-amapiano-album-sweet-and-dust/|title=Kabza De Small I Am The King Of Amapiano Album|work=[[iminathi]]|last=Mike|first=Steven|access-date=10 June 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/entertainment/2021-05-19-amapiano-star-kabza-de-small-tops-sama27-nominations/|title= Amapiano star Kabza De Small tops SAMA27 nominations|work=Sowetan Live |accessdate=16 June 2021}}</ref>

On September 17, 2021, he released a single "Abalele" with DJ Maphorisa featuring  Ami Faku.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kabza De Small & DJ Maphorisa Team Up With Ami Faku On “Abalele” |url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/newmusicfridays-kabza-de-small-dj-maphorisa-team-up-with-ami-faku-on-abalele/|access-date=17 September 2021|work=Zkhiphani}}</ref> The song reached number one on [[Entertainment Monitoring Africa]].{{cn|date=October 2021}}

In October 2021, he produced Congolese-born singer [[Tresor (singer)|TRESOR]]’s singles, "Makosa" and "Nyota" from the album, ''[[Motion (Tresor album)|Motion]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://museafrica.com/2021/10/01/tresor-features-kabza-de-small-dj-maphorisa-others-on-motion-album/|title=TRESOR features Kabza De Small, DJ Maphorisa, others on MOTION album|work=museafrica.com|accessdate=15 October 2021}}</ref>

==Impact and achievements==
Motha is often referred to as the "King of Amapiano".<ref name="dailysun"/> He is considered one of the best-selling music artists of [[amapiano]], and also one of the pioneers of the genre. With his breakthrough in 2018, he played an instrumental role in popularizing the amapiano genre.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.zkhiphani.co.za/icymi-kabza-de-small-drops-new-amapiano-mix/|title=ICYMI: Kabza De Small Graces Us With New Amapiano mix|website=Zkhipani}}</ref> On 3 December 2019, [[Spotify]] South Africa, named Motha as the most-streamed South African artist in the platform for 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.enca.com/life/kabza-de-small-tops-spotify-streaming-list-south-africa|title=Kabza De Small tops Spotify streaming list in South Africa|website=eNCA}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/kabza-de-small-most-streamed-south-african-artist-on-spotify-2019/|title=Kabza De Small is 2019's Most Streamed South African Artist on Spotify by SA Audiences|website=OkayAfrica}}</ref>
