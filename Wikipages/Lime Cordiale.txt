'''Lime Cordiale''' are an Australian pop rock group from Sydney, New South Wales, formed in 2009. The band consists of brothers Oli and Louis Leimbach, with additional touring members James Jennings, Felix Bornholt and Nicholas Polovineo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thezoo.com.au/lime-cordiale/|title=Lime Cordiale|date=21 August 2018}}</ref> They released their debut studio album ''[[Permanent Vacation (Lime Cordiale album)|Permanent Vacation]]'' in 2017. The group have performed at [[Groovin' the Moo]]<ref>{{cite web |title=LIME CORDIALE |url=http://www.anu.edu.au/events/lime-cordiale |website=ANU |publisher=ANU |access-date=2 Nov 2018}}</ref> and [[South by Southwest]] (SXSW).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://music.theaureview.com/reviews/sxsw-guest-review-lime-cordiale-catchy-but-not-catching-fire/|title=SXSW Guest Review: Lime Cordiale: Catchy but not catching fire|first=Ryan|last=Rhea|date=6 April 2018|publisher=The AU Review}}</ref> At the [[2020 ARIA Music Awards]] they were nominated in eight categories and won [[ARIA Award for Breakthrough Artist – Release|Breakthrough Artist – Release]] for their second album, ''[[14 Steps to a Better You]]'' (July 2020).

==Career==
===2009–2016: Early EPs===
Lime Cordiale formed in the Northern Beaches of Sydney, Australia in September 2009<ref name="AM">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lime-cordiale-mn0003220727/biography|title= Lime Cordiale|website=[[AllMusic]]|date=26 January 2020}}</ref> and consisted of brothers Oliver Leimbach and Louis Leimbach, Brendan Champion and James Jennings. The group came about after they recognised their mutual love of all things pop and their desire to create original and exciting music. They began to perform gigs around Sydney<ref name="EP launch">{{cite web |url=http://imcmusic.net/2012/06/27/lime-cordiale-sydney-ep-launch/ |title=Lime Cordiale Sydney EP Launch|website=IMC Music |access-date=1 July 2012}}</ref>

After spotting them at a band competition, [[Icehouse (band)|Icehouse]] frontman, Iva Davies promptly made contact and a mentoring partnership began with Iva and the band heading to the studio to produce some tracks. In 2011 Lime Cordiale supported Icehouse, on their comeback tour of 2011.<ref name="EP launch"/> In 2011, the duo released three singles via [[Bandcamp]] and played over 60 live shows.<ref name="EP launch"/>

In July 2012, Lime Cordiale released their debut extended play ''Faceless Cat'', which included the single "Pretty Girl".<ref name="Faceless Cat & Pretty Girl"/>

In 2013, Lime Cordiale released their second extended play ''Falling Up the Stairs'' which included the singles "Bullshit Aside" and "Sleeping At Your Door". The EP was produced by Daniel Denholm.<ref name="AM"/>

In 2015, they released three singles and in November 2015, released their third extended play ''Road to Paradise''.

===2017–present: ''Permanent Vacation'' and ''14 Steps to a Better You''===
In August 2017, Lime Cordiale released "Temper Temper", the lead single from their debut studio album, which was released in October 2017, titled ''[[Permanent Vacation (Lime Cordiale album)|Permanent Vacation]]'',<ref>{{cite web |last1=Toubro |first1=Tanja Brooks |title=Lime Cordiale's 'Permanent Vacation' is the debut album we were hoping for |url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/lime-cordiale-permanent-vacation-album-review/ |website=Tone Deaf |access-date=13 October 2017}}</ref> which was produced by Dave Hammer.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mack |first1=Emmy |title=Lime Cordiale Announce Debut Album 'Permanent Vacation' & Huge National Tour |url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/lime-cordiale-announce-debut-album-permanent-vacation-huge-national-tour/ |website=[[Music Feeds]] |access-date=30 August 2017}}</ref> ''Permanent Vacation'' peaked at number 79 on the ARIA charts.

In January 2019, their single "Dirt Cheap" placed 86th on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2018]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Triple J Hottest 100 |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/18/countdown/1-100/ |website=triple J |publisher=ABC |access-date=1 Jan 2019}}</ref>

In February 2019, they released the single "[[Money (Lime Cordiale song)|Money]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.umusicpub.com/au/News/2019/2/Lime-Cordiale-Release-New-Single-Money.aspx|title=Lime Cordiale Release new single Money|website=U Music Publishing| date= February 2019|access-date=29 October 2019}}</ref>
 
In May 2019, they premiered their single "[[Inappropriate Behaviour]]" on [[Triple J]] where they also talked about their forthcoming new album.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Newstead |first1=Al |title=First Spin: Lime Cordiale's sunny new song pushes them further into the limelight |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/musicnews/lime-cordiale-new-single-inappropriate-behaviour-interview/11120520 |website=triple J |publisher=ABC |access-date=17 May 2019}}</ref>

In September 2019, they released the single "[[Robbery (Lime Cordiale song)|Robbery]]", a song about "chasing after a girl who has stolen your heart". This came alongside the news of a national tour to support the release.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://musicfeeds.com.au/news/lime-cordiale-drop-single-robbery-with-video-add-more-dates-to-national-tour |title=Lime Cordiale drop single 'Robbery' with video & add more dates to national tour |work=[[Music Feeds]] |date=20 September 2019 |access-date=29 October 2019 |author=English, Laura}}</ref>

In January 2020, they had four songs in the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2019]], with "[[Robbery (Lime Cordiale song)|Robbery]]" placing at number 7.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.auspop.com.au/2020/01/billie-eilish-tops-triple-j-hottest-100 |title=Billie Eilish tops Triple J Hottest 100 |website=auspOp |date=25 January 2020 |access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref>

In July 2020, the band released ''[[14 Steps to a Better You]]'', which peaked at number 1 on the ARIA Charts. A reissue, ''14 Steps to a Better You (Relapse)'', was released on 13 November 2020 and includes an additional 6 tracks.

In November 2020, Lime Cordiale appeared on ABC TV's QandA.

In September 2021, Lime Cordiale announced a collaborative EP with actor and fellow musician [[Idris Elba]], titled ''Cordi Elba'' The EP is scheduled for release in January 2022.<ref name="Cordi Elba announcement"/>
