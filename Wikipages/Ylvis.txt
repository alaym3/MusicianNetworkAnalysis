'''Ylvis''' ({{IPA-no|ˈʏ̀lvɪs}}) is a [[Norway|Norwegian]] [[Comedy music|comedy]] duo consisting of brothers Vegard and Bård Ylvisåker. They debuted as professional variety artists in 2000 and have since appeared in several countries in variety shows, comedy concerts, television shows, radio shows and music videos. They hosted the Norwegian [[talk show]] {{lang|no|[[I Kveld med YLVIS|I kveld med Ylvis]]}} (''Tonight with Ylvis'') (2011-2016). Their song and music video "[[The Fox (What Does the Fox Say?)]]", written and filmed for the talk show, went [[viral video|viral]] on [[YouTube]] in September 2013. The video currently has over 1 billion views {{as of|April 2021||lc=yes}}.<ref name="wdtfs">{{YouTube|jofNR_WkoCE|"The Fox (What Does the Fox Say?)"}}</ref> They released an album called ''[[Ylvis: Volume I]]'', a compilation of ten older singles. In 2018 the brothers released a series titled [[Stories From Norway]], in which the Ylvis brothers collected headlining stories from Norway and presented them as short, musical documentaries.

== Early life and debut ==
Vegard Urheim (born 19 May 1979 in [[Trondheim]]) and Bård Urheim Ylvisåker (born 21 March 1982 in [[Bergen]]) were born to parents (Hans Terje Ylvisåker and Helga Urheim) from the [[Sogn]] district of Western Norway, the older two of three brothers. The youngest brother is Bjarte Urheim. Their early childhood years were spent in [[Angola]] and [[Mozambique]], where their father was an engineer during the civil wars there. After a few years in [[Africa]], the family moved back to Bergen, where the brothers received a musical upbringing and were trained in classical instruments. Vegard played the [[viola]] and Bård the [[violin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bt.no/bergenpuls/Ylvis-goes-philharmonic-2375028.html|title=Ylvis goes philharmonic|publisher=Bergens Tidende|access-date=20 September 2013}}</ref> However, both quit during their youth, Vegard instead focusing on guitar, voice, piano, [[double bass]] and comedy, and Bård on guitar, voice, comedy and acrobatics such as [[aerial silk]].

While attending high school at [[Fana Upper Secondary School|Fana gymnas]], they were heavily involved in school and variety shows as well as the school choir. During a performance, they were spotted by impresario Peter Brandt, who orchestrated their debut as professional variety artists at Ole Bull Teater in Bergen in 2000. Their debut show was called "{{lang|no|Ylvis en kabaret}}" ("Ylvis: A Cabaret")<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ylvis.com/om/|title=Om oss|publisher=Ylvis Holding ASA|access-date=20 September 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130914040612/http://www.ylvis.com/om/|archive-date=14 September 2013}}</ref> and was followed in 2003 by "{{lang|no|Ylvis en konsert}}" ("Ylvis: A Concert").

== 2006–09 ==
In 2006, the brothers debuted as hosts on national broadcasting with the radio show ''O-fag'' ({{lang|no|orienteringsfag}}, ''theoretical subject'', named after a subject previously taught in Norwegian primary schools, a combination of science and social studies) on NRK Radio.

In January 2007, they appeared onstage at Ole Bull Theater with a new variety show, ''Ylvis III''. They toured with the show for almost three years, the last show being played in [[Odda]] in western Norway in December 2009. The show was also recorded and published on [[DVD]]. Also in 2007, they debuted as hosts on national television with the show {{lang|no|Norges herligste}} (''Norway's Most Wonderful''), a version of the Swedish show {{lang|sv|100 höjdare}} (''100 Highs''). This was released on DVD as well as broadcast on Swedish TV.{{citation needed|date=September 2014}}

In 2008 they released a second series of the radio show ''O-fag'' and also a new TV show based on their {{lang|no|Ylvis møter veggen}} stage show on [[TVNorge]], a [[game show]] concept inspired by the "[[Brain Wall]]", a feature in the Japanese game show {{transl|ja|Tonneruzu no Minasan no Okage deshita}} on [[Fuji TV]].

This was followed up by another game show, {{lang|no|Hvem kan slå Ylvis}} (''Who can beat Ylvis?'') in 2009, based on the German show {{lang|de|[[Schlag den Raab]]}} (''Beat Raab''). Members of the public were invited to try to beat the brothers at various tasks, with a reward of up to one million [[Norwegian krone]]r.{{citation needed|date=September 2014}}

== 2009–present ==
In 2010, they hosted {{lang|no|Nordens herligste}} (''The Nordic’s Most Wonderful''), a pan-Nordic version of the show. In 2011 they reprised their variety stage show with "Ylvis 4", premiering at the Ole Bull Theater with the entire run sold out before opening night.{{citation needed|date=September 2014}} That year, the brothers also launched a comedy [[talk show|talkshow]] {{lang|no|I kveld med Ylvis}} (''Tonight with Ylvis'') on TVNorge, with Calle Hellevang-Larsen from comedy trio {{lang|no|[[Raske Menn]]|italic=no}} as a [[sidekick]].

Upon being renewed for a second season, the brothers created a production company, Concorde TV,<ref>{{cite web |title=Concorde TV AS - 998009812 - Firmapresentasjon |url=https://www.purehelp.no/m/company/details/concordetvas/998009812 |website=www.purehelp.no |language=no}}</ref> in order to retain rights and creative control of their work. The second series was broadcast in 2012, with [[David Batra]] replacing Hellevang-Larsen in the role of sidekick. In the Fall of 2013, Hellevang-Larsen returned for the third series of {{lang|no|I kveld med Ylvis}}. Despite technical difficulties during the broadcast, the first episode took the top ratings spot.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.seher.no/kjendis/ylvis-knuste-konkurrentene-65337|title=Ylvis knuste konkurrentene|publisher=Se og Hør|access-date=20 September 2013|language=no}}</ref>

A number of comedy and parody music videos, either originally featured on {{lang|no|I kveld med Ylvis}} or used to promote the show, have been released onto YouTube and other streaming media. "[[The Fox (What Does the Fox Say?)|The Fox]]" and "Stonehenge" have also been released as singles. "The Fox", produced by StarGate and M4SONIC, which was released on 3 September 2013 to promote the upcoming third series of {{lang|no|I kveld med Ylvis}}, went [[viral video|viral]] on [[YouTube]] and received 31 million views in its first two weeks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://au.ibtimes.com/articles/506891/20130918/fox-what-youtube-next-gangnam-style-billboard.htm%23.UmCsFH3LfK4|archive-url=https://archive.today/20131018033356/http://au.ibtimes.com/articles/506891/20130918/fox-what-youtube-next-gangnam-style-billboard.htm%23.UmCsFH3LfK4|url-status=dead|archive-date=18 October 2013|access-date=19 September 2018|title=The Fox: "What Did The Fox Say?" Hits 31 Million Views And Still Counting (With Video) - Entertainment & Stars}}</ref> It was the most watched video on YouTube worldwide in 2013,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/articles/2013/12/11/most-watched-youtube-videos-of-2013-ylvis-harlem-shake-more-watch.html|title=Most-Watched YouTube Videos of 2013: Ylvis, Harlem Shake & More|work=[[The Daily Beast]]|date=11 December 2013|access-date=5 October 2014}}</ref> and {{as of|August 2021|lc=yes||df=US}}, the video has over 1{{nbsp}} billion views.<ref name="wdtfs"/>

Ylvis received offers for interviews, concerts, and major label record contracts from several countries following the release of "The Fox".{{citation needed|date=September 2014}} Their first performance abroad was on ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'' in the United States, where they appeared on 20 September 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tv2.no/underholdning/fremfoerte-the-fox-for-foerste-gang-4124055.html|title=Fremførte "The Fox" for første gang|publisher=TV2|access-date=20 September 2013|language=no}}</ref> They also made an appearance at the iHeartRadio Music Festival in Las Vegas the next day. On 9 October 2013, [[Jimmy Fallon]] brought them to his late night show to perform live.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/10/12/arts/music/ylviss-unlikely-hit-started-as-a-joke.html?_r=0|last1=Itzkoff|first1=Dave|title=The Fox Says, 'I Can Make You Famous'|date=11 October 2013|newspaper=The New York Times |access-date=12 October 2013}}</ref> On 11 October 2013, they performed on ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|The Today Show]]'' outside the studios. On 15 November 2013 they performed on ''[[BBC Children in Need]]'' where they were joined on stage by [[Jedward]], [[The Cheeky Girls]] and [[Bucks Fizz (band)|Bucks Fizz]]. On 22 November 2013 Ylvis attended [[Mnet Asian Music Awards]] for the first time, and received the International Favorite Artist award.{{citation needed|date=September 2014}}

On 12 December 2013, they appeared on ''[[Live! with Kelly and Michael]]'' to promote their new children's book titled "What Does the Fox Say?" and later to perform. The book ''What Does the Fox Say?'' debuted as no. 1 on [[The New York Times Best Seller list|''The New York Times'' Best Seller list]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Ylvis book tops New York Times bestseller list |url=http://www.thelocal.no/20131220/ylvis-book-tops-new-york-times-list |newspaper=The Local - Norway's News in English |date=20 December 2012 |access-date=21 December 2013}}</ref>

On 15 October 2014 Ylvis released on [[iTunes]] a single titled "I Will Never Be A Star", which was recorded by their younger brother, Bjarte Ylvisåker. On 19 November 2015 Ylvis released their first album ''[[Ylvis: Volume I]]'' on iTunes, which is a collection of some of their popular YouTube hits. In 2015, Ylvis received a nomination at the Berlin Music Video Awards for Best Animation with their music video <nowiki>''Ytterst på tissen” (On the Tip of the Willy)''</nowiki>. The video was made by Julian Nazario Vargas from Animaskin.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Berlin Music Video Awards|date=16 April 2021|title=Berlin Music Video Awards 2015 Nominees - Set 4|url=https://www.berlinmva.com/official-selection-2/nominees-2015-set-4/|url-status=live|website=berlinmva.com}}</ref>

On 1 November 2017, it was revealed that the brothers would start a new television show entitled ''Stories from Norway''.<ref name="SFN">{{cite web|last1=Grindem|first1=Karianne|last2=Espeli|first2=Hedda Fannemel|title=Ylvis gjør comeback med spesielt program|url=https://www.dagbladet.no/kultur/ylvis-gjor-comeback-med-spesielt-program/68841569#_ga=2.268645937.111520804.1526881539-476357408.1526881539|website=[[Dagbladet]]|publisher=[[Aller Media]]|access-date=21 May 2018|date=1 November 2017}}</ref> The show, which blends the [[mockumentary]] genre with musical numbers,<ref name="SFN" /> premiered on 19 February 2018 on [[TVNorge]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Braseth|first1=Sofie|title=Ylvis snakker ut om USA-floppen|url=https://www.seher.no/kjendis/ylvis-snakker-ut-om-usa-floppen/69437071|website=[[Se og Hør]]|publisher=[[Aller Media]]|access-date=21 May 2018|date=9 February 2018}}</ref>

In 2019, both members appeared on the first season of ''Kongen Befaler'' (Norway's version of ''[[Taskmaster (TV series)|Taskmaster]]'').<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dplay.no/programmer/kongen-befaler|title=Dplay|website=www.dplay.no|access-date=16 November 2019}}</ref>
