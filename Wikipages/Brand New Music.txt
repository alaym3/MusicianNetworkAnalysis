'''Brand New Music''' ([[Hangul]]: 브랜뉴 뮤직, also stylized as '''BrandNew Music''' and initialized as '''BNM''') is a South Korean hip hop company founded in 2011 by rapper {{ill|Rhymer (rapper)|lt=Rhymer|ko|라이머}}.<ref name=":0">{{cite news|last1=Um|first1=Dong-jin|title=Rhymer finds success after years of struggle|url=http://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/article/article.aspx?aid=3003745|access-date=6 July 2017|agency=Korea JoongAng Daily|publisher=JoongAng Ilbo|date=4 May 2015}}</ref>

==History==
=== 2003–2012: Founding and first-generation artists ===
Kim Se-hwan (Hangul: 김세환), known as Rhymer, founded the record label IC Entertainment in 2003 after having failed to establish a career as a rapper since his debut in 1995.<ref name=":0"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.brandnewmusic.co.kr/artist/rhymer/about|title=Rhymer|website=Brand New Music|language=ko|access-date=31 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160325042037/http://www.brandnewmusic.co.kr/artist/rhymer/about#|archive-date=2016-03-25|url-status=dead}}</ref> The label later changed its name to Brand New Production.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/250086/20151020/san-e-verbal-jint-concert.htm|title=South Korean Rappers Verbal Jint And San E Plan Tokyo Showcase Concert For Next Month|date=20 October 2015|website=KpopStarz|access-date=31 March 2016|archive-date=16 April 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160416211238/http://www.kpopstarz.com/articles/250086/20151020/san-e-verbal-jint-concert.htm|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2009, Brand New Production merged with Future Flow, a record label owned by rapper and producer [[Cho PD]]. The combined company was named Brand New Stardom.<ref name=":0"/><ref name=":1"/>

Brand New Stardom split into two companies in 2011, with Cho PD founding [[Stardom Entertainment]] and Rhymer founding Brand New Music. Artists [[Verbal Jint]], Tae Hye Young, BNR, Keeproots, A-Man, and [[Miss S]] followed Rhymer to the new label.<ref name=":0"/><ref name=":1"/>

Following the split of Brand New Stardom in 2011, the newly founded Stardom Entertainment sued Brand New Music, saying that Oh Yoo-mi, a member of Miss S, was still contracted with Stardom. According to Brand New Music, the two companies had previously agreed that "neither company would restrict Miss $'s movement."<ref>{{cite news|title=Stardom Entertainment files lawsuit against Brand New Music over Miss $'s new single|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2012/09/stardom-entertainment-files-lawsuit-against-brand-new-music-over-miss-s-new-single|access-date=6 July 2017|agency=Allkpop|publisher=Allkpop|date=27 September 2012|archive-date=6 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180106223224/http://www.allkpop.com/article/2012/09/stardom-entertainment-files-lawsuit-against-brand-new-music-over-miss-s-new-single|url-status=live}}</ref>

Brand New Music released its first label collaboration, "Happy Brand New Year," at the end of 2012. Artists [[Verbal Jint]], [[Phantom (band)|Phantom]], [[As One (musical duo)|As One]], [[Miss S]], [[Swings (rapper)|Swings]], Shijin, and [[Bumkey]]  all contributed to the single.<ref>{{cite news|title=Brand New Music releases "Happy Brand New Year" MV|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2012/12/brand-new-music-releases-happy-brand-new-year-mv|access-date=31 March 2016|agency=Allkpop|publisher=Allkpop|date=5 December 2012|archive-date=22 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171222105435/http://www.allkpop.com/article/2012/12/brand-new-music-releases-happy-brand-new-year-mv|url-status=live}}</ref> It reached #24 on Korea's [[Gaon Digital Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|title=2012년 50주차 Digital Chart|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/online.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=ALL&targetTime=50&hitYear=2012&termGbn=week|website=Gaon Music Chart|publisher=Korea Music Content Industry Association|access-date=31 March 2016|archive-date=26 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180926211715/http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/online.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=ALL&targetTime=50&hitYear=2012&termGbn=week|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== 2017–present: Sub-label and third-generation artists ===
In 2017, Brand New Music launched its [[independent music|indie]] sub-label "Korean Roulette", led by Kiggen as president. The first act to sign under it was the duo SBGB.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news1.kr/articles/?2931787|title=키겐, 팬텀의 리더에서 코리안룰렛 대표로(인터뷰②)|access-date=2017-04-03|archive-date=2017-04-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170404044057/http://news1.kr/articles/?2931787|url-status=live}}</ref> In July 2017, Brand New formed a duo [[MXM (musical duo)|MXM]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xportsnews.com/?ac=article_view&entry_id=872621|script-title=ko:'프듀2' 임영민X김동현 "유닛 프로젝트 가동…7월말 선공개"(V라이브)|last=Kim|first=Mi-ji|date=2017-07-13|publisher=Xsportsmedia|language=ko|access-date=2017-07-28|archive-date=2017-07-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170728213049/http://www.xportsnews.com/jenter/?ac=article_view&entry_id=872621|url-status=live}}</ref>

On October 29, 2019, Brand New debuted [[BDC (group)|BDC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.joongboo.com/news/articleView.html?idxno=363373703|title=BDC "그룹명은 'BOYS DA CAPO' 약자, 초심 잃지 않겠다는 포부 담아"|publisher=Joongbu Ilbo|language=ko|date=2019-11-07|access-date=2019-12-25|archive-date=2019-11-10|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191110125657/http://www.joongboo.com/news/articleView.html?idxno=363373703|url-status=live}}</ref>
