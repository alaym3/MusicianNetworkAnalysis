{{nihongo|'''Kensuke Ushio'''|牛尾憲輔|Ushio Kensuke|born March 1, 1983|lead=yes}} is a [[Japan]]ese [[Musical composition|composer]], [[Rock music|rock]] and [[Electronic Body Music|EBM]] musician who performs under the moniker ''Agraph''.

He is a member of Japanese rock band [[Lama (Japanese band)|Lama]]. Together with Lama he released two full-length studio records ''New!'' in 2011 and ''Modanica'' a year later. Both albums charted on the [[Japanese Albums Chart]].<ref name="Lama">{{Cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/541711/rank/album/|title=Rama no Arubamu Uriage Rankingu|website=[[Oricon]]|language=Japanese|script-title=ja:LAMAのアルバム売上ランキング|trans-title=LAMA Album Sales Rankings|access-date=May 17, 2019}}</ref>

As an EBM musician he released three albums, all of which peaked in the Japanese Albums Chart.<ref name="Chartsagraph.">{{Cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/466100/rank/album/|title=Agraph no Arubamu Uriage Rankingu|website=[[Oricon]]|language=Japanese|script-title=ja:agraphのアルバム売上ランキング|trans-title=Agraph Album Sales Rankings|access-date=May 17, 2019}}</ref> He wrote and composed soundtracks for several anime television series and movies such as ''[[A Silent Voice (film)|A Silent Voice]]'', ''[[Space Dandy]]'', ''[[Devilman Crybaby]]'', ''[[Boogiepop]]'', ''[[Ping Pong (manga)|Ping Pong]]'', ''[[Liz and the Blue Bird]]'' and ''[[Sanī/32]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/432212/products/|title=Kensuke Ushio no Sakuhin|website=[[Oricon]]|language=Japanese|script-title=ja:kensuke ushioの作品|trans-title=Kensuke Ushio's Works|access-date=May 17, 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==
As a child Ushio learned playing [[piano]]. He studied arts and music at [[university]]. At the same time he learned the usage of the audio editor software [[Pro Tools]]. At the age of 20 Ushio got his first contact with electronic music such as [[Techno music|techno]].<ref name="MU">{{Cite web|url=https://natalie.mu/music/pp/agraph|title=agraph - Ongaku Natarī Tokushū Intabyū|last=Hashimoto|first=Shōhei|website=Natalie|publisher=Natasha|language=Japanese|script-title=ja:agraph - 音楽ナタリー 特集・インタビュー|trans-title=agraph - Music Natalie Feature / Interview|access-date=May 17, 2019}}</ref>

He started his solo career in 2007 under the moniker ''agraph.'' and creates electronic music. His debut album ''A Day, Phases'' which was produced by [[Takkyu Ishino]] was released in December 2008.<ref name="MU" /> Two years later his second album ''Equal'' was released which ranked in the [[Japanese Albums Chart]]s.<ref name="Chartsagraph." />

In 2011 Ushio and some former musicians of bands like [[Supercar (band)|Supercar]] and [[Number Girl]] formed a rock band called [[Lama (Japanese band)|Lama]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ponycan.us/news/pdf/2018/Liz%20and%20the%20Blue%20Bird%20Press%20Release%20PONY%20CANYON%2018%20AX.pdf|title=Liz and the Blue Bird Press Release|date=June 25, 2018|website=[[Pony Canyon]]|format=PDF|access-date=May 17, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oricon.co.jp/news/85250/|title=Nakamura Kōji, Furukawa Miki-ra ga, Shin Bando LAMA wo Shidō!|date=February 28, 2011|website=[[Oricon]]|language=Japanese|script-title=ja:中村弘二、フルカワミキらが、新バンドLAMAを始動!|trans-title=Kōji Nakamura, Miki Furukawa, and Others Start New Band LAMA!|access-date=May 17, 2019}}</ref> The band released two albums which both ranked on the official Albums Charts of Japan.<ref name="Lama" />

Due to his involvement in Lama, Ushio's third album named ''The Shader'' was released in 2016, six years after his second album. Ushio works as composer of film music as well, with a particular focus on animated projects. Among his film and television credits are music composition for ''[[Space Dandy]]'' (2014), ''[[A Silent Voice (film)|A Silent Voice]]'' (2016), ''[[Liz and the Blue Bird]]'' (2018), and ''[[Boogiepop
