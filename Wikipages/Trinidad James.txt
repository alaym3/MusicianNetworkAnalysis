'''Nicholaus Joseph Williams''' (born {{Birth date|mf=yes|1987|9|24}}), better known by his stage name '''Trinidad James''' (often stylized as '''Trinidad Jame$'''), is a Trinidadian-American rapper.<ref name="Early life">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/12/who-is-trinidad-james/growing-up-in-atlanta |title=Growing Up in Atlanta — Who Is Trinidad James? |publisher=Complex |date=December 7, 2012|access-date=February 20, 2013}}</ref> In 2012, he signed a recording contract with [[Def Jam Recordings]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Horowitz|first=Steven|title=Trinidad James Officially Announces Signing To Def Jam|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22205/title.trinidad-james-officially-announces-signing-to-def-jam|access-date=December 13, 2012|newspaper=HipHopDX|date=December 12, 2012}}</ref> The label dropped him in 2014 when he failed to release an album, although he continues to release music as an independent artist<ref>{{cite news|last=Markman|first=Rob|title=Trinidad James Reveals The Real Reason He Got Dropped By Def Jam|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1915290/trinidad-james-talks-failed-def-jam-deal/|access-date=February 1, 2015|newspaper=MTV.com|date=August 28, 2014}}</ref> and is signed to [[Artist Partner Group|Artist Publishing Group]] for songwriting.

== Early life ==
Nicholaus Williams was born in [[Port of Spain]], [[Trinidad and Tobago]].<ref name="Birth place" /> His family moved to [[Canada]] and [[Florida]] before settling in [[the Bronx]], [[New York City]].<ref name="Early life" /> As a child, he went to [[Catholic school]].<ref name="Early life" /> He has stated he cannot remember any parts of his life before the age of seven because of a head injury.<ref name="Early life" /> In the eighth grade, he moved to [[Atlanta]], where he began playing [[basketball]].<ref name="Early life" /> He began rapping in November 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/12/who-is-trinidad-james/learning-to-rap |title=Learning To Rap — Who Is Trinidad James? |publisher=Complex |access-date=February 20, 2013}}</ref> He has listed [[Jay-Z]], [[Cam'ron]], [[T.I.]], and [[Young Jeezy]] as his influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2012/12/who-is-trinidad-james/influences |title=Musical Influences — Who Is Trinidad James? |publisher=Complex |access-date=February 20, 2013}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2012, James released his official debut single, called "[[All Gold Everything]]", which charted in the top 40 of the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. The song was taken from his official debut mixtape, titled ''Don't Be S.A.F.E. (Sensitive As Fuck Everyday)'', which was released on July 31, 2012,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://trinidadjames.bandcamp.com/album/dont-be-s-a-f-e |title=Dont Be S.A.F.E. &#124; Trinidad Jame$ |publisher=Trinidadjames.bandcamp.com |date=July 31, 2012 |access-date=February 14, 2014}}</ref> and officially re-released with a [[music video]] on October 16, 2012. On December 13, 2012, it was announced James had signed a joint venture with [[Def Jam Records]] for approximately $2 million.<ref>{{cite web|author=12 May 2012 15:40:18 |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1698917/trinidad-james-def-jam-record-deal.jhtml |title=Trinidad James Signs Joint Venture With Def Jam – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV.com |date=December 13, 2012 |access-date=February 14, 2014}}</ref> In January, Def Jam re-released ''Don't Be S.A.F.E'' to iTunes, which included the remix to "All Gold Everything" featuring [[2 Chainz]], [[T.I.]], and [[Young Jeezy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/dont-be-s.a.f.e./id596601276 |title=iTunes – Music – Don't Be S.A.F.E. by Trinidad James |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=January 29, 2013 |access-date=February 14, 2014}}</ref>

On August 6, 2013, he announced that he would be releasing his second mixtape, titled ''10 PC Mild'', on August 13.<ref name="missinfo1">{{cite web|url=http://www.missinfo.tv/index.php/trinidad-james-10pc-mixtape-interview-exclusive/#more-100890 |title=Trinidad Jame$ Reveals His New Project In An Exclusive Sitdown with Miss Info #10PCMild |publisher=MissInfo.tv |date=August 6, 2013 |access-date=August 29, 2013}}</ref> After months of not releasing any new music, James announced that he had been dropped from Def Jam on August 1, 2014. He also indicated that instead of releasing his album through the label, he would make it available for free, and that the producers and rappers who contributed to the project should not expect any compensation because he had "no money."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/08/trinidad-james-relieved-of-his-duties-by-def-jam/|title=Trinidad Jame$ Dropped From Def Jam After Two Years|work=XXL}}</ref> In April 2016, James released the single "Just a Lil' Thick (She Juicy)" featuring [[Mystikal]] and [[Lil Dicky]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/just-lil-thick-she-juicy-feat./id1101313734?app=itunes|title=Just a Lil' Thick (She Juicy) [feat. Mystikal & Lil Dicky] – Single by Trinidad James|work=[[iTunes]]|date=April 15, 2016|access-date=May 19, 2016}}</ref> On December 31, 2016, James released a mixtape "The Wake Up 2" to streaming services such as [[Spotify]] and to mixtape websites alike.

On June 9, 2018, James released a single titled "M.M.M. (Marilyn Maryland Marilyn)" to streaming services.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/m-m-m-marilyn-maryland-marilyn-single/1403476877 |title=M.M.M. (Marilyn Maryland Marilyn) – Single by Trinidad James on Apple Music |website=Itunes.apple.com |date=July 9, 2018 |access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref> The music video, directed by Mike Marasco was uploaded to [[YouTube]] on the same day.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h0kLBTsJ-MY |title=Trinidad James – M.M.M. (Marilyn Maryland Marilyn) |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=July 9, 2018 |access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref>

On November 27, 2019, in an interview with popular rap talk show "No Jumper", James stated that his current favorite rappers were "Doja Cat and Lil Nas X, I love those guys, partly because their flows remind me of myself" <ref>{{Citation|title=Trinidad James Speaks on Blowing Up, If He Fell Off, His Impact, Joe Budden & More|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxBvdyUzciE|language=en|access-date=June 3, 2020}}</ref> and later in the interview when asked about what his hopes for his current favorite rappers James proclaimed "I would hate to see them quit. You know. The last thing I'd want to see is for them to fall off just like I have."<ref>{{Cite web|title=YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxBvdyUzciE&feature=youtu.be|access-date=June 3, 2020|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>

As of 2018, James is a host on a [[YouTube]] talk show, ''Full Size Run''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ESmXsZxjvCc&t=1s |title=Full Size Run Returns With a New Co-Host I Season 2 Trailer |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=August 16, 2018 |access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref> and he appeared in the Safdie Brothers' film ''[[Uncut Gems]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pop-culture/2019/12/making-of-a24-uncut-gems |title='Uncut Gems': The Making Of This Year's Best Film |website=Complex.com |date=December 23, 2019 |access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref>
