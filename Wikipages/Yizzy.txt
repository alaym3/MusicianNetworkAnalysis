'''Yizzy''' (born Yisrael Parkins,<ref>{{cite web |title=Yisrael Parkins - Credits |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/yisrael-parkins-mn0003970573/credits |website=AllMusic |access-date=9 May 2021 |language=en}}</ref> 30 December 1999) is an English [[Grime (music genre)|grime]] MC. In 2017, Yizzy released his debut EP ''This Is Life''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2017/04/premiere-young-yizzy-this-is-life|title=Premiere: Young Yizzy The Grime Kid Offers Up Bold Debut EP 'This Is Life'|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=2018-03-27|archive-date=2018-03-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180328044423/http://www.complex.com/music/2017/04/premiere-young-yizzy-this-is-life|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2018/01/ones-to-watch-13-british-emcees-destined-for-a-big-2018|title=Ones To Watch: 13 British Emcees Destined For A Big 2018|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=2018-03-27|archive-date=2018-03-28|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180328041316/http://www.complex.com/music/2018/01/ones-to-watch-13-british-emcees-destined-for-a-big-2018|url-status=live}}</ref> His follow-up EP, ''S.O.S.'',<ref>{{Cite web|title=Get To Know: Yizzy {{!}} MTV UK|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/get-to-know/news/get-to-know-yizzy|access-date=2021-06-09|website=www.mtv.co.uk|language=en}}</ref> was released in May 2018. He then released his third EP, ''Welcome To Grime Street'', in 2019 and his fourth EP, 'Prince of Grime' in 2020.

==Personal life==
Yizzy was born in Wales to a father from Jamaica and a Welsh mother.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47028482 |title=How did Lawro get on against grime star Yizzy? |work=BBC Sport |access-date=2019-01-29 |archive-date=2019-01-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190129213816/https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47028482 |url-status=live }}</ref> He is also a supporter of [[Arsenal F.C.]]<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/av/football/48155432 |title=UK grime artist Yizzy's 90-second freestyle rap on Arsenal |publisher=BBC Sport |date=5 May 2019 |access-date=30 December 2020 |archive-date=30 March 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210330080619/https://www.bbc.com/sport/av/football/48155432 |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Career ==
Yizzy (originally Young Yizzy) released 'Insanity EP' in 2016, with production credits featuring [[Jme (musician)|JME]], Swifta Beater and K1.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Who is Young Yizzy?
