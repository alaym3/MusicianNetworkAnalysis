'''Javarri Latre Walker''' (born June 13, 2000), known professionally as '''Hotboii''' (often stylized as {{nowrap|'''H O T B O I I'''}} or {{nowrap|'''HOTBOII'''}}), is an American rapper. His 2020 breakout single "Don't Need Time" has received over 58 million views on [[YouTube]] and spawned a remix featuring rapper [[Lil Baby]]. He is signed to [[Geffen Records]], [[Interscope Records]] and Rebel Music, in conjunction with Hitmaker Music Group and 22 Entertainment.<ref name="complex" />

== Early life ==
Javarri Latre Walker was born on June 13, 2000, in [[Orlando, Florida]], growing up in the [[Pine Hills, Florida|Pine Hills]] neighborhood on the westside as one of 17 siblings.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.the360mag.com/hotboii-anonymous/|title=HOTBOII – "ANONYMOUS"|work=360 Magazine|date=July 14, 2020|access-date=December 28, 2020}}</ref> He listened to artists such as [[Lil Wayne]], [[Rich Homie Quan]] and [[Kodak Black]].<ref name="complex">{{cite news|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/09/hotboii-interview|title=Hotboii Is Next Up From Florida|work=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]|first=Eric|last=Wells|date=September 15, 2020|access-date=December 29, 2020}}</ref> He began rapping at the age of seven and began releasing music in 2016 with the help of his mother,<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/hotboii-mn0003934738/biography|title=Artist Biography|first=Fred|last=Thomas|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=December 28, 2020}}</ref><ref name="XXL">{{cite news|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/hotboii-interview-the-break/|title=The Break Presents: Hotboii|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|first=Robby|last=Seabrook III|date=September 30, 2020|access-date=December 28, 2020}}</ref> beginning by uploading music onto [[SoundCloud]] and YouTube.<ref name="knock">{{cite news|url=https://theknockturnal.com/hotboii-talks-100k-management-fatherhood-and-upcoming-project-00-baby/|title=Exclusive: Hotboii Talks Fatherhood, 100k Management, and Debut Album|work=The Knockturnal|first=Nishat|last=Baig|date=October 2, 2020|access-date=December 29, 2020}}</ref>

Walker served two years in a [[juvenile detention]] program from 2016 to 2018 for [[breaking and entering]].<ref name=complex /><ref name=XXL /> It was during this time that he decided to take his career more seriously, further developing his songwriting  while in juvenile detention.<ref name=XXL /><ref name=knock />

== Career ==
Just before his incarceration, Walker had begun to receive some attention locally for his song "Switcharoo".<ref name=XXL /> After his release in 2018 he began to put out more singles, publishing his first music video that September for "Life of a Dog".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VlPcKu9D1XI|title=HOTBOII – LIFE OF A DOG (OFFICIAL MUSIC VIDEO) Shot by @Stbr_films|publisher=YouTube|date=September 28, 2018|accessdate=December 29, 2020}}</ref> In 2019 he released the single "YG's", a "detailed walk through [his] experiences and survival tactics in the street" that quickly gained traction.<ref name=complex /><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/meet-hotboii-another-amazing-artist-hailing-from-orlando-florida|title=Meet HOTBOII: Another amazing artist hailing from Orlando, Florida|work=Elevator Mag|date=August 5, 2019|access-date=January 2, 2021}}</ref> He began 2020 by securing features from [[Stunna 4 Vegas]] ("4PF Like Baby") and [[Rylo Rodriguez]] ("Sick of Cell") before releasing his breakout single "Don't Need Time" on April 27.<ref name=XXL /> Written in memory of his recently deceased friend Wolph, the piano-driven track was notably slower-paced and more self-reflective than his previous songs.<ref name=XXL /> In five months it accumulated more than nine million streams on [[Spotify]] and 24 million YouTube views on its music video, which was shot at Wolph's funeral.<ref name=XXL /> On August 25, a remix featuring [[Lil Baby]] was released along with a new music video that addressed the theme of [[police brutality]] following the killing of another one of Hotboii's friends, Salaythis Melvin, by an [[Orange County Sheriff's Office (Florida)|Orange County sheriff's deputy]] earlier that month.<ref name=XXL /><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/08/hotboii-lil-baby-dont-need-time-remix-video|title=HOTBOII and Lil Baby Drop Video for "Don't Need Time" Remix|work=[[Complex Networks|Complex]]|date=August 25, 2020|access-date=January 2, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.orlandosentinel.com/news/crime/os-ne-salaythis-melvin-vanshawn-sands-gang-war-20200828-wdovm4dlzva7failk66avumrvu-story.html|title=Deputies had the man they wanted a month before Salaythis Melvin shooting. They let him go|work=[[Orlando Sentinel]]|first=David|last=Harris|date=August 28, 2020|access-date=January 2, 2021}}</ref>

Hotboii released his first mixtape, ''Kut Da Fan On'', on May 22, 2020.<ref name=allmusic /> It included previous hits such as "Don't Need Time", "YG's", and "Goat Talk", as well as features from [[Plies (rapper)|Plies]], LPB Poody, Rico Cartel and 438 Tok. Some of the songs were written during his time served in the juvenile detention program.<ref name="mack">{{cite news|url=https://audiomack.com/world/post/hotboii-interview|title=HOTBOII Is Turning Pain Into Fuel|work=[[Audiomack]]|first=Donna-Claire|last=Chesman|date=December 11, 2020|access-date=January 3, 2021}}</ref> ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' noted how he "came into his own by polishing his blend of painful street tales and witty punchlines" on the project,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/hotboii-dim-new-song-listen/|title=The Ones: Hotboii's "Dim"|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|first=Alphonse|last=Pierre|date=November 4, 2020|access-date=January 2, 2021}}</ref> while [[Audiomack]] called it "41 hyper-realistic and melodic minutes of contemporary hip-hop goodness."<ref name=mack /> After releasing more music videos, he secured a feature from [[Polo G]] on "Goat Talk 2", a followup to the original which had by then become one of his most popular songs.

On December 11, 2020, he released his debut album ''Double O Baby'' with features from [[Lil Mosey]], [[Toosii]] and [[Pooh Shiesty]].<ref name="elevator">{{cite news|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/hotboii-drops-label-debut-project-double-o-baby|title=Hotboii Drops Label Debut Project 'Double O Baby'|work=Elevator Mag|date=December 11, 2020|access-date=January 3, 2021}}</ref> Expanding on the theme from his last project, songs like "Police Brutality", "Malcolm X" and "Problems (No Rights)" addressed problems facing the African-American community in the context of the recent [[George Floyd protests]] and specifically his friend Salaythis Melvin's killing by police.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/hotboii-gives-us-a-track-by-track-breakdown-of-double-o-baby|title=Hotboii Gives Us A Track By Track Breakdown Of 'Double O Baby'|work=Elevator Mag|date=December 18, 2020|access-date=January 3, 2021}}</ref> ''Elevator Mag'' praised his "masterful flow and soul-baring storytelling" on the album.<ref name=elevator /> Later that month he performed on the Rolling Loud 'Home for the Holidaze' livestream headlined by [[Rick Ross]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.59547/title.rick-ross-tapped-for-rolling-louds-home-for-the-holidaze-live-stream-featuring-pouya-fat-nick-hotboii-more|title=RICK ROSS TAPPED FOR ROLLING LOUD'S 'HOME FOR THE HOLIDAZE' LIVE STREAM FEATURING POUYA, FAT NICK, HOTBOII & MORE|work=[[HipHopDX]]|first=Marisa|last=Mendez|date=December 15, 2020|access-date=January 3, 2021}}</ref>

Following the February 2021 release of "Fuck Shit", Hotboii returned on May 28 with the single and music video for "Nobody Special", featuring [[Future (rapper)|Future]]. Hotboii will next perform at Rolling Loud Miami  from July 23 to 25.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/hotboii-future-nobody-special-video|title=Premiere: Hotboii and Future Team Up for "Nobody Special" Video|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|last=Mahadevan|first=Tara C.|date=May 28, 2021|access-date=June 1, 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Walker has a son.<ref name=knock /> Walker has symptoms of [[Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder|ADHD]].<ref name=complex />
