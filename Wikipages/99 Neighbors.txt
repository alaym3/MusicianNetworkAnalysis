'''99 Neighbors''' is an American [[Musical ensemble|music group]] formed in [[Burlington, Vermont|Burlington]], [[Vermont]]. It consists of seven members: Connor "Swank" Stankevich, Hank "HANKNATIVE" Collins, Sam Paulino, Aidan Ostby, Julian "Juju" Segar-Reid, Caleb "Somba" Hoh, and Jared Fier.<ref>{{Cite web|title=About|url=https://99nbrs.com/about|access-date=2020-07-31|website=99 Neighbors|language=en-US}}</ref> The members comprise [[Singing|singers]], [[Rapping|rappers]], and [[Record producer|producers]]. Its music - a combination of [[hip hop]], [[jazz]], [[Rock music|rock]], and [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]] influences - has drawn comparisons to [[Odd Future]] and [[Brockhampton (band)|Brockhampton]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2019-11-19-meet-99-neighbors-raps-latest-hydra-interview |title=Meet 99 Neighbors |website=www.allmusic.com |date=2020-06-08 |access-date=2020-07-08}}</ref>

==History==
Members Ostby, Paulino, and Collins graduated from [[South Burlington High School]] in [[South Burlington, Vermont]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Navarro |first1=Cass |title=99 Neighbors Are Keeping It In-house |url=https://acclaimmag.com/music/99-neighbors-are-keeping-it-in-house/ |website=Acclaim Magazine |date=November 8, 2019}}</ref> Paulino and Collins played on their high school's [[American football|football]] and [[lacrosse]] teams together. After graduating from high school, Paulino and Collins shared an apartment in [[Burlington, Vermont|Burlington]], where they began recording and producing music full time collectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.paradigmagency.com/music/ninety-nine-neighbors/#:~:text=SHORT%20BIO,DJ%20JFear%20and%20producer%20Somba. |title=99 Neighbors - Paradigm Talent Agency|website=www.paradigmagency.com |date=2019-07-29 |access-date=2020-07-08}}</ref> Hoh and Segar-Reid grew up together in [[Burlington, Vermont|Burlington]]. After discovering they lived near one another, Paulino contacted Hoh about a collaborative opportunity using [[SoundCloud]] and formed a group.

== Music release ==
On January 1, 2019, 99 Neighbors released its first studio album, ''Television''. It took the band two months to record the full-length debut album. In the same year, Fier became a DJ and started producing with the group.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|title=99 Neighbors - Paradigm Talent Agency|url=https://www.paradigmagency.com/music/ninety-nine-neighbors/|access-date=2020-07-23|website=www.paradigmagency.com}}</ref>

In July 2019, the group signed a deal with [[Warner Records]]' manager Pat Corcoran, who formally managed [[Chance the Rapper]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/07/pat-corcoran-announces-partnership-with-warner-records-99-neighbors |title=Pat Corcoran Announces Partnership With Warner Records, Introduces First Artist 99 Neighbors |website=www.complex.com |date=2019-07-29 |access-date=2020-07-08}}</ref>

Since signing with Warner Records, the group has released multiple singles.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Chesman|first=Donna-Claire|title=Meet 99 Neighbors, Rap’s Latest Hydra|url=https://djbooth.net/features/2019-11-19-meet-99-neighbors-raps-latest-hydra-interview|access-date=2020-07-23|website=DJBooth|language=en}}</ref> In October 2019, they released a song in collaboration with brass group [[Brasstracks]] and singer/songwriter PhiloSofie, titled "F**k No". The single has been streamed over 16 million times on [[Spotify]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/5uhqkMm8dyQvX83kl4Znq0 |title=99 Neighbors on Spotify |website=www.spotify.com |date=2020-07-09 |access-date=2020-07-09}}</ref> Other notable tracks by 99 Neighbors include "Fake Pods" and "Ripstick", which have amassed over 11 million streams on Spotify.
