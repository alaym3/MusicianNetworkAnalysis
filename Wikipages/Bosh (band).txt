'''Bosh''' were an English [[Christian rock]] band based in [[Bournemouth]], England. The band formed in 1996 around brothers [[Dave Griffiths (musician)|David]] and Michael Griffiths. Bosh are noted as one of bands that started the Nth Degree Community and Risen Records in the early 2000s.<ref name="cross">{{cite web|url=http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/articles/music/Bosh_The_Bournemouth_Rockers_On_Their_Live_CD_The_Nth_Fest__Future_Plans/27087/p1/|title=Tony Cummings quizzed BOSH frontman Dave Griffiths about the band's slow but sure move to bigger things.|publisher=Cross Rhythms|accessdate=16 February 2010}}</ref>

Bosh released four CDs on Risen Records, the EP ''VII'' in 2005, and the live album ''Middle of Somewhere'' in 2007. ''Sound the Alarms'' was released in 2008 produced by a team that included [[Steve Ennever]] and [[Paul Burton]]. ''The Gloaming Hour'' EP was released in 2009.<ref name="loud">{{cite web|url=http://www.louderthanthemusic.com/document.php?id=1063|title=Bosh Celebrate CD Release Of 'The Gloaming Hour' With Launch Gig|publisher=Louder than the Music|accessdate=16 February 2010}}</ref> They were also a regular feature in the Christian music scene in the UK, playing at Nth Fest, Creation Fest, [[Greenbelt festival]], Grace Festivals, and the [[Big Church Day Out Festival]].<ref name="cross" /> They also played outside the Christian music scene and opened for bands such as [[Frightened Rabbit]].{{citation needed|date=June 2019}}

The band's final line-up consisted of David Griffiths, Mike Griffiths, Steve Coates, James Grant, and touring keys player Dave Evans. Former keyboard player Grant Howard left the band in March 2010 to move to [[Kitchener, Ontario]], [[Canada]]. He was followed in July 2010 by guitarist Matt Gainsford who left to continue studying in [[Wisconsin]].{{citation needed|date=June 2019}}

In 2011, the band decided to enter indefinite hiatus to pursue other projects. They played their last show on 2 September 2011 in Bournemouth.<ref name="HopeFM">{{cite web|url=http://www.hopefm.com/index.php/component/content/article/274-boshgoodbye|title=HopeFM, Bournemouth Band say goodbye for now|publisher=HopeFM|accessdate=2 September 2011}}{{dead link|date=July 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> David Griffith's next project, Chaos Curb Collaboration, which included contributions from former members Mike, James, Grant, Dave Evans, was signed to Joining the Dots distribution in 2012. David Griffiths released a solo album in 2016 featuring contributions from former members Mike, Grant, James, and Dave Evans.{{citation needed|date=June 2019}}
