'''Kirk Jerel Randle''' (born August 20, 1989), better known by his stage name '''Kirko Bangz''', is an American [[rapping|rapper]], singer, songwriter and record producer from [[Houston, Texas]]. He is best known for his single, "[[Drank in My Cup]]" which was released in 2011 and peaked at number 28 on the US [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart. His name is derived from [[grunge rock]] singer [[Kurt Cobain]].

==Early life==
Kirko Bangz was born Kirk Jerel Randle in Houston, Texas.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.houstonpress.com/music/kirko-bangz-is-still-finding-his-way-9732531 |title=Kirko Bangz Is Still Finding His Way {{!}} Houston Press |last=Caldwell |first=Brandon |date=September 6, 2017 |work=[[Houston Press]]}}</ref> Bangz started rapping at the age of fifteen, due in part to witnessing his mother struggle as a single parent; using his mother's pain as motivation. He graduated from [[North Shore Senior High School (Houston, Texas)|North Shore High School]] in Houston and would later go on to attend [[Prairie View A&M University]] where he would major in communications.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rapper Kirko Bangz aka Mr. What Yo Name Iz?|url=http://thebarmag.com/rapper-kirko-bangz-aka-mr-what-yo-name-iz/|work=Thebarmag.com|access-date=September 2, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130715035148/http://thebarmag.com/rapper-kirko-bangz-aka-mr-what-yo-name-iz|archive-date=July 15, 2013}}</ref>

==Career==

=== 2009–12: Career beginnings and mixtapes ===
At Prairie View he was able to focus on his music, and in 2009 he released his first mixtape ''Procrastination Kills''. He got the attention of a fellow student and now manager D Will.<ref>[http://thebarmag.com/rapper-kirko-bangz-aka-mr-what-yo-name-iz Rapper Kirko Bangz aka Mr. What Yo Name Iz?] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130715035148/http://thebarmag.com/rapper-kirko-bangz-aka-mr-what-yo-name-iz |date=July 15, 2013 }}. Thebarmag.com (October 17, 2010). Retrieved August 31, 2012.</ref> His stage name is a play on late singer [[Kurt Cobain]]'s name.<ref>{{cite web|title = Kirko Bangz Creatively Honors Legend Kurt Cobain|url = http://rapfix.mtv.com/2011/04/05/kirko-bangz-creatively-honors-legend-kurt-cobain/|website = RapFix|access-date = May 30, 2015}}</ref> Bangz released his first official single titled "[[What Yo Name Iz?]]" on February 7, 2011. His first major hit actually began as a freestyle.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/What-Yo-Name-Iz-Explicit/dp/B004KRWFMK/ |title=What Yo Name Iz? [Explicit&#93;: Kirko Bangz: MP3 Downloads |website=Amazon |date=n.d.|access-date=February 18, 2012}}</ref> It has since peaked at number forty-one on the chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Top Hip-Hop/R&B Songs – Week of April 23, 2011 He is also known for his song "Drank in My Cup".
|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2011-04-23/r-b-hip-hop-songs?order=timeon|work=Billboard|access-date=February 18, 2012}}</ref> A remix was released on June 24, 2011 and features [[Big Sean]], [[Wale (rapper)|Wale]] and [[Bun B]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/what-yo-name-iz-feat.-wale/id444908800 |title=What Yo Name Iz? (feat. Wale, Big Sean, and Bun B)(Remix) – Single |publisher=iTunes.com |date=n.d.|access-date=February 18, 2012}}</ref> He released a mixtape shortly after on March 1, 2011 titled ''Procrastination Kills 3''.<ref>[http://www.datpiff.com/Kirko-Bangz-Procrastination-Kills-3-mixtape.204426.html Kirko Bangz Procrastination Kills 3]. Datpiff. Retrieved August 31, 2012.</ref>

His second single titled "[[Drank in My Cup]]" was released on September 16, 2011.<ref>[https://www.amazon.com/Drank-In-My-Cup-Explicit/dp/B005HJOJQA Drank In My Cup: Kirko Bangz: MP3 Downloads]. Amazon.com. Retrieved February 18, 2012.</ref> During the week of February 25, 2012, the single debuted at number ninety-six on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{cite web|title=U.S. Billboard Hot 100 – Week of February 25, 2012
|url=http://www.musicstop.org/this-week-billboard-hot-100|work=MusicStop|access-date=February 18, 2012}}</ref> It has since peaked at number 28 on the chart and at number one on the [[Hot Rap Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/305960/Kirko+Bangz/chart?f=379|title=Kirko Bangz – Chart history – Billboard|magazine=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/305960/Kirko+Bangz/chart?f=1222|title=Kirko Bangz – Chart history – Billboard|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> The song spawned many remixes and freestyles featuring hip-hop artists [[J. Cole]], [[2 Chainz]], [[Tyga]], [[Bow Wow (rapper)|Bow Wow]], [[Kid Ink]], [[Chamillionaire]] and singer [[Trey Songz]]. Bangz released a new mixtape called "Procrastination Kills 4" on September 4, 2012. His third single from his debut album titled "[[Keep It Trill]]" was released on November 30, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Keep-It-Trill-Explicit/dp/B00A9US1HA/ |title=Keep It Trill: Kirko Bangz: MP3 Downloads |website=Amazon |date=n.d.|access-date=December 4, 2012}}</ref>

=== 2013 to the present: Atlantic Records and 300 Entertainment ===
On March 26, 2013, it was announced that Bangz would be a part of [[XXL Magazine]]'s 2013 Freshman Class.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/03/xxl-freshman-class-2013-cover-revealed/2/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=March 27, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130329202138/http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/03/xxl-freshman-class-2013-cover-revealed/2/ |archive-date=March 29, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Three days later he would announce the title of his debut album to be, ''Bigger Than Me''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/03/kirko-bangz-says-that-he-should-have-been-on-i-been-on-remix-calls-omission-disrespectful|title=Kirko Bangz Says That He Should Have Been on "I Been On (Remix)," Calls Omission "Disrespectful"|website=Complex}}</ref> On July 4, 2013, Bangz announced that he would release another mixtape, ''Progression 3'' on August 1, 2013 prior to his debut album.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bangz|first=Kirko|title=Progression 3 Tweet|url=https://twitter.com/KirkoBangz/status/354437051074093056}}{{Primary source inline|date=March 2020}}</ref> He has also revealed the cover for the mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bangz|first=Kirko|title=Progression 3 Cover|url=https://twitter.com/KirkoBangz/status/359159885411262464|publisher=Twitter}}{{Primary source inline|date=March 2020}}</ref> However he would announce that it would be pushed back a week later.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kirko bangz pushes back Progression 3|url=https://twitter.com/KirkoBangz/status/362794655282233344|publisher=Twitter}}{{Primary source inline|date=March 2020}}</ref> The mixtape was released on August 12, 2013. Guest appearances came from Wale, [[Nipsey Hussle]], [[French Montana]], [[Z-Ro]], [[Paul Wall]], [[YG (rapper)|YG]] and [[Slim Thug]] among others. Production was handled by [[K.E. on the Track]], [[DJ Mustard]] and [[Jahlil Beats]] among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/08/kirko-bangz-releases-new-mixtape-progression-3/|title=Kirko Bangz Releases New Mixtape, 'Progression 3′ – XXL|website=XXL Mag}}</ref>

He was moved to [[Atlantic Records]] another subsidiarity of Warner Music Group. Then on January 28, 2014, Bangz released the album's first single "Hoe" featuring YG and [[Yo Gotti]]. His debut album will be released during 2014 by Atlantic Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboombox.com/kirko-bangz-hoe-yo-gotti-yg/|title=Kirko Bangz Delivers 'Hoe' Featuring Yo Gotti, YG|website=The Boombox}}</ref> From March 6 to April 17, 2014, Kirko Bangz went on The Trillest [[concert tour]] which was co-headlined by frequent collaborator Bun B.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/bun-b-announces-dates-for-the-trillest-tour-with-kirko-bangz|title=Bun B Announces Dates for 'The Trillest Tour' With Kirko Bangz|website=Complex}}</ref> On December 16, 2014, Bangz released the ''Progression V: Young Texas Playa'' mixtape, which was produced primarily by Austinite [[Kydd (rapper)|Kydd Jones]] and [[Sound M.O.B.]] (who produced Kirko's previous No. 1 hit "Drank in My Cup); the mixtape featured artists such as Texas heavyweights Bun B and [[Riff Raff (rapper)|Riff Raff]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/12/stream-kirko-bangz-progression-v-mixtape|title=Stream Kirko Bangz' "Progression V" Mixtape|website=Complex}}</ref> On July 21, 2015, it was announced that Bangz signed with [[300 Entertainment]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fashionably-early.com/2015/07/21/kirko-bangz-inks-deal-with-300-entertainment/|title=Kirko Bangz Inks Deal With 300 Entertainment – Fashionably-Early|date=July 21, 2015}}</ref> Although he released a few singles to promote his debut album ''Bigger Than Me,'' the album would go unreleased. Bangz stated in an interview that some of his unreleased material is owned by Warner Music Group, therefore being unable to release it. On August 31, 2015, Bangz released his first EP titled ''Fallin' Up Mix.'' On February 19, 2016, Bangz released his second [[Extended play]], titled ''[[Playa Made]]''. Then on August 26, 2016, Bangz released brand new mixtape titled ''Back Flossin'.'' On August 10, 2017, Bangz released another mixtape of the ''Progression'' series titled ''Progression 17.'' He is currently working on a new album titled ''Soul Food.''{{Citation needed|date=January 2020}}
