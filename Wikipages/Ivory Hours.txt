'''Ivory Hours''' is a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[indie pop]] band from [[London, Ontario|London]], [[Ontario]]. In 2018 the band members are guitarist and singer Luke Roes, Bassist Chris Levesque, and Drummer Thomas Perquin.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ivory Hours Bandcamp|url=https://ivoryhours.bandcamp.com/|website=Bandcamp|accessdate=28 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Ivory Hours on CBC |url=http://music.cbc.ca/#!/artists/Ivory-Hours |website=CBC |accessdate=28 August 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160301002442/http://music.cbc.ca/#!/blogs/2013/6/Rear-view-Mirror-Who-was-Suzanne-and-why-did-Leonard-Cohen-make-her-famous |archivedate= 1 March 2016 }}</ref>

==History==
Ivory Hours was founded in 2012 by brother and sister Luke and Annie Roes,<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lalama|first1=Adam|title=IVORY HOURS: LOVE AND LOSS|url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/blog/ivory-hours-mary-interview|website=noisey|accessdate=28 August 2015}}</ref>  The band released their full-length debut album 'Morning Light' on June 9, 2015.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Hudson|first1=Alex|title=Ivory Hours 'Morning Light'|url=http://exclaim.ca/Music/article/ivory_hours-morning_light_album_stream|accessdate=28 August 2015|publisher=Exclaim!|date=May 26, 2015}}</ref> The album was produced by [[Dan Brodbeck]] and engineered by João Carvalho. In the summer of 2015 the band won [[CFNY-FM|CFNY 102.1 the Edge's]] Next Big Thing contest out of 442 bands from across Canada.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Reaney|first1=James|title=London band Ivory Hours is the Next Big Thing|url=https://lfpress.com/2015/08/25/london-band-ivory-hours-is-the-next-big-thing|accessdate=28 August 2015|agency=London Free Press|date=August 25, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Citizen Edge|title=Ivory Hours crowned as the Edge Next Big Thing|url=http://www.edge.ca/2015/08/26/ivory-hours-crowned-as-the-edge-next-big-thing/|website=Edge.ca|accessdate=28 August 2015|date=August 26, 2015}}</ref>

The following autumn Ivory Hours were the Grand Prize winners of Canada's Walk of Fame's Emerging Artist Music Mentorship Program which awarded the band $25,000, studio time, and a performance during Canada's Walk of Fame events.<ref>{{cite news|title=Canada's Walk of Fame announces the winners of the 2015 'Emerging Artist Music Mentorship Program supported by RBC' valued at over $100,000|url=http://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/canadas-walk-of-fame-announces-the-winners-of-the-2015-emerging-artist-music-mentorship-program-supported-by-rbc-valued-at-over-100000-528652811.html|accessdate=25 June 2016|publisher=Canadian Newswire|date=22 Sep 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Stoute|first1=Lenny|title=BTW-Canadian Music Week Edition2 Dirty Frigs,Terra Lightfoot, Elliot Maginot, Ivory Hours, Monster Truck, Elephant Stone, The Brood, Kira Isabella, Brave Shores|url=http://cashboxcanada.ca/6408/btw-canadian-music-week-edition2-dirty-frigsterra-lightfoot-elliot-maginot-ivory-hours-monster-|website=Cashbox Canada|accessdate=25 June 2016}}</ref> The band were also finalists in [[CBC Music]]'s Searchlight competition, eventually placing fourth.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lamberink|first1=Liny|title=News|url=https://chrwradio.ca/content/ivory-hours-shines-searchlight-competition|website=CHRW Radio|accessdate=26 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Introducing the 4 Searchlight 2015 finalists |url=http://music.cbc.ca/#!/blogs/2015/5/Introducing-the-4-Searchlight-2015-finalists |website=CBC Music |publisher=CBC |accessdate=26 June 2016 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160301002442/http://music.cbc.ca/#!/blogs/2013/6/Rear-view-Mirror-Who-was-Suzanne-and-why-did-Leonard-Cohen-make-her-famous |archivedate= 1 March 2016 }}</ref>

Their first single from ''Morning Light'', "Warpaint", garnered significant airplay across Canada and charted on [[CBC Radio 2]]'s ''[[Radio 2 Top 20]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Godmere |first1=Emma |title=Radio 2 Top 20 January 15: Basia Bulat rockets up the chart, Cage the Elephant challenge Alessia Cara for the crown |url=http://music.cbc.ca/#!/blogs/2016/1/Radio-2-Top-20-January-15-Basia-Bulat-rockets-up-the-chart-Cage-the-Elephant-challenge-Alessia-Cara-for-the-crown |website=CBC Radio 2 |publisher=CBC |accessdate=26 June 2016 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160301002442/http://music.cbc.ca/#!/blogs/2013/6/Rear-view-Mirror-Who-was-Suzanne-and-why-did-Leonard-Cohen-make-her-famous |archivedate= 1 March 2016 }}</ref>

In the winter of 2016, Ivory Hours did their first cross-Canada tour in support of ''Morning Light''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Murphy|first1=Sarah|title=Ivory Hours Embark on Canadian Tour, Share Bombay Bicycle Club Cover|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/ivory_hours_embark_on_canadian_tour_share_bombay_bicycle_club_cover|website=Exclaim!|accessdate=26 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Toland|first1=Emma|title=Ivory Hours @ The Mod Club Toronto – Review|url=http://thescenemagazine.ca/ivory-hours-the-mod-club-toronto-review/|accessdate=26 June 2016|agency=The Scene|publisher=Scene Magazine|date=26 April 2016}}</ref>

Ivory Hours has opened for [[Sloan (band)|Sloan]],<ref>{{cite news|last1=Belanger|first1=Joe|title=Sloan, Ivory Hours rock New Year’s Eve|url=https://lfpress.com/2015/12/29/sloan-ivory-hours-rock-new-years-eve|accessdate=26 June 2016|agency=London Free Press|publisher=Sun Media|date=30 Dec 2015}}</ref> and shared the stage with other notable Canadian bands including [[Matthew Good]], [[The Zolas]], [[Awolnation]], and [[City and Colour]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Hey Rosetta!, Sheepdogs, Matthew Good among performers at Riverfest Elora|url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/kitchener-waterloo/hey-rosetta-sheepdogs-matthew-good-among-performers-at-riverfest-elora-1.3557092|website=CBC news|accessdate=26 June 2016}}</ref>
