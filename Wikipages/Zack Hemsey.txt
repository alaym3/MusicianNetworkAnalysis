'''Zack Hemsey''' (born May 30, 1983) is an American composer, songwriter, and record producer, whose work has been largely popularized through its inclusion in films and film trailers. A notable example is the film trailer for ''[[Inception]]'', which included his composed piece "Mind Heist."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xitHF0IPJSQ|title=- YouTube|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=26 October 2021}}</ref><ref name="slashfilm" /> It's a common misconception that the piece used for this film trailer was composed by [[Hans Zimmer]], who instead composed the score for the film.<ref>{{cite web|title=I am not Hans Zimmer|url=https://zackhemsey.blogspot.com/2010/05/with-all-confusion-going-around.html|website=ZackHemsey.Blogspot.com|date=May 12, 2010|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref> "Mind Heist" was also used as introductory music for the video game ''[[Madden NFL 12]]'', and is occasionally used by the television series ''[[America's Got Talent]]''. Hemsey's music has been featured in film trailers for ''[[The Town (2010 film)|The Town]]'', ''[[Lincoln (2012 film)|Lincoln]]'', ''[[2 Guns]]'', and others.<ref>{{cite web|title=Credits|url=https://www.zackhemsey.com/Zack_Hemsey/Placements.html|website=ZackHemsey.com|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref> Hemsey's song "Vengeance" was prominently featured in the film ''[[The Equalizer (2014 film)|The Equalizer]]'', as well as in trailers for the second season of the [[HBO]] program ''[[Game of Thrones]]'' and the [[21st Century Fox]] miniseries ''[[24: Live Another Day]]''; the latter aired during [[Fox NFL|Fox Sports]]' U.S. TV broadcast of [[Super Bowl XLVIII]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Zack Hemsey Discography|url=https://www.zackhemsey.com/Zack_Hemsey/Records.html|website=ZackHemsey.com|access-date=August 22, 2018}}</ref>

In 2020, Hemsey made his directorial debut, in which he shot, produced, and edited a feature length documentary titled ''BIRTH''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt13430676/?ref_=fn_tt_tt_6|title=Birth|date=7 October 2020|access-date=26 October 2021|website=IMDb.com}}</ref>  The film explores the psychology of labor and the birth experience, through the lens of an unmedicated homebirth.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.birththemovie.com/|title=WATCH NOW|website=Birththemovie.com|access-date=26 October 2021}}</ref>  The film was released online worldwide on the [[iTunes Store]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tv.apple.com/us/movie/birth/umc.cmc.4s3m869604v4u7x0hjehylk9r|title=Birth &#124; Apple TV|first=Zack|last=Hemsey|date=1 January 2020|website=Tv.apple.com|access-date=26 October 2021}}</ref> and [[Vimeo]] OnDemand.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/ondemand/birththemovie|title=Watch BIRTH Online &#124; Vimeo On Demand|first=Zack|last=Hemsey|website=Vimeo.com|date=17 May 2019|access-date=26 October 2021|via=Vimeo}}</ref>

== Personal life==
Hemsey grew up in [[New Jersey]] with his older sister Tara. He graduated from [[Palisades Park High School]] in 2001,{{citation needed|date=January 2019}} and studied music and philosophy at [[Rutgers University]], graduating with a [[summa cum laude]].

== Career ==
Hemsey began his career as the composer for an obscure hip-hop group called Nine Leaves, where he was one of five lyricists.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/ZackHemsey|title=YouTube "About Me"|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=December 28, 2010}}</ref> He began his solo career in 2010 with the release of his first album "Empty Room" and has continued to independently release his own music, which tends to combine elements of alternative hip-hop, alternative rock, classical orchestra, and/or world instrumentation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.zackhemsey.com/|title=Zack Hemsey|website=Zackhemsey.com|access-date=26 October 2021}}</ref>

Hemsey has written articles on a variety of subject matter, which he has published on his blog "Thoughts & Ramblings."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://zackhemsey.blogspot.com|title=thoughts &
