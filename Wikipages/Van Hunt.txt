'''Van Hunt''' (born March 8, 1970) is an American singer, songwriter, multi-instrumentalist and record producer. He released his debut album, ''[[Van Hunt (album)|Van Hunt]]'', in 2004, and a follow-up, ''[[On the Jungle Floor]]'', in 2006, both on [[Capitol Records]]. He won the [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals]] for appearing on the tribute version of the [[Sly & the Family Stone]] song, "[[Family Affair (Sly & the Family Stone song)|Family Affair]]", in 2007. He transitioned to [[Blue Note Records]] where his 2008 album, ''Popular'', was shelved and would not be released until 2017. He self-released the compilation album ''Use in Case of Emergency'' in 2009.

==Early life==
Hunt was born in [[Dayton, Ohio]]. His father, Van Hunt, Sr., was a friend of [[Ohio Players]] drummer Jimmy "Diamond" Williams. Hunt took up the drums at age 7, and saxophone at the age of 8, later adding bass and keyboards.<ref name="CreativeLoafing">Foster, Saptosa (September 30, 2004). "[http://atlanta.creativeloafing.com/gyrobase/Content?oid=oid%3A16911 Good Van Hunting ]". ''[[Creative Loafing (Atlanta)|Creative Loafing]]''. Retrieved on December 8, 2007.</ref> Guitar was the last instrument he learned to play; he played guitar in a rock band called Royalty. Hunt moved to Arlington, [[Texas]] for a short time before relocating to [[Atlanta, Georgia]] in 1996 to attend [[Morehouse College]], where he studied English, but soon dropped out.<ref name="CreativeLoafing"/><ref>Suggs, Kimberly. "[http://www.juicymagazine.us/entertainment/index.php?id=54 Unleashed: On the Floor with Van Hunt] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071223000739/http://www.juicymagazine.us/entertainment/index.php?id=54 |date=December 23, 2007 }}". ''Juicy Magazine''. Retrieved on December 7, 2007.</ref> In Atlanta, he started producing a few hip-hop [[demo (music)|demos]] for Atlanta rappers in order to pay bills. Hunt met up-and-coming record producers and artists like [[Dallas Austin]], [[Jermaine Dupri]] and [[TLC (band)|TLC]] through his work on demos.

==Career==
===Early career===
Hunt wrote and co-produced the song "Hopeless" for singer [[Dionne Farris]], formerly of [[Arrested Development (hip hop group)|Arrested Development]], and joined her band on guitar and keyboards. "Hopeless" appeared on the soundtrack for the film ''[[Love Jones (film)|Love Jones]]'' (1997). Hunt co-wrote several songs with [[Rahsaan Patterson]] on his album ''Love in Stereo'' (1999), and co-wrote with [[Cree Summer]], including the song "Mean Sleep", for her album ''[[Street Faërie]]'' (1999), produced by [[Lenny Kravitz]]. He also collaborated with [[Joi (singer)|Joi]] on the single "Missing You" (2002). Through Dionne Farris, Hunt met [[A&R]] person [[Randy Jackson]] (who later went on to be a judge on [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox's]] ''[[American Idol]]''). Jackson would become Hunt's manager in 2002.

===Capitol records===
Hunt recorded much of the songs from his debut album in 2000. Producer Dallas Austin took Hunt's recording to [[Capitol Records]], leading him to sign with the label in 2001. Hunt's first album, ''[[Van Hunt (album)|Van Hunt]]'', was released in February 2004. The album included the singles "Down Here in Hell (With You)", "Dust", and "Seconds of Pleasure", and was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best Urban/Alternative Performance]]. Hunt's second album, ''[[On the Jungle Floor]]'', was released in 2006. The album featured the single, "Character," a revisited "Mean Sleep", as a duet with [[Nikka Costa]], and a cover of "No Sense of Crime" by [[The Stooges]]. The album was co-produced by [[Bill Bottrell]]. Hunt has toured and appeared with [[Mary J. Blige]], [[Alicia Keys]], [[Boney James]], [[The Roots]], [[Seal (musician)|Seal]], [[Angie Stone]], [[Coldplay]], [[The Brand New Heavies]], the [[Dave Matthews Band]], and [[Kanye West]].

In 2006, Hunt appeared with Nikka Costa on the [[Sam Moore]] album, ''Overnight Sensational'', on the song "If I Had No Loot". Hunt was also featured on "Half the Fun", a track on the [[Count Bass D]] album, ''Act Your Waist Size'', released on Fat Beats Records.

In 2007, Hunt won a [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals]], along with [[John Legend]] and [[Joss Stone]], for their cover of the [[Sly & the Family Stone]] song, "[[Family Affair (Sly & the Family Stone song)|Family Affair]]", which appeared on the 2006 tribute album  ''[[Different Strokes by Different Folks]]''. Hunt described winning the award as "one of the bigger pleasures I've had" to ''The Athens Blur Magazine'' in 2009.<ref name="AthensBlur">Hackett, Will (2009). "Hunting for Goodness". ''The Athens Blur Magazine'', issue 9, p. 23</ref>

===Blue Note===
Hunt released the 4 track digital EP, ''The Popular Machine'', on August 7, 2007. Retrieved on November 20, 2021.</ref> Blue Note had taken over Hunt's recording contract after corporate restructuring at Blue Note and Capitol's parent company, [[EMI]]. ===Godless Hotspot and What Were You Hoping For?===
[[File:Van Hunt.jpg|thumb|250px|Van Hunt performing at Music Midtown, 2012.]]
On June 14, 2011 Van Hunt took to his Twitter to announce the release of his first song in 3 years. The song "June" was released on June 15 at midnight via Mike Ragogna of The Huffington Post along with the announcement of the title of his forthcoming album (''What were you hoping for''?) and its release date.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/van-hunts-free-june-downl_b_877200 |title=Van Hunt's Free "June" Download, Plus Chatting with Steve Cropper and Gomez's Tom Gray|first=Mike|last=Ragogna |publisher=HuffPost |date=June 15, 2011 |access-date=2015-07-26}}</ref> A subsequent longer article about the song "June" and the new album and Van's new approach would appear later that same day on the Music remedy website. This article would not only talk about the joint venture-between Hunt's own label Godless-hotspot and Thirty-Tigers. It also goes on to say that "June" is the first of a series of free songs that will be released from various sites up until the release of the new album. The next will be "eyes Like Pearls" the first official single from the album that is set to be released to radio sometime in July<ref>[http://musicremedy.com/v/van-hunt/album/celebrates-new-album-by-giving-away-songs-11592.html]  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111223115457/http://musicremedy.com/v/van-hunt/album/celebrates-new-album-by-giving-away-songs-11592.html |date=December 23, 2011 }}</ref>

===The Fun Rises, the Fun Sets===
Van Hunt released updates of work on his upcoming album via PledgeMusic during the course of 2014. In March 2015, he announced the title of his album as ''The Fun Rises, the Fun Sets'', due for release on May 5, 2015. He previewed the release of the new album with the new single "Vega (Stripes On)", which was available for release via iTunes and Amazon and followed this up with another single, "Old Hat". Both tracks had official videos accompany their releases. The album charted at #31 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]].
