'''Emin Araz oglu Agalarov''' ({{lang-az|Emin Araz oğlu Ağalarov}}, {{lang-ru|Эмин Аразович Агаларов; born December 12, 1979}}), also known as '''Emin Arazovich Agalarov''', is an [[Azerbaijani people|Azerbaijani]]-[[Russian people|Russian]] singer and businessman. He writes and performs songs in [[English language|English]] and [[Russian language|Russian]] and is widely popular in [[Azerbaijan]] and [[Russia]].<ref name="Quinn">{{cite web|last1=Quinn|first1=Gerry |title=Musician Emin is definitely not just in it for the money|url=http://www.irishexaminer.com/lifestyle/artsfilmtv/musician-emin-is-definitely-not-just-in-it-for-the-money-325040.html|website=[[Irish Examiner]]|access-date=12 July 2017|date=21 April 2015}}</ref><ref name="Bilger">{{cite news|last1=Bilger|first1=Oliver|title=Dieser Oligarchensohn will Europa mit Musik erobern|url= https://www.welt.de/vermischtes/article146348882/Dieser-Oligarchensohn-will-Europa-mit-Musik-erobern.html| newspaper=Die Welt |access-date=12 July 2017|date=13 September 2015|trans-title=Son of oligarch wants to conquer Europe with music}}</ref>{{Better source needed|date=October 2020}}

==Early life==
Emin Arasovich Agalarov was born in [[Baku]], Azerbaijan. He is the son of Azerbaijani–Russian [[Russian oligarch|oligarch]] [[Aras Agalarov]], head of the {{interlanguage link|Crocus Group|ru}}. 
Emin's father is Azerbaijani and his mother is [[History of the Jews in Azerbaijan|Jewish]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jewishtelegraph.com/prof_301.html|title=Rob Prepares Star Emin for International Fame and Glory|last=Yaffe|first=Simon|website=Jewish Telegraph|access-date=2018-02-10}}</ref> He considers himself a Muslim.<ref>{{cite web|first=Brian|last=Boyd |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/culture/music/not-eminem-just-emin-1.2147234 |title=Not Eminem. Just Emin |work=[[The Irish Times]] |date=22 March 2015 |access-date=11 July 2017}}</ref> His family moved to [[Moscow]] when Emin was four years old. When he was 13, his parents sent him to a school in [[Switzerland]].<ref name="school">{{cite web|url= http://crocusgroup.com/press-center/news/769/|title=Trust - Translation of interview the Agalarovs gave to Forbes by Crocus Group|date=March 27, 2013|access-date=March 6, 2018|publisher=[[Crocus Group]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://baku.ws/19821-emin-agalarov-na-oblozhke-forbes-life.html|title=April 2013 cover of Russian-language issue of Forbes Life|access-date=March 6, 2018|publisher=baku.ws}}</ref> Agalarov moved to [[Tenafly, New Jersey]] in 1994 and graduated from [[Tenafly High School]] in 1997.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Cowen|first1=Richard|last2=Shkolnikova|first2=Svetlana|url= https://www.northjersey.com/story/news/nation/2017/07/14/russian-pop-star-tied-trump-has-tenafly-roots/477758001/ |title=Russian pop star tied to Trump has Tenafly roots|work=[[The Record (Bergen County)|The Record]]|date=July 13, 2017|access-date=February 28, 2018|quote=Russian pop star Emin Agalarov wore black leather, drove a souped-up moped and wanted to be the next [[Elvis Presley]], having spent his teenage years living large and dreaming big on the streets of his adopted hometown, [[Tenafly]]. Twenty years after graduating from [[Tenafly High School]], the Russian crooner this week hit it really big.... Emin Agalarov as a senior in Tenafly High School's 1997 yearbook.}}</ref><ref name="school" /> He graduated from [[Marymount Manhattan College]] in [[New York City]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Calamur |first1=Krishnadev |title=Who Is Emin Agalarov? |url=https://www.theatlantic.com/politics/archive/2017/07/who-is-emin-agalarov/533127/ |access-date=7 June 2019 |publisher=The Atlantic |date=July 10, 2017}}</ref>

==Business career==
When he came back to Kuala Lumpur in 1995, he was named as commercial director of Crocus Group. In 2011, he was promoted to vice president of Crocus Group. Since 2014, he has held the position of executive vice president of Crocus Group. As part of his responsibilities, he oversees the following ventures:
* The F1RST chain of retail-entertainment complexes. At present there are two locations – VEGAS [[Kashira Highway|Kashirskoe Shosse]] (480,000&nbsp;m2) and VEGAS Crocus City complex, also in [[Moscow Oblast]], (285,000&nbsp;m2) – with a third location, VEGAS [[Kuntsevo District|Kuntsevo]], which opened in 2017.<ref>{{cite news|title=Crocus Group Opens Vegas Kuntsevo Shopping Mall|url=http://www.propertyxpress.com/news/Crocus_Group_Opens_Vegas_Kuntsevo_Shopping_Mall-040109|access-date=2 March 2018|publisher=Property Xpress|date=22 Sep 2017}}</ref>
* The restaurant portfolio of "Restaurants by Crocus Group," which includes the chain of the legendary [[Nobu restaurant]]s; the Zafferano franchise, dedicated to modern Azerbaijani cuisine; the Edoko franchise of Japanese of sushi-bars; the Rose Bar franchise; Forte Bello, serving family-style Italian cooking; and Backstage.<ref name="CG-Bio" />
* The [[Crocus City Mall]] luxury shopping center.<ref name="CG-Bio" />
* Crocus Fashion Retail, featuring designer boutiques from [[Sergio Rossi]] (2), Casadei, Le Silla, Alberto Guardiani, Artioli, Santoni, and [[Cesare Paciotti]], as well as the multi-brand boutique, [[Crocus Fashion]], three Crocus Stok stores, Crocus Atelier Couture, and the luxury watch-maker UBOAT.<ref name="CG-Bio" />
* [[Crocus City Hall]] concert venue.<ref name="CG-Bio" />
* Vegas City Hall, a concert venue slated to open in [[VEGAS Crocus City]] in 2016.<ref name="CG-Bio" />
* Sea Breeze Resort ([[Nardaran]], [[Azerbaijan]]).<ref name="CG-Bio">{{cite web|title=Emin Arasovich Agalarov, biography|url=http://crocusgroup.com/crocus/management/emin-agalarov/|website=[[Crocus Group]]}}</ref>

==Music career==
Emin's interest in music began in [[high school]] whilst in the [[United States]]. After graduating from high school, he returned to Russia, where he continued to write songs. In 2005 he began working with his vocal and music teacher, the Azerbaijani singer [[Muslim Magomayev (musician)|Muslim Magomayev]], to whom he pays tribute in his concerts.

In 2006 Emin gained attention for his debut studio album ''Still'' which in the first six months sold 70,000 copies in Russia and an additional 30,000 copies in [[Commonwealth of Independent States|CIS]] countries.

He then released an album per year: ''Incredible'' in 2007, ''Obsession'' (a set of classic [[Frank Sinatra]] and [[Elvis Presley]] songs) in 2008;<ref name="Luxe">{{cite web|url=http://www.luxemag.org/jet-set/emin-agalarov.html|title=Emin Agalarov: Singing Commercial Director|work=[[Luxe Magazine]]|date=19 November 2009|access-date=10 March 2011}}</ref> and in 2009, ''Devotion''. Each of these albums was supported by concerts in Moscow and Baku.

In 2010, Emin relocated to London to work with producer [[Brian Rawling]] and to focus on the UK music market. Emin's album ''Wonder'' was released in Russia and CIS in November 2010 and in the UK on 21 March 2011. The song "Heart Keeps Beating" was the first single in Russia from his second album ''After the Thunder'' and received good radio airplay on [[Russian Radio]] in 2012.

Emin's first single from his 2012 album ''After The Thunder'' was the song "[[Baby Get Higher]]" written by [[David Sneddon]], [[Graham Stack (record producer)|Graham Stack]], [[Mark Read (singer)|Mark Read]] and was added to the [[BBC Radio 2]] A-List in April 2012.

On May 26, 2012, Emin was the local interval act during intermission on the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2012|Eurovision Song Contest]] that was held in Baku. Emin performed "Never Enough", a track taken from the album ''After the Thunder''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wiwibloggs.com/2012/08/19/album-review-emins-after-the-thunder/19794/|title=Album Review: Emin's "After the Thunder"|last=Adams|first=William Lee|date=19 August 2012|website=wiwibloggs|access-date=18 May 2016}}</ref>

Emin was the opening act for [[Jennifer Lopez]]'s September 23, 2012, concert in Baku.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Gulustan|title=Jennifer Lopez gave concert in Baku (PHOTO)|url=http://gulustan.info/2012/09/j-lo-2/|access-date=11 July 2017|date=24 September 2012|archive-date=13 April 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180413185555/http://gulustan.info/2012/09/j-lo-2/|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="Warrick">{{cite web|last1=Warrick|first1=Joby|title=Tiny Azerbaijan unleashes pop-power against Iran's mullahs|url= https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/tiny-azerbaijan-unleashes-pop-power-against-irans-mullahs/2012/10/14/0a0819ec-14b3-11e2-bf18-a8a596df4bee_story.html|work=[[The Washington Post]]|access-date=11 July 2017|date=14 October 2012}}</ref>

Emin performed "In Another Life" in a music video with [[Donald Trump]] and Miss Universe 2013 contestants.<ref name="vid">{{YouTube|iuZUNjFsgS8|EMIN in Another Life Official Music Video ft. Donald Trump and Miss Universe 2013 Contestants}}</ref><ref name="crowley">{{cite news|last=Crowley|first=Michael|date=15 May 2016 |title=When Donald Trump brought Miss Universe to Moscow; How a 2013 beauty pageant explains Trump's love for Russia and obsession with Vladimir Putin. |url= http://www.politico.com/story/2016/05/donald-trump-russia-moscow-miss-universe-223173 |newspaper=[[Politico]] |access-date=2 August 2016}}</ref> Contestants were required to appear in the video without pay.<ref name="NYT11july17" />

In May 2014, Emin won the award for Best-selling Azerbaijan Artist at the [[2014 World Music Awards]].<ref name="Twice">{{cite web|title=World Music Awards 2014 Winners List|url= http://www.twiceasnice.co.uk/index.php/news/6632/world-music-awards-2014-winners-list/|website=Twice as Nice|access-date=11 July 2017|date=30 May 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20150623052010/http://www.twiceasnice.co.uk/index.php/news/6632/world-music-awards-2014-winners-list/|archive-date=23 June 2015|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

In 2016, Emin with Grigory Leps and Sergey Kozhevnikov started the [["ZHARA" International Music Festival]].

==Relationship with Donald Trump==
In January 2013, Agalarov and his father visited [[Las Vegas]], [[Nevada]], after [[Donald Trump]], the beauty pageant's owner, announced at [[Miss Universe 2012]] that the next competition would be hosted at [[Crocus City Hall]], owned by Emin's father Aras, in Moscow.<ref name="NYT11july17">{{cite news|last1=Twohey|first1=Megan|last2=Eder|first2=Steve|title=How a Pageant Led to a Trump Son's Meeting With a Russian Lawyer|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/07/10/us/politics/rob-goldstone-russia-trump.html| access-date=11 July 2017|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=11 July 2017|page=A16}}</ref>

In November 2013, Agalarov's father hosted [[Miss Universe 2013]] at his Crocus City Hall in Moscow.<ref name="NYT11july17" /> Agalarov performed during the evening gown competition section. He also made a music video with Trump and the contestants.<ref name="vid" /><ref name="crowley" /> Contestants were required to appear in the video without pay.<ref name="NYT11july17" /> Agalarov reportedly "offered to send prostitutes to Trump's hotel room" during Trump's trip to Moscow, "but the repeated offers were rejected by [[Keith Schiller]], Trump's longtime bodyguard."<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2017/07/12/world/europe/ap-eu-trump-russia-probe-middlemen-.html|title=Unlikely Middlemen: Trump Jr. Emails Point to Father-Son Duo|agency=[[Associated Press]]|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=2017-07-12|access-date=2017-07-23}}</ref>

[[Herman Gref]], who runs [[Sberbank of Russia]], Agalarov and his father hosted a dinner for Trump on the night of the pageant.<ref name="NYT11july17" /> While in Moscow, [[Phil Ruffin]], who is a partner in [[Trump International Hotel Las Vegas]], and Trump met with Agalarov and his father at the [[Ritz-Carlton]].<ref name="NYT11july17" />

Agalarov and his publicist, [[Rob Goldstone]], helped arrange the [[Trump campaign–Russian meeting|June 2016 meeting]] between [[Donald Trump Jr.]], [[Jared Kushner]], [[Paul Manafort]], Russian lawyer [[Natalia Veselnitskaya]], and [[Ike Kaveladze]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2017/07/18/politics/eighth-man-trump-tower-meeting-russia/index.html|title=8th person at Trump Tower meeting identified|publisher=[[CNN]]|last=Brown|first=Pamela|date=July 19, 2017}}</ref> Goldstone wrote in an email later published by Trump Jr. that the Russian government wanted to leak damaging information on [[Hillary Clinton]] to the Trump campaign.<ref name="HeLdWag">{{cite news|last1=Helderman|first1=Rosalind S.|last2=Wagner|first2=John|title=Donald Trump Jr. was told campaign meeting would be with 'Russian government attorney,' according to emails|url= https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/donald-trump-jr-was-told-campaign-meeting-would-be-with-russian-government-lawyer-according-to-emails/2017/07/11/70b957e2-664c-11e7-9928-22d00a47778f_story.html?hpid=hp_hp-top-table-main_russiatrump-1135am%3Ahomepage%2Fstory&tid=a_inl|work=[[The Washington Post]]|access-date=11 July 2017|date=11 July 2017}}</ref><ref name="Birnbaum">{{cite news|last1=Birnbaum|first1=Michael|title=Trump's Russian business partner had said his election would be 'an amazing breakthrough'|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/worldviews/wp/2017/07/11/trumps-russian-business-partner-had-said-his-election-would-be-an-amazing-breakthrough/|work=[[The Washington Post]]|access-date=11 July 2017|date=11 July 2017}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Emin Agalarov is the son of [[Aras Agalarov]], an Azerbaijani-Russian billionaire.<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-12481714|title=Emin: a singer with connections|publisher=[[BBC News]]|first=Neil| last=Smith|date=1 March 2011|access-date=11 March 2011}}</ref> From 2006 to 2015, he was married to [[Leyla Aliyeva]], the daughter of the [[President of Azerbaijan]], [[Ilham Aliyev]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iol.co.za/tonight/gossip/leyla-the-champagne-connection-1.1039319|title=Leyla, the champagne connection|publisher=iol.co.za|first=Paul|last=Harris|date=10 March 2011| access-date=11 March 2011}}</ref> The couple had twin sons in 2008 and adopted a girl, Amina, after their divorce, in 2015. In 2018, Agalarov married the former model and businesswoman Alyona Gavrilova and had a baby girl, Afina (Афина) .<ref>{{cite news |last1=Fitzharris |first1=Dustin |title=More Love From Emin: The Russian Pop Star Opens Up About His Music, Family and Wealth |url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/more-love-from-emin-the-r_b_7310182 |access-date=7 June 2019 |publisher=HuffPost |date=May 19, 2015}}</ref> The couple divorced in 2020.
