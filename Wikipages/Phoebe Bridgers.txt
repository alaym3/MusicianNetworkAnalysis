'''Phoebe Bridgers''' (born August 17, 1994<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|title=Phoebe Lucille Bridgers, Born 08/17/1994 in Orange County, California|url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/phoebe_lucille_bridgers_born_1994_22745290|access-date=October 14, 2020|website=[[California Birth Index]]}}</ref>) is an American singer-songwriter<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2021/01/16/957101075/not-my-job-we-quiz-singer-songwriter-phoebe-bridgers-on-feeble-bridges|title=Not My Job: We Quiz Singer Songwriter Phoebe Bridgers On Feeble Bridges|date=January 16, 2021|website=[[NPR]]|access-date=November 19, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2020-06-18/phoebe-bridgers-punisher|title=Phoebe Bridgers is a singer-songwriter for the ages. Especially this one|first=Mikael|last=Wood|date=June 18, 2020|website=[[Los Angeles Times]]|access-date=November 19, 2021|url-access=limited}}</ref> from Los Angeles. She made her solo debut with the studio album ''[[Stranger in the Alps]]'' (2017), followed by ''[[Punisher (album)|Punisher]]'' (2020), which earned Bridgers widespread critical acclaim and four [[Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] nominations, including [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]].

Bridgers is also a member of the musical groups [[boygenius]] (with [[Julien Baker]] and [[Lucy Dacus]]) and [[Better Oblivion Community Center]] (with [[Conor Oberst]]).

== Career ==

In January 2017, Bridgers released the single "Smoke Signals" and opened for [[Conor Oberst]] on his European tour.<ref name="BrooklynVegan">{{cite web |last1=Sacher |first1=Andrew |title=Phoebe Bridgers opening Joy Formidable acoustic shows, touring with Ryan Adams |url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/phoebe-bridgers-opening-joy-formidable-acoustic-shows-touring-with-ryan-adams/ |website=Brooklyn Vegan}}</ref> The two had met the previous summer at a secret showcase organized by Oberst at the Bootleg Theater in LA; he and his [[Bright Eyes (band)|Bright Eyes]] bandmate [[Mike Mogis]] contributed vocals and production to ''Stranger in the Alps''.<ref name="TheFaderPhoebe">{{cite web |last1=Barber-Way |first1=Mish |title=Cover Story: Phoebe Bridgers |url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/09/06/phoebe-bridgers-cover-story-interview |website=The Fader |publisher=The Fader}}</ref> Bridgers joined [[The Joy Formidable]] and Ryan Adams for select dates on their respective U.S. tours before playing at [[South by Southwest]] in March 2017.<ref name="BrooklynVegan" /> In June 2017, Bridgers signed to [[Independent record label|independent label]] [[Dead Oceans]].

=== 2018–2019: Boygenius and Better Oblivion Community Center ===
[[File:Phoebe Bridgers - Haldern Pop Festival 2018-5.jpg|thumb|170px|Bridgers performing onstage at the [[Haldern Pop Festival]] in 2018]]
In 2018, Bridgers teamed up with fellow [[Independent music|indie]] singer-songwriters [[Julien Baker]] and [[Lucy Dacus]] to form the group [[Boygenius]], signed to [[Matador Records]].<ref name="BGWMag">{{cite web |last1=McCarthy |first1=Lauren |title=A Conversation with Boygenius Gets Weird |url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/boygenius-tour-interview/?mbid=social_twitter |website=W Mag |publisher=W Magazine}}</ref> They released three songs in August 2018 and subsequently announced an [[boygenius (EP)|eponymous EP]], which was released on October 26, 2018, to widespread acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8471478/boygenius-announces-ep-tour-new-songs|title=Julian Baker, Lucy Dacus, Phoebe Bridgers Form Boygenius, Release Three Singles From EP: Listen |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=February 22, 2019}}</ref><ref name="BGMetacritic">{{cite web |title=Boygenius [EP] by boygenius Reviews and Tracks |url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/boygenius-ep/boygenius |website=Metacritic |publisher=Metacritic}}</ref> ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' called the collaboration "magic".<ref name="BGPitchfork">{{cite web |last1=Evans |first1=Dayna |title=boygenius EP Album Review |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/boygenius-boygenius-ep/ |website=Pitchfork |publisher=Conde Nast}}</ref> The band toured the U.S. in November, appearing on ''[[Late Night with Seth Meyers]]'' and the NPR Tiny Desk.<ref name="BGTinyDesk">{{cite web |last1=Boilen |first1=Bob |title=Boygenius: Tiny Desk Concert : NPR |url=https://www.npr.org/2018/11/15/668198418/boygenius-tiny-desk-concert |website=NPR.org |publisher=NPR}}</ref>

On December 5, 2018, Bridgers released a [[Spotify Singles]] session recorded at Spotify Studios NYC featuring a performance of "Scott Street" and a cover of [[The Cure]]'s "[[Friday I'm in Love]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Revisit Phoebe Bridgers' cover of The Cure song 'Friday I'm In Love' |url=https://faroutmagazine.co.uk/phoebe-bridgers-cover-the-cure-friday-im-in-love/|website=[[Far Out Magazine]] |access-date=April 3, 2021 |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/music/phoebe-bridgers/phoebe-bridgers-covers-the-cure-with-new-spotify-s/|title=Phoebe Bridgers Covers The Cure in New Spotify Singles|website=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |last=Kamp|first=Justin |date=December 5, 2018 |access-date=May 14, 2021|language=en}}</ref>

Bridgers and Conor Oberst announced the formation of their band, [[Better Oblivion Community Center]], on ''[[The Late Show with Stephen Colbert]]'' in January 2019. They released their [[Better Oblivion Community Center (album)|debut album]] later that month through [[Dead Oceans]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicnewsnet.com/2019/01/better-oblivion-community-center-phoebe-bridgers-conor-oberst-surprise-drop-long-rumored-debut-album.html|title=BETTER OBLIVION COMMUNITY CENTER: Phoebe Bridgers & Conor Oberst Surprise Drop Long Rumored Debut #Album|website=Music News Net |access-date=February 22, 2019}}</ref> The band appeared on ''[[CBS This Morning]],'' and Bridgers appeared for the third time on NPR's Tiny Desk series.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kaplan |first=Ilana |date=January 26, 2019 |title=See Conor Oberst, Phoebe Bridgers Perform 'Better Oblivion Community Center' Songs on 'CBS This Morning' |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/conor-oberst-phoebe-bridgers-better-oblivion-community-center-cbs-this-morning-784984/ |access-date=June 22, 2020 |website=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref> [[Bob Boilen]], creator of the Tiny Desk Concerts, said of their album, "It's that rare musical partnership where each injects vibrancy into the other's creative side."<ref>{{cite web|author=Bob Boilen |url=https://www.npr.org/2019/04/19/715201380/better-oblivion-community-center-tiny-desk-concert |title=Video: Watch Better Oblivion Community Center Perform At The Tiny Desk |publisher=NPR |access-date=April 1, 2020}}</ref>

=== 2020–present: ''Punisher'' ===
On February 26, Bridgers released the single "[[Garden Song (Phoebe Bridgers song)|Garden Song]]" alongside its music video.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Touros |first1=Cyrena |title=Hear Phoebe Bridgers' First Solo Release In Almost Three Years, 'Garden Song' |url=https://www.npr.org/2020/02/26/809389222/hear-phoebe-bridgers-first-solo-release-in-almost-three-years-garden-song |publisher=[[NPR]] |access-date=February 26, 2020}}</ref> In April, [[the 1975]] released the song "[[Jesus Christ 2005 God Bless America]]" with Bridgers, ahead of their studio album ''[[Notes on a Conditional Form]]'', which features Bridgers on three tracks. She was slated to tour with them in summer 2020 before the tour's cancellation due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="1975MusicWeek">{{cite web |last1=Homewood |first1=Ben |title=Matthew Healy on how The 1975 wrote Notes On A Conditional Form |url=https://www.musicweek.com/talent/read/matthew-healy-on-how-the-1975-wrote-notes-on-a-conditional-form/079615 |website=Music Week |publisher=Music Week}}</ref><ref name="1975NME">{{cite web |last=Clarke |first=Patrick |title=The 1975 announce massive run of North American shows with Phoebe Bridgers and Beabadoobee |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/the-1975-announce-massive-run-of-north-american-shows-with-phoebe-bridgers-and-beabadoobee-2597730 |website=[[NME]] |date=January 16, 2020 |access-date=August 14, 2021}}</ref> On April 9, 2020, Bridgers released "[[Kyoto (Phoebe Bridgers song)|Kyoto]]"<ref>{{Cite web|last=Strauss|first=Matthew |title=Phoebe Bridgers Announces New Album Punisher, Shares New Song "Kyoto" |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/phoebe-bridgers-announces-new-album-punisher-shares-new-song-kyoto-listen/|access-date=June 22, 2020 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] }}</ref> and announced on [[Instagram]] that her second album ''[[Punisher (album)|Punisher]]'' would be released on June 19, 2020. Bridgers released the album June 18, stating, "I'm not [delaying] the record until things go back to 'normal' because I don't think they should. Here it is a little early."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Phoebe Bridgers On Her New Album 'Punisher,' Surprise Released A Day Early|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/06/18/879873148/phoebe-bridgers-on-her-new-album-punisher-surprise-released-a-day-early |date=June 18, 2020 |access-date=June 19, 2020 |publisher=NPR}}</ref> The album received widespread positive reviews.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Daly|first=Rhian |date=June 19, 2020 |title=Phoebe Bridgers – 'Punisher' review |url=https://thefortyfive.com/opinion/reviews/phoebe-bridgers-punisher-review/ |access-date=June 25, 2020 |website=The Forty Five |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Sodomsky |first=Sam |date=June 22, 2020 |title=Phoebe Bridgers – 'Punisher'|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/phoebe-bridgers-punisher/|access-date=August 5, 2020 |website=Pitchfork}}</ref> In July 2020, Bridgers released the music video for her single "I Know the End".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Taysom|first=Joe|title=Phoebe Bridgers shares visuals for 'I Know The End'|url=https://faroutmagazine.co.uk/phoebe-bridgers-video-i-know-the-end/ |date=July 29, 2020 |access-date=February 16, 2021 |website=[[Far Out Magazine]] |language=en-US}}</ref> While working on ''Punisher'', Bridgers also produced [[Christian Lee Hutson]]'s album ''Beginners'', which was released on [[Anti- Records]] in May 2020.<ref name="VarietyPhoebe" /> That September, she played to an all-virtual audience of 4 million at [[Red Rocks Amphitheatre]] as part of their Unpaused Concert Series. In October, Bridgers announced the formation of her own label, Saddest Factory, an [[Imprint label|imprint]] of Dead Oceans.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cirisano|first=Tatiana|date=October 5, 2020 |access-date=August 14, 2021 |title=Phoebe Bridgers Launches Saddest Factory Label with Dead Oceans: Exclusive |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/9457162/phoebe-bridgers-saddest-factory-label-dead-oceans |work=Billboard}}</ref>

While awaiting the results of the [[2020 United States presidential election]] on November 3, Bridgers tweeted that she would cover "[[Iris (song)|Iris]]" by [[Goo Goo Dolls]] if then-president [[Donald Trump]] lost. The cover, which was recorded as a duet with [[Maggie Rogers]] under the name Phoebe & Maggie, was released exclusively on Bridgers' [[Bandcamp]] page for one day only on November 13. The song received 28,000 downloads with proceeds going to [[Stacey Abrams]]' [[Fair Fight Action]] organization to promote fair elections both in the state of [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]] and nationwide.<ref name=":6">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/phoebe-bridgers-and-maggie-rogers-team-up-for-goo-goo-dolls-iris-cover-2815883 |title=Phoebe Bridgers and Maggie Rogers team up for Goo Goo Dolls 'Iris' cover |first=Eddy |last=Lim |work=NME |date=November 13, 2020 |access-date=November 13, 2020}}</ref><ref name=":7">{{cite web |last1=Unterberger |first1=Andrew |title=Phoebe Bridgers & Maggie Rogers' 'Iris' Cover Sells 28,000 in Paid Downloads in One Day of Availability |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9484399/phoebe-bridgers-maggie-rogers-iris-cover-sales-week |work=Billboard |access-date=November 16, 2020 |date=November 14, 2020}}</ref> On November 10, Bridgers announced an EP of four reworked tracks from ''Punisher,'' entitled ''[[Copycat Killer]]'', in collaboration with [[Rob Moose]]. ''Copycat Killer'' was released digitally on November 20, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Phoebe Bridgers shares stirring version of 'Kyoto' from upcoming 'Copycat Killer' EP |url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/news/music/phoebe-bridgers-shares-stirring-version-of-kyoto-from-upcoming-copycat-killer-ep-2813912 |date=November 10, 2020 |first=Tom |last=Skinner |access-date=November 16, 2020 |website=NME}}</ref> On November 23, Bridgers released a cover of [[Merle Haggard|Merle Haggard's]] "[[If We Make It Through December]]", with proceeds going to [[Los Angeles]]' Downtown Women's Center.''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Strauss |first=Matthew |title=Phoebe Bridgers Covers Merle Haggard's "If We Make It Through December" |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/phoebe-bridgers-covers-merle-haggard-if-we-make-it-through-december-listen/ |access-date=November 24, 2020 |website=Pitchfork}}</ref>''

== Artistry ==
Bridgers' music has been categorized as [[indie rock]], [[indie folk]], and [[Emo|emo folk]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Linker|first=Damon|title=Phoebe Bridgers' Punisher was the best album of 2020|url=https://theweek.com/articles/956306/phoebe-bridgers-punisher-best-album-2020|work=[[The Week]]|date=December 22, 2020|access-date=September 12, 2021}}</ref> It often centers acoustic guitar, and incorporates atmospheric strings, production, and electronic instrumentation. Her music has been described as "anxious," "melancholy," and "haunting." Themes include death, trauma, therapy, depression, and strained relationships, "undercut by her dry wit" and "straightforward delivery."<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|date=June 18, 2020|title=The Story Behind Every Song On Phoebe Bridgers' New Album 'Punisher'|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2086915/phoebe-bridgers-interview-punisher-conor-oberst-1975-boygenius/interviews/footnotes-interview/ |access-date=January 12, 2021 |website=[[Stereogum]]}}</ref><ref name=":5">{{cite web |last=Petrusich |first=Amanda |title=Phoebe Bridgers's Frank, Anxious Music|url=https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2020/05/25/phoebe-bridgers-frank-anxious-music|website=[[The New Yorker]]|access-date=October 24, 2020|date=May 17, 2020}}</ref><ref name=":12">{{Citation|title=Punisher - Phoebe Bridgers {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits {{!}} AllMusic|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/punisher-mw0003375607|access-date=January 18, 2021}}</ref> Several of Bridgers' songs are about personal issues; "[[Kyoto (Phoebe Bridgers song)|Kyoto]]", for example, was inspired by her relationship with her father, while "[[Motion Sickness (Phoebe Bridgers song)|Motion Sickness]]" and "ICU" are about past breakups.<ref name=":9" /><ref name=":8" /> Her complex storytelling and use of evocative [[imagery]], such as in "[[Garden Song (Phoebe Bridgers song)|Garden Song]]," have also been commended.<ref>{{Citation|title=Listen to "Garden Song" by Phoebe Bridgers|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/phoebe-bridgers-garden-song/|language=en|access-date=January 12, 2021}}</ref><ref name=":10" /><ref name=":12" />

Bridgers has cited [[Elliott Smith]] as one of her favorite artists and the biggest influence on her songwriting and production style.<ref name=":5" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=It 'Feels Like A Graduation': Phoebe Bridgers On 'Punisher'|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/06/18/879873148/phoebe-bridgers-on-her-new-album-punisher-surprise-released-a-day-early|access-date=January 12, 2021|publisher=NPR |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Zoladz|first=Lindsay |date=June 19, 2020| title=Phoebe Bridgers Wants to Believe|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/06/19/arts/music/phoebe-bridgers-punisher-review.html|access-date=January 18, 2021|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> Her song "Punisher" explores her meeting Smith if he were still alive.<ref name=":5" /> Other musical influences include [[bluegrass music]], [[Bright Eyes (band)|Bright Eyes]], [[The Replacements (band)|The Replacements]], [[Blake Mills]], [[Tom Waits]], [[Avril Lavigne]], [[The Beatles]], Jackson Browne, and [[Nine Inch Nails]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Brodsky |first1=Rachel |title=Album reviews: Pale Waves – Who am I? and Django Django – |quote=Today, a new crop of indie performers cite Lavigne – and her brassy brand of MTV-friendly pop suffused with snarling rage against the machine – as a major influence: Soccer Mommy, Snail Mail, |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/lifestyle/8094955/listen-phoebe-bridgers-tbt-mixtape-playlist |access-date=September 5, 2020 |work=Billboard |quote=And I probably wouldn't have made it to Her music often features a wide variety of popular culture references<ref>{{Cite web|title=Phoebe Bridgers’s rock songs are casually sad as [[ASMR]] videos, television series ''[[Fleabag]],'' and true crime podcast ''[[My Favorite Murder]]'' were all influential in the making of [[Punisher (Phoebe Bridgers album)|''Punisher'']].<ref In 2021, she received the [[Libera Awards]] as both ''Best Live/Livestream Act'' and ''Record of the Year'' 2021 for her album ''Punisher'' (Dead Oceans) by the [[American Association of Independent Music]] (A2IM).<ref>{{Cite web |title=A2IM Libera Awards 2021 winners |url=https://liberaawards.com/2021-winners/ |access-date=2021-09-21 |website=liberaawards.com}}</ref>

Both of Bridgers' albums contain [[Halloween]]-themed visual motifs, with the album covers of ''[[Stranger in the Alps]]'' and ''[[Punisher (album)|Punisher]]'' containing ghost and skeleton imagery respectively.  She is known for wearing variations of a skeleton [[Onesie (jumpsuit)|onesie]], inspired by her reported love for "creepy and corny stuff."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Aswad|first=Jem|date=Aug 5, 2020|title=Phoebe Bridgers Talks Music, Punishers, Burger Records, ‘Fansplaining,’ and Stardom at Home|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/phoebe-bridgers-interview-punisher-burger-records-virtual-concerts-1234723402/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-09|website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
[[File:Phoebe Bridgers (42502909805).jpg|thumb|300px|Bridgers and Vore performing onstage at [[The Crocodile]] in Seattle in 2018]]
Bridgers is [[Bisexuality|bisexual]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wiggins |first1=Graeme |title=Phoebe Bridgers Embraces Her Inner Sext Machine {{!}} BeatRoute Magazine |url=http://beatroute.ca/2018/04/17/phoebe-bridgers-embraces-inner-sext-machine/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190327101817/http://beatroute.ca/2018/04/17/phoebe-bridgers-embraces-inner-sext-machine/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=March 27, 2019 |access-date=November 4, 2019 |date=March 27, 2019}}</ref> Bridgers dated [[Ryan Adams]] beginning in 2014, before eventually breaking up; her song "Motion Sickness" is about their relationship.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news|last=Jordan|first=Jerilyn|title=The fun and fractured world of Phoebe Bridgers|url=https://www.metrotimes.com/detroit/the-fun-and-fractured-world-of-phoebe-bridgers/Content?oid=9695821|access-date=October 7, 2020|newspaper=Detroit Metro Times|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Armstrong|first=Chuck|title=Phoebe Bridgers on Working With Ryan Adams|url=https://diffuser.fm/phoebe-bridgers-on-working-with-ryan-adams-who-compares-her-to-bob-dylan/|date=May 1, 2015|access-date=October 7, 2020|website=Diffuser.fm}}</ref> Along with several other women, Bridgers accused Adams of emotional abuse in a 2019 report by ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref name=":9">{{cite news |last1=Coscarelli |first1=Joe |last2=Ryzik |first2=Melena |title=Ryan Adams Dangled Success. Women Say They Paid a Price. |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/02/13/arts/music/ryan-adams-women-sex.html |access-date=October 8, 2020 |work=The New York Times |date=February 13, 2019}}</ref> Bridgers was in a relationship with touring drummer and musical collaborator Marshall Vore until around 2017. Bridgers and Vore co-wrote her single "ICU" about their relationship.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Martoccio |first1=Angie |title=Phoebe Bridgers Drops Stellar New Song, ‘I See You,’ Announces Virtual World Tour |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/phoebe-bridgers-i-see-you-world-tour-1002471/ |website=Rolling Stone |access-date=February 19, 2021}}</ref> Fans believe songs "Scott Street" and "Smoke Signals" are also about Vore. The two remain friends and collaborators.<ref name=":8" /> Since June 2021, Bridgers has been in a relationship with [[Irish people|Irish]] actor [[Paul Mescal]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2021-11-10|title=I Want What Noted Reply Guys Phoebe Bridgers and Paul Mescal Have|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/i-want-what-they-have-phoebe-bridgers-and-paul-mescal|access-date=2021-11-13|website=Vogue|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|date=2021-11-07|title=Paul Mescal and Phoebe Bridgers make their red carpet debut|url=https://www.rte.ie/lifestyle/fashion/2021/1107/1258413-paul-mescal-and-phoebe-bridgers-walk-the-red-carpet-together/|language=en}}</ref>

Bridgers has spoken about her struggles with [[major depressive disorder]] and [[anxiety]], for which she has sought [[Psychotherapy|therapy]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Phoebe Bridgers on Grammy noms, working with Phoebe Waller-Bridge and moving on from 'Punisher' |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rxqCnPpxGnI |publisher=The Forty-Five |access-date=17 December 2020}}</ref><ref name=":11" />

=== Politics ===
Bridgers, [[Fiona Apple]], and [[Matt Berninger]] released a cover of [[Simon and Garfunkel]]'s 1966 song "[[7 O'Clock News/Silent Night]]" updated to reflect events in 2019, including the [[murder of Botham Jean]], the [[opioid epidemic in the United States]] and the testimony of [[Mick Mulvaney]] in [[President of the United States|President]] [[Donald Trump]]'s [[First impeachment trial of Donald Trump|first impeachment trial]]. Bridgers released this song with a statement, "Happy Holidays to everyone whose family has been literally or figuratively torn apart by Donald Trump. And to my racist, xenophobic, misogynistic, hypocritical family members, fuck you."<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Shaffer|first1=Claire|title=Phoebe Bridgers Covers '7 O'Clock News/Silent Night' With Fiona Apple, Matt Berninger|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/phoebe-bridgers-7-oclock-news-silent-night-fiona-apple-matt-berninger-925711/|access-date=14 January 2021|magazine=Rolling Stone}}</ref> In 2020, she expressed support for and encouraged donations to [[racial justice]] charities via her website and called for the [[police abolition movement|abolition of police]] during ''Punisher's'' release amid the [[George Floyd protests]]<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Shaffer|first=Claire|date=June 18, 2020|title=Phoebe Bridgers Drops Album Early, Encourages Donations for Racial Justice|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/phoebe-bridgers-punisher-early-release-1017121/|access-date=January 12, 2021|magazine=Rolling Stone}}</ref> and released her and [[Maggie Rogers]]' cover of "[[Iris (Goo Goo Dolls song)|Iris]]" as a single specifically to raise money for [[Stacey Abrams]]' [[Fair Fight Action]], having vowed to release the cover if Trump lost the [[2020 United States presidential election|2020 presidential election]].<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":7" /> In October 2020, Bridgers performed as part of the virtual fundraiser festival "Village of Love", benefiting [[Planned Parenthood]] in Los Angeles and New York.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Minsker|first=Evan |date=October 26, 2020 |title=Angel Olsen, Mac DeMarco, Phoebe Bridgers, More Perform at Planned Parenthood Benefit Concert |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/watch-angel-olsen-mac-demarco-phoebe-bridgers-more-on-planned-parenthood-benefit-concert/ |access-date=February 20, 2021 |website=Pitchfork}}</ref>
