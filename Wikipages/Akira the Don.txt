'''Akira the Don''' (real name '''Adam Narkiewicz''') is a British musician, DJ, and producer.

His music and production traverses the boundaries between pop, hip-hop, indie and dance. He lists influences including [[Ice Cube]], [[Adam Ant]], [[Morrissey]], [[Big Bang]], [[Leonard Cohen]], [[David Bowie]] and the [[Wu-Tang Clan]]. His debut album ''When We Were Young'' was produced in collaboration with [[Danny Saber]] ([[U2]], [[Black Grape]]), James Brown ([[Nine Inch Nails]], Ash) and Emile ([[50 Cent]]). In 2017, Akira the Don released mixtapes in the form of a new genre he created, referred to as "Meaningwave"<ref>{{cite news|url=https://medium.com/@josephparrish/the-rise-of-meaningwave-music-794139f2d3fe|publisher=Joseph Parrish|title=The Rise of Meaningwave Music|newspaper=Medium|date=11 May 2019|accessdate=5 June 2020|last1=Parrish|first1=Joseph}}</ref> – a fusion of [[vaporwave|wave]] music with meaningful lyrical content and [[lo-fi]] [[hip-hop]].

==Life and career==
Born in West Bromwich and raised in North Wales, for most of his career he lived in London.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/wales/music/sites/akira-the-don/|publisher=BBC Wales|title=Akira the Don|accessdate=23 February 2019}}</ref> In 2014, he moved to Los Angeles. In 2020, he moved to Texas in order "to secure the future of Meaningwave, his son's childhood, and the very Don Dynasty itself."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.meaningwave.com/blogs/meaningwave/texodus|publisher=Meaningwave|title=TEXODUS|accessdate=13 July 2020}}</ref>

===Rapper===

Prior to launching a solo career, Akira the Don was a member of hip-hop group Crack Village.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/wales/music/sites/akira-the-don/pages/biography.shtml|title=BBC Wales - Music - Akira the Don - Biography}}</ref> In 2006, Akira rapped on [[Brave Captain]]'s album ''Distractions''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/13265-ye-gods-and-little-fishes/|publisher=Pitchfork|title=Martin Carr - Ye Gods (And Little Fishes)|accessdate=23 February 2019}}</ref> He released his first album ''When We Were Young'' the same year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.oxfordstudent.com/2010/11/04/interview-akira-the-don/|publisher=Oxford Student|title=Interview: Akira The Don|date=3 November 2010|accessdate=3 June 2020}}</ref>

In May 2011, he released his second LP, ''The Life Equation''. The album is co-produced with [[Stephen Hague]] and features collaborations with [[Gruff Rhys]] (of [[Super Furry Animals]]) and [[Envy (English rapper)|Envy]].<ref name="TLE">{{cite web|url=https://thequietus.com/articles/08034-akira-the-don-the-life-equation-review|title=Akira the Don - The Life Equation review|accessdate=23 February 2019|publisher=The Quietus}}</ref> Akira often produced his own videos.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/1780601-1780601|publisher=NME|accessdate=23 February 2019|title=Watch Akira The Don's Halloween Ready Video For 'Spooky Scary Skeletons'|date=26 October 2016}}</ref> In 2013, Akira performed at [[Grant Morrison]]'s MorrisonCon.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bleedingcool.com/2012/10/03/akira-the-dons-second-day-at-morrisoncon/|title=Akira The Don's Second Day At MorrisonCon|date=3 October 2012|publisher=Bleeding Cool}}</ref>

In March 2014, Akira released ''A.T.D.R.I.P.''. He played his final gig in London on 19 May,.<ref name="akirathedon.com">{{cite web |url=http://akirathedon.com/blog/akirathedonlately/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=4 May 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140504071439/http://akirathedon.com/blog/akirathedonlately/ |archivedate=4 May 2014  }}</ref>

===Producer===
Akira has produced songs for a number of artists including two top forty hits for [[Lethal Bizzle]]<ref name="guardreview">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/musicblog/2011/feb/22/akira-don-chilly-gonzales-weatherman|work=The Guardian|title=New music: Akira the Don and Chilly Gonzales – Don't Need a Weatherman|date=22 February 2011|accessdate=23 February 2018}}</ref> along with songs for [[Newham Generals]]' Footsie, [[Big Narstie]], [[Envy (English rapper)|Envy]], G-Mane, Littles, Marvin The Martian, [[Issues (band)|Issue]], and Time.

In 2011, Akira collaborated with [[Chilly Gonzales]] on mixtape.<ref name="guardreview" />

In 2015, Akira produced a cover of [[Adamski]]'s "[[Killer (Adamski song)|Killer]]" featuring [[Grant Morrison]].
<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bleedingcool.com/2015/06/02/grant-morrison-appears-in-akira-the-dons-new-music-video-for-killer/|title=Grant Morrison Appears In Akira The Don's New Music Video For Killer|date=2 June 2015|publisher=Bleeding Cool|accessdate=23 February 2019}}</ref>

Since 2017, Akira the Don has been integrating philosophically inspired lyrical content into his music. He created and developed the Meaningwave Universe which is aimed at inspiring people to find wholesomeness in their lives, and promotes the ideas of peak performance or [[Flow (psychology)|flow]] consciousness.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.prosoundweb.com/evolution-of-the-dj-an-interview-with-akira-the-don/|publisher=prosoundweb.com|title=Evolution Of The DJ: An Interview With Akira The Don|date=29 July 2019|accessdate=5 June 2020}}</ref> He's sampled soundbites from [[Jordan Peterson]], [[Alan Watts]], [[Joe Rogan]], [[Elon Musk]], [[Fred Rogers]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cltampa.com/music/new-music/article/21009625/akira-the-dons-mr-rogersthemed-vaporwave-mixtape-is-exactly-what-the-world-needs-right-now|publisher=CL Tampa|title=Akira the Don's Mr. Rogers-themed vaporwave mixtape is exactly what the world needs right now |accessdate=23 February 2019 }}</ref> [[Anthony Bourdain]], and others.<ref>Byebstaff (9 December 2016). "The Top 10 Most Important Music Memes of 2016". Electronic Beats. Archived from the original on 1 April 2017. Both vaporwave and its offshoot hardvapor are genres as well as memes</ref> In 2019, he released dozens of albums during a period of "hyper productivity" <ref>{{cite web|url=https://highexistence.com/akira-the-don-interview/|publisher=High Existence|title=Akira the Don: The DJ of the Intellectual Dark Web|date=26 April 2019|accessdate=3 June 2020}}</ref>

===DJ===
In October 2014 Akira relocated to Los Angeles, where he formed the party rap duo MIDNITEMEN with long time collaborator and nightclub organiser Wade Crescent, and began DJing every week in Hollywood nightspots like [[The Roosevelt Hotel]], Blind Dragon, Hooray Henrys and Bootsy Bellows. The latter is co-owned by [[David Arquette]], for whom Akira provided accent coaching for the actor's role in a stage production of Sherlock Holmes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.torontosun.com/2015/10/25/david-arquette-bringing-a-zany-sherlock-holmes-to-the-stage|title=David Arquette bringing a zany Sherlock Holmes to the stage|publisher=Toronto Sun}}</ref>

As resident DJ at Blind Dragon, he DJed parties for the likes of [[The Weeknd]], [[Nylon Magazine]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://getfirstlook.com/photo/w5CdES |title=Archived copy |accessdate=20 July 2016 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160816130935/http://getfirstlook.com/photo/w5CdES |archivedate=16 August 2016  }}</ref> and [[Harry Styles]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sugarscape.com/lads/news/a1074764/harry-styles-birthday-party-dj-politest-millionaire-ever/|publisher=Sugarscape.com|title=Harry Styles birthday party DJ politest millionaire ever}}</ref> He DJed for [[Justin Bieber]] at Hooray Henrys following his VMA's performance.

In 2020, Akira started to live-stream daily DJs sets on his [[YouTube]] channel, at times doing two sets per day.
