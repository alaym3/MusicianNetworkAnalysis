'''Senzo Brikka Vilakazi''' (born 11 August 1988) known professionally as '''Kwesta''', is a [[South African people|South African]] rapper and songwriter.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Correspondent|first=Local|date=2020-09-01|title=VIDEO: South African rapper, Kwesta unveils new project|url=https://ab-tc.com/video-south-african-rapper-kwesta-unveils-new-project/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=ABTC|language=en-GB}}</ref> 
Born in [[Katlehong]], [[Gauteng]], a township also known as "K1".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kwach|first=Julie|date=2018-11-15|title=Kwesta's biography: origin, career, albums, family|url=https://briefly.co.za/19398-kwesta-biography-age-hometown-family.html|access-date=2020-11-09|website=Briefly|language=en}}</ref> In 2003, at the age of 15, he and his friends formed a rap group '''Ghetto Fellaz''' which then changed to '''The Juvenylz''', in 2003.
He is currently signed to his own independent media company known as RapLyf Records with co-founder [[Kid X (rapper)|Kid X]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kwesta on his team: If I ran Raplyf, I'd run it to the ground|url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/tshisa-live/tshisa-live/2018-09-03-kwesta-on-his-team--if-i-ran-raplyf-id-run-it-to-the-ground/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=TimesLIVE|language=en-ZA}}</ref>

He released his debut studio album ''Special ReKwest'' (2010) and his second album ''DaKAR'' (2013), which peaked number 3 on the [[iTunes]] South African Hip-hop/Rap chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://zalebs.com/top-of-the/kwesta/kwesta-s-rise-to-fame|title=Kwesta's Rise To Fame|website=ZAlebs}}</ref>

Kwesta's third studio album, ''DaKAR II'' (2016),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/amp/tshisa-live/tshisa-live/2019-01-21-watch--kwestas-in-studio-cooking-up-dakar-iii--it-sounds-like-fire/|title=WATCH &#124; Kwesta's in studio cooking up Dakar III & it sounds like fire|website=TimesLIVE}}</ref> spawned with singles includes; "Ngud'," "Nomayini", "Day Ones", "Ngiyazifela Ngawe," "Mayibabo" and "Mmino". The album  was certified 7x Platinum by the [[Recording Industry of South Africa]] (RISA).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://briefly.co.za/44677-kwesta-celebrates-milestone-dakar-ii-sas-selling-album.html|title=Kwesta celebrates new milestone: Dakar II is SA's best-selling album|first=Deshni|last=Pillay|date=29 November 2019|website=briefly.co.za}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/kwesta-dakar11-7x-platinum/|title=Kwesta's 'DaKAR II' Album Has Gone 7x Platinum|website=okayafricasite}}</ref>
Having sold 210 000 copies in South Africa, DaKAR II became [[Bestseller (music)|best selling]] hip hop albums of all-time.<ref>https://sahiphopmag.co.za/2019/11/kwesta-becomes-sa-hip-hops-best-selling-album-reaching-new-milestone/amp/</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtvbase.com/news/Kwestas-Da-KAR-II-is-now-the-best-selling-SA-hip-hop-album-of-all-time/kstrys|title=Kwesta's Da KAR II is now the best selling SA hip hop album of all time &#124; MTV Africa|website=www.mtvbase.com}}</ref>

The single "Spirit" was released in 2017, featuring the American rapper Wale, and was certified platinum by RISA.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/amp/entertainment/2017-10-12-kwesta-drops-hit-single-spirit-in-collaboration-with-us-rapper-wale/|title=Kwesta drops hit single Spirit in collaboration with US rapper Wale|website=SowetanLIVE}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.capetalk.co.za/articles/279317/kwesta-is-in-high-spirits-for-his-new-track|title=Kwesta is in high Spirits for his new track|website=CapeTalk}}</ref> 

His fifth studio album ''g.o.d Guluva'' (2021), was released.

His most reputable [[accolades]] include 6 [[South African Music Awards]], 4 South African Hip Hop Awards, 1 MTV Africa Music Awards, and 1 Metro FM Awards.

==Life and career==

Kwesta attended [https://schooldirect.org/south-africa/gauteng/phumlani-secondary-school-2020-2021/ Phumlani Secondary School] in [[Katlehong]] for three years, and later [https://schooldirect.org/south-africa/gauteng/alafang-secondary-school-2020-2021/ Alafang Secondary School].

He developed an interest in [[poetry]] during his high school years and received positive feedback and encouragement from fellow students and teachers.

In 2003, Kwesta became a part of the group The Ghetto Fellas later known as The Juvenylz.<ref>{{Cite web|title=South African Band bookings – KWESTA|url=http://www.bands.co.za/kwesta_bands.htm|access-date=2020-11-06|website=www.bands.co.za}}</ref> The group disbanded after two years.

After the group's disbandment, Kwesta made an agreement with Africa's Most Southern Record Company for the use of their studios. During this time, Kwesta participated in competitions and events to provide visibility for his brand. He took part in YFM's Rap Activity Jam MC of the Month competition.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hi my name is Hip Hop: LOCAL RAPPER OF THE MONTH|url=http://himynameishiphop.blogspot.com/2010/10/local-rapper-of-month.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Hi my name is Hip Hop}}</ref> He topped ETV's Shiz Niz Freestyle Kings Special.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|title=Music - Kwesta aka Mr Cool {{!}} Presenter|url=https://www.expressoshow.com/articles/Music---Kwesta-aka-Mr-Cool?articleID=1803|access-date=2020-11-06|website=www.expressoshow.com}}</ref> Also, he performed at [https://randshow.co.za/ The Rand Show], [https://www.facebook.com/MissConfidenceSA/ Miss Confidence Show] and [https://www.facebook.com/pages/category/Performance---Event-Venue/Durban-Summer-Beach-Festival-20192020-1669817296636371/ The Durban Beach Festival].

The rapper dropped out of high school at the age of 16.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kwesta dropped out of school to follow music but wants to go back|url=https://www.timeslive.co.za/tshisa-live/tshisa-live/2018-10-18-kwesta-dropped-out-of-school-to-follow-music-but-wants-to-go-back/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=TimesLIVE|language=en-ZA}}</ref> After which he convinced his mother to give him the taxi fare necessary to get to [http://www.buttabing.co.za/ Buttabing Entertainment]'s offices. The meeting resulted in him being added to the record label's roster marking the beginning of his signed career.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kwesta leaves Buttabing Entertainment {{!}} MTV Africa|url=http://www.mtvbase.com/news/kwesta-leaves-buttabing-entertainment/tbslov|access-date=2020-11-06|website=www.mtvbase.com}}</ref>

===Career beginnings===
In 2007, Kwesta earned a judging gig on the [https://www.sowetanlive.co.za/news/2007-06-15-hip-hoop-tour-in-soweto/ Sprite Hip Hoop Tour] and a feature on [https://www.reverbnation.com/page_object/page_object_bio/artist_676066 The Ventilation Street Tape].<ref name="auto1"/> He was also part of The Nokia Defend Your Street Campaign, where he worked with other artists to create the theme song.<ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|title=Kwesta {{!}} HDS|url=https://hdsentertainment.co.za/kwesta/|access-date=2020-11-06|language=en-ZA}}</ref>

In the same year, Kwesta released his inaugural single ''[https://www.slikouronlife.co.za/song/18562/sharp-fede Sharp Fede].''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sharp Fede|url=https://hypem.com/track/2n6vz/Kwesta+-+Sharp+Fede|access-date=2020-11-06|website=Hype Machine|language=en}}</ref> He later released a mixtape in collaboration with [http://djc-live.blogspot.com/ DJ C-Live].

In 2010, Kwesta was among the few chosen as Brand SA Ambassadors. He performed during the national roadshows held before the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]] hosted in South Africa.<ref name="auto2"/> Kwesta worked alongside [[Kelly Rowland]], Jozi, and [[2face Idibia|2Face]] amongst other artist on the [[Everywhere You Go (World Cup song)|Everywhere You Go]] anthem for [[MTN Group|MTN]]. He also performed during the [[2010 FIFA World Cup]] closing ceremony.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kwesta - MTN Bushfire Festival 2020 : MTN Bushfire Festival 2020|url=https://www.bush-fire.com/bands/kwesta/|access-date=2020-11-06|website=www.bush-fire.com}}</ref>

===2010{{ndash}}2012: ''Special Rekwest'' ===
In September 2010, Kwesta released debut studio album titled ''[https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpncPM_hfbkd3heJ9ziS2faLIYvbK9Qu9 Special Rekwest]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=TheMusic.Today - Music album Special ReKwest by Kwesta|url=https://themusic.today/release/kwesta-special-rekwest/2370392|access-date=2020-11-06|website=themusic.today}}</ref> The album featured hit singles such as ''Babhemi'', ''Flash It'', ''Stomp'' and ''Pump It''.

''Flava'' received a synch-deal with [[DStv]] and got featured in the [[The Loeries|Loeries TV]]'s 2011, ad-campaign.

''Pump It'' received a synch-deal with [[DStv]] and [https://www.reddsapple.com/ Redds].

''Stomp'' was one of the soundtracks for [[Death Race 3: Inferno|Death Race 3]], an America action movie.

With the success of the album, Kwesta became South Africa's first rapper to have seven songs ranked on multiple radio charts in the same year. ''[https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpncPM_hfbkd3heJ9ziS2faLIYvbK9Qu9 Special Rekwest]'' earned [[South African Music Awards|SAMA]] nominations in 2011 for Best Newcomer and Best Rap Album.<ref>{{Cite web|title=2011 SAMA nominees announced|url=https://www.bizcommunity.com/Article/196/480/58732.html|access-date=2020-11-06|website=www.bizcommunity.com|language=en}}</ref>

===2013{{ndash}}2015: ''DaKAR'' ===
On 25 November 2013, Kwesta released his second studio album titled [[DaKAR|''DaKAR (Da King of Afrikan Rap)'']]. The album ranked third on the South African [[iTunes]] Hip-Hop Charts during its debut.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://hiphopafrican.com/2015/03/09/kwesta/|title=Kwesta:biography|last1=11|first1=tumelo on August|last2=Said|first2=2015 at 5:19 Am|website=HipHopAfrican|access-date=2016-03-23}}</ref>

=== 2016{{ndash}}2019: ''DaKAR II'' ===
In 2016, Kwesta released his third studio album titled ''[[DaKAR II]]''. On January 18, 2016,  ''[[Ngud']]'' was released featuring, [[Cassper Nyovest]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mytvnews.co.za/kwesta-drops-ngud-new-single-feat-cassper-nyovest/|title=Kwesta drops 'Ngud' new single feat. Cassper Nyovest - Mytvnews|author=|date=18 January 2016|work=Mytvnews|accessdate=1 July 2021}}</ref>

At annual   [[MTV Africa Music Awards 2016|MTV Africa Music Awards]], the single "[[Ngud']]" received a nominations for  the song of the Year.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Alyssa Klein |title=The Full List of MTV Africa Music Awards 2016 Winners |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/mtv-africa-music-awards-2016-winners-mtvmama2016/ |website=OkayAfrica|date=October 23, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author1=Alyssa Klein |title=Here's the Full List of Nominees at the 2016 MTV Africa Music Awards in Johannesburg |url=https://www.okayafrica.com/mtv-africa-music-awards-2016-nominees-mtvmama2016/ |website=OkayAfrica |date=October 22, 2016}}</ref> 

On April 26, 2018,  ''[[DaKAR II]]'' was certified 7x by the Recording Industry of South Africa (RiSA).<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-04-16|title=Kwesta's 'DaKAR II' Album Has Gone 7x Platinum|url=https://www.okayafrica.com/kwesta-dakar11-7x-platinum/|access-date=2020-11-09|website=OkayAfrica|language=en}}</ref>

===2021{{ndash}}present: ''g.o.d guluva''===
In March 2021, his single "Fire In The Ghetto" featuring Troublle was released, as album's lead single.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dakar ii hands over the batton to G.O.D Guluva - Soweto Urban |url=https://sowetourban.co.za/88026/dakar-ii-hands-over-the-batton-to-g-o-d-guluva/?pwa-amp&|date=16 April 2021|publisher=Soweto Urban |first=Mzwandile|last=Khathi}}</ref> In early April 2021, pre-order were made available.

On 30 April 2021, Kwesta released his fourth studio album, titled ''g.o.d guluva'' [acronym for "ghost of dakar"], it features South Africa artists Thabsie, Focalistic, TLT, Yanga Chief, K.O.<ref>https://www.okayafrica.com/amp/kwesta-releases-new-album-god-guluva-2652832221</ref> The album peaked No. 4 in South Africa Top 100 Albums, <ref>{{cite web|title=South Africa Top 100 Albums {{!}} Top Charts |url=https://www.top-charts.com/albums/all-genres/south-africa/total|work=Top Charts}}</ref> and received generally positive reviews from music critics.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kwesta "g.o.d Guluva" Album Review|publisher=Ubetoo |url=https://www.ubetoo.com/articles/kwesta-g-o-d-guluva-album-review/|first=John |last=Jabulani|access-date=2 May 2021}}</ref>

At the 2021 Mzansi Kwaito and House Music Awards, his single "Njandini" received a nomination for the Best Kwaito song.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicinafrica.net/magazine/mzansi-kwaito-and-house-music-awards-2021-all-nominees|title=Mzansi Kwaito and House Music Awards 2021: All the nominees|first= Ano |last=Shumba|date=18 June 2021 |work=Music In Africa}}</ref>
