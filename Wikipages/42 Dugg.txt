'''Dion Marquise Hayes''' (born November 25, 1995), known professionally as '''42 Dugg''' (pronounced "four-two dugg"),<ref>{{Cite web|title=42 Dugg Is Paid|url=https://www.complex.com/music/42-dugg-profile-interview-2021|access-date=August 2, 2021|website=Complex|language=en}}</ref> is an American rapper. He is jointly signed to [[Lil Baby]] and [[Yo Gotti]]'s respective labels [[Lil Baby#4PF|4 Pockets Full]] (4PF) and [[Yo Gotti#Collective Music Group|Collective Music Group]] (CMG), in conjunction with [[Interscope Records]].<ref name="ABC">{{cite web|url=http://abcnewsradioonline.com/music-news/2020/5/6/lil-baby-42-dogg-team-up-for-we-paid.html|title=Lil Baby & 42 Dugg team up for "We Paid"|website=[[ABC News Radio]]|date=May 6, 2020|access-date=June 15, 2020}}</ref> He is known for his collaborations with American rapper [[Lil Baby]], including "[[Grace (Lil Baby song)|Grace]]" and "[[We Paid]]", the latter becoming his first top 10 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. His second mixtape ''Young & Turnt 2'' garnered acclaim<!--Sourced in ref--> and marked his debut on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.<ref name="Boyz">{{cite web|url=https://thesource.com/2021/05/21/42-dugg-releases-new-project-free-dem-boyz/|title=42 Dugg Releases New Project 'Free Dem Boyz'|work=[[The Source]]|last=Grant|first=Shawn|date=May 21, 2021|access-date=May 21, 2021}}</ref> His fourth mixtape, ''[[Free Dem Boyz]]'', was released in 2021, peaking at number 8 on the  ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Olivia Rodrigo's 'Sour' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Albums Chart With 2021's Biggest Week|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9580294/olivia-rodrigo-sour-billboard-200-debut-2021-biggest-week/|access-date=July 25, 2021|magazine=Billboard|date=May 30, 2021|language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Dion Marquise Hayes was born on November 25, 1995, and raised on the East Side of [[Detroit]], [[Michigan]] near Whittier Avenue & Wayburn Street.<ref name="XXL">{{Cite web|title=The Break Presents: 42 Dugg|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/04/42-dugg-interview-the-break/|website=XXL|access-date=May 22, 2020}}</ref> The numbers "42" in Dugg's name is a numerical reference to a local clique known as the Hustle Boys. Hustle Boys are a Detroit branch of the 42 Gangster Crips of Los Angeles. At age 15 he was arrested and sentenced to 4 years in prison for carjacking, a fight with an inmate while incarcerated extended his prison time to a total of 6 years. He spent a month in solitary confinement on two occasions. During these times he practiced his lyric writing and he was released at age 22.<ref name="buford">{{Cite magazine|last=Buford|first=Jason|date=June 10, 2021|title=How 42 Dugg Went From Solitary to Rap Star
|url=https://www.gq.com/story/42-dugg-free-dem-boyz-profile=|access-date=September 18, 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> He met fellow rapper [[Lil Baby]] in 2017.<ref name=":0">{{Cite magazine|last=Saponara|first=Michael|date=April 13, 2020|title=Life's a Gamble: How Shooting Dice With Lil Baby Led to 42 Dugg's Blossoming Rap Career|url=https://assets.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9351463/42-dugg-young-and-turnt-vol-2-interview|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=May 22, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> He grew up listening to [[Yo Gotti]] and [[Jeezy]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/42-dugg/|title = 42 Dugg - 2021 XXL Freshman}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lyricallemonade.com/p/hard-times-dont-last-a-conversation-with-raps-newest-star-42-dugg|title = Lyrical Lemonade}}</ref>

== Career ==
Hayes first gained traction for tracks "The Streets" and "STFU". After writing [[slam poetry]] with [[Lil Baby]], he signed to Lil Baby's [[Lil Baby#4PF|4 Pockets Full]] (4PF) and [[Yo Gotti]]'s [[Yo Gotti#Collective Music Group|Collective Music Group]] (CMG), in conjunction with [[Interscope Records]].<ref name="XXL" /><ref name=":0" /> 42 Dugg received national attention when he was featured on Lil Baby's February 2020 track "[[Grace (Lil Baby song)|Grace]]", peaking at number 48 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. He was also featured on Lil Baby's track "[[We Paid]]" in May 2020, peaking at number 10 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. This boosted sales of his mixtape ''Young & Turnt 2'', peaking at number 58 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and number six on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Independent Albums chart.<ref name="Boyz" /> Hayes followed this success by doing guest features on songs for other artists such as [[Marshmello]], [[Lil Keed]], [[Meek Mill]], [[Blac Youngsta]], [[Big Sean]], [[T.I.]], [[Kaash Paige]], [[Tory Lanez]] and [[Mulatto (rapper)|Mulatto]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kaash-paige-reveals-teenage-fever-tracklist-news.115720.html?_amp|title=Kaash Paige Reveals "Teenage Fever" Tracklist Featuring Isaiah Rashad, K CAMP, & More|work=HotNewHipHop|last=Karlton|first=Jahmal|date=August 8, 2020|access-date=August 11, 2020}}</ref>

On February 21, a shooting occurred at [[Roddy Ricch]] and 42 Dugg's music video shoot, which left three people injured in Atlanta, Georgia. American hip hop artist [[OMB Peezy]] was later charged with the shooting with aggravated assault with a deadly weapon and possession of a firearm during the commission of a crime. All three injured survived.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Thorpe|first=Isha|date=February 21, 2021|title=42 Dugg confirms he's fine after gun shooting reports, Roddy Ricch also reportedly good|url=https://www.revolt.tv/news/2021/2/21/22294710/42-dogg-roddy-ricch-shooting|access-date=March 14, 2021|website=Revolt}}</ref>

In May 2021, ''Billboard'' named 42 Dugg the "R&B/Hip-Hop Rookie of the Month".<ref name="Boyz" /> That same week, on May 21, 2021, Dugg released the 19-track mixtape, titled ''Free Dem Boyz'', a dedication to all of his incarcerated friends. The project features the "standout" single, "[[4 Da Gang (42 Dugg and Roddy Ricch song)|4 da Gang]]" featuring Roddy Ricch; as well as other [[guest appearance|guest features]] from [[Lil Durk]], [[Rowdy Rebel]], and [[Fivio Foreign]].<ref name="Boyz" /> Also in 2021, he was featured on the annual [[XXL Freshman]] Class.

== Musical style ==
42 Dugg usually employs a slurred vocal style with [[auto-tune]] into his music.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/5/lil-baby-42-dugg-we-paid-music-video|title=Lil Baby Reconnects With 42 Dugg for "We Paid" Music Video|work=Hypebeast|last=Johnson|first=Patrick|date=May 6, 2020|access-date=June 21, 2020}}</ref> His whistle has been noted as his signature sound that also kicks off nearly every song on his 2020 mixtape ''Young and Turnt Vol 2''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/best-and-worst-of-rap-this-week-lil-baby-protest-song-noname-j-cole/amp/|title=The Best and Worst of Rap This Week: Why Lil Baby's Protest Song Should Not Be a Surprise and More|work=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]|last=Alphonse|first=Pierre|date=June 19, 2020|access-date=June 21, 2020}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
On March 10, 2020, Hayes was arrested on federal [[gun charges]].  surveillance video showed Hayes possessing, loading and firing a 9&nbsp;mm Glock pistol, according to the criminal complaint.

On June 5, 2020, Hayes fled from a [[traffic stop]] in a rented SUV after running through a stop sign and spent two months as a [[fugitive]]. Authorities worked with the rental company to try to catch Hayes. Police captured Hayes on August 4, 2020, and charged him with a third-degree felony for fleeing from police. He was released on a $20,000 bond.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cowen|first=Trace William|date=August 5, 2020|title=42 Dugg Arrested After being on the run for 2 months.|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/08/42-dugg-out-conditional-release-fleeing-cops-pulled-over-running-stop-sign|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=February 12, 2021|website=[[Complex.com|Complex]]}}</ref>
