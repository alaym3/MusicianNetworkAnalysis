'''Emily Wells''' (born November 20, 1981) is an [[United States|American]] multi-instrumentalist, [[vocalist]], [[composer]], [[arranger]], and [[Record producer|producer]] whose genres encompass [[alternative music|alternative]], [[experimental music|experimental]], [[Hip_hop|hip-hop]] and [[classical music|classical]].<ref name="bthesumphonies"/> In addition to playing instruments such as [[violin]] and [[analog synthesizer]]s, she uses loops, sample pads, and acoustic drums to achieve a layered effect.

==Early life==
Emily Wells was born in [[Amarillo, Texas]], United States.<ref name="breakingoutspin"/> Her father was a music minister,<ref name="emilywaa"/> and Wells began playing the violin at age four.<ref name="NPR" /><ref name="emilywaa"/> In 1990 she moved with her family to [[Indianapolis, Indiana]], where she lived until she began traveling in 2000.<ref name="roomthirteen"/> While traveling she made her residence primarily in New York, where she would later move after an 8-year stay in Los Angeles.<ref name="lassnarka"/><ref name="accesphase"/>

==Music career==

===Early solo albums (2000-2010)===
At age thirteen she began issuing her own recordings on cassette,<ref name="emilywaa"/>  starting with an unofficial release with 100 copies. After several unofficial tapes,<ref name="Emily Wellsdallmusic"/> in 2000 she "took up" with [[Epic Records]] but did not actually sign with them.<ref name="Emily Wellsdallmusic"/> ''Beautiful Sleepyhead & the Laughing Yaks'' was her official full-length debut in 2006,<ref name="Emily Wellsdallmusic"/> with all the tracks written, performed, produced, and mixed by Wells.<ref name="Emily Wellsdallmusic"/> Her sophomore album ''The Symphonies''  featured bassist Joey Reina and drummer Sam Halterman alongside Wells.<ref name="Emily Wellsdallmusic"/>

Wells was interviewed and performed "Symphony 1 in the Barrel of a Gun" on episode four of ''[[Last Call with Carson Daly]]'', which aired through [[NBC]] on September 18, 2009.<ref name="performsdaily"/> In September 2010, after recently moving from Los Angeles to New York, Wells was joined onstage by vocalist and harmonium player [[Shilpa Ray]]. The performance was positively reviewed by ''[[The New Yorker]]''.<ref name="dthenewyorker">

In 2012, she released her full-length album ''Mama'' through [[Partisan Records]].<ref name="Emily Wellsdallmusic"/>

The lead single, "Momma's Gonna give You Love," was featured in ''[[The New Yorker]]'' on April 10, 2012.<ref name="emilywarlisteningbooth"/> She also released a free remixed version of ''Mama'' on Partisan, with remixers such as [[Jeremiah Jae]], [[Kid Koala]], [[Baths (musician)|Baths]], and [[Deerhoof]].<ref name="emilywaq"/>  As of 2012 she was collaborating on material with [[Clint Mansell]], a scorewriter and former frontman of [[Pop Will Eat Itself]].<ref name="zjohnmurphy"/> That April 2012 she created a featured mixtape for ''Magnet Magazine'',<ref name="emilywaf"/> and Wells has periodically performed live sessions for recording studios, websites, and radio stations, including [[Daytrotter]] in April 2012,<ref name="emilywak"/> [[WYNC]]'s Soundcheck in May 2013,<ref name="emilywal"/> Laundro Matinee in October 2014,<ref name="emilywamwells"/> and [[KCRW]] in June 2013.<ref name="emilywanelectic"/>

===Tour diaries and soundtracks (2013)===
Wells has written tour diaries and articles for such publications as ''[[American Songwriter]]'',<ref name="emilywagsongwriter"/> ''Impose Magazine,''<ref name="emilywaecowboy"/> and ''[[The Huffington Post]]'',<ref name="huffpoastadvenutre"/> and she has appeared live multiple times on [[NPR]], with an interview on [[All Songs Considered]] on April 4, 2013, and a live performance on [[KCRW]] on July 11, 2013.<ref name="emilywab"/> In 2013 she composed a song for director [[Park Chan-Wook]]'s movie ''[[Stoker (film)|Stoker]]'' and toured South Korea promoting the film.<ref name="huffpoastadvenutre"/>  A single titled "Passenger" for a then upcoming acoustic version of ''Mama'' was released in 2013.<ref name="emilywaa"/> ''[[The Guardian]]'' named her New Band of the Week No. 1,503 on April 30, 2013.<ref name="emilywaa"/>

An acoustic version of ''Mama'' was released in on June 11, 2013 in the United States and United Kingdom through [[Partisan Records]].<ref name="xpopmatters"/> It had been recorded with a [[Tascam|Tascam 388]], with no digital effects added to the basic instrumentation.<ref name="emilywacperofmrer"/> Her songwriting methods were featured in a July 2013 edition of ''[[Performer Magazine]]''.<ref name="emilywacperofmrer"/> Among other shows, Wells performed at [[Le Guess Who?]] festival in [[The Netherlands]] in November 2013,<ref name="emilywad"/> and also that year she collaborated live with [[Questlove]] at [[Electronium]] in 2013.<ref name="emilywas"/> She performed with [[The Roots]] and [[Aloe Blacc]] at the [[Clinton Global Initiative|Rock the Clinton Global Initiative]] event that year as well.<ref name="emilywat"/>

===Recent projects and tours (2014-2015)===
"Momma's Gonna Give You Love" was used in the January 2015 trailer for season 3 of the TV series ''[[Bates Motel (TV series)|Bates Motel]]''.<ref name="emilywapbatesmotel"/> On March 27, 2015, Wells gave a well-received keynote at Hackfort2 at the fourth annual [[Treefort Music Fest]] in Boise, Idaho wherein she presented her methods of composition at the intersection of art and technology and the growth of her sensibility as a musician. Performing with a reduced kit ''sans'' drums, she played earlier and later versions of her song "So, Sunday."<ref name="boiseweeklY"/><ref name="kenoteatftreefort"/> She headlined on the main stage on the final day of the Treefort in 2015 and participated in the festival's Yogafort as well.<ref name="treefortmusicfest"/>

Wells was also broadcast on air by [[WQXR-FM|WQXR]] in March 2015, when she performed at Ecstatic Music Festival for the 80th birthday party of [[Terry Riley]], along with other musicians such as [[Marco Benevento]], [[Nancy Whang]], and [[Face the Music (music ensemble)|Face the Music]].<ref name="emilywaoxqxr"/> Since 2016 she has released three full-length albums on the imprint Thesis and Instinct.

==Style and influences==

In 2015, Wells stated that amongst her influences are the [[music theorist]] [[John Cage]], the [[minimalism|minimalist]] composer [[Philip Glass]], and the [[hip-hop]] group [[Wu-Tang Clan]].<ref name="boiseweeklY"/><ref name="kenoteatftreefort"/>
