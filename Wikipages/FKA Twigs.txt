'''Tahliah Debrett Barnett''' (born 17 January 1988<ref>{{cite web|url=https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/09186960/filing-history/MzEwNjAxNTY1NWFkaXF6a2N4/document?format=pdf&download=0|title=Certificate of Incorporation of a Private Limited Company {{!}} Female Reclining Ltd|access-date=18 February 2021}}</ref>), known professionally as '''FKA Twigs''' (stylized as '''FKA twigs'''), is an English singer-songwriter, record producer, dancer, and actress. <!--Occupations in lead intro must be inherent to the artist's NOTABILITY per reliable sources; it is not based on a summation of credits--> Born and raised in [[Cheltenham]], [[Gloucestershire]], she became a backup dancer after moving to [[South London]] at age 17. She made her musical debut with the [[extended play]] ''[[EP1 (FKA twigs EP)|EP1]]'' (2012).

Her debut studio album, ''[[LP1 (FKA Twigs album)|LP1]]'', was released in August 2014 to critical acclaim, peaking at number 16 on the [[UK Albums Chart]] and number 30 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. It was later nominated for the 2014 [[Mercury Prize]]. She released the ''[[Melissa (FKA twigs EP)|M3LL155X]]'' EP in 2015 to further critical acclaim, as well as her second studio album ''[[Magdalene (album)|Magdalene]]'' four years later. Her work has been described as "genre-bending",<ref name="vanityfair"/> drawing on various genres including [[electronic music]], [[trip hop]], [[contemporary R&B|R&B]], and  [[avant-garde]].

==Career==
===2012–2013: ''EP1'' and ''EP2''===
In August 2012, Barnett was photographed for the cover of ''[[i-D]]'' magazine.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://bleachlondon.co.uk/post/29336779893/twigs-for-i-d-magazine-the-just-kids-issue |title=Bleach Hair Salon |publisher=Bleach London |access-date=6 January 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131212113440/http://bleachlondon.co.uk/post/29336779893/twigs-for-i-d-magazine-the-just-kids-issue |archive-date=12 December 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She became known as Twigs for the way her [[Cracking joints|joints crack]].<ref name="pitchfork2"/> She added the initialism FKA to her name when another act called [[the Twigs]] – twin sisters active and recording since 1994 – asked her to change her stage name.<ref name="pitchfork2"/> Several sources have claimed that the "FKA" stands for "Formerly Known As",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2013/10/album-review-fka-twigs-ep2/ |title=Album Review: FKA Twigs – EP2 |publisher=Consequence of Sound |date=7 October 2013 |access-date=6 January 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/article/robert-pattinson-love-life-fka-twigs |title=5 Things To Know About Robert Pattinson's Fiancée, FKA twigs |work=People |date=9 September 2014 |access-date=17 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/legal-and-management/6465437/inside-the-legal-fight-over-fka-twigs-stage-name |title=Inside the Legal Fight Over FKA Twigs' Stage Name |publisher=Billboard |date=6 February 2015 |access-date=17 August 2015}}</ref> but Barnett has said in multiple interviews that the letters do not stand for anything in particular: "[I]t's just a collection of letters. I was gonna be like FK1 Twigs…or AFK Twigs…I just wanted a selection of letters that sounded quite kind of masculine and strong. FKA just worked. It doesn't stand for anything, it's just capital letters."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.seattletimes.com/soundposts/2014/11/18/what-does-fka-twigs-really-mean/ |title=What does 'FKA Twigs' really mean? |publisher=Seattle Times |date=18 November 2014 |access-date=17 August 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/20259/1/fka-twigs-future-shock |title=fka twigs: future shock |publisher=Dazed |date=19 June 2014 |access-date=17 August 2015}}</ref>

===2014–2017: ''LP1'' and ''M3LL155X''===
[[File:FKA Twigs 2015.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Barnett performing in [[Amsterdam]] in March 2015]]
In April 2014, Barnett appeared on the cover of ''[[The Fader]]'' for its 91st issue.<ref>{{cite web|last=Friedlander|first=Emilie|date=22 April 2014|title=FKA Twigs: Power Play|url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/04/22/fka-twigs-fader-cover-feature/#/0|access-date=3 July 2014|publisher=[[The Fader]]}}</ref> She began writing for her first studio album during a period of "self-hatred", which she considers "quite normal" for a young person.<ref name="complex" /> On 24 June, she released "[[Two Weeks (FKA Twigs song)|Two Weeks]]", the lead single from her forthcoming album, alongside a video. Barnett then directed and guest-starred in the music video for "Ouch Ouch" by rapper [[Lucki Eck$]]; she also produced the track.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Golden|first1=Zara|date=18 July 2014|title=Watch FKA Twigs' Transcendental Video for Lucki Eck$ "Ouch Ouch"|url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/07/18/watch-fka-twigs-transcendental-video-for-lucki-eck-ouch-ouch/|access-date=1 August 2014|website=[[The Fader]]}}</ref> The second single, "[[Pendulum (song)|Pendulum]]", was released on 29 July.

Her debut studio album, ''[[LP1 (FKA Twigs album)|LP1]]'', was released on 6 August through Young Turks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/55488-fka-twigs-announces-debut-album-lp1/|title=FKA twigs Announces Debut Album ''LP1''|last=Minsker|first=Evan|date=9 June 2014|website=Pitchfork|access-date=24 April 2021}}</ref> The album received widespread critical acclaim and placed high on several year-end critics' lists. [[Time (magazine)|''Time'']] gave a positive review, saying that Barnett has "made that transition to one of the most compelling and complex acts in R&B", and later ranked it, as did ''[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]'', #1 album of the year.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Josephs|first1=Brian|title=Review: FKA twigs Makes a Beautiful and Devastating Debut on LP1|url=https://time.com/3118648/fka-twigs-review/|website=time.com|access-date=17 August 2014}}</ref> She then announced a world tour starting on 2 October at [[Brighton Dome|The Dome]] in [[Brighton]], East Sussex and ending on 3 December in [[Orlando, Florida|Orlando]], Florida at [[Social (disambiguation)|The Social]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Copestick |first1=Julia |title=FKA Twigs Announces World Tour |url=http://www.mixmag.net/news/fka-twigs-announces-world-tour |access-date=17 August 2014 |date=13 August 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140819084606/http://www.mixmag.net/news/fka-twigs-announces-world-tour |archive-date=19 August 2014  }}</ref> That same August, Barnett was signed to London-based production company [[Academy Films]] as part of their A+ roster. On 20 August, she uploaded three videos to her YouTube channel which she directed: a video titled "Wet Wipez", starring the London dance crew of the same name; a video titled "tw-ache", which is a remix of the ''EP1'' song "Ache"; and a video for the Lucki Eck$ track "Ouch Ouch", which she also produced.

In September, ''LP1'' was revealed as one of the nominees for the 2014 [[Mercury Prize]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Jonze|first1=Tim|title=Mercury prize 2014 ignores big stars in favour of FKA Twigs, Young Fathers and East India Youth|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/sep/10/mercury-prize-2014-ignores-big-stars-in-favour-of-fka-twigs-young-fathers-and-east-india-youth|access-date=12 September 2014|date=11 September 2014}}</ref> The same month, Barnett performed on BBC's ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]''.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psOGsFGQsH8 FKA twigs – Two Weeks – Later... with Jools Holland – BBC Two], YouTube, 16 September 2014.</ref> In October, "Video Girl", the third and last single from the album, was released alongside a video a few days later, as well as an advert for [[Google Glass]] which Barnett directed.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6289260/fka-twigs-google-glass-commercial-throughglass|title = Watch FKA Twigs Make Google Glass Look Really, Really Cool in New Commercial|date = 21 October 2014|website = billboard.com|publisher = Nielsen|last = Payne|first = Chris}}</ref> She made her US television debut on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' on 4 November.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Boardman|first1=Madeline|title=FKA twigs Plays Tonight Show: Watch Robert Pattinson's Girlfriend Make Her US Television Debut|url=http://www.usmagazine.com/entertainment/news/fka-twigs-on-tonight-show-robert-pattinson-girlfriend-makes-us-debut-2014511|access-date=5 November 2014|date=5 November 2014}}</ref>

On 7 November 2014, producer [[Boots (musician)|Boots]] announced he was working with Barnett on her third EP.<ref>[https://genius.com/discussions/120353-Boots-working-on-fka-twigs-ep-3-and-much-more Boots working on Fka Twigs Ep 3, and much more], Genius, 7 November 2014.</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Pareles|first=Jon|date=14 February 2015|title=After His Coup With Beyoncé, Boots Strides Out of the Shadows|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/02/15/arts/music/after-his-coup-with-beyonce-boots-strides-out-of-the-shadows.html|work=The New York Times|access-date=24 April 2021|url-access=registration}}</ref> The video for "Glass & Patron", the first song released from this EP, was posted on her YouTube channel on 23 March 2015; it was directed by Barnett.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cNbFc-fa-ww| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211109/cNbFc-fa-ww| archive-date=2021-11-09 | url-status=live|title=FKA twigs: Glass & Patron (Official Music Video, YTMAs)|last=FKA twigs|date=23 March 2015|via=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref> In February 2015, she performed Congregata, a theatrical "coming together" and choreographed performance that visualised "the story of my life while making this album" at the Roundhouse in Camden London.<ref name="Telegraph20150220">{{cite news|last=Vincent|first=Alice|date=20 February 2015|title=FKA Twigs, Roundhouse, review: 'frustrating'|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/live-music-reviews/11423160/FKA-Twigs-Roundhouse-review.html|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|access-date=24 April 2021|url-access=limited}}</ref> On 15 May, on her Instagram account, she posted a picture of men posing with jackets on that had an image of Barnett's face from the "Papi Pacify" video with Barnett captioning the pic "coming soon... <3", hinting at either merchandise or ''EP3''<nowiki/>'s alleged release in the summer. During an interview with ''[[Complex Networks|Complex]]'' released in June, she stated that she had changed the title of the EP to ''Melissa'', and that it would be released within two months. She confirmed it would include the songs "Glass & Patron", "Mothercreep", "I'm Your Doll", "Figure 8", and "In Time".<ref name="complex" />

[[File:Fka twigs concert live in berlin (20024907315).jpg|thumb|250px|Barnett performing in [[Berlin]] in March 2015]]
The EP, stylised as ''[[M3LL155X]],'' was released on 13 August 2015, containing all five announced tracks and accompanied by four videos, which were directed by Barnett herself, featuring pregnancy, sex dolls, vogueing and [[Michèle Lamy|Michele Lamy]]. The title of the EP reads as "Melissa" and is named after the artist's "personal female energy". The record met critical acclaim.

On 18 February 2016, Barnett debuted a new song and music video titled "Good to Love".<ref>{{cite web|author=Zoe Camp |url=http://pitchfork.com/news/63638-fka-twigs-new-song-good-to-love-debuts-with-sensual-video/ |title=FKA twigs New Song "Good to Love" Debuts With Sensual Video |publisher=Pitchfork |date=18 February 2016 |access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> The song premiered earlier at ''Soundtrack 7'', her seven-day residency at [[Manchester International Festival#MIF 15|Manchester International Festival]] that took place in July 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://f-k-a-twigs.tumblr.com/|title=FKA twigs|first=F. K. A.|last=twigs|website=f-k-a-twigs.tumblr.com}}</ref> She also performed the song on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]]'' on 24 February.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbc.com/the-tonight-show/guest/fka-twigs/15746 |title=FKA twigs on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon |publisher=Nbc.com |access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> On 9 July, Barnett debuted a new stage show called "Radiant Me²" at [[Moscow]]'s Lastochka Festival, where she unveiled three previously unheard songs.<ref>{{cite web |last=Stutz |first=Colin |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7431377/fka-new-music-stage-show-radiant-me-russia-video |title=FKA Debuts New Music in Russia: Watch |work=Billboard |publisher=Prometheus Global Media |date=11 July 2016 |access-date=18 July 2016}}</ref>

In August 2016, the dance film ''Soundtrack 7'' was premiered. Directed by Barnett, the film was created, performed and filmed on-site across seven days at [[Manchester International Festival]] in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/69602-fka-twigs-shares-new-film-soundtrack-7/|title=Watch FKA twigs' New Film Soundtrack 7|website=Pitchfork|date=13 December 2016|access-date=19 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.creativereview.co.uk/creativeleaders50/leader/fka-twigs/|title=FKA twigs|website=[[Creative Review]]|access-date=19 October 2018}}</ref> She later did advertising work directing, narrating and starring in a television ad for [[Nike, Inc.|Nike]],<ref>[http://creativity-online.com/work/nike-do-you-believe-in-more/50546 FKA twigs directs and stars in a stunning spot for Nike Women], Alexandra Jardine, [[Advertising Age]], 12 January 2017.</ref> and dancing in [[Apple Inc.|Apple]]'s advertisement for [[HomePod]], thus gaining worldwide visibility.<ref>[http://creativity-online.com/work/apple-welcome-home/54025 FKA twigs dances her way out of depression in Spike Jonez's film for Apple HomePod], Alexandra Jardine, [[Advertising Age]], 22 March 2018.</ref>

===2018–2020: ''Magdalene''===
Barnett appeared on [[ASAP Rocky|A$AP Rocky's]] album ''[[Testing (album)|Testing]]'' in 2018, featuring on the track "Fukk Sleep".

On 24 April 2019, she released the single, "[[Cellophane (FKA Twigs song)|Cellophane]]",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/fka-twigs-returns-with-new-song-and-video-cellophane-watch/|title=FKA twigs Returns With New Song and Video "Cellophane": Watch|website=Pitchfork|last=Sodomsky|first=Sam|date=24 April 2019}}</ref> her first release since "Good to Love" in 2016. In September, Barnett announced that her second album, co-produced with [[Nicolas Jaar]] among others, would be titled ''[[Magdalene (album)|Magdalene]]'', supported by second single "Holy Terrain" (featuring [[Future (rapper)|Future]]) on 9 September.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/zmjny4/fka-twigs-interview-magdalene-new-album|title=how losing everything saved fka twigs|website=i-D|last=Dunn|first=Frankie|date=2 September 2019}}</ref> On 7 October, she released the third single, "Home with You", and announced that the album would be delayed until 8 November.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/fka-twigs-shares-video-for-new-song-home-with-you-watch/ |title=FKA twigs Shares Video for New Song "Home With You": Watch |last=Minkser |first=Evan |last2=Monroe |first2=Jazz |date=7 October 2019 |website=Pitchfork |access-date=7 October 2019 |quote=The new album [...] was originally set for release on October 25}}</ref> The fourth single, "[[Sad Day (FKA Twigs song)|Sad Day]]", was released on 4 November.

''Magdalene'' was released on 8 November 2019, as planned, and received universal critical acclaim. The album ranked No. 1 on four year-end lists (''[[The A.V. Club]]'', ''[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]'', ''[[Now (newspaper)|Now]]'', and ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'') and in top-five of seven other lists. The same day, film ''[[Honey Boy (film)|Honey Boy]]'', in which Barnett played Shy Girl, had a commercial premiere. The movie received positive reviews from critics.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/honey_boy|title=Honey Boy (2019)|website=[[Rotten Tomatoes]]|access-date=23 November 2019}}</ref>

In August 2020, she appeared in an [[OnlyFans]]-inspired music video for the song "Sum Bout U" with rapper 645AR.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Elder|first=Sajae|date=4 August 2020|title=645AR enlists FKA twigs for "Sum Bout U"|url=https://www.thefader.com/2020/08/04/645ar-fka-twigs-sum-bout-u-video|access-date=5 August 2020|work=[[The Fader]]}}</ref> Video for the "Sad Day", directed by [[Hiro Murai]], premiered on 28 August. In September, Barnett received 4 nominations for the 2020 [[UK Music Video Awards]], "Sad Day" won later the best cinematography in a video.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Copsey|first=Rob|date=30 September 2020|title=UK Music Video Awards: Dua Lipa, The Weeknd and Beyonce lead nominations|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/uk-music-video-awards-dua-lipa-the-weeknd-and-beyonce-lead-nominations__31116/|access-date=30 September 2020|website=[[Official Charts Company|Official Charts]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=5 November 2020|title=UK Music Video Awards 2020: all the winners!|url=https://www.promonews.tv/news/2020/11/05/uk-music-video-awards-2020-all-winners/67669|access-date=25 January 2021|website=Promonews.tv|language=en}}</ref>

In October, Barnett took part in a virtual chat on the Grammy Museum's Programs at Home series. During the chat, she revealed that she was "just finishing" a new album that she made largely in collaboration with Spanish musician and producer [[El Guincho]], and with other collaborators, many of whom she met for the first time via FaceTime calls.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bloom|first=Madison|title=FKA twigs Says She Made a New Album During Quarantine|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/fka-twigs-says-she-made-a-new-album-during-quarantine/|access-date=11 December 2020|website=Pitchfork|language=en-us}}</ref> In November, British singer [[Dua Lipa]] hosted a livestream concert, titled [[Studio 2054]], in which Barnett was invited to play as a guest. The two teased an upcoming collaboration, titled "Why Don't You Love Me", during Barnett's performance.<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 November 2020|title=Watch Dua Lipa & FKA twigs Tease Their New Song In Studio 2054 Livestream|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2108763/dua-lipa-fka-twigs-tease-new-song-studio-2054-livestream/news/|access-date=11 December 2020|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref>

=== 2021–present: Upcoming mixtape ===
On 25 January 2021, Barnett announced a new single "Don't Judge Me", featuring North London rapper [[Headie One]] and producer [[Fred Again|Fred again]], to be released the next day alongside a video co-directed by Dutch-Ghanaian director [[Emmanuel Adjei]] and herself.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Langford|first=Jackson|date=25 January 2021|title=FKA Twigs announces new single 'Don't Judge Me' with Headie One & Fred Again|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/fka-twigs-announces-new-single-dont-judge-me-with-headie-one-fred-again-2864617|access-date=26 January 2021|website=[[NME]]}}</ref> The same day, she took part in a podcast episode with [[Louis Theroux]] and discussed her previous known relationships and an upcoming album, stating "It was all via the internet...I have more collaborations and features on this album than I ever had before."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Theroux|first=Louis|date=25 January 2021|title=Grounded with Louis Theroux – 19. FKA twigs|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p091pg54|access-date=24 April 2021|publisher=[[BBC Radio 4]]}}</ref> In February, she described the album as a "going out" record, and revealed it will feature collaborations with Nigerian [[Afrobeats]] star [[Rema (musician)|Rema]], British hip-hop up-and-comer [[Pa Salieu]], as well as the aforementioned Headie One and Dua Lipa tracks.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Carlos|first=Marjon|date=17 February 2021|title='It's A Miracle I Came Out Alive'|url=https://www.elle.com/culture/celebrities/a35460385/fka-twigs-shia-la-beouf-abuse/|access-date=23 February 2021|website=[[Elle (magazine)|Elle US]]}}</ref> A few days later, Barnett, interviewed by British actress and screenwriter [[Michaela Coel]], revealed that she had changed the undisclosed, original title of the upcoming record due to a well-known artist titling a non-musical project the same thing.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Coel|first=Michaela|date=24 February 2021|title=Joyride: the renaissance of FKA twigs|url=https://theface.com/music/fka-twigs-interview-michaela-coel-i-may-destroy-you-pa-salieu-headie-one|magazine=[[The Face (magazine)|The Face]]|access-date=24 April 2021}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'' in May, Barnett revealed the album does not have a set release date, but she wants to release it in the northern summer of 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hobbs|first=Julia|date=18 May 2021|title=Here's Why Summer 2021 Belongs To FKA Twigs|url=https://www.vogue.co.uk/fashion/article/fka-twigs-burberry-olympia|website=British Vogue|language=en-GB|access-date=23 May 2021}}</ref> This did not come to materialize.

In July 2021, Barnett directed the music video for [[Koreless]]' "White Picket Fence" single.<ref>{{Cite web|date=13 July 2021|title=FKA twigs directs video for Koreless's 'White Picket Fence'|url=https://www.factmag.com/2021/07/13/koreless-fka-twigs-white-picket-fence-video/|access-date=14 July 2021|website=Fact Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

Barnett said: "I am not restricted by any musical genre. I like to experiment with sounds, generating emotions while putting my voice on certain atmospheres [...] I found my own way of playing punk. I like industrial sounds and incorporating everyday life's sounds like a car alarm."<ref>{{cite web|last=Binet|first=Stéphanie|date=29 October 2014|title=Pitchfork Festival : trois ans, et déjà la postérité pour le dubstep [including an interview with FKA Twigs]|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/musiques/article/2014/10/30/festival-pitchfork-trois-ans-et-deja-la-posterite-pour-le-dubstep_4514823_1654986.html|access-date=12 November 2014|work=[[Le Monde]]|quote=Je ne me plie à aucun genre musical, assure la métisse anglo-jamaïcaine. J'aime expérimenter avec les sons, provoquer des émotions en posant ma voix sur certaines atmosphères. Je viens de terminer mes derniers morceaux, et cela a beaucoup évolué par rapport à mon premier disque. Mes nouvelles musiques sont plus agressives. Plus jeune, je n'écoutais que du punk, Siouxsie and the Banshees, Sid Vicious, et là, j'ai trouvé ma propre manière de jouer du punk. J'aime les sons industriels, incorporer des bruits de la vie de tous les jours comme une alarme de voiture.}}</ref> She has been associated with the [[alternative R&B]] tag, though she herself has rejected the R&B label as related to her race:<blockquote>It's just because I'm [[mixed race]]. When I first released music and no one knew what I looked like, I would read comments like: 'I've never heard anything like this before, it's not in a genre.' And then my picture came out six months later, now she's an R&B singer. I share certain sonic threads with [[classical music]]; my song 'Preface' is like a [[hymn]]. So let's talk about that. If I was white and blonde and said I went to church all the time, you'd be talking about the 'choral aspect'. But you're not talking about that because I'm a mixed-race girl from [[south London]].<ref name="fader">{{cite web|last1=Cliff|first1=Aimee|title=FKA Twigs Is Right, "Alternative R&B" Must Die|url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/09/12/popping-off-fka-twigs-beyonce-alt-r-and-b|website=[[The Fader]]|access-date=20 August 2016}}</ref></blockquote>

The first singers who influenced Barnett were [[Billie Holiday]], [[Ella Fitzgerald]], and [[Marvin Gaye]]. When she started composing songs, she wanted to reproduce music she liked: "every bit of music that I made sounded like a pastiche of [[Siouxsie and the Banshees]] or [[Adam Ant]]. But through that I discovered myself".<ref>{{cite web |first=Ruth |last=Saxelby |title=Interview: Young Turks' FKA twigs |url=http://www.redbullmusicacademy.com/magazine/fka-twigs-interview |date=25 September 2013 |access-date=12 November 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141113202404/http://www.redbullmusicacademy.com/magazine/fka-twigs-interview |archive-date=13 November 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In an interview after being shortlisted for the 2014 [[Mercury Prize]], Barnett cited ''[[Germfree Adolescents]]'' by [[X-Ray Spex]] as her favourite album of all time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pe4eTsTcyEc | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211109/Pe4eTsTcyEc| archive-date=2021-11-09 | url-status=live|title=FKA twigs Q&A - 2014 Mercury Prize |publisher=YouTube |date=15 September 2014 |access-date=29 April 2019}}{{cbignore}}</ref>
