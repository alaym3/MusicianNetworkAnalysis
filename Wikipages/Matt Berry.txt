'''Matthew Charles Berry''' (born 2 May 1974) is an English actor, comedian, musician and writer. He has appeared in comedy series such as ''[[The IT Crowd]]'', ''[[Garth Marenghi's Darkplace]]'', ''[[The Mighty Boosh (TV series)|The Mighty Boosh]]'', ''[[Snuff Box (TV series)|Snuff Box]]'', and also co-created and starred in the [[Channel 4]] sitcom ''[[Toast of London]]'', for which he won the 2015 [[British Academy of Film and Television Arts|BAFTA Award]] for Best Male Performance in a Comedy Programme.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://awards.bafta.org/award/2015/television/male-performance-in-a-comedy-programme|title= Male Performance in a Comedy Programme in 2015 |work=BAFTA}}</ref> He has starred in the [[FX (TV channel)|FX]] television series [[What We Do in the Shadows (TV series)|''What We Do in the Shadows'']] since 2019. In addition to his acting career, Berry is a prolific musician and has released nine studio albums. His most recent, ''The Blue Elephant'', was released in May 2021.

==Early life==
Matthew Charles Berry was born on 2 May 1974<ref>{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/artist/matt-berry/180104600 |title=Matt Berry |publisher=Apple Music |access-date=29 January 2019}}</ref> in [[Bromham, Bedfordshire|Bromham]], [[Bedfordshire]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/matt-berry/bio/256579/ |title=Matt Berry |work=TV Guide |publisher=CBS Interactive |access-date=29 January 2019}}</ref> the son of Pauline (''née'' Acreman), a nurse, and Charles Berry, a taxi driver.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Matt Berry {{!}} TV Guide|url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/matt-berry/bio/256579|access-date=2020-10-26|website=TV Guide}}</ref> He attended [[Nottingham Trent University]], graduating in 1999 with a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] in contemporary arts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ntualumni.org.uk/your_alumni_association/notable_alumni/famous_and_successful |title=Famous and successful |publisher=Nottingham Trent University |access-date=14 March 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170926191704/http://www.ntualumni.org.uk/your_alumni_association/notable_alumni/famous_and_successful |archive-date=26 September 2017 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==
===Film and television===
Berry began his career as a [[Television_crew#Runner|runner]] before appearing in the video game magazine show ''Game Over'' on [[BSkyB]]'s computer and technology channel [[.tv (TV channel)|.tv]], between 1998 and 1999.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8Y6SP-yeKk4 | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211117/8Y6SP-yeKk4| archive-date=2021-11-17 | url-status=live|title=Episode of Game Over on Youtube, 1999 |publisher=[[YouTube]] |date=28 April 2007 |access-date=4 August 2010}}{{cbignore}}</ref> Though commissioned as an editorial show on the subject matter, episodes contained a large number of comedy sketches with Berry as the main performer.

==== 2004–2011: ''Garth Marenghi's Darkplace'', ''The IT Crowd'' and ''Snuff Box'' ====
Berry's first prominent television role was playing [[Todd Rivers]]/Lucien Sanchez in the cult 2004 comedy series ''[[Garth Marenghi's Darkplace]]'' and its 2006 spin-off, ''[[Man to Man with Dean Learner]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tv.com/man-to-man-with-dean-learner/show/69335/episode.html |title=Man to Man with Dean Learner episode guide |website=Tv.com |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref> He later appeared as eccentric and sinister tycoon [[List of recurring characters from The Mighty Boosh#Dixon Bainbridge|Dixon Bainbridge]] in ''[[The Mighty Boosh]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0416394/fullcredits#cast|title=The Mighty Boosh|date=20 May 2003|access-date=4 January 2017|website=IMDb.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://themightyboosh.blogspot.com/2004_03_01_archive.html |title=The Mighty Boosh News Updates, 31 March 2004 |website=Themightyboosh.blogspot.com |date=4 May 2004 |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref> Berry first met ''The Mighty Boosh'' duo while performing at the [[Hen and Chickens Theatre]], where they were resident in 2000.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/comedy/snuffbox/actors/interview_qa.shtml |title=Interview with Matt Berry and Rich Fulcher |publisher=BBC |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref> He can be seen in a cameo performance in ''The Mighty Boosh Live'' DVD.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0907128/combined|title=The Mighty Boosh Live (2006) (V)|website=IMDb.com}}</ref>

After meeting [[Rich Fulcher]] while filming ''The Mighty Boosh'', Berry wrote and starred in the comedy series ''[[Snuff Box]]'' with him on [[BBC Three]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/comedy/snuffbox/berryandfulcher/ |title=Official '&#39;Snuff Box'&#39; site |publisher=BBC |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Charlton |first=Dave |url=http://twitchfilm.net/archives/005210.html |title=Snuff Box at twitchfilm.com |work=Twitch |access-date=4 August 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071020012327/http://twitchfilm.net/archives/005210.html |archive-date=20 October 2007 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> Fulcher had co-starred as Bainbridge's lackey, [[List of recurring characters from The Mighty Boosh#Bob Fossil|Bob Fossil]] in ''The Mighty Boosh''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0416394/epcast|title="The Mighty Boosh" (2003) – Episodes cast|website=IMDb.com}}</ref> Berry also starred alongside Fulcher and [[Simon Farnaby]] on [[E4 (TV channel)|E4]]'s ''The Golf War'' in November 2007.<ref>{{cite news|title=27.09.2007|url=http://themightybooshsite.trinitystreetdirect.com/news.aspx|access-date=20 October 2007|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071014010237/http://themightybooshsite.trinitystreetdirect.com/news.aspx|archive-date=14 October 2007}}</ref>

In 2007, Berry joined ''[[The IT Crowd]]'' during its second series as [[List of The IT Crowd characters#Douglas Reynholm|Douglas Reynholm]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dvdtimes.co.uk/content.php?contentid=66057 |title=The IT Crowd 2.0 |website=Dvdtimes.co.uk |date=1 October 2007 |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref> He was nominated as Best Male Comedy Newcomer in the 2007 [[British Comedy Awards]] for this role, but lost out to [[James Corden]] for ''[[Gavin & Stacey]]''.<ref name=":0" />

In 2008, he became the face of [[Adult Swim]]'s spoof charity appeal "Save the Workers".<ref>{{cite web|year=2008|title=Save the Workers Campaign|url=http://savetheworkers.org/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080510141810/http://www.savetheworkers.org/|archive-date=10 May 2008|access-date=8 May 2008|website=Adultswim.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r-YVGxzx6Ww| archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211117/r-YVGxzx6Ww| archive-date=2021-11-17 | url-status=live|title=[adult swim.co.uk] Save The Workers|access-date=15 May 2021|publisher=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

In 2010, Berry played journalist Michael Duffy in the [[BBC]] production ''[[Five Daughters]]'', which portrayed events surrounding the Ipswich murders of 2006. In 2011, he provided the voice of Allen in the ''[[Aqua Teen Hunger Force]]'' episode "[[Allen (Aqua Unit Patrol Squad 1)|Allen Part Two]]." Berry appeared in the British comedy panel game ''[[Shooting Stars (British TV series)|Shooting Stars]]'', portraying [[Vangelis]].<ref>{{cite web|title=BBC Two – Shooting Stars, Series 8, Episode 3|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b013yynr|publisher=BBC}}</ref> He also appeared as an antiques expert in the [[ITV Network|ITV]] sketch show ''[[Monkey Trousers]]'' with [[Vic and Bob]] and [[Steve Coogan]].<ref>{{cite web|date=4 July 2005|title=Monkey Trousers DVD|url=http://www.babycow.co.uk/monkeydvd.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090327063833/http://www.babycow.co.uk/monkeydvd.html|archive-date=27 March 2009|access-date=4 August 2010|website=Babycow.co.uk}}</ref>

Berry also made a number of film appearances during this period, including ''[[The Devil's Chair]]'' (2006)<ref>{{cite web|author=Bifrostedflake|date=22 September 2007|title=The Devil's Chair (2007)|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0837791/|website=IMDb.com}}</ref> and ''[[Moon (2009 film)|Moon]]'' (2009). He also starred in ''The Search'' (2009) a short film about "a lonely man's search for the existence of life outside our universe takes a remarkable turn when he connects with a recently bereaved family".<ref>{{Citation|last=Buchanan|first=Mark|title=The Search|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1519424/|type=Short, Drama, Family, Romance, Sci-Fi|others=Matt Berry, Flora Montgomery, Tim Plester|publisher=Phase VI|access-date=2020-09-05}}</ref>

==== 2012–2018: ''Toast of London'' and other TV appearances ====
In 2012, BBC Radio 4 aired his comedy series ''I, Regress''. In it, he takes on the role of Dr. Berry, a brilliant but unorthodox regressive therapist. During each 15-minute episode, Berry explores the psyche of a guest patient, attempting to treat his or her problems in surreal dreamlike sequences.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2012/jan/08/andrew-hillsdon-i-regress|title=Hypnotherapist Andrew Hillsdon on I, Regress|author=Laura Barnett|work=The Guardian}}</ref> Berry appeared in the film adaptation of the [[David Nicholls (writer)|David Nicholls]] novel ''[[One Day (novel)|One Day]]'', playing the role of Aaron, Dexter's agent. He briefly featured in the closing ceremony of the [[2012 Summer Olympics closing ceremony|London 2012 Olympics]], introducing [[ELO]]'s "[[Mr. Blue Sky]]" by way of a mock weather forecast.

In July 2012, he appeared as vainglorious actor Steven Toast in the pilot episode of [[Channel 4]] sitcom ''[[Toast of London]]'', co-written with [[Arthur Mathews (writer)|Arthur Mathews]]. Notwithstanding mixed reviews, a series was commissioned. The first episode was broadcast on 20 October 2013. On 10 May 2015 Berry won a BAFTA award for Best Male Comedy Performance in the series ''Toast of London''. In 2015, Berry and Arthur Mathews published "Toast on Toast: Cautionary tales and candid advice", a spoof autobiography of Steven Toast. It was also released as an audiobook read by Berry.

[[File:Matt Berry 2013.jpg|thumb|left|Berry in 2013]]
Berry played the title character in the 2013 ''[[Portlandia (TV series)|Portlandia]]'' episode "Squiggleman." Co-creators [[Fred Armisen]] and [[Carrie Brownstein]] included the appearance on a list of their "proudest musical moments" from the show.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Willman|first=Chris|date=2018-06-23|title=‘Portlandia’: Fred Armisen and Carrie Brownstein Pick Their Proudest Musical Moments|url=https://variety.com/2018/tv/news/portlandia-music-fred-armisen-carrie-brownstein-proudest-moments-1202855733/|access-date=2020-09-05|website=Variety.com|language=en}}</ref>

Berry appears in episode nine of the sixth season of [[Dan Harmon]]'s television series ''[[Community (TV show)|Community]]'', released on [[Yahoo Screen]] on 5 May 2015. In December 2015, he played Professor Awfulshirt in ''Harry Hill in Professor Branestawm Returns'' on [[BBC One]].<ref name="Dowell 2015">{{cite web|last=Dowell|first=Ben|date=24 December 2015|title=Harry Hill in Professor Branestawm Returns - preview of the Christmas Eve comedy on BBC1|url=http://www.radiotimes.com/news/2015-12-24/harry-hills-professor-branestawm-is-back-for-a-second-christmas-and-hes-funnier-sharper-and-even-sillier|access-date=1 January 2016|website=Radio Times}}</ref>

Berry also took on a number of voice acting roles. He voiced Bubbles, an inter-dimensional dolphin, in the 2015 SpongeBob SquarePants film sequel, ''[[The SpongeBob Movie: Sponge Out of Water]]''. That year, he also voiced the main villainess, "The Butt Witch," in ''[[Twelve Forever]]'', a series pilot created by Julia Vickerman for [[Cartoon Network]] that was released on their website on 18 May 2015. The show was moved to [[Netflix]] in December 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cartoonnetwork.com/video/special/twelve-forever-episode.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=25 May 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150521234218/http://www.cartoonnetwork.com/video/special/twelve-forever-episode.html |archive-date=21 May 2015  }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Flores|first1=Terry|title=Netflix Picks Up New Animated Series 'Twelve Forever' for 2019|url=https://variety.com/2017/digital/news/netflix-animated-series-twelve-forever-1202627977/|website=Variety|access-date=2 December 2017|date=1 December 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201214149/http://variety.com/2017/digital/news/netflix-animated-series-twelve-forever-1202627977/|archive-date=1 December 2017}}</ref> From 2015 to 2016, Berry narrated ''Matt Berry Does ...'', a series of comedy shorts for the BBC.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BBC Two - Comedy Shorts, Matt Berry Does...|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p02mwdx3|access-date=2020-09-05|website=Bbc.co.uk}}</ref> Berry provided the voice of Prince Merkimer in [[Matt Groening]]'s animated series ''[[Disenchantment (TV series)|Disenchantment]]'', which premiered in 2018 on [[Netflix]].<ref name="disenchantment">{{cite news|last1=Fienberg|first1=Daniel|date=8 August 2018|title='Disenchantment': TV Review|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/disenchantment-review-1132172|access-date=7 December 2018}}</ref>

==== 2019–present: ''What We Do in the Shadows'' and ''Year of the Rabbit'' ====
Since 2019, Berry has starred in the FX [[What We Do in the Shadows (TV series)|TV adaptation]] of the film ''[[What We Do in the Shadows]]''; he plays Laszlo, one of four vampires living in [[Staten Island]].<ref name="PremiereTrailer">{{cite web |last1=Petski |first1=Denise |title=‘What We Do In The Shadows’: FX Vampire Comedy Series Unveils Trailer, Sets Premiere Date -TCA |url=https://deadline.com/2019/02/what-we-do-in-the-shadows-trailer-premiere-date-fx-vampire-comedy-series-tca-1202549095/ |website=Deadline Hollywood |access-date=February 4, 2019 |date=February 4, 2019}}</ref> The series was renewed for a second season in May 2019, and began airing in April 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2019/05/what-we-do-in-the-shadows-season-two-fx-1202608997/|title=FX Takes Second Bite Of Jermaine Clement & Taika Waititi's Vampire Comedy 'What We Do In The Shadows'|work=Deadline Hollywood|last=White|first=Peter|date=May 7, 2019|access-date=May 7, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-04|title=What We Do in the Shadows Season 2 Return Date, Trailer, and News|url=https://www.denofgeek.com/tv/what-we-do-in-the-shadows-season-2-news/|access-date=2021-07-19|website=Den of Geek|language=en-US}}</ref> The third season aired September 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Clark|first=Anne Victoria|date=2021-06-03|title=We Now Know When the Funny Vampires Are Coming Back|url=https://www.vulture.com/2021/06/what-we-do-in-the-shadows-season-3-release-date.html|access-date=2021-07-19|website=Vulture|language=en-us}}</ref> In an interview with collider.com, Berry said, "... [I]t's good fun. I like it. I enjoy it. It's different and it's very free. The scripts are starting points and then we go from there. That's how I like to work, anyway. So, for me, it was perfect. That's my background, improvisation. It was home for me in that department."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Matt Berry & Natasia Demetriou on the Joys of Improv in 'What We Do in the Shadows'|url=https://collider.com/what-we-do-in-the-shadows-matt-berry-natasia-demetriou-interview/|last=Radish|first=Christina|date=2020-04-29|website=Collider|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-13}}</ref>

In 2019, Berry also starred in the IFC/Channel 4 sitcom [[Year of the Rabbit (TV series)|''Year of the Rabbit'']]. Set in Victorian London, he plays the alcoholic Detective Inspector Rabbit. On 11 February 2020, the series was renewed for a second series of six episodes.<ref>{{cite web|title="Year of the Rabbit" Renewed for Season 2 by IFC and UK's Channel 4|url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news/2020/02/11/year-of-the-rabbit-renewed-for-season-2-by-ifc-and-uks-channel-4-701015/20200211ifc01/|work=[[The Futon Critic]]|date=February 11, 2020}}</ref> Citing budget cuts caused by the coronavirus pandemic, Channel 4 reversed their decision in January 2021, casting doubt on the show's future. Layla Smith, chief executive of the show's production company [[Objective Media Group|OMG]], stated that "''Year Of The Rabbit'' is a victim of the devastating effects of Covid [...] IFC are very committed to the show, but we will need to find another partner — and we’re working on that."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kanter|first=Jake|date=2021-01-21|title=Channel 4 Reverses Decision To Renew IFC Co-Produced Matt Berry Comedy ‘Year Of The Rabbit’|url=https://deadline.com/2021/01/year-of-the-rabbit-channel-4-ifc-objective-media-group-1234677514/|access-date=2021-07-19|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref>

In April 2020, BBC Two commissioned Berry for a mockumentary series titled ''Squeamish About …'' The four quarter-hour specials will be co-written by Berry and [[Arthur Mathews (writer)|Arthur Mathews]], and star Berry as "Michael Squeamish." The series uses a combination of archive footage and voiceover to produce a surreal perspective on the episodes' subject.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bennett|first=Steve|title=Matt Berry to make more BBC Two mockumentaries : News 2020 : Chortle : The UK Comedy Guide|url=http://www.chortle.co.uk/news/2020/04/21/45935/matt_berry_to_make_more_bbc_two_mockumentaries|access-date=2020-09-01|website=Chortle.co.uk|language=en}}</ref>

In 2021, after the second series of ''Year of the Rabbit'' was axed, Berry got a commission from the BBC to make a "Steven Toast in America" series for them. The show is due to be a six-part television programme for BBC One, which will be broadcast under the title ''Toast of Tinseltown'' and which will be co-written by Berry and Arthur Mathews.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/tv/toast-of-london-moves-bbc-one-sequel-series-2877244|title='Toast of London' moves to BBC One with sequel series|website=Nme.com|date=10 February 2021|access-date=15 May 2021}}</ref>

===Voiceovers===
Berry has provided many voiceovers for both radio and television advertising, including [[Absolute Radio]], [[Volvic (mineral water)|Volvic]], [[Müller (company)|Müller Corner]] adverts and characters on several adverts for [[The Natural Confectionery Company]]. He has also been in the sketch show ''[[The Wrong Door]]'' as a recurring bit-part character who runs into a [[Billiard hall|snooker hall]] and shouts "Stop playing snooker!" before whispering an implied impossible wager to one of the players and completing an unfeasible [[trick shot]]. In 2012, he worked with [[Team17]] on their video game title ''[[Worms Revolution]]'', providing voiceover, as fictional wildlife documentary maker Don Keystone, for both the game and the video advertisements produced for it.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.videogamer.com/xbox360/worms_revolution/news/matt_berry_lends_his_voice_to_worms_revolution.html|title=Matt Berry lends his voice to Worms Revolution|author=James Orry|work=VideoGamer.com}}</ref> In 2014, he read out the teamsheets at [[Luton Town F.C.|Luton Town]] before the game. In 2015, he provided the voice for an award-winning satirical anti-war film by the UK branch of [[Veterans for Peace]] called ''Action Man: Battlefield Casualties''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spellingmistakescostlives.com/actionman|title=Spelling Mistakes Cost Lives /// Darren Cullen|access-date=4 January 2017}}</ref>

Since 2019, Berry has narrated adverts for [[Moneysupermarket.com]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.campaignlive.co.uk/article/moneysupermarket-promises-help-people-get-money-calm-brand-relaunch/1578248|title=Moneysupermarket promises to help people 'Get money calm' in brand relaunch|website=Campaignlive.co.uk|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref> and also voices inserts for ''[[Absolute Radio]]''.

===Music===
Berry composed all the music for ''Snuff Box'' and ''Toast of London'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/comedy/snuffbox/articles/snuffbox_music_qa.shtml |title=Matt Berry on the Music of Snuff Box |publisher=BBC |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref> as well as the music for ''[[AD/BC: A Rock Opera]]'', which he co-wrote with [[Richard Ayoade]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0440004/|title=AD/BC: A Rock Opera (TV Movie 2004)|author=Adam_Howell_511|date=21 December 2004|website=IMDb.com}}</ref> ''AD/BC'' was a half-hour parody of overblown musicals in general and ''[[Jesus Christ Superstar]]'' in particular, telling the story of the innkeeper who allowed Mary, Joseph, and the baby Jesus to sleep in his manger. ''AD/BC'' was broadcast in December 2004 and featured Fulcher and ''Mighty Boosh'' stars [[Julian Barratt]] and [[Noel Fielding]]. He also performed a song for an episode of ''Garth Marenghi's Darkplace'' titled "One Track Lover," a spoof of cheesy romantic 1980s songs.

Berry also wrote music for the [[BBC 2]] [[Steve Coogan]] comedy ''[[Saxondale]]'' and appeared in the first and third episodes of the second series of the show.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/comedy/saxondale/ |title=Saxondale |publisher=BBC |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref> He composed the theme song for the [[Channel 4]] sketch show ''Blunder''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thebloomsbury.com/event/run/1020 |title=Bloomsbury Theatre |website=Thebloomsbury.com |date=24 May 2007 |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref> He is also credited on ''[[The Peter Serafinowicz Show]]''.

In 2017, a "companion piece"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.acidjazz.co.uk/matt-berry-new-album-night-terrors-out-now/|title=Acid Jazz Records {{!}} Matt Berry New Album 'Night Terrors' Out Now|website=Acidjazz.co.uk|access-date=27 March 2018}}</ref> to ''The Small Hours'' titled ''Night Terrors'' was released, featuring remixes by artists such as [[Saint Etienne (band)|Saint Etienne]]. He stated in the first issue of [[Bearded (magazine)|''Bearded'' Magazine]] in 2007 that he was producing and collaborating on new material with 1960s soul singer [[Geno Washington]] and was to record a new album.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://beardedmagazine.co.uk/viewissue.php |title='&#39;Bearded Magazine |website=Beardedmagazine.co.uk |access-date=4 August 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090727074211/http://www.beardedmagazine.co.uk/viewissue.php |archive-date=27 July 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref> This would become ''Witchazel'', which Berry originally released as a one-day only free download in March 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.witchazelfreedownload.com/|title=Witchazel|access-date=4 January 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090315085641/http://www.witchazelfreedownload.com/|archive-date=15 March 2009}}</ref> The album was later officially released as a CD and paid-for digital download.<ref name="acidjazz.co.uk"/>

Berry has toured with a number of bands including Jonas 3 and The Maypoles.<ref>{{cite web|title=Onion Talking: Matt Berry – The Devil Inside Me|url=http://thevelvetonion.com/2013/05/10/onion-talking-matt-berry-the-devil-inside-me/|website=thevelvetonion.com|access-date=26 August 2014}}</ref> The latter comprises former [[The Bluetones|Bluetones]] singer [[Mark Morriss]] and singer/clarinet player Cecilia Fage.<ref>{{cite web|title=Matt Berry and the Maypoles, Tunbridge Wells April 4th|url=http://beautiful-life-as-i-know-it.blogspot.co.uk/2014/04/matt-berry-and-maypoles-tunbridge-wells.html|website=beautiful-life-as-i-know-it.blogspot.co.uk|access-date=26 August 2014}}</ref> Rich Fulcher has joined Berry on stage for several appearances. [[Geno Washington]] has also joined Berry onstage at London gigs for encores of the ''Snuff Box'' theme.

In October 2007, Berry provided a brand new track "Cream Pie" to ''[[Bearded (magazine)|Bearded]]'' Magazine for readers to download for free,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.beardedmagazine.co.uk/viewarticle.php?id=83 |title='&#39;Bearded Magazine |website=Beardedmagazine.co.uk |access-date=4 August 2010}}</ref> followed by a cover of the [[Blur (band)|Blur]] song "[[Leisure (album)#Track listing|Sing]]" in November 2007. "Cream Pie" is still available to supporters of [[Bearded (magazine)|Beardaid]].

Berry voiced his appreciation of the television themes of [[Ronnie Hazlehurst]] in an episode of ''[[Charlie Brooker's Screenwipe]]'', and his band played out the ''Screenwipe'' Christmas special with a rendition of Hazlehurst's theme tune for 1980s British sitcom [[Sorry! (TV series)|''Sorry!'']].

Until 2010, Berry presented a show on Absolute Radio, where he still performs various voiceovers and is described as "the voice of Absolute Radio."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.absoluteradio.co.uk/djs_shows/djs/berry.html |title=Matt Berry's Profile on Absolute Radio |website=Absoluteradio.co.uk |access-date=4 August 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090711202608/http://www.absoluteradio.co.uk/djs_shows/djs/berry.html |archive-date=11 July 2009 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> The now-defunct Matt Berry Podcast regularly featured in the Top Ten of the [[iTunes]] podcast chart, and its producer Vince Lynch was nominated for Best Online Producer at the [[Radio Independents Group#Radio Production Awards|Radio Production Awards]].

In 2012, part of the ''[[Snuff Box (TV series)|Snuff Box]]'' theme appeared briefly in the film ''[[Dredd]]'', with the line "but it was bronze" playing from a computer terminal in the Grand Hall of Justice in the latter half of the film.

In 2015, he was the opening act for [[Steven Wilson]]'s second [[Royal Albert Hall]] concert.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://stevenwilsonhq.com/sw/opening-act-for-second-royal-albert-hall-show-announced/|title=Opening act for second Royal Albert Hall show announced|date=21 April 2015|website=Steven Wilson|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref>

In July 2018, Berry released an album titled ''Television Themes''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.acidjazz.co.uk/pre-order-matt-berrys-new-album-television-themes/|title=Acid Jazz Records {{!}}   Pre-Order Matt Berry’s New Album ‘Television Themes’|website=Acidjazz.co.uk|access-date=9 August 2018}}</ref> on [[Acid Jazz Records]]. This album featured covers of famous retro TV themes such as ''[[Sorry! (TV series)|Sorry!]]'', ''[[Doctor Who]]'' and ''[[Rainbow (TV series)|Rainbow]]'', and became Berry's first UK Top 40 album chart hit.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/20899/matt-berry/|title=MATT BERRY &#124; full Official Chart History &#124; Official Charts Company|website=Officialcharts.com|access-date=15 May 2021}}</ref>

In 2019, Berry was revealed as one of the contributing artists on the latest [[The Desert Sessions|Desert Sessions]] album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/josh-homme-announces-star-studded-line-desert-sessions-volumes-11-12-2551483|title=Josh Homme announces star-studded line-up for 'Desert Sessions' volumes 11 and 12|last=Andrew Trendell|date=2019-09-26|website=NME|language=en-US|access-date=2019-09-26}}</ref>

In October 2020, Berry's album ''Phantom Birds'' (also issued by Acid Jazz) charted on the UK albums chart and eclipsed the number 38 peak of ''Television Themes'', by getting to number 31.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/search/albums/phantom-birds/|title=phantom birds &#124; full Official Chart History &#124; Official Charts Company|website=Officialcharts.com|access-date=15 May 2021}}</ref> In December 2020, he appeared in [[Gorillaz]]'s livestreamed concert ''[[Song Machine]] Live'', performing the narration for the spoken-word song "Fire Coming Out of the Monkey's Head."<ref>{{cite web |last1=Blum |first1=Jordan |title=Gorillaz Bring the Song Machine to Life with a Multimedia Extravaganza: Review |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2020/12/concert-review-gorillaz-the-song-machine/ |website=Consequence of Sound |access-date=16 December 2020}}</ref>

On 14 May 2021, Berry released his ninth studio album, ''The Blue Elephant''. Like most of his previous albums, it was distributed by Acid Jazz.
