'''Dreamers''' is an American [[pop rock music|pop rock]] trio, featuring Nick Wold, Marc Nelson (known by his surname), and Jacob Lee Wick.

==History==
===Formation and first EP (2014)===
Nick Wold moved from Seattle to New York at the age of 18 to study jazz saxophone at [[New York University]]'s [[Steinhardt School]].<ref>Mathias Rosenzweig, [https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/the-magic-lives-in-the-in-between-moments-heres-dreamers-video-waste-my-night "The Magic Lives In the In-between Moments: Here's DREAMERS Video 'Waste My Night',"] [[Noisey]], October 14, 2014.</ref><ref name="mvain">Madison Vain, [http://ew.com/article/2015/12/03/dreamers-shooting-shadows-video-premiere/ "DREAMERS 'Shooting Shadows' video premiere,"] ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'', December 3, 2015.</ref><ref>Hailey Nuthals, [http://www.nyunews.com/2016/03/21/steinhardt-alum-talks-dreamers-jazz/ "Steinhardt Alum Talks DREAMERS, Jazz,"] ''[[Washington Square News]]'', March 21, 2016.</ref> In New York, Wold was the lead singer and guitarist in the grunge band Motive, along with drummer Chris Bagamery, bassist Andrew McGovern, and lead guitarist David Leondi.  McGovern, Bagamery, and Wold were longtime friends from Seattle who grew up playing jazz together.  Wold met Leondi at NYU, and the trio became a quartet. <ref name="CBS News">{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/brooklyn-band-motive-bursts-onto-the-scene/|title=Brooklyn band Motive bursts onto the scene|website=[[CBS News]]}}</ref> <ref name="Glide">https://glidemagazine.com/42112/motive-brooklyn-via-seattle-transplants-making-sudden-impact/</ref><ref name="Swerve">{{Cite web|url=http://www.theswervemagazine.com/motive.html|title = Motive}}</ref> <ref name="emcdermott">Emily McDermott, [http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/exclusive-song-premiere-wolves-dreamers/ "Exclusive Song Premiere: 'Wolves,' Dreamers,"] ''[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]]'', July 16, 2014.</ref> Hailed as "The Strokes 2.0", Motive shared the stage with The Vaccines, Walk The Moon, Bear Hands, and were featured on MTV2's show ''120 Minutes'' with Matt Pinfield, The New York Times, NYLON guys, and CBS News.  The band toured the US several times, and had 9 showcases at SXSW in 2013.<ref name="CBS News" /><ref name="Swerve" /><ref name="Glide" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pittnews.com/article/12548/archives/motive-brings-brooklyn-sound-to-pittsburgh-venue/|title=Motive brings Brooklyn sound to Pittsburgh venue|date=31 January 2013}}</ref>

Following the dissolution of Motive, Wold was living and writing songs in a Brooklyn rehearsal space. He and Bagamery met bassist Marc Nelson (known only by his surname), and the three teamed up to form the band.<ref name="spin051315">[http://www.spin.com/2015/05/dreamers-tour-diary-photos/ "Read Dreamers' Tour Diary From Their Time on the Road With Stone Temple Pilots,"] ''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]'', May 13, 2015.</ref><ref>Madison Dettlinger, [http://www.metrotimes.com/detroit/vibing-on-the-same-influences-with-brooklyns-dreamers/Content?oid=2357222 "Vibing on the same influences with Brooklyn's Dreamers,"] ''[[Detroit Metro Times]]'', July 22, 2015.</ref><ref name="amurphy">Austin Murphy, [http://www.independent.com/news/2016/jan/15/dreamers-come-town/ "DREAMERS Come to Town,"] ''Santa Barbara Independent'', January 15, 2016.</ref>

Their first single, "Wolves (You Got Me)", was released on July 16, 2014,<ref name="emcdermott"/> and was in rotation on [[Sirius XM]]'s [[Alt Nation]].<ref name="amurphy"/> It was included on their self-titled debut EP,<ref name="mvain"/> produced by Danny Kalb, which was recorded at Ravensworth Studio outside [[Charlottesville, Virginia]], and released on November 18, 2014.<ref name="amurphy"/><ref name="cpayne">Chris Payne, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6319734/dreamers-premiere-exclusive-ep-2014-stream "Unsigned Indie Poppers Dreamers Premiere Addictive Self-Titled EP,"] ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', November 14, 2014.</ref><ref>Emily McEnroe, [http://www.papermag.com/dreamers-1649950965.html "Catching Up With 'Cosmic Rock' Band, Dreamers,"] ''[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]'', March 8, 2016.</ref> The four-track EP was streamed on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' a week ahead of its release date.<ref name="cpayne"/> In 2014, ''[[Alternative Press (music magazine)|Alternative Press]]'' named Dreamers one of their 100 Bands You Need To Know.<ref name="tsharp">Tyler Sharp, [http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/stone_temple_pilots_tap_dreamers_as_direct_support_for_spring_tour "Stone Temple Pilots tap Dreamers as direct support for spring tour,"] ''[[Alternative Press (music magazine)|Alternative Press]]'', February 24, 2015.</ref>

===''You Are Here'' and ''This Album Does Not Exist'' (2015–2016)===
In February 2015, Dreamers signed a deal with Fairfax Recordings.<ref name="nwilliams020416">Nick Williams, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/6866874/indie-pop-trio-dreamers-return-with-you-are-here-ep-exclusive "Indie Pop Trio DREAMERS Return With 'You Are Here' EP,"] ''Billboard'', February 4, 2016.</ref> Drummer Jacob Lee Wick joined the band at the end of 2015, replacing Bagamery after they relocated to Los Angeles.<ref name="egonzalez">Estefany Gonzalez, [http://thebaybridged.com/2016/08/26/dreamers-album-not-exist/ "Dreamers release 'This Album Does Not Exist' at Amoeba tonight,"] The Bay Bridged, August 26, 2016.</ref><ref name="mbell">Mike Bell, [https://calgaryherald.com/entertainment/music/brooklyn-buzz-band-dreamers-ready-to-live-up-to-the-hype "Brooklyn buzz band Dreamers ready to live up to the hype,"] ''[[Calgary Herald]]'', January 26, 2016.</ref><ref name="jniesel">Jeff Niesel, [http://www.clevescene.com/cleveland/band-of-the-week-dreamers/Content?oid=4964262 "Band of the Week: Dreamers,"] ''[[Cleveland Scene]]'', September 28, 2016.</ref> That year, the trio started recording tracks with producer Kevin Agunas for their debut LP.<ref name="mbell"/> On December 3, 2015, they released the video for their second single, "Shooting Shadows", off their second EP, ''You Are Here''.<ref name="mvain"/><ref name="achoy">Amanda Choy, [https://www.theodysseyonline.com/dreamers "Philosophy, Grunge, And NYU: An Interview With Brooklyn Band DREAMERS,"] ''Odyssey'', December 8, 2015.</ref> The song was written by Wold and [[Atlas Genius]] lead singer Keith Jeffery.<ref name="achoy"/>    On February 5, 2016, ''You Are Here'' was released on Fairfax Recordings. It included their first single, "Wolves (You Got Me)", their new single "Drugs", and three additional tracks.<ref name="nwilliams020416"/> The video for "Drugs" premiered on ''Billboard'' on March 2, 2016.<ref name="nwilliams030216">Nick Williams, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/6897285/dreamers-return-new-clip-drugs-listen "Indie Pop Trio DREAMERS Return With Phone-Smashing New Clip for 'Drugs',"] ''Billboard'', March 2, 2016.</ref>

The first single off their debut LP ''This Album Does Not Exist'' was "Sweet Disaster", released on June 24, 2016.<ref>Kris Perez, [http://theindiesound.com/dreamers-an-indie-sound-feature/ "Dreamers Talk 'Sweet Disaster,' Touring With Zella Day and Lolla,"] The Indie Sound, June 30, 2016.</ref> The album was released on August 26, 2016.<ref>Eric King, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7486814/dreamers-premiere-painkiller-single-debut-album "DREAMERS Premiere 'Painkiller' from Upcoming Debut LP Out Friday,"] ''Billboard'', August 23, 2016.</ref> Much of the album was written by Wold in Brooklyn before the band's relocation to Los Angeles.<ref name="jniesel"/>

===EPs and ''Launch Fly Land'' (2018)===
On July 13, 2018, Dreamers released ''Launch'', a four-song EP. The EP serves as the first of a trilogy of releases.<ref>{{cite web |title=DREAMERS RELEASE "LAUNCH" EP |url=http://www.thedailyshuffle.com/dreamers-release-launch-ep/ |website=thedailyshuffle.com |date=15 July 2018 |accessdate=17 July 2018}}</ref> The album cover displays a picture of a [[SpaceX]] [[Falcon 9]] at liftoff with a purple hue.

On September 7, 2018, Dreamers released ''Fly'', a five-song EP and the second in the trilogy. It features the single "All Washed Out" and a stripped-down version of the single "Screws".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Klonowski |first1=Lauren |title=DREAMERS Releases New EP FLY |url=http://www.beyondthestagemagazine.com/dreamers-releases-new-ep-fly/ |website=beyondthestagemagazine.com |accessdate=13 September 2018}}</ref>

On February 21, 2019, "Die Happy" was released as the first single from what fans assumed was a third EP. However, on April 4, 2019, the band announced that their second album, ''Launch Fly Land'', would be released April 26, 2019, which includes "Die Happy", and released the second single from the album, "Insomniac".<ref>{{cite web |last1=White |first1=Logan |title=DREAMERS announce new album + release 'Insomniac' |url=https://substreammagazine.com/2019/04/dreamers-announce-new-album-release-insomniac/ |website=substreammagazine.com |date=5 April 2019 |accessdate=5 April 2019}}</ref> 

In April 2020, Dreamers released a new rendition of their single "Heat Seeker" with vocals from [[Grandson (musician)|Grandson]]. The solo version is featured in the video game ''[[Just Dance 2021]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Rutherford |first1=Kevin |title=Dreamers and Grandson Link Up For Trippy Animated 'Heat Seeker' Video |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9355706/dreamers-grandson-heat-seeker-video |accessdate=3 August 2020 |publisher=Billboard |date=10 April 2020}}</ref>

==Performances==
Dreamers were selected to open for [[Stone Temple Pilots]] on their US tour in April and May 2015, at venues including [[The Fillmore]] in San Francisco, [[Aztec Theatre (San Antonio)|Aztec Theatre]] in San Antonio, [[House of Blues]] in Chicago, [[Irving Plaza]] in New York City and at [[SunFest]] in West Palm Beach.<ref name="spin051315"/><ref name="tsharp"/> They were picked out of 500 bands who submitted to be the opener.<ref name="treimink">Troy Reimink, [http://www.freep.com/story/entertainment/music/2015/07/22/mo-pop-band-spotlight-dreamers/30487751/ "Mo Pop band spotlight: Dreamers,"] ''[[Detroit Free Press]]'', July 22, 2015.</ref>

From December 2015 through April 2016, Dreamers went on tour across North America.<ref name="mvain"/><ref name="nwilliams030216"/> This included their first foray into Canada, where they co-headlined with [[Arkells]], joining the band onstage to perform a cover of [[George Harrison]]'s version of "[[Got My Mind Set on You]]".<ref name="mbell"/> The bands also covered [[David Bowie]]'s "[[Modern Love (song)|Modern Love]]" together,<ref>Song River, [http://www.notey.com/@yabyumwest_unofficial/external/9983530/speaking-with-song-nick-wold-of-dreamers.html "Speaking with Song: Nick Wold of Dreamers,"] Yab Yum, March 8, 2016.</ref> and Dreamers have separately covered [[Blind Melon]]'s "[[No Rain]]".<ref name="abegrand">Adrien Begrand, [http://www.popmatters.com/post/196776-dreamers-no-rain-blind-melon-cover-video-premiere/ "Dreamers – 'No Rain (Blind Melon Cover)',"] ''[[PopMatters]]'', August 26, 2015.</ref>

Dreamers have also played with [[Atlas Genius]], [[Civil Twilight (band)|Civil Twilight]], [[The Airborne Toxic Event]], [[The Mowgli's]], [[Young Rising Sons]], The Vaccines, [[Weezer]], [[Catfish and the Bottlemen]], and [[All Time Low]].<ref name="jniesel"/><ref name="treimink"/><ref name="abegrand"/> They played [[Lollapalooza]] 2016 in Chicago,<ref name="egonzalez"/> and have also put in appearances at Bumbershoot, Firefly, and Coachella along the way, and they went on a full US tour with [[The Griswolds]] in 2017 as well as with Night Riots in support of The Maine later that year.<ref>Kirsten Spruch, [http://www.baeblemusic.com/musicblog/12-8-2016/baeble-first-play-dreamers-game-tastic-video-for-sweet-disaster.html "DREAMERS' Game-tastic Video for 'Sweet Disaster',"] Baeble, December 8, 2016.</ref> In 2018, they joined New Politics, alongside The Wrecks, on their Lost In Translation tour, named after their third album. In the fall of 2018, Dreamers went on their first US headlining tour with [[Weathers (band)|Weathers]], Morgxn, and Rad Horror.<ref>{{cite web |title=Cosmic, Alt-Rock Trio DREAMERS Releases Track "Screws": Listen |url=https://vergecampus.com/2018/06/cosmic-alt-rock-trio-dreamers-releases-track-screws/ |website=vergecampus.com |accessdate=17 July 2018}}</ref>  February and March 2019 took them on a multi-city trip around the States for the North American leg of You Me At Six's international tour for their album ''VI''. In 2019, they opened for [[Dirty Heads]] and [[311 (band)|311]].
