'''Marwan Ahmed Metaweh''' ('''{{lang-ar  |مروان احمد مطاوع}}''')<ref>{{Cite web|last=Abu Taleb|first=Maan|author-link=Maan Abu Taleb|date=2016-07-27|title=|script-title=ar:معادلة صعبة فشخ {{!}} مقابلة مع داما|trans-title=A damn difficult equation {{!}} An interview with Dama|url=https://ma3azef.com/داما-مروان-بابلو-مقابلة/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=26 Jan 2021|website=Ma3azef|language=ar}}</ref> (born November 22, 1995),<ref>{{Cite news|date=15 Feb 2020|script-title=ar:مروان بابلو.. تعرف على مشواره الفني وإعلانه اعتزال الغناء بشكل مفاجئ|language=Arabic|trans-title=Marwan Pablo .. Find out his musical career and the abrupt announcement of retirement|work=Cairo24|url=https://www.cairo24.com/arts/559539/%D9%85%D8%B1%D9%88%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%A8%D9%84%D9%88-%D8%AA%D8%B9%D8%B1%D9%81-%D8%B9%D9%84%D9%89-%D9%85%D8%B4%D9%88%D8%A7%D8%B1-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%81%D9%86%D9%8A-%D9%88%D8%A5%D8%B9%D9%84%D8%A7|url-status=live}}</ref> known by his [[stage name]] '''Marwan Pablo''' and previously known as '''Dama''', is an Egyptian rapper, singer, songwriter and [[record producer]] from [[Alexandria]], [[Egypt]]. Being referred to by the [[Vice Media|VICE Media]] and himself as "Egypt's Godfather of Trap", Pablo is considered one of the most important voices of the new generation of [[Trap music|trap]]/[[Hip hop|hip-hop]] musical landscape in Egypt.<ref>{{Cite AV media|url=https://id.vodlan.com/2019/10/marwan-pablo-egypt-godfather-of-trap.html|title=Marwan Pablo: Egypt's Godfather of Trap|date=3 Oct 2019|last=|first=|type=Documentary|language=ar|publisher=[[Vice Media]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Pillault|first=Théophile|date=7 Sep 2020|title=Skrrt Skrrt Habibi! Introducing Egyptian trap|url=https://pan-african-music.com/en/skrrt-skrrt-habibi-introducing-egyptian-trap/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=PAM - Pan African Music|language=en-US}}</ref> He is most known for his hits ‘Free’, 'Ghaba', ‘El Gemeza' and 'Sindbad'.

Influenced by the emerging [[Mahraganat|electronic music in Egypt]], Pablo and other trap musicians of his age are aiming to redefine the popularity of [[New school hip hop|New School Hip Hop]] and [[Trap music]] in Egypt, taking it from [[Underground music|underground]] into [[mainstream]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hdeena|first=|date=12 Feb 2020|title=Egyptian Rapper, Marwan Pablo, Makes a Bold Statement in his newest song "Free"|url=https://hiphopafrican.com/2020/02/12/egyptian-rapper-marwan-pablo-makes-a-bold-statement-in-his-newest-song-free/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=hiphopafrican.com}}</ref>

==Career==
Marwan Pablo first started making music in 2015, being known with the [[moniker]] "Dama" at the time. At one point, he decided to disappear and rediscover himself, only to came back in late 2017 with the single 'Al Gholaf X Ozoris' under the name of "Marwan Pablo" in reference to both [[Pablo Escobar]] and [[Pablo Picasso]] as he mentions in his single 'Free'. Pablo began his career writing his own lyrics and eventually evolved into producing the tracks as well.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Zahran|first=Yassin|date=15 May 2018|title=10 Tracks to Hook you on the Egyptian Hip Hop Scene|url=https://www.scenenoise.com/Features/10-tracks-to-hook-you-on-the-egyptian-hip-hop-scene|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Scene Noise}}</ref>

[[Cairo]] has long been the city for up and coming musicians, but Marwan Pablo was trying to change that by popularizing trap music in his hometown of [[Alexandria]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Wefy|first=Hazem|date=|year=2020|title=|script-title=ar:‎مروان بابلو .. العالم السفلي وموسيقى “التراپ”|trans-title=Marwan Pablo ... the underworld and "trap" music|url=http://www.arageek.com/art/marwan-bablo|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=arageek|language=ar}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=26 Dec 2019|title=19 Rappers Who Dominated the Game in the Middle East in 2019|url=http://www.scenearabia.com/Noise/19-best-arab-rappers-hip-hop-dominating-middle-east|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=SceneArabia}}</ref> For that end, Pablo shot to fame in 2018, starring in the "Rabka" documentary and releasing his first hits 'Sindbad', 'Abu Meca' and 'Folklor' in collaboration with the rising Egyptian producer "Molotof".

===Retirement===
On February 14 of 2020, Pablo announced that, for personal and religious reasons, he will no longer be making music.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ibrahim|first=Khalid|date=2020-03-18|title=|script-title=ar:مروان بابلو يعلن اعتزاله الغناء: "أنا بتوب لربنا حاليا"|trans-title=Marwan Pablo announces his retirement from singing: "I am repenting to our Lord now"|url=https://www.youm7.com/story/2020/3/18/مروان-بابلو-يعلن-اعتزاله-الغناء-أنا-بتوب-لربنا-حاليا/4676962|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=[[Youm7]]|language=ar}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=18 Mar 2020|title="This is Not Up for Discussion": Marwan Pablo’s Out of the Game for God|url=https://www.scenenoise.com/News/this-is-not-up-for-discussion-marwan-pablos-out-of-the-game-for-god|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=SceneNoise}}</ref> Shortly after, he blanked both his [[YouTube]] channel and [[Instagram]] account.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=15 Mar 2020|title=What's Going On with Marwan Pablo? Egyptian Trap Trailblazer Deletes All Videos from His YouTube Channel|url=https://www.scenenoise.com/News/whats-going-on-with-marwan-pablo-egyptian-trap-trailblazer-deletes-all-videos-from-his-youtube-channel|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=SceneNoise}}</ref> The artist explained that the main reason he retired is "the pressure that was forced on him, the different levels of expectations that people have cornered him within, and the constant labeling". He said that he needed a break to "recharge himself without being cornered".<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=7 Jan 2021|title=|script-title=ar:من النجاح الهائل للاعتزال المفاجئ و «التوبة».. هل يعود مروان بابلو للفن مجددا؟|trans-title=From tremendous success to sudden retirement and "repentance" .. Will Marwan Pablo return to art again?|url=https://akhbarak.net/galleries/4275-%D9%85%D9%86-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%86%D8%AC%D8%A7%D8%AD-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%87%D8%A7%D8%A6%D9%84-%D9%84%D9%84%D8%A7%D8%B9%D8%AA%D8%B2%D8%A7%D9%84-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%85%D9%81%D8%A7%D8%AC%D8%A6-%D9%88-%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%AA%D9%88%D8%A8%D8%A9-%D9%87%D9%84|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Akhbarak|language=ar}}</ref> On March 9, 2020, Pablo went live on Instagram in response to rumors of his illness or death. He expressed fury over the anecdotes and denied any relation between the retirement and his health or family members.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ahmed|first=Youssef|date=14 Mar 2020|title=Marwan Pablo, Egyptian Trap "Godfather", Quits his Music Career at 24|url=https://www.egyptianmillennials.com/post/marwan-pablo-egyptian-trap-godfather-quits-his-music-career-at-24|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Egyptian Millennials|language=en}}</ref>

Despite the retirement, his ''Denamet'' (with Molotof) was the runner-up in Egypt's top 5 list as of July 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Adel|first=Alaa|date=15 Jul 2020|title=|script-title=ar:مروان بابلو يتصدر قائمة التوب 5 رغم اعتزاله|trans-title=Marwan Pablo leading the top 5 chart despite his retirement|url=https://www.albawabhnews.com/4084436|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=[[Al Bawaba]]|language=ar}}</ref>

===Return===
In January 2021, Pablo's name was trending again when news circulated of his prospective return after his profile picture on [[Facebook]] was changed. The new picture gained a hundred thousand likes in a couple of hours, but he never made an official announcement about it.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Abdel Nasser|first=Gamal|date=8 Jan 2021|title=|script-title=ar:Marwan Pablo .. اعتزل الفن خوفا من الله ورجع بدون تبرير ويستعد لأغنية جديدة|trans-title=Marwan Pablo: Quit art for fear of God; now returning and preparing for a new song, no reasons given|url=https://www.youm7.com/story/2021/1/8/Marwan-Pablo-اعتزل-الفن-خوفا-من-الله-ورجع-بدون-تبرير/5148733|url-status=live|access-date=26 Jan 2021|website=[[Youm7]]|language=ar}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Nasser|first=Nehal|date=7 Jan 2021|title=|script-title=ar:مشوار مروان بابلو من الاعتزال المفاجئ إلي العودة الغامضة|trans-title=The Journey of Marwan Pablo from Abrupt Retirement to Mysterious Return|url=https://www.filfan.com/news/128185|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=filfan|language=ar}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Abdullah|first=Sarah|date=7 Jan 2021|title=|script-title=ar:عودة بعد الاعتزال.. مروان بابلو يتصدر تويتر بعد غياب طويل|trans-title=Comeback after retirement .. Marwan Pablo topping Twitter after a long disappearance|url=https://www.elbalad.news/4647132|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Sada El Balad|language=ar}}</ref>

On February 19, a leaked video on [[Twitter]] showcasing Pablo working on a new song, preparing for his return, went viral. His name topped the [[Twitter trends|trending list on Twitter]] that day in Egypt.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-02-20|title=The Low Down on the Egyptian Rap Scene: Moussa Vs Abyu Beef Eclipsed by the Return of Pablo|url=https://scoopempire.com/egyptian-rap-scene/|access-date=2021-02-21|website=Scoop Empire|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-02-20|title=Rapper Marwan Pablo’s latest video goes viral|url=https://lomazoma.com/breaking-news/8215195.html|access-date=2021-02-21|website=لوما نيوز|language=ar}}</ref>

On February 25, Pablo returned to the music industry with the release of '[[Ghaba]]' [Jungle] and reactivating all his social media accounts. Marwan Pablo's comeback music video surpassed 4,000,000 views on [[YouTube]] within the first 24 hours after release and hit the platform's top 10 trending worldwide.<ref>{{Cite web|date=25 Feb 2021|title=Egyptian Rapper Marwan Pablo Comes Out of ‘Retirement’ with New Track and Video 'Ghaba'|url=https://www.scenenoise.com/New-Music/egyptian-rapper-marwan-pablo-comes-out-of-retirement-with-new-track-and-video-ghaba|url-status=live|access-date=2021-02-26|website=SceneNoise}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Hammal|first=Marwa|date=25 Feb 2021|script-title=ar:كليب مروان بابلو "غابة" يقترب من 2 مليون مشاهدة بعد ساعات من طرحه|language=Arabic|trans-title=Marwan Pablo's music video, Ghaba [Jungle], approaches 2m views within hours of release|work=[[Youm7]]|url=https://www.youm7.com/story/2021/2/25/%D9%83%D9%84%D9%8A%D8%A8-%D9%85%D8%B1%D9%88%D8%A7%D9%86-%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%A8%D9%84%D9%88-%D8%BA%D8%A7%D8%A8%D8%A9-%D9%8A%D9%82%D8%AA%D8%B1%D8%A8-%D9%85%D9%86-2-%D9%85%D9%84%D9%8A%D9%88%D9%86-%D9%85%D8%B4%D8%A7%D9%87%D8%AF%D8%A9-%D8%A8%D8%B9%D8%AF/5222205|url-status=live|access-date=26 Feb 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Dosoki|first=Safwat|date=25 Feb 2021|script-title=ar:مروان بابلو.. "الغابة" تعيد "بيكاسو" من الاعتزال|language=Arabic|trans-title=Marwan Pablo .. The "Jungle" brings back "Picasso" from retirement|work=Al-Ain news|url=https://al-ain.com/article/marwan-pablo-thewoods|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Nasser|first=Nehal|date=25 Feb 2021|script-title=ar:"غابة" أول أغنية لـ مروان بابلو بعد العودة من الاعتزال|trans-title="Ghaba" [Jungle] is Marwan Pablo's first track after coming from retirement|url=https://www.filfan.com/news/130017|url-status=live|website=Filfan}}</ref>

On March 29 he announced an album named 'CTRL' containing 5 singles 'Ghaba', 'El Hob Fein', 'CTRL', 'DON' and 'Atary' which was a previously released unfinished song before Pablo's retirement.

On October 1, Pablo held his first concert since his comeback in [[New Cairo]], accompanied by [[State of Palestine|Palestinian]] rap group BLTNM members [[Shabjdeed]], Al Nather and Shabmouri. The tickets were sold out and the concert was attended by over 20 thousand people. Shortly after the concert, the Egyptian Musicians' Syndicate banned Pablo from performing because of Shabjdeed's actions during the concert, which included changing the lyrics of a popular religious chant by [[Sayed Al Naqshabandi]] to welcome Pablo on stage, as well as performing songs with obscene lyrics.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.filfan.com/news/138064|website=Filfan|title=10 Scenes from Marwan Pablo's Concert at "Al Manara" .. Attendance of Over 20 Thousand People and Chanting "Mawlay" Stirred Controversey.|access-date= 3 October 2021}}</ref>
