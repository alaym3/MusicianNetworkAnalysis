'''Zsuzsanna Eva Ward''' (born June 2, 1986) is an American singer-songwriter.

Ward released her debut EP, ''Criminal'', on May 8, 2012. Her debut album, ''[[Til the Casket Drops (ZZ Ward album)|Til the Casket Drops]]'', was released on October 16, 2012. "Put The Gun Down", her first single, broke into the top 10 on the [[Adult Album Alternative|AAA]] radio chart on February 3, 2013,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thetop22.com/2013/02/the-top-22-aaa-radio-songs-2-3-13/ |title=AAA Radio Songs: 2-3-13 |publisher=The Top 22 |date=February 4, 2013 |access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref> staying there for 10 weeks, and also broke into the top 40 on the ''Billboard'' [[Alternative Songs|Alternative]] chart. On March 2, 2017, Ward released the single "The Deep" (featuring [[Joey Purp]]). Her second studio album, ''[[The Storm (ZZ Ward album)|The Storm]]'', was released on June 30, 2017, peaking at number 1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Blues Albums Chart in July 2017.

==Early life==
Ward grew up in [[Roseburg, Oregon|Roseburg]], Oregon.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thetop22.com/featured/2012/10/editors-pick-zz-ward-exclusive-video/|title=Editor's Pick: ZZ Ward Exclusive Video|date=October 21, 2012|publisher=The Top 22|access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref> In Roseburg, she joined her first band, with her father, at the age of 12.<ref>Reyan Ali, [http://www.orlandoweekly.com/orlando/blues-artist-zz-ward-stops-by-the-social/Content?oid=2244484 "Blues artist ZZ Ward stops by the Social"], ''[[Orlando Weekly]]'', May 28, 2013.</ref> Ward recalled the first song she sang was "an [[Albert King]] track called 'As the Years Go Passing By'".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fuse.tv/2013/04/zz-ward-interview?campaign=scl%7Ctwt%7Cfsc |title=How ZZ Ward Went From Playing in Bars Underage to a Kendrick Lamar Collabo – Fuse Q&A – Fuse |publisher=Fuse.tv |date=May 3, 2013 |access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref>

Ward's maternal grandmother, Zsuzsanna Friedman, was a Jewish Hungarian who converted to Catholicism to avoid persecution during the Holocaust. On discovering this, Ward, who grew up in a secular household, took an interest in reclaiming her Jewish roots.<ref>[http://jewcy.com/jewish-arts-and-culture/spotlight-on-zz-ward-soulful-jewish-singer-on-the-rise Spotlight On: ZZ Ward, Soulful Jewish Singer on the Rise], ''Jewcy'', July 18, 2013</ref>

==Career==

===Debut EP and ''Til the Casket Drops'' (2011&ndash;15)===
After moving to Los Angeles and signing to [[Evan "Kidd" Bogart|E. Kidd Bogart]]'s Boardwalk Entertainment Group, Ward began recording the ''Criminal'' EP, as well as ''Eleven Roses'', a free mixtape on which she offered her interpretations of tracks by [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[Childish Gambino]], [[Tyler, the Creator]],<ref name="VEVO">{{cite web|url=http://blog.vevo.com/sxsw-2012-zz-ward/ |title=SXSW Report: ZZ Ward Interview |publisher=vevo.com |access-date=2012-06-29}}</ref> [[Freddie Gibbs]] and [[Wiz Khalifa]].<ref name="Cragg">{{cite news |last1=Cragg |first1=Michael |title=New music: ZZ Ward – Overdue |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/mar/09/zz-ward-overdue |access-date=20 April 2019 |work=The Guardian |date=9 March 2012}}</ref> ''Eleven Roses'' features ''Better Of Dead'', ''Got It Bad'', ''Overdue'', ''Criminal'', ''Morphine'' and ''Cinnamon Stix'', it was released on 3 November 2011.<ref>{{cite web |title=ZZ Ward - Eleven Roses (Mixtape) |url=https://www.discogs.com/ZZ-Ward-Eleven-Roses-Mixtape/master/965409 |website=Discogs |access-date=20 April 2019 |language=en}}</ref> For the recording of "Criminal", she sampled the beat from "Oil Money" by Freddie Gibbs, who was so impressed by Ward's remake that he asked to contribute a guest verse to the official version on the EP. The ''Criminal'' EP was released on May 8, 2012, and features the songs "Til the Casket Drops", "Put the Gun Down", "Move Like U Stole It", and "Criminal" (feat. Freddie Gibbs).

Ward's debut album, ''[[Til the Casket Drops (ZZ Ward album)|Til the Casket Drops]]'', was released on October 16, 2012.<ref name="Beatdropping">{{cite web|url=http://www.beatdropping.com/2012/06/interview-zz-ward-talks-instagram-hip.html |title=ZZ Ward Talks Instagram, Hip-Hop, and Attitude |publisher=beatdropping.com |access-date=2012-06-29}}</ref> It includes the singles "365 Days", "Put the Gun Down" and "Last Love Song", the song "Cryin' Wolf" with Kendrick Lamar, as well as appearances from [[Ryan Tedder]], [[Ali Shaheed Muhammad]], [[Theron "Neff-U" Feemster]] and Fitz of [[Fitz and the Tantrums]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.earmilk.com/2012/10/02/zz-ward-cryin-wolf-feat-kendrick-lamar/ |title=ZZ Ward – "Cryin' Wolf" (Feat. Kendrick Lamar) |publisher=Earmilk.Com |access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref> Ward performed the album's first single, "Put The Gun Down", on [[VH1]]'s ''[[Big Morning Buzz Live]]'' with [[Carrie Keagan]] on September 19, 2012,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vh1.com/video/misc/833736/zz-ward-performs-put-the-gun-down.jhtml |title="ZZ Ward Performs 'Put The Gun Down'" by ZZ Ward &#124; Show Performance |publisher=VH1.com |date=2012-09-19 |access-date=2013-07-11 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140514021947/http://www.vh1.com/video/misc/833736/zz-ward-performs-put-the-gun-down.jhtml |archive-date=2014-05-14}}</ref> ''[[The Tonight Show with Jay Leno]]'' on November 1, 2012,<ref>{{cite news|title=USA Today Story ZZ Ward on Jay Leno|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2012/11/01/aerosmith-avett-brothers-zz-ward-late-night-tv/1674929/ | first1=Edna | last1=Gundersen|date=November 1, 2012}}</ref> and ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]'' on January 10, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vRY8lnwZqQw |title=ZZ Ward "Put the Gun Down" 01/10/13 – CONAN on TBS |publisher=YouTube |date=January 11, 2013 |access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref> In March 2013, the ''[[New York Times]]'' said of Ward, "Her energy evokes Tina Turner's, her chops Aretha Franklin's and her soul Etta James's",<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2013/02/27/booming/if-you-like-rickie-lee-jones-try-zz-ward.html?pagewanted=all&_r=0 | work=The New York Times | first=Val | last=Haller | title=If You Like Rickie Lee Jones, Try ZZ Ward | date=March 1, 2013}}</ref> and she was named one of [[Fuse TV]]'s 30 must-see artists at [[SXSW]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fuse.tv/galleries/2013/02/sxsw-2013-must-see-bands-south-by-southwest |title=30 Must-See Acts at SXSW 2013 – Photos – South by Southwest – Festivals – Fuse |publisher=Fuse.tv |date=February 28, 2013 |access-date=2013-07-11}}</ref> Ward performed her second single, "365 Days", on ''[[Good Morning America]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/GMA/video/zz-ward-performs-365-days-gma-18676156 |title=ZZ Ward Performs '365 Days' on 'GMA' &#124; ===Second EP and ''The Storm'' (2015&ndash;present)===
Ward's second EP, ''Love and War'', was released on [[Hollywood Records]] on August 28, 2015.<ref name="lmckenzie">Lesley McKenzie, [http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/decked-zz-wards-onstage-style-818220 "Decked Out: ZZ Ward's Onstage Style Evolution"]''[[The Hollywood Reporter]]'', August 28, 2015.</ref>

On March 2, 2017, Ward released the single "The Deep", featuring Chicago MC [[Joey Purp]]. The track is built around a sample of "As Long As I've Got You" by The Charmels, which is also sampled in "[[C.R.E.A.M.]]" by [[Wu-Tang Clan]]. The song is about Ward's feelings of being trapped in a bad relationship.<ref>Myles Tanzer, [http://www.thefader.com/2017/03/02/zz-ward-the-deep-with-joey-purp "ZZ Ward And Joey Purp Pull From A Classic For 'The Deep'"], ''[[The Fader]]'', March 2, 2017.</ref> The song's music video premiered on ''[[W (magazine)|W]]'' magazine's website on March 9, 2017.<ref>Katherine Cusumano, [http://www.wmagazine.com/story/zz-ward-the-deep-music-video-premiere "Exclusive Music Video Premiere: ZZ Ward's 'The Deep' Is the First Look at Her Most Personal Record Yet"], ''[[W (magazine)|W]]'', March 9, 2017.</ref> Also in 2017, Ward collaborated with [[Gary Clark Jr.]] on "Ride" from the soundtrack to the [[Pixar]] film ''[[Cars 3]]''. Ward performed "Ride" on ''[[Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 24)|Dancing with the Stars]]'' on April 17, 2017.<ref>[http://www.pixarpost.com/2017/04/cars-3-soundtrack-first-listen-zz-ward.html "Cars 3 Soundtrack First Listen: ZZ Ward & Gary Clark Jr's Energetic Track, 'Ride' and Dancing With The Stars Performance"], Pixar Post, April 14, 2017.</ref>

On February 11, 2017, Ward performed at the [[Americana Music Association]]'s tribute concert to [[Loretta Lynn]] at the [[Troubadour (West Hollywood, California)|Troubadour]] in [[West Hollywood, California]], performing a cover of "[[The Home You're Tearing Down]]".<ref>Randy Lewis, [http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-loretta-lynn-salute-americana-music-assn-20170208-story.html "Jack Ingram, Joe Henry, ZZ Ward join Americana Music Assn. salute to Loretta Lynn"], ''Los Angeles Times'', February 8, 2017.</ref><ref>Chris Willman, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7686228/kesha-joins-americana-tribute-to-loretta-lynn-grammys "Kesha Joins Americana Artists at Pre-Grammy Salute to Loretta Lynn"], ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', February 12, 2017.</ref> She performed at the inaugural Arroyo Seco Weekend festival in June 2017 at the [[Rose Bowl (stadium)|Rose Bowl]] in [[Pasadena, California]].<ref>Alex Young, [http://consequenceofsound.net/2017/03/coachella-organizers-launch-new-two-day-festival-called-arroyo-seco-weekend/ "Coachella organizers launch new two-day festival called Arroyo Seco Weekend"], ''[[Consequence of Sound]]'', March 14, 2017.</ref>

Ward's second studio album, ''[[The Storm (ZZ Ward album)|The Storm]]'', was released on June 30, 2017 through [[Hollywood Records]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.axs.com/zz-ward-s-summer-tour-includes-denver-s-bluebird-and-la-s-roxy-117524 |title=ZZ Ward's summer tour includes Denver's Bluebird and LA's Roxy |work=AXS |date=April 20, 2017 |access-date=May 22, 2017 |author=Frederick, Brittany}}</ref> It peaked at number 1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Blues Albums chart in July 2017,<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/blues-albums |title=Blues Music: Top Blues Albums Chart |magazine=Billboard |access-date=2017-07-11}}</ref> at number 12 on the ''Billboard'' Rock Albums chart,<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/431743/zz-ward/chart?f=408 |title=Rock Albums Chart |magazine=Billboard |access-date=2017-07-13}}</ref> and at number 75 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/431743/zz-ward/chart?f=305 |title=Billboard 200 |magazine=Billboard |access-date=2017-07-13}}</ref> The music video for the single "Cannonball" (featuring [[Fantastic Negrito]]) was released in July 2017.<ref>Kaitlin Reilly, [http://www.refinery29.com/2017/07/161719/zz-ward-songs-cannonball-video "Video Premiere For ZZ Ward's 'Cannonball',"] [[Refinery29]], July 6, 2017.</ref>

==Personal life==
Ward married [[record producer]] [[E. Kidd Bogart]] in May 2017.<ref>{{cite news |title=In Blues Cruise Control, ZZ Ward Revs It Up on Way to 'Little Piece of Heaven' |url=https://popmatters.com/zz-ward-2018-interview-2583906726.html |access-date=February 28, 2021 |work=PopMatters |date=July 5, 2018}}</ref> Their first child, Ezra Jack Bogart, was born on February 3, 2021.<ref>{{Cite tweet |number=1359030172985397250 |user=ZZWard |title=Ezra Jack Bogart 02/03/21 the best gift I have ever received |author=ZZ Ward}}</ref> Ward announced her pregnancy in November 2020 in the music video for her song "Giant."<ref>{{cite news |last1=Aniftos |first1=Rania |title=ZZ Ward Announces Pregnancy in Empowering 'Giant' Video: Exclusive |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9484276/zz-ward-announces-pregnancy-giant-video/ |access-date=February 28, 2021 |work=Billboard |date=November 16, 2020}}</ref>
