'''Half Moon Run''' is a Canadian [[indie rock]] band based in [[Montreal]], [[Quebec]]. The group is known for their heavy use of layered percussion, group vocal harmonies, and for playing multiple instruments during live performances.

==Career==
===2009–2013: Formation and ''Dark Eyes''===
Half Moon Run was formed in 2009 in Montreal's [[Mile End, Montreal|Mile End]] neighbourhood. Conner Molander and Dylan Phillips, who had both moved to Montreal from [[Vancouver]], connected with Devon Portieje who had moved there from [[Ottawa]].<ref name="Half Moon Run: Bio">{{cite web | url=https://www.halfmoonrun.com/bio/ | title=Half Moon Run: Bio}}</ref>

Their debut album, ''[[Dark Eyes (Half Moon Run album)|Dark Eyes]]'', was released on 27 March 2012, with "Full Circle" being released on 19 March 2012 as the lead single. The entire album was co-produced with Daniel Lagacé and Nygel Asselin.<ref>http://halfmoonrun.indica.mu/en/news/half-moon-run-will-release-their-debut-album-dark-eyes/</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.discogs.com/Half-Moon-Run-Dark-Eyes/release/3904095 | title=Half Moon Run – Dark Eyes | publisher=Discogs | access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref> They have toured Europe, Australia and North America.<ref>(14 June 2012). "Half Moon Run are indie's rising stars", ''[[Daily Mercury]]'', p. 28.</ref> Critical acclaim has included praise for their three-part harmonies.<ref>Sadleir, Rose (17 January 2013). "Trio bask in moonlight", ''[[Gold Coast Bulletin]]'', p. 5.</ref>

Shortly after the release of ''Dark Eyes'',  Isaac Symonds, originally from [[Comox, British Columbia]], joined the lineup.<ref name="Half Moon Run: Bio"/>

Throughout 2012 and 2013, the band played at [[South by Southwest]], [[Osheaga Festival|Osheaga]], [[Canadian Music Week]], [[Glastonbury Festival 2013|Glastonbury]], [[WayHome Music & Arts Festival]], and opened for artists such as [[Of Monsters and Men]], [[Metric (band)|Metric]], [[Patrick Watson (musician)|Patrick Watson]], [[City and Colour]] and [[Mumford & Sons]].<ref>Lepage, Mark (7 December 2012). "[https://montrealgazette.com/entertainment/Montreal+trio+Half+Moon+Lunar+rovers+fast+lane/7662256/story.html Montreal trio Half Moon Run: Lunar rovers in the fast lane]", ''[[The Gazette (Montreal)|The Gazette]]''. Retrieved 16 February 2013.</ref>

In August 2013, Ubisoft launched a new ''[[Assassin's Creed IV: Black Flag]]'' trailer featuring their song "Full Circle".

[[File:Half Moon Run 2013.jpg|thumb|Half Moon Run in 2013]]

===2014–2018: ''Sun Leads Me On''===
In July 2014, they announced that work on their second album had begun.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/halfmoonrun?fref=ts|website=Facebook|access-date=8 September 2014 | title= Half Moon Run facebook}}</ref> They announced that it would be released in fall 2015.<ref>http://www.halfmoonrun.com</ref> That same year, they were the recipients of the International Achievement Award at the annual Francophone [[SOCAN]] Awards in Montreal.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.socan.ca/about/awards/2014-socan-awards |title=Archived copy |access-date=26 August 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170628132104/http://www.socan.ca/about/awards/2014-socan-awards |archive-date=28 June 2017 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

On 24 July 2015 the band announced a UK and European tour, which sees them playing shows in London, Paris, Dublin, Bristol, Manchester, Leeds, Glasgow, Nottingham, Brussels, Cologne, Amsterdam and Berlin.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.halfmoonrun.com/#tourdates | title=Tour Dates}}</ref>

On 7 August 2015 the band announced, via their Twitter page, the release of their second studio album, ''[[Sun Leads Me On]]'', on 23 October 2015. They simultaneously released a teaser track, "Trust", which was available as a free download with pre-orders of their album. Pre-orders via their website offers the album signed by all band members. A deluxe version is also set to be released, with details of its track listing to be revealed closer to release.

===2019–present: ''A Blemish in the Great Light'', Symond's Departure, and ''Seasons of Change''===
On 1 November 2019, Half Moon Run released their third full-length album ''[[A Blemish in the Great Light]]''. The album won the [[Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for Adult Alternative Album of the Year|Adult Alternative Album of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2020]].<ref>Stephen Cooke, [https://www.thechronicleherald.ca/lifestyles/local-lifestyles/virtual-2020-juno-awards-includes-breakthrough-win-for-neon-dreams-467730/ "Virtual 2020 Juno Awards includes breakthrough win for Neon Dreams"]. ''[[Halifax Chronicle-Herald]]'', June 29, 2020.</ref>

On 25 May 2020, the band announced that Isaac Symonds would be leaving the group.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/half_moon_run_part_ways_with_isaac_symonds|title=Half Moon Run Part Ways with Isaac Symonds}}</ref>

Throughout May and June 2020 while the band members had been self-isolating due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], they recorded and released videos of themselves performing "isolation versions" of their songs with Symonds included. They also released seven singles, six of which were later released together as an EP entitled ''Seasons of Change'' on 17 July.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/half_moon_run_deliver_new_song_all_at_once|title=Half Moon Run Deliver New Song "All At Once"|access-date=6 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/half_moon_run_deliver_pair_of_isolation-recorded_singles | title=Half Moon Run Deliver a Pair of Isolation-Recorded Singles | access-date=6 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/half_moon_run_share_6-song_ep_season_of_change |title=Half Moon Run Share 'Seasons of Change' EP |last=Gregory |first=Allie |date=17 July 2020 |website=Exclaim! |access-date=9 August 2020 }}</ref> They followed up in September with ''The Covideo Sessions'', an EP of live versions of previously recorded songs.

On 21 August 2020, Dylan Phillips released his debut solo EP entitled ''Undercurrents''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/half_moon_runs_dylan_phillips_announces_solo_debut_ep_undercurrents |title=Half Moon Run's Dylan Phillips Announces Debut Solo EP 'Undercurrents' |last=Gregory |first=Allie |date=29 July 2020 |website=Exclaim! |access-date=9 August 2020 }}</ref>

On 18 March 2021, the band released a single "How Come My Body" in preparation for a new release.
