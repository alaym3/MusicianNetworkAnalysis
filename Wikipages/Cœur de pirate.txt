'''Béatrice Martin''' ({{IPA-fr|beatʁis maʁtɛ̃}}; born September 22, 1989), better known by her stage name '''Cœur de pirate''' ({{IPA-fr|kœʁ də piʁat|}}; French for ''Pirate's Heart''), is a Canadian singer-songwriter and pianist. A francophone from [[Montreal]], she sings mostly in French and has been credited in ''[[Montreal Mirror]]'' with "bringing ''[[chanson|la chanson française]]'' to a whole new generation of Quebec youth".<ref name="mirror">{{cite news |url=http://www.montrealmirror.com/2008/120408/music3.html |title=Ride the Wave: Coeur de pirate ends her teens on a high note |last=Leijon |first=Eric |newspaper=[[Montreal Mirror]] |date=December 4, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090309150746/http://www.montrealmirror.com/2008/120408/music3.html |archive-date=March 9, 2009 |access-date=March 20, 2013 }}</ref>

==Career==
===Early beginnings===
Born in the province of [[Quebec]], Martin started playing the piano when she was only three years old.<ref name="sud-ouest">{{cite news |title=Mathieu Chédid et Coeur de Pirate : au Cœur du Mystère des Francofolies |date=July 8, 2010 |last=Deudon |first=Benjamin |newspaper=[[Sud-Ouest (newspaper)|Sud-Ouest]]
|url=http://www.sudouest.fr/2010/07/08/au-coeur-du-mystere-134920-722.php |language=fr |access-date=March 20, 2013 }}</ref> She entered the [[Conservatoire de musique du Québec à Montréal]] at age nine and studied there for five years.<ref name="sud-ouest"/><ref name="macleans">{{cite journal |title=Canadians under 25: Here are the ones to watch |date=April 19, 2012 |journal=[[Maclean's]] |last=Ballingall |first=Alex |url=http://www.macleans.ca/2012/04/19/the-ones-to-watch-2/ |access-date=March 20, 2013 }}</ref>

At age 15, she played as a keyboardist in the [[post-hardcore]] band December Strikes First, which starred her best friend, Francis, who would later become the subject of the song "Francis" on her debut album.{{citation needed|date=April 2013}} Martin had a brief stint as keyboardist for [[Bonjour Brumaire]] from late 2007 to April 2008.<ref name="mirror"/>

=== 2007–2009: Debut album and first success ===
In a report broadcast on French television, Martin explained that she chose the name "Cœur de pirate" to appear not "as an isolated musician, but to get a band identity from the start".<ref>"Télé Matin, France 2, 13 September 2012.</ref> She initially called herself Her Pirate Heart, but translated the name to French when she stopped writing songs in English.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.indieberlin.de/music/coeur-de-pirate-live.html|title=Interview with Coeur de Pirate – a musical mix of Gondry and Jarmusch – indieberlin|date=2016-04-18|website=indieberlin|language=en-GB|access-date=2016-05-11|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190628214459/https://www.indieberlin.de/music/coeur-de-pirate-live.html|archive-date=2019-06-28|url-status=dead}}</ref> She released her debut album ''[[Cœur de pirate (album)|Cœur de pirate]]'' on September 16, 2008, on [[Dare to Care Records|Grosse Boîte]].<ref>[http://www.cbcmusic.ca/posts/17548/cohen-gould-buffy-25-best-canadian-debut-albums "25 best Canadian debut albums ever"]. ''CBC Music'', June 16, 2017.</ref> Described as "rather simple" musically, it mostly featured her voice and piano playing<ref>''88 notes pour piano solo'', [[Jean-Pierre Thiollet]], Neva Editions, 2015, p. 202. {{ISBN|978-2-3505-5192-0}}</ref> along with some minor additional instrumentation.<ref>{{cite web |last=Birchmeier |first=Jason |title=''Cœur de pirate'' review |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/coeur-de-pirate-mw0001367236 |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=March 20, 2013 }}</ref>

In February 2009, Martin attracted wider media attention when a photographer from [[Quebec City]], Francis Vachon, used the song "Ensemble" as the soundtrack to a [[viral video]] on YouTube entitled "Time lapse of a baby playing with his toys", leading to coverage on ''[[Good Morning America]]'', [[Perez Hilton]]'s website, ''[[The Globe and Mail]]'', and elsewhere.<ref>{{cite news |title=The indie It-girl and a YouTube baby |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/the-indie-it-girl-and-a-youtube-baby/article1148849/ |last=Dixon |first=Guy |newspaper=[[The Globe and Mail]] |date=February 11, 2009 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Coeur de Pirate Selling Records Thanks To Cute Baby Video |last=Lawrence |first=Grant |author-link=Grant Lawrence |url=http://music.cbc.ca/blogs/2009/2/Coeur-de-Pirate-Selling-Records-Thanks-To-Cute-Baby-Video |publisher=[[CBC Music]] |date=February 23, 2009 |access-date=March 20, 2013 }}{{Dead link|date=November 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Francis Vachon Video that made it Viral |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8vNxjwt2AqY}}</ref>

=== 2009–2011: Side projects Pearls and Armistice ===
In March 2009, Martin released a song in English, "One for Me", under the name Pearls on [[MySpace]]. In a later interview she described the project as a joke and said she did not plan to continue it, though she did not rule out the possibility of releasing music in English at a later date.<ref>Martin, Béatrice (December 8, 2009). [http://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/interviews/tlobf-interview-coeur-de-pirate-22622 Interview with Anika Mottershaw]. ''The Line of Best Fit''. Accessed on March 20, 2013.</ref>

In June 2009, she made a special appearance on [[CBC Radio]]'s ''[[Q (radio show)|Q]]'' radio show. She performed her single "Ensemble" and a new song, "Place de la République".

Martin performed with [[Jay Malinowski]] of [[Bedouin Soundclash]] in May 2010 on ''Q'' and talked about collaborating with him in the future. She was later featured in a [[Bedouin Soundclash]] song, "Brutal Hearts", on the album ''[[Light the Horizon]]''. Martin, Malinowski, and several members of the American rock band [[The Bronx (band)|The Bronx]] subsequently collaborated on a five-song EP under the band name [[Armistice (band)|Armistice]], which was released on February 15, 2011.<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/News/couer_de_pirate_bedouin_soundclashs_jay_malinowski_announce_debut_ep_as_armistice "Coeur de Pirate and Bedouin Soundclash's Jay Malinowski Announce Debut EP as Armistice"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', January 12, 2011.</ref>

Martin contributed a new song, "La Reine", to the 2010 edition of [[CBC Radio 2]]'s ''[[Great Canadian Song Quest]]''.

=== 2011 to present ===
On March 27, 2011, Martin revealed via her Facebook page that she was to return to the studio to commence work on her second album the following day. Her second album, ''[[Blonde (Cœur de pirate album)|Blonde]]'', was released on November 7, 2011, in both digital and hard copy versions.<ref>[[CBC Radio 3]], September 12, 2011.</ref> "Adieu" and "Golden Baby" were released as singles from this LP.

After announcing her pregnancy in February 2012, Martin stopped touring during the second half of the year and gave birth to a daughter in September. In early 2013, Martin returned from her hiatus with a music video for "Place de la République", the first for which she acted as director, and an international tour beginning in March.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://quebec.huffingtonpost.ca/2013/01/09/coeur-de-pirate-place-de-la-republique_n_2440918.html |title=Cœur de pirate présente le clip officiel de "Place de la République", son nouvel extrait (VIDÉO/PHOTOS) |date=January 9, 2013 |access-date=January 10, 2013}}</ref><ref name="mtv-iggy">{{cite news |title=Coeur de Pirate Returns, All Grown Up |last=Bondy |first=Halley |date=January 18, 2013 |work=[[MTV]] Iggy |url=http://www.mtviggy.com/interviews/coeur-de-pirate-returns-all-grown-up/ |access-date=March 20, 2013 }}</ref>

In 2013 she recorded music for the fifth season of the Canadian drama series ''[[Trauma (Canadian TV series)|Trauma]]'', with a soundtrack album released on January 14, 2014.<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/News/coeur_de_pirate_covers_rolling_stones_amy_winehouse_bon_iver_for_new_trauma_soundtrack "Coeur de Pirate Covers the Rolling Stones, Amy Winehouse, Bon Iver for New 'Trauma' Soundtrack"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', December 10, 2013.</ref>

In 2014 she composed the soundtrack for the [[Ubisoft Montreal]]'s video game production ''[[Child of Light]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.journaldemontreal.com/2014/01/21/cur-de-pirate-dans-un-jeu-video |title=La musique de Coeur de Pirate dans un jeu vidéo) |access-date=March 9, 2014 |language=fr |trans-title=The music of Coeur de Pirate in a video game}}</ref> The soundtrack includes a song, "Off to Sleep", sung in English.

In April 2015, she released the single "Carry On" in both English and French.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rtl.fr/culture/arts-spectacles/coeur-de-pirate-devoile-sa-nouvelle-chanson-oublie-moi-7777309580 |title=Cœur de Pirate dévoile sa nouvelle chanson, "Oublie-moi" |access-date=April 12, 2015}}</ref> Both versions of the song appeared on her album, titled ''Roses''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://exclaim.ca/Music/article/coeur_de_pirate_stops_to_smell_roses_on_new_album |title=Coeur de Pirate Stops to Smell the 'Roses' on New Album |access-date=August 7, 2015}}</ref> The music video for it was filmed in the Ruins of St Raphael's Church, South Glengarry, Ontario.

On August 12, 2015, it was announced by [[PR Newswire]] that Martin has signed a record contract with American record imprints [[Cherrytree Records|Cherrytree]] and [[Interscope Records]]. Upon signing, she made a statement on Facebook: "It feels wonderful to have Cherrytree Records/Interscope Records join the team," "When I first started listening to music seriously, my musical heroes were all on Cherrytree. I'm sure I'll feel right at home. Huge thanks to Martin Kierszenbaum for believing in me. I can't wait to discover what's in store as I embark on this new adventure."<ref name="PR Newswire">{{cite news|title=Cherrytree Records/Interscope Records Signs Coeur De Pirate (Heart Of A Pirate), Solo Project Of Award-Winning Singer-Songwriter/Pianist Beatrice Martin|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/cherrytree-recordsinterscope-records-signs-coeur-de-pirate-heart-of-a-pirate-solo-project-of-award-winning-singer-songwriterpianist-beatrice-martin-300127840.html|access-date=August 13, 2015|work=[[PR Newswire]]|agency=Interscope|publisher=PR Newswire|date=August 12, 2015}}</ref>

On June 1, 2018, she released the album ''[[En cas de tempête, ce jardin sera fermé]]'', her fourth album which also marks the tenth anniversary of her career.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/c_ur_de_pirate-en_cas_de_temp_te_ce_jardin_sera_ferm |title=Cœur de pirate En cas de tempête, ce jardin sera fermé |access-date=June 4, 2018}}</ref>

On April 30, 2021, she released ''[[Perséides (album)|Perséides]],'' her fifth album.

==Collaborations==
Martin has appeared in concert with French singer Julien Doré, performing a duet of the [[Rihanna]] hit "[[Umbrella (song)|Umbrella]]". In 2009 she recorded a new version of her song "Pour un infidèle", with Doré featured as the male vocalist. They appeared together in the video for the song, styled as a 1960s-era celebrity couple.{{citation needed|date=May 2016}}

Martin contributed vocals on "Brutal Hearts", the fourth track on the 2010 [[Bedouin Soundclash]] album ''[[Light the Horizon]]''. Frontman Jay Malinowski and Martin subsequently collaborated under the band name Armistice, releasing a five-track self-titled album on February 11, 2011. In 2010 Martin also contributed vocals to the second track of David Usher's ''Mile End Sessions'' album, titled "Everyday Things".{{citation needed|date=May 2015}}

She also appeared in [[Peter Peter (Canadian musician)|Peter Peter]]'s eponymous debut album ''Peter Peter'' in the song "Tergiversate".{{citation needed|date=May 2016}}

Martin co-wrote the song "[[Jet Lag (Simple Plan song)|Jet Lag]]" with [[Simple Plan]] and a demo was recorded with her voice. The final version was recorded with [[Marie-Mai]]. The final version is shorter.{{citation needed|date=May 2016}}

She appears on the track "Voila les anges", a cover of the song recorded originally in 1988 by Gamine on the 2012 album ''Couleurs sur Paris'' by [[Nouvelle Vague (band)|Nouvelle Vague]].{{citation needed|date=May 2016}}

Martin contributed vocals for "Peace Sign" by Canadian synth-pop artist [[Lights (musician)|Lights]], on the acoustic version of her 2011 album ''[[Siberia (Lights album)|Siberia]]'', released on April 30, 2013.<ref>Lights to release Siberia acoustic in April – http://www.moshtix.com.au/v2/news/musicnews/lights-to-release-siberia-acoustic-in-april/9454 {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304044148/http://www.moshtix.com.au/v2/news/musicnews/lights-to-release-siberia-acoustic-in-april/9454 |date=March 4, 2016 }}</ref>

Martin also contributed vocals on "Suicide Bomber" and "All This (And More)" on the album "[[Shape Shift with Me]]" by punk band "[[Against Me!]]"

==Awards==
Cœur de pirate received a 2009 [[CBC Radio 3]] "[[Bucky Awards|Bucky]]" award determined by listener votes. Her song "Comme des enfants" received the 2009 "Bucky" award for "Best Reason to Learn French".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://music.cbc.ca/#!/blogs/2009/12/THE-2009-CBC-RADIO-3-BUCKY-AWARD-WINNERS|title=CBC Music|author=CBC Music}}</ref> In 2010, Martin won the International Achievement Award at the Francophone [[SOCAN]] Awards in Montreal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.socan.ca/press-release/21st-annual-socan-awards-montreal-2010-%E2%80%93-winners-list|title=21st Annual SOCAN Awards Montreal 2010 – Winners List – SOCAN|website=www.socan.ca|access-date=June 29, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180629211201/http://www.socan.ca/press-release/21st-annual-socan-awards-montreal-2010-%E2%80%93-winners-list|archive-date=June 29, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> Martin won the 2014 ''Original Light Mix Score, New IP'' award by National Academy of Video Game Trade Reviewers for her work on the video game ''[[Child of Light]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=NAVGTR Awards (2014)|url=http://navgtr.org/archives/2445|website=National Academy of Video Game Trade Reviewers|access-date=January 27, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170322064850/http://navgtr.org/archives/2445/|archive-date=March 22, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>
