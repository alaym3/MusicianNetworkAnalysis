'''Matthew Guion Maher''' (born November 10, 1974)<ref name="jesusfreakhideout.com">{{Cite web |title=Matt Maher Discography, Matt Maher Lyrics |url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/artists/MattMaher.asp |access-date=December 3, 2014}}</ref> is a Canadian [[contemporary Christian music]] (CCM) artist, [[songwriter]], and [[worship leader]] from [[Newfoundland and Labrador|Newfoundland]], Canada, who lives in the United States. He has written and produced nine solo albums to date. Three of his albums have reached the [[Christian Albums|Top 25 Christian Albums]] [[Billboard charts|''Billboard'' chart]] and four of his singles have reached the [[Hot Christian Songs|Top 25 Christian Songs]] chart. He is a practicing [[Catholic Church|Catholic]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Jones |first=Kim |date=April 29, 2006 |title=Matt Maher Interview |url=http://christianmusic.about.com/od/interviewsmz/a/aammaher05int.htm |access-date=June 7, 2010 |publisher=About.com}}</ref> Maher has been nominated for nine Grammy Awards in his career and was awarded the [[Dove Award for Songwriter of the Year|Songwriter of the Year]] for an artist, at the 2015 [[GMA Dove Award]]s.<ref>{{Cite web |title=2015 Winners |url=http://doveawards.com/awards/2015-winners/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180927030315/http://doveawards.com/awards/2015-winners/ |archive-date=September 27, 2018 |access-date=October 22, 2015 |publisher=[[GMA Dove Award]]}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Maher was born and raised in [[Newfoundland and Labrador|Newfoundland]], Canada.  His parents recognized his musical talent, and he grew up taking piano lessons and immersing himself in a broad variety of music and playing in concert and jazz ensembles, singing in a choir, and playing in a garage rock band.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20090123092601/http://mattmahermusic.com/bio.php Bio formerly at www.mattmahermusic.com] (archived at the Internet Archive's Wayback Machine).</ref>

Maher started his post-secondary studies at [[Memorial University of Newfoundland]] and continued his studies in the Jazz Department at [[Arizona State University]]. He studied [[jazz piano]] and earned a music degree there. He paid for his first three years of college by playing piano in a hotel.{{citation needed|date=October 2017}}

Maher is married to Kristin Fisher; they live in Nashville, Tennessee, with their three sons.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Shamout|first=Omar|date=2013-05-17|title=Catholic rocker Matt Maher finds cross-over appeal among evangelicals|language=en-US|work=Washington Post|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/national/on-faith/catholic-rocker-matt-maher-finds-cross-over-appeal-among-evangelicals/2013/05/17/214c53e8-bf2b-11e2-b537-ab47f0325f7c_story.html|access-date=2021-09-18|issn=0190-8286}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-06-09|title=Musician Matt Maher talks about life, music and his Catholic faith|url=https://catholicreview.org/musician-matt-maher-talks-about-life-music-and-his-catholic-faith/|access-date=2021-09-18|website=Catholic Review|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Matt Maher - once tuned out, now troubadour for God|url=https://legatus.org/news/matt-maher-once-tuned-out-now-troubadour-for-god|access-date=2021-09-18|website=Legatus|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
His first label was the Spirit and Song, a division of [[OCP Publications]], where he released his first three collections. Early in 2007 Maher signed to [[Essential Records (Christian)|Essential Records]]. He released his first big-label album (and his fourth album overall) ''[[Empty and Beautiful]]'' on April 8, 2008. His second album with Essential Records (and his fifth album overall), ''[[Alive Again (Matt Maher album)|Alive Again]]'', was released on September 22, 2009.<ref>{{Cite web |title=New Release Tuesday.com |url=http://www.newreleasetuesday.com/coming_soon.php |access-date=December 3, 2014}}</ref> In late 2009, Maher toured with [[Michael W. Smith]], [[Meredith Andrews]] and [[Phil Stacey]] on the "New Hallelujah Tour". ''Alive Again'' peaked at No. 6 on [[Billboard magazine]]'s Top Christian Albums chart in 2009.<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1636457|pure_url=yes}} ''Alive Again'' review] at [[Allmusic]]. Retrieved April 24, 2010.</ref>

When [[Pope Benedict XVI]] visited the United States in April 2008, Maher was asked to lead worship for crowds of thousands at the Rally for Youth and Seminarians in [[Yonkers, New York]], which also featured [[Kelly Clarkson]], [[tobyMac]] and [[Third Day]], among other musicians. He was subsequently interviewed on [[Fox News]], where he played his title track from ''Empty and Beautiful''.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Homepage |url=https://www.providentlabelgroup.com/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101128123020/http://providentpress.com/artists_pr.php?id=32 |archive-date=November 28, 2010 |website=Provident Label Group}}</ref>

Maher was a guest singer songwriter speaker at Crowder's Fantastical Church Music Conference at [[Baylor University]] in late 2010 put on by the [[David Crowder Band]].<ref>[http://www.davidcrowderband.com/fantastical/] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110325200135/http://www.davidcrowderband.com/fantastical/ |date=March 25, 2011 }}</ref> In early 2011 he toured the United States on the Rock And Worship Roadshow headlined by [[MercyMe]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 30, 2011 |title=MercyMe, Jars of Clay part of The Rock and Worship Road Show in Sioux City |work=[[Argus Leader]] |url=http://www.argusleader.com/article/20110131/ENT/110131012/MercyMe-Jars-Clay-part-Rock-Worship-Road-Show-Sioux-City |access-date=February 5, 2011}}{{dead link|date=June 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

Maher performed "[[Lord, I Need You]]" before a crowd of nearly four million, including [[Pope Francis]], for [[World Youth Day 2013]] in Rio, Brazil.<ref>{{Cite web |title=WYD Flashback With Matt Maher, And His Story On How The Song Came To Be Written… |url=http://www.patheos.com/blogs/yimcatholic/2013/08/wyd-flashback-with-matt-maher-and-his-story-on-how-the-song-came-to-be-written.html |access-date=December 3, 2014 |website=Why I Am Catholic}}</ref> Other youth activities he has participated in include the 2013, 2015, and 2017 [[National Catholic Youth Conference]]s, all held in [[Indianapolis]], [[Indiana]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=NCYC Post Conference Updates . . . |url=http://www.ncyc.info/2013/index.htm |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150302114426/http://www.ncyc.info/2013/index.htm |archive-date=March 2, 2015 |access-date=April 25, 2015 |df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Keynotes and Closing Liturgy |url=http://www.ncyc.info/program/keynotes.htm |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150429181317/http://www.ncyc.info/program/keynotes.htm |archive-date=April 29, 2015 |access-date=April 25, 2015 |df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>https://twitter.com/NFCYM/status/932665859876601856{{Primary source inline|date=September 2020}}</ref> He also sang at the 2014 [[March for Life (Washington, D.C.)|March for Life]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=January 20, 2014 |title=Press Release |url=https://marchforlife.org/press-release-0/}}</ref>

On March 17, 2015, Maher released his eighth studio album, ''[[Saints and Sinners (Matt Maher album)|Saints and Sinners]]'', through Essential Records.<ref>{{Cite web |title=iTunes - Music - Saints and Sinners by Matt Maher |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/saints-and-sinners/id971421925 |access-date=May 18, 2015 |publisher=iTunes Store}}</ref> "Because He Lives (Amen)" was the first single release off of the album,{{citation needed|date=May 2015}} and additional tracks on the album include songwriting collaborations with [[Needtobreathe]] members Bo and Bear Rinehart,{{citation needed|date=May 2015}} [[Jon Foreman]] of [[Switchfoot]].{{citation needed|date=May 2015|reason=the collaborations are listed at the album, but should be listed here as well.}} In September 2017, Maher's ninth studio album, ''Echoes'', released and earned Maher his ninth career Grammy Award nomination.{{citation needed|date=February 2018}}

He is a two-time BMI Songwriter of the Year and in 2015 was named NSAI Songwriter of the Year. Along with his nine GRAMMY Award nominations, he has also been nominated for more than 20 GMA/Gospel Music Association Dove Awards in his career, including "Song of the Year", "Worship Song of the Year", "Pop Contemporary Christian Performance of the Year", and "Pop Contemporary Song of the Year".

On October 12, 2018, his song "Lord, I Need You" was certified Platinum by [[RIAA]], his first certification.{{citation needed|date=October 2018}}

His single "Alive and Breathing" reached top-5 in the Christian singles chart, in June 2020 the song started to chart on mainstream lists as well, hitting No. 3 on ''Rolling Stone'''s trending 25 list as well as No. 25 on ''Billboard'' singles of the week.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mauro |first=J.-P. |title=Matt Maher's new hit lands high on secular charts |url=https://aleteia.org/2020/06/23/matt-mahers-new-hit-lands-high-on-secular-charts/ |access-date=September 25, 2020}}</ref>
