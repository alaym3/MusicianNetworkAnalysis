'''Pär Hulkoff''', (born in 25. August 1980 in Karungi) known by his stage name '''Hulkoff''', is a Swedish musician'''.''' His first releases were as the lead singer and guitarist for [[industrial metal]] band [[Raubtier]] in 2009, and then [[country rock|country-rock]] group Bourbon Boys.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Review Bourbon Boys - Hail To The Chief 2013|url=http://www.hallowed.se/english/music/reviews/2013/bourbon_boys-hail_to_the_chief.html|access-date=2020-06-08|website=www.hallowed.se}}</ref><ref name=":0"/> Since 2017 he has been active as a solo artist, making [[Nordic folk music|Nordic folk]] influenced [[power metal]] under the name Hulkoff.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=kanismaximus|date=2019-11-09|title=Heavy Metal: Hulkoff Announce Sophomore Album|url=https://powerthorn.com/2019/11/09/hulkoff-announce-sophomore-album/|access-date=2020-06-08|website=PowerThorn|language=en}}</ref>

Hulkoff's solo sound has been compared to that of fellow power metal band [[Sabaton (band)|Sabaton]]. In fact he featured their lead singer, [[Joakim Brodén]], on his 2017 album ''Kven''.<ref name="metal-archives.com">{{Cite web|title=Hulkoff - Kven - Reviews - Encyclopaedia Metallum: The Metal Archives|url=https://www.metal-archives.com/reviews/Hulkoff/Kven/681819/#:~:text=P%C3%A4r%20Hulkoff%27s%20first%20album%20of,choruses%20are,%20well,%20hulking.&text=Needless%20to%20say,%20it%27s%20one,best%20songs%20on%20the%20album.|access-date=2020-06-08|website=www.metal-archives.com}}</ref>

He started his interest in music around age nine, and he can sing fluently in both his native Swedish and English.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=interview_hulkoff|url=http://www.metalcovenant.com/pages/interviews/interview_hulkoff.htm|access-date=2020-06-08|website=www.metalcovenant.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=September 12|last2=2018|title=Interview Hulkoff|url=https://sickanddestroy.info/2018/09/12/interview-hulkoff/|access-date=2020-06-08|website=Sick And Destroy|language=en-US}}</ref> In his life outside of music, Hulkoff is a keen hunter and outdoors man.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Anders Ekdahl|title=HULKOFF – Battle Helm|url=http://battlehelm.com/interviews/hulkoff/|access-date=2020-06-08|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

== Works and style ==
Hulkoff's original band was Raubtier. They released their first album, ''Det finns bara krig,'' in 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2010-02-09|title=Raubtier - Skivrecensioner - Sydsvenskan - Nyheter dygnet runt|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100209023126/http://sydsvenskan.se/kultur-och-nojen/musik/skivrecensioner/article421562/Raubtier.html|access-date=2020-06-08|website=web.archive.org}}</ref> He released his first country-rock album with Bourbon Boys, ''Shotguns, Trucks & Cattle'', in 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Boris|first=Martin Wasala, Michael Neber, Julia Faehndrich, Heinz Christmann, Michael Ahlsdorf, Jens Mï¿½ller, Heide Heim, Dirk|title=RNR fragt, BOURBON BOYS antworten|url=https://www.rockandroad.de/musik/country/rnr+fragt+bourbon+boys+antworten_1310.html|access-date=2020-06-08|website=www.rockandroad.de|language=de}}</ref> The album was praised for its 'fast and catchy' tunes with '[[whisky]]-soaked vocals'.<ref>{{Cite web|last=skullbone76|date=2013-02-05|title=Bourbon Boys ‘Shotguns, Trucks and Cattle’ CD Review|url=https://skullbanger.net/2013/02/05/bourbon-boys-shotguns-trucks-and-cattle-cd-review/|access-date=2020-06-08|website=Skullbanger Media|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=ROCKTIMES - CD-Review / Bourbon Boys - Shotguns, Trucks & Cattle|url=https://www.rocktimes.info/Archiv/gesamt/b/bourbon_boys/shotguns_trucks_and_cattle.html|access-date=2020-06-08|website=www.rocktimes.info}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Boris|first=Martin Wasala, Michael Neber, Julia Faehndrich, Heinz Christmann, Michael Ahlsdorf, Jens Mï¿½ller, Heide Heim, Dirk|title=BOURBON BOYS - Hail To The Chief|url=https://www.rockandroad.de/musik/country/bourbon+boys+hail+to+the+chief_1310.html|access-date=2020-06-08|website=www.rockandroad.de|language=de}}</ref>

Hulkoff's first solo record ''Kven'' was released in 2017. It received more mixed reviews, with some critics praising its 'formidable force' as 'true warrior metal'.<ref name="metal-archives.com"/> However, others noted the fairly conventional song structure and 'genre-typical melodies' in some parts.<ref name="metal-archives.com"/><ref>{{Cite web|title=HULKOFF - Kven|url=https://www.rockhard.de/reviews/hulkoff-kven_414926.html|access-date=2020-06-08|website=ROCK HARD Heavy-Metal-Magazin|language=en}}</ref> That same year, Hulkoff also contributed a country-rock song, entitled ''These Guns,'' to the soundtrack of the [[Combat flight simulation game|flight simulator]] [[video game]] ''[[Flying Tigers: Shadows Over China]].''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pär Hulkoff|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm7957374/|access-date=2020-06-11|website=IMDb}}</ref>

In early 2020 he released a new solo single, ''Ingvar'', which he revealed was from an upcoming album entitled ''Pansarfolk.''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Peterson|first=Eric|date=2020-03-06|title=Hulkoff: releases new single "Ingvar," and gives Brutal Planet Magazine the story behind the song.|url=https://brutalplanetmag.com/hulkoff-releases-new-single-ingvar-and-gives-brutal-planet-magazine-the-story-behind-the-song/|access-date=2020-06-08|website=BPM|language=en-US}}</ref> The album will be released in two editions, one with the lyrics sung in English and the other in Swedish.<ref name=":1" /> ''Pansarfolk'' was released on the 25th of September and sold out of all physical copies on pre-order alone.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=Peterson|first=Eric|date=2020-09-21|title=Brutal Planet Magazine interviews Hulkoff|url=https://brutalplanetmag.com/brutal-planet-magazine-interviews-hulkoff/|access-date=2020-10-01|website=BPM|language=en-US}}</ref> It hit number one on the Swedish heavy metal album charts during the first week of release.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Veckolista Hårdrock, vecka 40|url=https://www.sverigetopplistan.se/chart/93|access-date=2020-10-02|website=Sverigetopplistan|language=sv}}</ref> [[Anders Johansson (drummer)|Anders Johannsson]], former drummer with [[HammerFall]] and now [[Manowar|ManOWar]], features on most tracks on the album.<ref name=":2" /> It also includes more traditional Nordic instrumentation than previous Hulkoff albums, including the [[Talharpa]] and the [[Nyckelharpa]].<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":2" />

Early reviewers called ''Pansarfolk'' "fighting folk metal, full of fervour and a primal passion that comes from the woods of northern Sweden"<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=Musicalypse|first=Guest|date=2020-09-23|title=(2020) Hulkoff: Pansarfolk|url=https://www.musicalypse.net/2020-hulkoff-pansarfolk/|access-date=2020-10-01|website=Musicalypse|language=en-US}}</ref> and "like ''[[The Devil Went Down to Georgia]]'' on power metal steroids"<ref name=":2" />
