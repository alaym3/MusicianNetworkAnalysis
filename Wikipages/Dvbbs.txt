'''Dvbbs''' (stylized in all caps; pronounced "dubs") is a Canadian production duo formed in 2012, composed of brothers Christopher van den Hoef and Alexandre van den Hoef. <ref name="splummer">Sean Plummer, [http://entertainment.ca.msn.com/music/features/toronto-brothers-chris-and-alex-power-dvbbs “Toronto brothers Chris and Alex power DVBBS,”] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130625005301/http://entertainment.ca.msn.com/music/features/toronto-brothers-chris-and-alex-power-dvbbs |date=2013-06-25 }} [[MSN Entertainment|MSN Entertainment Canada]], October 5, 2012.</ref><ref>[http://blog.muchmusic.com/dubbs-shocks-the-house/ “DUBBS Shocks the House,”] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110320032251/http://blog.muchmusic.com/dubbs-shocks-the-house/ |date=2011-03-20 }} [[MuchMusic]], November 22, 2010.</ref>

==History==

===Career===
Christopher and Alexandre Van den Hoef are natives of [[Orangeville, Ontario]].<ref name="jnunziato">Jordan Nunziato, [https://web.archive.org/web/20131012004126/http://www.citizen.on.ca/news/2013-06-13/Local_News/Orangeville_natives_DVBBS_to_headline_towns_150th_.html “Orangeville natives DVBBS to headline town’s 150th celebration,”] ''Orangeville Citizen'', June 13, 2013.</ref> Their father is Dutch and migrated to Canada, and their mother is Greek Canadian. The two DJs lived in [[Athens]], [[Greece]] for two years. They are currently based in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]],<ref name="jnunziato"/> as well as  [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]], and are signed with Josh Herman at STRVCTVRE Artist Management. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.laweekly.com/josh-herman/|title=DVBBS’ Manager Josh Herman Shares Six Tips on How to Become a Successful Music Producer|date=February 12, 2020|website=LA Weekly}}</ref>

Dvbbs spent two months recording their debut extended play, ''Initio'', released in March 2012.<ref name="splummer"/><ref>Bill Tremblay, [http://www.orangeville.com/community-story/3860009-dvbbs-headlines-eclectic-birthday-bash/ “DVBBS headlines eclectic birthday bash,”] Orangeville.com, June 27, 2013.</ref> The EP features influences from a variety of genres, including house, electronic, dubstep and reggae.<ref name="calmudevar">Cristina Almudevar, [http://www.thecord.ca/who-is-dvbbs/ “Who is DVBBS?”] ''[[The Cord Weekly]]'', November 28, 2012.</ref><ref name="gbelasco">Greg Belasco, [http://www.earmilk.com/2012/03/13/dvbbs-initio-ep-exclusive/ “DVBBS – Initio,”] Earmilk, March 13, 2012.</ref>

Following their 2012 debut EP, ‘Initio,’ DVBBS gained international acclaim via their chart-topping single, “Tsunami,” in 2013. As the duo’s first-ever certified hit, the platinum-selling “Tsunami” today counts over 1 billion streams. The 2014 vocal mix of the track, “Tsunami (Jump)” featuring English rapper Tinie Tempah, topped both the UK Singles and UK Dance charts, making DVBBS one of only 13 Canadian artists to ever chart at #1 on the Official UK Singles Chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/32037/dvbbs-and-borgeous-tinie-tempah/|title=DVBBS & BORGEOUS/TINIE TEMPAH &#124; full Official Chart History &#124; Official Charts Company|website=www.officialcharts.com}}</ref> DVBBS also count 12 platinum singles <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musiccanada.com/gold-platinum/|title=Gold/Platinum|website=Music Canada}}</ref> via "Tinted Eyes", "West Coast", "IDWK", “Tsunami” and “Not Going Home,” the latter of which also received a Juno Awards nomination for Dance Recording of the Year in 2018. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/artist/5X4LWwbUFNzPkEas04uU82|title=DVBBS|website=Spotify}}</ref>

Their hit single came with "[[Tsunami (Dvbbs and Borgeous song)|Tsunami]]". It had been promoted by Dutch DJ [[Sander van Doorn]], although he denied being the producer.<ref name="zmccarthy">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/code/5657709/sander-van-doorns-tsunami-mystery-almost-revealed|author=Zel McCarthy|title=Sander van Doorn's 'Tsunami' Mystery Almost Revealed|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=August 16, 2013|access-date=January 25, 2014}}</ref> The song was confirmed by [[Pete Tong]] to be the work of Dvbbs and Borgeous when Tong played the song on his [[BBC Radio 1]] show on August 16, 2013.<ref>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b037xlkx “Avicii World Exclusive, Ray Foxx, Felix Da Housecat, DJ W!ld & Chris Malinchak,”] [[BBC Radio 1]], August 16, 2013.</ref> It was officially released on Sander Van Doorn's label Doorn Records on August 19, 2013.<ref name="zmccarthy"/><ref>[http://www.djmag.ca/2013/08/16/dvbbs-borgeous-tsunami-preview/ “DVBBS & Borgeous – Tsunami (Preview),”] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130923075736/http://www.djmag.ca/2013/08/16/dvbbs-borgeous-tsunami-preview/ |date=2013-09-23 }} ''[[DJ Magazine]]'', August 16, 2013.</ref> It also reached #1 on the [[Beatport]] 100.<ref>Sean Lewis, [http://news.beatport.com/blog/2013/08/26/dvbbs-and-borgeous-storm-the-beatport-100-with-tsunami/ “DVBBS and Borgeous storm the Beatport 100 with ‘Tsunami’,”] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130926041904/http://news.beatport.com/blog/2013/08/26/dvbbs-and-borgeous-storm-the-beatport-100-with-tsunami/ |date=2013-09-26 }} [[Beatport]], August 26, 2013.</ref> A vocal remix featuring British rapper [[Tinie Tempah]] entitled "[[Tsunami (Dvbbs and Borgeous song)#Tsunami (Jump)|Tsunami (Jump)]]" was released in Tempah's native United Kingdom in March 2014, on [[Ministry of Sound|Ministry of Sound Recordings]]. Dvbbs released their debut album, 'Sleep' on October 22nd, 2021.

===Live performances===
[[File:DVBBS @ Airbeat One 2016.jpg|thumb|DVBBS live at [[:de:Airbeat One|Airbeat One]] Festival 2016 in Germany]]
As one of the top live acts in dance music—clocking in an impressive 750+ shows in the past five years—they have performed across the world’s top festivals. Dvbbs have performed with [[Tiësto]], [[NERVO]], [[Steve Aoki]], [[Martin Garrix]], [[Showtek]], [[Sander Van Doorn]], [[DJ Snake]] and others.<ref name="jnunziato"/><ref name="gbelasco"/>  They have performed at most major music festivals.<ref>Katie Moran, [http://thedjlist.com/news/2013/08/dvbbs_borgeous_hit_tsunami/ “DVBBS & Borgeous hit us with ‘Tsunami’,”] The DJ List, August 19, 2013.</ref> The duo have been playing at mainstage [[Tomorrowland (festival)|Tomorrowland]], [[Ultra Music Festival]], and [[Creamfields]], among many others.
