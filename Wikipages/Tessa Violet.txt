'''Tessa Violet Williams''' (born March 20, 1990), previously known as '''Meekakitty''', is an American singer-songwriter, [[Video blogging|video blogger]], actress, music video director, and former model. Originally a video blogger, over the years Tessa Violet's online content became devoted to her musical career with Violet having released two studio albums, ''[[Maybe Trapped Mostly Troubled]]'' in 2014 and ''[[Bad Ideas (album)|Bad Ideas]]'' in 2019.

==Early life and career==
Violet grew up in [[Ashland, Oregon]] and performed in theatre productions while in high school. She began her YouTube channel in July 2006 and started daily vlogging in 2007 for a school project under the username Meekakitty when working in [[Hong Kong]] and [[Thailand]] as a fashion model.<ref name="nypost">{{cite web|last=Cohen|first=Stefanie|url=http://www.nypost.com/p/news/business/cashin_in_on_video_6jtumyq5sPP1veR1iOoPJL|title=Cashin' in on video|work=[[New York Post]]|date=July 25, 2010|access-date=July 2, 2011}}</ref>  However, she quit modeling by 2009. Violet's early content focused on storytelling, skits, and music videos, including fan-made music videos for popular artists such as [[Relient K]], [[Family Force 5]], and [[Mika (singer)|Mika]].{{citation needed|date=September 2021}}

After moving to [[New York City]] in 2009, Violet gained attention after winning $100,000 in a YouTube competition by receiving the most comments on her video entry.<ref name="nypost" /><ref name="prnewswire">{{cite web|title=19-Year-Old YouTube Vlogger Receives Over One Million Views and Wins $100,000 in Lashes to Riches Giveaway|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/19-year-old-youtube-vlogger-receives-over-one-million-views-and-wins-100000-in-lashes-to-riches-giveaway-80689912.html|publisher=[[PR Newswire]] / Beauty Biosciences|access-date=July 2, 2011}}</ref> In 2011, Violet was featured in fellow YouTube creator Nanalew's music video for the song "[[Sail (song)|Sail]]" by [[Awolnation]]. The video went viral and has since amassed over 370 million views.{{citation needed|date=September 2021}} On September 24, 2012, Violet appeared in the music video for "Cray Button" by [[Family Force 5]].<ref name="breathe">{{cite web|url=http://breathecast.christianpost.com/articles/4692/20120925/family-force-5-lecrae-cray-button-video-news-christian.htm|title=Family Force 5 Hits the "Cray Button" with new Video Premiere Featuring Lecrae|date=September 25, 2012|publisher=[[The Christian Post]]|format=Web|work=Breathecast|author=Merge PR|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130119145454/http://breathecast.christianpost.com/articles/4692/20120925/family-force-5-lecrae-cray-button-video-news-christian.htm|archive-date=January 19, 2013}}</ref> She also directed the music video for the group's song "Chainsaw", featuring [[Tedashii]].{{citation needed|date=September 2021}}

Since beginning to record and release music in 2013, the focus of Violet's channel has shifted to her original music. She has also dropped the moniker ''Meekakitty'', instead using her real name, Tessa Violet, across all platforms. It later became an official music channel in 2019.{{citation needed|date=September 2021}}

==Music==
Violet released her<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/maybe-trapped-mostly-troubled-mw0002653423/credits|title=Maybe Trapped Mostly Troubled|publisher=All Music|access-date=December 8, 2016}}</ref> first album ''[[Maybe Trapped Mostly Troubled]]'' on March 18, 2014. The album was produced by Seth Earnest at [[Maker Studios]], with John Zappin as A&R.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/maybe-trapped-mostly-troubled-mw0002653423/ |title=Maybe Trapped Mostly Troubled |publisher=All Music |access-date=June 15, 2015}}</ref> Despite the lack of attention from traditional media sources, the record debuted at number 10 on the [[Billboard Heatseekers]] chart and sold 5,000 copies in the first three months.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=meekakitty|author=Tessa Violet|number=449287894075998208|date=March 27, 2014|title=My album is #10 on @billboard 's Heatseekers chart!!}}</ref>{{psc|date=September}}

Violet released the lead single "Dream" from her EP ''[[Halloway (EP)|Halloway]]'' on September 16, 2016.<ref>{{Citation|last=Tessa Violet|title=Tessa Violet - Dream (official music video)|date=September 16, 2016|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-4NLarMj4xU|access-date=September 27, 2016}}</ref>{{psc|date=September 2021}} The full EP was released October 14, 2016. She released videos for all songs on the project.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Tessa-Violet-Halloway/release/9060210|title=Tessa Violet - Halloway|website=Discogs}}</ref>{{ugc|date=September 2021|certain=y}}

In 2018, Violet revealed she was working on her second album, ''[[Bad Ideas (album)|Bad Ideas]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G2aBXLbz_jM&t=315s
|title=a year of bad ideas|last=Violet|first=Tessa|date=April 22, 2018|website=YouTube |language=en|access-date=April 9, 2019}}</ref>{{psc|date=September 2021}} "Crush," the first single from the record, was released June 15, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Aje3LK9reQU|title=crush teaser|via=YouTube}}</ref>{{psc|date=September 2021}} An accompanying music video directed by Big Forest was released on YouTube on the same day.<ref name="twitter.com">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/TessaViolet/status/1007720726525140992 |title=Tweet |website=twitter.com |access-date=2020-06-25}}</ref>{{psc|date=September 2021}} As of May 2021, the music video has reached over 82 million views on YouTube. The single led to Violet being featured on YouTube's Artist On The Rise.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SiAuAJBZuGs|title=Tessa Violet - Crush (Official Music Video)|via=YouTube}}</ref>{{psc|date=September 2021}} The entire album was set to release on August 3, 2018<ref>{{cite web|url= https://indiepulsemusic.com/2018/06/19/tessa-violet-crush/|title=Tessa Violet Announces New Album, Bad Ideas To Be Released August 3rd.|last=Timmons|first=Joseph|date=June 19, 2018|website=YouTube |language=en|access-date=April 9, 2019}}</ref> before an alteration in the album's release plan. The album's title track, "Bad Ideas," was released as a single on November 30, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/TessaViolet/status/1068372110739832832|title=Tweet|first=Tessa|last=Violet|date=November 29, 2018}}</ref>{{psc|date=September 2021}} "I Like (the idea of) You" came out in May 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3122895/tessa-violet-i-like-the-idea-of-you-interview/|title=Tessa Violet Describes The Real-Life Crush That Inspired Her 'Bold, Confident' New Song|first=Madeline|last=Roth|website=MTV News}}</ref> She released ''Bad Ideas (Act One)'' in July 2019, featuring remixes of the first three singles of the project.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lofficielusa.com/music/tessa-violet-remixes-2019|title=Fire Tunes Alert: Tessa Violet Just Released Remixes to Her Last Three Singles|website=www.lofficielusa.com|date=January 18, 2021}}</ref>

On October 25, Violet released her second album ''[[Bad Ideas (album)|Bad Ideas]].'' The album contains 11 tracks, 4 of which have been released as singles (''Crush'', ''Bad'' ''Ideas'', ''I Like (the idea of) You'', and ''Games''). The song was released via the label ''T∆G MUSIC''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://floodmagazine.com/70590/premiere-tessa-violet-knows-words-aint-enough-on-her-second-albums-lead-single/|title=PREMIERE: Tessa Violet Knows "Words Ain't Enough" on Her Second Album's Lead Single|website=FLOOD}}</ref>

Violet has also been involved in numerous musical collaborations. In December 2014, Violet teamed with fellow YouTube musician Rusty Clanton to form the band People You Know. The pair released a Christmas album called ''You, Me and Christmas'', featuring four cover songs and one original song entitled "You and Christmas."{{citation needed|date=September 2021}} She has also collaborated with [[Dodie Clark]], [[Lauren Aquilina]], [[Orla Gartland]] and [[Bry (singer)|Bry]].{{citation needed|date=September 2021}} Violet was also featured on a remix of the [[I Dont Know How But They Found Me]] song "New Invention."<ref>{{Cite web|title=iDKHOW share remix version of New Invention featuring Tessa Violet|url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/idkhow-share-remix-version-of-new-invention-featuring-tessa-violet/|access-date=2021-11-19|website=Kerrang!}}</ref>

Violet cites artists such as [[Lorde]], [[Bleachers (band)|Bleachers]], [[Lily Allen]], [[Taylor Swift]], [[Cavetown (musician)|Cavetown]], and [[Julia Michaels]] among her influences.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thefullest.com/2018/08/07/why-were-infatuated-with-tessa-violet/|title=Why We're Infatuated with Tessa Violet|date=August 7, 2018|website=The Fullest}}</ref>

==Film==
Violet played the role of Tricia in the 2016 independent film ''[[The Matchbreaker]]''. Her song "Cash Cash Money" was included on the film's soundtrack.{{citation needed|date=September 2021}}

==Internet meme==
At the beginning of 2019, Tessa Violet became a meme among [[Russian nationalists]] who began to publish photos and videos of the singer with the words “Let's Go Russians!” and jokingly proclaimed Tessa "Queen of the Russian state".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lenta.ru/news/2019/01/16/russianqueen/|title=Певицу из США признали символом России и атаковали лозунгами "Русские, вперед!"|website=lenta.ru}}</ref> This culminated in a small group of Russian nationalists yelling "Let's Go Russians" during Violet's performance in Moscow in October 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://memepedia.ru/tessa-vajolet-russkie-vpered-na-koncerte-v-moskve/|title=Тесса Вайолет про мем "Русские вперед!": Я это не поддерживаю|website=memepedia.ru|date=October 30, 2019}}</ref>
