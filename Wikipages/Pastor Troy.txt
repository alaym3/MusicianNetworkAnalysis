'''Micah LeVar "Pastor" Troy''' (born November 18, 1977) is an American [[rapper]] and record producer. He is also a member of the hardcore rap group D.S.G.B. (Down South Georgia Boyz). In 2020, he made several [[homophobic]] statements against [[Lil Nas X]] that generated considerable controversy.<ref>[https://www.revolt.tv/news/2020/1/29/21113747/twitter-pastor-troy-homophobic-lil-nas-x Twitter slams Pastor Troy for homophobic comments about Lil Nas X] revolt.tv, Tamantha Gunn, 29 January 2020</ref>

==Early life==
Micah LeVar Troy was born on November 18, 1977 in [[College Park, Georgia]]. His father, Alfred Troy, is a former [[drill instructor]] turned [[pastor]].<ref name="amg">{{cite web|last=Birchmeier|first=Jason|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=pastor-troy-p366212/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Pastor Troy Biography|publisher=allmusic|access-date=November 27, 2010}}</ref>

Troy graduated from [[Creekside High School (Fairburn, Georgia)|Creekside High School]] and attended [[Paine College]] in Augusta, Georgia, before deciding to fully pursue his career in rap. His rap name comes from his last name and his father's profession as a pastor, and is also a pun on the name Castor Troy, a character from [[John Woo]]'s 1997 film ''[[Face/Off]]'', played by [[Nicolas Cage]] and [[John Travolta]].  His fourth album is titled ''[[Face Off (Pastor Troy album)|Face Off]]'' in reference to the movie.{{citation needed|date=May 2021}}

==Career==
He released his first album, ''We Ready (I Declare War)'', in 1999. To generate attention and buzz, he attacked Master P verbally on the infamous "No Mo Play in G. A."{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}

Ludacris featured Troy on his album ''[[Back for the First Time]]'' for the song "Get Off Me".<ref>Birchmeier, Jason. [{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r503490|pure_url=yes}} "Back for the First Time" - Overview]. [[allmusic]]: 2000.</ref> In addition, Pastor Troy is the front man of the rap group D.S.G.B. (Down South Georgia Boyz). DSGB originated in Augusta, Georgia.<ref>Birhchmeier, Jason. [{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p510740|pure_url=yes}} DSGB biography] at [[allmusic]]</ref> He was also featured on the collaborative album ''[[Kings of Crunk]]'' by [[Lil Jon]] in the song "Throw it Up".<ref>Henderson, Alex. [{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r604626|pure_url=yes}} "Kings of Crunk" - Overview]. [[allmusic]]: 2002.</ref> In 2002, Troy's album ''[[Universal Soldier (Pastor Troy album)|Universal Soldier]]'' became popular in the [[Southern United States|South]], especially with the song "Are We Cuttin'" featuring [[Ms. Jade]], which was also featured on the soundtrack for the action film ''xXx'' that year.<ref>Reid, Shaheem. [http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1457741/20020923/troy_pastor.jhtml Pastor Troy Reps For Georgia]. [[MTV News]]: Sept. 23, 2002.</ref> The album debuted at #13 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>D'Angelo, Joe. [http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1457889/20021002/presley_elvis.jhtml Elvis' #1s LP To Hit #1]. [[MTV News]]: Oct. 2, 2002.</ref> In 2003, Troy appeared on [[Young Jeezy]]'s 2003 album ''[[Come Shop wit' Me]]'' on the track titled "GA".<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r680424|pure_url=yes}} "Come Shop Wit' Me"] - allmusic</ref>

Troy released ''[[By Any Means Necessary (Pastor Troy album)|By Any Means Necessary]]'' in 2004. Following this album, Troy was released from his Universal contract because of creativity disputes.<ref name="amg"/> He then released ''[[Face Off, Part II]]'', which addressed some issues with Lil Scrappy and BME. In 2005, he appeared with [[Killer Mike]] on [[Chamillionaire]]'s track "Southern Takeover" off of ''[[The Sound of Revenge]].''{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}

He released three albums in 2006, starting with ''[[Stay Tru]]'', then followed by ''By Choice Or By Force'' and ''[[Atlanta 2 Memphis]]'', which is a collaboration album with Memphis rapper Criminal Manne. ''Stay Tru'' debuted at the Billboard 200 at #150, selling 6,000 copies its first week.<ref>Harris, Chris. [http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1529510/20060426/t_i_.jhtml T.I., Pink, Shakira Can't Shake Rascal Flatts From Billboard #1]. [[MTV News]]: Apr. 26, 2006.</ref>

Troy released his 16th solo album, ''[[Ready For War]]'', in June 2009, and released seven more studio albums then and 2011. In 2012, he released ''The Last OutLaw'', and in 2013, he released ''The Streets Need You''. In 2014, he released Crown Royal Part 4 mixtape, as well as the album ''Welcome to the Rap Game'', while he released the sixth edition of his Crown Royal mixtape series in 2015 along with ''WAR (We Are Ready) in Atlanta'', which featured by Paul Wall and Bun B.{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}

In 2017, Troy announced his retirement and released his last album "O.G.P.T" in July. He also announced he was in the process of completing his second movie, titled Down 2 Come Up, which he wrote, directed, and starred in, which was slated for released on March 17, 2020. He later released two albums, "Clubber Lang" and "Enemy of the State".{{Citation needed|date=May 2021}}

In January 2020, Pastor Troy made [[Homophobia|homophobic]] comments on the outfit [[Lil Nas X]] wore during the [[Grammy Award]]s.<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Daw |first1=Stephen |title=Lil Nas X Responded to A Fellow Rapper's Homophobic Comment, And It Was Perfect |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8549572/lil-nas-x-responds-pastor-troy-homophobic-instagram-post-grammys/ |magazine=Billboard |language=en}}</ref><ref>[https://www.papermag.com/lil-nas-x-pastor-troy-2644970220.html?rebelltitem=12#rebelltitem12 Lil Nas X Responds to Pastor Troy's Homophobic Post] papermag, Sandra Song, 29 January 2020</ref><ref>[https://www.ajc.com/blog/music/lil-nas-responds-with-shrug-pastor-troy-homophobic-comments/mSExucPsHYYVRaRyh8dMFN/ Lil Nas X responds with a shrug to Pastor Troy's homophobic comments] ajc.com, Melissa Ruggieri, 30 January 2020</ref> In a subsequent interview, Pastor Troy said he was not being homophobic but then claimed that "being gay isn't right" and made several other homophobic comments.<ref>[https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/pastor-troy-talks-lil-nas-x-insists-he-isnt-homophobic-but-thinks-being-gay-isnt-right-news.102525.html Pastor Troy Talks Lil Nas X, Says He Isn't Homophobic But Being Gay Isn't Right] hotnewhiphop.com Erika Maria, 31 January 2020</ref><ref>[https://www.complex.com/music/2020/02/pastor-troy-homophobic-lil-nas-x-comments-lgbtq Pastor Troy Says He's Not Homophobic Because He's Taken Photos With LGBTQ Fans] complex.com, Joshua Espinoza, 1 February 2020</ref>

== Awards ==
On April 5, 2016, Pastor Troy received the Legends of ATL Award from [[Broadcast Music, Inc.|BMI]] for his contributions to music in Atlanta.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://atlantadailyworld.com/2016/04/08/pastor-troy-presented-atl-legend-award-at-bmi-urban-showcase/|title=Pastor Troy presented 'ATL Legend Award' at BMI Urban Showcase|last=reports|first=Atlanta Daily World staff|website=Atlanta Daily World|access-date=2016-04-09|date=2016-04-08}}</ref>
