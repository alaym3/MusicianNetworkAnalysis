'''Guystone Menga''',<ref name=NYT2019-03-13/><ref name=standard2019-01-11/><ref name=guardian2019-01-01/> better known by his pseudonym '''Yxng Bane''' (pronounced "young bane"<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fishwick|first=Samuel|date=2019-01-11|title=Yxng Bane on Fifa, fashion and foot fetishes|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/culture/music/yxng-bane-interview-real-name-rihanna-a4036346.html|access-date=2021-04-12|website=www.standard.co.uk|language=en}}</ref>), is a British rapper, singer and songwriter from [[Canning Town]], [[East London]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/features/yxng-bane-interview-rihanna-dr-martens-stand-for-something-secret-shows-new-music-a7955666.html|title=Yxng Bane on his Dr. Martens collaboration, 'Rihanna' and the UK music scene|date=19 September 2017|work=[[The Independent]]|access-date=5 February 2019}}</ref> He is also known by the name '''Guystone Menga'''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Yxng Bane shares advice from Kano that's stuck with him for years|url=https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/culture/article/yxng-bane-interview|access-date=2021-01-06|website=British GQ|language=en-GB}}</ref>

==Early and personal life==
Guystone grew up in [[Custom House, Newham|Custom House]], an industrial district of [[East London]]'s [[Canning Town]] neighbourhood.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/12/22/yxng-bane-gen-f-interview|title=Yxng Bane conquered his shyness by creating flirtatious afropop|work=[[The Fader]]|date=22 December 2017|access-date=23 March 2018}}</ref> His mother is Congolese  and his father is Angolan.<ref name="OkayAfrica">{{cite web|url=http://www.okayafrica.com/yxng-bane-afro-swing-visits-africa-first-time/|title=Yxng Bane Has No Other Competition But Himself|work=[[OkayAfrica]]|date=11 January 2018|access-date=23 March 2018}}</ref> He has five siblings and attended [[Eastlea Community School]] in [[Canning Town]] and later attended [[USP College|Palmers college]] in [[Grays, Essex|Grays]], [[Essex]]. He is an avid supporter of [[Arsenal F.C.]]<ref>{{cite news |last=Fraser|first=Tomas|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/39665/1/yxng-bane-diadora-interview |title=East London rapper Yxng Bane is living his best life |magazine=[[Dazed]] |date=19 April 2018|access-date=14 March 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
Yxng Bane started his musical career in 2016, by uploading songs onto his [[SoundCloud]] account.<ref name="MTV" /> In January 2016, he released his debut EP, ''Full Moon''.<ref name="EP" /> In January 2017, Bane was featured on a remix of [[Ed Sheeran]]'s hit single "[[Shape of You]]".<ref name="MTV" /> In June 2017, he was featured on the single "[[Bestie (song)|Bestie]]" by rapper Yungen, which peaked at number 10 on the [[UK Singles Chart]].<ref name="UKchart">Peaks in the UK:

* ''Any Minute Now'' and "Gucci Mane": {{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/artist/53934/d-block-europe-and-yxng-bane/|title=D-Block Europe and Yxng Bane {{!}} full Official Chart history|publisher=Official Charts Company|access-date=28 July 2018}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Bane has cited [[Michael Jackson]], [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[PartyNextDoor]], [[50 Cent]], [[Tinie Tempah]] and [[Wizkid (musician)|Wizkid]] as his influences.<ref name="MTV">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/brand-new-for-2018/pictures/13-things-you-need-to-know-about-yxng-bane|title=13 Things You Need to Know About Yxng Bane|work=MTV|access-date=23 March 2018}}</ref> While talking about his musical style in an interview with ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', he said; "In terms of music and artistry, I'm an artist who doesn't belong to a genre. I express myself however I need to express myself. Whether I need to rap about it or sing about it, however, I need to deliver the message, it will be delivered. In terms of genre, I think – no, it's not "I think." I know I don't belong to any genre because, for example, I'll do the "[[Rihanna (Yxng Bane song)|Rihanna]]," which is a slow, [[afroswing]] vibe and then I go and do "Froze," which is more the [[Trap music (hip hop)|trap]] sound – more U.K. [[Drill music|drill]] sound. So it's more about however I need to express it rather than belonging to a genre. It's more – I'm diverse."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8014883/yxng-bane-shape-of-you-rihanna-interview|title=East London Artist Yxng Bane Introduces Himself: 'I'm An Artist Who Doesn't Belong to a Genre': Interview|work=Billboard|date=30 October 2017|access-date=22 March 2018}}</ref>
