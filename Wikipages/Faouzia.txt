'''Faouzia Ouihya''' ({{lang-ar|ŸÅŸàÿ≤Ÿäÿ© ÿ£ŸàŸäÿ≠ŸäŸâ}}, {{lang|ar-Latn|Fawziya Uwƒ´·∏•iya}}; born 5 July 2000), known [[mononymously]] as '''Faouzia''', is a [[Moroccan Canadians|Moroccan-Canadian]] singer-songwriter and musician. Born in [[Morocco]], she moved with her family to [[Canada]] at a young age. She released several singles and collaborated with many musicians on vocals and songwriting prior to releasing her debut extended play (EP), ''Stripped'', in August 2020.

== Life and career ==
===2000‚Äì2014: Early life===
Faouzia Ouihya was born in [[Casablanca]], Morocco and moved with her family at the age of one to [[Notre-Dame-de-Lourdes, Manitoba]] in [[Canada]], before settling in the rural town of [[Carman, Manitoba]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.manitobamusic.com/news/read,article/6815/on-the-rise-faouzia-is-one-up-and-comer-you-won-t-be-able-to-miss|title=On The Rise: Faouzia is One Up-and-Comer You Won't Be Able to Miss|newspaper=Manitoba Music|last=Groening|first=Jillian|date=8 July 2018|access-date=24 August 2018}}</ref><ref name="GM1">{{cite news|url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/music/at-17-morocco-born-manitoban-makes-it-big-in-the-musiccity/article36279637/|title=Manitoba's Faouzia becomes first teenager to win Nashville music competition|last=Eul|first=Alexandra|date=15 September 2017|newspaper=[[The Globe and Mail]]|access-date=24 August 2018}}</ref> She has two sisters: Samia (one of her managers) and Kenza (her photographer).<ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1349401138404192256|user=PharmacistsMB|title=The Student Spotlight for our Winter 2021 Issue of Communication features Samia Ouihya, a brilliant pharmacy student working on getting her degree while managing rising international popstar Faouzia who, has collaborated with John Legend|date=13 January 2021}}</ref> She was raised [[Muslim]] and often traveled to her native country. Faouzia said she feels "very connected to the country and the region [North Africa]. Even though I grew up in Canada, I grew up eating Moroccan food, [and] wearing Moroccan attire."<ref name=GQ/> In an interview she revealed she felt excluded as a child, saying "maybe not just fitting in is the biggest thing I've had to overcome". Her first composition was inspired by this feeling of exclusion, in which she embraced people's differences.<ref name="GM1" /> Her passion for music began at the age of four when she watched her sister Samia playing the piano, wishing she could learn how to play it.<ref name=a-list/> Faouzia began writing songs and poems when she was five years old and playing piano at the age of six. She later studied how to play guitar and violin.<ref>{{cite news|last=Leiber|first=Sarah Jae|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Faouzia-Shares-Powerful-New-Single-The-Road-20200122|title=Faouzia Shares Powerful New Single 'The Road'|website=[[BroadwayWorld]]|date=22 January 2020|access-date=8 November 2021}}</ref> She speaks fluent English, French, and Arabic.<ref name="GM1" />

===2020‚Äìpresent: ''Stripped'' and upcoming album===
In early 2020, she was invited by [[Kelly Clarkson]] to translate her song "I Dare You" to Moroccan Arabic, which was released on 16 April.<ref>{{cite web |last=Kubota |first=Samantha |title=Kelly Clarkson reveals new music coming later this month |url=https://www.today.com/popculture/kelly-clarkson-reveals-new-music-coming-later-month-t177505 |website=[[Today (American TV program)|The Today Show]] |publisher=[[NBCUniversal]] |access-date=3 April 2020 |date=3 April 2020}}</ref> About a month later, the Swedish EDM duo [[Galantis]] invited her to feature in their song "I Fly" for the soundtrack of the movie ''[[SCOOB!]]''.<ref>{{cite news|last=Watson|first=Harrison|url=https://weraveyou.com/2020/05/galantis-and-faouzia-release-track-i-fly-to-accompany-new-scooby-doo-film/|title=Galantis and Faouzia release track ‚ÄòI Fly‚Äô to accompany new Scooby Doo film|work=We Rave You|date=17 May 2020|access-date=9 November 2021}}</ref>

Faouzia released her first [[extended play]] (EP), ''Stripped'', on 6 August 2020. It features 6 stripped (acoustic version) songs, 5 of which being previously released, and one of which, "100 Bandaids", being a new track. To promote the EP, Faouzia performed the tracks live in a concert at the [[Burton Cummings Theatre]] on 20 August.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vnCi_QV2J48|title=Stripped: Live In Concert from the Burton Cummings Theatre|work=YouTube|author=Faouzia|date=20 August 2020|access-date=4 November 2021}}</ref>

On 5 November 2020, she released the single "[[Minefields (Faouzia and John Legend song)|Minefields]]" alongside American singer-songwriter [[John Legend]]. In July 2021, Faouzia revealed that she has been working on her debut [[studio album]] for a few years.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://hypebae.com/2021/7/faouzia-hero-single-music-video-moroccan-canadian-artist-interview|title=BAEwatch: Faouzia|work=Hypebae|date=5 July 2021|access-date=9 November 2021}}</ref> She released the singles "Don't Tell Me I'm Pretty", "Hero" and "Puppet".

== Artistry ==
=== Musical style ===
She described her music as "emotional" and "intense".<ref name=fashion/> Her early songwriting was heavily inspired by people she was close to. However, her later songs were more personal since she "really wanted my heart in my story."<ref name=themanilatimes/>
Faouzia possesses a potential [[coloratura mezzo-soprano]] [[Voice type|vocal range]] that spans from [[E‚ô≠ (musical note)|E‚ô≠<sub>3</sub>]] to [[G‚ôØ (musical note)|G{{sharp}}<sub>5</sub>]] in mixed voice and [[A (musical note)|A<sub>6</sub>]] in whistle tones.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://note-store.com/musicians/faouzia/|title=Faouzia - piano sheet music|website=Note-store.com|access-date=9 November 2021}}</ref> Faouzia sings mostly in [[English language]], featuring [[Arabic language|Arabic tonalities]] in her vocals.<ref name=GQ/>
