'''Coma Cinema''' was an American [[indie pop]] band that was formed by Mat Cothran in 2005. Mat began writing songs for the band during his high school days in [[Spartanburg, South Carolina|Spartanburg]], but the band was later based in [[Asheville, North Carolina]].

Coma Cinema's ''Blue Suicide'' was released in March 2011,<ref>{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r2215036|pure_url=yes}}|title=''Blue Suicide'' > Overview|author= |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=2012-03-24}}</ref> on both cassette (via Wonderbeard Tapes) and vinyl (via [[Fork and Spoon Records]]), as well as digitally on the band's website.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/forkcast/15043-hell-her-sinking-sun/ |title=Forkcast: Coma Cinema: "Hell" / "Her Sinking Sun" |publisher=Pitchfork |date= |accessdate=2011-06-30}}</ref>

==History==
Cothran began writing songs under the name 'Coma Cinema' early in his teens.<ref name="pastemagazine1">{{cite web|author=Lead photo courtesy of Mat Cothran |url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2010/11/best-of-whats-next-coma-cinema.html |title=Best of What's Next: Coma Cinema :: Music :: Features :: Paste |publisher=Pastemagazine.com |date=2010-11-26 |accessdate=2011-06-30}}</ref> After years of performing locally, Cothran released a collection of his recordings in January 2009, entitled ''Baby Prayers''.

''Baby Prayers'' caught the attention of music blogs and websites—including [[Pitchfork.com]], who posted the music video for "Flower Pills" in February 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/forkcast/13885-flower-pills/ |title=Forkcast: Coma Cinema: "Flower Pills" |publisher=Pitchfork |date= |accessdate=2011-06-30}}</ref> In June 2010, Coma Cinema followed up with their second album, ''Stoned Alone''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tracksinstacks.com/tag/coma-cinema-review/ |title=» Coma Cinema review Tracks In Stacks |publisher=Tracksinstacks.com |date=2010-07-16 |accessdate=2011-06-30 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110717082802/http://www.tracksinstacks.com/tag/coma-cinema-review/ |archive-date=2011-07-17 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Coma Cinema's third album, ''Blue Suicide'', was released in March 2011 in both digital and physical formats (Wonderbeard Tapes released cassette tapes; Fork and Spoon Records pressed the vinyl).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://alteredzones.com/tags/coma-cinema/ |title=Posts tagged Coma Cinema |publisher=Altered Zones |accessdate=2011-06-30 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707120455/http://alteredzones.com/tags/coma-cinema/ |archive-date=2011-07-07 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Two tracks off ''Blue Suicide'' have already been released in a split 7-inch [[Single (music)|single]] with the band Teen Porn through AMDISCS: "Wondering" and "Greater Vultures."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.amdiscs.com/?page_id=2306 |title=Teen Porn/Coma Cinema 7″ split – 2010 (AMDS001) &#124; AMDISCS : Futures Reserve Label |publisher=Amdiscs |accessdate=2011-06-30 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110707130422/http://www.amdiscs.com/?page_id=2306 |archive-date=2011-07-07 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Coma Cinema released their fourth record, ''Posthumous Release'', on June 11, 2013, on cassette tape through [[Orchid Tapes]] and on vinyl through Fork and Spoon Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://summertimeinhell.tumblr.com/post/44788622810/coma-cinema-posthumous-release-may-25th-2013 |title=Coma Cinema - Posthumous Release  |publisher=Coma Cinema |date= |accessdate=2013-03-07}}</ref>

Their fifth and final record, titled ''Loss Memory'', was released on December 7, 2017.
