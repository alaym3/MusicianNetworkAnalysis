'''Tariq Trotter''' (born October 3, 1973), better known as '''Black Thought''', is an American [[Rapping|rapper]], [[Acting|actor]] and the lead [[Emcee (hip hop)|MC]] of the [[Philadelphia]]-based [[hip hop music|hip hop]] group [[the Roots]], which he co-founded with drummer [[Questlove]] (Ahmir Thompson). Regarded as "one of the most skilled, incisive, and prolific rappers of his time",<ref name="allmusic">[https://www.allmusic.com/artist/black-thought-mn0000080566/biography Black Thought – Biography]. AllMusic. Accessed on August 28, 2020.</ref> he is widely lauded for his live performance skills, continuous [[multisyllabic rhymes|multisyllabic]] rhyme schemes, complex lyricism, double entendres, and politically aware lyrics. With the Roots, he is a singer and rapper on ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]],'' frequently playing games with Fallon and his guests. 

==Early life==
Black Thought was born Tarik Luqmaan Trotter, to Thomas and Cassandra Trotter, both members of the [[Nation of Islam]]. His father was murdered when Trotter was one year old, and his mother was murdered when he was in high school.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/11/01/fashion/mens-style/black-thought-the-roots-jimmy-fallon-the-deuce.html|title=The Roots' Black Thought on Philadelphia Style. And His Beard.|last=Cooper|first=Barry Michael|date=2017-11-01|work=The New York Times|access-date=2018-02-18|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> He spent time [[Tagging (graffiti)|tagging]] "DT" or "Double T" with graffiti around Philadelphia. He sold [[crack cocaine]] briefly, and was sent to live with family in [[Detroit]] for a few months in high school.<ref name=":0" /> Trotter attended the [[Philadelphia High School for Creative and Performing Arts]] and [[Millersville University]], studying [[journalism]].  In 1987, Trotter became friends with drummer [[Questlove|Ahmir "Questlove" Thompson]] and formed a drummer/MC duo, performing on the streets of Philadelphia and at talent shows.<ref name="allmusic roots">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p168531|pure_url=yes}}|title=The Roots - Biography|last=Bush|first=John|year=2008|publisher=allmusic|access-date=2009-03-07}}</ref> Trotter would subsequently spend some time as one of two MCs in the group the Square Roots: the other one was [[Malik B.]], whom Tariq met in college.<ref name="Gibson">{{cite web|url=http://www.gibson.com/allaccessfeatures.aspx?aliaspath=/zTrash/AllAccess/Gibson%20Salutes%20Black%20Histo%20(5)|title=Gibson Salutes Black History Month - The Roots|date=2007-02-14|publisher=Gibson|access-date=2009-03-07|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120926182306/http://www.gibson.com/allaccessfeatures.aspx?aliaspath=%2FzTrash%2FAllAccess%2FGibson%20Salutes%20Black%20Histo%20%285%29|archive-date=2012-09-26}}</ref> In high school, Black Thought became interested in the lessons of the [[Five-Percent Nation|Nation of Gods and Earths]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Markman |first=Rob |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1666408/rakim-paid-in-full-anniversary.jhtml |title=Rakim Calls His Longevity In Rap Game 'A Blessing' - Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV |date=2011-06-24 |access-date=2012-03-29}}</ref> {{not in ref|date=August 2014}}

==The Roots==
The Square Roots renamed themselves the Roots and released their debut album ''[[Organix (album)|Organix]]'' in 1993. The Roots signed to DGC and followed up with ''[[Do You Want More?!!!??!]]'' in 1995. Recorded without any sampling, the album was more popular among alternative music fans than those of hip hop. Around the release of the album, the Roots performed at the [[Lollapalooza]] alternative music festival and [[Montreux Jazz Festival]]. ''[[Illadelph Halflife]]'', the band's 1996 album, became its first album to chart within the top 40 spots on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] because of the successful single "What They Do".<ref name="allmusic roots"/> ''[[Things Fall Apart (album)|Things Fall Apart]]'' followed in 1999, the year the band played at the [[Woodstock 99]] concert.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1427531/19990712/costello_elvis.jhtml|title=Elvis Costello, Roots, Megadeth, Others Added To Woodstock Lineup|date=1999-07-12|publisher=MTV News|access-date=2009-03-03}}</ref>

In 2000, the Roots won the Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group for "You Got Me", with guest performances by [[Erykah Badu]] and [[Eve (entertainer)|Eve]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1424818/20000224/aguilera_christina.jhtml|title=Santana, Aguilera, The Roots Ponder Grammy Honors|date=2000-02-24|publisher=MTV News|access-date=2009-03-03}}</ref> The Roots' album ''Things Fall Apart'' was nominated for the Best Rap Album award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://archives.cnn.com/2000/SHOWBIZ/Music/01/04/grammy.noms.01/list.html |title=42nd Annual Grammy Awards nominations |date=2000-01-04 |publisher=CNN |access-date=2009-03-03 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100124112009/http://archives.cnn.com/2000/SHOWBIZ/Music/01/04/grammy.noms.01/list.html |archive-date=2010-01-24 }}</ref> For [[Jay-Z]]'s acoustic concert for the television program ''[[MTV Unplugged]]'', the Roots provided instrumentals.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1451515/20011217/jay_z.jhtml|title=Roots' Questlove Gives Backstage Access To Jay-Z Unplugged|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|date=2001-12-17|publisher=MTV News|access-date=2009-03-03}}</ref> Succeeding albums were ''[[Phrenology (album)|Phrenology]]'' (2002), ''[[The Tipping Point (The Roots album)|The Tipping Point]]'' (2004), ''[[Game Theory (album)|Game Theory]]'' (2006), ''[[Rising Down]]'' (2008), ''[[How I Got Over (album)|How I Got Over]]'' (2010), ''[[Undun]]'' (2011), and ''[[…And Then You Shoot Your Cousin]]'' (2014).

==Other work==

Black Thought recorded a solo album, to be titled ''Masterpiece Theatre'' and released in 2001,<ref name=Demby/> but the project was scrapped after he learned that the album would not count toward the Roots' contractual commitments. Most of the songs from the project appeared on the Roots' ''[[Phrenology (album)|Phrenology]]'' album.

In 2006, he began working on a collaborative project with producer [[Danger Mouse (music producer)|Danger Mouse]], titled ''Dangerous Thoughts''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=3920 |title=The Paybacks |date=2006-08-18 |work=XXL |access-date=2009-03-07 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090313053504/http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=3920 |archive-date=2009-03-13 }}</ref> As of February 2021, the album had not been released.<ref>{{cite web|author1=Patrice Evans|title=The Danger Mouse Factor: Checking in on the DJ Auteur|url=http://grantland.com/hollywood-prospectus/the-danger-mouse-factor-checking-in-on-the-dj-auteur/|website=Grantland|publisher=ESPN Internet Ventures|access-date=8 September 2014|date=16 December 2011}}</ref>

In a June 2008 interview with Brian Kayser of the website HipHopGame, Black Thought spoke of another solo project that was scheduled for release on the Razor and Tie music corporation. He stated that there would be the possibility of Questlove working on production.<ref name=HHG>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopgame.com/index2.php3?page=blackthought|title=Black Thought|last=Kayser|first=Brian|date=2008-06-29|work=HipHopGame|access-date=2009-03-07|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090310185833/http://www.hiphopgame.com/index2.php3?page=blackthought|archive-date=2009-03-10|url-status=dead}}</ref> As of September 2014, neither Black Thought nor the solo album are listed on the Razor & Tie website.<ref>{{cite web|title=Artists|url=http://www.razorandtie.com|website=Razor & Tie|publisher=Razor & Tie Direct L.L.C.|access-date=8 September 2014|date=8 September 2014}}</ref>

==Legacy==
Black Thought is "widely recognized as one of the most skilled, incisive, and prolific rappers of his time", according to [[AllMusic]] critic Andy Kellman.<ref name="allmusic"/> Henry Adaso of [[Dotdash|About.com]] praises him as "a surgeon of [[rapping|emceeing]]",<ref>[http://rap.about.com/od/toppicks/ss/Top50Emcees_3.htm 50 Greatest Rappers of All Time]. About.com. Accessed on August 28, 2020.</ref> and he is highly regarded for his [[multisyllabic rhymes]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/editorials/id.3974/title.poet-laureate-black-thoughts-all-time-top-5-verses |title=Poet Laureate: Black Thought's All-Time Top 5 Verses |website=[[HipHopDX]] |access-date=August 28, 2020}}</ref> [[internal rhyme]]s,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/black-thought-streams-of-thought-vol-1/ |title=Black Thought – Streams of Thought, Vol. 1 |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=August 28, 2020}}</ref> [[rapping#Flow|flow]],<ref>Edwards (2009), p. 133.</ref> breath control,<ref name="howtorap"/> socially conscious lyrics,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/reviews/id.3167/title.review-black-thought-delivers-goat-level-lyricism-on-streams-of-thought-vol-1 |title=Review: Black Thought Delivers GOAT Level Lyricism On "Streams Of Thought Vol. 1" |website=[[HipHopDX]] |access-date=August 28, 2020}}</ref> and live performances.<ref name="howtorap"/> Questlove stated that Black Thought's clarity and logic also distinguishes him from other MCs,<ref>Coleman (2007), p. 377.</ref> and Stephen Kearse of ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]'' notes that his "ability to use words as textures as much as tools has always been a hallmark of his style".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/black-thought-streams-of-thought-vol-3-cane-and-abel-ep/ |title=Streams of Thought, Vol. 3: Cane & Able EP |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=November 14, 2020}}</ref> In the book ''[[How to Rap]]'', [[Kool G Rap]] described seeing the Roots perform his song "Men at Work" at a show; "I never really liked to perform it that much—because your breath control gotta be crazy. But somebody sent me a clip of the Roots &nbsp;... and [Black Thought] did all three fucking verses and I couldn't believe it—he killed that shit".<ref name="howtorap">Edwards (2009), p. 290.</ref> Black Thought has influenced several hip hop artists, including [[Logic (rapper)|Logic]],<ref>{{cite web|last=DelaCuesta|first=JP|title=Logic: Enter the Mind of the Maryland Spitter|url=http://allhiphop.com/2012/02/09/logic-enter-the-mind-of-the-maryland-spitter/|work=AllHipHop|access-date=August 28, 2020}}</ref> [[Joey Badass]],<ref>[https://www.allmusic.com/artist/joey-bada-mn0002960059/biography Joey Bada$$ – Biography]. Accessed on August 28, 2020.</ref> [[k-os]],<ref>[https://www.ionmagazine.ca/music/interviews/k-os-amalgamated-chaos K-OS – Amalgamated Chaos] ''ION Magazine''. Accessed on August 28, 2020.</ref> and [[Shad (rapper)|Shad]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cultmtl.com/2013/05/canadian-hip-hop-shad/|title=Shad raps about school, spirit & inspiration|website=CultMTL.com|access-date=August 28, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Black Thought married his wife Michelle in 2010.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Porter |first1=Lauren |title=Black Thought On His Beard |url=https://www.essence.com/celebrity/roots-black-thought-beards-wife-opinion/ |access-date=8 October 2018 |work=Essence |date=28 November 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Black Thought |url=https://www.biography.com/people/black-thought-5182017 |website=Biography |access-date=8 October 2018 |language=en-us |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181008214140/https://www.biography.com/people/black-thought-5182017 |archive-date=8 October 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He has four sons and one daughter.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bartos |first1=Adrian |last2=Garcia |first2=Robert |title=The Roots' Black Thought On How Art Saved His Life |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/transcript/transcript.php?storyId=648742732 |access-date=8 October 2018 |work=NPR.org |date=19 September 2018 |language=en}}</ref>
