'''Quadron''' is a [[Los Angeles]]-based [[Danish people|Danish]] [[Musical ensemble|duo]] consisting of singer [[Coco O.|Coco&nbsp;O.]] and musician/producer [[Robin Hannibal]]. The group considers their genre "[[Electronic music|electronic]] [[Soul music|soul]] music".

The self-titled debut album ''Quadron'' was released in late July 2009. ''[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] Magazine'' named Quadron "Best of What's Next" in August 2010,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2010/08/best-of-whats-next-quadron.html|title=Best of What's Next: Quadron|date=2010-08-26|publisher=Pastemagazine.com|access-date=2010-12-14}}</ref> and ''[[New York (magazine)|New York]]'' Magazine named Quadron's self-titled album number 7 in their top 10 best albums of the year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nymag.com/arts/cultureawards/2010/69902/|title=Nitsuh Abebe's Top 10 Pop Albums of 2010 – The 2010 Culture Awards – New York Magazine|last=Abebe|first=Nitsuh|date=2010-12-05|publisher=Nymag.com|access-date=2010-12-14}}</ref> The duo released their second studio album ''Avalanche'' in 2013.

== History ==
In 2009, Hannibal and [[Coco O.]] of electronica collective Boom Clap Bachelors formed the group Quadron.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2698978/robin-hannibal-quadron-avalanche/|title=Get to Know Quadron and Rhye's Robin Hannibal|last=Reeves|first=Mosi|website=MTV News|language=en|access-date=2020-04-25}}</ref> The name of the band refers to the multi-racial heritage of the band members (from the term "[[quadroon]]").<ref name=":0" /> Both Hannibal and Coco O. have one biracial parent.<ref name=":0" />

Quadron's first professional project, released 2009, was a collection of seven songs on the soundtrack for the [[Hella Joof]] movie, ''Hush Little Baby''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm3468551/filmogenre|title=Most Popular Titles With Quadron|work=IMDb|access-date=20 June 2015}} {{unreliable?|date=April 2020}}</ref> including the film's theme song "Slippin'". Their music gained wider prominence in the United States due to Hannibal's connections to US-based music industry professionals. They re-located to Los Angeles in 2010.<ref name=":0" />

The duo released their eponymous debut album under [[Plug Research]] in 2010. Their second album, ''[[Avalanche (Quadron album)|Avalanche]]'', was released in June 2013.<ref name=":0" />

In 2011, Quadron contributed the track "Samba De Verão" to the [[Red Hot Organization|Red Hot Organizations]] charitable album ''[[Red Hot Organization#Red Hot .2B Rio 2|Red Hot+Rio 2]]''. The album is a follow-up to the 1996 ''[[Red Hot + Rio]]''. Proceeds from the sales were donated to raise awareness and money to fight HIV/AIDS and related health and social issues. That year they also collaborated with American DJ and record producer [[Kaskade]] for the song "Waste Love" off his album ''[[Fire & Ice (Kaskade album)|Fire & Ice]]''.

In 2014 Quadron was booked as the opening act on [[Mayer Hawthorne]]'s U.S. tour.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Josephs|first1=Brian|url=http://theboombox.com/quadron-tour-dates-mayer-hawthorne/|title=Quadron Announce U.S. Tour Dates With Mayer Hawthorne|date=3 December 2013|work=The Boombox}}</ref>

[[File:Coco_O.jpg|thumb|right|Coco O. in 2010]]

*'''[[Coco O.|Cecilie Maja Hastrup Karshø]]'''<ref>{{Cite news|last=Lester|first=Paul|date=2014-03-13|title=Quadron: we partied with Prince and left Pharrell off our album|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/mar/13/quadron-avalanche-neo-soul-duo-party-with-prince-pharrell|access-date=2021-01-11|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> (known professionally as "'''Coco O.'''") was a member of the Boom Clap Bachelors prior to joining Quadron. She has contributed vocals to films such as [[Baz Luhrmann]]'s [[The Great Gatsby (2013 film)|adaptation]] of [[F. Scott Fitzgerald]]'s  ''[[The Great Gatsby]]'', produced by [[Jay-Z]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.indiewire.com/theplaylist/the-great-gatsby-soundtrack-features-new-songs-by-jay-z-lana-del-rey-jack-white-bryan-ferry-more-20130404|title='The Great Gatsby' Soundtrack Features New Songs By Jay-Z, Lana Del Rey, Jack White, Fergie & More|last=Jagernauth|first=Kevin|publisher=IndieWire}}</ref> She has also been a featured vocalist for French DJ/producer [[Cedric Gervais]]'s 2014 single "Through the Night", the track "Business Casual" on the album ''Mr. Finish Line'' by [[Vulfpeck]] released in 2017, and the track "Don't Want It to Be Over" on the 2018 album ''Inside Voice'' by [[Joey Dosik]].
