'''Ellen McLain''' is an American voice actress. She is best known for providing the voice of [[GLaDOS]], the primary antagonist of the ''[[Portal (series)|Portal]]'' video game series, the [[Combine (Half-Life)#Overwatch|Combine Overwatch]] in ''[[Half-Life 2]]'', and the Administrator, the announcer in ''[[Team Fortress 2]]''. Her voice roles also include the Jaeger [[Artificial intelligence|A.I.]] in ''[[Pacific Rim (film)|Pacific Rim]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kotaku.com/how-glad0s-is-glad0s-in-pacific-rim-pretty-glad0s-636252650|title=How GLaDOS is GLaDOS in Pacific Rim?|last=Hamilton|first=Kirk|date=July 1, 2013|website=Kotaku|access-date=April 19, 2014}}</ref> and The Witch in ''[[Left 4 Dead]]'' and ''[[Left 4 Dead 2]]''.

== Career ==
McLain provides voices for many characters in several video games from [[Valve Corporation|Valve]]. Among them are [[GLaDOS]], the primary antagonist of the ''[[Portal (series)|Portal]]'' video game series (for which she won an [[Academy of Interactive Arts & Sciences|AIAS]] [[D.I.C.E. Awards|Interactive Achievement Award for Outstanding Achievement in Character Performance]]), the announcer in ''[[Team Fortress 2]]''; and the voice of the [[Combine Overwatch]] for the ''[[Half-Life 2]]'' series.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://xbox360.ign.com/articles/831/831900p1.html|title=GLaDOS Speaks|first=Dameon|last=Hatfield|date=October 3, 2007|access-date=January 24, 2010|publisher=IGN}}</ref>

McLain sang "[[Still Alive]]" and "[[Music of Portal 2#"Want You Gone"|Want You Gone]]", the ending credits songs to ''Portal'' and ''Portal 2'', respectively, both of which were written by [[Jonathan Coulton]].<ref name="stillalive">{{cite web|title=Game Music Showdown: Mirror's Edge Vs. Portal|url=http://music.ign.com/articles/922/922638p2.html|publisher=IGN|access-date=August 25, 2009|date=October 2, 2008|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091221091929/http://music.ign.com/articles/922/922638p2.html|archive-date=December 21, 2009}}</ref> She sang "[[Music of Portal 2#Songs to Test By|Cara Mia Addio]]" at the end of ''Portal 2''. It is sometimes called the "Turret Opera".<ref name="Portal 2 Turret Opera">{{cite web|title=Portal 2 Turret Opera|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_kPyGvqNn4Y|publisher=[[Valve Corporation|Valve]]|access-date=June 15, 2011|date=April 1, 2011}}</ref> In December 2011, McLain won the [[Spike Video Game Awards|Spike Video Game Award]] in the category "Best Performance by a Human Female" for her voice acting as [[GLaDOS]] in ''Portal 2''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spike.com/articles/6aoa62/vgx-2013-every-vga-winner-from-years-past|title=EVERY VGA WINNER FROM YEARS PAST|author=Video Game Awards|publisher=SPIKE|date=December 1, 2011|access-date=February 5, 2017}}</ref>

In 2013, she lent her public support to gathering donations for the [[Kickstarter]]-funded LGBT gaming convention [[GaymerX]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Polansky|first1=Lana|title=Ellen McLain Summons GLaDOS to Voice Support for GaymerCon|url=http://www.gameranx.com/updates/id/8191/article/ellen-mclain-summons-glados-to-voice-support-for-gaymercon|website=Gameranx|access-date=September 12, 2014}}</ref> She later was a special guest at the convention in August 2013. In 2014, she portrayed Fairy Godmother in the online video series ''[Wish It Inc.]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3572838/|title=Wish It Inc. (2014) – IMDb}}</ref> In 2015, she portrayed Lisa Clarke in the gay independent film, ''Winning Dad''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3030580/|title=Winning Dad (2015) – IMDb}}</ref> This film was also promoted on McLain's YouTube channel.

McLain has played roles in many radio dramas on the radio program ''[[Imagination Theatre]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://mailchi.mp/4e681ca8808f/video-games-and-audio-dramas-meet-at-imagination-theater |website= Imagination Theatre |title=Video Games and Audio Dramas Meet at Imagination Theater |date=2020 |access-date= March 10, 2020}}</ref>

In 2021, McLain provided the voice of GLaDOS non-canonically, singing a verse in the Chalkeaters’ song “Count to Three” as a cameo.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=2021-03-29|title=Gabe Newell Makes Cameo In Valve Comedy Song "Count To Three"|url=https://www.thegamer.com/gabe-newell-valve-comedy-song-glados/|access-date=2021-03-31|website=TheGamer|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
McLain has been married to fellow voice actor [[John Patrick Lowrie]] since 1986.<ref name="Married to John Patrick Lowrie">{{cite web|title=Married to John Patrick Lowrie|url=http://johnpatricklowriebooks.com/about-john|access-date=October 7, 2016}}</ref> McLain now spends her days as a voice instructor, teaching at local community and children's theaters.{{citation needed|date=June 2021}}
