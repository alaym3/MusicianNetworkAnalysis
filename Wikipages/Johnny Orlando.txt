'''John Vincent Orlando''' (born January 24, 2003) or better known by his alias Johnny, is a Canadian singer-songwriter, vlogger and actor. Orlando first received attention on social media by posting covers of pop songs by artists such as [[Austin Mahone]], [[Taylor Swift]], [[Rihanna]], [[Justin Bieber]], and [[Shawn Mendes]] to his YouTube channel.<ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/johnny-orlando-singer-songwriter-toronto-1.4674253 "Johnny Orlando, Toronto teen signed to major label, ready to take centre stage"]. [[CBC News]], May 23, 2018.</ref> In 2019, Orlando was nominated for the [[Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for Breakthrough Artist of the Year|Breakthrough Artist of the Year]]. He has also won an [[MTV Europe Music Award]] for [[MTV Europe Music Award for Best Canadian Act|Best Canadian Act]] thrice, in 2019<ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/shawn-mendes-and-the-weeknd-lead-the-2019-juno-nominations-1.4992669 "Shawn Mendes and the Weeknd lead the 2019 Juno nominations" CBC Music, January 29, 2019]</ref> 2020, and 2021.

He signed with [[Universal Music Canada]] on May 18, 2018,<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://variety.com/2018/music/news/johnny-orlando-signs-record-deal-universal-music-canada-1202814088/|title=Johnny Orlando, YouTube and Social Media Star, Signs With Universal Music Canada|magazine=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|date=May 18, 2018|access-date=January 9, 2019}}</ref> and has released singles such as "Day and Night" and "What If" (both featuring [[Mackenzie Ziegler]]),<ref>{{cite web |last1=Croezen |first1=Alyssa |title=Johnny Orlando And Mackenzie Ziegler On Their New Single 'What If', Dating Rumours And Upcoming Summer Tour |url=https://etcanada.com/news/332916/johnny-orlando-and-mackenzie-ziegler-on-their-new-single-what-if-and-upcoming-summer-tour/ |website=ET Canada |access-date=30 May 2018 |date=30 May 2018}}</ref> "Last Summer",<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8479170/johnny-orlando-last-summer-music-video-exclusive "Johnny Orlando Has a Perfect Summer Day in 'Last Summer' Music Video: Exclusive Premiere"]. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', October 10, 2018.</ref> "Waste My Time", "All These Parties", "Phobias", "See You" and "Everybody Wants You".

His debut [[extended play|EP]], ''VXIIXI'' was released in 2015 when he was 12 years old. His second EP, ''Teenage Fever'', was released on March 15, 2019.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://musicmayhemmagazine.com/johnny-orlando-announces-debut-ep-teenage-fever-plots-headlining-tour-drops-new-single-sleep/|title=Johnny Orlando Announces Debut EP, Teenage Fever, Plots Headlining Tour & Drops New Single "Sleep"|magazine=Music Mayhem Magazine|date=February 15, 2019|author=Wendowski, Andrew}}</ref><ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/2019/01/johnny-orlando-ep-annoucement/ "Johnny Orlando Announces Completed EP"]. ''[[Tiger Beat]]'', January 25, 2019.</ref> Orlando embarked on his second headlining North American tour  in support of the EP, beginning on April 29, 2019, in Chicago and ending on May 22, 2019, in [[Vancouver]].<ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/2019/02/johnny-teenage-fever-tour/ "JOHNNY ORLANDO ANNOUNCES ‘TEENAGE FEVER’ TOUR"]. ''[[Tiger Beat]]'', February 15, 2019.</ref> It was supported by his close friends [[Hayden Summerall]] and Gus McMillan.<ref>[http://tigerbeat.com/2019/03/johnny-orlando-tour/ "JOHNNY ORLANDO ANNOUNCES TEENAGE FEVER TOUR OPENING ACTS HAYDEN SUMMERALL ,ANGELINA AND GUS"]. ''[[Tiger Beat]]'', March 3, 2019.</ref>

It was announced that [[MTV]] had signed a deal for costumer products with Orlando at the end of July 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Eggertsen |first1=Chris |title=The Deals: MTV Signs Talent Agreement With Johnny Orlando, Shania Twain Makes 'Texas'-Sized Detour |url=https://www.billboard.com/index.php/articles/business/9429992/deals-mtv-johnny-orlando-shania-twain-heart-of-texas |website=Billboard |access-date=October 14, 2020 |date=August 6, 2020}}</ref> In October 2020, he announced that his third EP, ''It's Never Really Over'' would be released on October 23, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=JOHNNY ORLANDO ANNOUNCES NEW EP, IT'S NEVER REALLY OVER FOR OCTOBER 23 |url=http://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/johnny-orlando-announces-new-ep-its-never-really-over-for-october-23/ |website=Umusic |access-date=October 16, 2020}}</ref>

==Early life==
{{More citations needed section|date=March 2021}}
Johnny Orlando was born in [[Mississauga]], Ontario, Canada on January 24, 2003. His father, Dale Vincent Orlando, is a lawyer <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.mcleishorlando.com/lawyers/dale-orlando/ |title=Mcleish Orlando| publisher=McleishOrlando.com|access-date=October 29, 2021}}</ref> and his mother, Meredith Orlando, is a housewife. Orlando has three sisters: his eldest sister, Madison, has a YouTube channel on which she regularly posts about her diet and health routine, the second eldest, Darian, helps Johnny with songwriting and promotion and the youngest, Lauren Orlando is a vlogger, YouTuber, singer, and an aspiring actress. Orlando is of Italian descent.

==Career==
{{More citations needed section|date=March 2021}}
Orlando made a YouTube channel, "JohnnyOsings" on December 6, 2011, and posted a cover of himself singing "[[Mistletoe (Justin Bieber song)|Mistletoe]]" with the help of his elder sister, Darian Orlando. Orlando and his sister recorded it in their bathroom because their bathroom had good acoustics. Initially, they expected at most 100 views, but to their surprise, the video went viral and got more than 100,000 views in a month.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.yahoo.com/rising-canadian-star-johnny-orlando-020454269.html|title=Rising Canadian star Johnny Orlando talks career start, acting|author=Associated Press|date=March 26, 2021|publisher=Yahoo.com|accessdate=May 1, 2021}}</ref> Citing the initial success of the first video, he and his sister continued posting covers of famous musical artists like [[Justin Bieber]], [[Shawn Mendes]], [[Taylor Swift]], [[Austin Mahone]], and [[Selena Gomez]] on his YouTube channel while Darian directed, shot, produced and edited all his videos.

==Artistry==
===Influences===
Orlando has cited [[Justin Bieber]] and [[Shawn Mendes]] among his primary musical inspirations.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Teen pop star Johnny Orlando wants Justin Bieber on his live series|url=https://www.outlookindia.com/newsscroll/teen-pop-star-johnny-orlando-wants-justin-bieber-on-his-live-series/1798778|website=outlookindia.com/|access-date=May 14, 2020}}</ref> He also stated that he looks up to [[Billie Eilish]], as well as [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[The Weeknd]], [[Alessia Cara]] and other fellow Canadian musicians who he looks forward to collaborating with in the future. In recent interviews,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Klonowski |first1=Lauren |title=Johnny Orlando: The Power of a YouTube Channel |url=https://www.beyondthestagemagazine.com/johnny-orlando-beyond-the-stage/ |website=Beyond The Stage Magazine |publisher=Beyond The Stage Magazine |access-date=August 19, 2020}}</ref> he's also shared that his dad played Pearl Jam and Otis Redding in the car, which helped cultivate his songwriting skills.
