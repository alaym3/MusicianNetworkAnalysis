'''Sigrid Solbakk Raabe''' (born 5 September 1996), known mononymously as '''Sigrid''', is a Norwegian singer and songwriter. In 2017, she released her debut [[extended play|EP]], ''[[Don't Kill My Vibe (EP)|Don't Kill My Vibe]]'', and later won the [[Sound of...#Sound of 2018|BBC Music Sound of 2018]]. Her single "[[Strangers (Sigrid song)|Strangers]]" reached number 10 on the [[UK Singles Chart]], and in March 2019, she released her debut studio album, ''[[Sucker Punch (Sigrid album)|Sucker Punch]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/reviews/sigrid-sucker-punch-album-release-date-stream-tracklist-tour-a8809971.html|title=Sigrid's debut album Sucker Punch has a crystalline self-assurance – review|date=2019-03-07|website=The Independent|language=en|access-date=2019-03-08}}</ref> which debuted at number one on Norwegian charts and at number four on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/e4q9hn/acts/aggmn3|title=Radio 1's Big Weekend 2019 - Sigrid|website=BBC Music Events|language=en|access-date=2019-08-12}}</ref>

==Early life==
Sigrid Solbakk Raabe was born in [[Ålesund]] to Håkon Raabe and Anette Sølberg Solbakk on 5 September 1996. She has older siblings, a brother, Tellef (also a musician), and two sisters, Johanne and Emma.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sigrid: Approaching pop from left-of-centre|website=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-40005056|last=Savage|first=Mark|date=28 May 2017}}</ref> During her childhood, she was inspired by [[Adele]], [[Joni Mitchell]], and [[Neil Young]]; but in her early youth she planned to become a teacher or lawyer, thinking a career in music would be too uncertain.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/may/20/one-to-watch-sigrid-scandi-pop-inner-steel |title=One to watch: Sigrid |work=The Guardian |date=20 May 2017 |access-date=22 August 2017}}</ref> When she was 17, she and Johanne started a band called Sala Says Mhyp, which was named after their late cat, Sala.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/evx47m/10-things-you-need-to-know-about-sigrid|title=10 things you need to know about sigrid|work=[[i-D]]|date=10 February 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
Sigrid began her career in 2013 by releasing her debut single, "Sun".<ref name="Sun">{{cite web|url=http://www.nrk.no/mr/sigrid-fra-alesund-er-ukas-urort-1.11049675|title=Sigrid fra Ålesund er Ukas Urørt|publisher=NRK|access-date=6 April 2017|language=no}}</ref> The song was seen as her national breakthrough in [[Norway]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nyttiuka.no/default.aspx?menu=617&id=53863|title=Sigrid Raabe skal opptre for nær én million seere på lørdag! – Startsiden|work=[[Nytt i uka]]|access-date=6 April 2017|language=no|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180622165407/http://www.nyttiuka.no/default.aspx?menu=617&id=53863|archive-date=22 June 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> She signed to Petroleum Records the following year, and performed at festivals such as [[Øyafestivalen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smp.no/kultur/article9872276.ece|title=Platekontrakt til Sigrid Raabe|first=Olav Sindre|last=Rise|date=1 July 2014|work=[[Sunnmørsposten]]|access-date=6 April 2017|language=no}}</ref>

In 2016, Sigrid signed with [[Island Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bt.no/kultur/Bergensartist-signerer-engelsk-storkontrakt-323883b.html|title=Hun kan være Bergens nye stjerneskudd|work=Bergens Tidende|access-date=6 April 2017|language=no}}</ref> She released her debut single with Island, "[[Don't Kill My Vibe]]" in 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nrk.no/mr/sigrids-gledestarer-i-bbc-studio-1.13397838 |title=Sigrids gledestårer i BBC-studio |date=25 February 2017 |publisher=NRK|language=no}}</ref> The EP achieved international success<ref name="nrk.no">{{cite web|url=https://www.nrk.no/mr/sigrids-gledestarer-i-bbc-studio-1.13397838|title=Sigrids gledestårer i BBC-studio|publisher=[[NRK]]|access-date=6 April 2017|language=no}}</ref> when it charted in Norway, Australia, and the United Kingdom.<ref name="NOR">{{cite web|url=http://norwegiancharts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Sigrid|title=Discography Sigrid|publisher=Norwegian Charts Portal. Hung Medien}}</ref><ref name="AUS"/><ref name="UK"/><ref name="SCO"/> Sigrid also performed on Park Stage at [[Glastonbury Festival]]. She was also a part of ''[[The Sims 4: Parenthood]]'' soundtrack with the [[Simlish]] version of her song, "Don't Kill My Vibe".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.islandrecords.com/news/sigrids-dont-killed-my-vibe-featured-in-the-sims-4/|title=Sigrid's 'Don't Killed My Vibe' Featured in the Sims 4|date=22 May 2017|publisher=Island Records|access-date=1 December 2017}}</ref> On 5 May 2017, Sigrid released her debut [[Extended play|EP]], named after title track, "Don’t Kill My Vibe".<ref>{{Citation|title=Don't Kill My Vibe - EP by Sigrid|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/dont-kill-my-vibe-ep/1447665211|language=en-gb|access-date=2019-01-28}}</ref> Sigrid performed at the [[Reading and Leeds Festivals|Reading Festival]] in August 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.readingfestival.com/line-up/artist/sigrid |title=Sigrid |access-date=14 August 2017 |publisher=Reading Festival |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170814143504/https://www.readingfestival.com/line-up/artist/sigrid |archive-date=14 August 2017 }}</ref> She is also featured on the 2017 [[Justice League (soundtrack)|''Justice League'' soundtrack]], performing a cover of [[Leonard Cohen]]'s 1988 classic "[[Everybody Knows (Leonard Cohen song)|Everybody Knows]]".

In 2018, Sigrid was announced as the winner of [[Sound of...|BBC Music Sound of 2018]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-42653599|title=Sigrid wins the BBC Sound of 2018|last=Savage|first=Mark|date=12 January 2018|publisher=BBC News|access-date=16 January 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref> On 10 February 2018, she was a guest presenter for an episode of ''[[The Playlist]]'' for [[CBBC (TV channel)|CBBC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/cbbc/joinin/the-playlist-sessions|title=The Playlist - The Playlist Sessions|publisher=BBC|access-date=4 February 2018}}</ref> The same month, Sigrid won the Newcomer of the Year award at [[Spellemannprisen]]. She announced on [[Twitter]] that she would release a song called "Schedules" on 11 July 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/thisissigrid/status/1016276116674367488|title=SCHEDULES. WEDNESDAY|last=Sigrid|date=9 July 2018|access-date=10 July 2018|via=Twitter}}</ref> The single was part of her second EP, titled ''Raw''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Raw - EP by Sigrid|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/raw-ep/1404098948|language=en-gb|access-date=2019-01-28}}</ref>

In December 2018, Sigrid announced that her debut album ''[[Sucker Punch (Sigrid album)|Sucker Punch]]'' would be released on 8 March 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/sigrids-long-awaited-debut-album-sucker-punch-released-next-spring-2417661/amp|title=Sigrid's long-awaited debut album, 'Sucker Punch' to be released next spring|website=www.nme.com|access-date=2019-01-28}}</ref> The album consists of 12 tracks, including five songs released prior to the album.<ref>{{Citation|title=Sucker Punch by Sigrid|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/sucker-punch/1442310677|language=en-us|access-date=2019-01-28}}</ref> In 2019, Sigrid began touring with American band [[Maroon 5]] for their [[Red Pill Blues Tour]] in Europe.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/thisissigrid/status/1036962573588066310?lang=en|title=Sigrid announces to join Maroon 5's Red Pill Blues Tour|author=Sigrid|date=4 September 2018|website=[[Twitter]]|access-date=14 September 2018}}</ref> She was also the support act for [[George Ezra]]'s tour across the United Kingdom.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.readdork.com/news/sigrid-is-joining-george-ezra-on-his-upcoming-tour-and-its-going-to-be-amazing|title=Sigrid is joining George Ezra on his upcoming tour and it's going to be amazing|website=Dork|language=en|access-date=2019-01-28}}</ref> In late 2019, Sigrid embarked on a headline tour across Europe and North America to promote the release of ''Sucker Punch''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2019/02/21/sigrid-uk-tour-autumn-2019-sucker-punch|title=Sigrid announces UK Tour|website=DIYMag|language=en|access-date=2019-02-21}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/thisissigrid/status/1110240188599595009|title=North America!! I am really, really excited to announce that we're coming over to play some shows!!!! We will be touring across the US and Canada starting this September. Presale begins tomorrow with code "invain" with all remaining tickets on sale this Friday at 10am. WOOOOOH!pic.twitter.com/npVu97hGRH|last=sigrid|date=2019-03-25|website=@thisissigrid|language=en|access-date=2019-03-28}}</ref> Sigrid contributed a song, "Home to You" for ''[[The Aeronauts (film)|The Aeronauts]]'' soundtrack.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.readdork.com/news/check-out-sigrids-new-track-home-to-you-from-the-aeronauts-film-soundtrack|title=Check out Sigrid's new track 'Home To You', from The Aeronauts' film soundtrack|website=Dork|language=en|access-date=2019-11-01}}</ref>

In 2020, Sigrid featured in a [[BBC Radio 1]] [[Live Lounge]] cover of the [[Foo Fighters]] song "[[Times Like These (song)#Live Lounge Allstars charity single|Times Like These]]", along with other artists such as [[Mabel (singer)|Mabel]], [[Dua Lipa]] and [[Rita Ora]]. This was organised in response to the ongoing [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/listen-to-radio-1s-live-lounge-charity-single-times-like-these-featuring-dua-lipa-chris-martin-and-bastille__29532/|title=Radio 1 unveil all-star Live Lounge charity single: Listen|last=Copsey|first=Rob|date=23 April 2020|website=[[Official Charts Company]]|language=en|access-date=23 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://listen.tidal.com/album/138377223/credits|title=Times Like These (BBC Radio 1 Stay Home Live Lounge) on Tidal|website=[[Tidal (service)|Tidal]]|language=en|access-date=23 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/foo-fighters-dave-grohl-and-taylor-hawkins-to-join-huge-times-like-these-cover-2652315|title=Foo Fighters' Dave Grohl and Taylor Hawkins to join huge 'Times Like These' cover|last=Reilly|first=Nick|date=23 April 2020|website=[[NME]]|language=en-GB|access-date=23 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7GElP4YdrBE|title=Live Lounge Allstars - Times Like These (BBC Radio 1 Stay Home Live Lounge)|date=23 April 2020|website=BBC Radio 1|publisher=YouTube|access-date=24 April 2020}}</ref> On 19 May 2021, after posting a snippet on her [[TikTok]] account, Sigrid announced that she would release her next song, "Mirror", which was released on 26 May 2021.<ref>{{Cite news|date=19 May 2021|title=Sigrid teases new single 'Mirror' on TikTok|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/sigrid-teases-new-single-mirror-on-tiktok-2944058|access-date=19 May 2021|work=[[NME]]}}</ref> Similarly, her next single "Burning Bridges" also got a [[TikTok]] snippet preview before being released and premiered on [[BBC Radio 1]]'s "Hottest Record" a few days later on 25 August 2021. <ref> {{Cite web|url=https://www.udiscovermusic.com/news/sigrid-burning-bridges/|title= Sigrid Drops Diamond-Sharp, Dancefloor-Ready New Single ‘Burning Bridges’|date=25 August 2021|website=discovermusic|access-date=6 September 2021}}</ref>
