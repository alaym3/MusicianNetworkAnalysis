'''Johnnyswim''' is an American [[Folk music|folk]], [[Soul music|soul]], [[blues]], [[Pop music|pop]] music duo, consisting of singer-songwriters [[Amanda Sudano]] and [[Abner Ramirez]].<ref name="Global Grind">{{cite web|title=Johnnyswim: The Duo You Need In Your Life|url=https://globalgrind.com/228362/johnnyswim-the-duo-you-need-in-your-life-interview/|publisher=Globalgrind.com|accessdate=May 26, 2012}}</ref>  The duo formed in 2005 in [[Nashville]], [[Tennessee]], United States.<ref name="Global Grind"/>

==Origin==
Ramirez trained as a musician at [[Douglas Anderson School of the Arts]] in his native [[Jacksonville, Florida]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Szaroleta|first=Tom|title=Jacksonville native uses homecoming to help promote new album|url=https://www.jacksonville.com/article/20140723/ENTERTAINMENT/801252350|accessdate=August 28, 2015|newspaper=The Florida Times-Union|date=July 23, 2014}}</ref>

Ramirez and Sudano first met after Sunday service at a church in [[Nashville]], [[Tennessee]].<ref name="Global Grind"/> Four years later, in 2005, Sudano attended a songwriting workshop held by Ramirez and became interested in writing songs with him.<ref name="Global Grind"/><ref name="johnnyswim">{{cite web|title=We Are Johnnyswim|url=https://www.johnnyswim.com/about|website=johnnyswim.com|publisher=johnnyswim.com|accessdate=9 June 2016|ref=jonnyswim}}</ref>

==Career==
Johnnyswim independently released their first self-titled EP on June 30, 2008.<ref name=Discography>{{cite web|last=Abner Ramirez|first=Amanda Sudano|title=Johnnyswim iTunes|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/johnnyswim/id292160052|publisher=Johnnyswim|accessdate=May 29, 2012}}</ref>

In 2009, the duo collaborated with [[Bruce Sudano]] on the track "Morning Song" which appeared on his record ''Life and the Romantic''.<ref>{{cite news|author1=Tennessean Music Team|title=Bruce Sudano’s ‘Wedding Day’ rings bells with listeners|url=http://blogs.tennessean.com/tunein/2009/07/24/bruce-sudanos-wedding-day-rings-bells-with-listeners/|newspaper=[[The Tennessean]]|accessdate=May 27, 2012|archiveurl=https://archive.today/20120707081634/http://blogs.tennessean.com/tunein/2009/07/24/bruce-sudanos-wedding-day-rings-bells-with-listeners/|archive-date=7 July 2012}}</ref>

In 2010, they released their second EP, ''5-8'' and toured around the country.<ref name="johnnyswim"/>

In 2011, they released the single "Bonsoir".<ref name=Discography /> In September that year, the duo traveled to [[India]] with VisitingOrphans.org.<ref name="Visiting Orphans">{{cite web|title=Johnnyswim|url=http://www.visitingorphans.org/who/clients_partners/artists/19|work=Visiting Orphans|publisher=Visitingorphans.org|accessdate=May 27, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130401085732/http://www.visitingorphans.org/who/clients_partners/artists/19|archive-date=April 1, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> Inspired by their experiences there, they wrote the track "Hallelujah" featuring Tulsi. It was released in December 2011 with all profits going to the efforts of the charity.<ref>{{cite web|last=Abner Ramirez|first=Amanda Sudano|title=We are so proud to present our new single...|url=http://blog.johnnyswim.com/post/13603370541/hallelujah|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120707064615/http://blog.johnnyswim.com/post/13603370541/hallelujah|url-status=dead|archive-date=July 7, 2012|work=Johnnyswim Blog|publisher=johnnyswim.com|accessdate=May 26, 2012}}</ref>

On April 24, 2012, they saw the release of their third album ''Home, Vol. 1'', a new collection of songs as well as the release of the single "Annie".<ref name=Annie>{{cite web|last=Sanfiorenzo|first=Dimas|title=Johnnyswim "Annie" (New Video)|url=http://globalgrind.com/music/johnnyswim-annie-new-video|work=Global Grind|publisher=globalgrind.com|accessdate=May 31, 2012}}</ref> The unsigned duo hit their stride and received support from major brands like [[Apple Inc.|Apple]], [[Louis Vuitton]] and [[Tommy Hilfiger]].<ref name="johnnyswim"/>

Johnnyswim were guests on the November 17, 2012 broadcast of the live radio variety show ''[[A Prairie Home Companion]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://prairiehome.org/shows/november-17-2012/ |title=November 17, 2012 |website=Prairiehome.org |date=2012-11-17 |accessdate=2016-02-01 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304002119/http://prairiehome.org/shows/november-17-2012/ |archive-date=March 4, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> They also appeared on ''[[The Tonight Show with Jay Leno]]'' on July 15, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3026588/ |title=The Tonight Show with Jay Leno (Episode #21.180) |website=imdb.com |date= |accessdate=2016-02-01}}</ref> Johnnyswim performed "Don't Let It Get You Down" from their album Heart Beats on ''[[The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson]]'' on September 13, 2013.<ref name="johnnyswim"/> 

The duo signed a recording contract with Big Picnic Records.<ref name="bigpicnic">{{cite web|title=Big Picnic Records|url=https://twitter.com/bigpicnic/|website=twitter.com|publisher=Big Picnic Records Twitter|accessdate=9 June 2016|ref=bigpicnic}}</ref> In 2014, Johnnyswim released their first full-length record, ''Diamonds''.<ref name="johnnyswim"/><ref name="bigpicnic"/> ''Diamonds'' held the number one spot on the Singer/Songwriter chart for several weeks.<ref name="johnnyswim"/> 

On February 13, 2014, Johnnyswim were guests on episode 65 of the program ''[[Live From Daryl's House]]'', joining Daryl Hall and the house band in performing seven songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.livefromdarylshouse.com/currentep.html?ep_id=80 |title=Episode 65: JOHNNYSWIM :: Live From Daryl's House with Daryl Hall :: Current Episode |website=Livefromdarylshouse.com |date= |accessdate=2016-02-01}}</ref> In April the same year, they performed a Tiny Desk Concert at NPR's ''All Songs Considered''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Thompson |first=Stephen |url=https://www.npr.org/event/music/307509202/johnnyswim-tiny-desk-concert?autoplay=true |title=Johnnyswim: Tiny Desk Concert |publisher=NPR |date= |accessdate=2016-02-01}}</ref>

In December 2014, [[Joanna Gaines]] announced on [[Twitter]] that the Johnnyswim song "Home" would become the theme song for the [[HGTV]] show ''[[Fixer Upper (TV series)|Fixer Upper]]''.<ref>{{Cite tweet |user=joannagaines|number=545807674466512897 |title=So excited to announce our theme song for season two}}</ref>

In 2016, Johnnyswim released the studio album ''Georgica Pond'' and the live album ''Live At Rockwood Music Hall''.<ref name="johnnyswim"/>

In 2019, Johnnyswim released their latest studio album ''Moonlight''. That same year, [[Chip Gaines]] and [[Joanna Gaines]] revealed plans for their own television network, Magnolia Network, and the first production show with the working title, ''Home on the Road'', which would follow Johnnyswim's Abner Ramirez and Amanda Sudano Ramirez, as well as their kids and band members, on their North American tour.<ref>{{cite web|last=McCarthy |first=Tyler |url=https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/chip-joanna-gaines-first-show-tv-network-johnnyswim|title=Chip and Joanna Gaines’ first show on their new TV network will feature favorite band Johnnyswim|publisher=Fox News |date=October 22, 2019|accessdate=October 24, 2019}}</ref>

In May 2021, Johnnyswim announced they were publishing a book called ''Home Sweet Road: Finding Love, Making Music, and Building a Life One City at a Time '' on June 8.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ventsmagazine.com/2021/05/03/johnnyswim-to-release-first-book-home-sweet-road-finding-love-making-music-and-building-a-life-one-city-at-a-time/|title=JOHNNYSWIM TO RELEASE FIRST BOOK|website=ventsmagazine.com}}</ref>
