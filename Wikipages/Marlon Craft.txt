'''Marlon Craft''' is an American rapper and songwriter from [[Manhattan, New York]]. He released his debut studio album, ''Funhouse Mirror'', in June 2019. He had also previously released numerous [[Extended play|EPs]] and [[mixtape]]s.

==Early life and education==

Marlon Craft was born in [[Hell's Kitchen, Manhattan|Hell's Kitchen]] in Manhattan. His father is a jazz drummer and his mother a producer for an [[Off-Broadway]] theater company. He grew up in [[Manhattan Plaza]], a Mitchell-Lama residential complex in Hell's Kitchen. He attended [[The Beacon School]], a selective college-preparatory high school also in Hell's Kitchen. He also played [[Amateur Athletic Union|AAU]] basketball in the [[South Bronx]].<ref name="NYER">{{cite news | last=Marantz| first=Andrew| title =Marlon Craft's Halal-Cart Listening Party| work=The New Yorker| date =29 July 2019 | url =https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2019/08/05/marlon-crafts-halal-cart-listening-party| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> After high school, Craft attended [[American University]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] where he designed his own major, Urban Education and Social Justice.<ref name="DNA">{{cite news| last=Rajamani| first=Maya| title=Hell's Kitchen Rapper Uses Neighborhood as Stage for Music Videos| work=DNA Info| date=5 February 2016| url=https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20160205/hells-kitchen-clinton/hells-kitchen-rapper-uses-neighborhood-as-stage-for-music-videos/| access-date=29 August 2019| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190829091446/https://www.dnainfo.com/new-york/20160205/hells-kitchen-clinton/hells-kitchen-rapper-uses-neighborhood-as-stage-for-music-videos/| archive-date=29 August 2019| url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==

After college, Craft moved back to Manhattan Plaza and began making [[Freestyle rap|freestyle]] videos and posting them to [[YouTube]].<ref name="NYER"/> His first collection of songs came in the form of the March 2015 mixtape, ''Pieces''.<ref name="Fashionably">{{cite news | title =Marlon Craft – Pieces (Mixtape)| work=Fashionably Early| date =1 April 2015 | url =https://www.fashionably-early.com/2015/04/01/marlon-craft-pieces-mixtape/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> That project featured a freestyle in which Craft rapped over the beat from "[[Shook Ones (song)|Shook Ones]]" by [[Mobb Deep]].<ref name="RVA">{{cite news | last=David| first=Amy| title =RVA hip hop review: Marlon Craft-Pieces| work=RVA Magazine| date =21 July 2015 | url =https://rvamag.com/music/rva-hip-hop-review-marlon-craft-pieces.html| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> He followed that with two EPs in 2016: ''So, What Are You Doing?''<ref name="DNA"/> and ''he looked like nothing''.<ref name="HLLN">{{cite news | last=O| first=Matt| title =Photos From Last Night: Marlon Craft's Headlines Webster Hall| work=Upcoming Hip Hop| date =8 January 2017 | url =http://upcominghiphop.net/2017/01/08/photos-last-night-marlon-crafts-headlines-webster-hall/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> The latter project featured the single, "Workin'".<ref name="FEWork">{{cite news | title =Marlon Craft – Workin' (Video)| work=Fashionably Early| date =5 October 2016 | url =https://www.fashionably-early.com/2016/10/05/marlon-craft-workin-video/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> In March 2017, Craft released a short film that was also called ''he looked like nothing''.<ref name="DJBHLLN">{{cite news | last=Bradley| first=Brent| title =NYC Rapper Marlon Craft Looks Like Nothing, Sounds Like Everything| work=DJBooth| date =7 March 2017 | url =https://djbooth.net/features/2017-03-07-marlon-craft-looks-like-nothing-sounds-like-everything| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> The month prior, he was featured in a [[Freestyle rap#Types of freestyles|cypher]] at [[Audiomack]] Studios with [[Radamiz]] and Angelo Mota.<ref name="DJBMack">{{cite news | last=Zisook| first=Brian| title =We Invited Marlon Craft, Radamiz & Angelo Mota to Audiomack Studios For a Cypher| work=DJBooth| date =13 February 2017 | url =https://djbooth.net/features/2017-02-13-audiomack-studios-cypher| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref>

In August 2017, Craft released his second mixtape, ''The Tunnel's End'', which featured the singles "The Feels" and "New York Shit" (featuring Radamiz). The album was briefly number 2 on the [[iTunes]] Hip-Hop chart behind [[Jay-Z]]'s ''[[4:44]]''.<ref name="DJBTunnel">{{cite news | last=Kakin| first=Cassidy| title =Marlon Craft is Making the New York Hip-Hop You've Been Missing on 'The Tunnel's End'| work=DJBooth| date =7 August 2017 | url =https://djbooth.net/features/2017-08-07-marlon-craft-the-tunnels-end-album-review| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> He also continued releasing freestyles including one that mentioned every [[National Basketball Association|NBA]] team, another on [[Statik Selektah]]'s [[Shade 45]] radio show,<ref name="2DBFree">{{cite news | last=Glynn| first=Patrick| title =NYC Rapper Marlon Craft Rips 'Show Off' Freestyle with Statik Selektah| work=2 Dope Boyz| date =28 June 2017 | url =https://2dopeboyz.com/2017/06/28/nyc-rapper-marlon-craft-rocks-showoff-freestyle-with-statik-selektah/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> and one that mentioned every member of the 2016 and 2017 ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' [[XXL (magazine)#Annual Freshman List|Freshman]] classes.<ref name="Revolt25">{{cite news | last=Orcutt| first=KC| title =Stream: Marlon Craft kicks off new EP series, 'A Dollar In Quarters'| work=Revolt| date =10 February 2018 | url =https://revolt.tv/stories/2018/02/10/stream-marlon-craft-kicks-ep-series-dollar-quarters-070067ad5c| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> In 2018, Craft opened for [[Dizzy Wright]] on his "Golden State of Mind Tour".<ref name="FOMO">{{cite news | title =Dizzy Wright Brings Love & Positivity To The El Rey On His Golden State Of Mind Tour| work=FOMO Blog| date =25 January 2018 | url =https://fomoblog.com/2018/01/25/dizzy-wright-brings-love-positivity-to-the-el-rey-on-his-golden-state-of-mind-tour/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> He also released a series of EPs entitled ''A Dollar in Quarters''. ''25c'' came out in February,<ref name="Revolt25"/> ''50c'' in March,<ref name="DJB50">{{cite news | last=Chesman| first=Donna-Claire| title ="New York Together Is Unstoppable": An Interview With Rapper Marlon Craft| work=DJBooth| date =16 March 2018 | url =https://djbooth.net/features/2018-03-16-marlon-craft-interview-new-york-is-unstoppable| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> and ''75c'' in May.<ref name="2DB75">{{cite news | title =Stream Marlon Craft's New EP, 'A Dollar In Quarters: 75c'| work=2 Dope Boyz| date =4 May 2018 | url =https://2dopeboyz.com/2018/05/04/marlon-craft-dollar-in-quarters-75c-stream/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref>

Later in 2018, Craft partnered with Same Plate Entertainment (a joint venture with Sony Music).<ref name="DeFP">{{cite news | last=Shepard| first=Ryan| title =#FirstToTheAux: Hell's Kitchen Product Marlon Craft Is The Truth| work=Def Pen| date =16 July 2018 | url =https://defpen.com/first-to-the-aux-marlon-craft/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> One of the first singles he released after signing to Same Plate was "NY Baby" featuring [[Bodega Bamz]].<ref name="RespectBaby">{{cite news | title =#PeepTheVisual: Marlon Craft feat. Bodega BAMZ – NY Baby| work=Respect| date =20 August 2018 | url =https://respect-mag.com/2018/08/peepthevisual-marlon-craft-feat-bodega-bamz-ny-baby/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> In November 2018, he was featured on the [[Masta Ace]] and [[Marco Polo (producer)|Marco Polo]] album, ''A Breukelen Story'', on the track "Wanna Be".<ref name="Vibe2018">{{cite news | last=Johnson| first=Zoe| title =Masta Ace And Marco Polo Deliver New Album, 'A Breukelen Story'| work=Vibe| date =9 November 2018 | url =https://www.vibe.com/2018/11/masta-ace-marco-polo-a-breukelen-story-album-stream| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> In 2019, Craft and his team formed an art collective, movement, and lifestyle brand called "OUR.S". The organization helped coordinate "WORKWEEK", a series of public events in March and April 2019 that highlighted various non-profit organizations. Craft released the single "Do The Work" in support of the WORKWEEK events.<ref name="2DBOURS">{{cite news | title =Marlon Craft – "Do The Work"| work=2 Dope Boyz| date =13 March 2019 | url =https://2dopeboyz.com/2019/03/13/marlon-craft-do-the-work-video/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref><ref name="DJBOURS">{{cite news | last=Craft| first=Marlon| title =Do the Work: A Guest Editorial by Marlon Craft| work=DJBooth| date =13 March 2019 | url =https://djbooth.net/features/2019-03-13-marlon-craft-guest-editorial-do-the-work| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref>

In May 2019, Craft released another single, "Shallow", featuring Dizzy Wright. As part of OUR.S, fans could access the song two days before its wide release.<ref name="RespectShallow">{{cite news | last=Worsham| first=Malcolm| title =Marlon Craft Embraces His "Shallow" Side In New Single Featuring Dizzy Wright| work=Respect| date =15 May 2019 | url =https://respect-mag.com/2019/05/marlon-craft-embraces-his-shallow-side-in-new-single-featuring-dizzy-wright/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> In June 2019, Craft released the single "Gang Shit". The song and its accompanying music video depicted Craft taking on three roles: a racist white police officer, a member of the [[Ku Klux Klan]], and a black man serving prison time for an armed robbery.<ref name="HNHHGang">{{cite news | last=P.| first=Milca| title =Marlon Craft Flips The Script In "Gang Shit" | work=HotNewHipHop| date =8 June 2019 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/marlon-craft-flips-the-script-in-gang-shit-new-song.1983308.html| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref>

The song served as the lead single to his debut studio album, ''Funhouse Mirror'', which was released later that month by Same Plate and Sony. The album featured guest appearances from Dizzy Wright, [[Pro Era|Nyck Caution]], Ricky Motion, and Evan Crommett.<ref name="HNHHAlbum">{{cite news | last=A.| first=Aron| title =Marlon Craft Releases Debut Album "Funhouse Mirror" Ft. Nyck Caution, Dizzy Wright & More| work=HotNewHipHop| date =20 June 2019 | url =https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/marlon-craft-releases-debut-album-funhouse-mirror-ft-nyck-caution-dizzy-wright-and-more-new-mixtape.119250.html| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> It reached number one on [[iTunes]]' Top Hip-Hop Albums chart the week it was released.<ref name="Region">{{cite news | title =Marlon Craft Tops iTunes' Hip-Hop Charts with New Album "Funhouse Mirror"| work=Respect My Region| date =23 June 2019 | url =https://www.respectmyregion.com/marlon-craft-funhouse-mirror/| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> It also received praise from artists and activists like [[Killer Mike]], [[T.I.]], [[Shaun King]], and [[La La Anthony]].<ref name="Bill2019">{{cite news | last=Lamarre| first=Carl| title =Marlon Craft Only Wants to Win and Leave a Hip-Hop Legacy: 'I'm Not Here For the Extra S--t'| work=Billboard| date =7 August 2019 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8526760/marlon-craft-interview-funhouse-mirror-mac-miller| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref> Shortly after the album's release, Craft sold out his first show headlining at [[SOB's]] in New York. The following month, he held a listening party at [[The Halal Guys]] food carts on [[53rd Street (Manhattan)|53rd Street]] in [[Manhattan]].<ref name="NYER"/> In July 2019, he appeared on ''[[Sway Calloway|Sway in the Morning]]'' and performed a "5 Fingers of Death" freestyle.<ref name="BillSway">{{cite news | last=Saponara| first=Michael| title =Marlon Craft Delivers Scathing '5 Fingers Of Death' Freestyle: Watch| work=Billboard| date =30 July 2019 | url =https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8524682/marlon-craft-5-fingers-of-death-freestyle-video| access-date =29 August 2019}}</ref>

In 2020 Craft left his record deal with Same Plate Entertainment/[[Sony Music]] and went back fully independent, launching The Center - an exclusive online subscription community of supporters. For $7 a month, fans can directly fund the creation of Craft's content, and in return receive exclusive access in the forms of hidden songs, weekly [[Zoom (software)|Zoom]] sessions, and more.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.58589/title.do-rappers-need-record-labels-marlon-craft-found-a-fan-funded-alternative-after-leaving-sony-music#|title = Do Rappers Need Record Labels? Marlon Craft Found a Fan-Funded Alternative After Leaving Sony Music|date = 22 October 2020}}</ref>

In early 2021, Craft announced that his second album, ''How We Intended'' would release in February. On January 19, a day before [[Joe Biden]]'s inauguration as president, Craft released "State of the Union",  a scathing rebuke of the state of affairs in America and in culture. On January 22, he appeared on ''[[The Beat with Ari Melber]]'' on [[MSNBC]] to break down the video and song with [[Ari Melber]] and [[Michael Steele]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msnbc.com/the-beat-with-ari/watch/-armageddon-see-the-chilling-warning-about-white-supremacy-before-the-capitol-riot-99895365760|title = 'Armageddon': See the chilling warning about white supremacy before the Capitol riot}}</ref>
