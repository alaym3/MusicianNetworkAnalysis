The '''Sidemen''' is a British [[YouTube]] group consisting of [[Internet celebrity|Internet personalities]] [[KSI]], Miniminter, [[Zerkaa]], [[TBJZL]], [[Behzinga]], [[Vikkstar123]] and W2S. The group is best known for producing videos of various challenges, sketches and video game commentaries across their multiple YouTube channels, which have a combined total of over 128 million subscribers {{as of|2021|10|lc=y}}.<ref name="CombinedStats" /><ref name="TheTimes">{{Cite news |date=23 October 2021 |title= Who are the Sidemen? How KSI and friends amassed 123 million followers|first=Andrew|last=Billen|work=[[The Times]] |url= https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/who-are-the-sidemen-how-ksi-and-friends-amassed-123-million-followers-cdnbjjswd |url-status=live |url-access=subscription |access-date=24 October 2021|archive-url= https://archive.today/5HlH9 |archive-date=24 October 2021}}</ref>

== History ==
{{expand section|date=November 2021}}
Some of the members knew each other before the group was formed. Bradley and Brown both attended [[Bexley Grammar School]] in London and Olatunji and Minter both attended [[Berkhamsted School]] in [[Hertfordshire]].<ref name="SidemenBook">{{Cite book|last=The Sidemen|title=Sidemen: The Book|date=18 October 2016|publisher=[[Coronet Books]]|isbn=978-1473648166|pages=52–57}}</ref>

The group originates from a [[Rockstar Games Social Club]] group made on 19 October 2013 in ''[[Grand Theft Auto Online]]'', called "The Ultimate Sidemen", which included all the members except for Lewis. In January 2014, Bradley met Lewis while at a ''[[FIFA (video game series)|FIFA]]'' gaming event in New York City and invited him to join the group.<ref name="SidemenBook" /> Describing the group name, Minter said in a video that "A sideman is basically someone's bitch who just follows them around [...] I was basically JJ's bitch that followed him around."<ref name="bi19">{{Cite news |url=https://www.scmp.com/lifestyle/entertainment/article/3037479/ksi-how-youtube-millionaire-who-beat-logan-paul-celebrity |title=KSI: how the YouTube millionaire, who beat Logan Paul in a celebrity boxing rematch, made his name |date=13 November 2019 |work=[[Business Insider]] |agency=[[South China Morning Post]] |access-date=8 March 2020}}</ref>

In February 2014, Olatunji, Minter, Bradley, and Barn moved into a house together near London, which they referred to as the "Sidemen House",<ref name="SidemenBook" /><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-52223908 |title=Coronavirus: UK's first 'TikTok house' opens during outbreak |last=Shaw |first=Dougal |date=19 April 2020 |work=[[BBC News]] |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200507192151/https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-52223908 |archive-date=7 May 2020 |access-date=7 May 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/newsround/52354393 |title=TikTok UK house: Who's in it and what do you need to know? |date=20 April 2020 |website=[[Newsround]] |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200610013433/https://www.bbc.co.uk/newsround/52354393 |archive-date=10 June 2020 |access-date=10 June 2020}}</ref> which allowed them to collaborate more often.<ref>{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2015/04/30/an-interview-with-youtube-sensation-vikkstar123.aspx |title=An Interview With YouTube Sensation Vikkstar123 |last=Reiner |first=Andrew |date=30 April 2015 |magazine=[[Game Informer]] |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200310035431/https://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2015/04/30/an-interview-with-youtube-sensation-vikkstar123.aspx |archive-date=10 March 2020 |access-date=10 March 2020}}</ref>

On 1 December 2020, the group's eponymous YouTube channel surpassed 10 million subscribers. Each member of the group was awarded their own [[YouTube Creator Awards|Diamond Play Button]] by YouTube to mark their milestone.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ginx.tv/en/youtube/the-sidemen-celebrate-huge-youtube-milestone|title= YouTuber's The Sidemen celebrate 10 million subscriber milestone|last=Savic |first=Nikola |date=2 December 2020| website=Ginx}}</ref>

=== YouTube content ===
The group has four YouTube channels, Sidemen, MoreSidemen, SidemenReacts and SidemenShorts across which it publishes a variety of videos including challenges, sketches and video game commentaries.<ref name="thenational1">{{Cite web |url=https://www.thenational.ae/arts-culture/insomnia-dubai-youtube-stars-sidemen-coming-to-gaming-convention-1.909130 |title=Insomnia Dubai: YouTube stars Sidemen coming to gaming convention |last=Andrews |first=Farah |work=[[The National (Abu Dhabi)|The National]] |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191027153116/https://www.thenational.ae/arts-culture/insomnia-dubai-youtube-stars-sidemen-coming-to-gaming-convention-1.909130 |archive-date=27 October 2019 |access-date=27 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://gulfnews.com/going-out/events/youtube-stars-the-sidemen-at-insomnia-dubai-gaming-festival-1.66332042 |title=YouTube stars The Sidemen at Insomnia Dubai Gaming Festival |date=10 September 2019 |work=[[Gulf News]] |access-date=27 October 2019 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191027153117/https://gulfnews.com/going-out/events/youtube-stars-the-sidemen-at-insomnia-dubai-gaming-festival-1.66332042 |archive-date=27 October 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.insider.com/what-youtubers-looked-like-first-video-vs-now-2019-10#ksi-205-million-followers-28 |title=Then And Now: How 30 of the world's most famous YouTubers have changed since their first videos |last=Dodgson |first=Lindsay |website=[[Insider (website)|Insider]] |access-date=27 October 2019}}</ref>

{{As of|2021|9}}, the Sidemen channel has over 13.4 million subscribers and 3.1 billion views,<ref name="SidemenStats" /> MoreSidemen has over 5.7 million subscribers and 1.9 billion views,<ref name="MoreSidemenStats" /> SidemenReacts has over 3.6 million subscribers and 835 million views,<ref name="SidemenReactsStats" /> and SidemenShorts has over 1.3 million subscribers and 330 million views.<ref name="SidemenShortsStats"/>

On 18 June 2018, the group released a [[web television]] series titled ''[[The Sidemen Show]]'', available exclusively on [[YouTube Premium]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://variety.com/2018/digital/news/youtube-the-sidemen-show-original-series-1202829109/ |title=YouTube Orders 'The Sidemen Show' Original Premium Series |last=Clarke |first=Stewart |date=4 June 2018 |website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191125045007/https://variety.com/2018/digital/news/youtube-the-sidemen-show-original-series-1202829109/ |archive-date=25 November 2019 |access-date=3 January 2020}}</ref> It comprises seven 30-minute episodes filmed around the world alongside a number of celebrity guests.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://deadline.com/2018/06/the-sidemen-show-youtube-debuts-first-trailer-dates-premium-reality-challenge-format-1202401935/ |title='The Sidemen Show': YouTube Debuts First Trailer & Dates Premium Reality Challenge Format |last=White |first=Peter |date=2 June 2018 |website=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191217081239/https://deadline.com/2018/06/the-sidemen-show-youtube-debuts-first-trailer-dates-premium-reality-challenge-format-1202401935/ |archive-date=17 December 2019 |access-date=3 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://realscreen.com/2018/06/04/youtube-commissions-blue-ants-sidemen-show/ |title=YouTube commissions Blue Ant's Sidemen Show |date=4 June 2018 |website=[[RealScreen]] |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200107024614/https://realscreen.com/2018/06/04/youtube-commissions-blue-ants-sidemen-show/ |archive-date=7 January 2020 |access-date=7 January 2020}}</ref>

In March 2020, the Sidemen released a twenty-minute YouTube video titled "#StayHome". The video featured the Sidemen and more than one hundred other YouTube video creators and other celebrities raising awareness of the UK's "stay at home" campaign which aimed to reduce the spread of [[COVID-19]] in the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom|UK]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-29|title=Coronavirus: YouTube stars urge fans to stay at home|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/technology-52084700|access-date=2020-03-29|website=[[BBC News Online|BBC News]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-29|title=KSI and Saffron Barker urge fans to stay inside as YouTube stars band together during coronavirus crisis|url=https://metro.co.uk/2020/03/29/ksi-saffron-barker-urge-fans-stay-inside-youtube-stars-band-together-coronavirus-crisis-12475625/|access-date=2020-03-29|website=[[Metro (British newspaper)|Metro]]}}</ref> All of the [[advertising revenue]] generated from the video was donated to the [[National Health Service|NHS]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-29|title=Coronavirus: YouTube stars and celebrities rally together to urge fans to 'stay home'|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/tech/coronavirus-youtube-stars-celebrities-rally-21778074|access-date=2020-03-29|website=[[Daily Mirror]]}}</ref>
