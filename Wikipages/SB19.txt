==Name==
The letters in the group name were from the initials of their Korean management company, ShowBT. The number 19 had derived from the addition or subtraction of the country codes of South Korea (82) and the Philippines (63). It symbolized the collaboration of the two countries in the formation of the group.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mandac |first=Bea |date=September 2, 2018 |title=EXCLUSIVE: SB19 talks about dreams, the road to debut |url=http://www.kstreetmanila.com/2018/09/exclusive-sb19-talks-about-chasing-dreams-the-road-to-debut/ |access-date=June 26, 2020 |work=KStreetManila |archive-date=June 9, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200609160935/http://www.kstreetmanila.com/2018/09/exclusive-sb19-talks-about-chasing-dreams-the-road-to-debut/ |url-status=live }}</ref> The members themselves, however, used the phrase "Sound Break"—for the goal to "break into the music scene" and improve the quality of Philippine boy groups.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |title=SB19: The future of Pinoy-pop is here |url=https://www.pep.ph/peptionary/149094/sb19-a721-20200202-lfrm |access-date=June 9, 2020 |date=February 2, 2020 |author=Tuazon, Nikko |work=PEP.ph |archive-date=June 9, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200609160926/https://www.pep.ph/peptionary/149094/sb19-a721-20200202-lfrm |url-status=live }}</ref>

==History==
=== 2016–2018: Formation and early years ===

SB19 was formed by ShowBT Philippines, the Philippine subsidiary of ShowBT Group in [[Korea]]. After the members were selected from hundreds of auditionees during a talent search in 2016, they underwent the same type of training provided to [[Korean idol|K-pop idols]]. They were supposed to be performers only on the company's events and activities but [[Chief executive officer|CEO]] Geong Seong Han decided to launch them as a boy group.<ref>{{Citation|title=Watch bunso SB19 Justin admit: "Hindi na po ako pure!" {{!}} SB19 Full PEP Live interview|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iNsrctqzyQQ|language=en|access-date=2021-05-08}}</ref> The group debuted with Josh, Pablo, Stell, Ken, and Justin on October 26, 2018, with the ballad single "Tilaluha". However, their initial efforts at promotions, mostly in schools and malls, were met with a "lukewarm" reception.<ref>{{Cite web|date=July 24, 2020|title=SB19's continuous growth is my fave underdog story|url=https://www.scoutmag.ph/music/sb19-interview-get-in-the-zone-debut-album-roginl-20200724|access-date=August 21, 2020|website=Scout Magazine|language=en-US|archive-date=August 25, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200825193031/https://www.scoutmag.ph/music/sb19-interview-get-in-the-zone-debut-album-roginl-20200724|url-status=live}}</ref>
