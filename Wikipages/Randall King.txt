'''Randall King''' (born April 2, 1950) is an American-born former professional [[tennis]] player. A native of [[Portland, Oregon]], King played [[Davis Cup]] tennis for Hong Kong during the 1980s.<ref name="HKTA">{{cite web |title=Hall of Fame Details Randall-King |url=http://www.tennishk.org/en/hall-of-fame-details-randall-king/ |website=Hong Kong Tennis Association}}</ref>

King reached a career high singles ranking of 269 in the world while competing on the professional tour and featured in the men's doubles main draw of the [[1977 US Open (tennis)|1977 US Open]].

From 1984 to 1986, by then based in Hong Kong, he represented the territory in five Davis Cup ties. In 1984 he won the doubles rubber in Hong Kong's historic 3-2 win over [[Chinese Taipei Davis Cup team|Taiwan]], which was their first ever victory in a Davis Cup tie.<ref name="HKTA"/><ref>{{cite news |title=Hong Kong netters lead Taiwan 2-1 |url=https://newspaperarchive.com/odessa-american-jan-15-1984-p-37 |work=[[Odessa American]] |date=January 15, 1984}}</ref>
