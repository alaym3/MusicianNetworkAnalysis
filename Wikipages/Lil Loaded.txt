'''Dashawn Maurice Robertson''' (August 1, 2000 – May 31, 2021), known professionally as '''Lil Loaded''', was an American rapper from [[Dallas]], [[Texas]]. He rose to fame in mid-2019 after his song "6locc 6a6y" went [[Viral video|viral]].<ref name=DallasNews>{{Cite web|last=Steele|first=Tom|date=May 31, 2021|title=Dallas rapper Lil Loaded has died, officials confirm|url=https://www.dallasnews.com/news/obituaries/2021/05/31/dallas-rapper-lil-loaded-has-died-officials-confirm/|url-status=live|access-date=May 31, 2021|website=[[Dallas News]]|language=en}}</ref>

==Early life==
Robertson was born in [[San Bernardino, California]], and raised in [[Dallas]], [[Texas]].<ref name=":0" />

==Career==
Robertson started rapping in late 2018. His first song was "B.O.S.", a take on [[YNW Melly]]'s song "Butter Pecan".<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Iasimone|first=Ashley|date=May 31, 2021|title=Lil Loaded, Dallas Rapper Behind '6locc 6a6y,' Dies at 20|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/obituary/9580442/lil-loaded-dead/|url-status=live|access-date=June 3, 2021|website=[[Billboard.com|Billboard]]|language=en}}</ref> He rose to prominence in 2019 when [[YouTuber]] Tommy Craze included his song "6locc 6a6y" in a [[reaction video]] in which he watched YouTube music videos that had 0 views. "6locc 6a6y" was [[Music recording certification|certified gold]] in May 2021, by which point it had received over 28{{Nbsp}}million views on YouTube.<ref name=TheWrap>{{cite news |last1=Noble |first1=Alex |title=Rapper Lil Loaded Dies at 20 |url=https://www.thewrap.com/rapper-lil-loaded-dies-at-20/ |access-date=May 31, 2021 |work=[[The Wrap]] |date=May 31, 2021}}</ref><ref name=Complex5.21>{{Cite web|last=Skelton|first=Eric|date=March 12, 2020|title=Lil Loaded Is Blowing Up On YouTube With Hard-Hitting Street Anthems|url=https://www.complex.com/music/lil-loaded-interview-6locc-6aby|url-status=live|access-date=May 31, 2021|website=[[Complex (website)|Complex]]|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Alexander|first=Bryan|date=May 31, 2021|title=Rapper Lil Loaded, whose song '6locc 6a6y' was a 2019 viral hit, dies at 20|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/music/2021/05/31/lil-loaded-rapper-dashawn-robertson-dead-at-20/5286288001/|url-status=live|access-date=June 3, 2021|website=[[USA Today]]|language=en-US}}</ref> Robertson later released "Gang Unit", which had garnered more than 44{{Nbsp}}million YouTube views by July 2021.<ref name=TheWrap/>

Robertson later signed with [[Epic Records]].<ref name=Complex5.21/> He released the mixtape ''6locc 6a6y'' in 2019, and the mixtape ''Criptape'' and his studio album ''A Demon in 6lue'' in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Saponara|first=Michael|date=May 31, 2021|title=Dallas Rapper Lil Loaded Has Died At 20|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.62412/title.dallas-rapper-lil-loaded-has-died-at-20|url-status=live|access-date=May 31, 2021|website=[[HipHopDX]]}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
On October 25, 2020, Robertson allegedly shot and killed a friend, 18-year-old Khalil Walker, while recording a music video.<ref name=Complex11.20>{{Cite web|last=Setaro|first=Shawn|date=November 10, 2020|title=Dallas Rapper Lil Loaded Arrested for Murder|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/11/lil-loaded-arrested-murder-report|url-status=live|website=[[Complex (website)|Complex]]}}</ref> Robertson turned himself in to police on November 9, 2020, after a warrant was issued for his arrest. He was indicted on a lesser charge of [[Manslaughter (United States law)|manslaughter]] in connection with the incident in February 2021. Robertson maintained that the incident was an accident until his death.<ref name=Complex11.20/><ref name=BBCNews>{{Cite news|date=June 1, 2021|title=Rapper Lil Loaded dies aged 20|language=en-GB|publisher=[[BBC News]]|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-57275571|access-date=June 1, 2021}}</ref>

==Death==
Robertson died, apparently by suicide via gunshot wound to the head, on May 31, 2021, at the age of 20.<ref name="BBCNews" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Lil Loaded Dead at 20|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/lil-loaded-dead/|url-status=live|access-date=June 1, 2021|website=XXL}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Woods |first1=Aleia |title=New Details Surface in Lil Loaded's Death |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/lil-loaded-death-details/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=16 November 2021}}</ref>
