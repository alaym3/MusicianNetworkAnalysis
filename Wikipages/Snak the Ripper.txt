'''William Scott Fyvie''' (born November 16, 1982), better known as '''Snak the Ripper''', is a Canadian rapper from [[New Westminster]], [[British Columbia]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/rb8bk5/keep-it-grimy-an-oral-history-of-ephin-and-stompdown-killaz|title=Keep it Grimy: An Oral History of Ephin and Stompdown Killaz|date=February 9, 2016|work=Noisey|access-date=October 31, 2017}}</ref>

== Affiliations ==
Snak the Ripper is one of the founding members of the Stompdown Killaz hip hop collective (SDK).<ref name=":0" /> In 2010, he became a member of 100MAD (hip hop collective founded by [[Fredro Starr]] and [[Sticky Fingaz]] of New York City rap group [[Onyx (hip hop group)|Onyx]]).<ref name=":0" /> He is also the founder, president and CEO of Stealth Bomb Records.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lC6uqf4h2Ho</ref>

== Early years ==
Snak the Ripper grew up in [[Maple Ridge, British Columbia]], a suburb located just outside [[Vancouver]]. He attended [[Garibaldi Secondary School]] from grade 8 through 11 before dropping out.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.chroniclejournal.com/entertainment/music_scene/snak-the-ripper-rises-from-the-dirt/article_4951f5ac-1d13-11e6-85d8-6bbf046386fa.html|title=Snak the Ripper rises from the dirt|last=VALENTE|first=MUSIC SCENE BY ANGIE|work=The Chronicle-Journal|access-date=October 31, 2017}}</ref> In 2001, he was charged with 150 counts of mischief, but due to lack of evidence, the charges were dropped. It was also in 2001 that he had acquired the name "Snak", adding "The Ripper" to his name when he started rapping in 2007.<ref name=":0" /> 

Between 2003 and 2006, Snak moved around between Vancouver, [[Montreal]] and [[Toronto]] to perfect his [[graffiti]] skills and build notoriety in the Canadian hip hop scene<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://senseslost.com/interviews/snak-the-ripper-interview/|title=Snak The Ripper Interview {{!}} Senses Lost|website=senseslost.com|access-date=October 31, 2017}}</ref> He was homeless and struggled with substance abuse, using his love for writing songs as an escape.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2009/04/interview-snak-the-ripper/|title=Getting To Know Vancouver's Snak The Ripper [Interview]|date=April 22, 2009|work=HipHopCanada.com|access-date=October 31, 2017}}</ref>

== Career ==
Snak released his debut album ''The Ripper'' in 2007. His 2016 release ''From the Dirt'' debuted at No. 1 on iTunes Hip Hop & Rap Charts.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.hiphopcanada.com/2016/06/snak-the-rippers-from-the-dirt-reaches-no-1-on-itunes-canada-hip-hop-chart/|title=Snak The Ripper's From The Dirt Reaches No. 1 On ITunes Canada Hip-Hop Chart|date=June 17, 2016|work=HipHopCanada.com|access-date=October 31, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://uptophiphop.ca/2016/06/18/new-album-snak-the-ripper-from-the-dirt/|title=*NEW ALBUM* Snak The Ripper – From The Dirt|date=June 18, 2016|work=Up Top HipHop – Canadian HipHop Media Site|access-date=October 31, 2017}}</ref> He was nominated for "Hip Hop Recording of the Year" at the [[Western Canadian Music Awards]] in 2015 and 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://breakoutwest.ca/blog/2016-wcma-nominees|title=2016 WCMA Nominees|work=BreakOut West|access-date=October 31, 2017}}</ref>
