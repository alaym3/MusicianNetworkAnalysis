'''Area21''' (stylized as '''AREA21''') is a Dutch-American [[electronic dance music]] duo consisting of [[Martin Garrix]] and [[Maejor]].<ref name=":0">{{cite news|url=http://www.youredm.com/2016/05/19/martin-garrixs-second-release-new-alias-falls-short/|title=Martin Garrix's New Release Misses The Mark Thanks To Terrible Vocals [LISTEN HERE]|last=Lucas|date=19 May 2016|work=Your EDM|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{cite news|url=http://www.youredm.com/2016/03/31/martin-garrix-just-start-side-project/|title=Mystery Artist Releases Martin Garrix Ultra ID & Nobody Knows What To Think|last=Kusnierek|first=Timmy|date=31 March 2016|work=Your EDM|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=http://www.youredm.com/2017/03/21/maejor-officially-added-ascap-area21-collaborator-martin-garrix/|title=Who is the 2nd Half of Martin Garrix's AREA21 Side Project? We Finally Know!|date=21 March 2017|work=YourEDM|accessdate=21 March 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://themusicessentials.com/revealed-mystery-collaborator-martin-garrixs-side-project-area-21|title=Revealed: The mystery collaborator on Martin Garrix's side project 'Area 21' {{!}} T.H.E - Music Essentials|last=Asher|first=Ranjiv|date=22 March 2017|work=Music Essentials|accessdate=22 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181009194305/http://themusicessentials.com/revealed-mystery-collaborator-martin-garrixs-side-project-area-21/|archive-date=9 October 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==
Area21 gained popularity after Garrix debuted their song "Spaceships" in his set during [[Ultra Music Festival|Ultra Music Festival 2016]] in which Garrix had headlined.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://wegotthiscovered.com/music/is-area21-martin-garrixs-side-project/|title=Is AREA21 Martin Garrix's Side Project?|date=1 April 2016|work=We Got This Covered|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.youredm.com/2016/05/18/stmpd-rcrds-teases-new-release-an21/|title=Martin Garrix Suddenly Reveals His Next Release Under Mysterious Side Project|last=Meadow|first=Matthew|date=18 May 2016|work=Your EDM|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref> The duo is signed to Garrix's record label [[Stmpd Rcrds]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7461645/5-things-you-didnt-know-martin-garrix-label-stmpd-rcrds|title=5 Things You Didn't Know About Martin Garrix's Stmpd Rcrds Label|work=Billboard|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref> Their identities were previously unknown despite speculations about Garrix being a part of it alongside one or more vocalists.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://edm.com/articles/2016/5/20/martin-garrix-side-project-area-21-girls|title=Martin Garrix Side Project Area 21 Releases Second Single "Girls"|date=20 May 2016|work=EDM.com|accessdate=6 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170207032402/http://edm.com/articles/2016/5/20/martin-garrix-side-project-area-21-girls|archive-date=7 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=":0" /> Throughout media and journalism, Area21 has been described as "Martin Garrix's new side project".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://wegotthiscovered.com/music/martin-garrixs-new-area21-release-is-here/|title=Martin Garrix's New AREA21 Release Is Here|date=19 May 2016|work=We Got This Covered|accessdate=2 February 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.edmtunes.com/2016/04/area21-martin-garrix-side-project/|title=Is AREA21 Martin Garrix's New Side Project? - EDMTunes|last=Julian|date=3 April 2016|work=EDMTunes|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref> Their logo consists of two aliens being inside a spaceship.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://edm.com/articles/2016/4/2/martin-garrix-new-side-project|title=Martin Garrix Has a New Side Project With Mystery Collaborator|date=2 April 2016|work=EDM.com|accessdate=6 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170207032141/http://edm.com/articles/2016/4/2/martin-garrix-new-side-project|archive-date=7 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[Asher Roth]] was initially speculated to be the unknown member of the group,<ref name=":1" /> however it was later revealed to be [[Maejor]]. Their song "Spaceships" peaked on the [[Spotify Viral 50]] chart at number 3.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/spotify-viral-50/2016-04-30|title=Spotify Viral 50|website=Billboard|accessdate=6 February 2017}}</ref> After one year, they released their third single "We Did It" on 23 June 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7842043/martin-garrix-area21-we-did-it-listen|title=Martin Garrix Side-Project Area21 Releases 'We Did It': Listen|work=Billboard|accessdate=6 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.edmsauce.com/2017/06/22/area-21-we-did-it/|title=Martin Garrix & Maejor Release AREA21's 'We Did It'|last=Stevo|date=22 June 2017|work=EDM Sauce|accessdate=6 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.youredm.com/2017/06/23/area21s-finally-sees-official-release/|title=Martin Garrix Finally Releases One Of His Most Highly Anticipated Singles|last=Meadow|first=Matthew|date=23 June 2017|work=Your EDM|accessdate=6 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.radioandmusic.com/entertainment/editorial/press-releases/170623-area21-releases-we-did-it|title=AREA21 releases 'We Did It'|accessdate=6 July 2017}}</ref>

On 1 December 2017, they released their fourth single "Glad You Came",<ref>Erik Mahal, [https://web.archive.org/web/20171202043058/https://www.edmsauce.com/2017/11/30/martin-garrix-maejor-reunite-area21-glad-came/ "Martin Garrix And Maejor Reunite As Area21 For 'Glad You Came'"], ''EDMSauce'', 30 November 2017. Retrieved 2 December 2017.</ref> which originally debuted during Garrix's Ultra Music 2017 set.

On 9 February 2018, Area21 released their fifth single titled "Happy" on Stmpd Rcrds.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8099034/martin-garrix-area21-happy |title=Martin Garrix's AREA21 Spreads Positivity on Feel-Good Jam 'Happy': Listen |last=Bein |first=Kat |date=2018-02-09 |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180210051247/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8099034/martin-garrix-area21-happy |archive-date=2018-02-10 |url-status=live |access-date=2018-02-10}}</ref> In a review by [[Dancing Astronaut]]'s Farrel Sweeney, the track is said to "emit a happy vibe via its simplicity and upbeat nature, as the title conveys".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://dancingastronaut.com/2018/02/area21-back-new-single-happy-accompanying-music-video-watch/ |title=AREA21 is back with new single ‘Happy’ and accompanying music video [Watch] |last=Sweeney |first=Farrel |date=2018-02-09 |website=[[Dancing Astronaut]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180210051414/http://dancingastronaut.com/2018/02/area21-back-new-single-happy-accompanying-music-video-watch/ |archive-date=2018-02-10 |url-status=live |access-date=2018-02-10}}</ref>

In April 2018, Garrix teased an unreleased single titled "Help" on [[Instagram]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://weraveyou.com/2018/04/new-area21-single-help-teased-by-martin-garrix/|title=Martin Garrix dons AREA21 alter-ego for new track 'Help'|date=2018-04-10|website=We Rave You|language=en-US|access-date=2019-02-20}}{{Dead link|date=June 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://djmag.com/content/martin-garrix-teases-new-area21-track-listen|title=Martin Garrix teases new Area21 track: Listen|date=2018-04-10|website=DJMag.com|access-date=2019-02-20}}</ref> and it was released on 3 March 2019. Its music video was released on 7 March 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=AREA21 - HELP (Official Video) |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yaqTZiqYvf4 |website=YouTube |accessdate=9 March 2019 |date=7 March 2019}}</ref>

On 24 October 2020, Garrix confirmed during an interview on [[TikTok]] that they will release an album in the spring of 2021. On 9 April 2021, Area21 released the single "La La La", off their forthcoming full-length album. The song's music video introduced the animated characters, which are aliens named M and M.<ref>{{cite news |last=Tryon |first=Oliver |title=AREA21 Are Back With First Album Single 'La La La' |url=https://www.cultr.com/news/area21-are-back-with-first-album-single-la-la-la/ |access-date=9 April 2021 |publisher=Cultr |date=9 April 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Goldenberg |first1=Ross |title=Martin Garrix, Maejor launch AREA21 back into space to introduce their LP with 'La La La' |url=https://dancingastronaut.com/2021/04/martin-garrix-maejor-launch-area21-back-into-space-to-introduce-their-lp-with-la-la-la/ |access-date=9 April 2021 |publisher=Dancing Astronaut |date=9 April 2021}}</ref> 

On 21 May 2021, they released the single "Pogo", and then released "Mona Lisa" on 18 June 2021. The track has had over 2.2 million streams on [[Spotify]]. Next up for the duo came "Lovin' Every Minute", a track Garrix had first played live in 2018 with fans speculating it to be a release with [[Justin Bieber]]; however this was not the case and fans had to wait 3 years for the official release which was by Area21. On 17 September 2021, Area21 released their eleventh single, "Followers", and announced during their first in-person appearance at the [[iHeartRadio Music Festival]] that their debut album would be released on 12 November 2021. On 15 October 2021 they released the single "Own The Night".<ref>{{cite news |last=Tryon |first=Oliver |title=AREA21 Deliver Next Album Single With 'Pogo' |url=https://www.cultr.com/news/area21-deliver-next-album-single-with-pogo/ |access-date=24 May 2021 |publisher=Cultr |date=21 May 2021}}</ref> They released their debut album, ''Greatest Hits Vol. 1'', on 12 November 2021.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Karakolis |first1=Konstantinos |title=MARTIN GARRIX AND MAEJOR UNVEIL DEBUT AREA21 ALBUM, "GREATEST HITS VOL. 1" |url=https://edm.com/music-releases/martin-garrix-maejor-area21-greatest-hits-vol-1 |access-date=19 November 2021 |publisher=edm.com |date=13 November 2021}}</ref>
