'''North Mississippi Allstars''' is an American [[blues]] and [[southern rock]] band from [[Hernando, Mississippi]], founded in 1996. The band is currently composed of brothers [[Luther Dickinson]] (guitar, [[lowebow]], vocals) and Cody Dickinson (drums, keyboards, electric washboard, vocals). Their most recent album ''[[Up and Rolling]]'' was released in 2019.

==History==
The group was formed in 1996 by brothers Luther and Cody Dickinson (sons of Memphis musician/producer [[Jim Dickinson]]), along with bassist Chris Chew, with the intention of combining the [[blues]] and [[Bluegrass music|bluegrass]] of the [[North Mississippi]] region with [[Rock music|rock]] and other modern forms.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=North Mississippi Allstars {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/north-mississippi-allstars-mn0000385393/biography|access-date=2021-05-13|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> Their first album ''[[Shake Hands with Shorty]]'' was nominated for a [[Grammy Award]] for Best Contemporary Blues Album. Their later albums ''[[51 Phantom]]'' and ''[[Electric Blue Watermelon]]'' have received nominations in the same category.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-15|title=North Mississippi Allstars|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/north-mississippi-allstars/13106|access-date=2021-05-13|website=GRAMMY.com|language=en}}</ref> The group also won a [[Blues Music Award]] for Best New Artist Debut in 2001.<ref name="WC Handy Blues Awards">{{cite news|last1=Wiederhorn|first1=Jon|title=B.B. King, Shemekia Copeland Top Winners At W.C. Handy Blues Awards|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1444077/bb-king-shemekia-copeland-top-winners-at-wc-handy-blues-awards/|access-date=7 April 2015|work=MTV News|date=25 May 2001}}</ref>

Starting in 2000, the Dickinson brothers and Chew have also participated in supergroup [[The Word (band)|The Word]] with [[Robert Randolph and the Family Band|Robert Randolph]] and [[John Medeski]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-04-27|title=Album Premiere: The Word _Soul Food_|url=https://relix.com/blogs/detail/album_premiere_the_word_soul_food/|access-date=2021-05-13|website=Relix Media|language=en-US}}</ref> The band made its network television debut in 2000 on ''[[Late Night with Conan O'Brien]]'' and served as the house band on the program ''[[Last Call with Carson Daly]]'' for a short period in 2004.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-06-09|title=North Mississippi Allstars to bring Southern heat to the Independent, GAMH|url=https://thebaybridged.com/2017/06/09/north-mississippi-allstars-bring-southern-heat-new-album-independent/|access-date=2021-05-13|website=The Bay Bridged - San Francisco Bay Area Indie Music|language=en-US}}</ref> Guitarist Duwayne Burnside, son of Mississippi blues musician [[R. L. Burnside]], has often collaborated with the group, and was added as an official member in 2003-2004.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Duwayne Burnside {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/duwayne-burnside-mn0000122630|access-date=2021-05-13|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> The band backed [[John Hiatt]] on the album ''[[Master of Disaster]]'' and the associated tour in 2005.<ref name=":1" />

North Mississippi Allstars albums are known for featuring many guest stars; for example, their 2005 album ''Electric Blue Watermelon'' included guest appearances by [[Lucinda Williams]], [[Robert Randolph and the Family Band|Robert Randolph]], the [[Dirty Dozen Brass Band]], and traditional musician [[Othar Turner|Otha Turner]]. Luther Dickinson joined the [[The Black Crowes|Black Crowes]] in 2007 and devoted time to both bands until 2011; he appeared on three Black Crowes studio albums.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://glidemagazine.com/140079/luther-dickinson-joins-the-black-crowes/|access-date=2021-05-13|website=glidemagazine.com|title=Luther Dickinson Joins the Black Crowes}}</ref> During that period, Cody Dickinson and Chris Chew formed the side project Hill Country Revue.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2009-06-28|title=Cody Dickinson and Hill Country Revue Make a Move|url=https://jambands.com/features/2009/06/28/cody-dickinson-and-hill-country-revue-make-a-move/|access-date=2021-05-13|website=Jambands|language=en-US}}</ref> 

Chew left the band in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-11-19|title=Ron Johnson Has Replaced Chris Chew in North Mississippi Allstars|url=https://jambands.com/news/2015/11/19/ron-johnson-has-replaced-chris-chew-in-north-mississippi-allstars/|access-date=2021-05-13|website=Jambands|language=en-US}}</ref> Since then, the Dickinson brothers have led the group with a rotating cast of supporting musicians. Their 2017 album ''[[Prayer for Peace (album)|Prayer for Peace]]'' reached number one on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Blues Albums Chart.<ref>{{cite web|title=Blues Music: Top Blues Albums Chart|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/blues-albums|access-date=2017-06-30|publisher=Billboard}}</ref> Their 2019 album ''[[Up and Rolling]]'' was selected as a "Favorite Blues Album" by [[AllMusic]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Favorite Blues Albums &#124; AllMusic 2019 in Review|url=https://www.allmusic.com/year-in-review/2019/favorite-blues|access-date=December 24, 2019|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref>
