'''Brian Michael Firkus''' (born August 23, 1989),<ref>{{Cite web|title=Trixie Mattel - Twitter|url=https://twitter.com/trixiemattel/status/1141961454498615296|url-status=live|access-date=2021-09-08|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> best known by her{{Efn|Mattel has stated that he is fine with any pronouns in a ''Proud Radio'' interview; this article uses both he/him and she/her pronouns.}} stage name '''Trixie Mattel''', is an American [[drag queen]], reality television personality, singer-songwriter, and entrepreneur from [[Milwaukee]], [[Wisconsin]]. Mattel is best known for competing on the [[RuPaul's Drag Race (season 7)|seventh season]] of ''[[RuPaul's Drag Race]]'' and being the winner of the [[RuPaul's Drag Race All Stars (season 3)|third season]] of ''[[RuPaul's Drag Race All Stars]]''.

Mattel is also known for co-hosting the [[web series]] ''[[UNHhhh]]'' and previously co-hosted its [[Viceland|Vice]] spin-off, ''[[The Trixie & Katya Show]]'', alongside fellow drag queen and frequent collaborator [[Katya Zamolodchikova]]. Mattel's first studio album, ''[[Two Birds (album)|Two Birds]]'', was released in 2017, followed by her second album, ''[[One Stone]]'', in 2018. Both charted on the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] Folk Albums chart at number 16.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Trixie Mattel|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/trixie-mattel|access-date=2021-08-13|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> Her third album ''[[Barbara (Trixie Mattel album)|Barbara]]'' was released in 2020, and her first [[Extended play|EP]] ''[[Full Coverage, Vol. 1]]'' which features Mattel's [[Cover version|covers]] of various songs was released in 2021.

Mattel and Katya wrote the book ''[[Trixie and Katya's Guide to Modern Womanhood]]'' in 2020, which became a ''[[The New York Times|New York Times]]'' [[The New York Times Best Seller list|bestseller]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Melissa|date=2020-07-23|title=Trixie and Katya Guide Women All the Way to the NYT Best Seller List|url=https://nerdalertnews.net/trixie-and-katya-guide-women-all-the-way-to-the-nyt-best-seller-list/|access-date=2020-12-01|website=Nerd Alert News|language=en-US}}</ref> Mattel is also the CEO and founder of Trixie Cosmetics, a [[cosmetics]] brand.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.seventeen.com/celebrity/a28904386/trixie-mattel-trixie-cosmetics/ |title=Trixie Mattel On Her New Makeup Line, Beauty Blogger Feuds and Her Time Slaying "Drag Race" |date=September 5, 2019 |access-date=May 14, 2021 |last=Twersky |first=Carolyn |magazine=Seventeen}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://onmilwaukee.com/articles/trixie-mattel-this-is-it |title=Drag legend Trixie Mattel partners with owner of local LGBTQ bar |last=Snyder |first=Molly |date=February 2, 2021 |access-date=May 14, 2021 |work=OnMilwaukee}}</ref> Mattel operates a [[YouTube]] channel where she showcases the brand's products, reviews products from other makeup brands, posts new music and music videos, and video collaborations with other drag queens and various celebrities. She also has a [[Twitch (service)|Twitch]] channel where she streams games such as ''[[The Sims 4]]'' and ''[[Dead by Daylight]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Trixie Mattel|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC0biFgrMdkv1hFAFLVePODQ|access-date=2021-02-08|website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Trixie Mattel|url=https://www.twitch.tv/trixiemattel/|website=[[Twitch (service)|Twitch]]}}</ref> In June 2019, she appeared on ''[[New York (magazine)|New York]]'' magazine's list of "the most powerful drag queens in America", ranking fourth.<ref name="nymag">{{cite news|author=The Editors|date=June 10, 2019|title=The Most Powerful Drag Queens in America: Ranking the new establishment.|magazine=[[New York (magazine)|New York]]|url=https://www.vulture.com/2019/06/most-powerful-drag-queens-in-america-ranked.html#|url-status=live|access-date=June 10, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190610184458/https://www.vulture.com/2019/06/most-powerful-drag-queens-in-america-ranked.html|archive-date=June 10, 2019}}</ref> Mattel is set to judge the drag queen singing competition series [[Queen of the Universe (TV series)|''Queen of the Universe'']] in 2021.<ref>{{cite web|title=Vanessa Williams, Trixie Mattel to Judge Drag Singing Competition Show|url=https://variety.com/2021/tv/news/queen-of-the-universe-judges-vanessa-williams-trixie-mattel-leona-lewis-michelle-visage-1235099240/|access-date=28 October 2021|website=Variety|date=October 28, 2021}}</ref>

==Early life==
Firkus was born in [[Milwaukee, Wisconsin]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Trixie Mattel Goes Undercover on Reddit, Twitter and YouTube {{!}} GQ|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g25sf_GmKaU|language=en|access-date=2021-09-08}}</ref> He is half [[Ojibwe]] and comes from a [[Native Americans in the United States|Native American]] family living in [[Crivitz, Wisconsin]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Brooke|first1=Zach|title=Point of View: Trixie Mattel|url= https://www.milwaukeemag.com/2015/09/08/qa-trixie-mattel/|magazine=[[Milwaukee Magazine]]|publisher=[[Quad/Graphics]]|date=September 8, 2015|access-date=August 18, 2017}}</ref><ref>[https://webelieveproject.wordpress.com/2015/05/13/when-you-are-true-to-yourself-amazing-things-can-happen/ When you are true to yourself amazing things can happen | This We Believe] Retrieved September 10, 2018.</ref> He had an abusive stepfather who would call him a "trixie" when he acted feminine, which would later inspire his drag name. He almost went by the name Cupcake before deciding on Trixie Mattel.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.metroweekly.com/2015/05/living-doll-trixie-|title=Living Doll: Trixie Mattel|work=[[Metro Weekly]]|access-date=May 5, 2015}}</ref> After graduating from high school, Firkus attended the [[University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee]], where he received a BFA in Music Theatre and was introduced to drag while performing in a production of ''[[The Rocky Horror Show]]'' at the [[Oriental Theatre (Milwaukee)|Oriental Theatre]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Kennedy|first1=Kerrie|date=June 3, 2015|title=For Brian Firkus, a childhood slur became the springboard for success as Trixie Mattel|url=http://www.wisconsingazette.com/entertainment/for-brian-firkus-a-childhood-slur-became-the-springboard-for/article_73f168f7-1e1c-567a-8386-e1acc6fcdb2e.html|access-date=August 18, 2017|website=Wisconsin Gazette}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dailyxtra.com/a-filthy-starry-night-with-trixie-mattel-67999|title=A filthy, starry night with Trixie Mattel|last1=Yerxa |first1=Mike |date=June 25, 2015|website=[[Daily Xtra]]|publisher=[[Pink Triangle Press]]|access-date=August 12, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Career==
===2008{{ndash}}2014: Career beginnings===
Firkus first performed in drag at LaCage NiteClub in Milwaukee.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://host.madison.com/ct/news/local/city-life/plan-b-host-trixie-mattel-to-strut-her-stuff-on/article_8cf12a91-fe1b-5ddf-bcf3-f30d750ae206.html |title=Plan B host Trixie Mattel to strut her stuff on 'RuPaul's Drag Race' |last1=Christians |first1=Lindasy |date=December 10, 2014 |website=[[The Capital Times]] |access-date=August 18, 2017}}</ref> Firkus became a staple of the Milwaukee drag scene while also performing in [[Chicago]] alongside drag queen friends of hers such as [[Kim Chi (drag queen)|Kim Chi]] and [[Shea Couleé]], who would later go on to also appear on ''RuPaul's Drag Race.''<ref>{{cite news |url=http://archive.jsonline.com/entertainment/arts/milwaukee-drag-queen-trixie-mattel-wins-spot-on-rupauls-drag-race-b99404841z1-285037501.html |title=Milwaukee drag queen Trixie Mattel wins spot on "RuPaul's Drag Race" |last1=Schumacher |first1=Mary Louise |date=December 7, 2014 |newspaper=[[Milwaukee Journal Sentinel]] |publisher=[[Gannett Company]] |access-date=August 18, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://screenrant.com/rupauls-drag-race-queens-best-friends/|title=RuPaul's Drag Race: 7 Queens Who Are Best Friends (And 8 Who Are Frenemies)|date=April 15, 2018|website=ScreenRant|language=en-US|access-date=January 16, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Kiki with Kim Chi|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=axQ8eq8cd8w|website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref> In 2014, Firkus attended [[Aveda|The Institute of Beauty and Wellness]] before withdrawing later that same year to participate in ''RuPaul's Drag Race''.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.timeout.com/chicago/blog/trixie-mattel-from-rupauls-drag-race-to-perform-in-chicago |title=Trixie Mattel from RuPaul's Drag Race to perform in Chicago |last1=Guerino |first1=Adam |date=June 9, 2015 |magazine=[[Time Out (magazine)|Time Out]] |access-date=August 18, 2017}}</ref>

===2015{{ndash}}2017: ''RuPaul's Drag Race'', ''UNHhhh'', ''Two Birds'', and ''The Trixie & Katya Show''===
[[File:Trixie and Katya's High School Reunion 3.jpg|thumb|right|[[Katya Zamolodchikova]] with Trixie Mattel]]
In 2015, Mattel first achieved notoriety as a competitor in [[RuPaul's Drag Race (season 7)|Season 7]] of ''[[RuPaul's Drag Race]]''. She was first eliminated in episode four and later reentered the competition in episode eight by winning the "Conjoined Twins" challenge with [[Pearl (drag queen)|Pearl Liaison]]. Mattel was once again eliminated in episode 10,<ref name=":1">{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/matthew-terrell/trixie-mattel-elimination_b_6936636.html|title=Trixie Mattel Elimination Casts Doubt on RPDR Reality|last1=Terrell|first1=Matthew|date=March 26, 2015|magazine=[[HuffPost|The Huffington Post]]|publisher=AOL Inc|access-date=December 4, 2016}}</ref> placing sixth overall and becoming the first queen to last more than one episode after returning to the competition.<ref name=":1" /> After her run on the show, Mattel performed her stand-up comedy tour ''Ages 3 and Up'' from 2015 to 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.trixiemattel.com/event/trixie-mattel-ages-3-and-up/|title="Trixie Mattel: Ages 3 and Up" {{!}} Trixie Mattel|work=Trixie Mattel|access-date=October 16, 2017|language=en-US}}</ref>

In October 2015, Mattel guest-starred in a special edition of [[World of Wonder (production company)|WOWPresents]]' Fashion Photo Review<ref>{{cite AV Media|publisher=WOWPresents|title=Trixie & Katya's Fashion Photo RuView, RuView of Raja & Raven|date=December 31, 2015|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xUtKgqq4epM&index=1&list=PLVmMNOHpsWfkF7E-ZYg_mufJfF6_xJBim|access-date=February 21, 2019|via=YouTube}}</ref> with fellow season seven contestant [[Katya Zamolodchikova]]. The positive reception for that episode resulted in World of Wonder launching ''[[UNHhhh]]'', a comedy web series featuring the duo. The series became one of the most successful shows on the ''WOW Presents'' YouTube channel and earned them their first [[Streamy Award]] nomination for Show of the Year at the [[7th Streamy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=7th Annual Nominees|url=https://www.streamys.org/nominees-winners/7th-annual-nominees/|access-date=2021-03-05|website=The Streamy Awards|language=en}}</ref> In November 2016 Mattel had a guest appearance as herself on ''[[American Horror Story: Roanoke]]''.

In addition to her comedic drag persona, Mattel is also a [[country folk]] singer-songwriter. Speaking to ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', Mattel listed her primary influences as [[June Carter Cash]] and [[Dolly Parton]] and states that she grew up on the music of [[George Jones]], [[Conway Twitty]] and [[Johnny Cash]]. She is also a fan of [[Jason Isbell]], [[Kris Kristofferson]], [[Aimee Mann]], [[Michelle Branch]] and [[Kacey Musgraves]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/features/rupaul-drag-race-trixie-mattel-country-albums-kacey-musgraves-w518524|title=Drag Queen Trixie Mattel on Her Country Albums, Kacey Musgraves Obsession|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=March 29, 2018}}</ref> Mattel self-released her debut studio album ''[[Two Birds (album)|Two Birds]]'' on May 2, 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/two-birds/id1225829683|title=Two Birds by Trixie Mattel on iTunes|publisher=iTunes|language=en|access-date=April 30, 2017}}</ref> The album debuted at number two on the [[Heatseekers Albums]] chart, number six on the [[Independent Albums]] chart, and number 16 on the [[Americana/Folk Albums]] chart.<ref name="Folk-Chart">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/trixie-mattel/chart-history/FLK|title=Trixie Mattel: Chart History - Americana/Folk Albums|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 15, 2021}}</ref><ref name="Heat-Chart">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/trixie-mattel/chart-history/TLN|title=Trixie Mattel: Chart History - Heatseekers Albums|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 15, 2021}}</ref><ref name="Indie-Chart">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/trixie-mattel/chart-history/IND|title=Trixie Mattel: Chart History - Independent Albums|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=January 15, 2021}}</ref> In December 2017, Mattel released ''Homemade Christmas'', an EP of three recorded Christmas songs that featured Zamolodchikova.<ref name="Instinct">{{Cite journal|url=http://instinctmagazine.com/post/trixie-mattel-serves-homemade-christmas-holiday-tracks|title=Trixie Mattel Serves Up a 'Homemade Christmas' with Holiday Tracks|first=David|last=Lopez|date=December 1, 2017|journal=[[Instinct (magazine)|Instinct]]|access-date=March 22, 2018|issn=1096-0058|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180317231953/http://instinctmagazine.com/post/trixie-mattel-serves-homemade-christmas-holiday-tracks|archive-date=March 17, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newnownext.com/queer-gay-christmas-holiday-albums-lgbt/11/2017/|title=Have Yourself a Queer Little Christmas with These New Holiday Albums|publisher=[[Logo TV]]|website=NewNowNext|first=Christopher|last=Rudolph|date=November 24, 2017|access-date=April 11, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180317231931/http://www.newnownext.com/queer-gay-christmas-holiday-albums-lgbt/11/2017/|archive-date=March 17, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

In October 2017, it was announced that ''UNHhhh'' would come to an end after its second season. Shortly after, a television [[spin-off (media)|spin-off]] of ''UNHhhh'' for [[Viceland]] called ''[[The Trixie and Katya Show]]'' premiered. The show ran until March 2018. Midway through the season, Zamolodchikova suffered a mental health crisis triggered by a relapse into addiction, which caused her to be replaced by [[Bob the Drag Queen]] for the remainder of the season.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.papermag.com/hold-on-to-your-wigs-katya-and-trixie-mattel-are-getting-their-own-tv-show-2474558266.html|title=Hold On to Your Wigs: Katya and Trixie Mattel Are Getting Their Own TV Show|last1=Cuby|first1=Michael|date=August 18, 2017|magazine=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|access-date=August 18, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/trixie-and-katya-show-tvs-best-new-talk-show-hosts-are-two-drag-queens|title='Trixie and Katya Show': TV's Best New Talk Show Hosts Are Two Drag Queens|first=Kevin|last=Fallon|date=November 15, 2017|magazine=[[The Daily Beast]]}}</ref> The duo later reunited in October 2018 for a third season of ''UNHhhh''.

In November 2017, Mattel and Zamolodchikova starred in a drag stage adaptation of the 1997 cult film ''[[Romy and Michele's High School Reunion]]'', entitled ''Trixie and Katya's High School Reunion''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newnownext.com/trixie-mattel-katya-romy-michele-high-school-reunion/11/2017/|title=Trixie Mattel And Katya Recreated The "Romy And Michele" Dance Sequence—And It Was Perfection|publisher=NewNowNext.com }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://worldofwonder.net/absolutely-hysterical-trixie-katya-recreate-romy-michele-dance-sequence/|title=Absolutely Hysterical: Trixie & Katya Recreate that Iconic "Romy And Michele" Dance Sequence|date=November 21, 2017|publisher=WorldOfWonder.net}}</ref> Also in November 2017, Mattel produced a video for [[GQ]] Magazine ''Trixie Mattel Makes a PB&J (and More Importantly, a Cocktail)'',<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mattel |first1=Trixie |title=Watch How to Make a Sandwich {{!}} Trixie Mattel Makes a PB&J (and More Importantly, a Cocktail) {{!}} GQ Video {{!}} CNE {{!}} Gq.com |url=https://www.gq.com/video/watch/how-to-make-a-sandwich-trixie-mattel-makes-a-pb-j-and-more-importantly-a-cocktail |website=GQ |language=en-us}}</ref> earning a nomination from the [[James Beard Foundation Award]]s in the humor category.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.jamesbeard.org/blog/the-2018-james-beard-award-nominees|title=The 2018 James Beard Award Nominees |publisher=JamesBeard.org|date=March 14, 2018}}</ref>

===2018{{ndash}}2019: ''All Stars'', ''One Stone'', and ''Moving Parts''===
Mattel later returned to the ''Drag Race'' franchise for the [[RuPaul's Drag Race All Stars (season 3)|third season]] of ''[[RuPaul's Drag Race: All Stars]]'', which started airing on January 25, 2018. On March 15, 2018, her fellow competitors voted for her to advance to the final two, where she ultimately won against runner-up [[Kennedy Davenport]] in the final lipsync battle.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://tvline.com/2018/03/15/rupauls-drag-race-all-stars-trixie-mattel-wins-season-3-finale-recap/|title=RuPaul's Drag Race All Stars Finale: Did the Right Queen Win Season 3?|last=Swift|first=Andy|date=March 16, 2018|work=TVLine|access-date=March 16, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=http://www.eonline.com/news/921102/rupaul-s-drag-race-all-stars-3-crowns-a-winner|title=RuPaul's Drag Race All Stars 3 Crowns a Winner|work=E! Online|access-date=March 16, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> In December 2018 she competed on the television special ''[[RuPaul's Drag Race Holi-slay Spectacular]]'' and won in a tie with the other competitors.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8482663/rupauls-drag-race-holiday-spectacular-trixie-shangela|title=Kim Chi, Shangela, Trixie Mattel & More to Compete in 'RuPaul's Drag Race Holi-Slay Spectacular'|work=Billboard|access-date=November 2, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite AV Media|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZSbFfSm0DgM|title=Fashion Photo Review: Holi-Slay Spectacular with Kameron Michaels and Asia O'Hara!|publisher=WOWPresents|via=YouTube|access-date=January 16, 2019}}</ref>

On February 3, 2018, Mattel released an acoustic version of her song "Moving Parts" as a tease for her upcoming album. It became her first charting single, peaking at number 83 on the [[Scottish Singles and Albums Charts|Scottish Single Chart]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/charts/scottish-singles-chart/20180202/41/|title=Official Scottish Singles Sales Chart Top 100|publisher=Official Charts Company|access-date=March 18, 2018|date=February 2–8, 2018|archive-date=March 19, 2018|archive-url=https://archive.today/20180319013244/http://www.officialcharts.com/charts/scottish-singles-chart/20180202/41/|url-status=live}}</ref> She dropped her second studio album, ''[[One Stone]]'', on March 15, 2018, the same night as the ''All Stars'' finale.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/entry/trixie-mattel-interview-one-stone-drag-race-all-stars_uk_5aaa58bce4b0f4aaa1141bbe|title=Trixie Mattel Reflects On Her 'All Stars' Performance And New Album 'One Stone'|date=March 15, 2018|work=HuffPost UK|access-date=March 16, 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref> The album peaked at number 16 on the Folk Albums chart, number ten on the Independent albums chart, and number one on the Heatseekers chart, becoming Mattel's first album to reach the top spot on a Billboard chart.<ref name="Heat-Chart"/><ref name="Folk-Chart"/><ref name="Indie-Chart"/>

In April 2018, she launched her new comedy world tour ''Now with Moving Parts'', followed by the ''Super Bowl Cut'' tour later that same year.<ref>{{Cite tweet|user=trixiemattel|number=966038334576386048|title=First 9 cities announced!|date=February 20, 2018|first=Trixie|last=Mattel|language=en|access-date=February 27, 2018}}</ref> A documentary film titled ''[[Trixie Mattel: Moving Parts]]'' premiered at the [[Tribeca Film Festival]] in April 2019, and screened until May 2019. It documents Mattel as she embarks on her "Moving Parts" tour following her ''All Stars'' win, as well as her personal struggles.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8501229/trixie-mattel-documentary-2019-tribeca-film-festival-premiere|title=Trixie Mattel Documentary Set to Premiere at 2019 Tribeca Film Festival|date=March 5, 2019|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 25, 2019}}</ref> The documentary was made available on [[Video On Demand]] platforms on December 3, 2019. An accompanying acoustic soundtrack was released later in December.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://ew.com/movies/2019/11/15/trixie-mattel-moving-parts-release-date/|title=Trixie Mattel's Moving Parts documentary finally has a release date|last=Nolfi|first=Joey|magazine=Entertainment Weekly|date=November 15, 2019|access-date=November 15, 2019}}</ref> Mattel's first comedy special, ''Trixie Mattel: Skinny Legend'', aired on [[OutTV]] in Canada on September 26, 2018.<ref name="Rudolph">{{cite web|url=http://www.newnownext.com/drag-race-comedy-specials-out-tv-trixie-mattel/07/2019/|title="Drag Race" Alums Return to TV in New Documentary, Comedy Specials|last=Rudolph|first=Christopher|publisher=NewNowNext|date=July 24, 2019|access-date=October 1, 2019}}</ref>

===2019{{ndash}}present: ''Barbara'', ''Trixie & Katya's Guide to Modern Womanhood'', ''The Bald and the Beautiful'', and ''Full Coverage, Vol. 1''===
In a September 2018 interview with ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', she announced that she was working on her third studio album, ''[[Barbara (Trixie Mattel album)|Barbara]],'' featuring an "[[electro-folk]]" sound''.''<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8474050/trixie-mattel-electro-folk-album-eminem|title=Trixie Mattel Talks Upcoming 'Electro-Folk' Album & Shares Erotic Eminem Fantasy|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> "Yellow Cloud" was released as the first single from the album on May 17, 2019, though it did not make the final cut. "Malibu" was released as the official first single on January 24, 2020 and the album was released a week later through [[Producer Entertainment Group]] and [[ATO Records]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8547761/trixie-mattel-barbara-album-teaser-video|title=Trixie Mattel Is Bringing Fans to the Beach With Official 'Barbara' Album Teaser: Watch|magazine=Billboard|last=Daw|first=Stephen|date=January 10, 2020|access-date=January 11, 2020}}</ref> The album later received a [[GLAAD Media Award]] nomination for Best Breakthrough artist.

In January 2019, Mattel and Zamolodchikova launched the fourth season of ''UNHhhh''. In June 2019, Mattel was one of 37 drag queens featured on the cover of ''[[New York (magazine)|New York]]'' magazine. The magazine ranked the 100 most powerful drag queens in America and placed Mattel at number 4.<ref>{{Cite press release|url=http://nymag.com/press/2019/06/on-the-cover-the-most-powerful-drag-queens-in-america.html|title=On ''New York''{{'}}s 37 Covers: The Most Powerful Drag Queens in America|date=June 10, 2019|work=[[New York (magazine)|New York]]}}</ref>

During the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], filming of the fifth season of ''UNHhhh'' was put on hold. In its place, Mattel and Zamolodchikova launched ''[[Trixie and Katya Save the World]]'', a spin-off filmed from their homes. Mattel also took the time to start building her [[YouTube]] channel, which previously featured make-up tutorials and music videos, and expanded to include content reflecting her pastime as a toy collector, with videos reviewing new [[Barbie]] collections, talking about doll history, or baking with [[Easy Bake Oven]]s.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://decider.com/what-to-watch/trixie-mattels-youtube-channel/|title=TRIXIE MATTEL'S YOUTUBE CHANNEL|last=White|first=Brett|website=Decider|date=January 28, 2021|access-date=February 14, 2021}}</ref> Mattel and Zamolodchikova resumed filming the fifth season of ''UNHhhh'' later in 2020 and launched their podcast ''[[The Bald and the Beautiful]]''.<ref name="Collab">{{cite web|url=https://www.dazeddigital.com/beauty/head/article/50585/1/trixie-cosmetics-katya-zamolodchikova-collaboration-red-scare-lipstick-drag-race|title=DRAG RACE'S KATYA IS RELEASING BEAUTY PRODUCTS WITH TRIXIE COSMETICS|last=Cadogan|first=Dominic|website=Dazed Digital|date=September 28, 2020|access-date=February 14, 2021}}</ref> On July 14, 2020, Mattel and Zamolodchikova released ''[[Trixie and Katya's Guide to Modern Womanhood]]'', a parody of women's [[self-help book]]s. The book was a ''[[The New York Times|New York Times]]'' [[The New York Times Best Seller list|best-seller]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/books/best-sellers/2020/08/02/advice-how-to-and-miscellaneous/|title = Advice, How-To & Miscellaneous Books - Best Sellers - Books - Aug. 2, 2020 - the New York Times|newspaper = The New York Times}}</ref>

In September 2020, Mattel released a cover of [[Lana Del Rey]]'s "[[Video Games (song)|Video Games]]", which charted at number 66 on the Scottish singles chart and number 89 on the UK Downloads chart. In December 2020, it was announced that Mattel and Zamolodchikova would co-host the [[10th Streamy Awards]] in [[Los Angeles]]. In the same ceremony, the duo was nominated for Show of the Year and Unscripted Series for ''UNHhhh'', winning the latter category.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/digital/news/youtube-streamy-awards-trixie-mattel-katya-1234845280/|title=YouTube Streamy Awards 2020 to Be Hosted by 'RuPaul's Drag Race' Alums Trixie Mattel and Katya|last=Spangler|first=Todd|work=Variety|date=December 3, 2020|access-date=December 8, 2020}}</ref>

In February 2021, Mattel released a cover of the [[Violent Femmes]] track "Blister in The Sun", and announced the EP ''[[Full Coverage, Vol. 1]]'', which included the two aforementioned singles, as well as  a cover of "[[Jackson (song)|Jackson]]" with [[Orville Peck]]. The EP was released on April 30, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Maas|first=Tyler|date=2021-04-30|title=Hear Trixie Mattel pay tribute to Johnny Cash, Cher, more on her new 'Full Coverage Vol. 1' EP|url=https://milwaukeerecord.com/music/hear-trixie-mattel-pay-tribute-to-johnny-cash-cher-more-on-her-new-full-coverage-vol-1-ep/|access-date=2021-04-30|website=Milwaukee Record|language=en-US}}</ref> In May 2021, it was announced that Mattel would star in ''Trixie Motel'', a reality series on [[Discovery+]] that will follow Mattel's efforts to renovate an old motel in [[Palm Springs]].<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Street |first1=Mikelle |title=Trixie Mattel Announces She's Getting Her Own TV Show, 'Trixie Motel' |journal=[[Out (magazine)|Out]] |date=May 20, 2021 |url=https://www.out.com/television/2021/5/20/trixie-mattel-announces-shes-getting-her-own-tv-show-trixie-motel |access-date=May 21, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbr.com/drag-race-star-trixie-mattel-discovery-show-trixie-motel/|title = Drag Race's Trixie Mattel Tackles Hotel Renovation in New Discovery+ Show|date = May 23, 2021}}</ref> In mid 2021 it was hinted then formally announced on July 1, 2021 that Mattel will co-star in a dating show with Zamolodchikova, to help her find a partner.<ref>{{cite AV media |url=https://shorefire.com/releases/entry/drag-superstar-katya-partners-with-taimi-on-forthcomingfrom-katya-with-loveshow |title=Drag Superstar Katya Partners With Taimi On Forthcoming From Katya Wit h Love Show |date=July 1, 2021 |website=Shore Fire Media |access-date=September 17, 2021}}</ref>

In August 2021, Mattel launched an advice newsletter, ''Gooped'', with Zamolodchikova.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Staff|first=Queer Forty|date=2021-08-20|title=Trixie & Katya announce GOOPED newsletter on Substack|url=https://queerforty.com/trixie-katya-announce-gooped-newsletter-on-substack|access-date=2021-08-21|website=Queer Forty|language=en-US}}</ref> In November 2021, Mattel announced "The Trixie Doll", a collectible fashion doll, in collaboration with [[Integrity Toys]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Trixie Doll|url=https://wclubforum.com/trixie/|url-status=live|website=The Trixie Doll}}</ref> On November 12, she released the single "Hello Hello" which is loosely inspired by [[1960s|60s]] [[power pop]]. The music video was choreographed by fellow drag race alumni [[Laganja Estranja]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Kirkl|first=Justin|date=2021-11-12|title=Trixie Mattel Has Gone Go-Go|url=https://www.esquire.com/entertainment/music/a38222577/trixie-mattel-hello-hello-music-video/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-11-16|website=[[Esquire (magazine)|Esquire]]|language=en-US}}</ref> Joseph Longo of [[Them (website)|''Them'']] said: "With “Hello Hello,” Mattel makes her case for pop star status — elaborate choreography, suave backup dancers, expressive makeup and a sparkly mini-dress."<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nast|first=Condé|date=2021-11-15|title=Trixie Mattel Is Making a Bid for Pop Stardom|url=https://www.them.us/story/trixie-mattel-new-single-hello-hello|access-date=2021-11-16|website=them.|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Firkus is openly [[gay]].<ref>{{cite tweet|number=888511437009698817|user=trixiemattel|title=I'm a gay person who specializes in gay activities.|date=July 21, 2017}}</ref> Since 2016, he has been in a relationship with filmmaker David Silver, who also produced his documentary ''Moving Parts''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stylesrant.com/inside-trixie-mattels-life/|title=Inside Trixie Mattel's life, her upbringing and boyfriend|date=February 9, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.queerty.com/trixie-mattel-time-moms-boyfriend-pulled-gun-teen-queen-20190428|title= Trixie Mattel on Katya's mental breakdown caught on film in her dragumentary, 'Moving Parts'|magazine=Queerty|last=Reddish|first=David|date=April 28, 2019|access-date=October 27, 2019}}</ref> He is a [[vegetarian]].<ref>{{cite tweet|user=trixiemattel|number=610533359714893824|title=I'm a vegetarian !|date=June 15, 2015|first=Trixie|last=Mattel|access-date=September 19, 2018|url-status=live|archive-date=May 19, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180519175211/https://twitter.com/trixiemattel/status/610533359714893824}}</ref><ref>{{cite tweet|access-date=September 19, 2018|user=trixiemattel|number=1012961007453528067|title=Triggered.
