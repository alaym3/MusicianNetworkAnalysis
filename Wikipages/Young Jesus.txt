'''Young Jesus''' is an American [[indie rock]] band from [[Chicago|Chicago, Illinois]]. Since 2014, the band has been based out of [[Los Angeles, California]].

==History==
Young Jesus began as a high school band in the [[Chicago suburbs]], consisting of two members who are no longer in the band.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Goldstein|first=Sam Jaffe|date=2017-10-05|title=There’s a Theory I’ve Got Cooking: An Interview with John Rossiter of Young Jesus|url=https://blog.lareviewofbooks.org/interviews/theres-theory-ive-got-cooking-interview-john-rossiter-young-jesus/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-08|website=BLARB|language=en-US}}</ref> While located in Chicago, there were two [[Side project|side projects]] featuring members of Young Jesus: ''Bummer'' and ''Wavepool''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Miller|first=David|date=2014-09-05|title=The re-introduction of Young Jesus|url=https://imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/young-jesus-g-mp3|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-08|website=[[Impose (magazine)|IMPOSE Magazine]]|language=en}}</ref> As of the release of their debut album ''Home'', in 2012, the band featured Rossiter, along with bassist Shawn Nystrand, lead guitarist Cody Kellogg, and drummer Peter Martin.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Coleman|first=Sophia|date=2012-02-20|title=Rocking out with Young Jesus|url=https://columbiachronicle.com/5023a583-2563-55b9-8e0e-85cfbc00663e|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-08|website=[[The Columbia Chronicle]]}}</ref> The only member of that lineup still in Young Jesus is Rossiter; Shevrin was a member of the band in 2015<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cohen|first=Ian|date=2018-08-14|title=Band To Watch: Young Jesus|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2009894/young-jesus-interview/interviews/band-to-watch/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-08|website=Stereogum|language=en}}</ref> and their current lineup, featuring Borbon and Haug, has existed since 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Schnipper|first=Matthew|date=2020-08-24|title=In Conversation: Young Jesus’ John Rossiter Pushes Past Emo|url=https://grandmasophiascookies.com/2020/08/24/in-conversation-young-jesus-john-rossiter-pushes-past-emo/|access-date=2021-02-08|website=Grandma Sophia's Cookies|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hyden|first=Steven|date=2020-08-12|title=Young Jesus’ ’Welcome To Conceptual Beach’ Is Brilliant Indie Rock|url=https://uproxx.com/indie/young-jesus-interview-welcome-to-conceptual-beach/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-08|website=Uproxx|language=en-US}}</ref>

Young Jesus released the full-length album ''Grow/Decompose'' on Gigantic Noise Records in 2015.<ref name="imposemagazine">{{cite web |last1=Miller |first1=David |title=The re-introduction of Young Jesus |url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/bytes/new-music/young-jesus-g-mp3 |publisher=[[Impose (magazine)|Impose]] |access-date=30 October 2018}}</ref> In 2017, Young Jesus signed to [[Saddle Creek Records]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sacher|first=Andrew|date=2017-11-14|title=Young Jesus sign to Saddle Creek, share ”Feeling” video|url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/young-jesus-sign-to-saddle-creek-share-feeling-video/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-08|website=[[BrooklynVegan]]|language=en}}</ref> where they have released three full length albums.<ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web|last1=Cohen|first1=Ian|date=2017-11-20|title=Young Jesus: S/T|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/young-jesus-st/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=30 October 2018|website=|publisher=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]}}</ref><ref name="pitchfork1">{{cite web|last1=Cohen|first1=Ian|date=2018-10-20|title=Young Jesus: The Whole Thing Is Just There|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/young-jesus-the-whole-thing-is-just-there/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=30 October 2018|website=|publisher=Pitchfork}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Cohen|first=Ian|date=2020-08-18|title=Young Jesus: Welcome to Conceptual Beach|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/young-jesus-welcome-to-conceptual-beach/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-08|website=Pitchfork|language=en}}</ref>

== Musical Style ==
Young Jesus is not a [[Christian rock]] band, although they have been mistaken for one due to their name.<ref name=":1" /> The band is influenced by [[Midwest emo]] and [[jazz]],<ref name=":0" /> and post-rock.<ref name="pitchfork1" /> Their lyrics are influenced by [[ancient Chinese poetry]], specifically from the [[Chan Buddhism|Chan Buddhist]] and [[Daoist|Taoist]] tradition.<ref name=":0" />
