'''Jessica Hilda Mauboy'''<ref name="5facts">{{cite web|url=http://www.take40.com/artists/3129/jessica-mauboy/top-5-facts|title=Jessica Mauboy Top 5 Facts|work=[[Take 40 Australia]]|access-date=14 August 2011}}</ref> (born 4 August 1989) is an Australian [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[Pop music|pop]] singer, magician, songwriter, and actress. Born and raised in [[Darwin, Northern Territory]], she rose to fame in 2006 on the [[Australian Idol (season 4)|fourth season]] of ''[[Australian Idol]]'', where she was runner-up and subsequently signed a recording contract with [[Sony Music Australia]]. After releasing a [[The Journey (Jessica Mauboy album)|live album of her ''Idol'' performances]] and briefly being a member of the girl group [[Young Divas]] in 2007, Mauboy released her debut studio album, ''[[Been Waiting]]'', the following year. It included her first number-one single, "[[Burn (Jessica Mauboy song)|Burn]]", and became the second highest-selling Australian album of 2009,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aria.com.au/pages/aria-charts-end-of-year-charts-top-australian-album-2009.htm |title=ARIA Charts – End Of Year Charts – Top 50 Australian Artist Albums 2009 |publisher=[[Australian Recording Industry Association]]|access-date=24 September 2009}}</ref> certified double platinum by the [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA).

Her second studio album, ''[[Get 'Em Girls]]'' (2010), showcased a harder-edged R&B sound, and produced four platinum singles. Her third studio album, ''[[Beautiful (Jessica Mauboy album)|Beautiful]]'' (2013), a mixture of [[Dance music|dance-oriented]] tracks, R&B and pop, included the top-ten hits "[[Pop a Bottle (Fill Me Up)]]", "[[Never Be the Same (Jessica Mauboy song)|Never Be the Same]]" and "[[Can I Get a Moment?]]". 

Aside from her music career, Mauboy has ventured into acting, with starring roles in the films ''[[Bran Nue Dae (film)|Bran Nue Dae]]'' (2010), and ''[[The Sapphires (film)|The Sapphires]]'' (2012), which earned her the [[AACTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role]]. She returned to acting in 2016 in the lead role in the television drama series ''[[The Secret Daughter]]'' for two seasons. It was her first major TV role and was written especially for her. She released [[Jessica Mauboy discography#Soundtrack albums|two soundtrack albums]] from the show, the first of which made her the first Indigenous artist with a number-one album on the [[ARIA Charts|ARIA Albums Chart]]. Her fourth studio album, ''[[Hilda (album)|Hilda]]'' (October 2019), debuted at number&nbsp;one.

Mauboy is one of Australia's most successful female artists.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.news.com.au/lifestyle/sunday-style/people/jessica-mauboy-fuses-art-and-fashion/news-story/539b715df77d5d0bcda8ad0fa182e015|title=Jessica Mauboy fuses art and fashion|last=Safe|first=Georgina|work=Sunday Style|publisher=[[News.com.au]]|date=22 September 2015|access-date=12 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/entertainment/confidential/why-hasnt-the-voice-produced-a-superstar-take-a-look-at-the-alltime-best-aussie-reality-showstoppers/news-story/c54904b1a28cd622f1e09c48d48b52a6|title=Why hasn't The Voice produced a superstar? Take a look at the all-time best Aussie reality showstoppers|last=Dennehy|first=Luke|work=[[Herald Sun]]|date=9 July 2014|access-date=12 August 2016}}</ref> She has achieved six top-ten albums (including two number-ones) and 16 top-twenty singles (including 9 top-ten hits). She has won two [[ARIA Music Awards]] from 25 nominations, and was ranked sixteenth on the ''[[Herald Sun]]''{{'}}s list of the "100 Greatest Australian Singers of All Time".<ref>{{cite news|last=Adams|first=Cameron|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/entertainment/music/john-farnham-tops-the-list-of-australias-greatest-singers-of-all-time/story-e6frf9hf-1226610374225|title=John Farnham tops the list of Australia's greatest singers of all time|work=Herald Sun|date=2 April 2013|access-date=8 April 2013}}</ref> She has collaborated with international artists such as [[Flo Rida]], [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Ludacris]], [[Jay Sean]], and [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]]. She has also toured with [[Beyoncé]] and [[Chris Brown]], and performed at many notable events, including the Australian visits for [[Oprah Winfrey]], [[Ellen DeGeneres]], and US President [[Barack Obama]]. She was a guest performer at the second semifinal of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2014|Eurovision Song Contest in 2014]]. She competed for Australia at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2018|2018 Eurovision Song Contest]] and reached 20th place.

==Music career==

===2006: ''Australian Idol''===
[[File:JessicaMauboy DamienLeith.jpg|thumb|right|upright=0.75|Mauboy with [[Damien Leith]] at the ''[[Australian Idol]]'' grand finale]]
In 2006, Mauboy auditioned for the [[Australian Idol (season 4)|fourth season]] of ''Australian Idol'' in [[Alice Springs]], Northern Territory, singing [[Whitney Houston]]'s "[[I Have Nothing]]".<ref name="louise"/> Her audition impressed all three judges, and she progressed to the semifinals,<ref name="louise"/> then to the top&nbsp;twelve. The media cited her previous Sony contract as grounds for dismissal, but since it had expired, the producers refused to remove her from the show.<ref name="allmusic"/> In the final eleven week, following her rendition of [[Kelly Clarkson]]'s "[[Walk Away (Kelly Clarkson song)|Walk Away]]", judge [[Kyle Sandilands]] commented on Mauboy's weight, and said that if she wanted to succeed in the music industry, she should "lose the jelly belly".<ref name="mclean">{{cite news|last=McLean |first=Sandra |url=http://www.couriermail.com.au/entertainment/tv/jelly-belly-jibe/story-e6freqj6-1111112231410 |title='Jelly belly' jibe |date=19 September 2006 |work=The Courier-Mail |access-date=28 July 2011}}</ref> Mauboy appeared stunned by the comment.<ref name="mclean"/> In an interview with ''[[Who (magazine)|Who]]'' magazine's Alicia Neil, two years after the comment, Mauboy said, "I kind of took it as a joke...&nbsp;I look back on it as a positive thing–it made me a stronger person."<ref>{{cite web|last=Neil |first=Alicia |url=http://au.lifestyle.yahoo.com/who/celebrity-interviews/article/-/5917824/jessica-mauboy/ |title=Jessica Mauboy |date=16 March 2009 |work=Who |access-date=1 February 2010}}</ref>

In the final ten-week, Mauboy received the first touchdown of the season from judge [[Mark Holden]] for her rendition of [[Christina Aguilera]]'s hit "[[Beautiful (Christina Aguilera song)|Beautiful]]".<ref name="5facts"/> During the final nine-week, she had a sore throat that resulted in a mediocre rendition of [[Phil Collins]]' "[[Another Day in Paradise]]" and near-elimination, landing her in the bottom three.<ref name="allmusic"/> She never landed in the bottom three again, and ended up progressing to the final show with [[Damien Leith]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Idols down to just two|url=http://www.news.com.au/idols-down-to-just-two/story-e6frfo60-1111112518541|publisher=news.com.au|date=14 November 2006|access-date=28 July 2011}}</ref> At the grand finale on 27 November at the [[Sydney Opera House]],<ref name="final countdown">{{cite news|last=Nicholson |first=Sarah |url=http://www.news.com.au/couriermail/story/0,23739,20795784-5003421,00.html |title=Final countdown for idols |date=21 November 2006 |work=[[The Courier-Mail]] |access-date=1 February 2010}}</ref> Mauboy was voted runner-up to Leith.<ref>{{cite news|last=Bodey |first=Michael |url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/news/nation/its-an-irish-australian-idol-to-be-sure/story-e6frg6nf-1111112590168 |title=It's an Irish Australian Idol, to be sure |work=The Australian |date=27 November 2006 |access-date=1 February 2010}}</ref> In December 2006, she signed a recording contract with Sony Music Australia, two weeks after ''Idol'' ended.<ref>{{cite news|last=Clune |first=Richard |title=Sony snaps up Dean and Jess|url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/news/nsw-act/sony-snaps-up-dean-and-jess/story-e6freuzi-1111112660752 |access-date=10 December 2006 |work=The Daily Telegraph |date=10 December 2006}}</ref>

===2010–12: ''Get 'Em Girls'' and ''The Sapphires'' soundtrack===
In January 2010, Mauboy signed to [[Universal Music]] for an exclusive worldwide long-term publishing agreement.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.getmusic.com.au/artist/news/detail.aspx?nid=1560&aid=59268 |title=Universal Music Group signs ARIA award winner, Jessica Mauboy |publisher=Get Music |date=19 January 2010|access-date=7 July 2010}}</ref> In May 2010, Mauboy collaborated with four other international artists representing their continents—[[Sean Kingston]] representing America, [[Jody Williams (Afrikaans singer)|Jody Williams]] representing Africa, [[Tabitha Nauser]] (Asia), [[Steve Appleton (musician)|Steve Appleton]] (Europe), with Mauboy representing Oceania—on the theme song for the [[2010 Summer Youth Olympics]] titled "[[Everyone (Olympics song)|Everyone]]". ===2019-2020: ''Hilda'' and Sony depart===
On 11 April 2019, Mauboy released her new single "[[Sunday (Jessica Mauboy song)|Sunday]]" from her upcoming fourth studio album, ''[[Hilda (album)|Hilda]]'' (October 2019).<ref name="Hilda">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/au/album/hilda/1468455639|title=Hilda by Jessica Mauboy|website=[[Apple Music]]|access-date=21 June 2019}}</ref> A video for the song was released on 30 April 2019, directed by Nick Waterman and produced by Jo Austin. The album's second single "[[Little Things (Jessica Mauboy song)|Little Things]]" was released on 21 June along with the pre-order for the album. ''Hilda'' debuted at number&nbsp;one on the ARIA Albums Chart.<ref name="ARIA Charts Oct19">{{cite web | url = https://www.ariacharts.com.au/news/2019/jessica-mauboy-debuts-at-1-with-hilda | title = Jessica Mauboy Debuts at #1 with ''Hilda''. | publisher = Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) | access-date = 27 October 2019 }}</ref> In October 2019 "Little Things" was certified as an ARIA platinum single. In December 2020, Mauboy left Sony after fourteen years and signed a new record deal with [[Warner Music Australia]],<ref>{{cite magazine|url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9499506/jessica-mauboy-signs-warner-music|title=Jessica Mauboy, Superstar Australian Pop Artist, Signs With Warner Music|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=15 December 2020|access-date=11 September 2021}}</ref> with new music to be released in 2021.
