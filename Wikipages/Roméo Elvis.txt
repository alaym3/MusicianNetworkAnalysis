'''Roméo Johnny Elvis Kiki Van Laeken''' ({{IPA-nl|vɑn ˈlaːkə(n)}}; born 13 December 1992), better known by his stage name '''Roméo Elvis'''<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.letemps.ch/culture/romeo-elvis-rap-cest-nouveau-rock|title=Romeo Elvis rap c'est nouveau rock|newspaper=Le Temps|date=9 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://m.huffingtonpost.fr/2018/03/03/romeo-elvis-nous-explique-la-difference-entre-le-rap-belge-le-rap-francais-et-nos-frites_a_23375691/|title=Romeo Elvis nous explique la difference entre le rap belge, le rap francais, et nos frites|website=HuffPost|date=3 March 2018|access-date=4 March 2018}}</ref> ({{IPA-fr|ʁɔmeo ɛlvis|lang}}), is a Belgian rapper and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] artist. 

His career began with the release of two [[Extended play|EPs]] in 2013 and 2014, titled ''Bruxelles c'est devenu la jungle'' and ''Famille nombreuse''. In 2016, his album EP ''Morale'' was released. In 2017 and 2018, two more albums were released: ''Morale 2'' and his reissue ''Morale 2 luxe'' (released from his collaboration with producer Le Motel). He has close connections with Belgian band [[L'Or du Commun]], duo Caballero & JeanJass, and French rapper [[Lomepal]].

== Biography ==
Roméo Elvis was born on 13 December 1992 in [[Uccle]], [[Belgium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.konbini.com/fr/entertainment-2/frenchmen-6-romeo-elvis/|title=FRENCHMEN No. 6 : Roméo Johnny Elvis Kiki Van Laeken, aka Roméo Elvis|first=Rachid|last=Majdoub|date=9 April 2017|website=Konbini.com|accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref> Interested in music since his youth,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leparisien.fr/culture-loisirs/musique/rap-romeo-elvis-roi-des-belges-a-la-conquete-de-la-france-11-02-2018-7552542.php|title=Rap : Roméo Elvis, roi des Belges à la conquête de la France|first=Emmanuel|last=Marolle|date=11 February 2018|website=Le Parisien|accessdate=27 December 2018}}</ref> he developed a style distinct from that of his father, the singer [[Marka (singer)|Marka]], his mother, the comedian Laurence Bibot, and his younger sister, the singer and pianist [[Angèle (singer)|Angèle]]. Roméo Elvis was raised in [[Linkebeek]], in the southern suburbs of [[Brussels]],<ref name=":2">{{cite web|title=Roméo Elvis, MC baryton|url=http://www.lalibre.be/culture/musique/romeo-elvis-mc-baryton-57f38b83cd70e9985fe9a675|agency=[[La Libre Belgique]]|date=6 October 2016|access-date=20 April 2017}}</ref> and later relocated to [[Forest, Belgium|Forest]].

After college, he continued his secondary studies at Saint-Luc Tournai Institute where he learned to paint and draw.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clique.tv/qui-es-tu-romeo-elvis-le-belge-qui-simpose-dans-le-rap-francais/|title=QUI ES-TU : Roméo Elvis, le Belge qui s'impose dans le rap français|date=15 February 2017|website=Clique.tc|accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref> It was during this time that Elvis began rapping with his friends. His artistic training continued at ESA 75, in Brussels, where he studied photo-journalism. While continuing to practice rap, he became close to members of L'Or du Commun.

While his first [[Extended play|EPs]] were being released, Roméo Elvis was working as a cashier at the supermarket chain [[Carrefour]]. Despite his success, he was not able to earn enough money from rapping alone. Shortly before the release of the album ''Bruxelles arrive'', he quit his job and devoted himself solely to music.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|title=Roméo Elvis : "Bruxelles est arrivée" L'Interview Nova serrés dans une caisse|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rlpysmNzV2A|via=YouTube|date=7 April 2017|access-date=21 April 2017}}</ref> His newest EP, ''Morale 2luxe'', was released on February 15, 2018. ''Morale 2luxe'' is a remake of his album, ''Morale 2'', released in March 2017.

Elvis suffers from [[tinnitus]], common among musicians. He has made references to this condition in many of his titles, such as ''L'oreille sifflante'' and ''Ma tête.''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/Romeo-elvis-ma-tete-lyrics|title=Roméo Elvis – Ma tête|website=Genius.com|accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref> In an interview with [[Radio Nova (France)|Radio Nova]], he explained that if he could earn a substantial income from rapping he would donate some of it to fight the disease. {{Cn|date=February 2020}}

In 2019 Elvis collaborated with French sportswear brand Lacoste to create a limited collection. However, after being named on social media for sexual assault he was dropped by the label.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://us.fashionnetwork.com/news/Lacoste-cuts-ties-with-rappers-moha-la-squale-romeo-elvis-both-accused-of-sexual-assault,1245485.html|title = Lacoste cuts ties with rappers Moha la Squale, Roméo Elvis, both accused of sexual assault}}</ref>

== Musical style and influence ==
The musical style of Roméo Elvis has evolved with the progression of his projects. While developing old-school instrumentals and a flow with similar ambiance to his first two EPs, his collaboration with the producer Le Motel on ''Morale'' and ''Morale 2'' profoundly changed his style. He currently allows himself to make more refrains and add electronic instrumentals, and in accord with the jazz of Le Motel, giving them a more current tone.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.surlmag.fr/romeo-elvis-interview-morale-2017# |title=Notre génération est plus inquiète que la précédente |access-date=1 October 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180713042945/http://www.surlmag.fr/romeo-elvis-interview-morale-2017/# |archive-date=13 July 2018 |url-status= |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Elvis' influences are numerous: the Belgian group L'or du Commun with which he made his rapping debut is one of his main ones. Their style can also be recognized in his first two projects with group members. He also frequently mentions the rapper Caballero as someone who greatly inspired him; they met while ''Bruxelles arrive'' was being recorded. Concerning French rap, Elvis considers the rapper Alpha Wann of the group [[1995 (band)|1995]] as "the rap master playing for me in terms of placement and in terms of how to say things in a very meaningful way". [[Fuzati]], leader of Klub des Loosers, strongly influences the rapper. Primarily in his somber aspect, but also in the manner that the rapper holds plenty of derision towards life. He also references the masked rapper in many of his titles, like in ''Drôle de décision.''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://genius.com/Romeo-elvis-drole-de-decision-lyrics|title=Roméo Elvis – Drôle de décision|website=Genius.com|accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref> Elvis is also a fan of the French artist [[Philippe Katerine]].

== Concerts and tours ==
While he began performing at concerts while growing closer to L'Or du Commun, it was during the release of ''Morale,'' in 2015, that Roméo Elvis and Le Motel began touring, with 19 performances across Belgium, France, and Luxembourg. The release of ''Morale 2 ''also marked the beginning of another, bigger tour with 29 performances and concerts in several large festivals such as [[Dour Festival]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dourfestival.eu/fr/program/lineup/overview/|title=Line Up – Dour Festival 2018 – 11+12+13+14+15 July|website=Dourfestival.eu|accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref> Rock en Seine,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rockenseine.com/programmation/#!programmation=artist$romeo-elvis-x-le-motel/101|title=Programmation – Rock en Seine – Festival – Paris|website=Rockenseine.com|accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref> le [[Printemps de Bourges]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.printemps-bourges.com/fr/programme/programme.html?jour=2017-04-20|title=Le Printemps de Bourges|first=Boris|last=Vedel|website=Printemps-bourges.com|accessdate=2 October 2018}}</ref> and Garorock.
