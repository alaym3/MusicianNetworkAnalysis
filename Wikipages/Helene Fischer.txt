'''Helene Fischer''' (German pronunciation: [[Help:IPA/Standard German|[heˈleːnə ˈfɪʃɐ]]]; born 5 August 1984)<ref name="topnews24" /> is a German singer, dancer, entertainer, television presenter, and actress. Since her debut in 2005, she has won numerous awards, including 17 [[Echo (music award)|Echo]] awards, four "Die Krone der Volksmusik" awards and three [[Bambi Award|Bambi]] awards. She has sold at least 15 million records.<ref name="certifications">Music certifications: [http://www.ifpi.at/?section=goldplatin AT] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130701062753/http://www.ifpi.at/?section=goldplatin |date=1 July 2013 }} [http://swisscharts.com/search_certifications.asp?search=helene+fischer CH] [http://www.musikindustrie.de/gold_platin_datenbank/#topSearch DE] [http://www.ifpi.dk/index.php?pk_menu=284 DK] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141219150604/http://www.ifpi.dk/index.php?pk_menu=284 |date=19 December 2014 }}</ref> In June 2014, her multi-platinum 2013 album ''[[Farbenspiel]]'' became the most downloaded album ever by a German artist and is currently the sixth bestselling album of all time in Germany.<ref name="downlo">{{cite web|url=http://www.rp-online.de/kultur/musik/helene-fischer-bricht-alltime-download-rekord-aid-1.4288647|title=Helene Fischer bricht Alltime-Download-Rekord|language=de|access-date=9 June 2014}}</ref> Her signature song "[[Atemlos durch die Nacht]]" was the bestselling song in Germany in 2014. She has had the best-selling album of the year in Germany five times, in 2013, 2014, 2015, 2017, and 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=TOP 100 ALBUM-JAHRESCHARTS - 2013 |url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/charts/album-jahr/for-date-2013 |website=OFFIZIELLE DEUTSCHE CHARTS}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=OFFIZIELLE DEUTSCHE CHARTS |url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/charts/album-jahr/for-date-2018 |website=TOP 100 ALBUM-JAHRESCHARTS - 2018 |access-date=12 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=OFFIZIELLE DEUTSCHE CHARTS |url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/charts/album-jahr/for-date-2017 |website=TOP 100 ALBUM-JAHRESCHARTS - 2017 |access-date=12 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=OFFIZIELLE DEUTSCHE CHARTS |url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/charts/album-jahr/for-date-2015 |website=TOP 100 ALBUM-JAHRESCHARTS - 2015 |access-date=12 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=OFFIZIELLE DEUTSCHE CHARTS |url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/charts/album-jahr/for-date-2014 |website=TOP 100 ALBUM-JAHRESCHARTS - 2014 |access-date=12 August 2019}}</ref> She ranked No. 8 on [[Forbes]]' list of "The World's Highest-Paid Women In Music 2018", earning $US32 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2018/11/19/highest-paid-women-in-music-2018-katy-perry-taylor-swift-beyonce/#4f19396a6a24|title=The World's Highest-Paid Women In Music 2018|language=en|access-date=3 August 2019}}</ref> Fischer has been referred to as the "Queen of [[Schlager music|Schlager]]".<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/jul/08/ja-ja-ding-dong-how-schlager-found-its-joyful-place-pop-eurovision |title=Ja Ja Ding Dong! ==Early life==
Helene Fischer was born Jelena Petrovna Fischer ({{lang-ru|Елена Петровна Фишер}}) in the Siberian city of [[Krasnoyarsk]], [[Russian SFSR]], [[Soviet Union]], to Pyotr and Marina Fischer.<ref name="topnews24" /> Her father worked as a physical education teacher and her mother as an engineer. Her paternal grandparents were [[Black Sea Germans]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aussiedlerbeauftragter.de/SharedDocs/Kurzmeldungen/AUSB/DE/koschyk-und-zertik-treffen-helene-fischer.html|title=Im Gespräch mit Helene Fischer im Deutschen Bundestag|website=Bundesministerium des Innern, für Bau und Heimat|language=de-DE|access-date=2018-11-16}}</ref> who were among those sent to [[Forced settlements in the Soviet Union#"Preventive" deportations of nationalities in 1941–1942|forced settlements in Kazakhstan and Siberia]].<ref name="topnews24">{{cite web| url=http://www.topnews24.ru/press/aif/12510-khelena-fisher-ja-rodilas-v-sibiri.html |title= Хелена Фишер: "Я родилась в Сибири" /Helene Fischer: "I was born in Siberia" | publisher= TopNews24.ru (Poslednie Novosti) | date=25 February 2009| access-date= June 2, 2017|language=ru|quote= Хелен ФИШЕР родилась 5 августа 1984 года в Красноярске. В июне 1998 года с родителями эмигрировала в Германию. После окончания школы Stage & Musical School / Helene Fisher was born in Krasnoyarsk, 5 August 1984. In June 1998, with her parents she emigrated to Germany. After graduation from Stage & Musical School in Frankfurt...; БАБУШКИ и дедушки Лены Фишер разделили судьбу сотен тысяч поволжских немцев, которых в 1941 году сослали в Казахстан и Сибирь. / Her grandparents shared the fate of hundreds of thousands of Volga Germans, who in 1941 were exiled to Kazakhstan and Siberia.|archive-date=October 5, 2015| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151005073208/http://gazeta.aif.ru/_/online/enisei/585/03_01|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 1988, at the age of three-and-a-half, she emigrated with her parents and her six-year older sister to [[Wöllstein]], West Germany.<ref name="westen">{{cite web|url=https://www.derwesten.de/kultur/freizeit/Helene-Fischer-Schoen-wie-im-Maerchen-id3720654.html|title=Helene Fischer: Schön wie im Märchen|access-date=5 November 2010|work=Der Westen|language=de}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ylyv8EgwFfU|title=- YouTube|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>

After graduating from school, Fischer attended the Frankfurt Stage & Musical School for three years, where she studied singing and acting.<ref name="bio">{{cite web|url=http://www.helene-fischer.de/hf/helene-fischer/biografie/helene-fischer-stationen-einer-karriere |title=Helene Fischer – Stationen einer Karriere |language=de |access-date=5 November 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110105110045/http://www.helene-fischer.de/hf/helene-fischer/biografie/helene-fischer-stationen-einer-karriere |archive-date=5 January 2011 }}</ref> During this time, she performed on stage at the ''[[Staatstheater Darmstadt]]'', as well as on the stage of the ''[[Volkstheater Frankfurt]]''.

==Career==
{{BLP sources section|date=July 2014}}
During her time at the Stage & Musical School, Fischer's mother secretly copied a demo CD with six of Fischer's songs and sent it to different recording studios to gauge the reaction of professionals.

Fischer's debut on stage took place on 14 May 2005, in a programme by the TV channel [[ARD (broadcaster)|ARD]]. It was a duet with the singer [[Florian Silbereisen]]. The two later became a couple.<ref name="westen" />

She has won 17 [[Echo Music Prize|Echo awards]], the "Goldene Henne" award seven times, and the "Krone der Volksmusik" prize four times. All her albums and DVDs have achieved multiple gold and double [[List of music recording certifications|platinum status]]. Her songs, with their stories of the everyday worries and woes of home-loving folks, are lyrically close to what could be called "country music" although musically quite different.

Her fanbase extends beyond Germany, Austria and Switzerland to Belgium, Canada, the Netherlands, Denmark, Norway, the United Kingdom, Finland, the United States, and New Zealand.

She released her first English album, ''The English Ones'', on 7 June 2010. The album was produced by {{ill|Jean Frankfurter|de}}, a German producer, composer and arranger. On singing in English, Fischer commented: "I have always dreamed of singing my songs in another language. English is the language of [[Country music|Country]], the music that we call 'Schlager'. Language is a tool, but it is important that what you sing comes from the heart – and that is what this album is about."
[[File:Helene Fischer Wiener Stadthalle Mai 2011.jpg|thumb|upright=0.9|Fischer on Tour with orchestra May 2011]]
In January 2013, she made her acting debut in an episode of the German TV series ''[[Das Traumschiff]]''.

On 4 October 2013, she released her album ''[[Farbenspiel]]'' in Germany. She went on tour in 2014 and a big stadium tour in 2015.<ref>[http://www.vorverkaufstarts.de/helene-fischer-tour-tickets/ Helene Fischer Tourdates]. Vorverkaufstarts.de Retrieved 5 October 2013.</ref> She played in the [[Olympiastadion (Berlin)|Olympia Stadium in Berlin]] in front of 120,000 people. The album received platinum after five days in Germany, Denmark and Austria. ''Farbenspiel'' is the most successful release of a German female artist in the first week of release in the last 10 years. To date, over 2.4 million copies of the album have been sold.

On 13 November 2015, Fischer released a Christmas album entitled ''Weihnachten''. It consists of two CDs, the first being Christmas songs sung in German and German dialects, and the second is songs sung in English.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Helene Fischer - Weihnachten|url=https://www.ukmix.org/forum/music/albums-aa/108931-helene-fischer-weihnachten|access-date=2021-04-27|website=UKMIX Forums|language=en}}</ref> The album reached number 1 of the year-end charts in both Germany and Austria and received altogether 14 platinums in both countries. It sold over 1.3 million copies.

In 2016, she sang the German version of "[[How Far I'll Go]]" (''Ich bin bereit''), the title song of Disney's ''[[Moana (2016 film)|Moana]]''.

On 12 May 2017, she released her eighth studio album, which bears her own name of ''Helene Fischer''. It was released by Polydor. The album debuted at number one on the Austrian, German, and Swiss Albums Chart and reached the top ten in the Netherlands and the [[Flemish Region|Flemish]] region of Belgium. In Germany, ''Helene Fischer'' sold more than 300,000 copies in its first week of release, making it the highest-selling debut since [[Herbert Grönemeyer]]'s 2002 album ''Mensch''. It won nine platinums in Germany and Austria and has sold over 1 million copies in both countries to date. She performed all her new songs and several hits from previous albums in the Kesselhaus, Munich and released the album ''Helene Fischer – Das Konzert aus dem Kesselhaus'' showcasing this performance. In June 2018, she started her second stadium tour, performing in Germany, Switzerland, Netherlands, and Austria. In several locations on this tour, she performed up to five times in a row. Her show was developed together with 45 Degrees of [[Cirque du Soleil]].<ref>[https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/helene-fischer-commissioned-45-degrees-cirque-du-soleils-events-and-special-projects-company-to-design-her-ground-breaking-new-tour-671249394.html NewsWire.com Retrieved 23 November 2018].</ref> In October 2018, ''[[The New York Times]]'' ranked Fischer seventh in terms of the world's biggest touring musician acts of the year. In the 2018 [[Forbes]] list of top-earning female artists worldwide, she ranked 8th.<ref>[https://www.forbes.com/sites/zackomalleygreenburg/2018/11/19/highest-paid-women-in-music-2018-katy-perry-taylor-swift-beyonce/ The World's Highest-Paid Women In Music 2018.] Forbes.com Retrieved 23 November 2018.</ref><ref name="paid">{{cite news|last=Grenier|first=Elizabeth|date=22 November 2018|title=Better paid than Britney Spears: Who is Helene Fischer?|work=Deutsche Welle|url=https://www.dw.com/en/better-paid-than-britney-spears-who-is-helene-fischer/a-46405431|access-date=8 July 2020}}</ref> This also makes her the currently most successful non-English singer.

===''Die Helene Fischer Show''===
Fischer hosts the yearly music entertainment show ''Die Helene Fischer Show'', which is broadcast during Christmas on German public TV. In this show, she has performed with various artists, including [[David Garrett (musician)|David Garrett]], [[Michael Bolton]], [[Tom Jones (singer)|Tom Jones]], [[Max Raabe]], [[Take That]], [[Udo Jürgens]], [[Olly Murs]], [[Andrea Berg]], [[Bryan Adams]], [[Unheilig]], [[Kiefer Sutherland]], [[Freya Ridings]], [[Nick Carter (musician)|Nick Carter]] and [[Queen (band)|Queen]]. She accompanied [[Lindsey Stirling]] with an acrobatic performance.
