'''Marius Lucas Antonio Listhrop'''<ref name="LXRD RECORDS LIMITED">{{cite web |title=LXRD RECORDS LIMITED |url=https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/11069397/officers |publisher=Companies House|accessdate=2 March 2019}}</ref> (born 19 June 1994), better known as '''Scarlxrd''' (pronounced "Scarlord"), is a British rapper. He is known for his unique musical style combining elements of [[trap music]] and [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]]. Formerly a [[YouTube]] personality known as '''Mazzi Maz''', Scarlxrd began a career in music as the vocalist for [[nu metal]] band Myth City, before debuting as a rapper under his Scarlxrd moniker.

He rose to prominence after the music video for his single "Heart Attack" which gained significant attention, holding over 90 million views on [[YouTube]] as of April 2021.<ref>{{cite web |title=scarlxrd - HEART ATTACK [Prod. JVCXB] |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OC7cNS0GINo |website=YouTube |publisher=scarlxrd |accessdate=23 June 2017}}</ref> He has since released other successful songs, namely "King, Scar", "6 Feet", and "Head Gxne".

== YouTube career ==
Before he started a career in music, Listhrop began uploading videos on [[YouTube]] as Mazzi Maz. His personality on the site was described as a "perky" and "super-smiley YouTuber" who recorded and published [[vlog]]s in his bedroom.<ref name="NME">{{cite web |last1=Bassett |first1=Jordan |title=The strange tale of Scarlxrd, the perky vlogger turned rap-metal overlord |url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/strange-tale-scarlxrd-perky-vlogger-turned-rap-metal-overlord-2321894 |work=[[NME]] |accessdate=5 June 2018}}</ref><ref name="MetalHammer" /> He often collaborated with friends and fellow YouTube personalities [[Sam Pepper]] and [[Caspar Lee]].<ref name="NME" /> From late-2013 to mid-2014, Listhrop and Pepper embarked on a worldwide comedy-music tour, "WDGAF Tour".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Campbell |first1=Tina |title=Sam Pepper: Meet the internet music and comedy sensation soon set to tour the UK |url=http://metro.co.uk/2014/04/21/sam-pepper-meet-the-internet-music-and-comedy-sensation-set-to-tour-the-uk-4704530/ |website=Metro |location=UK |accessdate=22 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=UK YouTube Stars To Launch Comedy Show at Avalon Sept. 13 |url=https://www.huffingtonpost.com/shira-lazar/uk-youtube-stars-to-launc_b_3817791.html |newspaper=HuffPost |accessdate=22 March 2016}}</ref> By February 2017, Listhrop removed all of his Mazzi Maz videos from his YouTube channel and has since rebranded it to his Scarlxrd moniker. In a 2018 Interview, Listhrop described his time as a YouTube personality and posting videos on the site as "soul-destroying".<ref name="NME" />

== Music career ==
=== 2014–2016: Myth City ===
[[File:Scarlxrd 2014.jpg|thumb|Mazzi Maz speaking at the 2014 VidCon at the Anaheim Convention Center in Anaheim, California]]
In 2014, Listhrop founded the [[nu metal]]/[[rap rock]] band Myth City, in which he performed as the vocalist.<ref name="NME" /><ref name="MetalHammer">{{cite web |last1=Morton |first1=Luke |title=Scarlxrd: The New Sound of the Underground |url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/scarlxrd-the-new-sound-of-the-underground |publisher=[[Metal Hammer]] |accessdate=9 June 2018}}</ref><ref name="DrillKing">{{cite web |last1=Washington |first1=Daquan |title=Who is Scarlxrd? |url=https://www.drillking.org/who-is-scarlxrd/ |publisher=Drill King |accessdate=5 June 2018}}</ref> Listhrop also described the band's sound as a fusion of [[Hip hop music|rap music]] and [[grindcore]].<ref name="MetalHammer" /> The band's YouTube channel quickly gained a following mainly consisting of Mazzi Maz fans, who gave the group a mixed reception; fans responded positively to Listhrop's [[Screaming (music)|screamed vocals]], but were confused on the contrast between the band's harsh musical style and Listhrop's "smiley" YouTube videos.<ref name="NME" /> Myth City released their self-titled debut EP on 28 February 2015.<ref>{{cite web |title=Myth City by Myth City on iTunes |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/myth-city/969591793 |publisher=[[iTunes Store]] |accessdate=9 June 2018}}</ref>

=== 2016–2018: Early albums and breakthrough ===
[[file:Scardlxrd - Cool Stage - Madrid (28543353977) (cropped).jpg|thumb|Listhrop performing in 2018]]
In August 2016, Scarlxrd debuted his new moniker with the release of "Girlfriend". The music video depicts him unmasked and rapping over a "humid, tropical hip-hop track".<ref name="NME" /><ref>{{cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0NoV66hzeWk |title=scarlxrd – GIRLFRIEND |date=27 August 2016 |via=YouTube}}</ref> By the end of 2016, he released two albums, his self-titled album (stylised in [[Japan]]ese characters as ''スカー藩主'') and ''Rxse''. After the release of two more albums, ''Cabin Fever'' and ''Chaxsthexry'', in April 2017, Scarlxrd released the video for "Chain$aw" on 31 May; a week later, he released the music video for "King, Scar".<ref name="Vice">{{cite AV media |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/ywxyak/noisey-raps-who-the-fxck-is-scarlxrd |title=Who the Fxck Is scarlxrd? |date=4 May 2018 |newspaper=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]}}</ref> On 23 June, he published the music video for "Heart Attack" and the video quickly gained popularity; he credits his consistency of releasing music videos, specifically the videos for "Chain$aw" and "King, Scar", for the video's popularity rise.<ref name="Vice" /> On 29 September, he released his fifth studio album ''Lxrdszn''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lxrdszn by Scarlxrd on iTunes |url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/lxrdszn/1287212974 |publisher=iTunes Store |accessdate=9 June 2018}}</ref> “King, Scar” and "Chain$aw" were also released as music kits in [[Counter-Strike: Global Offensive]].

In February 2018, it was announced that Scarlxrd would perform at the [[Reading and Leeds Festivals line-ups#2018|2018]] [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]].<ref>{{cite web |title=More acts announced for Reading & Leeds Festival 2018 |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/reading-leeds-festival-2018-more-acts-announced-2248380 |work=[[NME]] |accessdate=10 June 2018}}</ref> In April, it was revealed that he would be featured on [[Carnage (DJ)|Carnage]]'s second studio album, ''[[Battered Bruised & Bloody]]'', released on 13 April; Scarlxrd performed on the song "Up Nxw".<ref>{{cite web |title=Migos, Lil Pump and More Will Appear on Carnage's 'Battered, Bruised & Bloodied' Album |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/04/migos-lil-pump-carnage-battered-bruised-bloodied-album/ |magazine=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |accessdate=9 June 2018}}</ref> On 4 May, he released his sixth studio album and major label debut ''Dxxm'' via [[Island Records]].<ref name="MetalHammer" /><ref>{{cite web |title=Dxxm by Scarlxrd on iTunes |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/dxxm/1374691411 |publisher=iTunes Store |accessdate=9 June 2018}}</ref>

=== 2018–2020: Continued major label releases===
After releasing singles from mid-2018 to early-2019 in anticipation of an upcoming album, including "Hxw They Judge", "Berzerk", "Sx Sad", and "Head Gxne", Scarlxrd announced his seventh studio album, ''Infinity'', which was released on 15 March 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Hear Rap/Metal Crossover Artists Scarlxrd's Aggro New Burner 'Head Gxne' |url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/hear-rapmetal-crossover-artist-scarlxrds-aggro-new-burner-head-gxne |website=Revolver |accessdate=6 March 2019}}</ref> He was a nominee in the 2019 [[Kerrang! Awards|Kerrang! Award]]s for [[Kerrang! Awards 2019|Best British Breakthrough]].<ref>''Kerrang!'' magazine, issue 1774, May 2019, page 8.</ref><ref name=SoundMouth>{{cite web|title=Kerrang! Awards 2019 Preview|url=https://soundmouth.blogspot.com/2019/06/kerrang-awards-2019-preview-my-picks.html|work=SoundMouth|accessdate=14 June 2019|date=10 June 2019}}</ref> Later that year, "The Purge" was featured on the first season of the [[HBO]] television series ''[[Euphoria (American TV series)|Euphoria]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Here's The Complete Soundtrack To HBO's Euphoria |url=https://www.menshealth.com/entertainment/a28121565/euphoria-season-1-soundtrack-songs/ |website=Men's Health |accessdate=10 March 2020}}</ref> Scarlxrd released his second album of 2019 titled ''Immxrtalisatixn'' on 4 October.<ref name="immxr">{{Cite web |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/immxrtalisatixn/1466959478 |title=IMMXRTALISATIXN |date=2019-10-04 |website=Apple Music}}{{Cite web |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/immxrtalisatixn/1466959478 |title=IMMXRTALISATIXN |date=2019-10-04 |website=Apple Music}}</ref> He released his third 2019 studio album on 13 December, titled ''Acquired Taste Vxl 1''. On 19 December, "I Can Dx What I Want" was announced as the official theme song for the [[WWE]] [[NXT UK]] event, [[NXT UK TakeOver: Blackpool II]].

=== 2021–present: Independent releases ===
On 14 January 2021, Scarlxrd released "Let the Wxrld Burn" via his independent label Lxrd Records, marking his first release since his departure from Island Records. On 5 February, Scarlxrd released his 12th studio album ''Dxxm II'', a sequel to his 2018 project ''Dxxm''. Later that year, he featured on "Dead Desert" by [[Trippie Redd]], which also featured [[Zillakami]]. Scarlxrd also released a collaborative EP with [[Ghostemane]] titled ''Lxrdmage''.

== Artistry ==
[[File:Scarlxrd - Rock am Ring 2018-2659.jpg|thumb|Scarlxrd wearing his signature surgical mask at a concert in 2018]]
Scarlxrd's combination of [[trap music]] and [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] has been dubbed "[[trap metal]]", a style which multiple publications have described him as a pioneer of.<ref>{{cite web |title=NEWS: Scarlxrd drops video for brand new track, 'I Need Space'! |date=September 14, 2018 |url=http://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-scarlxrd-drops-video-for-brand-new-track-i-need-space/ |access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://afropunk.com/2018/05/unleash-your-fury-with-trap-metal-revolutionary-scarlxrds-explosive-dxxm/|title=Unleash your fury with trap metal revolutionary SCARLXRD's explosive 'DXXM' - AFROPUNK|date=May 23, 2018}}</ref><ref name=MetalHammer /><ref>{{cite magazine|last1=JAMIESON |first1=BRII |title=This is Trap Metal |journal=[[Kerrang!]] |date=23 May 2018}}</ref> He is often associated with [[SoundCloud rap]] artists.<ref>{{cite web |title=Carnage "Battered Bruised & Bloody" Review |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/carnage-battered-bruised-and-bloody-review-news.47828.html |publisher=HotNewHipHop |accessdate=9 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=6ix9ine's debut album is as fun as he is ridiculous |url=https://ndsmcobserver.com/2018/03/6ix9ine-day69-album/ |newspaper=The Observer |accessdate=9 June 2018}}</ref> In a 2018 article, ''[[NME]]'' described him as a "tormented outsider hip-hop artist".<ref name="NME" /> They also called him a "[[rap metal]] overlord" and his style to include "screamed vocals, claustrophobic trap beats and ear-shredding bursts of distorted guitar",<ref name="NME" /> as well as calling it a fusion of metal and "[[Nihilism|nihilistic]] rap".<ref>{{cite web |title=10 new acts that'll blow your mind at Reading & Leeds this weekend |url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/10-new-acts-thatll-blow-fucking-mind-reading-leeds-weekend-2370041 |publisher=[[NME]] |accessdate=17 September 2018}}</ref> Scarlxrd grew up listening to heavy metal music and cites hip hop artists including [[Missy Elliott]], [[Nelly]] and [[Eminem]] as influences.<ref name="NME" /> He also stated that he formed Myth City after listening to [[Linkin Park]], [[Rage Against the Machine]], [[Incubus (band)|Incubus]] and [[Deftones]].<ref name="MetalHammer" />

He is known to replace the letter "o" with "x", which is shown in his stage name and song titles. Scarlxrd's visual aesthetic within his music videos and clothing style is heavily influenced from [[Culture of Japan|Japanese culture]], especially [[anime]]. His signature [[surgical mask]], which he wears to distance himself from his YouTube image,<ref name="DrillKing" /> was inspired by the Japanese [[manga]] ''[[Tokyo Ghoul]]''.<ref name="MetalHammer" /> He began wearing the mask less often after the release of ''Infinity''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Scarlxrd Ditches YouTuber Lifestyle and His Black Mask for Screamo Success |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/video/2019/05/scarlxrd-interview/ |publisher=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |accessdate=6 October 2019}}</ref> Scarlxrd also cited American heavy metal band [[Slipknot (band)|Slipknot]] as an inspiration towards his visuals.<ref name="MetalHammer" />
