'''Miguel Ángel Durán Jr.''' (born September 24, 1981)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/fuego-mn0001011679|title=Fuego – Biography, Albums, Streaming Links|website=AllMusic|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> known professionally as '''Fuego''' ("fire"),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/fuego|title=Fuego – MTV UK|website=Mtv.co.uk|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter and founder of Fireboy Inc./Sauce Gang, currently signed to Lionfish Entertainment.

== Early life ==
Durán was born to Dominican parents in [[Washington, D.C.]] and was raised in [[Langley Park, Maryland]]. He grew up listening to his father's instrumental harmonies and listening to the timbales in Salsa and Merengue on a vinyl LP in his living room. Growing up with Spanish tropical music at home, Fuego came to appreciate Latin musicians such as [[Frankie Ruiz]], [[Juan Luis Guerra]] and [[Héctor Lavoe|Hector Lavoe]]. While on his own at school and with his friends he also developed an appreciation for American rappers such as [[Tupac Shakur|2Pac]], [[The Notorious B.I.G.]] and [[Bone Thugs-n-Harmony]].{{citation needed|date=August 2017}}

Fuego's parents sent him to the Dominican Republic, where he attended school for a couple of years and, with his brother Rickylindo, absorbed the culture there. In the late 1990s, Fuego formed part of a group called 3men2 (Tremendo) with friends and his brother. They started a local movement and performing at local venues. Later, after the group split, Durán meet DJ Menace, one of the first local DJs to play Latin music and introduce Reggaeton to the Washington, DC Area. Menace founded Migo Productions and booked Fuego shows in the area, helping to get exposure. Menace died in a car accident in 2003.{{citation needed|date=August 2017}}

In 2004, Fuego was booked as an opening act to Reggaeton concert at a local night club where Daddy Yankee and Reggaeton Duo Plan B were headlining, He sang "Aqui Con Migo" while a member of Plan B; Chencho watched him perform, he was shocked by Durán's talent an to see how the crowd was singing along word for word.{{citation needed|date=August 2017}}

== Career ==
=== 2005–2011: ''Chosen Few III: The Movie'' and ''La Musica del Futuro'' ===
In 2005, with the help of Chencho from reggaeton duo [[Plan B (duo)|Plan B]], he was featured in a compilation album (''[[El Draft Del Reggaeton|El Draft 2005]]'') with his song "Me Gustan Todas".<ref name="last.fm">{{cite web|url=https://www.last.fm/music/Fuego/+wiki|title=Fuego age, hometown, biography|website=Last.fm|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> After he was part of "El Draft Del Reggaeton" he was signed to Chosen Few Emerald Entertainment, Inc.<ref name="last.fm" /> In 2007, "Hustlin Time" featuring [[Rick Ross]] was released to promote ''Chosen Few III: The Movie''. In 2008, Fuego scored his first big hit "Mi Alma Se Muere", which was the lead single from ''Chosen Few III: The Movie''.{{citation needed|date=December 2018}} "Mi Alma Se Muere" Remix featuring [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]] and [[Omega "El Fuerte"]] was released later that year, and the remix peaked at No. 22 with over 24 weeks on the Top 100 Billboards Tropical.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.latinrapper.com/chosen-few-present-fuego.html |title=Latinrapper.com: Chosen Few Present Fuego |access-date=September 9, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111011143030/http://latinrapper.com/chosen-few-present-fuego.html |archive-date=October 11, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2009, he released "Super Estrella" featuring [[Omega y su Mambo Violento|Omega "El Fuerte"]] which was the first single to his debut album ''La Musica del Futuro''. In December 2009, his second single was released "Que Buena Tu Ta" featuring [[Deevani]] which peaked at No. 2 on the Top 100 Billboards Tropical for 12 weeks.<ref name="Tropical">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/tropical-songs|title=Tropical Music: Top Tropical Songs Charts|website=Billboard|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> In 2010, two remixes to "Que Buena Tu Ta" were released—one featuring [[Serani]] and a Dominican version featuring Mozart La Para, [[Black Point (artist)|Black Point]], [[Sensato del Patio]], [[Los Pepes]], Monkey Black and Villanosam. In April 2010, Fuego released his third single "Ya Te Olvide" and in June 2010 Fuego an online release titled "Una Vaina Loca" (which samples Gyptian's "[[Hold You (Gyptian song)|Hold Yuh]]"). By 2011, "Una Vaina Loca" became an international hit with more than 30 million plays on [[YouTube]]. Fuego's debut album ''La Musica del Futuro'' was released on August 3, 2010 (CD/Digital) and peaked at No. 36 on the ''Billboard'' Top Latin Albums chart. He was nominated for [[Premio Lo Nuestro 2011]] in the category Revelacion del año urbano.

=== 2012–2014: Touring and ''Fireboy Forever (The Mixtape)'' ===
In 2012 Fuego spent months touring the world, visiting Europe, Central America, South America and North America. In December 2012, Fuego prepared the release of his single "Mala Mia"; he released its music video in February 2013. The same month, Fuego's mother died, and he slowed promotion and releases for several months. Later that year, in August 2013, he was featured on the Mambo remix of [[Jencarlos Canela]]'s "I Love It", which was produced by Bones El Galactico. In October 2013, he released his second single, "Prendelo", which was followed by its music video in March 2014. Fuego followed this in April 2014 with the digital single "U La La", a reggaeton record produced by Scarspro and [[Don Omar]]'s producers A&X (Alcover & Xtassy), In early May 2014 Fuego's single "Prendelo" reached number 7 on the ''Billboard'' Latin Tropical chart.<ref name="Tropical" /> On Christmas Day 2014, Fuego released ''Fireboy Forever (The Mixtape)'', which featured 19 songs including collaborations with [[Sensato del Patio|Sensato]], [[Farruko]], Rickylindo, Notch, Dynasty ("The Prince of Dance Hall"), [[Zion & Lennox]] amongst others.

=== 2015: New music and creating ''Fireboy Forever 2'' ===
In early May 2015, Fuego released "iLike", produced by DJ Chino ([[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull]]'s DJ). This was followed in June 2015 by the reggaeton single "Solos", which was produced by Scarspro and Jaeycol Federal. In mid-June 2015 Fuego teamed up with Colombian artist [[Fanny Lu]] and released the song "Quiero Reir", a merengue electronico song produced by Maffio.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.2001.com.ve/en-el-chisme/104198/fuego-grabo-junto-a-fanny-lu-su-nuevo-hit--quiero-reir-.html|title=Fuego grabó junto a Fanny Lu su nuevo hit "Quiero Reír" – En el Chisme – 2001.com.ve|website=2001.com.ve|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> In early July 2015 Fuego landed a feature on [[Pitbull (rapper)|Pitbull's]] song "Mami Mami" from his Spanish album ''[[Dale (album)|Dale]]'', which was released on July 17, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/6634341/pitbull-donald-trump-album-new-album-dale-exclusive-interview|title=Pitbull on His New Spanish-Language Album 'Dale,' Donald Trump and Why He's the 'American Dream': Exclusive|website=Billboard|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>

In mid-August 2015 Fuego announces via his social media pages that he was working on ''Fireboy Forever 2: The Album''. In late August 2015 Fuego covered/remixed [[Drake (rapper)|Drake's]] song "[[Hotline Bling]]" in Spanish, calling it "Cuando Suena El Bling". It became a hit on SoundCloud. The blog ''The Muse'' on [[Jezebel (Website)|Jezebel]] called it "better than Drake's",<ref>[http://themuse.jezebel.com/this-spanish-version-of-hotline-bling-is-better-than-dr-1727675722 "This Spanish Version of Hotline Bling is Better Than Drake's"]. ''The Muse''. {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151008094241/http://themuse.jezebel.com/this-spanish-version-of-hotline-bling-is-better-than-dr-1727675722 |date=October 8, 2015 }}</ref> while the ''Más Sun Times'' complimented it, saying it "will make your Monday better".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mas.suntimes.com/entertainment/10/152/130753/fuego-hotline-bling|title=There is a Spanish version of 'Hotline Bling' that will make your Monday better|work=Más|last=Favelevic|first=Ivan|date=August 31, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305053624/http://mas.suntimes.com/entertainment/10/152/130753/fuego-hotline-bling|archive-date=March 5, 2016|access-date=December 5, 2018}}</ref> ''[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]'' magazine ranked it number two on their list of the best and worst covers of "Hotline Bling", and was the only Spanish version on the list.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.papermag.com/updated-who-is-the-hotline-king-ranking-the-best-and-worst-covers-of-h-1427651742.html|title=UPDATED: Who is the Hotline King? Ranking the Best (and Worst) Covers of "Hotline Bling"|date=September 28, 2015|website=Papaermag.com|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> ''[[Vice (magazine)|Noisey]]'' ranked it number three on their list of "Hotline Bling" remixes, making it again the only Spanish version to be included on the list.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/6e4mwp/ever-since-i-left-the-city-you-you-youuu-hotline-bling-remixes-ranked|title=Ever Since I Left the City You, You, Youuu: "Hotline Bling" Remixes, Ranked|first1=Craig|last1=Jenkins|first2=Jabbari|last2=Weekes|date=October 19, 2015|website=Noisey.vice.com|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> [[The Hundreds]] placed it at number three on their "10 Track Commandments, Vol. 6 :: Sh*t You Shouldn't Sleep On" list.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thehundreds.com/blogs/content/10-track-commandments-vol-6|title=10 TRACK COMMANDMENTS, VOL. 6 :: Shit You Shouldn't Sleep On|website=The Hundreds|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> ''Noisey'' later published an article breaking down the creation of the remix and some of Fuego's influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/6x8v9w/fuego-spanish-hotline-bling-interview|title=We Talked to Fuego, the Guy Who Made the Incredible Spanish Version of Drake's "Hotline Bling"|date=September 11, 2015|website=Noisey.vice.com|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>

Following the success of his previous cover, on September 13, 2015, Fuego released another, this time [[Yo Gotti]] and [[Young Thug]]'s song "Rihanna". The remix became a hit on SoundCloud once again, and on September 16, blog site Remezcla picked up the song and wrote an article stating that "This rework might just be better than Young Thug's English version, since we can't understand what Young Thug is saying anyway".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://remezcla.com/releases/music/fuego-track-rihanna/|title=Fuego Bodies His Spanish Remix of "Rihanna"|first=Janice|last=Llamoca|date=September 16, 2015|website=Remezcla|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> On September 20, the remix gained the attention of ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]'', who added it to episode 12 of their weekly show on [[Apple Music]]'s [[Beats 1]] station.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/6aznqg/vice-beats-1-radio-show-episode-twelve|title=Tune in Sunday for Episode 12 of VICE on Beats 1|author=Noisey Staff|date=September 19, 2015|website=Noisey.vice.com|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>

On October 5, 2015, Fuego's "Quiero Reir" record with Fanny Lu reached number one on Venezuela's [[National-Report|National Report]] chart in its Top Latino Category and in the general chart at a national level.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.notitarde.com/|title=Notitarde de hoy – Notitarde sucesos – Notitarde Valencia -|website=Notitarde.com|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>

On October 11, 2015, Fuego released another remix, turning [[Majid Jordan]] and Drake's "My Love" into a Spanish cover called "Mi Amor". Remezcla called Fuego the "undisputed king of Spanish remixes" and said that "Fuego continues making the type of remixes that are needed for a Spanish-speaking hip-hop-loving audience".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://remezcla.com/releases/music/fuego-my-love-spanish-remix/|title=Fuego Continues Slaying His Spanish Remixes With Majid Jordan's "My Love"|first=Janice|last=Llamoca|date=October 15, 2015|website=Remezcla.com|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>

On October 16, 2015, Fuego released "Loca Con La Vaina", the first single from his album ''Fireboy Forever 2''. The song, a Spanish trap/hip hop record with heavy bass, was produced by Luyo & Capi of the Associates. On October 29, 2015, Fuego released "Mambo Para Bailar", a merengue electronico song produced by Maffio.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://remezcla.com/releases/music/fuego-track-mambo-para-bailar/|title=Fuego Reigns Over More Than Just Spanish Remixes With "Mambo Para Bailar"|first=John|last=Calderon|date=October 29, 2015|website=Remezcla|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>

On November 16, 2015, Fuego released "Antidito", a Spanish remix of [[Travis Scott]]'s song "[[Antidote (Travis Scott song)|Antidote]]". On December 1, 2015, Fuego released the music video to "Cuando Suena El Bling" his Spanish version of "Hotline Bling".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://remezcla.com/releases/music/fuego-video-cuando-suena-el-bling/|title=Fuego Holds Court Over the Spanish Remix Kingdom With the "Cuando El Suena Bling" Video|first=John|last=Calderon|date=December 4, 2015|website=Remezcla|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>

On December 29, 2015, Fuego released two songs via SoundCloud/YouTube; the first, "Se Me Nota", was featured on ''Fireboy Forever 2'' and is a trap song with bachata influences, produced by Sango Beats. Later that day, Fuego released the official remix of his song "Mambo Para Bailar" featuring Puerto Rican Reggaeton artist [[Arcángel (singer)|Arcangel]], produced by Maffio.

=== 2016–present: ''Fireboy Forever 2'' ===
Fuego began 2016 by teasing his album ''Fireboy Forever 2'', which was scheduled to be released on January 12, 2016. It was preceded in 2015 by the singles "Loca Con La Vaina" and "Se Me Nota"; the third single "Diferente" was premiered with a music video via YouTube on January 7 before the actual audio was available, which was made available with the album.

=== Controversy with ''Dile Quien Soy'' ===
In March 2018 it was discovered that he used, without any permission, music from the video game ''[[To the Moon]]'' in his single "Dile Quien Soy".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/Reives_Freebird/status/972175802749009920|title=Wait a... did a To the Moon track get ripped into an official song by a reputable artist...? |first=Kan|last=Gao|date=March 9, 2018|via=Twitter|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref> Fuego's team assured that the producer Smash David composed the song independently, and that they were unaware of the illegitimate use of the theme song.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/Reives_Freebird/status/972248482718269442|title=Got in touch with someone on the artist management team; they are saying that the producer was a separate entity and they were not themselves aware of the ripped track. They're co-operative so far, and hopefully we can sort this out somehow soon. Thanks for the support everyone!|first=Kan|last=Gao|date=March 9, 2018|via=Twitter|access-date=December 4, 2018}}</ref>
