'''IC3PEAK''' are a [[Russia]]n experimental electronic music duo from [[Moscow]] that formed in October 2013. The duo initially recorded songs almost exclusively in English, however, they later began to record almost exclusively in Russian. In 2018, a series of the band's concerts were cancelled or disrupted by law enforcement, and the duo were detained in the Siberian city of [[Novosibirsk]] with no probable reason, and were not released from custody in time to perform at the show.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2018-11-26|title=News From Russia: What You Missed Over the Weekend|language=en|work=[[The Moscow Times]]|url=https://themoscowtimes.com/news/news-from-russia-what-you-missed-over-weekend-63590|accessdate=2018-11-29}}</ref>

== History ==
The duo currently consists of vocalist Anastasiya 'Nastya' Kreslina (born November 13, 1995) and producer Nikolay Kostylev (born August 31, 1995). The band describes itself as an "audio-visual project".<ref>{{Cite news|date=2017-11-02|title=IC3PEAK&nbsp;— "Грустная сука". Премьера клипа одной из&nbsp;самых успешных российских электронных групп, которая теперь поет&nbsp;по-русски|language=ru-RU|work=[[Meduza]]|url=https://meduza.io/shapito/2017/11/02/ic3peak-grustnaya-suka-premiera-klipa-odnoy-iz-samyh-uspeshnyh-rossiyskih-elektronnyh-grupp-kotoraya-teper-poet-po-russki|accessdate=2018-02-24}}</ref>

The duo’s first performance took place in Saint Petersburg, followed by a concert in Moscow as the second part of the series "W17CHØU7" ("Witchout"),<ref name="daily.afisha.ru">{{cite news|last=Макарский|first=Артем|date=2015-01-22|title=Почему в России так популярен витч-хаус|language=ru|work=[[Afisha]] Daily|publisher=ООО «Компания Афиша»|url=https://daily.afisha.ru/archive/volna/context/pochemu-v-rossii-tak-populyaren-vitchhaus/|accessdate=2017-12-23}}</ref> to an audience of around 600 people.<ref name="the-flow.ru">{{cite news|last=Редькин|first=Николай|date=2016-08-22|title=IC3PEAK: "аудиовизуальные террористы" из Москвы|language=ru|work=The Flow|url=http://the-flow.ru/new-flow/new-flow-vol13-ic3peak|accessdate=2017-12-23}}</ref> The band released their debut EP "Substances" on the [[Portland, Oregon|Portland]]-based label STYLSS in 2014, featuring the track "Ether", for which they released their first music video. The band then released the EP "Vacuum" later in the same year, on French label Stellar, Kinematics, allowing them to widen their audience in Europe. Their first European performances took place in [[Paris]], [[Bordeaux]], [[Riga]], [[Prague]] and [[Helsinki]], followed by more shows in their homeland of Russia.<ref name="Москва24">{{cite news|last=Кокоулин|first=Дмитрий|date=2014-10-24|title=Электронный дуэт IC3PEAK - о визуализации смерти, "терроре" и попрошайках в сети|language=ru|publisher=[[Moscow 24]]|url=https://www.m24.ru/articles/koncerty/23102014/58395|accessdate=2017-12-23}}</ref> In 2016, the band toured [[Brazil]] and filmed the music video for the track "Go With the Flow".<ref name="the-flow.ru" /> In the same year, their first tour of the U.S. and a performance in [[Mexico]] took place. In 2017, IC3PEAK won the Electronics category in the Jägermeister Music Awards<ref name="tntmusic.ru">{{cite news|date=2017-10-20|title=Объявлены шорт-листы премии Jägermeister Music Awards|language=ru|publisher=TNT Music|url=https://tntmusic.ru/2200-obyavleny-short-listy-premii-jaegermeister-music-awards/|accessdate=2017-12-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=2018-11-19|title=Монеточка стала "Лучшей группой" на Jager Music Awards|language=ru|publisher=[[InterMedia (agency)|Intermedia]]|url=http://www.intermedia.ru/news/329677|accessdate=2018-11-24}}</ref> and the Golden Gargoyle Award for "best experimental project of the year". In November 2017, the band released their first Russian-language album "Сладкая Жизнь" (Sweet Life), with the track "Грустная сука" (Sad Bitch),<ref name="Wonderzine">{{cite news|date=2017-11-02|title=IC3PEAK выпустили мрачный клип "Грустная сука"|language=ru|work=Wonderzine|url=http://www.wonderzine.com/wonderzine/entertainment/entertainment-news/230646-ic3peak-new|accessdate=2017-12-23}}</ref> receiving more than 10 million views on YouTube. 
The duo's second Russian language album "Сказка" (Fairytale) contains songs on social themes. In the song "Смерти Больше Нет" (Death No More), Nastya Kreslina sings from the perspective of a riot squad member, facing the [[Lubyanka Building|Lubyanka]] jail-headquarters of the FSB ([[Federal Security Service]]). The song features the lyrics "Together with others you will be twisted in the square" and "I go outside to caress a cat while a cop car runs it over".<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=Gorbachev|first=Aleksandr|date=2018-11-23|title=В Казани силовики сорвали концерт IC3PEAK, другие выступления тоже под угрозой. Недавно группа начала петь про митинги, Россию и смерть|url=https://meduza.io/feature/2018/11/23/v-kazani-siloviki-sorvali-kontsert-ic3peak-drugie-vystupleniya-tozhe-pod-ugrozoy-nedavno-gruppa-nachala-pet-pro-mitingi-rossiyu-i-smert|access-date=2020-10-30|website=[[Meduza]]|language=ru}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-10-30|title="Выхожу на улицу гладить кота, а его переезжает тачка мента" Премьера клипа IC3PEAK "Смерти больше нет"|url=https://meduza.io/feature/2018/10/30/vyhozhu-na-ulitsu-gladit-kota-a-ego-pereezzhaet-tachka-menta|access-date=2020-10-30|website=[[Meduza]]|language=ru}}</ref> In the fall of 2018, the group, like a number of other Russian independent performers, started having problems with the cancellation of concerts in Russian cities by local security forces.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-12-01|title=Участников группы IC3PEAK задержали в Новосибирске|url=https://zona.media/news/2018/12/01/nsk|accessdate=2018-12-01|website=[[MediaZona]]|language=ru-RU}}</ref> The band have been caught up in a crackdown on popular music acts across provincial Russia, where shows have been cancelled in a flurry of local pressure that is said to have provoked an intervention by the Kremlin.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Roth|first=Andrew|date=2018-12-12|title=‘Even a half-finished show is a victory’: Russian bands fight new crackdown|language=en-GB|work=[[The Guardian]]|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/dec/12/were-not-scared-bands-defy-russian-crackdown-on-political-music|access-date=2019-02-19|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>[[File:Against the isolation of Runet (2019-03-10) 103.jpg|thumb|Against the isolation of Runet (2019-03-10) 103]]
