'''TRU''' (an abbreviation of '''The Real Untouchables''') was an American [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] group from [[New Orleans, Louisiana|New Orleans]], active from 1989 to 2005. The group originally consisted of rappers on the [[New Orleans]]-founded [[record label]], [[No Limit Records]]. The members are brothers [[Master P]], [[C-Murder]], and [[Silkk the Shocker]].<ref name="Larkin90">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Virgin Encyclopedia of Nineties Music]]|editor=Colin Larkin|editor-link=Colin Larkin (writer)|publisher=[[Virgin Books]]|date=2000|edition=First|isbn=0-7535-0427-8|page=403}}</ref>

The group released six albums, incorporating the typical [[No Limit Records|No Limit]] formula of [[G-funk]] rhythms married to hardcore [[Gangsta rap|gangsta]] lyrics.<ref name="Larkin90"/>

==History==
The group originally consisted of [[Master P]], [[C-Murder]], [[Silkk the Shocker]]. King George, [[Big Ed (rapper)|Big Ed the Assassin]], Cali G, Sonya C, Chilee Powdah and Milkman, before being shortened to just Master P, C-Murder and Silkk the Shocker. The group's first two releases, 1993's ''[[Who's Da Killer?]]'' and 1992's ''[[Understanding the Criminal Mind]]'', were released independently through In-a-minute Records. In 1995, the group released their third album, ''[[True (TRU album)|True]]'' on [[No Limit Records]], which had been established the previous year.<ref name="Larkin90"/> In 1997, the group was shortened to include just the three Miller brothers and the trio released their fourth album, ''[[Tru 2 da Game]]'', followed by ''[[Da Crime Family]]'' in 1999.<ref name="Larkin90"/> However, by 2003, No Limit had fallen on hard times and was shut down. TRU returned in 2004 on [[Koch Records]], released their sixth and final album, ''[[The Truth (TRU album)|The Truth]]''.
