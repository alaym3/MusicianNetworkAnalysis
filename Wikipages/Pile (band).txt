'''Pile''' is an American [[indie rock]] band originally from [[Boston, Massachusetts]] now based in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. Starting as the solo act of Rick Maguire in the late 2000s, Pile has been a collective since the release of ''Magic Isn't Real'' in 2010.<ref name="Magic Isn't Real">{{cite web|url=http://pile.bandcamp.com/album/magic-isnt-real|title=magic isn't real|date=2010-10-02|website=Pile.bandcamp.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref> To date, Pile has released seven full-length albums. In recent years the band has gained increasing popularity, becoming a prominent part of the Boston indie rock scene and frequently touring through both the US and Europe.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/20209-youre-better-than-this/|title=Pile: You're Better Than This Album Review|date=2015-03-02|website=Pitchfork.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref><ref name="Pilemusic">{{cite web|url=http://pilemusic.tumblr.com/|title=PILE|website=Pilemusic.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref> While remaining outside of the mainstream, the band has been repeatedly recognized for captivating a particularly enthusiastic and dedicated fan base.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/arts/music/2017/03/28/pile-has-well-earned-reputation-uphold/Kmc1pDlXxLjCR61O64tWNM/story.html|title=Pile has a well-earned reputation to uphold|date=2017-03-29|website=Bostonglobe.com|accessdate=2017-04-03}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.avclub.com/article/pile-one-strangest-bands-rock-musicand-one-best-251870|title=Pile is one of the strangest bands in rock music—and one of the best|date=2017-03-30|website=Avclub.com|accessdate=2017-04-03}}</ref> Their sound has been described by ''[[The Village Voice]]'' as a "distinct blend of melted guitars" with "howling-wolf vocals and shred-free guitar harmonies".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/music/pile-are-now-homeless-still-excellent-and-still-hungry-7325549|title=Pile Are Now Homeless, Still Excellent and Still Hungry|date=2015-07-01|website=Villagevoice.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref>

== History ==
Pile released its first album ''[[Demonstration (Pile album)|Demonstration]]'' on July 7, 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pile.bandcamp.com/album/demonstration|title=demonstration|date=2007-07-07|website=Pile.bandcamp.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref> In 2009, a second album called ''Jerk Routine'' was released.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pile.bandcamp.com/album/jerk-routine|title=jerk routine|date=2009-05-01|website=Pile.bandcamp.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref> In 2010 Pile formed its current line-up as a band and released the album ''Magic Isn't Real''.<ref name="Magic Isn't Real"/> The band released a 7-inch single called "Big Web" in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pile.bandcamp.com/album/big-web-seven-inch|title=big web seven inch|date=2011-09-01|website=Pile.bandcamp.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref> After self-releasing three albums and one single, Pile signed with record label [[Exploding in Sound]] in October 2011, and released the first album under this label, ''Dripping'', on October 23, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/aux-out/the-oral-history-of-exploding-in-sound-records/|title=The Oral History of Exploding in Sound Records|date=2013-10-18|website=Consequenceofsound.net|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://explodinginsoundrecords.bandcamp.com/album/dripping|title=dripping|date=2012-10-23|website=Explodinginsoundrecords.bandcamp.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref> The band released a new single, "Special Snowflakes", on March 11, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://explodinginsoundrecords.bandcamp.com/album/pile-special-snowflakes-b-w-mamas-lipstick|title=Pile - Special Snowflakes b/w Mama's Lipstick|date=2014-03-11|website=Explodinginsoundrecords.bandcamp.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref> They continued to release a fifth album, their second under Exploding in Sound, called ''You're Better Than This'' on March 3, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pile.bandcamp.com/album/youre-better-than-this|title=You're Better Than This|date=2015-03-03|website=Pile.bandcamp.com|accessdate=2016-10-08}}</ref> Later that year, Pile released ''First Other Tape'' on August 4, 2015.

On March 31, 2017, they released their sixth album, ''A Hairshirt of Purpose.''<ref name="Pilemusic"/> On April 25, 2018, Pile released the single "Keep the Last Light On", which had previously been released as part of the ''Boston Sessions, Vol. 1: Beast'' compilation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://bostonsessions.bandcamp.com/album/boston-sessions-volume-1-beast|title=Boston Sessions, Volume 1: Beast, by The Record Co.|website=The Record Co.|access-date=2020-03-19}}</ref> It was featured on the compilation album ''Odds and Ends'', which collated a series of b-sides, compilation tracks and the like from across Pile's career.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2018/08/pile-odds-and-ends-compilation-cup/|title=Pile Announce 'Odds and Ends' Compilation, Release "Cup"|date=2018-08-15|website=Spin|access-date=2019-08-13}}</ref>

On August 28, 2018, Maguire announced, in an email to Pile's fans, that guitarist Matt Becker had stepped out of the band indefinitely and bass player Matt Connery had stepped out temporarily. Chappy Hull and Alex Molini would be playing guitar and bass on the next album and tour. It was later revealed that Maguire had moved from Boston, and was now living in a house with Hull and Molini in Nashville. On May 3, 2019, Pile released their seventh LP, ''Green and Gray.''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pile.bandcamp.com/album/green-and-gray|title=Green and Gray, by Pile|website=Pile.bandcamp.com|accessdate=24 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Ep 298 - Rick Maguire of Pile « Live from the Barrage|url=http://www.radionope.com/podcasts/barrage/?name=2020-06-26_live-from-the-barrage-0298-06-26-2020-rick_maguire_pile.mp3|access-date=2020-07-11|website=www.radionope.com|language=en}}</ref>

On October 2, 2020, Pile released ''Second Other Tape'', a new collection of "ideas, experiments, and songs that are only partially formed." Recorded between March and August, 2020, this release was exclusively available on cassette through the group's Bandcamp.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Acito |first1=Dominic |title=Pile - "Second Other Tape" {{!}} Album Review |url=http://post-trash.com/news/2020/11/25/pile-second-other-tape-album-review |website=post-trash |access-date=17 May 2021}}</ref> 2015's ''First Other Tape'' was also made available for purchase, and the two tapes were available through December 4, 2020. To celebrate 10 years since the release of ''Magic Isn't Real'', on April 17, 2021, Pile performed the album in its entirety during a video live stream on their Bandcamp account, using its lineup from 2011 - 2017 (Rick Maguire, Matt Becker, Matt Connery, and Kris Kuss). The band then released ''In the Corners of a Sphere-Filled Room'' on May 7, 2020. Unlike previous releases, this album is fully improvisational, with 14 tracks of collected noise recorded intermittently between December 2018 and January 2021 - primarily during ''Green and Gray'''s session.<ref>{{cite web |title=New release from Pile |url=https://pile.bandcamp.com/community?sid=17139352&st=nr |website=Bandcamp |access-date=17 May 2021}}</ref>
