'''White Denim''' is an American four-piece [[rock music|rock]] band from [[Austin, Texas]], United States. Their music is influenced by [[dub music|dub]], [[psychedelic rock]], [[blues]], [[punk rock]], [[progressive rock]], [[Soul music|soul]], [[jazz]], [[experimental rock]] with [[Home studio|home-based]] recording, [[Jam band|jamming]] approach, intense [[Music loop|looping]] work and unusual [[Song structure (popular music)|song structures]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cluas.com/indie-music/Interviews/tabid/106/EntryId/924/White-Denim.aspx |title=Interviews - White Denim |website=Cluas.com |date=2009-01-25 |access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

==History==
In March 2005, two bands, Parque Touch (Josh Block, James Petralli, Lucas Anderson) and Peach Train (Steve Terebecki), played a show together at Beerland in Austin, Texas.<ref name="DIS">Charles Ubaghs, [http://www.drownedinsound.com/articles/2684043 "DiScover: White Denim"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080204183403/http://www.drownedinsound.com/articles/2684043 |date=2008-02-04 }}, Drowned in Sound, retrieved February 1, 2008.</ref> After the show Steve was asked to play bass for Parque Touch and the band became four, playing under the [[pseudonym]]s Byshop Massive (Lucas), Bop English (James), Nicholas Mallard (Josh), and Terry Beckins (Steve); however, in February 2006, Lucas moved to Russia and the resulting [[power trio]] changed its name to White Denim.<ref name="DIS" /> The new line-up began playing the local Austin circuit while recording punk-infused, psychedelic blues-rock in Block's 1940s [[Spartan Aircraft Company|Spartan]] trailer.<ref name="DIS" /><ref name="RSM">Kevin O'Donnell, [https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/breaking-artist-white-denim-20080123 "Breaking Artist: White Denim"], ''Rolling Stone Magazine'', Issue 1045, pg. 26, January 7, 2008.</ref>

In 2007, the band self-released its first [[extended play|EP]] entitled ''[[Let's Talk About It EP|Let's Talk About It]]'' on [[7"]] only, but later these tracks became available on [[iTunes]].<ref name="PFM1">Eric Harvey, [http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/record_review/47790-lets-talk-about-it-ep "Record Reviews: ''Let's Talk About It'' EP"], Pitchfork Media, January 9, 2008.</ref> While on tour in 2007, the band recorded a nine-song "tour EP" entitled ''[[Workout Holiday]]'', which was only sold at shows. ''Workout Holiday'' caught the ear of new [[netlabel]] [[RCRD LBL]], and the band signed on to re-record three of the songs.<ref name="Movement">Max Michaels, [http://movementnews.blogspot.com/2007/12/white-denim.html "White Denim Release Debut Free Single on RCRD LBL Today"], ''Movement Magazine'', December 3, 2007.</ref> These songs were exclusively released as the ''[[Workout Holiday|RCRD LBL EP]]'', one [[MP3]] track at a time over the course of the first few months of 2008.<ref name="PFM1" /><ref>Mark Richardson, [http://www.pitchforkmedia.com/article/download/47749-white-denim-paint-silver-gold-mp3 "New Music: White Denim"], ''Pitchfork Media'', January 3, 2008.</ref>

Since these releases, the band has toured extensively, playing shows including [[South by Southwest|SXSW]] and [[CMJ|CMJ Music Marathon]], and also received the award of "Best New Band" at the 2008 Austin Music Awards.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/gyrobase/Awards/MusicAwards?Year=2007 |title=Austin Music Awards - 2013 - Best of the Year |newspaper=[[The Austin Chronicle]] |access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> In order to reach European listeners, the band signed to UK record label [[Full Time Hobby]] (home to [[The Hold Steady]] and [[Viva Voce (band)|Viva Voce]]) for an overseas release.<ref name="AusChron" /> The first release on Full Time Hobby was the single "Let's Talk About It" b/w "Darksided Computer Mouth". On June 23, 2008, the band released its debut album, the ''[[Workout Holiday LP|Workout Holiday]]'' LP, which consists of re-recorded songs from the band's first two self-released EPs along with some new tracks. The band later released two more singles in Europe from ''Workout Holiday'': "[[All You Really Have To Do]]" b/w "DCWYW" and "[[Shake Shake Shake (White Denim song)|Shake Shake Shake]]" b/w "All Truckers Roll".

Back in the U.S., White Denim released their debut U.S. full-length [[long play|LP]], entitled ''[[Exposion]]'' in late 2008. Originally sold during their Spring 2008 tour as a [[CD-R]] with the title ''[[Exposion|11 Songs]]'', the album was made available in digital format from their official website on October 19, 2008.<ref name="A360">Josh Gross, [http://www.austin360.com/arts/content/music/stories/2008/03/0313xlcover.html "Austin's White Denim prepares for the future"], ''Austin 360'', March 13, 2008.</ref><ref name="WD">White Denim, [http://www.whitedenimmusic.com/ "Official website"], WhiteDenimMusic.com, retrieved October 20, 2008.</ref> The band teamed up with Austin's Transmission Entertainment for the distribution of ''Exposion'' in the US; however, the two parted ways in mid-2009.<ref name="AusChron">Austin Powell, [http://www.austinchronicle.com/gyrobase/Issue/story?oid=oid:602221 "Spotlight: White Denim"], ''The Austin Chronicle'', March 14, 2008.</ref><ref name="WD" />

White Denim's third full-length album, ''[[Fits (album)|Fits]]'', was released via Full Time Hobby on June 22, 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/news/white-denim/43862 |title=White Denim return with new album |website=Nme.com |date=2009-04-03 |access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> The record was initially released in Europe only, but after signing their first US record deal with [[Downtown Records]] on July 10, the band release ''Fits'' on October 20, 2009. This album was also packaged with ''Exposion'' as a bonus disc, giving the band's second LP a proper US release.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/news/white-denim/46080 |title=White Denim announce US release details of sophomore album |website=Nme.com |date=2009-07-13 |access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

In September 2010 White Denim announced the addition of second guitarist Austin Jenkins to the band's line-up.<ref>{{cite news|last=Powell |first=Austin |url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/gyrobase/issue/column?oid=oid%3A1091487 |title=Off the Record: Music News - Music |newspaper=[[The Austin Chronicle]] |date=2010-10-01 |access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> On September 23, 2010 the band self-released a collection of 12 tracks under the title ''[[Last Day of Summer (White Denim album)|Last Day of Summer]]''. The release notes state ''"This record is something we made as a little summer retreat from our ongoing work on the third full length. Many of these tunes have been bouncing around since the formation of the band back in 06. We were super pumped to utilize a few fresh and casual musical approaches on this record."''. It was initially available to download for free (with an option to make a donation) from the band's official website,<ref>White Denim [http://whitedenimmusic.com/download-new-record-last-day-of-summer "Last Day of Summer"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101218165902/http://whitedenimmusic.com/download-new-record-last-day-of-summer |date=2010-12-18 }}, WhiteDenimMusic.com, September 23, 2010</ref> but on December 5, 2011 it was released by [[Downtown Records]] on CD and LP.

On May 24, 2011, White Denim released their fourth studio album ''[[D (White Denim album)|D]]'' on Downtown Records.<ref>[http://www.prefixmag.com/reviews/white-denim/d/51178/ White Denim: ''D''.] - Prefix Magazine.</ref> The first leg of White Denim's ''For D'' tour started on May 23 in Sail Inn, Tempe.<ref>[http://mvremix.com/rock_blogs/2011/03/29/white-denim-announce-first-leg-of-for-d-north-american-tour/ White Denim Anoounce First Leg of ''For D'' North American tour.]. Mvremix.com</ref>

In 2012, White Denim opened for [[Wilco]] on their Jan-Feb west coast tour and played at the 2012 Bonnaroo Music & Arts Festival and Outside Lands festival.

In 2015, James Petralli released his first solo album, ''Constant Bop'', under his pseudonym, Bop English.

On 7 January 2016, White Denim announced that their new album, ''[[Stiff (album)|Stiff]]'', would be released on 25 March 2016 via Downtown/Sony Red, with the track "Holda You (I'm Psycho)" shared on their SoundCloud.<ref>{{cite web|last1=White Denim return with ‘Holda You (I’m Psycho)’, album news, live dates |url= http://www.neverenoughnotes.co.uk/2016/01/white-denim-return-with-holda-you-im-psycho-album-news-live-dates/#ixzz3wZHwmi6t |title=Never Enough Notes |website=nenot.es |publisher=Never Enough Notes|access-date=7 January 2016}}</ref> A second track, "Ha Ha Ha Ha (Yeah)", was released on 1 February 2016. On
October 20, 2016, the song was used in the reveal trailer for the [[Nintendo Switch]].<ref>{{Citation|last=Nintendo|title=First Look at Nintendo Switch|date=2016-10-20|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f5uik5fgIaI|access-date=2017-01-06}}</ref>

On May 9, 2018, the band announced their eighth full-length album, ''[[Performance (White Denim album)|Performance]],'' scheduled for release on August 28 via [[City Slang|City Slang Records]].<ref>Robin Murray, [https://www.clashmusic.com/news/white-denim-return-with-new-album-performance "White Denim Return With New Album 'Performance'"], ''Clash.'' Retrieved August 10, 2018.</ref> The announcement was accompanied by the album's lead single, "Magazin," and followed by a second single, "It Might Get Dark," on June 12.<ref name=YouTube>{{cite web|title=White Denim - It Might Get Dark (Official Lyric Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XmL7i8L78FU|website=YouTube.com|access-date=August 10, 2018}}</ref>

On March 29, 2019, the band released the follow up to ''Performance'', called ''Side Effects''. Then, on October 29 of the same year, they released a live album, ''In Person''.

On March 14, 2020, the group announced that they intended to write, record and release a new album by April 17 that year.<ref>[https://www.instagram.com/p/B9uiEE7l1o8/] {{dead link|date=March 2020}}</ref>
