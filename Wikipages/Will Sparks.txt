'''William James "Will" Sparks''' (born 15 March 1993) is an Australian producer and [[Disc jockey|DJ]] from [[Melbourne]], Australia,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wearedance.de/will-sparks-teases-another-land/|title=Will Sparks teases "Another Land"|publisher=wearedance.de|date=21 October 2014|access-date=6 January 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150106040621/http://wearedance.de/will-sparks-teases-another-land/|archive-date=6 January 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> who is best known for his 2013 single "[[Bring It Back (Will Sparks & Joel Fletcher song)|Bring It Back]]" with [[Joel Fletcher]] and his 2014 single "[[Ah Yeah!|Ah Yeah So What!]]" featuring [[Wiley (rapper)|Wiley]] and [[Elen Levon]].

He attended [[Camberwell High School]] and [[Caulfield Grammar]] in Melbourne, but dropped out at the end of year 11 to pursue a musical career.<ref>Sydney Morning Herald (2013). [http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/sparks-flies-to-la-on-the-rhythm-of-melbourne-bounce-20131006-2v26i.html Sparks flies to LA on the rhythm of Melbourne Bounce]. Retrieved 6 May 2015.</ref>
His debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''Another Land'', was released in Australia and debuted at number 35 in December 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Will+Sparks&titel=Another+Land&cat=a |title=WILL SPARKS - ANOTHER LAND (ALBUM)  |publisher=www.australian-charts.com |access-date=6 January 2015}}</ref>

Sparks has toured America, Europe and Asia and played at festivals such as [[TomorrowWorld]], [[Tomorrowland (festival)|Tomorrowland]], [[Stereosonic]] and [[Future Music Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.willsparks.com.au/bio/|title=Will Sparks Bio|publisher=willsparks.com.au|access-date=6 January 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150106051555/http://www.willsparks.com.au/bio/|archive-date=6 January 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>{{sps|date=January 2019}}

He was named the No. 1 Australian DJ in 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://stereosonic.com.au/artist/will-sparks/ | title= Will Sparks | publisher=stereosonic.com.au | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150106045513/http://stereosonic.com.au/artist/will-sparks/ | archive-date=6 January 2015}}</ref>

In January 2014, Will Sparks partnered with electronic music lifestyle brand Electric Family to produce a collaboration bracelet for which 100% of the proceeds are donated to Beyond Blue, a company that is working to reduce the impact of anxiety, depression and suicide in the community by raising awareness and understanding, empowering people to seek help, and supporting recovery, management and resilience.

In May 2014, Sparks embarked on a month long European tour.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-05-07|title=Will Sparks Announces "When The Lights Go Out Europeean Tour" • Digital Tour Bus|url=https://digitaltourbus.com/news/will-sparks-announces-lights-go-europe-tour/|access-date=2021-08-07|website=Digital Tour Bus|language=en-US}}</ref> He has continued to tour Europe every year until 2020.

From August until November 2014, Sparks alongside [[Timmy Trumpet]] and [[Joel Fletcher]] toured [[North America]] as part of their Bounce Bus Tour. <ref>{{Cite web|title=Will Sparks, Joel Fletcher and Timmy Trumpet join forces for US tour|url=https://itm.junkee.com/will-sparks-joel-fletcher-and-timmy-trumpet-join-forces-for-us-tour/25154/amp|access-date=2021-08-07|website=itm.junkee.com}}</ref> Following the tour he returned home for a homecoming tour.<ref>{{Cite web|title=/|url=https://themusic.com.au/news/will-sparks-is-coming-home-with-a-national-tour/3SvO8fDz8vU/27-05-14/|access-date=2021-08-07|website=The Music|language=en}}</ref>

In September 2017, it was discovered that the video Spinnin Records produced for Sparks titled song "Take Me" was discovered to consist almost entirely of footage from award-winning and Oscar-nominated animated short film "Requiem for Romance"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/news/10-animated-shorts-advance-2013-oscarr-race |title=10 Animated Shorts Advance in 2013 Oscar® Race &#124; Oscars.org &#124; Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences |website=Oscars.org |date=2013-11-07 |access-date=2020-03-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.animationmagazine.net/people/jonathan-ng-discusses-requiem-romance/|title=Jonathan Ng Discusses His 'Requiem for Romance'|first=Ramin|last=Zahed|date=7 January 2014}}</ref> Spinnin' Records did not seek permission to use visuals from the creators of the film, including animator Jonathan Ng. While Ng's takedown notice of the YouTube clips of the music video succeeded, Spinnin' Records continued to use images from Ng's video on their website and publicity material. Ng is exploring legal options against Spinnin' Records. Sparks claimed he had no knowledge of the alleged copyright, as he only produced the music for the accompanying video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cartoonbrew.com/law/heres-animator-jonathan-ng-film-lifted-edm-artist-will-sparks-153848.html|title=Here's What Animator Jonathan Ng Did When His Film Was Lifted By EDM Artist Will Sparks|date=2 October 2017|website=Cartoon Brew}}</ref>

==Sparksmania==

In 2018, Will Sparks launched his own festival, Sparksmania.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lucky Ent, Bourne Recordings & Crown Melbourne Presents Sparks Mania |url=https://www.moshtix.com.au/v2/event/sparks-mania/101227 |website=Moshtix |access-date=23 July 2021}}</ref> Since the inaugural show, Sparksmania has been held in Adelaide and Brisbane featuring artists [[Marlo]], [[Joel Fletcher]], [[HP Boyz]] and [[Masked Wolf]].<ref>{{cite web |author1=Raquel Estrada |title=Will Sparks Brings His Sparksmania Show To Brisbane |url=https://www.thecreativeissue.com.au/will-sparks-brings-his-sparksmania-show-to-brisbane/ |website=The Creative Issue – News for Creatives |access-date=23 July 2021 |date=13 June 2021}}</ref>
