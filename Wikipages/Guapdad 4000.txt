'''Akeem Ali Douglas Hayes''' (born July 26, 1991), professionally known as '''Guapdad 4000''', is an American [[rapper]], [[singer]], and [[songwriter]] of Filipino and African-American descent. He is also a member of the hip-hop supergroup Zoink Gang, with [[JID]], [[Smino]] and [[Buddy (rapper)|Buddy]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Duck |first1=Steve |title=Interview: Keanu Beats is the Producer Reppin for Australia on 'Revenge Of The Dreamers III' |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/07/keanu-beats-australian-producer-dreamville-jcole-migos/ |website=Complex |accessdate=July 26, 2019}}</ref> He released his first mixtape ''Scamboy Color'' on December 6, 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=Stream: Guapdad 4000, 'SCAMBOY Color' |url=https://revolt.tv/stories/2017/12/08/stream-guapdad-4000-scamboy-color-07007525f4 |website=Revolt |accessdate=July 6, 2019}}</ref> His debut studio album ''[[Dior Deposits]]'' was released on October 25, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Guapdad 4000 Shares "Dior Deposits" Album |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.53267/title.guapdad-4000-shares-dior-deposits-album |website=HipHopDX}}</ref>

==Musical career==
On December 6, 2017, he released his first mixtape ''Scamboy Color'', which included production from [[Ducko McFli]], IAMSU, and Guapdad himself, among others.<ref>{{cite web |title=GUAPDAD4000 – SCAMBOY COLOR [MIXTAPE] |url=http://dailychiefers.com/guapdad4000-scamboy-color-mixtape/ |website=Daily Chiefers}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Guapdad 4000 – "Scamboy Color" Mixtape |url=https://rosecransave.com/2017/12/07/guapdad-4000-scamboy-color/ |website=Rosecrans Ave}}</ref> In 2018, he made a guest appearance on fellow west coast rapper [[Buddy (rapper)|Buddy]]'s album ''[[Harlan & Alondra]]'' on the song "Shameless".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.47737/title.buddy-drops-debut-album-harlan-alondra|title=Buddy Drops Debut Album "Harlan & Alondra"|first=HipHopDX -|last=http://hiphopdx.com|publisher=}}</ref>

On January 6, 2019, Hayes was invited to the [[Dreamville Records|Dreamville]] recording sessions for the compilation album ''[[Revenge of the Dreamers III]]''.<ref>{{cite web |title=These 106 Artists Are Working on Dreamville's New Compilation LP |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/01/artists-invited-record-dreamville-revenge-dreamers-iii-album/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |accessdate=January 8, 2019 |date=January 8, 2019}}</ref> He was featured on the songs "Don't Hit Me Right Now", "Wells Fargo", and "[[Costa Rica (Dreamville, Bas and JID song)|Costa Rica]]". He talked about the recording sessions in an interview saying "[It was] Life-changing. The best musical learning experience ever."<ref>{{cite web |title=Guapdad4000 Compares Dreamville Sessions To Epic Anime Tournament & Explains "Costa Rica" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/guapdad4000-compares-dreamville-sessions-to-epic-anime-tournament-and-explains-costa-rica-new-video.45549.html |website=HotNewHipHop |accessdate=July 6, 2019}}</ref>

Throughout 2018 and 2019, Guapdad 4000 released a handful of singles including "Flossin'", "Scammin", "First Things First", "Prada Process", and "Gucci Pajamas".<ref>{{cite web |title=Guapdad 4000 – Singles & EPs |url=https://music.apple.com/ca/artist/guapdad-4000/1312996649#see-all/single-albums |website=iTunes |accessdate=July 6, 2019}}</ref> On October 25, 2019, his debut album ''[[Dior Deposits]]'' was released, including features from [[Tory Lanez]], [[6lack]], [[Chance the Rapper]], [[G-Eazy]] and [[Buddy (rapper)|Buddy]], among others.<ref>{{cite web |title=Guapdad 4000's "Dior Deposits" Features Denzel Curry, Tory Lanez & More |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/guapdad-4000s-dior-deposits-features-denzel-curry-tory-lanez-and-more-news.93795.html |website=HotNewHipHop}}</ref>

In 2020, Hayes went on to work on a project that has a story and hidden clues of what this mysterious project is. He worked on the new project during Quarantine, due to [[COVID-19]]. On April 24, 2020, he released the EP ''Platinum Falcon Tape, Vol. 1''. On August 14, he released the sequel EP ''Platinum Falcon Returns'', featuring guest appearances from [[Denzel Curry]], [[Deante' Hitchcock]], and [[Boogie (rapper)|Boogie]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Listen to Guapdad 4000s latest EP 'Platinum Falcon Returns' |url=https://www.revolt.tv/new-music/2020/8/17/21372107/guapdad-4000-platinum-falcon-returns-ep |website=Revolt|date=August 17, 2020 }}</ref>

On March 19, 2021, he released a collaboration album with [[Illmind|!llmind]] titled ''[[1176 (album)|1176]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rose |first1=Jordan |title=Guapdad 4000 and !llmind Connect for New Album '1176′ f/ Buddy, P-Lo, and More |url=https://www.complex.com/music/guapdad-4000-illmind-1176-album-stream |website=Complex}}</ref>

==Artistry==
In an interview with ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', Hayes said he grew up listening to artists like [[Dom Kennedy]], [[Mac Dre]], [[Pharrell Williams]], and [[Kanye West]], saying "I take so much. I got so much from them. I like personalities because I got a big one." His vocals and style also get compared to [[Mase|Ma$e]], [[Fabolous]], and [[Cam'ron]].<ref>{{cite web |title=THE BREAK PRESENTS: GUAPDAD 4000 |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/07/guapdad-4000-interview-the-break/ |website=XXL |accessdate=August 3, 2019}}</ref>
