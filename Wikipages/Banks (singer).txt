'''Jillian Rose Banks''' (born June 16, 1988), known [[Mononymous person|mononymously]] as '''Banks''' (often stylized in [[all caps]]), is an American singer, songwriter, and poet. She is signed to [[Harvest Records]] and Good Years Recordings.<ref name="Billboard"/> Following the release of two [[extended play]]s, ''Fall Over'' and ''London'', in 2013, Banks released her debut album, ''[[Goddess (Banks album)|Goddess]]'', on September 5, 2014, to positive reviews from contemporary music critics. It reached number 12 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], while its most successful single, "[[Beggin for Thread]]", was certified platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1933622/lecrae-first-1-album-anomaly/|title=Lecrae Makes History With First #1 Album|website=Mtv.com|access-date=August 28, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Banks&ti=Beggin+for+Thread#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum - RIAA|website=Riaa.com|access-date=August 28, 2019}}</ref> Her second studio album, ''[[The Altar]]'', was released on September 30, 2016, to a similar positive reception, becoming her second top 20 album in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2016-10-22|title=Top 200 Albums|website=Billboard.com|access-date=August 28, 2019}}</ref> Her third album, ''[[III (Banks album)|III]]'', was released on July 12, 2019. It became her most critically acclaimed release to date<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/iii/banks|title=III by Banks|website=Metacritic.com|access-date=August 28, 2019}}</ref> and peaked at number 21 on the ''Billboard'' 200, as well as number 3 on the Top Albums Sales chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2019/07/22/banks-iii-debuts-at-3-on-billboard-top-album-sales-chart/|title=BANKS' "III" Debuts At #3 On Billboard Top Album Sales Chart|date=July 22, 2019|website=Headlineplanet.com|access-date=August 28, 2019}}</ref>

==Life and career==
===Early life and education===
Jillian Rose Banks was born on June 16, 1988, in [[Orange County, California]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/jillian_rose_banks_born_1988_18362295 |title=Jillian Rose Banks, Born 06/16/1988 in California |website=[[California Birth Index]] |access-date=June 16, 2018}}</ref><ref name="bbc-sound">{{cite news |last=Savage |first=Mark |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-25576571 |title=BBC Sound of 2014: Banks |work=[[BBC News]] |date=January 8, 2014 |access-date=September 13, 2014}}</ref> She moved to Los Angeles when she was one or two years old and lived in [[Tarzana, Los Angeles|Tarzana]], an affluent neighborhood in the [[San Fernando Valley]].<ref name="bbc-sound"/> Banks started writing songs at the age of 15.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite web |last=Lipshutz |first=Jason |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/1568655/banks-quick-rising-pop-starlet-explains-why-her-fans-have-her-cell |title=Banks, Quick-Rising Pop Starlet, Explains Why Her Fans Have Her Cell Number |work=Billboard |date=June 28, 2013 |access-date=October 25, 2013}}</ref> She taught herself piano when she received a keyboard from a friend to help her through her parents' divorce. She says she "felt very alone and helpless. I didn't know how to express what I was feeling or who to talk to."<ref name="Vogue">{{cite web |last=Brown |first=Jacob |url=http://www.vogue.com/872942/music-artist-of-the-week-banks/ |title=Artist of the Week: Banks |work=[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]] |date=August 9, 2013 |access-date=July 18, 2016}}</ref><ref name="bbc-interest">{{cite news |last=Savage |first=Mark |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-24957472 |title=Banks: Rising star with a high interest rate |work=BBC News |date=November 21, 2013 |access-date=July 18, 2016}}</ref> She later enrolled to study psychology at the [[University of Southern California]] (USC), where she wrote a thesis on the children of divorced parents,<ref name="observer">{{cite web |last=Price |first=Simon |author-link=Simon Price |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/mar/23/jillian-banks-trinity-bristol-live-review-simon-price |title=Banks review – hotline to the new priestess of pop |work=[[The Observer]] |date=March 24, 2014 |access-date=July 18, 2016}}</ref> eventually earning a [[bachelor's degree]] in psychology.<ref name="bbc-interest"/>

===2015–2017: ''The Altar''===
On November 4, 2015, Banks released the single "[[Better (Banks song)|Better]]", along with its accompanying music video.<ref>{{cite web |last=Roth |first=Madeline |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2371063/banks-better-video/ |title=BANKS Is Back And 'Better' With A Sinister New Song And Video |publisher=MTV News |date=November 4, 2015 |access-date=July 18, 2016}}</ref> Banks toured with The Weeknd for a second time, opening for him on his [[The Madness Fall Tour]] across North America from November to December 2015.<ref>{{cite web |last=Martin |first=Paley |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6671102/the-weeknd-the-madness-fall-tour-dates |title=The Weeknd Announces The Madness Fall Tour With Travi$ Scott, Halsey & Banks |work=Billboard |date=August 21, 2015 |access-date=July 18, 2016}}</ref> She announced on June 8, 2016 that she had finished work on her second album.<ref>{{cite web |last=Wass |first=Mike |url=http://www.idolator.com/7636125/banks-second-album-finished |title=BANKS Finishes Recording Her Sophomore LP |publisher=Idolator |date=June 8, 2016 |access-date=July 18, 2016}}</ref>

On July 12, 2016, Banks released "[[Fuck with Myself]]" as the lead single from her second studio album, titled ''[[The Altar]]''. She premiered the track on Zane Lowe's [[Beats 1]] radio show, where she noted it was the last song she wrote for the album. "There's so many meanings to it", she said of the song. "It could be like, 'I fuck with myself', like, 'I mess with myself more than anybody else.' It could be, 'I fuck with myself', kind of like, 'I'm feeling myself.' It means a lot of different things that I think a lot of people can relate to."<ref>{{cite web |last=Blistein |first=Jon |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/watch-banks-defiant-f--k-with-myself-video-20160712 |title=Watch Banks' Defiant 'F--k With Myself' Video |work=Rolling Stone |date=July 12, 2016 |access-date=July 17, 2016}}</ref> In an interview for [[Noisey]], ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]''{{'}}s music channel, Banks describes her conflict between self-criticism and self-love as the inspiration behind the track.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/8gxx43/banks-on-intimacy-depression-and-confronting-herself |title=BANKS on Intimacy, Depression, and Confronting Herself |work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |date=October 7, 2016 |access-date=September 17, 2017}}</ref> The song received acclaim from music critics, and reached 29 on the US [[Rock Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/watch-banks-defiant-f--k-with-myself-video-20160712|title=Watch Banks' Defiant 'F--k With Myself' Video|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2016/07/banks-shares-new-single-and-video-fuck-with-myself-watch/|title=Banks shares new single and video "Fuck With Myself" — watch|date=July 12, 2016|website=Consequenceofsound.net}}</ref><ref name="billboard.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/banks/chart-history/rock-songs|title=Banks Chart History|website=Billboard.com}}</ref>

The track was premiered on Lowe's Beats 1 radio show.<ref>{{cite web |last=Geslani |first=Michelle |url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/09/banks-releases-new-single-underdog-stream/ |title=Banks releases new single "Underdog": Stream |work=Consequence of Sound |date=September 28, 2017 |access-date=October 1, 2017}}</ref> On November 14, Banks' collaboration with [[6lack]], "In Between", was unveiled as one of the three new tracks to celebrate the one-year anniversary of 6lack's debut album, ''[[Free 6lack]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last=Street |first=Mikelle |url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/11/14/6lack-free-6lack-bonus |title=6lack released 3 bonus tracks for the anniversary of ''Free 6lack'' |work=[[The Fader]] |date=November 14, 2017 |access-date=November 14, 2017}}</ref> In 2018, "In Between" and "Poltergeist", a track from ''The Altar'', were both featured on the soundtrack of the fifth season of ''[[Power (TV series)|Power]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/power/season-5/65024|title=Music from Power S5E02|website=Tunefind.com|access-date=August 25, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tunefind.com/show/power/season-5/65851|title=Music from Power S5E05|website=Tunefind.com|access-date=August 25, 2018}}</ref>

===2019–2020: ''III''===
In December 2018, Banks said she was working on an album to be released in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/banks-new-song-gimme-828394/|title=Hear Banks' Electrifying New Song 'Gimme'|work=Rolling Stone|date=April 29, 2019}}</ref> On April 29, 2019, Banks released a new single titled "[[Gimme (Banks song)|Gimme]]". The song debuted as Zane Lowe's World Record on [[Apple Music]]'s Beats 1.<ref>{{cite web |last=Kaufman |first=Gil |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8509074/banks-gimme-song |title=Banks Returns With Dark Banger 'Gimme': Listen |work=Billboard |date=April 29, 2019 |access-date=April 30, 2019}}</ref> On June 11, 2019, Banks released another single, "Look What You're Doing To Me," featuring [[Francis and the Lights]], which she revealed is about falling and being in love.<ref>Claire Shaffer, [https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/banks-francis-and-the-lights-look-what-youre-doing-to-me-846672/ "Banks Unveils New Song 'Look What You're Doing to Me' With Francis and the Lights"], ''Rolling Stone'', June 11, 2019</ref> The final single from the album, "Contaminated", was released on July 10. Her third studio album, ''[[III (Banks album)|III]]'', was released on July 12, 2019. Banks describes the album as her transformation from a naive, hopeful romantic into a wise woman.<ref>Eli Enis, [http://read.tidal.com/article/banks-iii "BANKS Gets Wise On Her Upcoming Album III"], "Billboard", May 30, 2019</ref> Banks embarked on The III Tour on September 3 in support of the album.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kaufman |first1=Gil |title=Banks Announces North American Tour: See the Dates |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8514124/banks-tour-dates |website=Billboard |access-date=4 September 2019}}</ref>

===2021–present: Upcoming fourth studio album===

In June 2021, Banks teased a new song titled "The Devil" for an upcoming unnamed album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/banks-teases-new-single-landing-next-week|title=Banks teases new single landing next week|last=Kenneally|first=Cerys|date=June 6, 2021|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|access-date=September 30, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/banks-unveils-title-of-upcoming-single|title=Banks unveils title of upcoming single|last=Kenneally|first=Cerys|date=June 10, 2021|website=[[The Line of Best Fit]]|access-date=September 30, 2021}}</ref> Written and produced by Banks, with additional production by Al Shux and Jeff "Gitty" Gitelman, the song and its accompanying music video—co-directed by Banks and Jenna Marsh—were released on June 16, and marked the singer's first release as an independent artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/music/banks-/banks-new-single-the-devil/|title=BANKS Releases Video for New Song "The Devil"|last=Friedman|first=Jason|date=June 16, 2021|website=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]|access-date=September 30, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/banks-the-devil-1185669/|title=Banks Shares Hellish Music Video for 'The Devil'|last=Shaffer|first=Claire|date=June 17, 2021|website=Rolling Stone|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210617143622/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/banks-the-devil-1185669/|archive-date=June 17, 2021|url-status=dead|access-date=September 30, 2021|url-access=subscription}}</ref>

On August 25, Banks released "Skinnydipped" as the second single from her upcoming fourth album. The song is about "moving onward, finding her self-worth, and no longer drowning in toxic relationships".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://floodmagazine.com/92836/banks-skinnydipped-feature/|title=BANKS Is Embracing the Devil That's Been Inside Her All Along|last=Izquierdo|first=Michael|date=August 26, 2021|work=Flood Magazine|access-date=September 30, 2021}}</ref> Co-directed by Banks and Michael Stine, the music video features the singer in jewellery from [[Bulgari]]'s "Serpenti" collection, echoing the snake motif "represent[ative] of rebirth and shedding one's skin" used in her imagery.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.papermag.com/banks-skinnydipped-2654801875.html|title=BANKS Releases Her Siren Song|last=Silva|first=Dante|date=August 26, 2021|work=[[Paper (magazine)|Paper]]|access-date=September 30, 2021}}</ref>

==Public image==
Banks' reluctance to use social media is often noted by journalists. Although she has stated she lets her "management run that stuff",<ref name="bbc-interest"/> the singer did give out her telephone number on her Facebook fanpage.<ref name="Billboard"/> She has since become more active on her [[Instagram]] and [[Twitter]] accounts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8512881/banks-iii-new-album-interview|title=Banks Was Rising to the Top When She Disappeared. Here's Why|last=Ginsberg|first=Gab|date=May 24, 2019|website=Billboard}}</ref>{{failed verification|date=September 2019}}

==Artistry==
Banks' sound has been described as dark [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] and compared to [[Ellie Goulding]], [[The Weeknd]],<ref>{{cite web |last=Williott |first=Carl |url=http://www.idolator.com/7475462/banks-waiting-game-the-weeknd-tour |title=BANKS' "Waiting Game": Hear Why She's Opening For The Weeknd |publisher=Idolator |date=August 5, 2013}}</ref> and [[Aaliyah]],<ref name="rs">{{cite web |last=Hermes |first=Will |author-link=Will Hermes |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/banks-goddess-20140909 |title=BANKS: Goddess |work=Rolling Stone |date=September 9, 2014 |access-date=September 9, 2014}}</ref> although she cites [[Lauryn Hill]] and [[Fiona Apple]] as her biggest influences.<ref>{{cite web |last=Martins |first=Chris |url=http://www.spin.com/2013/03/banks-fall-over-song-stream/ |title=Hear Banks' Badu-Channeling R&Beats Bubbler 'Fall Over' |work=Spin |date=March 4, 2013 |access-date=July 18, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last=Selby |first=Jenn |url=http://www.glamourmagazine.co.uk/news/celebrity/2013/11/05/banks-singer-london-interview-music-news |title=Introducing BANKS. AKA, the next big name in pop |journal=[[Glamour (magazine)|Glamour]] |location=UK |date=November 5, 2013}}</ref> She says that music helps her release her emotions and for that reason kept her music private while she earned her bachelor's degree in psychology.<ref name="bbc-interest"/> Her vocals have frequently been described as "Aaliyah-like",<ref name="clash">{{cite web |last=Martin |first=Felicity |url=http://www.clashmusic.com/reviews/banks-goddess |title=Banks – Goddess |work=[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]] |date=August 28, 2014 |access-date=September 1, 2014}}</ref> in addition to ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' noting that "her rhapsodic voice possesses a frail vulnerability and recalls singers like [[Feist (singer)|Feist]] and [[Erykah Badu]]."<ref name="Billboard"/>

===Musical style===
Banks' music has been categorized as [[alternative pop]],<ref>{{cite web |last=Choi |first=Mary H.K. |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6244033/banks-pop-star-interview |title=Banks' Gorgeously Gloomy Music Has Made Her a Breakout Star |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=September 8, 2014 |access-date=September 13, 2014}}</ref> [[alternative R&B]],<ref name="observer"/> [[avant-pop]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/reviews/banks-iii |title=BANKS - III |website=[[Clash (magazine)]] |access-date=July 26, 2019}}</ref>  [[electropop]],<ref name="rs altar review">{{cite web |last=Johnston |first=Maura |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/review-banks-the-altar-w442112 |title=Review: Banks Rides Nervy Beats Into Heart of Darkness on 'The Altar' |work=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=September 30, 2016 |access-date=October 17, 2016}}</ref> [[Hip hop music|hip hop]],<ref name="Time Genres">{{cite web|url=https://time.com/5623999/banks-iii-interview/|title=Banks Had to 'Dig in the Mud' to Make Her New Album III|website=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|first=Raisa|last=Bruner|date=July 11, 2019|access-date=November 10, 2019}}</ref> [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]],<ref name="Time Genres"/> [[trap music|trap]]-[[pop music|pop]]<ref name="Guardian">{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2019/jul/12/banks-iii-review |title=Banks: III review – a break from dark R&B doesn't quite pay off |website=[[The Guardian]] |date=July 12, 2019 |access-date=July 21, 2019 |last=Cliff |first=Aimee}}</ref> and [[trip hop]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/latestnews/2013/sound-of-2014.html |title=BBC Sound of 2014 list announced |publisher=[[BBC Media Centre]] |date=December 2, 2013 |access-date=September 13, 2014}}</ref>
