'''Katriana Sandra Huguet''' (born July 29, 1990), better known by her stage name '''Kat Dahlia''' (formerly '''Kat Hue'''), is a [[Cuban-American]] recording artist. Born and raised in Miami, Florida, Dahlia is a singer, songwriter and [[rapper]], known for her "razor sharp" lyrics and her "unique, aggressive flow".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/kat-dahlia/biography/ |title=Kat Dahlia &#124; News, Music Performances and Show Video Clips |publisher=MTV |date= n.d.|accessdate=July 18, 2017}}</ref><ref name="VIBE">"[http://www.vibe.com/article/kat-dahlia-talks-musical-beginnings-and-working-sylvia-rhone Kat Dahlia Talks Musical Beginnings and Working With Sylvia Rhone]", ''[[Vibe (magazine)|Vibe]]'', January 26, 2013. Retrieved March 3, 2013</ref><ref name="Gangsta">{{cite web|title=Gangsta|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/bangers/2012/12/kat-dahlia-gangsta/|work=December 12, 2012|publisher=XXL|accessdate=March 14, 2013}}</ref> She released her debut single "Gangsta", in March 2013, to rave reviews. Dahlia released her debut studio album ''[[My Garden]]'', in January 2015, with Vested in Culture and [[Epic Records]], the latter of which she secured a recording contract with in 2012. In 2013, Dahlia ranked number eight on [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']]'s "Next Big Sound".<ref>{{cite web|title=Kat Dahlia – Chart history|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/1558468/kat-dahlia/chart?f=1167|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=March 15, 2014}}</ref>

==Early life==
Kat Dahlia was born Katriana Sandra Huguet on July 29, 1990, to a Cuban mother and her Lebanese-Cuban father, in [[Miami Beach]], Florida.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/04/who-is-kat-dahlia/growing-up-in-miami |title=Growing Up in Miami – Who Is Kat Dahlia? |website=Complex.com |date= n.d.|accessdate=July 18, 2017}}</ref> She began [[rapping]] at the age of eight and writing songs at the age of 15, [[ripping]] instrumentals from YouTube, in lieu of a band.<ref>{{cite web|last=Medina|first=Loren|title=Introducing Kat Dahlia|url=http://www.corrientelatina.com/introducing-cuban-american-rapper-singer-songwriter-kat-dahlia.html|work=March 8, 2013|publisher=Corriete Latina|accessdate=March 14, 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131009220149/http://www.corrientelatina.com/introducing-cuban-american-rapper-singer-songwriter-kat-dahlia.html|archivedate=October 9, 2013}}</ref> At the age of 18, after saving money from jobs as a waitress, Dahlia decided to leave Miami, and moved to New York City a month later, "on a whim."<ref name="VIBE" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Kat Dahlia Interview With Vibe TV|url=http://videos.huffingtonpost.com/healthy-living/kat-dahlia-interview-with-vibetv-517653837|work=March 13, 2013|accessdate=March 14, 2013}}</ref> Thereafter, Dahlia became involved in what she describes as a "toxic relationship", which she would later come to regard as a source of inspiration and "writer's gold."<ref>{{cite web|last=Gonzales|first=Nicole|title=New Artist Kat Dahlia|url=http://www.latinrecap.com/2013/02/28/new-artist-kat-dahlia|work=February 28, 2013|publisher=Latin Recap|accessdate=March 14, 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130323012958/http://www.latinrecap.com/2013/02/28/new-artist-kat-dahlia/|archivedate=March 23, 2013}}</ref> She chose the name Kat Dahlia as her stage name after her producer suggested it and kept it, because its soft and beautiful, but still has a dark undertone to it.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Lamarre|first1=Carl|title=Artist of the Week: Kat Dahlia|url=http://www.pepsi.com/en-us/d/content/15547259/Artist-of-the-Week:-Kat-Dahlia/|website=Pepsi|date=September 2, 2014|access-date=September 28, ==Musical career==
After self-financing an [[extended play]] (EP) and a music video, Dahlia was discovered by Vested in Culture VP of [[A&R]] Amanda Berkowitz. Berkowitz quickly brought Dahlia to the attention of veteran record executive [[Sylvia Rhone]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Kat Dahlia's Gangsta Video|url=http://music.broadwayworld.com/article/KAT-DAHLIA-Releases-Video-For-Single-Gangsta-20130305|work=March 5, 2013|publisher=Broadway World|accessdate=March 14, 2013}}</ref> In 2012, describing Dahlia as "more than just a pop artist", Rhone signed her to her newly launched label, Vested in Culture (VIC), a joint venture with [[Epic Records]]. Dahlia's debut album was originally set to come out in 2014, becoming VIC's inaugural release.<ref>{{cite web|last=Mitchell|first=Gail|title=Sylvia Rhone Talks Vested in Culture|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1537975/exclusive-sylvia-rhone-talks-new-label-vested-in-culture|work=February 5, 2013|accessdate=March 14, 2013}}</ref><ref name="Vena">Vena, Jocelyn (2013) "[http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1701143/kat-dahlia-gangsta-follow-up.jhtml Kat Dahlia Is Prepping 'Swaggy' Follow-Up To 'Gangsta']", [[MTV]], January 31, 2013. Retrieved March 3, 2013</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Sylvia Rhone Announces Joint Venture|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/sylvia-rhone-announces-joint-venture-416843|work=January 30, 2013|accessdate=March 14, 2013}}</ref>

Dahlia's first single and video for VIC, was the track "Gangsta", which [[MTV]] called "filled to the brim with fierce rapping". The video, filmed in Miami, reflects the difficulties of her earlier life.<ref>{{cite web|title=Buzzworthy|url=http://buzzworthy.mtv.com/2013/03/05/kat-dahlia-gangsta-video/|work=March 5, 2013|publisher=MTV|accessdate=March 14, 2013}}</ref> The video was directed by Samantha Lecca<ref name="lifethroughmic"/> and premiered via ''[[Vevo]]'', on March 5, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kat's Gangsta|url=http://the305.com/2013/03/05/video-kat-dahlia-gangsta/|work=March 5, 2013|publisher=The 305|accessdate=March 14, 2013}}</ref> Also in March, Dahlia was selected as a [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]] "Music Matters" artist.<ref>{{cite web|title=BET Music Matters|url=http://www.bet.com/music/music-matters.html|work=March 3, 2013|publisher=BET|accessdate=March 14, 2013}}</ref> "Gangsta" was featured in the background of the September 24, 2014 Season 16 Premiere of [[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]], ''Girls Disappeared''.

Dahlia digitally released three songs, "Gangsta", "Money Party", and "Mirror" on March 5, 2013. On December 17, 2013, she released her song "Crazy" for free on her website. Her debut ''[[My Garden]]'' was released on January 15, 2015.<ref name="lifethroughmic"/> The album debuted at number 54 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. ''My Garden'' received generally positive reviews from [[music criticism|music critic]]s. At [[Metacritic]], which assigns a [[standard score|normalized]] rating out of 100 to reviews from critics, the album received an [[weighted mean|average]] score of 63, which indicates "generally favorable reviews", based on four reviews.<ref name="metacritic.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/my-garden/kat-dahlia|title=Reviews for My Garden by Kat Dahlia |publisher=Metacritic|accessdate=February 2, 2015}}</ref>

Kat Dahlia released her third EP, titled ''20s, 50s, 100s'', via SoundCloud on May 9, 2016, which features three songs, "Run It Up", "Voices in My Head" and "Lion".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://rnbxclusive.org/kat-dahlia-20s-50s-100s-ep/ |title=Loading |website=Rnbxclusive.org |accessdate=July 18, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160613001942/http://rnbxclusive.org/kat-dahlia-20s-50s-100s-ep/# |archive-date=June 13, 2016 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

In May 2017, Dahlia released the song "Friday Night Majic", which was intended as the lead single from her planned second studio album, ''Naked Lady and a White Horse''.<ref name="FNM">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7801294/kat-dhalia-friday-night-magic-video|title=Watch Kat Dahlia's Stylish 'Friday Night Majic' Video|work=Billboard|last=Cobo|first=Leila|date=May 22, 2017|accessdate=June 21, 2019}}</ref> The album was produced by J Roc and originally scheduled to be released in July 2017,<ref name="FNM"/> but did not eventuate. On July 21, 2017, Dahlia released the single "Body and Soul" to all streaming platforms and also uploaded a lyric video to her YouTube Vevo channel.

On May 4, 2018, after creating his For the Culture Playlist, which included [[Miguel (singer)|Miguel]]'s single "Come Through and Chill", [[Salaam Remi]] released the EP ''South Beach Social Club'', a collaboration with Dahlia.

==Artistry==
Dahlia's musical style has been described as being "razor sharp" lyrics and her unique, aggressive flow.<ref name="VIBE"/><ref name="Gangsta"/> Dhalia sings and raps in both English and Spanish. Dahlia cites artists [[BB King]], [[Miles Davis]], [[Led Zeppelin]], [[The Doors]], [[Bob Marley]], [[Frank Sinatra]], and [[Celia Cruz]] as musical influences. Also, in interviews, Dahlia has mentioned that legendary [[reggae]] recording artist Bob Marley, has had a great influence on her musical style. She has said: "I listened to a lot of Bob Marley, especially down here. I used to go to Purdy Lounge on Monday night, they always had reggae bands. Reggae is just everywhere down here, [[dancehall]] is everywhere in the [[Nightclub|clubs]]. It's not only playing Hip Hop."<ref name="lifethroughmic"/>
