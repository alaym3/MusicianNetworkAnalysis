'''Prateek Kuhad''' is an Indian singer-songwriter and musician who makes [[independent music]] in Hindi and English.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Gurbaxani|first=Amit|date=27 October 2017|title=On the bus with Prateek Kuhad|url=https://www.livemint.com/Leisure/D4q3bZrOOwPD0oaq1oDB5I/On-the-bus-with-Prateek-Kuhad.html|access-date=21 December 2020|website=mint|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Majumdar|first=Meghna|date=1 December 2020|title=When Prateek Kuhad met Singapore's DJ Myrne|language=en-IN|work=The Hindu|url=https://www.thehindu.com/entertainment/music/when-prateek-kuhad-met-singapores-dj-myrne/article33223160.ece|access-date=21 December 2020|issn=0971-751X}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=11 December 2020|title=Prateek Kuhad on Bollywood music, writing songs, & picking his genre #HTLS2020|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/videos/htls/prateek-kuhad-on-bollywood-music-writing-songs-picking-his-genre-htls2020/video-p2IdsAHBL34L54E6N4gRCK.html|access-date=21 December 2020|website=Hindustan Times}}</ref> He is best known for his track "Cold/Mess".

==Early life and career==
Kuhad was born in [[Jaipur]], [[Rajasthan]] and has two sisters. He learnt to play the guitar at age of 16 and started songwriting in his late teens. He finished his high school from Maharaja Sawai Man Singh Vidyalaya, Jaipur. Later, He studied Maths and Economics at [[New York University]] before moving to [[Delhi]] to pursue a full-time career in music.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://lbb.in/delhi/in-conversation-prateek-kuhad/|title=You've Gotta Know Prateek Kuhad - LBB|access-date=19 August 2013|publisher=}}</ref> After graduating from [[New York University]], he returned to India, releasing a pair of EPs before releasing his debut album.<ref>{{Cite web|date=29 March 2019|title=Radio City Freedom Awards 2019: The Complete Winners List -|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/radio-city-freedom-awards-2019-complete-winners-list/|access-date=4 March 2020|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Prateek Kuhad to make UAE debut at The Rotunda|url=https://gulfnews.com/entertainment/music/prateek-kuhad-to-make-uae-debut-at-the-rotunda-1.67907136|access-date=4 March 2020|website=gulfnews.com|language=en}}</ref> He also signed a publishing deal with Los Angeles-based company Cutcraft Music.<ref>{{Cite web|date=20 October 2019|first=Lalitha|last=Suhasini|title=The rise and rise of Prateek Kuhad|url=https://punemirror.indiatimes.com/others/sunday-read/the-rise-and-rise-of-prateek-kuhad/articleshow/71666873.cms|access-date=21 December 2020|website=Pune Mirror|language=en}}</ref> Kuhad became the first Indian to get signed by the American record label [[Elektra Records]] in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|agency=ANI|date=7 October 2020|title=Singer-songwriter Prateek Kuhad signs to American label Elektra Records|url=http://businessworld.in/article/Singer-songwriter-Prateek-Kuhad-signs-to-American-label-Elektra-Records/07-10-2020-329046|access-date=2021-11-18|website=BW Businessworld|language=en}}</ref>

==Musical style and influences==
During his time in NYU, Kuhad discovered [[Elliott Smith]]'s music which had a great influence on him. He then discovered other singer-songwriters such as [[Bob Dylan]] and [[Woody Guthrie]] along with newer artists like [[Laura Marling]] and [[Fleet Foxes]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.prateekkuhad.com/about/|access-date=21 April 2021|title=About Prateek Kuhad|website=prateekkuhad.com}}</ref> These artists inspired him to get better at guitar and start writing his own songs. These went on to become his major influences.

Being from a modern family in Jaipur, Kuhad was comfortable with communicating in both English and Hindi from the start and has been writing in both the languages. In his childhood, as there was no internet service in Jaipur, he predominantly listened to [[Indian pop]] and [[Music of Bollywood|Bollywood music]] and only occasionally to Western music. He was also inspired by [[Lucky Ali]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Prateek Kuhad on Finding Success and Larger Audience|url=https://www.firstpost.com/entertainment/prateek-kuhad-on-finding-success-ever-larger-audiences-i-never-thought-my-music-would-get-so-popular-7891101.html|url-status=live|access-date=22 April 2021|website=First Post|date=15 January 2020}}</ref>
[[File:Prateek Kuhad (cropped).jpg|thumb|upright=0.9|Kuhad performing]]
The acoustic guitar is Kuhad's primary instrument while he also plays piano and electric guitar. His music leans towards [[Americana (music)|Americana]] and [[Folk music|folk]] genres with acoustic sounds and unconventional song structures for most parts. His music has been praised as being "beautifully haunting" by ''[[Us Weekly]]'' and his vocals as being "angelic" by ''[[BrooklynVegan]]''.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" />

He mostly writes about love, relationships and his experiences, with vague lyrics that are open to interpretation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j7o7DAy52P8|access-date=25 April 2021|title=Prateek Kuhad On Love Life Learnings, Songwritng Process and Success Mantras The Ranveer Show 66|website=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>

==Awards and recognition==

* Indian Indie Album of the Year 2019 by [[iTunes]] and [[Spotify]]: Top Indian Indie Hits
* The Best India Act at the 2016 [[MTV Europe Music Awards]]
* Best Pop Artist Award at the Radio City Freedom Awards 2018
