'''Algernod Lanier Washington''' (born July 1, 1976), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Plies''', is an American rapper. Born in [[Fort Myers, Florida]], Washington was a [[wide receiver]] on the [[Miami Redskins]] football team of [[Miami University]] in Ohio in 1996 and 1997 before beginning his musical career. After dropping out of college, he was signed to [[Slip-n-Slide Records]], he released four studio albums from 2007 until 2010. Plies debuted in 2007 with ''[[The Real Testament]]'' with successful singles "[[Shawty (Plies song)|Shawty]]" (featuring [[T-Pain]]) and "[[Hypnotized (Plies song)|Hypnotized]]" (featuring [[Akon]]). Plies also released two albums in 2008, ''[[Definition of Real]]'' and ''[[Da REAList]]'', and later released ''[[Goon Affiliated]]'' in 2010.

==Early life==
Plies was born Algernod Lanier Washington in [[Fort Myers, Florida]] and grew up in the [[East Dunbar, Fort Myers, Florida|East Dunbar]] area of Fort Myers.<ref name="ArtistDirect">{{cite web|url=http://www.artistdirect.com/artist/bio/plies/3978106|title=Plies Biography|publisher=ArtistDirect|access-date=December 27, 2009}}</ref> While at [[Fort Myers Senior High School]], he played receiver and defensive back in its football team, was crowned Homecoming King, was the [[valedictorian]] of his high school class, and was named the "Best Dressed" student of his class.<ref name="Vibe"/>

He attended [[Miami University]] and under the name Nod Washington was wide receiver on its [[Miami RedHawks football|football team]] from 1995 to 1997,<ref name="sports ref">{{cite web|title=Nod Washington Stats|url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/players/nod-washington-1.html|publisher=sports-reference.com|access-date=November 29, 2012}}</ref> then transferred to the [[University of Central Florida]] and dropped out.<ref name="Vibe">{{citation|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qiYEAAAAMBAJ|title=Guns And Roses|last=Meadows-Ingram|first=Benjamin|date=December 2008|volume=16|number=12|work=Vibe|pages=93–99}}</ref> As a freshman in 1995, Washington had 9 receptions for 69 yards.<ref name="sports ref"/> In 1996, his sophomore year, Washington had 25 receptions for 262 yards and 2 touchdowns. The next season, Washington had 5 receptions for 43 yards.<ref name="sports ref"/>

==Music career==
In the late 1990s, Plies and his stepbrother Ronnell Lawrence Lavatte, also known as Big Gates, established an independent record label, Big Gates Records. Although Plies initially refused to rap, after a demonstration for one of his artists, Big Gates decided to keep Plies' verse on the track "Tell Dem Krackers Dat". Big Gates and Plies promoted the single and traveled many times to [[Miami]], which eventually led to a deal for Plies on Slip-n-Slide Records.<ref name="ArtistDirect"/> After signing to Slip-n-Slide in 2004, Plies released several mixtapes.<ref name="Vibe"/>

===2007: ''The Real Testament''===
''[[The Real Testament]]'' was released in August 2007. His debut single "[[Shawty (Plies song)|Shawty]]" featuring [[T-Pain]] topped the ''Billboard'' Hot Rap Tracks chart and peaked at number 9 on the Hot 100. "[[Hypnotized (Plies song)|Hypnotized]]", the second single, featured [[Akon]], and peaked at number 3 on the Rap chart and 14 on the Hot 100.<ref name="singles">[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p880573|pure_url=yes}} Plies > Charts & Awards > Billboard Singles]. allmusic. accessed November 20, 2008.</ref> The third single was "I Am the Club". On February 29, 2008, the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA) certified the album Gold for having sold over 500,000 units; five days later the RIAA did the same for singles "Shawty" and "Hypnotized".<ref name="RIAA">[http://riaa.org/goldandplatinumdata.php?resultpage=1&table=SEARCH_RESULTS&action=&title=&artist=Plies&format=&debutLP=&category=&sex=&releaseDate=&requestNo=&type=&level=&label=&company=&certificationDate=&awardDescription=&catalogNo=&aSex=&rec_id=&charField=&gold=&platinum=&multiPlat=&level2=&certDate=&album=&id=&after=&before=&startMonth=1&endMonth=1&startYear=1958&endYear=2008&sort=Artist&perPage=25 RIAA database] search for artist "Plies". Accessed November 20, 2008</ref> Plies made guest performances on [[DJ Khaled]]'s "[[I'm So Hood]]", also featuring [[T-Pain]], [[Trick Daddy]] and [[Rick Ross]] in 2007, which peaked at #19 on the Hot 100 and is included on Khaled second studio album ''[[We the Best]]'', and [[Fat Joe]]'s single "[[Ain't Sayin' Nothin']]" from ''[[The Elephant in the Room (album)|The Elephant in the Room]]'' in early 2008.

===2008: ''Definition of Real'' and ''Da REAList''===
''[[Definition of Real]]'', his second album, was released in June 2008, 10 months after releasing his first album. The lead single was "[[Bust It Baby|Bust It Baby Pt. 2]]" featuring [[Ne-Yo]], which peaked at number 2 on both the Hot Rap Tracks and Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Singles & Tracks charts and number 7 on the Hot 100.<ref name="singles"/> The album debuted at number 2 on the ''Billboard'' 200, selling over 214,000 copies its first week.<ref>{{cite web|last=Hasty|first=Katie|title=Lil Wayne Crushes the Competition to Debut at No. 1|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1045047/lil-wayne-crushes-the-competition-to-debut-at-no-1|work=Billboard|date=2008-06-18|access-date=2008-11-20}}</ref> The next single was "[[Please Excuse My Hands]]", featuring [[Jamie Foxx]] and [[The-Dream]].<ref name="singles"/><ref>{{Cite news|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|title=Plies Lines Up Star-Studded Follow-Up Singles Featuring Jamie Foxx, Keyshia Cole|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1592227/20080805/plies.jhtml|publisher=MTV News|date=2008-08-05|access-date=2008-11-20}}</ref> RIAA certified Gold "Bust It Baby" on September 17 and ''Definition of Real'' October 14.<ref name="RIAA"/>

That same year Plies released his third album, ''[[Da REAList]]'', in December 2008, six months after the release of his second album. The first single off this album is "[[Put It on Ya]]", featuring Chris J. The album debuted at number 14 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with 114,000 copies sold in its first week.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|title=Plies Readies His Second LP Of 2008; Rick Ross Works On Mixtape, Two New Albums, In Mixtape Monday|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1595253/20080919/plies.jhtml|publisher=MTV News|date=2008-09-19|access-date=2008-11-20}}</ref> The second single is "[[Want It, Need It]]", featuring [[Ashanti (entertainer)|Ashanti]], and the third is "[[Plenty Money]]". He did a guest performance on [[Ludacris]]' single "[[Nasty Girl (Ludacris song)|Nasty Girl]]" from Ludacris' album ''[[Theater of the Mind]]''. He also appeared on DJ Khaled's song "[[Out Here Grindin']], also featuring [[Akon]], Ross, [[Young Jeezy]], [[Lil Boosie]], [[Ace Hood]] and Trick Daddy, from Khaled's third album ''[[We Global]]'', which peaked at #38 on the Hot 100.

===2009–2010: ''Goon Affiliated''===
In a November 2008 interview with Plies, and according to Slip-n-Slide Records, the rapper announced he completed a fourth album which he planned to release February 16, 2009, but stated the exact date depended on the success of his third album.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Plies Interview|url=http://www.djbooth.net/index/interviews/entry/plies-interview-1120081/|publisher=DJ Booth|date=2008-11-20|access-date=2008-11-20|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151224052839/http://www.djbooth.net/index/interviews/entry/plies-interview-1120081/|archive-date=2015-12-24|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Plies|url=http://www.slipnsliderecords.net/plies.html|publisher=Slip-N-Slide Records|access-date=2008-11-20|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081201071858/http://www.slipnsliderecords.net/plies.html|archive-date=2008-12-01}}</ref> The album, ''[[Goon Affiliated]]'', ended up being released on June 8, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.11229/title.fabolous-young-jeezy-keri-hilson-on-plies-goon-affiliated|title=Fabolous, Young Jeezy, Keri Hilson On Plies' "Goon Affiliated"|author=HipHopDX|date=11 May 2010|work=HipHopDX}}</ref> The album's first two singles are "[[Becky (song)|Becky]]" and "[[She Got It Made (song)|She Got It Made]]" featuring [[Bei Maejor]]. The album debuted at #5 on the ''Billboard'' 200, much more successful than his previous album. He continued to make guest appearances on Usher's "[[Hey Daddy (Daddy's Home)]]" (that version not making Usher's sixth studio album ''[[Raymond vs. Raymond]]''), [[Gucci Mane]]'s "[[Wasted (Gucci Mane song)|Wasted]]", from ''[[The State vs. Radric Davis]]'' and Young Jeezy's "[[Lose My Mind (Young Jeezy song)|Lose My Mind]]", from Jeezy's fourth album ''[[Thug Motivation 103: Hustlerz Ambition]]'', all of which made the top 40 of the Hot 100.

In 2010, Plies released a mixtape titled ''You Need People Like Me'' on September 3, which included a song titled "Boosie" dedicated to the incarcerated rapper [[Lil Boosie]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Plies-You-Need-People-Like-Me-mixtape.149287.html|title=Plies - You Need People Like Me Hosted by Scream|work=DatPiff}}</ref> On November 18, he released another mixtape titled ''You Need People Like Me Pt. 2''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Plies-You-Need-People-Like-Me-Pt-2-mixtape.170539.html|title=Plies - You Need People Like Me Pt 2 Hosted by DJ Scream|work=DatPiff}}</ref> On December 23, 2010, he released another mixtape titled ''No Chaser''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Plies-No-Chaser-mixtape.181400.html|title=Plies - No Chaser Hosted by DJ Scream|work=DatPiff}}</ref>

==Persona==
David Jeffries of [[AllMusic]] described Plies's debut album ''The Real Testament'' as tracks covering both [[gangsta rap|gang life]] and love.<ref>{{cite web|last=Jeffries|first=David|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1088217|pure_url=yes}}|title=The Real Testament: Review|date=August 7, 2007|publisher=allmusic|access-date=December 26, 2009}}</ref> Similarly, Jeffries described the lyrical content of ''Da REAList'' as having "a spectrum that runs from irresponsible gun talk to irresponsible sex talk".<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1448344|pure_url=yes}}|title=Da Realist: Review|last=Jeffries|first=David|date=December 16, 2008|publisher=allmusic|access-date=December 27, 2009}}</ref> A profile of Plies in the December 2008 issue of ''Vibe'' magazine observed that Plies constantly referred to himself as "real" in his music and album titles. However, it pointed out that the word "has virtually synonymous with 'criminal' and, in some cases, almost superseded the idea of being able to actually rap".<ref name="Vibe"/> In July 2008, hip hop website [[HipHopDX]] published an investigative report suggesting that Plies exaggerated his criminal background.<ref name="Plies lied">{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.7397/title.dx-clusive-plies-lied-about-criminal-past|title= DX-clusive: Plies Lied About Criminal Past?|last=Arnold|first=Paul W.|date=July 28, 2008|work=HipHopDX|access-date=December 26, 2009}}</ref>

===Legal issues===
On July 2, 2006, after a shooting at a [[Gainesville, Florida]] nightclub, Plies was charged with illegal possession of a concealed weapon, and members of his [[wiktionary:entourage|entourage]] were charged with attempted murder.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Kellman|first=Andy|title=Plies - Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/plies-p880573/biography|publisher=Allmusic|year=2007|access-date=2008-10-05}}</ref> The shooting, in which 5 people were injured, started after Plies' microphone was cut off for [[Lil Boosie]] to perform, after Plies' performance ran over time. According to the promoter Jonathan Smith, Plies became "enraged". A fight began with fists and shouting, and ended with at least six rounds of bullets being fired.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Pakkala|first=Tiffany|title=5 shot in downtown club during rap show|work=The Gainesville Sun|date=2006-07-03|url=http://www.gainesville.com/article/20060703/LOCAL/207030332?p=all&tc=pgall|access-date=2008-07-01}}</ref>

In January 2017, Plies was arrested in [[Wesley Chapel, Florida]] for driving under the influence. Plies refused a roadside blood alcohol content test and was taken into custody.<ref>{{cite web | title = Plies Arrested for DUI | publisher = XXL | date = January 17, 2017 | url = http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/01/plies-arrested-for-dui/}}</ref>

So I dropped his ass like any real nigga would do like bitch, fuck you bruh.".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6524159/rapper-plies-get-body-slammed-attacked-by-fan-at-florida-concert |title=Plies Attacked by Fan at Florida Concert |publisher=Billboard |access-date=2015-08-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vibe.com/2015/04/plies-body-slam-fan-attack-video/ |title=Plies Body Slammed Off Stage In Tallahassee |publisher=Vibe |date=2015-04-04 |access-date=2015-08-15}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/34520/20150406/video-plies-gets-bodyslammed-offstage-attacker-acknowledges-f-ck-bruh.htm |title=VIDEO: Plies Gets Slammed Offstage, Attacker Acknowledges 'I F*ck With You Bruh' : Buzz |publisher=Music Times |date=2015-04-06 |access-date=2015-08-15}}</ref> On April 29, 2015, Plies would release a single titled "Dayum!" addressing the attack.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://uproxx.com/smokingsection/2015/04/plies-dayum-new-music/ |title=Plies Discusses Being Suplexed Off The Stage In His New Track 'Dayum!' |publisher=Uproxx.com |date=2015-04-29 |access-date=2015-08-15}}</ref>
