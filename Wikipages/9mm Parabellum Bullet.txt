{{nihongo|'''9mm Parabellum Bullet'''|キューミリ・パラベラム・バレット|Kyūmiri Paraberamu Baretto}} is a Japanese [[Rock music|rock]] band, formed in March 2004 in [[Yokohama]]. It consists of [[vocalist]] and [[rhythm guitarist]] Takuro Sugawara, [[guitarist]] and [[backing vocalist]] Yoshimitsu Taki, [[bassist]] Kazuhiko Nakamura, and [[drummer]] Chihiro Kamijo, who came up with the band's name. Piano pieces included in some songs are performed by Taki, while the characteristic shouts are provided by Nakamura.

==History==

===2005–2009: Early releases and major label debut===
The band signed to under_bar, a sub-label of Reverb Records, and released two early mini-albums, ''[[Gjallarhorn (album)|Gjallarhorn]]'' in 2005 and ''[[Phantomime]]'' in 2006, and played live shows in [[Yokohama]] and [[Tokyo]].{{Citation needed|date=September 2010}}

2007 saw the band signed to [[EMI Music Japan]] and the releases of ''The World e.p.'' which featured newly recorded version of songs from ''Gjallarhorn'' and two new tracks, and the ''Discommunication e.p.'', which features the song "Discommunication" and a 35-minute live recording.<ref>{{cite web| title=[9mm Parabellum Bullet] 総決算CDをメジャーでリリース| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/434| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2007-02-26| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| title=[9mm Parabellum Bullet] 満を持してメジャー初シングル発売| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/2538| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2007-07-10| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> At the end of 2007 they released their first full album, ''[[Termination (album)|Termination]]'', after which they began their first solo tour.<ref>{{cite web| title=[9mm Parabellum Bullet] 激PRICEの1stアルバム＆全国ツアー| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/3355| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2007-09-10| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

In the summer of 2008 the band released the double A-side single "Supernova/Wanderland", which also included piano instrumentals of both songs and a third song called "Wildpitch".<ref>{{cite web| title=9mm、新作はライブの人気曲＆初アニソンの両A面| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/5831| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2008-03-03| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> 9mm Parabellum Bullet were invited to play at [[Asian Kung-Fu Generation]]'s Nano-Mugen Festival that year at the [[Yokohama Arena]], a festival notable for featuring up-and-coming acts alongside more well known and foreign acts. They covered "Motorbreath" for the October 2008 [[Metallica]] tribute album ''Metal-ikka''.<ref>{{cite web| title=偏愛に満ちた邦楽アーティストMETALLICAカバー集| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/9608| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2008-09-17| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

In October the band released their second full album ''[[Vampire (album)|Vampire]]'' and proceeded onto the Vampire Empire Tour 08/09. In April 2009, the band released their first live DVD, ''Act I'', which contains 4 concerts from different points in their career.

===2009–2011: ''Revolutionary'' and ''Movement''===
In late spring of 2009, the band released the mini-album ''Black Market Blues e.p.'', which also included a recording of their free live performance at [[Yoyogi Park]] as a B-side. 2009 also saw 9mm Parabellum Bullet's first nomination at the [[MTV Video Music Awards Japan]] for "Best Rock Video", where they were nominated for the video of their song "Living Dying Message". They also performed this song live at the show.

9mm Parabellum Bullet appeared at numerous rock festivals across Japan notably including [[Rock in Japan Festival]], [[Rising Sun Rock Festival]] and Space Shower's Sweet Love Shower. On September 9, 2009, 9mm Parabellum Bullet had their first show at the [[Nippon Budokan]] titled 999. This performance would appear on the ''Cold Edge e.p.'', released on September 30.

9mm Parabellum Bullet were invited to play at Countdown Japan 09/10. In early-January they released the single "Inochi no Zenmai", which was used as the theme for the live-action adaptation of the ''[[Higanjima]]'' manga. The single includes a cover of [[Linda Yamamoto]]'s 1972 song "Dou ni mo Tomaranai".<ref>{{cite web| title = 9mm Parabellum Bullet Single| url=http://www.jame-world.com/us/news-57940-9mm-parabellum-bullet-single.html| work=jame-world.com| date=2009-11-11| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> It was announced that April 21, 2010, would be the date of 9mm Parabellum Bullet's third full album release, titled ''[[Revolutionary (album)|Revolutionary]]''.<ref>{{cite web| title = 9mm Parabellum Bullet New Album| url=http://www.jame-world.com/us/news-60867-9mm-parabellum-bullet-new-album.html| work=jame-world.com| date=2010-02-01| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> It was followed by a tour across Japan. In late March, a music video was shot for the last track of the new album, called "The Revolutionary".

On May 18, 2011, the single "Atarashii Hikari" was released. The band's fourth album ''Movement'' was released on June 15. They also performed at [[Yokohama Arena]] on June 26.<ref>{{cite web| title=9mm初の横アリでまさかのアコースティックセッション| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/52157| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2011-06-29| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

===2012–present: ''Dawning'' and ''Waltz on Life Line''===
9mm Parabellum Bullet covered "Territorial Pissings" for the album ''[[Nevermind Tribute]]'', which was released on April 4, 2012 and is composed of various Japanese bands covering the entirety of [[Nirvana (band)|Nirvana]]'s highly acclaimed album ''[[Nevermind]]''.<ref>{{cite web | title = Nevermind Tribute | work = cdjapan.co.jp| url = http://www.cdjapan.co.jp/detailview.html?KEY=UMCF-1072| access-date = 2012-02-23}}</ref> Their first acoustic performance for ''[[MTV Unplugged]]'' was held at Billboard Live Tokyo with a limited capacity of 150 fans and released on home video on May 27. Their fourth single "Heart ni Hi wo Tsukete" followed on October 24, 2012.<ref>{{cite web| title = 9mm Parabellum Bullet's New Single| url=http://www.jame-world.com/us/news-89329-9mm-parabellum-bullet-s-new-single.html| work=jame-world.com| date=2012-08-15| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

On April 1, 2013, EMI Music Japan was completely merged into [[Universal Music Japan]] as a sublabel by the name [[EMI Records|EMI Records Japan]] as a result of [[EMI#Sony.2FUniversal sale|Universal Music's purchase of EMI]] in September 2012. All the artists from EMI Music Japan will continue releasing material at Universal Music Japan while still maintaining the catalogue code (TOCT).<ref>[http://www.universal-music.co.jp/press-releases/2013/03/11/ UNIVERSAL MUSIC JAPAN]</ref> Their fifth single "Answer and Answer" was released on May 29.<ref>{{cite web| title = 9mm Parabellum Bullet's New Single| url=http://www.jame-world.com/us/news-94638-9mm-parabellum-bullet-s-new-single.html| work=jame-world.com| date=2013-03-14| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> 9mm Parabellum Bullet released their fifth album ''Dawning'' on June 26, 2013.<ref>{{cite web| title=9mm、5thアルバム「Dawning」"9周年盤"に17曲PV集| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/88356| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2013-04-09| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

They performed a cover of "Instant Music" for the February 2014 [[The Pillows]] tribute album, ''Rock and Sympathy''.<ref>{{cite web| title = Second Tribute Album to the pillows| url=http://www.jame-world.com/us/news-98334-second-tribute-album-to-the-pillows.html| work=jame-world.com| date=2013-11-20| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

The band took part in the first day of [[Luna Sea]]'s Lunatic Fest at [[Makuhari Messe]] on June 27, 2015.<ref>{{cite web| title=LUNA SEA "LUNATIC FEST." LUNACY was announced as the opening act for both days| url=http://www.en.barks.jp/news/?id=1000005642| work=barks.jp| date=2015-06-01| access-date=2016-03-27| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160809134851/http://www.en.barks.jp/news/?id=1000005642| archive-date=2016-08-09| url-status=dead}}</ref> Luna Sea bassist [[J (musician)|J]] joined them onstage for "Cold Edge".<ref>{{cite web| title = LUNA SEA Celebrate 25th Anniversary With Star-studded LUNATIC FEST.| url=http://www.mtv81.com/features/live-reports/luna-sea-celebrate-25th-anniversary-with-star-studded-lunatic-fest/| work=[[MTV]]| date=2015-07-31| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> 9mm Parabellum Bullet released a quadruple A-side single, "Hangyaku no March/Dark Horse/Daremo Shiranai/Mad Pierrot", on September 9.<ref>{{cite web| title = New Single from 9mm Parabellum Bullet| url=http://www.jame-world.com/us/news-116406-new-single-from-9mm-parabellum-bullet.html| work=jame-world.com| date=2015-07-10| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> They also contributed a cover to a November 2015 tribute album for The Telephones, ''We are Disco!!! ~Tribute to The Telephones~''.<ref>{{cite web| title = the telephones Tribute Album| url=http://www.jame-world.com/us/news-117585-the-telephones-tribute-album.html| work=jame-world.com| date=2015-09-14| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>

In January 2016, 9mm Parabellum Bullet announced they had switched to the independent record label Sazanga Records.<ref>{{cite web| title=9mm Parabellum Bullet、6thアルバムは自主レーベルから発表| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/174240| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2016-01-29| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref> The album ''Waltz on Life Line'' was released on April 27, 2016. The band provided the opening theme songs "Inferno" and "Sacrifice" for the ''[[Berserk (2016 TV series)|Berserk]]'' anime adaptation.<ref>{{cite web| title=9mm Parabellum Bullet、アニメ「ベルセルク」オープニングテーマ担当| url=http://natalie.mu/music/news/181146| work=[[Natalie (website)|Natalie]]| language=ja| date=2016-03-26| access-date=2016-03-27}}</ref>
