'''Leland Tyler Wayne''' (born September 16, 1993), professionally known as '''Metro Boomin''' (also known as '''Young Metro''' or simply '''Metro'''), is an American [[Hip hop production|record producer]], record executive, and DJ managed by [[SAL&CO]].<ref name="faderconstruction1">{{cite web|last=Zeichner|first=Naomi|title=Beat Construction: Metro Boomin|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/08/14/beat-construction-metro-boomin/|publisher=The Fader|date=August 13, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Halperin|first=Shirley|date=August 27, 2019|title=Music Moguls of the Year: The Weeknd Manager Sal Slaiby Swears He'll Be 'The Greatest Immigrant' Trump Ever Saw|url=https://variety.com/2019/music/features/maverick-sal-slaiby-the-weeknd-1203314531/|accessdate=June 30, 2020|website=Variety}}</ref> Raised in [[St. Louis, Missouri|St. Louis]], Wayne began a production career while in high school and became known for his successful recordings with [[Atlanta]] [[hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[trap music|trap]] artists such as [[Future (rapper)|Future]], [[21 Savage]], [[Gucci Mane]] and [[Migos]] in the mid-2010s.<ref name="am">{{cite web|last=Kellman|first=Andy|title=Metro Boomin – Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/metro-boomin-mn0003138395/biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=December 5, 2017}}</ref> In 2017, ''[[Forbes]]'' called him "easily one of the most in-demand hitmakers in the world",<ref name="f">{{cite web|last1=McIntyre|first1=Hugh|title=Super Producer Metro Boomin Might Be The Next Big Star In Hip-Hop (As A Frontman This Time)|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2017/06/29/super-producer-metro-boomin-might-be-the-next-big-star-in-hip-hop/#6ca9b3dc7628|website=[[Forbes]]|accessdate=December 5, 2017}}</ref> while ''[[Stereogum]]'' described him as "one of the most original, vivid, important voices in rap right now."<ref>{{cite web |last=Breihan |first=Tom |title=Metro Boomin Is The Best Rap Producer To Come Along In Years |url=https://www.stereogum.com/1943691/metro-boomin-is-the-best-rap-producer-to-come-along-in-years/franchises/status-aint-hood/ |website=[[Stereogum]] |date=May 31, 2017 |accessdate=November 1, 2018}}</ref>

Early production success for Wayne came with tracks such as [[ILoveMakonnen]]'s 2014 hit "[[Tuesday (ILoveMakonnen song)|Tuesday]]" and [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] and [[Future (rapper)|Future]]'s 2015 single "[[Jumpman (song)|Jumpman]]". Since then, he has amassed over a dozen top 20 hits,<ref name="am" /> including "[[Mask Off]]" by Future, "[[Bank Account (song)|Bank Account]]" by 21 Savage, "[[Congratulations (Post Malone song)|Congratulations]]" by [[Post Malone]], "[[Tunnel Vision (Kodak Black song)|Tunnel Vision]]" by [[Kodak Black]], "[[Bad and Boujee]]" by [[Migos]], and "[[Heartless (The Weeknd song)|Heartless]]" by [[The Weeknd]], with the latter two reaching number one on the [[Billboard Hot 100]]<ref name="am" /><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/the-weeknd-heartless-billboard-hot-100/ |title=The Weeknd's "Heartless" Song Reaches No. 1 on Billboard Hot 100 |website=XXL |archive-date=December 9, 2019 |access-date=February 20, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|title=The Weeknd|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-weeknd/chart-history/HSI/song/1179013|access-date=May 20, 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Migos|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Migos/chart-history/HSI/song/996420|access-date=May 20, 2021|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>  He has also released full-length collaborations, including ''[[Savage Mode]]'' (2016) and ''[[Savage Mode II]]'' (2020) with [[21 Savage]], ''[[Perfect Timing (mixtape)|Perfect Timing]]'' (2017), with [[Nav (rapper)|Nav]], ''[[Double or Nothing (Big Sean and Metro Boomin album)|Double or Nothing]]'' (2017) with [[Big Sean]], and ''[[DropTopWop]]'' (2017) with Gucci Mane. His debut solo album ''[[Not All Heroes Wear Capes]]'' was released in November 2018, debuting at number one on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart. "Congratulations" by Post Malone featuring [[Quavo]] was his first diamond certification (10x platinum), being certified in 2019.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Post+Malone&ti=Congratulations#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum – RIAA|website=RIAA|url-status=live|archive-date=March 13, 2019|access-date=September 6, 2019}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Leland Tyler Wayne was born on September 16, 1993, in [[St. Louis, Missouri]],<ref name=TrapWorldHipHop>{{cite web|title=Metro Boomin Biography|url=https://www.trapworldhiphop.com/trap-artists/metro-boomin-biography/|publisher=TrapWorldHipHop|date=February 20, 2017}}</ref> where he attended [[Parkway North High School]]. He has four siblings.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/04/12/metro-boomin-cover-story-interview|title=How Metro Boomin Became The Most Trusted Guy In Rap|work=The Fader|date=April 12, 2016|accessdate=July 13, 2017}}</ref> After a brief stint playing bass guitar in his middle school band, he turned to making beats in the seventh grade at age 13. This happened when his mother bought him a laptop and he got a copy of the music production software [[FL Studio|FruityLoops]].<ref name="faderconstruction2">{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/08/14/beat-construction-metro-boomin/|title=Beat Construction: Metro Boomin|last=Zeichner|first=Naomi|date=August 13, 2013|publisher=The Fader}}</ref> In high school, Metro churned out five beats a day.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/style/wp/2014/09/12/the-real-rap-stars-of-atlanta-a-new-generation-of-producers-working-at-the-speed-of-sound/ | title=The real rap stars of Atlanta: A new generation of producers working at the speed of sound | work=The Washington Post | date=September 12, 2014 | accessdate=March 16, 2016 | author=Chris Richards}}</ref> Initially, Metro wanted to rap, and started making beats so that he could have music to rap over. However, he eventually turned his full attention toward hip hop production.<ref name=jenesismag>{{cite web|last=Agnew|first=Thomas|title=Metro Boomin: Elevated Movements|url=http://www.jenesismagazine.com/feature-metro-boomin-elevated-movements/|publisher=Jenesis Magazine|date=February 5, 2014}}</ref> As he continued to hone his production skills, while still in high school, he began to utilize online social media platforms, such as Twitter, to network with more established rap artists as well as beat submissions for potential music placements.<ref name=vibemag>{{cite web|last=Krishnamurthy|first=Sowmya|title=Metro Boomin Talks Producing 'Karate Chop'|url=http://www.vibe.com/article/metro-boomin-talks-producing-karate-chop-new-music-lil-durk-yg-schoolboy-q/|publisher=VIBE Magazine|date=May 15, 2013}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2009–2013: Career beginnings ===
During high school, Metro's mother would often drive him for over eight hours from [[St. Louis]] to [[Atlanta]] to collaborate with artists he met online. One of the first artists with whom he worked was Bricksquad Monopoly rapper Tay Don which then led him to collaborate with Bricksquad label artists such as [[OJ da Juiceman|OJ Da Juiceman]], [[Gucci Mane]], and eventually his frequent collaborator [[Future (rapper)|Future]], an artist that he continues to collaborate with to this day.<ref name="jenesismag" />

Metro Boomin first started traveling to Atlanta in the 11th grade to pursue a career in music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://top40-charts.com/artist.php?aid=19846|title=Metro Boomin Biography, Discography, Chart History|website=Top40-Charts.com|accessdate=April 1, 2019}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', Metro says that Atlanta rapper [[OJ Da Juiceman]] was the first popular artist to rap on his beats, a string of collaborations that ultimately led to Metro meeting and working with [[Gucci Mane]] during the summer between his junior and senior years of high school.<ref name="xxlmag">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2013/02/meet-the-man-behind-futures-karate-chop-metro-boomin/|title=Meet Metro Boomin, The Producer Behind Future's 'Karate Chop'|last=Martinez-Belkin|first=Neil|date=February 6, 2013|work=XXL Magazine}}</ref> Upon graduating high school, Metro Boomin moved to Atlanta to attend [[Morehouse College]], studying Business Management. Ultimately however, he chose to take a hiatus from school after a semester, because the schedule demands of a full-time music career became too much to balance.<ref name=xxlmag /> Since, Metro has collaborated with artists including [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Ludacris]], [[Juicy J]], [[Yo Gotti]], [[21 Savage]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Chief Keef]], [[The Weeknd]], [[YG (rapper)|YG]], [[Young Jeezy]], [[Meek Mill]], [[Travis Scott]], [[Ace Hood]], [[Young Scooter]], [[Young Thug]], [[Rich Homie Quan]], [[Trinidad James]], [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Lana Del Rey]], [[Lil Uzi Vert]], [[Migos]], [[DJ Khaled]], [[Schoolboy Q]], [[Post Malone]], [[Nav (rapper)|Nav]], [[Swae Lee]], [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]], [[Lil Wayne]], [[Waka Flocka Flame]], and [[Kanye West]]. Metro also regularly collaborates with other modern hip hop producers, including [[Sonny Digital]], [[808 Mafia|TM88]], [[Southside (record producer)|Southside]], [[Zaytoven]], [[Young Chop]], [[DJ Spinz]] and most recently [[Tay Keith]] on ''[[Not All Heroes Wear Capes]]''.<ref name=faderconstruction1 />

Metro Boomin has also worked extensively with popular rapper Future. The two first worked together on a song called "Hard", included on [[DJ Esco]]'s ''Welcome 2 Mollyworld'' mixtape,<ref name=faderconstruction2 /> and have collaborated on numerous songs since, including two singles from Future's second album, ''[[Honest (Future album)|Honest]]'', the album's lead single, "[[Karate Chop (song)|Karate Chop]]", and title track, "[[Honest (Future song)|Honest]]", co-produced by [[DJ Spinz]].<ref name=complexfeature>{{cite web|last=Gleckman|first=Alexander|title=Interview: Meet Metro Boomin Talks Working With Future, "Honest," and Sacrificing A Normal Life|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/09/metro-boomin-interview|publisher=Complex Magazine|date=September 11, 2013}}</ref>

=== 2013–2016: Rise to fame ===
In May 2013, Metro formally announced his debut mixtape, ''19 & Boomin''.<ref name=compleximserious>{{cite web|last=Nostro|first=Lauren|title=Premiere: Metro Boomin f/ Trinidad Jame$ & Curtis Williams "Serious"|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/05/metro-boomin-f-trinidad-james-curtis-williams-serious|publisher=Complex Magazine|date=May 30, 2013}}</ref> Following warm-up singles, featuring artists like Trinidad James, Gucci Mane, and others, Metro released the mixtape, hosted by popular mixtape website LiveMixtapes, on October 7, 2013.<ref name=faderboomin>{{cite web|last=Garvey|first=Meaghan|title=Download Metro Boomin's 19 & Boomin Mixtape|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/10/08/download-metro-boomins-19-boomin-mixtape/|publisher=FADER Magazine|date=October 8, 2013}}</ref> The mixtape, all original songs, included "Maison Margiela", featuring [[Future (rapper)|Future]], and "Some More", featuring [[Young Thug]], both of which were subsequently released as music videos.<ref name=somemore>{{cite web|last=Zeichner|first=Naomi|title=Video: Young Thug "Some More"|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/11/27/video-young-thug-some-more-prod-by-metro-boomin-tm88-and-sonny-digital/|publisher=FADER Magazine|date=November 27, 2013}}</ref><ref name=margiela>{{cite web|last=DeVille|first=Chris|title=Future – "Maison Margiela" Video|url=http://www.stereogum.com/1618902/future-maison-margiela-video/video/|publisher=Stereogum|date=January 5, 2014}}</ref>

In March 2014, Metro and [[Young Thug]] announced that they would release a collaborative album, performed and released under the moniker "Metro Thuggin". The album was to be self-titled and was planned to be released sometime in 2015. Along with the announcement, Metro Thuggin released the collaborative track, "The Blanguage".<ref name=metrothugginpfk>{{cite web|last=Minsker|first=Evan|title=Young Thug and Metro Boomin Team Up as Metro Thuggin, Share The Blanguage|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/54510-young-thug-and-metro-boomin-team-up-as-metro-thuggin-share-the-blanguage-listen/|publisher=Pitchfork Media|date=March 26, 2014}}</ref> The 'Metro Thuggin' project was ultimately scrapped, however photographer [[Cam Kirk]] claims to have a copy of the complete, finished album. A few leaked tracks from the project were found circulating the internet in late 2015, yet an official mixtape has never surfaced.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/10/cam-kirk-i-wish-the-world-got-to-hear-metro-thuggin|title=Cam Kirk Shares Photo of 'Metro Thuggin' Project: 'I Wish the World Got to Hear'|website=Complex|accessdate=February 6, 2019}}</ref>

In 2014, Metro made an appearance on [[Nicki Minaj]]'s third studio album, ''[[The Pinkprint]],'' producing the track "Want Some More".<ref>{{Citation|title="Nicki Minaj — The Pinkprint — Standard Edition"|date=December 15, 2014|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/the-pinkprint/1440848423|language=en-US|access-date=May 20, 2021}}</ref>

In October 2014, Metro executive produced [[Future (rapper)|Future]]'s ''[[Monster (mixtape)|Monster]]'' mixtape. This spawned the first appearance of the hit single, "[[Fuck Up Some Commas]]". Metro also produced the sixth single from Future's ''[[Honest (Future album)|Honest]]'', "[[I Won]]", which features [[Kanye West]].<ref name="complexiwon">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/04/future-f-kanye-west-i-won|title=Future f/ Kanye West "I Won" (Prod. x Metro Boomin)|last=Frydenlund|first=Zach|date=April 7, 2014|work=Complex Magazine}}</ref> The song became a single a month after the album was released.

Metro served as executive producer for [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] and Future's collaborative mixtape ''[[What a Time to Be Alive]]'', released on September 20, 2015. In addition, he produced or co-produced seven of the 11 tracks on the mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|title=Drake's Instagram|url=https://instagram.com/p/73qludjQDf/|date=September 20, 2015}}</ref>

Metro also served alongside [[DJ Esco]] as executive producer for Future's 2016 mixtape, ''[[Purple Reign]]''. In 2016, he won Producer of the Year at the [[2016 BET Hip Hop Awards|BET Hip Hop Awards]].<ref name="BETHH">{{cite web | url=http://www.thefader.com/2016/10/05/2016-bet-hip-hop-award-winners | title=Here Are All The Winners From The 2016 BET Hip Hop Awards | work=The Fader | date=October 5, 2016 | accessdate=July 30, 2017}}</ref>

In 2016, Metro Boomin was credited with produced charting hits such as "[[Jumpman (song)|Jumpman]]" by Future and [[Drake (rapper)|Drake]], "[[Bad and Boujee]]" by Migos, "[[Low Life (song)|Low Life]]" by Future and [[The Weeknd]], and the 21 Savage collaboration "[[X (21 Savage and Metro Boomin song)|X]]". That year, he also contributed to Kanye West's ''[[The Life of Pablo]]'', and he and 21 Savage released the EP ''[[Savage Mode]]''. In 2017, he produced charting singles such as "[[Tunnel Vision (Kodak Black song)|Tunnel Vision]]" by [[Kodak Black]], "[[Bounce Back (Big Sean song)|Bounce Back]]" by [[Big Sean]], "[[Mask Off]]" by [[Future (rapper)|Future]], and "[[Bank Account (song)|Bank Account]]" by [[21 Savage]].<ref name="am" />

=== 2017: ''Perfect Timing'', ''Without Warning'', ''Double or Nothing'' ===
On July 14, 2017, Metro and Canadian rapper and fellow record producer [[Nav (rapper)|Nav]] released two singles, "[[Perfect Timing (Intro)]]" and "[[Call Me (Nav and Metro Boomin song)|Call Me]]", the dual lead singles from their collaborative mixtape, ''[[Perfect Timing (mixtape)|Perfect Timing]]''. The mixtape was released by Boominati Worldwide, [[XO (record label)|XO]], and [[Republic Records]], exactly one week later, on July 21. It features guest appearances from [[Lil Uzi Vert]], [[Playboi Carti]], [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]], [[21 Savage]], [[Belly (rapper)|Belly]], and [[Gucci Mane]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/07/12/metro-boomin-nav-release-date-perfect-timing|title=Metro Boomin And NAV Announce Release Date For Perfect Timing Project|publisher=[[The Fader]]|accessdate=July 14, 2017}}</ref>

On October 31, 2017 ([[Halloween]]), Metro and American rappers 21 Savage and Offset released their collaborative studio album, ''[[Without Warning (21 Savage, Offset, and Metro Boomin album)|Without Warning]]'', as a surprise release. On the same day, "[[Ric Flair Drip]]", performed by Metro and Offset was released as the lead single. The album features guest appearances from [[Travis Scott]] and [[Quavo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/21-savage-offset-metro-boomin-without-warning/|title=21 Savage / Offset / Metro Boomin – 'Without Warning'|publisher=Pitchfork|accessdate=October 31, 2017}}</ref>

On November 3, 2017, Metro and American rapper [[Big Sean]] released a single titled "Pull Up n Wreck", featuring American rapper 21 Savage, the lead single from their collaborative studio album, ''[[Double or Nothing (Big Sean and Metro Boomin album)|Double or Nothing]]''. The mixtape was released on December 8, 2017. It features guest appearances from Travis Scott, [[2 Chainz]], 21 Savage, [[Kash Doll]], [[Young Thug]], and [[Swae Lee]]. On February 13, 2018, "So Good", featuring American rapper Kash Doll became the lead single.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/big-sean-metro-boomin-double-or-nothing/|title=Big Sean / Metro Boomin – 'Double or Nothing'|publisher=Pitchfork|accessdate=October 31, 2017}}</ref>

=== 2018–present: "Retirement", ''Not All Heroes Wear Capes'' and ''Savage Mode II'' ===
In January 2018, Metro Boomin was featured in a [[Gap (company)|Gap]] campaign with American singer [[SZA (singer)|SZA]], alongside a self-produced remix of "[[Hold Me Now (Thompson Twins song)|Hold Me Now]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/01/sza-metro-boomin-gap-ad|title=SZA Dances to Metro Boomin's Remix of an '80s Pop Song in New Gap Commercial|date=January 24, 2018|work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|accessdate=November 2, 2018}}</ref><ref name="XXLGAP">{{Cite news|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/01/metro-boomin-classic-1980s-pop-song-gap-remix-campaign/|title=METRO BOOMIN FLIPS A CLASSIC 1980'S POP SONG FOR GAP'S LOGO REMIX CAMPAIGN|date=January 24, 2018|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|accessdate=November 2, 2018}}</ref> The remix was released to digital platforms on the same day as the campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/hold-me-now-metro-boomin-mix-single/1334671887|title=Hold Me Now (Metro Boomin Mix) – Single by Thompson Twins on Apple Music|date=January 25, 2018|via=[[Apple (company)|Apple]]|accessdate=November 2, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/metro-boomin-releases-hold-me-now-remix-from-gap-logo-remix-campaign-new-song.1977122.html|title=Metro Boomin Releases "Hold Me Now (Remix)" From Gap "Logo Remix" Campaign|date=January 24, 2018|work=HotNewHipHop|accessdate=November 2, 2018}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'', explaining the campaign with Gap and the remix, he stated;

{{cquote|To remix the song, it was really about the [original] track leading the direction, I kept the rhythm of the song more uptempo, more of their style, with that old-school feel. But I put the 808s on it just to have that bounce. It was about mixing both worlds but not really losing the essence of the original.<ref name="XXLGAP" />}}

In April 2018, Metro Boomin announced his "retirement" from rap on his Instagram page, changing his bio to "Retired record producer/DJ".<ref>{{cite web |title=Metro Boomin Want More? Producer claims to have 'Retired from rap' |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/metro-boomin-retires-rap-producer-instagram-drake-future-kanye-migos-a8302806.html%3famp |website=[[The Independent]] |date=April 13, 2018 |access-date=July 14, 2018}}</ref> However, he since garnered production credits on American rapper [[Nicki Minaj]]'s fourth studio album ''[[Queen (Nicki Minaj album)|Queen]]'', as well as Minaj's labelmate [[Lil Wayne]], on his twelfth studio album, ''[[Tha Carter V]]'', which both debuted in the top 5 of the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

On October 26, 2018, multiple billboards appeared in [[Atlanta]] and [[New York City|New York]] depicting Metro Boomin as a "missing person".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/10/26/metro-boomin-missing-persons-billboard|title=What is up with these weird Metro Boomin "missing person" billboards?|publisher=The Fader|date=October 26, 2018|access-date=October 28, 2018}}</ref> It was later revealed to be a teaser for his debut studio album, ''[[Not All Heroes Wear Capes]]'', for his comeback, which he had been working on since 2015. The album was released on November 2, 2018, and features guest appearances from [[Gucci Mane]], [[Travis Scott]], [[21 Savage]], [[Swae Lee]], [[Gunna (rapper)|Gunna]], [[Young Thug]], [[Wizkid]], [[J. Balvin]], [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]], [[Kodak Black]], and [[Drake (musician)|Drake]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Metro Boomin Drops New Album With Drake, Travis Scott, Young Thug, More: Listen |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/metro-boomin-drops-new-album-with-drake-travis-scott-young-thug-more-listen/ |website=[[Pitchfork Media]] |date=November 2, 2018 |accessdate=November 2, 2018}}</ref> ''[[Not All Heroes Wear Capes]]'' debuted at number one on the ''Billboard'' 200 and number 16 on the [[UK Albums Chart|UK Top 40 Albums]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Caulfield |first=Keith |title=Metro Boomin's 'Not All Heroes Wear Capes' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Albums Chart |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8484308/metro-boomin-not-all-heroes-wear-capes-number-1-billboard-200 |accessdate=November 15, 2018 |work=Billboard |publisher=Hollywood Reporter-Billboard Media Group}}</ref>

On November 27, 2019, he co-produced Canadian singer [[The Weeknd]]'s fourth [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] number-one single, "[[Heartless (The Weeknd song)|Heartless]]", with [[Illangelo]] and [[Dre Moon]]. The song was released as the lead single from the singer's fourth studio album, ''[[After Hours (The Weeknd album)|After Hours]]'' (2020). Metro also helped produce three other tracks on the album, "[[Escape from LA (song)|Escape from LA]]", "[[Faith (The Weeknd song)|Faith]]" and "[[Until I Bleed Out (The Weeknd song)|Until I Bleed Out]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Credits / After Hours / The Weeknd – TIDAL|url=https://listen.tidal.com/album/134815153/credits|access-date=December 29, 2020|via=Tidal}}</ref>

On September 29, 2020, Metro and American rapper 21 Savage announced their fourth collaborative project, ''[[Savage Mode II]]'', a sequel to their EP, ''[[Savage Mode]]'' (2016). It features guest appearances from Drake, Young Thug, and [[Young Nudy]]. On October 13, 2020, the dual lead singles became "[[Runnin (21 Savage and Metro Boomin song)|Runnin]]" and "[[Mr. Right Now]]", the latter of which features Drake.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://variety.com/2020/music/news/21-savage-metro-boomin-savage-mode-2-morgan-freeman-1234785894/amp/|title=21 Savage and Metro Boomin Unveil New Album 'Savage Mode 2' With Help From Morgan Freeman|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|last=Ellise|first=Shafer|date=September 28, 2020|accessdate=October 1, 2020}}</ref> The album was released on October 2, 2020, and debuted atop on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, earning both artists their second number-one albums, that too, back-to-back.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=21 Savage & Metro Boomin's 'Savage Mode II' Debuts at No. 1 on Billboard 200 Chart|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9463907/21-savage-metro-boomin-savage-billboard-200-chart-debut|access-date=October 12, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
Specializing in the [[Trap music|trap]] sub-genre of [[Hip hop music|hip hop]], Metro Boomin utilizes a distinctive mix of heavy bass, rattling synthetic percussion and dark, gothic melodies.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-rap-producer-shaping-pop-music-1512216001|title=Metro Boomin: The Rap Producer Shaping Pop Music|last=Shah|first=Neil|date=December 2, 2017|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=February 13, 2019|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref> Metro Boomin hardly strays from hip hop, although he has produced 4 tracks from The Weeknd's [[contemporary R&B|R&B]] album [[After Hours (The Weeknd album)|After Hours]];<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/the-weeknds-after-hours-production-credits-revealed-news.106645.html |title=The Weeknd's "After Hours" Production Credits Revealed |website=HotNewHipHop |date=March 18, 2020}}</ref> in addition, he has also remixed pop songs such as "[[Hold Me Now (Thompson Twins song)|Hold Me Now]]" (produced as a Gap marketing collaboration).<ref name="XXLGAP" />

=== Producer tags ===
In 2014 Metro Boomin got the tag called "Metro Boomin wants some more", then in early 2016, Metro Boomin received widespread attention for his producer tag. Metro's most prominent and iconic tag "If Young Metro don't trust you I'm gon' shoot you" is performed by [[Future (rapper)|Future]].  The origin of the tag comes from the song "Right Now" by [[Uncle Murda]], also produced by Metro Boomin. The tag was first noticeably recognized by fans in [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s song, "[[Jumpman (song)|Jumpman]]" from the collaborative mixtape ''[[What a Time to Be Alive]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/krystieyandoli/if-young-metro-dont-trust-you|title=Here's The Story Behind The Phrase "If Young Metro Don't Trust You, they Gon' Shoot You"|last=Lee Yandoli|first=Krystie|accessdate=December 5, 2018|work=Buzzfeed}}</ref>

== Boominati Worldwide ==
Boominati Worldwide is a label owned by Metro Boomin. It was launched in June 2017 in partnership with [[Republic Records]] and [[Universal Music Group]]. Metro stated: "Launching Boominati Worldwide is the next step in my career as a producer, an artist and a businessman. I created the label to represent a collective of highly gifted individuals who have the shared gift of being able to influence the world through culture and art".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/metro-boomin-boominati-worldwide-republic-records-universal-music-group/|title=Metro Boomin Launches Boominati Worldwide With Republic and UMG|work=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]|last=Bryan|first=Hahn|date=June 28, 2017|accessdate=October 2, 2020}}</ref>
