'''AJR''' is an American [[indie pop]] trio composed of multi-instrumentalist brothers Adam, Jack, and Ryan Met. Their most successful songs include "[[I'm Ready (AJR song)|I'm Ready]]", "[[Sober Up]]", "[[Burn the House Down]]", "[[Way Less Sad]]", "100 Bad Days", "[[Weak (AJR song)|Weak]]", and "[[Bang! (AJR song)|Bang!]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5720546/nyc-brother-trio-ajr-ready-for-stardom|title=NYC Brother Trio AJR 'Ready' for Stardom|last=Hyman|first=Dan|date=September 24, 2013|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=December 28, 2016}}</ref> In 2019, their third album ''[[Neotheater]]'' debuted at number 8 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]],<ref name="Billboard 200 number 8">[https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8510980/ajr-first-top-rock-albums-neotheater AJR Scores First Top Rock Albums No. 1 With 'Neotheater'] Retrieved 13 June 2019</ref> and hit number one on the Top Rock Albums chart.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8510980/ajr-first-top-rock-albums-neotheater|title=AJR Scores First Top Rock Albums No. 1 With 'Neotheater'|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> "Bang!" is their highest-charting song and only song to reach the top 10, peaking at number 8 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] on January 22, 2021.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=AJR|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/ajr/chart-history/HSI/song/1189303|access-date=2021-03-13|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

The name "AJR" comes from the first initials of the three brothers, Adam, Jack, and Ryan. The band writes, produces, and mixes their music in the living room of their apartment in [[New York City]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/things-indie-pop-band-ajr/story?id=46798580|title=5 things to know about indie-pop band AJR|website=ABC News|language=en|access-date=2019-11-04}}</ref> Their musical style has been described as "eclectic," combining elements of [[Pop music|pop]], [[Doo-wop music|doo-wop]], [[Electronic music|electronic]], and [[dubstep]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nypost.com/2013/10/15/could-ajr-be-the-next-jonas-brothers/|title=Could AJR be the next Jonas Brothers?|last=Miller|first=Gregory E.|date=October 15, 2013|website=New York Post|access-date=December 28, 2016}}</ref>

== History ==

=== 2005–2013: Formation and debut album ===
The Met brothers (born Metzger) initially began writing, producing, and mixing their own material in the living room of their [[Chelsea, Manhattan|Chelsea]] apartment in Manhattan, New York, focusing on [[DIY]] [[indie pop]] music. They began performing around 2006, [[busking]] in [[Central Park]] and [[Washington Square Park]]. Initially, Jack played the harmonica, Ryan played the piano, and Adam played the bass. They later began playing various instruments. They began by performing [[cover (music)|covers]],<ref name="NYPost">{{cite news|url=https://nypost.com/2013/10/15/could-ajr-be-the-next-jonas-brothers/|title=Could AJR be the next Jonas Brothers?|last=Miller|first=Gregory E.|date=15 October 2013|newspaper=New York Post}}</ref> and also put out a now removed 2010 EP.

In November 2012, AJR's Ryan Met [[Twitter|tweeted]] a link to a video of their song "[[I'm Ready (AJR song)|I'm Ready]]" to about 80 celebrities, including Australian singer [[Sia Furler]].<ref name=NYPost /> Furler told her manager about the song, and he contacted [[Steve Greenberg (record producer)|Steve Greenberg]], former president of [[Columbia Records]] and current CEO and Founder of [[S-Curve Records]].<ref name=Billboard>{{cite news|last=Hyman|first=Dan|title=NYC Brother Trio AJR 'Ready' for Stardom|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5720546/nyc-brother-trio-ajr-ready-for-stardom|newspaper=Billboard|date=24 September 2013}}</ref> He acts as their co-manager.<ref name=NYPost /> AJR's debut [[single (music)|single]], "[[I'm Ready (AJR song)|I'm Ready]]", which features a sample of [[SpongeBob SquarePants (character)|SpongeBob SquarePants]] repeatedly singing his catchphrase "I'm ready" from the [[SpongeBob SquarePants|eponymous animated series]]' [[Help Wanted (SpongeBob SquarePants)|premiere episode]], was commercially released on August 22, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title="I'm Ready" on iTunes|website=[[iTunes]]|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/im-ready-single/id692863637|access-date=2013-12-12}}</ref> The song was placed in regular rotation on [[Sirius XM Radio]]'s [[Top 20 on 20]] and Hits 1 stations,<ref name=Billboard /> and they performed the song on [[Good Day New York]] and [[VH1]]'s ''Big Morning Buzz''.<ref>{{cite news|title=AJR brothers perform 'I'm Ready' |url=http://www.myfoxny.com/story/23717151/ajr |newspaper=MYFOXNY.COM |date=17 October 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131102041921/http://www.myfoxny.com/story/23717151/ajr |archive-date=2 November 2013 }}</ref> The official music video for "I'm Ready" premiered on [[VEVO]] on October 15, 2013.<ref name=NYPost /><ref>{{cite AV media |people=AJR |year=2013 |title=I'm Ready |medium=Music video |url=http://www.vevo.com/watch/x/x/QMGR31391375 |publisher=VEVO}}</ref>

AJR released their debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''6foot1'' (later renamed ''I'm Ready'' after the band signed to Warner Music Group), on December 20, 2013. They were named [[Clear Channel Communications|Clear Channel]]'s "Artist on the Rise" for the month of October 2013. By 2014, the band was already named [[IHeartRadio]]'s Artist of the Month for Top 40 in January,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.iheart.com/onair/on-the-verge-52262/ajr-pop-artist-of-the-month-11952952/|title=About iHeartRadio On The Verge Artist – Get Articles, Bio & Show Info – iHeartRadio|access-date=30 December 2016}}</ref> and a [[Myspace]] "One to Watch" in February,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://myspace.com/myspace/video/onetwowatch-ajr/109499883|title=AJR Video by ONETWOWATCH on Myspace|date=February 5, 2014|access-date=30 December 2016}}</ref> while "I'm Ready" officially impacted pop radio in April. There are now over 36 million YouTube views for "I'm Ready" as of July 2021. Many thousands of singles sold each week, features in ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' and the ''[[New York Post]]'', while climbing the Top 40 chart. "I'm Ready" hit number one on [[Next Big Sound]]'s chart in May 2014. The band performed the song on ''[[Today Show (NBC)|Today]]'' on July 29, 2014. "I'm Ready" has been certified Platinum, in Canada and Platinum in Australia.

===2014–2015: ''Infinity'' and ''Living Room''===
{{main|Living Room (AJR album)}}
The band's second EP, ''Infinity'', was released on September 23, 2014. It contains 5 tracks, including the lead single "Infinity".<ref name=Frometa>{{cite web|last1=Frometa |first1=RJ |title=AJR move release of debut album "Living Room" to Feb. 2105, "Infinity" EP due 9/23 |url=http://ventsmagazine.com/ajr-move-release-of-debut-album-living-room-to-feb-2015-infinity-ep-due-923/ |website=Vents |access-date=27 October 2014 |archive-date=27 February 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150227081615/http://ventsmagazine.com/ajr-move-release-of-debut-album-living-room-to-feb-2015-infinity-ep-due-923/ }}</ref> They released a lyric video for the single. A majority of the work for the video was done by AJR, including directing and producing.<ref name=Wass>{{cite web|last1=Wass|first1=Mike|title=AJR Return With Nostalgic New Single "Infinity": Watch The DIY Lyric Video (Idolator Premiere)|url=http://www.idolator.com/7534385/ajr-infinity-lyric-video|website=Idolator|date=September 11, 2014|access-date=27 October 2014}}</ref> The EP was released instead of their debut album, ''[[Living Room (AJR album)|Living Room]]'', which was pushed back to a release date of March 3, 2015. The band expressed the delay in their debut album was because they wanted to add some of their newer music to the project.{{Citation needed|date=May 2018}} Around this time, the brothers began to take on distinctive appearances. Adam grew a full beard (later he would grow his hair long), Jack also grew a beard and would wear a bomber hat (which he admits is his mother's),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZM_fdFk93fM|title=Jack from ajr talks about his hat|website=YouTube|publisher=Madison Mcdanel|date=March 12, 2018|access-date=December 31, 2020}}</ref> and Ryan wore glasses, was clean shaven, and had a cowlick hairstyle. On October 23, 2015 they released a bonus track from the album called "Let The Games Begin."

=== 2016–2018: ''The Click'' ===
{{main|The Click (album)}}
The brothers released their third EP, titled ''What Everyone's Thinking'', on September 16, 2016, which features the lead single "[[Weak (AJR song)|Weak]]". The lead single was written by the band in a day, within a few hours, without any idea of the future success the single would have.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/things-indie-pop-band-ajr/story?id=46798580|title=5 things to know about indie-pop band AJR|date=2017-04-14|website=ABC News|language=en|access-date=2019-01-17}}</ref> "Weak" had been certified Platinum in the U.S., Canada, Norway, Netherlands and Belgium, and Gold in Germany, Italy, Sweden, and Australia. It has over 500 million streams on [[Spotify]].

The band released its second studio album, ''[[The Click (album)|The Click]]'', on June 9, 2017, featuring the singles "[[Weak (AJR song)|Weak]]", "Drama" and "[[Sober Up]]", which features frontman [[Rivers Cuomo]] from [[Weezer]], who co-wrote the track. This song reached number one on the [[Mediabase]] Alternative Singles chart in 2018. The band's 2018 tour in support of the album, ''The Click Tour'', featured Hundred Handed, [[Grizfolk]], [[Ocean Park Standoff]], and [[Max Schneider|MAX]] as [[opening act|openers]].<ref>[https://www.etix.com/ticket/p/2551093/ajr-the-click-tourwith-ocean-park-standoffhundred-handed-portland-roseland-theater&cobrand=cascadetickets "AJR - The Click Tour, with Ocean Park Standoff, Hundred Handed,"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207005459/https://www.etix.com/ticket/p/2551093/ajr-the-click-tourwith-ocean-park-standoffhundred-handed-portland-roseland-theater%26cobrand%3Dcascadetickets |date=2018-02-07 }} etix.com. Retrieved February 6, 2018.</ref>

In 2017, AJR released "It's On Us", a single written in collaboration with the [[It's On Us]] campaign, which was launched by [[Barack Obama]] in 2014 to combat sexual assault on college campuses across the United States.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Civic Nation BrandVoice: It's On Us And AJR Partner On Charity Single To Change the Conversation Around Sexual Assault|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/civicnation/2017/03/31/its-on-us-and-ajr-partner-on-charity-single-to-change-the-conversation-around-sexual-assault/|last=Kaplan|first=Rebecca|website=Forbes|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200527234820/https://www.forbes.com/sites/civicnation/2017/03/31/its-on-us-and-ajr-partner-on-charity-single-to-change-the-conversation-around-sexual-assault/#20f9744b131e|archive-date=2020-05-27|access-date=2020-05-28}}</ref>

An extended version of ''The Click'' was released on September 21, 2018. ''The Click (Deluxe Edition)'' included new tracks such as "Role Models", "Normal" and "[[Burn the House Down]]", the latter which the band released in March 2018 as a single and reached number two on the Mediabase Alternative Singles chart. Also included was "Pretender – Acoustic", an acoustic version of "Pretender", which was a collaboration between AJR, EDM artist [[Steve Aoki]] and American rapper [[Lil Yachty]].

===2020–2021: ''OK Orchestra''===
{{Main|OK Orchestra}}
On February 12, 2020, the single "[[Bang! (AJR song)|Bang!]]" was released, with a music video following the next day.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8550845/ajr-bang-music-video|title=AJR Truly Go Out With a 'Bang!' in New Video: Watch|last=Mamo|first=Heran|magazine=Billboard|access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> The single at its release was intended to be part of the deluxe version of their album ''Neotheater'', but was scrapped and made the lead single for ''[[OK Orchestra]]''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Breaking down the production of Three-Thirty, Next Up Forever and Bang!|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CdjqrBN9QHY|language=en|access-date=2021-03-13}}</ref> A follow-up single was released on August 31, 2020, titled "Bummerland". On December 22, 2020, AJR released "My Play". AJR then took to their Twitter account and other social media accounts to announce ''[[OK Orchestra]]'', on December 20, 2020. On February 17, 2021, the band released "[[Way Less Sad]]" with a music video on the same day. ''[[OK Orchestra]]'' was then released on March 26, 2021. The album has 13 tracks, including a track with the [[Blue Man Group]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=OK ORCHESTRA by AJR|url=https://genius.com/albums/Ajr/Ok-orchestra|access-date=2021-03-13|website=Genius|language=en}}</ref> On March 21, 2021, AJR released ''OKO World'', an [[Interactivity|interactive]] game on AJR's website. The new merchandise for the album was released on March 23. Their success with the song "Bang!" lead them to win a Billboard Music Award for "Top Rock Song" In 2021. On August 30, they released a remix of "Record Player" with Daisy The Great in which they added their own verses and production.<ref>{{Citation|title=Daisy The Great x AJR - Record Player (Animated Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oyuIHyOBFsU|language=en|access-date=2021-09-10}}</ref>

On November 24, 2021, the band released a music video for their song “The Good Part” from their 2017 album ''[[The Click (album)|The Click]].'' <ref>{{Citation|title=AJR - The Good Part (Official Video)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hqrWXNMTaKY|language=en|access-date=2021-11-29}}</ref>

==Personal lives==
The brothers have described themselves as "[[cultural Judaism|culturally Jewish]]". Their parents, Gary Metzger and Laurie Marvald are architects. They grew up in [[Bayside, Queens]] until moving to [[Chelsea, Manhattan]] in 2002.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2002/05/05/realestate/habitats-chelsea-moving-to-manhattan-for-the-children-s-sake.html|title=Habitats/Chelsea; Moving to Manhattan, for the Children's Sake|newspaper=The New York Times|date=May 5, 2002|last1=Hall|first1=Trish}}</ref> They attended the [[Professional Children's School]] in Manhattan.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://nypost.com/2013/10/15/could-ajr-be-the-next-jonas-brothers/ |title=Could AJR be the next Jonas Brothers? |work=[[New York Post]] |date=October 15, 2013 |access-date=August 2, 2020 |author=E. Miller, Gregory}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.clevelandjewishnews.com/news/culture/hozier-sheryl-crow-ajr-on-tap-for-laurellive/article_bc376fb6-24c2-11e9-92b0-274825bd6d7f.amp.html/ |title=Hozier, Sheryl Crow, AJR on tap for LaurelLive |work=[[Cleveland Jewish News]] |date=Jan 28, 2019 |access-date=August 2, 2020 |author=Raspe, Becky}}</ref> Jack was a child actor, appearing in ''[[The Pink Panther 2]]'' as well as an episode of ''[[Law and Order: Criminal Intent]]''.<ref>{{cite tweet |last=Met |first=Jack |user=JackAJRbrothers |number=915680688090882048 |date=October 4, 2017 |title=This is Oscar worthy right? https://t.co/xk0cifGOlK |language=en |access-date=January 8, 2021}}</ref> Adam is a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] candidate at the [[University of Birmingham]], while Ryan and Jack study at [[Columbia University]].<ref name=sundays>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/05/17/nyregion/ajr-met-brothers-indie-music.html |title=How the members of the band AJR spend their Sundays |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=May 17, 2019 |access-date=August 3, 2020 |author=La Gorce, Tammy}}</ref>

Ryan has [[synesthesia]] and has said it helps him with his [[record producer|music production]] process, stating in a [[YouTube]] video: <blockquote>"I see visuals when I'm hearing the music, and that very much helps when I'm building … and choosing the right [[drum kit|drums]]."<ref>{{cite AV media |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CdjqrBN9QHY&feature=youtu.be&t=892 |title=Breaking down the production of Three-Thirty, Next Up Forever and Bang! |via=[[YouTube]] |date=20 March 2020 |access-date=27 May 2020}}</ref></blockquote>

Ryan and Jack live in the same apartment and have a [[Bouvier des Flandres|Bouvier]] dog named Shay.<ref name=sundays />
