'''Thomas Sanders'''<!--Do not add the Foley middle name without a reliable source that supports such adding.--> (born April 24, 1989) is an American singer, actor, scriptwriter and [[internet personality]] made famous by [[Vine (service)|Vine]] and [[YouTube]]. He is best known for his YouTube channel and Vine career, which lasted from April 2013 until the social media site was shut down by [[Twitter]] in 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://money.cnn.com/2017/01/17/technology/vine-shuts-down/index.html|title=Twitter officially shuts down Vine|author=Seth Fiegerman|date=January 17, 2017|access-date=October 5, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=What Happened To Vine? Twitter Shut It Down.|url=https://www.thesocialmediahat.com/blog/what-happened-to-vine-twitter-shut-it-down/|date=2016-10-27|website=The Social Media Hat|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Vine is shutting down, so don't forget to export your videos today|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2017/01/17/vine-is-shutting-down-today-so-dont-forget-to-export-your-videos/|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Vine FAQs|url=https://help.twitter.com/en/using-twitter/vine-faqs|website=help.twitter.com|language=en|access-date=2020-05-28}}</ref> After the shutdown of Vine, he continued releasing videos across various social media sites. Sanders creates long videos on YouTube, while simultaneously posting shorter videos in the style of Vine mainly to [[Instagram]], Tumblr and Twitter, but also premiering some on TikTok. His work consists of comedy sketches, pranks, stories, singing and civil rights activism.

Sanders is mainly known for the Vine series ''Narrating People's Lives,'' also known as ''Storytime'', for the prank series “Disney Pranks with friends,” and on YouTube for the series ''Sanders Sides''. He reached over 7.4&nbsp;billion loops and 8.3&nbsp;million followers on Vine,<ref name="sandersVine" /> making his career one of the most successful in this social media platform's short history. As of 2020, his account on TikTok has a following of 7 million followers.<ref>{{cite web |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200225164519/https://www.tiktok.com/@thomassanders|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@thomassanders|url-status=dead |title=Thomas Sanders on TikTok |date=February 25, 2020|archive-date=February 25, 2020}}</ref> On YouTube, he has two channels, "Thomas Sanders" and "Thomas Sanders and Friends". As of November 2020, his main channel has 3.49&nbsp;million subscribers and his second channel has around 376,000 subscribers. Sanders has won two [[Shorty Award]]s<ref name="shorty1" /><ref name="shorty2" /> and one [[Streamy Award]],<ref name="streamy" /> for best Viner and best YouTube comedian, and has been nominated for a [[Teen Choice Award]]<ref name="teenChoice" /> to "Choice Viner" among other recognitions.

==Personal life==
Sanders was born and raised in [[Gainesville, Florida]]<ref name="wuft" /> and currently still lives there. His great-grandfather on his mother's side was born in Ireland.<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7wDJIIn1KOY |title=Untold Family Tales |author=Thomas Sanders |time=00:03:07 |date=July 28, 2017 |access-date=November 17, 2017}}</ref> His great-grandmother was a [[Ziegfeld Follies]] [[Ziegfeld girl|girl]], something Sanders called his "ancestral connection with stage".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/X2qQkVEfYm/ |title=Discovered today that my great-grandmother was a Ziegfeld Follies girl |author=Thomas Sanders |publisher=Instagram |date=April 8, 2013 |access-date=October 22, 2018}}</ref> Sanders' father is a teacher.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2507946/thomas-sanders-vine-interview/ |title=Thomas Sanders shares how being a Vine star has helped him make a difference in the world |author=Stacey Grant |publisher=MTV |date=November 17, 2015 |access-date=August 30, 2018}}</ref> Sanders has three brothers named Patrick, Christian, and Shea. He started singing in public and took his first steps in acting in middle-school at Kanapaha Middle School, located in Gainesville, singing in several choruses and appearing in several school plays.<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W_yzyyHiG4o |title=Gender Roles |author=Thomas Sanders |time=00:04:18 |date=March 20, 2015 |access-date=September 3, 2017}}</ref> In high-school, he started combining acting and singing by doing his first roles in musical plays.<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XKlOcKyS5OM |title=Reacting to Old Musicals |author=Thomas Sanders |time=00:01:13 |date=May 17, 2017 |access-date=August 30, 2018}}</ref> Later, Sanders combined his studies at the [[University of Florida]] with [[Regional theater in the United States|community theater]], joining the Gainesville Community Playhouse from 2006 until 2015. When he graduated with a Bachelor of Science in [[chemical engineering]] in 2011,<ref name="anythingGoes">{{cite web |url=http://gcpgirl.blogspot.com.es/2011/07/anything-goes-cast-profile-thomas.html |title=Anything Goes Cast Profile: Thomas Sanders |author=GCP Girl |date=July 13, 2011 |access-date=January 20, 2018}}</ref><ref name="woods" /> he subsequently combined his daytime job as a manufacturing engineer at a pharmaceutical development company in [[Alachua, Florida|Alachua]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://gcpgirl.blogspot.com/2014/06/into-woods-cast-profile-thomas-sanders.html |title=Into the Woods Cast Profile: Thomas Sanders |author=GCPGirl |date=June 14, 2014 |access-date=August 30, 2018}}</ref> with his night-time job in theater, until his success on Vine prompted him to leave that first job and focus on Vine and theater.<ref name=wuft /> In June 2017, he publicly came out as gay.<ref name="pride">{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GkZaiKnojdU |title=Having Pride |author=Thomas Sanders |time=00:13:21 |date=June 12, 2017 |access-date=September 3, 2017}}</ref>

== Career ==

=== Vine and ''Sanders Shorts''===
Thomas Sanders made his debut on [[Vine (service)|Vine]] as "Foster Dawg", named after his first dog, Foster,<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0gTYDrH4fII |title=Reacting to Old Home Movies |author=Thomas Sanders |time=00:05:42 |date=February 20, 2017 |access-date=November 16, 2017}}</ref> on April 14, 2013. An impression of [[Stewie Griffin]] from ''[[Family Guy]]'' in a drive-thru was his first Vine to go viral, leading to continued success on the app.<ref name="wuft">{{cite web|url=https://www.wuft.org/news/2015/05/22/vine-makes-celebrity-out-of-local-actor/|title=Vine Makes Celebrity Out Of Local Actor|author=Bryan J. Faux|date=May 22, 2015|publisher=WUFT|access-date=November 6, 2017}}</ref> He later rebranded his channel to "Thomas Sanders". His channel reached 1 million followers on Vine in October 2013.<ref name="wuft" /> His biggest success on Vine was the ''Narrating People's Lives'' series, also known as ''Storytime'', where he approached random strangers, comically narrated normal everyday activities they were doing, and showed their reaction.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.aol.com/article/2016/08/11/how-vine-launched-thomas-sanders-comedic-career/21448348/ |title=How Vine launched Thomas Sanders' comedic career |author=Sam Koukoulas |website=aol.com |date=August 11, 2016 |access-date=January 11, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.smash.com/guy-surprises-random-strangers-hilariously-narrates-lives/ |title=Thomas Sanders (Who's That?) Surprises Random Strangers By Hilariously Narrating Their Lives |author=Kevin Holmes |website=smash.com |date=July 13, 2015 |access-date=January 11, 2018}}</ref> Other Vine series he is known for are ''[[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]] Pranks with Friends'', ''[[Pokémon]] Pranks'', ''Misleading Compliments'', and ''Shoutout Sunday'', among others.<ref name="sandersVine">{{cite web |url=https://vine.co/ThomasSanders/|title=Thomas Sanders|publisher=Vine|access-date=January 3, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170618122109/https://vine.co/u/935043086076256256|url-status=dead |archive-date=June 18, 2017}}</ref> By April 2015, Sanders' Vine account had over 5 million followers, which made Thomas Sanders the 17th most followed Viner at the time.<ref name="kidsReactVineRef">{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YtgL1oO2AmY|title=Kids React to Thomas Sanders Vines|author=Fine Brothers|author-link=Fine Brothers|time=00:02:51|date=April 12, 2015|access-date=November 13, 2017}}</ref>

When Twitter announced that it was closing Vine down at the end of 2016, Sanders announced that he would continue making Vines until the app's last day.<ref>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RyWh1YDPapU|title=The End of Vine|author=Thomas Sanders|time=00:02:12|date=October 27, 2016|access-date=November 14, 2017}}</ref> When Vine was shut down on January 17, 2017, Sanders had reached 8.3 million followers.<ref>{{cite web |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170618122109/https://vine.co/u/935043086076256256 |url=https://vine.co/u/935043086076256256|url-status=dead|title=Thomas Sanders on the Vine Archive |publisher=Vine |date=June 18, 2017 |access-date=October 14, 2018|archive-date=June 18, 2017}}</ref> Since that date, his short videos, now known as the ''Sanders Shorts'', are first released on his Instagram, where as of November 2018 he has 2.5 million followers.<ref>{{cite web|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180923065416/https://www.instagram.com/thomassanders/|url=https://www.instagram.com/thomassanders/|url-status=dead|title=Thomas Sanders Instagram on the Internet Archive|author=Thomas Sanders|date=September 23, 2018|access-date=September 24, 2018|archive-date=September 23, 2018}}</ref>

On February 24, 2015, Sanders appeared as a guest on ''[[The View (talk show)|The View]]'' on a segment featuring Vine stars, where he was interviewed about his popularity on Vine,<ref name="wuft" /> and one of his Vines was featured on ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'' in their section ''Vine after Vine''.<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.dailymotion.com/video/x3dst4z |title=The Ellen DGeneres Show: Vine after Vine |time=00:00:36 |year=2014 |access-date=June 19, 2018}}</ref> Sanders has collaborated with Viners such as Vincent Marcus,<ref>{{cite video |url=https://vine.co/v/iPQHJjTX1gU |title=Peter and Stewie Order Room Service |author=Vincent Marcus |date=April 30, 2016 |access-date=January 10, 2018}}</ref> Brandon Calvillo<ref>{{cite video |url=https://vine.co/v/eEwuJ0iaVzj |title=Guilty as Charged |author=Thomas Sanders |publisher=Vine |date=October 14, 2015 |access-date=January 10, 2018}}</ref> and Amymarie Gaertner,<ref>{{cite video |url=https://vine.co/v/OPhDQ1qLYxY |title=Always that one Friend |author=Amymarie Gaertner |publisher=Vine |date=February 12, 2015 |access-date=January 10, 2018}}</ref> and featured cameos and appearances from people such as [[Sean Bean]],<ref>{{cite video |url=https://vine.co/v/M9LWjV2DgId |title=I Think I Said it Better feat. === YouTube ===
Sanders opened his official YouTube account on March 15, 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/user/Thatsthat24/about |title=Thomas Sanders YouTube Channel Information |website=[[YouTube]] |date=March 15, 2009 |access-date=June 10, 2018}}</ref> After amassing a following through Vine, he started publishing more frequently through YouTube. He had his first YouTube collaborations throughout 2015, consisting on pranks, games and challenges. Up until 2014, his subscriber count grew at a lower rate, with around 80,000 subscribers at the end of that year.<ref>{{Cite web|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141219053546/https://www.youtube.com/user/Thatsthat24|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/Thatsthat24|url-status=dead |title=Thomas Sanders Account in the Internet Archive |website=[[YouTube]]|date=December 19, 2014 |access-date=June 10, 2018|archive-date=December 19, 2014}}</ref> From that date, his user count growth started progressively accelerating, having 200,000 subscribers by April 2015<ref name="kidsReactYoutubeRef">{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YtgL1oO2AmY|title=Kids React to Thomas Sanders Vines|author=Fine Brothers|author-link=Fine Brothers|time=00:03:26|date=April 12, 2015|access-date=November 13, 2017}}</ref> and more than 700,000 subscribers at the end of 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151230104803/https://www.youtube.com/user/Thatsthat24|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/Thatsthat24|url-status=dead|title=Thomas Sanders YouTube (December 30, 2015 archived version)|website=[[YouTube]]|date=December 30, 2015|access-date=November 20, 2017|archive-date=December 30, 2015}}</ref>

From the second half of 2016, at the same time his Vine activity got slightly diminished following Twitter's announcement of Vine's forthcoming closedown, Sanders announced he would be focusing on his YouTube content from then on in order to create more diversely formatted content. As of October 2021, his channel has 3.49&nbsp;million subscribers.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/user/Thatsthat24/videos |title=Thomas Sanders account |author=Thomas Sanders |website=[[YouTube]] |access-date=June 3, 2018}}</ref>

On October 29, 2018, he debuted a second YouTube channel called "Thomas Sanders & Friends", which would be dedicated to the unscripted shows he had done in the past in his main channel with the help of his guest friends and the monthly ''Sanders Shorts'' compilations from then on, while the main channel would be dedicated exclusively to scripted series and shows as well as his music videoclips.<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9SbyxYn1F1M |title=Channel Trailer |author=Thomas Sanders |date=October 29, 2018 |access-date=October 29, 2018}}</ref> His second channel has 396,000 subscribers as of August 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Thomas Sanders and Friends - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCU8JJqKH0JCyr8T_THGKSaQ?app=desktop|access-date=2021-08-26|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>

==== ''Sanders Sides'' ====
{{see also|List of Sanders Sides episodes}}
[[File:SandersSidesLogo.png|thumb|400px|Sanders Sides Series logo since September 1, 2017.]]
Since October 19, 2016, Thomas Sanders has run a YouTube web series called ''Sanders Sides'', co-written with Joan S.,<ref name=SSidesDesc /> in which he discusses personal or existential issues with four main characters, collectively known as the titular "Sanders Sides". ''Sanders Sides'' consists of two seasons with a total as of May 2020 of 30 episodes released on a variable periodicity. Season 1 has 17 episodes released from October 19, 2016, to July 19, 2017. Season 2 began on September 1, 2017, and as of May 2020, it has 13 episodes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S9xPOCk91mc&list=PLYA7DZ_sbUzvB1l6KsO5LZV2rrk2u1xl4|title=Sanders Sides Playlist|author=Thomas Sanders|website=[[YouTube]]|access-date=December 16, 2017}}</ref> It is a spin-off from the Vine/''Sanders Short''s series, as Patton, Roman and Logan are inspired by characters that had previously debuted on Vine in 2014, the Dad Guy, the Prince Guy, and the Teacher Guy respectively. The Sanders Sides are usually all portrayed by Sanders himself. ''Sanders Sides'' has featured sometimes appearances of guest stars like [[Lilly Singh]]<ref name="downAnxiety">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rjMxXTCGPu8|title=Taking On Anxiety with Lilly Singh|author=Thomas Sanders|website=[[YouTube]]|date=December 19, 2016|access-date=November 28, 2017}}</ref> or [[Butch Hartman]] who, apart from appearing as himself, created an animated sequence exclusively for ''Sanders Sides'' where Thomas and his Sides became cartoon characters. [[Tara Strong]], who had already appeared in several Vines and ''Sanders Shorts'', also made a voice cameo in Hartman's episode.<ref>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=--rJwJuXuOI|title=Sanders Sides – Becoming a Cartoon|author=Thomas Sanders|time=00:03:48|date=June 9, 2017|access-date=November 9, 2017}}</ref> On November 22, 2019, a companion series titled ''Sanders Asides'' was premiered, featuring the Sides in lighter stories in shorter episodes designed, in Sanders' words, to feature stories not directly linked to the main narrative.<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sX4RhMiPtNI |title=Sanders Asides: Are there Healthy Distractions? |time=00:18:46 |date=November 29, 2019 |access-date=March 12, 2020}}</ref>

The Sanders Sides are "physical-mental projections" of Thomas' mind,<ref>{{cite episode|title=Making Some Changes|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K6ZnRPMDjg8|access-date=December 7, 2017|series=Sanders Sides|author=Thomas Sanders|date=May 23, 2017|season=1|number=14|time=00:04:45|author2=Joan S.}}</ref> and represent different aspects of Thomas'{{refn|group=Note|The Thomas that appears as a character on ''Sanders Sides'' is a fictional character based on the real Thomas Sanders, who talks about him in third person.<ref name=SSidesDesc>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o3CaFMmKA7A |title=Reacting to Sanders Sides Cosplay |author=Thomas Sanders |time=00:01:48 |date=November 4, 2017 |access-date=December 4, 2017}}</ref>}} personality. Thomas usually starts each episode as an ordinary [[Video blog|vlog]] about a certain topic or dilemma. The series is mostly comedic with some elements being more dramatic in nature.

=== Music ===

Though critics often classified Sanders as a [[baritone]],<ref name="heathers3">{{cite web |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/miami/article/BWW-Review-Gen-Ys-Florida-Premiere-of-HEATHERS-THE-MUSICAL-is-So-Very-and-So-Much-More-20150817 |title=Gen Y's Florida Premiere of HEATHERS: THE MUSICAL is 'So Very' and So Much More |author=Matt Tamanini |website=Broadway World |date=August 17, 2015 |access-date=August 27, 2018}}</ref> he considers himself a [[Bass (voice type)|bass]] singer.<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XKlOcKyS5OM |title=Reacting to Old Musicals |author=Thomas Sanders |time=00:03:04 |date=May 17, 2017 |access-date=February 22, 2018}}</ref> He has sung primarily on Vine and YouTube. He has performed songs in a wide variety of genres but has personally showed a preference towards [[jazz]] music, mentioning [[Nat King Cole]] as one of his favorite singers.<ref>{{cite video |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r9z-smcfNDI |title=Ask Thomas Sanders Episode 3 |author=Thomas Sanders |time=00:02:57 |date=February 11, 2015 |access-date=December 30, 2017}}</ref> He has musically collaborated with [[Dodie Clark|dodie]], [[Jon Cozart]], [[Ben J. Pierce]], [[Deedee Magno Hall]][[Adam Pascal]], and [[AJ Rafael]]. He released his first [[Extended play|EP]], titled ''Merry Christmas'', on [[Bandcamp]] on December 21, 2013; the EP consisted of Christmas songs.<ref name="christmas">{{cite web |url=https://thomassanders.bandcamp.com/album/merry-christmas |title=Merry Christmas Album |website=Bandcamp.com |date=December 21, 2013 |access-date=November 10, 2017}}</ref><ref name="christmas2">{{cite web |url=https://vine.co/v/hEjthpUPdzX |title=Thomas Sanders Christmas Ep |author=Thomas Sanders |date=December 23, 2013 |access-date=April 30, 2018}}</ref> In 2016, he released the soundtrack of his ''Ultimate Storytime'' stage musical, recorded alongside his co-stars, Terrence Williams Jr., Nicole Visco, Jay Harper (also known as JayIsJo) and Leo Anderson (also known as Leo the Giant).<ref name="ultimateAlbum">{{cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/ultimate-storytime/id1164730576 |title=Ultimate Storytime album |publisher=iTunes Store |date=October 7, 2016 |access-date=November 10, 2017}}</ref> On July 22, 2017, Sanders released on YouTube a new original song titled "The Things We Used to Share",<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.inquisitr.com/4385832/thomas-sanders-original-song-things-we-used-to-share/|title=YouTube Fave Thomas Sanders Debuts Original Song, 'The Things We Used To Share'|date=July 24, 2017|work=The Inquisitr|access-date=September 9, 2017}}</ref> inspired by real events in Sanders' life.<ref name=share>{{Citation|title=The Things We Used to Share – Single by Thomas Sanders on Apple Music|date=July 27, 2017|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/the-things-we-used-to-share-single/id1264939739|access-date=September 9, 2017}}</ref> It was followed by more singles in 2018 and the announcement of a new EP.<ref>{{cite tweet |user=ThomasSanders |number=1012078050551455744 |title=Me Every Day |date=June 27, 2018 |access-date=July 5, 2018}}</ref>
