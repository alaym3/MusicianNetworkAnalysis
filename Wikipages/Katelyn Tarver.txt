'''Katelyn Tarver''' (born November 2, 1989<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hollywood.com/celebrities/katelyn-tarver-58019152/ |title=Katelyn Tarver |website=Hollywood.com |access-date=January 15, 2017}}{{dead link|date=June 2021}}</ref>) is an American actress and singer-songwriter.<ref>Abrams, Margaret (March 30, 2020). [https://www.standard.co.uk/insider/living/katelyn-tarver-spotify-social-distancing-playlist-a4402086.html "Katelyn Tarver shares her favorite songs to keep you company"]. ''www.standard.co.uk''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> She has independently released four EP's since 2011, most notably ''Tired Eyes'' in 2017.<ref name="billboard.com">[https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8461056/katelyn-tarvers-labels-premiere "Katelyn Tarver Takes On 'Labels' In Empowering New Song: Premiere"]. ''Billboard''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> She is also known for her recurring roles as [[Jo Taylor]] on the [[Nickelodeon]] series ''[[Big Time Rush]]'', Natalie on the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] series ''[[No Ordinary Family]]'', Jesse in [[HBO series]] ''[[Ballers]]'' and Mercedes on [[ABC Family]] ''[[The Secret Life of the American Teenager]]''.<ref name="riffmagazine.com">[https://riffmagazine.com/qa/katelyn-tarver-in-quarantine/ "Q&A: Katelyn Tarver on her mental health, trying new recipes during quarantine"]. April 27, 2020. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> She co-wrote "[[Crazy Stupid Love (song)|Crazy Stupid Love]]" for the British singer [[Cheryl (singer)|Cheryl]] which peaked at #1 on [[UK Singles Chart]] in 2014.<ref>[https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/singles-chart/20140727/7501/ "Official Singles Chart Top 100 | Official Charts Company"]. ''www.officialcharts.com''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref>

==Career==

===Music===
In 2003, Tarver took part in the reality television program ''[[American Juniors]]'', where she was one of the ''Top 10'' finalists, before being eliminated from the competition on August 12, 2003.<ref>Lo Grasso, Sandy, [http://www.starshinemag.com/content.php?article.74.0 "Katelyn Tarver: American Junior to Idol"], ''StarShine Magazine'', November 28, 2005.  {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060718222103/http://www.starshinemag.com/content.php?article.74.0 |date=July 18, 2006 }}</ref>

On June 14, 2011, Tarver independently released her debut EP, ''A Little More Free'', produced by [[Matt Grabe]].<ref name="auto">nylon,. [https://www.nylon.com/articles/playlisted-october-recap-gallery "Katelyn Tarver Serenaded Us At #Playlisted"]. ''Nylon''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> She was [[Honor Society (band)|Honor Society]]'s opening act on their June–July 2011 "Wherever You Are" tour.<ref>[https://patch.com/virginia/vienna/ev--honor-society-action-item-katelyn-tarver "Honor Society + Action Item + Katelyn Tarver + I Love Monsters"]. ''Vienna, VA Patch''. May 9, 2011. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> Tarver also toured alongside the bands [[The Cab]] and [[Parachute (band)|Parachute]] during the summer of 2012.<ref>[https://patch.com/massachusetts/foxborough/ev--the-cab-and-parachute-with-katelyn-tarver "The Cab and Parachute with Katelyn Tarver"]. ''Foxborough, MA Patch''. June 19, 2012. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref>

In 2014, Tarver co-wrote [[Cheryl Cole]]'s 2014 single "[[Crazy Stupid Love (song)|Crazy Stupid Love]]".<ref name="auto"/> Two more singles, "Weekend Millionaires" and "Nobody Like You" were released on the streaming platform [[SoundCloud]] in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/katelyntarver/weekend-millionaires|title=Weekend Millionaires|via=soundcloud.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/katelyntarver/nobody-like-you|title=Nobody LIke You|via=soundcloud.com}}</ref> "Weekend Millionaires" was re-released on [[iTunes]], [[Spotify]], and other digital retailers on July 28, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/weekend-millionaires-single/id1133935425|title="Weekend Millionares - Single by Katelyn Tarver on Apple Music"}}</ref> Another new track, "What Do We Know Now", was released in October,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/what-do-we-know-now-single/id1154935200|title="What Do We Know Now - Single by Katelyn Tarver on Apple Music"}}</ref> followed shortly by the [[Burns (musician)|BURNS]]-produced "Hate To Tell You" the following month.<ref>[http://www.clashmusic.com/news/listen-katelyn-tarver-hate-to-tell-you "Listen: Katelyn Tarver - 'Hate To Tell You'"], ''Clash Music'', November 11, 2016.</ref>

In 2015, Tarver was featured on the [[Lost Kings]] track "You", which was released by [[Spinnin' Records]] as a single off their EP ''The Bad''.<ref>Hartounian, Nanor. [https://www.nylon.com/articles/lost-kings-you-video-premiere "Lost Kings Team Up With Katelyn Tarver On "You""]. ''Nylon''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> In 2016, Tarver released a cover of [[Jeremih]]'s song "Planez".<ref>[https://www.clashmusic.com/news/premiere-katelyn-tarver-planez-live-session "Premiere: Katelyn Tarver - 'Planez' (Live Session)"]. ''Clash Magazine''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref>

Tarver's second EP, ''Tired Eyes'', was released on March 10, 2017.<ref name="billboard.com"/><ref name="auto1">Garafano, Lauren. [https://www.buzzfeed.com/laurengarafano/big-time-rush-then-vs-now "11 Side-By-Sides Of The "Big Time Rush" Cast Then Vs. Now"]. ''BuzzFeed''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> The song, "You Don't Know" from the ''Tired Eyes'', went viral on [[YouTube]] and amassed over 50 million views by the mid-2021.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BF-nZziUCCY "Katelyn Tarver - You Don't Know"]. Retrieved on June 19, 2021 &ndash; via www.youtube.com.</ref> In 2018, Tarver released her third EP, ''Kool Aid''.<ref name="billboard.com"/> The EP received positive reviews from multiple music review websites including ''[[Clash (magazine)|Clash]]''.<ref>[https://www.clashmusic.com/news/katelyn-tarvers-kool-aid-simply-sticks-with-you "Katelyn Tarver's 'Kool Aid' Simply Sticks With You"]. ''Clash Magazine''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref>

In 2019, Tarver released five singles as well as her fourth EP, ''Kool Aid: Sugar Free (Acoustic)''. In 2020, she released nine singles including "Feel Bad". The music video of "Feel Bad" was directed by Se Oh and premiered on March 26, 2020, with [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']].<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/9342571/katelyn-tarver-feel-bad-video/ "'Songland' Winner Katelyn Tarver Confronts the Messy in 'Feel Bad' Video: Exclusive"]. ''Billboard''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> Later that year, Tarver co-wrote Jake Scott's song "Like No One Does".<ref name="auto2">choover@wacotrib.com, CARL HOOVER. [https://wacotrib.com/entertainment/accesswaco/songwriters-jake-scott-and-katelyn-tarver-meet-new-audience-at-common-grounds/article_b1c45a11-c8ec-5787-83c1-5904b6e08273.html "Songwriters Jake Scott and Katelyn Tarver meet new audience at Common Grounds"]. ''WacoTrib.com''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> The single was released on April 7, 2020.<ref>[https://rollingstoneindia.com/premiere-jake-scott-meet-my-shadow-video/ "Premiere: American Singer-Songwriter Jake Scott Roams Hawaii for Openhearted ‘Meet My Shadow’ Video -"]. May 21, 2021. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref>

In 2021, Tarver collaborated with [[Will Anderson (singer)|Will Anderson]] and launched a side-project called REAVES. The debut EP, The First EP, of the band was released in June 4, 2021.<ref name="auto1"/>

Tarver was the winning songwriter on the [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]] episode of ''[[Songland]]'', with her song "Young" being featured in a [[Jeep]] commercial, in which Tarver also starred.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/dalebuss/2019/08/21/jeeps-hour-on-songland-and-90-second-commercial-seek-to-elevate-brand-placement/#7a3930887779|title=Jeep's Hour on NBC's 'Songland,' And 90-Second Commercial, Elevate Brand Placement|magazine=[[Forbes]]|last=Buss|first=Dale|date=August 21, 2019|access-date=November 6, 2019}}</ref><ref>Fern, Celia; ez, (August 22, 2019). [https://www.oprahdaily.com/entertainment/tv-movies/a28773617/songland-katelyn-tarver-old-dominion-young-facts/ "Songland: Why Katelyn Tarver Might Look so Familiar"]. ''Oprah Daily''. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref>

===Acting===
On January 29, 2010, she made her acting debut on ''[[Big Time Rush]]'' as Jo Taylor, a girl the boys admire. Her debut episode was "Big Time Love Song" which aired on February 5, 2010. She developed feelings for Kendall Knight ([[Kendall Schmidt]]) and they started dating. But the world tour changed everything: Jo became a television star and Kendall became a pop star. On "Big Time Break-Up" which aired on June 25, 2011, she landed a role in a movie in [[New Zealand]] which would keep her and Kendall apart for 3 years. She returned in season 3's episode "Big Time Surprise", which aired on September 22, 2012.<ref name="riffmagazine.com"/>

From 2010 to 2011, Tarver appeared as Natalie Poston in 5 episodes of the [[ABC Television Network|ABC]] series ''[[No Ordinary Family]]''.<ref>[https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/quarantine-diaries-la-pop-luminary-katelyn-tarver-quarantining-siblings-n-out-burger "Quarantine Diaries: L.A. Pop Singer Katelyn Tarver"]. ''GRAMMY.com''. March 24, 2020. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref> In 2012, she appeared as Mercedes in [[ABC Family]]'s series, ''[[The Secret Life of the American Teenager]]''.<ref name="auto2"/>

In 2018, Tarver appeared as Jesse in 8 episodes of [[HBO series]], ''[[Ballers]]''. She was cast as Cydney Walker in the [[ABC Signature]] television pilot, ''None of the Above'', in 2021.<ref>Porter, Rick; Porter, Rick (November 16, 2020). [https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-news/gregg-wayans-to-star-in-freeform-comedy-from-black-ish-veteran-4093037/ "Gregg Wayans to Star in Freeform Comedy From ‘Black-ish’ Veteran"]. Retrieved on June 19, 2021.</ref>

==Personal life==
Tarver married David Blaise on July 19, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.contactmusic.com/article/katelyn-tarver-david-blaise-married-lucy-hale-alexa-vega_4303892|title=Katelyn Tarver Ties The Knot With Fiance David Blaise|date=July 28, 2014|publisher=[[Contactmusic.com]]|access-date=August 7, 2014}}</ref>
