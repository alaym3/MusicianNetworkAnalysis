'''The Snuts''' are a Scottish [[indie rock]] band formed in 2015, originating from [[West Lothian]], Scotland. They have performed across the United Kingdom and Ireland.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Taysom|first=Joe|last2=|date=12 June 2020|title=The Snuts share the thumping new single 'Elephants'|url=https://faroutmagazine.co.uk/the-snuts-new-single-elephants/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Far Out Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":3" /><ref name=":4" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Hayes|first=Stuart|date=26 October 2019|title=The Snuts Live in Dublin|url=http://www.vanguard-online.co.uk/the-snuts-the-grand-social-dublin/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Vanguard-Online}}</ref> The band consists of Jack Cochrane (vocals and guitar), Joe McGillveray (guitar), Callum '29' Wilson (bass) and Jordan 'Joko' Mackay (drums) and was formed when the members were in school.

== History ==
Wilson, McGillveray and McKay went to primary school with each other and they met Cochrane at secondary school. 

Wilson has said "we’ve only ever really played music together so I can’t really imagine it any other way".<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=11 April 2019|title=Getting to know... The Snuts|url=https://www.readdork.com/features/the-snuts-interview-apr19|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Dork|language=en}}</ref> They have been influenced by the likes of [[the Libertines]] and [[Arctic Monkeys]], and Scottish [[Ned (Scottish)|Ned culture]] growing up.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Lennon|first=Holly|date=1 November 2018|title=The Snuts interview: 'Our biggest inspiration is not to sound exactly like everybody else'|url=https://www.glasgowtimes.co.uk/entertainment/17195172.snuts-interview-our-biggest-inspiration-not-sound-exactly-like-everybody-else/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Glasgow Times|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The Snuts &#124; Fred Perry UK|url=https://www.fredperry.com/subculture/articles/the-snuts|website=www.fredperry.com}}</ref> They have also said that "one of our biggest inspirations is not to sound exactly like everybody else".<ref name=":0" /> Before going full time as a band, Cochrane was a [[joiner]], Wilson was a [[slater]]/[[roofer]], Mackay was a mechanic and McGillveray got qualifications to be a [[Stonemasonry|stonemason]].<ref>{{cite AV media|date=15 April 2020|title=The Snuts interview – Jack Cochrane and Callum Wilson @ESNS|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CNTW7A1SWJU&feature=youtu.be|publisher=FaceCulture|website=YouTube|access-date=14 October 2020}}</ref>

The band's debut single, the demo "Glasgow" was described as a 'stunning track with heaps of melody' and quickly lead to an ever since growing fanbase.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=4 January 2018|title=EP Review: The Snuts – The Matador EP|url=https://www.indiecentralmusic.com/ep-review-the-snuts-the-matador-ep/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=IndieCentralMusic|language=en-US}}</ref> After releasing several other demo singles in 2016, the Snuts released the self-produced [[Extended play|EP]] ''The Matador'' in June 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=4 January 2018|title=EP Review: The Snuts – The Matador EP|url=https://www.indiecentralmusic.com/ep-review-the-snuts-the-matador-ep/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=IndieCentralMusic|language=en-US}}</ref>

The Snuts released their first 'proper' single, "Seasons" in June 2018, described as 'conventional in a much-needed and refreshing way', a 'total throwback to the last renaissance of alternative music' and 'impeccably pieced together, designed to detonate on impact'.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cushnie|first=Arissa|date=20 July 2018|title=The Snuts' "Seasons" is an Electrifying Throwback|url=https://twostorymelody.com/snuts-seasons-electrifying-throwback/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=twostorymelody.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Murray|first=Robin|date=22 June 2018|title=The Snuts Underline Their Potential On 'Seasons'|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/the-snuts-underline-their-potential-on-seasons|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> The following month, they then played at Scotland's [[TRNSMT]] Festival at the King Tut's Stage.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Trendell|first=Andrew|date=6 March 2018|title=Huge final TRNSMT Festival 2018 headliner and loads more names announced &#124; NME|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/final-trnsmt-festival-2018-headliners-loads-names-announced-tickets-2255736|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=[[NME]]}}</ref> In September, "Manhattan Project" was released, produced by [[Dave McCracken]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=7 September 2018|title=The Snuts – Manhattan Project|url=https://inflyteapp.com/r/l8e656|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=9 October 2020|website=inflyteapp.com}}</ref> In November 2018, the band signed with [[Parlophone]] Records.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=30 November 2018|title=The Snuts sign to Parlophone Records|url=http://vitalmusicgroup.com/the-snuts-sign-to-parlophone-records/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Vital Music|language=en-US}}</ref> In March 2019, the band made their US debut at the [[South by Southwest|South By South West]] festival in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], Texas,<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=23 December 2018|title=Music: The Snuts|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/arts_ents/17310868.music-snuts/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=[[The Herald (Glasgow)|The Herald]]|language=en}}</ref> and their first single under Parlophone, "All Your Friends", was released in May. It was produced by [[Inflo]] and was the first single from their second EP ''Mixtape''. It was described as having "an irresistibly catchy raw edge and a massive chorus" and Cochrane explained that it was a "new twist on a familiar social stigma around the drug epidemic that nobody seems to want to talk about".<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=LISTEN: The Snuts share new single ‘All Your Friends’ {{!}} Gigslutz|url=http://www.gigslutz.co.uk/listen-the-snuts-share-new-single-all-your-friends/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Gigslutz|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=24 May 2019|title=The Snuts Release New Single ‘All Your Friends’ Support Lewis Capaldi in November|url=https://www.mbcpr.com/the-snuts-release-new-single-all-your-friends-support-lewis-capaldi-in-november/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=MBC|language=en-GB}}</ref>

In July 2019 they performed again at TRNSMT, this time on the Main Stage;<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=11 July 2019|title=TRNSMT Festival 2019: Headliners, line-up, stage times & more|url=https://www.radiox.co.uk/festivals/trnsmt/2019-headliners-line-up-stage-times-rumours-info/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=[[Radio X (United Kingdom)|Radio X]]|language=en}}</ref> in August they performed at the [[Reading and Leeds Festivals]] and also at [[Victorious Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=LIVE REVIEW: Reading And Leeds Festival 2019 {{!}} O2 Academy Birmingham|url=https://academymusicgroup.com/o2academybirmingham/blogs/live-review-reading-and-leeds-festival-2019|access-date=8 October 2020|website=academymusicgroup.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=The Snuts Setlist at Victorious Festival 2019|url=https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/the-snuts/2019/southsea-common-portsmouth-england-3b9f0470.html|access-date=8 October 2020|website=setlist.fm|language=en}}</ref> The band's next single "Maybe California" was released in September 2019 and was produced by [[Rich Costey]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Murray|first=Robin|date=6 September 2019|title=The Snuts Share Surging New Single 'Maybe California'|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/the-snuts-share-surging-new-single-maybe-california|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Clash Magazine|language=en}}</ref> "Juan Belmonte", named after [[Juan Belmonte|the Spanish bullfighter]], and also produced by Costey, was released in October.<ref>{{Cite web|date=20 October 2019|title=The Snuts announce new single 'Juan Belmonte'|url=https://www.totalntertainment.com/music/the-snuts-announce-new-single-juan-belmonte/|access-date=8 October 2020|website=TotalNtertainment|language=en-GB}}</ref> In January 2020, the Snuts released a cover of [[Bruno Mars]]' "[[When I Was Your Man]]" from sessions at the Firepit Recording Studios in London. Two further singles, "Fatboy Slim" (in "homage of [[Fatboy Slim]]'s notorious set at [[Brighton]] beach" in 2002)<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pearson|first=Alex|date=21 February 2020|title=The Snuts drop floor-stomping new hit 'Fatboy Slim'|url=https://www.indiecentralmusic.com/the-snuts-drop-floor-stomping-new-hit-fatboy-slim/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=IndieCentralMusic|language=en-US}}</ref> and "Coffee & Cigarettes" were released before March, whereupon the EP ''Mixtape'' was released. The EP consists of 5 songs which showcase different influences and 4 interludes, which are [[Outtake|studio outtakes]], giving a kind of informal feel to the EP.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hill|first=Oliver|date=24 March 2020|title=The Snuts 'Mixtape EP' Review|url=https://www.liverpoolguildstudentmedia.co.uk/2020/03/24/the-snuts-mixtape-ep-review/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Liverpool Guild Student Media|language=en-GB}}</ref> The EP peaked at number 14 on the [[UK Albums Chart]] and also topped the [[Scottish Albums Chart]], as well as the [[Official Vinyl Albums Chart|UK Vinyl Albums Chart]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=SNUTS {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/56289/snuts/|access-date=8 October 2020|website=www.officialcharts.com}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Official Scottish Albums Chart Top 100 {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/scottish-albums-chart/20200320/40/|access-date=8 October 2020|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Official Vinyl Albums Chart Top 40 {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/vinyl-albums-chart/20200320/9/|access-date=8 October 2020|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref> Following the success of ''Mixtape'', the Snuts quickly released their next single, a cover of [[the Lovin' Spoonful]]'s "[[Summer in the City (The Lovin' Spoonful song)|Summer in the City]]". Like their previous cover song, this was a Firepit session.<ref>{{Cite web|date=25 March 2020|title=The Snuts release 'Summer In The City'|url=https://www.totalntertainment.com/music/the-snuts-release-summer-in-the-city/|access-date=8 October 2020|website=TotalNtertainment|language=en-GB}}</ref> It was also featured in an advert for [[Strongbow (cider)|Strongbow cider]] in August 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=3 August 2020|title=The Snuts Set To Feature in New TV Ad Covering The Lovin’|url=https://originalrock.net/2020/08/03/the-snuts-set-to-feature-in-new-tv-ad-covering-the-lovin/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=OriginalRock.net|language=en-US}}</ref>

The following single "Elephants" was released in June 2020 and was produced by [[Tony Hoffer]]. It topped the iTunes Singles Chart on the day of its release.<ref>{{Cite web|title=iTunesCharts.net: UK Songs Thursday, 11th June 2020|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/uk/charts/songs/2020/06/11|access-date=8 October 2020|website=www.itunescharts.net}}</ref> Cochrane described it as "another twist on our ever-growing diverse catalogue".<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=11 June 2020|title=The Snuts Return With New Single 'Elephants'|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/The-Snuts-Return-With-New-Single-Elephants-20200611|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=Broadway World|language=en}}</ref> In August, the band supported the Libertines at a [[Social distancing|socially-distanced]] performance at the [[Virgin Money Unity Arena]] in [[Newcastle upon Tyne|Newcastle]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Watson|first=Benedict|date=19 September 2020|title=Interview: Jack Cochrane of The Snuts on bouncing back and moving forward|url=https://www.themicmagazine.co.uk/post/interview-jack-cochrane-of-the-snuts-on-bouncing-back-and-moving-forward|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=The Mic|language=en}}</ref> "That's All It Is" was released in September and features on the soundtrack to the video game ''[[FIFA 21]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Whiffen|first=Marc|date=27 September 2020|title=Released Today: The Snuts share bonus tune 'That's All It Is'|url=https://theindiemasterplan.co.uk/released-today-the-snuts-share-bonus-tune-thats-all-it-is|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=The Indie Masterplan|language=en-GB}}</ref> A week later, the Snuts released "Always", with Cochrane saying "the song depicts the message of falling in love with someone who in turn allows you to love yourself".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Whiffen|first=Marc|date=3 October 2020|title=The Snuts release their second tune in a week with 'Always'|url=https://theindiemasterplan.co.uk/the-snuts-release-their-second-tune-in-a-week-with-always|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=8 October 2020|website=The Indie Masterplan|language=en-GB}}</ref> "Always" was the final number one single on the [[Scottish Singles Chart]] before it was discontinued in December 2020.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|title=Official Scottish Singles Sales Chart Top 100 {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/charts/scottish-singles-chart/|access-date=27 December 2020|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref>

In December, the band announced the release of their debut album ''[[W.L. (album)|W.L.]]'' on 19 March 2021, later pushed back to 2 April. It includes 10 previously released singles, three of which featured on the EP ''Mixtape'', and three previously unreleased songs. The deluxe edition also includes four previously unreleased bonus tracks.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Reilly|first=Nick|date=3 December 2020|title=The Snuts announce debut album 'W.L.' and massive UK tour for 2021|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/the-snuts-announce-debut-album-w-l-and-massive-uk-tour-for-2021-2830913|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=7 December 2020|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref> In February 2021, a new single, "Somebody Loves You" was released in support of the [[Scottish Refugee Council]], with the band donating the budget for the music video to the charity.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Snuts support refugees with their new single 'Somebody Loves You' {{!}} Gigwise|url=https://www.gigwise.com/news/3395863/the-snuts-support-refugees-with-their-new-single-somebody-loves-you|access-date=2021-02-05|website=www.gigwise.com}}</ref> Before its release, the Snuts performed the album at [[Stirling Castle]] and it was later released as a live album.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Fulton|first=Rick|date=2021-03-25|title=Snuts are "kings of Scotland" ahead of their Stirling Castle gig|url=https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/newsletter/newsletter-entertainment/snuts-kings-scotland-ahead-stirling-23796502|access-date=2021-04-07|website=Daily Record|language=en}}</ref> ''W.L.'' debuted at number one on the UK Albums Chart, making the Snuts the first Scottish band to do so with their debut album since [[The View (band)|The View]] in 2007.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Snuts win close race to score Number 1 debut album|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-snuts-wl-wins-close-race-to-become-first-number-1-debut-album-by-a-scottish-band-since-2007__32868/|access-date=2021-04-09|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref>
