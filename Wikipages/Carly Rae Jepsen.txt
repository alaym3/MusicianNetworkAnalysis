'''Carly Rae Jepsen''' (born November 21, 1985)<ref>{{cite web|title=Carly Rae Jepsen Biography|url=http://www.biography.com/people/carly-rae-jepsen-20858431#early-life|publisher=Biography.com|access-date=November 20, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151120100556/http://www.biography.com/people/carly-rae-jepsen-20858431#early-life|archive-date=November 20, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=AllMusic>{{cite web| url= http://www.allmusic.com/artist/carly-rae-jepsen-mn0002089077/biography |title=Carly Rae Jepsen Biography |first= James Christopher|last= Monger |website=AllMusic |access-date=September 30, 2015}}</ref> is a Canadian singer, songwriter, and actress. Born and raised in [[Mission, British Columbia]], Jepsen performed several lead roles in her high school's musical productions and pursued musical theatre at the Canadian College of Performing Arts in [[Victoria, British Columbia]]. After completing her studies, she relocated to [[Vancouver]] and later competed on the [[Canadian Idol (season 5)|fifth season]] of ''[[Canadian Idol]]'' in 2007, placing third. In 2008, Jepsen released her [[folk music|folk]]-influenced debut studio album, ''[[Tug of War (Carly Rae Jepsen album)|Tug of War]]'', in Canada.

Jepsen's breakthrough came in 2012, when her single "[[Call Me Maybe]]"<ref>{{Citation|title=Call me maybe by Carly Rae Jepsen|url=https://www.bitchute.com/video/Q4raQDoaw2Nl/|language=en|access-date=2021-08-13}}</ref> achieved significant mainstream popularity; the song was the best-selling single of that year, reaching number one in 18 countries.<ref name="CMM"/> As a result, she was signed to a joint worldwide record deal with [[School Boy Records]] and [[Interscope Records]]. Jepsen's second studio album, ''[[Kiss (Carly Rae Jepsen album)|Kiss]]'', was released later that year. It marked a greater shift into mainstream pop music and saw fair commercial success, reaching the top ten in Canada and the United States. In 2014, Jepsen made her [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] stage debut as the titular character in ''[[Cinderella (2013 Broadway production)|Cinderella]]''. The following year, she released her third studio album, ''[[Emotion (Carly Rae Jepsen album)|Emotion]]''. It is noted for its influence from [[1980s in music|1980s music]] as well as blending [[dance-pop]] and [[synth-pop]] with indie sensibilities. While less commercially successful than ''Kiss'', it saw the success of its lead single, "[[I Really Like You]]", and received critical acclaim. In 2016 Jepsen performed in the television special ''[[Grease: Live]]'' and lent her voice to the animated film ''[[Ballerina (2016 film)|Ballerina]]''. Her fourth studio album, ''[[Dedicated (Carly Rae Jepsen album)|Dedicated]]'', was released on May 17, 2019, along with a follow-up titled ''[[Dedicated Side B]]'' was distributed a year later.

Jepsen has received multiple accolades, including three [[Juno Awards]], a [[Billboard Music Award|''Billboard'' Music Award]], and an [[Allan Slaight Award]], in addition to various nominations for [[Grammy Awards]], [[MTV Video Music Awards]], [[Polaris Music Prize]] and [[People's Choice Awards]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/474480/carly-rae-jepsen-named-billboards-rising-star-of-2012|title=Carly Rae Jepsen Named Billboard's 'Rising Star' of 2012|date=October 25, 2012|work=Billboard|access-date=December 29, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.canadaswalkoffame.com/content/2013-allan-slaight-award-recipient-carly-rae-jepsen|title=2013 Allan Slaight Award Recipient Carly Rae Jepsen|publisher=canadaswalkoffame.com|access-date=December 29, 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131230235057/http://www.canadaswalkoffame.com/content/2013-allan-slaight-award-recipient-carly-rae-jepsen|archive-date=December 30, 2013}}</ref> {{as of|2015|May}}, Jepsen has sold over 25 million records worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tv.mtvema.com/_/carly-rae-jepsen|title=MTVA EMA Carly Rae Jepsen|publisher=MTV|access-date=September 15, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://utv.ie/Entertainment/2015/05/29/Carly-Rae-Jepsens-career-start-was-a-beautiful-fire-38152|title=Carly Rae Jepsen's career start was 'a beautiful fire'|publisher=utv.ie|access-date=September 15, 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150608202801/http://utv.ie/Entertainment/2015/05/29/Carly-Rae-Jepsens-career-start-was-a-beautiful-fire-38152|archive-date=June 8, 2015}}</ref>

==Life and career==
===1985−2006: Early life and career beginnings===
Jepsen was born in [[Mission, British Columbia]], to Alexandra Lanzarotta and Larry Jepsen, the second of their three children.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.pressreader.com/canada/vancouver-sun/20070905/281775624774817 |title=Contest gives singer hope |last=Zacharias |first=Yvonne |date=September 5, 2007 |work=Vancouver Sun |access-date=October 29, 2018}}</ref> She is of Danish, English and Scottish descent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://underholdning.tv2.dk/underholdning/2015-05-28-superstjernen-afsloerer-ja-jeg-har-danske-aner|title=Superstjernen afslører: Ja, jeg har danske aner|trans-title=Superstar reveals: Yes, I have Danish ancestors|work=Underholdning|language=da|date=May 28, 2015}}</ref> Jepsen has an older brother, Colin, and a younger sister, Katie. She attended Heritage Park Secondary School, and she pursued her passion for musical theatre by appearing in student productions of ''[[Annie (musical)|Annie]]'', ''[[Grease (musical)|Grease]]'', and ''[[The Wiz]]'', playing the respective lead roles of Annie, Sandy Olsson and [[Dorothy Gale]].<ref name="TheStar">{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/stage/2014/02/21/carly_rae_jepsen_the_big_interview.html |title=Carly Rae Jepsen: The Big Interview |work=Toronto Star |first= Richard |last= Ouzounian |date=February 21, 2014 |access-date= February 15, 2017}}</ref><ref name=buzzfeed>{{Cite news|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/reggieugwu/what-makes-carly-rae-jepsen-a-pop-star|title=What Makes Carly Rae Jepsen A Pop Star? |last=Ugwu|first=Reggie|date=December 15, 2015|work=BuzzFeed News|access-date=July 31, 2017 |language=en}}</ref> Her parents and stepparents were teachers, so Jepsen considered a career in music instruction as a second option.<ref name=buzzfeed /><ref name=CRJVEVO>{{Citation|last=CarlyRaeJepsenVEVO|title=Carly Rae Jepsen – #VevoCertified, Pt. 2: Carly on the Start|date=August 2, 2013|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HVjwevPdpSs|via=YouTube |access-date=July 31, 2017}}</ref> She applied to music-related programs, including those at [[Capilano University]] and the [[University of British Columbia]]. Her high school drama teacher, Beverly Holmes, persuaded her to audition for the Canadian College of Performing Arts in [[Victoria, British Columbia]]. She was one of 25 female students to gain admission into its year-long program: "It was very clear to me after that school, as much fun as I had, I really did want to pursue more of a purely musical career rather than acting and all the dancing."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.timescolonist.com/entertainment/before-call-me-maybe-carly-rae-jepsen-studied-at-victoria-s-canadian-college-of-performing-arts-1.22037|title=Before Call Me Maybe, Carly Rae Jepsen studied at Victoria's Canadian College of Performing Arts|last=Devlin|first=Mike|date=October 17, 2012|work=The Times Colonist|access-date=July 31, 2017}}</ref><ref name=CRJVEVO />

After graduation, Jepsen relocated to Vancouver's west side, and was fortunate to hold several jobs.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://vancouversun.com/entertainment/local-arts/carly-rae-jepsen-interview-weekend-extra|title=Carly Rae Jepsen wears her heart on her sleeve with Emotion |last=Conner|first=Shawn|date=May 13, 2016|work=The Vancouver Sun|access-date=July 31, 2017 |language=en-US}}</ref><ref name="TheStar" /> She worked at Trees Organic Coffee&nbsp;as a [[barista]] and pastry chef assistant, starting its [[open mic]] night.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bonappetit.com/people/article/carly-rae-jepsen-s-morning-routine|title=Carly Rae Jepsen's Morning Routine|last=Walsh|first=Danielle|date=November 27, 2012|work=Bon Appetit |access-date=July 30, 2017|language=en}}</ref> Jepsen describes this as "the happiest time in her life"; she slept on a pull-out sofa, wrote songs in her downtime with a guitar her parents gave her, and "performed multiple nights a week at musician-friendly holes in the wall".<ref name=buzzfeed /><ref name="TheStar" /> Bartending at the Media Club, Jepsen once requested an extra-late shift to see [[Sia (musician)|Sia]], prior to their collaboration.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nexusradio.fm/news/39268-2/|title=Carly Rae Jepsen Interview |last=Nunn|first=Jerry|date=February 19, 2016|work=Nexus Radio|access-date=July 31, 2017 |language=en-US}}</ref>

===2007–2010: ''Canadian Idol'' and ''Tug of War''===
[[File:Carly Rae Jepsen 2010 1.jpg|thumb|upright|right|160px|Jepsen performing on [[Canada Day]] in 2010]]
While assembling a swing band,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.maplemusic.com/artists/cra/bio.asp|title=Carly Rae Jepsen Biography|website=MapleMusic|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170731105810/http://www.maplemusic.com/artists/cra/bio.asp |archive-date=July 31, 2017|access-date=July 31, 2017}}</ref> Jepsen was convinced to audition for ''[[Canadian Idol]]'' by her drama teacher.<ref name="TheStar" /> She performed her original song "Sweet Talker"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V458He7UYSM |title=Carly Rae Jepsen-audition |via=YouTube |date=October 7, 2007 |access-date=August 24, 2015}}</ref> and finished in third place, which she considered the best possible outcome in retrospect: "It was like all the exposure without the devilish contract at the end."<ref name=buzzfeed /> Her [[demo recording]] attracted the attention of music manager Jonathan Simkin, who signed Jepsen to a management deal with [[604 Records]] that year.<ref name="BillboardBiz">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1092852/call-me-maybe-tops-the-hot-100|title='Call Me Maybe' Tops the Hot 100|work=Billboard|access-date=April 2, 2017|archive-date=June 18, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190618181456/https://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1092852/call-me-maybe-tops-the-hot-100|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===2011–2014: Mainstream success with ''Curiosity'' and ''Kiss''===

===Musical style===
Jepsen is classified as a [[soprano]].<ref>{{cite news| url = https://www.theverge.com/2012/9/19/3353434/carly-rae-jepsen-call-me-maybe-idiots-guide-to-appreciating| title = The Complete Idiot's Guide To Appreciating Carly Rae Jepsen For Dummies Maybe| work=[[The Verge]]| date=September 19, 2012| access-date=May 6, 2018| first=Trent| last=Wolbe}}</ref><ref name="Carly">{{cite news| url = http://www.laweekly.com/music/call-me-meh-by-carly-rae-jepsen-still-cant-escape-her-biggest-hit-6647712| title = Call Me "Meh"-by: Carly Rae Jepsen Still Can't Escape Her Biggest Hit| work=[[LA Weekly]]| date=February 26, 2016| access-date=May 6, 2018| first=Paul T.| last=Bradley}}</ref><ref name="jepsen">{{cite news| url = http://www.slate.com/articles/arts/music_box/2012/10/carly_rae_jepsen_s_kiss_and_p_nk_s_the_truth_about_love_reviewed.html| title = Call Me Melancholy| work=[[Slate (magazine)|Slate]]| date=October 19, 2012| access-date=May 6, 2018| first=Maura| last=Johnston}}</ref> Paul Bradley of ''[[LA Weekly]]'' describes Jepsen's voice as "hushed" and "flawless"<ref name="Carly"/> while Maura Johnston of ''[[Slate (magazine)|Slate Magazine]]'' characterizes it as "airy yet precise".<ref name="jepsen"/> She says she shares her parents' interest in [[folk music]] as a result of her upbringing, naming artists such as [[Leonard Cohen]], [[Bruce Springsteen]], [[James Taylor]], and [[Van Morrison]] as inspirations for her debut album, ''Tug of War'' (2008).<ref>{{cite news|last=Macsai |first=Dan |url=http://entertainment.time.com/2012/05/18/carly-rae-jepsen-on-gay-rights-call-me-maybe-and-singing-with-dreamy-john-mayer/ |title=Q&A with 'Call Me Maybe' Singer Carly Rae Jepsen &#124; ===Impact===
{{see also|List of awards and nominations received by Carly Rae Jepsen}}
Jepsen has been called the "[[Honorific nicknames in popular music#J|Queen of Pop]]" by multiple media sources.<ref name="NPRCRJ">{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/10/05/646424840/carly-rae-jepsen-is-the-21st-centurys-queen-of-a-million-kingdoms|title=Carly Rae Jepsen Is The 21st Century's Queen Of A Million Kingdoms|accessdate=May 27, 2021|website=NPR}}</ref><ref name="CRJDTK">{{Cite web|url=https://dresstokillmagazine.com/carly-rae-jepsen-from-pop-princess-to-full-blown-queen/|title=FROM POP PRINCESS TO FULL-BLOWN QUEEN|accessdate=May 27, 2021|website=Dress to Kill}}</ref><ref name="TIMECRJ">{{Cite magazine|url=https://time.com/5591169/carly-rae-jepsen-dedicated/|title=How Carly Rae Jepsen Became Pop's Most Beloved Underdog|accessdate=May 27, 2021|magazine=Time}}</ref><ref name="CRJPQOOT">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-48265955|accessdate=May 27, 2021|website=BBC|title=Carly Rae Jepsen: Pop's queen of over-thinking}}</ref> Lyndsey McKenna, writer for ''[[NPR]]'' wrote that "Jepsen's career is instructive. The internet's reception of Jepsen's music has given her a longevity and a lifespan beyond its limited, though influential, time on the charts".<ref name="NPRCRJ"/> McKenna went on to say that she "doesn't have to be queen of the charts [...] Instead, she can have her own world: technicolored-hued, insular but not exclusionary, where absurdity and enthusiasm are equally cherished".<ref name="NPRCRJ"/>

In 2019, ''[[Insider Inc.|Insider]]'' writer, Libby Torres, called Jepsen an "Artist of the Decade" saying that her "impact extends far beyond music, as she's often celebrated as a queer icon".<ref name="InsiderCRJ">{{Cite web|url=https://www.insider.com/carly-rae-jepsen-artist-of-the-decade-2010s-2019-11|title=Carly Rae Jepsen is the artist of the decade|accessdate=May 27, 2021|website=Insider}}</ref> She also stated that Jepsen's "subversively catchy lyrics, slick musical arrangements, and pop experimentation have made her a true star".<ref name="InsiderCRJ"/> ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' writer, Raisa Bruner, stated that Jepsen had a "cult of popularity" and mentioned the "ongoing compliment-spam that adorns her social media profiles, with each fan proclaiming her queen of something".<ref name="TIMECRJ"/> Marie-Ève Venne, writer for ''Dress to Kill'' stated that recently, Jepsen has "embraced a more mature pop queen persona, showcasing a modern and sophisticated sound".<ref name="CRJDTK"/>

==Activism==
Jepsen was scheduled to perform at the [[Boy Scouts of America]] [[2013 National Scout Jamboree]], along with the band [[Train (band)|Train]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://newsfeed.time.com/2013/03/05/carly-rae-jepsen-to-boy-scouts-dont-call-me-until-you-accept-gays/ |title=Carly Rae Jepsen and Train Refuse to Perform at Boy Scout Jamboree |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |date=March 5, 2013 |access-date=March 15, 2013}}</ref> but in March 2013, both cited the [[Boy Scouts of America membership controversies|BSA policy on homosexuals]] as a barrier to their performance.<ref>{{cite news| url= http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/03/04/train-boy-scouts-jamboree-ban-gay-scouts_n_2806881.html |title=Train Won't Play at Boy Scouts Jamboree Unless BSA Lifts Ban on Gay Scouts, Band Says |work=[[HuffPost]] |date= March 4, 2013|access-date=March 15, 2013 |first=Meredith |last=Bennettsmith| quote= [Train] strongly-opposes any kind of policy that questions the equality of any American citizen ... We look forward to participating in the Jamboree this summer, as long as they make the right decision before then.}}</ref> Jepsen released a statement stating "As an artist who believes in equality for all people, I will not be participating in the Boy Scouts of America Jamboree this summer."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/carly-rae-jepsen-drops-out-of-boy-scouts-event-over-gay-rights/ |title=Carly Rae Jepsen drops out of Boy Scouts Event| work=[[CBS News]] |date=March 5, 2013 |access-date=March 15, 2013}}</ref>
