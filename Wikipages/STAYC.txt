'''STAYC''' ({{korean|hangul=스테이씨}}, acronym for '''Star to a Young Culture''') is a South Korean [[girl group]] formed by High Up Entertainment. The group is composed of six members: Sumin, [[Park Si-eun (entertainer)|Sieun]], Isa, Seeun, Yoon, and J. They debuted on November 12, 2020, with the release of their debut single album ''[[Star to a Young Culture]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Yoon|first=Sang-geun|date=2020-11-12|title='블랙아이드필승 걸그룹' 스테이씨 오늘(12일) 데뷔 '틴프레시'|url=https://star.mt.co.kr/stview.php?no=2020111207100846482|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-17|website=Star News|language=ko|archive-date=May 17, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210517180447/https://star.mt.co.kr/stview.php?no=2020111207100846482}}</ref>

==Name==
The group's name, STAYC, is an acronym for "Star to a Young Culture", and is meant to "reflect the act's aim of dominating pop culture".<ref name=hermantamar>{{cite web|last1=Herman|first1=Tamar|title=K-pop's STAYC, new six-member girl group with some familiar faces, are out to dominate Korean pop culture|url=https://www.scmp.com/lifestyle/k-pop/bands/article/3110988/k-pops-stayc-new-six-member-girl-group-some-familiar-faces|website=South China Morning Post|date=November 23, 2020|publisher=South China Morning Post|access-date=November 30, 2020|archive-date=November 29, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201129140537/https://www.scmp.com/lifestyle/k-pop/bands/article/3110988/k-pops-stayc-new-six-member-girl-group-some-familiar-faces|url-status=live}}</ref>

==History==
===2016–2019: Pre-debut===
Sieun was well-known prior to debut as both the daughter of veteran singer Park Nam-jung, and for her roles in dramas such as ''[[The Good Wife (South Korean TV series)|The Good Wife]]'', ''[[Queen for Seven Days]]'', and ''[[The Crowned Clown]]''. She won the Youth Acting Award in [[2018 SBS Drama Awards]] for her role in ''[[Still 17]]''.<ref name="CULTR">{{cite web|last1=Dewi|first1=Chintya|title=Black Eyed Pilseung's Girl Group Officially Debuts As "STAYC" By Releasing "So Bad" MV|url=https://www.cultr.com/news/black-eyed-pilseungs-girl-group-officially-debuts-as-stayc-by-releasing-so-bad-mv/|website=CULTR|date=November 24, 2020|publisher=CULTR|access-date=November 30, 2020|archive-date=December 27, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201227081439/https://www.cultr.com/news/black-eyed-pilseungs-girl-group-officially-debuts-as-stayc-by-releasing-so-bad-mv/|url-status=live}}</ref>

Seeun was known as an actress, appearing in shows like ''[[The Guardians (South Korean TV series)|The Guardians]]'' and ''[[Circle (TV series)|Circle]]''.<ref name="hermantamar" />

Prior to debut, STAYC was known as "High Up Girls", named after their company, High Up Entertainment.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Choi|first=Hee-jae|date=2020-10-12|title=블랙아이드필승 제작 걸그룹, 스테이씨로 팀명 확정…11월 12일 데뷔 [공식입장]|url=https://www.xportsnews.com/?ac=article_view&entry_id=1335739|url-status=live|access-date=2021-05-17|website=Xports News|language=ko|archive-date=May 17, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210517184919/https://www.xportsnews.com/?ac=article_view&entry_id=1335739}}</ref> The members, along with unsuccessful trainees Chang Chien-Chien ("Lydia") and Kim Min Jung, were featured in the [[List of YouTube Premium original programming|Youtube Originals]] docuseries ''K-Pop Evolution'' during the last few months of their training before debut.<ref>{{Cite web|title=K-Pop Evolution - YouTube|url=https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLjq6DwYksrzxsfMkXHOU0sN6RVemYByW_|access-date=2021-10-18|website=www.youtube.com}}</ref>

===2020: Debut with ''Star to a Young Culture''===
[[File:STAYC logo.png|225px|thumb|right|The official logo of STAYC]]
On September 8, it was announced that [[Black Eyed Pilseung]] of High Up Entertainment would debut their first girl group, with Sieun named as its first member.<ref>{{cite news|work=koreajoongangdaily|url=https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/2020/09/08/entertainment/kpop/Black-Eyed-Pilseung-girl-group-debut/20200908185600379.html|title=Producer duo Black Eyed Pilseung to debut a girl group for the first time|date=September 8, 2020|language=en|access-date=November 30, 2020|archive-date=November 30, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201130170428/https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/2020/09/08/entertainment/kpop/Black-Eyed-Pilseung-girl-group-debut/20200908185600379.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

On October 8, High Up Entertainment announced that the group would be debuting in November.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hwang|first=Hye-jin|date=October 8, 2020|title=하이업걸즈 측 "박남정 딸 박시은 걸그룹 6인조 확정, 11월 데뷔"(공식)|url=https://entertain.naver.com/now/read?oid=609&aid=0000336955|url-status=live|access-date=June 24, 2021|website=Newsen|language=ko|archive-date=October 14, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201014141827/https://entertain.naver.com/now/read?oid=609&aid=0000336955}}</ref> Three last members - Isa, J, and Yoon were revealed via prologue films on October 12, 13 and 14 respectively. On October 22, the title of their single album was announced to be ''Star to a Young Culture'', with the lead single "So Bad".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/STAYC_official/status/1319292429720195083|title=STAYC(스테이씨) on Twitter|website=Twitter|publisher=@STAYC_official|access-date=November 30, 2020|archive-date=October 22, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201022171409/https://twitter.com/STAYC_official/status/1319292429720195083|url-status=live}}</ref> The teaser schedule was released the same day. The single album was announced to be produced by [[Black Eyed Pilseung]], known for previous hits such as "[[TT (song)|TT]]", "[[Touch My Body (Sistar song)|Touch My Body]]", "[[Roller Coaster (Chungha song)|Roller Coaster]]", and "[[Dumhdurum]]". The producing duo described the group's sound as "Teen Fresh", a combination of "Teen" and "Fresh", highlighting the group's "unique individual vocal colors".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Baek|first1=Ji-Eun|title=[일문일답] 블핑승 스테이씨, 인성-비주얼-실력 겸비한 걸그룹 자신|url=https://www.chosun.com/entertainments/entertain_photo/2020/11/11/CGFHPDBDWYF62UG4LTBE7BNGAA/|website=Chosun|date=November 11, 2020|access-date=November 30, 2020|language=KR|archive-date=December 27, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201227081440/https://www.chosun.com/entertainments/entertain_photo/2020/11/11/CGFHPDBDWYF62UG4LTBE7BNGAA/|url-status=live}}</ref> Prior to the album's release, promotions were done through [[1theK|1theK's]] [[YouTube]] page. These included dance covers of groups such as [[Blackpink]], [[BTS]], and [[Stray Kids]], which received over 1 million views,<ref>{{cite web|title=[4K] 2020년 K-POP 띵곡 커버한 STAYC(스테이씨) / BLACKPINK, BTS, SKZ, NCT / Cover Dance Medley / COUNTDANCE 카운트댄스|trans-title=[4K] STAYC Covers K-POP Songs in 2020 / BLACKPINK, BTS, SKZ, NCT / Cover Dance Medley / COUNTDANCE|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dbZIMJ2AA_w|website=[[YouTube]]|publisher=[[1theK Originals]] ([[Kakao M|Kakao M Corporation]])|access-date=November 30, 2020|archive-date=December 2, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201202154939/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dbZIMJ2AA_w|url-status=live}}</ref> and a vocal cover of songs by [[Twice]] and [[Red Velvet (group)|Red Velvet]] which received over 2 million views.<ref>{{cite web|title=SM 이수만이 BTS 불타오르네를 만들었다면 어떤 느낌일까? / STAYC, BTS, BLACKPINK, TWICE, Red Velvet / SWITCH ON|trans-title=How would it feel if SM Lee Soo-man made BTS "FIRE"? / STAYC, BTS, BLACKPINK, TWICE, Red Velvet / SWITCH ON|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UESYmSCCRHE|website=[[YouTube]]|publisher=[[1theK Originals]] ([[Kakao M|Kakao M Corporation]])|access-date=November 30, 2020|archive-date=November 16, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201116204319/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UESYmSCCRHE|url-status=live}}</ref>

On November 12, the music video for the album's lead single "So Bad" was released, receiving over 2.6 million views in the first 24 hours.<ref name=CULTR/> Their album was released on the same day, selling over 4,300 copies on its first day, the most for a debut girl group in 2020.<ref name=CULTR/> It went on to sell more than 10,000 copies on its first week, becoming the first debut album by a girl group in 2020 to do so.<ref name=hermantamar/> They promoted the album through a [[V Live]] debut showcase, promoting both the lead single, "So Bad" and [[B-side]] "Like This".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vlive.tv/video/222414|title=STAYC(스테이씨) on VLIVE|website=VLIVE|publisher=VLIVE|access-date=November 30, 2020|archive-date=November 11, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201111174947/https://www.vlive.tv/video/222414|url-status=live}}</ref> The group made their music show debut on November 13, 2020, at [[KBS Music Bank]],<ref name=CULTR/> which was followed up with performances at [[Show Champion]], [[Inkigayo]], and [[The Show (South Korean TV series)|The Show]]. The album debuted at number 17 on the weekly [[Gaon Album Chart]]. "So Bad" debuted at number 90 on ''Billboard's'' [[K-pop Hot 100]], and number 21 on the [[World Digital Song Sales]] chart.<ref name="world_songs_chart_forbes">{{cite web|last1=McIntyre|first1=Hugo|date=November 26, 2020|title=So Bad|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2020/11/26/bts-aespa-stayc-nicki-minaj-and-beyonc-notable-moves-on-this-weeks-world-songs-chart/?sh=2ccc89dd6f40|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201227081437/https://www.forbes.com/sites/hughmcintyre/2020/11/26/bts-aespa-stayc-nicki-minaj-and-beyonc-notable-moves-on-this-weeks-world-songs-chart/?sh=2ccc89dd6f40|archive-date=December 27, 2020|access-date=November 30, 2020|website=Forbes}}</ref>
