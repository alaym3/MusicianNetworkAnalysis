'''Jonathan David Buck''' (born November 11, 1974), known professionally as '''Jon B.''', is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer. His debut album, ''[[Bonafide (Jon B. album)|Bonafide]]'' (1995), went [[Platinum certification|platinum]].

==Early life==
Born in [[Providence, Rhode Island]], United States and raised in [[Altadena, California]], Buck comes from a musical family; with his father David, a professor of music, his mother Linda, a concert pianist and his siblings Deborah, a violinist and his brother Kevin, a cellist.<ref name="JON B: 'THAT GRAMMY & ME'">{{cite news|last1=Hunter|first1=Karen|title=JON B: 'THAT GRAMMY & ME'|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/jon-b-grammy-article-1.725311|newspaper=[[New York Daily News]]|access-date=2015-05-12}}</ref> His mother is Jewish and his father has Dutch ancestry. In 1992, Jon B. was shopping demos when he caught the attention of [[Tracey Edmonds]], then-president and CEO of Yab-Yum Records.<ref name="JON B: 'THAT GRAMMY & ME'"/>

==Career==
Jon B's early influences as a musician include: [[Duran Duran]], [[INXS]], [[Marvin Gaye]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Babyface (musician)|Babyface]], [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], and [[Sade (singer)|Sade]].  He spent the summer after high school writing, producing and recording 40 songs and began making his rounds to all major record labels.

===1994–97: ''Bonafide'' and ''Cool Relax''===
Jon B., before his rise to fame, used to be a songwriter and wrote songs for [[After 7]], [[Toni Braxton]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Color Me Badd]], the [[Spice Girls]] and others. Jon B.'s popularity began in the mid-1990s, he released his debut studio album in 1995 titled ''[[Bonafide (Jon B. album)|Bonafide]]''; the album spawned the popular hit and Grammy-nominated single "[[Someone to Love (Jon B. song)|Someone to Love]]", which featured the [[Grammy Award]]-winning artist [[Babyface (musician)|Babyface]]. The album was a commercial success as it went [[RIAA certification|platinum]], selling over 1 million copies.

He released his second studio album ''[[Cool Relax]]'' in 1997, the album spawned the top 10 single "[[They Don't Know (Jon B. song)|They Don't Know]]" and two other top 20 hits, including [[Are U Still Down]] featuring [[Tupac Shakur]]. ''Cool Relax'' received generally positive reviews from music critics, and it is his most successful album commercially; it has been certified as double platinum by the [[RIAA]].

While on tour promoting ''Cool Relax'', he formed a group with two of his back-up singers, Dominiquinn and Silky Deluxe, named Jack Herrera collectively.<ref>[http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1430674/02101999/jon_b.jhtml MTV News – Articles – 1430674 – 02101999<!-- Bot generated title -->]. MTV.</ref> A full album, titled ''Retro Futuristo'', was put together, but never officially released.
