'''Jonas Erik Altberg''' ({{IPA-sv|ˈjûːnas ˈêːrɪk ˈâltbærj}}; born 22 December 1984 in [[Halmstad]]), better known by his [[stage name]] '''Basshunter''' (also stylised as '''BassHunter'''),<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/basshunt|title=BassHunter|publisher=[[Twitter]]|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref> is a Swedish singer, record producer and DJ. He began producing music under the stage name "Basshunter" in 2001.

He recorded six studio albums: ''[[The Bassmachine]]'' (2004), ''[[LOL (Basshunter album)|LOL]]'' (2006), ''The Old Shit'' (2006), ''[[Now You're Gone – The Album]]'' (2008), ''[[Bass Generation]]'' (2009) and ''[[Calling Time]]'' (2013). In addition to his own music, he has written for El Capon, [[Mange Makers]] and [[Lana Scolaro]]. He also took part in the British [[Celebrity Big Brother (British series 7)|seventh series]] of ''[[Celebrity Big Brother (British TV series)|Celebrity Big Brother]]'', the Swedish ''[[Fort Boyard (game show)|Fångarna på fortet]]'' and the British ''[[Weakest Link (British game show)|Weakest Link]]'' in 2010.

He won awards such as the [[European Border Breakers Award]], the [[Grammis|Grammi]] award for Best Ringtone of the Year in 2006, and the [[World Music Awards|World Music Award]]. He was also nominated for the [[BT Digital Music Awards|BT Digital Music Award]], [[MTV Europe Music Awards|MTV Europe Music Award]], and [[Rockbjörnen]]. According to Balloon Records label figures, more than eight million Basshunter records have been sold.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://balloonrecords.com/artist/basshunter | title = Basshunter | website = Balloon Records | access-date = 7 November 2021}}</ref>{{efn|According to ''[[Svenska Dagbladet]]'' figures published in 2009, more than three million Basshunter records have been sold.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.svd.se/kan-man-brunsmeta-nagot-som-redan-stinker/i/Kultur | title = Upp till bevis för Basshunter | website = [[Svenska Dagbladet]] | author = Victor Johansson | date = 21 September 2009 | access-date = 7 November 2021}}</ref>}}

== Early life ==
[[File:Sturegymnasiet2.jpg|thumb|left|Sturegymnasiet in [[Halmstad]]]]
Jonas Altberg was born the elder of two boys and raised in Halmstad, Sweden, and lived with his parents, Karl Göran Altberg and his late mother Gunhild Elisabet Altberg,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.proff.se/befattning/gunhild-elisabet-altberg-/-/10228887/|title=Gunhild Elisabet Altberg|publisher=Proff|language=sv|access-date=11 October 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141016064307/http://www.proff.se/befattning/gunhild-elisabet-altberg-/-/10228887/|archive-date=16 October 2014}}</ref> and younger brother, Joakim,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/Basshunter.se/posts/10150687153120635|title=Basshunter|date=8 July 2011|publisher=[[Facebook]]|author=Jonas Altberg "Basshunter"|access-date=8 July 2011}}</ref> near [[Tylösand]] beach.<ref name="Bio 2010" /> His mother was a teacher and head of a [[secondary school]],<ref name="David Dolphin interview">{{cite web|url=http://pf.csn.ul.ie/blog/?p=63|title=Jonas Erik Altberg (Basshunter) Interview|date=3 May 2009|publisher=David Dolphin's Blog|access-date=13 August 2014|author=David Dolphin|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110721132801/http://pf.csn.ul.ie/blog/?p=63|archive-date=21 July 2011}}</ref> and she was union representative for [[Swedish Teachers' Union]].<ref>{{cite web | url = https://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=128&artikel=5259713 | title = De slår undan benen för personalen | website = [[Sveriges Radio]] | date = 6 September 2012 | access-date = 11 December 2020 | language = sv | trans-title = They beat the legs of the staff}}</ref> His father worked in a construction company. Basshunter first went to Kattegattgymnasiet  secondary school, a technical school. However, he moved to Sturegymnasiet  secondary school in Halmstad to study music.<ref name="SYDSVENSKAN">{{cite web|url=http://www.sydsvenskan.se/samtidigt/basshunter-fann-en-bot-mot-mobbarna/|title=Basshunter fann en bot mot mobbarna|date=20 January 2008|publisher=[[Sydsvenskan]]|language=sv|access-date=12 August 2014|author=Jonas Grönlund|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120707191426/http://www.sydsvenskan.se/samtidigt/basshunter-fann-en-bot-mot-mobbarna|archive-date=7 July 2012}}</ref> He also attended college but did not complete his program.<ref name="David Dolphin interview" />

In 1998,<ref name="In4merz">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qG99vMDl3tA|title=Basshunter Interview – EXCLUSIVE|date=5 November 2009|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=19 May 2013|author=Clare Hudson}}</ref> he started [[singing]] in a [[choir]] run by his mother which birthed his passion for singing.<ref name="Outlineonline.co.uk">{{cite web|url=http://www.outlineonline.co.uk/music/interviews/interview-with-basshunter|title=Interview with Basshunter|date=2009|publisher=Outline|access-date=13 August 2014}}</ref> He also sang in a rock band<ref name="FaceCulture 1">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=opFzJE-izlc|title=Interview Basshunter – Jonas Altberg (part 1)|date=7 December 2006|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=17 August 2014}}</ref> where he became acquainted with [[experimental music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ViDZYeg4iKo|title=Basshunter interview by Toazted|date=13 October 2006|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=17 August 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
=== 2001–2006: Beginnings ===
Altberg began producing music under the [[stage name]] "Basshunter" as a reference to his musical style in 2001,<ref name="In4merz" /> after six months of using [[FL Studio|FruityLoops]] (currently FL Studio).<ref name="Azul TV">{{cite web | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ImaZRwScb8w | title = Basshunter Interview with Azul TV | date = 12 September 2011 | access-date = 12 September 2011 | publisher = [[YouTube]]}}</ref>

His first studio album, ''[[The Bassmachine]]'', was released by Alex Music on 25 August 2004.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.alexmusic.se/releases.htm | title = RELEASES | access-date = 17 August 2014 | publisher = Alex Music}}</ref> In 2005, a remastered version of this album was released, with better sound quality.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://basshunter.se/ |title=BASSHUNTER – : : Recent News |access-date=12 March 2011 |publisher=basshunter.se |archive-url=https://archive.today/20060206194310/http://basshunter.se/ |archive-date=6 February 2006 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2006, Basshunter re-released ''The Bassmachine'' as ''The Bass Machine''<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.discogs.com/Basshunter-The-Bass-Machine/release/6790472 | title = Basshunter – The Bass Machine | access-date = 4 June 2015 | publisher = [[Discogs]]}}</ref> and he released his third studio album ''The Old Shit'' through his own web site.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.discogs.com/Basshunter-The-Old-Shit/release/4578737 | title = Basshunter – The Old Shit | access-date = 4 June 2015 | publisher = [[Discogs]]}}</ref> Basshunter's interest in music deepened as time went by.<!--Is this necessary information?--><ref name="Bio 2010">{{cite web | url = http://www.basshunter.se/bio/ | title = Bio | access-date = 27 March 2019 | publisher = basshunter.se | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20100528035145/http://www.basshunter.se/bio/ | archive-date = 28 May 2010}}</ref>

He published his music mainly through the [[Internet]] (among others, to chat channels and gaming websites)<ref name="Skämtet som blev sommarens första hit">{{cite web | url = https://www.metro.se/artikel/sk%C3%A4mtet-som-blev-sommarens-f%C3%B6rsta-hit-xr | title = Skämtet som blev sommarens första hit | date = 8 June 2006 | accessdate = 1 April 2019 | website = [[Metro (Swedish newspaper)|Metro]] |language=sv | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20190401142054/https://www.metro.se/artikel/sk%C3%A4mtet-som-blev-sommarens-f%C3%B6rsta-hit-xr | archivedate = 1 April 2019}}</ref> and for several years he was a [[Disc jockey|DJ]] in Swedish clubs.<ref name="Seattle Weekly">{{cite web | url = http://www.seattleweekly.com/2009-06-03/music/basshunter-turns-fruityloops-into-gold/ | title = Basshunter Turns FruityLoops Into Gold | date = 2 June 2009 | access-date = 10 August 2014 | author = Erika Hobart | publisher = [[Seattle Weekly]] | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20090607080130/http://www.seattleweekly.com/2009-06-03/music/basshunter-turns-fruityloops-into-gold/ | archive-date = 7 June 2009}}</ref> His single "[[Welcome to Rainbow]]" was released on 1 April 2006.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.discogs.com/release/1186658 | title = Basshunter – Welcome To Rainbow | access-date = 20 August 2010 | publisher = [[Discogs]] | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20090723144141/http://www.discogs.com/release/1186658 | archive-date = 23 July 2009 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> It included the "[[Boten Anna]]", released later as single that May.<ref name="Boten Anna SWE" /> In 2006, he moved to [[Malmö]].<ref name="SYDSVENSKAN" />

On 26 June 2013 Basshunter announced that he was retiring from singing and focusing on DJing, producing, and songwriting. He met new artists and invited them to sing on his songs. He made the decision after his exhaustive four performances in four days.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.contactmusic.com/basshunter/news/basshunter-quits-singing_3782532|title=Basshunter quits singing|date=26 June 2015|publisher=[[Contactmusic.com]]|access-date=25 March 2015}}</ref>

During his time on the [[reality television|reality show]] ''[[Celebrity Big Brother (British TV series)|Celebrity Big Brother]]'' he was in an intimate relationship with Ekaterina Ivanova.<ref name="Basshunters gyllene miss">{{cite web|url=http://www.svd.se/kultur/basshunters-gyllene-miss_4402301.svd|title=Basshunters gyllene miss|date=10 March 2010|publisher=[[Svenska Dagbladet]]|language=sv|access-date=16 May 2011|author=Harry Amster|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141011105616/http://www.svd.se/kultur/basshunters-gyllene-miss_4402301.svd|archive-date=11 October 2014}}</ref> After being a couple for three years, Basshunter married Tina Makhia Khayatsadeh on 19 January 2017. The wedding took place in the Dubai Polo & Equestrian Club in the presence of singers [[Arash (singer)|Arash]] and [[Margaret (singer)|Margaret]], [[Jan Thesleff]], Sweden's ambassador to the United Arab Emirates, and Jonas Siljemark, the chief executive officer of [[Warner Music Sweden]], his manager Henrik Uhlmann and Basshunter's brother Joakim.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.aftonbladet.se/nojesbladet/a/r7OBA/basshunter-har-gift-sig|title=Basshunter har gift sig|publisher=[[Aftonbladet]]|language=sv|author=Torbjörn Ek|date=20 January 2017|access-date=20 January 2017}}</ref>

On 10 December 2010, Basshunter was charged by [[Fife Constabulary]] over a [[sexual assault]] allegation on two female fans during a party at a nightclub in [[Kirkcaldy]], Scotland. He was released on bail. His manager insisted at the time that the allegations were "totally untrue".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-edinburgh-east-fife-11990845 |title=BBC News: Basshunter music star on sex attack charges |publisher=[[BBC]] |date=14 December 2010 |access-date=30 December 2010}}</ref> Basshunter pled not guilty to two charges of sexual assault against the two women at the hearing at the town's [[Sheriff court]] on 12 January 2011.<ref name="BBCJan">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-edinburgh-east-fife-12171434 |title=BBC News: Basshunter music star denies sex attack charges |publisher=[[BBC]]|date=12 January 2011 |access-date=18 January 2011}}</ref> On 14 June 2011, he was found not guilty with the sheriff describing the two accusers as "neither credible nor reliable"<ref name="bbcjun">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-edinburgh-east-fife-13768244 |title=DJ Basshunter cleared of Kirkcaldy club sex charges |publisher=[[BBC]]|date=14 June 2011 |access-date=14 June 2011}}</ref> and their testimony as "riddled with inconsistencies and improbabilities".<ref>{{Cite news|title=DJ cleared of nightclub sexual assault charges|page=2|date=15 June 2011|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]}}</ref>
