}}'''Death from Above 1979''' (also known as '''Death from Above''') is a Canadian [[Rock music|rock]] duo consisting of bassist [[Jesse F. Keeler]] and drummer and vocalist [[Sebastien Grainger]] from [[Toronto|Toronto, Ontario]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/sebby_g/status/20416741604|title=SEBASTIEN GRAINGER: any Wikipedia users out there? if you're so inclined can you change the DFA1979 site to state we were Toronto based, not Montreal. true say.|date=2010-08-05|publisher=Twitter.com|access-date=2011-07-15}}</ref> formed in 2001. The band released their debut album, ''[[You're a Woman, I'm a Machine]]'', in 2004 and broke up in 2006. They reformed in 2011 and released their second album, ''[[The Physical World]]'', in 2014. Since then the band has released 2 more albums, ''[[Outrage! Is Now]]'' in 2017 and ''[[Is 4 Lovers]]'' in 2021.

== History ==

=== Formation and ''Heads Up'' (2001–2003) ===
Grainger and Keeler reportedly met at a [[Sonic Youth]] concert,{{citation needed|date=July 2016}} though they sometimes jokingly claimed to have met in prison, on a pirate ship, or in a gay bar, leading some journalists and fans to believe these stories.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-blogs/why-everyone-is-losing-their-mind-over-a-new-death-from-above-1979-album-20087|title=Why Everyone Is Losing Their Mind Over A New Death From Above 1979 Album - NME|date=2014-06-12|work=NME|access-date=2017-06-11|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://emptylighthouse.com/death-above-1979-performs-trainwreck-1979-late-show-david-letterman-36255684|title=Death From Above 1979 Performs 'Trainwreck 1979' on The Late Show with David Letterman|last=Johnson|first=Brian|date=2014-09-11|work=Empty Lighthouse Magazine|access-date=2017-06-11}}</ref> Keeler has also said to have met Grainger when looking for a drummer to play in his [[hardcore punk]] band Femme Fatale, further stating "That's how Death from Above got started."<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://shop.lastgang.com/products/femme-fatale-from-the-abundance-of-the-heart-the-mouth-speaks|title=From The Abundance Of The Heart, The Mouth Speaks|website=Last Gang Shop|access-date=2017-04-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170403112001/https://shop.lastgang.com/products/femme-fatale-from-the-abundance-of-the-heart-the-mouth-speaks|archive-date=2017-04-03|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On December 15, 2002, the band released their debut release, the EP ''[[Heads Up (Death from Above EP)|Heads Up]]''.

=== ''You're a Woman, I'm a Machine'' and break up (2004–2006) ===
The band began recording for their debut album, ''[[You're a Woman, I'm a Machine]]'' from February to April 2004 at [[The Chemical Sound]] in [[Toronto]]. Additional recording was done at Studio Plateau in [[Montreal]] and the album was engineered and produced by [[Al-P]], with the exception of the Montreal sessions which were engineered by Drew Malamud.{{citation needed|date=July 2016}} The album was released in October, 2004.<ref>[http://www.cbcmusic.ca/posts/17548/cohen-gould-buffy-25-best-canadian-debut-albums "25 best Canadian debut albums ever"]. ''CBC Music'', June 16, 2017.</ref> The band released three singles to promote, ''You're a Woman, I'm a Machine'', these singles were "[[Romantic Rights (song)|Romantic Rights]]" on November 4, 2004, "[[Blood on Our Hands]]" on February 17, 2005 and "[[Black History Month (song)|Black History Month]]" on June 13, 2005.{{citation needed|date=July 2016}} In 2005, the video for "Blood on Our Hands" won a [[VideoFACT]] award at the [[MuchMusic Video Awards]].{{citation needed|date=July 2016}}

In 2004, the band changed their name to "Death from Above 1979" (previously named "Death from Above") due to a cease and desist letter that was filed against the band by [[James Murphy (electronic musician)|James Murphy]]'s label [[DFA Records|Death From Above Records]]. The band responded by attaching the legal minimum number of numerals required to keep the first part of the name.<ref name=":22">{{Cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/74026-death-from-above-1979-are-just-death-from-above-now/|title=Death From Above 1979 Are Just "Death From Above" Now {{!}} Pitchfork|website=pitchfork.com|language=en|access-date=2017-06-07}}</ref><ref name=":62">{{Cite news|url=http://www.nme.com/news/music/death-from-above-1979-name-change-2085085|title=Death From Above 1979 announce name change - NME|date=2017-06-06|work=NME|access-date=2017-06-08|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.avclub.com/article/death-above-drops-new-single-1979-its-name-256476|title=Death From Above drops new single, the "1979" from its name|date=2017-06-07|access-date=2017-06-08}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7824626/death-from-above-1979-name-new-song-freeze-me|title=Death From Above 1979 Drop the '1979' From Name, Share New Song 'Freeze Me'|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-06-08}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/sep/05/death-from-above-1979|title=Death From Above 1979: 'I wanted to get arrested. I felt fearless'|last=Hebblethwaite|first=Phil|date=2014-09-05|work=The Guardian|access-date=2017-06-08|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> On MuchMusic's television program ''[[The NewMusic|The New Music]]'', Keeler further explained why the band split. He claimed it was due to disagreements with bandmate Grainger on many levels, including creative differences and musical style.

=== Reunion and ''The Physical World'' (2011–2016) ===
[[File:Death From Above 1979 @ Wellington Square (25 9 2011) (6202562676).jpg|thumb|Sebastien Grainger performing at Parklife Festival 2011]]
On February 4, 2011, the band officially reunited.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://deathfromabove1979.com/some-words/|title=Some Words|publisher=DEATH FROM ABOVE 1979|access-date=2011-07-15|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110709004255/http://deathfromabove1979.com/some-words/|archive-date=2011-07-09}}</ref>

The band performed a new song while performing at [[Edgefest|EdgeFest]] on July 14, 2012 at Downsview Park in Toronto. On September 18, 2012, a Canadian tour was announced; the band revealed that they had written new songs, but needed to perform them live in order to "make them any good".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://deathfromabove1979.com/ok/|title=Ok|publisher=DEATH FROM ABOVE 1979|access-date=2012-09-21|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120921040150/http://deathfromabove1979.com/ok/|archive-date=2012-09-21}}</ref> On October 28, 2012, the band's blog was updated as they built excitement for their string of shows around Canada. In the post, Grainger cited that they were "coming to share new material, and to work out the kinks".{{citation needed|date=July 2016}} The band was originally scheduled to perform at [[Governors Ball Music Festival]] in June 2013, but later announced that they were having unexpected trouble while working on new music and cancelled their appearance.{{citation needed|date=July 2016}} On July 11, 2013, the band confirmed that a new record is in the works on their Facebook page after cancelling European shows due to a "medical emergency". They did, however, perform at Wakestock Music Festival in August and Rifflandia in September.{{citation needed|date=July 2016}} On July 8, 2014, the band released a single, "Trainwreck 1979", and announced more details of their upcoming studio album, ''[[The Physical World]]''.<ref name=":4">{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2014/07/death-from-above-1979-detail-new-album-world-tour/|title=Death From Above 1979 detail new album, world tour|publisher=Consequenceofsound.net|access-date=21 October 2014}}</ref> On September 9, 2014 the band released their second album, ''The Physical World'' through Last Gang and [[Warner Bros. Records]].<ref name=":4" />

On October 7, 2014 the band released a documentary ''Life After Death from Above 1979''. The documentary chronicled the history of the band and their reunion. It was directed by Grainger's wife, Eva Michon.

On April 22, 2016, the band released a live album, ''Live At Third Man Records'' which was recorded in 2015 at [[Jack White]]'s Third Man Records headquarters in Nashville.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2016/04/death-from-above-1979-announce-third-man-records-live-album-share-right-on-frankenstein-listen/|title=Death From Above 1979 announce Third Man Records live album, share "Right on Frankenstein" — listen|date=20 April 2016|website=Consequenceofsound.net|access-date=19 October 2017}}</ref>

=== ''Outrage! Is Now'' (2017–2020) ===
On June 6, 2017, the band announced an official name change back to the original "Death from Above".<ref name=":32" /> Although the band has said their social media handles will keep the "1979" suffix, all future music and shows from the duo will reportedly arrive under the new name.<ref name=":22" /><ref name=":62" /> Grainger said in an interview that the band decided to not include the "1979" suffix on a tour promo for their tour with the [[Eagles of Death Metal]] in early 2016, "just to see what would happen" and the band received no legal issues because of it which led to them doing it again on their tour promo with [[Black Rebel Motorcycle Club]] and once again there were no legal repercussions.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://audioboom.com/posts/6059213-122-death-from-above-sebastien-on-dropping-the-1979-his-ancient-fashion-label-and-dfa-s-latest-single-freeze-me|title=122 – DEATH FROM ABOVE – Sebastien on dropping the 1979, his Ancient Fashion label, and DFA's latest single "Freeze Me"|work=audioBoom|access-date=2017-08-01|language=en}}</ref> Sebastian has also said the making the album art for the band's single "Freeze Me" also was part of the decision, "The final straw was when I was making the art for our single ‘Freeze Me’. I wanted to write the name out in ice, so I went on Amazon and ordered an ice cube tray in the alphabet. It came and there were no numbers. That was that."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nme.com/news/music/death-from-above-1979-outrage-now-interview-name-change-2135207|title=Death From Above talk to us about their survival, 'Outrage! Is Now' and their name change - NME|date=2017-09-07|work=NME|access-date=2017-09-08|language=en-US}}</ref> Along with the name change, the duo announced the new single, "Freeze Me." The song premiered on BBC Radio 1 before its release on June 7.<ref name=":32">{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p0554sfv|title=Death From Above - Freeze Me, Jam sits in for Annie - BBC Radio 1|website=BBC|access-date=19 October 2017}}</ref>

=== ''Is 4 Lovers'' (2020–present) ===

In December 2020, the band wiped their social media pages. A series of posts to their [[Instagram]] grid revealed the band's logo, followed by a teaser video confirming the band's name change back to Death from Above 1979.<ref>{{cite web |title=DEATH FROM ABOVE 1979 (@DFA1979) |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CIoboPeHiTq/ |via=Instagram |access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref> After months of teaser posts, the band confirmed a new album on February 3 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Strauss |first1=Matthew |title=Death From Above 1979 Announce Album, Share Video for New Song 'One + One': Watch |url=https://pitchfork.com/news/death-from-above-1979-announce-album-share-video-for-new-song-one-one-watch/ |website=Pitchfork |access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref> The album, ''[[Is 4 Lovers]]'', was released on March 26. The announcement coincided with the release of a new single, "One + One".<ref>{{cite web |last=Trendell |first=Andrew |title=Death From Above 1979 return with dance-y single 'One + One' and tell us about 'playful' new album 'Is 4 Lovers' |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/death-from-above-1979-return-with-dance-y-single-one-one-and-tell-us-about-playful-new-album-is-4-lovers-2871790 |website=NME |access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
Throughout its career, the band has been described as [[dance-punk]],<ref name=":02">{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/why-death-from-above-1979-reunited-after-a-decade-apart-20140919|title=Why Death From Above 1979 Reunited After a Decade Apart|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2017-04-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/09/album-review-death-from-above-outrage-is-now/|title=Album Review: Death from Above – Outrage! Is Now|date=2017-09-04|work=Consequence of Sound|access-date=2017-10-08|language=en-US}}</ref> [[noise punk]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/death-from-above-1979-showcase-live-show-on-trainwreck-1979-video-20140911|title=Death From Above 1979 Showcase Live Show in New Video|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2017-04-12}}</ref> and [[punk rock]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noisey.vice.com/en_ca/article/life-after-death-from-above-movie-sebastien-grainger-interview|title=There's 'Life After Death From Above' in the New Movie From The Band|website=Noisey|language=en-ca|access-date=2017-04-12}}</ref> Grainger has stated when the band first started his and Keeler's aim was "to be as straight ahead as possible," and "to be the [[AC/DC]] of [[Hardcore punk|hardcore]]."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nme.com/news/music/death-from-above-1979-14-1246418|title=Death From Above 1979: 'We want to be the AC/DC of hardcore' - NME|date=2014-08-06|work=NME|access-date=2017-10-08|language=en-US}}</ref> ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' claims "Death From Above embodied the DIY dance-punk aesthetic that lived in home recordings and grimy basement clubs" and that the band's early work "combined noisy hardcore, gritty synths and earnest screams".<ref name=":02" />
