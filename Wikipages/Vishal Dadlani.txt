'''Vishal Dadlani''' (born 28 June 1973) is an Indian singer, songwriter and music composer. He is one half of the duo [[Vishal-Shekhar]], Front Man and vocalist of one of India's leading rock bands called [[Pentagram (Indian band)|Pentagram]]. As a singer he has sung various hit songs for various genre's notably ''Dhoom Again'', ''Kurban Hua'', ''Jee le Zara'', ''Marjaiyann'', ''I feel Good'', ''Jab Mila Tu'', ''Tu Meri'', ''[[Swag Se Swagat]]'', ''[[Bala (song)|Bala]]'', ''Har funn Maula'', ''[[Khuda Hafiz]]''. In an interview he admitted that he can sing from Rock to Romantic, any genre as he has particular voice texture for kind of genre.{{To whom?|date=August 2021}}{{Peacock term|date=August 2021}} 
As a vocalist of band [[Pentagram]], notable works are ''Voice, Tomorrow's Decided, Must I, Love Drug Climbdown'' and more to go. 
As music director, he has composed some Songs for Films such as [[Om Shanti Om]], Anjana Anjani, [[Dostana (2008 film)|Dostana]], I hate Love Stories, [[Bang Bang!|Bang Bang]], [[Sultan]], [[Student of the Year]], [[Befikre]], [[War (2019 film)|War]] etc.

Dadlani has been in collaboration with many international artists like [[Imogen Heap]], [[Diplo]], [[The Vamps (British band)|The Vamps]], and [[Akon]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/entertainment/music/happy-birthday-vishal-dadlani-the-pioneer-of-original-independent-music-turns-43-2881286/lite/|title=Happy Birthday Vishal Dadlani,The Indian Express}}</ref>{{citation needed|date=August 2021}}

==Early life==
Dadlani was born and raised in a [[Sindhi Hindus|Sindhi Hindu]] family in [[Bandra, West Bombay]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thehindubusinessline.com/blink/know/tonguetied-in-sindhi/article8418394.ece|title=Tongue-tied in Sindhi|work=The Hindu|date=1 April 2016|access-date=9 August 2016|first=Rutam|last=Vora}}</ref> He went to [[Hill Grange High School]] in [[Gopalrao Deshmukh Marg|Pedder Road]], [[Cumbala Hill]], [[South Mumbai|South Bombay]] and subsequently attended [[Jai Hind College]], [[University of Mumbai]] for two years (1989–90), after which he went to [[H.R. College of Commerce and Economics]], [[HSNC University]], both in [[Churchgate]], [[South Mumbai|South Bombay]]. He graduated in 1994 with a bachelor's degree in commerce.

==Career==
Dadlani's musical journey started in 1994 with the [[Mumbai]]-based electronica/indie-rock band [[Pentagram (Indian band)|Pentagram]], which he fronted. Pentagram has gained recognition as one of the pioneers of Indian independent music.<ref>[http://travel.cnn.com/mumbai/play/indie-rock-mumbai-decade-review-894176/ Travel CNN] – Retrieved 21 July 2010</ref><ref>[https://maverickvedam.wordpress.com/2011/03/24/best-rock-bands-of-india/ Maverickvedem Blog]. Retrieved 24 March 2011</ref>

While active with the band, Dadlani found fame as a [[Bollywood]] music composer, singer, and lyricist, with movies like [[Jhankaar Beats]], [[Bluffmaster!|Bluff Master]], and [[Salaam Namaste]].

===Collaboration with Shekhar===
{{Main|Vishal–Shekhar}}
In 1999, [[Vishal-Shekhar]], the Bollywood composing/producing and performing music duo, was formed. Dadlani and [[Shekhar Ravjiani]] have worked together on Hindi, Telugu, Tamil, and Marathi films. Their notable works include ''[[Jhankaar Beats]]'' (2003), ''[[Dus]]'' (2005), ''[[Bluffmaster]]'' (2005), ''[[I See You (2006 film)|I See You]]'' (2006), ''[[Om Shanti Om]]'' (2007), ''[[Bachna Ae Haseeno]]'' (2008), ''[[Dostana (2008 film)|Dostana]]'' (2008),  ''[[Anjaana Anjaani]]'' (2010), ''[[Ra.One]]'' (2011), ''[[Student Of The Year (film)|Student of the Year]]'' (2012), ''[[Chennai Express]]'' (2013), ''[[Bang Bang!]]'' (2014), ''[[Happy New Year (2014 film)|Happy New Year]]'' (2014), ''[[Sultan (2016 film)|Sultan]]'' (2016), and ''[[Befikre]]'' (2016).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.planetbollywood.com/displayReview.php?id=m092014054254 |title=Vishal Dadlani music director of Happy New Year  |publisher=PlanetBollywood |access-date=17 August 2015}}</ref> The duo has gained nationwide recognition, and today are credited with being amongst the architects of the modern Bollywood sound, having composed music for over 60 films, released over 300 songs, and performed over 1,000 shows worldwide. The two rose to prominence in 2003 when they wrote the score for the film ''Jhankaar Beats'', which included the song "Tu Aashiqui Hai". They won the [[Filmfare RD Burman Award for New Music Talent]] for their work on the film.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0347278/awards|title=Jhankaar Beats|publisher=IMDb}}</ref>

==Controversies==
* In the aftermath of the [[Panama Papers|Panama Paper]] leaks, the names of Dadlani and some of his family members surfaced due to investments they made through his company Sunny Blessing Holding Inc. in the [[British Virgin Islands]]. Some of these transactions are currently under investigation by the enforcement directorate.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://offshoreleaks.icij.org/search?c=&cat=1&e=&j=&q=Dadlani&utf8=%E2%9C%93|title=Offshoreleaks ICIJ (Indian Names)|date=28 August 2016|access-date=8 September 2016}}</ref>
* In 2016, Dadlani received strong opposition for criticizing the [[Jainism|Jain]] monk [[Tarun Sagar|Muni Tarun Sagar]] in a tweet. He was accused of hurting religious sentiments and several [[First information report]]s were filed against him, though the monk is said to not have taken the issue seriously. Dadlani later wrote an open letter, saying that the tweets had been his biggest mistake.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://indianexpress.com/article/india/india-news-india/vishal-dadlanis-tweet-on-jain-monk-unfortunate-delhi-cm-arvind-kejriwal-2999812/|title=Vishal Dadlani offers to quit political work after controversial tweet on Jain monk|date=28 August 2016|access-date=8 September 2016}}</ref> He was fined by the [[Punjab and Haryana High Court]] for the tweet.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/tehseen-poonawalla-vishal-dadlani-fined-20l-for-tweets-against-monk/articleshow/69122337.cms|title=Tehseen Poonawalla, Vishal Dadlani fined 20L for tweets against monk|date=1 May 2019|access-date=1 May 2019}}</ref>
