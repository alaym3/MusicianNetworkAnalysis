'''Ha*Ash''' is an American [[Latin pop]] duo from [[Lake Charles, Louisiana]]. The band was formed in 2002 by sisters Hanna Nicole (born 1985) and Ashley Grace (born 1987). The group's name is an [[portmanteau]] of letters from their first names. They have released five studio albums, with the most recent, ''[[30 de Febrero]]'', being released in 2017. From 2014 to 2017, all of their singles received at least a [[Music recording certification|gold certification]] from the [[Asociación Mexicana de Productores de Fonogramas y Videogramas|Mexican Association of Producers of Phonograms and Videograms]]. 

In addition to Latin pop, Ha*Ash incorporates [[country pop]], [[pop rock]], and [[singer-songwriter]] styles into their music, and they have cited artists such as [[Shania Twain]], [[Loretta Lynn]], and [[The Chicks]] as influences. They are also known for their [[philanthropy]] and [[Activism|social activism]], including their work on behalf of children. In 2007, Ha*Ash founded the nonprofit organization Fondo Ha*Ash, which supports [[Immigration|immigrants]] and [[Children's rights|children]] suffering from HIV/AIDS, among other causes.

== Early lives ==
Hanna Nicole Pérez (June 25, 1985) and Ashley Grace Pérez (January 27, 1987), were born in [[Lake Charles, Louisiana|Lake Charles]], Louisiana, United States, to Antonio and Mathilda Pérez ({{née|Mosa}}).<ref name=":lideresmexicanos">{{Cite web|title=Hanna y Ashley: Adictas a los aplausos|url=https://lideresmexicanos.com/entrevistas/hanna-ashley-adictas-a-los-aplausos/|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210112232003/https://lideresmexicanos.com/entrevistas/hanna-ashley-adictas-a-los-aplausos/|archive-date=January 12, 2021|access-date=March 2, 2019|website=Líderes Mexicanos}}</ref> Their grandfather is of [[Mexican American|Mexican]] descent,<ref name=":lideresmexicanos"/> originally from [[San Luis Potosí]], and their grandmothers were of German and Spanish ancestry. The family had been living in Lake Charles for generations.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/noticia/83141.ha-ash-presenta-pruebas-de-edad.html|title=Ha-Ash presenta pruebas de edad|date=April 13, 2004|website=[[El Siglo de Torreón]]|language=es|access-date=July 4, 2019|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210112225517/https://www.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/noticia/83141.ha-ash-presenta-pruebas-de-edad.html|archive-date=January 12, 2021}}</ref> Hanna and Ashley have two siblings and two half-siblings,<ref name=":lideresmexicanos" /> but due to their proximity in age, the two sisters grew up closer to one another than to any of their siblings.

They began performing in church (Pine Grove Baptist Church of [[DeQuincy, Louisiana|DeQuincy]]) at the ages of 5 and 4.<ref name=":billboardhaash">{{Cite web|title=Ha*Ash's Global Reach Highlighted by Music Dreams & Streams|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8455338/ha-ash-interview|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210112224331/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8455338/ha-ash-interview|archive-date=January 12, 2021|access-date=June 18, 2019|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|language=es}}</ref><ref name=":chron.com" /> Because their father worked outside the United States, Hanna and Ashley spent half of each year in [[Mexico City]] and half in Lake Charles.<ref name=":lideresmexicanos" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Ha*Ash: Las hermanas que pasaron de tocar en una prisión gringa a recorrer el mundo|url=https://www.publimetro.cl/cl/musica/2015/08/20/haash-hermanas-mexicanas-que-pasaron-tocar-prision-gringa-recorrer-mundo.html|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201021042606/https://www.publimetro.cl/cl/musica/2015/08/20/haash-hermanas-mexicanas-que-pasaron-tocar-prision-gringa-recorrer-mundo.html|archive-date=October 21, 2020|access-date=February 26, 2019|website=[[Metro International|Publimetro Chile]]|language=es}}</ref> Around 2001, they sang in the music festivals "Railroad Festival" and "The Louisiana Music Cavalcade".<ref name=":lideresmexicanos" />

== Career ==
=== 2002–2004: Formation and self-titled album ===
In April 2002, the sisters adopted the name Ha*Ash and signed to [[Sony Music Latin]] when they were 16 and 15. The same year, they signed a management deal with Seitrack Management ([[OCESA Seitrack]]).<ref name=":seitrackus"/> In April 2002 they released their first single, "[[Odio Amarte]]", which was a hit on Mexican radio stations, TV channels, and festivals.<ref name="Ha-Ash">{{Cite web|title=Ha-Ash|url=http://www.top10.com.mx/haash.htm|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190214051521/http://www.top10.com.mx/haash.htm|archive-date=February 14, 2019|access-date=December 26, 2018|website=www.top10.com.mx|language=es}}</ref> They recorded their self-titled debut album ''[[Ha*Ash (album)|Ha*Ash]]'' with the Mexican producer Áureo Baqueiro.<ref name=":seitrackus"/> The album was released on May 11, 2003, and peaked at No. 3 in the Mexican album charts and No. 19 in the US [[Latin Pop Albums|''Billboard'' Latin Pop Albums]] chart.<ref name=":LatinPopAlbums">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/haash/chart-history/LPP|title=Ha*Ash Chart History – Latin Pop Albums|website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=September 7, 2019}}</ref>
