'''Roland Collins''' (born November 23, 1985), better known by the [[stage name]] '''Troy Ave''', is an [[Americans|American]] [[rapper]] from the [[Crown Heights, Brooklyn|Crown Heights]] neighborhood of [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City]]. His moniker derives from Troy Avenue, a street near his childhood home.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.vice.com/read/we-interviewed-troy-ave |title=We Interviewed Troy Ave |author=Drew Millard |date=2012-06-13 |access-date=2013-11-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120616043125/http://www.vice.com/read/we-interviewed-troy-ave |archive-date=June 16, 2012 |quote=He derives his name from Troy Ave, the street he grew up on in Crown Heights. |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref> In November 2013, his first [[studio album]], ''[[New York City: The Album]]'', was released. It debuted at number 47 on the US ''Billboard'' [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart.<ref>{{cite web
|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/r-b-hip-hop-albums?page=4
|title=Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums
|publisher=Billboard
|date=2013-01-01
|access-date=2013-11-15}}</ref> He was named in ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' magazine's 2014 Freshmen Class.<ref>{{cite web|last=Sia|first=Niccole|title=XXL Freshmen 2014 Cover Revealed|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2014/05/xxl-freshmen-2014-cover-revealed/|publisher=XXL Magazine|access-date=6 May 2014}}</ref>

==Career==
Collins began his rap career in 2009.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k0epC7E-uN4 |title=Troy Ave Biography |website=Hotnewhiphop |access-date=May 28, 2016}}</ref>

Troy Ave announced that his new street album, ''Dope Boy Troy Vol. 2: NuPac'' was released on April 14, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vladtv.com/article/226712/troy-ave-to-drop-new-album-april-14th-called-nupac|title=Troy Ave to Drop New Album April 14th Called "NuPac"|website=vladtv.com|access-date=April 20, 2017}}</ref>

==Controversy==
===Legal issues===
On May 26, 2016, Collins was arrested for attempted murder and illegal weapon possession for shooting at a [[T.I.]] concert.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/05/27/nyregion/rapper-troy-ave-is-arrested-in-fatal-shooting-at-ti-concert.html?&moduleDetail=section-news-1&action=click&contentCollection=N.Y.%20%2F%20Region&region=Footer&module=MoreInSection&version=WhatsNext&contentID=WhatsNext&pgtype=article|title=Rapper Troy Ave Is Arrested After Fatal Shooting at T.I. Concert|work=[[New York Times]]|access-date=May 27, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/manhattan/nypd-close-arrest-deadly-shooting-irving-plaza-article-1.2650743|title=SEE IT: Rapper Troy Ave arrested after deadly shooting at T.I. concert at Irving Plaza|publisher=New York Daily News|access-date=May 27, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.hiphopearly.com/troy-ave-arrested-for-opening-fire-at-t-i-concert/|title=Troy Ave arrested for opening fire at T.I concert|publisher=Hip Hop Early|access-date=May 27, 2016}}</ref> During the event at [[Irving Plaza]], where T.I. and others were scheduled to perform, four people were shot, one fatally, by a shooter from the VIP section of the event. The man who died, Ronald McPhatter, 33, was said to be a friend of Collins, who was first taken to [[NYU Langone Medical Center]] and then transferred to nearby [[Bellevue Hospital|NYC Health + Hospitals/Bellevue]] where he was arrested as a primary suspect in the shooting.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abc7ny.com/news/video-shows-gunman-at-rap-concert;-troy-ave-arrested/1356567/|title=Rapper Troy Ave arrested after shooting during T.I. concert at Irving Plaza in Manhattan |website=[[WABC-TV]]|access-date=27 May 2016}}</ref><ref name="Shooting">{{cite web |url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/troy-aves-court-date-pushed-back-as-he-undergoes-surgery-for-shooting-himself-in-the-leg-news.21900.html? |title=Troy's Ave wounded self-inflicted |last=Walker |first=Angus |date=May 29, 2016 |website=Hotnewhiphop |access-date=May 30, 2016}}</ref> While at the hospital, it was alleged that the gunshot wound was self-inflicted.<ref name="Shooting"/>

Collins pleaded not guilty, saying he was using self-defense and that he and McPhatter were shot by an unnamed assailant. He was [[Remand (court procedure)|remanded]] without bail.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/troy-ave-pleaded-not-guilty-to-irving-plaza-shooting-remanded-without-bail/ |title=Troy Ave remanded without bail |last= <!--BrooklynVegan Staff--> |date=May 31, 2016 |website=[[BrooklynVegan]]|access-date=June 4, 2016}}</ref> His lawyer then put out a request for the public to help with any information that witnesses can provide.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/06/police-social-media-helping-troy-ave-case/ |title=Troy Ave's lawyer puts a request to help rapper's case with any information |last=Simmons |first=Ted |date=June 3, 2016 |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=June 3, 2016}}</ref>

Collins dropped a single called "Chuck Norris (Hoes & Gangstas)" and a mixtape called ''Free Troy Ave'', in reference to his current condition of being charged with shootings.<ref name="Mixtape release">{{cite news |last=Simmons |first=Ted |date=June 6, 2016 |title=Troy Ave changes title of mixtape due to predicament |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/06/free-troy-ave-mixtape-dropping-today/ |newspaper=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=June 6, 2016}}</ref> It was released on June 6, 2016.<ref name="Mixtape release"/> On July 11, 2016, he made the final bail and was temporarily released.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/nyc-crime/troy-ave-free-bail-authorities-investigate-fatal-shooting-article-1.2707296|title=Troy Ave free on bail|publisher=New York Daily News|access-date=July 12, 2016}}</ref>

===Feud with Joey Badass===
In June 2015, tensions began brewing between Troy Ave and a fellow Brooklyn-native rapper [[Joey Badass]], when the latter declared himself the "#1 [[Independent music|independent]] hip hop recording artist and a brand in the world" in a series of [[Twitter|tweets]], to which the former responded in another tweet by pointing out that he has been signed to [[Cinematic Music Group]], whose music is distributed by [[RED Distribution]], a subsidiary of [[Sony Music Entertainment]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2194241/joey-badass-troy-ave-indie/ |title=Joey Bada$$ And Troy Ave Are Trying To Out-Indie Each Other |date=June 23, 2015 |first=Nadeska |last=Alexis |website=[[MTV News]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref> Joey Badass later refuted reports of a feud between him and Troy Ave in another tweet.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-juice/6605777/troy-ave-joey-badass-twitter-beef-independent-hip-hop |title=Troy Ave & Joey Badass Duke It Out On Twitter Over Who Runs the (Independent) Rap Game |date=June 23, 2015 |first=Natalie |last=Wiener |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref>

On July 7, 2015, in an interview with [[Ebro Darden]] on [[Hot 97]], Joey Badass had words of praise for Troy Ave, stating: "At some point you gotta [sic] have some level of respect for Troy because, at the end of the day, we're both independent artists who are standing on major platforms next to a whole bunch of major artists and they know who we are."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/joey-badass-talks-troy-ave-tweets-and-steez-day-festival-on-ebro-in-the-morning-new-video.34488.html |title=Joey Bada$$ Talks Troy Ave Tweets & Steez Day Festival On Ebro In The Morning |date=July 7, 2015 |first=Kevin |last=Goddard |website=HotNewHipHop |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref> In February 2016, he reignited the feud in a single, titled "Ready", where he raps: "60k, first week for the badass / 200k to this day, I know you niggas mad / With that 80-20 split, my nigga do the math / my nigga Kirk just outsold Troy Ave" (in reference to [[Kirk Knight]]'s debut studio album ''[[Late Knight Special]]'' and Troy Ave's second studio album ''[[Major Without a Deal]]'').<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2016/02/joey-badass-ready/ |title=Joey Badass Throws Shots at Troy Ave on "Ready" |date=February 22, 2016 |first=Justin |last=Ivey |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/editorials/id.3239/title.joey-bada-troy-ave-beef-5-things-we-learned |title=Joey Bada$$ & Troy Ave Beef: 5 Things We Learned |date=March 6, 2016 |first=Hugh |last=Least |website=[[HipHopDX]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref> In response, Troy Ave released a [[diss (music)|diss track]], titled "Badass" the following week, in which he not only insulted Joey Badass, but also targeted fellow [[Pro Era]] member [[Capital Steez]], who committed [[suicide]] by jumping off a building in December 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.laweekly.com/music/joey-bada-and-pro-era-turn-tragedy-into-celebration-with-their-annual-steez-day-festival-7093577 |title=Joey Bada$$ and Pro Era Turn Tragedy Into Celebration With Their Annual Steez Day Festival |date=July 5, 2016 |first=Tara |last=Mahadevan |website=[[LA Weekly]] |access-date=August 30, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/11/26/capital-steez-king-capital |title=Capital STEEZ: King Capital |date=November 26, 2013 |first=Eli |last=Rosenberg |website=[[The Fader]] |access-date=August 30, 2016}}</ref>

Following the release of "Badass", Troy Ave called in at ''Sway in the Morning'' on [[Shade 45]], stating: "He didn't pass away; he killed himself. There's a difference. He took his own life. God gave you life, it ain't your right to take that. That's a fact. I got niggas in jail who got life sentences, they might as well be dead. They could've traded their life for his."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/03/troy-ave-joey-badass-capital-steez-lyrics/ |title=Troy Ave Speaks on Capital Steez Diss: "Don't Play With Me" |date=March 1, 2016 |first=Sidney |last=Madden |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=September 1, 2016}}</ref> He then received heavy criticism from fans and fellow artists alike, including [[Styles P]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allhiphop.com/2016/03/03/troy-ave-apologizes-for-suicide-comments-responds-to-joey-bada-exposing-his-old-tweets-about-nas/ |title=Troy Ave Apologizes For Suicide Comments & Responds To Joey Bada$$ Exposing His Old Tweets About Nas |date=March 3, 2016 |first=Yohance |last=Kyles |website=[[AllHipHop]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2016/03/03/styles-p-educates-troy-ave-on-twitter-regarding-his-suicide-comments/ |title=Styles P Educates Troy Ave On Twitter Regarding His Suicide Comments |date=March 3, 2016 |first=Rocko |last=Rathon |website=[[The Source]] |access-date=September 1, 2016}}</ref> ASAP Ant and [[ASAP Twelvyy]], who wrote, amongst a series of other tweets: "Wish I had a time machine to go back and give these niggas moms [sic] that abortion money."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allhiphop.com/2016/03/02/asap-mob-twelvy-ant-troy-ave-joey-badass-capital-steez/ |title=A$AP Mob Members Blast Troy Ave For Disrespecting Capital STEEZ; Troy Defends Dissing The Late Rapper |date=March 2, 2016 |first=Yohance |last=Kyles |website=[[AllHipHop]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref>

On March 29, 2016, in a [[Freestyle rap|freestyle]] on ''Sway in the Morning'', Joey Badass responded to "Badass" in a series of lines aimed at Troy Ave, though never referring to him directly. Among other lines, he rapped: "Now I won't even lie, can't agree with suicide / but in the darkest times, there's still a brighter side / He took a leap of faith and only brightened his light / You took a cheap shot at hate and only shortened your life."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/2859315/joey-badass-troy-ave-response-5-fingers-of-death-sway/ |title=Joey Bada$$ To Troy Ave: "You Committed Career Suicide" |date=March 30, 2016 |first=Adam |last=Fleischer |website=[[MTV News]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2016/03/joey-badss-claps-back-at-troy-ave-during-his-5-fingers-of-death-freestyle |title=Joey Bada$$ Claps Back at Troy Ave During His "5 Fingers of Death" Freestyle |date=March 30, 2016 |first=Chris |last=Mench |website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] |access-date=September 1, 2016}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[HipHopDX]]'' the following day, [[Fat Joe]] stated that he personally reached out to both Joey Badass and Troy Ave to try and squash the feud, but failed.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.38123/title.fat-joe-tried-to-stop-joey-bada-troy-ave-beef |title=Fat Joe Tried To Stop Joey Bada$$ & Troy Ave Beef |date=March 30, 2016 |first=Justin |last=Hunte |website=[[HipHopDX]] |access-date=August 31, 2016}}</ref>
