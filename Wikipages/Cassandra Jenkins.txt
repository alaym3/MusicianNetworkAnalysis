'''Cassandra Jenkins''' is an American musician, singer and songwriter based in [[Brooklyn]], [[New York (state)|New York]], United States.

==Early life and education==
Cassandra Jenkins was born into a musical New York family. Her parents played on cruise ships in the 1980s.<ref name="Jagota">{{Cite web|last=Jagota|first=Vrinda|date=1 March 2021|title=Cassandra Jenkins’ Hypnotic Folk Music Turns Everyday Moments Into Monuments|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/cassandra-jenkins-an-overview-on-phenomenal-nature-interview/|url-status=live|access-date=1 March 2021|website=Pitchfork.com}}</ref> She learned how to play guitar and sing at a young age. Before the age of 12, she toured with her family band playing folk music at festivals.

Jenkins graduated from the [[Rhode Island School of Design]] in 2006, where she studied visual arts.<ref name="Jagota"/> For two years, she worked as an editorial assistant at ''[[The New Yorker]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Dickinson|first=Chrissie|date=14 February 2018|title=Cassandra Jenkins steps into the limelight with 'Red Lips' video|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-ott-cassandra-jenkins-0216-story.html|url-status=live|access-date=22 February 2021|newspaper=[[Chicago Tribune]]}}</ref>

== Career ==
Jenkins released her debut EP, titled ''EP'', in April 2013,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Barlow|first=Matthew|date=18 February 2021|title=Cassandra Jenkins — An Overview on Phenomenal Nature|url=http://thetypescript.com/cassandra-jenkins-an-overview-on-phenomenal-nature/|url-status=live|access-date=22 February 2021|website=The Typescript}}</ref> with an album titled ''Play Till You Win'' following in 2017<ref>{{Cite web|last=Putnam|first=Jon|date=7 April 2017|title=Cassandra Jenkins takes on invigorating country rock with moody synths on her debut LP|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/reviews/albums/cassandra-jenkins-play-till-you-win|url-status=live|access-date=22 February 2021|website=The Line of Best Fit}}</ref> and receiving positive reviews.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Dickinson|first=Chrissie|date=2018-02-14|title=Cassandra Jenkins steps into the limelight with 'Red Lips' video|url=https://zephr.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/ct-ott-cassandra-jenkins-0216-story.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-23|newspaper=Chicago Tribune}} "[...] the album has earned her numerous critical raves as an evocative vocalist."</ref> Her second album, ''An Overview on Phenomenal Nature'', was released to critical acclaim in February 2021, featuring production by multi-instrumentalist [[Josh Kaufman (musician)|Josh Kaufman]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Leas|first=Ryan|date=19 February 2021|title=Stream Cassandra Jenkins’ Stunning New Album An Overview On Phenomenal Nature|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2116903/stream-cassandra-jenkins-an-overview-on-phenomenal-nature/music/#card-comments-section|url-status=live|access-date=22 February 2021|website=Stereogum.com}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Sodomsky|first=Sam|date=19 February 2021|title=Cassandra Jenkins - An Overview on Phenomenal Nature (album review)|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/cassandra-jenkins-an-overview-on-phenomenal-nature/|url-status=live|access-date=22 February 2021|website=Pitchfork.com}}</ref>

Jenkins performed as a back-up member for [[Eleanor Friedberger]] and [[Craig Finn]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=Welford|first=Ali|date=18 February 2021|title=Album Review: Cassandra Jenkins - An Overview on Phenomenal Nature|url=https://narcmagazine.com/album-review-cassandra-jenkins-an-overview-on-phenomenal-nature/|url-status=live|access-date=22 February 2021|website=Narcmagazine.com}}</ref> and was scheduled to tour with [[Purple Mountains]] before the death of [[David Berman (musician)|David Berman]] in 2019.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Bloom|first=Madison|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/david-berman-silver-jews-purple-mountains-dead-at-52/|title=David Berman (Silver Jews, Purple Mountains) dead at 52|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|date=7 August 2019|access-date=24 May 2021|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210117040444/https://pitchfork.com/news/david-berman-silver-jews-purple-mountains-dead-at-52|archive-date=17 January 2021}}</ref>
