'''Julien Doré''' ({{IPA-fr|ʒyljɛ̃ dɔʁe}}; born 7 July 1982) is a French singer-songwriter, musician and actor.

He is the winner of the fifth season of the television show ''[[Nouvelle Star]]'', aired on the French Television [[Métropole Télévision|M6]] channel. He is also the great-great-grandson of [[Gustave Doré]], a well-known illustrator of the 19th century.

==Life==
Doré was born in [[Alès]] ([[Gard]], [[Occitania (administrative region)|Occitanie]]), and grew up in [[Lunel, Hérault|Lunel]]. After taking his [[:fr:Baccalauréat littéraire|baccalauréat littéraire]] at the college [[Louis Feuillade]], he attended the [[École des Beaux-Arts]] of [[Nîmes]] for five years. He is the founder of the band Dig up [[Elvis]]. In 2006, he created with Guillaume de Molina the project "The [[Jean d'Ormesson]] disco suicide", "protean group" which covered pop and disco hits. In June 2007, Doré was voted number one in French ''[[Elle (magazine)|Elle]]'' Magazine's "15 Sexiest Men" poll. He dated [[Louise Bourgoin]], but the two separated in March 2009.

==''Nouvelle Star''==
In 2007, Doré was selected at the [[Casting (performing arts)|casting]] session in [[Marseille]] for the fifth season of the music competition ''[[Nouvelle Star]]'', where he presented himself with a ukulele. At the beginning, he only participated in the casting to get free publicity for his band. He convinced the jury when he sang "A la faveur de l'automne" (originally by [[Tété]]). His most memorable performances and revelations were while singing "[[Moi... Lolita]]" ([[Alizée]]) and "[[...Baby One More Time (song)|...Baby One More Time]]" ([[Britney Spears]]), [[dance music]] songs, in acoustics. He won the finale against [[Tigane (singer)|Tigane]].

==Career==
Doré has had a successful musical career after winning ''Nouvelle Star'' with three studio albums, ''[[Ersatz (album)|Ersatz]]'' (2008), ''[[Bichon (album)|Bichon]]'' (2011) and ''[[Løve (Julien Doré album)|Løve]]'' (2013), all reaching top four on the [[SNEP|official French Albums Chart]]. He has also gained chart success in Belgium and Switzerland. 
In 2009, he recorded a duet with Cœur de Pirate of her song, "Pour un infidèle." A video was released to promote the song, showing the two as a 1960s-era celebrity couple.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=heI6UT55ZZ0</ref> Also in 2009, Doré recorded the song "Helsinki" with French singer [[Mélanie Pain]]. Doré also collaborated with French chanteuse [[Sylvie Vartan]] on her 2010 album release, ''Soleil bleu.''<ref>Lemancel, Anne-Laure. "[http://www.rfimusique.com/musiquefr/articles/131/article_18151.asp Sylvie Vartan, toujours le même âge]." RFI Musique, 3 December 2010. Retrieved 20 June 2011.</ref>

He was a guest coach for candidates [[Quentin Bruno]] and Law on Season 4 of ''[[The Voice: la plus belle voix]]'' the French version of the reality television singing competition [[The Voice (TV series)|''The Voice'']].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.coulisses-tv.fr/index.php/dossier-the-voice/les-vid%C3%A9os/item/5858-replay-%E2%80%9Cthe-voice%E2%80%9D-la-battle-law-quentin-bruno-sur-%C2%AB-chou-wasabi-%C2%BB-de-julien-dor%C3%A9-vid%C3%A9o |title=The Voice : la Battle Law' / Quentin Bruno sur « Chou Wasabi de Julien Doré |publisher=Coulisses de la TV |author=Verdrel, Jean-Marc|date=28 February 2015 |language=fr}}</ref>
