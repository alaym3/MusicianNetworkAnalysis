'''Broods''' is a New Zealand musical duo from [[Nelson, New Zealand|Nelson]], composed of Georgia Josiena Nott on lead vocals, with older brother and multi-instrumentalist Caleb Allan Joseph Nott on production and backing vocals. They released the single "[[Bridges (Broods song)|Bridges]]", which went to No. 8 on the New Zealand singles chart, and signed with [[Capitol Records|Capitol]] and [[Polydor Records]]. They released their self-titled debut EP, ''[[Broods (album)|Broods]]'', on 30 January 2014, which was followed by a full-length album, ''[[Evergreen (Broods album)|Evergreen]]'', on 22 August 2014. The band has toured with [[Ellie Goulding]], [[Haim (band)|Haim]], [[Sam Smith]], [[Tove Lo]] and [[Taylor Swift]]. They have won ten [[New Zealand Music Awards]]. On 24 June 2016, Broods released their second album, ''[[Conscious (Broods album)|Conscious]]''. They released their third album ''[[Don't Feed the Pop Monster]]'' on 1 February 2019.

==History==
===2010–2013: Formation and early years===
Born in [[Nelson, New Zealand]], Caleb and Georgia Nott have performed together since childhood.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Video: Broods On George FM Breakfast |publisher=[[George FM]] |url=http://www.georgefm.co.nz/Video-Broods-On-George-FM-Breakfast/tabid/238/articleID/161530/Default.aspx |access-date=6 December 2013 }}</ref> They grew up in a musical family with three other siblings, and would go along with their parents to weddings and church performances.<ref name="interview"/><ref>{{Cite news |first=Tom |last=Lanham |date=5 March 2015 |title=Broods' Georgia Nott can't deny her pop roots |newspaper=[[The San Francisco Examiner]] |url=http://www.sfexaminer.com/sanfrancisco/broods-georgia-nott-cant-deny-her-pop-roots/Content?oid=2922271 |access-date=10 April 2015 }}</ref> They won a talent competition, "Richmond's Got Talent", as teenagers at Richmond Mall in 2010.<ref>{{Cite web |first=Josh |last=Reich |date=4 December 2010 |title= Winning contest was music to the ears of family duo |newspaper=[[The Nelson Mail]] |publisher=[[Stuff.co.nz]] |url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/nelson-mail/news/3573012/Winning-contest-was-music-to-the-ears-of-family-duo/ |access-date=29 January 2014 }}</ref> While attending [[Garin College]], they were members of the indie rock band, The Peasants, who won the [[Smokefreerockquest]] music competition in 2011. The band split in late 2012, shortly after winning a grant from [[NZ On Air]]. Georgia began studying popular music at the [[University of Auckland]].<ref>{{cite news |first=Sarah |last=Dunn |date=6 December 2013 |title=Nelson duo signed by US label |work=Stuff.co.nz |url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/entertainment/music/9486581/Nelson-duo-signed-by-US-label |access-date=6 December 2013 }}</ref> Caleb studied [[industrial design]].<ref>{{cite journal |title=Broods Co-Founder Caleb Nott Talks New Zealand, Lorde And Getting Along With His Talented Sister, Georgia |first=Alex M. |last=Smith |date=22 October 2014 |journal=Music Times |url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/13115/20141022/broods-co-founder-caleb-nott-talks-new-zealand-lorde-making.htm |access-date=23 April 2015}}</ref> They both dropped out of university to pursue Broods.<ref name="white">{{Cite web |title=An Interview With Georgia Nott of Broods |first=Caitlin |last=White |date=10 March 2014 |publisher=[[Noisey]] |url=http://noisey.vice.com/blog/broods-interview |access-date=13 March 2014}}</ref>

Broods was formed in [[Auckland]] in early 2013. The brother-sister project began collaborating with music producer [[Joel Little]], whom they met as a judge at the 2011 Smokefreerockquest. He produced [[Lorde]]'s 2013 hit single, "[[Royals (song)|Royals]]".<ref name="marlborough-express">{{Cite news |date=29 January 2014 |title=Little's success a thrill |newspaper=[[The Marlborough Express]] |url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/marlborough-express/news/9661111/Littles-success-a-thrill |via=Stuff.co.nz |access-date=29 January 2014 }}</ref> While considering names for the band, their manager suggested Broods.<ref name="interview">{{Cite news |first=Holly |last=Rubenstein |title=Broods, Thicker Than Water |magazine=[[Interview (magazine)|Interview]] |url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/broods |access-date=1 February 2014 }}</ref> The band liked the double meaning, relating to family and a feeling of brooding in their music.<ref name="white"/> They released their debut single, "[[Bridges (Broods song)|Bridges]]", online in October 2013.<ref>{{Cite news |first=John |last=Sakamoto |date=6 December 2013 |title= Why the next Lorde could be a 50-year-old guy |newspaper=[[Toronto Star]] |url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2013/12/06/why_the_next_lorde_could_be_a_50yearold_guy.html |access-date=6 December 2013 }}</ref> The song was picked up by [[Idolator (website)|Idolator]] and other [[music blog]]s, earning over 200,000 streams in a week.<ref>{{Cite news |first=Jason |last=Lipshutz |date=13 November 2013 |title= Is Broods New Zealand's Next Pop Wonder? Duo Taps Lorde's Producer For Stunning First Single |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5785941/is-broods-new-zealands-next-pop-wonder-duo-taps-lordes-producer |access-date=6 December 2013 }}</ref> 
[[MTV]] described the track as "a bittersweet synth-pop echo chamber".<ref>{{Cite web |first=John |last=Walker |date=14 October 2013 |title=Broods Is A Synth-Pop Duo You Need To Listen To Immediately! |publisher=[[MTV Buzzworthy]] |url=http://buzzworthy.mtv.com/2013/10/14/broods-bridges/ |access-date=7 December 2013 }}</ref> Broods signed with [[Capitol Records]] worldwide and [[Polydor]] in the UK and Europe in December 2013.<ref>{{Cite news |first=Jason |last=Lipshutz |date=5 December 2013 |title=New Zealand's Broods Signs To Capitol In U.S., Preps Debut Album with Lorde's Producer |magazine=Billboard |url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/legal-and-management/5819663/new-zealands-broods-signs-to-capitol-in-us-preps |access-date=6 December 2013 |archive-date=5 March 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140305185938/http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/legal-and-management/5819663/new-zealands-broods-signs-to-capitol-in-us-preps |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===2016–2018: ''Conscious'' and solo projects===
Broods began writing material for a follow up album immediately after releasing their debut. ===2018–present: ''Don't Feed the Pop Monster''===
On 8 August 2018, Broods released "Peach" – the first single from their third studio album ''[[Don't Feed the Pop Monster]].''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://libel.co.nz/home/broods-return-with-new-song-peach|title=Broods return with new song 'Peach'|website=Libel Music Australia & New Zealand|language=en-NZ|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> The music video was released on 5 September and offered the first glimpse into the new image that Broods would be presenting.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/art-books-music/a22987819/broods-peach-music-video/|title=Broods' "Peach" Video Is Lovably Bizarre and We Can't Look Away|last=Gonzales|first=Erica|date=5 September 2018|website=Harper's BAZAAR|language=en-US|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref> This new image was created by Georgia and Caleb after they were dropped by their label, [[Capitol Records]], following Broods' second album, ''[[Conscious (Broods album)|Conscious]].''<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/news/article.cfm?c_id=1501119&objectid=12102564|title=Broods on being axed by their label, battling depression, and their 'weird' new album|last=george.fenwick@nzherald.co.nz|first=George Fenwick George Fenwick is an entertainment writer for The New Zealand Herald|date=8 August 2018|access-date=2 August 2019|language=en-NZ|issn=1170-0777}}</ref> Previously, the creative direction of Broods had been mainly controlled by their label, however, this time around the siblings had full control over how they wanted to present themselves. The band released their third album ''Don't Feed the Pop Monster'' on 1 February 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hunt |first1=El |title=Broods – 'Don't Feed the Pop Monster' review |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/album/2441893 |website=NME |access-date=13 May 2019}}</ref>
