'''Jacquelyn "Jaci" Davette Velasquez''' (Spanish ''Jaci Velásquez'', born October 15, 1979) is an American actress and [[contemporary Christian music|Contemporary Christian]] and [[Latin pop]] singer and songwriter, performing in both English and Spanish.

Velasquez has sold almost three million albums in the US,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1045877/ask-billboard|title=Ask Billboard|work=Billboard|date=April 11, 2008|access-date=December 30, 2011}}</ref> recorded three Platinum and three Gold albums, and recorded 16 singles that hit No. 1 plus six more that entered the top 10.<ref name=FamilyFriendlyMorningShowAnnouncement /> She received a Latin Billboard Music Award in 2002 for her album "Mi Corazón", eight [[Dove Award]]s, including Best New Artist and Female Vocalist of the Year,<ref name=FamilyFriendlyMorningShowAnnouncement /> as well as three Grammy nominations, and an American Music Award nomination in the category of "Favorite Latin Artist" in 2002.<ref name="Jaci Velasquez - Biography">{{cite web|url=http://www.jacivelasquez.com/about.html|title=Jaci Velasquez - Biography|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141220230334/http://www.jacivelasquez.com/about.html|archive-date=December 20, 2014|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

Since late 2010, Velasquez has co-hosted a morning radio show with Doug Griffin.

==Biography==
===Early life===
Velasquez was born in [[Houston]], Texas. She is of Mexican descent.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.buenamusica.com/jaci-velasquez/biografia |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130403171751/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-113053490.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=April 3, 2013 |title=Jaci Velasquez: Chasing Papi star with a blazing trail of album hits – Main Cover – Cover Story |publisher=Findarticles.com |access-date=February 14, 2012}}</ref> She grew up in an evangelical church where her parents were both singers and pastors. Although she had described herself as a "[[non-denominational]]", she later stated in an interview with [[Morris Cerullo]] that "you're talking to an [[Assemblies of God]]'s girl".{{Citation needed|date=January 2011}}

At the age of nine, she began traveling with her father, who by that time was a full-time singing evangelist. She began by singing backup, then later sang solo, releasing three independent records. It was while on the road that she was discovered by a representative of [[Myrrh Records]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Jaci Velasquez Bio|url=http://www.musicianguide.com/biographies/1608002928/Jaci-Velasquez.html|publisher=Net Industries|access-date=September 22, 2011}}</ref>

===Music career===
{{BLP sources section|date=September 2011}}
Velasquez released her national debut album ''[[Heavenly Place]]'' on May 13, 1996; five of its songs became No. 1 hits. The album was later certified Platinum. Velasquez has been distinguished as the fastest selling solo debut recording artist in her genre and the first solo artist in Christian music history to reach gold status with a debut album. She has since released seven more English-language albums, two of which were certified Gold. Five Latin albums garnishing amazing success with [[Llegar A Ti]] going double-platinum and [[Mi Corazón]] going platinum. She has produced sixteen No. 1 Christian radio hits and six other top-10 singles, and has won nine Dove awards, including New Artist of the Year and two Female Vocalist of the Year wins. Velasquez has also done ad campaigns for [[Pepsi]], [[Target Corporation|Target]], [[Doritos]], John Freida's Frizz-Ease hair products and [[Helzberg Diamonds]].<ref name="Jaci Velasquez - Biography"/>

In 1998, she signed to [[Sony Music Latin|Sony Discos]] for a four-album deal and was introduced to [[Rudy Pérez]], who produced her first Spanish-language crossover album ''Llegar a Ti'' which topped the [[Billboard magazine|Billboard]] Hot Latin Tracks chart, the first Christian track to top that chart of its kind. This album enabled Velasquez to achieve crossover success by building a fan base among Hispanics. Her first single, "Llegar a Ti," topped the Billboard Hot Latin Tracks and became the first Christian song to top that chart. The song also gave her a [[Latin Grammy Award]] nomination for Best  Female Pop Vocal Performance.
[[File:Jacivelasquez.JPG|thumb|upright|left|Velasquez in 2001]]
In 2001, she released her second Spanish-language album, ''Mi Corazón''. The album's lead single, "Como Se Cura Una Herida," topped the ''[[Billboard magazine|Billboard]]'' Hot Latin Tracks and became one of the biggest singles of the year, receiving multiple award nominations and wins. The album received a [[Grammy Award]] nomination for Best Latin Pop Album. "Como Se Cura Una Herida" charted at No. 1 simultaneously with the song "Adore" at No. 1 on the CCM (Inspirational) chart giving Velasquez two simultaneous No. 1s in two different markets.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.christianactivities.com/jaci-velasquez-no-1-both-english-latin-charts|title=Jaci Velasquez No. 1 on Both English, Latin Charts - Christian Activities|website=www.christianactivities.com|access-date=June 6, 2018}}</ref>

Her Latin music success includes five [[Spanish language|Spanish-language]] releases, two No. 1 Latin ''Billboard'' Singles, two Platinum albums, a Premio Lo Nuestro Award for New Artist of the Year, two ''Billboard'' Latin Music Awards, and four [[Dove Awards]] for Spanish Language Album of the Year.

In September 2008, Velasquez was chosen by the State Department's Bureau of International Information Programs as one of the 15 Prominent Hispanic-Americans in the Arts in America.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.america.gov/multimedia/photogallery.html#/30145/hispanic_arts |title=Photo Gallery |publisher=America.gov |date=June 30, 2008 |access-date=September 16, 2011}}</ref>

In August 2011, Velasquez signed with a new label, [[Inpop Records]], and her album, ''[[Diamond (Jaci Velasquez album)|Diamond]]'', was released on February 7, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.christiantoday.com/article/jaci.velasquez.signs.to.inpop.records/28482.htm |title=Jaci Velasquez signs to Inpop Records |publisher=Christian Today |date=August 22, 2011 |access-date=September 16, 2011}}</ref> Velasquez released her first Spanish-language lullaby album, ''Buenas noches mi sol'', in 2012 with Fisher-Price.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/BuenasNochesMiSol.asp|title=Jaci Velasquez - Buenas Noches mi Sol|access-date=March 28, 2017}}</ref> Velasquez released a new project on March 31, 2017 through [[Integrity Music]], consisting of an English-language album, ''Trust'', and the Spanish-language version, ''Confío''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/3049-platinum-selling-bilingual-recording-artist-jaci-velasquez-to-release-first-new-album-in-five-years|title=Jaci Velasquez To Release First New Album in Five Years|publisher=The Christian Beat|date=January 23, 2017 |access-date=March 28, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://musica.gospelprime.com.br/trust-jaci-velasquez|title=Jaci Velasquez releases the album Trust|publisher=GospelPrime|date=March 31, 2017 |access-date=March 31, 2017}}</ref> At the 48th [[Dove Awards]], ''Confío'' won Spanish Language Album of the Year<ref>{{cite web|url=http://doveawards.com/2017-winners/|title=2017 Winners - The 48th Annual GMA Dove Awards|website=doveawards.com}}</ref>

===Acting career===
Velasquez made her acting debut in the 2003 film ''[[Chasing Papi]]'', which also featured [[Sofia Vergara]], [[Roselyn Sanchez]], and [[Eduardo Verastegui]], as well as [[Nicole Scherzinger]] in a cameo role.  It was released nationwide in the United States in April 2003. The movie reached No. 11 at the box office, and grossed $6&nbsp;million worldwide. Velasquez also starred in the Christian films ''[[The Encounter (2011 film)|The Encounter]]'' and ''[[Jerusalem Countdown (film)|Jerusalem Countdown]]'', both released in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.todayschristianmusic.com/article/about-jaci-velasquez/|title=About Jaci Velasquez|publisher=Today's Christian Music/CCM Magazine}}</ref>

In November 2010, Velasquez began co-hosting the ''Family Friendly Morning Show'' with Doug Griffin after being a guest host for several months.<ref name=FamilyFriendlyMorningShowAnnouncement>{{cite web|title=Jaci Velasquez Announced As New Co-host of The Family Friendly Morning Show with Doug Griffin|url=http://www.todayschristianmusic.com/artists/jaci-velasquez/news/jaci-velasquez-announced-as-new-co-host-of-the-family-friendly-morning-show-with-doug-griffin/|publisher=Today's Christian Music/CCM Magazine|access-date=October 14, 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120330021027/http://www.todayschristianmusic.com/artists/jaci-velasquez/news/jaci-velasquez-announced-as-new-co-host-of-the-family-friendly-morning-show-with-doug-griffin/|archive-date=March 30, 2012|df=mdy-all}}</ref> The show airs on over 100 U.S. radio stations for four hours each weekday morning.<ref name=FamilyFriendlyMorningShowAnnouncement />

In 2016, she played the drama teacher Mrs. Diaz in ''[[I'm Not Ashamed]]'', a 2016 biographical drama film based on the journals of [[Rachel Joy Scott]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.patheos.com/blogs/reelfaith/2017/09/jaci-velasquez-question-faith-christian-entertainment.html|title=Jaci Velasquez on ‘A Question of Faith,' Christian Entertainment|last=Faith|first=Reel|date=2017-09-29|website=Reel Faith|language=en|access-date=2019-01-27}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Velasquez married Darren Potuck, of the band AutoVaughn, on August 16, 2003. They were divorced in 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/music/news/2005/jacivelasquez.html |title=Jaci Velasquez Divorced |publisher=Christianitytoday.com |date=August 16, 2003 |access-date=September 16, 2011}}</ref>

On December 17, 2006, she married Nic Gonzales in a private ceremony in [[Austin, Texas]]. Gonzales is a member of [[Salvador (band)|Salvador]], a Christian group with whom she had been touring.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lovetripper.com/newswire07/010707-jaci-velasquez.html |title=Austin Wedding for Jaci Velasquez, Nic Gonzalez |publisher=Lovetripper.com |date=December 17, 2006 |access-date=September 16, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120214060416/http://www.lovetripper.com/newswire07/010707-jaci-velasquez.html |archive-date=February 14, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Their first son Zealand was born in 2007 and their second son Soren in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2009/03/02/jaci-velasquez-and-nic-gonzales-welcome-son-soren-arthur/ |title=Jaci Velásquez and Nic Gonzáles Welcome Son Soren Arthur – Moms & Babies – Moms & Babies - People.com |publisher=Celebritybabies.people.com |date=March 2, 2009 |access-date=December 4, 2012}}</ref>

Velasquez and her husband were featured in an episode of the home remodeling reality TV show ''Renovation Realities'' which aired December 6, 2014 on the [[DIY Network]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.diynetwork.com/renovation-realities/the-velazquez-job/index.html |title=THE VELASQUEZ JOB Episode DRVD-1309H |publisher=DIYNetwork.com |access-date=January 29, 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141204053020/http://www.diynetwork.com/renovation-realities/the-velazquez-job/index.html |archive-date=December 4, 2014 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>
