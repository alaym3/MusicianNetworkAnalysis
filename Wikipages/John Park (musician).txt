'''John Andrew Park''' (born September 13, 1988) is an American singer of Korean descent.  He was a semi-finalist on the [[American Idol (season 9)|ninth season]] of ''[[American Idol]]''; placing twentieth. He is the runner-up of ''[[Superstar K2]]'', a Korean singing contest held by [[M.net]].  He is currently signed to Music Farm Entertainment.<ref name="college">{{cite news |url=http://kr.ibtimes.com/article/news/20110331/3729889.htm |title=John Park |work=IB Times |date=March 31, 2011 |access-date=May 21, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110722235302/http://kr.ibtimes.com/article/news/20110331/3729889.htm |archive-date=July 22, 2011 }}</ref>

==Biography==

===Early life===
John Park was born in Chicago; prior to entering ''Superstar K2'', he was a student at [[Northwestern University]] majoring in Economics.<ref name="northwestern">{{cite web |last=Kim |first=Yoonji |url=http://www.dailynorthwestern.com/john-park-finishes-second-in-superstar-k-1.2379947 |title=John Park finishes second in 'Superstar K' |work=Daily Northwestern.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927005610/http://www.dailynorthwestern.com/john-park-finishes-second-in-superstar-k-1.2379947 |archive-date=September 27, 2011 }}</ref> He was a member of his college [[a cappella]] group [[Purple Haze (a cappella group)|Purple Haze]].<ref name="northwestern" />
He was a semi-finalist on [[American Idol]].  He later auditioned for the Korean equivalent of "American Idol", [[Superstar k2]], and finished second.

===Education===
Park attended [[Glenbrook North High School]].  He is an Economics major at [[Northwestern University]].<ref name="tribune">{{cite news|url=http://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/chi-idol-park20100216141824,0,1775136.photo |title=John Park |work=Chicago Tribune | date=February 16, 2010 | access-date=November 6, 2010}}</ref> He is currently taking a break from college to pursue a singing career in Korea.<ref>{{cite web |last=Brown |first=Claire |url=http://www.dailynorthwestern.com/northwestern-s-john-park-reaches-finals-in-korean-version-of-american-idol-1.2373625 |title=Northwestern's John Park reaches finals in Korean version of "American Idol" |date=October 19, 2010 |work=Daily Northwestern |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101023223858/http://www.dailynorthwestern.com/northwestern-s-john-park-reaches-finals-in-korean-version-of-american-idol-1.2373625 |archive-date=October 23, 2010 }}</ref>

===Musical background===
He is a fan of [[Stevie Wonder]], [[John Legend]], [[Gavin DeGraw]], [[Marc Broussard]] and [[Jamie Cullum]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.johnapark.com/biography.html |title=John Park Biography |publisher=Johnapark.com |access-date=August 10, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140812210616/http://www.johnapark.com/biography.html |archive-date=August 12, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==''American Idol'' 9==
Park auditioned in Chicago for the ninth season of American Idol with "I Love You More Than You'll Ever Know" by Blood, Sweat and Tears. The judges were impressed in his audition especially [[Shania Twain]] and he made it to the Hollywood round.

He also performed "Sweet Escape" in the group round along with Haeley Vaughn, Jessica Furney and Kelsey Madsen who was cut that round. He made it to the semi-finals. During the top 24, he sang Billie Holiday's "[[God Bless the Child (Billie Holiday song)|God Bless the Child]]" and John Mayer's "[[Gravity (John Mayer song)|Gravity]]" for the top 20, but he was eliminated.
