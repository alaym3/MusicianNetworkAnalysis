'''Pretty Sick''' is an American [[indie rock]] band from [[New York City]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. It currently consists of vocalist and bassist Sabrina Fuentes, guitarist Wade Oates (formerly of [[The Virgins]]), drummer Austin Williamson, and bassist Orazio Argentero.<ref>{{cite web|last=Bates|first=Matt|url=https://primarytalent.com/pretty-sick/|title=Primary Talent International, Pretty Sick|website=primarytalent.com|access-date=2021-03-07}}</ref>

Despite the band's long presence and tight-knit fanbase, especially in the [[Lower East Side]] area, it is only in recent years that they have garnered recognition from major national publications such as [[i-D]],<ref>{{cite web|last=DeMarco|first=Nicole|url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/5dppza/interview-with-sabrina-feuntes-of-pretty-sick|title=Pretty Sick: the NYC rock band here to save us all|website=i-d.vice.com|date=29 October 2020|access-date=2021-03-07}}</ref> [[Nylon (magazine)]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nylon.com/entertainment/pretty-sick-deep-divine-ep-interview|title=Pretty Sick Makes Honest-to-god Rock For Your Inner Romantic|website=nylon.com|access-date=2021-03-07}}</ref> and [[Pigeons and Planes]].<ref name="P&P Int">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qyuqurDShAw|title=Making It: Pretty Sick Interview|website=youtube.com|access-date=2021-03-07}}</ref> In May 2020, Pretty Sick signed to the British record label [[Dirty Hit]], known for acts such as [[The 1975]] and [[beabadoobee]]. The group has primarily performed throughout the greater New York City area and more recently around [[London]].<ref name="P&P Int" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://garage.vice.com/en_us/article/qj479x/sabrina-fuentes-has-lived-through-this|title=Sabrina Fuentes Has Lived Through This|website=garage.vice.com|access-date=2021-05-29}}</ref> 

In February 2021, the band produced the [[theme song]] to the ongoing [[variety series]] "Story of my Fucking Life", created by Manon Macasaet.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theface.com/culture/manon-macasaet-story-of-my-fucking-life-new-york-film-sabrina-fuentes-pretty-sick|title=The lo-fi gem turning reality TV on its head|website=theface.com|access-date=2021-05-24}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Pretty Sick's music is now primarily rooted in [[alternative-rock]], [[grunge]], [[riot grrrl]], and [[post-punk]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thecut.com/2017/09/the-seventeen-year-old-playing-in-a-riot-grrrl-band.html|title=The 17-Year-Old Playing in a Riot Grrrl Band|website=thecut.com|date=8 September 2017|access-date=2021-05-29}}</ref> while their earlier music was considered far more [[indie-rock]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://officemagazine.net/its-pretty-sick|title=It's Pretty Sick! Band|website=officemagazine.net|date=31 August 2020|access-date=2021-05-29}}</ref> Over the years, the production style has become increasingly gritty and dark.

Their [[music videos]] are equally notable for their transgressive nature, marked by bloody car-crashes,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=45EIpOBKKhk|title=Pretty Sick - Allen Street|website=youtube.com|access-date=2021-05-29}}</ref> [[operating rooms]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P9BXhoTymR0|title=Pretty Sick - Bet My Blood|website=youtube.com|access-date=2021-05-29}}</ref> and erotic aggression,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K1eHgtsb-Xc|title=Pretty Sick - Dumb|website=youtube.com|access-date=2021-05-29}}</ref> to list a few.
