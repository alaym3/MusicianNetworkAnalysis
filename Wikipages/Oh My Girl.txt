'''Oh My Girl''' ({{korean|hangul=오마이걸|rr=Omaigeol}}, also stylized as '''OH MY GIRL''' and '''OMG''') is a South Korean [[girl group]] formed by [[WM Entertainment]]. The group is composed of seven members: [[Hyojung]], Mimi, [[YooA]], [[Seunghee]], Jiho, Binnie and [[Arin (singer)|Arin]]. Originally an eight-piece group, JinE left the group in October 2017 due to health issues. Oh My Girl debuted on April 20, 2015, with an [[Oh My Girl (EP)|eponymous extended play]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Lee|first1=Soo Ah|title=B1A4′s Little Sister Group, Oh My Girl to Debut April 20|url=http://mwave.interest.me/en/kpop-news/article/90730/b1a4s-little-sister-group-oh-my-girl-to-debut-april-20|access-date=May 11, 2015|agency=Mwave|publisher=CJ DigitalMusic|date=April 7, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170419032856/http://mwave.interest.me/en/kpop-news/article/90730/b1a4s-little-sister-group-oh-my-girl-to-debut-april-20|archive-date=April 19, 2017}}</ref><ref name="asiaenglish20150407">{{cite news|last1=Hong|first1=Grace Danbi|title=B1A4′s Little Sister Group, Oh My Girl to Debut April 20|url=http://asiaenglish.visitkorea.or.kr/ena/HA/content/cms_view_1994568.jsp?nodeId=8012|access-date=July 7, 2017|agency=enewsWorld|publisher=Korea Tourism Organization|date=April 7, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304095304/http://asiaenglish.visitkorea.or.kr/ena/HA/content/cms_view_1994568.jsp?nodeId=8012|archive-date=March 4, 2016}}</ref>

==History==

===2015: Debut with ''Oh My Girl'' and ''Closer''===
[[File:Oh My Girl at an Inkigayo fanmeeting.jpg|thumb|left | Oh My Girl at an [[Inkigayo]] fan meet in October 2015]]
Oh My Girl was publicized as the sister group of label-mate group [[B1A4]]. On March 29, 2015, teaser images of the eight members were released as well as their concept images.<ref>{{Cite web|title=B1A4 여동생 그룹, 티저 공개에 서버 다운…'인형 아냐?'|date=March 26, 2015|access-date=October 26, 2019|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/022/0002803946|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=B1A4 여동생 걸그룹 팀명, '오마이걸'로 최종 확정|date=March 30, 2015|access-date=October 26, 2019|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/382/0000333802|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref> On April 20, the group released their first [[extended play]], ''[[Oh My Girl (EP)|Oh My Girl]]'', which included the single "Cupid". They held their debut showcase the same day. They first performed the single on [[SBS MTV]]'s ''[[The Show (South Korean TV series)|The Show]]'' on April 21.<ref>{{Cite web|title='B1A4 여동생' 오마이걸, 男心향해 큐피드 화살 정조준(종합)|date=April 20, 2015|access-date=October 26, 2019|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/213/0000672410|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=오마이걸, 아이돌 홍수 속 모두의 '마이 걸'이 될 수 있을까(종합)|date=April 20, 2015|access-date=October 26, 2019|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/312/0000088953|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref>

On June 21, the group announced that they were currently preparing a new album.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Oh My Girl on Twitter|date=June 21, 2015|access-date=October 26, 2019|url=https://twitter.com/WM_OHMYGIRL/status/612551932045963264?s=19|website=Twitter|language=ko}}</ref> Their second EP, ''[[Closer (Oh My Girl EP)|Closer]]'', was released on October 8. The single of the same name was performed on [[Mnet (TV channel)|Mnet]]'s ''[[M Countdown]]'' on October 7.<ref>{{Cite web|title=오마이걸, 앨범발매 전 '뮤콘' 쇼케이스 개최한다|date=October 7, 2015|access-date=October 26, 2019|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/213/0000777501|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title="역대급 콘셉트"…오마이걸, '뮤콘'서 '클로저' 컴백 쇼케이스 개최|date=October 7, 2015|access-date=October 26, 2019|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/312/0000135774|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref>

=== 2016: ''Pink Ocean'', ''Listen to My Word (A-ing)'', first concert, and JinE's absence for health ===
[[File:20160702 오마이걸 미라클 데이 단체.jpg|thumb|right | Oh My Girl at the Miracle Day fan meet in July 2016]]
Oh My Girl's third EP, ''[[Pink Ocean]]'', was released on March 28, 2016 with the title track "Liar Liar", and a pre-released single "Step by Step". On the same day, Oh My Girl announced their official fandom name, "Miracle", at their comeback showcase for ''Pink Ocean'' and also on the group's V-app.<ref>{{cite news|title=[단독]'억류 해프닝' 오마이걸, 올 봄 컴백..설 이후 준비 박차|url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=108&aid=0002499091|access-date=July 7, 2017|website=Naver|date=February 9, 2016|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=[뮤직톡톡] '컴백' 오마이걸에 입덕할 이유는 충분했다|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/109/0003288207|access-date=October 26, 2019|website=Naver|date=March 28, 2016|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=[종합] 소녀감성 더한 오마이걸, '판타지만화'에서 '순정만화'로|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/382/0000462240|access-date=October 26, 2019|website=Naver|date=March 28, 2016|language=ko}}</ref> The group promoted "Liar Liar" and "Step by Step", a B-side written by B1A4's Jinyoung,<ref>{{cite web|title=Pink Ocean|url=http://music.naver.com/album/index.nhn?albumId=625825|website=Naver Music|access-date=July 7, 2017|language=ko}}</ref> on their comeback stages and at guerrilla concerts in Hongdae and Sinchon.<ref>{{cite news|title='엠카' 오마이걸, 더욱 상큼하게 돌아온 소녀들 '라이어 라이어'|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/311/0000592661|access-date=October 26, 2019|website=Naver|date=March 31, 2016|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title='뮤직뱅크' 오마이걸, 깜찍 롤러 스케이트 패션 '발랄함 UP'|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/117/0002744942|access-date=October 26, 2019|website=Naver|date=April 1, 2016|language=ko}}</ref>

On May 26, 2016, the group released the reissue of ''Pink Ocean'', titled ''[[Pink Ocean#Windy Day|Windy Day]]'' with two new songs, "Windy Day" and "Stupid in Love", along with the Chinese version of "Liar Liar" (which was released in ''Pink Ocean'' exclusively on online streaming services in China).<ref>{{cite news|title=오마이걸 'WINDY DAY', 인도풍 사운드로 반응후끈 '돋보이는 청량함'|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/015/0003600210|access-date=October 26, 2019|website=Naver|date=May 26, 2016|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=오마이걸 'WINDY DAY', 26일 '엠카운트다운' 통해 첫 컴백 무대 공개|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/215/0000465752|access-date=October 26, 2019|website=Naver|date=May 26, 2016|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Pink Ocean|url=https://y.qq.com/n/yqq/album/002savbR1kXOSy.html|website=[[Tencent|QQ]] Music|access-date=June 13, 2016|language=zh-hans}}</ref> On August 1, they released a special summer [[single album]] containing four remake songs, including [[Papaya (band)|Papaya]]'s "Listen to My Word (A-ing)" featuring Skull and [[Haha (entertainer)|Haha]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Oh My Girl releases teaser image for new album|url=http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20160726000551|access-date=July 26, 2016|website=The Korea Herald|date=July 26, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=[스타일리뷰] '상큼청량' 오마이걸의 여름 안에 쿨~한 패션|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/076/0002965884|access-date=October 26, 2019|website=Naver|date=August 2, 2016|language=ko}}</ref> The album was the group's first to top the [[Gaon Album Chart|Gaon weekly charts]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/album.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=&targetTime=32&hitYear=2016&termGbn=week |title=2016년 32주차 Album Chart|website=Gaon Music Chart |language=ko|access-date=January 5, 2018}}</ref>

On August 20 and 21, the group held their first solo concert titled "Summer Fairy Tales" at the Blue Square Samsung Card Hall in [[Seoul]], [[South Korea]].<ref name="1stconcert">{{cite news|title='16개월 만에 콘서트'…오마이걸, 이유 있는 욕심 [종합]|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/article/213/0000900779|access-date=October 26, 2019|website=Naver|date=August 21, 2016|language=ko}}</ref> Tickets were available to purchase on July 22 and were sold out within three minutes, breaking the record for the fastest sold-out concert for a rookie group.<ref>{{cite news|title=Oh My Girl concert tickets sell out within minutes|url=http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20160724000093|access-date=July 25, 2016|website=The Korea Herald|date=July 24, 2016}}</ref>

On August 25, WM Entertainment released a statement announcing JinE's hiatus from the group's activities while she received treatment for [[anorexia nervosa]].<ref>{{cite news|title=JinE of K-pop girl band Oh My Girl has anorexia|url=http://www.koreatimes.co.kr/www/culture/2016/08/201_212783.html|access-date=September 3, 2016|website=The Korea Times|date=August 26, 2016}}</ref>

=== 2017: ''Coloring Book'' and JinE's departure ===
On March 13, WM Entertainment announced on Oh My Girl's fancafe that their next album is scheduled to be released in April. JinE will not be taking part in upcoming album promotions and activities in favor of continued treatment and recovery.<ref name="JinE Update March 2017">{{Cite web|title=오마이걸 측 "4월 컴백 목표..멤버 진이 제외 7인조 활동"(공식입장 전문)|date=March 13, 2017|access-date=March 13, 2017|url=http://pop.heraldcorp.com/view.php?ud=201703130805233091073_1|website=Herald Pop|language=ko}}</ref> WM has also stated that JinE's health has improved since last year, and WM is focusing on long-term treatment for JinE.<ref>{{Cite web|title=[피플IS] 오마이걸, 불완전체 컴백…진이 없어서 '오마이갓'?|date=March 13, 2017|access-date=March 13, 2017|url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=241&aid=0002650302|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref> Oh My Girl's fourth EP, ''[[Coloring Book (Oh My Girl EP)|Coloring Book]]'', was released on April 3, along with the title track of the same name. During Oh My Girl's Comeback Showcase, it was revealed that the pink dolphin in the Coloring Book MV, was made in reference to JinE.<ref>{{Cite web|title=오마이걸, 새 음반 타이틀은 '컬러링북'..첫 티저 공개|date=March 23, 2017|access-date=March 25, 2017|url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=109&aid=0003501812|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref>

On October 30, WM Entertainment confirmed that due to continued health issues, JinE's contract with the company had been terminated and that she had officially left the group.<ref name="jinEdeparture">{{cite web|url=http://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=109&aid=0003650949&gid=999339&cid=1073735|title=오마이걸 측 "진이, 전속계약 해지→7인조 재편..건강이 우선" |website=Naver|date=October 30, 2017|access-date=November 2, 2017|language=ko}}</ref>

===2020: ''Eternally'', ''Nonstop'', and commercial success===
On October 1, 2019, the group announced on their Japanese website that they would be releasing their third Japanese album ''Eternally'' on January 8, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ohmygirljapan.jp/contents/270634|title=2020年1月8日(水)発売 OH MY GIRL JAPAN 3rd ALBUM 「Eternally」リリース決定！＆店舗購入者特典も発表！|date=October 1, 2019|website=Oh My Girl Japan|language=ja|access-date=December 27, 2019}}</ref> Then, on October 30, 2019, the Japanese version of Bungee was released to signify the first single off of ''Eternally''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ohmygirljapan.jp/contents/276713|title=「BUNGEE Japanese ver.」配信スタート！|date=October 30, 2019|website=Oh My Girl Japan|language=ja|access-date=December 27, 2019}}</ref> In November, the group revealed the tracklist for ''Eternally'', stating that the album would consist of four new Japanese songs: "Eternally", "Precious Moment", "Fly to the Sky", and "Polaris". Along with this, it was stated that the album would contain the newly released finale single from ''Queendom'', Guerilla. Like their previous two Japanese albums, the group allowed their Japanese fan clubs to decide the remaining tracks on the album based on popularity.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ohmygirljapan.jp/contents/281667|title=1/8(水)発売 OH MY GIRL JAPAN 3rd ALBUM『Eternally』収録内容決定！ジャケット公開！|date=November 27, 2019|website=Oh My Girl Japan|language=ja|access-date=December 27, 2019}}</ref> On December 25, 2019, the group released their third Japanese album, ''Eternally'', digitally on music platforms globally, two weeks ahead of its January 8, 2020 physical release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/cr/album/japan-3rd-album-eternally-ep/1491010235?l=en|title=JAPAN 3rd ALBUM Eternally - EP|date=December 25, 2019|website=Apple Music|language=en|access-date=December 26, 2019}}</ref> Outside of Japan, this digital release includes the first five tracks of the album. Locally, the digital release included the first eight tracks, which includes the original Korean language versions for "Bungee (Fall in Love)", "Guerilla" and "Love O'Clock".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ohmygirljapan.jp/musics/8551|title=OH MY GIRL JAPAN 3rd ALBUM『Eternally』【初回限定盤A】|website=Oh My Girl Japan Official Site|language=ja|access-date=August 1, 2020}}</ref>

On April 13, WM Entertainment confirmed that the group would make a comeback on April 27, with Jiho returning from her hiatus. Subsequently, the group released a teaser announcing that their return will be with their seventh EP ''[[Nonstop (EP)|Nonstop]]'' with a game-themed concept.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://entertain.naver.com/now/read?oid=109&aid=0004193409|title=오마이걸, '컴백 D-10' 베일에 싸인 게임 보드판 공개..본격 카운트다운 돌입[공식]|date=April 17, 2020|website=Naver|language=ko|access-date=April 17, 2020}}</ref> The EP and the music video for the lead single "Nonstop" were released on April 27.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=144&aid=0000668943|title=오마이걸(OH MY GIRL) 신곡 "살짝 설렜어(Nonstop)" 컴백포인트 세가지|date=April 27, 2020|website=Naver|language=ko|access-date=April 27, 2020}}</ref> Both the single and B-side track "Dolphin" became the group's highest-charting releases at the time, peaking at second and ninth on the weekly [[Gaon Digital Chart]], respectively.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/online.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=ALL&targetTime=18&hitYear=2020&termGbn=week|title=2020년 18주차 Digital Chart|website=Gaon Chart|language=ko|access-date=May 7, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gaonchart.co.kr/main/section/chart/online.gaon?nationGbn=T&serviceGbn=ALL&targetTime=29&hitYear=2020&termGbn=week|title=2020년 29주차 Digital Chart|website=Gaon Chart|language=ko|access-date=July 23, 2020}}</ref>

According to Oh My Girl's Japanese Website, following certain milestones met on the [[LINE MUSIC]] app, Oh My Girl would have releases for three consecutive months.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ohmygirljapan.jp/contents/331827|title=OH MY GIRL LINE MUSIC キャンペーン実施！！|date=July 27, 2020|website=Oh My Girl Japan Official Site|language=ja|access-date=August 1, 2020}}</ref> The first release was the Japanese version of ''Nonstop'' and was released digitally across multiple platforms on June 29.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/nonstop-japanese-version-single/1518526726|title=Nonstop (Japanese Version) - Single|date=June 29, 2020|website=Apple Music|language=en|access-date=July 10, 2020}}</ref> About a month after, the second release, a Japanese single, Lemonade, released digitally on July 27.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/lemonade-single/1523254191|title=Lemonade - Single|date=July 27, 2020|website=Apple Music|language=en|access-date=August 1, 2020}}</ref> The third release, a Japanese single, Tear Rain, was released digitally on all music platforms on August 24, 2020 <ref>{{Cite web|url= https://open.spotify.com/track/4Cq2bU1Iyz5vUWjp7Z6H9y?si=rkGROVOfTQun_Dryc6awVw|title=Tear Rain|date=August 24, 2020|website=Spotify Music|access-date=August 24, 2020}}</ref> 

At the 2020 Soribada Best K-Music Awards, held on August 13, Oh My Girl received a Bonsang award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.news1.kr/articles/?4026651|title="멋진 음악인 되겠다" 오마이걸, '2020 소리바다어워즈' 본상 수상|date=August 14, 2020|website=News1|language=ko|access-date=September 15, 2020}}</ref> 

On August 16, Oh My Girl released a collaboration single with [[Pororo the Little Penguin]]<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.allkpop.com/video/2020/08/oh-my-girl-and-korean-cartoon-character-pororo-collaborate-as-po-my-girl-for-supadupa-mv|title=Oh My Girl and Korean cartoon character Pororo collaborate as 'Po My Girl' for 'SUPADUPA' MV|date=August 16, 2020|website=allkpop|access-date=August 24, 2020}}</ref> with the title "Supadupa" <ref>{{Cite web|url= https://open.spotify.com/track/0rzOt0WKIC2JcMD06OHWsQ?si=x9N4W70HR6-2uIHzccwg0w|title=Supadupa|date=August 16, 2020|website=Spotify Music|access-date=August 24, 2020}}</ref> and also a version of the original soundtrack of Pororo's "Barabam" <ref>{{Cite web|url= https://open.spotify.com/track/7noJZbvn9jdNpKopTXvouF?si=NGyOKmrQR4iRCdoA5XSp7A|title=Barabam|date=August 16, 2020|website=Spotify Music|access-date=August 24, 2020}}</ref>

Oh My Girl also teased a collaboration with Keanu Silva and Mougleta on August 21. The collaboration single "Rocket Ride" was released on August 28.<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.soompi.com/article/1420733wpp/listen-oh-my-girl-announces-new-single-rocket-ride-in-collaboration-with-keanu-silva-and-mougleta-shares-preview|title=Listen: Oh My Girl Announces New Single "Rocket Ride" In Collaboration With Keanu Silva And Mougleta + Shares Preview |date=August 22, 2020|website=Soompi|access-date=August 24, 2020}}</ref>

On September 23, Oh My Girl announced on their Japanese website that they will be releasing their first Japanese single "Etoile / Nonstop Japanese ver." on November 25. The single contains the previous three digital releases that were revealed over the past three months: Nonstop (Japanese ver.), Lemonade, and Tear Rain, along with the new single Etoile, which was released digitally on music platforms on October 8, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://music.apple.com/us/album/etoile/1533071957?i=1533071961|title=Etoile - Single|date=October 8, 2020|website=Apple Music|access-date=October 14, 2020}}</ref> There will be four versions of the single, each will contain a different instrumental track. There will also be a special "Anime Edition" that will contain an English Version of Etoile.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ohmygirljapan.jp/contents/362893|title=2020年11月25日 OH MY GIRL JAPAN 1st SINGLE『Etoile / Nonstop Japanese ver.』リリース決定！＆店舗購入者特典も発表！|date=September 23, 2020|website=Oh My Girl Japan Official Site|language=ja|access-date=October 12, 2020}}</ref> On October 15, the Korean version of Etoile was released digitally on all music platforms.<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://music.apple.com/us/album/etoile-korean-version-single/1535433314|title=Etoile Korean Version - Single|date=October 15, 2020|website=Apple Music|access-date=October 14, 2020}}</ref>

===2021: ''Dear OhMyGirl''===
On April 16, WM Entertainment announced that Oh My Girl would return with a new album in early May.<ref>{{Cite web|title=오마이걸, 5월 10일 컴백 확정 [공식]|date=April 16, 2021|access-date=April 16, 2021|url=https://n.news.naver.com/entertain/now/article/144/0000730887|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref>  Their eighth EP ''[[Dear OhMyGirl]]'' was released on May 10, alongside the lead single "Dun Dun Dance".<ref>{{Cite web|title='5월10일 컴백' 오마이걸, 타이틀곡 'DUN DUN DANCE' 티저 공개|date=April 19, 2021|access-date=April 19, 2021|url=https://entertain.naver.com/read?oid=609&aid=0000430100|website=Naver|language=ko}}</ref> The single peaked atop the Gaon Digital Chart, becoming the group's first chart-topper.

On July 14, Oh My Girl announced their second Japanese single "Dun Dun Dance Japanese ver.", which was on September 22.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ohmygirljapan.jp/contents/441451|script-title=ja:2021年9月22日 OH MY GIRL Japan 2nd Single『Dun Dun Dance Japanese ver.』リリース決定！ 7月21日から先行音源配信もスタート！！|website=Oh My Girl Japan Official|language=ja|access-date=July 14, 2021}}</ref>
