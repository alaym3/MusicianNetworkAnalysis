'''Arkeisha Antoinette Knight''', known professionally as '''Kash Doll''', is an American rapper from [[Detroit]], [[Michigan]]. Signed to [[Republic Records]], she is best known for her singles "For Everybody" (2017) and "Ice Me Out" (2018), the latter of which became the lead single from her debut album ''[[Stacked (album)|Stacked]]'' (2019). Kash Doll has collaborated with artists including [[Meek Mill]], [[Big Sean]], [[Iggy Azalea]], and [[K. Michelle]].<ref name=":5" />

== Early life ==
Kash Doll was born and raised in [[Detroit]], Michigan. The oldest of six siblings, she began working at a young age to help her single mother support their family.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.republicrecords.com/artists/kash-doll|title=Kash Doll|date=March 3, 2019|website=Republic Records|language=en|access-date=May 2, 2019}}</ref> She had the desire to rap at an early age and started freestyling and writing lyrics as a young girl.<ref name=":5">{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/kash-doll-the-break-interview/|title=The Break Presents: Kash Doll- XXL|last=C.M|first=Emmanuel|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=March 19, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.republicrecords.com/artists/kash-doll|title=Kash Doll|date=March 3, 2019|website=Republic Records|language=en|access-date=April 25, 2019}}</ref>

She attended [[Henry Ford College]] for business management but left after a year. To support the financial needs of her family, Kash became a strip club dancer.<ref name="25mill">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8507471/kash-doll-interview-hustla-meek-mill|title=Kash Doll on Finding Confidence and Bringing Meaning to Her Music|magazine=Billboard|access-date=April 25, 2019}}</ref> While working as a dancer, Kash would rap and eventually started earning money from patrons at the nightclub without having to dance. It was reported that one night she made $26,000.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2017/05/17/everything-you-need-to-know-kash-doll|title=Everything You Need To Know About Kash Doll|website=The FADER|language=en|access-date=March 19, 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==
Early in her career, Kash Doll performed in local clubs, schools and charity events in the Detroit area.<ref name="25mill" /> Kash Doll later signed a deal with a local recording company in Detroit which later fell through. She then began using social media to build a following by releasing short videos of herself rapping. [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] used Instagram to invite her to open a show for him on his [[Summer Sixteen Tour|Summer Sixteen tour]] stop in Detroit.<ref name=":5" /><ref name=":1" />

Kash Doll remixed [[Tinashe]]'s single "[[2 On]]" in 2015. She then released a video for a single called "Run Me My Money", which went viral in 2015. That same year she released her mixtape, ''Keisha vs Kash Doll''.<ref name=":5" /> In December 2017, Kash Doll appeared on [[Big Sean]] and [[Metro Boomin]]'s single "So Good".<ref name=":0" /> After being released from her previous deal, on July 16, 2018, she released an EP called ''The Vault'' on streaming platforms. One music video from the EP, "For Everybody", received over 10 million views on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://the360mag.com/kash-doll/|title=Kash Doll|last=wp360mag|date=August 24, 2018|website=360 Magazine|access-date=March 19, 2019}}</ref>

She released a mixtape called ''Brat Mail'' on her birthday in 2018. It was dedicated to her fans, who she refers to as "bratz".<ref name=":3">{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2018/03/kash-doll-brat-mail-mixtape/|title=Kash Doll Delivers 'Brat Mail' Mixtape – XXL|last=II|first=C. Vernon Coleman|website=XXL Mag|access-date=March 19, 2019}}</ref> In August 2018, Kash Doll signed a record deal with [[Republic Records]]. She then released her first single as a part of that deal, "Ice Me Out".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2018/08/kash-doll-ice-me-out-new-song/|title=Kash Doll "Ice Me Out": Listen to Stuntastic New Anthem – XXL|last=Berry|first=Peter A.|website=XXL Mag|access-date=March 19, 2019}}</ref> Kash Doll released a single called "Hustla" in April 2019, which highlights her experiences from working at strip clubs. She stated that she is trying to awareness for and advocate on behalf of the women in that industry.<ref name="25mill" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://nylon.com/articles/kash-doll-interview|title=Kash Doll Tells Us What It Was Like When Rihanna Slid Into Her DMs|date=October 11, 2018|website=NYLON|access-date=April 25, 2019}}</ref> On June 7, 2019, Kash Doll released a new single titled "Kitten" that features American rapper [[Lil Wayne]]. Kash Doll featured on Australian rapper [[Iggy Azalea]]'s single "[[Fuck It Up (Iggy Azalea song)|Fuck It Up]]"<ref name="zachyt">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FZxMIk0apM0|title=Iggy Azalea Talks "Started" + New Album – THAT'S A BOP!|publisher=Zachary Campbell ([[YouTube]] 26:10) |date=May 10, 2019}}</ref> from Azalea's 2019 studio album, ''[[In My Defense]]''. Kash Doll was also an opening performer for [[Meek Mill]]'s 2019 The Motivation Tour.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://nylon.com/articles/kash-doll-interview|title=Kash Doll Tells Us What It Was Like When Rihanna Slid Into Her DMs|date=October 11, 2018|website=Nylon|access-date=March 19, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8496548/lil-durk-kash-doll-meek-mill-the-motivation-tour-openers|title=Lil Durk & Kash Doll to Join Meek Mill's The Motivation Tour: Exclusive|magazine=Billboard|access-date=March 19, 2019}}</ref>

She made her TV debut as herself in 2 episodes of ''[[Empire (2015 TV series)|Empire]]'' in 2020. She has a recurring role as Monique in [[Starz]] series ''[[BMF (TV series)|BMF]]'' as of 2021.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://deadline.com/2020/12/black-mafia-family-russell-hornsby-steve-harris-kash-doll-curtis-50-cent-jackson-starz-1234658074/}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://thesource.com/2020/12/21/black-mafia-family-cast/}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbestheculture/2021/01/22/how-kash-doll-is-cashing-in-on-the-renaissance-for-black-women-rappers/?sh=360e512b1193}}</ref>

==Personal life==

In September 2021, Kash Doll announced on her pregnancy with her first child on Instagram.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.bet.com/article/c0i805/kash-doll-announces-pregnancy}}</ref> In November 2021, she posted a gender reveal to announce it was a boy.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/kash-doll-reveals-gender-her-213044085.html}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/kash-doll-reveals-gender-of-her-baby-news.142334.html}}</ref>
