'''Rehema Namakula''' (born24 April 1991), also known as '''Rema''', is a Ugandan recording artist.<ref name="Bio">{{cite web|url=http://ugblizz.com/facts-didnt-know-rema-namakula/ |title=Facts you Didn't Know About Rema Namakula
|date=12 July 2016 |author=Sean Musa Carter | accessdate=6 October 2017 | publisher=Ugblizz.com |location=Kampala}}</ref>

==Background and education==
Rema was born in [[Lubaga Hospital]], on 24 April 1991, to the late Hamida Nabbosa and the late Mukiibi Ssemakula.<ref name="Data">{{cite web| url=http://bigeye.ug/rema-namakula-shares-her-biography/ |location=Kampala | accessdate=6 October 2017 |publisher=Bigeye.ug (Big Eye Uganda) |first=Isaac |last=Mites |date=21 March 2015 |title=Rema Namakula Shares Her Biography}}</ref> She is the last-born in her family. She attended Kitante Primary School for her elementary school education. She studied at Saint Balikudembe Senior Secondary School for both O-Level and A-Level studies. Later, she joined [[Kyambogo University]].<ref name="Data"/> which she never completed and says she plans to do so in future  

==Career==
During her Senior 6 vacation, she began singing [[karaoke]]. Later, she became a back-up singer to another Ugandan female musician [[Halima Namakula]], who became her [[mentor]]. She continued her music career as a backup artist for Ugandan musician [[Bebe Cool]] in Gagamel, Bebe Cool's recording group. In 2013, Bebe Cool was watching television when he saw Rema talking about launching a solo album, which Bebe Cool didn't know about. So he fired her and she went solo.<ref name="Bio"/><ref>{{cite web| url=http://ugandaonline.net/news/view/14121/bebe_cool_fires_rema_namakula_from_gagamel |title=Bebe Cool Fires Rema Namakula from Gagamel | author=UOWD  |date=20 July 2013 | accessdate=6 October 2017 | location=Kampala |
publisher=Ugandaonline.net (UOWD)}}</ref> In 2013 she released "Oli Wange" which was written by [[Nince Henry]] which made her popular in the music industry of Uganda.<ref name="Bio"/>

In 2016, Rema Namakula was selected to represent Uganda at the fourth season of Coke Studio Africa 2016. Other Ugandan recording artists selected included [[Lydia Jazmine]], [[Eddy Kenzo]] and [[Radio and Weasel]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://utamu.ac.ug/newss/140-entertainment-news/1618-singer-rema-namakula-to-represent-uganda-in-coke-studio-africa-season-4 | title=Singer Rema Namakula to represent Uganda in Coke Studio Africa Season 4 |publisher=[[Uganda Technology and Management University]] (UTAMU) |
author=UTAMU Entertainment News |location=Kampala |accessdate=6 October 2017 | date=2016}}</ref> Other participants in the invitation-only event included 
[[2Baba]] (2Face Idibia) from [[Nigeria]] and [[Trey Songz]] from the [[United States]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Rema Namakula Talks All Things Music, Success and Love: 5 Things About Rema Namakula | accessdate=6 October 2017 | 
url=http://www.coca-cola.co.ke/cokestudio/home/blog/5-things-about-rema-namakula/| date=2016 |publisher=Coca Cola Kenya (CCK) | author=CCK}}</ref>

==Family==
Over time, Rema Namakula began a romantic relationship with Ugandan recording artist [[Eddy Kenzo]].<ref name="Home">{{cite web| date=23 June 2016 | url=https://www.newvision.co.ug/new_vision/news/1427637/respect-rema-kenzo-warns |title=You must respect Rema – Kenzo warns Ex |accessdate=6 October 2017 |first=Duncan | last=Kushaba |newspaper=[[New Vision (newspaper)|New Vision]] |
location=Kampla}}</ref> On 26 December 2014, Rema Namakula gave birth to a daughter at [[Paragon Hospital]], in the Kampala neighborhood of [[Bugoloobi]]. Kenzo (real name Edrisa Musuuza), who has another daughter (Maya Musuuza) from a previous relationship,<ref name="Home"/> acknowledged being the father and named the newborn Aamaal Musuuza.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://matookerepublic.com/2014/12/27/kenzo-confirms-he-is-the-father-of-remas-baby-names-her-aamaal/ |title=Kenzo confirms he is the father of Rema's baby, names her Aamaal | author=Matooke Republic |date=27 December 2014 |
location=Kampala | publisher=Matooke Republic}}</ref>

On Thursday, 14 November 2019, Rema Namakula introduced her new fiancé, Dr. Hamza Ssebunya, in a ceremony that took place at her home in [[Nabbingo]], near Kampala. The introduction was a national affair, as thousands camped out along the procession route to her home.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rema: Was Buganda Culture Abused? |newspaper=[[New Vision]] |date=16 November 2019 | url=https://www.newvision.co.ug/new_vision/news/1510687/rema-buganda-culture-abused |access-date=20 November 2019 | author=Administrator |place=Kampala}}</ref>
