'''Christian Jaime González Nodal ''' (born January 11, 1999) is a Mexican singer born and raised in [[Sonora, Mexico]].  His debut studio album, ''[[Me Dejé Llevar]]'' (2017), was met with critical acclaim and success. Nodal has won three [[Latin Grammy Awards]], a [[Lo Nuestro Awards|Lo Nuestro Award]], two [[Billboard Latin Music Awards|''Billboard'' Latin Music Awards]], and a [[Latin American Music Award]].

== Life and career ==
Christian Nodal was born and raised in [[Caborca, Sonora, Mexico]]. According to Nodal, he started singing at the age of four and later learned how to play the piano and guitar on his own.<ref>{{cite web|title=Biografía|url=http://christiannodal.com/christian-nodal-biografia/|website=Christian Nodal|publisher=Mario Ruiz Madrigal|accessdate=July 13, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Get to Know Mexican Singer-Songwriter Christian Nodal |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8095346/christian-nodal-you-should-know-video |accessdate=May 5, 2019 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=Prometheus Global Media |date=January 22, 2018}}</ref>

In January 2017, Nodal released "[[Adiós Amor (Christian Nodal song)|Adiós Amor]]" which quickly became a success in Mexico and the United States," reaching number one on the [[Monitor Latino|Top 20 General Mexican Songs Chart]] and number two on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Latin Songs]] chart in the United States.<ref name="adiosamor">{{cite news|last1=Mendoza|first1=Amaya|title=Christian Nodal Vaults Into Top 5 on Hot Latin Songs Chart|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7694097/christian-nodal-top-five-hot-latin-songs|accessdate=July 13, 2017|work=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|date=February 16, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://monitorlatino.com/los-artistas-1-de-america-latina-35/|title=Los artistas #1 de América Latina|language=Spanish|work=[[Monitor Latino]]|publisher=Monitor Latino|accessdate=July 13, 2017}}</ref> In June 2017, Nodal collaborated with the Spanish singer, [[David Bisbal]] and released "[[Probablemente]]". Bisbal told ''Billboard'', "...he [Nodal] a great interpreter but also a songwriter. I love what he's doing and it's an honor to be a part of the early stages in his career."<ref>{{cite news|last1=Flores|first1=Griselda|title=Christian Nodal's New Single & Music Video 'Probablemente' Feat. David Bisbal: Exclusive Premiere|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/7825469/christian-nodal-probablemente-david-bisbal-video|accessdate=July 13, 2017|work=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|date=June 8, 2017}}</ref>

In 2018, Nodal accompanied Mexican performer [[Pepe Aguilar]] and his family, brother [[Antonio Aguilar, hijo|Antonio Aguilar Jr.]], daughter [[Ángela Aguilar]] and son [[Leonardo Aguilar]] on their equestrian-musical style tour dubbed "Jaripeo Sin Fronteras".<ref>{{cite news |title=Pepe Aguilar brings Mexican entertainment spectacular 'Jaripeo Sin Fronteras' to El Paso |url=https://www.elpasotimes.com/story/entertainment/2018/08/22/pepe-aguilar-brings-jaripeo-sin-fronteras-spectacular-el-paso/996489002/ |accessdate=May 5, 2019 |work=[[El Paso Times]] |date=August 22, 2018}}</ref>

In August 2018, Nodal released his debut album, ''[[Me Dejé Llevar]]''. It topped music charts in Mexico and the United States.<ref>{{cite news |title=Christian Nodal Vaults Into Top 5 on Hot Latin Songs Chart |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7694097/christian-nodal-top-five-hot-latin-songs |accessdate=May 5, 2019 |work=Billboard |publisher=Prometheus Global Media |date=February 16, 2017}}</ref> Music critic Thom Jurek of [[Allmusic]], praised the album and called it "elegant."<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |last1=Jurek |first1=Thom |title=AllMusic Review |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/me-dej%C3%A9-llevar-mw0003078673 |website=[[Allmusic]] |publisher=[[TiVo Corporation]] |accessdate=April 1, 2019}}</ref> ''Me Dejé Llevar'' went on to be nominated for a [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Ranchero/Mariachi Album|Latin Grammy Award for Best Ranchero Album]] at the [[19th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]], while "Probablemente", won for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Regional Mexican Song|Best Regional Mexican Song]]. Nodal was also nominated for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] but lost to Colombian reggaeton singer [[Karol G]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.etonline.com/2018-latin-grammy-awards-nominations-see-the-complete-list-110043|title=2018 Latin GRAMMY Awards Nominations: See the Complete List|date=September 20, 2018|accessdate=September 25, 2018|work=ET Online}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Latin Grammys 2018 Winners List |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/latin/8485312/2018-latin-grammys-winners |accessdate=May 5, 2019 |work=Billboard magazine |publisher=Prometheus Global Media |date=November 16, 2018}}</ref>

In May 2019, Nodal released his second studio album, ''[[Ahora (Christian Nodal album)|Ahora]]''.<ref>{{cite news |title=Christian Nodal lanza su nuevo álbum titulado 'Ahora' |url=https://elmanana.com.mx/christian-nodal-lanza-su-nuevo-album-titulado-ahora/ |accessdate=May 24, 2019 |work=[[El Mañana (Nuevo Laredo)]] |date=May 10, 2019 |language=Spanish}}</ref> It debuted in the top ten of the [[Top Latin Albums|''Billboard'' Top Latin Albums]] chart in the United States.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Bustios |first1=Pamela |title=Christian Nodal Scores Second Top 10 Debut On Top Latin Albums Chart With 'Ahora' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8512737/christian-nodal-scores-second-top-10-debut-top-latin-albums |accessdate=May 25, 2019 |work=Billboard |date=May 22, 2019}}</ref> Nodal also initiated a tour to promote the album.<ref>{{cite news |title=Esta fue la razón por la que Christian Nodal no fue a los Latin Billboards: "Tenía un compromiso con mis fans" |url=https://peopleenespanol.com/musica/christian-nodal-ausente-eb-los-latin-billboards/ |accessdate=May 25, 2019 |work=[[People en Español]] |date=April 26, 2019 |language=Spanish}}</ref>
