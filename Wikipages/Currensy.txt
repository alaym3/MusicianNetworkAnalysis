'''Shante Scott Franklin''' (born April 4, 1981), better known by his stage name '''Currensy''' (stylized as '''Curren$y'''), is an American rapper, songwriter, and record executive. In 2011, he founded the label [[Jet Life Recordings]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2019/01/08/currensy-jet-life-apparel-jan-2019/|title=Curren$y Blesses His Cult Fan Base With New Jet Life Apparel to Start Off 2019|last=Higgins|first=Keenan|date=January 8, 2019|website=The Source|access-date=May 21, 2019}}</ref>

== Music career ==

=== 2002–2008: No Limit and Young Money ===
In 2002, Currensy was signed to [[Master P]]'s [[No Limit Records]] and was a late addition to the [[504 Boyz]].<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://centralsauce.com/curreny-spitta-new-orleans|title=How Curren$y Rose as the King of New Orleans, In His Own Words|date=April 4, 2019|website=CentralSauce|access-date=April 29, 2019}}</ref> The song "Get Back"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.discogs.com/504-Boyz-Get-Back/release/3147578 |title=504 Boyz – Get Back (CD) |publisher=Discogs.com |access-date=July 15, 2015}}</ref> by [[504 Boyz]], produced by [[Donald XL Robertson]], was featured on the 2003 film ''[[Malibu's Most Wanted (soundtrack)|Malibu's Most Wanted]]'' soundtrack. Currensy appeared on 5 songs in Master P's ''[[Good Side, Bad Side (Master P album)|Good Side, Bad Side]]'' album.<ref>{{cite news|title=Master P – Good Side, Bad Side (CD, Album)|work=discogs.com}}</ref> Even though he was signed with No Limit, he represented C-Murder's TRU label. Currensy has several songs with [[C-Murder]], and was featured on the late [[Soulja Slim]]'s album, ''[[Years Later]]'' (2002).

In 2004, Currensy signed with [[Cash Money Records]] and [[Lil Wayne]]'s [[Young Money Entertainment]]. He was featured on Lil Wayne's mixtape ''[[Dedication 2]]'', ''The Suffix'' and several unreleased songs while with Young Money. He released a mixtape entitled ''Welcome Back'' in 2004. He was featured in Lil Wayne's "Grown Man" on ''[[Tha Carter II]]''. While still at Young Money, Currensy created Fly Society with skateboarder [[Terry Kennedy (skateboarder)|Terry Kennedy]], first as a clothing company, then expanding to release music. He released the song, "Where da Cash At" as the lead single for his Young Money debut, ''Music To Fly To'', in 2006. In 2007, another mixtape was released entitled ''Life at 30,000 Feet''. He remained with Young Money until late 2007. In 2008, Currensy released ''Independence Day,'' his first project after leaving Young Money and the first of seven mixtapes to be released that year while he struck out as an independent artist.<ref name="auto" />

=== 2009–2010: ''This Ain't No Mixtape'', ''Jet Files'' and ''Pilot Talk'' ===
Currensy signed with [[Amalgam Digital]] to release his debut album, ''[[This Ain't No Mixtape]]'', in 2009.<ref>{{cite web |author=David D. |url=http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2009/05/sail-on-review-of-currenys-this-aint-no-mixtape |title=Review Of Currensy's This Ain't No Mixtape &#124; The Smoking Section |publisher=Smokingsection.uproxx.com |date=May 12, 2009 |access-date=February 20, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090518022928/http://smokingsection.uproxx.com/TSS/2009/05/sail-on-review-of-currenys-this-aint-no-mixtape |archive-date=May 18, 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref> This album was entirely produced by Monsta Beatz.  Currensy was also selected to XXL magazine's annual [[XXL (magazine)#XXL Annual Freshman List|"Freshman Class" list]], and released his second album, ''[[Jet Files]]'', through Amalgam Digital that same year.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thesource.com/2009/09/curreny-jet-files-tracklist-press-release/ |title=Sorry, the page you were looking for is not here |publisher=The Source |access-date=February 20, 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100223095203/http://www.thesource.com/2009/09/curreny-jet-files-tracklist-press-release |archive-date=February 23, 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Amalgam Digital released physical copies of This Ain't No Mixtape and Jet Files on May 31, 2010.

Currensy's third album, ''[[Pilot Talk]]'', was released on July 13, 2010. The album featured guest appearances by [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Big K.R.I.T.]] and [[Mos Def]], amongst others, with most of the songs produced by [[Ski (producer)|Ski Beatz]]. Prior to the album's release, there was news of ''Pilot Talk'' being released under a newly relaunched [[Roc-A-Fella Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1638284/20100503/dame_dash.jhtml  |title=Damon Dash Resurrects Roc-A-Fella To Start A 'Positive Movement' |publisher=Shaheem Reid }}</ref> However, Currensy stated in interviews with both [[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] and [[Complex (magazine)|Complex Magazine]] that the album would be released under [[Damon Dash]]'s [[DD172]] record label division, BluRoc Records<ref name="ddotomen1">{{cite web|url=http://www.ddotomen.com/2010/02/17/currenys-rocafella-chain-powered-by-bluroc/ |title=Curren$y's Rocafella Chain Powered By BLUROC |access-date=September 6, 2010 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=73349 |title=Curren$y, My Sophomore Year |access-date=September 6, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100410155940/http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=73349 |archive-date=April 10, 2010 }} [[XXL (magazine)|XXL Magazine]]</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/blogs/2010/07/13/interview-curreny-on-pilot-talk-dame-dash-and-his-relationship-with-lil-wayne/  |title=Interview: Curren$y On "Pilot Talk," Dame Dash, And His Relationship With Lil Wayne |publisher=Green, Dominic |access-date= September 6, 2010 }} [[Complex (magazine)|Complex Magazine]]</ref> and distributed through [[Def Jam Recordings]]. Currensy made numerous appearances on labelmate [[Ski (producer)|Ski Beatz]]' debut studio album, ''[[24 Hour Karate School]]'', which was released on September 21, 2010.

=== 2010–2011: ''Pilot Talk II'' and ''Weekend at Burnie's'' ===
Currensy's fourth album, ''[[Pilot Talk II]]'', was released on November 22, 2010,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.islanddefjam.com/artist/news_single.aspx?nid=8194&artistID=7422  |title=Video: Curren$y Pilot Talk 2 Coming November 22nd! |access-date=October 27, 2010 }}</ref> which featured [[Fiend (rapper)|Fiend]] and [[Raekwon]], among others, with many of the songs produced by [[Ski (producer)|Ski Beatz]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Vasquez |first=Andres |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.12118 |title=DX News Bits: Kid Cudi, Queen Latifah, Curren$y &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHopDX |date=September 2, 2010 |access-date=February 20, 2011}}</ref> 2010 also saw the release of mixtape Smokee Robinson with DJ [[Don Cannon]].

On February 1, 2011, it was announced that Currensy signed a label deal with [[Warner Bros. Records]] for his imprint, Jet Life Recordings.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2011/02/curreny-signs-to-warner-preps-alchemist-ep |title=Curren$y Signs to Warner, Preps EP With The Alchemist &#124; Hip-Hop On A Higher Level |publisher=Xxlmag.Com |access-date=February 20, 2011}}</ref> ''[[Covert Coup]]'' was his first project on his Jet Life Recordings imprint under Warner Bros. Released for free via Currensy's official website on April 20, the album contains ten songs completely produced by [[The Alchemist (producer)|The Alchemist]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Curren$y and Alchemist Release Free EP|url=http://www.pitchfork.com/news/42267-curreny-and-alchemist-release-free-ep/|work=Pitchfork.com|access-date=June 20, 2011}}</ref>  He released a mixtape, ''Weekend at Burnie's'' on June 28, 2011, featuring his Jet-Life anthem "#JetsGo", which was produced by Rahki. The rest of the mixtape was produced by frequent collaborator Monsta Beatz.<ref>{{cite web|title=Curren$y's New Album: Weekend at Burnie's|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/42493-currenys-new-mixtape-iweekend-at-burniesi/|work=Pitchfork.com|access-date=June 20, 2011}}</ref> In 2011, Currensy and his Jet Life crew released the album ''[[Jet World Order]]'', under Jets International / iHipHop. Every track on this album features Young Roddy and [[Trademark da Skydiver]]. Frequent collaborators of Currensy are also featured on this album, including Smoke DZA, [[The Cool Kids]]' Mikey Rocks, Cornerboy P, Fiend, Street Wiz, and Nesby Phips. Currensy himself appears on three tracks.

=== 2012–2013: ''The Stoned Immaculate'' and ''Live in Concert'' ===
On November 10, 2011, Currensy announced through Twitter the release of a new album. ''[[Muscle Car Chronicles]]'', a two-disc set, was released on February 14, 2012. Currensy also planned on releasing ''Pilot Talk III'' in 2013, and mentioned the possibility of it being a free album. In February 2012, Currensy released the EP ''Here...'', and a few days later released another EP ''#The1st28'' with [[Styles P]].

On April 21, 2012, the track listing for ''The Stoned Immaculate'' was released.<ref>{{cite web|title=Curren$y releases 'Stoned Immaculate' Tracklist|url=http://www.djsmallz.com/blog/2012/4/22/news-curreny-currensy_spitta-reveals-tracklist-to-the-stoned.html|publisher=DJ Smallz|access-date=April 21, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150414072953/http://www.djsmallz.com/blog/2012/4/22/news-curreny-currensy_spitta-reveals-tracklist-to-the-stoned.html|archive-date=April 14, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> In July 2012, Currensy released the EP featuring all production by Harry Fraud, ''[[Cigarette Boats]]''. On October 31, 2012, he released the mixtape ''Priest Andretti''.

He will release a new tape with [[Wiz Khalifa]], titled ''Live in Concert''.<ref name="www.hiphopdx.com2">{{cite web | title = Curren$y "#PriestAndretti" Mixtape Cover Art & Release Date | publisher = HipHop DX | url = http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.21660/title.curreny-priestandretti-mixtape-cover-art-release-date | access-date = October 27, 2012 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20121028072952/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.21660/title.curreny-priestandretti-mixtape-cover-art-release-date/ | archive-date = October 28, 2012 | url-status = dead }}</ref> In November 2012 Currensy gave an update on his and Wiz Khalifa's mixtape ''Live in Concert'' saying its release is being held up by sample issues.<ref name="www.hiphopdx.com">{{cite web | title = Curren$y Explains "Live In Concert" Delays, Says "New Jet City" May Be Free | publisher = HipHop DX | url = http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.21835/title.curreny-explains-live-in-concert-delays-says-new-jet-city-may-be-free | access-date = November 10, 2012 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20121112061406/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.21835/title.curreny-explains-live-in-concert-delays-says-new-jet-city-may-be-free | archive-date = November 12, 2012 | url-status = dead }}</ref>  In January 2013, Currensy said that due to these same legal issues, the mixtape would likely become a retail album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22479/title.curreny-says-live-in-concert-with-wiz-khalifa-will-release-at-retail|title=Curren$y Says "Live In Concert" With Wiz Khalifa Will Release At Retail|date=January 9, 2013|work=HipHopDX|access-date=March 9, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130528005533/http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.22479/title.curreny-says-live-in-concert-with-wiz-khalifa-will-release-at-retail|archive-date=May 28, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> It was released as an iTunes only EP on April 20, 2013.

On February 3, 2013, Currensy released his latest mixtape ''New Jet City'' with guest appearances from Wiz Khalifa, [[French Montana]], [[Jadakiss]], [[Juvenile (rapper)|Juvenile]], [[Juicy J]], [[Trinidad James]] and [[Rick Ross]] among others.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/02/mixtape-currensy-new-jet-city | title=Mixtape: Curren$y "New Jet City | publisher=Complex Media | work=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]] | date=February 3, 2013 | access-date=February 3, 2013 | author=Isenberg, Daniel}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
In March 2012, news surfaced of legal action being taken against [[Damon Dash]] by Currensy. In the lawsuit, the rapper claimed that he never legally signed to Dash's DD172 label, and the record executive released the pair of albums (''Pilot Talk I'' and ''Pilot Talk II'') as well as ''Muscle Car Chronicles'' without authorization. Currensy demanded that Dash immediately stop releasing his music for profit, and pay $1.5 million in damages.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ballerstatus.com/2012/03/25/curreny-filed-lawsuit-against-damon-dash-for-unauthorized-release-of-his-music/ |title=Curren$y Files Lawsuit Against Damon Dash For Unauthorized Release Of His Music |publisher=BallerStatus.com |date=March 25, 2012 }}</ref> On October 6, 2015, Currensy was awarded $3 million in settlement stemming from the 2012 lawsuit against Dash for releasing material of his music without his consent.<ref>{{cite news|last=Hernandez|first=Victoria|title=Curren$y & Damon Dash Reach $3 Million Agreement In Lawsuit|url=http://hiphopdx.com/news/id.35761/title.curreny-damon-dash-reach-3-million-agreement-in-lawsuit|work=HipHopDX|date=October 6, 2015|access-date=October 17, 2015}}</ref>
