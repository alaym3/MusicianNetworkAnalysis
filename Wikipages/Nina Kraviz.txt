'''Nina Kraviz''' ({{lang-ru|Нина Кравиц|Nina Kravits}}) is a Russian DJ, music producer and singer.<ref name="mixmag">{{cite web |last1=Dick |first1=Duncan |title=NINA KRAVIZ {{!}} Mixmag |url=http://www.mixmag.net/words/features/nina-kraviz |website=[[Mixmag]] |access-date=8 April 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120325170332/http://www.mixmag.net/words/features/nina-kraviz |archive-date=25 March 2012 |date=22 March 2012}}</ref>

==Early life and Career==
Kraviz was born and raised in [[Irkutsk]], [[Siberia, Russia]]. She moved to [[Moscow]] to study [[dentistry]]  where she later gained her [[residency (medicine)|residency]].<ref name="mixmag"/><ref>{{Cite web|title=Biography: Nina Kraviz (fabric 91) - fabric blog|url=https://www.fabriclondon.com/blog/view/biography-nina-kraviz|access-date=2020-06-15|website=fabric london}}</ref><ref name="theguardian">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2018/jul/12/techno-titan-nina-kraviz-people-were-suspicious-of-a-pretty-woman-making-music|title=Interview Techno titan Nina Kraviz: 'People were suspicious of a pretty woman making music'|last=Hutchinson|first=Kate|date=12 July 2018|work=The Guardian|access-date=13 July 2018}}</ref> Kraviz also worked in a number of roles in the early 2000s before her music career took off, including fixing "[[cosmonauts]]' teeth", hosting a local Irkutsk radio show, writing for a [[fanzine]], and held a [[Concert residency|club residency]] on Friday nights.<ref name="mixmag"/><ref name="theguardian"/>

In 2005, she was accepted into the [[Red Bull Music Academy]] in [[Seattle]], however couldn't attend as she couldn't obtain a [[Visa (document)|visa]], attending the following year in [[Melbourne]] instead.<ref name="mixmag"/> By 2008 she was playing a regular night at the Propaganda Club in [[Moscow]].<ref name="mixmag"/>

She also voiced her cameo in the game itself, in Russian and English versions.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bain |first1=Katie |title=Nina Kraviz's New Video Game Character Reminds the Producer of Her Former Career in Dentistry: 'It's Mental' |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/9497545/nina-kraviz-cyberpunk-2077-interview-dentist |website=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=7 July 2021 |language=en |date=10 December 2020}}</ref> In 2021 Nina Kraviz, along with other artists like Ben Sims, Oscar Mulero, and Marcel Dettmann participated in PX099: 100 Years Of Colombia: a techno compilation with tracks from the artists on the scene with the intention of raising awareness about Colombia’s social unrest.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Magazine|first=Wootmag|date=9 June 2021|title=100 Years Of Colombia.
