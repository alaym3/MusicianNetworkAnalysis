'''Faye Webster''' is an American [[indie folk]] musician, singer, and photographer based in [[Atlanta, Georgia]]. She self-released her debut album ''Run and Tell'' in 2013. Webster has released three albums since: ''Faye Webster'' (2017) on [[Awful Records]], ''[[Atlanta Millionaires Club]]'' (2019) on [[Secretly Canadian]], and her most recent, ''[[I Know I'm Funny Haha|I Know I'm Funny haha]]'', was released on June 25, 2021.

==Career==
Webster self-released her debut album, ''Run and Tell'' on October 30, 2013. An early review compared Webster's sound to the likes of [[Kacey Musgraves]] and [[Sherri DuPree]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theblueindian.com/albumreviews/faye-webster-run-tell/|title=Faye Webster – "Run and Tell"|website=The Blue Indian|last=White|first=Dawson|date=24 February 2014|access-date=3 August 2021|language=en}}</ref> Webster signed to Awful Records in 2017,<ref name="vice">{{cite web |title=faye webster is the alt-folk standout on atlanta's awful records |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_us/article/9kbygp/faye-webster-is-the-alt-folk-standout-on-atlantas-awful-records |work=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]] |date=15 June 2017 |accessdate=22 May 2019}}</ref> and released her sophomore album, the self-titled ''Faye Webster'' in 2017.<ref name="pitchfork">{{cite web |last1=Wicker |first1=Jewel |title=Get to Know Faye Webster, Who Makes Retro R&B for Homebodies |url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/get-to-know-faye-webster-who-makes-retro-randb-for-homebodies/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |accessdate=22 May 2019}}</ref><ref name="wmagazine">{{cite web |last1=Cusumano |first1=Katherine |title=Meet Teenage Musician and Photographer Faye Webster, Atlanta's New Double Threat |url=https://www.wmagazine.com/story/faye-webster-music-awful-records-atlanta |work=[[W Magazine]] |accessdate=22 May 2019}}</ref> Webster signed to the record label [[Secretly Canadian]] in late 2018.<ref name="pastemagazine">{{cite web |last1=Johnson |first1=Ellen |title=Faye Webster Signs to Secretly Canadian, Shares Video for New Song "Kingston" |url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2018/11/faye-webster-signs-to-secretly-canadian-shares-new.html |work=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]] |date=14 November 2018 |accessdate=22 May 2019}}</ref>

In April 2020, Webster released the single "In a Good Way".<ref>{{Cite web|title= Faye Webster- "In A Good Way"| url=https://www.stereogum.com/2081130/faye-webster-in-a-good-way/music/|website=[[Stereogum]]| date=15 April 2020| accessdate=7 February 2021}}</ref> In April 2021, she released the single "Cheers" and announced her fourth studio album ''[[I Know I'm Funny Haha|I Know I'm Funny haha]]'', which released on June 25, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://diymag.com/2021/04/27/faye-webster-album-i-know-im-funny-haha#|title=Faye Webster announces new album 'I Know I'm Funny haha'|website=[[Stereogum]]|date=27 April 2021|access-date=3 August 2021}}</ref>

Webster released an [[Extended play|EP]] titled ''Live at Electric Lady'' in October 2021, as part of a [[Spotify]] exclusive series of the same name. Recorded at [[Electric Lady Studios]] in [[New York City]], the EP features live recordings of songs from her previous two studio albums, as well as a [[Cover version|cover]] of the song "[[If You Need to, Keep Time on Me]]" by [[Fleet Foxes]].<ref name=":0" />
