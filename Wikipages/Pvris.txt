'''Pvris''' (pronounced "Paris" and stylized '''PVRIS''') is a [[pop rock]] act formed by multi-instrumentalist, singer, songwriter, and producer [[Lynn Gunn|Lyndsey Gunnulfsen]]. Over her decade-long career, Gunnulfsen has released three studio LPs: ''[[White Noise (Pvris album)|White Noise]]'' in November 2014 featuring the hits "You and I", and "My House", ''[[All We Know of Heaven, All We Need of Hell]]'' in 2017 featuring "What's Wrong", and ''[[Use Me (Pvris album)|Use Me]]'' in 2020 featuring "Hallucinations". In 2021, Pvris released their latest single via Warner Records.

==History==
===Formation and early releases (2012–2014)===
The band was formed in Lowell, Massachusetts,<ref name="Pearlman62">Pearlman 2015, p. 62</ref> in 2012<ref name="Columbiainterview">{{Cite web |url=https://columbiachronicle.com/fbdde0ea-ae89-11e4-aad9-3b7d8bacdecb |title=PVRIS turns heads with static sound |last=Scarpino |first=Gina |date=February 9, 2015 |website=The Columbia Chronicle |access-date=September 3, 2015}}</ref> under the name '''Operation Guillotine'''.<ref name="Blastinterview">{{Cite web |url=http://blastmagazine.com/2014/02/12/pvris-blast-interview/ |title=PVRIS: The Blast Interview |last=Williams |first=Ellie |date=February 12, 2014 |website=Blast Magazine |access-date=September 3, 2015}}</ref> They were originally a [[metalcore]] band,<ref name=Blastinterview/> consisting of five members,<ref name=Pearlman62/> with Kyle Anthony performing lead vocals.<ref name=Blastinterview/> Their line-up soon changed to vocalist/guitarist Lynn Gunn (born Lyndsey Gunnulfsen), guitarist Alex Babinski, bassist Brian Macdonald,<ref name="AMbio">{{Cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/pvris-mn0003316228/biography |title=PVRIS {{!}} Biography |last=Monger |first= Timothy |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=September 3, 2015}}</ref> and drummer Brad Griffin.<ref name=Columbiainterview/> On March 26, 2013, Pvris released their self-titled EP.<ref name=ParisEP/> The sound was described as post-hardcore.<ref name="idobireviewpvris">{{Cite web |url=http://idobi.com/review/pvris-white-noise/ |title=Album Review: PVRIS – White Noise |last=Grace |first=Eleanor |date=October 21, 2014 |website=idobi |access-date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> They played sporadic shows through the Spring, including a mini-tour with Love, Robot.

During the summer of 2013, the band played a week on [[Ernie Ball]]'s Battle of the Bands stage at [[Warped Tour]] after winning a contest.<ref name=Pearlman62/> On July 18, it was announced that they had signed with [[Tragic Hero Records]], though it was short-lived.<ref name="top40charts">{{Cite web |url=https://top40-charts.com/mnews.php?nid=91948&cat= |title=Pvris (pronounced Paris) Signs To Tragic Hero Records |website= T4C |access-date=June 7, 2021}}</ref> It was also during this time, on July 26, that the band officially changed their name from 'Paris' to 'Pvris', citing legal reasons.<ref name="ATNInterview">{{Cite web |url=https://above-thenoise.com/2017/06/04/interview-pvris-we-had-our-own-battles-our-ups-and-downs-its-really-hard-to-give-it-to-the-world-but-we-try-to-be-as-strong-as-everyone-and-we-let-it-out-hope-youll-like-i/ |title=Interview – PVRIS 'We had our own battles, our ups and downs.' |last=Bunel |first=Chloé |website=ABOVE THE NOISE |access-date=July 17, 2019}}</ref> The band went on The Rise Up Tour supporting [[A Skylit Drive]]<ref name="AltPTragicHero">{{Cite web |url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/pvris_sign_to_tragic_hero_records_details |title=Pvris sign to Tragic Hero Records, details |last=Mendyuk |first=Bridjet |date=July 17, 2013 |website=Alternative Press |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150825200150/http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/pvris_sign_to_tragic_hero_records_details |archive-date=August 25, 2015 |access-date=September 3, 2015}}</ref> in September and October.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/a_skylit_drive_announce_more_tour_dates |title=A Skylit Drive announce more tour dates |last=Mendyuk |first=Bridjet |date=August 7, 2013 |website=Alternative Press |access-date=September 3, 2015}}</ref> Shortly after the end of the tour, they parted ways with their drummer Brad Griffin, and continued as a trio. Pvris began teasing fans that they would soon be hitting the studio to write and record new material around this time. That recording process began in December when Pvris entered the studio with [[Blake Harnage]] of [[Versa (band)|Versa]]. On February 7, 2014, they officially announced that they were a part of an Acoustic 4 Way Split with [[A Loss For Words]], Wind in Sails and After Tonight which was set to release on April 1, 2014. Coinciding with the release of their Acoustic EP, Pvris joined A Loss For Words, Veara, City Lights, and Moms on the fifth anniversary of The Kids Can't Lose Tour from March 29, 2014, to April 13, 2014.

=== ''Hallucinations'', Warner/Reprise Records, ''Use Me'', and Babinski's departure (2019–2021) ===
{{Main|Hallucinations (EP)|Use Me (Pvris album)}}

The band signed to [[Reprise Records|Reprise]]/[[Warner Records]] after being with Rise, and on July 12, 2019, they released a new single "Death of Me", and an accompanying music video, as their lead single from their EP, ''Hallucinations''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.musicweek.com/labels/read/the-band-have-built-a-devout-fanbase-pvris-sign-to-warner-records/076777 |title='The band have built a devout fanbase': Pvris sign to Warner Records |website=Music Week}}</ref> The track debuted at number 1 on the [[Kerrang!#The Official Kerrang! Rock Chart|Kerrang Rock Chart]].<ref>Kerrang! Rock Chart, ''Kerrang!'', July 24, 2019, page 12.</ref>

On August 16, 2019 the band released the song/video "Hallucinations" as their second single, which saw the band continue their progression towards a more dance/EDM influenced sound.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://wallofsoundau.com/2019/08/16/pvris-give-you-hallucinations-on-new-single/ |title=PVRIS Give You 'Hallucinations' On New Single |date=August 15, 2019 |website=Wall Of Sound |access-date=August 16, 2019}}</ref> In the week leading up to the release of the single, the band sent exclusive vinyl copies of the single to their fans. Frontwoman Lynn Gunn later stated on Twitter that those vinyls were a limited edition of only 500 copies.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/pvris-hallucinations-vinyl/ |title=PVRIS SEND FANS EXCLUSIVE VINYL OF UNRELEASED SONG "HALLUCINATIONS |date=August 12, 2019 |website=Alternative Press |access-date=August 22, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/pvris-are-sending-their-fans-their-new-unreleased-single-on-vinyl |title=PVRIS ARE SENDING THEIR FANS THEIR NEW, UNRELEASED SINGLE ON VINYL |date=August 12, 2019 |website=RockSound |access-date=August 22, 2019}}</ref> The track saw commercial and critical success, charting for several weeks on Billboard's Alternative Airplay charts, peaking at No. 35. 

On October 19, 2019, they announced on social media that a five-track EP, ''[[Hallucinations (EP)|Hallucinations]]'', would be released on October 25, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B3yBzNaHPEt/ |title=PVRIS on Instagram, "Hallucinations EP 10.25.19 📷 @lindseybyrnes" |date=October 19, 2019 |website=Instagram |access-date=October 19, 2019}}</ref> On January 8, 2020 it was announced that they would join [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]] on her [[Manic World Tour]], which ended up being postponed indefinitely due to COVID-19.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/3151760/halsey-manic-tour-guests-chvrches-blackbear-pvris/ |title=Halsey Invites Chvrches, Blackbear, And Pvris To Join Her on the Manic Tour |last=Tilchen |first=Jordyn |publisher=MTV News |access-date=January 10, 2020}}</ref>

On March 4, 2020, through some teasing links via their social media channels, they announced their third album, ''[[Use Me (Pvris album)|Use Me]]''.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-pvris-announce-third-album-use-me/|title=NEWS: PVRIS announce third album, 'Use Me'!|website=Deadpress.co.uk|date=March 4, 2020|access-date=August 28, 2020}}</ref> On March 4, 2020 the band released "Dead Weight" as the first single from ''Use Me'', and announced a May 1 release date for the album.<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web |url=https://www.spin.com/2020/03/pvris-reveal-use-me-share-dead-weight-video/ |title=PVRIS Reveal New LP, Share 'Dead Weight' Video |date=March 4, 2020 |website=Spin|access-date=March 4, 2020}}</ref> "Dead Weight" premiered on [[Annie Mac]]'s [[BBC Radio 1]] show as the "Hottest Record in the World".<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/pvris-share-new-song-dead-weight-announce-third-album-use-me-2620499 |title=PVRIS share defiant new song 'Dead Weight' and announce third album 'Use Me' |date=March 4, 2020 |website=Nme.com |access-date=March 4, 2020}}</ref> [[K. Flay]] collaborated on the single, which was described as dark pop.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/pvris-lynn-gunn-dead-weight-use-me/ |title=PVRIS take driver's seat in "Dead Weight", announce third album 'Use Me' |last=Owens |first=Paige |date=March 4, 2020 |website=Alternative Press  |access-date=March 4, 2020}}</ref> The music video for "Dead Weight" was filmed in [[Milan]], Italy.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://floodmagazine.com/75257/watch-pvris-release-dead-weight-video-announce-tour-dates/ |title=Watch: PVRIS Release "Dead Weight" Video, Announce Tour Dates |website=FLOOD  |access-date=March 4, 2020}}</ref> On April 8, 2020, the band announced that the release of ''Use Me'' had been delayed to July 10, 2020,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-pvris-delay-release-of-third-album-use-me-to-july-2020/ |title=PVRIS delay release of third album, 'Use Me'! |date=April 10, 2020 |website=Deadpress.co.uk|access-date=April 10, 2020}}</ref> and on July 2 the band announced a further delay to August 28, 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/the-release-date-for-pvris-new-album-use-me-has-been-rescheduled|title=The Release Date For PVRIS' New Album 'Use Me' Has Been Rescheduled|website=Rocksound.tv|access-date=August 28, 2020}}</ref> A deluxe edition of the album was released on October 22, 2020.

On August 26, 2020, the band announced that guitarist Alex Babinski was no longer in the band due to sexual misconduct allegations.<ref>[https://twitter.com/ThisIsPVRIS/status/1298638462715756545] {{dead link|date=August 2020}}</ref> Babinski released a statement shortly after, strongly denying the allegations while stating he would take legal action, but stated that he understood the decision made by Gunn and MacDonald.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/pvris-announce-alex-babinski-is-no-longer-associated-with-band-following-sexual-harassment-allegations-2738153|title=PVRIS' Alex Babinski "no longer associated" with band following sexual harassment allegations|date=August 26, 2020|accessdate=April 27, 2021}}</ref>

==="Monster" (2021)===
In late July, Pvris began teasing their forthcoming single with a cryptic website [http://sweatblvvdtears.com sweatblvvdtears.com], containing ten Roman numerals that incrementally revealed symbols and clues pertaining to the song and its release. On July 28, the single artwork was revealed at the [[Père Lachaise Cemetery|Pere Lachaise]] cemetery in Paris, France, ahead of the single's official release on July 30. The music video, directed by Katharine White in Los Angeles, premiered on August 5 via YouTube.
