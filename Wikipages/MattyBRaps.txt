'''Matthew David Morris''' (born January 6, 2003),<ref>{{cite news|last=Wete |first=Brad |date=2013-09-25 |title=19. MattyB: 21 Under 21 (2013) |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5721571/mattyb-21-under-21-2013 |newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=2015-07-05 |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6ZntAfmSy?url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5721571/mattyb-21-under-21-2013 |archive-date=2015-07-05 |url-status=dead }}</ref> better known as '''MattyB''' or '''MattyBRaps''', is an American singer/songwriter, rapper, and dancer who launched his career by gaining notoriety from [[YouTube]], posting remix videos of popular music.

Morris has released numerous covers and remixes of mainstream music since he began his career in 2010.<ref name=Billboard1>[http://www.billboard.com/news/rapper-mattyb-9-bounds-onto-billboard-social-1006481352.story Rapper MattyB, 9, Bounds Onto Billboard Social 50 Chart] at ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', by William Gruger; published March 16, 2012; retrieved November 29, 2012</ref> His first cover was of "[[Eenie Meenie]]" by [[Justin Bieber]]. On August 1, 2014, MattyB's YouTube channel surpassed one billion views.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Morris|first1=Matthew|title=MattyB Summer 2014 - One Billion Views #ThankYou|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LnO2fmvyN8Y|website=YouTube|publisher=MattyBVlogs|access-date=8 January 2016}}</ref> As of July 13, 2015, he has posted over 90 additional covers and 20 original songs on his YouTube channel. On August 27, 2015, his debut EP premiered on YouTube, titled ''Outside the Lines''. The album consists of four original songs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/albums/Mattybraps/Outside-the-lines|title=Outside the Lines by MattyBRaps}}</ref> On June 7, 2016, Morris released a memoir book, ''That's a Rap'', with the help of writer Travis Thrasher.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carter|first1=Brooke|title=MattyB Net Worth 2017 - How Much Is the Singer Worth Now? - The Gazette Review|url=http://gazettereview.com/2017/06/mattyb-net-worth-much-singer-worth-now/|access-date=December 20, 2017|work=The Gazette Review|date=June 27, 2017}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Morris<ref name="about">[http://www.mattybraps.com/about/ About - MattyBRaps] Retrieved March 2, 2013.</ref> was born on January 6, 2003, in [[Duluth, Georgia]],<ref name="Fuse.tv"/> and has three older brothers and a younger sister.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web|url=http://www.exceptionalfamilytv.com/news/mattybraps-7-year-old-rapper |title=MattyBRaps 7 Year Old |publisher=Exceptional Family TV |date=2010-10-04 |access-date=2013-03-08}}</ref>

Morris' first song with original lyrics was a remix of "[[I Believe in You (Kylie Minogue song)|I Believe in You]]", released on June 22, 2010. His first song available for purchase was a remix of "[[Just the Way You Are (Bruno Mars song)|Just the Way You Are]]", released on August 11, 2010, featuring [[Tyler Ward]]. In late 2012, he peaked at 11th on the Billboard Social 50 Chart with the music video for "That's the Way", which doubled his number of followers on social media sites Facebook, Twitter and YouTube.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1098396/9-year-old-rapper-mattyb-bounds-onto-billboard-social-50-chart 9-Year Old Rapper MattyB Bounds Onto Billboard Social 50 Chart] Retrieved July 11, 2013.</ref>

Morris has made several appearances on television, performing on ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5721571/mattyb-21-under-21-2013 |title=Billboard: 21 under 21, MattyB |publisher=Billboard.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6ZntAfmSy?url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/5721571/mattyb-21-under-21-2013 |archive-date=July 5, 2015 }}</ref> ''[[The Wendy Williams Show]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wendyshow.com/2011/06/15/matty-b/ |title=7-Year-Old Rapper Matty B. &#124; The Wendy Williams Show |publisher=Wendyshow.com |date=2011-06-15 |access-date=2013-03-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303170645/http://www.wendyshow.com/2011/06/15/matty-b/ |archive-date=2016-03-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Teen.com |url=http://www.teen.com/2011/02/17/im-bored/matty-b-raps-born-this-way-video/ |title=Matty B Raps - Born This Way Rap |publisher=Teen.com |access-date=2013-03-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924113838/http://www.teen.com/2011/02/17/im-bored/matty-b-raps-born-this-way-video/ |archive-date=2015-09-24 |url-status=dead }}</ref> ''[[Dr. Phil (TV series)|Dr. Phil]]'', ''[[WWE]]'', and ''[[The Queen Latifah Show]]''. He has also featured other artists, including [[Vanilla Ice]], [[James Maslow]], and [[Carson Lueders]].
