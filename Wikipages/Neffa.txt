}}'''Giovanni Pellino''' (born 7 October 1967), best known as '''Neffa''', is an Italian singer-songwriter, rapper, composer, producer and musician.

==Life and career ==
Born in [[Scafati]], at young age Pellino moved to [[Bologna]] with his family. In the late 1980s he adopted the stage name '''Jeff Pellino''' and was a drummer in several rock and punk bands, notably [[Negazione]].<ref name=music>{{cite book|last=Eddy Anselmi|title=Festival di Sanremo: almanacco illustrato della canzone italiana|publisher=Panini Comics, 2009|isbn=8863462291|chapter=Neffa}}</ref> In 1991, he joined the hip hop ensemble Isola Posse All Stars and adopted the stage name "Neffa" in honour of Paraguayan footballer [[Gustavo Neffa]], at the time playing with [[U.S. Cremonese]].<ref name=Deregibus>{{cite book|last=Enrico Deregibus|title=Dizionario completo della Canzone Italiana|publisher=Giunti Editore, 2010|isbn=8809756258|chapter=Neffa}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Sebastiano Vernazza|title=Gustavo, il paraguaiano che ha dato il nome a Neffa|url=http://www.gazzetta.it/Calcio/Altro_Calcio/Primo_Piano/2007/01_Gennaio/12/neffa_vernazza.shtml|accessdate=13 October 2016|work=[[La Gazzetta dello Sport]]|date=12 January 2007}}</ref>

After the album ''SXM'' with the rap group [[Sangue Misto]], in 1996 Neffa started his solo career, with the successful album ''I messaggeri della dopa'' which was led by the single "Aspettando il sole".<ref name=music>{{cite book|last=Eddy Anselmi|title=Festival di Sanremo: almanacco illustrato della canzone italiana|publisher=Panini Comics, 2009|isbn=8863462291|chapter=Neffa}}</ref><ref name=Deregibus>{{cite book|last=Enrico Deregibus|title=Dizionario completo della Canzone Italiana|publisher=Giunti Editore, 2010|isbn=8809756258|chapter=Neffa}}</ref> In the following albums he spanned various styles including funky, pop, soul, jazz and  R&B, getting his major success in 2001 with the summer hit "La mia signorina" and the relevant album ''Arrivi e partenze''.<ref name=music>{{cite book|last=Eddy Anselmi|title=Festival di Sanremo: almanacco illustrato della canzone italiana|publisher=Panini Comics, 2009|isbn=8863462291|chapter=Neffa}}</ref><ref name=Deregibus>{{cite book|last=Enrico Deregibus|title=Dizionario completo della Canzone Italiana|publisher=Giunti Editore, 2010|isbn=8809756258|chapter=Neffa}}</ref> The same year he was nominated as best Italian act at the  [[MTV Europe Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite news|title=MTV European Music Award nominees|url=http://oldsite.curlio.com/article/1513/last/MTV_European_Music_Award_nominees|accessdate=13 October 2016|work=Curlio.com|date=27 September 2001}}</ref>  In 2004 he entered the main competition at the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2004|54th edition]] of the [[Sanremo Music Festival]], placing ninth with the song "Le ore piccole".<ref name=music>{{cite book|last=Eddy Anselmi|title=Festival di Sanremo: almanacco illustrato della canzone italiana|publisher=Panini Comics, 2009|isbn=8863462291|chapter=Neffa}}</ref> 

In 2007 Neffa composed the musical score of [[Ferzan Özpetek]]'s ''[[Saturn in Opposition]]'', for which he won a [[Globo d'oro]] for best score and a [[Nastro d'Argento]] and a [[Ciak d'oro]] for best original song ("Passione").<ref>{{cite news|last1=Felice Liperi|title=In concerto per l' Ecofest la 'passione' di Neffa|url=http://ricerca.repubblica.it/repubblica/archivio/repubblica/2007/09/28/in-concerto-per-ecofest-la-passione.html|accessdate=13 October 2016|work=[[La Repubblica]]|date=28 September 2007}}</ref> In 2010 he started the musical project Due di Picche with the rapper [[J-Ax]]. In 2016 he returned to compete at the [[Sanremo Music Festival 2016|66th  edition]] of the Sanremo Music Festival with the song "Sogni e nostalgia".<ref>{{cite news|title=Sanremo 2016, Neffa tra ‘Sogni e nostalgia’|url=http://www.ilrestodelcarlino.it/bologna/spettacoli/musica/sanremo-2016-canzoni-neffa-1.1711172|accessdate=13 October 2016|work=[[Il Resto del Carlino]]|date=7 February 2016}}</ref>
