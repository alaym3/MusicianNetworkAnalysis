'''Barnaby George Courtney'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mobile.ascap.com/aceclient/AceWeb/#ace/writer/681171840/COURTNEY%20BARNABY%20GEORGE|title=ASCAP Ace Search|last=Courtney|first=Barnaby George|publisher=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] (ASCAP)|access-date=18 June 2016|archive-date=6 January 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170106040548/https://mobile.ascap.com/aceclient/AceWeb/#ace/writer/681171840/COURTNEY%20BARNABY%20GEORGE|url-status=dead}}</ref> (born 17 November 1990)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenational.ae/arts-life/music/abu-dhabi-grand-prix-opener-barns-courtney-can-celebrate-success-after-the-tough-times|title=Abu Dhabi Grand Prix opener Barns Courtney can celebrate success after the tough times|work=[[The National (Abu Dhabi)|The National]]|access-date=19 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/barnscourtney/status/5972128876724224|title=Barns Courtney on Twitter|work=Barns Courtney|publisher=Twitter|access-date=19 October 2016}}</ref> is an English singer, songwriter, and musician.

==Early life ==
Courtney was born in <!-- do not add the specific place unless there is a final consensus on where he was born. Some sites state Ipswich, others state Aylesbury.--> England<ref>{{cite web|url=http://first-avenue.com/performer/barns-courtney|title=Barns Courtney|work=First Avenue|access-date=19 September 2017}}</ref> then moved with his family to [[Seattle, Washington]] when he was 4.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.1077theend.com/blogs/branden/barns-courtney-taking-his-career-new-heights-and-trying-soak-it-all |title=Barns Courtney is taking his career to new heights and trying to soak it all in |publisher=107.7 The End |date=24 March 2016 |access-date=28 November 2017}}</ref> He returned to the United Kingdom when 15.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.paradigmagency.com/divisions/artist/index/2858|title=Paradigm Talent Agency|access-date=28 May 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151213035922/http://www.paradigmagency.com/divisions/artist/index/2858|archive-date=13 December 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> Courtney previously led the British indie band SleeperCell, which was featured on [[Channel 4]]'s ''[[Orange unsignedAct|Orange Unsigned]]'' competition,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/suffolk/content/articles/2009/03/09/introducing_sleepercell_2009_feature.shtml/#|title=SleeperCell's future's bright - but not Orange|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> before fronting the indie band Dive Bella Dive, which signed to [[Island Records]] and performed at the [[Download Festival]] in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://clashfinder.com/m/donington2012/?user=09b58k.ve|website=Clashfinder.com|title=Download Festival 2012|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref>

After recording an album's worth of material for Island Records that was never released,<ref name="pastemagazine1">{{cite web|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2016/06/barns-courtney-the-best-of-whats-next.html|title=Barns Courtney: The Best of What's Next|website=Pastemagazine.com|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> Courtney was dropped by the label. Determined to pursue a career in music, but with no income or home, he supported himself by working at [[PC World (retailer)|PC World]] while sleeping on friends' sofas and in his girlfriend's car.<ref name="pastemagazine1"/>

==Solo career==
In 2015, Courtney released his first track as a solo artist, "Glitter & Gold", which received radio play on [[BBC Introducing]] Suffolk and by [[BBC Radio 1]]'s Phil Taggert,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.xsnoize.com/track-of-the-day-barns-courtney-glitter-and-gold/ |title=Track of the Day: Barns Courtney - Glitter and Gold|website=Xsnoize.com |access-date=28 November 2017}}</ref> and led to an appearance at [[BBC Radio 1's Big Weekend|Radio 1's Big Weekend]] in [[Exeter]] the following year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/e2f9rz/acts/a4r2rz#p03wl3h3|title=Barns Courtney: BBC Introducing Stage|work=[[BBC]]|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> "Glitter & Gold" also reached #1 on the UK [[Spotify]] Viral Chart, and #2 on the [[iTunes]] Alternative Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://slatethedisco.com/2016/01/in-conversation-barns-courtney//|title=Slate The Disco - Barns Courtney in Conversation|website=Slatethedisco.com|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> Glitter & Gold" was later used in the trailer for the 2016 film [[The Founder]] starring [[Michael Keaton]].

Also in 2015, "Fire" was featured in the [[Bradley Cooper]] movie ''[[Burnt (film)|Burnt]]''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/celebrity/how-bradley-cooper-harvey-weinstein-helped-singer-barns-courtney/|title=How Bradley Cooper, Harvey Weinstein Helped Ignite Singer Barns Courtney's Rising Star on the Burnt Soundtrack|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> and went on to receive airplay on American rock radio in 2016. "Fire" was later used in the advertisement of the 23rd season of "Top Gear". To date, "Fire" has been streamed more than 68 million times on Spotify, while "Glitter & Gold" has been streamed more than 98 million times. Courtney played at the [[South by Southwest#Music|SXSW Music Festival]] in [[Austin, Texas]], in a showcase for ''[[NME]]'' magazine in March 2016<ref>{{cite web|url=http://schedule.sxsw.com/2016/events/event_MS35017|title=SXSW Schedule 2016: Barns Courtney|website=Schedule.sxsw.com|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> and performed "Fire" on [[Conan O'Brien]]'s [[TBS (U.S. TV channel)|TBS]] talkshow ''[[Conan (talk show)|Conan]]'' in June.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://1039rxp.com/2016/06/16/barns-courtney-fire-on-conan-june-15-2016/|title=Barns Courtney "Fire" on Conan|website=1039rxp.com|access-date=14 January 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170116171903/http://1039rxp.com/2016/06/16/barns-courtney-fire-on-conan-june-15-2016/|archive-date=16 January 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Another track, "Hobo Rocket", was included on the soundtrack of the [[EA Sports]] video game ''[[FIFA 17]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.easports.com/fifa/news/2016/fifa-17-soundtrack|title=FIFA 17 Soundtrack |publisher=EA Sports|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> In early 2017, the ''It's All Indie'' website selected Courtney as its #1 Sound of 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.itsallindie.com/2017/01/sounds-of-2017-1-barns-courtney.html|title=It's All Indie - Sounds of  2017: #1 - Barns Courtney|website=Itsallindie.com|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref>
The track 'Fire' has also featured on a 2019 advert for [[Peloton (exercise equipment company)|Peloton]] home spinning bikes.
Courtney has opened for artists including [[The Who]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thewholive.net/support/index.php?id=96|title=The Who Concert Guide|website=Thewholive.net|access-date=17 January 2017}}</ref> [[The Libertines]], [[Ed Sheeran]], The Lumineers, [[Tom Odell]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.barnscourtney.com/home-north-america/#|title=Barns Courtney - North America Tour Dates|website=Barnscourtney.com|access-date=25 October 2016}}</ref> and [[Fitz and the Tantrums]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fitzandthetantrums.com/news/barns-courtney-will-be-joining-us-come-get-your-love-tour-23136|title=The Latest from the Band: Barns Courtney Will Be Joining Us On The Come Get Your Love Tour!|website=Fitzandthetantryms.com|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> and opened for [[Blur (band)|Blur]] at the closing party for the [[2016 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thenational.ae/arts-life/music/abu-dhabi-grand-prix-opener-barns-courtney-can-celebrate-success-after-the-tough-times|title=Abu Dhabi Grand Prix opener Barns Courtney can celebrate success after the tough times|work=[[The National (Abu Dhabi)|The National]]|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> He is currently working on his first full-length album,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/barns-courtney-mn0003449040/biography|title=Barns Courtney – Biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=30 May 2016}}</ref> and will be performing at the [[BottleRock Napa Valley]] festival in California,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bottlerocknapavalley.com/lineup-schedule/2017-artists/|title=BottleRock Festival: 2017 Artists|access-date=14 January 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170116162600/http://www.bottlerocknapavalley.com/lineup-schedule/2017-artists/|archive-date=16 January 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> the [[Governors Ball Music Festival]] in New York<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.governorsballmusicfestival.com/lineup/|title=Governors Ball Music Festival: 2017 Artists|access-date=14 January 2017}}</ref> and the LaureLive Music Festival in Cleveland, OH later in 2017.

Also in 2016 Barns Courtney played at the world famous Rock Werchter festival in Belgium on the KlubC stage.

In late January he also announced details of an extensive US headlining tour, in addition to the release of his debut EP, ''The Dull Drums''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.axs.com/barns-courtney-announces-tour-in-support-of-debut-release-114108|title=Barns Courtney announces tour in support of debut release|newspaper=AXS|access-date=6 February 2017}}</ref> His song, ""Hellfire", was named as the official theme song for [[Extreme Rules (2017)|Extreme Rules]] in 2017.

In 2017, His song 'Kicks' was featured in [[Need for Speed Payback]].

His song "Hands" was used in ''[[MLB The Show 17]]''

His song "Fire" was used in the 2017 documentary ''We, the Marines'', narrated by [[Gene Hackman]].{{citation needed|date=May 2019}}

Throughout the 2017 [[Australian Football League|AFL]] season, the [[Hawthorn Football Club]] used "Glitter and Gold" with a highlights package over the ground public address system in the final 60 seconds before the start of home games.

In November 2017, the BBC used his song "Champion" for their Rugby League World Cup advertising campaign. As well as being used by WWE for their pay-per-view Backlash 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://411mania.com/wrestling/theme-song-announced-wwe-backlash/|title=Theme Song Announced For WWE Backlash|website=411mania.com|date=30 April 2018}}</ref>

He performed on [[Steve Angello]]'s 2018 album ''[[Human (Steve Angello album)|Human]]'' on the song "Dopamine".

In May 2018, the Netflix series ''[[Safe (TV series)|Safe]]'' used his song "Glitter & Gold" as its intro song. Additionally they used the track "Fire" as the credit track.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ign.com/articles/2018/05/11/netflixs-safe-pilot-episode-review|title=Netflix's Safe: Pilot Episode Review|last=Fowler|first=Matt|date=11 May 2018|website=Ign.com|access-date=13 May 2018}}</ref>

His song "Champion" is featured in the [[San Diego Studio]] video game ''[[MLB The Show 18]]''.{{citation needed|date=May 2019}}

His song "Hellfire" is featured on the first episode of the first season of Marvel's ''[[Cloak & Dagger (TV series)|Cloak and Dagger]]'', initially aired 7 June 2018.{{citation needed|date=May 2019}}

Courtney is also credited as featuring on [[The Prodigy]] song "Give Me a Signal" from their new album ''[[No Tourists]]''.{{citation needed|date=May 2019}}

Courtney's song "Champion" was also used in the first gameplay trailer for the Battle Charge gameplay expansion for the popular battle royale shooter ''[[Apex Legends]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zsUd40fvFm8|title=Apex Legends Season 2 - Battle Charge Gameplay Trailer|publisher=[[YouTube]]|date=27 June 2019|access-date=2 July 2019}}</ref>

Courtney's song “Fun Never Ends” is featured on the soundtrack of the [[EA Sports]] video game [[NHL 20]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/playlist/nhl-20-soundtrack/pl.86ab9b11317d4d5084db906f457d6170|title=NHL 20 Soundtrack by EA Music|website=Apple Music|language=en-us|access-date=2020-02-05}}</ref>

His song "Sinners" was used as the background music for the first trailer of the video game ''As Dusk Falls'', presented on 23 July 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CXzxEBfBrcQ&list=PL0il2l-B_WwZADvZzZICZMX-jP8qf8RZf|title=As Dusk Falls|website=YouTube|language=en-us|access-date=2020-07-23}}</ref>
