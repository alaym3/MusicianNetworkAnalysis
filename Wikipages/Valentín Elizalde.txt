'''Valentín Elizalde Valencia''' ({{IPA-es|balenˈtin eliˈsalde βaˈlensja|lang}}; 1 February 1979 – 25 November 2006) was a [[Regional Mexican]] singer. Nicknamed "'''El Gallo de Oro'''" (The Golden Rooster),  he was known for his off-key style and his biggest hits included: "Vete Ya," "Ebrio de Amor", " Vete Con Él", "Vuelve Cariñito", "Cómo Me Duele", "Vencedor", " Mi Virgencita", and "Soy Así" (a cover of [[José José|José José's]] classic song).<ref name=tobar>Héctor Tobar, (25 November 2006). Mexican pop singer gunned down. ''[[Los Angeles Times]]''.</ref> Some of his songs were [[narcocorrido]]s eulogizing Mexican drug lords like [[Vicente Carrillo Fuentes]]. He was murdered in an ambush, allegedly by members of the drug trafficking gang [[Los Zetas]], which at that time served as the armed wing of the [[Gulf Cartel]].<ref>Manuel Roig-Franzia, [https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/04/08/AR2007040801005.html Mexican Drug Cartels Leave a Bloody Trail on YouTube], ''[[The Washington Post]]'', April 9, 2007; Page A01.</ref><ref name=asesino/>

==Personal life==
Elizalde was born in Jitonhueca, a village near of the city of [[Etchojoa]], [[Sonora]]. He then moved to [[Guadalajara]], [[Jalisco]], and later to [[Guasave]], [[Sinaloa]], where he, his father (also a singer), Everardo "Lalo" Elizalde, nicknamed "El Gallo" (The Rooster), and brothers resided for a few years. His father died in a car accident on the so-called "Curva de la muerte" (Curve of Death) in the city of [[Benito Juárez Municipality, Sonora|Villa Juárez, Sonora]]. Elizalde's ex-partner and mother of his second daughter, Blanca Vianey Durán Brambila, was murdered on June 20, 2016 in [[Cajeme]], Sonora.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://zetatijuana.com/2016/06/20/ejecutan-a-ex-pareja-de-valentin-elizalde/|title=Ejecutan a ex pareja de Valentín Elizalde|last=Zeta|website=ZETA - Libre como el viento|date=June 20, 2016|access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Valentín Elizalde 4.jpg|thumb|upright|Valentín Elizalde performing {{circa|2006}}]]
In 2007, Elizalde was nominated posthumously for the [[Grammy Awards]].

==Murder==
On November 25, 2006, Elizalde’s vehicle was gunned down shortly after leaving a concert in [[Reynosa|Reynosa, Tamaulipas]]. Elizalde, [[27 Club|aged 27 at the time]], was killed, along with his chauffeur and his assistant.<ref>{{cite web|title=Asesinato de Valentin Elizalde El Gallode Oro Muerte|language=es|url=http://www.lasnoticiasmexico.com/43617.html|access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref> It is widely believed that Elizalde was killed due to his concert performance of the [[corrido]], "A Mis Enemigos", which contains lyrics believed to antagonize drug trafficking gang [[Los Zetas]].<ref>{{cite book|title=In the Shadow of Saint Death: The Gulf Cartel and the Price of America's Drug War in Mexico|year=2014|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QPtABAAAQBAJ&pg=PA254|author=Michael Deibert|author-link=Michael Deibert|page=254|isbn=978-0-7627-9125-5}}</ref> [[Raúl Hernández Barrón]], alleged murderer of Elizalde and member of Los Zetas, was arrested on 22 March 2008, in Coatzintla, Veracruz.<ref name=asesino>{{cite web|title=Detienen a presunto asesino de Valentín Elizalde|trans-title=Alleged murderer of Valentín Elizalde has been arrested|url=https://www.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/noticia/340483.detienen-a-presunto-asesino-de-valentin-eliza.html|language=es|date=March 26, 2008|access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref>
