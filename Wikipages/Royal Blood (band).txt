'''Royal Blood''' are an English [[Rock music|rock]] duo formed by Mike Kerr (vocals, bass guitar) and Ben Thatcher (drums) in [[Worthing]] in 2011. Their sound is anchored by Kerr's unique bass playing style, which sees him using various [[effects pedal]]s and [[Guitar amplifier|amps]] to make his bass guitar sound like a standard electric guitar and bass guitar at the same time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://onlineguitarlessons.co.uk/mike-kerr-royal-blood-get-sound/ |title=How does Mike Kerr from Royal Blood sound so good? |first=Jon |last=Quinton |date=21 December 2016 |website=onlineguitarlessons.co.uk |access-date=20 October 2019}}</ref> Their [[Royal Blood (album)|self-titled debut album]] was released in August 2014, with their second album ''[[How Did We Get So Dark?]]'' following in June 2017, and their third album ''[[Typhoons (album)|Typhoons]]'' in April 2021.

==History==
===2011–2015: Formation and debut album===
[[File:Royalblood.jpg|thumb|upright|160px|Royal Blood performing in March 2014]]
Royal Blood consists of lead vocalist and bassist Mike Kerr (born 19&nbsp;June 1990) from [[Worthing]] and drummer Ben Thatcher (born 12&nbsp;February 1988) from [[Rustington]].<ref name="nmephotos">{{Cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/photos/royal-blood-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-brighton-duo/359555|title=Royal Blood: Everything You Need To Know About The Brighton Duo|work=[[NME]]|date=16 September 2014 |first=Emily |last=Barker |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181125162421/https://www.nme.com/photos/royal-blood-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-brighton-duo-1409001 |archive-date=2018-11-25 |url-status=dead}}</ref> They first met around 2005 when they were teenagers; for a brief period, they were part of the same four-piece band called Flavour Country, in which Kerr played [[Keyboard instrument|keyboards]] and the [[keytar]].<ref name="telegraphint">{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/music/music-news/11423707/Royal-Blood-interview-Theres-such-a-poor-community-for-new-bands.-Its-a-really-bad-thing-for-British-music.html|title=Royal Blood interview: 'There's such a poor community for new bands. It's a really bad thing for British music' |work=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]|date=20 February 2015 |first=Charlie |last=Parrish}}</ref>

Royal Blood formed in [[Worthing]] in March 2011,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/royal-blood-mn0003205165|title=Royal Blood – Biography|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=28 February 2015|last=Kerr|first=Scott}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://m.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/when-two-and-the-blues-is-more-than-enough-for-royal-blood-20150114-12ntj1.html|title=When two and the blues is more than enough for Royal Blood|work=[[Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=28 February 2015|access-date=28 February 2015|last=Zuel|first=Bernard}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/184800-royal-blood-royal-blood/|title=Royal Blood: Royal Blood|work=[[PopMatters]]|first=Zachary|last=Houle|date=23 September 2014|access-date=2 March 2015}}</ref> after Kerr met original drummer Matt Swan.<ref name="Swanny">{{cite web |title=MATT SWAN [swanny] - The B Side Agency |url=https://www.thebsideagency.com/matt-swanny-swan |website=The B-Side Agency}}</ref> They relocated to Swan's home country of Australia, where they recorded an [[Extended play|EP]] in [[Brisbane]]. The first single, "Leaving", premiered on Australian radio station [[Triple J]] in 2012; it would later be re-recorded and renamed "[[Come On Over (Royal Blood song)|Come On Over]]".<ref name="Leaving">{{cite web |title=Leaving by Royal Blood |url=https://jplay.com.au/song/40672/ |website=J Play |access-date=6 July 2019}}</ref> The band played around the Brisbane and [[Gold Coast, Queensland|Gold Coast]] area,<ref name="destination1">{{cite web |url=http://brisbanemusicandculture.wordpress.com/2012/04/12/royal-blood-at-oh-hello/ |title=Royal Blood at Oh Hello! |website=Brisbanemusicandculture.wordpress.com |date=12 April 2012 |access-date=9 August 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130710011056/http://brisbanemusicandculture.wordpress.com/2012/04/12/royal-blood-at-oh-hello/ |archive-date=10 July 2013 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> before Kerr eventually decided to return to Worthing.<ref name="triplej1">{{cite web |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/zan/blog/s3971960.htm |title=Royal Blood start a band after living on the Gold Coast... | publisher=[[Triple J]] |date= 24 March 2014 | archive-date=13 April 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140413085538/https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/zan/blog/s3971960.htm}}</ref><ref name="triplej2">{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/triplej/media/listen.htm?show=jukebox&xmlUrl=/triplej/media/xml/s3971934.xml&mediaType=audio&launchType=play&pluginTarget=undefined&pluginTitle=undefined&pluginColour=undefined |title=Royal Blood Interview | publisher=[[Triple J]] |date= 24 March 2014 | access-date=9 August 2014}}</ref> He was picked up at the airport by Thatcher and the two decided to form a band together while retaining the Royal Blood name.<ref name="telegraphint"/><ref name="argus1">{{cite web |url=http://www.theargus.co.uk/news/10848732.Brighton_s_Royal_Blood_shortlisted_for_BBC_s_Sound_of_2014/ |title=Brighton's Royal Blood shortlisted for BBC's Sound of 2014 | work=[[The Argus (Brighton)|The Argus]] |date= 2 December 2013 | access-date=8 February 2014}}</ref>

Initially, the new duo "couldn't get a gig in Worthing for months" and "ended up just playing a lot of open-mic nights with acoustic singer-songwriters".<ref name="telegraphint"/> The band developed their music in the studios of [[Brighton Electric]], and it was during this time that they were signed by [[Warner/Chappell Music]].<ref name="nmephotos"/> They joined the same management company as the band [[Arctic Monkeys]].<ref name="nmephotos"/> During the summer of 2013, Arctic Monkeys drummer [[Matt Helders]] wore a Royal Blood shirt during the Arctic Monkeys' [[Glastonbury Festival]] concert.<ref name="mojo1">{{cite web |url=http://www.mojo4music.com/821/royal-blood-figure-it-out/ |title=Royal Blood – Figure It Out |work=[[Mojo (magazine)|Mojo]] |date= 5 July 2013 | access-date=28 January 2014 |first=Jenny |last=Bulley}}</ref>

On 11 November 2013, Royal Blood released their official debut single, "[[Out of the Black (song)|Out of the Black]]",<ref name="thisisfakediy1">{{cite web |url=http://www.thisisfakediy.co.uk/articles/news/listen-royal-blood-share-out-of-the-black-track/ |title=Royal Blood Share 'Out Of The Black' Track |work=[[DIY (magazine)|This Is Fake DIY]] |date= 23 September 2013 |archive-date=26 September 2013 |first=Jamie |last=Milton |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130926080252/http://www.thisisfakediy.co.uk/articles/news/listen-royal-blood-share-out-of-the-black-track/}}</ref> which came with the [[A-side and B-side|B-side]] of the re-recorded "Come On Over".<ref name="guardian1">{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/dec/02/royal-blood |title=Royal Blood (New band of the day No 1,652) | work=[[The Guardian]] |date= 2 December 2013 | access-date=28 January 2014 |first=Paul |last=Lester}}</ref> In November 2013, it was announced that Royal Blood would be supporting Arctic Monkeys for two [[Finsbury Park]] shows in May 2014.<ref name="xfm1">{{cite web |url=https://www.xfm.co.uk/new-music/great-x-pectations/royal-blood/ |title=XFM's Great X-Pectations #1: Royal Blood | publisher=[[Radio X (United Kingdom)|XFM]] |date= 6 January 2014 | archive-date=26 January 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140126054004/https://www.xfm.co.uk/new-music/great-x-pectations/royal-blood/}}</ref> In December 2013, Royal Blood were nominated for [[Sound of...#Sound of 2014|BBC Sound of 2014]].<ref name="bbc1">{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-25151475 |title=BBC Sound of 2014 longlist revealed | publisher=[[BBC News Online|BBC News]] |date= 2 December 2013 | access-date=8 February 2014}}</ref> Royal Blood released their second single, "[[Little Monster (song)|Little Monster]]", on 11&nbsp;February 2014. The band also released a four-track EP ''[[Out of the Black (EP)|Out of the Black]]'', featuring "Out of the Black", "Little Monster", "Come On Over" and "Hole" on 11&nbsp;March 2014 in North America.<ref name="itunes.apple.com">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/out-of-the-black-ep/id834515548?uo=4&at=11lqtn|title=iTunes Store|publisher=[[iTunes]]|access-date=21 September 2014}}</ref>

Royal Blood performed at [[South by Southwest]] in [[Austin, Texas]] in March 2014;<ref name="star1">{{cite web |url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2014/03/15/uk_duo_royal_blood_heard_above_the_noise_at_sxsw.html |title=UK duo Royal Blood heard above the noise at SXSW |work=[[Toronto Star]] |date= 15 March 2014 | access-date=22 April 2014 |first=Ben |last=Rayner}}</ref> at [[Liverpool Sound City]] festival and at [[BBC Radio 1's Big Weekend]] in Glasgow in May;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.liverpoolsoundcity.co.uk/profile.aspx/View/510|title=Liverpool Sound City 2015|publisher=[[Liverpool Sound City]]|access-date=21 September 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140507003837/http://www.liverpoolsoundcity.co.uk/profile.aspx/View/510|archive-date=7 May 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref> and at [[Download Festival]] and [[Glastonbury Festival 2014|Glastonbury Festival]] in June.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/events/errnc8/performances |title=BBC – Glastonbury 2014 – Line up |publisher=[[BBC]] | access-date=22 April 2014}}</ref> They performed at [[Rock Werchter]] and [[T in the Park]] festival in July, and at [[Reading and Leeds Festivals|Reading Festival]], but got cut short at their Leeds Festival show, as well as Osheaga in August 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.readingfestival.com/news/arctic-monkeys-headline|title=Arctic Monkeys to headline|work=Reading Festival 2015|archive-date=27 October 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141027142510/https://www.readingfestival.com/news/arctic-monkeys-headline}}</ref>

Their debut album, ''[[Royal Blood (album)|Royal Blood]]'', was released on 25&nbsp;August 2014 on [[Warner Bros. Records]]. Well received by critics,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/royal-blood-60-1245654 |title=Royal Blood reveal debut album details with studio video – watch |work=[[NME]] |date=23 June 2014 |access-date=9 July 2014 |first=Jenny |last=Stevens}}</ref> it was verified by the [[Official Charts Company]] as the fastest-selling British rock debut album in three years in the UK.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Moss|first1=Liv|title=Royal Blood score fastest-selling rock debut in three years|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/royal-blood-score-fastest-selling-rock-debut-in-three-years-3085/|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]] |date=31 August 2014 |archive-date=10 January 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150110162500/https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/royal-blood-score-fastest-selling-rock-debut-in-three-years-3085/}}</ref> The cover artwork, by London-based contemporary artist Dan Hillier, won the [[Best Art Vinyl]] award in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|title=Best album cover art: See the winners of the Best Art Vinyl 2014 awards | url=http://www.digitalartsonline.co.uk/news/graphic-design/best-album-art-2014 |website=DigitalArtsOnline.co.uk|date=6 January 2015 |access-date=30 June 2017 |first=Michael |last=Burns}}</ref> A video clip for "Out of the Black" entered rotation as part of cable network [[AXN (Asia)|AXN Japan's]] "AXN Tunes" programme in October 2014; music videos aired on-the-hour as filler, in between their regularly scheduled timetabled programming.<ref name="AXN">[[AXN (Asia)|AXN Japan; Sony Pictures Entertainment]] |Royal Blood "Out of the Black" |[[Warner Music Group|Warner Music Japan]] |"AXN TUNES" video clip rotation, 9 October 2014~</ref>

In October 2014, Royal Blood announced details of their biggest European tour to date,<ref name="ReferenceA">{{cite web|url=http://www.fit4talent.com/news/royal-blood-announce-a-major-2015-european-tour/2011/|title=Royal Blood announce a major 2015 European tour|date=17 October 2014|website=Fit4Talent.com |archive-date=26 October 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141026020734/http://www.fit4talent.com/news/royal-blood-announce-a-major-2015-european-tour/2011/}}</ref> starting in Germany on 10&nbsp;January, and ending in Sweden on 23&nbsp;March. The band, along with [[Iggy Pop]], supported [[Foo Fighters]] on selected dates during their 2015 UK, US and Canada tours. In February 2015, at the [[Brit Awards]] in London, [[Jimmy Page]] presented them with the Best British Group Award.<ref name="BritAwards2015">{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/feb/25/royal-blood-win-best-british-group-at-brit-awards-2015|title=Royal Blood win best British group at Brit awards 2015|first=Michael|last=Hann|date=25 February 2015|work=[[The Guardian]]}}</ref> In April 2015, they played the [[Coachella Festival]] in California, in June 2015 they played twice at [[Rock Werchter]] and at Bunbury Music Festival in Cincinnati and, in September 2015, they played at the [[Rock in Rio]] festival, in [[Rio de Janeiro]].

On 29 and 30 August 2015, Royal Blood played the main stage at Reading and Leeds 2015. During both of these performances, the band revealed a new song titled "Hook, Line & Sinker". In October 2015, the band had finished touring for their debut album. The song "Careless" is used as the opening theme of the French version of the TV show ''Tattoo Fixers''.

===2016–2018: ''How Did We Get So Dark?''===
On 16 October 2016, a video was uploaded to the band's official [[Instagram]] account showing the band in the studio. The video showed Mike Kerr playing the [[Robbie Williams]] track "[[Let Me Entertain You (Robbie Williams song)|Let Me Entertain You]]" on piano, with Ben Thatcher recording the video and singing. The video was captioned "Day 1. Robbie Williams #royalblooddocovers". They then continued these short covers on Instagram, covering songs from [[Vanessa Carlton]], [[Gloria Gaynor]], [[Spandau Ballet]], [[Elton John]], [[Coldplay]], and [[Michael Jackson]]. On 29 March 2016, the band released a new track, "Where Are You Now?" inspired by the American TV series ''[[Vinyl (TV series)|Vinyl]]''. The song was later re-recorded during the making of their second album ''[[How Did We Get So Dark?]]''<ref name="howlandechoes">{{cite web|url=http://howlandechoes.com/2016/04/listen-new-royal-blood-track/ |title=Royal Blood's New Track Has The Makings Of A Modern Rock Hit |website=howlandechoes.com |date=1 April 2016 | first=Jessica |last=Testa}}</ref>

Following its announcement on 11 April 2017, Royal Blood released their second album, ''How Did We Get So Dark?'' on 16&nbsp;June 2017, preceded by the singles "[[Lights Out (Royal Blood song)|Lights Out]]", "Hook, Line & Sinker" and "I Only Lie When I Love You." It debuted at number one on the [[UK Albums Chart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/royal-blood-rule-the-official-albums-chart-to-score-a-second-number-1-with-how-did-we-get-so-dark__19494/|title=Royal Blood rule the Official Albums Chart to score a second Number 1 with How Did We Get So Dark?|last=Myers|first=Justin|date=23 June 2017|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]] |access-date=23 June 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nme.com/news/music/royal-blood-beating-lorde-nickelback-uk-albums-number-one-spot-2090687|title=Royal Blood beat Nickelback and Lorde to UK albums number one spot |last=Morgan Britton|first=Luke|date=23 June 2017|work=[[NME]]|access-date=23 June 2017}}</ref> Royal Blood were part of the [[WayHome Music & Arts Festival#2017 Line-up|WayHome Music & Arts Festival 2017 Line-up]] in Oro-Medonte, Ontario.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://proudfm.com/event/wayhome-music-festival/|title=WayHome Music & Arts Festival 2017 line-up|publisher=[[CIRR-FM|103.9 Proud FM]]|access-date=22 August 2018}}</ref> In April 2017, Royal Blood announced four UK shows, starting in Cambridge on 17&nbsp;May and ending in Leicester on 20&nbsp;May.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gettothefront.co.uk/royal-blood-new-2017-uk-shows/|title=Royal Blood – New 2017 UK Shows – Latest Music News + Gig Tickets From Get To The Front – Music News Magazine|date=19 April 2017}}</ref> In June 2017, Royal Blood played at Glastonbury on the Pyramid Stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/line-up/line-up-2017/?stage|title=Glastonbury Festival Line-up 2017: Wed 21st – Sun 25th June 2017 |publisher=[[Glastonbury Festival]]}}</ref> In addition, on 5&nbsp;June, the band announced a tour in Europe on their social media, which took place throughout October and November 2017. In May 2018, Royal Blood went on a headlining US tour in continued support of their second album, which included more acts alongside [[Queens of the Stone Age]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/12/royal-blood-announce-2018-us-headlining-tour/amp/|title=Royal Blood announce 2018 US headlining tour|work=[[Consequence of Sound]]|date=4 December 2017|access-date=4 November 2018|language=en-US |first=Lake |last=Schatz}}</ref>

===2019–present: ''Typhoons''===
In June 2019, Kerr and Thatcher presented [[Jimmy Page]] with the Icon Award at the [[Kerrang! Awards 2019|Kerrang! Awards]].<ref>''Kerrang!'', issue 1779, 29 June 2019, p. 28.</ref> The following month, Royal Blood debuted two new songs, "Boilermaker" and "King", in [[Wiesbaden]], Germany. In March 2020, the band announced they had begun recording their third studio album, though production was delayed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B9pGaHvlBkE/|title=Royal Blood on Instagram: "Self isolation has begun. For the next five weeks we will be in the studio recording an album. Stay tuned into our stories and I will keep…"|via=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2020-03-21}}</ref> On 24 September 2020, the band released "[[Trouble's Coming]]", the first single from their third studio album ''[[Typhoons (album)|Typhoons]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/royal-blood-troubles-coming-new-third-album-mike-kerr-interview-2760808|title=Royal Blood tell us about 'Trouble's Coming' and their new album: "We're seeing in colour for the first time"|date=24 September 2020}}</ref> In October 2020, the single was featured as a part of both the ''[[NHL 21]]'' and ''[[FIFA 21]]'' soundtracks. 

On 21&nbsp;January 2021, the second single "[[Typhoons (song)|Typhoons]]" was released, along with the announcement of the album itself, which was released on 30 April 2021 and features the previously previewed track "Boilermaker". Royal Blood contributed a cover of the Metallica song "[[Sad but True|Sad But True]]" to the charity tribute album ''[[The Metallica Blacklist]]'', released in September 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=He|first=Richard S.|date=2021-09-10|title=Every Metallica Blacklist cover ranked from worst to best|url=https://www.loudersound.com/features/every-metallica-blacklist-cover-ranked-from-worst-to-best|access-date=2021-10-22|website=loudersound|language=en}}</ref>
