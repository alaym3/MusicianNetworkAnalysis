'''Travis Montorius Greene''' (born January 17, 1984) is an American [[gospel music]]ian and pastor.

== Music career ==
Greene began his music career in 2007 with the release of ''The More'' via Greenelight Records. He has since released two [[studio albums]], ''Stretching Out'' in 2010, ''The Hill'' in 2015, and an [[extended play]], ''Intentional'' in 2015. Travis Greene's third album ''Crossover: Live From Music City'' was released in 2017 as a live album. Greene's music has charted on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Gospel Albums]] and the Top Gospel Songs charts and he has been nominated for multiple awards, including five [[Grammy Award]]s and 13 [[Stellar Awards]].

Greene and his wife lead Forward City Church in [[Columbia, South Carolina]].

== Early life ==
Greene was born, Travis Montorius Greene,<ref name="SESAC2">{{cite web|url=http://www.sesac.com/Repertory/SongsByWriter.aspx?affilNum=383167&writerAKAName=TRAVIS_MONTORIUS_GREENE|title=Songs by Writer :: Travis Montorius Greene|publisher=[[SESAC]]|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> on January 17,<ref name="Day2">{{cite web|url=https://instagram.com/p/x-VybgBjOp/|title=Spending my birthday at the BMI Trailblazers Awards with my bro Ricky!!|date=January 17, 2015|publisher=Instagram|author=Travis Greene (travisgreenetv)|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> 1984,<ref name="Intelius2">{{cite web|url=http://www.intelius.com/results.php?ReportType=1&formname=name&qf=Travis&qmi=M&qn=Greene&qcs=Warner+Robins%2C+GA&focusfirst=1|title=Travis M Greene in Warner Robins, GA|publisher=Intelius|author=Intelius|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> in [[Delaware]], to mother, Charleather Greene<ref name="Dream2">{{cite web|url=http://www.dreamstudiosga.com/dream-studios-interviews-gospel-artist-travis-greene|title=Dream Studios Interviews Gospel Artist Travis Greene|publisher=Dream Studios|author=Daily Dream Dose|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> and was raised in [[Warner Robins, Georgia]].<ref name="Dream2" /> Greene's father died of an [[aneurysm]] in 1989.{{citation needed|date=April 2017}} Greene was originally a still-born, but was resuscitated. A few years later, at the age of four he was resuscitated after a four-storied building fall, while he was in Germany.<ref name="AM Bio2">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/travis-greene-mn0002114297/biography|title=Travis Greene : Biography|last=Kellman|first=Andy|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref>

==Career==
Greene's music recording started in 2007, with the release, ''The More'', that came out on December 4, 2007,<ref name="NRT Album One2">{{cite web|url=http://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=5664|title=The More|date=July 5, 2008|publisher=New Release Today|author=TravisGreene|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> from Greenelight Records.<ref name="AM Disc3">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/travis-greene-mn0002114297/discography|title=Travis Greene : Discography|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|author=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> The subsequent release, ''Stretching Out'', a [[studio album]], was released on June 8, 2010,<ref name="NRT Album Two2">{{cite web|url=http://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=9135|title=Stretching Out|date=July 8, 2010|publisher=New Release Today|author=NRTTeamAdmin|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> by Pendulum Records.<ref name="AM Disc3"/>

The release of ''Stretching Out'' was Greene's introduction to the ''Billboard'' [[Top Gospel Albums]] chart, where it placed at No. 27.<ref name="AM Awards3">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/travis-greene-mn0002114297/awards|title=Travis Greene : Awards|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> Songs, "Still Here" and "Prove My Love", both charted on the ''Billboard'' [[Top Gospel Albums|Top Gospel]] Songs chart, at peaks of Nos. 17 and 29, respectively.<ref name="AM Awards3"/>

Greene released an [[extended play]], ''Intentional'', on August 21, 2015, with [[RCA Inspiration]], where this placed at No. 3 on the ''Billboard'' [[Top Gospel Albums]] chart.<ref name="AM Awards3"/> His song, "Intentional", peaked at No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Top Gospel Songs chart .<ref name="AM Awards3" />

Greene's second studio album, ''The Hill'', was released on October 30, 2015,<ref name="NRT Album Three2">{{cite web|url=http://www.newreleasetoday.com/albumdetail.php?album_id=15726|title=The Hill|date=October 29, 2015|publisher=New Release Today|author=NRTTeamAdmin|access-date=November 1, 2015}}</ref> from RCA Inspiration<ref name="AM Disc3"/> and charted at No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Gospel charts.

Greene's rise in the gospel genre has been reported in ''Billboard Magazine'',<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7511223/travis-greene-made-his-way-to-no-1-on-hot-gospel-songs-chart|title=Travis Greene 'Made' His 'Way' to No. 1 on Hot Gospel Songs Chart|last=Asker|first=Jim|magazine=Billboard|date=September 17, 2016}}</ref> ''Rolling Out Magazine'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rollingout.com/2017/01/27/travis-greenes-no-1-single-lands-wow-gospel-2017/|title=Travis Greene's No. 1 single lands on 'WOW Gospel 2017'|website=Rolling Out|last=Caslin|first=Yvette|date=January 27, 2017}}</ref> ''Jet'' magazine,<ref name="jetmag.com">{{cite web|url=https://www.jetmag.com/entertainment/is-travis-greene-the-future-of-gospel-music/|title=Is Travis Greene the Future of Gospel Music?|last=Young|first=Tamara|website=Jet Magazine|date=May 20, 2016}}</ref> with performances at the [[Essence Music Festival]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gettyimages.com/detail/news-photo/musician-travis-greene-performs-onstage-at-the-2016-essence-news-photo/544394798|title=Musician Travis Greene performs onstage at the 2016 ESSENCE Festival...|website=Getty Images|accessdate=July 22, 2021}}</ref> Trumpet Awards and BMI Trailblazers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://getuperica.com/24971/marvin-sapp-more-honored-at-2017-bmi-trailblazers-of-gospel-music-honors/|title=Marvin Sapp & More Honored at 2017 BMI Trailblazers Of Gospel Music Honors|date=January 16, 2017|accessdate=July 22, 2021}}</ref> In 2016, ''JET Magazine'' called Greene "The Future of Gospel".<ref name="jetmag.com"/>

In 2017, Greene received criticism for his decision to perform at President [[Donald Trump]]'s [[inaugural ball]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tmz.com/2017/01/17/donald-trump-inauguration-gospel-singer-travis-greene/|title=Gospel Singer Not Afraid to Say He's Performing at Donald Trump Inauguration (VIDEO)|website=TMZ|accessdate=July 22, 2021}}</ref>

On February 2, 2017, Greene recorded his third album ''Crossover: Live From Music City'' in Nashville, Tennessee.<ref>http://gospelflava.com/v2/travis-greene-heads-to-nashville-for-live-recording/</ref>

On March 24, 2017, Greene led the field of nominees at the 2017 Stellar Gospel Music Awards by taking home seven awards. At the awards, Greene performed a medley of "Made a Way" alongside Gospel recording artists [[Israel Houghton]], [[Jonathan McReynolds]] and [[Jonathan Butler]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.premierfestivals.com/blog/travis-greene-sweeps-seven-awards-stellar-awards|title=Travis Greene Sweeps Seven Awards at The Stellar Awards!|date=March 30, 2017|website=Premier Festivals|accessdate=July 22, 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Greene married Jackie Gyamfi and together they co-pastor Forward City Church in [[Columbia, South Carolina]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://forwardcity.tv/welcome/|title=This Is Home|website=Forward City Church|language=en-US|access-date=August 16, 2018}}</ref>
