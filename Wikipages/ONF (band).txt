'''ONF''' (pronounced as ''On and Off'', {{Korean|온앤오프}}) is a South Korean [[boy group]] formed and managed by [[WM Entertainment]]. Originally debuting as a seven-piece group on August 3, 2017 with their [[Extended play|EP]], the group is now composed of six members: Hyojin, E-Tion, J-Us, Wyatt, MK and U. The group's youngest member, Laun, left the group on August 23, 2019, shortly before the group's fourth extended play ''[[Go Live (EP)|Go Live]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=온앤오프 프로필|url=https://people.search.naver.com/search.naver?where=nexearch&sm=tab_ppn&query=%EC%98%A8%EC%95%A4%EC%98%A4%ED%94%84&os=6141730&ie=utf8&key=PeopleService|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190725065627/https://people.search.naver.com/search.naver?where=nexearch&sm=tab_ppn&query=%EC%98%A8%EC%95%A4%EC%98%A4%ED%94%84&os=6141730&ie=utf8&key=PeopleService|archive-date=July 25, 2019|access-date=July 25, 2019|publisher=Naver|language=ko}}</ref> Since their debut, ONF has since released six Korean extended plays, a full-length album, a reissue, and two Japanese singles following their Japanese debut on August 1, 2018.

== History ==

=== Formation and pre-debut activities ===
Prior to joining WM Entertainment, U was a trainee at [[JYP Entertainment]], while MK was a trainee at [[Starship Entertainment]].<ref>{{cite news|date=2 August 2017|title='데뷔' 온앤오프 유 "데뷔 전 JYP 연습생..한국어 위해 삼겹살 알바"|language=ko|publisher=Herald Corp|url=https://pop.heraldcorp.com/view.php?ud=201708021048112701652_1|access-date=July 25, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190725062823/https://pop.heraldcorp.com/view.php?ud=201708021048112701652_1|archive-date=July 25, 2019}}</ref> In 2015, MK participated in [[Mnet (TV channel)|Mnet]] and Starship Entertainment's survival program ''No.Mercy'' but was eliminated during episode 7.<ref>{{Cite web|author=1theK|date=Jan 22, 2015|title=[NO.MERCY(노머시)] Ep.7 3rd Debut Mission isn't Over Yet! 끝나지 않은 3차 데뷔 미션 [ENG SUB]|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BLq3GOdoFpg|website=YouTube}}</ref> During the group's first showcase, Laun revealed that he was a trainee at [[Big Hit Entertainment]] and used to train together with boy group [[BTS (band)|BTS]].<ref>{{cite news|title=온앤오프 라온 "방탄소년단과 데뷔 전 연습, 잘돼서 뿌듯해"|url=https://www.tvreport.co.kr/?c=news&m=newsview&idx=1000242|access-date=July 25, 2019|author=Lee Woo-in|agency=TV Report|publisher=OSEN|date=August 2, 2017|language=ko|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190725062945/https://www.tvreport.co.kr/?c=news&m=newsview&idx=1000242|archive-date=July 25, 2019}}</ref> All seven members were eventually introduced as WM Boys, and had their first appearance as [[B1A4]]'s backup dancers at the 2016 Dream Concert in [[Seoul]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=OSEN|date=2017-05-26|title=[단독] B1A4 남동생 그룹, 7인조 확정.."이르면 7월 데뷔"|url=https://mosen.mt.co.kr/article/G1110653284|access-date=2020-06-18|website=mosen.mt.co.kr|language=ko}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=May 26, 2017|title='아이돌콘' B1A4 남동생그룹 "첫 정식무대 많이 긴장, 영광스럽다"|url=https://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201705262013250410}}</ref> In addition, member J-Us, Laun, and Wyatt made a cameo appearance in labelmate B1A4's mini drama VCR "The Class", while E-Tion and Wyatt appeared in another B1A4 VCR called "Feeling". On May 25, 2017, all of the members of WM Boys attended IDOLCON (아이돌콘) and performed "Original", a B-side track from their upcoming debut extended play.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201705262013250410|title='아이돌콘' B1A4 남동생그룹 "첫 정식무대 많이 긴장, 영광스럽다"|author=Hwang Hye-jin|date=May 26, 2017|website=Newsen|publisher=Newsen|language=ko|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190725055055/https://www.newsen.com/news_view.php?uid=201705262013250410|archive-date=July 25, 2019|access-date=July 25, 2019}}</ref>
