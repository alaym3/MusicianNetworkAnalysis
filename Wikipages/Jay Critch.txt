'''Jason Cole Critchlow''' (born February 25, 1998),<ref>https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/search/workID/896979388 {{Bare URL inline|date=September 2021}}</ref> known professionally as '''Jay Critch''', is an American rapper, singer and songwriter. He is currently signed with Rich Forever Music.

==Early life==
Jason Cole Critchlow grew up in the [[Clinton Hill, Brooklyn|Clinton Hill]] neighborhood of [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City|New York]] on February 25, 1998 to a [[Trinidadian]] mother and a [[British people|British]]-[[Guyanese people|Guyanese]] father.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|date=March 2, 2018|title=Jay Critch is the new New York|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/03/02/jay-crtich-did-it-again-fashion-interview|url-status=live|website=[[The FADER]]|language=en}}</ref> As a child, he was fascinated by listening to his older brother rap.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jay Critch|url=https://www.interscope.com/artists/jay-critch|date=2018-06-04|website=Interscope Records|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref> Critch grew up listening to [[Fabolous]], [[Lil Wayne]], and [[Jay-Z]], as well as many rappers from New York City.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Break Presents: Jay Critch - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/10/jay-critch-interview-the-break/|last=Krastz|first=Roger|date=October 13, 2017|website=XXL Mag|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref>

==Career==
Jay Critch's first major collaboration was with [[Rowdy Rebel]] on the song "Man Down" in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hood favorite Jay Critch (Rich Forever & Talk Money Entertainment)|url=https://lemonwire.com/2018/10/19/hood-favorite-jay-critch-rich-forever-talk-money-entertainment/|last=Romaker|first=Ian|date=2018-10-19|website=LemonWire|access-date=2020-05-18}}</ref> In 2016, he began uploading songs on [[SoundCloud]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Jay Critch {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/jay-critch-mn0003638233/biography|website=AllMusic|access-date=2020-05-18}}</ref> He became the subject of widespread attention in 2016, when his song "Did It Again" was remixed by [[Rich the Kid]], who signed him to [[Rich Forever Music]] in November.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rich The Kid and Jay Critch Got a Hit With 'Did It Again (Remix)' - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/new-music/2016/12/rich-the-kid-and-jay-critch-got-a-hit-with-did-it-again-remix/|last=C.M|first=Emmanuel|date=December 1, 2016|website=XXL Mag|access-date=2020-05-18}}</ref> Their collaboration went viral, with the music video gaining over a million views in the first week of its release.<ref name=":0" />

In November 2018, as a newly signed artist, Jay Critch released his debut solo mixtape, ''Hood Favorite''. It features guest appearances from [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]], [[French Montana]] and [[Fabolous]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jay Critch 'Hood Favorite' Mixtape: Listen to 12 New Songs - XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/11/jay-critch-hood-favorite-mixtape-offset-french-montana/|last=Haffenden|first=Dayna|website=XXL Mag|access-date=2020-05-18}}</ref> He also was featured on [[Lil Tjay]]'s "[[True 2 Myself|Ruthless]]", which was certified platinum.

In December 2020, Jay Critch released his third mixtape, ''Signed with Love''.<ref name="CritchTape">{{cite web|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/jay-critch-returns-with-his-new-project-critch-tape-new-mixtape.121498.html%3f|title=Jay Critch Returns With His New Project "Critch Tape"|work=HotNewHipHop|last=A.|first=Aron|date=May 5, 2021|accessdate=May 7, 2021}}</ref>

On May 5, 2021, Jay returned with his mixtape, ''Critch Tape''. Announced just hours before its release, the project contains 23 songs, with features from [[Fivio Foreign]], [[Lil Tjay]], and [[Drakeo the Ruler]], among others.<ref name="CritchTape" />
