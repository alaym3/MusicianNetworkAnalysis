'''Lianne Charlotte Barnes''' (born 23 August 1989),<ref name="Stevenson">{{cite news|last=Stevenson |first=Jane |url=http://www.torontosun.com/2012/08/07/lianne-la-havas-always-in-good-company |title=Lianne La Havas always in good company &#124; Entertainment |newspaper=Toronto Sun |access-date=2015-07-31}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/music/2009/apr/22/new-band-paloma-faith |location= London |work= The Guardian |first=Paul |last=Lester |title= Paloma Faith (No 532) |date=22 April 2009}}</ref> known professionally as '''Lianne La Havas''' ({{IPAc-en|l|i|ˈ|æ|n|_|l|ə|ˈ|h|æ|v|ə|s}}),<ref>{{cite web |title=Lianne La Havas interview (part 2) |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UYSw-YyrG6E&t=183 |publisher=FaceCulture |access-date=26 December 2019}}</ref> is a British singer-songwriter and record producer. Her career began after being introduced to various musicians, including singer [[Paloma Faith]], for whom she sang backing vocals. In 2010, La Havas signed to [[Warner Bros. Records]], spending two years developing her songwriting, before releasing any music. La Havas' debut studio album, ''[[Is Your Love Big Enough?]]'' (2012), was released to positive reviews from critics and earned her a nomination for the BBC's [[Sound of...|Sound of 2012]] poll and awards for the [[iTunes]] Album of The Year 2012.<ref name=so12>{{cite web |url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/soundof/2012/artists/liannelahavas |title= Sound of – 2012 – Lianne La Havas |publisher=BBC |date=22 November 2011 |access-date= 1 January 2012}}</ref>

==Early life==
La Havas was born and raised in London, England, to a Greek father and Jamaican mother.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/LianneLaHavas?sk=info |title=Lianne La Havas – About |via=Facebook |date=1989-08-23 |access-date=2015-07-31}}{{Primary source inline|date=July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2011/oct/30/one-to-watch-la-havas |title=One to watch: Lianne La Havas  |author=Tim Lewis|newspaper=The Guardian |date=13 March 2014 |access-date=31 July 2015}}</ref> She was raised in [[Tooting]] and [[Streatham]], spending the majority of her time with her grandparents after her parents separated.<ref name="thisislondon.co.uk">{{cite web|author=Jasmine Gardner |url=http://www.thisislondon.co.uk/arts/music/sound-of-the-summer-lianne-le-havas-7637167.html |title=Sound of the summer: Lianne Le Havas |work=London Evening Standard |date=12 April 2012 |access-date=15 June 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120420010714/http://www.thisislondon.co.uk/arts/music/sound-of-the-summer-lianne-le-havas-7637167.html |archive-date=20 April 2012 }}</ref> La Havas began singing at seven, and cites her parents' diverse musical tastes as having the biggest influence on her music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nonesuch.com/journal/nonesuch-release-english-singer-songwriter-guitarist-lianne-la-havas-debut-album-us-summer-1st-la-show-may-22-2012-04-03 |title=Nonesuch to Release English Singer/Songwriter/Guitarist Lianne La Havas's Debut Album in US This Summer; First LA Show May 22  |publisher=Nonesuch Records – Journal |date=3 April 2012 |access-date=15 June 2012}}</ref> Her father, an accomplished multi-instrumentalist, taught her the basics of guitar and piano. La Havas wrote her first song at the age of 11, but did not learn to play the guitar until she was 18 years old. She sang in her school choir.<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|title = Soulful Sensation|last = Ramirez|first = E|date = 25 August 2012|journal = Billboard}}</ref> She attended [[Norbury Manor Business and Enterprise College for Girls]] in [[Thornton Heath]] where she studied art [[A-level]], and had planned to take an art foundation course before she decided to leave college to pursue a career in music full-time.<ref name="thisislondon.co.uk"/> Her birth name is Lianne Barnes, and her stage name is an adaptation of her [[Greeks|Greek]] father Henry Vlahavas' [[Greek name|surname]]. She lives in London.<ref name="Stevenson"/>

==Career==
===Beginnings and debut (2008–14)===
[[File:Lianne La Havas 2012.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Lianne La Havas in 2012.]]
Whilst attending sixth-form in Croydon, La Havas was introduced to musicians who would assist her in the recording of her first demos.<ref name="thisislondon.co.uk"/> Through that same friend La Havas was also introduced to British singer [[Paloma Faith]]; she later sang backing vocals on tour for Faith. La Havas was discovered on Myspace in 2008. Duncan Ellis, owner of Scruffy Bird Artist Management, first heard of La Havas through colleague Alex Hardee of the Coda Music Agency.<ref name=":0" /> When Ellis first saw La Havas perform, she was in the duo the Paris Parade, collaborating with future Elephant member Christian Pinchebeck (who designed the artwork for ''Lost & Found''); their short-lived career would kick off La Havas' career in commercial music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ligaturemagazine.co.uk/issue4/lianne-la-havas.html |title=Ligature Magazine. Retrieved October 17 |publisher=Ligaturemagazine.co.uk |access-date=18 October 2011}}</ref> In 2010, Lianne signed to [[Warner Bros. Records]], spending two years developing her songwriting skills before releasing any music publicly.<ref name="thisisfakediy.co.uk">{{cite web|url=http://www.thisisfakediy.co.uk/articles/first-on/lianne-la-havas-a-lot-has-happened-in-my-life/ |title=Lianne La Havas: 'A Lot Has Happened In My Life' &#124; DIY |publisher=Thisisfakediy.co.uk |access-date=15 June 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://yinnyang.co.uk/2011/06/3-uk-female-singers-to-look-out-for/ |title=3 UK Female Singers to Look Out For |publisher=Yinnyang.co.uk |date=22 June 2011 |access-date=18 October 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111109015634/http://yinnyang.co.uk/2011/06/3-uk-female-singers-to-look-out-for/ |archive-date=9 November 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Her first [[Extended play|EP]] ''Lost & Found'' was released on 21 October 2011 on the Labour of Love label,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/Lost-Found-Lianne-Havas/dp/B005VCASKY/ref=sr_1_1?s=music&ie=UTF8&qid=1318850946&sr=1-1 |title=Lost and Found: Lianne La Havas |date=9 September 2009 |access-date=18 October 2011}}</ref> featuring [[Willy Mason]] on the opening track "No Room For Doubt".<ref>Ligature Magazine.</ref> That same month, La Havas released the live EP ''Live From LA'', which was made available for free download on her website.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://pinboardblog.com/2011/10/freep-lianne-la-havas-live-in-l-a/ |title=FreEP: Lianne La Havas – "Live In L.A" " PinBoardBlog.com – Alt-Urban Specialists |publisher=Pinboardblog.com |access-date=18 October 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111015064259/http://pinboardblog.com/2011/10/freep-lianne-la-havas-live-in-l-a/ |archive-date=15 October 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Peter Vidani |url=http://ladybangbeat.com/post/11187161569/lianne-la-havas-has-two-new-releases-one-is |title=Lady. Bang. Beat |publisher=Lady. Bang. Beat. |date=8 October 2011 |access-date=18 October 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120330000908/http://ladybangbeat.com/post/11187161569/lianne-la-havas-has-two-new-releases-one-is |archive-date=30 March 2012 }}</ref> La Havas made her television debut on 21 October 2011 broadcast of [[BBC Two]]'s ''[[Later… with Jools Holland]]'', a programme that also featured Wisconsin folk band [[Bon Iver]];<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b016d61y |title=BBC BBC Two Programmes – Later... with Jools Holland, Series 39, Episode 5 |publisher=BBC |access-date=18 October 2011}}</ref> soon after this on 25 October 2011 it was announced that La Havas would be the supporting act for [[Bon Iver]]'s December 2011 North American tour.<ref>{{cite web |last=Souza |first=Kris De |url=http://thefourohfive.com/news/article/lianne-la-havas-confirmed-for-bon-iver-s-december-us-tour |title=Lianne La Havas confirmed for Bon Iver's December US tour |publisher=Thefourohfive.com |date=2011-10-25 |access-date=2015-07-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131225010637/http://thefourohfive.com/news/article/lianne-la-havas-confirmed-for-bon-iver-s-december-us-tour |archive-date=25 December 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Her official debut single "Lost & Found" was released in the UK on 30 April 2012,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.femalefirst.co.uk/music/musicnews/Lianne+La+Havas-230563.html |title=Lianne La Havas' Sold Out Scala Show To Be Streamed Live |publisher=Femalefirst.co.uk |date=6 March 2012 |access-date=15 June 2012}}</ref> and her debut album, ''[[Is Your Love Big Enough?]]'' was released on 9 July 2012 on Warner Brothers.<ref name="thisisfakediy.co.uk"/> At the end of 2012, ''Is Your Love Big Enough?'' was named iTunes Album of the Year.

On 24 September 2012 La Havas supported [[Alicia Keys]] at MTV 'Crashes' Manchester, a live performance in front of 1,000 people in Manchester Cathedral which was broadcast in 164 countries. On 31 December 2012, she appeared on [[BBC Two]]'s New Year's Eve show ''Jools' Annual Hootenanny'' singing ''Cow Cow Boogie''. On 9 June 2013, La Havas played at the [[RockNess]] music festival in [[Inverness]], Scotland,<ref name=STV_rockness>{{cite web|last=Lynn|first=Kirstin|title=RockNess 2013: Lianne La Havas raises the bar in supreme soul diva set|url=http://entertainment.stv.tv/music/228744-rockness-2013-lianne-la-havas-review/|publisher=STV|access-date=3 July 2013}}</ref> and on 30 June 2013 La Havas performed at the [[Glastonbury Festival 2013]].<ref name=Telegraph_glast13>{{cite news|last=Lachno|first=James|title=Glastonbury 2013, Day Three review: other stages: Bobby Womack, Lianne Le Havas, Sir Bruce Forsyth|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/glastonbury/10152062/Glastonbury-2013-Day-Three-review-other-stages-Bobby-Womack-Lianne-Le-Havas-Sir-Bruce-Forsyth.html|access-date=3 July 2013|newspaper=The Telegraph|date=1 July 2013|location=London}}</ref> La Havas also performed at the Isle of Wight Festival in June 2013.

In early 2014, [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] played a gig in her living room in London.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-26045864|title=Prince to charge $10 for live shows|publisher=BBC|date=5 February 2014|access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> She also appeared on [[Alt-J]]'s 2014 album ''[[This Is All Yours]]'' on the track "Warm Foothills". On 30 September Prince released ''[[Art Official Age]]'', featuring La Havas heavily through the album, singing in "Clouds" and providing vocals on "Affirmation I&II", "Way Back Home" and "Affirmation III".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/reviews/alt-j/15628|title=Alt-J – 'This Is All Yours'|publisher=Time Inc. (UK)|first=Mark|last=Beaumont|date=15 September 2014|access-date=1 November 2014}}</ref> La Havas performed with [[Prince (musician)|Prince]] on an episode of ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' as a featured vocalist on 1 November 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nonesuch.com/journal/watch-lianne-la-havas-performs-saturday-night-live-prince-2014-11-03|title=Watch:Lianne La Havas Performs on "Saturday Night Live" with Prince |publisher=Nonesuch |date=3 November 2014 |access-date=12 January 2015}}</ref> She collaborated with [[Aqualung (musician)|Aqualung]] on the song "Eggshells" from his album ''10 Features'', released on 19 January 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://diymag.com/2015/01/09/aqualung-unveils-video-for-eggshells-featuring-lianne-la-havas|title=AQUALUNG UNVEILS VIDEO FOR 'EGGSHELLS', FEATURING LIANNE LA HAVAS|publisher=DIY Magazine|date=9 January 2015 |access-date=12 January 2015}}</ref>

===2015–19: ''Blood''===
After touring was completed for her first album, La Havas and her mother travelled to Jamaica. During the trip, La Havas was inspired to write new material, which would eventually become part of her second album. She gave a live performance for her family with producer, [[Di Genius|Stephen McGregor]], who would later co-produce her forthcoming album. Many of the songs were inspired by La Havas' reconnection to her Greek and Jamaican heritage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marketwired.com/press-release/lianne-la-havas-debuts-new-song-unstoppable-april-21-2011765.htm|title=Lianne La Havas: 'debuts knew song|publisher=Marketwired.com|date=21 April 2015|access-date=25 April 2015}}</ref> The first single, "Unstoppable", from this album, ''[[Blood (Lianne La Havas album)|Blood]]'', premiered for streaming on 1 April 2015. The single, which was co-written with [[Paul Epworth]], was officially released in the UK on 1 June 2015. Its music video premiered on 12 May 2015 on La Havas' official YouTube channel. ''[[Blood (Lianne La Havas album)|Blood]]'' was released on 31 July 2015 for physical and digital formats. La Havas toured in the UK and Europe in support of album from mid-May to September 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a643314/lianne-la-havas-announces-new-album-blood-listen-to-single-unstoppable.html|title=Lianne La Havas: announces new album Blood|first=Harry|last=Fletcher|website=Digital Spy|date=21 April 2015|access-date=25 April 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nonesuch.com/journal/watch-lianne-la-havas-unveils-unstoppable-video-2015-05-12|title=Watch: Lianne La Havas Unveils 'Unstoppable' Video|website=Nonesuch|date=12 May 2015|access-date=13 May 2015}}</ref> La Havas features twice on [[Rudimental]]'s album, ''[[We the Generation]]'' (2015), on the songs "Needn't Speak" and "Breath". She appeared on the popular "The Late Show with Stephen Colbert" on CBS in the United States on 27 October 2015, singing with Colbert's regular band, New Orleans jazz bandleader [[Jon Batiste]] and Stay Human.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The Late Show with Stephen Colbert|url=http://www.cbs.com/shows/the-late-show-with-stephen-colbert/|publisher=CBS|access-date=2015-10-28}}</ref> She also performed a Tiny Desk Concert on NPR in October 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2015/10/02/445047157/lianne-la-havas-tiny-desk-concert|title=Lianne La Havas: Tiny Desk Concert|publisher=NPR|first=Suraya|last= Mohamed|date=2 October 2015|language=en|access-date=2019-05-14}}</ref>

La Havas plays a [[Semi-acoustic guitar|hollow-bodied]] 1964 [[Harmony Company|Harmony]] [[Stratotone|Alden Stratotone]] guitar, which is put through a [[Fender Musical Instruments Corporation|Fender]] Hot Rod Deluxe amp on stage with her band. For solo gigs she uses a SansAmp DI'd, and a [[Roland CUBE]] amp is used for practice.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.guitarworld.com/guitar-girld-your-love-big-enough-lianne-la-havas |title=Guitar Girl'd: 'Is Your Love Big Enough' for Lianne La Havas? |publisher=NewBay Media, LLC |date=8 September 2012 |access-date=20 January 2013}}</ref>

===2020: ''Lianne La Havas''===
La Havas released "Bittersweet" on 25 February 2020<ref>{{cite web|title=iTunes - Bittersweet - Single by Lianne La Havas|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/bittersweet-single/1498795793|publisher=[[iTunes Store]]}}</ref> as the lead single from her upcoming album. On 4 May 2020, La Havas released the second single "Paper Thin" and announced that her [[Lianne La Havas (album)|self-titled third studio album]] would be released on 17 July 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://twitter.com/liannelahavas/status/1257348634007519239?s=21|title=@liannelahavas on Twitter: I'm so glad I can finally say...MY NEW ALBUM IS COMING 17th JULY 2020 In ALL of the ways!!! I love you I love you I love you I love you ILOVEYOUUUU|via=Twitter}}</ref> Recording of the album took place between October 2019 and December 2019, with sessions taking place in London, Bath, LA and New York. The album's production was handled by La Havas along with long-term collaborator [[Aqualung (musician)|Matt Hales]], co-producers Beni Giles, Sam Crowe and guest co-producer Mura Masa. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/lianne-la-havas-mw0003386991/credits|title = Lianne la Havas - Lianne la Havas &#124; Credits &#124; AllMusic}}</ref> <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tapenotes.co.uk/project/tn61-lianne-la-havas-sam-crowe-beni-giles|title = TN:61 Lianne la Havas, Sam Crowe & Beni Giles}}</ref>

Following its release, the album received a number of positive reviews from major publishers.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|last=Pareles|first=Jon|date=2020-07-21|title=Lianne La Havas Traces the Arc of a Romance|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/07/21/arts/music/lianne-la-havas-review.html|access-date=2020-09-30|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Cragg|first=Michael|date=2020-07-19|title=Lianne La Havas: Lianne La Havas review – purposeful, tender and bold|url=http://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/jul/19/lianne-la-havas-self-titled-album-review-2020-weird-fishes|access-date=2020-09-30|website=The Guardian|language=en}}</ref> The ''New York Times''' chief pop music critic, [[Jon Pareles]], in his review, has mused: <blockquote>The songs illuminate passion, impulsiveness, ambivalence and uncertainty, yet the structures La Havas created are lucid and poised. While matters of the heart may be out of control, her fingers and voice are impeccable.<ref name=":1" /></blockquote>
