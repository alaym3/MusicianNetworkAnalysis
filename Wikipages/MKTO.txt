'''MKTO''' was an American [[Pop music|pop]] duo, consisting of [[Malcolm David Kelley|Malcolm Kelley]] and [[Tony Oller]]. Their [[MKTO (album)|self-titled album]] was released on 30 January 2014 by [[Columbia Records]]. In July 2015, the duo released their first [[extended play]], titled ''Bad Girls EP''. As of 2014, the band has sold over 1 million records worldwide.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sony.com/content/sony/en/en_us/SCA/company-news/press-releases/sony-music-entertainment/2014/mkto-to-release-debut-album-mkto-april-1-2014.html|title=MKTO to Release Debut Album|access-date=April 16, 2021}}</ref> 

==Career==

===2010–2012: Formation===
[[Malcolm David Kelley|Malcolm Kelley]] and [[Tony Oller]] met in 2010 while filming the [[Nickelodeon]] television series ''[[Gigantic (TV series)|Gigantic]]'', in which their characters played best friends.<ref name="The Edge">{{cite web|url=http://www.theedge.co.nz/MKTO/tabid/138/articleID/25227/Default.aspx|title=Uncover Discover|work=[[The Edge (radio station)|The Edge]]|publisher=MediaWorks Radio|date=January 29, 2013|access-date=March 16, 2013}}</ref> They later formed as a duo and came up with the name MKTO, which represents the combined initials of their names and surnames, MK for Malcolm Kelley and TO for Tony Oller. They elaborated upon the inspiration for their name, saying: "It also stands for what our album kind of is: Misfit Kids and Total Outcasts – just like the kids we were in high school".<ref name="PR Newswire">{{cite web|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/mkto-signs-with-columbia-records-186944001.html|title=MKTO Signs With Columbia Records|work=[[PR Newswire]]|publisher=[[UBM plc]]|date=January 15, 2013|access-date=March 16, 2013}}</ref>

===2012–2014: ''MKTO''===
The duo signed to Columbia Records and released their debut single "[[Thank You (MKTO song)|Thank You]]" on November 12, 2012. The song's genre is a crossover between [[Pop music|pop]] and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]]. The song has attained commercial success, reaching the top ten on both the Australian and New Zealand singles charts.<ref name="AUS"/><ref name="NZ"/> The song's music video was released on [[YouTube]] via [[Vevo]] on January 4, 2013, and garnered over 500,000 views within two days of being released. It now sits at over 25 million views.<ref name="PR Newswire" /> In the music video, [[Harold Perrineau]], who played the father of Kelley's character on ''[[Lost (TV series)|Lost]]'', appeared as a reference to the show. MKTO released their second single, "[[Classic (MKTO song)|Classic]]" along with the music video on June 20, 2013. Classic peaked at number 14 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|US ''Billboard'' Hot 100]], and the music video has since compiled over 180 million views on YouTube. The music video for their third single, "[[God Only Knows (MKTO song)|God Only Knows]]", was released on November 29, 2013 on YouTube and Vevo. God Only Knows peaked at number 11 in Australia and at number 19 in New Zealand. In 2013, they served as the opening for [[Emblem3]] on their summer tour across the United States. They also served as the opening for [[Emblem3]]'s #bandlife Tour and [[Demi Lovato]]'s "[[Demi World Tour|Demi: World Tour]]" in 2014. Their self-titled debut album was released on January 30, 2014<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/nz/album/mkto/id743071281|title=MKTO by MKTO on Apple Music|website=iTunes|language=en|access-date=2017-03-26}}</ref> in Australia and New Zealand and it was released on April 1 in North America.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hamadamania.com/2013/12/02/mkto-confirm-tracklisting-for-debut-album/ |title=MKTO confirm tracklisting for debut album &#124; Hamada Mania Music Blog |publisher=Hamadamania.com |date=2013-12-04 |access-date=2015-06-12}}</ref>

In February 2014, their debut album went to #1 in Australia and #6 in New Zealand. During the summer of 2014, MKTO went on their first headlining tour, the [http://www.wearemkto.com/news/ American Dream Tour]. This featured opening acts [[Tiffany Houghton]] and [[Action Item]]. The fourth single from their debut album in the United States is "[[American Dream (MKTO song)|American Dream]]" and peaked at number 40 in Australia and at number 12 in New Zealand. Despite not being singles, "Forever Until Tomorrow" reached number 23 on iTunes in Australia and "Heartbreak Holiday" reached number 30 on iTunes in Australia and number 42 in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/artists/music/mkto/|title=iTunes Charts - MKTO - International Chart Performance|access-date=2021-01-18}}</ref>

===2015–2016: ''Bad Girls EP''===
On July 22, 2015, MKTO released their first extended play ''Bad Girls EP'', which was preceded by the June 2015 release of its lead single "[[Bad Girls (MKTO song)|Bad Girls]]".<ref>{{cite web|last=Bowman |first=Patrick |url=http://www.idolator.com/7592470/mkto-new-single-bad-girls-preview |title=MKTO Preview New Single "Bad Girls": Listen To Funk-Pop Anthem |publisher=Idolator.com |access-date=2015-06-12}}</ref> The single peaked at number 80 in Australia and peaked at number 36 on the ''Billboard'' Mainstream Top 40. The EP peaked at number 32 on the iTunes chart in Australia and number 50 on the iTunes chart in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.itunescharts.net/artists/music/mkto/albums/bad-girls-ep/|title=MKTO - 'Bad Girls - EP' International iTunes Chart Performance|access-date=2021-01-18}}</ref> A [[A-side and B-side|double A-side]] single including two brand new tracks "Hands Off My Heart/Places You Go" was premiered exclusively on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' on March 9, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|last=Polanco|first=Luis|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/6971402/mkto-hands-off-my-heart-places-you-go-exclusive-premiere|title=MKTO Returns With New Song 'Hands Off My Heart'/'Places You Go': Exclusive Premiere|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 9, 2016|access-date=March 11, 2016}}</ref> "Superstitious" is the second single that was released in 2016 by the duo.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/mkto-superstitious-premiere|title=Get Into the Friday the 13th Spirit with MKTO’s Brand New Song|access-date=2021-01-18}}</ref>

===2017–present: Canceled second studio album, disbandment, return and Oller's departure===
On March 10, 2017, Oller announced on Twitter that the band had broken up.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.teen.com/2017/03/10/news/mkto-breakup-split/|title=Another One Bites the Dust — Popular Boy Band Suddenly Breaks Up|last=Teen.com|date=2017-03-10|website=Teen.com|access-date=2017-03-26}}</ref> However, according to their Twitter page,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/WeAreMKTO|title=MKTO (@WeAreMKTO) {{!}} Twitter|website=twitter.com|language=en|access-date=2018-04-04}}</ref> they plan to release new music very soon.
On June 12, 2018, MKTO announced on Twitter that they were back together.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/WeAreMKTO/status/1006613657696927744|title=Let it begin.....We're back|access-date=2018-06-21}}</ref> Three days later, on June 15, the band announced they had signed a new record deal with [[BMG Rights Management|BMG]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/WeAreMKTO/status/1007739926404739072|title=Us with our new label @BMG we're a excited for this new journey and thank all of you for the support|access-date=2018-06-21}}</ref> Following the announcement, the duo released a new single titled, "How Can I Forget" in September 2018. In June 2019, they released another single, "Shoulda Known Better". A third single, "Marry Those Eyes" was released on  September 6, 2019. "Consider Me Yours" was released on  November 22, 2019. In April 2020, MKTO released a music video for Just Imagine It. They also released 3 singles, "Simple Things", "Party With My Friends" and "How Much". "Party With My Friends" peaked at number 85 on iTunes<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.top-charts.com/s/party-with-my-friends-mkto|title=Top Charts - Party With My Friends MKTO|access-date=2021-01-18}}</ref> while "How Much" peaked at number 54 on iTunes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.top-charts.com/s/how-much-mkto|title=Top Charts - How Much MKTO|access-date=2021-01-18}}</ref> On August 17, 2021, Tony Oller announced via social media that he would be moving on from the band.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/FollowOller/status/1427776855919284227|title=Tony Oller - Twitter|access-date=August 18, 2021}}</ref>
