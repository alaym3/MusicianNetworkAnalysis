'''Ashlyn Rae Willson''' (born April 24, 1993),<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/ashlyn_rae_willson_born_1993_22648491|title=Ashlyn Rae Willson, Born 04/24/1993 in California|website=[[California Birth Index]]|access-date=February 27, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#ace/search/performer/ashe/title/moral%20of%20the%20story|title=ASCAP Ace Search|last=Willson|first=Ashlyn Rae|website=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] (ASCAP)|access-date=February 27, 2020}}</ref> better known as '''Ashe''', is an American singer and songwriter. She is best known for her 2019 single "[[Moral of the Story (song)|Moral of the Story]]", which was featured in the [[Netflix]] film ''[[To All the Boys: P.S. I Still Love You]]'' (2020) and was produced by Noah Conrad with additional production from [[Finneas O'Connell]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/a/who-is-25-year-old-singer-ashe-moral-of-the-story-is-a-netflix-viral-hit|title=In Search Of: Ashe's "Moral Of The Story" Is Blowing Up Because Of Netflix|last=Fu|first=Eddie|date=February 27, 2020|website=[[Genius (website)|Genius]]|language=en|access-date=February 27, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.popsugar.com/node/47215661|title=P.S. I Still Love You: I Can't Get the Dramatic "Moral of the Story" Song Out of My Head|last=Meredith|first=Karenna|date=February 12, 2020|website=[[Popsugar]]|language=en-US|access-date=February 27, 2020}}</ref> Ashe also co-wrote [[Demi Lovato]]'s 2017 single "[[You Don't Do It for Me Anymore]]" and has toured with [[Louis the Child (DJs)|Louis the Child]], [[Lauv]] and [[Whethan]]. She was nominated for a [[Juno Award]] for her gold-certified single "Let You Get Away" with [[Shaun Frank]]. Her debut studio album ''[[Ashlyn (album)|Ashlyn]]'' was released on May 7, 2021.

==Early life==
Ashlyn Rae Willson was born on April 24, 1993 in [[San Jose, California]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZtaoYYYNkyw|title=ASHE Interview- religion, marriage, ex-boyfriends, tinder, pop music standards|last=Engel|first=Lauren|date=November 28, 2017|website=Sidewalk Talk|publisher=YouTube|access-date=March 1, 2020}}</ref> She began attending piano and vocal lessons at the age of 8.<ref name=":1" /> Ashe claims that she grew up listening only to Christian radio and that she thanks her grandfather for introducing her to music from names like [[Bob Dylan]], [[The Beatles]] and [[Jefferson Airplane]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://b-sides.tv/video/20180204-video-interview-ashe-talks-beginnings-new-music|title=B-Sides On-Air: Interview â€“ Ashe Talks Beginnings, New Music|date=April 2, 2018|website=B-Sides|language=en-US|access-date=March 1, 2020}}</ref> She attended [[Berklee College of Music]] and graduated with a major in contemporary writing and production in 2015.<ref name="Berklee">{{cite web|url=https://berklee.edu/berklee-today/spring-2016/ashlyn-willson|title=Nashville Pros: Ashlyn Willson|last=Petrelli|first=David|date=|website=|publisher=Berklee College of Music|access-date=May 16, 2018}}</ref><ref name="AllMusic">{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ashe-mn0003496874/biography|title=Ashe: Biography & History|last=Collar|first=Matt|website=[[AllMusic]]|language=en-us|access-date=February 27, 2020}}</ref>

=== 2018â€“present: Solo debut ===
By the time Ashe had released her debut EP ''The Rabbit Hole'' in June 2018, she had clocked over 200 million accumulative [[Streaming media|streams]] and nine No. 1 songs on [[Hype Machine]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ventsmagazine.com/2018/06/27/ashe-releases-debut-ep-the-rabbit-hole/|title=Ashe Releases Debut EP The Rabbit Hole|last=Frometa|first=RJ|date=June 27, 2018|website=Vents Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=March 1, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Milk">{{cite web|url=https://milk.xyz/articles/premiere-ashe-gets-emotional-on-debut-ep-the-rabbit-hole/|title=PREMIERE: Ashe Gets Emotional on Debut EP, 'The Rabbit Hole'|last=Chou|first=Thomas|date=June 22, 2018|website=Milk.Xyz|access-date=June 23, 2018}}</ref> The EP was described as "a versatile and compelling seven-track affair."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://pilerats.com/music/electronic/ashe-the-rabbit-hole/|title=Meet Ashe and her summery debut EP The Rabbit Hole|last=Davies|first=Hayden|website=PILERATS|access-date=March 1, 2020}}</ref> Ashe supported [[Quinn XCII]] on his ''From Michigan with Love World Tour,'' during which she also released her single "[[Moral of the Story (song)|Moral of the Story]]"''.''<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/ashe-rabbit-hole-fresh-finds|title=You're Going to Want to Get to Know Ashe|last=Lambert|first=Eleanor|date=February 12, 2019|website=[[Teen Vogue]]|language=en|access-date=March 1, 2020}}</ref>

On April 5, 2019, Ashe released the four-track EP, ''Moral of the Story: Chapter 1'', which was entirely produced by [[Finneas O'Connell]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/ashe-moves-on-from-a-toxic-relationship-in-moral|title=Ashe Moves On From a Toxic Relationship in 'Moral Of The Story: Chapter 1' EP|last=Yung|first=Alyssa|date=April 8, 2019|website=Ones To Watch|access-date=April 8, 2019}}</ref> O'Connell also produced three of the four tracks on her sequel EP, ''Moral of the Story: Chapter 2.''<ref name=":2" />

In February 2020, the single "[[Moral of the Story (song)|Moral of the Story]]" was used in the [[Netflix]] teen [[Romantic comedy|romcom]] ''[[To All the Boys: P.S. I Still Love You]]'' (2020) which caused the song to reach number 2 on both Global and US [[Spotify Viral 50]] chart.<ref name="Viral50"/> Ashe made her debut chart appearance on both the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] with "Moral of the Story" and the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] with ''Moral of the Story: Chapter 1''. Ashe has also recently released a duet of "Moral of the Story" featuring [[Niall Horan]].<ref>{{cite tweet|user=billboardcharts|number=1234606160403222529|title=@ashemusic earns her first career entry on the #Hot100 this week, as "Moral of the Story" debuts at No. 71. |date=March 2, 2020|accessdate=February 3, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite tweet|number=1234595738354667525|user=billboardcharts|title=Debuts on this week's #Billboard200 (2/2):|author=Billboard Charts|date=March 2, 2020}}</ref> As of June 2020, the single has been used in over 901,400 videos on the online platform [[TikTok]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tiktok.com/music/Moral-of-the-Story-6793091579272759298?lang=en|title=Moral of the Story created by Ashe: Popular songs on TikTok|via=[[TikTok]]|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200301173107/https://www.tiktok.com/music/Moral-of-the-Story-6793091579272759298?lang=en|archive-date=March 1, 2020|access-date=March 1, 2020}}</ref> As of May 2021, her collaboration with [[Niall Horan]] has reached over 80 million streams on [[Spotify]].<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://www.hard8workinggroup.com/news/ashe-amp-niall-celebrate-20-million-streams-on-moral-of-the-story|title= Ashe & Niall Celebrate 20 Million Streams on "Moral Of The Story"|date=August 13, 2020|website= Hard8 Working Group|access-date=August 15, 2020}}</ref>

In March 2021, Ashe released another song with O'Connell titled "[[Till Forever Falls Apart]]". Her debut album ''[[Ashlyn (album)|Ashlyn]]'' was released on May 7, 2021, including the songs "I'm Fine" and "When I'm Older" issued prior to the album, along with "Me Without You", released on May 5.

''Ashlyn'' debuted at number 194 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] dated May 22, 2021.<ref name="Billboard 200"/>

== Artistry ==
She claims that jazz music, [[Diane Keaton]] and [[Carole King]] are major influences and inspirations to her own music.<ref name="UWM Post">{{cite web|url=http://uwmpost.com/fringe/exclusive-interview-ashe|title=A Chat With Singer Songwriter Ashe|last=Montana|first=Adam|date=November 6, 2017|website=[[UWM Post]]|access-date=May 16, 2018}}</ref> Ashe put an "e" on her stagename to pay homage to Carole King.<ref name="UWM Post" /> Artists she admires are [[Stevie Nicks]], King, [[John Mayer]], [[Justin Vernon]] and [[Bon Iver]].{{Citation needed|date=March 2020}}
