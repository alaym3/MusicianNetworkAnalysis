'''Linda Karlsson''' (born 21 July 1982), better known by her stage name '''Miss Li''', is a Swedish singer and [[songwriter]]. Since beginning her career in the early-2000s, she has released a total of eight studio albums, and several of her songs have been featured in Swedish commercials as well as numerous American television series.

==Life and career==
Linda Karlsson was born on 21 July 1982{{efn|Karlsson notes in a 2018 Facebook post that 21 July is her date of birth.<ref>{{cite web|work=[[Facebook]]|author=Karlsson, Linda|date=21 July 2018|title=I’m not skinny. I’m not tall. I don’t look like a model...|archive-url=https://archive.today/20190502020710/https://www.facebook.com/missliofficial/photos/a.766205500065095/1978416802177286/?type=1&theater|archive-date=2 May 2019|url=https://www.facebook.com/missliofficial/photos/a.766205500065095/1978416802177286/|access-date=2 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> [[AllMusic]] lists her birth year as 1982.<ref>{{cite web|work=[[AllMusic]]|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/miss-li-mn0001757662/biography|archive-url=https://archive.today/20190502020719/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/miss-li-mn0001757662/biography|archive-date=2 May 2019|title=Miss Li Biography|access-date=2 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>}} in [[Borlänge]], Sweden. In her adulthood, she relocated to [[Stockholm]].

Using the stage name Miss Li, she released her debut album, ''[[Late Night Heartbroken Blues]]'', in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|work=[[iTunes]]|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/late-night-heartbroken-blues/1442039539|title=Late Night Heartbroken Blues|date=22 November 2006|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref> Her single "Don't Try to Fool Me" has been featured on the [[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime]] original series ''[[Weeds (TV series)|Weeds]]'', as well as ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]'',<ref>{{cite episode|series=[[Grey's Anatomy]]|title=Kung Fu Fighting|airdate=1 November 2007|publisher=[[American Broadcasting Company]]}}</ref> both in 2007.
[[File:Miss Li and keyboard at Stockholm Pride 2009.jpg|thumb|right|Li performing at the 2009 [[Stockholm Pride]] Festival]]

Her song "Bourgeois Shangri-La" from her fourth studio album, ''[[Dancing the Whole Way Home]]'' (2009), was used by [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] in the [[iPod Nano]] 5G television commercial, and her song "Oh Boy"  was used in a 2010 [[Volvo C70]] commercial. Her track "True Love Stalker" was used in the television promo of an episode of ''[[Desperate Housewives]]''. The track "Forever Drunk" was featured in the opening scene of ''[[Grey's Anatomy]]''. Her song "My Heart Goes Boom" was featured on multiple adverts for the popular UK furniture store DFS in late 2012, shortly after her appearance on ''[[Så mycket bättre]]'' that same year. In 2013 "My Heart Goes Boom" was used in American television advertisements of women's clothes by White House Black Market.<ref>{{cite web|work=iSpot|url=http://www.ispot.tv/ad/7Inm/white-house-black-market-featuring-coco-rocha-song-by-miss-li|title=White House Black Market TV Commercial Featuring Coco Rocha, Song by Miss Li|date=2013|access-date=2 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612170434/https://www.ispot.tv/ad/7Inm/white-house-black-market-featuring-coco-rocha-song-by-miss-li|archive-date=12 June 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> After an extensive tour promoting her seventh album, ''[[Wolves (Miss Li album)|Wolves]]'' concluded in 2014, Li stated she sank into a [[depression (mood)|depression]], and went into seclusion.<ref name=dt>{{cite web|work=[[Dalarnas Tidningar]]|title=Linda "Miss Li" Carlsson om åren av tystnad|date=29 August 2016|last=Eriksson|first=Elin Bergvik|archive-url=https://archive.today/20190502030102/https://www.dt.se/artikel/linda-miss-li-carlsson-om-aren-av-tystnad|archive-date=2 May 2019|language=Swedish|url=https://www.dt.se/artikel/linda-miss-li-carlsson-om-aren-av-tystnad|url-status=live|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref> She subsequently attended a songwriters' camp in [[Los Angeles]], California, where she collaborated in the studio with [[Rihanna]], writing a track for the artist's album ''[[Anti (album)|Anti]]'' (2016).<ref name=dt/> However, The unnamed track did not appear on the album, though Li stated that Rihanna had purchased the rights to it.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2842&artikel=6517335|work=[[Sveriges Radio]]|title=Miss Li skrev musik till Rihanna|date=15 September 2016|archive-url=https://archive.today/20190502030707/https://sverigesradio.se/sida/artikel.aspx?programid=2842&artikel=6517335|archive-date=2 May 2019|language=Swedish|access-date=2 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>

Li released her eighth studio album, ''A Woman's Guide to Survival'', in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/a-womans-guide-to-survival-mw0003141351|work=[[AllMusic]]|title=A Woman's Guide to Survival|archive-url=https://archive.today/20190502022035/https://www.allmusic.com/album/a-womans-guide-to-survival-mw0003141351|archive-date=2 May 2019|access-date=2 May 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> The song "Aqualung" from the album was featured in the debut video for the 2019 [[Volvo XC40]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/watch/?v=1535023429854339|work=[[Sony Music|Sony Music Sweden]]|title=Miss Li - Aqualung / Volvo|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref> Her song, "I Can't Get You Off My Mind", is used for movie promos on the cable and satellite station [[Starz]].
