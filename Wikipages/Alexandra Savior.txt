'''Alexandra Savior McDermott'''<ref name=Graves/> (born June 14, 1995) is an American singer-songwriter originally from [[Portland, Oregon]]. Her debut album, ''[[Belladonna of Sadness (album)|Belladonna of Sadness]]'', was released by [[Columbia Records]] on April 7, 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/alexandra-savior/11774|title=Alexandra Savior announces debut album 'Belladonna of Sadness' + drops new song 'Mystery Girl'.|last=Graves|first=Shahlin|date=November 22, 2016|newspaper=Coup De Main Magazine|access-date=22 November 2016}}</ref> Savior's second album, ''[[The Archer (album)|The Archer]]'', was released by [[Danger Mouse (musician)|30th Century Records]] on January 10, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/8547611/alexandra-savior-the-archer-album-premiere|title=Alexandra Savior Finds Her Voice on 'The Archer': Album Premiere|website=Billboard|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref>

==Early life==
Savior was born in 1995 and attended school in the [[Portland metropolitan area]].<ref name=ozy/> She first became interested in songwriting at age 14.<ref name=Graves/> Different in attitude and appearance from many of the other female students, she had a difficult go of it socially and had to change high schools.<ref name=ozy/>

Savior, known then by her birth name, first achieved public attention in September 2012 when as a 17-year-old her rendition of "[[Big Jet Plane]]" was spotted on YouTube by [[Courtney Love]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://eventseeker.com/artist/58233-alexandra-savior|title=Alexandra Savior - Artist Profile|last=|first=|date=|website=eventseeker.com|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-03-24}}</ref> who said "This girl is gonna be huge!"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://gothamist.com/2012/09/13/video_courtney_love_declares_alexan.php|title=Video: Courtney Love Declares Alexandra McDermott The Next Big Thing|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170310185809/http://gothamist.com/2012/09/13/video_courtney_love_declares_alexan.php|archive-date=March 10, 2017}}</ref> By April 2013, she had done a little modeling for [[Erin Fetherston]] and said she hoped her first album would be out soon.<ref name=galore>{{cite web|url=https://galoremag.com/the-next-big-thing-alexandra-mcdermott/|title=The Next Big Thing: Alexandra McDermott – Galore|date=April 29, 2013}}</ref> She graduated from high school in June 2013.<ref name=ozy/> By October 2013, [[Linda Perry]] was publicly comparing her potential to that of [[Fiona Apple]].<ref name=ozy/> She scrapped tentative plans to attend [[art school]].<ref name=Graves/>

==Musical career==
Around September 2013, Savior was signed to recording contract by [[Columbia Records]] and had moved to Los Angeles and had entered the entertainment business, all steps that by her admission that she was not prepared for artistically or socially.<ref name=Graves/> But she has stated that Columbia was the best choice given that other labels were interested in modeling her after established artists such as [[Katy Perry]] or [[P!nk]].<ref name=gig/>

Previously titled ''Strange Portrait'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nightout.com/events/a/alexandra-savior|title=Alexandra Savior|website=nightout.com|access-date=22 November 2016}}</ref> her album-in-progress was written and recorded in Los Angeles with producers [[James Ford (musician)|James Ford]] and [[Alex Turner (musician)|Alex Turner]] in 2014–15.<ref name="Graves">{{Cite news|url=http://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/alexandra-savior/11541|title=Interview: Alexandra Savior on her upcoming debut album.|last=Graves|first=Shahlin|date=October 10, 2016|newspaper=Coup De Main Magazine|access-date=22 November 2016}}</ref><ref name=noisey >{{Cite web|url=http://noisey.vice.com/en_us/article/alexandra-savior-mystery-girl-interview|title=Alexandra Savior, Mystery Girl, Steps out of the Shade|website=Noisey|access-date=22 November 2016}}</ref> Her first demo "Risk" was featured on the soundtrack of [[True Detective (season 2)|the second season]] of the television show ''[[True Detective]]''.<ref name=true>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/alexandra-saviors-williamsburg-show-babys-all-right|title=Alexandra Savior's May 2016 Williamsburg Show Reviewed|date=May 27, 2016|newspaper=NME|access-date=22 November 2016}}</ref> For this appearance she was billed as Alexandra Semitone.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.shazam.com/track/268853741/risk|title=Shazam|website=Shazam.com|accessdate=January 18, 2021}}</ref> Subsequently, during the recording process she decided, based on a suggestion from Turner, to use her middle name as the last for her stage name.<ref name=Graves/>

2016}}</ref> The third single "[[Mystery Girl (song)|Mystery Girl]]" was released on November 21, 2016 alongside the album announcement.<ref name=gig>{{Cite visually have been what [[Gigwise]] described as "her lo-fi self-directed videos."<ref name=gig/>

Even though the album was essentially completed by June 2015, it did not come out; she remained "in development".<ref name=Graves/> She has given a few limited concert appearances, usually before industry audiences in the media capitals of Los Angeles, New York, and London.<ref name =gig/><ref name =noisey/><ref name=true/> These appearances have been using the group [[PAPA (band)|PAPA]] as her backing musicians.<ref name=noisey/><ref name=true/> Speaking of the delay in October 2016 she said "It's been very difficult."<ref name =Graves/> She also said: "The most important thing about the music industry I've learnt is that it's total bullshit, and that the thing you should focus on the most is just the art."<ref name=Graves/>

Savior appeared on Lea Michele's 2017 album ''[[Places (Lea Michele album)|Places]]'' as co-writer of the track "Sentimental Memories". In 2016, Savior appeared as a guest vocalist on the song "Providence Sky" by Brooklyn music group [[Dark Tea (musical group)|Dark Tea]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.coupdemainmagazine.com/alexandra-savior/11827|title=Listen: Alexandra Savior features on Dark Tea’s 'Providence Sky'.|last=Graves|first=Shahlin|date=December 1, 2016|work=Coup De Main Magazine|access-date=4 November 2018}}</ref>

In June 2019, two years after releasing ''[[Belladonna of Sadness (album)|Belladonna of Sadness]]'', Savior returned with the lead single from her second studio album, ''The Archer'', titled "Crying All The Time". It was released on June 14, 2019. The song was released on music streaming platforms through her newly signed recording label, 30th Century Records.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/brianleak/2019/06/13/premiere-alexandra-savior-shares-new-single-crying-all-the-time/|title=Premiere: Alexandra Savior Shares New Single, 'Crying All The Time'|date=June 13, 2019|work=Forbes|access-date=14 June 2019}}</ref> After releasing three more single, her sophomore studio album, ''[[The Archer (album)|The Archer]],'' was released by 30th Century Records on January 10, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=The Archer|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/the-archer/1481384499|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Music.apple.com}}</ref>

==Musical style==
Alexandra Savior has described her style as a "feminist angst horror film feel."<ref name = gig/> She has received positive notices for her early works. British music paper ''[[NME]]'' has said of one of her efforts,  "... the striking and smoky vocals belong solely to Savior and ooze a burgeoning star power well beyond her tender age of 21."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/blogs/nme-radar/listen-to-alexandra-savior-mtme-3404|title=Listen To Alexandra Savior's New Single 'M.T.M.E'|date=September 9, 2016}}</ref>

===Influences===
Savior's musical influences included [[Otis Redding]], [[Etta James]], [[Jack White]], [[Amy Winehouse]], and [[Adele]],<ref name=galore/> as well as the films of [[Terry Gilliam]].<ref name=gig/>

==Personal life==
Savior revealed on social media that she suffers from [[bipolar disorder]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CO6g8NeJUdE/|title=Having bipolar is a constant cycle of no longer recognizing yourself or the voice inside of your head. Your inner dialogue turns against you as a human being, as a body...|last=Savior|first=Alexandra|date=May 16, 2021|work=Instagram|access-date=May 16, 2021}}</ref>
