'''Samuel Fischer''' (born July 5, 1991) is an Australian [[pop rock]]<ref name="AM Bio"/> singer-songwriter and musician. Fischer released music independently before signing with [[RCA Records]] in 2019. RCA re-released his debut EP ''Not a Hobby'' in January 2020.<ref name="NaH">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/au/album/not-a-hobby-single/1494510232|title=Not a Hobby (EP) |website=Apple Music|date=10 January 2020|accessdate=1 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://themusicnetwork.com/tmn-ones-to-watch-2020/|title=TMN Ones to Watch in 2020|website=The Music Network|date=20 January 2020|accessdate=1 March 2020}}</ref>

==Life and career==
===Early life===
Fischer was born outside of Sydney in 1991<ref name="AM Bio"/> and grew up on a farm in the town of [[Grose Vale]], just outside [[Sydney, Australia]]. His musical life began at age three when he started playing [[violin]] after being inspired by seeing a performance by Queenie.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{Cite web |url= http://ghettoblastermagazine.com/trusting-process-interview-sam-fischer/ |title= Trusting Yourself And The Process; An Interview with Sam Fischer |website= Ghettoblaster |last= Johnson |first=Tommy |date=17 January 2018 |accessdate=14 February 2020}}</ref> He moved with his family to Sydney when he was seven, and at age 10 took up the [[saxophone]], later playing in his high school [[jazz]] band. By age 12, he was writing songs. He attended [[Sydney Grammar School]] and later the [[Conservatorium High School]] in Sydney. In 2010 he was accepted to [[Berklee College of Music]] in [[Boston]], and moved to that city to study music.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.elle.com.au/news/elle-interview-sam-fischer-6778 |title= ELLE Interview: Sam Fischer |website= Elle |date=14 December 2016 |accessdate=14 February 2020}}</ref> He is classically trained on saxophone and violin. At Berklee he joined the vocal troupe [[Pitch Slapped]], which won an [[a cappella]] competition in 2014.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}}

===2012–present: ''Not a Hobby''===
Fischer's debut release was as a featured artist on Alex Preston's single "Heartbeats" in July 2012.<ref name="heartbeats">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/au/album/heartbeat-feat-sam-fischer-single/541654662|title=Heartbeats|website=Apple Music|date= July 2012|accessdate=1 March 2020}}</ref> In September 2014, after graduating from Berklee,<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://themusicsite.com/music-news/press-releases/la-based-musician-sam-fischer-shares-new-single |title= Australian-Born, LA-Based Musician Sam Fischer Shares New Single 'Getting Older' |website= The Music Site |date=2 December 2017 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref> Fischer moved to [[Los Angeles]], where he was active in the local a cappella scene and sang backing vocals for the band [[Holychild|HOLYCHILD]]<ref name=autogenerated1 /> while writing songs and aiming for success as an artist on his own.

In May 2016, Fischer released his first single "Lean".<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.pauseandplay.com/new-songs-beck-shawn-mendes-maxwell-etc/ |title= New Songs: Beck, Shawn Mendes, Maxwell, etc. |website= Pause&Play |last= Galipault |first=Gerry |date=3 June 2016 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.stereogum.com/1874579/sam-fischer-lean-stereogum-premiere/mp3s/ |title= Sam Fischer – "Lean" (Stereogum Premiere) |website= Stereogum |last= Robinson |first=Collin |date=3 May 2016 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref> The following year he released the singles "Same Friends", which premiered at ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' on 10 November 2017<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8031173/sam-fischer-same-friends-premiere |title= Sam Fischer Shares Soulful New Track & Video 'Same Friends': Exclusive |website= Billboard |last= Stubblebine |first=Allison |date=10 November 2017 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref> and "Getting Older".<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://themusicsite.com/music-news/press-releases/la-based-musician-sam-fischer-shares-new-single |title= Australian-Born, LA-Based Musician Sam Fischer Shares New Single 'Getting Older' |website= The Music Site |author=Lily |date=2 December 2017 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref> Fischer featured on Opia's single "Secrets".<ref>{{Cite web |url= http://wefoundnewmusic.com/opia-secrets-ft-sam-fischer/ |title= Opia: Secrets (ft. Sam Fischer) |website= We Found New Music |last= Owens |first=Grant |date= 2017 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref>

In January 2018, Fischer released his debut EP, ''Not a Hobby''.<ref name="AM Bio"/> In May 2018, Fischer released a second EP titled ''Sam Fischer (Our Vinyl Sessions)'', featuring acoustic recordings of "Getting Older", "Same Friends" and "Lean".<ref name="OVS"/> In January 2019, Fischer collaboration with London's Black Saint on "Everybody Wants You" which became a dance in mid-2019.<ref name="AM Bio"/> The same year, he toured with [[Lewis Capaldi]].<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.vmusic.com.au/uncovered-sam-fischer |title= UNCO[V]ERED: SAM FISCHER |website= VMusic |date=14 December 2016 |accessdate=14 February 2020}}</ref> Later in 2019, Fischer was signed by [[RCA Records]] who re-released "[[This City (Sam Fischer song)|This City]]" in December 2019<ref>{{cite web |url= http://sportplaylists.com/sam-fischer-this-city |title= Australian singer-songwriter Sam Fischer drops "This City" single |date=20 December 2019 |accessdate=14 February 2020}}</ref> and re-released ''Not a Hobby'' EP in January 2020.<ref name="NaH"/> ''Not a Hobby'' peaked at number 15 on the Australian Artist Singles Chart on 9 March 2020.<ref name="9Mar20"/>

Fischer has worked with a variety of producers and has written songs for artists including [[Ciara]] ("[[Dose (Ciara song)|Dose]]"),<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.songmeaningsandfacts.com/dose-by-ciara/ |title= "Dose" by Ciara |website= Song Meanings and Facts |date=28 October 2018 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref> [[Elle King]], [[Sabrina Claudio]], [[Keith Urban]], [[Max (American singer)|MAX]], Christian French, [[Andy Grammer]], [[Saygrace]], and [[Jessie J]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://hitsdailydouble.com/news&id=312568 |title= 2018SONY/ATV UPS A&R PAIR |website= Hits Daily Double |date=11 July 2018 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url= https://hitsdailydouble.com/news&id=319765 |title= SONY U.K.'S CLASS OF 2020 |website= Hits Daily Double |date=30 January 2020 |accessdate=15 February 2020}}</ref>

In February 2021, Fischer released "[[What Other People Say]]", a collaboration with American singer, [[Demi Lovato]]. The track went on to peak within the top 100 in multiple regions, including the [[United Kingdom]], at 70, and [[Australia]], at 58, as well as within the top 20 in [[Scotland]]. The song was subsequently included on Lovato's seventh studio album, [[Dancing with the Devil... the Art of Starting Over|''Dancing with the Devil... The Art of Starting Over.'']]
