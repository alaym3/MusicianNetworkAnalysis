'''Clara Luciani''' ({{IPA-fr|klaʁa lutʃ(j)ani}}, {{IPA-fr|lysjani|}}; {{IPA-co|luˈtʃaːni|lang}}; born 10 July 1992) is a French musician.<ref name="Les Inrockuptibles - 14 April 2018 - Clara Luciani, la nouvelle perle pop français">{{cite web |last1=Conte |first1=Christophe |title=Clara Luciani, la nouvelle perle pop française |url=https://www.lesinrocks.com/2018/04/17/musique/sainte-victoire-lamour-monstre-selon-clara-luciani-111070997/ |website=[[Les Inrockuptibles]] |accessdate=24 January 2019}}|date= 17 April 2047</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leparisien.fr/laparisienne/loisirs-detente/culture/clara-luciani-petite-lumiere-de-la-chanson-francaise-03-04-2017-6820719.php |title=Clara Luciani: Clara Luciani, "petite lumière" de la chanson française |publisher=[[Le Parisien]]|date= 4 April 2017 |accessdate=25 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/m-gens-portrait/article/2018/04/07/clara-luciani-voix-joyeusement-grave_5282085_4497229.html |title=Clara Luciani, voix joyeusement grave |work=Le Monde|date=7 April 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lexpress.fr/culture/musique/clara-luciani-illumine-t-elle-la-pop-francaise_2000550.html |title=Clara Luciani illumine-t-elle la pop? |publisher=L'Express.fr |date=18 April 2018}}</ref>

==Biography==
Luciani comes from a [[Corsicans|Corsican]] family. Her grandfather, whom she did not know, was from Ajaccio.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.lesinrocks.com/2018/07/10/musique/clara-luciani-je-nai-pas-limpression-de-vivre-un-succes-111103572/ |title="Je n'ai pas l'impression de vivre un succès" |publisher=Les Inrocks |date=19 August 2018 }}</ref> Luciani grew up in [[Septèmes-les-Vallons]], in the suburbs of [[Marseille]]. Before her career in music, she studied art history and performed various jobs, including pizza chef, babysitter, sales at [[Zara (retailer)|Zara]], and teaching [[Teaching English as a second or foreign language|ESL]].{{Citation needed|date=July 2021}}

In 2011, she met the band [[La Femme (band)|La Femme]], where she became, for a time, one of the vocalists. She sang two songs on the album ''[[Psycho Tropical Berlin]]'', released in 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.franceinter.fr/emissions/dans-la-playlist-de-france-inter/dans-la-playlist-de-france-inter-12-mars-2018 |title=Clara Luciani : la "petite lumière" de la pop française |publisher=France Inter |date=12 March 2018 }}</ref> After leaving the band, she formed the duo Hologram with Maxime Sokolinski.

In 2015–2016, Luciani accompanied singer [[Raphaël Haroche|Raphaël]] onstage for his Somnambules tour.

In 2017, she performed with [[Benjamin Biolay]] and released her first [[Extended play|EP]], ''Monstre d'amour'', recorded with Benjamin Lebeau ([[The Shoes (French band)|The Shoes]]) and Ambroise Willaume ([[Revolver (French band)|Revolver]]), to critical acclaim.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://madame.lefigaro.fr/celebrites/clara-luciani-la-revue-du-madame-interview-120517-132223 |title=Clara Luciani : "Enfant, j'étais la petite fille mise sur le côté" |date=30 May 2017 |publisher=Le Figaro }}</ref>

On 6 April 2018, Luciani released her first studio album, ''Sainte-Victoire'', which was well received by the press.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.francetvinfo.fr/culture/musique/musique-clara-luciani-une-sainte-victoire-foudroyante_2687948.html |title=Clara Luciani, une "Sainte Victoire" foudroyante |publisher=Franceinfo |date=9 April 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.franceinter.fr/emissions/pop-co/pop-co-10-avril-2018 |title='Sainte-Victoire', le premier album de Clara Luciani est une belle réussite |publisher=France Inter |date=10 April 2018 }}</ref> The following winter, she toured Australia, performing at the So Frenchy So Chic festival series in [[Adelaide]], [[Melbourne]], [[Sydney]], and [[Brisbane]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/so-frenchy-so-fierce-cheers-to-a-fiery-female-line-up-20190114-p50r78.html |title=So Frenchy, so fierce: cheers to a fiery female line-up |work=The Sydney Morning Herald|date=14 January 2019 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://scenestr.com.au/music/so-frenchy-so-chic-review-brisbane-powerhouse-20190122 |title=So Frenchy So Chic Review @ Brisbane Powerhouse |publisher=scenestr |date=22 January 2019  }}</ref> She released a second album, ''Cœur'', in 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Horner |first1=Olivier |title="Coeur", le pouvoir d'attraction de Clara Luciani en reine disco-pop |url=https://www.rts.ch/info/culture/musiques/12284905-coeur-le-pouvoir-dattraction-de-clara-luciani-en-reine-discopop.html |website=rts.ch |access-date=21 July 2021 |language=fr |date=25 June 2021}}</ref>

Luciani claims [[Françoise Hardy]], [[Serge Gainsbourg]], [[Nico]], [[Michel Legrand]], and [[Paul McCartney]] as influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tsugi.fr/interview-reprises-feminisme-et-guilty-pleasures-avec-clara-luciani |title=Interview : reprises, féminisme et guilty pleasures avec Clara Luciani |publisher= TSUGI |date=3 July 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Cormier |first1=Sylvain |title=Coeur, Clara Luciani |url=https://www.ledevoir.com/culture/musique/616674/chanson-pop-coeur |website=Le Devoir |access-date=21 July 2021 |language=fr}}</ref>
