'''George Ramirez''' (born July 31, 1994), better known by his stage name '''Kap G''', is an American rapper.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|last=Diep |first=Eric |url=http://xxlmag.com/entry/view/id/85573 |title=The Break Presents: Kap G – XXL |publisher=xxlmag.com |date=April 28, 2014 |access-date=July 16, 2015}}</ref> He is best known for his song "Girlfriend", which peaked at number 10 on the [[Bubbling Under Hot 100]] chart.

== Early life ==
George Ramirez was born on July 31, 1994, in [[Long Beach, California]], the youngest of seven children. His parents hail from [[San Marcos, Guerrero]] in Mexico and emigrated to Long Beach in the late 1980s. He lived there with his family until an older brother joined a gang in grammar school, and they headed east, landing in College Park, Georgia, a suburb in the [[Atlanta Metropolitan Area]] where he would be raised. Kap G graduated from [[Tri-Cities High School]].<ref name=":1">{{cite web|title=Gen F: Kap G|url=http://www.thefader.com/2014/05/15/gen-f-kap-g-is-the-new-face-of-mexican-american-rap|last=Weiss|first=Jeff|date=May 15, 2014|publisher=The Fader|access-date=July 16, 2015}}</ref>

== Career ==
Kap G has collaborated with big-name artists, such as [[Chief Keef]], [[T.I.]], [[Wiz Khalifa]], [[Young Jeezy|Jeezy]] and [[Kirko Bangz]] among others. He released his debut single, titled "Tatted Like Amigos"; a collaboration with Chicago rapper Chief Keef, which garnered him mainstream attention. Since then, Kap G began remixing other artists' songs, which drew the attention of Dungeon Family A&R Representative Kawan Prather, who ultimately discovered Kap G, which landed him a signing deal with [[Atlantic Records]].

On March 6, 2014, Kap G released his mixtape, titled ''Like a Mexican'' via [[DatPiff]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://datpiff.com/tape/588494 |title=Like A Mexican – Kap G on DatPiff |publisher=datpiff.com |access-date=July 16, 2015}}</ref> He is currently in the studio recording his first [[extended play]] (EP), with the assistance of [[Pharrell Williams]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://latino.foxnews.com/latino/entertainment/2014/03/27/pharrell-williams-dubs-him-future-kap-g-drops-knowledge-on-mexican-american/ |title=Pharrell Williams Dubs Him 'The Future': Kap G Drops Knowledge On Mexican-American Roots Through Rap &#124; Fox News Latino |publisher=latino.foxnews.com |date=March 27, 2014 |access-date=July 16, 2015}}</ref> Kap G has been featured in numerous articles for [[XXL (magazine)|''XXL'' magazine]], and was nominated for XXL Freshman in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://xxlmag.com/entry/view/id/120175 |title=XXL Freshmen 2015: Kap G Pitch (Video) |publisher=xxlmag.com |date=March 18, 2015 |access-date=July 16, 2015}}</ref> On March 4, 2016, Kap G released his song "Girlfriend", the second single from highly anticipated mixtape titled "El Southside" which features special guest appearance from [[Young Thug]], [[Cash Out]], [[YFN Lucci]] and more.<ref>[https://twitter.com/TheRealKapG/status/650514971470790656 Tweet 650514971470790656]. Twitter. October 3, 2015.</ref> "Girlfriend" became his biggest song to date, and was certified Gold by the RIAA in August 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kap G's Song 'Girlfriend' Goes Gold – XXL|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/08/kap-g-song-girlfriend-goes-gold/|last=Berry|first=Peter A.|website=XXL Mag|language=en|access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref> On April 14, 2017, he released his debut studio album ''SupaJefe'', on which he collaborates with [[Chris Brown]], [[Dae Dae]] and Pharrell Williams. He was on the SupaJefe tour from July 6 to August 26, 2017. On June 12, 2017, XXL revealed Ramirez was included in their annual freshman class.

== Musical style ==
Much of his lyrical content pays homage and represents his [[Culture of Mexico|Mexican culture]] and is bilingual, switching between English and Spanish.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kap G {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kap-g-mn0003203819/biography|last=Jeffries|first=David|website=AllMusic|language=en-us|access-date=May 24, 2020}}</ref> Kap G has said that his goal in his career is to subvert stereotypes of Mexicans<ref name=":1" /> and "to be a part of opening doors for Latinos".<ref name=":0" /> In an ''XXL'' interview he also stated that his style has been compared to [[Outkast]] and [[South Park Mexican]].<ref name=":0" />
