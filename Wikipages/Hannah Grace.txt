'''Hannah Vivian-Byrne''', known professionally as '''Hannah Grace'''  (born 4 June 1993) is a Welsh singer-songwriter from [[Bridgend]] in South Wales.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/GchwFK4cnD9SncJVcD1vtr/hannah-grace |title= Hannah Grace|publisher=BBC|accessdate=14 March 2017}}</ref>

Her debut studio album, ''Remedy'', was released on 20 November 2020.<ref name="remedy">{{cite news |url=https://www.mnprmagazine.com/hannah-grace-announces-debut-album-remedy/ |title=Hannah Grace announces debut album 'Remedy'|website=mnprmagazine.com |date=2020-06-26 |accessdate=2020-08-05}}</ref>

==History==
Grace studied Jazz Vocals at [[Royal Welsh College of Music & Drama]] for 4 years and describes her sound as soul and jazz inspired. She is influenced by artists like [[Aretha Franklin]], [[Stevie Nicks]] and [[Eva Cassidy]], [[Joni Mitchell]] & [[Ella Fitzgerald]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/GchwFK4cnD9SncJVcD1vtr/hannah-grace |title=Hannah Grace Profile Hall|publisher=BBC|accessdate=29 November 2016}}</ref> While studying in Cardiff Grace was chosen to be part of the [[BBC Wales]] Horizons scheme that helps to support and guide new Welsh artists. During this time Grace played at various festivals, and recorded a live session at [[Maida Vale Studios]] in London.

Grace was signed to [[Never Fade Records]]([[Gabrielle Aplin]]) after she joined [[Gabrielle Aplin]] on her 2013 [[English Rain]] album tour.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://neverfaderecords.com/ |title=Never Fade Records|website=neverfaderecords.com|accessdate=13 January 2017}}</ref>

Her debut [[Extended play|EP]] ''Meant to Be Kind'' was released in July 2014 on [[Never Fade Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/meant-to-be-kind-ep/id889033131 |title=Meant To be Kind EP|website=iTunes Store|accessdate=29 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.scenewave.com/2014/08/introduction-to-hannah-grace/ |title=Introduction to Hannah Grace|website=scenewave.com|accessdate=14 September 2016}}</ref> "Broke", taken from the EP, was chosen as an [[iTunes]] 'Single of the Week'. Grace co-wrote this EP with producer Luke Potashnick who she has continued to work with throughout her career.

Grace released her second EP ''Mustang'' in June 2016. (also a collaboration with Luke Potashnick). After the release of 'Mustang' Hannah Grace caught the attention of [[Dermot O'Leary]] who invited the singer to perform on his Radio 2 programme where she performed "Mustang" and a cover of "Rather Go Blind" by Etta James.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b07nwwqz |title=Saturday Sessions from Lisa Hannigan and Hannah Grace|publisher=BBC|accessdate=14 September 2016}}</ref> She has since done a live session on [[Michael Ball]]s Radio two show, and been invited back to Dermot's show with [[Gabrielle Aplin]] to promote their collaborative Christmas EP 'December'.

Grace has supported artists like [[Hozier (musician)|Hozier]], [[Jess Glynne]], [[Gabrielle Aplin]], [[Orla Gartland]], [[Seal (musician)|Seal]], [[Train]], [[Lissie]] and [[Hudson Taylor (group)|Hudson Taylor]] on shows and tours in the UK and Europe. Grace also gained a support slot for [[Barbra Streisand]]'s Hyde Park summertime show in 2019. .<ref>{{cite web|url= https://touring.caa.com/profile/HannahGrace |title=Profile Hannah Grace|website=caa.com |accessdate=29 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.bristolpost.co.uk/review-seal-at-colston-hall/story-29477795-detail/story.html |title=Review: Seal at Colston Hall|website=bristolpost.co.uk|accessdate=14 November 2016}}</ref>

Graces singles, Praise You, With You, Blue, & How True Is Your Love all made it to the [[BBC Radio Two]] Playlist. Hannah Grace has also performed on [[BBC Radio Wales]] programmes numerous times, and had many of her singles playisted at her home radio station.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b08t3qsk |title=Hannah Grace sings live for BBC Music Day |publisher=BBC |accessdate=13 February 2018}}</ref>

In March 2017 [[Lady Gaga]] tweeted a video of Grace calling her a "superstar". In April 2017 Grace released her cover of the [[Fatboy Slim]] song '[[Praise You]]' which was chosen as the soundtrack for [[Lloyds Bank]] nationwide TV and radio campaign. Grace's version of the track has been used on numerous TV programmes including ''[[The X Factor (UK TV series)|The X Factor]]'' and has sold in excess of 60,000 copies in the UK. It has more recently been used for the  [[Lancôme]] 2020 global Christmas campaign featuring [[Julia Roberts]] & [[Amanda Seyfried]]<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.lloydsbankinggroup.com/Media/Press-Releases/press-releases-2017/lloyds-bank/new-advert-shows-lloyds-bank-is-by-britains-side/|title=Lloyds Bank New Advert Shows Lloyds Bank Is By Britains Side|website=lloydsbankinggroup.com |accessdate=14 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.umusicpub.co.uk/en-GB/FilmAndTv/2017/4/Lloyds-Bank-Praise-You.aspx |title=Fatboy Slim 'Praise You' covered for Lloyds Bank|website=umusicpub.co.uk|accessdate=14 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= http://celebmix.com/listen-hannah-grace-reworks-fatboy-slim/ |title= Hannah Grace reworks Fatboy Slim|website=celebmix.com|accessdate=18 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://hiskind.com/premiere-hannah-grace-takes-it-to-church-with-new-praise-you-live-session// |title= Hannah Grace takes it to church with new Praise You Live Session|website=hiskind.com|accessdate=18 August 2017}}</ref>

Grace released her third EP 'The Bed You Made' in March 2019. This was co-written and produced with Martin Luke Brown, and also includes songs written with Laura White, Jessica Sharman and Gabrielle Aplin.

Grace released six new singles in 2020 and in June, announced her debut studio album, ''Remedy'', which was released on 20 November 2020. The album was produced by a number of different acclaimed producers. The majority by production duo MyRiot ([[London Grammar]], [[Rae Morris]], [[Aurora]]). Long time collaborator Luke Potashnick ([[Gabrielle Aplin]], [[Katie Melua]]), Ian Barter, ([[Paloma Faith]], [[Amy Winehouse]], [[Izzy Bizu]]) and Martin Luke Brown (Victoria Canal, Nick Wilson). The album currently has a combined 7 million streams.<ref name="remedy"/>

In 2020 Grace was chosen as one of the new [[Virgin Money]] emerging stars, a programme that supports and helps to develop up and coming artists.
