'''City High''' was an American [[Hip hop music|hip hop]]/[[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] trio consisting of [[rapper]]s/singers Ryan Toby, Robbie Pardlo and [[Claudette Ortiz]]. City High is best known for their song, "[[What Would You Do? (City High song)|What Would You Do?]]", which earned a [[Grammy Award|Grammy]] nomination.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/959605/city-highs-robby-pardlo-faces-intervention-on-ae Billboard.com]</ref>

==Career==
City High issued "[[What Would You Do? (City High song)|What Would You Do?]]" from their 2001 self-titled album. Their follow-up single was "[[Caramel (City High song)|Caramel]]".  A remix was released, featuring rapper [[Eve (entertainer)|Eve]].<ref>[http://www.mtv.com/music/artist/city_high/artist.jhtml MTV's City High artist page] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120204192448/http://www.mtv.com/music/artist/city_high/artist.jhtml |date=4 February 2012 }}</ref> The final single from the group's debut and only album was "City High Anthem".

Prior to creation of City High, bandmates Claudette Ortiz and Robbie Pardlo dated throughout their high school years.<ref name="Dimewars.com">[http://dimewars.com/Blog/ViewBlogArticle.aspx?vn=Claudette+Ortiz+From+City+High+Defends+Herself+From+Wily+Temptress+Accusations&BlogID=dca44def-a324-43d4-bf6c-716e85fb98d9 Dimewars.com] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110710132137/http://dimewars.com/Blog/ViewBlogArticle.aspx?vn=Claudette+Ortiz+From+City+High+Defends+Herself+From+Wily+Temptress+Accusations&BlogID=dca44def-a324-43d4-bf6c-716e85fb98d9 |date=10 July 2011 }}</ref> After meeting and performing for [[Wyclef Jean]], they were signed to his Booga Basement recording label. As Wooga Booga already had a duo, they invited Ryan Toby and became the City High [[trio (music)|trio]] instead.<ref name="Dimewars.com"/> Following her breakup with Pardlo, Ortiz went on to date Toby. Ortiz and Toby married in 2004, but later divorced in 2007.<ref>[http://www.blackvoices.com/black_entertainment/bvbuzzcanvas?id=20060217133509990001 ]{{dead link|date=May 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://randb.about.com/od/artistinterviews/a/RyanTobyArticle.htm |title=Ryan Toby Interview |publisher=Randb.about.com |accessdate=7 May 2012}}</ref>
