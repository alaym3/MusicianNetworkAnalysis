== Early life and background ==
Mame Khan was born in Satto, a small village near [[Jaisalmer]] in Rajasthan.<ref name=bbc>{{cite news |last1=Chakraborty |first1=Srabanti |title=राजस्थान की मिट्टी से कोक स्टूडियो तक! |url=https://www.bbc.com/hindi/entertainment/2015/02/150216_mame_khan_manginyar_interview_ssm |publisher=[[BBC News]] |date=19 February 2015 |language=Hindi}}</ref> He belongs to the [[Manganiyar]] community of [[Rajasthan]], India. He was exposed to folk music of the Manganiyar community since childhood.<ref name=thehindu/> His father, Ustad Rana Khan was also a [[Rajasthani people|Rajasthani]] folk singer.<ref name=thehindu/><ref name=indiatoday/> At the age of 14, he was given a six-year scholarship by the [[Indian Council for Cultural Relations]] to pursue his studies in music and arts.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Verma |first1=Aditya |title=Manganiar's Creed: The Voice of The Desert |url=http://www.rsjonline.com/features/manganiars-creed-the-voice-of-the-desert.html |work=[[Rock Street Journal]]}}</ref>

== Career ==
[[File:Lensemble Badila (Auditorium du musée Guimet) (8255060622).jpg|thumb|Mame Khan at Auditorium du musée Guimet in 2012]]
Khan made his singing debut in Bollywood with [[Shankar Mahadevan]] for the film ''[[Luck By Chance]]'' in 2009.<ref name=asianage/> However, he shot into the spotlight, when he appeared in an episode of [[Coke Studio @ MTV]] (season 2), featuring the song ''Chaudhary'' in collaboration with music director [[Amit Trivedi]] in 2012.<ref name=dna/><ref name=bbc/> Mame also performed another title ''Badri badariya'' at [[Coke Studio @ MTV]], where he collaborated with singer [[Mili Nair]].<ref name=indianexpress/>
He sang for movies like ''[[Mirzya (film)|Mirzya]]'', ''[[I Am (2010 Indian film)|I Am]]'', ''[[No One Killed Jessica]]'' and ''[[Sonchiriya]]''.<ref name=asianage/><ref>{{cite news |last1=Paul |first1=Shreya |title=Sonchiriya music review: Vishal Bhardwaj's pensive, soulful soundtrack is filled to the brim with nostalgia |url=https://www.firstpost.com/entertainment/sonchiriya-music-review-vishal-bhardwajs-pensive-soulful-soundtrack-is-filled-to-the-brim-with-nostalgia-6163551.html |publisher=[[Firstpost]] |date=27 February 2019}}</ref> He also lent his voice for a Hindi song in [[Malayalam]] film, ''[[Monsoon Mangoes]]''.<ref name=asianage>{{cite news |title=Mame Khan is the name |url=http://www.asianage.com/entertainment/music/230218/mame-khan-is-the-name.html |publisher=[[The Asian Age]] |date=23 February 2018}}</ref>
