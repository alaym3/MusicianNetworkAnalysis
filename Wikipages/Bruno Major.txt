'''Bruno Major''' (born 15 July 1988)<ref name="bostonglobe"/> is a British singer-songwriter and guitarist. His debut album ''A Song for Every Moon'' was released in 2017.

==Career==
On 21 January 2014, Major released the four-song EP ''Live'' on [[Virgin Records]], where he worked with producer [[Ethan Johns]] before being released from the label.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Oswald |first1=Angelica |title=Bruno Major: The next big thing |url=https://www.clichemag.com/music/bruno-major-next-big-thing/ |accessdate=30 November 2018 |publisher=Cliche Magazine |date=21 February 2014}}</ref><ref name="awal">{{cite web |title=Bruno Major on How He Found Success as an Independent Artist |url=https://www.awal.com/blog/bruno-major-found-success-independent-artist |website=awal.com |accessdate=30 November 2018}}</ref> In August 2016, Major announced that he would write, record and release one song every month for a year, which resulted in the 12-song R&B album ''A Song for Every Moon'', released on 3 November 2017.<ref name="bostonglobe">{{cite news |last1=Feldberg |first1=Isaac |title=Bruno Major aims for the stars |url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/lifestyle/names/2018/03/01/bruno-major-aims-for-stars/q0VokoqCt1PBWyBXlhf6uM/story.html |accessdate=30 November 2018 |publisher=Boston Globe |date=1 March 2018}}</ref><ref name="billboard">{{cite news |last1=Machin |first1=Jennifer |title=Meet Bruno Major, British R&B Singer-Songwriter & Sam Smith Tourmate |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/8255116/bruno-major-sam-smith-tourmate-interview |accessdate=30 November 2018 |publisher=Billboard |date=20 March 2018}}</ref>

In November 2017, he went on The Trio Tour in six cities in the US and Europe.<ref name="awal"/> In 2018, he embarked on a tour across North America, followed by a UK arena tour opening for [[Sam Smith (singer)|Sam Smith]], a headline tour of Europe and the UK, and a headline tour of the United States.<ref name="billboard"/><ref name="independent">{{cite news |last1=O'Connor |first1=Roisin |title=Bruno Major shares stunning video for 'Places We Won't Walk' with footage from Jan Van Ijken short film |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/music/news/bruno-major-places-we-wont-walk-video-starlings-premiere-a8266731.html |accessdate=5 December 2018 |publisher=The Independent |date=21 March 2018}}</ref><ref name="guitar">{{cite news |last1=Wood |first1=James |title=Guitarist Bruno Major Talks Songwriting, Touring with Sam Smith |url=https://www.guitarworld.com/artists/guitarist-bruno-major-talks-songwriting-touring-with-sam-smith |accessdate=5 December 2018 |publisher=Guitar World |date=18 April 2018}}</ref> He appeared on ''[[The Late Late Show with James Corden|The Late Late Show]]'' on 22 February 2018, in his first television performance, performing his song "Easily".<ref name="bostonglobe"/><ref name="billboard"/> In June 2018, he performed at his first US festival, [[Bonnaroo Music Festival]].<ref name="billboard"/> In September and October, he went on an Asia tour for the first time.

On 19 February 2019, Major released a revised version of "Old Fashioned" from his "Live" EP. Following that, he released a string of five more singles over the year, until 20 March 2020, when Major announced his second full length album, ''To Let a Good Thing Die'', to be released on 5 June 2020. The album featured producer [[Finneas]] as a co-writer for "The Most Beautiful Thing".

His 2020 single "Nothing" reached number 63 on the [[Irish Singles Chart]] in November 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://irma.ie/index.cfm?page=irish-charts&chart=Singles|title=IRMA – Irish Charts|publisher=[[Irish Recorded Music Association]]|access-date=13 November 2021}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Major is originally from [[Northampton]], England.<ref name="independent"/> He moved to [[London]] in 2011.<ref name="interview">{{cite news |last1=Taylor |first1=Trey |title=BRUNO MAJOR’S MUSIC IS DESIGNED TO MAKE YOU CRY |url=https://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/bruno-majors-music-designed-make-cry |accessdate=5 December 2018 |publisher=Interview Magazine |date=19 March 2018}}</ref> Originally a jazz musician, he began his career as a session guitarist at age 16, for artists including [[Lalah Hathaway]]. Major also studied a degree in [[jazz]] at [[Leeds Conservatoire]] (formally Leeds College of Music).<ref name="guitar"/><ref>{{cite news |title=BRUNO MAJOR & THE PERFECT IMPERFECTIONS (INTERVIEW) |url=https://www.urbansoul.be/2016/11/14/bruno-major-the-perfect-imperfections-interview/ |accessdate=5 December 2018 |publisher=Urban Soul |date=14 November 2016}}</ref>

He is the older brother of Dominic 'Dot' Major of [[London Grammar]].
