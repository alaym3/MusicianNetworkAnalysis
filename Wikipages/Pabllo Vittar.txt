'''Phabullo Rodrigues da Silva''' (born November 1, 1994), known professionally as '''Pabllo Vittar''' ({{IPA-pt|ˈpablu viˈtaɾ}}) is a [[Brazilians|Brazilian]] drag queen, singer and songwriter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/international/7850064/pabllo-vittar-major-lazer-brazilian-drag-queen|title=Meet Pabllo Vittar: Major Lazer's Favorite Brazilian Drag Queen|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=June 30, 2018|last1=Billboard Brasil|author1-link=Billboard Brasil}}</ref>

==Life and career==
===1994-2016: Early life===
Born in [[São Luís, Maranhão|São Luís]], Phabullo is a [[dizygotic twin]] and has an older sister. Phabullo spent part of her childhood and adolescence living in the cities of Santa Inês and Caxias, both in the interior of [[Maranhão]], due to financial difficulties. She never knew her father, who abandoned her mother, a nurse, when she was pregnant with her.<ref name=DragNoSamba>{{cite web|url=http://epoca.globo.com/sociedade/noticia/2017/02/pabllo-vittar-tem-drag-no-samba.html|title=Pabllo Vittar: tem drag no samba|trans-title=Pabllo Vittar: there's drag in samba|date=February 27, 2017|access-date=August 5, 2017|publisher=[[Grupo Globo|G1]]|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20170302105407/https://epoca.globo.com/sociedade/noticia/2017/02/pabllo-vittar-tem-drag-no-samba.html|archivedate=March 2, 2017}}</ref>

During her childhood, Phabullo experienced [[bullying]] because of her delicate gestures and feminine voice. She was physically assaulted with a plate of hot soup thrown over her face.<ref name=jconline>{{cite web|url=http://jconline.ne10.uol.com.br/canal/cultura/musica/noticia/2017/02/17/pabllo-vittar-sobre-preconceito-jogaram-sopa-quente-na-minha-cara-271220.php|title=Pabllo Vittar sobre preconceito: "Jogaram sopa quente na minha cara"|trans-title=Pabllo Vittar about prejudice: "They threw hot soup over my face"|date=February 17, 2017|access-date=August 5, 2017|publisher=JCOnline/[[Universo Online|UOL]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.doistercos.com.br/jogaram-sopa-quente-na-minha-cara-diz-pabllo-vittar-a-revista-trip/|title="Jogaram sopa quente na minha cara", diz Pabllo Vittar a revista Trip|trans-title="They threw hot soup over my face", says Pabllo Vittar to Trip Magazine|date=February 18, 2017|access-date=August 5, 2017|publisher=Doistercos}}</ref> She attended ballet classes during this period.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://epoca.globo.com/sociedade/bruno-astuto/noticia/2017/08/pabllo-vittar-evita-rotulos-sobre-sua-sexualidade-e-diz-meu-negocio-e-transitar.html|title=Pabllo Vittar evita rótulos sobre sua sexualidade e diz: "Meu negócio é transitar"|trans-title=Pabllo Vittar avoids labels about her sexuality and says: "My thing is to transition"|last=Astuto|first=Bruno|date=August 1, 2017|access-date=August 12, 2017|magazine=[[Época (Brazilian magazine)|Época]]}}</ref>

At the age of 13, Phabullo began doing covers at family parties and school performances and joined a choir at a Catholic church.<ref name=mediumPost>{{cite web|url=https://medium.com/nada-errado/pabllo-vittar-estou-aqui-para-desconstruir-g%C3%AAnero-f41d6656dd30#.86f1r6cy5|title=Pabllo Vittar: "Estou aqui para desconstruir gêneros"|trans-title=Pabllo Vittar: "I'm here to deconstruct genders"|publisher=medium.com|first=Ricardo|last=Laranja|date=December 22, 2015|access-date=February 3, 2016}}</ref><ref name=ParaDarClose/> Soon after, she began songwriting. Later, she decided to become a professional, moving to [[São Paulo (city)|São Paulo]] with her sister, where she worked in several places, such as snack bars and beauty salons. Soon after, she moved to [[Uberlândia]] with her mother, who had recently married. At the age of 15, she came out to her mother, who had suspected that she was gay; her sister already knew about her [[sexual orientation]].<ref name=caronaPost/> Her mother, whom she talks to daily, is supportive of her sexual orientation.<ref name="guardian">{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2017/oct/20/pabllo-vittar-brazil-gay-drag-queen-pop-star|title=Brazil's LGBT pop sensation: 'I want to give them strength'|last=Phillips|first=Dom|date=October 21, 2017|website=The Guardian|access-date=March 14, 2018}}</ref>

{{Quote box|width=30%|align=left|quote="Pabllo Vittar is a boy. Who is a girl. Who has no gender. Who is not afraid. Who prefers a thousand times to be on a stage than anywhere else in the world."|source=<small>Phabullo on "Who is Pabllo Vittar?".<ref name=mediumPost/></small>}}
She then began to go out for the night in Maranhão, to make friends, and to participate as one of a selection of singers in a house of shows. She started performing her own songs and impersonating celebrities for a small fee, but it was not a professional job. She was now dressing feminine, but did not identify herself as a drag queen; the first time that she did drag was at age 17 in Uberlândia, to publicize a party of her friend by delivering pamphlets at the door of a nightclub.<ref name=DragNoSamba/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://revistatrip.uol.com.br/trip-tv/pabllo-vittar-drag-queen-genero-musica-vai-passar-mal-lgbt-lgbtfobia-carnaval-amor-e-sexo|title=Ninguém tomba Pabllo Vittar|trans-title=Nobody takes Pabllo Vittar down|first=Bianka|last=Vieira|date=February 16, 2017|access-date=August 6, 2017|publisher=Revista Trip/[[Universo Online|UOL]]}}</ref> With time, she started performing in LGBT parades in several cities of Maranhão and became known by the state's [[LGBT community]]. At age 18, in [[Minas Gerais]], Phabullo started participating in and winning some beauty contests in full drag, before beginning her professional career as a singer while adopting the stage name of "Pabllo Knowles", an homage to singer [[Beyoncé]].<ref name=mediumPost/> Her first performance as a drag queen occurred at the Belgrano nightclub, of producers Ian Hayashi and Leocádio Rezende (whom Phabullo would later call her "parents").<ref name=ParaDarClose>{{cite news|url=https://oglobo.globo.com/cultura/pabllo-vittar-pra-dar-close-gente-vai-ate-num-vulcao-em-erupcao-20830881|title=Pabllo Vittar: "Para dar close, a gente vai até num vulcão em erupção"|trans-title=Pabllo Vittar: "To look sickening, we even go to an erupting volcano"|date=January 27, 2017|access-date=August 6, 2017|newspaper=[[O Globo]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blogosdoisladosdamoeda.com.br/2015/12/fama-drag-queen-caxiense-bomba-na-web-e.html|title=FAMA: Drag Queen maranhense bomba na web e substitui Léo Jaime no "Amor e Sexo" da Rede Globo!!!|trans-title=FAME: Drag queen from Maranhão breaks the internet and replaces Léo Jaime on "Amor e Sexo", from Rede Globo!!!|first=Analio|last=Junior|date=December 22, 2015|access-date=February 3, 2016}}</ref>

Still in Minas Gerais, Phabullo took a [[Competitive examination|university entrance exam]] by the [[Federal University of Uberlândia]], where she was admitted to the full design course in 2013, but after some periods she dropped out of college due to her schedule of shows, which increased greatly due to the success of her music videos on the internet and her participation in the band of primetime television program ''[[Amor e Sexo|Amor & Sexo]]''.<ref name=ExtraGlobo>{{cite web|url=https://extra.globo.com/noticias/educacao/vida-de-calouro/cantora-do-amor-sexo-pabllo-vittar-tambem-estudante-de-design-de-interiores-20980817.html|title=Cantora do Amor & Sexo, Pabllo Vittar também é estudante de Design de Interiores|trans-title=Singer on "Amor e Sexo", Pabllo Vittar is also student of Design|date=February 26, 2017|access-date=October 5, 2017|work=[[Extra (newspaper)]]|publisher=Info Globo/[[Grupo Globo]]}}</ref>

When she attracted attention from entrepreneurs in the entertainment field,<ref name=caronaPost>{{cite web|url=http://globotv.globo.com/tv-integracao-triangulo-mineiro/carona/v/jovem-cantor-pabllo-vittar-conhece-a-tv-integracao/3812953/|title=Jovem cantor Pabllo Vittar conhece a TV Integração|trans-title=Young singer Pabllo Vittar meets TV Integração|publisher=Rede Globo|date=December 6, 2015|access-date=February 3, 2016}}</ref> Phabullo began her musical career professionally, performing in bars and concert halls in Uberlândia and cities of the region. In this period, Phabullo adopted the stage name of '''Pabllo Vittar'''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.correiodeuberlandia.com.br/entretenimento/sucesso-com-open-bar-pabllo-vittar-se-apresenta-em-uberlandia/|title=Sucesso com 'Open bar', Pabllo Vittar se apresenta em Uberlândia|trans-title=Success with "Open Bar", Pabllo Vittar performs in Uberlândia|publisher=Correio de Uberlândia|first=Carolina|last=Monteiro|date=December 18, 2015|access-date=February 3, 2016}}</ref>

Vittar appeared on TV for the first time in 2014, on a Brazilian talent singing show, performing "[[I Have Nothing]]" by [[Whitney Houston]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://watermarkonline.com/2019/10/09/international-drag-superstar-pabllo-vittar-headlines-orlandos-pride-stage/|title=International drag superstar Pabllo Vittar headlines Orlando's Pride Stage|work=Watermark Online|first=Jeremy|last=Williams|date=October 9, 2019|access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref> She became famous in the media in 2015 with the success of her music video "Open Bar",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.agambiarra.com/pabllo-vittar-open-bar-clipe/|title=Conheça a Pabllo Vittar, drag brasileira que ganhou o Diplo com clipe de estreia|access-date=October 8, 2015|website=A Gambiarra|language=pt|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181114100652/https://www.agambiarra.com/pabllo-vittar-open-bar-clipe/|archive-date=November 14, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> a Portuguese-language version of [[Major Lazer]]'s song "[[Lean On]]" with original lyrics, produced by [[Bonde do Rolê]] and [[Omulu]].<ref name="openPost">{{cite web|url=http://www.papelpop.com/2015/10/drag-brasileira-pabllo-vittar-lanca-clipe-de-estreia-transformando-major-lazer-em-sambinha/|title=Drag brasileiro Pabllo Vittar lança clipe de estreia transformando Major Lazer em sambinha|publisher=Tv Globo|first=Carolina|last=Sanches|date=January 26, 2016|access-date= February 3, 2016}}</ref> In less than four months, the video reached one million views on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://g1.globo.com/al/alagoas/musica/noticia/2016/01/pabllo-vittar-fala-de-carreira-sucesso-e-novos-projetos-em-entrevista-ao-g1.html|title=Pabllo Vittar fala de carreira, sucesso e novos projetos em entrevista ao G1|publisher=Tv Globo|first=Pedro|last=Rocha|date=October 8, 2015|access-date=February 3, 2016}}</ref> She also released an extended play with the same name, using pop hits' instrumentals with new arrangements and lyrics.

===2017: Vai Passar Mal===
On January 12, 2017, her debut studio album ''[[Vai Passar Mal]]'' was released – including the singles "Nêga", "[[Todo Dia]]", "[[K.O. (Pabllo Vittar song)|K.O.]]", "[[Corpo Sensual]]", "Então Vai" (with [[Diplo]]) and "Indestrutível". In the same month she released "Todo Dia" as the second single of the album, which became notorious at [[Brazilian Carnival]] due to its lyrics. The third single "K.O." became an even bigger hit and brought Vittar to nationwide fame and success; as of 2021, the song has over 381 million views on YouTube. The fourth single, "Corpo Sensual", was released on September 6, 2017, to similar success, resulting in over 321&nbsp;million views on YouTube.

Vittar has been featured in songs by international artists such as Major Lazer's "[[Sua Cara]]" (with fellow Brazilian singer [[Anitta (singer)|Anitta]]). The music video was released on July 30, 2017, and reached 10th place on the [[list of most-viewed online videos in the first 24 hours]], with 25 million views.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7882126/major-lazer-sua-cara-video-anitta-pabllo-vittar|title=Watch Major Lazer's Steamy 'Sua Cara' Video Featuring Anitta & Pabllo Vittar: Watch|work=Billboard|access-date=December 6, 2017}}</ref> The singer also appears on "[[I Got It (Charli XCX song)|I Got It]]", from [[Charli XCX]]'s fourth mixtape ''[[Pop 2 (mixtape)|Pop 2]]''. Vittar collaborated again with XCX on "[[Flash Pose]]", released on July 25, 2019; the single was later included on Vittar's album ''[[111 (Pabllo Vittar album)|111]]''. She also got to perform "Flash Pose" at the [[2019 MTV Europe Music Awards]].

===2018: Não Para Não===
On August 15, 2018, Pabllo Vittar released her second studio album ''[[Não Para Não]]'' – including the singles "Problema Seu", "Disk Me", "Seu Crime", "Buzina" and "Não Vou Deitar". Vittar toured in support of the album, in Brazil and internationally. An extended tour called NPN Pride saw Vittar performing in various [[Pride parade|pride parades]] throughout USA and Canada. The first two singles were released to great success, with "Disk Me" reaching over 85 million views and "Problema Seu" reaching over 112&nbsp;million views on Youtube. The singles "Seu Crime" and "Buzina" also were successful, albeit with smaller numbers, with both reaching over 25&nbsp;million views on Youtube. This album also featured Brazilian singers Dilsinho and [[Ludmilla (singer)|Ludmilla]] in "Trago seu Amor de Volta" and "Vai Embora", respectively.

===2019-2020: 111===
On October 31, 2019, Pabllo Vittar released the ''111 1'' [[Extended play|EP]] – including the singles "Parabéns" (with Brazilian rapper Psirico), "Amor de Que" and "Flash Pose" (with Charli XCX). Following the success of the ''111 1'' EP, "Amor de Que" rose to success thanks to the 2020 Brazilian Carnival, with the song reaching over 50 million views in just a month; as of 2021, the song has over 138&nbsp;million views. "Parabéns" was released as the second single and also enjoyed similar success, with over 127&nbsp;million views on Youtube as of 2020. "Flash Pose" had modest success, reaching over 25&nbsp;million views on YouTube.

On March 24, 2020, Vittar's third studio album ''111'' was released – including the singles "Clima Quente" (with Brazilian Jerry Smith), "[[Tímida]]" (with [[Thalía]]) and "Rajadão"; the songs from the EP were also included in the album. Vittar collaborated with various artists on the album, and included songs in Portuguese, English and Spanish.

With the nomination of "Sua Cara" at the [[19th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]], Vittar became the first [[Latin Grammy Awards|Grammy]]-nominated drag queen. She also became the first drag queen to win an [[MTV Europe Music Awards|EMA]] award in its 25-year run for [[MTV Europe Music Award for Best Brazilian Act|Best Brazilian Act]].The following year, she consecutively won again.

==Public image and impact==
{{Quote box|width=30%|align=right|quote="I think gender doesn't matter to me. If you write 'him,' I'll find it incredible, if you write 'her,' same. But when I get in drag... I won't stand in front of the mirror two hours putting on makeup for someone to call me 'he,' right? Call me 'she'! 'She' is beautiful, 'she' is a singer, 'she' is a drag queen! I like to be called in the feminine."|source=<small>Vittar on pronouns<ref name="popline" /></small>}} Vittar's music is considered as "unofficial anthems for Brazil's [[LGBT community|lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender community]]"<ref name="NYT-trans"/> and he is widely viewed as a voice for them. Because dangers to LGBT+ people have grown in Brazil,<ref>{{cite web |title=Brazil as World LGBT Murder Capital and Rio's Place in the Data |url=https://www.rioonwatch.org/?p=37249 |website=RioOnWatch |date=10 July 2017}}</ref> Vittar's openness about being gay has made him an icon to those living in a prejudiced environment. As an openly gay Brazilian drag queen, Vittar has served as a positive representation of homosexuality and gender fluidity. He used his visibility to support LGBT+ individuals in Brazil and in other countries in South America. He said: "They tell me a lot about their daily struggle to go out on the street being gay, being drag. I want to give them strength so they can continue being who they are."<ref name="guardian"/> Vittar also appeared on ''[[A Força do Querer]]'' ("Edge of Desire"), a popular soap opera about a [[transgender man]].<ref name="NYT-trans"/> After seeing Vittar perform at the [[Rock in Rio]] festival, actor [[Fábio Assunção]] wrote in a Facebook post: "You represent the possibility of truth in a sea of hypocrisy. Your public figure is the voice of many suffocated people."<ref name="guardian"/>

In an interview for ''Veja'' magazine, Vittar revealed that he chose a masculine stage name because he wanted to be truthful to the public, since he is not [[transgender]]. He only acts as a woman when he's on stage as Pabllo Vittar and has a more typical [[Masculinity|masculine]] [[gender expression]] in his private life.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.portalfamosos.com.br/pabllo-vittar-tira-duvidas-sobre-sua-identidade-de-genero-em-nova-entrevista-e-confirma-gravacao-de-clipes-para-k-o-e-indestrutivel-do-novo-album/|title=Pabllo Vittar tira dúvidas sobre sua identidade de gênero em nova entrevista e confirma gravação de clipes para "K.O." e "Indestrutível" do novo álbum|trans-title=Pabllo Vittar questions his gender identity in new interview and confirms music videos for "K.O." and "Indestructible", from his new album.|first=Matheus|last=Fabio|date=January 28, 2017|access-date=August 6, 2017|publisher=Portal Famosos}}</ref> During the same interview, Vittar said he is "making music for humans."<ref name="popline"/> Since his debut, Vittar became the most followed and most streamed drag queen in the world, surpassing [[RuPaul]].<ref name="vogue"/> In 2020, Vittar was featured in a campaign for [[Calvin Klein (company)|Calvin Klein]], as part of the designer's pride collection.<ref>{{Cite web|first=Jacqui |last=Palumbo|title=Black trans model Jari Jones fronts Calvin Klein's Pride campaign|url=https://www.cnn.com/style/article/calvin-klein-pride-campaign-jari-jones-trend/index.html|access-date=June 28, 2020|website=CNN|language=en}}</ref>
