'''Ill Niño''' (stylized as '''ill niño''') is an American [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band formed in [[Union City, New Jersey]], in 1998 by drummer and percussionist Dave Chavarri. They have released seven studio albums, two EPs and one compilation album, totaling in over 1.3 million worldwide album sales.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allheavymetal.net/news/article/aid/1276/Ill_Nino_15_Years_Of_Revolution_UK_Tour_this_March|title=Ill Nino 15 Years Of Revolution UK Tour this March|publisher=allheavymetal.net|date=February 28, 2017|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref>

==History==
Ill Niño was formed as El Niño by former [[Pro-Pain]] drummer David "Dave" Chavarri in May 1998.<ref name="ElNino">{{cite web |url=https://myglobalmind.com/2014/08/30/interview-dave-chavarri-drums-ill-nino/ |title=Interview with Dave Chavarri (Drums) (Ill Nino) |publisher=Myglobalmind |date=August 30, 2014}}</ref> He enlisted [[Merauder]] vocalist Jorge Rosado, guitarist Daniel Gomez and bassist Christian Machado and took on the position of drummer and percussionist himself.<ref name="IllNino">{{Cite book|last=Sharpe-Young|first=Garry|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uIIf03bGyAAC&dq=%22Ill+Ni%C3%B1o%22&pg=PA176|title=New Wave of American Heavy Metal|date=December 30, 2005|publisher=Zonda Books Limited|isbn=978-0-95826-840-0}}</ref> With this line-up,<ref name="ElNinoEP">{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/El-Ni%C3%B1o-El-Ni%C3%B1o/release/18212845|title= El Niño - El Niño (1998, CDr) {{!}} Discogs|website=Discogs.com}}</ref> the band released a self-titled EP. Gomez left the band soon after the EP's release, with a variety of guitarists filling in his spot. Among them were future fulltime member [[Marc Rizzo]],<ref name="MarcRizzo">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KH-cbH1INBU|title= Ill Niño Live - COMPLETE SHOW - New York, NY, USA (18th October, 1998) "Coney Island High"|website=Youtube.com}}</ref> Scott Roberts of [[Biohazard (band)|Biohazard]]<ref name="ScottRoberts">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LtCoHbDDEpg|title= ILL NINO 1998 CONEY ISLAND HIGH L.E.S NYC|website=Youtube.com}}</ref> and Derek Sykes of [[Demolition Hammer]].<ref name="DerekSykes">{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Various-M%C3%A9talo/release/3947619|title= Métalo (1999, CD) {{!}} Discogs|website=Discogs.com}}</ref> Sykes also appears on the compilation album ''Métalo'', released by Grita! Records in June 1999.<ref name="DerekSykes" /> By this time, the band had already renamed to Ill Niño.<ref name="DerekSykes" /> However, just a month later, the band was put on hold due to Chavarri taking over [[Roy Mayorga]]'s duties in [[Soulfly]] on the Bring Da Shit Tour.<ref name="IllNino" /> After two months on tour, Chavarri decided to reform his old band, now with Machado on lead vocals and joined by guitarists Marc Rizzo and Jardel Paisante, bassist Lazaro Pina and Roger Vasquez on additional percussions.<ref name="IllNino" /> In this formation, the band's first release was a [[Ill Niño EP|self-titled EP]] in 2000.<ref name="IllNino" /> They received exposure via the New York metal college radio station WSOU and opened for bands like [[Kittie]], [[Soulfly]] and [[Snapcase]]. Subsequently, the band was signed by [[Roadrunner Records]].

On September 18, 2001, Ill Niño released their debut album, ''[[Revolution Revolución]]''.<ref name="ReRe">{{cite web |url=https://shop.season-of-mist.com/ill-nino-revolution-revolucion-cd |title=Ill Nino - Revolution Revolucion - CD |publisher=season-of-mist.com |date=}}</ref> The album peaked at No. 28 in the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and went on to sell over 350,000 albums worldwide in the first two years after release.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.illnino.com/bio.aspx |title=ILL NINO |publisher=illnino.com |date=|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040718065213/http://www.illnino.com/bio.aspx |archive-date=July 18, 2004 }}</ref> In early 2003, just before Ill Niño was set to record the follow-up, the band parted ways with guitarist Marc Rizzo and percussionist Roger Vasquez.<ref name="MarcRoger">{{cite web |url=http://www.illnino.com/news.htm |title= THE ILL NINO EXPERIENCE - 5.09.2003 |publisher= illnino.com |date=May 9, 2003|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20030806063217/http://www.illnino.com/news.htm |archive-date= August 6, 2003 }}</ref> ''Confession'' was recorded in mid-2003 and released on September 30, 2003. It debuted at No. 37 in the ''Billboard'' 200 with first week sales of 27,863.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.de/page/News?&news_page=139&news_id=24342|title=ILL NIÑO: Billboard Top 200-Entry!|date=October 8, 2003|publisher=roadrunnerrecords.de|accessdate=August 8, 2003|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120217054905/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.de/page/News?&news_page=139&news_id=24342|archive-date=February 17, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> The lead single, "How Can I Live", was their first single to break into the ''Billboard'' charts, peaking at No. 26 on the Mainstream Rock chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/ill-nino/chart-history/hot-mainstream-rock-tracks/song/438269|title=Ill Niño – Chart History: Mainstream Rock Songs|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref>

The band's third effort'', One Nation Underground'', was released on September 27, 2005 and debuted at No. 101 on the ''Billboard'' 200 charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=14459|title=Ill Nino Post Song & Album Art Online|date=July 16, 2005|website=Metalunderground.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> On July 15, 2006, Ill Niño parted with Roadrunner Records and their guitarist Jardel Paisante.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=20197|title=Ill Nino Part Ways With Roadrunner Records|date=July 15, 2006|website=Metalunderground.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=20282|title=Ill Nino To Attempt Another Album|date=July 22, 2006|website=Metalunderground.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> Just a week later, they signed a new deal with the recently formed Cement Shoes Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=20341|title=Ill Nino Inks Deal With Cement Records|date=July 24, 2006|website=Metalunderground.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> Roadrunner released ''The Best of Ill Niño'' on September 12, 2006,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=20424|title=Best of Ill Nino Collection To Surface This Year|date=July 27, 2006|website=Metalunderground.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> as a contractual obligation to the label, and without the band's consent or involvement.
{{stack|[[File:Ill niño on live.jpg|thumb|Ill Niño in concert, August 2009]]}}
Through Cement Shoes Records they released ''The Under Cover Sessions'', an EP featuring three covers and two new songs,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=22271|title=Ill Nino To Release New EP In November|date=October 12, 2006|website=Metalunderground.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> and ''Enigma'', their fourth studio album, released in 2008 after multiple delays.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=32807|title=New Ill Nino Album Pushed Back|date=July 17, 2007|website=Metalunderground.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> The album sold 5,000 copies in the United States in its first week of release and debuted at No. 145 on the ''Billboard'' 200 chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=93225|title= ILL NINO: 'Enigma' First-Week Sales Revealed - Mar. 19, 2008 |date=March 19, 2008|publisher=roadrunnerrecords.com|accessdate=March 7, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080423224237/http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=93225|archive-date=April 23, 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In March 2010, Ill Niño signed with Victory Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2010/03/17/news/ill-nino-sign-deal-with-victory/|title=Ill Nino Sign Deal With Victory|date=March 17, 2010|website=Theprp.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> ''Dead New World'', the band's label debut, was released on October 25, 2010 and production was shared by Sahaj Ticotin and Clint Lowery of Sevendust.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalunderground.com/news/details.cfm?newsid=62387|title=Ill Niño Premieres New Music Video For "Against The Wall" Online|date=November 23, 2010|website=Metalunderground.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> ''Epidemia'', the band's sixth album, was released on October 22, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2012/08/28/news/ill-nino-reveal-epidemia-album-cover/|title=Ill Niño Reveal "Epidemia" Album Cover|date=August 28, 2012|website=Theprp.com|access-date=March 7, 2021}}</ref> This was their last release to feature long-time percussionist Daniel "Danny" Couto, who left the band in late 2013 and was replaced by Oscar Santiago. The band's seventh studio album, ''Till Death La Familia'', was released on July 22, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://loudwire.com/ill-nino-till-death-la-familia-album-new-single/|title=Ill Nino Set Release Date for 'Till Death, La Familia' Album, Unleash New Single|publisher=Loudwire.com|accessdate=March 7, 2021}}</ref> Afterwards, they toured in support of the new album and embarked on a worldwide "15 Years Of Revolution" tour to celebrate the 15th anniversary of ''Revolution Revolución'' in 2016 and 2017.

On January 15, 2019, Dave Chavarri announced that Cristian Machado, Ahrue Luster and Diego Verduzco had departed the band. They were replaced by Marcos Leal ([[Shattered Sun]]), Jes De Hoyos  ([[Sons of Texas]]) and Salvadore Dominguez (ex-[[Upon a Burning Body]]), respectively.<ref name="lineup">{{cite web|url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/ill-nino-announces-new-lineup-releases-sangre-single/|title= ILL NIÑO Announces New Lineup, Releases 'Sangre' Single |website=Blabbermouth.net|access-date=March 8, 2021}}</ref> This occasion also saw the return of Daniel "Danny" Couto on percussions.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://loudwire.com/ill-nino-new-members-new-song-sangre/|title= Ill Nino Unveil Three New Members + First Song in Five Years |website=Loudwire.com|access-date=March 8, 2021}}</ref> One day later, the three former members dismissed Chavarri's public statement and assured the band's fans that they had not departed the band, vowing to continue as "Ill Niño" instead.

On March 21, 2019, [[MetalSucks]] acquired a copy of the lawsuit paperwork filed by Machado, Luster and Verduzco accusing Chavarri of shady business deals, such as using his status as Ill Niño's manager to scam the band into taking a financial loss while repaying a personal loan, falsifying financial documents in order to keep tens of thousands of dollars for himself, pocketing show guarantees and tour advances for himself, sometimes even canceling tours and then refusing to return the promoter's advance, and refusing the band members their legally owed access to financial records among many other accusations.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.metalsucks.net/2019/03/21/read-the-ill-nino-lawsuit/|title=Read the Ill Niño Lawsuit|date=March 21, 2019|website=MetalSucks.net|access-date=March 25, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theprp.com/2019/03/21/news/the-ill-nino-lawsuit-features-numerous-ugly-accusations-against-drummer-dave-chavarri/|title=The Ill Niño Lawsuit Features Numerous Ugly Accusations Against Drummer Dave Chavarri|date=March 21, 2019|website=Theprp.com|access-date=March 25, 2019}}</ref>

In March 2020, Chavarri, Pina, Machado, Luster and Verduzco reached a mutual agreement, allowing Chavarri and Pina to continue with the Ill Niño bandname.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/ill-nino-members-reach-agreement-over-band-name/|title=ILL NIÑO Members Reach Agreement Over Band Name|date=March 26, 2020|website=BLABBERMOUTH.NET}}</ref> With the legal issues resolved, the new line-up began work on their eighth album and subsequently released a second single, "Máscara", featuring AJ Channer from [[Fire from the Gods]].<ref name="IllMortals">{{cite web|url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/ill-ninos-new-lineup-is-back-with-mascara-single-video/|title=ILL NIÑO's New Lineup Is Back With 'Máscara' Single/Video|publisher=[[Blabbermouth.net]]|access-date=December 10, 2020|date=December 10, 2020}}</ref> At the same time, they announced the title of their next album, ''IllMortals''.<ref name="IllMortals" /> They also held a one-day event dubbed "American Revolución" on January 30, 2021, along with Fire From the Gods and [[Hyro the Hero]]. Also present as guests were former members of the band, such as [[Marc Rizzo]], Jorge Rosado and Daniel Gomez. This marked the first time in over 18 years they performed with Ill Niño.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://knotfest.com/fire-from-the-gods-team-with-ill-nino-and-hyro-the-hero-for-american-revolucion-virtual-concert/|title=Fire From the Gods team with Ill Nino and Hyro The Hero for 'American Revolución' virtual concert|publisher=Knotfest.com|access-date=March 8, 2021|date=December 23, 2020}}</ref>

On October 6, 2021, Marc Rizzo announced that he would be rejoining Ill Niño, upon leaving [[Soulfly]], and had been recording for the new album and would be on tour with the band soon. 

On October 8, 2021, the band confirmed via Facebook that Rizzo had officially rejoined Ill Niño and was recording for their next album ''IllMortals'', bringing his signature flamenco style back. The band also confirmed the exit of lead guitarist Jes De Hoyos from the band, stating that he wanted to focus on his personal life.

== Musical style ==
Ill Niño are described as [[Latin metal]],<ref name="SK">{{cite web |url=http://www.stereokiller.com/newsreviews/article.cfm?intarticleid=11512 |title=Ill Nino - Epidemia Review |publisher=Stereokiller |date=December 2, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/ill-ni%C3%B1o-mn0000087741 |title=Ill Niño |publisher=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref> [[nu metal]],<ref name="SK"/><ref name="imciver">{{cite book |last = McIver |first =Joel |title=Nu Metal: The Next Generation of Rock & Punk |publisher=Omnibus Press |year=2002 |page=62 |isbn=978-0-7119-9209-2}}</ref><ref name="BNW-illnino">{{cite book |last=Udo |first=Tommy |title=Brave Nu World |publisher=Sanctuary Publishing |year=2002 |pages=[https://archive.org/details/bravenuworld00tomm/page/193 193–194, 242–243] |isbn=1-86074-415-X |url-access=registration |url=https://archive.org/details/bravenuworld00tomm/page/193 }}</ref> [[alternative metal]]<ref name="Best Of">{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/best-of-ill-ni%C3%B1o-mw0000575890 |title=Best of Ill Niño - Ill Niño |publisher=[[Allmusic]] |access-date=February 19, 2013}}</ref> and [[metalcore]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/cdreviews/till-death-la-familia.amp|title=Till Death La Familia - ILL NIÑO|website=Blabbermouth.net|access-date=January 20, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heavyblogisheavy.com/2010/06/18/bands-that-made-me-heavy-slipknot-trivium-ill-nino/|title=Bands That Made Me Heavy: Slipknot, Trivium, & Ill Nino|date=June 18, 2010|website=Heavyblogisheavy.com|access-date=January 20, 2019}}</ref>
