'''Louis Grandison''', known professionally as '''Dexta Daps''', is a Jamaican reggae singer known for his [[dancehall]] songs as well as ballads.<ref name=gleaner>{{cite web|url=http://jamaica-gleaner.com/article/entertainment/20170519/ny-fans-excited-about-dexta-daps-upcoming-memorial-day-performance|title=NY Fans Excited About Dexta Daps' Upcoming Memorial Day Performance|work=[[The Gleaner (newspaper)|The Gleaner]]|date=May 19, 2017|accessdate=April 15, 2019}}</ref> His songs include the 2014 ballad ''Morning Love'', 2015's ''7Eleven'' and the 2015 song ''Shabba Madda Pot''.<ref name=pigeon>{{cite web|url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/jamaicas-dexta-daps-going-global-163334484.html|title=Jamaica's Dexta Daps is Going Global|author=Reshma B|work=Pigeons & Planes|publisher=[[Yahoo! News]]|date=September 18, 2016|accessdate=April 15, 2019}}</ref> Following up on his singles, he launched his debut album ''Intro'' in 2017 with an erotic short film that received more than 1.6 million views on [[YouTube]].<ref name=gleaner/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://rebelradioconnectz.com/2017/05/16/dexta-daps-sexy-raunchy-short-film-album-intro-click-video/|title=Dexta Daps Sexy Raunchy Short Film For His Album 'Intro' [CLICK FOR VIDEO] -|date=May 16, 2017|accessdate=April 15, 2019}}</ref> He performed at [[Reggae Sumfest]] in 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.loopcayman.com/content/ladies-went-wild-dexta-daps-sumfest-0|title=Dexta Daps heads to Cayman after delivering at Reggae Sumfest|website=www.loopcayman.com|date=July 23, 2017|accessdate=April 15, 2019}}</ref> 
He's been in various collaborations including with [[M.I.A. (rapper)]] and [[Blakkman]]. In 2017, reggae artist [[D'Angel]] released the song ''Whipped'' as a response to Daps' song ''Owner''.<ref>{{cite news|author=Curtis Campbell|url=http://jamaica-star.com/article/entertainment/20170617/dangel-rides-dexta-daps-wave-whipped|title=D'Angel rides Dexta Daps' wave with 'Whipped'|work=The Star|date=June 17, 2017|accessdate=April 15, 2019}}</ref>

Daps grew up in the [[West Kingston, Jamaica|Kingston, Jamaica]] neighborhood of [[Seaview Gardens]], a neighborhood known for poverty as well as for its tight community and as the home of Jamaican reggae stars
[[Shabba Ranks]], [[Bounty Killer]], and [[Elephant Man]] among others.<ref name=pigeon/>
