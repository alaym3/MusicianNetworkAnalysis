'''LeParis Dade''', known professionally as '''OMB Peezy''', is an American rapper based in [[Sacramento, California]].

==Background and career==
OMB Peezy was born to a musical family in [[Mobile, Alabama]]<ref>{{cite web|title=OMB Peezy Is The New Voice Of Regional Rap|url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/04/19/omb-peezy-lay-down-loyalty-over-love-interview|website=The FADER|accessdate=July 18, 2017}}</ref> and later raised in [[Sacramento, California]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Truitt|first1=Nikita|title=OMB Peezy ' "Lay Down" (Video)|url=https://undergroundhiphopblog.com/2017/07/07/omb-peezy-lay-down-video/|website=UndergroundHipHopBLOG.com|accessdate=July 18, 2017|date=July 7, 2017}}</ref>

In 2016, he released the song "Lay Down" and its accompanying music video. In early 2017, he signed to [[Sick Wid It Records]] and later [[300 Entertainment]].<ref>{{cite web|title=OMB Peezy Signs To 300 Entertainment – Fashionably-Early|url=http://www.fashionably-early.com/2017/04/24/omb-peezy-signs-to-300-entertainment/|website=Fashionably-Early|accessdate=July 18, 2017|date=April 24, 2017}}</ref> He released "When I Was Down" in May 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=When I Was Down - Single by Omb Peezy on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/when-i-was-down-single/id1238473723|website=iTunes|accessdate=July 18, 2017}}</ref>

Peezy also featured in [[Nef the Pharaoh]]'s song, "Move 4", with Jay Ant, released in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Nef The Pharaoh Shares Artwork & Tracklist For "The Chang Project"|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/nef-the-pharaoh-shares-artwork-and-tracklist-for-the-chang-project-news.31420.html|website=HotNewHipHop|accessdate=July 18, 2017}}</ref> In June 2017, he released the single "Porch."<ref name="Porch">{{cite web|last1=Weinstein|first1=Max|title=OMB Peezy Jumps Off the 'Porch' in New Video - XXL|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/06/omb-peezy-porch-video/|website=XXL Mag|accessdate=July 18, 2017}}</ref> The single "Try Sumthin" featuring Yhung To was released in July 2017.<ref name="Porch" /><ref>{{cite web|last1=Johnson|first1=Tyrese|title=OMB Peezy talks moving to California, Loyalty Over Love & more (Exclusive Interview) - The latest Bay Area rap and hip-hop music - Thizzler On The Roof|url=http://www.thizzler.com/blog/2017/6/8/omb-peezy-talks-moving-to-california-loyalty-over-love-more.html|website=Thizzler On The Roof|accessdate=July 18, 2017|language=en}}</ref> He released the [[Extended play|EP]] ''Humble Beginnings'' on October 11, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Post|first1=Chantilly|title=OMB Peezy Drops EP "Humble Beginnings"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/omb-peezy-drops-ep-humble-beginnings-new-mixtape.117526.html|website=HotNewHipHop|accessdate=October 11, 2017}}</ref> In July 2018, he released the single "Yeah Yeah" featuring TK Kravitz. The mixtape ''Loyalty Over Love'' was released on August 10, 2018.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ivey |first1=Justin |title=OMB Peezy Shares Debut Album Details & Releases New Single |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.47593/title.omb-peezy-shares-debut-album-details-releases-new-single# |website=HipHopDX |accessdate=July 30, 2018}}</ref> In April 2019, he released the album ''Preacher to the Streets''.<ref name="preacher">{{cite web |last1=Ivey |first1=Justin |title=OMB Peezy Releases "Preacher To The Streets" LP |url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.51193/title.omb-peezy-releases-preacher-to-the-streets-lp# |website=HipHopDX |accessdate=May 1, 2019 |date=April 25, 2019}}</ref> In April 2020, he released the single "Everybody".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/4/omb-peezy-everybody-music-video|title=OMB Peezy Drops Lucid Lo-Fi Visuals for New Single "Everybody"|website=HYPEBEAST|access-date=April 29, 2020}}</ref> On May 15, he released the mixtape ''In The Meantime''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=OMB Peezy Releases New Mixtape "In The Meantime" (Prod. By Drumdummie)|url=https://respect-mag.com/2020/05/omb-peezy-releases-new-mixtape-in-the-meantime-prod-by-drumdummie/|date=May 15, 2020|website=RESPECT.|language=en-US|access-date=May 19, 2020}}</ref> In November 2020, he featured on [[The Hunna|The Hunna's]] remix of "Lost".<ref>{{Cite web|last=link|first=Get|last2=Facebook|last3=Twitter|last4=Pinterest|last5=Email|last6=Apps|first6=Other|title=The Hunna share new version of "Lost" featuring OMB Preezy|url=http://www.itsallindie.com/2020/11/the-hunna-share-new-version-of-lost.html|access-date=November 16, 2020}}</ref>

==Legal issues==
In March 2021, Peezy was charged with aggravated assault with a deadly weapon and possession of a firearm after allegedly shooting at [[Roddy Ricch]] and [[42 Dugg]] while filming a music video on set.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ganz|first=Jami|title=Rapper OMB Peezy charged in shooting on music video set: cops|url=https://www.nydailynews.com/snyde/ny-alabama-rapper-charged-georgia-shooting-20210305-s36kd4laardi5m3sr3mfuycj5i-story.html|access-date=March 9, 2021|website=nydailynews.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Rapper OMB Peezy arrested in Atlanta music video shooting|url=https://www.ajc.com/news/breaking-rapper-omb-peezy-arrested-in-atlanta-music-video-shooting/KBEZW5S4IJGVRDL76NZ5QMIMYM/|access-date=March 9, 2021|website=ajc|language=English}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Rapper OMB Peezy charged in shooting at Roddy Ricch, 42 Dugg music video set, police say|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/celebrities/2021/03/04/roddy-ricch-42-dugg-music-video-omb-peezy-charged-shooting/6917635002/|access-date=March 9, 2021|website=USA TODAY|language=en-US}}</ref>
