'''Christian Valentin "Val" Brunn''' (born 20 July 1994), better known by his stage name '''Virtual Riot''', is a German DJ and [[electronic music]] producer. He has released seventeen extended plays and one studio album, most notably his 2016 extended play ''[[Chemistry (Virtual Riot EP)|Chemistry]]'', which peaked at 20 on [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard's'']] [[Dance/Electronic Albums]] charts and his 2018 extended play ''[[German Engineering]]'', which peaked on the [[Dance/Electronic Digital Song Sales]] chart at the No. 11 position. He was signed to the independent music label Disciple Recordings in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/virtual-riot-mn0002992110/biography|title=Virtual Riot - Biography & History - AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=13 June 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170613201229/http://www.allmusic.com/artist/virtual-riot-mn0002992110/biography|archive-date=13 June 2017|df=dmy-all}}</ref> On 16 April 2019, he announced a publishing deal with [[Skrillex]]'s [[Owsla|OWSLA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BwVir4olej5/|title=Virtual Riot on Instagram: "Today I signed a publishing deal with @owsla . Thanks to @skrillex and my legendary managers for making this happen üíúüéâüçæ"|website=Instagram|access-date=17 April 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
Brunn has had numerous [[Beatport]] chart hits including "One For All, All For One" with Razihel and "Cali Born" with Helicopter Showdown.<ref name="autogenerated1">[https://web.archive.org/web/20150703150535/http://www.djz.com/news/exclusive-virtual-interview-with-virtual-riot/] </ref> Other electronic music outlets, such as Your EDM, have called his music "non-traditional" and "edgy", comparing him to artists like [[Savant (musician)|Savant]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.youredm.com/2013/10/30/edm-roadmap-electronic-dance-music-industry-explained/|title=An EDM Roadmap: The Electronic Dance Music Industry Explained|first=Brett|last=Edgerly|date=30 October 2013|website=Youredm.com|access-date=6 June 2017|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170325125736/http://www.youredm.com/2013/10/30/edm-roadmap-electronic-dance-music-industry-explained/|archive-date=25 March 2017|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Prior to producing under the alias "Virtual Riot", Brunn produced [[ambient music|ambient]] [[dubstep]] and [[future garage]] music under another alias, Your Personal Tranquilizer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://themusies.com/2016/08/exclusive-interview-with-virtual-riot-from-the-disciple-takeover-tour-in-philly/|title=Exclusive Interview with Virtual Riot from the Disciple Takeover Tour in Philly|date=30 August 2016|website=Themusies.com|access-date=6 June 2017|url-status=bot: unknown|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160830205357/http://themusies.com/2016/08/exclusive-interview-with-virtual-riot-from-the-disciple-takeover-tour-in-philly/|archive-date=30 August 2016|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Brunn has released several EPs under British record label Disciple Records, including his charted EP, ''Chemistry''.<ref name="Bassrush">{{Cite news|url=http://www.bassrush.com/virtual-riot-conjures-up-bad-chemistry/|title=Virtual Riot Conjures Up Bad Chemistry {{!}} Bassrush|date=2 May 2016|work=Bassrush|access-date=8 August 2017|language=en-US}}</ref> He produces in genres such as [[Riddim (EDM)|riddim]], [[future bass]], future garage, [[electro house]], and both bass-heavy and melodic [[dubstep]].

On August 9, 2021, Brunn announced his studio album titled ''Simulation'', which was released on September 10.<ref name=simulation />
