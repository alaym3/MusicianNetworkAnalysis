'''Thomas Grennan''' (born 8 June 1995) is an English singer-songwriter from [[Bedford]], who is based in London.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Tom Grennan {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/51252/tom-grennan/|access-date=18 May 2021|website=www.officialcharts.com}}</ref> Grennan found fame as the guest vocalist on [[Chase & Status]]'s "[[All Goes Wrong]]", which was featured as the Hottest Record on [[Annie Mac]]'s [[BBC Radio 1]] show, and later performed by Grennan on the BBC Radio 1 [[Live Lounge]] and on BBC Two's ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/271XmfJXh6QQZXnZ5HW64zW/tom-grennan|title=BBC Music – BBC Music Sound Of, 2017 – Tom Grennan|website=BBC|language=en-GB|access-date=28 May 2019}}</ref>

==Biography==
Grennan comes from an Irish family. He attended [[St Thomas More Catholic School, Bedford|St Thomas More Upper School]] in Bedford. At the age of 18, he was attacked on the street and was left with four metal plates and screws in his jaw that "still hurt when winter comes".<ref name="ox">{{Cite web|url=https://www.oxmag.co.uk/|title=Oxfordshire Magazine for What's on, Culture & Lifestyle in Oxford – OX Mag|website=Oxmag.co.uk}}</ref><ref name="guardian">{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2017/dec/28/tom-grennan-the-pop-wideboy-putting-dark-days-behind-him|title=Tom Grennan: the pop whiteboy putting dark days behind him|first=Hannah J.|last=Davies|date=28 December 2017|website=Theguardian.com}}</ref> He trained to become a professional [[association football|footballer]], playing for [[Luton Town]] for a while, also trying for [[Northampton Town F.C.|Northampton Town]] and [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]]; he was later released. Grennan is a lifelong supporter of [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.manutd.com/en/news/detail/my-united-interview-with-musician-tom-grennan|title=My United: Tom Grennan|date=22 June 2019|website=Manchester United F.C.|access-date=4 March 2021}}</ref> He also worked briefly at [[Costa Coffee]]. He told ''[[Music Week]]'': "I was close to playing over in the States but something was telling me not to and obviously it was the music".<ref name="bbcmusic">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/271XmfJXh6QQZXnZ5HW64zW/tom-grennan|title=BBC Music – BBC Music Sound Of, 2017 – Tom Grennan|website=BBC}}</ref><ref name="metro">{{Cite web|url=https://metro.co.uk/2017/11/12/all-eyes-on-tom-grennan-got-discovered-drunk-singing-at-a-party-now-hes-a-favourite-of-elton-john-7072276/|title=Tom Grennan got discovered doing drunk karaoke, now he's a favourite of Elton|first=Katie|last=Baillie|website=Metro.co.uk|date=12 November 2017}}</ref>

His musical beginnings are obscure, but he says he was at a house party where he sang "[[Inside In / Inside Out#Track listing|Seaside]]" by [[the Kooks]]. He did not remember it, but his friends were impressed and pushed him into performing more.<ref name="guardian" /><ref name="metro" /> He studied acting at [[St Mary's University, Twickenham|St. Mary's University]] in [[Twickenham]].<ref name="guardian" /> Aged 18, he started doing gigs around London with his acoustic guitar, mainly in small pub appearances for almost three years. After a performance at the Finsbury pub, a representative of Insanity Records heard him play and offered a contract.<ref name="metro" /> His debut EP, ''Something in the Water'' was produced by Charlie Hugall. He had his big break when he was featured in the [[Chase & Status]] 2016 single "[[All Goes Wrong]]" that was picked as "Hottest Record" on [[Annie Mac]]'s [[BBC Radio 1|Radio 1]] show. After that, he was invited for an appearance on the station's ''[[Live Lounge]]'', and a follow up appearance on [[BBC Two]]'s television programme ''[[Later... with Jools Holland]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thecalmzone.net/2017/09/tom-grennan-finds-hes-looking/|title=Tom Grennan finds what he's been looking for|date=21 September 2017|website=Campaign Against Living Miserably}}</ref> The song appeared on the UK Singles Chart, peaking at number 65.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/50273/chase-and-status-ft-tom-grennan/|title=CHASE & STATUS FT TOM GRENNAN {{!}} full Official Chart History|publisher=[[Official Charts Company]]}}</ref> It also made some European charts.{{citation needed|date=July 2021}}

In 2017, he was shortlisted for the [[MTV]] Brand New Award, singing at MTV Showcase at London's [[Electric Ballroom]] on 2 February 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://entertainment.toggle.sg/en/entertainment/hollywoodbuzz/article/tom-grennan-teased-about-singing-by-football-friends-7218310|title=Tom Grennan teased about singing by football – Toggle|date=27 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180527202921/https://entertainment.toggle.sg/en/entertainment/hollywoodbuzz/article/tom-grennan-teased-about-singing-by-football-friends-7218310|accessdate=11 February 2021|archive-date=27 May 2018}}</ref> The same year, he had a cameo appearance in [[Charli XCX]]'s music video for her song "[[Boys (Charli XCX song)|Boys]]", alongside many well-known artists and duetted with [[Grime (music genre)|grime]] MC [[Bugzy Malone]] on "Memory Lane".<ref name="guardian" /> Grennan performed in [[Trafalgar Square]] during the ''F1 Live in London'' show in support of [[Formula One]] drivers. He started an all UK tour in March 2018 in support of his debut album ''Lighting Matches'', released in July 2018. His song "Found What I've Been Looking For" appears on the ''[[FIFA 18]]'' soundtrack and is also used by [[Sky Sports]] as the theme song for ''[[Super Sunday (Sky Sports)|Super Sunday]]'' and RBI19 Baseball.{{citation needed|date=July 2021}}

In 2021, he had his first UK top 10 hit with "[[Little Bit of Love (Tom Grennan song)|Little Bit of Love]]", which peaked at number 7. It was awarded a Silver certification by the BPI, also charting around Europe. He subsequently achieved another top 10 hit with "[[Let's Go Home Together]]", a collaboration with [[Ella Henderson]].<ref name=":0" /> In the same year, he presented Radio 1's ''Future Sounds'' show between 30 August to 2 September, becoming one of the five musicians to do it for the month of August, alongside [[Olly Alexander]], [[Arlo Parks]], [[Yungblud]] and [[Charli XCX]].
