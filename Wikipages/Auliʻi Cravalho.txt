== Career ==
Cravalho has stated that she was initially not going to audition for ''[[Moana (2016 film)|Moana]]'' because "there were already so many great submissions over [[YouTube]]".<ref name="Rice">Rice, Lynette (October 7, 2015). [http://www.people.com/article/disney-princess-moana-aulii-cravalho "Meet the Next Disney Princess – and Get a First Look at Her Movie, Moana!"] ''[[People (magazine)|People]]''. New York: Time Inc. Retrieved on June 14, 2016.</ref> However, an [[Oahu]] talent agent discovered her at a charity competition and encouraged her to audition for the role.<ref name="Wang" /><ref name="Rice" /> [[The Walt Disney Company]] has stated that Cravalho was the last person to audition out of hundreds of actresses.<ref name="Wang" /><ref name="ledbetter">Ledbetter, Carly (October 8, 2015). [https://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/disney-princess-moana-aulii-cravalho_us_56166c96e4b0dbb8000d3a3d "Meet Your New Disney Princess 'Moana', Played By 14-Year-Old Auliʻi Cravalho"]. ''[[The Huffington Post]]''. Retrieved on June 14, 2016.</ref>

In February 2017, it was announced that she had been cast in the pilot for the [[NBC]] drama ''[[Rise (U.S. TV series)|Rise]]'', which was ordered to series on May 4, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2017/02/moana-star-aulii-cravalho-cast-drama-high-nbc-pilot-1202018615/|title=''Moana'' Star Auliʻi Cravalho Cast In NBC Pilot ''Drama High'' From Jason Katims & Jeffrey Seller|first=Nellie|last=Andreeva|website=Deadline Hollywood |date=February 27, 2017|access-date=March 21, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2017/05/rise-for-god-and-country-picked-upseries-nbc-1202084736/|title='Rise' & 'For God And Country' Picked Up To Series By NBC|last=Andreeva|first=Andreeva|website=Deadline Hollywood|date=May 4, 2017|access-date=May 13, 2017}}</ref> The series premiered on March 13, 2018, but NBC cancelled the series on May 15, 2018, due to low ratings.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/article/nbcs-theatre-focused-drama-rise-sets-march-premiere|title=NBC's Theatre-Focused Drama Rise Sets March Premiere {{!}} Playbill|website=Playbill|language=en|access-date=November 17, 2017}}</ref>

In November 2017, Cravalho announced she was going to reprise her role as Moana in the first [[Hawaiian language|Hawaiian-language]] dubbed Disney film.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BbAOujigpkd/|title=Auliʻi on Instagram: "Aloha nui kākou! Eia nō hoʻi au ʻo Auliʻi Cravalho ma Nuioka nei. A piha ʻeu ka hauʻoli i ka hana hou ʻia ʻana ʻo Moana... a i kēia…"|website=Instagram|language=en|access-date=May 19, 2018}}</ref> The dubbed ''Moana'' premiered on June 10, 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://koolina.com/press/disneys-moana-make-world-premiere-%CA%BBolelo-hawai%CA%BBi-ko-olinas-world-oceans-day-june-10/|title=Disney's Moana to make World Premiere in ʻŌlelo Hawaiʻi at Ko Olina's World Oceans Day, June 10 – Ko Olina|work=Ko Olina|access-date=May 19, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

On November 5, 2019, Cravalho portrayed [[Ariel (The Little Mermaid)|Ariel]] in [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]'s ''[[The Little Mermaid Live!]],'' a live-action concert rendition of ''[[The Little Mermaid (1989 film)|The Little Mermaid]]''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Levin |first1=Gary |title='The Little Mermaid': ABC resurfaces plans for a live musical version of the classic film with Queen Latifah |url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/tv/2019/08/05/the-little-mermaid-abc-swims-back-live-musical-version-film-aulil-cravalho/1921536001/ |website=USA Today |date=August 5, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Bentley |first1=Jean |title='Little Mermaid' Live Starring Auliʻi Cravalho Set at ABC |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/little-mermaid-live-starring-aulii-cravalho-set-at-abc-1229130 |website=The Hollywood Reporter |date=August 5, 2019}}</ref> In 2020, she starred in ''[[All Together Now (2020 film)|All Together Now]]'', directed by [[Brett Haley]] for [[Netflix]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2019/07/netflix-moana-disney-aulii-cravalho-sorta-like-rock-star-brett-haley-1202652414/|title='Moana' Star Auliʻi Cravalho To Lead Cast In Movie 'Sorta Like A Rock Star' For Netflix & Director Brett Haley|website=Deadline Hollywood|first=Andreas|last=Wiseman|date=July 24, 2019|access-date=October 16, 2019}}</ref>

In 2020, Cravalho participated in ''[[Acting for a Cause]]'', a live classic play and screenplay reading series created, directed and produced by Brando Crawford. Cravalho played Gwendolen Fairfax in ''[[The Importance of Being Earnest]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|last=Acting for a Cause|date=26 March 2020|title="The Importance of Being Earnest" Feat. Auli'i Cravalho, Justice Smith - Dir. Brando Crawford|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=amfRn2hb1fc|url-status=live|access-date=29 June 2021|website=YouTube}}</ref> [[Laertes (Hamlet)|Laertes]] in ''[[Hamlet]]'', [[Lady Catherine de Bourgh|Lady Catherine]] in ''[[Pride and Prejudice]]'', and Jeannie Bueller in ''[[Ferris Bueller's Day Off]]''. The reading raised funds for non-profit charities including [[Mount Sinai Medical Center]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=George|first=Doug|date=13 May 2020|title=Oak Parker stages Zoom plays as COVID-19 benefits, casting Florence Pugh and more young Hollywood actors|work=Chicago Tribune|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/coronavirus/ct-ent-hollywood-actors-play-readings-0509-20200508-uvu2aozegzepndqwrrl5tdljxq-story.html|access-date=29 June 2021}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
In April 2020, Cravalho publicly came out as [[bisexual]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/9355999/aulii-cravalho-comes-out-bisexual-tiktok/|title=Auliʻi Cravalho is Bisexual: ''Moana'' Star Comes Out On TikTok|last=Sommer|first=Ava|date=April 9, 2020|website=Billboard|language=en-US|access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref>
