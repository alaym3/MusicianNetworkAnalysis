'''Arkells''' are a Canadian [[rock music|rock]] band, formed in [[Hamilton, Ontario]]. In 2006, they signed with [[Dine Alone Records]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dinealonerecords.com/2013/index.php/artist/view/19|title=Arkells biography|work=DineAloneRecords.com|access-date=May 21, 2014}}</ref> and have since signed with Universal Records Canada<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.universalmusic.ca/press-releases/arkells-sign-with-universal-music-canada/|title=Arkells Sign With Universal Music Canada|work=UniversalMusic.ca|access-date=May 21, 2014}}</ref> and [[Last Gang Records]]. They are managed by Last Gang Management.  They have released six albums, two EPs and a number of singles that have charted in Canada. The band has won multiple [[Juno Award]]s, including one for their album ''High Noon'' in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/hamilton/news/arkells-win-twice-at-2015-junos-in-hamilton-1.2996306|title=Arkells win twice at 2015 Junos in Hamilton|access-date=August 10, 2016}}</ref>

== History ==
=== Formation (2006–2008) ===
During their first few live shows the band called themselves Charlemagne but changed their name when another band of the same name threatened to sue them. The band is named after Arkell Street in the [[Westdale, Hamilton|Westdale neighbourhood]] of Hamilton,<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://indie88.com/10-things-you-might-not-know-about-arkells/ | last=Albrecht | first=Brent | title=10 Things You Might Not Know About Arkells | website=Indie88.com | date=July 31, 2014 | access-date=October 23, 2017}}</ref> near [[McMaster University]], where they lived and would practice their music. All five original band members attended McMaster where lead singer Max Kerman graduated with an honours BA in Political Science. Kerman met guitarist Mike DeAngelis at a McMaster Welcome Week event where the two discovered they had an identical taste in music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dailynews.mcmaster.ca/article/arkells-proud-to-be-a-hamilton-band/ |title=Arkells 'proud to be a Hamilton band' &#124; McMaster Daily News |website=DailyNews.McMaster.ca |date=May 10, 2010 |access-date=August 2, 2016}}</ref>

=== ''Jackson Square'' (2008–2011) ===
Arkells' debut album ''[[Jackson Square (album)|Jackson Square]]'' was released 28 October 2008 on Dine Alone Records.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dinealonerecords.com/artists/arkells/|title=Arkells|website=Dinealonerecords.com|access-date=2020-04-01}}</ref> In late 2008, Arkells toured Canada as opening act for [[Matt Mays & El Torpedo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://petenema.com/article.php/matt_mays-08|title=Concert Review: Matt Mays & El Torpedo, Arkells|last=Nema|first=Pete|work=petenema.com|date=November 12, 2008|access-date=May 21, 2014}}</ref> Later that year, on November 22, they performed the halftime show at the [[Vanier Cup]] in their hometown.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vaniercup.ca/article/2008-desjardins-vanier-cup-packed-with-entertainment |title=2008 Desjardins Vanier Cup packed with entertainment |last=Rollo |first=Heather |work=vaniercup.ca |date=November 17, 2008 |access-date=May 21, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/5cZno8ap2?url=http://www.vaniercup.ca/article/2008-desjardins-vanier-cup-packed-with-entertainment |archive-date=November 24, 2008 }}</ref> In early 2009 they toured with [[Waking Eyes]] across Canada.<ref>Harper, Kate. "[http://www.chartattack.com/news/65056/arkells-waking-eyes-head-west Arkells and Waking Eyes Head West] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090129113856/http://www.chartattack.com/news/65056/arkells-waking-eyes-head-west |date=January 29, 2009 }}", ''Chart Attack'', 2009-01-19. Retrieved on 2009-01-31.</ref> Arkells were featured on [[Aux.tv]]'s Alt Sessions where they performed their full EP.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aux.tv/users/Arkells/ |title=Arkells' "Michigan Left" remixed by Bad Biz, featuring members of Tokyo Police Club |publisher=AUX.TV |date=2011-10-21 |access-date=2015-06-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101201054009/http://www.aux.tv/users/Arkells/ |archive-date=2010-12-01 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
[[File:The Arkells.jpg|thumb|right|Arkells performing at the [[Burlington Sound of Music]] Festival, June 2010]]
In October, 2009, Arkells won a favourite new artist [[CASBY Award]] (short for Canadian Artists Selected by You) from Toronto radio station [[CFNY-FM|102.1 The Edge]] at a ceremony and concert at [[Toronto]]'s [[Kool Haus]].<ref name="2009 CASBY Awards Winners Announced">[http://www.edge.ca/ConcertsandEvents/CasbysNews/Story.aspx?ID=1154467]  {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091102085227/http://www.edge.ca/ConcertsandEvents/CasbysNews/Story.aspx?ID=1154467 |date=November 2, 2009 }}</ref> The band also performed at the event.<ref name="2009 CASBY Awards Winners Announced"/>

In April 2010, Arkells won the [[Juno Awards of 2010|Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for New Group of the Year|New Group of the Year]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Juno Awards 2010 Press Release|date=April 18, 2010|url=http://junoawards.ca/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/2010GalaWinners.pdf|publisher=Junoawards.ca|access-date=2015-06-19}}</ref><ref>[https://www.insidebrockville.com/whatson-story/7109445-punchin-in-punchin-out-the-arkells-first-american-tour-the-good-the-bad-and-the-sweaty/ "Punchin' in Punchin' out | The Arkells’ first American tour: the good, the bad and the sweaty"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171024101500/https://www.insidebrockville.com/whatson-story/7109445-punchin-in-punchin-out-the-arkells-first-american-tour-the-good-the-bad-and-the-sweaty/ |date=2017-10-24 }}. ''Inside Brockville'', Feb 09, 2017 by Andrew Baulcomb Hamilton Spectator</ref> and on May 15, 2010, were picked to open for [[Them Crooked Vultures]] at the [[Air Canada Centre]] in [[Toronto]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.chartattack.com/news/2010/05/17/them-crooked-vultures-are-consistent-and-tiring/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=2017-10-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171024095606/http://www.chartattack.com/news/2010/05/17/them-crooked-vultures-are-consistent-and-tiring/ |archive-date=2017-10-24 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

They made headlines while playing a set at [[Horseshoe Tavern]] in Toronto after the 2010 [[Much Music Video Awards]], when rapper [[Shad (rapper)|Shad]] and pop singer [[Kesha]] joined them onstage to freestyle and sing along during a cover of [[Ms. Jackson]] by [[Outkast]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chartattack.com/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100625031634/http://www.chartattack.com/reviews/86569/arkells-cover-classics-with-keha-and-shad|url-status=dead|title=Chart Attack - Best Magazine 2020|archive-date=June 25, 2010|website=Chartattack.com}}</ref>

=== ''Michigan Left'' (2011–2014) ===
In 2011, the band wrote and recorded their second album ''[[Michigan Left (album)|Michigan Left]]'', which was released on October 18 of that year. The first single, "Whistleblower", was released to iTunes on July 5, 2011. "Whistleblower" has also been used as the Intro Music to the 2011–2012 [[Calgary Hitmen]] hockey team and featured in EA Sports' ''[[NHL 13]]'' video game.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/Arkells/posts/202134463247083|title=Arkells - Hockey fans and gamers, here is something for... |website=Facebook.com|access-date=2020-04-01}}</ref> The band also released a second song, "Kiss Cam", in July.

On 6 October 2011 it was announced that Dan Griffin would be leaving the band to go back to school.<ref name="A Band Update: Where's Dan">{{cite web|url=http://arkells.ca/2011/10/a-band-update-wheres-dan/ |access-date=October 19, 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120310182421/http://arkells.ca/2011/10/a-band-update-wheres-dan/|title=A Band Update: Where's Dan? |archive-date=March 10, 2012 }}</ref> He was replaced by Anthony Carone.<ref name="A Band Update: Where's Dan"/>

In 2012, Arkells won the [[Juno Awards of 2012|2012 Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for Group of the Year|Group of the Year]].<ref name=hamilton_juno>{{cite web|last=Rockingham|first=Graham|title=Hamilton's Arkells win big at Juno Awards gala|url=http://www.thespec.com/news/canada/article/697086--hamilton-s-arkells-win-big-at-juno-awards-gala|work=The Hamilton Spectator|access-date=3 April 2012}}</ref>
[[File:Arkells Live in Buffalo 2013.jpeg|thumb|Arkells performing live in Buffalo, New York, during the summer of 2013]]

The band has become known for performing spontaneous Motown cover sets, most recently at Canada House during the PyeongChang 2018 Olympics, and while stranded in St. John's Newfoundland.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_ca/article/rnwxvw/we-got-max-from-arkells-to-confront-the-criticisms-of-his-bands-biggest-fan|title=We Got Max from Arkells to Confront the Criticisms of His Band's Biggest Fan|first=Phil|last=Witmer|website=Vice.com|date=June 7, 2016|access-date=2020-04-01}}</ref> In September 2012, the band played Jian Ghomeshi's "1982" book launch in Toronto.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://beta.theglobeandmail.com/life/fashion-and-beauty/party-photos-of-the-week-jian-ghomeshis-book-launch/article4580565/|title=Party photos of the week: Jian Ghomeshi's book launch|date=October 1, 2012|website=TheGlobeAndMail.com|access-date=January 12, 2018}}</ref> The band has also started a tradition of sending fans signed postcards and free downloads at Christmas.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=10151172443866325&set=a.203199706324.131124.6940491324&type=1&theater |title=Arkells - Timeline Photos |publisher=Facebook.com |date=December 10, 2012 |access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref>

=== ''High Noon'' (2014–2016) ===
On April 7, 2014, Arkells released the first track, "Never Thought That This Would Happen", from their third album, which was produced by Tony Hoffer (Beck, The Kooks, M83). One month later, on May 13, the band released the album's first single "Come to Light", and confirmed the title of the record would be ''[[High Noon (Arkells album)|High Noon]]''. The record was released on Dine Alone/Universal Records, August 5, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|last=Hudson |first=Alex |url=http://exclaim.ca/News/arkells_unveil_high_noon_lp_premiere_new_lyric_video |title=Arkells Unveil 'High Noon' LP, Premiere New Lyric Video |publisher=Exclaim.ca |date=May 13, 2014 |access-date=June 19, 2015}}</ref>

Arkells have toured extensively, including tours in the United States with [[Lights (musician)|Lights]], [[Tokyo Police Club]], [[The Postelles]], [[X Ambassadors]], and [[Lydia (band)|Lydia]]; European touring with [[Billy Talent]], [[Augustines (band)|Augustines]], [[British Sea Power]], and [[Anti-Flag]] as well as Canadian touring with [[Metric (band)|Metric]], [[the Tragically Hip]], [[Hollerado]], and [[Sam Roberts]].

At the [[Juno Awards of 2015]], Arkells won the [[Juno Award for Group of the Year]] and the [[Juno Award for Rock Album of the Year]]. ''High Noon'' was also long listed for the Polaris prize in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|last=Brophy |first=Aaron |url=http://polarismusicprize.ca/blog/2015-polaris-music-prize-long-list/ |title=2015 Polaris Music Prize Long List Is Here - Polaris Music Prize |website=PolarisMusicPrize.ca |date=June 16, 2015 |access-date=August 2, 2016}}</ref>

On October 23, 2015, the band released a new EP, "Study Music", which they self-produced and released on [[Universal Music Canada]]/[[Dine Alone Records]].

=== ''Morning Report'' (2016–2018) ===
Arkells began teasing new material from their forthcoming album on tour in the spring of 2016. The fourth album, ''Morning Report'', was released on August 5, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://exclaim.ca/music/article/arkells-morning_report|title=Arkells Morning Report|last=Slingerland|first=Calum|date=August 3, 2016|website=Exclaim.ca|access-date=August 10, 2016}}</ref> The first single, "Private School", debuted on May 6, 2016, and charted at number 1 that summer.{{citation needed|date=March 2020}} The video features cameos from [[Lights (musician)|Lights]], Dave Monks of [[Tokyo Police Club]] and [[Steve Jocz]] (formerly of [[Sum 41]]), who also directed the video.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thezone.fm/now/how-to-get-enrolled-in-the-arkells-private-school/|title=How to Get Enrolled in The Arkells Private School | first=Jenny|last=West|website=Thezone.fm|date=29 May 2016|access-date=29 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160610114142/http://www.thezone.fm/now/how-to-get-enrolled-in-the-arkells-private-school/|archive-date=10 June 2016}}</ref>

The Arkells song "Round and Round" was featured in the soundtrack of the EA Sports game ''[[NHL 17]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.easports.com/nhl/news/2016/nhl-17-soundtrack|title=NHL 17 Soundtrack|date=July 27, 2016|website=Easports.com|access-date=January 12, 2018}}</ref>

At the [[Juno Awards of 2017]], Arkells were nominated for [[Juno Award for Group of the Year]] and the [[Juno Award for Rock Album of the Year]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://junoawards.ca/nominees/printer-friendly/?nomination-year=2017/|title=Nominees|publisher=[[Juno Awards]]|last=Woods|first=Graeme|access-date=April 2, 2017}}</ref> They performed their viral hit "Drake's Dad" live on the television broadcast.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.umusic.ca/2017/04/03/arkells-drakes-dad-2017-junos/|title=Arkells Brought Out Drake's Dad At The 2017 JUNOs|publisher=[[Universal Music]] Canada|date=3 April 2017|access-date=29 March 2020}}</ref>

Arkells released a new single on April 7, 2017, entitled "Knocking at the Door".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.much.com/arkells-knocking-at-the-door/|title=Arkells Hit Hard With Soaring Single 'Knocking At The Door'|first=Allison|last=Bowsher|publisher=[[Much (TV channel)]]|date=7 April 2017|access-date=29 March 2020}}</ref> The track shot to number 1 on the Canadian alt/rock charts, where it spent 14 weeks at #1.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/hamilton/arkells-knocking-at-the-door-1.4522931 |title=Knocking at the Door: How Arkells wrote a bona fide sports anthem |last=Carter |first=Adam |publisher=[[CBC.ca]]|date=February 6, 2018 |access-date=2018-09-08}}</ref> It also became their first #1 Active Rock single. They performed the track live that summer on the 2017 NHL Awards in Las Vegas and the 2017 iHeartRadio MMVAs in Toronto. In the summer of 2017, the band toured the festival circuit, playing [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.coachella.com/lineup/|title=Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|website=Coachella.com|access-date=2020-04-01|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170320040746/https://www.coachella.com/lineup/|archive-date=2017-03-20|url-status=dead}}</ref> [[Osheaga Festival]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.osheaga.com/en/artists|title=Artists |website=Osheaga.com|access-date=2020-04-01}}</ref> and [[Sasquatch! Music Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/01/sasquatch-reveals-2017-lineup-frank-ocean-chance-the-rapper-to-headline/|title=Sasquatch! reveals 2017 lineup: Frank Ocean, Chance the Rapper to headline|date=January 23, 2017}}</ref> Later in the year, the track cracked the US ALT chart, peaking at #39.{{Citation needed|date=April 2018}}

In February 2018, the band was invited to [[South Korea]] to perform for the Canadian Olympic team during the [[2018 Winter Olympics]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globalnews.ca/news/4046347/how-the-arkells-ended-up-performing-at-canada-house-during-the-olympics/|title=How the Arkells ended up performing at Canada House during the Olympics|first=Sean|last=Previl|publisher=[[Global News]]|date=25 February 2018|access-date=29 March 2020}}</ref> In June 2018, the band played at [[Tim Hortons Field]] in their hometown of Hamilton for a crowd of 24,000 people. It was the largest crowd for an outdoor show in Hamilton since [[Pink Floyd]] drew 50,000 to [[Ivor Wynne Stadium]] in 1975.<ref name="Ar">{{Cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/hamilton/hamilton-arkells-rally-1.4717991 |title=Arkells 'rally' set to take over Hamilton Saturday |last=Clementson |first=Laura|website=Cbc.ca |date= June 22, 2018 |access-date=2018-09-08}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/hamilton/hamilton-arkells-rally-1.4720090 |title=Hamilton rallies around hometown rock band the Arkells |last=Taekema |first=Dan|website=Cbc.ca |date=June 24, 2018 |access-date=2018-09-08}}</ref>

On August 15, 2018, the band announced that their fifth studio album, ''Rally Cry'', would be released on October 19, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globalnews.ca/news/4388660/arkells-release-new-videos-featuring-hamilton-rally-footage/|title=Arkells release new videos featuring Hamilton 'Rally' footage|publisher=[[Global News]]|date=15 August 2018|access-date=29 March 2020}}</ref> On September 20, 2018, the band performed at WE Day in Toronto.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dailyhive.com/toronto/toronto-we-day-lineup-2018|title=Everyone confirmed to attend WE Day Toronto this month|first=Ainsley|last=Smith|publisher=[[Daily Hive]]|date=13 September 2018|access-date=29 March 2020}}</ref>

=== ''Rally Cry'' (2018–2021) ===
The Arkells released their fifth studio album, ''[[Rally Cry (album)|Rally Cry]]'', on October 19, 2018, accompanied by a Canadian tour featuring [[Lord Huron]] as the opening act. The tour featured the Arkells' largest arena show at the [[Scotiabank Arena]] in Toronto on February 16, 2019.

On February 25, 2020 the band released the new single and video "Years in the Making".<ref>Sarah Murphy, [https://exclaim.ca/music/article/arkells_unveil_new_single_and_video_years_in_the_making "Arkells Unveil New Single and Video 'Years in the Making'". ''[[Exclaim!]]'', February 25, 2020.</ref>

On July 30, they released the single "Quitting You", and announced the release of ''Campfire Chords'', a full-length album featuring acoustic versions of earlier songs, on August 20.<ref>Brock Thiessen, [https://exclaim.ca/music/article/arkells_strip_down_for_new_album_campfire_chords "Arkells Strip Down for New Album 'Campfire Chords'"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', July 30, 2020.</ref> Each member recorded his part at home during the [[COVID-19]] quarantine and sent it to Carone, who then edited, mixed and produced the pieces into complete songs.

=== ''Blink Once'' (2021–present) ===
On March 25, 2021 the band released the new single and video "You Can Get It", featuring guest vocals by [[K.Flay]]<ref> [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gt3v7x5p4Fc&ab_channel=ArkellsMusic]</ref> The single “All Roads” was later released. Both of these singles were included on the band’s sixth studio album ''Blink Once'', which was released on September 22, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/arkellsmusic/status/1440530040739880961|url=https://twitter.com/arkellsmusic/status/1440530040739880961|access-date=2021-09-22|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref>

==Song influences==
Arkells prominently feature political motivations in their music. Examples include "Knocking at the Door," which was written about The Women's March on Washington;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/11759212/Arkells-knocking-at-the-door/Cant-walk-on-water-but-im-walking-through-an-intersection|title=Can't walk on water / But I'm walking through an intersection|website=Genius.com|access-date=2020-04-01}}</ref> "The Ballad of Hugo Chavez" about the Venezuelan President as a political prisoner, "People's Champ" in protest of US President Donald Trump; and "Whistleblower" about journalists blowing the whistle and exposing truth behind closed doors.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://extremenonchalance.com/post/40022967539/arkellsinterview|title=An Interview with Max Kerman of Arkells|website=Extremenonchalancebase|access-date=2020-04-01}}</ref>
Another example would be “No Champagne Socialist” about a student in New York of the 60s struggling with himself about trying to represent the working class as a member of the upper class.<ref>{{Cite web|title=No Champagne Socialist|url=https://genius.com/Arkells-no-champagne-socialist-lyrics|url-status=live|website=Genius}}</ref>

The band has also spoken about their diverse musical influences from Top 40 to Motown.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://indie88.com/10-things-you-might-not-know-about-arkells/|title=10 Things You Might Not Know About Arkells |website=Indie88.com|access-date=2020-04-01}}</ref>
