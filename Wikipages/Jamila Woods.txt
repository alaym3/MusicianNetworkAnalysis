'''Jamila Woods''' (born October 6, 1989) is a [[Chicago]]-based American singer, songwriter and poet. Woods is a graduate of [[St. Ignatius College Prep]] and [[Brown University]], where she received a BA in [[Africana Studies]] and Theater & Performance Studies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://national.suntimes.com/national-entertainment/7/72/2481954/jamila-woods-blk-girl-solider/|title=Jamila Woods releases powerful single, 'blk girl soldier'|date=24 January 2016|work=suntimes.com|accessdate=25 April 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170706174018/http://national.suntimes.com/national-entertainment/7/72/2481954/jamila-woods-blk-girl-solider/|archive-date=6 July 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/meet-hollis-wong-wear-and-jamila-woods-the-women-of-color-behind-macklemores-white-privilege-ii_us_56a63372e4b076aadcc728b9|title=Meet Hollis Wong-Wear And Jamila Woods, The Women Of Color Behind Macklemore's 'White Privilege II'|date=25 January 2016|publisher=|accessdate=25 April 2017|via=Huff Post}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://people2015.chicagoreader.com/who/jamila-woods/profile/|title=Jamila Woods|first=Chicago|last=Reader|date=10 December 2015|work=chicagoreader.com|accessdate=25 April 2017}}</ref> Her work focuses on themes of Black ancestry, [[Black feminism]], and Black identity, with recurring emphases on self-love and the City of Chicago.<ref name="Jamila Woods Writes"/>

== Career ==
=== Poetry ===
In 2012, Jamila Woods published her first chapbook, entitled ''The Truth About Dolls''. Her work can be found in the anthologies ''The Breakbeat Poets: New American Poetry in the Age of Hip-Hop'' (2015), ''Courage: Daring Poems for Gutsy Girls'' (2014), and ''The UnCommon Core: Contemporary Poems for Learning & Living'' (2013). Her influences include Lucille Clifton, Gwendolyn Brooks, Toni Morrison, and Frida Kahlo.<ref name="Jamila Woods Writes"/>

Woods was also one of three editors of ''The Breakbeat Poets Volume II'', entitled ''Black Girl Magic''. The 2018 publication is an [[anthology]] of poetry by contemporary Black women, “exploring themes of beauty, unapologetic blackness, [[intersectionality]], self-definition, and more.”<ref name="Black Girl Magic">{{cite web |last1=Lewis |first1=Eva |title="Black Girl Magic" Editor Jamila Woods and Poet Safia Elhillo on Putting a Spotlight on Black Women |url=https://www.teenvogue.com/story/black-girl-magic-jamila-woods-safia-elhillo |website=Teen Vogue |date=3 May 2018 |publisher=Teen Vogue |accessdate=14 December 2018}}</ref> Woods is a member of the [[Dark Noise Poetry Collective]] with fellow creatives [[Fatimah Asghar]], [[Franny Choi]], [[Nate Marshall]], [[Aaron Samuels]], and [[Danez Smith]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dark Noise: Fatimah Asghar, Franny Choi, Nate Marshall, Aaron Samuels, Danez Smith & Jamila Woods|url=https://www.poetryfoundation.org/events/74309/dark-noise-fatimah-asghar-franny-choi-nate-marshall-aaron-samuels-danez-smith-jamila-woods|last=Foundation|first=Poetry|date=2020-05-09|website=Poetry Foundation|language=en|access-date=2020-05-10}}</ref>

=== Community organizing ===
Woods is the Associate Artistic Director of [http://youngchicagoauthors.org Young Chicago Authors (YCA)], an organization in the Chicago dedicated to uplifting youth voices through arts education and mentorship. Through YCA, Woods helps to organize [[Louder Than a Bomb]], the world's largest youth poetry slam festival. She also facilitates poetry workshops and creates curriculum for Chicago Public Schools.<ref name="Jamila Woods Writes">{{cite web |last1=Woods |first1=Jamila |title=Home |url=https://jamilawoodswrites.com/ |website=Jamila Woods Writes |accessdate=14 December 2018}}</ref> While in Providence, Woods served as a volunteer at non-profit arts center [[New Urban Arts]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newurbanarts.org/2011/05/20/the-2011-art-party/|title=The 2011 Art Party|date=2011-05-20|website=New Urban Arts|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-04}}</ref>

=== Music ===
After completing studies at Brown, Woods and classmate Owen Hill formed soul-pop band Milo and Otis, or M&O, which released two albums: ''The Joy'' in 2012 and ''Almost Us'' in 2014.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Interview: Jamila Woods is one of Chicago's most multifaceted musicians|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/entertainment/music/redeye-jamila-woods-interview-heavn-chicago-20160619-story.html|last=Terry|first=Josh|website=chicagotribune.com|access-date=2020-05-10}}</ref> The song "Lift Up" on the band's debut album features Chance the Rapper. M&O disbanded in 2014. Woods is widely known for her other collaborative works with [[Chance the Rapper]] on the hit song "Sunday Candy" from the album ''[[Surf (Donnie Trumpet & The Social Experiment album)|Surf]]'' as well as "Blessings" from ''[[Coloring Book (mixtape)|Coloring Book]]''. Woods is also featured on the [[Macklemore & Ryan Lewis]] song "[[White Privilege II]]".<ref name=wickman>{{cite news|last1=Forrest|first1=Wickman|title=Macklemore's "White Privilege II" Isn't a Great Song, But as a Think Piece It's Not Terrible|url=http://www.slate.com/blogs/browbeat/2016/01/22/macklemore_s_white_privilege_ii_isn_t_a_great_song_but_as_a_lyrical_think.html|accessdate=23 January 2016|agency=[[Slate (magazine)|Slate]]|date=January 22, 2016}}</ref> In January 2016, Woods signed to Chicago's independent hip-hop label, Closed Sessions.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Jamila Woods Signs Label Deal With Chicago's Closed Sessions, Releases First Single (Exclusive) {{!}} Billboard|url = http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/6844165/jamila-woods-signs-closed-sessions-new-song|website = Billboard|access-date = 2016-02-22}}</ref> In that same year Woods released her debut solo album titled, “HEAVN”. Following that album in May 2019, she released the album, “LEGACY! LEGACY!” Each song is named after a prolific black creator.

In early 2020, Woods performed as the opening act for the R&B singer [[Raphael Saadiq]]'s [[Jimmy Lee (album)|Jimmy Lee Tour]].<ref name="Trageser">{{cite web|last=Trageser|first=Stephen|year=2020|url=https://local.nashvillescene.com/event/marathon-music-works/raphael-saadiq-wjamila-woods-and-dj-duggz|title=Raphael Saadiq w/Jamila Woods & DJ Duggz|website=[[Nashville Scene]]|accessdate=September 14, 2020}}</ref>

=== Heavn ===
Woods released her debut album ''Heavn'' on her SoundCloud page on July 11, 2016 to critical acclaim. The album features collaborations with [[Chance the Rapper]], [[Noname (rapper)|Noname]], [[Saba (rapper)|Saba]], Lorine Chia, Kweku Collins and [[Donnie Trumpet]].  ''Heavn'' was ranked as the 36th best album of 2016 by ''[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]''.  ''Heavn'' features a variety of producers, including oddCouple, a fellow Closed Sessions signee who produced five of the album's 12 tracks. In 2017, Woods partners with Jagjaguwar and Closed Sessions to re-release the album.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jagjaguwar.com/releases/|title=Jagjaguwar {{!}} JAG312|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-17}}</ref>

=== Legacy! Legacy! ===
Jamila released her second album ''Legacy! Legacy!'' via [[Jagjaguwar]] on May 10, 2019 to rave reviews.   The album features collaborations with [[Nitty Scott]], [[Saba (rapper)|Saba]], theMIND, Jasminfire, and [[Nico Segal]]. ''Legacy! Legacy!''   includes hook song "Eartha" that assists in displaying the history and lineage of a country obsessed with forgetting.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jagjaguwar.com/releases/|title=Jagjaguwar {{!}} JAG342|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-17}}</ref> Released along with "Legacy! Legacy!" were videos for her songs "SONIA/FRIDA".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/song-by-song/jamila-woods-legacy-legacy-interview/|title=Jamila Woods Breaks Down Every Song on Her New Album, LEGACY! LEGACY!}}</ref>
