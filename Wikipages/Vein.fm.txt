'''Vein.fm''' (formerly known as '''Vein''') is an American [[hardcore punk]] band from [[Boston, Massachusetts]] that formed in 2013. They are known for their frequent tours and have performed shows with groups such as [[Code Orange (band)|Code Orange]]<ref name="K!">{{cite web |last1=Fixell |first1=Ethan |title=Vein: The Most Explosive Live Act In Hardcore Today |url=http://www.kerrang.com/features/vein-the-most-explosive-live-act-in-hardcore-today/ |website=kerrange.com |publisher=Kerrang! |access-date=July 10, 2018}}</ref> and [[Twitching Tongues]] while in Europe.<ref name="forbes">{{cite web |last1=Scancarelli |first1=Derek |title=Vein Has One Objective: Extreme Sonic Assault |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/derekscancarelli/2018/06/22/vein-has-one-objective-extreme-sonic-assault/#1ab9489c237f |work=Forbes |access-date=July 10, 2018}}</ref> In 2017 the band was signed onto Closed Casket Activities, who would issue their first full-length work ''[[Errorzone]]'' to warm reception among critics, even appearing on ''[[Revolver (magazine)|Revolver]]''s early "Best Albums of 2018" list.<ref name="rev">{{cite web |last1=various |title=25 Best Albums of 2018 So Far |url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/25-best-albums-2018-so-far#yob-—-our-raw-heart |website=www.revolvermag.com |publisher=Revolver |access-date=July 10, 2018}}</ref> The album also managed to peak at number 21 on [[Billboard charts|Billboard]]'s Hard Rock album chart.<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/music/vein/chart-history/hard-rock-albums Vein chart history on Billboard website]</ref> In July 2020, after the release of their remix album, the band announced it changed its name to Vein.fm.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kennelty |first1=Greg |title=Vein Drops Remix Album, Band Changes Name to Vein.fm |url=https://metalinjection.net/news/vein-drops-remix-album-band-changes-name-to-vein-fm |website=Metal Injection |access-date=July 29, 2020 |date=July 29, 2020}}</ref>

== History ==

=== Formation and ''Demo/Terror's Realm'' (2013–2015) ===
In 2010, vocalist/bassist Anthony Didio and drummer Matt Wood were introduced by their sisters at a show in [[Haverhill, Massachusetts]].<ref name="Exclaim!">{{cite web |last1=Atkinson|first1=Connor|title=Vein Are About to Release Debut 'Errorzone,' But First Want to Settle Scores with .Gif From God|url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/vein_are_about_to_release_debut_errorzone_but_first_want_to_settle_scores_with_gif_from_god|website=[[Exclaim!]]|date=June 21, 2018|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> The band formed in 2013 in the Merrimack Valley region of Massachusetts by guitarist Josh Butts, guitarist Jeremy Martin, vocalist/bassist Anthony DiDio, and drummer Matt Wood.<ref name="Exclaim!" /><ref name="allmusic bio">{{cite web |last1=Yeung|first1=Neil Z. |title=Vein AllMusic Bio|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/vein-mn0003756418|website=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> The band released a self-titled demo in 2013.<ref name="allmusic bio" />

The band played many local shows in the greater Boston area and started to gain traction.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} The band then self-released their debut EP ''Terrors Realm'' on June 4, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Vein – Terrors Realm (EP)|url=http://www.metalstorm.net/bands/album.php?album_id=94802&band_id=&bandname=Vein|website=Metal Storm|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref>

=== ''Self-Destruct'' and breakout success (2015–2017) ===
In 2016, the band supposedly self-released a physical only {{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} demo to send to record labels, this demo is currently unobtainable online. On January 6, 2017, they released a split with Virginia metalcore/screamo band .Gif from God.<ref name="allmusic bio" />

Later that year, Vein signed to Closed Casket Activities{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} and re-released their songs from the split as an EP titled ''Self-Destruct'' on August 15, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Vein – Self-Destruct (EP)|url=http://www.metalstorm.net/bands/album.php?album_id=94801&band_id=10177&bandname=Vein|website=Metal Storm|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> That same year, the band played shows with [[Jesus Piece (band)|Jesus Piece]], [[Code Orange (band)|Code Orange]], and more.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} This included an appearance at Sound & Fury Fest as well as a breakout appearance at the 2017 edition of the This Is Hardcore fest in Philadelphia.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}}

This was followed by a series of successful tours including a spot on the popular reoccurring Life & Death tour alongside [[No Warning (band)|No Warning]], [[Backtrack (band)|Backtrack]], [[Twitching Tongues]], and [[Higher Power (band)|Higher Power]].<ref>{{cite web|title=No Warning, Backtrack, Twitching Tongues, Higher Power & Vein Tour Announced|url=https://www.theprp.com/2017/07/11/news/no-warning-backtrack-twitching-tongues-higher-power-vein-tour-announced/|website=The PRP|date=July 11, 2017|access-date=June 25, 2021}}</ref>

In early 2018, the band were given a supporting slot some high profile tours, including a US tour with [[Harm's Way (band)|Harms Way]] alongside [[Ringworm (band)|Ringworm]] and Queensway as well as another US tour supporting Code Orange alongside Twitching Tongues.<ref name="prp">{{cite web|title=Vein To Release New Album "Errorzone" In June, Debut "Virus://Vibrance" Music Video|url=https://www.theprp.com/2018/05/08/news/vein-to-release-new-album-errorzone-in-june-debut-virus-vibrance-music-video/|website=The PRP|date=May 8, 2018|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref><ref name="Revolver mag">{{cite web|first=Zoe|last=Camp|title=Hear Rising Spazcore Band Vein's Crushing New Song "Demise Automation"|url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/hear-rising-spazcore-band-veins-crushing-new-song-demise-automation|website=Revolver Magazine|date=May 24, 2018|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> This tour had support on select dates from [[Wicca Phase Springs Eternal]], [[Ghostemane]], [[Disembodied (band)|Disembodied]], [[Show Me the Body]], [[Nicole Dollanganger]], and Trail of Lies.<ref>{{cite web|first=Sara|last=Nuta|title=Code Orange Announce Headline Tour With Twitching Tongues And Vein|url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/code-orange-announce-headline-tour-with-twitching-tongues-and-vein/|website=[[Kerrang!]]|date=April 26, 2018|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref>

In May 2018, the band began teasing their debut studio album. On May 8, 2018, the band released the single, and accompanied music video for "Virus://Vibrance" and announced their debut album Errorzone would be out later that year via Closed Casket Activities.<ref name="prp" /> On May 24, 2018, the band released the single "Demise Automation."<ref name="Revolver mag" /> The album's final single, "Doomtech" was released on June 5, 2018.<ref name="stereogum">{{cite web|first=Sara|last=Nuta|title=Vein – "Doomtech"|url=https://www.stereogum.com/1998512/vein-doomtech/premiere/|website=Stereogum|date=June 5, 2018|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> The band's debut album ''[[Errorzone]]'' was released on June 22, 2018, via Closed Casket Activities.<ref name="allmusic bio" /><ref name="prp" /> The album was produced by Will Putney.<ref name="Revolver mag" /> The album was met with much praise from fans and critics alike.<ref>{{cite web |last1=various |title=25 Best Albums of 2018 So Far |url=https://www.revolvermag.com/music/25-best-albums-2018-so-far#yob-—-our-raw-heart |website=www.revolvermag.com |publisher=Revolver |accessdate=June 24, 2021}}</ref> This was followed by the band announcing and embarking on their first full US headlining tour with support from Sanction, Fuming Mouth, and Judiciary.<ref>{{cite web|title=Vein Announce Tour Dates with Fuming Mouth, Sanction and Judiciary|url=https://www.metalsucks.net/2018/08/03/vein-announce-tour-dates-with-fuming-mouth-sanction-and-judiciary/|website=Stereogum|date=August 3, 2018|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref>

On November 9, 2018, the band premiered the music video for "Demise Automation" and supported [[Every Time I Die]]'s 20th Anniversary tour alongside [[Turnstile (band)|Turnstile]] and [[Angel Dust (American band)|Angel Dust]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Robert|last=Ham|title=Every Time I Die to headline fall 2018 US tour with support from Turnstile, Angel Du$t, and Vein|url=https://consequence.net/2018/08/every-time-i-die-fall-2018-u-s-tour-with-turnstile-angel-dust-vein/|website=[[Consequence (publication)|Consequence]]|date=August 14, 2018|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> In 2019, due to the heavy use of experimental/electronic sounds on Errorzone, the band recruited disc jockey Benno Levine to join the band.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} In January 2019, the band embarked on their first UK headlining tour with support from Higher Power and [[Narrow Head]].{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} This was followed by the band's first-ever appearance in Japan supporting [[Crossfaith]] with [[Injury Reserve]] and Jin Dogg.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} The band's last headlining tour on the Errorzone touring cycle was in the US in Fall 2019 with support from Soft Kill and Higher Power with appearances on select dates from Modern Color, Dead Heat, Silenus, and Narrow Head.<ref>{{cite web|title=Vein Announce Fall North American Tour|url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/vein-announce-fall-north-american-tour/|website=[[Kerrang!]]|date=July 16, 2019|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref>

=== Remix album and name change (2020–present) ===
In January 2020, the band supported While She Sleeps and Every Time I Die on a UK/EU tour.<ref>{{cite web|title=While She Sleeps Have Announced A Tour With Every Time I Die And Vein|url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/while-she-sleeps-have-announced-a-tour-with-every-time-i-die-and-vein/|website=[[Kerrang!]]|date=July 15, 2019|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> On July 28, 2020, the band released the single and music video titled "20 seconds : 20 hours" this was a part of the surprise release of the remix album Old Data in a New Machine Vol. 1 the same day.<ref>{{cite web|first=Rob|last=Cella|title=Vein.fm Drop Remix Record Old Data in a New Machine Vol. 1|url=http://dreadmusicreview.com/2020/07/28/vein-fm-drop-remix-record-old-data-in-a-new-machine-vol-1/|website=Dread Music Review|date=July 28, 2020|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> The remix album included the new single, remixes, and demos of tracks off Errorzone, and remixed/remastered versions of three tracks from the band's debut EP Terrors Realm.<ref name="Breihan">{{cite web|first=Tom|last=Breihan|title=Vein Change Their Name To Vein.FM, Share Remix Album Old Data In A New Machine Vol. 1|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2093003/vein-change-their-name-to-vein-fm-share-remix-album-old-data-in-a-new-machine-vol-1/music/|website=Stereogum|date=July 28, 2020|access-date=June 24, 2021}}</ref> The release of the remix album was accompanied by the announcement that the band had changed their name.<ref name="Breihan" />

In November 2021, the band announced they would be embarking on a Spring 2022 US tour supporting [[Touché Amoré|Touche Amore]] alongside [[Dogleg (band)|Dogleg]] with Foxtails and Thirdface on select dates.

== Musical style ==
Vein have been categorised as [[hardcore punk]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=O'Connor |first1=Andy |title=Vein Errorzone |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/vein-errorzone/ |website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]] |access-date=July 28, 2018}}</ref><ref name="PM">{{cite web |last1=Brown |first1=Dean |title=Are Vein Hardcore's Big Next Thing? |url=https://www.popmatters.com/vein-errorzone-review-2579506292.html |website=[[PopMatters]] |date=June 22, 2018 |access-date=November 29, 2019}}</ref> [[nu metal]],<ref name="Engelhardt" /> and [[nu metalcore]].<ref name="WaPo">{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/music/as-the-world-shrivels-in-front-of-our-eyes-all-we-can-do-is-scream/2019/02/13/4bc180be-2be3-11e9-b011-d8500644dc98_story.html|title=As the world shrivels in front of our eyes, all we can do is scream|last=Chu|first=Hau|date=February 15, 2019|website=The Washington Post|access-date=June 25, 2021}}</ref> They take influence from styles such as [[screamo]], mathcore, [[drum and bass]], nu metal, and horror soundtracks to films and games.<ref name="allmusic bio" /><ref name="PM" /> with ''Errorzone'' especially noted for its [[grindcore]], [[Industrial music|industrial]], and nu metal influenced sound.<ref name="PM" /><ref name="6 Underground" /><ref name="p4k">{{cite web |last1=O'Conner |first1=Andy |title=Vein: Errorzone Album Review |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/vein-errorzone/ |website=pitchfork.com |publisher=Pitchfork Media |access-date=July 10, 2018}}</ref> Along with contemporaries [[Portrayal of Guilt]] and [[Infant Island]], [[The Washington Post]] described them as delivering "new life" to [[screamo]].<ref name="WaPo" />

== Side projects ==
{{sources|section|date=November 2021}}
In 2020, Anthony, Matt, and Anthony's girlfriend Marisa formed an alternative rock band called Fleshwater.<ref>{{cite web|last=Breihan|first=Tom Breihan|title=Stream Vein Side Project Fleshwater's New Demo|url=https://www.stereogum.com/2073761/stream-vein-side-project-fleshwaters-new-demo/music/|website=Stereogum|date=February 17, 2020|access-date=June 25, 2021}}</ref> The band released their demo on February 21, 2020. The demo was reissued later that year on cassette with a bonus track that was a cover of "Enjoy" by [[Björk|Bjork]].

As of 2020, Benno makes solo electronic/trip hop music under the name Venom Benzo. He self-released his debut EP ''Enclose and Illuminate'' on September 30, 2020. He released the single "Formative Rust" on December 20, 2020. On April 16, 2021, he self-released his second EP ''Fall to the Flow: Unredacted''.

In 2021, Jeremy, Jon, Nick Hermann (drummer of Vomit Forth and Separated), and Justin Legere (guitarist of Laid 2 Rest) formed a mathcore band called Living Weapon. They released an EP titled ''Paradise'' which later was released on both CD and LP through Closed Casket Activities.
