'''Spanish Love Songs''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]] that was founded in 2013.<ref name="popmatters genres"/><ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web|author=Rich Wilson|title=Spanish Love Songs AllMusic Bio|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/spanish-love-songs-mn0003490739/biography|work=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=October 19, 2020}}</ref> The band consists of lead vocalist and guitarist Dylan Slocum, guitarist Kyle McAulay, drummer Ruben Duarte, bassist Trevor Dietrich, and keyboardist Meredith Van Woert. Since their formation, Spanish Love Songs has released three studio albums: ''Giant Sings the Blues'' (2015), ''Schmaltz'' (2018), and ''[[Brave Faces Everyone]]'' (2020).

==History==

Spanish Love Songs was founded in 2013 in [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref name="AllMusic"/> They started as a trio with Dylan Slocum (vocals, guitar), Gabe Mayeshiro (bass), and Ruben Duarte (drums).<ref name="AllMusic"/> Guitarist Kyle McAulay joined shortly after, making them a four-piece.<ref name="AllMusic"/> The band played their first show in August 2013.<ref name="AllMusic"/> They began recording their debut EP which later became their debut studio album.<ref name="AllMusic"/> The band released their debut studio album in March 2015 titled ''Giant Sings the Blues''.<ref name="AllMusic"/><ref name="newnoisemagazine">{{cite web |title=Album Review: Spanish Love Songs – ‘Giant Sings The Blues’ |url=https://newnoisemagazine.com/album-review-spanish-love-songs-giant-sings-the-blues/ |publisher=New Noise Magazine |accessdate=27 February 2020}}</ref> The album was released through their own label.<ref name="AllMusic"/> In 2016, the band signed with Wiretap Records and rereleased their debut album with bonus tracks.<ref name="AllMusic"/> In 2017, keyboardist Meredith Van Woert joined the band, making them a five-piece.<ref name="AllMusic"/> In 2018, the band released their second studio album, ''Schmaltz''.<ref name="AllMusic"/><ref name="popmatters">{{cite web |last1=Carr |first1=Paul |title=Spanish Love Songs' 'Schmaltz' Is Cathartic for Anyone Seeking Sanctuary From Life's Unpredictability |url=https://www.popmatters.com/spanish-love-songs-schmaltz-2552573748.html |publisher=[[PopMatters]] |accessdate=27 February 2020}}</ref><ref name="atwoodmagazine">{{cite web |last1=Crowley |first1=James |title=Losers Forever: A Conversation with Spanish Love Songs |url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/slsl-losers-spanish-love-songs-interview-2019/ |publisher=[[Atwood Magazine]] |accessdate=27 February 2020}}</ref> In 2020, Spanish Love Songs released their third studio album titled ''[[Brave Faces Everyone]]''.<ref name="kerrang">{{cite web |title=Spanish Love Songs Announce New Album, Brave Faces Everyone |url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/spanish-love-songs-announce-new-album-brave-faces-everyone/ |publisher=[[Kerrang!]] |accessdate=27 February 2020}}</ref><ref name="alternativepress">{{cite web |last1=Leuthauser |first1=Adrian |title=Spanish Love Songs announce new album with honesty-fueled song "Kick" |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/spanish-love-songs-announce-new-album-brave-faces-everyone-kick/ |publisher=[[Alternative Press (magazine)|Alternative Press]] |accessdate=27 February 2020}}</ref> The album debuted at No. 21 on Vinyl Albums chart and No. 61 on the [[Top Album Sales]] chart with 2000 copies sold.<ref name="billboard genres">{{cite web|author=Glenn Rowley|title=Spanish Love Songs Are Here to Help You Survive the 'Bleak Stuff' With New Album: Emerging Artists Spotlight|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/8551513/spanish-love-songs-interview-emerging-artists-spotlight|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=February 20, 2020|accessdate=October 19, 2020}}</ref> Along with the album, the band announced a tour in the U.K. with [[The Menzingers]] and a U.S. tour with [[The Wonder Years (band)|The Wonder Years]].<ref name="alternativepress"/> The tour started on January 25, 2020.<ref name="alternativepress"/>

==Musical style==
Spanish Love Songs' musical style has been described as [[punk rock]],<ref name="billboard genres"/><ref name="kerrang genres">{{cite web|author=Glenn Rowley|title=Spanish Love Songs' New Single Is As Brutal As Honesty Gets|url=https://www.kerrang.com/the-news/spanish-love-songs-premiere/|work=[[Kerrang!]]|date=January 22, 2020|accessdate=October 19, 2020}}</ref><ref name="sputnik genres">{{cite web|title=Spanish Love Songs Are Here to Help You Survive the 'Bleak Stuff' With New Album: Emerging Artists Spotlight|url=https://www.sputnikmusic.com/bands/Spanish-Love-Songs/100642/|work=[[Sputnikmusic]]|accessdate=October 19, 2020}}</ref> [[emo]],<ref name="kerrang genres"/><ref name="sputnik genres"/><ref name="atwood">{{cite web|author=James Crowley|title=Review: Spanish Love Songs Enlighten a Horrible World with Brave Faces Everyone|url=https://atwoodmagazine.com/slbf-spanish-love-songs-brave-faces-everyone-album-review/|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=February 20, 2020|accessdate=October 19, 2020}}</ref> [[pop punk]],<ref name="billboard genres"/><ref name="sputnik genres"/><ref name="atwood"/> [[indie rock]],<ref name="atwood"/> [[pop rock]],<ref name="popmatters genres">{{cite web|url=https://www.popmatters.com/spanish-love-songs-brave-faces-2645046205.html|title=Punk's Spanish Love Songs Urge 'Brave Faces Everyone'|website=[[PopMatters]]|date=February 11, 2020|accessdate=September 28, 2020}}</ref> [[alternative rock]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kerrang.com/reviews/album-review-spanish-love-songs-brave-faces-everyone/|title=Album Review:Spanish Love Songs - Brave Faces Everyone|work=[[Kerrang!]]|last=Pearlman|first=Mischa|accessdate=October 19, 2020}}</ref> [[garage punk]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.punktastic.com/album-reviews/spanish-love-songs-brave-faces-everyone/|title=Spanish Love Songs - 'Brave Faces Everyone'|work=Punktastic|author=Andy Joice|date=February 4, 2020|accessdate=October 19, 2020}}</ref> and indie punk.<ref name="AllMusic"/> The band calls themselves "grouchrock".<ref name="AllMusic"/> Rich Wilson of [[AllMusic]] considers the band to be "[a continuation of] the emotional punk tradition started by bands such as [[Jawbreaker (band)|Jawbreaker]] and [[Hot Water Music]]."<ref name="AllMusic"/>
