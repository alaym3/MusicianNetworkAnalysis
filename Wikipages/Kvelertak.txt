'''Kvelertak''' is a Norwegian [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band from [[Stavanger]], formed in 2007. The group comprises vocalist Ivar Nikolaisen, guitarists Vidar Landa, Bjarte Lund Rolland and Maciek Ofstad, bassist Marvin Nygaard and drummer Håvard Takle Ohr. Founding member and lead singer Erlend Hjelvik left the group in 2018,<ref name="facebook1">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/Kvelertak/posts/10155456281650685 |title=Kvelertak - Hey, Erlend here, there is no easy way to say... |website=Facebook.com |access-date=14 March 2020}}</ref> being replaced by Ivar Nikolaisen.<ref name=IvarAnnouncement>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/Kvelertak/photos/a.386574020684.170939.58323625684/10155466132555685/?type=3&theater |title=Kvelertak - Ivar Nikolaisen, Welcome To The Kvelertak... |website=Facebook.com |date=20 July 2018 |access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref> Most of Kvelertak's songs have Norwegian lyrics, and their main influences are [[rock and roll]] and [[punk rock]].

The band's [[Kvelertak (album)|self-titled debut album]] was released in 2010 and sold more than 15,000 copies in Norway.<ref name="blab-gold">{{cite web| title = Kvelertak Receives Gold-Album Plaque From Dave Grohl| work = [[Blabbermouth.net]]| publisher = [[Roadrunner Records|Roadrunner]]| date = 1 July 2011| url = http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=160165| access-date = 1 July 2011| url-status = dead| archive-url = https://www.webcitation.org/6FkpfEftX?url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=160165| archive-date = 9 April 2013}}</ref> The second album, ''[[Meir (album)|Meir]]'', was released in 2013, followed by ''[[Nattesferd]]'' in 2016 and ''[[Splid]]'' in 2020. ''Splid'' was the band's first album with Ivar Nikolaisen on lead vocals.

== History ==
===Formation and debut album (2007–2011)===
[[File:Kvelertak - Erlend Hjelvik - Vocals - Singer.jpg|thumb|Former vocalist Erlend Hjelvik]]
Kvelertak were formed in [[Stavanger]], Norway, in 2007. The band's name is the Norwegian for "stranglehold" or "chokehold".<ref>{{cite web| last = Ramirez| first = Carlos| title = Kvelertak Strangle the Crowd in 'Ordsmedar Av Rang' -- Video Premiere| work = [[Noisecreep]]| publisher = [[AOL|AOL Music]]| date = 29 April 2011| url = http://www.noisecreep.com/2011/04/29/kvelertak-ordsmedar-av-rang-video/| access-date = 29 April 2011}}</ref> A demo, ''Westcoast Holocaust'', was self-released that year.<ref>[http://www.blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=74873 KVelertak: Eight Songs Available For Free Download - June 17, 2007]. [[Blabbermouth.net]].</ref><ref>[http://www.blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=78149 Kvelertak Replaces Helldriver At Norway's Øyafestivalen - Aug. 4, 2007]. [[Blabbermouth.net]].</ref> The band released their debut album, ''[[Kvelertak (album)|Kvelertak]]'', on 21 June 2010 via the Norwegian record label [[Indie Recordings]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/kvelertak-ordsmedar-av-rang-video-released/|title=KVELERTAK: 'Ordsmedar Av Rang' Video Released|website=Blabbermouth.net|date=20 April 2011|access-date=31 May 2016}}</ref> and on 15 March via [[The End Records]] in North America.<ref>[http://www.blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=158770 Kvelertak Interviewed In Berlin (Video) - May 29, 2011]. [[Blabbermouth.net]].</ref> The album was certified gold by [[International Federation of the Phonographic Industry|IFPI]] in Norway for selling over 15,000 copies.<ref name="blab-gold"/>

In March 2011, Kvelertak were presented with two [[Spellemannprisen]] Awards, a prestigious Norwegian music award similar to the American [[Grammy Award]]—for Best Newcomer and Best Rock Band.<ref>{{cite web| title = Kvelertak Wins Two Spellemann Awards| work = [[Blabbermouth.net]]| publisher = [[Roadrunner Records]]| date = 6 March 2011| url = http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=154941| access-date = 6 March 2011| url-status = dead| archive-url = https://www.webcitation.org/6FkpgkQxr?url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=154941| archive-date = 9 April 2013}}</ref> The Kvelertak song "Mjød" was used as the end music for the film [[The Troll Hunter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1740707/soundtrack|title=Trollhunter|date=29 October 2010|access-date=31 May 2016|website=IMDb.com}}</ref>

===''Meir'' and ''Nattesferd'' (2012–2017)===
[[File:Kvelertak - Rock am Ring 2019-2830.jpg|thumb|Kvelertak in 2019]]
The second album, ''[[Meir (album)|Meir]]'', was released on 25 March 2013 (26 March in the US) via [[Sony Music Entertainment|Sony Music Scandinavia]] in Scandinavia, [[Roadrunner Records]] in the rest of the world, and by [[Indie Recordings]] on [[Vinyl LP|vinyl]]. Kvelertak has also attracted a following from renowned artists such as [[Metallica]] front man [[James Hetfield]] who, along with the Norwegian [[Crown Prince Haakon]], watched the band perform in [[San Francisco]]. Hetfield also posted a video on his [[Instagram]] account of him watching the band and describing the front man Hjelvik as a "savage" frontman.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/kvelertak-is-almost-done-writing-material-for-next-album/|title=KVELERTAK Is Almost Done Writing Material For Next Album|website=Blabbermouth.net|date=29 November 2015|access-date=29 November 2015}}</ref>

The band's third studio album, titled ''[[Nattesferd]]'', was released on 13 May 2016.<ref>[http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/kvelertak-to-release-nattesferd-album-in-may-new-song-1985-streaming/ Kvelertak To Release 'Nattesferd' Album In May; New Song '1985' Streaming]. [[Blabbermouth.net]]. Published on 8 March 2016.</ref> They opened for [[Metallica]] on their [[WorldWired Tour]] between September 2017 and May 2018 in Europe, and was also the opening act for [[Ghost (Swedish band)|Ghost]] on their [[Popestar Tour]] in 2017.

===Lineup change and ''Splid'' (2018–present)===
[[File:Kvelertak - Rock am Ring 2019-1562.jpg|thumb|Ivar Nikolaisen in 2019]]
In July 2018, Erlend Hjelvik announced that he had left the band.<ref name="facebook1"/> His replacement, Ivar Nikolaisen (of Silver and The Good, The Bad and The Zugly), was announced on 20 July at their show in Fjellparkfestivalen, Flekkefjord<ref name=IvarAnnouncement /> - he had previously featured as a guest vocalist on 'Blodtørst'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Kvelertak-Kvelertak/release/2363845 |title=Kvelertak - Kvelertak (2010, Vinyl) |website=Discogs.com |access-date=14 January 2020}}</ref>

Kvelertak released their fourth studio album, ''[[Splid]]'', on 14 February 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/kvelertak-to-release-splid-album-in-february/|title=KVELERTAK To Release 'Splid' Album In February|website=[[Blabbermouth.net]]|access-date=27 November 2019|date=27 November 2019}}</ref> Hjelvik released his solo album, ''Welcome to Hel'', on 20 November 2020.
