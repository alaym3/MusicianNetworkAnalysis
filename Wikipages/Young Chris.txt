'''Christopher Francis Ries''' (born March 9, 1983), known professionally as '''Young Chris''', is an American rapper. He is a member of [[Philadelphia]] rap duo [[Young Gunz]] alongside his childhood friend, rapper Neef Buck, signed to [[Roc-A-Fella Records]]. The duo saw their greatest success with their 2003 debut single "[[Can't Stop, Won't Stop (Young Gunz song)|Can't Stop, Won't Stop]]".<ref name="billboard1">{{cite web | url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=young gunz|chart=all}} | title=Young Gunz History | access-date=April 3, 2011 | work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> The duo released two albums: ''[[Tough Luv]]'' (2004) and ''[[Brothers from Another]]'' (2005) and were apart of [[Beanie Sigel]]'s [[State Property (group)|State Property]] collective.<ref name="hiphopdx1">{{cite web|last=Koslow |first=Jessica |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.2165/title.hhdx-news-bits-Tuesday |title=HHDX News Bits (Tuesday) &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=December 9, 2003 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> Young Chris was signed in December 2010 as a solo artist to [[Universal Motown]] through producer/songwriter [[Rico Love]]'s label imprint Division1.<ref name="hiphopdx2">{{cite web|last=Burgess |first=Omar |url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/news/id.12553/title.young-chris-signs-to-division-one-universal-motown |title=Young Chris Signs To Division One/Universal Motown &#124; Get The Latest Hip Hop News, Rap News & Hip Hop Album Sales |publisher=HipHop DX |date=October 11, 2010 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> He released two albums through Division1: ''LIFE: Ladies In For Free'' and ''The Revival'', both in 2011. In 2014 he released the album ''Gunna Season'' through Jaccpot Entertainment. He has also had a large number of self-released mixtapes from 2007 to the present.

Young Chris is often confused with the rapper [[YC (rapper)|YC]], who used to perform as "Yung Chris", and is thus sometimes incorrectly credited as the performer of the YC song "[[Racks (song)|Racks]]".

== Music career ==

=== Young Gunz ===
Young Chris and [[Neef Buck]] (born Hanif Muhammad) had been friends since they were young boys on the block (C-Ave).<ref name="big shots">{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1476770/20030812/young_gunz.jhtml|title=Young Gunz Becoming The Big Shots Of State Property|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|date=August 12, 2003|publisher=MTV News|access-date=June 22, 2009}}</ref>  On "[[Takeover]]", a track from his 2001 album ''[[The Blueprint]]'', [[Jay-Z]] announced the duo as "Chris & Neef".<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p224505/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Young Gunz Biography |last=Birchmeier |first=Jason |year=2005|publisher=allmusic|access-date=June 22, 2009}}</ref>
In 2001, the duo performed on the ''[[State Property (soundtrack)|State Property]]'' soundtrack album.<ref name="allmusic" /> Young Chris and Neef starred in the titular movie.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0301893/|title=State Property (2002)|publisher=IMDb.com|access-date=August 9, 2014}}</ref> Also during this period of their career, Young Chris appeared throughout Dame Dash's ''Dream Team'' compilation, and Beanie Sigel's ''[[The Reason (Beanie Sigel album)|The Reason]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Love |first=Bret |url=http://allmusic.com/album/the-reason-r539895 |title=The Reason – Beanie Sigel |publisher=AllMusic |date=June 26, 2001 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> The duo performed together on guest appearances for albums like Jay-Z's ''[[The Blueprint 2]]'', Freeway's ''[[Philadelphia Freeway]]'', State Property's ''[[The Chain Gang Vol. 2]]'', and Memphis Bleek's ''[[M.A.D.E.]]''; all while recording their album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allmusic.com/artist/young-gunz-p224505/songs/all-songs |title=Young Gunz |publisher=AllMusic |date=October 9, 2003 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> The Young Gunz scored their first hit with "[[Can't Stop, Won't Stop (Young Gunz song)|Can't Stop, Won't Stop]]", the lead-off single from ''[[The Chain Gang Vol. 2]]'' album.<ref name="billboard1" />  Its song and video received major airplay on hip-hop radio, [[MTV2]], and [[Black Entertainment Television|BET]] and reached #14 on the U.S. [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart and #6 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot Rap Tracks chart.<ref name="billboard1" />

After the success of their single, "[[Can't Stop, Won't Stop (Young Gunz song)|Can't Stop, Won't Stop]]", the label jumped and was ready to release their debut album, ''[[Tough Luv]]''.<ref name="allmusic1">{{cite web|last=Kellman |first=Andy |url=http://allmusic.com/album/tough-luv-r680480 |title=Tough Luv – Young Gunz |publisher=AllMusic |date=February 24, 2004 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> It included the remix to "[[Can't Stop, Won't Stop (Young Gunz song)|Can't Stop, Won't Stop]]", which featured [[St. Louis]] rapper [[Chingy]].<ref name="allmusic1" /> Singles included "No Better Love" featuring former [[Roc-A-Fella Records]] crooner [[Gerrell Gaddis|Rell]] and the [[Just Blaze]]-produced "Friday Night".<ref name="allmusic1" /> ''[[Tough Luv]]'' debuted on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] at #3 after selling 128,000 copies in its first week.<ref name="billboard1" />  That week, the album at #2 was labelmate [[Kanye West]]'s debut album ''[[The College Dropout]]'', which was released just two weeks earlier.<ref>{{cite web|last=Martens|first=Todd|url=http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/search/google/article_display.jsp?vnu_content_id=1000451984|title=Norah Makes Comfy 'Home' At No. 1|work=Billboard|date=March 3, 2004}}</ref>

After a brief hiatus, the duo returned with [[Swizz Beatz]] produced "[[Set It Off (Young Gunz song)|Set It Off]]" from their second album, ''[[Brothers from Another]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Kellman |first=Andy |url=http://allmusic.com/album/brothers-from-another-r743190 |title=Brothers from Another – Young Gunz |publisher=AllMusic |date=May 24, 2005 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> It was the second release from the "new" [[Roc-A-Fella Records]], referencing when [[Jay-Z]] became president of [[Def Jam Records]], the first being [[Memphis Bleek]]'s [[534 (album)|534]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Conniff |first=Tamara |url=http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/6680078/ns/today-entertainment/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120928091228/http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/6680078/ns/today-entertainment/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=September 28, 2012 |title=Jay-Z named Def Jam president, CEO – Entertainment – Music – TODAY.com |publisher=Today.msnbc.msn.com |date=December 8, 2004 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Kellman |first=Andy |url=http://allmusic.com/album/534-r741428 |title=534 – Memphis Bleek |publisher=AllMusic |date=May 17, 2005 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> The album debuted on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] at #15, and included guest features from [[Kanye West]], [[Swizz Beatz]], [[Slim (singer)|Slim]] of [[112 (band)|112]] and [[John Legend]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/62729/a-welcome-exile-audioslave-opens-at-no-1 |title=A Welcome 'Exile': Audioslave Opens At No. 1 |publisher=Billboard |access-date=August 9, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://allmusic.com/album/brothers-from-another-r743190/credits |title=Brothers from Another – Young Gunz |publisher=AllMusic |date=May 24, 2005 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref>

=== Solo career ===
As early as March 2006, Young Chris thought about recording a solo album. He initially titled it ''Now or Never''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/bands/m/mixtape_monday/032006/|title=N.O.R.E. Makes Diddy Speak Spanish; Young Gunz Reload|last=Reid|first=Shaheem|date=March 20, 2006|work=Mixtape Monday|publisher=MTV News|access-date=June 22, 2009}}</ref> He stated to ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' magazine that he wanted to release a solo album by the end of 2008 and had been "95 percent done" by June 2008.<ref name="thomas1">{{cite web|last=Thomas|first=Anthony|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/online/?p=22543|title=Young Chris, "I'm up there with Wayne and Juelz"|work=XXL|date=June 26, 2008}}</ref>  In this article, Young Chris also states that he himself up there with [[Lil' Wayne]] and [[Juelz Santana]].<ref name="thomas1" /> Since this time, Chris has taken to the internet to solidify himself as a top solo artist, with his YoungChris.com Social Network, hitting the remix circuit, and his much anticipated "The Network" Mixtape series, hosted by [[DJ Don Cannon]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allmusic.com/artist/young-chris-p578339/songs |title=Young Chris |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prefixmag.com/reviews/young-chris/the-network-2/38468/ |title=Young Chris: The Network 2 (New Album) |publisher=Prefixmag.com |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref>

In December 2010 Young Chris signed with Division1, a label started by songwriter/producer [[Rico Love]], under the [[Universal Motown]] umbrella.<ref name="hiphopdx2" /> Love considers Young Chris his flagship artist, working closely with Young Chris on his debut album and pre-album/mixtape.<ref>{{cite web|last=Rodriguez |first=Jayson |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1652738/young-chris-gets-his-reintroduction-courtesy-usher-pal-rico-love.jhtml |title=Young Chris Gets His Re-Introduction Courtesy Of Usher Pal Rico Love – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV |date=November 22, 2010 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> The mixtape, called "The Re-Introduction", was released on November 20, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://youngchris.com/ |title=The Reintroduction Pre-Album &#124; Download Now! |publisher=Young Chris |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref> The aptly titled mixtape gives the spotlight to a newly signed Young Chris, as a solo artist, and also, re-introduces the combination of [[DJ Drama]] and [[Don Cannon]], the former "[[Affiliates]]" partners, hosting their first mixtape together, in several years.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1652738/20101119/dj_drama.jhtml|title=Young Chris Gets His Re-Introduction Courtesy Of Usher Pal Rico Love|last=Rodriguez|first=Jayson|date=November 22, 2010|work=Mixtape Daily|publisher=MTV News|access-date=November 22, 2010}}</ref>

On January 28, a song called "Philly Shit (Mega Mix)" featuring Young Chris, [[Eve (entertainer)|Eve]], [[Black Thought]], Money Malc, [[Fat Joe]], Fred The Godson, [[Diggy Simmons]], [[Jermaine Dupri]] & [[Game (rapper)|The Game]] was leaked.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.hiphopdx.com/index/singles/id.13665/title.young-chris-f-eve-black-thought-money-malc-fat-joe-fred-the-godson-diggy-simmons-jermaine-dupri-game-philly-shit-mega-mix|title=Young Chris f. Eve, Black Thought, Money Malc, Fat Joe, Fred The Godson, Diggy Simmons, Jermaine Dupri & Game "Philly Shit Mega-Mix"|date=January 29, 2011|publisher=HipHopDX.com|access-date=January 29, 2011}}</ref> On March 22, 2011, Young Chris' single "A$$ets" was commercially released.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/a-ets-feat-rico-love-single/id425509493 |title=A*$-ETS (feat. Rico Love) – Single by Young Chris – Download A*$-ETS (feat. Rico Love) – Single on iTunes |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=March 22, 2011 |access-date=April 3, 2011}}</ref>

In late 2013–2014, Young Chris began releasing a slew of freestyles, the latest being a reworking of Wale's "LoveHate Thing", released on May 6.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.worldstarhiphop.com/videos/video.php?v=wshh3UvTDr3ko9oy7277 |title=Young Chris – Love Hate Thing Freestyle &#124; New Video |publisher=Worldstarhiphop.com |date=May 6, 2014 |access-date=August 9, 2014}}</ref> He's currently working on a new studio album titled ''Alive.

On May 6, 2014, he released a mixtape on Datpiff called ''Gunna Seazon''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.datpiff.com/Young-Chris-Gunna-Season-mixtape.608957.html |title=Young Chris – Gunna Season Hosted by Jaccpot Ent // Free Mixtape @ |publisher=Datpiff.com |access-date=August 9, 2014}}</ref>
