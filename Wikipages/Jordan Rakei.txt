'''Jordan Rakei''' (born 23 May 1992) is a New Zealand-Australian musician, singer, songwriter and record producer currently based in [[London]], [[United Kingdom]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.noctismag.com/2017/11/07/jordan-rakei-5-minutes/|title=JORDAN RAKEI – 5 MINUTES WITH – noctismag|date=7 November 2017}}</ref> Rakei has also released and performed under the moniker Dan Kye.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mixmag.net/feature/get-to-know-jordan-rakei|title=Get to know Jordan Rakei, the Australian-raised soul singer and beatmaker}}</ref>

==Early life==
Rakei was born in the small town of [[Tokoroa]] on New Zealand's [[North Island]] to a New Zealander mother and a Pacific Islander father, a [[Māori people|Māori]] from the [[Cook Islands]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Jordan Rakei beat his struggle with anxiety by making music|url=https://www.rnz.co.nz/national/programmes/nat-music/audio/2018617757/jordan-rakei-beat-his-struggle-with-anxiety-by-making-music|date=2017-10-13|website=RNZ|language=en-nz|access-date=2020-05-06}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Jordan Rakei, the introverted multi-instrumentalist: 'My only hurdle is shyness'|url=https://thespinoff.co.nz/music/05-10-2017/jordan-rakei-the-introverted-multi-instrumentalist-my-only-hurdle-is-shyness/|date=2017-10-05|website=The Spinoff|access-date=2020-05-06}}</ref> At the age of three, Rakei and his family moved to Brisbane, Australia. He lived in Brisbane until he made the move to London in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nitelifeonline.com/words/interview-jordan-rakei/|title=Interview // Ninja Tune's Jordan Rakei on getting signed, working with Disclosure and changing his sound – Nitelife Magazine|date=6 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/features/sorcerer-the-perpetual-transition-of-jordan-rakei|title=Sorcerer: The Perpetual Transition Of Jordan Rakei}}</ref>

==Music career==
===2013–2016: Early career and debut album ===
Rakei's first releases were ''Live at Recording Oasis'' and ''Franklin's Room'' EPs in 2013, self-released, aged 21 years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://earmilk.com/2013/09/30/jordan-rakei-franklins-room-ep/|title=Jordan Rakei – Franklin's Room [EP] – EARMILK|date=30 September 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/franklins-room-ep/1033915887|title=Franklin's Room, by Jordan Rakei|publisher=iTunes Store}}</ref> Less than a year later in 2014, Rakei releases, ''Groove Curse'' EP,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/Groove-Curse-Jordan-Rakei/dp/B00R9SIGMK|title=Groove Curse, by Jordan Rakei}}</ref> was the first release on the Soul Has No Tempo label.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/label/714075-Soul-Has-No-Tempo|title=Soul Has No Tempo|publisher=discogs}}</ref> Less than a year after this EP, he made the move to London. 

After working with artists such as [[French Kiwi Juice|FKJ]] and [[Tom Misch]], in December 2015 he was part of a [[Grammy Award|Grammy]] nomination<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/grammys/6785974/grammy-nominations-2016-full-list|title=Grammy Nominations 2016: See the Full List of Nominees}}</ref> for his work on [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]]'s track "Masterpiece", released on their 2015 album ''[[Caracal (album)|Caracal]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/interview/9712-brothers-gonna-work-it-out-disclosure-break-down-every-song-on-their-new-lp/|title=Brothers Gonna Work It Out: Disclosure Break Down Every Song on Their New LP – Pitchfork|website=Pitchfork}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboardradiochina.com/story/2018-03-08BRITISH-ELECTRONIC-MUSIC-DUO-AND-GRAMMY-NOMINEE-Disclosure-en|title=Billboard Radio China – BRITISH ELECTRONIC MUSIC DUO AND GRAMMY NOMINEE Disclosure|website=billboardradiochina.com}}</ref>

Rakei released his debut album ''Cloak'' through his own label, 4101 Records on 3 June 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://howlandechoes.com/2016/05/jordan-rakei-announces-debut-album-listen-to-first-single-talk-to-me/|title=Jordan Rakei Announces Debut Album, Listen To First Single "Talk To Me" • Howl & Echoes|date=10 May 2016}}</ref> Later that year, he unveiled his dancefloor alias Dan Kye,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicismysanctuary.com/dan-kye-joy-ease-lightness-ep|title=Dan Kye's Upcoming Release Is Quite Literally A Future Classic|date=5 December 2016}}</ref> and released his first EP, titled ''Joy, Ease, Lightness'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.juno.co.uk/products/dan-kye-joy-ease-lightness/633183-01/|title=Joy, Ease, Lightness, by Dan Kye|website=Juno Records}}</ref> on Rhythm Section records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/Dan-Kye-Joy-Ease-Lightness/release/9459029|title=Dan Kye – Joy, Ease, Lightness|publisher=discogs}}</ref>

===2017–2020: ''Wallflower'' and subsequent releases ===
On 6 June 2017, it was announced that Rakei had signed to [[Ninja Tune]] records and would be releasing his next album with them.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.xlr8r.com/news/jordan-rakei-signs-to-ninja-tune|title=Jordan Rakei Signs to Ninja Tune}}</ref> With this announcement came the release of his new single "Sorceress".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theplayground.co.uk/jordan-rakeis-sorceress-released-via-ninja-tune/|title=Jordan Rakei's 'Sorceress' Released via Ninja Tune - The Playground|author=|date=|website=www.theplayground.co.uk|access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> Later that month, he made his [[Glastonbury Festival]] live debut on the Pussy Parlure stage.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lntvglobal.com/gb/article/glastonbury-2017-best-new-bands-to-see/|title = Glastonbury 2017: Best New Bands to See}}</ref>

On 22 September 2017, ''Wallflower'' was released on Ninja Tune. ''Wallflower'' was later shortlisted for Best Australian Album in the 2017 [[Australian Music Prize]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/nine-albums-shortlisted-2017-australian-music-prize/|title=These Nine Albums Are Shortlisted for the 2017 Australian Music Prize|date=24 January 2018}}</ref> 

A tour of North America in spring 2018 included a performance at [[SXSW]] Festival and a live session for [[Jason Bentley]]'s radio show on [[KCRW]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kcrw.com/music/shows/morning-becomes-eclectic/jordan-rakei|title=Jordan Rakei|date=6 March 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sxsw.com/music/2018/sxswfm-2018-showcasing-artist-interview-jordan-rakei/|title=SXSWfm 2018 Showcasing Artist Interview: Jordan Rakei|date=20 April 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/artists/1012458/jordan-rakei-tickets|title=Jordan Rakei|website=AXS}}</ref>

In 2018, Rakei released the single "Wildfire", released on 10 May 2018,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/news/latest-news/jordan-rakei-shares-seductive-summery-offering-wildfire|title=Jordan Rakei shares seductive, summery offering "Wildfire"}}</ref> and also provided the vocals on the 2018 [[Nightmares on Wax]] track "Typical".

On 26 February 2019, Rakei released "Mind's Eye", the first single from his then unannounced third album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.clashmusic.com/news/jordan-rakei-shares-fluttering-new-cut-minds-eye|title=Jordan Rakei Shares Fluttering New Cut 'Mind's Eye'|author=|date=|website=Clash Magazine|access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> On 23 April 2019, Rakei announced this new album, titled ''Origin'', and released a new single from it, "Say Something".<ref>{{cite web|title=Jordan Rakei on Instagram: "IT'S TIME! My new album 'ORIGIN' drops June 14th. The second single from the album 'SAY SOMETHING' is available for your digestion NOW! We…"|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BwmaVu-BaOX/|author=|date=|website=Instagram|access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> The album was released on 14 June 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2019/4/jordan-rakei-album-announcement-say-something-single-stream|title=Jordan Rakei Announces New LP 'Origin' & Drops Third Single "Say Something"|author=|date=23 April 2019|website=HYPEBEAST|access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref> Rakei supported the record with a series on music festival dates in Summer 2019 and then began a subsequent world tour, beginning that August in Brisbane and ending in November 2019 in Brooklyn, New York.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jordan Rakei Gigography, Tour History & Past Concerts – Songkick|url=https://www.songkick.com/artists/7562719-jordan-rakei/gigography|website=www.songkick.com|language=en|access-date=2020-05-06}}</ref> During the tour, he recorded a set for NPR Music's "[[Tiny Desk Concerts]]" series which was published online on 13 January 2020.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jordan Rakei appearance on NPR Music's "Tiny Desk Concerts" series.|url=https://www.npr.org/2020/01/13/795083915/jordan-rakei-tiny-desk-concert}}</ref>

===2021: ''What We Call Life''===
In September 2021, Rakei's fourth studio album ''What We Call Life'' was released, preceded by four singles. Rakei said the album is his "most vulnerable and intimate album to date"; something that he attributes to listening to singer-songwriters like [[Laura Marling]], [[Scott Matthews]], [[Joni Mitchell]] and [[John Martyn]] while writing the record.<ref name="unguarded"/>
