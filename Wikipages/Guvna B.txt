'''Isaac Borquaye''' (born 13 June 1989), better known as '''Guvna B''', is a rapper, author and broadcaster from [[Custom House, Newham|Custom House]], [[London]]. He has released 10 albums, 2 books and has produced segments for the BBC and a [[association football|football]] pundit for Sky Sports News' ''Good Morning Transfers''. He has also appeared on television to discuss topics that affect young people from underprivileged communities.

== Biography ==

Guvna B<ref>{{Cite web |date= 1 September 2019 |title=Rapping with a Purpose |url=http://www.selamtamagazine.com/stories/rapping-with-a-purpose|access-date=11 February 2021 |publisher=Selamta Magazine}}</ref> was born in London, England in June 1989, to Ghanaian parents from [[Accra]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Hearn |first=Sarah |date=18 June 2013 |title=Guvna B: UK Singer Makes Big Splash Across The Pond |url=https://positivelygospel.com/guvna-b-uk-singer-makes-big-splash-across-the-pond/ |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=PositivelyGospel.com}}</ref> Looking back on growing up in Custom House, in East London, he stated "My upbringing helped me find my faith. The negative things I saw in my society inspired me to do better and inspire people to be the best they can be instead of becoming stereotypical products of a negative environment"<ref>{{Cite web |title=Guvna B {{!}} Community & Support Interview |url=http://www.thisislime.net/interviews/community/Guvna_B |access-date=1 January 2014 |publisher=Thisislime.net}}</ref> He later graduated from [[University of Hertfordshire]] studying business and journalism<ref>{{Cite web |date=29 October 2010 |title=MOBO award for University of Hertfordshire Student |url=https://www.whtimes.co.uk/things-to-do/mobo-award-for-university-of-hertfordshire-student-5468340/ |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=Archant Community Media Ltd.}}</ref>

He has released ten albums and is the recipient of two [[MOBO Awards]] for Best Gospel Act,<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 November 2016 |title=Guvna B Made History 2x winner Best Gospel MOBO Awards 2016 |url=https://astepfwd.com/guvna-b-made-history-2x-winner-best-gospel-mobo-awards-2016/ |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=astepfwd.com}}</ref> three [[Urban Music Awards]] for Best Gospel Act or Artist, and a Gospel Music Award for Best Gospel Artist. He was the first rapper to top the Official Christian and Gospel Charts for his 2013 album ''Odd1Out''.<ref>{{Cite web |date=3 June 2013 |title=Guvna B becomes the first rapper to top the official Christian and Gospel |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/guvna-b-becomes-first-rapper-to-top-the-official-christian-and-gospel__3084/ |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=The Official UK Charts Company}}</ref> His story was shared with many when he was featured in the 22 March 2013 issue of the music industry magazine ''[[Music Week]]'' in a feature covering the gospel music industry in the UK.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=GuvnaB|author=Guvna B|number=316970937285226496|date=27 March 2013|title=Shouts to music week! They published this article on Christian and Gospel music.}}</ref> {{citation needed span|He has worked with [[Wretch 32]], [[Samm Henshaw]], [[Matt Redman]], [[Michelle Williams (singer)|Michelle Williams]] and [[Andy Mineo]].|date=February 2021|reason=It is unclear how the the subject has "worked with' these individuals. An itemised list, written in prose, would be the best option.}}

In January 2013, Guvna B launched his own urban clothing brand, Allo Mate.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://ireadmex.com/2013/01/allo-mate-the-clothing-line-from-guvna-b/ |title=‘Allo Mate’ The Clothing Line From Guvna B ‹ M.E.X Magazine |publisher=Ireadmex.com |date= |accessdate=1 January 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140102193726/http://ireadmex.com/2013/01/allo-mate-the-clothing-line-from-guvna-b/ |archive-date=2 January 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

''Unpopular Culture'' was published in 2017 by the [[Society for Promoting Christian Knowledge]]<ref>{{Cite web |date=4 June 2017 |title=Newham bred rapper Guvna B on his new book |url=https://www.newhamrecorder.co.uk/news/newham-bred-rapper-guvna-b-on-his-new-book-knife-3122952 |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=Archant Community Media Ltd}}</ref> and ''Unspoken: Toxic Masculinity and How I Faced the Man Within the Man'' was published by Harper Inspire, an imprint of [[HarperCollins]], in 2021 after his father's death to cancer.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Unspoken: Toxic Masculinity and How I Faced the Man Within the Man |url=https://harpercollins.co.uk/products/unspoken-toxic-masculinity-and-how-i-faced-the-man-within-the-man-guvna-b?variant=32610406596686 |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=Harper Collins Publishers Ltd.}}</ref>

He has presented documentaries on both radio and TV including BBC World Service's ''Gospel Meets Hip-Hop''<ref>{{Cite web |date=16 February 2020 |title=Gospel Meets hip-hop |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sounds/play/w3ct04m1 |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=BBC}}</ref> and BBC Radio 2's ''Keeping the Peace''.<ref>{{Cite web |year=2020 |title=Keeping The Peace with Guvna B |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/m0000v5g |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=BBC}}</ref> He has appeared as a pundit on Sky Sports News' ''Good Morning Transfers'', and as a guest on ''The Zoe Ball Breakfast'' show on BBC Radio 2.<ref>{{Cite web |year=2020 |title=Pause for thought |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p08f6xb0 |access-date=10 February 2021 |publisher=BBC}}</ref>

Guvna B lives in [[Greenwich]], London{{citation needed|date=February 2021}} with his wife and their son. He is a fan of [[West Ham United F.C.]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.whufc.com/news/articles/2018/september/21-september/ryan-fredericks-pre-match-playlist|title=Ryan Fredericks' Pre-Match Playlist|website=West Ham United F.C.|date=21 September 2021|access-date=5 March 2021}}</ref>
