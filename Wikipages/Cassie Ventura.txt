'''Casandra Elizabeth Ventura Fine'''<ref name=dailynews>{{cite news|url=https://www.nydailynews.com/homepage/ny-ent-diddy-cassie-split-20181017-story.html|title=Diddy and Cassie reportedly split following a summertime rumor that started in a strip club|work=[[New York Daily News|Daily News]]|location=New York City|first=Brian|last=Niemietz| date=October 17, 2018|access-date=June 13, 2019|archive-date=June 13, 2019 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20190613185712/https://www.nydailynews.com/homepage/ny-ent-diddy-cassie-split-20181017-story.html |url-status=live}}</ref> (born August 26, 1986),<ref name="cassiebiog" /> known professionally as '''Cassie''', is an American singer, songwriter, model, actress and dancer. Born in [[New London, Connecticut]], she began her career as a result of meeting record producer [[Ryan Leslie]] in late 2004, who later signed her to [[Ryan Leslie#NextSelection Lifestyle Group|NextSelection Lifestyle Group]]. During this time, [[Sean Combs|Diddy]] heard "[[Me & U (song)|Me & U]]" in a club, and Leslie convinced him to partner his [[Bad Boy Records]] with Leslie's NextSelection imprint for the release of Cassie's debut album.

Cassie's [[Cassie (album)|self titled debut studio album]] was released in August 2006, peaking at number four on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart and features the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 top three hit "Me & U".<ref name="DailyNews">Farber, Jim. ''Sounds of Summer'', ''[[Daily News (New York)|Daily News]]|location=New York''. August 1, 2006. NOW; Bullets & Bombs; page 34.</ref> In 2008, Cassie released the single "[[Official Girl]]" featuring [[Lil Wayne]]. In 2009, she released the singles "[[Must Be Love (Cassie song)|Must Be Love]]" featuring Diddy, and "[[Let's Get Crazy (Cassie song)|Let's Get Crazy]]" featuring [[Akon]], and signed a record deal with [[Interscope Records]]. In April 2013, Cassie released her debut mixtape ''[[RockaByeBaby]]'', which was promoted with the music videos of "Numb" featuring [[Rick Ross]] and "Paradise" featuring [[Wiz Khalifa]].

Apart from music, Cassie is signed to modeling agency [[Wilhelmina Models]] and One Management. Cassie has modeled for [[Calvin Klein|Calvin Klein one]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globalgrind.com/channel/culture/content/1907641/cassie-goes-bra-and-panties-for-ck1-photos |title=Cassie Goes Bra And Panties For CK1 (PHOTOS) |publisher=Global Grind |date=February 24, 2011 |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> and has been featured in magazines including ''[[GQ]]''<ref>{{cite web|last=Anthony |first=Mark |url=https://www.gq.com/entertainment/music/201210/cassie-photos-sophomore-album-gq-october-2012#slide=1 |title=Cassie Sexy GQ Photos – October 2012: Music |work=GQ |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> and ''[[Bust (magazine)|Bust]]'' and becoming the face of [[ASOS.com|ASOS]] 2013 spring collection,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globalgrind.com/style/cassie-ventura-asos-spring-campaign-photos |title=Cassie Ventura Stars As The Face of ASOS Spring Campaign (PHOTOS) |publisher=Global Grind |date=February 28, 2013 |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> Cassie has appeared in adverts for Delia's, [[Adidas]], [[Abercrombie & Fitch]], Seventeen, and a commercial for Clean and Clear.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehollywoodgossip.com/stars/cassie-ventura/bio.html#sthash.DVdyPm6h.dpuf |title=Cassie Ventura Biography |publisher=The Hollywood Gossip |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> Cassie has also ventured into acting; she made her film debut in ''[[Step Up 2: The Streets]]'' (2008).

==Career==

===2004–07: ''Cassie''===

[[Ryan Leslie]] spotted Cassie at clubs and parties in late 2004 frequently.<ref name="askmen1"/><ref name="last">{{cite web|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Cassie |title=Cassie – Discover music, concerts, stats, & pictures at |publisher=Last.fm |date=January 15, 2013 |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> The two wrote a duet called "Kiss Me", and after recording the track, Leslie played the song for music executive [[Tommy Mottola]].<ref name="askmen1"/><ref name="last" /> Mottola offered Cassie a management deal, and Leslie signed her to [[Ryan Leslie#NextSelection Lifestyle Group|NextSelection Lifestyle Group]], his music-media company he founded with online marketing partner Rasheed Richmond.<ref>[http://randb.about.com/od/artistinterviews/a/RyanLeslie.htm Ryan Leslie Interview]. Randb.about.com. Retrieved February 8, 2012.</ref><ref>[http://www.nextselection.com An Interactive Design Agency] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110803192445/http://www.nextselection.com/ |date=August 3, 2011 }}. NextSelection. Retrieved February 8, 2012.</ref> Leslie wrote and produced Cassie's first single, "[[Me & U (song)|Me & U]]", in 2005. The song became a club hit in Germany.<ref name="cassiebiog">{{cite web |title=Cassie |url=http://www.askmen.com/celebs/women/singer_300/343_cassie.html |publisher=[[AskMen.com]] |access-date=October 2, 2009|archive-date=August 23, 2008|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20080823184340/http://www.askmen.com/celebs/women/singer_300/343_cassie.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> During this time, [[Sean Combs|Diddy]] heard "Me & U" in a club, and Leslie convinced him to partner his [[Bad Boy Records]] with Leslie's NextSelection imprint for the release of Cassie's debut album.<ref name="cassiebiog"/>

[[Ryan Leslie]] produced most of the album, which is a mix of R&B/hip hop/pop. Ventura said in an interview, "I [[rapping|rap]], I sing, I do my R&B, I do my slow songs and stuff that the girls will love, I have a [[Southern United States#Music|down South]] joint, I have a [[rock music|rock]] song that I did with my girls this band called Pretty Boys."<ref>{{cite web

The album, ''[[Cassie (album)|Cassie]]'', was released on August 8, 2006 and sold 321,000 copies in the United States.<ref>{{cite web|last=Werde|first=Bill|title=Diddy: Cassie CD Will Catch People 'Off Guard'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1045930/diddy-cassie-cd-will-catch-people-off-guard|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=March 26, 2011|date=April 6, 2008}}</ref> The album's lead single "Me & U" peaked at number three on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], selling more than 1 million digital downloads.<ref name="debutsales">{{cite web |author=Werde, Bill |title=Diddy: Cassie CD Will Catch People "Off Guard" |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1045930/diddy-cassie-cd-will-catch-people-off-guard |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=April 7, 2008 |access-date=October 2, 2009}}</ref> To promote her album, Cassie performed on ''[[Total Request Live]]'' and ''[[106 & Park: BET's Top 10 Live]]''. Her performances were described as "rocky" and "less-than-stellar", but Diddy said that it was due to her inexperience.<ref name="cassiefright"/> He said that he would be "with her through her development", and that he has no "question on her ability [to sing]".<ref name="cassiefright"/> Cassie addressed the events on her MySpace page, saying that she is aware that her performances were "pretty bad", and that she was "still getting over stage fright".<ref name="cassiefright">{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1538701/20060816/story.jhtml |title=Diddy Brushes Off Cassie's Rocky Week |access-date=July 19, 2008 |author=Reid, Shaheem |date=August 16, 2006 |publisher=MTV}}</ref>

[[MTV News]] reported in June 2007 that contrary to rumors, Cassie had not been dropped from Bad Boy Records after releasing two singles from her self-titled [[Cassie (album)|debut album]]. Diddy said that she was in the studio working with producers [[Kanye West]] and [[Pharrell Williams]] on her second album.<ref>{{cite web|author=Reid, Shaheem |title=Diddy Says Cassie Hasn't Been Dropped – She's Just Busy With Kanye, Pharrell |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1563124/20070621/puff_daddy.jhtml |date=June 21, 2007 |publisher=[[MTV News]] |access-date=September 20, 2009}}</ref> It was also reported that Cassie was no longer collaborating with [[Ryan Leslie]], who had produced the majority of her debut album.<ref name="freepresslove"/> However, the singer later stated that she was open to collaborating with Leslie again.<ref name=title/> According to Bad Boy A&R Daniel 'Skid' Mitchell, rather than largely relying on the one songwriter-producer, the new album's mix of writers and producers, as well as her own co-write input, makes it a more personal record for her.<ref name="hitquarters.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/opar/intrview_Daniel_Mitchell_Interview.html |title=Interview With Daniel 'Skid' Mitchell |publisher=[[HitQuarters]] |date=October 25, 2010 |access-date=November 10, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101215231919/http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview%2Fopar%2Fintrview_Daniel_Mitchell_Interview.html |archive-date=December 15, 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
Additional production on the album came from [[Channel 7 (musician)|Seven]] and LV, as well as Diddy himself.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1046838/cassie-grows-up-on-sophomore-album |title=Cassie Grows Up on Sophomore Album |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=December 29, 2009 |date=January 11, 2008 |first=Mariel |last=Concepcion}}</ref> Cassie said that she had also collaborated with [[Akon]], [[The Neptunes]], [[Danja (musician)|Danja]], [[Eric Hudson]], The Surf Club and Rob Holiday, although she was not sure which tracks were going to be included on the album.<ref name=title/> Commenting on the number of producers on the album, Cassie said that she "collaborated with a lot of different producers and a lot of different writers, which was an amazing opportunity for me, because on my first album, one producer [Ryan Leslie] straight through...which was fun, but it was nice to experience other people."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2008/08/26/exclusive-cassie-grows-up-on-second-album/ |title=Exclusive: Cassie Grows Up on Second Album |date=August 26, 2008 |work=[[Rap-Up]] |access-date=December 29, 2009}}</ref>

===2008–13: Acting debut and ''RockaByeBaby''===
[[File:Cassie 2009.jpg|thumb|upright|left|180px|Cassie in 2009]]
In 2008, Cassie made her film debut as Sophie Donovan in the dance film ''[[Step Up 2: The Streets]]'', Cassie also sang the lead single "[[Is It You (Cassie song)|Is It You]]" to the [[Step Up 2: The Streets (soundtrack)|''Step Up'' soundtrack]]. The song was released on November 13, 2007,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/is-it-you-single/id267536497 |title=iTunes Music – Is It You – Single by Cassie |publisher=iTunes Store |date=November 13, 2007 |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> and peaked at eighty-five on the [[Canadian Hot 100]]<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/charts/2008-02-23/canadian-hot-100 "Is It You" (Canadian Hot 100)] at ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' online, February 23, 2008.</ref> and fifty-two in the [[The Official Charts Company|United Kingdom]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://acharts.us/song/33690|title=Cassie – Is It You|access-date=March 4, 2015}}</ref>
In July 2009, Cassie announced that her second studio album's title would be ''Electro Love''.<ref name=title>{{cite web |author=Ivey, Nile |title=Cassie: It's All ''Electro Love'' |url=http://www.bet.com/Music/interviews/music_cassieopensup_07.30.09.htm |date=July 30, 2009 |publisher=[[Black Entertainment Television]] |access-date=September 20, 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090827191509/http://www.bet.com/Music/interviews/music_cassieopensup_07.30.09.htm |archive-date=August 27, 2009 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> Cassie said that her new album will demonstrate more "independence"; there will be a "difference in vocals, a little bit more personality. And it's definitely a sensual album".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtv.ca/news/article.jhtml?id=10492 |title=Cassie's Single With Lil Wayne, ''Official Girl'', Shows Personality, Sensuality of Singer's Sophomore LP |access-date=August 23, 2008 |author=Vena, Jocelyn |date=August 18, 2008 |publisher=MTV |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110706185918/http://www.mtv.ca/news/article.jhtml?id=10492 |archive-date=July 6, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>
Three singles have been released from the album; "[[Official Girl]]" featuring [[Lil Wayne]] in August 2008, "[[Must Be Love (Cassie song)|Must Be Love]]" featuring [[Sean Combs|Diddy]] in April 2009, and "[[Let's Get Crazy (Cassie song)|Let's Get Crazy]]" featuring Akon in August 2009. All three singles failed to chart in the United States.<ref name="freepresslove">{{cite web |author=Adelman, Charlie |title=Waiting for Cassie to give us her Love |url=http://www.dailyfreepress.com/waiting-for-cassie-to-give-us-her-love-1.1881448 |date=September 16, 2009 |work=[[The Daily Free Press]] |access-date=September 20, 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091125022352/http://www.dailyfreepress.com/waiting-for-cassie-to-give-us-her-love-1.1881448 |archive-date=November 25, 2009 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

After several delays, it was announced in December 2009 that Cassie had signed a new record deal with [[Interscope Records]].<ref>{{cite web |author=Sir Platinum Graham |url=http://blog.singersroom.com/celebs/?p=8212 |title=Cassie To Interscope With Diddy, Red Cafe ? &#124; Singersroom Celebs |publisher=Blog.singersroom.com |date=December 11, 2009 |access-date=August 31, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716074648/http://blog.singersroom.com/celebs/?p=8212 |archive-date=July 16, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> She is featured in a spread in the August/September issue of ''[[Bust (magazine)|Bust]]'' in which she said a new single will be released in the fall. She has recently told her Twitter followers that she is still in the studio recording for her album. At the time of an October 2010 interview with [[HitQuarters]], A&R Daniel 'Skid' Mitchell said that Cassie had already recorded around fifty songs for the album. Mitchell said that she was taking her time over the album because she is keen for "it to be something that people are going to respect".<ref name="hitquarters.com"/> She also appeared in rapper [[Wiz Khalifa]]'s "[[Roll Up (Wiz Khalifa song)|Roll Up]]" music video as his love interest.

Cassie released the single, "[[King of Hearts (song)|King of Hearts]]", which was released in the United States on February 14, 2012, along with the official music video.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LI0Q0my8a64 |title=Cassie – King of Hearts |via=YouTube |date=February 14, 2012 |access-date=December 16, 2012}}</ref> On April 24, 2012, Cassie performed the single live for the first time at BET's [[106 & Park]] music video show. She concluded the performance with a brief dance number set to the Kanye West remix of the song, along with four dancers. After the performance Cassie and 106 & Park were worldwide [[trending topics]] on [[Twitter]] and it received mostly mixed to positive reviews from various online media outlets.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2012/04/24/cassie-performs-king-of-hearts-on-106-park/|title=Cassie Performs 'King of Hearts' on '106 & Park'|work=Rap-Up|access-date=March 4, 2015}}</ref> The single, "Balcony", featuring [[Young Jeezy]], was then sent to U.S. urban radio stations on September 18, 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allaccess.com/urban/future-releases |title=Urban/UAC Future Releases &#124; R&B, Hip Hop, Release Schedule and Street Dates &#124; |publisher=Allaccess.com |access-date=December 16, 2012}}</ref>

===2016–present: Second studio album and other endeavors===
In March 2015, Cassie's boyfriend, rapper Diddy announced he would return to producing and revealed he would be producing songs for Cassie's second studio album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musictimes.com/articles/31303/20150311/diddy-producing-new-music-kanye-west-pusha-t-the-weeknd-asap-ferg-mmm-album.htm|title=Sean "Diddy" Combs Producing New Music For Kanye West, Pusha T, The Weeknd, A$AP Ferg & More|first=Music|last=Times|date=March 11, 2015}}</ref> In 2016 it was announced Cassie would return to acting, playing the lead role in the romantic comedy ''The Perfect Match''. Released on March 11, 2016, the Billie Woodruff directed film, stars Cassie alongside Terrence J who plays Charlie, a successful playboy who doesn't believe in love until he crosses paths with the sexy Eva (Cassie).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rap-up.com/2016/01/19/the-perfect-match-trailer/|title=Watch Cassie, Brandy, & French Montana in 'The Perfect Match' Trailer|website=Rap-up.com|access-date=25 August 2019}}</ref> In late 2017, she released the singles "Love a Loser" featuring American rapper [[G-Eazy]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2017/08/stream-cassie-love-loser-featuring-g-eazy|title=Cassie Returns With Her Latest Single 'Love a Loser' f/ G-Eazy|work=Complex|date=August 4, 2017|access-date=August 4, 2017}}</ref> and "Don't Play It Safe."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/cassie-dont-play-it-safe/|title=Cassie - Don't Play It Safe review|work=Pitchfork|date=December 22, 2017|access-date=July 24, 2019}}</ref>

In January 2019, after ending her relationship with Diddy and leaving Bad Boy Records, Cassie entered the studio with the likes of producers Chris n Teeb and singer-songwriter [[Ro James]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thesource.com/2019/01/04/cassie-new-music/|title=Cassie Returns to the Studio Amid Split from Diddy|work=The Source|date=January 4, 2019|access-date=July 24, 2019}}</ref> Throughout Summer 2019, Cassie released several singles -- "Moments" with The Code, "Excuses," "Hungover," "Don't Let Go," "Speaking Of," "Rollercoaster" featuring Ro James, "Teach Me" and "Simple Things"—via her own Ventura Music label. The series of releases was dubbed "Free Fridays" by Cassie and serve as a teaser ahead of the release of her long-awaited second album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphop-n-more.com/2019/06/cassie-moments-new-song/|title=New Music: Cassie - 'Moments'|work=HipHopNMore|date=June 23, 2019|access-date=July 24, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://karencivil.com/2019/06/28/new-music-cassie-excuses/|title=New Music: Cassie - 'Excuses'|work=Karen Civil|date=June 28, 2019|access-date=July 24, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://hiphop-n-more.com/2019/07/cassie-hungover-new-song/|title=New Music: Cassie - 'Hungover'|work=HipHopNMore|date=July 5, 2019|access-date=July 24, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://rickeysmileymorningshow.com/2046181/cassie-dont-let-go/|title=Hate It Or Love It?! ==Artistry and public image==
[[File:Cassie 2012.png|thumb|upright|right|180px|Cassie in 2012]]
Cassie's musical sound is [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]], [[Pop music|pop]], and [[hip hop soul]]. Her voice has "character and ability".<ref name="allmusic1">{{cite web|last=Kellman |first=Andy |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/cassie-mw0000778854 |title=Cassie – Cassie : Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |publisher=AllMusic |date=August 8, 2006 |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> Cassie cites [[Aaliyah]], [[Jennifer Lopez]], [[Christina Aguilera]], [[Madonna]], [[Stevie Wonder]], [[Nine Inch Nails]], [[Britney Spears]], [[La Lupe]], [[Mariah Carey]], [[Faith Evans]] and [[Usher (singer)|Usher]] as musical influences. Cassie also refers to herself as a "die-hard [[Janet Jackson]] fan", continuing to say "I'd love to emulate Janet Jackson's career—totally, Janet Jackson is an incredible and fantastic, from her moves to her voice".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebritydays.com/cassie-ventura-profile-and-biography.html |title=Cassie Ventura Profile and biography |publisher=Celebrity Days |access-date=April 18, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111014164829/http://celebritydays.com/cassie-ventura-profile-and-biography.html |archive-date=October 14, 2011 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/cassie/related-artists/?filter=influencedBy|title=Cassie Music Influences|website=MTV Artists|access-date=April 18, 2016}}</ref> Cassie debut album was R&B and [[pop rock]] with urban and contemporary R&B styles, the album contained "looming synthesizer patterns" and "ice-cream-truck melody to give it a slightly twisted and threatening edge", as well as containing "flippant playfulness".<ref name="allmusic1"/>
''[[RockaByeBaby]]'' is an [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]] and [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] mixtape that featured "dark"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://globalgrind.com/music/cassie-rockabyebaby-mixtape-download |title=Cassie #RockaByeBaby (MIXTAPE DOWNLOAD) |publisher=Global Grind |date=April 11, 2013 |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> and a "raw, laid back vibe".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.vibevixen.com/2013/04/5-things-to-expect-from-cassies-rockabyebaby-mixtape/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=April 12, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131217154655/http://www.vibevixen.com/2013/04/5-things-to-expect-from-cassies-rockabyebaby-mixtape/ |archive-date=December 17, 2013 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

Cassie is known for being a style icon due to her "edgy style"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.zimbio.com/Cassie+Ventura |title=Cassie Ventura |publisher=Zimbio |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> and "feminine" and "sophisticated" fashion. Cassie is also known for setting the trend among women of shaving their hair, after Cassie shaved hers in 2009 influenced by [[punk fashion|punk]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Vena |first=Jocelyn |url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1696274/cassie-the-boys-shaved-head.jhtml |title=From Rihanna To Ellie Goulding: Cassie Breaks Down The Shaved-Head Trend – Music, Celebrity, Artist News |publisher=MTV |date=October 25, 2012 |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref> She has cited [[Kate Moss]] as her style influence.<ref name="rap-up1">{{cite web|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2013/04/10/cassie-brings-rockabyebaby-to-106-park/#more-154760 |title=Cassie Brings 'RockaByeBaby' to '106 & Park' |publisher=Rap-Up.com |access-date=April 18, 2013}}</ref>
