'''Claud Mintz''', known professionally as '''Claud''', is an American [[bedroom pop]] singer-songwriter from the suburbs of [[Chicago]]. Claud is non-binary and uses they/them pronouns.<ref name="billboard">{{cite web |last1=Williams |first1=Nick |title=Non-Binary Artist Claud Debuts New 'Sideline Star' EP & Shares Exclusive 'Gaylist' Mix |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8540685/non-binary-artist-claud-pride-playlist-exclusive-listen |publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |accessdate=22 November 2020}}</ref>

==History==
Claud began releasing music under the pseudonym '''Toast''', releasing an EP in 2018.<ref name="billboard"/> In 2019, they dropped out of college at [[Syracuse University]]<ref name="papermagaazine"/> to pursue music full-time.<ref name="billboard"/> Claud released an EP called ''Sideline Star'' on October 25, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Non-Binary Artist Claud Debuts New 'Sideline Star' EP & Shares Exclusive 'Gaylist' Mix|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8540685/non-binary-artist-claud-pride-playlist-exclusive-listen|access-date=2021-03-22|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref> In 2020, Claud formed a new band with [[Clairo]], Josh Mehling, and [[Noa Getzug|Noa Frances Getzug]], called Shelly.<ref name="stereogum">{{cite web |last1=DeVille |first1=Chris |title=Hear 2 Songs From Clairo, Claud, & Friends' New Band Shelly |date=30 October 2020 |url=https://www.stereogum.com/2104453/shelly-steeeam-natural-clairo-claud/music/ |publisher=[[Stereogum]] |accessdate=22 November 2020}}</ref> The group released two songs on October 30, 2020, titled “Steeeam” and “Natural”.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Clairo and Her Best Friends Have Formed Your New Favorite Band, Shelly / Ones To Watch|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/shelly-band-clairo-claud|access-date=2021-03-21|website=Ones To Watch}}</ref> That same year, Claud became the first artist to sign on with [[Phoebe Bridgers]]' record label Saddest Factory.<ref name="nme">{{cite web |last1=Reilly |first1=Nick |title=Claud announced as first act signed to Phoebe Bridgers' Saddest Factory Records and released the new song Gold. |date=14 October 2020 |url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/news/music/claud-announced-as-first-act-signed-to-phoebe-bridgers-saddest-factory-records-radar-2785024 |publisher=[[NME]] |accessdate=22 November 2020}}</ref> Claud's debut album, ''Super Monster'', was released on February 12, 2021.<ref name="nme1">{{cite web |last1=Triscari |first1=Caleb |title=Claud announces debut album 'Super Monster', shares new single |date=3 December 2020 |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/claud-announces-debut-album-super-monster-shares-new-single-2830646 |publisher=[[NME]] |access-date=5 December 2020}}</ref>
