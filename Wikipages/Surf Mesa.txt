'''Powell Aguirre''' (born April 10, 2000), known by the stage name '''Surf Mesa''', is an American electronic musician from [[Seattle]]. He gained fame for the single "[[ILY (I Love You Baby)]]" (2019), a song which sampled a cover of [[Frankie Valli]]'s "[[Can't Take My Eyes Off You]]" (1967) which gained popularity on [[TikTok]].<ref name="Wonderland"/>

== Biography ==

Aguirre was born in [[Seattle]], and is the son of jazz saxophonist Tony Aguirre.<ref name="Soundazed"/> He has been making music since the third grade of elementary school, first learning through [[FL Studio]] and production tutorials on YouTube.<ref name="Wonderland">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wonderlandmagazine.com/2020/06/12/surf-mesa-producer-be-kind/ |title=SURF MESA Meet the LA-hailed producer at the forefront of a new wave of music. |website=Wonderland Magazine |date=June 12, 2020 |access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref> He began working on the Surf Mesa project in high school, releasing music on [[SoundCloud]].<ref name="SSW">{{Cite web|url=https://surfingsoundwaves.com/surf-mesa-ily-i-love-you-baby-ft-emilee/ |title=Surf Mesa – ily (i love you baby) ft. Emilee |website=Surfing Sound Waves |date=February 3, 2020 |access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref> As Aguirre was planning a move to [[Arizona]] to study computer science at a technology school, he broke his leg and was bed-bound for three months.<ref name="VoyageLA">{{Cite web|url=http://voyagela.com/interview/meet-powell-aguirre-surf-mesa-venice/ |title=Meet Powell Aguirre of Surf Mesa in Venice |website=VoyageLA |date=February 3, 2020 |access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref> During this time, Aguirre was able to focus fully on producing music.<ref name="VoyageLA"/>

Aguirre decided to move to [[Los Angeles]] after visiting a girl he was interested in, as he realized the opportunities for musicians in the city.<ref name="VoyageLA"/> In 2019, Aguirre released his debut single "Taken Away" featuring Alexa Danielle,<ref name="Earmilk">{{Cite web|url=https://earmilk.com/2019/03/28/were-taken-away-with-surf-mesa-and-alexa-danielles-warm-collaboration/ |title=We're "Taken Away" with Surf Mesa and Alexa Danielle's warm collaboration |first=Vivian |last=Lin |website=Earmilk |date=March 28, 2019 |access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref> and his debut [[extended play]] ''Bedroom''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://umusic.co.nz/select/indie/ones-to-watch-surf-mesa-and-surfaces-two-different-artists-chasing-a-similar-sound/ |title=Ones To Watch: Surf Mesa and Surfaces – Two Different Artists Chasing A Similar Sound |first=Kellie |last=Given |website=UMusic |year=2020 |access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref> In November, Aguirre released "[[ILY (I Love You Baby)]]", a song based on singer Emilee's cover of "[[Can't Take My Eyes Off You]]".<ref name="Wonderland"/> The song gained massive popularity on video platform [[TikTok]], leading to Aguirre being signed by [[Astralwerks]] and [[Universal Music Group|Universal]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/pro/features/tik-tok-hits-changing-titles-983383/ |title=You Have a TikTok Hit! Now, Quick — Change the Title |website=Rolling Stone |first=Elias |last=Leight |date=April 18, 2020 |access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref> By June 2020, the song had become an international hit, reaching the top 10 in Austria, Germany, Switzerland, Scotland, the Netherlands, Malaysia and Singapore, and the top 5 of the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Dance/Electronic Songs|Hot Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/index.php/articles/business/chart-beat/9388795/surf-mesa-hot-dance-electronic-songs-chart-ily |title=Surf Mesa Hits Top 5 on Hot Dance/Electronic Songs Chart With Frankie Valli-Inspired 'ily' |website=Billboard |first=Gordon |last=Murray |date=May 21, 2020 |access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref>

In June 2020, Aguirre released an official remix for American electronic musician [[Marshmello]] and American singer [[Halsey (singer)|Halsey]]'s "[[Be Kind]]".<ref name="360Halsey">{{Cite web|url=https://www.the360mag.com/be-kind-surf-mesa-remix/ |title=Be Kind (Surf Mesa Remix) |website=The360Mag |date=June 12, 2020|access-date=July 29, 2020}}</ref>
