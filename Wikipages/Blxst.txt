'''Matthew Dean Burdette''' (born September 17, 1992),<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.californiabirthindex.org/birth/matthew_dean_burdette_born_1992_21239013}}</ref> known professionally as '''Blxst''' (pronounced "blast"), is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer from [[Los Angeles]], [[California]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Blxst Wants to Take the Torch for L.A. | url=https://www.xxlmag.com/blxst-interview-nipsey-hussle |website=XXL Mag}}</ref> His record label Evgle partnered with [[Red Bull Records]] for the release of his debut EP ''No Love Lost'' on September 4, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=The New Hype: Meet the Rapper-Singer Taking Over Los Angeles |url=https://www.theringer.com/music/2020/12/16/22177796/blxst-no-love-lost-interview |website=The Ringer|date=16 December 2020 }}</ref> He is perhaps best known for his hit single "[[Chosen (Blxst song)|Chosen]]" featuring [[Tyga]] and [[Ty Dolla Sign]].

==Early life==
Burdette grew up in [[South Los Angeles]] and began rapping and producing shortly after he finished high school.<ref>{{cite web |title=Act Like You Know: An Interview with Blxst |url=https://www.passionweiss.com/2020/10/09/act-like-you-know-an-interview-with-blxst/ |website=PassionWeiss|date=9 October 2020 }}</ref>

==Career==
Blxst launched his music career with a hip hop collective called TIU Muzic, where he gained experience in the music industry and gathered a local following. In 2014, Blxst produced "Do Yo Gudda (Remix)" by Hitta J3 featuring [[Kendrick Lamar]], [[YG (rapper)|YG]] and [[Problem (rapper)|Problem]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rap-up.com/2014/07/31/new-music-hitta-j3-f-problem-kendrick-lamar-yg-do-yo-gudda-remix/ |title=New Music: Hitta J3 f/ Problem, Kendrick Lamar, & YG – 'Do Yo Gudda (Remix)' |work=[[Rap-Up]] |publisher=Devin Lazerine |date=July 31, 2014}}</ref> In 2015, he founded his label Evgle, and became a creative director for his graphic design and music videos.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Draughorne|first1=Kenan|title=How 'Overrated' Rapper Blxst Went From Flooding Kendrick Lamar's Tweets to Working with Him|url=https://hiphopdx.com/news/id.59121/title.how-overrated-rapper-blxst-went-from-flooding-kendrick-lamars-tweets-to-working-with-him|website=HipHopDX|date=20 November 2020}}</ref> Blxst released his first song "Who Would've Thought" in 2016, with a string of independent singles in 2018 including a collaboration with [[Eric Bellinger]]. In 2019, he released his first solo hit single called "Hurt", with millions of streams.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Simpson |first1=Paul |title=Blxst - Biography |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/blxst-mn0003931650/biography |website=AllMusic}}</ref> On August 28, 2019, he released a collaboration EP with Bino Rideaux titled ''Sixtape''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Blxst & Bino Rideaux Link Up For New EP "Sixtape" |url=https://homegrownradio.net/blxst-unleash-new-ep-sixtape/ |website=HomeGrownRadio}}</ref>

In 2020, Evgle partnered with [[Red Bull Records]] for the release of his debut EP ''No Love Lost'' on September 4, with singles "Overrated", "No Love Lost", and "Be Alone".<ref>{{cite web |title=Blxst Off |url=https://www.redbull.com/us-en/theredbulletin/blxst-portrait |website=Red Bull}}</ref> The EP peaked at number 3 on the [[Top Heatseekers|''Billboard'' Heatseekers]] chart. The deluxe edition was released on December 4, with additional features from [[Ty Dolla Sign]], [[Tyga]], [[Dom Kennedy]] and Bino Rideaux.<ref>{{cite web |title=Blxst brings forth deluxe version of 'No Love Lost' album |url=https://www.revolt.tv/new-music/2020/12/4/22151588/blxst-no-love-lost-deluxe-album |website=Revolt|date=4 December 2020 }}</ref> On March 12, 2021, he released a two-track EP titled ''Just for Clarity'', including "Don't Forget" featuring [[Drakeo the Ruler]] and "Fck Boys" featuring [[Russ (rapper)|Russ]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Callender |first1=Brandon |title=The Ones: Blxst's "Don't Forget" [ft. Drakeo the Ruler] |url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/the-ones-blxst-dont-forget-ft-drakeo-the-ruler/ |website=Pitchfork|date=18 March 2021 }}</ref> That same year, he was listed on the annual [[XXL (magazine)#Annual Freshman Class List|''XXL'' Freshman Class]]. On July 16, Blxst released ''Sixtape 2'' with Bino Rideaux.<ref>{{cite web |title=Blxst and Bino Rideaux Reunite for New Single "Movie" |url=https://www.complex.com/music/blxst-and-bino-rideaux-reunite-for-new-single |website=Complex}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Blxst grew up listening to [[West Coast hip hop]] with influences from [[Ryan Leslie]], [[Pharrell Williams]] and [[Kanye West]]. His musical style has been compared to West Coast rapping and singing hybrids such as [[Ty Dolla Sign]] and [[Nate Dogg]].<ref>{{cite web |title=THE BREAK PRESENTS: BLXST |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/blxst-interview-the-break/ |website=XXL}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Weir |first1=Grayson |title=An Interview With Rapper-Singer Blxst: LAs' Most Unique Flow Keeps A Tight Circle, Plays The Role Of Superhero And Makes Great Tacos |url=https://brobible.com/culture/article/rapper-blxst-red-bull-dom-kennedy/ |website=Brobible|date=18 December 2020 }}</ref>
