'''Erica Banks''' is an American rapper from [[DeSoto, Texas]]. Following the release of her first three mixtapes, she signed to 1501 Certified Entertainment, who released her self-titled mixtape in June 2020. It spawned her first charting single, "[[Buss It]]", which went viral on [[TikTok]]. In January 2021, she signed to [[Warner Records]], on which "Buss It" was reissued.

== Early life ==
Banks was born and raised in [[DeSoto, Texas]].<ref name="uproxxint">{{cite web |last1=Johnson |first1=Cherise |title=Erica Banks Thinks Her Viral 'Buss It' Challenge Is A Beautiful Thing |url=https://uproxx.com/music/erica-banks-interview-buss-it-challenge-tiktok/ |website=[[Uproxx]] |access-date=January 25, 2021 |language=en |date=January 19, 2021}}</ref> At the age of 12, she began writing poetry, winning several school contests, and started rapping in high school. While attending [[Texas A&M University–Commerce]] as a nursing student, she recorded her first songs, leading to her to decide to drop out during her sophomore year to pursue her music career.<ref name="xxl">{{cite web |last1=Seabrook III |first1=Robby |title=The Break Presents: Erica Banks |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/erica-banks-interview-the-break/ |website=[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]] |access-date=January 25, 2021 |language=en |date=January 20, 2021}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2018, Banks released her first single, titled "Talk My Shit" independently through [[SoundCloud]]. On March 17, 2019, Banks subsequently self-released her first official mixtape, titled ''Art of the Hustle''. In April 2019, she signed with [[1501 Certified Entertainment]] after playing a snippet of her song "[[Buss It]]" for the label's CEO, American former baseball player [[Carl Crawford]], in an [[Instagram Live]] video.<ref name="xxl" /> Despite after signing with 1501 Certified, two of her mixtapes {{en dash}} ''Pressure'' and ''Cocky on Purpose'' {{en dash}} were subsequently self-released between June and November of that year.<ref name="complexbuss">{{cite web |last1=McKinney |first1=Jessica |title=What Is the Buss It Challenge? 2021's First Big Viral Trend, Explained |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2021/01/buss-it-challenge-explained/erica-banks |website=[[Complex Networks|Complex]] |access-date=January 25, 2021 |language=en |date=January 12, 2021}}</ref>

On June 19, 2020, Banks released her self-titled fourth mixtape through 1501 Certified. The mixtape spawned the singles, including a full version of the song "Buss It", and "Give Me That", the former of which became Banks's breakout song in January 2021 after becoming the subject of a viral trend on the video-sharing platform [[TikTok]], giving her her first entry on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] chart.<ref name="hb">{{cite web |last1=Gonzales |first1=Erica |title=Erica Banks Almost Didn't Release "Buss It"—and Now It's a Viral Sensation |url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/art-books-music/a35280921/erica-banks-buss-it-challenge-interview/ |website=[[Harper's Bazaar]] |access-date=January 25, 2021 |language=en |date=January 22, 2021}}</ref><ref name="thesource" /> In that same month, she signed a record deal with [[Warner Records]].<ref name="hb" /> Later in that same month, she was featured on American rapper [[Yella Beezy]]'s single "Star".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tigg |first1=FNR |title=Yella Beezy Connects With Erica Banks for New Song "Star" |url=https://www.complex.com/music/yella-beezy-erica-banks-star |website=Complex |access-date=January 30, 2021 |language=en |date=January 29, 2021}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Banks has listed American rappers [[Missy Elliott]], [[Nicki Minaj]] among her musical inspirations, also naming rappers [[Asian Doll]] and Cuban Doll as influential on her music and image.<ref name="nylonint">{{cite web |last1=Inman |first1=DeMicia |title=Texan Rapper Erica Banks On Her Song "Buss It" Going Viral |url=https://www.nylon.com/entertainment/erica-banks-buss-it-challenge |website=[[Nylon (magazine)|Nylon]] |access-date=January 25, 2021 |language=en |date=January 13, 2021}}</ref> She has been compared by fans to American rapper [[Megan Thee Stallion]] and Ti from the [[Taylor Girlz]].<ref name="dallaso">{{cite web |last1=Gonzalez |first1=Alex |title=Dallas Rapper Erica Banks Is Hotter Than Texas With 'Buss It' |url=https://www.dallasobserver.com/music/dallas-rapper-erica-banks-was-inspired-by-mentor-megan-thee-stallion-11923152 |website=[[Dallas Observer]] |access-date=January 25, 2021 |language=en |date=July 1, 2020}}</ref><ref name="xxl" />
