'''The Dangerous Summer''' is an American rock band from [[Ellicott City, Maryland]], United States. 
The band's name is taken from the book [[The Dangerous Summer|of the same name]] by [[Ernest Hemingway]]. The band has stated influences from [[Third Eye Blind]], [[Bright Eyes (band)|Bright Eyes]], [[U2]], [[Phil Collins]], [[Explosions in the Sky]], [[Jimmy Eat World]], and [[Andrew McMahon]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.baltimoresun.com/entertainment/bal-li.mixband16aug16-story.html|title=The Dangerous Summer|website=Baltimoresun.com}}</ref>
Over the years the band has sold over 60,000 albums and over 300,000 singles.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://towerrecords.com/products/the-dangerous-summer-the-dangerous-summer|title=The Dangerous Summer - The Dangerous Summer|website=towerrecords.com|access-date=2 April 2021}}</ref>

==History==
The band formed in [[Ellicott City, Maryland]], United States in the summer of 2006<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-dangerous-summer-mn0000586843/biography|title=The Dangerous Summer  - Biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=2 April 2021}}</ref> to write their first EP, ''There Is No Such Thing as Science'', which drew the attention of [[Hopeless Records]] and other labels in 2007. They signed with Hopeless Records within eight months (in April 2007) of starting the band during their senior year of high school.  They then re-released their debut EP under a new name, ''If You Could Only Keep Me Alive'', with new songs included.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.last.fm/music/The+Dangerous+Summer/+wiki|title=The Dangerous Summer - Biography|website=[[LastFM]]|access-date=2 April 2021}}</ref>

In April 2009, the band played ''[[The Bamboozle]]''.

The band's debut studio album, ''[[Reach for the Sun]]'', was released on May 5, 2009, and reached number 42 on the [[Billboard magazine|''Billboard'']] Heatseekers chart.<ref>[{{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p923051/charts-awards|pure_url=yes}} Charts], [[Allmusic]]</ref>

Their song "The Permanent Rain" was used in a trailer for the 2009 movie ''[[Love Happens (2009 film)|Love Happens]]'', starring [[Jennifer Aniston]] and [[Aaron Eckhart]].

An acoustic version of ''[[Reach for the Sun]]'' was digitally released on March 15, 2011.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/the_dangerous_summer_stream_acoustic_record/ | title=The Dangerous Summer stream acoustic record | accessdate=July 23, 2011 | date=March 15, 2011 | publisher=Alternative Press}}</ref>

The band's second album, ''[[War Paint (The Dangerous Summer album)|War Paint]]'', was released July 19, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Dangerous Summer Announce Their New Album, WAR PAINT|url=http://hopelessrecords.com/the-dangerous-summer-announce-their-new-album-war-paint/|accessdate=July 23, 2011|date=June 8, 2011|publisher=Hopeless Records}}</ref> It reached 149 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] on August 6, 2011 in its only week on the chart.<ref>{{cite web |url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=the dangerous summer|chart=all}} |title=War Paint - The Dangerous Summer |work=[[Billboard.com]] |publisher=[[Rovi Corporation]] |accessdate=July 29, 2012}}</ref>

They played on the [[Warped Tour 2011]] on the Nintendo 3DS stage.

The band finished their third album on February 26, 2013.<ref>{{cite web | title= Twitter / @dangeroussummer: New album is finished. | url=https://twitter.com/dangeroussummer/status/306423541232967680 | date=February 26, 2013  |publisher=[[Twitter]] }}</ref> 
<br>
They also began filming a documentary about the band and started a [[Kickstarter]] fund.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.drivenfaroff.com/2012/03/28/the-dangerous-summer-start-kickstarter-for-dvd|title=The Dangerous Summer Start Kickstarter For DVD|date=28 March 2012|website=drivenfaroff.com|access-date=2 April 2021}}</ref> The page hasn't been updated since 12 January 2014.

The album, ''Golden Record,'' was released on August 6, 2013. The first single, "Catholic Girls", was released on iTunes with an accompanying music video on June 4, 2013.<br>
In 2014, AJ Perdomo left the band, citing Cody Payne as the driving force behind the decision.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://underthegunreview.net/2014/03/29/aj-perdomo-parts-ways-with-the-dangerous-summer|title=AJ Perdomo Parts Ways With The Dangerous Summer|date=29 March 2014|website=underthegunreview.net|access-date=2 April 2021}}</ref>

In February 2017, Cody Payne was sentenced to a year in prison after being charged with a felony burglary conviction.<ref>{{cite web |title="We're Over All The Shit That Went On, We Feel Alive": The Dangerous Summer Return |url=https://www.kerrang.com/features/were-over-all-the-shit-that-went-on-we-feel-alive/ |website=[[Kerrang!]] |accessdate=September 18, 2020 |date=January 26, 2018 |quote=... Cody was arrested and jailed on charges including burglary.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=The Dangerous Summer have nothing left to prove |url=https://www.upsetmagazine.com/features/dangerous-summer-nothing-left-prove |website=[[Upset Magazine]] |publisher=The Bunker Publishing |accessdate=September 18, 2020 |language=en |date=February 1, 2018 |quote=... Cody Payne's arrest for burglary, ... Cody's sentencing in early 2017.}}</ref> In July 2017, the band's Twitter account was wiped clean of all tweets and the profile picture and cover photo changed to static. On July 8, 2017, the band uploaded a video that read "Hello",<ref>{{cite web|title=The Dangerous Summer Twitter|url=https://twitter.com/dangeroussummer/status/883728460614574080|website=Twitter.com|accessdate=July 9, 2017}}</ref> followed the next day by a video hinting at an announcement on July 10.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Dangerous Summer|url=https://twitter.com/dangeroussummer/status/884089241910747136|website=Twitter.com|accessdate=July 9, 2017}}</ref> Additionally, Cody Payne posted on Facebook that the band would be reuniting without him.<ref>{{cite web|title=Cody Michael Payne Facebook|url=https://m.facebook.com/story.php?story_fbid=3268900561104&id=1225020469&ref=content_filter|website=Twitter.com|accessdate= July 10, 2017}}</ref>

On October 19, 2017, The Dangerous Summer announced their next album ''The Dangerous Summer'', which was released on January 26, 2018, alongside a new single titled "Fire" from the upcoming album.

On February 19, 2019, the single "Where Were You When the Sky Opened Up" was released. The band's sixth album, ''Mother Nature'', was released on June 14, 2019.

In 2020, The Dangerous Summer ended their tenure with [[Hopeless Records]] and dropped their first self-released song "Fuck Them All" on Molly Water Music. After their drummer left they recruited [[Aaron Gillespie]] to finish the new EP and stand in for live shows.
