'''Dayseeker''' is an American [[post-hardcore]] band from [[Orange County, California]], United States. The band is currently signed to [[Spinefarm Records]]. To date, the band has released four albums: ''What It Means To Be Defeated'' in 2013, which was re-released a year later; ''Origin'' in 2015, which was also reissued in 2016; ''Dreaming Is Sinking /// Waking Is Rising'' in 2017; and ''Sleeptalk'' in 2019.

==History==

===Formation and debut album ''What It Means to Be Defeated'' (2012-2014)===
Dayseeker was formed in 2012 by [[Lead vocalist|vocalist]] Rory Rodriguez, [[guitar]]ists Alex Polk and Gino Sgambelluri, [[bass guitar|bassist]] Andrew Sharp and [[Drum kit|drummer]] Mike Karle in [[Orange, California|Orange]] near [[San Diego]], [[California]]. Rory Rodriguez and Alex Polk were members in a band called Arms Like Yours before forming Dayseeker.

The band released a series of demos including the songs "What It Means to Be Defeated", "Collision.Survive" and "Resurrect", which were released on their debut record as well as two untitled songs.<ref name="MEB">Steve Alcala: ''Minds Equal Blown'': [http://mindequalsblown.net/reviews/dayseeker-untitled-ep Dayseeker – ''Untitled'' EP Review]</ref> On July 7, 2013, the band was signed to [[inVogue Records]], which released their debut album ''[[What It Means to Be Defeated]]'' on October 29, 2013.<ref>Kriston McConnell: ''Under the Gun Review'': [http://www.underthegunreview.net/2013/07/07/dayseeker-sign-to-invogue-records-stream-new-song/ Dayseeker sign to InVogue Records, stream new song] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402150132/http://www.underthegunreview.net/2013/07/07/dayseeker-sign-to-invogue-records-stream-new-song/ |date=2015-04-02 }}</ref><ref>Michele Bird: ''[[Alternative Press (music magazine)|Alternative Press]]'': [http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/dayseeker_stream_new_album_what_it_means_to_be_defeated Dayseeker stream new album, 'What It Means To Be Defeated']</ref> It featured work by [[Joey Sturgis]], who mastered the record.<ref>''[[Allmusic]]'': [http://www.allmusic.com/album/what-it-means-to-be-defeated-mw0002582257/credits DAYSEEKER | What It Means to Be Defeated | Credits]</ref> On November 24, 2014 the album was re-released.<ref>Tyler Sharp: ''Alternative Press'': [http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/dayseeker_invogue_records_to_re_release_what_it_means_to_be_defeated Dayseeker (InVogue Records) to re-release ‘What It Means To Be Defeated’]</ref> [[Tom Denney]], a former guitarist of [[A Day to Remember]] was involved in producing the re-released version of the album. The re-issue features three acoustic tracks.<ref>''Top40-Charts.com'': [http://top40-charts.com/news.php?nid=104589 Dayseeker Re-Releasing "What It Means To Be Defeated" As Deluxe Edition On 11/24]</ref>

In November and December 2013 the band toured the [[United States]] on a three-week tour alongside Kingdom of Giants.<ref>''Lambgoat'': [http://lambgoat.com/news/comments.aspx?id=21448#0 Adestria, Kingdom of Giants, Dayseeker tour]</ref> In April and May 2014 the band was part of the Artery Across the Nation Tour, which featured [[Upon This Dawning]] and [[The Browning]] in the line-up.<ref>Matt Crane: ''Alternative Press'': [http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/upon_this_dawning_announce_u.s._headlining_tour Upon This Dawning announce U.S. headlining tour]</ref>

===''Origin'' (2014-2016)===
On December 23, 2014, the band announced they would be a part of the [[Hawthorne Heights]] tour between March and April 2015.<ref>Hayley Hoyle: ''Infectious Magazine'': [http://www.infectiousmagazine.com/hawthorne-heights-announces-acoustic-tour/ Hawthorne Heights Announces Acoustic Tour]</ref> After that tour, the band then shared the stage with [[Silent Planet]] in April and May 2015 to promote ''Origin''.<ref>Tyler Sharp: ''Alternative Press'': [http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/silent_planet_to_perform_the_night_god_slept_in_full_on_spring_headlining_t Silent Planet to perform 'The Night God Slept' in full on spring headlining tour]</ref>

On January 2015, the band announced they were to begin recording the follow-up to their debut album ''What It Means to Be Defeated'' with producer Nick Ingram, who had worked with [[Hotel Books]] and [[Before Their Eyes]] in the past.<ref name="MLA">Jeanette Hernandez: ''MUSE Local Artist'': [http://www.whosurmuse.com/m-u-s-e-local-artist-orange-county-rock-band-dayseeker/ M.U.S.E. Local Artist: Orange County Rock Band Dayseeker] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402142023/http://www.whosurmuse.com/m-u-s-e-local-artist-orange-county-rock-band-dayseeker/ |date=2015-04-02 }}</ref><ref name="MNN">Dan Harr: ''Musicnews Nashville'': [http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/dayseeker-releasing-new-lp-origin-on-april-21/ Dayseeker Releasing New LP 'Origin' on April 21] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402112106/http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/dayseeker-releasing-new-lp-origin-on-april-21/ |date=2015-04-02 }}</ref> On March 9, 2015, it was announced that the album was entitled ''[[Origin (Dayseeker album)|Origin]]'' and would be released on April 21, 2015. The same day the same-titled lead single as well as the pre-orders were announced, the band released a cover of [[Nick Jonas]]' song "[[Jealous (Nick Jonas song)|Jealous]]".<ref name="MNN" /><ref>Tyler Sharp: ''[[Alternative Press (music magazine)|Alternative Press]]'': [http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/dayseeker_invogue_records_announce_new_album_stream_nick_jonas_cover Dayseeker (InVogue Records) announce new album, stream Nick Jonas cover]</ref>

===Departure from InVogue and ''Dreaming Is Sinking / Waking Is Rising'' (2016-2018)===
The band members Alex Polk and Andrew Sharp departed from the band in the spring of 2016. Shawn Yates, former member of At the Skylines, served as the new guitarist. On December 15, 2016, the band announced their departure from InVogue Records and that they had signed with Spinefarm Records. Shortly after, they announced a US tour with headliner Silent Planet, along with Ghost Key and Hail the Sun, which began in February 2017. A year after the release of ''Origin'', Dayseeker announced a new full-length album set to release in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.spinefarmrecords.com/usa/news/2016/12/15/spinefarm-records-welcomes-dayseeker-to-the-roster/|title=&#124; SPINEFARM RECORDS WELCOMES DAYSEEKER TO THE ROSTER|website=Spinefarmrecords.com|accessdate=12 October 2020}}</ref> The album, ''Dreaming Is Sinking / Waking Is Rising'', was released by Spinefarm on July 14, 2017.<ref name="DIS iTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/dreaming-is-sinking-waking-is-rising/id1238535599|title=Dreaming Is Sinking / Waking Is Rising by Dayseeker on Apple Music|publisher=[[Apple Inc.]]|accessdate=July 14, 2017}}</ref>

===''Sleeptalk'' (2019-present)===
On September 27, 2019, Dayseeker released their fourth full length album entitled Sleeptalk.<ref name='AP Interview'>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/dayseeker-sleeptalk-interview/ |title=DAYSEEKER OPEN A PORTAL TO HIGHER GROUND ON ‘SLEEPTALK’ |date=2 October 2019 |work=altpres.com |publisher=Spinefarm |access-date=1 December 2020}}</ref>
