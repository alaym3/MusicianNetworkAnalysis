'''Free Throw''' is an American [[Emo music|emo]] band from [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. They are signed to [[Triple Crown Records]].

==History==
Free Throw was formed in April 2012 after the members had graduated high school and had met each other through the Nashville music scene while playing in previous bands. Vocalist Cory Castro described it as "a couple of beers and a couple of ideas".<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ek4px8VUXoE&t=1446s</ref> The band has since released four full-length albums, titled ''Those Days Are Gone,''<ref>{{cite web|last1=Anthony|first1=David|title=Stream all of Free Throw's debut album, Those Days Are Gone|url=http://www.avclub.com/article/stream-all-free-throws-debut-album-those-days-are--209010|publisher=A.V. Club|accessdate=19 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Sacher|first1=Andrew|title=Free Throw releasing an LP, touring w/ Empire! Empire! (dates and new song stream)|url=http://www.brooklynvegan.com/archives/2014/08/free_throw_rele.html|publisher=Brooklyn Vegan|accessdate=19 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Obenschain|first1=Philip|title=Free Throw stream debut full-length, 'Those Days Are Gone'|date=11 September 2014|url=http://www.altpress.com/news/entry/free_throw_stream_debut_full_length_those_days_are_gone|publisher=Alternative Press|accessdate=19 December 2014}}</ref> ''Bear Your Mind'', ''What's Past is Prologue'', and ''Piecing It Together''. They have also released three EPs, titled ''Free Throw'', ''Lavender Town'', and ''Missing Pieces''. After the release of their debut album, the band embarked on a tour opening for [[Empire! Empire! (I Was a Lonely Estate)]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Garza|first1=Mark|title=Free Throw To Tour With Empire! Empire!, Premiere New Single From Debut LP|url=http://www.underthegunreview.net/2014/08/21/free-throw-to-tour-with-empire-empire-premiere-new-single-from-debut-lp/|publisher=Under The Gun Review}}</ref> 

==Musical style and lyrics==
Free Throw's music has been described as [[emo]], [[punk rock]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/free-throw-mn0000197635/biography|title = Free Throw Biography, Songs, & Albums|website = [[AllMusic]]}}</ref> [[pop punk]], [[post-hardcore]] and [[indie rock]]. In an interview, vocalist Cory Castro defined the term "[[emo]]" as "taking your emotions and wearing them on your sleeve." The vocalist described the band's sound as being derived from "underground punk emo" bands such as [[Snowing (band)|Snowing]] and [[Algernon Cadwallader]], while also citing [[Fall Out Boy]] and [[My Chemical Romance]] as influences on the band's "pop sensibility".<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ek4px8VUXoE&t=1446s</ref> Lyrical themes often include topics such as alcoholism, addiction, relationship issues, and body image.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://observer.com/2017/05/emo-band-free-throw-singer-cory-castro-interview/|title=Free Throw is Crashing the Emo Revival Party, and They're Not Sorry|website=[[The New York Observer]]|date=12 May 2017}}</ref>

Vocalist Cory Castro is an avid fan of the ''[[Pokémon (franchise)|Pokémon]]'' video game franchise, and several of the band's song titles are direct references to the series. Examples include ''Lavender Town'', ''Pallet Town'', ''Cerulean City'', ''Better Have Burn Heal'', and ''Tail Whip, Struggle''. The frontman has a [[Poké Ball]] tattooed on his wrist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://observer.com/2017/05/emo-band-free-throw-singer-cory-castro-interview/|title=Free Throw is Crashing the Emo Revival Party, and They're Not Sorry|website=[[The New York Observer]]|date=12 May 2017}}</ref>
