'''Luke Albert Combs''' ({{IPAc-en|k|oʊ|m|z}};<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1fy7dvO007I</ref> born March 2, 1990) is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter. Born and raised in [[North Carolina]], Combs began performing as a child, most notably performing at [[Carnegie Hall]]. After dropping out of college to pursue a career in music, he moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], where he released his debut extended play, ''The Way She Rides'', in 2014.

In 2017, Combs released his debut album, ''[[This One's for You (Luke Combs album)|This One's for You]]'', which reached number four on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. Combs released his second album, [[What You See Is What You Get (Luke Combs album)|''What You See Is What You Get'']], on November 8, 2019. The album topped the charts in multiple territories, becoming his first to do so. A deluxe version of the album was released on October 23, 2020, including the song "[[Forever After All]]".

Combs' music has earned him two [[Grammy Award]] nominations, two [[IHeartRadio Music Awards|iHeart Radio music awards]], four [[Academy of Country Music Awards]] and six [[Country Music Association Awards]], including the 2021 [[Country Music Association Award for Entertainer of the Year|Entertainer of the Year]] award, their highest honor.

==Early life==
Combs, an [[only child]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rarecountry.com/awards/luke-combs-shares-his-cma-awards-success-with-his-mom-and-dad/|title=Luke Combs shares his CMA Awards success with his mom and dad|last=Kelly|first=Hunter|date=November 9, 2017|access-date=May 27, 2020|work=Rare Country}}</ref> was born in Huntersville, North Carolina, the son of Rhonda and Chester Combs,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.accessonline.com/articles/luke-combs-admits-wife-nicole-has-been-major-turning-point-in-his-career-after-earning-6-cma-nods|title=Luke Combs Admits Wife Nicole Has Been Major Turning Point in His Career After Earning 6 CMA Nods}}</ref> and moved to [[Asheville, North Carolina]], at the age of 8.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lukecombs.com/#!lukes-story/c11sx|title=The Official Website of Country Music Artist Luke Combs}}</ref> He has been performing as a [[Singing|vocalist]] since childhood. While attending [[A. C. Reynolds High School]], Combs played football and performed with multiple vocal groups, including performing a solo in the world-renowned [[Carnegie Hall]]. Later, he attended [[Appalachian State University]] where he worked as a bouncer at a bar before gaining stage time in that same bar.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.today.com/video/how-luke-combs-went-from-bouncer-to-bar-singer-in-college-79623237673|title=How Luke Combs went from bouncer to bar singer in college|website=TODAY.com|language=en|access-date=2020-03-04}}</ref> He played his first country music show at the Parthenon Cafe. With less than a month left in his senior year, he dropped out to pursue his country music career. He later moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], to pursue a career in music.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blueridgenow.com/article/20160311/entertainment/160319998?tc=ar|title=Luke Combs returns to perform in hometown|work=BlueRidgeNow.com}}</ref>

==Music==
Combs released his first EP, ''The Way She Rides'', in February 2014. Later that year, he released his second EP, ''Can I Get an Outlaw''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicbay.eu/artist/luke-combs/551907/|title=All discography of artist Luke Combs top albums of Luke Combs. Find best albums on musicbay.eu|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160424131805/http://www.musicbay.eu/artist/luke-combs/551907/|archive-date=2016-04-24}}</ref><ref name="allmusic"/> In 2016, Combs' first single, "[[Hurricane (Luke Combs song)|Hurricane]]", sold 15,000 copies in its first week,<ref name="roland">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/7550035/newcomer-luke-combs-blows-into-mainstream-with-hurricane|title=Newcomer Luke Combs Blows Into Mainstream With 'Hurricane'|last=Roland|first=Tom|date=October 25, 2016|work=Billboard}}</ref> and debuted at number 46 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite journal|date=June 25, 2015|title=Billboard Hot Country Songs|url=http://www.billboard.com/files/pdfs/bcu_mw_0625.pdf|journal=Billboard Country}}</ref> In November 2015, Combs released his third EP, ''[[This One's for You (Luke Combs EP)|This One's for You]]''. In late 2016, Combs was named one of Sounds Like Nashville's "Artists to Watch".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.soundslikenashville.com/lists/14-new-country-artists-to-watch-in-2017/ |title=14 New Country Artists to Watch in 2017 |date=December 15, 2016 |work=Sounds Like Nashville}}</ref>

Combs signed a deal with [[Sony Music Nashville]], and the debut single from ''This One's for You'', "[[Hurricane (Luke Combs song)|Hurricane]]", was re-released on Columbia Nashville.<ref name="roland" /> "Hurricane" reached number one on the country radio airplay chart on May 15, 2017, holding that spot for two weeks,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://headlineplanet.com/home/2017/05/21/luke-combs-hurricane-remains-1-country-radio-darius-rucker-enters-top-5/|title=Luke Combs' "Hurricane" Remains #1 At Country Radio; Darius Rucker Enters Top 5|date=2017-05-21|work=Headline Planet|access-date=2017-06-14}}</ref>

In June 2017, Combs released his major label debut album, also titled ''[[This One's for You (Luke Combs album)|This One's for You]]'' on [[Sony Music Nashville|Columbia Nashville]] and River House Artists.<ref name="allmusic"/> The album reached number one on ''Billboard''{{'}}s [[Top Country Albums]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7447068/luke-combs/chart?f=320|title=Luke Combs - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=www.billboard.com|language=en|access-date=2017-06-14}}</ref> and number five on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7447068/luke-combs/chart?f=305|title=Luke Combs - Chart history {{!}} Billboard|website=www.billboard.com|access-date=2017-06-14}}</ref> in its first week. The album's second single, "[[When It Rains It Pours (song)|When It Rains It Pours]]" was released to country radio on June 19, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/luke-combs-when-it-rains-it-pours/|title=Luke Combs, 'When It Rains It Pours' [Listen]|website=Taste of Country|access-date=2017-06-14}}</ref> It reached number one on the [[Country Airplay]] chart in October 2017. The album's third single, "[[One Number Away]]" was released to country radio on January 8, 2018. In June 2018, Combs released a deluxe version titled ''This One's for You Too'', with five bonus tracks, two of which ("[[She Got the Best of Me]]" and "[[Beautiful Crazy]]") were issued as singles with both reaching number-one. "[[Beautiful Crazy]]" reached number one on Country Airplay in February 2019, giving Combs five number-ones on the ''Billboard'' country chart with his first five singles.<ref name="billboard feb2019">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/8499758/luke-combs-beautiful-crazy-no-1-country-airplay-chart |title=Luke Combs Is First Artist to Send First Five Singles to No. 1 On Country Airplay Chart, With 'Beautiful Crazy'|date=February 25, 2019 |first= Jim |last=Asker|work=Billboard }}</ref> The song reached number one on three additional country charts &ndash; Hot Country Songs, Country Streaming Songs, and Country Digital Song Sales.<ref name="billboard feb2019"/>

On June 11, 2019, Combs was invited to become the newest member of the [[Grand Ole Opry]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/luke-combs-grand-ole-opry/|title=BREAKING: Luke Combs Invited to Join the Grand Ole Opry|first=Billy|last=Dukes|website=Taste of Country}}</ref>

In August 2019, ''This One's for You'' spent its 44th week at number one on [[Top Country Albums]], setting the record for the longest reign at the top of that chart by a male artist.<ref>[https://theboot.com/luke-combs-album-country-chart-record-no-1-beer-never-broke-my-heart/ Luke Combs' 'This One's for You' Spends 44th Week at No. 1, Sets New Record]</ref>

His second album [[What You See Is What You Get (Luke Combs album)|''What You See Is What You Get'']] was released on November 8, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|title=Luke Combs Announces His New Album's Release Date|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/luke-combs-new-album-details-release-date/|work=Taste of Country|last=Dukes|first=Billy|date=August 8, 2019}}</ref> The album has charted the singles "[[Beer Never Broke My Heart]]", "[[Even Though I'm Leaving]]",  "[[Does to Me]]", a duet with [[Eric Church]], and "[[Lovin' on You]]", all of which have reached number one on the [[Billboard Country Chart|US Country chart]].

In May 2020, Combs released the single "[[Six Feet Apart]]".<ref>https://saultonline.com/2017/06/northern-ontarios-two-day-summer-rock-concert-cancelled/</ref> The single quickly became popular to listeners as it illustrated the challenges of life during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/luke-combs-six-feet-apart-social-distancing-992555/|title = Luke Combs Releases Studio Version of 'Six Feet Apart'|magazine = [[Rolling Stone]]|date = May 2020}}</ref> In June 2020, Combs confirmed his next single would be "[[Lovin' on You]]", which was released on both his second album and the EP ''[[The Prequel (EP)|The Prequel]]'', which predated the album.

Combs announced a deluxe version of [[What You See Is What You Get (Luke Combs album)|''What You See Is What You Get'']], titled ''What You See Ain't Always What You Get'', with five new songs; it was released on October 23, 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Zisman|first=Erica|date=2020-10-23|title=Luke Combs: What You See Ain't Always What You Get - Deluxe Album Review|url=https://nycountryswag.com/luke-combs-album-review/|access-date=2020-11-30|website=NYCS|language=en-US}}</ref> The track "[[Forever After All]]" set streaming records in country music on [[Apple Music]] and [[Spotify]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.countrynow.com/luke-combs-shatters-streaming-records-with-what-you-see-aint-always-what-you-get|title=Luke Combs Shatters Streaming Records With 'What You See Ain't Always What You Get'|author=Black, Lauren Jo|website=Country Now|date=October 28, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In early 2016, Combs started dating Nicole Hocking, and the two became engaged in November 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/luke-combs-crosses-off-two-004557240.html|title=Luke Combs Crosses Off Two Bucket-List Items – and They're Both Classics|last1=Kruh|first1=Nacy|website=[[Yahoo!]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/luke-combs-is-engaged-to-girlfriend-nicole-hocking/|title=Luke Combs Is Engaged to Girlfriend Nicole Hocking |first=Wendy |last=Hermanson |date=November 29, 2018|work=Taste of Country}}</ref> They married in Florida on August 1, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mier |first1=Tomás |last2=Michaud |first2=Sarah |title=Luke Combs Marries Fiancée Nicole Hocking in Intimate Florida Ceremony: 'Here's to Forever' |url=https://people.com/country/luke-combs-marries-nicole-hocking/ |website=People.com |date=August 2, 2020}}</ref>

Combs began to struggle with [[anxiety]] and [[primarily obsessional obsessive compulsive disorder]] in adolescence through his college years; while it is something he has learned to control better, he still struggles with obsessive thoughts from time to time.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uDS3yKI8omM&feature=emb_title&ab_channel=AXSTV|title=Luke Combs Opens Up about Battling Anxiety on The Big Interview with Dan Rather.|date=November 30, 2020|website=YouTube|publisher=AXS TV|access-date=January 12, 2021}}</ref>
