'''Chord Paul Overstreet''' (born February 17, 1989)<ref name=tvg>{{cite web| title=Chord Overstreet|url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/chord-overstreet-305813/bio/|publisher=[[TV Guide]]|access-date=March 12, 2015}}</ref> is an American singer-songwriter, actor and musician. He is known for his role as [[Sam Evans]] on the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] television series ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]'' (2009–2015).

On August 26, 2016, he released his debut single, "Homeland", through [[Island Records]] and [[Safehouse Records]].

==Career==

===2009–2015: Beginnings and ''Glee''===
Overstreet began to pursue his passion of performing after his high school graduation in 2007. After two unsuccessful years, Overstreet started his acting career on the [[web series]] ''[[Private (Web series)|Private]]'' as Josh Hollis.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thewrap.com/television/column-post/chord-overstreet-joins-cast-glee-19556|title=Chord Overstreet Joins 'Glee' Cast|date=July 26, 2010|first=John|last=Consoli|publisher=[[Sharon Waxman|The Wrap]]|access-date=September 23, 2010}}</ref> He was also featured in an episode of ''[[iCarly]]'' titled "iSpeed Date" and starred in the unaired pilot of ''[[No Ordinary Family]]''.<ref name="Zap"/><ref name="Hump" /> His first film role was that of Teenage Boy in the 2009 [[Thriller (genre)|thriller]] ''[[The Hole (2009 film)|The Hole]]'', and he starred in the 2011 film ''[[A Warrior's Heart]]'' as Dupree, alongside [[Ashley Greene]] and [[Kellan Lutz]].<ref name="Hump">{{cite web|url=http://hollywoodcrush.mtv.com/2010/09/22/chord-overstreet-glee/|title='Glee'{{'s}} Chord Overstreet Sings His Way Into Our Hearts As This Week's Hump Day Hottie|date=September 22, 2010| first=Jocelyn|last=Vena|publisher=MTV. [[MTV Networks]]|access-date=September 23, 2010}}</ref>

[[File:Sam Evans.png|thumb|200px|right|Overstreet as Sam Evans on the [[Glee Live! In Concert!]] tour, 2011]]
Overstreet played the character of [[Sam Evans]], a transfer student and athlete, on the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|FOX]] comedy-drama television series ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]''.<ref name="Zap"/><ref>{{cite press release|url=http://foxflash.com/div.php/main/page?aID=1z2z2z268z7z8&ID=6500|title=Chord Overstreet Signs Up for New Directions|date=July 26, 2010|publisher=[[Fox Broadcasting Company]]|access-date=September 23, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101102205040/http://foxflash.com/div.php/main/page?aID=1z2z2z268z7z8&ID=6500|archive-date=November 2, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://ca.eonline.com/uberblog/marc_malkin/b194405_new_glee_star_chord_overstreet_wants.html|title=New ''Glee'' Star Chord Overstreet Wants to "Beat It"|date=August 10, 2010|first=Marc|last=Malkin|publisher=[[E!]]|access-date=September 23, 2010}}</ref> He landed the role after auditioning with the [[Commodores]]' song "[[Easy (Commodores song)|Easy]]" and [[Gavin DeGraw]]'s "[[I Don't Want to Be]]".<ref name="Vanity" /> He later sang "[[Billionaire (song)|Billionaire]]" by [[Travie McCoy]] featuring [[Bruno Mars]] as a studio test, and eventually sang this in his debut episode titled "[[Audition (Glee)|Audition]]". His cover of "[[Billionaire (song)|Billionaire]]" was released as a single and charted at #15 in [[Ireland]], #24 in [[Canada]], #28 in the United States, and #34 in [[Australia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chart-track.co.uk/index.jsp?c=p/musicvideo/music/archive/index_test.jsp&ct=240001&arch=t&lyr=2010&year=2010&week=39|title=Chart Track: TOP 50 SINGLES, WEEK ENDING 30 September 2010|publisher=[[Irish Singles Chart]]. GfK|access-date=October 2, 2010|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/68y655MR8?url=http://www.chart-track.co.uk/index.jsp?c=p%2Fmusicvideo%2Fmusic%2Farchive%2Findex_test.jsp&ct=240001&arch=t&lyr=2010&year=2010&week=39|archive-date=7 July 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2010-10-09/canadian-hot-100?order=gainer|title=Canadian Hot 100: Week of October 09, 2010 (Biggest Jump)|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|access-date=October 2, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2010-10-09/hot-100?order=gainer|title=Hot 100: Week of October 09, 2010 (Biggest Jump)|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|access-date=October 2, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://australian-charts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Glee+Cast&titel=Billionaire|title=Glee Cast - Billionaire (song)|publisher=australian-chart.com. Hung Medien|access-date=October 6, 2010}}</ref> On April 21, 2011, the video for [[Hot Chelle Rae]]'s "[[Tonight Tonight (Hot Chelle Rae song)|Tonight Tonight]]" was released on [[YouTube]] and featured a series of cameos of Overstreet alternately making out with a girl (whom he stole from the lead singer) on an office copier, DJing at a club, then playing guitar and mugging for the crowd with his brother, Hot Chelle Rae band member, Nash.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QzlNFcT2aOE Hot Chelle Rae - Tonight Tonight] JIVE Records, a unit of Sony Music Entertainment. Youtube.com. 2011.</ref>

On July 1, 2011, [[TVLine]]'s [[Michael Ausiello]] broke the news that Overstreet's contract option to become a series regular on ''Glee'' had not been picked up for season 3, but that he could possibly come back as an occasional guest star.<ref name="tvline.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.tvline.com/2011/07/glee-scoop-chord-overstreet-leaving/|title=Glee Exclusive: Darren Criss and Harry Shum Jr. In, Chord Overstreet Out!|last=Ausiello|first=Michael|date=July 1, 2011|work=[[TV Line]]|publisher=[[Mail.com Media]]|access-date=July 1, 2011}}</ref> In reaction to the announcement, fans launched a campaign to promote Overstreet's cover of "Billionaire" and various tags related to the actor, most notably "#DontCutTheChord",<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ology.com/screen/fight-keep-chord-overstreet-glee-has-begun|work=Screenology|date=July 5, 2011|title=The Fight To Keep Chord Overstreet On 'Glee' Has Begun!|access-date=July 15, 2011|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120908234147/http://www.ology.com/screen/fight-keep-chord-overstreet-glee-has-begun|archive-date=September 8, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> began trending on [[Twitter]]. The song made its way into the iTunes top 5 ''Glee'' charts during the campaign.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.masslive.com/television/index.ssf/2011/07/glee_chord_overstreet_fans_ral.html|work=MassLive|date=July 2, 2011|title={{-'}}Glee': Chord Overstreet fans rally for actor's role|access-date=July 6, 2011}}</ref>

At the 2011 [[San Diego Comic-Con International|Comic-Con]] event, ''Glee'' co-creator [[Brad Falchuk]] reported that they had offered Overstreet a ten-episode deal with a possibility to become a series regular at mid-season, but that Overstreet had declined the option.<ref>{{cite web|last=Webb|first=Matt|url=http://www.tvline.com/2011/07/glee-comic-con-michele-monteith-colfer-overstreet/|title=Glee Boss Sets the Record Straight on McKinley High's Mass Exodus|work=TVLine|date=July 24, 2011|access-date=May 8, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZSyAbCagsjY|title=Comic-Con 2011: Glee Panel 1/4|publisher=YouTube|date=July 24, 2011|access-date=May 8, 2012}}</ref> Overstreet later stated to Ausiello during an interview that he had decided to leave the show to focus on his music career, explaining: "They offered me the chance to come back for a few episodes, but there was nothing guaranteed so I decided to dive into the music thing."<ref>{{cite web|first=Michael|last=Ausiello|url=http://www.tvline.com/2011/08/chord-overstreet-interview-leaving-glee/|title=Exclusive: Chord Overstreet Speaks Out on Leaving Glee and What's Next (Music and The Middle!)|publisher=TVLine|date=August 25, 2011|access-date=May 8, 2012}}</ref> It was reported on October 18, 2011, and confirmed by series co-creator [[Ryan Murphy (writer)|Ryan Murphy]] six days later, that Overstreet would be returning to ''Glee'' as a recurring character starting with the third season's eighth episode.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tvline.com/2011/10/glee-chord-overstreet-to-return/|title=UPDATED Glee Exclusive: Chord Overstreet to Return in December!|last=Ausiello|first=Michael|date=October 18, 2011|work=[[TV Line]]|publisher=[[Mail.com Media]]|access-date=October 24, 2011}}</ref> On July 23, 2012, it was confirmed that Overstreet would be a series regular for the [[Glee (Season 4)|fourth season]] of ''Glee'', where he continued until the [[Glee (season 6)|last season]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tvline.com/2012/07/23/chord-overstreet-series-regular-glee-season-4/|title=''Glee'' It's Official: Chord Overstreet Now a Glee Series Regular|last=Ausiello|first=Michael|date=July 23, 2012|work=[[TV Line]]|access-date=July 23, 2012}}</ref>

===2015–present: Music career===
Chord has cited [[James Taylor]], [[David Gray (musician)|David Gray]], [[Jimmy Buffett]], [[Bob Dylan]], [[Paul Simon]], and [[Hall & Oates]] as musicians that inspire him.<ref name="Huffington Post">{{cite web|last1=Mahbub|first1=Mufsin|title=Chord Overstreet Talks 'Homeland,' His Father And Life After 'Glee'|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/chord-overstreet-talks-homeland-his-father-and_us_57df74bbe4b0d5920b5b2fe8|website=The Huffington Post|access-date=29 November 2016|date=19 September 2016}}</ref>
On December 15, 2015, Overstreet signed a recording contract with [[Safehouse Records]], a record label founded by [[Demi Lovato]], [[Nick Jonas]] and Phil McIntyre. He was the first artist to be signed to the label.<ref name="Teen Vogue">{{cite web|last1=Elizabeth|first1=De|title="Glee"'s Chord Overstreet Drops Video For Debut Single "Homeland"|url=http://www.teenvogue.com/story/chord-overstreet-homeland-music-video|website=Teen Vogue|access-date=29 November 2016}}</ref> He opened for Lovato and Jonas on several tour dates of their [[Future Now Tour]] in summer 2016.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Havens|first1=Lyndsey|title=Chord Overstreet Talks Going Off Script With His Identity, Plus New Nostalgia-Driven Track "Homeland"|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7496080/chord-overstreet-homeland|magazine=Billboard|date=6 September 2016|access-date=29 November 2016}}</ref>

On August 26, 2016, Chord released his first official solo single, "Homeland", through [[Island Records]] and Safehouse Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=Homeland - Single by Chord Overstreet|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/homeland-single/id1145923294|website=[[iTunes Store]] (US)|publisher=[[Apple Inc]]|access-date=7 September 2016}}</ref> Digital Journal called it "a fitting homage to his hometown", giving the song a five star rating.<ref name="DigitalJournal">{{cite web|last1=Papadatos|first1=Markos|title=Review: Chord Overstreet releases heartfelt debut single 'Homeland' (Includes first-hand account)|url=http://www.digitaljournal.com/entertainment/music/review-chord-overstreet-releases-heartfelt-new-single-homeland/article/476900|website=Digital Journal|access-date=12 December 2016|date=10 October 2016}}</ref> A music video for the song premiered through [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] on October 7, 2016.<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Stutz|first1=Colin|title=Chord Overstreet Returns to Nashville for 'Homeland' Video: Exclusive Premiere|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7534140/chord-overstreet-homeland-nashville-music-video|magazine=Billboard|date=7 October 2016|access-date=29 November 2016}}</ref> In a September 2016 interview with the [[Huffington Post]], Overstreet revealed that he was working on an EP, yet to be released.<ref name="Huffington Post" /> In December 2016, he released a cover of "All I Want For Christmas is a Real Good Tan", written by Overstreet's father and originally recorded by [[Kenny Chesney]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Blake|first1=Liza|title=Chord Overstreet Opts for a Caribbean Christmas With 'All I Want for Christmas Is a Real Good Tan'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7625485/chord-overstreet-all-i-want-for-christmas-is-a-real-good-tan|magazine=Billboard|date=16 December 2016|access-date=21 December 2016}}</ref> Overstreet released a new single, "Hold On", on February 3, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Hold On - Single by Chord Overstreet on Apple Music|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/hold-on-single/id1199518479|website=iTunes|access-date=3 February 2017|language=en}}</ref> The song peaked at number 31 on the Spotify Viral 50 chart and later earn double platinum certification by [[RIAA]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Chord Overstreet - Chart history - Billboard|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/7728903/chord-overstreet/chart?f=2206|website=www.billboard.com|access-date=7 June 2017|language=en}}</ref> He released his first EP consisting of four tracks on May 12, 2017, entitled ''Tree House Tapes''.<ref>{{cite web|title=''Tree House Tapes'' - EP|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/tree-house-tapes-ep/id1233373062|publisher=[[Apple Inc.|Apple]]|access-date=May 17, 2017}}</ref>

In June 2017, he was picked as [[Elvis Duran]]'s [[Elvis Duran's Artist of the Month|Artist of the Month]] and was featured on [[NBC]]'s ''[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]'' show broadcast nationally where he performed live his single "Hold On". He also performed the song on [[The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon]] on August 14, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Chaberski|first1=Chris|title=Chord Overstreet Performs 'Hold On' for 'The Tonight Show,' The Mooch Does Colbert: ICYMI|url=http://popcrush.com/chord-overstreet-hold-on-tonight-show-icymi/|website=PopCrush|access-date=15 August 2017}}</ref>
