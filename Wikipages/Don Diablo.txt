'''Don Pepijn Schipper''' ({{IPA-nl|ˈdɔn pɛˈpɛin ˈsxɪpər}}; born 27 February 1980), better known by his stage name '''Don Diablo''', is a [[Dutch people|Dutch]] DJ, record producer, musician and songwriter of [[electronic dance music]] from [[Coevorden]]. He is one of the pioneers of the [[future house]] genre and was ranked sixth in the Top 100 DJs – 2020 list by ''[[DJ Mag]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Top 100 DJs|url=https://djmag.com/top100djs|access-date=2020-12-09|website=DJMag.com}}</ref> He was also ranked number one Producer of the Year for 2018 by 1001Tracklists.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.1001tracklists.com/charts/year/2018/producer.html|title=Top 101 Producers Of 2018|date=30 September 2018}}</ref> In 2016, he was ranked the number one Future House Artist of the Year on [[Beatport]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.youredm.com/2016/12/29/don-diablo-named-1-future-house-artist-year-beatport/|title=Say Hello To The Newly Announced #1 Future House DJ In The World|date=29 December 2016}}</ref>

== Musical career ==
=== 1995–2005: Early years ===
Diablo played in clubs all over the world, and at various festivals.{{cn|date=August 2021}}

In 2004, he launched his separate music project called Divided. The project had two hits "The Music, the People" credited to Divided featuring Diablo in 2004, followed by "[[Easy Lover]]" in 2005, credited to Diablo presents Divided, a dance remake of the joint hit of [[Philip Bailey]] and [[Phil Collins]] in 1985, Divided remake coming after twenty years of the release of the original.

=== 2006–2009: ''Life Is a Festival'' ===
In 2006, Diablo was licensed to the UK top 3 chart hit "[[Exceeder]]" by [[Mason (musician)|Mason]]. The record was originally signed to Middle of the Road, a record label set up by Diablo and Amsterdam-based club promoter Roy Avni (Electro Nation). In 2008, Diablo released his debut album ''“Life Is a Festival''.
In 2009, Diablo released "[[Hooligans (song)|Hooligans]]", a collaboration with English singer, songwriter, rapper and record producer [[Example (musician)|Example]].

=== 2010–2013 ===
In 2010, a collaboration with [[Dragonette]] resulted in the hit single "[[Animale]]" that charted in the Dutch Singles Chart as well as the Belgian [[Ultratip]] chart.

In 2013 Diablo released new songs on [[Axwell]]'s Axtone label, [[Nicky Romero]]'s Protocol Recordings, [[Steve Angello]]'s Size X imprint and [[Spinnin' Records]] label. He created the theme song for the 2013 video game ''[[Batman: Arkham Origins]]''.

His song "Starlight (Could You Be Mine)", made with UK DJ/producer Matt Nash, was debuted during the last [[Swedish House Mafia]] concert, after Axwell signed it to his label.<ref>{{cite web|last=Berecz|first=Aaron|title=Don Diablo & Matt Nash - Starlight (Could You Be Mine)|url=http://www.djmag.ca/blog/2013/07/26/don-diablo-matt-nash-starlight-could-you-be-mine/|work=DJ Mag Canada|access-date=26 July 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150418185807/http://djmag.ca/blog/2013/07/26/don-diablo-matt-nash-starlight-could-you-be-mine/|archive-date=18 April 2015}}</ref> The song became a Beatport Top 5 hit, and later several of his other releases reaching the Beatport Top 10.<ref>{{cite web|title=Beatport Top Tracks August 6th 2013|url=http://www.bptoptracker.com/top/top100/0/2013-08-06#|work=Beatport|date=6 August 2013}}</ref> The music video, directed by Kyle Padilla, was published on 7 February 2014, around seven months after the song's release.

During 2013 [[Pete Tong]] debuted six of Don's releases on his BBC Radio 1 show. He had a residency at Las Vegas' night club "Light", played the Departures Ibiza closing party alongside [[Axwell]] and [[Sebastian Ingrosso]], and toured the UK with [[Nicky Romero]]. He had a sold out solo tour in Asia and the USA.

=== 2014–2016 ===
In 2014, Diablo premiered his new visual concept "The Hexagon" with Los Angeles-based visual company "VSquared Labs".

In October 2014, Diablo made his debut on DJ Mag's Top 100 DJs list at No. 82.<ref>{{cite web|title=DJ Mag Top 100 DJs 2014 - Don Diablo|url=https://djmag.com/content/poll-2014-don-diablo|work=DJ Mag|date=13 October 2014}}</ref> In October 2015, Diablo was awarded with [[DJ Magazine]]'s "highest climber of the year" prize, jumping 52 places to No. 30 on the Top 100 DJs list for 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=DJ Mag Top 100 DJs 2015 - Don Diablo|url=https://djmag.com/top-100-djs/poll-2015-don-diablo|work=DJ Mag|date=9 October 2015}}</ref>

Diablo launched his Hexagon record label. The first release, a remix of [[Alex Adair]]'s "[[Make Me Feel Better (Alex Adair song)|You Make Me Feel Better]]" held the no. 1 position on Beatport for almost two weeks and the label was awarded "best new label of the year".<ref>{{cite web|title=Beatport Breakthrough Labels of 2015: EDM|url=https://www.beatport.com/chart/breakthrough-labels-of-2015-edm/377832|work=Beatport|date=2 December 2015}}</ref>

In 2015 his HEXAGON radio show was aired in over 35 countries and holds a top 10 position in the iTunes podcast section every week.

Diablo has directed all of his own music videos. In 2013 Warner Brothers asked Don to create the worldwide theme song for the much acclaimed ''[[Batman: Arkham Origins]]'' game.

In 2015 Diablo collaborated with fellow Dutch DJ [[Tiësto]] on the track "[[Chemicals (Tiësto and Don Diablo song)|Chemicals]]" featuring vocals from Danish singer and producer [[Thomas Troelsen]].

Throughout 2016 and 2017, Diablo went on Tour Across Europe, this sold out across multiple venues including his first headline show in London.

=== 2017: ''Past.Present.Future'' ===
In 2017, Don Diablo released ''Past.Present.Future'', a compilation of previously released tracks, including hits "On My Mind," "Cutting Shapes," and his collaboration with [[Tiësto]] "Chemicals" featuring Thomas Troelsen.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/7FptVAJOcON7vHzDVT3Z57?si=xJMZvlZSTsu4wj1lWENtvw|title=Past.Present.Future |website=Spotify|access-date=2018-07-12}}</ref>

Diablo won DJ Mag's 2017 Highest Future House award and was ranked #11 overall in the [[DJ Mag]] Top 100 Poll.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://djmag.com/news/don-diablo-wins-dj-mag-top-100-djs-2017-highest-future-house |title=Don Diablo Wins DJ Mag Top 100 DJs 2017 Highest Future House |website=DJ Mag|access-date=2018-07-12}}</ref>

A packed global tour schedule saw him play the mainstages of Tomorrowland, EDC, Creamfields, Mysteryland, Lollapalooza, and Ultra Music Festival whilst selling out his Headline Artist shows in the UK, US & European markets.

On March 17, 2017, Don Diablo premiered a new collaboration with [[MARNIK]] and [[Galantis]]' unaccredited [[No Money (song)|''No Money'']] singer Reece Bullimore, titled "Children of a Miracle".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vaunter.co/music/electronic/don-diablo-marnik-new-single/0161510|title=Don Diablo partners with Marnik for new single, 'Children Of A Miracle'|website=Vaunter|access-date=2017-03-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170319061208/https://vaunter.co/music/electronic/don-diablo-marnik-new-single/0161510|archive-date=2017-03-19|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On September 6, 2017, Don Diablo released a single with Nashville-based singer / songwriter Holly Winter, titled "Don't Let Go".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dNfU2vjecz4|title=Don Diablo Ft Holly Winter, 'Don't Let Go'|website=Youtube|access-date=2017-09-06}}</ref>

In November 2017, Diablo released “[[Take Her Place]]” with electronic trio Arizona.

On December 15, 2017, he released his sixth single, entitled "You Can't Change Me". The music video stars BONETICS, a dancer that competed on [[Britain's Got Talent]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bBnUgWc7k-k|title=Don Diablo - You Can't Change Me Official Music Video|website=YouTube|access-date=2018-07-12}}</ref>

=== 2018–present: ''Future'' and ''Forever'' ===
On January 19, Don Diablo released the seventh single from ''Future'', "People Say", with actor/songwriter, Paije Richardson.

On February 8, he embarked on his sold out Future North American Tour, making stops at Los Angeles' Fonda Theatre and New York's Terminal 5, among others.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://djmag.com/news/don-diablo-announces-future-north-america-tour |title=Don Diablo Announces 2018 North American Tour |date=2017-11-10 |website=DJ Mag |access-date=2018-07-12}}</ref>

His second studio album, ''[[Future (Don Diablo album)|Future]]'', was released via his label Hexagon on 9 February 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.youredm.com/2018/01/20/don-diablo-reveals-title-release-date-highly-anticipated-album/ |title=Don Diablo Reveals His New Album At Last |date=2018-01-20 |website=Your EDM |access-date=2018-01-25}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/DonDiablo/status/958057553694478337 |title=FUTURE: 09.02.2018 |author=Diablo, Don |website=Twitter |access-date=2018-01-30 |date=30 January 2018}}</ref>

On March 13, he released the eighth single to ''Future'' entitled "Head Up" with singer [[James Newman (musician)|James Newman]]. On March 23, he released the ninth single to ''FUTURE'' entitled "Believe", available on the deluxe edition (released same day) with actor/singer-songwriter [[Ansel Elgort]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/8258193/don-diablo-future-deluxe-edition-ansel-elgort-believe |title=Don Diablo Drops FUTURE Deluxe Edition Feat. Ansel Elgort on Believe: Listen |date=2018-03-23 |website=Billboard |access-date=2018-07-12}}</ref> On April 25, he released the tenth single to ''FUTURE'' entitled "Give Me Love" with singer [[Calum Scott]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://weraveyou.com/2018/04/don-diablo-calum-scott-give-love/ |title=Don Diablo - Give Me Love (feat. Calum Scott) |date=2018-04-25 |website=We Rave You |access-date=2018-07-12}}</ref>

On May 11, his remix of [[Kygo]] and [[The Night Game]]'s song "[[Kids in Love (song)|Kids In Love]]" was released.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://itunes.apple.com/nl/album/kids-in-love-don-diablo-remix-single/1380753903 |title=Kids in Love (Don Diablo Remix) - Single |date=2018-05-11 |website=Apple Music |access-date=2018-07-12}}</ref> On July 12, he released his single "No Good", in partnership with artist Zonderling. On August 3, he released his new single, Heaven to Me, featuring Alex Clare.
On September the 9th, he released a single called "Survive" featuring Emeli Sandé and Gucci Mane.

On October 15, 2018, he ranked #1 as the world's most DJ-supported producer<ref name=":0" /> according to 1001Tracklists, a website logging DJ support from festival recordings, radio shows, and DJ mixes.

Diablo announced his third studio album ''Forever'' in mid-2019, but it was not released. He planned a world tour.<ref name="Forever">{{cite web|url=https://djmag.com/news/don-diablo-announces-new-album-and-world-tour|title=Don Diablo announces new album and world tour|work=[[DJ Mag]]|last=Devlin|first=Kieran|date=13 June 2019|access-date=14 June 2019}}</ref>

On September 18, 2020, launched ''Camp Kubrick'' with Denzel Chain, an 80s style inspired duo releasing the single "Johnny's Online".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Stone|first=Katie|date=2020-09-18|title=Don Diablo and Denzel Chain Launch 80's-Inspired "Camp Kubrick" Project with New Single "Johnny's Online"|url=https://edm.com/music-releases/don-diablo-denzel-chain-camp-kubrick-johnnys-online|access-date=2020-09-20|website=EDM.com - The Latest Electronic Dance Music News, Reviews & Artists|language=en}}</ref>
