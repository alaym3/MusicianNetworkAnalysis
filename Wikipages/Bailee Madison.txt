'''Bailee Madison''' (born October 15, 1999)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gettyimages.no/detail/news-photo/bailee-madison-celebrates-her-13th-birthday-on-october-20-news-photo/154489850|title=Bailee Madison celebrates her 13th birthday on October 20, 2012 in...|website=Getty Images}}</ref> is an American actress and singer. She first gained acclaim for her role as [[May Belle Aarons]] in the fantasy drama film ''[[Bridge to Terabithia (2007 film)|Bridge to Terabithia]]'' (2007). Madison received further recognition for her starring roles as Isabelle in the war drama film ''[[Brothers (2009 film)|Brothers]]'' (2009), Sally Hurst in the horror film ''[[Don't Be Afraid of the Dark (2010 film)|Don't Be Afraid of the Dark]]'' (2010), Maggie in the romantic comedy film ''[[Just Go with It]]'' (2011), Harper Simmons in the comedy film ''[[Parental Guidance (film)|Parental Guidance]]'' (2012), Clementine in the fantasy film ''[[Northpole (film)|Northpole]]'' (2014), Kinsey in the slasher film ''[[The Strangers: Prey at Night]]'' (2018), and Avery in the [[Netflix]] film ''[[A Week Away]]'' (2021).

On television, she appeared as [[Maxine Russo]] in the fantasy sitcom ''[[Wizards of Waverly Place]]'' (2011), young [[Snow White]] in the fantasy drama series ''[[Once Upon a Time (TV series)|Once Upon a Time]]'' (2012–16), Hillary Harrison in the sitcom ''[[Trophy Wife (TV series)|Trophy Wife]]'' (2013–14), and Sophia Quinn in the drama series ''[[The Fosters (2013 TV series)|The Fosters]]'' (2014–16). From 2015 to 2021, Madison starred as Grace Russell in the [[Hallmark Channel]] comedy-drama series ''[[Good Witch (TV series)|Good Witch]]''. In July 2021, it was announced that she would be a lead in ''[[Pretty Little Liars: Original Sin]]'', a reboot series of ''[[Pretty Little Liars]]''.

==Early life==
Madison was born in [[Fort Lauderdale, Florida]],<ref name="issue">{{Cite magazine | title = Bailee Madison | url = https://issuemagazine.com/bailee_madison/ | magazine = Issue Magazine | access-date = October 10, 2019 |quote=Where are you from? I was born in Fort Lauderdale, Florida! I grew up in a town called Lauderdale by the Sea, and I still go home whenever I can.}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fortlauderdaledaily.com/features/fort-lauderdale-native-and-actress-bailee-madison-talks-us-about-growing-south-florida-her|title=Bailee Madison Talks To Us About Growing Up In South Florida, Her Career And More|website=Fort Lauderdale Daily|language=en|access-date=August 20, 2019}}</ref> the youngest of seven children. She has four brothers and two sisters. Her older sister, [[Kaitlin Vilasuso]], is also an actress. Her mother is Patricia Riley.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.childreninfilm.com/Fame-Success-Stories-Bailee.aspx |title=Children in Film |access-date=March 12, 2014}}</ref> She began her career when she was two weeks old, appearing in an [[Office Depot]] commercial. Since then, she has appeared in several national commercials for major companies including [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]], [[SeaWorld]], and [[Cadillac]]. She also serves as a national youth spokesperson for the childhood-cancer charity [[Alex's Lemonade Stand|Alex's Lemonade Stand Foundation]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.channelguidemagblog.com/index.php/2011/12/09/bailee-madison-returns-to-the-hubs-haunting-hour-with-scarecrow/|author=Stacey Harrison|title=Bailee Madison returns to The Hub's "Haunting Hour" with "Scarecrow"|work=channelguidemag.com|date=December 9, 2011|access-date=December 12, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-o7nu1fTi0|author=I Am Second|title=I Am Second Bailee Madison – Child Actress|work=youtube.com|access-date=April 17, 2011}}</ref>

==Career==
===2000s===
[[File:Bailee Madison.jpg|thumb|left|upright|Madison at the Faith and Values Awards Gala in 2008]]
Madison made her major motion picture debut in the drama film ''[[Lonely Hearts (2006 film)|Lonely Hearts]]'', in which she plays Rainelle, a girl thrown into the midst of a world of crime. Madison also has a significant role in the [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]] fantasy drama film ''[[Bridge to Terabithia (2007 film)|Bridge to Terabithia]]'', portraying May Belle Aarons, the younger sister of the lead male character played by [[Josh Hutcherson]]. She also appears in the [[independent film]] ''[[Look (2007 film)|Look]]'' as Megan, a girl who is being watched and followed by a kidnapper.

Madison had a small role in ''[[Terminator: The Sarah Connor Chronicles]]'' during the season 1 finale, "What He Beheld", as a girl who becomes part of a short-lived hostage situation. In the DVD extras, it is said that they thought about changing the role slightly because of its violent nature, but she insisted that they not. In 2007, Madison appeared in [[Nickelodeon]]'s television film ''[[The Last Day of Summer (2007 film)|The Last Day of Summer]]''. She plays a girl who helps a friend during a difficult time. She also appeared in another Nickelodeon production, the television Christmas special ''[[Merry Christmas, Drake & Josh]]'', in which she played a girl who lived with a foster family.

Madison is seen in a featured supporting role in the 2009 drama film ''[[Brothers (2009 film)|Brothers]]'' as Isabelle Cahill, the older daughter of Captain Sam Cahill ([[Tobey Maguire]]) and Grace Cahill ([[Natalie Portman]]). For her portrayal in the film, she was nominated for both a [[Saturn Award]] and a [[BFCA|BFCA Critics' Choice Award]]. In 2010, Madison was in the drama film ''[[Letters to God]]'', where she portrayed Samantha Perryfield, best friend of the lead character who is fighting cancer.

===2010s===
[[File:Bailee Madison 2012.jpg|thumb|upright|Madison at the Golden Globes Gift Lounge on January 14, 2012]]
She is seen in ''[[Conviction (2010 film)|Conviction]]'' (2010), playing the younger version of [[Hilary Swank|Hilary Swank's]] character, Betty Anne Waters. She can be seen in the episode "Locum" from ''[[Law & Order: Special Victims Unit]]'' as Mackenzie Burton, a girl who was adopted and feels like she is only filling the place of her parents' older daughter who was abducted years ago. She appeared in three episodes of the children's horror series, ''[[The Haunting Hour: The Series]]''.

Also in 2010, Madison starred in her first leading role in the supernatural horror film ''[[Don't Be Afraid of the Dark (2011 film)|Don't Be Afraid of the Dark]]''. She starred alongside [[Katie Holmes]] and [[Guy Pearce]], portraying the role of Sally Hurst, a lonely withdrawn child who is sent to live with her father and his new girlfriend. [[Roger Ebert]] of the ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]'' gave the film 3½ stars out of 4, calling it "a very good haunted house film" and adding that it "milks our frustration deliciously."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://rogerebert.suntimes.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20110824/REVIEWS/110829997|work=Chicago Sun-Times|title=Don't Be Afraid of the Dark}}</ref>

In 2011, Madison had a recurring role in the [[Disney Channel]] sitcom ''[[Wizards of Waverly Place]]'' as Maxine, the girl form of [[Max Russo]]. She also appeared in ''[[Just Go with It]]'', alongside [[Adam Sandler]] and [[Jennifer Aniston]], as Maggie Murphy, a young girl who wants to become an actress. Madison appeared in the drama film ''[[An Invisible Sign]]'', as the younger version of [[Jessica Alba|Jessica Alba's]] character.

In 2012, Madison appeared in the [[Hallmark]] film ''[[A Taste of Romance]]'' alongside [[Teri Polo]] and [[James Patrick Stuart]]. She also had a guest role as the younger version of [[Ginnifer Goodwin]]'s character [[Snow White]] in the TV series ''[[Once Upon a Time (TV series)|Once Upon a Time]]''. She also could be seen as the main character in the film ''[[Cowgirls n' Angels]]'' where she played the feisty and rebellious Ida Clayton who has fantasies of finding her father, a rodeo rider. Madison appeared in another Hallmark film entitled ''Smart Cookie'' as Daisy, an eager girl hoping to prove herself as a Girl Scout. In December, she appeared in the family comedy film ''[[Parental Guidance (film)|Parental Guidance]]'', alongside [[Billy Crystal]] and [[Bette Midler]].

In 2013, Madison began playing Hillary on the TV sitcom ''[[Trophy Wife (TV series)|Trophy Wife]]'', replacing Gianna LePera who played the character in the pilot.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bailee Madison Trophy Wife|url=https://www.ign.com/articles/2013/09/09/trophy-wife-pilot-review|publisher=IGN|access-date=January 22, 2014}}</ref> In 2014, she began playing the recurring role of Sophia Quinn in the [[ABC Family]] drama ''[[The Fosters (2013 TV series)|The Fosters]]''. In 2015, she began playing Grace Russell, the daughter of Cassie Nightingale, in the Hallmark series ''[[Good Witch (TV series)|Good Witch]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title='Good Witch' Star Bailee Madison Teases Spellbinding Season 2|url=http://www.ibtimes.com/good-witch-star-bailee-madison-teases-season-2-drama-mysterious-newcomers-her-2354624|website=International Business Times|access-date=October 8, 2016|date=April 15, 2016}}</ref> Madison continued in the role until the end of the fifth season in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tvline.com/2019/07/03/good-witch-bailee-madison-leaving-grace-season-6/|title=Good Witch Twist: Bailee Madison Exits Ahead of Just-Ordered Season 6|first1=Michael|last1=Ausiello|date=July 3, 2019}}</ref>

In 2017, it was announced that Madison would star in an adaptation of Rachel Bateman's novel ''[[Someone Else's Summer]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last1=McNary|first1=Dave|title=Bailee Madison to Star in 'Someone Else's Summer' Movie Adaptation (EXCLUSIVE)|date=May 5, 2017|url=https://variety.com/2017/film/news/bailee-madison-someone-elses-summer-movie-1202410698/|access-date=May 29, 2018}}</ref>

In 2018, Madison portrayed the role of Kinsey, a rebellious child in the [[slasher film|slasher]] film ''[[The Strangers: Prey at Night]].'' She starred alongside [[Christina Hendricks]] and [[Martin Henderson]], as her parents. Although the film received a mixed response from critics, Madison's performance was praised and was relatively successful at the box office.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bleedingcool.com/2018/03/09/strangers-prey-night-review/|title=Review – Strangers: Prey at Night is Why Some Films Don't Need Sequels|date=March 9, 2018|work=Bleeding Cool News And Rumors|access-date=March 11, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/03/film-review-the-strangers-prey-at-night-is-a-quick-bloody-slasher-throwback/|title=Film Review: The Strangers: Prey at Night is a Quick, Bloody Slasher Throwback|date=March 8, 2018|work=Consequence of Sound|access-date=March 11, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

===2020s===
In 2021, Madison played the female lead in a teen Christian musical drama, ''[[A Week Away]]'', alongside [[Kevin Quinn (actor)|Kevin Quinn]]. Netflix released the film on March 26. Madison is set to appear as Imogen Abel in the [[HBO Max]] series ''[[Pretty Little Liars: Original Sin]]'', a reboot of ''[[Pretty Little Liars]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Del Rosario|first=Alexandra|date=July 27, 2021|title='Pretty Little Liars: Original Sin' Sets Bailee Madison To Star In HBO Max Reboot|url=https://deadline.com/2021/07/pretty-little-liars-original-sin-bailee-madison-hbo-max-reboot-1234800805/|access-date=August 12, 2021|website=Deadline|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Other ventures==
Madison has worked in [[voice-over|voice over]], with such projects as ''Breathe Bible''.<ref>[http://breathebible.com/voices/bailee-madison "Bailee Madison, The Voice of Young Mary"], BreatheBible.com; retrieved September 19, 2017.</ref>

Madison has served as the national youth spokesperson for [[Alex's Lemonade Stand Foundation]] since 2010, an organization that encourages kids to fundraise and spread awareness of pediatric cancer by running their own lemonade stands.<ref name="foxnews">[http://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/2012/06/12/young-actress-bailee-madison-sells-lemonade-for-charity-celebrates-girl-scouts "Young actress Bailee Madison sells lemonade for charity, celebrates Girl Scouts 100th anniversary with new role"], FoxNews.com, June 12, 2012.</ref> In January 2018, Madison's first novel was published, ''Losing Brave'', a [[Young adult fiction|Young-Adult]] Mystery which was co-written with Stefne Miller.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/34460488-losing-brave|title=Losing Brave|website=www.goodreads.com|access-date=January 29, 2019}}</ref> Since November 2018, she has co-hosted the podcast ''Just Between Us'' with her sister [[Kaitlin Vilasuso]].<ref>{{cite web |title=PodcastOne: Just Between Us with Bailee Madison & Kaitlin Vilasuso |url=https://www.podcastone.com/just-between-us |publisher=[[PodcastOne]] |access-date=January 21, 2019}}</ref>
