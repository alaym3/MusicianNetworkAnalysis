'''Movements''' are a [[post-hardcore]]<ref name="AXS">{{cite web|url=https://axs.com/watch-alt-rock-band-movements-discuss-how-mental-health-plays-a-part-i-124857|title=Watch alt-rock band Movements discuss how mental health plays a part in their lyrics and new music|first=Melanie|last=Moccia|date=November 1, 2017|publisher=[[AXS TV]]|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180202012248/https://www.axs.com/watch-alt-rock-band-movements-discuss-how-mental-health-plays-a-part-i-124857|archivedate=2 February 2018}}</ref> band from [[Rancho Santa Margarita, California]], formed in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fearlessrecords.com/artists/movements/|title=Movements - Fearless Records|publisher=Fearless Records|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201075207/http://www.fearlessrecords.com/artists/movements/|archivedate=1 February 2018}}</ref>

The band was signed to a record contract with [[Fearless Records]] after playing just one show in 2015. They have released an EP, ''[[Outgrown Things]]'' (2016), and two albums, ''[[Feel Something (Movements album)|Feel Something]]'' (2017) and ''[[No Good Left to Give]]'' (2020). ''Feel Something'' peaked at 190 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart.

==History==
Influenced by bands such as [[Title Fight]], [[Oceana (band)|Oceana]], [[Thrice]] and [[Balance and Composure]], Guitarist Brett Chiodo and Vocalist Patrick Miranda got together to finally create a band after jamming together casually for years. <ref name="form">{{Cite web |url=https://www.rocksound.tv/features/read/movements-patrick-miranda-i-dont-think-i-would-be-alive-if-it-wasnt-for-mus |title=Movements' Patrick Miranda: "I Don't Think I Would Be Alive If It Wasn't For Music |last=Rogers |first=Jack |date=January 8, 2018 |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207020024/https://www.rocksound.tv/features/read/movements-patrick-miranda-i-dont-think-i-would-be-alive-if-it-wasnt-for-mus |archivedate=February 7, 2018}}</ref>
Movements was formed in January 2015 by guitarist Brett Chiodo and Patrick Miranda. Brett Chiodo recruited drummer Spencer York, and Patrick Miranda, having been in a band together before, recruited bassist Austin Cressey.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/movements-mn0001584720/biography|title=Movements - Biography & History - AllMusic|first=Timothy|last=Monger|website=AllMusic|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131201126/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/movements-mn0001584720/biography|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://killthemusic.net/blog/fearless-records-signs-movements |title=Fearless Records Signs Movements |last=Rogers |first=Ami |date=August 12, 2015 |website=KillTheMusic.com |access-date=April 3, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180404035847/https://killthemusic.net/blog/fearless-records-signs-movements |archivedate=April 4, 2018}}</ref>

They self-released their first single, "Protection" on January 31, 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://movementsofficial.bandcamp.com/track/protection |title=Protection - MOVEMENTS on Bandcamp |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207005649/https://movementsofficial.bandcamp.com/track/protection |archivedate=2018-02-07}}</ref> Guitarist Brett Chiodo wrote the lyrics and music for “Protection” as well as created the band name. Vocalist Patrick Miranda and Brett Chiodo knew it was meant to be as soon as Patrick sang his melodies over the music for the first time. <ref name="form"/> The band played their first show opening for [[Have Mercy (band)|Have Mercy]] in March 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.facebook.com/MovementsCA/photos/a.716784178418308.1073741829.699061980190528/756621271101265/?type=3 |title=Movements on Facebook: We play our first show as a band tomorrow at Chain Reaction with Have Mercy and other amazing bands. |last=Miranda |first=Patrick |date=March 29, 2015 |access-date=April 3, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180404035847/https://www.facebook.com/MovementsCA/photos/a.716784178418308.1073741829.699061980190528/756621271101265/?type=3 |archivedate=April 4, 2018}}</ref><ref name="form"/> A second single, "Buried" was released on March 17.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://movementsofficial.bandcamp.com/track/buried |title=Buried - MOVEMENTS on Bandcamp |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207005955/https://movementsofficial.bandcamp.com/track/buried |archivedate=2018-02-07}}</ref> A third single, "Scripted" was released on April 14.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://movementsofficial.bandcamp.com/track/scripted |title=Scripted - MOVEMENTS on Bandcamp |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207062859/https://movementsofficial.bandcamp.com/track/scripted |archivedate=2018-02-07}}</ref> After only one local gig, the band signed a three record deal with [[Fearless Records]] in August 2015.<ref name="CPF"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/fearless_records_sign_movements|title=Fearless Records signs Movements|first=Chelsea|last=Scofield|date=August 12, 2015|website=Alternative Press|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131201419/https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/fearless_records_sign_movements|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref> The band chose Fearless over [[Hopeless Records]].<ref name="CPF">{{Cite web |url=http://casualpunkfan.com/2016/02/09/patrick-miranda/ |title=Patrick Miranda: Movements and Growing Up Fast |date=February 9, 2016 |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207122243/http://casualpunkfan.com/2016/02/09/patrick-miranda/ |archivedate=February 7, 2018}}</ref>

After recording their first EP, Outgrown Things, guitarist Brett Chiodo left the band in January 2016.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BAipT8Clql0/ |title=Brett Chiodo on Instagram |last=Chiodo |first=Brett |date=January 14, 2016 |access-date=April 3, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180404035847/https://www.instagram.com/p/BAipT8Clql0/ |archivedate=April 4, 2018}}</ref> Chiodo wrote all the demo songs as well as the Outgrown Things EP with the band<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BC6IwrdFqvr/ |title=Brett Chiodo on Instagram |last=Chiodo |first=Brett |date=March 13, 2016 |access-date=April 3, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180404035847/https://www.instagram.com/p/BC6IwrdFqvr/ |archivedate=April 4, 2018}}</ref> He was replaced by guitarist Ira George, who did not contribute to the writing or recording of Outgrown Things. 

Their debut EP, produced by [[Will Yip]], ''Outgrown Things'' was released on March 11, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundfiction.net/2016/03/11/movements-release-new-ep-premiere-new-music-video|title=Movements Release New EP; Premiere New Music Video - Soundfiction|author=Dissonance|date=11 March 2016|publisher=Soundfiction|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201094311/http://www.soundfiction.net/2016/03/11/movements-release-new-ep-premiere-new-music-video|archivedate=1 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/features/entry/movements_premiere_video_for_brand_new_track_kept|title=Movements premiere video for honest new track "Kept" (exclusive)|first=Sarah|last=Force|date=January 14, 2016|website=Alternative Press|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131201418/https://www.altpress.com/features/entry/movements_premiere_video_for_brand_new_track_kept|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref> The album reached 42 on the [[Independent Albums|Indie charts]].<ref name="charts"/> That month, the band toured with [[Real Friends (band)|Real Friends]] on a tour where tickets were only $5 and the bands played in unconventional places such as bowling alleys and skateparks.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/real_friends_announce_special_headlining_tour_all_tickets_5|title=Real Friends announce special headlining tour, all tickets $5|first=Caitlyn|last=Ralph|date=February 8, 2016|website=Alternative Press|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131201439/https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/real_friends_announce_special_headlining_tour_all_tickets_5|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref>

In April 2017, they were nominated for "Best Underground Band" at the 2017 [[Alternative Press Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/apmas_2017_nominees|title=Nominees for 2017 APMAs announced—vote now!|first=Rabab|last=Al-Sharif|date=April 25, 2017|website=Alternative Press|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131201416/https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/apmas_2017_nominees|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref>

The band played the entirety of the [[Warped Tour 2017|2017 Warped Tour]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.bandsintown.com/view-article/2017/03/22/vans-warped-tour-2017-lineup-announced-featuring-silverstein-the-ataris-bowling-for-soup/|title=Vans Warped Tour 2017 Lineup Announced Featuring Silverstein, The Ataris, Bowling For Soup|first=Dylan|last=Haas|date=March 22, 2017|publisher=[[Bandsintown]]|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201075228/https://news.bandsintown.com/view-article/2017/03/22/vans-warped-tour-2017-lineup-announced-featuring-silverstein-the-ataris-bowling-for-soup/|archivedate=1 February 2018}}</ref> During one set of the tour, vocalist Patrick Miranda helped fill-in for [[Counterparts (band)|Counterparts]] vocalist Brendan Murphy who had to miss the show due to a family emergency.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/watch_vocalists_fill_in_for_counterparts|title=Knocked Loose, Movements, more fill in for Counterparts vocalist during band’s Warped set|first=Kika|last=Chatterjee|date=July 28, 2017|website=Alternative Press|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131201205/https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/watch_vocalists_fill_in_for_counterparts|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref>

Over the course of the tours, the band spent over a year and a half writing new music for their upcoming debut album.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.tattoo.com/blog/tattoocom-interview-patrick-miranda-movements-latest-release-favorite-tattoo-artists-knuckle/ |title=Tattoo.com Interview: Patrick Miranda – Movements – Latest Release, Favorite Tattoo Artists, Knuckle Puck Tour |last=B. |first=Erica |date=November 13, 2017 |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207062754/https://www.tattoo.com/blog/tattoocom-interview-patrick-miranda-movements-latest-release-favorite-tattoo-artists-knuckle/ |archivedate=February 7, 2018}}</ref>
On September 28, 2017, the band released the music video for their first single of their debut album.<ref name="album"/> "Colorblind" features a music video in which the band's lyrics are projected onto the sides of houses and buildings.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.depthmag.com/movements-interview/ |title=Movements: Talking Color and Feeling with Patrick Miranda |last=Burch |first=Kel |date=October 24, 2017 |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180404035847/http://www.depthmag.com/movements-interview/ |archivedate=April 4, 2018}}</ref> The album, ''Feel Something'', was also produced by Will Yip, and released on October 20, 2017.<ref name="album">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7980971/movements-feel-something-album-colorblind|title=Movements Announce Debut Album, Share 'Colorblind' Video: Exclusive|first=Gary|last=Graff|authorlink=Gary Graff|date=September 28, 2017|publisher=Billboard.com|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20171001052037/http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/7980971/movements-feel-something-album-colorblind|archivedate=1 October 2017}}</ref> The album made reached number two on the [[Top Heatseekers|''Billboard''{{'}}s Top New Artist Albums Chart]] and peaked at number 190 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. The band themselves peaked at number 23 on the [[Billboard Twitter Real-Time|Emerging Artists]] chart.<ref name="charts">{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/movements|title=Movements|authorlink=Gary Graff|last=Graff|first=Gary|date=September 28, 2017|publisher=[[Billboard.com]]|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180327072949/https://www.billboard.com/music/movements|archivedate=27 March 2018}}</ref>

In November 2017, they were scheduled to tour Europe with [[Knuckle Puck]], [[Tiny Moving Parts]] and [[Have Mercy (band)|Have Mercy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.punknews.org/article/64403/tours-knuckle-puck-tiny-moving-parts-have-mercy-movements-eu-and-uk|title=Tours: Knuckle Puck/Tiny Moving Parts/Have Mercy/Movements (EU and UK)|first=Samantha|last=Barrett|date=August 2, 2017|website=www.punknews.org|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131201348/https://www.punknews.org/article/64403/tours-knuckle-puck-tiny-moving-parts-have-mercy-movements-eu-and-uk|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref> However, Knuckle Puck canceled the tour.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.allthingsloud.com/knuckle-puck-cancel-european-tour/|title=Knuckle Puck Cancel European Tour - All Things Loud|date=2017-11-20|work=All Things Loud|access-date=2018-05-11|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2018 they embarked on a headlining tour with [[Can't Swim]], [[Super Whatevr]] and [[Gleemer]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/movements_spring_north_american_tour_dates_2018 |title=Movements announce spring North American headlining tour |last=Dickman |first=Maggie |date=January 8, 2018 |website=Altpress.com |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207010824/https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/movements_spring_north_american_tour_dates_2018 |archivedate=February 7, 2018}}</ref> Afterwards, they will go on a UK tour with [[Muskets (band)|Muskets]] and [[Paerish]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/movements-have-announced-a-tour |title=Movements Have Announced A Tour |last=Rogers |first=Jack |date=2018-02-06 |website=Rocksound.TV |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207014519/https://www.rocksound.tv/news/read/movements-have-announced-a-tour |archivedate=2018-02-07}}</ref> The band performed a DJ set at Emo Nite Day hosted by Emo Nite in Los Angeles in December 2017.<ref name="LA Mag">{{Cite news|title= Emo Nite is Throwing the Emo-est Party Ever | publisher= [[Los Angeles (magazine)|Los Angeles]] |url=http://www.lamag.com/culturefiles/emo-nite-throwing-emo-est-party-ever/}}</ref>

In March 2018, the band released a music video for the song "Deadly Dull". The band teamed up with [[Alzheimer’s Association]]  for the video to spread awareness.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.sidestagemagazine.com/movements-partner-with-alzheimers-association-to-debut-emotional-video-for-deadly-dull-off-debut-album-feel-something/ |title=MOVEMENTS Partner With Alzheimer’s Association To Debut Emotional Video For "Deadly Dull", Off Debut Album 'Feel Something' |date=March 9, 2018 |access-date=March 14, 2018 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180314181515/http://www.sidestagemagazine.com/movements-partner-with-alzheimers-association-to-debut-emotional-video-for-deadly-dull-off-debut-album-feel-something/ |archivedate=March 14, 2018}}</ref>
In November 2018, they released a cover of [[R.E.M. (band)|R.E.M.]]'s "[[Losing My Religion]]" as part of ''[[Songs that Saved My Life]]'' compilation released on [[Hopeless Records]].<ref>[https://chorus.fm/news/hopeless-records-announce-songs-that-saved-my-life-compilation/ movements]</ref>

==Musical style, lyrics and influence==
The band's music is a mixture of [[post-hardcore]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.soundfiction.net/2017/10/19/album-review-movements-feel-something|title=Album Review: Movements - Feel Something - Soundfiction|author=Piper M.|date=19 October 2017|publisher=Soundfiction|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201025706/http://www.soundfiction.net/2017/10/19/album-review-movements-feel-something|archivedate=1 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://powermetal.de/review/review-Movements/Feel_Something,31655.html |title=Movements - Feel Something: Hochdosierter Emotionscocktail |last=Krause |first=Timon |date=October 31, 2017 |access-date=January 31, 2018 |trans-title=Movements - Feel Something: High-dose emotion cocktail |language=German |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201075332/http://powermetal.de/review/review-Movements/Feel_Something,31655.html |archivedate=February 1, 2018}}</ref> [[alternative rock]],<ref name="AXS"/> and [[spoken word]].<ref name="sw">{{cite web|url=http://www.soundfiction.net/2016/03/11/ep-review-movements-outgrown-things|title=EP Review: Movements - Outgrown Things - Soundfiction|author=Piper M.|date=11 March 2016|publisher=Sound Fiction|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201021215/http://www.soundfiction.net/2016/03/11/ep-review-movements-outgrown-things|archivedate=1 February 2018}}</ref><ref name="AXS"/> They are considered part of the [[emo revival]] movement.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ocweekly.com/music/south-countys-movements-want-you-to-feel-something-8523794|title=South County's Movements Want You to Feel Something|first=Josh|last=Chesler|date=25 October 2017|publisher=OC Weekly|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131200657/http://www.ocweekly.com/music/south-countys-movements-want-you-to-feel-something-8523794|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ocweekly.com/music/south-county-band-movements-spearhead-ocs-latest-emo-revival-7027678|title=South County Band Movements Spearhead OC's Latest Emo Revival|first=Alexander|last=Vincent|date=8 March 2016|publisher=OC Weekly|accessdate=31 January 2018|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180131201058/http://www.ocweekly.com/music/south-county-band-movements-spearhead-ocs-latest-emo-revival-7027678|archivedate=31 January 2018}}</ref>

Vocalist Patrick Miranda has been diagnosed with [[obsessive–compulsive disorder]] and has struggled with depression and anxiety, struggles that heavily influence the band's lyrics.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.kerrang.com/features/movements-patrick-miranda-opens-up-about-his-mental-health-experiences/ |title=Movements’ Patrick Miranda Talks Frankly About OCD And Depression |last=Richardson |first=Jake |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207005224/http://www.kerrang.com/features/movements-patrick-miranda-opens-up-about-his-mental-health-experiences/ |archivedate=2018-02-07}}</ref><ref name="CPF"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.therockpit.net/2017/interview-patrick-miranda-movements/ |title=INTERVIEW: Patrick Miranda – Movements |last=Massie |first=Andrew |date=November 23, 2017 |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207010730/https://www.therockpit.net/2017/interview-patrick-miranda-movements/ |archivedate=February 7, 2018}}</ref><ref name="AXS"/> Other lyrical themes include heartbreak and tense relationships with parents.<ref name="CPF"/> The song "Deadly Dull" is written about [[Alzheimer's disease]], which Miranda's grandmother suffered from.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.jamieorque.com/blog/2017/10/20/movements-interview |title=MOVEMENTS INTERVIEW |last=Orque |first=Jamie |date=October 20, 2017 |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207010131/http://www.jamieorque.com/blog/2017/10/20/movements-interview |archivedate=February 7, 2018}}</ref>

The band was influenced by artists such as [[Title Fight]], [[Balance and Composure]],  [[My Chemical Romance]], [[Underoath]], [[The Devil Wears Prada (band)|The Devil Wears Prada]] and [[Good Charlotte]].<ref name="form"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://infectiousmagazine.com/warped-tour-interview-pat-miranda-of-movements/ |title=WARPED TOUR INTERVIEW: Pat Miranda of Movements |last=Marshall |first=Sami |date=July 13, 2017 |access-date=2018-02-06 |url-status=live |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180207011135/https://infectiousmagazine.com/warped-tour-interview-pat-miranda-of-movements/ |archivedate=February 7, 2018}}</ref>
