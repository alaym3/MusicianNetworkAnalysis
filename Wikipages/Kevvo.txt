'''Kevin Manuel Rivera Allende''' (born February 10, 1998), known professionally as '''Kevvo''' (stylized as '''KEVVO'''), is a Puerto Rican rapper and singer from [[Toa Baja, Puerto Rico]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noticiassin.com/kevvo-promociona-en-rd-remix-con-figuras-del-genero-urbano/|title=Kevvo promociona en RD remix con figuras del género urbano|first=Scarlet|last=Hernández|date=October 9, 2019|website=Noticias SIN}}</ref>

He first came to prominence with his song 105F, which included a remix with multiple notable reggaeton artists such as [[Ñengo Flow]], [[Myke Towers]], [[Darell (rapper)|Darell]], [[Chencho Corleone]], [[Arcángel]], [[Farruko]], [[Faraón Love Shady]] and others.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://marvin.com.mx/kevvo-nueva-cancion-105f-remix-farruko-arcangel-nengo-flow-myke-towers-darell/|title=105F Remix, la canción de KEVVO que unió a los mejores del reggaeton}}</ref> The video surpassed 50 million views on [[YouTube]] one month after its release.<ref name="auto1"/>

==Origins==

He said that some of his  inspirations were [[Cosculluela]], [[Daddy Yankee]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://almomento.net/kevvo-promociona-105f-en-republica-dominicana/|title=Kevvo promociona 105°F en República Dominicana|first=G. Soluciones-Desarrollo web en santo|last=domingo|date=August 6, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metrord.do/do/entretencion/2019/08/07/kevvo-promociona-tema-las-emisoras-dominicanas.html|title=Kevvo promociona tema en las emisoras dominicanas|first=Carlos J. Basora|last=O|website=Metro Republica Dominicana}}</ref> [[Arcángel (singer)|Arcángel]], [[Ñengo Flow]], and [[Farruko]], because he grew up hearing their music.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.primerahora.com/entretenimiento/musica/nota/kevvocriticaelfaranduleo-1372606/|title=Kevvo critica el faranduleo|date=October 27, 2019|website=Primera Hora}}</ref>

Kevvo said in Spanish talking about his origins: {{quote|I started producing from my house, from my room. I started with a basic program, like everyone. Since I was 8 I was doing rhythms as a hobby. I didn’t do it for people to know me. It was simply that I liked it.<ref name="auto"/>}}

==Controversies==

In 2019, he had a diss battle with fellow Puerto Rican rapper Omy de Oro. Kevvo said in the Ecoween event in Eco Sports Park, "Subimos de rango un carajo", making reference of one of Omy's songs, which made him angry. Omy came to Kevvo, hit him in the face and got this sunglasses. Both artists were accompanied with bodyguards from their shows for safety reasons.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://elcalce.com/metroamp/jarana/omy-oro-kevvo-formaron-tremendo-corre-corre-videos/|title=Omy De Oro y Kevvo formaron tremendo corre-corre (VIDEOS) &#124; ElCalce|website=elcalce.com}}</ref>

In another incident, this time in 2020, one of his songs was leaked that contained material that some considered to be dissing fellow Puerto Rican rappers Eladio Carrión and [[Ozuna (singer)|Ozuna]], but that was later proven false. The controversy came when he made a statement in a line saying “Tocame y te dejo flow [[Prichard Colón]], jodio.”<ref name="elnuevodia.com">https://www.elnuevodia.com/entretenimiento/musica/nota/kevvosedisculpaporcomentariosofensivoscontraprichardcolon-2544111/</ref> People were offended by that line due to a dilemma the boxer faced when an illegal puch to the back of the head caused him a cerebral hemorrhage, he later fell in coma for almost a year, and now he is healing although he is still in a vegatative state.<ref>https://espndeportes.espn.com/boxeo/nota/_/id/3083128/suenos-destrozados-el-triste-caso-del-boxeador-prichard-colon</ref> Kevvo later apologized for the comment.<ref name="elnuevodia.com"/>
