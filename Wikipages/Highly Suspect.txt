'''Highly Suspect''' is an American [[Rock music|rock]] band from [[Cape Cod]], [[Massachusetts]]. The band consists of twin brothers Rich ([[bass guitar]], backing vocals) and Ryan Meyer ([[drums]], backing vocals), Johnny Stevens ([[guitar]], [[lead vocals]]), and Matt Kofos (guitar, synthesizer, backing vocals).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B1McZXYFACk/|title=SiriusXM Octane on Instagram: "I gladly joined the guys in Highly Suspect in their hazing of new band mate Matt. Hear Highly Suspect's new songs '16' and 'Upperdrugs'…"|via=Instagram|language=en|access-date=2019-08-15}}</ref> After starting as a bar cover band, they relocated to [[Brooklyn]], [[New York City|New York]], where they recorded ''The Worst Humans EP'' with producer [[Joel Hamilton]]. 

The band's first studio album, ''[[Mister Asylum]]'', was released on July 17, 2015,<ref name="billboard">{{cite web|first=Natalie|last=Weiner |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/rock/6583101/highly-suspect-claudeland-exclusive-mister-asylum|title=Highly Suspect Debuts Grungy New Track|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=2015-06-01|access-date=June 30, 2015}}</ref> earning Highly Suspect a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Album|Best Rock Album]] at the [[58th Annual Grammy Awards]]. The song "[[Lydia (Highly Suspect song)|Lydia]]" was also nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Song|Best Rock Song]].<ref name="grammys">{{cite web|url=http://www.wqlz.com/2015/12/07/grammy-nominations-announced-included-slipknot-sevendust-highly-suspect-and-ghost/|title=Grammy Nominations Announced; Included Slipknot, Sevendust, Highly Suspect and Ghost|publisher=[[WQLZ]]|date=2015-12-07|access-date=2015-12-07|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170719121150/http://www.wqlz.com/2015/12/07/grammy-nominations-announced-included-slipknot-sevendust-highly-suspect-and-ghost/|archive-date=July 19, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Their second studio album,<ref name="EW">{{cite web|last1=Vain|first1=Madison|title=Highly Suspect Detail Second Album, 'The Boy Who Died Wolf' -- exclusive|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2016/09/07/highly-suspect-boy-who-died-wolf|website=Entertainment Weekly |access-date=2 November 2016}}</ref> ''[[The Boy Who Died Wolf]]'', was released on November 18, 2016. Two singles have been released from the album, "[[My Name Is Human]]", which topped the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' US [[Mainstream Rock (chart)|Mainstream Rock Songs]] chart, and "[[Little One (Highly Suspect song)|Little One]]", which peaked at number 2 on the same chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/6531923/highly-suspect/chart?f=376|title=Highly Suspect - Chart history - Billboard|website=Billboard |access-date=January 10, 2019}}</ref> Highly Suspect's third studio album, ''[[MCID]]'', was released on November 1, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=New HIGHLY SUSPECT Album 'MCID' To Feature Collaboration With GOJIRA |date=August 16, 2019 |website=Blabbermouth.net |url=https://www.blabbermouth.net/news/new-highly-suspect-album-mcid-to-feature-collaboration-with-gojira/}}</ref>

==History==
===Formation and ''Mister Asylum'' (2009–2015)===
Highly Suspect got their start in 2009 as a cover band playing songs by [[Sublime (band)|Sublime]], [[Jimi Hendrix]] and [[Pink Floyd]] in local bars in the Cape Cod area, such as the British Beer Company, J.R Brody's Roadside Tavern, and Sundancers, also touring and playing a limited number of shows with bands 10 Years, Monster Truck and Sevendust.<ref name="newyorktimes">{{cite web|first=Joe|last=Coscarelli|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/02/15/arts/music/in-grammy-race-the-band-highly-suspect-marvels-at-its-surprising-double-shot.html?_r=0|title=In Grammy Race, the Band Highly Suspect Marvels at Its Surprising Double Shot |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=2016-02-14|access-date=2016-02-18}}</ref> They attended [[Dennis-Yarmouth Regional High School]].<ref name="enterprisenews">{{cite web|first=Jay N.|last=Miller |url=http://www.enterprisenews.com/article/20150528/ENTERTAINMENT/150526130|title=MUSIC SCENE: Highly Suspect Revel in the Growing Buzz|work=[[Enterprise News]] |date=2015-03-04|access-date=2015-06-25}}</ref>

''First Offense'' was Highly Suspect's first EP, released on July 15, 2009. The EP consists of "Life's a Fun Ride", "Not Me", and "Smile On", their first written and recorded songs after making the switch from a cover band ("Not Me" would later appear on the self-titled album ''Highly Suspect'').

''The Gang Lion EP'' was the band's second EP, released on October 3, 2010. It consists of "Gang Lion", "Big Bear", and "Then Mickey", all of which would later appear on their self-titled album, ''Highly Suspect''.

''The Worst Humans'' was the band's third EP, released July 13, 2012. The EP featured the songs "Bath Salts", "Gumshoe" and "The Go". The recordings were available for both physical purchase and digital download on [[Bandcamp]].

The band's fourth EP, ''Black Ocean'', was released in October 2013. It featured two tracks from their debut release as well as three new tracks, "Fuck Me Up", "Lydia", and "Guess What". This release was jointly produced by [[Joel Hamilton]] and [[Joe Duplantier]] of [[Gojira (band)|Gojira]].
[[File:HighlySuspect October2019.jpg|thumb|310x310px|Frontman Johnny Stevens performs live at [[Slim's]] - San Francisco]]
Their self-titled compilation album ''Highly Suspect'' was released in 2011. It contains fourteen tracks, consisting of several new songs as well as previously released but re-recorded songs, like "Gang Lion" and "Then Mickey".

The band toured the [[United States]] in early 2015 with [[Deftones]], [[Chevelle (band)|Chevelle]], [[Halestorm]], [[Catfish and the Bottlemen]] and [[Scott Weiland]] from [[Stone Temple Pilots]].<ref name="enterprisenews" /> The music video for the song "Lydia" premiered on [[MTV]].com on June 25, 2015.<ref name="MTVbio">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/highly-suspect/biography/|title=Artist Spotlight: Highly Suspect |work=[[MTV]] |access-date=June 25, 2015}}</ref>

Their first studio album<ref>{{cite web|title=10 New Artists You Need to Know Now|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-may-2015-20150526/highly-suspect-20150522|website=Rolling Stone|access-date=November 2, 2016}}</ref><ref name="Loudwire">{{cite web|last1=Childers|first1=Chad|title=Highly Suspect Reveal 'The Boy Who Died Wolf' Album Details|url=http://loudwire.com/highly-suspect-the-boy-who-died-wolf-album-details-my-name-is-human-single/|website=Loudwire|access-date=November 2, 2016}}</ref> ''[[Mister Asylum]]'' was released on July 17, 2015, via [[300 Entertainment]].<ref name="billboard" /> The first single from the album, titled "[[Lydia (Highly Suspect song)|Lydia]]", peaked at number 4 on the [[Mainstream Rock (chart)|US Mainstream Rock Songs]] chart and at number 24 on the US [[Alternative Songs]] chart. On July 24, 2015, the album debuted at number 39 on the [[Official New Zealand Music Chart|New Zealand Albums Chart]].<ref name="nztop40">{{cite web|url=http://nztop40.co.nz/chart/albums?chart=4100|title=NZ Top 40 Albums Chart|publisher=[[Recorded Music NZ]]|date=July 27, 2015|access-date=July 24, 2015}}</ref>

Highly Suspect was selected for iTunes' 2015 New Artist Spotlight by its editors.<ref name="iTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/mister-asylum/id975018114|title=Highly Suspect on iTunes|work=[[iTunes]]|access-date=June 30, 2015}}</ref> In November, the band released their second single "Bloodfeather", which peaked at number 5 on the US Mainstream Rock Songs chart.

===''The Boy Who Died Wolf'' (2016–2017)===
Their second studio album, ''[[The Boy Who Died Wolf]]'', was released November 18, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/the-boy-who-died-wolf-mw0002980448|title=The Boy Who Died Wolf - Highly Suspect |last=Erlewine|first=Stephen Thomas|website=AllMusic|access-date=2017-01-05}}</ref> The band released the single "[[My Name Is Human]]" on September 7, 2016.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Vain|first1=Madison|title=Highly Suspect Detail Second Album, 'The Boy Who Died Wolf' -- exclusive|url=http://www.ew.com/article/2016/09/07/highly-suspect-boy-who-died-wolf|website=Entertainment Weekly|access-date=8 September 2016}}</ref> A second single, "[[Little One (Highly Suspect song)|Little One]]", was released on April 26, 2017. On December 6, it was announced that "My Name Is Human" was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Song|Best Rock Song]] at the [[59th Annual Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/grammys-beyonce-drake-rihanna-kanye-west-lead-nominees-w453807|title=Grammys 2017: Beyonce, Drake, Rihanna, Kanye West Lead Nominees|newspaper=Rolling Stone|access-date=2017-01-05}}</ref>

===''MCID'' (2018–present)===
A single featuring [[Young Thug]] entitled "Tokyo Ghoul" and Stevens' hip-hop persona "Terrible Johnny" was released October 4, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Espinoza |first1=Joshua |title=Premiere: Highly Suspect Connects With Young Thug and Terrible Johnny on "Tokyo Ghoul" |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2019/10/highly-suspect-young-thug-tokyo-ghoul |website=Complex |access-date=4 October 2019 |language=en}}</ref> The fourth single, "Canals", was released on October 25, 2019.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Childers|first1=Chad|title=Highly Suspect Let Out Angst With New Song 'Canals'|url=https://loudwire.com/highly-suspect-canals/|website=[[Loudwire]]|access-date=25 October 2019|language=en}}</ref> The album was released on November 1.<ref>{{cite web |title=Highly Suspect's Third Album 'MCID' Out Now |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Highly-Suspects-Third-Album-MCID-Out-Now-20191101 |website=BroadwayWorld |access-date=1 November 2019 |language=en}}</ref>
