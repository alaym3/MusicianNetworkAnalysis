'''Liam Anthony''' "'''William'''" '''Singe''' (born 2 July 1992) is an Australian [[YouTube]]r, singer, songwriter, and producer. He is most notable for his online videos on [[YouTube]] and [[Facebook]].

Singe started his career as a member of [[The Collective (band)|The Collective]], an Australian [[boy band]] which was formed during the [[The X Factor (Australia season 4)|fourth season]] of ''[[The X Factor (Australian TV series)|The X Factor Australia]]'' in 2012, finishing in third place. The Collective subsequently signed with [[Sony Music Australia]] and released seven singles and a self-titled album. They disbanded after Singe left the group in 2015.

In 2014 and 2015, Singe recorded numerous R&B, pop, reggae, hip-hop and rap covers in his bedroom. In less than a year, he accumulated over two million Facebook fans, reached a number one position on [[Spotify]], 20 million Spotify streams, and earned more than 500 million video views and 2.6 million subscribers on [[YouTube]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/ogdenpayne/2016/04/29/how-william-singe-went-from-boy-band-to-a-viral-solo-sensation/#6f3b74091590|title=How William Singe Went From Boy Band To A Viral Solo Sensation|last=Payne|first=Ogden|website=Forbes|access-date=1 May 2016}}</ref>

==Early life==

William Singe was born and raised in [[Forestville, New South Wales|Forestville]], aka 'Forry', a suburb of northern Sydney. He is of [[Māori in Australia|Māori]] descent.<ref>{{cite web|last=Singe|first=Will|url=https://twitter.com/WillSinge/status/285291660533956609|title=@WillSinge Status|publisher=Twitter|date=30 December 2012|access-date=30 December 2012}}</ref>

William began recording music as a child, drawing inspiration from his father, a singer and songwriter from [[Ngāti Porou]], [[New Zealand]].<ref name="The Fern Events">{{cite web |url = http://www.thefernevents.com/#!SYDNEYS-WILLIAM-SINGE-SIGNED-WITH-US-AGENT-PARADIGM/colb/56237d9f0cf2c3576e6445d8 |title = Sydney's William Singe Signed with US Agent Paradigm |publisher = The Fern Events |date = 22 October 2015 |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref><ref name="coaching"/>

He was a student at [[St Augustine's College (New South Wales)|St Augustine's College]] in [[Brookvale, New South Wales|Brookvale]] and worked as a bank teller at the [[Commonwealth Bank]] in [[Dee Why, New South Wales|Dee Why]].<ref name="coaching">{{cite news|last=Bennett|first=Rod|url=http://origin.manly-daily.whereilive.com.au/news/story/manlys-margi-coen-speaks-of-coaching-x-factors-the-collective-to-stardom/|title=Manly's Margi Coen speaks of coaching The Collective's Will Singe to X Factor stardom |work=Manly Daily|publisher=News Community Media|date=16 November 2012|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6CFaKrDPv?url=http://origin.manly-daily.whereilive.com.au/news/story/manlys-margi-coen-speaks-of-coaching-x-factors-the-collective-to-stardom/|archive-date=17 November 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Bennett|first=Rod|url=http://origin.manly-daily.whereilive.com.au/news/story/will-singe-the-collective-x-factor-aussie-made/|title=Forestville's Will Singe and The Collective safe for another week on the X-Factor|work=Manly Daily|publisher=News Community Media|date=31 October 2012|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6CED9qftn?url=http://origin.manly-daily.whereilive.com.au/news/story/will-singe-the-collective-x-factor-aussie-made/|archive-date=16 November 2012}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2011, Singe auditioned for ''The X Factor'' in season three and made it to the end of the [[The X Factor (Australia season 3)#Bootcamp|bootcamp]] stage.<ref name="liveshows">{{cite news|last=Byrnes|first=Holly|url=http://www.news.com.au/entertainment/television/next-stop-the-live-shows-for-x-factors-top-12/story-e6frfmyi-1226472913585|title=Next stop, the live shows for X Factor's Top 12|work=[[The Daily Telegraph (Sydney)|The Daily Telegraph]]|publisher=News Limited|date=13 September 2012|access-date=23 November 2012}}</ref>

In 2012, he auditioned for the fourth season singing Justin Bieber's "[[One Less Lonely Girl]]" (with added rap lyrics that Singe wrote himself) and received positive comments from the judges.<ref name="thirdauditions">{{cite web|last=Lewdon|first=Courtney|url=http://www.throng.com.au/2012/08/the-x-factor-australia-season-four-the-third-auditions-angela-vayne-good-question-hayden-maurirere/|title=The X Factor Australia Season Four – The Third Auditions|publisher=Throng. Throng Media|date=22 August 2012|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6CED4EMmS?url=http://www.throng.com.au/2012/08/the-x-factor-australia-season-four-the-third-auditions-angela-vayne-good-question-hayden-maurirere/|archive-date=16 November 2012}}</ref> He progressed to the [[The X Factor (Australia season 4)#Super bootcamp|super bootcamp]] stage.<ref name="xfactorbio">{{cite web|url=http://au.tv.yahoo.com/x-factor/contestants/the-collective/|title=The Collective|work=[[The X Factor (Australian TV series)|The X Factor Australia]]|publisher=[[Yahoo!7]]|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6CCqbQdR1?url=http://au.tv.yahoo.com/x-factor/contestants/the-collective/|archive-date=16 November 2012}}</ref>

===2012–2014: The Collective===
{{see also|The Collective (band)}}

After Singe did not to make it into the top six of the Under-25 Boys category during super bootcamp, the judges decided to put him together with Trent Bell, Julian De Vizio, Zach Russell and Jayden Sierra to form a boy band named [[The Collective (band)|The Collective]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Lewdon|first=Courtney|url=http://www.throng.com.au/2012/09/the-x-factor-australia-season-four-the-2012-top-twelve-decided/|title=The X Factor Australia Season Four – The 2012 Top Twelve Decided!|publisher=Throng. Throng Media|date=12 September 2012|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6CEgmDcVd?url=http://www.throng.com.au/2012/09/the-x-factor-australia-season-four-the-2012-top-twelve-decided/|archive-date=17 November 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Knox|first=David|url=http://www.tvtonight.com.au/2012/09/the-x-factor-6.html|title=X-tra smiles for a hit show|publisher=[[TV Tonight]]|date=14 September 2012|access-date=16 November 2012}}</ref> On 20 November 2012, it was announced that The Collective finished in third place.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/tv-and-radio/jade-revives-career-on-x-factor-20121120-29ok8.html|title=Samantha Jade wins The X Factor Australia|work=The Sydney Morning Herald|publisher=Fairfax Media|date=20 November 2012|access-date=21 November 2012}}</ref>

On 22 November 2012, The Collective signed a recording contract with [[Sony Music Australia]].<ref>{{cite news|last=McCabe|first=Kathy|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/news/x-factor-winner-samantha-jades-single-what-youve-done-to-me-itunes-number-one/story-e6frg6n6-1226521427074|title=X Factor winner Samantha Jade's single What You've Done to Me iTunes number one|work=[[The Australian]]|publisher=[[News Limited]]|date=22 November 2012|access-date=23 November 2012}}</ref><ref name="Sunday Times Interview">{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/news/fans-turn-out-for-the-collective/story-fnii5t8l-1226686896787|title=Fans turn out for The Collective|work=[[The Sunday Times (Western Australia)|The Sunday Times]]|publisher=News Limited|date=27 July 2013|archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/6ISXSZQm4?url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/news/fans-turn-out-for-the-collective/story-fnii5t8l-1226686896787|archive-date=28 July 2013}}</ref> The group's self-titled [[Mini-LP|mini album]], ''[[The Collective (The Collective album)|The Collective]]'', was released on 14 December 2012.<ref name="debutalbum">{{cite web|url=http://www.sanity.com.au/products/2219046|title=The Collective – CD|publisher=[[Sanity (music store)|Sanity]]|access-date=29 November 2012}}</ref> The album debuted at number 11 on the ARIA Albums Chart and was certified gold.<ref name="AUS Albums">{{cite web|url=http://australian-charts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=The+Collective+%5BAU%5D|title=Discography The Collective|publisher=Australian Charts Portal. Hung Medien|access-date=30 September 2012}}</ref><ref name="Certification">{{cite web|url=http://www.aria.com.au/pages/httpwww.aria.com.aupagesaria-charts-accreditations-albums-2013.htm|title=ARIA Charts – Accreditations – 2013 Albums|publisher=Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA)|access-date=21 February 2013}}</ref>

In 2013, Singe was featured on fellow ''X Factor'' contestant [[Bella Ferraro]]'s single "Forgot You" which peaked at number 75 on the ARIA Singles Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/au/album/forgot-you-feat.-will-singe/id724212803|title=iTunes – Music – Forgot You (feat. On 18 February 2015, Singe announced on [[Facebook]] that he had left the group to pursue his solo career.<ref name="Singe Left">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/WilliamSinge/posts/10153683601615620|title=William Singe – Its been an incredible ride and I love my brothers...|publisher=[[Facebook]]|date=18 February 2015|access-date=24 February 2015}}</ref> The remaining members of The Collective disbanded that same month to also pursue solo careers.<ref>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_crcEVn1g8s|title=Banter with Ollie Geale Welcomes Julian De Vizio|publisher=[[YouTube]]|date=11 March 2015|access-date=11 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lA4q6tgOFeM|title=Jack & Jayden Sierra Radio Interview on 2RDJ|publisher=YouTube|date=28 April 2015|access-date=11 October 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite video|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kyy5eYl3twI|title=Trent Bell Radio Interview with Jase from The Stars of Tomorrow|publisher=YouTube|date=27 September 2015|access-date=11 October 2015}}</ref>

===2015–present: Solo career===
As a solo performer, Singe recorded cover tracks of contemporary singers, from [[Justin Bieber]] and [[Adele]] to [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Fetty Wap]] and [[The Weeknd]]. He sang in his bedroom and uploaded the videos to Facebook and YouTube. Starting 2015 with 6 cover videos and around 20 thousand followers on his Facebook page, Singe grew to more than two million followers by the end of the year with 44 additional cover videos.<ref>{{cite web |url = https://www.facebook.com/WilliamSinge/timeline?ref=page_internal |title = William Singe |publisher = Facebook |access-date = 24 December 2015}}</ref><ref name="Jonathon Moran">{{cite web |url = http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/entertainment/sydney-confidential/the-collective-band-member-will-singe-goes-solo-now-i-can-do-what-ive-always-wanted/story-fni0cvc9-1227637211323 |title = The Collective band member Will Singe goes solo: 'Now I can do what I've always wanted' |publisher = The Daily Telegraph |author = Jonathon Moran |date = 7 December 2015 |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref>

Singe announced on 14 October that he signed to [[Paradigm Talent Agency|Paradigm]], a US entertainment agency representing [[Ed Sheeran]], [[Coldplay]], [[Sia (musician)|Sia]] and [[Black Eyed Peas]] among others.

In September and October, [[One Direction]]'s [[Liam Payne]] and the singer [[T-Pain]] reached out via Twitter to connect with Singe, expressing interest to collaborate with him.<ref name="Jonathon Moran"/><ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.maifm.co.nz/T-Pain-keen-to-collab-with-William-Singe/tabid/76/articleID/17324/Default.aspx |title = T-Pain keen to collab with William Singe |publisher = Mai |date = 29 September 2015 |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.sugarscape.com/music/news/a1082894/liam-payne-will-singe/ |title = Liam Payne wants to work with producer Will Singe |publisher = Sugarscape |date = 8 October 2015 |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref>

Singe covered [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s song "[[Hotline Bling]]" on 24 September.<ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.capitalxtra.com/news/viral-videos/version-drake-hotline-bling-will-give-you-chills/#P0t0crRCRFBuW0C9.97 |title = This Cover Of Drake's 'Hotline Bling' Will Give You Chills |publisher = Capital Xtra |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref> It became his most popular video to date. In less than a month, the video earned close to 25 million views on YouTube and Facebook,<ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.vibe.com/2015/10/william-singe-hotline-bling-cover/ |title = YouTube Star William Singe's "Hotline Bling" Cover Might Give Everyone Else's A Run For Their Money |author = Shenequa Golding |publisher = Vibe |date = 13 October 2015 |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref> reaching a number one position on [[Spotify]]'s US Viral 50 chart.<ref>{{cite web |url = https://spotifycharts.com/viral/?country=us&recurrence=daily&date=2015-11-01 |title = United States Viral 50 |publisher = Spotify |date = 1 November 2015 |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url = https://scontent.fbne1-1.fna.fbcdn.net/hphotos-xpt1/t31.0-8/12182617_10154459458305620_1459252380805346411_o.jpg |title = Snapshot of #1 on United States Viral 50 |access-date = 26 December 2015}}</ref>

Singe co-wrote "What You Want" for and with [[Samantha Jade]], included on her album ''[[Nine (Samantha Jade album)|Nine]]'', which was released in 2015.

Noting Singe's success, [[Capital Xtra]] concluded: "YouTube sensation William Singe is a star in his own right, having racked up millions of views across his phenomenal covers of [[Fetty Wap]]'s '[[679 (song)|679]],' [[The Weeknd]]'s '[[Earned It]]' and now [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]'s '[[Hotline Bling]].' The 22-year-old Aussie singer-songwriter completely makes the song his own and it sounds incredible."<ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.capitalxtra.com/artists/drake/lists/best-hotline-bling-covers/#RoVa2ijlIY1IgCfR.97 |title = 10 Covers Of Drake's 'Hotline Bling' That Are As Good As The Original |publisher = Capital Xtra |date = 16 October 2015 |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref> The Fern Events, a South Sydney-based event solutions company, also commented that Singe was "just phenomenal, his perseverance is really paying off."<ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.thefernevents.com/#!SYDNEYS-WILLIAM-SINGE-SIGNED-WITH-US-AGENT-PARADIGM/colb/56237d9f0cf2c3576e6445d8 |title = Sydney's William Singe Signed With US Agent Paradigm |publisher = The Fern Events |access-date = 6 August 2016}}</ref>

On 29 July, Singe announced that he had officially signed with [[RCA Records]].<ref>{{cite web |url = https://www.forbes.com/sites/ogdenpayne/2016/07/30/viral-singersongwriter-william-singe-signs-a-record-deal-with-rca/#239357975a84 | title = Facebook Famous Singer/Songwriter William Singe Signs A Record Deal With RCA |work = Forbes |access-date = 6 August 2016}}</ref> His debut solo single was released on 3 February 2017, titled "[[Rush (William Singe song)|Rush]]".

On 5 May 2017, he appeared on the [[Jonas Blue]] single "[[Mama (Jonas Blue song)|Mama]]",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2017-06-29-interview-jonas-blue-on-mama-new-collaborations-his-live-show/|title=INTERVIEW: Jonas Blue on 'Mama', New Collaborations & His Live Show|last=Mastrogiannis|first=Nicole|date=6 July 2017|publisher=iHeartRadio|access-date=30 July 2018}}</ref> which became a top 10 hit in many countries, including Australia, UK, New Zealand, Germany, Norway, Denmark and South Africa.

On 13 June 2017, he reached 2 million subscribers on YouTube.

In January 2018, "Mama" was nominated for two Brit Awards. The ceremony would take place [[2018 Brit Awards|the following month]].<ref name="Nominations for The 2018 Brit Awards">{{cite web |title=Nominations for The 2018 Brit Awards |url=https://www.bellmedia.ca/the-lede/press/nominations-for-the-brit-awards-2018/ |website=Bell media |access-date=27 October 2020}}</ref>

== Concert tours ==

In January 2016, Singe embarked on ''The Introduction Tour'' across Australia, Asia, the United States and Europe. In February, March and April 2017, Will toured the United States with [[Alex Aiono]] on the "Changes Tour".<ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.moshtix.com.au/v2/event/william-singe-sold/82631 |title = WILLIAM SINGE - SOLD OUT Tickets |publisher = Moshtix |access-date = 24 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url = http://neumos.com/event/william-singe-the-introduction-tour/ |title = William Singe – The Introduction Tour |publisher = Neumos |date = 17 February 2015 |access-date = 24 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.bandsintown.com/WilliamSinge |title = William Singe: Tour Dates 2015 |publisher = Bandsintown |access-date = 24 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.williamsinge.com/introtour/ |title = The Introduction Tour |publisher = williamsinge.com |access-date = 24 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url = https://www.auspop.com.au/2015/12/touring-william-singe/ |title = Touring: William Singe |publisher = auspOp |date = 7 December 2015 |access-date = 23 December 2015}}</ref>
