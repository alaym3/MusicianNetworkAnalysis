'''Futurebirds''' are an American [[indie rock]] band formed in 2008 from [[Athens, Georgia]], United States.<ref name="Leahey">Leahey, Andrew "[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/futurebirds-p2173456/biography Futurebirds Biography]", [[Allmusic]], retrieved 2011-07-05</ref> Their first album, ''Hampton's Lullaby'', was released via the [[Aquarium Drunkard]] imprint, Autumn Tone Records, on July 27, 2010. Their self titled [[Extended play|EP]] was re-released in February 2011 and their second EP, ''Via Flamina'', was released in April 2011.

== Overview ==
Futurebirds are known for their high energy shows and [[country rock]] infused sound. Their music incorporates reverb-soaked four-part harmonies supported by [[pedal steel guitar]], rhythmic [[Percussion instrument|percussion]], [[banjo]], [[mandolin]], and acoustic and electric guitars with heavy feedback, and has been described as 'psychedelic country'.<ref name="Hyden">Hyden, Steven (2010) "[http://www.avclub.com/articles/futurebirds-hamptons-lullaby,43947/ Hampton’s Lullaby]", [[The A.V. Club]], August 10, 2010, retrieved 2011-07-05</ref><ref name="Maine">Maine, David (2010) "[http://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/130464-futurebirds-hamptons-lullaby Futurebirds: Hampton's Lullaby]", [[PopMatters]], September 14, 2010, retrieved 2011-07-05</ref><ref name="Rear">O'Rear, Caine (2011) "[http://www.americansongwriter.com/2011/06/school-of-athens-an-interview-with-futurebirds/ School Of Athens: An Interview With Futurebirds]", ''[[American Songwriter]]'', June 22, 2011, retrieved 2011-07-05</ref>

The band has often shared the stage with [[Widespread Panic]],  [[Drive-By Truckers]], [[Dead Confederate]],<ref name="AOL">"[http://www.spinnermusic.co.uk/2010/09/08/road-report-dead-confederate-head-west-with-futurebirds/ Road Report: Dead Confederate Head West With Futurebirds]", Spinnermusic.co.uk, September 8, 2010, retrieved 2011-07-05</ref> [[The Whigs (band)|The Whigs]], [[Blitzen Trapper]] and [[Jonny Corndawg]].
During summer 2011 the group toured with [[Grace Potter and the Nocturnals]] on the Bonnaroo Buzz Tour and performed twice during the [[Bonnaroo]] festival.<ref name="Sherfey">Sherfey, James (2011) "[http://onlineathens.com/stories/062311/mar_847828387.shtml Q&A with Futurebirds]", onlineathens.com, June 23, 2011, retrieved 2011-07-05</ref><ref name="Madison">Madison, Tjames (2011) "[http://www.soundspike.com/news/article/2260-futurebirds_news/ Q&A: Carter King of Futurebirds]", soundspike.com, June 1, 2011, retrieved 2011-07-05</ref>
