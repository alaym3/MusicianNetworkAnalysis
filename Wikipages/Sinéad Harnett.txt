'''Sinéad Monica Harnett''' (born 12 October 1990)<ref>{{citation|first=Sinéad|last=Harnett|title=Happy BUTTday !! Birthday twin x<br>Cass Lowe @CassLowe @SineadHarnett HAPPY BIRTHDAY TO YOUUU AND TO MEEEEE|website=Twitter.com|date=12 October 2015}}</ref><ref name=standard/> is an English singer/songwriter and actor.

==Career==
Born to a Thai mother and an Irish father, Harnett grew up in [[Finchley]], north London.<ref name=standard>{{cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/goingout/music/new-voice-for-ldn-sinead-harnett-interview-9997918.html|title=New voice for LDN: Sinead Harnett interview|date=23 January 2015|newspaper=[[London Evening Standard]]|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref><ref name=Mixmag/> She attended [[Arts University Bournemouth|Arts University College Bournemouth]], where she studied for a degree in acting and appeared in student productions of plays such as ''[[One Thousand and One Nights]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bournemouthecho.co.uk/leisure/reviews/8733060.The_First_Arabian_Night__AUCB_School_of_Performance__The_Arts_University__Dorset/|title=The First Arabian Night, AUCB School of Performance, The Arts University, Dorset|website=Bournemouthecho.co.uk|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://webdocs.aucb.ac.uk/Artscape%20-%20Autumn%202011.pdf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120702042949/http://webdocs.aucb.ac.uk/Artscape%20-%20Autumn%202011.pdf |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-07-02 |title=Artscape |access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref>

Harnett gained prominence as a singer in 2011 when, following an open request to her Twitter followers for suggestions and applicants, grime artist [[Wiley (rapper)|Wiley]] selected Harnett to feature on the single "Walk Away" from his album ''[[Chill Out Zone (album)|Chill Out Zone]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sickchirpse.com/coming-artist-sinead-harnett/|title=UP & COMING ARTIST: SINEAD HARNETT|date=16 July 2011|publisher=sickchirpse.com|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtviggy.com/articles/sinead-harnetts-powerful-soul-has-us-hooked/|title=New Music Videos, Reality TV Shows, Celebrity News, Pop Culture|publisher=mtviggy.com|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.buzzinunlimited.com/2011/05/british-talent-sinead-harnett.html|title=British Talent! – Sinead Harnett|publisher=buzzinunlimited.com|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> In 2012, she was featured on the [[Disclosure (band)|Disclosure]] track "Boiling" from their EP ''The Face''. She released her debut single, "Got Me", in 2013 via [[Black Butter Records]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/tracks/15404-got-me/|title="Got Me" by Sinead Harnett Review|work=Pitchfork|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> and contributed songwriting and guest vocals to [[Rudimental]]'s debut album, ''[[Home (Rudimental album)|Home]]''.<ref name=Mixmag/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/sinead-harnett-prepares-debut-single|title=Sinead Harnett Prepares Debut Single|work=Clash|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> She was also featured on [[Ryan Hemsworth]]'s solo album ''[[Guilt Trips]]'', on the opening track "Small + Lost",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.yahoo.com/artist-profile-sinead-harnett-161000150.html|title=Artist in profile: Sinead Harnett|publisher=Yahoo!|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> and provided uncredited vocals for [[Kidnap Kid]]'s single "So Close".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.black-butter.co.uk/kidnap-kid-so-close/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402131232/http://www.black-butter.co.uk/kidnap-kid-so-close/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=2015-04-02 |title=Kidnap Kid - So Close - Black Butter Records |access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref>

Her debut EP, ''N.O.W.'', was released in August 2014 through 333 Records;<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/the-wrap-up/blog/listen-sinead-harnett-paradise|title=Listen: Sinead Harnett 'Paradise' – MTV UK|publisher=MTV|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dazeddigital.com/music/article/20759/1/watch-sinead-harnetts-sultry-and-summery-new-video|title=Watch Sinead Harnett's sultry and summery new video|date=9 July 2014|publisher=dazeddigital.com|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> it includes the single "No Other Way", which ''[[The Guardian]]'' named track of the week.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/aug/29/sinead-harnett-no-other-way-review|title=Sinead Harnett's No Other Way, this week's best new track|first=Issy|last=Sampson|date=29 August 2014|work=The Guardian|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> Another track on the EP, "Paradise", received the same honour from ''[[The Huffington Post]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/08/14/songs-you-need-to-know_n_5678104.html|title=The Artist You Need To Know This Week Is Sinead Harnett|first1=Ryan Kristobak Ryan|last1=Kristobak|first2=HuffPost Associate Entertainment|last2=Editor|date=14 August 2014|work=HuffPost|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> Snakehips, who are featured on "No Other Way", featured Harnett on their single "Days with You".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/jul/11/new-band-of-the-week-snakehips-no-13|title=New band of the week: Snakehips (No 13)|first=Paul|last=Lester|date=11 July 2014|work=The Guardian|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> By late 2014, she was recording her debut album with producer [[Chris Loco]] for release on [[Virgin EMI Records]], and was shortlisted for [[MTV UK]]'s Brand New for 2015 award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/brand-new-for-2015/news/mtv-brand-new-for-2015-nominees-revealed|title=MTV Brand New For 2015 Nominees Revealed! – MTV UK|publisher=MTV|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> The album was originally due for release in 2015 and also features contributions from [[James Fauntleroy]], Chloe Martini, [[Stephen Kozmeniuk|KOZ]], [[TMS (production team)|TMS]], and [[Two Inch Punch]].<ref name=TheFader/> Its first single, "She Ain't Me", was released in June 2015, followed by "Do It Anyway" in September 2015.<ref name=TheFader>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2015/04/16/sinead-harnett-interview|title=Sinead Harnett Got Her Break Singing For Wiley And Disclosure, But Now It's Her Turn on Top|publisher=thefader.com|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thefourohfive.com/music/article/listen-to-sinead-harnett-s-new-single-do-it-anyway-143|title=Listen to Sinead Harnett's new single 'Do It Anyway'|publisher=Thefourohfive.com|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref>

In early 2016, Harnett featured on the single "Never Say Never?" by rapper [[Nick Brewer]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/features/in-conversation-sinead-harnett-x-nick-brewer |title=In Conversation: Sinead Harnett x Nick Brewer &#124; Features &#124; Clash Magazine |website=Clashmusic.com |date=2020-03-08 |access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref> In August, she released the EP ''Sinead Harnett'' via [[Rinse FM]]; it features collaborations with [[Grades (producer)|GRADES]], JD. Reid, [[Kaytranada]], and Snakehips, and has produced the singles "If You Let Me" and "Rather Be with You".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rwdmag.com/sinead-harnett-releases-self-titled-ep-prod-by-kaytranada-snakehips-jd-reid/ |title=Sinead Harnett releases self-titled EP prod. by Kaytranada, Snakehips & JD Reid &#124; RWD |website=Rwdmag.com |access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Sydney Gore |url=http://www.nylon.com/articles/sinead-harnett-rather-be-with-you-premiere |title=Sinead Harnett Will Mend Your Broken Heart With Her Cozy Voice |website=Nylon.com |date=2016-12-14 |access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref>

In June 2019, Harnett sang the National Anthem of the United Kingdom prior to Anthony Joshua's defeat at the hands of Andy Ruiz Jr at Madison Square Garden.

In 2020, Harnett played the role of influencer Paige Lee in the [[Wales Interactive]] movie Five Dates.

Outside of music, Harnett is a lifelong [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] fan having grown up in North London.

==Artistry==
[[File:Sinead Harnett 2015 (cropped).jpg|thumb|Harnett performing in 2015]]
Harnett's musical influences include [[Mariah Carey]], [[The Fugees]], [[Whitney Houston]], [[Lauryn Hill]], [[Michael Jackson]], [[Dizzee Rascal]], [[Tina Turner]], and [[Amy Winehouse]].<ref name=Mixmag>{{cite web|last=Gilgrass |first=Will |url=http://www.mixmag.net/words/features/hello-sinead-harnett |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130712221744/http://www.mixmag.net/words/features/hello-sinead-harnett |url-status=dead |archive-date=2013-07-12 |title=Mixmag &#124; Hello: Sinead Harnett |date=2013-07-09 |access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://uk.complex.com/music/2014/08/premiere-listen-to-sinead-harnett-paradise|title=Premiere: Listen to Sinead Harnett's "Paradise"|publisher=complex.com|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mancunianmatters.co.uk/content/050970503-singer-sinead-harnett-steps-spotlight-chadderton-baths-gig-after-eye-opening|title=Singer Sinead Harnett steps into spotlight at Chadderton Baths gig after 'eye opening' collaborations – Mancunian Matters|date=5 September 2014|publisher=mancunianmatters.co.uk|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref> ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' wrote in 2013 that "Harnett's rich, soulful voice and range has drawn comparisons to [[Adele]], but her recordings are more experimental and contemporary—a brand of jazz-tinged, melodic electronic pop a bit reminiscent of [[Jessie Ware]] or [[Jhené Aiko]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/global/1557271/backbeat-singer-sinead-harnett-lights-up-london-gig-for-full-house|title=Backbeat: Singer Sinead Harnett Lights Up London Gig for Full House of A&Rs|work=Billboard|access-date=10 January 2017}}</ref>
