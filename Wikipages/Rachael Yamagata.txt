'''Rachael Yamagata''' (born September 23, 1977)<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/music/artists/fdf9fd10-8a2a-4cb4-bed0-3c476c976ce0 |title=Rachael Yamagata |work=BBC Music |access-date=September 27, 2018}}</ref> is an American singer-songwriter and pianist from [[Arlington, Virginia|Arlington]], [[Virginia]]. She began her musical career with the band Bumpus before becoming a solo artist and releasing five EPs and four studio albums. Her songs have appeared on numerous television shows and she has collaborated with [[Jason Mraz]], [[Rhett Miller]], [[Bright Eyes (band)|Bright Eyes]], [[Ryan Adams]], [[Toots and the Maytals]] and [[Ray Lamontagne]].<ref name="Garcia">{{cite news |last=Garcia |first=Cathy Rose A. |title=Singer Rachael Yamagata to Take Stage for First Time in Seoul |url=https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/www/news/art/2009/04/141_42294.html |newspaper=[[The Korea Times]] |date=2009-03-31 |access-date=2009-05-18 }}</ref><ref name=jenkins>{{citation |last=Jenkins |first=Mark |title=Rachael Yamagata: Album review: "Chesapeake" | newspaper=[[Washington Post]] |date=October 28, 2011 |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/gog/music-events/rachael-yamagata,1211648/critic-review.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130217022402/http://www.washingtonpost.com/gog/music-events/rachael-yamagata,1211648/critic-review.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=February 17, 2013}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Yamagata was born to a [[Japanese-American]] father and an [[Italians|Italian]]-[[Germans|German]] mother. She graduated from the [[Holton-Arms School]]{{citation needed|date=September 2018}} and attended [[Northwestern University]] and [[Vassar College]].<ref>[https://www.communication.northwestern.edu/learn/alumni.php Notable Alumni, School of Communication], Communication.northwestern.edu</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://alums.vassar.edu/connect/subscribe/blogs.html |title=Vassar Hub |access-date=2015-05-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140829130337/http://alums.vassar.edu/connect/subscribe/blogs.html |archive-date=2014-08-29 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Career==
Yamagata became the vocalist for the [[Chicago]] group Bumpus and spent six years touring, writing and recording with the band before leaving in 2001 to begin a solo career. In September 2002, she obtained a two-record deal with [[Arista Records|Arista]]'s Private Music and her self-titled [[Extended play|EP]] produced by Malcolm Burn, ''[[Rachael Yamagata EP]]'' was released in October.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.digitaljournal.com/a-and-e/entertainment/rachael-yamagata-recounts-her-journey-to-musical-independence/article/449073 |title=Rachael Yamagata recounts her journey to musical independence  |author=Earl Dittman |publisher=Digital Journal |date=November 11, 2015 |access-date=October 13, 2016}}</ref> Her first full-length album, ''[[Happenstance (Rachael Yamagata album)|Happenstance]]'', followed in 2004. The album was produced by [[John Alagía]] at [[Compass Point Studios]]. Between 2003 and 2005, Yamagata played venues such as [[Amoeba Records]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.amoeba.com/live-shows/detail-435/|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20191007234233/https://www.amoeba.com/live-shows/detail-435/|archive-date = 2019-10-07|title = Rachel Yamagata - Live at Amoeba Music}}</ref> and [[The Hotel Café]], both in [[Hollywood]].

Yamagata was featured on the album [[True Love (Toots & the Maytals album)|''True Love'']] by [[Toots and the Maytals]], which won the [[Grammy Award]] in 2004 for Best Reggae Album, and showcased many notable musicians including [[Willie Nelson]], [[Eric Clapton]], [[Jeff Beck]], [[Trey Anastasio]], [[Gwen Stefani]] / [[No Doubt]], [[Ben Harper]], [[Bonnie Raitt]], [[Manu Chao]], [[The Roots]], [[Ryan Adams]], [[Keith Richards]], [[Toots Hibbert]], [[Paul Douglas (musician)|Paul Douglas]], Jackie Jackson, [[Ken Boothe]], and [[The Skatalites]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tootsandthemaytals.net/linear-cd-notes/ |title=Toots and the Maytals - In Depth - Linear Notes |website=Toots and the Maytals |access-date=November 9, 2016 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20161110054527/http://www.tootsandthemaytals.net/linear-cd-notes/ |archive-date=November 10, 2016 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>

Yamagata toured with [[Mandy Moore]] and contributed a song to Moore's 2007 album, ''[[Wild Hope]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mandy Moore's 'Wild Hope' Set for May Release|url=http://www.keyt.com/entertainment/5692661.html|website=KEYT3 Santa Barbara|access-date=August 12, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927010039/http://www.keyt.com/entertainment/5692661.html|archive-date=September 27, 2007|date=February 8, 2007}}</ref>

In May 2008, Yamagata released a three-song EP, ''[[Loose Ends (Rachael Yamagata EP)|Loose Ends]]''.  Her second full-length album ''[[Elephants...Teeth Sinking Into Heart]]'' was released in October 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/elephantsteeth-sinking-into-heart-mw0000797706 |title=Elephants...Teeth Sinking Into Heart - Rachael Yamagata |website=AllMusic.com |access-date=November 10, 2016}}</ref> ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' characterized the album as much darker and sadder in tone than its predecessor.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1054599/yamagata-wraps-darker-grittier-new-album|title=Yamagata Wraps 'Darker, Grittier' New Album |work=Billboard |date=February 22, 2007 |access-date=November 10, 2016 |author=Peters, Mitchell}}</ref> That month, her two-song live acoustic video performance appeared on ''LiveDaily Sessions'' and featured the songs "Faster" and "Sunday Afternoon".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.livedaily.com/sessions/36.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081010024634/http://www.livedaily.com/sessions/36.html |title=Rachael Yamagata: Exclusive Video Performance At LiveDaily Sessions |website=LiveDaily Sessions |archive-date=October 10, 2008 |access-date=November 10, 2016}}</ref> In April 2009, Yamagata performed her song "Elephants" on the television drama ''[[One Life to Live]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://soapnet.go.com/soapnet/show/path-showname_onelifetolive/section_recaps/year_2009/month_Mar/date_30/day_thursday/page_4?vnu_content_id=1003831577 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090807010101/http://soapnet.go.com/soapnet/show/path%2Dshowname_onelifetolive/section_recaps/year_2009/month_Mar/date_30/day_thursday/page_4?vnu_content_id%3D1003831577|title=One Life to Live News |website=SOAPnet.com |date=March 2009 |archive-date=August 7, 2009 |url-status=dead |access-date=April 2, 2009 }}</ref>

Yamagata digitally released a new album in the fall of 2011 through [[Pledgemusic]]. Her third studio album ''[[Chesapeake (Rachael Yamagata album)|Chesapeake]]'' was released through Frankenfish Records in October 2011.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://americansongwriter.com/2011/09/song-premiere-rachael-yamagata-the-way-it-seems-to-go/ |title=Song Premiere: Rachael Yamagata, "The Way It Seems To Go" |author=Evan Schlansky |magazine=American Songwriter |date=27 September 2011 |access-date=2016-10-12}}</ref> In 2012 Yamagata released her next EP called ''Heavyweight''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rachaelyamagata.spinshop.com/'HEAVYWEIGHT'%20EP%20Stuff/details/173001?parent_url=http%3A%2F%2Frachaelyamagata.com%2Fstore |title=Rachael Yamagata :: Store |publisher=Rachaelyamagata.spinshop.com |access-date=2014-08-12}}</ref> Yamagata has appeared numerous times as a guest vocalist for other artists including: [[Jason Mraz]]'s "Did You Get My Message?", "Fireflies" and "The Believer" by [[Rhett Miller]], on [[Toots & the Maytals]]'s album ''[[True Love (Toots & the Maytals album)|True Love]]'', the song "Barfly" by [[Ray Lamontagne]], and several songs on Ryan Adams' ''[[Cold Roses]]'' album. She contributed vocals to six songs on the [[Bright Eyes (band)|Bright Eyes]] album ''[[Cassadaga (album)|Cassadaga]]''.<ref>''Cassadaga'' liner notes</ref> Yamagata contributed writing, keyboards and vocals to the song "Kaleidoscope" by Jill Cunniff a former member of [[Luscious Jackson]]. Yamagata also performs with an ensemble cast on the ''[[30 Rock]]'' episode "[[Kidney Now!]]"<ref name=30RockFinale>[http://www.nbc.com/30_Rock/exclusives/kidney-now/celebrities.shtml ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090518121734/http://www.nbc.com/30_Rock/exclusives/kidney-now/celebrities.shtml |date=May 18, 2009 }}</ref> The soundtrack for the film ''[[Dear John (2010 film)|Dear John]]'' features the song "You Take My Troubles Away", Yamagata's duet with [[Dan Wilson (musician)|Dan Wilson]]. She also performs the [[Muppets]] song "[[I'm Going to Go Back There Someday]]" on a [[cover album]], ''[[Muppets: The Green Album]]''.<ref>Lifestyle News, Wawzenek, Bryan. [http://www.gibson.com/en-us/Lifestyle/News/muppets-0627-2011/ My Morning Jacket, Amy Lee, Weezer Cover the Muppets] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110703073326/http://www.gibson.com/en-us/Lifestyle/News/muppets-0627-2011/ |date=2011-07-03 }}, Gibson.com, 27 June 2011.</ref>

Yamagata again partnered with PledgeMusic for her album ''[[Tightrope Walker (album)|Tightrope Walker]]'', released on September 23, 2016. She also offered an acoustic digital version of ''Happenstance'' as a pledge reward.<ref name="DigiJournal">{{cite web |url=http://www.digitaljournal.com/a-and-e/entertainment/rachael-yamagata-recounts-her-journey-to-musical-independence/article/449073 |title=Rachael Yamagata recounts her journey to musical independence |work=Digital Journal |date=November 11, 2015 |access-date=July 14, 2016 |author=Dittman, Earl}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Yamagata has a twin brother, Benji.<ref name="Garcia"/> Her half brother, Josh Ruben, is an actor and director for [[CollegeHumor]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Ruben|first=Josh|title=JOSH'S MINDHOUSE|url=http://joshruben.tumblr.com/post/10166825843/heres-the-debut-of-even-if-i-dont-the-music-vid|access-date=22 September 2011}}</ref>
