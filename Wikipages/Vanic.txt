'''Jesse Hughes''' (born May 6, 1989), known professionally as '''Vanic''', is a Canadian [[DJ]] and [[Record producer|producer]], based in [[New Westminster]], [[British Columbia]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://thump.vice.com/en_ca/article/3dexaw/exploring-melodies-with-vanic|title=Exploring Melodies with Vanic|last=Kyte|first=Kathryn|date=July 24, 2014|website=[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]|language=en-ca|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.idolator.com/7655940/vanic-too-soon-maty-noyes|title=Vanic Teams Up With Maty Noyes For "Too Soon"|last=Wass|first=Mike|date=January 20, 2017|work=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|access-date=2017-08-29|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life ==
A [[Metal music|metal]] [[Enthusiasm|enthusiast]] when he was in middle school, Hughes developed an interest in [[hardstyle]] and later in musical production. He played piano at the age of four.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.unorthodoxmusic.co.uk/exclusive-interview-vanic-dj-producer/|title=Exclusive Interview : Vanic DJ / Producer - Unorthodox Music|website=www.unorthodoxmusic.co.uk|language=en-GB|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref> Hughes gained recognition for remixing songs for artists like Yeah Yeah Yeahs, Lana Del Rey and Birdy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/vanic-mn0003039469/biography|title=Vanic - Biography & History|last=Phares|first=Heather|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref> He told [[Forbes]] "Family and personal issues came up in my life that made me re-evaluate my goals and direction. I decided that I might as well spend my youth trying for something really cool and different, something I was passionate about. And music was the obvious choice, it's been one of the biggest parts of my life..."<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/isisbriones/2017/06/09/dj-vanic-starting-at-the-sun-single-career-start/#577072870e24|title=Why Vanic Is The Breakout Electronic Artist Everyone Needs To Know About|last=Briones|first=Isis|work=[[Forbes]]|access-date=2017-08-29|language=en}}</ref> Hughes, who studied business, dropped out of [[Simon Fraser University]] in an effort to pursue a successful musical career.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.straight.com/music/872141/vancouver-bred-vanic-dropout-no-regrets|title=Vancouver-bred Vanic is a dropout with no regrets|date=2017-02-22|work=[[Straight.com]]|access-date=August 29, 2017|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2014, he was featured on the song "Circles" by Machineheart, which received over a million streams in two weeks.<ref name=":0" /> In 2016, he signed a deal with [[Disruptor Records]], a joint venture of [[Sony Music]] and Adam Alpert.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/6835336/vanic-signs-disruptor-records-announces-north-american-road-to-coachella-tour|title=VANIC Signs to Disruptor Records & Announces North American Tour: Exclusive|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.youredm.com/2016/01/25/vanic-signs-to-distruptor-records-announces-road-to-coachella-tour/|title=VANIC Signs To Distruptor Records, Announces Road to Coachella Tour|last=Yau|first=Jeffrey|date=2016-01-25|website=Your EDM|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref> He said "Adam and Disruptor were the right fit. Everything they do seems to be spot on, and they're really good people to work with."<ref name=":1" /> He released his debut single "Samurai" via the label.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7511120/vanic-samurai-disruptor-records|title=Vanic Kicks It Like a 'Samurai' on First Original Single: Listen|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref> On January 20, 2017, he released the song "Too Soon" featuring Maty Noyes.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7668940/vanic-too-soon-maty-noyes|title=Listen to Vanic's Dynamic Track 'Too Soon' Feat. Maty Noyes|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref> According to Nielsen Music, the song received 740,000 [[Music streaming|streams]] in the United States and sold 1,000 downloads in its first tracking week. It debuted on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'s [[Dance/Electronic Songs]] chart at 40th.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7677812/billboard-dance-chart-upstarts-vanic-thomas-gold-austra|title=Billboard Dance Chart Upstarts: Vanic, Thomas Gold & Austra|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref> His song "Staring at the Sun" was also released via Disruptor and featured Clara Mae.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7817704/vanic-staring-at-the-sun-clara-mae|title=Vanic Welcomes the Light on 'Staring at the Sun': Listen|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-08-29}}</ref> The song became his third single to be released on the label.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://vergecampus.com/2017/06/vanic-staring-at-the-sun-clara-mae/|title=Listen to Vanic's New Bass Single "Staring At The Sun"|last=Blair|first=EJ|date=June 5, 2017|work=Verge Campus|access-date=2017-08-29|language=en-US}}</ref>
