'''Haile Salaam''' (born August 16, 2000),<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thefader.com/2019/09/04/xanman-heard-cuz-was-hot-interview|title=Xanman is the DMV's most prolific teen rapper|website=The FADER}}</ref> known professionally as '''XanMan''', is an [[United States|American]] rapper from [[Landover, Maryland]]. He gained significant attention in 2019 with his single "Gucci Down".<ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.okayplayer.com/music/xanman-xanman-interview-new-mixtape.html|title=DMV Rapper XanMan Might Be Funny — But He's No Joke|date=March 20, 2020|website=Okayplayer}}</ref> XanMan has worked with a number of other Maryland rappers including his cousin [[YungManny]] and frequent collaborator Goonew. <ref name="auto3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/pigeons-and-planes/2019/05/xanman-interview|title=Meet Xanman, the Most Quotable New Rapper From the DMV|website=Complex}}</ref>

==Early life==
XanMan was born Haile Salaam on August 16, 2000 in [[Landover, Maryland]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://rockvillerampage.com/14425/features/dmv-rappers-on-the-rise-yungmanny-xanman/|title=DMV Rappers On the Rise: YungManny, XanMan|first=Monica|last=Blassou}}</ref> Raised in [[Landover, Maryland|Landover]], a suburb of [[Washington D.C.]], he was exposed to music at an early age. He claims Brightseat Road in Landover as his home. Salaam began to take an interest in the drums at age 5, and was influenced by his uncle, who was a drummer for [[Go-go|Go-Go Music]] legend [[Chuck Brown]].<ref name="auto3"/> He began recording music while in elementary school, and became proficient with the music software program [[Pro Tools]] when he was 11 years old.<ref name="auto3"/>

==Music career==
XanMan released his first mixtape "''Finesse Lord''" in 2013.<ref name="auto2"/> He went on to release a number of mixtapes in the following years, including ''No Plug'' and ''Broken''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/artists/Xanman|title=Xanman|website=Genius}}</ref> As of 2020, XanMan has released 34 mixtapes and a long list of singles. Xanman has collaborated with a number of different artists including [[Lil Yachty]] and fellow DC-Area rapper [[Lil Dude]].<ref name="auto3"/> Their single ''Many Men'' was ranked 7th on the Washington Post's list of the best DMV rap songs and albums of 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/the-best-dmv-rap-songs-and-albums-of-2018/2018/12/13/73aadf8e-fd77-11e8-862a-b6a6f3ce8199_story.html|title=The best DMV rap songs and albums of 2018|last=Richards|first=Chris|date=December 14, 2018|work=[[The Washington Post]]|access-date=2019-01-31}}</ref> Citing his influences, Salaam named [[Lil Wayne]] as one of the most important people in sparking his interest in making rap music.<ref name="auto"/> 

Describing Xanman's energetic stage presence, [[Washington Post]] music critic Chris Richards wrote, "it signals a lust for life, a yearning, an exaggerated stride into the future."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/lifestyle/style/teen-rappers-xanman-and-yungmanny-master-the-art-of-getting-ahead-of-yourself/2019/07/29/62b8afe8-b222-11e9-8f6c-7828e68cb15f_story.html|title=Perspective &#124; Teen rappers Xanman and YungManny master the art of getting ahead of yourself|first=Chris |last=Richards |website=Washington Post}}</ref>

== Legal issues ==
Salaam was arrested on November 8, 2018 and charged with assault. He was sentenced to six months in jail. While incarcerated, a #FreeXan movement gained momentum on [[Twitter]]. <ref name="auto"/> Upon his release, XanMan dropped a song entitled ''First Day Out'' featuring his cousin and collaborator [[YungManny]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://genius.com/Xanman-and-yungmanny-first-day-out-lyrics|title=Xanman & YungManny – First Day Out|via=genius.com}}</ref>
