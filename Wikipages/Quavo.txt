'''Quavious Keyate Marshall''' (born April 2, 1991), known professionally as '''Quavo''' ({{IPAc-en|'|k|w|eɪ|v|oʊ}}), is an American rapper and singer. He is best known as the co-founder and current frontman of [[Hip hop music|hip hop]]/[[trap music (hip hop)|trap]] trio [[Migos]]. Quavo is related to his fellow Migos members, being [[Takeoff (rapper)|Takeoff]]'s uncle and [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]]'s cousin.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.biography.com/people/migos-8172017|title=Migos|website=Biography.com|language=en-us|access-date=2018-01-08}}</ref>

Outside of Migos, Quavo has been featured on four singles that have peaked within the top 10 of the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], including [[DJ Khaled]]'s "[[I'm the One (DJ Khaled song)|I'm the One]]" and "[[No Brainer (song)|No Brainer]]" the former of which reached number 1 along with "[[Congratulations (Post Malone song)|Congratulations]]", which peaked at number 8 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Post Malone|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/post-malone/chart-history/hsi/|access-date=2021-05-02|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> In 2018, he released his debut solo album ''[[Quavo Huncho]]'' which peaked at number 2 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].

== Early life ==
Quavious Keyate Marshall was born on April 2, 1991, in [[Athens, Georgia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/04/happy-birthday-quavo/|title=Happy Birthday, Quavo! - XXL|website=XXL Mag|access-date=2017-10-11}}</ref> Marshall's mother, Edna Marshall, was a hairdresser and his father died when he was four years old.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/07/18/interview-migos|title=Interview: Migos|work=The FADER|access-date=2017-10-11|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/the-three-a-migos-quavo-takeoff-and-offset-news.14196.html|title=The Three A-Migos: Quavo, Takeoff & Offset|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=2017-10-11}}</ref> The three members of Migos grew up together in [[Gwinnett County]], a mostly suburban area half an hour northeast of [[Atlanta]].<ref name=outrunning/>

Marshall attended [[Berkmar High School]] and was the starting quarterback of its football team during the 2009 season, his senior year.<ref name=outrunning>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/migos-young-rich-and-outrunning-trouble-20150624|title=Migos: Young, Rich and Outrunning Trouble|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2017-10-11}}</ref> Berkmar went 1–9 in the 2009 season, and Marshall went 19-of-25 for 201 yards and three touchdowns for the first win of the season.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.maxpreps.com/high-schools/berkmar-patriots-(lilburn,ga)/football-fall-09/schedule.htm|title=Berkmar High School 09-10 Football Schedule|website=MaxPreps.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-10-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.gwinnettdailypost.com/archive/berkmar-meadowcreek/article_597373ba-cc4c-5bea-bf59-3342dacf678e.html|title=Berkmar 33, Meadowcreek 6|last=Curtright|first=Guy|work=Gwinnett Daily Post|access-date=2017-10-11|language=en}}</ref> Quavo previously held the record by completing 28 passes in a game back in 2009.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://bleacherreport.com/articles/2703342-migos-hit-maker-quavo-was-actually-a-record-setting-high-school-quarterback|title=Migos Hit-Maker Quavo Was Actually a Record-Setting High School Quarterback|last=Weiner|first=Natalie|work=Bleacher Report|access-date=2017-10-11|language=en-US}}</ref> Despite finishing the football season for his senior year, Marshall dropped out of Berkmar months before graduation.

==Career==
Migos was formed in 2008 by Quavo and fellow rappers [[Takeoff (rapper)|Takeoff]] and [[Offset (rapper)|Offset]]. The three members are directly related and were raised together by Quavo's mother. Quavo is Takeoff's uncle, and Offset is Quavo's cousin.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/bad-and-boujee-inside-atlanta-rap-trio-migos-wild-world-w465205 |title=Migos' Wild World: One Night in the Studio With 'Bad and Boujee' Trio |work=Rolling Stone}}</ref> The group was originally known as Polo Club and are from [[Lawrenceville, Georgia]]. The group eventually decided to change their name to "Migos" after deciding Polo Club was too generic. The group released their first full-length project, a [[mixtape]] titled ''Juug Season'', on August 25, 2011. They followed with the mixtape ''No Label'', on June 1, 2012.

Migos initially rose to prominence in 2013 after the release of their single "[[Versace (song)|Versace]]". The song was remixed by [[Canadian]] rapper [[Drake (entertainer)|Drake]] which peaked at number 99 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100 chart and number 31 on the [[Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs]]. Quavo had his first lead single as a solo artist with the song "Champions" featuring several other artists, the song peaked at number 71 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/kanye-west/chart-history/hsi/|title=Kanye West Chart History|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-09-09}}</ref>

After the success of Migos' second studio album ''[[Culture (Migos album)|Culture]]'', which reached number one on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart, Quavo was subsequently featured on several popular songs outside of Migos, including "[[Congratulations (Post Malone song)|Congratulations]]", "[[I'm the One (DJ Khaled song)|I'm the One]]", "[[Portland (song)|Portland]]",[[File:Quavo Veld festival 2017.jpg|thumb|Quavo performing at the 2017 Veld festival]] and "[[No Brainer (song)|No Brainer]]". In an interview with ''[[GQ]]'', Houston-based rapper [[Travis Scott]], with whom Quavo previously worked with on the [[Young Thug]] collaboration "[[Pick Up the Phone (Young Thug and Travis Scott song)|Pick Up the Phone]]", revealed he had a collaborative album with Quavo in the works.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2017/05/travis-scott-teases-quavo-collab-project-astroworld-album-freestyle/|title=Travis Scott Teases Quavo Collab Project and 'AstroWorld' Album in New Freestyle|work=XXL|access-date=June 29, 2017}}</ref>

In April 2017, Quavo was featured on ''The Fate of the Furious: The Album'' on the song "[[Go Off (Lil Uzi Vert, Quavo and Travis Scott song)|Go Off]]" with [[Lil Uzi Vert]] and Travis Scott. The song was eventually certified Gold by the RIAA.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&se=Quavo#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum - RIAA|work=RIAA|access-date=2018-09-09|language=en-US}}</ref> Quavo also released "[[Ice Tray (song)|Ice Tray]]" with [[Lil Yachty]] on December 14, 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8071071/quavo-lil-yatchy-ice-tray-video-mock-joe-budden|title=Quavo and Lil Yatchy Mock Joe Budden in New 'Ice Tray' video|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-09-09}}</ref> The song peaked at number 74 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.

On December 21, 2017, Quavo announced that he would release ''[[Huncho Jack, Jack Huncho]]'' with [[Travis Scott]] on December 22, 2017 without any prior promotion.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8078453/quavo-travis-scott-huncho-jack-jack-huncho|title=Quavo & Travis Scott Move Up the Release Date for 'Huncho Jack, Jack Huncho': 'We Drop It Tonight'|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-12-21}}</ref> The album had eight songs that debuted on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.

On January 26, 2018, Migos released ''[[Culture II]]''. Quavo released three singles for his upcoming debut solo album,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thefader.com/2018/09/06/quavo-announces-debut-solo-album|title=Quavo announces debut solo album|work=The FADER|access-date=2018-09-09|language=en}}</ref> "[[Workin Me]]", "Lamb Talk" and "Bubble Gum", with "Workin Me" peaking at 52 on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100, becoming Quavo's highest charting single as a solo artist.

On May 18, 2019, Quavo performed "[[Future (Madonna and Quavo song)|Future]]" alongside [[Madonna (entertainer)|Madonna]] at the grand final of the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2019]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Legaspi |first1=Althea |title=Madonna and Quavo Welcome the 'Future' in New Song |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/madonna-quavo-future-song-listen-836542/ |access-date=22 May 2019 |work=Rolling Stone |date=17 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Smith |first1=Neil |title=Madonna Eurovision appearance confirmed |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-48303701 |access-date=22 May 2019 |work=BBC News |date=16 May 2019}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
On April 18, 2015, authorities stopped a Migos concert at [[Georgia Southern University]] and arrested all three members of the group, as well as several members of their entourage. Quavo was charged with possession of an unspecified [[Controlled Substances Act#Schedule II controlled substances|Schedule II]] narcotic, possession of marijuana, possession of a firearm in a school safety zone, and possession of a firearm during the commission of a crime.<ref>[http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2015/04/migos-denied-bond-after-georgia-southern-university-arrest/ Offset of the Migos Denied Bond After Georgia Southern University Arrest]. ''XXL''.</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/migos-members-arrested-on-guns-drugs-charges-after-police-end-concert-20150419|title=Migos Members Arrested on Guns, Drugs Charges After Concert|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2017-10-11}}</ref> He was released from jail on [[bail|bond]], and later pleaded [[no contest]] to misdemeanor marijuana charges and received a 12-month sentence, which was suspended based on payment of fines.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/quavo-and-Takeoff-released-from-jail-offsets-bail-denied-news.15020.html|title=Quavo And Takeoff Released From Jail, Offset's Bond Denied|work=HotNewHipHop|access-date=2017-10-11}}</ref><ref name="WSB-TV Plea">{{cite web|title=Plea deals keep rap group Migos out of jail|url=http://www.wsbtv.com/news/news/local/plea-deals-keep-rap-group-migos-out-jail/npchC/|publisher=WSB-TV|access-date=January 22, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160129051858/http://www.wsbtv.com/news/news/local/plea-deals-keep-rap-group-migos-out-jail/npchC/|archive-date=January 29, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Marshall received the MVP Award during the [[2018 NBA All-Star Weekend|2018 NBA All-Star Celebrity Game]] after a 19-point performance and victory.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.charlotteobserver.com/entertainment/celebrities/article226370710.html |title=The NBA All-Star Celebrity Game was looking weak. Then some strong basketball saved it. |last=Janes |first=Théoden |date=2019-02-16 |website=Charlotteobserver.com |language=en |access-date=2019-03-13}}</ref> In 2019, Marshall played on the "Away" roster during the [[2019 NBA All-Star Game#Celebrity Game|NBA All-Star Celebrity Game]] at the [[Bojangles' Coliseum]] in [[Charlotte, North Carolina|Charlotte]], [[North Carolina]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.nba.com/article/2019/02/06/2019-celebrity-game-participants-official-release |title=ESPN and NBA announce celebrity rosters and coaches for 2019 NBA All-Star Celebrity Game presented by Ruffles exclusively on ESPN |last=release |first=Official |website=NBA.com |language=en |access-date=2019-03-13}}</ref> and again during the [[2020 NBA All-Star Game#Celebrity Game|2020 All-Star Celebrity Game]] in Chicago.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nba.com/allstar/2020/events/celebrity-game|title=2020 All-Star Celebrity Game presented by Ruffles|website=NBA.com|date=February 15, 2020|access-date=February 22, 2020}}</ref>

Quavo began dating [[Saweetie]] in September 2018.<ref>{{cite web|last=Mendez|first=Marisa|title=Quavo Cops Saweetie a $75,000 Chain|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/01/quavo-saweetie-75000-dollars-snowflake-chain/|website=XXL Mag}}</ref> On March 19, 2021, Saweetie confirmed via social media that she and Quavo are no longer in a relationship.<ref>{{Cite web|last1=March 19|first1=Zoe JohnsonPublished|last2=2021|title=Saweetie Confirms Quavo Breakup|url=https://www.xxlmag.com/saweetie-quavo-breakup/|access-date=2021-03-19|website=XXL Mag|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Saweetie and Quavo Split After 2 Years as She Claims 'Intimacy' Was 'Given to Other Women'|url=https://people.com/music/saweetie-quavo-split-as-she-alleges-infidelity/|access-date=2021-03-19|website=PEOPLE.com|language=en}}</ref> In late March 2021, video footage surfaced showing the pair in a physical altercation that allegedly happened in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-03-30|title=Rappers Quavo and Saweetie shown in violent altercation before they broke up|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2021-03-30/quavo-saweetie-elevator-fight-video|access-date=2021-04-01|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US}}</ref>

In May 2020, Marshall announced on his [[Instagram]] account that he had graduated from high school after dropping out 11 years ago.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ebrahimji |first1=Alisha |title=Quavo from Migos finally gets his high school diploma at 29 - and releases a song to celebrate |url=https://www.cnn.com/2020/05/22/entertainment/quavo-graduates-high-school-trnd/index.html#:~:text=(CNN)%20Rapper%20Quavo%20of%20the,%22We%20Lit. |access-date=18 June 2020 |publisher=cnn.com |date=22 May 2020}}</ref>
