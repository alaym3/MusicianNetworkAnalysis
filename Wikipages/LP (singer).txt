'''Laura Pergolizzi''' (born March 18, 1981), known professionally as '''LP''', is an American singer and songwriter. LP has released five albums and three [[extended plays|EPs]]. LP has written songs for other artists including [[Cher]], [[Rihanna]], the [[Backstreet Boys]], [[Leona Lewis]], [[Mylène Farmer]], [[Céline Dion]] and [[Christina Aguilera]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop-shop/6106473/lp-forever-for-now-laura-pergolizzi |title=LP focuses on LP with First Album in a Decade |date=June 3, 2014 |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |last=Graff |first=Gary}}</ref>

==History==

===Early life and career===
LP Pergolizzi was born in 1981 on [[Long Island]],<ref name="iamlp bio">{{cite web|url=https://iamlp.blog/bio/ |title=Bio |work=LP Blog Official|date=March 17, 2018 |access-date=July 13, 2019}}</ref> to an Italian father and an Irish mother, her paternal grandfather coming from [[Palermo]] and grandmother from [[Naples]], Italy.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lastampa.it/2016/12/06/spettacoli/musica/nata-dai-locali-di-new-york-mi-nutro-di-pop-senza-confini-0kZU2XyphCkTAHxGs4w4lK/pagina.html |title="Nata dai locali di New York mi nutro di pop senza confini" - La Stampa |website=Lastampa.it |access-date=June 14, 2017}}</ref> Pergolizzi adopted the stage name "LP" after moving to New York City.<ref name="thefreelibrary">{{cite web|url=http://www.thefreelibrary.com/Not+a+tomboy+in+a+dress%3A+after+walking+away+from+a+contract+at+Island...-a0189051454 | title=Not a tomboy in a dress: after walking away from a contract at Island Records, rocker LP returns to her androgynous roots with her anticipated third album. |access-date=July 14, 2013 |publisher=thefreelibrary.com}}</ref> [[David Lowery (musician)|David Lowery]] of the band [[Cracker (band)|Cracker]] saw LP performing and featured her on "Cinderella", a hidden track on the band's 1998 album, ''[[Gentleman's Blues]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=News |url=http://www.lprock.com/news.html |publisher=lprock.com |access-date=November 15, 2013}}</ref> Lowery followed up by producing LP's debut album ''[[Heart-Shaped Scar]]'', released in 2001 through [[Koch Records]].

LP's second album, ''Suburban Sprawl & Alcohol'', was released in June 2004 through Lightswitch Records. On the record, she collaborated with songwriter and music producer [[Linda Perry]].<ref name="thefreelibrary" /> However, in spite of extensive touring to promote the release and positive reviews from music critics, the album failed to gain widespread recognition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.afterellen.com/archive/ellen/Music/102004/suburbansprawl.html |title=AfterEllen.com - Review of LP's Suburban Sprawl & Alcohol |last=Marti |first=Kris Scott |date=October 27, 2004 |website=afterellen.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070404014424/http://www.afterellen.com/archive/ellen/Music/102004/suburbansprawl.html |archive-date=April 4, 2007 |access-date=April 5, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===2006–2010===
In 2006, LP appeared at the music conference [[South by Southwest]] and reportedly sparked a bidding war amongst major music labels, eventually being signed by [[L.A. Reid]] to his [[Island Def Jam Music Group]] record label, part of the [[Universal Music Group]]. However, due to artistic differences LP left the contract. She signed with [[SoBe Entertainment]] in 2007, an independent record label, with the album tracks she had written at the major label.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bio: Who is LP?|url=http://www.lprock.com/bio.html |publisher=lprock.com}}</ref> "Love Will Keep You Up All Night", one of the tracks she had written at Island Def Jam Music Group with Billy Mann, was released in late 2007 on the [[Backstreet Boys]] album, ''[[Unbreakable (Backstreet Boys album)|Unbreakable]]''. LP wrote and performed "Wasted", from her album ''Suburban Sprawl & Alcohol'', which was the theme song for a television show, ''[[South of Nowhere]]'', on [[The N]], currently known as TeenNick. [[MTV]]'s ''[[The Hills (TV series)|The Hills]]'' also featured LP's song "Damage Is Done" in mid-2010, and it was released on [[iTunes]].

During 2009, LP started writing songs for other artists, including several songs which ended up on [[Heidi Montag]]'s album, ''[[Superficial (album)|Superficial]]'' (Warner Music).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.metroguiltypleasures.com/metro/laura-pergolizzi-on-sexuality-and-the-highs-and-lows-of-the-music-biz/ |title=Laura Pergolizzi Q&A on sexuality and the music biz |last=Harmsworth |first=Andrei |date=April 23, 2017 |website=www.metroguiltypleasures.com |publisher=Guilty Pleasures |access-date=May 4, 2017 |quote=You've also written for an eclectic mix of artists, from Rihanna to Heidi Montag. How did that come about?<br>No offence to Heidi, she's lovely, but I didn't write with her. A few of the songs I had, she just cut.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.idolator.com/7351112/heidi-montag-superficial-defense |title=In Defense Of Heidi Montag's 'Superficial'  |last=Lansky |first=Sam |date=September 28, 2012 |website=www.idolator.com |publisher=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]] |access-date=May 4, 2017 |quote=Contributors to the album include The Runners (Rihanna, Cher Lloyd), Kool Kojak (Ke$ha, One Direction), Steve Morales (Enrique Iglesias, Celine Dion), Stacy Barthe (Katy Perry, Brandy), Chris Rojas (Pink, Sophie Ellis-Bextor) and Laura Pergolizzi (Christina Aguilera, Rita Ora).}}</ref> LP co-wrote the single, "[[More Is More]]", as well as the album tracks, "Twisted", "Hey Boy" and "Love It or Leave It". She also co-wrote other tracks for and with Montag, such as the [[Cathy Dennis]] co-written, "Look How I'm Doin'", "I Do This" and "Who's That Girl". A song LP wrote with [[Alexander Kronlund]], "Standing Where You Left Me", was released on [[Erik Hassle]]'s debut UK album, ''[[Pieces (Erik Hassle album)|Pieces]]'' (Roxy/EMI/Universal). LP moved to Los Angeles in 2010.<ref name="cnn">{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2012/04/24/showbiz/lp-into-the-wild-music/index.html |title=Songwriter LP goes into the wild -- as a singer |last=Gumbrecht |first=Jamie |work=[[CNN]] |date=April 24, 2012 |access-date=July 14, 2013}}</ref>

===2011–2012===
In August 2010, it was announced that LP had signed with [[RedOne]]'s record label, [[2011 Records]]. LP landed her first major breakthrough as a songwriter, co-writing [[Rihanna|Rihanna's]] song, "[[Cheers (Drink to That)]]", featured on Rihanna's fifth studio album ''[[Loud (Rihanna album)|Loud]]'', released on November 12, 2010, on Def Jam Recordings. "[[Cheers (Drink to That)|Cheers]]" features a vocal hook performed by LP (taken from [[Avril Lavigne]]'s "[[I'm with You (Avril Lavigne song)|I'm with You]]"). In a 2010 interview with [[MTV News]], Rihanna said: "I love that song ("Cheers"). That is one of my favorite songs on the album. It makes you feel like celebrating. It gives you a great feeling inside like you want to go out and have a drink. ... People can't wait for the weekend."<ref name="mtv news">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1652434/rihanna-glad-avril-lavigne-appears-on-louds-cheers.jhtml |title=Rihanna Glad Avril Lavigne Appears On Loud's 'Cheers' |last=Vena |first=Jocelyn |publisher=MTV |date=November 17, 2010 |access-date=July 14, 2013}}</ref> LP's major label songwriting breakthroughs continued, as they co-wrote "Beautiful People", performed by [[Christina Aguilera]]. The song is featured on the ''[[Burlesque (soundtrack)|Burlesque: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack]]'' album, which was released on November 22, 2010 through [[RCA Records]].

In June 2011, LP co-wrote "[[Afraid to Sleep]]", which was performed by NBC's ''[[The Voice (TV series)|The Voice]]'' finalist [[Vicci Martinez]] and reached #10 on the iTunes Top Singles Chart. In September 2011, LP signed a deal with [[Warner Bros. Records]]. Shortly after, "[[Into the Wild (song)|Into the Wild]]", written and performed by LP, was prominently used in a [[Citibank]] national television advertisement campaign. In April 2012, LP released her first major label album, ''[[Into the Wild: Live at EastWest Studios]]'', a five-song live [[extended play]], and started touring extensively including festivals such as [[SXSW]], [[Bonnaroo]], [[Lollapalooza]], [[Bumbershoot]], [[Tropfest]] as well as [[Tokyo]]'s Sonic Boom and [[London]]'s Hyde Park. In 2012, LP became the first female [[Martin Guitar]] ambassador.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iamlp.com/blog/martin-guitar-ambassador |title=Martin Guitar Ambassador |publisher=iamlp.com |date=May 10, 2012 |access-date=July 14, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130906123851/http://www.iamlp.com/blog/martin-guitar-ambassador |archive-date=September 6, 2013 }}</ref>

===2013–present===
[[File:LP (singer songwriter) (ZMF 2018) jm76932.jpg|thumb|LP at the [[Zelt-Musik-Festival]] 2018 in [[Freiburg]], [[Germany]]]]
In May 2012, LP was featured as the Artist of the Week in ''[[Vogue (magazine)|Vogue]]'' magazine. Over the following two years, she completed the recording of her album that included collaborations with [[Billy Steinberg]], [[Isabella Summers|Isabella "Machine" Summers]] (from [[Florence and the Machine]]), [[Josh Alexander (songwriter)|Josh Alexander]], Claude Kelly, Justyn Pilbrow, Carl Ryden and Rob Kleiner.<ref>[http://www.discogs.com/LP-Forever-For-Now/release/5765341 Listing] of the ''Forever for Now'' album on Discogs.com, (accessed March 7, 2015).</ref> The album was produced by [[Rob Cavallo]], Warner Bros. Records' chairman and producer. On April 1, 2014, it was announced that LP's third full-length studio album would be titled ''[[Forever for Now (LP album)|Forever for Now]]'' and was scheduled to be released on June 3, 2014. The album was promoted by the lead single "Night Like This".<ref name=":0" /> Following the album, a second single, "Someday", was released in June 2014 in Canada.<ref name="Someday"/> On September 16, 2014, a music video for "Tokyo Sunrise" officially premiered on ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine's website.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://time.com/3387757/lp-tokyo-sunrise-video-premiere/ |title=Catch a 'Tokyo Sunrise' With LP and Her New Video: Premiere |work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |first=Nolan |last=Feeney |access-date=September 16, 2014}}</ref>

In September 2015, the song "Muddy Waters" was released as the first single from LP's fourth studio album ''[[Lost on You (album)|Lost on You]]''.<ref name="Muddy Waters"/><ref name="Lost on You CoS"/> In November 2015 one of LP's best known songs, "[[Lost on You]]" was released to great success.

In June 2016, "Muddy Waters" featured in the violent and emotionally charged closing scene of the season four finale of [[Netflix]]'s original series ''[[Orange Is The New Black]]''. It was also featured in the trailer for the [[NBC]] show ''[[Shades of Blue (TV series)|Shades of Blue]]''. "Muddy Waters" clarifies the ending of season 5 episode 4 of [[Starz]] [[Power (TV series)|Power]], as the layers of uncertainty and drama are revisited. In November, the album's [[Lost on You|eponymous second single]] was released, while LP held a residency at the No Vacancy club.<ref>{{cite web|title = LP's New Tune "Muddy Waters" Is a Sultry Prowl of a Song|url = http://noisey.vice.com/blog/premiere-lp-muddy-waters|website = NOISEY|access-date = December 13, 2015}}</ref> Both songs originate from a collaboration with [[Mike Del Rio]]. An EP<ref name="Lost on You CoS" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.interviewmagazine.com/music/lp/#_|title=LP After the Storm|last=Rosenzweig|first=Mathias|date=March 2, 2016|website=Interview Magazine|access-date=April 5, 2016}}</ref> titled ''Death Valley'' was released on June 17, 2016.

The song "Hi Ho Nobody Home" by [[David Baerwald]] featuring LP was used in an episode of [[Mr. Mercedes (TV series)|Mr. Mercedes]].
In 2017 LP performed the song "Back Where I Belong" ([[Otto Knows]] featuring [[Avicii]]) for episode 5 of season 2 of [[Netflix]]'s original series ''[[Sense8]]''.{{citation needed|date=May 2021}}

LP duets with [[Mylène Farmer]] on the single {{lang|fr|N'oublie pas}}, released on June 22. The single immediately hit number 1 on the French [[iTunes]] chart upon release.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://instagram.com/p/BkU4lvol8BJ/|title=Login • Instagram}}</ref> The track is the second single from Farmer's upcoming studio album {{lang|fr|Désobéissance}}.

On June 15, 2018, LP announced on her Facebook page that she had been working on a new album.<ref name="Facebook 'Girls Go Wild'">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/iamLP/videos/490100428128322/|title='Girls Gone Wild' Facebook Post|date=15 August 2018|publisher=[[Facebook]]|access-date=August 18, 2018}}</ref>  A day later, the first track from the record, "Girls Go Wild" was released.<ref name="Billboard">{{cite magazine |last=Bote |first=Joshua |title=LP Announces New Album, Shares New Single 'Girls Go Wild': Watch |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/pride/8470816/lp-new-album-single-girls-go-wild |magazine=Billboard |access-date=August 18, 2018}}</ref>

In September 2018, LP recorded two songs for the singer [[Morrissey]] for his new album of cover songs.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.morrissey-solo.com/threads/lp-laura-pergolizzi-and-morrissey-photos-9-total-lp-sings-on-two-tracks-morrissey-central.142896/|title=LP (Laura Pergolizzi) and Morrissey photos (9 total); LP sings on two tracks - Morrissey Central |publisher=[[Morrissey]] |access-date=2018-10-22|language=en-US}}</ref>

In October 2019, the song "Strange" was featured on the commercial for the [[Samsung]] Galaxy phone.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tvadvertsongs.com/samsung-its-your-galaxy/|title=TV Commercial Songs|date=October 2, 2019|access-date=October 21, 2019}}</ref>
