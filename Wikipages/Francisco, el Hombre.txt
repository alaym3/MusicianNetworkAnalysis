'''Francisco, el Hombre''' is a [[Brazil]]ian rock, Mexican, Brazilian and Latin American music band formed in 2013 by Mexican-born Brazilian<ref name="metropolis">{{cite web|title=Metrópolis: Francisco, el Hombre|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tEtPUPF2QZY|website=Metrópolis|publisher=[[Fundação Padre Anchieta]]|accessdate=19 January 2017|language=Portuguese|date=6 December 2016}}</ref> brothers Sebastián and Mateo Piracés-Ugarte in the city of [[Campinas]], [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]].<ref name="diariodaregiao">{{Cite web|last=Delalibera|first=Graziela|title=Banda Francisco, El Hombre se apresenta no Sesc|URL=http://www.diariodaregiao.com.br/divirtase/banda-francisco-el-hombre-se-apresenta-no-sesc-1.351781|work=[[Diário da Região]]|access-date=20 September 2016|date=21 July 2015}}</ref><ref name="bananada">{{Cite web|last=Rabassallo|first=Luciana|title=Bananada 2015: novatos do Francisco, el Hombre roubam a cena no festival em noite com encerramento do Pato Fu|URL=http://rollingstone.uol.com.br/noticia/bananada-2015-novatos-no-francisco-el-hombre-roubam-cena-do-festival-em-noite-com-encerramento-do-pato-fu/#imagem0|work=[[Rolling Stone]] Brasil|publisher=[[Grupo Spring de Comunicação]]|access-date=20 September 2016|date=16 May 2015}}</ref><ref name="g1prespru">{{Cite web|title=Francisco, El Hombre faz show bilíngue de folk dançante|URL=http://g1.globo.com/sp/presidente-prudente-regiao/noticia/2016/05/francisco-el-hombre-faz-show-bilingue-de-folk-dancante.html|work=G1|publisher=[[Grupo Globo]]|access-date=19 September 2016|date=13 May 2016}}</ref>

With three other Brazilian-born members,<ref name="inker">{{Cite web|title=Francisco, el Hombre disponibiliza o primeiro disco cheio da carreira, SOLTASBRUXA, no YouTube|URL=http://inkeragencia.com/2016/09/02/francisco-el-hombre-disponibiliza-o-primeiro-disco-cheio-da-carreira-soltasbruxa-no-youtube/|work=Inker Agência Cultural|access-date=20 September 2016|date=2 September 2016}}</ref> the group mixes musical elements from both countries and others from [[Latin America]], based on continental trips by the brothers, resulting in music sung in Spanish, Portuguese and English. Their 2017 song "Triste, Louca ou Má" (Sad, Crazy or Bad) was nominated for the [[18th Annual Latin Grammy Awards|2017]] [[Latin Grammy Awards|Latin Grammy award]] for [[Latin Grammy Award for Best Portuguese Language Song|Best Portuguese Language Song]].<ref name="billboardinter"/>

== History ==
After traveling around the world with their instruments, Mexican brothers Sebastián and Mateo Piracés-Ugarte moved to Brazil in the mid 2000s, naturalized themselves,<ref name="metropolis"/> settled in the district of [[Barão Geraldo]] in [[Campinas]], [[São Paulo (state)|São Paulo]],<ref name="20min"/><ref name="g1mochilazo"/><ref name="tnbrasil"/> and formed the band with the objective of "
quitting jobs, college and every 'bond with 
society'".<ref name="portaluai"/> The name of the group was inspired [[:es:Francisco el Hombre|by a figure of the same name]] of [[Colombia]]n folklore,<ref name="billboardinter"/> known for his street performances of the accordion.<ref name="diariodaregiao"/><ref name="portaluai"/><ref name="elguia"/><ref name="tnbrasil"/><ref name="RSBVPC">{{Cite web|title=Banda Francisco, el Hombre pretende realizar documentário em Cuba|URL=http://rollingstone.uol.com.br/noticia/banda-francisco-el-hombre-pretende-realizar-filme-documentario-em-cuba/|work=[[Rolling Stone]] Brasil]|publisher=[[Grupo Spring de Comunicação]]|access-date=20 September 2016|date=29 March 2016}}</ref>

Until 2015, they had already done two South-America tours, with the second being called "Mochilazo"<ref name="g1mochilazo"/> In the beginning of that year, after a show in [[Mendoza, Argentina|Mendoza]], [[Argentina]], they were robbed and lost all their belongings, including instruments and documents. They only made it back to Brazil after an on-line campaign, help from friends and the local community, and funds raised via public performances with borrowed instruments.<ref name="inker"/><ref name="elguia"/><ref name="g1assalto">{{Cite web|title=Banda brasileira é roubada na Argentina, cancela turnê e pede ajuda|URL=http://g1.globo.com/musica/noticia/2015/01/banda-brasileira-e-roubada-na-argentina-cancela-turne-e-pede-ajuda.html|work=G1|publisher=[[Grupo Globo]]|access-date=20 September 2016|date=16 January 2015}}</ref><ref name="faixa1">{{Cite web|last=Menezes|first=Caio|title=Após assalto na Argentina, Francisco, el Hombre planeja turnê e documentário em Cuba|URL=http://faixa1.ig.com.br/?p=75|work=[[Internet Group|Faixa 1]]|publisher=[[Ongoing Strategy Investments]]|access-date=20 September 2016|date=9 April 2016}}</ref>

In April 2015, they released their EP ''La Pachanga!'', with six original tracks.<ref name="g1prespru"/> The release event took place at the Centro Cultural São Paulo on 7 May, where they also recorded a video for the track "Dicen" (They Say), which featured Chilean singer [[Francisca Valenzuela]]<ref name="diariodaregiao"/> in the studio version. According to the founding brothers, the track was created so they could talk to their young nephews about dictatorship.<ref name="dicen">{{Cite web|last=Brêda|first=Lucas|title=Francisco, El Hombre esbanja vitalidade em performance de "Dicen"; assista|url=http://rollingstone.uol.com.br/blog/sobe-o-som/francisco-el-hombre-esbanja-vitalidade-em-performance-de-dicen-assista#imagem0|work=[[Rolling Stone]] Brasil|publisher=[[Grupo Spring de Comunicação]]|access-date=20 September 2016|date=6 August 2015}}</ref> The EP also features the track "Minha Casa" (My House), the only totally in Portuguese written in Africa.<ref name="diariodaregiao"/>

In 2016, they started the campaign #VaiPraCuba (#GoToCuba), through which they planned to finance a documentary about culture on the [[Cuba|communist island]], while on tour in the country to take part in the project "El Sur Suena", at the festival "AMPM – América por Su Música" in [[Havana]].<ref name="elguia"/><ref name="faixa1"/><ref name="radiorock">{{Cite web|title=Francisco, el Hombre lança financiamento coletivo para realizar documentário em Cuba|URL=http://www.radiorock.com.br/francisco-el-hombre-lanca-financiamento-coletivo-para-realizar-documentario-em-cuba|work=[[89 FM a Rádio Rock]]|publisher=[[Grupo Camargo de Comunicação]]|access-date=20 September 2016|date=22 March 2016}}</ref> In the middle of that year, they did another Latin America tour.<ref name="20min"/><ref name="RSBVPC"/>

In June 2016, they released a video for the track "Calor da Rua", produced by [[Curumin]] and Zé Nigro, which deals with [[domestic violence]] and would be part of their debut album.<ref name="jukebox">{{Cite web|title=Três videoclipes nacionais: Francisco, El Hombre, Rapha Moares e Zumbis do Espaço|URL=http://movethatjukebox.com/tres-videoclipes-nacionais-francisco-el-monbre-rapha-moraes-e-zumbis-do-espaco/|work=Move That Jukebox|access-date=20 September 2016|date=15 June 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160923012820/http://movethatjukebox.com/tres-videoclipes-nacionais-francisco-el-monbre-rapha-moraes-e-zumbis-do-espaco/|archive-date=23 September 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album, titled ''[[Soltasbruxa|SOLTASBRUXA]]'', was released on 2 September. It was procuded by Zé Nigro and it featured guest performances of Liniker and [[Apanhador Só]], besides political lyrics with social commentary.<ref name="inker"/>

In March 2019, they released their second studio album, ''[[Rasgacabeza|RASGACABEZA]]''.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web| title=Rolling Stone · Incendiária e combativa, Francisco El Hombre joga gasolina na monotonia com novo disco Rasgacabeza|url=https://rollingstone.uol.com.br/noticia/incendiaria-e-combativa-francisco-el-hombre-joga-gasolina-na-monotonia-com-novo-disco-rasgacabeza/|work=Rolling Stone|access-date=2019-03-18|language=pt-br|first=AMDB|last=Internet (amdb.com.br)}}</ref> In June 2020, they used a new single, "Despedida" (Farewell) to announce the departure of bassist Rafael Gomes, who had been with them for 6,5 years.<ref name="rafaelsai">{{cite web |last=Ferreira |first=Mauro |title=Banda Francisco El Hombre comunica saída de baixista com 'Despedida' |url=https://g1.globo.com/pop-arte/musica/blog/mauro-ferreira/post/2020/06/22/banda-francisco-el-hombre-comunica-saida-de-baixista-com-despedida.ghtml |work=[[G1 (website)|G1]] |publisher=[[Grupo Globo]] |access-date=30 June 2020 |date=22 June 2020 |lang=Portuguese}}</ref> He was replaced in the same year by Helena Papini, a name suggested by Gomes himself.<ref name="UFMGentre">{{cite web |last=Sartoreto |first=Filipe |title=Banda 'Francisco, El Hombre' lança terceiro álbum de estúdio acompanhado por reality show |trans-title=Band 'Francisco, El Hombre' releases third studio album accompanied by reality show |url=https://soundcloud.com/radioufmgeducativa/expresso-francisco-el-hombre-14-de-outubro?si=59fa18ce02864336a8358768948e1fc4 |work=Rádio UFMG Educativa |publisher=[[Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais|UFMG]] |access-date=8 November 2021 |date=14 October 2021}}</ref>

In October 2021, following a period of isolation, the band released their third studio album, ''[[Casa Francisco]]''.<ref name="UFMGentre"/>
