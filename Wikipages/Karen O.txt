'''Karen Lee Orzolek''' (born November 22, 1978),<ref name="birthyear">{{cite news|last=Marks|first=Craig|title=Yeah Yeah Yeahs: Stayin' Alive|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]|url=https://www.spin.com/2009/02/yeah-yeah-yeahs-stayin-alive/|access-date=November 22, 2010|date=February 25, 2009|quote=On a wintry evening in late November [2008], Karen O celebrated her 30th birthday by ...}}</ref> known professionally as '''Karen O''', is a South Korean-born American singer, songwriter, musician, and record producer. She is the [[lead vocalist]] for American rock band [[Yeah Yeah Yeahs]].

==Early life==

==Career==
O is best known as the lead vocalist for the band [[Yeah Yeah Yeahs]]. She has also been noted for her sense of [[fashion]], wearing ostentatious outfits made by her friend, fashion designer [[Christian Joy]]. In the early days of the band, she became well known for her outrageous antics during live shows.<ref name="goth"/> Their first ever gig was opening for [[The White Stripes]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Ganz |first=Caryn |url=https://www.spin.com/2013/04/yeah-yeah-yeahs-mosquito-karen-o-cover-story-march-2013/ |title=Yeah Yeah Yeahs Get Free |work=Spin |access-date=April 21, 2014}}</ref>

During a tour for the 2003 [[Livid (festival)|Livid]] Festival in Australia, at a sideshow at The Metro in Sydney, O accidentally danced off the stage and was reluctantly taken to a hospital. A few days later, at the Sydney leg of the Livid Festival, she appeared in a wheelchair pushed onstage by [[Angus Andrew]].<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.smh.com.au/articles/2003/10/12/1065917279681.html | work=The Sydney Morning Herald | title=Boys with the hoods clean up | date=October 13, 2003}}</ref>

In 2007, O was placed at number three on [[Spinner.com]]'s Women Who Rock Right Now.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spinner.com/2007/07/15/women-who-rock-right-now-no-3/|title=Women Who Rock Right Now: No. 3|date=July 15, 2007|website=Spinner.com|access-date=February 26, 2010}}</ref> She began working on a small side project called [[Native Korean Rock]] and the Fishnets with fellow NYC musicians in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prefixmag.com/media/native-korean-rock-the-fishnets/various-tracks-stream/20067|title=Various Tracks (Stream) - Prefixmag.com|first=Andrew|last=Winistorfer|website=Prefixmag.com|access-date=October 1, 2017}}</ref>

In 2011, she contributed vocals on the song "Pinky's Dream" on the David Lynch debut album ''[[Crazy Clown Time]]''. In 2012, she collaborated with experimental rock group [[Swans (band)|Swans]] on the song "Song for a Warrior" on their album ''[[The Seer (Swans album)|The Seer]]''. She also lends vocals to the song "GO!" on [[Santigold]]'s 2012 album ''[[Master of My Make-Believe]]''.

===Soundtrack work===
O was featured in the track "Cut Me Up" by [[Har Mar Superstar]] for the soundtrack to the 2005 horror film ''[[House of Wax (2005 film)|House of Wax]]''. For the movie ''[[Jackass 2]]'', O collaborated with electronic artist [[Peaches (musician)|Peaches]] and [[Johnny Knoxville]] to record a track entitled "Backass"; for ''[[Jackass 3D]]'', she covered the [[Roger Alan Wade]]'s "If You're Gonna Be Dumb" under the alternative title of "If You're Gonna Be Dumb, You Gotta Be Tough". In 2007, she also contributed vocals to a version of [[Bob Dylan]]'s "[[Highway 61 Revisited (song)|Highway 61 Revisited]]" for the ''[[I'm Not There]]'' film soundtrack.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/archives/new-karen-o-highway-61-revisited-stereogum-premier.html|title=stereogum: New Karen O – "Highway 61 Revisited"|publisher=[[Stereogum]]|access-date=October 1, 2017}}</ref> At the end of episode 409, "Brothers Cinco", of the ''[[Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job!]]'' she performed a short song. She also has a song titled "Strange Love" on the album "Frankenweenie Unleashed!"

O collaborated with [[Trent Reznor]] and [[Atticus Ross]] on a cover version of [[Led Zeppelin]]'s "[[Immigrant Song]]" for the soundtrack to the 2011 film ''[[The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (2011 film)|The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo]]'', for which Reznor and Ross composed the score.<ref>{{cite web|date=May 27, 2011|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/42655-trent-reznor-and-karen-o-cover-led-zeppelin/|title=Trent Reznor and Karen O Cover Led Zeppelin|author=Tom Breihan|work=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|access-date=May 29, 2011}}</ref> In December 2011, it was announced that the song would open the soundtrack album, be released as the opening single, and be available free to all who pre-ordered before the official release date of December 9.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1675322/girl-with-dragon-tattoo-soundtrack-listing.jhtml|title='Girl With The Dragon Tattoo' Track List Revealed|publisher=MTV|date=December 2, 2011|access-date=December 2, 2011|author=Gil Kaufman}}</ref>

====Work with Spike Jonze====
O and [[Spike Jonze]] collaborated on a 2005 [[Adidas]] commercial, ''[[Hello Tomorrow]]'', after Jonze had directed the video for the Yeah Yeah Yeahs' 2004 single "Y Control". O composed all songs on [[Where the Wild Things Are: Motion Picture Soundtrack|the soundtrack]] of Jonze's film ''[[Where the Wild Things Are (film)|Where the Wild Things Are]]'' (with the exception of a cover of the [[Daniel Johnston]] song "Worried Shoes") in collaboration with [[Carter Burwell]]. O also contributed "[[The Moon Song]]" to Jonze's 2013 film ''[[Her (film)|Her]]''.<ref>{{cite web|last=Minsker|first=Evan|title=Karen O Also Has a Song in Spike Jonze's Film Her|url=https://pitchfork.com/news/51856-karen-o-also-has-a-song-in-spike-jonzes-film-her/|work=Pitchfork|access-date=January 5, 2014}}</ref> O and Jonze were nominated in 2014 for an [[Academy Award for Best Original Song]] for "The Moon Song".<ref>{{cite web|title=2014 Oscar Nominess |url=http://oscar.go.com/nominees |publisher=Academy of Motion Arts and Sciences |access-date=January 21, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121229222408/http://oscar.go.com/nominees |archive-date=December 29, 2012 }}</ref>

===''KO at Home'' demo leak===
On December 10, 2006, a home-recorded album titled ''[[KO at Home]]'' was leaked via an invite-only [[BitTorrent (protocol)|BitTorrent]] tracking site. The album, originally a personal gift to [[Dave Sitek]] of [[TV on the Radio]], was discovered inside a suitcase that Sitek had left behind in a New York City apartment. The disc's 14 tracks and scan of the cover (a photograph of O with a poem written by [[Oscar Wilde]] on the back side of the photo) quickly spread. News sites broke the story of the leak when Sitek lashed out at the fan who leaked the demos.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/archives/004140.html|title=Karen (Dem)O|date=December 11, 2006|publisher=[[Stereogum]]|access-date=October 1, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stereogum.com/archives/004144.html|title=The Story Behind The Karen O Demo|date=December 11, 2006|publisher=Stereogum|access-date=October 1, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://youngliars.blogspot.com/2006_12_01_archive.html#39643304333086041|title=completely surrounded by no trees: 12/01/2006 - 01/01/2007|website=Youngliars.blogspot.com|access-date=October 1, 2017}}</ref> Eventually, Sitek followed up his comments with an apology letter.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www2.blogger.com/email-post.g?blogID=6198312&postID=7815441358413327623|title=Blogger|website=www2.blogger.com|access-date=October 1, 2017}}</ref> In response to the leak, O said that "[[shit happens]]", and although she was "a little grossed out", she offered commentary on which of the songs she liked the most.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/yeah-yeah-yeahs-113-1338521 |title=Yeah Yeah Yeahs discuss demo leak |work=NME |date=December 14, 2006 |access-date=October 7, 2017}}</ref>

==''Lux Prima''==
Lux Prima is the collaborative album from Karen O and musician-producer [[Danger Mouse (musician)|Danger Mouse]], released 15 March 2019. The two originally met to discuss collaboration in 2007 as mutual admirers, but careers and lives got in the way. In 2016 they entered the studio with no sonic parameters and a handful of references to make the album

===An Encounter with ''Lux Prima''===

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UNbrrS-J_7g “An Encounter with Lux Prima”] was an immersive art piece and communal listening experience soundtracked by Karen O and Danger Mouse's collaborative album Lux Prima. The installation premiered at Los Angeles' Marciano Art Foundation in April 2019 where it played to sold-out audiences.  The piece, consisting of groundbreaking audio-visual technology, saw the duo collaborate with an illustrious cast of fellow artists including creative director Barnaby Clay (David Bowie—“Life On Mars” Revisited, SHOT!), sound designer Ren Klyce (Star Wars: The Last Jedi, Fight Club), lighting designer Tobias Rylander (Robyn, The xx, FKA twigs), visual artist Davy Evans, collaborating with George Lucas’ Skywalker Sound and Meyer Sound on the audio design and build as well as MSG's Obscura Digital on projection mapping.
