'''Melesha Katrina O'Garro''' {{postnominals|country=GBR|size=100%|BEM}} (born 15 December 1988), known professionally as '''Lady Leshurr''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|l|iː|ʃ|ər}}),<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdmJYCFhXf8 "Lady Leshurr Reveals Origin Of Her Name and Correct Way To Pronounce It"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170818193046/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdmJYCFhXf8 |date=18 August 2017 }}.</ref> is a British rapper, singer, songwriter and producer.<ref name="progresslink">{{Cite web|url=http://progresslink.co.uk/LadyLeshurr/about/|title=About << LadyLeshurr.com|publisher=Progresslink.co.uk|access-date=18 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140505044349/http://progresslink.co.uk/LadyLeshurr/about/|archive-date=5 May 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> She is known for her ''Queen's Speech'' series of [[Freestyle rap|freestyles]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.whatsgoodonline.co.uk/lady-leshurr-talks-mona-leshurr/|title=Lady Leshurr: 7 interesting facts about the 'Queen's Speech' rapper|publisher=What's Good?|access-date=22 January 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160128001618/http://www.whatsgoodonline.co.uk/lady-leshurr-talks-mona-leshurr/|archive-date=28 January 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> the fourth of which became popular in 2016. Her subsequent freestyle, ''Queen's Speech 5'', was called "brilliant" and "2015's crowning freestyle" by ''[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]]''.<ref>Carley, Brennan [http://www.spin.com/2015/12/lady-leshurr-queens-speech-5-music-video-watch/ Lady Leshurr's Brilliant 'Queen's Speech 5' Is 2015's Crowning Freestyle] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160101054331/http://www.spin.com/2015/12/lady-leshurr-queens-speech-5-music-video-watch/ |date=1 January 2016 }} ''Spin''. 30 December 2015</ref> In 2021, she entered the Channel 4 series ''[[The Celebrity Circle]]'' for [[Stand Up to Cancer (UK)|Stand Up to Cancer]], catfishing as [[Big Narstie]], and won. She also competed in the [[Dancing on Ice (series 13)|thirteenth series]] of ''[[Dancing on Ice]]'', where she reached the semi-final.

==Early life==
Born to parents from the [[Caribbean]]<ref name="theguardian">{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/apr/20/lady-leshurr-interview-blazin-video|title=Lady Leshurr: 'The industry just doesn't know what to do with women'|work=[[The Guardian]]|date=20 April 2013|access-date=16 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140826114055/http://www.theguardian.com/music/2013/apr/20/lady-leshurr-interview-blazin-video|archive-date=26 August 2014|url-status=live}}</ref> Isle of [[Saint Kitts]],<ref name="sxsw"/> her first foray was into poetry and writing at age six<ref name="britishunderground">{{Cite web|url=http://britishunderground.net/bassculture/the-project/artists/lady-leshurr/|title=Lady Leshurr|publisher=Britishunderground.net|access-date=18 November 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130715073646/http://britishunderground.net/bassculture/the-project/artists/lady-leshurr/|archive-date=15 July 2013}}</ref> but she decided that writing music would bring her to a wider audience,<ref name="theguardian"/> releasing her first [[mixtape]] at age fourteen.<ref name="bbc">{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/glastonbury/2010/artists/ladyleshurr/|title=Glastonbury Festival – Lady Leshurr|publisher=[[BBC]]|access-date=16 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150925054236/http://www.bbc.co.uk/glastonbury/2010/artists/ladyleshurr/|archive-date=25 September 2015|url-status=live}}</ref> In her words:
{{quote|1=I grew up around music. My mum used to play a lot of reggae like [[Sister Nancy]] and [[Bob Marley]] around the house so basically, when I was around six, I was messing about and doing my own little raps and songs. My family are all rappers and singers anyway so I was always involved in it from a young age.|2=Lady Leshurr, 2011<ref name="independent">{{cite news|url=http://blogs.independent.co.uk/2011/11/30/lady-leshurr-the-female-busta-rhymes/|title=Lady Leshurr: The female Busta Rhymes|work=[[The Independent]]|access-date=16 October 2013|location=London|first=Errol|last=Anderson|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131015095141/http://blogs.independent.co.uk/2011/11/30/lady-leshurr-the-female-busta-rhymes/|archive-date=15 October 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>}}

==Career==
Leshurr played one of the lead female roles in Vertigo Films' 2009 film ''[[1 Day]]''.<ref name="wuawo">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/music/urban/202659-wrap-up-and-watch-out-lady-leshurr|title=WRAP UP AND WATCH OUT: LADY LESHURR|publisher=[[MTV UK]]|access-date=26 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203211724/http://www.mtv.co.uk/music/urban/202659-wrap-up-and-watch-out-lady-leshurr|archive-date=3 December 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> She won second place in the Best Female category of the Official Mixtape Awards 2011. To complement the music releases, she has designed a range of merchandise, the "Friggin L" clothing line<ref name="rtd">{{cite web|url=http://rtdproductionz.com/tag/lady-leshurr/page/2/|title=lady leshurr|publisher=RTD Productions|access-date=26 November 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20131126092648/http://rtdproductionz.com/tag/lady-leshurr/page/2/|archive-date=26 November 2013}}</ref> named after her 2011 mixtape ''Friggin L''.<ref name="soulculture">{{cite web|url=http://www.soulculture.co.uk/music-blog/newmusic/lady-leshurr-unleashes-friggin-l-mixtape-free-download/|title=Lady Leshurr unleashes Friggin L Mixtape|publisher=[[SoulCulture]]|access-date=26 November 2013|archive-url=https://archive.today/20131126092647/http://www.soulculture.co.uk/music-blog/newmusic/lady-leshurr-unleashes-friggin-l-mixtape-free-download/|archive-date=26 November 2013|url-status=live}}</ref>

In 2016, she won the [[MOBO Award]], celebrating excellence in black music, for Best Female Act. In 2019, she won the award for "Best Rap / Grime Act" and in 2020 she won "Best Solo Female" at the Birmingham Music Awards.[https://69-degrees.co.uk/musicevents/lady-leshurr-birmingham-music-awards/] In 2019, Leshurr performed at [[The Nicki Wrld Tour]] in [[Birmingham]] and [[Manchester]] as a special guest for rapper [[Nicki Minaj]]. She was awarded the [[British Empire Medal]] (BEM) in the [[2020 Birthday Honours]] for services to music and charity.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=63142|supp=y|page=B74|date=10 October 2020}}</ref> In 2020, she appeared in a commercial advertising for beauty brand [[Olay]] and became the first Black British woman to lead a campaign for the company.[https://metro.co.uk/2020/06/23/lady-leshurr-defends-olay-advert-trolls-accuse-losing-integrity-12890348/] In late 2020, it was announced that Lady Leshurr would be joining the line-up at [[BBC Radio 1Xtra]] with her own Saturday afternoon radio show.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-12-17|title=Nadia Jae announced as new 1Xtra Breakfast Show presenter|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/newsbeat-55347081|access-date=2021-11-27}}</ref> In 2021, she appeared as a contestant on the [[Dancing on Ice (series 13)|thirteenth series]] of ''[[Dancing on Ice]]'', partnered with [[Brendyn Hatfield]].<ref name="Name" /> After surviving three skate-offs during the competition, Leshurr was eliminated in the semi-final, leaving her in fourth place.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mirror.co.uk/tv/tv-news/breaking-dancing-ices-lady-leshurr-23625224|title=Dancing On Ice fans devastated as Lady Leshurr is 'unfairly' axed ahead of final|website=[[Daily Mirror]]|date=2021-03-07|accessdate=2021-03-07}}</ref> She also won ''[[The Celebrity Circle]]'' for [[Stand Up to Cancer (UK)|Stand Up to Cancer]]. In 2021, Leshurr announced a partnership with [https://www.themediaeye.com/News/fd178b03-99f4-42d6-8f17-adb7008d20a4 Kamo Premium Vodka], through which she became a co-owner.

==Influences==
Leshurr has named [[Ashley Walters (actor)|Ashley Walters]], [[Eminem]], [[Busta Rhymes]], Ms Dynamite, [[Missy Elliott]] and [[Lil Wayne]] as her primary influences.<ref name="mtv">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.co.uk/news/lady-leshurr/347869-lady-leshurr-loves-lil-wayne|title=Lady Leshurr Loves Lil Wayne|publisher=[[MTV]]|access-date=16 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150414231321/http://www.mtv.co.uk/news/lady-leshurr/347869-lady-leshurr-loves-lil-wayne|archive-date=14 April 2015|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120215144939/https://www.roc4life.com/profiles/blogs/on-the-rise-lady-leshurr|title=Lady Leshurr|url=https://www.roc4life.com/profiles/blogs/on-the-rise-lady-leshurr|archivedate=February 15, 2012}}</ref> Nia Kay, a competitor on ''[[The Rap Game]]'' season 2, stated that Leshurr was one of her influences due to her uniqueness and rap skill.<ref>{{cite web|date=22 September 2015|title=Everyone is talking about Lady Leshurr|url=http://www.shazam.com/news/everyone-is-talking-about-lady-leshurr|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208015220/http://www.shazam.com/news/everyone-is-talking-about-lady-leshurr|archive-date=8 December 2015|access-date=26 November 2015|work=[[Shazam (service)|Shazam]]}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Leshurr came out as [[pansexual]] in September 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gaytimes.co.uk/culture/113191/lady-leshurr-interview-october-2018/|title=Lady Leshurr comes out as pansexual: "If you can't take me for who I am then you can leave"|last=Johnson|first=Shakeena|website=[[Gay Times]]|date=21 September 2018|access-date=21 September 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180921112113/https://www.gaytimes.co.uk/culture/113191/lady-leshurr-interview-october-2018/|archive-date=21 September 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> In late 2019 Leshurr took time out from her music career following the death of her sister from breast cancer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Blush Winter 2021|url=https://blushmagazine.org.uk/magazines/blush-winter-2021/|access-date=2021-11-27|website=Blush Magazine|language=en-GB}}</ref>
