'''Sabrina Claudio''' (born September 19, 1996) is an American singer and songwriter.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-10-23|title=Sabrina Claudio Shares How Bossa Nova & Belly Dancing Influenced Her Music in New Doc|url=https://remezcla.com/lists/music/sabrina-claudio-apple-music-up-next/|access-date=2020-10-10|website=Remezcla|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-10-06|title=40 Under 40: Music's Top Young Power Players Revealed (2017) {{!}} Billboard|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/7989300/40-under-40-music-industry-power-players-2017-full-list|access-date=2020-06-19|website=Billboard}}</ref> In late 2016, Claudio uploaded several songs to [[SoundCloud]], before compiling a select collection as part of her debut [[extended play]], ''Confidently Lost'', which was released independently in March 2017. Her single "Unravel Me" peaked at number 22 on the [[Billboard Twitter Real-Time|''Billboard'' Twitter Emerging Artists]] chart, and her single "Belong to You" peaked at number two on the same and was certified Gold by the RIAA. Her debut mixtape, ''[[About Time (mixtape)|About Time]]'' was released on October 5, 2017. It peaked at number 13 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums]] chart.

==Life and career==
Claudio grew up in [[Fort Lauderdale]]. She is of [[Cubans|Cuban]] and [[Puerto Ricans|Puerto Rican]] descent. She later moved to [[Los Angeles]], where she began her music career in earnest.<ref name="LAT">{{cite news | last=Kennedy| first=Gerrick D.| title =What to listen to now: Sabrina Claudio, Little Dragon, Charlie Puth and Brad Paisley | newspaper =Los Angeles Times| date =April 26, 2017 | url =http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-what-listen-now-20170426-story.html| access-date =August 14, 2017}}</ref> She first began recording and releasing video [[Cover version|covers]] on [[Twitter]] and [[YouTube]] before transitioning to original tracks which she released on [[SoundCloud]].<ref name="HarberBazaar">{{cite news |last1=Gonzales |first1=Erica |title=Sabrina Claudio Went from Posting YouTube Covers to Being R&B’s Rising Star |url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/art-books-music/a13763242/sabrina-claudio-interview/ |access-date=April 17, 2019 |publisher=Harpar's Bazaar |date=November 29, 2017}}</ref> Over the course of 2016, Claudio released several singles, including "Runnin' Thru Lovers,"<ref name="Vulture">{{cite news | last=McCraw| first=Justin| title =7 Best New Songs of the Week| publisher =Vulture| date =April 19, 2016 | url =http://www.vulture.com/2016/04/this-weeks-best-new-music-skepta-missy-elliott.html| access-date =August 14, 2017}}</ref> "Orion's Belt,"<ref name="Remez">{{cite news | last=Calderon| first=John| title =8 Songs We Had on Repeat in May| publisher =Remezcla| date =May 31, 2016 | url =http://remezcla.com/lists/music/8-songs-repeat-may/| access-date =August 14, 2017}}</ref> and ''Confidently Lost.''<ref name="Respect">{{cite news | title =Sabrina Claudio’s New Video for "Confidently Lost" + EP| publisher =Respect | date =October 18, 2016 | url =http://respect-mag.com/2016/10/sabrina-claudios-new-video-confidently-lost/| access-date =August 14, 2017}}</ref>

These songs would form part of her EP, ''Confidently Lost'', which she originally released independently on SoundCloud. The EP received a wide release in March 2017 via SC Entertainment.<ref name="Fader">{{cite magazine | last=Tanzer| first=Myles| title =Sabrina Claudio Meets Her Own Reflection In "Belong To You"| magazine =The Fader| date =July 28, 2017 | url =http://www.thefader.com/2017/07/28/sabrina-claudio-belong-to-you-interview| access-date =August 16, 2017}}</ref> In May 2017, she released a single, "Unravel Me," off of an upcoming project due to be released later in 2017.<ref name="Complex">{{cite magazine | last=Tharpe| first=Frazier| title =Forget About Katy Perry's New Single and Listen to the Song Right Under It| magazine =Complex| date =May 19, 2017 | url =http://www.complex.com/music/2017/05/sabrina-claudio-new-song-katy-perry| access-date =August 16, 2017}}</ref> The song would go on to peak at #22 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Twitter]] Emerging Artists chart. She released another single for that project entitled "Belong to You" in July 2017, which peaked at #2 on the same chart.<ref name="Fader"/>

It was announced in August 2017 that Claudio would be touring [[North America]] with [[6lack]] on his Free 6lack Tour.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Robles|first1=Jonathan|title=Sabrina Claudio Will Open for 6LACK on Upcoming Fall Tour|url=http://variancemagazine.com/news/8216-sabrina-claudio-opening-for-6lack-tour|access-date=August 16, 2017|publisher=Variance Magazine|date=August 16, 2017}}</ref>

On October 5, 2017, Claudio's debut full-length digital mixtape, ''[[About Time (mixtape)|About Time]]'', was officially released. The mixtape is supported by the singles "Unravel Me" and "Belong to You".<ref name="VibeSC">{{cite news |last1=Ellis |first1=Stacy-Ann |title=Sabrina Claudio Insists It's 'About Time' We Welcome Her Soothing Voice Into The Fall |url=https://www.vibe.com/2017/10/sabrina-claudio-about-time |access-date=April 17, 2019 |publisher=Vibe |date=October 6, 2017}}</ref> From October through November 2017, [[Apple Music]] promoted Claudio as their ''[[Apple Music Up Next|Up Next]]'' artist, a series that focuses on breakthrough artists by documenting their journey, inspiration and influences through exclusive interviews, live performances and a mini-documentary.<ref name="UpNext">{{cite magazine|last=Penrose|first=Nerisha|title=Sabrina Claudio Is Apple Music's Newest 'Up Next' Artist: Exclusive|magazine=Billboard| date=October 19, 2017| url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8006564/apple-music-up-next-artist-sabrina-claudio-video|access-date=November 5, 2017}}|work=The Source|access-date=November 20, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

On April 2, 2018, Claudio released the single "All to You" followed by "Don't Let Me Down" featuring [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]] on April 4. Her debut studio album ''[[No Rain, No Flowers]]'', was released on August 15, 2018, preceded by the single "Messages From Her".<ref>{{cite news|title=New Music: Sabrina Claudio - 'All to You'|website=[[Rap-Up]]|date=April 2, 2018|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2018/04/02/new-music-sabrina-claudio-all-to-you/|access-date=April 3, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|first=Mike|last=Wass|title=Sabrina Claudio Returns With Sexy Bedroom Anthem "All To You"|website=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]|date=April 2, 2018|url=https://www.idolator.com/7676809/sabrina-claudio-new-single-all-to-you|access-date=April 3, 2018}}</ref> Her management is SAL&CO/Neal Atweh.

== Controversies ==
In April 2018, a series of [[screenshot]]s began to circulate around the social media app [[Twitter]] showcasing Claudio's alleged use of several [[racial slurs]], and other offensive language directed at minorities.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ngwadla|first=Nkosazana|title=Sabrina Claudio apologises for racist comments against black women|url=https://www.news24.com/drum/Celebs/sabrina-claudio-apologises-for-racist-comments-against-black-women-20180410|access-date=2020-12-10|website=Drum|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=sabrina claudio racist Archives|url=https://thesource.com/tag/sabrina-claudio-racist/|access-date=2020-12-10|website=The Source|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-04-11|title=Sabrina Claudio is the latest artist who profits from Black culture but hates blackness|url=https://afropunk.com/2018/04/sabrina-claudio-is-the-latest-artist-who-profits-from-black-culture-but-hates-blackness/|access-date=2020-12-10|website=AFROPUNK|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=R&B singer Sabrina Claudio apologizes for use of racial slurs in tweets.|url=https://www.mobo.com/news/rb-singer-sabrina-claudio-apologises-racist-social-media-comments|website=Mobo}}</ref> She also was accused of using a second account on Twitter to share racist messages.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-04-12|title=Twitter Drags Latina Singer Sabrina Claudio For Verbally Attacking Black Women|url=https://fierce.wearemitu.com/things-that-matter/sabrina-claudio-old-account-attack-black-women-proving-once-more-white-latinas-need-get-it-tf-together/|access-date=2020-12-10|website=Fierce|language=en-US}}</ref>

Claudio has since issued a now-deleted apology on Twitter for her actions.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sabrina Claudio Apologizes for 'Past Ignorance' Following Social Media Backlash Over Use of 'Insensitive Words'|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8301542/sabrina-claudio-racist-comments-apology|access-date=2020-12-10|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=R&B Singer Sabrina Claudio Apologizes (We Guess) for Derogatory Comments Targeting Black Women|url=https://thegrapevine.theroot.com/r-b-singer-sabrina-claudio-apologizes-we-guess-for-de-1825214399|access-date=2020-12-10|website=The Grapevine|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Sabrina Claudio Opens Up About 'Shameful' Comments, Readies New Project|url=https://www.rap-up.com/2018/08/09/sabrina-claudio-apologizes-for-shameful-comments/|access-date=2020-12-10|website=Rap-Up|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-04-13|title=Singer Sabrina Claudio Apologizes For Alleged Racist Comments On Social Media|url=https://www.oxygen.com/very-real/singer-sabrina-claudio-apologizes-racist-comments-social-media|access-date=2020-12-10|website=Oxygen Official Site|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Sabrina Claudio Apologizes For Racist Social Media Remarks|url=https://www.yahoo.com/lifestyle/sabrina-claudio-apologizes-racist-social-103741792.html|access-date=2020-12-10|website=www.yahoo.com|language=en-US}}</ref>
