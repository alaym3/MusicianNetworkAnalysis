'''Tione Jayden Merritt''' (born April 30, 2001), known professionally as '''Lil Tjay''',<ref name="Tjay Complex">{{cite web|url=https://amp.www.complex.com/music/2019/10/lil-tjay-everything-you-need-to-know|title=Everything You Need to Know About Lil Tjay|website=[[Complex (website)|Complex]]|last=Callas|first=Brad|date=October 11, 2019|accessdate=April 4, 2021}}</ref> is an American rapper and singer. His stage name is derived from the first letter of his first name and first three letters of his middle name.

In 2018, Lil Tjay signed to [[Columbia Records]]. Lil Tjay's debut studio album, ''[[True 2 Myself]]'', was released in 2019. The following year, he released the mixtape ''[[State of Emergency (Lil Tjay EP)|State of Emergency]]'' that features only [[Brooklyn drill|drill]] artists from New York City. ''True 2 Myself'' debuted at number 5 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], marking the album his first charted release. His second studio album, ''[[Destined 2 Win]]'', was released in 2021 and included the single "[[Calling My Phone]]", a collaboration with American singer [[6lack]], his highest-charting song yet.<ref name=":0" />

==Early life==
Merritt was born in the [[Bronx]] borough of New York City.<ref name="Tjay Complex"/> He was raised by a single mother and had two younger siblings in what was described as a "roomy" apartment. Merritt called himself the problematic child out of the three, as he would often get himself into petty robberies and school fights. At the age of 15, Merritt was arrested for one of his robberies and was sentenced for a year to a [[youth detention center]] where he would begin to write raps; they included one of his hit songs "Resume", which he released on [[SoundCloud]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/get-to-know-hip-hop-heartthrob-lil-tjay-who-wants-to-be-the-next-usher/|title=Get to Know Hip-Hop Heartthrob Lil Tjay, Who Wants to Be the Next Usher|website=[[Pitchfork (website)|Pitchfork]]|last=Pierre|first=Alphonse|date=January 18, 2019|accessdate=April 4, 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
===2016â€“2019: Beginnings, record deal, and ''True 2 Myself''===
In 2016, Lil Tjay began releasing music on [[SoundCloud]].<ref name="pitchfork.com">{{Cite web|url=https://pitchfork.com/features/rising/get-to-know-hip-hop-heartthrob-lil-tjay-who-wants-to-be-the-next-usher/|title=Get to Know Hip-Hop Heartthrob Lil Tjay, Who Wants to Be the Next Usher|website=Pitchfork|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref> "Resume", one of his first songs, was released when Lil Tjay was 16 years old, and quickly began spreading online.

On March 10, 2018, Lil Tjay competed and placed first at the Coast 2 Coast LIVE NYC All Ages Edition, where his performance gained the attention of a record label [[Artists and repertoire|A&R]] who was in attendance to judge the showcase.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://coast2coastlive.com/video/lil-tjay-performs-at-coast-2-coast-live-nyc-all-ages-edition-3-10-18-1st-place|title=Lil tjay â€“ Lil tjay Performs at Coast 2 Coast LIVE {{!}} NYC All Ages Edition 3/10/18 â€“ 1st Place {{!}} Videos {{!}} Coast 2 Coast LIVE {{!}} Largest Artist Showcase in the World|website=Coast 2 Coast Live|access-date=April 5, 2019}}</ref> Soon after, Lil Tjay released the single "[[Brothers (Lil Tjay song)|Brothers]]", which became his biggest song at the time and led to him signing with [[Columbia Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/garysuarez/2018/12/26/5-hip-hop-singles-to-watch-this-winter-on-the-billboard-hot-100/|title=5 Hip-Hop Singles To Watch This Winter On The Billboard Hot 100|last=Suarez|first=Gary|website=Forbes|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref>

Within the first ten months of Lil Tjay's music career, he had released five tracks that recorded over one million plays on [[SoundCloud]].<ref name="Elevator Exploding">{{Cite web|url=https://www.elevatormag.com/exploding-nyc-rapper-lil-tjay-doesnt-want-none-of-your-love/|title=Exploding NYC rapper Lil Tjay doesn't want 'None Of Your Love'|date=August 2, 2018|website=Elevator|access-date=April 5, 2019}}</ref><ref name="AllMusic Bio" /> "Brothers", "Resume", "Goat", and "Leaked" each accumulated tens of millions of streams.<ref name="SoundCloud Lil Tjay">{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/liltjay|title=Lil Tjay|website=SoundCloud|access-date=April 2, 2019}}</ref> In July 2018, Lil Tjay worked with producer [[CashMoneyAP]] to release "None of Your Love", which also amassed tens of millions of plays.<ref name="Elevator Exploding" /> Lil Tjay released his debut EP ''No Comparison'' at the end of 2018.<ref name="AllMusic Bio" />

In January 2019, Lil Tjay was featured on fellow American rapper [[Polo G]]'s single "[[Pop Out]]", which peaked at number 11 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref name="Itunes Lil Tjay">{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/lil-tjay/1436446949|title=Lil Tjay on Apple Music|website=Apple Music|access-date=April 2, 2019}}</ref> In July, he was featured on boy band [[PrettyMuch]]'s "Lying".<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Spanos|first=Brittany|date=July 19, 2019|title=Watch PrettyMuch, Lil Tjay's Graffiti-Covered 'Lying' Video|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/prettymuch-lying-lil-tjay-new-video-fomo-tour-861070/|url-status=live|access-date=March 5, 2021|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> Later that year, he released an EP titled ''F.N''; the project's lead single, "[[F.N (song)|F.N]]", peaked at number 56 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and became his first solo charting song. Lil Tjay released his debut album ''[[True 2 Myself]]'' on October 11, 2019. It debuted and peaked at number 5 on the US [[Billboard Hot 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. In December, he was featured on a remix of [[24kGoldn]]'s song "[[Valentino (24kGoldn song)|Valentino]]".<ref>{{Cite web|last=A.|first=Aron|date=January 3, 2020|title=24KGOLDN Taps Lil Tjay For "Valentino (Remix)"|url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/24kgoldn-taps-lil-tjay-for-valentino-remix-new-song.1985681.html|url-status=live|access-date=March 5, 2021|website=HotNewHipHop|language=en}}</ref>

==Artistry==
Influenced by his upbringing in [[the Bronx]], Lil Tjay is a melodic rapper who frequently incorporates [[Auto-Tune]] in his songs.<ref name="Rolling Stone Feel Old">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/lil-tjay-profile-artist-you-need-to-know-773450/|title=Rising Bronx Rapper Lil Tjay Will Make You Feel Old|last=Holmes|first=Charles|date=January 3, 2019|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=February 10, 2019}}</ref> He has drawn comparisons to fellow [[New York (state)|New York]] rapper [[A Boogie wit da Hoodie]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://grittyvibes.com/2018/06/lil-tjay-is-new-yorks-next-big-star/|title=Lil Tjay is New York's next big STAR|last=Hunt|first=Relly|date=June 21, 2018|website=Gritty Vibes Magazine|access-date=April 3, 2019}}</ref> In an interview with ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', he revealed that his biggest musical influences are [[Drake (musician)|Drake]], [[Meek Mill]], and [[Usher (musician)|Usher]].<ref name="Rolling Stone Feel Old" /> In the same interview, Lil Tjay described his music as having a melodic sound when singing or rapping about his past struggles. Lil Tjay also credits his success to his year spent in prison, where he claims that his ability to write songs peaked.<ref name="AllMusic Bio">{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/lil-tjay-mn0003807005/biography|title=Lil Tjay {{!}} Biography & History|website=AllMusic|access-date=April 3, 2019}}</ref> In [[Pitchfork (website)|''Pitchfork'']]'s featured article on Lil Tjay, he is referred to as a "hip-hop heartthrob".<ref name="pitchfork.com" /> Later in the interview, Lil Tjay explained that he is often called the "Bronx Justin Bieber" because of his sampling [[Justin Bieber]]'s "[[Baby (Justin Bieber song)|Baby]]".<ref name="pitchfork.com" />
