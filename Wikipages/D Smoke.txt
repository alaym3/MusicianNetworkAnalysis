'''Daniel Anthony Farris''' (born October 17, 1985), known professionally as '''D Smoke''', is an American rapper and songwriter from [[Inglewood, California|Inglewood]], [[California]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vibe.com/2019/11/d-smoke-rhythm-and-flow-interview|title=Meet D Smoke, Inglewood And Hip-Hop's Next Hometown Hero|date=2019-11-14|website=Vibe|language=en|access-date=2019-11-28}}</ref> He gained attention after winning the first season of the [[Netflix]] music competition show ''[[Rhythm + Flow|Rhythm + Flow]]'' in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newsweek.com/rhythm-flow-d-smoke-winner-netflix-1468552|title='Rhythm + Flow' winner D Smoke talks Kendrick Lamar comparisons, new music and life after Netflix|last=EDT|first=Janice Williams On 10/30/19 at 12:49 PM|date=2019-10-30|website=Newsweek|language=en|access-date=2019-11-28}}</ref> The following year, he independently released his debut album ''Black Habits'', which earned him nominations for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Album|Best Rap Album]] and [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]].

==Musical career==
Farris grew up in a musical family with his mother, brothers, and cousin being [[gospel music|gospel]] singers in [[Inglewood, California]]. His brother [[Sir (singer)|SiR]] is signed to [[Top Dawg Entertainment]]. In 2006, he started a songwriting group called WoodWorks with his brothers and his cousin [[Tiffany Gouché]], who wrote songs for [[Ginuwine]] and [[The Pussycat Dolls]], and is credited for co-writing "[[Never (Jaheim song)|Never]]" by [[Jaheim]].<ref name="Vibe">{{cite web |title=Meet D Smoke, Inglewood And Hip-Hop's Next Hometown Hero |url=https://www.vibe.com/2019/11/d-smoke-rhythm-and-flow-interview |website=Vibe |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title='RHYTHM + FLOW' WINNER D SMOKE TALKS NEW MUSIC, KENDRICK LAMAR COMPARISONS AND LIFE AFTER NETFLIX SHOW |url=https://www.newsweek.com/rhythm-flow-d-smoke-winner-netflix-1468552 |website=News Week |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref> He was also in a musical trio with his brother called N3D.<ref>{{cite web |title=D Smoke Reveals What’s Next For Him After Winning Netflix's "Rhythm + Flow" Home Grown Radio |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sSnNgAYuDR4 |website=YouTube |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref> On May 9, 2006, he released his first album called ''Producer of the Year''.<ref>{{cite web |title=D Smoke - Producer of the Year |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/producer-of-the-year-mw0001515625 |website=AllMusic |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref> In 2015, he appeared on SiR's independent album ''Seven Sundays'' on the song "You Ain't Ready".<ref>{{cite web |title=Future Classic: SiR "Seven Sundays" |url=http://www.musicismysanctuary.com/future-classic-sir-seven-sundays |website=Musicismysanctuary}}</ref>

In 2019, Farris was a contestant on the Netflix competition show ''[[Rhythm + Flow|Rhythm + Flow]]'', and was named the inaugural winner of the three-week series.<ref>{{cite web |title=Rhythm + Flow: Why D Smoke Deserved to Win |url=https://screenrant.com/rhythm-flow-d-smoke-win-deserved/ |website=ScreenRant |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref> On October 24, he released his debut EP ''Inglewood High'', a 7-track project including a feature from Gouché. ''[[HipHopDX]]'' gave the EP a positive review, saying he could "utilize multiple flows, deliver introspective storytelling, could rap in Spanish incredibly well and had an ear for quality."<ref>{{cite web |title=Review: "Inglewood High" Proves "Rhythm + Flow" Winner D. Smoke Is A Future Star |url=https://hiphopdx.com/reviews/id.3560/title.review-inglewood-high-proves-rhythm-flow-winner-d-smoke-is-a-future-star |website=HipHopDX |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref> He performed at the [[2019 Soul Train Music Awards]] with SiR.<ref>{{cite web |title=SiR & His Brother D Smoke Wow Soul Train Awards Crowd With Unforgettable Performance |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/sir-and-his-brother-d-smoke-wow-soul-train-awards-crowd-with-unforgettable-performance-news.95886.html |website=HotNewHipHop |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref> He also appeared on [[The Game (rapper)|The Game]]'s album ''[[Born 2 Rap]]'' on the song "Cross on Jesus Back".<ref>{{cite web |title=The Game's Final Album "Born 2 Rap" Features Nipsey Hussle, 21 Savage, D Smoke, & More |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/the-games-final-album-born-2-rap-features-nipsey-hussle-21-savage-d-smoke-and-more-new-mixtape.119835.html |website=HotNewHipHop |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Farris graduated from [[UCLA]] and was a Spanish and music theory teacher at [[Inglewood High School (California)|Inglewood High School]].<ref>{{cite web |title='Rhythm + Flow' Champ D Smoke Reveals Why Cardi B Got Lawyers Involved During 'Battles' Round (Exclusive) |url=https://www.etonline.com/rhythm-flow-champ-d-smoke-reveals-why-cardi-b-got-lawyers-involved-during-battles-round-exclusive |website=ET Online |accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref>
