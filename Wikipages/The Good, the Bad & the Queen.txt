'''The Good, the Bad & the Queen''' were<!-- Do not change to "was" per British English --> an [[English people|English]] [[art rock]] [[Supergroup (music)|supergroup]] composed of singer [[Damon Albarn]] of [[Blur (band)|Blur]] and [[Gorillaz]], bassist [[Paul Simonon]] of the [[The Clash|Clash]], guitarist [[Simon Tong]] of the [[The Verve|Verve]], and [[Fela Kuti]] drummer [[Tony Allen (musician)|Tony Allen]]. They released their [[The Good, the Bad & the Queen (album)|self-titled debut album]] in 2007. Their second album, ''[[Merrie Land]],'' coproduced with [[Tony Visconti]], was released in 2018. They disbanded in 2019; Allen died in 2020.

== History ==

===Formation and debut album===
The Good, the Bad & the Queen began as a solo project by [[Damon Albarn]] with production by [[Danger Mouse (musician)|Danger Mouse]]. However, by July 2006, the project had become a band,<ref name="NME.com 1">{{cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/news/blur/23752|title=Damon Albarn forms new band|date=28 July 2006|work=[[NME]]|access-date=28 July 2006}}</ref> with bassist [[Paul Simonon]] of the [[The Clash|Clash]], guitarist [[Simon Tong]] of the [[The Verve|Verve]], and [[Fela Kuti]] drummer [[Tony Allen (musician)|Tony Allen]]. Albarn met Simonon at the wedding of Clash singer [[Joe Strummer]] in 1997, and Tong had worked with Albarn on [[Blur (band)|Blur]]'s 2002 [[Think Tank (Blur album)|''Think Tank'']] tour, filling in as guitarist following the departure of [[Graham Coxon]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|last=Williams|first=Murphy|date=2007-01-20|title=Songs of experience|language=en-GB|work=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/3662657/Songs-of-experience.html|access-date=2020-04-16|issn=0307-1235}}</ref> Allen contacted Albarn after hearing the 2000 Blur single "[[Music Is My Radar]]", which references him.<ref name=":2" />

The Good, the Bad & the Queen played their debut gig in a village pub in Devon on 20 October, followed by a performance at the [[Roundhouse (venue)|London Roundhouse]] on 26 October as part of the [[BBC Electric Proms]].<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/the-good-the-bad-and-the-queen-7-1347974|title=Damon Albarn launches new band with tiny pub gig|last=NME|date=2006-10-21|website=NME|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-29}}</ref> They released their first single, "[[Herculean (song)|Herculean]]", on 30 October.<ref>''[[Uncut (magazine)|Uncut]]'' (Nov. 2006, pp. 86–88)</ref> On 12 December, the band performed a secret launch gig exclusive to 300 chosen fans for [[Myspace]]'s new feature ''The List'' in [[Wilton's Music Hall]], East London.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/the-good-the-bad-and-the-queen/25245|title=Damon Albarn's new band announce new single|date=28 November 2006|website=[[NME]]}}</ref>

The band released their [[The Good, the Bad & the Queen (album)|self-titled debut album]] on 22 January 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/reviews-the-good-the-bad-and-the-queen-8116-316676|title=The Good, The Bad & The Queen: The Good, The Bad & The Queen|first=Hamish|last=MacBain|website=NME|language=en-US|access-date=2020-12-11}}</ref> It was voted by the ''[[The Observer|Observer Music Magazine]]'' as the Best Album of 2007.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2007/dec/09/15|title=2007: The best 50 albums|date=2007-12-09|work=The Observer|access-date=2020-04-16|language=en-GB|issn=0029-7712}}</ref> At this point, Albarn said ''The Good, the Bad & the Queen'' was only the album title and that the band was "nameless".<ref name=":0" /> Simonon said "we didn't properly name the band, because a name is for a marriage".<ref name=":1" />

Albarn said the group had "permanently finished" in 2007; however, they reunited to perform at the 2008 [[Love Music Hate Racism]] carnival.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/the-good-the-bad-and-the-queen-27-1332598|title=The Good, The Bad And The Queen reforming after secret split|last=NME|date=2008-04-24|website=NME|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-29}}</ref> Tong and Simonon appeared on Albarn's next project, the [[Gorillaz]] album ''[[Plastic Beach]]'' (2010)'','' and participated in the ''Plastic Beach'' tour. In November 2011, the Good, the Bad & the Queen played a show at London's Coronet Theatre for the 40th anniversary of [[Greenpeace]], the first time they had played together in almost three years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blurballs.com/2011/10/damon-albarn-to-revive-good-bad-and.html|title=Damon Albarn to revive The Good, The Bad and The Queen for Greenpeace charity gig|last=BlurBalls|website=BlurBalls - Latest Blur, Gorillaz, Damon Albarn and Graham Coxon news|access-date=2020-04-24}}</ref> Allen and Tong appeared on Albarn's soundtrack for the opera ''[[Dr Dee#Soundtrack|Dr Dee]]'' (2012) and Allen collaborated with Albarn again on the 2012 album ''[[Rocket Juice & the Moon]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/music/rocket-juice-and-the-moon/rocket-juice-and-the-moon-rocket-juice-and-the-moo/|title=Rocket Juice and the Moon: Rocket Juice and the Moon|first=Ryan|last=Reed|date=2012-03-28|website=Paste Magazine|language=en-US|access-date=2020-12-11}}</ref>

=== ''Merrie Land'' and disbandment ===
In October 2014, Albarn announced that a new album had been written and was waiting to be recorded.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://pitchfork.com/news/57140-damon-albarn-prepping-new-gorillaz-and-the-good-the-bad-the-queen-albums/|title=Damon Albarn Prepping New Gorillaz and the Good, the Bad & the Queen Albums|last1=Minsker|first1=Evan|work=Pitchfork|access-date=20 October 2014}}</ref> In April 2017, in a new interview with ''[[Q (magazine)|Q]]'', it was reported that an album was still in the making but that recent events had caused the group to start afresh. Albarn told ''Q'' that [[Brexit]] had "given us a wonderful starting point".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2017/04/damon-albarn-is-working-on-the-good-the-bad-the-queens-long-awaited-new-album/|title=Damon Albarn is working on The Good the Bad & the Queen's long-awaited new album|last1=Young|first1=Alex|website=Consequence of Sound|access-date=10 April 2017}}</ref>

After a seven-year interval since the last time the band members performed together in 2011, the band released the title track "Merrie Land" of their second album ''Merrie Land'' on 23 October 2018 with releasing its music video on YouTube. Later, they made their first public appearance in seven years with the title track on ''[[Later... with Jools Holland|Later…With Jools Holland]]'' on 30 October 2018. On 5 November 2018, they performed the second single, "[[Gun to the Head]]", on the same show.{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}}

On 17 October 2018, the band announced their second album, ''[[Merrie Land]]'', produced by [[Tony Visconti]]. It was released on 16 November, as “the right circumstances came about”, under the new label Studio 13 created by Albarn.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://consequenceofsound.net/2018/10/the-good-the-bad-the-queen-merrie-land-details/amp/|title=The Good The Bad & The Queen to release new album, Merrie Land, in November|last1=Young|first1=Alex|website=Consequence of Sound|access-date=18 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.essexlive.news/whats-on/music-nightlife/damon-albarn-talks-how-brexit-2197986|title=Damon Albarn talks about how Brexit inspired his new album|last=Webber|first=Darryl|date=2018-11-08|website=essexlive|access-date=2020-04-24}}</ref> This album was described by Albarn as “a reluctant goodbye letter” responding to the occurrent Brexit process and “a series of observations and reflections on Britishness in 2018.”<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.scotsman.com/arts-and-culture/damon-albarns-band-good-bad-queen-announce-new-brexit-inspired-album-and-glasgow-date-555146|title=Damon Albarn's band The Good, The Bad & the Queen announce new Brexit-inspired album and Glasgow date|website=Scotsman.com|language=en|access-date=2020-04-16}}</ref>

The band performed the first gig for this album in Tynemouth on 26 November 2018, which was the start of an eight-date warm-up tour in UK. The band then started their Europe tour from March to August in 2019.{{Citation needed|date=April 2020}} On 16 August 2019, on the last performance of the tour in support of the album at Lowland Festival, Albarn told the crowd: "This is our last gig. After this, poof. Done."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/damon-albarn-the-good-the-bad-the-queen-played-last-gig-lowlands-festival-2539172|title=Damon Albarn says The Good, The Bad & The Queen have played their "last gig" at Lowlands Festival|last=Daly|first=Rhian|date=2019-08-16|website=NME|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-29}}</ref> Allen died in April 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Snapes|first=Laura|last2=France-Presse|first2=Agence|date=2020-05-01|title=Tony Allen, legendary drummer and Afrobeat co-founder, dies aged 79|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/may/01/tony-allen-legendary-drummer-and-afrobeat-co-founder-dies-aged-79|access-date=2020-05-01|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>
