'''Isaiah Rashad McClain''' (born May 16, 1991) is an American rapper from [[Chattanooga, Tennessee]]. His first big break would be performing on the 2012 Smoker's Club Tour with rappers [[Juicy J]], [[Joey Badass]] and [[Smoke DZA]] among others. He is also a founding member of the Chattanooga hip hop collective The House along with fellow Chattanooga rappers [[YGTUT]], Michael Da Vinci (MikeyD),  Chris P, and Brian Brown.

In March 2013, Rashad signed to the [[Carson, California]]-based independent record label [[Top Dawg Entertainment]]. On January 28, 2014, he released ''[[Cilvia Demo]]'', his first [[extended play]], via digital distribution. ''Cilvia Demo'' was met with widespread acclaim from [[music critic]]s and debuted at number 40 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. Off the buzz from the release of the EP, Rashad was named to the 2014 ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' freshman class. On September 2, 2016, he released his debut studio album, ''[[The Sun's Tirade]]'', to further acclaim. The album peaked at number 17 on the ''Billboard'' 200. On July 30, 2021, he released his long-awaited sophomore album, ''[[The House Is Burning (album)|The House Is Burning]]''.

== Early life ==
Isaiah Rashad McClain was born in [[Chattanooga, Tennessee]]. He was raised primarily by his mother, who is a hairdresser. When he was young, Rashad's father would let him stay up late while they listened to [[Too $hort]] and [[Scarface (rapper)|Scarface]].<ref name="thefader1">{{cite web|url=http://www.thefader.com/2013/11/25/gen-f-isaiah-rashad/#/0 |title=GEN F: Isaiah Rashad |publisher=[[The Fader]] |date=November 25, 2013 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref> Then one day when Rashad was three, his father left.<ref name="complex1">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/who-is-isaiah-rashad/ |title=Who Is Isaiah Rashad? |publisher=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=January 9, 2014 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref> Rashad briefly had ambitions of becoming a preacher, until his stepbrother lent him a copy of [[OutKast]]'s ''[[ATLiens]]'' in junior high, and his mindset shifted. Shortly after, Rashad started spending most of his time [[rapping]].<ref name="thefader1" /> In his youth, Isaiah's mother and stepfather converted to Islam and were a part of the [[Five Percent Nation]]. He also recounts going to mosque every weekend.

== Musical career ==

=== 2009–2012: beginnings ===
Rashad first began rapping seriously in tenth grade, while he and his friends would record on laptops.<ref name="xxlmag1">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/10/the-come-up-isaiah-rashad/3/ |title=The Come Up: Isaiah Rashad – Page 3 of 8 |work=XXL |publisher=Townsquare Media |date=October 16, 2013 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref> At one point, the first stage name he used was Zay Taylor.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/isaiahrashad/status/1316147449711390721 |title=Twitter / isaiahrashad: zay taylor |publisher=[[Twitter]] |date=October 13, 2020 |access-date=October 13, 2020}}</ref> After high school, he went to [[Middle Tennessee State University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/who-is-isaiah-rashad/going-to-college |title=Going To College — Who Is Isaiah Rashad? |publisher=Complex |date=January 9, 2014 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref> Then his friend who went to school for music production introduced Rashad to a friend who had a recording studio. As it turned out, the man happened to be his cousin and he let Rashad record there for free. Once his cousin moved on to a different job, Rashad bounced around a couple of local studios. He stopped going to school and lived wherever he could record.<ref name="xxlmag1" />

He began getting industry connects, forging relationships with DJ Z from DJBooth.net and Jeff Weiss who writes for ''[[Rolling Stone]]''.<ref name="complex2">{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/who-is-isaiah-rashad/buzzing |title=How He Got Buzzing — Who Is Isaiah Rashad? |publisher=Complex |date=January 9, 2014 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref> From that his music begun getting passed around to various [[A&R]]'s.<ref name="complex2" /> From July 13 to August 23, 2012, Isaiah Rashad toured with [[Juicy J]], [[Joey Badass]] and [[Smoke DZA]] among others, on the 2012 Smoker's Club Tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themaskedgorilla.com/the-smokers-club-summer-2012-tour-dates/ |title=The Smokers Club Summer 2012 Tour Dates |publisher=The Masked Gorilla |date=June 2, 2012 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref> In December 2012, Rashad begun getting attention from various record labels, most of which he showed no interest in. He also released many free singles on SoundCloud using beats from well known producers such as [[MF Doom]] and [[Flying Lotus]], which helped increase his popularity.<ref name="xxlmag2">{{cite web|url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/2013/10/the-come-up-isaiah-rashad/5/ |title=The Come Up: Isaiah Rashad – Page 5 of 8 |work=XXL |publisher=Townsquare Media |date=October 16, 2013 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
[[File:Isaiah Rashad Feb 2014.jpg|thumb|right|upright|Rashad in 2014]]
Rashad's music is characterized by its woozy, often nostalgic tone and conscious lyrics that touch on topics of [[Depression (mood)|depression]], [[drug addiction]], and [[family]]. Jason Moore of ''[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]'' discussed him saying, "Rashad spits thoughtfully, intelligently, and with precision, morphing his delivery to fit different production styles and making each line count."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2013/07/25-new-rappers-to-watch-out-for/isaiah-rashad |title=Isaiah Rashad — 25 New Rappers to Watch Out For |publisher=Complex |date=July 25, 2013 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref> Rashad's debut EP ''[[Cilvia Demo]]'' is a [[concept album]], which according to Rashad, sums up his adolescent life and mindset in a short period of time, while his album ''[[The Sun's Tirade]]'' details his experiences in overcoming drug addiction and depression.

=== Influences ===
Growing up and to this day Rashad says he listens or listened to [[Jay-Z]], [[Nas]], [[Snoop Dogg]], [[Outkast|OutKast]], [[No Limit Records]], [[Cash Money Records]], [[Erykah Badu]], [[The Fugees]], [[KRS-One]], [[Dead Prez]], [[Kanye West]], [[Little Brother (group)|Little Brother]], [[Three 6 Mafia]], [[8Ball & MJG]], [[UGK]], [[George Clinton (funk musician)|George Clinton]], [[James Brown (singer)|James Brown]], [[The Temptations]], [[Smokey Robinson]], [[Gorillaz]] and [[Danger Mouse (musician)|Danger Mouse]].<ref name="hiphopdx1" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.complex.com/music/2014/01/who-is-isaiah-rashad/influences |title=Musical Influences — Who Is Isaiah Rashad? |publisher=Complex |date=January 9, 2014 |access-date=January 28, 2014}}</ref> Moreover, he credits OutKast, namely [[Big Boi]]; [[Scarface (rapper)|Scarface]]; and [[Jay-Z]] for being the biggest influences to his music.<ref name="hiphopdx1" /> Then in a later interview with ''XXL'' he said his main influences are also OutKast, Erykah Badu, [[Gnarls Barkley]], ScHoolboy Q, [[T.I.]] and [[Lil Wayne]].<ref name="xxlmag.com" />

== Personal life ==
Rashad has a son and a daughter. Upon signing to Top Dawg Entertainment he got a tattoo to commemorate the partnership."<ref name="xxlmag.com" />

During promotion of ''The Sun's Tirade'', Rashad revealed that he was addicted to [[Xanax]] and [[alcohol (drug)|alcohol]], and was suffering from [[depression (mood)|depression]], [[anxiety]] and [[Solitude|isolation]] during the time between the release of ''Cilvia Demo'' and ''The Sun's Tirade''. He further stated that he "fucked up", and almost got dropped from [[Top Dawg Entertainment]] a total of three times during the recording of his album.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://djbooth.net/news/entry/2016-08-30-isaiah-rashad-addiction|title=Isaiah Rashad on His Addiction: "I Fucked Up, I Almost Got Dropped Three Times" – DJBooth Article|work=DJBooth.net|access-date=July 5, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.hotnewhiphop.com/isaiah-rashad-describes-how-xanax-and-alcohol-addiction-nearly-derailed-his-career-news.23785.html|title=Isaiah Rashad Describes How Xanax & Alcohol Addiction Nearly Derailed His Career|website=HotNewHipHop.com|access-date=July 5, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2016/08/isaiah-rashad-xanax-alcohol-addiction-almost-got-dropped-tde|title=Isaiah Rashad Says His Xanax And Alcohol Addiction Almost Got Him Dropped From TDE|website=Complex|language=en|access-date=July 5, 2017}}</ref>

In an interview with ''[[Vice (magazine)|Vice]]'', Rashad revealed that at 19 he had suicidal tendencies and attempted suicide a few times however he "chickened out", saying that it was just one of the many adventurous moments in his life.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/69vwvr/isaiah-rashad-interview-toronto|title=Isaiah Rashad: Motion Activated|first=Slava|last=Pastuk|website=Vice.com|date=March 6, 2014}}</ref>
