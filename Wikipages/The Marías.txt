'''The Marías''' are an [[indie pop]] band from Los Angeles, California, composed of [[Puerto Rican people|Puerto Rican]] lead singer [[María Zardoya]] and [[Los Angeles]] native and drummer Josh Conway. They are known for performing songs in both [[English language|English]] and [[Spanish language|Spanish]], as well as infusing their music with elements like [[jazz]] [[Percussion instrument|percussion]], guitar [[Riff|riffs]], smoke-velvet vocals, and [[Horn (instrument)|horn]] solos.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/pa5epb/the-marias-interview-over-the-moon-video-premiere-2018|title=There's Something About The Marías|work=Vice}}</ref> In their live show, with Zardoya on lead vocals and Conway on drums, they are joined by Jesse Perlman on guitar and Edward James on keyboards.<ref name=LATimes>{{cite web|url=https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-the-marias-interview-20190329-story.html|title=Why the bilingual Marías are 'the perfect indie band for L.A. kids'|work=Los Angeles Times|date=29 March 2019}}</ref> 

== History ==
The band is named after its lead singer María Zardoya who was born in [[Puerto Rico]] and raised in [[Atlanta]], Georgia. She and her partner Josh Conway, the drummer, met at a show at the Kibitz Room, the bar and music venue inside [[Canter's|Canter's Deli]] in Los Angeles.<ref name="remezcla.com">{{Cite web|url=https://remezcla.com/features/music/the-marias-interview/|title=Meet the Marías, the LA Band Making Dreamy Vintage Pop for Lazy Pool Days}}</ref> She was performing on the bill and he was managing the sound, something he had never done before. They began writing almost immediately after meeting, and subsequently began dating.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.vogue.com/article/maria-the-marias-frontwoman-beauty?verso=true|title=Meet María, the Frontwoman Mastering '90s Minimalist Makeup and Self-Care on Tour|work=Vogue|date=15 August 2019}}</ref> Soon they recruited close friends to join as band members; Edward James on keyboards, and guitarist Jesse Perlman.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metrotimes.com/detroit/bilingual-indie-group-the-marias-blur-the-lines-between-fantasy-and-reality/Content?oid=15713238|title=Bilingual Indie group the Marías blurs the lines between fantasy and reality|work=Detroit Metro Times}}</ref> They were offered an opportunity to make songs for television, which helped them understand the visual and sonic feel that they were striving for <ref name="remezcla.com"/> but when it didn't materialize, they used their recordings to make an [[extended play|EP]], entitled ''Superclean Vol. I''.<ref name=LATimes/> ''Vol. I'' was released in 2017, while its counterpart ''Vol. II'' was released in 2018.<ref name=AP>{{cite web|url=https://www.apnews.com/6bb706244be443d38ddb8953e1ab5927|title=MUSIC REVIEW: Superclean, Vol. II by The Marías|work=Associated Press|date=25 October 2018}}</ref> In September 2021, their single "Hush" topped the Billboard Adult Alternative Airplay chart, becoming their first chart-topping single. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/the-marias/chart-history/AAA|title=Chart History: The Marias|work=Billboard Magazine}}</ref>

=== Influences ===
María Zardoya has stated that her influences include the late [[Tejano]] singer [[Selena]], [[Norah Jones]], [[Sade (singer)|Sade]], [[Nina Simone]], [[Billie Holiday]], [[Carla Morrison]], [[Julieta Venegas]], [[Erykah Badu]], and filmmaker [[Pedro Almodóvar]], while Conway is influenced by [[Tame Impala]], [[Radiohead]], [[D'Angelo]], and [[The Strokes]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.onestowatch.com/blog/qa-the-mar%C3%ADas-craft-sensual-timeless-music|title=Q&A: The Marías Craft Sensual & Timeless Music That Will Make You Fall in Love (or Lust)|work=onestowatch}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.timeoutmexico.mx/ciudad-de-mexico/musica/maria-de-the-marias-nos-platica-sobre-su-participacion-en-grrrl-noise-2019|title=Maria, de The Marías, nos platica sobre su participación en GRRRL Noise 2019|work=[[Time Out (magazine)|Time Out]] México}}</ref>

Zardoya stated how Conway was not familiar with her Spanish roots, but the band was open to experimenting with new styles, ultimately leading to several Spanish-language tracks across their releases.<ref name="remezcla.com"/>
