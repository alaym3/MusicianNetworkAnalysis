'''Trevor Daniel Neill''' (born September 28, 1994), is an American singer and songwriter. He rose to prominence with his 2018 single "[[Falling (Trevor Daniel song)|Falling]]", which hit the charts in more than twenty countries after going viral in 2019. His debut [[studio album]] ''[[Nicotine (album)|Nicotine]]'' was released in March 2020.

== Career ==
Daniel started his career uploading his music to [[SoundCloud]]. His 2017 single "Pretend" eventually took off after being upload by a popular [[YouTube]] channel. He later got a call from [[Internet Money]]'s Tez Taylor and signed a joint record contract with them along Alamo and [[Interscope Records|Interscope]] in July 2018. He released  the EPs ''Homesick'' in 2018 and ''Restless'' in 2019. The former featured the single "[[Falling (Trevor Daniel song)|Falling]]", coming out in October that year. Despite the release of a [[Blackbear (musician)|Blackbear]] remix of the track it didn't become popular until 2019, after going viral on [[Online video platform|video-sharing]] app [[TikTok]].<ref == Personal life ==
Daniel lived in [[Baton Rouge|Baton Rouge, Louisiana]]. His family moved to [[Fort Worth|Fort Worth, Texas]] and later settled down in [[Houston|Houston, Texas]] where the singer calls home.<ref name="nhm">{{cite web|url=http://www.newhoustonmusic.com/2017/04/15/trevor-daniel-talks-senses-album-wins-artist-year|title= Trevor Daniel Talks "Senses" Album, Writing Sad Songs and Wins Artist of Year|website=New Houstin Music|date=April 15, 2017|access-date=April 15, 2017}}</ref> Daniel admits that music became important to him in his life as a child and started playing drums in second grade. In high school, he begun making music in [[Mixcraft|Mixcraft 5]] and recording vocals in his bedroom closet with the advice of [[YouTube]] tutorials. He decided to pursue music professionally in 2017.<ref name="Billboard"/><ref name="Teen Vogue"/>

== Musical influences ==
Daniel has been quoted revealing that his earliest influences were [[rap music|rap]], [[rock music|rock]], [[house music|house]] and [[pop music|pop]].<ref name="nhm"/> He has cited [[Kanye West]], [[Drake (musician)|Drake]] and [[Kid Cudi]] as influences.<ref name="Teen Vogue"/>
