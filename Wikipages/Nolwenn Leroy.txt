'''Nolwenn Le Magueresse''' ({{IPA-fr|nolwɛn lə maɡəʁɛs}}; born 28 September 1982), known by her stage name '''Nolwenn Leroy''' ({{IPA-fr|nolwɛn ləʁwa|pron}}), is a French singer-songwriter, musician and actress.

Originally classically trained (violin and opera singing), she rose to fame after winning the second season of the French television music competition ''[[Star Academy France|Star Academy]]'' in 2002. She has since recorded seven studio albums and scored two number one singles, "[[Cassé]]" and "[[Nolwenn Ohwo!]]", on the French charts.<ref name="lescharts.com">{{Cite web | title=Nolwenn Leroy's Singles French Chart positions| publisher=lescharts.com| url=http://lescharts.com/search.asp?search=Nolwenn+Leroy&cat=s | access-date= 16 September 2007}}</ref> In 2012, her album ''[[Bretonne (album)|Bretonne]]'' was certified two times diamond for sales exceeding one million copies.<ref name="Bretonne certification">{{Cite web | title=Nolwenn Leroy : "Bretonne" double disque de diamant ! |language=fr | publisher=Universal Music France| url=https://www.universalmusic.fr/1703-nolwenn-leroy-bretonne-double-disque-de-diamant.html | date= 12 February 2012 | access-date= 25 May 2018}}</ref>

Leroy is fluent in English, having spent a year in the US as an exchange student. She sings in many languages, including French, [[Breton language|Breton]], English and [[Irish language|Irish]].

Leroy has received numerous awards and nominations. In January 2015, she was ranked 17th on ''[[Le Journal du Dimanche]]'''s 50 Most Loved Celebrities in France, making her the top female singer on the list since December 2012.<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.ozap.com/actu/jean-jacques-goldman-omar-sy-et-jean-dujardin-personnalites-preferees-des-francais/460655 "Jean-Jacques Goldman, Omar Sy et Jean Dujardin, personnalités préférées des Français"]. ''Ozap''. 4 January 2015. Retrieved 4 January 2015.</ref><ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.ifop.com/?option=com_publication&type=poll&id=2091 "Le Top 50 des personnalités – Décembre 2012"]. Ifop. 2 January 2013. Retrieved 4 January 2015.</ref>

== Early life and career beginnings ==
Leroy was born in Saint-Renan in 1982. Leroy's parents left [[Saint-Renan]] ([[Finistère]]) when she was four years old. After living in Paris, [[Lille]], and [[Guingamp]], her mother, Muriel Leroy, and her younger sister, Kay, settled with Leroy's grandparents in [[Saint-Yorre]]. Her mother divorced from her father, professional footballer [[Jean-Luc Le Magueresse]], in 1993.<ref name=RFI>{{cite web|url=http://www.rfimusic.com/artist/chanson/nolwenn-leroy/biography |title=Nolwenn Leroy |work=[[Radio France International]] |access-date=25 March 2012}}</ref>

Nolwenn studied at the ''"Collège des Célestins"'' in [[Vichy]]. When Leroy was eleven, her music teacher noticed her musical talents and encouraged her to learn the violin. At the age of thirteen she won ''"Les écoles du désert"'', a contest sponsored by the [[Cora (hypermarket)|Cora supermarket chain]], which allowed her to travel with a humanitarian mission from [[Gao]] to [[Timbuktu]], [[Mali]]; she later claimed this had a profound influence on her.<ref name=RFI/>

In July 1998, Leroy was awarded a scholarship by the Vichy [[Rotary Club]] to travel to [[Hamilton, Ohio|Hamilton]], Ohio, United States, as an exchange student. While attending Hamilton High School, she took music lessons at the [[School for Creative and Performing Arts|Performing Arts School]] and became fluent in English.<ref name=RFI/> When she returned to France, she began classical singing classes at the [[Vichy]] music conservatory. In 2001, she enrolled in the [[University of Clermont-Ferrand]] to study law for a potential alternative career to music.<ref name=RFI/>

==Career==

===2002: ''Star Academy''===
After watching the first season of ''[[Star Academy (France)|Star Academy]]'' broadcast on [[TF1]] in 2001, Leroy was impressed by [[Armande Altaï]], a singing teacher and one of the show's judges. She subsequently joined Altaï's singing classes in Paris for over six months. In 2002, Nolwenn was selected for the second season of Star Academy, but she was also given the part of [[Scarlett O'Hara]] in the French stage musical ''[[Autant en emporte le vent]]'' (''[[Gone with the Wind (novel)|Gone with the Wind]]'') by [[Gérard Presgurvic]].<ref name=RFI/> Leroy ended up choosing Star Academy over the musical. As a contestant on the show, Leroy spent four months in a castle located in [[Dammarie-lès-Lys]], and followed acting, dancing and singing classes. On 21 December 2002, she was declared the winner ahead of fellow finalist, [[Houcine Camara]].

===2003–2004: Eponymous debut album===
Leroy's first album, ''[[Nolwenn (album)|Nolwenn]]'', was released in March 2003 and was [[Music recording sales certification|certified]] platinum by November<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=http://www.disqueenfrance.com/fr/page-259165.xml?year=2003&type=12 |language=fr |work=Disque en France |publisher=[[Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique]] |access-date=25 March 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120303063032/http://www.disqueenfrance.com/fr/page-259165.xml?year=2003&type=12 |archive-date=3 March 2012 }}</ref> for sales exceeding 300,000 copies.<ref name="France Lowers Gold Standard">{{cite magazine |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=JhYEAAAAMBAJ&q=SNEP%20reduces%20Platinum%20certification&pg=PA16|title=France Lowers Gold Standard|magazine=Billboard|access-date=25 March 2012|date=26 July 2006}}</ref> In 2006, the album was certified two times platinum by the [[Syndicat national de l'édition phonographique]] (SNEP) for more than 600,000 copies sold.<ref name=":2">{{cite web |url=http://www.disqueenfrance.com/fr/page-259165.xml?year=2006&type=13 |title=<nowiki>Certifications Albums Double Platine – année 2006 [Double-Platinum album certifications – year 2006]</nowiki> |language=fr |work=Disque en France |publisher=[[Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique]] |access-date=25 March 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110710135351/http://www.disqueenfrance.com/fr/page-259165.xml?year=2006 |archive-date=10 July 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> Both the album and the first single "[[Cassé]]" topped the charts in France and [[Belgium]]. Three other songs from the album were released as singles: "Une Femme cachée", "Suivre une étoile" and "Inévitablement". Leroy went on her first solo tour in France, Belgium and [[Switzerland]] in late 2003 to promote songs from the album.

===2005–2008: ''Histoires Naturelles''===
Leroy's second album, ''[[Histoires Naturelles (album)|Histoires Naturelles]]'', was released on 5 December 2005. It was produced by [[Laurent Voulzy]] and Frank Eulry. The album's lead single "[[Nolwenn Ohwo!]]", co-written by [[Alain Souchon]], Voulzy and Leroy herself, topped the French charts one week after its release.<ref name="Nolwenn Ohwo! Chart Performance - lescharts.com">[http://lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Nolwenn+Leroy&titel=Nolwenn+ohwo!&cat=s "Nolwenn Ohwo! – Chart performance"]</ref> The second single was the title track, "Histoire Naturelle". Music videos were made for both songs; the video for "Histoire Naturelle" expressed the main theme of the album with Leroy being portrayed as natural history museum exhibits. Of the other single releases, "Mon Ange" was distributed digitally while "J'aimais tant l'aimer" and "Reste Encore" were promotional-only. The album reached platinum status<ref name="TV5 Monde">{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.tv5monde.com/cms/chaine-francophone/Musique/p-14237-Nolwenn-Leroy.htm?artiste=273 "Nolwenn Leroy – Biographie"]. TV5 Monde. Retrieved 2 November 2014.</ref> with sales exceeding 400,000 copies.<ref name="HN sales">{{in lang|fr}} [http://archives.lesoir.be/joyeux-titre-nolwenn-titre-_t-20091216-00RDXN.html "Joyeux Nolwenn"]. ''Le Soir''. 16 December 2009. Retrieved 2 November 2014.</ref> Leroy performed songs from both ''Histoires Naturelles'' and ''Nolwenn'' on her second solo tour, which started in September 2006. Leroy's first live album, ''Histoires Naturelles Tour'', was subsequently released in late October 2007.

===2009–2010: ''Le Cheshire Cat et Moi''===
[[File:Nolwenn Leroy singing.jpg|thumb|Leroy performing at the 2010 [[Les Francofolies de Spa|Francofolies]] in [[Spa, Belgium|Spa]] ([[Belgium]]).]]
''[[Le Cheshire Cat et moi|Le Cheshire Cat & Moi]]'' was a project that began in 2007, co-written by Leroy and [[Teitur Lassen]], with contributions from [[Jonatha Brooke]], [[Michelle Featherstone]], [[Mike Errico]] and [[Rupert Hine]]. It was arranged and produced by Lassen, and recorded in [[Sweden]] and the [[Faroe Islands]]. The album's lead single, "Faut-il, faut-il pas ?", was released in November 2009 along with a music video directed by [[Yoann Lemoine]].<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.elle.fr/Loisirs/Musique/Dossiers/Rencontre-avec-Nolwenn-et-son-Cheshire-Cat-1090289# "Rencontre avec Nolwenn et son Cheshire Cat"]. ''ELLE''. 4 December 2009. Retrieved 7 October 2015.</ref> With the album, Leroy presented "a lighter sound than on previous releases"<ref>{{cite web|url={{allmusic|pure_url=yes|id=r1701264|class=album}} |title=Le Cheshire Cat et Moi |first=James Christopher |last=Monger |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=25 March 2012}}</ref> and she was given the opportunity to write all the lyrics.<ref name=RFI/> The album was released on 7 December 2009 and was later certified gold.<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.journaldesfemmes.com/people/nolwenn-leroy/ "Nolwenn Leroy"]. ''Le Journal des Femmes''. Retrieved 7 October 2015.</ref> A tour entitled "Le Cheshire Cat & Vous" to promote the record was held in 2010.

===2010–2012: ''Bretonne''===
Leroy released a fourth studio album, ''[[Bretonne (album)|Bretonne]]'', on 6 December 2010. Cover versions of traditional [[celtic music|Celtic]] songs such as "[[Mná na h-Éireann]]", "[[Tri Martolod]]", "[[La Jument de Michao]]", and contemporary songs such as [[Christophe Miossec]]'s "Brest" were included on the album. Most of the songs related to [[Brittany]], the singer's birthplace. Four of them were sung in [[Breton language|Breton]] ("Tri Martolod", "[[Suite Sudarmoricaine]]", "[[Bro Gozh ma Zadoù]]", "Karantez Vro"), one in Irish ("Mná na h-Éireann"), and the rest in French and English. Leroy collaborated with [[Jon Kelly]] for the musical arrangements.<ref name="Fourth studio album - Collaborations">[http://forum.nolwenn.org/index.php?t=msg&th=22015&start=0&rid=3293&S=dc4ec7ea13eb8b52edb6158fffc385ff "Fourth Studio Album Release Information"]</ref> The album topped the [[List of number-one hits of 2011 (France)#Albums chart|French album charts]] for 7 weeks and the Belgian album charts for 5 weeks. It was certified double diamond<ref name="Bretonne double diamond">{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.cheriefm.fr/actualites/musique/nolwenn-leroy-juin-le-mois-de-tous-les-succes-287214 "Nolwenn Leroy : juin, le mois de tous les succès !"]. Chérie FM. Retrieved 21 July 2017.</ref> by the [[Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique|SNEP]] for sales exceeding one million copies.<ref name="Bretonne certification"/>

The album was re-released in November 2011 as a ''Deluxe Edition'' with 7 additional tracks in English, including "[[Amazing Grace]]", "[[Scarborough Fair (ballad)|Scarborough Fair]]" and two [[Mike Oldfield]] tracks, "[[Moonlight Shadow]]" and "[[To France]]". The American version of the album, retitled ''Nolwenn'', was released on 8 January 2013 with a different track listing. The album peaked at number 10 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'''s World Album chart.<ref name="Bretonne Billboard"/> Leroy made her New York City debut at Drom.<ref>[http://www.dromnyc.com/events/2177/nolwenn-leroy-ny-debut "Nolwenn Leroy NYC Debut"]. Drom. Retrieved 18 April 2015.</ref>

As part of the Bretonne Tour, Leroy played over 100 dates<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://jactiv.ouest-france.fr/sortir/musique/live-nolwenn-leroy-tourne-vendredi-saint-brieuc-27454 "Un live de Nolwenn Leroy tourné vendredi à Saint-Brieuc !"]. ''Ouest-France''. 28 January 2014. Retrieved 9 October 2015.</ref> in France, Belgium, Switzerland and Germany. In June 2012, she performed as a special guest with [[The Chieftains]] in Paris.<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [https://sortir.telerama.fr/concerts/the-chieftains,76217.php "Concerts – The Chieftains"]. ''Télérama''.</ref>

===2012–2016: ''Ô Filles de l'Eau''===
[[File:Nolwenn Leroy - Alan Stivell.jpg|thumb|Leroy performing "Brian Boru" with [[Alan Stivell]] at the [[Paris Olympia]] in France on 16 February 2012]]
Leroy's fifth album, ''[[Ô Filles de l'eau]]'', was released on 26 November 2012, preceded by the lead single "Juste pour me souvenir". The album was produced by [[Jon Kelly]] and recorded in London. The title reads 'O Daughters of the Water' but is phonetically similar to 'au fil de l'eau' ('with the current/flow'). Like ''[[Bretonne (album)|Bretonne]]'', it contains Celtic inspired music but with more ocean themed lyrics. It includes two tracks in English ("Homeland" and "Limitless"). "Homeland" contains the [[James Horner]] composed theme of the film ''[[Braveheart]]''. "Ahès" is the only [[Breton language|Breton]]-language song of the album. Leroy wrote and co-composed 9 songs, including the singles "Juste pour me souvenir", "Sixième continent", "J'ai volé le lit de la mer" and "Ophélia".<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.letelegramme.fr/ig/loisirs/musique/nolwenn-leroy-chanson-par-chanson-elle-commente-son-album-12-11-2012-1904196.php "Nolwenn Leroy. Retrieved 8 May 2015.</ref> Reviews of the album were strong, with some praising Leroy's vocal ability and Celtic music and lyrics, noting that her singing in English and Breton was brave and proved her to be an evolving artist, with strong sales to match. Two months after its release, the album was certified triple platinum by the [[Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique|SNEP]].<ref name=":3">{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.nolwenn.org/actu/nrj-music-awards-2013-triple-disque-de-platine/ "NRJ Music Awards 2013 – Triple disque de platine"]. ==Medical research==
Leroy's music was studied for its neurological impact in [[geriatric]] populations.<ref name="Study 1">[http://www.clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT00272272?term=nolwenn+leroy&rank=1 "Fall Prevention in a Geriatric Nursing Home Setting Using the Music of Nolwenn Leroy"] at ClinicalTrials.gov</ref><ref name="Study 2">[http://www.clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT00304564?term=nolwenn+leroy&rank=2 "Study of the Effect of the Music of Nolwenn Leroy in Fall Prevention Strategies in Texas Nursing Homes"] at ClinicalTrials.gov</ref><ref name="Study 3">[http://www.clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT00368511?term=nolwenn+leroy&rank=3 "Fall Prevention Strategies Including Music and Posturography"] at ClinicalTrials.gov</ref> Researchers found that Leroy's recordings might have a more beneficial effect than other music, noting that: "the music of Nolwenn Leroy was found to be significantly superior to other music tested". To describe the phenomenon they coined the term ''the Nolwenn Effect'', saying: "the music of Nolwenn Leroy appears to have a different effect on brain-based modulation of gait and stance than other music tested to date".

Mozart and Dutch and French singers were tested; only Mozart and Leroy's music were specified. The U.S. study was completed in February 2008, but no substantial results appear to be  published.<ref name="IR article">[http://education.guardian.co.uk/egweekly/story/0,,2259878,00.html Falling over.] ''The Guardian''.</ref>

== Philanthropy ==
In 2006, she joined the [[Les Enfoirés]] charity ensemble and she became one of the Abbé Pierre Foundation's patrons<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fondation-abbe-pierre.fr/la-fondation-abbe-pierre/organisation/le-comite-damis-et-parrains |title=Le Comité d'Amis et Parrains |language=fr |publisher=Abbé Pierre Foundation's official website |access-date=11 March 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140329170444/http://www.fondation-abbe-pierre.fr/la-fondation-abbe-pierre/organisation/le-comite-damis-et-parrains |archive-date=29 March 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> for housing people in need.

On 27 November 2015, she participated together with [[Camélia Jordana]] and [[Yael Naim]] at the national memorial day for the victims of the [[November 2015 Paris attacks]] singing the song "Quand on n'a que l'amour" from [[Jacques Brel]].<ref name=lomondex1 >{{cite news |url = http://www.lemonde.fr/attaques-a-paris/article/2015/11/26/hommage-aux-invalides-pour-les-victimes-des-attentats_4818546_4809495.html |title = Hommage aux morts des attentats aux invalides |author1=Emeline Cazi|author2=David Revault d'Allonnes | access-date = 29 November 2015 |work = [[Le Monde]] |language=fr|date = 26 November 2015 }}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Since 2008, she has been in a relationship with [[Arnaud Clément]], former French professional tennis player and captain of the France Davis Cup team.<ref>{{in lang|fr}} [http://www.marieclaire.fr/nolwenn-leroy-arnaud-clement,1186390.asp "Love story : Nolwenn Leroy et Arnaud Clément"]. ''Marie Claire''. 19 July 2017. Retrieved 7 September 2017.</ref>
On July 12, 2017, they welcomed their first child, a son called Marin.
