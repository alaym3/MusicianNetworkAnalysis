'''Senida Hajdarpašić''' ({{lang-cnr|Сенида Хајдарпашић}}, {{IPA-sh|senǐda xǎjdarpaʃitɕ|pron}}; born 9 April 1985), known by her stage name '''Senidah''' (pronounced {{IPAc-en|s|ɛ|ˈ|n|iː|d|ə}}), is a Slovenian singer-songwriter. Born in [[Ljubljana]], [[Slovenia]], she rose to prominence as a lead singer of the Slovenian band Muff. She is often dubbed "the [[Balkans|Balkan]] Trap Diva" by the media.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.seadancefestival.me/svjetske-zvijezde-vanvremenskim-hitovima-pokrecu-novu-eru-sea-dance-festivala|title=Svjetske zvijezde vanvremenskim hitovima pokreću novu eru Sea Dance festivala|date=29 July 2019|website=seadancefestival.me|language=cnr|access-date=2 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://kolektiv.me/140365/zgodna-manekenka-pokazala-misice-uz-veliki-senidin-hit|title=Zgodna manekenka pokazala "mišiće" uz veliki Senidin hit|date=3 August 2019|website=kolektiv.me|language=cnr|access-date=2 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.exitfest.org/svetske-zvezde-vanvremenskim-hitovima-pokrecu-novu-eru-sea-dance-festivala|title=Svetske zvezde vanvremenskim hitovima pokreću novu eru Sea Dance festivala|date=29 July 2019|website=exitfest.org|language=sr|access-date=2 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.blic.rs/zabava/exit-2019/svetske-zvezde-vanvremenskim-hitovima-pokrecu-novu-eru-sea-dance-festivala/wwr99sq|title=Svetske zvezde vanvremenskim hitovima pokreću novu eru Sea Dance festivala|date=29 July 2019|website=blic.rs|language=sr|access-date=2 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.slovenskenovice.si/suzy/luka-pahor-se-je-podal-v-glasbene-vode-103884|title=Luka Pahor se je podal v glasbene vode|date=22 October 2018|website=slovenskenovice.si|language=sl|access-date=2 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://esarajevo.com/otasevic-strugar-micalovic-poznati-ne-propustaju-sea-dance|title=Otašević, Strugar, Mićalović… Poznati ne propuštaju Sea Dance!|date=19 August 2019|website=esarajevo.com|language=sr|access-date=2 September 2019}}</ref>

Senidah attracted [[Former Yugoslavia|regional]] fame with the release of her single "[[Slađana (song)|Slađana]]" in March 2018. The song was named "Hip-Hop/Rap Song of the Year" at [[2019 Music Awards Ceremony]]. It was the lead single off her debut studio album ''[[Bez Tebe (album)|Bez Tebe]]'', released in March 2019 for the [[Belgrade]]-based label Bassivity Music. The album was followed by a string of commercially successful singles, including "Mišići", "[[Kamikaza]]", "[[100% (Senidah song)|100%]]" and "[[Dođi (song)|Dođi]]".

==Career==

=== Early beginnings ===
Senidah's singing talent was discovered by her sister, who would record her singing and post it on the internet.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Kanić|first=Petra|date=3 January 2020|title=Senidin talent otkrila je sestra, a nagađalo se kako voli žene...|language=Croatian|work=24sata|url=https://www.24sata.hr/show/senidin-talent-otkrila-je-sestra-a-nagadalo-se-kako-voli-zene-668350|url-status=live|access-date=20 May 2020}}</ref>

In February 2011, Senidah released her debut [[R&B]]-influenced single "Pustinjom" (''Through a Desert''), but soon after joined [[electro (music)|electro]] [[soul-pop]] band Muff as the lead singer.<ref name="Noizz.rs">{{cite web|url=https://noizz.rs/nightlife/senidah-nasa-nova-opsesija/yrrssfh|title=Senidah – naša nova opsesija|date=August 2018|publisher=Noizz.rs|language=Serbian}}</ref> Their first single "Naj sije v očeh" (''Keep the Glow in the Eye'') was awarded the song of the year in Slovenia.<ref name="Noizz.rs" /> In March 2014, Muff took part in the [[Slovenia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2014#EMA 2014|preselection contest]] for [[Slovenia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2014|Slovenia]]'s representative at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2014]] with "Let Me Be (Myself)", finishing as the runner-ups to [[Tinkara Kovač]] and "[[Round and Round (Tinkara Kovač song)|Round and Round]]". Their debut eponymous album, released in November, was then declared the album of the year.<ref name="Noizz.rs" />

=== 2018–2019: Breakthrough, "Slađana" and ''Bez Tebe'' ===
In March 2018, Senidah released her solo [[trap music|trap]] single "[[Slađana (Senidah song)|Slađana]]" in [[Serbian language|Serbian]] to lackluster success. However, after being discovered by Serbian rappers and producers Reksona and [[Slobodan Veljković Coby|Coby]] from the [[Belgrade]]-based [[hip-hop]] label Bassivity Digital, she accepted their offer to re-upload the music video to their official [[YouTube]] channel.<ref name="vice">{{cite web|url=https://www.vice.com/rs/article/8xk8kk/senidah-za-vice-otkriva-ko-je-sladana-i-da-li-planira-selidbu-u-srbiju|title=Senidah za Vice otkriva ko je Slađana i da li planira da se preseli u Srbiju|date=March 2018|publisher=Vice.com|language=Serbian}}</ref> Soon, "Slađana" went viral and has accumulated more than fifty million views.{{efn|As of 21 April 2020}} Senidah successfully continued collaborating with Bassivity, through which she released her following single "Belo" (''White'') in June. She was also featured on "4 strane sveta" (''4 Sides of the World'') by Coby as part of the soundtrack to Serbian film ''[[South Wind (film)|South Wind]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://noizz.rs/nightlife/senidah-ponovo-dominira/7rsqf8p|title=Senidah ponovo dominira|date=June 2018|publisher=Noizz|language=Serbian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://noizz.rs/intervju/senidah-i-coby-za-noizz-o-odrastanju-devedesetih-i-juznom-vetru/efjcber|title=Senidah i Coby za Noizz o odrastanju devedesetih i Južnom Vetru|date=August 2018|publisher=Noizz.rs|language=Serbian}}</ref> At the last day of the year she released two more singles, titled "Bez tebe" (''Without You'') and "Nisi bio tu" (''You Weren't Here'').

In January the following year, Senidah won the Hip-Hop/Rap Song of the Year Award with "Slađana" at the [[2019 Music Awards Ceremony]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Spektakl je završen! Ovo su dobitnici MAC muzičkih nagrada |url=https://www.prva.rs/show-biz/vesti/312936/ovo-su-dobitnici-mac-muzickih-nagrada-uzivo |website=prva.rs|language=sr}}</ref> In March, she released a single in English, titled "Ride", under [[Universal Music Group|Universal Serbia]], which had been recorded a couple of years prior. Her debut album, ''[[Bez Tebe (album)|Bez Tebe]]'', was eventually released on 25 March, entirely in Serbian.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rts.rs/page/magazine/sr/story/431/muzika/3460150/balkanska-muzicka-zvezda-senidah-objavljuje-prvi-album.html|title=Balkanska muzička zvezda Senidah objavljuje prvi album|date=March 2019|publisher=RTS|language=Serbian}}</ref>

Uncorrupted people.</blockquote> The response sparked debates about the singer's possible [[bisexuality]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.blic.rs/zabava/senidah-priznala-da-je-privlace-zene-pevacica-zaintrigirala-sve-odgovorom-na-pitanje/lnblc4l|title=SENIDAH PRIZNALA DA JE PRIVLAČE ŽENE?
