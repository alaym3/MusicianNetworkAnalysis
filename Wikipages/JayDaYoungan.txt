'''Javorius Tykies Scott''' (born July 15, 1998), known professionally as '''JayDaYoungan''', is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter.<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web |last1=Thomas |first1=Fred |title=JayDaYoungan |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/jaydayoungan-mn0003734540/biography |website=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref> Scott is best known for his singles "23 Island", "Elimination" and "Opps", which have received 49, 27 and 26 million respective streams on [[SoundCloud]] as of November 2020. Scott had released the mixtapes ''Forever 23'' (2018), ''Endless Pain'' (2019), and his debut album ''Misunderstood'' (2019), which peaked at number 43 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. He was first exposed to the mainstream with his mixtapes ''Ruffwayy'' and ''The Real Jumpman 23'', which included the tracks "Sliding Freestyle" and "Muddy Situation".<ref name="Genius">{{cite web |last1=Fu |first1=Eddie |title=In Search Of: 20-Year-Old Louisiana Rapper JayDaYoungan Is Quietly Building His Fanbase |url=https://genius.com/a/who-is-20-year-old-louisiana-rapper-jaydayoungan-with-viral-hits-elimination-thot-thot |website=[[Genius (website)|Genius]] |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref>

== Career ==
===2017â€“2018: Beginnings, "Interstate", and ''Forever 23''===
In February 2017, Scott released his mixtape ''Ruffwayy'', which became a regional success and gave him local recognition; his song "Interstate" broke 4 million views on YouTube in under a month, he was then considered for a spot on the ''[[XXL (magazine)|XXL]]'' freshman list in 2018, a list of up-and-coming rappers.<ref>{{cite web |title=JAYDAYOUNGAN'S PITCH FOR 2018 XXL FRESHMAN |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2018/03/jaydayoungan-pitch-2018-xxl-freshman/ |website=XXL |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref>

Following that project, Scott issued the mixtape ''The Real Jumpman 23'', which had 13 tracks totalling 32 minutes, including the songs "Sliding Freestyle" and "Muddy Situation". He then released mixtapes ''Wake Up'' and ''Taking Off'', the latter of which featured rappers FG Famous and NBA 3Three.

His first nationwide hit came with the song "Elimination" on the mixtape ''23'', which has over 19.3&nbsp;million streams on [[Spotify]] as of January 2020. He was interviewed by the lyric company [[Genius (website)|Genius]] to break down the meaning of the song. The mixtape had 10 tracks at 28 minutes with no features.<ref name="Elimination">{{cite web |last=Reneau |first=Steven |title=JayDaYoungan Breaks Down The Meaning Of "Elimination" |url=https://genius.com/a/jaydayoungan-breaks-down-the-meaning-of-elimination |website=[[Genius (website)|Genius]] |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref> He then released the mixtape ''Forever 23'', including the tracks "Thot Thot" and "Purge", which have garnered 18.1 and 10 million streams respectively on Spotify and helped push him into international recognition. The critic site AllMusic rated the mixtape 3.5/5 stars, and despite it having no features, the mixtape reached number 86 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] becoming his first entry. It also hit number 37 on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums.<ref name="Forever 23 Review">{{cite web |last=Thomas |first=Fred |title=Forever 23 |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/forever-23-mw0003237620 |website=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref>

===2019â€“present: ''Endless Pain'', "23 Island", ''Misunderstood''  and ''23 Is Back''===

Following this, JayDaYoungan released the mixtape ''Endless Pain'', which became known for the tracks "Repo" and "War Ready" which stand at 6.5 and 4 million streams respectively on [[SoundCloud]] as of January 5, 2020. The album peaked at number 70 and number 37 on ''Billboard'' 200 and the Top R&B/Hip-Hop albums, respectively.<ref>{{cite web |last=Ch |first=Devin |title=Stream JayDaYoungan New Project "Endless Pain" |url=https://www.hotnewhiphop.com/stream-jaydayoungan-new-project-endless-pain-new-mixtape.119152.html |website=HotNewHipHop |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref> He and rapper Yungeen Ace then released the collaborative mixtape ''Can't Speak On It'', which included the songs "Opps" and "Creep Behind". He then released another mixtape titled ''Misunderstood'', which included the single "23 Island", which garnered 17.8&nbsp;million streams on Spotify as of January 5, 2020, and reached number 9 of the [[Bubbling Under R&B/Hip-Hop Singles|Bubbling Under R&B/Hip-Hop Chart]]. The mixtape had features from [[YFN Lucci]], [[Boosie Badazz]], FG Famous, JetSoo, and [[Lil Durk]]. Durk remixed the track "Dum" which was featured on the mixtape ''Endless Pain''. It featured 19 tracks at 53 minutes, and charted on the ''Billboard'' 200, peaking at number 43.<ref>{{cite magazine |title=Jaydayoungan |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/jaydayoungan/chart-history |magazine=Billboard |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref> JayDaYoungan and rapper [[Yung Bans]] had an online feud over the title of the album, as Bans released his debut album earlier in the year, also titled ''Misunderstood''.<ref>{{cite web |title=Yung Bans & JayDaYoungan exchange shots over "Misunderstood" album title |url=https://rapcurrent.com/2019/07/yung-bans-jaydayoungan-exchange-shots-over-misunderstood-album-title/ |website=RapCurrent |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref>

On January 3, 2020, Jay reached number 36 on the ''Billboard'' Emerging Artists Chart.<ref>{{cite magazine |title=JayDaYoungan |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/jaydayoungan/chart-history/AAF/song/1155402 |magazine=Billboard |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref>

In May 2020, JayDaYoungan released "Touch Your Toes" featuring rapper [[Latto]], as a single from his debut album ''Baby23'', which was released on June 5, 2020 and  named after his first child who was also born the same night.<ref>{{Cite web|title=JayDaYounganðŸ’” on Instagram: "MY FIRST BORN @jr_baby23 & MY FIRST DEBUT ALBUM is finally here ! On June 11, 2021, Jay released the album, ''23 Is Back'', a 13-track project which has no features.<ref name="IsBack">{{Cite web|title=JayDaYoungan - 23 Is Back|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/23-is-back/1569803798|access-date=2021-06-11|via=Apple Music}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
Scott stated he grew up listening to [[Kevin Gates]], [[Boosie Badazz]], and [[Chief Keef]]. Musically, his flow and style has been compared to Boosie and [[Youngboy Never Broke Again]]. He said, "[I] don't see me sounding similar to nobody else", and that he is not trying to imitate anybody else. He was noted for a style similar to that of [[Lil Wayne]] and Kevin Gates by critic Fred Thomas.<ref name="XXL" /><ref name="Forever 23 Review" />

On his goals, Scott said "Later on in my career, when I get to a certain point, I will be signing artists, starting my own label. Just perfecting my craft each and every day."<ref name="Elimination" />

==Personal life==
Scott was born on July 15, 1998, in [[Bogalusa, Louisiana|Bogalusa]], Louisiana. He began his rapping career at the age of 18 and dropped out of high school to pursue his career.

=== Legal issues ===
On May 11, 2019, Scott was charged with felony possession of a controlled substance without a prescription after being pulled over by Florida Highway Patrol in [[Gadsden County]], Florida. When pulled over, police officers found 20 grams of marijuana, drug paraphernalia, and oxycodone pills. Someone riding with him was also found to be in possession of a firearm. The people riding with him were given various other charges relating to the drugs and firearm.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hatfield |first1=William |title=Rap artist JayDaYoungan arrested by Florida Highway Patrol for felony possession |url=https://www.tallahassee.com/story/news/2019/05/12/rap-artist-jaydayoungan-arrested-florida-highway-patrol-felony-possession-controlled-substance-drugs/1182547001/ |website=The Tallahassee Democrat |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref> On May 12, Scott was released on a $2,500 bail. Scott was scheduled to perform at the Rolling Loud Music Festival and was unable to due to his arrest.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Centeno |first1=Tony |title=Jaydayoungan released from jail after felony drug possession arrest |url=https://www.xxlmag.com/news/2019/05/jaydayoungan-released-jail-arrest/ |website=XXL |accessdate=January 5, 2020}}</ref> Scott has not yet stood trial.

In March 2020, after it was alleged he assaulted a pregnant woman, police raided Scott's house, seizing $24,000 in cash, Promethazine, Oxycodone, and multiple firearms. Police arrested Scott after he was allegedly "found hiding in an attic." Scott was charged with assaulting a pregnant woman and illegal drug possession, with bail set at $20,000. Another man at his house, Kardell Robertson, was arrested for unlawful possession of a firearm and granted a $100 bail.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Espinoza |first1=Joshua |title=JayDaYoungan Arrested After Being Accused of Assaulting Pregnant Woman |url=https://www.complex.com/music/2020/02/jaydayoungan-arrested-for-domestic-abuse-found-hiding-in-attic |website=Complex |accessdate=March 13, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Ponder |first=Erica |title=Rapper JayDaYoungan faces charges after being accused of assaulting a pregnant woman, officials say |date=February 7, 2020 |url=https://www.click2houston.com/news/local/2020/02/07/houston-rapper-faces-charges-after-being-accused-of-assaulting-a-pregnant-woman-officials-say/ |accessdate=March 13, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Stunson |first=Mike |title=Rapper JayDaYoungan accused of hitting pregnant woman was hiding in attic, TX cops say |url=https://www.miamiherald.com/news/nation-world/national/article240087268.html |website=Miami Herald |accessdate=March 13, 2020}}</ref>

On September 16, 2021, Scott was arrested on charges of accessory to second-degree murder and obstruction of justice, stemming from a fatal shooting that occurred in August 2020. Four days later, Scott was released on a $175,000 bond.<ref>{{cite news|date = September 20, 2021|title = Popular rapper JayDaYoungan arrested as accessory in Roseland Trail Ride murder, sheriff says|url = https://www.theadvocate.com/baton_rouge/news/article_aa45a65c-1a46-11ec-bd77-4fc31ed4204f.html|author = Finn, James|access-date = September 27, 2021}}</ref>
