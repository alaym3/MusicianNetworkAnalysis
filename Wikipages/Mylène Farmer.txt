'''Mylène Jeanne Gautier'''<ref>The American society of composers, authors and publishers [https://archive.today/20120526231953/http://www.ascap.com/ace/search.cfm?requesttimeout=300&mode=results&searchstr=1522016&search_in=c&search_type=exact&search_det=t,s,w,p,b,v&results_pp=20&start=1].{{Dead link|date=June 2012}}. Retrieved 26 April 2009.</ref> ({{IPA-fr|milɛn ʒan ɡotje|lang}}; born 12 September 1961), known professionally as '''Mylène Farmer''' ({{IPA-fr|milɛn faʁmœʁ|}}), is a Canadian-born French singer, songwriter, occasional actress, writer, and entrepreneur. Having sold more than 30 million records in France,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.infodisc.fr/Vente_Artiste.php|title=Bilan des Ventes globales (Albums & Chansons) par Artiste|website=www.infodisc.fr}}</ref> she is among the most successful recording artists of all time in that country.<ref>{{cite web |author=e-TF1 |url=http://lci.tf1.fr/biographies/mylene-farmer-4883698.html |title=Mylène Farmer – Actualité, vidéos et photos – MYTF1News |publisher=Lci.tf1.fr |access-date=14 March 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140314064858/http://lci.tf1.fr/biographies/mylene-farmer-4883698.html |archive-date=14 March 2014  }}</ref> She holds the record for the most number one hit singles in the French charts, with twenty-one to date – eight of which were consecutive.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-74585.html |title=Mylène Farmer : n°1 avec "Lonely Lisa" |publisher=Chartsinfrance.net |access-date=14 March 2014}}</ref>

Born in [[Pierrefonds, Quebec|Pierrefonds]], [[Quebec]], [[Canada]] (a former city that is now part of the [[Boroughs of Montreal|Montreal borough]] of [[Pierrefonds-Roxboro]]),<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-07-19|title=Au pays de Mylène Farmer|url=https://plus.lapresse.ca/screens/4bac51e3-823c-443f-b33b-3b0d81b38fab__7C___0.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=La Presse+|language=fr}}</ref> to French parents, the family soon relocated to [[Chaville]], near Paris, where Farmer pursued a career in modelling and acting.<ref name=":16">{{Cite web|work=Paris Match|title=Mylène Farmer, sa maman est décédée|url=https://www.parismatch.com/People/Musique/Mylene-Farmer-sa-maman-est-decedee-1019542|access-date=2021-04-16|language=fr}}</ref> She later gained prominence as a recording artist with the release of her debut single "[[Maman a tort]]" in 1984.<ref>{{Cite news|date=4 April 1984|title=Maman a tort|work=[[La Dépêche du Midi]]|url=http://www.mylene.net/presse/presse1984/mylene-farmer_presse_la-depeche_04041984.jpg|access-date=20 March 2008}}</ref> In 1988, she released her second studio album, ''[[Ainsi soit je...]]'', which sold 1.5 million copies in France.<ref name=":17">{{Cite web|title=Album Ainsi soit je...|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/d_ainsisoitje.php|url-status=live|access-date=16 April 2021|website=Mylene.net}}</ref> The album spawned Farmer's first number one on the French singles chart, "[[Pourvu qu'elles soient douces]]".<ref name=":18">{{Cite web|title=lescharts.com - Les charts français|url=https://lescharts.com/search.asp?todo=notfound|access-date=2021-04-16|website=lescharts.com}}</ref> Her third studio album, ''[[L'Autre...]]'', sold two million copies, becoming Farmer's best-selling album to date.<ref>{{Cite news|date=11 September 2009|title=Les spectacles: Mylène Farmer, les dessous du mythe|work=[[Le Parisien]]|url=http://www.mylene.net/presse/presse2009/mylene-farmer_presse_le-parisien_11-09-2009_002.jpg|access-date=5 February 2018}}</ref> Its lead single, "[[Désenchantée]]", became Farmer's most successful single, and is often described as her [[signature song]].<ref>{{Cite book|title=Quid|year=1993}}</ref>

Departing from the [[synth-pop]] and [[Europop]] soundscape of her first albums, Farmer reinvented her musical style with the release of her fourth studio album, ''[[Anamorphosée]]'', in 1995.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=21 March 2005|title=Anamorphosée - Mylène Farmer - CD album - Achat & prix {{!}} fnac|url=https://www.fnac.com/a1643087/Mylene-Farmer-Anamorphosee-CD-album|url-status=live|access-date=16 April 2021|website=[[fnac]]}}</ref> Recorded in the United States, the album features more live instrumentation and shows rock and R&B-oriented influences.<ref name=":0" /> Her fifth studio album, ''[[Innamoramento]]'', was released in 1999, featuring more [[techno]] influences.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Innamoramento|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/Innamoramento-a100921165.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> In the second part of the 2000s, Farmer continued exploring a range of genres on her albums, namely [[Acoustic music|acoustic pop music]] on ''[[Avant que l'ombre...]]''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Album Avant que l'ombre...|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/mylene-farmer_avant-que-l-ombre.php|url-status=live|access-date=16 April 2021|website=Mylene.net}}</ref> and [[electronica]] on ''[[Point de suture]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=FARMER, MYLENE - Point de Suture - Amazon.com Music|url=https://www.amazon.com/Point-Suture-MYLENE-FARMER/dp/B001BSH10M|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.amazon.com}}</ref>

In 2010, Farmer released her eighth studio album, ''[[Bleu noir]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Universal Music France - Infos et exclus sur vos artistes préférés|url=https://www.universalmusic.fr/|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.universalmusic.fr|language=fr-FR}}</ref> The album marked Farmer's return to the dark synth-pop and Europop production of her first albums.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|title=FARMER, MYLENE - Bleu Noir - Amazon.com Music|url=https://www.amazon.com/Bleu-Noir-MYLENE-FARMER/dp/B0049W64CI|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.amazon.com}}</ref> The lead single, "[[Oui mais... non]]", sold over 170.000 copies in France.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Oui mais... non - Classements|url=http://mylene.net/mylene/mylene-farmer_oui-mais-non_classements.php|url-status=live|access-date=9 February 2011|website=Mylene.net}}</ref> In 2015, Farmer's tenth studio album, ''[[Interstellaires]]'', debuted at number one in France;<ref name="Mylène Farmer - Interstellaires">{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Interstellaires|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/Interstellaires-a121780048.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> the album was preceded by the lead single [[Stolen Car (Take Me Dancing)|"Stolen Car"]], a duet with [[Sting (musician)|Sting]], which reached the number one position on the French singles chart in September 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer et Sting au top : "Stolen Car" numéro 1 des ventes de singles|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-98739.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> Her eleventh studio album, ''[[Désobéissance]]'', was released in 2018, debuting at number one and selling over 265.000 copies.<ref name="chartsinfrance.net">{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer : numéro un des ventes, "Désobéissance" signe un démarrage tonitruant|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-108230.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref>

In addition to her music career, Farmer also worked as a songwriter for French singer [[Alizée]], while also being responsible for her image and public appearances.<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|title=Alizée : "Il faut provoquer sa chance"|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/actualite/interview-49503.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> Her debut single, "[[Moi... Lolita]]", written by Farmer, reached number one in several countries in Europe,<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|title=ultratop.be - ULTRATOP BELGIAN CHARTS|url=https://www.ultratop.be/nl/search.asp?todo=notfound|access-date=2021-04-16|website=ultratop.be}}</ref><ref name=":9">{{Cite web|title=danishcharts.com - Danish charts portal|url=https://danishcharts.dk/search.asp?todo=notfound|access-date=2021-04-16|website=danishcharts.dk}}</ref><ref name=":10">{{Cite web|title=Offizielle Deutsche Charts - Offizielle Deutsche Charts|url=https://www.offiziellecharts.de/titel-details-4377|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.offiziellecharts.de}}</ref><ref name=":11">{{Cite web|title=italiancharts.com - Italian charts portal|url=https://italiancharts.com/search.asp?todo=notfound|access-date=2021-04-16|website=italiancharts.com}}</ref> and peaked at number nine on the [[UK Singles Chart]] in February 2002.<ref name=":12">{{Cite web|title=moi lolita {{!}} full Official Chart History {{!}} Official Charts Company|url=https://www.officialcharts.com/search/singles/moi-lolita/|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.officialcharts.com|language=en}}</ref> Farmer made her film debut in 1994, starring in ''[[Giorgino]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|last=AlloCine|title=Giorgino|url=https://www.allocine.fr/film/fichefilm_gen_cfilm=47743.html|language=fr|access-date=2021-04-16}}</ref> and in 2018, she starred in the English-language horror film ''[[Ghostland]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ghostland (2018)|url=https://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/incident_in_a_ghost_land_2018|language=en|access-date=2021-04-16}}</ref>

Throughout her career, Farmer has broken a number of records. She is the best-selling French singer since 1984,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer, la machine à cash d'Universal rejoint Sony Music|url=https://www.challenges.fr/high-tech/grand-public/mylene-farmer-la-machine-a-cash-d-universal-rejoint-sony-music_460922|access-date=2021-04-16|website=Challenges|date=16 March 2017|language=fr}}</ref> has a record number of diamond records,<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer : l'album "Monkey Me" certifié disque de diamant en moins d'un mois|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-83609.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> and she is the only artist to have obtained a diamond album in four consecutive decades.<ref name=":1" /> Selling over two million copies, her 2001 greatest hits album ''[[Les Mots (album)|Les Mots]]'' is the highest-selling compilation album by a French recording artist.<ref name=":13">{{Cite news|date=January 2016|title=Mylène Farmer exclusive|work=Gael Magazine|url=https://www.mylene.net/presse-mylene-farmer/presse/gael-magazine-janvier-2016}}</ref>

==Biography and career==

=== 1961–1983: Early years and career beginnings ===
Mylène Jeanne Gautier was born 12 September 1961 in [[Pierrefonds, Quebec|Pierrefonds]], [[Quebec]], [[Canada]] (a former city now part of the present-day [[Boroughs of Montreal|Montreal borough]] of [[Pierrefonds-Roxboro]]). Her father, Max Gautier was a dam engineer; her mother, Marguerite (née Martin) was a homemaker.<ref name=":19">{{Cite web|date=2018-03-12|title=Enfant caché, drame de sa vie...Ce que vous ne savez pas sur Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.planet.fr/la-vie-des-stars-enfant-cache-drame-de-sa-vie-ce-que-vous-ne-savez-pas-sur-mylene-farmer.1523225.764617.html|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Planet|language=fr}}</ref> The family moved from France in the late 1950s due to her father's work responsibilities.<ref name=":19" /> Her family moved back to France when Farmer was eight, settling in the Parisian [[banlieue]] of [[Chaville]].<ref name=":16" /> When she first arrived in France, she took speech classes after her school declared her [[Quebec French|Québécois]] accent to be "improper".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gala.fr/l_actu/news_de_stars/mylene_farmer_mise_a_nu_138455 |title=Mylène Farmer mise à nu – Gala |language=fr |publisher=Gala.fr |date=9 November 2010 |access-date=23 August 2012}}</ref> At the age of 17, Farmer discovered acting and undertook a three-year course at the [[Cours Florent]], a drama school in Paris.<ref name=":20">{{Cite web|title=De Zézette à "Ghostland"... Mylène Farmer a toujours joué la comédie|url=https://www.20minutes.fr/culture/2236179-20180312-pere-noel-ordure-ghostland-mylene-farmer-toujours-joue-comedie|access-date=2021-04-18|website=www.20minutes.fr|language=fr}}</ref> Changing her name to Mylène Farmer as a tribute to her idol, 1930s Hollywood actress [[Frances Farmer]], she began to earn a living as a model acting in several TV adverts, such as those for [[IKEA]], [[Fiskars]] and Caisse d'Epargne.<ref>{{Cite web|last=mylenefarmer|date=2007-01-30|title=Pub Ikéa|url=https://mylenefarmer.skyrock.com/704310550-Pub-Ikea.html|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Skyrock|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Pub Fiskars avec Mylène Farmer 1983 Restaurated SD Up To HD|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LBe-fTmv34M|language=en|access-date=2021-04-18}}</ref> Farmer also appeared in a local stage production of ''[[Le Pere Noel est une ordure]]''.<ref name=":20" />

In 1984, Farmer met [[Laurent Boutonnat]], a young film student, after answering a newspaper advert for an actress for a small film project he was working on.<ref name=":21" /> Farmer and Boutonnat became friends and forged a creative partnership, writing and producing the music.<ref name=":22">{{Cite web|title=Biographie de Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.innamoramento.net/mylene-farmer/page/biographie|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Innamoramento.net|language=fr}}</ref> Boutonnat, whose ambition was to become a film director, became the creative force behind Farmer's videos.<ref name=":22" /> In 1984, Farmer recorded and released the song "[[Maman a tort]]", written by Boutonnat and Jérôme Dahan.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Musée SACEM : Maman a tort|url=https://musee.sacem.fr/index.php/ExhibitionCMS/Chroniques/SimpleExhibitions?id=2471|access-date=2021-04-18|website=musee.sacem.fr|language=fr}}</ref> The trio continued to work together on what would become Farmer's first studio album.

=== 1984–1992: ''Cendres de lune,'' ''Ainsi soit je...'' and ''L'Autre...'' ===
Farmer's debut studio album, ''[[Cendres de lune]]'', was released in 1986.<ref name=":24"/> During the recording process, it was decided that Farmer would write lyrics and Boutonnat would write music and direct the music videos.<ref name=":22" /> "[[Libertine (song)|Libertine]]" was released as the lead single of the album, accompanied by a music video in the style of a [[short film]], inspired by 19th century literature.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Boutonnat|first=Laurent|title=Mylène Farmer: Libertine|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0126393/|type=Short, Action, Adventure, Music|others=Mylène Farmer, Gérard Nublat, Sophie Tellier, Rambo Kowalsky|publisher=Movie-Box, PolyGram Filmed Entertainment|access-date=2021-04-18}}</ref>

In 1988, Boutonnat and Farmer started work on her sophomore album, ''[[Ainsi soit je...]]''. The album sold 1.5 million copies in France<ref name=":17" /> and spawned Farmer's first number one on the French singles chart, "[[Pourvu qu'elles soient douces]]".<ref name=":18" /> The album also features the songs "[[Sans contrefaçon]]" and the [[Juliette Gréco]] cover "[[Déshabillez-moi]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ainsi soit-je by Mylène Farmer|date=January 1988|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/ainsi-soit-je/1444007897|language=en-us|access-date=2021-04-18}}</ref> ''Ainsi soit je...'' was the best-selling female album of the 1980s in France.<ref>''Les 100 albums les plus vendus des années 80'', Gilles Verlant, 2007, {{ISBN|88-6112-051-2}}</ref>

Following the positive response to small-scale concerts at the [[Dôme de Paris|Palais de Sport]],<ref>{{Cite news|date=17 May 1989|title=Mylène Farmer enfin sur scène "sans contrefaçon"|work=L'Union|url=http://www.devant-soi.com/Monde-de-Myl%E8ne/presse/articles-presse/1989/L'UNION_170589a.jpg|access-date=30 March 2008}}</ref> Farmer agreed to embark on a 50-date [[Mylène Farmer en concert|tour]] throughout [[Organisation internationale de la Francophonie|francophone]] Europe in 1989.<ref name=":23">{{Cite web|title=Concerts En Concert 89 de Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.innamoramento.net/mylene-farmer/concerts/en-concert-89|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Innamoramento.net|language=fr}}</ref> The tour saw Farmer play in arenas and featured dancers and outfits designed by French fashion designer [[Thierry Mugler]].<ref name=":23" /> A live album documenting the tour was released at the end of that year, titled ''[[En Concert (Mylène Farmer album)|En Concert]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylene Farmer* - En Concert|url=https://www.discogs.com/Mylene-Farmer-En-Concert/master/63006|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Discogs|language=en}}</ref>

Farmer released the song "[[Désenchantée]]" in March 1991. An uptempo pop song, "Désenchantée" talks about a disillusioned generation.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2005-08-12|title=Désenchantée|language=fr|work=Le Monde.fr|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/culture/article/2005/08/12/desenchantee_679631_3246.html|access-date=2021-04-18}}</ref> The song was preceded by a number of demonstrations in Paris, led by high-school students, who were "protest[ing] against overcrowded classrooms, inadequate security and too few teachers".<ref>{{Cite news|last=Riding|first=Alan|date=1990-11-14|title=As Paris Mends, Students Plan More Protests|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1990/11/14/world/as-paris-mends-students-plan-more-protests.html|access-date=2021-04-18|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=1990-11-13|title=Students Riot, Battle Police in Paris Protest|url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1990-11-13-mn-4548-story.html|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Los Angeles Times|language=en-US}}</ref> The song ended up spending nine weeks atop the French singles chart,<ref name=":24">{{Cite web|title=lescharts.com - Les charts français|url=https://lescharts.com/search.asp?todo=notfound|access-date=2021-04-18|website=lescharts.com}}</ref> becoming Farmer's most successful single to date and her [[List of signature songs|signature song]]. The song was also successful in Belgium, where it spent six weeks at number one, and it was a top ten hit in several European countries.<ref name=":24" /> Its accompanying music video, shot in Budapest, sees Farmer play a rebelling prisoner in a [[concentration camp]].<ref>''Instant-Mag'', Nº. 20, 2005, p. 9</ref>

Led by the success of "Désenchantée", ''[[L'autre...]]'' was released in April 1991. The album spent twenty consecutive weeks on top of the French album charts, becoming Farmer's best-selling studio album to date with two million copies sold.<ref name="leparisien1">{{cite web|date=11 September 2009|title=Les spectacles: Mylène Farmer, les dessous du mythe|url=http://www.mylene.net/presse/presse2009/mylene-farmer_presse_le-parisien_11-09-2009_002.jpg|access-date=18 August 2016|website=[[Le Parisien]]|language=fr}}</ref> The album also charted in Switzerland, Sweden and Germany.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Album L'autre... de Mylène Farmer (1991)|url=https://www.innamoramento.net/mylene-farmer/albums/l-autre|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Innamoramento.net|language=fr}}</ref> To further promote the album, the singles "[[Regrets (Mylène Farmer and Jean-Louis Murat song)|Regrets]]", "[[Je t'aime mélancolie]]" and "[[Beyond My Control]]" were released.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylene Farmer* - L'Autre...|url=https://www.discogs.com/Mylene-Farmer-LAutre/master/63042|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Discogs|language=en}}</ref>

1992 saw the release of the remix compilation ''[[Dance Remixes]]''. It was led by the single "[[Que mon cœur lâche]]", which deals with the topic of AIDS and sexual relations.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Beausir|first=Jean-Claude|title=Présentation et analyse de l'unvers Farmérien|url=https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/35428077.pdf|journal=Core.ac.uk}}</ref> It was accompanied by a video directed by [[Luc Besson]], in which Farmer plays an angel sent down to earth by God to report on the changing ways people experience love.<ref>{{Cite web|last=AlloCine|title=Que mon coeur lâche|url=https://www.allocine.fr/film/fichefilm_gen_cfilm=148145.html|language=fr|access-date=2021-04-18}}</ref>

=== 1992–2000: ''Anamorphosée'' and ''Innamoramento'' ===
In 1994, Farmer starred in the film ''[[Giorgino]]'', written and directed by Boutonnat.<ref>{{Cite web|title=GIORGINO (1994)|url=https://www2.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b7c82638d|access-date=2021-04-18|website=BFI|language=en}}</ref> The film received mixed-to-negative reviews from critics<ref>{{Cite news|date=5 October 1994|title=Le Parisien}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=12 October 1994|title=La voix du Nord}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|last=Vatrican|first=Vincent|title=Giorgino|publisher=Cahiers du cinéma|year=1994|pages=1994}}</ref> and was a [[box-office bomb]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=AlloCine|title=Saga "Mylène Farmer fait son cinéma", 2ème partie : l'échec du film "Giorgino" et les rendez-vous manqués|url=https://www.allocine.fr/article/fichearticle_gen_carticle=18626753.html|access-date=2021-04-18|website=AlloCiné|language=fr}}</ref> Following this, Farmer relocated to [[Los Angeles]].<ref name=":2" />

While in the U.S., Farmer and Boutonnat started working on her fourth studio album, ''[[Anamorphosée]]''. The album marked an artistic turn for Farmer, moving away from her early [[synth-pop]] music in favour of [[pop rock]], [[Rhythm and blues|R&B]] and [[new jack swing]] influences.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Amazon.de:Kundenrezensionen: Les Mots|url=https://www.amazon.de/Mots-Mylene-Farmer/product-reviews/B000063TDX?reviewerType=all_reviews|access-date=2021-04-18|website=www.amazon.de}}</ref> The album was launched by "[[XXL (Mylène Farmer song)|XXL]]", the music video of which was directed by [[Marcus Nispel]] and features Farmer strapped to the front of a moving train.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer: XXL (1995)|url=https://letterboxd.com/film/mylene-farmer-xxl/|language=en|access-date=2021-04-18}}</ref> To further promote the album, the singles "[[L'Instant X]]", "[[California (Mylène Farmer song)|California]]", "[[Comme j'ai mal]]" and "[[Rêver]]" were released. The music video for "California" was directed by American filmmaker [[Abel Ferrara]] and features Farmer in a dual role.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Macaulay|first=Scott|title=ABEL FERRARA'S "CALIFORNIA"|url=https://filmmakermagazine.com/3459-abel-ferraras-california/|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Filmmaker Magazine|date=18 May 2008|language=en-US}}</ref> The album differs lyrically from Farmer's previous work: "[[Rêver]]" deals with [[the Holocaust]] and features a call for tolerance,<ref>{{Cite book|last=Lambert M. Surhone, Miriam T. Timpledon|title=Rever: Mylene Farmer, Single, Anamorphosee, Laurent Boutonnat, Mylenium Tour, Avant que l'Ombre... A Bercy|id={{ASIN|6130436483|country=fr}}}}</ref> "[[XXL (Mylène Farmer song)|XXL]]" talks about universal love from a [[Feminism|feminist]] perspective,<ref>{{Cite book|last=Royer|first=Hugues|title=Mylène, biographie|publisher=Flammarion|year=2008|location=Spain|pages=342}}</ref> and "[[L'Instant X]]" offers a humorous look at [[Global catastrophic risk|the end of the world]].<ref>{{Cite book|last=Cachin|first=Benoît|title=Le Dictionnaire des Chansons de Mylène Farmer|publisher=Tournon|year=2006|pages=130–134}}</ref>

In summer 1996, Farmer embarked on her second concert tour, during which she performed 21 shows in arenas around France, Belgium and Switzerland.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer Concert Map by tour: Tour 1996 {{!}} setlist.fm|url=https://www.setlist.fm/stats/concert-map/mylene-farmer-4bd6b7a2.html?tour=7bd076b4|access-date=2021-04-18|website=www.setlist.fm}}</ref> Inspired by American concert tours,<ref>{{Cite news|last=Morel|first=Alain|date=31 May 1996|title=Mylène Farmer métamorphosée|work=[[Le Parisien]]|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/mylene-farmer_interview_le-parisien_31-mai-1996|access-date=18 April 2021}}</ref> Farmer performed the majority of songs from ''Anamorphosée'', songs from her previous studio albums, and a cover of "[[La Poupée qui fait non]]" with French singer [[Khaled (musician)|Khaled]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer Tour Statistics: Tour 1996 {{!}} setlist.fm|url=https://www.setlist.fm/stats/mylene-farmer-4bd6b7a2.html?tour=7bd076b4|access-date=2021-04-18|website=www.setlist.fm}}</ref> The corresponding live album, ''[[Live à Bercy]]'', was released in May 1997.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Live à Bercy by Mylène Farmer|date = January 1997|url=https://music.apple.com/fr/album/live-%C3%A0-bercy/1452849069?l=en|language=en-gb|access-date=2021-04-18}}</ref>

Farmer released her fifth studio album ''[[Innamoramento]]'' in 1999. The album was preceded by "[[L'Âme-stram-gram]]", an uptempo techno song with a music video inspired by the film ''[[A Chinese Ghost Story]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylene Farmer: Music Videos, Vol. 2 & 3|url=https://www.amazon.com/Mylene-Farmer-Music-Videos-Vol/product-reviews/B000053W9P?pageNumber=2|access-date=2021-04-18|website=www.amazon.com}}</ref> Both the single and the album reached the second position on the French charts.<ref>{{cite web|title=Innamoramento.net : Classements|url=http://www.innamoramento.net/classements|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110316115036/http://www.innamoramento.net/classements/|archive-date=16 March 2011|publisher=Innamoramento.net}}</ref> The music video for the second single, "[[Je te rends ton amour]]", sparked controversy because of its religious and transgressive imagery, and was censored by French TV channel [[M6 (TV channel)|M6]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2014-10-01|title=100 Chansons censurées : Le clip "intolérable" de Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.franceinter.fr/musique/100-chansons-censurees-le-clip-intolerable-de-mylene-farmer|access-date=2021-04-18|website=www.franceinter.fr|language=fr}}</ref> Consequently, the video was released on VHS, with the profits donated to French charity [[Sidaction]] to support [[HIV/AIDS research]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/hashtagnp/status/1360273580454522881|url=https://twitter.com/hashtagnp/status/1360273580454522881|access-date=2021-04-18|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> Furthermore, the singles "[[Souviens-toi du jour]]", "[[Optimistique-moi]]" and "[[Innamoramento (single)|Innamoramento]]" were released in promotion of the album.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylene Farmer* - In late 1999, Farmer embarked on her third concert tour, the ''[[Mylenium Tour]]''.<ref name=":15">{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Mylenium Tour - Dates et Villes|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/concerts_mylenium-tour_dates.php|url-status=live|access-date=18 April 2021|website=Mylene.net}}</ref> The stage of the show featured aerial structures and a pharaonic statue at the centre of the stage. Farmer performed 42 shows in total, including three shows in Russia, her first concerts in a non-French-speaking country.<ref name=":15" /> The tour concluded in March 2000.<ref name=":15" />

=== 2000–2007: Alizée, ''Les mots'' and ''Avant que l'ombre...'' ===
{{Main|Alizée}}

In 2000, Farmer and Boutonnat started working on songs and video ideas they felt appropriate for a younger singer. They discovered [[Alizée Jacotey]] on the French television show ''Graines de stars''. Farmer and Boutonnat wrote and produced Alizée's albums ''[[Gourmandises]]'' (2001)<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gourmandises by Alizée|date=4 July 2018|url=https://music.apple.com/us/music-video/gourmandises/1391360743|language=en-us|access-date=2021-04-17}}</ref> and ''[[Mes courants électriques]]'' (2003).<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mes courants électriques by Alizée|date=15 June 2018|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/mes-courants-%C3%A9lectriques/1391337513|language=en-us|access-date=2021-04-17}}</ref> In addition, Farmer was also responsible for Alizée's image and public appearances.<ref name=":7" /> Her debut single, "[[Moi... Lolita]]", written by Farmer, reached number one in several countries in Europe,<ref name=":8" /><ref name=":9" /><ref name=":10" /><ref name=":11" /> and peaked at number nine on the [[UK Singles Chart]] in February 2002.<ref name=":12" /> It also went on to sell more than one million copies in France.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alizée - La biographie de Alizée avec Gala.fr|url=https://www.gala.fr/stars_et_gotha/alizee|access-date=2021-04-17|website=Gala.fr|language=fr}}</ref> In 2005, after two albums and a concert tour, Alizée amicably parted ways with the duo to work with different songwriters and producers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teemix.aufeminin.com/mag/musique/d2802/x16633.html |title=Mylène Farmer et Alizée – Interview vidéo d'Alizée, Psychédélices – Teemix |publisher=Teemix.aufeminin.com |access-date=14 March 2014}}</ref>

In 2001, Farmer recorded "[[L'Histoire d'une fée, c'est...]]" for the animated film ''[[Rugrats in Paris: The Movie]].'' At the end of the year, Universal released Farmer's first greatest hits collection: ''[[Les Mots (album)|Les Mots]].'' ''Les Mots'' was the best selling album of 2001 and 2002,<ref name=":13" /> and remains the best selling greatest hits album in France with more than 1.5 million copies sold.<ref>{{Cite web|title=InfoDisc : Les Meilleures Ventes de CD (Albums) depuis 2000|url=http://www.infodisc.fr/Ventes_Albums_Depuis_2000.php|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.infodisc.fr}}</ref> The album also featured new tracks, including [[Les Mots (song)|the title track]], a duet with American singer [[Seal (musician)|Seal]], "[[C'est une belle journée]]" and "[[Pardonne-moi (Mylène Farmer song)|Pardonne-moi]]".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Les mots by Mylène Farmer on Apple Music - iTunes - Apple|url=https://music.apple.com/be/album/77625490?i=77625593&ign-mpt=uo%3D6|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-17|website=music.apple.com}}</ref>

After a hiatus of four years, Farmer held a press conference in 2005, announcing her new album, ''[[Avant que l'ombre...]],'' the single "[[Fuck Them All]]", as well as a special 13-night concert engagement at the [[Palais Omnisports de Paris-Bercy]] in January 2006.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Farmer|first=Emilio / Mylene|title=Mylène Farmer - Conférence de Presse - 2005 - MCM - Vidéo Dailymotion|url=https://www.dailymotion.com/video/x3eolrl|access-date=2021-04-17|website=Dailymotion|date=20 November 2015|language=fr-FR}}</ref> The residency in Bercy featured a complex set designed by [[Mark Fisher (architect)|Mark Fisher]], including two stages, a mobile bridge to link the two stages and a water curtain used to project words and the singer's silhouette.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Interview de Mark Fisher en 2006|url=https://www.innamoramento.net/mylene-farmer/confidences-interviews/2006-mark-fisher|access-date=2021-04-17|website=Innamoramento.net|language=fr}}</ref> A live album and a concert DVD, ''[[Avant que l'ombre... à Bercy]]'', were released in December 2006.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mylene.net/img_data/pm06.jpg|title=Paris Match, 2008-03-20}}</ref>

Following this, Farmer and American electronic musician [[Moby]] worked on the French-language duet version of the singer's song "[[Slipping Away (Moby song)|Slipping Away]]", with the French lyrics translated by Farmer herself.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Moby & Mylène Farmer - Slipping away (Crier la vie) - Histoire|url=http://www.mylene.net/mylene/d_s_clv_histoire.php|url-status=live|access-date=17 April 2021|website=Mylene.net}}</ref> In 2007, Farmer recorded a song "Devant soi" for the soundtrack of the film ''[[Jacquou le Croquant]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|last=Radio|first=M.|date=2020-06-30|title=M Radio|url=https://mradio.fr/news/event/41946/mylene-farmer-devant-soi-enfin-disponible-en-streaming|access-date=2021-04-17|website=M Radio|language=fr-FR}}</ref> and worked as a voice actress in [[Luc Besson]]'s animated feature ''[[Arthur and the Minimoys]]'', lending her voice to Princess Selenia.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2006-12-13|title=Madonna et Mylène Farmer, les voix de Sélénia|url=https://www.dhnet.be/archive/madonna-et-mylene-farmer-les-voix-de-selenia-51b7f855e4b0de6db99b9ebb|access-date=2021-04-17|website=DH Les Sports +|language=fr}}</ref> Farmer would later reprise her role in the film's two sequels.

=== 2008–2014: ''Point de suture'', ''Bleu noir'' and ''Monkey Me'' ===
Farmer's seventh studio album, ''[[Point de Suture]]'', was released on 25 August 2008, debuting at number one on the French album chart.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Point de suture|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/Point-de-suture-ac13412.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> Along with its lead single "[[Dégénération]]", the album features more electronic instrumentation and is predominately uptempo.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Point de Suture - MYLENE FARMER|url=https://www.amazon.com/Point-Suture-MYLENE-FARMER/dp/B001BSH10M|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.amazon.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Release group "Point de suture" by Mylène Farmer - MusicBrainz|url=https://musicbrainz.org/release-group/f2fecc64-53c9-3c85-ac6d-8c50920730e4|access-date=2021-04-17|website=musicbrainz.org}}</ref> Other singles released from the album include "[[Appelle mon numéro]]" and "[[C'est dans l'air]]", bothof which charted at number one on the French singles chart.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Appelle Mon Numéro|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/Appelle-Mon-Numero--a104530415.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.chartsinfrance.net}}</ref>

[[File:Cassar & Farmer.jpg|left|thumb|Farmer on stage at [[Stade de France]] during her 2009 concert tour.]]
Following the release of the album, Farmer embarked on her fifth concert tour entitled ''[[Mylène Farmer en tournée]]''. The tour saw Farmer play in stadiums for the first time in her career, giving concerts at the [[Stade de France]], [[Stade de Genève]] and [[King Baudouin Stadium]], as well as a number of arenas in France and Russia.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-06-06|title=Mylène Farmer at Stade de France|url=https://www.stadefrance.com/en/stadium/mylene-farmer-stade-de-france|access-date=2021-04-17|website=Stade de France|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2009-09-04|title=Mylène Farmer crée l'évènement à Genève|url=https://www.rts.ch/info/culture/1058019-mylene-farmer-cree-levenement-a-geneve.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=rts.ch|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2009-09-17|title=Mylène Farmer s'offre le stade Roi Baudouin|url=https://www.dhnet.be/medias/musique/mylene-farmer-s-offre-le-stade-roi-baudouin-51b7aa1fe4b0de6db9868392|access-date=2021-04-17|website=DH Les Sports +|language=fr}}</ref> A live album, ''[[N°5 on Tour]]'', was released on 7 December 2009, which was followed by a DVD of her Stade de France concerts.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer Stade de France: Movies & TV - Amazon.com|url=https://www.amazon.com/Myl%C3%A8ne-Farmer-Stade-de-France/dp/B0035LXVMA|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.amazon.com}}</ref>

Farmer recorded a duet with French [[Chanson]] singer [[Line Renaud]] entitled "C'est pas l'heure".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Musique : Line Renaud enregistre un titre avec Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.cosmopolitan.fr/,musique-line-renaud-enregistre-un-titre-avec-mylene-farmer,2151,1346694.asp|access-date=2021-04-17|website=Cosmopolitan.fr|language=fr}}</ref> In November 2010, Farmer recorded her vocals for a cover of "[[Never Tear Us Apart]]" with [[Ben Harper]] for Australian band [[INXS]]'s album [[Original Sin (INXS album)|''Original Sin'']].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-07-08|title=Hear a new version of INXS's 'Never Tear Us Apart' with Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.nme.com/news/music/new-version-inxs-never-tear-us-apart-mylene-farmer-2525564|access-date=2021-04-17|website=NME|language=en-GB}}</ref>

2010 also saw the release of Farmer's eighth studio album, ''[[Bleu noir]]''. Produced by [[RedOne]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=RedOne prêt à retravailler avec Mylène Farmer : "Elle a de bonnes vibrations"|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-101848.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> [[Moby]]<ref name=":14">{{Cite web|last=Farmer|first=Mylène|title=Bleu noir / Mylène Farmer|date=2010|url=http://lfa.essonne.fr/Default/doc/SYRACUSE/940953/bleu-noir-mylene-farmer|publisher=Stuffed Monkey|access-date=2021-04-17}}</ref> and British alternative band [[Archive (band)|Archive]],<ref name=":14" /> it entered the French album chart at number one and remained at the top for three consecutive weeks. It was the 9th best-selling album in France in 2010, with more than 300,000 copies sold in only three weeks.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Bleu Noir|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/Bleu-Noir-ac19230.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> "[[Oui mais... non]]", the album's lead single was released in October 2010 and went on to become the singer's most successful single since 2002.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Oui Mais... Non|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/Oui-Mais-Non-a109650135.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> In promotion of the album, the singles "[[Bleu Noir (song)|Bleu Noir]]" and "[[Lonely Lisa]]" were released.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Écoutez le nouveau single de Mylène Farmer : "Bleu noir"|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-71904.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer est "Lonely Lisa"|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-73418.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref>

Farmer's second greatest hits collection, titled ''[[2001.2011]],'' was released in late 2011, featuring all singles recorded after 2001's ''[[Les Mots (album)|Les Mots]]'', and a new single titled "[[Du temps]]''".''<ref>{{Cite web|title=2001 - 2011 by Mylène Farmer|date=5 December 2011|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/2001-2011/1538614708|language=en-gb|access-date=2021-04-17}}</ref>

In early 2012, Farmer said that she was recording a new album which was to be released late 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|last=magazine|first=Le Point|date=2012-09-27|title=Mylène Farmer : nouvel album le 3 décembre et tournée à l'automne 2013|url=https://www.lepoint.fr/people/mylene-farmer-nouvel-album-le-3-decembre-et-tournee-a-l-automne-2013-27-09-2012-1510762_2116.php|access-date=2021-04-17|website=Le Point|language=fr}}</ref> The lead single, "[[À l'ombre]]" had its radio premiere in October 2012, and the album, ''[[Monkey Me]]'' entered the French charts at number one in December 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Monkey Me|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/Monkey-Me-a115953597.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.chartsinfrance.net}}</ref>[[File:Mylène Farmer - Timeless 2013 - 17.09.2013 - 05.jpg|thumb|Farmer performing with [[Gary Jules]] in 2013|216x216px]]In support of ''Bleu noir'' and ''Monkey Me'', Farmer embarked on the ''[[Timeless (Mylène Farmer)|Timeless 2013]]'' concert tour, starting in September 2013.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer 'Timeless 2013' : nouvelles dates en réservation|url=https://www.infoconcert.com/news/mylene-farmer-timeless-2013--nouvelles-dates-en-reservation-7024.html|access-date=2021-04-17|website=www.infoconcert.com|language=fr}}</ref> More than 500,000 people in total attended the tour, which visited arenas in France, Belgium, Switzerland, Belarus and Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/PascalNegre/status/411848127021613056 |title=Twitter / PascalNegre: Mylène Farmer "Diabolique |publisher=Twitter |date=14 December 2013 |access-date=14 March 2014}}{{Primary source inline|date=October 2019}}</ref> The live album of the tour was released in December, with an eponymous film shot in widescreen during the shows in Lyon.<ref>{{cite web|date=4 December 2013|title=Mylène Farmer – Trailer Timeless 2013|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kt6qlNQtBxc|access-date=14 March 2014|via=YouTube}}</ref>

=== 2015–present: ''Interstellaires'', ''Désobéissance'' and ''Histoires de'' ===
In 2015, Farmer collaborated with French photographer Sylvie Lancrenon on a [[coffee table book]] entitled ''Fragile''.<ref>{{Cite book|title = Fragile|last = Lancrenon|first = Sylvie|publisher = Anne Carrièr|date = 15 May 2015|isbn = 978-2-8433-7784-6|location = France|url = http://www.anne-carriere.fr/ouvrage_fragile-sylvie-lancrenon--amp;-mylene-farmer-252.html}}</ref> Featuring 90 photos of Farmer taken by Lancrenon, the imagery consists of intimate photographs of the singer covered in talc and clay, focusing on the juxtaposition of fragility and powerfulness.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-06-08|title=Paris : Mylène Farmer by Sylvie Lancrenon at acte2gallery|url=https://loeildelaphotographie.com/fr/paris-mylene-farmer-par-sylvie-lancrenon-a-acte2gallerie/|access-date=2021-04-16|website=The Eye of Photography Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

In August 2015, a new site was launched, announcing new material from Farmer. The first single to be released was "[[Stolen Car (Take Me Dancing)#Mylène Farmer and Sting version|Stolen Car]]",<ref>{{Cite web|title = Stolen Car – Single par Mylène Farmer & Sting|url = https://itunes.apple.com/fr/album/stolen-car-single/id1034196483|publisher = iTunes Store|access-date = 28 August 2015}}</ref> a duet with [[Sting (musician)|Sting]], who originally recorded the song for his 2003 album ''[[Sacred Love]]''. The single reached number one on the French and Belgian (Wallonia) singles chart<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-98739.html|title=Mylène Farmer et Sting au top : "Stolen Car" numéro 1 des ventes de singles|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> and also marks Farmer's debut on top of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'''s [[Dance Club Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.voici.fr/news-people/actu-people/mylene-farmer-cartonne-aux-usa-son-duo-avec-sting-est-n-1-au-classement-billboard-583337|title=Mylène Farmer cartonne aux USA : son duo avec Sting est N°1 au classement Billboard – Voici|first=Prisma|last=Média}}</ref> Its parent record, ''[[Interstellaires]]'', was released on 6 November 2015, becoming Farmer's 12th number one album in France.<ref name="Mylène Farmer - Interstellaires"/> Farmer collaborated with American producer [[Martin Kierszenbaum]] and French DJ [[The Avener]] on the album, which also features a cover of "[[I Want You to Want Me]]",  a song by American rock band [[Cheap Trick]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Interstellaires by Mylène Farmer|date=6 November 2015|url=https://music.apple.com/nz/album/interstellaires/1538615124|language=en-au|access-date=2021-04-16}}</ref> To promote the album and ''Stolen Car'' in the U.S., the singer was interviewed on ''[[Late Night with Jimmy Fallon]]'' accompanied by Sting, and the two also performed a live rendition of the song.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sting & Mylène Farmer on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon|url=https://www.nbc.com/the-tonight-show/guests/sting-myl-ne-farmer/48c1ae8560ec38a398ff57395c108c7b4472ed11|access-date=2021-04-16|website=NBC|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2017, it was announced that Farmer had signed a new record deal with [[Sony Music Entertainment France]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lefigaro.fr/musique/2017/03/17/03006-20170317ARTFIG00312-mylene-farmer-rejoint-sony-le-debut-d-une-hemorragie-pour-universal.php|title=Mylène Farmer rejoint Sony : le début d'une hémorragie pour Universal?|date=17 March 2017}}</ref> In January 2018, the single "Rolling Stone" was unveiled, making it her first release under her new label.<ref>{{Cite web|title="Rolling Stone" : Mylène Farmer de nouveau numéro un des ventes avec son single|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-106752.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> It was followed up by a duet with American singer-songwriter [[LP (singer)|LP]], entitled "N’oublie pas".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Rolling Stone (single)|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/Rolling-Stone-s45257.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> The song was co-written by LP, and the video, set in Iceland, marked the artistic return of Farmer's long-time collaborator, [[Laurent Boutonnat]]. Farmer's eleventh studio album, ''[[Désobéissance]]'', was released in September 2018. The album was commercially and critically successful, topping the French album chart in its first week,<ref>{{Cite web|date=5 October 2018|title=Le Top de la semaine : Top Albums Téléchargés du 5 octobre 2018|url=http://snepmusique.com/les-tops/le-top-de-la-semaine/top-albums/?semaine=40&annee=2018|website=snepmusique.com}}</ref> and being described as Farmer's best album in 15 years.<ref name="chartsinfrance.net"/><ref>{{Cite web|last=Béglé|first=Jérôme|date=2018-09-29|title=Mylène Farmer désobéit (encore)|url=https://www.lepoint.fr/musique/mylene-farmer-desobeit-encore-29-09-2018-2258901_38.php|access-date=2021-04-16|website=Le Point|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=à 19h43|first=Par Eric BureauLe 27 septembre 2018|date=2018-09-27|title=On aime le nouveau Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.leparisien.fr/culture-loisirs/musique/on-aime-le-nouveau-mylene-farmer-26-09-2018-7903809.php|access-date=2021-04-16|website=leparisien.fr|language=fr-FR}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-09-28|title=Mylène Farmer surprend tout le monde avec son nouvel album "Désobéissance"|url=https://www.dhnet.be/medias/musique/mylene-farmer-surprend-tout-le-monde-avec-son-nouvel-album-desobeissance-5bae3a7acd70d3638d952306|access-date=2021-04-16|website=DH Les Sports +|language=fr}}</ref>

To promote the albums ''Interstellaires'' and ''Désobéissance'', Farmer began a nine-date concert residency titled ''[[Mylène Farmer 2019]]'' at the [[Paris La Défense Arena]] in June 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lefigaro.fr/musique/2018/10/01/03006-20181001ARTFIG00067-mylene-farmer-sur-scene-pour-six-dates-en-juin-2019-a-paris-la-defense-arena.php|title=Mylène Farmer sur scène pour six dates en juin 2019 à Paris La Défense Arena|website=LEFIGARO|date=October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-109411.html|title=Mylène Farmer ajoute un concert à Paris La Défense Arena le 22 juin 2019, c'est officiel|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> A live album titled ''[[Live 2019]]'' was released in October that year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Mylene-Farmer/news-114413.html|title=Mylène Farmer : 100.000 ventes pour l'album "Live 2019"|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref>

In 2020, Farmer and Boutonnat started working with upcoming French singer [[Julia (French singer)|Julia]]. Her album ''Passe... comme tu sais'' was released on 19 June 2020, featuring songs penned by Farmer and produced by Boutonnat.<ref>https://www.ouest-france.fr/culture/musiques/julia-l-heritage-rebelle-de-mylene-farmer-6914730</ref><ref>https://www.aficia.info/musique/actualite-musique/julia-5/196641</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Julia/news-114194.html|title=Julia souhaite un deuxième album avec Mylène Farmer|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref>

2020 also saw the premiere of ''Mylène Farmer - L'Ultime Création'', a behind-the-scenes documentary about her [[Mylène Farmer 2019|2019 residency concerts]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vogue.fr/fashion-culture/article/mylene-farmer-documentary-series-amazon-prime-video|title=Mylène Farmer stars in a documentary series on Amazon Prime Video|website=Vogue Paris|date=21 September 2020}}</ref> In order to promote the documentary, and a subsequent greatest hits album entitled ''[[Histoires de]]'', the new single "L'âme dans l'eau" was announced, along with a music video consisting of footage from the upcoming documentary. A collection of live and studio recordings between 1984 and 2020, ''Histoires de'' debuted at number two on the French charts on 4 December 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=MYLENE FARMER - HISTOIRES DE (ALBUM)|url=http://www.lescharts.com/showitem.asp?interpret=Myl%E8ne+Farmer&titel=Histoires+de&cat=a|url-status=live|access-date=17 April 2021|website=Lescharts.com}}</ref>

In 2021, Mylène Farmer became a member of the jury at [[2021 Cannes Film Festival|Cannes Film Festival]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/sheenascott/2021/06/24/cannes-film-festival-2021-announces-its-jury/?sh=63bb7d2ce6b4 |title= Cannes Film Festival 2021 Announces Its Jury |last=Scott |first=Sheena |date=24 June 2021 |website=[[Forbes]] |access-date=8 July 2021 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.programme-tv.net/news/evenement/festival-de-cannes-Cannes%202021/279486-spike-lee-jury-du-festival-de-cannes-2021-avec-mylene-farmer-je-nen-avais-jamais-entendu-parler/ |title=Spike Lee, jury du Festival de Cannes 2021 avec Mylène Farmer : "Je n'avais jamais entendu parler d'elle" |last=Gonçalves |first=Virginie |date=7 July 2021 |website=Télé-Loisirs |access-date=8 July 2021}}</ref>

== Artistry ==
Farmer's influences include musicians, actors and literary figues. The singer adopted her stage name as an homage to Hollywood actress [[Frances Farmer]], whose character always fascinated her.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylene Farmer - europopmusic|url=https://www.europopmusic.eu/France_pages/Farmer.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.europopmusic.eu}}</ref> Other cinematic figures Farmer says she is inspired by include [[Greta Garbo]],<ref>{{Cite news|date=13 December 1985|title=Mylène Farmer - Interview|work=Pollen - France Inter|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/mylene-farmer-interview-pollen-france-inter-13-decembre-1985.php}}</ref> [[Michelle Pfeiffer]],<ref>{{Cite news|date=14 December 1995|title=Mylène Farmer - Interview|work=Studio Gabriel - France 2|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/mylene-farmer_interview_studio-gabriel_france-2_14-decembre-1995}}</ref> [[Steven Spielberg]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=AlloCine|title=Mylène Farmer : réservez vos places pour le film de son dernier spectacle|url=https://www.allocine.fr/article/fichearticle_gen_carticle=18682327.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=AlloCiné|language=fr}}</ref> and [[Andrei Tarkovsky]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=7 April 1991|title=Mylène Farmer - Interview|work=Pour un clip avec toi - M6|access-date=}}</ref> In addition, she has named [[Depeche Mode]], [[Kate Bush]] and [[Jacques Brel]] as musical influences.<ref>{{Cite news|date=8 June 1987|title=Mylène Farmer - Interview|work=Top 50}}</ref> In turn, Farmer's work has influenced a number of French recording artists, including [[Christine and the Queens]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Chris(tine) évoque son admiration pour Mylène Farmer, "un personnage fascinant"|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Christine-and-the-Queens/news-107796.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref> [[Damso]]<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-12-09|title=Damso: le protégé de Booba "rêve d'un feat avec Mylène Farmer" (part 2)|url=https://mcetv.fr/mon-mag-culture/mon-mag-musique/damso-protege-de-booba-reve-dun-feat-mylene-farmer-interview-part-2-0912/|access-date=2021-04-16|website=MCE TV|language=fr-FR}}</ref> and [[Gims]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Maître Gims lance un appel à Mylène Farmer pour enregistrer un duo (VIDÉO)|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Maitre-Gims/news-106479.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=chartsinfrance.net}}</ref>

=== Lyrical influences ===
Farmer is known for her poetic songwriting, which often features philosophical ideas, symbolism, transgression, religious imagery, alliterations, [[Double entendre|double entendres]] and neologisms.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Caroline Bee, Antoine Bioy and Benjamin Thiry|title=Mylène Farmer, La part d'ombre|publisher=L'Archipel|year=2003}}</ref><ref name=":5">{{Cite book|last=Benoît Cachin|title=Mylène Farmer, Au fil des mots|publisher=Gründ|year=2016}}</ref> Common topics of her lyrics include sex, death, religion and love.<ref name=":5" /> Farmer's literary influences include [[Charles Baudelaire]], [[Edgar Allan Poe]], [[Pierre Reverdy]], [[Stefan Zweig]] and [[Søren Kierkegaard]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Influences - Textes|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/mylene-farmer-influences-analyses.php|url-status=live|access-date=17 April 2021|website=Mylene.net}}</ref>

=== Musical style ===
Musically, Farmer is usually described as a [[Pop music|pop]] and [[French pop]] artist;<ref name=":21">{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/myl%C3%A8ne-farmer-mn0000936800/biography|access-date=2021-04-16|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> nevertheless, she is known for reinventing her style with every album cycle.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylene Farmer / YMMV|url=https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/YMMV/MyleneFarmer|access-date=2021-04-16|website=TV Tropes}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-21|title=Mylène Farmer Returns With "L'âme Dans L'eau"|url=https://www.idolator.com/7914670/mylene-farmer-returns-with-lame-dans-leau?view-all|access-date=2021-04-16|website=idolator}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=adzmkva1999|date=2020-05-04|title=Mylène Farmer: A French Pop Legend|url=http://www.reddit.com/r/popheads/comments/gd4dxy/mylène_farmer_a_french_pop_legend/|access-date=2021-04-16|website=r/popheads}}</ref> All her albums between 1984 and 2008 were produced by her collaborator, [[Laurent Boutonnat]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Laurent Boutonnat : le complice de Mylène Farmer en couple et père de famille - Gala|url=https://www.gala.fr/l_actu/news_de_stars/laurent-boutonnat-le-complice-de-mylene-farmer-en-couple-et-pere-de-famille_447535|access-date=2021-04-16|website=Gala.fr|language=fr}}</ref> Her first albums, ''[[Cendres de lune]]'', ''[[Ainsi soit je...]]'' and ''[[L'Autre...]]'' feature dark [[synth-pop]] and [[New wave music|new wave]] instrumentation. 1995 saw the release of ''[[Anamorphosée]]'', which features more simplistic lyrics and pop rock-influenced music.<ref>{{Cite web|title=MYLENE FARMER - ANAMORPHOSEE CD / chanson, pop rock|url=https://www.osta.ee/mylene-farmer-anamorphosee-cd-chanson-pop-rock-141399239.html|access-date=2021-04-16|website=Osta.ee|language=en}}</ref> ''[[Innamoramento]]'' and ''[[Avant que l'ombre...]]'' are often described as merging acoustic instruments with electronic beats.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylene Farmer – Innamoramento (1999)|url=http://theblues-thatjazz.com/en/latin-french-italian/2100-mylene-farmer/19315-mylene-farmer--innamoramento-1999.pdf|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-16|website=theblues-thatjazz.com}}</ref> Farmer then experimented with electronica and club music on 2008's ''[[Point de suture]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Point de Suture - Mylène Farmer {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits {{!}} AllMusic|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/point-de-suture-mw0001243239|language=en|access-date=2021-04-16}}</ref> In 2010, Farmer released her first album without Laurent Boutonnat, ''[[Bleu noir]]'', featuring songs produced by [[RedOne]], [[Moby]] and British alternative band [[Archive (band)|Archive]].<ref name=":4" /> The album saw Farmer return to the darker synth-pop and [[Europop]] production of her debut album.<ref name=":4" /> For 2012's ''[[Monkey Me]]'', Farmer collaborated with Boutonnat in the studio for the last time. ''[[Interstellaires]]'', the singer's tenth studio album shows influences of [[pop rock]] and [[adult contemporary]] styles, produced by French DJ [[The Avener]] and [[Martin Kierszenbaum]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylene Farmer – Interstellaires (2015, Vinyl)|url=https://www.discogs.com/Mylene-Farmer-Interstellaires/release/7690919|language=en|access-date=2021-04-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer - Interstellaires|url=https://forum.popjustice.com/threads/mylene-farmer-interstellaires.57429/|access-date=2021-04-16|website=The Popjustice Forum|language=en-US}}</ref> On ''[[Désobéissance]]'', Farmer collaborated with French DJ [[Feder (DJ)|Feder]], American singer-songwriter [[LP (singer)|LP]] and American producer [[Mike Del Rio]], marking a return to [[electronic dance music]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Lonereviewer|date=2018-10-14|title=Mylene Farmer : Désobéissance Review{{!}} Lone Reviewer|url=https://lone-reviewer.com/mylene-farmer-desobeissance/|access-date=2021-04-16|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Public image ==
In 1988, she refused to appear at the ''Victoire de la musique'' awards ceremony due to the behind the scenes hypocrisy{{clarification needed|date=October 2021}} she experienced while rehearsing for the show.<ref>{{Cite news|date=12 October 1989|title=Télé Moustique interview|work=Télé Moustique}}</ref>

In November 1991, Laurent Berger, a disturbed man who had been stalking Farmer, entered the Polydor Records headquarters in Paris and held employees at gunpoint demanding to talk to her. After not being granted the singer's address, the man killed the receptionist with a gunshot.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer : Ses fans en sont fous ! - France Dimanche|url=https://www.francedimanche.fr/actualites/mylene-farmer-ses-fans-en-sont-fous|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.francedimanche.fr}}</ref> It is said that the incident contributed to Farmer's absence in the media.<ref>{{Cite web|title=En mars 2010, les quelques artistes français connaissant un grand succès en Russie, dont Mylène Farmer, furent invités à l'Élysée pour une soirée en l'honneur du président russe|url=http://www.liberation.fr/culture/0101622743-mylene-farmer-passe-sur-la-pointe-des-pieds-a-l-elysee|url-status=live|archive-url=http://archive.wikiwix.com/cache/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.liberation.fr%2Fculture%2F0101622743-mylene-farmer-passe-sur-la-pointe-des-pieds-a-l-elysee|archive-date=5 March 2010|website=Libération}}</ref>

Farmer is regarded as a [[gay icon]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=2007-02-17|title=Ces chanteuses vénérées par les gays|language=fr|work=Le Monde.fr|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/societe/article/2007/02/17/ces-chanteuses-venerees-par-les-gays_868516_3224.html|access-date=2021-04-16}}</ref><ref name=":6">{{Cite web|title=POURQUOI ET COMMENT MYLENE FARMER EST DEVENUE UNE ICÔNE GAY?|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/mylene-farmer-icone-gay.php|url-status=live|website=Mylene.net}}</ref> Her status has been attributed to her lyrics, which often feature references to homosexual love and sexuality, and her [[tomboy]] image early in her career.<ref name=":6" /> She publicly accepted her position as a gay icon in 2008 by saying "We've been following each other for many years, that's important to me. I also think that I share with the gay public, as with other audiences, the feeling of being "different", a feeling that causes difficulty to live in this world."<ref>{{Cite journal|title=Mylène Farmer "les gays & moi"|journal=Têtu|volume=136 September 2008}}</ref>

In January 2015, following the [[January 2015 Île-de-France attacks|Île-de-France attacks]], Farmer declared her support for the [[Je suis Charlie]] movement.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Béglé|first=Jérôme|date=2015-01-10|title=Mylène Farmer aussi est Charlie !|url=https://www.lepoint.fr/culture/mylene-farmer-aussi-est-charlie-10-01-2015-1895469_3.php|access-date=2021-04-16|website=Le Point|language=fr}}</ref> In March 2020, Farmer's management shared her drawing in support of doctors and nurses working in French hospitals amidst the [[COVID-19 pandemic]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=#MyleneFarmer #virusatort|url=https://twitter.com/hashtagnp/status/1241303612707536897?ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.ladepeche.fr%2F2020%2F04%2F21%2Fcoronavirus-mylene-farmer-sengage-au-profit-du-personnel-des-ehpad8856498.php|url-status=live|access-date=2021-04-16|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref> and another unpublished drawing of hers was later auctioned to support the purchase of protective equipment for caregivers.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Coronavirus : Mylène Farmer s'engage au profit du personnel des Ehpad|url=https://www.ladepeche.fr/2020/04/21/coronavirus-mylene-farmer-sengage-au-profit-du-personnel-des-ehpad,8856498.php|access-date=2021-04-16|website=ladepeche.fr|language=fr}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Farmer met American actor, singer and guitarist [[Jeff Dahlgren]] on the set of their 1994 movie ''[[Giorgino]].''<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|last=HOFER|first=Julien AUTIER, Philippe LEZE, Guillaume DATEZ & Sarah|title=Mylene.Net - Le site référence sur Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.mylene.net/mylene/jeff-dahlgren.php|access-date=2021-04-16|website=mylene.net}}</ref> Following the commercial failure of the film, the couple relocated to [[Los Angeles]].<ref name=":2" /> Dahlgren played the guitar on the singer's 1990s albums ''[[Anamorphosée]]'' and ''[[Innamoramento]]'', and accompanied her on stage during her [[Mylenium Tour (tour)|''Mylenium Tour'']].<ref>{{Cite web|title=HOME|url=https://www.jeffdahlgren.net/|access-date=2021-04-16|website=JD|language=en}}</ref> The couple ended their relationship in 1999.<ref name=":3"/>

Since 2002, Farmer has been in a relationship with French director and producer [[Benoît di Sabatino]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|title=Les amis et amours de Mylène Farmer|url=https://www.innamoramento.net/mylene-farmer/page/amis-amours|access-date=2021-04-16|website=Innamoramento.net|language=fr}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Mylène Farmer amoureuse : qui est son discret compagnon Benoît Di Sabatino ?|url=https://www.purepeople.com/article/mylene-farmer-amoureuse-qui-est-son-discret-compagnon-benoit-di-sabatino_a412676/1|access-date=2021-04-16|website=www.purepeople.com|language=fr}}</ref> The couple met while creating the animated music video for Farmer's single "[[C'est une belle journée]]".<ref name=":3" /> Di Sabatino also directed the music video for "[[L'amour n'est rien...]]" in 2006 and "[[Appelle mon numéro]]" in 2008.<ref name=":3" />
