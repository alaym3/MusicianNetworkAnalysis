'''Pritam Chakraborty''' (born 14 June 1971), also known mononymously as '''Pritam''', is an Indian composer, instrumentalist, guitarist and singer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/pritam-and-the-politics-of-bollywood-music/|title=Pritam and the Politics of Bollywood Music -|date=10 March 2017|access-date=19 November 2019}}</ref>

==Early life==
Pritam was born in a [[Bengalis|Bengali]] Brahmin  family to Anuradha Chakraborty and Prabodh Chakraborty. He gained his early training in music from his father and learned to play the guitar while he was still in school.{{citation needed|date=May 2020}}

He went to St. James' School and has a bachelor's degree in Geology from [[Presidency College, Calcutta|Presidency College]]. Pritam joined [[Film and Television Institute of India|FTII]], [[Pune]] where he took up Sound Recording and Engineering in 1994.<ref>{{cite news|title=The new melody moguls|url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1051210/asp/weekend/story_5563284.asp|access-date=7 March 2016|work=The Telegraph|date=10 December 2005}}</ref> During this time, he was offered a chance to compose background music for a film by the Hungarian filmmaker [[Istvan Gaal]].

Pritam formed a band with his Presidency batchmates, called "Jotugriher Pakhi" for which, he used to play the guitar.<ref>{{cite news|title=The other Bong connection|url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1060320/asp/calcutta/story_5989775.asp|access-date=7 March 2016|work=The Telegraph|date=20 March 2006}}</ref> They even have a cassette released to their credit at that time. Pritam joined a Bangla band, [[Chandrabindoo (band)|Chandrabindoo]]. Later Pritam started band Metro with James, Suhail, Soham and Eric during his film called [[Life in a... Metro]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Music of Metro|url=https://www.livemint.com/Consumer/qWKCGaY7Lai3WyLGuNFqOK/Music-of-Life-in-a-Metro-launched.html|access-date=13 December 2019|publisher=LiveMint|date=29 March 2007}}</ref>

==Career==
===Pre-Bollywood===
After completing his sound engineering course from [[Film and Television Institute of India|FTII Pune]], Pritam Acharjee came to [[Mumbai]] in 1997. He started composing ad jingles where he met other budding artists such as [[Shantanu Moitra]], [[Rajkumar Hirani]], [[Sanjay Gadhvi]] and [[Jeet Gannguli]]. Pritam composed renowned jingles for brands like [[Hyundai Santro|Santro]],<ref>{{cite news|title=The Music of Dhoom Had To Be Zip-Zap-Zoom!|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/report/pritam/20040831.htm|access-date=7 March 2016|work=Rediff.com|date=31 August 2004}}</ref> [[Emami]], [[McDonald's]],<ref>{{cite news|title=Soham Chakraborty on in Dino|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2007/jul/09soham.htm|access-date=7 March 2016|work=Rediff.com|date=9 July 2007}}</ref> [[Head & Shoulders]], [[Thums Up]], [[Limca]], [[Complan]] and composed title tracks for TV serials such as ''[[Astitva...Ek Prem Kahani|Astitva]]'', ''[[Kkavyanjali]]'', ''[[Ye Meri Life Hai]]'', ''[[Remix (TV series)|Remix]]'', ''[[Kashmeer (TV series)|Kashmeer]]'', ''[[Miilee]]'', and ''Dil Kya Kare''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pritam Chakraborty|url=http://www.cinemagigs.com/2013/09/pritam-chakraborty-movie-list-biography.html|publisher=Cinemagig|access-date=13 February 2016}}</ref>

===Early Bollywood career (with Jeet Gannguli)===
Pritam got his first break for Tere Liye (released December 2001).<ref>{{cite news|title=Neighbours, now melody-makers – Jeet-Pritam: Two boys from Bengali strike a crescendo in Bollywood|url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1020927/asp/calcutta/story_1237428.asp|access-date=7 March 2016|work=The Telegraph|date=27 September 2002}}</ref> Though the music was well-received, the film missed the mark. In 2002, The Jeet-Pritam duo once again composed music for [[Yash Raj Films]]'  ''[[Mere Yaar Ki Shaadi Hai]].'' All the songs from that movie were chartbusters. 
Soon after, over some misunderstanding, Jeet decided to split and the partners went their separate ways.

===Solo career===
Pritam had built a name for his compositions by finely blending Indian classical music with western influences. His compositions for Gadhvi's second movie ''[[Dhoom]]'' were a runaway hit.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Sinha|first1=Lata|title=The Telegraph|url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1041231/asp/etc/story_4196246.asp|access-date=6 October 2016|location=Kolkota|date=31 December 2004}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Sharma|first1=Devesh|title=Dhoom machale!|url=http://www.filmfare.com/features/dhoom-machale-15282.html|access-date=6 October 2016}}</ref> The [[Dhoom Dhoom|title-track]] of ''Dhoom'' in two versions – Hindi (by [[Sunidhi Chauhan]]) and English (by [[Tata Young]]) – broke geographic barriers becoming popular in pubs in the UK, USA and the East Asia and won him a [[Zee Cine Award for Best Track of the Year]]. He received two nominations for ''Dhoom''.

He followed this up with compositions for films like ''[[Ajab Prem Ki Ghazab Kahani]]'', ''[[Race (2008 film)|Race]]'', ''[[Kismat Konnection]]'', ''[[Jannat (2008 film)|Jannat]]'', ''[[Gangster (2006 film)|Gangster]]''<ref>{{cite web|title=Interesting facts about music of Gangster|url=http://www.indiaglitz.com/interesting-facts-about-music-of-gangster-hindi-news-21148.html|website=Indiaglitz|access-date=6 October 2016|date=20 March 2006}}</ref> and ''[[Dhoom 2]]''. His compositions for [[Anurag Basu]]'s ''[[Life in a... Metro]]'' garnered him very positive reviews.<ref>{{cite news|title=Life in a... Metro |url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/movie-reviews/Life-in-a-Metro/articleshow/2036256.cms|access-date=6 October 2016|agency=TNN|work=The Times of India|date=12 March 2007}}</ref> He formed a band called [[Life in a... Metro#Metro Band|Metro]] with Suhail Kaul, Sohu pet name Chakraborty, Eric Pillai and Bangladeshi singer [[James (musician)|James]] for the movie.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Gopal|first1=Sangita|title=Conjugations: Marriage and Form in New Bollywood Cinema|date=2012|publisher=University of Chicago Press|location=Chicago, IL|isbn=9780226304274|page=153|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Music of 'Life in a Metro' launched|url=http://www.livemint.com/Consumer/qWKCGaY7Lai3WyLGuNFqOK/Music-of-Life-in-a-Metro-launched.html|website=livemint.com|access-date=6 October 2016|date=29 March 2007}}</ref>

He won his second [[Zee Cine Award for Best Track of the Year]] for the song "Mauja hi Mauja" from ''[[Jab We Met]]'' by [[Imtiaz Ali (director)|Imtiaz Ali]]. ''Jab We Met'' won him his first [[Apsara Award for Best Music Director]]. He composed for two more Imtiaz Ali films, ''[[Love Aaj Kal]]'' and ''Cocktail'' which were critically and commercially successful.

His [[Sufi music|Sufi]] songs for the 2010 movie ''[[Once Upon A Time in Mumbaai]]'' were immensely popular with the masses<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sen|first1=Raja|title=The best Bollywood soundtracks of 2010|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/slide-show/slide-show-1-best-bollywood-soundtracks-of-2010/20101220.htm|date=20 December 2010|access-date=6 October 2016}}</ref> and songs like "Pee loon" and "Tum Jo Aaye" were declared chartbusters.<ref>{{cite news|title=Top 10 Bollywood songs of 2010 – The Express Tribune|url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/93673/top-10-bollywood-songs-of-2010/|access-date=6 October 2016|agency=Indo-Asian News Service|date=23 December 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Pahwa|first1=Kiran|title='Pee loon' stands out in 'Once Upon A Time...' soundtrack|url=http://topnews.in/light/pee-loon-stands-out-once-upon-time-soundtrack-225484|access-date=6 October 2016|date=2 July 2010}}</ref> He teamed up again with [[Mahesh Bhatt]] and [[Vishesh Films]] for music of ''[[Crook (film)|Crook]]''<ref>{{cite web|last1=Tuteja|first1=Joginder|title=Emraan-Pritam 'jodi' strikes again with CROOK - bollywood news : glamsham.com|url=http://www.glamsham.com/movies/news/10/sep/28-emraan-pritam-jodi-strikes-again-with-crook-091011.asp|website=glamsham.com|access-date=6 October 2016}}</ref> of which the song "Mere Bina" got instant recognition with public. He recreated the famous 1971 song, "[[Dum Maro Dum (song)|Dum Maro Dum]]" for [[Ramesh Sippy]]'s 2011 movie of the same name.<ref>{{cite news|title=Rohan Sippy bags remake rights of '70s hit song 'Dum Maro Dum'|url=http://archive.indianexpress.com/news/rohan-sippy-bags-remake-rights-of-70s-hit-song-dum-maro-dum/718689/|access-date=6 October 2016|work=The Indian Express|date=1 December 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Sinha|first1=Meenakshi|title=Action replay: Bollywood grooves to retro classics|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/Action-replay-Bollywood-grooves-to-retro-classics/articleshow/8139073.cms|access-date=6 October 2016|agency=TNN|work=The Times of India|date=2 May 2011}}</ref> He then composed for the romance [[Mausam (2011 film)|''Mausam'']] which had a strong influence of [[Punjabi language|Punjabi]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Abid|title=Mausam music review|url=http://www.glamsham.com/music/reviews/17-mausam-music-review-081110.asp|website=glamsham.com}}</ref> The songs were phenomenal hits in Northern India with "Rabba Main To Mar Gaya Oye" being a rage among people.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Yount|first1=Stacey|title=Top 50 Songs of 2011|url=http://bollyspice.com/top-50-songs-of-2011/|access-date=6 October 2016|date=11 January 2012}}</ref>

In 2012, he composed several successful songs for films like [[Players (2012 film)|''Players'']], [[Agent Vinod (2012 film)|''Agent Vinod'']], ''[[Jannat 2]]'' and [[Vidhu Vinod Chopra]]’s ''[[Ferrari Ki Sawaari]]'', where he finally got to work with him and [[Rajkumar Hirani]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Vijayakar|first1=R.M.|title=Composer Pritam's Musical Experiments Are Paying Off|url=http://119.82.71.89/indiawest/news/970-composer-pritam-s-musical-experiments-are-paying-off.html|access-date=7 March 2016|publisher=Indiawest.com|date=30 August 2011}}</ref> Later that year, he scored music for ''[[Barfi!]]'' which won him two [[Filmfare Awards]].<ref name="auto">{{cite web|title=Live Blog: 58th Idea Filmfare Awards 2013|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/58th-idea-filmfare-awards-2013/liveblog/18102956.cms|work=[[The Times of India]]|date=21 February 2013|access-date=16 February 2016}}</ref> ''Barfi'' is considered to be one of the most critically acclaimed soundtrack album of his career. ==Musical style==
Pritam has composed and covered some popular songs in a variety of genres including Rock (''[[Life in a... Metro]]''), [[Sufi music|Sufi]] (''[[Once Upon A Time in Mumbaai]]'') and even [[Ghazals]] (''[[Barfi!|Barfi]]''). His musical style is mainly characterized as a delicate fusion of [[Indian classical music]] with contemporary undertones.<ref>{{cite web|title=Pritam Chakraborty Biography – Saavn|url=http://www.saavn.com/s/artist/pritam-chakraborty-bio/OaFg9HPZgq8_|publisher=Saavn|access-date=13 February 2016}}</ref>

== Allegations of plagiarism ==
Pritam has been alleged to have plagiarized numerous songs from the beginning of his film music career. Hits such as "Pehli Nazar Main" was plagiarized from the Korean song "Sarang Hae Yo" by Kim Hyung Su (2005)'''.'''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.scoopwhoop.com/bollywood-songs-copied-from-other-songs/|title=24 Popular Bollywood Songs You Never Knew Were Copied From Other Songs|last=Singh|first=Sandeep|date=7 July 2016|website=ScoopWhoop|access-date=11 August 2019}}</ref> His critically acclaimed soundtrack ''[[Ae Dil Hai Mushkil]]'' also faced allegations of plagiarism as the guitar riff of the song "Bulleya" was lifted from the Papa Roach song [[Last Resort (song)|"Last Resort"]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.india.com/showbiz/ae-dil-hai-mushkil-song-bulleya-plagiarism-controversy-pritam-copied-adhm-sufi-song-from-papa-roach-iron-maiden-1491970/|title=Pritam copied ADHM Sufi song from Papa Roach & Iron Maiden!|last=Desk|first=India com Entertainment|date=16 September 2016|website=India.com|access-date=11 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/entertainment/hindi/bollywood/news/Pritam-accused-of-plagiarism-yet-again-with-ADHM-track-Bulleya/articleshow/54426791.cms|title=Pritam accused of plagiarism yet again with 'ADHM' track 'Bulleya'|website=The Times of India|access-date=11 August 2019}}</ref>

[[Rolling Stone]] India says, Indian music plagiarism tracking site www.itwofs.com alleges 52 instances between 2004 and 2010 where Pritam's songs are said to be "lifted", "copied", "plagiarized", or "similar" to those of tunes by other composers, who range from Arab and East Asian artists (such as Ihab Tawfik, Yuri Mrakadi and Kim Hyung-sub) to Western musicians like [[Boney M.]] and [[Damien Rice]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://rollingstoneindia.com/pritam-and-the-politics-of-bollywood-music/|title=Pritam and the Politics of Bollywood Music -|date=10 March 2017|website=My Site|access-date=11 August 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.firstpost.com/entertainment/bollywood/jagga-jasoos-pritam-embroiled-in-plagiarism-controversy-over-song-galti-se-mistake-3717311.html|title=Jagga Jasoos: Pritam embroiled in plagiarism controversy over song 'Galti Se Mistake'?- Entertainment News, Firstpost|date=20 June 2017|website=Firstpost|access-date=11 August 2019}}</ref>

"I made mistakes initially.. ", Pritam said in an interview with [[Hindustan Times]] on his plagiarism charges.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/music/i-made-mistakes-initially-pritam-on-plagiarising-charges/story-UoUN2GZXvlKEiAWdhRlBMI.html|title=I made mistakes initially: Pritam on plagiarising charges|date=20 February 2016|website=Hindustan Times|access-date=11 August 2019}}</ref>

==JAM8==

{{Advert section|date=June 2021}}
[[JAM8]] is a music incubation platform founded by music composer Pritam in 2016 for budding
musicians. Being associated with Prime Focus Ltd, a media service powerhouse and KWAN, a talent
management company, JAM8 uses high-quality technology and infrastructure to offer solutions to music
across all segments covering movies, advertisements and the OTT space, providing the best in quality music
output.

JAM8 has over a brief period given music for brands like Duracell, Maruti Suzuki, Close Up, Nerolac amongst other prominent brands. Some of their film work consists of songs like [[Zaalima]], Saason Ke, Dhingaana
from [[Raees (2017 film)|Raees]], Main Hoon Sath Tere from [[Shaadi Mein Zaroor Aana]] and Sajan Bade Senti from [[Badhaai Ho]], the whole album of [[Bhangra Paa Le]] movie.
