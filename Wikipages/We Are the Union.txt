'''We Are the Union''' is an American [[ska punk]] band from [[Ann Arbor, Michigan]] formed in 2005.

==History==

===Formation and ''Who We Are'' (2005-2008)===
Formed in August 2005 in Ann Arbor, Michigan,<ref name=audiotree/> the band formed due to them being "unhappy with the current state of ska music."<ref name=about/> Their debut album, ''Who We Are'', was released in 2007.<ref>{{cite web |title=We Are the Union "Who We Are" Vinyl Re-Issue |url=https://www.badtimerecords.com/press/2018/8/7/we-are-the-union-who-we-are-vinyl-re-issue |website=Bad Time Records |access-date=26 January 2021}}</ref>

===''Great Leaps Forward'', ''You Can't Hide The Sun'' and break-up/hiatus  (2010-2013)===
''Who We Are'' would be followed by albums ''Great Leaps Forward'' in 2010<ref>{{cite web |last1=Schultz |first1=Brian |title=We Are the Union - Great Leaps Forward |url=https://www.altpress.com/reviews/greatleapsforward/ |website=Alt Press |date=17 May 2010 |access-date=26 January 2021}}</ref> and ''You Can't Hide the Sun'' in 2012.<ref>{{cite web |title=Album Premiere: We Are the Union, "You Can't Hide the Sun" |url=https://www.altpress.com/features/album_premiere_we_are_the_union_you_cant_hide_the_sun/ |website=Alt Press |date=9 October 2012 |access-date=26 January 2021}}</ref>  In 2013, We Are the Union announced they were going on an indefinite hiatus,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mandell |first1=Xan |title=Interviews: Reed Michael Wolcott (We Are the Union) |url=https://www.punknews.org/article/56377/interviews-reed-michael-wolcott-we-are-the-union |website=Punk News |access-date=26 January 2021}}</ref> stating: "We've decided to take a hiatus from active status. […] We were a band. Maybe someday we will be again. We've had some amazing times, and we truly appreciate those of you who've contributed to our existence. We've never been a supercool hype band or uberpunx beardy band, so we thank each and every one of you who listened to our music over the years.".<ref name=punknews/>

===Reformation, ''Self Care'', ''Ordinary Life'' and recent activity (2018-present)===
Following the two-year hiatus,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Chiasson |first1=Alex |title=We Are the Union Premiere Two New Songs, Announce "Keep It Down" EP |url=https://www.bostonska.net/news/we-are-the-union-premiere-two-new-songs-announce-keep-it-down-ep/3420/ |website=Boston Ska (dot) net |date=2 May 2015 |access-date=26 January 2021}}</ref> Jeremy Hunter of the YouTube channel Ska Tune Network joined the band in 2015,<ref name=skatunenetwork>{{cite web |last1=Hudson |first1=Nick |title=Gainesville musician runs successful "Skatune Network" YouTube channel |url=https://www.alligator.org/article/2019/08/gainesville-musician-runs-successful-skatune-network-youtube-channel |website=The Independent Florida Alligator |access-date=26 January 2021}}</ref> and the band released their fourth studio album, ''Self Care'', in 2018. Following this album release, the band would go on to appear on [[Audiotree]],<ref>{{cite web |title=We Are the Union -- Audiotree |url=https://audiotree.tv/session/we-are-the-union |access-date=26 January 2021}}</ref> and release new singles in 2020.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Sacher |first1=Andrew |title=We Are the Union deliver super catchy ska-punk on new single "Your Way, Your Time" |url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/we-are-the-union-deliver-super-catchy-ska-punk-on-new-single-your-way-your-time/ |website=Brooklyn Vegan |access-date=26 January 2021}}</ref>
In April 2021, We Are The Union announced a new album, ''Ordinary Life'', released on June 4, 2021 via Bad Time Records.<ref name=ordinarylife>{{cite web |last1=Sacher |first1=Andrew |title=We Are The Union announce 'Ordinary Life' LP – BV-exclusive vinyl pre-order & new song! |url=https://www.brooklynvegan.com/we-are-the-union-announce-ordinary-life-lp-bv-exclusive-vinyl-pre-order-new-song/ |website=Brooklyn Vegan |access-date=23 April 2021}}</ref> Along with the album announcement, lead vocalist Reade Wolcott came out as a trans woman, with much of the album exploring topics surrounding her transition.<ref name=readeinterview>{{cite web |last1=Stratis |first1=Niko |title=Dysphoria Dies at MAGFest: We Are the Union's Reade Wolcott Comes Out As Trans|url=https://www.spin.com/2021/04/reade-wolcott-we-are-the-union-comes-out-as-trans/ |website=SPIN |date=23 April 2021 |access-date=23 April 2021}}</ref>

==Influences and musical style==
We Are the Union cites [[Lifetime (band)|Lifetime]], [[Kid Dynamite (band)|Kid Dynamite]], [[Slapstick (band)|Slapstick]], [[Less Than Jake]], and This Is A Standoff as influences.<ref name=about/> More recently, We Are the Union has been influenced by [[indie pop]] and [[pop music|pop]] music.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kFYJ9QIpDlQ|title=20 NEW SKA Releases in 2020|accessdate=December 30, 2020|via=[[YouTube]]}}</ref>
