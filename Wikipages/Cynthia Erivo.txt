'''Cynthia Erivo''' ({{IPAc-en|ə|ˈ|r|iː|v|oʊ}};<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-dDrowuWSI8|title=Cynthia Erivo Explores ASMR|work=[[W (magazine)|W]]|date=12 October 2018|access-date=20 April 2019}}</ref> born 8 January 1987) is an English actress, singer and songwriter. She is the recipient of [[List of awards and nominations received by Cynthia Erivo|several accolades]], including a [[Daytime Emmy Award]], a [[Grammy Award]] and a [[Tony Award]], in addition to nominations for two [[Academy Awards]], two [[Golden Globe Awards]], a [[Primetime Emmy Award]], and a [[Screen Actors Guild Award]].

Erivo began acting in a 2011 stage production of ''[[The Umbrellas of Cherbourg#Stage adaptation|The Umbrellas of Cherbourg]]''. She gained recognition for starring in the [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] revival of ''[[The Color Purple (musical)|The Color Purple]]'' from 2015 to 2017, for which she won the 2016 [[Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical]] and the [[Grammy Award for Best Musical Theater Album]]. Erivo ventured into films in 2018, playing roles in the heist film ''[[Widows (2018 film)|Widows]]'' and the thriller ''[[Bad Times at the El Royale]]''. For her portrayal of American abolitionist [[Harriet Tubman]] in the biopic ''[[Harriet (film)|Harriet]]'' (2019), Erivo received a nomination for the [[Academy Award for Best Actress]]; she also wrote and performed the song "[[Stand Up (Cynthia Erivo song)|Stand Up]]" on its soundtrack, which garnered her a nomination in the [[Academy Award for Best Original Song|Best Original Song]] category.

On television, Erivo had her first role in the British series ''[[Chewing Gum (TV series)|Chewing Gum]]'' (2015). She went on to star in the crime drama miniseries ''[[The Outsider (miniseries)|The Outsider]]'' (2020), and received a nomination for the [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Limited or Anthology Series or Movie]] for her portrayal of American singer [[Aretha Franklin]] in [[National Geographic (American TV channel)|National Geographic]]'s anthology series ''[[Genius (American TV series)|Genius: Aretha]]'' (2021).

Erivo was born to Nigerian parents in [[Stockwell]], South London.<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web|last1=Wolf|first1=Matt|title=A Star Is Born! Erivo began a music psychology degree at the [[University of East London]]; however, a year into her degree, she applied to,<ref name="Guard">{{cite web | url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/jul/27/cynthia-erivo-the-color-purple-broadway-production | title=Move over Whoopi! ==Career==
===Career beginnings and theatrical roles (2011–2017)===
Erivo first appeared in roles on British television programmes such as ''[[Chewing Gum (TV series)|Chewing Gum]]'' and ''[[The Tunnel (TV series)|The Tunnel]]''. Her first stage role was in ''[[Marine Parade (play)|Marine Parade]]'' by [[Simon Stephens]] at the [[Brighton Festival]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.thestage.co.uk/features/interviews/2015/big-interview-cynthia-erivo/ | title=Cynthia Erivo: 'You have to drag me off the stage' | work=[[The Stage]] | date=26 July 2015 | access-date=13 December 2015 | author=Hemley, Matthew}}</ref> Her first musical role was in [[John Adams (composer)|John Adams']] and [[June Jordan|June Jordan's]] ''[[I Was Looking at the Ceiling and Then I Saw the Sky]]'' at [[Theatre Royal Stratford East]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theartsdesk.com/opera/i-was-looking-ceiling-and-then-i-saw-sky-theatre-royal-stratford-east|title=I Was Looking at the Ceiling and Then I Saw The Sky, Theatre Royal Stratford East|website=The Arts Desk|date=8 July 2010|access-date=17 September 2018}}</ref>

In 2013, Erivo played the role of Celie Harris in the [[Menier Chocolate Factory]] production of ''[[The Color Purple (musical)|The Color Purple]]'',<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/jul/21/color-purple-menier-chocolate-factory | title=The Color Purple – review | work=[[The Guardian]] | date=20 July 2013 | access-date=13 December 2015 | last=McCrum|first=Robert}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://london.broadway.com/buzz/169866/complete-casting-announced-for-the-color-purple-at-londons-menier-chocolate-factory/ | title=Complete Casting Announced for The Color Purple at London's Menier Chocolate Factory | work=Theatre.com | date=3 June 2013 | access-date=13 December 2015 | last=Gilbert|first=Ryan}}</ref> a role which [[Whoopi Goldberg]] had originated onscreen. Erivo had previously portrayed Sister Mary Clarence / Deloris Van Cartier in a UK tour of the stage musical ''[[Sister Act (musical)|Sister Act]]'',<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/u.k.-tour-of-sister-act-will-be-led-by-denise-black-michael-starke-and-cynt-180299 | title=U.K. Tour of Sister Act Will Be Led by Denise Black, Michael Starke and Cynthia Erivo | work=[[Playbill]] | date=22 June 2011 | access-date=13 December 2015 | last=Shenton|first=Mark}}</ref> which Goldberg had originated in its film adaptation. She is also featured on the soundtrack to the musical drama film ''[[Beyond the Lights]]'', co-writing and performing the song "Fly Before You Fall".<ref name=autogenerated1 />

She made her [[Broadway theatre|Broadway]] debut in the 2015 Broadway revival transfer of the [[Menier Chocolate Factory]] production of [[The Color Purple (musical)|''The Color Purple'']],<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-35069932 | title=The Color Purple: Cynthia Erivo wows critics in Broadway debut | work=[[BBC News]] | date=11 December 2015 | access-date=13 December 2015}}</ref> reprising her role as Celie Harris alongside American actresses [[Jennifer Hudson]] as Shug Avery and [[Danielle Brooks]] as Sofia.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/mar/17/cynthia-erivo-to-join-jennifer-hudson-in-the-color-purple-bernard-b-jacobs-theatre | title=Cynthia Erivo to join Jennifer Hudson in 'The Color Purple'|date=17 March 2015| work=[[The Guardian]] | agency=[[Associated Press]] }}</ref> The production began performances at [[Bernard B. Proceeds from the performance went to the [[Brady Campaign|Brady Center]], a national gun violence organization.<ref>{{cite web|last=Piepenburg|first=Erik|title=Cynthia Erivo and Joshua Henry to Star in 'Last Five Years' Benefit|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/13/theater/cynthia-erivo-and-joshua-henry-to-star-in-last-five-years-benefit.html|work=[[The New York Times]]|access-date=13 August 2016|date=11 August 2016}}</ref> In February 2017, Erivo performed "[[God Only Knows]]" as a tribute to musicians who had died over the past year alongside [[John Legend]] at the [[59th Annual Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://people.com/music/grammys-2017-john-legend-cynthia-erivo-in-memorium-performance//|title=John Legend and Cynthia Erivo Honor Those We've Lost at Grammys|first=Eric|last=Brown|date=12 February 2017|work=[[People (magazine)|People]]|access-date=23 February 2017}}</ref>

Erivo is [[Roman Catholic]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Paulson|first1=Michael|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/12/23/theater/the-actress-cynthia-erivo-rises-with-the-color-purple.html|title=The Actress Cynthia Erivo Rises With 'The Color Purple'|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=22 December 2015|access-date=16 August 2020}}</ref> In 2016, she dated actor and singer [[Dean John-Wilson]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-36549967|title=Cynthia Erivo: Tony award win has not sunk in|work=[[BBC News]]|date=16 June 2016|access-date=16 August 2020}}</ref> She began dating Mario Martinez in 2017.<ref>{{cite web |first=Yohana|last=Desta |title=How Cynthia Erivo Found Her Voice |url=https://www.vanityfair.com/hollywood/2019/10/how-cynthia-erivo-found-her-voice |work=[[Vanity Fair (magazine)|Vanity Fair]] |date=15 October 2019 |access-date=16 August 2020 }}</ref>
