'''Jesús Manuel Nieves Cortez''' (born April 9, 1993), known professionally as '''Jhay Cortez''', is a Puerto Rican singer and rapper.

== Biography ==
Cortez was born in [[Río Piedras, Puerto Rico|Río Piedras]], Puerto Rico. Later, he moved to live Brooklyn [[New York City|New York]], United States. As a result, Cortez had a bilingual childhood.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Diaz|first=Camila|date=May 24, 2019|title=Jhay Cortez|url=https://historia-biografia.com/jhay-cortez/|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=Historia y biografía de|language=es}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|date=April 10, 2018|title=¿Quién es Jhay Cortez, el artista que J Balvin presentó en su Instagram?|url=https://heabbi.com/jhay-cortez-artista-nuevo-j-balvin-instagram|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=Heabbi.com|language=es}}</ref>

He began composing songs at age 15, this made producer [[Eddie Dee]] interested in him and he was known by producers Lele "El arma secreta" and [[Eliel (producer)|Eliel]], with whom he began to write songs and who connect him to work with him. duo [[Zion & Lennox]], for which he composed two songs, "Como curar" and "Detective de tu amor", in addition to producing "Soltera" under the pseudonym Jay "El Superdotado", the three songs forming part of the album ''[[Los Verdaderos]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Le llegó el turno a Jhay Cortez|url=https://www.primerahora.com/entretenimiento/musica/notas/le-llego-el-turno-a-jhay-cortez/|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=Primera Hora|date=May 14, 2018|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Los Verdaderos – Zion & Lennox {{!}} Credits {{!}} AllMusic|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/los-verdaderos-mw0002036495/credits|language=en|access-date=April 20, 2021}}</ref>

Later he composed five singles for [[Tito El Bambino]], among them: "Llama al sol" for his album ''Invencible'' published in 2011, which would be the winner of a [[Latin Grammy Award|Latin Grammy]] in the category of ''best tropical album''.<ref name=":0" />

== Musical career ==
=== 2013–2016: Beginnings and productions ===
In 2013, he began as a singer as part of a Latin pop group called Stereo 4, which was born as an idea of Joel Báez who takes on the task with Roberto Sánchez and his inseparable working duo, Jhay Nieves (vocalist of the group ), to find those who would soon be part of this musical group. Stereo 4 was the mix of four totally different voices.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title="¿Que tienen en común el Reggaeton, Un Grammy y este grupo juvenil conocido como StereO 4?"|url=https://www.rapeton.com/que-tienen-en-comun-el-reggaeton-un-grammy-y-este-grupo-juvenil-conocido-por-stereo-4/|url-status=live|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=www.rapeton.com}}</ref> The youth group was made up of four young Puerto Ricans: Jafet Cortes, Eduardo Esteras, Rubén Chinea and Jesús M. Nieves.<ref name=":1" /> Some time later, Cortez launched himself as a soloist.<ref name=":1" /> He collaborated with the group for the last time in 2016 with the song "Te quiero ver", which was a collaboration with his former group and with which he debuted as a soloist.<ref>{{Cite web|last=CMTV|title=CMTV – Biografía de Jhay Cortez|url=https://www.cmtv.com.ar/biografia/show.php?bnid=2623&banda=Jhay_Cortez|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=CMTV|language=es}}</ref>

=== 2017–Present: Universal Music Group ===
On August 22, 2017, Cortez signed a contract with the [[Universal Music Latin Entertainment]] discography under the House of Haze movement in alliance with the producer HAZE aka Fino, better known as El Haze, with which he released his first single under this label, "Donde no se vea", with the collaborations of Jory Boy and [[Pusho]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|date=December 13, 2018|title=JHAY CORTEZ FIRMA CONTRATO DISCOGRÁFICO CON UNIVERSAL MUSIC LATIN ENTERTAINMENT – JHAY CORTEZ FIRMA CONTRATO DISCOGRÁFICO CON UNIVERSAL MUSIC LATIN ENTERTAINMENT – Universal Music Latin Entertainment|url=http://www.universalmusica.com/jhay-cortez-firma-contrato-discografico-con-universal-music-latin-entertainment/|access-date=April 20, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181213020307/http://www.universalmusica.com/jhay-cortez-firma-contrato-discografico-con-universal-music-latin-entertainment/|archive-date=December 13, 2018}}</ref>

On March 16, 2018, Cortez performed at a concert that benefited victims of natural disasters with the name of SXSW as one of the artists of the Port Relief Showcase.<ref name=":0" /> Later he helped in the composition of the songs "[[Criminal (Natti Natasha and Ozuna song)|Criminal]] "by [[Natti Natasha]] with [[Ozuna (singer)|Ozuna]] and" Mi Religión "by [[Yandel]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2" /> On May 16 he released the EP ''Eyez On Me'' under the Universal Music Latin record label.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Eyez On Me Álbum – Buscar con Google|url=https://www.google.com/search?q=Eyez+On+Me+%C3%81lbum&stick=H4sIAAAAAAAAAOPgE-LVT9c3NMywSEsrL0rPVoJw00zjzczLLQ21xLOTrfRzS4szk_UTi0oyi0usEnOSSnOLF7EKulamVin45yn4piocbgQJAgD_w842TQAAAA&dpr=1.5|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=www.google.com}}</ref> At the end of 2018 he released his single "Costear" with Almighty, which led to the release of two remixes.

On February 22, 2019, he released his single "[[No Me Conoce]]" which reached the top of several Latin American music charts. It was certified triple platinum by [[Productores de Música de España|PROMUSICAE]] of Spain; the song also featured a remix with [[J Balvin]] and [[Bad Bunny]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=LOS40|date=May 21, 2019|title=¿Quién es Jhay Cortez? It was ranked 164 on the US [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] chart,<ref name=":3" /> and was certified triple platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America|RIAA]].<ref>{{Cite web|title="JHAY CORTEZ lanza su Nuevo Álbum "FAMOUZ""|url=https://www.rapeton.com/jhay-cortez-lanza-su-nuevo-album-famouz/|url-status=live|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=www.rapeton.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title="Famouz" con picardía sexual|url=https://www.primerahora.com/entretenimiento/musica/notas/famouz-con-picardia-sexual/|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=Primera Hora|date=June 10, 2019|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Gold & Platinum|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=RIAA|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Controversies ==
On May 12, 2020, Cortez released a topic called "24 horas", a tirade for [[Bryant Myers]] after both had a strong argument through Twitter, which tried to offend and challenged Myers to respond to him in less than 24 hours.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last=LOS40|date=May 20, 2020|title=Guerra entre Jhay Cortez y Bryant Myers: la historia de canciones 'no escritas'|url=https://los40.com/los40/2020/05/20/los40urban/1589968355_492308.html|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=LOS40|language=es}}</ref> After three days Myers replied with the subject "El que no escribe". However, he confessed that he did not publish it in less than 24 hours because no one tells him what to do, in a direct video from Instagram.<ref>{{Cite web|date=May 15, 2020|title=Bryant Myers le responde a Jhay Cortez con "El Que No Escribe"|url=https://www.slang.fm/noticias/bryant-myers-responde-a-las-24-horas-de-jhay-cortez-con-el-que-no-escribe/|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=Slang|language=es-MX}}</ref> This would become the first strip that Myers put out, since on similar occasions with other artists, he had not decided to respond. Shortly after, Cortez published another tiraera against him called "Game over",<ref>{{Cite web|date=May 16, 2020|title=Game Over de Jhay Cortez, segunda round del beef El Zocco|url=https://elzocco.com/musica/game-over-de-jhay-cortez/|access-date=April 20, 2021|website=El Zocco|language=es}}</ref> which Rohena did not want to answer as she did not consider it good enough. Some time later, Cortez acknowledged in an interview that the two were disrespectful and said he could make amends in the future when time has passed and they had matured.<ref name=":4" />
