'''Noah Kahan''' (born January 1, 1997<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/playlists/8342346/noah-kahan-playlist-takeover-tuesday-mexico-trip|title=Takeover Tuesday|website=Billboard|access-date=2018-03-22|language=en-US}}</ref>) is an American singer-songwriter<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/noah-kahan-mn0003592649/biography|title=Noah Kahan {{!}} Biography & History {{!}} AllMusic|website=AllMusic|access-date=2018-03-22}}</ref> of folk-infused pop who signed to [[Republic Records]] in 2017. Kahan released his first single, "Young Blood" in January 2017 and released four other singles over the course of 2017. His fans are known as "busyheads". Kahan made his television debut in 2018 on ''[[The Late Show with Stephen Colbert]]'' performing the single "[[Hurt Somebody]]".<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.noahkahan.com/news/noah-makes-his-us-television-debut|title=Noah Makes His US Television Debut|date=2018-03-02|work=Noah Kahan|access-date=2018-03-22|language=en}}</ref> Kahan released his debut Extended Play (EP) carrying the same name in January 2018, including an updated version of titular song with fellow Republic Records artist [[Julia Michaels]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://atwoodmagazine.com/hs-noah-kahan-hurt-somebody/|title=EP Review: Noah Kahan's 'Hurt Somebody' Triumphs with Heartache & Doubt - Atwood Magazine|date=2018-01-17|access-date=2018-03-22|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Biography ==
=== Early life ===
Noah Kahan was born in [[Hanover, New Hampshire]], and he attended Hanover High School in [[Hanover, New Hampshire]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thefix.nine.com.au/2018/05/15/21/30/noah-kahan-hurt-somebody-interview|title=Exclusive Interview with Noah Kahan|website=The Fix|access-date=2018-05-19}}</ref> Kahan began writing songs at age 8 and uploaded them to [[SoundCloud]] where his music began to gain exposure.

At age 17, Kahan began working with school friend music producers (Cwenga Matanzima). This attracted the attention of his current manager Drew Simmons of Foundations Artist Management. His music also caught the attention of songwriters such as [[Dan Wilson (musician)|Dan Wilson]] known for his work with [[Adele]], [[Scott Harris (songwriter)|Scott Harris]],  [[Carrie Underwood]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://foundationsmusic.com/noah-kahan|title=Noah Kahan Biography}}</ref> Kahan deferred his admission to [[Tulane University]] to pursue his developing music career.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imposemagazine.com/features/noah-kahan-shares-young-blood-talks-signing-to-republic-and-surrounding-yourself-with-good-people|title=Noah Kahan Shares "Young Blood", Talks Signing to Republic and Surrounding Yourself With Good People - Features - Impose Magazine|date=27 January 2017|publisher=}}</ref> Kahan has said that two career goals he wished for were to be verified on [[Instagram]] and to have a [[Wikipedia]] page.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://soundcloud.com/the-405/the-405-podcast-noah-kahan|title=The 405 Podcast: Noah Kahan|website=SoundCloud|access-date=2018-05-19}}</ref>

Kahan cites some of his inspirations as [[Paul Simon]], [[Cat Stevens]], [[Counting Crows]], [[Hozier (musician)|Hozier]], and [[Mumford and Sons]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://chicago.gopride.com/news/interview.cfm/articleid/1184116|title=Singer Noah Kahan leaves small town life behind|website=Chicago Pride|access-date=2018-05-19}}</ref>

=== 2017–present: music career ===
In 2016, Kahan signed to [[Republic Records]] and he began working with producer [[Joel Little]], known for his work with [[Lorde]] and [[Khalid (singer)|Khalid]], with whom he recorded six songs. Kahan released his first single "Young Blood" in January 2017 and gained over 9 million streams.

In September 2017, Kahan released "[[Hurt Somebody]]", the lead single from his forthcoming debut extended play of the same name. "Hurt Somebody" was re-recorded later in 2017 featuring [[Grammy]] nominated artist [[Julia Michaels]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.idolator.com/7673592/noah-kahan-julia-michaels-hurt-somebody?chrome=1|title=Noah Kahan Calls On Julia Michaels For A Duet Version Of "Hurt Somebody|website=Idolator|access-date=2018-05-19}}</ref> ''Hurt Somebody'' was released in January 2018.

On April 8, 2019, Kahan announced his debut full-length studio album entitled ''[[Busyhead]]'', and included the duet version of "Hurt Somebody" as well as two other singles "False Confidence" and "Mess". It was released on June 14, 2019.<ref>[https://www.idolator.com/7743882/noah-kahan-debut-album-busyhead-tour?chrome=1 Finally! Noah Kahan Announces Debut LP, ‘Busyhead’] - ''[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]'' (published April 8, 2019)</ref>

On April 30, 2020, Noah announced on his Instagram that he would drop an EP at midnight. He then dropped "Cape Elizabeth." The album was recorded over one week at Kahan's friend Phin Choukas' home studio in Vermont exactly one week after the singer left New York City in March to avoid [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID-19]]. Cape Elizabeth was then released on May 1, 2020.

On June 17, 2021 Kahan announced his second full-length album titled ''I Was/I Am''. Five days later he announced the ''I Was/I Am'' tour. On September 17, 2021, the album was released. Throughout the summer leading up to the release of the album Kahan released “Part of Me”, “Godlight”, and “Animal” as singles ahead of the album drop. “Part of Me” was the only one of the three singles to be dropped before the album announcement.

== Tours ==
Kahan opened for [[Ben Folds]] and [[Anderson East]] in the United States in February 2017 and then went on to open for [[Milky Chance]] in March 2017. Later opening on an East Coast tour with [[The Strumbellas]] that began on October 12, 2017, in [[Milwaukee, Wisconsin]], and concluded on November 14, 2017, in [[Buffalo, New York]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7965585/noah-kahan-new-song-hurt-somebody|title=Noah Kahan Doesn't Want to 'Hurt' Anyone on New Single|website=Billboard| access-date=2018-05-19}}</ref> He performed at [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']]'s Industry Night on November 16, 2017, at the Ludlow House in [[New York City]], showcasing his pop-folk song "Fine".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/concerts/8039416/billboard-industry-nights-tor-miller-noah-kahan-cape-francis|title=Billboard's Industry Nights Kicked Off With Performances by Tor Miller, Noah Kahan & Cape Francis|website=Billboard| access-date=2018-05-19}}</ref> In April and May 2018, Kahan supported [[George Ezra]] on his North American tour<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://pancakesandwhiskey.com/2018/05/03/noah-kahan-george-ezra-at-terminal-5/|title=Noah Kahan & George Ezra Get Cozy at Terminal 5|website=Pancakes and Whiskey| access-date=2018-05-19}}</ref> and then directly after embarked on his own tour, headlining shows across Europe throughout May 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.noahkahan.com/tour|title=Tour Dates|website=Noah Kahan| access-date=2018-05-19}}</ref> Kahan then embarked on a world tour in October and November 2018, with Dean Lewis joining him in support for the North American leg.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.noahkahan.com/tour|title=Tour Dates|website=Noah Kahan| access-date=2018-10-11}}</ref> He later opened for Dean Lewis on his tour of Europe in February 2019, then joining [[James Bay (singer)|James Bay]] on his North American tour for the following month. Kahan toured for 'The Busyhead Tour', in which he headlined shows across North America from September to November 2019.


In October 2021, Kahan headlined his 'I Am / I Was Tour', with support from Blake Rose and Genevieve Stokes.
