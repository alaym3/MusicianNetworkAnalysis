'''Karly-Marina Loaiza''' (born July 17, 1994), known professionally as '''Kali Uchis''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|uː|tʃ|i|s}} {{respell|OO|chees}}), is an American singer and songwriter. She released her debut mixtape, ''Drunken Babble'', in 2012, which was followed by her debut EP, ''[[Por Vida]]'', released in 2015. In 2018, Uchis released her debut studio album ''[[Isolation (Kali Uchis album)|Isolation]]'' to widespread acclaim. Uchis's second studio album and her first Spanish language project, ''[[Sin Miedo (del Amor y Otros Demonios)]]'', was released in 2020. The album spawned the single "[[Telepatía]]", which became Uchis' first solo charting hit on the US [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]]. She won a [[Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording]] for her feature on [[Kaytranada]]'s single "10%".

==Personal life==
Karly-Marina Loaiza was born on July 17, 1994, in [[Alexandria, Virginia]].<ref name="Fader" /><ref>{{cite web |last=Kellman |first=Andy |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kali-uchis-mn0003446629/biography |title=Kali Uchis {{!}} Biography & History |work=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=November 19, 2018 |archive-date=June 13, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180613014258/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kali-uchis-mn0003446629/biography |url-status=live }}</ref> Her father moved from [[Pereira, Colombia]], to the United States, where he met Karly's mother during the early 1990s. Her mother had children from a previous marriage. Karly is their only child together. Her father moved back to Colombia when Karly was in high school in order to take care of his mother, who had fallen ill. Uchis stayed in Colombia with her father and aunts during the summer. Growing up in an immigrant and working-class home, her home life was often filled with hard manual labor.

In high school, Loaiza learned to play piano and saxophone.<ref>{{cite web |last=Beaudoin |first=Kate |url=https://mic.com/articles/112410/this-incredible-colombian-pop-star-is-about-to-make-it-big-in-america |title=This Incredible Colombian Pop Star Is About to Make It Big in America |work=[[Mic (media company)|Mic]] |date=March 12, 2015 |access-date=May 28, 2018 |archive-date=September 22, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200922183544/https://www.mic.com/articles/112410/this-incredible-colombian-pop-star-is-about-to-make-it-big-in-america |url-status=live }}</ref> She participated in a jazz band,<ref name="WP">{{cite news |last1=Estaff |first1=Remezcla |title=Kali Uchis has consistently visited Colombia to Donate Food & Supplies to Families in Need |url=http://remezcla.com/music/kali-uchis-colombia-charity/ |access-date=November 19, 2018 |work=Remezcla |date=August 23, 2018 |archive-date=November 20, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181120095451/http://remezcla.com/music/kali-uchis-colombia-charity/ |url-status=live }}</ref> before her graduation from [[T. C. Williams High School]]. She often skipped classes to spend time at the photo lab, making experimental short films.<ref name="Fader"/><ref name="MetroTimes">{{cite web |last=Wisniewkski |first=Angela |url=https://www.metrotimes.com/detroit/kali-uchis-talks-immigration-show-girls-and-her-deceptively-sweet-music/Content?oid=5552973 |title=Kali Uchis talks immigration, 'Showgirls,' and her deceptively sweet music |work=[[Metro Times]] |date=September 13, 2017 |access-date=November 19, 2018 |archive-date=June 12, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612144125/https://www.metrotimes.com/detroit/kali-uchis-talks-immigration-show-girls-and-her-deceptively-sweet-music/Content?oid=5552973 |url-status=live }}</ref> Her interest in photography led to her creating mixtape cover art.<ref name="WP"/> Skipping class and breaking the curfew set by her parents led to her being kicked out of her home. During this time, she lived in her car and wrote songs on her keyboard which would later come in the form of her lo-fi mixtape, ''Drunken Babble''.<ref name="Savage">{{cite news|last=Savage|first=Mark|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-45005019|title=How Kali Uchis went from living out of a car to pop stardom|date=August 1, 2018|work=BBC News|access-date=March 10, 2020|language=en-GB|archive-date=June 28, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200628022804/https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-45005019|url-status=live}}</ref> She also wrote poetry, songs and music, but did not initially intend to sing, being rather more interested in directing films than being in the spotlight. She was given the nickname "Kali Uchis" by her father.<ref name="Fader">{{cite news |last1=Diaz |first1=Jaquira |author-link1=Jaquira Díaz |title=Who Is The Real Kali Uchis? |url=http://www.thefader.com/2017/07/13/kali-uchis-cover-story-album-tyrant-interview |access-date=July 17, 2020 |work=[[The Fader]] |date=July 13, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190202223601/http://www.thefader.com/2017/07/13/kali-uchis-cover-story-album-tyrant-interview |archive-date=February 2, 2019}}</ref>

Loaiza is openly [[Bisexuality|bisexual]].<ref>{{cite tweet |last= |first=  |user=kaliuchisbra|number=1252698592667729924 |date= April 21, 2020 |title= i've openly been bi my whole life :)|access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref>

==Artistry==
===Influences===
Uchis stated that she is influenced by music of the 1960s, with its mix of early soul, R&B and doo-wop, saying: "Musically and aesthetically, the culture of it just inspires me."<ref name="i-D"/> She also mentioned that she enjoys jazz, stating during her career beginnings that she draws musical inspiration from [[Ella Fitzgerald]] and [[Billie Holiday]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Smith |first=Thomas |url=http://www.nme.com/music-interviews/kali-uchis-making-modern-icon-2293948 |title=Kali Uchis: The Making of A Modern Icon |work=[[NME]] |date=April 16, 2018 |access-date=May 28, 2018 |archive-date=May 29, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180529130200/http://www.nme.com/music-interviews/kali-uchis-making-modern-icon-2293948 |url-status=live }}</ref> Other musicians she cited as influences on her sound are [[Curtis Mayfield]], [[Loose Ends (band)|Loose Ends]], [[Ralfi Pagan]], and [[Irma Thomas]].<ref name="i-D">{{cite web |last=Wilkinson |first=Dan |url=https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/pabgyb/2468-we-appreciate-kali-uchis |title=2,4,6,8... we appreciate kali uchis |work=[[i-D]] |date=November 24, 2014 |access-date=May 28, 2018 |archive-date=May 29, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180529054142/https://i-d.vice.com/en_uk/article/pabgyb/2468-we-appreciate-kali-uchis |url-status=live }}</ref> She also takes influences from [[Celia Cruz]], [[Salma Hayek]], [[La Lupe]], [[Selena]], [[Shakira]] and [[Ivy Queen]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://latina.com/becoming-kali-uchis/|title=Becoming Kali Uchis|work=Latina|author=García, Sandra|date=|access-date=October 4, 2021}}</ref> Uchis also admires singer [[Mariah Carey]].<ref>https://twitter.com/KALIUCHIS/status/1147943456276267008 {{Bare URL inline|date=November 2021}}</ref>

==Philanthropy==
{{quote box|quote="My family is still there, and my parents are there now, I got to experience both worlds in a way. I feel like that was really nice to have as a kid, and I would definitely want to give that to my own kid, the ability to have multiple places to call home."|source= -Uchis, ''[[Washington Post]]''<ref name="WP"/>|width=20%|align=right|style=padding:8px;|border=2px}}
In 2018, Uchis started selling clothing through social shopping app [[Depop]] and donated the proceeds to communities around her hometown.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/music/kali-uchis-used-to-live-out-of-her-car-now-shes-one-of-pop-musics-rising-stars/2018/10/25/46afaa0a-d310-11e8-b2d2-f397227b43f0_story.html?noredirect=on|title=Kali Uchis used to live out of her car. Now, she's one of pop music's rising stars.|last1=Kimble|first1=Julian|date=October 25, 2018|work=[[Washington Post]]|access-date=November 19, 2018|language=en|archive-date=December 16, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181216120708/https://www.washingtonpost.com/entertainment/music/kali-uchis-used-to-live-out-of-her-car-now-shes-one-of-pop-musics-rising-stars/2018/10/25/46afaa0a-d310-11e8-b2d2-f397227b43f0_story.html?noredirect=on|url-status=live}}</ref> On an [[Instagram]] post, she captioned "in my hometown of Colombia I was able to contribute medicine, groceries, school supplies, new shoes, household appliances, & even make house modifications for some families suffering from disabilities, illness, & others who had nothing after fleeing from guerrilla warfare". In early August 2018, Uchis and her family in Pereira planned to work and help with communities in her hometown, Pereira, and in [[Medellín]].<ref name="WP"/>
