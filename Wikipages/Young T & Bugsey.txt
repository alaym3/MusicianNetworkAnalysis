'''Young T & Bugsey''' are a British [[hip hop music|hip hop]] duo from [[Nottingham]], consisting of rappers Ra'chard "Young T" Tucker and Doyin "Bugsey" Julius.<ref name="Chartbreaker">{{Cite web|last=Glicksman|first=Josh|date=7 July 2020|title=Chartbreaker: Young T & Bugsey Spent Years Preparing For Fame — Then One Viral Video Changed Everything|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/9414625/young-t-bugsey-dont-rush-chartbreaker-interview|access-date=3 November 2020|website=Billboard|language=en}}</ref><ref name="Champagne">{{Cite news|last=Aroesti|first=Rachel|date=20 March 2020|title=Young T & Bugsey: Plead the 5th review – champagne-soaked retro rap|language=en-GB|work=The Guardian|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2020/mar/20/young-t-bugsey-plead-the-5th-review-black-butter-records|access-date=3 November 2020|issn=0261-3077}}</ref><ref name="Wireless">{{cite web|url=https://worthofmouth.co.uk/2017/02/23/youngt-bugsey/|title=Young T and Bugsey: The Teens Who Went From The 5ive to Wireless Festival|website=worthofmouth.co.uk|date=23 February 2017|access-date=29 July 2019|first=Piolo|last=Ravina}}</ref><ref name="Accents">{{Cite web|last=Phillips|first=Noel|date=19 January 2020|title=Regional rappers on the rise as industry gets hip to accents|url=https://uk.news.yahoo.com/regional-rappers-rise-industry-gets-hip-accents-160500971.html|access-date=3 November 2020|website=uk.news.yahoo.com|language=en-GB}}</ref> 

==History==
They rose to prominence outside the UK due to the #DontRushChallenge on [[TikTok]], with their song "[[Don't Rush (Young T & Bugsey song)|Don't Rush]]", where a group of people in a video compilation start off each part of their segment in their "home attire", then cover the camera with an object (usually a makeup brush for women or a beard brush for men) and reveal a glammed-up look after pulling the object away.{{Cn|date=November 2021}}
