'''David Sánchez Badillo''' (born September 25, 1977), better known as '''Tempo''', is a Puerto Rican rapper and songwriter.<ref name="Bonacich">Bonacich, Drago "[http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tempo-p519947/biography Tempo Biography]", [[Allmusic]], retrieved December 12, 2010</ref> He was the leading figure in the reggaeton scene from the late 1990s until his arrest in 2002. He was released in 2013 after spending 11 years in prison. Although Tempo had success during the emergence of reggaeton, his popularity faded following his imprisonment; he is nowadays regarded as a controversial figure within the genre.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://heabbi.com/tempo-lo-seria-nunca-fue|title=Tempo: La historia de un artista que pudo ser grande y ahí quedó|website=Heabbi}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://heabbi.com/tempo-anuel-exito-prision|title=¿Por qué Tempo no tuvo el mismo éxito que Anuel al salir de prisión?|website=Heabbi}}</ref>

== Early life ==
David Sanchez Badillo was born in [[Ponce, Puerto Rico]], in 1977. Tempo studied at [[Escuela Libre de Música de Ponce]].<ref name="school">{{cite web|url=http://www.reggaeton-in-cuba.com/en/biography-singers-tempo.htm|title=Biography Tempo|publisher=Reggaeton en Cuba. |accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref> When Tempo was five, his family moved to upstate Haverstraw, New York. They lived in [[Rockland County, New York|Rockland County]] for four years and returned to Puerto Rico when Tempo was nine years old. He began writing rap lyrics at the age of 11.{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}}

== Career ==

=== Beginnings (1995–1998) ===
Tempo first appeared in ''DJ Joe Volume 3'' (1995) and year later in ''DJ Joe Volume 4''.{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}}  Initially, Tempo was a member of {{Lang|es|El Escuadron Del Panico}} (The Panic Squadron) alongside artists [[Master Joe]], [[OG Black]], Genio, [[Trebol Clan]], and [[Ranking Stone]]. DJ Joe was the group's musical producer.  Despite underground recognition and a hit single with "Figura De Campeona" (Figure of a Champion) alongside DJ Joe, Tempo was not satisfied with his musical prospects and joined the [[Nuyorican]] rap group S.P.I.C. (Spanish People In Control). S.P.I.C. was produced by veteran hip hop promoter, producer, and artist James De La Raza.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/de-la-raza-mw0000024828|title=De La Raza - James DeLaRaza - Songs, Reviews, Credits|website=AllMusic}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/SPIC-Spanish-People-In-Control-SPIC-Spanish-People-In-Control/release/6388559|title=S.P.I.C. (Spanish People In Control) - S.P.I.C. - Spanish People In Control|website=Discogs}}</ref>

Tempo returned to Puerto Rico to restart his solo career in 1998 and again collaborated with {{Lang|es|El Escuadron Del Panico}}.  Around this time, thanks to his burgeoning friendship and performances with [[Mexicano 777 (Puerto Rican rapper)|Mexicano 777]], Tempo met [[DJ Playero]]. Tempo was added to DJ Playero's roster and he recorded "{{Lang|es|Bailando Quiero Verlas}}" (I Want to See Them [Ladies] Dancing) in 1998 which was released on DJ Playero's album ''[[Playero 41: Past Present & Future]]''. Tempo's first song with DJ Playero was a collaboration with Mexicano 777—"{{Lang|es|Hagan Ruido Las Pistolas}}" (Make Noise Pistols); it is considered one of the classic Latin hip hop [[Diss Track|diss tracks]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/mexicano-777-mn0000383251/biography|title=Mexicano 777 - Biography & History|website=AllMusic}}</ref>{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}}

=== Career highlights (1999–2002) ===
Tempo released ''Game Over'' in 1999 on the [[Buddha's Family|Buddha's Productions]] label; the album had appearances by [[Héctor & Tito]] and [[MC Ceja]].{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}} [[New Game (Tempo album)|''New Game'']] was released the next year and had appearances by [[Baby Rasta & Gringo]] and [[Maicol & Manuel]]. Both albums achieved gold record certification.{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}} Tempo also acted as co-producer on the album ''[[Buddha's Family]]'', which again received a gold record certification.{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}} He produced the album ''[[Vida Eterna]]'' for [[Getto & Gastam]] in 2002. During this time he also recorded "{{Lang|es|El Que Tenia Que Llegar}}" for DJ Frank's ''Time to Kill'', "{{Lang|es|Empiezo La Accion}}" for ''Boricuas NY 2''<ref name="single one">{{cite web|url=http://www.cduniverse.com/search/xx/music/pid/6779450/a/boricuas+ny2.htm|title= Boricuas NY 2 album|publisher=[[CD Universe]], retrieved April 21, 2015}}</ref> and "{{Lang|es|Tu Y Quien Mas}}" for the compilation album from [[Alex Gárgolas]] titled ''Gargolas, Vol 3''.<ref name="single two">"{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/gargolas-vol-3-mw0000561625|title=Gargolas vol 3 album|publisher=[[Allmusic]], retrieved April 21, 2015}}</ref>

By 2001, Tempo was the most successful hip hop and reggaeton artist in Puerto Rico with multiple gold and platinum certified albums.{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}} On the island he outsold mainstream Puerto Rican acts such as [[Chayanne]] and [[Ricky Martin]].{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}}  In an interview with ''Alofoke'', Tempo said that Sony Latin representatives sought the artist out in Puerto Rico and managed to get him a meeting with legendary record executive [[Tommy Mottola]] via videophone.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://alofokemusic.net/historica-entrevista-tempo-habla-arcangel-residente-cosculluela-ultimo-album/|title=HISTÓRICA ENTREVISTA - Tempo habla de Arcangel / Residente / Cosculluela / su ultimo álbum!!!|date=June 20, 2017|website=ALOFOKE MUSIC}}</ref>  Tempo received an undisclosed seven digit sum from Sony to sign for a multiple album deal.{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}}

The [[Éxitos (Tempo album)|''Éxitos'']] compilation was released by [[Sony Records|Sony]] in 2002 and contained a mix of old and new material.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/exitos-mw0000218716|title=Exitos - Tempo - Songs, Reviews, Credits|website=AllMusic}}</ref>  ''Éxitos'' sold over 500,000 copies worldwide.{{Citation needed|date=April 2019}} It was re-released digitally by Tempo's independent label.{{Citation needed|date=May 2019}} 

=== {{Anchor|“Free Tempo” (2002–2013)}}''Free Tempo'' (2002–2013) ===
On October 10, 2002, Tempo was arrested and subsequently sentenced to 24 years in prison for alleged drug trafficking and conspiracy to commit such acts. Tempo always declared his innocence and the charges were never proven, which led to his eventual release in 2013.<ref name="Tempo carcel">{{cite news|url=http://www.noticiassin.com/2011/04/rapero-tempo-puede-quedar-libre-en-los-proximos-seis-anos-gracias-a-acuerdo/|title=Rapero Tempo puede quedar libre en los próximos seis años gracias a acuerdo|date=April 28, 2011|accessdate=April 28, 2011|publisher=Noticias Sin.|language=Spanish|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110430001654/http://www.noticiassin.com/2011/04/rapero-tempo-puede-quedar-libre-en-los-proximos-seis-anos-gracias-a-acuerdo/|archive-date=April 30, 2011|url-status=dead|df=mdy-all}}</ref> While in prison, previously recorded songs were released on various compilation albums: "{{Lang|es|Quien Quiere Guerra}}" was featured on ''Desafio'' in 2004, and "{{Lang|es|Van A Ver Lo Que Es Muerte}}" was released on ''Las Plagas 2'' in 2003. In 2004 the song "{{Lang|es|Que Es Lo Que Quiere}}" was released on DJ Nelson's [[Flow la Discoteka|''Flow La Discoteka'']].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Flow-La-Discoteka-DJ-Nelson/dp/B000BYAD6G|title=DJ Nelson's Flow la Discoteka|publisher=Amazon, retrieved April 25, 2015}}</ref>

Tempo completed his album deal with Sony by releasing the ''[[Free Tempo]]'' album from prison in June 2009, which included the hit single "{{Lang|es|Deja Que Hable El Dembow}}" featuring [[Wisin y Yandel]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/free-tempo-mw0000816954|title=Free Tempo - Tempo - Songs, Reviews, Credits|website=AllMusic}}</ref> The album received a nomination for Best Long Form Music Video at the [[10th Annual Latin Grammy Awards]] for the video which featured the [[London Symphony Orchestra]].<ref name="Latin Grammy">{{cite news|url=http://www.primerahora.com/entretenimiento/farandula/nota/tempopilardelgenerourbano-960980/|title=Tempo: Pilar del género urbano|last=Tirado|first=Frances|date=October 10, 2013|accessdate=April 28, 2011|publisher=Primera Hora.|language=Spanish}}</ref>

=== Release and probation (2013–present) ===
Tempo was released from prison on October 9, 2013, and was required to live at a halfway house in Puerto Rico for six months to finish his sentence.<ref name="liberacion">{{cite news|last=Vasquez|first=Jose|url=http://www.lamezcla.com/home/tempo-returns-to-puerto-rico/|title=Tempo Returns to Puerto Rico|date=October 10, 2013|publisher=La Mezcla. |accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref> During this time he was able to record the mixtape ''Free Music''; it was released as a free download on his official website on December 21, 2013.

His first concert after release was on May 17, 2014, in the [[Jose Miguel Agrelot Coliseum]].<ref name="concierto primero">{{cite web|url=http://www.eluniversal.com/arte-y-entretenimiento/140516/tempo-ofrecera-su-primer-concierto-tras-11-anos-preso|title=Tempo ofrecerá su primer concierto tras 11 años preso|language=Spanish|date=May 16, 2014|publisher=El Universal. |accessdate=April 28, 2011}}</ref>

Tempo's initial releases after prison received mixed reviews.{{Citation needed|date=April 2019}} The single "{{Lang|es|Tu Historia}}" was released on February 14, 2015, and was considered a return to form for the artist. The song sharply criticizes modern hip hop reggaeton and Tempo's views on how the genre lost its essence in favor of mainstream propaganda.<ref name="tiraera dos">{{cite web|url=http://www.rapeton.com/flex-responde-al-verso-de-tu-historia-donde-tempo-lo-menciona/|title=Flex Responde al Verse de Tu Historia donde Tempo lo Menciona|language=Spanish|date=February 18, 2015|publisher=Rapeton. |accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref><ref name="tiraera tres">{{cite web|url=http://flowhot.net/noticias-de-reggaeton/rkm-reacciona-ante-mencion-de-tempo-en-su-nueva-cancion-tu-historia/|title=RKM Reacciona Ante Mencion de Tempo En Su Nueva Cancion Tu Historia|language=Spanish|date=February 17, 2015|publisher=FlowHot. |accessdate=April 22, 2015}}</ref><ref name="panama">{{cite news|last=Aspirilla|first=Yuselin|url=http://www.critica.com.pa/eva-revista/panas-le-tiran-tempo-pa-que-respete-379559|title=Panas le Tiran Tempo pa que Respete|language=Spanish|date=February 27, 2015|publisher=Critica. |accessdate=April 21, 2015}}</ref>

On September 25, 2018, Tempo released the album ''Back To The Game''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/Back-Game-Explicit-Tempo/dp/B07H9XCJDK|title=Amazon.com|website=www.amazon.com|access-date=2019-04-08}}</ref>
