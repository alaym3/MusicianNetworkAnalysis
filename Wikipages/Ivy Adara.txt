'''Ivy Adara''' is an Australia singer and composer born in 1995.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Ivy Adara {{!}} Biography & History|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ivy-adara-mn0003665638/biography|access-date=2021-08-16|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> Adara has written for artists such as [[Selena Gomez]] and Hailee Steinfeld and started her career by competing in the eighth season of [[The X Factor (Australian TV series)|The X-Factor]] Australia.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|title=Q&A: Australian Pop Sensation Ivy Adara Talks Inspiration Behind Debut EP, ‘Intraduction’ / Ones To Watch|url=http://www.onestowatch.com/blog/qa-australian-pop-sensation-ivy-adara-talks|access-date=2021-08-16|website=Ones To Watch}}</ref>

== Career and early life ==
Adara wrote her first song at age seven and knew music was going to be in her life and often turned chores into musical productions.<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4">{{Cite web|last=thomasbleach|date=2018-09-07|title=INTERVIEW: IVY ADARA|url=https://thomasbleach.com/2018/09/07/interview-ivy-adara/|access-date=2021-08-16|website=ThomasBleach|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2016, Adara joined the eight season of [[The X Factor (Australian season 8)|The X-Factor Australia]] where she performed [[Sia (musician)|Sia]]'s song "[[Alive (Sia song)|Alive]]" but didn't make it past the early rounds of the show despite a standing ovation from [[Guy Sebastian]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|title=Ivy ADARA – Aussie Grown Radio|url=https://aussiegrownradio.com/artists/ivy-adara/|access-date=2021-08-16|language=en-AU}}</ref>

After the X-Factor she relocated to Los Angeles where she has co-wrote songs for [[Dami Im]], [[Natalie Conway]], [[Jessica Mauboy|Jessica Mouboy]], [[Selena Gomez]], [[Jason Derulo|Jason DeRulo]], [[Jennifer Lopez]], [[Rita Ora]], [[G-Eazy]] and [[Hailee Steinfeld]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ivy Adara Premieres New Song “Currency”|url=https://www.pmstudio.com/music/music.html?page=20180429-7022|access-date=2021-08-16|website=pm studio world wide news|language=ja}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=360|first=pop culture|date=2021|title=GRYFFIN SHARES STUNNING NEW SINGLE “BYE BYE” FEAT. IVY ADARA|url=https://www.the360mag.com/gryffin-shares-stunning-new-single-bye-bye-feat-ivy-adara/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-16|website=www.the360mag.com}}</ref><ref name=":4" /><ref name=":5">{{Cite web|title=Ivy Adara Stranger Interview|url=https://www.female.com.au/ivy-adara-stranger-interview.htm|access-date=2021-08-16|website=www.female.com.au|language=en}}</ref> Adara experienced a number one song on Australian iTunes with the song she wrote for [[Jessica Mauboy]], "[[Fallin' (Jessica Mauboy song)|Fallin]]" which was nominated for 5 [[Australian Recording Industry Association|ARIA]] & 3 [[APRA AMCOS|APRA]] awards including Song Of the Year.<ref name=":4" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Ivy Adara Releases Official Video For “Callgirl”! – Volatile Weekly|url=https://volatileweekly.com/2019/03/ivy-adara-releases-official-video-for-callgirl/|access-date=2021-08-16|language=en-US}}</ref> 

In 2017, Adara released the songs "Famous" and "Everybody Hurts (Feat. [[The Him]])".<ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-09-29|title=PREMIERE {{!}} Rising Duo The Him Share Upbeat Original “Everybody Hurts” Ft. Ivy Adara + Exclusive Interview|url=https://thissongissick.com/post/premiere-the-him-everybody-hurts-feat-ivy-adara-exclusive-interview/|access-date=2021-08-16|website=This Song Is Sick|language=en-US}}</ref> 

In 2018, Adara released her solo EP "Intraduction" that was created in collaboration with hitmakers [[Cirkut]], [[Jon Hume]] and Lindsey Jackson.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Album review: Ivy Adara, Intraduction|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/entertainment/album-review-ivy-adara-intraduction/M6LEZU2V5RYCBABM2LMYJSPAYE/|access-date=2021-08-16|website=NZ Herald|language=en-NZ}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Artvoice|date=2018-10-06|title=IVY ADARA Releases Debut EP Intraduction|url=https://www.artvoice.com/2018/10/05/ivy-adara-releases-debut-ep-intraduction/|access-date=2021-08-16|website=Artvoice|language=en-US}}</ref> In the same year, the producer [[Gryffin]] released the song [[Bye Bye (Gryffin song)|"Bye Bye"]] featuring Adara, who co-wrote the song.<ref name=":3" /><ref>{{Cite web|last=Allen|first=Audrey|date=2019-01-31|title=Gryffin - Bye Bye (feat. Ivy Adara)|url=https://www.edmtunes.com/2019/01/gryffin-bye-bye/|access-date=2021-08-16|website=EDMTunes|language=en-US}}</ref> They toured together and performed it at [[Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival|Coachella]].<ref name=":5" />

In 2019, TikTok star [[Loren Gray]] released the song "Lie Like That" which was co-written by Adara.<ref>{{Cite web|title=LOREN GRAY SHARES TWO NEW SINGLES – “OPTIONS” AND “LIE LIKE THAT”|url=https://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/loren-gray-shares-two-new-singles-options-and-lie-like-that/|access-date=2021-08-16|website=Umusic|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2021, the singles "Pressure" and "Stranger" were released as part of her solo project.<ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/ivyadara/status/1424551092852072453|url=https://twitter.com/ivyadara/status/1424551092852072453|access-date=2021-08-16|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=https://twitter.com/ivyadara/status/1384651863790817284|url=https://twitter.com/ivyadara/status/1384651863790817284|access-date=2021-08-16|website=Twitter|language=en}}</ref><ref name=":5" /> The song Stranger came about when Adara had been feeling depressed and her father flew to LA to see her from Sydney. She stated that she picked up her pen and guitar and the song just flowed out.<ref name=":5" /> In 2022, Adara has upcoming releases that will feature in the [[Jennifer Lopez]] movie, [[Marry Me (2022 film)|Marry Me]].<ref name=":5" />

Adara's discography has over 200 million streams on [[Spotify]] alone as of 2021.<ref name=":5" /> She describes her music as hopeful, dark, warm, empowering, vulnerable and authentic.<ref name=":5" />

== Personal life ==
Inspiration for her music comes mostly from Adara's personal experiences and she looks up to [[Lorde]], Khalid and [[Troye Sivan]].<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":4" /> 

Adara was supposed to marry on August 6 2021, but the COVID-19 pandemic lockdowns meant it was cancelled.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Adara|first=Ivy|date=2021|title=Post about marriage|url=https://www.facebook.com/ivyadaramusic/posts/262035302400750|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-16|website=www.facebook.com|language=en}}</ref>

Adara resides in Los Angeles.<ref name=":5" />
