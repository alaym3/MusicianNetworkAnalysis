'''Anthony Clemons Jr.''' (born September 26, 1991) is an American singer. He rose to prominence as a guest artist on [[Kanye West]]'s 2018 single "[[All Mine (Kanye West song)|All Mine]]". Clemons has since worked with a multitude of artists, including West, [[Ty Dolla Sign]], [[Beyoncé]], [[Camila Cabello]], [[Justin Timberlake]], the [[Sunday Service Choir]] and more. Clemons released his debut EP ''Happy 2 Be Here'' in March 2020, featuring Ty Dolla Sign, [[Timbaland]], and [[Pharrell Williams]]. The project received a nomination at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]] for [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Album|Best R&B Album]].

== Early life ==
Clemons was born and raised in [[Willingboro Township, New Jersey|Willingboro, New Jersey]].<ref name="NJ" /> His musical taste was shaped by his parents playing [[Michael Jackson]], [[The Notorious B.I.G.]] and [[Stevie Wonder]].<ref name="NJ" /> At age four, Clemons began performing at birthday parties as a Jackson impersonator.<ref name="NJ" /> While studying at [[Burlington County Institute of Technology Westampton Campus|Burlington County Institute of Technology]], Clemons was part of the youth choir at the Sharon Baptist Church and joined The Heritage Players and Showstoppers at [[Willingboro High School]].<ref name="NJ" /> At family events, Clemons would sing with his sisters Ashley and Amber as the "Clemons Trio".<ref name="NJ" /> In 2009, during his parents' divorce, Clemons began his interest in songwriting.<ref name="NJ" /> Clemons began recording himself and producing music with [[Pro Tools]] and [[Logic Pro|Logic]].<ref name="NJ" /> Clemons attended a semester at [[Rowan College at Burlington County|Burlington County College]] before dropping out.<ref name="NJ" />

== Career ==
Clemons began his songwriting career in 2016.<ref name="Sleep">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/kanye-west-jesus-is-king-ant-clemons-904042/|title=Ant Clemons Went From Sleeping on Floors to Singing With Kanye West|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|author=Elias Leight|date=October 29, 2019|accessdate=March 30, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191214193153/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/kanye-west-jesus-is-king-ant-clemons-904042/|archive-date=December 14, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> Clemons was working at [[Red Lobster]] in Cherry Hill and flying to [[Los Angeles]] every two weeks as an attempt to meet songwriters and producers.<ref name="Sleep" /> He would return to Philadelphia every time he ran out of money.<ref name="Sleep" /> Clemons' mother eventually supported him in permanently moving to Los Angeles, where Clemons slept on two friends' floor.<ref name="Sleep" /> Clemons wrote a song a day in exchange for rent.<ref name="Sleep" /> In Los Angeles, Clemons met [[Ryan Toby]] who gave him the opportunity to work on [[Luke James (singer)|Luke James]]' single "Drip".<ref name="Sleep" />

In January 2018, Clemons met American producer Bongo ByTheWay, where upon meeting each other for the first time, they recorded eleven tracks together in one day.<ref name="NJ" /> After the two quickly became close to one another, Bongo ByTheWay introduced Clemons to American singer [[Jeremih]].<ref name="Sleep" /> Clemons recorded the hook for a Jeremih demo titled "[[All Mine (Kanye West song)|All Mine]]".<ref name="AllMine" /> Jeremih was invited to [[Wyoming]] by American rapper [[Kanye West]] and played the demo.<ref name="AllMine" /> West then incorporated "All Mine" into his eighth studio album ''[[Ye (album)|Ye]]'' (2018).<ref name="AllMine">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/kanye-west-jesus-is-king-ant-clemons-904042/|title=Ant Clemons Went From Sleeping on Floors to Singing With Kanye West|author=Leight, Elia|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=October 29, 2019|accessdate=March 29, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191105045958/https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/kanye-west-jesus-is-king-ant-clemons-904042/|archive-date=November 5, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref> West then invited Clemons to Wyoming to write for [[Teyana Taylor]]'s second studio album ''[[K.T.S.E.]]''., providing additional vocals on the track "Hurry (feat. Kanye West).<ref name="Sleep" /> Clemons saw an increase in fame after working with West.<ref>{{cite magazine |author=Billboard Staff |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8477024/hip-hop-power-list-2018 |title=Drake, Kanye & More Among Billboard's 2018 R&B/Hip-Hop 100 Power Players [List] |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=September 27, 2018 |accessdate=March 29, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181001220443/https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/hip-hop/8477024/hip-hop-power-list-2018 |archive-date=October 1, 2018 |url-status=live}}</ref> In September 2018, Clemons was invited to [[Chicago]] by West and [[Chance the Rapper]] to work on West's ninth studio album.<ref name="Sleep" /> The album was initially titled ''Yandhi'' and Clemons was to feature on seven out of eight tracks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.complex.com/music/2018/09/kanyes-yandhi-8-tracks-long|title=Kanye West's 'Yandhi' May Be 8 Tracks Long|author=Joe Price|website=[[Complex (magazine)|Complex]]|date=September 30, 2018|accessdate=March 30, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181022232549/https://www.complex.com/music/2018/09/kanyes-yandhi-8-tracks-long|archive-date=October 22, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Several [[Internet leak|leaked]] songs from the album featured contributions from Clemons.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hiphop-n-more.com/2020/02/ant-clemons-debut-album-happy-2-be-here-ft-timbaland-pharrell-ty-dolla-sign-stream/|title=Ant Clemons Releases Debut Album 'Happy 2 Be Here' Ft. Timbaland, Pharrell, Ty Dolla Sign|date=February 28, 2020|accessdate=January 13, 2021|author=Navjosh}}</ref> ''Yandhi'' was reworked and released as ''[[Jesus Is King]]'' in October 2019, where Clemons was credited on the tracks "[[Selah (Kanye West song)|Selah]]", "[[Everything We Need]]", and "[[Water (Kanye West song)|Water]]".<ref name="Sleep" />

2019|url-status=live}}</ref> That same year, Clemons worked with [[Beyoncé]] to write "Mood 4 Eva" for the soundtrack album ''[[The Lion King: The Gift]]''.<ref name="H2BH">{{Cite web|url=https://hypebeast.com/2020/3/ant-clemons-happy-2-be-here-album-stream|title=Ant Clemons Enlists Timbaland, Ty Dolla $ign & Pharrell For New EP 'HAPPY 2 BE HERE'|website=Hypebeast|date=March 2, 2020|accessdate=March 30, 2020|author=Sophie Caraan}}</ref> Clemons was invited to the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards]] for his work with Beyoncé, [[Cordae]], Chance the Rapper, [[Skrillex]], [[Boys Noize]], and [[Ty Dolla Sign]].<ref name="NJ" /> Clemons co-wrote [[Camila Cabello]]'s 2020 single "[[My Oh My (Camila Cabello song)|My Oh My]]" featuring [[DaBaby]];<ref name="NJ" /> the song peaked at number 12 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/Camila-Cabello/chart-history/HSI|title=Camila Cabello Chart History (Hot 100)|accessdate=March 30, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200313150815/https://www.billboard.com/music/camila-cabello/chart-history/HSI|archive-date=March 13, 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> In March 2020, Clemons released his debut project ''Happy 2 Be Here'', featuring Ty Dolla Sign, [[Timbaland]], and [[Pharrell Williams]].<ref name="H2BH" /> In December 2020, the EP was listed as a nominee at the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]] for [[Grammy Award for Best R&B Album|Best R&B Album]].<ref name="BetterDays">{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/ant-clemons-justin-timberlake-better-days-rock-the-runoff-1098716/|title=Ant Clemons, Justin Timberlake Sing 'Better Days' for Rock the Runoff|magazine=Rolling Stone|date=December 4, 2020|accessdate=January 13, 2021|author=Claire Shaffer}}</ref>

In December 2020, Clemons released his collaboration with [[Justin Timberlake]], "Better Days". The duo debuted the song at a virtual concert fundraiser organized by [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] politician [[Stacey Abrams]], with proceeds going towards the [[2020–21 United States Senate election in Georgia|Senate runoff election in Georgia]].<ref name="BetterDays" /> Clemons, along with Timberlake, [[Bruce Springsteen]], [[Demi Lovato]], and [[Bon Jovi]], performed at the [[inauguration of Joe Biden]] on January 20, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://variety.com/2021/music/news/joe-biden-inauguration-special-tom-hanks-1234884378/|title=Tom Hanks to Host Inauguration TV Special With Performances by Demi Lovato, Justin Timberlake and More|author=Jordan Moreau|date=January 13, 2021|accessdate=January 13, 2021|website=Variety}}</ref>

On February 26, 2021, Clemons released a reissue of his debut project ''Happy 2 Be Here'' as ''HAPPY 2 BE HERE (Anniversary Edition)'' with an additional track named "June 1st", commemorating the release of his 2018 breakthrough hit "All Mine" with Kanye West.
