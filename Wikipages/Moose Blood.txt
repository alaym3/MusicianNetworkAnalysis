'''Moose Blood''' were an English [[emo]] band based in [[City of Canterbury|Canterbury]], [[Kent]]. They formed in 2012 and were signed to [[Hopeless Records]].

==History==
Moose Blood formed in August 2012 and published a self-released demo, ''Bukowski Demo (Summer '12)'', in the same month. The group's line-up consisted of Eddy Brewerton on vocals and guitar, Mark E. Osbourne on guitar, Kyle Todd on bass and Glenn Harvey on drums.<ref name=AMbio>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/moose-blood-mn0003102954/biography|title=Moose Blood {{!}} Biography & History |work=AllMusic|publisher=All Media Network, LLC|author=Yeung, Neil Z.|accessdate=30 January 2017}}</ref> On 11 February 2013, an EP, ''[[Moving Home]]'', was released through Fist in the Air.<ref name=iTunesMHEP>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/moving-home-ep/id591391395|title=Moving Home - EP by Moose Blood|work=iTunes|publisher=Apple Inc.|accessdate=19 March 2016}}</ref> "Boston" and "Orlando" were released together as a single through Venn Records<ref name=AP.netBOsingle>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3154001|title=Moose Blood Release "Boston/Orlando" Digital Single - News Article|work=AbsolutePunk|publisher=SpinMedia|author=DiVincenzo, Alex |date=29 April 2013|accessdate=19 March 2016}}</ref> on 26 April.<ref name=iTunesBOsingle>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/boston-orlando-single/id637609873|title=Boston/Orlando - Single by Moose Blood|work=iTunes|publisher=Apple Inc.|accessdate=19 March 2016}}</ref> The band released a split EP with Departures through No Sleep on 12 November.<ref name=AP.netDMBsplit>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3431261|title=Departures & Moose Blood Announce Split, Stream Song - News Article|work=AbsolutePunk|publisher=SpinMedia|author=Gardner, Ryan |date=17 September 2013|accessdate=19 March 2016}}</ref> On 16 April 2014, it was announced that the band had signed to [[No Sleep Records|No Sleep]] and would release their debut album later in the year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3697300|title=Moose Blood Join No Sleep Records- News Article|work=AbsolutePunk|publisher=SpinMedia|author=Huntington, Kyle |date=16 April 2014|accessdate=19 March 2016}}</ref> In May, the band supported [[I Am the Avalanche]] on their tour of the UK.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3653021|title=I Am The Avalanche Reveal UK Dates - News Article|work=AbsolutePunk|publisher=SpinMedia|author=Biddulph, Andy |date=4 February 2014|accessdate=19 March 2016}}</ref> Their debut album ''[[I'll Keep You in Mind, From Time to Time]]'' charted at number 45 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Heatseekers]] chart.<ref name=BillHeat>{{cite web|title=Moose Blood - Chart History (Heatseekers Albums)|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6282615/moose-blood/chart|work=Billboard|publisher=Prometheus Global Media|accessdate=21 November 2014}}</ref> They played on the 2015 [[Warped Tour]].

Moose Blood announced in January 2016 that they would be touring the UK throughout April 2016, supported by The Winter Passing and Greywind.<ref name="apriltour-originalrock">{{cite web |url=https://originalrock.net/2016/03/21/moose-blood-announce-support-for-upcoming-tour/ |title=Moose Blood announce support for upcoming tour |date=March 21, 2016  |author=<!-- No author byline --> |accessdate= July 28, 2016}}</ref> The tour - which would include headline shows at venues such as the [[O2 Academy Islington]] in London, and [[Joiners Arms|Joiners]] in Southampton - ended up selling out before it had begun.<ref name="greywind-uktour">{{cite web |url=http://greywindband.com/post/142677494663/our-uk-tour-with-moose-blood-is-now-completely |title=Greywind – Our UK tour with Moose Blood is now completely... |website=greywindband.com |date=April 12, 2016 |author=<!-- No author byline --> |accessdate=July 28, 2016}}</ref> Moose Blood announced, via their Facebook and Twitter accounts, on 13 April that they had signed to [[Hopeless Records]] for their second album.<ref name="upsetmagazine">{{cite web |url=http://www.upsetmagazine.com/read/moose-blood-sign-hopeless-records/ |title=Moose Blood sign to Hopeless Records for their second album |newspaper=Upsetmagazine.com |date=<!-- Not accurate date --> |author=Ali Shutler |accessdate= July 28, 2016}}</ref> On 17 April that the first single titled "Honey" was played on the Radio 1 Rock Show as the Rockest Record. Eddy also announced that the new album would be released on 5 August and would be titled ''Blush''.<ref name="talknerdy-honey">{{cite web|url=http://talknerdywithus.com/2016/04/18/moose-blood-releases-single-announces-new-album/|title=Moose Blood Releases Single, Announces New Album – TalkNerdyWithUs|date=April 18, 2016|author=Nathan Badley|accessdate=July 27, 2016}}</ref>

In March 2017, Moose Blood announced the departure of drummer Glenn Harvey in the wake of sexual harassment allegations.<ref name="Facebook">{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/MooseBlood/posts/1601551663191793|title=Moose Blood Statement|date=March 22, 2017|author=Moose Blood|accessdate=November 12, 2017}}</ref> Harvey was alleged to have sent an unsolicited image to a woman during 2015.<ref name="AltPress">{{cite web|url=https://www.altpress.com/news/entry/moose_blood_remove_drummer_following_sexual_harassment_allegations|title=Moose Blood remove drummer following sexual harassment allegations that he masterbated into a sink|date=March 22, 2017|author=Alt Press|accessdate=November 12, 2017}}</ref>

On 24 September 2018, a few hours after being announced as support for [[Good Charlotte]] for their headline tour across Europe and the UK they released a statement saying that they "need to step away", hinting at a hiatus or a break up.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-moose-blood-hint-at-hiatus-in-statement-say-they-need-to-step-away|title=NEWS: Moose Blood hint at hiatus in statement; say they "need to step away"! |publisher=DEAD PRESS! |author=Zach Redrup |date=September 24, 2018 |accessdate=September 24, 2018}}</ref>

On 3 May 2021, Mark Osborne and Lee Munday announced a new band Hurtless that also features Brian MacDonald from [[PVRIS]]. Lee's Instagram post included the caption "Moving on..." hinting at a full break up for Moose Blood.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Munday |first1=Lee |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/COayLXqp2C4Ho0YvVMM2Tg5ThVesw3uiaA1IMw0/?utm_medium=copy_link |website=Instagram}}</ref> The next month Hurtless released their debut single, ''Slower''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.deadpress.co.uk/news-hurtless-drop-debut-single-slower/|title = NEWS: Hurtless drop debut single, 'Slower'!|date = 8 June 2021}}</ref>

==Reception==
Moose Blood has been described as [[emo]],<ref name=AP.netBOsingle/> [[pop punk]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.punknews.org/review/14566/moose-blood-blush|title=Moose Blood Blush|publisher=Punknews.org|date=August 17, 2016|accessdate=April 23, 2018}}</ref> and [[emo pop]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Walsh|first1=Grace|title=Moose Blood "Blush" Review|url=https://theboar.org/2016/08/moose-blood-blush-review/|accessdate=19 February 2018}}</ref> Their 2013 EP ''[[Moving Home]]'' is "like a combination of [[Brand New (band)|Brand New]]'s ''[[Deja Entendu]]'', [[The Get Up Kids]]' ''[[Something to Write Home About]]'' and [[American Football (band)|American Football]]'s [[American Football (1999 album)|self-titled]]."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3162201|title=ABSOLUTExclusive & Featured Stream: Moose Blood - News Article|work=AbsolutePunk|publisher=SpinMedia|author=DiVincenzo, Alex |date=3 May 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20150101051755/http://www.absolutepunk.net/showthread.php?t=3162201 |archive-date=January 1, 2015 |accessdate=19 March 2016}}</ref>
