'''Wild Rivers''' is a Canadian folk band from [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]]. It consists of Devan Glover (vocals), Khalid Yassein (vocals and guitar), and Andrew Oliver (guitar and bass). The band released their self-titled debut album in 2016. Their second studio album ''Sidelines'' will be released on February 4, 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Wild Rivers Release New Track And Announce Highly Anticipated New Album SIDELINES Out February 4th {{!}} Nettwerk|url=http://nettwerk.com/news/2021/wild-rivers-release-new-track-and-announce-highly-anticipated-new-album-sidelines-out|access-date=2021-09-10|website=nettwerk.com}}</ref>

==History==
Wild Rivers was formed at [[Queen's University at Kingston|Queen's University]] in [[Kingston, Ontario]], originally as a two piece with Devan Glover and Khalid Yassein.<ref name="thevarsity">{{cite web |last1=Cohen |first1=Reut |title=In conversation with Wild Rivers |url=https://thevarsity.ca/2018/11/19/wild-rivers-indie-band-toronto-paper-kites/ |publisher=[[The Varsity (newspaper)|The Varsity]] |access-date=21 June 2021}}</ref> 

In April 2016, the band released their self-titled debut album.<ref name="americansongwriter">{{cite web |title=Bringin’ It Backwards: Interview with Wild Rivers |url=https://americansongwriter.com/bringin-it-backwards-interview-with-wild-rivers/ |publisher=[[American Songwriter]] |access-date=21 June 2021}}</ref><ref name="exclaim">{{cite web |last1=Murphy |first1=Sarah |title=Wild Rivers 'Wild Rivers' (album stream) |url=https://exclaim.ca/music/article/wild_rivers-wild_rivers_album_stream |publisher=[[Exclaim!]] |access-date=21 June 2021}}</ref> In 2018, the group opened for Australian band [[The Paper Kites]] on the North American leg of their tour and released an EP titled ''Eighty-Eight''.<ref name="thevarsity" /> 

In 2019 the band released a song titled "I Do". The following year the band released their second EP, ''Songs To Break Up To'', through [[Nettwerk Music Group]].<ref name="americansongwriter1">{{cite web |last1=Crone |first1=Madeline |title=Wild Rivers Releases “Kinda Feels Alright,” Announces 2020 Tour Dates |url=https://americansongwriter.com/wild-rivers-releases-kinda-feels-alright-announces-2020-tour-dates/ |publisher=American Songwriter |access-date=21 June 2021}}</ref> Later in 2020, Wild Rivers released "Pink Shades".<ref name="americansongwriter2">{{cite web |title=Wild Rivers Releases New Song, “Pink Shades” |url=https://americansongwriter.com/wild-rivers-releases-new-song-pink-shades/ |publisher=American Songwriter |access-date=21 June 2021}}</ref> The group released another in January 2021 titled "Love Gone Wrong".<ref name="americansongwriter3">{{cite web |title=Wild Rivers Share Soul-Stirring New Song “Love Gone Wrong” |url=https://americansongwriter.com/wild-rivers-share-soul-stirring-new-song-love-gone-wrong/ |publisher=American Songwriter |access-date=21 June 2021}}</ref> In June, Wild Rivers released their latest song, "Amsterdam".<ref name="indie88">{{cite web |last1=Subic |first1=Meg |title=Listen: Toronto indie-trio Wild Rivers share new single ‘Amsterdam’ |url=https://indie88.com/listen-toronto-indie-trio-wild-rivers-share-new-single-amsterdam/ |publisher=[[CIND-FM]] |access-date=21 June 2021}}</ref>

Their second studio album ''Sidelines'' will be released on February 4, 2022.
