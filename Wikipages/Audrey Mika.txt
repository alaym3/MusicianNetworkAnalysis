'''Audrey Mika Armacost''' (born July 28, 2000), known professionally as '''Audrey Mika''', is an American singer-songwriter.

==Early life==
Audrey Mika Armacost<ref>{{cite web |title=Audrey Mika Armacost |url=https://clicheteenjournalblog.weebly.com/audrey-mika-armacost.html |website=Cliché Teen Journal Blog & Feature Page |accessdate=October 5, 2020 |language=en}}</ref> grew up in [[Oakland, California|Oakland]], [[California]]. She grew up listening to [[Jazz music|jazz]] artists including [[Ella Fitzgerald]] and [[Louis Armstrong]], and was trained in [[vocal jazz]] throughout her childhood.<ref name="harper" /> Prior to starting her music career, Mika danced competitively for 14 years until quitting in 2018.<ref name="paper">{{cite web |last1=Wetmore |first1=Brendan |title=Audrey MiKa Is Outgrowing Her Toy Microphone |url=https://www.papermag.com/audrey-mika-youtube-2640065973.html |website=PAPER |accessdate=October 4, 2020 |language=en |date=August 28, 2019}}</ref> She posted her first video, an original song, to [[YouTube]] at the age of 15.

==Career==
Mika released her debut studio album, ''Are We There Yet'', in September 2018, through [[Spinnup]].<ref name="paper" /> Following this release, she began posting covers of songs by artists such as [[Billie Eilish]] and [[Ariana Grande]] to YouTube using a [[Magic Mic|toy microphone]] from her bedroom.<ref name="paper" /> Her second studio album, ''Level Up'', was a [[bedroom pop]] effort made entirely using [[GarageBand]], and released in February 2019.<ref name="paper" /> In June 2019, she released the single "Y U Gotta Be Like That", which went viral on the video-sharing platform [[TikTok]].<ref name="harper">{{cite web |last1=Gonzales |first1=Erica |title=Audrey Mika Went from Singing into a Toy Microphone to Selling Out Shows |url=https://www.harpersbazaar.com/culture/art-books-music/a31995946/audrey-mika-interview/ |website=Harper's BAZAAR |accessdate=October 4, 2020 |date=March 31, 2020}}</ref>

Her debut [[extended play]], ''5 A.M.'', was released in February 2020 through [[RCA Records]]. ==Artistry==
Mika has listed [[Ariana Grande]], [[Imagine Dragons]], and [[Marina and the Diamonds|MARINA]] as influences on her music during her childhood, and listed [[Jhené Aiko]], [[Dominic Fike]], [[Omar Apollo]], and [[Daniel Caesar]] as influences as well.<ref name="harper" />
