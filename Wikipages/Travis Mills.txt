'''Travis Tatum Mills''' (born April 12, 1989), previously known by his stage name '''T. Mills''', is an American rapper, singer, and actor from [[Riverside, California]]. Following his name change from T. Mills to Travis Mills, he began hosting an eponymous show on [[Apple Music 1]].<ref name="iamtravismills1">{{cite web|url=http://iamtravismills.com |title=I Am Travis Mills – Homepage |publisher=Iamtravismills.com |access-date=July 10, 2015}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Travis Tatum Mills was born on April 12, 1989 in [[Riverside, California]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Fleischer |first=Adam |url=http://www.xxlmag.com/rap-music/the-break/2012/01/the-break-presents-t-mills/ |title=The Break Presents: T. Mills – XXL |publisher=Xxlmag.com |date=January 25, 2012 |access-date=July 10, 2015}}</ref> At the age of 5, Mills' uncle gave him his first guitar and at the age of 15 Mills stopped playing sports to start a [[punk rock]] band.<ref name="www.theboombox.com">{{cite web | title = T. Mills Raps of Willpower in 'Diemonds,' Reveals New Song – The Boombox | url = http://www.theboombox.com/2012/09/21/t-mills-diemonds/ | date =n.d.| access-date = November 2, 2012}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== 2008–2010: Career beginnings and ''Ready, Fire, Aim!'' ===
At the age of 17, Mills began writing songs in his bedroom, using music software such as Apple's Garage Band. At the age of 18, Mills began posting some of his music on his [[Myspace]] page.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/t-mills-2/biography/ |title=Bio &#124; Career |publisher=MTV |date=n.d.|access-date=July 10, 2015}}</ref> In 2008, Mills signed a deal to the indie record label [[Uprising Records]].

=== 2016–present: Signing to First Access Entertainment, ''While You Wait'' and Girlfriends ===
On January 22, 2016, Travis announced the release of his new single, called "Don't Need Much".<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.idolator.com/7620891/travis-mills-dont-need-much-premiere |title = Travis Mills Kicks off 2016 with "Don't Need Much": Idolator Premiere|date = January 22, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/dont-need-much-single/id1072821239?app=itunes&ign-mpt=uo%3D4 |title = Don't Need Much – Single by T. Mills}}</ref> The song was released through [[Republic Records]], which was made available for streaming on [[SoundCloud]] on the same day of release.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://soundcloud.com/tmills/dontneedmuch | title=Don't Need Much}}</ref> The beat from "Don't Need Much" was sampled from a single named "[[Drank & Drugs]]", performed by Dutch rappers [[Lil' Kleine]] and Ronnie Flex. Travis signed a new label deal with First Access Entertainment and released his fourth EP under the label, titled ''While You Wait'' on April 8, 2016. The EP contains six tracks.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://djbooth.net/albums/review/04086-travis-mills-while-you-wait-ep|title=Travis Mills – While You Wait EP|work=DJBooth|publisher=The DJ Booth LLC|author=Brendan V|date=April 9, 2016}}</ref><ref name="WYWiTunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/while-you-wait-ep/id1100433242|title=While You Wait – EP by Travis Mills on Apple Music|work=[[iTunes]], [[Apple Music]]|date=April 8, 2017}}</ref> Travis walked in the New York and Paris fashion shows in July 2016, and was named one of the best dressed by ''[[GQ Magazine]]''.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.gq.com/gallery/ten-best-dressed-men-of-the-week-7-16-16 |title = The 10 Best Dressed Men of the Week}}</ref>

In 2020, Mills formed the band Girlfriends with [[Nick Gross]], with the duo citing [[Blink-182]], [[5 Seconds of Summer]] and [[All Time Low]] as the band's musical influences.<ref>{{Cite web|title=girlfriends|url=https://bignoise.com/talent/girlfriends/|access-date=2020-08-21|website=Big Noise|language=en-US}}</ref> The band released their first self titled album on October 23, 2020<ref>{{cite web|url=http://highlightmagazine.net/2020/10/02/girlfriends-announce-debut-self-titled-album/|title=girlfriends announce debut self-titled album|work=Highlight Magazine|last=Dowd|first=Rachael|date=October 2, 2020|access-date=October 2, 2020}}</ref> featuring 14 tracks. The production led by [[John Feldmann]] was a success with almost 20 songs being written in a few months.<ref>{{Cite web|title=girlfriends Announce Debut Self-Titled Album Produced By John Feldmann|url=https://www.thehypemagazine.com/2020/10/girlfriends-announce-debut-self-titled-album-produced-by-john-feldmann/|access-date=2021-01-28|website=The Hype Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Musical style ==
Mills' music has been described as genre-blending due to his mix of singing and rapping, being mostly labeled as a [[Hip hop music|hip hop]] and [[synthpop]] artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.younghollywood.com/videos/music/hip-hop/t-mills-backstage-diaries.html|title=T. Mills: Backstage Diaries|work=[[Young Hollywood]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://987ampradio.cbslocal.com/2014/02/26/artist-on-the-rise-t-mills/|title=Artist on the Rise: T. Mills|work=[[98.7 FM]]|date=February 26, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://chippewa.com/dunnconnect/news/local/columbia-records-singer-t-mills-to-perform-april-in-great/article_dfb5aef4-43b3-5ad9-807f-9ed361ef36d8.html|title=Columbia Records singer T. Mills to perform April 1 in Great Hall|work=Dunn County News|author=Barbara|date=March 27, 2015}}</ref> He is known for mixing pop and electronic songs with hip hop and [[Contemporary R&B|R&B]].<ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/brianpetchers/2012/12/10/t-mills-the-playlist-artist/ |title=T.Mills: The Playlist Artist |work=Forbes |date=April 18, 2012 |access-date=April 1, 2013}}</ref> Mills says that his genre-blending music is a result of his exposure to many different genres during childhood.<ref name="aol.com">{{cite web  | title = AOL.com Video – Rapper T. Mills on His Beginnings as a Musician | url = http://www.aol.com/video/rapper-t-mills-on-his-beginnings-as-a-musician/517348293/ | date =n.d.| access-date = November 3, 2012 }}</ref> He has said that "[my] mom was a huge [[Queen (band)|Queen]] fan, my dad's a diehard [[Elvis]] fan, my uncle is a hip-hop and R&B head, my sister listened to [[Britney Spears]], [[country music|country]] and boy bands and I was into [[pop punk]], [[punk rock]], [[Heavy metal music|metal]] and just into everything."<ref name=autogenerated5 /> He indicated that his rapping style was influenced by [[Kanye West]] and [[Bone Thugs-n-Harmony]], among others.<ref name=autogenerated4 />

== Acting career ==
In March 2016, Mills made his acting debut in the [[Netflix]] [[comedy series|original comedy series]] ''[[Flaked]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2015/08/emily-bergl-cast-abc-american-crime-travis-mills-flaked-1201507008/|title=Emily Bergl Joins ABC's 'American Crime'; Travis Mills In 'Flaked'|last1=Petski|first1=Denise|date=August 26, 2015|website= Daedline|access-date= March 8, 2016}}</ref>

== Reality TV gigs ==

In 2019, he co-starred in a spinoff of ''[[Catfish: The TV Show]]'' called ''[[ghosting (relationships)|Ghosted: Love Gone Missing]]'', where he teamed up with [[The Bachelorette (American TV series)|Bachelorette]] star [[Rachel Lindsay (television personality)|Rachel Lindsay]] to help people find those who have ghosted them and find the real reasons for the sudden disappearance in the ghosted persons life.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.eonline.com/news/1061248/mtv-taking-on-ghosting-with-new-reality-show-starring-rachel-lindsay-and-travis-mills|title= MTV Taking on Ghosting With New Reality Show Starring Rachel Lindsay and Travis Mills |last=Hawkins|first=Amber|date=September 25, 2019|access-date=September 25, 2019}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Mills is heavily tattooed, which includes a full sleeve on one arm and a [[Bone Thugs-n-Harmony]] tattoo on his chest, in addition to many others. He also designated the letters "FTH" ("Fuck The Haters", "Forever The Highest") on his forehead, the "O" with a heart behind his left ear representing his sisters name and the word "Patience" tatted on his knuckles.<ref name="buzzworthy.mtv.com"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/videos/interview/t-mills/719314/buzzworthy-blog-exclusive-t-mill-q.jhtml |title=Buzzworthy Blog Exclusive: T. Mills |via=YouTube |date=November 30, 2012 |access-date=April 1, 2013}}</ref> Mills also got the word "Gnarly" tattooed across his stomach at age 18. A couple months after that he gave himself a tattoo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thesource.com/articles/258602/%23TatTales-Week-2%3A-T.-Mills-(@ilovetmills)/160/Culture?thesource-prod=0u5egnikv3a24t3v93238rtbd0 |title=#TatTales Week 2: T.
